# Gray Cowl of Nocturnal

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg/200px-OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg) Gray Cowl of Nocturnal (Oblivion) *You are beautiful. Shadowed, beautiful, and hidden. They cannot see you, darkness clings like a lover. They will not remember you, gray upon their mind. She breathed you into being. Be the deeper darkness.*
—Romien Garvette The Gray Cowl of Nocturnal (or the **Shadow Queen's Cowl**) is a daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that once belonged to [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). It takes the form of a dark leather cowl, which obscures the face of the wearer. Nocturnal is revered as a god by thieves across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HWTS-1)</sup> Her reputation as the Mistress of Shadows has sometimes led thieves to attempt to steal an item from her to prove their greatness. As Nocturnal is usually depicted wearing a cloak and a cowl, it is around these two items that legends have arisen. The story of the theft of Nocturnal's cloak is probably fiction,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PS-2)</sup> as is at least one story of the theft of the cowl,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SOH-3)</sup> but the cowl is known to have left her possession.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IGC-4)</sup> It appears to have been stolen by [Emer Dareloth](Lore_Emer_Dareloth.md), a guildmaster of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBE-5)</sup> although a curse bestowed upon the cowl by Nocturnal meant that the name of cowl's owner is lost to mortal memory.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IGC-4)</sup> *"Whosoever wears it shall be lost in the shadows. His true nature shall be unknown to all who meet him. His identity shall be struck from all records and histories. Memory will hide in the shadows, refusing to record the name of the owner to any who meet him. He shall be known by the cowl and only by the cowl."* This curse was broken through the use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md), causing history to be rewritten as a result, as such was the strength of the curse lifted that lifting it [altered time](Lore_Time_Magic.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBE-5)</sup>

The cowl is inscribed with the phrase "Shadow hide [y]ou" written in the [Daedric Alphabet](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md), though as is common in the Daedric alphabet, the letter Yahkem (Y) is omitted from the inscription. The phrase is also used as a code between thieves, either as a form of praise to Nocturnal or a literal expression of hope.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HWTS-1)</sup> The [Nightingales](Lore_Nightingales.md) of Nocturnal believe the true meaning of the phrase is a reference to their fate after death, when their souls journey to the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) and become one with the shadow, the cloak that covers their fellow thieves.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NGV1-6)</sup> The Office of the Unseeing Eye, a section of the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), expressed a desire to find the cowl in order to see if the curse does exist, and research further on how to remove it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IGC-4)</sup>

## History
*I am [The Stranger](Lore_The_Stranger.md). That is all you need to know. That, and I am no-one to be trifled with.*
— [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md), under the effect of Nocturnal's curse<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The cowl was constructed with soft thieves' leather that resemble Huntlands-Blessed Leather from [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md) with different runes, but the majority of the materials originate from Nocturnal's [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md). Examples include the stiches made from the realm's [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md), [Gloamium](Lore_Gloamium.md) rivets that trap light, and the materials were coated in an oil that imbued the cowl with [literal shadows](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md). The [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)'s Antiquarian Circle excavated the cowl alongside poetry associated with the [Blackfeather Court](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DDFII-8)</sup>

The *[Song of Hrormir](Lore_Song_of_Hrormir.md)* attributes Nocturnal as a "Shadowy Hag" who hid her hideous form with her gray cowl. In the epic narrative, a soul of a man named Darfang was captured by Nocturnal, and Darfang was being puppeted to fight his companion Hrormir. Seeing his friend's grief, Hrormir bargained to take his place as Nocturnal's plaything to free his friend's soul. Hrormir exploited wordplay in his oath to make his loyalty non-binding, and then stole her cowl from her and brought back light to the lands. In the morning after Hrormir had bedded Nocturnal, everyone saw her hideous form free from obstruction. In exchange for the cowl, Nocturnal released Hrormir and accepted her defeat.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SOH-3)</sup> The veracity of the story is unknown, but an artifact mentioned in the story named [Hrormir's Icestaff](Lore_Hrormir%27s_Icestaff.md) exists.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HI-9)</sup>

After having been stolen by Emer Dareloth, the cursed cowl was handed down from one guildmaster to the next in the [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) branch of the Thieves Guild. This forced the guildmasters to work under the persona of the [Gray Fox](Lore_The_Gray_Fox.md). Thought to be a myth by most members of the public, the identity of the Gray Fox came about over the course of three hundred years, as the guildmasters lost their individual identities and became known only as the bearer of the Cowl. Legends propagated around the mysterious master thief, who was thought to possess immortality and supernatural thieving abilities. He was viewed as a hero by the beggars and the lower classes of Cyrodiil, but he was hated by the nobility and the [Imperial Watch](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). The curse was broken in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), when Count [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md), the contemporary Gray Fox, organized the theft of an Elder Scroll from the Imperial Library in the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md). Umbranox used the Scroll to remove Nocturnal's curse and as a result history was rewritten for such was the power of the curse that lifting it could "alter time itself". Without the curse, the past guildmasters were able to work openly, and the Thieves Guild became more prosperous. Umbranox regained his identity and his title as Count of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), and the Cowl was passed on to [a new guildmaster](Lore_Hero.md). Even without the curse, the Cowl still had the ability to cloak the true identity of the wearer, and it was still identified with the infamous Gray Fox.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBE-5)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Gray Fox remains a revered character among thieves.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SR-11)</sup>

At an unknown time, the Gray Cowl made its way into [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and so did the mantle of the Gray Fox to a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) by the name of Pjofr Ice-Blade. Instead of becoming the head of the Ice-Blade clan, he left it, pursuing his own interests out of greed, and as a result the clan declined. <sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SJ-12)</sup> Scoundrels then targeted the Ice-Blade clan, attempting to steal their wealth. Erikur, the Thane of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) along with other conspirators plotted to kill Pjofr's sister, leaving her son, Smaref, as easy pickings. By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), They succeeded in making her cause of death look like it was due to an illness. Smaref was left an orphan and last of the Ice-Blades, and the scoundrels began manipulating him. They obtained the boy's consent to transfer his family's lands over to them, and sent him to [Riften](Lore_Riften.md). <sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LTG-13)</sup> After hearing of the passing of his sister, Pjofr wanted to step into Smaref's life and take the role of his guardian. He investigated from the sidelines, he heard of Smaref's adoption by one of Riften's wealthy. He saw that while his new father figure was caring, his wife considered the child nothing but a street urchin. He was let into their household for dinner one day, and saw that the wife wasn't good for Smaref. She revealed that Bolli was asked to take in the child for adoption by Solitude's Jarl.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-NJ-14)</sup> Pjofr investigated further into this and discovered the plot to strip the Ice-Blades of their wealth.

Pjofr decided that the person that deserved to inherit the Gray Cowl of Nocturnal was the person who was willing to help him get back at Erikur, and faked the death of the Gray Fox. A stranger investigated the grave of the Gray Fox, and discovered a note that it was a fabrication, and took up the task to gain the Cowl. Following Pjofr's instructions, the adventurer swiped various important documents with fabrications, ruining the scoundrel's claims to the Ice-Blade clan's lands. Finally, the adventurer delved into the Nordic ruin of Silverdrift Lair, and took The ancestral Sword of Clan Ice–Blade, so that Pjofr could prove his ancestry. With the tasks done, the adventurer returned to the Gray Fox's grave, and Pjofr came in-person to grant the Cowl to them, passing on the Gray Fox's mantle.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
