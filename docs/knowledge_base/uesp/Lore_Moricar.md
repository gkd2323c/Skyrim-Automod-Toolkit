Moricar (also called **Moricar the Middling**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWo S-1)</sup> was an Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the second emperor of the [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) dynasty called the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), who ruled the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). He was the longest-reigning Longhouse Emperor, ruling for over twenty years. During his reign, he worked to fulfill the bargain known as the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md) that his father made with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup> In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), Moricar led an invasion of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md), but High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md)'s army attacked and routed his army outside of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), leaving Moricar gravely wounded. As a result of his wounds, Moricar died the following year.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> He was preceded by his father, [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md), and succeeded by his son, [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life
Moricar was born in [2E 518](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_518) to the Reachman chieftain, [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) the Black Drake.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> Moricar spent most of his youth in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), so he was raised in the ancient traditions and culture of the Reachmen.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup> In [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533), Durcorach and his horde of Reachmen conquered [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and he was crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HMSIS-5)</sup>

After the conquest, Durcorach appointed [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) tutors to educate Moricar, including High Chancellor [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup> Moricar's Imperial education blunted his upbringing in the Reach, which enabled him to navigate both the Imperial and Reachfolk worlds in a way that his father never could.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup> By [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), Moricar had become a vital advisor to his father.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> In 2E 541<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TOAM3-6)</sup> or [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-IEGTB-7)</sup> Durcorach invaded [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and was killed in a battle outside of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TOAM3-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-8)</sup> As soon as the news of Durcorach's death reached the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), Moricar declared himself emperor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> Moricar's son, [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), was born in 2E 542.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup>

### Emperor of Cyrodiil
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Moricar%27s_Coronation.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Moricar%27s_Coronation.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Moricar%27s_Coronation.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Moricar%27s_Coronation.jpg) Moricar's Coronation Immediately after Moricar's accession to the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md), he had to decide the best course of action to bring High Rock under control in the aftermath of Durcorach's campaign in the province. The Emperor debated with High Chancellor Abnur Tharn and Chief Councilor [Tarnian Lovidicus](Lore_Tarnian_Lovidicus.md) about the risks and benefits of the different solutions. He considered the risks and benefits, and when he reached a decision, the Emperor ordered Tharn to negotiate peace with High Rock, as he considered the invasion to be his father's quagmire and did not want it to become his own.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup> Moricar also ordered an aide to the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) named Sentanus Marillin to prepare a decree recognizing the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), an alliance of High Rock's kingdoms formed in response to Durcorach's invasion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-8)</sup> There were reports of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) attempting to establish a presence in [Bloodrun Cave](Lore_Bloodrun_Cave.md) early in Moricar's reign, but the assassins were driven out.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CTLd-9)</sup>

Moricar's Imperial Court was said to differ from his father's.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup> Some had viewed Durcorach as a "savage playing at emperor" who lacked understanding of the procedures, protocols, and culture of the Imperial Court.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> In contrast, Moricar's Imperial education enabled him to understand these things and engage in the court's political nuances.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> He could also demonstrate the ferocity and ruthlessness that made his father so feared, but Moricar's fear included a grudging respect.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup> His upbringing in the Reach, combined with his Imperial education, turned him into a strong and capable leader.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Awt LE-4)</sup>

In [2E 559](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_559), Moricar saw the need to establish some degree of Imperial authority over the increasingly restless clans of the Reach, so he chose to appoint a trusted [kinfolk](Lore_Blackdrake_Clan.md) to the task.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HMSIS-5)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RDM-10)</sup> He selected his kinsman, [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), and sent him to the City of Stone to serve as the Imperial Governor of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HMSIS-5)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RDM-10)</sup> To reassure the clan chiefs that he did not intend to impose Imperial law in its entirety on the free Reachmen, Moricar limited Caddach's writ to Markarth and the lands immediately surrounding the city.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RDM-10)</sup> Caddach's role was to both rule the city and maintain peace between the restive [Reachfolk Clans](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HMSIS-5)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RDM-10)</sup>

At some point during his reign, when Leovic came back from a successful campaign against border raiders, Moricar, the [Tharn](Lore_House_Tharn.md) family, the Elder Council, and great nobles were in the Great Hall to receive him. Moricar praised his son and decreed that he could name his reward. Prince Leovic did not hesitate, walking right up to the Tharns and saying, "Your Majesty, I want the Chancellor's daughter." He spoke of Abnur Tharn's daughter [Clivia](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), and though the Chancellor had many daughters, it was understood by all which one he meant.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCCT-11)</sup>

### Four Ambitions
Prior to Durcorach's conquest of the [Imperial Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), he entered into a fifty-year bargain with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) to ensure the success of his conquest and secure his rule for generations.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ADi S-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-XBo C-13)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup> Upon Durcorach's death, Moricar inherited the bargain that his father had made with the Prince of Destruction, which involved creating the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-XBo C-13)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> Moricar made his own bargain with Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup> As part of the bargain, Moricar permitted servants of Mehrunes Dagon to abduct Elegian, a [moth priest](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) and renowned seer.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOMPEd-14)</sup> The Emperor then induced Elegian to produce visions of how Dagon might spread his cataclysms across [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and gain control over the mortal world, which resulted in the designing of the [cataclysts](Lore_Cataclyst.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOTAd-15)</sup> Moricar made his bargain with the Lord of Fire and Flood to ensure his imperial line would gain power above all other mortals.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOTAd-15)</sup>

Moricar understood that the bargains with Mehrunes Dagon made it imperative to ensure the survival of his bloodline, so he took consorts and fathered a number of children. He acknowledged some of the children, while others were unacknowledged, and some were not even aware that Moricar was their father.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup>

Moricar had a number of advisors, among them witchfolk from the Reach, mystics, charlatans, favor-seekers, and influence-peddlers. In [2E 556](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_556) or [557](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_557), [Celdina](Lore_Celdina.md), a member of the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md), arrived at the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and presented herself to Moricar, who rewarded her with a minor court appointment. Celdina was constantly by the Emperor's side, but she rarely offered him any counsel in front of the rest of the Imperial Court and disappeared for months at a time.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MTCSC-16)</sup> The true purpose of Celdina's presence in the Imperial Court was that Mehrunes Dagon appeared to her in a dream, commanded her to become Moricar's consort, and instructed her to bear a child for the Emperor.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-17)</sup> Dagon also forbade anyone from knowing the child's parentage.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-17)</sup> Celdina became pregnant with Moricar's daughter around [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), causing her to leave the Imperial Court.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MTCSC-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-17)</sup> Later, Celdina gave birth to their daughter [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-17)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg) Moricar witnesses the empowerment of Calia and Destron The preparations and rituals required to create the Four Ambitions were conducted in secret by the Order of the Waking Flame.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> In [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561), Moricar and Leovic spent much of their time in the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) preparing sacrifices to Mehrunes Dagon and rituals that would see the creation of the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> During this time, he witnessed [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md), the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame and a member of the Elder Council, sacrifice Ramin and Lestra Brolus and imbue Calia and Destron with parts of Mehrunes Dagon's [egonymic](Lore_Egonymic.md), which transformed them into two of the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Wo D-18)</sup> Around this time, he also oversaw the rituals that transformed his daughter Mairead and Sombren into Ambitions.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOMd-19)</sup>

He did not trust Mehrunes Dagon to uphold his end of the bargain, and as a contingency, he prepared Mairead as a weapon that could be used to banish Dagon, modifying the ritual used to imbue her with part of Dagon's egonymic for that purpose.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOMd-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PHJ-20)</sup>

### Invasion of Western Skyrim
In [2E 562](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_562), Moricar began planning an invasion of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWo S-1)</sup> In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), Moricar led his forces out of the Reach and into High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md)'s territory.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> Moricar and the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) met no resistance until they approached the gates of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> At Solitude, Svargrim's army fell upon Moricar's forces and routed them in a single battle.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWo S-1)</sup> Moricar survived and managed to make it back to the Imperial City, but he was gravely wounded.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> After his return to the Imperial City, Moricar ordered the punishment of the legionaries who survived the invasion.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOHd-21)</sup> Moricar never fully recovered from his injuries, despite the best efforts of both Imperial and Reachfolk healers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup> He succumbed to his injuries in [2E 564](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_564) and was succeeded by Leovic.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup>

### Afterlife in the Deadlands
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/ON-npc-Emperor_Moricar_03.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Emperor_Moricar_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Emperor_Moricar_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Emperor_Moricar_03.jpg) Emperor Moricar's soul in the Deadlands Upon his death, Moricar's [soul](Lore_Soul.md) was sent to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLA-22)</sup> Moricar passed responsibility for the Four Ambitions to Leovic.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-So MI-2)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOMd-19)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOLd-23)</sup> Leovic was killed in the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), and he failed to pass responsibility for the Four Ambitions to an heir, leading to the declaration that the Longhouse Emperors had defaulted on their bargains with Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SHot LE-3)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-XBo C-13)</sup> Eventually, the souls of Moricar, Durcorach, and Leovic were imprisoned in the Brandfire Reformatory in the Deadlands, where [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) tortured them by fusing the Longhouse Emperors' souls with a chunk of [iron](Lore_Iron.md) slag.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLA-22)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOTAd-15)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Mairead began working to defeat Mehrunes Dagon, and she underwent a ritual to uncover any information in her memories that could aid in defeating the Prince, which revealed that Moricar's soul was imprisoned in the Brandfire Reformatory.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLA-22)</sup> Mairead and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) used Moricar's amulet to lead them to the Brandfire Reformatory Forge, where they released the Longhouse Emperors' souls from a chunk of iron slag.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLA-22)</sup> The Vestige then spoke with Moricar, who informed them that Mairead was one of the Four Ambitions and that she was his contingency in case Mehrunes Dagon reneged on his promise to the Longhouse Emperors.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOMd-19)</sup> Moricar also told them that they could use Mairead and the other Ambitions to banish the Prince of Destruction, and that they could find all the details on the Isle of Joys.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOMd-19)</sup> Sombren and Calia, along with [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md) and [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md), interrupted the conversation when they entered the Reformatory Forge through a portal.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLA-22)</sup> Sombren then destroyed the Longhouse Emperors' spirits with frost magic, although he claimed their spirits would reform in the Deadlands eventually.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLA-22)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOSom-24)</sup>

The Vestige and Eveli Sharp-Arrow recovered Moricar's journal from the Isle of Joys and then a Daedric relic from Doomvault Vulpinaz, which explained that they could banish Mehrunes Dagon by using his egonymic.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PHJ-20)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLA-22)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOAE-25)</sup> Afterward, Mairead, with the help of the Vestige, banished Dagon by using his egonymic, preventing Dagon from merging the Deadlands with Nirn.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOAE-25)</sup>
