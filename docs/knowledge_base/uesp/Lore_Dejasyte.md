[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg/200px-MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg) Map of Tamriel (*Morrowind*) The Dejasyte is a desert made up of monochrome grey and tan boulders and dunes,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Seed-1)</sup> located somewhere in the southeast midlands of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Wiwi-2)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

In [Marobar Sul](Lore_Marobar_Sul.md)'s version of *[the Seed](Lore_The_Seed.md)*, the [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) village of Lorikh, located in the Dejasyte, is its main setting. The original story was created in southern Morrowind by [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) [slaves](Lore_Slave.md), and is typically set in [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md) with [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) characters. Marobar Sul later claimed that the original was a retelling of his version.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Seed-1)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_nb1) | The French version of *The Seed* has an in-world [Translator's Notes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ancient_Tales_of_the_Dwemer_Translator%27s_Notes#Volume_II:_The_Seed) that explains that the Dejasyte (there spelled Déjasyte and Déjayste in two instances) is a desert located between [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Wiwi-2)</sup> (of note, *[The Elder Scrolls: Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* had much of mainland Morrowind, northern Black Marsh, the southern Imperial Province, and northern Elsweyr share a biome).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-3)</sup> It also explains that the region is littered with Dwarven ruins, including the site of Lorikh.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Wiwi-2)</sup> The English version (which modified the "Translator's Notes" to "Publisher's Notes") still mentions the Dejasyte region, but does not have the Translator's Note explaining where it is located, and it also casts doubt on Lorikh existing at all.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Seed-1)</sup> |
