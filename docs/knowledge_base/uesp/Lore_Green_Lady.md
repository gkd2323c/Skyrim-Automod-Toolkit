*From the wild came a Bosmer unlike any other. Her eyes were of fire, hair of wind and rain, and all who gazed upon her quaked in fear. All except the Silvenar.*
— *[A Tale Forever Told](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_Tale_Forever_Told)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/ON-place-Treehenge.jpg/200px-ON-place-Treehenge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Treehenge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Treehenge.jpg) Treehenge, the resting place of the Green Ladies [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg/200px-ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg) The Handfasting of Gwaering and Indaenir The Green Lady is one of the two spiritual leaders of the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) people, also known as the **Hunter**, the **Protector**, the **Vengeance of the Green**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YB-1)</sup> **Vengeance of the Woods**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GLPC-2)</sup> and **Her Verdant Highness**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Her existence as the embodiment of the primal ferocity, health, strength, and physicality of the Bosmer is comparable to that of a [demigod](Lore_Demigod.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lo G-4)</sup> The Green Lady and [the Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) are profoundly linked in both story and reality, and there must always be a pair. When one perishes, the other will soon follow.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGLF-5)</sup>

After the death of the previous Green Lady and Silvenar, the [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) will typically reveal the next person to carry on the role. Even before their predecessor dies, a potential Green Lady will often display indications of such through near-supernatural feats.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TGLG-6)</sup> As opposed to the Silvenar, the potential to be the Green Lady seems to be something that can be passed down. An above-average number of Green Ladies have been born within the village of [Deepwoods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deepwoods)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Rondor Diag-7)</sup> and there have even been multiple Green Ladies from within the same family, although this is decidedly uncommon.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Lan Diag-8)</sup>

Green Hunters serve as an extension of the Green Lady, functioning as her scouts, warriors, and her enforcers.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

## Origin

- *"By the blood we consume, by the hills and vales we protect, let the spirit of the Silvenar and the body of the Green Lady become as one. Before Y'ffre and those gathered, for this generation, and those who came before and go after ... be joined!"'* — The Handfast ceremony <sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Dothriel-10)</sup>

As the Green Lady and Silvenar are a constant in the Valenwood, it is difficult to determine who, if anyone, was the first. According to the Green Lady, her memories stretch back to '[the formless times](Lore_Dawn_Era.md)'.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YB-1)</sup> One folktale regarding [the Eldest](Lore_The_Eldest.md), an ancient [strangler](Lore_Strangler.md) that is one of the first of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)'s creations, states that the first pilgrims ever to have visited it were an elderly, childless couple. They tended to the Eldest and, in turn, were granted the roles of Green Lady and Silvenar as thanks.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Te APT-11)</sup>

Whoever the original individual might be, that fact is ultimately unimportant for Bosmer. The Green Lady exists as a story first and foremost, and their beginning is a tale forever told. Within the story, the Silvenar begins their existence as a being who can see all aspects of Valenwood, yet is heartbroken and left empty by the wildness of it all. They then meet the Green Lady, the embodiment of the wild nature of the Bosmer. The Green Lady attacks the Silvenar but the Silvenar does not fight back. Instead, they show her the beauty of [the Green](Lore_Green_Pact.md). As The Green Lady comes to see the beauty of Valenwood as the Silvenar does, the Silvenar's emptiness is filled by her passion for her people. The two of them become bound to another, taming the wilds and empowering the forest.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ATFT-12)</sup>

This story, though nominally their origin, is one that is repeated constantly across Valenwood's history. it reflects their beginning, their now, and even their future.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SIDiag-13)</sup> Each time a Green Lady dies a new one is found, and she will inevitably be bound to the new Silvenar and once more tame the wilds. This is typically done in a special ceremony known as a Handfasting that takes place in the city of [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DDt H-14)</sup> As such, there is no true beginning or end to the story of the Green Lady, only interludes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YB-1)</sup>

## Abilities
Once joined, the Green Lady will become a reflection of the Bosmer, much like the Silvenar. Just as the Silvenar represents the civilized nature of the current Bosmer, the Green Lady represents the raw strength and passion of their primal nature.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DDt H-14)</sup> This naturally will tend to mean the Silvenar is often the first to expire of the two of them, though the Green Lady will always follow soon after.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESODed-15)</sup>

The responsibilities of the Green Lady are much the same as the responsibilities of the Silvenar—the protection of the Bosmer of Valenwood. Unlike the Silvenar, however, the Green Lady will protect her people with far more extreme methods, especially when the Silvenar isn't around to restrain her. When hunting to keep the Green safe for the Bosmer, the Green Lady risks losing herself entirely. Fortunately, her Silvenar is capable of bringing her back.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Wot G-16)</sup>

Once the Silvenar dies, their union is broken and the Green Lady's bestial nature is irrevocably unleashed. Her fury is then turned upon all those involved until she joins him in death. One example of this was seen in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when the Green Lady [Finoriell](Lore_Finoriell.md) swam all the way to [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) to seek vengeance for the murder of her Silvenar.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGLF-5)</sup> After she too inevitably falls, the former Green Lady's spirit will return to the ancient grove of Treehenge. Here, they transition from being a Green Lady into being a Matron, rooting themselves in place and becoming a part of the Green so that the memories of the Green Lady are preserved. In doing so, they will also leave the power of the Green Lady behind for the next person to begin the story.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOHL-17)</sup> Once again, the next Green Lady will take the force in and be revealed by the Spinners, wed to the Silvenar, and continue on the cycle until her own death.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YB-1)</sup>

## History
The village of Deepwoods was originally settled around the shrine of the Green Lady, a sacred tree which is said to be the embodiment of the first Green Lady. Some believe that the tree marks the grave of the first Green Lady, who perished prior to the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md); others believe that the tree is the first Green Lady herself. This sacred site is blessed by Y'ffre and is regularly visited by pilgrims.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Oraneth-18)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Silvenar [Edhelorn](Lore_Edhelorn.md) was assassinated by local [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) during a diplomatic visit to the small island of [Khenarthi's Roost](Lore_Khenarthi%27s_Roost.md) on behalf of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). Unwilling to die without extracting vengeance upon her husband's killers, the Green Lady Finoriell swam the whole way to the southern island of Pyandonea and killed the Maormer indiscriminately until her death.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGLF-5)</sup> Her niece [Gwaering](Lore_Gwaering.md) replaced her as the next Green Lady.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TGLG-6)</sup>

Later that year, Gwaering's jealous ex-lover [Ulthorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulthorn_the_Hound) was manipulated by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) into attempting to usurp the Silvenar's position by taking part in the Handfasting ceremony in his stead. Taking on the moniker of "the Hound", Ulthorn and his forces captured the city of Silvenar and put Gwaering under Hircine's sway.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESORt S-19)</sup> Ulthorn speculated that the reason Hircine wished for him to usurp the Silvenar was that the Green Lady could be seen as the ultimate prey, being the strongest of the Bosmer, the greatest hunters of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSOU-20)</sup> The city was retaken when Ulthorn was defeated by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the new Silvenar, [Indaenir](Lore_Indaenir.md). Gwaering was subsequently rescued and performed the Handfasting ceremony with Indaenir.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESORt S-19)</sup>

Treehenge was also invaded that year by [Worm Cultists](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) who sought to capture the spirits of the previous Green Ladies. The Vestige defeated the cultists and saved their souls, allowing the soul of Finoriell to finally become rooted and join her sisters in the grove.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGLF-5)</sup>
