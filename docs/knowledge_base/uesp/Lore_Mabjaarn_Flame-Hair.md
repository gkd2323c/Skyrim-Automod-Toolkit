#### Maalanrie Direnni (fl. before 2E 582)
[Maalanrie Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maalanrie_Direnni) was one of the members of the influential [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) clan [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md), and the pioneer conjuration mage. She is accredited with the summoning of the first [Iron Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Atronach) [Senche](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-Cat) in history.

The exact details of this event are unknown, however, at some point in history before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) she was captured by a group of bandits and imprisoned inside a cage. According to certain sources she "walked through dreams" and managed to call upon the mighty Atronach steed to free herself from captivity and destroy the cage. She then killed her captors and rode on its back to freedom.

By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the art of conjuring Iron Atronachs shaped like senche was popular among conjuration mages.

#### Maarselok (? - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Maarselok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maarselok), was a powerful azure [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) who emerged in the [Tenmar jungle](Lore_Tenmar.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), having awoken from his resting place deep beneath the mountains of eastern [Grahtwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahtwood) with the release of the great dragon [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid) and his dragon followers from the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus). He used his [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to unleash a corruptive plague called the [Azure Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azure_Blight), it rapidly spread through the jungle, corrupting the life within and bringing it under the dragon's thrall, and threatened all of Grahtwood, and even the [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root) tree, [Green-Sap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Towers), and the [Green](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green) itself. Maarselok's ultimate goal was to use his blight to take control of the [Changeling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Changeling) high priestess of [Shagrath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shagrath), [Selene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Selene), and combine her power with his own, which he believed would give him the ability to shape the world to his liking. According to Selene, Maarselok sought to mold the world to his liking, and was depraved even by dragon standards.

#### King Madanach (fl. 4E 174 - 201)
[Madanach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Madanach), known as the **King in Rags**, was the [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) king of the short-lived independent kingdom in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) that existed from [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174) to [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176) and the leader of the [Forsworn Rebellion](Lore_Forsworn_Rebellion.md). He was a proficient spellcaster, specializing in the arts of ice magic and conjuration. Taking advantage of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)'s preoccupation with the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), in 4E 174, he led other disgruntled Reachmen in the uprising that overthrew the [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) Jarl [Hrolfdir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrolfdir) of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). According to some historians, the two-year period of Reachman independence was relatively peaceful, with only the cruelest Nord landowners being put to death, and the nascent kingdom made overtures to the Empire seeking formal recognition.

However, Hrolfdir and his son, [Igmund](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Igmund), desperate to reclaim the Reach, hired [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) and his militia to retake it in exchange for allowing [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship in the hold, hoping that the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) would not hear of this violation of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md). In 4E 176, Ulfric and his militia besieged and then took Markarth, capturing Madanach in the process.

#### Chief Maddorfa (fl. before 2E 582)

During his tenure Eagleseers were feuding with [Six-Ford Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Six-Ford_Clan). He led his clan to one of the most known battles between those two clans, the Battle of Karthspire Lea, during which he suffered a mortal wound.

#### Emperor Magnus Septim ([3E 79](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_79) - [3E 145](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_145))
[Magnus Septim](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) (sometimes enumerated as **Magnus I**) was the eleventh emperor of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for five years, from [3E 140](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_140) to [3E 145](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_145). During the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), Magnus fought alongside his brother [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) against their nephew [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_III.md) and their sister [Potema](Lore_Potema.md). In [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137), Magnus took part in the final battle of the war, the month-long [Siege of Solitude](Lore_Siege_of_Solitude.md), alongside Cephorus I, which ended when Potema died in her [castle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Palace). Magnus was already sixty-one when he acceded to the throne, and the business of punishing the traitorous kings of the War of the Red Diamond drained much of his remaining strength. He was preceded by his older brother, [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md), and was succeeded by his son, [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_III.md).

#### Warlord Mahgzoor Rockhand (fl. early-mid 1E)
[Mahgzoor Rockhand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mahgzoor_Rockhand) (also spelled **Mahzgoor**), also known as **Bone-Hewer**, a name shared with his blade, was a notorious giant [Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) Warlord who was responsible for leading the Endwise Army, a horde of tusk-folk, into Hammerfell, wreaking havoc and destruction upon the people. #### M'aiq the Liar (fl. [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) to [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md))
[M'aiq the Liar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:M%27aiq_the_Liar) (or **M'Aiq the Liar**) is the name used by certain peculiar [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) from throughout [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history. Each known individual with this name is a strange male Khajiit, who claims great knowledge but speaks of many false or implausible things, with only occasional truths. Comments made by some of these liars, and an inscription on a flute discovered in [Crow's Wood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow%27s_Wood), seem to imply there is a long bloodline of Khajiit with the same name. However, some of the comments made by M'aiq also imply they might somehow be a single immortal individual, given M'aiq's presence throughout at least three different eras. If he was ever asked directly about the topic, it is thought he would likely lie.

M'aiq the Liar is a notorious figure among the inhabitants of Tamriel, with rumors even existing (likely spread by M'aiq himself) about him being responsible for slaying [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur), solving the [Oblivion crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), and ending the [war in Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md). At least one organization regularly sought out M'aiq for information. He is the namesake of a non-alcoholic drink made by Khajiit in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), called the "M'aiq Tale". Some of the Khajiit named M'aiq made cryptic implications regarding [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), which occasionally proved to be true, though it cannot be said whether these comments spoke to deeper or [prophetic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) knowledge, or were simply matters of happenstance.

#### Malborn (fl. 4E 201)
[Malborn](Lore_Malborn.md) is a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who became a crucial informant for the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). His real name isn't Malborn but an alias. The [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) wiped out his family in [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) during a purge, with their murders being covered up as an accident caused by a fire. Ever since the death of his family, he developed a hatred for the Thalmor, and at some point got in contact with [Delphine](Lore_Delphine.md), an agent of the blades.

Delphine later arranged a meeting between Malborn and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). There, Malborn discussed plans to smuggle the Dragonborn's supplies into the [Thalmor Embassy](Lore_Thalmor_Embassy.md) during a party hosted by [Elenwen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elenwen), the First Emissary of the Thalmor in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). At the party, after the Dragonborn influenced a guest to cause a distraction, Malborn quietly led the Dragonborn into a back room to retrieve their supplies and infiltrate the Embassy. Later, Malborn was apprehended by Thalmor soldiers and escorted to a dungeon, where the Dragonborn was in the middle of collecting information and freeing a prisoner. Malborn was rescued,<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Survival)</sup> and fled to [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), where he would later reunite with the Dragonborn. He ended up asking for their help dealing with a Thalmor assassin, and when that was done, Malborn fled to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).

#### General Malgoth (fl. before 2E 582)
[General Malgoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malgoth) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who led the Blackroot Clan and attempted to unite all the Bosmer under his leadership and conquer [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Malgoth traveled throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and saw the greatness that came to those who conquered through military might, a status that he hoped to achieve. Malgoth led the Blackroot Clan during the Bosmer tribal wars. Malgoth and his clan eventually set their sights on the settlement of [Vullain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dread_Vullain), intending to make an example of them for the other villages, with the goal of uniting the Bosmer to strengthen them against their enemies, the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).

Word eventually reached the inhabitants of Vullain that Malgoth intended to march on Vullain. With no way to match the military might of the Blackroot Clan, the villagers turned to the local [nature guardians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirits), the [Nereids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nereid). Knowing that Malgoth and his clan would adhere to the [Meat Mandate](Lore_Green_Pact.md), the villagers convinced the Nereids to provide them with elixirs to poison their bodies to cause the deaths of the Blackroot warriors when they consumed their corpses.

As expected, Malgoth and his army easily defeated the inhabitants of Vullain and consumed their remains per the Meat Mandate. Eventually realizing the trap they had fallen into, Malgoth himself pleaded with the Nereids for a cure to the poison, but they ignored him. Malgoth and the Blackroot Clan soon succumbed to the poison. Following their deaths, Malgoth and his people became bound to the [Ooze](Lore_Ooze.md).

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the site of Dread Vullain was visited by the [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance). One of the servants of the Shade of Prince [Naemon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naemon) resurrected the Blackroot Clan, forcing them to serve him through the [Rite of Theft](Lore_Bosmer.md#The_Rite_of_Theft) by stealing Malgoth's bow. An [agent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soulless_One) of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), along with the mage [Indaenir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indaenir), traversed Dread Vullain and uncovered the mystery surrounding the site. Once the two found Malgoth's remains, they were brought to the [Shadow Wood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Wood), a corrupted form of Valenwood created by the Shade of Naemon. It was there that the two acquired the bow of Malgoth. The agent invoked the Rite of Theft, putting Malgoth's forces at their command. Whether the agent chose to use the clan in the battle against Naemon or released them is unknown; regardless, Dread Vullain was liberated from Naemon.

#### Malkur Valos (fl. 2E 578 - 582)
[Malkur Valos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malkur_Valos) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) member of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and an expert on [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).

Malkur was born the only child of the wizard Maros Valos. When Malkur's mother died in childbirth, Maros summoned a [Golden Saint](Lore_Golden_Saint.md) to protect and care for his son. Malkur was taken to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) by his Daedric guardian and raised among the [Aureals](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aureal) after Maros was killed in a mage's duel. Under their tutelage he acquired steadfast principles of honor and duty, although the inescapable influence of the Isles imbued him with a measure of unpredictability. Eventually, Malkur was drawn to return to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), where his natural magical ability caught the attention of [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md). After joining the Mages Guild, the principles and objectives of the guild became the unwavering core driving his every action, though that was not always apparent to superficial observers.

Following the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) of [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), Malkur was seemingly involved in investigating [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s growing influence, as evidenced by his encounter with [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) in [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Malkur was among those assembled by the Mages Guild to take part in the invasion of Coldharbour. However, after entering the portal on the island of [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md), the invasion force was scattered across the realm by Molag Bal's defenses. Along with a group of fellow mages, Malkur ended up near the Court of Contempt, where the harsh laws of Coldharbour are enforced. The group was beset by [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) and brought before the court, although Malkur himself evaded capture by transforming into a [scamp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp). The trial was nothing but a farce, and the mages were quickly condemned to death. With the help of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), Malkur fought through the Daedric guards and rescued his peers from the court.

He then journeyed to [the Hollow City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Hollow_City), where the scattered invasion force was assembling. He was responsible for opening and maintaining portals to Tamriel, which brought the Hollow City to life and allowed for supply lines and reinforcements to enter Coldharbour safely. Malkur also partook in the invasion's final assault, when he entered the Endless Stair and helped put an end to the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).

Many prominent figures active in the decade following the onset of the Planemeld were immortalized in a painting commemorating their adventures. Among them was Malkur, portrayed in a conversation with [Azandar al-Cybiades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azandar_al-Cybiades) and [Telenger the Artificer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telenger).

The famed summoner Gavis Velas and his brother Ovis Velas were descendants of Malkur. They moved to [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). Ovis was killed after publicly assaulting the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), and Gavis died attempting to avenge him.

#### Mannimarco (?b - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) is the **God of Worms**, the founder and object of worship of the [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm). Before his apotheosis he was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) mage ([Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), by his own account) who had studied with the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). He eventually found his own path after he was exiled for practicing [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy), a discipline the Order deemed dangerous and heretical. He became the most powerful necromancer that [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) has ever seen, becoming widely known as the **King of Worms** and head and archnecromancer of his often renamed Worm Cult. While the exact timing of his transformation into a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) is unknown, he is remembered in later records as the "world’s first of the undying liches," a designation of uncertain meaning.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Lich)</sup> Through his political brilliance, his revival of necromancy from obscurity, and his ascension into godhood, he has left a nearly unparalleled impact on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s history.

Mannimarco's early years are unclear. Should his claim to being an Aldmer be true, he may have lived since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). He was expelled from Artaeum for practicing necromancy around the year [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230), after which he gathered followers who formed the Order of the Black Worm. His rivalry with [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) defined much of his known early history, especially as Mannimarco spread soul trapping and necromantic teachings throughout Tamriel. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) as well as [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) and his faithful were those he would come to consider his greatest enemies in life and undeath.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_KW)</sup> A master manipulator, Mannimarco engaged in politics, gaining and maintaining influence by maneuvering through the courts of rising and falling empires. His most significant political achievement was wielding the true power behind the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) from the shadows during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).

During his attempt to attain godhood, Mannimarco suffered repeated failures, including the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), where he served [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) but was punished by him for attempting to usurp the Prince’s station. These setbacks did not deter him, and his persistence finally bore fruit with his ascension during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). Despite his ascension, he remained directly physically active in Tamriel in some capacity until at least 3E 433, the last known death of his physical body. He ultimately accomplished his main goals: he slew Vanus Galerion, possibly even making a trophy out of his corpse, and Mannimarco's divine body, the **[Necromancer's Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancer%27s_Moon)** (or **the Revenant**), thwarts Arkay by eclipsing his [eponymous planet](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md) every eight days, enabling the creation of [black soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) for his followers to utilize for ensnaring the souls of the sapient.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Mannimarco.md).

By [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), they had been reincorporated into the Empire under the rule of [Titus Mede I](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md); however, Leyawiin remained restless. As of 4E 48, Caro still reigned as count, and his daughters had become infamous for poisoning his guests while they were visiting Leyawiin's court.

#### Prophet Marukh (fl. early 1E)
[The Prophet Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md) (sometimes spelled **Maruhk**; also called **Marukh the [Seer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight)**, the **Prophet-Most-Simian**, and **Monkey Prophet Maruhk**) was a [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) [Imga](Lore_Imga.md) prophet who rose to prominence during the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) and became especially esteemed among the peoples of [Alessian](Lore_Alessia.md) [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Marukh's teachings sparked the massive cultural and spiritual movement known as the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order), a theocracy that transformed the Empire and the continent. He also wrote the [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md), which provided the basis of law during the Order's rule of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md). Marukh is credited with the credo "all are guilty until they have proven themselves innocent", which went on to become the basis for the [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) legal system.

Marukh's teachings both brought identity to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), codifying the pantheon most civilized Tamriellians worship to this day, and brought conflict due to the more severe strictures he espoused. His teachings began to question the validity of Elven rule after speaking to the "Enlightened One", Saint Alessia, in a vision. These sentiments led to an increasingly abstract and unknowable depiction of a [Single God](Lore_The_One.md). They heavily influenced the Alessian Order and their secret sect, the [Marukhati Selective](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukhati_Selective). The priesthood of Marukh saw no difference between spiritual and political matters. This was the religion of the Alessian Empire, and it taught that to resist the Emperor was to resist the Gods.

As Marukh's teachings began to spread, hostility grew between Camoran Dynasty and the Empire. One of Marukh's chief supporters, the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) High King, [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md), traveled to Cyrodiil to urge a joint war against the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), but fell victim of Valenwood's infamous [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md). This ultimately led to the eventual dissolution of the Alessian Reform of Marukh in Valenwood.

#### Moon-Emperor (?b - [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461))

During his rule the lands now known as Elsweyr were open to all sixteen clans, allowing them to roam freely to fulfill their roles under the Moon-Emperor’s guidance. During this time, the sixteen Khajiiti clans wandered throughout the province, each performing a distinct function. For instance, the [Ne Quin-al](Lore_Anequina.md) was made up of warriors who trained their members in martial disciplines and battle tactics. While some claimed that these clans were tied to particular regions or realms, this view was deemed overly simplistic. The Khajiit traveled to where they were needed and desired. As the clans honed their skills and external pressures began to force them into defined roles and smaller geographical areas, the idea of tribal regions started to take shape within the Khajiiti perspective.

The last recorded Moon-Emperor was overthrown by [Darloc Brae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darloc_Brae), marking the beginning of the [Anequine Conquests](Lore_Anequine_Conquests.md) of 1E 461.

Examples of the graceful architecture of the Khajiit could be seen in the city of Dune, which paid tribute to the divine nature of the moons and evoked the ancient memories of Tamriel, when the Khajiiti Moon-Emperor ruled over this entire province.

#### Queen Morgiah ([3E 384](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_384) - ?d)
[Queen Morgiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morgiah) was the daughter of [Queen Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) and [General Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus) of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), and the younger sister of Prince (and future King) [Hlaalu Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Helseth).

Morgiah was named after Symmachus' mother, and was known to be well loved by her father. Sadly, the princess was born in troubled times; the kingdom was plagued with civil unrest as a result of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md). In [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391), at the age of eight, Morgiah accompanied her mother and brother on a journey to the Imperial City. Barenziah was there to plead with [the emperor](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) for aid, and hoped the presence of the children would soften his heart. However the (false) emperor remained unmoved, and that evening word came that Symmachus was killed in a revolt. The surviving royals were forced into exile. That same evening, Barenziah met King [Eadwyre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eadwyre), and within a few years the pair were wed. Morgiah again became a princess, now of the Kingdom of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md).

In the years prior to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Princess Morgiah had garnered a reputation as a "manipulative witch" among the people of the kingdom. She saw little future in Wayrest, as Eadwyre's children, Helseth and [Elysana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elysana), fought for succession. As a result, Morgiah sought to arrange a marriage with King [Reman Karoodil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Karoodil), ruler of the Kingdom of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md). To secure the king's hand, she endeavored to give him that which he desired most—to speak again with a son who had died. To this end, Morgiah made contact with the notorious necromancer [Mannimarco, the King of Worms](Lore_Mannimarco.md), in the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains) of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). With the help of an [Agent of the Blades](Lore_The_Agent.md), the deal was struck. Mannimarco held up his end of the bargain, and in return Morgiah promised to give him her "first", though exactly what this meant remains a mystery. Morgiah and Reman were wed after a three year engagement.

As a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), Queen Morgiah was not particularly well-liked by the culturally xenophobic [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of Firsthold either, and is known to have quelled a revolt of [Trebbite Monks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trebbite_Monks) during the early years of her rule. The couple had two children by the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Goranthir and Rinnala, who were both Dunmer in appearance despite being half-Altmer. Regardless of appearances, however, the children were considered rightful heirs to the throne. In [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) successfully overthrew the monarchy in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) seizing control of the region, leaving Morgiah and her family to an unknown fate.

He was preceded by his father, [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach), and succeeded by his son, [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic).

#### Movarth Piquine (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Movarth Piquine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Movarth_Piquine) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) vampire who likely lived from sometime in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) to [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).

In life, Movarth was ironically a skilled vampire hunter. He began his career as a trainer in the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), which taught him skill in battle and the value of asking questions. He claimed mastery of unarmed combat. He was stoic and almost never surprised due to his dedication to hunt vampires. Tissina Gray directed him to a priest in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to learn about vampires. Using the priest's knowledge, Movarth hunted many different types of vampires across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), including the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the many tribes of northern [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Movarth failed to find any members of the [Cyrodiil Vampyrum Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrodiil_Vampyrum_Order) due to their ability to blend into society. When he returned to his mentor, the priest revealed that he was actually a Cyrodilic vampire himself. Taken by surprise, Movarth fell to the priest, who fed on him and turned him into a vampire. The priest later anonymously published the story in the book [*Immortal Blood*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Immortal_Blood).

Suffering and anguish had marked him early in his life, destroying the features which would have otherwise been handsome. Movarth went on to become a master vampire and create his own coven. Although he was believed to have been destroyed a century earlier, Movarth resurfaced in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) and planned to take over the town of [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) in Skyrim. He turned a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) woman named Alva into a vampire, and commanded her to seduce the guardsmen of Morthal and infect them as well in preparation for a takeover. Movarth could then use the town for protection, and the townsfolk as cattle. His plans were undone when suspicions were raised and the Jarl ordered a [stranger](Lore_Hero.md) to look into the strange events in town. Movarth and his coven were slain when a mob of villagers stormed his underground lair northeast of town.
