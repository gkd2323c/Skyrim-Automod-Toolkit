*Every Wood Elf knows that the more inward the part, the better it tastes. While other races cook their meat until it is dry and bloodless, and waste entrails and brains, the Wood Elf knows that these parts are the juiciest and therefore the most flavorful.*
— *[The Wood Elf Gourmet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Wood_Elf_Gourmet,_Ch._1)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWEG-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg/200px-ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Bosmer_Cuisine.jpg) A selection of meals from Valenwood The Bosmer are bound by the terms of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) to not harm the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The Bosmer do not eat anything made from plant life;<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> fruits are only allowed if they have already fallen from the tree, and the seeds must be replanted, lest they face the wrath of the forest.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OCBB-3)</sup> As such, their recipes tend to include animal products.

The Green Pact is interpreted differently depending on the tribe—foodstuffs such as fruits that fell from the trees, dairy products, honey, mushrooms, and insects are eaten with zest by all but the most ultra-zealous of Pact Bosmer.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Ritualistic cannibalism is a part of Bosmeri culture and a requirement for the upholding of the Green Pact; they traditionally eat their dead and even their enemies as part of upholding the meat mandate although the practice of cannibalism has fallen out of favor in Valenwood during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), and is only practiced by the most zealous upholders of the Green.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WCOTTB-6)</sup>

## Utensils and Tableware
Like other Bosmer tools, their tableware and utensils are often crafted from animal products such as bone, leather, and shell. Exceptions to this like [silver](Lore_Silver.md) cutlery are highly valued.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Bosmer clans use ceremonial skewers to roast a hunter's first kill.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Tweezers, like those made from [monkey](Lore_Monkey.md) bone are used to catch live bugs for eating.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Marrow spoons are used to scoop edible marrow from cracked bones<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SSMS-10)</sup> and pointed eye-spoons are used as well.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

Dishes are carved from the bones of large game<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> and other things like [Mudcrab](Lore_Mudcrab.md) shells.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> large insect shells are lacquered and used for serving platters.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Some wooden dishes do exist however such as those that are sung into existence by Bosmer [magic](Lore_Magic.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> and other more elaborate dishes may even be crafted from porcelain.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Spice shakers are used to store and dispense exotic Bosmeri spices.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

Drinkware includes waterskins fashioned from animal stomachs<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> and supple leather flasks.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Drink pitchers are fashioned from [scorpion](Lore_Scorpion.md) shell<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> and mudcrab chitin.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Mudcrab chitin is also enameled and used to make shot glasses.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> On special occasions like weddings, chalices made of crystal are used for toasts.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

| **Jagga** | **Jagga** is an alcoholic drink made from fermented pig's milk which is popular in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ADIF-39)</sup> In order to prepare it one must: <br> <br> Set several shallow pans of boiled pig's milk cream where the cat can't step on them and leave alone for a day, then stir several times per day thereafter. Strain the whey into a separate vessel, keeping the curds in their pans. Break the curds apart with a spoon, then allow the curds to dry into shards. Stir daily to keep them from spoiling. Once dry, use these shards to thicken skimmed pig's milk, or to add more cream to plain pig's milk. Bottle and let ferment for 3 weeks. <br> <br> One can drink the whey immediately, or use it in other recipes.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-AAC-44)</sup> | | |
| **[Rotmeth](Lore_Rotmeth.md)** | **Rotmeth** is a Bosmer alcohol produced by fermenting meat that is commonly enjoyed at weddings and other celebrations. The meat and organs of the timber mammoth are often preferred but the offal of any large game can be used. These parts are added to spring water and mixed with [Thunderbug](Lore_Thunderbug.md) organs<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-45)</sup> and shells. The mash is left to ferment in large vats in sunny forest clearings where it will be stirred over several weeks to break down the decomposing meat. This process causes an elastic gelatin to form on the surface where it is skimmed off and used for other purposes. Once this process is complete the liquid is strained and the solid remnants are dried and powdered for use in other foods. While it is drinkable after only a few weeks the best rotmeth is left to rest in clay or glass fermenters for years until its strained one final time. <br> <br> The taste is polarizing with those who enjoy it describing its flavor as pleasantly gamey and salty<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ADF7-46)</sup> while its detractors find it to have the flavors of foot fungus and calcite with an aftertaste of desiccated mammoth hide.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-OR-47)</sup> | | |
| **Sun's Dusk Ale** | **Sun's Dusk Ale** is a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) ale made from meat, crosswings, mimics, and green mold. It smells like "juicy steak marinated with plum wine and coriander". [Imga](Lore_Imga.md) dislike the taste of the Ale. To make it one must stretch seventeen strings of [Alfiq](Lore_Alfiq.md) -gut over each barrel, then fresh (or perhaps just aged but not yet "properly" rotted) [boar](Lore_Boar.md) meat is pressed onto the strings, with the brewer being careful not to let any fall before it has properly rotted. Then the meat is dusted with the finely chopped skins of the red-striped frog to make sure the crosswings are happily drunk when they fall. This is done every month for three or more years, until there is enough meat and bugs to fill the barrel. Then it is sealed for about nine years or even longer for the growth of green mold to complete. <br> <br> Enchantments and spells are used in the process as well to attract the crosswings and mimics, spells to keep out the flipbacks, tree-hoppers, and [hoarvor](Lore_Hoarvor.md) young and spells to encourage the green mold and prevent the brown and white molds. <sup>[[48]](#cite_note-48)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-49)</sup> | | |
| **Root Liqueur** | **Root Liqueur** is a rare [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) delicacy that was created by a group of Bosmer in secret using [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) clippings, which is considered blasphemous against the Green Pact by the Bosmer.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-50)</sup> | | |
| **Bloodfroth** | Bloodfroth is a Bosmeri drink.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESOFinnegil-51)</sup> In order to prepare the drink one must: Pour fresh blood into shallow pans and place in the cold cave till nearly congealed. Meanwhile, whip together a couple of chicken egg yolks and melted tallow. <br> <br> Using a sharp knife, slice the blood into chunks, then dice. This is why you let it congeal first—it's impossible to dice fresh blood! <br> <br> Whip the blood slivers with the yolks and tallow, adding more tallow if needed to thin into a beverage. Add salt to taste. <br> <br> Set aside for a month to allow it to ferment.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-AAC-44)</sup> | | |
| **Fermented Honey Liquor** | In order to prepare this liquor one must combine two hives' of strained honey with twice the amount of water, and a handful of hops, cover and let it ferment for a season before tasting. Afterwards it needs to be ladled into bottles which are then sealed with beeswax and put away. It tastes better with age and as such it is advised to let it sit unopened for a significant amount of time.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-AAC-44)</sup> | | |
