*"I stand where I have always stood. Here in Morthal. Now is not the time to fight amongst ourselves. I fear dark days approach and all of Skyrim's strength will be needed."* Idgrod Ravencrone was the Jarl of [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) who ruled at the time of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Idgrod claimed to have [visions](Lore_Sight.md) granted to her by the [Eight](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), which were caused by her subconscious manipulation of Magicka, an ability that her children inherited. As of 4E 201, she was unlike any other jarl in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md); she was a hunched old woman who spoke in riddles and parables, rarely spoke directly, and relied on her visions to govern her hold. However, her wisdom was sometimes misunderstood as the ramblings of an old woman, causing some of her subjects to begin losing faith in her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup>

## Biography
Idgrod experienced visions that were caused by her subconscious manipulation of Magicka, although she did not realize that was how she achieved them; instead, she believed that they were granted to her by the Divines.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Idgrod-2)</sup> Idgrod spent her younger years wandering [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in search of wisdom. During the time she spent wandering Tamriel, Idgrod met a young warrior named Aslfur. Aslfur protected Idgrod during her journey, and the two were married before returning to her home in [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md). Some said that she returned from her journey "touched."<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup> She had two children with Aslfur: a daughter, Idgrod the Younger, and a son, Joric—both of whom inherited her visions, especially Joric.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Joric-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Iggy Jr-4)</sup>

Idgrod was a supporter of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) during the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), but the Empire noted that under pressure, Idgrod would ultimately seek to protect the interests of Morthal, not the Empire.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-THo S-5)</sup> She was also wary of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Idgrod-2)</sup> Idgrod spoke in riddles and parables, rarely spoke directly, and relied on her visions to govern [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup> Her people were largely bemused and frustrated by her leadership during the rebellion and the subsequent return of the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), as they felt that Idgrod was not doing enough to protect them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Jorgen-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Benor-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Thonnir-8)</sup> Idgrod almost never spoke to her subjects, which, when combined with her reliance on her visions, caused some of them to perceive her as an uncaring mystic, not a leader.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Jorgen-6)</sup> Aslfur served as her steward and often insisted that if anyone had business with Idgrod, they should speak to him first, not wanting people to waste her time with mundane problems and believing they did not understand her advice.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Aslfur-9)</sup>

Idgrod allowed the wizard [Falion](Lore_Falion.md) to reside in Morthal, a decision that was unpopular with the townsfolk, who did not trust him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:440</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Idgrod-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Jorgen-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Benor-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Falion-10)</sup> Despite the concerns of Morthal's citizens, Idgrod stood by her decision to allow Falion to live there.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Idgrod-2)</sup>

Idgrod's housecarl, Gorm, protected her for a long time, but as her visions became worse, he came to believe that she was not fit to rule Hjaalmarch during the trying times.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Gorm-11)</sup> Gorm formulated a plan to depose Idgrod, even discussing it with Aldis, a captain in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) who was stationed in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GL-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Aldis-13)</sup> Idgrod and Aslfur were both aware of his plan, but Idgrod refused to allow Aslfur to act on the knowledge, insisting that there was a larger role for Gorm to play.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup>

## Family
- Aslfur — Aslfur was Idgrod's husband. Aslfur served as Idgrod's advisor and steward, and in her old age (and some said her insanity), he became more protective of her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup>
- Idgrod the Younger — Idgrod the Younger was the daughter of Idgrod Ravencrone and Aslfur. The elder Idgrod taught her to seek insight and wisdom.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup> The younger Idgrod inherited her mother's visions, but hers were milder, and she was much better at separating her visions from reality. She was heir of Jarl Idgrod, and it was believed that she would make an excellent jarl.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Iggy Jr-4)</sup>
- Joric — Joric was the youngest child of Idgrod and Aslfur.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup> He suffered from intense visions that frequently left him confused and melancholic.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Joric-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Iggy Jr-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-IN-14)</sup> Joric had frequent nightmares caused by his visions and took potions provided by healers of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) to ease them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-IN-14)</sup> When he was taken to see the healers at the [Temple of Kynareth](Lore_Temple_of_Kynareth.md) in [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md), they said he was otherwise a healthy boy.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Iggy Jr-4)</sup> Some people believed that he was insane because of his visions.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Joric-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Iggy Jr-4)</sup>
