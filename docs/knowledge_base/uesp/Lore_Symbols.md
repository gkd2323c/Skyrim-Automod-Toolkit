## Symbols

### Anhk
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/AR-concept-Temple II-2.jpg/200px-AR-concept-Temple II-2.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-concept-Temple II-2.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-concept-Temple II-2.jpg) The Anhk *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* The anhk *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RG1-1)</sup> is common iconography associated with the architecture of temples throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARTemples-2)</sup> as well as the signage of temples throughout the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) region.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFTemples-3)</sup> The [Moth Priest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Ancestor_Moth) [Kellen](Lore_Kellen.md) wore an anhk shaped amulet.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LGKellen-4)</sup>

### Aurbic Eye
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/OBbsign_Ritual.jpg/200px-OBbsign_Ritual.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBbsign_Ritual.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBbsign_Ritual.jpg) The Aurbic Eye The [Aurbic Eye](Lore_Aurbic_Eye.md) is a mystical symbol in the shape of eye, often surrounded by elegant lines and sometimes various scripts. It is named for the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) and symbolizes a connection to the [magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magicka) essence of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), and those who wear it emphasize their own anima essence. The symbol is used in the signs of many mage shops and guilds, most notably the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md). It often shares the same shape as the [Ritual](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Ritual) constellation.

The College refers to it as the **Eye of Shalidor** or **Eye of Magnus** (sharing a name with [the artifact](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md)), a symbol born from the legacy of [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) that looks upon the College with a burning gaze. Those who join the College have Shalidor's magic imprinted on their bodies invisibly, and as they pass trials marks of the Eye gradually begin to appear on them. According to [Taleon Mythmaker](Lore_Taleon_Mythmaker.md) the College of Winterhold's eye symbolized leadership over both mundane and arcane mysteries.

It is worn on circlets and hoods as a "[third](Lore_Third_Eye.md)" eye, and those who pass the final test during the trials find Shalidor's eye marked across their brow. In the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) lesson of the divine body, the phrase *"The Eye of Magnus is always upon us, in the spells and enchantments that devout Mages conjure."* is recited by religious preachers.

### Aurbic Phoenix
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/SR-skill-Restoration.png/200px-SR-skill-Restoration.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-skill-Restoration.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-skill-Restoration.png) The Aurbic Phoenix [Phoenixes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phoenix) are mythical [bird](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird) creatures present in [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) folklore. Little is known about phoenixes except for their intense red plumage. **Aurbic Phoenix** imagery is associated with the school of [Restoration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restoration).

### Black Hand
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/SR-book-Dbhand.png/200px-SR-book-Dbhand.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Dbhand.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Dbhand.png) Black Hand The [Black Hand](Lore_Black_Hand.md) is the symbol of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). It is a black handprint, which symbolizes their ruling body, which is a council made up of four Speakers and one Listener, represented by the four fingers and thumb.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBLucien-5)</sup>

### Blessed Life-Tree
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-furnishing-Ayleid_Relief%2C_Blessed_Life-Tree.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Ayleid_Relief%2C_Blessed_Life-Tree.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Ayleid_Relief,_Blessed_Life-Tree.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Ayleid_Relief,_Blessed_Life-Tree.jpg) The Blessed Life-Tree The [Blessed Life-Tree](Lore_Blessed_Life-Tree.md) is a symbol common in [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) culture. The brilliant hue of the Ayleid life-tree glows upon those who sought knowledge from the Ayleids.

Reliefs of the life-tree are commonly found in [Ayleid ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Ruins). Some who return from endeavors into these ruins return with the markings of the blessed life-tree on their face, seemingly by gaining favor from one of their spirits. It is believed that having these markings can furthermore win the favor of lingering ghosts. [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) followers of the [Dawnway](Lore_Dawnway.md) also make use of the life-tree as a symbol of their Ayleid ancestry.

### Cross
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/CT-decoration-Green_Banner.png/200px-CT-decoration-Green_Banner.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Green_Banner.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Green_Banner.png) A cross The [cross](Lore_Cross.md) is a symbol depicting two intersecting lines, associated with various faiths and used in torture. Crossing ones heart is declaration of honesty. The cross is sometimes used as a symbol of [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia). The [Toxicruciform](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Toxicruciform) is a curio associated with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), found in the form of a cross. During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), monks, priests, and friars all throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) were known to wear golden crosses.

### Ouroboros
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Icon-Transparent_Logo.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Icon-Transparent_Logo.png) An ouroboros The [Ouroboros](Lore_Ouroboros.md) is a symbol linked to [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal), depicted as a circular image portraying a [serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snake) consuming its own tail. Another version of this symbol, featuring three heads resembling an [eagle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eagle), [lion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lion), and [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), became connected to the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), and the Stirk Fellowship.

### Red Diamond
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-banner-Imperial.png/200px-ON-banner-Imperial.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Imperial.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Imperial.png) The Red Diamond The [Red Diamond](Lore_Red_Diamond.md) is the symbol of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), and the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> It is based on the [Chim-el Adabal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chim-el_Adabal), the cut red [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) in the center of the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

### Red Dragon
[![](https://images.uesp.net/b/b9/MW-banner-Imperial.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Imperial.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Imperial.png) A Red Dragon The Red Dragon is the symbol the Third Empire, founded by Tiber Septim. The symbol was inspired by the Red Dragons used as war mounts by the Akaviri.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1Cyro-11)</sup>

Despite the end of Tiber Septim's line, and the destruction of the Amulet of Kings during the Oblivion Crisis, the Mede Dynasty of Emperor's continued to use it as a symbol of the Empire.

### Scarab
[![](https://images.uesp.net/3/3e/ON-concept-House_Banner.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-House_Banner.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-House_Banner.png) A scarab symbol [Scarabs](Lore_Scarab.md) are a variety of [beetle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beetle) that have cultural significance in various parts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They have been used for their shells and as pets, and many hold them as an important symbol. [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), and [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) have all placed importance on the scarab.

Scarabs have significant symbolic and practical importance to the Dunmer. The **Black Scarab** is the House Symbol of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) and it is considered sacred by its members, their armors are often adorned with the symbol. Scarab elytra are used in the crafting of House Redoran equipment, Redoran shields appear to be fashioned from the overlapping chitin plates of a gigantic scarab.

In the [36 Lessons of Vivec](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) reiterates a speech by [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) to [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar). In this speech Boethiah refers to [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) as "the Frame-maker, the Scarab". The [Commentaries](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md) of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) also speak to this divine significance of the Scarab. Mankar claims [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is the realm of Lorkhan in some of his teachings with the phrase 'TAMRIEL AE DAEDROTH'. In his commentaries, he conveys the same message with the phrase 'SCARAB AE AURBEX'. Seemingly linked to this concept, the Sixth [Walking Way](Lore_Walking_Ways.md) has been referred to as the Scarab. In an account of the legend of Boethiah and [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) and his [followers](Lore_Orc.md) were balled into a singular sphere as "the scarab gathers dung".

### Third Eye
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/ON-banner-House_Dagoth.png/200px-ON-banner-House_Dagoth.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-House_Dagoth.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-House_Dagoth.png) Third eye symbols on a House Dagoth banner The [Third Eye](Lore_Third_Eye.md) (or **Third-Eye**) is a [magical](Lore_Magic.md) concept and symbol associated with [seeing the past and future](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight), or the otherwise unseen. Fortune tellers could be described as having "the third's eye fire". Scholars believe that certain [Deep Elf](Lore_Dwemer.md) goggles augmented Dwemeri vision by adding an elevated third eye that added [time-perspective](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Time_Magic) to depth perception. Some individuals wear a [gemstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crystals) on their forehead that is known as a third eye.

[House Dagoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dagoth) is heavily associated with the third eye, as they took it as their symbol. Their sigil was that of a [beetle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beetle) with three circles on its back. [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) wore a [mask](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mask_of_Dagoth_Ur) after his resurrection that featured a prominent third eye. His seven [Ash Vampire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Vampire) brethren also each possessed a third eye. Dagoth Ur's followers regularly commented on his or their own "discerning eye", and were known to have the third eye symbol on their palms. [Akulakhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akulakhan) was built with a groove in its forehead that seeped [blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Blight), though the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) was said to actually be its third eye.

### Triquetra
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/BL-item-Silver_Garnet_Necklace.jpg/200px-BL-item-Silver_Garnet_Necklace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Silver_Garnet_Necklace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Silver_Garnet_Necklace.jpg) A triquetra The [triquetra](Lore_Triquetra.md) is an ancient symbol that can be described as a three-sided knot that has been present in many [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md) cultures. Evidence of its earliest known usage comes from an [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) [fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fox) totem depicting [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor) with the blending of [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) and [Atmoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atmoran) artisan techniques. This totem features the standard triquetra along with a variation of it seen in Snow Elf equipment. However, the symbol is most commonly used by the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) in the modern day. The origins of Bretonic usage dates back to around [1E 330](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_330) on the [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago): the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) dissolved due to a religious schism and split into the [Stonelore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonelore_Circle), [Eldertide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eldertide_Circle), and [Firesong Circles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Firesong_Circle). The three druidic circles thus began shaping knot-work into the triquetra, to symbolize their shared goals and ultimate unity. When mainland Bretons adopted the triquetra is unknown, but today it can be found emblazoned on many things associated with Bretons, such as their armor, [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md), furniture, and clothing.

### Triskelion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-logo-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.png/200px-ON-logo-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-logo-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-logo-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.png) A triskelion The [triskelion](Lore_Triskelion.md) is a symbol consisting of three interlocked spirals radiating from a common center. The triskelion is characterized by its triple spiral structure. It holds significance in various cultures and religions, including those of the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), and [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk).

## Notes
- A symbol identical to the *[fluer-de-lis](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fleur-de-lis)* appears in games since *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, though it is never named. - In *Daggerfall*, the fleur-de-lis can be [seen alongside](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Aubk-i.png) the Kingdom of Daggerfall's dragon logo.
- In ESO, the fluer-de-lis can be seen on Imperial armor, such as the [West Weald Legion Style](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:West_Weald_Legion_Style) armor. It can also be found in Breton things like the [House Dufort Banneret Style](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:House_Dufort_Banneret_Style) and the [Order of the Albatross banner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Order_of_the_Albatross.png). It is also present on the light [Hollowjack Style](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Hollowjack_Style).
- The [UK cover for *Daggerfall*](File_DF-cover-Daggerfall_(UK).jpg.md) displays papers that feature many random symbols such as the [Seal of Solomon](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seal_of_Solomon), [musical notes](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Note_value), [Urdu script](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urdu_alphabet), and [Chinese characters](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_characters), among others.
