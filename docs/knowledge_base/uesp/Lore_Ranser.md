Ranser was the King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) and the last monarch from [House Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md), reigning from [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546) to [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567). Ranser ruled during what many consider the high point of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md)'s history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup> After [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland became the King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) in [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), he began courting Ranser's daughter, [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md). However, before the marriage contracts were worked out, Emeric chose instead to marry Princess [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), infuriating Ranser and leading to the start of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> Ranser and his army besieged [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) from Last Seed 2E 566 until the spring of [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), when Emeric's allies broke the siege and drove them out of [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md) and back into Rivenspire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> Emeric's allies subsequently almost completely annihilated Ranser's army at the Battle of [Markwasten Moor](Lore_Markwasten_Moor.md), but he was able to escape with a small contingent of soldiers and aides and retreat to the top of a nameless tor overlooking Shornhelm to make their last stand.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup> However, at the tor, Ranser was murdered by his most trusted general, Dathieu, bringing an end to Ranser's War.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATT-6)</sup>

## Biography

### Rise to Power and King of Shornhelm
Ranser was the illegitimate son of the King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md) of [House Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md), and a "poor [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) cousin". In [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546), Hurlburt died, instigating a conflict between Ranser and his legitimate younger half-brother [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md) over Shornhelm's throne.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup>

Phylgeon's advisors contended that as the legitimate son, he was the proper heir to the throne. Although Phylgeon was the legitimate heir to the throne, Houses Branquette and Tamrith supported Ranser despite him being King Hurlburt's illegitimate son. The fourteen-year-old Phylgeon was descended from [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md), and his claim was supported by the codicil to the "Bretonnick Natalitie", which declared "Howse Mount Clayre" the royal house of Shornhelm. However, the codicil was missing when the Council of the North met to discuss the two claimants. To counter Phylgeon's claim, Prince Ranser presented a long-lost [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) decree that named House Branquette as their Breton Royal Delegates in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md). Ranser narrowly won the throne by the vote of the council and was crowned King of Shornhelm. Some of Phylgeon's advisors urged him to fight for the crown, but the young prince declined, preferring to remain simply the Baron of Montclair.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup>

According to a piece of propaganda produced by House Tamrith, after acceding to the throne, Ranser (known in this work as Lancer) married Lady Ignort of House Dull. According to the propaganda, he fathered his daughter named [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md) with Ignort.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSo PE-8)</sup> After [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland became the King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) in [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), he began courting Rayelle. However, before the marriage contracts were worked out, Emeric chose instead to marry Princess [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), a strategic move to strengthen trade between High Rock and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> Emeric not marrying Rayelle infuriated Ranser, leading him to recall his ambassador from the court of Wayrest. Although Ranser, along with the other kings of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), was invited to Emeric's wedding to Maraya in the spring of [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), he stayed in Shornhelm.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup>

### Ranser's War
With Emeric distracted by his new bride and trade issues around the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), Ranser, still furious, began building up his forces to attack [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup> Ranser called the noble houses of Rivenspire to arms, including Houses [Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md), Montclair, and Tamrith, who provided the king with their troops.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTARH-9)</sup> [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) was the only noble house of Rivenspire that did not support him, choosing instead to remain neutral.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTARH-9)</sup> [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md), the ruling family of the Duchy of [Systres](Lore_Systres.md), supported Ranser by providing him with knights and arms.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EJ-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ho HM-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Ho HD-12)</sup> Finally, Ranser emptied his treasury to hire mercenaries to bolster his forces.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup> His brother Phylgeon was uncertain of the rightness of Ranser's cause and offered to both Kings Ranser and Emeric to serve as a peace envoy between the two sides. Emeric's reply has been lost to history, but it is known that Ranser refused. After that, Phylgeon deferred to his older brother and provided the knights of House Montclair to fight in Ranser's army.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup>

In Last Seed 2E 566, Ranser initiated what later became called [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) when he led his army out of Rivenspire and into [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> He and his army advanced through [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md) and most of [Menevia](Lore_Menevia.md) before Emeric's forces were even aware of their approach.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup> When Ranser's [Oldgate](Lore_Oldgate.md) Lancers reached the gates of Wayrest, Emeric led the gate guards and his household troops out against them. The Oldgate Lancers were unable to withstand the assaults from Emeric's armored veterans, compounded by a sudden thunderstorm that frightened their horses, ultimately forcing them to retreat from the city gates. By the time Ranser's main forces reached the city, the gates were shut tight and all of Wayrest's troops were within the walls.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup> Ranser had hoped for a swift victory; instead, he was forced into a protracted siege.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup>

Shortly after the outbreak of the war, House Tamrith withdrew its support of him and petitioned for peace, and House Dorell quickly followed suit.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTARH-9)</sup> The siege of Wayrest continued into the spring of [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), when the forces of the rest of the Daggerfall Covenant— [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), and [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) —arrived to protect the city.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> Attacked from Wayrest and the surrounding countryside, Ranser's army stood firm; his mercenaries were well paid and prepared for bloodshed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup> However, the arrival of battalions of elite [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) warriors from Sentinel turned the tide.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> In a matter of weeks, Wayrest's alliance routed Ranser's forces and drove them out of Stormhaven.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup>

When Ranser returned to Shornhelm, he found that the city had been set ablaze by [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) under [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](Lore_Kurog_gro-Bagrakh.md), who had allied with Emeric after he pledged to return [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) to them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> It was at this point that House Montclair surrendered to Wayrest's alliance.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTARH-9)</sup> When Ranser learned of the Orcs' alliance with Emeric, he ordered his spymaster, Gerard Althen, to arrest and interrogate all Orcs in Rivenspire, and if they resisted or seemed suspicious, he had them executed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-GAd-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Md-14)</sup> With Ranser and his troops caught between Emeric's allies, they went east into [Markwasten Moor](Lore_Markwasten_Moor.md) to make their final stand.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup> At the ensuing Battle of Markwasten Moor, Wayrest's alliance almost completely annihilated Ranser's army.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup> Although some believed that he died at the battle, Ranser was able to escape with a small contingent of soldiers and aides and retreat to the top of a nameless tor overlooking Shornhelm to make their true last stand.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup>

The Orcs eventually tracked them to the tor and surrounded it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RMd-15)</sup> Ranser's most trusted general, Dathieu, attempted to defend the walls of the tor's fortress against the attackers, even using unconventional methods such as sleep scrolls against them.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GDd-16)</sup> However, Dathieu's efforts were to no avail as the Orcs breached the crumbling walls.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GDd-16)</sup> Dathieu saw the situation as hopeless and believed surrender was the only viable option that remained, but Ranser dismissed this, instead preferring for his troops to continue fighting until the end.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GDd-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-KRd-17)</sup> By this point, Ranser's hatred for Emeric had seemingly consumed him entirely.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GDd-16)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-KRT-18)</sup> The King's court magician, Reezal-Jul, told him about a ritual that would allow them to still win the battle and perhaps the war by transforming his soldiers into [undead](Lore_Undead.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup> Despite his uncertainty about the ritual's purpose and his own doubts, Dathieu sent a lieutenant to execute the King's plan, but he said that if it did not lead to victory, he would attempt to persuade Ranser to surrender.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATT-6)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GDd-16)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale.jpg/200px-ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale.jpg) Ranser is killed by Dathieu This lieutenant, whose name is lost to history, executed their orders and completed the necessary steps for the ritual, which they subsequently reported to Ranser.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATT-6)</sup> Ranser then sent orders to Captain Taern and the King's Guard to charge where the Orcs had breached the north wall and commanded the lieutenant to tell Dathieu to prepare his troops as well.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-KRd-17)</sup> Upon receiving the order, Dathieu became convinced that the King was mad and refused to follow it, not willing to waste more lives on a lost cause.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GDd-16)</sup> Dathieu then confronted Ranser at the fortress's north tower, where he killed the King by stabbing him in the back.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATT-6)</sup> As the final step of Reezal-Jul's ritual required a blood sacrifice, the murder of Ranser completed it, transforming his soldiers into undead.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup> Ranser immediately rose from the dead after his murder and killed Dathieu for his betrayal.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATT-6)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-KRd-17)</sup> After that, the King ordered his now undead skeletal soldiers to strengthen their position and prepare to defend the tor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-5)</sup>

### Rule at Traitor's Tor
Ranser had Dathieu's bones scattered around the tor's fortress to punish him for his treachery.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-KRd-17)</sup> Following Ranser's murder and the transformation of him and his soldiers into undead, the tor became known as Traitor's Tor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTARH-9)</sup> His soldiers continued to defend Traitor's Tor for the next fifteen years, which prevented anyone from learning the exact circumstances of Ranser's death and why they were transformed into undead.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCd-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Traitor%27s_Tale_03.jpg) Undead Ranser and Dathieu fight In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a scholar named Cantier and an adventurer entered Traitor's Tor to learn about Ranser's death and why he and his soldiers were transformed into undead. In the tor's central tower, they discovered Dathieu's skull, which showed the adventurer a vision of Ranser's murder and the reason behind the transformation of him and his soldiers into undead. After seeing the vision, the adventurer gathered Dathieu's scattered bones, which enabled the general to reform as a [skeleton](Lore_Skeleton.md), and they then confronted Ranser at the fortress's north tower. At the tower, Dathieu and the adventurer fought Ranser and defeated him, lifting the curse of Traitor's Tor and laying the spirits of the King and his troops to rest.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATT-6)</sup>

## Aftermath and Legacy
Ranser's death at the Battle of Traitor's Tor ended Ranser's War.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSo PE-8)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RMd-15)</sup> His death ended the dynasty of House Branquette, and much of the house's domain was parceled out to Houses Tamrith and Dorell.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup> The Crown of Shornhelm went missing during the Battle of Traitor's Tor, and the Direnni decree that elevated Ranser to the throne has likewise not been seen since.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup> After the war, Rayelle disappeared, and a rumor spread throughout Stormhaven that Ranser had engaged in incestuous relationships with several siblings in the royal line, resulting in Rayelle's birth; however, scholars dismiss this claim.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSo PE-8)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LABHI-19)</sup> Many of the people of Rivenspire remembered Ranser as a madman.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SPPT-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-RJJ-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-DHd-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Fd-23)</sup>

Stormhaven, Rivenspire, and Wrothgar were all indelibly marked by the events of Ranser's War.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup> In the immediate aftermath of Ranser's death, Rivenspire fell into chaos.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup> To restore order to Rivenspire, Baron [Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md) proposed the formation of a ruling triumvirate composed of the leaders of Houses Dorell, Montclair, and Tamrith to govern the region in the name of Emeric.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTARH-9)</sup> Because Emeric did not trust any of Rivenspire's nobles to be King of Shornhelm, he approved of the formation of the triumvirate Council of the North to govern the region, but he also promised to eventually select a new king.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTARH-9)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-HKEd-24)</sup> Ranser was the last monarch of Shornhelm until 2E 582.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-7)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TCo S-25)</sup> Ranser's defeat led to the formation of a second or greater Daggerfall Covenant composed of the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) kingdoms, the Redguards of Sentinel, and Kurog gro-Bagrakh's Orcs.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TOAM10-26)</sup> Emeric, as the architect of the new Daggerfall Covenant, claimed supreme leadership, becoming its high king.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup>

## Notes
- During the mid-Second Era, Shornhelm hosted an annual play called *"Ranser! You Madman!"*.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SPPT-20)</sup>
- Ranser is referred to as a hillman in the book *[The Fury of King Ranser](Lore_The_Fury_of_King_Ranser.md)*. - Rivenspire in ESO was [originally planned](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Cut_Content/Rivenspire#The_Hillmen_of_Rivenspire) to include the spirits of a race of giants that were called "hill giants". The region would have also had Bretons with hill giant heritage called hillmen that were larger than the average person, similar to the [half-giant](Lore_Half-Giant.md) race. These hillmen would have also had some Nordic heritage. Only a loading screen hinting at the hill giants' existence remains in the final game, but the term hillmen still appears as a demonym for Rivenspire's people in the book *The Fury of King Ranser* in reference to Ranser.
