## [Ammonite](Lore_Ammonite.md)
[Ammonites](Lore_Ammonite.md) (also known as **Eltheric Ammonites**) are extinct aquatic [mollusks](Lore_Mollusk.md) once native to the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) and known only for the beautiful shells they left behind. Although the true origin of these shells is unknown, the consensus among naturalists is that ammonites predate even the oldest of [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) ruins. Fossilized ammonites, along with [trilobites](Lore_Trilobite.md), could also be found in the stone walls of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).

Ammonites are most commonly harvested for use by mages and [druids](Lore_Druids.md) due to the fact that they contain stores of raw [magicka](Lore_Magic.md). The capacity varies depending on the shell, with some only holding trace amounts of magicka and others holding nearly as much as a lesser-grade [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gems.md). However, ammonites cannot be recharged and suffer from arcane decay, losing potency within a month of being harvested.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Treasures)

## Chert
[Chert](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chert) is a type of rock. It was mined by [Imperials](Lore_Imperials.md).

Kingscrest Cavern, located north of Cheydinhal in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md), was a known source of chert and [flint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flint), occasionally utilized by the vassals of [House Tharn](Lore_House_Tharn.md). By the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), it had been abandoned and taken over by wild creatures, a state it remained in during [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). A specific part of the cavern was referred to as the Chert Galleries.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Kingscrest_Cavern#Kingscrest_Cavern_Chert_Galleries), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Kingscrest_Cavern)

## [Coral](Lore_Coral.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/ON-place-Coral_Forest.jpg/200px-ON-place-Coral_Forest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Coral_Forest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Coral_Forest.jpg) The [Coral Forest](Lore_Coral_Forest.md) [Coral](Lore_Coral.md) is a type of aquatic rocklike structure, formed from the bones of tiny sac-animals, that comes in many different varieties.

In [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) society, it is prominently used in their architecture. Dense corals supposedly provide the entire foundation of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) and the [Coral Tower](Lore_Coral_Tower_(Thras).md) itself was made from blood-red coral before its collapse. The Sload are also said to use tangled nests of coral formations to move around, and it's even used in their armorsmithing, with different types of corals providing different advantages in battle, such as lightness or durability.

Some of the most ancient ruins of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) are thought to have been built by the Sload and are made of coral, including the original architecture of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md). In Morrowind there is a naturally-occurring bioluminescent, and possibly sentient, coral species that grows on the lands around the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md).

## [Heart Stone](Lore_Heart_Stone.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/SR-item-Heart_Stone_Deposit.jpg/200px-SR-item-Heart_Stone_Deposit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Heart_Stone_Deposit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Heart_Stone_Deposit.jpg) A heart stone deposit A [Heart Stone](Lore_Heart_Stone.md) (also spelled **Heartstone**) is an unusual type of ore mined from deposits which appeared in [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). Though deposits of them have been known to exist in the [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(place).md) ruins in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It is suspected that the deposits on Solstheim surfaced (or *landed* on the surface) during the cataclysms of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md); Master [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) suspected that the material came from [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) and was flung along with other materials to Solstheim during its eruption, and may continue to land on Solstheim from smaller eruptions. Even two centuries later, they still smolder with the heat of the mountain. As the molten rocks had laid next to the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) for millennia, some vestige of its power has seeped into them. Heart stones sometimes raise [Ash Spawn](Lore_Ash_Spawn.md) from the bits of bone buried in the nearby ash. Through trial and error, Neloth's apprentice Talvas Fathryon learned that a stone was needed to prevent an [ash guardian](Lore_Ash_Guardian.md) summoned through [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) from attacking the caster. The stones can also be used for enchanting purposes.

Neloth's previous apprentice, [Ildari Sarothril](Lore_Ildari_Sarothril.md), was killed during an experiment and brought back to life when one of these stones was grafted to her. Ildari learned that a stone could be used to prevent [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) spells from wearing off. She used them to bolster her necromantic spells used to revive [Falx Carius](Lore_Falx_Carius.md). Those revived with the stones in their chest felt constant pain, and had "voices" in their head, often telling them to distrust others.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Heart_Stone.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
