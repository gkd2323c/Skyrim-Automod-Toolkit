## Gas Blossom
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/ON-flora-Gas_Blossom.jpg/200px-ON-flora-Gas_Blossom.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Gas_Blossom.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Gas_Blossom.jpg) Gas Blossom Gas Blossoms are a variety of carnivorous plant which can be found in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md). The blossom part of the plant resembles a fanged mouth which splits into three "petals" when it opens, which contains a poisonous gas which the Gas Blossom releases when it senses prey or a threat. The rest of the plant is a mass of thick, tentacle-like roots which can easily burrow into most surfaces.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)

## Giant Xylaria
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-flora-Giant_Xylaria.jpg/200px-ON-flora-Giant_Xylaria.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Giant_Xylaria.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Giant_Xylaria.jpg) Giant Xylaria Giant Xylaria is a fungus that grows almost exclusively in the [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VFAF-1)</sup> They resemble giant spikes growing out of the ground and accidentally walking into one would likely be fatal. ## Gildergreen

## Ginkgo
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/53/OB-flora-Ginkgo_Tree.jpg/200px-OB-flora-Ginkgo_Tree.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-flora-Ginkgo_Tree.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-flora-Ginkgo_Tree.jpg) Ginkgo Tree The Ginkgo Tree (also called **Gingko**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFGingko-3)</sup> is a tree found throughout the provinces of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), as well as the rivers and lakes of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). For the most part, these green trees are inconspicuous to the simple eye, but are noticeable by their peculiar half-moon shaped leaves, which is said to edible and very sweet-tasting. According to local legend, when mixing the leaves with the pulp of [aloe vera](Lore_Aloe_Vera.md), it can increase the consumer's stamina for a short time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SFOT-4)</sup> Eventually, these trees start blooming with white flowers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONFurnish Ginkgo-5)</sup> They were also found in [Gaiar Alata](Lore_Gaiar_Alata.md) before its destruction.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Gingko_leaves)
- [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Ginkgo_Leaf)
- [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Ginkgo)

**Harvested Ingredient:** - [Ginkgo Leaves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gingko_leaves)

## [Grove Carpet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grove_Carpet)
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fragment-Grove_Carpet_Buds.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fragment-Grove_Carpet_Buds.png) Grove Carpet Buds [Grove Carpet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grove_Carpet) is a type of moss indigenous to [Galen](Lore_Galen.md). It is known to possess remarkable properties. [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) utilized living budlings of this moss in their rituals and transformation-based magic. Surprisingly, Grove Carpet is known for its near-indestructibility in bud form. Even ancient budlings, when sprinkled with water, can swiftly grow into lush plants within a matter of days. It's uncertain whether different druidic circles utilized those buds differently. However, the Druids of the [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md) were known for their specific utilization of Grove Carpet, though the exact purposes for which they employed it remain a mystery.

By employing magic alongside a variety of other ingredients and tool, including [scarab](Lore_Scarab.md) -host boxes, pulsating [amber](Lore_Amber.md) hearts, tablets carved from [petrified tree bark](Lore_Other_Minerals.md#Petrified_Wood), clay, [chimera](Lore_Chimera.md) hair brushes, writhing vine mass, mulch, living bark, and pigment sourced from [phoenix](Lore_Phoenix.md) [moths](Lore_Moth.md#Phoenix_Moth), grove carpet buds were used to alter ones their appearance as a tribute to changing seasons and to mirror the likeness of trees. This visage was known as Barkroot Blessing.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Galen_Antiquities#Grove_Carpet_Buds)
