Cephorus Septim I was the tenth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for thirteen years, from [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127) to [3E 140](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_140). In [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121), his nephew [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_III.md) usurped [Kintyra II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md), so Cephorus and his brother [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) allied to oppose Uriel III in the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> In 3E 127, after six years of fighting, he defeated and captured Uriel III at the Battle of Ichidag. However, a mob killed Uriel while he was en route to stand trial in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), leading to Cephorus' proclamation as emperor. Cephorus' accession marked the official end of the War of the Red Diamond, but he spent the next decade fighting his sister [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) until her death at the [Siege of Solitude](Lore_Siege_of_Solitude.md) in [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> He was preceded by his nephew, Uriel III, and was succeeded by his younger brother, Magnus.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life and King of Gilane
Cephorus Septim was born in [3E 77](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_77) to Prince [Pelagius Septim](Lore_Pelagius_II.md) (later Emperor Pelagius II) and his second wife, [Quintilla](Lore_Quintilla.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Mother)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWQ1-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup> He had three siblings: [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md), [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup> By [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98), Cephorus was betrothed to Bianki, the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) Queen of [Gilane](Lore_Gilane.md).<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Wife_and_children)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup> In 3E 98 and [3E 99](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_99), Cephorus attended the funeral of Pelagius II and the coronation of his successor, Antiochus, in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup> By [3E 109](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_109), Bianki had borne Cephorus several children.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Wife_and_children)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ4-7)</sup> As of [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119), he was considered to have ruled the Kingdom of Gilane well during his reign.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWQ5-8)</sup>

In [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120), after the death of Antiochus, he attended his funeral and the coronation of his niece [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) in the Imperial City.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWQ5-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup> Before the funeral of Antiochus, Potema gave a speech before the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) in which she asserted that Kintyra was not the daughter of Antiochus.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWQ5-8)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup> Cephorus' nephew, [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_III.md), made a similar accusation, referring to the infamous decadence of the Imperial City during the reign of Kintyra's father.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup> After hearing of this accusation, Kintyra banished Potema and Uriel from the Imperial Court, and they returned to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWQ5-8)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup> On 3 First Seed, 3E 120, he attended the coronation of Kintyra II as Empress of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup>

### War of the Red Diamond
In [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121), the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) broke out and split the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in half.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TKot N-10)</sup> Uriel and Potema captured Kintyra II at [Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup> Following Kintyra II's capture, Uriel invaded the [Imperial Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), capturing the under-defended Imperial City after a fortnight-long siege and then assuming the Ruby Throne as Uriel Septim III.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup> After Uriel III was crowned emperor, Cephorus allied with Magnus to oppose him.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TMOP-12)</sup> The kings of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), southern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) mostly supported Cephorus and Magnus, while Uriel III had the combined support of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), northern Morrowind, and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TMOP-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup>

Accounts describe Cephorus as a kind and intelligent man, but he also proved himself to be a great warrior.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> Cephorus concentrated his army on the war in High Rock, which was the location of some of the bloodiest battles of the war.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TMOP-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup> In the summer of [3E 125](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_125), he conquered western High Rock, and by [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127), all of High Rock had defected to the side of Cephorus and Magnus.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup> In 3E 127, Cephorus and his army defeated Uriel III and the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) at the [Battle of Ichidag](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) in Hammerfell and captured the Emperor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TMOP-12)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TDC-13)</sup> Uriel III was placed in a carriage for transport to the Imperial City to stand trial, but while en route, a mob overtook his carriage and burned him alive within it.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup> After Uriel III's death, Cephorus was proclaimed Emperor of Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TET-14)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TDC-13)</sup>

### Emperor of Tamriel
Following Cephorus I's accession, many of the troops formerly loyal to Uriel III and Potema pledged themselves to him.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup> In return for their support, the provincial nobility demanded and received greater autonomy and independence from the Empire.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup> In [3E 131](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_131), Cephorus I issued a decree that officially dissolved the [Knights of the Nine](Lore_Knights_of_the_Nine.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TKot N-10)</sup>

Cephorus I's accession marked the official end of the War of the Red Diamond, but Potema continued to fight him and Magnus for the next decade.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup> Potema mounted a series of attacks against her brothers by using her army of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and [undead](Lore_Undead.md), but her allies began abandoning her until only the [Kingdom of Solitude](Lore_Haafingar.md) remained under her control.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup> In [3E 136](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_136), his wife, Bianki, died.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup> By [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137), all of Cephorus' children had died from fighting in the war and because of the famines caused by the large number of crops that had been burned.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup> In 3E 137, Cephorus ended the almost two decades of warfare that nearly tore the Empire apart when Potema died after a month-long siege of the [Blue Palace](Lore_Blue_Palace.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BWQ-6)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup> After the siege, the Emperor placed his nephew, [Pelagius](Lore_Pelagius_III.md), on the throne of Solitude.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TMOP-12)</sup>

In [3E 140](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_140), Cephorus I died after falling from his horse, and he was succeeded by Magnus.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ8-5)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TMOP-12)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TET-14)</sup>
