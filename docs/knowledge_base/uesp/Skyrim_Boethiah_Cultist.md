[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/SR-npc-Boethiah_Cultist.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Boethiah_Cultist.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Boethiah_Cultist.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Boethiah_Cultist.jpg) A female Dunmer Boethiah Cultist, located at the Sacellum of Boethiah The **Boethiah Cultists** are worshippers of the Daedric Prince [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and are located at the [Sacellum of Boethiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sacellum_of_Boethiah). They play a part in the quest [Boethiah's Calling](Skyrim_Boethiah%27s_Calling.md), but they can also be found as a [random encounter](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Boethiah_Cultist_attack), carrying *[Boethiah's Proving](Skyrim_Boethiah%27s_Proving.md)* in their inventory and initiating the quest. They can be easily recognized by a unique sign tattooed in black on the right side of their faces.

## Bugs
- The Boethiah Cultist who attacks you in a [random encounter](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md) (WEDA02Cultist) doesn't become hostile until he's been very close to you for some time. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.9, addresses this issue. An alias property needed to be adjusted to make sure he immediately attacks on sight.
- Looting the book from the dead Boethiah Cultist may fail to close the objective to investigate the cultists once the book is read. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.0.8, fixes this bug.
- After A Matter of Pride is finished, some Boethiah Cultists may respawn in the [Arcanaeum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Arcanaeum) when the area resets. From there, they can follow you wherever you go, and they can appear anywhere, including inside player homes. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.629, fixes this bug.
