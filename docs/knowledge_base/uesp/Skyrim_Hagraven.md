[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg) A Hagraven **Hagravens** are a horrific cross between an old crone and a bird. They are extremely dangerous and equally hostile up close or when casting various [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) spells from afar, usually preferring area-of-effect spells such as [Fireball](Skyrim_Fireball.md). They can often be found in the company of the [Forsworn](Skyrim_Forsworn.md), or dwelling in secret groves and lairs in the wilderness with other [witches](Skyrim_Witch.md) and [hags](Skyrim_Hag.md). The Forsworn revere hagravens as their matriarchs and leaders, and it is hagravens who create [Forsworn Briarhearts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn_Briarheart), as seen at [Lost Valley Redoubt](Skyrim_Lost_Valley_Redoubt.md).

Hagravens were once witches that have undergone a ritual, as seen in the quest [Repentance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Repentance). According to *[Herbalist's Guide to Skyrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Herbalist%27s_Guide_to_Skyrim)*, these creatures have traded in their humanity for access to powerful magics, and the transformations they undergo infuse their entire beings with some element of that power.

Hagravens will tend to appear either individually or in groups of three.

You'll find yourself engaged to [one](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Moira) after [drinking too much](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Night_To_Remember) in the wrong [company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sam_Guevenne).

A unique hagraven encounter is located at [Hag's End](Skyrim_Hag%27s_End.md), while it is possible to randomly encounter hagravens fighting spriggans in the wilderness.

### Tips
Heavily armored warriors may find it easier to charge in and fight toe-to-toe, because your armor will better protect you against their claws than their Destruction spells. Characters with more elemental resistance might wish to hang back and fight from a distance for similar reasons. Ice magic hagravens are easier to deal with than fire-based ones, as the ice-based hagravens lack an area-of-effect spell.

It's also worth noting that a hagraven's claw attack is rather slow and easy to dodge. It is entirely possible, and somewhat easy, for even a low level character to defeat one just by moving back slightly after every time you take a swing at them. Hagravens also have a slow turning speed and even slower walking speed, which can also be taken into consideration.
