# Chimer

— *[The Changed Ones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Changed_Ones)* The Chimer (literally, the **People of the North**),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWSavant-1)</sup> also called the **Changed Folk**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> **Velothi**, **Changed Ones**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAA-3)</sup> or the archaic **Maliache**<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-RPTrial Of Vehk-4)</sup> were the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) tribes of dissidents who abandoned the ways of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) and embarked on a great exodus to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWDialogue-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TCO-6)</sup> They followed the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md), who spoke to them on behalf of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), Prince of Plots, and two other "[Good Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra)". The teachings of Veloth and the Daedra spawned a new culture on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and a new people, sparking the period of the High Velothi culture. They were dynamic, ambitious, long-lived Elven clans devoted to fundamentalist ancestor worship.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-7)</sup> They closely resembled the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), though their skin bore a paler golden hue.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONChimer-8)</sup> [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) apparently maintained her appearance as a Chimer (as did [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), partially) despite the Curse which changed the Chimer into the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Morrowind-9)</sup>

## History
The history of the Chimer begins with the Velothi dissident movement in the Summerset Isle. Some sources seem to differ on when exactly this happened. Some say it took place in the Late Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-7)</sup> and others trace it back to the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTNOO-10)</sup> One source says it took place before the shattering of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TM-11)</sup> and another says that it was after [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) and others had explored mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOTN-12)</sup>

### The Exodus
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/LO-misc-Velothi.png/200px-LO-misc-Velothi.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Velothi.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Velothi.png) Depiction of Veloth leading the Chimer to Morrowind [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/LO-people-Veloth.jpg/200px-LO-people-Veloth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) A fresco depicting Veloth leading the Chimer to Morrowind Trinimac, strongest of the Altmeri ancestor spirits, fought enemies of Altmeri society both without and within. As such, Trinimac and his followers became engaged in trying to halt the [Velothi](Lore_Veloth.md) dissident movement.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTNOO-10)</sup> Boethiah, the "Devourer of Trinimac",<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRDialogue-13)</sup> took exception to the "lies" Trinimac had been spreading about Lorkhan.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TCO-6)</sup> He tricked Trinimac, ate him, assumed his form, and the dung he excreted became [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> The followers of Boethiah and Trinimac rubbed this excrement on themselves to change their skins.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TA-14)</sup> It was the followers of Boethiah (i.e. the followers of Veloth) who became the Chimer. Boethiah and Mephala told the masses about the Tri-Angled Truth, the rules of Psijic Endeavor, how to build Houses, and demonstrated the "right way to wear their skin".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TCO-6)</sup> Mephala took a third of the followers in attendance and took them to the [southeast](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), as she alone did not fear the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), where they became the [Cantemiric Velothi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cantemiric_Velothi). Azura took a third and took them to the [star-wounded east](Lore_Morrowind.md), and Boethiah took the final third to a land that drifted into the [ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md) and would have stories to tell in times yet to come.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-FEE-15)</sup> Altmeri propaganda portrayed this as a lesson on the dangers of Velothi influence,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> while Velothi propaganda regarded Trinimac's transformation as punishment for attempting to interfere with their exodus. Malacath himself derided this narrative as being too "literal-minded" (implying the essence of the story, while metaphorical, is somewhat accurate).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LOS-16)</sup>

### High Velothi Culture
Nerevar: *"What makes a Chimer great?"*
Vivec: *"Making sure the other dies before you."* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/ON-concept-Shrine_of_Veloth.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Shrine_of_Veloth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Shrine_of_Veloth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Shrine_of_Veloth.jpg) Shrine of Veloth [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/TR-interior-Basilica_Divine_Whispers.jpg/200px-TR-interior-Basilica_Divine_Whispers.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-interior-Basilica_Divine_Whispers.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-interior-Basilica_Divine_Whispers.jpg) Chimer-era Daedric ruin So, the Chimer departed. They credited the Good Daedra with teaching them the principles of their new way of life.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TCO-6)</sup> Worship of [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra), such as [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), disappeared from their legends.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> They typically associate most of the things they learned with Boethiah, but Mephala is credited with teaching them the ways of evasion and assassination, and [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) with teaching them how to be different from the Altmer.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TA-14)</sup> The Good Daedra also warned the Chimer of the "Bad Daedra", such as [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), who sought to spoil Chimeri "purity".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> In their new homeland, which they called [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md), they quickly came into conflict with the [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede), [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTNOO-10)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3M-17)</sup> With the Dwemer, especially, territorial disputes combined with religious animosity created a great deal of conflict.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3M-17)</sup> Historians blame the Chimer for provoking these conflicts.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-7)</sup> Regardless of who started it, the Chimer were generally on the losing end of these conflicts. Though they wielded great magic and had fine warriors, the Chimer were a small group, and the Dwemer had an incredible technological advantage.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TA-14)</sup> It was around this time that [kwama](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama) were domesticated and the Chimer made strides in the agrarian development of their early settlements.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ND-18)</sup>

The Late Merethic Era saw the precipitous decline of Velothi culture. Some Chimer settled in villages near declining and abandoned ancient Velothi towers. During this period, Velothi high culture disappeared on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The earliest Dwemer Freehold colonies date from this period, suggesting they were created to secure Dwemeri lands from Chimeri incursions. The Velothi devolved into tribal cultures which, in time, either evolved into the modern Great Houses of Morrowind, or persisted as the [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) culture.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-7)</sup> There was some cultural interchange with the other races as well, such as the Dwemeri influence on Chimer garbs in the latter half of the Merethic Era.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ND-18)</sup>

### The Curse
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7d/MW-npc-Divayth_Fyr.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Divayth_Fyr.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Divayth_Fyr.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Divayth_Fyr.jpg) Divayth Fyr, a Dunmer whom was previously Chimer [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/MW-concept-Almalexia.jpg/200px-MW-concept-Almalexia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Almalexia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Almalexia.jpg) Lady Almalexia, who resisted the Curse and maintained her Chimer form *And then, in that moment, all Chimer were changed into Dunmer, and our skins turned ashen and our eyes into fire. Of course, we only knew at that time that this had happened to us, but Azura said "This is not my act, but your act. You have chosen your fate, and the fate of your people, and all the Dunmer shall share your fate, from now to the end of time. You think yourselves gods, but you are blind, and all is darkness." And Azura left us alone, in darkness, and we were all afraid, but we put on brave faces, and went forth from Red Mountain to build the new world of our dreams.*
—Vivec Around [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-KE10-19)</sup> the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) under High King [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md) conquered all of Morrowind, making it part of the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) (though the Dwemer kingdom of Vvardenfell remained independent).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE1M-21)</sup> The Nords' use of the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) against the Chimer made them practically unstoppable,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TAOWM-22)</sup> although some blame the god [Sai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai) for favoring the Nords.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup> They would remain under the Nords' heel until around [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416), when they joined forces with the Dwemer to repulse the invaders.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3All-24)</sup> The friendship between Chimer leader [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) and the Dwemer leader [Dumac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dumac) forged the basis for an alliance between the races under the First Council, leading to almost three hundred years of peace. Eventually, a dispute over the Dwemer's discovery, the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), led to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), which culminated in the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md). A few years later, the betrayal of an oath by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) led to the Curse:<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWSavant-1)</sup> the Chimer's skin turned black as ash, and their eyes became red. The Chimer ceased to exist, and the days of the Dunmer began.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TBORM-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-NARM-26)</sup> Some Dunmer don't consider the darkening of their skin to be a curse, rather they see their sober complexion as a gift to mark the ascent of the Tribunal.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Vunal-27)</sup>

Though [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) was one of the few who could resist the curse, he chose to embrace the new Dunmer appearance.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Divayth Fyr CWC-28)</sup> Vivec took on the guise of both Chimer and Dunmer, splitting his being down the middle between the two.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-LOV31-29)</sup> While Almalexia resisted the Dunmer visage entirely, retaining her Chimeri skin-tone <sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRAlmalexia Ap-30)</sup>

Following [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the Dunmer people stopped worshipping the Tribunal, but despite their [return to worship](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Temple) of the Good Daedra, it is thought that they were not changed back into Chimer by Azura because of Boethiah's instructions to become the "color of bruise" and show the most vital part of being a people of the exodus: pain.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-31)</sup>

### Chimeri Remnants
Dunmer theology speaks of an island occupied by Chimer that was separated from Tamriel and drifted into the [sea](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md), where it would remain untouched and have many stories to tell in times yet to come.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-FEE-15)</sup> Dubious [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) records speak of a tribal remnant of Chimer called the Chimeri-quey inhabiting [Cathnoquey](Lore_Cathnoquey.md) in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), composed of fisherfolk who live on the coastlines in grand flotilla-townships of advanced design.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PG2-32)</sup>

Statements published by the [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One) allege that remnants of the Dunmeri peoples' "Chimeric past" still remain, citing the: "bronze faces" of the Tribunal God-Kings, [Queen Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) the "bronze daughter" as she was "pawned to the ailing throne of Wayrest," and the "bronze depictions" of Boethiah at sites of ancient Morrowind.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-RFM-33)</sup> Additionally, there was a Chimer who lived during the [Age of Heroes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Age_of_Heroes) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) —who served as a priest at the [Shrine of Saint Veloth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shrine_of_Saint_Veloth).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Malvari-34)</sup>

## Notes
- The term "Velothi" can be used to refer to any followers of Veloth.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-MWSinnammu-35)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-MWNibani-36)</sup> However, by the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), it was also often used to refer to former [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) who had abandoned their nomadic lives in favor of the Great Houses of Morrowind.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Ash Cult-37)</sup>
- Sources sometimes refer to the Chimer as the Dunmer in events dated before the Battle of Red Mountain, before the Dunmer actually existed.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE1M-21)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-20)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-38)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SN-39)</sup>
- One of the masterpieces of the great artist [Cherim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cherim) depicted Veloth leading the Chimer into Morrowind.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-CHOA-40)</sup>
- Malacath's moon symbol represents the "Curse of Shadow" in [House of Troubles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Troubles) imagery, suggesting it is the Dunmer's name for their skin change and that they associate Malacath with it.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-41)</sup>
- [King Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Helseth) wore an ancient crown of the Chimer that appears as five points curving inward like blades, supporting one in the middle. What it symbolized in elder days was debatable, but in the modern day, it meant "[Five Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) Under One King".<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-42)</sup>
