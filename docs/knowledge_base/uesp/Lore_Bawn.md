[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/OB-place-Bawn.jpg/200px-OB-place-Bawn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bawn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bawn.jpg) Bawn circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Bawn is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) found southeast of Bravil, located on the shores of [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md) at the estuary of the [Larsius River](Lore_Larsius_River.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBMap-1)</sup> Its two subsections of *Bawn Loria* and *Bawn Latta* translate to "Darkness" and "Light" respectively in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).

## History
Following the fall of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) at the end of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the clan of Bawn escaped largely intact as they fled south beneath the canopy of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). They are presumed to have settled there and adopted the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) under the terms of the native [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), eventually being absorbed into their local population.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup> The burgeoning [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) toppled the remaining Ayleid cities of Cyrodiil one by one, and Bawn subsequently fell into ruin.

By the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the halls of the ruin were swarming with undead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OB-3)</sup> The mysterious ghost of the Forlorn Watchman, later identified as Grantham Blakely, haunted the grounds near the ruins until his spirit was freed by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TFW-4)</sup> The nearby eponymously named empty campsite of Bawnwatch Camp sat on an island just north of the ruins.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OB-3)</sup>
