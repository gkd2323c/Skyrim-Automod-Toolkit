## Quick Walkthrough
1. Talk to [Jarl Balgruuf](Skyrim_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md).
2. Meet [Irileth](Skyrim_Irileth.md) near the [Western Watchtower](Skyrim_Western_Watchtower.md).
3. Kill [Mirmulnir](Skyrim_Mirmulnir.md), a dragon.
4. (Optional) Use your new Shout power.
5. Report back to Jarl Balgruuf.

## Detailed Walkthrough
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/SR-quest-Dragon_Rising.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Dragon_Rising.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragon_Rising.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragon_Rising.jpg) Irileth rallying the guards
### Talk to Jarl Balgruuf
Shortly after you return the Dragonstone to [Farengar](Skyrim_Farengar_Secret-Fire.md), Irileth will approach to inform him that there has been a sighting of a dragon at Whiterun's [Western Watchtower](Skyrim_Western_Watchtower.md) not far west of the city. Irileth will then ask Farengar and you to meet with Jarl Balgruuf. Follow them there, where a somewhat unnerved [Whiterun guard](Skyrim_Whiterun_Guard.md) will recount seeing a [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md) flying over the watchtower. Balgruuf will ask you to accompany [Irileth](Skyrim_Irileth.md), as she goes to investigate and, if necessary, do battle. She will recruit several Whiterun Guards along the way.

### Meet Irileth near the Western Watchtower
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_02.jpg) *"No! Get back! It's still here somewhere! Hroki and Tor just got grabbed when they tried to make a run for it!"* Follow Irileth to the [Guard Barracks](Skyrim_Guard_Barracks_(Gate).md) just inside the city gates. After she delivers a speech to rally her soldiers, follow them west towards the tower.

You will gather near the road close to the watchtower to find the watchtower burned and smoking, evidently signs of a recent attack by a dragon. After a short talk, Irileth will give orders to search for survivors. On the north side of the tower, on or around the ramp leading to the interior, you will encounter the lone surviving Whiterun Guard, stammering about what he's just experienced. Shortly after hearing this, you will hear several warnings that the dragon has returned. Irileth will advise taking cover against the dragon's attacks and to *"make every arrow count"*.

### Kill the Dragon
The dragon, [Mirmulnir](Skyrim_Mirmulnir.md), will approach from the south and will immediately begin attacking the group. Use [dragon combat strategies](Skyrim_Dragon.md) during the battle. He will land, fight, and then fly off after losing about a quarter of his health. Once further wounded, he will remain on the ground. While at a level of around 20 or lower, Mirmulnir will be a dragon that uses fire attacks, is resistant to [fire damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage), and is weak against [frost damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Damage). Ranged weapons, frost or shock attacks, and melee weapons will be effective choices. If you play this quest at higher levels, Mirmulnir may attack with [frost](Skyrim_Frost_Breath.md).

When in the air, the dragon will use [shouts](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md), typically [Fire Breath](Skyrim_Fire_Breath.md), against you, the others, and any other living creature who may happen to be in the area. Dragons are indiscriminate in their attacks, so the presence of multiple targets should relieve you of some of the onslaught. He may also use the somewhat more powerful [Fireball](Skyrim_Fireball.md) shout. When his attention is focused elsewhere, attack with a ranged weapon or spell. If he begins to aim his face toward you while in the air, seek cover to shield you against the powerful shout that will ensue. If you get inside the watchtower, or near its door, you can effectively use its walls for this, and then step back out, or use a window or doorway to counter-attack when his shout has dissipated.

When on the ground, Mirmulnir uses both physical and magic attacks. If facing you from a moderate distance, he will use his Thu'um (shouts) against you, as long as you are in his line of sight. When up-close, he will attack frontally with his jaws. If you are behind him, he will beat his tail on the ground, trying to hit you. Both of these types of attacks deal heavy physical damage.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragon_Rising_03.jpg) Mirmulnir approaches The dragon's wing attacks, while very strong, are the least powerful of his physical attacks. When the dragon is in the air, attack at every safe opportunity until he is forced to land. When he's on the ground, the best approach is from the side. Irileth is also useful in this fight. As an [essential character](Skyrim_Essential_NPCs.md) in the game, she can be weakened but not killed in the attacks.

Once killed, Mirmulnir will explode in flames, and then release his soul in a blast of energy. As the [Dovahkiin](Skyrim_Dovahkiin.md) (or "Dragonborn"), the dragon's soul will enter your body, automatically unlocking the first word of the [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) shout, which you learned previously during the [Bleak Falls Barrow quest](Skyrim_Bleak_Falls_Barrow_(quest).md).

After witnessing you consume the dragon's soul, a nearby guard will engage you in dialogue and exclaim that you are Dragonborn. Some of the other surviving guards will express their skepticism. Irileth will discard the notion as a myth and order you to report back to the jarl. One guard will remark that if you are Dragonborn, you can shout, and ask if you've tried. You can use your shout at this time, completing the optional quest objective, or leave them wondering.

Search the dragon's body, taking [dragon bones](Skyrim_Dragon_Bone.md) and [dragon scales](Skyrim_Dragon_Scales.md) along with other loot. These can be used to forge and improve [dragonbone and dragonscale armor and weapons](Skyrim_Dragon_Armor.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> once your [Smithing skills](Skyrim_Smithing.md) are highly advanced. In the lengthy intervening period, you can sell these valuable items. Search in and around the tower for any loot you can take from dead guards.

### Report Back to Jarl Balgruuf
As you make your way back to Whiterun (note that the Dragonsreach marker will vanish temporarily during this quest), you should hear thundering voices boom out *"DOVAHKIIN!"*, reverberate across the sky. Return to Dragonsreach and report to Jarl Balgruuf.

He will ask you to recount events at the watchtower and then conclude that the voices were the [Greybeards](Skyrim_Greybeards.md), *"masters of the way of the Voice"*, summoning you to their secluded mountain peak residence at [High Hrothgar](Skyrim_High_Hrothgar.md). The jarl will inform you that the greybeards can teach you to use your gift. The jarl advises you to travel to the Greybeards immediately, as their summons is a *"great honor"*.

During your exchange with the jarl, his brother Hrongar will counsel confidence in you as the Dragonborn, in a calm debate with [Proventus Avenicci](Skyrim_Proventus_Avenicci.md), the jarl's [Steward](Skyrim_Steward.md), who openly expresses suspicion and skepticism toward you.

For your assistance in slaying the dragon, the jarl will appoint you [Thane](Skyrim_Thane.md) of Whiterun, which entitles you to a personal [housecarl](Skyrim_Housecarl.md), [Lydia](Skyrim_Lydia.md). She will reside at Dragonsreach until you purchase [Breezehome](Skyrim_Breezehome.md), at which point she will move in with you. Balgruuf will also reward you with the Axe of Whiterun as your symbol of office. The axe is a leveled weapon that carries a random [enchantment effect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Enchantments#Weapon_Effects).

Lydia will greet you if you head toward the front door of Dragonsreach, at which point you can make her a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md) by telling her that you need her help.

### Achievement
After Mirmulnir is killed, you will earn:

## Notes
- This quest was originally tied to the [civil war](Skyrim_Civil_War.md), and would've had two versions, similar to [The Jagged Crown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Jagged_Crown). That quest was originally known internally as `MQ103`, and still has a lot of leftovers related to this earlier placement. The main differences being that it was to part of the Main Questline (MQ) instead of the Civil War quests (CW), and that it would've been the prerequisite to unlocking dragon spawns, as this quest would've followed it, as it stayed `MQ104`. `MQ104A` would've been the Legion version, with `MQ104B` being the Stormcloak version. Both versions have numerous leftovers still in the files, and the finalized quest reuses markers, AI packages, and dialogue from both. The Stormcloak version would've seen you investigate reports of a dragon attack on [Fort Amol](Skyrim_Fort_Amol.md) alongside an NPC referred to only as "Sifnar", while the Legion version would've seen you travel with [Captain Metilius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Metilius) to investigate similar sightings at [Fort Hraggstad](Skyrim_Fort_Hraggstad.md). Both versions would've also had nameless troops aid you and the designated commander.
- [Dunmer](Skyrim_Dark_Elf.md) or [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) player characters will enjoy an advantage in the dragon fight, depending on whether Mirmulnir uses fire or frost attacks. If Mirmulnir uses fire breath, Dunmer characters will take only half damage due to their racial fire resistance. If Mirmulnir uses frost attacks, Nords will instead have this advantage. If Mirmulnir is using frost damage (which occurs when the player is between levels 18 and 26), the Nord guards fighting alongside the player will be more resistant to his damage, allowing them to last longer and help the player more. If Mirmulnir uses fire damage (which occurs when the player is either above level 26 or below level 18), Irileth will gain this advantage instead.
- One of the guards in the Western Watchtower mentions Hroki and Tor, two men who were stationed there before Mirmulnir apparently grabbed and ate them. Once you defeat Mirmulnir, you can find a partial Whiterun Guard's Armor set on his corpse, and it's more than likely the remains of one of them; it's unknown what happened to the other one. Coincidentally, there is a [Hroki](Skyrim_Hroki.md) who lives in [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), though it's unlikely that Hroki would have survived, and the two are most likely unrelated, anyway.
- You can hide in the tower and let the guards take care of the dragon. Because Irileth is essential, they will always win. However, casualties will be high.
- If you have a [bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bows) or [crossbow](Skyrim_Crossbow.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> in your inventory, you can use Mirmulnir as target practice to improve your [Archery](Skyrim_Archery.md) skill. Likewise, you can use [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) spells in the same manner.
- Once Mirmulnir is dead, other [dragons](Skyrim_Dragon.md) will begin appearing in Skyrim, except between the end of [The Horn of Jurgen Windcaller](Skyrim_The_Horn_of_Jurgen_Windcaller.md) and the completion of [A Blade in the Dark](Skyrim_A_Blade_in_the_Dark.md).
- Before you start this quest, Western Watchtower will be an unmarked location and appear destroyed, though guards will be there, and there will be no fire.
- You don't need to wait for Balgruuf to make his speech before going to fight Mirmulnir. Right when Irileth interrupts Farengar, you can make your way to the Western Watchtower, and a quest marker will already be there.
- The Axe of Whiterun is generated in Jarl Balgruuf's inventory when you give Farengar Secret-Fire the Dragonstone. You can take a look in the Jarl's inventory and reload to generate an axe with a different enchantment. If you don't already possess one, a weapon that carries a [fire damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage) effect may be especially helpful if you plan to soon proceed to the next quest in the main quest line, The Way of the Voice.
- After defeating Mirmulnir, you will be unable to fast travel directly to Dragonsreach, as its map marker will be temporarily removed. The Whiterun city gates will still be available for fast travel. However, if you open the map quickly enough, you can still fast travel to Dragonsreach.
- If you don't return to Whiterun, the Greybeards will call you if you approach High Hrothgar. After 24 hours, they will call you no matter where you are, provided you're outdoors. The main quest line will progress normally, though Dragon Rising will remain in your quest log until you speak to Balgruuf. - If you DO return to Whiterun immediately, the Greybeards will call you shortly after you get there.
- You must finish this quest before Message to Whiterun can be completed, regardless of who you sided with (the [Imperials](Skyrim_Message_to_Whiterun_(Imperial).md) or the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Message_to_Whiterun_(Stormcloaks).md)).
- Very rarely, a guard can pull out a horn and blow it to summon reinforcements.
- Absorbing [Vulthuryol](Skyrim_Vulthuryol.md)'s dragon soul in Blackreach will have the same effect as absorbing Mirmulnir's, if you fight Vulthuryol first.
- This quest is the only time [Whiterun Guards](Skyrim_Whiterun_Guard.md) will be seen wearing [hide helmets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hide_Helmet), [leather helmets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Helmet), and [scaled helmets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Scaled_Helmet), instead of their [Whiterun Guard's Helmets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun_Guard%27s_Helmet). It is also one of the few times you'll see them armed with [iron](Skyrim_Iron.md) and/or [steel](Skyrim_Steel.md) weaponry, instead of their standard [imperial swords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Sword) and [Whiterun Guard's Shields](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun_Guard%27s_Shield). If any die during the assault, fresh ones will replace them, potentially allowing you to collect a full set of [Whiterun Guard's Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Whiterun_Guard%27s_Armor).
- During the assault with Mirmulnir, several [burnt corpses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Burnt_Corpse) can be found in the courtyard east the Western Watchtower. You can loot them for gold, arrows, [bone meal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Meal), and more.
- Mirmulnir's death is scripted to unlock the first word of [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md). Killing dragons after this will allow you to collect dragon souls, though you won't be able to unlock any other shouts until you meet the Greybeards at High Hrothgar later on.
- Mirmulnir's corpse, the corpses of any fallen Whiterun Guards, and the burnt corpses will despawn from the Western Watchtower once the quest is finished, so be sure to loot them before returning to Dragonsreach.
- Version 4.3.3 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch) replaces the "Dohvakiin! No!!" recording with the French version.

## Bugs
- After finishing Bleak Falls Barrow by turning in the Dragonstone to Farengar, the quest Dragon Rising may fail to start. - This appears to be caused by Irileth being completely missing in the game. You have to first enable her through the console (`prid 0001a67f` followed by `enable`), then place her at you while you are still in Dragonsreach (`moveto player`) and finally, advance the quest (`setstage MQ104 10` then `setstage MQ104 40`). The quest rewards will be added to your inventory and Irileth will start running towards the tower.
- If you have a save before turning the Dragonstone in to Farengar but no save from before getting the Dragonstone or simply starting the [Bleak Falls Barrow](Skyrim_Bleak_Falls_Barrow_(quest).md) quest, go into Dragonsreach and use the console command (`completequest MQ104`), which should advance the quest to the point of turning in the stone. You can then complete it again, and Dragon Rising should start.
- Lydia may fail to spawn properly upon completion of the quest. - Once you purchase Breezehome and enter it, Lydia should spawn.
- The quest reward, the Axe of Whiterun, may not appear in your inventory.
- Irileth may not always appear. - To fix this, wait in one hour increments and she should appear.
- With the Dragonborn add-on installed, it may be impossible to absorb the dragon soul. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch), version 1.0.1, fixes this bug.

- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) This can be worked around by entering `setstage MQ104 90` into the console.
- Just as you defeat Mirmulnir, you'll see *"Dovahkiin, No!"* with subtitles enabled. However, no audio file exists for it. Similarly, you may see the following subtitles during battle: *"You are brave. Balaan hokoron. Your defeat brings me honor."*, *"Brit grah. I had forgotten what fine sport you mortals can provide!"*, or *"Thurri du hin sille ko [Sovngarde](Skyrim_Sovngarde_(place).md)!."* - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.5, fixes this bug.
- Couriers may deliver [letters](Skyrim_Letter_from_a_Friend.md) to find Words of Power if you use [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) at the Western Watchtower. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.6, addresses this issue. The event which calls WICast Magic04 failed to check and make sure this quest was far enough along.
