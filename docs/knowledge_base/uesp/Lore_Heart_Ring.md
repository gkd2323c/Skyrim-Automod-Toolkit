# Heart Ring

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/MW-item-Heart_Ring.jpg/200px-MW-item-Heart_Ring.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Heart_Ring.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Heart_Ring.jpg) Heart Ring *Dagoth Ur's kin have become markedly more powerful in recent decades, after remaining stable for thousands of years. If they can be individually isolated and destroyed, they will not be able to support Dagoth Ur in later stages of the war. It may also be that the dramatic increase in their power comes from items enchanted by Dagoth Ur. Salvage of such items might contribute to our resources.*
— [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PDDU-1)</sup> The Heart Ring is an artifact ring associated with the [Sixth House](Lore_Sixth_House.md). [Enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) and worn by [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) himself. The ring greatly enhanced resistance to magic as well as to frost, fire, shock, and paralysis. It also allowed the wearer to dispel magical effects from themselves and caused attackers to be struck by lightning whenever they landed a hit.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HR-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/MW-creature-Dagoth_Ur.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Dagoth_Ur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Dagoth_Ur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Dagoth_Ur.jpg) Dagoth Ur [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) Heart of Lorkhan The exact time of the Heart Ring's creation is unknown. However, when Dagoth Ur awoke from divine slumber in [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882) alongside his Ash Vampire brothers, he soon emerged with them from beneath [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) into the [Heart of Lorkhan's](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) Chamber and ritually bound them as heartwights in a ritual of his own devising.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DUP-3)</sup> Each of the seven Ash Vampires was bestowed a unique item personally enchanted by Dagoth Ur,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PDDU-1)</sup> renowned for his mastery of enchantment. [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) speculated that Dagoth Ur likely shared a portion of his immortality with them, though he did not rule out more conventional sorcery.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vivec-4)</sup> The Heart Ring was one of the Ash Vampire artifacts retained by Dagoth Ur for his own use.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HR-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:111</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) had spent the previous twenty years unsuccessfully pursuing a plan to defeat Dagoth Ur. Part of this effort involved eliminating his Ash Vampire brethren. Vivec speculated that their greatly increased power may have come from items enchanted by Dagoth Ur, and believed that recovering such artifacts could strengthen the Tribunal's own resources.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PDDU-1)</sup> In the same year Vivec shared these plans with [the Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vivec-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCO6H-6)</sup> who went on to challenge the members of the Sixth House. The Nerevarine defeated Dagoth Ur's lieutenants: Dagoth Araynys, Dagoth Endus, Dagoth Gilvoth, Dagoth Odros, Dagoth Tureynul, Dagoth Uthol, and Dagoth Vemyn,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCO6H-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Dagoths-7)</sup> and recovered their Ash Vampire artifacts: the [Amulet of Heartfire](Lore_Amulet_of_Heartfire.md), [Amulet of Heartheal](Lore_Amulet_of_Heartheal.md), [Amulet of Heartrime](Lore_Amulet_of_Heartrime.md), [Amulet of Heartthrum](Lore_Amulet_of_Heartthrum.md), [Belt of Heartfire](Lore_Belt_of_Heartfire.md), [Blood Ring](Lore_Blood_Ring.md), and [Soul Ring](Lore_Soul_Ring.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCO6H-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Artifacts-8)</sup> Those artifacts were of great use for the Nerevarine in their confrontation against the Dagoth Ur.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCO6H-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:110</sup> Later that year, the Nerevarine confronted Dagoth Ur beneath Red Mountain. Dagoth wielded the Heart Ring in battle, and although he was slain, the ring remained behind as he reformed within Akulakhan's Chamber. The Nerevarine recovered the ring before the final confrontation and ultimately defeated Dagoth Ur for good.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCO6H-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:111</sup>
