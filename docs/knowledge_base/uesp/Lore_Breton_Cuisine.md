— *[Picnic at Pelin](Lore_Picnic_at_Pelin_(A_Horror_Story).md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Pa P-1)</sup> Breton cuisine is considered to be some of the very best in Tamriel, with many of Tamriel's greatest cooks having been [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Bretons enjoy prosperous trade along the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md)'s fertile soil begets numerous crops. However, the rigid economic hierarchy of Breton culture is also reflected in their cuisine: professional cooks prepare the most elaborate and expensive dishes for the aristocracy, while the impoverished peasantry must make do with what they can grow or forage themselves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESOC-3)</sup><sup>:12</sup> The [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres.md) is known for spices<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> and a variety of wines and spirits.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Wot S-5)</sup>

A notable Breton chef was [Donolon](Lore_Donolon.md) who earned a variety of titles such as the Wizard of Whisking, the Prince of Pastry, and the Patron of Pies during the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). A legendary confectioner, his clientele included the likes of nobles and even [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). Regardless of a person or entity's status, they might have to wait for years to even taste his most coveted creation, the Jubilee Cake.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONCD-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONAFC-7)</sup> The [Surilie](Lore_Surilie.md) family are Bretonic vintners whose history stretches through at least three eras.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOAMA-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRSBW-9)</sup> They produce some of the best wines in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), with it being particularly famous in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBRCS-10)</sup> By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), their base of operations was located at the wine-producing capital of Tamriel, [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBGS-11)</sup> where they established the Surilie Vineyards.

## Utensils and Tableware
Breton utensils and tableware are often of a high quality, with the material they're made from changing depending on which part of Breton society produced them. The [Wyrd Sisters](Lore_Wyrd_Covens.md) may use an egg whisk carved from a single piece of [oak](Lore_Oak.md)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup> while "city" Bretons pour their milk from decorative porcelain pitchers<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup> and [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) use cutlery made from wood that was grown into shape, no carving required.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

Some Breton utensils are popularized by royalty which spreads their use among the populace, an example being oversized wooden utensils.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Others are found only among royalty such as ebony-inlaid pudding bowls.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RPB-16)</sup> Almost comically small forks are used to navigate delicate seafoods.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Drinkware includes travel mugs crafted from pewter and adorned with symbols of High Rock<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> alongside quality waterskins made from [rabbit](Lore_Rabbit.md) hide.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Some drinking cups are carved from [sabre cat](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md) tusks and engraved with Breton symbols.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Decorative ceramic drink pitchers take the shape of legendary creatures such as [Ithguleior](Lore_Ithguleoir.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-21)</sup> In the Systres, tall, bulbous glasses are used for mixed drinks<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-22)</sup> and novelty tea infusers are found in the archipelago as well.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Breton tea service sets from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) are kept by some as family heirlooms many years later.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

Other cookware and kitchen tools include cheese graters, used by both the elite and the smallfolk,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup> large meat grinders<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup> and handheld flour sifters.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-HFH-27)</sup> High quality spice shakers are made from silver.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup>.
