# Oblivion Crisis

| --- |

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg/200px-OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg) A gate to the planes of Oblivion The Oblivion Crisis, also known as the **Great Anguish**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RT-1)</sup> was a total war between [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) hordes and the population of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It began with the assassination of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and all of his known heirs in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) at the hands of a fanatical cult of worshippers known as the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBT-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOC-3)</sup>

Shortly afterward, Oblivion Gates to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) began opening, unleashing Daedra across Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBMQ-4)</sup> With each Gate that opened, the [barriers](Lore_Dragonfires.md) between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [Firmament](Lore_Astronomy.md), would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBMartin-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBMankar-6)</sup> Widespread devastation and casualties resulted across entire provinces. The Daedra besieged [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and laid waste to the [Old Holds](Lore_Hold.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBRumors-7)</sup> In [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) called back many [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to fight off the Daedra. According to the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), the Daedra were forced to close the Gates in Black Marsh due to an Argonian counter invasion.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIC-8)</sup> In [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the city of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) was entirely destroyed.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBKvatch-9)</sup>

Adril Arano, a Second Councilor of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) from the [third century](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#Third_Century) of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), claimed that the Empire pulled most of its forces out of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) during the Crisis.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DBArano-10)</sup> However, this contradicts the words of [High Chancellor Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) himself during the Crisis, who stated that Cyrodiil and cities such as Bruma were virtually defenseless precisely because he was both unable and unwilling to pull any troops out of the other provinces.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ob Ocato-11)</sup> Indeed, the Imperial Province was left almost entirely to its own devices, and no foreign legions were ever found within Cyrodiil for the entirety of the Crisis or its immediate aftermath.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OB-12)</sup> Still, a history of the Raven Rock colony records that the bulk of the Imperial Guard stationed there was recalled to Cyrodiil from the then-Nordic Solstheim, though the author's source for this matter are the [Redoran](Lore_Redoran.md) [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of the settlement.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ho RRv1-13)</sup>

Although the Empire was near collapse, Uriel VII's illegitimate son, [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), ended the invasion with the help of [a mysterious hero](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md). The cost, however, was enormous. Martin, the last of the [Septim bloodline](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), sacrificed himself and the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) to become an Avatar of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and cast Mehrunes Dagon back into Oblivion. This event marked the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBLD-14)</sup> The sacrifice of Martin Septim at the conclusion of the Crisis "sealed the gates of Oblivion forever". The new barrier was noted to be too powerful for a [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md) to breach for more than fleeting moments, even though "a thousand [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) would likely dissolve" in the presence of such a Stone.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBMartin-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Excavation-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vonos Journal-16)</sup>

## History

### Prelude
*For centuries, prophets have spoken of the day when the aetherial walls that separate our dimension, Mundus, from Oblivion, the land of the Daedra, will crack asunder.*
—A member of the Mages Guild [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png/200px-LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Crisis_Begins.png) Uriel Septim VII's assassination Mehrunes Dagon long had desires upon Tamriel, starting in the Interregnum period. Initially Mehrunes Dagon had little interest in [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), as other Daedric Princes sought to expand their influence in the absence of the Dragonblood Emperor's maintaining Akatosh's Covenant. [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) initially invaded Tamriel at this time with his attempted Planemeld, and then the Triad of [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) and [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) attempted to gain influence on Nirn and bypass [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s Coldharbour Compact. But Kinlady Estre, dissatisfied with the tolerant direction the High Queen of the Aldmeri Dominion, had made a pact with Mehrunes Dagon, leading to his invasion of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), but this plot was foiled by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md). This may have been the start of his greater ambitions for Tamriel.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Skyrim-17)</sup>

The Mythic Dawn has unclear origins, but may have been present since the time of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-OBTar Meena-18)</sup> They apparently believed that Mundus was the plane of Oblivion of the Daedric Prince [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), who was murdered by the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), and the world was trapped in lies and illusion in order to prop up their illegitimate rule.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBMankar-6)</sup> They also believed that Mehrunes Dagon's true domain was Hope and Change,<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup> and that he would 'liberate' Mundus from the Aedra, granting his followers 'eternal paradise', which would manifest itself as a small plane of Oblivion.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/OB-misc-Anniversary_Art_23.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Anniversary_Art_23.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Anniversary_Art_23.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Anniversary_Art_23.jpg) The Hero of Kvatch fighting a Dremora Lord within a Sigillum Sanguis The infamous [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md), who employed armies of Daedra and undead to conquer much of western Tamriel, was aided by a mysterious 'Master', who may have been Mehrunes Dagon. However, it is thought the Usurper lost favor with this Master, hobbling his conquests leading up to his eventual defeat. The same day as the Usurper's defeat, his mistress supposedly gave birth to Mankar.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TR-19)</sup>

It's also thought that Mehrunes Dagon played an active role during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), when Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) impersonated the Emperor for ten years.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BHOTE-20)</sup> Tharn made a deal to allow Dagon access to the Imperial [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), which he invaded, eliminating powerful battlemages that may have threatened his rule.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BS-21)</sup> During this time, Mehrunes Dagon was apparently strengthening his hold of Oblivion, invading realms such as the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md) and [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), as well as recruiting several [Seducers](Lore_Seducer.md) to his army.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BSFrost-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-BSIdeal-23)</sup> While Tharn was eventually overthrown, Dagon was banished from the Battlespire, and the forces of the Empire hunted down several of Dagon's agents, they were unable to stop the rise of the Mythic Dawn.

### Major Battles
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png/200px-LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png) A Siege Crawler laying waste to a city | - **Siege of Kvatch**: Gates to Oblivion were opened outside [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), from which a horde of Daedra and a [siege crawler](Lore_Siege_Crawler.md) emerged to overwhelm the guard and sack the city. Much of the population was killed, including Count [Ormellius Goldwine](Lore_Ormellius_Goldwine.md) and [Saint Jiub](Lore_Jiub.md). After the city was occupied by the Daedric forces, most of the gates were closed, leaving only one lesser gate blocking the city's gates. This gate was eventually closed by a mysterious hero who would later be dubbed the Hero of Kvatch, who then helped the remaining city guard retake the city.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBKvatch-9)</sup> <br> - **Fall of Ald'ruhn**: The city of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) was destroyed by the Daedra.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ho RRv1-13)</sup> In an attempt to defend the city, the [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) resurrected the Ald Skar Emperor Crab, which also fell.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-24)</sup> <br> - **The Black Marsh Theater**: The Hist were able to somehow anticipate the Crisis and recalled much of the Argonian population back to their home province shortly before, where the Argonians were invited into the Hist trees and somehow strengthened physically. The An-Xileel claim that Argonians were so successful in defending their homeland they were able to counter-invade the Oblivion Gates that were summoned, forcing the Daedra to close them.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIC-8)</sup> <br> - **Collapse of the Crystal Tower**: The [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) on the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), the bastion of all magical learning before the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) was built, was destroyed by Mehrunes Dagon's invading Daedra, despite the efforts of the powerful [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) wizards.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RT-1)</sup> <br> - **Defense of Bruma**: Martin Septim led an army of Cyrodilic city guards to victory over the invading Daedric hordes. A statue was erected within the city's walls in honor of the lone hero who charged into Oblivion in order to shut the Great Gate and prevent the destruction of Bruma.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-OBBruma-25)</sup> <br> - **Attack on the Imperial City**: High Chancellor Ocato, Martin Septim, his [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) and Imperial Legionary escorts fought their way through an Oblivion-besieged [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). During this battle, dozens of Oblivion Gates opened around the city and the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon himself appeared, marking the fall of the barriers between Oblivion and Nirn. The battle ended when Martin Septim, in a desperate last ditch effort, shattered the Amulet of Kings and became the Avatar of Akatosh. The Avatar banished Dagon back into Oblivion, then turned to stone, a monument to Martin's sacrifice.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBLD-14)</sup> |

### Aftermath
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg/200px-GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-The_Fall_of_Ald%27ruhn.jpg) The city of Ald'ruhn fell to Daedra The Oblivion Crisis created a renewed fear of the Daedra, of dark magic, as well as magic in general, particularly in hard-hit places like Skyrim. The Vigil of Stendarr was founded in response to the Oblivion Crisis with the goal of hunting down any Daedra or practitioners of the Dark Arts.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Vo SDialogue-26)</sup> The Mythic Dawn were hunted down by numerous warrior groups and thoroughly eradicated, despite later attempts to rebuild the cult. One group, known as the [Keepers of the Razor](Lore_Keepers_of_the_Razor.md), sought out [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md) and broke it into three pieces, passing them along to their descendants in order to keep them out of the hands of potential cultists.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TKOTR-27)</sup>

The death of Martin Septim marked the end of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), and the Third Era. While Mehrunes Dagon had failed to conquer Tamriel, the damage to the continent would greatly weaken the Empire. Most provinces in the Empire were already restive before the Crisis had broken out, and without a clear heir to the throne, separatist sentiments grew even greater,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Ocato Dialogue-28)</sup> with common rumors in Cyrodiil already speaking of provinces wanting to secede from the Empire.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Rumors-29)</sup> High Chancellor Ocato would become Potentate of the Empire and attempt to stabilize it. However, Black Marsh under the nationalistic An-Xileel soon seceded and renamed the land Argonia. Argonia would then take advantage of the chaos of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) to invade Morrowind, weakening the Empire further and fueling more anti-imperial sentiment.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIC-8)</sup>

The assassination of Potentate Ocato in [4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15) led to the start of the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md), which ended when [Titus Mede I](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md), the founder of the [Mede Dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md), captured the Imperial City in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22). During this chaos, [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) was sacked once again by [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIC-8)</sup>

The Oblivion Crisis helped propel the marginalized [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) of the Summerset Isles to new prominence, as they were able to take most of the credit for the defense of the Isles. The Thalmor would push to secede entirely from the Empire in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), and eventually seized [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), reforming the Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RT-1)</sup>

## Notes
- Similar invasions of Tamriel by Mehrunes Dagon include the attacks on [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), [Whisper Grove](Lore_Whisper_Grove.md), and [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESO-30)</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the reformed Mythic Dawn, led by a new prophet named [Vonos](Lore_Vonos.md), sought to bring about a new invasion via the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md). The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) unwittingly helped the cult to open a new Oblivion Gate due to their unique connection to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). The Dragonborn ventured into Deadlands and slayed the invasion force's two heralds, delaying the invasion. The [Vigil of Stendarr](Lore_Vigil_of_Stendarr.md) then wrested control of the Gate from the Mythic Dawn, and stood guard over it to ensure no Daedra came through from the other side.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Cause-31)</sup>
