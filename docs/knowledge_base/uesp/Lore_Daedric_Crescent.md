# Daedric Crescent

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/MW-item-Daedric_Crescent_02.png/200px-MW-item-Daedric_Crescent_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Crescent_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Crescent_02.png) Daedric Crescent (Morrowind) The Daedric Crescent Blade<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Eo M-1)</sup> (also called a **Battle-scythe blade**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-2)</sup> is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). It has the power to paralyze those it strikes, and puts heavy wear on their armor; it has also been known to create a green ball of energy, but its effects are unknown. There were once many Crescents, which were used by Dagon's forces to take the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md). When the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) later reclaimed the ruined academy, the Crescents were gathered up and destroyed - all but one. Unknown to the Empire, one of the unique blades remained in existence somewhere in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), although it is said that none had ever seen it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup>

Daedric Crescent [Staves](Lore_Staff.md) and Daggers existed,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VCDAS-4)</sup> and the crescent also appears quite broadly in architectural features across the various [Daedric ruins](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) extant from ancient [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## History

### Third Era
The last Daedric Crescent Blade saw action during Mehrunes Dagon's occupation of the Battlespire.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-6)</sup><sup>:241</sup>

The last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), [Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWYB-7)</sup> At Fyr's behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote a book known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-6)</sup><sup>:241</sup> from the notes that he gathered over the centuries, listing the last Daedric Crescent among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". The book also provided some general information on the artifact to its readers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup> Fyr himself hoped to utilize Yagrum's findings in his search for the Crescent and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWFR-8)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), this last Crescent was discovered by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md). It had been in the possession of [Lord Dregas Volar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lord_Dregas_Volar), a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) who dwelled in the Daedric shrine of Magas Volar, a Daedric sanctuary inaccessible without teleportation. Divayth Fyr of [Tel Fyr](Lore_Tel_Fyr.md) had come to possess an amulet which would teleport the wearer to the hidden shrine; the Nerevarine used the amulet and defeated Lord Volar, claiming the last known Daedric Crescent.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Eo M-1)</sup>

## Notes
- Although their imagery is associated with Mehrunes Dagon, Dregas Volar used Daedric Crescent Daggers when he served as a general in [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s service.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Anchor G-9)</sup> He was tasked with protecting Dark Anchors, and was slain by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) while doing so.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Anchor-10)</sup> After his defeat, his crescent daggers and a staff were recovered from the Dark Anchor he defended.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VCDAS-4)</sup> The [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md), [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), was also known to have crescent weapons.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONATS-11)</sup> Which may or may not have been implemented in reference to [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s later involvement with *Battlespire* where the Daedric Crescents were employed<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BSIntro-12)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup> (also of note, Mehrunes Dagon is said to have given Jagar Tharn weapons for his Imperial coup).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Gamepen-13)</sup>
- The Crescent is engraved with the same [Daedric writing](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) ABMNOPRHVIM in both [*Battlespire*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire) and [*Morrowind*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind), which can be transliterated to "*ABMNOPRHVIM*".
- The Daedric Crescent Blade appears many times visually in [*Battlespire*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire), but not as a playable weapon.
- The Daedric Crescent's shape may be in reference to Mehrunes Dagon's non-expositional relationship to the moon (expressed via the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](Lore_Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver.md), a sword created by Dagon).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Of note, in the game's introduction cutscene of *Battlespire* a Dark Seducer wielding a Daedric Crescent Blade holds it to a crescent moon as it glows upon Battlespire.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BSIntro-12)</sup>
