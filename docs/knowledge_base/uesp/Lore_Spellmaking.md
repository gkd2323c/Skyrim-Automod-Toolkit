# Spellmaking

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/LG-cardart-Altar_of_Spellmaking.png/200px-LG-cardart-Altar_of_Spellmaking.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Altar_of_Spellmaking.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Altar_of_Spellmaking.png) Altar of Spellmaking [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg) Ulfsild the Evergreen Spellmaking,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spellmakers TES3-1)</sup> also known as **spell making**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OSM-2)</sup> **spell-crafting**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FR-3)</sup> or **spellcrafting**,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is the [magical](Lore_Magic.md) craft of creating new spells.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OSM-2)</sup> Spells taught at institutions of learning, known as circinate spells are standardized and more readily available. However, while it is possible to simply buy a standardized and premade spell from institutions such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) even without understanding of the underlying magical theory, which is said to be how spells are usually acquired, such spells are generally not as powerful as those crafted and cast by a mage with real knowledge.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BW-4)</sup> Thus, for more advanced practitioners of magic seeking more unique effects, spellmaking is an option.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DQ-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DUG-6)</sup><sup>:64</sup>

[Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild.md) collaborated with the [Luminaries of Magic](Lore_Luminaries_of_Magic.md) to create **Scribing**, the "metamagical theoryset"<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Largesse-7)</sup> which was the precursor to the modern spellcrafting system, in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Crow-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ON2024-9)</sup> With the strength of the Luminaries fueling it, the Scribing Altar could achieve incredible feats of magic, allowing it to "shape the very heart of magic itself" and translate its very essence into mortal hands with the Luminary as the intermediary, thereby changing, or rewriting, both the magic and the mortal, as the practicioner scrawls their [soul](Lore_Souls.md) upon the page. As beings of pure magic the Luminaries are said to have understood magic in a way mortals never could and so, their power, freely given, allowed one to access arcane mysteries most could only dream of. Indeed, Scribing is said to have been somehow destined to channel the power of the Luminaries from the start.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Crow-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TI-10)</sup>

The power of the [Indrik](Lore_The_Indrik.md) infused every drop of ink, every sheet of parchment, every whorl and dot upon the pages of the texts used for the Scribing process.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TI-10)</sup> The [Gryphon](Lore_The_Gryphon.md), sure in himself and direct in his actions, was associated with the development of Focus Scripts, arcane scripts which defined what effect the Scribed magic would have. The [Netch](Lore_The_Netch.md) worked with Ulfsild to create the Luminous Ink, the substrate of power into which the magic of the Scribing altar was vested, and served as the binding agent that drew magicka and intent together upon the page. The [Dragon](Lore_The_Dragon.md), keen of mind and eager to listen, was associated with the development of Signature elements, Scripts that could adapt to the magical and fighting styles of the person holding the parchment, named for all the ways in which Scribing could interact with the goals and magics of other disciplines that Ulfsild had discovered.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-How-11)</sup>

When Scribing was rediscovered, the great power of the Luminaries caused new [Ley Line](Lore_Ley_Lines.md) activity across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), thus making Affix Scripts, Signature Scripts and Luminous Ink appear across the land.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TI-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Sigil Dragon-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SOTIA-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Sigil Gryphon-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Sigil Netch-15)</sup> The rediscovery caught the attention of [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md), who envisioned altars in every Mages Guild hall, and saw that Scribing would influence even roving mages out in the wilds.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAS-16)</sup> Indeed, some sources claim the first nine Scribing Scripts disseminated among the Mages Guild were hand copied and examined by Galerion himself, thus providing reference materials deemed, in some cases, even better than the original texts, though this was disputed by a guild Chronicler who claimed the Scripts were his own work instead.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Largesse-7)</sup> Some sources claim that the praxes and rituals used by all modern potionmakers, itemmakers, and spellmakers were originally codified by Vanus Galerion.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Feyfolken-17)</sup> Spellcrafters have existed outside Ulfsild's Scribing and Vanus Galerion's modernization, however, and have created spells outside their contributions.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AMA-18)</sup>

Those who practice spellmaking are referred to as spellmakers,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spellmakers TES3-1)</sup> spellcrafters,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PM-19)</sup> and spellsmiths.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> They are known as the Isolationists within the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Iso-20)</sup>

Spellmaking Altars,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FSM-21)</sup> and Spell Makers, serve as the supplementary tools for mages to craft spells.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DQ-5)</sup> Magetallow candles are utilized to power some of those tools.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FSM-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MCB-22)</sup> However, some mages are capable of inventing spells without utilizing these altars, opting instead to cast them directly to assess their effects, sometimes on willing subjects.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Neloth-23)</sup>

Spells were sometimes named after their creators, such as Merien's White Mask devised by Merien Sellan of [Eagle's Brook](Lore_Eagle%27s_Brook.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Sellan-24)</sup> or [Shalidor's Mirror](Lore_Spellmirror.md) created by the legendary Arch-Mage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MW-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Shalidor-26)</sup> Master [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) also lent his name to his experimental spells.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Neloth-23)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Powers-27)</sup> Sometimes spells and magically enchanted [scrolls](Lore_Scrolls.md) are named after other individuals. Tarhiel's spell and scrolls of Icarian Flight bore Icarian's name.<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup>

While usually associated with magical institutions like the Mages Guild and similar organizations,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Iso-20)</sup> as well as temples such as the [Temple](Lore_Temple_of_Kynareth.md) of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SOK-28)</sup> spell invention is a practice observed among mages throughout Tamriel,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spellmakers TES3-1)</sup> and beyond,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-GTNS-29)</sup> spanning from elite practitioners like Master Neloth, or Archmagister Mavon of House Telvanni,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Neloth-23)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AMA-18)</sup> to more commonplace spellcasters, whether from urban, rural, or tribal backgrounds.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spellmakers TES3-1)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Sellan-24)</sup>

[Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) law mandates the Mages Guild to instruct any eager learner in the commonly practiced spells of magic, yet it does not extend the same obligation to the crafting of magical items or the acquisition of components essential for creating new spells, and Advanced Spellcrafting was considered potentially dangerous to the public at large. Accessing the guild's spellmaking and enchanting services necessitates higher-ranked membership in good standing by the Council of Mages, and entry to the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-PRIMA-31)</sup><sup>:64</sup>

[Frostcrag Spire](Lore_Frostcrag_Spire.md) and the Praxographical Center of the Arcane University are known for their spellmaking equipment.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FSM-21)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-AUPC-32)</sup> Master Spellmakers are the mages who resided in the Arcane University and offered their services to other members of the guild. In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Borissean and Gaspar Stegine were considered the premier spellmakers of the university.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-GSAB-33)</sup>
