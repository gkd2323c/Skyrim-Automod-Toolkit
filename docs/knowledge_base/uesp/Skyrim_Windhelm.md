[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/SR-place-Windhelm.jpg/200px-SR-place-Windhelm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Windhelm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Windhelm.jpg) Windhelm **Windhelm** is a major city in the northeast of Skyrim near the [Dunmeth Pass](Skyrim_Dunmeth_Pass.md) to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and the capital of [Eastmarch](Skyrim_Eastmarch.md). Lying near the coast at the northern tip of Eastmarch, the city is extremely cold and frequently experiences blizzards. The ruler of Windhelm is [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) [Ulfric Stormcloak](Skyrim_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), the leader of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md), who resides in the [Palace of the Kings](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md). The city does not comply with the Imperial ban on [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship and features a [Temple of Talos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Temple_of_Talos), and anti-foreign sentiment is common among the [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) population.

The temple and [inn](Skyrim_Candlehearth_Hall.md) lie along the main avenue of Windhelm, in the Stone Quarter. To the west is an open-air market, where [Niranye](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Niranye) operates a general goods stall with a nearby [arcane enchanter](Skyrim_Arcane_Enchanter.md), [Aval Atheron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Aval_Atheron) sells fresh meats, and [Hillevi Cruel-Sea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hillevi_Cruel-Sea) sells produce. The oldest section of the city is Valunstrad, "Avenue of Valor" in the old Nord language, which contains the palace and several residential buildings. Because of its proximity to Morrowind, Windhelm also hosts a sizable population of [Dark Elves](Skyrim_Dark_Elf.md) who sought refuge in Skyrim after the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). The Dark Elves are confined to a cramped slum below the rest of Windhelm called the Gray Quarter, previously known as the Snow Quarter. The Dunmer are not violently persecuted, but the local Nords are suspicious and outwardly xenophobic, while Ulfric Stormcloak turns a blind eye to their plight. [Argonians](Skyrim_Argonian.md) also populate Windhelm, but are prohibited from entering the city itself and are forced to live and work on the docks outside the walls. [Brunwulf Free-Winter](Skyrim_Brunwulf_Free-Winter.md), a supporter of the Argonians and Dark Elves, believes the Argonians must remain outside the city for their own safety from the prejudiced Nords who follow Ulfric's beliefs.

For more information on Windhelm, see the [lore article](Lore_Windhelm.md).

## Notes
- Although the name "Valunstrad" does not appear in the game, the northwest district of the city (including the palace, barracks, and mansions north of the market) is named this in the *[Prima Official Game Guide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V__Skyrim__Prima_Official_Game_Guide)*.
- [Hjerim](Skyrim_Hjerim.md) is a [house](Skyrim_Houses.md) available for purchase after completion of certain quests related to the [Civil War](Skyrim_Civil_War.md). It may be purchased from the steward for 8,000 to 12,000 gold.
- [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) adds a permanently disabled map marker for Windhelm Docks.
- Version 2.1.2 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) marks the everything in docks as owned, regardless whether they were owned originally or not.
- Windhelm also appears in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Windhelm)* and [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Windhelm).
- The conversation between [Malthyr Elenil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Malthyr_Elenil) and [Brunwulf Free-Winter](Skyrim_Brunwulf_Free-Winter.md) in front of [Sadri's Used Wares](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sadri%27s_Used_Wares) only takes place between 8am~8pm, and if the player has not spoken to Brunwulf Free-Winter before.

- One of the barrels on the docks may clip with barrels stacked below it. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
