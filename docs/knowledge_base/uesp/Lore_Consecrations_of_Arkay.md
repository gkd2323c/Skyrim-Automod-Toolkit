# Consecrations of Arkay

The Consecrations of Arkay, also known as the **Three Consecrations**, are a set of three blessings associated with the god [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md). They are administered by members of the [Order of Arkay](Lore_Order_of_Arkay.md) to protect mortal souls from necromancy. According to the teachings of the Order, these three blessings were bestowed upon them by Arkay.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOA-1)</sup> Followers of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) rely upon the Consecrations of Arkay to safeguard their souls during the passage to the afterlife.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-APAYQ-2)</sup> Performing the rites required many years of proper priestly training.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MB-3)</sup> It is not uncommon for some victims of large-scale battles to go without the rites of Arkay due to the sheer scale of such conflicts.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Hallfrida-4)</sup> Whether the Consecrations of Arkay applied to [soul magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md) was a subject of scholarly debate between [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) and [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) during their years in the Psijic Order. Vanus Galerion argued against necromancy, while Mannimarco was proponent of it.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSDOMAG-5)</sup> Some rites required use of sacred oil or water.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HPI-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VMF-7)</sup>

## Three Consecrations

### Arkay's Grace
Arkay's Grace is bestowed at birth by priests to protect the souls of the innocent until they are old enough to exercise their own volition.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOA-1)</sup> Powerful priests, such as High Priest [Ingurt](Lore_Ingurt.md) could also employ it to ward off [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HPI-6)</sup> and in some cases, to cure the early stages of vampirism, and, according to some accounts, could even restore those who have fully undergone vampiric transformation, provided the afflicted was truly repentant. It was also believed by some to be capable of curing fully contracted [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md), though performing such a rite was said to be exceptionally difficult.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RS-8)</sup>

Priests of Arkay swore by Arkay's grace.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CA-9)</sup> [House Tamrith's](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) motto was, "By Arkay's grace we live and die, but there is no end to the Tamrith line."<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RA-10)</sup>

### Arkay's Blessing
*Arkay bless this soul and sanctify this broken vessel.
May the light keep you in its warm and soothing embrace.
May the light cleanse this vessel and let it rest in peace.
By the light's holy radiance, let the dead stay dead.*
—Examples of Breton prayers performed during the rites for the dead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MB-3)</sup> Arkay's Blessing is bestowed by priests upon the dying to prevent their souls from being used without consent.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOA-1)</sup> At least some variants of the rite involved drinking sanctified water, which could protect souls from being claimed by Daedric influence.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VMF-7)</sup> According to Mannimarco, the rite was indeed effective, prompting him to instruct [his followers](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) to discredit such practices whenever possible. However, he was of the opinion that Arkay's Blessing could be overcome by those with sufficient daring.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ATE-11)</sup> According to Vanus Galerion, these rites are by no means infallible. Arkay's Blessing can be circumvented through underhanded methods of securing consent, or, under the right conditions, ignored entirely by a skilled necromancer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSDOMAG-5)</sup>

Arkay's Blessings were also used in other contexts, such as granting the blessed greater strength of will,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ST-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-KOTN-13)</sup> or protection from the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Buffs ESO-14)</sup>

"Arkay's Blessing" was also used as a general term for otherwise mundane rites of Arkay.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESO1-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Runil-16)</sup>

### Arkay's Law
Arkay's Law was bestowed by the priests upon the deceased, that their corporeal forms may not be raised to unlawful servitude.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOA-1)</sup> In [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), almost all dead were subject to the identical rites,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-CP-17)</sup> which were performed by the Thronekeepers of [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Luala-18)</sup> Despite venerating Arkay, relatively few [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) practiced his rites, due to their adherence to the [Meat Mandate](Lore_Green_Pact.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-CP-17)</sup> Unlike other rites, Mannimarco asserted that Arkay's Law was unwavering, preventing its followers from raising the dead. He also sought ways to discredit the priests and limit the practice's use.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ATE-11)</sup>

People split from Arkay's Law are referenced in certain poetic texts.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Wulfric-19)</sup>
