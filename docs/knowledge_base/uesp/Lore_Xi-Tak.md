[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins.jpg/200px-ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins.jpg) Xi-Tak Ruins circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Xi-Tak was an ancient [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) site located in the northern region of the isle of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md). By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it had long been abandoned, though the Collegium Praxis of [Clan Corelanya](Lore_Clan_Corelanya.md) had laid plans for its restoration and expansion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LV-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-XTR-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Xi-Tak_Ruins_02.jpg) Argonian structures in Xi-Tak [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-npc-Lector_Volonaro.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Lector_Volonaro.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lector_Volonaro.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lector_Volonaro.jpg) A representative of the Collegium Praxis Little is known of Xi-Tak's past. It was situated near [Sunport Bay](Lore_Sunport_Bay.md) Forest,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LV-1)</sup> not far from the later-constructed Broken Light Temple dedicated to [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) most of its structures had succumbed to time, with only fragments of its walls and a few Argonian stone statues remaining.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-XTR-2)</sup>

By that time, the College of Stones of the Collegium Praxis had taken an interest in restoring the Xi-Tak ruins, seeing their potential for controlling the island's waterways. However, the efforts were hampered by aggressive wildlife. To address this, they decided to hire mercenaries to deploy alchemical deterrents developed by the College of Cultivation. These would temporarily discourage predators from approaching the site without permanently harming the local ecosystem, giving the builders a window to work more safely and efficiently. Their request was eventually fulfilled. With the deterrents in place, restoration resumed with minimal disruption and far fewer complaints of bites and bruises.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LV-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-STR-3)</sup>
