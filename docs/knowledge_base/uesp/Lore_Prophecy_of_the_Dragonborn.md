# Prophecy of the Dragonborn


## The Prophecy
When misrule takes its place at the eight corners of the world

When the Brass Tower walks and Time is reshaped

When the thrice-blessed fail and the Red Tower trembles

When the Dragonborn Ruler loses his throne, and the White Tower falls

When the Snow Tower lies sundered, kingless, bleeding

The World-Eater wakes, and the Wheel turns upon the Last Dragonborn.

## Alduin's Wall
The Prophecy of the Dragonborn was recorded on Alduin's Wall, a large Akaviri mural found within [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md). As foretold in the prophecy, the five key historical events that would preface Alduin's return are depicted.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRAL-3)</sup>

The first half of Alduin's Wall depicts the historic events of the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) and the banishment of Alduin by the ancient [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) heroes. The other half foretells the omens. These include:

### The Staff of Chaos
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/LO-misc-Staff_of_Chaos_Wall.png/200px-LO-misc-Staff_of_Chaos_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Staff_of_Chaos_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Staff_of_Chaos_Wall.png) The shattered Staff of Chaos *"When misrule takes its place at the eight corners of the world..."* This omen refers to the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md), a powerful relic that could open gateways to other worlds and obliterate all living beings. In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Imperial battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) used the staff to imprison the Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) within the realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), after which he broke it into eight pieces and scattered them across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arena-4)</sup>

### Numidium and the Warp in the West
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png/200px-LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png) Numidium *"When the Brass Tower walks and Time is reshaped..."* The depiction of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) relates to the second omen in the prophecy. The enormous brass golem was reassembled and activated during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Time reshaped when each of the seven powers in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) took ownership of the golem—simultaneously—and each of them achieved their goals to some degree.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-5)</sup> This paradox was later known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Wit W-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WWYDB-7)</sup>

### The Red Year
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/LO-misc-Red_Mountain_Wall.png/200px-LO-misc-Red_Mountain_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Red_Mountain_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Red_Mountain_Wall.png) Red Mountain erupting *"When the thrice-blessed fail and the Red Tower trembles..."* The third omen of the prophecy foretells of the fate that would befall the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) at the hands of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Morrowind-8)</sup> The fulfilment of the [Nerevarine Prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) resulted in [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) losing their immortality. This in turn caused [Baar Dau](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baar_Dau) to resume its long-delayed fall, obliterating the city of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md) and causing [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to erupt.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TRY-9)</sup>

### The Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/LO-misc-Oblivion_Crisis_Wall.png/200px-LO-misc-Oblivion_Crisis_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Oblivion_Crisis_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Oblivion_Crisis_Wall.png) The Oblivion Crisis *"When the Dragonborn Ruler loses his throne, and the White Tower falls..."* The fourth omen foretold by the prophecy is the assassination of Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), resulting in the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Oblivion-10)</sup> The fall of the White Tower refers to the capture and sacking of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) during The [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) between the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TGW-11)</sup>

### Skyrim Civil War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/LO-misc-Snow_Tower_Wall.png/200px-LO-misc-Snow_Tower_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Snow_Tower_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Snow_Tower_Wall.png) Sundered Snow Tower *"When the Snow Tower lies sundered, kingless, bleeding..."* The final omen that heralds Alduin's return refers to the bloody civil war that erupted in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) after the death of the High King [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Skyrim-12)</sup> The tallest mountain in Skyrim, the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md), is depicted on Alduin's Wall as cleft in half.

### Last Dragonborn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d9/LO-misc-Dragonborn_Wall.png/200px-LO-misc-Dragonborn_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Dragonborn_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Dragonborn_Wall.png) The Last Dragonborn *"...the World-Eater wakes, and the Wheel turns upon the Last Dragonborn."* The prophecy ends with the awakening of Alduin and the appearance of the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), both events that came to pass in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Alduin emerged from the [Time Wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md) at the summit of the Throat of the World and sought to resurrect his kin, while the Last Dragonborn was discovered when repelling a Dragon attack in [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Skyrim-12)</sup>
