# Nordic

Nordic was the language spoken by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-1)</sup> It is sometimes referred to as **Old Nord**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> or the **northern tongue**, referring to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIC-3)</sup><sup>:22</sup> The Nordic god [Jhunal](Lore_Jhunal.md) is considered the father of languages.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup>

Nordic first saw use in the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDW-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Sabatons-6)</sup> It was considered an old tongue by [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EGTS-7)</sup> but saw limited use into even the [Fourth](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIC-3)</sup><sup>:22</sup> Next to nothing was known of Atmoran by the Second Era, with instances of the language being increasingly hard to find.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-HMTWM-8)</sup>

## Language Family and Genetic Relationship
The original [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) tongue likely descended from [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md), with both tongues using the term [Talos](Lore_Talos.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1C-9)</sup> and was entirely oral, bearing no written form until the Late Merethic Era. [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md), considered the first [human](Lore_Man.md) historian, created the runic alphabet of Atmoran speech using the principles of [Elvish](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-HMTWM-8)</sup> When the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) established its firm grip and dominion over Skyrim, [Dragon-tongue](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) became the dominant language. Use of the tongue was regulated by the ruling [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), who prevented the usage of certain words.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDW-5)</sup> Nords copied the [Dragon alphabet](Lore_Dragon_Alphabet.md) after watching the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) themselves carve the runes with their claws. They used this script for works of importance, such as burials,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DLMn M-10)</sup> but used a lettering system, colloquially referred to as ancient Nord, for other, common purposes.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nurnhilde-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Sleep-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Draugr-13)</sup> Nordic likely descends from the Atmoran tongue, with the two languages sharing some words.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1C-9)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TAH-14)</sup> It developed around the same time as the ancient Nord variation of Dragon-tongue, as words for Dragon from both languages were used around the same time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDW-5)</sup>
