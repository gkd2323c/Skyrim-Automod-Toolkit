# Reachmen


At least some Reachmen are known to be descended from the [Keptu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keptu) clan of [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mt C-11)</sup> Although they are thought to share descent, Bretons do not consider themselves kin to the Reachmen, and the Reachmen do not see themselves as Bretons.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Lore Answers-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ABAYQ-13)</sup> This sentiment is shared by other races.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOContraband OEEC-14)</sup>

Unlike their Breton cousins, who are typically pale and of slight build,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HOPTTO-15)</sup> Reachfolk are more diverse in appearance, ranging from short and slight framed<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESONelldena-16)</sup> to taller, broader, and muscular builds, more typical for [Nords](Lore_Nord.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOBradan-17)</sup> Some of the Reachfolk have traits closer to those typical to the inhabitants of the [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOGorbarth-18)</sup> and a few bear a distinctive red eye color.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOApprentice Fialyn-19)</sup>

Their culture is tribal and diverse; certain clans make little use of advanced technology, while some others employ [Dwemer animunculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) and advanced alchemy<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DPIOM-20)</sup>. Many clans utilize hides, bones, and copper to make gear, while others use properly forged weapons and armor<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-CM54-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CM55-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Bloodrage-23)</sup> and craft weapons and tools from materials such as [dwarven metal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Metal),<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONArdanir-24)</sup> silver,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-CM97Wayward_Guardian-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOContraband RM-26)</sup> and [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOContraband HBS-27)</sup> and even accessories from [malachite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malachite).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESOContraband Malachite Hairpin-28)</sup> Leather and wool are traditionally used as materials for Reachfolk garments.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOAyddelsa-29)</sup> As they are shunned by most of Nord and Breton society, they typically inhabit makeshift fortresses built into caves and ruins throughout the Reach's battle-scarred landscape.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TMOTR-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-7)</sup> There are also some similarities in the things worn by the Nedic [Keptu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keptu) and the Reachmen, showing that a cultural exchange might have taken place.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Keptu-Horn Skull Sallet-30)</sup>

Reachfolk, in a similar way to the Bretons, are often attributed with traits associated with elven cultures, such as secretive, haughty demeanor, and arrogance by their Imperial neighbors.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-31)</sup>

The Reachmen are also infamous for their advanced hedge magic. Some Reachman mages are known to wield magic with which they can poison or corrupt nature.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Schemes Reachmage-32)</sup> They are also said to have learned to control beastfolk magic, a wild hedge-wizardry.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> Some [Wyrd Covens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd), such as the Hagfeather Coven, the Rimerock Wyrd, and the Markarth Sisters, are known to have close relations with the Reachmen and their magic.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TGW-33)</sup>

Many clans worship [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), the most primal of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md). The top of their warrior ranks typically commune with Hircine so that they may ritualistically replace their hearts with [poisoned briars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briar_Heart), allowing them to become powerful warriors known as [Briarhearts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briarheart). Due to their reverence for Hircine, it is not uncommon to see lycanthropes within the Reachmen's ranks.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-31)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOBerinodh-34)</sup> Some Reachmen clans are known to also dedicate ceremonies to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and [Namira](Lore_Namira.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-AOLH-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ONLoading-36)</sup> [Shamans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Shamans) of some Reachfolk clans venerate [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) matrons, who in turn grant them knowledge of nature magic.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> Many other Reachfolk clans, however, fear and revile them.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONMD-37)</sup>

Following the [Markarth Incident](Lore_Forsworn.md#The_Markarth_Incident), Reachmen rebels in Skyrim became known as the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md). Hagravens led splinter groups encamped in various ruins and caves across the Reach.

## History
The Reachmen have long held that they are the true owners of the Reach, and for centuries have only begrudgingly accepted the rule of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TMOTR-5)</sup> Long before the conquests of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) and Nords in the Karth region, [human tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) dwelled within the caves in the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md),<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONLVR-38)</sup> where, according to the Vateshrans, they fled soon after Lorkh's sacrifice.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-NZLS-39)</sup> Those people, the ancestors of the modern Reachfolk, lived there already "when the world was young".<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONArdanir-24)</sup>

### Early Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-statue-Red_Eagle.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Red_Eagle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Red_Eagle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Red_Eagle.jpg) Depiction of legendary hero Red Eagle At an unknown point in history between the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and [1E 221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221), in the ancient past, Reachfolk were ruled by the [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) of the [Nighthollow Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nighthollow_Clan), who coveted their blood. The vampiric faction, known to them as "Night Lords", governed them from the depths of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-RBS-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONGVerandis-41)</sup> Eventually, the influence of the vampires waned, and accounts of their terror were reduced to mere bedtime stories.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-RBS-40)</sup>

During the [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) spawned to kill [High King Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md) in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369),<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-PGE3V-42)</sup> the Reachfolk captured one of the monstrosities that were spawned from the event in Skyrim.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-KCHR-43)</sup>

Circa [1E 358](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_358),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MGP-44)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Xan Timeline-45)</sup> just 3 years after the Direnni Hegemony rose into power in High Rock, the elven overlords had a presence in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). The area was later claimed by the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md). Following the collapse of this Nordic Empire in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), the Western Reach was retaken by the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md),<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Once-46)</sup> who slaughtered the majority of Nord colonists; as a result, the Nordic ancestry of the Reachmen is comparatively weak.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-7)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-46FS-47)</sup> Eventually, the Aldmeri overseers were eliminated, but traces of their blood forever remained in the veins of the native Reachmen.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-31)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-7)</sup>

The proximity of Reachmen settlements to [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) villages meant that the Reachmen frequently traded goods and customs with their mountain neighbors, and it is partly from the Orcs that the Reachmen learned to use hedge-magic.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-7)</sup>

High King [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) later reconquered the Reach for Skyrim at some point during his reign between [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) and [1E 452](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_452).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Plaque-48)</sup>

A few years after the disappearance of [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), several clans of Reachmen took refuge in the ruins of [Nchuand-Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nchuand-Zel) during times of seasonal hardship. By [1E 930](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_930), the community had developed into a fully-fledged settlement that was active all year round. The Reachmen called it ["Mar-Karth"](Lore_Markarth.md), which means "Above the Karth" in the language of the Reach. The strongest chieftain that lived in the town was known as the "Ard", known in the Reach tongue as the "King of the Fort".<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-49)</sup>

During the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md) in [1E 948](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_948), Reachmen mercenaries under the command of "the Beast of the Reach" [Mercedene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mercedene) of Clan [Winterborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterborn) fought alongside Bretons and Redguards.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-LT-50)</sup>

Granite mined from Markarth's quarries by the Reachmen was exported to places such as [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), a Breton city founded in [1E 983](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_983). Evermore Palace was built out of Markarth granite.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TRHo KE-51)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup>

*[The Legend of Red Eagle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Legend_of_Red_Eagle)*, an ancient tale from Reach folklore, suggests that during the time of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), the Reach was ruled by ten kings, and that "though men were free, the people were scattered and warred amongst themselves". The legend also makes reference to the Reachmen's reverence for "the ancient and venerable Hagravens". Despite the quarrels between the Reach kingdoms, they nevertheless expanded and prospered until [Empress Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra) and her armies arrived to pacify the Reach in [1E 1028](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1028); one by one, the kings of the Reach swore fealty or fell to her. The only king who refused to yield was [Faolan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Faolan) of [Sundered Hills](Lore_Sundered_Hills.md). In order to gain the power needed to defend his homeland, he sacrificed his heart and became the first [Briarheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briarheart).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Antiquity-52)</sup> Faolan and his followers engaged in an extended guerrilla campaign which, while tactically successful, was not enough to stem the seemingly endless waves of Imperial reinforcements. Hestra's generals returned with an even bigger invasion force and laid siege to Red Eagle's stronghold until he himself came forth for battle and killed a thousand soldiers before they brought him down in his final stand in [1E 1030](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1030).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-TLORE-53)</sup> He became the most famous hero of the Reachfolk.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Antiquity-52)</sup>

In [1E 2704](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2704), following the defeat of the Akaviri Invaders and the establishment of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), Emperor [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) focused on "the madmen of the Reach" and sliced the Reach in two pieces: [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) controlled the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) controlled the Eastern Reach. With the growth of the Second Empire, not one decade passed when armies - often sent from [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) or [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) - didn't have to suppress the Reachmen.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup>

In [1E 2801](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2801) Emperor [Kastav](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kastav) ordered the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) to seize hostages from Markarth and Hroldan to ensure that the jarls met their conscription quotas, which worsened the relations of the Empire with both native Reachfolk and their Nordic occupants. Three years later, in [1E 2804](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2804), Reachfolk made arrangements with the representatives of the Dragonguard and provided the order with food when the Emperor cut off supplies as a result of their refusal to help suppress the [Winterhold Rebellion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterhold_Rebellion).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AOTD-6)</sup>

With the end of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) and the start of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the Reachfolk reclaimed their independence and once again took Markarth as their capital, taking advantage of its defenses to stave off attacks from both Bretons and Nords.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-49)</sup>

### The Longhouse Emperors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg) Emperor Durcorach The [Tagh Droiloch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tagh_Droiloch), a secretive coven of witch-men, was founded c. [2E 516](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_516). They became a powerful faction and, over time, made themselves the secret masters of the Reach. For years, the Tagh Droiloch preferred to act from behind the scenes and were content to bide their time, allowing the dread of their name and whispers of their displeasure to govern the unruly Reach for them. In [2E 527](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_527) an exceptionally strong and cunning Reachfolk chieftain by the name of [Durcorach the Black Drake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) began building a following in the Reach. The Coven decided to ally with the promising warlord. Together, they were able to subdue all the rival clans and effectively unify the Reach. By [2E 528](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_528), Durcorach had become a warlord in command of two thousand soldiers, a number that had risen to ten thousand by [2E 529](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_529).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-HCP:TTD-54)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-SHOTLE-55)</sup>

During the 6th century of the Second Era, the Reachmen seem to have grown particularly powerful, eventually leading to the foundation of a Reachmen dynasty in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) known as the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).

In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), the first Longhouse Emperor, Durcorach the Black Drake, invaded High Rock and swiftly captured [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai), Evermore, and [Hallin's Stand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hallin%27s_Stand). Within days, his army was across the [Bjoulsae](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) and bearing down upon [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). Durcorach did not have siege engines, so after fifty-seven days of besieging Wayrest, he left part of his army around the town's walls and marched off west into [Glenumbra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenumbra). Taken by surprise, the newly-independent city-state of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) fell and was sacked. The Reachmen were defeated near Daggerfall's city gates after being charged from behind by [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md)'s Heavy Dragoons, and Durcorach was killed by Emeric himself.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Ta Toa M-56)</sup>

Nevertheless, Durcorach's dynasty continued to rule in Cyrodiil until the reign of [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic), Durcorach's grandson. In [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573), Leovic was the first Emperor to commission a Guide to Tamriel, labeled 'The Emperor's Guide to Tamriel'.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-EGTAuthor Foreword-57)</sup> In [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Leovic was [overthrown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen%27s_Rebellion) by his erstwhile supporter [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios), the Duke of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol), after he legalized Daedra worship within the Empire. This marked the end of the dynasty of Longhouse Emperors.

After the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), several Reach clans allied themselves with the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult), and remained powerful enough to launch attacks on the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) and [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md)'s territories, even attacking as far afield as [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) and Glenumbra.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ESO-58)</sup> Concurrently, one clan of Reachmen known as the Winterborn fought a lengthy war with the Orcs for control of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) after King [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog) reestablished [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) in the region. The war finally ended when the last Winterborn warlord, [Urfon Ice-Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Urfon_Ice-Heart), was slain at [Frostbreak Fortress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostbreak_Fortress).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ESO-58)</sup>

During this era, a larger number of Reachfolk than ever ventured out in search of their fortunes.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-49)</sup> Entire clans could be found within Cyrodiil.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Medresi_Guvron-59)</sup> While they were eventually expelled from Cyrodiil during the reign of Varen Aquilarios,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Arvnir-60)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-MTCMC-61)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-COT5C-62)</sup> by 2E 582, some could still be encountered in the former Imperial territories, including Blackwood.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Trano_Coventina-63)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TR-64)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Gellsoane-65)</sup> These Reachfolk came to be known as [Imperialized Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperialized_Reachfolk).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Tisfira-66)</sup> However, many of them eventually made their way back to the Reach.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Senan-68)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Arloakh-69)</sup> Reachfolk traveled abroad to the lands of Aldmeri Dominion, Daggerfall Covenant, and Ebonheart Pact,<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Bastheladir-70)</sup> and could also be encountered in foreign lands,<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-GSC-71)</sup> affiliating themselves with various foreign factions,<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-AAR-72)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Hroltar-73)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Sibellec-74)</sup> and some even delving into the realms of Oblivion, such as [Fargrave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fargrave),<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Kelaer-75)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Iglytha-76)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Anchorite-77)</sup> the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md),<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Caruggan-78)</sup> and [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Cipher_Cianrella-79)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Cipher_Devkeln-80)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Cipher_Bernollach-81)</sup>

### Battle of Old Hrol'dan
In the 9th century of the Second Era, some Reachmen allied with the [Second Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#Second_Dominion). As a people, the Reachmen remained largely independent – although they frequently clashed with the Nords to the east – until the arrival of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) at the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-TAH-82)</sup> In [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852), King Cuhlecain's general successfully broke through the Reachmen's lines, forcing them to fortify themselves at [Old Hrol'dan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Hrol%27dan). The next day he approached the gates of the besieged city, protected by the winds created by a storm following his trail. Reports say that the ancient Nord art of thu'um was used to shout down the walls of Old Hrol'dan, after which the army of Colovian troops and Nord berserkers took the city with relative ease.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-TAH-82)</sup> Markarth and the lands of the Reach were soon occupied by the Nords,<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-SRKibell-83)</sup> while Imperial propaganda branded the Reachmen as rebellious and lawless mongrels.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-7)</sup>

### The Forsworn Uprising
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/LG-cardart-Forsworn_Looter.png/200px-LG-cardart-Forsworn_Looter.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Forsworn_Looter.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Forsworn_Looter.png) One of the Forsworn In [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174), during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), when the Empire did not have the resources available to maintain the outer provinces, a group of Reachmen led by [Madanach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Madanach) (who would become their king) commenced what would later be known as the "[Forsworn Uprising](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forsworn_Uprising)", gaining control of the Reach and creating an independent kingdom.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TBOM-84)</sup> According to Arrianus Arius, they administered the kingdom relatively peacefully, with only a few of the harshest Nord landowners put to death.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TBOM-84)</sup> After two years, their experiment with independence seemed largely successful, and the leaders of the Reachmen were beginning the process of seeking recognition from the Empire.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TBOM-84)</sup> However, in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176), desperate to retake the Reach and with no Imperial Legions available due to the Great War, the previously deposed Jarl Hrolfdir enlisted the aid of a Nord militia led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) to retake the Reach by promising them free worship of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md). In that year, the Nord militia successfully drove the Reachmen from the city of Markarth and reclaimed the Reach, executing officials who worked for the Forsworn and those citizens who did not join the Ulfric's forces, including Nords, elderly, and children deemed old enough to be able to fight, as well as torturing natives to give up names of the rebels who fled into the wilds.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TBOM-84)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-CL-85)</sup> The survivors of the uprising fled into the wilds of the Reach and became known as the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md).

Driven from Markarth, the Forsworn spread across the Reach, fortifying any defensible spot they could find. In the wilds, the Forsworn function as a terrorist organization; they are composed of a series of cells, usually led by hagraven "matrons" or Briarheart warriors, with their main tactics being caravan raids and attacks on outlying settlements.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-AEGTS-86)</sup> The ongoing dispute caused consternation for those Reachmen who were not allied with the Forsworn. Native landowners were frequently under duress by both the Nords and the Forsworn, each party thinking the individual was working for the other.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-SRAinethach-87)</sup> Many Reachmen also lamented that so many of their friends and loved ones were fighting and dying in service of a long lost cause.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-SRBothela-88)</sup>

## Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/ON-npc-Ardanir.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ardanir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ardanir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ardanir.jpg) A member of the Wayward Guardians The Reachfolk live in scores of fractured clans, each with their own traditions and character. There is no uniform tradition among the clans, though many of them share deities and practices. Some clans are nomadic, while others settle in one place and build villages. Some people in a clan may be related to each other, and others adopt clan names to mark their allegiance. New clans are synthesized all the time as like-minded individuals come together. Sometimes they seek to settle new land or follow herds of prey, and other form to plunder and raid neighboring lands. In this way, the clans are fluid, breaking up and reforming throughout history. Many clans try to live in relative peace, though some, such as the [Boneshaper Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boneshaper_Clan), are overtly hostile towards outsiders. Most clans are minor, consisting of small villages, bands of nomads, or groups of marauders that hole up in caves or old forts. However, there are larger, more prominent clans.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-89)</sup> Some clans of the Reachfolk build hunting lodges decorated with Alrabeg's imagery,<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-CM65Huntsman-90)</sup> and ritual huts.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-OTNOR-91)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ETTW-92)</sup> Reachfolk have a huge variety of body markings that are based on the clan they belong to.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-ESORichien_Thelin-93)</sup>

Each clan is led by a chief, who may be called a chieftain, speaker, elder, or king depending on the clan and who the leader is. Most Reachfolk believe that chiefs who call themselves "kings" are just putting on airs. The clans are many and don't agree on everything, thus it is impossible to unify the clans beneath a single power. Any clan-chief can call himself a king. In fact, there have been points in history where several chiefs have claimed the title of "king" for themselves simultaneously. The Reachfolk have a saying: "anyone can be a king in the Reach, but no one is King of the Reach". Durcorach the Black Drake never took up the title of king for this reason. The Reachfolk viewed the Longhouse Emperors as war-leaders they followed of their own volition as opposed to a uniting force that ruled over the Reach as a whole. From the Reachfolks' perspective, kneeling to a king, no matter what race they are or if they've claimed the throne of Cyrodiil, is something that weaker peoples do.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-89)</sup>

Many clans live upon the shores of the Karth River, a place as harsh and uncaring as much of the rest of the Reach. Its fierce rapids veer around sharp stones, and it rides over steep cliffs that create powerful torrents. The people who rely upon the river live only if the river allows it, and its inhabitants respect it for its dangers and what it provides. The Reachfolk bathe in it, wash their clothes in it, drink from it, and cook their food with it. They regard the Karth river with respect, and at the very least, consideration. The folk who live upon the river's banks see what the river takes from them as a small price to pay for what it provides. They take from the river, and it extracts a price in return. The people of the Karth River don't blame it when it sweeps away structures and people; they accept that life near the river has its hardships, and they endure.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-LOTKR-94)</sup>

### Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/LO-misc-Reachfolk_Daedric_Script.png/90px-LO-misc-Reachfolk_Daedric_Script.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Reachfolk_Daedric_Script.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Reachfolk_Daedric_Script.png) Daedric script on Reachfolk weapons *Dirt is a part of life. The real discipline comes from wading through mud knowing that it'll be on you until you next find water.*
—Gorrof The Reachfolk have rich oral traditions and don't commit their stories to paper as scholars from most other provinces do.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TBOKL-95)</sup> Many from clans who live in the wilderness are illiterate and do not care to learn.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-AIIM-96)</sup> Some Reachfolk are even opposed to the idea of learning to read and write.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-LOTKR-94)</sup> Some Reachfolk who served the Longhouse Emperors in Cyrodiil during the Second Era encouraged their children to learn their letters. Following the fall of the Longhouse Dynasty, there was not much call for reading and writing in the Karth Valley, barring major settlements like Markarth, where such skills were more widespread.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONArdanir-24)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Arloakh-69)</sup>

Reachfolk occasionally adorn their weapons with Daedric inscriptions.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-Ancestral Reach-97)</sup> They also utilize glyphs.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-ESOContraband SWH-98)</sup> Verses of the Red Eagle's Song were carved into stone pillars in the sacred cave in [Red Eagle Redoubt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Eagle_Redoubt).<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-RESQ-99)</sup> Several major clans of the Reach such as Winterborn,<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-IHJ-100)</sup> Dreadhorn,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mt C-11)</sup> Icereach,<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-LTMC-101)</sup> and Doomfang<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DPIOM-20)</sup> shown high proficiency in writing. The [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md) also were known to read and write.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-FNHE-102)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-FNSB-103)</sup>

The Reachfolk value hard work and struggle. They hold people who regularly work with their hands or perform manual labor in high regard, and respect them more outwardly than some other cultures do.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-ESOHereline-104)</sup> The Reachfolk understand that hardship is inevitable, but instead of bemoaning their sorrows, they embrace the pain as wisdom.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-105)</sup>

Reachfolk do not condone stealing from other Reachmen, and theft is a very rare occurrence unless the clans are feuding.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-AYATEC-106)</sup> Theft is considered a serious offense among the Reachfolk, whether they dwell in the city of Markarth or in the rocky hills of the Karth Valley.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOGorbarth-18)</sup><sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ESOMulzurbesh-107)</sup> While the customs of the clans that live in the mountains are varied, the Reachfolk of Markarth punish theft severely. One of the customary punishments for such a crime is hand amputation.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ESOMulzurbesh-107)</sup>

Reachfolk are known to dance often and are perceived as great dancers by the outsiders.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-ESOJotep-Mota-108)</sup> Their dances are often related to ancient rituals, complicated in nature and involve numerous clan members.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ND-109)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-ESOFeathermoth-110)</sup> Some clans value dancing skills very high.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-ONJ.27ghanor-111)</sup>

Reachfolk deal in bargains, respect strength, and skill, and believe in rewarding favors.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONGVerandis-41)</sup> Offering favor for a favor is considered one of the more common ways of how business works in the Reach,<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-ARDC-112)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-LADBEL-113)</sup> and is one of the old and respected customs.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-IV-114)</sup>

#### Rites of Passage
When children from the [Spiritblood Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spiritblood_Clan) grow strong enough, they perform the [Vateshran's Rites](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vateshran%27s_Rites) to ascend to adulthood. The rites separate the strong from those too weak to live in the Reach.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ESORM-115)</sup> If they survive the challenge, the child emerges a full warrior, and carries all the privileges and responsibilities that adulthood in the clan offers.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-ESOAngair-116)</sup> They train their entire lives for that day, and begin their trial knowing that the Rites could kill them.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-ESOCaddgarn-117)</sup> To complete the Rites, a child must traverse Vateshran Hollows, a cavern filled with summoned Daedra.

### Reach-magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-place-Vernim_Woods.jpg/200px-ON-place-Vernim_Woods.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vernim_Woods.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vernim_Woods.jpg) Bloodthorn/Bloodroot Vine magic Reachmen are often referred to as the Witchmen of High Rock, a name given to them due to their use of dark "Reach-magic". It is believed that they learned much of this magic through trading with the [Orsimer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orsimer), their neighbors who also occupy the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-2)</sup> Another source for their magic comes from [hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md), which involves "ecstatic ceremonial heres[ies]", such as sacrifices, to acquire dark nature magic.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-31)</sup> Hircine is believed to be the source of where Reach-magic is drawn from.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-118)</sup> The dark magic has been outlawed within the Mages Guild, though that has not stopped the art from being widely studied.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-2)</sup> The ban is for good reason, however, as one of the most notable conjurations can be devastating. Indeed, by using Reach-magic on the remains of ritual sacrifices,<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-COTRAG-119)</sup> one can create the bloodthirtsy,<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-ONBloodroot Spriggan-120)</sup> thorned, and giant twisting roots known as Bloodthorn/Bloodroot vines,<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-ESOBVS-121)</sup><sup>[[120]](#cite_note-ESOVBG-122)</sup> which can be used to corrupt the land,<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ONZK-123)</sup> or shaped into "rough mockeries of life".<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-COTRAG-119)</sup> An example can be found within the Huntsman's Bloodroot Forge. The Dreadhorn Clan's hagraven shaped the vines that thrived within the Forge into Nirnblooded creatures,<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-ONCaillaoife-124)</sup> primal creatures comparable to those of the Bosmer's Wild Hunt, but infused with the power of Nirncrux.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-ESOHOTRBR-125)</sup> Transformed, these vines still hunger for blood.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-ONBRWHS-126)</sup>

Molag Bal can grant a similar power to gravesingers. The name "gravesinger" is given to some Reachman necromancers who broker deals with Bal<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ONZK-123)</sup> in exchange for the power to command the loathsome dead.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-31)</sup> Indeed, gravesingers can command corpses by planting the seeds of foul vines in their victims' bodies. Molag Bal's "gifts" may leave a gravesinger's body in a state of decay.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-ESOAngof-127)</sup>

[Mysticism](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism) was common magical art among the mystic shamans during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-ONRee-128)</sup> Reachfolk consider themselves to be among the finest [healers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restoration) in the world.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-AG-129)</sup>

### Transformations
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/ON-npc-Cinder-Heart_Infernal.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Cinder-Heart_Infernal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cinder-Heart_Infernal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cinder-Heart_Infernal.jpg) A Reachman with a Briarheart [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg) Gherig Bullblood, a Blood-Forged Many of the transformations Reachmen undergo are associated with Hircine:

The [briarheart ritual](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briarheart) is reflective of the Reachfolk's need to become better to overcome hardships. It involves communing with Hircine to ritualistically replace the heart of a skilled Reachfolk warrior with [poisoned briars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briar_Heart).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-31)</sup> This ritual is representative of Lorkh's immortal sacrifice,<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-GSR5-130)</sup> which is reflected by the sacrifice of ones' own life; the end result is a warrior being resurrected<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-ONFaorin-131)</sup> as a living weapon with great strength and endurance known as a Briarheart.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONMD-37)</sup> They will bear the suffering of constant pain<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-ONK-132)</sup> to use their transformation's benefit to protect their clan.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONMD-37)</sup> In a similar vein to Briarhearts, lycanthropy is viewed by most Reachfolk as self-sacrifice, for it is seen as more of a useful condition rather than a gift, one which one suffers with so they can better benefit their clan.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-GSR2-133)</sup> Some Reach Clans however have proven to be zealous about the gift, as conflicts between them and the Viridian Wyrd coven have been reported due to them offering a cure to Lycanthropy.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TGW-33)</sup><sup>[[132]](#cite_note-ONHeart-134)</sup>

Another transformation is associated with one of Hircine's creation, the [Bloodroot Forge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodroot_Forge), which is capable of turning people into [Blood-Forged](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blood-Forged). They are comparable to Briarhearts, but their creation instead involves a nirncrux heartstone being used in place of a briar heart,<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-ONF-135)</sup> resulting in a being that is an amalgamation of blood, nirncrux, and iron.<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-ONBlood Forged-136)</sup>

There have also been instances among the Winterborn of their warriors transforming themselves into briarheart tree [lurchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lurcher) through incredibly powerful Hagraven rituals of "fire, vines, and blood".<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-Corintthac-137)</sup><sup>[[136]](#cite_note-Ushang-138)</sup>

### Food
*Main Article: [Reachman Cuisine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Cuisine)* Alcohol is a commodity that the more open-minded clans of the Reach are willing to trade for. Much of the Reach is unsuitable for the barley fields, vineyards, apiaries with which other cultures brew their drinks. Clans who don't have much contact with the outside world make do with what they have. Reach-made drinks are found primarily in the form of hard ciders. The flavor profile of Reach cider is varied. Dark, musty brews tend to be sweet. There are also clear, sour brews that have been described by visiting Nords as an acquired taste. The flavor depends on the variety of apple used and the time of year they're pressed. Apple orchards are uncommon in the Reach, but many apples grow in the region's forests and river valleys. Each clan that lives where the fruit grows has their own preferred method for brewing cider.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-DOTR-139)</sup> Reachfolk cider is praised for its quality by some while others consider certain varieties to be a sour acquired taste.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-ESORichien_Thelin-93)</sup><sup>[[137]](#cite_note-DOTR-139)</sup>

Leather and wool are traditionally used as materials for Reachfolk garments.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOAyddelsa-29)</sup> Most of a Reachfolk clothing is purely functional, although the bright colors are favored.<sup>[[139]](#cite_note-CM12Barbaric-142)</sup> Imagery of knotwork resonates with Reachfolk.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-TRFL-143)</sup>

Reachmen prefer open-fingered hand wrappings to full gloves. During seasons of cold weather, they often wear mittens made of hide or fleece. Occasionally studs made of carved bone are added to the gloves.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-CM94Ancestral_Reach-144)</sup> Reachfolk of Icereach prefer full gloves and occasionally adorn their fingertips with sharp metal akin to claws of Hagravens.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-CM82Icereach_Coven-146)</sup>

Reachfolk headgear is very diverse. While most Reachfolk helmets are made from leather, they are also occasionally utilizing skulls of various animals. ## Religion
Every clan of the Reach has slightly varied approach to the religion. They worship both ancestors, various [lesser spirits](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) that are unique to each clan as well as more powerful entities called Great Spirits.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-GSR2-133)</sup> Certain [foxes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fox) and [elks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elk) held sacred status, and their leathers were reserved for special occasions.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-LOTQF-168)</sup><sup>[[166]](#cite_note-RWJ-169)</sup> Some clans revered the [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) associated with animals or the nearby springs.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-GSR2-133)</sup> It was customary to thank to nature spirits before felling trees. Subsequently, the Reachfolk often would plant new saplings in the vacant space left by the fallen tree.<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-Malug-170)</sup> The Reachfolk do not worship the Daedra in the sense that people in other parts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) worship the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md); rather, the beings they call spirits play an important role in their daily lives. They have what can be described as a give and take relationship with the Daedra, forming agreements for assistance with everyday tasks and challenges.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ND-109)</sup> Religion of certain clans of the Reach does not involve Daedra at all. Only a part of Markarth's city clans engaged in Daedra worship,<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-ONVilia-171)</sup><sup>[[169]](#cite_note-Uxitldish-172)</sup> and within this subset, certain Daedra, such as Malacath were even less popular.<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-ONRulfalash-173)</sup> Certain aspects of the Reach theology share similarities with the religious practices of the [Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids).<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-ONNighthunter Cowl-174)</sup><sup>[[172]](#cite_note-ONKEM-175)</sup> As of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), they referred to the spirits as the Old Gods.<sup>[[173]](#cite_note-Cedran-176)</sup>

**[Namira](Lore_Namira.md)** (*The Spirit Queen / the Goddess of Death*) Namira is the spirit of darkness and endings, but also a spirit of rebirth. She is considered to be a powerful force in the natural world.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ND-109)</sup>

**[Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath)** (*The Lord of Ash and Bone*) Malacath's true chosen people is the Reachfolk, according to some Reachmen tribes, and he created [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), [Ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre), and [Trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll) to test them.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOMR-8)</sup> He is not a popular spirit among the Reachfolk of Markarth.<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-ONRulfalash-173)</sup>

**[Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md)** (*Master of Tasks / Lord of Order*) is a revered spirit in the Reachmen pantheon that shares many characteristics with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), in pivotal ways such as being attributed to "time, rigid natural order, (and) draconic imagery". This has lead to scholars concluding that there was a cultural diffusion between the early ancestors of [Men](Lore_Men.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) in the Northwestern parts of Tamriel. To describe Peryite as a necessary evil would be a misnomer, for while the Reachfolk see him as necessary, they see the good in him maintaining a balance as a force of nature. While a Reachmen may be claimed by a blight or plague, they will come out of these natural tragedies healthier. This keeps the dangers of overpopulation in check, and allows a new generation of Reachfolk to build immunities to what Peryite throws at them.<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-GSR4-181)</sup>

### Origin
The Reachfolk believe in the existence of only two worlds; the worlds of flesh and spirit.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-GSR3-177)</sup> However, the world of flesh didn't always exist. Legends speak of Lorkh having an epiphany when he visited the darkness; that what is perceived as nothingness is ripe for possibility, for he saw the potential for there being more than just the realm of spirit.<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-208)</sup> And so, Lorkh approached Namira, the queen of the spirit world,<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-GSR3-177)</sup> seeking a place within her infinite void that he could create a realm for wayward spirits. But it was not without a cost. Lorkh sacrificed himself to create a harsh realm, one that is unforgiving and intended to teach through suffering.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-GSR5-130)</sup> As a result, the Reachfolk look to less conventional gods for worship, who serve as lessons rather than beliefs.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-GSR3-177)</sup>

What ensured is shrouded in the allegory "And unto the mountains they fled, for the world grew dark with shadows which sprung from the heart of Lorkh, who though greatly sundered still believed in the light of Man".<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-NZLS-39)</sup> After the fall of Lorkh, Hircine became the Lord of the Arena,<sup>[[204]](#cite_note-ESOLAG-209)</sup> and took the mantle of Lorkh's creation, becoming the sovereign of the realm of flesh.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-GSR3-177)</sup> He became the most prominent deity to the Reachfolk, as he took an active leadership role, and shaped them to become survivors in the harshness of Nirn.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-GSR2-133)</sup>

### Celebrations
Reachmen celebrate the [end of the old year](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Life_Festival) and the [beginning of the new year](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival).<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-NLD-210)</sup> They believe that with the coming of the New Life Festival, two of Hircine's aspects come to conflict with each other. New Life marks the [winter solstice](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md#Winter_Solstice), when the days begin to lengthen due to the return of [the sun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus). Storihbeg the Man-Beast thus pursues the sun in an attempt to eat it, an effort to keep the nights longer. To prevent this, Reachmen summon [Hrokkibeg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrokkibeg) the Mighty Bear to keep Storihbeg constrained and protect Magnus.<sup>[[193]](#cite_note-NLFI-198)</sup>

Namira's Dance is a ceremony performed by Reachfolk of the [Boldclaw Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boldclaw_Clan). All clansmen unite together to pay respects to Namira, goddess of death and rebirth. Some are dressed in dark colors, with black facial paint, others arrive naked, to symbolize the beginning of life. Those who are able dance together in unison. During the celebrations, human blood is spilled for Namira, yet no violence is involved. Members of the clan do various things with the collected blood. Some sweep the blood under their eyes, others press bloody handprints to the ground. Despite the presence of blood, these celebrations are joyous in nature.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ND-109)</sup>

### Death Beliefs
*Main Article: [Reachman Death Beliefs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Death_Beliefs)* The Reachfolk believe the world is divided in two realms: the world of flesh is the mortal plane, which they believe is governed by Hircine. The world of spirit governed by Namira. Hircine and Namira's roles complement each other. While Reachfolk dwell in the world of the living, Hircine is a guide that helps them navigate Nirn's hardships.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONArdanir-24)</sup> Upon death, Namira acts as a psychopomp for Reachman souls that gives and takes lives, until their spirits are enlightened. Thus, Namira is seen as the avatar of primal dualisms, such as beginnings and ends, which stem from her world of spirit.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-GSR3-177)</sup> Their afterlife is described as a tranquil glade, free from cold mists, where loved ones walk side by side, reunited,<sup>[[206]](#cite_note-TTWOT2-211)</sup> and where the deceased share stories of battles they fought.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONArdanir-24)</sup>

### Sky Tales
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-item-Reach_Glyph_Snake.jpg/200px-ON-item-Reach_Glyph_Snake.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Reach_Glyph_Snake.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Reach_Glyph_Snake.jpg) Snake Glyph [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-crown_store-Wayward_Guardian_Style.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Wayward_Guardian_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Wayward_Guardian_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Wayward_Guardian_Style.jpg) Wayward Guardians invoked the Witch, Hunter, and Headsman names for the protection [Sky Tales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sky_Tales) are Reachfolk term for the [constellations](Lore_Constellations.md).<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup> Some Reachfolk Witches can read the stars and see the future and hidden truths in their light.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup>

Three **Guardian Constellations** are known as **Reach Guardians**<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup> and **Guardians in the Sky**.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-CM97Wayward_Guardian-25)</sup> [The Witch in the Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Witch_in_the_Stars), [the Hunter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Hunter), and [the Headsman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Headsman) work together to protect the Lesser Stars from the malevolent Sky Tale known as [the Snake in the Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Snake_in_the_Stars). Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the [Wayward Guardians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wayward_Guardians) led by [Arana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arana) recognized the power of the Reach Guardians and invoked their names for the protection against the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-CM97Wayward_Guardian-25)</sup>

**[The Witch in the Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Witch_in_the_Stars)** The Mage Constellation is known as **the Witch in the Stars**, which is often shortened to **the Witch**. She is one of the three Reach Guardians and shines her light on covens that protect the Reach from outsiders and others who mean to do Reachfolk harm. Her good eye sees through everything, be it rock, water or flesh to find the hard truths. Whenever she speaks, she never lies.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup>

**[The Hunter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Hunter)** The Thief Constellation is known as **the Hunter**. She is one of the three Reach Guardians and is believed to be the wiliest of the sky tales. She is as clever as an old fox and as swift as a young one. One of her duties is to observe the Snake in the Stars movements. She always finds it no matter where it slithers. Teachings of the Hunter are known to the Reachfolk. She taught them how to keep their feet quiet and their kills as silent as the night.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup>

**[The Headsman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Headsman)** The Warrior Constellation is known as **the Headsman**. He is one of the three Reach Guardians and is typically depicted carrying the axe. The Headsman stands for swift ends and payment in kind. He delivers justice and punishment in equal measure.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup>

**[The Snake in the Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Snake_in_the_Stars)** The Serpent Constellation is known as the **Snake in the Stars**, which is often shortened to **the Snake**. It is also occasionally referred to as **the Enemy** and **the Corrupter**. The Serpent is the enemy of the Reach Guardians. Reachfolk believe that if permitted, Snake would consume all the Lesser Stars without hesitation.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup>

In the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Arana and the Witch-Rebels agreed to form a proper alliance with [House Ravenwatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Ravenwatch) and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), but only once the ritual honoring [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) was performed to finalize the binding pact between parties. The ritual was successful and revealed the sigil of the Snake in the Stars. Initially, Arana interpreted it as the substitute for their prey but soon realized that the clue sent by the Daedric Prince referred to the Sky Tales.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup> The Dwemer plaques led to Bthar-Zel, where they hoped to find materials to arm themselves against the invasion of the Gray Host.<sup>[[207]](#cite_note-CVR-212)</sup> One of the Reachfolk [glyphs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reach-tongue#Glyphs_with_unknown_meaning) depict the snake.<sup>[[208]](#cite_note-Markarth-213)</sup> Its connection to the Snake in the Stars is unknown.

**Lesser Stars** Other Constellations, widely known as the **charges** are known as the **Lesser Stars**. Reach Guardians protect them from being devoured by the Snake in the Stars.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ARANA-67)</sup>
