# Flask of Lillandril

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png/200px-RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) The Flask of Lillandril The Flask of Lillandril is a magical flask that absorbs and reflects magic of all kinds. Its [anti-magic](Lore_Anti-Magicka.md) property also prevents mages from detecting its location.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGJffer-1)</sup> The flask was discovered around the year [1E 470](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_470) by a group of elves living in the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) city of [Lillandril](Lore_Lillandril.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EA3-2)</sup> The flask itself was not of Elvish make, but its creator is unknown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGJffer-1)</sup> The elves spread news of their discovery, enough to make it an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of song and legend.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGGerrick-3)</sup> It became the city's symbol and was displayed on their banners.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

The elves used the flask to perform favors for the barons of Summerset Isle by keeping in check the magical power of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EA3-2)</sup> One legend says that a stable boy defeated the [High Wizard of Valenwood](Lore_High_Wizard_of_Valenwood.md) using only the flask.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGJffer-1)</sup> After some time, the elves who discovered the flask feared that Bosmer assassins would kill them, so they fled for [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), hoping to buy safety from the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). The elves shipwrecked off the southern coast of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), killing all of them according to legend, and the Flask of Lillandril was lost.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EA3-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGGerrick-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RGFalicia-5)</sup>

The general location of the flask, however, was not lost. Sometime after the shipwreck, a map was created showing its location relative to a wayshrine to [Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md) in the town of [Saintsport](Lore_Saintsport.md). The map was torn into four pieces; one of them was later inscribed on a ship model made of silver and another on the back of a [Restless League](Lore_Restless_League.md) insignia.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RGFalicia-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Some think there was a survivor of the shipwreck who hid the flask on land and made the map.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGGerrick-3)</sup> Additionally, ancient fragments of a manuscript called the Lagan Text contain references for a second map that clarifies the orientation of the first map.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EA3-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

Into the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), treasure hunters from all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) searched for the flask to no avail.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGMaiko-8)</sup> During its occupation of Stros M'Kai, the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) also searched to some degree, possibly to dispel the magical defenses of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and start an invasion.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-RGDreekius-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ALegendary Flask-10)</sup>

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) learned about the flask and was able to piece together the map showing its location. He discovered it contained within a chest buried in the sand not far west of Saintsport.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ALegendary Flask-10)</sup> With this flask, he was able to defeat the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) necromancer [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md) by reflecting the spells cast at him. He gave it to [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) in exchange for his [sister](Lore_Iszara.md)'s soul, but Vile took it as a present instead and required that he solve a riddle to win back the soul.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Rescue Iszara-11)</sup>
