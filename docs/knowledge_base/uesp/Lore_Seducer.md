# Mazken


## Hierarchy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/LG-cardart-Dark_Seducer.png/200px-LG-cardart-Dark_Seducer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dark_Seducer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dark_Seducer.png) Dark Seducer (Legends) The Mazken are the spawn of [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), and their home is [Pinnacle Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pinnacle_Rock) in [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Pinnacle Rock contains the Wellspring of the Mazken, which serves as a beacon to guide their reformation from the Waters of Oblivion. Details surrounding their [chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) is a mystery, but their Wellspring flows with a green liquid fitting of them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SIHelpless-6)</sup> Many of the Mazken are notoriously clanless and treacherous, owing allegiance to many [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), such as [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Battlespire-3)</sup> [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Wt HW-7)</sup> Male Mazken are notably weaker than their female counterparts and because of this, Mazken society views all males as inferior. Unlike their counterparts, the [Golden Saints](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aureal), Dark Seducers are generally polite and patient when interacting with mortals.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-S.26S-8)</sup>

The Mazken make up one half of the Mad God's army, along with the Aureal. The two groups have a great rivalry, each believing they are Sheogorath's favored. The Mazken are charged with guarding [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md), apparently due to a masochistic streak.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SISheogorath-9)</sup> In times of peace, they act as guards, patrolling the realm for criminals and hostile creatures. In times of war, they favor blunt weapons and are placed under the command of the Duke of Dementia. The Mazken have a strict hierarchy, ascending in rank as follows: Kiskengo; Kiskella; Kiskedrig; Grakendo; Grakella; Grakedrig; Autkendo.

## Equipment
The Mazken are linked to the [Madness Ore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Madness_Ore) that occurs naturally in the Shivering Isles. Experimenting with Madness Ore may summon or even bind a Mazken.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RAFECC-10)</sup>

In combat, the Mazken wear strong but extremely revealing armor that exposes most of their skin to the elements, although this is compensated by their fast-running speed and strong resistance to magical attacks. Mortals have struggled to replicate [Daedric equipment](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment), and the Dremora [Lyranth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyranth) believes their efforts remain pale imitations of true Daedric craftsmanship, limited by the inferior materials found on Nirn.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-11)</sup> A mortal managed to accomplish crafting their serpentine arms and armaments of the Mazken, but it is a dangerous endeavor, as one can lose their mind making it. The process requires finesse as one must balance shaping [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) with malleable quicksilver. Heat must be applied precisely, or the shape and integrity of the equipment will be ruined. Finally, a [Daedra's Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy_D#Daedra_Heart) is put into the forge when the equipment is being heated, adding to its hardness.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRCC-12)</sup> The intricate artifact [Ruin's Edge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruin%27s_Edge) is a dark Mazken bow that belonged to [Syl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syl), the Duchess of Dementia.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SISyl-13)</sup> The Mazken longsword Serpentstrike is known for being highly venomous, leading to speculation that it was tempered with the blood of [Basilisks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Basilisk).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BLST-14)</sup>

Sometimes they do not use their serpentine equipment, and have been known to wear revealing black outfits and use their alluring form to trick mortal opponents into mistaking them for harmless human women before striking down their unsuspecting adversaries with ease. However, they have sometimes exhibited a willingness to behave passively around mortals that understand the Daedric language to a certain degree.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DFSkills-15)</sup>

## History

### Second Era

#### Interregnum
Many Mazken were in the service of Molag Bal during the events of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). Among the most infamous were dubbed the Seductive Sisters, which consisted of the seducers known as [Dylora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dylora), Vika, and Jansa. They were considered the most deadly Mazken in Molag Bal's service,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Anchor-16)</sup> and served as generals for his invading forces.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Anchor G-17)</sup> They were tasked with protecting Dark Anchors, but were slain by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) that sought to destroy them.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Anchor-16)</sup>

Many who sought refuge from the horrors of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) were lured into the Manor of Revelry, a mansion cloaked in the illusions that mimic the familiarity of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). There, they were greeted by the Mazken sisters, Nuzara, Rayyaima, and Shayaifa, who lured their guests by feeding them and providing a luxurious experience. In reality, the guests were being pampered so that they were fattened and lowered their guards so that they could be consumed. All that was left was their skeletal remains, which were then reanimated and cloaked within the illusion to appear as living guests. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Tamrielic Invasion of Coldharbour was launched, and members of the expedition were scattered throughout Molag Bal's realm, and some of the members wandered into the comforts of the Manor. A Mazken agent of Meridia, Fatahala, knew well of how her kind operated, and sought the help of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to dismantle the illusion of the Manor and save its still living inhabitants.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOTHB-18)</sup>

### Third Era

#### The Invasion of the Battlespire
During the Daedric invasion of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) recruited many Mazken to his ranks. They turned on their previous mistress, Nocturnal Lieutenant [Jaciel Morgen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jaciel_Morgen), and captured one of their (former) patron [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)'s realms, [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), for Dagon. Those who willingly shifted their allegiance were rewarded by being remade in the image of Dagon's own greed and treachery—becoming not only physically stronger, but also gifted with powerful bat-winged forms.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BSDeyanira-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BSDark Seducer-20)</sup>

The Mazken attacked the Battlespire, each wielding a [Daedric Crescent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Crescent), before retiring back to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and leaving the [scamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp) and [vermai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vermai) to clean up. Other Daedric clans, notably [Clan Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora_Clan) and their master [Imago Storm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imago_Storm), were displeased with Dagon's acceptance of the Mazken. Dagon himself held no such prejudices, and assigned some of them into important positions. Dark Seducer Si'ess was given the role of Master of the Hunt, and oversaw the [Chimera of Desolation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chimera_of_Desolation)'s [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md). Si'ess was also his bodyguard and lover, and she was gifted with the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver). She was slain by an [apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md), who then used the sword to banish Dagon, destroying the Battlespire in the process.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Battlespire-3)</sup>

#### The Warp in the West
Following the destruction of the Battlespire, winged Mazken were regularly sighted in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) area in the years leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-2)</sup>

The Daedric Prince [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) was betrayed by a Mazken whom he had gifted with power and beauty. The Prince had a mysterious [agent](Lore_Hero.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) track her down to a dungeon in the Iliac Bay area and slay her in return for [Volendrung](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volendrung).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DFMalacath-21)</sup> The agent was also tricked by an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) warlord into taking a Daedric lodestone from a Mazken, with the promise of the artifact [Chrysamere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chrysamere), which the warlord did not possess. The Mazken revealed the Orc's plot to the agent, providing a magical pearl to break the illusion, but the ultimate result of this conflict is unknown.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-DFImpostor-22)</sup>

Also of note, Princess [Elysana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elysana) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) contracted the agent to return a robe she borrowed to Lord [Castellian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castellian), the senior member of Wayrest's elder council, while he was on holiday. Castellian had the ear of Elysana's father, [King Eadwyre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eadwyre), and made no secret of his desire to wed the princess. Perhaps due in part to these facts, Elysana had the robe enchanted so that when worn, it would summon a small horde of Mazken to attack and kill the wearer. It is unknown if Lord Castellian ultimately received it or if the agent decided to try it on; regardless, the Daedra were sent back to Oblivion, taking the cursed garment with them.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-DFElysana-23)</sup>

#### The Greymarch
By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Dark Seducer Sisters, Dylora, Vika, and Jansa, had changed their allegiance from Molag Bal into serving Sheogorath. Jansa became the Captain of the Palace Guard of [New Sheoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Sheoth), and Vika became interested in the conflict of [Cylarne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cylarne). That year, Dylora was serving as the commander of Mazken's home of [Pinnacle Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pinnacle_Rock) in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) when the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) began. The forces of Order invaded Pinnacle Rock to cripple the realm's army, and Dylora was taken prisoner.<sup>[[nb1]](#intnote_A)</sup> She was freed by Sheogorath's [Champion](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) and aided in the liberation of the stronghold. Although she perished with the other Mazken when the Wellspring of the Mazken was frozen by Order, they were revived when the Champion destroyed the crystals stopping the flow of [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md). Dylora would then lead the Mazken to remove any remnants of Order from their home, allowing them to resume their normal duties.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-THASI-24)</sup>

## Notes
- [<sup>nb1</sup>](#note_A) This event only occurs if the player chooses to become the Duke/Duchess of Mania during the [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles) main quest. This will lead Duchess [Syl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syl) to betray Sheogorath for the death of [Thadon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thadon). This betrayal led the Mazken to be caught off-guard, allowing for the invasion of Pinnacle Rock. If the player instead chooses to become the Duke/Duchess of Dementia , Thadon will survive and attack the [Aureal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aureal)'s home of [Brellach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brellach) in retaliation for Syl's death.
- The term "Mazken" appeared for the first time in the [*Shivering Isles*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles) expansion for [*Oblivion*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion); the Seducer enemies also appeared under slightly differing names in [*Daggerfall*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall) and [*Battlespire*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire).
- The wings attached to Dark Seducers, or "Daedra Seducers" as they are called in *Daggerfall*, appear to gradually vanish when folded, only to reappear when fully expanded. This seems to imply that the wings are either conjured by a spell, or simply become invisible while unused. The former is consistent with most of their later appearances (barring *Battlespire*), where Dark Seducers completely lack any signs of wings.
- The box art for *[Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire)* depicts a winged Dark Seducer wielding a [Daedric Crescent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Crescent).
