# Dorach Gusal

| Dorach Gusal | | | |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Race | Unknown | Gender | Male |
| Resided in | [Anudnabia](Lore_Anudnabia.md) | | |

Dorach Gusal was a master [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md), known for his skill in creating powerful and enchanted [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md). Along with the legendary [weaponsmith](Lore_Smithing.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) [Hilbongard Rolamus](Lore_Hilbongard_Rolamus.md), he crafted the amazing warhammer known as the [Skull Crusher](Lore_Skull_Crusher.md). This weapon was created in the forge of [Anudnabia](Lore_Anudnabia.md) during the Skyrim Captivity,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-1)</sup> in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HW-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/MW-quest-Skull-Crusher.jpg/200px-MW-quest-Skull-Crusher.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Skull-Crusher.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Skull-Crusher.jpg) The Forge of Hilbongard [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-place-Anudnabia.jpg/200px-ON-place-Anudnabia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anudnabia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anudnabia.jpg) Anudnabia Little is known of the past of Dorach Gusal. At some point, Dorach Gusal and Hilbongard joined forces and set up their forge within Anudnabia and worked together to create the Skull Crusher. Dorach Gusal magically fueled the fires in which the Skull Crusher was forged, while also magically hardening the steel used to create it. This made the warhammer incredibly light yet deadly in its blows, with some accounts stating that it was even more powerful than the weapons of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). In addition to crafting weapons, he [conjured](Lore_Conjuration.md) daedra to both guard their vaults and assist him in various enchantment rituals.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWDunmer Scout-3)</sup>

Despite its intended use as an attraction for a local festival, the Skull Crusher was stolen before it could be displayed. As a result, Dorach and Hilbongard placed a curse on the warhammer, causing it to betray any unlawful possessors and search Tamriel for its creators.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TL-4)</sup>

Hilbongard and Dorach lived in Anudnabia well after the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) were pushed out of Resdayn and became well-known throughout the region. It is unknown when Dorach Gusal passed away, but he was present at the wake of [Mordrin Hanin](Lore_Mordrin_Hanin.md) several years after the creation of the Skull Crusher, during the reign of High King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md). At this event, many attendees died from Hanin's deadly drink, although it is unclear if Dorach was among them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HW-2)</sup>

## Legacy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/MW-interior-Anudnabia.jpg/200px-MW-interior-Anudnabia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-interior-Anudnabia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-interior-Anudnabia.jpg) One of the Daedra summoned by Dorach Gusal [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/MW-item-Skull_Crusher.jpg/200px-MW-item-Skull_Crusher.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Skull_Crusher.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Skull_Crusher.jpg) Skull Crusher Regardless of his fate, his legacy lived on through the legendary weapon he created. Eventually, the Skull Crusher found its way back to the forge of Anudnabia. In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), an [acolyte](Lore_Nerevarine.md) of the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) was sent to retrieve the warhammer. Since the lower levels of Anudnabia had been closed off for some time, the acolyte had to traverse through the Omaren Ancestral Tomb to enter the forgotten vaults of Anudnabia, protected by the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who served Dorach Gusal in his enchantment rituals. They eventually found the Skull Crusher in a chest above Hilbongard's forge and returned it to the representatives of the Imperial Cult in [Castle Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWSkull-5)</sup>

Later that year, the Skull Crusher was sold to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md) and put on display in her Museum of Artifacts in Mournhold.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRMuseum-6)</sup>

Centuries later, [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) worshippers on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) built a hidden temple called Ashfall's Tear north of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) and stored various relics there, including Skull Crusher. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) discovered the temple in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) and retrieved the warhammer and other artifacts.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Got T-9)</sup> These were used to defeat remnants of the Sixth House led by the Dunmer Erden Relvel, who had been corrupted by Dagoth Ur's mask.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
