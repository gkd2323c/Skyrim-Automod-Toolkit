# Lifespan

This article documents the lifespans of the various [races](Lore_Races.md) of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), as well as the various methods of life-extension and resurrection.

## Racial Lifespans
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png/150px-LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png) Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) died of natural causes at the age of 108. [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/LG-cardart-Divayth_Fyr_02.jpg/150px-LG-cardart-Divayth_Fyr_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Divayth_Fyr_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Divayth_Fyr_02.jpg) Divayth Fyr is a 4,000-year-old sorcerer.
### Beastfolk
[Beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) tend to live a maximum of one hundred years.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AUA4-1)</sup> [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) do not conform to this standard, as no natural limit to their lifespan has ever been observed or recorded. The eldest among them must remain buoyed by water, lest they collapse under their own immense weight.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1TWR-2)</sup> [Centaurs](Lore_Centaur.md) have been known to call other races 'mortals', implying they have an extended lifespan.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Centaur-3)</sup> The lifespan of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) varies by furstock, with the [Senche-raht](Lore_Khajiit.md#Senche-raht) being the longest-lived.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AOPF-4)</sup>

### Men
[Men](Lore_Men.md) tend to live a maximum of one hundred years.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AUA4-1)</sup> Half-Elven men, despite their parentage, age as any other men do.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHE2-5)</sup> An exceptional case of unnatural human longevity has been recorded with [Sirollus Saccus](Lore_Sirollus_Saccus.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAC-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Sirollus-7)</sup> though the underlying cause remains uncertain, with magical influence being the most likely explanation.

According to [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) myth, [Orkey](Lore_Orkey.md) is responsible for the shortened lifespan of men, as they once had the same long-lives as elves. However, Orkey's machinations led to him instructing [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) to eat the Nords' lifespans down to just six years. This was undone by [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) and [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), but in Wulfharth's haste to save everyone, he accidentally aged himself to death. Most of this curse was then supposedly thrown on to the Orcs.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FSo KW-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Vo FTN-9)</sup> Prior to their migration to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), some [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md) have been documented living long lives, such as commoner-turned-Elden Yokeda [Randic Torn](Lore_Randic_Torn.md), who reigned the [Yokudan Empire](Lore_Yokudan_Empire.md) for 120 years.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RTHATH-10)</sup>

### Mer
[Mer](Lore_Mer.md) tend to live for several centuries up to a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-IWADE-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRB2-13)</sup> In this regard, among the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), being well over a century old is considered young.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Leramil-14)</sup> It is said that [Phynaster](Lore_Phynaster.md), the hero-god of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), taught the Altmer how to extend their natural lifespans by an additional one hundred years through the practice of shortening their stride.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Vo FTHE-15)</sup> Some of the most ancient and powerful Elven mages are also known to have extended their lives. Notable examples include [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) and [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), as well as individuals who have endured for millennia through advanced magical practices, such as [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) and [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-FOA-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PMB-17)</sup>

The [Orsimer](Lore_Orsimer.md) are a notable exception among the mer, as they generally live significantly shorter lives.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Gurlak-18)</sup> According to Nordic tradition, this condition is the result of Orkey's defeat at the hands of Shor,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Vo FTN-9)</sup> though the Orsimer themselves believe it to be a punishment imposed by [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-UGK-19)</sup>

### Akaviri
Although literature often attributes immortality to the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MA-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Hot FG-21)</sup> descendants of Tsaesci refugees in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) claim that longevity is not a trait of the race and speculate that the lengthy longevity of figures such as the [Potentates](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) had magical origins.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Avita-22)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Avita Pitio-23)</sup> Survivors of the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) from the [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) race reportedly lived in hiding for over two hundred years.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TSAI-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-PGE1TEC-25)</sup>

## Methods of Life Extension

### Alchemy
[Gyron Vardengroet](Lore_Gyron_Vardengroet.md), in an effort to prevent others from experiencing the same grief he went through after his parents' deaths, set out to make a potion that achieved biological [immortality](Lore_Immortality.md). After poring over research from the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) and gathering [flora](Lore_Flora.md) from all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), he eventually succeeded in halting his aging with a potion he had brewed. However, he was unsure which of the many combinations he mixed resulted in the successful tonic.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TS-26)</sup> The [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) brewed potions which could temporarily transform the drinker into [Werewolf Behemoths](Lore_Lycanthropy.md#Werewolf_Behemoth),<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SG-27)</sup> which are noted immortals.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOUlfra-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup> [Brazzefk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brazzefk), a [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) alchemist, created and consumed a potion intended to make him into an immortal stone [golem](Lore_Golem.md), but it instead turned him into an immovable stone.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Ho B-30)</sup> The honey of the [Isgareth Bee](Lore_Bee.md) of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) allegedly halted aging upon consumption, but would wear off and need repeated consumption in order to maintain the effect.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-OI-31)</sup> The consumption of [Gildergreen](Lore_Gildergreen.md) fruits was thought to extend one's lifespan.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Ardwen-32)</sup>

### Apotheosis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/SI-npc-Haskill.jpg/200px-SI-npc-Haskill.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-npc-Haskill.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-npc-Haskill.jpg) Haskill, an immortal Vestige of a man who once mantled the Mad God Ascension to the status of [godhood](Lore_Gods.md) is one of the hardest and most mysterious methods of achieving immortality. The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) infamously ascended using [Kagrenac's Tools](Lore_Kagrenac%27s_Tools.md) on the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md), utilizing the Heart's innate divine energy. [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) formed a connection to the Heart without the tools through unknown means, likewise becoming a god.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Pt DDU-33)</sup> During the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the rogue [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostle.md) [Mecinar](Lore_Mecinar.md) used his reconstructed body to obtain divine power from the [Mechanical Heart](Lore_Mechanical_Heart.md) in order to become a god. The [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) might've also tapped into the Mechanical Heart via [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s replicas of Kagrenac's Tools, in order to briefly achieve a form of godhood and stop Mecinar.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LRCCMT-34)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-RCWC-35)</sup>

Several mortals underwent a process known as [mantling](Lore_Mantling.md)<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Jyg-36)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Haskill-37)</sup> during the [Greymarches](Lore_Greymarch.md) of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), becoming [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Jyg-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Sheo S-38)</sup> until the following Greymarch, when they were replaced by another and made into immortal [Vestiges](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md) of their mortal selves.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Haskill-37)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Altmer [Aranias](Lore_Aranias.md) took up the crown of the [Wilderking](Lore_Wilderking.md), taking his place and becoming the Wilderqueen.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Wilderking-39)</sup>

According to legend, [Sai](Lore_Sai.md) was made a god by [Reymon Ebonarm](Lore_Reymon_Ebonarm.md) to assist in balancing the forces of mortals with his innate lucky presence.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-KE9-40)</sup> Similar legends involve [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes.md) being raised to godhood by [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) being raised to godhood by [Mara](Lore_Mara.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TAX-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-42)</sup>

The [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) could be used to transcend [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and become a god.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Lot E-43)</sup>

The [Duraki](Lore_Duraki.md) spawning pools of Skyreach were theorized to harness the energy of [Nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) and use it to elevate mortal creatures and people to godly [celestial manifestations](Lore_Celestial.md) of the [constellations](Lore_Constellations.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Dot EV-44)</sup> [Jode's Core](Lore_Jode%27s_Core.md), if properly channeled through [aeonstone](Lore_Aeonstone.md), was thought to be capable of granting godhood.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Kaal-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-AT-46)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Badwell-47)</sup> [Tosh Raka](Lore_Tosh_Raka.md) of [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(place).md) was said to have become a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MA-20)</sup>

During the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417),<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TWit W-48)</sup> [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) used the power of the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md) to fuel his transfiguration and apotheosis, creating the [Necromancer's Moon](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Lot E-43)</sup> In an [alternate timeline](Lore_Many_Paths.md), that world's Mannimarco succeeded in his scheme of becoming a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), by using the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) to imprison [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) within the gem and take his place as a god, and came to rule over the merged worlds.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-49)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-50)</sup>

According to some sources, when [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) shattered the Amulet of Kings and transformed into the Avatar of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) at the end of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the event resulted in him [Mantling](Lore_Mantling.md) Akatosh, and thus achieving Apotheosis.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-51)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-GIKurt Kuhlmann-52)</sup>

During the [Umbriel Crisis](Lore_Umbriel_Crisis.md), [Hierem](Lore_Hierem.md) schemed to use the power of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) to siphon the thousands of souls within [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md)'s [Ingenium](Lore_Ingenium.md), an act which he believed would turn him into a god, but he was killed before he could make the attempt.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-LOS-53)</sup>

Some dubious sources claim that [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) achieved apotheosis by [absorbing](Lore_Dragonborn.md) the essences of several dragons.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-TBD-54)</sup>

### Body Alteration
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/MW-creature-Yagrum_Bagarn.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Yagrum_Bagarn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Yagrum_Bagarn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Yagrum_Bagarn.jpg) The last living Dwemer, Yagrum Bagarn, disfigured by Corprus [Diseases](Lore_Disease.md) of divine origin occasionally bestow immortality. [Vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) are immortal, courtesy of their [undead](Lore_Death.md) nature.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-DUG-55)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-56)</sup> The Divine Disease, [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md), renders those afflicted immune to disease and aging, alongside other debilitating side-effects.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Divayth M-57)</sup> The mechanical bodies of the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) grant unending life if their internal organs are replaced. Those who do have no need for eating, breathing, or sleeping.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Mt CPL-58)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Luciana-59)</sup> [Briarheart](Lore_Briarheart.md) implantations may be used to raise the dead.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TCa Fo B-60)</sup> [Heart Stones](Lore_Heart_Stone.md) function similarly to Briarhearts, but prevent the bearer from dying altogether, keeping them between life and death.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Neloth-61)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Ildari1-62)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Ildari2-63)</sup>

[Witches](Lore_Witch.md) who transform themselves into [hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md) seem to experience an extended lifespan,<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-64)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-65)</sup> as do [hags](Lore_Hag.md).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Rites-66)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-67)</sup> Some witches were known to extend their lifespans without the need for transformation.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Rites-66)</sup> At least some have achieved this through Daedric pacts,<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-TP-68)</sup> but it is not known whether this is the only method used.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Rites-66)</sup> [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) rituals could turn the caster into a being of water and coral,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Taleria-69)</sup> which could supposedly reform after death.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Tideborn-70)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ESOAG-71)</sup> [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) necromancers resurrected [Emperor Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) by transforming him into a [Flesh Atronach](Lore_Flesh_Atronach.md).<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Emp Leovic-72)</sup> [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who undergo the irreversible [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) live forever, at the cost of their sanity.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-PGE1V-73)</sup>

### Divine Intervention
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg/200px-ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg) Narilmor, one of Meridia's Purified Rarely, gods will bestow unending life if it is requested of them or to those they deem fit. In ancient times, [Wilderking](Lore_Wilderking.md) would open his realm, the [Shrouded Vale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shrouded_Vale), to Nirn every century to recruit a new caretaker for the [Fading Tree](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fading_Tree). The Wilderking would provide for the caretaker, granting them long life and sustenance.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Henodras-74)</sup> After connecting with the Heart of Lorkhan, Dagoth Ur shared his divine essence with his followers,<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Dagoth-75)</sup> granting them eternal life.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Vivec M-76)</sup> Gavos, a priest of [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), saw his life extended by a miracle from the god for his hard work and toil to keep the [Golden Anvil](Lore_Zenithar.md#Golden_Anvil) safe.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Daluion-77)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Gavos-78)</sup> During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), ancient scroll that magically appeared within the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) granted [eight priests](Lore_Immortal_Eight.md) of the [Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) immortality, returning to their mortal form after death.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Drake-79)</sup> It was thought that [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) was behind the scroll's magic.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Arrianus-80)</sup> It has been theorized that rarely, in times of crisis, the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) may revive individuals of importance to assist Mundus.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Jad-81)</sup> Through the [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md), [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md) may resurrect individuals to serve as [Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md).<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Indaenir-82)</sup>

[Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) are able to bestow longevity<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-TP-68)</sup> and immortality in a variety of ways. [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) folk tales of [Tancia](Lore_Tancia.md) tell of her entering into a bargain with a Prince, causing her to lose her sanity and become an immortal scourge.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-STot D3-83)</sup> [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) has granted immortality to those who set out to hunt his finest beasts, returning their aging after their beast has been felled.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Haras-84)</sup> The first turned, the original beings who were first afflicted with [Lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) by Hircine in the [Early Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Early_Merethic_Era),<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ACFL-85)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-SSAYQ-86)</sup> were immortal.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Vykosa-87)</sup> [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md)'s [Purified](Lore_Purified.md) were given immortality in exchange for subservience, willing or otherwise.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Tharayya-88)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Narilmor-89)</sup> [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) once entered into a pact which granted [Chimere Graegyn](Lore_Chimere_Graegyn.md) immortality, but kept his aging, eventually leaving him crippled with arthritis.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TMKR-90)</sup> Princes can bless a mortal with a Daedric form, seemingly fit with all of the form's abilities, but may take it away later.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Aelif-91)</sup> Some [Daedric Artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) grant eternal life, such as the [Ring of Hircine](Lore_Ring_of_Hircine.md).<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Tharsten-92)</sup>

[Cernunnon](Lore_Cernunnon.md) was known to grant immortality to their champions. As long as the spirit remained undefeated, their oath-bound enjoyed effective immortality, as it was Cernunnon who had the authority to determine their deaths.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-3Reachmen-93)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Cernunnon-94)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Oathbreaker-95)</sup> According to Nordic legend, [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) resurrected [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) from his ashes.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FSo KW-8)</sup> Wulfharth then became a being of ash, capable of sustaining a solid form with rest. When grievously struck, rather than dying, he was merely reduced to a formless ash state.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TAH-96)</sup> [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) was able to return a follower of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) to life when in close proximity of his shrine.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-House-97)</sup> The Daedric Prince [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) has been known to sometimes resurrect spirits whose regrets prevented them from entering the afterlife even though they'd been accepted.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-KJ-98)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-Zerith-99)</sup>

### Spellcraft
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/eb/ON-npc-Ritemaster_Iachesis.jpg/250px-ON-npc-Ritemaster_Iachesis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ritemaster_Iachesis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ritemaster_Iachesis.jpg) [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) was the leader of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), and led it for at least three and a half millennia. Spells applied directly to the body have been known to extend life.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-Shalidor ESO-100)</sup> The [Psijics](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) were renowned for practicing life extension magic.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-UGK-19)</sup> Talented mages were capable of significantly prolonging their lifespans.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-AT-46)</sup><sup>[[98]](#cite_note-SA-101)</sup> [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) lived for over four thousand years using magic,<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-Alfe-102)</sup> but did not consider himself an immortal.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-Fyr L-103)</sup> [Arch-Mage Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) is said to have lived for many lifetimes and to have known the secret of "life everlasting", a secret he could share with others to also extend their life, as he wished to do with his wife, [Ulfsild](Lore_Ulfsild.md), who ultimately rejected immortality.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-OS-104)</sup> [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) claimed to possess the ability to grant others everlasting life, using the prospect both as a potential reward for those who'd serve him and as a potential punishment for his foes, whose suffering he claimed would span millennia.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-JT-105)</sup> [Void magic](Lore_Void_Magic.md) can transform individuals into immortal [voidmothers](Lore_Voidmother.md) through soul collection and offerings to the [Void](Lore_Void.md).<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-SN-106)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Nathari-107)</sup> Powerful arcane experiments that go wrong can destroy the user's corporeal form, fusing them to the magical energies surrounding them. These individuals may manifest as a maelstrom of magicka.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-108)</sup><sup>:172</sup> [Grimkell](Lore_Grimkell.md), an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmoran.md) sorcerer of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), turned three of his former allies into stones in an ambush. As the Dragon magic faded, the [Draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) of the Priests' tombs would perform rituals on their sarcophagi to sustain their life.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Kireth-110)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-Dat DC-111)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-At D-112)</sup> To achieve a heightened state of lichdom, [Hevnoraak](Lore_Hevnoraak.md) planned to transfer his blood back into his body, reawakening him at the peak of his power.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-Valdar-113)</sup> It is said that siphoning a soul's essence can prolong one's life.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-SJ-114)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/SR-npc-Augur_of_Dunlain.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Augur_of_Dunlain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Augur_of_Dunlain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Augur_of_Dunlain.jpg) The maelstrom form of the Augur of Dunlain post-incident Resurrection has been accomplished with spells while maintaining the recipient's intelligence. Rare [scrolls](Lore_Scrolls.md) have been known to rejuvenate and resurrect fallen individuals moments after their death.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-Blades Revive-115)</sup> Although some have thought it impossible to completely resurrect a dead person whose soul belonged to a Daedric Prince,<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ADP-116)</sup> [Angof the Gravesinger](Lore_Angof.md) managed to revive [Faolchu](Lore_Faolchu.md), a Werewolf, centuries after his death.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Vo A-117)</sup> An obscure arcane discipline known as life magic was used by the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) in order to resurrect vampires and werewolves.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-Gwendis-118)</sup> The souls of the fallen Host members were drawn into [stone husks](Lore_Stone_Husk.md), inanimate vessels alchemically designed to house their life energy.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-Tzinghalis-119)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-Arkasis-120)</sup> In a final ritual, the stone husks shattered and the mortal forms of the long-dead members were restored.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-Tharda-121)</sup>

[Necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) may be used to turn one's self into a [Draugr](Lore_Draugr.md), halting their aging infinitely.<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-Aesliip-122)</sup> During the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md), [Corgrad](Lore_Corgrad_Wastes.md) was decimated by the disease. The lords of Corgrad performed sorcery to contain the disease which drained its ravaging effects into themselves. Those who were cured and their descendants gave their tainted blood to the lords, keeping them alive for millennia.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-Nemfarion-123)</sup><sup>[[121]](#cite_note-Arillas-124)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), in a bid to cure their decrepit condition once and for all, the lords kidnapped a fellow Altmer of untainted blood and prepared sorcery to turn him into one of their condition, making him immortal until later generations of pure blood could restore him.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-Nemfarion-123)</sup> Certain binding rituals prevent the aging of the prisoner, rendering them unaging and invulnerable.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Gurlak-18)</sup> Daedra may be bound in a [blood sacrifice](Lore_Blood_Magic.md), allowing the binder to live as long as they remain bound.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-OI-31)</sup> [Perena](Lore_Perena.md) [soul magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md) could grant immortality at the sacrifice of becoming an eternal watcher.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-125)</sup><sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Watchers-126)</sup> A similar ritual could be performed at the [Dragonfire Cathedral](Lore_Dragonfire_Cathedral.md), making the caster immortal at the cost of binding them to the Cathedral's premises.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Drake-79)</sup>

### Spirit Transference
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/OB-npc-Celedaen.jpg/200px-OB-npc-Celedaen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-npc-Celedaen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-npc-Celedaen.jpg) A necromancer preparing the lichdom process [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) Moon-Singer Talbira, a spirit who reincarnated herself into a living body Transferring the [soul](Lore_Soul.md) out of a mortal body and into an object or unaging living vessel prevents aging, but can cause drastic changes in behavior.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-Terari-127)</sup> Transference has occasionally been used as a curse, binding an unwilling subject to an unmoving object.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-Broken-128)</sup> The most advanced form of transference is [lichdom](Lore_Lich.md), a process in which necromancers place their soul within - and travel through - a physical object known as a phylactery.<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-APt L-129)</sup> If the advanced process is successful, the lich will survive in spirit form after being killed and can eventually reform its body.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-Mannimarco-130)</sup> After becoming a lich, the lich's physical body is left aged and withered. However, this can be circumvented with [Illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) magic.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-Vastarie-131)</sup>

Mortals who are soul-trapped before their death by worshippers of Molag Bal become [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md), reforming a replica of their body in Coldharbour. This replica is typically flawed due to the strong [Padomaic](Lore_Padomay.md) influences of Oblivion.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-CCATP-132)</sup> If they die, their bodies reform, eventually turning them into mindless, feral beasts.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-Cadwell-133)</sup> Those who lost their mind before becoming Soul Shriven are seemingly immune to the degrading effects.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-Lyris-134)</sup> Individuals with a unique [Anuic](Lore_Anu.md) aspect can reform their body perfectly, retaining their full abilities and intelligence.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-CCATP-132)</sup>

Unlike the Vestiges of Daedra, the souls of mortals are described as not fettered to existence, with the proper rites they can be "decoupled" from the body entirely and persist as immortal spirits that sustain themselves by consuming other souls. This unmooring process is not the same as the soul being separated from the body at death because then the resulting spirit is dead, a [Ghost](Lore_Ghost.md), whereas if separated prior to death the mortal becomes a living spirit.<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-Uldazaan-135)</sup> The mutability of a mortal soul is said to extend to its vessel, making it possible to change one's corporeal form through some basic magic, a change a number of mages have undergone in an effort to attain longer life or greater power, taking on forms such as that of a Voriplasm , a tree, a [Tomeshell](Lore_Tomeshell.md), a [Giant](Lore_Giant.md), a [Golem](Lore_Golem.md) or [Flesh Atronach](Lore_Flesh_Atronach.md), or even a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) skeleton.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-Rectavius-136)</sup><sup>[[134]](#cite_note-Vorm-137)</sup><sup>[[135]](#cite_note-Orryn-138)</sup><sup>[[136]](#cite_note-Thallik-139)</sup><sup>[[137]](#cite_note-Sapling-140)</sup><sup>[[138]](#cite_note-ESORD-141)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-AHing-142)</sup>

There have been cases where the spirit of a dead mortal has entered the body of a living being and integrated itself with the living soul residing there thus attaining a form of second life. The integration phenomenon results in the soul of the spirit merging with the soul of the living host without exerting control, thus the spirit is effectively reborn or [reincarnated](Lore_Reincarnation.md) into the living host, who will than start to experience the memories of that spirit as if they'd lived through them, and manifesting the knowledge and abilities the soul reincarnated within them possessed and feeling its emotions as their own. The phenomenon has also been described as a spirit being resurrected as the "twin soul" of its host. The initial entry of the soul into the living body is accompanied by a brief bout of intense pain, after the rebirth, other spirits who knew the reincarnated soul in life can sense its presence within its reincarnated form and realize the host soul and the reincarnated soul as now being the same. Though such spiritual rebirth is possible, it is said that it is relatively rare for spirits to behave in that manner.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-Zerith-99)</sup><sup>[[140]](#cite_note-Cantor-143)</sup><sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Notes-144)</sup><sup>[[142]](#cite_note-Journal-145)</sup>

### Time Dilation
When in [realms](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) outside of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), time passes differently than mortals are conditioned to. Planes, while affected by time, are not subject to it and its limitations<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Haskill-37)</sup> and may adopt degrees of it by the choice of its host.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-Nuit2-146)</sup> Mortals in realms with little-to-no passage of time experience a prolonged existence,<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-Do ME-147)</sup><sup>[[145]](#cite_note-CCTAP-148)</sup> perceiving time at a slower rate than those on Nirn.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-Faven-149)</sup><sup>[[145]](#cite_note-CCTAP-148)</sup> Jagar Tharn imprisoned [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) in a dimension of his own creation where time flowed at a different rate than on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), making so it would take centuries for the emperor to finally die.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-CHot E4-151)</sup> [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) is said to have stolen [Glamoril](Lore_Glamoril.md), the secret of life, from [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-AMM-152)</sup> The exact function of this artifact is unknown, but it was related to life extension.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-Shalidor ESO-100)</sup> One of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md)'s many theories as to its function was that it allowed the Arch-Mage to live multiple lifetimes in a short span of time.<sup>[[150]](#cite_note-Urag-153)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/CT-decoration-Green_Grimoire.png/175px-CT-decoration-Green_Grimoire.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Green_Grimoire.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Green_Grimoire.png) A Green Grimoire said to contain the secret to youthful energy (Castles) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg) The Dragon Luminary, theorized to be an Ascended Ancestor [Azra Nightwielder](Lore_Azra_Nightwielder.md) survived for centuries suspended within a crystal.<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-Azra-154)</sup> A magical relic known as the Great Source resided within the [Aetherian Archive](Lore_Aetherian_Archive.md) during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). A group known as the [Firstmages](Lore_Firstmages.md) harnessed the Source's power, resulting in the extension of their life.<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-Arnure-155)</sup> It is rumored that several pools and springs throughout Tamriel can grant immortality, but no maps exist which depict these locations.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-OI-31)</sup> [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md)'s realm, [Paradise](Lore_Paradise.md), made his followers immortal. They would reform in the realm if they ever died, wherever their death took place.<sup>[[153]](#cite_note-Ruma-156)</sup><sup>[[154]](#cite_note-Eldamil-157)</sup> These 'ascended immortals'<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-158)</sup> weren't considered true immortals, and were referred to distastefully as 'unmortals'.<sup>[[156]](#cite_note-Kathuet-159)</sup> Those whose souls go to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) may escape their designated realm with their mortal form intact and capable of returning to Nirn.<sup>[[157]](#cite_note-Lyris Harrow-160)</sup>

Some sources claim that mortal spirits experience a change upon reaching [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), with many souls of mortals becoming spirits of a different sort in that place.<sup>[[158]](#cite_note-ONGirnalin-161)</sup> Many reports from across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) describe a phenomenon where the souls of the dead adopt a variety of forms to return and speak with the living. Animals, plants, or even fearsome visions, are all reported manifestations. It is theorized that the [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md), immortal beings of pure magic, might have originated as mortal souls, being the spirits of Ascended Ancestors that transformed into their current state after death due to this described phenomenon. As the Luminaries themselves can't recall their origins, this theory remains unproven.<sup>[[159]](#cite_note-LAS-162)</sup><sup>[[160]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-163)</sup> Whatever the case, however, a Luminary can indeed be created out of the essence of a mortal soul, as was the case with the Crow Luminary, who was created through a ritual devised by Ulfsild the Evergreen out of a portion of her own essence. Though the process is straining and the restoration of the spirit takes time, a soul that was used in such a manner will eventually recover.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-Crow-164)</sup><sup>[[162]](#cite_note-ONU-165)</sup><sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Fable-166)</sup>
