*What is better - to be born good, or to overcome your evil nature through great effort?*
—Paarthurnax Paarthurnax, a legendary [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Ambition Overlord Cruelty" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), is the leader of the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) who resides at the top of the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md). His younger dragon brothers refer to him as "the **Old One**".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SROdahviing-1)</sup> He was once the lieutenant of Alduin, but rebelled against him and with the help of [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) taught humanity how to use the Thu'um.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ETgo Home-2)</sup> He has since lived out a peaceful life, constantly struggling against the primal need to conquer and dominate, and teaching the Greybeards under [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md)'s philosophy of the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Leamon Interview-4)</sup> When Alduin reappeared in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), he aided the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) in defeating him.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-5)</sup> In the aftermath, Paarthurnax sought to teach the dragons now scattered across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) the truth and rightness of the Way of the Voice.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup> Interestingly, Paarthurnax is missing the tip of his right horn.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax Appearance-6)</sup>

## Biography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/MER-art-Skyrim_Dragons.webp/200px-MER-art-Skyrim_Dragons.webp)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Skyrim_Dragons.webp) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Skyrim_Dragons.webp) Paarthurnax and his brother, Alduin
### Merethic Era
Paarthurnax acted as the lieutenant of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), his older brother, during the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), purportedly committing many atrocities in the process.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Esbern-7)</sup> During this time, he taught other dovah how to become masters of the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LGDragon-8)</sup> Eventually, Paarthurnax rebelled against Alduin and instead taught mortals how to use the thu'um. Nords believe this was ordered by, or at least willed by, the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md). Paarthurnax implies that one of his motivations for rebellion was that Alduin claimed the lordship of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDW-9)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ETgo Home-2)</sup> When Alduin was defeated by being cast forward in time through use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), Paarthurnax used meditation to overcome his draconic instinct and lust for power.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-5)</sup>

Paarthurnax took up an eternal vigil at the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md), where Alduin had been banished. There he would also teach mortals in the Way of the Voice, and took up practice of the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md), a philosophy discovered by [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md) following his long meditation.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Arngeir-10)</sup> He served as the leader of the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), a monastic order dedicated to the Way.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-5)</sup> Paarthurnax lived under the protection provided by the Greybeards, and then by the [Emperors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tamrielic_Emperors), although he remained a high-priority target of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) for centuries.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Esbern-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Ao D-11)</sup>

### First Era
Throughout the eras, Paarthurnax spent most of his time meditating, rarely communicating even with the Greybeards and never with outsiders. Being allowed to see him is a great privilege, and only those whose Voice is strong can find and walk the perilous path to him.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Arngeir-10)</sup> He recalls visiting the captured dragon, [Numinex](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Numinex), in Dragonsreach around the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He attributes the decline of the dragon race to the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md), who slaughtered his brothers in the late First Era and dwindled their numbers drastically.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup>

### Second Era
By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), rumors of a dragon "monk" living alongside the Nordic monks at the Throat of the World were known.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LADITSE-12)</sup>

In the late Second Era, a young hero who would later become known as [Emperor Tiber Septim I](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) came to High Hrothgar to study under the Greybeards.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Arngeir-10)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Etched Tablets-13)</sup> Paarthurnax was then still the Master of the Greybeards, and it was said that the subsequent line of Dragonborn Emperors begun by Tiber Septim protected Paarthurnax from the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) killing him (who sought to destroy all the dragons in the distant past when they first arrived to Tamriel in the First Era and became the [Akaviri Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md)).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Alduin reappeared and the prophesied [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) arrived in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The Dragonborn was soon summoned to [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md) by the Greybeards, and eventually met with Paarthurnax, who had not trained another in over a hundred years<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup> and aided the Dragonborn in defeating Alduin.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-5)</sup> When confronting Paarthurnax at the Throat of the World, Alduin noted that his brother had weakened in power and offered him the chance to join him once more or perish, but Paarthurnax refused.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup> Paarthurnax then took it upon himself to teach the Way of the Voice to the remaining dragons who had been resurrected by Alduin, so that they too could control their lust for power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SROdahviing-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup> However, the Blades discovered Paarthurnax's whereabouts, and appealed to the Dragonborn to kill Paarthurnax as punishment for the atrocities he was said to have committed. Paarthurnax's fate is currently unknown.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
