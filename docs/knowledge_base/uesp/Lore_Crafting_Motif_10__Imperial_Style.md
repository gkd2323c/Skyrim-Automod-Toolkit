(Dr. Lupus was Imperial Ethnographer for Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) from [2E 418](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_418) to 431)

The [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). I used to love it here. When I was young my native town of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) seemed hopelessly provincial to me, and I looked forward all year long to going along with mother on her annual trip to the [Heartland](Lore_Heartlands.md). For me, the capital was the epitome of learning, of culture, of everything I held dear.

I walk the avenues now, from district to district. And I look. Skingrad seemed provincial, yes, but it was [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md): direct, forthright, with clean lines and a certain spare, ascetic look to it. And its people are much the same way.

The Imperial City, except for the walls and the [Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), which are [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md), is … [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md). Refined. Decorative. Subtle. Nuanced.

Decadent. Corrupt.

Like its people. And the people it attracts.

I was too late.

[Morian](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) is gone. With the help of [Divayth](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md), cursed Divayth, he fulfilled his dream and traveled to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). According to [Seif-ij](Lore_Seif-ij_Hidja.md), he went to [Moonshadow](Lore_Moonshadow.md) as planned, but he didn't stay there. He went on, to [Ashpit](Lore_Ashpit.md), to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), to [Quagmire](Lore_Quagmire.md). To [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).

And there, in Apocrypha, he stayed.

Seif-ij told me, emotion quivering even in his flat reptilian voice, of how once he entered Oblivion Morian seemed to become more reckless, more enraptured, with each portal to a new plane. How he ignored his assistant's pleas to return. How Apocrypha … entranced him.

Seif-ij Hidja was beside himself, holding his head with its drooping spines, clearly at his wit's-end. It was up to me. I ran to Divayth's room, though Seif-ij said he was gone, hoping he'd left some way to get in touch with him, hoping he would respond to my appeals for help.

I found only a book, open on his desk, a book titled "[Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus](Lore_Fragmentae_Abyssum_Hermaeus_Morus.md)." It was open to what seemed to be a summoning ritual for the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), specifying that "whatever price is named shall be met."

A ritual to Hermaeus Mora. The Lord of Apocrypha.

I ran to Morian's laboratory. It was looted, ransacked. The only thing of interest was a crumpled note. It read, "When thou enterest into Oblivion, Oblivion entereth into thee."

Morian is gone. Gone to Apocrypha. Where he stays.

And so I walk, from district to district. Wondering. What price had the Lord of Apocrypha named to Divayth Fyr? What price for the entrancement, the captivity of Morian Zenas?

I walk the streets, the avenues and alleys. Wondering.

Wondering when I, too, will be ready to pay the price.
