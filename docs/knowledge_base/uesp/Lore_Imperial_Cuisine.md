— *[Wines of Blackwood](Lore_Wines_of_Blackwood.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOB-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/ON-misc-Colovian Food.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Colovian Food.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Colovian Food.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Colovian Food.jpg) A selection of Colovian food and drink [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) cooks approach food the same way they face the rest of life: in a straightforward and proud manner. As shrewd diplomats and traders, they stock their larders with many of the best ingredients [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) has to offer. [Cyrodiil's](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) central location in Tamriel, paired with its wealth and power, assures that the Imperials eat well from a variety of meats, vegetables, and spices from across the continent.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOC-2)</sup><sup>:11</sup> There are many culinary texts written about Imperial cuisine of particular regions such as the [Niben](Lore_Niben_River.md) Watershed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFSC1-3)</sup>

The Imperial love of [wine](Lore_Wine.md) is a cultural and economic cornerstone in Cyrodiil. Vintners in the different regions of Cyrodiil produce their own distinct vintages that are made from many different varieties of grape or other fruits and renowned vineyard owners are known to be invited to wine tasting events.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> One of the largest and most well known wine producing regions is the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) which is dotted with vineyards due to its favorable climate and rich, loamy, mildly acidic soil. The best yields of the jazby, surilie, pinot, and allegran grape varieties are grown there. Some of the wines from the western Battlehorn region are rare and sought after by collectors.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> The city of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) is surrounded by vineyards that employ citizens from across the Empire every season.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WOWW-6)</sup>

To the east in [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md), the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) region also boasts a number of renowned wines. The warm, humid climate means that different growing techniques are used compared to the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) west. The left bank of the Niben River enjoys mild winters and a long growing season and the red grape varieties of Queen's Tear and Nerianth Fine which produce sweet, robust wines do well here. The eastern regions are hotter and more humid so are not suited for red grapes. Instead, white grape varieties like Petite Gray, White Moon, and Great Ambrosia are grown here. The resulting Niben Forest wines are sweet and fruity but also crisp and refreshing. On the borders between Cyrodiil and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) the sweltering heat and humidity prohibits any grape vineyards at all. The Imperials in these regions instead sate their love of wine by using different fruits including figs, blackberries, blueberries, and peaches. Many of these country wines are cloyingly sweet and low quality but others are surprisingly balanced and tend to go well with the cuisine of the region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WOB-1)</sup>

A high class Imperial feast will typically follow a set selection of courses. The first course may consist of introductory savories meant to enliven the palate while the second may consist of fish based dishes. As is custom the fish course is followed by a course of vegetables. Up next is a course of grain based dishes, this is typically the last course in less wealthy households who don't readily have access to meat and poultry. Otherwise, the fifth course will consist of game and poultry which is then followed by the main meat course. Finally, the penultimate course is made up of desserts and the feast is ended with a final course of digestifs such as cheeses and nuts. It was once customary for high end Imperial banquets to end with two courses of desserts. It eventually became more popular to end a meal with just one however.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TFSC5-7)</sup>

## Utensils and Tableware
Imperials approach their utensils and tableware with an emphasis on quality. The materials they're crafted from include simple wicker and stone, multicolored clay, metal, and fine ceramics and silver among other materials.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Nibenese [crystal](Lore_Crystals.md) is known for its precise, transparent finish and is used to make beautiful serving plates among other works.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Other serving platters may be crafted from [brass](Lore_Brass.md) or [silver](Lore_Silver.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Rich Colovian timber is used to make mugs as its believed that its scent enriches any liquid poured into it, especially beloved Imperial wines.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> A well used wine jug is not an uncommon sight on Cyrodiilic tables.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> Some Imperial steins are decorated with [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) symbology<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> while legionnaires themselves are issued metal canteens.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The finest chalices are formed from [gold](Lore_Gold.md) and studded with red diamonds.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Fancy carafes are made from crystal and sometimes blown into specific shapes.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

Imperials are known to use two-tine forks<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> and Imperial chefs are well known for their skill with extremely sharp cutlery.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> A unique utensil is the hollow handled Colovian whistle spoon that can be used as a ladle, straw, and [musical](Lore_Music.md) instrument.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> The nobility has access to napkin holders made from rare materials such as [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

Some specialty items used by Imperials include small wine presses that are used to produce personal batches.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Large vintners will use decanting cradles to ensure proper wine aeration.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Tea sets have been made that have a representation of the Imperial [ruby](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) on every piece.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Elaborate centerpieces sometimes decorate Imperial tables, these can be modeled after Cyrodiilic landmarks such as the picturesque [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) lighthouse.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> Imperial artists produce ivory spice shakers in stylized shapes of Imperial Knights.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>
