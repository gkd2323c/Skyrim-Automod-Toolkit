# Umbra

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/LG-cardart-Umbra.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Umbra.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Umbra.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Umbra.jpg) Umbra (Legends) The Umbra Sword (or simply **Umbra**) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) designed with the sole purpose of the entrapment of souls. Sometime before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Witchwise Headdress-1)</sup> the ancient witch [Naenra Waerr](Lore_Naenra_Waerr.md) created the sword<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> at the request of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), who wanted the sword as a tool of mischief which would send him souls from the mortal realm.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup> Naenra created the sword but it was unstable, requiring Vile to give the witch a piece of his power to be imbued in the sword in order to stabilize it. However, Naenra (sometimes accredited with being [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) in disguise) supposedly tricked him, as the piece of Vile's power imbued in the sword became a sentient being which called itself **Umbra**. Naenra was executed for her evil creation, but not before she hid the sword. The sword took on a life of its own, remaining hidden until a worthy wielder was found. Umbra then slowly takes control of the wielder. The Umbra Sword has the ability to change shape, but it is always in the form of a bladed weapon. It has been reported as a black and silver claymore, a jet black longsword and as a black sword emblazoned with red markings.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup> The sword is also known to have the ability to absorb the health and stamina of those it strikes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CC-4)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWYB-5)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-6)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing Umbra among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TA-7)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Umbra and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWFR-8)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) warrior who went by the name of his sword, Umbra, was spotted in the mountains outside the settlement of [Suran](Lore_Suran.md) on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The Orc's only desire was to die in battle, but no foe could defeat him. At his request, the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) bested the Orc in mortal combat and claimed the Umbra Sword.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWUmbra-9)</sup> The sword came into the possession of [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md) later that year, and was put on display in her museum in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tribunal-10)</sup>

Sometime after the events of 3E 427, the sword was discovered by a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) named Lenwin, from the small settlement of [Pell's Gate](Lore_Pell%27s_Gate.md) in the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). She became bloodthirsty, and began calling herself Umbra after the sword. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Clavicus Vile sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to retrieve the Umbra Sword. [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md), Vile's Hound, advised the Hero against bringing him the sword, foreseeing that it would bring ruin to the Prince. It is unknown if the Hero went through with the prince's request to return the sword, and receive the [Masque](Lore_Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile.md) in return, or if the Hero kept Umbra for themself.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBClavicus-11)</sup> However, the sword eventually found itself in the hands of Clavicus Vile.

### Fourth Era

#### Umbriel Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/BK-Umbriel.jpg/200px-BK-Umbriel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-Umbriel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-Umbriel.jpg) Umbriel Clavicus Vile had played a role in the creation of the [Ingenium](Lore_Ingenium.md) during the early days of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). The device's purpose was to hold Baar Dau over Vivec City after the disappearance of Vivec. It functioned by creating a [rift](Lore_Portals.md) to the Fields of Regret through which [souls](Lore_Souls.md) were funneled to Clavicus Vile, and the energies of the [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) were leeched to hold the moon in place. The mage Sul, who was one of the designers of Ingenium, theorized that the Daedric Prince did not care for the stolen energies as he valued the souls he received in exchange more, and that the other architect of the project, Vuhon, might have even made a formal pact with Vile for him to allow the energies to be taken.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ICity-12)</sup> This process started by using the souls of prisoners and undesirables, which resulted in their immediate deaths. Eventually, a method was found that let souls power the Ingenium without causing immediate death. When Sul's lover, Ilzheven, was taken to power the Ingenium, he destroyed it in an attempt to save her. This caused Baar Dau to crash into Vivec City, resulting in the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) and the destruction of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and part of mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), an event that became known as the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md). The event also resulted in Vuhon and Sul falling through the rift and into the realm of Clavicus Vile<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup>

Once the sword had arrived in Vile's [realm](Lore_Clavicus_Vile%27s_Realm.md), Umbra escaped and cut away some of the Prince's power for itself. Umbra took the form of a dark being, in the shape of a man but with eyes like holes into nothing.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup> The Prince did not want Umbra to escape with his power, so he changed the dimensional walls of his realm to be impermeable, essentially trapping Umbra there. Using his stolen power, Umbra was able to conceal himself in one of the cities at the edge of Vile's realm, fearing that Vile would return him to the sword. In the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Umbra was attracted to the influx of souls coming from the rift in the realm caused by the first Ingenium. Casting a fortune, Umbra learned that the rift would one day become wide enough to throw the Umbra Sword through, lessening the threat posed by Vile. That day came when the Ingenium was destroyed; Umbra threw the sword to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), while at the same time Sul and Vuhon, were sucked through into Clavicus Vile's realm.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup>

Umbra took them captive, and in return for his life Vuhon promised to build a new ingenium, which would tear a piece of Vile's realm away and let Umbra "escape", in a sense. Umbra agreed, and the two created [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md), a floating city powered by souls. Umbra fused with Vuhon and placed their joined soul into the new ingenium. Vuhon then began to call himself Umbriel, and ruled the floating city. The city of Umbriel was called to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) circa [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48) by Prime Minister [Hierem](Lore_Hierem.md) and the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), who had gotten in touch with the city via the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), who in turn had made contact with the related sentient trees which ran the ecosystem on Umbriel. Hierem had promised use of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) to separate Umbriel from Vile's realm and render Umbra into a truly independent being, but Vuhon first went north to the ruins of Vivec, fearing that the Umbra Sword may be used to trap Umbra.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup>

However, when Sul and [Prince Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) arrived at [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md) on Vvardenfell in search of the Umbra Sword, it was nowhere to be found.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup> The sword had already been retrieved by the minor House Sathil eight years previous. Without Umbra stabilizing the sword, it drove whoever touched it insane. [Elhul Sathil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Elhul_Sathil) had been the one to recover it, and had been driven into a killing frenzy. He was chained down and brought back to Castle Sathil on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). Once there, he regained possession of the sword and would not relinquish his grip. His father, Lord [Hleryn Sathil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Hleryn_Sathil), constructed a dungeon to hold his son until a cure could be found. Elhul stopped eating after the first year, and his father spent years reading up on the sword. Sul and Attrebus Mede eventually tracked down the sword, and were locked in Elhul's prison by [Nirai Sathil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Nirai_Sathil), who feared the sword. The two defeated Elhul, who had become skeletal and impervious to pain, and used the sword to teleport to Vile's realm.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup>

With his power stolen by Umbra, Vile and his realm were weakened. The Prince tried to take the sword, but he couldn't handle it without Umbra stabilizing it. The pair attempted to bargain with the Prince. Vile initially refused, claiming that he'd remained as powerful as ever by spreading himself less thin, that Umbra was nothing but an aspect of his with delusions of being its own person, that he had other followers that could retrieve Umbra, and that he'd only offer aid in exchange for one of their souls. However, Attrebus pointed out that Umbriel was making its way to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) in order to reach the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), the power of which could be used to make Umbra truly independent from Vile. Forced to concede, Vile agreed to help. He could not transport the two to Umbriel directly, as Vuhon had made it so the island was invisible to him, but he could take them to the Imperial City, where the island was headed. Before transporting them to their destination, Vile warned that they'd come to regret their deal by the end.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup>

Sul and Attrebus found their way into Umbriel, and Sul wielded the sword in battle against Vuhon. It had no effect on him, as his soul was in the ingenium. In a final battle beside the ingenium, Attrebus was forced to wield the Umbra Sword and plunged it into the machine. This separated Umbra from Vuhon, trapping Umbra in the sword and mortally wounding Vuhon. With the veil over Umbriel destroyed, Clavicus Vile could enter. The Prince possessed Attrebus and used the Umbra Sword to stab Sul. Sul punched Attrebus and returned him to normality, before jumping on the orb of the ingenium with the sword still embedded in his chest, as Vile's laughter echoed in his ears.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup>

Sul, the Umbra Sword, and the Ingenium than dissolved into a wave of black smoke, which reformed itself into a giant avatar of Clavicus Vile. The still living Vuhon informed the now freed Attrebus that Vile likely intended to drain the souls of everyone remaining on Umbriel before letting it drop onto the Imperial City itself. The only hope was to have the Umbriel-Hist banish Vile and Umbriel with the aid of [Mere-Glim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Mere-Glim), who could help due to now being part of the island. Vuhon still retained the power he originally had, something which Vile could not take, and so he distracted the Prince long enough for the Hist and Mere-Glim to complete the banishment.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup> Thus Vile was able to regain the power he lost due to the crisis, seemingly reclaiming the spirit of Umbra and absorbing the thousands of souls within Umbriel's Ingenium in the process, but failing to carry out his plan to unleash more chaos in Tamriel. <sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup>

### Appearance in Skyrim
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/SR-prerelease-Umbra.jpg/200px-SR-prerelease-Umbra.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-prerelease-Umbra.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-prerelease-Umbra.jpg) Umbra and its Soul Images In late [4E 179](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_179), the Umbra Sword mysteriously returned to the affairs of mortals when an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) treasure hunter named Cresitus fell down a hole in the mountains east of [Shor's Stone](Lore_Shor%27s_Stone.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and broke both of his legs. He found himself within the long-lost crypts known as Champion's Rest, an ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruin once used as a gladiatorial arena. Stranded and dying, Cresitus began to dream that he was swimming in a sea of black, with the Umbra Sword calling out for him to claim it. He awoke to find the sword next to him; his legs immediately began to heal, as if by magic. Over the next several days, Umbra began to whisper to Cresitus in his sleep, hungering for souls. Eventually, Cresitus set up camp and gave up any plans to escape the ruin, finding himself content among the restless souls of so many fallen warriors. His psyche fused with the sword, and, donning a set of ebony armor, Cresitus began to call himself Umbra.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> He remained undisturbed for many years, until circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) when [silver](Lore_Silver.md) prospectors from Shor's Stone broke through into Champion's Rest and encountered an armored "ghost" wielding a whispering sword. This ghost was in fact a soul image of Umbra, a physical manifestation which he could split from his person at will.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-VR-15)</sup>

Champion's Rest was subsequently investigated by the [Vigil of Stendarr](Lore_Vigil_of_Stendarr.md), who correctly surmised that the Umbra Sword—thought to have been destroyed—was the only Daedric artifact which matched the miners' description.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-VR-15)</sup> Hearing rumors of the ruin's discovery, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) arrived at Champion's Rest and entered the [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) -filled ruin to put a stop to Umbra before he could threaten Shor's Stone. Umbra and his soul images faced the Dragonborn in the ancient Nordic arena, possessing powerful magic and physical immunities, but the Dragonborn was successful in killing Cresitus and claiming the sword to prevent it from falling into the wrong hands.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CC-4)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Whispers-16)</sup>
