[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/OB-place-Nonungalo.jpg/200px-OB-place-Nonungalo.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Nonungalo.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Nonungalo.jpg) Nonungalo circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Nonungalo ([Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md): *"place where few have lived"*)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LS-1)</sup> is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located in the [Imperial Reserve](Lore_Imperial_Reserve.md) of the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md), between [Fort Ontus](Lore_Fort_Ontus.md) and Fort Dirich, northwest of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) and southwest of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OB-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBMap-3)</sup> One of the subsections of the complex was known as the **Halls of Nonungalo**. A significant number of [Motherwort](Lore_Motherwort.md) plants can be found nearby the ruined complex.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OB-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MS-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/ON-place-Nonungalo.jpg/200px-ON-place-Nonungalo.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nonungalo.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nonungalo.jpg) Nonungalo ca. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Nonungalo found itself in the middle of the Wildburn, a strip of devastated land separating the newly grown jungle of Dawnood from the rest of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), which appeared as a result of the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md)'s actions. The ruin attracted a group of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) who tried to raise an army of undead, but were killed by the invading [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) forces of [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md). The necromancers rose from the dead and attempted to complete the ritual, but were stopped by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and a priest of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONUnfinished Business-5)</sup>

By the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the halls of the ruin had become home to a group of hostile creatures. The origin of these beasts was unknown, however.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OB-2)</sup>
