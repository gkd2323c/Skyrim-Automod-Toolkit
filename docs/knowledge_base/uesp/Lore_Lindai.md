## History
During the time of the Ayleids, Lindai was an independent city-state and a fierce rival of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md). The infamous Heretic of Lindai was from the city, and was seemingly a worshipper of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Following the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), Lindai was abandoned and fell into ruin. Along with the Crown of Lindai, the last king of Lindai was entombed in the royal burial chamber, which was then sealed.

During the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a squad of [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) soldiers based in [Fort Glademist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Glademist) were ordered to clear the nearby Ayleid ruins, including Lindai, in search of enemy troops.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> However, Lindai had been taken over by a cell of [Shadowed Path](Lore_Shadowed_Path.md) cultists, who began raising the dead. Their efforts culminated in the creation of a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) to lead the cult, although it was destroyed by a passing soldier on behalf of [Weynon Priory](Lore_Weynon_Priory.md) before it could leave Lindai. The priests at Weynon henceforth kept a close eye on the ruins to prevent any further necromantic activity.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholar of the Ayleids named [Herminia Cinna](Lore_Herminia_Cinna.md) came into possession of a key which would unseal the royal burial chambers. When [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md), an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) collector of Ayleid antiquities, sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md) to buy the [Crown of Nenalata](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md) from her, Cinna suggested that the Hero use the key to recover the Crown of Lindai instead. Cinna rightly feared Umbacano's ulterior motives, as he planned to use the Crown of Nenalata to restore the power of Ayleid civilization in Cyrodiil by proclaiming himself king in the throne room of Nenalata. Although Umbacano would be unable to distinguish between the two crowns, the magical enchantments in Nenalata's throne room would destroy anyone who would proclaim himself king while wearing the crown of their rivals. It is unknown which crown Umbacano was given before his fatal expedition to Nenalata, or indeed whether or not the Hero entered the undead-infested ruins of Lindai at all.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBSecrets-5)</sup>
