# Goblin Gate

— *[Notes For Redguard History](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Notes_For_Redguard_History)* The Goblin gate was a great "[rent in space time](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md)" that hung high in the sky over [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup> serving as a gateway between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and the world (allegedly another [dimension](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) or [planet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planets))<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> from which a breed of [giant Goblins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Giant_Goblin) originated.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> Little is known about the origins of the gateway or the world it led to, other than that the gate was said to have been made by the giant Goblins themselves.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup> The giant Goblins were generally described as taller and less "cowardly" than Goblins of the later Eras.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TKF-3)</sup> They worshipped and were led by the [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:God) [Malooc](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malooc), and were sometimes known as "Malooc's Horde".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-T26OFSIU-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-EGE-6)</sup>

## History

### First Known Opening
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg) Volenfell, the ruined former Capital of Clan Rourken *Long, long ago, when the world was in its springtime, before the Redguards came and the glorious Septim Empire was formed, but after the goblins had driven the dwarves out of Hammerfell [...]*
— *[King Edward, Part I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:King_Edward,_Part_I)* At some point between [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) and [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), when the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) inhabited Hammerfell,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3H-7)</sup> the giant Goblins invaded via their Goblin gate.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> They possessed a huge army of fighters, and appeared in the middle of the province, where they would pillage and raze towns to the ground, driving the Dwarves out of the region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> The giant Goblins would go on to establish strongholds across the [Alik'r Desert](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alik%27r_Desert),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TKF-3)</sup> and make enemies of other groups that settled the desert such as the [Corelanyan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> and the [Elves](Lore_Mer.md) (and later [Manmeri](Lore_Breton.md)) that lived in what would become [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3H-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EGH-10)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png/200px-ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png) Depiction of Frandar Hunding standing victorious over many Goblins Around [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780), the first wave of [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada), led by [Frandar Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frandar_Hunding), departed from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) and would eventually arrive in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) circa [1E 785](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_785) or later.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-So A-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONSHV2-12)</sup> Soon after arriving they would enter into a war with the giant Goblins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TKF-3)</sup> The Goblins were led in battle by their god Malooc, but Malooc fled east when the army of the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding) overtook his Goblin hordes.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> Despite his initial success in driving many of the Goblins out of Hammerfell,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-RGTalisman-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DFHoliday-14)</sup> Frandar would be killed during one of the bloodiest battles of the campaign.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TKF-3)</sup>

The Goblins would receive reinforcements from the Goblin gate, launching an invasion on the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) settlers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> Frandar's son [Divad Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divad_Hunding) would go on to win the war by repelling their invasion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TKF-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SDH-15)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup> He and his band of [Sword Singers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword-singers) worked their sacred forges and consulted eldritch tomes for many long years to create five great swords, a mix of [Shehai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shehai) and [smithing](Lore_Smithing.md), which five great [Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ansei) warriors took and rose to the sky, using their power to close the Goblin gate.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SDH-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DHGhost-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> By some accounts, this also cursed the Goblins who were left behind, diminishing them into their modern size.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TTT-17)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TKF-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> Despite this, at least one— [Mahgzoor Rockhand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mahgzoor_Rockhand) —was said to have escaped being diminished.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TTT-17)</sup>

### Second Invasion
Generations after their battle with Divad Hunding, the Goblin gate would re-open. The giant Goblins returned with a new invasion force,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DHGhost-16)</sup> having managed to open the path from their world to Nirn again.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> The Redguards struggled greatly against their horde, as the giant Goblins received a constant supply of arms and reinforcements from the re-opened Goblin gate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup> The powerful Ansei [Derik Hallin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Derik_Hallin) was responsible for reassembling the five magic swords which Divad had previously used to close the Goblin gate. He and his companions hurled the swords into the rent in the sky, sealing the giant Goblins in the void between their world and Nirn forever.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGH-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DHGhost-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DHShrine-18)</sup>

Many years after the second invasion, Mahgzoor Rockhand was the last known remaining giant Goblin on Nirn, having escaped the Curse of Divad through a cunning trick. He was killed during his invasion of the city of [Ojwambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ojwambu), but his enemy Derik Hallin also fell defending the city.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TTT-17)</sup>

### Legacy
*Ironically, it was in our fight against the hideous Goblins that we came to love the desert and its simple beauty.*
—Throne Keeper Farvad<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TTT-17)</sup> Though some modern scholars consider Hunding's war with the giant Goblins to be a myth,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DFHoliday-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-2)</sup> the victory of the early [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) is still celebrated during the annual [Festival of Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Festival_of_Blades).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DFHoliday-14)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-T26OFSIU-5)</sup> Porcelain bowls were created for the festival depicting Redguards fighting against the giant Goblins.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Contra Bowl-19)</sup> One publication theorized that the giant Goblins were actually [Gargoyles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gargoyle) due to superficial similarities,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TMo GS-20)</sup> when in actuality they were completely unrelated.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

One of the giant Goblin tribes slaughtered by the Ra Gada was the [Flint-Tooth Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flint-Tooth_Tribe). Following their defeat, the [spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) of this tribe became bound to the ruins of Hoon Ding's Watch in the Alik'r Desert. These spirits serve to test pilgrims hoping to understand the trials of the Ra Gada, who came to love the desert's simple beauty during their fight against the Goblins.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONMOTR-22)</sup>

The city of [Port Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Port_Hunding) on [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) was named in Frandar Hunding's honor and is home to [a vast statue of him](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Statues_Notable_Figures#Frandar_Hunding) standing on a Goblin's head and looking out towards [Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hunding_Bay). Many believe that the goblins of Stros M'Kai were banished beneath the earth thanks to him.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-RGTrithik-23)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-RGTalisman-13)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-24)</sup> Some Redguards who lived there would warn [Goblin-ken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Goblin-ken) such as [Gremlins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gremlin) to return to their caves, lest Hunding turn them into stone.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-RGTrithik-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-RGMariah-25)</sup> The feats of Frandar and Divad are sometimes conflated under their shared name of Hunding by modern Redguards.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-24)</sup><sup>:62</sup>

"Goblin-meat" is a [derogatory term](Lore_Profanity.md#Racial_Slurs) for Redguards,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup> likely referencing their long history of conflict with the race. Due to these conflicts,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Truth-27)</sup> modern Redguards are known to have biases against or harbor ancestral hated for the Goblin race.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Desc-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup> Redguards have occasionally referred to modern Goblins as "Malooc's squalor".<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^1](#note_nb1) | Series writer [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) explained that during his tenure at Bethesda, the [*Redguard*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard) and [*Morrowind*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind) team had conceptualized a new backstory for the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) based on the backstory for Goblins that had already been established in [*Daggerfall*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall) (which he described as "making-no-sense-planar-nonsense"), in an attempt to distance the Orcs away from their typical [noble savage](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noble_savage) depiction in fantasy media. The idea was to take the "weird and cool" samurai-esque armor which the Orcs had worn in *Daggerfall* and narratively mesh that aesthetic with the "Giant Goblins from Planet X" lore concept established in *[Notes for Redguard History](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Notes_For_Redguard_History)*, with this "Orc Prime" being a sort of "Orc Atlantis" where "Teat Shoguns gave mystical orders and wore strangely-sculpted Trinimasks," with [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) acting as a "conduit" to the realm. The idea ended up on the cutting room floor, and Kirkbride admitted that the concept of "Giant Goblins from Planet X" was largely ignored in his writings.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-31)</sup> |
