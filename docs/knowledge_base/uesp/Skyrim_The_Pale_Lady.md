## Quick Walkthrough
1. Find [Frostmere Crypt](Skyrim_Frostmere_Crypt.md).
2. Learn about theft.
3. Fight deeper into the crypt.
4. Banish [The Pale Lady](Skyrim_The_Pale_Lady_(creature).md) one way or another.

## Detailed Walkthrough
While traveling near [Frostmere Crypt](Skyrim_Frostmere_Crypt.md), you may come upon several bandits attacking one of their own. Whether to help or not is up to you. Regardless of your choice, you will learn that some sort of thievery has occurred inside the crypt and the quest will begin, requiring you to investigate further. Alternately, you may start the quest without witnessing the bandit attack if [Eisa Blackthorn](Skyrim_Eisa_Blackthorn.md) is present outside the Crypt. She has [her journal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Eisa%27s_Journal) in her inventory which once read, also starts the Pale Lady quest, but this is not necessary if you save her during the attack.

Fighting through the crypt, you can find two journals, one belonging to a leader of the bandits, [Kyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kyr), and one to a woman met earlier, Eisa. Reading both journals will update optional quest steps and reveal that the bandits found a hidden underground forest while digging for treasures. It is also revealed that Eisa's friend, [Ra'jirr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ra%27jirr), seems to have started losing his mind soon after the forest was discovered.

The opening area of Frostmere Crypt has two bandits talking at a table, and a third bandit off to the right in a side chamber. Opening the iron gate leads further down into the crypt. An iron door barred from the other side will force you into a large cavern on an upper walkway with bandits below. One bandit near a door lever from a west side position can be killed from a distance.

Moving forward will bring you to a T-intersection - to the left is a guarded room with a [grindstone](Skyrim_Grindstone.md), supplies and a bored bandit guard outside. The path to the right opens up into a large area leading immediately to three rooms. To the left is Kyr's room where there is one of the two journals, a chest and some basic loot. Straight ahead is another sleeping room with [Ra'jirr's Note](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ra%27jirr%27s_Note) and [Eisa's Journal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Eisa%27s_Journal). To the right is a lower level area and two more bandits.

Two exits lead from the room. One is a drawbridge in the raised position, controlled by the west side position bandit above, so the only option is to pass through the door. Two more bandits are in this room with low level loot. A further passage leads around to a rock face being excavated and a bandit controlling the bridge lever. Activating the lever opens up the lower level mining area, with two more bandits and an [iron ore](Skyrim_Iron.md) deposit. A passage to the west leads down to another chamber with two more bandits and more mining tunnels.

A tunnel leads downward and to the south, but be careful of a [dead-fall trap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falling_Boulders). Another T-intersection leads to two choices. To the east is a small chamber with an empty chest and an ore deposit close by. To the west, the mine tunnels change more to a crypt-like setting and another bandit working a [silver ore](Skyrim_Silver.md) deposit. A door in this chamber leads to Frostmere Depths.

Eventually you will reach the underground forest only to find the dying bandit leader mumbling his last words and Ra'jirr attacking [The Pale Lady](Skyrim_The_Pale_Lady_(creature).md), a named [Wispmother](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wispmother).

At this point you have three options:

- Quickly run up to Ra'jirr's body, loot the sword—appropriately named "[The Pale Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Pale_Blade)"—and place it onto the pedestal nearby, thus finishing the quest and banishing the Pale Lady.
- Save Ra'jirr from The Pale Lady using either [Whirlwind Sprint](Skyrim_Whirlwind_Sprint.md) or [Slow Time](Skyrim_Slow_Time.md) before she has the chance to attack him. [Pickpocket](Skyrim_Pickpocket.md) The Pale Blade from him, then place it on the pedestal. Ra'jirr will remain standing in front of the pedestal and will attack on sight, despite your having saved his life.
- Fight the Pale Lady. You can do this after having done one of the above by simply removing the sword from the pedestal. If desired, it can be placed back on the pedestal, re-banishing the Pale Lady, to give you a break during combat. Once the Pale Lady is killed, the quest will be complete with The Pale Blade as your reward.

After you have slain The Pale Lady, make sure that you follow the path west-southwest of the pedestal until you get to the end of a rock formation, turn right and climb up to find another path that leads to a master-locked chest. If you follow the stream located on the eastern side of the pedestal, closer to the word wall, you'll find an orichalcum vein. On your way out, next to the iron doors, is a boss-leveled chest, and near it is a sword.

On top of the staircase leaving the forest is a trigger-plate that will release poisonous darts. The next room has a bridge leading over a pool of water back to the entrance of the ruins. On the bottom is an adept-locked chest with some gold and a leveled magicka and a healing potion. Two soul gems, a piece of armor, a random potion and a leveled Battleaxe is also there.

## Notes
- Eisa and Ra'jirr were originally part of Hajvarr Iron-Hand's gang. They then mutinied and escaped, according to [Hajvarr's journal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hajvarr%27s_Journal).
- The Pale Blade can be recharged at any time by entering the crypt again and placing the blade upon its pedestal. It can then be removed and used once more.
- It's possible to never start the quest if you attack and kill Eisa outside the barrow before she can speak to you and never read her journal if you take it from her body afterwards. You can still go through the ruin like normal and claim The Pale Blade from Ra'jirr, however. All quest-related notes and journals inside the ruin won't give the quest if you avoid reading Eisa's, and reading them after claiming the weapon won't trigger the quest (since its technically "finished" at this point), though placing the blade on the altar banishes the Pale Lady as usual.

## Bugs
- Approaching Frostmere Crypt from the north may not trigger the quest. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.9.26.0.8, fixes this bug.
- It's possible to find Eisa standing outside Frostmere Crypt, unharmed and alone. In order to get her journal, you have to either pickpocket or kill her. If you attempt to enter and exit the crypt with her standing outside, she may disappear from the game. Another option is to reload a different save file and approach Frostmere Crypt from the west road to trigger the event in which three bandits should be attacking Eisa. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.1, fixes this bug.
- The scene where the bridge is being lowered doesn't play because the property was never set. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.7, fixes this bug.
- If Eisa becomes hostile, she may never trigger the quest, even if she is later calmed and talks to you about it. <sup>**?**</sup>
- It's possible for The Pale Blade to become unobtainable, and the quest to become incompletable. The Pale Blade will appear on the ground, unable to be picked up, and not in Ra'jirr's inventory. Resetting a save at the entrance seems to have no effect. <sup>**?**</sup>
