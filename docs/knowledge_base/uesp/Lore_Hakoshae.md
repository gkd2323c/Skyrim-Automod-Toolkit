[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Hakoshae (also known as **Valley Guard**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup> is a settlement found in the region of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), within the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). It is neighbored by several settlements, including [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) to the north, Darkarn Place to the southwest, and [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md) in the southeast.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Map Arena-2)</sup>

It is one of the few villages across Northern Elsweyr that was built by the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) refugees that fled to Elsweyr following the death of Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430). Hakoshae is an isolated and quiet community that promotes their Akaviri background with pride. It is populated by [Rim-Men](Lore_Rim-Men.md) who descend from the Akaviri refugees.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMagnate-3)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/ON-crown_store-Hakoshae_Festival_Costume.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Hakoshae_Festival_Costume.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Hakoshae_Festival_Costume.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Hakoshae_Festival_Costume.jpg) Hakoshae Proving Festival After the assassination of Savirien-Chorak, the Akaviri fled into Elsweyr during the brief reign of Emperor [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(warlord).md), who expelled the Akaviri from Cyrodiil to rid the province of the foreigners who had ruled it during the first half of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1E-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OABR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTS-6)</sup> The Akaviri eventually took refuge in [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), the northern region of Elsweyr, where the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) granted them asylum.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1E-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OABR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTS-6)</sup> For roughly a century, the town believed their magnate descended from Proconsul [Beloren-Kaie](Lore_Beloren-Kaie.md), who was an heir of Savirien-Chorak.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMagnate-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Lt FD-7)</sup> In reality, the Magnate family descended from [Lein-Barduik](Lore_Lein-Barduik.md), Beloren-Kaie's bodyguard who fled when the Proconsul was attacked and killed by the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-8)</sup> After Beloren-Kaie's death, Lein-Barduik looted his corpse and assumed his identity.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-8)</sup> Lein-Barduik then founded the town of Hakoshae in the [Rim Territories](Lore_Rim_Territories.md) south of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-8)</sup> The people of Hakoshae lived in peace, away from Khajiit and [Imperial](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) politics. These people, along with other refugees, were known as the "[Rim-Men](Lore_Rim-Men.md)" to the Khajiit.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1E-4)</sup>

Shortly after Hakoshae's founding, Magnate Lein-Barduik held their first [Proving Festival](Lore_Proving_Festival.md), a festival where the people prove themselves to their ancestors.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TPF-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMagnate-3)</sup> The Proving Festival has three trials, known as the Three Proofs. The Proof of Reverence involves placing a paper lotus into the river, calling on one's ancestors to witness their deeds. The Proof of Strength involves collecting a flower that grows in a [senche-lion](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) lair to display one's might and bravery. The Proof of Wisdom involves solving a riddle to test one's knowledge and wit.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Online Dot P-10)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMagnate-3)</sup> In the final phase of the festival, the participants ask their ancestors to judge whether they have honored them worthily and dutifully.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TPF-9)</sup> The ancient Akaviri rites invoked during the final stage of the festival can compel ancestor spirits to enter the realm of the living and confront their descendants directly.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-8)</sup> During the first Proving Festival, Lein-Barduik summoned Beloren-Kaie, who attempted to kill him.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-8)</sup> In response, Lein-Barduik cast him back into the realm of the dead.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-8)</sup> Summoning Beloren-Kaie caused Lein-Barduik to cancel future Proving Festivals, and it was eventually forgotten by the people of Hakoshae.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TPF-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMagnate-3)</sup> Lein-Barduik later passed away, and his descendants continued to rule Hakoshae.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-8)</sup> He was interred in the Hakoshae Tombs.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HT-11)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Hakoshae was ruled by Magnate Feina-Darak, a descendant of Lein-Barduik. By this point, marriage between the Akaviri and Imperials had caused the people of Hakoshae to have more Imperial blood than Akaviri, but they still honored their ancestors by maintaining some of their customs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMagnate-3)</sup> Circa 2E 582, Feina-Darak found a text referencing the Proving Festival and held the town's first festival since Lein-Barduik's time.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Online Dot P-10)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMagnate-3)</sup> During the festival, a grandson of Beloren-Kaie's assassin, Ashur, arrived in Hakoshae to investigate a rumor that one of Savirien-Chorak's heirs had survived. Enlisting the help of a [traveler](Lore_The_Vestige.md), the two investigated Hakoshae, while the latter partook in the Proving Festival. The investigation eventually led them to the Hakoshae Tombs after Feina-Darak was taken by the spirit of Beloren-Kaie, whom she had summoned during the festival. In the tombs, they encountered the spirit of Lein-Barduik, who unsuccessfully attempted to stop Ashur and the traveler from learning the truth. After defeating the spirit of Lein-Barduik, they spoke with Beloren-Kaie's spirit, who revealed the truth behind the town's founding. Ultimately, Feina-Darak was spared, announced her true heritage, and attempted to renounce her title of magnate. The inhabitants of Hakoshae, however, persuaded her to continue as magnate because of her leadership skills, regardless of her heritage. The festival continued, Ashur left Hakoshae knowing that his grandfather had succeeded in killing Beloren-Kaie, and the truth behind Lein-Barduik's treachery was revealed.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Online Dot P-10)</sup>

The Rim-men, presumably including those from Hakoshae, lived in relative seclusion until remnants of the [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) who had served [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572) resurfaced in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and seized the Ruby Throne from the successors of Attrebus. The Rim-men then decided to join their brothers to try to rebuild the Empire. Their efforts to rebuild the Empire ultimately failed, and the Khajiit attempted to reclaim their lands through a series of bloody wars.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1E-4)</sup>

### Third Era
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), it was known as Valley Guard and was ruled by Lady Moahin. It had a rivalry with [Darkarn Place](Lore_Darkarn_Place.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup>
