# Poetic Edda

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/SR-place-Bards_College.jpg/200px-SR-place-Bards_College.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bards_College.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bards_College.jpg) Bards College (Skyrim) The Poetic Edda is an ancient collection of poems, stories, and legends that depict the extensive history of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), which is why it earned the moniker, *"the living history of Skyrim."* For an untold amount of time, bards and [skalds](Lore_Skald.md) have contributed to the Poetic Edda, which is often kept by the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRViarmo-1)</sup>

The most infamous contribution to the collection was [King Olaf's Verse](Lore_King_Olaf%27s_Verse.md), which was created by the early- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) bard, [Svaknir](Lore_Svaknir.md), as a serious critique of the then newly-named High King of Skyrim, [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRViarmo-1)</sup> However, it was eventually lost to time and not recovered until [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) by the Bards College, roughly thousands of years after it was made. It was kept in [Dead Men's Respite](Lore_Dead_Men%27s_Respite.md), the burial site of both Svaknir and Olaf. Because of its ancient condition, the college was forced to fill in the holes that were lost to time.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRTt F-2)</sup> Azzadal, a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) bard, once performed the entire Poetic Edda before he jumped off [Bard's Leap Summit](Lore_Bard%27s_Leap_Summit.md). However, like many others before him, he died in the process.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRAzzadal-3)</sup>

## Notes
- The Poetic Edda is based on the real-world [Poetic Edda](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poetic_Edda), the modern name for an untitled collection of Old Norse poems contributed anonymously. Information in the Edda can also be found in the [Codex Regius](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Codex_Regius), an important source of Norse mythology and Germanic heroic legends.
