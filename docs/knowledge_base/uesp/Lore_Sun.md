# Sun


The common story for its creation is that it formed when the Aedra [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus) fled the mortal world in the final stages of its creation, to prevent himself being trapped.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TM-6)</sup> He was joined by the [Magna Ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge), who left similar but smaller "holes" which became known as the [stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Eo MN-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TM-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-36Lo V33-9)</sup> In the Anuadic creation story, prior to the shattering of the twelve worlds and the formation of Nirn, [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) in his grief at the loss of [Nir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nir), hid himself in the sun and slept.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TAA-10)</sup>

Sunlight is necessary for surface plantlife to grow.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-NNSS-11)</sup> In locations which lack natural sunlight, such as the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), biological plantlife will not thrive unless the lifegiving properties of Nirn's sun can be simulated successfully.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-NNSOOT-12)</sup> This was discovered by the Clockwork Apostles from the Everwound Wellspring, and to produce viable amounts for a harvest, the life force of beings would be required to simulate on a small scale what the Sun produces naturally.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-NNSOOT-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOOIAMD-13)</sup>

However, despite the lifegiving properties of the sun, health issues can occur due to overexposure. This is colloquially known as sunburn or "Aetherial exposure", various alchemical concoctions can be used to lessen the effects.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-JOSC-14)</sup> For [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), Aetherial exposure has more extreme effects, as most of the vampire breeds will burn when they enter direct sunlight.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OOM-15)</sup>

The 1st day of [Midyear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Midyear) is considered the hottest day of the year.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-2)</sup> The light of the sun can sometimes be blocked from reaching Nirn by the movements of the [moons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons) in an event known as an **[eclipse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eclipse)**, also known as a [Vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) Day<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOTwitter-16)</sup>.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CTEclipse-17)</sup> Sunlight may also be blotted out by magical means.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Such events are considered dangerous, as the darkness makes it difficult to work without artificial light, and the lack of sunlight provides cover for vampires, [ghosts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost), and other [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead) to run amok, leading to increased activity from such beings.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CTEclipse-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup> The phenomenon is sometimes thought to represent the loss of the favor of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and to be possible to undo with diligent prayer to that deity. Tamrielic kings have also been known to sometimes use [time altering](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Time_Magic) magical items such as Arcane Hourglasses to fast forward time and undo the phenomenon. Inhabitants of Tamriel can sometimes receive [warning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) of an impending eclipse in the form of prophetic nightmares revolving around it and the subsequent surge of spirits and undead.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CTEclipse-17)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Castles Items-20)</sup>

## Cultural Interpretations

### Altmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png/200px-ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png) Prismatic Sunbird Feather The Altmer consider sunlight to be the radiance of Magnus.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Salas En AD-21)</sup> The [Sun Birds of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun_Birds_of_Alinor) were [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of the Merethic Era who were known to have traveled to Aetherius in ships called sunbirds.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3-22)</sup> Sunbirds have been described as being made of crystal and solidified sunlight; having gem-encrusted mini-trebuchets, fit for sailing, that fire pure aetheric fire; and having banners listing their ancestors all the way back to the Dawn.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-23)</sup>

One of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md)' major cities is [Sunhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunhold) on the south coast of the [main island](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LOMap-24)</sup> The city's symbol is a yellow sun with a red outline on a white background.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONBanner-25)</sup> The way the sun sets over the southeastern mountains and covers the coast in shadows is how the city of [Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dusk) acquired its name.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOLive SF-26)</sup>

Three is one of the Sacred Numbers, which the Altmer recognize as integral to the universe's existence. Three, in particular, represents the Prime [Celestials](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Celestial), which are embodied by the Sun and the two moons.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TOTSN-27)</sup>

### Argonian
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/ON-item-Vakka_Stone.jpg/200px-ON-item-Vakka_Stone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Vakka_Stone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Vakka_Stone.jpg) Vakka Stone Marsh-born Argonians enjoy basking in the sun.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Contrary to what some foreign scholars believe, Argonians do not worship the sun. Rather, they enjoy its warmth<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-29)</sup> and bask in its light.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-30)</sup> Argonian ladies may carry a parasol to keep their scales from drying out in the hot sun.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-31)</sup> Reclusive Argonians that live in places away from sunlight (such as subterranean caverns) will lose the color in their scales.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONMarshmist Paleskin-32)</sup>

[Vakka Stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vakka_Stone) (literally "Sun Stones" in the [Jel](Lore_Jel.md) language)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TST:AJP-4)</sup> are bright yellow crystals used by the ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to store the raw magicka that falls from the sun.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Kass-33)</sup>

Prior to [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md), some tribes of ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) were directed by their [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree to worship the [sun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus) or even to collect its power.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-VBX-34)</sup> Known as the Sun-Blessed, they were eventually betrayed by their [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis) -worshipping brethren, and the practice faded into obscurity.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-35)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-36)</sup>

Another myth associated with the pre-Duskfall Argonians, is of a tribe of golden-scaled Argonians that built their city upwards to reach the sun. However the ending of the story can differ, with one version claiming the sun destroyed them, while another says the tribe cracked the sun open "like an egg" and became gods.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LTOTE2-37)</sup>

### Breton
The Priory of the Golden Staff were peaceful followers of Magnus headquartered near [Stormhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhaven),<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Mt C:SC-38)</sup> teaching of Magnus's grand design for the Mundus, and his disappointment at the flaws introduced into his creation. According to them Magnus watches from the sun, waiting to repair what the others set amiss.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DCT-39)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SC-40)</sup> [Nynelle Dumaris](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nynelle_Dumaris), a famous Breton poet authored a poem *[Fearless as the Sun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fearless_as_the_Sun)*.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-FATS-41)</sup>

King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) wrote in his autobiography, *[Triumphs of a Monarch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Triumphs_of_a_Monarch)*, that a Halo of Gold outlined the sun on the day of his coronation. This was supposedly an omen of approval from the Divines that ended doubts to his rule and turned even his most envious rivals into allies.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TOAM10-42)</sup>

### Dunmer
*"Under sun and sky. We greet you warmly"* is a common greeting in Morrowind.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-MWGen Greeting-43)</sup> [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) legends, specifically from [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s famous sermons claim that [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) was banished from the library of the sun by the power of Magnus.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LOV-44)</sup>

### Dwemer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Miniature_Sun%2C_Portable.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Miniature_Sun%2C_Portable.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Miniature_Sun,_Portable.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Miniature_Sun,_Portable.jpg) Dwarven Miniature Sun According to scholars studying Dwarven lore, the Dwemer paid tribute to the sun in form of the "Solar Arc" iconography. Some of their [animunculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) were surmounted with double golden crescents representing the sun. It was a rare occurrence, however.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-SADS-45)</sup> As they often lived underground, they created large "miniature suns" to light the dark caves.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-MDS-46)</sup>

### Imperial
A shrine to [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) was built at [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor) by the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) to house the [Armor of Tiber Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Armor_of_Tiber_Septim). The shrine was once an important Imperial pilgrimage site, and a riddle referring to the Sun's course across the sky was used to test pilgrims who came to witness the armor.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-47)</sup>

### Khajiit
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/ON-crown_store-Dragonscale_Solar_Horse.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Dragonscale_Solar_Horse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dragonscale_Solar_Horse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dragonscale_Solar_Horse.jpg) A Solar Horse, believed to be a creature of Magrus The oppressive climate of Elsweyr makes it impractical to wear heavy clothing and armor. The typical [bipedal Khajiit](#Morphology) dresses in an ample shawl of brightly-colored cloth, known as a budi, for defense against the harsh rays of the sun. This shirt is tightly fastened in braids down the right side, to prevent any part of the torso fur from being seen, as the exposure of such in public is deemed both offensive and unsightly.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1E-48)</sup> The Khajiit in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) would sometimes use the exclamation, *"Desert Sun"*.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-DK-49)</sup> There is a variation of [Rawlith Khaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawlith_Khaj) based on the Sun.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-IWTB-50)</sup>

In [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) religion [Magrus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magrus) is known as the **Sun God** or the **Cat's Eye**. And like his counterpart, he is also associated with magic, and is revered by some Khajiit magi. However, there are several versions of Magrus' association with the sun.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TSS-51)</sup> In the works of [Clan Mother Ahnissi](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Clan_Mother_Ahnissi), Magrus is given his authority over the Sun by [Ahnurr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ahnurr).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Wo CMA-52)</sup> However, this is elaborated on in earlier stories which predate the religious reforms of the [Riddle'Thar Epiphany](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar_Epiphany) and is where his other title, the **Third Eye of Azurah** originates. In these tales, Magrus had been blinded in one eye while being pursued by [Boethra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boethra) and [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj) and then fell into the [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) realm [Moonshadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonshadow). Here [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah) found and judged him, in the versions more sympathetic to Magrus, he willingly sacrifices his eye to Azurah for the benefit of the Khajiit. In the other known version, Azurah deems him as too afraid to have authority over a sphere and takes his second eye and Magrus is left blinded.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TSS-51)</sup>

Azurah was then said to have turned the eye into a stone known as the *Aether Prism*, which would reflect the Varliance Gate. This story is used to explain sunrise and sunset on Nirn, where it is said to be the daily opening and closing of the Aether Prism.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TSS-51)</sup> The [Sunspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunspire) is a holy temple dedicated to the Dragon God of Cats, [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh). It is located on the southern edge of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), near the [Tenmar Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tenmar_Forest) and it is where the deity fought [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ONSunspire-53)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ONAzin-54)</sup> Creatures known as [Solar Horses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Solar_Horse) are said to be associated with Magrus.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-DSH-55)</sup>

### Nede
The council of kings under [High King Durac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durac) of Skyreach, among seven other titles, had a Sun King. These kings served as representative leaders of the various tribes under Skyreach's jurisdiction.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-56)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-57)</sup> The Nedes of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) also held significance in the sun, feeling dismayed on the fifth of First Seed, [1E 393](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_393), when the sun darkened. They predicted a great event would occur after, which was proven correct within the year when the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) of [Malada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malada) emerged from their fane and afflicted the local farmland.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-58)</sup>

### Nord
Ancient Nords used glass-like sunstones to determine the sun's location even on a cloudy day. [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) was said to use one such device during his voyages.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ONAnti Sunstone-59)</sup> When it was still active, the [Meridian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia) monks of [Kilkreath Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kilkreath_Temple) in northwestern Skyrim conducted secret rituals during the feasts of High Sun and Low Sun. Travelers were advised to avoid the area during this time.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-GTWSH-60)</sup> In [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), a regional type of [cheese](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheese) called Bjenost is sliced into golden disks during the High Spring Fest. Locals claim it is meant to represent the sun in the springtime.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-COSTRTP-61)</sup> [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md)'s name is inspired by the sun as it rises over the town at dawn.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ARDawnstar-62)</sup> In southern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), there is a stronghold on a plateau called [Fort Sungard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Sungard) and it protects the road between [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and central Skyrim.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-SRSungard-63)</sup>

### Orc
The practice known as the death of sun and feathers was a form of execution practiced by the [Iron Orcs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Orcs). The sentenced was nailed to the stone and cut once for each of the ten iron oaths and then the sun burned until the crows gather. It was considered a proper death for liars and traitors.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Agganor.27s_Journal-64)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-LTB-65)</sup>

### Redguard
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/RG-misc-Glide_Sun_Compare_%28Glide%29.bmp/200px-RG-misc-Glide_Sun_Compare_%28Glide%29.bmp.png)](File_RG-misc-Glide_Sun_Compare_(Glide).bmp.md) [](File_RG-misc-Glide_Sun_Compare_(Glide).bmp.md) Daibethe The [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) refer to the sun as **Daibethe**. On the 1st of [Midyear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Midyear), the people of [Abibon-Gora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abibon-Gora) honor it through the festival of [Drigh R'Zimb](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drigh_R%27Zimb).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-2)</sup> The Redguard spirit of the air, [Tava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tava) is often associated with elements of the sun<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-TUAAYQ-66)</sup> and factions in reverence to her such as the Glinting Talons used references to the sun and light frequently.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-GT-67)</sup> The Redguards in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) would sometimes use the exclamation, *"by the Sun"*.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-DR-68)</sup>

The Pillar of the Singing Sun is a cenotaph dedicated to [Frandar Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frandar_Hunding). It is built on a small island in [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), specifically west of the [Ogres Tooth Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogres_Tooth_Mountains).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-AFBTW-69)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ONPillar-70)</sup> The town of [Sunkeep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunkeep) is located on the western coast between [Hegathe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hegathe) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ARSunkeep-71)</sup>

### Reachfolk
According to the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) legends every new year in [the vale](Lore_The_Reach.md) of [Karth](Lore_Karth_River.md) as autumn wanes the wolf aspect of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), the Storihbeg pursues the sun in an attempt to devour the orb and prevent the coming of the [New Life](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival). [Reachfolk Shamans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Shamans) perform the ritual to summon the great Hrokkibeg to interpose his might to drive the beast off and shorten the night. They believe that those who hate the sunlight dance rites to hinder the return of Magnus, but their attempts are doomed to fail.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-NLFI-72)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Auriel's Bow
[Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md), (also spelled **Auri-El's Bow**) is an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) used by the elven god [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel). Although it commonly takes the form of a modest elven [moonstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonstone) bow, it has also appeared made of [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md). Either way, it is one of the most powerful weapons in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

The bow draws its power from [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) itself, channeling it through the sun. It has the ability to turn any arrow into a "missile of death", although its enchantment effects vary: it has been known to cause magical fire or shock damage, drain an opponent's stamina and magicka reserves, or harness the power of the sun. It can also make the wielder immune to lesser attacks, and is especially devastating when used against the undead. Occasionally it can be seemingly unenchanted. Without Auriel's power behind it, however, the bow uses its own store of energy for its power. Once exhausted of this energy, the bow will vanish, abandoning its owner to reappear elsewhere.

## Notes
- "The jacinth wakes the rising sun" is a riddle of an obscure [alchemical](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy) formula<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TDOO-76)</sup> that can provide access to the [Fields of Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fields_of_Regret), the domain of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md). The "Rising Sun" refers to [Orc's blood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orc%27s_blood) and the "Jacinth" is [Amber](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amber).<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-RGIszara-77)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-NGS-78)</sup> The master conjurer, [Morian Zenas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morian_Zenas) labels the complete process a Door of Oblivion.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TDOO-76)</sup>
- Originally, the Sun was meant to set in the east and rise in the west, and the directions of east and west were intended to be reversed across the board in the *Elder Scrolls* setting. Remnants of this design can be found all throughout *Arena*. Ultimately this was inconsistent throughout the game's design, and was abandoned for future titles.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Ted Posts-79)</sup> Dawn's light rising in the west is mentioned in *[The Legend of Lovers Lament](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Legend_of_Lovers_Lament)* from *Daggerfall*, however.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-80)</sup>
