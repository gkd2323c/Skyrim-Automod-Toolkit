This is a list of all the known **[Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) names**, compiled from the games.

## Naming Convention
Khajiit names mostly describe the talents or skills of the to-be-named.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IWTB-1)</sup> In the games of [*Arena*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena) and [*Daggerfall*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall) Khajiit also had family names. However, since [*Redguard*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard) this concept is no longer followed. [*Legends*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends) once again introduced a Khajiit with a possible family name.

Sometimes Khajiit take a byname or nickname with meaning, either [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodilic.md) or in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md). They can be taken additionally to the Khajiiti name, either as suffix (e.g. [M'Aiq **the Liar**](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:M%27Aiq_the_Liar) or [Ra'zaym **Iron-Ear**](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Ra%27zaym_Iron-Ear)), or a prefix (e.g. [**Krin** Ren-dro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Krin_Ren-dro) or [**Sugar-Lips** Habasi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Sugar-Lips_Habasi)). Nicknames also can be used in appropriate situation, but note that they do not replace the name entirely. (e.g. [Eats-Spiders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Eats-Spiders) or [Kink-Tail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Kink-Tail)). In this case, they never have an honorific added.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IWTB-1)</sup>

The suffix *-morna* is added to the names of [Moon-Singers](Lore_Moon-Singers.md) and [Twilight Cantors](Lore_Twilight_Cantors.md) that have succumbed to the [Bent Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bent_Dance) of [Namiira](Lore_Namiira.md) and become a Rajaska, a special type of [dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

#### Honorifics
Another important aspect of Khajiit names are honorifics, more commonly among males than females. They reflect their status in life or society, and can change accordingly. Thus, it is considered improper and insulting to apply a wrong honorific.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IWTB-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KH-3)</sup> Commonly a Khajiit takes up just one honorific at a time, as using two either shows great ignorance or great pride.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IWTB-1)</sup> They also sometimes are given in terms of endearment, banter or deceit.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KH-3)</sup> Rarely, even non-Khajiit take up Khajiit honorifics.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ildrasai-daro-4)</sup>

Honorifics are usually a prefix before a Khajiit's name, usually separated by an apostrophe, uncommonly a hyphen, or rarely without separation. During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) however, suffixed honorifics were the norm, separated by a hyphen, with prefixed honorifics being seen as a fashionable fad among the youth of [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KH-3)</sup> If a honorific is taken as prefix, they can be shortened to one letter. Suffixed honorifics never are shortened. Ta'agra names can also contain apostrophes or hyphens, which does not necessarily make parts of names an honorific.

Capitalization in Khajiit names with honorifics varies: The name after a prefix can have either a capital or minuscule first letter, while suffixes are usually—but not always—in minuscule. Honorifics are usually gender-specific, though gender-swapped honorifics exist.

## Khajiit Surnames
Since [*Redguard*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard), Khajiit as a rule do not have family names, but in the earlier games they did. In [*Legends*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends), once more a Khajiit appeared whose surname may be a family name.

### Arena
[Ap'Kolthis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:People#Turamane_Ap.27Kolthis)

### Arena and Daggerfall
- Surnames for Khajiit in Arena and Daggerfall seem to consist of one of fourteen prefixes and one of fifteen suffixes.
