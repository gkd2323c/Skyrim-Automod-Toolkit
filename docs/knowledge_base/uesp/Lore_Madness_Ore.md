# Madness Ore

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/SI-vein-Madness_Ore_Deposit.jpg/200px-SI-vein-Madness_Ore_Deposit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-vein-Madness_Ore_Deposit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-vein-Madness_Ore_Deposit.jpg) A madness ore deposit (Shivering Isles) *I do delicate things with Madness Ore. It tingles across the flesh when it cuts.*
—Cutter [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/SR-item-Madness_Ore.jpg/200px-SR-item-Madness_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Madness_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Madness_Ore.jpg) Madness ore (Skyrim Creation Club) Madness Ore (often shortened to **madness**) is a flexible, dark black or brown metal marbled with glowing gold veins, which can be found in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIAppearance-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CC-2)</sup> Deposits occur naturally in ancient ruins left behind by previous [Greymarches](Lore_Greymarch.md), where the spirits of ancient souls<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SICutter-3)</sup> and the tattered minds of dead heroes<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EJCC-4)</sup> become trapped in the ore. Madness ore [smithing](Lore_Smithing.md) is an ancient art of the mortal inhabitants of the realm. The master smith of [Crucible](Lore_Crucible.md) has the ability to make heavy madness weapons and armor. Before the master dies, he will hide matrices throughout the realm and train an apprentice to continue the tradition. [Grummites](Lore_Grummite.md) are known to occupy places such as abandoned subterranean halls where Madness Ore is commonly found, due to their strange attraction towards the material;<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SICutter-3)</sup> indeed, they are known to collect shards of the ore.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Madness matrices are hidden throughout the Isles to soak up the magical essence of the realm. Using a matrix will produce an enchanted piece of equipment.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SICutter-3)</sup> The quality and enchantment power varies greatly between pieces of madness equipment, and is divided into eight grades: impure, unpolished, lesser, a standard grade, fine, very fine, grand and perfect. Impure madness equipment is little better than crude iron, while perfect madness equipment rivals even [Daedric](Lore_Daedric.md) or [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) Bone items.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIAppearance-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOMadness Equipment-6)</sup> Crafting madness armor requires madness ore to be treated callously, and runs the risk of turning the crafter mad. It must be hammered repeatedly and bent like sinew.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EJCC-4)</sup> At some stage in the crafting process, [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) is added. All this turns the initially soft and flexible metal into a hard one.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOMadness Equipment-6)</sup>

Like many things in the Shivering Isles, madness ore has a polar opposite: [amber](Lore_Amber.md). Heavy madness equipment is associated with [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md), while lightweight amber equipment is aligned with [Mania](Lore_Mania.md). The madness ore smith of Crucible is bitter rivals with the amber smith of [Bliss](Lore_Bliss_(place).md), both of whom vie to become the master smith of [New Sheoth](Lore_New_Sheoth.md). Similarly, the two materials also align with the rivalry between the [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md) and the [Aureal](Lore_Aureal.md); madness ore may be used to summon or even bind a Mazken, while amber can be used for the same purpose with an Aureal.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RAFECC-7)</sup> The two materials have been combined before, a notable example being [Nerveshatter](Lore_Nerveshatter.md), a hammer made with both amber and madness ore.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIAppearance-1)</sup>

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), chunks of madness ore and amber began to appear all over [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in seemingly impossible locations. This was caused by the breakdown of the barriers between the Shivering Isles and [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in the sewers beneath [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), where a manic mage named [Thoron](Lore_Thoron.md) had been experimenting on the [Sword of Jyggalag](Lore_Sword_of_Jyggalag.md). Thoron arranged to deliver these exotic materials to a master arcane blacksmith named Evethra, who managed to learn the secrets of crafting with both madness ore and amber, albeit losing her mind in the process.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EJCC-4)</sup> Both of these individuals were killed by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who used Evethra's notes to learn how to craft with these materials.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CC-2)</sup>
