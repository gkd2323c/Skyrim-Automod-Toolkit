The Third Empire (also called the **Septim Empire**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHot E-1)</sup> **Tamrielic Empire**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3Foreword-3)</sup> **Holy Cyrodiilic Empire of Tamriel**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WQ1-4)</sup> and the **Glorious Empire**)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue2-5)</sup> was declared by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) in [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854) after the chaos of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), and was forged through a decades-long conflict to unite [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) known as the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1C-6)</sup> It lasted throughout the whole of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) and two centuries into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), when it began to decline.

## Septim Dynasty
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png/200px-LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png) Emperor Tiber Septim The [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) was founded by Tiber Septim upon using [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) to conquer the whole of Tamriel, after which he declared the start of a new era, [3E 0](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_0).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHot E1-7)</sup> His rule of 38 years was prosperous and relatively peaceful. His grandson [Pelagius](Lore_Pelagius_I.md) succeeded him, and was poised to continue the golden age, until he was assassinated by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) after reigning for less than three years.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHot E1-7)</sup> Pelagius had no living children, thus the Ruby Throne passed to Tiber's niece, [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_I.md), in [3E 41](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_41). Kintyra and her son, [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_I.md), were successful rulers who achieved a level of unity within the Empire that would not be equaled for centuries.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BHot E4-8)</sup> [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_II.md), who took the throne in [3E 64](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_64), had his 18-year reign blighted by plagues and insurrections, and accomplished little during his rule. Instead, he left many problems, and a great deal of debt, to [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md). In response, Pelagius dismissed the entire [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and allowed only those who paid a princely sum to return to their seats. While this tactic brought some prosperity back to Tamriel, it ended when Pelagius was poisoned in [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98), purportedly by a disgruntled former Council member.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BWQ-9)</sup> His son, [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md), was more interested in debauchery than politics, known across the Empire as a womanizer even before taking the throne. His reign was marred by civil war and other conflicts, and the province of Summerset was almost lost to invading [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110). Antiochus died in [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120), leaving the throne to his daughter, [Kintyra II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md). The succession was contested by Antiochus' nephew and sister and would lead to civil war.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-WQ6-10)</sup>

In [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121), Kintyra II was imprisoned by [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), the infamous Wolf Queen of Solitude, and murdered two years later. This triggered the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), a civil war between Potema and her son [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_III.md) on one side, and Potema's brothers [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) on the other. Cephorus defeated Uriel III at the Battle of Ichidag, and Uriel III was captured but killed by a mob while en route to the Imperial City to stand trial, so Cephorus was proclaimed emperor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BWQ-9)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHot E-1)</sup>

The Empire gained and lost power and land unpredictably during the next century until the rise of [Katariah Ra'Athim](Lore_Katariah.md). The first (and only) Elven ruler of the Empire, Katariah was the first in 100 years to secure a steady, iron grip over most of Tamriel, save [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Ultimately, this would be the Empress' undoing, as she died in [3E 200](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_200) in a minor skirmish in Black Marsh.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHot E2-11)</sup>

Another civil war broke out after the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) voted to disinherit [Andorak](Lore_Andorak_Septim.md), the son of [Uriel IV](Lore_Uriel_IV.md), and offer the Imperial crown to his cousin [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_II.md) instead, as they considered Cephorus to be more closely related to the true Septim bloodline. The war lasted nine years until Andorak was mollified by the offer of the throne of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).

After acceding to the throne in [3E 268](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_268), [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_V.md) led the conquests of [Roscrea](Lore_Roscrea.md), [Cathnoquey](Lore_Cathnoquey.md), [Yneslea](Lore_Yneslea.md), and [Esroniet](Lore_Esroniet.md), as well as the Tamrielic [Invasion of Akavir](Lore_Invasion_of_Akavir.md), but he was killed during the invasion of Akavir.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DAI-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BHot E3-13)</sup> Uriel V's son [Uriel VI](Lore_Uriel_VI.md) was crowned after his father's death but was unable to take the throne until [3E 307](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_307), due to his young age at the time of his father's death and his mother Thonica's attempts to stay in power as regent. When Uriel VI fell from his horse and died in [3E 313](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_313), his half-sister came to power as Empress [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md). Morihatha slowly brought her rebellious vassals back under imperial control, and by the end of her reign in [3E 339](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_339), Tamriel was nearing a sort of unity. Her nephew, [Pelagius IV](Lore_Pelagius_IV.md), continued his aunt's work, bringing the Empire closer to unity than it had been since the days of Uriel I.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BHot E4-8)</sup>

[Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md), son of Pelagius IV, came to power after his father's death in 3E 368. In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), Uriel VII's battlemage, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), managed to imprison the Emperor in a dimension of Tharn's own creation. For ten years, a period later known as the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), Tharn ruled in Uriel's place, but showing none of the latter's wisdom. Before Tharn could entirely drive the Empire into the ground, Uriel was rescued and restored to the throne in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHot E-1)</sup>

Contemporarily to this period, some institutions of the Empire were suspended in so-called Pocket Universe, to achieve a state of immaculate virtual reality. One such institution was [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), the proving ground and sophisticated war college of prospective Imperial Battlemages; located deep in the ethereal plane.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-14)</sup>

The Empire recovered from Tharn's deceit, and Uriel VII united the Empire for the first time in centuries. By the end of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), and the [Vvardenfell Crisis](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the Empire was even closer to unity than at the end of Pelagius IV's reign. After his [assassination](Lore_Assassination!.md) in 3E 433, Uriel VII's illegitimate son, [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), ended the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the Third Era, and the Septim Dynasty.

[High Chancellor Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) became Potentate Ocato when no candidate to replace Martin could be found. Ocato kept the Empire more or less intact in the tumultuous years after the Oblivion Crisis, but was assassinated circa [4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15), which ushered in the seven-year [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RT-15)</sup>

## Mede Dynasty
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/LG-cardart-Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_02.jpg) Emperor Titus Mede II At some point in the seven years following Ocato's assassination, a Nibenese witch-warrior named [Thules the Gibbering](Lore_Thules_the_Gibbering.md) was crowned Emperor. Although he was not well liked by the people, the Elder Council favored him over [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md), a [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) warlord who sought the throne. Eventually, in 4E 22, Titus Mede captured the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) from Thules and crowned himself the first emperor of the [Mede Dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md), eventually persuading the Elder Council to accept him as a liberator rather than a conqueror.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RT-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOS-16)</sup>

During his rule, the province of Hammerfell was reclaimed, alongside the city-states of Leyawiin and Bravil.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RT-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TIC-17)</sup>

In 4E 168, Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) acceded to the throne. The Empire was at that time a shadow of its former glory. [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) had been lost to the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RT-15)</sup>, and Black Marsh had been lost to Imperial rule since the Oblivion Crisis. [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) had yet to fully recover from the eruption of Red Mountain, and had ceased to be an Imperial territory at some point prior to [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DBArano-18)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TIC-17)</sup><sup>:109</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOS-16)</sup><sup>:16</sup> [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) was plagued by infighting between the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md). Only [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) remained prosperous and peaceful.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TGW-19)</sup>

In 4E 171, the Great War began when armies from the Aldmeri Dominion invaded the Imperial provinces of Hammerfell and Cyrodiil after Titus II rejected an ultimatum to make massive concessions to the Thalmor. An army led by Thalmor general Lord [Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md) emerged from hidden camps in northern Elsweyr and assaulted southern Cyrodiil, flanking Imperial defenses along the Valenwood border. [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) fell to the invaders, and [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) was surrounded and besieged. Simultaneously, an army under Lady [Arannelya](Lore_Arannelya.md) crossed western Cyrodiil, bypassing [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and entering Hammerfell. This army was joined by smaller forces landing on Hammerfell's coast. Imperial troops were forced into retreat across the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md). The [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) was besieged, and fell after the Emperor fled with the remnants of his army to Skyrim. The war ended a year later with the liberation of the Imperial City after the [Battle Of The Red Ring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Red_Ring.md), and with the subsequent Imperial acceptance of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md). This treaty contained terms almost identical to those rejected before the war, specifically the outlaw of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship, which became one of the main causes of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in Skyrim. The Empire was forced to cede Hammerfell after the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) refused to give up large sections of their land, and the Redguards continued the war against the Dominion alone until the two sides signed the [Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_Second_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180). This left the Empire in a diminished and less stable condition, though it retained control of the provinces of Cyrodiil, High Rock, and Skyrim.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TGW-19)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Stormcloak Rebellion broke out in Skyrim. The belligerents were the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), who fought for the independence of Skyrim, and the Empire's Imperial Legion forces, led by General [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md), with the intention of holding the Empire together in the face of growing political tensions between them and the Thalmor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Eo S-20)</sup>

## Administrative Titles
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-nobilityfactionicon.jpg) The exact hierarchy of ranks, the nuances between them, and the trajectory of advancement has not been spelled out, but can generally be understood as categorized below.

- **[Emperor](Lore_Emperors.md)**, the ruler of the Tamrielic Empire who directly governs the [Imperial Province](Lore_Imperial_Province.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Pn P-21)</sup>
- **Potentate **, When the Elder Council is the ruler of the Tamrielic Empire in the absence of an Emperor. <sup>[[22]](#cite_note-RT3-22)</sup>
- **[High Chancellor](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md)** (sometimes **Imperial Battlemage**), the head of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> - **Councilor**, a member of the Elder Council.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Big Book-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Background Game-25)</sup> Dukes of Cyrodiil seemingly have seats on the Council.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TAC-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Official Fan Ob Int4-27)</sup>
- **Military Governor**, an Imperial Legion General appointed to be Governor of an Imperial Province.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TRB3-28)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFGDL-29)</sup>
- **Imperial Proconsul**, a non-military role that seemingly acts as the chief Governor of an Imperial Province.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Po M-30)</sup>
- **Provincial Governor**, A Governor of an Imperial Province. <sup>[[30]](#cite_note-PGE1p-31)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-32)</sup>
- **Prime Minister**, Head of the Government of the Province under the Emperor in Cyrodiil or Governor in another Province.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TRB3-28)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TIC-17)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOS-16)</sup>
- **Duke**, the Governor of a District within an Imperial Province.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-GTV-33)</sup>
- **Governor**, an individual seemingly appointed to be the government administrator of a region or outpost by the Emperor.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-DS-34)</sup>
- **Provisional Governor**, a Governor of a Region within an Imperial Province.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-PGE1h-35)</sup>

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-Emperor_Zero_36-0) Cuhlecain proclaimed himself emperor upon capturing the Imperial City, but he was assassinated before he could be formally crowned. His successor, Tiber Septim, later honored Cuhlecain as "[Emperor Zero](Lore_Cult_of_Emperor_Zero.md)".
2. [^](#cite_ref-Cuhlecain_38-0) Who was responsible for the assassination of Cuhlecain is controversial. The *Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition* states that Cuhlecain was assassinated by a nightblade from the Western Reach, while *[The Arcturian Heresy](Lore_The_Arcturian_Heresy.md)* states that Tiber Septim assassinated Cuhlecain and covered it up by claiming that a nightblade from the Western Reach was actually behind the assassination.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1C-6)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TAH-37)</sup>
3. [^](#cite_ref-PSIRt TS_40-0) There is some disagreement regarding Pelagius' relationship with Tiber Septim. A version of *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire,_Part_I)* and the *[Biography of Barenziah](Lore_Biography_of_Barenziah,_v_2.md)* refer to Pelagius as Tiber's son, but a revised version of *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire_v_1)*, *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition_All_the_Eras_of_Man.md)*, and *The Arcturian Heresy* refer to Pelagius as Tiber's grandson. Additionally, the original version of *[The Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah,_Part_VII.md)* refers to Pelagius as Tiber's son, while the revised [version of the book](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah,_v_4.md) instead refers to him as both Tiber's grandson and son, directly contradicting itself. *[The Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah,_v_3.md)* suggests that Tiber's wife, simply referred to as the Empress, had multiple children. The likeliest explanations include that Tiber and his wife had female children otherwise unrecorded in history. Other possibilities include that the Empress had children that were not Tiber's children, that the two of them had secret offspring, or simply a mistake on the part of the author. Regardless, the throne ended up with Kintyra I.
4. [^](#cite_ref-PSIIdod_45-0) *The Third Era Timeline* and *[Biography of the Wolf Queen](Lore_Biography_of_the_Wolf_Queen.md)* both state that Pelagius' reign ended in [3E 99](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_99); this is likely a rounded figure, however, as *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v3.md)* states that he died on the 15th of [Evening Star](Lore_Calendar.md#Evening_Star), [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98) (Evening Star is the last month in the year). *[The Daggerfall Chronicles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles)* also places the death of Pelagius in 3E 98.
5. [^](#cite_ref-Antiochus_47-0) *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v4.md)*, *The Third Era Timeline*, and *Biography of the Wolf Queen* state that Antiochus was crowned in 3E 99. *The Daggerfall Chronicles* states that he was crowned in 3E 98. And *Brief History of the Empire* states Antiochus assumed the throne twelve years prior to 3E 110, which also places his accession in 3E 98.
6. [^](#cite_ref-Ant D_50-0) *[The Wolf Queen, v5](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v5.md)* states that Antiochus fell into a coma in [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119) and that his funeral was in [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120). *[The Madness of Pelagius](Lore_The_Madness_of_Pelagius.md)* states that Antiochus died when Pelagius was just over a year old (Pelagius was born in 3E 119), which places Antiochus' death in 3E 120. Both books are contradicted by the *Biography of the Wolf Queen*, which explicitly places his death in [3E 112](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_112). The year 3E 120 can be assumed to be correct, since more sources state that to be the correct year.
7. [^](#cite_ref-KSII_53-0) There is uncertainty over the date of Kintyra's death. According to the *Biography of the Wolf Queen*, Kinytra was executed in [3E 114](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_114), but *The Third Era Timeline* refers to this date as the "reported death of Empress Kintyra II". *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen,_v7.md)* lists 3E 121 and [3E 125](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_125) as possible years for Kinytra's execution, while *The Third Era Timeline* and *[Broken Diamonds](Lore_Broken_Diamonds.md)* state that her death occurred on the 23rd of Frostfall, 3E 123. According to *Brief History of the Empire*, Kintyra was imprisoned for two years after her capture and then killed, placing her death in 3E 123. As more sources confirm 3E 123 as the year of Kintyra's death, this can be assumed to be correct.
8. [^](#cite_ref-Kat D_55-0) Both *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire_v_2)* and *The Madness of Pelagius* put the length of Katariah's reign at forty-six years, and *The Third Era Timeline* puts her death in 3E 200. A possible explanation of the stated length of Katariah's reign by *Brief History of the Empire* and *The Madness of Pelagius* could be that Katariah's reign began in late 3E 153 but ended in early 3E 200, meaning that her reign may have in reality lasted forty-six years and some months, but not quite forty-seven years.
9. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Cass D_56-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Cass D_56-1)***</sup> *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire)* makes two contradictory statements regarding Cassynder's year of death. First, it says that "in two years, [after his reign began] he was dead"; In the next volume, however, it contradicts itself by saying that Cassynder died "three years after [his accession]". It is likely that the second statement is incorrect, as *The Third Era Timeline* lists Cassynder's death as occurring in 3E 202, which is two years after he became emperor.
10. [^](#cite_ref-US4_57-0) *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire_v_2)* states that Uriel IV's reign lasted 43 years, but this is clearly an error since both *The Daggerfall Chronicles* and *The Third Era Timeline* place Uriel's death in 3E 247 and *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire_v_3)* places the accession of Uriel's successor Cephorus II in 3E 247.
11. [^](#cite_ref-CS2_59-0) Although *Brief History of the Empire* states Cephorus II became emperor in [3E 268](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_268), this is obviously an error and was corrected in a revised [version](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Brief_History_of_the_Empire,_v3) of the book. The same book states that Uriel Septim V also became emperor in 3E 268, so it is more logical to assume that 3E 247 is the correct year. *The Daggerfall Chronicles* and *The Third Era Timeline* both use 3E 247 as the date of Cephorus' accession.
12. [^](#cite_ref-C2lod_60-0) Cephorus is described as "a cousin more closely related to the original Septim line", although his precise line of descent is not clear.
13. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-PS4_62-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-PS4_62-1)***</sup> *Brief History of the Empire* states that Pelagius reigned for 49 years, but this has been acknowledged as an error - the correct span is 29 years.
14. [^](#cite_ref-Stormcrown_68-0) Ocato's death and the subsequent beginning of the Stormcrown Interregnum are never given precise dates. In *[Rising Threat](Lore_Rising_Threat,_Vol._IV.md)*, the accession of Titus Mede I and therefore the end of the Stormcrown Interregnum, which lasted for seven years, is placed around the same time as the Thalmor takeover of the Summerset Isles, which *[The Great War](Lore_The_Great_War_(book).md)* states occurred in 4E 22. Dating seven years back from this date yields the year 4E 15.
15. [^](#cite_ref-Attrebus_Mede_69-0) Although it is never stated that Attrebus Mede succeeded his father as emperor, it can be assumed that he did because he was crown prince as of 4E 48; there was no indication of other heirs to the throne at the time, and the Mede Dynasty remained the ruling imperial dynasty until at least the end of Titus II's reign.
16. [^](#cite_ref-Unknown_Medes_70-0) It is unknown how many Mede Emperors reigned between Attrebus and Titus II. Given the 142-year gap between Attrebus' birth in 4E 26 and Titus II's accession to the throne in 4E 168, and the fact that Titus II is stated to have inherited the Empire, it is inferable that at least one Mede reigned as emperor between Attrebus and Titus II.
