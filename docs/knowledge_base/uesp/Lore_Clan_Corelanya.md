# Corelanya Clan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/ON-place-Sunport_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Sunport_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sunport_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sunport_02.jpg) Sunport, the Corelanyan capital on Solstice The Corelanya Clan (also known as **House Corelanya**, **Corelanyan High Elves**, **Corelanyan Elves**, or simply the **Corelanyas** or **Corelanyans**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho CC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> is a group of [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) who reside on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice.md), primarily in the city of [Sunport](Lore_Sunport.md), located south of mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They follow a divergent form of [Velothi](Lore_Velothi.md) traditions, having chosen to remain behind during [Saint Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) ’s exodus from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) rather than join him. Eventually, the Corelanyans were exiled and relocated to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) around the [sixth century of the First Era](Lore_First_Era.md#Sixth_Century). There, they founded coastal settlements, often repurposing and expanding upon structures originally built by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ho CC2-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONThwarting-4)</sup> However, around [1E 853](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_853), the invading [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) forced them to abandon the mainland.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ho CC3-5)</sup> They then settled on the island the native [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) called Oztet-Ta (“Sprouting Root” in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md));<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SST-6)</sup> while this name persisted among the Argonians, the Corelanyans came to call it Solstice, with one scholarly interpretation claiming that it reflects the their connection to the Three Queens, with [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) as Day, [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) as Night, and [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) as the Dawn and Dusk that bridge them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Solstice LA-7)</sup>

## History

### Hammerfell Expansion
[![Banner of Sunport](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/ON-banner-Sunport.png/150px-ON-banner-Sunport.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Sunport.png) The earliest known history of the Corelanyans dates back to the time of Saint Veloth,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> who succeeded in converting Kinlady Valarmil Corela to the worship of the Daedra. However, when the Velothi movement faced suppression on Summerset, Saint Veloth was compelled to lead his followers to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) where they could practice their faith free from religious persecution. Some of Veloth’s followers feared venturing into the unknown, while others were held back by ties to their homeland, whether through wealth, family, or sworn oaths, and thus could not abandon Summerset. Among those that remained behind were the Corelanyans, who continued their worship in secret and eventually evolved into a noble house with considerable influence and assets.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ho CC1-8)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/ON-place-Salas_En.jpg/200px-ON-place-Salas_En.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Salas_En.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Salas_En.jpg) The Ayleid ruin of Salas En, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) When the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), known for its intolerance of Daedra worship, grew in significant influence, [High King Tenalarion](Lore_Tenalarion.md) reacted by imposing severe bans on Velothi beliefs. Facing the threats of being stripped of their property and being imprisoned, the Corelanyans chose self-exile. Around [1E 522](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_522), under the leadership of Sundavar Corelanya, they set sail for Hammerfell and established themselves in the ancient Ayleid city of [Salas En](Lore_Salas_En.md), which they declared their new capital.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ho CC1-8)</sup> Notably, Salas En housed a [portal](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) capable of transporting entire armies across the continent. This portal remained functional by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), maintaining a link with another gateway at Ne Salas in [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONPassage Denied-9)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONThwarting-4)</sup>

The Corelanyans flourished in Hammerfell, but they carefully maintained a stance of neutrality toward the High Kings and Queens of Summerset. They were friendly with [Clan Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) to the north and were active participants in the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) coastal trade.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ho CC1-8)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASEE-10)</sup> As the Alessians and Hammerfell's native desert tribes, such as the [Giant Goblins](Lore_Giant_Goblin.md), hindered their in-land expansion, the Corelanyans turned to the sea for further endevours.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ho CC2-3)</sup> Their merchant vessels roamed the Abecean Sea, and their intrepid explorers pushed eastward along distant shores, reaching as far as [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ho CC1-8)</sup> Around [1E 723](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_723), Kinheir Iniel dared to venture farther than any of her clansmen before her, discovering the remote island of Oztet-Ta<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SST-6)</sup> (later called Solstice). By [1E 726](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_726), she returned with a fleet intent on conquering it. Although her efforts were ultimately thwarted by the island's native Argonians, she succeeded in plundering the abandoned citadel of Mor Naril, unearthing its secrets of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ho CC2-3)</sup> Despite this conflict, Corelanyan merchants continued to trade with the wary Argonians of the island.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ho CC4-11)</sup> Iniel would eventually rise to the rank of Kinlady, but her actions alienated potential allies, support she would later come to realize was crucial in the conflict ahead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ho CC2-3)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/51/ON-place-Ash%27abah_Pass.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ash%27abah_Pass.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ash%27abah_Pass.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ash%27abah_Pass.jpg) Ash'abah Pass, the last stand of the Hammerfell Corelanyans In [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808) invading [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md), known as the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), landed on the shores of Hammerfell. With the wars against the [Lefthanded Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md) in Yokuda still fresh in their minds, they set out to eradicate the Corelanya elves wherever they found them.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONloadash-12)</sup> The second warrior wave from the island of [Yath](Lore_Yath.md) was largely responsible for the attacks on the Corelanya.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASEE-10)</sup> In [1E 853](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_853) they were led by King [Xakhwan](Lore_Xakhwan.md), who in a final push to drive the Corelanya from Hammerfell, sent his two sons to kill their leader. Prince Haqmir and Sameer led the charge into the Ash'abah Pass, and were successful in killing Kinlady [Iniel Corelanya](Lore_Iniel_Corelanya.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONPH-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONTI-14)</sup> Unexpectedly, the Corelanya tricked the Yokudans into leaving their King vulnerable. A necromancer named Wraithmaster [Venerien](Lore_Venerien.md) killed King Xakhwan and raised his corpse. Desperate to survive, the Corelanya also began to raise the corpses of their own dead to fight back. Ultimately, their resistance was unsuccessful and the Corelanya were slaughtered by the Yokudans.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONTI-14)</sup>

Only a few Corelanyans survived the sacking of Salas En.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ho CC2-3)</sup> However, what followed was several months of the Ra Gada hunting down and slaughtering any Corelanya they could find throughout Hammerfell, furious over their dead being raised against them. Fortunately, a third of House Corelanya had settled in overseas mercantile outposts, escaping the Ra Gada’s reach. However, with their lands in Hammerfell lost and their outposts unsustainable, the Corelanya returned to Summerset, where they still held some property obtained during their mercantile empire. During this time, Earlenque Corelanya, cousin to Iniel, became their leader. Iniel's Daedric ties and use of necromancy, however, damaged the house’s standing. Fearing potential political fallout among his people and reluctant to start a war with the Ra Gada, High King Naurnindon distanced himself from the Corelanyans. After years of failed diplomacy, Earlenque allied with Kinlady Arlinwe of Firsthold in an unsuccessful attempt to install her as High Queen. In [1E 909](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_909), Naurnindon gave the Corelanya a choice: surrender their nobility and assets or face exile. They chose exile, but they took many of Arlinwe’s followers with them.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ho CC3-5)</sup>

### Solstice Relocation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg/200px-ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg) Solstice [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/ON-load-Solstice.png/200px-ON-load-Solstice.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Solstice.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Solstice.png) Sunport The Corelanyans had multiple holdings they could move to, but they chose Solstice as their new home for its seclusion, ample space for growth, and distance from any territory under the High King of Summerset’s rule.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ho CC3-5)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ho CC4-11)</sup> Around [1E 909](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_909), they arrived to find that the more advanced Argonian tribes had relocated to eastern Solstice. This left the western bay open for Earlenque to establish the city of Sunport atop the Argonian ruins of Tluthik-Tzel<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SST-6)</sup> and assume the title of Kinlady. Determined not to repeat Iniel's mistakes, she prioritized maintaining neutrality with the island's native inhabitants. This gave the Corelanyans time to grow stronger and build Sunport's defenses. One of the earliest settlements built by the Corelanya on the island was [Tuniria](Lore_Tuniria.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Morlia-15)</sup> However, when Earlenque passed in [1E 909](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_909), her son Orelmar took on a different stance when he became kinlord. The Corelanyans initially only expanded on unoccupied territories, but their growing ambitions eventually brought them into confrontation with the native inhabitants. While the Tide-Born Argonians were quickly overpowered by the Altmer, the Stone-Nest Argonians proved to be formidable opponents. The ensuing bloodshed marked the beginning of a conflict that would span two centuries, claiming the lives of [Psynande](Lore_Psynande_Corelanya.md), [Kevarian](Lore_Kevarian_Corelanya.md), and several other kinlords. It was not until the rise of Kinlady [Torinwe](Lore_Torinwe_Corelanya.md) that a call for peace was finally championed. In [1E 1244](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1244), the Peace of Xor-Hist was established between the Corelanyans and the Stone-Nest tribes. The treaty officially recognized Corelanya control over their current territories, prohibited the construction of permanent settlements within each other's lands, and granted both peoples the freedom to travel across the island. Remarkably, these borders are still honored to the present day.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ho CC4-11)</sup>

It is said that the Tide-Born tribe helped broker the peace between the Corelanyans and the Stone-Nest, and that without them, peace wouldn't have been possible. The Corelanyans began to have a strong and fruitful relationship with the Tide-Born and Stone-Nest tribes. Both tribes knew the ebb and flow of Solstice weather well. The wet storms that blew in from the sea were devastating to early Corelanyan architecture, and the tribes were the ones that taught them the spells, wards, and building techniques to bear up under the weather of the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md). They also taught the elves the ways to husband fruits, plants, and animals that would thrive under the Solstice sun, that could withstand the storms and surf.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Solstice LA-7)</sup>

In the later years of the First Era, tensions within the Corelanya clan began to rise. In [1E 2631](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2631), [Istamil Corelanya](Lore_Istamil_Corelanya.md), who was held in check by his elder brother Kinlord [Ryethamil](Lore_Ryethamil_Corelanya.md), murdered him and seized his power. This act of fratricide plunged the region into civil war, pitting members of the Corelanya family against each other. By [1E 2633](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2633), Istamil had lost many allies, with only the College of Rituals backing his claim. In a desperate bid to maintain his control, he turned to the Daedric Prince Molag Bal at Mor Naril. Empowered by this dark pact, Istamil unleashed Daedra and violent necromancy upon his enemies. In response, some native Argonians allied with his opposition, determined to dispose of him. His reign came to an end when his cousin Vinutilmo Corelanya, guided by Azura, Meridia, and Nocturnal, confronted and slew him within the Temple of the Final Dark. With Istamil vanquished, the gates of Mor Naril were sealed, and the Corelanyans returned to normalcy.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ho CC4-11)</sup>

Vinutilmo laid an example for future Corelanya to follow, popularizing the worship of his patrons under the Three Queens name. He also retired the title of Kinlord and Kinlady, instead making way for regents who ruled through power that they are entrusted with.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ho CC4-11)</sup> During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the clan still lived on the island of Solstice, primarily in the city of Sunport.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho CC-1)</sup>

## Religion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens.jpg/200px-ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens.jpg) Cathedral of the Three Queens [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg) Shrine dedicated to the Three Queens The Corelanyans have a unique religious tradition, being descended from Altmer who embraced the dissident teachings of the prophet Veloth. While most of the Elves who followed Veloth eventually came to venerate the living gods of the Tribunal, the Corelanyans (like the Ashlanders of Morrowind) continue to revere Daedric powers.

While the Corelanyans of old had dealings with a number of Daedric powers, during their long exile on Solstice their faith coalesced around three particular Daedra: [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). The Elves of Solstice refer to them as the Queen of Light, the Queen of Dusk, and the Queen of Darkness. These Three Queens reign over all aspects of life, both the good and the bad.

Meridia, The Queen of Light, is a ruling goddess who grants each mortal potential and purpose. She is a goddess of light, life, and order, the arbiter of all things as they are meant to be. The Elves of Solstice strive to live up to Meridia's perfection, knowing they fall short but loving her nonetheless.

Azura, The Queen of Dusk, comforts and guides mortals through the vicissitudes of life. A goddess of beauty, love, and fortune, she is seen as an intercessor for mortals with her sterner sisters.

Finally, Nocturnal, the Queen of Darkness gathers mortals into her embrace at the end of their days and judges them. Nocturnal presides over death and oversees final justice. The Elves of Solstice respect Nocturnal, but do not love her; she is a stern judge. However, those who live their lives well have nothing to fear when the Queen of Darkness comes for them.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vo FS-16)</sup>

The Corelanyans believe that the Three Queens teach them the cyclical nature of their clan is the circle of history writ large. Dawn, Dusk, and back again, is a cycle that they see as a beautiful truth for a complicated world. The Corelanyans also believe that the Three Queens are allied in their love and support of their clan, though their support for and alignment with each other is a subject of much debate in their temples.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Solstice LA-7)</sup>

Along with the Three Queens, another Daedric Prince who appeals to certain members of Solstice's population is Sanguine, the Prince of Revelry. Because Sunporters tend to enjoy a good time and aren't overly judgmental, Revelers of the Rose have not only flourished, they've found acceptance as they allow anyone who wishes to attend their festivities.

Brought to Sunport by Imperial traders from Leyawiin, the patron of commerce and crafting, Zenithar, has also found a strong following among the merchants and traders of Sunport.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vo FS-16)</sup> Some Corelanyan Altmer also followed [Mara](Lore_Mara.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Nuralanya-17)</sup>

## Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/ON-creature-Three-Toed_Sloth.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Three-Toed_Sloth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Three-Toed_Sloth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Three-Toed_Sloth.jpg) A sloth The Corelanyans are a peaceful people, and they have laid down their swords long ago. They don't have a standing army, as they have made peace with the Argonians a long time ago, and share their island with peaceful merchants and seafarers.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Cirellor-18)</sup>

Sunport is one of the, and perhaps the only, High Elf settlement that doesn't attack [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) on sight. Many brave and adventurous Sea Elves risk their ship and crew to make their way to Solstice, for trade there is good, weather is fair, and the people welcome them and show no fear.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Firyanume-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Sarragaer-20)</sup>

Corelanyan cuisine is exotic and flavorful, and Altmer cuisine in comparison has been regarded as bland with muted flavors. It is nearly impossible to grow wheat in Solstice, as the soil is too sandy or soft to support their cultivation. Instead, while some nobles have managed to establish private wheat fields for their personal use, most people in Solstice utilize a common cereal grain known as [saiewin](Lore_Saiewin.md). Saiewin noodles, bread, sweetrolls, and batter for frying fish are just some of the ways Corelanyans utilize this grain. Saiewin is made from saie blades, which resembles a light green grass that grows in rows of stalks that can reach as high as a tall Altmer. The young stalks are rigid and have a high starch content. As the plants age, they sprout reddish brown flowers with strands of spiky seeds. Once these growths sprout, the flowers turn the starchy blades bitter and the spiky seeds are poisonous to everyone except Argonians. The saie blades must be harvested while they are young and reach between two and six feet in height, which usually takes a year, and they are best harvested in winter.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-CC-21)</sup>

It is said that a young acolyte created a special sauce for a festival on Azura's worship day. The sauce was so well received that it is said that Azura herself took him to her realm of [Moonshadow](Lore_Moonshadow.md) and rewarded him. Later, the recipe was adjusted to add ingredients to honor Meridia and Nocturnal as well, creating the fabulous and versatile sauce known as Queens Sauce. The ingredients were Midnight Peppercorns for Nocturnal, Dusk Dillweed for Azura, and Sunleaves for Meridia.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-CC-21)</sup>

Delvers of underground spaces often bring back small pieces of Bismuth that are used to make flower-laden Tiaras. Merchants have been interested in bringing back these baubles to Tamriel as beautiful curiosities.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Tiara-22)</sup>

The [sloths](Lore_Sloth.md) of Solstice have long been favored pets of Sunport residents, especially children. It's not uncommon for teachers to perform "sloth checks" to make sure enterprising students aren't attempting to use their bags to smuggle sloths into lessons. Manuals on sloth care were widely circulated across the island.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TS2-23)</sup> Some ceramic bowls used for children's morning meals were decorated with sloth imagery,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TC-24)</sup> Sloth shaped dolls fashioned from seaweed, stone and sticks were a common sight on the island.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TS-25)</sup> Businesses were occasionally named after them, such as the Sleepy Sloth Inn in Sunport, where the creatures could also be seen lounging about.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-VGTWS-26)</sup>

### The Collegium Praxis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/ON-npc-Morlia.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Morlia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Morlia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Morlia.jpg) A member of the College of Tomes The Collegium Praxis are the premier organization of guilds and societies sponsored by the Regency of Sunport. They are equivalent to the Guilds of mainland Tamriel. Each of the colleges are equal parts professional guild, secret-society lodge, and place of learning. It is a common misunderstanding that the Collegium Praxis are like education colleges. They are a series of interconnected guilds that, while they train their novices and apprentices, they are not colleges in the educational sense.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TCP-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Tazurr-28)</sup>

**The College of Sea and Sword** serves as the premier guild for merchants, explorers, and warriors on Solstice. It gives its members a chance to explore the world beyond the limits of Sunport. They train their adepts, novices, and apprentices to endure strenuous expeditions across the island and even out to sea. Higher-ranking marines fend off threats lurking in the sea or within the depths of the jungle, and their naval force keeps marauders and unfriendly pirate vessels at bay. The explorers guide ships through the treacherous sea around Solstice and investigate previously unexplored sites across the island. Finally, the merchants of this college help transport and sell goods in distant ports, returning with cargo not readily available on our small island. <sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TCP-27)</sup>

**The College of Tomes** hold rigorous study and endless practice as the duties of their guild of mages and scholars. Research, rituals, and the arcane focus that protects Sunport are the stock and trade of this arcane union. The College of Tomes uses the ruins of [Tuniria](Lore_Tuniria.md), an old Corelanyan settlement, as a training ground for their members.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TCP-27)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Morlia-15)</sup>

**The College of Gardens** is a guild of herbalists, agriculturists, and healers, and they play a vital role in the health and well-being of Sunport and the surrounding Solstice countryside. They also maintain cordial relations with their Tide-Born neighbors. Some believe this college sprang from the Corelanyans desire to mend the hurt their clan inflicted but prior to arriving on Solstice and when they first settled on the island. Not only do they play a vital role in keeping the people of Sunport fed, they also help maintain the natural beauty of the island.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TCP-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Tazurr-28)</sup>

**The College of Stone** is a guild of builders, crafters, and artisans of all descriptions. Its roots date back to the clan's earliest days in Summerset to when they took possession of parts of Hammerfell in the First Era. Now, members of the guild are responsible for expanding and maintaining Sunport's infrastructure, as well as creating art and trade goods for market. The guild began as a society of stoneworkers, but they have long since encouraged aspiring artisans to craft with whatever material suits their purpose. Stone for them means solid and well-built, in whatever medium they choose to employ.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TCP-27)</sup>

**The College of Rituals** serves as the keepers of tradition and law, and most of its members are incredibly devoted to the ideals of legislation and the rule of law. While they help the regent govern Sunport, rumors say that a certain element within the guild stills entertains traditions that the clan abandoned long ago, that certain forces in their leadership want to see a return to their darker, Daedric and necromantic past.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TCP-27)</sup>
