Jagar Tharn ([/ˈ dʒeɪɡɑɹ θɑɹn/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ***jay** -gar tharn* Link ⏵)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CES-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSAd-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBJd-4)</sup> was the [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) and later Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He secretly imprisoned Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and used [Illusion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion) magic to masquerade as Uriel VII for a decade.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSAd-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AIS-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup> This period, from [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) to [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), became known as the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), during which Tharn reigned over the war-torn [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3All-8)</sup> It is not entirely known what Tharn's goals and personal accomplishments were during his reign.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup>

## Biography

### Background
*We [Tharns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Tharn) have held positions of power throughout [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) since the days of the [Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md). We are prized for our loyalty to the Empire, our deft political machinations, and our ruthless subjugation or elimination of dissenters within Imperial territories. What we do is grim work, but it is necessary if the Empire is to endure.*
— [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn), Grand Chancellor of the Elder Council<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-COTFC4-9)</sup> Jagar Tharn was the descendant of [House Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Tharn) of [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md), a prestigious [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) family; perhaps having ancestry tying him back to the prolific [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) of the [Age of Heroes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Age_of_Heroes).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-HTo N-10)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GIESO2012-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Wiki ESOLW-12)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-GZPosts-13)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-U42AUA-14)</sup> Another source states that Tharn was born in southern [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) mother, that he lacked human ancestry, and that his racial traits were a result of interracial relationships between [elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elf), being part [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), part [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), and perhaps part something else as well.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup> Additionally, he was described as a descendant of the [Ra'athim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra%27athim) Clan of [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart) and King [Moraelyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moraelyn).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup> Ultimately, Tharn was said to be of a newly emerging race, which had been bred into existence over many generations, much like the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) that emerged from breeding between men and mer long ago.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TPP-16)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

At some point prior to his appointment as [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md), Tharn served as a mage-priest at the [Temple of Sethiete](Lore_Brotherhood_of_Seth.md) in [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mo T4-17)</sup> Tharn was known to align himself with [The Dark](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Dark), better known as [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) or [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TLAD-18)</sup><sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Forum Madness-19)</sup> Tharn was more than likely a member of the [Spire Council](Lore_Battlespire.md#Spire_Council), the administrative body of the [Imperial Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), as it was customary at this time for the future Imperial Battlemage to be selected from their ranks.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:17</sup>

### Theft of the Staff of Chaos
[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/29/AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) The Staff of Chaos When Tharn began planning the theft of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) is unclear. During his time as a mage-priest at the Temple of Sethiete, Tharn stole information on powerful spells, the location of the Staff of Chaos, and how the staff could be used.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mo T4-17)</sup> Circa [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376), a bard known as the Nightingale seduced Queen [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and she revealed the location of the Staff of Chaos to the Nightingale, who then stole the staff from its sanctuary beneath the city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RFT-21)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRB4-22)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-24)</sup> Barenziah later recognized the Nightingale to be Jagar Tharn.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup>

However, an alternative account asserts that the bard who stole the Staff of Chaos was Drayven Indoril, a master thief and member of the [Nightingale Trinity](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md) who Tharn hired to steal the staff, and that the truth was covered up for political reasons. The account also claims that when Indoril gave the staff to Tharn, he attempted to kill Indoril, but his Nightingale abilities allowed him to escape. Tharn then began searching for Indoril, but he eventually abandoned the search.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-24)</sup> Nevertheless, both versions of events state that it was Tharn who was ultimately behind the theft and took possession of the Staff of Chaos.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-24)</sup>

Soon after the theft of the Staff of Chaos, Barenziah's consort [Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus) sent urgent communiqués to [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRB4-22)</sup> Uriel VII expressed shock and displeasure at the staff's theft and urged Symmachus to make every effort to locate its whereabouts and communicate developments to the newly appointed Imperial Battlemage, Jagar Tharn, who was tasked with tracking down the staff and those responsible for stealing it.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup>

### Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel
During the two decades after Uriel VII acceded to the [Ruby Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne) in [3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368), he expanded and consolidated Imperial influence and culture throughout the Empire, especially in Morrowind and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DC-25)</sup> As Uriel VII's close advisor, Tharn provided shrewd counsel and arcane support to complement the Emperor's ambition and agile mind in these endeavors. Uriel VII and the Empire greatly benefited from Tharn's arcane powers and wisdom.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup>

Uriel VII eventually surpassed his teacher at balancing the skills of threat and diplomacy, and Tharn's role became less important as time went on. It is believed that he carried on the façade of an outpaced counselor in order to feed Uriel VII's ego and self-assurance and earn the Emperor's complete trust, all the while Tharn executed his own plans in the background.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup>

At some point, Tharn began planning to usurp Uriel VII. In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), Tharn attended the [Mid Year's festival](Lore_Holidays.md#Mid_Year) in the Emperor's banquet hall.<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup> At the festival, Tharn listened to the leader of the [Imperial Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Guard), General [Talin Warhaft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talin_Warhaft), tell an anecdote to Princess [Ariella Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ariella_Septim) about when his friend, Marten the Blade, used an illusion spell to change his own appearance into that of another, so that Marten might trick a group of bandits into thinking he was their leader. This anecdote served as the inspiration Tharn needed to complete his plan and finally take Uriel VII's place on the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup>

As a cover story for his future absence, he requested a leave of absence to pursue magical studies.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> Uriel VII was troubled by the potential absence of his powerful and wise Imperial Battlemage, but nonetheless, he accepted the request, wished him success in his endeavors, and asked only that Tharn select a replacement Imperial Battlemage for the duration of his absence.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup> Tharn appointed [Ria Silmane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ria_Silmane), his oldest apprentice and a powerful mage, to fill his position.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> The appointment was fully approved by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md).<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup>

### Betrayal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/AR-scene-Intro_07.jpg/200px-AR-scene-Intro_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_07.jpg) Tharn's betrayal of Uriel VII [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/AR-scene-Intro_10.jpg/200px-AR-scene-Intro_10.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_10.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_10.jpg) Tharn kills Ria Silmane Tharn plotted for months to bring his plan to fruition.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AIS-6)</sup> Later in 3E 389, he requested to meet with the Emperor to discuss rumors of treachery.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AIS-6)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DC-25)</sup> However, when Uriel VII and General Warhaft arrived to discuss the rumors of treachery, Tharn used the Staff of Chaos to imprison them in an [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) [plane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) of his own creation.<sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_nb4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AIS-6)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:180</sup> Time moved much slower in this plane of Oblivion; thus, it would be centuries before Uriel VII died, and he would experience nothing but inexplicable and haunting nightmares.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ARMISEV-27)</sup> Tharn refrained from killing Uriel VII because the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) would have warned the Elder Council of the Emperor's death.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ARMISEV-27)</sup> When Ria Silmane learned of what Tharn had done to Uriel VII, she attempted to inform the Elder Council, but Tharn used the Staff of Chaos to kill her before she could inform them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AIS-6)</sup>

With his plot successful, Tharn drained the Staff of Chaos of its power and imbued its energy, along with his own life force, into the [Jewel of Fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jewel_of_Fire).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AJTd-28)</sup> Then, he divided the staff into eight pieces, and hid them throughout the Empire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> He took these precautions because the Staff of Chaos was the only item that could be used to free Uriel VII from Oblivion and because if someone did discover his deception, they would have to recover the pieces and reassemble the staff, only to discover it to be useless without the Jewel of Fire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AJTd-28)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg/200px-AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg) Tharn assuming the appearance of Uriel VII Tharn used Illusion magic to assume the appearance of Uriel VII, and then he took the imprisoned Emperor's place on the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AIS-6)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup> He dismissed Uriel VII's inner circle and replaced the Imperial Guards with [Fire Daemons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fire_Daemon), whom Tharn cast an illusion spell on to give them the appearance of mortal Imperial Guardsmen.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AIS-6)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-26)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ARMISEV-27)</sup> After his usurpation of Uriel VII, he exiled Uriel VII's wife, [Caula Voria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caula_Voria), to the [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One) and sent the imprisoned Emperor's children to various prisons across Tamriel.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup>

### Emperor of Tamriel and Imperial Simulacrum
*-old troubles resurfaced, forgotten grudges rekindled, and wars flared throughout the land. In the east, Morrowind attacked Black Marsh in the [Arnesian War](Lore_Arnesian_War.md); in the north, [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) battled [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) in the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md); in the south, [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) took arms against [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) in the [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md); in the west, Valenwood also lost land to its old ally [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in the [War of the Blue Divide](Lore_War_of_the_Blue_Divide.md).*
— [*Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition: All the Eras of Man*](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition_All_the_Eras_of_Man.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/AR-misc-Arena-Birthday-Jagar-Tharn-Crop.jpg/200px-AR-misc-Arena-Birthday-Jagar-Tharn-Crop.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-misc-Arena-Birthday-Jagar-Tharn-Crop.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-misc-Arena-Birthday-Jagar-Tharn-Crop.jpg) Jagar Tharn on the Ruby Throne [![](https://images.uesp.net/5/5e/SK-narrative-Start2.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-narrative-Start2.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-narrative-Start2.png) Jagar Tharn The Empire suffered terribly during Tharn's decade-long reign, a period later known as the Imperial Simulacrum.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ASEEHA-29)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Es AC-30)</sup> Tharn's neglect and mismanagement of Imperial affairs resulted in a steady decline in the Empire's economic prosperity.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Es AC-30)</sup> The decline of Imperial authority in the [provinces](Lore_Tamriel.md#Provinces) that accompanied this neglect and mismanagement caused many of the petty lords and kings to take advantage of the situation by engaging in [wars](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) of expansion.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3All-8)</sup> However, little is known of what Tharn sought to accomplish during this time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup> It is known, however, that Tharn instituted a crackdown on political dissidents "and other undesirables."<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-31)</sup>

Circa [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), a [shadowmage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Magic) named [Skelos Undriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skelos_Undriel) was driven from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) by Tharn's agents and fled to the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md), a region at the time engulfed in the War of the Bend'r-mahk.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TESTS-32)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Sk SUd-33)</sup> Tharn ordered his agents to track down Undriel.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TESTS-32)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Sk SUd-33)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SKBS-34)</sup> Undriel hid in the village of [Azra's Crossing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azra%27s_Crossing), where bandits employed by Tharn captured him, but he was later rescued.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TESTS-32)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Eo RP-35)</sup>

In 3E 397, Tharn and a shadowmage named [Pergan Asuul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pergan_Asuul) vied to gain control of the [Umbra' Keth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umbra%27_Keth), a "Shadow of Conflict", which was created by the ongoing War of the Bend'r-mahk.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SKBS-34)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Sk SUd-33)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Sk Co H3-36)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Nym-37)</sup> To strengthen the Shadow of Conflict and advance Imperial influence in the region, Tharn bribed many prominent families in Hammerfell to support Skyrim in the war.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-SKRS-38)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Eo BP-39)</sup> To further strengthen the Umbra' Keth, he provided funding for a bandit leader named Lakvan to kill soldiers fighting on the side of Hammerfell.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SKBS-34)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-SKRS-38)</sup> Tharn's plans to gain control of the Umbra' Keth were foiled when it was destroyed.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Sk Co H3-36)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Sk MQ-40)</sup>

In [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396), Tharn made a deal with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESBT-41)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:18</sup> Tharn gave the Battlespire to Mehrunes Dagon, and in exchange, Dagon's forces were required to slay the five [battlemage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Battlemages) administrators who governed the Battlespire.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESBT-41)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Es AC-30)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:18</sup> [Xivilai Moath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivilai_Moath) helped engineer Tharn's involvement in the fall of the Battlespire.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:158</sup> He made this deal to eliminate the competition for his position as Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel, as the Battlespire administrators were customarily selected for the position.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:17</sup> The deal also served as partial recompense for the assistance that Mehrunes Dagon provided Tharn in his usurpation of Uriel VII.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSAd-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBJd-4)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-OOGG-42)</sup><sup>:2</sup> He had an association with a worshipper of Mehrunes Dagon, the mage Carecalmo, who was involved in Tharn's plot against Uriel VII.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-MLVd-43)</sup>

In [3E 398](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_398), Tharn sent one of his retainers, [Sirran Angada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sirran_Angada), to the Battlespire.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSAd-3)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESBT-41)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-BJTL-44)</sup> Angada used a special transmorph spell to take on the appearance of Lomegan Mariel, Uriel VII's Personal Liaison to the Battlespire, which allowed him to infiltrate the facility.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-BJTL-44)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-BPBd-45)</sup> At the Battlespire, Angada bribed [Paxti Bittor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Paxti_Bittor) to betray the Battlespire to Mehrunes Dagon's forces, and Angada gave the facility's portal keys to the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) invaders.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-BPBd-45)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Lft BH-46)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-BLt M-47)</sup> The invasion largely accomplished Tharn's goal, as only [Clarentavious Valisious](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clarentavious_Valisious) survived.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-BCVd-48)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:92</sup> After Mehrunes Dagon's forces captured the Battlespire, Tharn assigned Angada to read the vagaries of the [magicka](Lore_Magic.md) tides for the Prince of Destruction.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSAd-3)</sup> An [apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) later used Mehrunes Dagon's [neonymic](Lore_Nymic.md) to banish him, but when the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) recaptured the Battlespire in the aftermath of the invasion, the facility was in ruins.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-BEo L7-49)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TDo O-50)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-FAo TL-51)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:180</sup>

### Downfall
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/AR-endcut2-Deluxe.png/200px-AR-endcut2-Deluxe.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-endcut2-Deluxe.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-endcut2-Deluxe.png) The Jewel of Fire As a consequence of the banishment of Mehrunes Dagon, Tharn no longer had Dagon to bolster his regime.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Es AC-30)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BA-20)</sup><sup>:180</sup> When Tharn used the Staff of Chaos to kill Ria Silmane, he destroyed her corporeal form, but he made the costly mistake of not destroying her spirit.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AJTd-28)</sup>

According to both the official biography *[Biography of Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Biography_of_Barenziah)* and the scandalous "unofficial" biography of *[The Real Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Real_Barenziah)*, circa [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391), the spirit of Silmane appeared in the dreams of [Eadwyre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eadwyre), King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), and told him of what Tharn had done to her.<sup>[[nb 5]](#intnote_nb5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RFT-21)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> Silmane also told Eadwyre about a [prisoner](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) and friend of hers who had the potential to defeat Tharn.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> This prisoner was formerly a minor member of Tharn's Imperial Court, and for this reason, he did not view them as a threat.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup> Eadwyre divulged Silmane's information to Barenziah, who had suspicions about the Emperor after an audience with him, and the monarchs formulated a plan to gain Tharn's confidence.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> Tharn was aware of the fact that Barenziah and Eadwyre had deduced that he was not Uriel VII.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup> Nonetheless, Barenziah managed to charm and eventually befriend him, which allowed her to slowly learn the locations of the eight pieces of the Staff of Chaos.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> Barenziah also obtained a copy of the key to the cell of Silmane's friend and bribed a guard to leave the key there.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup> The unofficial biography also claims Barenziah became pregnant with Tharn's son during this time.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup>

In [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), Silmane entered the dreams of the prisoner and informed them of what needed to be done to defeat Tharn and restore Uriel VII to the throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ARMISEV-27)</sup> After receiving this information, Silmane's champion escaped their cell by using the ruby key left by the bribed guard, and they escaped the prison by traveling through a Shift Gate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARRd-1)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ARMISEV-27)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-AEf P-52)</sup> Silmane's champion was indefatigable and unstoppable in their journey across Tamriel to retrieve the pieces of the Staff of Chaos.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-AEot MQ-53)</sup> As the champion retrieved each piece of the staff, Tharn used magical visions to threaten and taunt them and sent his minions to try and defeat them.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AJTd-28)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-AEot MQ-53)</sup> Finally, the champion retrieved the final piece of the Staff of Chaos from the mountain of [Dagoth-Ur](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), but when they reassembled the staff, Tharn mocked them and told them that he had drained the staff of its power.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AJTd-28)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ADU-54)</sup> He taunted Silmane's champion to confront him in the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and retrieve the Jewel of Fire.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AJTd-28)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/AR-flc-NUJAGDTH.GIF/200px-AR-flc-NUJAGDTH.GIF)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-flc-NUJAGDTH.GIF) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-flc-NUJAGDTH.GIF) The death of Jagar Tharn The champion entered the Imperial Palace and fought their way through the halls of the palace until they reached Tharn.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ATIP-55)</sup> The champion fought and defeated Tharn and then used the Staff of Chaos to drain the power of the Jewel of Fire, which destroyed Tharn, and Uriel VII was returned to Tamriel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ASEEHA-29)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Es AC-30)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ATIP-55)</sup> The Emperor named his liberator the Eternal Champion, and granted them a place by his side.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ATIP-55)</sup> On the topic of Tharn's demise, [High Chancellor](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) [Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) reportedly said: "Tharn is dead. He is actually worse off, but I will not discuss those details."<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TPP-16)</sup>

### Legacy
When Uriel VII was restored to the Ruby Throne, he began a period of healing for the Empire known as the Restoration.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-23)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ASEEHA-29)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Es AC-30)</sup> After his emancipation, Uriel VII worked diligently to reunite Tamriel, although he did so through more subtle means, no longer displaying the brashness he had before his imprisonment.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-7)</sup> Uriel VII stated that his change in strategy and disposition was a reaction to and revulsion to the early teachings and counsel of Tharn.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ALOUS-5)</sup> Due to the audacity and scope of his plot, Tharn was remembered in history as the arch-traitor to the Empire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CES-2)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Bs IS-56)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Rt BS-57)</sup> His plot might not have been completely unknown to those with magical talents, as certain magical circles spoke with envy at Tharn's success and dared not move against him.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-31)</sup> In the following years, supporters of Tharn were still active in rebel bands in the Imperial Province.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-31)</sup>

Decades after his death, circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), it was rumored that Uriel VII's heirs had been replaced by Tharn's doppelgangers.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-MLR-58)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-MSHH-59)</sup> The Imperial Guard killed many people when it charged a mob that demanded the destruction of the "false heirs".<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-MLR-58)</sup> Circa [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), the last of Tharn's known associates were prisoners awaiting their deaths in [The Rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackrose_Prison), an infamous prison in Black Marsh.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-PGE3A-60)</sup>
