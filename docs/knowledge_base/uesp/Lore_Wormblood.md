Wormblood was a powerful [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) and nephew of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) who led the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) following the demise of his uncle during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MNESO-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup>

## History
Little is known about Wormblood's past. According to a report by [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md), an agent of Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) and the leader of the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md), some of Wormblood's followers believed he was a close relative of Mannimarco, such as a cousin or nephew. The same account claims that Wormblood renounced his birth name in defiance of a family that had disowned him for pursuing forbidden magical practices.

He appeared as a tall, emaciated High Elf with pale skin and shadowed eyes. He was known for speaking in a whispering, almost silent voice. Some speculated he might have been a [lich](Lore_Lich.md), though this was not confirmed to be true. He wielded the [Staff of Worms](Lore_Staff_of_Worms.md), a powerful symbol of authority within the Worm Cult. According to one account, Wormblood was long designated as Mannimarco's chosen successor and the leader-in-waiting of the Worm Cult. It was said that Mannimarco established a secret Worm Nest, concealed not only from his enemies throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) but also from other branches of the cult: this hidden cell was charged with resurfacing to restore the cult should disaster befall Mannimarco or the Worm Cult itself. Wormblood has embraced this role, declaring himself a vessel for Mannimarco's ambitions and enforcing strict adherence to the King of Worms' doctrines and designs.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup> He was determined to restore the Worm Cult to its former glory.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOD-3)</sup>

Wormblood was the mastermind behind the resurgence of the Worm Cult across Tamriel,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup> orchestrating attacks against both the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), as well as the development of the Soul Reapers. His schemes led to the death of the leader of the Fighters Guild [Merric at-Aswala](Lore_Merric_at-Aswala.md) and the abduction of the Archmage [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md). In the wake of these events, [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md) and [Azah](Lore_Azah.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) assumed temporary leadership of the Mages Guild and Fighters Guild, respectively, ultimately resulting in the formation of the Stirk Fellowship.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AGIC-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-JFTF-5)</sup>
