[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c9/OB-place-Gold_Coast.jpg/200px-OB-place-Gold_Coast.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Gold_Coast.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Gold_Coast.jpg) Gold Coast (Oblivion) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/OB-map-Gold_Coast.jpg/200px-OB-map-Gold_Coast.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Gold_Coast.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Gold_Coast.jpg) Map of the Gold Coast (Oblivion) The Gold Coast, also called the **Strident Coast**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AMGC-3)</sup> makes up the western coastline of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), bounded by the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md) to the north and the [Strid](Lore_Strid_River.md) to the south.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-4)</sup> Aside from the occasional farmlands, the main feature of the region is [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), a major port city. Other historical settlements in the area include [Crowhaven](Lore_Crowhaven.md), [Garlas Malatar](Lore_Garlas_Malatar.md), [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), and [Sutch](Lore_Sutch.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oblivion-5)</sup>

The Cove of the Guiding Light is an isolated cove along region's the northern coast, named for the great beacon of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) that once shone above Garlas Malatar and served as a major landmark for sailors prior to its extinguishment after the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

## History
The Gold Coast was once dominated by [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) settlements, which were later reduced to broken marble and subterranean dangers after the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AMGC-3)</sup> The Cyro-Nords were determined to conquer the frontier, their primitive ferocity preferring bloody engagement and plunder over magic and the need for industry. They soon captured the [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) port-cities of the Strident Coast, becoming known as the "Westerners" (and later [Colovians](Lore_Colovia.md)), embarking on voyages from the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) to colonize new lands.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup>

During [Varen's Rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md), the rebels built a stone wall known as [Varen's Wall](Lore_Varen%27s_Wall_(place).md) to separate the Gold Coast from the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) and thereby protect the cities of Anvil and Kvatch from retribution by the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VW-8)</sup> This wall isolated the Gold Coast from much of the chaos of the later [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md), though Anvil was captured by a group of pirates known as the Red Sails. Their leader, Fortunata ap Dugal, named herself governor of the city and proclaimed it a free port. She later claimed dominion over the whole of the Gold Coast, including Kvatch, and declared it to be an independent state. Kvatch's loyalty remained with the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), however, and it became locked in a struggle with Anvil for control of the region.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PQot GC-9)</sup>

By the time of the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) and the formation of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), no trace remained of Varen's Wall and the area had been reincorporated into Cyrodiil, where it remained well into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). Anvil was besieged by the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) early on in the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), and fell in [4E 172](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_172). The city was returned to the Empire when the war ended three years later.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TGW-10)</sup>

## Notes
- The Gold Coast shares its name with the real-life [Gold Coast](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold_Coast,_Queensland) of [Australia](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia). Whether this was intentional remains unknown.
