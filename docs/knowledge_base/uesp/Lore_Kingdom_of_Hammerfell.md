# Kingdom of Hammerfell

The Crowns retained the rights of noble council and the Forebears were granted rights of ownership within their tribal districts.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-3)</sup> High-ranking nobles were called Magnifico and Magnifica, depending on gender.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Magnifica-4)</sup> Similarly, Grandee and Grandeya were titles associated with various roles, including provincial governance, leading armed forces or members of the court.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHusni2-5)</sup>

### Interregnum
After the fall of the Second Empire in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), control of Hammerfell reverted back to the hereditary monarchy of the Na-Totambu, giving birth to the various kingdoms of Hammerfell. The new High King, in a bold move, shifted the capital from Hegathe to the more prosperous Forebear city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), which had, by this time, mastered a third of the trade of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1H-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/ON-place-Sentinel.jpg/200px-ON-place-Sentinel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sentinel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sentinel.jpg) Sentinel In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), King [Ramzi the Distrait](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ramzi_the_Distrait) ruled the Kingdom of Sentinel, but was self-serving and weak-willed and failed to protect his subjects from the Knahaten Flu. As death spread to his courtiers, Ramzi sealed himself within [Samuruik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Samuruik) palace and slowly starved to death, leaving the kingdom in despair. A young Forebear noble named [Fahara'jad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fahara%27jad), descendant of Ar-Azal, traveled from Bergama and declared himself king of Hammerfell.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-3)</sup> Support for the new king wasn't unanimous and Hammerfell wasn't unified under Fahara'jad. The Crown nobility in the southern cities of [Hegathe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hegathe), [Gilane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gilane), [Taneth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Taneth) and [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) supported [Grandee Zizzeen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zizzeen)'s claim to throne instead.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRLo S-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cont Zizeen-7)</sup> The Crowns suspected King Ramzi was usurped and acted with vehement repudiation against Fahara'jad, however Forebear viziers and northern chieftains ratified his accession.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-3)</sup>

Around this time, the Forebears were progressives who firmly support the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) while the traditionalists Crowns rejected the practices of Tamriel and clung to their old ways.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONHusni-8)</sup> Possibly sensing an uprising from the south, Fahara'jad allied himself with the Daggerfall Covenant to strengthen his position and pronounced himself High King of all Hammerfell, and even arranged the marriage of his daughter to High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Cont Musk-9)</sup> This action further infuriated the Crown nobility in the south, such as the royal family of Hegathe, as they openly opposed Fahara'jad's accession. Despite being unable to challenge his claims, they secretly plotted against and spied on Fahara'jad's kingdom.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRLo S-6)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Sa M-10)</sup> While Fahara'jad ruled Sentinel, the Alik'r and beyond, its unknown if Hammerfell's other kingdoms, such as the Kingdom of Taneth, recognized him as High King.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Cont Taneth-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTI-12)</sup>

### The Tiber War
The line of High Kings that ruled from Sentinel continued with [Thassad II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thassad_II), but the city had switched to the rule of the Crowns. As [the conquests](Lore_Tiber_War.md) of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) began to expand and enroach on his domain, Thassad II resisted all forms of Imperial invasion. When the High King passed away in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862) the capital was retaken by the Forebears under the command of Baron [Volag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volag), ensuing a vicious civil war between the Crowns and Forebears. When the latter enlisted the help of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the Forebears gained the advantage and resulting in the Crowns losing their territories until [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) was left. The [Battle of Stros M'Kai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Stros_M%27Kai) was won and the Empire swiftly took control over all of Hammerfell, imposing several provisional governors to rule its regions. Both factions reconciled a year later after an uprising against the Empire, which forced Tiber Septim to sign a more favorable treaty that formally established the Imperial Province of Hammerfell. Sentinel, however, remained as its provincial capital.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1E-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RGIntro-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Redguard-15)</sup> It is unknown when the next High King ascended to the throne after Hammerfell was absorbed into the Empire, but the rift in Hammerfell society was not mended.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3H-16)</sup>

### Third and Fourth Eras
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) erupted in [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396). [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) defeated the forces of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and High Rock and claimed many miles of the [eastern parts of those provinces](Lore_Western_Reach.md), including the cities of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-17)</sup> Some of these territories were claimed to have been lost in previous wars at the expense of Skyrim.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3High-18)</sup> By [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403) [King Camaron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camaron) of Sentinel was in power during the province's [short but vicious land dispute](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) with [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) over the island of [Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-NWOB-19)</sup> Following the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), Sentinel under [King Lhotun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lhotun) greatly expanded to take over the entire northern coast of Hammerfell.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3H-16)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TWITW-20)</sup>

Centuries later, the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) would attempt to seize control of Hammerfell from the Third Empire. Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md)'s refusal to concede to Thalmor demands triggered the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171). Aldmeri forces crossed western Cyrodiil and invaded Hammerfell's southern coast, easily sweeping through the scattered resistance put up by the disunited Redguards. Initially only seeking to conquer Hammerfell, the Dominion launched a full-scale invasion of Cyrodiil when it realized the Empire was weaker than previously believed. Imperial fortunes took a turn for the better in Hammerfell two years later, when a Forebear army from Sentinel broke the siege of the Crown city of Hegathe, leading to the reconciliation of the two factions. After the Empire's hard-won victory in the Battle of the Red Ring in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Empire and Dominion signed the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) and ended the war. Outraged that the terms of the treaty called for a large portion of southern Hammerfell to be turned over to the Dominion, however, the Redguards soundly rejected it. Titus II was forced to officially renounce Hammerfell as an Imperial province to preserve the peace, and fighting between the Aldmeri and Redguards continued. In [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), the Dominion agreed to the [Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_Second_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and finally withdrew from the region, leaving Hammerfell an independent albeit devastated nation.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TGW-21)</sup>
