## [Hackwing](Lore_Hackwing.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/ON-creature-Hackwing.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Hackwing.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Hackwing.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Hackwing.jpg) A hackwing [Hackwings](Lore_Hackwing.md) are described as both large [birds](Lore_Bird.md) and flying [lizards](Lore_Lizard.md) with saw-like beaks, both expressions which are often interchanged. They travel in large flocks and have the tendency to swarm when they feel threatened. They strike quickly in an attempt to wound or knock down their prey. They will fly off after wounding their victim, only to come back when the victim is almost dead from blood loss. Their victims are then ripped apart with the hackwings' saw-like beaks.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Hackwing), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Hadolid](Lore_Hadolid.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-creature-The_Euphotic_Gatekeeper.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Euphotic_Gatekeeper.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Euphotic_Gatekeeper.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Euphotic_Gatekeeper.jpg) A hadolid [Hadolids](Lore_Hadolid.md) are a [race](Lore_Race.md) of amphibious, bipedal arthropodal [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) (or "shellfolk") native to the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). Due to traits they share with crustaceans, they have also been referred to as **crabfolk**. There are two distinct types of Hadolids, those more [crablike](Lore_Crab.md), with wide flat heads and four claws, and those more [lobsterlike](Lore_Lobster.md), with elongated heads as well as two claws and two hands. They are visually comparable to [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md), though they are unrelated, and their shells are somewhat similar to [mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md).

Hadolids are capable of diving underwater to depths that surpass even the ability of [Argonians](Lore_Argonians.md), leaving a major part of their lifecycle a mystery to researchers. Their odor is reminiscent of rotting meat. They travel in hunting packs and are capable of using powerful [magic](Lore_Magic.md) and [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md).

Hadolids are migratory, and can stay underwater for a period of a decade or more before returning to land for a similar length of time. They are commonly seen venturing onto land in the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md) and the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice.md). However, isolated groups were also sighted in [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md)

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Hadolid.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Hadolid)

## [Hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg) A hagraven [Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md) are creatures of corruption and decay that appear as a horrific cross between an old woman and a [bird](Lore_Bird.md). They were once [witches](Lore_Witches.md), but have undergone a ritual, trading in their humanity for access to powerful [magic](Lore_Magic.md), and the transformations they undergo infuse their entire beings with some element of that power. Hagravens consume people alive, and especially love eating fresh eyeballs.

Few creatures match the cunning, depravity, and repulsiveness of the hagravens. They will take through savagery what they cannot win through guile. They are usually extremely hostile to outsiders, but they retain the ability to communicate. They are equally dangerous up close or when casting spells from afar. They are known for their foul stench. They possess an ability to turn into a murder of [crows](Lore_Crow.md) and attack their foes from a hundred directions at once.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Hagraven.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Hagraven), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call_to_Arms:Models), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Haj Mota](Lore_Haj_Mota.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/ON-creature-Haj_Mota.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Haj_Mota.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Haj_Mota.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Haj_Mota.jpg) A Haj Mota [Haj Mota](Lore_Haj_Mota.md) (nicknamed "devil- [tortoise](Lore_Turtle.md)") are large [turtles](Lore_Turtle.md) with jagged shells and powerful webbed tails. They are ambush predators, and wait patiently in concealment until their prey comes into their attack range. They spew poison and are known to burrow underground. Haj mota mostly inhabit [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), but they can also be found in the marshes of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md)'s [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md) peninsula. The beasts are exceptionally hard to tame, although hatchlings are sometimes kept as pets. The verdigris haj mota hides in Black Marsh's algae-covered pools, where its color acts as camouflage.

A female haj mota is called a "doe". Haj motas lay eggs (around three to a clutch), and are extremely protective of their nests. The shells of their eggs are mottled and brittle. Argonians sometimes use these shells to craft bowls, but they're hard to work with, as they're bound to crack unless the artisan works with utmost care and expertise. When a mated pair produces a clutch of eggs, both parents will tend to the nest. One parent can stalk the marsh safely while the other stays with the nest.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Haj_Mota), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Spiny Haj Mota), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Harpy](Lore_Harpy.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/ON-creature-Harpy.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Harpy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Harpy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Harpy.jpg) A harpy [Harpies](Lore_Harpy.md) are a [race](Lore_Races.md) of fierce, carnivorous, feminine humanoid [beast-women](Lore_Beastfolk.md) with the wings of a [bird](Lore_Bird.md). They are common nuisance around the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) area, particularly in [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md), but are found wherever there are rocky cliffs and crags suitable for their nests. They also build their nests in abandoned ruins. While their maniacal nature makes them somewhat less-than-brilliant tacticians, their sharp talons and frenzied behavior often force kingdoms to abandon lands to their use. Mercenary groups like the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) are sometimes hired to clear out harpy nests for groups that pass through their territory, such as traveling merchants. Harpy feathers are very valuable, prized by tailors and quill merchants alike and their down can be spun with fine [sheep](Lore_Sheep.md)'s wool to make a sturdy material for mages' robes. Harpies tend to smell foul.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Harpy), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Harpy), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Harpy), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Harvester](Lore_Harvester.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/ON-creature-Drel.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Drel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Drel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Drel.jpg) A Harvester [Harvesters](Lore_Harvester.md) are intelligent, four-armed [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who primarily serve [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Their duty among his servants is to preside over sacrifices or the stealing of souls. They are known to feed on souls and magicka. They act alone, and care little for concepts such as societal rank, unlike the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md). Though they do have some measure of intelligence, their lack of culture or language places them below the Dremora in Daedric hierarchy. Harvesters have the torso of a woman and the body of a serpent. They have piercing blue eyes just like their master. Barbed gauntlets adorn their wrists, matching the harsh spikes running down their backs and the frilled crown of horns atop their heads. Dark plates of ebony are fused to their skin.

Despite having many arms, their hands are not as suited for wielding blades as one might think. Instead, Harvesters opt to utilize the arts of conjuring and illusion. They possess the ability to tear a mortal's soul straight from its body. Harvesters feast on mortal vitality using their powerful sorcery. Said vitality appears in the form of dark orbs that emerge from their victims and gravitate towards the Harvester. When broken, these orbs will return the victim's strength.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Harvester)

## [Herne](Lore_Herne_(creature).md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/BS-creature-Herne_02.gif/200px-BS-creature-Herne_02.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Herne_02.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Herne_02.gif) A Herne [Hernes](Lore_Herne_(creature).md) (also called **Mastema**) are a race of bipedal [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), described as aggressive, orderly, and deliberate warrior-sportsmen. These horned Daedra somewhat ressemble [Skaafin](Lore_Skaafin.md), but differ in that they possess furred digitigrade legs, cloven hoofs, and a pointed tail. Fitting to their nature, Hernes establish hunting groups in which they refer to each other as "brothers", "playbrothers", and "lapmates". While some are entrusted with spears, many simply use bones as blunt weapons.

Though Hernes commonly have a collected disposition and enjoy tormenting their prey through parley, they're self-admitted "sensitive creatures", that are quick to take offense to personal slights and whose bonds between themselves and their leaders are felt deeply. When a Herne's oathkin or master is slain, sending their [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) back to the [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md), the Herne describe a physical feeling of an emptiness, pain, and change within themselves from the loss.

Hernes have often loyally served [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) such as [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Noonan)</sup> Many Hernes were known to serve as retainers of [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md)'s [clan](Lore_Shardai_Clan.md), in service to [Clan Dagon](Lore_Clan_Dagon.md). These Hernes would participate happily in the [Ritual of the Innocent Quarry](Lore_Ritual_of_the_Innocent_Quarry.md) following the invasion of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Herne), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Herne_Ghorev)

## [Hoarvor](Lore_Hoarvor.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-creature-Hoarvor.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Hoarvor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Hoarvor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Hoarvor.jpg) A hoarvor [Hoarvors](Lore_Hoarvor.md) are giant [ticks](Lore_Tick.md) that drink large amounts of blood to survive. They can generally be found among dense foliage and in damp locations. Riverbeds and filth-ridden areas are a good place to find them. Hoarvors are not quick to strike a lone enemy. However, in groups or in the presence of abundant food, they are known to strike swiftly and with ferocity. They don't hunt so much as they come upon their prey. Hoarvors will commonly feed on sleeping, injured, dead and dying creatures. They are skilled at locating areas of strong blood flow from which to feed, where they drink from using a tongue-like proboscis.

Hoarvor infestations are notoriously difficult to stamp out. Simple things like a drunkard left passed out in the streets at night or a pet left uncared for is enough to attract an infestation. It is said that placing frost salts in a circle around one's home is useful when trying to deter hoarvors from approaching. [Jagga](Lore_Jagga.md) and similar substances are known to drive them into a frenzy. Hoarvors are associated with [Namiira](Lore_Namiira.md), and can cross from the [Dark Behind the World](Lore_Dark_Behind_the_World.md) to Nirn in areas ravaged by the [Dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Hoarvor), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Hob](Lore_Hob.md)
[Hobs](Lore_Hob.md) are a [race](Lore_Race.md) of hairless [monkey](Lore_Monkey.md) -like creatures of unclear origin, who inhabited the floating island [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md). They have long arms and legs, and very long, thin, delicate fingers. Their faces are described as [goblinesque](Lore_Goblin-ken.md), with huge toothless mouths and large green eyes.

Hobs can speak very simple [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md), though they seem to have their own language as well. They were used as assistants by chefs in the kitchens of Umbriel, and were usually tasked with fetching specific ingredients. Troops of them could sometimes be found harvesting [bloodball](Lore_Bloodball.md) fruit in the Marrow Sump. Supposedly, some chefs would train their Hobs to perform "[unconventional](Lore_Sex.md)" favors for them.
