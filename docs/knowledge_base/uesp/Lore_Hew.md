Prince Hubalajad, more commonly known as **Prince Hew**, was a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) noble who colonized [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md) during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Although notable in his establishment of [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md), he is best remembered as an unkind and comedic figure who was foiled at every turn, and whose endeavors resulted in failure.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDo PH-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/ON-map-Hew%27s_Bane.jpg/200px-ON-map-Hew%27s_Bane.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Hew%27s_Bane.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Hew%27s_Bane.jpg) Hew's Bane Little is known about Prince Hew, and those few references that are available are primarily third-hand documents or apocryphal tales that some scholars believe embellish the facts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDo PH-1)</sup> Prince Hew sailed to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) as part of the "Tavan" [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) invasion,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDo PH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CM28-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HB-3)</sup> circa [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PG-4)</sup> Prince Hew established the city of [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md), and named it in honor of [Ra Abah](Lore_Ra_Abah.md), a member of his guard who was first to land at the future site of the city.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TCOH-5)</sup> He also constructed a lavish palace within the city walls, from which his descendants would rule for several generations.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Hubalajad_Palace-6)</sup> He also ordered construction of second palace built solely to better catch the light of the morning sun.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PDP-7)</sup> The prince also erected [a vast statue of himself](Lore_Statues_Notable_Figures.md#Hubalajad) near the city docks. The statue was an idealized sculpture of Hubalajad himself, entwined by a sep adder. Enormous, dramatic, and curiously shirtless, as if to suggest he was invulnerable. Though the statue portrays a powerful, fit warrior,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONReflection-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Cist Zeira-9)</sup> some texts describe Hubalajad as pudgy.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PHAHM-10)</sup> He also was credited with raising the imposing No Shira Citadel to the south of the city, although the fortification was quickly undermined by seasonal flooding and had to be abandoned.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDo PH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CM28-2)</sup>

Notable members of his family include his uncle, Magnifico Bahraha, the leader of the [Bleak Veil](Lore_Bleak_Veil.md), who was magically sealed within the royal tomb along with his followers when he was discovered to be a [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md), something completely repugnant to devout Yokudans.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TBVE-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOSab-12)</sup>

Contemporary sources depict Prince Hew in an unkind light, characterized as a wasteful, flamboyant and gullible individual. Once such example is a handbill produced by one of the Abah's Landing's original residents, which delivers a scathing admonishment of the Prince's fondness of extravagant and expensive hairstyles and wigs, all while his people starved or become homeless due to the sinking of some sections of the city.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ACFCH-13)</sup> Other evidence of these characteristics includes the discovery of various trinkets he once owned, including a reputedly magical dowsing [wand](Lore_Wand.md), sold to him as an infallible guide to a fountain of immortality, a "two-bosom harness" worn by him in the title role for the play *"Lady Morwha's Latest Marriage"* and a [Haj Mota](Lore_Haj_Mota.md) -shaped toupee with emerald eyes.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Cont Wig-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cont Gold-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Cont Double-16)</sup> It was even said he accidentally burned down Abah's Landing shipyards, resulting in the production of commemorative gold plates.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Cont Plate-17)</sup>

## Legacy
When Prince Hew died, he was buried in a shuttered tomb beneath a great rock formation in the southern deserts. Such was his reputation among his subjects that his funerary urn went on to become the resting place for letters regarding his unpaid debts in his final years.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Cont Urn-18)</sup> Despite his debt and poor reputation, his descendants took over the throne and remained in power of Abah's Landing for many generations, ruling from Hubalajad Palace.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Hubalajad_Palace-6)</sup>

By the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the peninsula began to go by the name of "Hew's Bane" rather than Khefrem's Boot.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Popular myth had transformed Prince Hew into a comedic figure, replacing his supposed tenacity with a perceived thick-headedness.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDo PH-1)</sup> Indeed, by the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) his name became synonymous with a lack of intelligence or common sense among the citizens of Abah's Landing.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSig-20)</sup> Similarly, an oft-repeated [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) expression for wasting money is *"laying foundation with Hubalajad's coin"*.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDo PH-1)</sup> Some even went as far as to write fiction, as exhibited by the highly-collectible series of books known as "Prince Hew's Fiascos", which depict the prince is a series of humorous tales.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HCALH-21)</sup>
