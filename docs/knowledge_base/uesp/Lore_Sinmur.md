Sinmur, also known as **Sinmur the Terrible** and **Giant of Legend**, was a [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) half- [Giant](Lore_Giant.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSvari-1)</sup> chieftain from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Leading hundreds of his kin, he is perhaps best remembered in his epic conflict against [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) and the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sot R27-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONUN-3)</sup>

## Ysgramor's Return
When Ysgramor returned to Skyrim in the Merethic Era, the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) were not the only obstacles that stood in his path for expansion. A large number of Giant clans inhabited the land, and were ruled by a powerful chieftain named Sinmur. While many enemies fled before Ysgramor, only Sinmur dared to face him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSvari-1)</sup> According to legend, Ysgramor and the Five Hundred Companions fought a large-scale war with Sinmur and his kin, and both sides suffered severe casualties. The conflict culminated in a final battle between the two leaders in Sinmur's barrow. As they dueled, Ysgramor shattered Sinmur's club and finally slayed him with his legendary battleaxe, [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sot R27-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSvari-1)</sup>

The war with the Giants was over and the companions rejoiced. A ballad named *"The Song of Sinmur"* was even written documenting the conflict.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONSA-4)</sup> As a precautionary measure. great steps were taken to ensure Sinmur would never return. His bones were interred deep within the mines of [Shor's Stone](Lore_Shor%27s_Stone.md). Sinmur's tomb was then locked with a magical seal. The spirits of two Companions who fell in the war, Jalarma Fireheart and Bron Darkhammer, were bound to the tomb to keep the unwary away from his remains.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SGOL-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONUN-3)</sup> The spirits of the Companions Hakra and [Valdur](Lore_Valdur.md), who also fell in the war, were bound to [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) as caretakers to guard against threats.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONValdur-6)</sup>

## Sinmur's Return
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg/200px-ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) Wuuthrad being used to raise Sinmur During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md), led by the [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) Thallik Wormfather, sought to reanimate Sinmur and an army of his kin.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Online-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TO-8)</sup> With the aid of a [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) tribe they attacked Shor's Stone, infiltrated Sinmur's tomb and awakened his spirit.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONUN-3)</sup> They also stole Wuuthrad from [Fallowstone Hall](Lore_Fallowstone_Hall.md), as the axe had become imbued with great power after slaying Sinmur. The Worm Cult believed this power could be used to raise Sinmur and use him in their schemes.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONVigrod-9)</sup>

In order to empower Sinmur further, the Worm Cult scoured the Rift to locate spirits of deceased Companions. Their plan was to enslave these fallen warriors and bind them to Sinmur's spirit. The spirit's of Harka and Valdur were found, but the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) intervened and stopped the Worm Cult.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONGlow-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONKireth-11)</sup>

Eventually the spirit of Sinmur made its way to [Arcwind Point](Lore_Arcwind_Point.md), where Thallik Wormfather began conducting a ritual to raise the spirit of Sinmur, using the power of Wuuthrad. Thallik's plan was foiled when the Vestige interrupted the ritual and killed Thallik, with the aid of the ancient Companions Hakra and Valdur. Unexpectedly, Wuuthrad was shattered in the process and three fragments of the axe were scattered across the [Smokefrost Peaks](Lore_Smokefrost_Peaks.md). It later came to light that Thallik didn't die, but instead used the remaining power within Wuthraad to fuse his spirit with Sinmur.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONHakra-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONSH-13)</sup>

With Sinmur reanimated, the giant left Arcwind Point and rampaged east towards [Riften](Lore_Riften.md). Causing vast damage to the north of the city and the surrounding farmsteads, he continued onwards to the summit of [Mount Trolhetta](Lore_Mount_Trolhetta.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONWK-14)</sup> All the shards were eventually recovered by the Vestige for the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) to be used against Sinmur and the Worm Cult.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Online-7)</sup> Because Wuuthrad was originally used to slay Sinmur, even the shards would do him harm. A method of harnessing this power was discovered by the Pact, involving placing the shards in runic braziers.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONVigrod-9)</sup> The Vestige, [Jorunn Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) and a legion of Ebonheart Pact soldiers traveled to the summit of Trolhetta to face Sinmur. Using the shards, the spirit of Sinmur and Thallik was torn asunder and Sinmur was severely weakened. This allowed the Pact to deal the killing blow and finally banish Sinmur.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONSS-15)</sup>
