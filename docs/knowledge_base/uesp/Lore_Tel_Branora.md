[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/MW-place-Tel_Branora.jpg/200px-MW-place-Tel_Branora.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Tel_Branora.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Tel_Branora.jpg) Tel Branora Tel Branora was perhaps the southernmost town in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), on an island far to the southeast. It was the wizard tower of [Mistress Therana](Lore_Therana.md), possibly the most eccentric (some say irrational) of the [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) Mage Lords, who held it since at least the sixth century of the Second Era.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Therana Dialogue ESO-1)</sup> Because Therana was certainly eccentric, perhaps unbalanced, the Telvanni code would generally have seen her disposed of, unless she was capable of adequately defending herself. However, she obviously held great importance to the Council, or she would not have existed as a Master.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Therana-2)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) Tel Branora was easily reached by ship from [Castle Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md), [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md) or [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md).

The docks were about half a league from the tower and surrounding community. There were a few hovels near the docks themselves, but the main community was up at the tower. While the tower had a range of magic related services, the town itself had nothing but a [smith](Lore_Smithing.md) and an adequate public house.

Although the town survived the [eruption](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5) of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), it was sacked by Argonians inflicting their revenge for centuries of slavery.
