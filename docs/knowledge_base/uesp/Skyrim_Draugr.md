# Draugr

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/SR-quest-Evil_in_Waiting_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Evil_in_Waiting_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Evil_in_Waiting_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Evil_in_Waiting_03.jpg) A draugr using the thu'um [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/SR-creature-Draugr_Deathlord_female.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Draugr_Deathlord_female.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Draugr_Deathlord_female.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Draugr_Deathlord_female.jpg) A female draugr **Draugr** are ancient Nordic warriors who have been reanimated. Most draugr were followers of [dragon priests](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md) in ancient times. When these priests died, their followers were killed, reanimated, and then sealed into the tomb where they would periodically awaken in order to worship the priests and transfer energy to them to maintain their undead state. Some draugr instead guard nobility and treasures buried in [barrows and tombs](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md). They often rest in catacombs or sarcophagi to awaken and fight when disturbed or alerted.

## Appearance and Types
Despite the advanced embalming techniques of the ancient Nords, time has taken its toll on draugr's physical appearance. Their soft tissues are severely decomposed, and their armor does little to conceal the fact that much of their flesh is rotting away, exposing the underlying skeletal structure. All draugr have piercing blue eyes. Male and female draugr appear at similar rates, but at higher levels, males far outnumber females. Higher rank draugr are clearly recognizable by their increasingly more impressive helmets and equipment. The Draugr Deathlords and Death Overlords will almost always carry [ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md) equipment and are most recognizable by their unobtainable versions of the [ancient Nord helmets](Skyrim_Ancient_Nord_Helmet.md).

## Characteristics
Most draugr are [immune to poison](Skyrim_Resist_Poison.md) and have a 50% [resistance to frost](Skyrim_Resist_Frost.md). However, the Hallowed Dead found in [Labyrinthian](Skyrim_Labyrinthian.md) are not immune to poison and are 33% [weaker to magic](Skyrim_Weakness_to_Magic.md).

The specific variety of draugr that you meet is generally level-dependent. However, this also depends on the location at which they're encountered. In addition, draugr may be spellcasters, archers, one-handed melee fighters, or two-handed melee fighters; this is unspecified by their names, which only indicate their class. Draugr that begin appearing early in the game are equipped with [ancient Nordic weapons](Skyrim_Specialty_Gear.md#Ancient_Nord_Weapons), which are similar in strength to steel weaponry, and [ancient Nord armor](Skyrim_Ancient_Nord_Armor.md), similar in quality to iron armor, which is unlootable. The more powerful draugr that appear at higher levels often use [ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md) weapons and equipment, and may have the ability to use one or more [dragon shouts](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) in combat, including [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md), [Disarm](Skyrim_Disarm.md), and sometimes even [Frost Breath](Skyrim_Frost_Breath.md). Most types of draugr, apart from the very weakest, will employ [Frost Damage](Skyrim_Frost_Damage.md) in combat, whether through spells, shouts, or enchanted weapons. Draugr have a high ratio of health to their level when compared to most enemy types, such as [Vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md) and enemy mages, making them all the more dangerous.

You will often encounter draugr enthralled by [necromancers](Skyrim_Necromancer.md) or hostile mages, such as those found in the Nordic ruin of [Ansilvund](Skyrim_Ansilvund.md) where the draugr are forced to mine.

For information about dead, static draugr which can be looted, see [this page](Skyrim_Containers.md#Draugr).

### Combat Strategies
Eliminate weaker types before fighting the stronger ones. Archers can deal a lot of damage during a battle, but they often shoot from a distance, sometimes making it easy to hide or take cover from them while dealing with nearer enemies. Stronger draugr can be very dangerous to most players, and should be attacked from a distance when possible. If you are a mage, avoid the use of frost spells, but concentrate on the two remaining elements: flames to constantly inflict damage, and shock to drain the enemy's magicka pool. Flaming Familiars, Flame Atronachs and Storm Atronachs will be particularly potent.

You can often perform a [sneak attack](Skyrim_Sneak.md#Sneak_Attacks) on a draugr sleeping in its alcove and kill it before it is able to attack. Even if you fail to kill it immediately, you should have time for one or two more hits while it climbs out of its resting place. [Remaining undetected](Skyrim_Sneak.md#Remaining_Undetected), even by sleeping draugr, can be challenging, as they are quite sensitive, and this increases with stronger versions. [Dragon priests](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md), who guard some Nordic ruins, are extremely dangerous opponents, but can often be defeated from a distance by using the [Invisibility](Skyrim_Invisibility_(effect).md) and [Muffle](Skyrim_Muffle_(effect).md) effects to evade detection.

You can lure draugr into many of the [traps](Skyrim_Traps.md) common in Nordic ruins, which can be very effective. [Dart traps](Skyrim_Dart_Trap.md) are not helpful in this regard, as most draugr are [immune to poison](Skyrim_Resist_Poison.md).

When engaging with high level draugr that use the [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) and [Disarm](Skyrim_Disarm.md) shouts, you may wish to save frequently in case a treasured weapon is thrown and you cannot find it again. [Wards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ward) (including the ward effect that the unique shield [Spellbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spellbreaker) provides when blocking) will render any shout harmless, from draugr or other sources.

Draugr are unable to attack and have no defenses while the animation plays as they emerge from their tomb. Attacking them during this time can quickly eliminate them before they can strike.

### Draugr Deathlord

Their health and attacks are brutal on their own, but they also use [thu'um](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) - and unlike their lesser counterparts, a Draugr Deathlord's [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) shout is powerful enough to ragdoll you at times, making them even more of a challenge to the unprepared adventurer.

## Notes
- All draugr belong to the [Creature Faction](Skyrim_Creature_Faction.md) and the [Draugr Faction](Skyrim_Draugr Faction.md). The [Draugr Thrall](#Draugr_Thrall) belongs to the [dun Folgunthur Thrall Faction](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dun Folgunthur Thrall Faction) as well.
- Several spectral draugr can be found in [Labyrinthian](Skyrim_Labyrinthian.md) during the quest [The Staff of Magnus](Skyrim_The_Staff_of_Magnus.md), along with [Spectral Warhounds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spectral_Warhound). These draugr variations drop spectral weapons: [Drainheart Swords](Skyrim_Drainheart_Sword.md), [Drainblood Battleaxes](Skyrim_Drainblood_Battleaxe.md), and the [Drainspell Bow](Skyrim_Drainspell_Bow.md).
- For draugr at rest (in a niche or standing) in a catacomb a good rule of thumb is that if it is wearing clothes, it can come to life and attack.
- Draugr that are naked and nearly skeletal, draugr on the second tier of niches, and draugr wrapped like mummies cannot come to life. These draugr act like burial urns and if you have the [Prowler's Profit](Skyrim_Prowler%27s_Profit.md) ability from the [Thieves Guild](Skyrim_Thieves_Guild_(faction).md) [Stones of Barenziah](Skyrim_No_Stone_Unturned.md) quest, they will contain gemstones.
- Draugr and Draugr Deathlords do not have a magic-using variant; they can only be archers, one-handed fighters or two-handed fighters. Hulking Draugr cannot be magic users, archers or two-handed melee fighters. Draugr Death Overlords can only be one-handed melee fighters and two-handed fighters. All non-named boss Draugr also cannot cast spells, and instead will be either a one-handed or two-handed melee fighter; boss Draugr also cannot belong to the archery class.
- If you manage to disarm a draugr (either through the [Disarm](Skyrim_Disarm.md) shout or the Disarming Bash perk in the [Block](Skyrim_Block.md) skill tree), they will have a unique animation for unarmed attacks and deal very low unarmed damage. If there is a replacement weapon around (such as an Iron Battleaxe), they will equip and use it, even if they weren't a two-handed melee fighter in the first place.
- Halldir's clones and Jyrik Gauldurson are the only two Draugr in the game that use all elements (fire, frost and shock) against you. All other spellsword Draugr use frost magic exclusively.
- Red Eagle, Warlord Gathrik, and Yngol may appear as [Dragon Priests](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md) at higher levels. This is determined by the [radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md) system.
- The two Draugr that you can make in the [Trophy Room](Skyrim_Trophy_Room.md) of your homestead are the same two models seen in the [loading screens](Skyrim_Loading_Screens.md).

## Bugs
- Dragons and draugr are considered enemies, even though dragon priests are allies to both. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2, addresses this issue. Dragons and draugr are no longer hostile to one another.
- Some female draugr can appear with a beard. This is caused by the Lvl Draugr Ambush Warlock Female specifically, which allows the use of male templates with female bodies. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) fixes this bug.
- Although Red Eagle, Warlord Gathrik, and Yngol can all spawn as Dragon Priests at higher levels, the backstories behind these characters makes this extremely unlikely to have been the case. Plus, in the case of Warlord Gathrik, spawning him as a dragon priest will break the quest at Ironbind Barrow. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.2, fixes this bug.
