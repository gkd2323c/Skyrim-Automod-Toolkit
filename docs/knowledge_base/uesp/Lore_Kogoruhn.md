It is virtually surrounded on all sides by numerous mountains and rocky slopes, including [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) towards the south, and is found near the ruins of Valenvaryon and Ebernanit. Its location has often been considered lost to time by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) people, and the [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) consider it a bad place.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SM-2)</sup> It is called **Kogoran** in the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) book the *[Five Songs of King Wulfharth](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md)* and sometimes called **Lost Kogoruhn** by the Temple.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FSo KW-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UL-4)</sup>

## History
[Voryn Dagoth](Lore_Voryn_Dagoth.md), wanting the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) dead as the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) did, went to Kogoruhn and summoned his House chap'thil to the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), made up of wizards, archers, [nix-hounds](Lore_Nix-Hound.md), and stolen [automatons](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FSo KW-3)</sup> Following the Battle of Red Mountain and the defeat of Voryn Dagoth in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), House Dagoth was dissolved and its remaining members were absorbed into other houses, resulting in Kogoruhn being abandoned. Texts describing it were either marked out or restricted by the Tribunal Temple afterwards.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ND-5)</sup>

During the [First Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md), a Failed Incarnate of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) named Idrenie Nerothan traveled to the ruined Kogoruhn to loot it, and perished during her attempt.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-IN-6)</sup> Later on around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), descendants of the Sixth House formed a cult and attempted to revive the ancient House, setting up operations in the area around Kogoruhn known as the Forgotten Wastes. They set up excavations in the caverns and ruins beneath the stronghold, led by Wakener Maras and Mountain-Caller Hlaren. The cultists' leaders were slain and their plans foiled by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and an [Ordinator](Lore_Ordinator.md) named Tythis Nirith.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOMW-7)</sup>

Dagoth Ur later reawakened in [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882) and made plans to establish a major operational base at Kogoruhn to expand the Sixth House and set up further operations in the ash wastes. By [3E 400](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_400), the [Ash Vampire](Lore_Ash_Vampire.md) Dagoth Uthol reoccupied the stronghold, fortifying it and using it as an advance base. As a result, [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) proceeded to become more widespread and soul sickness spread in regions close to Red Mountain, furthering Dagoth Ur's goals.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) traveled to the citadel. By then, many members of the Sixth House occupied the stronghold, and extensive tunnels that traveled past the [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md) had been made. Dagoth Uthol was slain in Kogoruhn, and Dagoth Ur soon followed, dying beneath Red Mountain and bringing an end to the presence of House Dagoth.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
