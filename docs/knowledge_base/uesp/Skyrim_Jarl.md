# Jarl


The game begins with nine jarls ruling the holds of Skyrim, four affiliated with the [Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md), four with the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md), and [Balgruuf the Greater](Skyrim_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md) of [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun_Hold.md) who is initially neutral. All except [Elisif the Fair](Skyrim_Elisif_the_Fair.md) can be supplanted by a new jarl who is more supportive to the opposing force, based on your actions during the game. This depends on which force you choose to ally with in the [Civil War](Skyrim_Civil_War.md), and jarls will be replaced when you help a side to capture a city they do not control, or through the negotiations made during [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md). Newly-instated jarls will gain a "Jarl" prefix to their name and move into the jarl's residence for that hold.

Deposed Imperial governments will generally seek refuge in the basement of the [Blue Palace](Skyrim_Blue_Palace.md) in [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), while deposed Stormcloak governments will go into exile in the [Palace of the Kings](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md) in [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), where they will be found seated around the dining table. The jarl, their housecarl, their steward, and their family will be exiled together, and due to the part you played in their exile, they will dislike you. However, if you trade a hold during Season Unending and retake that hold later on, the original jarl, their housecarl, their steward, and their family will return together, and due to the part you played in their freedom, they will like you once again. That said, however, the replacement jarl, their housecarl, their steward, and their family will be exiled together, and they will dislike you due to the part you played in their exile and the original jarl's return.

Several jarls, however, will go elsewhere if they are ousted from government. [Jarl Korir](Skyrim_Korir.md) and his family will move into [Brunwulf Free-Winter's House](Skyrim_Brunwulf_Free-Winter%27s_House.md) in [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) and [Dengeir of Stuhn](Skyrim_Dengeir_of_Stuhn.md) will go to [Candlehearth Hall](Skyrim_Candlehearth_Hall.md). [Brina Merilis](Skyrim_Brina_Merilis.md) will move to [The Winking Skeever](Skyrim_The_Winking_Skeever.md) in [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md) and [Kraldar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kraldar) will move into [Erikur's House](Skyrim_Erikur%27s_House.md). [Maven Black-Briar](Skyrim_Maven_Black-Briar.md) will simply return to her normal routine in [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md), as will [Sorli the Builder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sorli_the_Builder) to [Stonehills](Skyrim_Stonehills.md) if either jarl is removed. [Vignar Gray-Mane](Skyrim_Vignar_Gray-Mane.md) would have gone to [Viola Giordano's House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Viola_Giordano%27s_House) if exiled, but it is impossible for this to happen.

## Notes
- The Jarl of [the Pale](Skyrim_The_Pale.md) (either [Skald](Skyrim_Skald.md) or [Brina Merilis](Skyrim_Brina_Merilis.md)) does not have a [steward](Skyrim_Steward.md).
- Strangely, Jarl Laila Law-Giver's family will not be exiled with her should the Empire take Riften. Though [Saerlund](Skyrim_Saerlund.md) possibly remains due to his Imperial sympathies, her other son [Harrald](Skyrim_Harrald.md), who is a staunch Stormcloak, will remain also, and both will still behave as if the Stormcloaks control Riften (Saerlund will still warn of Stormcloak censorship, for example). The same also applies to Jarl Balgruuf the Greater's children. They will instead hide in the basement of [Dragonsreach](Skyrim_Dragonsreach.md), likely because their initially planned roles in [The Whispering Door](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Whispering_Door) would complicate the quest should they be exiled.
- The Jarls of [Falkreath](Skyrim_Falkreath_Hold.md), [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold_(region).md), and [Hjaalmarch](Skyrim_Hjaalmarch.md) do not have [court wizards](Skyrim_Court_Wizard.md).
- Brunwulf Free-Winter is the only Jarl who never has a [housecarl](Skyrim_Housecarl.md).
- Kraldar was originally the incumbent jarl of Winterhold, with Korir being a farmer and his possible replacement. At some point this was swapped around, but dialogue referring to their original roles can still be heard during Season Unending.
- If you join the Stormcloaks and complete the [Battle for Solitude](Skyrim_Battle_for_Solitude.md), instead of being exiled or killed, Jarl Elisif the Fair will be captured by Ulfric and coerced into renouncing her support for the Imperial Legion in favor of the Stormcloaks. Her steward, [Falk Firebeard](Skyrim_Falk_Firebeard.md), interprets the act of allowing Elisif to maintain her Jarlship as a masterful political stratagem on Ulfric's part.
- [Erikur](Skyrim_Erikur.md) and [Hrongar](Skyrim_Hrongar.md) were originally intended to become jarls, and still remain in the jarl faction. Erikur was to replace [Elisif the Fair](Skyrim_Elisif_the_Fair.md), who was to be murdered in [Boethiah's Bidding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Boethiah%27s_Bidding). Hrongar was to replace [Balgruuf the Greater](Skyrim_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md) after the latter was killed by his children during [The Whispering Door](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Whispering_Door). Somehow killing the incumbent jarls can trigger dialogue referencing the scrapped quests. [Bryling](Skyrim_Bryling.md) is also in the jarl faction for unknown reasons, and has dialogue recorded to grant thaneships in Solitude, implying she was also originally intended to serve as jarl at some point, possibly as a Stormcloak replacement for Elisif or Erikur, judging by her seemingly pro-Stormcloak disposition.

## Bugs
- Sitting on a Jarl's throne will activate third-person view and lock the camera in place, making it impossible to move around. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.4, addresses this issue. ([details](https://afktrack.afkmods.com/index.php?a=issues&i=12079)) This is caused by the "Furniture Force3rd Person" keyword being attached to all relevant throne instances, whereas they don't need it. The patch subsequently removes the keyword from these and other thrones.
- After becoming Jarl, Brunwulf Free-Winter may lack the "Jarl" prefix to his name when most other new Jarls get it automatically. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.4, fixes this bug.
