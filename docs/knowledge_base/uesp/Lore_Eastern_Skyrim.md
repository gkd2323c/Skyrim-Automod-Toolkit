# Eastern Skyrim

—Description of an Easterner<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBBR-1)</sup> Eastern Skyrim was the kingdom that formed in the eastern half of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431) as a result of the succession schism between High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md)'s daughter, [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md), and Jarl [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) over the province's high kingship. In 2E 431, High King Logrolf was assassinated, leaving his daughter Freydis as his heir presumptive. However, Jarl Svartr of Solitude claimed that Freydis was illegitimate, forcing a [moot](Lore_Moot.md) to be convened to determine the next ruler of Skyrim. Although Freydis wore the [Crown of Verity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown_of_Verity) and the moot in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) chose her as High Queen, a partial moot in Solitude chose Svartr as High King. These events caused a schism, and thereafter, Skyrim was divided between Eastern Skyrim ruled by Freydis and her heirs and [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) ruled by Svartr and his successors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCo F-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MTCHKS-3)</sup>

Eastern Skyrim was composed of the holds of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), and [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCo F-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESSO-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LESORPZOSI-5)</sup> which collectively are the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE-6)</sup>

In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), a [Kamali](Lore_Kamal_(place).md) fleet, under the command of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal), [invaded](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) northern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and sacked Windhelm. United by a common enemy, the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Eastern Skyrim, the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) jointly defeated the Akaviri invaders and formed an alliance, the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GTTEP-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TBW-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Jt SK-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSAI-10)</sup> Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and her heir, [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md), were killed when the Kamal sacked Windhelm, resulting in a brief succession dispute between Mabjaarn's twin sons, [Jorunn the Skald-Prince](Lore_Jorunn.md) and [Fildgor Strong-Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fildgor). To prevent a civil war, Jorunn challenged Fildgor to single combat, which the Strong-Prince accepted and ended with Jorunn emerging victorious.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TBW-8)</sup> After his victory, Jorunn was crowned high king and became the leader of the Ebonheart Pact.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GTTEP-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TBW-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Jt SK-9)</sup>

Eastern Skyrim fought alongside the rest of the Ebonheart Pact during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Poba W-11)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Fildgor led a rebellion against Jorunn in Eastmarch over Eastern Skyrim's throne, but Jorunn and the Ebonheart Pact ultimately suppressed it in a decisive battle at [Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Eastmarch-12)</sup> An improvement in relations occurred between Eastern and Western Skyrim in 2E 582 after Jorunn sent aid to the west in response to the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md)'s devastating attacks in [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), and [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Jorunn-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Svana-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Irnskar-15)</sup> Later that year, Jorunn, Jarl [Svana](Lore_Svana.md) of Western Skyrim, and Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) signed a peace agreement between Eastern Skyrim, Western Skyrim, and [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Jorunn-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Svana-14)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Caddach-16)</sup>

Eastern Skyrim was eventually reunified with the rest of Skyrim into a single realm by the end of the Second Era.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TAH-17)</sup>
