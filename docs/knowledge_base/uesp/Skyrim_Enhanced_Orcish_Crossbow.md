## Orcish Smithing
Orcish Smithing is the second perk in the heavy armor branch of the [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill's perk tree. Unlocking it requires a Smithing skill of 50, and the [Dwarven Smithing](Skyrim_Dwarven_Smithing.md) perk must have already been unlocked. Orcish Smithing allows both Orcish armor and Orcish weapons to be crafted.

The Orcish Smithing perk also causes tempering of Orcish weapons and armor to be twice as effective. This perk applies to both unenchanted Orcish gear and enchanted gear ([generic armor](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md), [generic weapons](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md), or [custom](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked. Tempering of [Shagrol's Warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shagrol%27s_Warhammer) and [The Longhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Longhammer) is also improved by Orcish Smithing.

### Orcish Armor
Orcish armor is a variety of heavy armor that is better than [Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md) and [Steel Plate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate), but inferior to [Ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md). It appears in leveled lists starting at level 25 (enchanted varieties at level 26). All items can be tempered using 1 [Orichalcum Ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orichalcum_Ingot), and having the Orcish Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's orcish gauntlets, a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 14 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

### Orcish Plate Armor<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Orcish_Plate.md)</sup>
Orcish Plate is a heavy armor variant added by the [Alternative Armors - Orcish Plate](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Orcish_Plate.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md).

Every piece has a higher value, and a slightly higher armor rating than their regular counterpart, except for the helmet, which retains the armor rating of the regular version (possibly due to an oversight). It also lacks a shield variant. Its total armor rating is a bit lower than [ebony armor](Skyrim_Ebony_Armor.md).

### Orcish Weapons
Orcish [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) are relatively low-quality weapons, better than only [Iron](Skyrim_Iron.md) and [Steel](Skyrim_Steel.md) in quality. Notably, the weapons are lower quality than [Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md) weapons, even though Dwarven Smithing is a prerequisite for Orcish Smithing. They are lighter than Dwarven weapons, though. The weapons appear in leveled lists starting at level 6 (enchanted varieties at level 7) with the exception of the Orcish Battleaxe which has been left out of the standard leveled lists, making it harder to find in random loot. All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using 1 Orichalcum Ingot, and having the Orcish Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement. Arrows can only be smithed if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed. Bolts are only available with the [Rare Curios](Skyrim_Rare_Curios.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed. Crossbows are only available with the [Expanded Crossbow Pack](Skyrim_Expanded_Crossbow_Pack.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

## Notes
- Orichalcum (ore or ingot) is one of four items needed at the [Atronach Forge](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md) to create a [Staff of the Storm Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_the_Storm_Atronach).
- Orichalcum Ore can be sold to [Gharol](Skyrim_Gharol.md) or [Shuftharz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shuftharz) for the quest [Mine Ore](Skyrim_Mine_Ore.md).
- One special, quest-specific [Orichalcum Ingot](Skyrim_Quest_Items.md#Orichalcum_Ingot) is part of the quest [Hunt and Gather](Skyrim_Hunt_and_Gather.md). It has no uses -- it *cannot* be used in place of a standard ingot.
- Ore veins (all types) sometimes do not respawn normally. See [Mining (Bugs)](Skyrim_Mining.md#Bugs) and elsewhere on that article and its talk page.
