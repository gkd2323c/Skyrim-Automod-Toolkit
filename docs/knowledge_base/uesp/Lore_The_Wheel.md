# The Towers

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg/200px-OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) White-Gold, one of the Towers This article is about the metaphysical structures. For the constellation, see [The Tower](Lore_The_Tower.md).

*Well, some think that the White-Gold Tower—and some other towers around Tamriel—help, well, hold the world up, or something like that. Others believe that before the Dragon broke, the tower helped protect us from invasion from Oblivion* [...] *They help keep Mundus—the World—from dissolving back into Oblivion. Or something like that. Anyway, everyone seems to agree it has power, but no one knows exactly what kind.*
—Prince [Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md), *[The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City)* **The Towers** are ancient structures on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) which date back to as early as the end of the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md). They are immensely powerful, and are responsible for the stabilization of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) as well as a wide array of other fantastical functions. The Towers are capable of causing extreme alterations in reality that can result in [Dragon Breaks](Lore_Dragon_Break.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MDC3-1)</sup>

## The Metaphysical Tower
The Towers of Nirn are magical and physical echoes of [Ada-Mantia](#Ada-Mantia) which are believed to strengthen and reinforce the structures of Mundus.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup> The Towers are said to be idols modeled off of the metaphysical Tower,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36L21-3)</sup> and understanding both is necessary to understand either.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup>

### The Wheel
The Wheel is the structure of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). The Spokes are "eight components of chaos", believed to be referring to the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) and the Aurbis. They are the "[lent bones](Lore_Planets.md)" (i.e. [Earth Bones](Lore_Ehlnofey.md)) of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md), those who formed the earth upon which mortals tread and gave them the blending of the primordial forces of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and Padomay: "static change" or [Time](Lore_Akatosh.md). Outside the spokes is the Aurbis,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36L21-3)</sup> though this is probably better understood in context as [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), the spirit realm, the rim of the Wheel.

The spaces between the Spokes are [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), the realms of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md). Mundus is the Wheel's hub.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36L21-3)</sup>

### The Secret
The Tower represents an objective view of reality, and those trying to follow [Lorkhan's](Lore_Lorkhan.md) path attempt to achieve this view without sacrificing their own sense of agency and personhood. If a mortal is successful in observing the Tower, they can achieve [CHIM](Lore_CHIM.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-VT-5)</sup>

The [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) is often depicted in the form of the Wheel. Turned on its side, it can be viewed as a Tower.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36L21-3)</sup> At the center of the Wheel of Aurbis, lies a smaller circle, [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), or "Nirn", which is considered the heart of all creation. The Secret, when viewed from the side, is revealed to be a Tower as well.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OTDOTS-6)</sup> This is known as the "I", or the "Secret Tower". The [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and all others are built to emulate this "Tower" depiction of the Aurbis.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36L21-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-VT-5)</sup> Mundus is a reflection of the Wheel as a whole, being its hub (The Center). Mortals, living within this 'hub', are able to perceive the Tower within Mundus without leaving the 'Wheel'.

Those who reach a true understanding on the nature of the universe, the nature of the Tower, are sometimes said to know a "Right Reaching".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OTDOTS-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36L21-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIOD-7)</sup> [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) also wrote of the "secret triangular gate" of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) through which mortals could perceive the secret Tower.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36L21-3)</sup>

Those who take a favorable view of the creation of the mortal plane assert that Lorkhan came up with this idea so that others could find what he did.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-VT-5)</sup> Lorkhan's motivations for this have been heavily disputed throughout history.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SONGOM-8)</sup> All agree that he failed,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TM-9)</sup> but some assert that he sabotaged his experiment with the Tower intentionally so that others would know how to avoid doing so.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-VT-5)</sup>

## The Towers of Nirn
*The Aldmeri or Merethic Elves were singular of purpose only so long as it took them to realize that other Towers, with their own Stones, could tell different stories* [...]
— *[Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree](Lore_Aurbic_Enigma_4__The_Elden_Tree.md)* According to legend, when the Aedra undertook the creation of Mundus, the physical flesh of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) was hung on a skeleton of joints, which are known as the Earth Bones. Each of them radiated a palpable reality. The Adamantine Tower was erected for Convention atop one of these joint-points.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCAS-10)</sup>

During the Merethic Era, the Aldmeri devised a way for "Variorum Architects" to inscribe rules for new Towers. They self-refracted, with different groups following different Towers and Stones, resulting in the proliferation of the Towers which are known today,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> each of them built on other "joint-points".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCAS-10)</sup> These legends suggest that while time was linear in the Merethic Era, it was not singular: by building their own Tower, each group could create their own narrative, distinct but equal to those around it. And so, the Towers did their "Dance",<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> each harnessing their own reality and making it conform to their desires. It is speculated that the Towers will conform their spheres of reality according to the desires of whomever claims them; this is offered as a possible explanation for an apparent climate change in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCAS-10)</sup>

The Towers exist apart from ordinary space and time.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOCelarus-12)</sup> Each Tower has, for the most part, its own unique appearance and abilities. Some Towers are meticulously crafted reflections of Ada-Mantia, as is the case with White-Gold.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BOTZ-13)</sup> Others, like Crystal-Like-Law, took a more liberal approach to their designs. Some Towers are even natural formations, such as Snow Throat.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Some, though echoes of Ada-Mantia, are considered refutations of that Tower, at least one ([Walk-Brass](#Walk-Brass)) to such a great degree that it apparently refuted itself into nonexistence.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup>

Every Tower has its Stone.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> They are magical and physical echoes of Ada-Mantia's Zero Stone, by which a Tower might focus its energy to mold creation.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> While Aetherius is the source of "creatia", accessing the spirit realm is difficult and requires great expenditures.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE3Arena-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> Therefore, the general rule is that Stones cultivate creatia indirectly, borrowing surplus creatia which has washed into Oblivion from Aetherius. The Stones then graft this creatia to the terrestrial domain of their respective Towers.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup>

"Tower Lore" is notoriously riddled with scholarly disagreement and controversy.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCAS-10)</sup> Much of the lore surrounding the subject is thought to be muddled by lies and misinterpretations.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> Below are the most well-known of the Towers of Nirn, though it's possible others may exist. In some instances, a Tower's physical avatar within Mundus has been inferred.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg/200px-ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) The Adamantine Tower
### Ada-Mantia
*[A]s the world congealed into reality, the Gods made a great tower to discuss how best to proceed with the making of Mundus. The physical, temporal, spiritual, and magical elements of Nirn were set at this Convention, and the tower itself remained behind even as some of the Gods disappeared into Aetherius. Today it is the Adamantine Tower on the little island of Balfiera between High Rock and Hammerfell in the Iliac Bay. That such a humanoid structure remains the sole footprint of the Aedra speaks perhaps of the essentially mortal nature of our world.*
— *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, Third Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition_All_the_Eras_of_Man.md)* The tower on the [Isle of Balfiera](Lore_Balfiera.md) is known by many names: Tower Zero,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BOTZ-13)</sup> Adamantine, Ada-Mantia,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> Direnni,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TOA-16)</sup> and the Ur-Tower<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> are but a few. It was the site of [Convention](Lore_Convention.md), and is the single oldest building in all the known world.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BTAOM-17)</sup> Legend holds that linear time began around the tower - which defined the structure of reality within its vicinity<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> - and spread from there in a stepped fashion.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> It was this tower which would be emulated across Tamriel.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> Architects have noted that building a tower to its proportions should not be possible.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TOA-16)</sup>

Ada-Mantia's Stone is the Zero Stone, which is still within the Foundation Vault of the tower.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> It is undoubtedly filled with immense power, but has remained inert and immutable throughout history despite attempts by some of the greatest mages in history to unravel its secrets.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Once-18)</sup> It was through this Stone that the powers of Ada-Mantia at Convention determined the spread of creation and their parts in it.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup>

Convention consisted of two known decisions: the judgment of Lorkhan, and the decision of the et'Ada to either leave Mundus or stay.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BTAOM-17)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TM-9)</sup> The apex of Ada-Mantia is where [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) is said to have slain Lorkhan, impaled his Heart on an arrow, and launched it across the world.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TOA-16)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TM-9)</sup> The [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) elected to make their exit from mortal affairs, "leaving the terrestrial sphere in their excess, for its own good", due to the danger their continued presence posed to the mortal world, which was rendered highly unstable, and even to the timeless continuity of existence. With the departure of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) in the mythic sense, linear history could finally begin, and the Dawn Era ended.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BTAOM-17)</sup> Even after the Aedra departed, Ada-Mantia continued to dictate reality in its vicinity, bringing unrelenting order to the chaos of the Dawn.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup>

A different view of the events of Convention is present in the beliefs of the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md). According to them, the mortal world was actually the Oblivion [plane](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of Lorkhan, who was actually a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). Per this belief, the Aedra, the gods mortals generally worship, were actually Lorkhan's betrayers, who stole the realm from its true deity and intentionally split Lorkhan's progeny from their divine sparks, so that they themselves would be viewed as the sole exit from the current world. [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) names this the "First Tower Dictate", which he describes as *"render the mutant bound where he may do no more harm. As God of the Mundus, alike shall be his progeny, split from their divine sparks. We are Eight time eight Exarchs. Let the home of Padomay see us as sole exit"*.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Camoran-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Commentaries-20)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MDC3-1)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MDC3-1)</sup>

The façade has changed over the years, but the core of the tower has remained entirely the same: a single great, seamless, impregnable spire of ageless metal which is at least half-embedded in the ground.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE1High-21)</sup> It is entirely smooth, except for one point known as the Argent Aperture which is thought to be a door. Adjacent to the Zero Stone, this door has a lock of thirteen slowly counter-rotating rings. It has never been opened.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Once-18)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/ON-place-Red_Mountain.jpg/200px-ON-place-Red_Mountain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Red_Mountain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Red_Mountain.jpg) The Red Tower of Vvardenfell
### Red Tower
*The vast Volcano of Tamriel, this giant mountain dominates the north of Morrowind. It is a small continent all to itself, riven from the rest of Morrowind by the remains of a colossal crater* [...] *Legend attributes its birth to the fall of a god to earth* [...]
— *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, First Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Morrowind.md)* When the Merethic elves took their own paths, the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) followed Red-Heart.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> When the Heart of Lorkhan fell to the ground in what is modern-day [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) (also known as the Red Tower) rose around it, the second of the Towers whose creation can be directly attributed to the Aedra themselves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TM-9)</sup>

Auri-El launched the Heart of Lorkhan across the world because he could not destroy it. It is the Heart of the World, and merely laughed at his efforts. As the Aedra sought to leave the world for its own good at [Convention](Lore_Convention.md), the raw power of the Heart was essential in order for the world to survive in their absence.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TM-9)</sup> The Dwemer found the Heart buried deep beneath the mountain, and High Craftlord [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md) sought to harness its power in the building of a mechanical god for the Dwemer.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup> This is where the story of the Tower of [Walk-Brass](#Walk-Brass) begins.

Using Kagrenac's achievements, four Chimer eventually tapped into the power of the Heart: the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and Vivec wielded the divine power of the Heart for thousands of years for the benefit of their people.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup> Their former compatriot, [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), awoke beneath the mountain circa [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882). Dagoth Ur would eventually succeed in hoarding the power of the Heart of Lorkhan for himself, and the Tribunal would lose access to the stone of Red Mountain entirely.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DAP-23)</sup>

However, with the fulfillment of the [Nerevarine Prophecies](Lore_Nerevarine.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Kagrenac's bindings upon the Heart were destroyed: the Heart of the World was set free. All who had stolen power from it lost their divine spark, and the threat Dagoth Ur posed to Tamriel was quickly eliminated.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Morrowind-24)</sup>

### White-Gold
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg/200px-OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) The White-Gold Tower *But of all the Prismatic Mer, none were more presumptuous than the Ayleids of the Heartland. They built their tower in open emulation of Ada-Mantia, using as Founding-Stone the great red diamond they had uncovered: Chim-el-Adabal* [...]
— *[Aurbic Enigma 4: The Elden Tree](Lore_Aurbic_Enigma_4__The_Elden_Tree.md)* The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) built their [White Gold Tower](Lore_White_Gold_Tower.md) to reflect Tower Zero, [Ada-Mantia](#Ada-Mantia), in the center of Tamriel, in order to best echo and even amplify Ada-Mantia's mystical properties.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCAS-10)</sup> They used the [Chim-el-Adabal](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) as their Stone, as it was purportedly the crystallized blood from the Heart of Lorkhan which fell to the ground as the Heart sailed over the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md). Through their powerful machinations, their tower of White-Gold became Tower One (though history records that [Crystal-Like-Law](#Crystal-Like-Law) was erected before the White Gold Tower).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BTAOM-17)</sup> It has also been called simply the **White Tower**.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TBOTD-25)</sup> The Ayleids' purpose in building their Tower was to counteract the spiritual bleeding from Mundus caused by the [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) at Ada-Mantia, to make a focus point for reuniting the mortal realm with the divine. To this end, they entered into a pact with the Daedric Princes to harvest castaway creatia from the Void.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup>

It is noteworthy that the Ayleids built more than just the central tower: with eight lesser towers forming a ring around White-Gold, the Ayleids had built their own Wheel, which apparently gave the Tower of White-Gold a power over creatia unlike any other Tower. This special power also made White-Gold especially dangerous, for if the Ayleids succeeded in making their own Wheel, their own microcosm within a microcosm, then if and when it was meddled with, the consequences could prove absolutely disastrous.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> Indeed, some scholars believe that, were enough [Tel Var Stones](Lore_Tel_Var_Stones.md), used by the Ayleids as substitutes for the Zero Stone, to be removed from the Tower's walls, Creation could be undone and the Mundus could dissolve into Oblivion.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONPhrastus-26)</sup> The Tower's role as a focus of mystical power meant that it being pulled into Oblivion during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), could have resulted in the rest of Tamriel being pulled in with it.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-White Gold-27)</sup>

Thus the [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) Throne Room at the base of the White-Gold Tower is one of the most important metaphysical foci in all of Mundus, so much so it could almost be said to exist adjacent to all time and space in Aedric creation.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Time Lost-28)</sup> The White-Gold Tower itself is said to exist on a level beyond the physical, existing apart or detached from time.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOCelarus-12)</sup> The nature of the Tower and its Time-Lost Throne Room allows it to serve as a catalyst for the use of powerful [Time Magic](Lore_Time_Magic.md), with the Ayleids having invented a ritual that could rewrite time through the use of the [Staff of Towers](Lore_Staff_of_Towers.md) in conjuction with the Tower throne room. The ritual is highly dangerous, as it has the potential to create an "impossibly dense Aurbic gyre" of power that could create a new [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md), or a catastrophe even worse than that.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOCelarus-12)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOJosajeh-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Augur_of_the_Obscure-30)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Towers Fall-31)</sup>

In [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243), the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) came to a head, and the armies of man, led by [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md), took the White Gold Tower.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-VOF-32)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE3All-33)</sup> Though the Ayleids lost their Tower, it did not fall. Rather, the legend of Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and her new Covenant with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) began. It is said that her rebellion was blessed by Akatosh, who looked with pity upon the plight of her people. He drew his own blood and blessed Alessia with it, forging their pact: so long as Alessia's generations were true to the [dragon blood](Lore_Dragonborn.md), Akatosh would endeavor to seal tight the [Gates of Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion_Gates.md) and deny the armies of Daedra and undead to their enemies. In token of this Covenant, Akatosh gave to Alessia and her descendants the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) (whose centerpiece was the Chim-el Adabal), as well as the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). So long as the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) maintained its worship of Akatosh and his kin, and so long as Alessia's heirs bore the Amulet of Kings, Akatosh and his divine kin would maintain a strong barrier protecting the mortal plane.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TAOK-34)</sup>

Mortals did not always prove capable of holding to this Covenant. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Amulet of Kings had no master, with devastating results for all of Tamriel.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ESO-35)</sup> Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), servants of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) removed many [Tel Var Stones](Lore_Tel_Var_Stones.md) from the Tower, weakening it, before their Prince's attack on Tamriel could be thwarted.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-36)</sup> But an age later, servants of a different Prince, [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), brought about the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) by slaying the Emperor and any other who could lay claim to the Amulet, until all that was left was [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), the slain Emperor's bastard son. As Dagon and his armies rained devastation down upon the Imperial City to thwart his coronation, Martin ended the Crisis by forging a new agreement between mortals and the gods, again written in blood — this time, his own. He shattered the Amulet of Kings, transforming himself into an avatar of Akatosh and vanquishing Dagon, jettisoning the Prince of Destruction back into Oblivion.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LTD-37)</sup> With the Amulet gone, the Dragonfires quenched, and the last Dragonblood Emperor dead, the barrier to Oblivion is sealed forever.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TOC-38)</sup> At the cost of his own life, Martin ensured that the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) would never again tread upon the mortal realm.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-OBInterview-39)</sup>

### Crystal-Like-Law
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg/200px-ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg) Crystal-Like Law *I stood transfixed as the heart of my homeland was torn as if from my own breast. The unthinkable, the incomprehensible... the tower of Crystal-Like-Law cast to the ground, with all the dignity of a beggar meeting an iron-clad fist. An eternity I watched, trying to reconcile what I knew with what I saw.*
— *[Rising Threat](Lore_Rising_Threat.md)* The [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) was one of the first towers erected in emulation of [Ada-Mantia](#Ada-Mantia), possibly the very first.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BTAOM-17)</sup> In erecting Crystal-Like-Law, the elves of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) sought to focus on "dracochrysalis", or keeping elder magic bound before it could change into something lesser (an act which, ironically, required aetherial surplus).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> The Crystal Tower was a manifestation of ancient magic and a symbol for the Altmer. It protected Summerset, and connected every reality by existing in every planar realm at the same time. [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) theorized that it functioned as a doorway or watchtower, and described it as the "key to omnipresence".<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Sotha NA-40)</sup> The [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md), Summerset's greatest sages, served as the Tower's curators.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis TS-41)</sup>

The white interior of the tower was said to radiate light.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-KE-42)</sup> The Stone of Crystal-Like-Law is [Transparent Law](Lore_Transparent_Law.md), a large red crystal which sits at the top of the Tower and serves as the source of its power, anchoring the Tower to other realities.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Sotha NA-40)</sup> Transparent law was theorized by one of the Sapiarchs to be a fragment of divinity given physical substance. It was thought to absorb the "drive for perfection that marks the Altmer" and reflect it back thus "driving away any imperfections that would weaken or endanger the island". <sup>[[40]](#cite_note-t Cot T-43)</sup> Embedded within Transparent Law is a smaller gem called the Heart of Transparent Law, which is said to awaken a dark urge to seize it for themselves in those who look upon it.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-replica-44)</sup>. If the Heart of Transparent Law is removed from the crystal, the protection provided by the Tower slowly weakens. At some point in the early Second Era, the Psijic Ritemaster [Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) was compelled to steal the Heart from the Crystal Tower.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis BM-45)</sup> An [outsider](Lore_The_Vestige.md) used the divine power of the Dawnbreaker to reforge it in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOTCT-46)</sup> For the people of Summerset, the tower was the heart of their homeland, a heart which was ripped out during the Oblivion Crisis. Before Martin Septim could close shut the jaws of Oblivion, the hordes of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) attacking the homeland of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) were able to use magic to topple their tower. Crystal-Like-Law had fallen.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-RT-47)</sup> However, appearances may be deceiving, for this bastion of sorcery seemingly transcended normal conceptions, existing in many [planes](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) other than Mundus.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis BM-45)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOTCT-46)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-48)</sup>

### Snow Throat
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg/200px-SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg) Snow Throat *This is the highest mountain in Skyrim, and the highest in Tamriel aside from Vvardenfell in Morrowind. The Nords believe men were formed on this mountain when the sky breathed onto the land. Hence the Song of Return refers not only to Ysgramor's return to Tamriel after the destruction of Saarthal, but to the Nords' return to what they believe was their original homeland.*
— *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, First Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Skyrim.md)* Snow Throat, or the Snow Tower,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TBOTD-25)</sup> is represented in the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md). Its apex is said to be only "half here",<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-4)</sup> and its Stone is thought to be a cave.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MKIRC-49)</sup> It was here that many significant historical and mythical events for the Nordic people took place, not the least of which is [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) purportedly creating mankind at its summit.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-VOF-32)</sup>

In the late Merethic Era, the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) was ended atop the Throat of the World when three proto-Nordic warriors utilized an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) atop Snow-Throat to cast the dragon god [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) out of the world.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Skyrim-50)</sup> This action resulted in the creation of a Time Wound, or "Tiid-Ahraan", at the peak, which the turncoat dragon [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md) guarded against for when Alduin inevitably returned.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-51)</sup> Eventually, [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md) would come to the summit in the early First Era and built [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md), a monastery for individuals to contemplate the nature of the universe and peacefully master their [thu'um's](Lore_Thu%27um.md).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PGE1S-52)</sup>

"[The Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_The_Book_of_the_Dragonborn.md)", which is usually attributed to the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) (though it's sometimes called an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) prophecy), mentions several other Towers while holding a special significance for Snow Throat:

- *When misrule takes its place at the eight corners of the world
*
- *When the [Brass Tower](#Walk-Brass) walks and [Time is reshaped](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md)
*
- *When the [thrice-blessed](Lore_Tribunal.md) [fail](Lore_Nerevarine.md) and the [Red Tower](#Red_Tower) trembles
*
- *When the [Dragonborn Ruler](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) loses his throne, and the [White Tower](#White-Gold) falls
*
- *When the Snow Tower lies sundered, kingless, bleeding
*
- *The [World-Eater](Lore_Alduin.md) wakes, and [the Wheel](#The_Wheel) turns upon the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md).*

This prophecy is shown on the right-hand side of [Alduin's Wall](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md#Alduin.27s_Wall) in Sky Haven Temple, where the Snow Tower is depicted as a cracked mountain.

The Prophecy's reference to the Snow Tower has been widely construed as referring to the plight of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), wherein High King [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) was killed and the province was launched into a bitter civil war. Following this event, Alduin was at last able to re-emerge from the Time Wound and began the process of rebuilding his dragon army. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) would climb the Throat of the World and learn the thu'um the ancient Atmorans used to weaken him through the use of the Time Wound and another Elder Scroll.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Skyrim-50)</sup>

### Green-Sap
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-place-Elden_Root.jpg/200px-ON-place-Elden_Root.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Elden_Root.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Elden_Root.jpg) The Elden Tree, one of the great Graht Oaks *As for the Osseous Orrery: can anyone really claim to comprehend the full intentions of Anumaril in his metamorphosis to instrumentality? Do we even know how many axes of circumpenetration its arms rotate through?*
— [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md), *Commentaries on Beredalmo*<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Orrery Load-53)</sup> The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) did not build a Tower; they grew it, a great [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) whose roots sprang from a Perchance Acorn. This was their Stone. And because the Acorn might perchance have been elsewhere, thus was Green-Sap manifold and several. Each could walk, and sing their own songs. From the interplay of the manifold Green-Sap, the Bosmer learned the songs which "made the trees dance", and which dances they desired to perform.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> In other words, each Green-Sap was able to sing their own song, tell their own story — create their own reality. And the Bosmer took joy in this, and in picking the song which appealed to them, as it was a reflection of their own nature.

Each Green-Sap was also every Green-Sap. Within each were told all the stories of the Green, with every ending true, though none any truer than another. When the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) caused the Ayleids to flee Cyrodiil, many of them were welcomed by the Bosmer of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), so long as their cousins agreed not to dissonate the greensong of Green-Sap. All agreed to this, save the arch-mage Anumaril.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup>

Before the taking of the [White Gold Tower](#White-Gold) by [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) during the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243),<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-VOF-32)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE3All-33)</sup> Anumaril created the [Staff of Towers](Lore_Staff_of_Towers.md), which was composed of eight segments, each one a semblance of a Tower in its Dance. White-Gold Knights took seven of these segments and carried them off to hide them in distant Fold-Places. Anumaril left with the eighth, disguised as a femur - this was Segment One, a semblance of Tower One, and thus also a reflection of Tower Zero.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg/200px-ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart_of_Valenwood.jpg) The Valenheart in Hectahame [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/ON-interior-The_Orrery.jpg/200px-ON-interior-The_Orrery.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Orrery.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Orrery.jpg) The Orrery of Elden Root When Anumaril fled to Valenwood, he still carried with him Segment One. He asked the Great [Camoran](Lore_Camoran.md) to show him Green-Sap, and was brought to one that by happenstance stood then in [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md). There, he found a Perchance Acorn. To this golden nut, Anumaril showed the segment. For the first time, the greensong knew only one ending. And so, that ending became definite, and the Perchance Acorn became a Definite Acorn. That [Elden Tree](Lore_Elden_Tree.md) would not walk again.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup> This tree, also called the First Tree, or the Great Tree, is found in the [Elden Grove](Lore_Elden_Grove.md). Life itself seems to be stronger around it,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TBOTGT-54)</sup> and it is believed to be the tree that seeded all of Valenwood.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ACOV-55)</sup> As written in an Ayleid prophecy, "All things to the Tree. From the Tree, all things".<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TBOTGT-54)</sup>

Using his dentition as tonal instruments, Anumaril dismantled his bones and built of them a Mundus-machine that mirrored Nirn and its planets. And when he had used all his substance in fangling this orrery, the Orrery of Elden Root, he placed the segment-sceptre within, hiding it between the Moons. Through these efforts, he had hoped to convert Green-Sap into White-Gold, and thereby remake the Ayleids' realm. But Anumaril's plan went awry for reasons he could not have anticipated. His Ayleid magic was concerned with "Will, and Shall, and Must—but under Green-Sap, all is Perchance".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup>

According to legend, if any person stepped into the center of this orrery when it was activated, it would reveal their inner self. Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md), younger brother to Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), learned this lesson well shortly before his death circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). After betraying his sister and entering the orrery, he briefly glimpsed a vision of the White Gold Tower, and then found himself painfully transformed into a large [ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md). He flew into a rage and was quickly killed.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ESO-35)</sup>

Anumaril's plan did not necessarily fail, as his machinations presumably continue. So, while the ending to the story of Green-Sap is determined, that ending still remains a mystery.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4TET-11)</sup>

### Orichalc
Orichalc is one of the most mysterious of the Towers; little is known beyond the name.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-AOD-56)</sup> Some Redguards venerate [Diagna](Lore_Diagna.md), the "Orichalc God of the Sideways Blade", an avatar of the [Hoon Ding](Lore_HoonDing.md) which managed to "achieve permanence" and thus entered into the pantheon of the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-VOFCR-57)</sup> The Stone of Orichalc is said to be a sword.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MKIRC-49)</sup> According to a story, Diagna crashed his fist against The Hoon Ding Gong and swords fell from the walls of the Orichalc Tower like needles from a dry pine.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-LAB-58)</sup> It is suspected that Orichalc was found in [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), and may have been related to the [Lefthanded Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md). With Yokuda thought to have gone underwater, Orichalc is supposedly sunken along with it.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-AOD-56)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), while aiding the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) in restoring the [Staff of Towers](Lore_Staff_of_Towers.md), the [Augur of the Obscure](Lore_Augur_of_the_Obscure.md) described it as an "ugly, sunken, long-forgotten [thing]" when the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) found the fragment associated with this Tower.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-AOD-56)</sup> This commentary lends credence to the theory that it sank with Yokuda, although the Augur was regarded by the Psijics as a notorious liar when it was not being asked a direct question.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-59)</sup>

### Walk-Brass
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg/200px-MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) Sketch of Walk-Brass In Walk-Brass, also called the Brass Tower and the Brass God, the Dwemer sought to make their own god. Their ambulatory, usually anthropomorphic Tower is better known to most as the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) or Anumidium.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TBOTD-25)</sup> The book *[Divine Metaphysics](Lore_Divine_Metaphysics.md)* is an explanation of how the Dwemer planned to make this new god using [Kagrenac's tools](Lore_Kagrenac%27s_Tools.md) and the sacred tones of the Heart of Lorkhan.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-MWBaladas-60)</sup>

It is thought that Anumidium was meant to be used many times to transcend Nirn. High Craftlord Kagrenac conceived its construction, which required that Dwemer sacrifice their souls to create its body, with the intention of leading them to transcendence and salvation.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Po M-61)</sup> Kagrenac's plan was interrupted by Dagoth Ur's discovery of his intentions, which led to the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) wherein all Dwemer on Nirn disappeared.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup> Misuse of the golem they left behind (which, historically, has proven to be *every* known use) is believed to have terrible long-term metaphysical consequences.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-WWYDB-62)</sup> Some believe that the Numidium is intrinsically evil: a ranking [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) refers to it as the "most profane" of the Dwemer's creations, a "walking horror", the "Antipodal-God-Thing that reigns on the darkest pole of the sacred Nirn-Sphere", and the "welded knot at the center of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) that must be untied". The Apostles considered the Numidium the greatest threat to [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s goals of bringing about the Nirn-Ensuing and the Anuic convergence of Anuvanna'si. Indeed, in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Clockwork City's](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) supervising Factotum, Aios, would name Numidium first among the existential threats to the City.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Aios-63)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Ti S5-64)</sup>

Pieces of the golem, called Numidium, fell into the hands of the Tribunal, who eventually traded them to [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) as part of their [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) with the latter's [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896). In the town of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), it was slowly reassembled and finally activated.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-WWYDB-62)</sup> Though it was meant to be infused with the Heart of Lorkhan, Tiber's Battlemage [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md) devised a way to create the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md) to function as the Numidium's heart.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TAH-65)</sup> Tiber was able to conquer the whole continent using the Numidium, becoming the first Emperor of Tamriel and ending the Second Era.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TAH-65)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-MWDarius-66)</sup> The Numidium was used again in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417) to bring about the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Miracle_of_Peace.md) in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region,<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-67)</sup> but it was apparently destroyed in the process.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-MWJanand-68)</sup>

These successes served as inspiration for a new golem, known as [Akulakhan](Lore_Akulakhan.md) or the Second Numidium, which Dagoth Ur was building beneath Red Mountain as part of his plan to drive the foreigners out of Morrowind and then conquer Tamriel. Like Anumidium, Akulakhan was to be powered by the Heart of Lorkhan.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DAP-23)</sup> When the Nerevarine freed the Heart from Kagrenac's bindings in 3E 427, this Second Numidium was destroyed.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Morrowind-24)</sup>
