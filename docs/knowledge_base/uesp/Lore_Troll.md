*The troll has certainly haunted [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) folklore from time immemorial, known to nearly every culture and appearing in tales of all sorts, from children's fables to the histories of the ancients. Indeed, they are mentioned in [Khosey](Lore_Khosey.md)'s venerable "[Tamrilean Tractates](Lore_Tamrilean_Tractates.md)," which speak of "ye Trohl of Rapace, thrice-orbed Enormity [that] Eateth ye Fleshe of [both] Commons and Kyngs."*
*After our long history on Tamriel with them, we [men](Lore_Men.md) and [mer](Lore_Mer.md) should really know quite a bit about trolls, should we not? Perhaps so—but we don't.*
— [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) in *[Field Guide to River Trolls](Lore_Field_Guide_to_River_Trolls.md)* Trolls (known as **Trohls** in ancient text)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> are large, [ape](Lore_Ape.md) -like,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBPrima-2)</sup><sup>:212</sup> bestial humanoids endowed with three eyes.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup> Known as formidable monsters throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> they have a powerful regeneration ability that makes them almost unkillable, but they also have a crippling weakness to [fire](Lore_Elements.md#Fire).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RT-4)</sup> Trolls are found all over the continent, mostly in caves and forested areas.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SR-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTROLL-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TVS-8)</sup> They are typically reclusive creatures; their lairs are usually far from commonly traversed paths.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> They are also truly ancient creatures,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BLATROLL-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Old Life-10)</sup> having roamed Nirn since the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Serpent-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TAO-12)</sup> Despite this, the origin of trolls is completely unknown to scholars,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> and much about their [reproductive](Lore_Sex.md) biology is not well understood.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NORP-13)</sup>

## Biology

### Morphology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/BL-event-A_Darker_Dungeon.png/200px-BL-event-A_Darker_Dungeon.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-event-A_Darker_Dungeon.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-event-A_Darker_Dungeon.png) An Abyssal Troll, a variant of troll with prominent horns (Blades) *The first step in your hunt is the proper identification of your quarry. Trolls are roughly man-shaped, with lengthy, muscular arms that end in claw-tipped fingers. The creature's large mouth is filled with jagged teeth, all the better to crunch the bones of foolish hunters who didn't purchase my book.*
*Without a doubt, the troll's most distinctive and unusual feature is the [third eye](Lore_Third_Eye.md) nestled in the center of its forehead.*
— *[Troll Slaying](Lore_Troll_Slaying.md)* Trolls possess a distinct ape-like physique, characterized by long arms and short legs, which facilitates the knuckle-walking they tend to do.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup> Their surprisingly swift movements can defy expectations for a creature of their size.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup>

Trolls are known for their formidable strength. When they attack, they typically employ their powerful arms and sharp claws to overpower their prey,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TSG-15)</sup> and they are strong enough to hurl large objects such as boulders.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTROLL-7)</sup> They are sometimes known to attack with their legs as well,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-5)</sup> and are capable of leaping an impressive distance.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTROLL-7)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup> Their jaws are said to be powerful enough to snap off a [horse](Lore_Horse.md)'s leg in one bite.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Scared Frost-16)</sup>

Trolls are well-known for being three-eyed,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> though two-eyed,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Arena T-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-BLUD-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BMGrahl-19)</sup> and even one-eyed trolls are also known to exist.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-20)</sup> There is also some evidence for the existence of five-eyed trolls,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TBConcept-21)</sup> though tales about them are considered farcical.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Mo NF-22)</sup> Most trolls have tapered ears, while some sport additional features such as horns,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTROLL-7)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-BLTROLL-24)</sup> or manes of thick, scraggly hair.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TMN-25)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTROLL-7)</sup>

Trolls are known to occasionally grow to tremendous sizes,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTROLL-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-The Menagerie-26)</sup> sometimes even rivaling the size of [Giants](Lore_Giant.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Kyne C-27)</sup> Trolls are capable of living for centuries,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BLATROLL-9)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TAO-12)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SS-S-28)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-AJUD-29)</sup> possibly due to their unique regeneration ability. Trolls are said to have been much larger and more powerful during the chaos of the Dawn Era.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Serpent-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TAO-12)</sup> The [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) notably claimed that "troll blood has thinned in the modern era".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Serpent-11)</sup>

Trolls have been known to carry parasites or [diseases](Lore_Disease.md) such as [gutworm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gutworm) and [winter worn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winter_Worn).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-30)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-31)</sup> [Troll thirst](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll_Thirst) is a disease one can get in the wilds of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), where trolls are known to frequent.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-32)</sup> Trolls were susceptible to corruption from the [Wildburn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-33)</sup>

### Regeneration
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png/200px-LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png) Fire is a troll's greatest weakness [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-icon-ingredient-Blood2.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-icon-ingredient-Blood2.png) Troll's Blood (Daggerfall) *Would a small piece of troll meat continue to grow, so the pantry would constantly restock itself? I think so, but I need a piece of troll meat to test this theory. Also, there's the fire issue. Troll meat really doesn't react well to fire, and I can't see the foresters eating raw troll flesh.*
— *[A New Recipe?](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_New_Recipe%3F)* Trolls possess the unique trait of being able to rapidly regenerate their tissue,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-BAJ-34)</sup> an ability that is only annulled if exposed to fire.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup> Their own [blood](Lore_Alchemy_T.md#Troll_Blood) is also known to have healing properties.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Al Blood-35)</sup>

There have been rare cases where a troll's regeneration can prevent their death completely, making the scorching of its corpse the only way to truly kill it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RT-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Arena T-17)</sup> Though some claim that needing to burn trolls to permanently kill them is a myth.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-BAJ-34)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-36)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBPrima-2)</sup><sup>:55</sup> One cook theorized that troll meat could continuously regenerate even after their death,<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ANR-37)</sup> and it has been alleged that the eyes of taxidermied troll heads are still surprisingly alert, following people's movements hungrily.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-38)</sup>

The exact nature of troll regeneration is somewhat mysterious. The ability is of great interest to certain battlemages, and they have occasionally tried to acquire live specimens to study it.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup> One [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) who lived among trolls believed that praying to the [god](Lore_Gods.md) [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) would unlock the secrets of their regeneration.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TH-39)</sup> There exists a strange form of life-giving energy that was capable of imbuing other creatures with a regeneration ability akin to that of trolls.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-WOTUD-40)</sup>

## Ecology

### Diet
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/SR-wallpaper-steam-Troll.jpg/200px-SR-wallpaper-steam-Troll.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-wallpaper-steam-Troll.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-wallpaper-steam-Troll.jpg) A frost troll dining in its lair Trolls are formidable carnivores that have strong enough jaws to eat any part of a carcass, including bone. In his book *[Field Guide to River Trolls](Lore_Field_Guide_to_River_Trolls.md)*, the scholar [Phrastus](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) described trolls as capable of "devouring anything they can catch down to fingernails and marrow".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> The author of *[Troll Slaying](Lore_Troll_Slaying.md)* similarly commented on a troll's ability to bite through bone.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup> The remains of the hunter [Svenja Snow-Song](Lore_Svenja_Snow-Song.md) were recovered from the stomach of the infamous troll known as the [Uderfrykte Matron](Lore_Uderfrykte_Matron.md) with her limbs, her torso, and her head still largely intact.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-UM-41)</sup> Her husband, [Agnar the Unwavering](Lore_Agnar_the_Unwavering.md), described her death as instantaneous, with her body being "consumed by the beast nearly whole".<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-42)</sup>

Trolls have voracious appetites, and are characterized as being "always hungry".<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Hungry-43)</sup> Members of the humanoid races are common prey of trolls.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-44)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Hungry-43)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Krish Dulk-45)</sup> An [Iron Orc](Lore_Iron_Orc.md) known as Gulzog the Butcher fed the trained trolls in his care the remains of human captives, and instructed those serving under him to do the same.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Ca F-46)</sup> Children are warned of the dangers of troll contact, such as in *[A Gold Coast Children's Bestiary](Lore_A_Gold_Coast_Children%27s_Bestiary.md)*, which conveys its warning of troll predation in rhyme.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-47)</sup> On occasion trolls will even capture humanoid prey alive to save for later.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-GD-48)</sup> Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) was known to have rescued several people that had been taken hostage by trolls.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Vt US-49)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-BLDN-50)</sup> Frost trolls will sometimes hang their human prey from the ceilings of ice caves.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-51)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-52)</sup>

Despite these examples, humanoids are not an essential part of a trolls diet,<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-53)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Lowland-54)</sup> and they can be satisfied with other meats.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-The Menagerie-26)</sup> They have been known to kill and eat livestock.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-20)</sup> There are also a few accounts of trolls who drink [alcohol](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Trollcide-55)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-56)</sup> Trained or captive trolls are sometimes sated with ale,<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Ca F-46)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-The Menagerie-26)</sup> and drunk trolls were known to attack travelers in the Pale.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-57)</sup> Trolls are also occasionally preyed upon by other creatures. [Sabre cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md) native to the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) region of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) will often seek out and hunt trolls as a pack, even as cubs.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-58)</sup>

### Behavior
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-creature-Troll_Rockthrower.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Troll_Rockthrower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Troll_Rockthrower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Troll_Rockthrower.jpg) A troll throwing a large rock (ESO) Despite their typically reclusive nature,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> trolls are known to often live in patriarchal packs.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TSRN-59)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-60)</sup> These packs are led by powerful male alphas,<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-RWQ-61)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Tograz-62)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-63)</sup> sometimes called Troll Kings.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-64)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ATA-65)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-TKMobile-66)</sup> Female trolls are referred to as she-trolls,<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-MTH-67)</sup> troll infestations can often be traced back to a Troll Mother, a particularly powerful she-troll who may have moved into the area. Eliminating the mother is an effective means of ending the threat, although the individual trolls will still need to be hunted down.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-TTH-68)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-AJUD-29)</sup>

According to the council of healers at the [Imperial University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), the reproductive biology of trolls is not well understood.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NORP-13)</sup> However there is at least one famous account of troll copulation. Circa [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565), unseasonably warm weather in Skyrim drove hordes of trolls across the western half of the province while locked in "unspeakably horrid acts". [High King Svargrim](Lore_High_King_Svargrim.md) personally stopped the rampage, splitting many of the mating trolls. As a result of this, trolls tend to avoid the areas closest to the walls of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). This event was later known as "the Great Troll Rut".<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-69)</sup>

Trolls are prone to entering a violent bloodlust if they smell the blood of an open wound.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Tograz-62)</sup> Trolls are afraid of fire due to their weakness to it.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-BLCG-70)</sup> Most trolls do not swim, as they usually dislike water as well.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-71)</sup> Trolls are known to occasionally steal [gold](Lore_Currency.md#Gold) or treasure.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ATA-65)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-72)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-GD-48)</sup> Some trolls seemed oddly attracted to a mysterious ore found in Lost Knife Cave in [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md).<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-73)</sup>

### Intelligence
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/AR-creature-Troll.gif/200px-AR-creature-Troll.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Troll.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Troll.gif) A Troll (Arena) *This breach is surrounded by trolls. They're a lot smarter than you think. They're banging rocks together now, sure—but once they start wearing pants? Look out.*
— [Augur of the Obscure](Lore_Augur_of_the_Obscure.md)<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Augur-74)</sup> The exact intelligence of trolls is unclear, or at least varies wildly. Trolls are generally thought to possess only rudimentary intelligence,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-AD-75)</sup> but they are acknowledged as having some cunning. As they are smart enough to be able to avoid traps,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup> or even use them to their advantage.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-GD-48)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Vt US-49)</sup> It is said trolls are easily tricked, causing some jesters to postulate that if one was attacked from enough different angles, it would be possible to confuse one to death.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Kyne C-27)</sup><sup>:212</sup> Some sources refer to them as a [race](Lore_Race.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NORP-13)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> while others see them only as somewhat intelligent animals.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-76)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Exterminate-77)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup> They are typically classified as "monsters", alongside bestial beings such as [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md), [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md), and [Harpies](Lore_Harpy.md).<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-78)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-79)</sup>

Trolls are capable of using clubs or other objects as simple bludgeoning weapons,<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-80)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Arena T-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-BLUD-18)</sup> and some have even been known to use axes or warhammers.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-81)</sup> Despite this they do not appear to be capable of making complex tools,<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Augur-74)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Ted Posts-82)</sup> and are believed to be incapable of speech,<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ATA-65)</sup> though they have on occasion been garbed with loincloths.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Arena T-17)</sup> They sometimes organize themselves into primitive tribal structures,<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-83)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-CDTP-84)</sup><sup>[[82]](#cite_note-85)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-OOa T-86)</sup> such as the Azhklan, a group of trolls that have resided in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) since the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-87)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-88)</sup> or the caliper-obsessed Kalperklan.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-89)</sup> Some trolls even seem to be capable of basic literacy.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Trollcide-55)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-CTArena-90)</sup> Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) discovered a dead troll under a bridge with a poorly scrawled note. The note appeared to be a suicide note written by the troll itself, lamenting that it wasn't scary enough and that no one paid its bridge toll.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Trollcide-55)</sup> One [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) mentioned trading with trolls for their excellent [mead](Lore_Mead.md), although this may have been just a joke.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-91)</sup>

The tamed trolls used as guards in the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) were especially intelligent. They were entrusted with complex tasks such as guarding and feeding the creatures in the tower's Bestiary, and seemed to be capable of writing in a script known as "trollish".<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-CTArena-90)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-KEPV-92)</sup> These trolls were written of in the historical fiction [King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md), set in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md),<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-KEPV-92)</sup> in addition to being encountered by the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-CTArena-90)</sup> The [Augur of the Obscure](Lore_Augur_of_the_Obscure.md), an all-knowing relic which was known to lie, claimed that trolls are more intelligent than they might appear to be, and that while they are still primitive, they may be on the verge of further advancement.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Augur-74)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-LTP-93)</sup>

## Variants

### Common Trolls
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/DS-creature-trollbody.png/200px-DS-creature-trollbody.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DS-creature-trollbody.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DS-creature-trollbody.png) Various trolls (Dawnstar) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/BL-misc-Trolls.png/200px-BL-misc-Trolls.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Trolls.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Trolls.png) Trolls of differing varieties and ages (Blades) Trolls live in a range of habitats and seem to exist in variations appropriate to their environment of origin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> The most common, and therefore best known varieties of trolls, include Cave Trolls, Forest Trolls, and Frost Trolls.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup> The brownish Cave Trolls are the most common variety of troll in Skyrim,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SR-6)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-94)</sup> but the green Forest Trolls are more common in the wilds of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-95)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-5)</sup>

The white Frost Trolls (or Snow Trolls)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup> commonly found at higher elevations or in colder climates, are much stronger than their lowland cousins.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-96)</sup><sup>[[93]](#cite_note-97)</sup> The frost trolls of Skyrim are sometimes known as "The Other Terror of the North".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-EGTS-14)</sup> Frost trolls are quite common in the ruins of [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md),<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-98)</sup><sup>[[95]](#cite_note-99)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-ARLAB-100)</sup> making the location a target for smugglers who want to obtain them.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-Cammona-101)</sup> In [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), attacks from frost trolls were one of the many problems plaguing [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md).<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-102)</sup> The rarely-seen [Grahl](Lore_Grahl.md), native to the ice caves of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), are sometimes called ice trolls.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-GEGP-103)</sup><sup>[[100]](#cite_note-104)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-LGCC-105)</sup> The native [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) do not know their true nature, suspecting them to be either great beasts or evil spirits.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Gen Grahl-106)</sup> These ice trolls have regeneration and vulnerability to fire in common with their mainland cousins,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BMGrahl-19)</sup> and some grahl possess a third eye.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-GEGP-103)</sup>

Other varieties of common trolls include Swamp Trolls,<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-OBST-107)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-108)</sup> Mountain Trolls,<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-109)</sup> and Lowland Trolls.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Lowland-54)</sup> Weak trolls are sometimes known as Trollkin.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-110)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> There are also uncommon trolls with completely black fur.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-The Menagerie-26)</sup>

### Exotic Trolls
There are several more exotic types of troll. River Trolls are an elusive breed of semi-aquatic trolls, recorded as the "Trohl of Rapace" as far back as [1E 200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_200) in the *[Tamrilean Tractates](Lore_Tamrilean_Tractates.md)*. They typically reside in riverside caves or burrows, and can hold their breath for long periods, lurking unseen in the shallows. They are clad in shiny water-shedding scales, silver-blue in color, that are both durable and flexible. Common prey for river trolls include [mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md), large [fish](Lore_Fish.md), [crocodiles](Lore_Crocodile.md), and unwary travelers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-RTESO-111)</sup> Sea Trolls are a similar type of troll. One such Sea Troll was Skullbreaker, who resided on a beach in [Silvenar Vale](Lore_Silvenar_Vale.md).<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-112)</sup>

Lava Trolls are an extinct breed said to have lived in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Their existence is believed by some to only be a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) myth. If they ever did exist, they are believed to be certainly extinct circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup>

The [Udyrfrykte](Lore_Udyrfrykte.md)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-BLUD-18)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-113)</sup> (also spelled Uderfrykte<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-AJUD-29)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TV-114)</sup> or Udefrykte)<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-SRUD-115)</sup> is the name given to members of a bloodline of legendary trolls who are the spawn of the mysterious [Uderfrykte Matron](Lore_Uderfrykte_Matron.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-AJUD-29)</sup> Her lineage has plagued the Nords since ancient times.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SS-S-28)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TV-114)</sup> The Matron was killed in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433),<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-UM-41)</sup> though at least one of her offspring outlived her.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-SRUD-115)</sup>

### Modified or Artificial Trolls
Normal trolls have sometimes been modified in unusual ways. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Iron Orcs of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) fell under the influence of the [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md). By infusing the skin and armor of trolls with [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md), the Iron Orcs hoped to raise an army of giant primordial trolls for the [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md).<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-116)</sup><sup>[[112]](#cite_note-LTEB-117)</sup> This operation was sabotaged before very many trolls could undergo the procedure, although the smuggling of these nirncrux-infused trolls out of Craglorn seemingly continued.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-Common Good-118)</sup>

Additionally, there have been cases of magically created artificial trolls. [Golems](Lore_Golem.md) in the form of animated troll [statues](Lore_Statues.md) are one such variant.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-119)</sup> [Painted Trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Painted_Troll) are a strange form of troll created by the [Brush of Truepaint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brush_of_Truepaint),<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-OBPT-120)</sup><sup>[[116]](#cite_note-121)</sup> whose [fat](Lore_Painted_Troll_Fat.md) has become a rare and sought-after ingredient due to its alchemical properties.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-SRElgrim-122)</sup> Polymorph spells exist that allow one to take on the apperance of a frost troll.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-123)</sup>

## Interactions with People

### Domestication
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-creature-Armored_Troll.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Armored_Troll.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Armored_Troll.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Armored_Troll.jpg) An Armored Troll (Skyrim) *Alright. Now take note. He'll mind you and us Dawnguard, but there's no accounting for other folk. And heed also this. He's no pet. Mistreat the beast or leave him alone for too long, and he'll return to the wild forgetting you ever existed. Now, you be careful with the creature.*
—Gunmar after selling an armored troll<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-Gunmar-168)</sup> Troll breeding is an unusual hobby of some people.<sup>[[162]](#cite_note-169)</sup> Trolls can be kept mostly docile in captivity if sated with plenty of meat and ale,<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Ca F-46)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-The Menagerie-26)</sup> and can be trained to follow commands. However, they cannot be kept as simple pets. Trained trolls that are mistreated or left alone for too long will usually return to the wild, forgetting that their owner ever existed.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-Gunmar-168)</sup> Additionally, there have been cases where otherwise tame trolls have gone into violent killing frenzies when they caught the scent of blood.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Tograz-62)</sup>

Trolls are often trained and armored for use as guards or war beasts, these armored trolls are typically armed with cestuses or similar weapons.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-DG-170)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTROLL-7)</sup><sup>[[164]](#cite_note-Chung-171)</sup> Tamed trolls have been utilized by primitive peoples such as [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md),<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-172)</sup> [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md),<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-IS-173)</sup> and the [Ice Tribes](Lore_Ice_Tribes.md).<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-174)</sup> Powerful mages, such as those in the Mages Guild, have been known to use trolls as guardians.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-AD-75)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-CTArena-90)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Arena T-17)</sup> A bank in [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) was known to use trolls to guard their treasury,<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-175)</sup> with staff even claiming that their trolls could fend off other trolls due to being [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md).<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-Vamp Troll-176)</sup> Trolls were known to be among [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s legions during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-177)</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Dawnguard](Lore_Dawnguard.md) tamed trolls native to Skyrim and used them in their crusade against [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md).<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-DG-170)</sup> Trolls are sometimes even used for manual labor; the Iron Orcs of the Scaled Court would force their trolls to dig for nirncrux.<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-178)</sup>

Trolls have occasionally been sought out for menageries or to be otherwise displayed.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-CDTP-84)</sup><sup>[[172]](#cite_note-179)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-The Menagerie-26)</sup> Trolls were among the many creatures kept in [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s Feral Gallery, a collection of creatures in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) maintained by the Daedric Prince.<sup>[[173]](#cite_note-180)</sup> At some point in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), trolls were brought to the [Padomaic Crest](Lore_Padomaic_Crest.md), where they were not originally native, as part of an attempt by the [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) to create a menagerie of beasts to rival the famous [Sil-Var-Woad](Lore_Sil-Var-Woad.md) of Summerset. Said attempt failed, and the small tribes of trolls that escaped would continuously be hunted by mercenaries trying cover up the failure.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-CDTP-84)</sup> Many frost trolls were also smuggled into the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md) by the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md), who had captured the beasts in Labyrinthian.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-Cammona-101)</sup> Trolls are also often captured for use in gladiatorial arenas,<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-181)</sup> such as in the famous [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) [Arena](Lore_Imperial_City.md#Arena_District),<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-182)</sup> or at various locations in [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md).<sup>[[176]](#cite_note-On Troll Arena-183)</sup><sup>[[177]](#cite_note-184)</sup><sup>[[178]](#cite_note-185)</sup>

### Socialization
*We were fools to think we could live so close to such creatures and live peacefully. I should've headed back to camp with you after we placed the torches down here. I thought these trolls would be different, that they would somehow understand that we didn't want to hurt them. I am now cornered and it's only a matter of time before one of the trolls decides to finish me off. I hope it is a quick death.*
— [Darkfall Cave Note](Skyrim_Darkfall_Cave_Note.md) Attempts have been made to socialize or befriend wild trolls,<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TSRN-59)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ATA-65)</sup> but these typically don't end well.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-Troll F-186)</sup><sup>[[180]](#cite_note-Dead Man-187)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-BLCG-70)</sup> For instance, when a rebellious Riekling known as Krish attempted to enlist the help of several grahl to overthrow [Castle Karstaag](Lore_Castle_Karstaag.md), the creatures turned on the Rieklings and ate many of them.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Krish Dulk-45)</sup> Trying to offer wild trolls food or goods has proven to do little towards decreasing their aggression.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-BLCG-70)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ATA-65)</sup><sup>[[180]](#cite_note-Dead Man-187)</sup>

However there have been exceptions where people have managed to live peacefully among wild trolls, at least for a time. For example: In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the Orc researcher Grularz managed to temporarily interact safely with a group of trolls in Crestshade Mine. By adopting the troll's behavior and mannerisms, they tolerated her presence, even deep into their territory. She was ultimately killed after the conclusion of her study, when she attempted to hug the troll's patriarch.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TSRN-59)</sup><sup>[[179]](#cite_note-Troll F-186)</sup> A more successful example is that of the Trinimac worshipper Ghorkul, who somehow managed to gain the trust of a group of wild trolls in his exile.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TH-39)</sup>

The relationship between trolls and [Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md) is somewhat unclear, as they have been documented having varied interactions. In some circumstances, trolls and spriggans fight against each other.<sup>[[181]](#cite_note-188)</sup> However, certain powerful Spriggans, such as Spriggan Matriarchs, are able to control and command trolls as they would other any other animal.<sup>[[182]](#cite_note-189)</sup> The [Viridian Sentinel](Lore_Viridian_Sentinel.md) of northern [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) was known for their ability to ward off trolls and other wild creatures of nature.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TVS-8)</sup>

### Troll Slaying
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/SR-concept-Frost_Troll_Fight.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Frost_Troll_Fight.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Frost_Troll_Fight.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Frost_Troll_Fight.jpg) A battle against a frost troll *I was a sapling when frost trolls attacked my small village in [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md). I survived, but I swore vengeance against any brutish ravaging creature.*
—Margretha Ire‑Heart<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-190)</sup> While trolls are seen as incredibly deadly to the average person,<sup>[[184]](#cite_note-191)</sup><sup>[[185]](#cite_note-192)</sup> certain warriors or adventurers style themselves as troll hunters or trollslayers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TS-3)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-TTH-68)</sup><sup>[[186]](#cite_note-193)</sup> One famous example was Bagrar, an Orc shield-wife who earned the title of "Troll-Slayer" through her impressive combat prowess.<sup>[[187]](#cite_note-AC-194)</sup> Troll hunters are known use troll musk to mask their scent while hunting,<sup>[[188]](#cite_note-195)</sup> and some even create enchanted weapons or special armor especially effective for fighting trolls.<sup>[[189]](#cite_note-196)</sup><sup>[[190]](#cite_note-197)</sup><sup>[[191]](#cite_note-TSmash-198)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TSG-15)</sup><sup>[[192]](#cite_note-199)</sup> The [Lord's Mail](Lore_Lord%27s_Mail.md), the famous cuirass of the [Demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md), is sometimes referred to as the "bane of trolls everywhere".<sup>[[193]](#cite_note-200)</sup> Lunar [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md), a breed of wolf from Solstheim, were allegedly bred by [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) and are competent at battling trolls.<sup>[[194]](#cite_note-201)</sup>

Often hunters without a specialization in trolls may attempt to kill the creatures, either for sport or mercenary work.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Kyne C-27)</sup><sup>[[195]](#cite_note-202)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-20)</sup> The [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) are often hired to exterminate trolls that attack farmers and travelers.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Exterminate-77)</sup> The Rite of Akatosh is an endeavor held by Cyrodilic elders during the [Old Life Festival](Lore_Old_Life_Festival.md) in preparation for the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md), in which their party goes on a pilgrimage to rid the nearby forests of "Old Life": monsters like trolls and Goblin-ken.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Old Life-10)</sup>

## Cultural Impact

### Art and Religion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/SR-book-Trollbook.png/200px-SR-book-Trollbook.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Trollbook.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Trollbook.png) Artwork of a troll from the book *Troll Slaying* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/ON-tapestry-Orcish_Tapestry%2C_Hunt.jpg/200px-ON-tapestry-Orcish_Tapestry%2C_Hunt.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tapestry-Orcish_Tapestry,_Hunt.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tapestry-Orcish_Tapestry,_Hunt.jpg) An Orcish tapestry depicting a troll hunt (ESO) *The origin of the race of trolls is somewhat obscure—which is another way of saying we know absolutely nothing about where they come from, how they got here, or why they exist at all.*
— [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) in *[Field Guide to River Trolls](Lore_Field_Guide_to_River_Trolls.md)* Trolls have been present in Tamrielic folklore "from time immemorial",<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> having existed since the Dawn Era.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Serpent-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TAO-12)</sup> They are known to nearly every culture and have appeared in tales of all sorts, from children's fables to the histories of the ancients.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup><sup>[[196]](#cite_note-203)</sup><sup>[[197]](#cite_note-204)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-KEPV-92)</sup> While their origins are considered unknown by most academics,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt RT-1)</sup> several cultures believe trolls to be inherently adversarial beings. The [Winterborn](Lore_Winterborn.md) tribe of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) believe that Malacath created trolls, along with Orcs and Ogres, to provide hardships and to test his chosen followers.<sup>[[198]](#cite_note-MATR-205)</sup> [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) believed that trolls, along with Orcs and Goblins, were among "the miscreated", malicious and brutish creatures who, as [the One](Lore_The_One.md) teaches, must be united against by all sentient folk of good will instead of striving against each other.<sup>[[199]](#cite_note-206)</sup> Skaal myth speaks of grahl as unholy beasts, and aspects of [the Adversary](Lore_The_Adversary.md).<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-Aevar-207)</sup> Some conflate trolls with [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), the [undead](Lore_Undead.md), or other creatures of [the Dark](Lore_Padomay.md).<sup>[[201]](#cite_note-208)</sup>

Trolls are typically considered natural creatures,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TVS-8)</sup> and as such have some association with the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), who is known as the "Mother of Men and beasts".<sup>[[202]](#cite_note-FWB-209)</sup> One of her most powerful [guardian spirit](Lore_Guardian_Spirit.md) champions, a being faced during the goddess's sacred trials, is known to take the form of a troll.<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-210)</sup><sup>[[202]](#cite_note-FWB-209)</sup> In addition, some people etch Kyne's War Prayer into troll teeth.<sup>[[204]](#cite_note-211)</sup> [Morachellis Hag-Husband](Lore_Morachellis_Hag-Husband.md), a legendary [skald](Lore_Skald.md), once posited the idea that a troll's third eye might be attuned to the ethereal realm of spirits parallel to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), rather than the mundane world. However, it's important to note that his views on the subject were not considered particularly reliable, as he made many other dubious claims.<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-TFG-212)</sup>

Various forms of art depicting trolls exist.<sup>[[206]](#cite_note-Plate-213)</sup><sup>[[207]](#cite_note-214)</sup><sup>[[208]](#cite_note-King Bust-215)</sup> A painting known as the "Frolicking Trolls of Eastmarch" is said to depict how [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) imagine the frost trolls of Skyrim; the painting depicts frost trolls smiling and dancing in the snow.<sup>[[209]](#cite_note-216)</sup> Small troll statues carved from [ironwood](Lore_Ironwood.md) by the Matrons of [Shor's Stone](Lore_Shor%27s_Stone.md), known as garden trolls, are used to protect gardens from unwanted pests.<sup>[[210]](#cite_note-217)</sup> Bookends modeled after trolls have been made, but they frightened potential buyers so much that few sets still exist.<sup>[[211]](#cite_note-218)</sup>

There are several types of children's toys that are made to resemble trolls,<sup>[[212]](#cite_note-219)</sup> typically dolls.<sup>[[213]](#cite_note-220)</sup><sup>[[214]](#cite_note-221)</sup><sup>[[215]](#cite_note-222)</sup> Troll claws with prayers carved into them are sometimes given to children and are intended to resemble a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md)'s claw.<sup>[[216]](#cite_note-223)</sup>

### Linguistics
Trolls are commonly referred to in Tamrielic phrases and [exclamations](Lore_Exclamations.md). "Troll's blood" is a common Nordic exclamation,<sup>[[217]](#cite_note-224)</sup><sup>[[218]](#cite_note-225)</sup><sup>[[219]](#cite_note-226)</sup> while "son of a troll" is used more by Orcs.<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-Guards-227)</sup><sup>[[221]](#cite_note-228)</sup><sup>[[222]](#cite_note-229)</sup> Various insults related to trolls include: "troll-bait",<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-Guards-227)</sup><sup>[[223]](#cite_note-230)</sup><sup>[[224]](#cite_note-231)</sup> "troll-breath",<sup>[[225]](#cite_note-ONKurog-232)</sup><sup>[[220]](#cite_note-Guards-227)</sup> "troll lover",<sup>[[225]](#cite_note-ONKurog-232)</sup> "troll-face",<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-Guards-227)</sup> and "gutter-troll".<sup>[[226]](#cite_note-233)</sup> "She-troll" is occasionally used as an insulting name for women.<sup>[[227]](#cite_note-234)</sup><sup>[[228]](#cite_note-235)</sup> Troll dung is often referred to in [expletives](Lore_Profanity.md) and insults,<sup>[[164]](#cite_note-Chung-171)</sup><sup>[[229]](#cite_note-236)</sup> typically by Orcs.<sup>[[157]](#cite_note-SBHam-162)</sup><sup>[[230]](#cite_note-237)</sup><sup>[[158]](#cite_note-Stilga-163)</sup> "When the troll dung hit the [horker](Lore_Horker.md)" is a phrase that refers to something going horribly wrong.<sup>[[231]](#cite_note-238)</sup>

Trolls are the namesake of certain spells, such as "Troll's Blood", a regeneration spell, and "Troll Strength", a strength fortifying spell.<sup>[[232]](#cite_note-239)</sup><sup>[[233]](#cite_note-240)</sup> Those born under the sign of [the Lord](Lore_The_Lord.md) are sometimes referred to as having the "Trollkin curse" due to their innate weakness to fire.<sup>[[234]](#cite_note-241)</sup> Some Nords have "Half-Troll" as their last name,<sup>[[235]](#cite_note-242)</sup><sup>[[236]](#cite_note-243)</sup> but considering typical Nordic [naming conventions](Lore_Nord_Names.md), it is unlikely this is literal.

## Notable Trolls
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/BL-creature-The_Beast_of_Gorrham.jpg/200px-BL-creature-The_Beast_of_Gorrham.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-creature-The_Beast_of_Gorrham.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-creature-The_Beast_of_Gorrham.jpg) The Beast of Gorrham (Blades) - **The Ancient One**: An ancient troll, said to have roamed Nirn since the dawn of time. Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), a group of [Aureal](Lore_Aureal.md) who served [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) were rumored to have made some sort of alliance with the beast. [The Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) ended up following these rumors through a portal and managed to slay the monstrous creature.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TAO-12)</sup>

- **The Beast of Gorrham**: A very large troll with distinct horns and spikes that attacked the village of Gorrham on the outskirts of the [Bloodfall Kingdom](Lore_Bloodfall_Kingdom.md), causing it to be burned down.<sup>[[237]](#cite_note-244)</sup> Notably, unlike most trolls,<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-BLCG-70)</sup> it did not fear fire. It was slain by the Warrior circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-BLDN-50)</sup>

- **Boulder the Blood-drinker**: An impressive troll with several feats ascribed to it. He is said to have single-handedly halted a [Colovian](Lore_Colovian.md) attack, and to have defeated three giants by himself. He was eventually captured and made to fight in the Trollfight Arena in Reaper's March circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[238]](#cite_note-Khaba-245)</sup><sup>[[239]](#cite_note-246)</sup><sup>[[176]](#cite_note-On Troll Arena-183)</sup>

- **Death-In-Winter**: An alpha troll whose pack once terrorized the town of [Darkwater Crossing](Lore_Darkwater_Crossing.md), until it was slain by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-RWQ-61)</sup><sup>[[240]](#cite_note-247)</sup>

- **Frostmaul Facecleaver**: A fearsome troll claimed to have once terrorized the back country of Reaper's March, including places like Vinedusk Village. He was eventually captured by a trainer and made to fight in the Trollfight Arena circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[238]](#cite_note-Khaba-245)</sup><sup>[[241]](#cite_note-248)</sup><sup>[[176]](#cite_note-On Troll Arena-183)</sup>

- **Icestalker**: A large, powerful frost troll who had been outfitted with [Dwarven](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md) armor by the Rieklings of the Coldsnap Tribe. It dwelt in the [Vault of Mhuvnak](Lore_Vault_of_Mhuvnak.md), a Dwarven ruin in [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), and was slain by members of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-IS-173)</sup>

- **Ironfang**: A powerful nirncrux-infused troll from Craglorn that was smuggled to the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) by the [Gold Coast Trading Company](Lore_Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.md). It was housed in the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin [Garlas Agea](Lore_Garlas_Agea.md), with it escaping being considered a threat to the entire region.<sup>[[242]](#cite_note-249)</sup> It was slain by the Vestige circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-Common Good-118)</sup>

- **The Troll King**: A gigantic and merciless troll originally from Vvardenfell. The Troll King was eventually captured, armored, and made to fight in the Blessed Crucible, a gladiatorial arena in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md).<sup>[[243]](#cite_note-250)</sup> He was slain by members of the Undaunted circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), and his head was severed and taken as a trophy.<sup>[[244]](#cite_note-251)</sup><sup>[[137]](#cite_note-King Head-142)</sup> A decorative bust, as well as arms and armor based on his appearance would also be created.<sup>[[208]](#cite_note-King Bust-215)</sup><sup>[[245]](#cite_note-252)</sup>

- **The Udyrfrykte, the Uderfrykte Matron, and the Udefrykte**: See [this article](Lore_Udyrfrykte.md).

## Notes
- A manifestation of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) that spoke to the Vestige in [Abamath](Lore_Abamath.md) called [Mauloch](Lore_Mauloch.md) a "troll-herder".<sup>[[246]](#cite_note-ESOBoethiah-253)</sup>
- [Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson), when discussing potential fates of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), once posited the idea that they were transformed into something that didn't have the ability to keep technology, using trolls as an example.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Ted Posts-82)</sup>
