[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/SR-place-Forelhost.jpg/200px-SR-place-Forelhost.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Forelhost.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Forelhost.jpg) Forelhost circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-place-Forelhost.jpg/200px-ON-place-Forelhost.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Forelhost.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Forelhost.jpg) Forelhost circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Forelhost is an ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruin with a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) located in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It is located high atop the mountain of the same name and suffers from a dangerously cold climate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>

During the time of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md), Forelhost served as a great monastery. It was home to two notable [dragon priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) and their respective [masks](Lore_Dragon_Priest_Masks.md): [Rahgot](Lore_Rahgot.md) and [Vosis](Lore_Dragon_Priest_Masks.md#Vosis). Following the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), Forelhost became the last known holdout of the cult. It was discovered by the forces of [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) in [1E 139](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_139), who besieged the monastery for several months under the command of [Skorm Snow-Strider](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Skorm_Snow-Strider).

Despite the cold, the besiegers eventually succeeded in breaking through the defenses in [1E 140](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_140), using the [Voice](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to bring down the main gate. Once inside however, they discovered that the cultists had committed ritual suicide, going so far as to poison the well water. Their sacrifice created a magical barrier, sealing the upper courtyard for decades afterwards and preventing Skorm's advance.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> The cultists had believed they would be returned to life when the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) returned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), as had been foreseen by a cultist named Thorallod.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The monastery subsequently fell into ruin, haunted by [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) and the ghosts of the cultists.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-4)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) was scouring the Rift for shards of [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md), a group of cultists led an attack on Forelhost when their leader, Nordahl, attempted to recover the mask of Vosis. The cultists were defeated by the undead and driven off, and Vosis claimed their axe shard for himself. He was then slain by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) on behalf of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), which was also seeking shards.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOuest-5)</sup>

Sometime circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a legendary [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) thief known as Sails-Through-Storms came into possession of the journal of a great adventurer who had come into possession of [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md) and had sealed it within Forelhost. Following the journal, she journeyed to Forelhost, accompanied by the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) and a fellow Argonian thief named Swims-at-Night. The group discovered that the ring had been sealed within the tomb of a dragon priest which could only be entered by ingesting poison. Sails-Through-Storms turned back, but the other two concocted a poison which would not quite kill them, allowing them to enter the tomb. They managed to defeat the awakened dragon priest within, but were set upon by a group of treasure hunters led by [Mecinar](Lore_Mecinar.md), a former [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostle.md). In their weakened state, the adventurers were unable to stop the treasure hunters from claiming the ring and using it to enter the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). However, Swims-at-Night grabbed onto one of them at the last second and was teleported to the City along with them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Later that year, an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) named Valmir was sent by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) to retrieve Rahgot's mask from within the ruins of Forelhost. He was ordered to trick [Imperial Legionnaires](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and [Stormcloak Soldiers](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) into recovering the mask to aid the war effort during the ongoing [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), and then to bring the mask to [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md), likely in an effort to retrieve [Konahrik](Lore_Dragon_Priest_Masks.md#Konahrik). Valmir recruited the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) who successfully retrieved the mask, but discovered Valmir's ploy and killed him.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

That same year, a ghost known as the Lost Paladin was sighted at Forelhost. The Lost Paladin had been the wielder of the legendary blade [Chrysamere](Lore_Chrysamere.md) in life, and he was subsequently slain by the Last Dragonborn who then claimed Chrysamere.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>
