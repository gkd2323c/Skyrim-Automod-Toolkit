# Spear of the Snow Prince

*The spinning of that gleaming spear whistled a dirge to all those who would stand in the way of the Snow Prince, and our mightiest fell before him that day.*
— *[Fall of the Snow Prince](Lore_Fall_of_the_Snow_Prince.md)* by [Lokheim](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Lokheim)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOSP-1)</sup> The Spear of the Snow Prince is a powerful steel weapon once wielded by the legendary [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elves.md) [Snow Prince](Lore_Snow_Prince.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOSP-1)</sup> The spear was imbued with a potent [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) that both inflicted frost damage and left those it struck more vulnerable to the chill, while simultaneously corroding and disintegrating their armor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWSpear-2)</sup> [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) accounts describe both the Snow Prince's spear and his armor as bearing a radiant, fearsome glow of unknown magicka, causing him to appear more wight than warrior.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOSP-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/BM-interior-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg/200px-BM-interior-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-interior-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-interior-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg) Snow Prince's Armor, Spear, and the *Fall of the Snow Prince* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/BM-place-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg/200px-BM-place-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-place-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-place-Jolgeirr_Barrow.jpg) Jolgeirr Barrow Little is known of the spear's origins, save that it was wielded by the Snow Prince at the [Battle of the Moesring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Moesring.md). In his hands it proved devastating, cutting down Nordic champions such as [Ulfgi Anvil-Hand](Lore_Ulfgi_Anvil-Hand.md), [Strom the White](Lore_Strom_the_White.md), [Freida Oaken-Wand](Lore_Freida_Oaken-Wand.md), and [Heimdall the Frenzied](Lore_Heimdall_the_Frenzied.md). At last, however, he fell to Finna, a young girl who avenged her mother. With the Snow Prince slain, the Nords soon claimed victory. When the fighting ceased, his radiant armor and spear still gleamed, inspiring awe even in death. Though fallen foes were usually burned, the Nordic chieftains chose to honor him instead. His body was wrapped in fine silks and entombed within a barrow, while his armor and spear were placed upon a pedestal. He was denied the protection of [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md), however, as it was reserved for the Nord dead alone.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOSP-1)</sup>

The site later became known as Jolgeirr Barrow and was guarded for a time. Though the Snow Prince's helmet was stolen, his spear remained undisturbed,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OWN-3)</sup> and in time the barrow was abandoned.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ISOTF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-JB-5)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Jolgeirr Barrow was rediscovered and looted. Within, an ancient account of the Battle of the Moesring, *The Fall of the Snow Prince*, recorded by Lokheim, was recovered. It came into the possession of Athellor, an Altmer scholar of the long-believed-extinct Snow Elves, through the efforts of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ISOTF-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Athellor-6)</sup> who also located the stolen helmet elsewhere on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Frykte-7)</sup> and claimed the Snow Prince's remaining armaments, including the spear.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ISOTF-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Athellor-6)</sup>
