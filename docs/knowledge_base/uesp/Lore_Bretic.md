# Bretic

Bretic is the language of the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KEPI-1)</sup> Despite the many divides among Breton clans their language, bardic traditions, and heroic legends are a unifying common legacy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POT-2)</sup> [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) speak in their own language. Occasional Druidic words have made their way into the local language of the Bretons from the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Isobel-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LABHI-4)</sup>

## History

### First Era
Written in [1E 200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_200), the [Tamrilean Tractates](Lore_Tamrilean_Tractates.md) speak of an early encounter between a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) hunting party and the earliest Bretons, the Manmer. These Manmer were scarcely recognizable as Men, and the Nords attacked them thinking they were another strain of Aldmeri. The Nords ceased their assault when one of the Manmer spoke in "broken Nordic", and the hunting party brought word of this "newly discovered" race of Men back to [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). The Nords believed the Manmeri from beyond the Reach were descended from human slaves taken during the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1High-5)</sup> If this "broken Nordic" language is related to Bretic or Old Bretic is unknown.

Before [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md)'s integration into the Alessian Empire circa [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSOHR-6)</sup> some Breton nobility were known to write in [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md) as a sign of their pride for their Elvish heritage.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONHTOS-7)</sup>

In [1E 2327](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2327), despite being under Imperial administration, the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md)'s thriving labor communities were predominantly Bretonic in language, culture, and custom.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SH-8)</sup>

### Third Era
The Sephavre (the song traditionally sung during the observance of [Broken Diamonds](Lore_Broken_Diamonds.md)) is written in **Old Bretic**, and each generation only knows it because they were taught by their parents.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BD-9)</sup>
