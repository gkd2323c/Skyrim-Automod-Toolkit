Marius Caro was the Count of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) during the late [Third](Lore_Third_Era.md) and early [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt L-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:26</sup> He was the heir to a long lineage of counts, as House Caro had ruled Leyawiin since the early days of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGt L-3)</sup> He and his father were loved by the people of Leyawiin.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Wit-4)</sup> Circa [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), he married [Alessia Valga](Lore_Alessia_Caro.md), daughter of Count [Charus Valga](Lore_Charus_Valga.md) and Countess [Arriana Valga](Lore_Arriana_Valga.md) of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt L-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3C-5)</sup>

Many of Leyawiin's citizens were unhappy with his marriage to Alessia, as she had a strong dislike for non- [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) cultures and races, and she wished to introduce an Imperial-dominated culture to Leyawiin.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OSS-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Tsa-7)</sup> The union of Marius and Alessia was considered a perfect blend of love and wise political maneuvering, as they seemed to be well-matched on a personal level and were both staunch supporters of Imperialization in Leyawiin.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3C-5)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt L-1)</sup> There were rumors that Marius and Alessia had a secret torture room in [Castle Leyawiin](Lore_Castle_Leyawiin.md), where they tortured [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) immigrants from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-HM-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Dt S-9)</sup>

During Caro's reign, County Leyawiin was troubled by the [Renrijra Krin](Lore_Renrijra_Krin.md) insurgency, which desired to reclaim the long-disputed land that the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) had recently ceded to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), land that the insurgents felt belonged to [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3E-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AT-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Obrs-12)</sup> The Count took steps to deal with the insurgency, but he did not have enough manpower to patrol County Leyawiin's borders.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Obrs-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MC-13)</sup> Caro created the [Knights of the White Stallion](Lore_Knights_of_the_White_Stallion.md), a chivalric order dedicated to eradicating banditry in County Leyawiin.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MC-13)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), Caro believed that High Chancellor [Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) and the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) focused obsessively on the provinces and left Cyrodiil to fend for itself. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), after an Oblivion Gate opened outside of Leyawiin, the city was beset by the forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), but they received no help from the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MC-13)</sup> Despite this, he provided military aid for [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) once the Oblivion Gate outside Leyawiin was closed.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Af B-14)</sup>

After the Empire's collapse in the early Fourth Era, [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) and Leyawiin both became independent and were often at odds with each other.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:223</sup> By [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), they had been reincorporated into the Empire under the rule of [Titus Mede I](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md); however, Leyawiin remained restless.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:155, 223</sup> As of 4E 48, Caro still reigned as count, and his daughters had become infamous for poisoning his guests while they were visiting Leyawiin's court.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:26</sup>
