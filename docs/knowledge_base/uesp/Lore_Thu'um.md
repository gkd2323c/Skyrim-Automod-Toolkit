# Thu'um

"Tongue" redirects here. For language, see [Linguistics](Lore_Linguistics.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png/200px-LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png) The Last Dragonborn using the Thu'um, as seen in *[Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)* The Thu'um, also referred to as the **Masculine Breath**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1I-1)</sup> the **Storm Voice**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> or simply the **Voice**, is a form of magic most commonly practiced by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and some others which uses the words of the [language of the Dragons](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) to form "Shouts", the equivalent of spells, of immense power. The word actually means "shout" in the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) language. It is said that dragons make no distinction between debating and fighting, and so their words have always been magical and powerful, for those who take the time to learn and understand their meaning.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup> The Nords believe that [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), the embodiment of the wind who is viewed as the Nordic aspect of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), breathed onto the land at the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) to form them.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> As such, the Nords believe that their voice and breath is their very essence, and that channeling this life essence is how the thu'um operates. Those who can wield this power are called Tongues by the Nords. Most, if not all, people have some capacity for the thu'um, but it takes a particular talent and many, many years of study and training to become a Tongue. The thu'um can be used for a wide variety of purposes, anything from sharpening blades to quickly traveling across the land, even controlling animals or killing enemies. Some stories suggest that the ancient Tongues even had the power to "sing [Shor](Lore_Shor.md)'s ghost into the world".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup> The most powerful Tongues must be careful when they speak and are often gagged for safety, as their voice can cause great destruction.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> [Word Walls](Lore_Word_Walls.md) resonate with the energies of the Thu'um.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Arngeir-6)</sup>

## Origins
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/LG-cardart-Paarthurnax.png/200px-LG-cardart-Paarthurnax.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Paarthurnax.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Paarthurnax.png) Paarthurnax, who taught the Thu'um to the Nords Thu'um can be traced to ancient [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MOTLAHSF-7)</sup> with the first known human users of the Thu'um being the [priests of the Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGATD-8)</sup> who were likely granted this ability from the bond they had with their respective Dragon lord.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Zaan Dialogue-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-THOZTS-10)</sup>

In the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the dragon [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md) taught the Thu'um to the humans who would rebel against the Dragon Cult and their dragon overlords, who were worshiped as gods.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-3)</sup> These early Nords would become known as the Tongues, and began using their newfound Shouting abilities to slay the dragons.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDW-11)</sup> The three chiefs of the Tongues devised the shout Dragonrend to render [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) temporarily mortal, but were unable to kill him and were forced to employ the use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) to banish him.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Skyrim-12)</sup> Most contemporary Nords believe the Thu'um was taught directly to mortals by the daughters of Kyne, while the truth and existence of Paarthurnax are kept secret.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup>

Before and during the Skyrim Conquests around [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240), the greatest Nordic war chiefs were also Tongues: ([Derek the Tall](Lore_Derek_the_Tall.md), [Jorg Helmbolg](Lore_Jorg_Helmbolg.md), [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md)).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> They needed no conventional siege weapons when attacking a city, instead using the Voice to shout down the gates and allow their armies to storm in.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTS-13)</sup> During the first [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) invasion in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703) the invaders faced [Reman I](Lore_Reman_I.md) in a climactic battle at [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md). When they heard Reman's voice, they realized he was who they were searching for, and swore fealty to him.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-14)</sup>

## Decline
In the early First Era, Skyrim's military suffered an ignominious defeat against Morrowind at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). Present at the battle was [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md), an extremely powerful Tongue. He was disturbed by the inability of the thu'um to secure victory, and meditated for seven years on "how Strong Voices could fail".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Hrothgar-15)</sup> His eventual conclusions formed the basis of the philosophy of the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md), stating that the thu'um should only be used in times of true need, not as a routine tool of conquest. Since then, the most powerful Tongues have typically become [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), a group of monks who live at [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md), a settlement near the summit of the Throat of the World, where they silently study, meditate, and train until if and when a time of true need arrives. They speak only on the rarest of occasions, such as to announce the destiny of the great [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), who united [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) into the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and later became [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), the Ninth Divine.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Greybeards spoke again to call the [Dovahkiin](Lore_Hero.md) to High Hrothgar for instruction on how to use the thu'um.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Drag Ris-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Wot V-17)</sup>

A Dragonborn (or Dovahkiin in the Dragon tongue) possesses the ability not only to absorb the souls of slain dragons, but to thereby absorb knowledge of their language, thus learning in a short time what it would take other Tongues a lifetime of intense training and study to achieve.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Wot V-17)</sup>

## Known Shouts
- Animal Allegiance - Raan Mir tah, meaning Animal Allegiance Pack, is a shout that can call for the beasts of the wilderness to aid the Tongue in combat.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Aura Whisper - Laas yah Nir, meaning Life Seek Hunt, is a shout that reveals the life force of any and all beings nearby.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Battle Fury - Mid Vur Shaan, meaning Loyal Valor Inspire, is a shout that allows the allies of the Tongue to fight faster and with more fury.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SDB-19)</sup>
- Become Ethereal - Feim Zii Gron, meaning Fade Spirit Bind, is a shout that turns the Tongue into an intangible ghost-like form, unable to be harmed.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Bend Will - Gol Hah Dov, meaning Earth Mind Dragon, allows the Tongue to bend and control the will of others, even dragons.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SDB-19)</sup>
- Blade Sharpening - It is said that shouts can be used to sharpen blades.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTS-13)</sup>
- Blizzard - Some dragons in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) used a shout that formed a blizzard around them, similar to the [Destruction](Lore_Destruction.md) spell of the same name.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Bravery - It is said that a strong Nord can instill bravery in men with his battle-cry.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTS-13)</sup>
- Call Dragon - The name of a dragon is a shout, and calling them will summon them. The dragon is not compelled to answer the call.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Call of Valor - Hun Kaal Zoor, meaning Hero Champion Legend, is a shout that can summon the ghost of a hero from Sovngarde to aid the Tongue in battle.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Call Person - It is said that the greatest of the Nords can call to specific people over hundreds of miles.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTS-13)</sup>
- Clear Skies - Lok Vah Koor, meaning Sky Spring Summer, is a shout that can dispel storms and clouds, and even magical mists.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Command - It is said that the power of command is a common effect of shouting.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTS-13)</sup>
- Cyclone - Ven Gaar Nos, meaning Wind Unleash Strike, is a shout that can summon a whirling tornado.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SDB-19)</sup>
- Disarm - Zun Haal Viik, meaning Weapon Hand Defeat, is a shout that rips a weapon out of an opponent's hands.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Dismay - Faas Ru Maar, meaning Fear Run Terror, is a shout that forces enemies to flee in terror from the Tongue.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTS-13)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Dragon Aspect - Mul Qah Diiv, meaning Strength Armor Wyrm, is a shout that gives the Tongue the strength to deliver colossal blows, magical armor to withstand powerful attacks, and an empowered Thu'um. This shout can also summon an ancient [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) to aid the Tongue in battle.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SDB-19)</sup>
- Dragonrend - Joor Zah Frul, meaning Mortal Finite Temporary, is a shout that induces mortality on a dragon by lashing at its very soul. This shout was created by the Nords to slay these otherwise immortal beasts.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Dragon Storm Call / Meteor Storm - This is a shout commonly used by [Alduin the World-Eater](Lore_Alduin.md), though other dragons have been shown to know it. The shout turns the sky red and rains down flaming boulders from the sky in a [meteor](Lore_Meteors.md) storm.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Drain Vitality - Gaan Lah Haas, meaning Stamina Magicka Health, is a shout that drains magical and mortal energies from enemies.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SDG-21)</sup>
- Earth Spike - Some dragons in the Second Era used a shout that casts balls of fire to the ground, summoning molten stalagmites to emerge where they land. These spikes could then explode in fire and lava when an enemy walked nearby.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Elemental Fury - Su Grah Dun, meaning Air Battle Grace, is a shout that allows the Tongue to fight faster and with more fury.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Event Denouement - Event Denouement is a shout used by Barfok, Maid of Planes, to guarantee her victory in any battle she fought.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LOV9-22)</sup>
- Fireball - Many dragons have used a shout that casts a fireball towards their enemy, similar to the Destruction spell of the same name.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Fire Breath - Yol Toor Shul, meaning Fire Inferno Sun, allows the Tongue to exhale a torrent of flames.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Form Change - This shout could be used by Bhag, the Two-Tongued, though it is unknown if this refers to an ability to shapeshift or his ability to speak in two-layers.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LOV9-22)</sup>
- Frostbolt - Some dragons in the Second Era used a shout that ball of ice towards their enemy.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Frost Breath - Fo Krah Diin, meaning Frost Cold Freeze, is a shout that allows the Tongue to breathe freezing cold winds.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Heart Roaring - This is a shout used by Chemua, the Running Hunger, to turn an opponent against their own allies.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LOV9-22)</sup>
- Ice Fields - Some dragons in the Second Era used a shout that made sharp icicles of ice emerge from the ground.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Ice Form - Iiz Slen Nus, meaning Ice Flesh Statue, is a shout that encases an opponent in ice, freezing them solid.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Ice Storm - Some dragons in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) used a shout that casts a swirling storm of ice, similar to the Destruction spell of the same name.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Kyne's Peace - Kaan Drem Ov, meaning Kyne Peace Trust, is a shout that calms nearby animals into docility, making them unwilling to fight or flee.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>
- Lighting Bolt - Some dragons in the Second Era used a shout that casts a lightning bolt towards their enemy, similar to the Destruction spell of the same name.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Lightning Breath - Some dragons in the Second Era could exhale arcs of lightning towards their enemies.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ODS-20)</sup>
- Marked For Death - Krii Lun Aus, meaning Kill Leech Suffer, is a shout that weakens the armor and drains the life of an opponent, spelling their doom.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>

- Bex, meaning Open, is a shout that can open doors.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SS-18)</sup>

## Known Tongues
Tongues are individuals who have the ability to use the Thu'um. They usually require a great deal of study and training to develop their power, but in some cases the power comes naturally to them.

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-23) It is unknown if this teleportation shout mentioned in the book is simply a description of the Whirlwind Sprint shout.
2. [^](#cite_ref-30) Despite predating the "first mortal masters of the voice" Ysgramor is described in Nordic legends with feats such as "Shouting Some Sense" into the Nords,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-FSKW-26)</sup> and with "breath weighted with power sounds".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-27)</sup> This could be due to a nature possibly interlinked with Dragons - be it that he was a literal Dragon,<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-UOL4-28)</sup> or (more likely given his stature during the time period) he was a Dragon Priest.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-29)</sup>
3. [^](#cite_ref-31) Nurarion of Summerset was said to [possess a voice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Moon-Singer_Feziya-ko#A_War_Story) that could "cut lightning bolts in half and turn mountains to rubble". He was said to use this gift to calm the seas and banish [King Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md)'s storms "to the ends of the earth" with a scream. This power was granted to him by [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). While it's never explicitly called the Thu'um, these abilities align with its power.

- Akaviri swordsmen have been described as possessing a power similar to the thu'um, called "[kiai](Lore_Kiai.md)".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTS-13)</sup>
- Following his transformative process from using the Mehrunes Razor, Mankar Camoran claimed that he could now speak with another tongue and "speak fire".<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MDC1-34)</sup>
- The Red Dome Templars (also known as the Red Templars) were unstable crusaders who drank the blood of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) in order to obtain short-term martial shouting powers. They were hated by the rest of the Legion and a significant number of the members of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) wanted them dispersed. Due to this, they were mainly sent off to remote places like [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Po M-35)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-UOL4-28)</sup>
- While almost all Dragon Shouts possess only three Words of Power, there is record of a possible shout that uses four. The alleged shout "Ziil Los Dii Du", which roughly means "your soul is mine to devour", was shouted by Miraak in conjunction with the names of targeted dragons to absorb their souls.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DBMiraak-36)</sup> Whether this is a shout Miraak learned from the forbidden teachings of Hermaeus Mora or this is some other power of the Thu'um is unclear.
