# Ogrim

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/ON-creature-Orulu.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Orulu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Orulu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Orulu.jpg) An Ogrim Ogrim are large dim-witted [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) with very little intellect,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DD-1)</sup> which are described in appearance as "fat-faced [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md)".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOKOR-2)</sup> They are chiefly sent into the mortal world to menace living things for the amusement of Daedra Princes. Ogrim are primarily associated with [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DD-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup> They are sturdy and powerful,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KCAHC-4)</sup> and can regenerate health by pounding their chests.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIIGTT-5)</sup> For clothing, most Ogrim wear minimal garments such as loincloths,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-EOC-6)</sup> though the higher-ranking ones are often suited in armor.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GK-7)</sup> Due to their immense weight, belly spoons made from curved piece of obsidian are used in easing Ogrim torsos into armor or through narrow doorways.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBS-8)</sup> Tooth sharpener picks are made specifically for cleaning out the stubborn crevices between ogrim teeth.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OTS-9)</sup> Many of them wear jewelry including nipple piercings,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWOgrim-10)</sup> nipple caps adorned with [copper](Lore_Copper.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ogrim Nipple Caps-11)</sup> and nose shackles etched with jagged runes that signify an Ogrim's obeisance to a Daedric master.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Trophy NS-12)</sup>

Every Daedric Prince is said to possess legions of Ogrims among other common [Lesser Daedra](Lore_Lesser_Daedra.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md) uses ogrim as bouncers for his parties.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LA:Lyranth-14)</sup> Ogrim are also known to serve [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-NJIC-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Molag Bal often employs them as guards<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIIGTT-5)</sup> and torturers.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-APJ-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOSkord-18)</sup> The lumbering ogrim is cruel, often entertaining itself by tormenting its underlings.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIIGTT-5)</sup> Ogrim Toss is a game where a crude ball is kicked around by ogrims, though it is unclear if it has any actual rules.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Ogrim Toss-19)</sup>
