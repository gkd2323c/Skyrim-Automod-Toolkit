Fiendroths are small blue [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) pests native to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that can commonly be found wherever Daedric influence persists.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Do A-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOFiend-2)</sup> They resemble hulking, armored insects with very short antennae. Fiendroths possess steel mandibles and a venomous stinger, and purportedly rely upon the agonized cries of mortals for sustenance.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFiend Pet-3)</sup> They appear to be scavengers as they may feed on things such as [daedrat](Lore_Daedrat.md) remains and Deadlands [scorpions](Lore_Scorpion.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ao I-4)</sup> Fiendroths are known to form silken cocoons, which can be used in rituals to draw hives of fiendroths to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Cocoon-5)</sup>

Fiendroths, like [daedrats](Lore_Daedrat.md), are considered a form of common Daedric vermin that appear to exist in all corners of Oblivion, scuttling about and scavenging in much the same way that [rats](Lore_Rat.md) and [insects](Lore_Insect.md) plague [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Do A-1)</sup> Some of the realms they can be found in include: [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), and [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md). They often can be found on Nirn in places with Daedric influence. They can often be found around [Daedric Ruins](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Many appeared in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOFiend-2)</sup> They have occasionally been kept by mortals as pets,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFiend Pet-3)</sup> with some even pitting them against each other in races.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> In a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) tale about a kindly [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md), she is tricked by a bystander who was yelling about being accosted by a pack of Fiendroths, only to end up mocked mercilessly by the person they ran to assist.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
