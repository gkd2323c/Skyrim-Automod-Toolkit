# Orkey

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg) Orkey *Orkey tests all mortals, He likes to start all quarrels. A snake no one can flee, None may fool wary Orkey.*
— *[The Song of Gods](Lore_The_Song_of_Gods.md)* Orkey, also known as **Old Knocker**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vo F:TN-1)</sup> and the **Death-God**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAX-2)</sup> is the god of mortality in the religion of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). Some sources describe him as a "loan-god" of the Nords who combines aspects of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) and whose worship was taken up during [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) rule of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vo F:TN-1)</sup> while others consider Orkey to be a primal Atmoran god with Arkay instead being a fusion of Atmoran and Aldmeri elements.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAX-2)</sup> In Nordic iconography, Orkey is represented by a [snake](Lore_Snake.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSo G-3)</sup>

## Mythology
According to [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholars, the Nords believe they once lived as long as [Elves](Lore_Mer.md) until Orkey appeared; through heathen trickery, he fooled them into a bargain that "bound them to the count of winters", and the Nordic lifespan was reduced to six years due to Orkey's foul magic. Through unknown means, Shor showed up and removed the curse, throwing most of it onto the nearby [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vo F:TN-1)</sup>

Nordic legend goes into greater detail with the story of Orkey and clarifies some of the details. Their stories claim that Orkey had long plagued them, and had stolen their years away while they were still in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). During the reign of High King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Orkey summoned [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), and nearly every Nord was "eaten down" to six years old by the Time-Eater. King Wulfharth, who had become a boy, pleaded to [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) for help. Shor's own ghost then fought Alduin on the spirit plane, as he did at the beginning of time, and he won, and Orkey's folk, the Orcs, were ruined. It was by watching this battle in the sky that Wulfharth learned how to use the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to reverse what Orkey and Alduin had done to the Nords. His effort, while successful, cost Wulfharth his life.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FSOKW-4)</sup>

## Worship
Priests of Orkey are known to look after Halls of the Dead and ensure that corpses are properly consecrated and cared for.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Orkey, being a death-obsessed god who likes "all quarrels"<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSo G-3)</sup> (and perhaps became associated with the Orsimer for that very reason), is widely regarded as one of the Testing Gods of the Nord religion, those figures the religion advises to guard against rather than to worship amiably.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Dat N-7)</sup> Some Nords keep small statues of Testing Gods in their households and try to keep these gods appeased.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Contraband N-8)</sup> Nevertheless, maybe due to the established priesthood of Orkey performing embalming rites in the Halls of the Dead, some sources do not list him among the Testing Gods for the Nords, and instead list Orkey alongside Mara, Stuhn, and the other central gods.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vo F:TN-1)</sup>

According to a fragmentary translated text, The People of the Serpent were a group of worshippers from Atmora, devoted to the Serpent, whom they associated with death.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TACW-9)</sup>

## Notes
- There is a hazardous cave on [Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md) named [Orkey's Hollow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Orkey%27s_Hollow).
- In Sedulus' "Theory of Arkayn Convergence", they proposed that the Arkay originated as a fusion of aspects of the Elven deity [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes.md) with those of the primal Atmoran death-god Orkey.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAX-2)</sup>
- One of the Five Hundred [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) known as Kjhelt was a member of the Cult of Orkey.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
