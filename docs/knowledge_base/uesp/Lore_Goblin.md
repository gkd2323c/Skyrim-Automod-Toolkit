*Goblins are a bundle of contradictions. Quarrelsome, but dangerous in packs. Dim-witted, but deviously cunning. Prone to fits of screeching, but remarkably quiet when stalking their prey. I must confess a grudging admiration!*
—Scholar Morven Palielle (shortly before being killed by Goblins)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Goblins, known as the **Children of Muluk**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cho Muluk-2)</sup> or derogatorily called **Gobs**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Garfield-3)</sup> are primitive humanoids that have been found in virtually every part of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and even on islands off the coast of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MA-4)</sup> Goblins can often be found living in caves, ruins, and sewers in small clan-based societies. Goblins are fiercely territorial, and typically violent towards those not of their tribes or the more societally developed races in general. Many regard them as simply monsters or nuisances. Despite this Goblins are still an intelligent race, and are capable of living in peace with others and showing empathy.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Love Archive-5)</sup> They are the namesake of the [Goblin-ken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Goblin-ken) family, and as such are considered kin to [Ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre), [Gremlins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gremlin), and sometimes even [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).

## History

### Origins
*What the [scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) I've read say about the Goblin-kin has not been kind, Tim. Yours is a people from a different age. Don't look to prophecy to save you.*
—Sister Chana Nirine answering the question of "Goblin Tim"<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAMoth-6)</sup> The exact origins of Goblins are unclear. Goblin-kin have been described as "a people from a different age",<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAMoth-6)</sup> or as an example of "Old Life".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Some sources from [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) history have suggested that Goblins may be extra-planar in origin, originating from some other [world](Lore_Goblin_Gate.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGH-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-10)</sup>

Their origins are sometimes attributed to the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath), whom they know as Muluk, or the Blue God.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cho Muluk-2)</sup> Some [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) refer to Goblins as the "spawn of [Malak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malak)'s putrid feces",<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-11)</sup> while Redguards have likewise referred to them as "[Malooc](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malooc)'s squalor".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup> [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) believed that Goblins, along with Orcs and [trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll), were among "the miscreated", malicious and brutish creatures who, as [the One](Lore_The_One.md) teaches, must be united against by all sentient folk of good will, instead of striving against each other.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-13)</sup> Some fanciful historical parchments speak of Goblins as "Fetid Corruptions of Orcs".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GF-14)</sup>

Regardless of their origins, Goblins are recorded to be among the earliest inhabitants of Tamriel.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LScreen-15)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-KHPLScreen-16)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KHSR-17)</sup> Researchers have tied Goblins to some of the earliest inhabited sites on the continent, predating the arrival of [Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elf) from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) and [Men](Lore_Men.md) from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOMT7-18)</sup> Goblins are thought to have once had a more grand and complex culture,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOMT7-18)</sup> the workmanship of obelisks created by Goblins in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) are said to speak to a better time in Goblin history.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

### Early Contact
*These tablets show the Goblins weren't always viciously territorial. Elves and Goblins coexisted, traded—until the Elves wanted King's Haven Pass badly enough to betray that trust! It flies in the face of every existing account of this region!*
—Mehdze<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Truth-20)</sup> Goblins are native to the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), and were there when the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) first arrived.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LScreen-15)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-KHPLScreen-16)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KHSR-17)</sup>

There is a unique history between the Altmer and the Goblins, some of which revolved around [King's Haven Pass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:King%27s_Haven_Pass). Scouts of the King of Alinor discovered the passage, claiming that it would make a perfect outpost, citing its natural defenses, the network of caverns, and the fertile land, but it was inhabited by the Coral-Splitter Tribe of Goblins, who lived off the wealth of the land as an agrarian society.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KHSR-17)</sup> Because of their hospitality, the High Elves were able to establish trade with the Coral-Splitters, bargaining for the ore in the caves. The Altmer had secured trade agreements with nearly every tribe in the region, trading fish, textiles, and tools for the fruits of the Goblins hunting and trapping skills, as these Goblins were adept at capturing and taming [welwas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welwa) and [gryphons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gryphon).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-21)</sup> As trade increased, the King of Alinor became wary about the dealings since these Goblins did not swear their undying loyalty to him, so he orchestrated an agreement with the Coral-Splitter's chieftain for the crags in the east, but it did not go through.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-22)</sup> What followed was a great battle between the elves and the goblins that ended their relationship.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Truth-20)</sup> Altmer traditionally used goblins to reinforce their armies.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FH-23)</sup>

### First Era
Following the enforcement of the [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md) and the start of the Ayleid Pogrom, [King Dynar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar) was given an ultimatum by the Alessian [Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ami-El). He and his people were eventually forced to abandon [Nenalata](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nenalata) and flee to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) between [1E 372](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_372) - [374](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_374) in order to escape the Pogrom, being "nibbled to death by Goblins" on their journey there.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-24)</sup>

#### First Opening of the Goblin Gate
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg) The ghost of a giant Goblin warchief (ESO) *Filthy Ra Gada! I'll pick my teeth with your bones!*
—A Flint-Tooth Warchief At some point between [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) and [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), when the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) inhabited [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3H-25)</sup> a new breed of giant Goblins invaded via a rent in space and time known as the [Goblin gate](Lore_Goblin_Gate.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup> These Goblins were generally described as taller and less "cowardly" than Goblins of the later Eras.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKF-26)</sup> They possessed a huge army of fighters, and appeared in the middle of the province, where they would pillage and raze towns to the ground, driving the Dwarves out of the province.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGH-8)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-27)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup> The giant Goblins would go on to establish strongholds all over Hammerfell,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKF-26)</sup> and would eventually make enemies of the [Corelanyan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) Altmer who settled in [Salas En](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salas_En) circa [1E 522](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_522).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-28)</sup> [Elves](Lore_Mer.md) and later [Manmeri](Lore_Breton.md) established an outpost at what is now the city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) in order to protect fisherfolk and seafaring merchants from inland Goblin raiders, [desert Orc](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Desert_Orcs) marauders, and other hazards of the desert.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3H-25)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-EGH-29)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png/200px-ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png) Depiction of Frandar Hunding standing victorious over many Goblins In [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780), the first recorded warrior wave of the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) occurred, lead by [Frandar Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frandar_Hunding). After years of sailing, they eventually arrived in Hammerfell circa [1E 785](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_785) or later,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-So A-30)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONSHV2-31)</sup> and discovered the vast numbers of giant Goblin strongholds littering the entirety of Hammerfell.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKF-26)</sup> Redguard scribes describe the encounter as them being confronted by an immense Goblin horde, baying and praying to their god Malooc the Horde King.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-EGE-32)</sup> Frandar refused to allow these Goblins to exist, and so went to war with the giant Goblins of the Flint-Tooth tribe.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKF-26)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Garg-33)</sup> The Goblins were led in battle by Malooc himself, and were sometimes known as Malooc's Horde,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-T26OFSIU-34)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-EGE-32)</sup> but Malooc fled east when the army of the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding) overtook his Goblin hordes.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-VOF-35)</sup> Despite his initial success in driving many of the Goblins out of Hammerfell,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-RGTalisman-36)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-DFHoliday-37)</sup> Frandar would be killed during one of the bloodiest battles of the campaign.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKF-26)</sup>

The Goblins would receive reinforcements from the Goblin gate, launching an invasion on the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) settlers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGH-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup> Frandar's son [Divad Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divad_Hunding), would go on to win the war by repelling their invasion.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKF-26)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SDH-38)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGH-8)</sup> He and his band of [Sword Singers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword-singers) worked their sacred forges and consulted eldritch tomes for many long years to create five great swords, a mix of [Shehai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shehai) and [smithing](Lore_Smithing.md), which five of those great [Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ansei) warriors took and rose to the sky, using their power to close the Goblin gate.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SDH-38)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DHGhost-39)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup> By some accounts, this also cursed the Goblins who were left behind, diminishing them into their modern-size.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TTT-40)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKF-26)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup> Though some modern scholars consider the war with the giant Goblins a myth,<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-DFHoliday-37)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup> the victory of the Redguards is still celebrated during the annual [Festival of Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Festival_of_Blades).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-DFHoliday-37)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-T26OFSIU-34)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Contra Bowl-41)</sup>

#### Second Invasion
In [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808), another warrior wave of the Ra Gada would arrive on mainland Tamriel. The Redguards would continue to fight against and drive Goblins and their Orcish brethren from Hammerfell.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-AMOB-42)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TTT-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE1-43)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-44)</sup> The constant Goblin aggression led to the Redguard mages of old enchanting special shields to safeguard their bearers from the impish menace.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-45)</sup> Many years after their battle with Divad Hunding, the giant Goblins would return with a new invasion,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGH-8)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DHGhost-39)</sup> having managed to open the path from their world to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) again.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-9)</sup> The Redguards struggled greatly against their horde, as the giant Goblins received a constant supply of arms and reinforcements from the re-opened Goblin gate.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGH-8)</sup> The powerful Ansei [Derik Hallin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Derik_Hallin) was responsible for reassembling the five magic swords which Divad had previously used close the Goblin gate. He and his companions hurled the swords into the rent in the sky, sealing the giant Goblins in the void between their world and Nirn forever.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGH-8)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DHGhost-39)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-DHShrine-46)</sup>

#### Founding of Orsinium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg) Old Orsinium (concept art) At some point during the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), hundreds of Goblins, Orcs, Ogres, Gremlins, and other beastfolk were set free by the rulers of the Summerset Isles, and allowed to settle lands north of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). They chose to settle in an uninhabited mountain region known as the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains), described as close to [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), as the Orcish tribes were dependent on rare, shaggy, giant [centipede](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Centipede) herd-beasts that can only survive at high altitudes. Here, they would found the first city of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE1-43)</sup> The exact dating of this event is unclear, but various pieces of evidence place the founding of Orsinium in the ninth or tenth century of the First Era.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-WJOTB-47)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-BSHK-48)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-PGE3O-49)</sup> Some [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) historians believed the Goblin tribes never actually lived in the city itself, instead maintaining a truce with the Orc clans in the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains), but never mingling.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-AMOB-42)</sup>

In [1E 874](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_874), when the Orcish Warlord [Thulgeg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thulgeg)'s army of Orcs and Goblins was driven from Hammerfell by the Redguards, the Bretons of [Bangkorai Garrison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Pass) denied them passage through the Pass and forced them to flee northeast, trudging all the way through the Dragontail Mountains before they finally reached Orsinium. Not a single Goblin made its way through the pickets into High Rock.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-BSHK-48)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TM-50)</sup>

#### Warlord of the Dragontail Mountains
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg) The Dragontail Mountains (ESO) In an event theorized by scholars to take place sometime between 1E 874 and [1E 950](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_950),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> and sometime after the foundation of the Redguard city of [Ojwambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ojwambu), a surviving giant Goblin warlord by the name of [Mahgzoor Rockhand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mahgzoor_Rockhand), who had escaped the Curse of Divad through a cunning trick, became warlord of all the Goblin-ken tribes in the Dragontail Mountains. Mahgzoor rose his great blade 'Bone-Hewer' and roared in a mighty voice like an earthquake, and declared that the day of vengeance, at last, was at hand. In what was believed to be retaliation for his people being driven from Hammerfell,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-AMOB-42)</sup> he led his "Endwise army" down the Dragontail Mountains and into Hammerfell, and the people of the [Fallen Wastes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fallen_Wastes) fled from this Goblin army to Ojwambu, where the city became full of refugees. As the people started to leave the city to travel to the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md), Goblin scouts saw that the Redguards had a considerable number of Ansei warriors, and the smallest Goblin scout was forced to return to Mahgzoor by the other scouts. Once this scout returned, Mahgzoor struck his head off and then told the Goblins to destroy the crop fields and block up the rivers so that nobody in the city could eat or drink, unaware that only the elderly Derik Hallin and his few students were in the city. After a long time of waiting, Mahgzoor became suspicious as the city should have starved at that point, so he called forth his Goblin shamans, asking them if the Redguard warriors were just "shadows". To see if this was the case, the shamans sacrificed twin infants and sent a scullion to the East Gate, and this scullion was killed by a spear from Hallin. The shamans returned to Mahgzoor and told him that they were real warriors, and in response to this, Mahgzoor was enraged and killed the shamans. He then ordered his army to get ready to assault the city. This assault lasted for 17 days, where no Ansei survived except for Hallin and at the end of this assault, Hallin and Mahgzoor clashed swords for a whole day. Eventually, both were mortally wounded, with Hallin striking off the head of the Warlord Mahzgoor. They both died, but Hallin was smiling knowing he had won. The Redguards who had been using this time to train with the Book of Circles then marched to retake the lands captured by the Goblins, renaming Ojwambu to Hallin's Stand.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TTT-40)</sup>

### Second Era
Goblins were not exempt from catching the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) between [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560) and [2E 603](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_603). The Flu caused the green skin of Goblins to become yellow and have buboes blister around their mouths.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-EGE-32)</sup> It would also cause them to go into coughing fits, which meant that the shamans could not cast magic properly.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-KC-51)</sup>

In the Second Era, the prophecies of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) made references to Goblins and Goblin-kin, although to a lesser extent than in the past. The scrolls were said to not speak kindly about the Goblin-kin, as they were a people of a different age.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAMoth-6)</sup>

Circa [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), Goblins were known to once again inhabit Orsinium,<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE1-43)</sup> as well as the Goblin Caverns of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md). Many people who lived on the island believed that the Goblins of Stros M'Kai were banished beneath the earth thanks to Frandar Hunding.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-RGTrithik-52)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-RGTalisman-36)</sup> Some Redguards who lived there would warn Goblin-ken, such as Gremlins, to return to their caves, lest Hunding turn them into stone.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-RGTrithik-52)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-RGMariah-53)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/TR-creature-Warchief_Kurog.jpg/200px-TR-creature-Warchief_Kurog.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-creature-Warchief_Kurog.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-creature-Warchief_Kurog.jpg) Warchief Kurog Goblins were known to be found all throughout Tamriel during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), and many were known to serve in [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s legions.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-54)</sup> Some were among the creatures guarding the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md)'s cell in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) sewers.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-55)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-56)</sup> Sometime during the Imperial Simulacrum, a band of Goblins led by a [necromancer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) called [Golthog the Dark](Lore_Golthog_the_Dark.md) attacked and looted the palace of [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md). These Goblins also stole a parchment that held clues on the location of [Fang Lair](Lore_Fang_Lair.md). The Goblins were known to be hiding in the citadel of [Stonekeep](Lore_Stonekeep.md), so Queen Blubamka informed the Eternal Champion of this. The Champion would go on to dispatch the Goblins and retrieve the parchment.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-57)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-58)</sup>

According to one account of [the Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md)'s backstory, in [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405) a Goblin cloaked in shadow and armed with only a dagger snuck into the court of [Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tamrielic_Emperors) [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md). This Goblin was thwarted by the Agent, who according to this account was a Knight's squire at the time.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-59)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), many Goblins were imported from the Summerset Isles by [King Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Helseth) of Morrowind to be used as an army. They were brought to the sewers of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), where they were trained and kept as soldiers by two Altmer trainers. They had two war chiefs, Durgoc and Kurog. These Goblins and their Altmer trainers were killed by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-60)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Rock Biter Goblins and the Bloody Hand Goblins were engaged in a Goblin war because the Rock Biter Goblins stole the tribal Totem Staff from the Bloody Hand Goblins. This was preventing the town of [Cropsford](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cropsford) from being built, so the farmers who were building Cropsford asked the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to help them. It's not fully known how the hero dealt with the Goblins, but the war was ended and Cropsford was fully finished.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-61)</sup> In this same year, the Sharp Tooth and White Skin tribes were engaged in a war due to the White Skin totem being stolen by the Sharp Tooth tribe. The White Skins' chief was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) named Goblin Jim.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-62)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/SR-creature-Gogh_%28combat%29.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Gogh_%28combat%29.jpg)](File_SR-creature-Gogh_(combat).jpg.md) [](File_SR-creature-Gogh_(combat).jpg.md) Gogh wielding the Spear of Bitter Mercy (Skyrim) Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), the town of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) took part in a campaign against three Goblin tribes who were working together and with a bandit group known as the Greencaps, these tribes were the White Ghosts, the Red Fangs and the Iron Skulls. An Imperial envoy known as Gaius Platorius was in charge of dealing with this Goblin "infestation" on behalf of the Empire, and worked alongside the [Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) to destroy these alliances. The Warrior destroyed these alliances by performing false flag attacks on each tribe, planting each tribe's banners at the site of Goblin massacres, and stealing a statue of the Blue God. This caused the alliance to collapse, but the female Goblin shaman, known as Arruluk Snakemother, who had caused the alliance to occur was still alive, so Gaius ordered the Warrior to kill her, thus ending the proliferation of the goblins in the area and the potential of another Goblin alliance from forming.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-63)</sup>

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a tribe of Goblins migrated out of Cyrodiil using subterranean tunnels beneath the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). They emerged into the Rift and took up occupation of a cave known as Gromm's Pass. The hunter of this group, a Goblin named [Gogh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gogh), was seemingly blessed by [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) as he was in possession of the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy). Gogh was fiercely protective of the weapon, which rejected anyone else that tried to claim it. The tribe subsequently encountered a helpless Orc from the nearby stronghold of Largashbur, who happened to be stained blue by the [Steel Blue Entoloma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy_S#Steel_Blue_Entoloma) mushrooms which grow in abundance within Gromm's Pass. Believing him to be the Blue God, an aspect of Malacath worshipped by Goblins, the tribe quickly made him their shaman and began to obey him as their god. However, the tunnel to Cyrodiil collapsed soon after, cutting the tribe off from any reinforcements. Gogh then spotted the Orcish shaman applying blue dye to maintain his appearance and was thus sentenced by the Orc to death, however, the rest of the tribe defended the Orc not knowing this. The Last Dragonborn saved Gogh by killing the Orc and his remaining Goblin followers. Gogh thereafter entered into the Dragonborn's service and continued to wield the Spear and its powers in battle.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-BINF-64)</sup>

### Arms, Armor, and Crafts
*You might look at the gear sported by a fully-equipped Goblin warchief and think, "What a mismatched conglomeration of primitive paraphernalia." But you would be wrong. Each item that warchief is wearing was carefully selected for its proven utility, and represents a decision backed by millennia of tradition. This is a style of arms and armor we ethnographers call the "Primal," and it is as distinctive and recognizable as any other culture set.*
— [Crafting Motif 13: Primal Style](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crafting_Motif_13__Primal_Style) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Coldsnap_Style.jpg) Arms and armor of the Coldsnap tribe Goblins take a rather traditional view to their outfits, with some pieces even dating back to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). This is called the "Primal" culture set by ethnographers, however, it is unknown if they inherited it from previous generations, looted it from ancient ruins, scavenged it from other civilizations, or forged it themselves.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-RM13P-79)</sup> Much of their scavenged equipment is said to be of poor quality, and falls apart easily.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GF-14)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-KC-51)</sup>

Goblins are commonly known to make things out of bones, such as their arms and armors.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Goblin Weapons-80)</sup> They are also known to carve femurs into flutes.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Gob Frek-81)</sup> Goblins have been known to string their bows with the tendons of beasts.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Eyepierce-82)</sup>

Goblin-ken such as those in the Coldsnap Tribe, have been observed progressing into a basic metal age by using scrap metal from [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruins, with them creating daggers by fusing scrap together in open fires and beating it into a rough blade form. Prior to the Dwarven influence, their helmets were either hide or bone, with a troll crest being most prized. After recognizing the superiority of metal, however, the Goblins then widely employed a horned skullcap reminiscent of a troll. The tribe also began to embed [animunculus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) scrap into layers of baser metals to create shields, which greatly improved the strength of these shields. As the Goblins became more confident with metalworking, they created swords of fused metal, which although heavy, are very sharp.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-CSS-83)</sup>

Goblins were likely the only inhabitants of Stros M'Kai between the Dwemer's disappearance and the Redguard colonization, perhaps allowing them to develop more than Goblins typically do. They developed tiny waterwheels made from iron and the flexible bark of tropical trees, which were then attached to Dwemer steam pipes to power tools.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-84)</sup>

The [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) clans were known to be particularly advanced, having the ability to create and inscribe magical runes onto handcrafted obelisks, with some saying that the workmanship speaks to a better time in Goblin history.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-85)</sup>

There are a couple of artifacts relating to Goblins, such as Shadowsilk gems, which grant the user the appearance of a Goblin. Another is the Emerald Chalice, which when drunk from, turns the drinker into a Goblin, the only way to reverse this is by using blood from a human.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-86)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-87)</sup>

### Animal Husbandry
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-npc-Coldsnap_Frostbiter.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Coldsnap_Frostbiter.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Coldsnap_Frostbiter.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Coldsnap_Frostbiter.jpg) A Goblin and his Durzog pet (ESO) [Durzogs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durzog) are reptilian dog-like creatures that Goblins raise for war and to hunt down their victims.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FH-23)</sup> Goblins also heavily use durzogs for a multitude of other uses, mostly relating to the crafting of items requiring hides, Goblin belts for example are made of the hides. Goblin durzog handlers often have thick hide gauntlets. The Coldsnap Tribe uses the guts of durzogs to string their bows. Some Goblin Chiefs have also been known to make makeshift saddles to ride durzogs into battle.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-KC-51)</sup> Many tribes practice more advanced husbandry and breeding of durzogs,<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-ED-88)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-89)</sup> some creating war mounts known as "Warzogs".<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-90)</sup> Some have characterized the durzog pets of Goblins as being more intelligent than their masters.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FH-23)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-ED-88)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-KC-51)</sup>

Goblins have been known to tame other creatures as well; they often raise rats for food,<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-OBPrima-91)</sup><sup>:53</sup> and some keep [skeevers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skeever) as pets.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-92)</sup> The Goblin tribes of Morrowind have been known to farm large arthropods such as spiders and kwama.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Muck V-93)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ARL-94)</sup> The Goblins of [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) are one of the many pastoral societies there to have domesticated [Welwa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welwa).<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-95)</sup>

### Diet
The diet of Goblins varies, with some tribes raising and grilling [rats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rat) for meat,<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-96)</sup> Goblins are believed to have over a dozen ways to prepare rat meat.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-97)</sup> [Sugar leaf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sugar_Leaf) is also a favorite for Goblins.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-98)</sup> Goblins will also eat rotting vegetables, bread, and meat of any type. Goblin tribes also commonly farm different types of mushrooms with different properties in caves, with the Goblins in [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and the Western Reach Goblins being particular examples.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-99)</sup> The Goblins of the [Valus Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valus_Mountains) go back and forth between [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) using tunnels and secret passes. Some tribes have imported subterranean agricultural practices from their cousins to the east, namely the farming of kwama and spiders.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Muck V-93)</sup>

## Interactions with Other Races

### Conflict
Current Goblin society is known to be primitive, and often poses a threat to the more advanced societies that surround them.

### Cooperation
*Tired of Elf-speak. All lies! Not want any of you, just to be left alone.*
—Chief Koth Goblins generally appear to coexist more often with other races of Goblin-ken and Orcs than they do other humanoid races. Freed Goblin slaves were among those responsible for the original founding of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) along with Orcs, Ogres, and Gremlins.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE1-43)</sup> Goblin tribes have commonly allowed Ogres within their tribes,<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ESOgre-108)</sup> with the Ogres often ranking high in them or leading them.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-BGServants-109)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-110)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-111)</sup>

Goblins are also more than capable of not being hostile to humans who observe them, with the Stonechewer Tribe shamans stopping the warriors from killing a scholar known as Nellic Sterone, with there being no further incidents of hostility and them tolerating his presence as long as he kept a distance from the female Goblins and their offspring.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-SROTS-68)</sup> Certain bottles of clear, odorless green liquid are sold to mask one's scent from Goblins.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-112)</sup> There are even accounts of Goblins guiding children back to their villages out of empathy.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Love Archive-5)</sup>

Goblins have also been known to want to be recognized as equals, and have worked with groups like the [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance) to attempt to achieve this,<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-113)</sup> where they formed a contract that the Goblins thought would make the Elf kings and lords stop harming them. The Goblin chief Koth was told that the Veiled Heritance had lied to them, and instead of going mad, just said that the Goblins wanted to be left alone. Koth had previously been a slave and attempted to ask for better living conditions from his Altmer master for him and his fellow Goblins.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-114)</sup><sup>[[112]](#cite_note-115)</sup>

Scholarly pursuits into the intelligence of Goblins are often met with rejection, as the premise that Goblins can be domesticated is viewed as "ridiculous" by academia.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ARL-94)</sup> One such avenue of research was a scholar who had pointed out the fact that Goblins are not as primitive as they are perceived, saying that Goblins are capable of taming creatures such as [spiders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider) and [kwama](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama).<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ARL-94)</sup>

### Slavery
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/AR-prerelease-Slave_Market.gif/200px-AR-prerelease-Slave_Market.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-prerelease-Slave_Market.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-prerelease-Slave_Market.gif) A slave market with Goblin slaves
(Arena Pre-release) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/ON-npc-Goblin_Slave.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Goblin_Slave.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Goblin_Slave.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Goblin_Slave.jpg) A House Dres goblin slave (ESO) Goblins have been slaves in many societies, with the Altmer having goblin slaves to do menial tasks such as mucking out dung pits and cleaning floors, often being beaten by their elven owners, this has been known to give the goblins forms of mental trauma, with escaped goblins still sweeping and being frightened of others.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-byz-100)</sup> The Altmer also use heavy leg-irons for restraining Goblin slaves who are flight risks.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-116)</sup> Goblins have also been used traditionally by the Altmer to supplement their armies.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FH-23)</sup>

[House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) also keeps goblin slaves.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-AD-117)</sup> Some [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) sympathize with goblins due to this fact and wish to free these goblins, as they have experienced slavery often at the hands of House Dres.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-118)</sup> Goblins were also used by Dunmer as slaves for mines when the [Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was formed in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), due to Argonian slaves being freed. These goblins were controlled by the Dunmer by them owning the goblin tribe's totem, one example of this was the Gnaw-Root Tribe, who rose up against their Dunmer master in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by taking back their totem, making the goblins think they were unstoppable.

The [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) are known to enslave the goblins of the surrounding isles, who they use for labor and fresh blood.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MA-4)</sup>

Goblins require a lot of education before they are slave worthy. However, they are worth a lot, and they were a highly valued commodity in the Second Era, one slave trader having sold an entire tribe to a high ranking political minister in High Rock.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-AD-117)</sup>

### Uses and Cultural Impact
Some people have been known collect Goblin teeth or toenails,<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-119)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-120)</sup> using the teeth as paperweights or to make blessed charms.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-121)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-122)</sup>

Some have been known to craft small [pewter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pewter) "Pocket Goblins" that are said to bring good luck when dropped into any drink.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-123)</sup>
