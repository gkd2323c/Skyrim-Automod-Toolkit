# Vamidium

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/ON-mount-Dwarven_Horse.jpg/200px-ON-mount-Dwarven_Horse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Dwarven_Horse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Dwarven_Horse.jpg) A Dwarven Horse Vamidium is the [Dwemeris](Lore_Dwemer_Language.md) word for "mount".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-horse-1)</sup> It is used to refer to the many different types of [animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) constructed by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) for use as steam-powered mechanical mounts. Many of these constructs were made to imitate nature, taking the form of animals commonly used as mounts by the other [races](Lore_Races.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Like other animunculi, vamidiums appear to utilize [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) and [dynamo cores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dynamo_Cores) as a power source.

Although primarily used as a terrestrial means of transportation, vamidiums are also capable of swimming.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> The Dwemer did not rely entirely on mechanical vamidiums for use as mounts. For example, the existence of ancient Dwarven [horse](Lore_Horse.md) armor points to the use of war horses by the Dwemer.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Many vamidiums were collected by the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and stored in the vaults below the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). When the forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) overran the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), many of these rare artifacts were smuggled out of the city and began to appear on the open market all over Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Dwarven vamidiums are not to be confused with [fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md), partially organic mechanical constructs created by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) which are also sometimes used as mounts. They should also not be confused with "Dwemervamidium", the name for steam-assisted semi-mechanized heavy Dwarven armor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## Varieties of Vamidium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/9/9d/RG-constellation-Atronach_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Atronach_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Atronach_02.png) Depiction of a Dwemer Warmachine The Dwemer did not restrict themselves to a single design when constructing mounts for their use. This is evident in the sheer number of animal-based designs that exist, and the different combinations of materials that have been noted in recovered specimens.

- **Dwarven Bears** are vamidiums which resemble [bears](Lore_Bear.md). Guylaine Marilie, a noted Dwemer scholar, speculated that Dwarven Bears were reserved only for Dwemer of high rank or status due to their impressive size and intimidating appearance.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
- **Dwarven Camels** are vamidiums built to resemble [camels](Lore_Camel.md). They are most often found among the ruins of the desert-dwelling [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) clan of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
- **Dwarven Guars** are vamidiums which resemble [guars](Lore_Guar.md). They are most often found at Dwemer sites in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), as might be expected given the widespread use of guars as pack animals and mounts in that region.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/ON-mount-Ebon_Dwarven_Senche.jpg/200px-ON-mount-Ebon_Dwarven_Senche.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Ebon_Dwarven_Senche.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Ebon_Dwarven_Senche.jpg) An Ebon Dwarven Senche - **Dwarven Horses** are automata constructed to look like their [living counterparts](Lore_Horse.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-horse-1)</sup> Though usually made of so-called [Dwarven Metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md), there have been rare examples of vamidiums with parts forged from one of the rarest materials on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), a super-hard whitish alloy known as [Dwarven Adamant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Adamant).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
- **Dwarven Senche** are vamidiums which resemble the [senche-tigers](Lore_Senche-Tiger.md) of southern Tamriel. Scholars of the Dwemer have long wondered why they chose to make animunculi resembling senche-tigers, as Dwemer civilization was restricted mainly to northeast Tamriel, where the only large feline is the [sabre cat](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> An unusual kind of Dwarven senche with key mechanical components forged from [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and [ebony-steel](Lore_Ebony-steel.md) alloys has been discovered.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>
- **[Dwarven Spiders](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md)** are a common and highly versatile type of animunculi, found in almost every Dwemer ruin. Although often used for combat or repair purposes, large Dwarven Spider vamidiums were also created.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Several types of Dwarven Spider mounts have been repurposed by the nomadic [Baandari](Lore_Baandari.md) peddlers of Elsweyr for use transporting goods over long distances, often retrofitting them with magical lunar compasses to aid in navigation.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

- Dwarven Spiders composed of ebony alloys also exist.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ebony Spider-15)</sup> However, those composed of an [Ebon Steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebon_Steel) alloy rather than the usual [Dwarven Metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md) are rarer, and highly prized by collectors. <sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The Infernium Dwarven Spider are a dark red variant that is capable of emitting searing flames, having seemingly been created by a single Dwemer artisan using the facility known as the Infernium Forge.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Chroma-Blue Dwarven Spider is the rarest of all Dwarven Spider vamidiums, powered by a gem that emits a cold blue light which is said to reflect the frigid stolen soul of the Dwemer engineer trapped within it.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

- **Dwarven Wolves** are vamidiums which resemble the large [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) often used as mounts. They are most often found in proximity to Dwemer ruins in northern and western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where wolves are most common in the wild. The loping gait of these constructs is said to give the most comfortable ride of any mechanical mount.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Though usually made of so-called Dwarven Metal, there have been rare examples of silver-colored vamidiums with critical components made of [vitrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vitrine), a sort of Dwemeri [glass](Lore_Malachite.md). Of all the known vamidiums, vitrine Dwarven wolves are one of the rarest.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>
