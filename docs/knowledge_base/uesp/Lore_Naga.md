- *"Nasty characters. Puff adders with legs and arms, seven feet tall, eight when they're mad. Come from the inner swamp, and they don't like it here much so they're particularly peevish."* — *[The Argonian Account](Lore_The_Argonian_Account.md)*

Nagas are a breed of [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) that live in the inner parts of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Nagas have some distinct differences in their appearance compared to the Argonian most commonly seen throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They are described as having "huge mouths filled with dripping needle-like fangs". Nagas range from anywhere between seven to eight feet tall, though the [Naga-Kur](Lore_Naga-Kur.md) tribe found within [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) appears to be about the same height as the common variety of Argonian. Naga are sometimes called 'puff adders with legs and arms', suggesting [snake](Lore_Snake.md) -like features.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAA-1)</sup> Like other Argonians, Nagas lay eggs, and Naga hatchlings are able to walk right after they hatch.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Naga tend to be drawn to war and violence more than other breeds of Argonians.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Jela-3)</sup>

Nagas also live on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice.md), although their numbers have steadily dwindled still the arrival of the [Corelanya](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). By the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the Nagas of Solstice were scattered across the island and their tribe had been subsumed into the [Tide-Born](Lore_Tide-Born.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## Culture
When [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) interests were attempting to maintain plantations in Argonia, the Nagas generally acted as highway robbers; however, following the cessation of these efforts, they have since left the roads and have gone back into the inner swamps.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAA-1)</sup> They and several of the other [Middle Argonia](Lore_Middle_Argonia.md) tribes are reputed to kill outsiders.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAA4-5)</sup>

### [Blackguards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackguards)
The [Blackguards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackguards) were a bandit group consisting mainly of Nagas that operated out of [Blackrose Prison](Lore_Blackrose_Prison.md) in the mid [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). This prison was home to some of the Empire's worst prisoners and the Naga suffered the most at the hands of the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) jailers. The descendants of these Naga captives violently took over the prison and called themselves the Blackguards, reclaiming the prison to be their fortress and wielding the very chains that once bound them.

Drakeeh the Naga became the "raj-kaal", the leader of the Blackguards. Originally only consisting of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), the Blackguards would later open membership to "dryskins", the non-Argonian races. The Gauntlet was born as an arena into which were thrown people that questioned Drakeeh's leadership and people who tried to leave the Blackguards. The Blackguards' contraband operations were profitable and widespread, stretching as far as [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) and [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) were tasked with putting an end to the nefarious activities of the Blackguards. The Undaunted launched an assault on Blackrose Prison, which the Blackguards had repurposed as a fortress, and managed to slay Drakeeh the Unchained.

### [Naga-Kur](Lore_Naga-Kur.md)
The [Naga-Kur](Lore_Naga-Kur.md) (known as the **Dead-Water Tribe** in Tamrielic) is a tribe of Nagas, a rare breed of [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md). Their village is known as [Dead-Water Village](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dead-Water_Village) and they control vast swaths of northern [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) and are greatly feared by the Argonians of the surrounding villages.

### [Sul-Xan](Lore_Sul-Xan.md)
The [Sul-Xan](Lore_Sul-Xan.md) is a tribe or cult of Nagas once led by Flame-Herald Bahsei, who worship chaos and [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) as the Razor Prince and the True Egg-Child of [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md). Their homes are known as the legendary [Xi-Tsei](Lore_Xi-Tsei.md) xanmeer, the conquered [Rockgrove](Lore_Rockgrove.md) and Silent Halls, and they raid the surrounding region of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) and are greatly feared by the Argonians of the surrounding villages. They perform ritual sacrifices and create wide soul snares with the intent to summon powerful spirits of destruction.

### [Tide-Born](Lore_Tide-Born.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village.jpg) Shell-Tide Village The [Tide-Born](Lore_Tide-Born.md), also known as the **Izta-Ahuak** are one of the two [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) cultural groups native to [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) (The other being the Stone-Nest) and have various settlements on the western half of the island. Their ancestors arrived to the island prior to the [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) and thus, unlike their mainlander cousins, they are not linked to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) and instead revere [the Tides](Lore_The_Tide.md). This relates to their belief that the Tide-Born are the reborn memories of Argonians with an unfinished destiny.

The Tide-Born also have a few known sub-tribes found around Western Solstice; [Salt-Cured](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salt-Cured), Palm-Backed and [Mihuitleel](Lore_Mihuitleel.md). In recent times, they also absorbed extant members of a destroyed Naga tribe once found on the island.

## Notes
- The [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree belonging to the Dead-Water Tribe has blue flowers instead of the usual red. It is unknown if this is specific to their tribe or to Nagas in general.
- A prerelease version of *[Tribes of Murkmire: Ghost People](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Tribes_of_Murkmire:_Ghost_People)* described the Naga as similar to [serpents](Lore_Snake.md), [frogs](Lore_Frog.md), or "[cuddlefish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cuttlefish)".
