He who permits their words and deeds to languish breaks his own blade and casts it to the burning wind."*
—The Unveiled Azadiyeh<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POE-1)</sup> **The Unveiled** Azadiyeh, the **Songbird of Satakalaam** was a renowned and well-known [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) historian and cultural scholar of the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), traditionalists dedicated to preserving their [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) roots.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LA-2)</sup> She authored numerous texts of a scholarly,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SJA-3)</sup> religious, artistic,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BBS-4)</sup> agitative,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POE-1)</sup> and didactic nature,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ZMH-5)</sup> detailed various locales of historical significance to her people,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Loading1-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Loading2-7)</sup> and critiqued the politics of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), High King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) and [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POE-1)</sup> She was also a [spellbinder](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GOT-8)</sup>

## History
*Take up the sword. Draw strength from the ancestors. Forge yourself in the fire of righteousness and you too will be sharpened with true honor."*
—The Unveiled Azadiyeh<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WWSC-9)</sup> Little is known of Azadiyeh's past. She was a Crown follower of the Yokudan pantheon from [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md), who eventually became known as a scholar and author, earning her title Songbird of Satakalaam.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LA-2)</sup>

At some point she attended a sermon by High Priest Zuladr of the High Temple, who condemned womanhood as inherently wicked, citing examples like the Temptress Shakhari and Queen [Jezerei](Lore_Jezerei.md). He argued that monsters like [hags](Lore_Hag.md) and [hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md) are all women and therefore wicked. She questioned this view, pointing out that men have also committed atrocities, such as Grandee Kwarizm who killed his wives, and noting that typically male creatures like [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md), [giants](Lore_Giant.md), and [minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md) are also considered evil. Even [Sep](Lore_Sep.md), the God-Deceiver, was male. Confronting the priest about his bias, he called her immodest. Undeterred, she challenged him further by disrobing and asking if this was the wickedness he feared. He reacted with desire, but she pushed him away, emphasizing that a true woman doesn't share her gifts with those who don't respect them. Her account of these events was published under the title *[Hags, Harpies, and Hagravens](Lore_Hags,_Harpies,_and_Hagravens.md)*.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-HHH-10)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-place-High_Temple.jpg/200px-ON-place-High_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-High_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-High_Temple.jpg) High Temple of Satakalaam [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr_02.jpg) High Priest Zuladr Following Hammerfell's decision to join the Daggerfall Covenant, she authored *[Plea for Open Eyes](Lore_Plea_for_Open_Eyes.md)* in an attempt to dissuade her people from supporting King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md)'s war efforts and instead advocate a return to their traditional ways.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POE-1)</sup> She also authored *[Blessed, Blessed Satakalaam](Lore_Blessed,_Blessed_Satakalaam.md)*, an ode to the Yokudan pantheon,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BBS-4)</sup> and *[Sentinel, the Jewel of Alik'r](Lore_Sentinel,_the_Jewel_of_Alik%27r.md)*, a retelling of the Yokudan legend surrounding the establishment of Sentinel,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SJA-3)</sup> *[The Hunger of Sep](Lore_The_Hunger_of_Sep.md)*, a tale of Yokuda, the ancestral Redguard homeland, warning of the dangers posed by the Time of Ending.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HOS-11)</sup> She also penned *[Ghosts of the Old Tower](Lore_Ghosts_of_the_Old_Tower.md)*, recounting the tale of Captain Fayda, which she presented to a vizier seeking her wisdom,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GOT-8)</sup> as well as *[Zakhin's Many Heroes](Lore_Zakhin%27s_Many_Heroes.md)*, a didactic tale for children.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ZMH-5)</sup>

Azadiyeh met Lady Clarisse Laurent in Satakalam at some point before 2E 582. As of then she was a young poet. She shared with Laurent that the modern tradition of Walkabout, a rite of passage for Redguard youth, was inspired by the ancient Yokudan legend of the Hero's Labors. In this tale, a great warrior from the Dawn Era journeys through the zodiac, facing a challenge at each constellation.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-12)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she answered questions regarding the topics of Redguard culture through correspondence. Among the topics she addressed were the Redguards' stance on the potential connection between [Tava](Lore_Tava.md) and other gods of similar spheres and iconography from different cultures, and their views on the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) and possible links to the Redguard deity [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md). As a Crown Redguard, she claimed that [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) and [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) are merely shadows of Tava, images seen dimly through the dust of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the snow of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), considering [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) and Cyrodilic understanding faulty and misguided. She also stated that [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) has no connection whatsoever to Tava. Similarly, she deemed Orgnum merely a mortal, asserting that he would not remain undying when faced with the true Satakal. She was also asked for information about isolated Redguard communities such as the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md), the [Silverhoof Horsemen](Lore_Silverhoof_Horsemen.md), and the [Exiled Sun-Eaters](Lore_Exiled_Sun-Eaters.md) of Numaneh. However, she refused to provide details about the last, as telling their tale is forbidden by tradition.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LA-2)</sup>

Many of her words were quoted when describing a variety of mounts of the Warrior Wave, primarily excerpts from *Plea for Open Eyes*,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Warrior Wave-13)</sup> though not exclusively.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WWSC-9)</sup>
