*No trust dark worm N'Gasta. Powerful, yes, is N'Gasta -- but wicked. Soulstealer. Old and evil.*
—Joto<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGJoto-1)</sup> N'Gasta the Sload<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sarnir-2)</sup> (or simply **N'gasta**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> was a legendary [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) and a famed servant of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) throughout the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sarnir-2)</sup> He lived in the old wizard's tower on his [eponymous Isle](Lore_Isle_of_N%27Gasta.md) west of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and terrorized the island with his Soul Snare until he was killed in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) by [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md), who wielded the [Flask of Lillandril](Lore_Flask_of_Lillandril.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rescue Iszara-4)</sup> Clavicus Vile viewed him as "a character of remarkable charm and complexity."<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CVR-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rescue Iszara-4)</sup> N'Gasta is remembered by [necromancers](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) as one of the greats.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

## History
*This one is comfortable with the trappings of his vocation, even if his victims lack such comfort.*
—N'Gasta (listen Link ⏵) Little is known about N'Gasta's life prior to his arrival on his Isle in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). However he is described as having had a wizard's tower in northernmost [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), seemingly located just to the east of the village of [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md) in the [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Artisa-7)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sarnir-2)</sup>

The High King of Hammerfell, [Thassad II](Lore_Thassad_II.md), allowed N'Gasta to occupy the tower west of Stros M'Kai in the better years of his reign. Presumably around the same time, N'Gasta's liege-lord, the [King of Worms](Lore_King_of_Worms.md), granted him the surrounding area to practice necromancy. Up until the end of the Redguard civil war in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), N'Gasta was "but a tale in passing, a shiver in riddle".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGNGasta-8)</sup>

At the end of the war, N'Gasta set his soul snare over Stros M'Kai to capture the souls of spirits either unprotected by holy rituals or buried in unhallowed ground.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-RGAvik-9)</sup> Many of these he offered to his patron [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) in return for favors. The new provisional governor [Richton](Lore_Amiel_Richton.md) did not restrict N'Gasta's practices, believing him to be a better friend than enemy, and this policy enabled N'Gasta to become very powerful.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RGNGasta-8)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RGNidal-10)</sup>

Three months before Cyrus arrived on Stros M'Kai, his sister [Iszara](Lore_Iszara.md) visited N'Gasta seeking help to restore [Prince A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md) from a soul gem. N'Gasta agreed to offer his services in exchange for Iszara's soul, an offer she accepted. Typical of his behavior, he lied and kept the soul gem for himself, fashioning it into an amulet that appeared to be a red diamond within a dragon setting. He intended to deliver this to Richton to acknowledge the governor's generosity and hired a middleman to find a courier.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-NGastas Amulet-11)</sup>

Either through this middleman or on his own, Cyrus arrived at N'Gasta's tower and accepted the job to deliver the amulet, unaware of its true nature. In time, he learned that Iszara had sought out N'Gasta's help. He discovered the location of the legendary [Flask of Lillandril](Lore_Flask_of_Lillandril.md) in order to defeat N'Gasta should he turn hostile. At this time, the island was covered with countless reanimated [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md) and [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md), the waters surrounding it and the Spine were filled with the tentacles of mysterious beasts with passage only offered by a skeletal [boatman](Lore_Boatman.md), and the tower was guarded by [Vermai](Lore_Vermai.md) whom N'Gasta had made into his servants using [pacifying helmets](Lore_Mind_Magic.md#Pacifying_Helmets). At the top of his tower, N'Gasta appeared and revealed his actions, including that Iszara's soul was sent to Clavicus Vile. Utilizing the flask, Cyrus managed to kill the necromancer and then opened a portal to Vile's [realm](Lore_Fields_of_Regret.md) and rescued his sister. As Cyrus stated during their confrontation, it is quite possible that N'Gasta was caught in his own soul snare.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rescue Iszara-4)</sup>

N'Gasta's legacy lived on even into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) through his book *[N'Gasta! Kvata! Kvakis!](Lore_N%27Gasta!_Kvata!_Kvakis!.md)* (many copies of which were found on Vvardenfell),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Artisa-7)</sup> most likely a work about necromancy.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The Dagon Fel-based necromancer Sorkvild the Raven took an interest in N'Gasta, speaking to others of topics regarding Clavicus Vile and N'Gasta<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sarnir-2)</sup> and hiring agents to ask questions about N'Gasta.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Artisa-7)</sup>
