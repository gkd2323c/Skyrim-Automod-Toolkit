#### Y'ffer, the Elden Shaper
**Y'ffer** is the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) analogue of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), and one of the Bastard Sons of [Ahnurr](Lore_Ahnurr.md). He was said to be "wise and kind", and created the first flower in order to convince [Nirni](Lore_Nirni.md) to be his mate.

Y'ffer was later corrupted by the Great Darkness and killed Nirni. In revenge, [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md), [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md), and [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) killed Y'ffer and made a [cairn for Nirni out of his bones](Lore_Earth_Bones.md). The Khajiit no longer speak of him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWS-1)</sup>

#### Y'ffre, God of the Forest
[Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) (also spelled **Y'frre**, or known as **Jephre** or **Y'ffer**), the **Singer**, the **Storyteller**, the **God of Song and Forest**, and the **Spirit of the Now**, is the most important deity of the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) pantheon, also worshipped by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), and [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md). He (occasionally she) was one of the strongest of the recognizable spirits that crystallized shortly after the beginning of time, and played an important role in the coalescing of the physical world during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).

He is said to have been the first of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) to transform himself into an Earth Bone, a law of nature, giving rise to the rules and principles of nature and life on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). Y'ffre's "Earthbones" are said to have been created from his corporeal form to establish safety and the laws of nature. These laws took the shape of stories, and there is great debate between Bosmer tribes competing to interpret Y'ffre's ways and knowledge of the times of chaos, before Y'ffre arrived on the [mortal plane](Lore_Mundus.md). He thus formed the frame upon which nature is woven, said to have gained sight-perception of all that occurs within the sight of birds and the reflection of waters through his song, and to echo their songs and crashing waves. While seeing and hearing nothing himself, his sight-perception and song are loomed through the laws he established, for mortals to interpret through study of the Sea's mystery. Day, night, and the places between are the manifestation of his interpretation of the time-law [Anui-El](Lore_Anui-El.md) is said to have established within Nirn. Y'ffre is strongly associated with songs and stories, as a mythical expression of his role in shaping the story of nature. His acts of creation are described as "tales", and he is said to have given the first name to every living creature, solidifying their place in the world. Y'ffre sang to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), weaving songs so beautiful that the [stars](Lore_Stars.md) were compelled to dance and sway, continuing to wink and blink afterward in memory of that song as the [realms](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of Aetherius which those stars are windows to likewise dance and sway to Y'ffre's music. Y'ffre's priests, called [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md), also experience life as one long story, as they not only keep the histories and laws of their people, but also narrate events that have yet to occur. As Y'ffre instructed the world and the first Bosmer through tales, so too do the Spinners elucidate and educate their kin through elaborate, sometimes befuddling, metaphors, that nonetheless always contain an essential truth. Spinners can use their "narrative magic" to profoundly alter the stories of others, even altering their memories of past events.

Bosmer priests known as Namespinners can perceive another person's [protonymic](Lore_Nymic.md), unweave it slightly and then chant a new suffix into it in order to alter the physical form of a person. This ability can be used to give antlers to Bosmer who are deemed worthy of this decoration.

#### Ysmir, Dragon of the North
[Ysmir](Lore_Ysmir.md), the "Dragon of the North", is the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) name of kings.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TTM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1C-3)</sup> As such, any given use of the term could be referring to one of several historical figures depending on context, or even all of them generally. Ysmir was described by [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholars as the Nordic aspect of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup>
