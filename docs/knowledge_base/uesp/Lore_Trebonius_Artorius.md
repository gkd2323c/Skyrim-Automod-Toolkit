Trebonius Artorius was the Arch-Mage of the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) branch of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). As Arch-Mage, he resided in the guild hall in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), which served as the Vvardenfell branch's headquarters. It was believed he was promoted to the position to remove him from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where he originally served the guild.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-STS-1)</sup>

Artorius was under constant scrutiny from other members of the guild because of his apparent lack of competency as a leader, showing little awareness of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) culture,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RA-2)</sup> being completely oblivious to a [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) spy in his midst,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ca S-3)</sup> and giving asinine, often impossible tasks to his subordinates. The duties that he asked of his colleagues included digging a hole to the mainland, despite it being separated from Vvardenfell by the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup> learning the language of the [Silt Striders](Lore_Silt_Strider.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SM-5)</sup> building a new [Anumidium](Lore_Numidium.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-JM-6)</sup> moving the Foreign Quarter canton of Vivec City by chaining [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) together and having them tow it,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CJ-7)</sup> and making an inventory of all the plates and bottles in the entirety of Vvardenfell.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FF-8)</sup>

Despite his shortcomings as a leader, Artorius was a powerful battlemage.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EE-9)</sup> He possessed his personal staff and the legendary [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md), both powerful artifacts.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TAI-10)</sup> Artorius was said to be arrogant, and in one display of his power, he cursed an [Ordinator](Lore_Ordinators.md) with an affliction over a drunken insult.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TB-11)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) ascended the ranks of the Vvardenfell Mages Guild and eventually found themselves before Artorius. Artorius provided a far-fetched task: learn the secret of the disappearance of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TA-12)</sup> The Nerevarine was determined to complete the task, speaking with various scholars on the subject before ultimately hearing the theories of the last living Dwarf, [Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-YB-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Mot D-14)</sup>

When the Nerevarine returned with the information, Artorius seemed overwhelmed that one of his tasks had been undertaken so thoroughly. He followed up with another tall task: kill every Telvanni councilor.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TA-12)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Kt TC-15)</sup> Artorius was deeply paranoid about the Telvanni and did not believe the Mages Guild and the Great House could coexist in peace.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TA-12)</sup> The Nerevarine was advised not to attempt this task, since even if they succeeded in killing the councilors, House Telvanni's reaction would be completely unpredictable.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Dialogue HT-16)</sup> The Nerevarine ultimately decided to not kill all the councilors.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Neloth-17)</sup>

The Nerevarine eventually positioned themselves to replace Artorius as Arch-Mage, and High Chancellor [Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) sent a letter ordering him to retire and cede leadership to the Nerevarine.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LTO-18)</sup> Reports conflict on whether the Nerevarine delivered Ocato's letter to Artorius or challenged him to open combat in the Vivec City Arena and killed him.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MWArch-Mage-19)</sup>
