# Grove Carpet

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fragment-Grove_Carpet_Buds.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fragment-Grove_Carpet_Buds.png) Grove Carpet Buds [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/ON-skin-Barkroot_Blessing_%28male%29.jpg/200px-ON-skin-Barkroot_Blessing_%28male%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skin-Barkroot_Blessing_(male).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skin-Barkroot_Blessing_(male).jpg) Barkroot Blessing Grove Carpet is a type of moss indigenous to [Galen](Lore_Galen.md). It is known to possess remarkable properties. [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) utilized living budlings of this moss in their rituals and transformation-based magic. Surprisingly, Grove Carpet is known for its near-indestructibility in bud form. Even ancient budlings, when sprinkled with water, can swiftly grow into lush plants within a matter of days.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BB-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GA-2)</sup> It's uncertain whether different druidic circles utilized those buds differently. However, the Druids of the [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md) were known for their specific utilization of Grove Carpet, though the exact purposes for which they employed it remain a mystery.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DR-3)</sup>

By employing magic alongside a variety of other ingredients and tool, including [scarab](Lore_Scarab.md) -host boxes, pulsating [amber](Lore_Amber.md) hearts, tablets carved from [petrified tree bark](Lore_Other_Minerals.md#Petrified_Wood), clay, [chimera](Lore_Chimera.md) hair brushes, writhing vine mass, mulch, living bark, and pigment sourced from [phoenix](Lore_Phoenix.md) [moths](Lore_Moth.md#Phoenix_Moth), grove carpet buds were used to alter ones their appearance as a tribute to changing seasons and to mirror the likeness of trees. This visage was known as Barkroot Blessing.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BB-1)</sup>
