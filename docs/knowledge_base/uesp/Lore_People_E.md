#### King Eadwyre ([3E 340](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_340) - 418)
[Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) was the King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) in the [late Fourth](Lore_Third_Era.md#Fourth_Century) and [early Fifth Centuries](Lore_Third_Era.md#Fifth_Century) of the Third Era. He was the husband of Queen [Carolyna](Lore_Carolyna.md), with whom he had a daughter, [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md). Later in life, Eadwyre wed the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), and became stepfather to her children, [Helseth](Lore_Helseth.md) and [Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md).

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), Eadwyre plotted with Barenziah to bring about the downfall of [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), the Imperial Battlemage who they learned was masquerading as the [Emperor](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md). In the years prior to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Eadwyre was known to be one of several factions which fought for possession of the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md).

Eadwyre died in [3E 418](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_418) at the age of 78, and was succeeded by Queen Elysana.

#### King Eamond (2E? b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Eamond](Lore_Eamond.md) was the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) King of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) in the late sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and the second monarch from [House Guimard](Lore_Guimard.md). After his father, King [Blaise](Lore_Blaise_Guimard.md), allied with King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) during [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), Eamond was wed to one of Emeric's cousins, [Arzhela](Lore_Arzhela.md), as a reward. In [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568), Eamond ascended to the throne after his father's death. During his reign, he and Arzhela had two children, Prince Adrien and Princess [Elara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elara). He had a brother named Renchant.

#### Kinlady Earlenque Corelanya the Great (?b - [1E 1017](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1017))
**Kinlady** [Earlenque Corelanya](Lore_Earlenque_Corelanya.md) **the Great** was the founder of the [Corelanya](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) realm on [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) and served as the matriarch of the [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) during their exodus to the island. #### Prince Ebel Septim (ca. [3E 380](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_380) - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Ebel](Lore_Ebel_Septim.md) of the [Septim House](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) was the third born son of [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) and [Empress Caula Voria](Lore_Caula_Voria.md), and therefore a [Dragonborn Prince](Lore_Dragonborn.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). Ebel was described as "affable and reasonably handsome for a man of middle age," but not nearly as handsome as his brother [Enman](Lore_Enman_Septim.md). Some close to the family described Ebel and his brothers as "preening fools", and Uriel VII's biographer described the children as being "victims of their parents' unhappy marriage." Ebel and his brother Enman never married, leaving them open for political alliance should the need arise.

#### Edhelorn (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Edhelorn](Lore_Edhelorn.md) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who served as [the Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). As the Silvenar he was "The Voice of the People", the embodiment of the will of the Bosmer. He also represented the spiritual side of the Bosmer, while his counterpart and wife [Finoriell](Lore_Finoriell.md) represented the physicality of the Bosmer as the [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md). Both loved each other dearly.

He was instrumental in the formation of the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), helping bring together the Bosmer of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). Edhelorn acted as a diplomatic envoy from the inception of the Dominion and gained a reputation as an unrivalled diplomat.

#### King Edward (fl. early 1E)
[Edward](Lore_Edward.md) was the legendary King of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) during the early years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), amidst the waning years of [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) control over [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). King Edward's legacy has been told through stories that have been passed down as far as the late years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), spanning across all of Tamriel. It is shared with his trusted allies, the Witch-King of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md), [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md) and his [companions](Lore_Moraelyn%27s_Companions.md). His legend has been documented in the historical fiction, [King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md), which circulated in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). He was a skilled warrior in magic, wielding a mithril staff and the dagger, [Dragon's Tooth](Lore_Dragon%27s_Tooth.md).

#### Jarl Eerika Skjoralmor (fl. 2E 561 - 582)

#### Elante of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) (fl. 3E 427 - 433)
[Elante of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elante_of_Alinor) was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) archaeologist from [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). She was a scholar of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) shrines, and authored a set of [research notes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elante%27s_Notes).

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Elante and her colleague Badama Andarys uncovered a Daedric ruin in a cave found along [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md)'s northern coastline in the [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) region. This turned out to be the fabled Tomb of [Mordrin Hanin](Lore_Mordrin_Hanin.md). The expedition ended in disaster when their summonings failed to conjure anything more than [scamps](Lore_Scamp.md) to aid their excavation and the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) looted the cave and tomb of treasure, killing Badama in the process.

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Elante's research pointed her towards a cave along [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md), which likely contained a Daedric shrine. She hired a new recruit from the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) to help her navigate the cave and slay the many hostile Daedra who inhabited it. Elante discovered a lost shrine to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) deep inside the cave, which helped to further the understanding of Daedra.

#### Princess Elara (fl. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Elara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elara) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) princess of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) in the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was the daughter of [King Eamond](Lore_Eamond.md) and [Queen Arzhela](Lore_Arzhela.md) and had a brother named Adrien. Through her father, Elara was a member of [House Guimard](Lore_Guimard.md), and she was related to High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) through her mother.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Elara was ambushed, gravely injured, and presumed dead. She was found in a coma on the side of a road by Stenwick Gurles, the [Viridian Sentinel](Lore_Viridian_Sentinel.md), who rescued her and took her back to the Viridian Woods to attempt to heal her. Gurles rescued Elara because he planned for her to succeed him as the Viridian Sentinel. With the help of an adventurer, Gurles was able to awaken Elara by channeling his energy into a salve to heal her. Gurles used all his energy to heal Elara, and he died soon afterward. Before his death, Gurles sent the adventurer to retrieve a bud from one of the Voice of the Forest's branches so Elara could undergo the ritual to become the Viridian Sentinel, which was the only way for Elara to survive for more than a fortnight. It is unknown if Elara survived the ritual to become the Viridian Sentinel.

#### Duchess Elea Dufort (fl. 2E 582)
[Elea Dufort](Lore_Elea_Dufort.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) Duchess of the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) and the leader of [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was the daughter of Donoven Dufort, who was the first Duke of the Systres from House Dufort. She was the sister of [Count Damard Dufort](Lore_Damard_Dufort.md), who acted as her court mage. Her residence was Castle Navire.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when the [Ascendant Order](Lore_Ascendant_Order.md) emerged, Elea investigated and worked to combat it. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) traveled to Castle Navire to investigate the Order and questioned Elea. Elea expressed suspicion that [House Mornard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mornard) was involved with the Ascendant Order because of the animosity between the Dufort and Mornard families. While inspecting her new flagship, the Silver Swan, Elea's steward, Hercian Stalo, locked her in her cabin aboard the ship on the orders of Damard, who was the Ascendant Magus, the second-in-command of the Ascendant Order. Afterward, the Vestige rescued her. The Vestige later killed Damard, and Elea expressed regret over his death, but she realized there was no other way.

After Lady [Arabelle Davaux](Lore_Arabelle_Davaux.md) was poisoned by gifted wine, the Vestige learned that the order of wine was placed by Elea's seneschal; however, it was actually from the Ascendant Order. Elea also received poisoned wine and a letter from the Ascendant Order, but Knight Commander Jourvel intervened, insisting that the gift be tested before consumption. The Vestige later investigated the wine and letter and questioned Elea about them. After Lord [Bacaro Volorus](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md) was revealed to be the Ascendant Lord and killed, Elea was present for the peace talks between the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) on All Flags Islet.

#### Knight Eleidon (fl. before 1E 5th century)
[Eleidon](Lore_Eleidon.md) was a renowned holy [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [knight](Lore_Breton_Knightly_Orders.md) of legend. He was recorded in Breton history as a highly esteemed individual, admired for his bravery and unwavering commitment to set all wrongs right and fight against any injustices. Many stories exist about Eleidon's gallant and brave deeds, including a particularly well-known tale in which he saved a baron's daughter from certain death at the hands of a warlord. In recognition of his noble acts, the baron expressed his gratitude by commissioning the creation of an enchanted shield, known as [Eleidon's Ward](Lore_Eleidon%27s_Ward.md). This artifact was imbued with magical properties, allowing the wielder to heal wounds.

#### Elenwen (fl. 4E 201)
[Elenwen](Lore_Elenwen.md) was the First Emissary of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

During the Great War she served as an interrogator, where she oversaw the interrogation of Ulfric Stormcloak following his capture. He was made to falsely believe that the information he gave up under torture aided the capture of the Imperial City, before being allowed to escape.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), during the time of the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md), she oversaw the Thalmor Justiciars and the outlaw of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship within Skyrim in accordance to the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md). She resided in the [Thalmor Embassy](Lore_Thalmor_Embassy.md), located in [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md) hold. She would often host lavish parties at the embassy, inviting Skyrim's most influential and powerful people. During one such party she unknowingly met the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md). After the Dragonborn infiltrated and escaped from the embassy, Elenwen was reportedly furious. Reports conflict on whether she attended a temporary truce between the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) hosted by the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) and whether she was asked to leave on [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md)'s request.

#### Jarl Elisif the Fair (fl. 4E 201)
[Elisif the Fair](Lore_Elisif_the_Fair.md) was the beloved wife of [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md), High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), before his death at the beginning of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md). Following Torygg's death, she took his place as Jarl of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), though the decision on who would assume the role of High King (or Queen) was delayed due to the outbreak of the rebellion. Elisif believed that the throne of Skyrim was her right, as High King Torygg's widow. However, she believed it best not to formally announce her intentions until the rebellion was quashed. She was a popular leader among her people, although some of her courtiers had concerns about her young age and inexperience, and believed she was under the thumb of [General Tullius](Lore_General_Tullius.md).

#### Elysana ([3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) - ?d)
[Queen Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md) was the daughter of [Queen Carolyna](Lore_Carolyna.md) and [King Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md). Carolyna died in [3E 392](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_392), and a few years later, Eadwyre remarried. [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) became Elysana's stepmother, and the princess gained elder step-siblings in [Prince Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) and [Princess Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md).

In the years prior to the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Miracle_of_Peace.md), the princess became locked in a power struggle with her stepbrother for the throne. The law of succession favored Elysana, but Helseth was now the eldest, and he (and Barenziah) held much influence. While Elysana cultivated an air of innocent vapidity, in reality she had become as ruthless as the prince; among her allies was the influential noble [Lord Woodborne](Lore_Woodborne.md), to whom she was betrothed—and who held the key to an [artifact of immense power](Lore_Numidium.md).

This period saw the arrival of an [agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), sent to investigate the mysterious circumstances surrounding the death of [King Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md). The Agent was drawn into the siblings' machinations; Helseth hatched a scheme to blackmail [Lord Castellian](Lore_Castellian.md), head of the Elder Council, into changing the law of succession in his favor. Elysana retaliated by manipulating the same Agent into unwittingly helping her assassinate Lord Castellian. The princess later attempted to have the Agent assassinated as well, but this plan failed.

Elysana's plans for the throne were dealt another blow when Lord Woodborne was exposed by the Emperor's Agent as Lysandus' murderer, and killed. However, when King Eadwyre finally died in [3E 418](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_418) after the Warp in the West, Elysana emerged victorious. After ascending to the throne, the new queen banished Helseth and Barenziah from the court of Wayrest.

By the close of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Queen Elysana had borne two children by her new royal consort, a son named Eadwyre "Wyre" II after his grandfather, and a daughter named Tymona. The queen also forged an alliance with Wayrest's old enemies, the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), and was considered by many contemporaries to be the most feared ruler in western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) she attended the Engagement Ball of [Geldall Septim](Lore_Geldall_Septim.md), but he and his brothers were assassinated by a coordinated attack by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) during the event, and she may have been among the assassinated heirs.

#### High King Emeric ([2E 521](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_521) - ?d)
[Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) (also known as **Emeric of Cumberland** or **Emeric Cumberland**) was a member of the Cumberland Dynasty in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), and one of the major faction leaders of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). Emeric was a merchant lord who advocated for free trade throughout Tamriel, and he competed against [Queen Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) and [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) for the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md) so he could restore "the true flame of the Empire of Man".

#### Baron Esmark Tamrith (2E? b - 2E 582)
[Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md) was the Baron of [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Ranser called Baron Tamrith and House Tamrith, along with the other noble houses of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), to arms, who complied and provided their troops despite their strong ties with Wayrest. Shortly after the outbreak of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), House Tamrith withdrew its support of him and petitioned for peace, and [House Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) quickly followed suit. In the immediate aftermath of Ranser's death, Rivenspire fell into chaos. Because Emeric did not trust any of Rivenspire's nobles to be King of Shornhelm, he approved of the formation of the triumvirate Council of the North to govern the region, but he also promised to eventually select a new king. He reportedly disliked the House Montclair representative on the Council of the North, Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md).

Esmark married Janece of House Elde, combining their fortunes and creating an even stronger political entity. With Janece, Esmark had two daughters, [Eselde](Lore_Eselde_Tamrith.md), who took after her mother, and Janeve, who took after her father. In [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), Eselde left Rivenspire to further her education and religious studies in [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md), while Janeve, against Esmark's wishes, joined the Shornhelm Guard. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Tamrith died of natural causes, and his funeral was held in Shornhelm. After his death, Eselde succeeded him as the leader of House Tamrith. He was remembered as a sensible, strong, and pragmatic leader.

#### Esthlainder (fl. early 2E)
[Estlainder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Esthlainder) was the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) **Elder** of the Council of Wizards of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md). Esthlainder was greatly respected by his fellow mages; he was an imposing figure decked out in indigo blue robes with gold trim, a high pointed wizard hat, carrying a beautiful intricately carved staff. In the early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) prodigy by the name of [Gyron Vardengroet](Lore_Gyron_Vardengroet.md) arrived to the Crystal Tower in the hopes of learning more about magic.

Esthlainder approached Gyron and informed him that his arrival was foretold by the [gods](Lore_Gods.md). Esthlainder and the other masters taught Gyron all they could but the day came where Esthlainder informed Gyron that the Crystal Tower could teach him no more and it was time for him to move on and seek further wisdom. Esthlainder gave Gyron a package to open when he was clear of the Tower. Inside the package for Gyron were robes, a pointed wizard hat, and an intricately carved staff identical to that of Esthlainder's. Esthlainder left a letter explaining that the garments were indestructible and the staff had great magical properties, he also informed Gyron he would be known from then on as the Sage.

#### High Kinlady Estre (fl. 2E 582)
[Estre](Lore_Estre.md) of [Errinorne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Errinorne) was the High Kinlady and Canonreeve of [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md) and the leader of the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md) as the Veiled Queen. She was the wife of Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md) and the sister-in-law of Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md). As the leader of the Veiled Heritance, Estre sought to supplant Ayrenn, destroy the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), and expel all non-Altmer from the Summerset Isles. To achieve this goal, Estre and her faction employed a variety of terroristic methods, received assistance from [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and formed a pact with Sealord [Malleroth](Lore_Malleroth.md) of the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) High Command. During Ayrenn's sojourn in [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Veiled Heritance attempted to assassinate her, but they were ultimately defeated, and Estre was killed.

#### Regent Estunirion Corelanya (? - [2E 439](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_439))
**Regent** [Estunirion Corelanya](Lore_Estunirion_Corelanya.md) was the ruler of [Sunport](Lore_Sunport.md) and the head of the [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

#### Etra of the Many Spears (fl. before 2E 582)
[Etra of the Many Spears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Etra_of_the_Many_Spears) was a [Naga](Lore_Naga.md) hero of the [Dead-Water](Lore_Naga-Kur.md) tribe. According to tribal elders, his soul was older than the tribe itself and before he had even hatched his egg-minders could hear him crying out war-chants.

In life, he was a fearsome warrior who was said to have felled great trees and slain huge beasts. After defeating other warriors, he would often eat their hearts or claim their grave-stakes as a trophy, which he would store in a large stone vault known as the Tomb of Many Spears. When a great [Voriplasm](Lore_Voriplasm.md) claimed his vault, a furious Etra fought a great battle with it. Etra was ultimately killed and the vault became his tomb. Over the years, many Dead-Water kaals attempted to raid the tomb and claim a piece of Etra's remains to forge a mighty weapon, but they all ended in failure.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) ventured into Etra's tomb and defeated the remains of the Naga warrior in order to recover his grave-stake and learn more about the [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md).

#### Grandmaster Ettiene Volusus (fl. 1E 2879 - 2902)
[Ettiene Volusus](Lore_Ettiene_Volusus.md) was one of the leaders of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), who was known for being the one who chronicled the history of the Order. She was remembered as one of the four greatest Grandmasters the Dragonguard have ever known, and as the last one honored in the [Wind Scour Temple](Lore_Wind_Scour_Temple.md). She served as the Grandmaster from [1E 2879](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2879) to [1E 2902](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2902). She was known to be modest, honest, and astute.

After her death, she was buried in Wind Scour Temple in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and honored with a [statue](Lore_Statues.md), prayer, faith and brazier's blaze.

#### Queen Euraxia Tharn (2E ?b - 582)
[Queen Euraxia Tharn](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md) was the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) queen of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Euraxia hailed from the royal [House Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md). Euraxia had become the strict ruler of Rimmen following an event known as the [Frostfall Coup](Lore_Frostfall_Coup.md) in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), where her militia, known as the [Euraxians](Lore_Euraxians.md) conquered Rimmen, and she proclaimed herself as Queen. She was the mother of [Javad Tharn](Lore_Javad_Tharn.md), who led an invasion against the region of [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md), but was defeated by forces allied to the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). She was also the half-sister of [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), chancellor of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), and an ancestor of unknown relationship to [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md).

#### Eustacia (fl. c. 3E 427)
**Eustacia** was a healer and [seer](Lore_Sight.md) in [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md). The governor at the time, [Cyril Vinticae](Lore_Cyril_Vinticae.md), had banished his four champions, as one among them was secretly a traitor who had set the [Ice Tribes](Lore_Ice_Tribes.md) upon their raid of the town. He sent for the [Hero of Dawnstar](Lore_Hero_of_Dawnstar.md) to interrogate the champions, ordering Eustacia to provide healing for the soon-to-be Hero. Her oracular visions pertaining to the raid also proved vital to the Hero, providing crucial information that allowed the Hero to cut through the lies and reveal the traitor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DSevents-21)</sup>

#### Evandene Tusk Collector (fl. 1E 10th century)
[Evandene Tusk Collector](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Evandene_Tusk_Collector) was a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) leader of the [Winterborn](Lore_Winterborn.md) clan during the tenth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). She was the mother of [Mercedene](Lore_Mercedene.md), the Beast of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), and a distant ancestor of several members of the Winterborn clan as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

#### Eveli Sharp-Arrow (fl. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md) was a Bosmer adventurer of some renown active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) who came from an extensive bloodline of hunter, scouts, and warriors. Her familial traditions predisposed her to becoming a great archer, as well as to how confidently she carried herself. Like all Bosmer of renown, she earned a byname which hinted at her great skill. At a young age, her bow was like an extension of her own body, and she was capable of quickly unloading her quiver and accurately hitting her intended targets. She was also very nimble, with one of her feats being distracting [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) while subsequently dodging his massive axe and his fists. In her spare time, she enjoyed reading literature regardless of genre, though she had a soft spot for stories about adventures and mysteries. Her favorite literary figure was [Investigator Vale](Lore_Investigator_Vale.md). She had twelve siblings, a notable one was Beragon Sharp-Dagger, who was an adventurer and later a scribe in the court of Emperor [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md).

#### The Exalted Viper
[The Exalted Viper](Lore_The_Exalted_Viper.md) was a short-lived [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) created in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). She was intended to replace [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md), and thus shared identical aspects.

As a mortal, the Exalted Viper was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) mage named **Cassipia**. When the [constellations](Lore_Constellations.md) fell from the sky above [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) circa [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), the Serpent took form as a Celestial and began a cult called the [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md). Attracted by his rhetorical goals of shedding corruption from the world, Cassipia left the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and was appointed Regent of Serpentine Stratagems within the Court. However, she soon discovered that the Serpent's plans involved destroying civilization to restore [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) to a primal state. Along with her [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) companion Little Leaf, Cassipia secretly defected from the Scaled Court and began plotting to usurp the Serpent's place in the sky by becoming a Celestial of equal power.

Aided by Little Leaf and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), Regent Cassipia used her authority to steal and disrupt the Court's research on [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md). She learned how the ancient [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) first created Celestials and assisted in sabotaging the creation of [mantikoras](Lore_Mantikora.md), as well as the [Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md) imbuing iron and flesh with nirncrux. Her efforts crippled the Serpent's attempt at raising an army in Upper Craglorn in the process, although her treachery went undiscovered and she retreated to Skyreach Temple with her followers to begin the transformation.

Kelmen Locke, an old acquaintance of Cassipia's, attempted to dissuade her from performing the ritual, but instead was killed. His death brought [the Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md)'s attention to her plans, and she journeyed to Skyreach Temple with the Vestige to prevent the creation of the Exalted Viper. Although the ritual had been interrupted, the death of Little Leaf triggered Cassipia's transformation, and the Exalted Viper was born in battle. However, she was promptly slain in her mortal form.

#### Minister of Punition Excoraeus Tharn (fl. late 1E)
[Excoraeus Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Excoraeus_Tharn) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) member of the royal [House Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md), ancestor and descendant of numerous famous figures.. He served under [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Kastav](Lore_Kastav.md) as the **Minister of Punition**. The exact years of service are unknown, but he had to serve at some point between the coronation of Kastav in [1E 2762](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2762) and [1E 2804](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2804) when his reign ended.
