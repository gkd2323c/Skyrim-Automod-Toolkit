[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/SR-place-Morvunskar.jpg/200px-SR-place-Morvunskar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Morvunskar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Morvunskar.jpg) Morvunskar circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ad/ON-place-Fort_Morvunskar.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fort_Morvunskar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Morvunskar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Morvunskar.jpg) Fort Morvunskar circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Morvunskar (or **Fort Morvunskar**) is a military settlement located in the mountains southwest of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

The fort is located on strategic high ground overlooking the main roads to Windhelm. However, it serves no true military purpose; instead, it was built to guard the crypts located there. Many great [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warriors were buried at Morvunskar, and in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) it was used by Skyrim's nobility. Notably, [Queen Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md) was buried there with the [Crown of Freydis](Lore_Crown_of_Freydis.md) after her death in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the forces of [Fildgor Orc-Thane](Lore_Fildgor.md) stormed the fort in order to steal Nurnhilde's corpse and the Crown. Morvunskar was quickly taken back by [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md)'s forces, but Fildgor escaped with his gruesome prize. At the time, plans to build an even larger fort were in place.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Blindsided-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SFTD-3)</sup>

Morvunskar eventually fell into disuse and was abandoned. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md) and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) planned to hold a wedding at the ruins after a night spent drunkenly exploring Skyrim. The ruins were under the control of a group of hostile warlocks at the time, led by an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) named Naris the Wicked. When the Dragonborn journeyed to the ruins the next day, Sanguine opened a portal to the [Misty Grove](Lore_Misty_Grove.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>
