The Akaviri Potentate<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> (also called the **Akaviri Empire**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOA-2)</sup> or the **Akaviri Dynasty**),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOBOD-3)</sup> additionally known as the **Colovian Emperor-Potentate** or the **Colovian Dynasty**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-JLDND-5)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> was the period of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) after the assassination of [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III) and the end of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). Prior to this period, potentate was the title of the chief advisor of the Reman Emperors.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-6)</sup>

## History

### First Era
At the battle of [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), [Reman I](Lore_Reman.md) decisively defeated an [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) invasion but other events forced him to offer the defeated army amnesty in return for their service in his own army. This led to Akaviri influence in other spheres of life, and over time the position of Potentate was usually filled by one of the snake-people.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> Under the Reman Dynasty, the emperor's chief advisor was the leader of the Akaviri faction on the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), who bore the title of potentate.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-6)</sup> In addition to being the emperor's chief advisor, Potentate [Sidri-Ashak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sidri-Ashak) was delegated nearly all issues of policy and politics by Emperor [Brazollus Dor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brazollus_Dor) due to his lack of interest in ruling, effectively making the Potentate the Empire's de facto ruler.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TEot V-7)</sup>

After the assassination of Emperor [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III) in the year [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie), who is believed to have ordered the assassination, declared Reman's line to be extinct. On 22 Evening Star, 1E 2920, Versidue-Shaie declared in a speech that he was taking over the leadership of the empire but retaining his title of Potentate. In the same speech, he declared the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the start of the [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-2920ES-8)</sup>

### Second Era
After becoming the ruler of the Empire, Versidue-Shaie began persecuting those who opposed his rule and remained loyal to the Reman Dynasty, a period known as the [Reman Purges](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_Purges).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Systres-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONBlackmarrow-10)</sup> By [2E 283](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_283), Versidue-Shaie was faced with a disintegrating empire. The vassal kingdoms throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) had reached a new height of rebellion, openly challenging Imperial rule by refusing to pay taxes and leading sorties against Imperial garrisons. To suppress the rebellious armies, Versidue-Shaie declared martial law in 2E 283, initiating the War of Subjugation, which finally ended in Imperial victory in [2E 320](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_320).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Hot FG-11)</sup> The immense cost of the War of Subjugation resulted in the impoverishment of both the Empire and its constituent kingdoms. To maintain order without incurring the expense of a standing army, Versidue-Shaie agreed in [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321) to a proposal from his kinsman Dinieras-Ves, which established what would later be known as the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FGC-12)</sup>

In [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324), Versidue-Shaie was assassinated by the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) in the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Fa D-13)</sup> and succeeded by his son, [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup> The Morag Tong were outlawed across the continent and reformed secretly as the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TDC-14)</sup><sup>:6</sup> In [2E 400](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_400), Savirien-Chorak held a celebration to commemorate four centuries of potentate rule.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PCo V-15)</sup> Savirien-Chorak and all his heirs were assassinated by the Dark Brotherhood on a night in the month of Sun's Dawn [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430) —within a fortnight the Akaviri Potentate crumbled.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-KRP-16)</sup> By [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), civil wars and insurrection had dissolved the Empire, ushering in the [Imperial Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TDC-14)</sup><sup>:6</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^1](#note_nb1) | In the original lore of *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* the "Akaviri Potentates" were not defined as [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md). Instead the potentates were called the "Colovian Dynasty" and Versidue-Shaie is called the "Colovian Emperor-Potentate" of the "[Tamrielic Empire](Lore_Empire.md),"<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TDC-14)</sup><sup>:6</sup> with the cultural background of the potentate being entirely undefined, though Versidue-Shaie is mentioned as having had a palace in the [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup> *[The Brothers of Darkness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Brothers_of_Darkness)* book was the first and only source across all of *Daggerfall*'s materials to refer to any "Colovian" peoples.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo D-4)</sup> When authoring the first *[Pocket Guide to the Empire](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Cyrodiil.md)* for *[Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)*, writer [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) recalled nabbing the name "Colovian" from *Daggerfall*'s documents and using it for the name of the Imperial Province's westernmost region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-MKP-19)</sup> Additionally, the *Pocket Guide* added the Akaviri contextualization to the potentates.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> *The Brothers of Darkness* book has appeared in every main *Elder Scrolls* release after *Daggerfall*, wherein the potentates' description retained the "Colovian" contextualization mentioned previously, but the [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online) version of the book altered the name from "Colovian Dynasty" to "Akaviri Dynasty."<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOBOD-3)</sup> Ultimately, the potentates' relationship to [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and/or the [Colovian](Lore_Imperial.md#Colovians) people as established in *Daggerfall* has yet to be explored in the lore. |

- For the development of *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* there were [D&D campaigns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Pen_%26_Paper_Worldbuilding) used to formulate background lore. In one of the pages from the campaign archives there is a line which reads: "*430 Fall Colovian*"—this refers to the exact year the Colovians fell on the timeline, and affirms the intent for there to have been a "Colovian" dynasty in power historically.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-JLDND-5)</sup>
- | [^2](#note_SCd) | Most sources such as *[The Brothers of Darkness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Brothers_of_Darkness)*, *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition)*, and *[Systres History: Volume 7](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_History__Volume_7)* state that Savirien-Chorak was assassinated in 2E 430, but other sources such as the *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition)* and the *[Crafting Motifs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crafting_Motifs)* series state that Savirien-Chorak was assassinated in 2E 431. Since more sources state that Savirien-Chorak was assassinated in 2E 430, this year is presumably the correct year of the assassination. |

- [Sidri-Ashak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sidri-Ashak) was an obscure and lesser known potentate. He is perhaps best known for [five runestones](Lore_Standing_Stones.md#Runestones) dotted across the Imperial province that bear his name. He advised Emperor [Brazollus Dor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brazollus_Dor), who ruled between the reigns of [Reman II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II) and [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III). Neither interested nor skilled in matters of state, Brazollus Dor left most of the governing to him, and he proved to be a capable statesman.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-6)</sup>
- The Akaviri Potentate's peculiar armorsmithing practices were said to have changed the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) forever.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONSS-20)</sup>
- The Magnates of the town of [Hakoshae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hakoshae) in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) believed themselves to be descendants of the Potentates through Proconsul [Beloren-Kaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beloren-Kaie),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Magnate-21)</sup> but it was revealed in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) that they were actually descended from [Lein-Barduik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lein-Barduik), the bodyguard of the Proconsul, who abandoned him to assassins and assumed his identity.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-22)</sup>
- The next Potentate to rule the Empire was [Ocato of Firsthold](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md), who assumed control of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) at the beginning of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) after the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) failed to select a new emperor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-23)</sup>
