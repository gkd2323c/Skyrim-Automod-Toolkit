[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly.jpg/200px-SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly.jpg) Japhet's Folly **Japhet's Folly** is a self-contained [worldspace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Worldspaces) far northeast of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) containing [Blood Horkers](Skyrim_Blood_Horker.md) and their leader [Haldyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Haldyn). It contains only one zone, *[Japhet's Folly Towers](#Japhet.27s_Folly_Towers)*.

It is on the remote Rockjoint Island in the [Sea of Ghosts](Skyrim_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), and may only be visited during the quest [Rise in the East](Skyrim_Rise_in_the_East.md) (see [bugs](#Bugs)).

## Related Quests
- **[Rise in the East](Skyrim_Rise_in_the_East.md)**: Help the [East Empire Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:East_Empire_Company) take care of some [pirates](Skyrim_Blood_Horker.md).

## Walkthrough

### Japhet's Folly Towers
Entry is made via the [Sea Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sea_Cave) under the fort, and can only happen during the related quest (see [bugs](#Bugs) for details). The door into the fort leads to the bottom of a curving staircase typical in Nordic forts. On your right is a storage area containing many crates, barrels, and sacks of [foodstuffs](Skyrim_Food.md), as well as two sets of shelves holding mainly clutter, four loose [coins](Skyrim_Gold.md), and a random [potion of healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing), [magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka), or [stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina). There is a [woodcutter's axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe) on a stack of crates, and by the bottom of the stairs is a long wooden table with seven loose coins and a bottle of [Honningbrew Mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Honningbrew_Mead) on top. You can hear some of the Blood Horkers talking above, so caution is advised.

At the top of the first flight of stairs is a [pressure plate](Skyrim_Pressure_Plate.md) that triggers a [spike wall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spike_Wall) on the left. There are two barrels on the right with a bottle of [Nord mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead) on top. There is a second flight of stairs rising behind. A Blood Horker is patrolling up and down this second flight of stairs, and another is resting in the room at the top. At the top of the stairs is a table with two loose [iron arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Arrow), a [hunting bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow), five loose coins, and a random quiver of [arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Arrows) on top. To the right is a circular room containing three unowned bedrolls, a tall wardrobe containing [clothes](Skyrim_Clothes.md), and an open, empty chest. There are also several sacks around the room. To the southeast is a wooden door, and to the southwest is an alcove, where more stairs would have originally been; however, these have caved in. There is a table and chair near the cave-in, with a bottle of Nord mead and a loaf of [bread](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bread) on top. Beside the table is a stack of crates with a [goat cheese wheel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Goat_Cheese_Wheel) on top, several sacks, and another open, empty chest.

Behind the door to the southeast is a wide passage with a set of shelves on the left and several stacks of crates along it. The shelves hold three bottles of Nord mead, two bottles of [wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wine), and another cheese wheel. A bit farther along is a small shelving unit on the right with an unlocked chest on top. Next on the left is a key-locked wooden door leading to the [exterior](#Exterior). To the left of the door is a stack of crates. This door will serve as your exit later.

Past the door is a table on the right and a couple more stacks of crates near the end. The passage opens into a large circular room with a single Blood Horker patrolling from this room and down some stairs to the southwest. There are also more stairs to the northeast climbing higher. The room itself contains a table against the wall to the north with a pair of [iron gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Gauntlets) on top. A set of shelves to the east has an [iron helmet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Helmet) on top. In the western corner, behind some rubble near the top of the stairs the Blood Horker is patrolling, an [apothecary's satchel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Apothecary%27s_Satchel) and a random [dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger) are on the ground. The stairs descend, passing some barrels and sacks on the first landing, then a wardrobe with a [potion of health](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Health) on top on the second landing. At the bottom is a long table with seven loose coins and a small [coin purse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse) on top. Beyond this is a small table and chairs where two Blood Horkers sit. There are another nineteen loose coins on this table, suggesting the bandits were gambling. Beyond this is another circular room on the left, and past this another table with three samples of [slaughterfish scales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Scales), two [briar hearts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Briar_Heart), a [gourd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gourd), and a [vigor poison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vigor_Poison) on top. Against the rear wall are more crates with another gourd on top. There is also an expert-locked wooden door to the northeast.

The circular room contains a table that has been used for butchering meat, with two fillets of [salmon meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Meat), two [raw rabbit legs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Raw_Rabbit_Leg), a [pheasant breast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pheasant_Breast), a haunch of raw [venison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Venison), and an [iron war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_War_Axe) on top. An apprentice-locked chest is nearby, as are two sets of shelves holding four loaves of bread, four [cabbages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Cabbage), a [seared slaughterfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Seared_Slaughterfish) steak, a sample of slaughterfish scales, a haunch of raw venison, several pieces of cheese, two fillets of [horker meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Horker_Meat), a [leg of goat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leg_of_Goat), and a random [potion](Skyrim_Potions.md). Between the two shelving units is a barrel with another fillet of horker meat on top. There are also several racks hanging from the ceiling holding a total of eight [rabbits](Skyrim_Rabbit_(dead).md) and six [pheasants](Skyrim_Pheasant.md). Finally, there is a barrel by a central pillar containing [ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md).

Behind the expert-locked door is a small room filled with cobwebs, with a small table with the body of [Japhet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet), who carries a few coins, in front. On the table is his [journal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet%27s_Journal) and a random [sword](Skyrim_Sword.md), and in the corner is a master-locked chest.

Climbing back up to the room above and taking the exit to the northeast, there are spear holes in the ground in the doorway, and a table by the bottom of the stairs leading up, with two random potions of magicka, an empty [petty soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Petty_Soul_Gem), and an [iron dagger](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md) on top. As you climb the stairs, you will hear the spears blocking the route back. At the top of the first flight is a small table with a sample of [small antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Small_Antlers) and an empty petty soul gem on top. Haldyn will be alerted to your presence; he uses strong shock magic and is wearing leveled heavy armor. He carries a copy of the [Japhet's Folly Key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Japhet%27s_Folly_Key). Once he is dead, the East Empire Company will start the bombardment.

The room at the top contains two sets of shelves holding a bunch of [tundra cotton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Tundra_Cotton), two [grass pods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Grass_Pod), a [nightshade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nightshade) flower, and several items of crockery including a [bowl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bowl) containing two [silver ingots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Ingot), as well as a small table with a leveled, empty [soul gem](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md) on top. Hanging from the ceiling are three bunches of dried [frost mirriam](Skyrim_Frost_Mirriam.md) and two bunches of [elves ear](Skyrim_Elves_Ear.md). To the southeast is a room containing an [arcane enchanter](Skyrim_Arcane_Enchanter.md), a small shelving unit holding four common [books](Skyrim_Books.md) and a random potion of healing, magicka, or stamina, an unlocked chest, a dresser of clothes, and a soul gem stand holding a random filled soul gem to the right of the dresser. When Haldyn dies, the spears at the bottom of the stairs lower, allowing access back the way you came. Returning to the wide corridor and the door to the outside, you will see the end of the passage has collapsed due to the bombardment. Thus, your only option is to exit through the door out onto the island.

### Exterior
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Japhet%27s_Folly_02.jpg) Japhet's Folly under attack The exterior before the bombardment starts consists of a series of buildings on descending levels, with wooden palisades separating the levels descending toward the docks. The first wooden palisade has gates in the middle. Through the gates are a total of four Blood Horkers, including two magic users who can be very challenging, as they use strong fire and storm spells. The first level has a campfire between two shacks. The shack on the left has a woodshed containing a woodcutter's axe, and inside the shack are two unowned bedrolls, a barrel, and a small table with a fillet of horker meat and a loaf of bread on top. The shack on the right contains a counter and several small tables and chairs, and is set up as a bar. There is a bottle of Nord mead on the counter, and behind it is a cupboard with four more bottles of Nord mead and three pieces of [raw beef](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Raw_Beef) on the shelves.

Through a gap in the next wooden palisade are two more shacks. The shack on the right has an unowned bedroll inside. The shack on the left has two sets of shelves holding four fillets of salmon meat, three fillets of horker meat, four seared slaughterfish steaks, and two samples of slaughterfish scales. There is a firepit beside this shack, with a rack holding seven whole [salmon](Skyrim_Salmon_(dead).md) hanging over it. From here there is a wooden ramp down between another wooden palisade to another level with three more shacks. The left shack contains a long wooden table with [light armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) consisting of three random [helmets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Helmet), two pairs of random [gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gauntlets), and a random [shield](Skyrim_Shield.md) on top, and two pairs of random [boots](Skyrim_Armor.md#Boots) underneath. In front of this shack is a table with many unusable barrels stacked behind, and eight bottles of Nord mead on and around the table. The middle shack is completely empty. The right shack has a [grindstone](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) and a [workbench](Skyrim_Workbench.md) in front, with a [steel greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Greatsword) on top of the workbench, and two baskets beside the workbench containing three [iron maces](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Mace) and an [iron war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_War_Axe). Inside the shack is a long table with four [iron daggers](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md), an iron mace, and an [iron battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Battleaxe) on top.

Through another gate in another wooden palisade and down another wooden ramp is a final level, where there are two more shacks. The shack on the left contains an unowned single bed at one end and two unowned bedrolls at the other. The last shack on the right contains only unusable barrels. Down on the docks is a fifth Blood Horker, who is an archer guarding the docks and two boats. The boat to the southeast has a table on deck, with two fillets of horker meat and several pieces of cheese on top. The second boat to the northwest has three weapon racks holding two random [bows](Skyrim_Bow.md), and a table with a [salmon steak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Steak) on top.

Once you have killed Haldyn, the ships brought by the East Empire Company start a bombardment which destroys all the shacks, sets fire to them, and scatters the items everywhere. You may find a few of the items lying around, but most will be lost. Taking on the Blood Horkers during the bombardment can make the battle much harder. The key carried by Haldyn opens the master-locked stockade gate closest to the docks. When you reach the table and barrels and the completely empty shack, there is now an unlocked chest containing minor loot partially buried in the floor of the shack. Down by the docks, you may find and get attacked by a single [bandit](Skyrim_Bandit.md) before reaching and speaking to [Adelaisa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Adelaisa_Vendicci).

## Notes
- You are unable to travel to Japhet's Folly before the quest Rise in the East.
- Do not return to Japhet's Folly via the Sea Cave after the bombardment has started, or you will be trapped. The top area of Japhet's Folly will be blocked by rubble, and the door from the Sea Cave will be gone, preventing you from returning back the way you came.
- Do not store anything in containers outside the fort. The containers may be destroyed and disappear once the bombardment has begun, and their contents may be lost.
- If you attempt to swim away from the island, you will be blocked by an invisible barrier in the water.
- Japhet and his journal are in the lowest part of the tower in a locked room. A locked chest can also be found here.
- According to the game data, the fort area is under the encounter zone of Rockjoint Island.

## Bugs
- Once you have discovered the location via the quest, do not return, as you will be unable to depart. Japhet's Folly has its own worldspace in the game, and has invisible boundaries which you cannot swim past. You are also unable to fast travel away from Japhet's Folly; you are presented with the same message as when trying to fast travel from an indoor place. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.9, fixes this bug.

- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) You can avoid being locked in Japhet's Folly by using the console command `coc whiterun` to teleport out.
- The standalone bandit on the island isn't affiliated with the Blood Horkers like everyone else. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.5, fixes this bug.
- If you go to Japhet's Folly with a follower, your follower may disappear.
- The explosions bombarding the tower are invisible arrows with no name. You can collect these arrows by getting hit; to use them, you must use your last standard arrow, and the invisible arrows will then become equipped.
- If you have exited the towers and proceeded through the quest to the point when the bombardment has finished, then go back into the tower and back out, the bombardment will continue endlessly.
- [![On Xbox](https://images.uesp.net/3/33/Xbox.svg)](Skyrim_Xbox.md) After returning from Japhet's Folly to complete the quest, the location icon will not appear on the map. You can visit the island as shown on the map, but all of Japhet's Folly will be gone.
