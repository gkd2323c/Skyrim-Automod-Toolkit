# Crown of Ascension

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/ON-misc-Crown_of_Ascension.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Crown_of_Ascension.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Crown_of_Ascension.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Crown_of_Ascension.jpg) The Crown of Ascension The Crown of Ascension is an ancient [Barsaebic](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). In appearance it is a simple crown, although it once supposedly possessed powerful enchantments. It is considered a treasure of great value.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Zahra-1)</sup>

## History
The Crown of Ascension was created by the [Fenlords](Lore_Fenlords.md), Ayleid [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) kings who once ruled over [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Zahra-1)</sup> The Fenlords were Barsaebic Ayleids, a subculture that emerged from the exiled [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) -worshipping Ayleids who were driven out of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) following the [Scouring of Wendelbek](Lore_Scouring_of_Wendelbek.md) in [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup> The Fenlords eventually faded from history, possibly due to being overrun by the forces of [Saint Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) during their assaults on the Ayleids of Black Marsh.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Zahra-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Captured Time-3)</sup> Other sources contradict this, claiming that the Barsaebics were spared from the Alessian pogroms.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3A-4)</sup> Some say the Fenlords created the Crown of Ascension and left [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) forever.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Zahra-1)</sup>

The Crown was ultimately lost to the ages in the depths of [Gandranen Ruins](Lore_Gandranen_Ruins.md), an Ayleid tomb and library located in the [Venomous Fens](Lore_Venomous_Fens.md) of southwestern [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md). The ruin's treasures were well guarded by the undead servants of the Fenlords, who were executed and cursed by their former masters to protect their tombs for eternity after a failed uprising. Over time, the very nature of the Crown was forgotten. Some claimed it was a jewel-encrusted crown that reveals all places and possibilities at once. Others believed the Crown was actually a tomb, where the Fenlords were said to have encased themselves in ice to wait out the long ages.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Zahra-1)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) treasure hunter named Zahra journeyed to Gandranen to discover the location of the Crown, and possibly to recover it if it was small enough to transport. Zahra was unable to overcome the undead guardians, and enlisted the aid of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to explore the ruin's interior. After facing an undead [mummified](Lore_Mummy.md) Fenlord in the depths of the ruins, the two eventually found and recovered the Crown.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Zahra subsequently brought the Crown to [Alten Corimont](Lore_Alten_Corimont.md) to be appraised by the compatriots of Captain One-Eye. Although the Crown's enchantments were greatly diminished, it was still found to be an object worthy of study. The pirates of Alten Corimont found a buyer in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and agreed to take it off her hands.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Zahra-1)</sup>
