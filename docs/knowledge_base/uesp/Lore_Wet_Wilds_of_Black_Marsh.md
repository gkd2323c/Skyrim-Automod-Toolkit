Wet Wilds of Black Marsh **by [Cirantille](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Cirantille)** *An unflattering appraisal of the wilds of Black Marsh*
---
During the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), the vast swamplands encompassing [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) were claimed as Imperial territory. Naturally, the obtuse [Elves](Lore_Mer.md) (and other admirers of this seeping pustule on the buttocks of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)) favor the name Argonia, an ancient battleground where their forebears were put to death. Perhaps because of this, it was deemed appropriate to give the primordial tribes of lizard-folk the name "Argonian" in our common tongue. What cannot be argued is the pitiful state of this province; it positively oozes with the devastated and fetid: the scars of battles past and plunderers present permanently disfigure these already-inhospitable borderlands. Wade inward, though, and the dark heart of Black Marsh will elude you; its elements combine to infect explorers with poxes both real and imagined.

The inhabitants of this province enjoy an anonymity not seen elsewhere. The early [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorer and poet [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) described "manlike reptiles, fleet of foot and running the length of this great mire," and gave the impression of an abandoned place, unlivable to settlers. However, primitive [Men](Lore_Man.md) such as the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), primal Mer like the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md), and relatives of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) like the vulpine [Lilmothiit](Lore_Lilmothiit.md) all fought for their own pieces of this noisome refuge.
