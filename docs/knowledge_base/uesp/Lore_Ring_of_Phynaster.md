# Ring of Phynaster

The Ring of Phynaster is an artifact created by the [eponymous](Lore_Phynaster.md) Hero-God of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md). The Ring improves its wearer's overall resistance to poison, magicka, and shock. The ring added much needed security for Phynaster to survive his adventurous lifestyle. Because of this, he lived for hundreds of years, and it has since been passed from person to person. The ring has become synonymous with Phynaster, so much so that it will not stay with anyone, other than the Hero-God himself. When it is kept long enough, it will disappear to another resting place, for the next adventurer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup>

## History
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWYB-2)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-3)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Ring of Phynaster among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TA-4)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Ring of Phynaster and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWFR-5)</sup>

Several rumors circulated during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) on how the ring was lost to Phynaster. One states that he was killed by the [Wharf Rats](Lore_Wharf_Rats.md), a group of outlaws and cutthroats. To cement their claim over the ring, the Wharf Rats placed a charm on the ring so that it would always return to one of their hundreds of underground sanctuaries. Another tale states that Phynaster had simply lost his ring,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Artifact-6)</sup> with supporting theories claiming that it's influenced by its need to be beside Phynaster.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Regardless, a traveler had ultimately found a map to the Ring of Phynaster's resting place, and transcribed the location to the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md). After delving through the dungeon, the champion retrieved the Ring of Phynaster.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Artifact-6)</sup>

By [the Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) outbreak in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the Ring of Phynaster was located at the Senim Ancestral Tomb on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), on the corpse of Pop Je.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Later that year, the Nerevarine sold the ring to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md), who put it on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The Ring of Phynaster was later found in a body of water by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) while fishing circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
