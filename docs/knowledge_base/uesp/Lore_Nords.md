# Nord

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/LO-race-Nord.png/200px-LO-race-Nord.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Nord.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Nord.png) Illustration of a typical Nord male **Nords**, also known as **Northmen**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vaload-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> **Bron** in the [Language of Dragons](Lore_Dragon_Language.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PRIMA-3)</sup><sup>:1096</sup> or the **Children of the Sky**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTS-4)</sup> are a race of tall and fair-haired [humans](Lore_Man.md) from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) who are known for their incredible resistance to cold and magical frost. They are fierce, strong and enthusiastic warriors, and many become renowned warriors, soldiers and mercenaries all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HOTFG-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLSC-6)</sup> Eager to augment their martial skills beyond the traditional methods of Skyrim, they excel in all manner of warfare, and are known as a militant people by their neighbors.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ASHOM-8)</sup> Nords were known for a time for their nautical prowess when they migrated from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ASHOM-8)</sup> Seafaring merchants or smugglers are not unheard of, as was the case with [Tobias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Tobias).

When the descendants of the Atmorans transitioned into calling themselves Nords is blurred. The inhabitants of Skyrim during the Dragon War were variously labeled Nords<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-9)</sup> and Atmorans.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FSL2-10)</sup> The truth may lie somewhere in-between; while the two are considered distinct races,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TOo C-11)</sup> the men living in the transition period between the terms' respective usage have been considered 'proto-Nords'.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-EGTS-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-High King Sunstone-14)</sup> [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) started a trend when he relinquished all holdings in distant and lost Atmora and united the disparate Nordic people to formally create the Kingdom of Skyrim.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> What followed was the "first Nords" under Harald wished to distinguish themselves from their Atmoran ancestors, but they still felt pride for their ancient heritage.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CM87AN-15)</sup> According to *[Great Harbingers](Lore_Great_Harbingers.md)*, these people didn't consider themselves Nords until sometime after the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) arrived to Tamriel.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GH-16)</sup> However, some Redguards were known to have inhabited the continent long prior to their mass migration in [1E 800](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_800).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), the land of the Nords Although Nords intermingled with other races over the years, it is primarily to [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), the northernmost known place on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), that they trace their lineage.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-12)</sup> Atmora is likely a human corruption of "Altmora", a name found in old Elvish records which means "Elder Wood".<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MA-18)</sup> Legends say that Atmora was once very green and prosperous, until "the freezing" turned it into an inhospitable wasteland plagued by civil war, causing its people to migrate to Tamriel in waves throughout the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and [First](Lore_First_Era.md) eras.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SOTR-19)</sup> During this migration, the chieftain [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) rallied people from all sides who desired to live in peace and set sail south, eventually arriving at [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md), at the extreme northern tip of Skyrim's [Broken Cape](Lore_Broken_Cape.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> They named the new land "Mereth" in tribute to the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) Elves who had already settled most of the continent. Contrary to many stories, which apparently credit Ysgramor with being the leader of the first group of human settlers,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TAA-21)</sup> he and his colonists were the latest in a long line of emigrants from Atmora to Skyrim, and many had already migrated to other places around Tamriel.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PGE3S-22)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-12)</sup>

### The Return
Elves and men lived in relative peace and prosperity for a great deal of time, but racial tensions grew along with the human population, and eventually violence erupted. It's unclear how it started, but the Elves razed the Nordic capital city of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md), killing the defenders and everyone unable to flee, in a slaughter now known as the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-20)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-IROS-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-NOT-24)</sup> According to legend, the only humans to survive were Ysgramor and his two sons, who fled back to Atmora, where they gathered the famous Five Hundred Companions and sailed back to expediently slaughter any Elves they came across, founding new cities as they went and clearing the way for new settlers.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-20)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GH-16)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-YATS-25)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SOTR-19)</sup> During this time, the Nords also frequently waged war with the various [Giant](Lore_Giant.md) clans of Skyrim and Ysgramor purportedly slaughtered hundreds of Giants himself.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Sot R27-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONSvari-27)</sup> Despite these early conflicts, the presence of Giants remained throughout the province in the eras that followed, although both races largely kept to themselves.

The Five Hundred's great achievements and acts of heroism cemented them and their leader Ysgramor as role models in Nordic culture.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SOTR-19)</sup> Their legacy is purportedly carried on by the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md), a group of nonpartisan mercenaries who are renowned as impartial arbiters on matters of honor.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GH-16)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SOTR-19)</sup> Ysgramor's descendant High King Harald, who is credited with first uniting the province under its customary borders drove the last of the Elves out of the province in [1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> Despite heavy resistance, Elves were also driven from [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), a small island northeast of Skyrim, which would be predominantly inhabited by Nords for thousands of years to come.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-FOTSP-28)</sup>

### The Dragon War
[Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) were revered as part of the [Nordic religion](#Religion).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup> Dragon priests, on par with kings in terms of the power they wielded, acted as intermediaries between the people and the serpentine "god-kings", whose name could not even be uttered by the common folk.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup> Temples were built to honor and appease the dragons, many of which survive today as ancient ruins haunted by [Draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) and undead dragon priests.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ATD-30)</sup> The dragon priests in Tamriel became more tyrannical and the populace eventually rebelled some time in the Merethic Era, leading to the legendary Dragon War.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TBDr-31)</sup> Some dragons turned against their own kind and taught the Nords powerful magic that allowed them to turn the tide of the war in their favor.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup> After a long and bloody campaign, the rule of the dragons was ended, and the remaining wyrms fled to remote areas.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup> The [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) survived for a time, but was marginalized and eventually died out.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup> The last Dragon Cult Stronghold was found and besieged in [1E 140](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_140).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SSSJ-32)</sup> Not long after, worship of animal gods was replaced by the then- [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) in mainstream Nord culture.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TIEGTTS-33)</sup> Other traditional Nordic beliefs, known as the Old Ways, continued on, and remained prevalent among Nords even after the introduction of the Divines.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-RCSO-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SRFroki-35)</sup>

### The Skyrim Conquests
In [1E 241](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_241), [King Vrage the Gifted](Lore_Vrage.md) (Harald's son) began the aggressive expansion now known as the Skyrim Conquests, which would culminate in the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-12)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-KEX-36)</sup> Within a span of fifty years, the descendants of Ysgramor ruled all of northern Tamriel, including most of present-day [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and the whole of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-12)</sup> Some Nord leaders wanted to turn south to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), but the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) proved to be too big a barrier, and northern Cyrodiil too poor a prize.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLSC-6)</sup> However the Nordic Empire allied with Alessia's slave rebellion, and aided in overturning the Ayleid Empire, and in return were given territory in the Nibenay Valley.

Over the next few centuries, Skyrim expanded and contracted as battles were won and lost.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PGE3S-22)</sup> The Conquests, and the Empire, came to an end in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369) with the death of King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md), the last of the Ysgramor line, during the [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLSC-6)</sup> When the ruling council or [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) was unable to choose [Jarl Hanse](Lore_Jarl_Hanse.md) of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) (considered the obvious choice by [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholars), as the new High King, the ensuing civil war tore the Empire apart.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> The war concluded in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) with the [Pact of Chieftains](Lore_Pact_of_Chieftains.md), but the Empire lost its holdings in High Rock and Morrowind, and Skyrim was divided into independent kingdoms.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> The Nords were overthrown by a combined alliance of Chimer and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) who formed the First Council. The failure of the Nord Tongues in Morrowind prompted [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md) to begin a seven-year meditation to understand the failure, leading to the foundation of the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Hrothgar-37)</sup>

The Nords would make an alliance with Orcs to try to reconquer Morrowind around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), partaking in the War of the First Council, but it is unclear whether they allied with the Chimer, the Dwemer, or acted as a third party. Nevertheless the Nords failed to increase their territory, and the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) would protect Morrowind from invasion for thousands of years to come.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-36LS9-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-39)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ASHOM-8)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-NARM-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-FSOKW-41)</sup>

### The Second Empire and the Interregnum
Late in the First Era, an invasion from [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) cut through Skyrim.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-42)</sup> Even though Nords "don't meet invasions with pitchers of mead", they were unable to stop the mighty Akaviri Dragonguard, and it took the united armies of Cyrodiil to stop their advance at the Battle of Pale Pass.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-42)</sup> The Nords were understandably impressed, and for the first time, the whole of Skyrim pledged allegiance to one man: [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman_Cyrodiil.md), one of the first Dragonborn recorded in history and the founder of the Second Empire of Man.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-42)</sup> The Nords would generally support the Empire, even under the [Potentates](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), for hundreds of years to come.

Only a year after the assassination of the last Potentate in 430 and the official dissolution of the 2nd Empire, High King Logrolf was assassinated and Skyrim was partitioned between the east and west following a succession dispute. Western Skyrim would consist of Haafingar, Hjaalmarch, Falkreath, Whiterun and a portion of the Reach named Karthald, and Eastern Skyrim was composed of Winterhold, Eastmarch, The Pale, and the Rift. Various factions vyed for control of Cyrodiil and its claim of Empire, most climactically in the Three Banners War. While Western Skyrim maintained neutrality, Eastern Skyrim would help form the Ebonheart Pact. Eastern Skyrim was ravaged by the second Akaviri invasion in 2E 572, with Windhelm sacked, but the Nords pursued the invading Akaviri into Morrowind, where they were joined by the defending Dunmer. The Akaviri forces were successfully wiped out thanks to the unexpected arrival of Argonian armies from Black Marsh's northern tribes. The victors of the battle formed the Ebonheart Pact (though House Telvannis abstained in protest with the precondition of ending the enslavement of Argonians) with the aim to maintain their mutual independence and defense, and would war against the Greater Daggerfall Covenant and 1st Aldmeri Dominion for control of Cyrodiil. However the Ebonheart Pact would ultimately dissolve at some point during the Second Era, as would their main competitors in the Alliance War.

The chaos of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) brought the Nords an opportunity for glorious battle against their neighbors. Together with the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of High Rock, they once again looked south, to Cyrodiil, for room to expand.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-BOST-43)</sup> Despite some initial success, they did not count on [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). According to Nordic Tradition, Tiber Septim was born in Atmora under the name Hjalti Early-Beard (though Bretons claim he was born instead on the island of Alcaire). He served as General to Cuhlecain, king of Falkreath and the Colovian Estates. Hjalti Early-Beard used the power of the Thu'um to shout down the walls at Old Hroldan, allowing Culhecain to conquer the Reach. This led the Greybeards to summon him to High Hrothgar where they named him Talos.

The Nords were suspicious of the rising power to the south, and allied with their Breton neighbors to subdue them, leading to the Battle of Sancre Tor. Despite being seemingly impregnable, a spy within the Nordic ranks allowed Tiber Septim to take the ancient city, and upon demonstrating his Thu'um the Nords reportedly bent their knee and pledged fealty to Tiber Septim (reportedly the Nords had already grown suspicious of their Breton allies). This marks the beginning of the integration of Skyrim in what would soon become the Third Empire.

### Under the Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/BM-prerelease-Bear_Nord.jpg/200px-BM-prerelease-Bear_Nord.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-prerelease-Bear_Nord.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-prerelease-Bear_Nord.jpg) A Third Era Nord Skyrim was absorbed comparatively peacefully into the empire of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), the Battle of Sancre Tor around [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852) and other clashes notwithstanding.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-BOST-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TMOTR-44)</sup> Many Nords found employment in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), as Talos generally nurtured relations with the north.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-BOST-43)</sup> In the centuries after the Empire's founding, Skyrim was drawn into several major conflicts. One of these was the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) wherein Skyrim supported [Queen Potema](Lore_Potema.md) against the Empire.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-45)</sup> The civil war nearly tore the Empire apart and it would take seventeen long years before Potema was finally defeated and peace was restored.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-45)</sup> However, a strong underground movement called the Hörme, believing that Potema and her deposed son were the last of Tiber Septim's true blood, continued to work against Imperial interests in Skyrim.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PGE3S-22)</sup>

Close to the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the kingdoms of Skyrim instigated several wars to expand their territory. [The War of the Bend'r-Mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) increased Nordic holdings considerably, swallowing up many miles of territory traditionally belonging to eastern High Rock and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and they have their eyes on Morrowind, which is no longer protected by the Tribunal.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PGE3S-22)</sup>

### The Fourth Era
The [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) brought significant changes for Nords. Late in the Third Era and in the beginning years of the Fourth Era, Solstheim became overrun by Dunmer refugees from Morrowind, which had been devastated by natural disasters and then invaded by the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) who had already conquered much of Morrowind.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SOTGQ-46)</sup> The Nords of Solstheim wanted to win independence from the Empire, and planned to destroy Fort Frostmoth.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Oblivion-47)</sup> Many Dunmer also fled west on the mainland, establishing a strong presence in cities in eastern Skyrim.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-DOS-48)</sup> Following the sacking of [Nova Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), many Orc refugees were escorted into Skyrim by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-LOS-49)</sup> Many Orcs choose to segregate their society and live in Orc Strongholds scattered throughout the wilderness, but some others have, like the Dunmer, chosen to live in the "civilized" settlements of Skyrim. Despite some trepidation at these newcomers, the Nords remained relatively peaceful and prosperous, with some exceptions,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-OCD-50)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-OTGC-51)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TBOM-52)</sup> in the tumultuous aftermath of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SOTGQ-46)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-TGW-53)</sup> However, their peace would not last.

Around [4E 200](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_200), a little over twenty years after the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) ended, the High King of Skyrim was killed. Resentments created by the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) which ended the Great War, launched Skyrim into the bloody [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), named after the leader of the rebels, Jarl [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-TGW-53)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Skyrim-54)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-FFTT-55)</sup> Even more disturbing was the [return](Lore_Return_of_the_Dragons.md) of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), the ancient enemy from the Dragon War who had led the massacres against them, which Nordic religion had long held would herald the end of the world.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Skyrim-54)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SOSR-56)</sup>

## Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/SR-concept-Ice_Wraiths.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Ice_Wraiths.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Ice_Wraiths.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Ice_Wraiths.jpg) A drawing of Ice Wraiths, which are hunted by young Nords as a rite of passage [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/SR-creature-Falmer.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Falmer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer.jpg) A [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), source of many Nord superstitions, and a very real and bitter race of elves Nords value physical strength and skill very highly. They were also known to hold powerful mages to similar esteem, though this has fallen out of favor in recent history.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Tsun-57)</sup> There are two things most Nords love: music and [mead](Lore_Mead.md).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SOSR-56)</sup> Most Nords wear animal skins or cotton clothing.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-HGTS-58)</sup> They are generally tolerant of outsiders to Skyrim, though often do not make them feel welcome.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GH-16)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-DOS-48)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-GITS-59)</sup> In particular, Nords often still hold a bitter resentment towards elves. [The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), one of the nine [Holds](Lore_Hold.md) of Skyrim, has only a slight Nord majority, and cities in the east have become heavily influenced by the Dunmer.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-DOS-48)</sup> The northern and eastern holds - [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), [The Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), and [The Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) —are known collectively as the Old Holds, where the influence of old Nordic traditions is still relatively strong and outsiders are rare.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> The young men there go out for weeks into the high peaks in the dead of winter, hunting the ice wraiths that give them claim to full status as citizens.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> The Reach and the Rift both have long histories of lawlessness, and are generally associated with criminal activity.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-TCOS-60)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-AEGTS-61)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TMOTR-44)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TBOM-52)</sup>

Nords tend to be superstitious, and their folklore reflects this.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-AEGTS-61)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-THOTR-62)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-SOTAM-63)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-LL-64)</sup> [Nordic names](Lore_Nord_Names.md), often chosen based on omens, are given in a special ceremony when the child is young.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-THOTR-62)</sup> Any unexplained misfortune is often blamed on the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), or Snow Elves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> The Falmer, long believed to be extinct after the vengeful Nordic invasion into their lands, were actually driven deep underground, where they became feral and purportedly seek to kill all those who dwell above them,<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-TFAS-65)</sup> so it's possible there's truth in some of these claims.

Nords practice wergild, a traditional rite of retribution. When a life is taken, a life must be accounted for. Items of high value can serve as fitting payment.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Kolfina-66)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-67)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-DDO6-68)</sup>

### Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/SR-place-Falkreath.jpg/200px-SR-place-Falkreath.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath.jpg) The typical Nord buildings of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) Nords are known as masters of wood and timber construction.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> Besides the cities of Skyrim, their architectural style is reflected in [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), situated in northern Cyrodiil.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-GTB-69)</sup> Nord buildings are built partly underground to conserve heat, and are made with stones, with wood used only for support, and roofs of straw above the wood. These houses are essential to keep in heat in the freezing climate. Similar designs are also seen in Solstheim.

Nordic buildings are known for being able to withstand the harshest elements for thousands of years.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> Old Fort, one of the royal bastions constructed by the First Empire to guard its southern frontier, is a fine example: "towering walls of huge, irregular porphyry blocks fit together without seam or mortar" have stood since the First Era.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup>

### Fashion: Armaments, Cosmetics, etc.
Nordic armor is typically lined with fur for heat retention, with leather trim work and steel plating on top. Typically, the hides from [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md), [horkers](Lore_Horker.md), [sabre cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md), or [mammoths](Lore_Mammoth.md) are used for undergarment protection. Nordic weapons are thick and practical, with strong oak utilized for the wood of various weapon types like axes, maces, and staves. Leather grips are not uncommon, with steel being the most common material used in weaponry like swords.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ILAANC-70)</sup> However, Nordic steel is known for being a combination of iron, steel, and corundum which gives it an edge over many kinds of steel. A widespread superstition among Nord smiths is by adding a drop of wild [bee](Lore_Bee.md) honey into all their creations, believing it will give them more cutting power.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-ONNAA-71)</sup> Massive steel weaponry can be forged according to the secrets developed by Nord smiths, where runes are engraved on them like Skyrim's legendary witch-warriors.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-MWGaro-72)</sup> The [Skyforge](Lore_Skyforge.md) is known for producing Skyforge Steel<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Merenfire-73)</sup> said to be the highest quality steel in all of Tamriel.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Eorlund-74)</sup> It is said that a blade forged in its fires can cut through sinew as if it were parchment.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-75)</sup> Knotwork, animalistic symbolism, or patterns such as the cross of Ysgramor can be found on Nordic equipment.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ILAANC-70)</sup>

Nords make use of trollbone and trollskin as materials for crafting armor.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-76)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-77)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-78)</sup> Troll teeth are sometimes used for arms or jewelry.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-79)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-80)</sup> The Imperial scholar Reginus Buca once mentioned having heard unconfirmed reports that some Nords use troll spit to enhance their grip in battle, he suggested they might also use it to bind books.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-81)</sup>

Ancient Nords used Dragonscale and Dragonbone arms and armor during the Merethic Era.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-82)</sup> There is considerable evidence that orthodox Dragon Priests had their armor crafted from genuine remains of the great creatures.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-CM59-83)</sup> Some Dragons have awarded pieces of themselves willingly as part of an alliance or bargain. The Dragon [Numinex](Lore_Numinex.md) provided Draconic armor in exchange for his life.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Dov-rha_Sabatons-84)</sup>

[Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) is a rare form of enchanted ice found only on the island on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). Throughout history it has served various uses. Originally it was used in the burial rituals of ancient Nords,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Fot SP-85)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-DBloading-86)</sup> It has also served as a crafting material and, with the proper tools and techniques, can be used to create weapons, armor, clothing, and even jewelry.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-BM-87)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-88)</sup> The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) believe the art of forging Stalhrim into weapons and armor is sacred, and certain members of the Skaal were charged as keepers of this ancient tradition.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Deor-89)</sup> As such, knowledge of stalhrim smithing is very rare and typically known only to the Skaal.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-DBloading-86)</sup> However, local Nords and the warriors of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) have also been known to possess such knowledge.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-SRHIF-90)</sup>

Jarls may wear crowns made from [horker](Lore_Horker.md) leather and reused metal from weapons.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-91)</sup> Braided hair styles are popular among the Nords, so to protect these braids as they sleep, hair nets made from silver mesh are used.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-92)</sup> Oil sourced from springtime [spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md) may be used in the upkeep of their beards.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-93)</sup>

Nords tend to favor rustic scents that bring to mind things like winter,<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-94)</sup> or products made with ingredients harvested from flora and fauna native to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) like the musk of [frostbite spiders](Lore_Spider.md).<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-95)</sup> The essence of [mammoths](Lore_Mammoth.md) is a fragrance created in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) that is popular with both maiden and matron alike.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-96)</sup> The [jazbay](Lore_Jazbay.md) grapes native to the province are used in a tangy scented cologne.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-97)</sup>

### Food
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/ON-crown_store-Deepsnow_Prairie_Dog.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Deepsnow_Prairie_Dog.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Deepsnow_Prairie_Dog.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Deepsnow_Prairie_Dog.jpg) A Deepsnow Prairie Dog, considered a morsel to Nords
- *Main Article: [Nord Cuisine](Lore_Nord_Cuisine.md)*

[Nord cuisine](Lore_Nord_Cuisine.md) reflects the harsh climate and rugged landscape that has shaped their warrior culture. Farming is difficult, but wild game supplies a good deal of their nutrition. These meats are preserved through smoking, drying, pickling, or salting. Fish are also a prominent feature in Nord cooking, such as the salmon endemic to the White River. The Nords are reputed for their great, warm, hearty feasts. Large roasts of [mammoth](Lore_Mammoth.md), [horker](Lore_Horker.md), and assorted game meats are cooked over a spit in the center of the hall, while rustic desserts made with juniper or snowberries are baked in cob ovens. The fruits available in [Skyrim's](Lore_Skyrim.md) scattered bushes have also made their way into the mead that is much-loved by this rugged race, which has developed a reputation abroad for being sweet and strong.

## Religion
The Nords are considered to be a devout people with grim religious beliefs. Among other things, they have long believed that Alduin, the World-Eater, would eventually return and bring about the end of the world.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup> As dark as their belief systems are viewed to be, all Nordic traditions extend one bright, shining hope for Nords: [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), the Hall of Valor, where Nords who have proven their mettle in battle or died valiantly are welcome to experience euphoric bliss and camaraderie, free from time and boredom.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-SAR-120)</sup><sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ADOS-121)</sup> Some stories claim the place was built by, and still inhabited by, the elusive [Shor](Lore_Shor.md).<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-SAR-120)</sup> It is little surprise that cowardice is the worst trait a Nord can exhibit, for "a Nord is judged not by the manner in which he lived, but the manner in which he died".<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ADOS-121)</sup> Nords cross the whalebone bridge to reach Sovngarde, and flying whales are a cultural metaphor for the transition from Nirn to Aetherius.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-Carved Whale-122)</sup>

### The Old Ways
Ysgramor and the Atmorans brought with them the worship of animal gods: the hawk, wolf, snake, moth, owl, whale, bear, fox, and most importantly the dragon.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup> Over time, as Nord beliefs evolved, the traditional Nordic Pantheon of [Divines](Lore_Gods.md) emerged as personifications of natural forces and ideas.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup> Many scholars believe that the Nordic Pantheon is the same as the orthodox pantheon, merely with different names (there are certainly many parallels), and many Nords who have adopted the Divines evidently have this understanding.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg/200px-Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg) A map of Solstheim during the Third Era The isolated Nordic tribe in [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md), carry on a tradition very similar to the old tradition of animal worship. The Skaal venerate all of nature, believing that certain parts of their environment, such as the winds, the trees and the sun, were given to them by the All-Maker, a benevolent, unknowable creator deity. Wolves and bears are especially sacred to the small tribe. All aspects of nature must constantly be in harmony, for this is what gives the Skaal their shamanic powers. The Skaal also tell tales of the Adversary, the enemy of mankind, and his lieutenant, the Greedy Man. In this regard, the faith of the Skaal is nearly the opposite of the pantheon of their kin in Skyrim.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-TSOASS-123)</sup> Although the Skaal do not worship them, they also acknowledge the existence of the Daedric Princes.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-Storn DB-124)</sup>

### The Nordic Pantheon
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/SR-place-Hall_of_Valor.jpg/200px-SR-place-Hall_of_Valor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Hall_of_Valor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Hall_of_Valor.jpg) The Hall of Valor in [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), the Nordic afterlife. It is a huge mead-hall which houses the souls of honorable heroes, by will of [Shor](Lore_Shor.md). Like most modern Tamrielic races, Nordic religion is focused on the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) and their old ally, [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), which they call Shor.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-SATD-125)</sup> However, the Nord's Sky Goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) is notably more assertive and warlike than the nature-loving [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md).<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup> Although [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) is present in her role as a mother goddess, she is thought of as a mere handmaiden to Kyne, the actual mother of the Nords and the widow to Shor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTS-4)</sup><sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup> She is also credited with sending her son [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) (and perhaps [Pelinal](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md)) to the aid of the [Cyro-Nordic](Lore_Cyro-Nord.md) slaves in their uprising against the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) around [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242).<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-SATD-125)</sup> Shortly after, the Nordic pantheon of gods would be fused with the Aldmeri pantheon by [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) into the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) (although this new belief system would be bucked occasionally).<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-SATD-125)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-FSOKW-41)</sup> Interestingly, certain [Daedra](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), notably [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), are present in Nordic mythos.

Alduin, the Dragon
- Dreadful World-Eater, and Twilight God that will usher in the next cycle.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-129)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-DATN-128)</sup> It is unclear if at one point the Dragon Totem was held by Akatosh (potentially under a different name) as distinct from Alduin under the Dragon Cult, prior to Alduin. It is possible that the shift from Akatosh to Alduin as the Dragon Totem occurred during the Cult's lifetime due to Alduin's hunger for Akatosh's crown <sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-9)</sup><sup>[[130]](#cite_note-TWa S-130)</sup>, or later under the religious reforms of King Wulfharth.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-TWa S-130)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-FSOKW-41)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/SR-concept-Alduin.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Alduin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Alduin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Alduin.jpg) Alduin, the World-Eater The former chief of the Nordic pantheon of Skyrim is Shor. He was the king of the gods and a champion of men in their struggles against the Elves,<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup> until being treacherously slain by elven giants, and consigned to serve as god of the underworld. He may still have an impact on the mortal world in the form of the [Shezarrine](Lore_Shezarrine.md).<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-TFS-131)</sup> Shor was left out of the Eight Divines, but is still represented in a way acceptable to some Nords as "the spirit behind all human undertaking" in the Cyrodilic pantheon.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-SATD-125)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-FSOKW-41)</sup> Common wisdom holds that Shor, [Shezarr](Lore_Shezarr.md), [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md) and [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) are the same entity.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup><sup>[[125]](#cite_note-SATD-125)</sup><sup>[[132]](#cite_note-TTOD-132)</sup><sup>[[130]](#cite_note-TWa S-130)</sup>

The traditional Nordic pantheon has had a very muddled history with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), the Dragon God of Time, and misconceptions abound. The totem animals of the Old Ways included the dragon, whom scholars correlate with the worship of Akatosh, but the veneration of dragons understandably dwindled among Nords after the Dragon War.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup> The Nords of the First Era thus only came to know Akatosh as Auri-El, the Elven deity, whom they demonized, and as a name for Alduin, in contrast to most others at the time who made the opposite claim.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-APQ-133)</sup><sup>[[134]](#cite_note-DC-134)</sup>.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-SATD-125)</sup> Although scholarly thought itself in Cyrodiil was not unanimous on the Alduin/Akatosh dichotomy, as some declared Alduin the Nordic Akatosh.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-VOFIT-135)</sup> Nevertheless, Akatosh was reintroduced in Alessia's compromised pantheon. In contrast to the earlier eras of scholarly thought in Cyrodiil and Alinor, recognizing Akatosh and Alduin as distinct, by the Fourth Era common thought has aligned with the earlier Nordic view that they are one and the same.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup><sup>[[136]](#cite_note-TAAD-136)</sup><sup>[[137]](#cite_note-AIR-137)</sup> However the Nords themselves have seemingly switched in opinion in the meantime, now viewing them as distinct and citing ancient oral traditions, as well as the aid Akatosh gave Martin Septim, as evidence they are distinct. These new views on Alduin and Akatosh from the Nords however, are dismissed by modern scholars.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup><sup>[[136]](#cite_note-TAAD-136)</sup><sup>[[137]](#cite_note-AIR-137)</sup>

### The Thu'um and the Dragonborn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg/200px-SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Throat_of_the_World.jpg) The Throat of the World [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fa/Lore-dragon-Poem.png/200px-Lore-dragon-Poem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Lore-dragon-Poem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Lore-dragon-Poem.png) A poem in the Dragon Language The spiritual relationship between the Nords and breath is crucial to understanding Nordic beliefs and motivations. They believe Kyne breathed life into them at the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md), the highest mountain in Skyrim.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTS-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> Other Nordic legends claim the race originated from Atmora.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-Psijic Bear-138)</sup> Another creation myth involves the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), wandering progenitors of the [Mythic Era](Lore_Mythic_Era.md), some of whom were displaced to Atmora during the Ehlnofey wars of the Dawn Era and became the Nords.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TAA-21)</sup> The Nords themselves do not believe in any single creation myth, as they hate to waste time thinking over it, and for many Nords the closest allegory they have for one is simply the oldest story they know.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONLCM-139)</sup> The two stories are not necessarily in conflict, as they both seem to suggest that Nordic ancestors emerged in Skyrim, flourished in Atmora, then returned. Regardless, the Nords believe that their breath and voice are their vital essence, and that by uttering shouts in the [tongue of the dragons](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), they can channel their essence to perform incredible feats.

Nords have been able to use the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), a magical shout capable of extraordinary power, as a nearly unstoppable weapon against their adversaries as far back as the late Mythic Era.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TDW-29)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-TAOWM-140)</sup> They view it as a gift from Kyne, and those with the talent to wield it are called "Tongues".<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-VOF-115)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTS-4)</sup> The ancient [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), masters of the Thu'um, still sit atop the 7000 steps leading to the settlement of [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md), near the summit of the Throat of the World, where they practice the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> Their leader, [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md), brought about a ban on the use of the Thu'um outside of times of "True Need".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Hrothgar-37)</sup> Tiber Septim established the Imperial College of the Voice in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) in an attempt to turn the Way of the Voice to warfare.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup> While some Tongues like Ulfric Stormcloak have proved willing to use the Thu'um for violence, the use of the Thu'um in warfare has remained extremely rare among Nords. It is false to assume, however, that the Way of the Voice demands pacifism: while the Greybeards don't take up arms directly, they occasionally speak, and thereby set titanic events in motion. They have spoken together on only two known occasions: to announce the destiny of Tiber Septim and, later, to do the same for the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Skyrim-54)</sup>

Today, Ysmir is the name by which Nords recognize the divinity of Talos. However, several indiviudals have held the title of Ysmir.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-TAH-141)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-36LS9-38)</sup> The title seemed to have originated with [Ysmir Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), the Atmoran-born ruler of ancient Skyrim whose Thu'um was so powerful that he could not speak without causing destruction.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTS-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-FSOKW-41)</sup> This is actually rather typical of the greatest masters (powerful Tongues are often gagged for safety).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTS-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1S-7)</sup>

In Nordic society, the Dragonborn is an archetype for what a Nord should be, and any Dragonborn is treated with a deep respect.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SOSR-56)</sup> A Dragonborn can not only wield the power of the Thu'um like other Tongues, but can also absorb the souls of dragons, as well as knowledge of the Thu'um, thereby achieving in a short time what it takes others a lifetime to learn.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-TBOTD-142)</sup> The "Dragonborn Emperors" were able to rely on this cultural influence to cement the fealty of the Nords, while the Emperors of the Fourth Era were not.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-TBOTD-142)</sup><sup>[[142]](#cite_note-TOC-143)</sup> It is likely because of this that so many Nords are unwilling to give up the worship of Talos, even in the face of a ban by the Empire.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-FFTT-55)</sup>

### Funerary Customs and Ancestor Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/SR-concept-Nordic Barrow Interior Ruins.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Nordic Barrow Interior Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Nordic Barrow Interior Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Nordic Barrow Interior Ruins.jpg) Ancient nordic barrow [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_%28Whiterun%29_03.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_%28Whiterun%29_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_(Whiterun)_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_(Whiterun)_03.jpg) Hall of the Dead
- *Main Article: [Nord Death Beliefs](Lore_Nord_Death_Beliefs.md)*

#### Ancient
The ancient Nords built great barrows and underground structures to bury their dead. Often these tombs were built for one person, a king, a priest or a great hero, but were further used for their followers or family.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

The way corpses were entombed was varied. Sometimes small ossuaries were embedded in the pillars of the barrow, where bones and skulls were stacked on top of one each other.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-SKBB-144)</sup><sup>[[144]](#cite_note-SKCA-145)</sup> Some corpses were placed in sturdy and heavy sarcophagi, but most of them were placed to rest in open grave niches in the tunnels of the catacombs.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

In some cases the funerary practices involved a ship burial. Olmgerd the Outlaw in Tukushapal was given such a burial.<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-Ennbjof-146)</sup><sup>[[146]](#cite_note-MT-147)</sup>.

Ancient burial practices on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) involved covering the deceased with [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md), a magical and nearly unbreakable material, to protect the body.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

#### Modern
Some Nords may still use their family's tombs, but nowadays it is common for them to fill an urn or a coffin in one of the [halls of the dead](Lore_Hall_of_the_Dead.md), where a priest of Arkay watches over their remains.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

#### Ancestor Worship

- *Main Article: [Ancestor Worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md#Other_Traditions)*

The Nords honor their ancestors and place offerings in or at their graves. Apples, wine, gold coins or [snowberries](Skyrim_Snowberries.md) are often offered to the ancestors.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-148)</sup>

## Artifacts

#### [Bow of the Fox](Lore_Bow_of_the_Fox.md)
[Bow of the Fox](Lore_Bow_of_the_Fox.md), also known as **Bow of Shor**, is a legendary weapon linked to [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) wielded by Njor the Brave, celebrated in the legends and songs of the [Haefriding](Lore_Haefriding.md) [Nords](Lore_Nords.md). According to the legend it manifested from a dream, transforming from the visage of the [fox](Lore_Fox.md) into a bow and a set of arrows. The tales describe the bow as glowing, and gleaming with power.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg/150px-SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg) Dragon Priest Masks
#### [Dragon Priest Masks](Lore_Dragon_Priest_Masks.md)
The [masks](Lore_Dragon_Priest_Masks.md) of the [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) are strange [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). The highest ranking priests of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) were granted magical masks that defy the laws of time and possess powerful, mysterious enchantments. The individual masks are made of varying materials, and each bears the same name as the Dragon Priest that possessed it. The names are in the [dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), and can be translated. The number of masks in existence is unknown, and some are apparently older than others. The masks were buried with their owners, until in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) the return of the dragons caused many of the undead Dragon Priests to awake from their slumber.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/SR-item-Fists_of_Randagulf.jpg/150px-SR-item-Fists_of_Randagulf.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Fists_of_Randagulf.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Fists_of_Randagulf.jpg) Fists of Randagulf
#### [Fists of Randagulf](Lore_Fists_of_Randagulf.md)
The [Fists of Randagulf](Lore_Fists_of_Randagulf.md) were worn by Randagulf of Clan Begalin, one of the mightiest warriors from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history. He finally lost the gloves, and his life, when High King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) conquered Skyrim circa [1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143) - [1E 221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221). As a sign of respect, Harald took Randagulf's gauntlets for himself. After the High King died, the gauntlets disappeared. The Fists grant the bearer added strength.

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Jagged_Crown.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Jagged_Crown.png) The Jagged Crown
#### [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md)
The [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md) is a symbol of office for the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), a symbol of the might and power of the ruler of the Nords going back to the time of High King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) ([1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143) - [221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221)), or even before. Legend holds that it was forged from the bones and teeth of ancient [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) by Harald himself. A portion of the power of every king who has worn it is said to be contained within.

#### [Long-Launcher](Lore_Long-Launcher.md)
[Long-Launcher](Lore_Long-Launcher.md) was the legendary longbow [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md). He used it during many hunts and battles throughout the course of his life in both the [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) [Frostwood](Lore_Atmoran_Frostwood.md) and in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). During his time in Atmora, the bow once needed to be re-strung, so Ysgramor collected the laments of the [Giant](Lore_Giant.md) -Wives and brought them to individuals known as [Froa](Lore_Froa.md) and [Grosta](Lore_Grosta.md) who twisted them into the Woeful Bowstring. When the bow made use of this new bowstring, it took on a form of sentience, sighing when Ysgramor held it and moaning whenever it was shot.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg/150px-SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) Wuuthrad
#### [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md)
[Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md), meaning "Storm's Tears" in the language of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), was the legendary battleaxe wielded by [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) during his conquest of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). With Wuuthrad in his hands, Ysgramor and his [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) slaughtered an untold number of [elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md), which culminated in the founding of the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md). For the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md), Wuuthrad is a sacred relic which holds a great deal of respect and reverence, and through it they can trace their line straight back to the first Harbinger. It is held to be the greatest of all Nord [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md).

## Notes
- Common Nord names, as well as certain aspects of Nord culture and tradition, are inspired by real-world [Nordic](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nordic_countries) cultures and [Norse mythology](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norse_mythology). Their burial practices are based off [Ancient Egypt](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt), with the idea behind Nordic ruins being to have magnificent stone structures that would stand the test of time, like the pyramids of Egypt. [Adam Adamowicz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Adam_Adamowicz) came up with having them shaped like ribcages, giving the developers the sense that they were made out of dead dragon skeletons, and were filled with iconography similar to egyptian heiroglyphs.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-149)</sup> Other inspirations in naming and culture periodically come from [Germanic](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germanic) and [Slavic](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavic) motifs.
