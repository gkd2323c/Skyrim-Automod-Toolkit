# Numidium

— [Anonymous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:The_Mantella_Revealed#Letters_You_May_Receive) Numidium,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DM-1)</sup> also known as **Anumidium**, **Anumidum**, the **Brass God**, the **Prime Gestalt**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> **NM**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ti S5-3)</sup> the **[Brass Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md)**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBOTD-4)</sup> **Walk-Brass**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONAugur-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-6)</sup> **Big Walker**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WWYDB-7)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> **Walking Star**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-S36-8)</sup> and the **Divine Skin**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> was a colossal [construct](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) of [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) origin.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup> It was constructed by Dwemeri [Tonal Architect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tonal_Architecture) Lord [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-POT-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWBaladas-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup> to be a new god, powered by the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Vivec MW-13)</sup> made to retake [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) from the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NARM-14)</sup> and possibly to make the Dwemer race [immortal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Immortality).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup> The Dwemer vanished from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) before the Brass God could be activated, though their [disappearance](Lore_Disappearance_of_the_Dwarves.md) may be directly related to this endeavor.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup>

The Numidium was, in practice, a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) of devastating power. A "thousand foot tall automaton, a [golem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem) or an atronach<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> of sorts", it was used by Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to complete his [conquest](Lore_Tiber_War.md) of Tamriel at the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), but was destroyed afterward.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DFMantella-15)</sup> The golem was rebuilt and reactivated centuries later, and became the center of the phenomenon known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), after which it was destroyed again.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup> [Temporal distortions](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) have accompanied each known use of the Numidium,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WWYDB-7)</sup> to the point that the Warp in the West is sometimes known as the "Second Numidian Effect".<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LOTE-16)</sup>

Some believe that the Numidium was intrinsically evil: a ranking [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) called it the "most profane" of the Dwemer's creations, a "walking horror", the "Antipodal-God-Thing that reigned on the darkest pole of the sacred Nirn-Sphere", and the "welded knot at the center of [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) that must be untied". The Apostles considered the Numidium the greatest threat to [Sotha Sil's](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) goals of bringing about the Nirn-Ensuing and the Anuic convergence of Anuvanna'si. Indeed, in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Clockwork City's](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) supervising Factotum, Aios, would name Numidium first among the existential threats to the City.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Aios-17)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ti S5-3)</sup>

A second Numidium, [Akulakhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akulakhan), was partially constructed in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the late Third Era by [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur). However, the destruction of Kagrenac's enchantments on the Heart of Lorkhan in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) prevented its activation.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Morrowind-18)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png/200px-LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Numidium_Wall.png) Numidium on Alduin's Wall Around the time of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup> the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) arch-mage [Anumaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril) created the [Staff of Towers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff_of_Towers), a powerful time-bending artifact composed of eight segments, each corresponding to one of the metaphysical [Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md) of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). One of these fragments, a masterfully-crafted length of metal affixed to a hunk of polished brass, corresponded to Walk-Brass, despite the fact that the Tower did not yet exist. It is not known how Anumaril knew of its future construction.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONAugur-5)</sup>

At some point by the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) was discovered beneath [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-KT-20)</sup> There are many differing stories about the series of events that followed, but most agree that the Dwemer High Craftlord [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup> created a set of [tools](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac%27s_Tools) imbued with the power to forge mythopoeic enchantments,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup> which made it possible for their bearers to tap the Heart's power for themselves.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-KT-20)</sup> Kagrenac believed he could use his tools to create a new [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods), Anumidium, which would allow the Dwemer to transcend the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWBaladas-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> Numidium was the vessel for this apotheosis.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DM-1)</sup>

According to the [legendary sermons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec) of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), Kagrenac summoned Numidium during the climax of the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) which caused great devastation to the Chimer armies and the land itself before finally being defeated by the combined might of Almsivi.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-S36-8)</sup>

Some among the Dwemer believed that tapping the Heart of Lorkhan posed unknown dangers and unjustifiable risks, but Kagrenac and his followers, such as [Bthuand Mzahnch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthuand_Mzahnch) refuted these ideas.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-EOT-21)</sup> It has been hypothesized that the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) may have led Kagrenac to carry out his experiments prematurely, and that the sudden disappearance of the Dwemer during the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NARM-14)</sup> was the result of one of these unknown risks. However, it is also speculated that their disappearance may have been the result of successfully granting the Dwemer eternal life, in some form.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-12)</sup> Regardless of the circumstances, the Dwemer vanished from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), leaving behind their creations. Kagrenac's tools fell into the possession of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), who used them to tap the Heart of Lorkhan and make themselves into living gods.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Morrowind-18)</sup>

Numidium was included in the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md), where it was predicted to ["reshape Time"](#Third_Era).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TBot D-22)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/LG-cardart-Halls_of_Colossus.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Halls_of_Colossus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Halls_of_Colossus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Halls_of_Colossus.jpg) The [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus) In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) alluded to Numidium needing to be rebuilt.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LLL-23)</sup>

By the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Tribunal had possession of Numidium, while [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) had conquered most of Tamriel and was turning his attention to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TAH-24)</sup> However, in [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882) [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) had returned and cut off the Tribunal's access to the Heart of Lorkhan, preventing them from restoring themselves with Kagrenac's tools.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DUP-25)</sup> Rather than fight in their weakened states, the Tribunes signed an [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) with the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md); as part of the treaty, the Empire received Numidium.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-OM-26)</sup>

Tiber Septim tasked Imperial Battlemage [Zurin Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zurin_Arctus) with the Numidium's repair.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TAH-24)</sup> There are rumors that the Emperor had the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus) constructed in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) for this purpose, leaving the area still uninhabitable as a result;<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> there is overwhelming evidence the Halls have existed since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESO-27)</sup> On the Emperor's orders, Arctus created the [Totem of Tiber Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Totem_of_Tiber_Septim), an artifact which allowed a bearer of royal lineage to control the Numidium. Since it would not function without the Heart of Lorkhan, he devised a replacement based on his research—a huge soul gem called the [Mantella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantella).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup> The gem required the life force, or "heart" of a powerful being, so Arctus used his own<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DFMantella-15)</sup> (though it is also claimed he used the soul of [Ysmir Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), an undead being who may have been a mortal avatar of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TAH-24)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-28)</sup>

In [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), the Numidium was activated near the city of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) in Elsweyr<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WWYDB-7)</sup> and proved very effective in bringing down the mighty [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), thus completing Tiber Septim's conquest of Tamriel.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TAH-24)</sup> The official story says that after defeating all his enemies, Tiber Septim began using Numidium to destroy the neutral royal families of Tamriel so that he could enthrone persons he knew to be loyal. Zurin Arctus disagreed with these actions, and attempted to reclaim his heart from within the Mantella. In the ensuing battle, both the Battlemage and the construct were destroyed; the heart they shared was blown into [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DFMantella-15)</sup> With the Mantella lost, Arctus couldn't truly die, and became an undead being known as the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup> The heretical version of events claims that Zurin Arctus had perished while trapping Wulfharth's soul, and soon after the conquest of the Summerset Isles an undead wizard (the Underking, but Wulfharth) appeared and destroyed the automaton.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TAH-24)</sup> The remains of Numidium were scattered throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/DF-misc-Numidium.jpg/200px-DF-misc-Numidium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Numidium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Numidium.jpg) Numidium reactivated In the centuries that followed, the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) were entrusted with the task of collecting the pieces of Numidium.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ES10A-29)</sup> During the reign of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), Numidium's reconstruction was nearly completed, save for the Totem of Tiber Septim and the Mantella. Around [3E 401](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_401), both were located; the Mad Witch [Nulfaga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nulfaga) divined the Mantella's resting place in Aetherius through her astral travels,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ES10A-29)</sup> and the Totem was rediscovered in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) region. The latter discovery nearly set off a civil war among the dozens of independent city-states in the region, beginning with the murder of King [Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md). Ultimately, an [agent of the Blades](Lore_Hero.md#The_Agent), sent by the Emperor, recovered the Totem. A bidding war ensued among the major powers of the region, and [somehow](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) seven of them ended up in simultaneous possession of the Totem. The agent then traveled to the [Mantellan Crux](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantellan_Crux) in Aetherius to recover the Mantella.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup>

As a result, in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417) each of the seven powers activated their own Numidium, and at least five Numiduma ravaged large areas of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). This event, known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) or the Miracle of Peace, greatly reshaped the political landscape of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay). It is believed the Underking reclaimed his heart from the Mantella, leading to Numidium's subsequent destruction.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WWYDB-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-9)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TWITW-30)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup>

A similar golem known as [Akulakhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akulakhan), or the Second Numidium, was built by [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), based on Kagrenac's designs for Anumidium and powered by the Heart of Lorkhan. It was destroyed by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Morrowind-18)</sup>
