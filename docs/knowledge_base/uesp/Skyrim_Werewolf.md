# Werewolf


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/SR-creature-Werewolf_02.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Werewolf_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Werewolf_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Werewolf_02.jpg) Werewolf **Werewolves** are monstrous, wolf-like creatures that roam Skyrim. They are taller and broader than men and mer, and are capable of moving on two limbs or sprinting on all four. They have a weakness to [silver](Skyrim_Silver.md) weapons, but are incredibly strong, very fast, and are immune to [diseases](Skyrim_Disease.md) and [paralysis](Skyrim_Paralysis.md) effects. You can become a werewolf through a ritual during [The Companions](Skyrim_The_Companions.md) questline.

In Skyrim, several named [NPCs](Skyrim_NPCs.md) are werewolves. The [Silver Hand](Skyrim_Silver_Hand.md) faction is known to hunt them and werewolves are often found in their encampments locked in cells (as in [Gallows Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gallows_Rock) and [Driftshade Refuge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Driftshade_Refuge)); you can recognize a Silver Hand base by the presence of werewolf heads on pikes. Many werewolves in Skyrim are seen in both human and werewolf form, such as the members of The Companions or the Frostmoon Pack on Solstheim in [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md), while werewolves encountered in the wild are always seen in their beast form, with the exception of two [random encounters](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Hostile_Encounters). Hostile werewolves can only be encountered in the wild through the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md).

Werewolves in Skyrim are strongly tied to the Daedric Lord [Hircine](Skyrim_Hircine.md); in fact, the members of [The Circle](Skyrim_The_Circle.md) were turned into werewolves by the [Glenmoril witches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glenmoril_Witch), who serve Hircine. Those with the beast blood are prone to violence, whether accidental or intentional, as they live with a constant bloodlust which drives them to kill and devour their enemies. Werewolves are widely feared and hated in Skyrim as in other provinces, and will be attacked on sight if spotted in beast form. They possess deadly armor-piercing claws and incredible strength, and are capable of easily knocking most creatures they encounter to the ground.

### Werewolves
Werewolves carry no weapons and wear no armor, but there is a 50% chance they will be wearing a pair of [ragged trousers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ragged_Trousers#0008f19a). If Dawnguard is installed, werewolves will drop a [wolf pelt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf_Pelt) and [human flesh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Human_Flesh), and have a small chance of dropping a random assortment of [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md), [rings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ring), and a few pieces of [gold](Skyrim_Gold.md) when killed.

The "Werewolf Claws" spell used by the werewolves listed below is unique to non-player werewolves. It is a constant-effect spell that gives werewolves a [Fortify Unarmed Damage](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) bonus, which becomes increasingly powerful for higher-level werewolves.

### Werewolf Bosses
These boss-level werewolves are equipped with level-dependent armor ([banded iron armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Banded_Iron_Armor), [steel armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Armor), [steel plate armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate_Armor), [Orcish armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Armor), or [ebony armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Armor)). Werewolf bosses of level 23 and higher also carry a leveled two-handed weapon:

- **Battleaxe:** [iron battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Battleaxe), [steel battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Battleaxe), [Orcish battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Battleaxe), [Dwarven battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Battleaxe), [elven battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Battleaxe), [glass battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Battleaxe), or [ebony battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Battleaxe)
- **Greatsword:** [iron greatsword](Skyrim_Iron_Greatsword.md), [steel greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Greatsword), [Orcish greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Greatsword), [Dwarven greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Greatsword), [elven greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Greatsword), [glass greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Greatsword), or [ebony greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Greatsword)
- **Warhammer:** [iron warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Warhammer), [steel warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Warhammer), [Orcish warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Warhammer), [Dwarven warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Warhammer), [elven warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Warhammer), [glass warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Warhammer), or [ebony warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Warhammer)

If Dawnguard is installed, werewolves carry no weapons and the leveled armor is replaced with the same outfit as their generic counterparts (a 50% chance of a pair of ragged trousers). They will also drop the same items as generic werewolves (Dawnguard only).

## Notes
- Dead werewolves cannot be raised with [Dead Thrall](Skyrim_Dead_Thrall.md), but can be raised multiple times with [the Ritual Stone](Skyrim_The_Ritual_Stone.md).
- The [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) add-on adds [werebears](Skyrim_Werebear.md), a new type of lycanthrope. However, players cannot transform into these creatures.

## Bugs
- Werewolf kill moves may occasionally not finish animating properly. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.6, fixes this bug.
- Werewolves entering bleedout mode may simply stand in place and not move. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.9, addresses this issue. This was due to an idle animation oversight that left the proper event out.
