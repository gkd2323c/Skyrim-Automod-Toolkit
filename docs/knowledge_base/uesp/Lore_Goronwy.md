*Wood Elves and Reachfolk are both quite skilled in the [healing arts](Lore_Restoration.md). Imagine what we could learn from each other!*
—Ambassador Goronwy<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AG-1)</sup> Goronwy was a [Reachman](Lore_Reachfolk.md) diplomat and a [mage](Lore_Magic.md) in the service of Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). He served as Caddach's ambassador to the city of Vashabar in the region of Dawnwood, a [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) -controlled territory in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AG-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/ON-render-Ard_Caddach_02.jpg/200px-ON-render-Ard_Caddach_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Ard_Caddach_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Ard_Caddach_02.jpg) Ard Caddach of Markarth Little is known about Goronwy's background, beyond the fact that he hailed from [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and was a subject of Ard Caddach.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AG-1)</sup>

Although diplomatic missions were occasionally sent to the Reach during the tenure of Caddach,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bralthahawn-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MTC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-4)</sup> the number of Reachfolk ambassadors abroad was relatively few. Goronwy was one such individual. Serving as one of Caddach's few ambassadors was considered a true honor. Soon after the foundation of the independent Bosmeri nation in the region of Dawnwood by King [Nantharion](Lore_Nantharion.md), Caddach seized the opportunity to send an ambassador to the fledgling kingdom. Goronwy was fortunate to secure the post.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AG-1)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Goronwy left the Reach and traveled to the city of Vashabar to assume his post as ambassador. As ambassador, Goronwy advocated for cooperation between the Bosmer and Reachmen, emphasizing the mutual benefits they could gain, particularly in the area of healing magic. A practitioner himself, he intended to showcase Reachfolk healing arts during the cultural exchange. He was also keen on sharing [culinary](Lore_Cuisine.md) traditions, such as [traditional Reachfolk coney stew recipes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk_Cuisine). He also learned about some local dishes, although he needed to adjust to [Bosmeri cuisine](Lore_Bosmer_Cuisine.md). Goronwy enjoyed his stay in Dawnwood and was willing to engage with anyone passing through the area.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AG-1)</sup> He was warmly accepted by some of the Bosmer population, spreading information about the Reach and attracting their interest.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Marealdain-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Liinia-6)</sup>

After the death of King Nantharion in the same year, his mission remained unaffected.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AG-1)</sup> He remained in the city after the King's death and Greenspeaker Darolith's ascension to the role of local leader.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AG-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GD-7)</sup> His presence was considered a part of the local West Weald culture.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WAW-8)</sup>

## Notes
- Around the same time as Goronwy was serving as ambassador, a Reachfolk diplomat in the nearby city of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) requested that Count [Calantius](Lore_Calantius_of_Skingrad.md) return items of cultural significance to the Reach, which were left in the region following the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md). The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) in the area was tasked with retrieving these artifacts, and they hired mercenaries for the job. Ultimately, the trinkets were returned, although it is unclear whether Goronwy or another Reachman diplomat was responsible for issuing this request.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CS-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFTR-10)</sup>
