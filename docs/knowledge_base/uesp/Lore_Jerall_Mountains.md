[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-place-Jerall_Mountains.jpg/200px-ON-place-Jerall_Mountains.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jerall_Mountains.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jerall_Mountains.jpg) The Jerall Mountains seen from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/OB-map-Jerall_Mountains.jpg/200px-OB-map-Jerall_Mountains.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Jerall_Mountains.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Jerall_Mountains.jpg) Map of the Jerall Mountains in Cyrodiil The Jerall Mountains are a mountain range on the border between [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They meet the [Velothi](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) and [Valus Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valus_Mountains) at the [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) border, and the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains) at the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) border. The Jeralls are rugged, steep, and inhospitable, with only a few traversable passes. Of particular note is the [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), which was the site of the abrupt end to the [First Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), after which its location was lost, only being rediscovered in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOTD-1)</sup>

## Cyrodiil
The largest town in the Cyrodilic part of the Jerall Mountains is [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), which is connected to the rest of Cyrodiil via the [Silver Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silver_Road) and [Orange Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orange_Road). The forests of the High Jeralls provide wood for the region.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> [Dive Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dive_Rock) and [Gnoll Mountain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnoll_Mountain) are among the few named peaks in the region. Other settlements include [Applewatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Applewatch), [Cloud Ruler Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloud_Ruler_Temple), [Frostcrag Spire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostcrag_Spire), [Lord Rugdumph's Estate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lord_Rugdumph%27s_Estate), and the Temple of the Ancestor Moth. Shrines to [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura), [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) and [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) are located high up in the mountains, away from scrutinizing eyes.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OB-3)</sup>

## Skyrim
The mountains border the [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) hold of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) to the north and [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) to the northeast. Its harsh, cold climate has made the mountains nearly inhospitable, with few if any inhabitants living there and no major settlements throughout history. Some points of interest include [Fort Neugrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Neugrad), the dragon shrines of Ancient's Ascent and Lost Tongue Overlook, the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruins of Rkund, or the large [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruin of [Arcwind Point](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arcwind_Point). In the easternmost part, where the mountain range meets [Velothi](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) and [Valus Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valus_Mountains), the volcanic peak of [Trolhetta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trolhetta) can be found.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ON-5)</sup>

Here in the mountains, the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) built strongholds to defend their southern frontier. One of these bastions was Old Fort, an example of the Nords' affinity for wood and timber construction.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1E-6)</sup> It was still standing by the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TEG-7)</sup>

During the conquests of the First Nordic Empire, the Jeralls were considered too well fortified by the Ayleids, and the potential gain too poor to be worth invading. This was something that irked the southern Nordic Kings, as Nords conquered both east and west. Things changed when the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) occurred, and the Nords swiftly allied with them. The Ayleid Empire was soon overthrown, and in exchange, the Nords were granted several territories in the Niben Basin.<sup>[*verification needed — see [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:talk_Jerall_Mountains)*]</sup>

During the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) the Jeralls provided a natural barrier that impeded Imperial reinforcement to Skyrim, especially once an avalanche cut off Pale Pass. Combined with the Empire's need to keep troops on the border with the Aldmeri Dominion, the Legion was forced to rely excessively on manpower reserves within Skyrim to fight the Stormcloaks.

## Notes
- According to [Esbern](Skyrim_Esbern.md), the end of Frostfall is nearly the beginning of winter here.
