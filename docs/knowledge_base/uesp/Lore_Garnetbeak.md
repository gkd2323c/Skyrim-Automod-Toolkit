Garnetbeaks are an extinct species of [bird](Lore_Birds.md). During the reign of [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md) in the late years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the **Topal Garnetbeak**, a variety of garnetbeak native to [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md), was a common dish on noble tables in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and thus a part of [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [cuisine](Lore_Imperial_Cuisine.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOSC-1)</sup> Their name was partially derived from the [garnet](Lore_Garnet.md) [gemstone](Lore_Crystals.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Garnet-2)</sup>

Garnetbeaks were served during the infamous Feast of [Saint](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) [Coellicia](Lore_Coellicia.md). These birds were often drowned in fortified wine, plucked just before serving, and eaten whole by diners, who typically draped cloths over their heads. While this was said to trap the vapors of the wine, it also helped contain the viscera and juices that would often violently eject from the bird as it was consumed. This practice contributed to the rapid decline in garnetbeak numbers, and by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the species was long extinct.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOSC-1)</sup>

## Notes
- Topal Garnetbeak bears many similarities to the real-life [ortolan bunting](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ortolan_bunting). Just as described in *[The Feast of Saint Coellicia](Lore_The_Feast_of_Saint_Coellicia.md)*, these birds were drowned in wine, roasted, plucked, and consumed whole, and diners would traditionally wear a napkin over their heads while eating them.
