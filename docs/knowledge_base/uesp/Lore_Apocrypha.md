Apocrypha is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Knowledge and [Fate](Lore_Fate.md). The geography of the realm varies, with some areas being open with fields containing odd flora, other areas are an endless library consisting of untitled books with black covers, where all forbidden knowledge can be found, and the crackling towers of learning mingle with archways of despair and confusion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup> The realm is haunted by the ghosts of mortals forever searching for knowledge.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDOB-3)</sup> Stacks of books form mazes and spiraling pillars that can reach as high as the illuminating green sky. Intricate ruins and monstrous carvings can be found among the stacks. The plane is covered in a sea of ink and roiling tentacles, while Hermaeus Mora is omnipresent over all, taking glee in tormenting mortals from his realm.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup> Some areas of the realm are consumed by darkness which can kill any who enter it. Discarded pages and floating tomes fill the fetid air,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DB-4)</sup> as if pulled by invisible string.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup> Storms of notorious strength purportedly rage over Apocrypha.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pieron-5)</sup>

Apart from magical teleportation, Apocrypha is most easily entered by mortals through the reading of [Black Books](Lore_Black_Books.md), tomes of forbidden knowledge displaced from time and scattered across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). An apparition of the reader's body remains in [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), tethering their life force. Most mortals who gain access to the secrets within Apocrypha are driven insane by unending revelations. Such was the fate of the mage [Morian Zenas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morian_Zenas), who famously disappeared in Apocrypha during his voyages through Oblivion.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDOB-3)</sup> However, those who successfully journey through Apocrypha are known to discover powerful knowledge. The laws of the mortal world hold no power in this realm.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup>

### Endless Library
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-render-Apocrypha.jpg/200px-ON-render-Apocrypha.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Apocrypha.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Apocrypha.jpg) The halls of Apocrypha Hermaeus Mora's infinite library of forbidden knowledge is described as a turbulent mire, glistening against the writhing brightness of a green sky, where every tome reveals secrets damning and inhumane, catalogued in chaos. Stacks of black-covered books adorn every edifice, and the dark halls are constantly elongating and retracting. Scryes are used to navigate these structures by elongating steps, opening gates, and performing other actions. Magicka and stamina fonts are found throughout the area. It is perched atop the black churning ocean of Apocrypha, which is described as an infinite sea of black ooze and thrashing tentacles. Lurker-Summoning Chambers are circular tentacle pools filled with black ooze that lurkers arise from.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup> [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) found that the seemingly infinite books about mortal sex made decent structural support in his Endless Library.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ETHMR-6)</sup>

### Servants of the Great Eye
Apocrypha is inhabited by grotesque Daedric creatures malformed by Mora, who are his frightening servants and stalk the constantly elongating and retracting halls.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup> Hermaeus Mora frequently employs Watchers as messengers and recorders, tasking them with obscure clerical duties in the hidden bureaucracy that quietly administers Apocrypha.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Do A-7)</sup> Alongside bureaucracy, Watchlings and Watchers are responsible for observing all things and cataloguing all knowledge, so that they may act as his eyes.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Many Watchers simply organize books or valuables.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

[Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md) guard forbidden knowledge, banishing foes with sound attacks. [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md) dwell within the inky tide, arising from the depths to attack invaders. Disembodied tentacles hide in the waters and hang from the sky, whipping at any who pass.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DB-4)</sup> [Wisp-Lanterns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wisp-Lantern) float through the fetid air of the realm, acting as sources of light.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DB-4)</sup>

### Mortals
*By Seventh Umbra and the Dismal Name, I bid thee open, Cephaliarch's fane.*
—An incantation used to enter Apocrypha<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Mora resides "everywhere and nowhere" but takes glee in tormenting mortals from his realm in Oblivion. Apocrypha is "incomprehensible" to mortals who have been lured into its unseen and unspeakable depths. It is said that this is partially because laws of the mortal world hold no power in Apocrypha.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup> The realm is haunted by the ghosts of mortals forever searching for knowledge.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDOB-3)</sup>

[Cipher's Midden](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cipher%27s_Midden) is a mortal settlement located in the center of Apocrypha. It is primarily inhabited by the Ciphers of the Eye, mortals scholars who were brought to Apocrypha by Hermaeus Mora and exist there with his blessing. No cipher has authority over another; the only law in Cipher's Midden is that of Hermaeus Mora, and he takes no interest in mortal dealings. Instead, the ciphers choose to cooperate on custom.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

The [Hushed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hushed) are the remnants of mortals who have been consumed by the Daedric knowledge of Apocrypha.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TDot H-12)</sup> Most Hushed ignore mortals who encounter them. Hermaeus Mora uses these creatures to guard locations he wishes to keep private,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Do A-7)</sup> such as the Infinite Panopticon, where they wander the halls as guardians of sorts.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OIP-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-WIP-14)</sup> It is advised to not interfere with the Hushed as they attend their duties, and that they may turn on unsuspecting mortals with no warning at all.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Do A-7)</sup>

### Fossils
The gigantic fossilized remains of ancient sea creatures, not known on Nirn, dot Apocrypha. Faithful of Mora believe that these fossils are remnants of another place or realm, the origin point of the Daedric Prince of Knowledge.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Fossil-15)</sup> Antediluvian hunting horns carved from these fossils have been found, engraved with depictions of Mora driving beasts before him.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

Some fossils, massive in size and varied in form, have been examined by the [Antiquarian Circle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Antiquarian_Circle), providing theorized descriptions for the creatures they belonged to. These include a tentacled predator that scooped prey into a mouth situated at the center of its body,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Fossil-15)</sup>, a number of larval creatures covered in segments of chitin and boasting a horned tail for defense,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Fossil Bones-17)</sup> a worm-like creature with massive mandibles,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Fossil Worm-18)</sup> a long worm-like creature with chitin placed on the inside of its body akin to a fungal cell wall,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Fossil Arch-19)</sup> a [tentacled beast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wall_Beast) that stuck to surfaces,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Fossil Wall-20)</sup> giant slug-like creatures,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Fossil Slug-21)</sup> and giant [nautilus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nautilus) fossils, theorized to be the origin of nautili in Nirn, who are speculated to have originated as severed appendages that gained sentience.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Fossil Nautilus-22)</sup>

## History
The [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md) sought the knowledge of Apocrypha in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), and used what he learned of the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to revolt against his [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) masters. Although he slew many, their retaliation was swift and furious, and Miraak's temple was destroyed. He retreated to Apocrypha with his dragon servants, where he dwelled for many eras. <sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DB-4)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Miraak had in motion a plan to harness the power of the [All-Maker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:All-Maker) Stones of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) to enable his return to Tamriel. Seeing his corrupting influence on the lands and people of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) entered Apocrypha through the reading of Black Books, and confronted Miraak. This came at the cost of making a deal with Hermaeus Mora, the traditions and oral history of the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) tribe of Solstheim, that Hermaeus Mora took by killing Storn Crag-Strider, the shaman of the Skaal. The two Dragonborn battled, and, when Miraak was close to defeat, he was killed by Mora, who then accepted the Last Dragonborn as his new champion.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

## Sub-Realms

### Infinite Archive
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/ON-place-Destozuno%27s_Library.jpg/200px-ON-place-Destozuno%27s_Library.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Destozuno%27s_Library.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Destozuno%27s_Library.jpg) The Library tended by Destozuno The [Infinite Archive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Archive) is a border realm of Apocrypha, an adjunct plane or subrealm which lies within the greater fabric of Apocrypha, created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). It is an endless library consisting primarily of historical accounts, written records of events that were or could yet be. Contemplating the scope of the Archive's function was known to leave mortals, even its caretaker Master Malkhest, in a daze.

### Infinite Panopticon
The [Infinite Panopticon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Panopticon) is a pocket realm inside [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s realm of Apocrypha. It is described as an endless library of rooms and passages inside a limitless pocket [dimension](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) situated outside the time and space of Apocrypha itself, and is said to be one of the most secure repositories of knowledge within Apocrypha. The Panopticon is said to hold infinite secrets, including some of Mora's most closely guarded ones, as the information and secrets inside are considered too precious to be known by anyone but Hermaeus Mora himself.

In order to protect the information stored within, the entrance to the Panopticon constantly changes, never appearing in the same place twice. The interior of the realm also changes at random; nothing within the extradimensional space conforms to reality as [mortals](Lore_Souls.md) understand it. The defenses that protect the secrets in the Panopticon are said to include shifting chambers and corridors as well as an army of [Hushed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hushed), [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md), [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md) and other [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) guardians, which are said to "defy description", being too powerful and obscure to describe accurately.

## Notes
- [Apocrypha](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/apocrypha) is an English word meaning "something, as a writing, that is of doubtful authorship or authority". It derives from the medieval Latin adjective [apocryphus](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/apocryphus) meaning "secret, or non-canonical", which is in turn derived from the Greek adjective [ἀπόκρυφος](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/%E1%BC%80%CF%80%CF%8C%CE%BA%CF%81%CF%85%CF%86%CE%BF%CF%82) ('apokryphos'), meaning "obscure".
- Apocrypha alludes to both [Biblical apocrypha](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biblical_apocrypha) and generally vague [non-canon religious scripture](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apocrypha).
- Both [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) and [Word Walls](Lore_Word_Walls.md) could be found within the realm.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESApoc-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Apocrypha WD-25)</sup>
