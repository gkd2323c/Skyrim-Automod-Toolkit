[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/OB-place-Borderwatch.jpg/200px-OB-place-Borderwatch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Borderwatch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Borderwatch.jpg) Borderwatch village circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Borderwatch (also spelled **Border Watch**) is a settlement in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) along [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Green Road](Lore_Green_Road.md) between [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) and [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md), close to the [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) border.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLocation-1)</sup>

## History
The settlement was extant prior to [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), with the fort having been built over an [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) by an unnamed architect. Her tomb in the crypts marked the entrance to the ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTeldundindo-2)</sup> There were gatehouses on the [Green Road](Lore_Green_Road.md) into the [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md) and on the road into Anequina.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLocation-1)</sup> The [Ivory Brigade](Lore_Ivory_Brigade.md) garrisoned the fort and kept watch for possible incursions into Blackwood from the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLoading-3)</sup> However, they were driven out by a mercenary outfit called the Painted Eye who posed as revolutionaries, taking civilian hostages and drawing up a list of unrealistic political demands.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONDot PE-4)</sup> This dissuaded the Ivory Brigade from counterattacking and the fort's commanding officer, Commander Axius, asked [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to infiltrate the fort.

With the help of two [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) members, the Vestige was able to rescue the hostages and discovered the Painted Eye were trying to acquire and use the same powerful [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) artifact the Thieves Guild were hired to recover.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONKeeps-5)</sup> The ritual was successfully stopped and the Painted Eye leader was killed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONKeeps-5)</sup> The Ivory Brigade planned to retake the fort later and the broken relic was sold to an undisclosed [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) buyer.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONAxius-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSeeks-7)</sup> Axius may have later sent soldiers to help defend [Fort Redmane](Lore_Fort_Redmane.md) against the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONPyre-8)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), no trace of the fort remained and the village was occupied by a superstitious community of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) ministered by their shaman Ri'Bassa. The villagers believed in the K'Sharra Prophecy, a prediction made by their forefathers that foretold the end of the world. There were three parts to the prophecy: the Plague of Vermin, the Plague of Famine, and the Plague of Fear.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBRi.27Bassa-9)</sup> Finding the village to be dull, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) tasked the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) with carrying out the first two prophecies by attracting vermin and killing all the livestock. Sheogorath then unleashed a rain of burning dogs to fulfill the final prophecy, which sent the villagers into a frenzied panic.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBSheogorath-10)</sup> The rest of Cyrodiil looked upon the strange happenings in Borderwatch with bemusement.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBRo BD-11)</sup>
