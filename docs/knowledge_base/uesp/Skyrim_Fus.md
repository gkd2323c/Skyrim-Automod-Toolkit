**Unrelenting Force** is a [shout](Skyrim_Shout.md) that pushes objects and entities. Weak versions of the shout stagger enemies and slightly push objects, while the strongest version will throw objects a great distance and turn enemies into a ragdoll, briefly rendering them helpless.

When you first meet the [Greybeards](Skyrim_Greybeards.md) they will insist that you identify yourself as Dragonborn by your use of this shout on [Arngeir](Skyrim_Arngeir.md).

## Meditation
Meditating on the word *Fus* with [Paarthurnax](Skyrim_Paarthurnax_(dragon).md) grants you the ability [Force Without Effort](Skyrim_Force_Without_Effort.md). This ability causes enemies to be staggered 25% more, while you are recoiled 25% less, applying to both Unrelenting Force as well as power attacks and any other form of stagger.

## Dragonborn Force<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
After completing the [quest](Skyrim_The_Gardener_of_Men.md) inside the Black Book *[Epistolary Acumen](Skyrim_Black_Book__Epistolary_Acumen_(book).md)* there are three abilities to choose from. One of them, [Dragonborn Force](Skyrim_Dragonborn_Force.md), increases the damage output of Unrelenting Force, and there is a chance of disintegrating enemies when using all three words if your foe's health is low enough. If the damage taken from the shout kills the enemy, their body is replaced by an ash pile. Combined with the aforementioned ability Force without Effort, this makes Unrelenting Force one of the most devastating shouts in-game, especially against weak enemies that are standing on cliffs, mountains or other high areas where they will sustain fall damage, or it is also incredibly useful within dungeons.

## Possible Uses
- Unrelenting Force can be useful when fighting an enemy who can be pushed off a high location: [falling](Skyrim_Combat.md#Falling_Damage) damages and kills NPCs easier than it does you.
- Use this shout at low power to momentarily stun a foe, opening up their defenses for an attack.
- Use the shout to open [draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md) tombs that lie on the ground in dungeons (though not those propped against walls).
- Use it to detonate rune traps and tripwires from a safe distance.
- If you use the shout against fish in water, they will die and float on the water, allowing you to collect them. It won't work on [Slaughterfish](Skyrim_Slaughterfish.md), though. - You can use it on salmon as a way to collect [salmon meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Meat) and [Salmon Roe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Roe)<sup>[HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md)</sup>.
- The shout will also kill insects in the air, dropping their remains to the ground where they can be collected for use in alchemy.

## Notes
- Fall damage is not applied to the victim until a few seconds after the target has come to a complete stop, right before they start getting back up. As such, you may find that an opponent may appear to spontaneously die with no apparent explanation after surviving a fall.
- Even with all three words of this shout and the augmentations of Dragonborn Force and Force Without Effort, the shout can, at most, stagger a [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md). It cannot put a dragon into a "ragdoll" state. This same immunity also exists for [Dwarven automatons](Skyrim_Dwarven_Automatons.md) (barring Dwarven Spiders, which are not immune), [mammoths](Skyrim_Mammoth.md), and other enemies in Skyrim. Other creatures and NPCs which cannot be ragdolled using this shout include: [Arngeir](Skyrim_Arngeir.md), [ash guardians](Skyrim_Ash_Guardian.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, [Borri](Skyrim_Borri.md), [dragon priests](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md), [Einarth](Skyrim_Einarth.md), [Felldir the Old](Skyrim_Felldir_the_Old.md), [flame atronachs](Skyrim_Flame_Atronach.md), [frost atronachs](Skyrim_Frost_Atronach.md), [Gormlaith Golden-Hilt](Skyrim_Gormlaith_Golden-Hilt.md), [Hakon One-Eye](Skyrim_Hakon_One-Eye.md), [Karstaag](Skyrim_Karstaag.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, [lurkers](Skyrim_Lurker.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, [Miraak](Skyrim_Miraak_(person).md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, [mounted rieklings](Skyrim_Riekling.md#Mounted)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, [seekers](Skyrim_Seeker.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, [storm atronachs](Skyrim_Storm_Atronach.md), [Tsun](Skyrim_Tsun.md), and [Wulfgar](Skyrim_Wulfgar.md). - Those immune sometimes include creatures that fall apart when dying and entering a ragdoll state or are plot-critical NPCs located around an area with a large drop nearby.
- This is the most commonly used shout by draugr, often making you ragdoll, which may make it appear as though you have died. To distinguish this ragdoll from real death, look for the pulsing red hue on your screen; if it is not there, you are still alive. If it is there, either you are close to death or have already died. For death, the game will abruptly cut music and replace it with a somber tone. It is also possible to jump during the animation of getting back up, even while your body is still in the prone position where it first landed.
- Use caution when attacking enemies that use this shout while you are [riding a dragon](Skyrim_Dragon_Riding.md). It can knock you off your dragon and kill through falling damage.
- The effects of this shout are governed by the [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) skill. As such, among other things, [Ward](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ward) spells (including the ward created by blocking with [Spellbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spellbreaker)) completely negate the effects of draugr's Unrelenting Force shout on you, preventing the ragdoll effect.
- Draugr use two different types of this shout, though the words are still the same (easily seen when the general subtitles are turned on). Draugr from the Scourge to the lowest-leveled Deathlord use the version which only staggers, while the highest-level Deathlords and Death Overlords use the version which can knock you and allies off their feet. The two different versions also have different sound effects: the one that only staggers has a "wind" sound effect, while the one which knocks down has one which is a much louder "thunder" sound effect.
- This shout has a very long range which is not shown with the range of the visual effect of the shout itself (the blue cone-like effect). You can easily effect enemies 200 to 300 feet away even though it seems by the visual effect it won't go that far, though it takes two or three seconds to reach NPCs and creatures at that distance.
- [Alduin](Skyrim_Alduin.md) can be heard using this shout at the beginning of the game, when your character is about to be executed. If subtitles are turned on, he can be seen saying the last 2 words, *"Ro...Dah!"*

## Bugs
- Sometimes when using the shout, it may fail to work. - This is often caused by the game believing that you're not on solid ground or are otherwise occupied. Casting a spell, jumping, or using a shield bash can often fix this.
