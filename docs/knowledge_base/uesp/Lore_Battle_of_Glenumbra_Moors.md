# Battle of Glenumbra Moors

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg/200px-ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg) [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), the site of the battle, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The Battle of Glenumbra Moors was a battle fought between the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) and [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) elves of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LSS-2)</sup> The events leading up to the battle were set in motion in [1E 478](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_478), when King [Rislav Larich](Lore_Rislav_Larich.md) of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) soundly defeated Emperor [Gorieus](Lore_Gorieus.md) of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), subsequently inspiring [Ryain Direnni](Lore_Ryain_Direnni.md) to outlaw the Alessian Doctrines in High Rock and harass adjacent Imperial-held lands.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RTR-3)</sup> The Alessians, who had been pushing their empire westward toward High Rock,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3C-4)</sup> were defeated by [Aiden Direnni](Lore_Aiden_Direnni.md) in a series of battles in [1E 480](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_480),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFL-5)</sup> but apparently continued their advance undeterred. By 1E 482,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-1)</sup> a vast Alessian army confronted a greatly outnumbered force of Direnni defenders to decide the ultimate fate of High Rock.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-O-6)</sup>

Few details of the battle itself are known, but the Direnni carried the day with the help of the magic of the enchantress [Raven Direnni](Lore_Raven_Direnni.md)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DRD-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AHOD-8)</sup> and the military prowess of [Aiden Direnni](Lore_Aiden_Direnni.md), who commanded his clan's forces.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-O-6)</sup> King [Laloriaran Dynar](Lore_Laloriaran_Dynar.md) of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md), the so-called Last King of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) and a skilled tactician in his own right, also aided the Direnni,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LSS-2)</sup> as well as a much more unlikely ally: the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md). The [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) chief provided his assistance in spite of his sharing the Alessians' xenophobia, as several years before, the Alessian Empire had humiliated the Nords in the [Battle of Sungard](Lore_Battle_of_Sungard.md) and slain Hoag's predecessor, [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RTR-3)</sup> Hoag perished in the fighting.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FSOKW-9)</sup> The artifact [Chrysamere](Lore_Chrysamere.md) is also said to have played a minor yet important role in the conflict.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LAIA-10)</sup>

On the Alessian side there was a General known as [Faolchu the Changeling](Lore_Faolchu.md), who was actually a [werewolf lord](Lore_Lycanthropy.md#Werewolf_Lord). He had a reputation for being nigh invulnerable in battle, earning him nicknames such as the "Invincible General".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOCJ-11)</sup> However, he was slain by a nameless member of the Direnni.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOLD-12)</sup>

The Battle of Glenumbra Moors ended in a decisive victory for the Direnni,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-1)</sup> with Ryain, Aiden, and Raven Direnni hailed among their clan as heroes who brought an end to the Alessians' tyranny,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-O-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DRD-7)</sup> but it is remembered for its long-term consequences. The Alessian Order was expelled from High Rock and lost all ambition of conquering the region; indeed, so crushing was the loss that it was often considered a leading cause of the Order's decline and eventual dissolution, and ranked among the Order's greatest follies.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1High-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SATD-14)</sup> The Direnni were weakened to such an extent by the battle, however, that they were mostly driven from the province by the emerging [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobility by [1E 500](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_500). Pockets of High Rock remained under elven rule long afterward, but the Direnni Hegemony was irreparably broken.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1High-13)</sup>

## Notes
- The exact date of the battle is unclear. *[The Last King of the Ayleids](Lore_The_Last_King_of_the_Ayleids.md)* gives the date as 1E 482. *[Five Songs of King Wulfharth](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md)* notes that King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) took the throne following Hoag Merkiller's death at the battle, and a [stone etching](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-misc-Wulfharth_Plaque.png) in [*TES V: Skyrim*](Skyrim_Skyrim.md) says Wulfharth's reign began in 1E 480. If the Battle of Glenumbra Moors did occur in 1E 480, then it might have a more direct relationship with the series of battles Aiden Direnni fought that year; Glenumbra Moors could have been either a more prolonged conflict than implied, or the most decisive clash out of several that occurred between the Direnni and Alessians.
