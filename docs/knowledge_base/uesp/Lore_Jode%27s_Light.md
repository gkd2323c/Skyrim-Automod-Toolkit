[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Jode's Light (*Ja'zennji Siir* in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md))<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CTTF-1)</sup> is a ruined temple located on the borderlands of [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md), in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). Named after the larger moon, [Jode](Lore_Jode.md), the temple was founded as a sanctum and haven for every monk and acolyte, no matter their [furstock](Lore_Furstock.md) or background, making it a place where the greatest philosophical minds of ancient Elsweyr gathered.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad Jode-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FOTD-3)</sup>

## History
Jode's Light was founded sometime in the mid- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by the Moon-Bishop, Thizzrini<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad Jode-2)</sup> during the sixteen kingdoms' period.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FOTD-3)</sup> It also dates back to the days of the oldest [Manes](Lore_Mane.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CTTF-1)</sup> As a node to the ancient [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) queen, [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](Lore_Anequina_Sharp-Tongue.md), who basked in Jode's moonlight.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONSatarri-4)</sup> The temple kept one of the triangular pieces that could decipher the [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md)'s map in their Full Moons Chamber.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONDemon-5)</sup> In the [fable](Lore_Fable_of_the_Dragon.md) of Mizbi the Bard and her [Dragon](Lore_The_Dragon.md), the two came to Jode's Light to ask the moon monks a riddle. "What is it that can be broken with only a single word?" The monks struggled to find the answer and begged Mizbi for it. She remained quiet and held them in suspense until she shouted, "Silence"! She then left atop of her dragon to Do'Krin Monastery to the south.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FOTD-3)</sup> The temple was eventually abandoned when the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) created countless casualties in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad Jode-2)</sup>

Sometime later, the temple was re-populated and became active but once again fell to ruin when the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) invaded [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). The ruins became infested by [thunderbugs](Lore_Thunderbug.md), [giant bats](Lore_Giant_Bat.md), the [Rotblood Tribe](Lore_Rotblood_Tribe.md) of [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) and was rumored to also have been haunted. In that same year, a Khajiiti local from S'ren-ja, Yenadar traveled to Jode's Light in the hopes of driving out the monsters<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KLJ-6)</sup> and meditating in the two moon's light in [Second Seed](Lore_Second_Seed.md). In two different accounts from his writings, he claims that the ruins are intuned with his ancestor spirits and serene atmosphere,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-YJ-7)</sup> and in another, he felt a dark presence come from the decrepit walls as early as his arrival. A soldier arrived at the ruins seeking the same solace as Yenadar but succumbed to the [Bent Dance](Lore_Bent_Dance.md) and became a [Dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KLJ-6)</sup> Eventually, Yenadar became possessed by the same dark magic and also turned into one.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-YJ-7)</sup>

After Yenadar's transformation, [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) delved into the ruins and defeated the corrupted monk. They came across his journal and returned it to his sister, Kala in S'ren-ja.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TOF-8)</sup> That same year, the Vestige and [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) traveled to Jode's Light to find the final piece needed to decipher the Wrathstone's map and uncover the "Demon Weapon". After solving the puzzle, the two left for their camp in [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md) and deduced the location to the Demon Weapon as the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONDemon-5)</sup>
