[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/OB-place-Culotte.jpg/200px-OB-place-Culotte.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Culotte.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Culotte.jpg) Culotte circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c9/ON-place-Culotte.jpg/200px-ON-place-Culotte.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Culotte.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Culotte.jpg) Culotte circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Culotte is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin located along the banks of the [Upper Niben](Lore_Upper_Niben.md), in the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

During the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), Culotte became home to one of the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md), Ayleid statues originally held in the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) which were dispersed to cities across Cyrodiil for safekeeping.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-1)</sup> Culotte subsequently fell into ruin after the Ayleids' downfall.

The ruins of the settlement were once known to attract undead of all kinds. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the nearby settlement of [Cropsford](Lore_Cropsford.md) was occasionally threatened by the undead that flocked to Culotte, prompting the townsfolk to periodically clear the ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTDC-2)</sup> Eventually Culotte was cleared of undead. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md) entered the ruins and retrieved the ancient Ayleid statue, causing the dead to rise from their graves and once again plague the ruins. The statue was later sold to [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBCollector-3)</sup>

## Notes
- The settlement's original Ayleid name has been lost to time. The name "Culotte" only came into use some time after the fall of White-Gold.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-1)</sup>
- The nearby Culotte Bridge that once spanned the Upper Niben was named after the ruins.
