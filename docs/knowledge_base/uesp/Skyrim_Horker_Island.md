## Walkthrough
The entrance to [Gyldenhul Barrow](Skyrim_Gyldenhul_Barrow.md) is on the northwest shore, while the horkers are mainly found on the eastern and northern shores. There are numerous [spiky grass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ashen_Grass_Pod) plants found on the island, and an [ebony ore vein](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Ore_Vein) on the eastern shore against a large rock. For an easy approach, either the southern or northwestern beaches offer the shortest distance over water. The island has a rocky spine limiting access from north to south or east to west. There are several [Felsaad terns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Felsaad_Tern) flying over the island at any time, no matter the weather.

The beach to the south has an overturned rowboat with several barrels around it, two of which are fish barrels. [Saden's Journal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Saden%27s_Journal) lies on top of a crate. Traveling counter-clockwise around the island to the eastern shore, there are several horkers including a named horker, [Lord Tusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Tusk), a total of four dead [reavers](Skyrim_Reaver.md) along the beach, the gory remains of more humans amid blood splatters, and the broken remains of a larger boat, with the front half wedged up on the rocks at the shoreline and other half under the water. Just below the visible portion of the boat underwater is an unlocked [chest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chest) beside an expert-locked [East Empire Company strongbox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:East_Empire_Company_Strongbox) and a [clam](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Clam). There are three [Nordic barnacle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nordic_Barnacle) clusters and a patch of [slaughterfish eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Egg) on the other half of the boat, either on the deck or the hull.

Heading inland due west from the boat will lead you to the corpse of [Saden](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Saden) and a unique [mace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mace), [Horksbane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Horksbane), which as the name suggests does increased damage to horkers. Continuing up the rise from the corpse will bring you to an area covered in human skulls and bones, with an unlocked chest, a random enchanted [weapon](Skyrim_Weapons.md), and a pair of random [boots](Skyrim_Armor.md#Boots). Farther along the shore to the north is the mast of another sunken ship sticking out of the water. There is another Nordic barnacle cluster on the hull below the mast, while farther north on the beach is a master-locked wooden chest.

Continuing northwest along the isle's edge past a short section without a beach, with only rocks disappearing into the water, you will reach the western shore. There is a [quicksilver ore vein](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ore_Vein) against the rocks near two rowboats pulled up onshore. Behind several tall rocks standing upright along the shore is the entrance to Gyldenhul Barrow. A [snowberry](Skyrim_Snowberries.md) bush grows just to the left of the master-locked entrance, which is guarded by two leveled reavers. Once you have found and emptied all four chests during the relevant [quest](Skyrim_Deathbrand_(quest).md), you will receive a [key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gyldenhul_Barrow_Key) that will open this door.

## Notes
- Notably, Saden's Journal is the only book in the game with a value of 1 gold. The only book worth less is the [Imperial Missive](Skyrim_Imperial_Missive_(Battle-Born).md) found in the [House of Clan Battle-Born](Skyrim_House_of_Clan_Battle-Born.md).
- Lord Tusk respawns every time the island resets.
