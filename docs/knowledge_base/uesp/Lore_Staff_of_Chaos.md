The Staff of Unity and Chaos,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRBdf-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> more commonly known as the **Staff of Chaos**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AEot MQ-2)</sup> or the **Chaos Staff**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TMS-3)</sup> was a [staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff) of great power and great historical significance to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AEot MQ-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRWall-4)</sup> The staff has also been known as the **Balac-thurm**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Final Lesson-5)</sup> and the **Rod of Septim**.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Vijay Interview-6)</sup>

### Origin & Abilities
The Staff of Chaos was originally named "Balac-thurm" by its creator, a legendary enchanter known as [Loreth the Fangleman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Loreth_the_Fangleman) circa [1E 480](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_480).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Final Lesson-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-WRit AUA-7)</sup>

Having been fangled from the essence of the [Land](Lore_Tamriel.md) itself, the staff was nigh-indestructible.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARSilmane-8)</sup> So too, it is said that the staff cannot be removed from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) without the "direst consequences to the land."<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBB-9)</sup> In addition, the staff's wielder may "sow the seeds strife and dissension" wherever they go should they wish.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBB-9)</sup> The power to destroy the corporeal form of one's enemies has also been witnessed among the staff's many abilities.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARSilmane-8)</sup> Some have speculated that the orb at the head of the staff may have been a [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md), as the Balac had the inherent ability to "open the gate between worlds."<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HJ-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARSilmane-8)</sup> A descendant of Loreth [later attempted](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md#Legacy) to recreate the magic potential of the Balac-thurm within two staves, which had meant harnessing the power of the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md) and [Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons) within each — powers which were ultimately determined to enter into an "unknown madness."<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HJ-10)</sup>

### Staff of Towers
Some scholars believe that the [Staff of Towers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff_of_Towers), originally created by the Ayleid Lord [Anumaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril) in the early First Era (and later used by the [Marukhati Selectives](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukhati_Selective) during the [Middle Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Middle_Dawn) event), is the same artifact as the Staff of Chaos; but others point out that this would be impossible, as they each originate from entirely different time periods.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-WRit AUA-7)</sup> Additionally, the physical appearances and magical properties of the two staves are notably very different,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AEot MQ-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOPsijic-11)</sup> Notably, both these staves' shared a magical association with the [Land](Lore_Tamriel.md) and the [Towers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Towers).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARSilmane-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AE4t ET-12)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-BTMQ-13)</sup>

### Prophecy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/GEN-alduin-Info3.jpg/200px-GEN-alduin-Info3.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-alduin-Info3.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-alduin-Info3.jpg) Depiction on Alduin's Wall
<center>*"When misrule takes its place at the eight corners of the world"*</center> The splitting of the Balac-thurm during a time of misrule was the first [Event](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Event) of prophecy depicted on [Alduin's Wall](Lore_Alduin%27s_Wall.md), built by the [Akaviri Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) in the [1E 2800 s](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2800) to depict in stone the events that would preface the Waking of the [World-Eater](Lore_Alduin.md), a revelation said to have originated from an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md); this legend became known as the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SREsbern-14)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TBot D-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Secrets Wall-16)</sup> The staff is depicted as having been split apart into eight pieces, representing misrule taking its place at the eight corners of the world — an event that would later come to pass during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) of Tamriel's Third Era.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AEot MQ-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Secrets Wall-16)</sup>

Of similar prophetic note, the Daedric Prince of Madness, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), rather prehumously refers to the reassemblement of the Staff of Chaos in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), while dialoguing with [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LLL-17)</sup>

### Age of Tiber Septim
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_16.jpg) Tiber Septim The journey of the Staff of Chaos from the hands of Loreth the Fangler to the grip of Emperor Tiber Septim is an unknown one, all that is known is that Septim possessed the thing during his [war](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBB-9)</sup>

There were several battles in Tiber Septim's conquest of Morrowind.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Bio B-18)</sup> In one of these battles, [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) is said to have been laid waste.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Bio B-18)</sup> Tiber had allied himself with a male member of the [Ra'athim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra%27athim) lineage, who was on the rag-tail end of the family. With the help of this Ra'athim, the Empire recovered a powerful artifact from the depths of Mournhold that can see one clear from danger, the [Horn of Summoning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horn_of_Summoning); which had been stored inside an ancient shrine dedicated to the ancestor god Ephen, who was entrusted to safeguard the artifact in ancient times by [his brother](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moraelyn).<sup>[[nb 6]](#intnote_nb6)</sup> Tiber Septim claimed the Horn, but left the Staff of Chaos where it had laid for safekeeping under the watchful eye of General Symmachus.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBB-9)</sup>

This Ra'athim was later rewarded by the Empire, having the fiefdom of Ebonheart bequeathed upon him for supplying his aid during the conflict. Additionally, General Symmachus of the Imperial Army later became the Prime Minister of Mournhold.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBB-9)</sup> The Hlaalu of Ebonheart ultimately remained jealous that the Staff's safekeeping was assigned to the rulers in Mournhold, and not to their own fief, and claimed that rightful guardianship of the Staff of Chaos should be entrusted to Ebonheart. Mournhold responded that Moraelyn himself before had placed the Horn in the safekeeping of Ephen whose own bailiwick was Mournhold, and that Mournhold was arguably the god's birthplace, giving them the greater claim to the honor.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBB-9)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

### Theft from Mournhold
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/TR-place-Mournhold_Temple.jpg/200px-TR-place-Mournhold_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Mournhold_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Mournhold_Temple.jpg) Mournhold When Jagar Tharn began planning the theft of the Staff of Chaos is unclear. During his time as a mage-priest at the Temple of [Sethiete](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sethiete) in [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), Jagar stole information on powerful spells, the location of the Staff of Chaos, and how the Staff could be used.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Mo T4-19)</sup> Circa [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376), a bard known as the Nightingale seduced Queen [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and she unwittingly revealed the location of the Staff of Chaos to the Nightingale, who then stole the Staff from its sanctuary beneath the city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RFT-20)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRB4-21)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-22)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-23)</sup> Queen Barenziah later recognized the Nightingale to be Jagar Tharn.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRB5-24)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-22)</sup> However, an alternative account asserts that the bard who stole the Staff of Chaos was actually a master thief named Drayven Indoril, a member of the [Nightingale Trinity](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md), and that the truth was covered up for political reasons.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-23)</sup> After the Staff was given to Jagar Tharn, he attempted to kill Drayven Indoril, but Drayven escaped.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-23)</sup> Jagar searched for Drayven but eventually abandoned the search.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-23)</sup> Nevertheless, both versions of events state that it was Jagar Tharn who was ultimately behind the theft and took possession of the Staff of Chaos.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRB5-24)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-22)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TN2-23)</sup>

Soon after the theft of the Staff of Chaos, Barenziah's consort [Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus) sent urgent communiqués to [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRB4-21)</sup> Uriel VII expressed shock and displeasure at the Staff's theft and urged Symmachus to make every effort to locate its whereabouts and communicate developments to the newly appointed Imperial Battlemage, who was tasked with tracking down the Staff and those responsible for stealing it.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRB5-24)</sup>

### Imperial Simulacrum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/AR-scene-Intro_06.jpg/200px-AR-scene-Intro_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_06.jpg) Jagar Tharn wielding the Staff of Chaos The staff played a central role in the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md). Jagar Tharn plotted for months to bring his plan to fruition.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AIS-25)</sup> Later in 3E 389, he requested to meet with the Emperor to discuss rumors of treachery.<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AIS-25)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DC-26)</sup> But when Uriel VII and General Warhaft arrived to discuss this, Jagar used the Staff of Chaos to imprison Uriel VII and General Warhaft in an [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) [plane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) of his own creation.<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-27)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ALOUS-28)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AIS-25)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-29)</sup><sup>:180</sup> Time moved much slower in this plane of Oblivion; thus, it would be centuries before Uriel VII died, and he would experience nothing but inexplicable and haunting nightmares.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ALOUS-28)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ARMIS-30)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ARMISEV-31)</sup> When Ria Silmane learned of what Jagar Tharn had done to Uriel VII, she attempted to inform the Elder Council, but before she could inform them, Jagar used the Staff of Chaos to kill her.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ARRd-32)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-22)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AIS-25)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg/200px-AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Intro_11.jpg) Tharn transmorphing himself using the Staff With his plot successful, Jagar drained the Staff of Chaos of its power, and he imbued its energy, along with his own life force, into the [Jewel of Fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jewel_of_Fire).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ARRd-32)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AJTd-33)</sup> Then, he divided the Staff into eight pieces, and hid them throughout the Empire.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ARRd-32)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOQB3-22)</sup> He took these precautions because the Staff of Chaos was the only item that could be used to free Uriel VII from Oblivion and because if someone did discover his deception, they would have to recover the pieces and reassemble the Staff, only to discover it to be useless without the Jewel of Fire.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ARRd-32)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AJTd-33)</sup> Jagar used Illusion magic to assume the appearance of Uriel VII, and then he took the imprisoned Emperor's place on the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AIS-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ARMIS-30)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-27)</sup>

As the Champion retrieved each piece of the Staff, Jagar used magical visions to threaten and taunt them, and Jagar sent his minions to try and defeat them.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AJTd-33)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AEot MQ-2)</sup> Finally, the Champion retrieved the final piece of the Staff of Chaos from the mountain of [Dagoth-Ur](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), but when they reassembled the Staff, Jagar mocked them, and he told them that he had drained the Staff of its power.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AJTd-33)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ADU-34)</sup> He taunted Ria's Champion to confront him in the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and retrieve the Jewel of Fire.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AJTd-33)</sup> The Champion entered the Imperial Palace, and they fought their way through the halls of the Palace until they reached Jagar.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ATIP-35)</sup> The Champion fought Jagar and defeated him, and then they used the Staff of Chaos to drain the power of the Jewel of Fire, which destroyed Jagar Tharn, and Uriel VII was returned to Tamriel.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ATIP-35)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ALOUS-28)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-27)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ASEEHA-36)</sup>

### Fate
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/AR-flc-END02.GIF/200px-AR-flc-END02.GIF)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-flc-END02.GIF) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-flc-END02.GIF) The Staff of Chaos dissolves Ria Silmane speculated that touching the Staff to the Jewel of Fire would be enough to open the gate between worlds to free Emperor Uriel Septim VII, kill Jagar Tharn, and destroy the Staff in one fell swoop.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARSilmane-8)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> The Staff was last witnessed floating around the Jewel of Fire and then dissolving to open the gate for Uriel, killing Tharn, which may or may not have destroyed the Jewel and the Staff itself in the process.<sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_nb4)</sup> The ultimate fate of the Staff of Chaos (and its power that had been infused in the Jewel of Fire) is unknown,<sup>[[nb 5]](#intnote_nb5)</sup> though it has been said that the Staff had been shattered into pieces and lost to time.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Chaos Arms-37)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HJ-10)</sup>

### Legacy
An unknown descendant of Loreth later attempted to recreate the magic of the legendary Staff of Chaos by creating two new [staves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staves): the Arm of the Sun and the Arm of the Moon, together known as the [Arms of Chaos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arms_of_Chaos), which reemerged during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-CC-38)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HJ-10)</sup>

The alteration of the Horn to the Staff change doesn't make much sense in context, and was seemingly a confused mistake on the part of the editor rather than an intentional lore alteration. Though, if it were intentional, the change would have a variety of lore implications. |
