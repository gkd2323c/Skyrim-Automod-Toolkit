# House Montclair

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/ON-place-Montclair_Manor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Montclair_Manor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Montclair_Manor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Montclair_Manor.jpg) Montclair Manor House Montclair was a prominent [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble house from [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) that later became a vampire clan because of the Lightless Remnant of the [Doomcrag](Lore_Erokii.md). One of the earliest references to the Montclairs was at the [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md) in [1E 480](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_480), in an event remembered as the Charge of the Montclair Knights (often erroneously called the Charge of the Shornhelm Knights). At their peak, the Montclairs, who were known for their humility, guided the people of Rivenspire through times of hardship.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup> After the events of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), the Montclairs, House Tamrith, and House Dorell formed the triumvirate Council of the North that ruled Rivenspire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HTARH-2)</sup>

## History
The Montclairs were made barons in [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029), when [Empress Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) brought [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) into the [First Empire](Lore_First_Empire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> By the Second Era, Shornhelm was ruled by [House Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md) and its twenty-first count, [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md), who reigned as King of Shornhelm from [2E 522](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_522) to [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546). Hurlburt married Countess [Iphilia of House Montclair](Lore_Iphilia_Montclair.md), with whom he sired a son, [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup> In [2E 544](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_544), Iphilia died, making Phylgeon the thirty-eighth Baron of House Montclair. In 2E 546, Hurlburt died, and the noble houses of Rivenspire disagreed on which son should take the throne of Shornhelm.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup>

Fourteen-year-old Phylgeon was supported by House Montclair for the throne, but House Branquette and House Tamrith endorsed Phylgeon's older illegitimate half-brother, [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md). Baron Phylgeon's advisors contended that he was the legitimate heir to the throne, not only because of his heritage but also by quoting the codicil, the Bretonnick Natalitie, which declared that "Howse Mount Clayre" was the royal house of Shornhelm. The Council of the North reviewed the claim, but one day, the codicil disappeared, and Ranser came forward with a long-lost decree from the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) that named House Branquette their royal delegates of Rivenspire. In a narrow victory, Ranser won the crown and was proclaimed King of Shornhelm, and while some of Phylgeon's advisors wanted him to fight for the crown, he declined, preferring to stay the Baron of Montclair.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup>

### Ranser's War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg/200px-ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg) King Ranser of Shornhelm Ranser later had a daughter, [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md), and when [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) became King of Wayrest, he began courting her. Before Emeric's marriage contract to Rayelle was worked out, he instead married Princess [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), a strategic move to strengthen trade between High Rock and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TFOKR-4)</sup> Emeric not marrying Rayelle infuriated Ranser, causing him to initiate a civil war with the rest of the Daggerfall Covenant in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TOAM6-5)</sup> [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) began in Last Seed of that year, and nearly every noble house of Rivenspire, including Houses Montclair, Dorell, and Tamrith, rallied behind the king. Phylgeon did not have faith in his brother's cause and offered to serve as a peace envoy between the two kings. While Emeric's response is lost to history, Ranser's angry answer was quite clear and well-known, so Phylgeon relented and provided the Montclair knights for Ranser's armies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup>

With Ranser's defeat at the Battle of Markwasten Moor, not only was he presumed dead, but with him was House Branquette's dynasty. The Crown of Shornhelm was also lost to the war. Without a king, the Council of the North, composed of Baron [Alard Dorell](Lore_Alard_Dorell.md), Baron Esmark Tamrith, and Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), governed the kingdom and tried their best to reinstate order and peace across the region. There was a common sentiment across Rivenspire that the kingdom needed a king, something that Wylon Montclair believed that he could accomplish. Not only was he a grandson of King Hurlburt through his father, Phylgeon, but he also rediscovered the Bretonnick Natalitie, which claimed that "Howse Mount Clayre" was appointed rulership of "Sharn Helm" and its contingent land. For these reasons, Baron Montclair concocted a plan to acquire the throne of Shornhelm.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup>

### Becoming a Vampire Clan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_02.jpg) The Montclairs transformed into vampires by the Lightless Remnant At some point, Wylon Montclair had become friends with Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md), the [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) count who ruled over Castle Ravenwatch.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vera-6)</sup> Wylon Montclair was a possessive man who, after his wife fell ill, became willing to go to any lengths to keep her alive. Regardless of her personal wishes, he thought it better for her to exist as a vampire than succumb to her illness, leading him to ask Verandis to turn Leila into a vampire.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BWM-7)</sup> However, either Verandis or Leila rejected this proposal.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DWID-8)</sup> This failure led to the events that transpired at [Erokii](Lore_Erokii.md). Reezal-Jul, an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) necromancer, became involved in the effort to heal Leila. However, unknown to both Wylon and Verandis, he was manipulating them as part of his plan to attain revenge for the fall of [King Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-RJJ-9)</sup> Reezal-Jul gave a mysterious [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) shard to Verandis. After receiving the relic from Verandis, Wylon used it to heal Leila, but her health soon began to deteriorate once again. Guided by Reezal-Jul, the three Montclairs and Verandis ascended the Doomcrag in search of the Lightless Remnant, which they believed would heal Leila permanently. When they reached the summit, they attempted to use the artifact to cure Lady Leila's illness.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DWID-8)</sup>

Unbeknownst to the Verandis and the Montclairs, the artifact had been corrupted. Instead of healing the dying Leila, it turned her and the rest of her family into vampires. Leila's transformation went terribly wrong, and Verandis killed her in an act of mercy. However, blinded by anger, Wylon turned on his old friend.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DWID-8)</sup> Now infected with vampirism and corrupted by the influence of the artifact, Wylon sought to conquer all of Rivenspire and attain revenge on the members of [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) and the other noble houses.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BOR-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Lot-11)</sup> Ignorant of Reezal-Jul's true motives, Wylon employed the deceitful necromancer as his general.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LTRJ-12)</sup>

### Montclair's War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-quest-Shornhelm_Divided.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Shornhelm_Divided.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Shornhelm_Divided.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Shornhelm_Divided.jpg) High King Emeric's summit after the battle [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/ON-quest-The_Crown_of_Shornhelm.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Crown_of_Shornhelm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crown_of_Shornhelm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crown_of_Shornhelm.jpg) Shornhelm's celebration after the war After all these years of Wylon Montclair's preparations, the Baron's plans came to fruition with his annexation of Shornhelm's upper district in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). He bribed the city guard and attacked other nobles before he took the upper district. As the Shornhelm City Guard were unable to withstand the Montclair Knights, the Daggerfall Covenant was tasked in the city's retaking, as they assembled south in Oldgate.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONDarien-13)</sup> Countess [Eselde Tamrith](Lore_Eselde_Tamrith.md) returned to the kingdom took up the mantle of head of House Tamrith after news of her father's death a few months prior,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HTARH-2)</sup> only to find the capital city entrapped in conflict.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONEselde-14)</sup> The Countess, Baron Alard Dorell, and Count Verandis of [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) gathered at the Chapel of the Divines, where they would devise their strategy to retake the upper city.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONDivided-15)</sup>

An [Agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Covenant approached the meeting and was caught up in the situation. They delved into the occupied upper district and defeated the two traitorous guards that allowed Montclair's annexation. The Montclair court mage, Reezal-Jul kidnapped three nobles and attempted to flee via the Fevered Mews before he was confronted by both the Agent and Verandis Ravenwatch. Despite their best efforts, the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) escaped west into the Eyebright Feld but regardless, the city was liberated but the looming threat that House Montclair posed was ever-present. High King Emeric arrived after the city was freed and believed that the threat was far greater than it may have seemed. With his blessing and his forces, the pursuit for both Reezal-Jul and Baron Montclair commenced,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONDivided-15)</sup> but before the Agent could continue further, he was sent to Castle Ravenwatch by the High King, where they would assist Count Verandis and learn about Montclair's history.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONDream-16)</sup>

With the corrupted artifact at their disposal, Wylon and his daughter Lleraya Montclair turned many of their kinsmen into vampires. They also orchestrated the [bloodfiend](Lore_Bloodfiend.md) crisis in Rivenspire, using the power of the Lightless Remnant to change many of the citizens into bloodfiends. Among their many victims, only the lucky ones became true vampires.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BOR-10)</sup> Lady Lleraya Montclair had set her sights on Northpoint. She arrived in the city and immediately got an audience with Ellic Dorell, with the promise of help.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONEllic-17)</sup> She advised him to gather the city's people and as they gathered en masse, she used magic from the Lightless Remnant to turn them into [bloodfiends](Lore_Bloodfiend.md). Some people managed to escape with the Dorell Blademaster, Qariar to the Sloshing Tankard inn, but most of the city's citizens were turned into feral vampires and Lady Montclair sealed Northpoint from the outside.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONQariar-18)</sup> In Dorell Manor, Lady Montclair used the remnant for a simple [illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) spell, a veil that charmed the nobles into her favor.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONLleraya-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONVerandis-20)</sup>

Outside of the city, Baron Alard Dorell and the armies of Shornhelm amassed to retake the city. He sent several agents across the field to find a way into the city and discovered a smuggler's tunnel underneath the lighthouse, on the north shore. A Shornhelm guardsman, Skordo the Knife devised a plan for the members of the invasion force to stealthily enter the city and find a safe house to further plan their attack. They eventually took over the manor of Baron Chirane and rendezvoused with Blademaster Qariar. Baron Alard sent an [Agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) to confront Lady Lleraya Montclair and find Ellic Dorell. With help from [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md), the Agent broke the veil and defeated the vampire mistress. For allowing Lady Montclair to take over the city and causing the death of many people, Baron Alard punished his son and relieved him of his status.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONLiberation-21)</sup> After securing Northpoint, the forces of Shornhelm gathered at the base of the Shrouded Pass for a final attack against Baron Wylon Montclair.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONPuzzle-22)</sup>

Despite being a vampire himself, Verandis' strong sense of morality and temperance allowed him to resist the power and allure of the artifact.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DWID-8)</sup> Subsequently, he and the rest of House Ravenwatch worked to end the threat of the artifact and the mad Montclairs. With the aid of the Agent, Verandis successfully stopped his old friend, although it ultimately cost him his life. After Wylon was defeated by the Agent, the Lightless Remnant began to crack and release its terrible power, a sight which led the vindictive Wylon to rejoice with his last breath. Fearing the devastation the artifact's destruction would wreak on Rivenspire, Verandis sacrificed his life to transport it to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOTLR-23)</sup>

After the Baron's defeat at the hands of both Count Ravenwatch and the Agent, all of Rivenspire celebrated and gathered in Shornhelm for the coronation of the kingdom's new ruler. It was decided that either Countess Eselde Tamrith or Baron Alard Dorell would be given the crown but it was the High King that chose one of them to lead. The Countess believed that she would make a thoughtful leader, one that had the people's best interest at heart. Her first act as Queen would have been to restore the people's faith and return their lives to some form of normalcy.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONEselde-14)</sup> The Baron on the other hand believed that Rivenspire needed a decisive leader and a firm hand to guide them in troubled times. His first act as King would have been to bolster the kingdom's defenses and enlist hundreds of youth to restore their strength.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONAlard-24)</sup> It is unknown who was chosen at the end of the day, but Rivenspire was finally at peace.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONCrown-25)</sup>
