#### B'Korgen (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[B'Korgen](Lore_B%27Korgen.md) (also referred to as **Bekor-gen**) was a [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) sorcerer proficient in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md), [fleshcrafting](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md), and [mind magic](Lore_Mind_Magic.md). #### Babette (fl. [3E 334](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_334) - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Babette](Lore_Babette.md) is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) assassin and member of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). She is a master [alchemist](Lore_Alchemy.md). While she looks like a ten-year-old girl, Babette is in fact a three hundred-year-old [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md). She was bitten as a child and, as a result, retained her childlike appearance. She uses this to her advantage to complete her contracts; her targets do not perceive her as a threat, and she often plays the part of a scared, lost little girl to gain their trust. Due to her age, it can be inferred that she was born and turned during the reign of Empress [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md).

Babette resided in a Dark Brotherhood sanctuary in the outskirts of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), where the last known chapter of the Brotherhood was still active in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). That same year, Babette witnessed the arrival of [Cicero](Lore_Cicero.md) and the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) to the Falkreath Sanctuary and the appointment of a new [Listener](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md). She was absent from the Sanctuary when the chapter was massacred by the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md), and subsequently moved to the Dawnstar Sanctuary with her surviving Dark Brotherhood family. Conflicting reports suggest that the Falkreath Sanctuary was wiped out without the appointment of a Listener. In any case, Babette remains one of the few known surviving members of the Dark Brotherhood in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

#### Bacaro Volorus ([2E 540](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_540) - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Lord Bacaro Volorus](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md) was a half- [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md), half- [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) philanthropist and founder of the [Society of the Steadfast](Lore_Society_of_the_Steadfast.md), which provided aid to the hungry, as well as sanctuary and rehabilitation to the victims of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). Bacaro's Imperial heritage came from his father of House Volorus of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and his Breton roots came from his mother of [House Merchad](Lore_House_Merchad.md). His Steadfast Society's symbol consisted of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md)'s symbol with three pouring chalices which was reminiscent of the symbol of his mother's noble house, but the Imperial [Red Diamond](Lore_Red_Diamond.md) was also used.

Volorus was secretly the **Ascendant Lord**, leader of the [Ascendant Order](Lore_Ascendant_Order.md), a Breton-influenced terrorist organization that likened themselves to [knights](Lore_Knights.md). He wished that one day chroniclers would look back at what he hoped to accomplish, and see that him operating by an ends justify the means basis was a necessity. Volorus' ultimate goal was to become the new [Druid King](Lore_Druid_King.md), destroy the alliances responsible for the Three Banners War, and replace the fallen [Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) with his own "Ivy Throne".

As the Ascendant Lord, Bacaro was extremely well trained and exceedingly skilled at martial combat. Upon becoming a false Druid King, he merged with the [Spirit of Mount Firesong](Lore_Spirit_of_Mount_Firesong.md) and commanded its powers. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) confronted him at [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md), defeating Volorus and saving the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) from destruction. The Ascendant Order had amassed a treasure trove when they were active, and when it was raided, a sculpted statue of the Ascendant Lord was discovered among the treasure which captured his might and presence perfectly.

#### Balagog gro-Nolob (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Balagog gro-Nolob](Lore_Balagog_gro-Nolob.md), operating under the alias **the Gourmet**, was a mysterious [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) [culinary](Lore_Cuisine.md) genius, known for concealing his true identity. His origins, race, and gender remained unknown to the general populace, including his devoted fans, many of whom speculated about his race. One example of speculation was that he might be a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) due to his extensive knowledge of woodland herbs. He authored *[Uncommon Taste](Lore_Uncommon_Taste.md)*, a widely acclaimed cookbook across Tamriel. As of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), this culinary masterpiece has become quite a phenomenon. He was a wealthy individual known to indulge in high-quality wine.

He catered for esteemed individuals, including the court of [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md), and served as his personal cook.

#### Jarl Balgruuf the Greater (fl. 4E 201)
[Balgruuf the Greater](Lore_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md) was the [Jarl](Lore_Skyrim%27s_Rule.md) of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun.md), and ruled from the prosperous and strategically-located city of the same name. A proud [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) and a descendant of High King [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md), Balgruuf admired the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) and once made the pilgrimage to [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md) as a young man. His three children were Frothar, Dagny, and Nelkir, but he had no spouse in 4E 201. While considered a fair and shrewd ruler, and an honorable warrior who put his heart and soul into his service, he was also not considered a patient man. <sup>[*verification needed — his character description needs development and verification, and last comment seems to be a single remark by Ulfric*]</sup>

In his youth Balgruuf adventured in Morrowind , where he became battle companions with Irileth, a Dunmer who he appointed as his Housecarl once he became Jarl. He faced numerous assassination attempts.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md).

#### Barbas
[Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md) is the Hound of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md). He is a shapeshifting [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), although he most commonly takes on the form of a [dog](Lore_Dog.md) or [Skaafin](Lore_Skaafin.md). Barbas acts as a repository for around half of Vile's power, though behaves as his loyal servant rather than equal, often pining for his approval to little avail. According to Vile, he likes being a dog—as well as many other things—on sunny days. *"When he's like this, I think he's somewhere else. It's all quite significant, of course... But who knows what it means."* Although Vile is always depicted along with Barbas, the Prince has at times grown tired of and abandoned him. While Barbas seems to be subservient to Clavicus Vile, he also often warns people against Vile's plots, suggesting he has some discretion and free will. He sometimes takes the form of a musically talented [scamp](Lore_Scamp.md) merchant named **Creeper**.

Barbas' origins are unknown, though ancient idols and cave paintings dating back to the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) depict Clavicus Vile standing with a large hound by his side. It is sometimes believed that Barbas is merely a greater Daedric servant to Vile, though others suggest that Vile and Barbas are in a sense the same person, split into two beings, as Clavicus Vile's very social nature would make it impossible for him to not have a companion.

#### Queen Barenziah ([2E 893](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_893) - ?d)
[Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) is one of the most famous figures of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). She became the first Queen of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), a vassal ruler to the Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Her consort was [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md), former general of Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) (and the Emperor himself was rumored to be one of Barenziah's former lovers). Though her position was largely ceremonial for most of the Third Era, the couple were said to be popular with the people of Morrowind. The Queen was considered a loyal vassal throughout the era, and sided against [Uriel Septim III](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md) and Queen [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) during the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) circa [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120). However, circa [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391), she went into exile due to unrest in her province. Symmachus was killed in a riot, and she was forced to abdicate her throne to her uncle [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md). Of all her adventures, she is most well-known for going on to help thwart the impostor [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) and bring about an end to the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) by [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399). She married King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), who she had forged a bond with while working to defeat Tharn and free the captive Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md). In [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), she and Eadwyre were among the political leaders of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) who sought to acquire the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md) from an [agent](Lore_Hero.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), and who benefited greatly from the ensuing [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Miracle_of_Peace.md), or "Warp in the West". Barenziah claimed ignorance on what exactly transpired during this mysterious two-day phenomenon. Several years later, when Eadwyre died, she returned to Morrowind, where her son Prince [Helseth](Lore_Helseth.md) soon became King, while she took on the title of Queen Mother. Her other child, Princess [Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md), had already gone on to become Queen of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md). She may have had other children, one of many disputed facets of her life. For more information, see the [lore article](Lore_Barenziah.md).

#### Baron of Breakers
The [Baron of Breakers](Lore_Baron_of_Breakers.md) was an obscure [Apocryphal](Lore_Apocrypha.md) entity associated with the runes of the [Arcanists](Lore_Arcanists.md). One way for an Arcanist to harness their power was by inscribing the runes of the Baron of Breakers to create objects, such as spinning discs called tidal chakrams, which could grant magical protective shields. Similarly to the [Fate Crone](Lore_Fate_Crone.md) and [Blind Man](Lore_Blind_Man.md) he was associated with such discs.

Arcanists sometimes attempt to seek power from the Baron of the Breakers, and [Azandar al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md) was known to have done so. In one instance, Azandar humorously recounted a dream in which the Baron offered power, but Azandar requested tea instead, much to the Baron's displeasure. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), hair clips etched with runes associated with the Baron were in circulation on the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md).

#### Vateshran Barth (fl. 2E 582)
**Vateshran** [Barth](Lore_Barth.md) was one of the most prominent [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [historians and storytellers](Lore_Vateshrans.md) of the period of [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). #### Baurus (fl. [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))

#### King Bazrag gro-Fharun (fl. 2E 582)
[Bazrag gro-Fharun](Lore_Bazrag_gro-Fharun.md) was the clan chief of [Fharun Stronghold](Lore_Fharun.md) and the King of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md); he was a traditionalist and worshipper of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). Once a staunch ally of [Kurog](Lore_Kurog_gro-Orsinium.md), he eventually came to oppose Kurog's rule and the worship of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), as he felt it was a plot by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) to spread their religion to [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md). Unlike Kurog, he had the backing of the Orcish clans due to his traditionalist stances, and for this reason, he was considered equal to Kurog in terms of influence. Bazrag believed that patience and adherence to tradition were key to uniting the Orcish clans and rebuilding Orsinium.

#### Beela-Kaar (fl. Merethic Era)
[Beela-Kaar](Lore_Beela-Kaar.md) was a powerful [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) [sorcerer](Lore_Magic.md) who lived before the [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md), during the height of the Argonian civilization in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). According to the oral stories passed down by Argonian elders, Beela-Kaar was renowned for his great power and skill in the arcane arts, and served as a trusted advisor to the Saxhleel leaders, using his magic to help the people of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md), until he turned to dark [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) in an attempt to save his beloved mate, leading to his downfall and the creation of a cursed artifact, the Golden Skull of Beela-Kaar.

#### Emperor Belharza the Man-Bull (fl. 1E 266)

Little is known about the emperor's life and reign as most records of the period were destroyed following the rise of the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OM-4)</sup> Private correspondence between Belharza and Alessia is held in the Library of Dusk in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), though its contents are unspecified.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLo DRB-5)</sup> A surviving document from an Alessian Order sect indirectly implies that Belharza was troubled by large [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) hordes.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-COTF-6)</sup> One controversial interpretation of an ancient tablet fragment called the Belharza Stone asserts that the precursors of the xenophobic Order may have openly clashed with Belharza, and violently purged the minotaurs from the Empire.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OM-4)</sup>

#### Proconsul Beloren-Kaie (2E? - 2E 5th century)

#### King Bendu Olo (fl. 1E 2241 - 2290)

His notes became a rare source of information on the Sload, and were disseminated by scholars for many years.

#### Boziikkodstrun
[Boziikkodstrun](Lore_Boziikkodstrun.md) was a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose skeleton was used by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to serve as the basis for the first [Daedric Titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md).

In the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), dragons ruled [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and sought domination over all mortals. Their reign ended following the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) when [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) was banished, and those who were not slain were driven into hiding. It was then that Boziikkodstrun exerted his nigh-divine will in an attempt to fly beyond the borders of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). Although he failed, Boziikkodstrun's effort drew the attention of Molag Bal, who recognized that the Dragons had conquered much of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). He approached the dragon and offered him a place of honor and privilege in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), and in his weakened state Boziikkodstrun accepted.

Bal subsequently opened a portal, granting Boziikkodstrun passage into Coldharbour. Immediately upon his arrival, Boziikkodstrun was bound in chains of cold ebon iron and imprisoned in the depths of the Tower of Lies. Hoping to learn the secrets of his race's dominion over mortals, Molag Bal had Boziikkodstrun tortured and interrogated. Boziikkodstrun was tormented for ages, but was indignant at his host's foul treatment of him; thus, he refused to speak in his native [language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) as the Daedric Prince requested. Frustrated by his defiance, Bal punished the Dragon by slowly eating the flesh from his bones. When only Boziikkodstrun's skeleton remained, the Prince declared that he would create his own dragons, which would be mightier than those of Nirn.

Boziikkodstrun's skeleton was taken to the [Vile Laboratory](Lore_Vile_Laboratory.md), where it was infused with the blood-of-darkness in order to reawaken it as a [Vestige](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md). It can be assumed that this process was similar to the creation of the [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md), mortals who have their [soul](Lore_Souls.md) substituted with the [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) of a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Bal had the skeleton adjusted to his specifications, then it was then submerged in the Azure Chasm to allow it to reform in [creatia](Lore_Azure_Plasm.md).

Within a nanaeon, the first Daedric Titan arose from the chasm and bounded nimbly up the Endless Stair. It was soon determined to be a powerful new addition to Molag Bal's war-slaves. Many more Daedric Titans were made in Boziikkodstrun's image, and were released upon Tamriel during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

However, after King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) was killed during the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md), Brisienna found herself out of favor with the new regime and was forced into hiding. Nevertheless, she remained the Emperor's emissary in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), assisting another [agent](Lore_Hero.md#The_Agent) sent by the Emperor to investigate Lysandus' death. Lady Magnessen was instrumental in helping this agent recover a powerful [artifact](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), an act which led directly to the event known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).

At some point, Lady Brisienna evidently caught some of the public's imagination, as she was featured as a character in the popular historical fiction *[Mystery of Talara](Lore_Mystery_of_Talara.md)*.

#### Brynjolf (fl. 4E 201)
[Brynjolf](Lore_Brynjolf.md) is a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) thief, a member of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) and the [Nightingales](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md) from the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), one day Brynjolf noticed a new face in Riften, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), and approached them, revealing he was a member of the Thieves Guild. He offered them a chance to join his organization. Brynjolf wanted the Dragonborn to plant a merchant's ring on another merchant as a test. Conflicting reports claim whether this operation was successful or not; whichever the case, Brynjolf saw potential in the Dragonborn and sent them to the Thieves Guild's headguarters, the Ragged Flagon in Riften's Ratway. He tasked the Dragonborn with collecting debt from different business owners, sent them to Goldenglow Estate, and sent them to Maven Black-Briar, who tasked them with sabotaging one of her business rivals. When the Dragonborn was sent to squeeze information from a smuggler, Gulum-Ei, he advised the Dragonborn to spare him.

Later on, [Karliah](Lore_Karliah.md), a former guild member and lover of the previous Guild Master, [Gallus Desidenius](Lore_Gallus_Desidenius.md) returned to the Ragged Flagon accompanied by the Dragonborn. She revealed that [Mercer Frey](Lore_Mercer_Frey.md), Gallus' successor, had betrayed and murdered Gallus, framed Karliah, and stolen from the guild for years. Furious, Brynjolf joined Karliah and the Dragonborn, becoming a Nightingale. They tracked Mercer down to [Irkngthand](Lore_Irkngthand.md), where the Dragonborn defeated him. Brynjolf then elected the Dragonborn as the new Guild Master and was present for the ceremony.

#### Bthuand Mzahnch (fl. before 1E 700)
[Bthuand Mzahnch](Lore_Bthuand_Mzahnch.md) was a prominent [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [smith](Lore_Smithing.md), [tonal architect](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) and scholar. He worked under the leadership of [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md) alongside other notable figures such as [Dahrk Mezalf](Lore_Dahrk_Mezalf.md). His contributions to the Dwemer's technological advancements were significant.

#### Bujold the Intrepid (fl. 4E 201)
[Bujold the Intrepid](Lore_Bujold_the_Intrepid.md), also known as **Bujold the Unworthy**, was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) chieftain of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) who lived in the third century of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

She became Chieftain of Thirsk after slaying the great beast of Ilfark and presenting its stomach as a battle trophy, at some point before or during [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).

Circa 4E 201, under her leadership, the occupants of the Thirsk Mead Hall had succumbed to a preference for feasting and mead over martial pursuits, resulting in the decline of their physical prowess and unpreparedness. This was exploited by a clan of [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md), who invaded Thirsk and forcibly displaced the residents from their headquarters. Bujold, acknowledging her failure to maintain the martial prowess of her people, led her comrades in a retreat from their abode in the aftermath of the Rieklings' assault.

#### Sir Byric of the Flame (fl. 2E 542)
**Sir** [Byric](Lore_Byric.md) **of the Flame** was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [knight](Lore_Breton_Knightly_Orders.md). In an act of valor, Sir Byric set the fields surrounding Alcaire Castle ablaze to repel [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) invaders in [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542). This heroic feat led to the renaming of the [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md) Knights to the [Knights of the Flame](Lore_Knights_of_the_Flame.md), a tribute to Sir Byric's courageous and determined actions. A [monument](Lore_Statues.md) stood behind Alcaire Castle, honoring his heroic actions.
