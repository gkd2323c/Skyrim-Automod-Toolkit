Duchess Martinne Guimard of House [Guimard](Lore_Guimard.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble who is best remembered as the leader of a consortium of [coin-barons](Lore_Coin-barons.md) that purchased the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago from the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) kings in [1E 2327](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2327). Described as the canniest ruler of her time, she amassed her vast wealth through the markets, including shrewd lending, smuggling, land acquisitions, and draconian tax policy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SH4-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SDM-2)</sup>

When Martinne acquired the Systres for an undisclosed amount of money, her star rose higher at court than even she had hoped. Guimard, along with several of her contemporaries visited the Systres in [1E 2328](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2328) for an official induction ceremony and dubbed the largest island "[High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md)," in honor of the Bretons' ancestral homeland, [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Although House Guimard sought to exploit the islands' natural resources, they quickly learned that the true value of the archipelago was cultural. Given its inhabitants were thoroughly Bretonic in language, culture, and custom, combined with the discovery of druidic ruins on several islands, fixed the Systres in High Rock's collective imagination as an untouched cradle of Breton history, occupied by Bretons and now owned by Bretons.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SH4-1)</sup>

House Guimard ruled the region for a century and a half, and even a [statue](Lore_Statues.md) of Duchess Martinne was built on High Isle at some point before the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), however they eventually fell from power after a failed marriage proposal and ambitious House [Mantel](Lore_Mantel.md) took ownership of the islands.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SH4-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SH5-3)</sup>
