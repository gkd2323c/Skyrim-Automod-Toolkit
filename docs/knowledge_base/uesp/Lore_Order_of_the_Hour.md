# Order of the Hour


## Overview
The Order of the Hour has historically been based out of [Cyrodiil's](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md), but it has also operated chapters in other provinces to carry out its sacred mission. The Order's first headquarters, now known as Knightsgrave, was established on the hills around the [Brena River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brena_River), north of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and west of [Sutch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sutch). By the middle of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Knightsgrave had been abandoned in favor of another site named the Enclave of the Hourglass, which was built further inland, directly south of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and the [Gold Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold_Road). The knightly order's code of conduct was formed during its second iteration, formed by [Cavor Merula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cavor_Merula) and his followers, [Amminus Entius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amminus_Entius) and [Justia Desticus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Justia_Desticus). Desticus was the original First Sword of Merula's order, standing as his right-hand and forming the *"Rigors of Akatosh,"* which are eight traits that the knights must aspire to.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-So JD-5)</sup> All the while, Entius, the order's first quartermaster, created an infrastructure that provided the proper benefits for the knights in Kvatch such as proper equipment and training.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-So AE-6)</sup>

The highest rank in the order is the Paladin, whose armor is made from [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md). Much like any other knightly order, the Order of the Hour focuses on several skills, which determine a knight's ranking in the order. Some of these include archery, proper etiquette, the long blade, and medical knowledge. They even focus on languages such as [Dragonish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonish) and [Giantish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Giantish).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFKnights-7)</sup> The weapons and arms of the Order are designed to embody the virtues of the old Empire of Cyrodiil. Aspects of their armor are inspired by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) of the Second Empire, like their chest pieces and helmets. Their bows are made from the [ash](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash) and yew trees, courtesy of the [Colovian West](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Colovian_West).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CM40Oot H-8)</sup>

In order to honor the memory of the founder, members of the order wear magically protective suits. Those suits were used for centuries.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DM-9)</sup>

## History

### Ennon Decan's Iteration
The Order of the Hour traces its origins to the early years of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), when it was founded by [Ennon Decan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ennon_Decan). It remained active throughout the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) up until the dawn of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) when it fell into obscurity. Decan built his headquarters on the hills of the Gold Coast, in an ancient cave where Akatosh worship was once prevalent, possibly from the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TBDJ2-10)</sup> The site was converted into a stronghold for the Order, a place to train and even pray on the words of Akatosh. When Decan passed away, the Order built statues honoring the fallen and expanded the fort even more. But eventually, this first phase of the Order had disbanded and fallen into obscurity at the coming of a new era. The fort was locked away and given to the Chantry for safekeeping.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Oot H-4)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-KLo L-11)</sup>

### Cavor Merula's Iteration
The second phase of the Order of the Hour was created in [2E 432](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_432) by the warrior-priest, [Cavor Merula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cavor_Merula) in response to the fall of the Second Empire.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Longhouse)</sup> Merula formed the order from Kvatch, out of fear for the safety of the Akatosh Chantry and its many historic and religious memorabilia. He gathered a group of warrior-priests to defend the cathedral, and thus the Order of the Hour was reborn. One of Cavor's first acts was to dedicate the old fort to the previous incarnation of the Order and to bless the dead that haunted the ruins. These ruins became Knightsgrave and Merula built the new headquarters, the Enclave of the Hourglass found south of Kvatch.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-KLo L-11)</sup> Although, the Divine retribution that Merula feared never came to fruition, and the Order of the Hour expanded to an elite militia sworn to protect the Cathedral, more ceremonial than militaristic, only called at the direst situation.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Oot H-4)</sup>

There have been several stories regarding the Order of the Hour and their protection of Kvatch in the span of a little over a century. In [2E 437](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_437), the [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm) infested the city of Kvatch, but they were swiftly dispatched by the warrior-priest, [Rusio Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rusio_Olo), who killed eight cultists and the leader of the enclave single-handedly.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-So RO-12)</sup> In [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467), the Kvatch Bread Riots raged across the county-seat and the fearful common-folk were enraged by the Chantry, believing that they were doing nothing to fix the situation. An angry mob, with the intent to inflict damage on the chantry, formed at the steps of the cathedral, only to be encountered by the Order of the Hour. Sister Severa slaughtered half of the crowd before any damage appeared on the cathedral. The Primate prayed for the end of the red wheat blight, and by the end of the season, the blight died out. By [2E 480](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_480), the Primate of Akatosh created a writ that authorized the Order to form an army and protect the city in cases of religious emergencies. Once the situation was dealt with, the Order returned to their regular duties. One such occasion was a siege from an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) horde led by the marauder Baz Swordbreaker.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Oot H-4)</sup>

### Primate Artorius Ponticus and the Dark Brotherhood
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/ON-npc-Red_Sails_Cutthroat_03.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Red_Sails_Cutthroat_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Red_Sails_Cutthroat_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Red_Sails_Cutthroat_03.jpg) The Order of the Hour recruiting Red Sails into their order [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-npc-Primate_Artorius.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Primate_Artorius.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Primate_Artorius.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Primate_Artorius.jpg) Primate Artorius Ponticus (ESO) The Order of the Hour grew greatly after the collapse of the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) in 2E 580. After the Empire's collapse, the Akatosh Chantry sought to expand the Order of the Hour. Primate [Artorius Ponticus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Artorius_Ponticus) ordered the Hour Commander Marcus Scipio to bolster his ranks beyond the Kvatch city limits. The expansion of the Order of the Hour caused it to became part of the triad of political powers in the Gold Coast, along with the Court of Kvatch and the Court of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Oot H-4)</sup> However, things changed when the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) emerged in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), as the Brotherhood waged an underground war with the Order of the Hour and assassinated several key figures (i.e., Lord Quintus Jarol, Commander Marcus Scipio, and Grand Sermonizer Fithia at-Himah). All the while, the Order's First Sword, [The Black Dragon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Black_Dragon) hunted down members of the Brotherhood's chapter. Ultimately, everything came to an end when the remaining Dark Brotherhood members stormed the Cathedral of Akatosh and slew Primate Artorius.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESODB-13)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), the Order of the Hour was serving the Akatosh Chantry in their chapters in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay). At that time, a member of the Order had killed one of the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) from the Chantry's sacred grove, in a failed attempt to free them from their clutches. Forced to go into hiding, the Chantry sent one of their [own](Lore_The_Agent.md) to assassinate the dragonslayer.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DFAt D-14)</sup>

Amid the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) was recruited by Order of the Hour priest [Ilav Dralgoner](Lore_Ilav_Dralgoner.md) of Kvatch to investigate the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins of [Beldaburo](Lore_Beldaburo.md), which the priest believed to harbor an evil force causing disappearances across the Gold Coast. When the Hero discovered a [time wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md) within the ruins that obstructed further exploration, Ilav concluded that the evil was utilizing an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) that had been stolen from the Order in recent years. The culprit was a lich named Corelian, who had been using the missing victims in magical experiments. Corelian had sought out the Order of the Hour's hidden Enclave and stolen the Elder Scroll it held in order to create the time wound, travel back in time, and prevent their past self from undergoing the maddening transformation into a lich, a misguided plan that threatened to collapse reality itself. Empowered with a divine blessing provided by the priest, the Hero of Kvatch breached the time wound in Beldaburo, slew the lich, and recovered the Elder Scroll. Ilav sensed that time had indeed been healed by this intervention, and in gratitude, he inducted the Hero of Kvatch into the Order of the Hour as a paladin.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONCD-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

### Fourth Era
The order continued their service in the Iliac Bay, specifically the Kingdom of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), well into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). In [4E 188](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_188), the head of the Order was Markam Hawksmire, who was known for being corrupt.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LGKTH-19)</sup> He was assassinated by an associate of the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-LGD-20)</sup>
