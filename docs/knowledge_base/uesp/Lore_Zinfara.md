## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl%2C_Nirncrux.png/200px-ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl%2C_Nirncrux.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl,_Nirncrux.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl,_Nirncrux.png) Bowl of nirncrux powder Very little is known of Zinfara's life. They were a legendary figure of the Duraki oral tradition, dating back to the middle Merethic Era. They are mentioned in *The Footsteps of Shezarr*, a text by [Sister Priscia Stolvo](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Sister_Priscia_Stolvo) that reexamines the worship of Shezarr among early Nedes through a modern [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) lens. According to one tale, Zinfara was taught by a mysterious stranger—believed to be Shezarr—how to call nirncrux from the roots of the mountains and how to use stoneworking knowledge acquired from the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOS-1)</sup>

The practice of using nirncrux has survived among both the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONDSS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONF-3)</sup> and [Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md) in modern times.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LL-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LTEB-5)</sup>
