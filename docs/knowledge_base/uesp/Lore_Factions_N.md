## Naga-Kur
The [Naga-Kur](Lore_Naga-Kur.md) (known as the **Dead-Water Tribe** in Tamrielic) is a tribe of [Nagas](Lore_Naga.md), a rare breed of [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md). Their village is known as [Dead-Water Village](Lore_Dead-Water_Village.md) and they control vast swaths of northern [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) and are greatly feared by the Argonians of the surrounding villages.

## Natives
**Natives** were an insurgent group based in [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) County that operated sometime in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). Known for their distinctive arrows and modes of attacks, such as beheading the people they ambushed. They were allegedly funded by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIC-1)</sup>

## Natural Order
The [Natural Order](Lore_Natural_Order.md) was a cult devoted to the worship of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Pestilence, [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md). They were a venerated group filled with nobles and aristocrats who secretly followed the Prince. Little is known about them, but Larina Hanus, a scholar on the Daedric cults, claimed that they are on the opposite end of the spectrum of demographics, in comparison to the Diseased, another cult of Peryite, which conversely consists almost entirely of self-described social castaways.

## Nerano
The [Nerano](Lore_Nerano.md) clan is an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) that lives in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They are one of the thirty revered families of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and are a sub-faction of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md). The **Nerano Ancestral Tomb** is located in the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) region, west of [Vos](Lore_Vos.md) and south of Kushtashpi. **Nerano Manor** is a lavish residence in [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) owned by noble member of the family.

## Nerevarine Cult
The [Nerevarine Cult](Lore_Nerevarine_Cult.md), associated with the [Urshilaku Ashlanders](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md), believes that the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) —the three god-kings [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) —are false gods whose power is stolen from the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md). Instead of worshipping the Tribunal, the cult is devoted to a mortal leader who died of his wounds after victory in the Battle of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md): Lord Indoril [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md). Vivec was his lieutenant, Almalexia was his queen, and Sotha Sil was his wizardly advisor. The cult also believes that, in the end, Nerevar will return in a new incarnation (the Nerevarine or Incarnate) to reunite the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and bring down the gods they see to be false.

## Night Knives
The [Night Knives](Lore_Night_Knives.md) were a minor cult of [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) worshipers headquartered in Western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) sometime during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). They were known to have techniques in rimming their tables with umbral frames; shadows made up of coherence energies specific to Namira and/or [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).

## Nightblades
[Nightblades](Lore_Nightblades.md) are opportunistic adventurers known for their ability for getting in and out of trouble. They wield magic and use it to enhance their mobility, concealment, and stealthy close combat. They move swiftly and with haste by darkness, and use spells to benefit their circumstances. Nightblades thrive on conflict and misfortune, spell and shadow are their friends. Believed to be the most intriguing and shadowy of people, they trust their luck, cunning, and intelligence to survive, combining the skills of thieves with the powers of mages. They have a sinister reputation, as many nightblades act as thieves, enforcers, murderers, or secret agents. The mastery of their arts allows them to thrive as infiltrators and spies. Their presence is seldom seen but certainly felt.

Trickery, illusion, stealth, and agility make for a deadly combination, giving nightblades an edge in war. Nightblades also wield **Night Magic**, which not only grants them additional magicka, but increases the accuracy of their spells. They are capable of creating night runes, which ravage the souls of foes while siphoning life-force from them. In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), nightblades were known for their use of [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md), of which they are said to draw out the most through the art of Siphoning. With it, they call to the blood of their victims, cursing and draining them in manners considered diabolical. Assassination is more than just an action for Nightblades, instead it is both a martial and magical skill-set. Despite shadow stepping being commonplace in a nightblade's repertoire during this time, [teleportation spells](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md#Short_Range_Spells) were seemingly no longer associated with them come the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). The term nightblade was considered by magical educators a fancy title with little relevance.

## Nighthollow Clan
The [Nighthollow Clan](Lore_Nighthollow_Clan.md), also known as the **Night Lords**, was one of the first [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) clans to emerge, and thus is almost as old as the curse itself. They inhabited a section of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md) located beneath [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). They feasted on the power of the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md) at the risk of being consumed by it. Their vampiric features were masked by pallid grey skin and deep black eyes, a side-effect of their reliance on the Heart. The clan had their own written language, recorded in a script known as Void Runes.

## Nightingale Trinity
The [Nightingale Trinity](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md), usually called simply the **Nightingales**, are a trio of highly skilled thieves dedicated to the service of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). Members of the order take the Oath: the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md) must remain at the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md), the Temple of Nocturnal, which must be protected from all threats, even in death, until Nocturnal accepts the spirit of a Nightingale into the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md), her plane of Oblivion. In return, Nightingales use the abilities granted to them for whatever they see fit to do in life.

Members are typically chosen from the higher echelons of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md). Although there is no formal association between the two factions and most guild members are unaware of this arrangement, the Trinity is usually very influential on how the guild operates. Nightingales claim they created the guild phrase "walk with the shadows." Contrary to popularized misconceptions, the Oath is considered a business transaction rather than a religious matter. While most members certainly take the Oath very seriously, they are not worshipping Nocturnal as a deity, but respecting her as the patron of thieves.

## Nightmare Host
The [Nightmare Host](Lore_Nightmare_Host.md) was an undead horde led by a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) consisting of [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md), [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), and [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md). They served the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) and fought in the [Battle of Wightmoor](Lore_Battle_of_Wightmoor.md), which was one of many conflicts during the [greater conflict](Lore_Camoran_Usurper%27s_Invasion.md) in which the Camoran Usurper invaded western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

## Nisswo
A [Nisswo](Lore_Nisswo.md) (literally "**Nothing-Speaker**" in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md)) is a priest of a caste dedicated to [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md). They are native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and their ranks consist primarily of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).

Nisswos have served two very different roles in Argonian society over the years, shifting from a ruling class in the pre- [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) era of Black Marsh to a priest class. In the modern day, Nisswo priests are collectively known as the 'Clutch of Nisswo', and share the goal of spreading the many truths of Sithis, no matter how contradictory they might be.

## Ninth Legion
The [Ninth Legion](Lore_Ninth_Legion.md) is a legion of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), the militant arm of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). The Ninth Legion was for the longest time defunct when it disappeared deep in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and cemented themselves in local legend as the **Lost Legion**. By the mid-Third Era, they were restored and played a minor role in Emperor [Uriel Septim V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md)'s conquest of the eastern kingdoms between [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md).

## Norvayn
The [Norvayn](Lore_Norvayn.md) clan is an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) that lives in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They are one of the thirty revered families of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and are a sub-faction of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md). The **Norvayn Ancestral Tomb** is located in the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) region, northwest of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) and northeast of Hlormaren Stronghold.

## Nycotic Cult
The [Nycotic Cult](Lore_Nycotic_Cult.md) was an organization of [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worshippers that were active during the middle of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and had activities to the west of the region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), with bases both in the Shulk Ore Mine and in and around the ancient Hlormaren Stronghold. Despite being daedric worshippers, they pledged their allegiance to none of the known princes and instead worshipped a being known only as "Nycot" which is also the origin of the name of the organization and could be a mistranslation of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), the Prince of Bargains.
