*Jobasha reads books, buys books, sells books. What more can Jobasha say? Jobasha knows a little about everything. So you ask, and maybe Jobasha will talk.*
—Jobasha<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup> Jobasha was a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) scholar and bookseller active in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). A staunch opponent of slavery, he was an abolitionist associated with the [Twin Lamps](Lore_Twin_Lamps.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMP-2)</sup><sup>:213</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/MW-interior-Jobasha%27s_Rare_Books_02.jpg/200px-MW-interior-Jobasha%27s_Rare_Books_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-interior-Jobasha%27s_Rare_Books_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-interior-Jobasha%27s_Rare_Books_02.jpg) Jobasha's Rare Books [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/MW-flora-Charles.jpg/200px-MW-flora-Charles.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-flora-Charles.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-flora-Charles.jpg) Charles the Plant It is unknown exactly when Jobasha opened his bookstore, but Jobasha's Rare Books, located in the Foreign Quarter of Vivec City, became a repository for a remarkable collection of literature. The shop boasted a wide selection of rare and obscure works, considered by some to be the finest on Vvardenfell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-JRB-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Huleeya-4)</sup> Jobasha himself was well educated and well spoken individual, with deep knowledge of local affairs. He also held passionate moral and political views, which he shared freely, and as a result, both he and his bookshop inspired strong reactions from some locals.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Huleeya-4)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), [the Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), acting on the orders of Caius Cosades, went to meet Huleeya, an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) informant and member of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md). Huleeya was a close friend of Jobasha, and the two relocated to Jobasha's bookstore, where they were able to speak more freely.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Huleeya-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VI-5)</sup> There, the Argonian informant provided the Nerevarine with notes concerning the history of Nerevar.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VI-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NFH-6)</sup> Another informant, [Mehra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mehra) Milo, insisted that the Nerevarine needs to obtain a copy of *[Progress of Truth](Lore_Progress_of_Truth.md)* and suggested that local booksellers in Vivec City might carry it. While it is unknown exactly how the Nerevarine acquired the book, Jobasha was known to possess a copy and was willing to provide it to them.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VI-5)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup>

Jobasha's paths crossed with the Nerevarine on several occasions, most often through his assistance in procuring rare books for them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup> It is possible that Jobasha offered *[Confessions of a Dunmer Skooma-Eater](Lore_Confessions_of_a_Skooma-Eater.md)* for sale to the Nerevarine, who was willing to assist Ahnassi in helping her former partner, J'Dhannar, overcome his [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) addiction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AASF-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GDC2-8)</sup> On another occasion, it is possible that the Nerevarine purchased *[Boethiah's Glory](Lore_Boethiah%27s_Glory.md)* from him while pursuing the restoration of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md)'s shrine at [Khartag Point](Lore_Khartag_Point.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BQ-9)</sup> At some point, the Nerevarine may have sought rare tomes on behalf of [Baladas Demnevanni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baladas_Demnevanni), and Jobasha was potentially among those who supplied them with his copies of *[Antecedents of Dwemer Law](Lore_Antecedents_of_Dwemer_Law.md)* and *[Chronicles of Nchuleft](Lore_Chronicles_of_Nchuleft.md)*, along with guidance on where to seek another rare tome *[Nchunak's Fire and Faith](Lore_Nchunak%27s_Fire_and_Faith.md)*. Alternatively, the Nerevarine may have obtained these works elsewhere through illicit means.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DB-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GDC-11)</sup> *Chronicles of Nchuleft* were also sought out by Edwinna Elbert of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CON-12)</sup> While searching for *[Vampires of Vvardenfell, v. II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampires_of_Vvardenfell,_v_II)* on behalf of Skink-in-Tree's-Shade of the Mages Guild, the Nerevarine learned that Jobasha possibly owned a copy of the rare, banned tome. Jobasha was willing to trade it, though he admitted that the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) strongly disapproved of the book and could punish him for possessing or selling it if they became aware. The Nerevarine could also obtain the volume elsewhere, though.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-VOV-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VOVT-14)</sup> Another book sought by Skink-in-Tree's-Shade, *[Galur Rithari's Papers](Lore_Galur_Rithari%27s_Papers.md)*, was not in Jobasha's possession, but he informed the Nerevarine about its exact location.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-GRP-15)</sup>

Jobasha was also an associate of the [Twin Lamps](Lore_Twin_Lamps.md), an abolitionist secret organization dedicated to freeing the [slaves](Lore_Slavery.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TL-16)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMP-2)</sup><sup>:213</sup> According to some accounts, the Nerevarine freed many of the enslaved people of the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and Jobasha supported this cause by offering them a collection of valuable tomes with important practical knowledge, including *[The Armorer's Challenge](Lore_The_Armorer%27s_Challenge.md)*, *[Realizations of Acrobacy](Lore_Realizations_of_Acrobacy.md)*, and *[The Marksmanship Lesson](Lore_The_Marksmanship_Lesson.md)*.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-FTS-17)</sup>

Jobasha was once interviewed alongside fellow booksellers Dorisa Darvel and Codus Callonus. During the interview, he offered a variety of insights into Khajiiti culture, discussing topics such as their [names and honorifics](Lore_Khajiit_Names.md), the [martial art of Claw-Dances](Lore_Claw-Dances.md), and [Khajiiti furstocks along with their connections to the Lunar Lattice](Lore_Khajiit.md#Morphology).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-IWTB-18)</sup> He also provided notes about the phrases and expressions in his native language, [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPAE-19)</sup> On another occasion, he shared information during an interview, once more focusing mainly on the Ta'agra language, though he also discussed [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) tomes, the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), the future of slavery in Morrowind, and the identity of the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md), among other topics.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-JI-20)</sup>

[Frathen Drothan](Lore_Frathen_Drothan.md) purchased a damaged copy of *[Treatise on Ayleidic Cities](Lore_Treatise_on_Ayleidic_Cities.md)*, as well as *[The Lusty Argonian Maid](Lore_The_Lusty_Argonian_Maid.md)* from Jobasha prior to his actions in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Receipt-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-UMR-22)</sup>

A unique [plant](Lore_Flora.md) known as [Charles the Plant](Lore_Charles_the_Plant.md), a source of meteor slime, could be found in his store.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-CTP-23)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMP-2)</sup><sup>:238</sup> During an interview, he cryptically asked his interviewer, Sinder Velvin, if they knew anyone who needed a potted plant.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-JI-20)</sup>

## Notes
- Jobasha acquired the tome *The Dreadful Theft of the Sun's Dusk Ale* by Rascien Wickersly, noting that a nervous Breton once sold him autobiographical fiction. He believed that this book may be one such example.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TDTOTSDA-24)</sup> The same Breton also sold him *Minutes of the Lusty Argonian Historical Society*.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TDTOTSDA-24)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MOAS-25)</sup>
- Jobasha was a good friend of [Cherim](Lore_Cherim.md), the famous weaver. He once cryptically revealed that many sermons hide their secrets and mentioned the idea of studying Sermon Zero, as well as Cherim's exquisite tapestries. When asked if the Cherim who makes tapestries and the Cherim of Sermon Zero were the same, Jobasha did not deny, but also said that it's "a common misunderstanding".<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-DGP-26)</sup><sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-TSZA-27)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-Sermon-28)</sup> *[Cherim's Heart of Anequina](Lore_Cherim%27s_Heart_of_Anequina.md)* was among the books sold by Jobasha.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jobasha-1)</sup>
- Jobasha was asked by [Kier-Jo Chorvak](Lore_Kier-Jo_Chorvak.md) to relay a few messages. He analyzed some passages but ultimately dismissed others as meaningless ramblings.<sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-MAS-29)</sup>
- Jobasha was considered specialist on Beastfolk mythologies, historical takes, and intrigue.<sup>[[UOL 9]](#cite_note-TPBP-30)</sup>
- The developer [Douglas Goodall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Douglas_Goodall) frequently used the alias Jobasha, and often roleplayed as the character.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-DGP-26)</sup><sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-TSZA-27)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-Sermon-28)</sup>
