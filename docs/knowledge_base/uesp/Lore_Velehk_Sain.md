Velehk Sain, also known as the **Pirate King of the Abecean**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PKA-1)</sup> and the **Destroyer**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BLVD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Suraj-3)</sup> is an infamous [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) pirate known for terrorizing the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) to the west of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He and his crew are feared for their ruthless and sadistic raiding of [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) and [elven](Lore_Mer.md) vessels alike. He is the **Captain of the Caterwaul**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Suraj-3)</sup> a distinctive ship that is the subject to a legend that claims that blood on the water signals his approach.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PKA-1)</sup> His desire to sail the seas of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) eclipses even his duty to his master, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Above all, Velekh yearns for conquest and the thrill of plunder.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCL-4)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
Velehk Sain was already an established pirate on [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) by the time of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), when he was summoned to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) to serve Molag Bal. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), he was transported back to Tamriel to protect various dolmens. However, he was quickly banished by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and the [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchor.md) were lifted.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

After the end of the Planemeld, Velekh was ordered to go out to sea to retrieve the [Crown of Bones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown_of_Bones) from Captain One-Eye in an effort to regain Molag Bal’s favor. Whether One-Eye had kept or destroyed the artifact remains unknown. Upon learning that she was being pursued, One-Eye commandeered the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) vessel *Bonnie Spriggan* and set sail across Coldharbour’s azure sea, engaging in several close encounters with the Pirate King along the way. On one occasion, One-Eye's crew boldly boarded the *Caterwaul* during a heated sea battle, looted part of its stores, and escaped.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONBSL-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCL-4)</sup> Velekh's crew had managed to purge a fair number of the undead serving One-Eye, but desperate mortals lost in Coldharbour sought refuge aboard her vessel and replenished her numbers. Though One-Eye’s supplies were dwindling, Velekh’s fortunes shifted when a vortex tore open in the sea of Coldharbour, spilling into the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md). And for all the ill luck it brought, it offered Velekh exactly what he craved: a chance to be unleashed upon the seas of mortals once more and plunder their fortunes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCL-4)</sup>

Now at the coastline of the [Solstice](Lore_Solstice.md), an island where the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) worked to carry out the Reverse Planemeld, Captain One-Eye sought to banish Velekh back to Coldharbour to prevent him from joining up with the rest of Molag Bal's forces. With the Vestige’s aid, she brought about the sabotage of the Caterwaul and rallied those long oppressed by Velekh to defect to her side. These actions, along with the slaying of Velekh's quartermaster, Xynark, drew the Pirate King’s full wrath. Velekh fled to sea, and the One-Eye’s crew pursued. When the *Bonnie Spriggan* overtook the *Cauterwaul*, the two ships clashed, but the *Spriggan* ’s crew prevailed, banishing Velekh’s forces back to Coldharbour.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOArd-7)</sup>

### Fourth Era
He would later return to Tamriel and continue his piracy. Velehk the Destroyer was said to have been sighted and slain by [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) on Tamriel circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BLVD-2)</sup>

At some point in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), four students of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) attempted to summon Velehk Sain using four enchanted rings and a gauntlet sculpture. They all died from severe conjurer's burn and their souls became eternally trapped in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). The College assumed their summoning had failed and put the four rings into safekeeping.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> However, the Dremora had in fact been successfully bound to the sculpture, and in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) broke the binding by returning the rings to the gauntlet. Velehk Sain attempted to bargain by promising to reveal a stash of magically hidden treasure along the coast of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), which his crew had hidden after sinking an Imperial galleon in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) many years previous. It is unknown if the Dragonborn released the Dremora to return to his ship on the Abecean, or banished him back to the [Void](Lore_Void.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
