**Note:** The Third Era of Tamriel is also referred to as the *[Septim Era](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md),* *Imperial Era*, or *Third Age*.

## First Century

3E 0 — Beginning of the Third Era

- The Third Era is proclaimed by Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) at the end of [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), after [the unification](Lore_Tiber_Wars.md) of all provinces in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). However alternative sources contradict this, indicating that the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) did not end until the final year of the millenium (ca. 2E 999, which is 103 years after 2E 896).

3E 38 — Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) dies, Emperor [Pelagius Septim I](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_I.md) is crowned

- Tiber Septim, conqueror and ruler of all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for 38 years, dies and is succeeded by his grandson, Pelagius.

3E 41 — Emperor [Pelagius Septim I](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_I.md) dies, Empress [Kintyra Septim I](Lore_Kintyra_Septim_I.md) is crowned

- Emperor Pelagius is assassinated by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). With no direct heirs to the throne, the crown passes to the departed Emperor's first cousin.

3E 53 — Empress [Kintyra Septim I](Lore_Kintyra_Septim_I.md) dies, Emperor [Uriel Septim I](Lore_Uriel_Septim_I.md) is crowned

- Empress Kintyra dies. Kintyra's son is crowned after her death, the first Emperor of Tamriel to use the Imperial name Uriel.

3E 64 — Emperor [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_Septim_I.md) dies, Emperor [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_Septim_II.md) is crowned

- Emperor Uriel I dies and the kingdom is taken over by his son, Emperor Uriel II, the Fifth Emperor. Tragically, the rule of Uriel II is cursed with blights, plagues, and insurrections. The tenderheartedness inherited from his father does not serve the land well, and little justice is done.

3E 82 — Emperor [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_Septim_II.md) dies, Emperor [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_II.md) is crowned

- Uriel II dies after an 18-year reign. The Emperor is succeeded by his son, Pelagius II. Pelagius II inherits not only the throne, but the debt from his father's poor financial and judicial management.

3E 98, 15th of Evening Star — Emperor [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md) dies

- The Emperor dies in the waning days of the year after a seventeen-year reign, and his successor isn't appointed until the following year.

3E 99 — Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) is crowned

- Emperor [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md) dies and his son, Antiochus, inherits the kingdom. His reign is rife with civil wars, surpassing even that of his grandfather, [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_Septim_II.md).

## Second Century

3E 110 — [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md)

- During the reign of Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md), the province of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) is nearly lost to the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). The united alliance of the kings of Summerset and Antiochus only manage to defeat King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) of the island-kingdom of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) due to a freak storm. Legend later credits the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) of the [Isle of Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) with the magic behind the storm.

3E 120 — Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) dies, Empress [Kintyra Septim II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) is crowned

- Princess Kintyra is chosen by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) over King [Uriel Mantiarco](Lore_Uriel_Mantiarco.md), despite the lobbying efforts of his mother [Potema](Lore_Potema.md).<sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_nb4)</sup>

3E 121 — [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md) is proclaimed Emperor

- The [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) itself is taken over in only a fortnight, as two other attacks draw away Imperial forces. Uriel III is proclaimed Emperor of Tamriel. He abandons his father's surname of Mantiarco and takes the Septim name. Empress [Kintyra Septim II](Lore_Kintyra_Septim_II.md) is taken captive in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) as [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) takes sides between Uriel III and his uncles.

3E 121 — [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md)

- This war was between the three surviving children of [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_II.md): [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md), and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), and their respective offspring. Potema, of course, supports her son [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md), and has the support of all of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and northern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). With the efforts of Cephorus and Magnus, all of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) eventually supports them. [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) were divided, but most kings supported Cephorus and Magnus.

3E 123, 23rd of Frost Fall — Empress [Kintyra Septim II](Lore_Kintyra_Septim_II.md) dies in her cell

- Although reported to have died in 3E 114, the captive Empress Kintyra dies in secret in her cell. The 23rd of Frostfall is later named the day of Broken Diamond in her memory.

3E 127 — Emperor [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md) dies, Emperor [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) is crowned

- Uriel III is captured at the Battle of Ichidag in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). En route to his trial in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), a mob overtakes his carriage and burns him alive within it. His captor and uncle continues on to the Imperial City, and by common acclaim becomes Emperor Cephorus I. Cephorus' reign is marked by nothing but war. The Empire acquiesces to demands for greater autonomy from the nobility of all the outlying provinces except [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). It takes Cephorus an additional ten years of constant warfare to defeat his sister [Potema](Lore_Potema.md).

3E 137 — [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), Queen of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), dies

- The so-called Wolf Queen of Solitude dies during the siege of her castle.

3E 140 — Emperor [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) dies, Emperor [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) is crowned

- Cephorus never had the time to marry, so it is his brother, the fourth child of [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_II.md), who assumes the throne. The Emperor Magnus is elderly, and the business of punishing the traitorous kings of the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) drains much of his health.

3E 145, 8th of Second Seed — Emperor [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) dies, Emperor [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) is crowned

- Pelagius leaves the throne of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) to his sister Jolethe. Almost from the start, Pelagius III, sometimes called Pelagius the Mad, becomes noted for his eccentric behavior. He embarrasses dignitaries, offends his vassal kings, and, on one occasion, marks the end of a grand ball by attempting to hang himself.

3E 153, 2nd of Suns Dawn — Emperor [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) dies, Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) is crowned

- After being committed to an asylum due to his madness, on a warm night after a brief fever, the 34-year-old Emperor Pelagius III dies in his cell at the Temple of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), on the Isle of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md). Katariah, Duchess of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and Empress Regent following the commitment of her husband, formally becomes the controversial new Empress. Katariah's forty-six year reign is later remembered one of the most glorious in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s history (assertions to the contrary being dismissed as racist). Katariah travels extensively throughout the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), as no Emperor had since [Tiber](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s day. She repairs much of the damage that broken alliances and bungled diplomacy had created. The people of Tamriel come to love their Empress, though many nobles are not as fond.

## Third Century

3E 200 — Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) dies, Emperor [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md) is crowned

- Katariah's death in a minor skirmish in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) later becomes a favorite topic of conspiracy-minded historians. It later comes to light that a disenfranchised branch of the [Septim family](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) is involved. When Cassynder assumes the throne at the death of his mother, he is already middle-aged and in poor health.<sup>[[nb 5]](#intnote_nb5)</sup>

3E 202 — Emperor [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md) dies, Emperor [Uriel IV](Lore_Uriel_Septim_IV.md) is crowned

- Cassynder dies after a two-year reign. Uriel Lariat, Cassynder's half-brother and the child of [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) I and her Imperial consort, abdicates the throne of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) to reign as Emperor Uriel IV. Legally, he was Uriel Septim IV: Cassynder had adopted him into the family when he had become King of Wayrest. Nevertheless, to many of the people of Tamriel, Uriel IV is a bastard child of Katariah. Uriel does not possess the dynamism of his mother, and his long forty-five year reign becomes a hotbed of sedition.

3E 247 — Emperor [Uriel IV](Lore_Uriel_Septim_IV.md) dies, Emperor [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md) is crowned

- The [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md)'s last victory over Uriel IV is posthumous: they vote to disinherit [Andorak](Lore_Andorak.md), Uriel IV's son. They also vote to coronate a cousin more closely related to the original Septim line as Emperor Cephorus II. Cephorus, formerly a [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) king, battles those loyal to Andorak for nine years. The Council controversially grants Andorak the [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) kingdom of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) to end the war.

3E 249 — [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) invades

- During the reign of [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md), the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) leads an army of [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and [undead](Lore_Undead.md) warriors on a rampage through [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), conquering the whole province in two years, then turns to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Cephorus II sends more and more mercenaries into Hammerfell to stop the Usurper's northward march, but they are bribed, turned into undead, or slaughtered. Animosity grows against the seemingly ineffective Empire.

3E 267 — [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) is defeated

- Baron [Othrok](Lore_Othrok.md) and his less legendary allies, the rulers of [Ykalon](Lore_Ykalon.md), [Phrygias](Lore_Phrygias.md), and [Kambria](Lore_Kambria.md), assembles the greatest navy to date along the [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) edge of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), and defeats the Usurper's forces in the [Battle of Firewaves](Lore_Battle_of_Firewaves.md).
- [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), son of the Camoran Usurper and future leader of the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md), was born in the midst of this battle.

3E 268 — Emperor [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md) is crowned

- Uriel V changes the perception of a weak Empire by embarking on a series of invasions almost from the moment he takes the throne.

3E 288, 23rd of Rain's Hand — Emperor [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md) invades [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md)

- Uriel V sets sail with the largest fleet assembled in recorded history to invade the continent of Akavir. They arrive in [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(place).md) six weeks later.

3E 290 — Emperor [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md) dies, Emperor [Uriel VI](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VI.md) is crowned

- Uriel V is killed in [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) on the battlefield of [Ionith](Lore_Ionith.md). His five-year-old son becomes Emperor Uriel Septim VI. The consort [Thonica](Lore_Thonica.md), as Uriel VI's mother, is given a restricted Regency until Uriel VI reaches the age of majority. The [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) retains the real power, as they had since the days of [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md).

## Fourth Century

3E 320 — Emperor [Uriel VI](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VI.md) dies, Empress [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md) is crowned

- Uriel VI falls from his horse and cannot be saved by the finest Imperial healers. His beloved sister Morihatha takes the throne.

3E 339 — Empress [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md) is assassinated, Emperor [Pelagius IV](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_IV.md) is crowned

- Assassins kill Empress Morihatha, and her nephew becomes Emperor Pelagius IV.

3E 368 — Emperor [Pelagius IV](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_IV.md) dies, Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) is crowned

- Emperor Pelagius IV dies after a twenty-nine year reign, with [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) closer to unity than it had been since the days of [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_I.md). Emperor Uriel Septim VII succeeds him, and continues with the unification of Tamriel. He focuses his efforts in eastern Tamriel, and is greatly aided by his Imperial Battlemage, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md).

3E 389 — The [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) begins

- On Tirdas, the 1st of Hearthfire, Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) is betrayed by the Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) and imprisoned in a dimension of Tharn's creation.

3E 391 — [Prime Minister Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) is killed by a rebel force at [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), the rebels' leader is delcared the [King of Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md#Known_Rulers).

- Symmachus, the Prime Minister of Morrowind Province and husband to [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), is killed by [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) members and a rebel force at [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). The rebel leader is named King of Morrowind (or King of Mournhold) by the [Emperor](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

3E 395 — [Regional wars](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md#Wars_during_the_Imperial_Simulacrum.2C_3E_389_-_3E_399) rage throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)

- The [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) breaks out, the first of several major regional wars in Tamriel over the next several years.
- [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) makes a deal with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). Tharn offers Dagon control of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).

3E 397 — [Umbra' Keth](Lore_Umbra%27_Keth.md) is destroyed

- A Shadow of Conflict being created by the ongoing [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) is destroyed by an unknown [hero](Lore_Hero.md) wielding the power of the seven Star Teeth.

3E 398 — [The Apprentice](Lore_Hero.md) places second in a competition determining the next pupils of [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), and is sent to the citadel.
3E 399 — [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) is killed, bringing an end to the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md); [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) is refounded; [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) abdicates

- Right around the end of the decade, [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) fled from the [Imperial Heartland](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) under [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s rule to the city-state of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). From afar Barenziah appointed a regent to rule in her stead at [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), but then about a year later, upon marrying [King Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), Barenziah abdicates the throne to [her uncle](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md).
- A [hero](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) defeats the Battlemage in the dungeons of the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md) and frees Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) from his other-dimensional cell, ending the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in an event historians entitled "the Restoration."
- [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md) refounds the nation of Orsinium and begins fighting for its independence.
- The [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) ends.

## Fifth Century

3E 401 — [Nulfaga](Lore_Nulfaga.md) finds the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md)

- Nulfaga of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) finds the exact location of the Mantella, the heart of [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).
- [Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md), King of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) and husband to Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md), dies.

3E 417, 9th of Frost Fall — The Miracle of Peace, or the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md)

- A mysterious event takes place between the 9th and 11th of Frostfall in which the forty-four independent kingdoms, counties, baronies, and duchies surrounding the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) were condensed into four: [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), and [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).

3E 417 — The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) lose [Kagrenac's Tools](Lore_Kagrenac%27s_Tools.md)

- [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) lose the artifacts [Keening](Lore_Keening.md) and [Sunder](Lore_Sunder.md) to the minions of [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) during a battle at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) rescues them, but fails to recover the artifacts. They retreat from Red Mountain in disorder and return to their respective capitals, continuing to perform their ritual functions, but soon stop appearing in public altogether. They grow weaker without access to the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md), and because of resources required to support the [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md). The inner circle of the Temple priesthood begin to suspect the Tribunes have suffered seriously from wounds and demoralization in the wake of reverses at Red Mountain, but do not recognize the scale of the problem.

3E 421 — The [Levitation Act](Lore_Levitation_Act.md) is passed.
3E 427 — [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md)

- The [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) government, already weakened in power over questions of authority, is further threatened by the re-awakening of the ancient curse of the Blight from the giant volcano [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).
- On the 16th of Last Seed, the legendary [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) arrives in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) by order of the Emperor, destined to kill [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) and bring peace to Morrowind.

3E 427 — Discord among the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md)

- After the loss of her divine powers, [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) of the Almsivi becomes insane and kills her fellow Tribune [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md). The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) reforges [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md)'s blade - [Trueflame](Lore_Trueflame.md) - and uses it to kill the mortal Almalexia in the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).

3E 427 — [Bloodmoon Prophecy](Lore_Bloodmoon_Prophecy.md)

- As the prophecy foretold, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) performs a ritual hunt on the frozen island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), as he does once every era. The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) is chosen to play a part in it, and ultimately defeats Hircine at his own game.

3E 430 — King [Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) outlaws slavery in Morrowind, causing unrest and the houses of Indoril and Redoran to stage a revolution against him.
3E 433 — [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md)

- On the 27th of Last Seed, Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and his heirs are murdered by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md), a secret cult devoted to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). [Oblivion Gates](Lore_Oblivion_Gate.md) open all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) as Dagon begins to invade the [mortal plane](Lore_Mundus.md). [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) is destroyed.
- In [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) (which have foreseen the crisis) calls [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) from across the Empire back to Black Marsh. Led by the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), the Argonian armies defeat the invading [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).
- The [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) is destroyed by daedra.
- [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) is sacked by daedra.
- [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) goes remarkably unscathed; there's no record of any Oblivion Gates opening on the island.
- The Daedric invasion of Tamriel is ended after the Battle of Bruma, during the Battle of the Imperial City. [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), escorted by the Hero of Kvatch, transforms into the [Avatar of Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and defeats [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), ending the Third Era.

3E 433 — Count [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md) of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) reappears

- After 10 years, Corvus Umbranox returns to his wife in Anvil.
- A new [Gray Fox](Lore_The_Gray_Fox.md) leads the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md). History is rewritten by the use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), and the [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) branch of the guild flourishes.

3E 433 — Death of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)

- Mannimarco, "The King of Worms", falls in a duel in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) against the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md)'s newest Arch-Mage, with the help of the previous Arch-Mage [Hannibal Traven](Lore_Hannibal_Traven.md).

3E 433 — The [Blackwood Company](Lore_Blackwood_Company.md) is destroyed by the Fighters Guild
3E 433 — Purification of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md)

- Internal strife caused by a traitorous Speaker results in the assassination of half of the Black Hand and the decimation of the [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) sanctuary. The [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) appoints a new Listener after the traitor is killed.

3E 433 — Reformation of the [Knights of the Nine](Lore_Knights_of_the_Nine.md)

- Following daedric attacks on clergymen, an unknown pilgrim receives a vision of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), reunites the [Crusader's Relics](Lore_Crusader%27s_Relics.md), and reorganizes the Knights of the Nine.

3E 433 — The [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md)

- After a portal to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) opens in [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s curse is broken, which allows him to remain as [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md). Sheogorath's champion takes up the Mad God's mantle.
