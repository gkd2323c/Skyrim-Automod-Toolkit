[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/AR-map-Black_Gate.png/200px-AR-map-Black_Gate.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Black_Gate.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Black_Gate.png) Location of Black Gate in Morrowind Black Gate is a strategic fortress in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), located just southwest of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARDungeon Map-1)</sup> Black Gate was a stronghold utilized during the time of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), most notably during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).

In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), Black Gate was besieged by the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), around the 23rd of [Sun's Height](Lore_Sun%27s_Height.md), amidst the negotiations after the war between the Crown Prince [Juilek Cyrodiil](Lore_Juilek.md) and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> The Prince made no plans to attack Black Gate. It was instead the brainchild of his father, Emperor [Reman Cyrodiil III](Lore_Reman_III.md), and the Akaviri Potentate, [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md). Regardless, the negotiations had continued at the settlement of Ald Lambasi.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

At one point in time, Black Gate had fallen into disrepair and ruin. The [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) artifact known as the [Hammer of Gharen](Lore_Hammer_of_Gharen.md) was last known to be located in the depths of Black Gate. King [Casik](Lore_Casik.md) of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) had the [Anvil of Mithas](Lore_Anvil_of_Mithas.md) in his possession, and sought out the Hammer. The king tasked the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) with delving into Black Gate to retrieve the Hammer. With it, King Casik was able to use the two artifacts to [craft](Lore_Smithing.md) the finest armor and weapons in the realm. In return, he revealed the location of the final piece of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md), in the Dwarven Mines of [Dagoth-Ur](Lore_Dagoth-Ur_(place).md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Following his death, the Eternal Champion became somewhat of a culture hero to [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) Loyalists, many of whom would visit Black Gate as part of a pilgrimage retracing the Champion's steps.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AMM-5)</sup>

## Notes
- Black Gate is filled with unique sprites of dead warriors, with their skeletons damaged by weapons lying nearby. Whether the fortress fell to an attack or was occupied later on by a group of people that was defeated is unknown.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Arena-6)</sup>
