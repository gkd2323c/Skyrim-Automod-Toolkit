# Pyandonea

| --- | --- |
| Type | Archipelago |
| Demonym(s) | Pyandonean |

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/ON-place-Serpent%27s_Kiss.jpg/200px-ON-place-Serpent%27s_Kiss.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Serpent%27s_Kiss.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Serpent%27s_Kiss.jpg) A Pyandonean ship **Pyandonea**, meaning "the Veil of Mist"<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-1)</sup> or "the Mist-Veiled Isles",<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMo P-2)</sup> is an archipelago located to the far south of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It is home to the elusive [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), a race of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) exiled there by the ancient [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IRLS-3)</sup> For all of recorded history, [King Orgnum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgnum) has ruled the land.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1W-4)</sup> It is said to have metropolises comparable to Tamriel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VGSD-5)</sup>

The sources differ on the archipelago's location, suggesting it could lie either within the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Pearls-6)</sup> or in the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Co BS-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Grotto LS-8)</sup> which the Maormer are said to rule.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LATSFS-9)</sup> Some sources refer to Pyandonea as a continent<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ4-10)</sup> or as part of a continental system.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-1)</sup> However, other lists of the known continents of Nirn exclude Pyandonea.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1P-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TAA-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MA-13)</sup> Others refer to it as a single island.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dhulef-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Orgnum Deck-15)</sup>

## Environment
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_%28crop%29.png/200px-ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_%28crop%29.png)](File_ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_(crop).png.md) [](File_ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_(crop).png.md) A Maormer Serpentguard Rider on the back of a Sea Serpent (Tales of Tribute card art) Pyandonea is surrounded by a nearly impenetrable mist, serving to forever keep them in exile from Aldmeris.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TCPA-16)</sup> However, this mist would not keep them from interacting with Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-1)</sup> [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) shipmen under King [Hidellith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hidellith) described Pyandonea as a sea jungle barraged by mist storms. Massive plateaus spill over with vegetation which form mazes around valleys of ocean. Waving tendrils of kelp trap all but the Maormer's own ships, and provide a well-camouflaged home for the [sea serpents](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Serpent) that are Orgnum's guards and occasional mounts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-1)</sup> While Pyandonea was regarded as unseen and uncharted in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Galvendier-17)</sup> the continent could be found on some maps in the Third Era.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TWG5-18)</sup>

Pyandonea is said to have a location known as the "breeding pits".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Writing AUA-19)</sup> Yew is one of the more common trees in the Pyandonea and is typically used in crafting bows.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CM64:PS-20)</sup> Pyandonean swallowtails are [butterflies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Butterfly) believed to originate from Pyandonea.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-2920ES-21)</sup> The island is covered mostly in dense rain forests which provide a habitat for the southern water spirits.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1W-4)</sup> It is marshy, full of insects,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IRLS-3)</sup> has white sand beaches, and forests of trees. In addition to the Maormer, a [beast](Lore_Beastfolk.md) race of quadruped reptiles dubbed [lizard bulls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard_Bull) are known to occupy the outer islands of the continent.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SWS5-22)</sup> Other native beastfolk are also said to live on the islands, with some scholars speculating that the Maormer intermingling with these beastfolk was the source of the Maormer's violent nature.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ACo V-23)</sup>

## Society
The Maormer practice a powerful form of [snake magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snake_Magic) which they use to tame the sea serpents of Pyandonea for use as mounts and warbeasts.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1W-4)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PE-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-CSSSC-25)</sup> Pyandoneans have insect-like ships made with membranous sails and chitinous hulls, somewhat similar to some ships in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ4-10)</sup> While Orgnum is the king of all Maormer,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Siravaen-26)</sup> there are lesser coastal princes, who were known for their saying, "You can't cross the strait just by staring at the water."<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MVV-27)</sup> Pyanondea has a native language<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Oi M-28)</sup> which is reportedly more complex than [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ4-10)</sup>

[Gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold) is recognized as [currency](Lore_Currency.md) in Pyandonea, used by King Orgnum to fund the kingdom's vast navy. The wealth and maritime might of the nation once made it a world power. However, with the loss of [King Orgnum's Coffer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:King_Orgnum%27s_Coffer) in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), a magical chest containing endless gold owned by the king, Pyandonea's large fleet could not be sustained, leading to the nation's decline in the global scene.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AQD-29)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-CS-30)</sup><sup>:128</sup>

Traditional Maormer diplomacy features few alliances, seeing most foreign entities as prey rather than allies. Most negotiations end in bloodshed.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Siravaen-26)</sup> However, some records indicate diplomatic relations between Maormer and [Sload](Lore_Sload.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-FNot S-31)</sup> During the [Second Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#Second_Aldmeri_Dominion), Pyandonea was allied with Summerset and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md),<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-32)</sup> but their outposts on the mainland were eliminated without intercession by the Dominion.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-PGE3S-33)</sup> Occasionally, Pyandonean ships have utilized neutral ports,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Siravaen-26)</sup> such as [Mistral](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mistral).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-To KR-34)</sup>

To the Maormer, it is a sign of a fateful match to end up stranded at the open sea with someone. Consorting with an Altmer is considered treason.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Castire-35)</sup> It is not the Maormer way to sacrifice one's self to save others.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Siravaen-26)</sup> On Pyandonea, Maormer have been documented skirmishing with the local lizard bulls.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SWS6-36)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-place-Embassy.jpg/200px-ON-place-Embassy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Embassy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Embassy.jpg) The Maormer embassy in Mistral In the [Dawn](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) or [Early Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Early_Merethic_Era),<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-37)</sup> Orgnum led a rebellion against the powers of Aldmeris in an attempt to usurp control. After his defeat, he and his followers were exiled to Pyandonea. The exile proved effective, as neither Orgnum nor his followers ever disturbed Aldmeris again.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMo P-2)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TFRot M-38)</sup>

In the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), an Aldmeri exploratory ship, the *Pasquiniel*, set sail south from [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) in the direction of Pyandonea. It was never heard from again, and it is popularly assumed they were lost at sea or died at the hands of the Pyandoneans.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-FOTN-39)</sup> For almost every year of the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and Second eras, Pyandonean forces led by King Orgnum harassed the coastlines of Summerset.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-1)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-40)</sup> Attempts to retaliate against the menace were made, but the impenetrable mist surrounding the continent made navigation impossible.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Pandermalion.E2.80.8E-41)</sup>

According to [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) tales, [Gjadil the Keel-Hauler](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gjadil_the_Keel-Hauler), an explorer from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), sailed to Pyandonea and raided King Orgnum's palace, burying the treasure at [Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dusk).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-IRB-42)</sup> The tale of treasure was false, as Gjadil instead buried a message telling her descendants to value family,<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ESOGjadil-43)</sup> but the veracity of her assault is unclear.

After the assassination of [Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) [Savirien Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak) in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) entered a period of civil war. Pyandonean forces capitalized on the chaos, capturing swaths of the province's coast.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-PGE3V-44)</sup> The [Wood Elven](Lore_Bosmer.md) cities of [Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haven) and [Woodhearth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Woodhearth) were utterly destroyed by the Maormer incursion. Even [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root), the seat of [Camoran](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) rule, and its Great Tree were plundered by the encroachment.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ACo V-23)</sup>

One of the only glimpses into the actual landscape of Pyandonea came in [2E 486](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_486), when a small Maormeri fleet was sighted off the coast of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) and King Hidellith ordered his navy to give chase. The navy followed the ships through uncharted waters and were ambushed at Pyandonea itself. Most of the fleet was destroyed, but one warship and its crew returned to Summerset to record their account.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-1)</sup>

At some point during the early Interregnum, a group of pirates journeyed to Pyandonea under the pretense of 'establishing trade routes', though truly intending to pillage the isle. Instead, they discovered lizard bulls inhabiting an isle off the coast. The trident-wielding monstrosities initially were dead set on attacking them, though soon turned their sight on the Maormer after one of their ships arrived, leaving the crew enough time to escape.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SWS6-36)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/ON-creature-The_Pyandonean_Wind_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Pyandonean_Wind_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Pyandonean_Wind_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Pyandonean_Wind_02.jpg) Pyandonean Air Atronach (ESO) In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Green Lady](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Lady) [Finoriell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Finoriell) swam to Pyandonea, seeking vengeance for the murder of [Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Silvenar) [Edhelorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Edhelorn) on [Khenarthi's Roost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi%27s_Roost). She killed the Maormer indiscriminately until she was spent. As she lay dying atop the highest sea-tower, the Sea Elves' greatest warrior came to claim her head as a trophy. Instead, she grabbed him and threw herself to the waves far below.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-45)</sup>

In the same year, [High Kinlady Estre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Estre) of [Skywatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skywatch) contacted and arranged a deal with [Sealord Malleroth of Pyandonea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malleroth). In exchange for her providing Altmer naval schedules and assisting in navigating the Auridon Strait, she would be made Viceroy of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) after Summerset was conquered.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TTVQ-46)</sup> Estre did not intend for such an outcome to come to fruition, instead using the chaos the attempt would spark to destabilize the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ODt SE-47)</sup>

The last contact with the island was in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110) during the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md), when the Altmer, with the help of the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) and the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), eventually vanquished a Pyandonean fleet.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-FOA-48)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TWG5-18)</sup> Legend credits the Psijics with creating a freak storm that decimated the Pyandonean fleet.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-49)</sup> After their defeat in the war, Pyandonea lost its status as a major player on the global stage.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ARArtifact-50)</sup>
