Regulus Terentius was the Count of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He came from the respectable Terentius family, a noble family that had resided in Bravil since at least the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt B-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IEGTC-2)</sup> He had a son, Gellius.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt B-1)</sup>

In his younger days, Terentius was a noted tournament champion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt B-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBHBN-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBAB-4)</sup> During this time, the people of Bravil regarded him as a great and kind man.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBVL-5)</sup> However, by [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), power and idleness had spoiled him, and he was disliked by the residents of Bravil, who had come to regard him as a loafer and philanderer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt B-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBAB-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBVL-5)</sup> The Count's unpopularity can also be attributed to Gellius, who was considered spoiled and was a [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) addict.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gt B-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBAB-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBA-6)</sup> Terentius preferred to spend his time engaged in his pastimes instead of ruling County Bravil, so responsibility for his duties fell to his steward, Dro'Nahrahe.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBDN-7)</sup>

During Terentius' reign, County Bravil was troubled by the [Renrijra Krin](Lore_Renrijra_Krin.md) insurgency, so he appointed Drels Theran as his special inquisitor in charge of suppressing the insurgents.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBDT-8)</sup> During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), Terentius believed that High Chancellor [Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) planned to use his position as head of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) to become emperor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBCRT-9)</sup> When an Oblivion Gate opened outside of Bravil, he put the city guard on high alert and ordered them to hold the walls.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBCRT-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBBG-10)</sup> After the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) closed the Oblivion Gate, the Count provided military aid for the defense of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Af B-11)</sup>
