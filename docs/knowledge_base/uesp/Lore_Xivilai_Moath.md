*You've gotten this far against considerable opposition. Therefore, you are able to protect yourself. You are talking to us. Therefore you have something to sell. We will hear your plea – simple and direct. Then we will consider your appeal, or call your bluff.*
—Xivilai Moath to the [Apprentice](Lore_Hero.md#The_Apprentice) (listen Link ⏵) Xivilai Moath (or simply **Moath**) is a [Xivilai](Lore_Xivilai.md) [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-1)</sup><sup>:59</sup> and the leader of [Xivilai's Clan](Lore_Xivilai%27s_Clan.md) that is in the service of [Clan Dagon](Lore_Clan_Dagon.md). He along with [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md) are [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s adoptive children.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-2)</sup><sup>:156,159</sup> Faydra and [Imago Storm](Lore_Imago_Storm.md) are his oathkin. His [incantory neonymic](Lore_Nymic.md) was once **Wegerohseh-chehkohieu** (listen Link ⏵).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> He has a prominent red eye on the right side of his face.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BSMoath-4)</sup>

The Constrictions of Moath are named after him. This is a procedure known to some Xivilai where a subject is crushed by a conjured hand. Mortals may die from the experience, but those that survive report a feeling of bodily relief.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONO-5)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/BT-concept-Xivilai_Moath.webp/200px-BT-concept-Xivilai_Moath.webp)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Xivilai_Moath.webp) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Xivilai_Moath.webp) Moath (Betrayal of the Second Era) Sometime before the start of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-6)</sup><sup>:52</sup> the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) took an interest in stopping the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md)'s activities in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). This was a goal they shared with the Soldiers of the Void, who had a mutual interest in ensuring that Mehrunes Dagon was not summoned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The Waking Flame planned to perform ritualistic sacrifices to summon Moath, who was to serve as Dagon's emissary. However, the Psijics and the Soldiers of the Void interrupted and tampered with the summoning rituals, which resulted in Moath manifesting in a weakened enough state to be banished back to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-6)</sup><sup>:23</sup> The Soldiers of the Void alone were not enough to slay Moath, but he was successfully vanquished with the arrival of the Psijic Order's forces.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-6)</sup><sup>:44</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), as part of the machinations of [Septima Tharn](Lore_Septima_Tharn.md), the [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Garrison.md) was targeted as a point of interest to aid in [Seventh Legion](Lore_Seventh_Legion.md)'s annexation of the region. The regiment managed to open a [Dark Anchor](Lore_Dark_Anchor.md) into [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) above the garrison for [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) reinforcements. Moath appeared to lead the invasion; however, he was swiftly banished by the combined might of the [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md) and the [Knights of Saint Pelin](Lore_Knights_of_Saint_Pelin.md), and the Anchor was destroyed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Moath)</sup>

Sometime after [Bazrag gro-Fharun](Lore_Bazrag_gro-Fharun.md) was crowned the king of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:18</sup> the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md) received a tip indicating that the Soldiers of the Void were doing nefarious deeds in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md)'s [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md), located in enemy [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) territory. However, it was also thought that the information was a trap intended to lure the Eyes of the Queen into an area of their enemy's choosing.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:24,30</sup> [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md) and an agent ventured into the Doomcrag and encountered Moath after defeating various daedra and corrupted creatures. The Daedra Lord was snooping around the Soldiers of the Void's abandoned encampment and was not happy to see Razum-dar and company and decided to attack on sight. Though Moath was defeated, the encounter left Razum-dar's fur standing on end, and his companion injured with a limp.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:30,43</sup>

### Third Era
After his father's numerous failed attempts at invading the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) in the past,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SL-9)</sup> Moath took it upon himself to engineer [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s involvement in the Spire's fall.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-2)</sup><sup>:158</sup> Indeed, Mehrunes Dagon and his forces were free to invade this war college thanks to the resulting bargain,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Codex-10)</sup> and in exchange they were to slay the five mighty [battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) administers who governed it. Jagar hoped this would free him from the competition for his position as [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md), as these administers were traditionally selected for it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-2)</sup><sup>:18</sup>

Moath's clan served alongside the [Shardai](Lore_Shardai_Clan.md) and [Dremora Clan](Lore_Dremora_Clan.md) during the occupation of the Battlespire. At this time, [Morphoid Daedra](Lore_Morphoid_Daedra.md), [Frost Daedra](Lore_Frost_Daedra.md), and [Clannfears](Lore_Clannfear.md) were retainers of Xivilai's Clan.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BSMD-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSFRD-12)</sup> On the orders of Faydra Shardai, the Frost Daedra helped to invade the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md) along with their hated enemies, the [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md). To prevent infighting, Moath made a pact with Faydra, forcing them to work together.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSFID-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSFRD-12)</sup> This relationship did not hold, as an [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md) from the Battlespire, following the retreating Daedric armies, brought the [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) into conflict over control of the realm.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BSL4-14)</sup>

Moath's Clan was blamed for failing to stop the liberation of Shade Perilous, leaving Faydra's Clan with a higher status,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSFRD-12)</sup> at least momentarily. After escaping the Shade Perilous, the apprentice made it into the [Chimera of Desolation](Lore_Chimera_of_Desolation.md), where they thrust into the middle of a ritualistic hunt known as the [Ritual of the Innocent Quarry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ritual_of_the_Innocent_Quarry). Here, Faydra's forces took the forefront of attempting to slay the hero (with Moath's forces providing additional support), though this attempt failed despite Faydra's [Hernes](Lore_Herne_(creature).md) cheating, which gave the apprentice the opportunity to add both the [Armor of the Savior's Hide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Armor_of_the_Savior%27s_Hide) and the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md) artifacts to their arsenal to use against Dagon's forces.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BSL5-15)</sup>

The apprentice subsequently traveled to the [Havoc Wellhead](Lore_Havoc_Wellhead.md), a provincial governmental center housing Moath and other Dagonic lieutenants. The increasing rivalry between Faydra and Moath displeased Grand Vizier [Imago Storm](Lore_Imago_Storm.md) of the Dremora Clan. Indeed, Imago saw this sibling conflict as a cause for disaster, such as Moath instigating the conquest of the Battlespire<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-2)</sup> which may attract the attention of the other Daedric Princes ([Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) in particular),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-2)</sup><sup>:159</sup> and Faydra's failure to mend the situation.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BSL5-15)</sup> As a result, Imago made a deal with the Battlespire apprentice in an attempt to restore order to Clan Dagon.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BSIS-16)</sup> Xivilai Moath was tricked into aiding the apprentice and brought his clan into open conflict with Shardai Clan, and Faydra was banished using her neonymic. It is unknown if Moath himself was banished with his neonymic, or if he was coerced into granting the apprentice safe passage into through the Havoc Wellhead.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BSL6-17)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Moath) | Moath's relationship to Molag Bal is vague, though there is precedence for Dagon's reputable troops being hired out to other Daedric Princes to accomplish infeasible missions.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONCRR-18)</sup> Alternatively, he may have been summoned and bound to work against his will, akin to the scenario mentioned in the book *[I was Summoned by a Mortal](Lore_I_was_Summoned_by_a_Mortal.md)*. |
| --- | --- |
- Xivilai Moath's first name was later adopted as the species name of the daedra known as the Xivilai in their introduction in *[The Elder Scrolls IV: Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*. In his subsequent appearance in [The Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online), he is simply known as Moath.
