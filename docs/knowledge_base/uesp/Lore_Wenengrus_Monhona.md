# Wenengrus Monhona

| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Race | Unknown | Gender | Unknown |

*So long as the Empire shall maintain its worship of Akatosh and his kin, and so long as Alessia's heirs shall bear the Amulet of Kings, Akatosh and his divine kin maintain a strong barrier between Tamriel and Oblivion, so that mortal man need never again fear the devastating summoned hosts of the Daedra Lords.*
—Wenengrus Monhona<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAOK-1)</sup> Wenengrus Monhona, also known as **Wenegrus Monhana**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EOMN-2)</sup> was a renowned scholar of the [Nedes](Lore_Nedes.md) and [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) and translator. They are the author of *[The Amulet of Kings](Lore_The_Amulet_of_Kings.md)*, a study examining the [legendary relic itself](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), the Covenant between [Saint](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), and the downfall of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) civilization.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAOK-1)</sup> Monhona also helped translate fragments of the mysterious Tract of Merid-Nunda, an early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) manuscript detailing the origin myth of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) alongside [Herminia Cinna](Lore_Herminia_Cinna.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EOMN-2)</sup> Theatrical retellings of the story of the Amulet of Kings attributed to Wenengrus Monhona were performed in the courts of various rulers across different periods of time.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSC-3)</sup>

## Notes
- It is unclear when Wenengrus Monhona lived. Some versions of their works discovered in the late Third Era and early Fourth Era refer to the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) and the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md) and therefore cannot predate the apotheosis of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAOK3-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAOK4-5)</sup> Some versions refer simply to the Divines without specifying their number.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSC-3)</sup> Other versions, found in the mid Second Era, instead mention the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) and make no references to the Septim line. Their writings were also present in that earlier period,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAOK2-6)</sup> and were cited by scholars active during that period such as [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EOMN-2)</sup> It is possible that Monhona's *The Amulet of Kings* was among books displaced in time, due to the magic of the library of [Gandranen](Lore_Gandranen.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GR-7)</sup>
