## Nautilus
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/ON-creature-Nautilus_%28Apocrypha%29.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nautilus_%28Apocrypha%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nautilus_(Apocrypha).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nautilus_(Apocrypha).jpg) A giant Nautilus Nautiluses are aquatic [cephalopods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cephalopod) with whorled shells, commonly found in the seas west of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), such as the [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AFNS-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SA-6)</sup> Though they can also be found in southern waters like the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Their shells are utilized in [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) armor, the nautiluses found in the waters surrounding [Agonio](Lore_Agonio.md) are known to grow particularly large. Since it was the largest island in the [Thrassian Archipelago](Lore_Thras.md), it is theorized its abundant resources allowed them to grow to larger sizes.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SA-6)</sup> The Topal Nautilus is a rare species of nautilus found only in the [Topal Sea](Lore_Topal_Sea.md), off the coast of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOFish-8)</sup>

Giant fossilized nautilus shells, much larger than any found on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), can be found littering [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). Their presence there has led some to theorize that Tamrielic nautili actually originated from Apocrypha.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AFNS-5)</sup> Though the faithful of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) believe that the many fossils embedded across the realm of Apocrypha are remnants of another place, another [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md), which was the true origin point of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Knowledge.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

## [Nereid](Lore_Nereid.md)

## [Netch](Lore_Netch.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/SR-creature-Bull_Netch.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Bull_Netch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Bull_Netch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Bull_Netch.jpg) A bull netch [Netch](Lore_Netch.md) (or **netches**) are large and generally peaceful creatures native to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) that resemble airborne [jellyfish](Lore_Jellyfish.md) with the size and disposition of [cattle](Lore_Cattle.md), floating through the air supported by internal sacks of magical gases. The barbs on a netch's tentacles contain active nematocysts, making them deadly if provoked. Adolescent netches are called calves or netchlings and resemble female ("betty") netches. Male ("bull") netches are larger than female ("betty") netches; the latter can be provoked into aggression more easily. They are usually peaceful but can be defensive of their territory, particularly during breeding season. When betty netches and calves feel threatened, they secrete pheromone oils that drive bull netches mad, turning them aggressive.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Netch), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Netch.md)<sup>([DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md))</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Netch), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Netch)

## [Nightmare Animal](Lore_Nightmare_Animal.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-mount-Nightmare_Courser.jpg/200px-ON-mount-Nightmare_Courser.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Nightmare_Courser.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Nightmare_Courser.jpg) A Nightmare Courser [Nightmare animals](Lore_Nightmare_Animal.md) are [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) creatures that originate from the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), and are rumored to have been created by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) himself. These creatures have black fur, burning extremities, and flames hissing from their face. ## [Nirnblooded](Lore_Nirnblooded.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/ON-creature-Nirnblooded_Senche.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nirnblooded_Senche.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nirnblooded_Senche.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nirnblooded_Senche.jpg) A Nirnblooded Senche [Nirnblooded](Lore_Nirnblooded.md) are magical creatures imbued with [Nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md). They were created by the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) with the aid of the blood rituals. Clan led by [Gherig Bullblood](Lore_Gherig_Bullblood.md) utilized Nirnblooded animals and spriggans as their defenders in the ancient [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) site - [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md). They have been summoned by a powerful Hagraven Caillaoife and by some of the Minotaur shamans to assist their clansmen in combat.

The blood magic ritual that alters and imbues with Nirncrux can be performed on wide variety of beings. Animals such as [senche](Lore_Senche.md), [bears](Lore_Bear.md) and [guars](Lore_Guar.md) were subjected to the process of magical empowerment. The ritual affects the minds of animals, making them bloodthirsty and revel in the gore of their rider's battle fury.

Aside from increased strength and bloodlust certain Nirnblooded creatures obtain the ability to summon plant life such as stranglers and command lesser Nirnblooded beasts.

The ritual that alters animals with the power of Nirncrux can be performed on other beings than animals. Spriggans can also be subjected to this rite.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Nirnblooded_Senche)

## [Nix-Ox](Lore_Nix-Ox.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-creature-Nix-Ox.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nix-Ox.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nix-Ox.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nix-Ox.jpg) A Nix-Ox [Nix-Oxen](Lore_Nix-Ox.md) (sometimes referred to as simply **nixes**) are large [arthropods](Lore_Arthropod.md) from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) that were once particularly prevalent on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). They are distant relatives of the [Nix-Hound](Lore_Nix-Hound.md), and are considered to be in the [nix creature](Lore_Nix_Creature.md) family. They resemble giant [fleas](Lore_Flea.md), and are approximately the size of a [horse](Lore_Horse.md), but move on six hairy, stilt-like legs. They are typically gentle giants that feed on fungus, they use their mandibles to break open hardened mushrooms, and lap up the soft matter inside.

[House Dunmer](Lore_Great_Houses.md) culture holds that Nix-oxen did not exist prior to the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)'s arrival in Resdayn. Myth holds that the nix-oxen were a gift to the Prophet Veloth from Boethiah herself. The remaking of a tiny insect into a creature capable of carrying her favored daughters and sons into the future she saw for them all. Nix-oxen were first domesticated by the [Erabenimsun Tribe](Lore_Erabenimsun_Tribe.md), whose legendary Ashkhan, Ashu-Ammu, rode one into battle. Occasionally, Nix-oxen can grow to be so large and aggressive that local communities may find it to be within their best interest to kill the beast. The title of "Great Zexxin" may be given to one such nix-ox, as it indicates that the creature is the most dangerous nix-ox in the land.

By the late Third Era, the Nix-Oxen population on Vvardenfell were wiped out by the [Ash Blight](Lore_Ash_Blight.md). Though Nix-Oxen and similar species were known to live throughout the mainland.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Nix-Ox), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Nix-Ox)

## [Nixad](Lore_Nixad.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/ON-creature-Nixad.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nixad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nixad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nixad.jpg) A Nixad [Nixads](Lore_Nixad.md) are mischievous flying [fay](Lore_Faerie.md) creatures that resemble humanoid [insects](Lore_Insect.md), and inhabit various forests and glades throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Nixads have an affinity for the [elements](Lore_Elements.md) and have a penchant for stealing valuables and sugary sweets like [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md). While typically small, some Nixads are larger than a [man](Lore_Man.md) or [elf](Lore_Elf.md) in size. It is sometimes speculated that Nixads are only able to fly thanks to their inherently [magical](Lore_Magic.md) nature. They are often compared to other fay such as [pixies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pixie) and [sprites](Lore_Sprite.md).

Nixads have shown some intelligence; they speak in garbled voices to mock others, and have been seen drawing things such as faces. Many Nixads have a strong taste for sugars and spices, and some are said to "live on a diet of treacle and poetry". They are known to dislike the bitter scent of [coffee](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coffee). While often gregarious and mostly harmless, Nixads are commonly thought of as nuisances, and have occasionally been known to be cruel or violent tricksters. Nixad infestations are an occasional problem on [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md). Nixads will often sing annoyingly and leave droppings everywhere. Some Nixads are known to be helpful, and others have served as companions to people.

In addition to Cyrodiil, Nixads can be found in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres.md), [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). In addition to being found on Tamriel, Nixads can also be found in the planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), such as in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), and the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md). The range of Nixads even extends to the [moons](Lore_Moons.md), as they can even be found on the [Plane of Jode](Lore_Jode.md). Despite being found in Oblivion, Nixads are not [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Nixad)

## [Nocturnal Shrike](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/ON-npc-Night_Sister_Kamira.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Night_Sister_Kamira.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Night_Sister_Kamira.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Night_Sister_Kamira.jpg) A Nocturnal Shrike [Nocturnal Shrikes](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md) (sometimes simply called **Nocturnals** or **Shrikes**) are a race of incredibly intelligent [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) found in the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md), [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)'s realm of Oblivion. In appearance, they resemble tall, pale, scantily clad, beautiful women. Despite their power and intelligence, Unusual for Daedra, Nocturnal Shrikes are prone to bouts of debilitating melancholy and fluctuating emotional states. While other Daedra are eerily calm or consistently mercurial, Shrike tend to have two states. The race is seemingly caste-based, with the terms "lieutenant" and "subaltern" used to draw distinctions between two individuals of varying power. Other Daedra such as the [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md) who once occupied [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), refer to this power dynamic as "greater" and "lesser" Nocturnals.

Subalterns serve lieutenants faithfully, offering counsel, and have even been known to make sacrifices out of love for their mistresses. The Nocturnals of Shade Perilous are led by the Nocturnal Lieutenant [Jaciel Morgen](Lore_Jaciel_Morgen.md). Nocturnal Shrikes have various other ranks and titles, such as Crowcaller, Executioner, Gloom Siren, Nightmaiden, Night Sister, Shadowweaver, Shroudweaver, and Talon.

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Deyanira_Katrece), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Shrike)

## [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg/200px-MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) Anumidium, as seen in *[Divine Metaphysics](Lore_Divine_Metaphysics.md)* [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), also known as **Anumidium**, **Anumidum**, the **Brass God**, the **Prime Gestalt**, **NM**, the **[Brass Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md)**, **Walk-Brass**, **Big Walker**, **Walking Star**, and the **Divine Skin**, was a colossal [construct](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) of [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) origin. It was constructed by Dwemeri [Tonal Architect](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) Lord [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md) to be a new god, powered by the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md), made to retake [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) from the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and possibly to make the Dwemer race [immortal](Lore_Immortality.md). The Dwemer vanished from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) before the Brass God could be activated, though their [disappearance](Lore_Disappearance_of_the_Dwarves.md) may be directly related to this endeavor.

The Numidium was, in practice, a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) of devastating power. A "thousand foot tall automaton, a [golem](Lore_Golem.md) or an atronach<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> of sorts", it was used by Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to complete his [conquest](Lore_Tiber_War.md) of Tamriel at the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), but was destroyed afterward. The golem was rebuilt and reactivated centuries later, and became the center of the phenomenon known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), after which it was destroyed again. [Temporal distortions](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) have accompanied each known use of the Numidium, to the point that the Warp in the West is sometimes known as the "Second Numidian Effect".

Some believe that the Numidium was intrinsically evil: a ranking [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) called it the "most profane" of the Dwemer's creations, a "walking horror", the "Antipodal-God-Thing that reigned on the darkest pole of the sacred Nirn-Sphere", and the "welded knot at the center of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) that must be untied". The Apostles considered the Numidium the greatest threat to [Sotha Sil's](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) goals of bringing about the Nirn-Ensuing and the Anuic convergence of Anuvanna'si. Indeed, in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Clockwork City's](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) supervising Factotum, Aios, would name Numidium first among the existential threats to the City.

A second Numidium, [Akulakhan](Lore_Akulakhan.md), was partially constructed in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the late Third Era by [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md). However, the destruction of Kagrenac's enchantments on the Heart of Lorkhan in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) prevented its activation.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)

## [Nymph](Lore_Nymph.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/6/6c/DF-creature-Nymph.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-creature-Nymph.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-creature-Nymph.gif) A nymph [Nymphs](Lore_Nymph.md) (also called **Woodland Nymphs**) are a mysterious [race](Lore_Race.md) of [magical](Lore_Magic.md) spirits that resemble traditionally beautiful, naked women. They are known to live in the forests and woodlands of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region and the island of [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md), often residing in lakes. They are said to be very intelligent and have been known to refer to others as "mortals", suggesting that they possess a greater lifespan.

Nymphs have their own melodious [tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Nymph_(skill)), which sounds similar to [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleids.md) and [faerie](Lore_Faerie.md) but shares none of the vocabulary. They are known to be aggressive towards those that cannot speak it. Nymphs have some inherent magical abilities, and are capable of draining their opponents' health and stamina. Nymphs can only be harmed by [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) of [elven](Lore_Moonstone.md) craft or better.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Nymph)
