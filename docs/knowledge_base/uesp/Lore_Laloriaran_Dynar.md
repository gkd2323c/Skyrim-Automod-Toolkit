Laloriaran Dynar (meaning "Ruler in Dark Times")<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLaloriaran-1)</sup> was the famous **Last King of the Ayleids**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLKot A-2)</sup> He ruled the city of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and later founded the city of [Bisnensel](Lore_Bisnensel.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup> In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), he played a prominent role in stopping [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOFinal-4)</sup>

Laloriaran was a member of House Dynar, the royal line of Nenalata. The emblem of the royal house was a [triquetra](Lore_Triquetra.md), and the sorcerer-kings of Nenalata was long associated with "feathered jewels" and "blue gems". Laloriaran himself is believed to have worn a piece of jewelry known as the Torc of the Last Ayleid King, a neck ring made from [palladium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Palladium) and [meteoric iron](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md) and inlaid with a central jewel cut from [welkynd stone](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTorc-5)</sup> The [Crown of Nenalata](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md) was another important symbol of his rule; this elven helmet enabled its wearer to command the allegiance of the Ayleids of Nenalata, even in death.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OB-6)</sup>

## History

### King of Nenalata
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/OB-place-Nenalata.jpg/200px-OB-place-Nenalata.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Nenalata.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Nenalata.jpg) Ruins of Nenalata, King Dynar's city of origin Laloriaran Dynar was born in [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md), an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city located on the eastern shore of the [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md) at the mouth of the [Silverfish River](Lore_Silverfish_River.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Nenalata was incorporated into the Empire as a vassal city-state following the Alessian victory. This allowed Nenalata to last longer than most of the other Ayleid city-states.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLaloriaran-1)</sup> Sometime between [1E 263](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_263) and [1E 331](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_331), he became the king of the city-state, succeeding his father, who had been humiliated by Empress [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s victory. He inherited a state in wrenching transition—a shift to a slave-less economy and the forced adoption of Alessia's Eight Divines.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup>

With the rise of the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) in the 1E 300s, the remaining vassal states dwindled, and soon Nenalata became the last of them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup> In the years [1E 372](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_372) - [374](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_374), King Dynar was given an ultimatum by the [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) [Emperor](Lore_Ami-El.md). He and his people were eventually forced to abandon Nenalata and flee to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in order to escape the anti-Ayleid pogroms.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup> During their exodus, hunters among the Nenalata Ayleids teamed up with senche mounts daily to track and kill game to help feed the evacuees. King Dynar joined them, riding a senche named Ceydarre, feeding her from his own rations.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NA-7)</sup>

### The Founding of Bisnensel
In High Rock, King Dynar and his people were welcomed by the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) clan. After displacing previous Orc residents, they settled on the shores of the [Halcyon Lake](Lore_Halcyon_Lake.md). There, King Dynar founded the new city of [Bisnensel](Lore_Bisnensel.md), which was a miniature copy of Nenalata.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BOAR-8)</sup>

In the years [1E 453](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_453) - [460](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_460), the Primeval Seekers, a cult devoted to [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), was formed in Bisnensel. Their leader, High Priest [Uluscant](Lore_Uluscant.md), demanded more of a say in municipal affairs as the cult grew in power. King Dynar denied these demands. As a result, after a few months Dynar was deposed and forced to flee from Bisnensel.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BOAR-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ULUSCANT-9)</sup>

### Battle of Glenumbra Moors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg/200px-ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg) [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), the site of the great battle King Dynar and his family fled to [Balfiera](Lore_Balfiera.md), where they were granted asylum among the Direnni. Later, Dynar became a highly regarded tactician and strategist in the Direnni army.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup>

In the late 400s, the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) invaded High Rock. The invasion culminated in the great [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md), in which King Dynar participated.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLKot A-2)</sup> The battle was won by the Direnni and was a fatal blow to the Alessian Order.

### Return to Nenalata and Imprisonment
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/ON-place-The_Lightless_Oubliette.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Lightless_Oubliette.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Lightless_Oubliette.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Lightless_Oubliette.jpg) [The Lightless Oubliette](Lore_Lightless_Oubliette.md), where King Dynar was imprisoned for over three thousand years In the years [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482) - [484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_484), King Dynar returned to the [Heartland](Lore_Heartland.md) and was lured to Nenalata, where he fell into [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s trap and was imprisoned in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), in a [replica of the White-Gold Tower](Lore_The_Lightless_Oubliette.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-3)</sup> He would remain there for over three thousand years.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Other sources state that King Dynar was not captured by Molag Bal in Nenalata, but instead was present in the [Hollow City](Lore_Hollow_City.md) while it was still in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), was pushed into Coldharbour along with it, and captured shortly afterwards.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONGroundskeeper-11)</sup>

### Thwarting the Planemeld
In the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), King Dynar was finally freed by the [Vestige](Lore_Hero.md), who infiltrated Coldharbour with the [Fighters](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Mages Guilds](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in an effort to stop Molag Bal's terrible [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). The Ayleid king assumed command over the Fighters Guild forces, who had been leaderless after their captain had been slain. The invasion force, led by the Vestige and aided by [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), eventually managed to stop the Planemeld inside the Planar Vortex, but King Dynar was mortally wounded while trying to close a Daedric portal. He died soon afterwards in the Hollow City, in Meridia's Chapel of Light.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOFinal-4)</sup> His spirit is believed to have gone to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

### Legacy
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) excavated and reassembled fragments of what they believed to be the Torc of the Last Ayleid King from archaeological dig sites in the realm of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTorc-5)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the crown of King Dynar (whose name had apparently become forgotten by then) was in possession of an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholar of the Ayleids named [Herminia Cinna](Lore_Herminia_Cinna.md). A rival collector named [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to obtain the crown, which he wanted to use to transform himself into the King of Nenalata and restore Ayleid civilization by raising an undead army within Nenalata. Cinna, however, did not want to sell it, instead hoping to trick Umbacano into accepting the Crown of Lindai. It is unknown which crown the Hero chose to deliver to Umbacano. Regardless, Umbacano was killed later that year during an expedition to Nenalata.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OB-6)</sup>
