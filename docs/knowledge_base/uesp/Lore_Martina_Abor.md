— [Azandar al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAC-1)</sup> Martina Abor was a powerful [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [arcanist](Lore_Arcanist.md), author, and scholar, renowned for their research on the topic of [fate](Lore_Fate.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Tem-2)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/LO-book-Azandar_al-Cybiades.jpg/200px-LO-book-Azandar_al-Cybiades.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Azandar_al-Cybiades.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Azandar_al-Cybiades.jpg) Azandar al-Cybiades, Martina's partner [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg) Fateweaver Key Little is known about Martina's past. At some point they became an arcanist and began collaborating with Azandar-al Cybiades, although the exact timing is unclear. Martina and Azandar worked closely in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) during the early days of their collaboration. By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), their partnership had long passed as a result of their goals diverging. Azandar referred to them as a brilliant collaborator, consummate professional and a talented researcher. Azandar considered them clever, shrewd, and contemplative, a meticulous and zealous note-taker, and far more venturesome than himself. They were always willing to push boundaries in pursuit of new knowledge. Martina was nimble-witted, stubborn, and known for their silent strength, and astute observations. Azandar described Martina as peculiar, with a tendency to call magical instruments "gubbins".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAC-1)</sup> Martina was also dubbed arcanist of the abyssal sea and illuminatus of the threads of fate.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Tem-2)</sup>

Martina was known for their interest in the transliminal properties of exogenic materials, as well as the writings of [Denogorath](Lore_Denogorath.md) the Dread Archivist. Together, they used to make a game of proposing the strangest notions they could think of, then attempting to defend them from rigorous evaluation. Azandar's work in developing the [Fateweaver Key](Lore_Fateweaver_Key.md) was inspired by conversations he had with Martina.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAC-1)</sup> He considered sending a message to Martina after the Key's first successful test.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RN-3)</sup>

Martina Abor ventured into the Underweave multiple times. During their fourth expedition, they were accompanied by a student, [Bahtra at-Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bahtra_at-Hunding), a recently initiated Arcanist. Martina admired Bahtra's insightful nature, curiosity, and their direct, sparing manner of speech. They did not hesitate to recommend Bahtra if the need arose.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-4)</sup> At some point they authored *[The Never-Woven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Never-Woven)*, a journal exploring the art of fateweaving.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TNV-5)</sup>

In [578](Lore_Era.md#578) or [579](Lore_Era.md#579),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Martina withdrew into seclusion, retreating to the sanctum they had established at some point within the Underweave.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TU-6)</sup> It was a common tradition among powerful Arcanists to create such sanctums for personal research and isolation. They scried for the fates frayed from their timeline, also utilizing their own blood in the experiment. Their research ultimately led to their untimely demise. They realized their experiment was spiraling out of control and attempted to contain the damage. Professional to the end, their efforts costed them life.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAC-1)</sup>

### Legacy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/LO-book-Bahtra_at-Hunding.jpg/200px-LO-book-Bahtra_at-Hunding.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Bahtra_at-Hunding.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Bahtra_at-Hunding.jpg) Bahtra at-Huding, Martina's student In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), while searching for them, Azandar sought communion with the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) entity Tem, the Omen of Destiny, hoping to learn their current whereabouts.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TFC-7)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Tem-2)</sup> Thanks to the information from the Daedra, he was able to trace Martina steps and discover the path to their sanctum in the Underweave. He was however too late. Azandar found Martina's body in their sanctum, and after ensuring their remains were properly honored, he took steps to ensure that their research would live on. Azandar wished he had had the chance to repair his relationship with Martina before they died. Using Martina's notes, he modified the Fateweaver Key, hoping to reap the rewards of knowledge, insight, and scholarship, just as Martina would have wanted.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TFC-7)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAC-1)</sup>

One of the arcanist spells used by Azandar is named after Martina, Abor's Augmented Ward.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AAW-8)</sup>
