# Night of Tears


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/SR-quest-The_Eye_of_Magnus.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Eye_of_Magnus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Eye_of_Magnus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Eye_of_Magnus.jpg) The Eye of Magnus, the supposed cause for the Night of Tears The **Night of Tears** (also known as the **Crying Night**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> is the night an Elven force attacked and burned the first human city of Skyrim, [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md), built by the first Nordic inhabitants.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-No T-2)</sup> In retaliation, [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md), the leader of the human colonization effort and the founder of the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md), drove the elves out of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-No T-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Fot SP-4)</sup>

The reason for the Night of Tears has been lost to time, but it is speculated that it was spurred by a discovery of something beneath the city of Saarthal, which prompted the elves to destroy the city to claim the power for themselves.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-No T-2)</sup> While it is impossible to know if this was the true cause for the Night of Tears, it was later discovered by the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) that a powerful artifact was hidden within the ruins of Saarthal, which they dubbed "[The Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md)".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Skyrim-5)</sup> Another theory, proposed by Imperial scholars, is that the elves feared that the humans' culture would surpass their own and so sought to drive the humans from their lands.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup>

## Notes
- [Reynir the Destroyer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reynir_the_Destroyer), also known as Reynir of Saarthal, claimed to have survived the Night of Tears, and sailed to [Mereth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mereth) with the Five Hundred Companions on the ship commanded by [Ylgar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ylgar), son of Ysgramor. His body was buried in the crypts beneath Windhelm.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
