Morian Zenas is a master conjurer and the author of the book *[On Oblivion](Lore_On_Oblivion.md)*. As Professor of Transliminal Studies at the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), of which he was a founding member,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RM4-1)</sup> he made it his life's work to find a way into the realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), trying many different methods, but always failing.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RM1-2)</sup> He would eventually succeed with the help of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) wizard [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CM10IS-3)</sup>

## History
During his time attempting to access Oblivion, he obtained the ability to telepathically connect his mind with that of his [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) pupil, [Seif-ij Hidja](Lore_Seif-ij_Hidja.md), an ability which the two seemed to accidentally develop. Eventually, however, Morian Zenas was able to find a way into Oblivion after meeting with the Dunmer wizard Divayth Fyr, who taught him a method that involved exploiting a series of portals to various realms created by a [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) wizard who was long missing and had been presumed dead. Before his departure from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), he visited a [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) embassy to participate in a reception for [High King Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RM4-1)</sup> and became romantically involved with Doctor [Alfidia Lupus](Lore_Alfidia_Lupus.md), who he would win the heart of during a visit to a [Baandari Pedlars](Lore_Baandari_Pedlars.md) camp.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RM2-4)</sup>

Zenas would enter Oblivion with Divayth Fyr's help, using his mind connection ability to continuously inform Seif-ij Hidja of his experiences. During his travels, he crossed through five of the [Planes of Oblivion](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md): [Ashpit](Lore_Ashpit.md), [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), [Moonshadow](Lore_Moonshadow.md), [Quagmire](Lore_Quagmire.md), and [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM-5)</sup> Morian would become a powerful figure in Apocrypha, and influenced [arcanist](Lore_Arcanist.md) spellcasting at some point, with his obscure magics making their way into a common arcanist's repertoire.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ASZED-7)</sup> Eventually, his hunger for knowledge took over his mind as he gradually grew quieter, seldom whispering any thoughts into his pupil's mind, and when he did, they came to be very cryptic until finally, his whispers became unintelligible.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TDOO-8)</sup> His fate was sealed when he discovered a "Known Thing", a truth powerful enough to destroy the minds of those exposed to it.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Morian survived his exposure to a Known Thing, being the only mortal on record to have done so, but the experience transformed him as a result. The Morian of the past learned all he could to share the knowledge, while the "new" Morian's purpose was to learn for the sake of enjoyment.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONMZ-10)</sup>

Morian recorded his writings of his time in Apocrypha in the books *[Secrets Overheard in Apocrypha‎](Lore_Secrets_Overheard_in_Apocrypha.md)* and *Studies in Apocrypha*.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Although his dates of birth and death are unknown, it can be inferred that he lived sometime between 2E 418 and 2E 431, as he was a contemporary of Doctor Alfidia Lupus, who was Imperial Ethnographer for Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) during that period.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RM5-12)</sup> By that time, he was already at least sixty years old.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CM3-13)</sup> Dr. Lupus came to believe that Fyr's "aid" in Zenas's disappearance into Oblivion was not entirely benign, and that the Dunmer sorcerer was envious of how she favored him, thus, tricking him into a perilous endeavor from which he was unlikely to return. This is supported by at least one instance where Zenas argued with Fyr over her.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RM5-12)</sup>

After what he perceived as three years,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Morian Zenas confined himself to Hermaeus Mora's menagerie of the Feral Gallery, where he was provided protection from powerful enemies and a plethora of books that he could read. Within its confines, he wrote a criticism on *The Doors of Oblivion*, claiming that the Morian depicted there is a "former edition cut by revisions".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Zenas)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the forces of the Dreamcarvers, a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) clan serving [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md), attacked the Feral Gallery, causing the inhabitants to run rampant. With the aid of an adventurer, Morian Zenas escaped the menagerie.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Zenas) | Morian's "former edition cut by revisions" comment on how he is presented in *[The Doors of Oblivion](Lore_The_Doors_of_Oblivion.md)* is likely a reference to the concept of *[retroactive continuity](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retroactive_continuity)*. Given the fact he was orignally written to be a Third Era contemporary character. |
| --- | --- |

- An attempt was made to rewrite the book in The Elder Scrolls Online to make it appropriate for the Second Era by removing a section referencing the destroyed [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md). However, an error in a later section was not noticed, and states that [Celarus](Lore_Celarus.md) was the current leader of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) instead of [Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md). Additionally, it claims that the Dreaming Cave had been sealed off, when it was still operational at that time. The book also makes reference to the [Mantellan Crux](Lore_Mantellan_Crux.md), even though the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md) was sent to Aetherius at the end of the Second Era.
- Morian Zenas was one of the few individuals to have knowledge of [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md) as a Daedric Prince.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OO-17)</sup> Fellow author [Waughin Jarth](Lore_Waughin_Jarth.md) assumed that his knowledge of this was a practical joke played on him by the other Princes, unaware that Jyggalag was real.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Writers-18)</sup>
- According to Morian Zenas, the mortal mind is forced to interpret its surroundings as best it can when transported to a "place that is not a place".<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TROA-19)</sup>
