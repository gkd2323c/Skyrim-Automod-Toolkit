# Sigil Stone


Due to [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md)'s sacrifice at the end of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the Liminal Barrier constructed by the event is too strong for even a Sigil Stone to breach for more than a fleeting moment.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vonos Journal-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBMartin-5)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Excavation-2)</sup>

## Uses
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/LG-cardart-Sigil_Keeper.png/200px-LG-cardart-Sigil_Keeper.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Sigil_Keeper.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Sigil_Keeper.png) A Sigil Keeper Although mainly used by Mehrunes Dagon, Sigil Stones are known to also be used in other realms of Oblivion such as [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Sigil Stones have other uses apart from sustaining liminal bridges. One was used as an opposing force to a Great Welkynd Stone to open a portal to [Gaiar Alata](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gaiar_Alata). Daedric Siege Crawlers use them as a power source. The arcane power of a Stone can be used in enchanting, consuming the Stone in the process.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup> Powerful conjurers require Sigil Stones for use in rituals to summon items and Daedra from Oblivion when using the [Atronach Forge](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md), although the Stone isn't necessary when summoning lesser objects.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup> Sigil Stones can also be used to create less permanent portals to Oblivion, which can be opened at will as long as the Stone remains intact, although this method binds the stone to a particular location in Mundus.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## History
During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the loss of the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) caused the liminal barrier to slowly weaken and allowed these liminal bridges to remain open indefinitely.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> This was exploited by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) and Mehrunes Dagon, who created Oblivion Gates to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), allowing the Daedric forces to invade [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). A method of destroying these Gates was discovered by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) at the Sack of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md): removing the Sigil Stones from the well-defended keeps in the Deadlands where they were hidden would break the connection, teleporting those near the Stone back to Mundus and destroying both the Oblivion Gate and its Sigil Keep in the process. Occasionally, Gates would have multiple Sigil Stones supporting the link, and all of them would need to be removed to shut the Gate;<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBMobile-10)</sup> similarly, one Sigil Stone may support multiple Gates. This technique for opening portals was exploited at the Battle of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) by the united forces of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) (led by [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md)) to procure a **Great Sigil Stone**, a more powerful specimen used to sustain larger portals such as the "Great Gates". Each Oblivion Gate which was opened weakened the liminal barrier, eventually completely diminishing it and allowing Mehrunes Dagon pass fully into Mundus. The barriers were restored when the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) was smashed, summoning the power of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to banish Dagon.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup>

The legendary [Arms of Chaos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arms_of_Chaos) were created through the use of Sigil Stones, from which they derived their power. An Orange Sigil Stone was embedded in the [Arm of the Sun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arm_of_the_Sun), and a Green Sigil Stone was affixed to the [Arm of the Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arm_of_the_Moon). A mage named Ellane later discovered the means by which these [staves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staves) were created, and used a Blue Sigil Stone to create the [Warlock's Mark](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Warlock%27s_Mark), an enchanted necklace.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HJ-11)</sup> However, the stones were subsequently removed and returned to Oblivion, to be guarded by three Dremora known as the Guardians of the Sigil Stones. Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) the Guardians were summoned and defeated by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) in order to recover the three Sigil Stones and restore the disassembled artifacts.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CC-12)</sup>
