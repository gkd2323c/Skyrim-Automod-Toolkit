# Marriage


## Marriage Under Mara
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/SR-activity-Marriage.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Marriage.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Marriage.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Marriage.jpg) A wedding ceremony in a Temple of Mara [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara) is worshipped as the Goddess of Love and the patron of marriages by [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) and [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). Priests of Mara (or her [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) counterpart, [Morwha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morwha), under [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md) form) officiate weddings all across Tamriel. Because of this, the Pledge of Mara is the most common form of union.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VFE-1)</sup> The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), primarily the [Barsaebics](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barsaebics), though some [Daedraphiles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedraphiles) as well, worshipped Mara, at times even alongside the Daedra,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DWTA-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> not unlike the Khajiit,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CMA-5)</sup> and the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONVAO-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBOM-7)</sup> [Falmer](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) (before their corruption),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> and [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Water-9)</sup> worshipped Mara too.

Lowborns usually marry for love, while nobles will be more likely to take part in an arranged marriage to strengthen relationships between royal or noble houses. Reasons for marriage may differ among the different cultures.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-QA1-10)</sup>

Imperial and Breton nobles are usually married between both close and distant noble families to sealed political alliances.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The Redguards hold a largely similar position on the matter.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

Temples to the [Eight](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) and [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines) hold that Mother Mara loves all her children regardless of form. When two mortals, regardless of their race or gender, are married, it is their souls, not their bodies, that are united. This way, same-sex and interracial marriages are allowed under the Divines.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> However, discrimination towards interracial couples exists, and civil administration sometimes disapproves this kind of union.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cf/ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg) An altar bearing the seal of Mara, located at Hroldan Ring Typically, priests of Mara officiate most marriages in their temples. The ceremony is arranged a certain time before by one of the members of the future union. The priest says a few words to the attendants before asking the participants to confirm their intention to marry one another. The priest declares them married, and the betrothed exchange matching rings. The exchange of rings between the couple will finish the ceremony, marking the start of the marriage. The couple is typically accompanied by their closest friends and family during the ceremony.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

Couples can marry without the assistance of a priest of Mara as well. Often, this is done by devoting themselves to the Goddess of Love at a Shrine of Mara, where the participants exchange [Rings of Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rings_of_Mara) to seal the wedding before the Divines.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

The [Temple of Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Mara) allows posthumous marriages in certain circumstances.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TF-18)</sup>

### Divorce
Divorce is the process by which married couples may be formally separated.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Tharayya-19)</sup> When two partners are divorced, their marriage is annulled.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Volunidai-20)</sup> Despite being officially separated, divorcees who have yet to remarry by the time their former spouse dies are still termed widows.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Quintus-21)</sup>

## Altmer
Altmer are famous for their interest in lineage and heritage over marriage out of attraction.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-QA1-10)</sup> [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) High Elves' marriages are commonly arranged and often loveless.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Highborn Altmer take the matter most seriously, which begins when a matchmaker is contacted to research the couple's [star charts](Lore_Constellations.md) and pedigree documents. Family, physicality and other attributes are also taken into account. The couple seek guidance from priests, who can supposedly divine the auspiciousness of the future union: from the very suitability of the couple to the date of the wedding. Matchmaking usually lasts years or decades to solidify into a marriage.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOEsterdel-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESONiralin-24)</sup> Breaking an arranged betrothal usually leaves those involved labeled as outcasts to their own families and communities.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOEsterdel-23)</sup> Priests of Mara hold no fixed doctrine on how the goddess regards arranged marriages, though they recount many tales of unexpected love blossoming within even the coldest unions. They teach that the Divine Mother is a patient gardener. Regarding affairs arising from loveless marriages, they do not treat all cases as the same. Instead, they urge those involved in secret trysts to ask themselves whose garden they are truly tending.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LAHDMM-25)</sup>

Inter-class marriages are not unheard of, but they are despised by Summerset society.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup> Maintaining pure blood is the most important part of the relationship, so secret vampires and [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) may turn to suicide or murder as a way out while keeping their own family's honor (and that of their betrothed) intact.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-27)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESONiralin-24)</sup> This focus on bloodline purity restricts interracial marriage between Altmer (especially Summerset Altmer) and members of the other races, though there have been notable exceptions throughout history. For example, Clan [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) intermingled with their [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) vassals, and [Morgiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morgiah) (a Dunmer and the daughter of Queen [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah)) married the High Kinlord of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup> Lower-class Altmer living outside Summerset also married non-Altmer, though little is known of the consequences they faced in the Isles for doing so.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Telline-30)</sup>

## Argonians
Argonian customs differ among the cultures of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).

Some Argonians believe the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) ensures that life-long mates meet and find love in each other. They think that the sentient holy trees make it so the future marriage to meet in place and in time, exactly when both of them are prepared for love and, later, marriage.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-THF-31)</sup> Despite the fact that the traditional Argonians don't follow Mara some adopted the custom of exchanging Rings of Mara.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-LA-32)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg) A Hist tree One tradition dictates that the one who proposes will have to present a unique wedding ring to their potential mate to represent their future bond. The jewel is supposed to be forged in gold and to have three amethysts as part of the design. Two of the gems, the ones on the outside, represent the couple to be married: one for each partner. The amethyst in the center represents the Hist, the entity that all Argonians consider a sacred part of their lives.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TJD-33)</sup>

Some Argonians don betrothal veils woven of the wings of [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen) [wasps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wasp).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-CW-34)</sup>

### Murkmire
The Argonians of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) have bonding rituals. Outsiders often compare the bonding ritual to a wedding, but such comparisons aren't wholly accurate. A bonding ritual isn't necessarily a "marriage", per se.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOChal-35)</sup> During the bonding ritual, the tribe chooses those who will lay and sire the next clutch of eggs.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-36)</sup> Some partners choose each other, and present gifts to their partner or perform other customs in accordance with their tribe's traditions. While there is respect between the bonded pair, romantic feelings are not strictly necessary.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOChal-35)</sup>

Each tribe has their own bonding traditions, but there is overlap. A tree-minder performs the ceremony before the Hist. The bonded pair are blessed with good health and vibrant fertility, and the couple mates. The process of choosing a partner also varies. Sometimes, the decision is based on an emotional connection; others bond out of physical attraction. The [Bright-Throat Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bright-Throat_Tribe) uses their ceremony to strengthen their political connections with other tribes.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOChal-35)</sup> The closest comparison to the Bright-Throats' bonding practice in other cultures is political marriages. The intention of the union between individuals of differing factions is similar, as the union strengthens relationships between factions.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-37)</sup> However, the bonding ritual isn't a binding contract between two individuals that cements them together for life; there's more of an emphasis on increasing the tribe's numbers.

All tribes celebrate their own bonding in one way or another, but the Bright-Throat Tribe is especially notable for the liveliness of their festivities. The Bright-Throats invite suitors from neighboring tribes to participate in their bonding ceremony. It is a jubilant time where the other tribes are welcomed with feasts and phlegmwine. The Black-Tongues, the Moss-Skins, and the Root-House people commonly send suitors to bond with the Bright-Throats, being long-time allies of the tribe.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ESOBGT-38)</sup> Participants are paired with a bonding partner, and a ceremony is held to celebrate the couples, the tribe's renewal, and the bonds of blood and kinship between the participating tribes. After the festivities, the couples retire to their nuptial huts and begin the process of bonding, wherein they procreate.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ESOBGT-38)</sup> The Bright-Throat bonding ceremony is more than a mere display, it is a renewal of the tribe's vows with their neighbors. Canceling a bonding ceremony abruptly would give the other tribes cause to call the Bright-Throats' commitment to their allies into question.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

## Bosmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg/200px-ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Handfast.jpg) The Handfast, a Bosmeri ceremonial wedding between the Silvenar and the Green Lady A ceremonial wedding known as "the Handfast" is spiritually important to the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) Bosmer of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). This marriage is a binding between [the Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Silvenar) and the [Green Lady](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Lady), religious figures whose union represents the joining of the spiritual and physical sides of the Bosmer people.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-40)</sup>

In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) city of Vashabar, neither Green Pact nor [Dawnway](Lore_Dawnway.md) Bosmer would accept a marriage unless the couple received their parents' blessing.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-41)</sup> However, this custom could be overridden by the [Rite of Propagation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rite_of_Propagation), an ancient Valenwood rite that was rarely invoked. According to the teachings of [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre), stagnation stands against [the Green](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green). To encourage growth, the rite allows two supplicants to invite their community to stand with them; the outcome of such a decision will be respected by the families.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-42)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-43)</sup>

## Breton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-npc_Elder_Pitof_and_Elder_Gwenaelle.jpg/200px-ON-npc_Elder_Pitof_and_Elder_Gwenaelle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc_Elder_Pitof_and_Elder_Gwenaelle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc_Elder_Pitof_and_Elder_Gwenaelle.jpg) A married Druid couple Arranging fortuitous marriages are the norm among Breton noble families,<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-44)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ONHCP-45)</sup> but having courtships before choosing a partner is another option. In a courtship where one has a choice, suitors may present gifts of varying extravagance to the target in hopes of wooing them.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-46)</sup> Other options may include a bouquet of rare flowers, or a serenade.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-47)</sup> The practice of dowries exists in Breton marriages.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-MTCAlga-48)</sup> There is some societal expectations in your partner. A noble lady in a relation with her manservant would be considered forbidden love for example.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-49)</sup> Bretons have the Law of Consanguinity, preventing marriage between those that are too close in blood relation.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TSo PE-50)</sup> Love charms are popular among Breton nobles and merchant lords who seek to improve their love lives.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-51)</sup> A woman seeking to marry into the nobility may try to use an updo traditional to Breton high society.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONBBBB-52)</sup>

[Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids) are known to enter into marriages despite not adhering to the teachings of Mara, the Goddess of Love. Nevertheless, there is limited knowledge available regarding the specifics of Druid marriage ceremonies and customs.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-EP-53)</sup>

Some sources claim that in certain regions of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and rural [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) regions, [spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan) are said to be charmed to participate in weddings by ensnaring the hands and feet of the betrothed with roots. Upon release, the spriggan can either leave peacefully or pose a threat to the couple. The outcome is viewed as an omen for the marriage's success. If the spriggan becomes hostile, it is defeated, and the couple consumes its sap to ward off any perceived curse. Scholars speculate on the tradition's possible connection to the Snow Elves.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TW-54)</sup>

## Dunmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/ON-place-Shrine_of_Mother_Morrowind.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shrine_of_Mother_Morrowind.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_of_Mother_Morrowind.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_of_Mother_Morrowind.jpg) A Shrine of Mother Morrowind Both [House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) honored their departed ancestors when they wed. Therefore, marriage traditions did not change when the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) rose during the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) (when the Dark Elves were still known as [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)), or when they fell in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Aa D-55)</sup> During the Tribunal's reign, House Dunmer wedding ceremonies took place at shrines to [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia), also known as Mother Morrowind.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-STMM-56)</sup>

Dunmeri men join the House of his wife. He binds himself through ritual and oath into the new clan, and gains communication and benefits from its ancestors; however, his access is lesser than those born into the bloodline. Despite this new bond, he retains some access to the ancestors of his own bloodline.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Aa D-55)</sup>

Motivations behind Dunmer relationships and marriages are political in some cases when a high-ranking House Dunmer is involved.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Aa D-55)</sup> However, for most of the common people, both House Dunmer and Ashlanders, attraction plays a key role in mating and selecting partners, who will marry or not in the future.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-QA1-10)</sup>

In the past, House and Ashlander Dunmer usually did not marry between themselves due to their religious differences during the Tribunal rule; however, intercultural weddings were not unheard of.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ASH1-57)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-58)</sup> After the rise of the [New Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Temple), the previously persecuted Ashlanders, who had continued to worship the three Good Daedra throughout the Tribunal's rule, are now lauded as the keepers of the old ways. It is now quite common for many of the House Dunmer to make the arduous pilgrimages into the ash wastes, tightening ties between the two peoples.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TR-59)</sup>

Although most Dunmer marriages are monogamous, polygamy is practiced in some situations. Same-sex marriages and relationships are accepted within Dunmer society.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-60)</sup>

## Khajiit
The Kingdom of Elsweyr was founded when [Eshita](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eshita) of Pellitine married [Keirgo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keirgo) of Anequina in [2E 309](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_309).<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-PGE1E-61)</sup> The blessing of one's parent beforehand is usually customary for marriage proposals (the daughter getting the blessing from the mother, and the son getting the blessing from the father).<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-ACS-62)</sup> Khajiiti weddings are typically week-long celebrations and are often elaborate and costly. Securing the approval and support of the spouse's parent is considered of paramount importance in the arrangement of the wedding.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-LAHWMM-63)</sup> Sister-by-Mara and Brother-by-Mara were terms used by the Khajiit to refer to the spouses of their siblings.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Adara.27hai-64)</sup>

## Maormer
Little is known about the marriage rites of the Maormer, as they closely guard their traditions. One recorded account of a union between a Maormer and a Khajiit described the couple partaking in a Maormeri custom - standing in the surf with their clasped hands bound by a [sea serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Serpent) hatchling. The serpent symbolized new life binding new love and tested the pair by lashing and biting them. The couple sang their vows without faltering, calming the creature and proving their devotion. According to tradition, failure to convince the serpent of their love would result in the pair being dragged beneath the waves and claimed by the sea. In the recorded case, however, the lovers emerged safely from the rising tide, still hand in hand.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LAHDMM-25)</sup>

## Nords
It is a common misconception that the early Nordic term "War-Wives" refers to wedded wives. The term is used interchangeably with "Shield-Sisters", and refers to women warriors. The term "War-Wife" also has little to do with the amorous relations she has with others; a War-Wife can be married to a Shield-Brother (warrior men) or a non-warrior. The relationships between War-Wives and their betrothed were mostly monogamous.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-QA1-10)</sup>

As life is considered short and harsh in Skyrim, courtships are brief. Generally those interested in marriage wear an amulet of Mara to announce their willingness to marry.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Maramal-65)</sup>

## Orcs
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/SR-interior-Burguk%27s_Longhouse.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Burguk%27s_Longhouse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Burguk%27s_Longhouse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Burguk%27s_Longhouse.jpg) Orcish polygamy [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) Stronghold marriages under the Code of Malacath are typically between the chief and numerous partners. The only male allowed to be married and to father children is the chief of the clan, a title gained and lost through challenge and combat.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TCOM-66)</sup> He will marry an unlimited number of women, who become his hearth-wives, forge-wives, hunt-wives, and shield-wives.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Vo FO-67)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TCOM-66)</sup> Hearth-wives are the ones who will most commonly sleep with her chief and take care of household business; forge-wives are very appreciated among her strongholds, as they master the fires of the forges; hunt-wives are more common in isolated strongholds, where hunting is more important to the economy of her clan; and shield-wives are known to be fighters and accompany her chiefs in battle.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-CBD-68)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-KKD-69)</sup>

Despite the Code of Malacath regulations, the chief and his wives aren't the only Orcs who effectively procreate. Many of the Stronghold Orcs are forming unions, that are not official according to the Malacath faith, despite the regulations imposed by the Code.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Ashgel-70)</sup>

Traditionally, the title of chief can only be achieved by male Orcs, but in certain exceptional circumstances, a female Orc may rule her clan.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Ush D-71)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Vo FO-67)</sup> During the successive foundations of Orsinium, Kings acted as *primus inter pares* chief and married a large number of other chiefs' daughters to strengthen the kingdom.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-KKD-69)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-FMA-72)</sup> In cases where a female Orc breaks away from tradition, they will sometimes take on multiple husbands, and the husbands will fill the traditional role of the wives.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Snar-73)</sup> Some strongholds like [Mor Khazgur](Lore_Mor_Khazgur.md) have abandoned the more cruder aspects of the Code of Malacath and allow members of the stronghold to marry whoever they wish without restriction.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-OT-74)</sup>

There exists a certain Orcish traditional engagement ritual.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Rlfl-75)</sup> First, the suitor must smell of battle and of his enemy's blood. An Orc often fights a powerful foe before wooing a wife.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Ytz-76)</sup> Then he must give the best [echatere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Echatere) cheese and spiced ale to the maiden as a present, which they are thought to have aphrodisiac properties.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Rlfl-75)</sup> Finally, the wooer should recite a love poem, typically with war, blood, or revenge background, but at the same time, flattering.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Brsd-77)</sup> Once performed, the maiden may or may not choose to accept the proposal.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Rlfl-75)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Lzdt-78)</sup>

The sons of the clan will not be able to marry until they become chiefs of their own clan. Those unable to defeat their own fathers are destined to lowly duty to their clans or exile. The chieftain is replaced by whichever member of the clan grows strong enough to challenge and, usually, kill him.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TCOM-66)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-HWD-79)</sup>

When a chief passes away, the mother of the next chief (and widow of the previous one) becomes the "mother" of the stronghold (or kingdom, in some cases). The name combinations are the same: Hearth-Mother, Forge-Mother, Hunt-Mother, or Shield-Mother.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-FMA-72)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TCOM-66)</sup>

The daughters of the clan will often be betrothed to other chiefs to strengthen the relationship between allied clans or to forge new alliances. These arranged marriages are most valued. Orc women may want to escape being "just another wife" to the chieftain. They leave to join the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), see the world, or otherwise seek their fortune; some eventually return to the strongholds, but many do not.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TCOM-66)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Lkr D-80)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-HWD-79)</sup>

Marriage customs among [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) -worshiping Orsimer differ, as they do not follow the Code of Malacath.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-KKD-69)</sup> The High King of the Orcs, historically the ruler of Orsinium, is allowed to practice polygamy, often marrying many more wives than even stronghold chieftains.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-KKD-69)</sup> According to the teachings of High Priestess Solgra, if two Orcs are married at heart, it is as valid as a real marriage.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Ashgel-70)</sup> Depending on the political situation, an Orc chief may have no choice but to let other married Trinimac-worshiping Orcs reside in a stronghold.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Balarkh-81)</sup>

Orcs who live in the cities with other races are likely to face prejudice. Interbreeding with other races of Tamriel is so rare that it is commonly thought impossible.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-NORP-82)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-JOTLL-83)</sup>

Traditionally, Orcs are married at the stroke of midnight.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-TWWD-84)</sup>

## Redguards
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/RG-comic-Page_20-Hakan_Iszara_Wedding.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_20-Hakan_Iszara_Wedding.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_20-Hakan_Iszara_Wedding.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_20-Hakan_Iszara_Wedding.jpg) A Redguard wedding in Sentinel It is uncommon for Crowns and [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) to marry each other. In [Bergama](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bergama), such a thing is almost unheard of, as the Crowns and Forebears' allegiances have remained unchanged for many years.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ESOJagnas-85)</sup> Some Forebears believe that their kin shouldn't associate themselves with a Crown, let alone marry one, and vice versa.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-86)</sup> Some Redguards believe that such a marriage would never last because the Crowns and Forebears are so different.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ESOJagnas-85)</sup> The Redguard High King [Ar-Azal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ar-Azal) was polygamous, and married two noblewomen: [Fereshtah‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fereshtah) and [Arlimahera‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arlimahera) in an attempt to secure peace between Crowns and Forebears.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-87)</sup>

## Reachmen
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths.jpg) Ceremony of Handfasting [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/ON-place-Hroldan_Ring_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hroldan_Ring_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hroldan_Ring_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hroldan_Ring_02.jpg) Handfasting Cave Reachfolk marriages are traditionally preceded by handfasting, and are often political in nature. The promises can be organized without approval of the couple,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ONES-88)</sup> though their agreement is needed for the marriage to be officiated. They are sometimes called "marriage pacts",<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-WGS-89)</sup> and are used to end wars and strengthen alliances between clans. The handfasting vows are spoken in front of a mediator such as the [Chief-of-Chiefs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Clans#The_Chief-of-Chiefs) instead of a priest or shaman.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-ESOCCC-90)</sup> A Reachman can be promised to more than one suitor at a time,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ONES-88)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-91)</sup> but Reachfolk marriages are monogamous, and all other promises are broken upon the official ceremony of handfasting.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONVAO-6)</sup> The [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) of the [Eagleseer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eagleseer_Clan) and [Six-Ford](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Six-Ford_Clan) clans intended to hold a handfasting ceremony upon an altar of Mara at [Hroldan Ring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hroldan_Ring) to unite the two clans circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONVAO-6)</sup>

Intended couples usually are isolated for a short period of time at the handfasting cave so they can learn about one another before the rites are pefrormed in case the suitors never meet each other prior to the ceremony.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-ESOCCC-90)</sup>

Handfasting rites are represented by traditional knotwork, which is often carved on rings.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-92)</sup> The handfasting rite can be also performed in secrecy.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-93)</sup> One way to perform handfasting rites is by sharing words of promise and exchanging the rings made of birch-wood.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-TTWT-94)</sup> Small beads carved from blue stone are used in Reach courtship rituals.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ESOBead-95)</sup>

Some couples fashion the Lover's Coil, a circular<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ESOLC1-96)</sup> totem made of stone symbolizing the couple's love by representing the spirals of the participants' lives entwining. If such a totem is destroyed, it can be interpreted as the spirits' displeasure and lack of the approval for the couple's union. This belief is not shared uniformly by all Reachfolk, as some find it superstitious.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-97)</sup>

Unions and marriages between Reachfolk of the clans that venerate Hagravens (such as the [Crow-Wife Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow-Wife_Clan)) and Hagravens are permitted.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-WN-98)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-ALBAB-99)</sup> Married Hagravens are referred to as Hag-wives, while male partners of Hagravens are given the title of Hag-husband. Those who marry Hagravens are often respected and feared by the members of their clan.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-ALBAB-99)</sup>

Some sources claim that in certain regions of the Reach and rural High Rock regions, spriggans are said to be charmed to participate in weddings by ensnaring the hands and feet of the betrothed with roots. Upon release, the spriggan can either leave peacefully or pose a threat to the couple. The outcome is viewed as an omen for the marriage's success. If the spriggan becomes hostile, it is defeated, and the couple consumes its sap to ward off any perceived curse. Scholars speculate on the tradition's possible connection to the Snow Elves.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TW-54)</sup> During the Interregnum, the city of Markarth, not unlike many others was decorated with Amulets of Mara in celebration of Heart's Day.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-Markarth Mara-100)</sup> By the Fourth Era, the practice of marrying under Mara's guidance was also widespread.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBOM-7)</sup>

## Other
[Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) are known to have had marriage,<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-ADT-101)</sup> a tradition they shared with the Imperial denizens of [Hakoshae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hakoshae).<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-Ashur-102)</sup> The Kothringi are likewise known to have practiced marriage,<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-Shul-103)</sup> but nothing is known of the specifics. Some sources claim that [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) also engaged in marriages, and some speculate that remnants of their practices may have influenced the marriage customs of modern Bretons and Reachfolk.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TW-54)</sup>

Little is known of the marriage customs of the Dwemer, or if they even practiced marriage.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-Tales X-104)</sup>
