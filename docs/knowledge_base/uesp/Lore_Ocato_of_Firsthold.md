Ocato of Firsthold<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Df UGH-1)</sup> ([/ˈ ˈə͡ʊ-kˈɑː-tˈə͡ʊ/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) *Owe- **cah** -toe* Link ⏵) was the [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) and Lord High Chancellor of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) under [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). After Uriel VII's assassination in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Ocato, as head of the Elder Council, served as the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)'s de facto ruler, and he helped lead the Empire through the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). At the end of the Oblivion Crisis and the Third Era, [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), an illegitimate son of Uriel VII, sacrificed himself to end the crisis, but this rendered the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) extinct. Ocato convened the full Elder Council to select a new emperor, but the council failed to make a selection.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup> Without an emperor, imperial control outside of Cyrodiil quickly began to dissipate, so Ocato reluctantly agreed to become potentate, serving in the position until he was assassinated circa [4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Stormcrown)</sup> After his assassination, the Empire entered the chaotic period known as the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup>

## Biography

### Background
Little is known of Ocato's early life other than that he originally hailed from the city of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Df UGH-1)</sup>

### Elder Council and Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/DF-misc-Ocato.jpg/150px-DF-misc-Ocato.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Ocato.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Ocato.jpg) Ocato in 3E 405 By the Third Era's fifth century, Ocato was considered "long of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md)". He alone earned the position of the Emperor's confidant and chief advisor, being appointed [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) and High Chancellor of the Elder Council following [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s defeat and the restoration of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) to the [Dragon Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne) in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Df UGH-1)</sup>

In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), Ocato was present in the audience chamber of the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) when Uriel VII sent an [Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) to the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) to investigate the restless [ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) of King [Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus) of [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) and to find and destroy a letter the Emperor sent to Queen [Mynisera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mynisera) of Daggerfall.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Df UGH-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Df IS-4)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Ocato sent a letter ordering [Trebonius Artorius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trebonius_Artorius), the Arch-Mage of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), to retire.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Lf O-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWAM-6)</sup> By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), some believed that Ocato and the Elder Council had been the real rulers of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) for fifteen years.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Lt M-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MSHH-8)</sup>

### Oblivion Crisis
On 27 Last Seed, 3E 433, Uriel VII and his heirs were assassinated by the Dagonist [Order of the Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md), extinguishing the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) and triggering an invasion from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) known as the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBTut-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOC-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHCSEA.21-11)</sup> After the assassinations, Ocato and the Elder Council were named the Empire's regents, following the ancient precedent of the Elder Council ruling the empire until the crowning of a new emperor.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHCSEA.21-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBJd-12)</sup> Ocato, as head of the Elder Council, was the de facto ruler of the Empire.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CAVd-13)</sup> Ocato, speaking publicly for the Elder Council, asked the imperial citizenry to remain calm and remember the Emperor, his sons, and the Elder Council in their prayers.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHCSEA.21-11)</sup> Ocato committed the available resources of the Elder Council, the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), the [Imperial Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Guard), the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), and the [Imperial Battle College](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Battle_College) to try to bring the assassins to justice.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHCSEA.21-11)</sup> He also gave assurances that the Elder Council was in control of the situation and could effectively govern the Empire until a new heir was found.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HCOd-14)</sup>

Most of the counts and countesses of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) applauded the High Chancellor's efforts to hold the Empire together and commiserated with the difficult situation he faced.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CMUd-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CNCd-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-CAId-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-CJHd-18)</sup> However, a few of the counts did not approve of Ocato's actions during the crisis. Count [Marius Caro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marius_Caro) of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) believed Ocato and the Elder Council were overly concerned with the other provinces and were leaving the Cyrodilic counties to fend for themselves.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CMCd-19)</sup> Count [Regulus Terentius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Regulus_Terentius) of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) stated that he believed the High Chancellor planned to use his position as head of the Elder Council to become emperor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-CRTd-20)</sup>

According to Ocato, imperial generals informed him that the forces of the Imperial Army in the other provinces were already fully committed and could not send troops to defend Cyrodiil. Ocato also believed there would be a political crisis if he tried to recall any troops from the other provinces. As a result, Ocato was unable to send the Imperial Legion to help defend Cyrodiil.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HCOd-14)</sup> However, according to [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) Second Councilor Adril Arano, the Legion forces stationed in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) were recalled to defend Cyrodiil.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DBAA-21)</sup> Another source claims that the bulk of the imperial guards stationed in [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) were recalled as well.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-HRRVI-22)</sup>

Despite the best efforts of Ocato and the Elder Council, the Empire began disintegrating because of the crisis.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBJd-12)</sup> [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), an illegitimate son of Uriel VII, with the help of the Blades and the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), recovered the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) from the leader of the Mythic Dawn, [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), to relight the Dragonfires.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOC-10)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OBPar-23)</sup> After the amulet's recovery, Martin sent a messenger to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to inform Ocato that he wished to formally present his claim to the Imperial Throne to the Elder Council.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-OBMSd-24)</sup> When Martin arrived at the Elder Council Chambers in the Imperial Palace, Ocato informed him that the Elder Council accepted his claim to the throne and recognized him as emperor.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOC-10)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-OBLt D-25)</sup> Immediately afterward, Oblivion Gates opened in the Imperial City, so Ocato helped escort Martin to the [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One) to relight the Dragonfires.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-OBLt D-25)</sup> At the Temple of the One, Martin sacrificed himself by shattering the Amulet of Kings to become the avatar of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). In the ensuing battle, the avatar defeated [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) and sent the Prince of Destruction back to Oblivion, ending the Oblivion Crisis.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOC-10)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-OBLt D-25)</sup> Martin's sacrifice marked the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) and the beginning of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-OBES-26)</sup>

After Mehrunes Dagon's defeat, Ocato entered the temple, and the Hero of Kvatch informed him of Martin's fate.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HCOd-14)</sup> Ocato rewarded the Hero of Kvatch for their service to the Empire by proclaiming them Champion of Cyrodiil, the highest rank in the [Order of the Dragon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Dragon), and ordering them a suit of Imperial Dragon armor.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HCOd-14)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-OBIDA-27)</sup>

### Potentate of the Third Empire
While Martin's sacrifice ended the Oblivion Crisis, it rendered the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) extinct, which once again made Ocato and the Elder Council the rulers of the Empire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HCOd-14)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-OBES-26)</sup> Ocato anticipated trouble for the Empire's future, as the provinces were restive even prior to the Oblivion Crisis.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HCOd-14)</sup> Soon after the crisis, rumors circulated that some of the provinces were considering seceding from the Empire.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-OBGD-28)</sup>

Ocato believed that the Elder Council could hold the Empire together short-term, but that it needed an emperor to hold it together long-term, so his primary concern after the Oblivion Crisis was to select a new emperor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HCOd-14)</sup> Ocato convened the full Elder Council to select a new emperor, but the council failed to make a selection. Without an emperor, imperial control outside of Cyrodiil quickly began to dissipate. In [4E 1](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_1), Ocato reluctantly agreed to assume the position of potentate in accordance with the Elder Council Charter until imperial rule was reestablished.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup>

Ocato worked to stabilize the Empire and prevent its breakup, and by [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), he had made some progress on these fronts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup> In 4E 5, [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) on the island of Vvardenfell erupted, which devastated Morrowind and plunged the province into chaos.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RT3-2)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DBAA-21)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TGW-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TIC-30)</sup><sup>:234, 261</sup> Following the eruption of Red Mountain, [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) seceded from the Empire.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TGW-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TIC-30)</sup><sup>:206</sup> The following year, in [4E 6](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_6), the [Argonians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Argonians) exploited the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md)'s weakness by invading Morrowind in what became known as the [Accession War](Lore_Accession_War.md), which further devastated the province.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TIC-30)</sup><sup>:234, 261</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-LOS-31)</sup><sup>:133</sup>

Ocato's reign as potentate witnessed the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md)'s reemergence as a dominant political force in the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup> The Thalmor had always been a powerful faction in the Summerset Isles, but they had been a minority voice prior to the Oblivion Crisis.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TGW-29)</sup> However, during the crisis, the Thalmor were granted more power and authority, and they were credited with saving the Summerset Isles from the Daedric invaders, which boosted their popularity among the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TGW-29)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-RT2-32)</sup> Following this, the Thalmor began consolidating their power in the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-RT2-32)</sup> Possibly because he was an Altmer, Ocato reportedly took the reemergence of the Thalmor as a dominant political force more seriously than most.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup> However, before he could address the Thalmor threat, Ocato was assassinated circa [4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Stormcrown)</sup> It was believed that the Thalmor ordered his assassination. Following Ocato's assassination, the Empire entered the chaotic period known as the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RT4-3)</sup>
