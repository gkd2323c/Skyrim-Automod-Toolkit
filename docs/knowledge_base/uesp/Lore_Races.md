# Races


Several specific groupings, such as [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede), beastfolk, and [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit), have been considered so broad as to be technically worthless.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONSelene-5)</sup> However they are still often grouped together by most sources due to shared traits, as opposed to direct relations. For information on people of mixed descent, see the article on **[multiraciality](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Multiraciality)**.

There are several instances of otherwise animalistic or primitive [creatures](Lore_Bestiary.md), such as [trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll) and [mudcrabs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mudcrab),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> being capable of speech or higher intelligence; these are usually unique exceptions or characters in tales either fictional or shrouded in myth.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOViri-9)</sup> These fringe examples are not included on the page as they are not considered reflective of those species as a whole. Additionally, some [constructs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Construct) such as [Factotums](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Factotum), despite not being included on this page, can be capable of speech, higher intelligence, and even self-awareness.

Races in **bold** have been playable in the games.
