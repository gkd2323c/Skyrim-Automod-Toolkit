# Ohmes-raht

*When Jode is New and Jone is Waxing
Then shall be born the Ohmes-Raht
For Lorkhaj favored those he called Men
And told Azurah they would need guidance
Ohmes-Raht you cannot tell are Khajiit at all
Save that they have more hair than most
So long as they hide their tail
You may find them among Kings of Men.*
— *[All Our Perfect Forms](Lore_All_Our_Perfect_Forms.md)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/1/1e/Mnew_Swaxing.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mnew_Swaxing.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mnew_Swaxing.jpg) Ohmes-raht are born when Masser is new and Secunda is waxing The Ohmes-raht are a bipedal [furstock](Lore_Khajiit.md#Furstocks) of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Like the shorter [Ohmes](Lore_Ohmes.md), they can easily be mistaken as [men](Lore_Men.md) or [elves](Lore_Elves.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MUT-3)</sup> though have a stronger resemblance to humans.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AOPF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IWTB-5)</sup> They are one of the furstocks that walk plantigrade, much like humans.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IWTB-5)</sup> Some early literature claims their resemblance to men is because [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md) showed favor to the races of men, and had [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) shape the Ohmes-Raht in their image to provide guidance to humanity. As a result, they could be found among the kings of men.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AOPF-4)</sup>

However, despite their similarities to men and mer, they still display features that mark them as Khajiit. The Ohmes-raht have tails and their bodies tend to be covered with a layer of thin fur.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AOPF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IWTB-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Survival G-6)</sup> Much like their Ohmes kin, they also [paint](Lore_Cosmetics.md) their faces to resemble a feline-aspect.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RDKDF-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Survival G-6)</sup>

The spirit [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md) has been depicted as distinct from any known furstock, but displays some similarity to the Ohmes-raht.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOSB-8)</sup>
