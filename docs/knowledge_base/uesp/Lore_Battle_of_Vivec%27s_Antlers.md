# Battle of Vivec's Antlers

The **Battle of Vivec's Antlers** or **Battle of Ebonheart Beach**<sup>[[nb 1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> of [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572) was the decisive military confrontation of the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) (known as the Liberation War among Argonians). An unprecedented coalition of [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) and [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) troops, later reinforced by the unexpected intervention of free [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), managed to defeat the fierce army of [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) snow demons on the beach of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) just west of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md), later known as [Vivec's Antlers](Lore_Vivec%27s_Antlers.md). In the aftermath, the victorious Nords, Dunmer and Argonians formed the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), an unlikely multiracial alliance that instantly became one of the great powers in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It also led to a large-scale (but not complete) abolition of slavery among members of the Pact, especially for Argonians in recognition of their crucial aid during the battle.

This conflict should not be confused with the [Battle at Vivec's Antlers](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md#Battle_at_Vivec.27s_Antlers) 10 years later, in 2E 582, between the Daggerfall Covenant and the Ebonheart Pact.

## Background
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/ON-place-Fort_Virak_Bridge.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fort_Virak_Bridge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Virak_Bridge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Virak_Bridge.jpg) The invaders met fierce Tamrielic resistance north of [Fort Virak](Lore_Fort_Virak.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-At S-2)</sup> *Further information: [Second Era Wars § Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Era_Wars.md#Second_Akaviri_Invasion)*
Contemporaries wondered why the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) invaded and what they were after; their objectives and troop movements were difficult to explain.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-At S-2)</sup> Reportedly, their King [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md) was searching for the "[Ordained Receptacle](Lore_Ordained_Receptacle.md)", but whether this term was correctly translated from Akaviri remains uncertain, much less what it even meant.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> Very little is known about the Kamali language and culture in general;<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> Tamrielic sources of the time referred to the snow demons as 'Snakes'<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-At S-2)</sup> or 'Snake-Men'.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTot EP-4)</sup>

After the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) suffered a devastating defeat at the hands of the Akaviri invaders in the Battle of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) (7 Sun's Height – 7 Last Seed 2E 572),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SIR-5)</sup> [Jorunn the Skald-Prince](Lore_Jorunn.md) of Eastern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) allegedly sought the aid of the reclusive [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), who summoned the mythical Ash-King, [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-JTSK-6)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> With the aid of the so-called Wulfharth, Jorunn rallied his Nordic troops and fortified [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), expecting an imminent assault.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> However, the invaders simply bypassed the city, passing into [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) in western [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> Their next target may have been [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Keshu Adventures1-7)</sup>

Entering Morrowind, the Akaviri invaders were opposed by Dark Elf forces commanded by general Tanval of [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), who staged a fighting retreat as the Akaviri slowly advanced towards eastern Stonefalls.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> A battle took place between Riften and [Fort Virak](Lore_Fort_Virak.md), where the outnumbered defenders held out for some time against the Akaviri before the latter broke through, and afterwards also captured Fort Virak.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-At S-2)</sup>

## The Battle

### Nordic–Dunmeri Pincer Attack
The battle had begun between the Akaviri, led by Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal, and the Dark Elves under the command of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and her leading general, Grandmaster Tanval Indoril. On a beach of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) just to the west of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) that would later be named [Vivec's Antlers](Lore_Vivec%27s_Antlers.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESO-11)</sup> Dunmeri troops managed to halt the invaders, and engaged them at their prepared defenses.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> Meanwhile Jorunn and the so-called Wulfharth (and reportedly the Stormfirst Brigade<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSC-8)</sup>) pursued the Akaviri force into a surprise pincer attack.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> As the Nords under Jorunn and Wulfharth landed and fell upon the Akaviri rear, the tide of battle turned. The fighting was fierce, but the Nord army was overextended, the Kamali lines remained intact as they retreated to the Inner Sea, where they desperately tried to hold out for evacuation.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup>

### Argonian Intervention
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg/250px-ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg) Vivec's Antlers 10 years later The battle hung in the balance until an Argonian phalanx of Shellbacks unexpectedly reinforced the Dunmeri army, turning a stalemate into a slaughter.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UA-12)</sup> Sources disagree on how many groups of Argonians intervened, and who their commanders were. Self-liberated slave [Heita-Meen](Lore_Heita-Meen.md) recounted that, after receiving a vision from a [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree that she had to save the Dunmer and Nords from certain death, she took control of the [Archein](Lore_Archein.md) Guard in Thornmarsh and joined up with the Shellbacks of Walks-in-Ash in [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FAt S-10)</sup> She then allowed any Saxhleel who objected to rescuing the endangered Dark Elves to return home, marched to Stonefalls with a still strong force and entered the fray.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FAt S-10)</sup> A Western Skyrim historian confirms 'two legions of Argonians' entering the battle from the south at the last moment;<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> however, Jorunn's biographer mentions 'a phalanx of Argonian shellbacks, led by a trio of reptilian battlemages',<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-JTSK-6)</sup> suggesting a third leader. Biographer Mee-See stated that [Keshu the Black Fin](Lore_Keshu_the_Black_Fin.md)'s Legion played an important part in driving back the Akaviri invaders in general, and that Keshu took part in the formation of the Pact in Ebonheart on the next day, but is vague about whether the Black Fin Legion participated in the Battle of Vivec's Antlers.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Keshu Adventures1-7)</sup>

At first, the Dark Elves were alarmed to see a force of armed Argonians heading towards them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-At S-2)</sup> According to rumors, some initially even turned their weapons on the Shellbacks, either because they feared the ex-slaves wanted to take revenge,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FAt S-10)</sup> or because they felt too proud to be rescued by Argonians.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-At S-2)</sup> The Dunmer were shocked when the free Argonians actually joined their ranks.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FAt S-10)</sup> A Nord soldier, whose father was present, wrote: 'No one expected this. I still cannot believe it. The Dunmer once held the Argonians as slaves, yet on that day, the Argonians' arrival changed the course of history. From the jungles they came, adorned with blood and mud. Akaviri fell beneath their claws, as well as the swords and spells of their Nord and Dunmeri allies.'<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UA-12)</sup>

### Outcome
Finally, the combined Tamrielic forces broke the Akaviri line and defeated the invaders.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> There is no agreement in sources on the exact outcome: either the Akaviri were slaughtered until there were none left because their fleet (already visible on the horizon) arrived too late to evacuate them,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup> or they were driven into the sea and drowned by the thousands,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-JTSK-6)</sup> or the remaining Akaviri were forced to flee.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FAt S-10)</sup> According to the *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition.md)* (published 2E 864), centuries later, remnants of the Kamal invasion force who had somehow survived the battle resurfaced some time before [2E 812](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_812) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), briefly seizing the Ruby Throne before being defeated again.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGTTE1E-13)</sup>

Some later Dunmeri sources claim that, at some point during the battle, Lord [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) assisted by drowning (some of) the Akaviri at Vivec's Antlers. This narrative referenced in later Dunmeri culture,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup> but contemporary sources do not mention Vivec's interference, nor a flood.<sup>[[nb 2]](#cite_note-Vivec_flood-9)</sup> Nevertheless, within a decade, the previously unnamed beach with its coral maze where the battle took place had become known as 'Vivec's Antlers' among the Dunmer, as some had come to believe that Vivec had saved them during the Akaviri invasion, and the corals were Vivec's gift to the Dark Elves.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-WIA-15)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Regardless, even some Dark Elves seemed not to have been convinced, as evidenced by an anonymous anti-Tribunal pamphlet spread in the aftermath which denied intervention by the Three.<sup>[[nb 4]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

## Aftermath
*Further information: [Argonian § The Akaviri Invasion and the Formation of the Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Argonian.md#The_Akaviri_Invasion_and_the_Formation_of_the_Ebonheart_Pact)*

### Formation of the Ebonheart Pact
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/ON-place-Ebonheart.jpg/250px-ON-place-Ebonheart.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ebonheart.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ebonheart.jpg) [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) in 2E 582. [The Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was formed here after the Battle of Vivec's Antlers. The conflict united three races who had held great animosity towards each other for centuries, leading to the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UA-12)</sup> As a show of thanks for the Argonians' timely intervention, the Dunmer formally ended the enslavement of Lizard Folk. The practice of slavery itself did not end, despite the Argonians' liberation.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-GTTEP-19)</sup> The most detailed account of the Pact's formation is recorded by Mee-See, aide and biographer of [Keshu the Black Fin](Lore_Keshu_the_Black_Fin.md), an Argonian anti-slavery freedom fighter and friend of Jorunn's who had joined the war at his request:<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Keshu War2-20)</sup>

*When the fighting finally ended near the city of Ebonheart and victory was ours, Keshu hurried to meet with the leaders of the other two factions. I, as her loyal squire, followed in her wake. (...) Jorunn stepped forward and greeted Keshu like an old friend. "We owe you a great debt, Black Fin," Jorunn said in his loud, booming voice. "What can the Nords and the Dark Elves offer you for your invaluable assistance this day?"* *Keshu was silent for a few long moments, looking intently at Jorunn first, and then turning her attention to Almalexia. With her eyes locked upon the Mother of Morrowind, she finally replied, "No more Argonian slaves. Set my people free."*

*Almalexia and Jorunn exchanged a look of their own, and the large Nord's gaze never wavered. After a time, the Dark Elf leader made a slight nod of her head and said, "A reasonable request. And one the Dark Elves shall honor—with one provision. The Argonians must join the Dark Elves and the Nords in a mutual pact of cooperation and defense. In that way, all three of our nations will remain free."*

*Thus began a series of negotiations that lasted well into the next day and ended with the formation of the Ebonheart Pact.*
— *Mee-See*<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Keshu Adventures1-7)</sup> Shortly after its formation, the Pact widely distributed a [pamphlet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pact_Pamphlet__Congratulations!), proudly announcing: 'By Order of the alliance herein referred to as the Ebonheart Pact, and with the agreement of the ruling bodies of Skyrim, Morrowind, and Black Marsh, let it be known now and forever that slavery has been abolished in all Pact territories and principalities.' However, it stated that only the [Houses Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), and [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) had agreed to the stipulation that all members of all races were now free and independent citizens, and that 'Citizenship and freedom are not guaranteed in other Dark Elf territories.'<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-21)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ZOSCorrespondence-22)</sup>

In order to hold the Pact together against all odds, the three races represented in the [Great Moot](Lore_Great_Moot.md) were required to operate with mutual respect and amazing will, and to mollify the pride of the Nords and the Dark Elves while addressing the injuries suffered by the once-enslaved Argonians.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-GTTEP-19)</sup>

### Groups Opting Out
- **Skyrim:** The Nords of Western Skyrim did not join the Ebonheart Pact, as they had not been affected by the Second Akaviri Invasion and thus saw no need for an alliance, were skeptical that a Nordic–Dunmeri–Argonian alliance could work given their historic emnity, and had also been at odds with their fellow Nords of Eastern Skyrim for a long time.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSAI-3)</sup>

- **Morrowind:** Some Dunmer rejected the terms and therefore refused to enter the Pact. Most notably, and much to the chagrin of the other [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md), [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) opted out, because it wanted to keep its Argonian slaves.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ZOSCorrespondence-22)</sup> It also banished all troops of other Dunmer houses from its territory.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TGHTH-23)</sup> By contrast, [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) reached a compromise agreement by which it did join the Pact, and did manumit its Argonian slaves, but retained its [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) slaves.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ZOSCorrespondence-22)</sup>

- **Black Marsh:** Many Argonians felt that the Dunmer deserved retribution for their crimes.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-AFAM-24)</sup> Some Argonians left their homeland entirely and settled in other provinces.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-26)</sup> According to Heita-Meen, 'not every tribe has joined us—only those of Thornmarsh, Shadowfen, and Murkmire.' She attributed the unwillingness of the other Saxhleel tribes to accede to the Pact to the 'bitter blood' that was still flowing between them and the Dark Elves, hoping that full reconciliation and comradery would be possible in the future.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FAt S-10)</sup>

### Brothers' War
As Jorunn the Skald-Prince returned home to Windhelm, a succession dispute arose between him and his twin-brother [Fildgor Strong-Prince](Lore_Fildgor.md), who had jointly defeated the Akaviri invaders after the Sack of Windhelm in which their elder sister [Queen Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md) fell.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-27)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSC-8)</sup> The brothers disagreed on who should succeed their sister and how to rule the land going forward, leading Jorunn to challenge Fildgor to single combat.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-27)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSC-8)</sup> This long and fierce duel, known as the [Brothers' War](Lore_The_Brothers%27_War.md), was won by Jorunn, who exiled Fildgor and was crowned the Skald-King of Eastern Skyrim,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-27)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSC-8)</sup> three weeks after the Battle of Vivec's Antlers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-JTSK-6)</sup>

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-1) Sources do not provide a common name for this event. All that can be corroborated is that it took place on the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md)'s beach west of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md). At the time of the events in 2E 572, this area had no name. The expression "Vivec's Antlers" has been attested 10 years later, but only amongst some Dunmeri devotees of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) who believe this deity intervened in the events; a claim which, however, cannot be confirmed by any non-Dunmer sources, and is even rejected by one anti-Tribunal Dunmer source. Furthermore, the [Land Coral](Lore_Coral.md#Land_Coral) on Ebonheart beach, which some Dark Elves afterwards began calling "antlers of Vivec" as a remnant of the battle, appear to have existed on the Stonefalls shore of the Inner Sea already before the battle, as well on the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) side of the sea. The [Coral Heart](Lore_Coral_Heart.md) that was found amongst the Land Corals on Ebonheart beach in 2E 582 allegedly already existed in the Merethic Era, when it was used to imprison Sadal, one of the Brothers of Strife. Whether the Land Corals and Vivec had anything to do with the Akaviri–Tamrielic beach battle of 2E 572 is therefore dubious.
2. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Vivec_flood_9-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Vivec_flood_9-1)***</sup> According to a legend recorded centuries later in *[Varieties of Faith in the Empire](Lore_Varieties_of_Faith....md)*, first attested in 3E 427, Vivec 'taught [the Dunmer] how to breathe water for a day so that he/she could flood Morrowind and kill the Akaviri invaders, ca. 2E572.' This version of the battle is not found in sources closer to the events. An apparent earlier version of the same book, *[Varieties of Faith in Tamriel](Lore_Varieties_of_Faith_in_Tamriel.md)*, allegedly written by the same author but first attested in 2E 582, makes no mention of this miracle of Vivec. Nordic and Argonian accounts of the battle do not mention Vivec, and even the pro-Dunmeri author Alla Llaleth [writing](Lore_The_Time_of_the_Ebonheart_Pact.md) about 8 to 10 years after the events, and praising the 'three Living Gods' in general, makes no mention of either Vivec or a flood during the Akaviri invasion, instead emphasizing the Eastern Nords' contributions to the victory.

His children breathed the holy water while the Akaviri drowned. It was a great victory for Morrowind.' She made no mention of the Nordic contributions, nor the Argonian intervention.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

We were the ones who fought on the battlefield. We were the ones who gave our blood and lost our sons and daughters.'
