*"This is Destruction's Solace, twin to Ardent Hope," Mehrunes Dagon replied. "Once I reigned over all my realm from this place. I filled these halls with my servants and the trophies of my countless victories. But then I laid it to waste and destroyed all within."* *"But why, Lord Dagon?" I asked, shivering in my terror.*

*"Because I am Destruction, and it is what I must do." Dagon swept an arm across the empty court. "Remember the ghosts, measure the devastation, bear witness to the finality of my purpose. When you return to your mortal form, tell all whom you meet of what you have seen. By my hand alone shall their ends be given meaning. Now go."*
— *[A Vision of the Twin Citadels](Lore_A_Vision_of_the_Twin_Citadels.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AVot TC-1)</sup> Mehrunes Dagon (formally, **the Exalted and Most Puissant Lord, Gerent of Dagon, Mehrunes**),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSDremora-2)</sup> also called **the great Daedra Lord**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSDremora-2)</sup> **the Black Daedra Lord**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum B-3)</sup> **Lord of Change**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Silus-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ranaline-5)</sup> **Prince of Ambition**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> **Prince of Disaster and Destruction**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-7)</sup> **Master of Razors**,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BV1-8)</sup> **Sovereign of Destruction**, the **Flame Tyrant**, the **Father of Cataclysm**,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTISV8-9)</sup> the **Golden Flame**,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Titan-11)</sup> the **Flame-Father**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> and the **Mer-Pride's End**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-7)</sup> is the **[Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Darkness and Destruction**,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-13)</sup><sup>:18</sup> whose sphere encompasses destruction, change, revolution, energy, and ambition.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TBOD-14)</sup> The **Lord of Fire and Flood**<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-7)</sup> is associated with natural dangers like fires or earthquakes.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OO-15)</sup> Flash floods, thunderstorms, and other natural disasters have been linked to communions between him and his cultists.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BV1-8)</sup> The Prince is also said to be the author of the [*Mysterium Xarxes*](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MX-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MDC1-17)</sup> Mehrunes Dagon has an insatiable hunger for destruction.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FAAYQ-18)</sup>

Dagon's destruction is not without purpose, as it is believed to be a means of change and violent rebellion.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Bow Of Rebellion-19)</sup> Though Dagon is best known for his domain of destruction, it is said that that is actually just a portion of his sphere of influence. In actuality, all of his aspects are rooted in Change, one of the [Eleven Forces](Lore_Eleven_Forces.md), thus that force flows very strongly within his realm.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONChange-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONValsirenn-21)</sup>

[Xivilai Moath](Lore_Xivilai_Moath.md) and [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md) are Dagon's adoptive children.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-13)</sup><sup>:156,159</sup> At one point, the [Dark Seducer](Lore_Dark_Seducer.md) Si'ess was his paramour and his bodyguard.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BSSiess-22)</sup> There is a belief that Mehrunes Dagon is allied with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb_1)</sup> though [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md) claims that if such an arrangement exists, it would be obscure outside their own circle, and that the relationship between Daedric Princes cannot be captured by "cheap mortal dualisms" of "Friend and Foe". Nonetheless Dagon and Hircine do sometimes pursue mutual interests that make them act in concert.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-23)</sup> Enemies of the Prince of Destruction include [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md),<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DFFactions-24)</sup> [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Vonos-25)</sup> and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), with whom Dagon shares a bitter rivalry—the two have been known to meddle in each other's schemes when the opportunity arises.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FAAYQ-18)</sup> It is also rumored that the Prince hates [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-LFTBH-26)</sup> [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) considers Dagon among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as he was one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md), and claims he "makes art of destruction".<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONAzura-27)</sup> Dagon's [protonymic](Lore_Nymic.md) is **Lehkelogah** (listen Link ⏵)<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BSChimere-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TMKR-29)</sup> and his neonymic is **Djehkeleho-dehbe-effehezepeh**. (listen Link ⏵)<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-INAN-30)</sup> The Prince's summoning day coincides with the [Warriors Festival](Lore_Warriors_Festival.md), the 20<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Dusk.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-31)</sup>

## Mythology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg/200px-OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg) Page one of the *Mysterium Xarxes*
### The *Mysterium Xarxes*
*When I walk the earth again, the faithful among you shall receive your reward. To be set above all other mortals, forever. As for the rest, the weak shall be winnowed, the timid shall be cast down, the mighty shall tremble at my feet and pray for pardon.*
—Mehrunes Dagon, *[Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes_(book).md)*<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MX-16)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TILTranslating-32)</sup> Little is known of the contents of the *Mysterium Xarxes* outside of the alleged translations given in [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md)'s *[Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md)*.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MX-16)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MDC-33)</sup> While the *Commentaries* were known to be used in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) to surreptitiously recruit members into Dagon's cult, the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md),<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-OBPath-34)</sup> they also contain a peculiar account of the Prince's origins.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MDC-33)</sup>

According to the *Commentaries*, the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) secretly created Mehrunes Dagon in "the very bowels of [Lyg](Lore_Lyg.md)".<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MDC4-35)</sup> Lyg, it is claimed, is an "Adjacent Place", a sort of parallel version of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKAMA-36)</sup> and the domain of a deity known as "the Upstart who vanishes".<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MDC4-35)</sup> Lyg, the "Mundex Terrene", was once ruled by [dreugh-kings](Lore_Dreugh.md) from the "nineteen and nine and nine" seas, who waged endless wars against each other's "slave oceans".

Dagon was created to be a prince of good, and was imbued with [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md)'s most precious asset, hope. The Prince began a revolution, "threw down Lyg", and "cracked his face".<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MDC4-35)</sup> He let the lowly slaves know free will, and stirred them to violence against their masters.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-MDC2-37)</sup> His "red legion" overthrew the tyrant dreughs, toppled the towers of CHIM-EL GHARJYG, destroyed great cities, and slaughtered the templars of the Upstart. Hope spread like a "brush-fire" as the world was set Free,<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MDC4-35)</sup> hence his title of the **Sea's Flame Redeemer**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-7)</sup>

Regardless of the veracity of this account, the tome played a role in the assassination of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and his heirs, starting the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MDC4-35)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Oblivion-38)</sup>

A story from obscure [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) myth provides an alternative origin of the Daedric Prince, claiming him to have once been a kinder being who was cursed by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

## Worship and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/ON-crown_store-Deadlands_Excoriated.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Deadlands_Excoriated.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Deadlands_Excoriated.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Deadlands_Excoriated.jpg) Cultist markings painted with sacred crimson ink into flensed skin [![](https://images.uesp.net/b/bc/ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes_Dagon-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes_Dagon-emblem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes_Dagon-emblem.png) An emblem representing Mehrunes Dagon Worshippers of the Prince of Destruction are known as Dagonites<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MDC3-40)</sup> and Dagonists.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DTt A-41)</sup>

He is revered by many groups Daedra, such as the [Crimson Oath](Lore_Crimson_Oath.md), who know the **Great Prince** by many names and call themselves the oathsworn of the **Red Father**, scarlet reavers of the **Razor King**, and bloodletters of the **Fire-Veined God**.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-CM105-42)</sup>

In most cultures, Mehrunes Dagon is little more than a god of bloodshed and betrayal.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-VOF-43)</sup> Dagon revels in destruction on a grand scale, from mass murder to deaths resulting from floods or earthquakes. He leaves a swath of devastation whenever he manifests. In fact, even attempting to summon the Prince is frequently fatal for the conjurer and any bystanders. Trafficking with Dagon or his cultists is abhorred by most of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and is outright illegal in many places, where the punishment can be as severe as death.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FAAYQ-18)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TTDMD-44)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BV1-8)</sup>.

He has a vested interest in showing flagrant displays of power to attract followers to his various cults and assert his influence on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TTDMD-44)</sup> He is an especially important deity in Morrowind, where he is the Second of the Four Corners of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-THOT-45)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-MWTholer-46)</sup> Worshippers of Dagon tend to be arsonists, setting buildings, animals, and even people on fire. Signs of cultist activity include unusual tremors, an uncharacteristic lack of rain and sudden onsets of unseasonably heavy rain as they attempt to incite natural disasters. Those who worship the Prince tend to bear the symbol of a fiery, rising sun.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TTDMD-44)</sup> Dagon's cults have a tendency to plan deep and long-burgeoning conspiracies.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FAAYQ-18)</sup>

Dagon's faithful followers are sometimes physically transformed by the Prince's blessing, an act which is said to forever change them. After undergoing bloody rites of devotion, Dagon's most zealous followers earn the right to shed their mortal form through undergoing hours of ritual scourging of their flesh. When the process is complete their skin is replaced by a coat of gleaming crimson scales. Another ritual involves flensing of the skin to prepare the cultist receive the sacred crimson ink that will give them markings reminiscent of those of Dagon himself.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ONScourger-47)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONExcoriated-48)</sup> More experienced cultists of Mehrunes Dagon are noted to have a high tolerance for heat, and it is said it takes a lot of it to make them uncomfortable.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ONTilgo-49)</sup>

### Argonians
The [Sul-Xan](Lore_Sul-Xan.md) Tribe consist of [naga](Lore_Naga.md) worshippers of chaos and death, and see Mehrunes Dagon as the **True Egg-Child of [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md)**.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-ESOLive1-50)</sup> This title can be understood from a pre- [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) perspective, when the old [ruling class](Lore_Nisswo.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) believed that Sithis was a malevolent destructive force,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Xode-51)</sup> much like Dagon. The Sul-Xan embrace only "the darkest, cruelest beliefs, and scorn the rest as weakness."<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ONBS-52)</sup> Thus, they sacrifice outsiders and other Argonians alike in hopes of one day summoning Mehrunes Dagon to bring blood and destruction across Black Marsh,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONSXN-53)</sup> and eventually the entire world.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Jela-54)</sup>

The Sul-Xan's worship of the **Razor Prince**<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-55)</sup> is an anomaly, as Argonian society at large does not partake in the veneration of powers from Oblivion. In fact, there are no words in their language [Jel](Lore_Jel.md) to describe any of the Daedra. As explained by a native speaker during the Interregnum, the Argonians do not "taint their language with words for abominations from Oblivion". Instead, they use common words from other more widely-spoken languages to refer to the beasts.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-KCAHC-56)</sup>

### Dunmer
In [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), Mehrunes Dagon is the **God of Destruction**<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-VOFTDE-57)</sup> **the Horror**,<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-DBOA-58)</sup> the **Destroyer-God**,<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-CM:104-59)</sup> the **Dark One**,<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-60)</sup> **Lord of Blood and Flame**,<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TMF-61)</sup> **Oblivion's Favored Son**, **Calamity's Tyrant King**,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TDo M-62)</sup> and the **King of Blood**<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-63)</sup> (contrasting his title of the **Hope of Blood**).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-7)</sup> It is said that when the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) led the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) to Resdayn (now [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)), they were joined by a number of Daedric ancestral spirits, among them Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-THOT-45)</sup> At first, these Daedra Lords were worshipped as gods.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TA-64)</sup> However, when the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) came to power in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Daedra were venerated as lesser spirits, subservient to the [Almsivi](Lore_Almsivi.md).<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-DD-65)</sup>

Not content with this power shift, [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), and Dagon rebelled against the new Temple, which caused much strife among the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_House.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-THOT-45)</sup> These Rebel Daedra, or Bad Daedra,<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-DD-65)</sup> became the Four Corners of the House of Troubles, who tempt the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) away from the orthodoxy.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TA-64)</sup> Nevertheless, the Dunmer consider these four to be holy in that they serve as Testing Gods who must be appeased.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-THOT-45)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-VOFTDE-57)</sup> Dagon represents Morrowind's near-inhospitable terrain,<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-VOFTDE-57)</sup> and also tests the Dunmeri will to live and prosper.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-THOT-45)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/MW-quest-Mehrunes_Dagon_of_the_House_of_Troubles.jpg/200px-MW-quest-Mehrunes_Dagon_of_the_House_of_Troubles.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Mehrunes_Dagon_of_the_House_of_Troubles.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Mehrunes_Dagon_of_the_House_of_Troubles.jpg) Mehrunes Dagon's shrine in [Ald Sotha](Lore_Ald_Sotha.md) According to legend, Mehrunes Dagon once threatened to heave a large rock at the Dunmer of [Maar Gan](Lore_Maar_Gan.md). [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) outwitted the Prince, taunting Dagon and making himself the rock's target. The rock itself was enshrined there, becoming known as the "Magic Rock of Maar Gan", and it became a pilgrimage site. Pilgrims would have to re-create the situation during a pilgrimage. A [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) named [Anhaedra](Lore_Anhaedra.md) was bound by Vivec to fill Dagon's role, and would have to endure taunts from pilgrims and try to harm them while they were protected by a powerful blessing.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-MWPilgrimage-66)</sup>

Another legend involves a duel between Vivec and the Prince of Destruction, the latter of whom was unarmed. Rather than dishonoring himself by fighting an unarmed opponent, Vivec offered Dagon his own silver longsword. The Prince's Dremora were impressed by this honorable act of chivalry. The Temple claims that although they are enemies, the Dremora share a bond of respect and courtesy with the followers of the Tribunal. As part of the Pilgrimages of the Seven Graces, pilgrims seeking to join the Tribunal Temple must visit the Shrine of Courtesy in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md)'s Puzzle Canal. A Dremora named Krazzt was bound here. Filling the role of Vivec, the pilgrim must be courteous and offer a silver longsword to Krazzt, who represents Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-67)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TPP-68)</sup>

### Khajiit
In the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) pantheon, Mehrunes Dagon is **Merrunz**,<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONJerkin-69)</sup> (also spelled **Mehrunnez**)<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONMehrunnez-70)</sup> who is also called **Daegon**,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-HFL-71)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TAS-72)</sup> the **Fire-Cat**,<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ONNisuzi-73)</sup> and the **Demon Cat**.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TAS-72)</sup> One of the children of [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md)'s second litter, Merrunz was given the nickname **Ja'Khajiit** —"kitten" in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md) —by his mother due to his wild, destructive nature.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-WOCMA-74)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TAS-72)</sup> Similarly, the Khajiit often depict Merrunz as a "destructive kitten".<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONJerkin-69)</sup>

According to pre- [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) texts, at some point in time, Merrunz was exiled by his father, [Ahnurr](Lore_Ahnurr.md). Rather than journeying through the [Many Paths of Time](Lore_Many_Paths.md), Merrunz chose to venture into the [Great Darkness](Lore_Great_Darkness.md). There, he was defeated by the Demon King [Molagh](Lore_Molagh.md) and tortured until the world was created. Ultimately, Merrunz was freed by Molagh's wife, who used his destructive nature to attack the [Lunar Lattice](Lore_Lunar_Lattice.md). Merrunz enjoyed this and became a "kinslayer", and has since been known as the demon **Dagon**.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-WOCMA-74)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TAS-72)</sup>

Pre-ri'Datta Khajiit myths speak of an alliance of the Adversarial Spirits [Molagh](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Merid-Nunda](Lore_Meridia.md) and Merrunz attacking the [Lunar Lattice](Lore_Moons.md), only to be repelled by its defenders, Azurah, [Boethra](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Mafala](Lore_Mephala.md) who also receive the aid of [Noctra](Lore_Nocturnal.md). In such myths the Lattice is described as burning spirits that touch it, causing Molagh to fall into the darkness below when pressed against it. It is said that no spirit had ever managed to damage it until Merrunz, released in those myths to serve as a weapon against the Lattice, cracked it with his axe. Though the Adversarial Spirits are repelled, the myths describe this exact conflict and events having repeated many times in the past, so many even the deities involved don't recall the number. During this conflict Mafala briefly ensnared Merrunz in her web and attempted to devour the knowledge he'd gained during his time in the Great Darkness.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TAS-72)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Boethra-75)</sup>

The duality of Khajiiti worship of the Fire-Cat can be seen early in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s history. During [Darloc Brae](Lore_Darloc_Brae.md)'s reign, cultists of Merrunz burned down the Grand Archive in [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md), which contained the province's records from the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). However, despite his worship of the Prince of Destruction, the warlord went through great lengths to preserve the remaining texts and hide them across Elsweyr, placing them in smaller vaults and archives.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-76)</sup> Darloc Brae showcased an ambition much like Merrunz, conquering [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) and the surrounding territories, with some areas erected in Daegon's name.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ESOLoad Darkpool-77)</sup>

### Reachmen
To the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md), Mehrunes Dagon is the spirit of ambition and destruction, and is known as the **Lord of Ambition**. Life in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) is harsh, and its inhabitants always strive to improve. This drive to become greater eventually leads some to turn to Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Aydolan-78)</sup>

### Order of the Waking Flame
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/ON-concept-Dagon_02.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Dagon_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dagon_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dagon_02.jpg) Dagon's connection to Magnus and the Magna Ge depicted The [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md) embraced the beliefs Sister [Celdina](Lore_Celdina.md) formed from the forbidden texts in [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md)'s Priory of the Golden Staff, that Mehrunes Dagon was created by the Magna Ge as a weapon to unmake the flawed creation of Mundus in accordance to [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md)'s plans. It is said that Magnus the Architect watches from the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md) until the day the destruction of the flawed world is achieved, so he can return and make it anew without its flaws.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-DCT-79)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-MTCSC-80)</sup> To them, Mundus is a place of pain and suffering, thus they looked to Dagon to move past their mortal perils, hoping that he would purge the impurities of existence so that it may emerge anew.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Eo MDV1-81)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ONCeldina-82)</sup> This contrasted the ideas of Lyranth, who believes that when the Magna Ge fled, they spat on the conviction of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) that remained to finish creating Mundus, and Dagon's emergence and sphere may be reflective of the destruction caused by their abandonment.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-23)</sup>

The cult's High Chronicler documented epics involving Mehrunes Dagon. One teaches that Dagon does not back away from bargains. Summoned by the Enchanter Arebath for his dominion over fire and flood, which would allow him to ensure her safety. Arabeth cast a [spell](Lore_Spells.md) to call the ocean's waters to rejuvenate a dead forest. However, having miscalculated the strength of the spell, she soon found herself in mortal peril, a mountainous wave crashing down on her. Remembering his bargain Dagon unleashed a great wave of flame that turned the water to steam, the clash between Dagon's flames and Arabeth's tidal spell purged the local blighted wildlife, ensuring that lush flora would rise from the ashes.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Eo MDV1-81)</sup> Another story tells of the Prince invading [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) to rescue the enslaved souls of his followers from Molag Bal. The true believers that refused to bend instead took it upon themselves to engage in subterfuge, thwarting Molag Bal and his minions whenever they could. Mehrunes Dagon rewarded those that endured with the means of their escape; a powerful explosion that rattled Coldharbour. The destruction allowed Dagon to manifest within the realm, looming over the mountains of Coldharbour, where he engaged in combat with Molag Bal. The two Princes exchanged furious strikes, their blows blurring together with speed and sending ear-shattering thunderclaps throughout the realm. Though Dagon's mere avatar was not able to defeat Molag Bal in his full power, this distraction bought enough time for him to transport his followers that refused to bend the knee to the Deadlands. Those that had submitted, however, were left behind, struck to the ground with bodies broken and ears bleeding from the devastation of Molag Bal and Mehrunes Dagon's duel.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Eo MDV2-83)</sup>

One account by a Devastator of the Waking Flame documents a vision that Mehrunes Dagon gave him in a dream. In the dream, the cultist is shown Dagon's citadel [Ardent Hope](Lore_Ardent_Hope.md) and is told to commit to memory the might of the citadel and the vastness of Dagon's legions, which demonstrate that his victory is inevitable and that only in serving him can the lives of his followers find meaning. After this, Dagon transformed into a cloud of burning cinders and flew upwards to his old citadel, Destruction's Solace, the cultist dragged along with him. At Destruction's Solace the cultist witnessed a ruined citadel populated only by tormented [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md). When he asked what had reduced the citadel and those within to its current state, Dagon revealed he'd destroyed them himself. Terrified by the revelation, the Devastator asked Dagon for a reason. Dagon responded that he did it because he is Destruction and so it is what he must do. Before dismissing the cultist's spirit back to the waking world of Nirn, Dagon urged him to measure the devastation so he could remember the finality of his purpose, and that only through Dagon would the end of his follower's lives be given meaning.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AVot TC-1)</sup>

The Waking Flame were known to sometimes use [Agonymium](Lore_Agonymium.md) Stones inscribed with Daedric runes, to absorb the mortal pain and suffering in the surroundings of the Stones and turn them into magical power.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-84)</sup>

### The Mythic Dawn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg/120px-OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg) Banner of the Mythic Dawn The [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) was once one of the most secretive and mysterious of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) cults<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-OBTar-Meena-85)</sup> before gaining infamy due to its role in the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Oblivion-38)</sup> The cult's beliefs were largely shaped by the teachings of its leader, [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), and his [*Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes*](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MDC-33)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Oblivion-38)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-TOC-86)</sup> The name "Mythic Dawn" is a reference to the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), the time before the creation of the world. Camoran believed he and his disciples were destined to rule over a reborn, "liberated" [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), ushering in a new Dawn.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MDC1-17)</sup>

Central to the cult's beliefs was the claim that the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) are not gods, merely pretenders who have tricked mortals ever since they betrayed and attempted to destroy [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md). The cult believes the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) are the true gods of the universe, as they cannot die. Camoran also asserted that Lorkhan is one of the Daedra, and offered this as to why the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) could not be destroyed. Furthermore, as a Daedric Prince, Lorkhan has his own [plane](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) —Tamriel.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb_2)</sup> Thus, Mehrunes Dagon undertook the Oblivion Crisis not to destroy Tamriel, but to "liberate the Occupied Lands" from the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md).<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-OBMankar-87)</sup>

In Tamriel, the cult called their enemies the "Dragon's Blood"—a reference to the covenant between [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and Queen [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) which protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-TAOK-88)</sup> More prosaically, it refers to the line of [rulers](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) keeping the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) alight.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MDC3-40)</sup> In particular, Mankar Camoran had a "long duel" with the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md),<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-OBMankar-87)</sup> dating back to the reign of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), when it is speculated the *Commentaries* were written.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-OBTar-Meena-85)</sup>

**Mehrunes the Razor, Mehrunes the Thieftaker, Mehrunes Godsbody**, and **Mehrunes the Red Arms That Went Up** are among some of the names given to the Prince of Destruction in the *Mythic Dawn Commentaries*.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MDC4-35)</sup>

The beliefs of the Mythic Dawn were met with derision by Lyranth the Foolkiller. She described the assertion that [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) is not distinct from Oblivion but rather one of its constitutive realms as false, and mocked the claim that the realm belongs to Dagon by birthright as "convenient". Lyranth questioned whether the information had come from Dagon himself or simply been spread by his cultists, and advised that "one should pay less attention to idiotic cultists".<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-23)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg) The ruins of Varsa Baalim In ancient times, Dagon created a construct known as the Nefarivigum to challenge those who drew near to undertake a trial. One who passed the trial would be deemed worthy of wielding [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md). Located in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the Nefarivigum was discovered by the ancient [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), who arrogantly believed they could turn away any who sought the Razor. To this end, they built the vast, ringed city of [Varsa Baalim](Lore_Varsa_Baalim.md) during the height of Ayleid civilization. For many years, pilgrims came to Varsa Baalim seeking the Razor, but the Ayleids turned them away. One day in the early years of the First Era, a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) infiltrated the city. Before long, the city was infested with undead and thrust into chaos. The city suddenly and mysteriously disappeared. None know what happened, but Varsa Baalim seemingly vanished into the mountains of the Eastern [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md).<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-TOAC-89)</sup>

Mehrunes Dagon and his forces destroyed [Ald Sotha](Lore_Ald_Sotha.md), home of [House Sotha](Lore_House_Sotha.md), sometime in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). A young [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) was the only known survivor of this incident, having been rescued by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md).<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ABHOAS-90)</sup>

In response to the destruction of [Gilverdale](Lore_Gilverdale.md) at the hands of Molag Bal, Sotha Sil, now a [living god](Lore_Tribunal.md), sought out eight of the most influential Daedric Princes. Mehrunes Dagon was among them, and between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), a deal was made which would become known as the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md).<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-2920v4-91)</sup> Little is known about the Compact, but it is theorized that it seeks to prevent the Princes from directly manifesting on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md),<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TCC-92)</sup> and only permits them to commune with certain intermediaries, such as witches and sorcerers.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-2920v4-91)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg/200px-MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg) Depiction of Almalexia's battle with Mehrunes Dagon Mehrunes Dagon's general attitude toward the Coldharbour Compact was that of indifference. To him, Sotha Sil was a mere mortal mechanist that was to be crushed like he did his childhood home, and no compact was to keep him from destroying Nirn.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-7)</sup> This sentiment would manifest a few months later in Sun's Dusk of 1E 2920. The [Skeffington Coven](Lore_Skeffington_Coven.md) of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) took in an exiled Dunmeri woman named [Turala](Lore_Turala.md), the former lover of the Duke of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), [Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Brindisi_Dorom.md).<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-93)</sup> However, after a trip to [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), the Skeffington Coven was massacred by an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) raiding party. Turala was falsely led to believe that the massacre was the work of assassins sent by Brindisi Dorom himself. Enraged, the witch called forth the Prince of Destruction to destroy Mournhold and slay her former lover, and he complied.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-94)</sup>

During the carnage, [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and Sotha Sil arrived to stop the Prince. The battlefield was lit by a barrage of flame which fell indiscriminately. Almalexia plunged [Hopesfire](Lore_Hopesfire.md) into Dagon's flesh and carved him from within. Meanwhile, Sotha Sil, enacting vengeance for Ald Sotha, used "god-bronze whips" to lash the Prince into submission. Wounded, Sil allegedly whispered Dagon's Nymic, and the Prince "exploded throughout all time".<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-TTIS8-95)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-2920v11-96)</sup> Defeated, Dagon sought to have a final laugh, and flung a piece of Mournhold into his realm with the inhabitants intact.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-97)</sup> After his banishment, Sotha Sil sealed the planar doors between Nirn and the Deadlands, blocking off all access for Dagon as further punishment for breaking the Coldharbour Compact.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ONLyranth-98)</sup>

The [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) believe that this battle between Mehrunes Dagon and the Tribunal marked the birth of "Sarmissonays'um ghoul-thing[s]", which emerged from Dagon's own flayed skin.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTISV8-9)</sup> Indeed, the [Ruinachs](Lore_Ruinach.md), a four-armed race of Daedra, are thought by some scholars, mages, and even the Ruinachs themselves, to have emerged from Dagon's flesh in the wake of his battle against Almalexia.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-BPTMR-99)</sup>

It is unknown what event prompted Dagon to begin working on a method to breach the Luminal Barriers. Some possibilities include (but are not limited to) when the Dragonfires were established by [Saint Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md),<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-To SA-100)</sup> or after Dagon was punished for breaking the Coldharbour Compact.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ONLyranth-98)</sup> Regardless, Dagon began working on methods to bridge a path between his realm and Nirn to start his invasion, and so he began refining his dimension hopping technology.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-BPOP-101)</sup> The initial stages involved simple circular Oblivion Portals, but this would later develop into the framed doorways known as Oblivion Gates.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-ESOLive2-102)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Dagon.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Dagon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Dagon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Dagon.jpg) Mehrunes Dagon bargaining with the Longhouse Emperors Several smaller, less influential cults devoted to Mehrunes Dagon were also known to be in operation during the Second Era, such as the [Brotherhood of Destruction](Lore_Brotherhood_of_Destruction.md) between 2E 115 and 2E 140, and the [Bloodfire Cult](Lore_Bloodfire_Cult.md) in 2E 243 and 2E 244.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DTt A-41)</sup> In particular, the Brotherhood of Destruction's attack on merchants of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) would be the first known transgression of Dagonist cultists against the Imperial capital.

At some point in the Second Era, Hranvard Frostfinger, a [witch](Lore_Witch.md) of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) sacrificed thirteen people to Dagon in a single night, prompting the Prince to open a [portal](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) to the Deadlands along the coastline of the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). Though Hranvard was ultimately slain by a party of witch-hunters of the [Direfrost Clan](Lore_Direfrost_Keep.md), her ritual was not stopped in time. A rift to Oblivion opened and the air started to boil before a torrent of flame poured out, engulfing thirty of the hunters in flame. From the portal, Dagon's voice boomed that he'd allow the one survivor to leave because he'd suffer more among the living. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) expressed skepticism of the surviving hunter's story despite the mirrored sand left behind at the coastline of the Sea of Ghosts, the missing hunters, and the remains of a ritual circle that matched the one described by the witch-hunter, dismissing the event as a meteorological phenomenon. Nonetheless the event resulted in the Direfrost Clan intensifying their war against the witch covens of Eastmarch.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BV1-8)</sup>

At some point in the Second Era, Mehrunes Dagon commissioned the [Mad Architect](Lore_Mad_Architect.md) to design the razor pits of the Deadlands for him. The Architect complied, creating razor pits said to never grow dull and to be able to cut flesh, bone, and even spirit essence, in ways that even the most powerful [healers](Lore_Restoration.md) can never mend.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-TAWPT-103)</sup>

#### After the Soulburst
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/ON-quest-Blessings_of_the_Eight_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Blessings_of_the_Eight_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Blessings_of_the_Eight_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Blessings_of_the_Eight_02.jpg) Dagon communes with the Veiled Queen through a flame [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/ON-quest-Of_Stone_and_Steam.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Of_Stone_and_Steam.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Of_Stone_and_Steam.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Of_Stone_and_Steam.jpg) Souls being drawn into the Oblivion Gate at Rockgrove Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), displeased with the rise of the tolerant [Queen Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) and her [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), [High Kinlady Estre](Lore_Estre.md) donned the role of the leader of the Altmeri separatists known as the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md). In her desperate attempt to impede the pass of Queen Ayrenn and her troops to [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), she made a pact with Dagon. Estre summoned Daedra from the Deadlands in [Dawnbreak](Lore_Dawnbreak.md)'s abandoned mine. The Daedra set the town alight and murdered its citizens until the First Auridon Marines intervened and organized an evacuation. The marines managed to stop the hordes from spreading across the area, and an [Eye of the Queen](Lore_Hero.md#Soulless One) came and defeated the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) that orchestrated the assault.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-ESOAshes-104)</sup>

Per her agreement with Mehrunes Dagon, High Kinlady Estre unleashed more Daedra upon [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md), a holy site of the Eight Divines in the north. She then moved on to her ultimate target, the city of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md).<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-ESOBOTE-105)</sup> Here, she was let into the city by its ruler, High Kinlord Rilis XIII, who remained oblivious to her true nature. Once inside, she opened four Oblivion Gates across the city, allowing her Daedric army to lay siege to Firsthold and kidnap the kinlord. Estre was slain by a hero and the Oblivion Gates were closed, but most of Firsthold was left in ruins.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-ESOSAT-106)</sup>

Later that year, Dagon's Razor Master, Erthas, attacked the Bosmeri village of [Whisper Grove](Lore_Whisper_Grove.md). The forest was set ablaze, the village was overrun by Dagonites, and a portal to the Deadlands was opened to stir the chaos. The [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) were hired by the village's leader, Gilraen, to slay the Dagonites and push back the Daedra. After Erthas' death, Mehrunes Dagon himself offered to seal the portal if Gilraen agreed to meet with him in the Deadlands. Gilraen agreed, and the portal was shut.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-ESORE-107)</sup> Mehrunes Dagon gave Gilraen the means to survive in the Deadlands as a Razor Master, namely his eponymous Razor.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Gilraen RTA-108)</sup>

Several months later, Gilraen's husband, Fingaenion, reopened the portal in a desperate bid to save his wife. He hired the Undaunted to enter Oblivion and retrieve her, but they failed.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-109)</sup> Another group of Undaunted arrived hours later and successfully intervened. The adventurers went through the portal Fingaenion re-opened to enter the Knives of Discord, a region ruled by one of Mehrunes Dagon's lieutenants, [Valkyn Skoria](Lore_Valkyn_Skoria.md).<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Gilraen RTA-108)</sup> The Undaunted rescued Gilraen and their comrades, slew the Valkyn, and closed the portal.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-ESORA-110)</sup> Gilraen was worried that Mehrunes Dagon would seek her out, given that she facilitated the slaying of one of his lieutenants and fled the realm with his Razor.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Gilraen RTA-108)</sup>

In Elsweyr, an aspect of Darloc Brae, a worshipper of Merrunz, reanimated his ancient forces in the Rimmen Necropolis and dueled with the Dremora forces of a champion of Mehrunnez (Merrunz).<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Ot Tot FM-111)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-ESORNGE-112)</sup> Both sides were eventually defeated by [wandering travelers](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-ESORNGE-112)</sup> In the Darkpool Mine, a cult called the Claws of Daegon were using an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of the Prince called the [Deadland Hammer](Lore_Deadland_Hammer.md) to create a force of [Iron Atronachs](Lore_Iron_Atronach.md). The Vestige infiltrated the mine, slew the leader of the cult, and retrieved the hammer.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-IB-113)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-MD-114)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-DH-115)</sup>

During that same period, the Sul-Xan, a tribe of Argonians faithful to Dagon, raided the ancient city of Rockgrove. The Sul-Xan hoped to use the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of the slain together with the powerful tides of flame and steam in the region in order to summon a powerful Dagonic [Harvester](Lore_Harvester.md) named Xalvakka into Nirn. This plan would be foiled when a group of [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) raided Rockgrove, slew the Sul-Xan performing the ritual, and entered the Deadlands themselves to slay Xalvakka before she could cross over into Tamriel.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-Plea-116)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Steam-117)</sup><sup>[[112]](#cite_note-Dust-118)</sup>

#### Prelude to the Four Ambitions
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/ON-wallpaper-Ottoman_Gates_of_Oblivion-3840x2160_Full.jpg/200px-ON-wallpaper-Ottoman_Gates_of_Oblivion-3840x2160_Full.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Ottoman_Gates_of_Oblivion-3840x2160_Full.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Ottoman_Gates_of_Oblivion-3840x2160_Full.jpg) Artistic depiction of the events of Dagon's Second Era invasion The [Reachman](Lore_Reachfolk.md) chieftain Durcorach began building a following in the Reach and allied with the [Tagh Droiloch](Lore_Tagh_Droiloch.md) coven in [2E 527](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_527), subduing all rival clans with their help through means involving Daedric bargains.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-HCP:TTD-119)</sup> A deal was struck with Mehrunes Dagon to have Durcorach crowned Emperor of Cyrodiil, with his sorcerers performing countless tasks in wait for the day when the "final part of the arrangement" would be carried out and Dagon will "claim his due" with the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-ADi S-120)</sup> With years of preparation, Durcorach summoned a great horde of Reach warriors and descended upon Cyrodiil in [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533), seizing the Ruby Throne for his own.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-Ho M:ASo S-121)</sup> For the Four Ambitions to come to fruition, [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) noted that everything "must be ready when the time is right". Within the Longhouse Emperor's dynasty, "promises were made" and bargains were "set in blood and stone" with Dagon as the Prince's way of not accepting failure.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-DID-122)</sup>

After Emperorship passed to him, [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md) "forged" the Ambitions, which were later entrusted to his son, [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md). Moricar had an annex built beneath Imperial City to house the last of their secrets, a safe haven for others to find so they can carry out the work. Although the bargain was intended to maintain the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md)' grip over Tamriel long after Durcorach's death, Leovic's decision to legalize Daedra worship led to a [rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md), which ended in Duke [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) slaying Leovic in the Imperial Throne Room.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-EFEV-123)</sup> With Varen Aquilarios at the gates of the Imperial City in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), toward the end of [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md), Leovic gathered those who knew part of the plan in the annex to appeal to the Prince of Destruction and seek his help again. Only his steward Farrul Lupus and the leader of the Order of the Waking Flame knew the entire truth, and even they were not made privy to the changes needed to be made. The Four Ambitions were not ready by this time, with their "time" still a few years away, but had they been ready Leovic would have used them then to destroy Varen and save his legacy.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-DID-122)</sup><sup>[[118]](#cite_note-TJo EL-124)</sup> Leovic planned that if he failed, the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame would have to deal with the others and determine where he moved the Ambitions.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-DID-122)</sup><sup>[[118]](#cite_note-TJo EL-124)</sup>

#### Three of the Four Ambitions
In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Farrul Lupus sent [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md) to find a book that put her on the trail of Emperor Leovic's secret, informing her that the Emperor's secret concerned a deal made with Mehrunes Dagon and the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-ESOEveli-126)</sup> Lupus was assassinated by the Order of the Waking Flame posing as the Dark Brotherhood,<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ESORenna-127)</sup> prompting Eveli and the Vestige to travel to the Ne Salas Cache Annex. They recovered a ritual that indicated that the cult planned to use the shrine there to learn the location of the Ambitions, and followed Doombringer Eshil into the Deadlands. Eshil spoke with Dagon, revealing that the High Priest sent him, with Dagon expressing that he invested a high amount of effort into the Ambitions and to his dismay the Ambitions were misplaced and the bargain struck remained unfulfilled, telling Eshil to kill the intruders to get the Ambitions.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-ESODoombringer-128)</sup> Eshil was killed by Eveli and the Vestige, who planned to find the people on Leovic's list, warn them about the Dark Brotherhood, and find out what they knew about the Ambitions.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-ESOEveli-126)</sup><sup>[[123]](#cite_note-ESOTES-129)</sup>

The Vestige worked with Eveli Sharp-Arrow to uncover the mystery, and warn the surviving councilors, many of whom were known to be in the Cyrodilic region of Blackwood. Most of the councilors had reconciled themselves to the fall of the Longhouse Emperors, and managed to hang on to positions of importance—for instance, Councilor [Tarnian Lovidicus](Lore_Tarnian_Lovidicus.md) served as a member of the Chamber of Legates of Leyawiin.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-RL-130)</sup> However, Councilor [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md), the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame, still harbored designs of seizing power for himself—the Daedric power of the pact with Dagon, the Imperial Throne, and the Four Ambitions. Vandacia and his Waking Flame murdered many of his fellow councilors, but framed the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-AUA-131)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-quest-Pyre_of_Ambition.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Pyre_of_Ambition.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Pyre_of_Ambition.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Pyre_of_Ambition.jpg) Mehrunes Dagon, invading Fort Redmane as the realms merge The Vestige and their allies raced to find the Four Ambitions before Vandacia could acquire them. The Ambitions would turn out not to be conventional weapons but were instead people empowered at birth through the mixing of Daedric power with the essence of a mortal [soul](Lore_Souls.md), a combined essence that would grow throughout their lives until it could be used as the spark that would merge Nirn with the Deadlands and summon Dagon's fire from Oblivion through their sacrifice.<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-Weapons-132)</sup><sup>[[127]](#cite_note-ESOCalia-133)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-ESOSombren-134)</sup><sup>[[129]](#cite_note-ESOVandacia-135)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ONCeldina-82)</sup> After the emptying of the Ambitions's Doomvaults, special locations that were actually pocket realms, described as bottled up pieces of Nirn that were created to contain the volatile power of the Ambitions, which would rapidly grow once outside, threatening to ignite cities and consume the Ambitions themselves until they learned to control it, they were quickly moved from the indefensible city of Gideon to the much more sturdy Imperial-built [Fort Redmane](Lore_Fort_Redmane.md).<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-Weapons-132)</sup><sup>[[130]](#cite_note-ESOMirri-136)</sup><sup>[[131]](#cite_note-ESOScattered-137)</sup><sup>[[127]](#cite_note-ESOCalia-133)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-ESOSombren-134)</sup> Vandacia deployed an army to capture the three known Ambitions. The bulk of his army was defeated outside the walls, but Vandacia and a powerful group of cultists were able to get into the fort with portal magic.<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-Po A-138)</sup>

The Ambition, Destron, was sacrificed by Vandacia, who seized his power and attempted to merge Nirn into the Deadlands, a dangerous process which had the potential to destroy both realms if disrupted.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-ESOVandacia-135)</sup> This enabled Mehrunes Dagon to participate in the assault on the fort. However, though he'd attained great power by siphoning a little of the power used during the ritual to create the Ambitions for himself,<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-ESOSombren-134)</sup> Vandacia was slain through the aid of Calia, who used her power as an Ambition to drain his Daedric power,<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-ESOCalia-133)</sup> ending the merge and banishing Dagon. The two known surviving Ambitions, Sombren and Calia, realized that their power was too uncontrollable to remain in Tamriel until they could channel it, and they would have to hide in the Deadlands, a realm that would naturally siphon and stabilize their power for as long as they remained there,<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-ESOCalia-133)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-ESOSombren-134)</sup> meanwhile, the Vestige resolved to search for the missing fourth Ambition.<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-Po A-138)</sup>

#### Last of the Four Ambitions
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/ON-quest-Ambition%27s_End.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Ambition%27s_End.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Ambition%27s_End.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Ambition%27s_End.jpg) The realms merging at Fort Sundercliff Mehrunes Dagon made moves to claim the demi plane of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), as it was a convenient way station between Nirn and the rest of Oblivion, which would ally him to bypass the restrictions [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) had imposed on his realm due to his breaking of the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md), sealing the planar connections between the Deadlands and Nirn.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-ESOLyranth Foolkiller-139)</sup> The Waking Flame, attempted to seize control of Fargrave through the inscription of "Votive Runes", glyphs containing Dagonic prayers. in various locations. The runes would siphon the emotional energy of the mortal inhabitants, placing them in a state of increased emotion before overwhelming their minds and rendering them unresponsive. The drained energy would than sweep across Fargrave, discorporating all Daedra, including the [Grasp of the Stricture](Lore_Grasp_of_the_Stricture.md) at once, leaving the realm defenseless for Dagon's followers to seize. This plan was ultimately foiled by the Vestige<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-ESOLyranth Foolkiller-139)</sup><sup>[[134]](#cite_note-140)</sup>

Unfortunately for the Prince, infighting erupted between Sister Celdina's Waking Flame and Valkynaz Nokvroz of the Ruinblood Clan over who would take credit over the utilization of the Cataclysts; machines that remake Daedra into living disasters known as [Incarnates](Lore_Incarnate.md) through the same power and process which was used to create the Ambitions.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-ESOLyranth Key-141)</sup> Valkynaz Nokvroz seized the Ambition [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md) and used her blood to transform himself into a "Perfected Incarnate", an elemental incarnation of fire, but was defeated by the Vestige.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-142)</sup> In the end the infighting only hampered Dagon's plans, and allowed for the defeat of both factions.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-143)</sup>

After it was discovered that the Four Ambitions were actually imbued with Dagon's nymic, the Prince of Destruction was summoned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by the Ambition Sombren, who stole the power of three of the Ambitions and used it to summon Dagon, influenced by both his study of the [Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md), and the part of all Ambitions which belonged to Mehrunes Dagon, his Egonymic, which made them susceptible to Dagon's dark impulses.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-ESOCalia-133)</sup> A portal was opened between [Fort Sundercliff](Lore_Fort_Sundercliff.md) and Dagon's capital of Destruction's Solace in the Deadlands, resulting in the instant destruction of the fort and the surrounding town. Upon manifesting on Tamriel, Dagon once more began the process of merging his realm with Nirn using the power of the Ambitions. However, Dagon and Sombren were followed by a force of mortals and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) led by the Vestige, who interrupted the ritual.

Another Ambition, Mairead, who had secretly been prepared as a weapon that could banish Mehrunes Dagon should the need arise by emperor Moricar's modification of the Ambition ritual,.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-ESOMd-144)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-PHJ-145)</sup> was able to steal power back from Sombren and used Dagon's [egonymic](Lore_Egonymic.md) to bind and weaken the Prince. With the help of the Vestige, Dagon's physical form was destroyed and he was banished to the furthest corner of the Deadlands via the use of his egonymic. This reversed the attempted merging of the realms, mitigating the destruction that had been caused to Sundercliff and the surrounding landscape.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-146)</sup> After Dagon's banishment, the power of the Ambitions was stolen by Lyranth the Foolkiller, who sought to use their power, which she described as the power to truly change one's nature, even that of a Daedra, for some unknown purpose.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-ESOLyranth Foolkiller-139)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg/200px-BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg) The remains of a village on Caecilly Island At some point before the year [3E 172](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_172) (and possibly before the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md)),<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb_3)</sup> [Chimere Graegyn](Lore_Chimere.md), a [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) retainer and noted Daedra summoner, made a pact with Mehrunes Dagon. The conjurer deceived the Prince, tricking him into swearing an oath he had no intention of keeping. Chimere wore the [Armor of the Savior's Hide](Lore_Savior%27s_Hide.md), and it protected him from the blows of oathbreakers (as Dagon now was), which gave Chimere enough time to banish Dagon to Oblivion by invoking the Prince's [protonymic](Lore_Protonymic.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BSChimere-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TMKR-29)</sup>

As per the initial pact, Graegyn desired to "live forever in his home town among the happy voices of his friends and countrymen". Dagon reinterpreted this request literally, dragging Chimere and the entirety of his home of Caecilly Island into Oblivion with him. Everyone else was instantly slain, and Chimere (still aging) was cursed to live forever, surrounded by the voices of his friends. The plane where the island wound up became known as the [Chimera of Desolation](Lore_Chimera_of_Desolation.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TMKR-29)</sup><sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Battlespire-147)</sup>

#### The Imperial Simulacrum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_%28Deluxe%29.png/200px-AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_%28Deluxe%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_(Deluxe).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_(Deluxe).png) Jagar Tharn Mehrunes Dagon was instrumental in [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s imposture and assumption of the [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) throne on [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389).<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-BSAngada-148)</sup> After Jagar banished Emperor Uriel Septim VII and [Talin Warhaft](Lore_Talin_Warhaft.md)<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-149)</sup> with the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md),<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-TRBB-150)</sup><sup>[[145]](#cite_note-ARSilmane-151)</sup> the sovereign was kept in bondage by Dagon in one of his dark prisons<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-13)</sup><sup>:180</sup> somewhere in Oblivion, where he experienced nothing but inexplicable and haunting nightmares.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-ALOUS-152)</sup> This period where Jagar ruled would become known as the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/BS-place-The_Battlespire.png/200px-BS-place-The_Battlespire.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-place-The_Battlespire.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-place-The_Battlespire.png) The Battlespire After his father's numerous failed attempts at invading the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) in the past,<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-SL-153)</sup> Xivilai Moath took it upon himself to engineer Jagar Tharn's involvement in the Spire's fall.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-13)</sup><sup>:158</sup> Indeed, Mehrunes Dagon and his forces were free to invade this war college thanks to the resulting bargain,<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Codex-154)</sup> and in exchange they were to slay the five mighty battlemage administers who governed it. Jagar hoped this would free him from the troublesome competition for his position as [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md), as these administers were traditionally selected for it.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum-13)</sup><sup>:18</sup> In [3E 398](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_398), Mehrunes Dagon collected his prize.<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-ESTimeline-155)</sup> The Prince's forces, led by [Imago Storm](Lore_Imago_Storm.md) of [Clan Dremora](Lore_Clan_Dremora.md), mustered in the [Havoc Wellhead](Lore_Havoc_Wellhead.md), then rampaged through two other planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) —the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md) of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-LFTBH-26)</sup><sup>[[150]](#cite_note-BSFrost-156)</sup> and the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md) —before invading the Battlespire itself.<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-BSIdeal-157)</sup>

Shade Perilous was home to two Daedra clans, the lordless [Seducers](Lore_Seducer.md) and the [Nocturnals](Lore_Nocturnals.md) who had given them refuge. The Nocturnals were imprisoned in their own demense as the Seducers were bent to Dagon's will. However, some Seducers willingly entered his service, and these converts were remade in the image of Dagon's greed and treachery, making them much more powerful—and giving them wings.<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-BSDeyanira-158)</sup><sup>[[153]](#cite_note-BSDark Seducer-159)</sup> These "Dark Seducers"<sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_nb_4)</sup> joined the ranks of the invasion forces, with some being given [Daedric Crescents](Lore_Daedric_Crescent.md) to wield in battle.<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-BSIntro-160)</sup> One Dark Seducer in particular, Si'ess, became Mehrunes Dagon's bodyguard and lover, and was entrusted by the Prince with one of his artifacts, the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](Lore_Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver.md).<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-LFTD-161)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BSSiess-22)</sup> Once the Battlespire was taken, the [Weir Gate](Lore_Weir_Gate.md), the portal to Tamriel, was sealed with Dagon's personal warding sigil.<sup>[[156]](#cite_note-LTM-162)</sup><sup>[[157]](#cite_note-BSImago-163)</sup>

Unaware of these events, a pair of [apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) battlemages, [Shadow Legion](Lore_Shadow_Legion.md) aspirants, entered the Battlespire after it had fallen. One apprentice went ahead, leaving behind notes and helpful items, but was ultimately captured by the Daedra and brought to [Dagon's hunting lodge](Lore_Dagon%27s_Hunting_Lodge.md) before the Prince himself.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Battlespire-147)</sup><sup>[[158]](#cite_note-BSInformant-164)</sup> The other apprentice went in search of the first (and for a way back to Tamriel) by fighting through, bluffing past, and making deals with the various forces, Daedric and otherwise, along the way.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Battlespire-147)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/BS-npc-Faydra_Shardai.png/200px-BS-npc-Faydra_Shardai.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-npc-Faydra_Shardai.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-npc-Faydra_Shardai.png) Faydra Shardai, adopted daughter of Dagon After following the trail back to the Shade Perilous, the apprentice befriended the Nocturnals and freed their leader, [Jaciel Morgen](Lore_Jaciel_Morgen.md).<sup>[[159]](#cite_note-BSLevel4-165)</sup> Curiously, Clan Dremora also offered its assistance, for while Mehrunes Dagon had allowed his armies to enter Shade Perilous and use it as a waystation for the assault on the Battlespire, he had not sanctioned its overthrow or occupation. Instead of negotiating for passage or showing restraint, Dagon's protégés and adoptive children, Faydra Shardai of the [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md) clan and Xivilai Moath of the [Frost Daedra](Lore_Frost_Daedra.md) clan, decided to crush their long-time enemies, the Nocturnals. The Dremora believed this needed to be undone in order to rectify things.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSDremora-2)</sup>

However, the path from the Shade Perilous led to the [Chimera of Desolation](Lore_Chimera_of_Desolation.md), where the apprentice became the hare in a [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md).<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-BSLevel5-166)</sup> While the Dremora could offer little help there,<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-LFTD-161)</sup> the apprentice was able to survive and locate (the now very old) Chimere Graegyn. Chimere drew on his past experience with Mehrunes Dagon to help the apprentice escape the realm, offering up the [Savior's Hide](Lore_Savior%27s_Hide.md) and Dagon's protonymic in the process, though by this time, the Prince had added an unknown neonymic as well.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BSChimere-28)</sup><sup>[[160]](#cite_note-BSLevel5-166)</sup>

He felt it best if Faydra, Xivilai, and Mehrunes Dagon himself were temporarily removed from the picture so he could restore order and stability to Clan Dagon.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-BSLevel6-167)</sup> Storm then gave the neonymics of all three to the apprentice, as the mortal had little choice but to go along with this plan.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-INAN-30)</sup><sup>[[157]](#cite_note-BSImago-163)</sup>

After dealing with the lesser Daedra, the apprentice portaled into the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) near Dagon's hunting lodge, the Prince's personal pleasure palace.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSDremora-2)</sup> According to the Dremora, banishing Mehrunes Dagon required the Armor of the Savior's Hide, his proto- and neonymics, the "aid of absent friends", a bit of hope, and a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) made from Dagon's own substance, the Sword of the Moon Reiver.<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-LFTD-161)</sup> This last item was soon taken by force from Dagon's Dark Seducer lover, and the apprentices battled up a mountainside and entered the lodge to face Dagon himself.<sup>[[162]](#cite_note-BSLevel7-168)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/87/BS-screenshot-Bs1a.jpg/200px-BS-screenshot-Bs1a.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-screenshot-Bs1a.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-screenshot-Bs1a.jpg) Dagon is defeated by the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) Mehrunes Dagon was waiting in his inner sanctum with the first aspirant, who was chained from the ceiling and paralyzed. But in that moment, the apprentice summoned Jaciel to the battle; her sudden appearance momentarily distracted Dagon, giving the apprentice enough time to strike.<sup>[[162]](#cite_note-BSLevel7-168)</sup> Dagon was sucked back into the [Void](Lore_Void.md), being "compressed into an ever-smaller volume" before vanishing.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-FPCOJCCTUS-169)</sup> Though Dagon was banished, the force unleashed by the event devastated the Battlespire, reducing it partly to ruins.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Battlespire-147)</sup>

With Mehrunes Dagon gone, Jagar Tharn lost his backing and was destined to fail.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Codex-154)</sup> Circa [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), when the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) reunited the eight pieces of the Staff of Chaos and linked it to the Jewel of Fire, the combined energies of Tharn's lifeforce and the staff were released in a powerful surge of energy, culminating in the destruction of the Battlemage. #### Fifth Century
Mehrunes Dagon was not gone long. In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) visited Mehrunes Dagon's shrine off the coast of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and summoned the Prince. For, though the Tribunal had long "subverted [Divine](Lore_Nine_Divines.md) rule", their protection of the [First Stone](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) should have remained intact, as it was a shield provided to the Empire by its enemies against its other enemies.<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-NMI-174)</sup>

#### The Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png/200px-LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png) A Daedric Siege Crawler deployed by Mehrunes Dagon's forces during the Crisis *Suns were riven as your red legions moved from Lyg to the hinterlands of chill, a legion for each Get, and Kuri was thrown down and Djaf was thrown down and Horma-Gile was crushed with coldsalt and forevermore called Hor and so shall it be again under the time of Gates.*
—Mankar Camoran<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MDC4-35)</sup> After Jagar Tharn's defeat, Dagon was no longer content with playing the gray eminence behind the throne and sought to invade [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) itself, where he is free to inspire permanent destruction (unlike in Oblivion, where nothing can truly be destroyed in any permanent sense).<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-TESIVOOGG-175)</sup><sup>:4</sup> Dagon plotted to destroy the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) to get revenge for his defeat at Battlespire,<sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-GIKurt Kuhlmann-176)</sup> whose destruction would enable his allowance into the mortal plane, thereby allowing explanar invasion. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the final year of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Mehrunes Dagon invaded Nirn in what came to be known as the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-TOC-86)</sup> The [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md), cult-worshippers of Mehrunes Dagon, believed [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) was Dagon's plane to begin with.The Mythic Dawn worked to bring about the coming of Dagon by assassinating the [Emperor](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and his three known heirs thus breaking the [Covenant](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) that protected the mortal world. With each [Oblivion Gate](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) that opened, the [barriers](Lore_Dragonfires.md) between Nirn and Oblivion weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [Firmament](Lore_Astronomy.md), would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined.<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-OBMartin-177)</sup><sup>[[82]](#cite_note-OBMankar-87)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/OBR-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires_03.jpg/200px-OBR-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires_03.jpg) Mehrunes Dagon fights Akatosh The Mythic Dawn focused on the eternal dawn on which Dagon would come to cleanse the world of all non-followers. In order to recover the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) and foil Dagon's plans, the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) had to recover the [*Mysterium Xarxes*](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md), a book written by Dagon himself, so that [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) could open a portal to the [Paradise](Lore_Paradise.md) of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), where the amulet was being kept. Dagon himself made a short but dramatic appearance as a gigantic four-armed humanoid armed with an axe and talons at the climax of the Crisis.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Oblivion-38)</sup> At the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md)'s [Temple of the One](Lore_Temple_of_the_One.md), Martin Septim shattered the Amulet of Kings, transforming himself into an avatar of Akatosh. Dagon was handily defeated, after which the avatar turned into a [statue](Lore_Statues.md) at the center of the ruined temple, a reminder of Martin's sacrifice, which led to the common belief that the forces of Oblivion could never again openly threaten the people of Tamriel.<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-OBLight-178)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/OBR-quest-The_Crimson_Cave.jpg/200px-OBR-quest-The_Crimson_Cave.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-The_Crimson_Cave.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-The_Crimson_Cave.jpg) Mehrunes' Harbinger with Mythic Dawn cultists in Mingo Cave During the Crisis, the Hero of Kvatch investigated a rumor that lead them to follow a series of hidden clues left behind by the Mythic Dawn cult. After retrieving all the hidden notes from the shrines of Hircine and Molag Bal, and from the Lucky Old Lady statue in [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md), the hero arrived at the Lonely Suitor Lodge. From there, the Mythic Dawn sleeper agent, Ranaline, directed them to Mingo Cave, where a hidden congregation of the cult awaited. At the cave, the hero either disguised themselves as a member of the Mythic Dawn and carried out a sacrifice that the cult demanded as proof of enmity towards the Septims, or entered the cave and slew the cultists inside. Either way, Mehrunes' Harbinger, a Dremora servant of Dagon, congratulated the hero for pleasing her lord, either through doing his will, or through killing those who were too weak to serve him, something she deemed as not being a true act of opposition, since Dagon was eternal and so would always prevail in the end. The Harbinger granted the hero the ability to summon her and receive armor and weapons imbued with Dagon's power, which she claimed were beyond the ability of mortals to craft, and so could only be given by her directly.<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-OBTrail-179)</sup><sup>[[172]](#cite_note-OBCrimson Cave-180)</sup><sup>[[173]](#cite_note-OBHarbinger-181)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c7/SR-quest-The_Consequences.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Consequences.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Consequences.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Consequences.jpg) The unclosable Oblivion Gate, bypassing Martin's sacrifice (Skyrim) Despite his defeat in the Oblivion Crisis, Mehrunes Dagon was still determined to invade Nirn, and set up a scheme to bypass the liminal barrier. He was aware of the existence of the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md), and sought to use it to his advantage. For this purpose, he reached out through dreams to attract a new following to his side. Dagon had a Dunmer named Vonos revive the Mythic Dawn starting in [4E 200](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_200), 12th of Mid Year.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Vonos-25)</sup> He led them to a buried Mythic Dawn temple within [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where they were tasked with reconstructing a dormant Oblivion Gate. The Gate to Dagon's Deadlands was cleansed with personal blood sacrifices.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-SRSJ-182)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Mehrunes' Razor was in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), having been broken into three pieces. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) collected these for the Mythic Dawn Museum in [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), and was offered the restored Razor by Dagon in exchange for killing the museum's proprietor, Silus Vesuius.<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-SRPieces-183)</sup>

Later the Dragonborn would receive word of the activities of Vonos' cult and attempt to stop them. After stopping the cult's retrieval of the [Great Welkynd Stone](Lore_Great_Welkynd_Stone.md) from the Ayleid ruins of [Rielle](Lore_Rielle.md), the Last Dragonborn journeyed to the cult's temple. There, they slew Vonos with the Great Welkynd stone in their belongings, and it was revealed that this was what was needed to open the Gate, as Vonos had offered himself as a sacrifice. As the Last Dragonborn was blessed by Akatosh himself, the same being that sealed the jaws of Oblivion would by extension be the key to opening them again.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Vonos-25)</sup>

Though it was only one Oblivion gate, this unwitting sacrifice produced a foothold that could not be closed as the Sigil Stone was disposed of to prevent its closure; even if Dagon was defeated here, he could still rebuild and try again.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Vonos-25)</sup> Regardless, the Last Dragonborn entered into the Deadlands and defeated Valkyn [Methats](Lore_Dremora_Methats.md) and Gatanas, who were to serve as heralds for the initial assault on Tamriel. Afterward, the [Vigil of Stendarr](Lore_Vigil_of_Stendarr.md) arrived and put it upon themselves to guard the portal.<sup>[[176]](#cite_note-The Cause-184)</sup>

### Daedric Crescent
The [Daedric Crescent Blade](Lore_Daedric_Crescent.md) (also called a Battle-scythe blade) is a Daedric artifact created by the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon. It has the power to paralyze those it strikes, and puts heavy wear on their armor; it has also been known to create a green ball of energy, but its effects are unknown. There were once many Crescents, which were used by Dagon's forces to take the Battlespire during the Imperial Simulacrum. When the Third Empire later reclaimed the ruined academy, the Crescents were gathered up and destroyed - all but one. Unknown to the Empire, one of the unique blades remained in existence somewhere in Tamriel, although it is said that none had ever seen it.

### Mehrunes Dagon's Axe
[Mehrunes Dagon's Axe](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon%27s_Axe.md) is an artifact associated with Daedric Prince of Destruction Mehrunes Dagon. It is a large and fiery double-bitted axe. It is said that no mortal has earned enough favor to be entrusted with Dagon's own axe, or to know its name.

Mehrunes Dagon was known to wield his axe on numerous occasions, including his attempts to invade Nirn in 1E 2920, 2E 582, and 3E 433, as well as during his assault on the Lunar Lattice in the ancient past. Numerous statues also depict him wielding the weapon, including—but not limited to—those found in the Daedric ruins of Ald Sotha, Ebernanit, and [Ularradallaku](Lore_Ularradallaku.md) on the isle of Vvardenfell, as well as in the mountains of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) in the province of Skyrim. The statue in [Plaza Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom.md) in the southern part of the city of Mournhold, commemorating Almalexia's victory over Mehrunes Dagon, also depicts him wielding an axe during their battle.

### Mehrunes' Razor

### Mysterium Xarxes
The *[Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md)* was [a tome](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes_(book).md) written by Mehrunes Dagon, who scribed it in "the deserts of rust and wounds". It was an artifact of great—and evil—power. The *Xarxes* was said to have a vile corrupting influence; reading from the book required magical protection from its powers, even merely handling it was said to be dangerous. Because of the hazards it poses to its reader, this book was classified as a [Tract Perilous](Lore_Tracts_Perilous.md).

In 2E 582, the Xarxes was stolen from the retreat of Emperor Leovic by Eveli Sharp-Arrow. Calia, one of the Four Ambitions of Mehrunes Dagon, would eventually use the book to learn how to use her power to drain Daedric energies, an ability which would aid in the defeat of high priest of the Waking Flame Egonymic in the process of performing the ritual to create the Ambitions. After Vandacia was defeated and his attempt to merge Nirn and the Deadlands using the power of the Ambition Destron was foiled, the book was given to the Ambition Sombren, who intended to study it to learn more about the Four Ambitions. However, the power of the book took its toll. Influenced by the artifact, Sombren joined Dagon and stole the power of two of the other Ambitions, which he used to start a merging of the Deadlands and Nirn. The crisis was ended when fourth Ambition, Mankar Camoran. After studying the tome, Mankar wrote the *Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes* (also known as the *Mythic Dawn Commentaries*). Inspired by the prophecies and promises within the book, Mankar founded the Mythic Dawn, a Daedric cult devoted to Mehrunes Dagon. The *Xarxes* acted as the cult's holy book, and was stored in the Mythic Dawn's hidden shrine in the caverns beneath [Lake Arrius](Lore_Lake_Arrius.md) in Cyrodiil. Using the power of the book, Mankar created Gaiar Alata, or "Paradise", an alternate realm where the souls of Mythic Dawn cultists went in death, to be resurrected as 'Ascended Immortals', who were revived continuously whenever slain.

In 3E 433, following the Mythic Dawn's assassination of Emperor Uriel Septim VII and all of his legitimate heirs, the Blades infiltrated the cult and stole the *Mysterium Xarxes* from their shrine to Mehrunes Dagon. It was taken to [Cloud Ruler Temple](Lore_Cloud_Ruler_Temple.md), where Martin Septim, the illegitimate son of Uriel and the heir to the throne, translated the *Xarxes* and discovered a way to create a portal to Camoran's Paradise. The ritual required a Great Welkynd Stone, a [Great Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md), a Daedric artifact, and an Aedric artifact. The Hero of Kvatch entered Gaiar Alata and defeated Camoran, retrieving the Amulet of Kings. The *Mysterium Xarxes* was destroyed in the process.

### Spear of Bitter Mercy
The [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md) is an artifact that is an enigma to Tamrielians, but many believe it to be of Daedric origin despite a lack of recorded history. However, multiple Spears of Bitter Mercy may exist. Originally forged by Mehrunes Dagon, it has become the signature weapon of his "ally" Hircine, who has become the Daedric Prince that is most closely associated with the Spear.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Noonan)</sup> The name Spear with Five Points serves not only as an alternative title for the artifact, but also as one of the many epithets the Reachfolk use for Hircine. The weapon was once in Sheogorath's possession, but his association with it and how he came to obtain it is unknown. He has notably stolen artifacts from his fellow Princes in the past for his own amusement.

Mehrunes Dagon imbued the Spear with his power for use in the [Ritual of the Innocent Quarry](Lore_Great_Hunt.md#The_Ritual_of_the_Innocent_Quarry). This enchantment consists of powerful and maleficent energies capable of instantly killing all but the "High Daedra Lords". Thus it is forbidden from being removed from the site of the Great Hunt, and it cannot be wielded by any mortals or immortals, except those sanctified to the Hunt and bound to its rules. Because Dagon twisted his pact with Chimere Graegyn, an unsanctioned wearer of the Armor of the Savior's Hide can bear the Spear; the armor's enchantment offers protection from the energies of the oathbreaker. Since the rules only apply within the Hunt itself, the artifact can be safely wielded if it were outside its intended locale. However, the spear can still seemingly reject those unworthy of wielding it.

According to a Reachman myth, Hircine wields the Spear of Bitter Mercy when he takes on the aspect of Alrabeg the Hunter. It is one of the [Symbols of the Five Aspects](Lore_Symbols_of_the_Five_Aspects.md), the other four being the [Totems of Hircine](Lore_Totems_of_Hircine.md) (composed of three artifacts), and the [Totem of Claw and Fang](Lore_Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang.md). The weapon grants its wielder a magical shield as well as the ability to summon Storm Atronachss. In appearance it is a metallic spear, usually with two or four protruding prongs surrounding the central spearhead. It is sometimes emblazoned with Daedric [Hekem](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) sigils, representing the letter H.

## The Deadlands
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/ON-load-The_Deadlands.png/200px-ON-load-The_Deadlands.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Deadlands.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Deadlands.png) The Deadlands The [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) are a Daedric realm of Oblivion created and ruled over by the Daedric Prince of Destruction, Mehrunes Dagon. The embodiment of its creator's sphere, the dimension is fraught with natural disasters and destructive change. The area known as the Burn is covered by an ocean of lava, scattered with scorched volcanic islands and ruined structures. Many lesser Daedra roam the realm freely, but Dremora dominate the hierarchy. The Sever is a region of the Deadlands with light soil covering jagged rock with sulfur pools throughout, fierce winds, and constant lightning storms. Destructive tornadoes and extremes in temperature characterize the region. The capital of the Deadlands is known as Destruction's Solace.

Although mortals have attempted to map the Deadlands, the realm's many rivers of fire reverse course every few hundred years, rendering these maps useless. The realm's unformed chaotic creatia appears as quivering pools of lava.

The force of Change, one of the Eleven Forces, flows strongly in the Deadlands, as all aspects of Dagon's sphere are rooted in Change. The currents flow so strongly that one who attempts to tap into change within the realm runs the risk of being swept away, and entering a synchroseisiac state, becoming out of sync with reality and being scattered across time and space. Thus the realm is especially suited for those wishing to study that force. Some Daedra that reside in the Deadlands have thus learned how to read the flow of Change, granting them such clarity that they are practically [precognitive](Lore_Sight.md).

### Other Realms

#### The Chimera of Desolation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/BS-Level5_Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry.png/200px-BS-Level5_Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-Level5_Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-Level5_Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry.png) The Chapel of the Innocent Quarry in the Chimera of Desolation The [Chimera of Desolation](Lore_Chimera_of_Desolation.md) is a small realm of Oblivion created by the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon.

The Chimera was once Caecilly Island, a small island off the coast of [Northmoor](Lore_Northmoor.md) (or [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), according to some sources) in High Rock. The entire island was cast into the void during the Third Era as an act of revenge against Chimere Graegyn, a mortal conjurer living there. The abandoned towns of Trybador and Granvellusa still stand on the desolate island.

The plane was once accessible via the Battlespire, but this link was severed following Dagon's invasion of the edifice during the Imperial Simulacrum. Upon Dagon's banishment during the Oblivion Crisis, Caecilly was returned back to Tamriel.

#### Deadlight
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/ON-place-Deadlight.jpg/200px-ON-place-Deadlight.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Deadlight.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Deadlight.jpg) Realm of Deadlight [Deadlight](Lore_Deadlight.md) is a shattered plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), said to have been destroyed in the ancient past by Mehrunes Dagon.

According to legend, the realm was once the domain of a forgotten [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) who earned Dagon's wrath. In response, Dagon made war upon the Prince, destroying their home. Nothing remained of the realm but a shattered fragment, a place illuminated only by a green, ghostly [aurora](Lore_Aurora.md). The realm possess a ruined keep, which stood beneath a starless sky. The plane itself is devoid of life and energy, which is unusual for a realm of Oblivion. The surrounding landscape consists of jagged stone peaks, dotted with lightning rods and flora typical of the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) such as [bloodgrass](Lore_Bloodgrass.md).

The only known entrance to Deadlight is a crumbling [portal dais](Lore_Portal_Dais.md) called the Western Gate, located in a region of the Deadlands known as the Burn.

#### The Havoc Wellhead
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/BS-level6-Point_B.jpg/200px-BS-level6-Point_B.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level6-Point_B.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level6-Point_B.jpg) The gates to a Daedric encampment The [Havoc Wellhead](Lore_Havoc_Wellhead.md) (sometimes styled Havok Wellhead) is a realm of Oblivion controlled by many Daedric clans in the service of Mehrunes Dagon. It acts as one of Dagon's many provincial governmental centers. It was accessible from the Battlespire, but the connection was lost after its destruction during the Imperial Simulacrum.

#### Citadels
[Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) claims that Mehrunes Dagon houses his legions in seven hundred and seventy-seven impregnable citadels.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ONLyranth-98)</sup>
