# Lefthanded Elves

Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-texture-Warrior_Wave_Illustrated_Tome_01.png) A depiction of the Ra Gada facing pointy-eared enemies The Lefthanded Elves (also **Left-Handed Elves**, or simply **Lefthanders**), also known as ** Kanuryai** in their native tongue<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CFEM-1)</sup> and **[Sinistral](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/sinistral#Adjective) Mer** (or **Sinestral Elves**),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CFEM-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLSB-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UAAYQ-3)</sup> were a race of [elves](Lore_Elf.md) from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). They were the enemies of the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) and fought long and bitter conflicts with them for a thousand years,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Pass-4)</sup> with the [Orichalc Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md#Orichalc) being supposedly involved in their wargames.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LHClasp-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup> It is said the [Lefthand Empire](Lore_Lefthand_Empire.md) was four times the size of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) under [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), which itself accounted for the entirety of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LVSMCt R-8)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) Yokuda, homeland of the Lefthanded Elves
### War with the Yokudans
During the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md) fought to a standstill among themselves to decide who would lead the charge against the Lefthanded Elves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Vo F-9)</sup> Their swordmasters, though, were so skilled in the "Best Known Cuts" as to be matched evenly. [Leki](Lore_Leki.md) intervened, introducing the Ephemeral Feint, and a victor emerged, and war was subsequently declared on the Elves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Vo F-9)</sup> [Diagna](Lore_Diagna.md), an avatar of the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding), brought [orichalc](Lore_Orichalcum.md) weapons to the Yokudans, which were instrumental in defeating the Elves.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Vo F:CR-10)</sup> The swords of the [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) sent the Lefthanded Elves into the oceans.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LVSMCt R-8)</sup> before the Yokudans themselves plunged into a wars which eventually ended in the destruction of their continent.

### Systres Invasion
Despite being driven out of Yokuda in the Merethic Era, a number of bedraggled Lefthand remnants fled by sea and survived into the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). In [1E 660](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_660), one of these fleets sailed east and arrived at the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago with the intent to conquer it from the native [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [druids](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-7)</sup> They found a foothold on [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md) and built a fortress on it to serve as their base while they assaulted the other islands and laid siege to [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-So A-11)</sup> The protracted siege of the Systres resulted in the deaths of hundreds of fighters on both sides, but they were unable to conquer the rest of the archipelago and their campaign ultimately ended when [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md) of Y'ffelon erupted in [1E 676](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_676), consuming both druids and Elves in a cataclysm. In the end, the Lefthanded Elves kept hold of the archipelago, and the druids faded into the background of history.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-7)</sup> Most druid records were burned during the invasion, and as a result very little is known about the early days of the archipelago.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SH1-12)</sup>

The remaining Lefthanded Elves continued to live in the Systres, but their victory was relatively short-lived when in [1E 785](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_785) the first wave of [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) warriors arrived in the Systres and annihilated the starving remnants before heading to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). The Ra Gada's hatred of elves was such that they exterminated any and all elven colonies along the coast of Hammerfell.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Pass-4)</sup> The Redguards no longer speak of the Lefthanded Elves, as recalling the elves' abominations serves only to darken their days.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UAAYQ-3)</sup>

### Legacy
The Lefthanded Elves' defeat by the Yokudans and the subsequent sinking of Yokuda imply that the race may be extinct, although it has been speculated that there may be a link between them and the southern [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UAAYQ-3)</sup> They are believed to have sailed near [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) as well, leading some to believe the buoyant steel salvaged by the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) came from their shipwrecks, though it was much more likely [Frog-Metal](Lore_Frog-Metal.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TSBS-13)</sup> Due to suffering under Elven rule in Yokuda, some elven-loan words made their way into the [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md) [language](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Language).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-RFM-14)</sup>

## Society and Culture

- *"There is bone and dirt. Blood and smoke. Flesh and metal. This is the Real. While many stages of death exist, in the final after there is nothing. Knowing this makes our people strong. We tell no stories for comfort, so we fight to stay in the here and now."* — *[Cries from Empty Mouths](Lore_Cries_from_Empty_Mouths.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CFEM-1)</sup>

Much of the what is known about the Lefthanded Elves themselves is scant and derived from Yokudan or Druid histories and sources. They were said to use a type of stone known as gypsum alabaster in many of their structures.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLSB-2)</sup> The boundary between pet and livestock was supposedly a hazy line for the Lefthanded Elves. Of note was the Yath Asp, a reptile whose stout leather was resistant to cuts and gouges. This made it the perfect material to protect their abdomen, which they believed was the "throne of the soul".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LHAsp-15)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LHDisk-16)</sup> Lefthanded Elves were believe to possess superior gem-cutting skills and operated [onyx](Lore_Onyx.md) peak-mines on Hattu Mountain before they were seized by the pre-Ra Gada Yokudans as spoils of war.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LHTotambu-17)</sup>

Most sources deduce that they were a variant of [Elf](Lore_Mer.md), presumably from [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) stock. Others question this narrative, claiming this was a Yokudan transcription error where "Elf" and "enemy" were essentially the same term, and that Lefthanded Elves were simply left-handed enemies.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LHbelt-18)</sup> One piece of evidence that questions the elven theory are ancient [druidic](Lore_Druid.md) texts that describe these peoples as "both long-of-face and long-of-ear", which appears to allude to elves. However some scholars explain this through the design of Yokudan helms, which were often stylized with a metallic depiction of their winged deity [Tava](Lore_Tava.md). It gives their helmets a [hawk](Lore_Hawk.md) -like crest shape where the wings strongly resembled Elven ears. However, the same druidic texts argue against them being Yokudans; it later describes these left-handed warriors using shields in battle, which runs counter to everything known about Yokudan military practice.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-7)</sup>

Singersteel is a rare alloy composed of nine parts [steel](Lore_Steel.md) and one part [orichalcum](Lore_Orichalcum.md). Its origins are unclear, but some scholars speculate the Lefthanded Elves obtained orichalcum during their war with the Yokudans and used it to form an alloy to create their own arms and armor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LHClasp-5)</sup> Funerary urns crafted from the purest orichalcum, fashioned in an exceedingly rare style, were discovered in the Systres Archipelago. The inscriptions on these urns were distinctly different from those found on the Tamrielic continent, and the artwork prominently featured winged figures. It has been speculated that these urns could be remnants of the Sinistral Elven culture.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OBU-19)</sup>

The text *[Cries from Empty Mouths](Lore_Cries_from_Empty_Mouths.md)*, originally written in the Sinestral language, was translated by Varederil, a member of the Psijic Order. The text features an intriguing switch between verse and prose. It is unclear whether this was an artistic flourish or a cultural detail. The poem sheds light on the potential beliefs of the Kanuryai, who hold that nothing awaits past the final afterlife. While many stages of death exist, the final afterlife holds nothing. They believe that knowing this makes their people strong. They tell no comforting stories, encouraging them to stay in the here and now.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CFEM-1)</sup>
