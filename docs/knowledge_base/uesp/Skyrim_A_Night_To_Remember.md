## Quick Walkthrough
1. Meet [Sam Guevenne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sam_Guevenne) and accept the drinking contest.
2. Help clean up the [Temple of Dibella](Skyrim_Temple_of_Dibella.md).
3. Ask about Sam and the staff in [Rorikstead](Skyrim_Rorikstead.md).
4. Talk to [Ysolda](Skyrim_Ysolda.md) in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) about the staff.
5. Head to [Morvunskar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Morvunskar).
6. Search Morvunskar for Sam and the staff.
7. Receive your reward from the Daedric Lord [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine).

## Detailed Walkthrough

### Drinking Competition
Once you reach level 14, you may meet [Sam Guevenne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sam_Guevenne) in any [inn or tavern](Skyrim_Inns.md). He will challenge you to a drinking contest, offering to award you a staff if you win. Sam reaches his limit after his second drink. You can win by taking a third drink, after which a drunken Sam declares you the winner. However, before awarding the staff, Sam wants to take his new drinking buddy to "a place where the wine flows like water". At this point your mental blackout begins and the screen fades to black until the next day.

### The Hangover
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_03.jpg) *"Wake up! That's right, it's time to wake up, you drunken blasphemer!"* You will be woken by [Senna](Skyrim_Senna.md), a priestess, in the temple of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) in [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md). When you ask about what happened, she states you were blathering about a wedding and a goat, or blaspheming the statuaries by fondling. You apparently made quite a mess of the temple, so she asks you to clean up the temple and make amends.

| Option | Dialogue |
| --- | --- |
| Was a man named Sam with me? | Dibella teaches love and compassion, but that doesn't mean we're just going to tell you what you want to know and let you walk away from this. Pick up your mess, then apologize, and if we think you're sincere we'll consider lending you aid. |
| I'm sorry, I don't even remember how I got here. **(Persuade)** | **Passed**: Well, you were deep in your cups when you got here. You were ranting but most of it was slurred. You said something about Rorikstead. <br> **Failed**: Oh, I'd love to help you figure it out, but I'm so busy cleaning up the mess you made of our temple... Now if you were to help tidy up and perhaps apologize afterwards... I might be able to help you. |
| Tell me how I got here and I'll pay for the damages. **(Bribe)** | **Passed**: Well, we can always use contributions. You were ranting when you got here but most of it was slurred. You did say something about Rorikstead . Maybe you should take a look there. <br> **Failed**: Oh you must be nobility of some sort. Running around partying and paying others to clean it up. I don't think so. Clean up your own mess. |

If you apologize, you will learn a little bit more information about what happened. You will have to pick up several seemingly meaningless items, namely, a giant's toe, two bottles of Alto Wine, and a confusing [note](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Repair_Supplies) from Sam. According to the note, you need these items, now marked as quest items, and a Hagraven Feather to fix the staff you were promised. You can skip the cleanup through [persuasion (Average, Speech Skill 50) or bribe](Skyrim_Persuasion.md). Note that the [Shrine of Dibella](Skyrim_Shrines.md#Shrine_of_Dibella) in the same room will grant you a +10 Speechcraft blessing for 8 hours, which can potentially help you for all stages of this quest. If you avoid cleaning up, you need not worry about trying to get the items.

After you clean up the temple, the priestess tells you that when you got there you were saying something about [Rorikstead](Skyrim_Rorikstead.md).

### Grok and a Goat
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/15/SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_04.jpg/200px-SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_04.jpg) Grok won't let go of Gleda ... Head on to Rorikstead and when you arrive, look for [Ennis](Skyrim_Ennis.md). He will indignantly rebuke you for stealing his prized goat, named [Gleda](Skyrim_Gleda_the_Goat.md), and selling her to a giant. To get the information you need about Sam and the staff, you can pay Ennis 1000 gold for the goat, persuade him, intimidate him, or simply retrieve his goat.

| Option | Dialogue |
| --- | --- |
| What happened with Gleda again? I'm a little fuzzy on that. | You really don't remember stealing a goat and selling her to a Giant? Are you thick? Go get her! At least she's bound to follow you back - you smell just like the fermented feed she likes. |
| Did I say anything about where I left a staff? | You mentioned something like that... when you were running off with my goat! Tell you what - you bring back my goat, and maybe I'll give a damn about your staff. |
| Help me retrace my steps and I'll pay you for the goat. **(1000 gold)** | **Passed**: Fine, but I'll still be missing my Gleda. Doubt I'll ever have a prize winning goat again. Most of what you said was gibberish but you left a note. The only bit I could read was "after repaying Ysolda in Whiterun." <br> **Failed**: There's not enough money in the world to replace Gleda. |
| Tell me everything and you won't end up like your goat. **(Intimidate)** | **Passed**: Okay, okay. Most of what you said didn't make sense but you left a note. The only bit that I could read said "after repaying Ysolda in Whiterun." That's all I know. <br> **Failed**: Do what you have to do, I'm not giving up on Gleda. |
| I need to find Sam and the staff, in order to get Gleda back. **(Persuade)** | **Passed**: I suppose that makes sense. You didn't mention a Sam and nothing you said about the staff made sense. You left a note but it was mostly gibberish, the only bit I could make out was "after repaying Ysolda in Whiterun." <br> **Failed**: You didn't seem to need either to sell Gleda to the Giant in the first place. To get Gleda, head south to find the goat wandering around near a giant named [Grok](Skyrim_Grok.md). The giant is non-hostile, but once you get the goat to follow you, Grok will decide to attack. It is possible to steal Gleda without incurring Grok's wrath if you sneak up and "talk" to Gleda and then immediately run before Grok notices you, or yield to Grok if he does attack you. Gleda will follow you no matter how fast you run. Another option is to cast [Calm](Skyrim_Calm.md) on Grok before engaging with Gleda. Grok will not care that you have taken Gleda for the duration of the spell. If you are skilled enough, you can kill Grok.

Lead the goat back to Ennis to learn that you mentioned something about paying [Ysolda](Skyrim_Ysolda.md) in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) for a wedding ring.

### The Blushing Bride
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_05.jpg/200px-SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_05.jpg)... and Moira won't let go of the wedding ring Go to Whiterun and find Ysolda. She says that you bought a wedding ring on credit from her, but since there isn't going to be a wedding, she wants the ring back. Your options are much the same as earlier conversations: you can persuade or intimidate her to get the information about Sam, pay her 2000 gold for the ring, or find the ring.

If you choose to get the ring, find your "fiancée", [Moira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Moira) the [hagraven](Skyrim_Hagraven.md), who awaits you at her hut in [Witchmist Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Witchmist_Grove):

*"Darling! I've been waiting for you to return, to consummate our love!"*

Your only option is to ask for the ring back. Moira gets mad and turns hostile:

*"What? You want it for that hussy Esmerelda, with the dark feathers – don't you? I won't let her have you!"*

Kill her and take the [wedding ring](Skyrim_Quest_Items.md#Wedding_Ring) from her inventory.

Once you have the ring, return to Whiterun and give it to Ysolda to learn where you were going to have the ceremony: [Morvunskar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Morvunskar).

### The "Perfect" Wedding Location
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember.jpg/200px-SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember.jpg) Entering the Misty Grove [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_06.jpg/200px-SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-A_Night_To_Remember_06.jpg) *"You're here! I was beginning to think you might not make it."* Having learned of the wedding's location, head over to Morvunskar. You will find a lot of hostile mages both outside and inside there. Fight your way into the fort. Eventually, after descending one, then ascending another stairway, a ball of blue light will appear. Enter the light and you will be transported to [Misty Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Misty_Grove). Follow the path and you will find a party already in full swing, including your drinking buddy Sam. Sam will eventually reveal that he is actually the [Daedric Prince of Debauchery](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine). He will tell you that he was introducing some merriment and subversion into the world and was encouraging you to go out and "have fun". The items he had you bring to repair the staff, it turns out, are pointless, and will no longer be quest items. Sanguine will reward your efforts to track him down with the [Sanguine Rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine_Rose) and will teleport you back to the pub where you met him.

## Notes
- If you bypass Rorikstead in this quest, thus never seeing Ennis between cleaning up the Temple of Dibella and speaking to Ysolda, Ennis will not push the issue of Gleda if you talk to him later. Gameplay-wise, this is because he has nothing to offer that you don't already know.
- [Patch 1.5](Skyrim_Patch.md#Version_1.5) sets Senna, Ennis, and Ysolda as essential. Although the patch notes the change as essential "until [their] part in A Night To Remember is completed", they are simply set as essential for the entirety of the game.
- Once the scenes with Senna, Ennis, and Ysolda are finished, they will go back to addressing you as they did before the quest began, and no more mention of the latter will be made.
- After the quest is completed, you may have a random encounter with an Argonian called [Deep-In-His-Cups](Skyrim_Deep-In-His-Cups.md) who, according to him, was a part of the debauchery with you and Sam. He states that you and Sam owe him 10,000 gold for a bet because he ran into a bandit camp and almost died. If you say that you don't have 10,000 gold, he will attack you unless you pass an easy [Speech check](Skyrim_Speech.md#Persuasion_Options). If you pass the speech check, he will lower your debt to 750 coins. If you pay the debt (either 10,000 or 750 coins), he will go back to Black Marsh.
- Sam can spawn at any of the [inns](Skyrim_Inns.md) across Skyrim, including those in [towns](Skyrim_Towns.md), not just the ones in [hold capitals](Skyrim_Cities.md). This includes [The Retching Netch](Skyrim_The_Retching_Netch.md) in [Raven Rock](Skyrim_Raven_Rock.md) with the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) add-on. - If Sam was present at the Retching Netch when the drinking contest took place, you'll be sent back there once the quest is finished.
- All of the items in Sam's note are indirectly related to the quest: the giant's toe is a reference to Grok, holy water is a reference to Dibella Shrine's well, and a Hagraven feather is a reference to Moira. - Even if you never picked up Sam's note (for instance, by using persuasion or payment at the temple of Dibella) and thus never learned about the items supposedly necessary to repair the staff, dialogue options with Sanguine will still refer to you bringing along those items.
- Teleporting to the temple will not count as "discovering" Markarth; if you have not been to Markarth before and fast travel away immediately upon exiting the temple, you will not be able to fast travel back. Instead, you may want to head to the city gates, which *will* initiate discovering the city. You can also discover [Understone Keep](Skyrim_Understone_Keep.md) before Markarth.
- Only the [Alto Wine](Skyrim_Alto_Wine.md) variant with the basket on the bottom counts as a quest item; the one that lacks it doesn't. Likewise, [regular wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wine), [Spiced Wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spiced_Wine), and [empty wine bottles](Skyrim_Empty_Wine_Bottle.md) also do not count.
- The blue light is on a platform at the top of a stairway, but if you enter the small storeroom below the platform you may be transported to Misty Grove prematurely from there, curtailing collection of loot.
- The items on Sanguine's dining table are unowned, and therefore, free to take.
- If you have already cleared Morvunskar before starting the quest, enemies will respawn in its exterior cell, but the fort's interiors will stay empty. Loot will also not respawn.
- Anything you pick up while in Misty Grove will transfer with you to the real world once the quest is complete.
- Neither Ysolda's, nor Senna's dialogue will change if you are married to them. Regardless of where she may be currently living, Senna will still be at the temple of Dibella when you wake up. Ysolda will retain her location if moved somewhere other than Whiterun, and you will need to speak to her there.
- Many pieces of cut content related to this quest can still be found in the files: - The "Holy Water" mentioned in Sam's list, actually exists as an [unused item](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Holy_Water). Despite its mention in the note, generic Alto Wine appears in its stead.
- The giant's toe found in the temple was originally meant to be a [Giant's club](Skyrim_Giant_Club.md), the ref of the former still refers to it as the latter.
- The priestess [Orla](Skyrim_Orla.md) was originally also meant to be present when you woke up in the temple. A marker for her is defined, but the code meant to move her to it is commented out in the quest script.
- Priestessess Orla and [Anwen](Skyrim_Anwen.md) both have dialogue related to this quest which can *technically* be heard, but it needs extreme conditions in order to succeed. You would have to brak into the expert-locked inner sanctum of the temple in order to find them and then speak to them before their dialogue gets overridden by the generic trespassing dialogue. Also, while Orla's dialogue is technically linked to the correct stage 30, Anwen's is linked to stage 40, at which point it would've already been cleaned up. Their lines are also conditioned to play before stage 2000 and 5000, respectively, which don't exist. It's possible that Anwen's dialogue was deliberately conditioned to not play, as it erroneously has her mentioning herself in third person, instead of Senna.
- The developer message `DA14Temp Fade In Message` also mentions multiple priestesses being present when you awaken, and they would've all spoken to you.
- There's a list called `DA14NPCs` which contains all NPCs (supposedly) related to this quest. In addition to the other two Dibella priestessess mentioned above, [Rorik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rorik) and [Reldith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reldith) are also listed, but it's unclear what their role could've been, as they have no other leftovers at all.
- Grok the giant was meant to have a line of unique attack dialogue, but as Giants can't talk in the final game, it goes unused: *"You cannot eat Gleda! Gleda is protected by Grok!"*
- An NPC called [Drunk Cultist](Skyrim_Drunk_Cultist.md) was meant to be part of this quest, and she is still placed in Morvunskar, near the base of the portal to Misty Grove, but her ref is never activated. Since she doesn't have any dialogue leftover, it's unknown what purpose she would've served.
- There is dialogue present in three different voice types which was meant for (apparent) bandits that you would've encountered in another random encounter after completion of the quest. The bandits would've been familiar with you, and it seems that you and Sam wronged them in some way, as they would apparently attack after saying the following lines: *"Once you leave the party you can't just come back."*, *"Lets * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * see how tough you are without Sam keeping an eye on you."*, *"No Sam, no protection. Time to die."*
- Version 4.1.2 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch) replaces the 2 bottles of Alto Wine in the Temple of Dibella with Holy Water.
