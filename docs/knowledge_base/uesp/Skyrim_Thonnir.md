[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/SR-npc-Thonnir.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Thonnir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Thonnir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Thonnir.jpg) Thonnir **Thonnir** is a [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) [lumberjack](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lumberjack) living in [Morthal](Skyrim_Morthal.md) with his son, [Virkmund](Skyrim_Virkmund.md). From 8am he can be found working at [Jorgen](Skyrim_Jorgen.md)'s mill, and from 11pm he can be found sleeping in his [house](Skyrim_Thonnir%27s_House.md) located not far from the [Thaumaturgist's Hut](Skyrim_Thaumaturgist%27s_Hut.md). He will sell [lumber](Skyrim_Sawn_Log.md)<sup>[HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md)</sup> to you if [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) is installed.

Among the other recent odd occurrences in this small township is the disappearance of his wife, [Laelette](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Laelette_the_Vampire), although according to him it's simply a case of her abandoning her family to join the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md). When the [truth](Skyrim_Laid_to_Rest.md) is eventually discovered, he forms a posse with other Morthal residents to take revenge, but is the only one who doesn't chicken out in the end. You have a choice to take him with you into [Movarth's Lair](Skyrim_Movarth%27s_Lair.md), or to let him go back to Morthal. However, you don't need to worry too much about him as he is [essential](Skyrim_Essential_NPCs.md) and can't be killed.

Afterwards, Thonnir returns to his simple life, although he has yet to tell his son what really happened to his mother.

Thonnir wears a set of farm [clothes](Skyrim_Clothing.md#000261c0) along with a pair of [boots](Skyrim_Clothing.md#000261bd). He wields a [steel war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_War_Axe) and carries the [key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Thonnir%27s_House) to his house, a [torch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Torch), and a selection of [common loot and gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Common).

## Quest-Related Events

### Laid to Rest
Once you agree to find [Helgi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Helgi%27s_Ghost), Thonnir will start looking for his wife, Laelette. If he's spoken to during this time, he'll say:

*"Have you seen Laelette? Have you seen my wife?"* After finding Helgi and dispatching Laelette, Thonnir will come running up to the scene and make a grim discovery:

*"Laelette! She's dead! Ysmir's beard! She's...she's a vampire!"* At this point, you can talk to a saddened Thonnir, who'll say:

*"She's dead. Laelette is dead."* **What can you tell me about your wife?** *"Laelette? I thought she left to join the Stormcloaks. Ah! My poor Laelette!"* **Did you notice anything strange before she left?** *"She began to spend a lot of time with Alva. Yet just a week before, she despised her. In fact, the night she disappeared, she was supposed to meet Alva. Alva told me later that she never showed up. I never got to tell her good bye."* **I think they may have met after all.** *"You think Alva...but that means... Ye gods! You think Alva is a vampire?"* **It's a possibility we can't ignore.** *"No! You're wrong. You must be wrong. Laelette may have met her fate out in the marsh. I refuse to believe Alva had anything to do with this. There is no way you can prove it to the Jarl."* He then ends conversation by saying:

*"I hope Alva is not what you think."* If spoken to after this point, he'll be curious and ask you:

*"Have you found out anything about Alva?"* At any point, you can also ask him to repeat what he knew about his wife's disappearance:

**Tell me again about your wife?** *"I thought Laelette left to join the war. I hadn't heard from her since she left."* Once you have shown Morthal's jarl the evidence of Alva's vampirism, she will gather a band of able-bodied townsfolk in order to help you clear out [Movarth's Lair](Skyrim_Movarth%27s_Lair.md). Thonnir will be at the forefront of gathering these people.

**Thonnir**: *"I'm going to Movarth's Lair to kill that monster. Are you with me?"*
**[Lami](Skyrim_Lami.md)**: *"Yes!"* **Jorgen**: *"Yeah!"* **[Lurbuk](Skyrim_Lurbuk.md)**: *"Take us!"* **[Benor](Skyrim_Benor.md)**: *"Yeah!"* **Thonnir**: *"Kill the vampire!"* **Benor**: *"Kill him!"* **Jorgen**: *"Let's kill him!"* **Lurbuk**: *"Destroy him!"* **Lami**: *"Kill him!"* **Thonnir**: *"Take us to Movarth's Lair!"* **(to you)** Once the mob arrives at the lair, however, their tune changes remarkably:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/SR-quest-Laid_to_Rest_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Laid_to_Rest_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Laid_to_Rest_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Laid_to_Rest_02.jpg) The mob outside [Movarth's Lair](Skyrim_Movarth%27s_Lair.md) **Lami**: *"Um...this place looks dangerous."* **Jorgen**: *"Yeah. Kind of scary too."* **Benor**: *"And it's full of vampires?"* **Thonnir**: *"Cowards! We must kill the vampires. We have to make them pay!"* **Lurbuk**: *"Of course. But why not let him/her go in first?"* **Thonnir**: *"Morthal is full of cowards! **I have to go into the vampire's lair alone?** *"They may be cowards, but I'm not. I'll go with you."* **I think it would be better if you waited here.** *"I suppose you're right. In the game's Dialogue Views files, an early line of dialogue can be found for Thonnir. It seems that after the mob had gathered, you could've originally spoken to Thonnir to ask about his intentions:

**You want to help me kill the vampire?** *"I want vengeance for my wife! Vengeance for Laelette! We're marching on Ghorunn * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * Hall whether you come with us or not."* The line also contains the earlier name of Movarth's Lair, showing it was removed before being rewritten.

## Notes
- Once you have accepted him to accompany you, he will not part your company until the quest is complete, even if you choose the conversation option "I think it would be better you waited here." This could make him useful if you want or need an essential follower for other quests.
- If you attack him (or accidentally strike him in combat) he will not accept a yield, and will continue to target you. As he is an essential NPC, this can make combat situations very precarious. If you do strike him, whether by accident or on purpose, the only way to prevent him from fighting you is to load a previous save, or by entering *stopcombat* command to Thonnir in console.
