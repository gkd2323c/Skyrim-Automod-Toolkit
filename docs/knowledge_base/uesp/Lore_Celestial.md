# Celestial

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png/200px-ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png) A Nedic tapestry depicting the Guardian Celestials and their foe, the Serpent [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-npc-Celestials.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Celestials.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Celestials.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Celestials.jpg) The three Guardian Celestials: the Warrior, the Thief, and the Mage The Celestials are powerful ethereal beings formed by the power of the [stars](Lore_Stars.md). The most powerful Celestials represent the [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md). It is unknown if these beings are from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) or if they are mortals who have achieved a state of ascension. <sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MV-1)</sup> When asked if she really was [the Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) constellation, the Thief Celestial answered she was, in a way, just as one might look at a picture of the constellation in the stars and identify it from the image, she was the Thief, or "one way of seeing" it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Thief Gazers-2)</sup>

The major Celestials are said to be part of the natural order of the world, and thus even those that cause chaos like [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) can only be contained, as their death would cause even greater peril.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOMage-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Thief-5)</sup> Major Celestials are said to possess the power to remake the world, and have been theorized to be capable of [altering time](Lore_Time_Magic.md) in a manner likened to ["hiding behind a broken Dragon"](Lore_Dragon_Break.md), traveling the "line" of time and than cutting it to wrap themselves in a "context where none can ever follow".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Thief Viper-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Learned-7)</sup> Because of this immense power, they can only safely manifest on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) through the use of Apex Stones, it is said that should an Apex Stone be destroyed the corresponding Celestial's full power would be unleashed upon the mortal plane, a force [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) could not withstand.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Valla-8)</sup>

## History

### Merethic Era
It was theorized that the first Celestial beings were created in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) by the ancient [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) [Cult of the Stars](Lore_Cult_of_the_Stars.md). The kings of [Skyreach](Lore_Skyreach.md) in Upper [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) constructed a great laboratory at Skyreach Pinnacle, where they used [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) to harness the power of the stars. At first, they created only weak Celestials that mimicked mundane animals and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). These ranged in power from the lowly Celestial Spiders to the fearsome entity known as [Aetherion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherion). All of these volatile creatures were bound within the Pinnacle by Nedic wards, and the tower was later sealed by the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) invaders that conquered the region circa [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SW-9)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MV-1)</sup> The origin of the more powerful godlike Celestials that make up the constellations is completely unknown. More established myth claims that the stars were formed by the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) fleeing Creation and creating holes to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b6/ON-place-Spellscar_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Spellscar_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spellscar_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spellscar_02.jpg) The Mage's Staff [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-place-The_Mage%27s_Staff.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Mage%27s_Staff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mage%27s_Staff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mage%27s_Staff.jpg) The realm within the Mage's Staff The Constellations fell from the sky circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mage Quest-11)</sup> and took on mortal form. As is his nature, [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) used this opportunity to attack the other Celestials by corrupting the Guardian Constellations through their Nedic Apex Stones. The Serpent believed that civilization stifled natural progress and growth, he claimed that the blood of Nirnian creatures, such as [Trolls](Lore_Trolls.md), had "thinned", and viewed their cities as nothing but a "foul crust" upon it. The Serpent sought to escape and return the world to its primordial form which was nearly uninhabitable, which he claimed he'd achieve by waking the fire under the land, making the seas boil, and making the jungles reclaim the land from civilization under his guidance.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOSerpent-12)</sup> Quickly establishing a cult known as the [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md), the Serpent gained control of [the Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md) beneath the city of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md) in the Ruins of Kardala. Under his influence, the Mage took control of an army of [atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) that had been summoned to the region recently to repel [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), an army which the mages had [summoned](Lore_Conjuration.md) by invoking the Mage's own power in the first place, and created the powerful new [Air Monarch](Lore_Air_Atronach.md) and [Foundation Stone Atronachs](Lore_Foundation_Stone_Atronach.md), before being sundered into many aspects scattered throughout Craglorn by the act of the Serpent, who knew her power was too great for him to swallow her whole and so sought to corrupt her divided aspects one by one instead.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTd EA-13)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Valla-8)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOMage-4)</sup> The Warrior was forced to summon Emperor [Tarish-Zi](Lore_Tarish-Zi.md) from the past so that he could raise an undead army of [Anka-Ra](Lore_Anka-Ra.md) warriors from the many Yokudan tombs in the region, but not before he summoned his own champion, a contemporary Imperial warrior named Titus Valerius.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOWarrior-14)</sup> A great celestial obelisk known as [the Mage's Staff](Lore_The_Mage%27s_Staff.md) also crashed to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) at that time. The Staff's impact created a desolate region known as the Spellscar and the great [magical energy](Lore_Magic.md) emanated by it attracted local Atronachs and drove many insane, creating the Spellfiends. The interior of the Obelisk contained a different [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) where its magical core was housed. The Staff's core threatened to explode and turn Craglorn into a crater, but was stabilized through the intervention of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), who made it past the aspect of the Mage guarding the core and stabilized it.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SBMass-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Spellscar-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Zozu-17)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/ON-prerelease-Aetherian_Archive.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Aetherian_Archive.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Aetherian_Archive.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Aetherian_Archive.jpg) The warped Aetherian Archive [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/ON-item-Shehai_Sword.jpg/200px-ON-item-Shehai_Sword.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shehai_Sword.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shehai_Sword.jpg) The Warrior wielding his [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md) within Hel Ra Citadel As the only guardian remaining, [the Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) hid her Apex Stone in the ruins of [Reinhold's Retreat](Lore_Reinhold%27s_Retreat.md) beneath the city of [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md). She then assumed the guise of a blind [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) woman named Hara, and took control of the [Star-Gazers](Lore_Star-Gazers.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TTd MA-18)</sup> Recruiting scholars and warriors from across Tamriel, Hara sent the Star-Gazers to search for the missing Guardians and prevent more Anka-Ra from being summoned. With the help of Kelmen Locke, the only surviving summoner of the atronach army, the Star-Gazers managed to locate the uncorrupted aspects of the Mage so that they could overpower the Serpent. The restored Mage subsequently fled to the [Aetherian Archive](Lore_Aetherian_Archive.md) in an attempt to escape, there her presence shattered the magical source that granted the Firstmages their long lives and warped the upper levels of the tower. Many of the Firstmages took to worshiping the Mage. One of the Firstmages theorized the Mage's ultimate plan was to escape by traveling to an unknown time and "cutting the line" behind her, thus making it impossible for anyone to follow, an act described as "hiding behind a broken Dragon".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Learned-7)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOMage-4)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Anirwen-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Earil-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Arnure-21)</sup> Meanwhile, Titus Valerius helped the Star-Gazers return Tarish-Zi to his grave.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWC-22)</sup> However, the Serpent soon learned of the Thief's location and attempted to corrupt her, but was prevented by Locke, Valerius and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TMG-23)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg/200px-ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg) A desolate Tamriel, a place and time where the Serpent briefly transported his enemies [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-quest-The_Oldest_Ghost_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Oldest_Ghost_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Oldest_Ghost_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Oldest_Ghost_02.jpg) The Serpent before its Apex Stone After being forced to reveal her true identity, rumors of the Thief's appearance in Belkarth drew the attention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md). Groups of Undaunted launched an assault on the Aetherian Archive and [Hel Ra Citadel](Lore_Hel_Ra_Citadel.md) to free the Mage and the Warrior from the Serpent's influence. Regardless of this, the Serpent continued his attempt to raise armies, planning to return [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) to a primal state. However, the Scaled Court was betrayed from within by [Cassipia](Lore_Cassipia.md), the Regent of Serpentine Stratagems. Along with her companion Little Leaf, Cassipia secretly worked alongside the Vestige in stopping the Court's creation of [mantikora](Lore_Mantikora.md) and dismantling the [Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md)' attempt at raising an army of primal, nirncrux-infused [trolls](Lore_Troll.md). Cassipia's true motives were revealed when she subsequently declared herself [the Exalted Viper](Lore_The_Exalted_Viper.md) and sought to usurp the Serpent's place in the sky. She briefly succeeded in becoming a Celestial, but was killed in battle against the Thief and the Vestige.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DOt EV-24)</sup> The Undaunted later entered the Scaled Court's stronghold of Sanctum Ophidia and defeated the Serpent, returning him to his Apex Stone, with a renewed seal which the Mage claimed he'd be unable to break even with the blood of a thousand mortals.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOMage-4)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOOTheldest Ghost-25)</sup>

The wards at Skyreach Pinnacle were also broken by the Scaled Court that year, but were quickly resealed before the Celestials could escape.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-UC-26)</sup> Other minor Celestials were known to have appeared on Tamriel around this time. Celestial Rams became notorious for wandering the rocky terrain of Craglorn, as they sometimes entered gardens and ate everything that had been planted.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Nebulous Star-Born Steeds were Celestial [horses](Lore_Horse.md) that originated in [Spellscar](Lore_Spellscar.md),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> a magically polluted area created when the Mage's Staff fell to Tamriel.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SBMass-15)</sup> Starchaser Senches also appeared at this time; these creatures resembled [senche-cats](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) composed of floating onyx shards similar to stone found at Spellscar.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> Many Tamrielic folktales spoke of Celestial Lynxes, describing the creatures as either stalking their prey across the night sky, or serving as agile companions for worthy hunters prowling the darkest nights.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESOLynx-30)</sup>

The fallen Constellations later reassumed their place in the sky.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/ON-skin-Thief_Starskin_%28female%29.jpg/200px-ON-skin-Thief_Starskin_%28female%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skin-Thief_Starskin_(female).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skin-Thief_Starskin_(female).jpg) A champion who has taken on the blessing of the Thief - Some beings seem to be especially aligned with the constellations, to the point where their skin starts resembling the stars in the sky and take an astral gleam.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Starskin Astral-31)</sup> These beings are imbued with the stars' power and confidence,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Starborn Bear-32)</sup> and are typically called Star-Born.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Starborn Senche-33)</sup> - It is said that when the [Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior.md) constellation shines brightly, those born under its [sign](Lore_Birthsigns.md) are assured of victory in battle. Individuals who seek to win through force of arms are thus said to take on the mantle of the Warrior himself in times of great crisis, with their form being transformed to resemble that constellation.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Starskin Warrior-34)</sup>
- It is said that when the [Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) constellation shines brightest, those who favor stealth have the protection of shadow. Some invoke this protection every day, with their form being transformed to resemble that constellation.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Starskin Thief-35)</sup>
- People born under the [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) are said to herald life's harshest distresses and its sweetest celebrations, with some embracing the Scales of the Serpent and being transformed to resemble it.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Starskin Serpent-36)</sup>
- The Celestial [Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md) referred to the [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) as "Malazar", and he referred to her as "Valla", a name also used by one of her aspects, suggesting the greater Celestials might have names beyond their titles.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONThe Mage-37)</sup>
