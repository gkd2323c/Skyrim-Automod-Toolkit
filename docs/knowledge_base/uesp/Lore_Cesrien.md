Cesrien was the third Count of [Vitharn](Lore_Vitharn.md) in the [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) region of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Cesrien's birth was the result of an incestuous relationship between [Csaran](Lore_Csaran.md), the second Count of Vitharn, and Nweala, who was Csaran's full sister. By Csaran's marriage to Countess Sheen-in-Glade, Cesrien had an older half-brother who disappeared from the Shivering Isles at the age of twenty.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOV-1)</sup>

Cesrien was born with anisomelia and breathed with a laborious rasp. He was violent-natured, quick-tempered, and slow-witted. As a youth, tutors found it difficult to educate him. Due to his poor health, Cesrien was surrounded by midwives and nurses who attended to his every ailment with balms and vapors from across the Shivering Isles. However, upon reaching adulthood, he sent them away, often becoming violent in their dismissal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOV-1)</sup>

Cesrien eventually succeeded his father as count. He ignored most of his duties as Count of Vitharn. True to his violent nature, Cesrien sought enemies where none existed. The early days of his reign saw the extermination of every tribe of [man](Lore_Man.md), [mer](Lore_Mer.md), and [beast](Lore_Beastfolk.md) within sight of Vitharn until none remained. During his brief reign, much of the southeastern coastline of Dementia was unsafe to travel, and it was littered with the bodies of trespassers in the lands of Vitharn, which were staked to trees as territorial markers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOV-1)</sup>

As his reign progressed, Cesrien became increasingly introverted, allowing only a select few courtiers in his presence. He was seen in public only when organizing his vassal Fanatics for raids on the countryside. Atypically heeding the counsel of his advisors, Cesrien paused his plundering to take a wife to ensure the continuation of Vitharn's comital line. The increasingly ill count chose a vibrant peasant woman named Jideen, who hailed from a [Heretic](Lore_Heretics.md) commune in the wilds of [Mania](Lore_Mania.md), as his bride. With Jideen, he sired a son, [Cirion](Lore_Cirion.md), whom he would beat.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOV-1)</sup>

Tensions escalated between Cesrien and the vassal Fanatics, who had long been loyal to their ancestral agreement with his grandfather, Count [Vitraen](Lore_Vitraen.md), because of the Count's marriage to the Heretic Jideen. Tensions escalated further after Cesrien died as a result of his lifelong health issues, and Cirion ascended the throne of Vitharn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOV-1)</sup> Cesrien was entombed in Vitharn's Mausoleum.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VM-2)</sup>
