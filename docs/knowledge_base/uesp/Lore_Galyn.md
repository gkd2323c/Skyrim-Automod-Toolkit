[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/LG-quest-Journey%27s_End_02.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Journey%27s_End_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Journey%27s_End_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Journey%27s_End_02.jpg) Galyn ministering to the Apostles Galyn the Shelterer was a Dunmer [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) within the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). At some point in time Galyn became the leader of the Clockwork Apostles of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md). Galyn and the Apostles were aware of the death of their god at the hands of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Despite Sotha Sil's death, Galyn and the Apostles continued to inhabit the city, which continued to function and in fact thrive thanks to Sotha Sil's [Mechanical Heart](Lore_Mechanical_Heart.md) contingency.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRMTCC-1)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Galyn and his Apostles would face adversity when a former Apostle and apprentice of Sotha Sil [Mecinar](Lore_Mecinar.md) returned to the Clockwork City. Mecinar was previously banished from the city by Sotha Sil for profane experiments Mecinar had committed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LSSDTB-2)</sup> Mecinar used [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md) to access the realm.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LRTCCTDK-3)</sup> Pursuing Mecinar into the realm was the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) who intended to stop Mecinar's nefarious plans to overtake the Clockwork City.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RCWC-4)</sup>

Galyn and the Apostles realizing the common enemy they shared with the Forgotten Hero would help the hero in their quest to take down Mecinar. The Forgotten Hero would ultimately defeat Mecinar although it's unclear what the fate of the Clockwork City was following these events.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RCWC-4)</sup>
