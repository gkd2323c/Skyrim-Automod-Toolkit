*There once was a hardy young lad of the sea... A [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) of courage and honor was he... Sail on, my Cyrus, sail on...*
— [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) sea shanty<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup> Cyrus the Redguard, also known as **Cyrus the Restless**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Restless-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vivecmeeting-3)</sup> and **Sura**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-4)</sup> **Sura of the Bend'r-Mahk *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]***,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vivecmeeting-3)</sup> or **Sura-do-Hega**<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Tiber Meeting-5)</sup> in [Yoku](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yoku), (b. [2E 831](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_831))<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Cyrus-6)</sup> was a [hero](Lore_Hero.md) best known for leading the [Stros M'Kai Uprising](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stros_M%27Kai_Uprising) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Having spent the early years of his career under the wing of the [Cyro-Nordic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyro-Nord) pirate captain [Tobias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tobias), Cyrus traveled to the island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in search of his missing sister, [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), where he ended up leading his fellow [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) in a rebellion against the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) alongside her.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/9/93/RG-comic-Cover-Young_Cyrus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Cover-Young_Cyrus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Cover-Young_Cyrus.jpg) Cyrus as a child Cyrus was born in the city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) in [2E 831](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_831)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Cyrus-6)</sup> (or [2E 834](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_834)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-8)</sup> or [2E 839](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_839)<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Xan Timeline-9)</sup> according to some sources). His father was a loyal [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md) and a famous comedic playwright, and he had an older sister named [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara). As a boy, Cyrus worked on the Sentinel docks and longed to become a pirate. It was here that he met the half- [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) pirate captain [Tobias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tobias). At the onset of the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) in [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852), amidst the threat of civil war between Crowns and [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), Iszara married a Forebear actor named Hakan.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Origin-10)</sup>

### Hakan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/RG-comic-Page_21-Redguard_Common_Era_Dinner.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_21-Redguard_Common_Era_Dinner.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_21-Redguard_Common_Era_Dinner.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_21-Redguard_Common_Era_Dinner.jpg) Cyrus has dinner with his family [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/RG-comic-Page_27-Cyrus_Kills_Hakan.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_27-Cyrus_Kills_Hakan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_27-Cyrus_Kills_Hakan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_27-Cyrus_Kills_Hakan.jpg) Cyrus kills Hakan, an act that changes his life forever Hakan's political views influenced Cyrus as they trained in the art of sword fighting. Eventually, Cyrus confronted his father over their family's loyalty to High King [Thassad II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thassad_II). Seeking to put an end to the conflict, Cyrus' father wrote a noble play about the shared history of the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) and the [Na-Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Na-Totambu), the predecessors of Hammerfell's two political factions. The play was a huge success and managed to abate the civil war for several years. Hakan, however, was furious at his father-in-law for causing his Forebear militia to disband. During a night of celebration, Hakan drunkenly confronted him, and struck Iszara when she tried to intervene. Cyrus came to his sister's defense and dueled Hakan to the death. Unable to face his family, he fled Sentinel to join Tobias' crew.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Origin-10)</sup>

### Mercenary Work
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/RG-comic-Page_11-Tobias_and_Cyrus_02.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_11-Tobias_and_Cyrus_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_11-Tobias_and_Cyrus_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_11-Tobias_and_Cyrus_02.jpg) Cyrus and Tobias as mercenaries [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/RG-comic-Page_11-Argonia.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_11-Argonia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_11-Argonia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_11-Argonia.jpg) Mercenary work in Argonia For the next few years, Cyrus worked as a mercenary and assassin in the employ of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) crime boss [S'rathra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:S%27rathra), traveling all across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), including [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Argonia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Argonia), and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It was during that time that S'rathra tasked the crew with liberating his kinsmen from a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) slaver camp near [Tear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tear). After the Raid on Tear, Cyrus parted ways with Tobias, doing jobs for S'rathra alone. This work continued until [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), with the last job being a contract to help the half- [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) mercenary lord [Duadeen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Duadeen). As the men were preparing to leave, he received a letter from Tobias stating that his sister had apparently gone missing on the island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) following the [Battle of Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Hunding_Bay). S'rathra provided coin from Duadeen's retainer to pay for passage, but Duadeen stopped him, wanting an explanation of his sudden departure. Cyrus killed one of Duadeen's men to prove his point. He then left High Rock and the mercenary life behind, never to return.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Origin-10)</sup>

### Stros M'Kai
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/RG-scene-Introduction_38.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_38.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_38.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_38.jpg) Cyrus receives a letter from Tobias saying that Iszara is missing [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/RG-scene-Ending.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Ending.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Ending.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Ending.jpg) Cyrus defeats Lord Richton On his way to Stros M'Kai, Cyrus fought off a shipping raid by a group of pirates known as the [Restless League](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restless_League). Upon arriving on the island, he hoped to find out about Iszara's whereabouts through an audience with the provincial governor, [Amiel Richton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Richton).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-11)</sup> He took a job to deliver an amulet from the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) necromancer [N'Gasta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:N%27Gasta) to the governor. Meeting with Richton, Cyrus learned that Iszara was affiliated with the Restless League. He threatened to cause trouble if Richton did not explain everything, prompting the governor to have him imprisoned in the palace's catacombs for questioning.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

Cyrus found out how to signal a League ship from a dying prisoner in his cell, then escaped the catacombs.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-13)</sup> He made contact with the rebels at [Saintsport](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saintsport) and met their leader, Basil, at the League's hideout. It became clear that Iszara had been working with them up until her disappearance.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Following a series of clues, Cyrus discovered that Iszara had gone to N'Gasta hoping to free [Prince A'tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A%27tor)'s from a soul gem. However, N'Gasta tricked her into selling her soul to [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), and he kept the soul gem to gain favor with Lord Richton, passing it off as the "amulet" Cyrus had delivered.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Using the [Flask of Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flask_of_Lillandril), Cyrus defeated N'Gasta and rescued his sister's soul from the [Fields of Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fields_of_Regret), Vile's realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOBarbas-17)</sup>

During his time on the island, Cyrus also found [Archmage Voa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voa)'s ring in the [goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) caverns, defeating many goblins and a large [ogre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre) along the way.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-18)</sup> He also defeated a [Dwarven mechanical giant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Centurion) and retrieved an ancient gear from the [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruins of [Bthzark](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthzark) to repair the island's observatory.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Using the observatory's orrery, he helped the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) witchmother Saban complete a burial ritual to save her dead son from N'Gasta's soul snare.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

Iszara, Cyrus, and the Restless League formed a plan to recover A'tor's soul gem, now being guarded in the palace catacombs. There, Cyrus killed the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Nafaalilargus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nafaalilargus), who Richton had ordered to guard the gem.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-21)</sup> With the soul gem, Voa's ring, and Saban's magic, the League forces covertly entered the Temple of Arkay in Stros M'Kai, where A'tor's body was held in stasis. Saban attempted to resurrect A'tor, but instead his soul fused with his sword, creating a powerful [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) known as the [Soul Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Sword). Cyrus rallied the disheartened rebels by taking up the sword and leading an attack on the palace. He killed Richton and the assassin [Dram](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dram) as they tried to escape aboard an airship. With the death of the island's provisional governor, the rebels were able to negotiate a [peace treaty](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md) more favorable to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), although it remained a part of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

### Later Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/GEN-misc-Cyrus_Cv C.jpg/200px-GEN-misc-Cyrus_Cv C.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-Cyrus_Cv C.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-Cyrus_Cv C.jpg) Older Cyrus the Restless [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/SR-interior-Dead_Man%27s_Dread.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Dead_Man%27s_Dread.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Dead_Man%27s_Dread.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Dead_Man%27s_Dread.jpg) Cyrus' Sabre onboard *Dead Man's Dread* Cyrus' later life can only be guessed at from an assortment of legends. He shared a competitive ambition with Tobias of being the first to find the [Eye of Argonia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eye_of_Argonia) in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-23)</sup> a feat he may have accomplished with his own crew.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Tiber Meeting-5)</sup> Some say he sailed west, to lands uncharted.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Restless-2)</sup> A tale of him facing [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) in [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) supports this idea.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vivecmeeting-3)</sup> Further adventures of dubious authenticity involve [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) on [Masser](Lore_Masser.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Tiber Meeting-5)</sup>

Claims of unknown origin suggest that he took command of the legendary ship *Dead Man's Dread* and sailed north to the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), drawn there by the stories of Tobias, where he amassed a great fortune and lived out the rest of his days on calm waters.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Restless-2)</sup> By [3E 39](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_39), the *Dead Man's Dread* was captained by an unknown individual who had come into possession of several relics once belonging to Cyrus, including his sabre and clothing. On the 2nd of Frostfall 3E 39, the crew of the *Dead Man's Dread* sailed into a subterranean pirates den on [Blackbone Isle](Lore_Blackbone_Isle.md) to winter there after their captain died under mysterious circumstances. The ship and crew became trapped underground after the entrance to the grotto collapsed that night. It transpired that both the captain's death and the tunnel's collapse were the work of a Forebear named K'avir, who had orchestrated the death of the entire crew due to their allegiance to the Crowns. Realizing their dire circumstances, the crewmembers decided to commit suicide rather than face starvation. They each prayed to [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) to bind their souls to the ship, in order to honor their captain's dying wish and protect the relics of Cyrus in undeath.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) sailed to Blackbone Isle chasing rumors of Cyrus' lost treasure and cleansed the island of [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead), claiming both the relics and the ship in the process.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-25)</sup> The Dragonborn later received a note containing a word of advice from a pirate who only identified themselves as "a restless soul".<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PN-26)</sup>

## Notes
- [*Varieties of Faith in the Empire*](Lore_Varieties_of_Faith....md) states that the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding) materialized during the Tiber War, when it was said to have incarnated as "either a sword or a crown, or both".<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-27)</sup> The sword is presumably a reference to the Soul Sword, while the crown likely refers to Cyrus himself, who comes from a family of Crowns. It can therefore be surmised that Cyrus became the Make Way God when he wielded the Soul Sword during the rebellion on Stros M'Kai. This would match the description of the Hoon Ding, said to be a Yokudan spirit of "perseverance over infidels", as the actions of Cyrus and the Soul Sword led directly to the signing of the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md), allowing the Redguards to continue to rule themselves as a province of the Empire. - Furthermore, developer commentary has stated that Cyrus was more than just a Redguard, which is one of the reasons why he was able to slay the legendary dragon Nafaalilargus.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-EMODIT-28)</sup>
- Cyrus' reputation continued to live on in the form of an old sea shanty.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup>
- Cyrus' height was known to be approximately 1.85 meters and his weight was 80 kilograms.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-29)</sup>
- Cyrus' clothes are similar in appearance to the "Swift-Winds Sailor" style of clothing. Similar outfits have been worn by Redguard sailors since the [first Warrior Wave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada#Frandar_Hunding); certain elements can be traced back to these early Ra Gada crews, providing the freedom of movement needed by sailors to go about their duties on deck.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-30)</sup>
