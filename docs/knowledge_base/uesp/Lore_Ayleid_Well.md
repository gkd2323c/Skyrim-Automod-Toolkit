# Meteoric Iron

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/OB-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg/200px-OB-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg) An Ayleid Well Meteoric Iron is a variety of [Aetherial Fragment](Lore_Aetherial_Fragments.md). It is the most common fragment to fall to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). All known samples of the metal are a rusty brown in color. It is prized by armorers and enchanters for its properties in the forging of enchanted weapons and armors.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mft S-1)</sup> The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), known for harnessing Aetherial Fragments, incorporated the metal into their architecture. As such, it is a common sight in Ayleid ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Meteoric Iron is resistant to most elements, and it's believed to have been used by the Akaviri [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) to forge bulwarks against the Dragons' elemental breath.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

## Ayleid Wells
Meteoric Iron is the primary component of Ayleid Wells, enchanted artifacts found dotted throughout [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The wells are often located in the wilderness, far away from known Ayleid settlements, although they can sometimes be found outside Ayleid ruins. It is presumed that they harvest magical power from starlight. It is also suggested, without evidence or support, that they are located at the meeting points of ancient [lines of magical power](Lore_Ley_Lines.md); however, modern arcane arts have discovered no perceptible evidence of such lines of power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mft S-1)</sup> Ayleid Wells are regarded by scholars as the apex achievement of Ayleid magical art, and are believed to use Aetherial crystal to aggregate magicka from starlight even in subterranean locations.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Similar relics known as Aetherial Wells are also found on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), where they were constructed by the [Ancients](Lore_Ancients.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Charged wells radiate magical power back into the sky. Some scholars theorize that they are of religious or ritualistic significance, perhaps a means of offering magic back to the heavens. Those with magical talents can draw magicka from the wells to restore and buff their own reserves of power. No ritual or arcane knowledge is necessary, suggesting that the wells were designed to serve unskilled magic users. Drained wells replenish their magicka at midnight.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mft S-1)</sup> Drawing power from these wells has also been known to bless the user with improved vigor,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> and Aetherial Wells have even been known to fully restore the user.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> [Azandar al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md) theorized that the power Ayleid Wells reroute back towards the heavens was being collected by someone living on [Arkay](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md) or [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh_(planet).md), though it is widely thought that no mortal beings are found there.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-8)</sup>

According to legend, the [Chim-el Adabal](Lore_Chim-el_Adabal.md) formed from the blood of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) when a drop fell down into an Ayleid Well.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
