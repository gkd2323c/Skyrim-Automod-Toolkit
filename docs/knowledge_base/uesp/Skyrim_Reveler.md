[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/SR-npc-Reveler.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Reveler.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Reveler.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Reveler.jpg) Three Revelers The **Revelers** are three happily-intoxicated [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) [farmers](Skyrim_Farmer_(class).md) you may meet at [random locations](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Drinking_Companions) while traveling the roads of Skyrim. All three wear random farm clothes, carry two bottles of [Honningbrew Mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Honningbrew_Mead), and immediately invite you to join the party.

## Dialogue
As you approach, one of them will seek you out and greet you:

*"Hail, friend! It's good to see another merry soul enjoying this fine day. Ah, but you look tired. Come, share a bottle of Honningbrew Mead with me!"* When you ask him to elaborate on Honningbrew Mead, he will provide a passionate description:

**Honningbrew Mead?** *"You've never had a taste of sweet Honningbrew? Why, it's only the finest mead outside of Sovngarde! Its sweetness is like a fair maiden's kiss on a starry night. And it's got enough kick to put a frost troll on his hind quarters! Ha! But enough talk. Come! Won't you share a drink with me and my companions?"* Three options are available at this point, depending on your inventory; if you don't have any Honningbrew Mead, the Reveler will hand you a bottle with the words:

**I would love to share a drink with you, friend!** *"Ha, nothing like fine spirits to help raise your... well, your spirits! Cheers, my friend! May your adventures find you fame and fortune!"* If you already own a bottle, and suggest that you split it with your newfound friends, he will be excited:

**Why only have one bottle when you can have two?** *"Ysmir's beard! You're one after my own heart. I would love another bottle. This good deed should not go unrewarded. Here, take this! Cheers, my friend! May your adventures find you fame and fortune!"* He will then hand you a [Charmed Necklace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Charmed_Necklace).

However, if you only own a bottle of [Black-Briar Mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Black-Briar_Mead), the Revelers will get angry:

**Perhaps you'd like to share this Black Briar *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* Mead with me instead?** *"Black Briar * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] *? How dare you shove that swill in my face. I'd rather drink mammoth drool!"* After that insult, you will no longer be able to communicate with them:

*"I don't talk to people who drink that Black Briar * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * drench."*
## Conversations
When you meet the three Revelers in the wilderness, they will often be engaged in conversation:

**Reveler #1**: *"Ah, is there any way you'd rather spend the day than drinking Honningbrew Mead?"*
**Reveler #2**: *"Drink Honningbrew Mead with a wench on each arm? Ha ha!"*
**Reveler #1**: *"I'd rather have two bottles of mead then * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * two wenches, friend."*
**Reveler #2**: *"Why's that?"*
**Reveler #1**: *"The meads won't argue your ear off about which one should go first!"*
**Reveler #2**: *"Ha! Too true, my friend. Too true..."*
## Notes
- If they are not killed by a hostile NPC or creature, they can sometimes be found working as laborers at one of the settlements near to where you encounter them.
- If you don't have any Honningbrew Mead on you and you are quick (and you don't mind pickpocketing) you can sneak up to a Reveler and pickpocket a bottle. You must do this before the NPC approaches and engages you in conversation. Then when the NPC engages you in conversation you have a bottle to swap for the necklace.
- Any attempt at using magic on the Revelers, even healing magic, results in them turning hostile. However they will not chase you very far.
- Having other kinds of mead in your inventory (Ashfire, Dragonbreath, Nord) does not affect possible dialogue options.

The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.4, fixes this bug.
