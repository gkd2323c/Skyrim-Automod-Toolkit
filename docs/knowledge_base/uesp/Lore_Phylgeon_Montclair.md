[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg/200px-ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Ranser.jpg) King Ranser, Phylgeon's older half-brother [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair.jpg) Baron Wylon Montclair, Phylgeon's son Phylgeon Montclair was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman who reigned as the thirty-eighth Baron of [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He was a son of [King Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) and Countess [Iphilia Montclair](Lore_Iphilia_Montclair.md). His mother, Iphilia, died in [2E 544](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_544), when he was twelve years old, making him the baron of House Montclair. Two years later, in [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546), his father died, which instigated a conflict between Phylgeon and his older illegitimate half-brother [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) over Shornhelm's throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup>

Phylgeon's advisors contended that he, as the legitimate son, was the proper heir to the throne. Although Phylgeon was the legitimate heir to the throne, [Houses Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) supported Ranser despite him being Hurlburt's illegitimate son. The fourteen-year-old Phylgeon was descended from [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md), and his claim was supported by the codicil to the "Bretonnick Natalitie", which declared "Howse Mount Clayre" the royal house of Shornhelm. However, the codicil was missing when the Council of the North met to discuss the two claimants. Prince Ranser presented a long-lost [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) decree that named House Branquette their Breton Royal Delegates in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), winning the crown by the vote of the council. The vote of the council was a narrow victory for Ranser. Some of Phylgeon's advisors urged him to fight for the crown, but the young prince declined, preferring to remain simply the Baron of Montclair.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup>

According to Phylgeon's son, his humility was one of his defining traits, which led to the tragic events of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566). Phylgeon was uncertain of the rightness of Ranser's cause and offered to both Kings Ranser and [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) to serve as a peace envoy between the two sides. Emeric's reply has been lost to history, but it is known that Ranser refused. After that, Phylgeon deferred to his older brother and provided the knights of House Montclair to fight in Ranser's army.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup> House Montclair did not surrender to [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md)'s alliance until just before the Battle of [Markwasten Moor](Lore_Markwasten_Moor.md), the second to last battle of Ranser's War, in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HTARH-2)</sup>

After the death of Ranser in 2E 567, Rivenspire fell into chaos.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup> With no king to rule the region, Rivenspire was governed by the triumvirate Council of the North, composed of the heads of Houses Tamrith, Montclair, and [Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-NAA-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HTARH-2)</sup> Although Phylgeon had a strong claim to the throne of Shornhelm, he never became king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup>

At some point, Phylgeon fathered a son, [Wylon](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), who, by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), had succeeded him as the Baron of House Montclair.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup> Wylon argued that as Phylgeon's son and therefore Hurlburt's grandson, he had the strongest claim to the throne of Shornhelm, but he was ultimately killed attempting to become king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO-4)</sup>
