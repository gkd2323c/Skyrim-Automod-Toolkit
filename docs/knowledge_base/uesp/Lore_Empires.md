# Empires


The following is an approximate chronological account of all the notable empires, kingdoms and dynasties that have existed on Nirn.

## Kingdom of Pyandonea
[![ON-banner-Sea Elves.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-banner-Sea_Elves.png/100px-ON-banner-Sea_Elves.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Sea_Elves.png) **Early Merethic Era — Unknown** *Main article: [Kingdom of Pyandonea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kingdom_of_Pyandonea)* Very little is know about the [Kingdom of Pyandonea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kingdom_of_Pyandonea), one of the first ruling kingdoms on Nirn, other than scant details discerned from interactions with the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), hearsay and legends. According to long-untranslated tapestries within the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), the kingdom was established in the Early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) after a phenomenally wealthy [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) nobleman named [Orgnum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgnum) attempted to instigate a rebellion against the rulers of Aldmeris - the ancestral homeland of the Aldmer. The coup failed and Orgnum and several other clans was exiled from Aldmeris to an island continent surrounded by an impenetrable mist, known as Pyandonea.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFRM-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

It was on Pyandonea that Orgnum styled himself as king and "Voice of the Free Maormer People" over the other exiled clans most loyal to him. He then began marshalling his organized and well-disciplined forces in preparation for a triumphant return.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TCPA-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFRM-1)</sup> Not all Maormer remained part of his kingdom and over time some breakaway clans descended into near-barbarism.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM64:PS-5)</sup>

The veil of mist surrounding Pyandonea prevented Orgnum from ever disturbing Aldmeris, but that changed when the Aldmer migrated from their homeland and settled on the Summerset Isles, located north of Pyandonea.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> The Kingdom of Pyandonea made countless attempts to invade the Summerset Isle backed by a strong revanchist policy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFRM-1)</sup> For almost every year of the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md) eras, Pyandonean forces led by King Orgnum harassed the coastlines of Summerset.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3SI-6)</sup> They were also known to have pushed further north, as well, and harassed the coastline of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3V-7)</sup>

The extent of the kingdom's territory outside of the island continent remains unknown, but they have been known to sign treaties with independent states. One such treaty was with Khenarthi's Roost in the mid-Second Era whereby the Maormer Enforcement Squadrons agreed to not perpetrate unprovoked acts of aggression towards merchant ships in or around the port city of Mistral, in return for fifteen percent of all registry fees and excises.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-To KR-8)</sup> Orgnum has supposedly ruled the kingdom as its sole monarch since its founding in the Merethic Era until at least [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), partly due to his purported status as "a deathless wizard" who grows more youthful by the century.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup>

## Kingdom of Alinor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/LO-map-Summerset_Isle_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Summerset_Isle_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Summerset_Isle_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Summerset_Isle_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) The Summerset Isles **Early Merethic Era — [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22)** *Main article: [Kingdom of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md)* The [Kingdom of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) is one of the earliest known ruling kingdoms on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). It can be traced backed to the [Early Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Early_Merethic_Era) when the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) fled [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) and made landfall on the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in southwestern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) was one of the earliest settlements in the kingdom, established by High Lord [Torinaan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torinaan), but soon other kinholds followed his example and prospered. These sophisticated, literate and technologically advanced elves displaced the native [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) and built the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) - firmly establishing their empire. From [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) ruled an unknown High King, whose dynasty was said to extend unbroken until at least the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md)..

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg/200px-ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg) Alinor The mysterious Scrolls of Praxis played an enormous role in Aldmeri monarchic rule. According to the traditions of Elven Ceremoniarchy, every decision a ruler made must be based on the precedents laid down in the scrolls. Such is their import that heirs to the throne were required to study Altmeri Regal Praxis and Ceremoniarchy under the [Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Sapiarchs) for a requisite 3,555 days prior to receiving the crown. The coronation itself supposedly involved an eighty-eight day liturgy recited by the monarch themselves. When a new monarch inherited the throne, they traveled to [Tanzelwil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tanzelwil), vast ruins on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) to commune with the dead and seek their approval to the throne. Afterward, the College of Sapiarchs serve as a powerful resource to the Throne of Alinor, offering advice and counsel when called upon.

Provincial rulers were known as High Kinlords and Kinladys, such as High Kinlady [Estre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Estre) of [Skywatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skywatch) or High Kinlord [Rilis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rilis_XII) of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md). Other offices of power include Canonreeve - similar to a mayor, it's an official title given to diplomats and bureaucrats who administer county seats.

Firsthold later became the staging ground for several naval expeditions, by order of the Crystal Tower. Three ships were chosen to explore uncharted territories and find a way back to Aldmeris. Although only one ship returned, they successfully mapped numerous coastlines of mainland Tamriel, setting the stage for the eventual Aldmeri diaspora. According to some legends, the Aldmer didn't stop at mere naval exploration. A mythical order known as the [Sun Birds of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun_Birds_of_Alinor) attempted extra-planetary expeditions to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).

From the ancient wellspring of Summerset, the Aldmer eventually split along cultural and religious lines resulting in the plethora of distinct Elven races we know of today. As various groups of Elves began to emigrate from Summerset, some (such as the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md)) continued to swear fealty to the High King of Alinor. Others, such as the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) clan, became independent from the kingdom and sought their own path. Arguabally one of the most notable schisms in the kingdom was the first recorded large-scale religious sect espousing [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship by the so-called prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md). When the Sapiarchs of Alinor prohibited this schism, Veloth led the clans loyal to him out of the Isles and across the seas to the far side of Tamriel. Another notable schism in the kingdom was when the Aldmer began to move away from [worshipping their own ancestors](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md), instead worshipping the ancestors of the elite. A group of dissident elders, known as the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), rejected this change and left Summerset to settle on the nearby island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) to continue practicing the "Old Ways of Aldmeris". By the Late Merethic Era numerous groups of elves had left the kingdom to settle other parts of Tamriel and tribute-lands, such as [Cyrod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrod) under the Ayleids, detached themselves from the kingdom completely. The Aldmer that remained on Summerset became known as the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and in the following eras became largely removed from the conflicts and rise and fall of empires on mainland Tamriel.

One of the main threats the kingdom faced was the Kingdom of Pyandonea to the south. King Orgnum made countless attempts to invade the Summerset Isle backed by a strong revanchist policy. For almost every year of the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md) eras, Pyandonean forces harassed the coastlines of Summerset. The Kingdom of Alinor was forced to build a great navy to defend itself, which resulted in the Altmer excelling in naval combat. The Royal Navy keeps the sea lanes as clear as possible and serves as a deterrent to external threats.

One of the few instances the Kingdom of Alinor involved itself with external conflicts pre-Second Era was when Admiral [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo) proclaimed his intent to punish [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) for the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) in [1E 2241](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2241). In response, the shipyards in Alinor worked in concert with other nations to build a fleet and attack Thras. Additionally, the great warlock [Syrabane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syrabane) lent aid given his prowess in arcane naval warfare.

### [The Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md)
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) The Kingdom of Alinor allied with its neighbors to form the First and Second Aldmeri Dominion during the Second Era. While the justifications for each incarnation differed, the objective was the same objective: to reassert Elven dominance over Tamriel. The First Dominion saw the Summerset Isles allied with the Bosmer of [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr.md) in the Elden Accord. To that end, the kingdom fought in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) against the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) for control of mainland Tamriel.

The Second Dominion was formed in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830) when the Kingdom of Alinor conquered Valenwood over a dynastic dispute. When the heirs of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) sought to capture Valenwood's throne, a faction of Bosmer attempted to make peace with their enemies in the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) by offering part of the Valenwood territory in exchange for the faction's own claimant. The Kingdom of Alinor, upon hearing of this, invaded Valenwood, citing a stewardship clause in a thousand-year-old treaty between their nations. The Altmer established the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) as the new government in Valenwood. Over the next several years, the Thalmor strengthened their hold on Valenwood and Bosmer tribes continued to skirmish with the Colovian Estates. Meanwhile, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) rose to power in Cyrodiil and began his conquest of Tamriel with the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). The nascent [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) expanded and encroached on Dominion territory, forcing the Bosmer attacks to subside. Despite their military setbacks on land, Alinor's Royal Navy was considerably more powerful and steadfastly held the southern waters from the [Cape of the Blue Divide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cape_of_the_Blue_Divide) to the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The catastrophic use of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) ended the long confrontation in [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), resulting in the surrender of the Kingdom of Alinor and its absorption into the Empire.

A Third Dominion was established centuries later in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), in the aftermath of the Oblivion Crisis. By painting themselves as the saviors of the Summerset Isles, the Thalmor solidified their position and seized total control of the Summerset Isles. They abolished the monarchy across the Summerset Isles, ending one of the oldest kingdoms on Nirn. Seven years later, a Thalmor-backed coup overthrew Valenwood's government. Alinor and Valenwood proclaimed a union independent of the Empire, and the Aldmeri Dominion was reborn. Elsweyr split into the kingdoms of Anequina and Pelletine in [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115) and subsequently became client states of the Dominion.ki

## Snow Elf Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_%28Vale%29.jpg/200px-SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_%28Vale%29.jpg)](File_SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_(Vale).jpg.md) [](File_SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_(Vale).jpg.md) The Inner Sanctum of the Chantry of Auri-El - the last known remnant of the Snow Elf empire. **Middle Merethic Era — Early First Era** *Main article: [Snow Elf Empire](Lore_Snow_Elf_Empire.md)* Little is known about the empire of the [Snow Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Elves) other than what has been derived from scholarly works, legends and myths. Given that the religious pantheon of the Snow Elves overlaps significantly with the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) pantheon (which itself was first established on the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md)) suggests the Snow Elves split from their Aldmer brethren on Summerset in the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VOF-10)</sup> When they arrived in the land known today as [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), they founded their empire and prospered greatly, and according to some scholars possessed a degree of sophistication and power unparalleled in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) at that time.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-NOT-11)</sup> Its unknown the extent to which their borders reached, as virtually no Snow Elf structures or cities remain in Skyrim, [except one that was discovered underground](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Greenwall) near the city of [Riften](Lore_Riften.md).

When the [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) migrated to their lands in the Late Merethic Era, the two races live in relative peace for some time, and the Atmorans thrived, summoning more of their kin from Atmora to build the city of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1S-12)</sup> The peace didn't last and soon the Snow Elves found themselves in constant conflict.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-9)</sup> The catalyst that would lead to the destruction of the Snow Elf Empire was the infamous [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md). Why the elves instigated the massacre of Saarthal is disputed; attributed motives include fear, religious and military provocations, territorial disputes, and a desire to possess [an ancient power](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md) the humans had uncovered.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1S-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-NOT-11)</sup>

The elves drove the Atmoran survivors from their shores, but [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) soon returned with his [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Companions.md) and began his onslaught on the elven empire. It is unknown how long the conflict, known as [the Return](Lore_The_Return.md), lasted but by the beginning of the First Era the elves built the [Chantry of Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chantry_of_Auri-El) in the isolated [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) to serve as the epicenter of their religion.<sup>[[nb 1]](#cite_note-13)</sup> Whatever resistance the elves put up was no match for the Atmoran horde and the war continued in their favor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-9)</sup> After wiping out organized elven resistance in Skyrim, the Nordic armies sought their enemy out on the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). The elves made a last stand in the [Battle of the Moesring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_the_Moesring): after an intense battle, their leader, the [Snow Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Prince), was killed, and the empire of the Snow Elves was destroyed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-FOTSP-14)</sup> The survivors fled underground to hide and those who remained on the surface were exterminated in the following decades, even as late as [1E 140](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_140).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SSSJ-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DOFA-16)</sup>

Around three centuries passed until the Dwemer arrived in Skyrim sometime after [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DI1-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AP-18)</sup>. They Dwemer established their own kingdom [under Skyrim itself](Lore_Blackreach.md), and many Snow Elves turned to their underground brethren for help.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TFAS-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-JOMA-20)</sup> The Dwemer offered to provide them a haven, but forced their wards into servitude, only allowing them to consume a toxic fungus which rendered them blind. Over time, the servants became slaves, but despite this the Snow Elves would rise up against their Dwarven Masters waging the unseen War of the Crag, which was at a standstill for centuries until the dissapearance of the dwarves. Unfortunately winning the war against the Dwarves by default did not stop the death knell of the Snow Elves, as they still degenerated into the bestial race known today as the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TFAS-19)</sup>

## Ayleid Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg/200px-OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg) The White-Gold Tower **Middle Merethic Era — [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243)** *Main article: [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md)* The [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) began in the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) when [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) from the [Kingdom of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) left the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and settled the [Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands) of what is now modern-day [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The Ayleid settlements initially remained as tribute-land to the High King of Alinor, however the long lines of communication from the Summerset Isles afforded them a degree of isolation and many had even taken to Daedric worship - although this itself sometime led to internal conflict. The Ayleids remained subservient to the Kingdom of Alinor until at least [1E 1](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1), when the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), known then as the Temple of the Ancestors, was established as an independent city-state.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-21)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3SI-6)</sup>

Although it was been described as "first Tamrielic empire",<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-WGTAP-22)</sup> the term "Ayleid Empire" itself is a misnomer as there was never one King of the Ayleids. Instead the empire was divided into independent city-states, each ruled by a sorcerer-king. Each sorcerer-king's power waxed and waned over the course of the Empire,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Herminia-23)</sup> and city-states often did not allow their neighbors within their walls.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MTCKN-24)</sup> The position of sorcerer-king was known to be passed hereditarily upon death,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-25)</sup> but reign rarely passed to heirs without some kind of arcane intrigue.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

The Empire, though united at times, warred within itself on multiple occasions, with some notable conflicts being the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md),<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ASIV-27)</sup> the war between [Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abagarlas) and [Delodiil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Delodiil),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TWo D-28)</sup> and the rampage of [Celemaril Light-Bringer](Lore_Celemaril.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Saashi-29)</sup> Over time the Ayleids went from a society that worshipped the Aedra, to both Daedra and Aedra, until the Daedraphiles gained complete political dominance, pushing out the remaining Aedra-worshipping Ayleids into Black Marsh. While the Ayleids had long enslaved the Nedic population of Cyrodiil (and potentially the beastfolk races) the shift of power to the Daedraphilic Ayleids lead to decadent and unspeakable horrors heaped upon the enslaved populations, backed by armies of undead and Daedra.

The Ayleid Empire ultimately fell after [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), a [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) slave of the Ayleids, launched a [rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) against her slavemasters in [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-SATD-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TLST-31)</sup> This in turn resulted in the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md) and the eventual extinction of the Ayleids.

**See the [main article](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) for more information.**

## Lefthand Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) Yokuda, homeland of the Lefthanded Elves **Middle Merethic Era — Late Merethic Era** Very little is known about the empire of the [Lefthanded Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lefthanded_Elves) that existed on [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). It is best remembered from its conflict with the ancient Yokudans, who were said to have fought with them for a thousand years.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONLoad-32)</sup>

While most sources deduce that they were a variant of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) (presumably from [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) stock), others question this narrative and claim this was a Yokudan transcription error where "Elf" and "enemy" are essentially the same, and that Lefthanded Elves were simply Lefthanded enemies.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SH2-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LHbelt-34)</sup>

Regardless, it is said their vast empire was four times the size the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) under [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), which itself accounted for the entirety of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LVSMCt R-35)</sup> Eventually the Yokudans defeated the Lefthanded Elves and drove them out of Yokuda in the Merethic Era, although some remnants briefly settled on [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md) in [1E 660](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_660) before being wiped out by the encroaching [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) in [1E 785](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_785).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SH2-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-So A-36)</sup>

## Sixteen Kingdoms of Elsweyr
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c7/ON-misc-Pieces_of_History.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Pieces_of_History.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Pieces_of_History.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Pieces_of_History.jpg) A tapestry depicting the tribes of Khajiit **Merethic Era — [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260)** During the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) the province now called [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) was inhabited by sixteen [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) tribes that eventually grew into sixteen kingdoms ruled by a single [Moon-Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon-Emperor).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOAPAI-37)</sup> These were not comparable to the kingdoms of Men and Mer in terms of attaching tribal names to ownership over certain locations, rather the clans moved as they needed to across the province. The kingdoms didn't initially fight each other for power or land, rather they existing in harmony and complemented each other. Each kingdom was specialized in providing goods or services and shared it with their neighbors.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-EGTE-38)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOAPAI-37)</sup> [Ne Quin-al](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ne_Quin-al), also known as [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), for example provided their excellent trained fighters of the [Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Two-Moons_Dance) to [Torval](Lore_Torval.md) in exchange for fish and other goods from the sea. Influence and power shifted constantly, and the dominance of the regions cycled through the kingdoms depending on the phases of [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md). For example, Ne Quin-al was in power at full moons, while [Torval](Lore_Torval.md) was when both moons were half, or [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) when they were both new.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE3E-39)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-PGE1E-40)</sup>

These early kingdoms faced external threats however, such as when [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid) and his horde of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) attempted to dominate the land<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Kat D-41)</sup> or when [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) led a pogrom against the Khajiit, having mistaken them for another strain of [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-VOFTK-42)</sup>. In [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461) the Golden Beast of Anequina, [Darloc Brae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darloc_Brae), expanded the territory of Ne Quin-al in the process of the [Anequine Conquests](Lore_Anequine_Conquests.md) until it ranged from Khaj Rawlith in the west, over [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia), up to the north at [Larsius River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Larsius_River), and [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) in the east.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-RTR-43)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LSKR-44)</sup>

The political balance in the sixteen Khajiiti kingdoms proved stable enough for thousands of years. Neither the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) nor the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) dared to extend their borders too far into the lands of the Khajiit, but caused increasing animosity between the Khajiiti realms.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE3E-39)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-EGTE-38)</sup> This balance of the sixteen kingdoms changed drastically in the aftermath of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-EGTE-38)</sup> Expanding along the trading routes deep into the realm of the Khajiit, the plague caused a mass mortality and decimated the sixteen kingdoms, forcing the survivors into roles they did not choose. Of the original sixteen kingdoms, only two remained: The northern [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) with its many warrior clans, and the wealthy southern [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md). Both kingdoms fought for centuries, but none of both could ever gain the upper hand.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE3E-39)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-EGTE-38)</sup>

At some point in the First Era, the role of Moon-Emperor as a ruler of the Khajiit disappeared from the historical record and was seemingly replaced by the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md). Although it is unknown exactly when the role of Mane was established, Mane [Jinninji-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jinninji-ri) was known to have ruled towards the very end of the First Era in [1E 2902](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2902), centuries before the supposed "First Mane" [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ONShazah-45)</sup>

With the rise of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), the newfound [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) began to expand. The two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine could not resist the Empire's power and were conquered following the Reman Conquests.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-MBHAYQ-46)</sup> The kingdoms remained intact and their forces were absorbed into the Empire and even fought under its banner. For example when Queen Naghea of [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) lead the left flank for the Empire in the [Battle of Bodrum](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-2920FS-47)</sup> Also in the same year the King of Senchal, [Dro'Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro%27Zel), became appointed advisor to the court of [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-2920SD-48)</sup> Despite being part of the Second Empire, Anequina and Pellitine never stopped rebelling against it at times.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE3E-39)</sup> The Empire was also unable to stop the conflicts between the two Khajiiti kingdoms.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-PGE1E-40)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-EGTE-38)</sup> The two kingdoms eventually resolved their differences, albeit temporarily, when they merged to form the [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md) in [2E 309](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_309).

## Dwemer Kingdoms
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/MW-place-Arkngthand.jpg/200px-MW-place-Arkngthand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Arkngthand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Arkngthand.jpg) Arkngthand on Vvardenfell [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/SR-place-Mzulft.jpg/200px-SR-place-Mzulft.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mzulft.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mzulft.jpg) Mzulft in Skyrim **Late Middle Merethic Era — [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700)** The earliest [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) settled on the land known today as [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) around the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). According to some legends, the Dwemer originated from the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), and were treated as another tribe, and later, as a secular [Great House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-56)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-57)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-58)</sup> Other sources however claim they already occupied the northeast of Tamriel when [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) and his people arrived there.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PGE3S-59)</sup> Regarless of their origin, by the Late Middle Merethic Era they had established underground cities in the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) and the Kingdom of Dwemereth was born. During this time the Dwemer frequently warred with their Chimer neighbors, who provoked them with minor raids and territorial disputes for centuries.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-21)</sup> Some claim these battles were fueled by the conflicting religious beliefs while others claim it was due to territory and resources.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-PGE1M-60)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-21)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-PGE3M-61)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-21)</sup>

By the Late Merethic Era, High Velothi culture began to decline and the Chimer devolved into tribal cultures. This coincided with the founding of the earliest Dwemer freehold colonies on Vvardenfell, which by conflicting accounts was either a separate island or part of the mainland.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BTAOM-21)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TSLT-62)</sup> Over the next few centuries the Dwemer flourished and established a wide network of cities that became known as the Kingdom of Vvardenfell,<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-PGE1M-60)</sup> although its unclear if this was considered part of the Kingdom of Dwemereth or was an independent kingdom.

When the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) invaded Dwemereth in [1E 222](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_222), the Kingdom of Vvardenfell was said to have been a united group with a collective identity. They were able to resist the Nordic conquest and remained independent. Conversely, the divided and feuding Chimer clans on mainland Morrowind fell to the Nords.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-PGE1M-60)</sup> After almost two hundred years of oppression under the Nords, the Dwemer and the Chimer put aside their differences and formed an alliance to fight back. [General Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) of the Chimer and [King Dumac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dumac) of Vvardenfell were successful in driving the invaders out in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416) and went on to form the [First Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Council). Under the joint-kingdom of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md), Nerevar and Dumac ruled and worked together to maintain peace, despite Nerevar's close allies pushing him to claim all Resdayn for the Chimer.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Na RM-63)</sup>

In the following years, Lord [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac) was known as the chief Dwemer architect of the great "**Second Empire** freeholds".<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-GA-64)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Yagrum-65)</sup> Exactly what empire this refers to is unclear, and implies a "first" Dwemer empire existed too - possibly referring to the previously mentioned Dwemer kingdoms of Dwemereth and Vvardenfell. By extension, this would place the joint-kingdom of Resdayn as their Second Empire. Regardless, while Resdayn prospered the Dwemer chieftain of the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) Clan protested against an alliance with the Chimer. In [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) the Rourken Clan exiled themselves from Resdayn and traveled to the lands known today as [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) to establish their own independent kingdom named [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md).<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-PGE3H-66)</sup>

Following the path laid out by the Rourken, other Dwemer clans began to migrate from Resdayn and establish their own freeholds and settlements where they could live in greater isolation. Indeed, some scholars suggest that the many Dwemer cities in Skyrim and Hammerfell were built only after the Rourken exodus.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DI1-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AP-18)</sup> The [Kragen](Lore_Kragen.md) Clan were first to establish their presence in Skyrim with the founding of [Arkngthamz](Lore_Arkngthamz.md). While migrating they discovered a considerable source of [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md) and built the city to research it. The city flourished, so much so that it encouraged other clans to expand their own holdings to the west. The city-states of Bthar-zel, Raldbthar, and Mzulft joined them, and this loose alliance of clans effectively established a pocket empire within Skyrim. The Nords of course took exception to the Dwemer settling in their lands and regularly attacked them, but with little success. It was during this time their own empire had collapsed and they were in the midst of fighting the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) and [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AP-18)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TAW-67)</sup>

Eventually, internal disputes would occur over the Aetherium, and the alliance between the city-states shattered. This conflict (which could have only occurred at some point between [1E 533](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_533) and around [1E 580](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_580) at the latest)<sup>[[nb 2]](#cite_note-68)</sup> lasted decades and was known as the Aetherium Wars. This internal fighting weakened the Dwemer, which allowed King [Gellir](Lore_Gellir.md) of Skyrim to briefly conquer the city-states, although the Dwemer reclaimed most of their lands a century later.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TAW-67)</sup>

Ultimately, the peace between the Dwemer and Chimer back in Resdayn dissolved and sparked the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md). It ended at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), which saw the simultanious collapse of all Dwemer kingdoms on Nirn after the entire Dwemer race suddennly disappeared.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-TBORM-69)</sup>

## Ysgramor Dynasty
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg/200px-SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) Ysgramor's palace in Windhelm **Late Merethic Era — [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369)** *Main article: [Ysgramor Dynasty](Lore_Ysgramor_Dynasty.md)* The [Ysgramor Dynasty](Lore_Ysgramor_Dynasty.md) (also called the **Ysgramor Clan**) is the name given to the series of kings that ruled [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) after the time of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) in the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). It pre-dates and overlaps with the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) by centuries. Initially, Ysgramor established his kingdom after his victory against the [Snow Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Elves). In the aftermath of [The Return](Lore_The_Return.md), Ysgramor laid the foundations of the first human Empire by building the city of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and the [Palace of the Kings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Palace_of_the_Kings) which dominated the center of the Old City. [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), and [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), collectively known as the "Old Holds", were also established.

After Ysgramor died, his descendants went on to rule his kingdom for centuries after. However, it was also around this time the authority of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) took hold and [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) ruled the land with an iron fist. The relationship between the cult and Ysgramor's Dynasty in respect to ruling is unknown, but some sources speculate that Ysgramor could have been a Dragon Priest himself (or at least associated with them) given his stature at the time. Also, weaponry and heirlooms bearing dragon-headed mofits were known to be a mark of the royal line.

The Dragon Cult was eventually overthrown after the Dragon War and the line of kings from Ysgramor's Dynasty continued in the following centuries. While many of the kings that came after Ysgramor remain unknown, one prominent ruler stands out. King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) was the thirteenth in Ysgramor's line and is remembered for being the first king to relinquish all holdings in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). Establishing the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) as a separate people, he was responsible for the consolidation of Skyrim as a centralized kingdom.

The Jagged Crown dates back to around Harald's reign and served as the symbol of office for the High King. The period of its usage is remembered as a time when the High King reigned by right of arms and collective adulation. Around [1E 184](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_184), Harald established the first [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) - a gathering of representatives from each Hold to choose the next King from qualified members of the royal family. This continued as the system of succession for centuries.

Ancient records indicate that Ysgramor's heirs battled a mysterious figure named Aumriel for decades until it was sealed away in [Frostmere Crypt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostmere_Crypt). The Nordic goal of conquering the vast new land of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) was fully realized under Harald's son, [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md). King Vrage initiated multiple military expansions in [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240) that pushed past the borders of Ysgramor's kingdom and into [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and heralded a greater empire known as the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md). While it is unknown exactly how many rulers were part of the dynasty, the Ysgramor's lineage ended [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369) with the death of [King Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md).

## Camoran Dynasty
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), the realm over which the Camoran Dynasty ruled **[1E 0](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_0) — [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267)** *Main article: [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md)* The [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md), also known as the **Camorian Empire**, was an aristocratic dynasty that ruled [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) for much of its history. One of the earliest known dynasties in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), its founding by King [Eplear](Lore_Eplear.md) is an event traditionally regarded as the beginning of [Year Zero](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_0) of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Eplear's success in uniting the inherently wild [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) has been argued to be one of the greatest military feats in Tamriel's history. The Camorans remained in power for centuries afterwards, even resisting the aggressive expansion of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), until Valenwood was conquered by the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) in [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714) following years of unrelenting warfare and the devastation of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md). The Camoran Dynasty survived, but the Empire broke its influence by granting independence to Valenwood's lesser nobles.

When the Second Empire fell in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), the Camorans unsuccessfully attempted to reestablish their reign, but Valenwood's territories had drifted too far apart culturally to be united and the region descended into conflict. Valenwood would briefly achieve unity under Camoran rule only twice during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), and then only as a member of the first and second [Aldmeri Dominions](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). At the time of the first Aldmeri Dominion in the Sixth Century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran) was crowned king and joined the Dominion to defend his realm against encroaching [Colovians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Colovian) in the north. Viewed by some Bosmer as disrespectful of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), Aeradan was forced to fight a civil war against the Blacksap Rebellion led by his cousin Gelthior Camoran, and later suppressed an attempted second insurrection by Gelthior's sons Gorinir and Lorchon in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). A subsequent dispute over the succession between the Camoran heirs in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830) led to the intervention of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and the crowning of [Camoran Anaxemes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camoran_Anaxemes), who pledged fealty to the [king of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) and thereby ushered in the second Dominion. Anaxemes was then forced to cede much of his power to the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), a congress of Bosmeri chieftains and Altmeri diplomats who proved modestly effective until [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) swept them away. Gorinir family was a cadet branch of the Camoran royal line.

Under the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the Camorans retained the throne of Valenwood as figurehead monarchs. The year [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249) saw another dispute arise over the Camoran succession, a dispute which soon escalated into a massive war that threatened the whole of the Empire. One claimant to the throne, Camoran Kaltos, conquered most of Valenwood in two years with the aid of [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md), also called Haymon Hart-King, another claimant and Kaltos's chief advisor and sorcerer. Against Kaltos's wishes, however, Haymon declared war on the people of Colovia and marched his armies north. Kaltos pleaded with Haymon to return to Valenwood, but Haymon betrayed him and claimed the Camoran throne for himself, thus becoming the Camoran Usurper. The Usurper's armies ravaged western Tamriel for nearly twenty years, driving through [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) all the way to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) while Kaltos unsuccessfully fought to stop him. At last the Principality of [Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen) marshaled the other city-states of High Rock and joined forces with Camoran Kaltos to defeat the Camoran Usurper at the [Battle of Firewaves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Firewaves); Haymon Hart-King was killed in the battle.

## Kingdom of Orsinium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg) King Kurog's Orsinium circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) **Early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) — [1E 980](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_980), [2E 418](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_418) — Late [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399)<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-HOPTTO-70)</sup> — [4E 11](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_11)**,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Lo S-71)</sup> **[4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15) — Present** *Main article: [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md)* Numerous incarnations of Orsinium have risen and fallen over the eras, but only three are known to history. The Kingdom of Orsinium was first built by [King Torug](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torug_gro-Igron) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), straddling the boundary between western [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) and [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire). It was later bolstered by many goblin-ken migrations upon their emancipation from slavery, notably [Thulgeg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thulgeg)'s migration in [1E 874](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_874),<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-BSHR-72)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TM-73)</sup> before it was destroyed by [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) forces during the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md). In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the city was relocated to eastern Wrothgar under King [Kurog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog). In [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), it was relocated again to a site between [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), [Menevia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Menevia), and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains), but among the chaos of the Stormcrown Interregnum was sacked between [4E 11](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_11) and [4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15).<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Lo S-71)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Orsinium had moved once more, farther south to [an area](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) between [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

Although Orsinium was accepted as a territory of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) during the reign of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), it largely remained as an independent state.

## Yokudan Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) The remnants of Yokuda in the Third Era **Early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) — [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792)** *Main article: [Yokudan Empire](Lore_Yokudan_Empire.md)* The Yokudans had existed on [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and during this time warred with the Lefthand Empire for a thousand years. The Yokudans eventually defeated the [Lefthanded Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lefthanded_Elves) and prospered in their own right, culminating in the establishment of their own empire.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-RTHATH-74)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VOF-10)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ONload Nilata-75)</sup> The ruling body of the Yokudan people was known as the [Na-Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Na-Totambu), a council of kings who represented the various nations of Yokuda.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-TNTOY-76)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ONYokudan-77)</sup>

By the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Yokuda had become a vast empire, and developed a tradition of being ruled by a single emperor, however this was eventually abolished in [1E 376](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_376). Although each successive emperor remained as the figurehead of the empire, his powers were very much reduced. As a result, the nation descended into three hundred years of continuous civil war between the provincial lords, warrior monks, and brigands, all fighting each other for land and power.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-RTHATH-74)</sup> In [1E 609](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_609), [Mansel Sesnit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mansel_Sesnit) became the Elden Yokeda, or military dictator and waged many of the battles of unification and succeeded in gaining control of almost the entire empire until he was assassinated in [1E 617](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_617). His successor, [Randic Torn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Randic_Torn), took over and continued the work of Sesnit, however by the time of his death in [1E 737](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_737) internal disturbances still had not been completely eliminated. After his death, another vicious civil war broke out.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-RTHATH-74)</sup>

In the chaotic aftermath, the "Last Emperor" of Yokuda, [Hira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hira), rose to power and attempted to wrestle control of the empire from the people by exterminating the [sword-singers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword-singers). This conflict became known as the [War of the Singers](Lore_War_of_the_Singers.md). In [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780), it came to a climax at the foot of [Hattu Mountain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hattu_Mountain), where [Frandar Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frandar_Hunding)'s army of singers crushed the emperor's forces.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-DTS-78)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-RTHATH-74)</sup>

While the Yokudan Empire in its original form fell in earnest after Yokuda sank into the ocean around [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792), the Na-Totambu ruling class who migrated to Hammerfell attempted to reproduce the culture and rule of Yokuda as close as possible, with mixed results. This meant that while the empire itself was gone, the Yokudans (and later the Redguards) continued the tradition of monarchic rule in some capacity.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ONload Totambu-79)</sup> One notable example was a Yokudan noble named [Tarish-Zi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tarish-Zi) who proclaimed himself emperor and led his warrior band to [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) in order to establish his own empire. While he ruled in the ancient Yokudan way, eventually his empire faded into obscurity in the years that followed.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-ONTitus-80)</sup> Another example was a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) noble named [Prince Hubalajad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hubalajad) who colonized [Hew's Bane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hew%27s_Bane) and established [Abah's Landing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abah%27s_Landing), from where his descendants would rule for several generations before fading away.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-IDo PH-81)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Load-82)</sup> Another example of parts of Hammerfell being ruled under a monarchy was the rise of Queen [Afsar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Afsar) of [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md). According to legend, the ruling king died in the late First Era with no heir, which led to squabbling between the Na-Totambu over succession to the throne. Afsar was said to have seized the throne the ancient way by killing the opposition and solidifying her rule. Despite its bloody start, her reign was supposedly very peaceful.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ONRAG-83)</sup>

The last known monarch of Hammerfell during the First Era was High King [Ar-Azal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ar-Azal) of the [Phyllocid Dynasty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phyllocid_Dynasty), who was best remembered for healing the rift between the [Na-Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Na-Totambu) ruling class and the warriors of the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) by marrying the daughters of their greatest Grandees.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-TWAAHD-84)</sup> When Hammerfell fell to [Reman I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_I) and was absorbed into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), the role of High King was seemingly abolished. Instead, the Na-Totambu and Ra Gada (now known as [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) respectively) would go on to aid [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s administration of Hammerfell. The Crowns retained the rights of noble council and the Forebears were granted rights of ownership within their tribal districts.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-85)</sup> This however didn't last, and the [Kingdom of Hammerfell](Lore_Kingdom_of_Hammerfell.md) that emerged during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) almost seven hundred years later could be considered the successor to the Yokudan Empire, given the Na-Totambu ruling class took power and reverted back to a hereditary monarchy.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-PGE1H-86)</sup>

## First Empire of the Nords
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), the province in which the First Nordic Empire was based **[1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240) — [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420)** *Main article: [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md)* The [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), also known as the **Nord Empire**, is the collective term for the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) kingdom during a phase of expansion in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), known as the Skyrim Conquests. Its capital was the city of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where a First Empire palace still stands. The empire was formed when [King Vrage the Gifted](Lore_Vrage.md) began a campaign to conquer [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) (a feat that is attributed by some to [Sai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai), the God of Luck) circa [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240). By the end of the conquests, the First Empire of the Nords encompassed Skyrim, parts or all of High Rock (sources conflict), Morrowind with the exception of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) kingdom of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and a large portion of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley). These invasions upset the balance in High Rock and Cyrodiil, and although the Nords' empire lasted less than two centuries, the result was decisive; from then on, power in those regions shifted from the elves to the humans.

Though the last ruler, King Borgas, died in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369), the ultimate end of the First Nordic Empire came in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), when the Pact of Chieftains ended the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) and the empire finally shattered; Skyrim eventually lost all of its holdings in Morrowind, Cyrodiil, and High Rock, and many of the Nordic kingdoms began to declare independence. The Nords were driven from Morrowind by force when [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) and [Dumac Dwarfking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dumac_Dwarfking) joined together, repelling the invaders in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416).

## Alessian Empire
[![ON-misc-Alessian Empire Emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/ON-misc-Alessian_Empire_Emblem.png/200px-ON-misc-Alessian_Empire_Emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Alessian_Empire_Emblem.png) **[1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) — [1E 2331](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2331)** *Main article: [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md)* The [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), also called the **First Empire**, the **Empire of Cyrodiil**, and the **Empire of Men**, was established by the former [Nede](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) slaves of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) following the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). The Empire traces its founding to the moment when Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) received the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) and the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) from [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md); she used the Amulet to drive the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) out of the mortal realm. Their empire would last for over two thousand years. Akatosh told Alessia that as long as the Dragonfires remained lit and [her heirs](Lore_Dragonborn.md) wore the Amulet of Kings, the gates of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) would remain shut and [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) would be spared the depredations of the Daedra.

Only a few rulers of the First Empire are now known: Queen Alessia, its founder and spiritual leader; [Belharza the Man-Bull](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Belharza), Alessia's heir by her consort [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus), crowned in [1E 266](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_266); [Gorieus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorieus), crowned in [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461); and [Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra), who expanded the reaches of the Empire all the way to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029). One of the defining events of the Empire was the rise of the zealously monotheistic [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order), which followed the teachings of the prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md). The Order's influence spread rapidly until, in [1E 361](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_361), its puritanical doctrines were formally adopted and enforced by the Empire. Thereafter, the arch-prelates of the Order were said to wield almost as much power as the emperors themselves. It was a sect of the Order called the Marukhati Selective that was responsible for the [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) known as the "Middle Dawn".

The Alessian Empire's territory fluctuated considerably throughout its history. At its inception it encompassed East [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), including the [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley) and the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), which later became the centerpiece of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md); West Cyrodiil remained a patchwork of independent [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) kingdoms that resisted the Alessian Order's dogma. A number of Ayleid vassal-states also briefly existed under the Empire's umbrella until the xenophobic Alessian Order methodically extinguished them. The allied city-state of [Miscarcand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miscarcand) initially resisted the Alessian Order by force, but over time its inhabitants fled to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to avoid persecution. Following the defeat and death of High King [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md) in [1E 478](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_478), the Empire seized portions of southern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), but failed in its bid to acquire the Kingdom of [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad). A westward expansion and attempt to annex High Rock was likewise thwarted at the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors) in [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482), which would be remembered as one of the Order's most disastrous mistakes. High Rock would later be inducted into the Empire in [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029) after the Empress [Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra) defeated King [Styriche](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Styriche), the Vampire of [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Hammerfell).md), only to reassert its independence in [1E 2305](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2305) in response to the Alessian Order's excesses. In [1E 1033](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1033) the Alessians under Hestra invaded [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) to suppress the pirates led by [Red Bramman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Bramman) operating from the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). An unknown Alessian Emperor agreed to fund the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md)'s campaign against the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) in [1E 2241](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2241). Under the command of King [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo), the fleet sailed to Thras in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260) to mete vengeance upon the Sload for releasing the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) on the people of Tamriel.

The final days of the Empire arrived in [1E 2321](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2321), when tensions within the Alessian Order's bloated priesthood gave way to internal strife, prompting the kingdoms of West Cyrodiil to completely sever ties with the Empire and establish their own government, the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md). Hemorrhaging money and land, the Empire finally broke under the strain and the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) erupted. After a decade of violence that wiped out half of the population of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) and saw the loss of the Order's extensive monastic complex at [Lake Canulus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Canulus), among untold other atrocities, the war ended in [1E 2331](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2331) with the dissolution of the Alessian Order and the enthronement of a puppet emperor by the king of Skingrad. Cyrodiil and Tamriel would remain divided until the rise of [Reman I](Lore_Reman.md) and the foundation of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703).

## Direnni Hegemony
[![ON-banner-Direnni.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/ON-banner-Direnni.png/200px-ON-banner-Direnni.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Direnni.png) **[1E 355](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_355) — [1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498)** *Main article: [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md)* The [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md) was an ancient Elven power that rose to dominance in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) around [1E 355](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_355), but some consider their rule in the years leading up to its establishment as part of the Direnni Hegemony as well. The [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) Hegemony fractured in [1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498), with one source claiming that the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) remained dominant until the middle of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), during which the whole of High Rock was commonly called the Direnni Hegemony. As an economic and military power, they were notable for their victories over the battle-hardened [Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the nascent [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The Direnni clan had migrated to High Rock during the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) diaspora of the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), alongside several other families. The progenitor of Clan Direnni was [Direnni Cygnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Direnni_Cygnus), who was said to have discovered the [Isle of Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balfiera) and the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) and claimed them for her own clan. Some believe the Direnni left the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) as they were following their destiny to rule from Balfiera, while others believed that they were exiled.

During the Merethic Era the Direnni came into contact with a [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) peoples, known today as the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) (or proto-Bretons). Whether these Nedes were native to High Rock or migrated is still debated in scholarly circles, but regardless Clan Direnni exerted its influence upon them and established the feudal system whereby they ruled the Nedes as a noble caste.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/LO-map-Morrowind_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Morrowind_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Morrowind_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Morrowind_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Kingdom of Resdayn **[1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416) — [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700)** The Kingdom of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) refers to the short-lived alliance between the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) had initially occupied [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) during the [Skyrim Conquests](Lore_Skyrim_Conquests.md) in [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240), but this eventually led to a rebellion led by [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) of the Chimer and [Dumac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dumac) of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). The peace between the Dwemer and Chimer brought prosperity, but the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) broke out when relations went sour. It ended with the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), which saw the death of both leaders. In the aftermath, the Chimer changed into the Dunmer, while the entire Dwemer race suddenly disappeared,<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-TBORM-69)</sup> and the golden era of the Tribunate began.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-MWAne-87)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-MWIdrenie-88)</sup>

## Kingdoms of Skyrim
**[1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) — Present** [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/79/ON-load-Western_Skyrim.png/200px-ON-load-Western_Skyrim.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Western_Skyrim.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Western_Skyrim.png) Solitude, the capital of Western Skyrim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-place-Windhelm2.jpg/200px-SR-place-Windhelm2.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Windhelm2.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Windhelm2.jpg) Windhelm, the capital of Eastern Skyrim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg) A battle between the Legion and the Stormcloaks *Main article: [Kingdoms of Skyrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kingdoms_of_Skyrim)* After the disastrous [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md), the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) crumbled as it lost control of its territories in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). While Skyrim as a nation remained relatively intact, its people were historically divided into [Holds](Lore_Skyrim.md#Holds), ruled by a patchwork of clan-heads, kings, and councils. Among the Old Holds, the people still revered their hereditary leaders, while the other Holds governed themselves by elected moots.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1S-12)</sup>

In the aftermath of the war in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), it became evident the former system of succession, known as the Moot, had become partisan and ineffective. When King Olaf died without a clear heir a new method of electing a High King became necessary.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1S-12)</sup> This new method involved the creation of the [Crown of Verity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown_of_Verity) - a magical artifact used to test the worthiness of potential rulers of Skyrim. Throughout the remainder [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the Crown of Verity was been passed down from High King to High King, used as a tool by the Moot to ascertain the worthiness of any candidate for the throne.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-COF-89)</sup>

While the new system of succession was purported to be an overwhelming success in later eras (to the degree that Skyrim's leadership was supposedly never disputed on the field of battle in the following 3,000 years), this was not entirely true.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1S-12)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-COF-89)</sup> For brief periods, a single ruler managed to unite all of Skyrim, but the Nord character is one essentially of conflict, and the confederacies never lasted.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PGE3S-59)</sup> For example, in the late-First Era High King [Irthvyd](Lore_Irthvyd.md) of Solitude died without an heir. His senior advisor, [Temylda](Lore_Temylda.md) ascended the throne after duelling several jarls in single combat. Forty years later, Temylda's reign ended and a new leader was chosen out of bloodshed and violence. The warrior [Alldimar](Lore_Alldimar.md), known for his many skirmishes with the Reachmen, wrestled the title from an untested child heir using his years of knowledge in arcane magic and his great sense of warfare.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-SOSR-90)</sup>

When the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) was established in [1E 2704](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2704), Skyrim was either conquered or allied with [Reman I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_I) relatively quickly as troops from [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) were frequently sent into [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to pacify rebellious [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md).<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-EGTNBat M-91)</sup> Despite now being part of the Second Empire, Skyrim's retained their ancient system of leadership with Jarls, High Kings, and succession using the Crown of Verity.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-COF-89)</sup>

After the Second Empire fell, High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) was assassinated [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431) heralding a major change in Skyrim. Naturally, the throne would have been passed to the High King's heir, Jarl [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md) of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). However, tension arose between the east and west when Jarl [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of Solitude challenged her claim to leadership and called for a moot. Freydis was accepted by the Crown of Verity and named High Queen, though part of the Moot chose Svartr as High King. What followed was a decisive schism that split Skyrim in two, creating distinct Western and Eastern Kingdoms.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TCo F-92)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-MTCHKS-93)</sup>

The Kingdom of Eastern Skyrim briefly became part of a greater alliance called the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), the city of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) was sacked and Queen [Mabjaarn](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and her heir [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md) were slain, leaving the crown to Mabjaarn's son, [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md).<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-JTSK-94)</sup> In an unprecedented show of cooperation between races with a great deal of historical enmity, the Nords, Dunmer and Argonians fought together to encircle the Akaviri forces in [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls) and drove the invaders back into the sea. This allegiance forged in battle was ratified with treaties, and thus the Ebonheart Pact was born.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-UA-95)</sup>

Eastern Skyrim fought in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) as a member of the Ebonheart Pact, while Western Skyrim remained neutral.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-TSAI-96)</sup> At that time, [The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) did not belong to either kingdom and the Reachmen had formed an independent nation under Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the so-called Despot of Markarth, who briefly allied himself with [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md).<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-97)</sup>

The two kingdoms of Skyrim, as well as The Reach, were eventually reunified into a single realm.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TAH-98)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-SRKibell-99)</sup>. In the last days of the Interregnum, a petty king of the Colovian Estates, Cuhlecain, came to power alongside the leader of his legions, General Talos. A year later more than half of the Cyrodilic Empire was reclaimed or consolidated, and Cuhlecain captured the Imperial City and proclaim himself Emperor. Skyrim and High Rock, which bitterly opposed a return to Cyrodilic rule, gathered their armies for a joint invasion of the Colovian West. Talos met them on the field of Sancre Tor. The Nords that had come to oppose the Empire soon joined the General's forces, for when they heard his Thu'um they believed him to be a son of Skyrim and the heir to the Empires of Men. Cuhlecain was soon assassinated soon after and [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) rose to power, eventually conquering all of Tamriel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1S-12)</sup>

As with the Second Empire, Skyrim retained many its ancient customs of leadership as a province under Tiber Septim, and was considered the Empire's "strong arm".<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-SR-100)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PGE3S-59)</sup> Traditionally each Hold was ruled by a Jarl who in turn were ruled by the High King, but ultimately the High King swore fealty to the Emperor. [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) became the city most directly influenced by [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) culture and politics, thus it became the capital city and its Jarl served as the Imperial-sanctioned High King for centuries thereafter.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-SR-100)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-SRThaer-101)</sup> Indeed, many of Solitude's Jarls were connected to the Septim bloodline, such as [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), the "Wolf Queen of Solitude".<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-SRLoading-102)</sup> As such, the Moot (and by extension the Crown of Verity) became formality and theater as opposed to official succession, leading some to view Skyrim's ruler as a puppet.

At times, some hold capitals were sometimes referred to as kingdoms in their own right, and their Jarls referred to as kings or queens, particularly by outsiders.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-BWQ-103)</sup><sup>[[99]](#cite_note-SRTaurinus-104)</sup> For example, the city of Solitude was oft referred to as the Kingdom of Solitude during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), or Queen [Macalla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Macalla) of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), who married King [Thian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thian) of Solitude.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PGE3S-59)</sup>

In [4E 10](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_10) the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md) began, resulting much unrest. After seven long, bloody years the Stormcrown Interregnum was ended when a Colovian warlord by the name of [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede.md) seized the crown in [4E 168](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_168). He proved a shrewd and capable leader, such that Skyrim endorsed him as Emperor. Although the Empire was greatly weakened, Skyrim was one of the few provinces that remained prosperous and peaceful.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-RT4-105)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-TGW-106)</sup>

That changed during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), when Reachmen briefly seized [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and most of the Reach in [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174) in an uprising, however a militia led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) retook the hold two years later.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-TBOM-107)</sup> However this would pale in comparison to the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) - an event that drastically shifted in the political landscape and plunged Skyrim into another civil war. Jarl [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), seeking independence from Imperial rule, killed High King [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) resulting in the province being split two. One side vying for independence and a return to the old ways, and the other seeking to remain under the rule of the Empire.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-NA-108)</sup>

## Kingdoms of High Rock
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/LO-map-High_Rock_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-High_Rock_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-High_Rock_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-High_Rock_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) High Rock **[1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498) — Present** *Main article: [Kingdoms of High Rock](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md)*

After the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) was defeated at the [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md), [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) power in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) began to wane. Year-after-year the Direnni could no longer challenge the emerging [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobility, who seized power throughout most of High Rock by [1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498).<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-109)</sup><sup>[[105]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-110)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-TBMOP-111)</sup> Clan Direnni's retreat to [Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balfiera) left a vacancy in the way of a united government, and High Rock was left with a hundred fiefdoms to squabble over power.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-110)</sup>

The rise of kingdoms initially started through villages founded in tactical locations, such as [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), prospering and becoming fully fledged powers.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-WJOTB-112)</sup>. Breton kingdoms grew around the eight major cities of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn_(kingdom).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md) and [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md).<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-EWR-113)</sup> These kingdoms gave rise to many dynastic families and houses, such as the Cumberland Dynasty, [Gardner Dynasty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gardner) and [Spenard Dynasty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Spenard).

## Reachman Kingdoms
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/SR-place-The_Reach.jpg/200px-SR-place-The_Reach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Reach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-The_Reach.jpg) The Reach **[1E 1030](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1030) — Various** *"Anyone can be a king in the Reach. No one can be a king of the Reach." — [Reachman proverb](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Lady_Belain)* Throughout history, [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) have lived in separate clans across [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). On occasion the clans have put aside their differences and banded together to form a loose kingdom, usually to unite against a common foe. The first recorded instance was around [1E 1030](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1030), when the Reach was ruled by ten warring Reachman kings. Most of the kings submitted to the invading [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) forces of Empress [Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra); however, a legendary Reachmen known as [Red Eagle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Eagle) united the clans and led a rebellion. The King of the Reach pushed back against the invaders for two years and was hailed a hero, but the Alessians ultimately were successful and killed the rebel king.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Lo RE-114)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-TRC-115)</sup>

Following the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md)'s collapse in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), the Reach was left to its own devices. It eventually became an independent kingdom ruled by the Reachmen themselves under the infamous [Durcorach the Black Drake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach), who conquered the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) and installed himself as the first of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-COTFC4-116)</sup> Durcorach met his demise during a failed invasion of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542).<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-TATOAM-117)</sup>

Following the overthrow of the Longhouse Emperors in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), the Reach returned to independence and Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the so-called Despot of Markarth, emerged as the most powerful figure in the region.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-EGTNBat M-91)</sup> Amid the chaos of both the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) and the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), numerous Reachman tribes attempted to extend their influence into High Rock and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), but met fierce resistance.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ESO-118)</sup> Shortly before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), The Kingdom of Western Skyrim carved a new hold named [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md) from the northern territory of the Reach to use as a bulwark against Reachman incursions into [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md) and [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md).<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Gt WSK-119)</sup>

In [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174), disgruntled Reachmen took advantage of the forces of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) fighting the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md). The Reachmen rose up, seizing [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and most of the hold, ruling the Reach as an independent kingdom for two years under [Madanach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Madanach), the King in Rags. The government of Skyrim, growing desperate, enlisted a militia led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) which retook the Reach in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176). The survivors of the revolt were scattered into the wilderness, becoming the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md).<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-TBOM-107)</sup>

The history of the Second Empire is divided into two main periods: the reign of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) that witnessed the final centuries of the First Era, and the subsequent [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), sometimes stylized as the **Akaviri Empire**, that ushered in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

## Elsweyr Confederacy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/LO-map-Elsweyr_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Elsweyr_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Elsweyr_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Elsweyr_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) The Elsweyr Confederacy **[2E 309](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_309) — [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115)** *Main article: [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md)* The [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md) was formed when the warring [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) kingdoms of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) and [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) merged in [2E 309](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_309). Aware of the political impact, [Keirgo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keirgo) of Anequina and [Eshita](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eshita) of Pellitine married and combined the two kingdoms into the nation of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). The formation of the new kingdom did not bring the expected peace. The cleft between tribal culture of the north and nobility of the south was still present. The power between the two former kingdoms, each with their own central government and allied tribes, shifted to the nobility. The chieftains of the north felt betrayed by both their ruling classes and forgot about their vendettas only to sign treaties of their own. Soon the cities of former Anequina were under constant attack, leaving King Keirgo no choice than to petition to the Second Empire for aid. The Empire itself was in similar unrest after their own ruler, [Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie), was assassinated in his palace in Senchal in [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324). As [Ne Quin-al](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ne_Quin-al), the old capital, fell to the rebels, the young confederation seemed ready to break apart.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg/200px-ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg) [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance) of [Rawl'kha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawl%27kha) As the rebellion continued, [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta), the nonpartisan spiritual leader of the Khajiit, revealed the [Riddle'Thar Epiphany](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar) in the [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance) of [Rawl'kha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawl%27kha) in [2E 311](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_311). The Mane used Riddle'Thar to proclaim a new system of dividing the powers in Elsweyr. Bound to the dance of the two moons—their different phases—the power in a region shifted between the nobility of the cities and the chieftains of the deserts. The Mane itself was overseeing this shift of powers, and as this system was implemented it finally brought peace to Elsweyr some time around or after [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324) —lasting under many more Manes. The edicts from this key event in Khajiiti theology however were not accepted by everyone at this time, leading to those exiled and their temples deconsecrated.

Two hundred years later during the chaos of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) ravaged the land of the Khajiit. As the Khajiit were focused on fighting against their own extinction, they saw aggression from the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) and [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md). Colovian armies, during the reign of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), invaded northern Elsweyr and annexed it. Their invasion went as far as [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) and [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md), until they were pushed out around [2E 562](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_562). This didn't stop the Colovians, and in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), [Euraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxia_Tharn) became Queen of Rimmen after staging the [Frostfall Coup](Lore_Frostfall_Coup.md). The occupation was short-lived after Euraxia was killed and Rimmen was brought back under the control of the Khajiit in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), although the Elsweyr Confederacy was left in shambles.

Knahaten Flu continued to spread across Elsweyr until aid came unexpectedly from the Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) and the [Kingdom of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md), bringing direly needed physicians, healers, and supplies. This aid gradually defeated the Knahaten Flu and saved the Khajiit. In return for, Queen Ayrenn asked the Khajiit to stand by her side as invading armies moved towards southwest Tamriel, to which the Elsweyr Confederacy agreed. The signing of the [Elden Accord](Lore_Elden_Accord.md) by Queen Ayrenn, [Lord Gharesh-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Lord_Gharesh-ri) and King [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran) formed the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580). Under the united banner of the Dominion, they entered the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) The Elsweyr Confederacy contended with numerous other threats during the Three Banners War. Colovians once again invaded northern Elsweyr under [Javad Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Javad_Tharn) and worked together with the Stonefire Cult to corrupt the current Mane [Akkhuz-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Akkhuz-ri) and anoint his own Dark Mane, thus corrupting the spiritual soul of the Khajiit. The conflict came to a head in the city of [Dune](Lore_Dune.md), where Javed launched an attack by opening Daedric portals and allowing Daedra into the streets. The Khajiit fought back and his plan failed, ending the Daedric threat.

In an effort to stop the Three Banners War, [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) inadvertently unleashed the Dragons [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid), [Laatvulon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laatvulon) and their minions from their containment from the Halls of Colossus. Kaalgrontiid continued pursuing the [Prophecy of the New Moon](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_New_Moon.md) that would see him become the Dark Aeon and ascending to godhood. A Khajiiti dragon cult, known as the [New Moon Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Moon_Cult) appeared around this time to aid Kaalgrontiid's schemes. Queen [Khamira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khamira) and the recently reformed Dragonguard forged an alliance with the red dragon [Nahfahlaar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nahfahlaar). Through their combined effort, the eventually went on to defeat Laatvulon and later Kaalgrontiid.

Although Rimmen briefly seceded from the Elswey Confederacy in [2E 812](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_812), the ascending [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) began conquering parts of Anequina during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). In the Halls of Colossus, Tiber Septim reconstructed and reactivated the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md). Not long after, Senchal was besieged by [Imperial](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) troops under General Pottreid. The population did not surrender and was slain entirely. Ultimately, Elsweyr was annexed to become a province of Tiber Septim's [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).

While Elsweyr thrived under the Empire for most of the Third Era, in [4E 10](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_10) the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md) began - only ten years after the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). Elsweyr once again found itself independent of the Empire, but was granted no peace. In or around [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48) the Mane was assassinated, sinking the south of Elsweyr into chaos. During this time, an unknown figure proclaimed himself Potentate of Rimmen and surrounding areas. In [4E 98](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_98), Masser and Secunda vanished from the sky for two entire years. The so-called [Void Nights](Lore_Void_Nights.md) ended in [4E 100](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_100) with the return of the moons, while the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) took credit for bringing them back. Whether or not they were actually responsible for this turnaround remains unresolved. Nevertheless, as the Khajiit people and culture are tied inexorably close to the moons, the Thalmor were able to win great influence over them as their apparent saviors. Within the following fifteen years, until [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115), the influence of the Empire dwindled so much it was unable to stop the coup which dissolved the Elsweyr Confederacy and recreated the original kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine as client states of the [Third Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).

## Empire of Cyrodiil
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/ON-map-Tamriel.jpg/200px-ON-map-Tamriel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Tamriel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Tamriel.jpg) The Empire of Cyrodiil (green) surrounded by the alliance powers **[2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430) — [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854)** *Main article: [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md)* The Empire of Cyrodiil (also known as the Imperial Empire) refers the remnants of Second Empire that existed during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Although identified as an empire, it differed from the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), and [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in that its only permanent dependency was [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) itself, and it was at no time ruled by a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) emperor. While there were periods of relative stability, such as under the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), the Ruby Throne saw many pretender-kings. Of note was the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) where armies of the Dominion, Covenant, and Pact invaded Cyrodiil in an effort to topple the throne and take power.

## Longhouse Dynasty
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation_Longhouse_Sigil.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation_Longhouse_Sigil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation_Longhouse_Sigil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation_Longhouse_Sigil.jpg) A sigil associated with the Longhouse Emperors **[2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533) — [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577)** *Main article: [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md)* The [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) were a dynasty of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) who seized the Ruby Throne and ruled the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) for a period of forty-four years during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Regarded as eccentric at best and utterly alien at worst by [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s populace, the Longhouse Emperors' reign began when the Reachman warlord [Durcorach the Black Drake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) and his horde of Reachmen conquered Cyrodiil in [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533), a feat believed to have been made possible with the aid of sympathetic [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) nobles and secret [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worshipers who struck a deal with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). In an effort to solidify his claim to the throne, Durcorach married [Veraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veraxia_Tharn) of the prominent [Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Tharn) family of [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) in [2E 534](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_534) and also reforged the membership of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md). Durcorach eventually met his end while leading an army of Reachmen in an invasion of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541); after sacking and besieging several cities, Durcorach's army was broken outside [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall), and the Black Drake himself was slain by [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland.

[Moricar the Middling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moricar), Durcorach's son, succeeded him at the age of twenty three. Moricar had been born and raised in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) like his father, but had spent his teenage years being educated by Imperial tutors and was said to better understand Cyrodilic culture. In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), Moricar launched an invasion of Western Skyrim. Accounts of the campaign differ. In one, Moricar remained in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) rather than lead the army as his father would have. This decision likely spared his life, as his forces were humiliatingly routed in a single battle by the army of High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md), but he was consequently lambasted as a coward by Western Skyrim's bards. In another account, Moricar led his troops to the gates of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) before Svargrim's army attacked in overwhelming numbers. Moricar was gravely wounded in the battle and returned to Cyrodiil, where, despite the best efforts of Imperial and Reach healers, he died the following year.

In [2E 564](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_564), Moricar's son [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic) inherited the Ruby Throne. Like his grandfather, Leovic married a Tharn, [Clivia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn). Leovic's reign was initially only marked by his peculiar behaviors, which the Cyrodilic nobility blamed on his Reachman upbringing. However, in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), Leovic crossed the line when he legalized [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship, allegedly at the behest of corrupt advisors, drawing the ire of many nobles who had once been counted among his supporters. One of these erstwhile supporters, Duke [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol), marshaled the armies of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) and led them against Leovic in open rebellion from 2E 576 to [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577). In 2E 577, Leovic was killed by Varen in the Imperial Throne Room, bringing an end to the period of the Longhouse Emperors. Durcorach's line was survived by his kin in the Reach, the [Blackdrake Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackdrake_Clan), and after Leovic's demise, the clan's chieftainship was claimed by [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), a cousin who had been installed as the Imperial Governor of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) by Moricar.

## Kingdom of Hammerfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Hammerfell_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Hammerfell_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Hammerfall **[2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430) — Unknown** *Main article: [Kingdom of Hammerfell](Lore_Kingdom_of_Hammerfell.md)* The [Kingdom of Hammerfell](Lore_Kingdom_of_Hammerfell.md) that emerged during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) can be considered the successor to the [Yokudan Empire](Lore_Yokudan_Empire.md), given the [Na-Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Na-Totambu) ruling class took power and reverted [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) back to a hereditary monarchy. Before being absorbed into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), Hammerfell was ruled by the [Phyllocid Dynasty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phyllocid_Dynasty). King Ar-Azal was best remembered for healing the rift between the [Na-Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Na-Totambu) ruling class and the warriors of the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) by marrying the daughters of their greatest Grandees.

Instead, the Na-Totambu and Ra Gada (now known as [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) respectively) would go on to aid [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s administration of Hammerfell. The Crowns retained the rights of noble council and the Forebears were granted rights of ownership within their tribal districts. High-ranking nobles were called Magnifico and Magnifica, depending on gender. Similarly, Grandee and Grandeya were titles associated with various roles, including provincial governance, leading armed forces or members of the court.

### Interregnum
After the fall of the Second Empire in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), control of Hammerfell reverted back to the hereditary monarchy of the Na-Totambu, giving birth to the various kingdoms of Hammerfell. The new High King, in a bold move, shifted the capital from Hegathe to the more prosperous Forebear city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), which had, by this time, mastered a third of the trade of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/ON-place-Sentinel.jpg/200px-ON-place-Sentinel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sentinel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sentinel.jpg) Sentinel In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), King [Ramzi the Distrait](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ramzi_the_Distrait) ruled the Kingdom of Sentinel, but was self-serving and weak-willed and failed to protect his subjects from the Knahaten Flu. As death spread to his courtiers, Ramzi sealed himself within [Samuruik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Samuruik) palace and slowly starved to death, leaving the kingdom in despair. A young Forebear noble named [Fahara'jad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fahara%27jad), descendant of Ar-Azal, traveled from Bergama and declared himself king of Hammerfell. Support for the new king wasn't unanimous and Hammerfell wasn't unified under Fahara'jad. The Crown nobility in the southern cities of [Hegathe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hegathe), [Gilane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gilane), [Taneth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Taneth) and [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) supported [Grandee Zizzeen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zizzeen)'s claim to throne instead. The Crowns suspected King Ramzi was usurped and acted with vehement repudiation against Fahara'jad, however Forebear viziers and northern chieftains ratified his accession.

Around this time, the Forebears were progressives who firmly support the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) while the traditionalists Crowns rejected the practices of Tamriel and clung to their old ways. Possibly sensing an uprising from the south, Fahara'jad allied himself with the Daggerfall Covenant to strengthen his position and pronounced himself High King of all Hammerfell, and even arranged the marriage of his daughter to High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). This action further infuriated the Crown nobility in the south, such as the royal family of Hegathe, as they openly opposed Fahara'jad's accession. Despite being unable to challenge his claims, they secretly plotted against and spied on Fahara'jad's kingdom. While Fahara'jad ruled Sentinel, the Alik'r and beyond, its unknown if Hammerfell's other kingdoms, such as the Kingdom of Taneth, recognized him as High King.

### The Tiber War
The line of High Kings that ruled from Sentinel continued with [Thassad II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thassad_II), but the city had switched to the rule of the Crowns. As [the conquests](Lore_Tiber_War.md) of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) began to expand and enroach on his domain, Thassad II resisted all forms of Imperial invasion. When the High King passed away in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862) the capital was retaken by the Forebears under the command of Baron [Volag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volag), ensuing a vicious civil war between the Crowns and Forebears. When the latter enlisted the help of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the Forebears gained the advantage and resulting in the Crowns losing their territories until [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) was left. The [Battle of Stros M'Kai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Stros_M%27Kai) was won and the Empire swiftly took control over all of Hammerfell, imposing several provisional governors to rule its regions. Both factions reconciled a year later after an uprising against the Empire, which forced Tiber Septim to sign a more favorable treaty that formally established the Imperial Province of Hammerfell. Sentinel, however, remained as its provincial capital. It is unknown when the next High King ascended to the throne after Hammerfell was absorbed into the Empire, but the rift in Hammerfell society was not mended.

### Third and Fourth Eras
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) erupted in [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396). [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) defeated the forces of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and High Rock and claimed many miles of the [eastern parts of those provinces](Lore_Western_Reach.md), including the cities of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md). Some of these territories were claimed to have been lost in previous wars at the expense of Skyrim. By [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403) [King Camaron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camaron) of Sentinel was in power during the province's [short but vicious land dispute](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) with [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) over the island of [Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony). Following the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), Sentinel under [King Lhotun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lhotun) greatly expanded to take over the entire northern coast of Hammerfell.

Centuries later, the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) would attempt to seize control of Hammerfell from the Third Empire. Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md)'s refusal to concede to Thalmor demands triggered the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171). Aldmeri forces crossed western Cyrodiil and invaded Hammerfell's southern coast, easily sweeping through the scattered resistance put up by the disunited Redguards. Initially only seeking to conquer Hammerfell, the Dominion launched a full-scale invasion of Cyrodiil when it realized the Empire was weaker than previously believed. Imperial fortunes took a turn for the better in Hammerfell two years later, when a Forebear army from Sentinel broke the siege of the Crown city of Hegathe, leading to the reconciliation of the two factions. After the Empire's hard-won victory in the Battle of the Red Ring in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Empire and Dominion signed the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) and ended the war. Outraged that the terms of the treaty called for a large portion of southern Hammerfell to be turned over to the Dominion, however, the Redguards soundly rejected it. Titus II was forced to officially renounce Hammerfell as an Imperial province to preserve the peace, and fighting between the Aldmeri and Redguards continued. In [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), the Dominion agreed to the [Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_Second_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and finally withdrew from the region, leaving Hammerfell an independent albeit devastated nation.

## Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/LO-map-Tamriel_%28Oblivion_Codex_Compilation%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Tamriel_%28Oblivion_Codex_Compilation%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Tamriel_(Oblivion_Codex_Compilation).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Tamriel_(Oblivion_Codex_Compilation).jpg.md) The Third Empire encompassed all of Tamriel **[2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854) — Present** *Main article: [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)* The Third Empire (also called the Septim Empire) was declared by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) in [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854) after the chaos of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), and was forged through a decades-long conflict to unite [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) known as the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). It lasted throughout the whole of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) and two centuries into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), when it began to decline. By this time it was comprised of two dynasties, the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) and the [Mede Dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md). It was also ruled briefly under a Potentate during the Stormcrown Interregnum.

**See the [main article](Lore_Third_Empire.md) for more information.**

## Kingdom of Black Marsh
*Main article: [Kingdom of Black Marsh](Lore_Kingdom_of_Black_Marsh.md)* Almost nothing is known about the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) kingdom in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), also known as the **Kingdom of Argonia**.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-Teinaava-120)</sup>. Almost all mentions of the king (dating as far back as the mid-Second Era) involve the [Shadowscales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowscales), who were said to be ancient protectors of the royal family, the Argonian Royal Court and the king's personal assassins.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-SRVeezara-121)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-SRAOV-122)</sup><sup>[[118]](#cite_note-Antiquity-123)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-Scar-Tail-124)</sup> Conversely, some believe this is simple misinterpreted information, and that no such king has ruled since the pre- [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) days when Argonian civilization was at its height. The so-called "Scalded Throne" has supposedly been empty for centuries (if it existed at all), and the monarch's role as commander of the Shadowscales is said to be overstated.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-IWTAS-125)</sup> In the pre-Duskfall era, the priests of Sithis (also known as [Nisswo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nisswo) or Nothing-Talkers) ruled as kings and would often make sacrifices to Sithis.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ON-126)</sup>

Whatever the existence of an Argonian monarchy may be, by the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) a political group known as the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md) formed and were instrumental in defending Black Marsh from [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s invasion. The kingdom later seceded from the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) after the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) incited the Argonians to mount a massive uprising against southern Morrowind.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-RT4-105)</sup> The exact structure of the An-Xileel is unknown, but they were known to have had an Archwarden by the name of Qajalil in [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48),<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-TIC-127)</sup><sup>:30</sup> and were led by **The Organism**, the ruling council based in [Lilmoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lilmoth).<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-TIC-127)</sup><sup>:37</sup>

## Coral Kingdoms of Thras
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/LO-race-Sload.jpg/200px-LO-race-Sload.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Sload.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Sload.jpg) A Sload *Main article: [Thras](Lore_Thras.md)* Coral Kingdoms of Thras are a group of small kingdoms located on the Thrassian Archipelago, surrounded by the [Sea of Pearls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_of_Pearls).<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Mot SS-128)</sup> It was the source of the deadly [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) that swept [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200).<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-SH3-129)</sup> Home to the amphibious [Sload](Lore_Sload.md), the extent of territory the Coral Kingdoms controls is unknown. Over the centuries maps charted by spies reveal the number and size of islands has varied, suggesting they have a volatile kingdom which fluctuates its land mass.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> Some kingdoms, such as [Ul'vor Kus](Lore_Ul%27vor_Kus.md), are located undersea and are ruled by the mysterious [Sea Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Sload).<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Mot SS-128)</sup>

While the Sload organize themselves into kingdoms, their Sea Sloads brethren form small cabals of like-minded individuals who cooperate to attain some specific goal, such as the Abyssal Cabal in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). These cabals break up, reform, and reorganize as interests and necessity warrant.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Mot SS-128)</sup> The leadership of these Thrassian kingdoms is largely unknown, despite the various conflicts over the years. The only insight Tamrielic nations have into Thrassian cultural leadership is found in a damaged journal of a diplomat to Thras written prior to [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260). In it the diplomat describes a curious ritual they witnessed after gaining an audience in the submerged tower of an "Elder Distended One". This being is further described having an "impressively corpulent body and strangely pulsating head" that then disgorged an unknown material from three eyes that emerged from its belly, which was consumed eagerly by attendants.

Some scholars speculate the Sload may have been original inhabitants of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), which possibly explains their aggressive disposition towards [Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alinor). Though they never succeeded in reclaiming Summerset they have visited numerous horrors upon other nations, such as the Sack of [Skywatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skywatch), the [War of the Uvichil](Lore_War_of_the_Uvichil.md) and the infamous Thrassian Plague.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-SH3-129)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> The latter was severely punished by the [All-Flags Navy](Lore_All-Flags_Navy.md) when they sank the coral kingdoms into the sea in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260), however by [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) there were reports that Thras had risen again and that the Sload were seen in various parts of Tamriel.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-130)</sup>

## Tiger-Dragon's Empire
[Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(place).md) (meaning **Tiger-Dragon's Empire**), originally called **Po Tun**, is a nation of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). It is inhabited by a race of cat-folk also called the [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md). They are ruled by [Tosh Raka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tosh_Raka), the divine Tiger-Dragon who bears similarities to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). Ka Po' Tun has become a very great empire, stronger than [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(place).md) in all but naval battle. When the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) attempted to enslave the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), the black dragons fled to Po' Tun. A great war was waged, which left both the cat-folk and the snakes weak, and resulted in the death of the dragons. Since that time, the cat-folk have tried to become the dragons. Tosh Raka was the first to succeed, and renamed the nation to Ka Po' Tun. Ka Po' Tun plans to kill the Tsaesci and then invade [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).
