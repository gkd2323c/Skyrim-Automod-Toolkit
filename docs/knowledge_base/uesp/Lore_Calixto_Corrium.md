Calixto Corrium was a retired [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) adventurer that was active in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). For most of his life, he was accompanied by his sister and traveled all over. In the late days of his life, he became an infamous serial killer who stalked [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md)'s streets under the alias of **the Butcher**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BJ1-1)</sup>

## History
Sometime in the second century of the Fourth Era, the siblings Calixto and Lucilla Corrium inherited a large sum of money and decided to explore the world together. In their travels, they acquired many rare items. These included the Book of Fate, the Dancer's Flute, and [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s Soup Spoon. However, through unknown circumstances, Lucilla died and Calixto mourned greatly over her death.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRCalixto-2)</sup> He was determined to bring her back to life by any means necessary. His journey eventually brought him to the world of forbidden magic, and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where he spent time with the mages at the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md). He felt resentment towards them as they only studied established magics, and not something beyond normal convention, like [flesh magic](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md), which he took a particular interest in.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BJ1-1)</sup>

His quest took him southeast into Windhelm, where he felt it provided the necessary tools to conduct his research and experiments, namely the flesh and blood of the people.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BJ1-1)</sup> As a ploy to blend with the public, Calixto opened a store called Calixto's House of Curiosities, where he put on display the various trinkets he and his sister gathered over the years.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRCalixto-2)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), he was embedded among the locals and had set into motion a plan to prey on the young women of Windhelm. According to the local priestess of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), he used embalming tools from ancient Nordic times on their remains.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRHelgird-3)</sup>

Calixto had an identifiable pattern of killings related to his [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) rituals.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRWuunferth-4)</sup> Some of his first known victims were a woman named Isabella,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRAdonato-5)</sup> Fjotli of the Clan Cruel-Sea<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRNiranye-6)</sup> and Friga of the [Clan Shatter-Shield](Lore_Shatter-Shield_Clan.md). Hjerim, a manor in the Valunstrad District,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-7)</sup> had been Friga's home, and proved to be a useful base of operations once Calixto had her key. With the [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md) in his possession, he used its hidden backroom to conduct his experiments on a bloodied altar.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRBlood-8)</sup> With the city guard focused elsewhere, a few others followed the case, notably Viola Giordano, who kept with the story for months and put up various pamphlets to spread awareness,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SRViola-9)</sup> as well as the Jarl's Court Wizard, Wuunferth the Unliving.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRWuunferth-4)</sup> Calixto stored his journals and Viola's pamphlets in Hjerim's backroom.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRBlood-8)</sup>

Not long after his fourth victim, the barmaid Susanna the Wicked, an [adventurer](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) was employed by the steward to crack the case. Working with Viola Giordano, they snuck into Hjerim and found the Necromancer's Amulet in the backroom. Unbeknownst to his true identity, they showed Calixto the amulet, hoping he could identify it. He told them it was the Wheelstone, an heirloom symbol of power in Windhelm, and hinted at its ownership to the Court Wizard, Wuunferth the Unliving. Wuunferth, who was also investigating the Butcher, immediately recognized it as the Necromancer's Amulet and coordinated with the adventurer to catch the killer. Calixto was ultimately confronted in the Stone Quarter and brought down by the adventurer.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRBlood-8)</sup>
