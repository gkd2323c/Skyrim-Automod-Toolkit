# Quest Timing

## Quests to Start Immediately
These are quests with no hard level requirements, and no consequences that impact future events or other possible quests unless the impact is trivial or undesirable anyway, in which case it would be noted. If a quest has prerequisites, those must also qualify under these rules. Quests here also do not require any more than minor skill usage, because of the levelups incurred as a result, but keep in mind that quests that require outdoor travel may result in combat from random hostile encounters, possibly resulting in unwanted leveling. If the benefit comes from an intermediate part of the quest rather than as a reward for finishing the quest, that would be mentioned in the **Notes** section.

### Early-Game Items
These quests have little to no skill use and allow very easy access to items that are better than what can be found normally at the beginning of the game. They aren't as useful to players past the early game stage.

**[Greatsword for a Great Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword_for_a_Great_Man)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: [Balgruuf's Greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Balgruuf%27s_Greatsword) (temporarily)
- **Notes**: The delivery quest reward is only 20 gold. You will be given the sword at the start of the quest and may use it as long as you like. It is better than any other early equivalent item. Or you can just finish the quest to become a friend of [Proventus Avenicci](Skyrim_Proventus_Avenicci.md), to sweep all silverwares/books in [Dragonsreach](Skyrim_Dragonsreach.md) for early money.
- **Beginning Region**: [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md)

**[Take Up Arms](Skyrim_Take_Up_Arms.md)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: [Vilkas's Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vilkas%27s_Sword) (temporarily)
- **Notes**: After training with him, [Vilkas](Skyrim_Vilkas.md) asks you to take his sword to [Eorlund Gray-Mane](Skyrim_Eorlund_Gray-Mane.md) for sharpening. As is the case with Balgruuf's greatsword, you can use this weapon for however long you desire. Vilkas's sword is identical to a [Skyforge Steel Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skyforge_Steel_Sword) in stats and appearance, and a sword of similar quality is not normally obtainable until you start the Companions questline.
- **Beginning Region**: [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md)

**[Andurs' Arkay Amulet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Andurs%27_Arkay_Amulet)**

- **Skill Use**: Limited combat
- **Why**: [Andurs' Amulet of Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Andurs%27_Amulet_of_Arkay) (+10 [Health](Skyrim_Fortify_Health.md))
- **Notes**: The delivery quest reward is only 15 gold. There is no time limit on use of the amulet. A follower will easily handle the combat. The [unusual gem](Skyrim_Unusual_Gem.md) located in the catacombs will trigger the Thieves Guild quest [No Stone Unturned](Skyrim_No_Stone_Unturned.md), leading you to [A Chance Arrangement](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Chance_Arrangement) (below).
- **Beginning Region**: [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md)

**[Argonian Ale Extraction](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Argonian_Ale_Extraction)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: [Potion of Vigorous Healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Vigorous_Healing) ([Restore Health](Skyrim_Restore_Health.md) 100 points)
- **Notes**: If you wish to do this quest without stealing, chop some wood outside the inn and sell it to [Hulda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hulda). Her disposition will rise enough so that you can take low-value items, including the ale, from the inn without it being theft.
- **Beginning Region**: [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun_Hold.md)

**[Pilgrimage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pilgrimage)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: [Alessandra's Dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Alessandra%27s_Dagger) (temporarily)
- **Notes**: You will be given the dagger at the start of the quest and may use it as long as you like. You should delay delivering it until you've reached level 40 of [Restoration](Skyrim_Restoration.md), as a bug prevents you from receiving the quest rewards otherwise. It is very light and increases speed for [dual-wielding](Skyrim_Combat.md#Dual-Wielding).
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

**[The Taste of Death](Skyrim_The_Taste_of_Death.md)** (first part)

- **Skill Use**: One Speech use
- **Why**: [Amulet of Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Amulet_of_Arkay) (+10 [Health](Skyrim_Fortify_Health.md))
- **Beginning Region**: [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md)

### Guild Quests
The first quests for joining some of Skyrim's guilds offer some interesting benefits, so if the player has an interest in joining one, it might be worth to do it ASAP.

**[A Chance Arrangement](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Chance_Arrangement)**, **[Taking Care of Business](Skyrim_Taking_Care_of_Business.md)** and **[Meet the Family](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Meet_the_Family)**

- **Skill Use**: Normally moderate (lockpicking, 1 pickpocket, fighting bandits), but can be minimized, as noted on the quest pages
- **Why**: [Thieves Guild Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_Armor) (including a hood that is one of the few non-necklaces providing [Fortify Barter](Skyrim_Fortify_Barter.md)); access to a [fence](Skyrim_Fence_(merchant).md)
- **Why Not**: Takes some preparation to complete quests without skill usage.
- **Notes**: Takes place entirely in Riften (midpoint Innocence Lost).
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

**[Innocence Lost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Innocence_Lost)**, and **[With Friends Like These...](Skyrim_With_Friends_Like_These....md)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: [Shrouded Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shrouded_Armor), which is superior to even dragonscale at low skill levels if you equip all four pieces; a word for [Marked for Death](Skyrim_Marked_for_Death.md); several minor benefits. You can also obtain a partial set of [scaled armor](Skyrim_Scaled_Armor.md) off of [Fultheim the Fearless](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fultheim_the_Fearless) if you kill him, which has an even better base armor rating than the Shrouded Armor, and is not normally available to you until later in the game.
- **Why Not**: If you complete With Friends Like These... in favor of the Dark Brotherhood, [Destroy the Dark Brotherhood!](Skyrim_Destroy_the_Dark_Brotherhood!.md) will not be accessible. Although the latter provides the same immediate benefits, it is a rather difficult quest, and it removes the rest of the Dark Brotherhood questline and related rewards.
- **Beginning Region**: [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md)

**[First Lessons](Skyrim_First_Lessons.md)** and **[Under Saarthal](Skyrim_Under_Saarthal.md)**

- **Skill Use**: Two spellcastings
- **Why**: - An apprentice-level spell for 30 gold (First Lessons)
- Lesser Ward for free (First Lessons)
- An [unusual gem](Skyrim_Unusual_Gem.md) in the Arch-Mage's Quarters (after First Lessons)
- Access to the [Atronach Forge](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md) (after First Lessons)
- Three [Enchanted Rings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Enchanted_Ring) that give +20 [Health](Skyrim_Fortify_Health.md) (First part of Under Saarthal)
- One word of the [Ice Form](Skyrim_Ice_Form.md) shout (at the end of Under Saarthal)
- Access to several [miscellaneous quests](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(faction).md#Optional_Quests) that can be completed without any skill usage
- **Why not**: You need at least 135 [Magicka](Skyrim_Magicka.md). You may not be able to cast the spell required without [Fortify Magicka](Skyrim_Fortify_Magicka.md) apparel or potions, so come prepared.
- **Notes:** If you stop before entering [Saarthal](Skyrim_Saarthal.md) proper (second zone), a follower will easily handle the combat. No departing carriage, but fast travel to Winterhold.
- **Beginning Region**: [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold.md)

**[Tending the Flames](Skyrim_Tending_the_Flames.md)**

- **Difficulty**: Medium
- **Time**: Single dungeon and a bit lengthy event
- **Why**: "The Gift of Gab" grants speech skill increase 15% faster. Stop shifty merchant giving you a raw trade during early game as soon as possible.
- **Note**: Requires [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch).
- **Beginning Region**: [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md)

### Other Benefits
**[A Lovely Letter](Skyrim_A_Lovely_Letter.md)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: 25 gold, a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md)
- **Notes**: Potential [Archery](Skyrim_Archery.md) trainer. Takes place entirely in [Riverwood](Skyrim_Riverwood.md) (the first town discovered by most players).
- **Beginning Region**: [Riverwood](Skyrim_Riverwood.md)

**[The Book of Love](Skyrim_The_Book_of_Love.md)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: [Amulet of Mara](Skyrim_Amulet_of_Mara.md) ([marriage](Skyrim_Marriage.md), [Restoration](Skyrim_Restoration.md) spells cost 10% less magicka to cast), [Agent of Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Agent_of_Mara) (+15% [Resist Magic](Skyrim_Resist_Magic.md))
- **Why Not**: Need to visit four locations spread across Skyrim, two of which cannot be reached by carriage.
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

**[Bloody Nose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bloody_Nose)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: A borrowed [horse](Skyrim_Horse.md) and 100 gold
- **Notes**: Takes place entirely in Riften.
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

**[Stabilized](Skyrim_Stabilized.md)**

- **Skill Use**: Trivial
- **Why**: A borrowed [horse](Skyrim_Horse.md) and leveled [Potion of Invisibility](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Brief_Invisibility)
- **Notes**: Takes place entirely in Riften. Completing [Taking Care of Business](Skyrim_Taking_Care_of_Business.md) removes the need to spend any gold to complete this quest.
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

**[Few and Far Between](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Few_and_Far_Between)**

- **Time**: 10-15 minutes
- **Skill Use**: None (flower-picking)
- **Why**: Free potions and poisons every few days
- **Notes**: See quest page for easy locations of ingredients.
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

**[Gather Wheat](Skyrim_Gather_Wheat.md)** at [Katla's Farm](Skyrim_Katla%27s_Farm.md)

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: A borrowed [horse](Skyrim_Horse.md), several minor benefits
- **Beginning Region**: [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md)

**[The Mind of Madness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_Madness)**

- **Skill Use**: One Speech or Pickpocket use
- **Why**: [Wabbajack](Skyrim_Wabbajack.md)
- **Notes**: Takes place entirely in [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md).
- **Beginning Region**: [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md)

**[Salt for Arcadia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_for_Arcadia), [Bothela's Discreet Delivery](Skyrim_Bothela%27s_Discreet_Delivery.md), [Truth Ore Consequences](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Truth_Ore_Consequences), [No News is Good News](Skyrim_No_News_is_Good_News.md), and [Rare Gifts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rare_Gifts) for [Lami](Skyrim_Lami.md)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: Many of these quests will have rewards of their own, but the main reward - a reward that lasts the rest of the game - is the privilege to take their low-value items without stealing. These are the most valuable NPCs to obtain this status with, since they own apothecary stores. These supposedly "low-value" ingredients can be mixed into high-value potions that can be used to make a good amount of money. It is worth noting that, while [Frida](Skyrim_Frida.md) of [Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md) and [Nurelion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nurelion) of [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) both offer quests that provide this reward for *their* apothecary stores, these quests actually require some dungeon-delving.
- **Note**: After [Truth Ore Consequences](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Truth_Ore_Consequences) taking low-value items from [Elgrim's Elixirs](Skyrim_Elgrim%27s_Elixirs.md) without stealing requires the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch).
- **Beginning Region**: [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun_Hold.md), [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md), [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), [Morthal](Skyrim_Morthal.md) respectively

**[Coming of Age](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Coming_of_Age)**

- **Skill Use**: None
- **Why**: [Steel Battleaxe of Fiery Souls](Skyrim_Steel_Battleaxe_of_Fiery_Souls.md), which has a very useful enchantment to learn in the early game, and a word of the [Become Ethereal](Skyrim_Become_Ethereal.md) Shout.
- **Why Not**: [Salma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salma) may grab the aforementioned weapon during the fight before you can take it yourself.
- **Notes**: Although [Ironbind Barrow](Skyrim_Ironbind_Barrow.md) is full of draugr, you can simply let [Salma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salma) and [Beem-Ja](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Beem-Ja) rush ahead and do all the fighting. They won't die unless hit by friendly fire, so they can clear the entire dungeon for you while you tag along and gather all the loot. When you reach [Gathrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Warlord_Gathrik) let them and a follower distract him while you pelt him from range. By that point, Beem-Ja will be weakened from the boss fight, and can be killed easily once he betrays you. At low levels, Beem-Ja will not even use magic, so the earlier you do this quest, the better.
- **Beginning Region**: [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) near [Nightgate Inn](Skyrim_Nightgate_Inn.md)

**[The House of Horrors](Skyrim_The_House_of_Horrors.md)** (first part)

- **Skill Use**: Limited combat
- **Why**: [Apprentice Robes of Restoration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Apprentice_Robes_of_Restoration#Apprentice_Robes_of_Restoration) (Regen Magicka 75%, Fortify Restoration 15%), [Apprentice Hood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Apprentice_Hood#Apprentice_Hood) (+40 Magicka), [Amulet of Stendarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Amulet_of_Stendarr) (+10 [Block](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Block)), access to a free [house](Skyrim_Abandoned_House.md)
- **Notes**: A follower will easily handle the combat.
- **Beginning Region**: [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md)

**[That Was Always There](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:That_Was_Always_There)**

- **Skill Use**: One lockpick or pickpocket
- **Why**: Speech training from [Revyn Sadri](Skyrim_Revyn_Sadri.md)
- **Notes**: Revyn Sadri is the only [Speech](Skyrim_Speech.md) trainer who is also a general trader available at the beginning of the game. He can train you up to 50, at which point you can get the Merchant perk, which is very useful if you manage to get it early on. Once you have the perk, you can take advantage of every other trainer who is also a merchant of some kind, selling them loot and crafted items to get your gold back. This quest does require you to either pickpocket [Viola Giordano](Skyrim_Viola_Giordano.md)'s key, or pick the expert lock to her home, but it's not really that hard to pick expert locks at low skill levels. If you prefer the pickpocketing route, [Silda the Unseen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silda_the_Unseen), the expert trainer in Pickpocket, also lives in Windhelm.
- **Beginning Region**: [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md)

## Quests to Do as Soon as Possible
This section is for quests that do not qualify for "to do immediately" because they have a level requirement, or a nontrivial impact on the game (or a prerequisite quest does), or skill usage requirements that will force levelups, but once you reach a certain point, there is a benefit to completing these quickly.

Most **[Daedric quests](Skyrim_Daedric_Quests.md)** fall into this category, since they all reward you with unleveled items. However, most of those items are useless to too many characters to merit mention here.

All of these quests require a significant amount of combat or other skill use that will result in levelups (potentially several, at low levels).

**[Siddgeir's Rare Gift](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rare_Gifts), [Kill the Bandit Leader (Jarl)](Skyrim_Kill_the_Bandit_Leader_(Jarl).md), and [Thane of Falkreath](Skyrim_Thane_of_Falkreath.md)**

- **Skill Use**: Moderate
- **Why**: [Thane](Skyrim_Thane.md) of Falkreath. Also Siddgeir's Rare Gift becomes unavailable at level 9.
- **Notes**: The reason this should be done as soon as possible is because there are only a handful of citizens in the hold who have favors you can do for them, many of whom are not essential. Falkreath is especially vulnerable to random dragon attacks, due to its close proximity to [Ancient's Ascent](Skyrim_Ancient%27s_Ascent.md). [Morthal](Skyrim_Morthal.md) gets its fair share of random dragon attacks, too, but A) Becoming Thane of that hold requires a [potentially deadly struggle with a vampire](Skyrim_Laid_to_Rest.md), and B) there are plenty of quest NPCs in that hold (many of whom spend a majority of time indoors, safe from dragon attacks) that the loss of a few of them won't hurt as much as in Falkreath. There is a bottle of [Black-Briar Mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Black-Briar_Mead) at the tail end of the tutorial dungeon, just beyond the spot where your partner crouches to warn you about the bear, just in front of the partner to his right. This bottle can be used to quickly and easily complete the first of these three quests.
- **Beginning Region**: [Falkreath](Skyrim_Falkreath.md)

**[Blood on the Ice](Skyrim_Blood_on_the_Ice.md)**

- **When**: Special travel timing. See [Blood on the Ice notes](Skyrim_Blood_on_the_Ice.md#Notes) for details.
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Time**: Around 10 minutes, depending on your preferred style of play
- **Why**: The [Necromancer Amulet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancer_Amulet) is a legendary-quality item very useful for low-level spellcasting characters, especially those interested in Conjuration, and can be sold for a lot of gold for a low-level character. There are numerous glitches associated with this quest, and the best fix is to start the quest as soon as possible and complete it all at once. Additionally, during [Mourning Never Comes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mourning_Never_Comes), you are given the option to kill [Nilsine Shatter Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nilsine_Shatter-Shield) for a bonus. Doing this will cause her mother to kill herself. This can cause a fair amount of problems with quest progression, so it's best to complete Blood on the Ice first.
- **Notes**: Assuming you have previously arrived in [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) completing the [Innocence Lost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Innocence_Lost) quest, the next time you arrive in the evening will likely start this quest.
- **Beginning Region**: [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md)

**[Paid in Full](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Paid_in_Full)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>**

- **When**: After the quest [Taking Care of Business](Skyrim_Taking_Care_of_Business.md).
- **Difficulty**: Easy
- **Time**: Around 10 minutes, depending on your preferred style of play
- **Why**: Access to the hidden room in [Glover Mallory's](Skyrim_Glover_Mallory.md) house, which is filled with valuable respawning enchanted gear.
- **Notes**: Little combat and skill usage is involved, and you may travel to [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md) as early as level one if you pay 250 gold to sail on the [Northern Maiden](Skyrim_Northern_Maiden.md). This cost is easily offset by the [Blackguard's Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Blackguard%27s_Armor#Blackguard.27s_Armor) set, which is more powerful than the Thieves Guild Guild Master's complete armor set and has stronger enchantments.
- **Beginning Region**: [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md)

**[A Night To Remember](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Night_To_Remember)**

- **When**: Level 14
- **Difficulty**: Easy. With speech skill of 50 and two invisibility potions no fighting is required.
- **Why**: The Daedric artifact the [Sanguine Rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine_Rose) is a very powerful staff for a player or follower.
- **Why Not**: Choosing to fight and/or buy one's way through this quest can be quite challenging at low levels.
- **Notes**: The speech checks required for peaceful and/or inexpensive progress can be achieved using skill boosting potions, shrines and blessings.
- **Beginning Region**: any tavern

**[The Break of Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Break_of_Dawn)**

- **When**: Level 12 (required), 24 (suggested) or 40 (recommended)
- **Why**: If you favor one-handed weapons or have a follower who does, Dawnbreaker's unique enchantment is exceptionally powerful and can turn difficult undead areas into cakewalks.
- **Why Not**: [Kilkreath Ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kilkreath_Ruins) have minimum level 24. The quest's "enemy boss" can be extremely difficult to beat, but has a maximum level 40. You may want to wait a few levels, or even longer, if you don't really have a use for the sword. If you are level 38 or higher, the creatures inside the dungeon will have [grand souls](Skyrim_Souls.md), making them extremely useful for [Enchanting](Skyrim_Enchanting.md).

**[The Forsworn Conspiracy](Skyrim_The_Forsworn_Conspiracy.md) and [No One Escapes Cidhna Mine](Skyrim_No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine.md)**

- **Difficulty**: Easy/Medium
- **Time**: Around 20 minutes, depending on your preferred style of play
- **Why**: [Armor of the Old Gods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Armor_of_the_Old_Gods) and/or [Silver-Blood Family Ring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silver-Blood_Family_Ring), also can make some [Forsworn](Skyrim_Forsworn.md) non-hostile depending on how you complete the quests.
- **Notes**: These quests do not qualify for "to do early" because of their skill use demands. Assuming you arrive in [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md) on [The Book of Love](Skyrim_The_Book_of_Love.md) quest, the timing will be about right for some minor skill usage without excessive level raising. Only the first two investigative steps of Forsworn Conspiracy should be done initially, and the remainder delayed until conditions are ripe. Note that if you side with the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md) in the [Civil War](Skyrim_Civil_War.md), if you complete Forsworn Conspiracy before [Compelling Tribute](Skyrim_Compelling_Tribute_(Stormcloaks).md) and then wait until after Compelling Tribute to start Cidhna Mine, it will become unavailable permanently. It is also possible to have the same effect result from completing Forsworn Conspiracy, then completing [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md) before Cidhna Mine, even if you have not joined the Stormcloaks.

**[Take Up Arms](Skyrim_Take_Up_Arms.md)** and **[Proving Honor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Proving_Honor)**

- **Difficulty**: Medium
- **Time**: Around 30 minutes, depending on your preferred style of play
- **Why**: After completing both these quests you are given a Skyforge steel weapon (which are equivalent to elven weapons but lighter), and you can buy more of them if you wish. Also note that Farkas is badly bugged, so if you leave it for too long it will become much harder to complete until you reach very high levels.

**[Frostflow Abyss](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frostflow_Abyss)**

- **When**: Level 20
- **Why**: 10% bonus to healing spells.
- **Why Not**: This quest may be difficult for some characters due to the large number of [Falmer](Skyrim_Falmer.md) and [chaurus](Skyrim_Chaurus.md), and is of no benefit if you rely on potions for all your healing.

**[The Heart of Dibella](Skyrim_The_Heart_of_Dibella.md)**

- **Difficulty**: Medium
- **Time**: Around 30 minutes, depending on your preferred style of play
- **Why**: 10% bonus melee damage to the opposite sex, access to the master-level trainer in Enchanting.
- **Notes**: This quest does not qualify for "to do early" because of its skill use demands, and it also has a major bug that can break it. It's certainly worth completing as soon as you're comfortable doing so though, especially for female characters.
- **Beginning Region**: [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md)

**[Ill Met by Moonlight](Skyrim_Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.md)**

- **When**: Before contracting lycanthropy.
- **Difficulty**: Easy/Trivial
- **Time**: Around 15 minutes
- **Why**: The Cursed Ring only affects [werewolves](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md), so getting this out of the way before that's relevant means not having it trigger at random and getting you a massive bounty or even killed. Sinding will easily handle all of the killing for you, especially at low levels. However, if you choose to kill Sinding, he is a difficult fight.
- **Notes**: Assuming you arrive in [Falkreath](Skyrim_Falkreath.md) during the [With Friends Like These...](Skyrim_With_Friends_Like_These....md) quest, the timing will be about right for some minor skill usage without excessive level raising.
- **Beginning Region**: [Falkreath](Skyrim_Falkreath.md)

**[Lost to the Ages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lost_to_the_Ages)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>**

- **When**: As soon as possible. See [Aetherial Crown](Skyrim_Aetherial_Crown.md) for more details.
- **Difficulty**: Medium/Hard
- **Time**: Around 2 hours, depending on your preferred style of play
- **Why**: The unique bow [Zephyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Zephyr#Zephyr) and the [Aetherial Crown](Skyrim_Aetherial_Crown.md). The Aetherial Crown lets you combine two [Standing Stones](Skyrim_Standing_Stone.md) at once, leading to powerful combinations such as [The Apprentice Stone](Skyrim_The_Apprentice_Stone.md) and [The Atronach Stone](Skyrim_The_Atronach_Stone.md), or faster leveling such as [The Lover Stone](Skyrim_The_Lover_Stone.md) (+15% XP for all skills) and one of [The Guardian Stones](Skyrim_The_Guardian_Stones.md) (+20% XP for magic, stealth or combat skills).
- **Why Not**: Killing Alain Dufont before starting [Mourning Never Comes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mourning_Never_Comes) can limit options for Mourning Never Comes (due to [conflicts](#Conflicts))
- **Notes**: A fair amount of travel, combat, and skill usage is involved in this quest, making it difficult to avoid leveling.

**[No Stone Unturned](Skyrim_No_Stone_Unturned.md)**

- **Difficulty**: Easy/Medium
- **Time**: A while, but worth it!
- **Why**: The reward you receive at the end of a long chain of quests, [Prowler's Profit](Skyrim_Prowler%27s_Profit.md), will make your character rich, quickly and fairly!
- **Why Not**: Thieving may not appeal to some players and finding all 24 gems takes a very long time (you will need to traverse many locales and clear many of them, unless your sneaking is good). Also one of them in [Thalmor Embassy](Skyrim_Thalmor_Embassy.md) is not accessible without finishing [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md). This issue is less of a problem after patch 1.4 for the original version of the game, since that update moves the gem to the unmarked Reeking Cave below the Embassy.

**[Promises to Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Promises_to_Keep)**

- **Difficulty**: Medium
- **Time**: About 20 minutes
- **Why**: A free [horse](Skyrim_Horse.md)
- **Why Not**: Requires fairly good combat or stealth skills.
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

**[Unfathomable Depths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unfathomable_Depths)**

- **When**: Level 15
- **Why**: Smithing increases 15% faster; permanent 25% increase to armor rating while wearing Dwarven armor.
- **Why Not**: Not much value to characters that wear no armor, or those who don't use Smithing.
- **Note**: Ancient Knowledge actually gives a 25% bonus while wearing *any* type of armor *except* Dwarven and increases effective Smithing skill by 15% when tempering weapons and armor instead of giving a Smithing leveling bonus of 15%. This bug was supposed to be fixed by the final version of the [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), but was not. The armor bonuses it actually gives are therefore useful for a wide variety of characters.
- **Beginning Region**: [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md)

## Quests to Save for Later
This section is for quests that have unique leveled rewards with genuine value, or sweeping impact on the world or player.

**[Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md)**

- **Why**: Completing this quest early ensures a large number of Dragon Souls by exploring, if you are capable of surviving the dragon's attacks.
- **Why Not**: After completing this quest, Skyrim will be full of dragons. Deferring it until you're in the mood to fight dragons lets you explore the world in relative peace and allows you to learn [Words](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) from [Dragon Lairs](Skyrim_Dragon_Lairs.md) more easily. Note that starting the quest will not cause dragons to appear; only after killing [Mirmulnir](Skyrim_Mirmulnir.md) will dragons start spawning.
- **Note**: While [A Blade in the Dark](Skyrim_A_Blade_in_the_Dark.md) is active dragon locations will be limited to dragon walls. Other than dragon walls, random dragon encounters are nearly eliminated while A Blade in the Dark is active allowing for controlled access to dragon fights.

**[Joining the Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Joining_the_Stormcloaks.md)** or **[Joining the Legion](Skyrim_Joining_the_Legion.md)**

- **Why Not**: Joining either the Stormcloaks or the Legion removes access to all the opposing faction's camps and causes "random encounter" fights to occur. Capturing towns has been known to break certain quests.

**[Bards College](Skyrim_Bards_College.md) miscellaneous quests, and [other quests that offer skill-ups as rewards](Skyrim_Free_Skill_Boosts.md)**

- **Why Not**: Early in the game, skill-ups will come easily enough as it is. You should turn in these quests when the corresponding skills are at late 80s. (Like master trainers, skill does not increase if it is greater than or equal to 90.)
- **Note**: Some radiant quests cannot be kept since they become unavailable when you move to another city. See the bug in [Miscellaneous Quests](Skyrim_Miscellaneous_Quests.md).

**[Pilgrimage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pilgrimage)**

- **When**: Start it immediately to enjoy the weightless dagger. However, turn the quest in when your restoration skill is at level 40 or higher.
- **Why Not**: A glitch in the quest causes you to not receive the quest reward if your restoration skill is less than 40.

**[The Rising Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Rising_Dead)**<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Plague_of_the_Dead.md)</sup>

- **When**: Level 11 (available from level 5)
- **Why**: Finishing this quest will grant access to [zombie](Skyrim_Zombie.md) conjuration spells and will cause [zombie hordes](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Zombie_Attack) to start appearing randomly across Skyrim. This is the only way to obtain [Mort Flesh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mort_Flesh).
- **Why Not**: The zombie hordes are potentially dangerous for lower level characters. The spell tome rewards from this quest are also leveled: you are guaranteed to get two copies of *[Spell Tome: Conjure Zombie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome__Conjure_Zombie)*, but to get a copy of *[Spell Tome: Conjure Foul Zombie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome__Conjure_Foul_Zombie)* you must be at least level 11.

**[The Silver Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Silver_Hand)**

- **Why Not**: This quest grants [Beast Form](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md#Beast_Form), which disables Rested bonus. Although it can be cured during quest line afterwards, it costs a unique item with limited number.

## Quests to Start but not Finish
These are typically quests that involve a temporary follower who is essential, allowing you to add an unkillable ally to your party. The best of these by far is [Barbas](Skyrim_Barbas.md) from [A Daedra's Best Friend](Skyrim_A_Daedra%27s_Best_Friend.md), who is uniquely invulnerable rather than merely unkillable, though his behavior can make him hard to tolerate and [the reward](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile) is more helpful in making money.

**[Darkness Returns](Skyrim_Darkness_Returns.md)**

- **Why**: Completing the previous quest [Blindsighted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Blindsighted), which automatically starts this quest, will gain you the [Skeleton Key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton_Key), an unbreakable lockpick. The powers you gain access to for completing this quest, which results in the loss of the Skeleton Key, can be easily acquired by other means.

**[The Taste of Death](Skyrim_The_Taste_of_Death.md)**

- **Why**: [Brother Verulus](Skyrim_Brother_Verulus.md) will follow you in addition to any other followers you may have.

**[Speaking With Silence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Speaking_With_Silence)**

- **Why**: [Mercer Frey](Skyrim_Mercer_Frey.md) is a fast, invincible killing machine who dual-wields two powerful weapons and has a variety of powerful combat skill perks, including [Dual Flurry](Skyrim_Dual_Flurry.md) and [Savage Strike](Skyrim_Savage_Strike.md).
- **Why Not**: Since Frey can't be told to wait, he can often give away your position when you're trying to be stealthy.

**[Trinity Restored](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Trinity_Restored)**

- **Why**: [Brynjolf](Skyrim_Brynjolf.md) is just as good as Mercer as a follower.
- **Why Not**: He has the same drawback as a follower that Mercer does.

**[Blindsighted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Blindsighted)**

- **Why**: Once you meet them after the initial portion of [Irkngthand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Irkngthand), both Brynjolf and Karliah - two powerful fighters in their own right - will follow you, even outside the dungeon.
- **Why Not**: Two followers who can't be told to wait means two times the risk when trying to be stealthy.

**[An Axe to Find](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:An_Axe_to_Find)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>**

- **Why**: A weightless [pickaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nordic_Pickaxe) you can mine with.

**[Bloodline](Skyrim_Bloodline.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>**

- **Why**: [Serana](Skyrim_Serana.md) will follow you in addition to any other followers you may have. Unlike other temporary followers, she can be told to wait.

## Quests that Conflict
This section is mainly for quests that "break" another quest (i.e. prevent it from being completed), and quests that stop you from fully completing another (e.g. preventing you from obtaining all possible rewards). Quests that merely "interrupt" each other (e.g. delay the completion of another) should only have such information on the affected pages.

The new quest system in Skyrim (known as "Radiant" quests) means that there are few quests that *don't* potentially interact with others (sometimes to your benefit, sometimes not) on top of all the typical quest collision issues in a sandbox game of this size. Quests that can be interrupted by radiant quests, usually by making you clear the dungeon that quest takes place in resulting in waiting for it to repopulate before the new quest can be completed, should have that possibility listed on their own page ([Purity of Revenge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Purity_of_Revenge) is a known example). Radiant quests can even do this to other radiant quests. However, radiant quests that break other quests should be listed here.

The [Civil War](Skyrim_Civil_War.md) questline has been known to cause problems with multiple quests all over the realm. You may want to leave them for as long as possible. The problems mainly occur when Jarls are replaced with their respective controlling-faction counterparts. One known conflict is [Search and Seizure](Skyrim_Search_and_Seizure.md) in Markarth. If the Stormcloaks take over the city then the quest giver is removed from the game. These problems can also arise because of [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md) (a main quest) when you exchange ownership of some holds (in this case [The Forsworn Conspiracy](Skyrim_The_Forsworn_Conspiracy.md) and [No One Escapes Cidhna Mine](Skyrim_No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine.md) have been known to be broken).

Start **[The Forgemaster's Fingers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Forgemaster%27s_Fingers)** before completing any of the 8 **[Blood-Kin](Skyrim_Blood-Kin.md)** quests.

- **Why**: You will be unable to obtain The Forgemaster's Fingers, granting the wielder a +12 bonus to [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md).

Start **[Kill the Bandit Leader](Skyrim_Kill_the_Bandit_Leader.md) ([Ahtar](Skyrim_Ahtar.md))** before **[Lights Out!](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lights_Out!)**

- **Why**: During Lights Out! you are required to trek through [Broken Oar Grotto](Skyrim_Broken_Oar_Grotto.md), where [Captain Hargar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Hargar) lives. If you kill him before starting Kill the Bandit Leader, you will never be able to start that quest and be unable to recruit a potential follower. - [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) fixes this problem by adding [Orphan's Tear](Skyrim_Orphan%27s_Tear.md) and [Ironback Hideout](Skyrim_Ironback_Hideout.md) as possible targets of Ahtar.

Do **[Harsh Master](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Harsh_Master)** before starting **[Summerset Shadows](Skyrim_Summerset_Shadows.md)**.

- **Why**: If the radiant location is [Uttering Hills Camp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Uttering_Hills_Camp), the bandit leader that you have to kill will be permanently replaced by [Linwe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Linwe) once "Summerset Shadows" starts, making "Harsh Master" uncompletable.

Do **[The Straw that Broke](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Straw_that_Broke)** before **[Kill Narfi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kill_Narfi)**

- **Why**: As soon as you start Kill Narfi, you will be unable to start or complete The Straw that Broke.

Do **[Build Your Own Home (Falkreath)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Build_Your_Own_Home)**<sup>[HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md)</sup> before **[Kill Helvard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kill_Helvard)**

- **Why**: Killing Helvard may make it impossible to have [Siddgeir](Skyrim_Siddgeir.md) like you enough to sell you a plot of land in Falkreath. This can be overcome if at any time the Stormcloaks take over and replace Siddgeir as jarl, otherwise you miss out on a house and some [achievements/trophies](Skyrim_Achievements.md).

Do **[The Spiced Wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Spiced_Wine)** before **[Bound Until Death](Skyrim_Bound_Until_Death.md)**

- **Why**: As soon as you start Bound Until Death, The Spiced Wine becomes unavailable.

Do **[Blood on the Ice](Skyrim_Blood_on_the_Ice.md)** before **[Mourning Never Comes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mourning_Never_Comes)**

- **Why**: During Mourning Never Comes, you are given the option to kill [Nilsine Shatter Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nilsine_Shatter-Shield) for a bonus. Doing this will cause her mother to kill herself. This can cause a fair amount of problems with quest progression, so it's best to complete Blood on the Ice first.

Do **[Rare Gifts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rare_Gifts) ([Torbjorn Shatter-Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Torbjorn_Shatter-Shield))** before **[Mourning Never Comes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mourning_Never_Comes)**

- **Why**: During Mourning Never Comes, you are given the option to kill [Nilsine Shatter Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nilsine_Shatter-Shield) for a bonus. Doing this will cause her mother to kill herself. Torbjorn will no longer need the [Amulet of Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Amulet_of_Arkay) for his wife.

Do **[Mourning Never Comes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mourning_Never_Comes)** before **[Lost to the Ages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lost_to_the_Ages)**<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>

- **Why**: During Lost to the Ages you are required to trek through [Raldbthar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Raldbthar), where [Alain Dufont](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Alain_Dufont) lives. If you kill him before starting Mourning Never Comes, you cannot obtain the bonus reward in that quest. Alain can be avoided as he will only turn hostile if you directly attack him or get too close.

Do **[Search and Seizure](Skyrim_Search_and_Seizure.md)** before **[The Battle for Fort Sungard (Stormcloaks)](Skyrim_The_Battle_for_Fort_Sungard_(Stormcloaks).md)**/**[Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md)**

- **Why**: Ondolemar will not spawn if the Reach is under Stormcloak control.

Do **[Rare Gifts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rare_Gifts) ([Siddgeir](Skyrim_Siddgeir.md))** before **[A False Front (Stormcloaks)](Skyrim_A_False_Front_(Stormcloaks).md)**/**[Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md)**

- **Why**: Siddgeir will not give you the quest if he is not jarl.
