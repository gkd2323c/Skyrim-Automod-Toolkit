—Mochveda, one of the Cernunnon's oath-bound<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mochveda-1)</sup> Cernunnon is a spirit<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive FALK-2)</sup> of importance to the [Dreadhorn](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) [clan](Lore_Reachfolk_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FD-3)</sup> Dreadhorn Clan, similarly to many other clans of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) venerated and made pacts with spirits, both lesser and greater.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GSOR2-4)</sup> Cernunnon was summoned to aid them during the siege of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FD-3)</sup>

Cernunnon was capable of granting immortality to their champions, also known as their oath-bound and the spiritmasters.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Oathbreaker-5)</sup> So long Cernunnon was undefeated, their oath-bound were effectively immortal. It was Cernunnon who determined when they die.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mochveda-1)</sup> The oath made by their followers sustained them and protected them from death.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Erbogar-6)</sup> Cernunnon could grant power to their followers, who held Cernunnon's power within them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Tuecille-7)</sup> The oath, also referred to as a compact made between Cernunnon and their oath-bound was believed to be unbreakable and eternal.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Tuecille-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-C-8)</sup>

Cernunnon wielded powers associated with the [Void](Lore_Void.md) and could manipulate it to pull the souls of their foes. Cernunnon could also possess and reanimate the corpses of the deceased.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-C-8)</sup> Oath-bound of Cernunnon were known for spilling their enemies' "blood for the Hunt-Father" and offering their "soul for Cernunnon".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mochveda-1)</sup>

In the Reachfolk culture the void is associated with [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) and the realm of spirits.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GSOR-9)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/ON-npc-Mochveda.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Mochveda.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mochveda.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mochveda.jpg) Mochveda, one of the Cernunnon's oath-bound [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/ON-npc-Erbogar.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Erbogar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Erbogar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Erbogar.jpg) Erbogar, one of the Cernunnon's oath-bound In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Cernunnon was summoned to aid Reachfolk of the Dreadhorn Clan during their siege of [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) city of Falkreath and to reanimate the dead in the Nordic crypts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FD-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive FALK-2)</sup> They took the form akin to the [Bone Colossus](Lore_Bone_Colossus.md) and illuminated the [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) sigils set up by the entrance to the Hall of the Dead. During that time three members of the Dreadhorn Clan were sworn to Cernunnon: Erbogar the hunter, Mochveda the witch, and Tuecille the warrior.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mochveda-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Tuecille-7)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Erbogar-6)</sup> The [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) who aided [Eerika Skjoralmor](Lore_Eerika_Skjoralmor.md) fought against the Reachfolk and managed to defeat Cernunnon and their oath-bound. Although Cernunnon was believed to grant them immortality, the spirit abandoned them after their defeat, and reanimated and possessed the corpse of the ancestor of the modern leader of Falkreath [Hjurgol Skjoralmor](Lore_Hjurgol_Skjoralmor.md), Jarl [Bjarfrud Skjoralmor](Lore_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.md). Cernunnon set him against the Undaunted, but the group of warriors managed to put the reanimated Jarl back to the eternal rest.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-C-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DBS-10)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FD-3)</sup> The further fate of Cernunnon is unknown, but the Dreadhorn Clan was ultimately defeated, and its leaders, Patriarch [Gherig Bullblood](Lore_Gherig_Bullblood.md) and [Domihaus the Bloody-Horned](Lore_Domihaus.md), slain.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FD-3)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BFB-11)</sup>
