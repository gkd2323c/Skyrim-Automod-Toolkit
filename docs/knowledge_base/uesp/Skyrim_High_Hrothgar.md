[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg/200px-SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) High Hrothgar **High Hrothgar** is a small [settlement](Skyrim_Settlements.md) southeast of [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) roughly halfway up the [Throat of the World](Skyrim_Throat_of_the_World.md). It is a major landmark and home to the reclusive [Greybeards](Skyrim_Greybeards.md), who are masters of [The Voice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Voice).

Traveling to High Hrothgar requires scaling the "[7,000 steps](Skyrim_Climb_the_Steps.md)" from [Ivarstead](Skyrim_Ivarstead.md). On the way up to High Hrothgar there will be at least one [frost troll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Troll) on the path regardless of your level (though you can avoid fighting it), and you will potentially encounter several more cold-themed enemies like [snow bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Snow_Bear) and [ice wraiths](Skyrim_Ice_Wraith.md). In addition to enemy encounters, a few travelers will be seen on the way to the top - pilgrims who are exploring and reading the wisdom etched on the [ancient tablets](#Etched_Tablets). Reading all of the tablets grants the [Voice of the Sky](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Voice_of_the_Sky) effect for 24 hours, which prevents animals from either attacking or fleeing from you; the tablets do not need to be read in numerical order to obtain this effect.

## Walkthrough

### Exterior
Between the two curved exterior stairwells that lead up to the building itself, there is a blue chest, three large sacks, and a smaller sack which are all set as non-respawning containers and can thus be safely used for storage.

### High Hrothgar
Once you have completed [The Horn of Jurgen Windcaller](Skyrim_The_Horn_of_Jurgen_Windcaller.md), you can safely take any items from within High Hrothgar. If you later kill Paarthurnax during the quest [Paarthurnax](Skyrim_Paarthurnax_(quest).md), taking items here will then be counted as stealing.

## Notes
- It seems that early in *Skyrim'* s development, you would have been able to freely enter High Hrothgar before [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md) was completed, as Arngeir has unique dialogue should he be spoken to before that quest is finished. It is still possible to enter High Hrothgar early through the back doors by climbing the mountain around it on a horse.
- [Todd Howard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Todd_Howard) jokingly mentioned in an interview that he would count all the steps leading up to High Hrothgar to make sure it's 7,000.
- Despite being referenced by local NPCs including [Gwilin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gwilin) and [Lynly Star-Sung](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lynly_Star-Sung) as the "7,000 steps," there are only around 700-800 steps on the path between Ivarstead to High Hrothgar. When walking, it takes approximately 2,400 footsteps from High Hrothgar to reach the base of the path. Walking 7,000 footsteps from High Hrothgar will actually bring you to the town center of Whiterun.
- There are banners hanging throughout the High Hrothgar interior with two separate phrases written on them; either "LOK BO" or "THUUM TUUM". Unfortunately, it is impossible to translate them completely as not enough of the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) is known. We DO, however, know that "Lok" means "Sky" and "Thuum" means "Shout" or "Voice". Therefore, the most likely translation is a phrase that [Arngeir](Skyrim_Arngeir.md) commonly says when you engage or exit conversation with him: "Sky above, voice within."
- The wind barrier in the courtyard deals 25 points of [cold damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Damage) per second, making it impossible to pass without [Clear Skies](Skyrim_Clear_Skies.md).
- You can ask all [Ivarstead](Skyrim_Ivarstead.md) residents what they know about High Hrothgar, except for [Narfi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Narfi).
- If you bring [Serana](Skyrim_Serana.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> here, she will kneel and meditate the same way as the Greybeards.
- If you transform into a [Vampire Lord](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> when you're here, the Greybeards will attack immediately.
- According to [Balgruuf](Skyrim_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md), High Hrothgar is neutral territory regarding the [Civil War](Skyrim_Civil_War.md).
- The Greybeards can occasionally be seen shouting in the courtyard during the day.

## Bugs
- The compass directions inside High Hrothgar are turned 90 degrees counterclockwise compared to outside.
- The gate near the mountainous edge of the back courtyard of High Hrothgar has very odd collision issues. While it is opening, part of its left door will lose its hitbox and pass through you. This will also cause its hitbox to distort and move to the south a short distance, which will persist even after the gate is closed. <sup>**?**</sup> - It isn't possible to interact with the gate from the distorted hitbox, as you can only close it from where the model is.
- These collision bugs don't occur with the right side of the gate.
- The end tables behind the beds have been placed such that the drawer of each one clips through the bed's headboard each time you open them. <sup>**?**</sup>
- When speaking to Einarth, Borri, or Wulfgar after The Horn of Jurgen Windcaller, the rumble as each one says "Dovahkiin" starts before the word is spoken. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2, addresses this issue. It will now start AFTER the word is spoken.
