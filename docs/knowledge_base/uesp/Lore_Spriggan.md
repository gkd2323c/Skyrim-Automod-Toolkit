*Yes I did wreak vengeance upon the slayers of wood. Know that they did slay seedlings of mine. Would you stand rooted while someone slew your seedlings? I have no quarrel with you. Convince the woodsmen to prune with care, and I shall leave them in peace.*
—A Spriggan negotiating with the Agent<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hunt-1)</sup> Spriggans, often called **Nature's Guardians**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBRumours-2)</sup> are [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md) that typically take the form of [tree](Lore_Flora.md) -like female humanoids,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FGTS-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBPrima-4)</sup><sup>:55</sup> though some also take animal-like forms.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SB-5)</sup> They have also been described as woodland [fairykin](Lore_Faerie.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DUG-6)</sup> They possess moderate intelligence, but despite being revered they are usually hostile towards travelers. They are usually found protecting secluded glades and groves all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), often blending in with the plant life and catching trespassers unaware. Spriggans attack with their long, sharp fingers as well as with their teeth, and have the ability to poison enemies. They have incredible regenerative abilities, resulting in adventurers often having to defeat them several times before they remain dead. If threatened, they can call nearby woodland animals to aid them in battle, and some even have the ability to magically summon [black bears](Lore_Black_Bear.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBlivion-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-8)</sup> They also often utilize swarms of insects to defend themselves.

According to Nordic myth, Spriggans are immortal, and become larger and more powerful each time they are slain.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DUG-6)</sup>

## Origins and Religion
Spriggans are considered by Nords to be creations of the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md). Spriggans are also associated with [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), and regard the [Boots of the Crusader](Lore_Crusader%27s_Relics.md#Boots_of_the_Crusader) as sacred.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> They oftentimes serve as the guardians of ancient trees, such as the [Eldergleam](Lore_Eldergleam.md) and the First Tree. According to [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) legend, the Spriggans of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) came into being when the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md) breathed life into the land, and his Breath blew through the trees.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

## Behavior and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/ON-npc-Spriggan_Venombow.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Spriggan_Venombow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Spriggan_Venombow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Spriggan_Venombow.jpg) A Spriggan using a natural bow (ESO) Spriggans are known to form hierarchies, with powerful elder Spriggans such as Spriggan Matrons and the even more powerful Spriggan Earth Mothers acting as Matriarchs.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Skyrim Lead-12)</sup> Though the scholar [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) was convinced that Spriggans are incapable of such intelligent behavior, and that they only act according to instinct.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FGTS-3)</sup>

Spriggans have their own unique [language](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Language), which can be learned by scholars and used to calm the creatures.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Language-13)</sup> Some Spriggans are even capable of speaking [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md), and are capable of negotiating with others.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hunt-1)</sup>

Spriggans are known to to grow from seeds, which eventually sprout into saplings.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hunt-1)</sup>

## Variants and Related Creatures
There are several varieties of Spriggan. Regular Spriggans appear as female humanoids made of wood, sometimes covered in foliage or emitting magical green light. Some scholars have speculated that the color of their floral fixtures change to match the current season of the year, accounting for "the so-called spring, summer, autumn, and winter" variants found amidst Tamriel's deciduous forests.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FGTS-3)</sup>

### Animals
Spriggans occasionally take the shapes of animals, such as bears and [senche-cats](Lore_Senche-Cat.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SB-5)</sup>

They are similar in appearance to [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) animals, creatures that were caught in the proximity of Bosmeri Wild Hunts that have forgotten their Y'ffre-taught shapes and shift into unnatural forms.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-WHC-14)</sup>

### Burnt Spriggans
Spriggans have occasionally been altered by certain firey stimulus, such as the firey Ashen Spriggans found during volcanic activity in the Systres Archipelago.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> After the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the [Hirstaang Forest](Lore_Hirstaang_Forest.md) on Solstheim was scorched by the eruptions from [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), which gave rise to mysterious [Burnt Spriggans](Lore_Burnt_Spriggan.md). These twisted creatures glow from eternally-smouldering embers, and have the ability to attack travelers with magical fire. They wander the ashlands of southern Solstheim and are the subject of research for the [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) of [Tel Mithryn](Lore_Tel_Mithryn.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

### Related Creatures

#### Gnarls
[Gnarls](Lore_Gnarl.md), also known as **Walking Trees**, are one of the strangest creatures of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Like the [Elytra](Lore_Elytra.md), this animated plant roams almost anywhere on the Isles. Said to be one of [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s truly unique creations, the Gnarl has the most unusual trait of being able to use magic cast upon it, and harness that power to bolster its own defense, becoming resistant to just that element for a short time.

There is much not understood about them, such as whether they have genders and how they propagate. They are believed to be caretakers of the Shivering Isles' [Root System](Lore_Root.md), severe trauma to the root system walls results in the formation of [amber](Lore_Amber.md) deposits as part of its natural defense mechanism, Gnarls clear away the amber and sometimes bring it to the surface. They also have a truce of sorts with other creatures of the Isles such as the [Baliwog](Lore_Baliwog.md) and [Elytra](Lore_Elytra.md). They seem to have some relation to Spriggans, and are sometimes classified with them.

[Gnarl bark](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnarl_Bark) is often harvested from the bodies of Gnarls for use in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md). The bark is sometimes turned into gnarl-fiber to be used as shoelaces. Such laces can still be alive and have been known to attack people. With sufficient hydration and nutrients, it's possible a whole new Gnarl could be grown from just these fibers.

#### Lurchers
[Lurchers](Lore_Lurcher.md) (or **Log-striders**) are lumbering [plant](Lore_Flora.md) -like creatures of multiple origins. They often created as a form of wooden [golem](Lore_Golem.md), described as constructs made from bark, moss, and evil [magic](Lore_Magic.md). Lurchers often attack by expelling clouds of choking pollen from their glowing core. This pollen has been known to spread [disease](Lore_Diseases.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Within the torso of Lurchers are knotted hearts, which are described as fist-sized acorns that beat like a heart.

[Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) create them by binding evil [spirits](Lore_Spirit.md) to crudely made bodies of vines, twisted branches, and knurled bark. There also have been instances of [Briarhearts](Lore_Briarheart.md) from the [Winterborn](Lore_Winterborn.md) clan transforming themselves into [briar heart tree](Lore_Briar_Heart_Tree.md) lurchers through [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) rituals involving fire, vines, and blood. Lurchers have been described by [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) as the "accursed brothers" or "polluted cousins" of Spriggans. Their crude artificial bodies are said to contrast them with the lithe forms of the Spriggans created by [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md). The [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) performs a perverted ritual using Spriggans as a base, twisting the creatures into lurchers and leaving them as shells of their former selves. Members of the [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md) considered these lurchers abominations.

Lurchers are looked upon positively in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), where they are created by the local [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) frequently. The Bosmer create Lurchers using the local flora as a base, adding other ingredients such as bug parts to give their lurchers special [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) qualities. The Shimmering Kollopi Tree produces seeds that can be grown into vengeful lurchers. In [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md), the tribal Bosmer see lurchers as gifts from the [Wilderking](Lore_Wilderking.md), and "embodiments of the raw energy of the forest". The lurchers that resided within [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) were known to coexist peacefully with the native wildlife and [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md), some of them having resided in the area since ancient times.

#### Marsh Giant
[Marsh Giants](Lore_Marsh_Giant.md) are a [giant](Lore_Giant.md) -like [race](Lore_Race.md) native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). These manifestations of nature are a form of [woodland spirit](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md) considered "cousins" to spriggans. They are not related to the common giants of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), but they do share their size and pastoral activities. Their exact level of intelligence is unknown due to limited interactions. However, the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) apparently have a long and acrimonious history with the Marsh Giants, and urge the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to avoid the creatures if they can.

They play an important role in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md)'s ecosystem by knocking down dead trees, turning the soil and making room for younger vegetation to grow. This encourages the growth of mosses and suppresses the development of invasive fungi. Marsh giants also hinder loggers, contribute to [avian](Lore_Birds.md) diversity and ward off certain predators, such as feral [cats](Lore_Cat.md).

## Uses
The bodies of Spriggans provide [Taproot](Lore_Taproot.md) (also called Supple Roots), a valuable alchemical ingredient. They can also occasionally be harvested for [Belladonna](Lore_Belladonna.md) berries or rare [Heartwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartwood). [Spriggan Sap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan_Sap), [Spriggan Ears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan%27s_Ear),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Spriggan Bark, Primal Sproutlings,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESO-18)</sup> and [Burnt Spriggan Wood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Burnt_Spriggan_Wood) can all be obtained from Spriggans, and some can be of alchemical use. Once a year, Spriggans shed branches and leaves known as a Spriggan's Veil. These are prized by herbalists and wizards, and are believed to have symbolic meaning in [Druidic](Lore_Druid.md) practices.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Contraband_S-21)</sup> The [Glenmoril Wyrd](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md) are thought to use spriggan sap in their tattoo ink, so that the wearer may form a more intimate bond with nature.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

Witches sacrifice Spriggans and use their defiled body parts in their rituals. The artifact [Nettlebane](Lore_Nettlebane.md) was made by witches for this purpose.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>
