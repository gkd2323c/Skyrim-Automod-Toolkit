[Crafting Motifs](Lore_Crafting_Motifs.md) 2: The Dark Elves **by [Doctor Alfidia Lupus](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Doctor_Alfidia_Lupus)** *Notes on materials and designs used in Dark Elf armor* *
---
(Dr. Lupus was Imperial Ethnographer for Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) from [2E 418](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_418) to [431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431))

From the [High Elves](Lore_High_Elves.md) it is natural to next consider the [Dark Elves](Lore_Dark_Elves.md), as they originated in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) before their migration to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Their culture can thus be considered an offshoot of the Altmeri, though in many ways that of the Dunmer is a reaction to that of Summerset rather than an extension.

Also, [Morian](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) introduced me to a Dark Elven associate of his, [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md), who is helping him on his "transliminal sojourn" project. I don't know what that's all about, but Divayth offered to help me with references on Dunmeri culture, and I accepted.

Elegance is as much a goal for the Dark Elves as it is for the High Elves, but beyond that their styles could not be more different. Morrowind is a far harsher environment than fair Summerset, and that rigor is reflected in Dunmeri designs. The Dark Elves also draw on nature for their inspiration, but in place of avian and floral motifs, Dunmeri artifice draws on the curved and spiky forms of the carapaces of the giant insects that inhabit Morrowind. Elegant these are, but also fearsome, a constant reminder that the Dunmer daily fight for their very existence.

[Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) is the favored metal for Dark Elven heavy armor, but even in their lighter armors and shields, steel and steel alloys are often lacquered in dark tones to appear ebony-like. Clothing, armor included, is often accented by flaring extensions at shoulder, crest, or hip, with overlapping geometric designs that may have been borrowed from [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) culture, though Divayth bristled at the idea of any Dwemeri influence on the Dunmer.

In truth, I find the dark sorcerer from [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) exerts a strangely compelling attraction. He doesn't seem old, but he referred to Morian, who is at least sixty, as a "young man." I wonder how old he really is. In fact, I wonder many things about him. He has those crimson eyes that seem to look right through you. It's a little bit thrilling.

He's offered to take me with him to visit a [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) tavern down on the waterfront. I may do it.
