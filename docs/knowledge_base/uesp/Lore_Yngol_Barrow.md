[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg/200px-SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) Yngol Barrow circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Yngol Barrow (also called the **Barrow of Yngol**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Return-1)</sup> is an ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruin found in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), in the region of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md). It is known as the burial ground of [Yngol](Lore_Yngol.md), the eldest son of the Harbinger, [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md). In some sources, Yngol Barrow is found below the cliffs of [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Yngol-2)</sup> though an entrance can be found along the [White River](Lore_White_River.md). It is constructed in the [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) tradition.

## History
During a trip across the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), Ysgramor and the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) were caught in a terrible storm which caused the loss of both Yngol and the crew of the Harakh.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Notes-4)</sup> When Ysgramor claimed his son's body he had it buried in the Atmoran barrow-hill tradition near where the White River met the Sea of Ghosts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Yngol-2)</sup> After the disbanding of the Five Hundred Companions, Ysgramor would reencounter Yngol Barrow and decreed that a [great city](Lore_Windhelm.md) should be built near by in order that he have a view of the barrow from his seat of power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Return-1)</sup>

During the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Yngol Barrow was largely untouched and had become [Draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) -infested. After acquiring the Coral Dragon Claw in [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), an [adventurer](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) would delve Yngol Barrow. There they encountered Yngol's Shade and claimed the Helm of Yngol upon his defeat.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
