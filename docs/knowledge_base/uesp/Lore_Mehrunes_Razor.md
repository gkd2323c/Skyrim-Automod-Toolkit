# Mehrunes' Razor

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/OBR-item-Mehrunes_Razor_02.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Mehrunes_Razor_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Mehrunes_Razor_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Mehrunes_Razor_02.jpg) Mehrunes Razor (Oblivion Remastered) *The Razor is Mehrunes Dagon's personal artifact. It has always heralded bloody change and carnage.*
—Silus Vesuius Mehrunes' Razor (sometimes **Mehrunes Razor**), also called the **Dagger of the Final Wounds**, the **Bane of the Righteous**, and the **Kingslayer**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Silus-1)</sup> is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). This powerful [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) dagger has the ability to kill instantly,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFJ-2)</sup> as there is a small chance that Dagon will claim the souls of those struck by the Razor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAC-3)</sup> It is Daedric in appearance, emblazoned with Daedric script and seemingly able to drink the light that hits it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SSMR-4)</sup>

## History

### Origins
Mehrunes' Razor was believed to be one of the Daedric artifacts formed through the long-term binding of lesser daedric entities by Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DD-5)</sup>

### First Era
In ancient times, Dagon created a construct known as the Nefarivigum to challenge those who drew near to undertake a trial. One who passed the trial would be deemed worthy of wielding Mehrunes' Razor. Located in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the Nefarivigum was discovered by the ancient [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), who arrogantly believed they could turn away any who sought the Razor. To this end, they built the vast, ringed city of [Varsa Baalim](Lore_Varsa_Baalim.md) during the height of Ayleid civilization. For many years, pilgrims came to Varsa Baalim seeking the Razor, but the Ayleids turned them away. One day in the early years of the First Era, a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) infiltrated the city. Before long, the city was infested with undead and thrust into chaos. The city suddenly and mysteriously disappeared. None know what happened, but Varsa Baalim seemingly vanished into the mountains of the Eastern [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAC-3)</sup>

Despite the establishment of the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md) between the 3rd and 5th of Rain's Hand in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), Dagon felt nothing was to impede him from destroying Nirn.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-6)</sup> This sentiment would manifest a few months later in Sun's Dusk of 1E 2920.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> A witch of the [Skeffington Coven](Lore_Skeffington_Coven.md) of High Rock was deceived into calling forth the Prince of Destruction to destroy Mournhold and slay her former lover, and he complied.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> During the carnage, [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) arrived with [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) to Mournhold, and engaged Dagon in the battlefield that was lit by a barrage of flame which fell indiscriminately. During this time, Dagon was seen wielding four of his great razors.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Razor)</sup> Almalexia plunged [Hopesfire](Lore_Hopesfire.md) into Dagon's flesh and carved him from within. Meanwhile, Sotha Sil, enacting vengeance for his birthplace of [Ald Sotha](Lore_Ald_Sotha.md), used "god-bronze whips" to lash the Prince into submission. Wounded, Sil allegedly whispered Dagon's Nymic, and the Prince "exploded throughout all time".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTIS8-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-2920v11-10)</sup> After his banishment, Sotha Sil sealed the planar doors between Nirn and the Deadlands, blocking off all access for Dagon as further punishment for breaking the Coldharbour Compact.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONLyranth-11)</sup>

### Second Era
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWYB-12)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-13)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing Mehrunes' Razor among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TA-14)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Mehrunes' Razor and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-MWFR-15)</sup>

In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) named Trouble-Finder reported coming across a cheap dagger for sale in [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md). It is unknown whether this was actually the Razor, but several cultists believed it was and chased him down for it.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Trouble-Finder-16)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Mehrunes' Razor had been shattered into several shards.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SSMR-4)</sup> It was also seen at that time in the possession of a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) woman named Gilraen, a native of [Whisper Grove](Lore_Whisper_Grove.md). The city was sacked by a servant of Dagon, Razor Master Erthas, who opened an Oblivion Gate within the city. Seeking to protect her family, Gilraen entered the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) and made a pact with Dagon; she would remain in the realm as a Razor Master, and Whisper Grove would be protected from the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) in return. This pact allowed Gilraen to survive indefinitely in the Deadlands, where she visited her wrath upon the region known as the Knives of Discord. However, her husband Fingaenion reopened the Oblivion Gate in a failed attempt to find and rescue her. In doing so and subsequently being menaced by Daedra, he unknowingly broke the pact. After taking the [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md) and sealing the Gate permanently, Gilraen was free to return home to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), still in possession of Mehrunes' Razor.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

### Third Era
It is said that the [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) master of [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md), [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md), was once in possession of the blade and used it to inspire his troops in battle against the rival Telvanni master of [Tel Aruhn](Lore_Tel_Aruhn.md), Gothren, before it was stolen by a troupe of acrobats whom he had wronged. The troupe used the dagger for a time in their acts, Master Mearvis in particular using it to turn [marshmerrow reeds](Lore_Marshmerrow.md) into papyrus.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ROA-18)</sup> Circa [3E 325](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_325), the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) was decimated by a vicious internal power struggle, and it is suspected that the Razor was involved.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-19)</sup>

Around the time of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), an [unknown agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) was given the weapon by Dagon for slaying a rebellious [frost atronach](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DFMDQ-20)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) recovered the Razor from Alas Ancestral Tomb, where it lay disused and rusted by the corpse of Varner Hleras. In return, Dagon imbued the Razor with his power and returned it to its former glory.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a rogue Telvanni Arch-Mage named [Frathen Drothan](Lore_Frathen_Drothan.md) assembled an army of mercenaries and searched for the Razor in [Sundercliff Watch](Lore_Sundercliff_Watch.md), an abandoned Imperial outpost and iron mine. It was rumored that the entrance to the ancient Ayleid city of Varsa Baalim had been uncovered, and with it the Nefarivigum.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Msirae Faythung, a previous champion of Dagon, stood guard over the Razor after having failed the Prince. As punishment, the [Kyn](Lore_Kyn.md) cut open his chest to reveal his heart and cursed him to stand statue-like in the Nefarivigum for eternity. Drothan decrypted the runes in the Nefarivigum, which summoned the Razor,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFJ-2)</sup> but before he could recover it the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) defeated him and claimed the dagger.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OBMehrunes-23)</sup>

### Fourth Era
After the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), a group devoted to eradicating the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) from Tamriel led by Lenne of [Clan Axe-Bearer](Lore_Clan_Axe-Bearer.md) stumbled upon the Razor. They broke the Razor into pieces and divided them between the three most senior members of their order, pledging to keep the pieces safe. They renamed themselves "[The Keepers of the Razor](Lore_Keepers_of_the_Razor.md)" in honor of their new role.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TKOTR-24)</sup> The hilt, pommel, and blade shards of the Razor were passed down through the Keepers' descendants, but the scabbard was not.

Despite being broken, the complete Razor was reportedly acquired twice during this time period, both times by individuals from the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) that went into hiding from the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) after the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> One belonged to a party known as the [Sheathed Blades](Lore_Sheathed_Blades.md), and they looted Mehrunes' Razor sometime between [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176) and [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) off the remains of a Storm Dwarven Centurion they defeated in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> The second was the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md), who is thought to have looted the dagger around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180) from an extravagant chest.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> The reason for these anomalous appearances of the artifact is unknown.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) was contacted by [Silus Vesuius](Lore_Silus_Vesuius.md), a Dagon enthusiast trying to establish a Mythic Dawn museum, who had found the scabbard. He hired the Dragonborn to collect the three missing pieces. The two journeyed to a shrine to Mehrunes Dagon to have the Razor repaired. Dagon demanded the life of Silus Vesuius in return. Vesuius offered the Dragonborn gold to ignore Dagon's request, wanting to add the shattered pieces to his museum if he couldn't have the restored artifact. It is unknown whether the Dragonborn accepted his offer or killed Vesuius and claimed the dagger.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Skyrim-28)</sup>
