# House Davaux

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-place-Wayrest.jpg/200px-ON-place-Wayrest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wayrest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wayrest.jpg) Wayrest *I come from a long line of Davaux's back in Wayrest. Our house has been serving High Rock and Tamriel for generations.*
—Captain Isara Davaux<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CID-1)</sup> House Davaux is a prominent [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble house based in the [kingdom](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), with a history of influence spanning across the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second Eras](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CID-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRIV-2)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-place-All_Flags_Islet_06.jpg/200px-ON-place-All_Flags_Islet_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-All_Flags_Islet_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-All_Flags_Islet_06.jpg) Port Sargo on All Flags Islet houses the plaque honoring Captain Isara Davaux. [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/ON-npc-Captain_Isara_Davaux.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Captain_Isara_Davaux.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Captain_Isara_Davaux.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Captain_Isara_Davaux.jpg) A spirit of Captain Isara Davaux Although the exact date of House Davaux's establishment remains obscure, the oldest records documenting its existence predate the formation of the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) and [Bendu Olo](Lore_Bendu_Olo.md)'s assault on [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260). By that time, the Davaux lineage had already been regarded as ancient, and the House has been dedicated to the service of High Rock and Tamriel for numerous generations.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CID-1)</sup>

The Davaux family played a notable role in the establishment and early endeavors of the All Flags Navy. During the assembly of the original fourteen ships that served as the cornerstone of this powerful naval force, Captain Isara Davaux took command of one of these vessels, named *The King's Jest*. A plaque, commemorating her name, was installed on the All Flags Islet.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAFNSH-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CID-1)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/ON-render-Jorvuld_Davaux.jpg/200px-ON-render-Jorvuld_Davaux.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Jorvuld_Davaux.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Jorvuld_Davaux.jpg) Jorvuld Davaux leading the expedition [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/44/LO-book-Lady_Arabelle.jpg/200px-LO-book-Lady_Arabelle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Lady_Arabelle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Lady_Arabelle.jpg) Lady Arabelle partaking in a meeting During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in the mid-Second Era, Lady [Arabelle Davaux](Lore_Arabelle_Davaux.md) was a prominent member of the house. She held the rank of [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md) captain, worked with the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md) intelligence agency, and was associated with the [Society of the Steadfast](Lore_Society_of_the_Steadfast.md), advocating for peace talks during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAD-4)</sup> Some believed she was either the author or inspiration for the popular [Investigator Vale](Lore_Investigator_Vale.md) detective novels.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRIV-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mt CA-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MTCLA-6)</sup>

From the same era, another known individual from the Davaux family was Jorvuld Davaux. Jorvuld was a mage, healer, scholar, and historian with expertise in the history of the [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Jorvuld-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-THOZ-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CM-9)</sup> He led the expedition to explore Scalecaller Peak and successfully thwarted the resurgence of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) branch, influenced by Zaan and the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md). He also played a vital role in stopping the terrible plague, though it is unknown whether he succumbed to it or not.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-POP-10)</sup> His Breton family adhered to a practice of adopting [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) given names, a tradition rooted in his maternal line's proud legacy of Nord scholars. It is unclear if this tradition extended to all members of the Davaux family.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCJD-11)</sup>

In the mid-Second Era, during the Interregnum, Davaux was a common surname among Bretons. People with the Davaux surname could be found in various roles, from innkeepers to servants of local nobles, particularly in the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) region.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ID-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BD-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RD-14)</sup> Moreover, the name Davaux was present in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), with Anais Davaux serving as a messenger representing Chancellor [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AD-15)</sup>
