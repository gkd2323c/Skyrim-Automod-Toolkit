*Alchemy description:* **Illusion spells are <mag>% stronger for <dur> seconds.**
*Enchanting description:* **Illusion spells cost <mag>% less to cast.**
*Vampirism description:* **Illusion spells are 25% more powerful.**

There are three distinct **Fortify [Illusion](Skyrim_Illusion.md)** effects: one from [Alchemy](Skyrim_Alchemy.md) -created potions, one granted by [enchanting](Skyrim_Enchanting.md) armor, and one from [vampirism](Skyrim_Vampirism.md). Their effects are as follows.

## Alchemy
In potions, Fortify Illusion increases the magnitude of Illusion spells. This allows targeted Illusion spells to affect higher-level creatures, and is applied **before** perks. For example, a [Fury](Skyrim_Illusion_Spells.md#Fury) spell cast with a Fortify Illusion +50% potion and the Rage perk will affect creatures up to level (6 * 1.5) + 12 = 21 (without the potion it would be 18). Similarly, a [Frenzy](Skyrim_Frenzy_(effect).md) spell cast with the same potion but without perks will affect creatures up to level (14 * 1.5) = 21 (without the potion it would be 14). Thus this bonus is best used on high-level spells and is effective even if the user does not have Illusion perks.

## Enchanting
In enchantments, Fortify Illusion decreases the [cost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Cost) of Illusion spells, and the use cost of Illusion based enchantments.

The following items use this effect. You can learn how to enchant custom items with Fortify Illusion if you find one of the following items and disenchant it:

- All varieties of Armor, Helmets, and Rings *[of Illusion](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md#Fortify_Illusion)* - Includes the enchantments *of Minor Illusion*, *of Major Illusion*, *of Extreme Illusion*, *of Eminent Illusion*, and *of Peerless Illusion.*
- All varieties of Robes of Illusion

Artifacts and unique items that use the effect but cannot be disenchanted include:

- [Archmage's Robes](Skyrim_Archmage%27s_Robes.md)
- [Diadem of the Savant](Skyrim_Diadem_of_the_Savant.md)
- [Helm of the Crusader](Skyrim_Helm_of_the_Crusader.md) and [Reforged Helm of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reforged_Helm_of_the_Crusader)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Divine_Crusader.md)</sup>
- [Mentor's Ring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mentor%27s_Ring)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Fishing.md)</sup>

## Vampirism
**[Vampirism](Skyrim_Vampirism.md)** grants the Champion of the Night effect. This allows targeted Illusion spells to affect higher-level creatures but is applied *after* perks, making it very powerful for high-level Illusionists. For example, a [Fury](Skyrim_Fury.md) spell cast with [Champion of the Night](Skyrim_Champion_of_the_Night.md) and the [Rage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rage) perk will affect creatures up to level (6 + 12) * 1.25 = 22.5, rounded down to 22. Without vampirism it would be 18, an increase of 4.

On the other hand, a [Frenzy](Skyrim_Frenzy_(effect).md) spell cast with Champion of the Night but no perks will affect creatures up to level 14 * 1.25 = 18. Without vampirism it would be 14, an increase of 4. Thus, this bonus works best for characters who have invested in Illusion perks.

Keep in mind that dual casting an Illusion spell will double its strength—including any bonuses from Alchemy and/or vampirism—in exchange for almost tripling the cost.
