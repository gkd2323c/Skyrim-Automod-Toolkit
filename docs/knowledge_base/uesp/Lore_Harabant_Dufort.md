Harabant Dufort, also known as **Harabant Handless**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBo MF-1)</sup> was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman who reigned as the first Baron of [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Harabant built the Meadow Fort, a smaller holding in the Oldstone Woods of [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md). It was at the fort that Harabant fought the Battle of Meadow Fort, which was the final battle of an unknown conflict. The battle started at dawn and continued without cessation until sunset.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBo MF-1)</sup>

Harabant's famed sword, Redheart, broke during the last hour of the battle. Despite Redheart breaking, Harabant defeated the dread sorcerer Fellbore by hurling the blade and driving it through the sorcerer's heart. Harabant lost his hand because of wounds sustained in the battle. He subsequently became known as Harabant Handless until he was granted a barony. It was said that he had Redheart's blade melted down and crafted into a hand to replace the one he had lost.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBo MF-1)</sup>

At some point following the Battle of Meadow Fort, officials in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) decided that the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) needed legitimate nobility to truly prosper, so it was decreed that Harabant and House Dufort would be granted a barony.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ho HD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ho HM-3)</sup> As the first Baron Dufort, many of Harabant's subjects attested to his irritability and were bruised by his hand. He later replaced the Meadow Fort with Castle Navire to the west, which became House Dufort's primary residence.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBo MF-1)</sup>

The Meadow Fort eventually became overgrown and fell into ruin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBo MF-1)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an adventurer and Isobel Veloise recovered Redheart's hilt from the ruins of the Meadow Fort and presented it to the [Order of the Iron Knot](Lore_Order_of_the_Iron_Knot.md) as part of the Sapphire Tourney.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Tot H-4)</sup>
