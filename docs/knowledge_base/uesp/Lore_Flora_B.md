## Bamboo
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/ON-furnishing-Grass%2C_Young_Bamboo_Shoots.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Grass%2C_Young_Bamboo_Shoots.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Grass,_Young_Bamboo_Shoots.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Grass,_Young_Bamboo_Shoots.jpg) Bamboo A variety of a grass plant which has tree-like properties. It can be found around Tamriel and is used as a material for a variety of things, such as furnishings and dry goods and in [Alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) where it has restorative properties for stamina.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFBamboo-1)</sup> Known varieties of Bamboo include the Mud Tree bamboo <sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOFrog Gig-2)</sup> and Mountain bamboo <sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOSai Sahan-3)</sup>. Parts of the Old Imperial Quarter in [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md) are said to be overgrown with Bamboo.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

**Found in:** - [The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Plants), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Conservatory_Furnishings/Hedges), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Bamboo)

**Harvested Ingredient:** [Bamboo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Bamboo)
## Banyan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/ON-furnishing-Tree%2C_Ancient_Banyan.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Tree%2C_Ancient_Banyan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tree,_Ancient_Banyan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tree,_Ancient_Banyan.jpg) A Banyan Tree Banyan trees are one of the most common trees in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM103-5)</sup> but can often be found growing in swampy areas all around Tamriel. The tree's wood is too soft and spongy to make into a bow, but its roots are tough and durable, and it does not wrap or swell in Black Marsh's humid environment. The Black Fin Legion, a militia based in [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md) makes their bows out of the tree's roots.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM103-5)</sup>

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)

## Bittergreen
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/MW-flora-Bittergreen_Pod.jpg/200px-MW-flora-Bittergreen_Pod.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-flora-Bittergreen_Pod.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-flora-Bittergreen_Pod.jpg) Bittergreen Pod A fast-growing slime triggered by rain vine that usually appears in thickets.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MWGen Dia Food-13)</sup> It grows into a red flowering plant,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> and is abundant in the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Bittergreen is a safe and nourishing food when it is boiled but is highly toxic if eaten raw.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MWGen Dia Food-13)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> It is often used to make tea.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The petals have alchemical value.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Raw bittergreen can be used to poison trees at their roots' water supply,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> and specially treated bittergreen petals make for a swift-acting poison.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Empty Bittergreen pods are attractive sitting locations for [kwama](Lore_Kwama.md) workers and scribs. If left where they can access them, kwama warriors may try to enter the pods when they obviously can't fit.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinator.md) stuck a sprig of bittergreen below the right pauldron during the final step in putting on their armor.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)

**Harvested Ingredient:** [Bittergreen Petals](Lore_Bittergreen_Petals.md)

## Bleeding Tree
The [Bleeding Tree](Lore_Bleeding_Tree.md) is a mysterious and legendary plant that holds some significance in both [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) traditions. Bretons incorporate its symbolic significance tied to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) in the naming conventions of their temples, while the Reachmen possess a vibrant oral tradition that weaves tales about the tree, often associating it with some kind of [forest spirit](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md).

## Bloodthorn Vines
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-quest-Swine_Thief.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Swine_Thief.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Swine_Thief.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Swine_Thief.jpg) Bloodthorn Vines Bloodthorn Vines, also known as Bloodroot Vines, are giant, writhing, thorned vines associated with dark [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) magic. They are used by the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) and the [Boneshaper Clan](Lore_Boneshaper_Clan.md). The plant is not native to the Reach, but is cultivated well by the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md). The Boneshapers use the vines in an unusual number of rituals, from lacing or growing them through the skeletons of their living sacrifices to shaping them into rough effigies before battle in mockery of life. Despite the use of the dead in several rituals, the Boneshapers forbid the practice of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md). On the other hand, the Bloodthorn Cult used the vines for a much more malevolent purpose, spreading them to corrupt the land and planting the seeds in the corpses of the dead to raise them as [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md). These vine-covered zombies lived in a painful state of undeath. The cult's leader, [Angof the Gravesinger](Lore_Angof.md), suffered from a similar existence to the zombies after making a pact with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), although he seemed to think that it could be reversed. During the cult's campaign in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the vines were spread throughout [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) and [Betony](Lore_Betony.md). Their grapes can be used to produce high quality wine.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)

## Brackenleaf
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg) Brackenleaf [Brackenleaf](Lore_Brackenleaf_(tree).md) is believed to be one of the first trees of Grahtwood. The story goes that when [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) spoke their world-shaping tale, she planted her words in the earth and there grew Brackenleaf. Indeed, Brackenleaf was compared to Elden Root, mentioned as one of many more ancient seeds.

Brackenleaf's heart is a sought after relic as legends say the heart holds the power of Y'ffre, the same magic used to create the world. Due to this, it's been under threat since the beginning of time itself, with foes seeking to take its power for themselves. The Outsider is a prominent example of such a foe.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Brackenleaf_(creature))

## Briar Heart Tree
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-flora-Briar_Heart_Tree.jpg/200px-ON-flora-Briar_Heart_Tree.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Briar_Heart_Tree.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Briar_Heart_Tree.jpg) A Briarheart Tree A strange tree with red leaves cultivated by [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) to produce Briar Heart Seeds, the source of the infamous [Briarheart](Lore_Briarheart.md) warriors. Briarheart Saplings, a juvenile form of the tree, are planted in corpses. Any corpse can serve this purpose. The smaller trees connect to the large tree through a system of roots. The fruit produced by the trees is used in rituals by [Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md) to resurrect Reachman warriors as Briarhearts. It is a great honor to be chosen for this transformation. The fruit is plunged into the chest cavity of a dead warrior to replace their heart, and they are resurrected with greater strength and fortitude.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)
