Telenger the Artificer was a celebrated genius, writer, explorer, and scholar on topics of anthropology and [magic](Lore_Magic.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> as well as an archmage,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Neetra-2)</sup> a canonreeve, a powerful [conjurer](Lore_Conjuration.md) and magical engineer.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTIEGTTSI-3)</sup> He was in service to both the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTIEGTTSI-3)</sup> He was a favored figure of the Mages Guild, a well-known personality in [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), and a researcher who traveled widely across the world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCACA-4)</sup> He was a man of considerable repute, regarded by some of his compatriots as a national treasure, an Archmage and a living connection to the past.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oinara-5)</sup>

## History

### Mages Guild Career
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/ON-place-Mages_Guild_%28Skywatch%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Mages_Guild_%28Skywatch%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mages_Guild_(Skywatch).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mages_Guild_(Skywatch).jpg) Mages Guild chapter in Skywatch Little is known of Telenger's early life, though by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) he was regarded as an old and accomplished mage.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Andewen-6)</sup> Over the years he had taken on numerous apprentices, both former and current, and held a high position within the Mages Guild.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> He was also known to have a long-standing acquaintance with the Guild's founder, [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md). While their interactions were not always entirely respectful, they nevertheless regarded one another as friends.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VG-7)</sup> They worked together to ensure the Guild's future success.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RNC-8)</sup> He held a leadership position in the chapter of the Guild at [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Neetra-2)</sup> the city he called home.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GC-9)</sup>

Telenger once led students on an expedition to [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) to study [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins. The venture ended poorly for most, though a few escaped unscathed. His students were forbidden from discussing the incident.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Raelys-10)</sup> In [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581), he attended a debutante ball in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), where he drew the attention of a cousin of Elydrina Nathriin of [Shad Astula](Lore_Shad_Astula.md), who appeared to take a romantic interest in him, though little is known of any outcome.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCACA-4)</sup>

Telenger taught many students over the years. At some point, Merormo, an Altmeri mage, was banished from the College by Telenger due to his continued ill-advised experiments.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DL-11)</sup> These magical experiments later caused various troubles in Auridon, for which he may have faced severe consequences.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Meromo-12)</sup> Another of Telenger's former pupils, after years of studying lenses and hues, discovered a method to create red lenses that did not fade over time.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CM-13)</sup> Another member of the Guild, Neetra, once attempted to repair her damaged [Dwarven spider](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md) companion. Telenger forbade the effort, arguing that the Dwemer components required were too valuable to be spent on what he considered a mere pet. Determined to restore the construct regardless, Neetra secretly enlisted the help of an adventurer to gather the necessary parts and repair it despite Telenger's ban.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Neetra-2)</sup> One of Telenger's former students, Pircalmo, later left the Guild and stole one of his wands. After falling into danger, he managed to resolve the situation with the help of an adventurer and notes left by his former mentor, eventually becoming the town mage of Willowgrove.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OPER-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Pircalmo-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-QC-16)</sup>

Telenger was admired by many members of the Guild,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ghadar-17)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Raelys-10)</sup> who sought his favor,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ruucaramil-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Candangarilde-19)</sup> and opportunity to work with him was met with great excitement,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Aillard-20)</sup> and considered a rare honor.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SS-21)</sup> Others found him stubborn, insufferable,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Umbarile-22)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Neetra-2)</sup> and unstable,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Meryon-23)</sup> yet many were still willing to do almost anything to secure his recommendation.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ruucaramil-18)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Camaldetuile-24)</sup> Some accounts suggested that some of those who served the Artificer were prone to dangerous accidents, as many, though not all, sought to impress him and advance in rank, sometimes despite lacking the skill their recommendations claimed. It was said that while Telenger's mastery of archaeology was vast, he could at times be naïve to the ambitions and motives of those in his service.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Fistanawe-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Ejukki-26)</sup> His word carried great sway within the guild, and his recommendation was considered highly valuable, as many of those he endorsed went on to successful careers, some attaining positions such as Wizard, or even Archmagister, like Astrinque, who had once served as his assistant.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Camaldetuile-24)</sup> Telenger received letters of recommendation from other guild members, such as Namolelcare of Firsthold, who praised promising students and urged Telenger to accept them for further study under him.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LTT-27)</sup> Some admirers regarded Telenger as one of the greatest mages of his time, praising his many honors, his travels across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in search of lost lore,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SE-28)</sup> and even his appearance,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SE-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Naarcaano-29)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCACA-4)</sup> considering him the most decorated living member of the Mages Guild.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SE-28)</sup> Many hopeful scholars sought apprenticeship under Telenger. One such candidate was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) named Rollin, who was entrusted with several relics to study and write analyses on. However, he lost them after a night of heavy drinking in [Rawl'kha](Lore_Rawl%27kha.md). Although the relics were recovered, it is unclear whether they were returned to Rollin so he could complete his report and present it to Telenger's apprentice, Meldil, or given directly to Meldil, who, upon learning of Rollin's negligence, intended to report the matter to Telenger and end the Bosmer's hopes of apprenticeship.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Meldil-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Rollin-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ANTF-32)</sup>

### Dune and the Queen's Return
*My High Elf companion couldn't quite hide the haughty nature of his upbringing, but his presence was certainly a vast improvement on past escorts. We discussed many highbrow topics, disagreeing on much but never coming to blows: Little wonder Telenger is regarded as a fine diplomat, or "canonreeve."*
—Flaccus Terentius<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTIEGTTSI-3)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-concept-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg) An Altmeri print celebrating Ayrenn's return [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/ON-place-Dune.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dune.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dune.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dune.jpg) Dune At some point between [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578) and 2E 581, Telenger served as an envoy of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in [Dune](Lore_Dune.md). On one occasion, he dined with Moon-Bishop Hunal and the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholar [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md). Though somewhat haughty and prone to lofty scholarly discussions, he was regarded as a capable diplomat and canonreeve, and he later arranged passage for Flaccus aboard his Swan Ship from [Arenthia](Lore_Arenthia.md) to [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) in northern Auridon.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TTIEGTT-33)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTIEGTTSI-3)</sup>

During the voyage he demonstrated his magical ability by summoning [storm](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md) and [flesh](Lore_Flesh_Atronach.md) [atronachs](Lore_Atronach.md), and in Firsthold he shared gossip about Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md)'s adventures before her return to the Isles. While there, during the celebrations of Queen's return to the Summerset Isles Telenger introduced Flaccus to Falarel, a former jester of [Rilis XII](Lore_Rilis_XII.md) and a bard of some renown, who later led them into the old castle vaults claiming to have encountered the [ghost](Lore_Ghost.md) of his former master. The encounter proved disastrous when the spirit of Rilis XII appeared, leading to chaos within the tombs. In the aftermath Flaccus was arrested for trespass, and later deported to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Little is known about how the incident affected Telenger, as he appeared to have retained an important position in Summerset afterward, though his whereabouts were unknown to Flaccus.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TTIEGTT-33)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTIEGTTSI-3)</sup>

### Skywatch
*Telenger the Artificer calls our fair city home! He runs a small shop and exhibition space for the Mages Guild on the west side of town. You can see relics from his travels, magical displays—all kinds of things!*
—Guide Culast<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GC-9)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-place-Telenger%27s_Emporium_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-Telenger%27s_Emporium_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Telenger%27s_Emporium_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Telenger%27s_Emporium_03.jpg) Telenger's Emporium Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Skywatch_Sanctuary.jpg) Skywatch Sanctuary,a ruin in Skywatch, once studied by Telenger before later being used as a hideout by Azandar al-Cybiades Little is known of Telenger's exact origins, though he was known to consider Skywatch his home.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GC-9)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Andewen-6)</sup> He was known to have a leadership position within the Mages Guild of Skywatch.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Neetra-2)</sup>

Telenger spent years excavating a magically potent [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) ruin at the heart of Skywatch,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCACA-4)</sup> in the middle of the city, in the royal courtyard and gardens,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GC-9)</sup> taking a careful approach to avoid unleashing dangerous forces. He invited the powerful [arcanist](Lore_Arcanists.md) [Azandar al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md) to assist as a consultant on the deep aetheric constructs. Two weeks later, Telenger returned to find the planar gates sealed, most magicka wells capped, and Azandar moving some of his belongings onto the site, claiming the portals were "pedestrian pablum only interesting to children" and that he was doing Telenger a favor by closing the enigmatum that had existed there since the times of the Aldmer.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCACA-4)</sup> The incident became a major scandal and greatly strained relations between the two scholars.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-AAC-34)</sup> As a result, the site was officially closed to everyone. The entrance was later guarded by a member of the Mages Guild, who was forbidden to speak of it at Telenger's request.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Wizard Nisira-35)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GC-9)</sup> In reality, those guards were the result of a compromise Azandar made with Telenger.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-AAC-34)</sup>

Telenger's Emporium was a shop in the west side of Skywatch,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GC-9)</sup> located close to the local chapter of the Mages Guild. The establishment displayed various curios and relics gathered by Telenger,<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TE-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SC-37)</sup> including a [Dwemer Centurion](Lore_Dwarven_Centurion.md) recovered from a ruin in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Eldwen-38)</sup> that visitors could briefly activate for demonstration.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TE-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SC-37)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-RMSIHSY-39)</sup> The construct also attracted Azandar's attention.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-AAC-34)</sup> The shop often drew scholars, guild members, and curious onlookers, many of whom admired Telenger's collection of unusual artifacts.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TE-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SC-37)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-RMSIHSY-39)</sup> One of his students, Eldwen, studied [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) relics there under Telenger's guidance,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Eldwen-38)</sup> while the shop itself was managed by Nerassil the Bound, a spirit magically bound by Telenger. Nerassil begrudgingly served as the Emporium's enchanter and merchant, selling miscellaneous objects recovered from Telenger's expeditions, a task he frequently complained about while expressing deep resentment toward his master.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-NTB-40)</sup>

### Ezduiin
*I am Telenger the Artificer—renowned genius, author, and explorer. Ezduiin is my latest endeavor. I will bring further glory and honor to the Mages Guild!*
—Telenger the Artificer<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/ON-place-Ezduiin.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ezduiin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ezduiin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ezduiin.jpg) Ezduiin [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/ON-interior-Ezduiin_Undercroft.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Ezduiin_Undercroft.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Ezduiin_Undercroft.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Ezduiin_Undercroft.jpg) Ezduiin Undercroft In 2E 582, Telenger led an expedition to Ezduiin, north of Skywatch.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TE-36)</sup> It was considered to be an important project of the Mages Guild.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Erronwe-41)</sup> The venture encountered numerous difficulties, beginning with the appearance of spirits and later worsening due to problems with supplies.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TE-36)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-RHE-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Parmtilir-43)</sup> Matters grew more serious when his chief scholar and assistant, Andewen, a former student of his and now a Master of Incunabula in Skywatch, and her team went missing.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Umbarile-22)</sup> The expedition had been searching for the ancient relic Mallari-Mora, which Telenger intended to present to the Council in hopes of earning recognition and reward, though Valaste expressed doubts about the undertaking. Telenger requested a help of an adventurer to salvage the situation.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> They found Andewen and learned that some of the spirits that had appeared seized the researchers before vanishing. The adventurer ventured into the ruin's undercroft. The one responsible was Uricantar,<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TMM-44)</sup> a former student of Telenger's whom he had once cherished almost like a son.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Aerchelas-45)</sup> Despite this Telenger had been forced to cast him out of the guild and forbid him from using magic, as Uricantar could never accept that power must have limits and that some prices are too great to pay.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Andewen-6)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Meldil-30)</sup> Obsessed with power and legacy, Uricantar sought the Mallari-Mora for his own purposes, viewing Telenger's students and others as pawns in a game of magical dominance.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Uricantar-46)</sup> He seized the Mallari-Mora, rekindled the ruin's ancient spirits, and began using them as guardians and servants. When Telenger arrived with Andewen, Uricantar attempted to assert control over the relic and claim his own ascendancy.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-UJ-47)</sup> Thanks to the intervention of an adventurer, the group, largely composed of Telenger's students, was rescued, and Uricantar was killed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TMM-44)</sup> He rewarded the adventurer with a staff bearing his personal mark.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Artificer.27s Bolt-48)</sup> The spirits awakened at the site were also laid to proper rest.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Sirinque-49)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-AAOK-50)</sup> Once the situation at Ezduiin was resolved, he expressed his eagerness to present his findings to the council alongside Andewen, noting that he and Valaste would have much to discuss.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup>

### Falinesti
*No! How dare you, mortal?* —Mephala<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Mephala-51)</sup>
*I am the Artificer, madam! That's why I dare!* —Telenger the Artificer<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-place-Falinesti_Autumn_Site.jpg/200px-ON-place-Falinesti_Autumn_Site.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Falinesti_Autumn_Site.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Falinesti_Autumn_Site.jpg) Falinesti Autumn Site [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/ON-quest-The_Arbordawn_Cult.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Arbordawn_Cult.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Arbordawn_Cult.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Arbordawn_Cult.jpg) Telenger caught in Mephala's web Telenger's next excavation was planned for Valenwood. He had already intended to venture there during his time at Ezduiin. In the frontier of Valenwood, he chose to study the [Falinesti Autumn Site](Lore_Falinesti_Autumn_Site.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> It was there that Telenger led another project, regarded by the members of the Guild as highly interesting.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Calircarya-52)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Berdonion-53)</sup> Telenger, the Artificer, led a Mages Guild expedition near the Arbordawn Cult's site,<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-TJOITP-54)</sup> uncovering the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) temple of [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md),<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-TJOITP-54)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> which the cult believed held the key to Falinesti's return. Though he forbade them from opening it, the cultists claimed to feel its power calling, certain that once unleashed, the city would be restored.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-TJOITP-54)</sup> Telenger disregarded the cult's warnings and explored the deeper tunnels leading to the Halls of Ichor, where the Arbordawn Cult had sealed passages with corruption stones. Fascinated by the relics he found, he pressed onward.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-FR-55)</sup> Though the cultists attacked the researchers, they were swiftly dealt with by Telenger,<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-K-56)</sup> who judged them little threat and transformed their leader, Indring into harmless poultry.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-FR-55)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Indring-57)</sup> Believing the Daedra to be the true threat, Telenger led the assistant into the temple, where they encountered Mephala's forces, and learned that the Arbordawn cultists had become her servants, the [Deathweavers](Lore_Deathweavers.md). Recognizing the danger, Telenger used powerful magic to reseal the temple and cut off Mephala's influence, after which he concluded the threat was contained, though the mystery of the vanished city of Falinesti remained unsolved.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TAC-58)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Mephala-51)</sup> He rewarded the adventurer with a garment he had personally enchanted, resistant to all but the most powerful spells.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-AV-59)</sup>

While still at the Falinesti site, Telenger had already begun planning his next project, which would likely involve one of the planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), remarking that it had been some time since he last explored [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). Little is known about this endeavor or whether it ever took place.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup>

### Coldharbour
*I'm sure Telenger's around here somewhere. He's really quite clever. And dashing, to boot! He reminds me of my … my good friend, Stibbons.*
—Lady Clarisse Laurent<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-LCL-60)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/ON-quest-The_Library_of_Dusk_06.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Library_of_Dusk_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Library_of_Dusk_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Library_of_Dusk_06.jpg) Telenger in the Library Vault [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/ON-place-Library_of_Dusk.jpg/200px-ON-place-Library_of_Dusk.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Library_of_Dusk.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Library_of_Dusk.jpg) The Library of Dusk Telenger participated in the meeting of the three alliances at Stirk, recognizing the challenge of uniting the leaders and organizing the expeditionary force to Coldharbour, and chose to join the force himself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TWOTC-61)</sup>

At some point during the campaign against the forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) Telenger, together with two other mages and scholars Raynor Vanos, and Clarisse Laurent decided to take advantage of their good fortune and explore the Library of Dusk. The potential knowledge to be gained far outweighed any perceived risks.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-RV-62)</sup> Both of his companions eventually became enthralled by a powerful Daedric spell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-LCL-60)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TTDL-63)</sup> He was kept prisoner in a cell with a banner of the [Star](Lore_Stars.md) of [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) and blue [crystals](Lore_Crystals.md), and was regarded as a powerful, capable mage by his captors.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-PT-64)</sup> By the time the rescue arrived, he already managed to leave his cell but remained within the complex.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-IYCRTOI-65)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-TLOD-66)</sup>

Telenger concealed a note with a spell that made it invisible to Daedra so that any mortal who discovered it could be trusted. In the message he warned his fellow captives, Clarisse and Raynor, about the overwhelming magical illusions within the place and admitted that even he had underestimated their power. Realizing that drastic measures were necessary, Telenger resolved to seal himself inside a vault in the lower levels, hoping to remain clear-headed long enough to devise a plan of escape and eventually flee alongside the other captives.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-IYCRTOI-65)</sup> In the vault, Telenger discovered a portal radiating overwhelming power, sensing that whatever lay beyond sought to pierce the veil of illusion over the Library of Dusk. Recognizing its potential use in the battle against Molag Bal, he identified it as one of the [Lights of Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lights_of_Meridia), legendary sources of power once used to hurl the Hollow City into Coldharbour. He entrusted the adventurer who had come to rescue the scholars with a Teleportation Trajectory Token, a runic device he attuned to the portal that would ensure a safe return. He instructed them to enter the portal, recover the Light, and bring it back to the camp outside the ruins. Once freed, he continued contributing to the the war effort in the [Hollow City](Lore_Hollow_City.md), intending to use the Light to aid the war effort, study its powers, and investigate ways to dismantle Molag Bal's [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) mechanism.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TTTT-67)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-TLOD-66)</sup>

### After the Planemeld

### Alinor
*Now, by Altmeri standards Telenger is almost as reckless as Your Majesty, so he decided that he would perform a feat of teleportation that would prove his peerless skill! And how better to do this than to find a way to travel to and from the vanished Isle of Artaeum, mystically removed from Nirn by wizardry of mysterious Psijic Order?*
— [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md)<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-RDJ-70)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/ON-npc-Penewen.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Penewen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Penewen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Penewen.jpg) High Magistrate Rinwary [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-npc-Razum-dar.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Razum-dar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Razum-dar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Razum-dar.jpg) Razum-dar Aside from his headquarters in Skywatch, Telenger also maintained his own workshop in the capital of the Summerset Isles, [Alinor](Lore_Alinor.md).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-RDJ-70)</sup>

In 2E 582, Telenger became involved in uncovering a conspiracy against Proxy Queen [Alwinarwe](Lore_Alwinarwe.md) orchestrated by [Rinwaray](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rinwaray). While speaking with [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md), the leader of the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md), in Alinor, Telenger lamented that despite considering himself the greatest living expert in [teleportation magic](Lore_Teleportation.md), he could not obtain the prestigious post of [Sapiarch](Lore_College_of_Sapiarchs.md) of Transliminal Travel as [Camilonwe of Alinor](Lore_Camilonwe_of_Alinor.md) still held the position. Seeking to prove his superiority, Telenger devised an ambitious magical experiment. After a month of work, he constructed a device called the [Hyperagonal Locational Determinator](Lore_Hyperagonal_Locational_Determinator.md), capable of locating any place in the [Gray Maybe](Lore_Gray_Maybe.md), even those hidden by powerful wards. Using it alongside a projection device, he successfully transported himself to the vanished island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), infiltrated [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s quarters in [Ceporah Tower](Lore_Ceporah_Tower.md), and returned with research notes belonging to Sotha Sil as proof of the feat before the [Psijics](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) could discover his intrusion. Unable to reveal this exploit openly due to the numerous laws he had broken to perform this feat, Telenger confided in an advisor named Penewen. At her suggestion, he used the same devices to send her to the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) so she could later testify to his magical prowess.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-RDJ-70)</sup> She was, in fact, on a mission and hoped to find a connection between the Clockwork City and Artaeum.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Penewen-71)</sup> After a few days there, she returned with the help of Telenger's devices, showered him with effusive praise, and then essentially disappeared from the artificer's life. This mystery led Razum-dar to investigate further, ultimately uncovering connections that helped expose the treacherous schemes unfolding within the Proxy Queen's court.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-RDJ-70)</sup>

Penewen, who was in reality High Magistrate Rinwaray in disguise, used the opportunity to obtain and test Sotha Sil's [Affect Inducer](Lore_Seht%27s_Affect_Inducer.md), a device capable of instilling emotions in living people. Her actions, enabled by Telenger's devices, became part of a wider conspiracy within the Proxy Queen's court, in which Rinwaray intended to use the Inducer to emotionally enslave Proxy Queen Alwinarwe. Razum-dar's investigation, aided by various artifacts created by Teleger and his travels across multiple planes, ultimately uncovered a dangerous scheme. During this time, Rinwaray returned to the Summerset Isles and made her way to Teleger's workshop. There, she used Seht's Affect Inducer on Teleger and ensured he was bound near a stone jetty on the shore. Razum-dar later found him tied to the backside of a pier, barely keeping his head above the incoming tide. Once freed, Teleger provided Razum-dar with a personal silk scarf belonging to Rinwaray, which allowed the [Clairvoyeur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clairvoyeur), another of his creations, to track her. Using the device, Razum-dar located Rinwaray and confronted her, ultimately defeating her and putting an end to her nefarious plot.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-RDJ-70)</sup>

## Research

### Publications
Telenger was a prolific writer, authoring many books on a variety of subjects. His interests tended more toward practical studies than metaphysical ones.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-FNOTS-72)</sup> One of his primary research interests was history,<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-THOWE-73)</sup> and the anthropological study of various cultures, including the Bosmer,<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-THOWE-73)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-WENAB-74)</sup> [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md),<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-FOTSV-75)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-CMP-76)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-SKO-77)</sup> and [Sload](Lore_Sload.md).<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-FNOTS-72)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ALC-78)</sup> He also worked on texts focused on the area of [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md).<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-EOTR-79)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-WAP-80)</sup>

At the request of Queen Ayrenn's advisor Nuulehtel, he compiled a brief account of the Maormer of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) titled *[Fang of the Sea Vipers](Lore_Fang_of_the_Sea_Vipers.md)*, urging his Altmer readers to exercise caution but also to have courage.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-FOTSV-75)</sup> In his other works he also described the armaments of the Maormer.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-CMP-76)</sup> His works also focused on Bosmer culture. *[The Humor of Wood Elves](Lore_The_Humor_of_Wood_Elves.md)* was a compilation of Bosmeri jokes,<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-THOWE-73)</sup> while *[Wood Elf Nicknames and Bynames](Lore_Wood_Elf_Nicknames_and_Bynames.md)* detailed the differences between Bosmeri bynames and nicknames and the Bosmeri cultural traditions surrounding those appellations.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-WENAB-74)</sup> *[A Loathsome Civilization](Lore_A_Loathsome_Civilization.md)* was Telenger's work on the Sload based on a text appropriated from a defeated Maormeri fleet, which he speculated to be the journal of a diplomat to [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) from before [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260). He also sent the journal to his colleagues in Alinor to verify its authenticity.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ALC-78)</sup> Another of his works, *[Further Notes on the Sload](Lore_Further_Notes_on_the_Sload.md)*, focused on the Sload's conflicts with the Maormer, the nature of the [Coral Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coral_Tower) and [Sload soap](Lore_Sload_Soap.md), and the maturation process of the Sload.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-FNOTS-72)</sup> Sapiarch Hannayel of the College of Sapiarchs was among the scholars who reviewed Telenger's works on the Sload. The two had met before at a dinner, though Telenger was noticeably displeased that the Sapiarch could not recall every line of his writings.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-LTI-81)</sup> He also took an interest in studying Khajiiti riddles.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Zuzik-82)</sup>

Telenger was also inspired after attending a stage production of *[The Lusty Argonian Maid](Lore_The_Lusty_Argonian_Maid.md)*. This led him to write *[The Argonian Maid—An Oral Tradition](Lore_The_Argonian_Maid%E2%80%94An_Oral_Tradition.md)*, in which he researched the complicated origins of the bawdy tale.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-TAMOT-83)</sup> His *[Enigma of the Runestones](Lore_Enigma_of_the_Runestones.md)* focused on the origins of the [runestones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Runestones) and their classification, as well as mentioning various scholars' debates on the subject.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-EOTR-79)</sup> According to Gabrielle Benele, Telenger's treatises on runic praxis discussed several possible origins of runestones, though none of his theories suggested a connection to Oblivion. Later, however, members of the Antiquarian Circle uncovered evidence indicating that runes could in fact transcend the veil between realms.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-DES-84)</sup>

In his *[Words and Power](Lore_Words_and_Power.md)*, Telenger proposed the theory that languages in Tamriel may possess inherent magical properties. Using examples such as runestones, enchanting glyphs, and historical texts, he argued that words and symbols might channel or invoke magical power even when not fully understood.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-WAP-80)</sup> In *[On Pircalmo's Emergency Reversal](Lore_On_Pircalmo%27s_Emergency_Reversal.md)*, Telenger wrote a farewell letter to one of his former students. In it, he offered encouragement and outlined a ritual meant to help Pircalmo on his new path as a mage.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OPER-14)</sup> He also provided answers to many scholarly questions through correspondence, particularly on topics such as [soul magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md), enchantment, as well as the Sload and the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) associated with them.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TTAAYQ-85)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-TTAAYQ2-86)</sup>

### Artifice and Spellcraft
Telenger the Artificer was also known for crafting a variety of curios and mechanical novelties. Among these were alchemical igniters, gold-plated devices designed to light smoking pipes.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TAAI-87)</sup> Another device attributed to him was the Perpetual Motion Gyroscope, said to spin endlessly despite having no visible source of power.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-TPMG-88)</sup> Both of these items bore Telenger's personal mark.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TAAI-87)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-TPMG-88)</sup> He also designed intricate toys such as clockwork soldiers, likely intended as gifts for the children of nobles.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-TCCTS-89)</sup> Other creations included the Artificer's Bolt, a lightning staff known for its scent of rain and marked with his personal sigil,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Artificer.27s Bolt-48)</sup> as well as the Artificer's Vestments, a garment he personally enchanted to resist all but the most powerful spells,<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-AV-59)</sup> both of which he gifted to the Vestige in gratitude for their assistance.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup>

Despite his mastery of magical artifice, Telenger was openly critical of using arcane magic to replace ordinary labor. On one occasion he mocked such excess, remarking that summoning a meteor to mine ore rather than performing the work properly was ostentatious and ridiculous.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-MM-90)</sup> He was also a creator of some spells, such as Wonderful Linking Cantrip.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OPER-14)</sup>

### Artifacts
Aside from a variety of tools, curios,<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TAAI-87)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-TPMG-88)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-TCCTS-89)</sup> magical weapons,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Artificer.27s Bolt-48)</sup> and armaments,<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-AV-59)</sup> Telenger also crafted multiple very powerful unique items.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-RDJ-70)</sup>

#### Clairvoyeur
The [Clairvoyeur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clairvoyeur) is a powerful device crafted by Teleger the Artificer for the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md). It can project a golden, glowing trail to a chosen person, provided a personal item is placed in its "Sniff Slot."

#### Hyperagonal Locational Determinator
The [Hyperagonal Locational Determinator](Lore_Hyperagonal_Locational_Determinator.md), also called **the Finder** was a device created by Telenger the Artificer around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It allowed the user to locate any location. When used alongside [the Projector](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telenger%27s_Projector) it allowed its wielder to teleport themselves anywhere they desired, even to places that were protected by magical defenses such as [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) and [The Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).

#### Telenger's Projector
[Telenger's Projector](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telenger%27s_Projector) (also called **the Projector**, real name unknown) is a powerful magical device created by Telenger the Artificer for the purposes of [teleportation](Lore_Teleportation.md). When used in conjunction with the [Hyperagonal Locational Determinator](Lore_Hyperagonal_Locational_Determinator.md), it allows its wielder to teleport to virtually any location they desired, including places protected by powerful magical defenses such as [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) and the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).
