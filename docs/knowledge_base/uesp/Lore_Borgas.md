Borgas of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), the last from the line of the [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) dynasty,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1S-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHo D-2)</sup> and [Harald](Lore_Harald.md)'s final descendant.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TCF-3)</sup> He is also believed to have been the last monarch to wear the legendary [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-4)</sup> During his reign, the traditional Nordic [pantheon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods#Pantheons) of deities was replaced in favor of a doctrine based on that of the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order). This act would become known as the "shadow of King Borgas" and lasted for over a hundred years until it was violently reversed by High King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

He was also one of [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md)'s chief supporters. As the prophet's teachings began to spread, hostility grew between the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). Borgas traveled to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to urge a joint war against the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), but fell victim to Valenwood's infamous [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) during the journey in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHo D-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3V-6)</sup> When he was killed, he was carrying his horn, called the "Horn of Borgas".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONOrthuna-7)</sup> [Eric of Guis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eric_of_Guis) wrote that he was killed for the "iniquities" of his Alessian faith.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-8)</sup> His body was presumed destroyed during the Hunt,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONOrthuna-7)</sup> but was secretly returned to Skyrim and buried with the Jagged Crown in the tomb of [Korvanjund](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Korvanjund).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-4)</sup>

Following his death, a [moot](Lore_Moot.md) was held to determine his successor as he had no children.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TCF-3)</sup> Supposedly, [Jarl Hanse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jarl_Hanse) of Winterhold was the obvious choice for succession; however, the Moot's failure to appoint him High King ultimately ignited the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md). Skyrim would spend the next fifty years in turmoil.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1S-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3V-6)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Galmar Stone-Fist, the second-in-command of the [Stormcloak](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) rebellion, learned of Borgas' place of burial while searching for the fabled Jagged Crown. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) recovered the crown from Korvanjund amid a scramble for its recovery between the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and the Stormcloaks, who fought in the ruins. Deep within the ruin, Borgas' body was found slumped on a throne wearing the crown—his ancient corpse having taken the form of a powerful [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md). The crown was recovered from Borgas, but it is unknown whether the crown was given to the Stormcloak leader [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) or the Imperial claimant, [Elisif the Fair](Lore_Elisif_the_Fair.md). Whatever the truth, it was said that the crown would have been a powerful symbol of legitimacy for either Jarl in their claim to the High Kingship of Skyrim.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Skyrim-9)</sup>
