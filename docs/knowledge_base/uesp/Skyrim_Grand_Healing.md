**Grand Healing** is an expert level [Restoration](Skyrim_Restoration.md) spell that will instantly [restore the health](Skyrim_Restore_Health.md) of the caster and every ally near them by 200 points.

## Effects
- [Restore Health Area](Skyrim_Restore_Health.md), 200 pts in 15 ft
- [Restore Stamina Area](Skyrim_Restore_Stamina.md), 200 pts in 15 ft; if the [Respite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Respite) perk is unlocked.
- [Restore Health](Skyrim_Restore_Health.md), 200 pts
- [Restore Stamina](Skyrim_Restore_Stamina.md), 200 pts; if the Respite perk is unlocked.

## Perks
- [Regeneration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Regeneration), increases health (and stamina with the Respite perk) restoration to 300.

## Notes
- This spell will not heal enemies within range, only your allies and neutral targets.

## Bugs
- The Ao E stamina healing effect unlocked when you have the Respite perk will heal the stamina of undead, machines, and daedra within range of the effect due to there being no condition checks to prevent it. The Ao E healing effect, on the other hand, will not affect undead, machines, or daedra within range. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.5, fixes this bug.
- Grand Healing has been known to heal your enemies (or your follower's enemies) when it's not supposed to. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.1, fixes this bug.
- Attacking an enemy while undetected will still allow the spell to heal them. Example: Hitting a Giant inside a camp, then casting Grand Healing will sometimes heal it. <sup>**?**</sup>
