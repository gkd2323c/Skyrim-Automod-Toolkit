# Sea Witches

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-npc-Minahel.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Minahel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Minahel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Minahel.jpg) A Sea Witch [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/ON-npc-Veshin.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Veshin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Veshin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Veshin.jpg) A Sea Witch Apprentice | *We assign tasks, perform rites, do some healing. I focus on defending Llanshara from intruders. No one escapes my notice.* <br> —Veshin<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Veshin-1)</sup> |
| --- |

Sea Witches are [witches](Lore_Witches.md) who serve as both religious figures and [magic](Lore_Magic.md) leaders among the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Umindior-2)</sup> They have many duties within Maormer society. They can be found among sailing crews,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Veshin-1)</sup> such as the Dreadsails,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Minahel-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DSW-4)</sup> where they provide magical assistance, such as infusing sailcloths with counter-magic spells. They also assign tasks, perform rites, provide healing, craft magical items, and, in some cases, defend their sites from intruders.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Veshin-1)</sup> They are known for their connection to the sea,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ellorien-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DCSS-6)</sup> and hold a certain authority within the crews, including the right to punish and imprison fellow crewmates.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ellorien-5)</sup> Some, in fact, acted as leaders of entire fleets.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AG-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FQT-8)</sup> Some Maormer believed that they saw themselves as superior to others due to their connection to the sea.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ellorien-5)</sup> They were known for their Weather Totems, capable of [altering the weather](Lore_Weather_Magic.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SWWT-9)</sup> Some of them are so connected to the ocean that the water itself responds to their call. One renowned Sea Witch, celebrating their sixth century on the waves, created a steed formed entirely out of water.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DCSS-6)</sup> Sea Witches fashioned [sea serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md) fangs into talismans of magical power.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AG-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Talisman-10)</sup>

Among their sites of study was Llanshara on the isle of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md). They studied within cavernous chambers set along the cliffside, which allowed them to observe other Dreadsails from above.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Veshin-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Quen-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DSWS-12)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), during their stay there, an ancient druidic relic was activated. The Sea Witches worked on a solution to undo its disastrous effects, which had compelled people toward [flirtatious and lewd behavior](Lore_Sex.md). Ultimately, the effects were resolved due to the intervention of an [adventurer](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MN-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TSRR-14)</sup>

## Notable Sea Witches

## Notes
- The term Sea Witch is also used outside Maormer culture. A notorious [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) pirate, Gjargrida, adopted the title of Sea Witch due to her unusual powers over the sea and wind.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-POTB-15)</sup> In a [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) text, *Dwynnen Lai* an unknown Sea Witch used an [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) mirror to turn [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) into loyal and devoted servants.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-16)</sup>
