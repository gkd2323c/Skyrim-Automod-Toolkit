# Familiar

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/LG-cardart-Conjurer%27s_Spirit.png/200px-LG-cardart-Conjurer%27s_Spirit.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Conjurer%27s_Spirit.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Conjurer%27s_Spirit.png) A Conjurer and their Familiars (Legends) *I heard an imp familiar escaped from the Mages Guild. They say that a wizard that loses his familiar is vulnerable to his enemies' spells.*
—A citizen of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loose-1)</sup> Familiar is a term for any creature that is used as a servant,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Origins-2)</sup> assistant,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CAC-3)</sup> or guardian by a [magic](Lore_Magic.md) user.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATRMAN-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AG-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Most familiars are [summoned](Lore_Conjuration.md) creatures such as [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SUFS-8)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SF-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Origins-2)</sup> or artificially created beings like [golems](Lore_Golem.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATRMAN-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Familiars can be used to perform a variety of tasks, such as serving as basic assistants or test subjects,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loose-1)</sup> some are used for more advanced tasks such as serving as vendors.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CAC-3)</sup> Many others are used for assisting in combat,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SF-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SUFS-8)</sup> guarding important locations,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VG-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ADHom-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ADTroll-12)</sup> or even protecting against the spells of their master's enemies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loose-1)</sup> Some familiars are treated more like pets than assistants.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Cat-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-14)</sup><sup>:315</sup>

## Types of Familiars
One of the most common forms of familiar is a specific type of conjured [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) [spirit](Lore_Spirit.md), that is synonymous with the name. They are summoned to assist their masters in combat or to help pick up scents.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SF-4)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> A flaming variation can also be summoned by more skilled mages.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SF-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/OB-interior-Frostcrag_Spire_Altars.jpg/200px-OB-interior-Frostcrag_Spire_Altars.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Frostcrag_Spire_Altars.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Frostcrag_Spire_Altars.jpg) The Atronach Altar of Frostcrag Spire (Oblivion) Common lesser Daedra used as familiars include [Scamps](Lore_Scamp.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SUFS-8)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> [Banekin](Lore_Banekin.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> [Clannfear](Lore_Clannfear.md),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Kyne C-19)</sup><sup>:159</sup> and various [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FSM-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AFs-21)</sup> Scamps are most commonly used for simple tasks like gruntwork,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> but some could be entrusted with more complex tasks. One Scamp familiar was even granted full membership in the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), serving as an assistant chronicler and merchant.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CAC-3)</sup> Atronachs were among the first Daedra used as familiars by [Clan Direnni](Lore_Clan_Direnni.md) during the early development of Conjuration magic.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Origins-2)</sup> The Atronach Altar of [Frostcrag Spire](Lore_Frostcrag_Spire.md) can be used to summon and bind [Flame](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md), [Frost](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md), and [Storm Atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md) as permanent familiars.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AFs-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FSM-20)</sup>

[Imps](Lore_Imp.md) are another common type of familiar,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATRMAN-5)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> they were often used as such by the Mages Guild.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loose-1)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Rogue-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> They are occasionally used by powerful wizards as vault guardians,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VG-10)</sup> similarly to [homonculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Homonculus) and [trolls](Lore_Troll.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ADHom-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ADTroll-12)</sup> Imp familiars have been subject to cruel experimentation by their masters, and have been known to go rogue in response.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loose-1)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Rogue-24)</sup> [Gremlins](Lore_Gremlin.md) are another small intelligent species who were used as familiars by the Mages Guild.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

Animals are quite popular as familiars. [Witches](Lore_Witch.md) often use black [cats](Lore_Cat.md) as familiars.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Cat-13)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Mages in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), such as those of the [Shad Astula Academy](Lore_Shad_Astula.md), have utilized conjured familiars in the forms of [kwama](Lore_Kwama.md), [guar](Lore_Guar.md), [kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md), and [pigs](Lore_Pig.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Other animals used as familiars include [nighthawks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nighthawk),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Real B4-29)</sup> [bears](Lore_Bear.md),<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup> [scuttlers](Lore_Scuttler.md),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Kyne C-19)</sup><sup>:102</sup> [mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Kyne C-19)</sup><sup>:88</sup> and [frostbite spiders](Lore_Frostbite_Spider.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-14)</sup><sup>:315</sup>

## Notable Familiars
- [The Crow](Lore_The_Crow.md) is a being known as a [Luminary](Lore_Luminary.md), who was born from the essence of [Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md) and served as her familiar.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Crow-31)</sup>
- [Skakmat](Lore_Skakmat.md) is a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) who served as the familiar of the renowned mystic [Nulfaga](Lore_Nulfaga.md), the Dowager Queen of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DFCN-32)</sup>
