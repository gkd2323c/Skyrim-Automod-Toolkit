# Thalmor


In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the new incarnation of the Thalmor is a shadowy cabal that rules the Dominion, dedicated to Elven supremacy and "the pacification and purification of all of Tamriel – to bring about a new [Merethic era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md)".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LGLoading-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:154</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/ON-prerelease-Divine_Prosecution_02.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Divine_Prosecution_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Divine_Prosecution_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Divine_Prosecution_02.jpg) Agents of the Divine Prosecution The Thalmor began as an obscure Altmeri council within the government of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) that was tasked with safeguarding Altmeri heritage. With the ascension of [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) to the Altmeri throne and the establishment of the First Aldmeri Dominion in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), the Thalmor's role was expanded to become the executive arm of the alliance, a bureaucracy that managed the Dominion's day-to-day affairs, investigate and enforce Dominion law, and liaised between its various local leaders and factions. It incorporated representatives from all three races in the Dominion's territory, in ratios appropriate to their administrative abilities. The Thalmor Inner Council served as Ayrenn's personal advisors, and included such notables as King [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran) of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and Lord Gharesh-ri of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), as well as a number of other Bosmeri and Khajiiti nobles who were included largely as figureheads.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOSAYQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Parwinel-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Some organizations that existed prior to the Thalmor being elevated into a governmental position were later controlled by, or turned into direct divisions of the Thalmor. Two examples being the 'Fists of Thalmor'<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOSAYQ-3)</sup> and the [Divine Prosecution](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divine_Prosecution).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Carawen-6)</sup>

A secret sub-organization composed of Thalmor members was active at the same time: the [Ceythalmor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceythalmor), meaning "Shadow Thalmor" in old Ayleidoon. Its objective was to get the Dominion as close to "perfection" as possible and to create a "perfect Dominion". It attempted a coup in order to assassinate queen Ayrenn and have her replaced by a member of the royal family affiliated with the Ceythalmor, but failed against the Garland, an organization of spies, which was forced to reveal itself to save Ayrenn. It was linked to the [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance), another faction promoting elven supremacy.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Garland-7)</sup>

## Second Aldmeri Dominion
During the Second Aldmeri Dominion, formed in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830), the Altmer reconstituted the Thalmor as the new governing body of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). They claimed they had the authority to create this government on behalf of a claimant to Valenwood's throne, [Camoran Anaxemes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camoran_Anaxemes), whose ancestors had signed an ancient treaty with the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-8)</sup> As Valenwood previously possessed no true, unifying government, the Thalmor purported to maintain order in the region to uplift their Bosmer brethren. In reality, the formation of the Thalmor was far from an altruistic gesture: the Altmer desired to control Valenwood because the region, along with southern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), harbored various pirate groups which had long threatened the Summerset Isles. Fed up with the constant pirate attacks, the Altmer hoped to secure the shores of Summerset for good by denying the pirates any safe haven from which to attack them.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3V-9)</sup> A significant figure within the Second Aldmeri Dominion, [Andel Crodo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Andel_Crodo), enforced a policy of non-intervention in non-Dominion lands, which few on the Thalmor sought to oppose.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1PR-10)</sup> The Thalmor was dissolved a short time later when [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) brought Valenwood into his [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3V-9)</sup>

## Third Aldmeri Dominion

### Rise
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1b/LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg) Thalmor Justiciar (Legends) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-icon-map-Thalmor.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-icon-map-Thalmor.png) Thalmor symbol Sometime prior to the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Thalmor resurfaced as a radical and xenophobic political movement that espoused the absolute supremacy of Elves over all other Tamrielic peoples. For years they were largely ignored, but in the aftermath of the Oblivion Crisis their role in the defense of Summerset enabled them to proclaim themselves the saviors of the Altmeri people,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> which greatly enhanced their influence.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

In [4E 6](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_6), the Thalmor incited the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to mount a massive uprising against Morrowind and the Empire that was known as the [Accession War](Lore_Accession_War.md). [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) was lost to the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), but the Thalmor too lost what influence they had over the Argonians. In [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), the Thalmor overthrew the Altmeri monarchy and seized control of the Summerset Isles, renaming them Alinor.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-RT4-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TGW-14)</sup>

Valenwood was brought under the Thalmor's control in 4E 29, creating the Third Aldmeri Dominion.

The [Night of Green Fire](Lore_Night_of_Green_Fire.md) occurred in 4E 42, when the Thalmor attacked Altmer dissident refugees who had fled persecution in Alinor and were living in the city of Sentinel, in Hammerfell. According to a Legion officer claiming to be an eyewitness, there was an explosion of magic in the refugee quarter, where Thalmor mages were attacking the Altmer dissidents who, in turn, were resisting with magic of their own. Running to the scene with other Legionaries who were stationed there, the eyewitness found the entire refugee quarter a smoking ruin, and that everyone had been slaughtered. This night appears to have been part of a broader effort by the Thalmor, whose agents harassed the refugee communities in Sentinel and Balfiera, were somehow involved in Elsweyr, funded insurgent groups,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup> and were connected to a pattern of murders where the victims were all of mixed blood or had associations considered by the Aldmeri Dominion to be "unclean".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lo S-15)</sup>

In [4E 47](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_47), Prime Minister [Hierem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hierem) made a secret trip to Black Marsh to negotiate with the An-Xileel leaders supposedly to propose an alliance against the Thalmor.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lo S-15)</sup><sup>:65</sup> Intendant Marall of the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md) had Colin Vineben investigate if the Thalmor had summoned [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md), but he could find no evidence.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lo S-15)</sup>

The [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr.md) voluntarily dissolved and reformed the ancient kingdoms of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) and [Pelletine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelletine) in [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115), which then became client states of the Thalmor.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TGW-14)</sup>

### Great War
It is said that of all of his enemies, Emperor [Titus Mede I](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md) never managed to defeat the Thalmor,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup> and it would be [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) who would pay the price. When Titus II inherited the Imperial throne, the Thalmor instigated the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171) by delivering a series of ultimatums to the Imperial City, then presenting the head of every [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) agent who had been stationed in the Aldmeri Dominion when Titus II refused their demands. After four years of bloody war, a stalemate was reached. The Emperor was forced to sign the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md), essentially giving in to the Thalmor's original demands. One of the Concordat's terms mandated that the Empire relinquish large tracts of land in southern Hammerfell to the Dominion, which outraged the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) and resulted in the secession of Hammerfell from the Empire. Fighting continued in Hammerfell, but the Redguards eventually forced the Dominion to the negotiation table and the [Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_Second_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md), which stipulated that Aldmeri forces withdraw from Hammerfell, was signed in [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180). This led the Redguards to believe that the Concordat was a mistake, and Titus II had overestimated the Thalmor's strength when he signed it. Another term of the Concordat was the outlawing of the worship of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), and the right of the Thalmor to enforce that ban within the Empire.

### Rivercrest
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/BL-npc-Aranande.jpg/200px-BL-npc-Aranande.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-npc-Aranande.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-npc-Aranande.jpg) Justiciar Aranande [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/BL-banner-Thalmor Banner.png/135px-BL-banner-Thalmor Banner.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-banner-Thalmor Banner.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-banner-Thalmor Banner.png) Thalmor Banner Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), members of the Thalmor lead by Justiciar Aranande arrived in [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) and other surrounding towns and villages, where they stated they were investigating for Blades agents. Henrik and the Warrior worked to hide their identities by leading the Thalmor on suicide missions and giving them false leads, with creatures such as bears and undead Ayleids killing Thalmor soldiers as they fell for these tricks.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> The Thalmor were also tipped off on the location of a temple of Talos and intended to kill the worshippers there, however, the Warrior discovered the location of the Talos worshippers and warned them before the Thalmor could get to them.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> After this, the Warrior also managed to kill a Thalmor messenger before they could relay information on who the hidden Blades were.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Eventually the Thalmor discovered the identity of Henrik and had him killed, but the Warrior avenged him by killing Justiciar Aranande.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

### Skyrim Civil War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/SR-quest-Diplomatic_Immunity_05.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Diplomatic_Immunity_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Diplomatic_Immunity_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Diplomatic_Immunity_05.jpg) Thalmor torturing a prisoner [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg) Elenwen, First Emissary of the Thalmor in Skyrim (Skyrim) In the aftermath of the [Markarth Incident](Lore_The_Bear_of_Markarth.md) in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176), the Empire was forced to maintain the White-Gold Concordat by cracking down on the worship of Talos in Skyrim, where the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) were known to especially revere Talos as both a god and a folk hero. The Empire permitted the Thalmor to operate inside Skyrim and suppress Talos worship when it had previously gone largely ignored. By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Thalmor were using the Concordat as a justification to carry out a violent campaign of persecution against Talos worshippers from their embassy in northwest Skyrim, this persecution being a notable factor which led to the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SKAlvor-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-dossierulfric-22)</sup> The Thalmor's branch in Skyrim at this time was overseen by [Elenwen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elenwen), and they were known to secretly capture and imprison any Nords who questioned their doctrines or beliefs.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SRLoading-23)</sup> As of that time, there were reports indicating that Thalmor justiciars openly expressed hostility towards the Nords, with many individuals advocating for a genocidal agenda.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-J-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup> At that time, important figures within both the Dominion and Empire believed that the Thalmor were preparing for a second war.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Tullius-26)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Ondolemar-27)</sup>

The Thalmor were unsure which side of the Civil War to support, though officially they supported the Empire in the name of stability. While they believed an independent Skyrim would weaken the Empire whom they were planning renewed conflict, an independent Skyrim led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) would have meant the end of their influence within the province they were enjoying. As such they adopted a policy to attempt to prolong the conflict for as long as possible. Though direct intervention was disincentivized, an exception was made to attempt to save Ulfric when he was captured for execution by Imperial forces.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-dossierulfric-22)</sup> This stands in contrast to cocurrent rumors that the Thalmor had assisted in Ulfric's capture.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Prima-28)</sup><sup>:347</sup>

The sudden [return of the Dragons](Lore_Return_of_the_Dragons.md) was blamed on the Thalmor by remnants of the Blades within Skyrim. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) infiltrated a party at the Thalmor Embassy on these grounds, only to find the Thalmor as in the dark as the Blades were.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Delphine SR-29)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DPImmunity-30)</sup>

A Thalmor agent, [Ancano](Lore_Ancano.md), advised the Arch-Mage of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) at his pleasure.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Ancano-31)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Mirabelle-32)</sup> Another agent assisted Ancano in his plot to secure the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Estormo-33)</sup>

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a Thalmor crew landed on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), sent on orders to retrieve weapons made of [Stalhrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stalhrim).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Ancarion-34)</sup>

In addition to the Thalmor Embassy in [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), the Thalmor in Skyrim maintained a headquarters within Castle Dour and a fortress known as Northwatch Keep.
