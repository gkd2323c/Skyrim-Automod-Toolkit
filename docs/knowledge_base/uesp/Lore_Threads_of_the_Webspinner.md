# Threads of the Webspinner

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/56/MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg/200px-MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Threads_of_the_Webspinner.jpg) The Threads of the Webspinner The Threads of the Webspinner are 27 pieces of extravagant enchanted clothing and jewelry associated with both [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md). The story goes that Mephala sought to reward her devoted followers within the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), and turned to Sanguine to create them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Eno MW-1)</sup> Each Thread enhances a single skill, such as weapon skills, speechcraft, and magic.

## History
At an unknown time, the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) stole the Threads of the Webspinner from the Morag Tong, but Mephala's subtle manipulations would draw them back into [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Eno MW-1)</sup> The Brotherhood gained a foothold in both Morrowind's mainland, and in the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), with those stationed on the island having the artifacts. On Vvardenfell, they negotiated with the cult of Mehrunes Dagon so they could be sheltered by them in their associated [Daedric ruins](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) throughout Vvardenfell. Eno Hlaalu, Grandmaster of the Morag Tong, took notice and began ordering an assassin to wipe out the strongholds of the Brotherhood, and recovering the threads. Eno heard about a powerful Dagonite, an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) by the name of Carecalmo, whose influence with the cult of Mehrunes Dagon was great, and ordered the agent to offer an ultimatum to the him to stop providing refuge for the Brotherhood, but the cultist attacked the agent of Mephala for daring to command him. One of the Threads was recovered from Carecalmo's corpse and given to Eno Hlaalu for safekeeping.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWUC-2)</sup>

The assassin would continue recovering the Threads for the Tong and wiping out the Brotherhood and the Dagonites from Vvardenfell. The culmination of this task was the writ of execution being issued for [Severa Magia](Lore_Severa_Magia.md), the Night Mother of the Dark Brotherhood. The assassin was sent to the ruins of [Ald Sotha](Lore_Ald_Sotha.md), which served as the headquarters of the Brotherhood in Vvardenfell. There, the Tong infiltrated the ruins and executed the Severa Magia, which helped to put an end to the Dark Brotherhood's activities on Vvardenfell. From the aftermath of the battle, the Tong liberated six Threads of the Webspinner.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWESM-3)</sup>

Eventually all the Threads were discovered. After eradicating the Dark Brotherhood and the Dagonites from Vvardenfell, the Threads were given to Eno Hlaalu for safekeeping, and the assassin was blessed with Mephala's Skill as a reward.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWTW-4)</sup> In their desire to advance to the rank of Grandmaster, it's unknown if the assassin allowed Eno Hlaalu to live and retire to [Vounoura](Lore_Vounoura.md), and take the Threads with him, or slew him to advance per tradition, and retrieved the Threads for themselves.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWG-5)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Amulet of Sanguine Enterprise
The Amulet of Sanguine Enterprise made its wearer more skilled in trade and commerce. By 3E 427, it was already in the possession of Eno Hlaalu.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AOSE-6)</sup>

### Amulet of Sanguine Glib Speech
The Amulet of Sanguine Glib Speech made its wearer skilled in persuasion and speechcraft. By 3E 427, it belonged to Shotherra of [Khuul](Lore_Khuul.md), before it was claimed by the member of the Morag Tong.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AOSG-7)</sup>

### Amulet of Sanguine Nimble Armor
The Amulet of Sanguine Nimble Armor made its wearer more skilled at using light armor. By 3E 427, it belonged to Severa Magia, before it was claimed by the member of the Morag Tong.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AOSNA-8)</sup>

### Belt of Sanguine Balanced Armor
The Belt of Sanguine Balanced Armor made its wearer more skilled at using medium armor. By 3E 427, it belonged to Relas Arothan, before it was claimed by the member of the Morag Tong.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AOSBA-9)</sup>

### Belt of Sanguine Deep Biting
The Belt of Sanguine Deep Biting made its wearer more skilled at using axes. By 3E 427, it belonged to Relas Arothan, before it was claimed by the member of the Morag Tong.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AOSDB-10)</sup>

### Belt of Sanguine Denial
The Belt of Sanguine Denial made its wearer more skilled at blocking the incoming strikes. By 3E 427, it belonged to Movis Darys,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BOSD-11)</sup> before he gave it to the member of the Morag Tong as a sign of a good faith.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MD-12)</sup>

### Belt of Sanguine Fleetness
