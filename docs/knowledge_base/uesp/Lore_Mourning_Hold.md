Mournhold (also known as **Mourning Hold**)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-36LOVS8-4)</sup> is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARMap-5)</sup> located in the heart of the [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) region. The city is perhaps the largest and potentially oldest in Morrowind, and the district seat of the [Great House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), staunch followers of Dunmer faith and firm believers in preserving their native culture.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-UHI-8)</sup> It is also the epicenter of Dunmer religion, where the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) reigned over the province<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> until they were replaced by the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TR-9)</sup>

The author [Enric Milres](Lore_Enric_Milres.md) described the city as "dark and brooding",<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TA-10)</sup> but to others, it is noted for its grand vastness and majestic architecture.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POM-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-EGT-11)</sup> For many years, it was the home of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), one of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god-kings of Morrowind.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> As her holy city, it attracted pilgrims and temple priests to her palace, [Mournhold Temple](Lore_Mournhold_Temple.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> Both the city and the greater one that surrounds it are known as **[Almalexia](#The_City_of_Almalexia_and_Mournhold.27s_environs)**.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BOB1-13)</sup> For much of the Dunmer's history, Mournhold had been the capital of Morrowind<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> until the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), when it was moved to [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DBAdril-14)</sup> The city is known by many titles, such as the **City of Light and Magic**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TRGen Voiced-3)</sup> the **City of Gems**,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRB1-15)</sup> and the **City of Love and Charity**.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MWGen Rumors-16)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e4/TR-map-Mournhold.jpg/200px-TR-map-Mournhold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-map-Mournhold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-map-Mournhold.jpg) Map of Mournhold in the late Third Era [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/TR-place-Mournhold%2C_Great_Bazaar.jpg/200px-TR-place-Mournhold%2C_Great_Bazaar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Mournhold,_Great_Bazaar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Mournhold,_Great_Bazaar.jpg) The Great Bazaar ca. 3E 427 Mournhold is located in south-central Morrowind, in the [Lagomere](Lore_Lagomere.md) region of Deshaan.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONMournhold-17)</sup> The region is described as a broad and fertile plain that slowly shifts into the swamps of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> Mournhold lies in its very center, surrounded by large mountains, low-lying wetlands, a river that flows west beside the city. Many of the region's roads converge here, whether it is from the northwest by the Obsidian Gorge or the main southern road.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONMournhold-17)</sup> Outside the city lies a crawling area of homes, citizens, vendors,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SSLW-18)</sup> as well as noble estates, such as Quondam Indorilia, a luxury manor for a noble Indoril family,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONQuondam-19)</sup> and even King Hlaalu Helseth's palace.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AGAD-20)</sup>

There is a lot of buried ruins and subterranean complexes under Mournhold, such as the ruins of [Bamz-Amschend](Lore_Bamz-Amschend.md) and Norenen-dur, and the caves of [Old Mournhold](Lore_Old_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TRMournhold-21)</sup> Mournhold is neighbored by several settlements, such as [Amber Forest](Lore_Amber_Forest.md) to the east, [Darnim Watch](Lore_Darnim_Watch.md) to the north, [Eagle Moor](Lore_Eagle_Moor.md) to the west, [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) to the west-northwest, Old Keep to the southwest, and [Silnim Dale](Lore_Silnim_Dale.md) to the south.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARMap-5)</sup>

Mournhold is noted by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) chronicler, [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) for its *"graceful green spires, soaring basalt archways, and golden finery along even the most modest buildings."*<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-EGT-11)</sup> Mournhold proper is centered around the large, central building. In the mid-Second Era, this building was the City Center, which housed its bank and administration,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONMournhold-17)</sup> but in the late-Third Era, this was the Royal Palace, the city's seat of power.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TRMournhold-21)</sup> Generally speaking, south of its center is [Plaza Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONMournhold-17)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TRMap-22)</sup> an open parkland where Mournhold's people come to seek solace and pray to the Tribunal.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> Its main landmark was a statue depicting Almalexia and Mehrunes Dagon's legendary battle in the late First Era, but in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), House Indoril erected a new building there to house pilgrims and priests.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TMFN-23)</sup>

Mournhold has several markets and bazaars,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> like the Royal Bazaar and the Mourner's Market.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONMournhold-17)</sup> But the Great Bazaar is the city's largest.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> In the late Third Era, it was located east of the Royal Palace and was divided by a large bridge. The eastern half housed several establishments, while the western half featured an outdoor theater and several merchant stalls. Its residential district is known as Godsreach, and it was west of the Royal Palace in the late Third Era. It contained several noble manors and a building for artisans called the Craftsmen's Hall.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TRMournhold-21)</sup> North of the city's center is Mournhold Temple, the former seat of power of Almalexia. Ever since her downfall, renovations were made to remove her mark and a new sanctuary was added.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TMFN-23)</sup> It is surrounded by the Temple Courtyard, a wide, green parkland tended to by gardeners. The foliage and trimmed paths were considered hymns of praise to Almalexia.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRPop-24)</sup>

### The City of Almalexia and Mournhold's environs
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/LG-cardart-Mournhold_Pilgrim.png/200px-LG-cardart-Mournhold_Pilgrim.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Mournhold_Pilgrim.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Mournhold_Pilgrim.png) A Tribunal pilgrim in Mournhold **Almalexia** is the larger city that surrounds Mournhold and is named after the Dunmer goddess, Almalexia, who made the city her home.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> It was built in response to [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s growing power in the late years of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Zeni Max Interview-29)</sup> Almalexia is described as grand, in a vast, sprawling way<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POM-2)</sup> while Mournhold is described by the 1st Edition of the *[Pocket Guide to the Empire](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire.md)* as a sprawling temple/palace, a city within a city.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup>

The **Kingdom of Mournhold** refers to the political region that surrounds the city.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BOB1-13)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup> The Lorddom of Mournhold owns several mines across the kingdom, and its people often work there as laborers.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRB1-15)</sup> According to *The Real Barenziah* series, the Staff of Chaos was supposedly kept in [Ephen](Lore_Ephen.md)'s tomb hidden in the kingdom's mines.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup>

Similarly, Almalexia is the central location for the Indoril District, the political territory ruled by House Indoril. In the late Third Era, this land encompassed the Morrowind heartland, from the southern [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) coast to the eastern mainland coast.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup> Some locations historically under their governance include [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md),<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONDavon-31)</sup> [Draloris](Lore_Draloris.md),<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MWMap Concept-32)</sup> and [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONGivyn-33)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/TR-interior-Manor_District_%28East_Building%29.jpg/200px-TR-interior-Manor_District_%28East_Building%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-interior-Manor_District_(East_Building).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-interior-Manor_District_(East_Building).jpg) The ruins of the original Mournhold *The rivalry between Ebonheart and Mournhold reached back almost to the dawn of Morrowind's history. Once the two nations had been one, all the lucrative mines held in fief by the Ra'athims, whose nobility retained the High Kingship of Morrowind. Ebonheart had split into two separate city-states, Ebonheart and Mournhold, when Queen Lian's twin sons -- grandsons of the legendary King Moraelyn -- were left as joint heirs.*
—An excerpt from *[The Real Barenziah, v 5](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah,_v_5.md)* Mournhold's earliest history is unclear, surrounded by hearsay and contradictory sources. The 1st Edition of the *Pocket Guide to the Empire* claims that Mournhold is the oldest city in Morrowind;<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> however, *[The Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md)* book series claims that [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) is the eldest city-state, with its rulers often quoted saying, *"first among equals"*.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup>

Modern Mournhold is also built over the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city of [Bamz-Amschend](Lore_Bamz-Amschend.md). The rumor of its existence has been repeatedly denied by the people of Mournhold,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> but the ruins are undoubtedly beneath it. Bamz-Amschend's history before the Dwemer's disappearance is unknown, though it hosted a Dwemer Council Meet.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CON-34)</sup> Mournhold is also known as the lost city-state of the [First Empire](Lore_First_Empire_(Mournhold).md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AR-25)</sup> but what this is referring to is unknown.

Mournhold and Ebonheart's rivalry dates back to the dawn of Morrowind's history. They were originally part of one nation ruled by the [Ra'athim Clan](Lore_Ra%27athim.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup> a noble family from Ebonheart with an affinity for mining. One of Mournhold's earliest known natives is [Ephen](Lore_Ephen.md), remembered now as a deity of a temple cult and but in life was the step-brother to the Ra'athim Kings, [Cruethys](Lore_Cruethys.md) and [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-KEVII-35)</sup> The Ra'athim nation was eventually split between two city-states, modern Ebonheart and Mournhold, when Queen Lian's sons, grandsons of King Moraelyn, became joint heirs.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup> The Ra'athim Clan was affiliated with [House Mora](Lore_House_Mora.md), but by the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Mournhold was ruled by House Indoril. The Great House was founded sometime in the early First Era<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-UHI-8)</sup> but its early history with Mournhold is unknown.

The earliest dated information comes from [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s [thirty-six lessons](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md). Mourning Hold was the capital of Veloth, referring to ancient Morrowind, and was located in the lands of House Indoril.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-36LOVS5-36)</sup> When [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) arrived with the [simulacrum](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) that bore Vivec, they were greeted by [Ayem](Lore_Ayem.md), the Queen of Mournhold and her "husband-state", a flickering image channeled to her needs. She ruled with her council called [the Shouts](Lore_The_Shouts.md), a now forgotten guild who carried the whims of the [Velothi](Lore_Chimer.md) people.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-36LOVS8-4)</sup><sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Ayem)</sup> She also commanded the Orphanage, a legion that assisted Nerevar in a battle with rebellious Dwemer.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-36LOVS30-37)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-WMBTM-38)</sup> Shortly after this was the legendary [Conquest of Morrowind](Lore_Conquest_of_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-36LOVS8-4)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-36LOVS9-39)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-PGE1S-40)</sup> one of many battles in the [Skyrim Conquests](Lore_Skyrim_Conquests.md) between [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240) and [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TLST-41)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TDC-42)</sup><sup>:5</sup>

The [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) was a significant moment in ancient Morrowind's history. The conflict began when [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) erupted for the first time in [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668), known in history as [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md). The eruption laid waste to at least half of Morrowind, causing a permanent shift of population south into Deshaan,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> where Mournhold presides.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONMournhold-17)</sup> The Dwemer and their automatons attacked Mournhold, destroying its eleven gates. According to Vivec's [thirty-sixth sermon](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_36.md), Almalexia retaliated by turning into the *"Face-Snaked Queen of Three in One,"* and those who looked upon her were *"overcome by the meaning of [stars](Lore_Stars.md)"*.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-36LOVS36-26)</sup>

### The Fall of Mournhold and Almalexia's Governance
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_03.jpg/200px-MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_03.jpg) The Statue of Almalexia and Mehrunes Dagon's battle [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/ON-concept-Tribunal_Temple.png/200px-ON-concept-Tribunal_Temple.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Tribunal_Temple.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Tribunal_Temple.png) The Tribunal Temple of Mournhold By the late years of the First Era, Mournhold was ruled by Duke-Prince [Indoril Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Brindisi_Dorom.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-2920V8-43)</sup> As the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) raged at this time, the Duke often conducted slave-raids in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), creating a particular grudge with Warchief [Ulaqth](Lore_Ulaqth.md) of [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-2920V3-44)</sup> This manifested in the Battle of [Bodrum](Lore_Bodrum.md) when Ulaqth's flank attacked a company waving Mournhold's flag. The Imperial Army was defeated when they fell into a wall of spears in the [Pryai River](Lore_Pryai_River.md). Against the Tribunal's wishes, Brindisi Dorom met with the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md), leader of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), to plan out Emperor [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md)'s assassination, but officially, it was never fulfilled.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-2920V8-43)</sup>

The Duke was present with the Tribunal and Prince [Juilek](Lore_Juilek.md) in Fervinthil to sign the [Cervant Truce](Lore_Cervant_Truce.md), and later in Ald Lambasi to celebrate the end of the Four-Score War.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-2920V8-43)</sup> In early [Sun's Dusk](Lore_Sun%27s_Dusk.md), a red mist hung over Mournhold as lightning flashed in the sky and hard winds bellowed across the city. Then a gout of ever-erupting fire burst out of Castle Mournhold, and heavy smoke filled the streets, igniting anything into fire. [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) had been summoned to Tamriel and unleashed his army of [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) onto Mournhold. Citizens were plucked from their hiding places and thrown to their deaths.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-2920V11-45)</sup>

Mournhold tried in vain to fight back, like those who defended Mournhold Temple, but many of its people, including Duke Brindisi Dorom, were lost. The only thing that kept the city from burning was the sputtered blood of its people.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-2920V11-45)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ONSelveni-46)</sup> In a thin needle of light, Almalexia and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) appeared before Mehrunes Dagon and engaged in combat. Almalexia unsheathed her blade [Hopesfire](Lore_Hopesfire.md) and stabbed him in his breast, while Sotha Sil lashed at him with whips of [god-bronze](Lore_Bronze.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-2920V11-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TTIS8-47)</sup> Chunks of his flesh fell and turned into a multitude of creatures now called [Ruinachs](Lore_Ruinach.md).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TTIS8-47)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-BPTMR-48)</sup> Now with a large wave of daedra, Almalexia singlehandedly killed them as Sotha Sil unleashed a devastating blow. But Dagon landed a final attack on Sotha Sil's jaw.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TTIS8-47)</sup>

The Battle of Mournhold ended when Sotha Sil whispered a final [chrononymic](Lore_Nymic.md) death-word and exploded Dagon back into Oblivion. His banishment caused a rift between Tamriel and Oblivion until Sotha Sil sealed it tightly,<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TTIS8-47)</sup> but by then, Dagon had taken so much of Mournhold back with him. The [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of heroes in the battle, namely Ordinator Nilera and Priest Sovisa were sent to False Martyrs' Folly in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md),<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ONSelveni-46)</sup> and [chunks of the city](Lore_Wretched_Spire.md) itself were flung into Oblivion, along with any citizens trapped in them.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ONFaven-49)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-WTSF-50)</sup>

After the battle, Almalexia and Sotha Sil healed in [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md)<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-2920V11-45)</sup> as Mournhold's people were left to rebuild. Almalexia returned to the city and found inspiration in both the people's spirits and the resilience of its plantlife. Brindisi Dorom's heir was coming from the north to take his father's place. It was there she decided to make the city her home and believed it had a bright future ahead.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-2920V12-51)</sup> How long it took for Mournhold to rebuild is unknown. The modern Mournhold and its sewers were built over the ruins of the previous one, now known as [Old Mournhold](Lore_Old_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TREffe-52)</sup> One of the city's current districts became known as Plaza Brindisi Dorom, likely in honor of the former Duke and a statue depicting Almalexia and Dagon's battle was erected in its center.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TRMournhold-21)</sup>

### Mournhold in the Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/ON-place-City_Center_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-City_Center_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-City_Center_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-City_Center_02.jpg) The Mournhold City Center ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/ON-concept-Ebonheart_Pact_banner.png/145px-ON-concept-Ebonheart_Pact_banner.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Ebonheart_Pact_banner.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Ebonheart_Pact_banner.png) Banner of the Ebonheart Pact Mournhold was an active settlement in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). After the Sack of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), the invading [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md), led by [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md), traveled south and bypassed [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) to target Mournhold.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-JTSK-53)</sup> Having sensed the impending attack, Almalexia made a widespread call-to-arms that brought Dunmer from across Morrowind and their occupied territories.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-FATS-54)</sup> The Tribunal Army, led by the Indoril Grandmaster, [Tanval Indoril](Lore_Tanval_Indoril.md) and Almalexia staged a fighting retreat into the ashlands and eventually corner them in central [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-TSAI-55)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-EGTS-56)</sup>

At the [Battle of Vivec's Antlers](Lore_Battle_of_Vivec%27s_Antlers.md) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), the Dunmer, together with the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) and the free [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of northern [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) defeated the Akaviri invaders.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-EGT-11)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-JTSK-53)</sup> An agreement was signed in Ebonheart for the three races to unite and the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was created thereafter,<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-UA-57)</sup> with Mournhold chosen as its capital. From there, the Pact's chosen leader, [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn_the_Skald-King.md) presided over the [Great Moot](Lore_Great_Moot.md), a council that represented the three equal races.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-AUAEP2-58)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ITTLOTESO-59)</sup>

Among the Tribunal, Almalexia was especially involved in the Pact.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-AUAEP2-58)</sup> Leaders of the Pact, such as [Keshu the Black Fin](Lore_Keshu_the_Black_Fin.md) frequented Mournhold and camped outside the city for months as they discussed the alliance and its affairs.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TBFFA2-60)</sup> The Tribunal then received an intense vision, and as tensions elevated in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), they insisted on the Pact to form an army and prepare for war, just as the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) and the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) did. The [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) was in full effect.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-UA-57)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TBFFA4-61)</sup> At some point in time, a mer radicalized by [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) named Meram Vox tried to steal the [Judgment of Veloth](Lore_Veloth%27s_Judgment.md) from Mournhold Temple and in doing so, killed many temple faithful. He was eventually caught and put to death by Almalexia.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ONAspera-62)</sup>

### The Llodos Plague and Late Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7f/ON-npc-Magistrix_Vox.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Magistrix_Vox.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Magistrix_Vox.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Magistrix_Vox.jpg) Magistrix Urili Vox In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a devastating disease known as the [Llodos Plague](Lore_Llodos_Plague.md) began to spread across Deshaan, causing uproar in the region. Many people from Narsis fled east to Mournhold to escape the plague<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-AJ-63)</sup> and to keep order, Mournhold enforced a law for citizens to register at the city center.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-ONRegister-64)</sup> The Maulborn Cult, who created the plague gathered in the Mournhold Sewers, preparing to make their move on the city. After registering, [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) received a formal invitation to meet Almalexia in Mournhold Temple. There they informed her about the Maulborn, and with her blessing, began an investigation with the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) agent, [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ONFavor-65)</sup>

Their sleuthing brought them to the [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) farmstead, where they found a hatch to the Mournhold Sewers. Delving deeper, they confronted the Maulborn Commander, Kalara and her captive Farona Telvanni. Just then, Almalexia intervened and used powerful magic to destroy the Maulborn.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONUnderground-66)</sup> But as that happened, the Maulborn's leader, Magistrix [Urili Vox](Lore_Urili_Vox.md) used daedric magic to open Mournhold Temple's wards, allowing her cultists and daedra to storm its halls. Her plan was to defile the temple, steal countless holy artifacts, and let the daedra loose into Mournhold.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONThree-67)</sup>

In the High Chapel, the Vestige defeated the Maulborn's champion, Vanus Sathram and restored Mournhold Temple, but Magistrix Vox made off with the Judgment of Veloth and fled east into the [Siltreen](Lore_Siltreen.md) region. For their heroism, Almalexia named the Vestige one of her [Hands](Lore_Hands_of_Almalexia.md) and let them pursue Vox, with their first stop being Selfora.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONThree-67)</sup> Ultimately, Magistrix Vox was defeated in Eidolon's Hollow and the Judgment of Veloth was safely returned.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-ONVeloth-68)</sup> Sometime later, [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) traveled with the Vestige to the alliance capitals, hoping to gain their cooperation for a final attack against [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and his ongoing [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ONMessage-69)</sup>

In the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) came to rule a [new Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in Cyrodiil and began a grand conquest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) called the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). As the war was focused elsewhere, Almalexia commissioned the project of a larger city surrounding Mournhold in response to his rising power.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Zeni Max Interview-29)</sup> The greater city of Almalexia was created, while the original city and Temple compound in the middle retained its name of Mournhold.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup>

After [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md)'s annexation into the Empire, Tiber Septim and the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Army.md) turned their sights to Morrowind. House Indoril and the Temple proposed to fight to the death, with Houses Redoran and Dres at their sides<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-OM-70)</sup> and trusting in their powerful magicks.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BOB1-13)</sup> But over time, the Great Houses could not organize a coordinated attack. After several destructive battles, the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) was signed, formally integrating Morrowind as an Imperial Province.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-OM-70)</sup> Mournhold was the site of a significant battle that laid it to ruin. What became of the city's rulers is unknown, but their child, the five-year-old [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) and her nurse were found in the wreckage. An Imperial General, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) suggested to Tiber Septim that she may be useful and had her sent away until she could return to Morrowind.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BOB1-13)</sup>

### Post-Tiber War and Barenziah's reign
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/LG-cardart-Queen_Barenziah_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Queen_Barenziah_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Queen_Barenziah_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Queen_Barenziah_02.jpg) Queen Barenziah of Morrowind *"Behold the Lady Barenziah, born to the land of Mournhold! Grant her thy blessings and thy counsel, ye kind gods, that she may ever rule well over Mournhold, its ken and its weal, its kith and its ilk."

"Bless her, bless her," all the people intoned along with their Lord and Lady, hands upraised.*
—Excerpt from *[The Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md)* Ever since Morrowind's integration into the Cyrodilic Empire, Mournhold was under military rule, but it slowly returned to a civilian government by the time Barenziah was sixteen years old. While she was sent away to the Imperial City, Symmachus returned to Mournhold to rule as governor until Barenziah was old enough to inherit the throne.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TRB2-71)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-BOB2-72)</sup>

Symmachus fought hard to keep Mournhold alive and healthy, when its counselors would have picked it bone dry. Tiber Septim in his conquest of Morrowind, took the [Horn of Summoning](Lore_Horn_of_Summoning.md) from Ephen's tomb and left the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) with Mournhold.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup> Ebonheart was jealous that they were trusted with its safekeeping and claimed it was their right as the eldest city-state to have it. Mournhold contended that it was King Moraelyn himself who trusted Ephen to keep it, and they were undoubtedly his birthplace.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup> With help from a rag-tail end of the Ra'athim Clan, Symmachus successfully hid the Staff of Chaos in Ephen's tomb, where it sat for years.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup>

Barenziah's time to inherit the throne eventually came when she turned eighteen, so she embarked from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to Morrowind.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-BOB2-72)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-TRB3-73)</sup> At the Mournhold border, she was greeted by Symmachus and continued to the city, where she was met with a grand parade of entry and several speeches of welcome. The ceremony was a blend of ancient Mournhold traditions and those mandated by Imperial decree. With her ascension, she took an oath and was sworn to serve the Empire, Tiber Septim, and the people of Morrowind.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup> Her title as Queen of Morrowind was more ceremonial<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup> as power was divided across the Great Houses.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup> It derives from early [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) traditions of a "high chief of the clans", a role similar to the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) High King.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-KRP-75)</sup> The Armistice was signed to give Morrowind autonomy under the Empire's banner, but Tiber Septim desired a central figure in the east that could represent him, so Barenziah was appointed, having descended from Mournhold's rulers.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup>

Upon her ascension, Symmachus became her Prime Minister, but in time, he proposed to Barenziah and became her royal consort.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-BOB3-76)</sup> Queen Barenziah was unshakeably popular across Morrowind. Despite her upbringing in the Empire, the Dunmer viewed her as one of their own, "held captive" as they were by the West. Symmachus was viewed with suspicion by Morrowind's stiff-necked folk for his peasant ancestry and ties to the Empire, but Emperor [Pelagius I](Lore_Pelagius_I.md) trusted him more than Barenziah. As the years went on, both Barenziah and Symmachus were widely beloved.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup>

As the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) flourished under many different monarchs, Barenziah held the same role as Queen of Morrowind, and Mournhold remained loyal allies to the Empire.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TMOP-77)</sup> For a time, the future Emperor, [Magnus Septim](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) served in their court as a counselor when he was 19 years old.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-TWQ3-78)</sup> Thirty or so years later, Symmachus fought alongside Kings [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) and Magnus Septim in the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), winning many battles against the [Wolf-Queen](Lore_Potema.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude_(kingdom).md) and Emperor [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_III.md).<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TMOP-77)</sup> Barenziah's reign proceeded for years with its own triumphs and tribulations. It had its fair share of crises, storms, famines, plots, and conspirators, but it also prospered. Mournhold's people were fed and productive. The only issue was the lack of heirs, and after hundreds of years, they grew concerned about their lack of progress.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup>

### Civil Unrest and Barenziah's flight from Mournhold
[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/29/AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) The Staff of Chaos *May his spirit rest among his ancestors. But he was king in name only. The Dunmer do not, and never have, submitted to kings like the Westerners, regardless of what the Empire says.*
—Mournhold citizens on King Llethan's death<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TRGen King-79)</sup> In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), the [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) impersonated Emperor [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md), bringing about a decade of chaos across Tamriel called the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md). He achieved this by having the Staff of Chaos, an artifact of immense power from Mournhold's mines. The story of how Jagar Tharn acquired the staff involved Barenziah and her encounter with the figure known as the Nightingale.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-BOB3-76)</sup>

Widely published works such as *The Real Barenziah* and *[Biography of Queen Barenziah](Lore_Biography_of_Queen_Barenziah.md)* series claim that Jagar Tharn posed as the Nightingale, coerced her to reveal Ephen's tomb, and took the staff, only to reveal his hand much later.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup> An obscure tome on the [Nightingales](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md) asserts that the thief was Drayven Indoril, at the time, one of Skyrim's greatest thieves, and was hired by Jagar Tharn to do it.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-TN2-80)</sup> In any case, the Staff was gone, and for a time, nothing came of it.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-BOB3-76)</sup>

Shortly after, Barenziah and Symmachus' first child, Prince [Helseth](Lore_Helseth.md) was born in [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376). Eight years later, Princess [Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md) was born and named after Symmachus' mother.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TDC-42)</sup><sup>:15</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-BOB3-76)</sup> Mournhold's relationship with the Empire was worsening for seemingly no reason as taxes and quotas increased over years. Suspecting this was a test of loyalty, Symmachus lengthened work hours and raised tariffs, unintentionally bringing civil unrest between commoners and nobles alike. In fear of the growing troubles, Barenziah took her children to the Imperial City, hoping to gain an audience with the Emperor and persuading him against the rising taxes. In the meantime, Symmachus stayed behind in Mournhold to deal with any complaints and stave off a potential insurrection.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-BOB3-76)</sup>

The troubles in Mournhold escalated when the peasants organized a few disgruntled nobles and together called for Symmachus' resignation.<sup>[[Note 2]](#intnote_Revolt)</sup> Only the [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) and retainers of Barenziah's house were at his side, but despite hostilities, Symmachus was still in control.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup> That was until [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391),<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TDC-42)</sup><sup>:15</sup> when Symmachus was killed in battle. The Imperial Guard deserted him and joined the rebels, while any loyal troops left suffered greatly from the loss. Imperial envoys recognized the rebel leader as the new King of Mournhold (and Morrowind in general),<sup>[[Note 3]](#intnote_King)</sup> while Barenziah and her family were stranded in the Imperial City. By then, she realized Jagar Tharn replaced the Emperor and was responsible for the Staff of Chaos' disappearance.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-BOB3-76)</sup>

What Mournhold was like under the rebel leader's reign is unknown, but throughout the Imperial Simulacrum, it was still an active settlement. At the time, it had a rivalry with Ebonheart, [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md), [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md), and [Tear](Lore_Tear.md) and was ruled by a certain King Zirik.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AR-25)</sup> In time, the chaos in Mournhold died down, but by then, Barenziah abdicated the throne and married King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md). In her stead, her uncle from [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md) became the new King of Morrowind.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-BOB3-76)</sup> Unlike the previous rulers, Athyn Llethan was disliked by his people and few took him seriously, but he ruled with dignity.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TRGen King-79)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TREffe-52)</sup> His reign would last for about 36 years and he groomed his nephew, [Talen Vandas](Lore_Talen_Vandas.md) to succeed him in due time.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-81)</sup>

### Enter the New King of Morrowind
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg/200px-TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg) King Hlaalu Helseth *The new king is manipulative, ruthless, and calculating. He is exactly what Morrowind needs.*
—Famous quote of a Nord diplomat<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup> While Athyn Llethan ruled in Mournhold, Barenziah and her children were in High Rock as members of the royal House of Wayrest. With Helseth as the oldest child, he was in line to inherit the kingdom's throne after Eadwyre's death. But this put him in direct competition with Princess [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md), Eadwyre's first child from his previous marriage. Their power struggle ended after Eadwyre's death in [3E 418](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_418), and Elysana became the Queen of Wayrest. One of her first decrees was to banish Helseth and Barenziah from her kingdom.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AGAD-20)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-LRPTESP-82)</sup> Morgiah, by then, was already married to King [Karoodil](Lore_Karoodil.md) of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) and lived in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). As Morrowind returned to peace, the two returned to Mournhold and were well received by Athyn Llethan and the court.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AGAD-20)</sup>

Barenziah, however, had no interest in resuming her role as Queen and wanted to retire to her family estates. Helseth, meanwhile, lingered around Llethan's court, and rumors circulated over his ambitions for the throne.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AGAD-20)</sup> At some point, Talen Vandas passed away, reportedly from a hunting accident despite his talents as a warrior.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-TRHelseth-83)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-TRMero-84)</sup> Then suddenly, Athyn Llethan passes away, with official sources claiming it was from old age. Without anyone else to take the throne, Helseth ascended as the new King of Morrowind.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TRGen King-79)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup>

Helseth's rise to power was controversial and sparked much discussion. To outside observers and his own people, he was an enigma.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup> Many people questioned the circumstances around Talen Vandas and Athyn Llethan's death.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TRGen King-79)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-81)</sup> Ravani Llethan, the late King's widow, [Fedris Hler](Lore_Fedris_Hler.md), steward to Almalexia, and many other Dunmer believed Helseth had some hand in his death<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-TRRavani-85)</sup><sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TRHler-86)</sup> but whether that was the case is unknown. A newspaper that circulated Mournhold at the time, known as the Common Tongue, brought to light many examples of Helseth's manipulative tactics, from his time as the Prince of Wayrest.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-TCT-87)</sup> The newspaper helped spread rumors of his potential assassination attempt on King Llethan.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-TRTienius-88)</sup>

Though there was always a problem between the Tribunal Temple and Imperial administration, Helseth's ascencion made things worse. Then in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Sixth House](Lore_Sixth_House.md) and [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) returned to wreak havoc on Vvardenfell. Since then, Almalexia changed her demeanor, becoming a harsher and unsympathetic person.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TREffe-52)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TRBarenziah-89)</sup> But rumors of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) began to spread wide across Morrowind,<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup> and as they handled the situation on Vvardenfell, Helseth's informants grew wary of their presence and felt they could pose a threat to the King. Because of this, Helseth hired the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) to attack them. When they failed, it instead brought the hero to Mournhold.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-TRMain-90)</sup>

### The Nerevarine in Mournhold and the Death of a Mad God
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/TR-quest-The_Blade_of_Nerevar.jpg/200px-TR-quest-The_Blade_of_Nerevar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-quest-The_Blade_of_Nerevar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-quest-The_Blade_of_Nerevar.jpg) Pieces of Trueflame, the Blade of Nerevar *Your work in Morrowind is not finished, Nerevarine. The Tribunal are dead, and the faith of my people is shaken. Protect them. Defend these lands. The skies of Mournhold are clear once again. Let these people suffer no longer. Now go, mortal. Embrace your destiny, and go with my blessing.*
— [Azura](Lore_Azura.md)'s words to the Nerevarine The Nerevarine eventually tracked the Brotherhood to the Manor District in Old Mournhold, where they destroyed the guild. Since then, they roamed the city, doing quests for Mournhold Temple and even the Royal Palace. For the temple, they cleansed the Shrine of the Dead and acquired [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md) for Almalexia.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-TRMain-90)</sup> As per the [Nerevarine Prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md), the Tribunal's source of divine power, the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) was destroyed and their influence slowly faded.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-MWCitadels-91)</sup>

Almalexia was unable to reconcile with this fact and in the background, grew mad and unstable. With the Mazed Band, she teleported to the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) to kill Sotha Sil and unleash his [fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md) onto Plaza Brindisi Dorom.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-TRAlmalexia-92)</sup> The Nerevarine destroyed these invaders and investigated the newly opened Bamz-Amschend to find the [Dwemer animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) also attacking them, much to their confusion.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-TRMain-90)</sup>

After their investigation, the Nerevarine began to work directly under Almalexia but since the attack, the Tribunal's faithful began to lose hope in them. Eno Romari was a local in Mournhold who founded the [End of Times](Lore_End_of_Times.md) cult. He believed that with the Tribunal's loss in power, the next era will come and with a violent transition where the gates of Oblivion will open and flood Tamriel with daedra. Salas Valor was one of Almalexia's Hands but left her service as she became distant. Now he roamed the city's streets, frigthening the citizens and his fellow Ordinators. Almalexia sent the Nerevarine to deal with each of them. As a show of power to her people, Almalexia sent the hero to Bamz-Amschend to activate the long-abandoned [Weather Witch](Lore_Weather_Witch.md) hidden inside and manifest ashstorms in Mournhold.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-TRMain-90)</sup>

After much time had passed, Almalexia fully recognized the hero as the Nerevarine and gave them the task of reforging [Trueflame](Lore_Trueflame.md), Nerevar's legendary sword. Their quest brought them to [Karrod](Lore_Karrod.md), Helseth's champion who possessed a piece of the broken blade, [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md), a wealthy collecter of artifacts, who gave them a Dwemer Battle Shield in exchange for two artifacts, Craftsmen's Hall, where the master smith, [Yagak gro-Gluk](Lore_Yagak_gro-Gluk.md) mended the pieces together, and finally the ruins of Bamz-Amschend, where the [Dwarven Spectre](Lore_Dwarven_Spectre.md), [Radac Stungnthumz](Lore_Radac_Stungnthumz.md) reignited the sword's magical fire.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-TRBlade-93)</sup>

With Trueflame restored, Almalexia gave the hero her final mission, to enter the Clockwork City and defeat Sotha Sil, who she claimed went mad and became unstable. In reality, she planned to lure the Nerevarine away and kill them, turning them into a martyr that died fighting the mad god Sotha Sil. In turn, she would unite her people under one god, one faith, and one divine law, without Helseth or the Empire. In the end, Almalexia was slain and with the Mazed Band, the Nerevarine left the Clockwork City.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-TRMad-94)</sup>

### Fall of the Tribunal and Helseth's Declaration
[![](https://images.uesp.net/5/53/MW-banner-Helseth.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Helseth.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Helseth.png) Hlaalu Helseth's banner [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/LG-cardart-The_Red_Year_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-The_Red_Year_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Red_Year_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Red_Year_02.jpg) The Red Year in Mournhold With Almalexia's death and the Tribunal's fall from power, a great deal of change swept through Mournhold and Morrowind. Helseth immediately, and unapolegetically, assumed power from the vacuum left behind by the Tribunal.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-LRPPH-95)</sup> His first edicts tackled the traditional power structures of the Dunmer, such as the outlawing of [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md) in Morrowind.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup> Helseth believed that while it served its purpose in the past, did more harm than good and felt no discussion was needed to proceed with its abolishment. In doing so, he anticipated slave revolts but warned House Dres ahead of time.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-LRPPH-95)</sup> Helseth is quoted for saying, *"if there is to be a revolution, it is best done by a King."<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup>*

On the other hand, Helseth's reformation of the [Grand Council](Lore_Grand_Council.md), including the heads of the Great Houses, was seen by many as a return to Dunmer tradition.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup> Helseth married Dres Vendama of [Bodrom Mora](Lore_Bodrum.md), and their ceremony was a mix of ancient Velothi and modern Imperial traditions. After their marriage, she was known as Queen Dinara and together with Queen-Mother Barenziah, assisted Helseth by parlaying with allied Houses and the [Imperial Court](Lore_Imperial_Court.md).<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-LRPPH-95)</sup>

By the tail-end of the Third Era, the Great Houses had adjusted to the shift of power dynamics after many years of stagnation. Houses Dres and Hlaalu embraced the new changes, and renounced the slave trade. In turn, they were propelled into success, but on the other hand, the Indoril and Redoran struggled to adapt,<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-OBRadiant-96)</sup> having given no time to prepare for Morrowind's emacipation. Their reaction was violent. On the night of Helseth and Dinara's wedding, House Redoran seized Bodrom Mora and held Dinara's brother, Dres Obael captive. Shortly after, Indoril assassins made an attempt on Barenziah's life and in retaliation, Helseth attacked the Indoril stronghold, Bal Therec.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-LRPPH-95)</sup>

These events led to alienation between Helseth's kingdom and the Great Houses Indoril and Redoran. In time, civil war broke out in Morrowind. While Helseth reigned over most of the mainland, the Indoril Redoran Alliance held the north and south regions, claiming most of Vvardenfell, land around Bodrom Mora and former Dres lands.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-LRPPH-95)</sup> The outcome of this civil war is unknown but in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) occurred and it laid waste across Tamriel. The Empire was stretched thin and pulled most of their forces from Morrowind to combat the daedra in Cyrodiil. With no standing army, they only relied on scattered and uncoordinated resistances. In the end, House Redoran took charge and rallied an army that could fight back.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DBAdril-14)</sup><sup>[[Note 4]](#intnote_Fight)</sup>

By the Fourth Era, the Empire releashed Morrowind from its control. For their loyalty to the westerners, House Hlaalu was stripped of their position as a Great House and lost their seat on the Grand Council. They became a scapegoat for all of the Dunmer's suffering.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DBAdril-14)</sup> How this affected King Hlaalu Helseth, the Imperial King of Morrowind and Mournhold is unknown. By the Fourth Era, the Royal Palace was unoccupied<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TMFN-23)</sup> and Helseth and his family's fate were left unknown.

### The Fourth Era and Onward
Five years later in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), otherwise known as the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) erupted for the first time in years, causing the widespread destruction of Vvardenfell and other natural disasters in Morrowind. Mournhold was relatively unscathed,<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-TRYII-97)</sup> save for heavy ashstorms.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-LGYear-98)</sup> A few Dunmer believed it was the Tribunal protecting them, while others blamed the Tribunal for it happening. Some disagreements even turned into fights. But with news of countless deaths, there was not a single night for a month where people openly weeped and grieved for deceased relatives.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-TRYII-97)</sup>

A Redoran councilor living in Mournhold organized a relief effort a month later to rebuild Vvardenfell, especially settlements that were hit the hardest like Balmora.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-TRYII-97)</sup> Only a year later, however, Mournhold was sacked by the Argonians in the [Accession War](Lore_Accession_War.md). In their conquest of Morrowind, the Argonians cut a bloody swathe across the southern region but the [Redoran Guard](Lore_Redoran_Guard.md) pushed them back before they could destroy the rest of the province. With Mournhold in disarray, the capital was changed to Blacklight in northwest Morrowind<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DBAdril-14)</sup> and the Redoran Guard stayed in Mournhold.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-99)</sup>

House Indoril, having been intertwined with the Tribunal Temple suffered greatly from the Tribunal's downfall. Inner conflict plagued the Temple priesthood until the [Dissident Priests](Lore_Dissident_Priests.md) assumed control, with Tribunal loyalist cast out. House Indoril and the New Temple, who served the original Good Daedra, now known as the Reclamations, had become one of the same, but power was entirely passed to the New Temple.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TR-9)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Mournhold felt emptier than it normally was. The Royal Palace was still unoccupied and the Redoran Guard were reassigned to other parts of Morrowind. Renovations were still made to Mournhold Temple as vestiges of Almalexia's influences were removed and a new sanctuary was added. House Indoril built a new building in Plaza Brindisi Dorom for priests and pilgrims to pay respects at the temple.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TMFN-23)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/TR-prerelease-High_Ordinator.jpg/200px-TR-prerelease-High_Ordinator.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-prerelease-High_Ordinator.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-prerelease-High_Ordinator.jpg) A High Ordinator and Mournhold Temple [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-banner-House_Indoril.png/125px-ON-banner-House_Indoril.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-House_Indoril.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-House_Indoril.png) Banner of House Indoril
- *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Dunmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Dunmer.md#Society)** sections on the Dunmer page.*

Mournhold is a central location in Morrowind; not only has it been the province's capital for much of its history, but it is also the seat of power for both House Indoril and its Temple faiths. Ever since Red Mountain's first eruption, Morrowind's population shifted south toward Deshaan and southern Morrowind. Since then, Mournhold has become the province's largest city.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> Citizens are not known for being cordial or encouraging to visitors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POM-2)</sup> Rumors during the Imperial Simulacrum described a "great evil" that hid under the city and drove its citizens to [madness](Lore_Madness_(disease).md). Another claimed that no one has ever seen the bodies of the slain after a battle on its soil.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AR-25)</sup>

Mournhold is seen as the mercantile heart of Morrowind and hosts many merchant districts. Most famously, the Great Bazaar is generally located in the eastern portion of the city,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> southeast of the Tribunal Temple.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TRMap-22)</sup> It is the largest and heavily secured market in Mournhold.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> Some of the highest-priced items available in Almalexia and nearby Narsis include the flying insect mounts of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup> House Hlaalu held great sway in Mournhold and Deshaan in general.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGHATU-111)</sup> To the south of Mournhold is Deepcrag Den, the city's primary source for fresh [kwama](Lore_Kwama.md) eggs. Though it is not as successful as [Darkshade Caverns](Lore_Darkshade_Caverns.md), it has the clear-cut advantage of being so close to the city.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-ONLoad Deepcrag-112)</sup> An outdoor theater is present in the Great Bazaar and hosts seasonal performances, from plays to choirs.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-APGTM-12)</sup> The Mournhold Players was the local theater troupe that frequently performed in the Great Bazaar.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-TRMeryn-113)</sup>

Mining is a significant source of income and work in Mournhold,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRB1-15)</sup> going as far back as when it was a Ra'athim fief.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup> Commoners were put to work in the mines, even the future royal consort, Symmachus, and his father before him were miners.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRB1-15)</sup> The native Indalen clan are known for excavating [adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md) from Old Mournhold's deepest caves and crevices.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-TRBols-114)</sup> The obscure deity known as Ephen, who hailed from Mournhold, is depicted with a hammer in hand and poised on an adamantium anvil.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TRB4-30)</sup> Mournhold has a long-established rivalry with Ebonheart, the other half of the original Ra'athim nation. This rivalry has surfaced a few times throughout history, once regarding the Staff of Chaos' ownership<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRB5-1)</sup> and another time during the Imperial Simulacrum.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AR-25)</sup>

The [Mournhold Sewers](Lore_Mournhold_Sewers.md) are expansive, and although they are under the city administration's watch, there are large sections of it that are completely forgotten<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-ONLoad Sewers-104)</sup> and as a result, occupied by the city's poor and lawless.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TREffe-52)</sup> The city's outlaws refuge is hidden here, in the ruins of a Tribunal chapel.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-ONLoad Outlaws-115)</sup> Throughout the years, daedric wards have been placed throughout the sewers to allow travel across the city under no suspicion from the Ordinators.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-ONNaryu-116)</sup> Deeper into the sewers are the ruins of Old Mournhold, where many First Era treasures lie in secret and attract adventurers to explore it.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TREffe-52)</sup>

### House Indoril and the Temples
Mournhold is the capital of House Indoril, one of Morrowind's Great Houses. The Indoril are known for their strong ties to the Dunmer religions. For much of their history, they served as stewards to the Tribunal and held positions within the Tribunal Temple faith.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup> Even when [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) fell out of favor,<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-TRGavas-117)</sup> House Indoril still revered their ancestors.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-UHI-8)</sup> When the Reclamations replaced the Tribunal, House Indoril still wielded power but became one with the temple faith.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TR-9)</sup> They also believe in the absolute preservation of traditional Dunmer culture and see change as an enemy that would shake their foundation.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-UHI-8)</sup> This was especially seen when Morrowind was incorporated into the Empire. Many nobles across House Indoril committed suicide, rather than accepting the Empire, causing the House to lose significant power.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-OM-70)</sup> Since then, House Indoril has been openly hostile to Imperial culture and religion.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup>

The Mournhold aesthetic of religious design centers on Almalexia, as she was the city's patron deity. Reginus Buca of the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) claims that Almalexia worship stylistically resembles general Tamrielic norms.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-ONDais Anti-118)</sup> Almalexian priests are given Mother Stones, dark crystals that teleport their user back to Mournhold once it is attuned.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ONSelveni-46)</sup><sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ONMother-119)</sup> Under her reign, levitation was banned in the city. It is said that no one could raise their head higher than Almalexia.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TREffe-52)</sup>

Mournhold's main peacekeepers are the [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md), the holy-guard and priest soldiers of the Temple.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-MWGen Ordinator-120)</sup> They are noted for their armor rich in [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and [glass](Lore_Malachite.md), and their ever-watchful gaze.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-EGT-11)</sup> In the mid-Second Era, the city was guarded by the regular sect of Ordinators, the same seen in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md),<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-ONOrdinators-121)</sup> but in the late Third Era, it was guarded by the [High Ordinators](Lore_High_Ordinators.md), those who were deemed fit to guard Almalexia's holy city and the strongest warriors in Morrowind.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-TRFedris-122)</sup> The Ordinators at large are divided into four orders and all of them answer to the [Alma Rula](Lore_Alma_Rula.md), who resides in Almalexia city.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-MWGen Ordinator-120)</sup>

Unlike other parts of Morrowind, like Necrom, Mournhold is more cosmopolitan and open-minded when it comes to burying foreigners. At most, one will find names on a plaque beside a tomb.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-LAHT-123)</sup> The Shrine of the Dead in Old Mournhold was previously a source of great power, one that allowed the living to channel the ancestors and harness their power. It was abandoned after the Tribunal became Morrowind's main faith, but was restored in the late Third Era.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-TRGavas-117)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Ayem) | Vivec's *36 Lessons* indicates that Almalexia already lived in Mournhold by the early First Era. However, the *[2920](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)* series suggests that Almalexia decided to live in Mournhold after its destruction caused by Mehrunes Dagon. *2920'* s author, [Carlovac Townway](Lore_Carlovac_Townway.md) claimed that none of it is fiction, however, there is conjecture based on research and that events in the distant past are open to interpretation.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-IWTW-124)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_Revolt) | The *3rd Edition of the [Pocket Guide to the Empire](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire.md)* attributes the Mournhold revolt to the [Arnesian War](Lore_Arnesian_War.md), one of the main conflicts of the Imperial Simulacrum. However, the war began with a slave revolt in House Dres' territory in 3E 396, while Symmachus had died five years earlier.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-PGE3-74)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_King) | The King of Mournhold during *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was known as King Zirik;<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AR-25)</sup> however, there is no source confirming that he is the same person as the rebel leader. |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_Fight) | Adril Arano claimed that the Empire left Morrowind virtually defenseless by pulling its forces to help defend Cyrodiil from the daedra.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DBAdril-14)</sup> But [Ocato of Firsthold](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) could not spare any legionnaires to Cyrodiil's cities, claiming that the Imperial Army was fully committed and that pulling soldiers from other provinces would start a full-on political crisis.<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-OBOcato-125)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Mournhold's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Blade Dancers".<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-126)</sup> One of the earliest ideas for the game was to hear rumors about a gladiator named "Kretos the Dark" in Mournhold, and to have the player go to the city to recruit him to their team.<sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-Vijay Making Of-127)</sup> *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)* brought back many of these arena teams to compete in an event known as the Dragon Games, but renamed Mournholds's arena team into the "Mournhold Mudcrabs".<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-128)</sup>
