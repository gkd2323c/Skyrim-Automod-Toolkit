Fortunata ap Dugal, known as the **Pirate Queen**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup> was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) pirate captain, leader of the Red Sails, and the Provincial Governor of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Fortunata began her career as a minor functionary in the [Gold Coast Trading Company](Lore_Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.md) before eventually becoming one of the shipping magnates at the top of the organization.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup> Fortunata used her position in the Gold Coast Trading Company to convince the Red Sail pirates to join her, assembling a formidable armada of merchant vessels and pirate ships.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rot RS-4)</sup> When the time was right, in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Fortunata and her armada sailed into Anvil Harbor and seized control of the city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rot RS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup>

After capturing the city, Fortunata took the title of Provincial Governor of Anvil and declared the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) to be a free and independent state, initiating a conflict with [Carolus Aquilarios](Lore_Carolus_Aquilarios.md), the Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup> Fortunata's pirates subsequently slaughtered Carolus' cohort of the [Second Legion](Lore_Second_Legion.md) in the Gottshaw Massacre, leaving him with no choice but to swear fealty to an independent Gold Coast.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup> These events marked the start of a three-way power struggle over the Gold Coast between Fortunata, Carolus, and [Artorius Ponticus](Lore_Artorius_Ponticus.md), the Primate of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup> Fortunata continued to plot to gain control of the entire Gold Coast, but she was killed by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) at the behest of Carolus before she could realize this ambition.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONASR-6)</sup>

## Biography

### Background and Rise to Power
Fortunata ap Dugal is believed to have been born around the year [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542) into a non-noble family.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-7)</sup> She had a brother named Codus, whom she eventually became estranged from.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ca D-8)</sup>

Fortunata began her career as a minor functionary in the [Gold Coast Trading Company](Lore_Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.md) before eventually becoming one of the shipping magnates at the top of the organization and securing a place on the ruling council.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup> During her time as a shipping magnate, she filled the Gold Coast Trading Company's coffers with substantial amounts of gold.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup> However, her ambitions went beyond being a shipping magnate.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup> To realize her ambitions, she used her connections in the Gold Coast Trading Company to convince the Red Sails pirates, an unremarkable band of raiders who plundered along the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md), to join her.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rot RS-4)</sup>

Under Fortunata's ambitious and fearless leadership, the Red Sails made their first voyage out to the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). Fortunata and her raiders encountered a larger pirate vessel and boarded it after its captain ignored their approach in a smaller riverboat. With cunning and brutality, Fortunata and her crew seized the captain and forced him to surrender. Offering to accept the oath of any member of the enemy crew willing to serve her as captain, she ordered that the former captain and his officers be strung up on the rigging and slaughtered. Fortunata then raised the sails that would become her crew's emblem: a white banner emblazoned with a blood-red saber.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rot RS-4)</sup>

Using her connections as a merchant lord and the growing power of her Red Sails, Fortunata turned a band of outlaws into a feudal hierarchy of lords and vassals in the form of ship captains and their crews. After a number of successful boarding actions that resulted in the capture of pirate and merchant vessels alike, more and more captains began to voluntarily flock to her banner. Fortunata eventually assembled an armada that was estimated to consist of more than two dozen merchant vessels and pirate ships. The fearsome reputation of Fortunata, who was now being called the Pirate Queen, and her Red Sails spread rapidly from port to port as the tales of their attacks became the main topic of discussion in inns and taverns.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rot RS-4)</sup> Fortunata's exploits inspired tales of the Pirate Empress Thusa ap Lagud.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup>

### Capture of Anvil and Provincial Governor of Anvil
By [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) was practically defenseless due to its conflict with [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), which had started in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWat D-9)</sup> When the time was right, in 2E 577, Fortunata and her armada of merchant vessels and pirate ships sailed into Anvil Harbor, took control of the docks, and disembarked a group of sailors and pirate warriors that quickly overpowered the few remaining Imperial soldiers in the city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rot RS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup> [Ephrem Benirus](Lore_Ephrem_Benirus.md), the Count of Anvil, was deposed, and Fortunata ordered the destruction of items that depicted him.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CEBC-10)</sup> In less than a day, Fortunata and her pirates took control of Anvil.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup> After capturing the city, Fortunata took the title of Provincial Governor of Anvil and declared the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) to be a free and independent state, claiming the entire region as her domain.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rot RS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup>

Using strong-arm tactics, Fortunata convinced Anvil's three main merchant houses to swear fealty to her.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-7)</sup> Other city leaders saw the value in accepting the new regime and quickly set out to gain Fortunata's favor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup> She declared the port of Anvil to be free and open to ships and sailors of all kinds and creeds.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FMot P-11)</sup> Additionally, she increased docking fees.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AT-12)</sup>

### Conflict with Carolus Aquilarios
After her declaration of independence, Fortunata requested that Kvatch join Anvil as part of an independent Gold Coast, but the city's count, [Carolus Aquilarios](Lore_Carolus_Aquilarios.md), refused, preferring to remain loyal to his uncle, Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md). Fortunata did not feel confident enough at this time to press the demand, but she would not forget the Count's lack of support.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup>

After Varen disappeared in the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), Fortunata took advantage of Kvatch's sudden isolation to make her demands more forceful. Anvil began pressing Kvatch to join the independent Gold Coast, both diplomatically and by increasingly underhanded means. Marauders and bandits began to appear along the [Gold Road](Lore_Gold_Road.md) around Kvatch, disrupting trade and harassing travelers. Kvatch's famed defenders, the Warders, were dispatched to deal with the bandits, but each time they were able to escape to the Strid River. The bandits returned in greater numbers, amplifying their attacks by raiding outlying farms and sending frightened refugees into Kvatch for protection.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup>

Despite these acts of aggression, Carolus remained staunch and refused to negotiate with the Pirate Queen. Infuriated, Fortunata announced her intention to annex Kvatch, and she sent the Anvil Guard to take control of the city. Carolus responded by putting the Kvatch Warders upon the city walls and sending the cohort of the [Second Legion](Lore_Second_Legion.md) under his command to confront Fortunata's forces. The opposing forces met on the Gold Road, near the [Gottshaw Inn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gottshaw_Inn), where the Second Legion cohort scattered the undisciplined Anvil guards. An army of Fortunata's pirates then ambushed and slaughtered the cohort in what became known as the Gottshaw Massacre.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup>

Without the support of the cohort, Carolus had no choice but to swear fealty to an independent Gold Coast.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-5)</sup> With his back against the wall, Carolus reluctantly agreed to an alliance with the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) and [Artorius Ponticus](Lore_Artorius_Ponticus.md), the Primate of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), to protect Kvatch.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONCCA-13)</sup> After the Gottshaw Massacre, an increasing number of pirates joined Fortunata, and tax revenue continued to flow into Anvil, providing her with additional resources to attempt to take over the Gold Coast.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-1)</sup>

### The Dark Brotherhood
Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Anvil and Kvatch experienced a notable increase in murders, which some suspected to be the work of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CGF-3)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONCCA-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONCF-14)</sup> In response to the increase in murders and the other threats to Kvatch, Carolus granted the Order of the Hour expanded rights and duties to supplement the efforts of the Kvatch Guard.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GCN-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONCCA-13)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OOTH-15)</sup> Carolus also granted Artorius the powers to once again convert the Order into an army.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONCCA-13)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OOTH-15)</sup> These actions unsettled Fortunata, who believed that Carolus and Artorius were building an army to eventually attack Anvil.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-16)</sup> Around this time, Fortunata ordered her Rat Master to uncover information regarding an individual known as the [Black Dragon](Lore_Black_Dragon.md), who had been linked to the murders of a number of people—including at least one who was believed to have been a member of the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TBD-17)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Signed_in_Blood_02.jpg) Fortunata at the Jarol Estate meeting In 2E 582, Fortunata asked an Imperial nobleman, Lord Quintus Jarol, to host a meeting at his [estate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jarol_Estate) with Carolus and Artorius to discuss the Dark Brotherhood threat and the expansion of the Order of the Hour, which Jarol agreed to.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lft G-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LJDT-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSi B-20)</sup> Only Carolus attended the meeting with Fortunata, as Cathedral business forced Artorius to remain in Kvatch.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONCCA-13)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSi B-20)</sup> In this meeting, Carolus gave his word that the Order of the Hour would return to the Cathedral of Akatosh once the Dark Brotherhood threat was resolved.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONCCA-13)</sup> However, the meeting was interrupted when an assassin penetrated the estate, but Jarol had a contingency that allowed Carolus and Fortunata to depart safely.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSi B-20)</sup>

Despite Carolus' assurances at the Jarol Estate meeting, Fortunata still viewed the expansion of the Order of the Hour as a threat.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-16)</sup> Therefore, not long after the meeting, she contracted the Brotherhood to kill Commander Marcus Scipio to weaken the Order and show Carolus that they could not protect him.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONASR-6)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-16)</sup> When Fortunata contracted the Brotherhood, she refused to perform the [Black Sacrament](Lore_Black_Sacrament.md) and made an unusual request to meet the assigned assassin before paying the assassination fee.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONGFAD-16)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONAC-21)</sup> The [Black Hand](Lore_Black_Hand.md), the ruling body of the Brotherhood, accepted the request, so the assassin was sent to [Anvil Castle](Lore_Anvil_Castle.md), where Fortunata personally conveyed her orders to them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONASR-6)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONAC-21)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/ON-quest-A_Special_Request_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-A_Special_Request_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Special_Request_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Special_Request_02.jpg) Fortunata being served poisoned wine The Brotherhood murdered Scipio in Castle Kvatch; however, in an unexpected turn of events, Carolus performed the Black Sacrament to have the Brotherhood kill Fortunata, as he believed this was the only way to protect Kvatch.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONCCA-13)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONASR-6)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CC-22)</sup> The Brotherhood accepted Carolus' contract and agreed to assassinate Fortunata. After the assassin who killed Scipio informed Fortunata of the contract's completion, she offered them wine on the balcony of Anvil Castle. On the balcony, Mirabelle Motierre, a Brotherhood assassin disguised as a servant, served Fortunata poisoned wine. Fortunata then drank the poisoned wine, which caused her to stumble, fall from the balcony, and land on the rocks below, killing her.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONASR-6)</sup>

## Notes
- Fortunata shares a surname with [Torradan ap Dugal](Lore_Torradan_ap_Dugal.md), a famous pirate lord from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) who terrorized the Abecean Sea and the Gold Coast during the mid- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). However, there is no evidence of any direct familial connection.
