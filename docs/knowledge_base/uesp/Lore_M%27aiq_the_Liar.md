*M'aiq knows much, tells some. M'aiq knows many things others do not.*
—M'aiq the Liar, circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion-1)</sup> M'aiq the Liar (or **M'Aiq the Liar**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup> is the name used by certain peculiar [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) from throughout [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history. Each known individual with this name is a strange male Khajiit, who claims great knowledge but speaks of many false or implausible things,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-4)</sup> with only occasional truths.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:216-217</sup> Comments made by some of these liars, and an inscription on a flute discovered in [Crow's Wood](Lore_Crow%27s_Wood.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Flute-6)</sup> seem to imply there is a long bloodline of Khajiit with the same name.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> However, some of the comments made by M'aiq also imply they might somehow be a single immortal individual, given M'aiq's presence throughout at least three different eras.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Castles-7)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-8)</sup> If he was ever asked directly about the topic, it is thought he would likely lie.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Imperial Library Tsaesci-9)</sup>

M'aiq the Liar is a notorious figure among the inhabitants of Tamriel,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-11)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup> with rumors even existing (likely spread by M'aiq himself) about him being responsible for slaying [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), solving the [Oblivion crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), and ending the [war in Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Castles-7)</sup> At least one organization regularly sought out M'aiq for information.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-11)</sup> He is the namesake of a non-alcoholic drink made by Khajiit in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), called the "M'aiq Tale".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-10)</sup> Some of the Khajiit named M'aiq made cryptic implications regarding [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), which occasionally proved to be true,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion-1)</sup> though it cannot be said whether these comments spoke to deeper or [prophetic](Lore_Sight.md) knowledge, or were simply matters of happenstance.

## Sightings
*M'aiq's father was also called M'aiq. As was M'aiq's father's father. At least, that's what his father said.*
—M'aiq the Liar, circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-4)</sup> Several [heroes](Lore_Hero.md) from throughout Tamrielic history have had encounters with the enigmatic Khajiit. Most notably: [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md).

### 2E 582
*You have heard that the Khajiit love naps, yes? So does M'aiq. Though maybe M'aiq is napping now and this conversation is but a dream?*
—M'aiq the Liar, circa 2E 582<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> This M'aiq was a [Cathay](Lore_Cathay.md) who traveled across the whole of Tamriel circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOAppear-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-House-14)</sup> though he appeared to reside primarily in [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOAppear E-15)</sup> While this M'aiq is the earliest known encountered, he made mention of his father, who he claimed was named **Qia'm**, from a long line of Qia'ms, but also remarked that he didn't believe this as his father was "a known liar".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> An inscription on a flute discovered in Crow's Wood seemed to suggest that his father was in-fact simply named M'aiq.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Flute-6)</sup> He also claimed to have an identical brother; "**N'aiq the Fibber**". This M'aiq was encountered many times by the Vestige, whom M'aiq grew to see as a friend, even visiting their abode.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> This M'aiq attracted the attention of the secret [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) organization, the Garland Ring. One of its members, Verbena, was tasked with keeping watch over him. Said operation had a large discretionary budget, with most of it being spent on [wine](Lore_Wine.md) to "keep the cat talking".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-11)</sup>

Many prominent figures active in the decade following the onset of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) were immortalized in a painting commemorating their adventures. Among them was M'aiq, who was seen dueling Braghas in [Tales of Tribute](Lore_Tales_of_Tribute.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

### 3E 427
*There is no mystery. M'Aiq knows all. The dwarves were here, and now they are not! They were very short folks... Or perhaps they were not. It all depends on your perspective. I'm sure they thought they were about the right height.*
—M'Aiq the Liar, circa 3E 427<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup> This M'Aiq was a [Suthay-raht](Lore_Suthay-raht.md) who resided on a small island southeast of [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md) in the [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Morrowind Appear-17)</sup> This M'Aiq declared himself an expert on many things but spoke mostly nonsense; however he did share a few verifiable facts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:216-217</sup> Most notably, he was able to inform the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) about the location of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md)'s sunken shrine.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MWBoethiah-18)</sup> He also appeared to have knowledge about a mysterious talking [Mudcrab](Lore_Mudcrab.md) merchant<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup> who was confirmed to exist,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-19)</sup> though he additionally claimed; "They've got all the money. Mudcrabs taking over everything. They already run [Pelagiad](Lore_Pelagiad.md)."<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup> This M'Aiq possessed a unique [magical](Lore_Magic.md) ability to walk on water.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

### 3E 433
*I have seen dragons. Perhaps you will see a dragon. I won't say where I saw one. Perhaps I did not.*
—M'aiq the Liar, circa 3E 433<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion-1)</sup> This M'aiq was a Cathay who resided in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), regularly traveling between [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) and [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) in search of calipers.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Oblivion Appear-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBPrima-22)</sup><sup>:293</sup> Despite belonging to a different [furstock](Lore_Khajiit.md#Morphology), this Khajiit has been claimed to be the same individual as the one encountered on Vvardenfell.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBPrima-22)</sup><sup>:293</sup>

### 4E 201
*M'aiq has heard that the people of Skyrim are better looking than the ones in Cyrodiil. He has no opinion on the matter. All people are beautiful to him.*
—M'aiq the Liar, circa 4E 201<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-4)</sup> This M'aiq was a Cathay wandering traveler in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) who carried [Skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) with him.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Skyrim Appear-23)</sup> Claims he made about his past included: him being [soul trapped](Lore_Soul_Trap.md), him getting in trouble with the law in [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) and fleeing to [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), and him walking the 7,000 steps to [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md). He also claimed to not remember his childhood, wondering if he ever had one, despite also claiming to remember his father named M'aiq.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skyrim-4)</sup>

## Notes
- M'aiq the Liar was originally planned to appear in *[Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)*, with voice files for him [entering play](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-audio-M_aiq_the_liar_enter_play.ogg) and [attacking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-audio-M_aiq_the_liar_attack.ogg) having been datamined. The game files also included [unused art](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-art-CRDL_01_738.png) resembling M'aiq. - When asked if M'aiq would be playable in the game, [Pete Hines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Pete_Hines) said, *"When I asked him he said he was in the game. I guess he was lying."*<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PHP-24)</sup>
