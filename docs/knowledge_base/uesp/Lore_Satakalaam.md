[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/DF-map-Satakalaam_%28city%29.png/200px-DF-map-Satakalaam_%28city%29.png)](File_DF-map-Satakalaam_(city).png.md) [](File_DF-map-Satakalaam_(city).png.md) Map of Satakalaam in the region Satakalaam is the capital city of the eponymous county in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) region of northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). The people of Satakalaam have upheld the worship of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md)'s ancient pantheon since their departure from the west.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BBS-1)</sup> The name draws an immediate connection to the deity, [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal) the Serpent God, a spirit observed by the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and popular among [Alik'ri](Lore_Alik%27r.md) nomads affiliated with them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFCR-2)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg/200px-ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg) Montalion Necropolis [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/DF-Location-23297-Automap.png/200px-DF-Location-23297-Automap.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-Location-23297-Automap.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-Location-23297-Automap.png) Map of Satakalaam ca. 3E 405 Satakalaam anchors the Alik'r Desert in the east to [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) on the other side of the region and is surrounded by the canyons of [Tigonus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tigonus).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAlik.27r-3)</sup><sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Location)</sup> Despite that, the city connects with [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai) and the nearest city, [Hallin's Stand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hallin%27s_Stand) to the east via several secret passes and caves through the mountains. Although there are maps depicting the elaborate routes,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONContraband Map-4)</sup> there are hidden tunnels meant for illicit travel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONKhorshad-5)</sup>

Satakalaam in the mid-Second Era is an open city surrounded by a natural barrier<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAlik.27r-3)</sup> but in the late Third Era, it was walled.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFSatakalaam-6)</sup> The city is a compact settlement surrounded by mountains on all fronts but the south end, where its main entrance is located. The southern half of the city is partially separated from the larger, open north side by a wall and each side can be reached by an upper level of streets.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAlik.27r-3)</sup>

The south side contains several homes and warehouses, as well as stables and a guard tower. Satakalaam's north side is centered around the large thoroughfare that contains the town square and runs between the large manor house on the northeast corner of town and the city's High Temple.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAlik.27r-3)</sup> [Goshawks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Goshawk) are known to nest high on the temple's tower and the town grows [pomegranates](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pomegranate) and [figs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fig), which local [bees](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bee) tend to pollinate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BBS-1)</sup> The walkways above the city tower over the main street and interconnect across Satakalaam. The western gate is behind the High Temple and leads into the Motalion Necropolis,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAlik.27r-3)</sup> Satakalaam's most well-known feature and the largest crypt in the Alik'r Desert.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONThoda-7)</sup>

## History
In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Seventh Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seventh_Legion) used the smuggling tunnels underneath the mountains to infiltrate Satakalaam and conquer the Alik'r Desert. They intimidated local Magnifico, Khorshad, and utilized his manor as headquarters for their takeover of the city.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONKhorshad-5)</sup> The Imperials ransacked not just the town but the High Temple as well by killing priests, defiling the holy site and looting it of prized treasures: [Sword of Shabnam‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword_of_Shabnam), [Majeel's Scroll‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Majeel%27s_Scroll) and [Lamp of the Satakalaam‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZuladr-8)</sup> General Thoda of Sentinel's armies launched a counterattack and lead the retaking of Satakalaam. All the while, an [Agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) tracked a Withered Hand operative to the city and assisted the general in his mission. They managed to secure hostages across town and eventually destroyed the tunnels with barrels of kindlepitch. With their connection to the east severed, the Seventh Legion was immediately defeated, holy relics were recovered and Satakalaam had been secured.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONIncursion-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONTreasures-10)</sup> The Agent then made their way into Motalion Necropolis, where they would confront the operative, Shiri and defeat her father, the [lich](Lore_Lich.md) Suturah.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONAmputate-11)</sup>

By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the County of Satakalaam was ruled by an unknown Count and hosted the [School of Julianos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:School_of_Julianos).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFSatakalaam-6)</sup> In [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), the city and its environs were conquered by the [Kingdom of Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) at the time ruled by Queen [Akorithi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akorithi) in an event known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). The region itself marked the easternmost end of the newly expanded kingdom.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TWITW-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE3-13)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg) The Coil of Satakal [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-place-High_Temple.jpg/200px-ON-place-High_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-High_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-High_Temple.jpg) The High Temple of Satakalaam *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Redguard.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Redguard.md#Society)** sections on the Redguard page.* The High Temple in Satakalaam's western section is an important place of worship in the region. The High Priest protects three relics that represent the body, mind, and spirit respectively called the Sword of Shabnam, Majeel's scroll, and the Lamp of the Satakalaam.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZuladr-8)</sup> Satakalaam nobles carry love pouches and others will place brightly made notes in each others pouches as part of an adult game.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONContraband Love-15)</sup>

Much like the rest of the Satakalaam region, the city observes the [Serpent's Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Serpent%27s_Dance) on the 3rd of [Sun's Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun%27s_Dusk), an old holiday that was once in honor of a Serpent God but nowadays is a day of grand celebration with street festivals. The significance of the date has since been lost to Serpent Priests.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-HOTIB-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFSerpent-17)</sup> However, as a nation in the Alik'r Desert, the people celebrate other holidays such as [Ovank'a](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ovank%27a) (12th of [Morning Star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morning_Star)), the [Festival of Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Festival_of_Blades) (26th of [First Seed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Seed)), [Fiery Night](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fiery_Night) (29th of [Sun's Height](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun%27s_Height)), [Dirij Tereur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dirij_Tereur) (5th of [Frostfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostfall)), and [Baranth Do](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baranth_Do) (18th of [Evening Star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Evening_Star)).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-18)</sup>

One of the city's most well-known citizens was the Unveiled [Azadiyeh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azadiyeh), known by the epithet, the "Songbird of Satakalaam". She was an expert in old Redguard lore and venerated the original Yokudan pantheon.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BBS-1)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-STJOA-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TUAAYQ-20)</sup> When she was active, she criticized the then High King of Hammerfell, [Fahara'jad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fahara%27jad) and the Daggerfall Covenant.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PFOE-21)</sup> Fahara'jad's oldest daughter, [Maraya](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maraya) was known as the "Jewel of Satakalaam".<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TRLOS-22)</sup>
