# Udhendra Nibenu


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/OB-place-Leyawiin_%28Torval_the_Pilot_-_Cropped%29.jpg/200px-OB-place-Leyawiin_%28Torval_the_Pilot_-_Cropped%29.jpg)](File_OB-place-Leyawiin_(Torval_the_Pilot_-_Cropped).jpg.md) [](File_OB-place-Leyawiin_(Torval_the_Pilot_-_Cropped).jpg.md) Topal the Pilot The Udhendra Nibenu ([Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md) for *"Father of the Niben"*)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTN-1)</sup> is an epic depicting [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md)'s journey charting [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and its waterways, as well as his many discoveries along the way. It remains the only written record of him and his ship; the Niben's voyages.<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Only)</sup> All that is left, however, are four fragments, which detail moments across his travels, including his journey through the [Niben River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_River) and his discovery of the [Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands), where the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) would eventually be build.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTN-1)</sup>

## History
The origins and nature of this text are unknown, as is the identity of its writer. Based on the fragments, it is a third-hand account of the journey from someone other than Topal or a Niben crewmate. It was penned sometime after their return to the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in the middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). It is considered some of Topal's lasting legacy left on Tamriel, along with his ancient maps held in the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md). How it has survived over the eras and how it became fragmented are also unknown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTN-1)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md),<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Era)</sup> the scholar Florin Jaliil decided to translate the Udhendra Nibenu's fragments in [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md) for the sake of "simple coherence". Not only does he provide his translation but gives his own interpretations of the text.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTN-1)</sup> Through his translations, people were able to read it and vicariously live through Topal's journey. Some people have grown up on the stories.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONStorbarda-2)</sup> Scholars have referenced it over the years, like in their treatises and observations of the Argonians.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WWOBM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LTS-4)</sup>

## The Fragments
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png/200px-DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png) The Iliac Bay [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg/200px-OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg) The Imperial City and Lake Rumare The [first fragment](Lore_Father_Of_The_Niben.md#Fragment_First:) mentions two of three ships that had left [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) to find the lost continent of [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), the third of which was Topal's ship, the Niben. It begins by referencing the second ship; the Pasquiniel helmed by Illio, whose [waystone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waystone) pointed south to where it is presumed to be [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md). It further explains how the Niben was the only ship to return after "eighty moons", and with a wealth of goods and curiosities. After sixty-six days and nights, he reached the mouth of a [great bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) and landed on a "[sun-kissed meadow of gentle dells](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anticlere)" where he was attacked by [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTN-1)</sup>

Considering that the [second fragment](Lore_Father_Of_The_Niben.md#Fragment_Two:) is presumed to take place in eastern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) on the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md), the time difference is believed by Florin Jaliil to be eighty months. At this point, Topal is trying to find his way back to Firsthold when he could not find Aldmeris. The cliff coastline provided no westward passage for the Niben so they kept traveling south. They found harborage on a sandy, forested [island](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorne) but were attacked by a great "[bat lizard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bat_Lizard)" believed to be ancient [cliff racers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cliff_Racer). Topal successfully brought it down with his bow and arrow, and for another forty days and nights, the Niben kept sailing south.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTN-1)</sup>

The [third fragment](Lore_Father_Of_The_Niben.md#Fragment_Three:) is set on the southern [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) coast, where he witnessed the ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and eventually discovered the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The [fourth fragment](Lore_Father_Of_The_Niben.md#Fragment_Four:) tells the tale of how they sailed through the Niben River and witnessed "cat demons" that Florin Jaliil believed were ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). This fragment, in particular, is important because it is the only text that mentions the lost [bird beastfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Birdfolk) of Cyrodiil. In the fragment, Topal provides them with the gift of literacy, and in exchange, they name him their lord and offer the [eight islands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eight_Islands) as a gift. It ends with Topal's promise to return and the Niben's departure.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOTN-1)</sup>

*[The Pilot's Purchase](Lore_Loremaster%27s_Archive_-_Elder_Scrolls_%26_Moth_Priests.md#The_Pilot.27s_Purchase)* was theorized by [Amalien](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amalien) of the [Antiquarian Circle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Antiquarian_Circle) of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) to be one of the unknown fragments of the Udhendra Nibenu. A fragment of the text was shared by Sister Chana Nirine of the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) during her interview with the University in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Since the interview, Amalien had sent multiple letters to Sister Nirine and her Order requesting clarification on the matter but had yet to receive a response.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LAESMP-5)</sup>
