# Dreadhorn Clan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-concept-Horns_of_the_Reach_%28Dreadhorn%29.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Horns_of_the_Reach_%28Dreadhorn%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Horns_of_the_Reach_(Dreadhorn).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Horns_of_the_Reach_(Dreadhorn).jpg) Dreadhorn Clan performing a blood sacrifice [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Gherig_Bullblood.jpg) Clan Patriarch Gherig Bullblood circa 2E 582 The Dreadhorn Clan was a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) group of the [Keptu](Lore_Keptu.md) origins, that was active in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). They made an alliance with powerful [minotaur](Lore_Minotaur.md) warlord [Domihaus the Bloody-Horned](Lore_Domihaus.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCD-1)</sup> After forming the alliance they uncovered the long forgotten [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOBB-2)</sup> and besieged [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md). Many of the members of the clan went through arcane ritual that empowered them and turned into powerful warriors known as [Blood Forged](Lore_Blood-Forged.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFD-3)</sup> They worshipped [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), made pacts with [Cernunnon](Lore_Cernunnon.md) and utilized magic related to [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFD-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive FALK-4)</sup>

The **Bloodforge Smiths** were the Reachfolk of the Dreadhorn Clan who worked the ancient [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md). Both the clan leader [Gherig Bullblood](Lore_Gherig_Bullblood.md) and [Stoneheart the Heartstone](Lore_Stoneheart.md) outlined the sacred schema that defined the trappings of the Bloodforge Smiths and the Dreadhorn clansmen as a whole.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM54Bloodforge-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM55-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Store-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg) Domihaus the Bloody-Horned
### Ancient Past
Most Nedic cultures were in decline by the time the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) set foot in [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md) circa [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Not D-8)</sup> Keptu had cultural interchange with the clans of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), who adopted parts of their cultural styles.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOKeptu Skull-9)</sup> The Dreadhorn Clan descends from the ancient people of Keptu origin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCD-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9f/ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg) The Bloodroot Forge
### Second Era
Sometime during the early years of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), Gherig Bullblood's Reachmen clan followed the signs laid by their [Crow Mother](Lore_Hagraven.md) for 30 days and nights, tracing crow-picked corpses through the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md), until finally, Hircine revealed to them his champion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCD-1)</sup> The champion was the minotaur known as [Domihaus the Bloody-Horned](Lore_Domihaus.md), who grabbed Gherig by the neck, taking from him his pendant, which indicated that he was a descendant to the Keptu clan. Noting his heritage, Domihaus began a chant, making the stones reshape, and initiated a blood ritual which both Domihaus and Gherig took part in. This ritual linked Gherig to the home of his ancient ancestors, a home which called to him to rediscover it. With the union of blood, Gherig's clan was reborn as the Dreadhorn Clan, which revered Hircine through his champion Domihaus,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM55-6)</sup> whom the clan hoped would lead them to reclaim the lands their ancestors lost to the Nords.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTCD-1)</sup>

The Dreadhorn Clan traveled to Craglorn and discovered Hircine's Boon, the Bloodroot Forge, and began to work it. Any settlers near the area were either sacrificed or enslaved, and forced to mine nirncrux to feed the forge. It is known that [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) of great power were created using the forge.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOHOTR-10)</sup> Weaponry and armor of the clan members reflected their allegiance to tradition as well as to their newfound minotaur allies. The quality of their weaponry and armor was high—they used properly forged weapons and armor and often adorned their weapons with ornaments. They considered the bull to be their emblem after beginning their alliance with the minotaurs and embellished their belongings with bull symbolism. Their clothing and weapons was ornamented with imagery of bull and the coiled vine. They were also known for their traditional signature kilts made of leather.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM54Bloodforge-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM55-6)</sup> The bull imagery is not limited to items—the members of the clan pay homage to the strength of their minotaur allies with intricate tattoos.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOBMCCR-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOHMCCR-12)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/ON-interior-Jarl%27s_Hall_Warhorn.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Jarl%27s_Hall_Warhorn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Jarl%27s_Hall_Warhorn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Jarl%27s_Hall_Warhorn.jpg) Typical warhorn of the Dreadhorn Clan Dreadhorn Clan members utilized Hircine's [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md) to create [nirnblooded creatures](Lore_Nirnblooded.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONNB-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HOTRBFP-14)</sup> The human members of the clan as well as minotaurs turned themselves into the [Blood-Forged](Lore_Blood-Forged.md). They are comparable to [Briarhearts](Lore_Briarheart.md), but their creation instead involves a nirncrux [heartstone](Lore_Heartstone.md) being used in place of a [briar heart](Lore_Briar_Heart.md),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONF-15)</sup> resulting in a being that is an amalgamation of blood, nirncrux, and iron.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONBlood Forged-16)</sup> The shamans of the Dreadhorn Clan were known for painting symbols using nirncrux powder and blood on their skin for the purposes of various rituals.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONDSS-17)</sup> Spigots crafted from the hollowed horns of minotaurs, and adorned with intricate engravings featuring the symbols of the clan were employed to extract blood from sacrifices dedicated to Hircine.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MHS-18)</sup>

By 2E 582, the Dreadhorn Clan were greatly emboldened by the Bloodroot Forge, and Domihaus led an organized attack on [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) [Hold](Lore_Hold.md). The Dreadhorn Clan and their minotaur allies utilized relatively advanced siege weapons<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFD-3)</sup> and the power of shamanistic [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) -magic during their assault.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive FALK-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-place-Falkreath_Hold_05.jpg/200px-ON-place-Falkreath_Hold_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Falkreath_Hold_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Falkreath_Hold_05.jpg) Siege machines of the Dreadhorn Clan The Jarl of Falkreath took to hiding in his longhouse. His daughter and thane, [Eerika Skjoralmor](Lore_Eerika_Skjoralmor.md), led the remnants of the resistance.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Bo F-19)</sup> She held the town for some time until the intervention of the Undaunted, who crossed the battlefield and aided the thane in her effort to save the city. The group was able to defeat Domihaus at the jarl's keep and witness the coronation of Jarl Eerika.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFD-3)</sup>

With Hircine's forge shut down and his champion Domihaus slain, the Dreadhorn Clan was devastated. Members of the Undaunted speculated that since the forge was uncovered, it was unlikely that it would remain unclaimed.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONUCB-20)</sup> Gherig Bullblood remained in the forge to continue creating terrible weapons. The threat of the Dreadhorn Clan grabbed the attention of the Undaunted, which led to an assault on the forge, and the death of Gherig Bullblood.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOBB-2)</sup> However, the forge called to all that within its boundaries to dwell in crazed and feral thoughts, clamoring for blood.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONF-15)</sup> Their Dreadhorn Clan's victims felt compelled to use the forge to seek revenge on the Reachmen, reversing the roles of hunter and prey.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONRH-21)</sup>

The significance of the Dreadhorn Clan allowed them to be documented within the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md) in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). There, copies of them were subsequently created as [maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), creatures composed of vivified ink manifested by stories from books, and serve as mindless extensions of the [Daedric Lord](Lore_Daedric_Lord.md) [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LAIA-22)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MM-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Enemies-24)</sup>
