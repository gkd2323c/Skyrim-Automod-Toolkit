[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ad/SR-place-Riften.jpg/200px-SR-place-Riften.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Riften.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Riften.jpg) Riften circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/56/ON-place-Riften_08.jpg/200px-ON-place-Riften_08.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Riften_08.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Riften_08.jpg) Riften circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Riften (sometimes called **Rifton**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> is one of the nine major cities in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Map Arena-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Map Skyrim-4)</sup> and is the hold capital of [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md). Built on the shores of [Lake Honrich](Lore_Lake_Honrich.md), nestled within the Fall Forest,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOS-5)</sup> Riften lies close to Skyrim's borders with [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> and enjoys robust trade with the latter via the nearby pass through the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OCD-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SOTGQ-8)</sup> Riften is also the home of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in Skyrim,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Glory-9)</sup> and has become infamous as a town where the aspiring rogue or thief can rise among the criminal underworld.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Profit-10)</sup>

## Layout & Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/SR-symbol-Riften.png/200px-SR-symbol-Riften.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-symbol-Riften.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-symbol-Riften.png) Riften's crest circa 4E 201 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) A map of Skyrim Riften lies on the eastern shore of [Lake Honrich](Lore_Lake_Honrich.md), in a region of the Rift known as the [Smokefrost Peaks](Lore_Smokefrost_Peaks.md), and is surrounded by the expansive Fall Forest. Lake Honrich is one of the largest lakes in northern Tamriel, and is connected to the smaller [Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md) in the northwest by the winding [Treva River](Lore_Treva_River.md). In the center of Lake Honrich lies the [Isle of Gold](Lore_Isle_of_Gold.md). Riften, as with the rest of the Rift, enjoys a relatively temperate climate compared to the other [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md) thanks to the mountains of [Ysgramor's Teeth](Lore_Ysgramor%27s_Teeth.md), which separates the Rift from [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) and the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), blocking the cold weather that comes from the north. One of the [Standing Stones](Lore_Standing_Stones.md), the Shadow Stone, can be found overlooking the city from the southern gate. Much of the land surrounding Riften is farmland, such as the Merryfair Farm, the Snow-Shod Farm, and the Thornwell Farm, among others. [Fort Greenwall](Lore_Fort_Greenwall.md) serves an important bulwark against incursions from the north. Riften was also neighbored by several settlements, including [Greenwall](Lore_Greenwall.md) to the west, [Vernim Wood](Lore_Vernim_Wood.md) to the north, and Morrowind to the east, towards [Silgrad Tower](Lore_Silgrad_Tower.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Map Arena-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/SR-place-Mistveil_Keep.jpg/200px-SR-place-Mistveil_Keep.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mistveil_Keep.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mistveil_Keep.jpg) Mistveil Keep Riften's architecture and layout reflect the local environment and its role as a center of trade. In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), buildings are made of wood from the forest, and the heart of the city is its market square and dockyards, which together are known as the Plankside district. Plankside is separated from the rest of the city, Dryside, by a large canal with water-level entrances to lower quarters and a complex sewer system.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-12)</sup><sup>:597</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-12)</sup><sup>:601</sup> These sewers, affectionately called The Ratway, are used as a refuge for the impoverished and outlaws, and also hold the headquarters of the Thieves Guild in Skyrim, who can trace their origins in Riften back to the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). There is a [Temple of Mara](Lore_Temple_of_Mara.md) on the city's east side, a chapter of the [Benevolence of Mara](Lore_Benevolence_of_Mara.md).

Several noble families inhabited Riften, most notoriously the [Black-Briar](Lore_Black-Briar.md) family, canny political manipulators who owned the Black-Briar Meadery. Another clan was the Snow-Shod family, who were staunch supporters of the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) in the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md). [Mistveil Keep](Lore_Mistveil_Keep.md) is the seat of the Jarl of Riften and contains the barracks of the city guard.

## History

### Second Era
At some point in the Second Era, Riften was raided by Dark Elves of an unknown creed, in which they hit several farms across the area. Not long after, a local Nord, [Unnvald Ironhand](Lore_Unnvald_Ironhand.md) had gathered warriors from all across Skyrim and began his series of campaigns across Morrowind's coast come springtide. He sailed off with an army of warriors once or twice a year, and wrought havoc across the province. One such example was when he sacked the port-town of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md). His exploits were documented and celebrated for several years. Statues depicting Unnvald were erected in Riften and Windhelm.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONHallfrida-13)</sup> Riften was once known for its famous Riften Cavalry that used to patrol roads throughout the Rift, though their fame has faded by the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Contraband Rift C-14)</sup>

In his youth, [Prince Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) studied at [Skald's Retreat](Lore_Skald%27s_Retreat.md) on the Isle of Gold in Lake Honrich. When the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) occurred in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), Jorunn and his closest comrades, the Pack of Bards, were in Riften when they received the news. They fought their way north to Windhelm, but were too late to stop the sacking of the city by the [Snow Demons](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) of [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(place).md) and the slaying of Jorunn's mother and sister, Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and Princess [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md). Jorunn barely escaped the sacking alive. After journeying to the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) for aid and rallying the Nords of Eastern Skyrim, he claimed the title of Skald-King and amassed an army to defend Riften from the approaching [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md). Upon finding Riften heavily fortified, [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md) decided to divert his army away from the city and towards Morrowind. The Kamal were later defeated at a decisive battle in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) and an alliance known as the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was formed, with Jorunn leading the helm as the newly-crowned High King of Eastern Skyrim. Riften joined the Ebonheart Pact with the rest of Eastern Skyrim,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Jorunn-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Akaviri-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> but in doing so, they took down Unnvald's statues and essentially erased any information regarding his legacy. He was forced to retire and accept the Thanedom of Fullhelm Fort.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONHallfrida-13)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg/200px-ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) Wuuthrad & the Spirit of Sinmur During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the city of Riften was a small yet bustling mercantile hub. Riften suffered considerable damage in a conflict against the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) that ravaged the Rift, and involved the Ebonheart Pact as well as the spirits of the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). Thallik Wormfather summoned the spirit of the giant chieftain [Sinmur](Lore_Sinmur.md) the Terrible,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> who then rampaged through Riften's northern wall and left nearby farmsteads in ruins on his way to [Trolhetta](Lore_Trolhetta.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> The city guard, under the command of Captain Viveka, tended to the damage, with an [Agent](Lore_Soulless_One.md) of the Pact aiding the effort.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Meanwhile, the Worm Cult unleashed zombies in the back alleys of Riften at the direction of the Anchorite Garmar, who possessed a shard of [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md) that was needed to end Sinmur's threat.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Once the situation in Riften was resolved, the Pact's army marched to Trolhetta while the Agent gathered more pieces of Wuuthrad across the Smokefrost Peaks.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg/200px-SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg) The Ratway Around this time, the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) began showing an interest in Riften, and an agent was sent to scout it and compile a report on its layout, merchants, and guard patrols.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Profit-10)</sup> The city had a small sewer system used only as a "rat pathway" which the Guild believed would be expanded as Riften grew, and it was hoped that the tunnels could someday be utilized in their activities.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Profit-10)</sup> This proved prescient, and by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the enlarged sewers would become a den of criminal activity known as The Ratway.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> A cistern beneath the city had also been converted into an outlaws refuge, and was occupied by a gang of local Nords called the Skull-Brethren.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c5/AR-place-Riften.jpg/200px-AR-place-Riften.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Riften.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Riften.jpg) Riften during the Imperial Simulacrum In the early [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the young [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) traveled to Riften and became acquainted with the Thieves Guild. On one fateful day, Barenziah and her guild associates attempted to rob the Imperial Commandment's building in the city. It was here that [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) re-encountered Barenziah and brought her back to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Barenziah Real-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Barenziah Bio-27)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late Third Era, the city of Riften was ruled by King Bjis, and had a rivalry with [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Arena-28)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Riften continued to prosper well into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), when both its size and affluence reached their zenith. However, the city fell into a period of decline beginning in [4E 98](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_98), when the Jarl was assassinated. Hosgunn Crossed-Daggers was installed as the next Jarl of Riften, and although many believed Hosgunn was responsible for the previous Jarl's murder, he used the city guard to brutally quash any dissent and solidify his rule. Over the course of his long reign, the new Jarl imposed strict curfews, harsh punishments, and unreasonably high taxes on the people, spending most of the accrued gold on the construction of a massive and lavish wooden castle for himself. As Riften sank into squalor and its people succumbed to disease and famine, the city finally reached its breaking point and a popular revolt broke out in [4E 129](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_129). Hosgunn's castle, by then derisively known as "Hosgunn's Folly", was set alight, creating a conflagration that quickly consumed the entire city. Although Jarl Hosgunn's tyranny was brought to an end that night, many citizens perished, and Riften was left in ruins. The city was rebuilt over the next five years into a smaller, gloomy, fortress-like town of wooden structures and rough stonework, which it remained as of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OCD-7)</sup>

During the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Riften and the Rift were affiliated with the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), the rebel group that sought to split Skyrim from the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and crown [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) as High King of Skyrim. [Laila Law-Giver](Lore_Laila_Law-Giver.md) was the Jarl of Riften at the time, and despite aligning with Ulfric, she was not fully convinced by the Stormcloak cause or Ulfric's leadership.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Laila-29)</sup> The Snow-Shod clan avidly supported the Stormcloaks, while the Black-Briars were friendly to the Empire.

Meanwhile, the Thieves Guild was experiencing a long streak of misfortune that had resulted in the loss of much of its influence in Skyrim.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Glory-9)</sup> There were many theories about their sudden downfall, but the troubles began after Guildmaster [Gallus Desidenius](Lore_Gallus_Desidenius.md) was killed in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176) by one of his allies, [Mercer Frey](Lore_Mercer_Frey.md). During Gallus' time as the guildmaster, he maintained relationships with the influential families of Skyrim and kept the Thieves Guild's business safe across the province.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Glory-9)</sup> With his death, the Thieves Guild had fallen into obscurity, but continued their alliance with the matriarch of the Black-Briar family, Maven Black-Briar. A [new recruit](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) eventually helped the Thieves Guild regain its lost glory, expose the murder of Gallus, and reassert itself throughout Skyrim.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>
