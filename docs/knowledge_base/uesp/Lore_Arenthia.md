[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-place-Arenthia.jpg/200px-ON-place-Arenthia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arenthia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arenthia.jpg) Arenthia circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) The province of Valenwood Arenthia is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Map Arena-2)</sup> serving as the province's largest settlement in the borderlands of [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md). Arenthia is the resulting amalgamation of various cultures that reside in, and have spread their influence throughout, the march. It is an important trade city that is nestled on the fork of both the [Crescent River](Lore_Crescent_River.md) and the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md). It was named after the [first King](Lore_Arenthia_(person).md) of the [Wood Elves](Lore_Bosmer.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

## Layout & Geography
The city-state of Arenthia is situated in the region of Reaper's March, near the point where the borders of Valenwood, [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), (Western [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)) meet. Arenthia's architecture and culture emphasize this cultural triumvirate. Arenthia's general area is known as the [Northern Woods](Lore_Northern_Woods.md), which was at one point called the South Weald.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> It is built on a fork where the Crescent River flows into the Strid River, which makes up a large portion of the northern provincial border and continues into Cyrodiil. Arenthia has two gates; the southern one leads into the rest of Reaper's March and Valenwood, while the northern bridge crosses the river, and enters the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md). The Strid River is surrounded by large hills and mounds. The outskirts consist of [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins such as [Senalana](Lore_Senalana.md), and Colovian Forts, such as the town of [Greenhill](Lore_Greenhill.md), and the well-known Moon Hunter Keep. Following the Crescent River will lead into the [Falinesti Autumn Site](Lore_Falinesti_Autumn_Site.md), and the trading town of [Rawl'kha](Lore_Rawl%27kha.md). Rawl'kha's roads eventually led to Elsweyr and the city-state of [Dune](Lore_Dune.md). Arenthia is neighbored by several settlements, including [Thormar Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thormar_Keep) to the southwest, the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to the northeast, and Elsweyr to the east, towards [Dune](Lore_Dune.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Map Arena-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Arenthia is somewhat of a walled-off settlement, but the only defense comes from the natural mountains that cradle the city, and the Strid River's canyon. Arenthia is split into four districts. Outer Arenthia is surrounded by a traditional Imperial square, and various merchant stalls are set throughout the branching roads. Northwest of the square leads into the Strid bridge, while the southwest goes into Reaper's March. North of it is a small harbor, where many ships dock from the Strid River. Local guilds are found in west Arenthia. The Tower District is found in southern Arenthia, and it somewhat serves as a residential district. It is four buildings and a large tower that overlooks the city. The Temple District in the southeast contains a Temple to the Divines. It was abandoned following the Colovian's retreat from Reaper's March. It is said that [Skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) Smugglers use the old Temple as their base of operations. Finally, there is the Residential District, found in east Arenthia. It contains several private homes and the Pious-Arenthi Inn, a rather large Imperial-style Inn that sits atop a mound that looks over the area.

## History
Darloc Brae, known as The Beast of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), led the [Anequine Conquests](Lore_Wars.md#Anequine_Conquests) in the Fifth Century of the First Era, when the Cat-Folk soldiers of the Golden Beast ruled the land from the [Larsius River](Lore_Larsius_River.md) to the [Strid](Lore_Strid_River.md), seizing the Bosmer city of Arenthia from Valenwood and adding it to his numerous military victories.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOFG-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

In [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714), the city, along with the rest of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), was conquered by the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), after centuries of unrelenting warfare along the border with [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and a plague from [Thras](Lore_Thras.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Although the province became occupied by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), they continued to experienced resistance from some natives for several years.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFARORB-10)</sup>

During the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), the city was re-founded, building a [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) -style citadel over the older Bosmer settlement to serve as a center of trade between Colovia, Anequina and Valenwood. As the location of Arenthia was militarily strategic, the citadel was heavily fortified with tall walls and defensive towers, and two stone bridges were constructed to cross the western river at this point, which eased the troop movements between Cyrodiil, Valenwood and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). From therefore, Arenthia consists of two well differentiated areas: the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) -style citadel, to the south, and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) and Bosmer shantytown, to the north and by the river. The Cathedral of the settlement also was built in this time. <sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

In [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Arenthia was incorporated into the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) along with the rest of Camoran-controlled Valenwood after the Blacksap Rebellion was put down by the combined forces of [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) and [Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-WTNAI-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TROC-14)</sup> During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the city was invaded by [Colovians](Lore_Colovia.md) under General Lavinia, leader of the Legion of the West Weald, and annexed to the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), only to discover that the cathedral, the principal symbol of Imperial occupation, had been abandoned during the early years of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Although nominally part of Valenwood, the eminent Khajiit presence in Arenthia elevated a Khajiit woman to leader of the Resistance against Colovian occupation. After the eventual defeat of General Lavinia with the help of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), a Clan Mother began to rule the settlement in behalf of the Dominion, without discussing if the city should be part of Valenwood or Elsweyr. This situation remained until the eventual dissolution of the Dominion. After this, the majority, if not all, of the Imperials fled the city.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ON-15)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a circle of scholars from Arenthia was interested in forming relations with [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md) in exchange for knowledge. They opened an embassy in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and worked with various [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) chiefs.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Bralthahawn-16)</sup>

Again in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830), the city was annexed by the Second Aldmeri Dominion after the intervention of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in another Camoran civil war.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-17)</sup> However, the use of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) by Tiber Septim resulted in the conquest of both the entirely of Valenwood and the Summerset Isles in [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), ending the Second Aldmeri Dominion control of the settlement, which was incorporated to the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-BHot E1-18)</sup>

Since the beginning of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Arenthia became part of the Province of Valenwood. Between [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249) and [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267), it was also reputedly the place where the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) first began his war of conquest, collecting a huge army of daedra and undead before first attacking Valenwood, then [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> For the rest of the Third Era, Arenthia remained an integral part of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Arenthia was an active settlement, being ruled by King Coman. It had a rivalry with [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

The [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) that ended the Third Era and weakened the Empire provided the opportunity for the Thalmor to seized the Summerset Isles. In [4E 29](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_29), a Thalmor-backed coup overthrew Valenwood's government, routing Imperial forces and their Bosmer supporters. Alinor and Valenwood then proclaimed a union, and a new Aldmeri Dominion was formed. The city fell into Aldmeri hands for the third time in history.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TGW-21)</sup> Though it was not under Imperial control in [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), there was thought to still be some sentiment for the Empire by the inhabitants, as [Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) believed the city could be taken with a thousand men and the support of the locals.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TIC-22)</sup><sup>:104</sup>

## Culture
As of the Fourth Era, the city produces a vintage red wine, [Arenthia Red](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md#Arenthia_Red).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> [Arenthian Brandy](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md#Arenthian_Brandy) was produced in the Second Era.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOCAAA-24)</sup> Squeeze Boxes, hand-held musical reed instruments played by squeezing them in and out, were made in the city and popular with [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) travelers by the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOABAB-25)</sup>
