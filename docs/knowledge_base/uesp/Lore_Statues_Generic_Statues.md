## Generic Statues
These are statues that are solely decorative or are of unconfirmed persons but are included here for completeness and to illustrate the wide variety across cultures and time.

### Yokudan and Redguard
Ancient Yokudan and Redguard statuary in Hammerfell are almost synonymous, largely due to the strong cultural influence of Yokuda that survived its fall. All known statuary in Hammerfell dates back to the First Era, and was built shortly after the arrival of the Yokudans in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808). Examples include the various statues found in the lost city of Na Totambu, Al Danobia tomb or Ash'abah Pass. The Yokudans, in an effort to recreate the culture of Yokuda on Hammerfell, built their statuary in the same fashion as that which would have been found on Yokuda.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONNa Totambu-1)</sup> Once the Yokudans came to be known as Redguards decades later, the style of their statuary remained largely the same until at least the Siege of Orsinium in [1E 950](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_950), as evinced by the various statuary found in Honor's Rest.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONHonor Rest-2)</sup>

According to historians, it's rare to find Yokudan statuary that isn't wielding a sword.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morwha-3)</sup> Examples include statues of the numerous [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warriors, such as [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md), some of which are performing ancient sword techniques.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Cont Yokudan Warrior Statuette-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Makela Leki Figurine-5)</sup> During the Ra Gada conquests in the early First Era, sculptors rarely took the time to render softer details in their work. In addition, Yokudan statuary normally featured a metallic element, such as [orichalc](Lore_Orichalc.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morwha-3)</sup> Some ancient orichalc statues were known to depict [Diagna](Lore_Diagna.md), God of the Sideways Blade, holding a severed head.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cont Orichalc Diagna Statuette-6)</sup> Others sculptures were modeled after the house idols that were popular with the Yokudan people,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cont Yokudan House Idol-7)</sup> or notable Sword-Singers in the history of the Alik'r.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cont Golden Sword Singer Bust-8)</sup> Brilliant battlefield tacticians often became venerated heroes among the Yokudan warriors and were immortalized as statuettes.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Yokudan Scholar-9)</sup> The Ra Gada were believed to have upheld the tradition of entombing their dead alongside gilded funerary statues of various animals, such as the [cat](Lore_Cat.md) or [ibis](Lore_Ibis.md) (which itself was associated with [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md)).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONAl Danobia Statues-10)</sup>

The Yokudans also mastered a unique [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) technique that could turn living beings into stone. This was discovered during the mid-Second Era, when Lady Clarisse Laurent began studying the [gargoyle](Lore_Gargoyle.md) statues of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). She found that the Yokudans, inspired by these gargoyles, had experimented with their own flesh and bone to create a "stoneflesh" potion that could turn them into stone. It was also discovered the Yokudans invented a potion that could reverse the effects, brewed using the tongue of a living gargoyle.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONTonguesof Stone-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONLaurent-12)</sup> Many of these resulting statues could be found in various ancient Yokudan ruins, such as Kardala, Unhallowed Grave or Al-Danobia Tomb. These statues would serve as sentries, and could suddenly come to life and fight off any invaders or tomb robbers.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Yoku Statues-13)</sup>

### Nordic
Much of the statuary found in Skyrim dates back to the Merethic and First Era, and is found mostly in Nordic tombs or ancient temples. These statues largely depict Nordic heroes or animals who represent their ancient pantheon of gods. After the fall of the Dragon Cult, some Nords continued to worship Alduin in secret as evinced by the existence of numerous jewel-encrusted Dragon carvings found during the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Cont Dragon Cult Idol-14)</sup> In a tradition shared with Orcs, some Nords kept tiny trunk guardian statues, believing they would protect their stored goods.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cont Nord Trunk Guardian-15)</sup>

### Orcish
Given that [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) has been razed and rebuilt numerous times, much of their ancient statuary has either been destroyed or forgotten. Almost all known [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) statuary that survived dates back to around the time of the first Orsinium in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and could be found around the [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) region. This included statues, bas-reliefs, and various sculptures. Of note was the ancient clans who built Orsinium and carved their seals into blocks of stone that would be used as decoration around the ancient city.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONOld Orsinium2-22)</sup>

When Orsinium was rebuilt during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), some of the ancient forms of statuary was revived and recreated, alongside contemporary statuary. Busts of prominent rulers, such as King Golkaar and [Kurog](Lore_Kurog.md), crafted from [orichalc](Lore_Orichalc.md) and [alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md) were found during this time.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Cont Kurog Bust-23)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Cont Golkarr Bust-24)</sup> In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md) graced the newly founded city of [Nova Orsinium](Lore_Nova_Orsinium.md) with [iron](Lore_Iron.md) statues of Orc heroes including [Mauloch](Lore_Mauloch.md) and [Torug gro-Igron](Lore_Torug_gro-Igron.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3S-25)</sup> Some examples of less prominent statuary included finely crafted wooden fertility totems that depicted a pregnant Orc holding a large acorn.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Cont Oaken Fertility Totem-26)</sup> According to Wrothgar tradition, tiny trunk guardian statues were kept by some Orcs, who believed they would protect their stored goods.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Cont Orc Trunk Guardian-27)</sup>

### Altmeri
Altmeri statuary found across the Summerset Isles is often created from white [marble](Lore_Marble.md), much like their architecture. They often depict various figures or heroes from their history, including members of the [royal family](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Cont Marble Naemon-38)</sup>. Although the Altmer maintain some of their most ancient statues represent their honored ancestors, some scholars insist they're of Ayleid design.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Ancient High Elf Statue-39)</sup>. The [gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md) and [eagle](Lore_Eagle.md) are common motifs used in their statuary and can be found in their most ancient artwork. For the Altmer, the high-soaring eagle represents their ancestors the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md), who they believe came from the heavens and were trapped in physical form by the creation of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Knowledge Base-40)</sup> Hearkening back to the Altmer's ancient roots, gleaming [platinum](Lore_Platinum.md) statuettes depicting the [Old Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) were common during the mid-Second Era, appearing vaguely Elven in shape, but featureless.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Elusive Ehlnofey Statuette-41)</sup>

### Druidic
[Druidic](Lore_Druid.md) statuary is largely found across the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago, dating back to at least the mid-Second Era. According to some scholars, stone held importance in druid culture given it was no less natural than trees, but what's unique was the druid belief in their master stonecutters' ability to enhance or perfect nature. Some of the sculptures bear religious significance to the various Druid circles native to the isles and are tended to by them. Megaliths (also known as "storystones") depict major figures in their ruling council, the Draoife, mark major events, or even serve as stargazing tools.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Draoife Storystone-42)</sup>

Notable is the skillfully crafted Druidic stonework at the entrance of [Clohaigh](Lore_Clohaigh.md). Faces depicted on the stone conveyed a message of respect for all possible outcomes and reactions. Multiple faces were depicted on the statue, one with an angry expression and the other with a genuine smile, although the sinister look in its eyes gave an impression of danger. It was known that locals left offerings by the statue and it was speculated that the offerings left at the angry face were meant for someone who was in serious danger.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Craetia-43)</sup> Carvings within the cavern portrayed several scenes in the ancient druidic style on a number of pillars, including Frii cultivating the sacred seeds to create the king's regalia and the crowning of the new Druid King.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Laurel-44)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOClohaigh-45)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Frii-46)</sup>

The [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md)'s statuary reflects their reverence for [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md), the active volcano that towers above [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md). Many of their sculptures are built from dark, igneous rocks and are much more abstract in nature when compared to statuary of other Druid circles.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-OSRCodex-47)</sup>

### Bosmeri
Traditional statuary is almost nonexistent in [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) culture. Instead of carving statues from stone, the Bosmer create artwork using nature itself.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-PME-90)</sup> One example can be seen in [Deepwoods](Lore_Deepwoods.md) village in [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md), where a shrine depicting the [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md) is fused with a living tree sprouting from the earth.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Deepwoods-91)</sup> Exaggerated [jade](Lore_Jade.md) fertility statuettes of the Green Lady can also be found at her shrines.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Fertility Fetish-92)</sup> Other minor examples include camp guardians, tiny effigies made of sticks and twigs that the Bosmer bless to [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) and place them in their camps for protection.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Yffre_Camp-93)</sup>

### Reachmen
[Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) statuary is relatively uncommon, and the most prominent statues depict [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) in the aspect of Alrabeg - his most malevolent form. These statues served as shrines and altars and played a part in Reachman hunting rituals.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-TEGB-94)</sup> A notable example of this is in the [Shrine of the Hunt-Father](Lore_Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.md) in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), where a large statue of Hircine was erected during the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-Statue Hunt Father-95)</sup> Other forms of their statuary sometimes depict [Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md), the matriarchal witches who lead Reachmen clans.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-Contra Defaced Hagmother Figurine-96)</sup> Examples include a stone carving of a Hagraven with a distended belly, possibly representing fertility or engorging on small children.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-Cont Hagmother Statue-97)</sup> Reachmen were also known to carve primitive totems made of skulls and bones to mark their territory and strike fear into the unwary or foolish.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-TEGB-94)</sup>

### Akaviri
[Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) statuary and bas-reliefs are exceeding rare on mainland Tamriel, despite the great influence they had on Imperial culture during the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-99)</sup> [Alduin's Wall](Lore_Alduin%27s_Wall.md), found within [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md), was considered to be one of the best-preserved examples of early Akaviri sculptural relief, as well as a wonder of the ancient world. Although Akaviri in origin, craftsmen were said to have incorporated the flowing Nordic style into its design.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-SREsbern-100)</sup> The Dragonguard often used stone tablets or pillars carved with glyphs to unlock the entrances of ancient Dragonguard temples.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-ONThe Dragonguard-101)</sup><sup>[[100]](#cite_note-SRAL-102)</sup> The Glyph of Bones was sometimes used to mark dragon mounds in Skyrim.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-ONDragonmound-103)</sup>

### Goblin-ken
[Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) and their [kin](Lore_Goblin-ken.md) don't have much in the way of advanced stone or metal work, they instead often combine common materials or things created by other races together to create simple sculptures. Goblins are known to paint idols of Malacath blue in order to worship their interpretation of him, the [Blue God](Lore_Blue_God.md).<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-Blades Blue God-105)</sup> The goblins of the Western Reach were able to create impressive magical obelisks.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-106)</sup> A big part of [riekling](Lore_Riekling.md) culture is collecting trinkets created by other races,<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-LS-107)</sup> they are known to create strange objects of worship out of a number of materials.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-108)</sup>
