# Oaken Order

[![Oaken Order Banner](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/ON-banner-The_Oaken_Order.png/60px-ON-banner-The_Oaken_Order.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-The_Oaken_Order.png) The Oaken Order (or the **Oaken Knights**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONGST-1)</sup> was a [Breton Knightly Order](Lore_Breton_Knightly_Orders.md) active in the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Because the Oaken Order prioritized magic over martial combat, members of the Oaken Order were typically battlemages, conjurers, and support spellcasters. Their order was speculated to have been created by [druids](Lore_Druid.md), though membership also extended to outsiders (even non-mages) willing to learn [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)'s teachings.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Isobel-2)</sup> They maintained their druidic connections, often citing them as their foundation for their high level of magical understanding.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SKO-3)</sup> Some members of their order have overshadowed [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) members in the art of [Destruction](Lore_Destruction.md) and [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) magic. The guild's conjurers were especially threatened by the Oaken Order's skills, downplaying them as practitioners of hedge magic. The [Order of the Iron Knot](Lore_Order_of_the_Iron_Knot.md) saw the Oaken Order as lacking chivalry and respect for combat in the way a traditional knight would.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SKO-3)</sup>

The Sapphire Tournament, hosted by [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) at Castle Navire,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> serves as a proving ground for squires seeking entry into the Systrean knightly orders. The Oaken Order is one of the three orders that take part, with the other two being the [Order of the Albatross](Lore_Order_of_the_Albatross.md) and the [Order of the Iron Knot](Lore_Order_of_the_Iron_Knot.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONGST-1)</sup> Each sets its own distinctive trial. Participation earns favor, though each order selects only one or two aspirants for knighthood. Most contenders attempt a single challenge, but those who succeed in all three are granted a powerful boon.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Isobel-2)</sup>

- Though it isn't specified which druid circle created the Oaken Order, the [Stonelore Circle](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md) was friendly and took willing converts, while the [Eldertide](Lore_Eldertide_Circle.md) and [Firesong](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md) were at that point hostile to outsiders.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DP-6)</sup> The Stonelore were known to have taken in Oaken Knights to study among them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
