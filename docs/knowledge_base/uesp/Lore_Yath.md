[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) The only known map showing the location of Yath Yath (also spelled **Vathi**) is a large island due south of [Akos Kasaz](Lore_Akos_Kasaz.md) in the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) archipelago, between the [Azurian Sea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurian_Sea) and the [Sea of Pearls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_of_Pearls). Yath is arid and rugged, with a spine of jagged mountains.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLIOOY-2)</sup> The small island of [Bahia](Lore_Bahia.md) is its nearest neighbor. One known settlement is Makan, found on the west side of the island.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Yath is home to a breed of [snake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snake) called the Yath Asp, which according to [the Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) are huge and have a big temper. They used to serve [Lefthanded Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lefthanded_Elves) as pets, and Asp-Leather was fashioned by them into garments resistant to cuts and gouges.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAB-4)</sup> It was also known for its turquoise tilework and body decorations adorned with turquoise accents, the glint of steel, and the wings associated with the Warrior Wave.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YT-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WWPYT-5)</sup>

## History
Colonists from Yath, led by Grandee [Yaghoub](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yaghoub)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HCC2-6)</sup> and later King [Xakhwan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xakhwan), were responsible for exterminating the [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) along the coasts of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) during the Yokudan migration in the [ninth century of the First Era](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808). They also settled in the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins of [Salas En](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salas_En), inhabiting the site for over a thousand years until being driven out around the time of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSEE-7)</sup>
