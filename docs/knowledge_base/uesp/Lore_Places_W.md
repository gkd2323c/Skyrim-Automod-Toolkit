## Wasten Brukbrook

## Wasten Coridale
[Wasten Coridale](Lore_Wasten_Coridale.md) (also called **Wasten Coraldale**) is an island in the [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md), off the coast of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). It contains a complex system of underground caves and fertile grottos that resembles a wild honeycomb. Sea creatures from the [Reef Viper](Lore_Reef_Viper.md) to the [Salamander](Lore_Salamander.md) live and thrive in these caves, and because of this, there are many fishermen from nearby [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) that come for an abundance of harvest.

## Water's Edge
[Water's Edge](Lore_Water%27s_Edge.md) is a settlement located in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), along [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Green Road](Lore_Green_Road.md). ## Wayrest
[Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) is a major city in the center of the [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md) region of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), at the mouth of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md). It serves as the capital of a [kingdom](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) that forms one of High Rock's provinces.

The city is split into seven districts. The Residential District lies in the northern part of the city. The Merchant District and Market Square lie west of the river and south of the Residential District. The Temple and Palace Districts are off to the west, while the Banking District can be found in the east. To the south, on the banks of Iliac Bay, sit Wayrest Docks.

## Wayrest (kingdom)
[Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) is one of the nine historical [Breton kingdoms](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md) in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It is located in the center of the province, at the mouth of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md). Wayrest's fortune and immense wealth come from a unique, symbiotic alliance between its merchant lords and the crown dating back to its founding in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Even its kings, except for the Septim Dynasty of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), can trace their lineage back to a merchant prince. By the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the kingdom had the largest and richest population of High Rock. It is named after its capital city, [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md).

## Wayshrines
[Wayshrines](Lore_Wayshrines.md) are shrines built to honor the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md), most often built at sanctified sites far from civilization. Many of these holy sites were chosen by the devout due to specific miracles or blessed events that marked them, though others simply inspire feelings of reverence. It is theorized that each wayshrine corresponds to a star in the sky, acting as a conduit between [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). Indeed, it is commonly believed that the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of the dead are drawn to the nearest wayshrine for spiritual escort to the afterlife. Wayshrines vary from culture to culture, built to reflect local religious beliefs and to honor the most prominent regional Divines. The only consistent feature appears to be a central brazier or basin. The practice of building wayshrines has been present on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) since time immemorial and even extends into the various realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).

## Weatherleah
[Weatherleah](Lore_Weatherleah.md) is a historical settlement on the northern reaches of the [Imperial Reserve](Lore_Imperial_Reserve.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Throughout the eras, it has been the site of a private family estate; a fine example of a [Colovian](Lore_Colovian.md) -style manor. It shares its name with the eponymous family of Colovian nobles, the Weatherleahs. In its prime, some would call it the jewel of the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md). In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), it was the property of the Jemane family of [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) who built its most modern iteration.

## Weir Gate
The [Weir Gate](Lore_Weir_Gate.md) was a semi-permanent portal between Tamriel and the [Slipstream Realm](Lore_Slipstream_Realm.md) in which the [Battlespire Academy](Lore_Battlespire.md) was later established. It was known to have existed since at least [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It was connected to the [pillar of light](Lore_Pillar_of_Light.md) on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md); once the pillar was entered, the user would arrive at the Weir Gate in the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).

## Welke

## Wendelbek
[Wendelbek](Lore_Wendelbek.md) is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located on the northern shore of the [Panther River](Lore_Panther_River.md) within the deeper parts of the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md). It is noted for its large subterranean halls and [Welkynd stone](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) supply, and for being the namesake for the Scouring of Wendelbek.

## Wendir

## Wenyandawik

## West Gash
The [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md) is one of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md)'s largest contiguous areas, stretching from the city of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) and the [Odai River](Lore_Odai_River.md), to the northwest coastline around historically [Redoran](Lore_Redoran.md) -owned settlements (i.e., [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md), [Ald Velothi](Lore_Ald_Velothi.md) and [Khuul](Lore_Khuul.md)) The region is mostly rocky shrubland throughout the interior, marked by natural rocky bridges and arches, as well as the sparse highland along the north coast. The land between the Salothran Ancestral Tomb to the north, to [Caldera](Lore_Caldera.md) and later Balmora to the south is a long stretch of land that separates the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) from the [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md) like a gash in the earth.

## West Weald
The [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) is an open countryside of the [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) region in southern [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), well known for its vineyards and tomato farms. The city of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) is located here. Within its province, the West Weald shares it western and northern borders with the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) and its western border with the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md). A portion of the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) region called the "North Weald" reaches the northern border of the West Weald. To the south of the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md) it borders the [Northern Woods](Lore_Northern_Woods.md) region, sometimes called the "South Weald", within the provinces of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).

## Western Reach
The [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) is the western part of the historic territory of the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), which was split from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s hold of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) by Emperor [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman.md) in the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) along the line of the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md). It is traditionally considered to be part of the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), but the region can also include the areas of northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) which also border Skyrim. It is divided from the rest of High Rock by the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md).

## Westmark Moor
[Westmark Moor](Lore_Westmark_Moor.md) (also called **Markwasten Moor**) is a rural region in southeastern [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It is specifically found along the [Great Bay](Lore_Great_Bay.md) area where it meets the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) to the south. The region is mostly farmland, with its largest settlements being Hoarfrost Downs and Fell's Run. But southeast toward the coast are large gravesites and burial grounds, from the [Sanguine Barrows](Lore_Sanguine_Barrows.md) and Tribulation Crypt where Rivenspire's nobility are buried, to the legendary dungeon called the [Crypts of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md). The latter is considered part of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md).

## Whalefall

It acquired its name for all of the [whale](Lore_Whale.md) bones that litter it but for a long period of time, it was primarily used by the jailors of nearby Amenos Station, either as a training ground for their personnel or as a hunting ground to kill prisoners for sport.

## Whisper Grove
[Whisper Grove](Lore_Whisper_Grove.md) (later known as the **City of Ash**) was a [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) city in northern [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md). A [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) settlement located deep in the region's pristine jungles, many Bosmer who were unhappy with [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) joining the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) retreated to Whisper Grove as a refuge. The leader of the community, Erthas, voiced the dangers they saw in allying with the Altmeri and the Khajiit. They built the city at his behest and attracted some of the brightest Bosmer to live their in harmony for many years. However, he was secretly leading a cult of [Dagonites](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) as their Razor Master, and attacked Whisper Grove, opening a portal to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) and burning the settlement to the ground. With the help of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), the Bosmer defenders were able to push the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) back. After the death of Erthas, Mehrunes Dagon himself agreed to seal the portal in return for the settlement's leader, Gilraen.

## White River
The [White River](Lore_White_River.md) is the longest river in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), stretching from the south of the province to the northeast corner. Its source is [Lake Ilinalta](Lore_Lake_Ilinalta.md), north of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md). It flows northeast to join the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) past [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), the mouth of the river is called Slaughterfish Bay and is part of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md)'s borders. Along the way, it passes through [Riverwood](Lore_Riverwood.md) and near [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md). [Darkwater River](Lore_Darkwater_River.md) and [River Yorgrim](Lore_River_Yorgrim.md) are the two main tributaries of the White River.

## White Rose River
The [White Rose River](Lore_White_Rose_River.md) is an odd, roughly H-shaped river in central [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). There are two separate sources, both rising in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), just south of the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md). Both flow due north and are joined halfway along their lengths by a large stream before separating again and passing under the [Red Ring Road](Lore_Red_Ring_Road.md), emptying into [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md). This odd formation means a large island is formed between the river's lower reaches and its "cross-piece".

## Whiterun
[Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) is one of the nine major cities in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and it serves as the capital of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md). The city lies in the very heart of Skyrim and functions as the province's center of trade. It also holds great cultural and historical significance, having ties with the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md), who first discovered it in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). Whiterun has been known by many titles, such as the **Great City**, the **Jewel of the North**, and the **Heart of Skyrim**. In the [dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), the city is known as **Ahrolsedovah**, which means *Hill-of-Dragon*.

## Whiterun Hold
[Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md) is a [hold](Lore_Hold.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), located roughly in the middle of the province. Roads are numerous and well maintained, and visibility across the [tundra plains](Lore_Tundra_Plain.md) is excellent. The hold is relatively flat, surrounded the by mountains of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to the west of the [Tundra Plateau](Lore_Tundra_Plateau.md), the [Skyborn Range](Lore_Skyborn_Range.md) of [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md) and the [Yorgrim Forest](Lore_Yorgrim_Forest.md) of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) to the north, and the [Brittleshin Hills](Lore_Brittleshin_Hills.md) of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) to the south. However, Whiterun's southeastern corner is dominated by the gigantic and soaring [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) —the highest mountain in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The peak of [Lone Mountain](Lore_Lone_Mountain.md) is found in the center of the hold.

## Wilderking Court
The [Wilderking Court](Lore_Wilderking_Court.md) (also called the **Court of the Wilderking**, or simply, **The Court**) is a subregion of [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md), the southwestern region of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The cities of [Greenheart](Lore_Greenheart.md) and [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) are anchored on each side of the region, with the latter found on the crossroad towards [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md). The name earns its name through the presence of the [Wilderking](Lore_Wilderking.md), a god worshipped by the tribal [Wood Elves](Lore_Wood_Elves.md) that live in the region, such as the ones that live in [Bramblebreach](Lore_Bramblebreach.md) and the [Shrouded Vale](Lore_Shrouded_Vale.md). The Wilderking is worshipped across the land, and his will is enforced through the [Hollow](Lore_Hollow.md), stone constructs that live in the land, in ruins such as Shadows Crawl and the Labyrinth.

## Winterhold (city)
[Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) (also spelled out as **Winter Hold**) is one of the nine major cities in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and it serves as the capital city of the eponymous hold, [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md). For the longest time, Winterhold served as the capital of Skyrim, one with a rich and illustrious history, and even in its prime, it rivaled [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) to the west. Found on the once larger [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md), the city had an impressive harbor and many large districts for most of its history. Most of the city was lost when an event known as the [Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md) had cast most of it into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) in [4E 122](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_122). The most well-known landmark in the city is the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), Skyrim's premier institute in the [arcane arts](Lore_Magic.md). The [Fortress of Ice](Lore_Fortress_of_Ice.md), found somewhere to the south of the city, is also known as the great Ice Fortress of Winterhold.

## Winterhold (region)
[Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md) is a historical region located on the northeast coast of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), known for its inhospitable terrain and snow-blown landscape. Centered around the city of the [same name](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), it is one of the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md) of Skyrim, which are generally isolated by geography and choice. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Winterhold are simple fisherfolk and mostly work on the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) since there is little pasture for grazing and the cold winds prove brutal for livestock. One of the region's most noteworthy landmarks is the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), the province's premier school of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) and center of both academia and arcana.

## Woda
[Woda](Lore_Woda.md) is a regional duchy located somewhere in the jungles of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md); which flourished since before the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Empire.md) was founded and on beyond the reign of the [Dragonborn Emperor](Lore_Dragonborn.md) [Pelagius Septim IV](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_IV.md).

## Wolf's Eye Lighthouse
[Wolf's Eye Lighthouse](Lore_Wolf%27s_Eye_Lighthouse.md) also known as **Solitude Lighthouse** is a lighthouse at the northernmost coast of [Haafingar Hold](Lore_Haafingar.md). The lighthouse is very important for trade, with it being crucial for sailors navigating to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) by preventing ships crashing into the rocky terrain. The lighthouse has been around since at least [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). The lighthouse is looked after by a keeper, with the keeper in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) being a retired [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) sailor.

## Wolfskull Cave
[Wolfskull Cave](Lore_Wolfskull_Cave.md) is a natural cavern found in the alps of [Mount Kilkreath](Lore_Mount_Kilkreath.md) in the [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md) Hold, amidst the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It acquired its name from the Wolf-Queen of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), [Potema Septim](Lore_Potema.md), who used the underground fortress in the cavern for [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) rituals. Her reign of terror ended in [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137) when her kingdom was sacked by the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in the aftermath of the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md). Ever since then, the locals have been convinced that it is haunted by a malevolent spirit.

## Wolverine Hall
[Wolverine Hall](Lore_Wolverine_Hall.md) is a settlement situated just south of [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md). It was an attempt on the part of the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) to project power in a region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) where they were relatively powerless. The [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) tolerated the presence of the fort mostly because nobody cared enough to knock it down. Wolverine Hall housed the services not permitted in the town itself, such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) shrine. These were concealed within the fort itself. The Mages Guild and Fighters Guild chapter in Sadrith Mora was established in Wolverine Hall under lease from the Telvanni Council.

## Woodborne Hall
[Woodborne Hall](Lore_Woodborne_Hall.md) was the ample castle of the minor noble and traitor [Lord Woodborne](Lore_Woodborne.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md). The Hall itself was built by Lord Woodborne to withstand invasions and so was designed with a multitude of levers that only allowed access when pulled in the correct order. It is located on the north shore of Wayrest's lake, near the three-way point between Wayrest, [Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md), and the [Orsinium Area](Lore_Orsinium_Area.md). Just to the southeast is the village of Tuncart.

## Woodhearth
[Woodhearth](Lore_Woodhearth.md) is a coastal city in southwestern [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and the capital of the [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) region.

## Wretched Spire
The [Wretched Spire](Lore_Wretched_Spire.md) is a ruined settlement, hidden in the southern hills of the Sever, a region in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md). The settlement is a haven for denizens of the Deadlands, both mortal and [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) alike. It is because the Wretched Spire is the one place in [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s domain that cannot be influenced by them. And so because of this, daedra that are not bound to a summoner or faction, and even escaped mortal slaves can inhabit the town without interference. The southern section of the Wretched Spire is called the **Fleapits District** and it is where the uncivilized and dangerous residents live.

## Wrothgaria
[Wrothgaria](Lore_Wrothgaria.md) was the main city within the unconquered territories of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) immediately prior to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). The mountains bordered [Phrygias](Lore_Phrygias.md), [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md), [Kambria](Lore_Kambria.md), [Koegria](Lore_Koegria.md), [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire_(fiefdom).md), [Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md), [Orsinium Area](Lore_Orsinium_Area.md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), and [Gavaudon](Lore_Gavaudon_(fiefdom).md). The dominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline of the region was [Lyrezi](Lore_Lyrezi.md), and the regional deity was [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md). After the Warp in the West, Wrothgaria and much of the surrounding area became a part of the kingdom of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).

## Wrothgarian Mountains
The [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), also known as **Wrothgar**, or the **Wrothgar Mountains**, are a large region in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located at the northeast corner of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). The region is inhabited mostly by [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md), and the region was once inhabited by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). [Riekr](Lore_Riekr.md), the ice goblins, make their home in the Wrothgar Mountains as well. A portal to [Maelstrom](Lore_Maelstrom.md) can be found within the foothills of the Wrothgarian Mountains. The main vampire bloodline found here is the [Lyrezi](Lore_Lyrezi.md).
