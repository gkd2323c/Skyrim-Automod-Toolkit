# Twil

Twil is an obscure place associated with King [Hrol](Lore_Hrol.md) and Duke Quarl.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Remanada-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ROS-2)</sup>

## History

### Birth of the Alessian Empire
According to the official records of the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) when Empress Alessia's forces captured [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), all the dangerous magical relics of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) were taken and destroyed. Duke Quarl of Twil maintained until his last breath that an Elf King had fled with the chief artifacts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ROS-2)</sup>

### Birth of the Reman Empire
*[Remanada](Lore_Remanada.md)* states that Hrol was a king from lands beyond the lost Twil who along with sixteen western questing [knights](Lore_Knights.md), set out to heal the borders of his forebears as he had visions of the [Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) that was to come. At a point within the journey, Hrol and his men came upon the spirit of the Slave Queen, Empress [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) who bore the [Dragonfire Covenant](Lore_Dragonfires.md) in one hand, and the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) in the other. In a perhaps mad, divine driven attempt to restore the Empire unity of old, Hrol made union unto a hillock which he believed Alessia's spirit resided in, eventually leading to birth of [Reman](Lore_Reman.md). Two of his companions returned to their homeland beyond Twil said nothing about Hrol, and acted ashamed for him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Remanada-1)</sup>

## Notes
- A quote from [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) *King Hrol (seeker/Healer of Kingdom), "from the lands beyond lost Twil". Twil as Twilight. Grey Maybe. Aurbis. His knights numbered "eighteen less one," the number of the Hurling Disk.* links Twil to Twilight and [Gray Maybe](Lore_Gray_Maybe.md),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-3)</sup> a term used to refer to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Monomyth-4)</sup> [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE-5)</sup> and [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) on varying ocasions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Monomyth-4)</sup> He also linked the name to [Bendu Olo](Lore_Bendu_Olo.md), and asserted that he likes the name Twil as it is short, magical, and always in the west.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FC-6)</sup>
