Vvardenfell, also called the **Black Isle**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> is a large island located inside the bay-like [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), and is surrounded by mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) with the exception of its northern coast, which meets the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). The island is dominated by the great volcano, [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). The island itself is named after the original [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemeris.md) name of Red Mountain, literally translating to "City of the Strong Shield".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup> It is characterized by arid wastes, rocky highlands and coastal wetlands filled with unusual and unique flora and fauna. Red Mountain's lava flows, eruptions, and ash-fall cause Vvardenfell's landscape to undergo a constant cycle of death and rebirth. The soil becomes greatly fertilized thanks to the volcanic activity, and new plants and fungi emerge from the cooling ashes. It also causes the island to expand as the lava hardens.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VFAFLB-3)</sup>

Vvardenfell was initially inhabited by [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md), the only [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) clan to have their capital, [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md), on the island, as well as a [kingdom](Lore_Empires.md#Dwemer_Kingdoms) of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FSo KW-4)</sup> Following the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) that resulted in the erasure of House Dagoth, [disappearance](Lore_Disappearance_of_the_Dwarves.md) of the Dwemer, and ascension of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TBORM-5)</sup> all of the Vvardenfell Territory became an area held in trust for the people of Morrowind by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KRP-6)</sup> With the rise of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), Vvardenfell later become a preserve administrated by the Temple as part of the [Treaty of the Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), and except for a few Great House settlements sanctioned by the Temple, trade and development were often restricted. In [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), Vvardenfell was reorganized from a Temple preserve under Imperial protection into an Imperial Provincial District. The new District was divided into [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), and Temple Districts, each separately administered by local House Councils or Temple Priesthoods, and all under the advice and consent of [Duke Dren](Lore_Vedam_Dren.md) and the District Council in [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md). Local law became a mixture of House Law and Imperial Law in House Districts, jointly enforced by House guards and Legion guards, with Temple law and Imperial law enforced in the Temple district by [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ASHOM-7)</sup> Exploration and colonization by House [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and Imperial "outlanders" increased heavily in the years following the reorganization, and by [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), only an average of one in two individuals on Vvardenfell were Dunmer.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BBo3E-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Dialogue Races-9)</sup>

The corruption of Dagoth Ur's Ash Blight would have a dramatic effect on the environment and its inhabitants. By the late Third Era, Vvardenfell was noticeably less vibrant, and [Cliff Striders](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md), [Nix-Oxen](Lore_Nix-Ox.md), and [Vvardvarks](Lore_Vvardvark.md) were wiped out.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-VTANESO-10)</sup> During the [Red Year of 4E 5](Lore_Red_Year.md), Vvardenfell was devastated after the [Ministry of Truth](Lore_Ministry_of_Truth.md) crashed into the [city of Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md), causing Red Mountain to erupt. Most of the island was covered in ash, forcing much of the population to evacuate.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRY-11)</sup>

## History

### Early History
There are conflicting reports on the origins of Vvardenfell. Some sources accredit an eruption of Red Mountain to its separation from the mainland,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ro KZ-12)</sup> whereas Vivec's Sermons point to the usage of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TSLT-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DI1-14)</sup> However, some sources attest that the Inner Sea was present before both the first major eruption of Red Mountain and the activation of the Numidium.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TBOS-15)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup> Furthermore, [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) left behind maps of Vvardenfell's coastline dating to the [Middle Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Middle_Merethic_Era), far before the first Dwemer colonies.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-FOTN-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BTAOM-17)</sup> Ancient [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) maps showed no island in northern modern Morrowind, only a region labeled "Dwemereth" which encompassed the island of Vvardenfell and a large swath of what is now the mainland, with the coastline extending further north than Vvardenfell does in the present. Upon further examination, Hasphat Antabolis posited that in ancient times Vvardenfell was part of the mainland.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

When [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorers mapped out the coasts of Vvardenfell in the middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), they established wizard towers across the coast, such as [Ald Redaynia](Lore_Ald_Redaynia.md), [Bal Fell](Lore_Bal_Fell.md), and [Tel Mora](Lore_Tel_Mora.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BTAOM-17)</sup> [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md) initially held domain over the region, with their capital located at [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FSo KW-4)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SM-19)</sup> During the time of the [Nord Conquest](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), a Dwemer kingdom ruled in northern Morrowind in the region of Red Mountain on the island of Vvardenfell. The steadfastness and unity of the Dwemeri allowed them to remain embedded in the region compared to the divided and feuding [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) clans, and the kingdom remained independent when the rest of Morrowind ultimately fell to the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup>

Before she was married to Emperor [Pelagius Septim III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md), [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) was the Duchess of Vvardenfell.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TWQ8-20)</sup> She was also the heiress to the noble family, the [Ra'athim Clan](Lore_Ra%27athim.md) of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Vvardenfell was open to the rest of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and contained three known settlements; [Markgran Forest](Lore_Markgran_Forest.md), [Old Run](Lore_Old_Run.md), and [Stoneforest](Lore_Stoneforest.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ARMorrowind-22)</sup> After he banished Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), the battlemage, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) broke the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) into seven pieces and placed one of them in Dagoth-Ur, the dwarven mines underneath Red Mountain. Sometime later, the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) retrieved the [Hammer of Gharen](Lore_Hammer_of_Gharen.md) for the King of Ebonheart, who then struck it on the [Anvil of Mithas](Lore_Anvil_of_Mithas.md) and revealed the mines' location. The Champion then traveled to the island and acquired their last piece to assemble the staff.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ARDagoth-23)</sup>

### Imperial Administration
Previously a [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) preserve under [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) protection, Vvardenfell Territory was reorganized in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414) as an Imperial Provincial District. It was one of six Imperial administrative districts of the Morrowind province. Vvardenfell had been maintained as a preserve administrated by the Temple since the Treaty of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) in [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), and except for the independent [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) tribes and a few [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) settlements sanctioned by the Temple, Vvardenfell was previously undeveloped.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ASHOM-7)</sup> The region was host to large populations before the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md); few remained by the time of the reorganization, perhaps due to [the Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md). When the centuries-old Temple ban on trade and settlement of Vvardenfell was revoked by King [Llethan](Lore_Llethan.md) of Morrowind, a flood of Imperial colonists and Great House Dunmer came to Vvardenfell, expanding old settlements and building new ones.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ASHOM-7)</sup>

The new District was divided into [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), and Temple Districts, each separately administered by local House Councils or Temple Priesthoods, and all under the advice and consent of [Vedam Dren](Lore_Vedam_Dren.md) and the District Council in [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ASHOM-7)</sup>

### The Red Year
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg/200px-SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) Vvardenfell, with Red Mountain billowing ash In the absence of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), the Ministry of Truth, also known as the moon [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md), became unstable as the spells Vivec wove to keep it suspended in mid-air above the city of Vivec began to dissipate. The Dunmer Vuhon created a device known as the Ingenium that consumed souls to keep the Ministry of Truth from falling. Initially, only undesirables and criminals were fed to the Ingenium, but eventually souls of greater strength, which could feed the Ingenium for a time without causing the subject's immediate death, came to be preferred. In [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), when the Dunmer woman Ilzheven was taken to power the machine, her former lover Sul rescued her. Without a power source, the Ingenium destabilized and exploded. This caused the Ministry of Truth to resume its long-delayed descent and crash with full force into the city of Vivec.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TIC-24)</sup> Mere minutes after the destruction of the city, Red Mountain erupted.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRY-11)</sup>

The eruption obliterated Gnisis and inflicted widespread devastation on Sadrith Mora, Balmora, and Ald'ruhn. Beyond Vvardenfell, earthquakes from the eruption were felt as far away as [Tear](Lore_Tear.md) in the far south of mainland Morrowind, although [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), which was located closer to the epicenter, was spared any damage. Relief efforts were organized in the months following the disaster to bring aid to the outlying settlements of Vvardenfell, and at least one town, Balmora, was rebuilt.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRY-11)</sup> The Grazelands to the northeast of Red Mountain were known to have survived the eruption.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-25)</sup>

With the Dunmer distracted by the disaster and its aftermath, the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) saw an opportunity to enact vengeance for centuries of enslavement and exploitation, and instigated the [Accession War](Lore_Accession_War.md). The Argonian invasion delved deep into Morrowind, and elements of their forces advanced as far as Vvardenfell's southern coast, where they carried out rituals of unknown purpose at [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md), the boiling, water-filled crater where the city of Vivec once stood.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TIC-24)</sup>

## Politics
Following the Imperial reorganization, Vvardenfell was ruled by the Grand Council in Ebonheart, which consisted of representatives from the main powers present on Vvardenfell: The three Great Houses Hlaalu, Redoran and Telvanni, the Tribunal Temple, the Imperial Commission and the Imperial Magistrate. Duke Vedam Dren of House Hlaalu was the head of the council, representative of the Emperor and leader of the Vvardenfell district.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

Local law became a mixture of House Law and Imperial Law in House Districts, jointly enforced by House guards and Legion guards, with Temple law and Imperial law enforced in the Temple district by Ordinators. The Temple was still recognized as the majority religion, but some Dunmer such as the Ashlanders worship some [Daedra](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), and worship of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md) was protected by the legions and encouraged by Imperial cult missions.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

## Commerce
Imports and exports are tightly controlled by the [East Empire Company](Lore_East_Empire_Company.md), which deals largely with Imperial-friendly House Hlaalu. The company mines raw ebony and glass on the island. It holds exclusive charters on these items and most other native commodities, such as local spirits and agricultural products. Outside these official channels, the Empire forbids the trading of [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md), [Skooma](Lore_Skooma.md), and Dwemer artifacts. As such, smuggling is big business. Slavery is legal in Morrowind, and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) competes with smugglers to supply the market with forced labor.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

## Geography
Created by the last giant eruption of Red Mountain, Vvardenfell has several regions: [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md), [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md), [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md), [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md), [Firemoth](Lore_Firemoth.md), [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md), [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md), [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md), [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md) and [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md). The central and southeastern regions are mountainous wastes regularly scoured by ash storms.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

### Foyada
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/ON-place-Foyada.jpg/200px-ON-place-Foyada.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Foyada.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Foyada.jpg) An active foyada running down the Velothi tower of Shishara [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/MW-place-Molag_Amur.jpg/200px-MW-place-Molag_Amur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Molag_Amur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Molag_Amur.jpg) A foyada in the Molag Amur region **Foyadas**, or *fire-rivers* in the native Ashlander language, are deep, ash-dark ravines which run down from the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) volcano on Vvardenfell.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-MWGen F-27)</sup> The lava from Red Mountain is very fluid and runs almost like water. During an eruption, lava pours down the mountainside and leaves the area bare of all vegetation. Once a Foyada is created, the chance of a new one lessens, as the lava will normally run down the existing one. They are a good alternative to roads when heading towards the rugged mountain, as they have good footing, are clear of brush, and run for long distances.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-MWGen F-27)</sup>

[Foyada Mamaea](Lore_Foyada_Mamaea.md) is one of the largest foyadas running down the slopes of Red Mountain. It runs from the southern lip of the crater of the volcano through [Ghostgate](Lore_Ghostgate.md), which is built in the center of the Foyada, then generally south-southwest, past [Moonmoth Legion Fort](Lore_Moonmoth_Legion_Fort.md) before finally ending a bit east of [Hla Oad](Lore_Hla_Oad.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MWGen FM-28)</sup>

Eight other named foyadas exist on Vvardenfell: Foyada Ashur-Dan; Foyada Bani-Dad; [Foyada Drura](Lore_Foyada_Drura.md); Foyada Esannudan; Foyada Ilibaal; Foyada Nadanat; Foyada Zabirbael; and the lost [Valley of the Wind](Lore_Valley_of_the_Wind.md).

### Settlements
Some of the earliest remaining remanants of native elven settlements on Vvardenfell come in the form of several strongholds dotted throughout the island to fend off Nord incursions in the era before the Great Houses would emerge in early Dunmer culture.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-29)</sup> Before the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the biggest towns were [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) and [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md). Within the island are various other remains of distant times found in form of Daedric Shrines, ancient Velothi Towers, and Dwemer Ruins.

## People
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/MW-concept-Dunmer_Stronghold.jpg/200px-MW-concept-Dunmer_Stronghold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Dunmer_Stronghold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Dunmer_Stronghold.jpg) Concept art of a Dunmer Stronghold (Morrowind) Native to Vvardenfell since the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) are the dark-skinned, red-eyed [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) (known as the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) ages ago), which make about half of the population. The other half are divided roughly equally among the other nine [Races](Lore_Races.md) of Tamriel. Inhabitants of Vvardenfell include members of three of the five Great Houses, Velothi commoners, nomadic [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) and members of the Tribunal Temple, to which all wilderness areas ostensibly belong. Outlanders are not particularly welcome, especially in Telvanni-controlled areas. The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and Imperial guilds are well established though and accept foreigners openly. House Hlaalu is also rather welcoming to them, as evidenced by their two non-Dunmer Councilors.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-place-Vvardenfell.jpg/200px-ON-place-Vvardenfell.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) Vvardenfell seen from [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Dunmer inhabitants of Vvardenfell have a keenly-developed sense of identity, as they live in a sacred place that has been host to many of the defining moments in Dunmeri history. The common people regard themselves as servants of the Living Gods and the heirs of the Velothi movement.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup> Saint Veloth is credited with bringing the Chimer and by extension, the Dunmer to their proper place in the world. Daedra worship has long-since fallen out of favor with most of the population, but it still holds a valued place in culture and tradition, as the Tribunal are regarded as either complementing or amicably replacing the worship of the Lords of Misrule.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

Despite the decline in their worship, many species of Daedra, ranging from the bestial [Clannfear](Lore_Clannfear.md) and [Daedroths](Lore_Daedroth.md) to the sentient [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) and [Golden Saints](Lore_Aureal.md), are a common sight on the island. These Daedra are usually found lurking among the ruins of Daedric shrines or in Vvardenfell's wilderness and tend to attack approaching mortals on sight. Summoned Daedra were also utilized by the Temple for ritualistic purposes, and by high-ranking members of the Great Houses (particularly of House Telvanni) as security.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

Central to the psyche of the Vvardenfell Dunmer is Red Mountain, whose geography and silhouette dominates the region. Constant storms dictate the course of everyday life and give the different environments their distinctive characters. The volcano also keeps the island in a constant state of flux, as evidenced by old maps showing the entire island made up of Ashlands, lava rivers running to the sea, and accounts of ash falling onto the streets of the city of Vivec.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

### Transportation
Inhabitants of Vvardenfell rely on beasts of burden, such as [Guar](Lore_Guar.md) or [Silt Striders](Lore_Silt_Strider.md) (giant insects), or make use of various seagoing vessels to transport people and cargo. Guild Guides provided by the Mages Guild can also provide transportation.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-MGTransit Dialog-30)</sup>

## Religion
Since the early settlement of the Chimer people on Vvardenfell, [ancestor](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) and [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship was the most widespread religious belief, but since the rise of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), the worship of the Tribunes has become more widespread among the settled house Dunmer. Vvardenfell became the location of the Pilgrimages of the Seven Graces and site of the living god [Vivec's](Lore_Vivec.md) [eponymous city](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Vivec_(city)).<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>
When the Empire settled on Vvardenfell after it was opened to colonisation in 3E 414, it brought over the worship of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Gods_N.md#Nine_Divines) through the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), though the Temple remained the dominant religion among the native population.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

The [ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) of Vvardenfell had their own separate beliefs, shunning the Tribunal Temple in favor of traditional Velothi ancestor and daedra worship, along with the [Nerevarine Cult](Lore_Nerevarine_Cult.md). Before the [Nerevarine](Lore_People_N.md#Nerevarine) proved themselves to Vivec as the true reincarnation of [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), the Temple's had used their [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) to persecute the Nerevarine Cult.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup> Among some of the persecuted followers of the Nerevarine prophecies were the [dissident priests](Lore_Dissident_Priests.md), who were a sect of reformists who questioned the legitimacy of the Tribunal Temple.
The Ordinators of the Tribunal Temple also had an active role in hunting worshipers of the so-called [Bad Daedra](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md). Despite their persecution by the Temple, many cults devoted to the House of Troubles had many shrines and sites of worship throughout Vvardenfell, mostly located in daedric ruins in the wilderness. Despite the religious freedom the Empire had brought, the Temple had been given the right to persecute these heretics under the terms of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md).<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

After Red Year and the fall of the Tribunal, the main focus of Morrowind religion has shifted back to daedra worship with the [New Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md#The_New_Temple), revering the Reclamations (Boethiah, Azura, and Mephala), with the previous deprecated Tribunes demoted to the status of saints and heroes.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

Since the destruction of Vivec City and Red Mountain's erruption on Vvardenfell, it's unknown if the island presents much spiritual significance anymore.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>
