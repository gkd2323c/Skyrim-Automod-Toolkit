# Traps

| --- |

During your travels in [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md), you will encounter a variety of **traps** and physical hazards. Most traps are activated by a specific "trigger", which may be a physical object or an invisible proximity trigger built into the game. Several [puzzles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Puzzles) are trapped if you fail to solve them correctly. Traps often blend into their environment or may be hidden inside walls or other features, making them difficult to spot and avoid. With experience, most traps can be avoided altogether, or even turned against your enemies.

## Triggers
Triggers are distinct objects that may activate traps or other devices, such as doors or gates, through direct physical contact or manual activation. Trigger activation is not limited to player characters; [followers](Skyrim_Followers.md), [NPCs](Skyrim_NPCs.md), and sometimes [creatures](Skyrim_Creatures.md) can interact with a trigger device. Some triggers can be activated at a distance by firing an arrow or a [Magelight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Magelight) spell at them.

### Button
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md), [military forts](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md), and [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) <br> **Description:** Look for small inset plaques; buttons in Dwarven ruins are usually set in ornate pedestals and have a small blue orb. <br> **Effect:** Pressing the button will activate its associated object, which may be a door or a trap. <br> **Notes:** Some buttons can be activated multiple times, allowing the associated doors to open and close or causing traps to re-fire. |

Buttons may open and close doors or gates, or may trigger a nearby trap. They are sometimes used in puzzles (like the one used to activate the Oculory in the [Tower of Mzark](Skyrim_Tower_of_Mzark.md) during [Elder Knowledge](Skyrim_Elder_Knowledge.md)), where they usually must be activated in a specific order to complete the puzzle. The button closest to a closed door may activate a trap instead of opening the door as expected (in these cases, the correct button is usually located elsewhere in the room or around a corner). Some buttons may be hidden or found locked behind a barrier, which must be unlocked before they can be activated.

Followers can be instructed to activate switches, including buttons.

### Lever
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md), [military forts](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md), and [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) <br> **Description:** Levers have a wooden or metal handle in a rounded metal base, usually mounted on floors or walls. Levers appear in plain, Nordic, and Dwemer styles, but their functionality is always the same. <br> **Effect:** Pulling the lever will activate its associated object, which may be a door or a trap. <br> **Notes:** Some levers can be activated multiple times, allowing the associated doors to open and close or causing traps to re-fire. |

Levers are commonly used to open doors, gates, or grates, but may also be used to deactivate a trap. When used as a puzzle element, levers usually must be activated in the correct order to solve the puzzle; activating the levers in the incorrect order may trigger a trap, such as a barrage of [darts](#Darts). You may also be required to throw a lever after solving another puzzle (such as a series of [rune totems](#Spinning_Symbol.2FRune_Totems)) in order to open a door, like one found in [Bleak Falls Barrow](Skyrim_Bleak_Falls_Barrow_(place).md) during its [related quest](Skyrim_Bleak_Falls_Barrow_(quest).md). The lever closest to a closed door may activate a trap instead of opening the door as expected (in these cases, the correct lever is usually located elsewhere in the room or around a corner). Some levers may also be hidden or found locked in small wall-mounted cages, which must be unlocked before they can be activated.

Followers can be instructed to activate switches, including levers.

### Pressure Plate
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md), [military forts](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md), and [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) <br> **Description:** Pressure plates may look like a circular metal plate, a round stone with a Nordic symbol, a plain raised stone, or a square Dwemer floor tile. A noticeable clacking sound can be heard when the plate is triggered. <br> **Effect:** When enough weight is applied to the plate, the associated trap will activate. Firing an arrow at the plate will also activate the trigger. <br> **Notes:** After the weight is removed, the trigger will reset, allowing its associated trap to trigger again. |

Pressure plates are used to trigger a variety of traps, including [battering rams](#Battering_Ram), [spike walls](#Spike_Wall), and [darts](#Dart_Trap). They are sometimes used as an element in puzzles. Although pressure plates cannot be disarmed, they are most often placed in the center of a hallway or passage, and activation can often be avoided by hugging the walls or simply jumping over the trigger surface. You can also place a heavy object, such as a body, on the plate; the trap will be triggered, but the weight of the object on the plate will prevent the trap from firing again.

The [sneak](Skyrim_Sneak.md) perk *[Light Foot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Light_Foot)* will allow you to walk and run directly over pressure plates without activating them, even when in [werewolf](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md) form.

### Pull Chain
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md), [military forts](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md), and [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) <br> **Description:** A pull chain is a wall-mounted switch that consists of a length of chain with a ring attached to the end. Pull chains appear in plain, Nordic, and Falmer styles, but their functionality is always the same. <br> **Effect:** Pulling the chain will activate its associated object, which may be a door or a trap. <br> **Notes:** Some pull chains can be activated multiple times, allowing the associated doors to open and close or causing traps to re-fire. |

Pull chains are most commonly used to open gates or doors or to disable traps. You may have to activate two or more pull chains in order to open a particular barrier. Pull chains may also be used as an element in puzzles, such as the third test in the [Twilight Sepulcher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Twilight_Sepulcher) during [Darkness Returns](Skyrim_Darkness_Returns.md). The pull chain closest to a closed door may activate a trap or a different door instead of opening the door you expected (in these cases, the correct button is usually located elsewhere in the room or around a corner). Pull chains can blend into dark dungeon walls, making them difficult to spot. They may also be hidden or found locked behind a barrier, which must be unlocked before they can be activated.

Followers can be instructed to activate switches, including pull chains.

### Tension Cable
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md), [military forts](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md), [Skyrim:Caves](Skyrim_Caves.md) and [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) <br> **Description:** Look for a small box mechanism attached to a door or chest with a cable connecting to a nearby pin. A snapping sound is heard when the cable breaks. <br> **Effect:** When the door or chest or opened, the cable snaps, triggering the associated trap. <br> **Notes:** Tension cables lose their functionality after they have been broken. They can be disabled with a [lockpick](Skyrim_Lockpicking.md#Skill_Usage). |

Tension cables are typically attached to locked doors or chests, set to trigger traps such as [darts](#darts) when the container is opened by an unsuspecting adventurer. While the mechanism attached to the cable is sometimes in plain view, it is advisable to check both sides of a container before attempting to open it. If you do not have any [lockpicks](Skyrim_Lockpick.md) or choose not to disarm the cable, you can usually avoid any damage by simply by analyzing the trap and dodging it after intentionally triggering it. Arrows or spells aimed at the tension cable can do this from a safe distance—preferably somewhere near a corner, doorway, or solid object you can use for cover from projectile traps.

### Tripwire
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md), [military forts](Skyrim_Military_Forts.md), [mines](Skyrim_Mines.md), and [towers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Towers) <br> **Description:** You will see a cord stretched across an area of floor a few inches above the ground, often secured to wooden stakes. A snapping sound is heard when the wire breaks. <br> **Effect:** Passing through the wire causes the cord to snap, activating the associated trap. <br> **Notes:** Tripwires lose their functionality after they have been broken. |

Because tripwires can only work once, they most commonly trigger single use traps like [falling rocks](#Falling_Boulders), [swinging maces](#Swinging_Mace), and [battering rams](#Battering_Ram). You can avoid the trap by jumping over the cord. You can also break the wire manually with the [activate](Skyrim_Controls.md#Activate) button, or by shooting at the wire with an arrow or spell.

### Valve
| |

These [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) mechanisms utilize steam energy to keep a trap ready until it is needed; when the valve is opened, the steam pressure releases the trap on the unsuspecting victim. Valves can also be used to operate doors or gates.

### Weight Sensitive Plate
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) <br> **Description:** Look for pedestal topped with a weight-sensitive slab, usually bearing a desirable object such as a potion or a piece of equipment. <br> **Effect:** When the item is removed, the trap is triggered. <br> **Notes:** The plate will only activate once and cannot be disabled. |

You can use the [Alteration](Skyrim_Alteration.md) spell [Telekinesis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Telekinesis_Effect) to remove items from weight sensitive plates from a safe distance or angle. Items can also be knocked free with arrows or spells, but may be more difficult to find after they have fallen. The [Slow Time](Skyrim_Slow_Time.md) [shout](Skyrim_Shouts.md) can also be used to quickly snag the item and run away before the harmful effects of the trap are triggered. It is also possible to switch the object on the plate with an item from your inventory if you wish to avoid activating the trap at all.

## Physical Traps
These traps deal physical damage and may forcibly move the victim on impact. With the right timing, many of these traps can be successfully used against nearby enemies.

| **Found in:** [Mines](Skyrim_Mines.md) and [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) <br> **Description:** These large, Nordic-carved logs are mounted to the ceiling with a bracket. <br> **Effect:** When triggered, the log swings down and forward, causing physical damage and pushing its target backward. <br> **Notes:** Battering rams may reset or may only fire once, depending on the triggering mechanism. |

The majority of battering rams are activated by trigger mechanisms such as [pressure plates](#Pressure_Plate) or [tripwires](#Tripwire) placed in the middle of a passage, and are designed to strike an area slightly ahead of the trigger (intended to catch you as you run past the trigger). Battering rams attached to chains will only deploy once, while those attached to metal frames reset after a few seconds. Watch the floor for possible triggers and the ceiling for any rigged logs in order to avoid this trap. If you accidentally trigger a battering ram, back up quickly to avoid being struck by the heavy log. Since battering rams strike a fairly small area, it is possible to sidestep the ram as you continue moving forward, but this feature also makes it more difficult to use a ram against enemies.

You can also manually move bear traps by dragging them, in order to place them on more tactical locations.

There is a very small chance that you will contract Ataxia, Bone Break Fever, Brain Rot, Rattles, Rockjoint, or Witbane from swinging blade traps.

## Magical Traps
These traps take on a variety of forms, but they all cause elemental or magical damage. The effects of magical traps can be resisted through [racial](Skyrim_Races.md) [powers](Skyrim_Powers.md), [enchantments](Skyrim_Enchanting.md), and [alchemy](Skyrim_Alchemy.md) [potions](Skyrim_Potions.md).

| |

Flammable gases are only found in a few places, but can be quite dangerous. The simplest way to disable these traps is to quickly run up and grab the soul gem; grabbing it from behind is a better way to avoid damage, but is not always possible depending on how the trap is placed. It is possible to knock the soul gem out of its stand from a range using a spell, shout, or well-placed arrow, but the trap may not always be disabled if the gem is only knocked askew. The [Telekinesis](Skyrim_Telekinesis.md) spell is very handy for dealing with these traps, you can use it to lift the soul gem completely off its stand.<sup>[*verification needed — see [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:talk_Traps#Telekinesis)*]</sup> It is also possible for one of these traps to dislodge its own soul gem.

Lava can be found in the area surrounding the Aetherium Forge. Coming into contact with lava is dangerous; the moment you touch it, you will receive 150 pts of fire damage, and will be burned by an additional 15 pts of fire damage per second for four seconds.

Lava is also encountered in the Summer's Chords section of [Runoff Caverns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Runoff_Caverns)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Forgotten_Seasons.md)</sup>.

### Lightning<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>

Random lightning strikes frequently occur while in the Soul Cairn. Generally, these are harmless unless they happen to strike you directly.

Constant lightning strikes are encountered within [Runoff Caverns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Runoff_Caverns)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Forgotten_Seasons.md)</sup> prior to completing the [related quest](Skyrim_Forgotten_Seasons_(quest).md) and can be a bit more dangerous due to the enclosed indoor spaces.

### River Rapids
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** [Rivers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rivers) <br> [Darkfall Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Darkfall_Cave) **Description:** Watch for rapidly flowing sections of river, usually at the crests of waterfalls. <br> **Effect:** When you are caught in rapids, the water pushes you in the direction of flow. <br> **Notes:** This hazard is passive and cannot be disabled. |

Rapids do not damage you directly and are generally harmless, though it can make it difficult to find a slope to get back onto the shore. If you become swept up in rapids, you risk being washed over waterfalls, which may prove harmful or fatal depending on the height of the falls. One is necessary to discover the Forgotten Vale, and the [Vale](Skyrim_Forgotten_Vale.md) uses many of these rapids for transportation between areas.

### Wind
| |
| --- |
| **Found in:** The slopes between [High Hrothgar](Skyrim_High_Hrothgar.md) and the [Throat of the World](Skyrim_Throat_of_the_World.md) <br> **Description:** Powerful swirls of wind and snow block your path. <br> **Effect:** Standing in the wind causes severe [frost damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Damage). <br> **Notes:** The [Clear Skies](Skyrim_Clear_Skies.md) shout will temporarily dispel the wind. |

Although Clear Skies will clear a path for you through the wind, it will return after a few seconds, so you'll need to move quickly and shout multiple times to traverse the path to the top of the mountain. It is possible to [climb](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mountain_Climbing) around the areas blocked by wind, but this can be difficult, and falling off the mountain could result in instant death. Even with a high level of [frost resistance](Skyrim_Resist_Frost.md), the magical wind can kill you quickly.
