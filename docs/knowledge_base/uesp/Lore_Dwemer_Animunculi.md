# Dwemer Animunculi

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/LG-cardart-Reconstruction_Engine.png/200px-LG-cardart-Reconstruction_Engine.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Reconstruction_Engine.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Reconstruction_Engine.png) An automaton spider being reconstructed Dwemer Animunculi<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> (also known as **Steam Animunculi**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Loading-2)</sup> **Dwemer Constructs**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Adamant Horse-3)</sup> **Dwarven Constructs**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> **Dwarven Automatons**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HB-5)</sup> **Dwemer Warbots**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> **Dwarven Centurions**, and **Dwarven Centurion Robots**)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RGSphere-7)</sup> are mechanical [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) created by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). Made out of [Dwarven metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md), animunculi were used as war machines and guards. Many of them continue to protect the underground ruins of Dwemer cities long after the disappearance of their creators in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700). They are commonly encountered by scholars and adventurers who venture into the ruins; the animunculi perceive them as invaders, and even worker constructs will attack trespassers on sight.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TESConstruct-8)</sup>

Dwemer constructs are made with a variety of [Dwarven Metals](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md), with Dwarven brass being the most common. Rarer are constructs forged with alloys of [Adamant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Adamant),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Adamant Horse-3)</sup> [Glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vitrine),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Vitrine Wolf-9)</sup> and [Ebon Steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebon_Steel).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Ebon Steel Spider-10)</sup> Constructs made within the Infernium Forge are especially unusual, and are thought to be crafted by a single Dwarven artisan.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOInfernium-11)</sup>

It is inferred from old Chimer stories that the Dwarves had long held the ability to create Animunculi, [Chimarvamidium](Lore_Chimarvamidium.md) details an era in which Dwemer Animunculi were common and well known to Chimer tribes, but the use of Dwarven Heavy Plate-Mail was new and unknown to them, so much so that a Dwemer in heavy armor could disguise themselves as a Dwemer construct.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Chimarvamidium-12)</sup>

## Function
Dwemer animunculi function on steam power, magic and [tonal architecture](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md). Constructs around [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), such as in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), are equipped with soul gems and [dynamo cores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dynamo_Cores).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SR-13)</sup> While soul gems play an important role in keeping Dwemer Animunculi "charged",<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONNeramo-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> they are not their main power source.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Research_Notes-17)</sup> Dynamo Cores serve the purpose of distributing the energy of the soul gems into resonators which create "[harmonic resonances](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md)" which heat the boilers that create the mechanical steam power that allows them to function.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Juli-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOBedyni-19)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Research_Notes-17)</sup> Constructs from [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) that are removed from the region are unable to function in other parts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Returning the animunculus to the region's vicinity somehow restores its functionality.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Senilius-20)</sup> Research in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) suggested the animunculi found in much of western Tamriel only deactivated when removed from a ruin as a security measure, but with specialized tools and knowledge it was possible to reprogram them to perform other tasks.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONNeramo-14)</sup>

Some researchers, such as Neramo and [Aicantar](Lore_Aicantar%27s_Lab_Journal.md), have managed to control automatons by reverse-engineering and readjusting them. The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) was also known to have tinkered with Dwemer constructs, beyond the capabilities of even the most talented individuals.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Divayth Fyr-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> His mechanical creations, known as [Fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md) and [Factotums](Lore_Factotum.md), are similar, but not considered animunculi.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-creature-Sentinel_of_Rkugamz_04.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Sentinel_of_Rkugamz_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Sentinel_of_Rkugamz_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Sentinel_of_Rkugamz_04.jpg) A blue glow can indicate the use of lightning Some Dwemer animunculi have the interesting ability to power up, emitting a colored glow from the runes engraved on their metal plating that seem to indicate a function. Dwemer Spiders can emit a small electric barrier which they can use to charge up, turning their runes blue. The creation of such a barrier alerts other nearby constructs to absorb power as well. Constructs charged in such a manner can fire off bursts of electricity to attack invaders. When powered constructs are destroyed, they violently explode, releasing their stored electricity.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOSpider Charge-23)</sup> Sometime in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) adventurers journeyed into [Darkshade Caverns](Lore_Darkshade_Caverns.md) beneath [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md), discovering the Dwemer ruins of Rkugamz. It was guarded by the Sentinel of Rkugamz, which was unique in that it could change color without assistance from other constructs. Its coloration signaled its functions. Green indicated that the Sentinel was calling Dwarven Spiders with regenerative functions, blue signaled that the Guardian was about to fire off electric blasts, and red signaled to the adventurers that the Sentinel was about to spin its blades around wildly in pursuit of its target.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOSOR-24)</sup>

Later that year, the Undaunted explored Rkugamz, where they discovered a Dwemer machine known as the Great Engine with the aid of a member of the Mages Guild. The Engine Guardian, a giant dwarven spider constructed to protect the Great Engine, hindered the Undaunted's efforts to disable the machine so Rkugamz could be explored further. The Engine Guardian shared the Sentinel's lightning-based properties when it glowed blue. However, its functions while glowing red and green differed from the Sentinel of Rkugamz. Instead of providing a means to regenerate itself, the Guardian emitted poisonous gas when it glowed green. Its red glow was a signal that the Engine Guardian had activated fire cannons that were installed below its mantle.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOEG-25)</sup> The Sentinel of Rkugamz and the Engine Guardian are two notable examples of how the runes engraved on a construct can glow to indicate different functions. While some colors, like blue, can maintain some semblance of uniformity in what they represent, others aren't always as easily predictable.

Not all glowing runes exist to signal that a combative function is being performed. Some construct colors exist for cosmetic purposes. For example, there exists rare ebony constructs which possess a golden double crescent known as a "Solar Arc," which some scholars believe gives tribute to the sun. This iconography isn't seen in many of the Dwemer's other works.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONSolar Arc-26)</sup>

## Varieties
There are many types of animunculi found in Dwemer ruins all over Tamriel.

### Architon
Dwarven Architons are humanoid automatons located in [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md), mostly within [The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). They protect the ancient dwemer ruins. They are imbued with [aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md) crystals.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

The Dynastor served as a carriage of sorts. Several Dwarven Spheres were housed in the central carapace. At the appropriate time, they would spring from a concealed chute and presumably operate in tandem to defeat whatever foe they encountered.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup>

### [Centurion](Lore_Dwarven_Centurion.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg) A Dwarven Centurion **Dwarven Centurions** (also known as **Dwemer Centurions** and **Steam Centurions**) are heavily armored constructs with either an extendable mace or hammer on one arm and a spring-loaded blade in the other. Some can emit a scalding blast of steam. They are not as common of a sight as lesser constructs in Dwarven ruins. *[Ruins of Kemel-Ze](Lore_Ruins_of_Kemel-Ze.md)* implies that the centurions have a weakness to frost and magic attacks, due to the steam that drives them, although they are highly resistant against fire, frost, and shock. They are sometimes found attached to a charging gantry when not in use.

While they are typically taller than most men, they can vary in size. Many of the Steam Centurions found in Morrowind are actually shorter than men.

**Found in:** - [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Dwarven_Mechanical_Giant), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Steam_Centurion), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Dwarven_Centurion.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Dwarven_Centurion), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Dwarven_Centurion)

### Numidium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg/200px-MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) Anumidium, as seen in *[Divine Metaphysics](Lore_Divine_Metaphysics.md)* [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), also known as **Anumidium**, **Anumidum**, the **Brass God**, the **Prime Gestalt**, **NM**, the **[Brass Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md)**, **Walk-Brass**, **Big Walker**, **Walking Star**, and the **Divine Skin**, was a colossal construct of [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) origin. It was constructed by Dwemeri [Tonal Architect](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) Lord [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md) to be a new god, powered by the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md), made to retake [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) from the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and possibly to make the Dwemer race [immortal](Lore_Immortality.md). The Dwemer vanished from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) before the Brass God could be activated, though their [disappearance](Lore_Disappearance_of_the_Dwarves.md) may be directly related to this endeavor.

The Numidium was, in practice, a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) of devastating power. A "thousand foot tall automaton, a [golem](Lore_Golem.md) or an atronach<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> of sorts", it was used by Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to complete his [conquest](Lore_Tiber_War.md) of Tamriel at the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), but was destroyed afterward. The golem was rebuilt and reactivated centuries later, and became the center of the phenomenon known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), after which it was destroyed again. [Temporal distortions](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) have accompanied each known use of the Numidium, to the point that the Warp in the West is sometimes known as the "Second Numidian Effect".

Some believe that the Numidium was intrinsically evil: a ranking [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) called it the "most profane" of the Dwemer's creations, a "walking horror", the "Antipodal-God-Thing that reigned on the darkest pole of the sacred Nirn-Sphere", and the "welded knot at the center of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) that must be untied". The Apostles considered the Numidium the greatest threat to [Sotha Sil's](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) goals of bringing about the Nirn-Ensuing and the Anuic convergence of Anuvanna'si. Indeed, in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Clockwork City's](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) supervising Factotum, Aios, would name Numidium first among the existential threats to the City.

A second Numidium, [Akulakhan](Lore_Akulakhan.md), was partially constructed in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the late Third Era by [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md). However, the destruction of Kagrenac's enchantments on the Heart of Lorkhan in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) prevented its activation. [Akulakhan](Lore_Akulakhan.md) was based on Numidium's design.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Dagoth_Ur_(facility))

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fa/ON-pet-Dwarven_Scarab.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Dwarven_Scarab.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Dwarven_Scarab.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Dwarven_Scarab.jpg) Dwemer Scarab **[Dwarven Scarabs](Lore_Dwarven_Scarab.md)** or **Dwemer Scarabs** are animunculi that resemble organic [scarabs](Lore_Scarab.md) and vary in size, with the most notable example being the non-animate one in the Dwemer ruins of [Bthzark](Lore_Bthzark.md) on [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), which could be transformed into the shape of Man. Various Dwemer Scarab schematics were discovered in Bthzark, and in Mzuleft, located on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Many of the small Dwemer Scarab constructs are present at the Dwarven ruins around [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and in [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md), and in the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md).

**Found in:** - [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Investigate_the_Ruins), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Dwarven_Scarab_(creature))

### [Sentry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Sentry)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/ON-creature-Dwarven_Sentry.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Dwarven_Sentry.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Dwarven_Sentry.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Dwarven_Sentry.jpg) A Dwarven Sentry **Dwarven Sentries** (also referred to as **Dwarven Theodolites**) are spherical animunculi found in [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md). Sentries patrol ruins in a similar fashion to [Dwarven spheres](Lore_Dwarven_Sphere.md), extending their necks to emit an electrical shock designed to incapacitate intruders. They were primarily used as an automated measuring device designed to explore and survey subterranean areas. They can also generate a barrier to protect nearby animunculi. Sentries have been observed to lock themselves into mechanical frames to become fixed long-range attackers. Although they are robotic in nature, they have been observed to show intelligent movement, as if reacting with emotions.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Dwarven_Sentry), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Power_Sphere)

### [Sphere](Lore_Dwarven_Sphere.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg) A Dwarven Sphere **Dwarven Spheres** (also known as **Centurion Spheres**, **Sphere Bots**, **Dwarven Centurion Robots**, and simply **Dwarven Centurions**) are a common form of Dwemer animunculus that have proven to be agile fighters, and are much stronger than the smaller [Dwarven Spider](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md). Dwarven Spheres patrol ruins in a spherical form until they encounter an opponent. At this point, they will emerge from their "shells" to attack.

Dwarven Spheres can be armed with different tools. Some are armed with an extendable sword and a shield, while others, such as the Centurion Archers of [Bamz-Amschend](Lore_Bamz-Amschend.md), are equipped with crossbows. A specialized version of the sphere known as the Dwarven Arquebus has a lightning cannon in place of blades.

**Found in:** - [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Dwarven_Centurion), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Centurion_Sphere), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Dwarven_Sphere.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Dwarven_Sphere), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Dwarven_Sphere)

### [Spider](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/ON-creature-Has-Many-Legs.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Has-Many-Legs.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Has-Many-Legs.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Has-Many-Legs.jpg) A Dwarven Spider [Dwarven Spiders](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md) (also known as **Dwemer Spiders** and **Centurion Spiders**) are small constructs resembling [spiders](Lore_Spider.md) that are very common in [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruins. They are versatile automatons and perform a variety of functions, such as guarding locations. They can often be found scuttling around ruins, sometimes repairing things. Swarming spiders are present in some ruins, where they burst out of pressure vaults to attack intruders.

### [Vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Horse.jpeg/200px-ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Horse.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Horse.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Horse.jpeg) A horse Vamidium [Vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md) is the [Dwemeris](Lore_Dwemer_Language.md) word for "mount". It is used to refer to the many different types of animunculi constructed by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) for use as steam-powered mechanical mounts. Many of these constructs were made to imitate nature, taking the form of animals commonly used as mounts by the other [races](Lore_Races.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Like other animunculi, vamidiums appear to utilize [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) and [dynamo cores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dynamo_Cores) as a power source.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)

## Notes
- Dwemer Animunculi were documented within the Infinite Archive in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). There, copies of them were subsequently created as [maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), creatures composed of vivified ink manifested by stories from books, and serve as mindless extensions of the [Daedric Lord](Lore_Daedric_Lord.md) [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Q.26A-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-MM-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Enemies-37)</sup>
