The Adabal-a **by [Morihaus](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Morihaus)** *The memoirs of Morihaus, consort to Alessia and Taker of the Citadel* *
---
*Editor's Note: The Adabal-a is traditionally believed to be the memoirs of [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md), consort to [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) the Slave Queen. While this cannot be historically verified, the Adabal-a is certainly among the oldest written accounts to come down to us from the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).*

### PELINAL'S DEATH

- And in the blood-floored [throne room](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md) of [White-Gold](Skyrim_White-Gold_Tower.md), the severed head of [Pelinal](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) spoke to the winged-bull, Morihaus, demigod lover of Al-Esh, saying, "Our enemies have undone me, and spread my body into hiding. In mockery of [divine](Lore_Aedra.md) purpose, the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) cut me into [eighths](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), for they are obsessed with this number."

- And Morihaus, confused, snorted through his ring, saying, "Your crusades went beyond her counsel, Whitestrake, but I am a bull, and therefore reckless in my wit. I think I would go and gore our prisoners if you had left any alive. You are blood-made-glorious, uncle, and will [come again](Lore_Shezarrine.md), as fox animal or light. [Cyrod](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) is still ours."

- Then Pelinal spoke again for the last time: "Beware, Morihaus, beware! With the foresight of death I know now that my foe yet lives, bitter knowledge to take to my grave. Better that I had died believing myself the victor. Although cast beyond the [doors of night](Lore_Oblivion.md), he will return. Be vigilant! I can no longer shield the host of Men from [Umaril](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md)'s retribution."

### ALESSIA'S YOUTH DURING THE SLAVE-YEARS

- [Perrif's](Lore_Perrif.md) original tribe is unknown, but she grew up in Sard, anon [Sardarvar Leed](Lore_Sardavar_Leed.md), where the Ayleids herded in men from across all the [Niben](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md): [kothri](Lore_Kothri.md), [nede](Lore_Nede.md), [al-gemha](Lore_Al-Gemha.md), [men-of-'kreath](Lore_Men-of-kreath.md) (though these were later known to be imported from the [North](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)), [keptu](Lore_Keptu.md), [men-of-ge](Lore_Men-of-ge.md) (who were eventually destroyed when the Flower King [Nilichi](Lore_Nilichi.md) made great sacrifice to an [insect god](Lore_Insect_God.md) named [lost]), [al-hared](Lore_Al-Hared.md), [men-of-ket](Lore_Men-of-ket.md), others; but this was Cyrod, the heart of the [imperatum saliache](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md), where men knew no freedom, even to keep family, or choice of name except in secret, and so to their alien masters all of these designations were irrelevant.

- Men were given over to the lifting of stones, and the draining of the fields, and the upkeep of temple and road; or to become art-tortures for strange pleasures, as in the wailing wheels of [Vindasel](Lore_Vindasel.md) and the gut-gardens of [Sercen](Lore_Sercen.md); and [flesh-sculpture](Lore_Flesh_Sculpture.md), which was everywhere among the slaves of the Ayleids in those days; or, worse, the realms of the Fire King [Hadhuul](Lore_Hadhuul.md), where the begetting of [drugs](Lore_Drugs.md) drawn from the admixture of daedrons into living hosts let one inhale new visions of torment, and children were set aflame for nighttime tiger sport.

### MORIHAUS EXPLAINS ALESSIA'S NAMES

- Then Morihaus said to them: "In your tales you have many names for her: Al-Esh, given to her in awe, that when translated sounds like a redundancy, 'the high high', from which come the more familiar corruptions: Aleshut, Esha, Alessia. You knew her as Paravant, given to her when crowned, 'first of its kind', by which the gods meant a mortal worthy of the majesty that is killing-questing-healing, which is also Paraval, Pevesh, Perrethu, Perrif, and, in my case, for it is what I called her when we were lovers: Paravania."

- "Though she is gone to me, she remains bathed in stars, first [Empress](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), Lady of Heaven, Queen-ut-Cyrod."

- And they considered themselves full-answered, and departed.
