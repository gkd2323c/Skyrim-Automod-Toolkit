## Geography
Morrowind is the northeasternmost nation of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), bounded on the north and east by the Sea of Ghosts, on the northwest by Skyrim, on the southwest by [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and on the south by [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Po T-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gt V-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Dialogue-10)</sup> There are numerous crossings on Morrowind's borders with the other provinces, such as Chinzinch Pass,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CON-11)</sup> [Shadowgate Pass](Lore_Shadowgate_Pass.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup> [Kavas Rim Pass](Lore_Kavas_Rim_Pass.md) along Cyrodiil's border,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-2920v5-12)</sup> as well as [Dunmeth Pass](Lore_Dunmeth_Pass.md) and potentially Frimvorn Pass near Skyrim's border.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1S-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ATOTD-14)</sup>

Morrowind is the seat of intense volcanic activity that shapes its landscapes. The [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) separate Morrowind from Skyrim, and the [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md) separate it from Cyrodiil; all of these ranges and borders intersect at the peak of [Trolhetta](Lore_Trolhetta.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONTrolhetta-15)</sup> As a result of this isolation, Morrowind has developed a unique ecosystem. The area south of the Inner Sea is composed of the tormented volcanic coasts of [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) and [Bal Foyen](Lore_Bal_Foyen.md). They lead further south to the fertile [Deshaan Plain](Lore_Deshaan.md) and the marshes that surrounds [Tear](Lore_Tear.md). Most of the modern population of Morrowind is gathered in the high hilly uplands and rich fertile river valleys of central Morrowind, especially around the Inner Sea.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Po T-8)</sup>

Cloud movement shows that the province is predominantly exposed to upper-level southerly winds, resulting in a mild, though often hot, climate. Morrowind is mostly free from snow and ice (excluding Solstheim), unlike other parts of Tamriel at the same latitude. Depending on the Era, the island of Solstheim to the northwest is part of either Morrowind or Skyrim. The [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) created by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), one of the three living gods of Morrowind, exists outside of the reality of Nirn.

Morrowind is composed of several districts:

- The [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) District on the eponymous great island, dominated by the huge Red Mountain and separated from the continent to the west, south and east by the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md). It was formerly the domain of House Dagoth and the Dwemer.
- The Dres District in the south and southeast, amid the plains near the border with Black Marsh.
- The Indoril District in the central heartlands and eastern coast, amid the fertile river valleys.
- The Telvanni District in the extreme northeast, made up of their remaining island settlements.
- The Redoran District in northwest, found in the lands along the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) with Skyrim's borders and the Inner Sea.
- The Sadras District of unknown coverage. It is presumably in the same area as its predecessor, the Hlaalu District, within the central/southwest mainland near Cyrodiil's borders.

### Vvardenfell

- *This article is about the island. For the island's central volcano, also known as Vvardenfell, see [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).*

[Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), also called the **Black Isle**, is a large island located inside the bay-like [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), and is surrounded by mainland Morrowind with the exception of its northern coast, which meets the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). The island is dominated by the great volcano, [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). The island itself is named after the original [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemeris.md) name of Red Mountain, literally translating to "City of the Strong Shield". It is characterized by arid wastes, rocky highlands and coastal wetlands filled with unusual and unique flora and fauna.

In ancient times, the majority of the population in Morrowind (then named Resdayn) was concentrated in Vvardenfell. After the [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md) eruption in [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668) made the island largely inhospitable, there was a permanent population shift to the mainlaind.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3M-1)</sup> Most of the remaining population of Vvardenfell was confined to the relatively hospitable west and southwest coasts.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Dialogue-10)</sup>

Before the eruption of Red Mountain in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), the landscape of Vvardenfell was diverse, with the grasslands of the [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md) and [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md), the dry plains of the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md), the volcanic crags of the [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md), [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md) and Red Mountain, and the coastal areas of [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md), the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md), [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md) and the [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) region.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup>

### Stonefalls
[Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) is a region situated in the western mainland along the southwestern shore of the Inner Sea.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSAI-16)</sup> It borders Bal Foyen to the east, Deshaan to the south, Cyrodiil to the west, [The Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) in Skyrim to the northwest, and the Morrowind part of the Velothi Mountains to the north. The region contains the cities of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md), [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md), and [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md). Other notable places include the necropolis of [Othrenis](Lore_Othrenis.md) and two great volcanoes, [Ash Mountain](Lore_Ash_Mountain.md) and [Tormented Spire](Lore_Tormented_Spire.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ON-17)</sup>

The region's landscape fluctuates from volcanic crags to fertile fields and fungal forests.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ON-17)</sup>

### Bal Foyen
[Bal Foyen](Lore_Bal_Foyen.md) is a small region on the southern coast of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) of Morrowind, east of [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md). It was a wild expanse of marshland and volcanic landscape until the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md). Much of it was turned over to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md)'s former [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) slaves, who planted [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) and herded [guar](Lore_Guar.md) in the former wastes. The Dunmer-built Fort Zeren is found in southern Bal Foyen, previously used as an entry point for the slaves brought from Black Marsh, and the Argonians established the town of [Dhalmora](Lore_Dhalmora.md) in the east.

Cases of [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md) were reported in the region in [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581). During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) invaded Bal Foyen as part of a large-scale invasion of Pact territory. The local defenders succeeded in driving off the invaders and warning the city of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md) to the west due to the arrival of refugees from [Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md), who had been forced to flee their homes after the island was sacked by the Covenant. However, the Pact suffered heavy losses and civilian casualties during a simultaneous attack on both the dockyards and Fort Zeren, crippling the region.

### Deshaan
[Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) (also called the **Deshaan Plain** or **the Deshaan**) is a broad, fertile plain in the southern mainland of Morrowind. Most of the Morrowind population is gathered in the high uplands and fertile [Thir River](Lore_Thir_River.md) valleys of central Morrowind, in and around Deshaan. It borders [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) and the [Fungal Lowlands](Lore_Fungal_Lowlands.md) to the north, [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to the west, as well as the Dejasyte desert, [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), and [Thornmarsh](Lore_Thornmarsh.md) regions of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) to the south. It is composed of three smaller regions: [Redolent Loam](Lore_Redolent_Loam.md) in the west, [Lagomere](Lore_Lagomere.md) in the center, and [Siltreen](Lore_Siltreen.md) in the east.

The region is mainly composed of fertile plains and lush fungal forests which slope down and merge with [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) farther south, with sizable swamps making up the southeastern part of the region.. The local [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) take advantage of the exceptionally fertile black soil, cultivating [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) and cash crops, and breeding [kwama](Lore_Kwama.md) in deep mines for their eggs. Like elsewhere in Morrowind, [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) ruins, [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruins, and [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) tombs dot the landscape. The western area of Redolent Loam is home to the city of [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md), situated on the southern banks of [Lake Hlaalu](Lore_Lake_Hlaalu.md). Deshaan's largest city, [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), sits in the center of Lagomere, surrounded by [Lake Amaya](Lore_Lake_Amaya_(Deshaan).md). The Vale of the Ghost Snake, a sacred site of the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) [Mabrigash](Lore_Mabrigash.md) tribe, lies in the southeastern swamps. The eastern region, Siltreen, contains a large shrine devoted to [Saint Veloth](Lore_Saint_Veloth.md), where his relics are kept. The eastern town of Selfora is home to the [Tree of Summerset](Lore_Tree_of_Summerset.md), a sacred tree said to have been planted by Veloth from seeds taken from his homeland.

### Southern Morrowind
[House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) rules the southern sweep of Morrowind, where the fertile Deshaan Plain merges with the swamps of Black Marsh.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Dialogue-10)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWElone-18)</sup> The southeast is a warm coastal plain with broad cultivated areas and marshes and swamps similar to those found in Black Marsh. Southeast Morrowind is flat, wet, and warm.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Dialogue-10)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWElone-18)</sup> The area around [Tear](Lore_Tear.md) is much hotter than the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) and is "practically Argonia".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MWDrulene-19)</sup> Settlements in this area include [Silnim Dale](Lore_Silnim_Dale.md) and [Rathendis Falls](Lore_Rathendis_Falls.md), with some also theorizing the entrance to [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) was hidden here.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWConcept Map-20)</sup>

### Northwest Morrowind
The west region is found east of the Velothi Mountains bordering Skyrim and has settlements such as [Cormaris View](Lore_Cormaris_View.md), [Silgrad Tower](Lore_Silgrad_Tower.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OM-21)</sup> [Selethis](Lore_Selethis.md), as well as a region called the Great Valley.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWConcept Map-20)</sup> The city of [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md), a Redoran stronghold, is located at its northern extremity, near the Dunmeth Pass. The [Mountain Orcs](Lore_Mountain_Orcs.md) are found here as well. Slaves with [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) features were commonly kept in this part of the province, even by the time of the Third Empire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup>

[House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) guards the western flank of Morrowind.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup> The Nords of Skyrim are still resentful of the land lost in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416), and enviously covet the House Redoran-ruled coast and highlands of Morrowind's northwest borders, at times assaulting them.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OBRumors-22)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Nord Invade-23)</sup> The Nords have never been comfortable with the fact that the Dark Elves hold the west coast of the Inner Sea all the way up to Blacklight; and in some cases their maps elide that fact entirely.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LSP-24)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Dialogue-10)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWElone-18)</sup>

### Fungal Lowlands
The **Fungal Lowlands** are a coastal region in east-central Morrowind, found within the [Indoril District](Lore_House_Indoril.md). It is a spacious tract of wilderness and fertile river valleys spanning along the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), with a number of secluded clearings between the roads and the coast. The [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md) eruption of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) laid waste at least half of Morrowind and led to a permanent shift of population south towards the broad southern plains, where the flat land is now often for camping along the river. It borders the regions of [Bal Foyen](Lore_Bal_Foyen.md) to the west, the [Padomaic Crest](Lore_Padomaic_Crest.md) to the east, and the region of [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) to the south. Together with the Padomaic Crest, it was considered one of the two regions making up the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md). The settlements of Ald Isra and Camonnaruhn were once found here, while the town of [Sailenmora](Lore_Sailen_Vulgate.md) emerged between the lowlands and the Crest.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-place-Padomaic_Crest.jpg/200px-ON-place-Padomaic_Crest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Padomaic_Crest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Padomaic_Crest.jpg) Padomaic Crest
### Padomaic Crest
The [Padomaic Crest](Lore_Padomaic_Crest.md) (or **Central Highlands**) is a coastal region in east-central Morrowind, presently within the [Indoril District](Lore_House_Indoril.md). It borders the region of [Fungal Lowlands](Lore_Fungal_Lowlands.md) to the west, and the region of [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) to the south. Together with the Fungal Lowlands, it was considered one of the two regions making up the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md). The region is home to the city of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md), as well as the isle of [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md) off the coast. The [Dres](Lore_Dres.md) town of [Alavelis](Lore_Alavelis.md) is located along the south near a volcano, and is home to a [glass](Lore_Malachite.md) mine.

The high uplands and treacherous cliff coastline gives way to craggy rocks along the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md). [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) described seeing large "[bat lizards](Lore_Bat_Lizard.md)" in the area, which are believed to be the ancestors of modern [cliff racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md). Tiny tribes of [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) and [trolls](Lore_Troll.md) also patrol the highlands.

### Northeast Morrowind
The northeast region of Morrowind includes all the land north of Necrom. The region is characterized with rocky coasts and islands, as well as a number of settlements.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWElone-18)</sup> Its main city is [Firewatch](Lore_Firewatch.md) at its northern end. [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) controls the coastline,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-GHOM-25)</sup> as well as settlements such as [Draloris](Lore_Draloris.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWConcept Map-20)</sup>

The northern lands are very dry compared to the temperate southern lands,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-26)</sup> and the landscape consists of fertile plains in the east and badlands in the west,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWConcept Map-20)</sup> the north in particular having arid desert plateaus, mesas, and high steppes.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TPMorrowind Moonlight-27)</sup> Inhabitants of the mainland wear "Dust Adept" clothing to adapt to this dry environment.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TDA-28)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CA-29)</sup>

The island of [Vounoura](Lore_Vounoura.md) is found near the area. The Telvanni capital city of [Port Telvannis](Lore_Port_Telvannis.md) is found in an [archipelago](Lore_Northeast_Archipelago.md) of the Sea of Ghosts in the extreme northeast.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LAHT-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWConcept Map-20)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> The archipelago of the northeast has a mix of flat and rocky rugged terrain with a few settlements.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue-31)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Morrowind Map-32)</sup>

### Clockwork City
The eponymous city of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), better known as the **Clockwork City**, and also known as the **Brass-Throat**, and the **Oil-Slick Tower of Seamless Assembly**, is a metaphysical clockwork realm that is the greatest creation of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), one of the living gods of Morrowind, to replicate the mythic structures of Nirn in metallic miniature. It supposedly exists "outside space and time", and in the physical world, it is represented in the form of a Clockwork Globe that is no larger than a good-sized [netch](Lore_Netch.md), and requires an individual to be magically shrunk to enter. Although represented in miniature, the realm is expansive on the inside and encompasses an entire world. It is said that, in many respects, the Clockwork City is Sotha Sil himself, thus aspects of the realm, such as how food tastes, can be decided by his will, with there being no guarantee that even magic could change what Sil has decided if he doesn't allow it.

The Clockwork City has been described by [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) as an "incomprehensibly powerful world-shaping device". [Mephala's](Lore_Mephala.md) servant, the former [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) Earl Leythen, claimed it was a "place of power", similar to the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), that can "change the rules of reality and remake the world". It is said that, as the Clockwork City functions as a simulacrum of the [corporeal plane](Lore_Nirn.md), making a change to the Clockwork City could also change some aspect of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) itself, as a consequence.

### Solstheim
[Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) (meaning Sun's Home) is a large island in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) to the north of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) were the first inhabitants of the region, and built many grand settlements that far outlasted their mainland holdings. It officially became a region of Morrowind in [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16). The island has a long history of habitation by other races, but it has traditionally been primarily [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) in influence.

## History
"Dwemereth" had been inhabited by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) since time immemorial, but the history of Morrowind under their rule is a mystery. When the exiled [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) led by the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) left [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and came to what they considered their promised land,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASHOM-33)</sup> they found the Dwemer were already well-established in their underground colonies. The two races would not live in peace, as disputes over land and religion regularly led to conflict.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3M-1)</sup> They would, however, unite against a common enemy following almost two hundred years of oppression by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Skyrim, who invaded the area circa [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1S-13)</sup> Due to the personal friendship between [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) of the Chimer and the Dwarf-King [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md) of the Dwemer, an alliance was formed between them, and the Nords were pushed out by [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416). This alliance born out of need formed the [First Council](Lore_First_Council.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup>

### Resdayn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/LO-people-Veloth.jpg/200px-LO-people-Veloth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) A fresco depicting Veloth leading the Chimeri exodus Nerevar and Dumac ruled jointly over Resdayn. The peace between the Dwemer and Chimer brought prosperity, but the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) broke out when relations went sour. It ended with the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), which saw the death of both leaders. [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), and [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) tapped the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) to attain god-like powers (though Dagoth Ur would disappear until [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882)).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TBORM-34)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DUP-35)</sup> As a result, the Chimer changed into the Dunmer.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TBORM-34)</sup> Red Mountain erupted (during the battle, apparently),<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TAH-36)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-FSOKW-37)</sup> laying waste to half of Morrowind and beyond.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup> [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668) is known as "Sun's Death" and the "Year of Winter in Summer".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1S-13)</sup> It's believed that this eruption created the Inner Sea and destroyed entire cities,<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-VOF-38)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ro KZ-39)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TNM-40)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-36L36-41)</sup> and led to a population shift to the south. It was sometime after this eruption that the land became known as Morrowind.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3M-1)</sup> Some sources seem to suggest this name wasn't popularized until the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASHOM-33)</sup>

### Tribunal Morrowind
Morrowind under the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) marks the beginning of [Dunmeri history](Lore_Dunmer.md#History). The Tribunal consolidated their power and succeeded in incorporating themselves into the Chimeri belief system of ancestor and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship, at the expense of the worship of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md), whom the Dunmer know as the "Bad Daedra".<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-THOT-42)</sup> Massive shrines to those four [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) still dot Morrowind, though at the close of the Third Era, they were abandoned ruins typically inhabited by monsters and criminals.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup> The Tribunal replaced the First Council with the Grand Council of Morrowind,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OM-21)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-43)</sup> and performed many amazing deeds as the Dunmer's immortal protectors and leaders. Cyrodiil sought to incorporate Morrowind into the [Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md), perpetrating the bloody [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), which ultimately ended in a treaty at the close of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3M-1)</sup> In [2E 373](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_373), the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) reported the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) Ahbiilok to be lairing somewhere in Morrowind.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-AOD-44)</sup>

In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), the Akaviri army of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md) invaded northern Tamriel. They were defeated at Stonefalls by the combined forces of the Nords (along with [Wulfharth Ash-King](Lore_Wulfharth.md)), Dunmer legions of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) Shellbacks.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSAI-16)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Jt SK-45)</sup> This event led to the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), an uneasy alliance between powers from all three races. The Pact later participated in the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-AWON-46)</sup>

Much to the surprise of almost everyone, no such conflict occurred at the rise of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), and [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s [ambitions of conquest](Lore_Tiber_Wars.md) were fulfilled through diplomacy. Vivec and Tiber Septim signed the [Treaty of the Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) in the early years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Armistice)</sup> The Dunmer became Imperial citizens, accepting Imperial garrisoning forces and the incursion of Cyrodilic institutions and business interests. However, they retained self-rule, house traditions, and religious practices. Vivec secretly supplied the Emperor's agents with the golem [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) as part of this deal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3M-1)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OM-21)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-MWArtisa-47)</sup>

The Dunmer, and the Chimer before them, divided Morrowind into political districts based on their [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md). An unknown number of Great Houses rose and fell over the years, but in the late Third Era, [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) controlled the western mainland bordering Cyrodiil and Skyrim. [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) controlled the central western part of the mainland. [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) controlled the central eastern part of the mainland. [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) controlled the area forming the border with Black Marsh. [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) controlled the eastern region of the nation.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-GHOM-25)</sup> For ages, Vvardenfell was a [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) preserve predominantly inhabited by a few Great House settlements and [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md), but it was opened to greater settlement in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASHOM-33)</sup> House Telvanni snatched up the east, House Hlaalu took some of the center and the marshy southwest, and House Redoran held dominion over the remaining areas, though it largely refused to expand settlement.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASHOM-33)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-GHOM-25)</sup>

In the early-mid [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Prince [Uriel Mantiarco](Lore_Uriel_Mantiarco.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) garnered support from many disgruntled kings of Morrowind,<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-48)</sup> including [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md)<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TMOP-49)</sup> and other northern provinces, and coordinated three attacks across the Empire. The second attack was in eastern Morrowind and targeted Imperial garrisons across the coastal islands. [Kintyra II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md)'s consort, Kontin Arynx died while defending these forts. While the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Army.md) was split between eastern Morrowind and western [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), Prince Uriel used that time to invade the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and proclaim himself Emperor Uriel Septim III.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-48)</sup>

The [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) henceforth was underway. Emperor Uriel III and his mother [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) had support across all of Skyrim and northern Morrowind, while High Rock was contentious when the Emperor's uncle, King [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) of [Gilane](Lore_Gilane.md) re-conquered western High Rock.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-50)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TWQ7-51)</sup> King [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) of [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md) led his Argonian army in the battle for Morrowind. In the summer months, Magnus' warriors fought well but had to retreat during the winter. The second century sage Inzolicus believed that this setback made the war last two more years than needed.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TWQ7-51)</sup> Meanwhile, the Empire's historical ally [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), led by King [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md), won many battles.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TMOP-49)</sup>

The war ended when Uriel III was defeated by Cephorus and killed in a riot in [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127). Potema died during the [Siege of Solitude](Lore_Siege_of_Solitude.md) in [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137). When Cephorus became Emperor of Tamriel as Cephorus Septim I, the kings across Tamriel offered their complete support in exchange for new levels of autonomy and independence.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TWQ8-52)</sup> When the Emperor passed away only three years after the Siege of Solitude ended, his brother Magnus replaced him and spent the rest of his reign mending political damage from the war, either by forming new relations or punishing traitorous kings.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-50)</sup> To form a clean slate with Ebonheart, Emperor Magnus I arranged the marriage of his son, King [Pelagius](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) of Solitude, to [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md), the heiress of the [Ra'athim](Lore_Ra%27athim.md) clan.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TMOP-49)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/MW-place-Sadrith_Mora_Slave_Market.jpg/200px-MW-place-Sadrith_Mora_Slave_Market.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Sadrith_Mora_Slave_Market.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Sadrith_Mora_Slave_Market.jpg) Telvanni slave pens (Morrowind) Morrowind under the Tribunal was distinct from most of Tamriel in many ways. For one, their legalization of assassination set them apart.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup> Also, the Dunmer banned [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) in their land long before the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) saw fit to do so.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-CP-53)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-TBAOT-54)</sup> However, necromancers could continue to operate secretly thanks to another peculiar Morrowind institution: [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-CP-53)</sup> For ages, the Dunmer had enslaved humans and elves, but mainly concentrated on [Beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md), namely [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), Argonians, and [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md). The right to retain this tradition was guaranteed by the Armistice, and it continued for virtually all of the Third Era, mainly due to the lobbying of Houses Dres and Telvanni, and despite the general abhorrence of the institution by the rest of Tamriel.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1M-4)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TEP-55)</sup> In the final years of the era, the new King [Helseth](Lore_Helseth.md), looking for a way to exert his authority, banned the controversial practice, eliciting an initially bloody reaction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3M-1)</sup> As the Dunmer would find out, though, their transgressions were not forgotten.

Dagoth Ur awoke beneath Red Mountain in 2E 882, heralding enormous changes in Morrowind. The Tribunal led campaigns against him and his monsters for over four hundred years.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DUP-35)</sup> Around [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), the [Great Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md) was erected to contain the threat,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-TRMehra-56)</sup> though some sources suggest the Ghostfence existed for centuries before this.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASHOM-33)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-MWGDG-57)</sup> The enormous shimmering-blue barrier encircled the entire Red Mountain region of Vvardenfell, but still, Dagoth Ur's monsters and [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) managed to seep through. With the advent of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Almalexia, Sotha Sil, and Dagoth Ur were soon dead, and Vivec went missing. The Dunmer were still struggling to come to terms with this at the close of the Third Era.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribunal-58)</sup>

### The Fourth Era
After the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the Nords and [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) launched attacks.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TIC-59)</sup> In Vivec's absence, the [Ministry of Truth](Lore_Ministry_of_Truth.md) eventually fell, destroying Vivec City and causing Red Mountain to erupt, covering the rest of Vvardenfell in ash and lava. Since this [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), Red Mountain has been spewing ash even more regularly than before, and Vvardenfell remains largely uninhabitable. When Solstheim came under Dunmer rule, the Redoran took over its administration, though the Telvanni also established the settlement of [Tel Mithryn](Lore_Tel_Mithryn.md).<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-60)</sup>

The Argonians of Black Marsh, stronger than ever, soon invaded Morrowind, seizing large tracts of land that had long ago belonged to them.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TIC-59)</sup> They also sacked many cities, including Mournhold, in southern Morrowind. Though a Redoran army halted their advance, countless Dunmer were killed or displaced,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-DBAdril-61)</sup> and they were forced to establish dozens of colonies elsewhere, including the island of Solstheim.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-HRR-62)</sup> Apart from organized colonies, many Dunmer refugees have settled in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), especially in the so-called Gray Quarter of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). Though Mournhold was rebuilt,<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-TMFN-63)</sup> the capital of Morrowind was moved to the [Rootspire](Lore_Rootspire.md) in the Redoran city of Blacklight, as the leadership House Redoran had exhibited had greatly increased their power and influence.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-DBCindiri-64)</sup>

The Empire's ineffectual protection and seeming indifference to Morrowind's plight made them reviled by the Dunmer. After the Empire "released its grasp" on Morrowind,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-DBAdril-61)</sup> House Hlaalu's Imperial ties resulted in it being replaced by [House Sadras](Lore_House_Sadras.md) on the Council of Great Houses.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-HORR3-65)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-DBLleril-66)</sup>

## Flora
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/SR-flora-Emperor_Parasol_Moss.jpg/200px-SR-flora-Emperor_Parasol_Moss.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-flora-Emperor_Parasol_Moss.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-flora-Emperor_Parasol_Moss.jpg) Emperor Parasols (Dragonborn) [Tibrol](Lore_Tibrol.md) is a species of tree that grows in Morrowind, known for its longevity and for serving as nesting spots for birds.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-36L1-67)</sup> The largest plantlife in Morrowind is the mighty emperor parasol mushroom, a fungus the size of a tree. They dot the landscape of Morrowind, particularly in Telvanni regions. Other types of fungi include [bloatspore](Lore_Bloatspore.md), [bungler's bane](Lore_Bungler%27s_Bane.md), [hypha facia](Lore_Hypha_Facia.md), [luminous russula](Lore_Luminous_Russula.md), and [violet coprinus](Lore_Violet_Coprinus.md). Much of the flora of Morrowind must be adapted to its harsh environment, like the [black lichen](Lore_Black_Lichen.md), fire fern, and [Chokeweed](Lore_Chokeweed.md), which are found in many rocky, infertile areas. [Kreshweed](Lore_Kreshweed.md) and [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) appear to thrive in salt water, and can be found along Morrowind's extensive coastline. Some plants haven't adapted and thus are rare, like [red](Lore_Red_Lichen.md) and [green lichen](Lore_Green_Lichen.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribunal-58)</sup> Other tree species found in the province include ash laurels, cedars, and poplars.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Home Goods-68)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/SR-flora-Scathecraw.jpg/200px-SR-flora-Scathecraw.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-flora-Scathecraw.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-flora-Scathecraw.jpg) Scathecraw (Dragonborn) The Dunmer have managed to make do with what they've been given. They have domesticated many plants, such as [scrib cabbage plants](Lore_Scrib_Cabbage.md), which are commonly grown within indoor planters. [Wickwheat](Lore_Wickwheat.md) is an edible grain that grows in the wild. [Saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) is both an important food crop and the main ingredient in the popular alcoholic beverage [mazte](Lore_Mazte.md). [Muck](Lore_Muck.md) is prepared from the common [muckspunge](Lore_Muckspunge.md) plant. The roots of [trama](Lore_Trama.md) shrubs are used to make tea. The berries of the [comberry plant](Lore_Comberry_Plant.md) are edible, though they are known for their bitterness. Something tastier, like the [hackle-lo](Lore_Hackle-Lo.md) leaf or [marshmerrow](Lore_Marshmerrow.md) pulp, are more preferred. [Corkbulb](Lore_Corkbulb.md) is grown because its tough roots are used for construction in lieu of large amounts of wood. Dunmer have long made [ash yams](Lore_Ash_Yam.md) a staple of their diet, as the hardy vegetable can thrive when planted in ash (hence its name).

Some of their vegetation is sought for the alchemical ingredients they offer, such as [draggle tail](Lore_Draggle_Tail.md), the [moss of an emperor parasol](Lore_Emperor_Parasol_Moss.md), [meadow rye](Lore_Meadow_Rye.md), [nirthfly](Lore_Nirthfly.md), [roobrush](Lore_Roobrush.md), [scathecraw](Lore_Scathecraw.md), [slough ferns](Lore_Slough_Fern.md), [spike grass](Lore_Spiky_Grass.md), [spiny lloramor](Lore_Spiny_Lloramor.md), and [sweetbarrel](Lore_Sweetbarrel.md). Flowering [bittergreen](Lore_Bittergreen.md) vines can be found in wet and dry regions alike. Various other flowers include the [black anther](Lore_Black_Anther_Plant.md), [gold kanet](Lore_Gold_Kanet.md), [golden sedge](Lore_Golden_Sedge.md), [heather](Lore_Heather.md), [horn lily](Lore_Horn_Lily.md), [noble sedge](Lore_Noble_Sedge.md), [stoneflower](Lore_Stoneflower.md), [timsa-come-by](Lore_Timsa-Come-By.md), and the [willow flower](Lore_Willow_Flower.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribunal-58)</sup>

## Fauna
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/SR-quest-Fetch_the_Netch.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Fetch_the_Netch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Fetch_the_Netch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Fetch_the_Netch.jpg) Netches (Dragonborn) Much of the fauna of Morrowind is uniquely adapted to, and dependent on, the ashfall from Red Mountain.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> Due to their domestication, [Silt Striders](Lore_Silt_Strider.md), [netches](Lore_Netch.md), and [guar](Lore_Guar.md) are some of the most iconic beasts of Morrowind. But not to be forgotten are the [kwama](Lore_Kwama.md), enormous cave-dwelling insects in Vvardenfell whose eggs the Dunmer have made an industry of "mining" for food. The [sand kwoom](Lore_Sand_Kwoom.md) and long-legged [velks](Lore_Velk.md) are species not often seen outside of the province due to their reliance on kresh-weed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> [Slaughterfish](Lore_Slaughterfish.md) and [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) fill the waters of Morrowind, and [mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md) line the shores. Bothersome [cliff racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md) dominate the sky in numbers so great they are said to have driven off the dragons (though the racers themselves were purportedly driven from Vvardenfell by [Saint Jiub](Lore_Jiub.md)). Some claim instead that the strange floating gas bags known as [skylamps](Lore_Skylamp.md) ate the Dragons.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup>

The large, black [shalk](Lore_Shalk.md) bugs in the wilderness can attack with magical fire if threatened. Various Daedra, such as the [Winged Twilight](Lore_Winged_Twilight.md), roam the landscape along with various ash creatures and more natural beasts like the [alit](Lore_Alit.md), [kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md), and packs of [nix-hounds](Lore_Nix-Hound.md). [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) have been known to inhabit the depths of Morrowind, where they kept [durzogs](Lore_Durzog.md), ferocious dog-like reptiles, as patrols and war animals. Ash-Fowl are a type of creature eaten in the province.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-69)</sup> And, like virtually every other place in Tamriel, [rats](Lore_Rat.md) are common.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribunal-58)</sup>

The island of Vvardenfell used to hold more unique animals such as the [cliff strider](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md), the [nix-ox](Lore_Nix-Ox.md), and [vvardvark](Lore_Vvardvark.md), but by the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Ash Blight had wiped them out on the island (though all three species were also known to live on mainland Morrowind). [Verminous](Lore_Verminous.md) were bipedal therapods once common across Velothi lands that went extinct long ago.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-LACW-70)</sup> Another exotic thing even among the alien wildlife in Morrowind is the bizarre, bioluminescent, and possibly sentient [land-coral](Lore_Land-Coral.md) species that grows on the land around the Inner Sea of mainland Morrowind and Vvardenfell.

## Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/51/MW-place-Tel_Naga.jpg/200px-MW-place-Tel_Naga.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Tel_Naga.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Tel_Naga.jpg) Tel Naga, a typical Telvanni tower (Morrowind) Several architectural styles can be seen in Morrowind:

- **Dunmeri styles:** - **Redoran style**, with organic buildings shaped like (and sometimes made from) crustaceans and mollusks. This style was present in Vvardenfell in the Second<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ESOMW-71)</sup> and Third Eras.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup> The most impressive Redoran structure was [Under-Skar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Under-Skar), the mostly subterranean but palatial manor district of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md), built inside, below, and around the giant shell of an [Emperor Crab](Lore_Emperor_Crab.md).
- **Hlaalu style**, with simple, stuccoed stone, and a multi-level design often involving stairways and ramps. The style—squarish with rounded edges, and often buttressed—was present in Vvardenfell in the Second<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ESOMW-71)</sup> and Third Eras.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup>
- **Telvanni style**, with bizarre, twisted mushroom towers and dwellings, which are magically grown rather than built. This style was present in Vvardenfell in the Second<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ONFiri-72)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ESOMW-71)</sup> and Third Eras,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup> as well as in eastern Solstheim in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-60)</sup>
- **Velothi style**, which is seen in ancestral tombs, ancient towers, and Temple buildings.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ON-17)</sup> Of the later styles, it most clearly influenced Hlaalu.
- **Responsible Architecture**, a style that is attributed to the Daedric Prince [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-VOF-38)</sup> This style was similar to that seen in various Daedric ruins in Morrowind, and it was used by the famed architect Guylaine Marilie to build a prison to contain Daedra at the site where Fort Arand was later constructed.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-FALT-73)</sup>
- An unnamed style, featuring ornate stone buildings with peaked, shingled roofs, was common in mainland Morrowind in the Second Era and even extended into Cyrodiil. The city of Mournhold retained the style in the Third Era.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ON-17)</sup>

- **Other styles:** - The Imperial settlements founded in Vvardenfell after [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414) were built in the traditional Imperial style.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup>
- Daedric and Dwemer ruins dated to the early First Era are a common sight in Morrowind, both in Vvardenfell and on the mainland.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ON-17)</sup> The heavy curved vaults of the Daedric shrines are considered comfortable by the Dunmer due to their sturdiness, but outsiders regard them as brutal and oppressive.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-LOTSS-74)</sup>
- [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) architecture in the form of bulbous, gourd-like mud structures flourished for a time in mainland Morrowind after the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) and the freeing of Argonian slaves in the Second Era.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ON-17)</sup>
