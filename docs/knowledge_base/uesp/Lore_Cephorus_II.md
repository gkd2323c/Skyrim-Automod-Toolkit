Cephorus Septim II was the sixteenth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for twenty-one years, from [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247) to [3E 268](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_268). Conflict and rebellion characterized Cephorus II's reign, with the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md)'s [war](Lore_Camoran_Usurper%27s_Invasion.md) in western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) being the most significant.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOTU-2)</sup> The Usurper's eventual defeat had little to do with Cephorus II's efforts, which brought increased hostility toward the inefficacious Empire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup> As a result of this increased hostility and Cephorus II's weak and ineffectual leadership, rebellions and civil wars beset the Empire for over half a century after his reign.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-3)</sup> He was preceded by [Uriel IV](Lore_Uriel_IV.md), and was succeeded by his son, [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_V.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup>

## Biography
Little is known of Cephorus Septim's life prior to becoming emperor. At some point, he had a son, [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_V.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup>

In [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247), Cephorus' cousin Emperor [Uriel IV](Lore_Uriel_IV.md) died, but the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) voted to disinherit Uriel IV's son [Andorak](Lore_Andorak_Septim.md) and proclaimed Cephorus emperor because he was more closely related to the original Septim line.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TET-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DC-5)</sup> Following Cephorus' proclamation as emperor, Andorak's loyalists initiated a rebellion against the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup>

Cephorus II was the first emperor of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) who was neither a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nor had spent any of his childhood in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Moreover, he was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md), whose sympathies lay with [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The difference between Cephorus II and his cousin and predecessor, Uriel IV, a Breton and former King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), appalled the people of High Rock. Consequently, many of the people in High Rock detested Cephorus II.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOTU-2)</sup>

In [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249), while Cephorus II was attempting to suppress Andorak's rebellion, [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md), known as the Camoran Usurper and a member of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md), initiated a war in western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TET-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DC-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3EV-6)</sup> The Usurper, with his army of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead), and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) mercenaries, quickly conquered [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOTU-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3EV-6)</sup> The Usurper and his army then marched north and invaded [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where they sacked the city of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TR-7)</sup> In [3E 253](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_253), the Usurper and his army defeated the armies of [Taneth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Taneth) and [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) at the [Battle of Dragontooth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Dragontooth) and took control of southern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DC-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3EH-8)</sup>

Cephorus II dispatched numerous mercenaries into Hammerfell in an attempt to halt the Usurper's northward march, but they were either bribed or killed and reanimated as undead.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup> The [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) was also dispatched to fight the Usurper, but this enabled [Torradan ap Dugal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torradan_ap_Dugal) and the [Red Sabre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Sabre) pirates to raid merchant and supply ships throughout the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CDJ-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OB-DL-10)</sup> In an act that the Sage [Eraintine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eraintine) described as "[Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s heart beating no more", the Elder Council granted Andorak the Kingdom of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) in [3E 256](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_256), ending his rebellion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup>

News of the Usurper's conquests reached High Rock in early [3E 266](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_266), but preparations to repel the invasion did not begin until [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267). The delay in preparations occurred due to the weak monarchs of the major powers in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), who could not unite the region against the Camoran Usurper's armies. Additionally, eight local leaders formed secret allegiances with the Usurper to safeguard their lands. Finally, many of the common men in High Rock detested Cephorus II, which led them to be sympathetic toward the Usurper, whom they viewed as the Emperor's archfoe.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOTU-2)</sup> With no clear leadership from the major regional powers or the Empire, [Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen), [Kambria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kambria), [Phrygias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phrygias), and [Ykalon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ykalon) united under the Baron of Dwynnen, [Othrok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Othrok), to defeat the Usurper at the [Battle of Firewaves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Firewaves) in 3E 267.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOTU-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TET-4)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3EHR-11)</sup>

The Usurper's demise had little to do with Cephorus II's efforts, and instead, his lack of assistance brought increased hostility toward the inefficacious Empire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup> As a result of this increased hostility and Cephorus II's weak and ineffectual leadership, rebellions and civil wars beset the Empire for decades after his reign.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-3)</sup> A few months after the defeat of the Usurper in 3E 267, Cephorus II provided Commodore [Fasil Umbranox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fasil_Umbranox) with whatever resources were necessary for a campaign against Torradan ap Dugal and the Red Sabre pirates, despite the [Imperial Treasury](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Treasury) being depleted from the war in High Rock.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OB-DL-10)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CDJ3-12)</sup>

In [3E 268](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_268), Cephorus II's son Uriel V succeeded him as emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TET-4)</sup>

## Legacy
Uriel V's reign helped improve the people's opinion of the Empire, but the rebellions and civil wars from Cephorus II's reign went unchallenged for decades, until the reign of his granddaughter, [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-3)</sup> During her reign, Morihatha slowly brought the rebellious kingdoms, duchies, and baronies back under imperial control. Cephorus II's great-grandson [Pelagius IV](Lore_Pelagius_IV.md) continued Morihatha's efforts, and by the end of his reign, Tamriel was closer to unity than it had been since the reign of [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_I.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-3)</sup>
