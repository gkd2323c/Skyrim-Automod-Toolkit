## Wasp
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/ON-creature-Giant_Wasp.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Giant_Wasp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Giant_Wasp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Giant_Wasp.jpg) A giant wasp Wasps are stinging insects found throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They are known to vary in size greatly, ordinary wasps are small and only a minor nuisance. Giant wasps are known to grow to the size of [ponies](Lore_Horse.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EGTBM-9)</sup> and they are very protective of their nests. They sting and bite to defend themselves. Wasps are known to have queens,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> however giant wasps can also reproduce rapidly by injecting eggs into the bodies of other creatures, which quickly emerge as young wasps.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

The outer shell of the giant wasp is very durable.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The second row of wings on a wasp's back are gossamer-thin and refract light in a pleasing manner, making them desirable trophies.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> Both ordinary and giant wasp wings are sometimes used by [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to create betrothal veils.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Paper wasps are known to infest the docks of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DPW-15)</sup>

- See also: [Bee](Lore_Bee.md), [Hornet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hornet), and [Parrapton](Lore_Parrapton.md)

## [Watcher](Lore_Watcher.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/ON-creature-Cres.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Cres.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Cres.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Cres.jpg) A watchling [Watchers](Lore_Watcher.md) (also called *Doom-Truths*) are flying tentacled [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) with a large eye in the center of their body. Watchers are quite nimble, and can shoot beams of energy from their large eye. They may be the most intelligent of the Daedra, and are known to have hierarchical ranks. They are knowledge gatherers and minions found in the service of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), modeled after the Prince's appearance. He often employs them as overseers and guardians, with them tending to

his important concerns. However, they are known to serve other [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), such as [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), and [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). Locking eyes with a Watcher is dangerous; a mortal's soul can be eaten away if one stares too long into their large central eye. Some Watchers have a necrotic gaze, causing the skin on a mortal's face to tear away if they stare too long. They are able separate the lies from the truth, which comes in handy to Mephala, adding to her powers of deception.

The term **Watchling** refers to watchers that are far smaller in size than the average watcher. Some of them consider the term an insulting diminutive. They apparently have a name for themselves, but it is not as commonly known. Another term used to describle small watchers is **Pocket Watcher**. These are occasionally kept as pets or assistants by some [warlocks](Lore_Warlocks.md).

Watchlings or Pocket Watchers are no bigger than a human's fist and are not the offspring of Watchers. They are described as runts of their kind and curiosities as opposed to terrors. It is unknown whether they keep their form forever or eventually transform into the larger versions. Hermaeus Mora frequently employs Watchlings as messengers and recorders, tasking them with obscure clerical duties in the hidden bureaucracy that quietly administers [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Watcher), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Web Mother](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Web_Mother)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/BL-creature-The_Web_Mother_%28The_Web_Mother%27s_Trap%29.jpg/200px-BL-creature-The_Web_Mother_%28The_Web_Mother%27s_Trap%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-creature-The_Web_Mother_(The_Web_Mother%27s_Trap).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-creature-The_Web_Mother_(The_Web_Mother%27s_Trap).jpg) A web mother [Web Mothers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Web_Mother) are a monstrous type of queen [spider](Lore_Spider.md) that can be found rarely in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They are distinguished by their red and black coloration and the numerous spider eggs attached to their abdomen. These queen spiders are usually found in the depths of the nest, where they can reproduce and protect their eggs.

Web Mothers have been known to defend themselves by launching their own young at foes. These resemble a variety of [exploding poison spider](Lore_Spider_Crafting.md#Poison_Spiders).

A town in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) once kept giant spiders as livestock to harvest their silk. The farmers used specific scents to trick the nest's Web Mother into thinking they were friendly. Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), this trick stopped working, and the town was soon overrun with spiders. [The Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) arrived and killed the spiders, destroying the eggs that were laid in the town. The Hero then slew the Web Mother, who had grown too large to leave the nest.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Web_Mother)<sup>([CC](Skyrim_The_Contest.md))</sup>, [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Web_Mothers)

## [Welwa](Lore_Welwa.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-creature-Welwa_03.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Welwa_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Welwa_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Welwa_03.jpg) A welwa [Welwa](Lore_Welwa.md) are great four-eyed pack hunting beasts, with thick pelts, tails, and savage teeth. They often serve as war mounts.

According to tapestries in the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), the early [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) settlers of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) encountered welwa, depicted as strange, holy ravagers, and saviors of the land. Led by [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md), the Aldmer of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) drove them back using ancestral magics, and eventually the welwa of Summerset were driven to extinction. In [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) they were considered myths, and described in their ancient texts as having "the jaws of a bear, and the charging strength of a boar".

Welwa continued to inhabit the wilds of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) on mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), as well as parts of the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) that border the region. They are often domesticated and armored by the [Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md) of the region, who use them as war beasts. History indicates that every pastoral society in Craglorn's long history: Nedes, Yokudans, Goblins, and Orcs has domesticated them at some point. They also have been found in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md)

In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) of the Crystal Tower reintroduced welwa to [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) by bringing in mated pairs from Craglorn. These welwa quickly spread across the wilderness of Summerset, although many inhabitants of the Isle were displeased with their reintroduction.

Welwa could be transformed into reptilian forms through [Naga](Lore_Naga.md) [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) rituals or exposure to special [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) sap.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Welwa)

## [Werewolf](Lore_Lycanthropy.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/SR-creature-Werewolf_03.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Werewolf_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Werewolf_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Werewolf_03.jpg) A werewolf Werewolves are the most common of [lycanthropes](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) found in Tamriel. They are monstrous, [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) -like creatures taller and broader than man and mer, and are capable of moving on two limbs or sprinting on all four. They can attack with both their claws and teeth, inflicting lycanthropy with their bite. Those with the beast blood are prone to violence, whether accidental or intentional, as they live with a constant bloodlust which drives them to kill and devour their enemies. Werewolves are widely feared and hated in Tamriel, and will be attacked on sight if spotted in beast form. While [Sanies Lupinus](Lore_Sanies_Lupinus.md) causes all werewolves to transform under the influences of the moons, the members of [the Circle](Lore_Companions.md) can control their beast forms. In return they must serve [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) in the afterlife. Silver weaponry is very effective against werewolves.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Werewolf), [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Werewolf), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Werewolf.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Werewolf_(NPC)), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Werewolf), [Skyrim VSE](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Enemies#Werewolf), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call_to_Arms:Models)

Wraiths are often found guarding objects or patrolling certain areas. They are known to cast spells, see invisible foes, and attack those that trespass into their domain. In addition to a normal haunting, wraiths can also be attached to cursed objects, such as a mirror. Some wraiths are described as being the spirits of long dead mages, trapped into this state of existence either by the circumstances of their death or by being called by a powerful wizard. ## [Wrath of Sithis](Lore_Wrath_of_Sithis.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/LG-cardart-Wrath_of_Sithis.png/200px-LG-cardart-Wrath_of_Sithis.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Wrath_of_Sithis.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Wrath_of_Sithis.png) The Wrath of Sithis The [Wrath of Sithis](Lore_Wrath_of_Sithis.md) refers to the mysterious punishment threatened by [*The Five Tenets*](Lore_The_Five_Tenets.md) of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). It is a [spirit](Lore_Spirit.md) of vengeance that acts as a conduit for [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md)' wrath, targeting Dark Brotherhood members that break one of the Five Tenets. It often takes the appearance of a [gloom wraith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gloom_Wraith), or a [shadowy](Lore_Shadow.md) [ghost](Lore_Ghost.md).

The Wrath of Sithis is said to be a worse punishment than expulsion for traitors of the Brotherhood. The spirit has been known to take the very [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of its targets. Those who have been exiled from the Dark Brotherhood will be allowed to rejoin if they survive a visit from the Wrath of Sithis. This can only occur twice; individuals become forever exiled if they break one of the Tenets a third time, even if they manage to slay the spirit.

In addition to the curses and magical attacks typical of other gloom wraiths, the Wrath of Sithis typically wields an enchanted [silver](Lore_Silver.md) dagger known as the Dagger of Discipline. These daggers can be claimed by those who defeat the spirit and survive its visitations.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Wrath_of_Sithis), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Wrath_of_Sithis_(creature)), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Wrath_of_Sithis)

## Wrathman
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/SR-creature-Wrathman.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Wrathman.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Wrathman.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Wrathman.jpg) A wrathman The Wrathmen of the [Soul Guard](Lore_Soul_Guard.md), known singularly as the Soul Which Never Dies, either appear as [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md) or [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) -like [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) [undead](Lore_Undead.md), but are ultimately husks of their former selves. They are void of most emotions, except are in a state of eternal suffering, and can only truly be freed from their bonds of torment through a ritual. Should one encounter wrathmen, they will seek to sate their hunger for life by feasting on them.

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Wraith), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Wrathman.md)<sup>([DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md))</sup>, [Skyrim TAG](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Skyrim_-_The_Adventure_Game)

## Wraxu
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_03.jpg/200px-ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_03.jpg) The Gave of Sithis, adorned with wraxu feather Wraxu are an extinct species of [bird](Lore_Bird.md) related to the [terror birds](Lore_Terror_Bird.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) that were indigenous to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). They are described as having been about as tall as a [mammoth](Lore_Mammoth.md) with tails as long as wagon tongues. Their species was but one victim of the Great Burn in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They also had a symbolic connection with [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) dating back to the [xanmeer period](Lore_Duskfall.md).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PWF-49)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Gaze_of_Sithis#Preserved_Wraxu_Feathers)

## Wyrm
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/SR-creature-Fire_Wyrm.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Fire_Wyrm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Fire_Wyrm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Fire_Wyrm.jpg) A fire wyrm Wyrms are ethereal [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) -like creatures that can be summoned by dragons themselves. Fire Wyrms (or Flame Wyrms) are a variant summoned by fire Dragons but can also emerge from the corpses of those slain by the fire breath of a powerful [Tongue](Lore_Thu%27um.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-SRFire Wyrm-51)</sup> Storm Wyrms (or Boltwyrms) are a type that can be summoned by storm Dragons.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOStorm Wyrm-52)</sup> Azureblight Wyrms are an odd variety that were summoned by the Dragon who created th mysterious [Azure Blight](Lore_Azure_Blight.md) [disease](Lore_Disease.md).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ESOBlight Wyrm-53)</sup>

Wyrm is a name often used for Dragons themselves.

## Wyvern
Wyverns are [fire](Lore_Elements.md#Fire) -breathing creatures of doubtful existence.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Alchemy-54)</sup> The spell "Wyvern's Sting" is named after them.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-55)</sup> According to a fib from Alchemy, a member of the [House of Reveries](Lore_House_of_Reveries.md), those born to a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) and a mortal can turn into a wyvern during a full [moon](Lore_Moons.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Alchemy-54)</sup> Due to the joking nature of her statement, this description of wyverns is highly unlikely. [Lizardmen](Lore_Lizard_Man.md) have similarly been described as "a hybrid of a man and a dragon".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-56)</sup>
