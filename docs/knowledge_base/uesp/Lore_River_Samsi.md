[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/MW-place-River_Samsi.jpg/200px-MW-place-River_Samsi.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-River_Samsi.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-River_Samsi.jpg) River Samsi The River Samsi, or **Ouada Samsi**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> was a large waterway in the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md) region, upon the northwestern coast of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). It created the peninsula which would become home to [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md) and [Ald Velothi](Lore_Ald_Velothi.md). Its headwaters were northeast of Gnisis, and the river flowed south and then west, wrapping around the city and emptying into the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup>

Many ancestral tombs of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) were nearby. The banks of this river were very steep, so steep it was sometimes called a gorge. This could make crossings difficult, especially since it wasn't deep enough to allow boat transport, but the Dunmer made do with a series of rope bridges. [Kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md) had been known to pose a problem for travelers nearby.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind-2)</sup>

During the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HORR-3)</sup> the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) devastated the area, ostensibly wiping Gnisis from the face of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). An eyewitness reported that the waters around Gnisis were vaporized into steam by a wall of fire so high it blotted out the sun. She may have only survived because she was knocked into the dried riverbed, sparing her from some of the flames.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRY-4)</sup>
