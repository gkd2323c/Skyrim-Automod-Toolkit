[3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Dagon Fel (sometimes spelled **Dagon Fell**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VFMap-1)</sup> is the only settlement on the [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) archipelago, in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The town has historically been occupied by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), ever since the ancient invasion of Morrowind by the [Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md). It was mostly populated by fishermen and as the central settlement in the region, many [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholars and adventurers came here to explore the [Dwemer ruins](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md). It is built atop of the ruins of a [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city known as Mzuleft.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDagon Fel-2)</sup>

## History
Dagon Fel traces its origins to the [Conquest of Morrowind](Lore_Conquest_of_Morrowind.md) in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). At this time, High King [Vrage the Butcher](Lore_Vrage.md) ruled over the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), and invaded surrounding territories such as Resdaynia and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FC-3)</sup> But when both the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) had driven out the Nords in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416), the Dwarves built their own city on Dagon Fel's ruins, known as Mzuleft. But like many other Dwemer settlements, Mzuleft was later abandoned.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDagon Fel-2)</sup> Sometime in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there was a settlement called Dagon Fel with an active port,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONGravius-4)</sup> known for its [Nix-Oxen](Lore_Nix-Ox.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DFNO-5)</sup>

Thousands of years later in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), the Treaty of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) was lifted on the island territory of Vvardenfell, as well as surrounding islands like Sheogorad. Nordic fishermen came to the area and re-established the town of Dagon Fel, atop of the Dwemer ruins. Because of its location, Imperial scholars and adventurers arrive in the area to explore the nearby Daedric and Dwemer ruins, of which the region is riddled.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDagon Fel-2)</sup>

Sometime around [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the Nordic necromancer, Sorkvild the Raven occupied a dwemer tower just outside of Dagon Fel, much to the dismay of the local populous. The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) of Fort Moonmoth sent one of their soldiers to take him out, as a way to show the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of Vvardenfell that they can rely on the Imperial Legion to destroy necromancy. With Sorkvild dealt with, the town of Dagon Fel was at peace and appreciative to the Imperial Legion.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWSorkvild-6)</sup>
