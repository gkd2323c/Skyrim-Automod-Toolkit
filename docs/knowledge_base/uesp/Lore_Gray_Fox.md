# Gray Fox

| Appears in | [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Gray_Fox), [Skyrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pjofr_Ice-Blade), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:The_Gray_Fox) |

The Gray Fox is more accurately described as an identity rather than a person. Although some doubt his existence, the general populace of Tamriel that believes in him sees the persona as one mysterious master thief, who had been the leader of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for three hundred years by the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). In reality a series of different people had actually held the title throughout the years. The persona of the Gray Fox is created by the [Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md), which was originally stolen from [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) by [Emer Dareloth](Lore_Emer_Dareloth.md). Nocturnal cursed the cowl to steal the identity of the wearer. Once a person dons the cowl, the curse transforms them into the Gray Fox and their original identity is lost. The cowl had been passed from one guildmaster to the next, preventing the guildmaster from appearing in public due to the infamous reputation of the Gray Fox.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion-1)</sup>

## History
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-icon-faction-Thieves_Guild-Gray_Fox.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-icon-faction-Thieves_Guild-Gray_Fox.png) Gray Fox heraldry (Oblivion Remastered) In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Hieronymus Lex, a Watch Captain of the [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), began a personal vendetta to bring the Gray Fox to justice, although the Thieves Guild eventually conspired to put an end to his hunt. The current Gray Fox, previously Count [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md) of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), had spent 11 years attempting to break Nocturnal's curse, and eventually succeeded. His [best thief](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) stole an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) from the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md), and used its power to rewrite history. Without the curse, the Thieves Guild had flourished, and the Cowl's power to hide one's identity could be fully manipulated. The persona of the Gray Fox continued to lead the guild, with the Cowl being passed down from the redeemed Count Umbranox to the master thief who aided him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion-1)</sup>

At an unknown time, the Gray Cowl made its way into [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and so did the mantle of the Gray Fox to a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) by the name of Pjofr Ice-Blade. Instead of becoming the head of the Ice-Blade clan, he left it, pursuing his own interests out of greed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SJ-2)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), conspirators succeeded in making the death of Pjofr's sister seem like it was caused by an illness. Her son, Smaref, was left an orphan and last of the Ice-Blades. The scoundrels obtained the boy's consent to transfer his family's lands over to them, then sent him to [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) before he became old enough to suspect their motives.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LTG-3)</sup> After hearing of the passing of his sister, Pjofr learned of Smaref's adoption by one of Riften's wealthy. When Pjofr attended dinner at their household, he saw that this new father figure was caring, while his wife wanted nothing to do with the child. She revealed that her husband was asked by a Thane of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) to take in the urchin as a charity case.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NJ-4)</sup>

Pjofr faked the death of the Gray Fox. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) investigating the unmarked grave took up the quest to gain the Gray Cowl. Following a series of instructions, the Dragonborn swapped important documents with forgeries, ruining the conspirators claims to the Ice-Blade clan's lands. Finally, the Dragonborn delved into the Nordic ruin of Silverdrift Lair, and retrieved the ancestral Sword of Clan Ice–Blade. With the tasks done, the Dragonborn returned to the Gray Fox's grave, and Pjofr came in-person to pass on the Gray Fox's mantle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## Notes
- One document written by an individual known as Ilrathion, Master Enchanter suggests that the Gray Fox was leading the Riften Thieves Guild and recruited the young [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah) and her Khajiit ally Therris as hired blades of the guild and eventually tasked Barenziah to steal a valuable gem from a rival thieves guild in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CPE-6)</sup> The veracity of this account is questionable as it does not align with the events found within the other known biographies of Barenziah's life.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRB-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BOQB-8)</sup>
- The [Fable of the Gryphon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fable_of_the_Gryphon) tells the story of the talking fox Gray, who ran from the pursuit of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) alongside his friend Zariah.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Fot C-9)</sup> The Gray Fox was the persona of a legendary thief, created by the curse placed on the [Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md), which had been stolen by [Emer Dareloth](Lore_Emer_Dareloth.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Gray Fox-10)</sup> The [Fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Fox) is a thieving, talking, [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) fox, associated with the [Luminary Gryphon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Gryphon) who, by his own account, hates the [Evergloam](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Evergloam).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Fox-11)</sup> Given the similarities between the names and events, its possible there might be some connection between the stories.
