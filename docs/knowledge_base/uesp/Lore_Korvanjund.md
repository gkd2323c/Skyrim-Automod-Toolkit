[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/SR-place-Korvanjund.jpg/200px-SR-place-Korvanjund.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Korvanjund.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Korvanjund.jpg) Korvanjund ca. 4E 201 Korvanjund is a Nordic ruin located in the winter forests of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It was once an active settlement that traded in hides and meat,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HOJG-1)</sup> but most notably, it is the secret burial place of High King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md), the last ruler of [Ysgramor's dynasty](Lore_Ysgramor_Dynasty.md) and his symbol of power, the [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-2)</sup>

Korvanjund was active as early as potentially the post- [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) era in Skyrim, as theorized by [Farengar Secret-Fire](Lore_Farengar_Secret-Fire.md) and based on its inclusion in the *[Holdings of Jarl Gjalund](Lore_Holdings_of_Jarl_Gjalund.md)*.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRFarengar-3)</sup> At the time, it was a small fortified settlement<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HOJG-1)</sup> with a dedicated temple, crypt and a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRKorvanjund-4)</sup> A section of the ruins known as the Hall of Stories supposedly tells how Korvanjund was founded but its carvings have become illegible over time.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-2)</sup>

At the time of Jarl Gjalund's reign over [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md), Korvanjund fell in its borders,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HOJG-1)</sup> sometime before its inclusion in the Pale's modern holdings.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRKorvanjund-4)</sup> In [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AHOD-5)</sup> High King Borgas died in distant [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) trying to engage war with the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) in the name of his [Alessian faith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Faith).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-6)</sup> With his death, the Jagged Crown's location was lost to time but legend has it that Borgas' body was secretly returned to Skyrim and buried with the crown in Korvanjund.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-2)</sup> In time, Korvanjund fell into ruin and obscurity, while Borgas' tomb went undisturbed over thousands of years.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRKorvanjund-4)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), as the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) embroiled the province, the [Stormcloak](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) General, Galmar Stone-Fist devised a plan to recover the Jagged Crown and further legitimize [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md)'s claim to the High Kingship of Skyrim. Through nameless sources, Galmar tracked the Jagged Crown's location to Korvanjund and mobilized the Stormcloaks to the ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-2)</sup> Chief Lieutenant of the Skyrim [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), Legate [Rikke](Lore_Rikke.md) received intel from her agents that Galmar found the crown. Knowing his intuition, Rikke sent soldiers to secure it for themselves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRRikke-7)</sup> A conflict ensued in and around Korvanjund but it is unknown which side was victorious. The victors entered Korvanjund and confronted an awakened Borgas in his tomb. From his body, they recovered the Jagged Crown.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRJagged SC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SRJagged IL-9)</sup>
