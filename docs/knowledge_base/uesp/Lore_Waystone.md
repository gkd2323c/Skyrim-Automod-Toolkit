# Lodestone

This article is about the metal. For the lyre also called the Lodestone, see [Callisos' Lodestone](Lore_Callisos%27_Lodestone.md).

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Lodestone.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Lodestone.png) A lodestone Lodestones are [metals](Lore_Metals.md) with strong magnetic energy. They can attract dust, debris, and other metals like [iron](Lore_Iron.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAetheric-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRB1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONMagnet-3)</sup> They are commonly used for navigation, especially as compasses for [sea travel](Lore_Sea_Transportation.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONRagada-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONZa.27ji-5)</sup> Lodestones can also manipulate electricity and its associated magic. The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), who often travel the sea and use storm magic, use lodestones for several functions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONEalcil-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMastengwe-7)</sup> But lodestones can also have magical properties. [Enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) lodestones can focus [magicka](Lore_Magicka.md) over a distance and usually come in pairs to form sympathetic links between them. They can also be focused for navigation spells<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONValessea-8)</sup> and through the links, trace each other's location.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TP-9)</sup> Lodestones are also used in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and can enhance one's jumping abilities.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DFLodestone-10)</sup>

**Waystones** are magical, crystalline balls of stone that the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) of the mid- [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and even [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) used for sea travel. Throughout history, archaeologists have discovered these pieces across ancient Aldmeri shipwrecks and docks and were left to theorize their functions. In was not until as recent as the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md),<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Era)</sup> that people realized they tend to rotate on an axis in a specific direction. Though modern-day historians are still left wondering how they work, they know they are attuned to an unknown power. There are three kinds of waystones. Ones that point southward, northwest, and northeast. [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) used a waystone that pointed northeast from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) to eventually discover [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOTN-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTreasures A-12)</sup>

The [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) used enchanted lodestones to navigate to the [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md) and keep numerous ships in formation and on course.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-IOV-13)</sup> The [druids](Lore_Druids.md) of the [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md) create rough-hewn lodestone compasses that always point to their home island, [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONFiresong-14)</sup> Similarly, some people enchant talismans to always point to [Oldgate](Lore_Oldgate.md) in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONTrasures Talisman-15)</sup>

The [Tarnished](Lore_Tarnished.md) of Slag Town, the slums in the [Brass Fortress](Lore_Brass_Fortress.md) use artificial lodestones to find metal scraps and metallic threads to make anything from clothing and air filters.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONAvarya-16)</sup> [Storm Atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md) can drop lodestones crackling with lightning. Adventurers tend to collect them as trophies.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONTrophies-17)</sup> Aetheric Lodestones are extremely rare and come in the form of a perfectly round orb threaded with [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAetheric-1)</sup>
