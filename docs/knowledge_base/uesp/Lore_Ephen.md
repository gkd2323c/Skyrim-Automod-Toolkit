Ephen (also called **S'ephen**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KEXII-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Surname)</sup> was a [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Chimer)</sup> noble that was active during the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He is the center of an obscure temple-cult who worships him as **the God of the Wild**<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-2)</sup> that was created in homage by his half-brother, [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md), the Witch-King of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KEXII-1)</sup> In death, he was given the duty of safeguarding the [Horn of Summoning](Lore_Horn_of_Summoning.md) and eventually the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md). Statues of Ephen depicted him wielding a hammer, atop of an anvil of [adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRB4-3)</sup> and shrines dedicated to him have been active since the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), as far west as the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFShrines-4)</sup>

## History
S'ephen was born in the city-state of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), in the early First Era. He was the stepson of King [Kronin](Lore_Kronin.md) of Ebonheart, who was active during the [Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md)'s conquest over [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). S'ephen was not considered a pure-blooded member of the [Clan R'Aathim](Lore_R%27Aathim.md), closely related to his mother instead of his father.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KEXII-1)</sup> He was named after an occult nature god.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TP-5)</sup> Regardless, he grew up with his father and his two brothers, [Cruethys](Lore_Cruethys.md) and [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md). When the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) claimed Ebonheart, S'ephen and his family turned to raiding across the countryside, as many Dark Elves did during that time. He passed away sometime after the R'Aathim Clan retook their kingdom, along with the rest of the province.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KEXII-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KEX-6)</sup>

King Moraelyn created a temple-cult to Ephen which was worshipped in his hometown of Mournhold. When King [Edward](Lore_Edward.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) passed away, he gave the Horn of Summoning to his old friend Moraelyn, who in turn left the horn in Ephen's tomb underneath Mournhold. However, when [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) conquered the province of Morrowind, he took the horn. General [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) left the Staff of Chaos in the tomb ever since.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRB4-3)</sup> But eventually, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) claimed the staff and brought about the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) during the reign of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRB5-7)</sup> Worship of Ephen has managed to thrive beyond its founding, as far as [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405). Temples erected to Ephen were active throughout the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFShrines-4)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Surname) | At the time of *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* and *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, apostrophes were used exclusively for Dark Elven last names and never in first names. Of note, "S'-" was a starting particle used for surnames in the games' name generator.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFDENames-8)</sup> This may indicate that "S'ephen" was actually a last name of some sort. |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_Chimer) | The book *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)* was written before the introduction of the Chimer in later game entries. The Chimer would not become Dunmer until around 1E 700, which is after the timeline of the book. |
| --- | --- |
- In the *[Daggerfall Preview](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall_Preview)*, the first of three demos for [The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), there is a list of gods in the character creation, and among these is Ephen, who is described as the *"God of the Wild."* In the files of the beta, Ephen is associated with two factions called the *"Fellowship of Ephen"* and *"the Wildlords."*
- According to [Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson) both Ephen and Jephre are nature gods, and that Ephen the god predated Ephen the Chimer, who was named after the god.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
