[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/SR-item-Chillrend.jpg/200px-SR-item-Chillrend.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Chillrend.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Chillrend.jpg) Chillrend Chillrend is a unique [glass](Skyrim_Malachite.md) sword found during [The Pursuit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Pursuit) in [Mercer Frey](Skyrim_Mercer_Frey.md)'s [house](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Riftweald_Manor) in a display case with an expert-level lock. It deals [frost damage](Skyrim_Frost_Damage.md) as well as having a chance to [paralyze](Skyrim_Paralyze_(effect).md) anything it strikes. [Tempering](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) Chillrend requires one [refined malachite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Refined_Malachite) and the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk, with the tempering being twice as effective if you have gained the [Glass Smithing](Skyrim_Glass_Smithing.md) perk. It would be identical in appearance to a [glass sword](Skyrim_Glass_Sword.md) were it not for its blue hue.

## Notes
- Chillrend also [appeared](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Chillrend) in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* with a similar enchantment as a reward for successfully completing the quest [The Killing Field](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Killing_Field).
- The level 46+ version of Chillrend has the highest base damage of any one-handed sword in vanilla Skyrim. The heavier [dragonbone sword](Skyrim_Dragonbone_Sword.md) from [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) equals its base damage, while only the 60+ version of [Miraak's Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Miraak%27s_Sword) from [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) surpasses it. - When including enchantments, Chillrend is the most devastating one-handed weapon available as-is, regardless of add-ons. (Besides the [Dawnguard Rune Axe](Skyrim_Dawnguard_Rune_Axe.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> or [Dragonbane](Skyrim_Dragonbane.md), when used on undead or dragons, respectively.)
- The name of the unique enchantment on the weapon is called "Frostfeed". - The frost damage effect on Chillrend is affected by the [Augmented Frost](Skyrim_Augmented_Frost.md) perks in the [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) perk tree. The damage will increase to 37 with the first, and 45 with the second perk.
- Chillrend has a unique frost sound effect and has frosty mists falling from the blade when drawn.
- [Shadowfoot Sanctum](Skyrim_Shadowfoot_Sanctum_(place).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Shadowfoot_Sanctum.md)</sup> contains a special holder for this item.
- It is not necessarily a requirement to join the Thieves Guild to obtain this weapon: with creative jumping from a brazier in front of the nearby Temple of Mara, you can mount the roof of the Temple, then leap onto the locked gate and balance yourself and then jump over (or use [Whirlwind Sprint](Skyrim_Whirlwind_Sprint.md)) to the roof of Mercer's house and ascend it until you can hop down to the ledge with the door leading to his house. From there, pick the lock, enter his house and head down to the basement to retrieve Chillrend.
- The part of the enchantment of Chillrend that induces paralysis is not a guarantee. Instead, it functions identically to other weapons with an enchantment of paralysis: there is a 26% chance it will paralyze the target struck with the weapon.

## Bugs
- Unlike ordinary frost damage enchantments, Chillrend's frost damage does *not* damage the target's stamina. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 4.1.5, fixes this bug.
- The frost sound effect may loop indefinitely for various reasons, including rapidly switching between Chillrend and other weapons. - Transforming into a Werewolf or Vampire Lord temporarily ends the sound loop until a save is loaded.
