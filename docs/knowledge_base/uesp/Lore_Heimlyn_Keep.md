[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-place-Heimlyn_Keep_02.jpeg/200px-ON-place-Heimlyn_Keep_02.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heimlyn_Keep_02.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heimlyn_Keep_02.jpeg) Heimlyn Keep ca. 2E 582 Heimlyn Keep (sometimes simply called **Heimlyn**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONBeron-1)</sup> is a fortified settlement built on a mesa overlooking the [Zabamat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zabamat) region in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Despite its location far from their main settlements, it is a foothold owned by [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) in central Morrowind. The town serves primarily as a research center where they study ancient [magic](Lore_Magic.md), weapons, and relics that are too dangerous for circulation. These items are stored in a reliquary underneath it and its guardians have been ages ago from their knowledge.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONBeron-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad Heimlyn-2)</sup>

## History
When Heimlyn Keep was founded is unknown, however, it reportedly predated the nearby city of [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup> In an unspecified time, when the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) conflicted with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), two mendicant worshippers of [Namira](Lore_Namira.md), Rendrasa Malena and Adras Velae developed a deadly curse called the Spellplague that proved to dangerous and uncontrollable for use. The Chimer lords bound the weapon in a vault, killed and imprisoned its creators with it. The vault became the Heimlyn Keep Reliquary and its guardian was Keeper Vildras Telvanni.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONVildras-4)</sup>

For generations, members of House Telvanni have chosen caretakers of the keep who also oversee a group of assistants. By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the reliquary was cared for by the couple, Beron and Merarii Telvanni.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONBeron-1)</sup> At that time, the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) invaded the Stonefalls region and launched an attack on Heimlyn Keep. Though the Telvanni mages held them back as much as they could, the Covenant entered the vault and smashed a relic, unleashing the spellplague. By doing so, people in town became afflicted and after death, they were resurrected as cursed [spirits](Lore_Ghost.md). The caretakers were able to create a potion to cure them but ran out.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONBeron-1)</sup>

An [adventurer](Lore_The_Vestige.md) happened upon the settlement and offered their help. They gathered the potion ingredients for Merarii and created an Etheric Totem, which allowed any remaining assistants and citizens to escape.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCurse-5)</sup> The adventurer later approached the spirit of Keeper Vildras, who gave them the mission of destroying the mendicant mages and the source of the spellplague, Mendicant Sulinor. After a battle in the reliquary, the threat to Heimlyn Keep was destroyed and the town was saved.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONUndone-6)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Heimlyn Keep was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lady Shelelle and had a rivalry with [Riverbridge](Lore_Riverbridge.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AR-7)</sup> Heimlyn Keep was neighbored by several settlements, such as [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md) to the southeast and [Oaktown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oaktown) to the west.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ARMap-8)</sup>

## Notes
- In an early concept map of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), the town of Heimlyn Keep is shown as an occupied territory of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
- According to unused text in earlier versions of *[ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*, Heimlyn Keep was originally a trading settlement owned by [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) and it was embroiled in corruption. It shared its name with an active volcano to the east known as Mount Heimlyn.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup>
