Tzirzhalir, the **nightmare behemoth**, was a powerful seemingly [undead](Lore_Undead.md) [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) entity that went by many names, and resembled a gargantuan [bone colossus](Lore_Bone_Colossus.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tzirzhalir-1)</sup> The creature was described as profoundly evil.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Fennorian-2)</sup> He was confined to the Pit of Sorrows at the heart of the Unhallowed Grave in [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). Aside from his massive size, he could unleash a wide cloud of freezing air with his breath and hurl flaming boulders.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tzirzhalir-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/ON-npc-Kjalnar_Tombskald.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Kjalnar_Tombskald.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Kjalnar_Tombskald.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Kjalnar_Tombskald.jpg) Kjalnar Tombskald Tzirzhalir's origins were unclear, though it is known that he was already sealed within the Unhallowed Grave during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), or possibly even earlier, as he was present there before the site was used to hold the ashes of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRN-3)</sup>

Those ashes were then sealed within the Unhallowed Grave by the soldiers of the [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Garrison.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UG-4)</sup> and the Redguard order of the Pyre Watch,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRN-3)</sup> locking away an evil too great to be left unguarded.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UG-4)</sup> The remnants of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) were treated much the same way as Tzirzhalir had been. The Pyre Watch buried the Gray Host's ashes in the Unhallowed Grave for a reason, as something evil already dwelled there.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRN-3)</sup> Centuries later, adventurers and explorers still gave this place a wide berth.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UG-4)</sup>

According to the research of Kjalnar Tombskald, something evil and impossibly dangerous was already kept there. He described Tzirzhalir as a harbinger of shrieking fates, destined to usher in an age of torment the likes of which Tamriel had never seen. This creature went by many names, most of which were wiped from history, and few pronounceable by mortal tongues. Kjalnar found only one: Tzirzhalir, the nightmare behemoth. He planned to reach the heart of the Unhallowed Grave and call it by name to awaken it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRN-3)</sup> The Pyre Watch were instructed never to speak the names of those interred unless necessary, out of caution.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PWP-5)</sup>

Kjalnar had grand ambitions, a dream that once they emerged from that cavernous prison, no one would forget the name of Tzirzhalir or his own.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRN-3)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), he acted to make that vision a reality. However, he was challenged by the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), who assisted Shelaria of Pyre Watch and Fennorian of [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md). During the battle, Kjalnar briefly awakened Tzirzhalir, but he was killed, and with his death, the creature fell back into the vast pit where it had dwelled before.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KT-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-UGQ-7)</sup> The Undaunted later fashioned trophies and busts in Tzirzhalir's likeness to commemorate their victory.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BTTT-8)</sup>

## Notes
- Tzirzhalir being an ancient creature of bone bares resemblance to a group of supposed lingering [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) known as the "Old Bones". There is some similarity between the name of Tzirzhalir, that of Mzrelnir in Lucent Citadel,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mzrelnir-9)</sup> and those of some of the "Old Bones" such as Goradir or Fildenir.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONGoradir-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONFildenir-11)</sup>
