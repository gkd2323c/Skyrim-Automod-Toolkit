As the central region of Tamriel, Cyrodiil is also known as "the Heartland." The capital of both Cyrodiil and the [Holy Cyrodilic Empire of Tamriel](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md), the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), is located on [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md) in the center of the province; at the heart of the city is [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), possibly the most renowned monument in Tamriel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-POT-5)</sup> The Imperial City itself has been considered the sole city-state of the Imperial Province, with [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) being outlying territories of the state of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LOMap-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Arena Imp Pro-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Ted Imp Prov-9)</sup> The name "Cyrodiil" itself is ancient elven in origin,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrus Comic-10)</sup> and the "-iil" suffix it features means "folk."<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Land War Poetry-11)</sup> The name is a pretty blanket term almost every element of the Imperial Province: the people are called "[the Cyrodiils](Lore_Imperial.md)," the city-state of the land is called "[Cyrodiil](Lore_Imperial_City.md)," and the ruler of the land no matter their origin is called "[The Cyrodiil](Lore_Emperor.md)." This is derived from a king-is-the-land tradition that reaches back to when the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruled the land<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrus Comic-10)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LOMap-6)</sup> (hence the land's "Red King" sobriquet).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup>

Despite some accounts describing Cyrodiil as a jungle,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-POT-5)</sup> it has a primarily temperate climate.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Oblivion-13)</sup> One scholar of the middle [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) attributed this discrepancy to errors made during the transcription of historical texts,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-THOC-14)</sup> while another speculated that White-Gold Tower itself adapted the climate to suit the region's inhabitants.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SCAS-15)</sup> Later sources asserted that Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) altered Cyrodiil's climate after he united all the provinces under his rule.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Heimskr-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-FTMHT-17)</sup>

## History

### Merethic and First Eras
When the Aldmeri landed in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), they did not move far inland, but told of ancient beasts before men or [mer](Lore_Mer.md). Unfortunately, oral tradition only gives unclear glimpses of what they may have looked like. One account describes the ancient inhabitants of City Isle as a race of [Bird Men](Lore_Bird_Men.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Fot N-18)</sup> The only definite information of Cyrodiil's history is after the beginning of recorded history when the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) came to call Cyrodiil their home. Described in *A Pocket Guide to the Empire*, "The Ayleids were ancient [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), cousins of all the elven races that exist to this day. Over time, they became a distinct people, crafting a civilization whose ruins still puzzle and fascinate modern archaeologists and adventurers." The [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) people of the region became slave labor for the Ayleids once man-mer tensions in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) reached a boiling point. One of the most important events of Cyrodilic history is the slave rebellion of [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242). The men and mer had been fighting in Skyrim for quite a while. This revolt was led by [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), who handed the entire region to the insurgent humans. "The heart of Tamriel was going to belong to these former slaves, present day Cyrodilics or Imperials, forever more."<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3C-2)</sup>

In an alliance with Skyrim, the Alessian Empire pushed to the west towards [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) which at the time was under the control of the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). Another big change was present at this time: as described in *A Pocket Guide to the Empire*, "The prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md)'s teachings brought both identity to Cyrodiil, codifying the pantheon most civilized Tamriellians worship to this day." It also caused conflict due to the more severe strictures he espoused.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3C-2)</sup> The next large event in the history of Cyrodiil occurred in the year [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703): the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) invasion. This event not only affected Cyrodiil, but it brought all of Tamriel together to fight the new threat. The Akaviri were driven off, and the people of Tamriel subsequently became more cooperative with one another. Under the rule of [Emperor Reman I](Lore_Reman.md), Cyrodiil became cosmopolitan and incorporated aspects of High Rock, [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md), [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md), and even [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) culture. The [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) of Cyrodiil continued to expand, engulfing almost all of Tamriel. All was normal until the assassination of [Emperor Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md) and his son [Juilek](Lore_Juilek.md). The assassinations marked the end of the First Era and the Second Empire.

### Second Era
The government was under the control of [Akaviri Potentates](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), and it continued to evolve until the last Potentate was killed in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430). Throughout the rest of the Second Era, Tamriel was in the time known as the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Nibenay and Colovia split apart; farms, villages, and even highways were in ruin. Many tried to claim the Imperial throne and this chaos lasted for centuries. The only hope for Tamriel was [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). He started the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) by unifying Cyrodiil and then the rest of Tamriel during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).

### Third Era
The Empire has both thrived and struggled since its founding; conflicts like the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), [The Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), and [fractious wars](Lore_Fractious_Wars.md) proved to weaken the empire.

### Fourth Era
At the end of the Third Era, Tamriel was left with no emperor due to the events of the Oblivion Crisis. In the early Fourth Era, the Empire nearly collapsed and Cyrodiil's counties of Leyawiin and Bravil seceded until circa 4E 22, when [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede.md) led an army to conquer the Imperial City and was crowned the first emperor of the Mede Dynasty. Cyrodiil was again reunited but would face a new era of bloodshed against the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) starting in 4E 171, triggering the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) after the Thalmor's ultimatum was rejected by [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md). Cyrodiil was invaded by the Thalmor, who took the Imperial City by force and committed many atrocities on the helpless population. It wasn't until 4E 175 that the Imperial Legion was able to retake the Imperial City during the Battle of the Red Ring, resulting in the destruction of all Aldmeri forces in Cyrodiil. Although the Imperials emerged victorious, they felt they were unable to continue the war and ended the Great War with the signing of the White-Gold Concordat, banning the worship of Talos and ceding a large portion of southern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) to the Dominion, leading to Skyrim's distrust.

## Home of the Red King Once Jungled
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg/200px-ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Serpent%27s_Image.jpg) A primordial vision of Cyrodiil in a future where all of Tamriel is jungled *[CHIM](Lore_CHIM.md). Those who know it can reshape the land. Witness the home of the [Red King](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) Once Jungled.*
— *[Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes, Book Three](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries_3.md)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/ON-prerelease-Dawnwood.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Dawnwood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Dawnwood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Dawnwood.jpg) The Dawnwood Cyrodiil, despite its typically temperate climate,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Oblivion-13)</sup> has mysteriously been jungled or at least described as jungled at several points in its history. The Serpent was a being who wished to return Tamriel to its primordial state, in a vision created by it depicting a future in which it succeeded, the Imperial City was overgrown by a jungle.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-19)</sup> In the early First Era, during the reign of the Ayleid Empire, contemporary sources (such as the *Hosiric Lays* and the [*Tamrilean Tractates*](Lore_Tamrilean_Tractates.md)) described Cyrodiil as having jungle foliage.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SCAS-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-20)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TSOP2-21)</sup> [*King Edward*](Lore_King_Edward.md), a historical fiction set in the First Era, described Cyrodiil as temperate.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-KEVIII-22)</sup> As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Cyrodiil was visibly temperate.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-12)</sup> Scholars of the time debated the nature of Cyrodiil's supposed jungles. In his book [*The Heartland of Cyrodiil*](Lore_The_Heartland_of_Cyrodiil.md), [Phrastus](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) held the opinion that Cyrodiil ever having been jungled was a matter of transcription errors.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-THOC-14)</sup> However, his intellectual rival [Lady Cinnabar](Lore_Lady_Cinnabar.md) of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md), had a more involved theory in her book, [*Subtropical Cyrodiil: A Speculation*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Subtropical_Cyrodiil__A_Speculation). She posited the idea that the White-Gold Tower, which possesses incredible metaphysical power, is capable of changing the climate of Cyrodiil to suit the current civilization in power. As the Ayleids were from the tropical Summerset Isles, she believes Cyrodiil was changed when they were in power to reflect their tastes, with Men coming into power causing a cooler shift in climate.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SCAS-15)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a large tropical forest known as the Dawnwood was grown near Skingrad by the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), a cult of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who believed the Ayleids were their ancestors and sought to reclaim their ancient lands.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Their plans were stopped before they could expand the Dawnwood to more of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-GR-24)</sup> However, by [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), Cyrodiil was recorded as being fully jungled. The first edition of the [Pocket Guide to the Empire](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition.md) described the province at this time as mostly endless jungle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> Certain writings claim that at the onset of his fully unified reign, Tiber Septim acknowledged that the dense jungles that encompassed Cyrodiil were hated by his people, thus he proclaimed to have [breathed](Lore_Thu%27um.md) in [royalty](Lore_CHIM.md) to reshape the very land that his Red Legions dwelt upon, supposedly ridding it of the endless jungle.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Heimskr-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-FTMHT-17)</sup> However some citizens of the Empire described the province's climate as jungled circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427),<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-25)</sup> and books describing Cyrodiil as mostly jungled were in circulation at the same time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-POT-5)</sup> However it is known that by atleast [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Cyrodiil's landscape featured a temperate climate.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Oblivion-13)</sup>

## Geography
Cyrodiil has a varied environment, and is divided into several subregions:

- [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md): This vast region dominates Cyrodiil, containing all the area that drains into the [River Niben](Lore_Niben_River.md). The area around the Imperial City and the Upper Niben is often known as "the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md)". East of the river, the region consists of mostly open fields while to the west and north it is more wooded. The Imperial City covers [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md) in the center of [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md), the source of the Niben, and the city of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) is located on the banks of [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md) in the "[Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md)". The [Caledon River](Lore_Caledon_River.md) and [Yrinthi River](Lore_Yrinthi_River.md) are accessible within the region.
- [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md): An immense woodland area just west of the Imperial City.
- [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md): A hilly, forested region of western Cyrodiil with the town of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) as its main settlement. - [Imperial Reserve](Lore_Imperial_Reserve.md)
- [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md): The open countryside of southern Cyrodiil that is well known for its vineyards. The city of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) is located here. - [Strid Vale](Lore_Strid_Vale.md) / Dawnwood
- [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md): The western coastline of Cyrodiil that is dotted by farms. The main settlements of the region are the port city of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), and the city of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) further inland.
- [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md): A mountain range in northern Cyrodiil on the [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) border, containing the town of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md).
- [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md): A mountain range in eastern Cyrodiil on the [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) border. The city of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) is located at the foothills.
- [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md): A swampland in southern Cyrodiil east of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), along the [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) border.

## Fauna
Cyrodiil is home to many carnivorous creatures such as [bears](Lore_Bear.md), [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md), and [mountain lions](Lore_Mountain_Lion.md), which all often prey upon [deer](Lore_Deer.md). River-Newts are a type of [newt](Lore_Newt.md) whose scales are used to make armor by [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) guards in [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-River Newt Armor-26)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Giant bugs such as [hoarvors](Lore_Hoarvor.md) and [shalks](Lore_Shalk.md) are uncommonly seen, but they are known to live deep underground in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-28)</sup>

## Notable Places

[Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md)

Artemon
- A landlocked city located somewhere in Cyrodiil.

- A squalid city on the west bank of the Niben.

- An island of Cyrodiil province located in the western Abecean Sea.
[Topal Island](Lore_Topal_Island.md)

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/80/AR-map-Imperial_Province_Dungeon_Map.png/200px-AR-map-Imperial_Province_Dungeon_Map.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Imperial_Province_Dungeon_Map.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Imperial_Province_Dungeon_Map.png) Dungeons of the Imperial Province - Like all the other provinces of Tamriel in *[The Elder Scrolls: Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, the Imperial Province has a set of fourteen dungeons which were hand placed on the map by the developers. However, since there are no obtainable extraneous quests in the Imperial Province, these dungeons are never added to your world map and cannot be travelled to. Additionally there is a [fifteenth dungeon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Maps#Additional_Unused_Dungeon_Toggled_On) on the western coast which is loaded in separately from the previous set, and seems to have been intended for a cut penultimate dungeon delve before the [Imperial Palace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:The_Imperial_Palace) finale.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-TES1Dun Maps-29)</sup>
- Prior to *[The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)*, Cyrodiil was known as the "Imperial Province". According to [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride), "Cyrodiil" was originally labeled as an empire on an internal calendar, which he then stamped onto the Imperial Province when it had to be given a name for *Redguard*. Colovia was also present on the internal calendar, but had no representation geographically, hence it was also merged into the new worldbuilding of the Imperial Province.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-8)</sup>
- The [King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md) books refer to Cyrodiil as either the **Wilderland**<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-KEVIII-22)</sup> or the **Wilderness**.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-KEX-30)</sup> The character [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md) describes it as a *"wide green land of rolling hills with only a few stands of trees. It seemed to spread on forever."* He later claims in the next line, *"Be on your guard. It seems a pleasant land, but no king's writ runs here. Each man's hand is against every other's -- and there are worse than men. All the races of Tamriel meet here, and clash, save thine, perhaps."*
