#### Baan Dar, the Bandit God
[Baan Dar](Lore_Baan_Dar.md) (known as **the Bandit God**, **the Man of a Thousand Faces**, **the Pariah**, **the Legend**, the **Robber Baron**, **Master of Shadows**, the **Undying Avenger** or simply the **Avenger**) is the trickster spirit that is worshipped by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), but is known to have influence in places such as [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). In his Khajiiti aspect, the Pariah manifests as the cleverness or desperate genius of the long-suffering Khajiit, outplaying their fellow mortals, the [humans](Lore_Men.md) and the [elves](Lore_Mer.md). The nature of Baan Dar is a topic of speculation by scholars across the continent, believed by some to be more than an entity of tricks, but a powerful force that shapes the land and lives of the people in its wake, for the better. Baan Dar's name has been used liberally through the provinces; it has been used by the traveling merchant tribe, the [Baandari Pedlars](Lore_Baandari_Pedlars.md), while the criminal malcontent often refer to themselves in groups as the Baan Dars or the Bandits. His domain is known as the [Five Fingers Dance](Lore_Five_Fingers_Dance.md).

#### Blind God
The [Blind God](Lore_Blind_God.md) is an obscure god who ruled over and guarded the [Mantellan Crux](Lore_Mantellan_Crux.md), a realm of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). He was known to get quite jealous, and [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), who was a known visitor to this realm and had dealings with the Blind God, considered him to be a pompous upstart of a god. He manifested as a large skull with empty eye sockets, and had many smaller skulls in his service. Circa [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417) an unknown [agent](Lore_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) infiltrated the Crux with the express purpose of attaining the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md). Among numerous other obstacles, this agent passed the trial the Blind God set forth and ultimately retrieved the Mantella.

#### Boethiah, Goddess of Destruction
[Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) (sometimes spelled **Boethia** or **Boet-hi-Ah**), known as the **Prince of Plots**, the **Daedra Prince of Cruelty and Torture**, the **Dark Warrior**, **Deceiver of Nations**, **Queen of Shadows**, **Goddess of Destruction**, **Hunger**, and **Fount of Inspiration**, **He-Who-Destroys and She-Who-Erases**, **Boethiah the Schemer** , **The Dark Mistress**, and called **Boethra** by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) who rules over deceit, conspiracy, secret plots of murder, assassination, treason, and unlawful overthrow of authority. This sphere is destructive in nature, and Boethiah's destructiveness comes from inspiring the arms of mortal warriors. The Dark Warrior has been described as "cold" and "impersonal", "cruel", and one of the more "demonic" of the Daedra lords. Worshippers are known to hold bloody competitions in Boethiah's honor, battling—even killing—each other, for Boethiah is said to care only for their blood. In Morrowind, the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) known as the [Hunger](Lore_Hunger.md), foul creatures that are eternally bone thin, are associated with the Prince.

Boethiah sees battle as a blessing, and is frequently depicted as a great caped warrior, often in a stoic pose. While sometimes described as male, Boethiah is also commonly referred to as female, sometimes within the same text. Notably, Boethiah is usually depicted as male in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), but is also known for taking the form of a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) "warrior-queen".

She rules from the realm of [Attribution's Share](Lore_Attribution%27s_Share.md). Boethiah often manifests using tricky, unpredictable avatars, which can appear as a male or female of any race. These avatars become involved with important or momentous events, working behind the scenes to achieve an outcome that serves Boethiah's will. They will often challenge mortals, seeking to test the mettle of heroes and commoners alike. After a trial ends, the avatar is likely to vanish, leading to strange and mysterious stories and legends among the participants.

Despite the dark undertones of Boethiah's sphere and influence on Tamriel, the Prince was considered in Morrowind to be one of the three "[Good Daedra](Lore_Good_Daedra.md)" and the Anticipation of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) by the old [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), and one of the Reclamations by the New Temple. The Prince of Plots has a scion, the [demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](Lore_Fa-Nuit-Hen.md). Despite this, the information broker Madam Whim believes that the demiprince did not actually originate from Boethiah, while also stating that "Boethiah supports their claim of scionship". In the same interview, Whim would describe how Daedric Princes have been known to adopt scions in the past. In the *[Five Songs of King Wulfharth](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md)*, [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) states that [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) is the son of Boethiah, one of the strongest Padomaics, but it is unclear how literal the statement was. [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), as one [Corner of the House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md), is considered Boethiah's "arch-enemy" in Morrowind and they are in a perennial fight. Boethiah can be summoned on [Gauntlet](Lore_Gauntlet.md), the 2<sup>nd</sup> of [Sun's Dusk](Lore_Sun%27s_Dusk.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Boethiah.md).

#### Brackenleaf
[Brackenleaf](Lore_Brackenleaf_(tree).md) is believed to be one of the first trees of Grahtwood. The story goes that when [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) spoke their world-shaping tale, she planted her words in the earth and there grew Brackenleaf. Indeed, Brackenleaf was compared to Elden Root, mentioned as one of many more ancient seeds. Brackenleaf's heart is a sought after relic as legends say the heart holds the power of Y'ffre, the same magic used to create the world. Due to this, it's been under threat since the beginning of time itself, with foes seeking to take its power for themselves. The Outsider is a prominent example of such a foe.
