Undead Ayleid **Wights** appear in Skyrim within the ruins of [Rielle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rielle) and differ from [Draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md) in several respects. They come in two varieties, Wights and [Wight Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wight_Lord), the latter being described on a separate page. While they lack the power of the [Thu'um](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) which higher-level Nord undead have access to, they also have 50% resistance to frost, and some Wights also use frost magic in combat. Wights are less talkative than Draugr, and hit harder in combat since even the lower-level Wights use [Elven weapons](Skyrim_Elven.md#Elven_Weapons), along with matching shields if the weapon is one-handed. Unlike Thalmor Soldiers which only use one-handed weapons, Wights also come in two-handed specialist varieties.

Wights all share a similar appearance, but can be very different levels.

Similar to their appearance in *[Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Wight_Lord)*, Wight Lords wear distinctive horned helmets of moonstone that differ greatly from the avian-themed helms used by the [Altmer](Skyrim_High_Elf.md) in the present day.

Some Wight Lords are exclusively mages, casting Frost magic and having no melee weapons. However, their Frost Atronachs and melee-class fellow undead can make up for this weakness.

Wights scale up to level 30. However, they possess [perks](Skyrim_Damage.md#NPC-Only) that greatly increase their physical damage output, making them incredibly dangerous opponents who should be taken out as quickly as possible.
