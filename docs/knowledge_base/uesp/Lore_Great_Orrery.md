[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png/200px-LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png) The Summerset Orrery (Legends) The Great Orrery (also called the **Summerset Orrery**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LGOrrery-1)</sup> and the **Orrery of Firsthold**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-2)</sup> is an orrery built in [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), a significant city in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3-3)</sup> It is overseen by the Varmaster<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-2)</sup> and it is the centerpiece of a large dome structure with an open ceiling revealing the sky and images of the thirteen [constellations](Lore_Constellations.md) around it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LGOrrery-1)</sup>

The orrery is the lasting legacy of the [Sunbirds of Alinor](Lore_Sun_Birds_of_Alinor.md), pioneer explorers of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) and the void of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). It is also made of celestial materials gathered by these [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) explorers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3-3)</sup>

In [2E 235](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_235), High Kinlord [Rilis XII](Lore_Rilis_XII.md) asked the Varmaster of the Orrery to predict his future. When he was informed that the Orrery did not possess [divination](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divination), he dismissed the Varmaster and had the Great Orrery disassembled. At the time, it was a massive scandal in the scholarly world across Tamriel. The pieces were carefully put into storage, and by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), well into Rilis XIII's reign, it was believed to still be beneath Firsthold Castle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-2)</sup>

This, however, would contradict other information as supposedly, when Princess [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) disappeared from the Summerset Isles in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573), the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) reported many strange signs and portents like the Great Orrery spinning backward.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ATUQ-4)</sup>
