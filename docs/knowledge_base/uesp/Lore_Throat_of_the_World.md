Mother Wind) by the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOJG-2)</sup> as well as in common parlance,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Adventure Game-3)</sup><sup>:311, 321-328</sup> **Mount Hrothgar**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FOTG-4)</sup> **High Hrothgar**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LRPFTA-6)</sup> or **Throat Mountain**)<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TSWATD-7)</sup> is the highest mountain in all of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and one of the tallest peaks in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), second only to [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) in the east. Ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) tradition dictates that [man](Lore_Man.md) was created when the sky breathed on the land through the mountain, hence its name.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-8)</sup> It is because of this, one of Skyrim's nicknames is also the Throat of the World.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-COTS-9)</sup> The mountain is believed to be one of the mythical [Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md) and is known through this legend as the **Snow Throat**<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONTowers-10)</sup> or the **Snow Tower**.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TBOTD-11)</sup> The Snow-Throat's founding stone is described as "the cave".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-MKP-12)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Cave)</sup>

## General Information

### Cultural Interpretations
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg/200px-SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) High Hrothgar [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c5/SR-place-Throat_of_the_World2.jpg/200px-SR-place-Throat_of_the_World2.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Throat_of_the_World2.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Throat_of_the_World2.jpg) The Throat of the World's summit The only people known to live on the Throat of the World are the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), reclusive Nordic monks that worship [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), from their ancient monastery of [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-8)</sup> The mountain itself is sacred to Kyne, as well as [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), her [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) aspect among the main [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) pantheon. Both are spirits of the winds; their great temple is the sky and the natural world. They were also the ones to gift the ancient Nords speech in the [dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) and the use of their [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), or more commonly known as Voice.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VOF-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRArngeir-14)</sup>

The Nordic myth is that the sky breathed down the mountain, creating man and establishing Skyrim as their original homeland, as opposed to [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) in the far north. This idea also gives a second meaning to the ancient Nordic stories, the *[Songs of the Return](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return.md)*.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-8)</sup> It is considered the most sacred mountain in Skyrim, where the ancient [Tongues](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tongues) mastered the voice and defeated the World-Eater, [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-1)</sup>

A tradition among [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) is to climb the Throat of the World via the **Seven Thousand Steps** to High Hrothgar, a long ascending road that spirals around the mountain, starting from its southeastern base in [Ivarstead](Lore_Ivarstead.md). Many pilgrims come from all over Skyrim to walk the steps and meditate in absolute silence, reflecting on their life, honoring the Greybeards, or attuning themselves to the sky above.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-8)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRBarknar-15)</sup> However, pilgrims run the risk of encountering beasts and monsters between prayer posts.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-EGT-16)</sup> Along this path are ten stone etched tablets that detail Skyrim's history with the voice and the Greybeards' history from Jurgen Windcaller to Tiber Septim.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRTablets-17)</sup>

Its summit was the roost of [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md), Alduin's former lieutenant and later master of the Greybeards.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRArngeir-14)</sup> To the broader world, people know him through the rumors of a dragon monk who dwells with the Greybeards on the Throat of the World,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LADITSE-18)</sup> but the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) and their modern iteration, the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) had been familiar with him since at least the early Second Era. An established policy was to avoid confrontation with the Greybeards while waiting for the moment to enact justice on him.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AOD-19)</sup> The path to the summit is perilous, covered in a neverending blizzard that only wielders of the voice can climb.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRArngeir-14)</sup>

[Calcelmo's Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calcelmo%27s_Stone), a large stone tablet with inscriptions in both [Dwemeris](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemeris) and the [Falmer language](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Falmer_Language) uses the words *"kemelmzulchond"* and *"spantelepe-laelia"* to translate "snow-throated".<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TOCS-20)</sup> As the Snow Tower, the Throat of the World is a vital part in the ancient [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md), which is sometimes attributed to the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) or the ancient [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md). The line, "*when the Snow Tower lies sundered, kingless, bleeding..."* has been construed to refer to the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md), which broke out just when Alduin returned in time and the Last Dragonborn appeared. The next line elaborates further by stating, *"The World-Eater wakes, and the Wheel turns upon the Last Dragonborn."*<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TBOTD-11)</sup> [Alduin's Wall](Lore_Alduin%27s_Wall.md), which displays the prophecy in full, features the Throat of the World split or sundered in half.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SRWall-21)</sup> A piece of the [Staff of Towers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff_of_Towers), created by the former [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) king, [Anumaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril), is based on the Snow-Throat.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SOT-22)</sup> As with all Towers, the Throat of the World is said to exist beyond the physical and in some sense apart, or detached, from ordinary space and time.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONCelarus-23)</sup>

### Geography and Ecology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/SR-place-South_Skybound_Watch.jpg/200px-SR-place-South_Skybound_Watch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-South_Skybound_Watch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-South_Skybound_Watch.jpg) South Skybound Watch [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/LO-map-Skyrim_%28PGE1%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Skyrim_%28PGE1%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(PGE1).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(PGE1).jpg.md) Map of Skyrim, with Hrothgar in its center Though the Greybeards are neutral in Skyrim's politics, High Hrothgar and the entire Throat of the World fall under [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md)'s borders, even when the only entrance to the Seven Thousand Steps is in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md). However, both High Hrothgar and [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) city are visible from each other on a clear day.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRThroat-24)</sup> More settlements are found along its base, such as [Riverwood](Lore_Riverwood.md) on the eastern valley of Whiterun Hold. There are other notable landmarks like the [Ritual Stone](Lore_Standing_Stones.md#Standing_Stones) on the [White River](Lore_White_River.md), Orphan Rock, a secluded grove to the south, Skybound Watch Pass, a small Nordic ruin overlooking Helgen and Riverwood,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SRSkybound-25)</sup> and Hillgrund's Tomb, a Nordic family crypt in [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRThroat-24)</sup>

The Throat of the World is surrounded by several mountains, but mostly separated by valleys, like the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) to the south, split by the [Lion's Den](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lion%27s_Den) valley, the [Brittleshin Hills](Lore_Brittleshin_Hills.md) and [Shearpoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shearpoint) to the west and north respectively, divided by the White River valley, and [Ysgramor's Teeth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ysgramor%27s_Teeth) by the waterfalls flowing from [Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md) to the [Darkwater River](Lore_Darkwater_River.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRThroat-24)</sup> Somewhere around the mountain is the long never-before-seen valley of waterfalls called Vakkervanir, initially named by [Waughin Jarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waughin_Jarth). Mount Einar is another neighboring mountain.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LRPFTA-6)</sup>

Underneath the mountain are several cave systems, such as the White River Watch, a common bandit holdout near [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md), Honeystrand Cave near the Rift's southwest crossroad,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SRHoneystrand-26)</sup> and [Pinepeak Cavern](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pinepeak_Cavern), a safe place for locals in Ivarstead and the tomb of Hakra, one of the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) and a protector-spirit of the Rift.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONPinepeak-27)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONNetapatuu-28)</sup>

The [Snow Throat Fruit Bat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bat#Snow_Throat_Fruit_Bat) is a pure-white native species that blends easily in the winter weather.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONBat-29)</sup> All kinds of magical and rare phenomena occur on the mountain. On the mountain's peak is a [Time Wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md), an anomaly where [time](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Time) is broken. It was there, the ancient Nordic tongues defeated Alduin and sent him forward in time.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-1)</sup> The snow on the mountain is magically infused and was used to create the legendary bottle, the [White Phial](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:White_Phial).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SRNurelion-30)</sup> Frostheart Blossoms are rare, blue flowers that only grow in Skyrim's snow-blown country and mountains like the Throat of the World.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONMizrali-31)</sup> The path up the mountain is home to dangerous creatures such as [ice wolves](Lore_Ice_Wolf.md) and [frost trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Troll).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PGE3-32)</sup> [Ice Wraiths](Lore_Ice_Wraith.md), a [nature spirit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) made out of pure ice, live on the mountain,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONWraith-33)</sup> especially higher towards the peak.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-SRWraith-34)</sup>

## History

### Ancient History
*"Hold, Alduin on the Wing! Sister Hawk, grant us your sacred breath to make this contract heard! Begone, World-Eater! By words with older bones than your own we break your perch on this age and send you out! You are banished! Alduin, we shout you out from all our endings unto the last!"*
—Felldir's chant to banish Alduin [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/SR-npc-Jurgen_Windcaller.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Jurgen_Windcaller.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Jurgen_Windcaller.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Jurgen_Windcaller.jpg) Jurgen Windcaller, also called Jurgen the Calm Snow-Throat's origin lies in the myths surrounding the Towers. From the speculative thoughts of Lady [Cinnabar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cinnabar_of_Taneth) and other sources, when the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) created the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), the physical flesh of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) hung on a skeleton of joints, with each joint radiating a palpable reality. The first point became the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) and its founding stone was the Zero Stone. With its creation, the [Aldmeri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aldmeri) of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) saw to emulate it and create their own towers.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SCAS-35)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-NMI4-36)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-NMI8-37)</sup>

However, the creation of multiple towers suggests that the Aldmer did not cooperate together.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SCAS-35)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-NMI4-36)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-NMI8-37)</sup> Some towers are artifical constructs, but others like Snow Throat are natural.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRThroat-24)</sup> Using these towers, the elves shaped the land and conformed it to their desires.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SCAS-35)</sup> Each of the towers are associated with one of the modern elven races, like the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and the [Crystal-Like-Law](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crystal-Like-Law), or the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and the Red-Heart,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-AE4TET-38)</sup> but Snow Throat has no confirmed association. The ancient [Falmer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Elves), however, lived in Skyrim before Ysgramor arrived in Tamriel.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-8)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-SRGelebor-39)</sup>

Before the Greybeards lived on the Throat of the World, its summit had been visited since the days of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md). A [word wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) buried in snow is the only structure at the top of the mountain.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SRFire-40)</sup> Walls like this were created by the ancient Nords in the Dragon language.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-DLMNM-41)</sup> The [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) of the late Merethic Era, when the ancient Nords rebelled against their dragon overlords, ended with Alduin's defeat on the Throat of the World.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TOTDC-42)</sup> The tongue warrior, [Felldir the Old](Lore_Felldir_the_Old.md) devised a plan to bring Alduin to the mountain by issuing a challenge and sending him forward in time with an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SRBane-43)</sup> With his defeat, a time-wound was left in its place. Alduin's brother and lieutenant, Paarthurnax, decided to roost on the battlefield, waiting for the day of his return.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-1)</sup>

After the [Conquest of Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conquest_of_Morrowind) in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md),<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_End)</sup> one of the most powerful [tongues](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tongues) in the [Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md) began contemplating on the thu'um and how it failed them in [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md). Jurgen began his seven-year meditation and sat silently, eventually developing his new philosophy, the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md). Part of this creed was denouncing the thu'um's use in war. Seventeen tongues, who disputed his way of thinking, challenged him and failed. With Jurgen as their leader, they built High Hrothgar high on the mountain and sat in silence for many years.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-8)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRTablets-17)</sup> Paarthurnax also began to study the Way of the Voice and used his mastery to overcome his innate dominate nature and teach future generations of Greybeards.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRArngeir-14)</sup>

The seven thousand steps were built from the base next to Ivarstead to High Hrothgar. A local in town will deliver food to High Hrothgar and regularly walk the steps.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SRKlimmek-44)</sup> One of High King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md)'s many deeds to the Old Gods was repairing the 418th step toward High Hrothgar after it was damaged by a dragon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

### The Interregnum and beyond
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ad/SR-concept-Shrine_Of_Talos.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Shrine_Of_Talos.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Shrine_Of_Talos.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Shrine_Of_Talos.jpg) A Shrine of Talos [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c7/LG-cardart-Duel_Atop_the_World_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Duel_Atop_the_World_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Duel_Atop_the_World_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Duel_Atop_the_World_02.jpg) Alduin and Paarthurnax's duel atop the world As the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) occurred in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), the newly-named King of [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md), [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) stealthily left [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) traveled up the Seven Thousand Steps to speak with the Greybeards in High Hrothgar.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-EGT-16)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-JTSK-45)</sup> Scholars such as [Yngmaer Raven-Quill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngmaer_Raven-Quill) of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) were suspicious of these claims and sought funding from High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md) to investigate.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-TSAI-46)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an avalanche blocked the steps and prevented anyone from moving between High Hrothgar and Ivarstead.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONMizrali-31)</sup>

As it goes in legend, before he was named [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) and created the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), Tiber Septim climbed the steps and entered High Hrothgar. He came answering the Greybeards' summons, the first time they spoke in years since Jurgen the Calm's time.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRTablets-17)</sup> According to *The Song of Tiber Septim*, this was after the [Battle of Old Hroldan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Old_Hroldan), the event that revealed his ability to use the thu'um, and shortly before he left for [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) with King [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TAH-47)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-PGE1C-48)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Greybeards summoned the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) with their voice after they absorbed their first [dragon soul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon_Souls). The warrior traveled to Ivarstead and climbed the steps to reach High Hrothgar.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-SRWay-49)</sup> In time, the hero learned about the Dragonrend shout and further scaled the mountain to its pinnacle where they met Paarthurnax.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-SRWorld-50)</sup>

Paarthurnax assisted the Dragonborn on their quest to fulfill the [prophecy](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md) and with his guidance, the hero used an Elder Scroll on the Time-Wound to see into the past and learn the Dragonrend shout. Only moments after learning the shout, Alduin confronted the Dragonborn and Paarthurnax on the summit. Alduin, on the verge of his defeat, fled to [Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md) to swallow more [souls](Lore_Souls.md) and regain his power.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SRBane-43)</sup>

The cave of Monahven was used as a sanctuary by Oviron Salith and the Chosen of Boethiah, where they opened a portal to [Attribution's Share](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Attribution%27s_Share) -- the realm of Prince [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Adventure Game-3)</sup> The cave had numerous entrances throughout the immediate region surrounding Monahven.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Adventure Game-3)</sup>
