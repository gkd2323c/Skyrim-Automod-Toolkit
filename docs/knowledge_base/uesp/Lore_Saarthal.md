[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/SR-place-Saarthal.jpg/200px-SR-place-Saarthal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Saarthal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Saarthal.jpg) The ruins of Saarthal Saarthal was the first city built in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) after the arrival of [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) sometime in the middle of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-2)</sup> Saarthal was also the first capital of Nord civilization in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), as well as the largest of the ancient Nordic cities.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Tolf Dial-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-No T-4)</sup> The city was destroyed late in the Merethic Era by the Snow Elves, and then rebuilt by [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) after he retook the city.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-No T-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> It was destroyed and finally abandoned sometime in the late Merethic or early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DVN-6)</sup> In the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the ruins of the city were located by archaeologists near the modern city of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> After [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) and his followers were corrupted into [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) and the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), they fled to the northern wastes near Saarthal.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-interior-Saarthal.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Saarthal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Saarthal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Saarthal.jpg) The Saarthal excavation, circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) During the city's construction, the Nords unearthed the [Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md), which they attempted to keep secret by burying it under the city. The [Snow Elves](Lore_Falmer.md) learned of the powerful artifact, however, and wanted it for themselves. They attacked the city in order to secure this power, an event known as the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-No T-4)</sup> One of the participants was [Serenarth](Lore_Serenarth_(Snow_Elf).md), a Snow Elf wizard who made a bargain with malevolent forces after being shot by Ysgramor, and has been encased in ice ever since with the threat of thawing out and exacting vengeance.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-8)</sup> Everyone except for Ysgramor and his two sons, [Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol) and [Ylgar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ylgar), were killed and the city was burned to the ground.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Sot Rv2-9)</sup> Ysgramor fled back to Atmora to gather an army with which to fight the Elves.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Sot Rv5-10)</sup> Before the Snow Elves could find their prize, Ysgramor and his Five Hundred Companions returned, strengthened by powerful enchanted weapons and armor created by [Ahzidal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ahzidal),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> drove the Elves back from Saarthal, and began a campaign to drive them from Skyrim entirely.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-2)</sup> Reports from Dominion scouts suggest the ruins were still visible during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONVole-12)</sup> Although the city was lost, the Eye remained buried under Saarthal until the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) unearthed it in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The local Nords, however, did not approve of the college mages digging among the dead of the ruined city.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

Jyrik Gauldurson, one of the three sons of [Archmage Gauldur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gauldur), fled to Saarthal after his treachery in seizing the Archmage's powerful amulet. He fought and killed ten of [Archmage Geirmund](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Geirmund)'s battlemages before finally succumbing, and was sealed within the ruins.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DVN-6)</sup>

One of Ysgramor's Five Hundred Companions, the legendary warrior Reynir, claimed to have survived the Night of Tears and sailed back to Atmora with Ylgar.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Rt D-15)</sup>

## Notes
- The fall of Saarthal is thought to be responsible for the creation of [Sheor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheor), a demonized version of the Nordic god [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
- Saarthal was known for its [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Saarthal-17)</sup>
- Unofficial texts authored by [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) refer to Saarthal as *Sarthaal*.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-7F-18)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-500MC-19)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TTRS-20)</sup>
