Tarnian Lovidicus was the chief councilor of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During his nearly fifty-year tenure as chief councilor, Lovidicus served under Emperors [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md), [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), and Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDRt EC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mot EC-3)</sup> After the collapse of the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) and the dissolution of the Elder Council, Lovidicus fled to the city of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), and he was selected by Countess [Nevenia Caro](Lore_Nevenia_Caro.md) to serve on the city's governing body, the Chamber of Legates, eventually becoming its de facto leader.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AIw CC-4)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Dagonist cult known as the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md) targeted Lovidicus and other former members of the Elder Council to discover the locations of the Daedric weapons known as the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ADS-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CTLd-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Pt S-7)</sup> Lovidicus played a part in investigating the assassinations of his old colleagues and revealing former councilor [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md) as the high priest of the Waking Flame.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ADS-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AUA-8)</sup>

## Biography

### Chief Councilor of the Elder Council
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg/200px-LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg) Lovidicus (second from the left) with other members of the Elder Council Little is known about Tarnian Lovidicus' life prior to his appointment to the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md). Prior to his appointment to the Elder Council, he was presumably a member of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md)'s Chamber of Legates.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDRt EC-1)</sup> On 1 Sun's Dawn, [2E 534](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_534), Emperor [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) appointed Lovidicus to the Elder Council as the representative of the [Trans-Niben](Lore_Trans-Niben.md) and named him chief councilor because of the respect and honor that he was held in throughout [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDRt EC-1)</sup> As chief councilor, his power was second only to High Chancellor [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup>

After Durcorach was killed campaigning in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), his son and successor, [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), debated with Lovidicus and Tharn about how to bring the province under control in the aftermath, and he listened to them present the risks and benefits of different options.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Awt LE-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup> After considering the options, Moricar commanded Tharn to negotiate peace with High Rock and ordered an aide to the Elder Council to prepare a decree recognizing the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), an alliance of [High Rock's kingdoms](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md) formed in response to Durcorach's invasion.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Awt LE-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-11)</sup> In [2E 556](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_556) or [557](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_557), a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) woman named [Celdina](Lore_Celdina.md) arrived at the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), and Moricar rewarded her with a minor court appointment, although Lovidicus was unsure what her purpose was in the Imperial Court. Eventually, Lovidicus hired investigators to see what they could learn about Celdina and how she might be advising Moricar. The investigators uncovered some information about Celdina's background but little else. After Celdina departed from the Imperial Court around [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), he never saw her again.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MTCSC-12)</sup>

In [2E 564](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_564), Moricar died, and his son [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) succeeded him as emperor, and he retained Lovidicus as chief councilor.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TJOEL-13)</sup> After [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) overthrew Leovic and declared himself emperor in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Lovidicus continued to serve as chief councilor during his reign and [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md)'s regency.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup> Lovidicus attended the Elder Council meeting that was held near the beginning of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) on the 16th of Second Seed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mot EC-3)</sup> At the council meeting, he noted that discretionary spending under Clivia Tharn had increased seven-fold over the annual expenditures of Varen's reign, and he made a motion to have General Nipia send an armed escort to force High Chancellor Tharn to attend the next council meeting; however, the motion was voted down.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mot EC-3)</sup> After the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) collapsed due to the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s Daedric forces invaded the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Elder Council dissolved, and Lovidicus fled to Leyawiin.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CATAYQ2-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-15)</sup>

### Chamber of Legates
After fleeing to Leyawiin, Countess [Nevenia Caro](Lore_Nevenia_Caro.md) selected Lovidicus to serve as a member of the city's governing body, the Chamber of the Legates.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AIw CC-4)</sup> In the Chamber of Legates, he served with Tebeza-ko, a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), and Am-Halu, an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md), each representing one of Leyawiin's three primary races.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AIw CC-4)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-15)</sup> Although all three members of the Chamber of Legates had equal standing, Lovidicus eventually became its de facto leader.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOLTkd-16)</sup>

During his tenure as a member of the Chamber of Legates, Lovidicus was determined to address the piracy problem of [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) and the [Lower Niben](Lore_Lower_Niben.md), but Leyawiin had neither the funds nor warships to deal with them.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Po TB-17)</sup> The collapse of the Empire left Leyawiin as an independent city-state, so Lovidicus proposed the creation of an army to defend Leyawiin and the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) region against the Three Banners War by recruiting unpaid imperial legionaries and native Blackwooders.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-2)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot IB-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CM101IBS-19)</sup> This proposal eventually became the [Ivory Brigade](Lore_Ivory_Brigade.md), led by Captain Rian Liore, formerly of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot IB-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CM101IBS-19)</sup>

### Four Ambitions
In 2E 582, Lovidicus' former Elder Council colleague [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md), who was secretly the High Priest of the Dagonic [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md), ordered his cultists to discover the locations of the Daedric weapons known as the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Pt S-7)</sup> To locate and preserve the secret of the Four Ambitions, Vandacia ordered his Waking Flame cultists to kill anybody with knowledge of them—that being Leovic's former steward, Farrul Lupus, and former Elder Councilors Abor, Faleria, [Itinia](Lore_Itinia.md), Jirich, and Velan Sophus—and then retrieve their documents regarding the Ambitions.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Pt S-7)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DZJ-20)</sup> Lovidicus knew nothing about the Four Ambitions, but Vandacia felt that the Waking Flame might have to kill him as well.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CTLd-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Pt S-7)</sup> Vandacia instructed the cultists to frame the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) for the murders to deflect suspicion from the Order of the Waking Flame for as long as possible.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Pt S-7)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HPO-21)</sup>

After the Waking Flame killed Farrul Lupus, [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md) arrived in Leyawiin and informed Lovidicus of Lupus' death and that he, Abor, Faleria, Itinia, Jirich, Sophus, and Vandacia were in danger because of their knowledge of the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-15)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOESAd-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Lt CL-23)</sup> Afterward, Lovidicus and Captain Liore offered Ivory Brigade protection to Abor, Faleria, Jirich, and Sophus; however, only Faleria accepted, and Abor fled to [Fort Blueblood](Lore_Fort_Blueblood.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CTLd-6)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-15)</sup> Additionally, he sent messages to Itinia and Vandacia and explained the situation to the other members of the Chamber of Legates.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CTLd-6)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOLTkd-16)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOLAHd-24)</sup>

Later, [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), who was working with Eveli, informed Lovidicus that Jirich and Sophus had been killed and gave him their encoded documents related to the Four Ambitions. Lovidicus then asked the Vestige and Eveli to convince Abor to return to Leyawiin, where she would be better protected.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ADS-5)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg) Vandacia strangling Lovidicus with magic By the time they reached Fort Blueblood, Abor had been killed, but the Vestige managed to get Faleria and Vandacia to [Castle Leyawiin](Lore_Castle_Leyawiin.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ADS-5)</sup> At Castle Leyawiin, Lovidicus asked the Vestige and Eveli to go to [Bloodrun Cave](Lore_Bloodrun_Cave.md) and deal with the Brotherhood while they waited for for the former imperial scribe Beragon to arrive to decode the documents.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AUA-8)</sup> After the Vestige and Eveli departed, Beragon arrived and began decoding the documents, but Waking Flame cultists then opened portals in Castle Leyawiin and initiated an attack.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CTLd-6)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-15)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOBd-25)</sup> In the chaos, Vandacia took the encoded documents from Beragon.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CTLd-6)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-15)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOBd-25)</sup> Vandacia then killed Faleria and attempted to strangle Lovidicus with magic in the castle courtyard, but when the Vestige and Eveli arrived, the high priest summoned the Disciple of Aspiration and teleported away.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AUA-8)</sup>

After the Vestige defeated the Disciple of Aspiration, Lovidicus sent them to [Keshu the Black Fin](Lore_Keshu_the_Black_Fin.md), the governor of [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md), to discover the exact location of the Ojel-Bak, where it was believed that the Ambitions were hidden.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AUA-8)</sup> Ultimately, the Vestige and Eveli managed to find the Ambitions, and they, with the assistance of the Ivory Brigade and the Black Fin Legion, defeated Vandacia and the Waking Flame in a battle at [Fort Redmane](Lore_Fort_Redmane.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-AHV-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-AME-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Po A-28)</sup> After the Waking Flame's defeat, Lovidicus attended the victory celebration in Gideon.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Ho B-29)</sup>
