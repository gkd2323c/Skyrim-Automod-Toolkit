# Skull Crusher

The Skull Crusher (also spelled **Skull-Crusher**) is an amazingly large and powerful warhammer created together by the wizard, [Dorach Gusal](Lore_Dorach_Gusal.md) and the great [weaponsmith](Lore_Smithing.md), [Hilbongard Rolamus](Lore_Hilbongard_Rolamus.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-1)</sup> in the forge of [Anudnabia](Lore_Anudnabia.md) sometime in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HW-2)</sup> during the [Skyrim Captivity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skyrim_Captivity).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-1)</sup> The creation of this [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) is riddled in magic, such as the fires it was forged in and the [steel](Lore_Steel.md) that was tempered for it. It is incredibly light but hard, which provides for its deadly blows compared to those of a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The artifact was intended to be used as an attraction for a local festival, but it was stolen beforehand. However, a curse was unleashed so Skull Crusher would betray the unlawful,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-1)</sup> and so that it would search [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for its creators.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TL-4)</sup>

## History
It is uncertain when either Dorach or Hilbongard died, but they were both in attendance at the wake of [Mordrin Hanin](Lore_Mordrin_Hanin.md), which took place several years later in the First Era. Here many other attendees died from Hanin's deadly drink. But it is unknown if they were among them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HW-2)</sup> Regardless of their fate, the artifact returned to the place of its creation.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWSkull-5)</sup>

[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWYB-6)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-7)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing Skull Crusher among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TA-8)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Skull Crusher and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWFR-9)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), an [acolyte](Lore_Nerevarine.md) of the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) was sent to retrieve it. Since the lower levels of Anudnabia have long since been closed off, the acolyte traversed through the Omaren Ancestral Tomb and entered Anudnabia's forgotten vaults. They recovered the Skull Crusher from a chest above the forge of Hilbongard and returned it to the Imperial Cult in [Castle Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWSkull-5)</sup> Later that year, the warhammer was sold to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md), who put it on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRMuseum-10)</sup>

Sometime after worship of the Tribunal as gods was deemed as heretical by the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> one cult of Almalexia worshippers constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md). The Ashfall's Tear cult gathered and preserved many relics from Morrowind in the temple's armory, including Skull Crusher. Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) was alerted to the cult's existence when they attempted to recover several additional artifacts. It is unknown if the Dragonborn agreed to help the cult or if it was wiped out.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Got T-13)</sup> Regardless, Skull Crusher and other relics were retrieved from the armory, and this arsenal was used to defeat remnants of the [Sixth House](Lore_Sixth_House.md)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive-14)</sup> led by the Dunmer Erden Relvel, who became a priest of [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) after becoming corrupted by his mask.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
