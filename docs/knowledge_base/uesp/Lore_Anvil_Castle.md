[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg/200px-ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg) Anvil Castle circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Anvil Castle (also called **Anvil Keep** or **Castle Anvil**) is a large stone castle found across the bay facing the southern walls of the city of Anvil. When [King Bendu Olo](Lore_Bendu_Olo.md) led the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) against the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1W-1)</sup> he returned to the city and began converting what was then known as **Anvil Fortress** into Anvil Castle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-2)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Governor [Fortunata ap Dugal](Lore_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.md) captured the city and took over the castle as her residence.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

During the battle in Anvil Bay between Commodore [Fasil Umbranox](Lore_Fasil_Umbranox.md) and [Torradan ap Dugal](Lore_Torradan_ap_Dugal.md) circa [3E 271](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_271), the town of Anvil was set alight.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CDJ-4)</sup> Dugal tried to flee, but Umbranox's battlemages caused a rocky outcropping to collapse over his ship, the *Black Flag*. As a reward for destroying the pirates, Umbranox was given the title of Count of Anvil, as well as the funds to build a city over the ashes of Old Anvil. He built a new Castle Anvil on the same island the *Black Flag* was buried under.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-DL-5)</sup> Umbranox's [descendants](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md) would rule for centuries afterwards; the throne was still held by an Umbranox circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Oblivion-6)</sup> The crew of the buried *Black Flag* survived the collapse, but remained trapped underground in a cavern they named "Dunbarrow Cove", after Dugal's birthplace of [Dunbarrow](Lore_Dunbarrow.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CDJ-4)</sup> The cavern was still haunted by the undead skeletons of its crew when the cavern was uncovered circa 3E 433; their souls were duly put to rest, and the cavern converted into a thieves den.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

## Notes
- It is difficult to reconcile the existence of Anvil Castle and the island upon which it sits during [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)'s time period with the origin story presented with the [Thieves Den](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Thieves_Den) DLC for *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*. This appears to be a retcon, given the existence of [Anvil Castle Caves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Anvil_Castle#Anvil_Castle_Caves) in ESO.
- A ruin known as [Anvil Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Anvil_Keep) appears as a job location in *[Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Blades)*, but it is unknown if this is intended to be Anvil Castle or a separate keep.
