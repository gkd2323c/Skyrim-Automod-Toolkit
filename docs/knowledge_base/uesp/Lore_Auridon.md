— *[Ode to Auridon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ode_to_Auridon)* Auridon, also known as **Moridunon**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOA-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> or **Kaelann**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-JLDND-2)</sup> is the second largest island in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), located off the far eastern coast of Summerset,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTM-3)</sup> and north of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOA-1)</sup> between the main island and the continent of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The main cities on the island are [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) to the north, [Skywatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skywatch) to the east, and [Vulkhel Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vulkhel_Guard) to the south.

## Geography
Auridon is one of fourteen islands of varying sizes that form the Summerset Isles, and is the eastern bulwark of the Summerset archipelago. The island was well defended throughout history not only by its veteran Elven warriors but also due to its unique position and natural geography. Walls of vast, craggy cliffs dotted its northern coast, and its strategic location protected mainland Summerset from numerous invasions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset Isles-4)</sup> Auridon loans its name to the body of water that separates it from the main island, the [Auridon Strait](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auridon_Strait).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTVQ-5)</sup> The [Diceto River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Diceto_River) flows through the city of Firsthold and empties into the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) on the island's northern coast.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WAP-6)</sup>

The jagged promontories of the north softened and rose even further into white granite mountains in the island's middle. Vast deciduous forests of laurel, maple, and larch trees granted the land a lush yet unkempt appearance. Auridon also contained towering escarpments, emerald woodland, and pale off-white sand dunes. While the High Elves sought to control and tame the wilds (a stark and divisive difference in ideology from their [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) counterparts), such efforts only extended as far as their nearest surroundings, with much of Auridon remaining a wild, untouched forest that shrouded ruins from another era. While the unspoilt wilderness was considered beautiful to those who resided here, it was not without danger. Wild boars and bears hunted in the wilderness, along with gemlike arachnids and other unmentionable entities left to forage for centuries by Altmeri mages.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset Isles-4)</sup>

## Fauna
Fauna varies in Auridon, with common [wolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolf) and [bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear) being found, however stone monoliths in the woods surrounding [Shattered Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shattered_Grove), make these normally hostile species peaceful towards villagers and travelers.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Large [spiders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider) have also been known to inhabit abandoned mines in the area.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup> [Giant bats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Giant_Bat) live in some of the caves in Auridon. [Deer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deer) dot the forests of Auridon alongside [monkeys](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Monkey) and [foxes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fox). [Imps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imp) have been known to inhabit [Errinorne Isle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Errinorne_Isle) and [Calambar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calambar).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Crocodiles have been sighted in Calambar as well but their numbers are very low.

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/ON-concept-Auridon_02.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Auridon_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Auridon_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Auridon_02.jpg) A map of Auridon *Auridon: excellent abode.
[Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) repel: expel all [Sload](Lore_Sload.md).*
— *[The Lay of Firsthold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Lay_of_Firsthold)* According to ancient myth, Auridon was settled by [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) (ancient ancestors of the modern-day [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md)) hailing from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) in the Early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). These [Ancients](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancients) reputedly made landfall at the northernmost tip of Auridon, later known as Nine Prows Landing, and would eventually expand and colonize the rest of the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Aur Exp-10)</sup> One celebrated early explorer was Torinaan, who claimed all of Auridon as a new Kinhold, fighting with his people against the native [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) and other inhabitants of the Isle.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Lo F-11)</sup> Over thousands of years, these immigrants became the Altmer, creating a robust civilization and exterminating those species they could not subjugate. The native many-eyed forest giants known as the Ilyadi, the Gheatus, and the Welwa of Auridon were all killed off in subsequent skirmishes with the Aldmer.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset Isles-4)</sup>

Through conquest and struggle, the island ultimately fell into the hands of the Elves.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Lo F-11)</sup> By the time the first stones of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) were laid, the early Elves farmed the land, had limited hierarchical political aspirations, and worshipped their ancestors which they believed were directly descended from the Divines. Despite sharing a common lineage, cracks would form in their pre-existing societal structure. The High Nobles carved out and ruled territories, the Wise (middle nobility) consisted of learned persons such as teachers and priests who'd studied the ancient lore and applied their knowledge to society. Following the Wise were the artisans— artists, farm holders, merchants, and soldiers. Lastly came the largest class, the workers, comprising peasants, laborers, foreigners, and Goblin slaves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset Isles-4)</sup>

After this, the rest of Tamriel was settled in the second Aldmeri exodus, and the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), and [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) staked claim to different regions. By the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), animosity between the city-states of the Isles was certainly evident, as internal conflicts between the cities of [Skywatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skywatch) and [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) (the oldest city on Auridon) often sparked into full-scale war during the First and [Second Eras](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-12)</sup> Foreign intruders also spilled High Elven blood. For millennia, the [sloads](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md), notorious for their necromantic mastery, terrorized the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), conjuring sea monsters along the coasts and laying siege to Skywatch.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-13)</sup> The horrors they have visited upon the land are still remembered today: the Sack of Skywatch in [1E 1301](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1301) and the War of the Uvichil from [1E 2911](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2911) to [1E 2917](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2917) are surely among the most terrible events in Tamriel's history.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-12)</sup> Other long-held enemies included the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset Isles-4)</sup>

In [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230), the King of Firsthold, Rilis XII, met with [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) in the "Charter Conclave" before approving the establishment of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OOTMG-14)</sup>

By [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Auridon would be part of the First Aldmeri Dominion led by [Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn), who was determined to see Elven rule over the entirety of Tamriel. However, long-held prejudices against the Dominion's allies, the [Wood Elves](Lore_Bosmer.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), coupled with aggressive recruitment practices against the lower classes resulted in a popular resistance movement, the [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance) taking up arms on Auridon.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESO-15)</sup>

Among the many organizations on Auridon, the [Trebbite Monks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trebbite_Monks) are known for the good they do for the peasants, as they value tradition above every other virtue. Their credo has ever been for pure Altmer bloodlines on Summerset, and among the royal families most of all. During the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) the Trebbite Monks worked with Lady Gialene, daughter of the King of Skywatch, to start a revolution against the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) Queen of Firsthold, [Morgiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morgiah). Despite the growing resentment in the countryside from peasants who called Morgiah the "Black Queen", they stopped short of insurrection, and the revolutionary army was primarily composed of the Monks themselves and a horde of mercenaries. The revolt ended in a panicked, unorganized retreat, and after Lady Gialene was tortured and confessed her treachery, she was sent back with an escort to her father's court of Skywatch.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TFR-16)</sup>

Auridon was part of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the only Empire in history to conquer the entirety of Tamriel.

By [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), Auridon had become part of the Third Aldmeri Dominion, and the Summerset Isles were renamed Alinor. Those kings, queens, and countrymen that did not assent to the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md)'s rise to power would be rooted out, exiled, and killed.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RT-17)</sup>
