[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg/200px-MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg) Map of Tamriel (*Morrowind*) Bodrum (sometimes called **Bodrum-on- [Prytasus](Lore_Pryai_River.md)**<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> or **Bodrom Mora**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPMorrowind Moonlight-1)</sup> is a hamlet in westernmost [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), located atop a steep bluff just above the [Pryai River](Lore_Pryai_River.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup> Bodrum is located south of [Varanis](Lore_Varanis.md) and [Omaynis](Lore_Omaynis.md), and slightly northeast of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) across the [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-3)</sup> An adjacent subregion to the hamlet called the **Bodrums** is implied to exist.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup>

## History

### First Era
During the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), Morrowind marched their armies to Bodrum and steaded there for an evening. The next morning, the Morrowind commanders had dammed the Pryai River, flooding the plains beyond and forcing their [Cyrodilic enemies](Lore_Second_Empire.md) to trudge through mud into battle, before the Morrowind forces released the dam, and flooded their enemies out completely.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup>

### Third Era
By [3E 430](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_430), Bodrum was a fiefdom under the umbrella of [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md). And served as the [kinhouse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kinhouse) of Lady Dres Vendama, who later married King [Hlaalu Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPMorrowind Moonlight-1)</sup>
