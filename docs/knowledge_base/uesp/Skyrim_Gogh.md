[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/SR-creature-Gogh.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Gogh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Gogh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Gogh.jpg) Gogh [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/SR-creature-Gogh_%28combat%29.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Gogh_%28combat%29.jpg)](File_SR-creature-Gogh_(combat).jpg.md) [](File_SR-creature-Gogh_(combat).jpg.md) Gogh in combat alongside his storm atronach summon **Gogh** is a [goblin](Skyrim_Goblin.md) hunter from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and an apparent champion of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) who has been sentenced to death for discovering the truth about his tribe's new leader, the supposed [Blue God](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Blue_God), posing as an aspect of [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) through the use of blue dye. He can be found inside [Gromm's Pass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gromm%27s_Pass) where he is battling for his life in a fighting pit. If the Blue God is slain and Gogh is freed from the pit, Gogh can be recruited as a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md).

His appearance can be altered through dialogue, and he wields the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Skyrim_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md), a Daedric artifact which summons a [storm atronach](Skyrim_Storm_Atronach.md). The spear cannot be taken from him, and is only usable if equipped via [console commands](Skyrim_Console.md). The armor options available are: no armor; leather or iron armor; and/or no helmet, a helmet of leather or iron, or a skull mask. These options can vary Gogh's damage resist statistic from 14 with no armor to 35 with iron armor and the skull mask. The latter option represents only a 3% reduction in actual damage suffered; this is not significant given that he is essential.

Gogh cannot be ordered to stay at one of your [houses](Skyrim_Houses.md). Instead, he will return to the throne area within Gromm's Pass when he leaves your service as a follower.

## Dialogue
You can ask Gogh various questions, but will be unable to understand him as he will only respond with snorting and grunts.

When you first meet him:

**Are you all right?** **Did you kill all these goblins?** **I take it you realized that orc wasn't a god.** **Wait, why am I talking to a goblin?** **Do you think you'd want to follow me?** **You can handle yourself in a fight. I could use someone like you.** **You seem friendly, but so does a bear until it's chewing your face off.** **I've always wanted a goblin for a friend. What do you say?** **All right then, let's go.** After recruiting him:

**I want to talk about your spear.** **Where did you find it?** **Can I try it out?** **Does it have any special abilities?** **Does your spear have a name?** **I don't understand.** **This is a waste of time.** **I'm not sure why I expected an actual answer.** **That's interesting. Well, good talk.** **I wonder if they teach goblin at the Bard's College.** If you asked if you could try out the spear, you can respond with:

**I'm guessing that's a "No."** Asking to trade things will prompt him to give you a Skull or Troll Skull, but you cannot give him anything. How you choose to respond to the gift is up to you.

## Notes
- Gogh functions similarly to the [Riekling Warrior](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Riekling_Warrior) follower. Like Rieklings, [dogs](Skyrim_Dog.md), and Steadfast Dwarven automatons, he takes up the pet slot as a helper in combat.
- He is the only goblin that uses a weapon.
- If Gogh is brought home to your family, your children will sometimes ask if he's your pet and comment on how scary he is.
