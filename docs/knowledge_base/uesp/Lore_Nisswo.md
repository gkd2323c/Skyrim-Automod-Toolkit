# Nisswo

*No comfort will they offer you
No gentle truths to placate fear
But listen closely to their words
Look upon their nothing eyes.*
— *[Speakers of Nothing](Lore_Speakers_of_Nothing.md)* A Nisswo (literally "**Nothing-Speaker**" in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md)) is a priest of a caste dedicated to [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md). They are native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and their ranks consist primarily of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).

Nisswos have served two very different roles in Argonian society over the years, shifting from a ruling class in the pre- [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) era of Black Marsh to a priest class.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup> In the modern day, Nisswo priests are collectively known as the 'Clutch of Nisswo', and share the goal of spreading the many truths of Sithis, no matter how contradictory they might be.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Uaxal-2)</sup>

## The Nisswo Kings
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Ghost_from_the_Past_04.jpg) A statue of Sithis inside the Xith-Izkul Xanmeer Prior to [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md#Days_of_Stone_Nests), Nisswos were the ruling class of Black Marsh. In modern times, Nisswos of this era are sometimes referred to as "Nisswo-Kings". The Nisswo priest-kings would make sacrifices in Sithis's name at the city of Xul-Thuxis in the [Deepmire](Lore_Deepmire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Xukas-3)</sup>

The old Nisswos worshipped Sithis solely as an entity that brings destruction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup> They believed Sithis is always watching, waiting to destroy the world and return existence to the Void. The priests appeased Sithis in the hopes they would be spared from destruction. Their most infamous gifts to the Void were live blood sacrifices.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup> Ceremonial altars and [staves](Lore_Staves.md) were created for that specific purpose.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Soul Tender-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Motif-5)</sup> There were also more consistent physical depictions of Sithis in pre-Duskfall times. Statues of Sithis were carved in important temples,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> and symbols of Sithis were woven into armor and clothing.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Motif-5)</sup> The largest temple to Sithis in Murkmire is the Teeth of Sithis, where many sacrifices took place. It continues to be cared for by the Nisswos, as it is still sacred, despite not serving its original purpose.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup>

These beliefs were characterized by later generations as "shunatei", a state of dwelling on the past.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Vozei-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Talen-8)</sup> Modern Argonians believe that many of the old practices derived from this mindset of fearing Sithis, and have sought to distance themselves from it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Xukas-3)</sup> For example, ancient Argonians built structures out of permanent materials like stone,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Xukas-3)</sup> most notably the monumental [xanmeers](Lore_Xanmeer.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup> Modern Argonians contrast themselves from their ancestors by rejecting this shunatei and building structures that are specifically designed not to last.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Xukas-3)</sup>

Though Duskfall has long since come to pass, some Nisswo-Kings have managed to persist though other means, such as [vampirism](Lore_Vampire.md). One example is the High Priestess of the Teeth, [Xulneihavu Shuxaltsei](Lore_Shuxaltsei.md), who in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) was revived after millennia of dormancy by the Bloodshed caused by the Blackguards. Though she briefly retook control of the Teeth of Sithis from the Clutch of Nisswo, she was expelled again with the help of the Soulless One.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

## The Clutch of Nisswo
Eventually, the old priesthood was succeeded by a loose coalition of Sithis preachers known collectively as the Clutch of Nisswo. The Clutch of Nisswo recognizes that Sithis is a creator as well as a destroyer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup> New members are inducted to the Clutch by apprenticing under a full-fledged Nisswo, during which time they are known as voh-vastei.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Uaxal-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Para-10)</sup>

### Core Beliefs
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change_02.jpg) A shrine to Raj-Sithis in Bright-Throat Village The Clutch of Nisswo worships Sithis not just as a force of destruction, but a catalyst of creation and change.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Art-11)</sup> This version of Sithis is referred to as "Raj-Sithis," and the Clutch of Nisswo name him both First Creator and Final Destroyer,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> contrasting it with Sithis the Destroyer of old.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xode-1)</sup> They believe that the version of Sithis the Dark Brotherhood and the pre-Duskfall Argonians worship either no longer exists, or never existed to begin with.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Lies-13)</sup>

The Clutch of Nisswo held that their ancestor's fear of Sithis was misplaced and that their attempts to resist change were not only futile, but may have accelerated their decline.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Uaxal-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Void-14)</sup> Argonian culture as a whole had shifted to accepting and overcoming shunatei as opposed to fighting against the current and living in the past. For example, buildings are constructed out of materials that can be easily replaced as opposed to stone. The Argonians recognize that their structures will be destroyed in time, no matter what they do to try to stop it. These structures will need to be rebuilt, hence the use of easily replaceable materials.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Xukas-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Talen-8)</sup>

The Clutch of Nisswo sees it as their duty to educate the public about the cycle of change and how to let go of loss. Members are traveling priests, each with their own method of teaching the masses. One such technique is to make works of art that are then intentionally destroyed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Uaxal-2)</sup> Another is to steal things from someone but leave clues so that the items might be recovered. <sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ro C-15)</sup> Other, more traditional techniques such as sermons, writing, and meditation are also employed.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Kahei-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ajul-17)</sup>

### Practices
The Clutch of Nisswo is a loose fellowship with no central governance and no consistent interpretation of Sithis. Rather, the Clutch accepts these contradictions as a representation of the many paths of Sithis. One of the reasons Nisswos travel so often, in fact, is to ensure each village has the opportunity to hear the many different interpretations of Sithis and gain a better understanding of its entire truth.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Uaxal-2)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Speakers-18)</sup> The Nisswos look down on dogmatic organizations like the Dark Brotherhood for espousing a 'single untruth' which humanizes Sithis. <sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Lies-13)</sup> For their part, the Dark Brotherhood tend to leave Nisswos alone, and have been known to deny writs should the target be protected by the Nisswo.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Ned-19)</sup>

Each Nisswo may differ in how they teach the word of Sithis. This includes what kind of offerings they make, and who should be responsible for making them. Some Nisswos leave personal offerings, often in the form of art or writing. Others may ask for voluntary sacrifices from their followers, up to and including blood sacrifice.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Uaxal-2)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/ON-quest-Art_of_the_Nisswo.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Art_of_the_Nisswo.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Art_of_the_Nisswo.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Art_of_the_Nisswo.jpg) Nisswo art, soon to be destroyed as an offering to Sithis Some nisswo carry large pieces of black onyx for use in various meditation practices.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

### Role in Society
In Argonian culture, Nisswos are widely recognized as ku-vastei, or bringers of necessary change.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Ku-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ku Song-22)</sup> The Clutch of Nisswo are welcome in almost all of the villages of Black Marsh, including notoriously insular tribes such as the [Naga-Kur](Lore_Naga-Kur.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> However, their preaching isn't always heeded; villages with strong traditions may ignore their teachings, and some individuals are skeptical of their beliefs.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Uaxal3-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

The Nisswos hold great respect for the [Shadowscales](Lore_Shadowscales.md), whom both they and Argonian culture at large see as fellow ku-vastei.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ku Song-22)</sup> Nisswos believe that the Shadowscales' training by the Dark Brotherhood is a necessary evil, but urge them to turn away from Brotherhood ideology when their training is complete.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

Outside of Black Marsh, Nisswos are viewed with suspicion. Even in academic institutions such as the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md), Nisswos are commonly associated with blood sacrifice and other pre-Duskfall rituals. Some scholars even falsely believed that Nisswos as a whole disappeared post-Duskfall.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Soul Tender-4)</sup>
