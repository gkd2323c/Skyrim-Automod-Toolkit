# Armor Artifacts

There are a number of artifact armor that can be found throughout the game of [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md).

The definition used for artifacts on this article is that the item must meet *all three* of the following requirements:

- Be unique in appearance.
- Only appear once in the game.
- Have a unique and useful enchantment.

None of these artifacts can be disenchanted to learn the enchantment. Some of them may be improved by [smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md). The material and perk relevant to each item is given below. Improving enchanted items requires the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk. However, many artifacts do not have an applicable perk that allows improvement past *flawless* quality without a Smithing skill of greater than 100.

Many of the artifacts are of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) origin. These items are formed from the essence of a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), providing the items with their power. Nineteen *Daedric Artifacts* are available in the game; most are rewards for the [Daedric Quests](Skyrim_Daedric_Quests.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/SR-item-Ebony_Mail.jpg/200px-SR-item-Ebony_Mail.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ebony_Mail.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ebony_Mail.jpg) Ebony Mail The [Ebony Mail](Skyrim_Ebony_Mail.md) is an artifact created by the Daedric Prince [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md). It is [heavy armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor_(item).md) and enchanted with a [muffle](Skyrim_Muffle_(effect).md) effect as well as a unique area effect that inflicts 5 points of [poison damage](Skyrim_Poison_Damage.md) per second on nearby opponents. It can be [improved](Skyrim_Workbench.md) with an [ebony ingot](Skyrim_Ebony_Ingot.md). It is awarded to you by Boethiah for completing the quest [Boethiah's Calling](Skyrim_Boethiah%27s_Calling.md).

In appearance, the Ebony Mail is not too different from ordinary [ebony armor](Skyrim_Ebony_Armor.md), aside from the absence of shoulder pauldrons, a black chainmail texture replacing the cloth areas, and minor color changes. The most unique feature of the Ebony Mail is its "poison cloak" enchantment, which activates when the wearer is sneaking or when an enemy is nearby. While active, the armor gives off a black/green-colored smoke and all parts of the wearer (including weapons but not hair and facial hair) appear a shadowy black. Most hostile creatures or NPCs affected by the poison cloak will take on a similar silhouetted appearance.

For more information, see the [Ebony Mail](Skyrim_Ebony_Mail.md) article.

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Shield_of_the_Crusader.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Shield_of_the_Crusader.png) Fastened Shield of the Crusader The [Fastened Shield of the Crusader](Skyrim_Fastened_Shield_of_the_Crusader.md) is an artifact. It is acquired through the [Relics of the Crusader](Skyrim_Relics_of_the_Crusader.md) quest, in which you also claim the other relics from the [Bandit Thieves](Skyrim_Bandit_Thief.md) at [Four Skull Lookout](Skyrim_Four_Skull_Lookout.md). Improving the Fastened Shield of the Crusader requires a [Corundum Ingot](Skyrim_Corundum_Ingot.md).

The Fastened Shield of the Crusader is not actually a [shield](Skyrim_Shield.md); rather, it is the only item in the game to utilize the "shoulder" equipment slot. As such, it can simply be equipped for an additional armor boost without affecting any of your other gear. Notably, it does not negate the *[Mage Armor](Skyrim_Mage_Armor.md)* perk.

For more information, see the [Fastened Shield of the Crusader](Skyrim_Fastened_Shield_of_the_Crusader.md) article.
