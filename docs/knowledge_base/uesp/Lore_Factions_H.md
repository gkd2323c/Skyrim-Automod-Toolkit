## Haarvenu

## Hands of Almalexia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/ON-tribute-almalexia-Hand_of_Almalexia.png/200px-ON-tribute-almalexia-Hand_of_Almalexia.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-almalexia-Hand_of_Almalexia.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-almalexia-Hand_of_Almalexia.png) Her Hands fighting off undead (Tales of Tribute) The [Hands of Almalexia](Lore_Hands_of_Almalexia.md) (or simply **Her Hands**) are private temple guards for the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) goddess [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), chosen from the most skilled warriors in the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md). Their bodies and souls are fortified by divine magic, and their armor and [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) bear fearsome divine enchantments.

## Healers Guild
The [Healers Guild](Lore_Healers_Guild.md) is an officially sanctioned guild. As their name implies, they employ healers from across the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).

During the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) in [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321), [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) gave his approval for the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md), which sanctioned businesses such as the Healers Guild. They were hence protected by the Empire of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), even into the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), but would be required to pay them if they wanted to expand their influence. The Empire had subsequently strengthened since the Guild Act was passed.

## Hearteater Clan
[Hearteater Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hearteater_Clan), also known as **Hearteaters** was a [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) that lived during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They were a force that raided northern [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) and razed and pillaged a settlement known as the Highlander Camp.

## Hee-Tepsleel
The [Hee-Tepsleel](Lore_Hee-Tepsleel.md) are one of the many [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) tribes of the [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), they inhabited [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md), a coastal region in southern parts of the province. They are noted for their agriculture, growing and trading alchemical ingredients with tribes like the [Black-Tongue Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black-Tongue_Tribe).

In his work *[Tribes of Murkmire: Tribal Connections](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tribes_of_Murkmire__Tribal_Connections)*, [Emmanubeth Hurrent](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Emmanubeth_Hurrent) of the Wayfarers' Society of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) provided a description of the Hee-Tepsleel tribe, showcasing their inter-tribal relations.

Hee-Tepsleel consider [hermit crabs](Lore_Hermit_Crab.md) a delicacy. Xoko, an Argonian chef from the Hee-Tepsleel tribe, described their meat as "as juicy as the fruit about to drop off a tree."

## Heran Clan
The [Heran](Lore_Heran.md) clan is an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) that lives in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The Heran Clan is one of the thirty revered families of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and a sub-faction of the Great [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md). The **Heran Ancestral Tomb** is found on the waters of the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md), off the road between the [Odai River](Lore_Odai_River.md) and the shanty-village of [Hla Oad](Lore_Hla_Oad.md). It is known for containing hundreds of years' worth of family treasure, safeguarded by snap-wire traps and deadfalls.

## Heretics
The [Heretics](Lore_Heretics.md) of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) are a group of outcast mortals found in [Mania](Lore_Mania.md). They follow the Manic teachings of [Arden-Sul](Lore_Arden-Sul.md) to a fault, believing him to be the true lord of the Shivering Isles. They view [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) as a mortal posing as a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md), and seek to depose him for being unjust and insane. According to the Heretics, [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) cannot be princes, only vile servants. As such, they specialize in summoning and binding [Hungers](Lore_Hunger.md) to do their bidding. Their ultimate goal is to sack [New Sheoth](Lore_New_Sheoth.md) and force the other mortals to convert to their beliefs and live in the wilderness following the destruction of Sheogorath's mortal body. The Heretics are hostile towards most other inhabitants of the Isles, and wear uniform robes to distinguish themselves. They have a particular vendetta against the [Zealots](Lore_Zealots.md), their [Demented](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) counterparts. The highest ranks of the Heretics were said to be "transformed".

The [Apostles of Light](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Apostles_of_Light) were a faction of Heretics whose opposition to Sheogorath stemmed from an intimate knowledge of the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md). A group of Heretics in [Fain](Lore_Fain.md) worshipped a being known as [Gyub](Lore_Gyub.md), Lord of the Pit.

## Hidden Armigers
The [Hidden Armigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hidden_Armigers) were a network of spies working as the intelligence arm of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) during its existence in the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) period. They were rumored to have been an alchemy group from within [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) who later jumped ship. They were based out of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) and led by a mysterious [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named Thrush, although they ultimately answered to High King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) of East [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It's unknown if they were related to the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md), a military order of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md).

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Hidden Armigers were responsible for several attempts at the lives of the members of the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md). They were also receiving confidential information about the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) war effort from Krin Ren-dro, a traitor and a former member of the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md), until this plot was discovered by the Aldmeri Dominion and their informant was eliminated. Later in the same year, the Hidden Armigers worked closely with the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), the outlawed [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), the Eyes of the Queen, and the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md) in arranging ceasefire talks on [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) for the leaders of the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md).

## Hillhunter Clan
The [Hillhunter Clan](Lore_Hillhunter_Clan.md) was a prominent [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) that lived in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), a region in western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Hillhunters were nomadic hunters who lived primarily in the mountains south of Markarth. They are known for their woodcraft, knowledge of the terrain, and stealth. According to members of the other clans, no one can track a Hillhunter unless they choose to be tracked. They were not numerous clans and typically kept out of the quarrels of the more populous clans.

The [Hoïnarts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ho%C3%AFnart) were a noble family of Bretons active in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) during the closing days of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) and the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Empire.md). ## Hollow Moon
The [Hollow Moon](Lore_Hollow_Moon_(faction).md) was a charitable organization of thieves which operated at the Redfur Trading Post in [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Lead by a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) named Valirr, they embodied what the leader of the trading coster at the time called the "Senchal lifestyle" — they stole from wealthy merchants and gave what they took to those in need. Gold to buy food, silks to trade for other goods or gold to buy food and medicine for the sickly were among the goods the Hollow Moon gave to the poor. Agents of the organization would often give simple rings called Hollow Moon Tokens to the infirm, so their need was not questioned when they petitioned the Hollow Moon for aid.

The Hollow Moon often clashed with [Jode's Chariot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode%27s_Chariot), the local law enforcement group. The two organizations generally had mutual respect for one another. Both sides agreed that giving to the poor was a noble cause, and thieves were good for business at the trading post. The Hollow Moon kept the market fluid and stopped too much wealth from accumulating in one place. However, agents of the Hollow Moon couldn't just waltz up to a merchant and steal their goods; Jode's Chariot was still contractually obligated to stop thieves. Aspiring agents of the Hollow Moon had two choices: spend time in the stocks, or become better thieves.

## Hollowfang Clan
The [Hollowfang Clan](Lore_Hollowfang_Clan.md) was a [Khajiit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Khajiit) [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) clan that resided in northern [Elsweyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Elsweyr) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They give thanks to [Sangiin](Lore_Sangiin.md), the blood god. The members of the clan are learned in the arts of blood magic and alchemy.

When they struck down the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) Sahrotnax from the sky and he crash-landed in their den at Moongrave Fane, they held it captive and planned to strengthen themselves by drinking its blood. At this time, a Nord vampire and member of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) named [Grundwulf](Lore_Grundwulf.md) was convinced by the clan to carry out this task, and dubbed "The Devourer" by the Clan Mother, Nisaazda. Their endeavors were stopped by the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) and [Chevalier Renald](Lore_Chevalier_Renald.md). They killed the Clan Mother and Grundwulf, but not after he drained the dragon of his power.

## Horn-Stride Clan
[Horn-Stride Clan](Lore_Horn-Stride_Clan.md) was an ancient and legendary [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md). According to the tales they were active during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). They feature prominently in oral tales of the Reachfolk [Vateshrans](Lore_Vateshrans.md), though their depictions vary significantly from work to work. In some versions of the [Song of Gwyna](Lore_The_Translated_Works_of_Tosmorn,_III.md), the eponymous character [Gwyna](Lore_Gwyna.md) was portrayed as a huntress of the Horn-Stride Clan. However, in other depictions she was presented as a kindred to Chief [Rowolan](Lore_Rowolan.md), descendant of [Demigoddess](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Dearola](Lore_Dearola.md) instead. Vateshran songs state that the Horn-Stride clan was eradicated by Rowolan and his kin. This feat, however, cost chief Rowolan his life.

## Horsemen
The [Silverhoof Horsemen](Lore_Silverhoof_Horsemen.md) (also known as the **Horse-Folk of Silverhoof**, or simply **Horsemen**) are a tribe of [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) abiding in the pastoral basin known as the [Vale of Silverhoof](Lore_Silverhoof_Vale.md). Described as horse nomads, the Horsemen take care of horse herds as their livelihood, and also train steeds for others in exchange for goods. The Horsemen venerate a divine animist spirit they call the Herd Mother.

## Host of the Horn
The [Host of the Horn](Lore_Host_of_the_Horn.md) is the official military force of [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md), the country in the southern [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) and part of the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). They are the sworn protectors of the town and royal family of Lainlyn, and their duties range from espionage and tax collecting, to straight warfare in the name of their lord. ## House Aurmine
[House Aurmine](Lore_House_Aurmine.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble family dating back to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and the reign of King Festrien Spenard I. They were known to be his staunch supporters, sworn enemies of the House [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). They were known vassals of the [House Spenard](Lore_House_Spenard.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), and patrons of the Knights of the Pale Order. The Knightly Order, under their patronage, slaughtered the Direnni wholesale during the latter years of their flight from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).

House Aurmine's heraldry was deemed omnious and was rendered as the shield flanked by boars and snares, or according to other sources, noses.

Many of the vassals of the Spenard dynasty were commonly accused of [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md), though there is no known evidence or proof of members of House Aurmine being vampires.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a member of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)'s Antiquarian Circle discovered and unearthed an ancestral signet of the House Aurmine bearing the insignia of the family.

Although as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the scholars considered the house ancient and obscure, the house remained active as of [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). A noblewoman known as Maurrie Aurmine visited [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) at that time. It is, however, unknown whether or not the Aurmines of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) were connected to the Aurmines of the First Era.

## House Branquette
[House Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble house and royal dynasty from the region of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).

The earliest known historical reference to House Branquette is found in a potentially forged [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) decree that named the house as their Breton Royal Delegates in Rivenspire. In [2E 522](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_522), [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md), the twenty-first count of House Branquette, became the King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md). Hurlburt was considered by some as the greatest post- [Reman](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) monarch of Shornhelm. He wed Countess [Iphilia](Lore_Iphilia_Montclair.md) of [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md), with whom he fathered a son named [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md) in [2E 532](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_532). He also fathered an illegitimate elder son, [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md), with a [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) cousin. In [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546), Hurlburt died, instigating a succession dispute between Phylgeon and Ranser over the throne of Shornhelm. Although Ranser was illegitimate, Houses Branquette and Tamrith supported him over the legitimate son, Phylgeon. Ultimately, Ranser narrowly won the throne of Shornhelm by presenting to the Council of the North the long-lost Direnni decree that named House Branquette as their Breton Royal Delegates in Rivenspire.

After becoming King of Shornhelm, Ranser fathered a daughter named [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md). After [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland became the King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) in [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), he began courting Rayelle. However, before the marriage contracts were worked out, Emeric chose instead to marry Princess [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), infuriating Ranser and leading to the start of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566). Ranser's murder at the Battle of Traitor's Tor in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567) ended Ranser's War and the line of House Branquette. After Ranser's death, much of House Branquette's domain was parceled out to Houses Tamrith and [Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md).

## House Dagoth
'*Pity Dagoth Ur and the Sixth House. All they do, all they are is foul and evil, but they began in brightness and honor, and the cause of their fall was their loyal service to you, Lord Nerevar.*'
— [Peakstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peakstar) [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md) (also called **Clan Dagoth**, the **Sixth House**, **Shadow House**, **Sleeping House**, **Lost House**, **Traitor House**, **House of Lord Dagoth**, the **Tribe Unmourned** or "unmourned house") is the defunct sixth [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) led by [Voryn Dagoth](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), later known as Dagoth Ur. The lost clan's ancient homeland was swallowed up by what is now [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), being the only Great House to have their capital, [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md), located in the region. In the [First Age](Lore_First_Era.md), House Dagoth betrayed the other Great Houses and allied with the Dwemer during the War of the First Council (though some sources claim they fought against the Dwemer), being branded a traitor house and was destroyed for their treason. All records of the house's existence were destroyed following the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) and its members were either killed or absorbed into the remaining houses.

Unbeknownst to all, Lord Dagoth had survived and spent centuries slowly building his strength and influence from within [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). This culminated with Dagoth's attempt to re-create the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) god-construct [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) in order to overthrow the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) and conquer [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Dagoth Ur was slain and his plans halted with the destruction of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).

## House Deleyn
[House Deleyn](Lore_House_Deleyn.md) is a royal family of [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) from the kingdom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The Deleyns are descendants of proud workers, whether it was [horse](Lore_Horse.md) breeders, farmhands, or cooks. Perhaps one of their most famous ancestors was Erric Deleyn, who is remembered today as the "Ivory Lord". When Donel Deleyn became the King of Daggerfall, he ushered the **Deleyn Dynasty**, the kingdom's ruling family throughout the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

## House Dorell
[House Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) is a prominent [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble house and former royal dynasty from [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The founder of House Dorell, [Yric Flowdys](Lore_Yric_Flowdys.md), established [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md), which is the house's seat of power, and their lands in and around the city are integral to their operations. For most of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), House Dorell exemplified the type of entrepreneurial merchant princes that brought great prosperity to High Rock. By the late First Era, House Dorell had established itself as one of the most prominent noble houses in Rivenspire, alongside [Houses Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md), and all three houses had developed rivalries with one another. During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), they established themselves as a maritime and mercantile power, achieving a level of wealth that was seldom seen in Rivenspire. Following the conclusion of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), Houses Dorell, Montclair, and Tamrith formed the triumvirate Council of the North to jointly rule Rivenspire until the selection of a new king of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).

## House Drenim
[House Drenim](Lore_House_Drenim.md) is a minor house of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) based in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They are featured in the traditional ballad called *[The Battle of Molag Beran](Lore_The_Battle_of_Molag_Beran.md)*, in which they defeated House Retheran on the hills of Molag Beran. Many House Dunmer with this name served in [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), such as Galas Drenim, who represented House Telvanni on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md)'s Grand Council, and Mavon Drenim, the highest-ranking Telvanni noble that lived in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).

## House Dres
[House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) (also called **Clan Dres**) is one of the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), with presence only on the mainland. It governs the Dres District of southeastern Morrowind from its ancestral capital city of [Tear](Lore_Tear.md), also called 'Dres' and noted for its proximity to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The district also included parts of the fertile [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) plain, the southern areas of the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), and bordered the [Indoril District](Lore_House_Indoril.md), [Hlaalu District](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), and the swamps and marshes of the Black Marsh regions of [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) and [Thornmarsh](Lore_Thornmarsh.md). In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Dres also controlled the cities of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) and [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md) in central Morrowind. At least once, House Dres managed to gain a minor foothold in the Telvanni lands of the eponymous peninsula. House Dres has had no recorded settlements on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Despite this, some Dres families lived on the island in ancient times before relocating to the mainland, and some Dres ancestral tombs may still be found on the isle. Among them is the ancient Dreloth clan, a cadet branch of the Great House, as well as the Maren, Othrelas, Serano, and [Velas](Lore_Velas.md) clans. It was formally established as a Great House before the middle of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by Grandmaster [Thalthil Dres](Lore_Thalthil_Dres.md) after a slave-raid over [Thorn](Lore_Thorn.md).

The Dres have a mainly rural but still very wealthy agrarian agricultural society, maintaining vast [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) plantations on the plains and marshes surrounding Tear. The Dres were also the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) who shipped the majority of slaves to the other Great Houses (on par with the Indoril and Telvanni), keeping thousands of captives, mostly [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) in their infamous slave-pens of Tear and the surrounding plantations. The Dres are also known among the Great Houses for maintaining a persistent tradition of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) [worship](Lore_Good_Daedra.md) and [ancestor reverence](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md), their staunch support of unity between the Great Houses, and their emphasis on inter-House relations and the improvement of Dunmeri society as a whole.

## House Hlaalu
[House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) (also called **Clan Hlaalu**, the **Yellow Party of Hlaalu** or "Yellow Hats", and formerly **Great House Hlaalu**) was one of the five traditional [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of the settled [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). It governed the Hlaalu District of western-central [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from its ancient capital of [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md), ruled by a leader known as the High Hlaalu. In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Hlaalu's presence on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) was limited to the port town of [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md). By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) the house had expanded exponentially, with its council seat located in the ex-Redoran city of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), and other Hlaalu-run towns including [Suran](Lore_Suran.md) (also a former Redoran possession), [Hla Oad](Lore_Hla_Oad.md) and [Gnaar Mok](Lore_Gnaar_Mok.md). On the mainland, the formerly Dres cities of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) and [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md) were obtained by Hlaalu. Early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), soon after the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) released Morrowind, anti- [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) sentiment and House Hlaalu's association with the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) led to the stripping of its rank as a Great House, and House Hlaalu was removed from the Grand Council of Morrowind. Their position on the council was filled by [House Sadras](Lore_House_Sadras.md).

House Hlaalu could be characterized as adaptable and opportunistic, and any morals they might have had came second to *business*. The House was largely concerned with business and diplomacy, seeking to strengthen ties with at times unconventional allies such as the Empire, and although profit was often a primary objective, traditional Hlaalu practice recognized the value of a good reputation over money. This was their great strength - they were fast talkers and intelligent traders - and their great liability; most Hlaalu were bribable, either with gold or with other favors, with their leaders as no exception. House Hlaalu retainers were also masters of thievery, sneaking, lock-picking, blackmailing and backstabbing, both literal and metaphorical. While some councilors were honest and fair, others were more under-handed and corrupt. However, House Hlaalu's most distinguishing characteristic was its willingness to live in harmony with the other races, setting it apart from the other, occasionally xenophobic, Dunmer Great Houses.

House Hlaalu maintained oftentimes tumultuous relationships with the other factions of Morrowind, prompting their eventual fall from grace. Though not as favored as [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) or [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) retained an overall positive relationship with House Hlaalu. They had less friendly relationships with the rest of the Great Houses, with animosity - and more rarely, outright hostility - existing between themselves and the Indoril, [Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), and Redoran throughout history; though they were formally allied with the latter on at least one occasion. Like the rest of the Great Houses, Hlaalu relations with the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribes were strained, and loathing existed for [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and [Sixth House cultists](Lore_House_Dagoth.md). Of particular note was their relationship with House Indoril, with the longstanding animosity between the Great Houses being particularly noted by Imperial observers. In the Third Era, the Hlaalu retained a positive relationship with the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md) crime syndicate, and most of the Imperial guilds; including the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), and [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). They were less approving of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md).

House Hlaalu adopted the merchant's scale as their heraldry, referencing the source of their power and their pre-eminence in mercantile affairs. They likewise adopted the color yellow as a designation. Their emblem was commonly referred to as the "Golden Scales", and was frequently present on various items associated with the house. Saint [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) the Pilgrim was the patron saint of House Hlaalu.

## House Indoril
[House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) (also called **Clan Indoril**, the **Blue Party of Indoril** or "Blue Hats") is one of the five [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). It governs the Indoril District in the heartland of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from their historic ancestral capital city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), also the capital of Morrowind until the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). The Indoril District comprises the east-central lands south and east of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), including large swaths of eastern [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) and [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) in the high uplands and fertile river valleys, as well as parts of the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md) and the eastern coast along the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md). They also control a number of islands in the province, such as the lush isle of [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md). An Indoril settlement of note was the port city of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md), next to [Bal Foyen](Lore_Bal_Foyen.md). House Indoril has never claimed land on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), though ancestral tombs for Indoril families may still be found on the isle, such as those of the Aran, [Ieneth](Lore_Ienith.md), Raviro, Salothran, and Verelnim families.

The Indoril were orthodox and conservative supporters of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) during its millennia-long reign over Morrowind. Claiming kinship with the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) themselves and boasting such ancient heroes as the Hortator, [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), House Indoril has dominated Temple authority since time immemorial and controlled the levers of government for 3,000 years. They used their control of the Tribunal priesthood to dominate the other Houses, which accounted for Indoril's preeminence among the five clans, gaining much territory and becoming the largest one. They had an undisputed hegemony over the province throughout the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second Eras](Lore_Second_Era.md), until their unbroken streak of power was finally overthrown during the signing of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md). They have always held prominent positions within the Temple hierarchy, and throughout history the fates of House Indoril and the Temple have been closely linked. Through the Temple, House Indoril's influence was felt across Morrowind, even beyond their traditional borders. As of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), House Indoril is considered one and the same with the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md).

Like the other Great Houses, House Redoran loathed the cultists of the [Sixth House](Lore_House_Dagoth.md) and the [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) of Vvardenfell, and disapproved of the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md)'s illegal activities.

## House Telvanni
[House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) (also called **Clan Telvanni**, the **Brown Party of Telvanni** or "brown hats") is one of the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It governs the Telvanni District of northeastern Morrowind, including the eastern portion of the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) District from its council seat in [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md), and the coastal settlements of Tel Mothrivra and [Port Telvannis](Lore_Port_Telvannis.md), the latter of which is home of the Great House's main leadership: the Parliament of Bugs. They once controlled strongholds on the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), but no longer have any properties on the mainland as of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). They value talent, knowledge, and initiative. The house is comprised primarily of wizard-lords who have traditionally isolated themselves and protected commoners in their mushroom-hut villages, pursuing wisdom and mastery in solitude, and generally being quite peaceful. The Telvanni don't care about politics or what others think, staying out of matters beyond their lands as to not trouble anyone, and only want to be left alone in peace to do as they wish and live comfortably. The wizard-lords and their mercenaries keep the people secure, and otherwise leave them alone. By bringing prosperity to their tower, honor to the house, and security to kinsmen and retainers, wizard-lords thereby insure the health, wealth, and security of their house and its people. They are called the most xenophobic of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and shun contact with outsiders, preferring to sell bug-musk and tend their herds of giant insects amid the rocky hills and islands of the extreme far-off ends of the province.

Members of the House can rise through the ranks by eliminating or otherwise 'indisposing' other members, and stealing from other Telvanni and living shows that you deserve it. Most Telvanni lords want little more than to be left alone to their research and ambitions, although many did enjoy the friendly company of their neighbors rather than remaining in isolation. As passionate isolationists, they disdain to claim new holdings. A minority of wizards-lords called "rogue Telvanni" are highly ambitious and egocentric, and were known to compete ruthlessly among themselves for arcane mastery and to control and exploit Vvardenfell's resources. The House believed that these young wizards were expendable, and would be less trouble to the rest of the Telvanni if they were off on Vvardenfell island stirring up trouble with the other colonists. House Telvanni publicly denies supporting the rogue settlements.

They neighbor the Indoril District, and through their kinship with the legendary [Tribunes](Lore_Tribunal.md) the [Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) dominated many of their borders on the mainland for over 3,000 years up until the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), resulting in the Telvanni keeping to themselves on the far-off edges of the province. In general, the Telvanni did not interact with other Houses or Guilds, nor did they have many enemies save for Abolitionists and the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and did not intend to be presided over by any other institutions (particularly the Guild of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) and their monopoly). However, the Telvanni themselves were universally disliked or disapproved of by all other factions found in Vvardenfell as late as [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). On the other hand, the Telvanni did not have the usual hatred of vampires that most other factions held. Their lords and Mouths were willing to speak to [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md); wizards did not fear vampires nearly as much as other folk did.

## House Tharn
[House Tharn](Lore_House_Tharn.md) is a noble [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) family from [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md). They have held estates in and around the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md) since the early parts of the First Era. Once the family established itself as one of reliability and utmost loyalty, they were used more and more to subjugate and eliminate Imperial dissenters. By the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) the family had established itself as one of the most important and involved families in the Empire. The Tharns have had connections to the [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) in the ancient past, and have kept their worship to them a secret throughout generations.
