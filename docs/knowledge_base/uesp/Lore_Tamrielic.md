# Tamrielic


Tamrielic, colloquially referred to as **Cyrodilic**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWQv4-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> **Cyrodiilic**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> and sometimes **Imperial Speech**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWHuleeya-4)</sup> is the common tongue of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) and the language used most extensively throughout modern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWAltmer-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TESVPrima-6)</sup><sup>:7</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TDC-7)</sup><sup>:6</sup> Tamrielic is descended from [Old Cyrodilic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Cyrodilic)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TDC-7)</sup><sup>:6</sup> and is based on the speech and writing of [High Elvish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Elvish)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWAltmer-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TESVPrima-6)</sup><sup>:7</sup> and [Ayleidoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleidoon).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWE-8)</sup>

The [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Gideon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gideon) are known to speak better Cyrodilic than the other parts of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) due to their history with and exposure to the nearby [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TOBGATB-9)</sup>

## Notes
- [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) refer to the common tongue as **Old Elf**.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Yakub Hair-10)</sup>
- [Tibedetha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tibedetha) gets its name from the middle Tamrielic word for "Tibers Day".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-11)</sup> This indicates the transition from middle Tamrielic to Tamrielic occurred sometime after the rise of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).
- **Pig-Cyrodilic** is a variant of Tamrielic sometimes spoken by non-native speakers.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ACA2-12)</sup>
- Tamrielic (including middle Tamrielic) is the language shown to the audience as English (or whatever language the game is played in) in *The Elder Scrolls* media. - [Early Modern English](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Modern_English) -style speech was common across menus, books, and NPCs in *Arena* and *Daggerfall*, but was dropped in all later games in favor of [present-day English](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modern_English). Some exceptions still remain, with [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) being quoted as speaking in an Early Modern English dialect.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TSOP10-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONCP-14)</sup>
