I'ric Harad Egun (sometimes shortened to **I'ric**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFShrines-1)</sup> was the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) archmagister of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), believed to be alive sometime in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KEI-2)</sup> His name is widely known and is comparable to the likes of [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md), the Witch-King of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md), and [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md), the God of War.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLat D-3)</sup> According to the historical fiction, *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)*, his name is implied to have significant weight, and so throughout the story, he is simply referred as the **Arch Magister**.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KEII-4)</sup> He is worshipped at altars in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFShrines-1)</sup>

I'ric Harad Egun was active sometime in the early-mid First Era and had become acquainted with Moraelyn, who was a well-renowned warrior across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). When he was the Archmagister, he inducted Moraelyn into the Crystal Tower, making him the first non-High Elf to learn from there.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KEIV-5)</sup> In his time at the tower, he utilized [trolls](Lore_Troll.md) as guards, to keep festering [rats](Lore_Rat.md) away. Many years later, both I'ric and Moraelyn traveled to the Kingdom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), to take the eight-year-old Prince [Edward](Lore_Edward.md) from the castle, and reunite him with his mother at the distant city-state of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KEI-2)</sup> Sometime later, I'ric inducted Edward into the Crystal Tower, where he personally trained him to use [magic](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KEV-6)</sup>

His history is largely unknown. However, he garnered widespread notoriety and was worshipped in small altars across the wilderness, specifically in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). These altars remained active as far as the late- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), around [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFShrines-1)</sup>
