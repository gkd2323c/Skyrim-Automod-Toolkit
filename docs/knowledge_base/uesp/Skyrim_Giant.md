[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg) A Giant **Giants** are enormous humanoids with a primitive, albeit very artistic, culture, and can often be found at their [camps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Giant_Camps). They are not initially hostile, but if you walk too close to them, they will raise their [clubs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Giant_Club) above their heads and shake them at you. If you continue to approach, they will stomp on the ground and become hostile. Giants are normally seen herding [mammoths](Skyrim_Mammoth.md); if you hurt their mammoths or threaten them in any way, the giants will attack. Unprovoked giants are passive creatures.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Giant.md).

## Combat
Giants' main method of attack is using their large clubs. Every time a giant uses a power attack with its club and the attack kills the targeted opponent, the victim will be sent flying hundreds of feet into the air, from where much of the surrounding landscape can be seen. This affects all entities, including you. Hostile giants refuse to enter water, and will eventually walk away if you remain in a body of water.

Battling giants early game is an extremely dangerous proposition. Giants are level 32, and their mammoths, who are likely to aid their masters, are level 38. Their attacks, even if successfully [blocked](Skyrim_Block.md), can kill you in one hit. Early on, the best defense is dodging their attacks entirely. Attempting to kill them with melee attacks at this time is practically impossible.

Giants can be ragdolled with all three words of [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md), the [Cyclone](Skyrim_Cyclone.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> [shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md), a [werewolf](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md)'s moving or sprinting [power attacks](Skyrim_Power_Attack.md), or a [vampire lord](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md)'s [Vampiric Grip](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vampiric_Grip)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> [spell](Skyrim_Spells.md). These powers can make it a lot easier to defeat them in the early game. A werewolf can kill a giant with one sprinting power-attack. A magician can attack from afar with relative little fear of retaliation, using Frost spells to slow them down, or an archer can snipe them from a vantage point.

The [Command Animal](Skyrim_Command_Animal.md) [power](Skyrim_Powers.md) or [Animal Allegiance](Skyrim_Animal_Allegiance.md) shout can be doubly helpful when used to turn mammoths against giants, considering the mammoths would likely be attacking you otherwise.

Exploiting the terrain by staying on cliffs where giants can't reach you can help survival rates, but be wary of their long weapon reach. Alternatively, you can lure a giant from its camp to an area that is simply inaccessible for it, for example, hiding in a doorway that it cannot come in from, and pelting it with attacks. You can also use big boulders to keep a distance between you and a giant, as you whittle down their health.

## Notes
- Occasionally, you may [encounter](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Giant_leading_a_painted_cow_to_camp) a giant leading a [cow](Skyrim_Cow.md) painted with symbols along one of Skyrim's roads. These giants will only turn hostile if directly attacked. Various groups of [NPCs](Skyrim_NPCs.md) have been known to attack giants that have strayed too close to the road.
- Giants do not level up with you, but instead have their level set at 32. In contrast, most [dragons](Skyrim_Dragon.md) in the game level up with you. Because of this, a giant can easily win a fight against a dragon when you are at low levels, and you can take advantage of this by leading a dragon to a giant to be killed. Be careful not to stay near the giant once the dragon is close or you may end up having them both attack you.
- It is possible to loot a giant's treasure chest right in front of them, as long as you don't stay near them long enough to turn them hostile.
- [Frost Giants](Skyrim_Frost_Giant.md) are added with the [Dawnguard add-on](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md). [Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Karstaag), the spirit of a named frost giant, is added by [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md).
- There are three different appearances for giants. Each appearance consists of a different set of animal hides and furs.
- The giant encountered at [Pelagia Farm](Skyrim_Pelagia_Farm.md) has different statistics than other giants. It does only 5 damage with its club instead of the usual 60, and has less health at lower levels. Unlike most giants, however, the one at Pelagia Farm is leveled, so from level 28 onwards, it will actually have more hit points than other giants. Also, the corpse does not have the regular giant loot on it, so there is no hides, gems, armor or weapons like usual.
- Giants have 13 different grunts they use in battle, but they all share the same line of dialogue: *"Ayarg garag gar!"*, and it is untranslatable.
- The only named giant in the game is [Grok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Grok). Another one, named Goldar, was meant to play a role in a Civil War quest, but this quest didn't make it into the final game.
- Jonah Lobe, the artist responsible for the giant model in Skyrim, created them to look like his father.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>
- If you hop onto a surface (or into a body of water) they cannot reach, they will sheathe their club (it disappears from their hand) and retreat to a certain distance and stay there until you either exit the surface/water or they forget your presence.
- The [Wabbajack](Skyrim_Wabbajack.md) will exclusively deal elemental damage to giants, and cannot be used to transmogrify or instantly kill them.
- It is possible to dodge the club slam (usually performed by the giant while he is running towards you) and the stomp by sprinting directly past the giant as he performs the attack. You might still get staggered and a tiny bit of damage, but the damage from the attacks are situated directly in front of the giant, not area-of-effect, like what might be expected.
- Only NPCs (including followers) will be sent flying high into the air by the club slam; you, atronachs and dwarven automatons do not get sent flying but rather get heavily staggered. However, if you are killed by the attack, your character will end up flying high into the sky.
- There is a giant named the Mournful Giant whose mammoth died. Unlike other giants, this one won't attack unless you attack him yourself.

## Bugs
- Sometimes, after killing him, a giant's club will start to float slowly up into the sky until it is no longer visible.
- If you instruct a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md) to pick up a giant's club, they will equip the club they picked up and use it in combat. Unfortunately, it will be impossible to remove the club from their inventory afterwards. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.1.1, fixes this bug.
