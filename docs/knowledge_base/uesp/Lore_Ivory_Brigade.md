# Ivory Brigade

[![](https://images.uesp.net/a/a7/OB-banner-Leyawiin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Leyawiin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Leyawiin.jpg) The banner of Leyawiin, featuring the Ivory Brigade's coat of arms [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-npc-Captain_Rian_Liore.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Captain_Rian_Liore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Captain_Rian_Liore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Captain_Rian_Liore.jpg) Captain Rian Liore The **Ivory Brigade** was a military force based out of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). They were established to protect [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) against the three great alliances.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ot IB-1)</sup> The Ivory Brigade was founded by Rian Liore, a former legionary. He selected an ivory [horse](Lore_Horse.md) on a field of green, the ancient symbol of Leyawiin, to be the group's coat of arms.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Motif-2)</sup> After being debated by Leyawiin's Chamber of Legates, the Brigade's name and function were officially implemented.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ot IB-1)</sup> After the fall of the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), the Brigade rose to replace its [legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rian-3)</sup> recruiting veterans of the Three Banners War,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> native Blackwooders, and former legionnaires,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ot IB-1)</sup> as well as using their armaments.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Motif-2)</sup>

The Brigade experienced friction with the native [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) as they expanded to the farthest reaches of Blackwood. Seeking to establish trade routes,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Viria-5)</sup> the group reached out to the locals in negotiation.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BJ-6)</sup> Due to previous experiences with false promises, the natives refused, leading to distrust between the two forces when the Brigade refused to leave.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Heem-7)</sup> After a series of kidnappings, one of the brigades were captured by Argonian forces for questioning. Though tensions rose to the point of considering execution,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Heem-7)</sup> the militiamen were released following an investigation which revealed the culprit was one of the native Argonians.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sit S-8)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md), a cult dedicated to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), grew in activity within Blackwood. As their plans culminated in an all-out assault on [Fort Redmane](Lore_Fort_Redmane.md), Captain Rian Liore committed half of the Ivory Brigade to the fortress' defense, keeping the remaining division in Leyawiin.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rian-3)</sup> Together with the Black Fin Legion of General [Keshu](Lore_Keshu.md), governor of [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md), the Brigade defended the front gate of the fortress, staving off cultist hordes until Mehrunes Dagon appeared in physical form. Both defending forces took to hiding as the [Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) began assaulting the structure. Through the actions of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), Dagon was banished and the battle was won.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Pyre-9)</sup> Captain Liore and General Keshu remained optimistic for a friendship between the Black Fin Legion and Ivory Brigade in the future.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rian-3)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Keshu-10)</sup>
