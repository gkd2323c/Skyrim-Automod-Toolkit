# Bloodfiend


Feral vampires are vampires who have lost their minds over the years, until they eventually become deranged with hunger. A vampire can become a feral vampire when they are unable to feed, refuse to feed, or go for long without blood.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-JOTLL-2)</sup> The most notable example of a vampire that has gone feral in this manner is [Lord Lovidicus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lovidicus).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-JOTLL-2)</sup>

The "blood cursed" are a type of bloodfiend who resemble blood-starved vampires.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOBloodfiend-3)</sup> Unlike traditional vampirism, the infection sets in very quickly, and the process completes in a very short time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bo R-4)</sup> What makes these bloodfiends distinct from normal vampires is the fact that potential bloodfiends are not required to be alive for the process to reach completion.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOHeloi US-5)</sup> Bloodfiends can come about after a victim is drained dry by a vampire.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOUS-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTBOTE-7)</sup> A relic known as the Lightless Remnant was capable of transforming mortals into bloodfiends, and almost unleashed a bloodfiend plague upon Rivenspire before it was given to Molag Bal.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOVerandis-8)</sup>

**Harrowfiends** are another variety of bloodfiend, which were created when certain people were caught in [Harrowstorms](Lore_Harrowstorms.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> They had more of their life energies stripped from them than the mindless [harrowed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Harrowed) and as such were incapable of being cured.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Bloodfiends care less about the sunlight than their more sane counterparts.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOValeric-11)</sup>
