[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Varen's Wall is a ruined stone wall that is found in the western highlands of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Varen's Wall is named after the eponymous Emperor of Cyrodiil, [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), one of the many kings that ruled during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). This wall was created to split the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) (primarily [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md)) from the rest of the Colovian Estates, in the efforts to protect Varen's homeland from the powerful [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) of Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VW-1)</sup> The main gate of the stone wall is known as **Varen's Wall Gatehouse**.

## History
Varen's Wall was constructed during the Interregnum, by the commission of Duke Varen Aquilarios of Colovia, during the early years of the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md). It was hastily built to protect his birthplace and the original seat of power, Kvatch from the Emperor's retaliation. Construction was administrated by the well-known engineer, Juros Truptor, who designed it to be built with whatever material that the workers can find throughout the region. There were many people that enlisted to help build this stone wall, in a fast, efficient way, with no care for aesthetics. It was built with a variety of materials, such as stone, mismatched rocks, ruined structures, and even blocks from various noble estates.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ett W-2)</sup> Towers were made across the wall in an irregular pattern for defense.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VW-1)</sup>

Jaros' efforts in building the wall and obtaining more volunteers helped Varen's greater cause even more. And within a year, Varen's Wall was finished, although it barred several landmarks, including the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md) (except for the river estuary), [Trumbe](Lore_Trumbe.md), [Fort Istirus](Lore_Fort_Istirus.md) and [Sutch](Lore_Sutch.md). The wall had exceeded Varen's reign and continued to support the Gold Coast as far as the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VW-1)</sup> By the time the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) occurred in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), there was no sign of Varen's Wall anywhere it once stood.

Varen's Wall construction caused complications for the traders and scholars of the Gold Coast and [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Thahiri-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GF-4)</sup>

Shrines devoted to the [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) were constructed along Varen's Wall. As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) they were considered old constructions.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Crafty_Lerisa-5)</sup>

Maps of the region of the Gold Coast drawn before and after the construction of Varen's Wall are noticeably distinct from each other and tell different tales about the land and its people.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ANT-6)</sup>
