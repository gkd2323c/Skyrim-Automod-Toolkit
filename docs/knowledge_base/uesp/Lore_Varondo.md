Although the meaning of *Beldastare* is unknown, *Varlasel* translates to "Hall of Stars" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

## History
During the time of the Ayleids, an event known as the "Harrowing of Varondo" took place. [Arox the Mutilator](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arox) was present at the city the day it occurred, and burned down an army of Ayleids was "like dry grass in a wildfire" using his fiery breath, garnering him the title the "Ruin of Varondo".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arox-4)</sup>

Following the fall of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) at the end of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the clan of Varondo escaped largely intact as they fled south beneath the canopy of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). They are presumed to have settled there and adopted the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) under the terms of the native [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), eventually being absorbed into their local population.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ASi V-5)</sup> The burgeoning [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) toppled the remaining Ayleid cities of Cyrodiil one by one, and Varondo subsequently fell into ruin.

Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the ruin was occupied by the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), who were working to restore [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md) to power.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONGold Road-6)</sup>

By the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the outer part of the ruins had worn to a shadow of their former state, but the massive, convoluted, multi-level interior still remained intact. At the time, the ruins contained a coven of [conjurers](Lore_Conjuration.md) and Daedra.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OB-7)</sup>
