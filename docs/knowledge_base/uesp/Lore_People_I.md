#### Archmagister I'ric Harad Egun (fl. early-mid 1E)
[I'ric Harad Egun](Lore_I%27ric_Harad_Egun.md) (sometimes shortened to **I'ric**) was the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) archmagister of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), believed to be alive sometime in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). His name is widely known and is comparable to the likes of [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md), the Witch-King of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md), and [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md), the God of War. According to the historical fiction, *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)*, his name is implied to have significant weight, and so throughout the story, he is simply referred as the **Arch Magister**. He is worshipped at altars in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

#### Ritemaster Iachesis (Merethic Era ?b - 2E 582)
**Ritemaster** [Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) was the first leader of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), a long-standing monastic group of mages dedicated to studying the [Old Ways](Lore_Mysticism.md). He was widely considered one of the wisest and most powerful mages in history, and led the Order for at least three and a half millennia.

#### Jarl Idgrod Ravencrone (fl. 4E 201)
[Idgrod Ravencrone](Lore_Idgrod_Ravencrone.md) was the Jarl of [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) who ruled at the time of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Idgrod claimed to have [visions](Lore_Sight.md) granted to her by the [Eight](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), which were caused by her subconscious manipulation of Magicka, an ability that her children inherited. As of 4E 201, she was unlike any other jarl in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md); she was a hunched old woman who spoke in riddles and parables, rarely spoke directly, and relied on her visions to govern her hold. However, her wisdom was sometimes misunderstood as the ramblings of an old woman, causing some of her subjects to begin losing faith in her.

#### Jarl Igmund (fl. 4E 176 - 201)
[Igmund](Lore_Igmund.md) was the Jarl of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) at the time of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). His father, [Hrolfdir](Lore_Hrolfdir.md), was killed by the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md). Prior to his death, Igmund's father and the Empire hired a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) militia that was led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) in order to regain control of Markarth, offering the militia the freedom to worship [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) in exchange for retaking Markarth from the Forsworn, a promise which they were forced to renege on when the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) objected. Igmund became Jarl of Markarth at a young age, after his father's death around [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176). Like his father, he was aligned with the Empire and continued the fight against the Forsworn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Iggy-1)</sup>

#### The Impresario (fl. Merethic Era - 2E 582)
[The Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md) was an eccentric, enigmatic, and immortal traveling exotic goods merchant. She was a figure shrouded in mystery. While she took an appearance of an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there were various speculations about her true nature. Some believed that she might be an obscure [Demi-Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demi-Prince) and/or a [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) assuming mortal form to interact with humans, while she herself implied that she was not of mortal origins. Others thought of her as a just wealthy eccentric whose riches had driven her mad. There were even rumors of her being a [witch](Lore_Witches.md) [conjuring](Lore_Conjuration.md) strange things like [spider](Lore_Spider.md) legs, [lizard](Lore_Lizard.md) eyes, and bubbling cauldrons. She was exclusively recognized as the Impresario, and any endeavor to ascertain her birth name was generally deemed inadvisable due to the potential for unfavorable consequences.

#### The Indrik (?b - ?d)
[The Indrik](Lore_The_Indrik.md), also known more specifically as the **Luminary Indrik**, was one of the [Luminaries of Magic](Lore_Luminary.md), mysterious immortal beings of pure [magic](Lore_Magic.md), spirits likened to beams of sunlight from [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) himself, his chosen form was that of an [Indrik](Lore_Indrik.md). The Indrik was the first of the Luminaries to come into existence, and was considered more powerful than the rest. He played an important role in the life of [Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md), collaborating with her alongside the other Luminaries to develop the system of Scribing, a "metamagical theoryset" that was the precursor to the modern [Spellcrafting](Lore_Spellcrafting.md) system.

The Indrik viewed himself as being akin to a blade, as he believed that all power has to be wielded with clarity of purpose. Thus he was driven by the question of how power should be used and, to lend his strength to others, he required that they lend their strength in turn, to help him right wrongs and take another step on that path. The Indrik viewed truth as a form of power, and believed that the [gods](Lore_Aedra.md) wanted their charges to learn by doing. In his view, power expressed against one's own will was no power at all. Despite his views, the Indrik would sometimes act arrogantly and without thought, a fault he acknowledged within himself.

As with all of the original Luminaries, the Indrik himself was not aware of his true origins, and had his own theories on his birth. He believed the Luminaries to be eddies in the current of the river that is magic, not masters of it, but part of it, who were one with it as much as it was one with them. The Indrik believed himself to have been created as a manifestation of pure thought, the invention of a mind that craved to make sense of the world through stories. He viewed the realms of the Luminaries as being the result of the wisdom of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), which would not allow the Luminaries to be both alone and wandering.

Of all the original Luminaries, the Indrik was the first to form a bond with Ulfsild, and was viewed as the one who would abandon his loyalty to her last. The Indrik's beliefs were viewed with some skepticism by the other Luminaries, who viewed his ruminations on responsibility, power, and the nature of the Luminaries, as either pompous and futile, or, in the case of the [Luminary Gryphon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Luminary_Gryphon), as a frivolous waste of the power he could be using to protect others.

According to [The Netch](Lore_The_Netch.md), of the Luminaries, people were drawn to The Indrik for his power and might. As with all Luminaries, The Indrik was said to possess a mastery of magic that made the average archmage look like a neophyte. As a Luminary, he saw the world and magic in a way that was different from mortals, and so was not particularly fond of mages and would only lend them his power if his challenges were completed. As beings of pure magic the Luminaries were said to have understood magic in a way mortals never could and so, their power, freely given, allowed one to access arcane mysteries most could only dream of. Indeed, Scribing is said to have been somehow destined to channel the power of the Luminaries from the start.

#### The Insatiable (fl. 2E 582)
[The Insatiable](Lore_The_Insatiable_(creature).md) is a powerful [Hunger](Lore_Hunger.md) that resides in [the Gorge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Gorge), a vestigial realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) where brutal [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) spend eternity devouring anything they can to feed a boundless hunger that will never be satiated. The Insatiable is pinnacle among these beings; said to be an embodiment of ceaseless hunger, it is a Hunger of great cunning and power.

The Insatiable is sometimes summoned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for the purpose of stripping a place of life and causing untold terror. It snatches away its victims to its lair in the Gorge, where they are kept alive while it savors their innards in an excruciatingly slow feast. Its power is heightened due to its ability to easily slip in and out of Tamriel, returning to the Gorge in order to stalk its prey or escape from danger.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md) ordered a cultist named Teraniel to strike a deal with the Insatiable. She created an anchor to the realm using an ancient [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) altar in the ruined Vaults of Heinarwe on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), and subsequently summoned the Insatiable to the nearby Cey-Tarn Keep in order to occupy the keep's defenders. The hunger began to hunt and devour the occupants, forcing the [Divine Prosecution](Lore_Divine_Prosecution.md) to seal the keep and begin an investigation. Little progress was made until Silurie, an ex-member of the Divine Prosecution, arrived at the keep and used [Stendarran](Lore_Stendarr.md) magic she learned in [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) to track down the Daedra. Together with [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and members of the Divine Prosecution, Silurie entered the Gorge and tracked the Insatiable to its lair. It was swiftly banished, although part of the group became trapped within the realm and were captured by Teraniel, who planned to sacrifice them in order to restore the Insatiable. However, the Vestige located Teraniel's anchor and used it to reenter the Gorge to kill Teraniel and rescue the trapped group members.

#### Investigator Vale
[Investigator Vale](Lore_Investigator_Vale.md) is a fictional [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) detective and the main protagonist of the eponymous series of mystery novels, which were extremely popular during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Her adventures take place across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and often make reference to the current political or social climate. Some people believed that Investigator Vale was based on Lady [Arabelle Davaux](Lore_Arabelle_Davaux.md), a retired member of the [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md) and the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md). The author of the series was officially unknown, but some people claimed it was Arabelle. Arabelle officially denied any connection with the Investigator Vale novels, though she did in fact author them. It has been claimed that her adventures are "true-to-life" reimaginings of actual historical events.

#### High King Irthvyd the Impassive (fl. late 1E)
[Irthvyd the Impassive](Lore_Irthvyd.md) was a High King of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) during the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).

Sometime in the late first era, Irthvyd was a young jarl who rose to prominence among the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) during the reign of High Queen [Roleke](Lore_Roleke.md). It is unknown which hold Irthvyd hailed from. His stoic reserve and calm presence earned him the epithet of "the Impassive." When the people of Solitude rose up against Roleke, Irthvyd and his warriors heard of the rising disquiet and headed toward the city. According to legends and oral tradition, when he reached Solitude's city gate, [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) herself appeared to him and stated that Roleke had died at the hands of her progeny only hours before his arrival. All Roleke's children died during the siege, by her hand, leaving Solitude with no High King or blood heirs. Irthvyd then rode into the city to help the citizens of Solitude restore order during the resulting confusion. Hours later, he was crowned High King of Solitude, with the blessings of Kyne.

Irthvyd's steady rule brought peace to the holds after years of Roleke's bloody reign. Irthvyd ruled for decades, but he never took a spouse. While the lack of heirs is not an inordinate problem among Nord royalty, it did raise questions. Irthvyd refused both a spouse and the possibility of heirs. The title of high king eventually passed to Irthvyd's most senior advisor, [Temylda](Lore_Temylda.md), who battled several jarls in single combat and won.

#### Isran (fl. 4E 201)
[Isran](Lore_Isran.md) was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) who served as the leader of the [Dawnguard](Lore_Dawnguard.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) after re-founding it. Incredibly driven, Isran has worked tirelessly to further his cause, being stubborn enough to ignore those who told him otherwise.

Originally from the town of [Helland](Lore_Helland.md), Isran was a [Vigilant of Stendarr](Lore_Vigil_of_Stendarr.md) who struck out on his own when he realized the Order was too soft and didn't really have what it took to defend against the evil in the world. He knew they'd be overrun one day, and he wasn't going to wait around for it to happen. While he won't discuss it with anyone, he lost his family to vampires years ago, and it's warped him. He now hates vampires far more than he cares about saving people and would willingly sacrifice just about anyone if it gave him a clear shot. In his mind, the ends justify the means every time. While never a member of the Vigilants, Sorine Jurard crossed paths with Isran in the past and appreciated his tenacity and survival instincts, and would gladly have worked with him again if necessary had they not parted on such unpleasant terms.

Isran believed, among other things, that hatred granted strength. He reformed the [Dawnguard](Lore_Dawnguard.md), with himself as its leader, after he spent years renovating Fort Dawnguard in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md). He was considered a lone wolf, and had difficulty maintaining amicable working relationships. Nevertheless, he called upon many past allies to help him fight the emerging threat of the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md) vampires.

#### Istlod (fl. 4E 2nd century)
[Istlod](Lore_Istlod.md) was the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and Jarl of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) during the late second century of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He was credited with holding Skyrim together for nearly twenty-five years. After the signing of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) by the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), Istlod was forced to make outlaws of all [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) agents residing in his province. In some accounts, he is said to have formally placed bounties on their heads. Upon Istlod's death, his son [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) succeeded him as Jarl of Solitude, and the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) elected him High King of Skyrim.

#### Iszara (c. 2E 820s b - ?d)

#### Councilor Itinia (fl. 2E 533 - 582)
[Itinia](Lore_Itinia.md) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) member of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) before and during the reigns of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), and [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), during the turbulent [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). As a member of the Elder Council, she was a representative of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md). During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), she attempted to secure the Cyrodilic throne by proposing the enthronement of Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), a relative of the Longhouse Emperors; however, her efforts were unsuccessful, as the Despot of Markarth declined the proposal.
