# Daedric Princes

—Sir Cadwell of Codswallop<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Daedric Princes (also known as **Daedric Regents**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFDPQ-2)</sup> **[Daedra Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Lords)<sup>[†](#intnote_qc)</sup>**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MH-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTNOO-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> **Demon Lords of Misrule**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-To SA-6)</sup> **Lords and Ladies of Oblivion**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NRM-7)</sup> **Dark Princes**, **gods of torment**,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DUG-8)</sup> or **The Greater Princes**<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LFNHATRAYQ2-9)</sup>) are the most powerful of the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), and thus most commonly worshipped as [gods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VOF-10)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MH-3)</sup> Each has a particular sphere, which it is said to govern. In fact, [Demiprince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demiprince) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen) stated that "a broad and well-defined sphere of influence that enables clarity of purpose and focused expression of will" is what made a Prince a Prince and claimed that because of this, though Demiprinces such as himself far outstrip any mortal in power and consequence, they are still "but trivial compared to the Greater Princes".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LFNHATRAYQ2-9)</sup> In various cultures, the Princes are viewed as siblings,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VOF-10)</sup> with they themselves having been known to refer to each other as such, regardless of their actual origins.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-16Accords12-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Sheo-13)</sup> Daedric Princes may assume a typically masculine or a typically feminine form, sometimes both.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TA-14)</sup> They are usually referred to as Princes regardless of what gender they appear most frequently as.

The various Daedric Princes and their spheres are listed below. In all, there are seventeen powerful Princes known to mortals: [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag), [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Namira](Lore_Namira.md), [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine), [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), and [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TBOD-15)</sup> Another Prince, [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia) has been forgotten by all but Hermaeus Mora and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Ithelia IMO-16)</sup>

The title [Ur-dra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ur-dra) is used to denote the "eldest and most powerful" of the Daedric Princes.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lilatha-17)</sup>. [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) and the [Imperial Geocraphical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) attribute the title to the Daedric Prince Nocturnal,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-18)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Census-19)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lilatha-17)</sup> and the Society claims nearly all the royalty of Oblivion recognize the claim. However, the ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) used the title to describe Namiira instead.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TDS-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TFDOF-21)</sup> According to Azura, the Princes who attended the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md): Boethiah, Hermaeus Mora, Hircine, Malacath, Mehrunes Dagon, Molag Bal, Sheogorath, and Azura herself, are among the most powerful of the Princes, who "stand above their lesser kith".<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONAzura-22)</sup> [Lyranth the Foolkiller](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyranth) claimed that "cheap mortal dualisms" of "Friend" and "Foe" cannot capture the complexity of the relationship between Daedric Princes and that the Princes ultimately keep their own counsel on matters of this scale, though they do sometimes pursue parallel interests that make them act in concert.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-23)</sup> According to [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), it is not possible to truly defeat a Daedric Prince through force of arms.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-18)</sup>

Each Daedric Prince, with the exception of Jyggalag, is said to have their own [plane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OO-24)</sup> formed out of [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md),<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Fox-25)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LSP-26)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LMA_FNH-27)</sup> over which they have control, with the realms themselves being extensions of their masters.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-EGRN-28)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Whim-29)</sup> It is said that Oblivion realms can be infinite in size and shape, and that all proper Oblivion realms, such as the main realms of each Prince, are infinite in scale.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Leramil Wise-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-TESONRWRL-31)</sup> The realm of each Prince is said to be fundamentally shaped and curated by their will and change to accommodate their slightest whim, to the extent that it has been said by some that the Prince is the realm and the realm is the Prince.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-23)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Malkhest Archive-32)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-EGRN-28)</sup> Though for most Daedric beings creating a realm is an enormous undertaking as it requires enormous will and power, the Daedric Princes are said to possess such might that in ages past they formed vast and practically limitless domains around the initial seed of their realm. As such some of them are known to also have sway over Adjunct Realms or border realms, such as the [Infinite Archive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Archive) of Apocrypha, which lie within their plane's "greater fabric".<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Malkhest Archive-32)</sup> Some of these border realms have been described as having extensive or even infinite scale, and not lying within the same time and space as their Prince's main plane.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Panopticon-33)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Working-34)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Leramil-35)</sup>. Indeed, according to Sotha Sil, were a Prince to simultaneously invade the realms of the other Princes to seize the power within through the use of something like the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), they'd gain infinite power through the infinite connections made.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-18)</sup> The limits on their planes, powers, and influence are not readily understood, as there are over 37,000 other planes (such as the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md) and the [Chimera of Desolation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chimera_of_Desolation), as well as chaos realms and pocket realms) over which they are not known to exert control,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OO-24)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TDOB-36)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-DDCFA-37)</sup> and indeed there exists far more than that, with accounts from the Daedra describing the minor realms in Oblivion as overall being infinite in both number and complexity.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-23)</sup>. The planes of Oblivion are said to exhibit infinite variation, much like the Daedra that inhabit them, thus it is said they share little in common, being "the very definition of change and variation, manifesting all possibilities, and validating all understanding and misunderstanding".<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Jackdaw-38)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LA:Lt F-39)</sup> Indeed, it is said that what the mortal senses perceive while in an Oblivion realm is crafted from illusion and metaphor, the result of their mind attempting to make sense of the surrounding chaos. Because of this phenomenon things like the "grass" one might perceive while in an Oblivion realm are actually just the memory of that thing as interpreted by the mortal mind.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Telbaril-40)</sup> The smaller planes are said to often "twist into hyperogonal paradoxes" in an attempt to resist any sort of order being imposed on them, or wink in and out of existence, making it difficult for Daedra to gain a foothold there.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-23)</sup> The term "pocket realm" is said to refer to any minor plane of Oblivion, whether it is autonomous from the rule of a Daedric Prince, semi-autonomously under the rule of a Prince-appointed "functionary", or fully incorporated by a Prince.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LFNHATRAYQ2-9)</sup> The realms comprising Oblivion are described as being part of a different universe or a different dimension in relation to Mundus.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Atronite-41)</sup>

Daedric Princes are bound by the [Pact Primordial](Lore_Pact_Primordial.md), an ancient treaty which prevents interdimensional incursions from one Prince into the realm of another. Certain rites such as those that exist within the Tormenting Eye [Black Book](Lore_Black_Books.md) can open special [portals](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) that allow for the Pact to be circumvented so a Prince can enter another's realm uninvited, when combined with methods such as possessing a dead mortal body.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-42)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-43)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-OTPP-44)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Conclave-45)</sup> The ten Princes that are signatories of the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md) with Sotha Sil have also had restrictions placed upon their ability to directly interact with Nirn, being able to manifest directly only when called by certain intermediaries such as [witches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witch) or sorcerers. As the Compact binds both ways, with the other party being all mortals, this has also placed restrictions on mortal travel to the realms of those Princes.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LY:V4-46)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TCC-47)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-CWCSotha Dialogue-48)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-SOi A-49)</sup>

The Daedric Princes seem to view Men and Mer as little more than minor amusements, occasionally applauding the actions of mortals when they exceed their expectations. They do not know the mortal sense of "good" and "evil", and usually have extremist tendencies, which is why Men and Mer fear them greatly. However, several princes do seem to take genuine pleasure in tremendous acts of devastation, in particular Boethiah, Molag Bal, Vaermina, Mehrunes Dagon, and Peryite.

Although the beings are considered evil by most, they are widely worshipped in the realms of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Elaborate shrines are created to honor the Daedra as gods. They often take a keen interest in their worshippers, and it is speculated that this is either because of the obvious ego-gratification of being somebody's god, or because the Daedra like to keep an eye on potential future subjects (assuming people of demonic disposition enter Oblivion after death, that is; there are as many afterlife theories as there are religions in the world). Mainstream religious authorities discourage Daedra worship, and often mount witch-hunting expeditions to drive out Daedra worshippers from the local area. During these encounters, they are often surprised at the marginal sanity that comes of worshipping the Daedric Princes. For the most part, however, dealing with the Daedra, one gets the distinct impression of being mused over as a person peering under an upturned rock may momentarily wonder at the lives of the bugs living ignorantly there.

The Daedric Princes can be summoned, and there is a popular notion that the summoned lords will then give the summoner a quest or task to fulfill and that the quest, once completed, will make the questor richly rewarded. Mostly these tasks are merely for the entertainment of the Daedra. However, this is probably not true of most Daedra summons, and the summoner would do well to consider the consequences of his actions in advance. A consecrated shrine is the most reliable conduit for contacting a Daedric Prince, though there are other ways to make contact with the powers of Oblivion.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-50)</sup>

While Aedra and Daedra are precisely defined, there are still some beings who challenge this dichotomy in some respects. Malacath, who legend holds was once the Aedra [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), is particularly hostile to other Daedra; he even created [Scourge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scourge), Bane of Daedra.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TBOD-15)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTNOO-4)</sup> According to [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) is a Daedric Prince whose sphere is [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-OBMankar-51)</sup> However, this is uncorroborated, and little is known of the mysterious Trickster deity; while some stories suggest Lorkhan was impervious to death, similar to a Daedra, he is also credited with helping to bring about creation (in fact, per many sources, it was allegedly his idea).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TM-52)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-SONGOM-53)</sup> Obscure stories also question the origins of Meridia<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Merid-54)</sup> and Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-7fights-55)</sup>

Just like with other beings, each reality along the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) contains its own version of each entity, be they mortal or Daedra, even deities such as the Daedric Princes have their own alternate versions within the realities. These alternate versions are said to be "reflections" of one another: different in some ways, like images in a cracked mirror, but originating from the same seed. However, there is at least one reality where concepts such as [magic](Lore_Magic.md) and Daedra do not exist at all.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Ithelia-56)</sup>

For information on other Daedric creatures, and the characteristics of Daedric beings more broadly, see the entry on [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).

## Azura
[![ON-concept-Prince-Azura-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/ON-concept-Prince-Azura-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Azura-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Azura-emblem.png) **[Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) of the Crimson Gate**, also known as: **Queen of Dawn and Dusk**, the **Mother Soul**, **Moonshadow**, **Mother of the Rose**, **Queen of the Night Sky**, **Twilight Queen**, the **Rim of all Holes**, the **Cosmic Severer**, **the Great Lady** and called **Azurah** by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), is a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) whose sphere is dawn and dusk—the magic in-between realms of twilight—as well as mystery and magic, fate and prophecy, and vanity and egotism. Azura is one of the few Daedra who maintains the appearance of being "good" by mortal standards, and reportedly feels more concern for the well-being of her mortal subjects than other Daedric Princes. It is said she wants their love above all else, and for her worshippers to love themselves; it pains her when they do not. This attitude leads to an extremely devoted following. She is also one of the few Princes who constantly maintains a female image, and is perceived accordingly. In Khajiiti creation myths, Azurah is credited with introducing [moon sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar) to Khajiit, and this belief is also told within tales aimed at Khajiiti children.

The *[Five Songs of King Wulfharth](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md)* claims that [Alandro Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alandro_Sul) is the immortal son of Azura. While Azura is considered one of the "good" Daedra by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), elsewhere she has been known to be allied with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), who is known to the Dunmer as one of the [Four Corners of the House of Troubles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Troubles). The people of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) region believe that Azura bewitches some of her followers to become her "lovers" and "virtual slaves". Her followers acknowledge that she is "cruel but wise".

In addition to being one of her many sobriquets, "[Moonshadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonshadow)" is also the name of Azura's realm in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Azura's Star, the Twilight Star, is visible at dawn and dusk low on the horizon below the [constellation](Lore_Astronomy.md) of the [Steed](Lore_Astronomy.md#The_Steed). Her summoning day is [Hogithum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hogithum), a festival on the 21<sup>st</sup> of First Seed.

For more information, see the [main lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura).

## Boethiah
[![ON-concept-Prince-Boethia-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/ON-concept-Prince-Boethia-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Boethia-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Boethia-emblem.png) [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) (sometimes spelled **Boethia** or **Boet-hi-Ah**), known as the **Prince of Plots**, the **Daedra Prince of Cruelty and Torture**, the **Dark Warrior**, **Deceiver of Nations**, **Queen of Shadows**, **Goddess of Destruction**, **Hunger**, and **Fount of Inspiration**, **He-Who-Destroys and She-Who-Erases**, **Boethiah the Schemer** , **The Dark Mistress**, and called **Boethra** by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), is a Daedric Prince who rules over deceit, conspiracy, secret plots of murder, assassination, treason, and unlawful overthrow of authority. This sphere is destructive in nature, and Boethiah's destructiveness comes from inspiring the arms of mortal warriors. The Dark Warrior has been described as "cold" and "impersonal", "cruel", and one of the more "demonic" of the Daedra lords. Worshippers are known to hold bloody competitions in Boethiah's honor, battling—even killing—each other, for Boethiah is said to care only for their blood. In Morrowind, the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) known as the [Hunger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hunger), foul creatures that are eternally bone thin, are associated with the Prince.

Boethiah sees battle as a blessing, and is frequently depicted as a great caped warrior, often in a stoic pose. While sometimes described as male, Boethiah is also commonly referred to as female, sometimes within the same text. Notably, Boethiah is usually depicted as male in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), but is also known for taking the form of a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) "warrior-queen".

She rules from the realm of [Attribution's Share](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Attribution%27s_Share). Boethiah often manifests using tricky, unpredictable avatars, which can appear as a male or female of any race. These avatars become involved with important or momentous events, working behind the scenes to achieve an outcome that serves Boethiah's will. They will often challenge mortals, seeking to test the mettle of heroes and commoners alike. After a trial ends, the avatar is likely to vanish, leading to strange and mysterious stories and legends among the participants.

Despite the dark undertones of Boethiah's sphere and influence on Tamriel, the Prince was considered in Morrowind to be one of the three "[Good Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra)" and the Anticipation of [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) by the old [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), and one of the Reclamations by the New Temple. The Prince of Plots has a scion, the [demiprince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demiprince) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen). Despite this, the information broker Madam Whim believes that the demiprince did not actually originate from Boethiah, while also stating that "Boethiah supports their claim of scionship". In the same interview, Whim would describe how Daedric Princes have been known to adopt scions in the past. In the *[Five Songs of King Wulfharth](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md)*, [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) states that [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) is the son of Boethiah, one of the strongest Padomaics, but it is unclear how literal the statement was. [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), as one [Corner of the House of Troubles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Troubles), is considered Boethiah's "arch-enemy" in Morrowind and they are in a perennial fight. Boethiah can be summoned on [Gauntlet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gauntlet), the 2<sup>nd</sup> of [Sun's Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun%27s_Dusk).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Boethiah.md).

## Clavicus Vile
[![ON-concept-Prince-Clavicus Vile-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/79/ON-concept-Prince-Clavicus_Vile-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Clavicus_Vile-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Clavicus_Vile-emblem.png) [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), also known as the **Prince of Trickery and Bargains**, the **Consummate Politician of Oblivion**, the **Master of Insidious Wishes**, and **Child-god of the Morningstar**, is a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) whose sphere is the granting of power and wishes through ritual invocations and pacts. He is seen as one of the more "sophisticated" of the Daedric Princes, and often appears in the guise of a jovial [Skaafin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skaafin), a race of horned Daedra that inhabit the [Fields of Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fields_of_Regret), his realm in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).

Clavicus Vile is also known as **Nycot**. According to some, this name was created by the mortal [Nycotic Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nycotic_Cult), who didn't have the knowledge to properly read the [Daedric alphabet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Alphabet), misreading the first five letters of Vile's Daedric name as "Nycot" upon mistaking the runes for Tamrielic letters. However, the name was used by a Skaafin to refer to Vile over three hundred years before the cult's prominence.

Clavicus finds eternity to be "boring", so he finds entertainment in watching mortals and occasionally meddling in their affairs. He has been known to be the patron to vampires, gracing them with social stature, reason and savvy, allowing them to not only live among regular mortals, but to hold powerful positions in society. However, not all of Vile's machinations are necessarily insidious; he has been known to reward those who, on his direction, eliminate threats to the general public (while still serving his own interests).

Vile is rarely seen without his loyal companion, [Barbas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barbas). Barbas typically assumes the form of a dog, but is also known to appear as a [Scamp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp) named [Creeper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Creeper). The two are so close, Barbas is almost universally depicted alongside his master in artwork and statuary. In fact, Vile has been known to store up to half his power within Barbas. These facts have led to speculation that the pair are, in fact, two halves of the same being, as Vile's very social nature would not allow him to exist without a companion.

It is said Clavicus Vile is the most carefully neutral Daedric Prince, though he as at times created alliances, such as his role in [The Triad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Triad). He is an enemy of [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm). Clavicus Vile's summoning day coincides with the [New Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival), the 1<sup>st</sup> of Morning Star.

According to some sources Vile views himself as a playful and fun-loving god who basks in the sun alongside his favorite companion, Barbas. This mindset is said to be reflected in the idyllic form his realm takes, making his domain less a normal place like one would find on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and more a "state of mind". Despite this view of himself Vile's "games" are noted to often be dangerous, making caution necessary when approaching him.

## Hermaeus Mora
[![ON-concept-Prince-Hermaeus Mora-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/ON-concept-Prince-Hermaeus_Mora-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Hermaeus_Mora-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Hermaeus_Mora-emblem.png) [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) (sometimes spelled "**Hoermius**", "**Hormaius**", "**Herm'us**", or "**Herma** **Mora**") is the **Daedric Prince of Forbidden Knowledge**, hidden shoals, whirlpools, and sudden squalls. His sphere also encompasses destiny; he scries the tides of [Fate](Lore_Fate.md), weaving through the past and future as interpreted in the stars and heavens, and his dominion is said to hold "the treasures of knowledge and memory". His curiosity is insatiable and he seeks all knowledge, and not to change but to observe, so that all becomes known. He is known by many titles, such as the **Demon of Knowledge**, the **One Who Knows**, the **Master of the Tides of Fate**, **Gardener of Men**, **Prince of Fate**, **Lord of Secrets**, the **Keeper of Forbidden Knowledge**, the **Golden Eye**, the **Great Eye**, the **Eye that Wanders**, the **Eye-of-All** or **Eye-Who-Sees-All**, **Ur-Daedra**, the **Abyssal Cephaliarch**, **Old Antecedent**, **Scryer**, **He Who Records All Knowledge**, **Inevitable Knower**, and the **Lord of Fate and Knowledge**. To the ancient [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), he is **Herma-Mora**, the **Woodland Man**, or "**that which speaks in the wastes**." To the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), he was **Hyrma Mora**. The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) know him as the **Tide King** and **Hermorah**.

He "weeps cold tears" at the plague of ignorance, where knowledge is forsaken out of fear of misuse, and he allegedly seeks to enlighten all to all mysteries that have ever and will ever exist. One follower of Mora stated that the ultimate purpose of the Daedra lords is to instruct and improve the "generally deplorable" character of mortals. This benevolence presented by his followers may be a façade, as Mora is said to hoard his knowledge and give nothing away for free. Like all Daedric Princes, he's not to be trifled with, but he is subtler than most of his ilk and ensnares mortals with his secrets. He may seem cruel at times, but he's not irrational. Mora hoards and safeguards any knowledge that could potentially endanger reality. He is described as crafty and good at hiding things, and is said to love traps. According to some sources, the Mythos, the core of Mora's realm of Apocrypha, is where the Prince "whispered the first secret" and "imagined the concept of knowledge", thus also conceiving Apocrypha itself in that same act.

Always lurking, he is the void and the ever-seeing eyes. Mora has been called the wisest of the Daedric Princes, with a mind as old as [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and a body of slime, though he describes himself as *"the riddle unsolveable. The door unopenable. The book unreadable. The question unanswerable."* Unlike most Princes, Hermaeus Mora typically does not take on a humanoid form, manifesting instead as black clouds of varied, grotesque assemblages of eyes, tentacles, and claws, or a featureless purple vortex known as the Wretched Abyss,. However, according to some sources, he occasionally took on a form resembling that of a mortal. He speaks with a thundering bray, and has been described as being composed of countless eyes or "orbs without end". The energies and essences of both Hermaeus Mora and [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) are said to take the form of "roiling darkness", and are so similar to one another that additional decorative elements are required to determine which of the two an artifact belongs to. This strong similarity has caused some scholars to theorize that the two Daedric Princes share a close relationship and are linked to one another in some manner.

According to Mora himself, he is "multitudes", and only adopts a singular guise for the sake of the mortals, whose minds would crumble when confronted with such a concept. The form in which he appears before mortals is described as a "delimited projection", although he is said to not communicate with them often. The [Watchlings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) of the Prince are known to sometimes serve as his proxies, as he can see through their eyes and even take control of their forms to speak through them. It is said that all statues of Hermaeus Mora serve as access points from his realm to Nirn, thus one should think carefully before placing a representation of the Prince close to their person. Mora can be invoked in certain locations in Nirn using a "condensate of planar possibilities", which takes a form similar to ink to summon him. He is said to always be using [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) as a result of always being involved in various ongoing projects.

He resides everywhere and nowhere but takes glee in tormenting mortals from his realm in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), the [plane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Oblivion) called [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md): an endless library where the infinite pages of countless books containing all forbidden knowledge are found. Each book sports a black cover without a title, and the realm is reported to be haunted by ghosts forever searching for knowledge. His servants include [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) such as [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md), [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md), and [Watchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher), but he is known to have mortal worshipers too. Sailors and seafaring folk refer to **Herm'us Mora** as the **Tide King**, master of the seas and One-Whose-Tentacles-Rise-From-the-Deep, a being who lurks in the oceans and pulls vessels down into the abyss. Similarly, the Khajiiti Hermorah controls the tides and lives in a vast library under the sea.

Hermaeus Mora is tangentially related to the origins of the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) by association with his sibling, [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). Mora's enemies are said to be [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm) and [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina), and his [summoning day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hermaeus_Mora%27s_Summoning_Day) is the 5<sup>th</sup> of First Seed.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) considers Mora among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md). According to Mora's [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md), due to the Compact, the Prince made a pact with *every* mortal on Nirn to stop directly interfering in [Mundial](Lore_Mundus.md) affairs, and was paid something he dearly desired in exchange. The compact appeared to bind in both directions, thus, mortals cannot come to Apocrypha as they did before.

## Hircine
[![ON-concept-Prince-Hircine-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/ON-concept-Prince-Hircine-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Hircine-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Hircine-emblem.png) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), known as **the Huntsman**, **Lord of the Hunt**, **Master of Beasts**, **Wolves' Father** and the **Master of the Chase**, is a Daedric Prince whose sphere is the Hunt, the Sport of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), the Greatest Game, and the Chase and Sacrifice of Mortals. His realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) is the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md), an endless forest where the Prince and his Huntsmen hunt great beasts, people, and even other Daedra. Hircine is typically portrayed with a great spear and either the head or skull of an [elk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elk), the latter of which Khajiiti myths say belonged to [Y'ffer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffer)'s champion, the [Graht-Elk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Graht-Elk).

Hircine is one of the more widely worshipped Daedric Princes in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), despite never actively seeking worship or reverence. His appeal lies in his sphere, which encompasses the thrill of the hunt, though this can be taken to extremes. His advocacy for sportsmanship is another lure, as the Law of Fair Hunt which he holds to prohibits cheating prey (referred to as "the Hare") out of a genuine chance at escape. He admires the Hare that overcomes whatever is thrown against them, no matter the odds. For example, one who turns the game on its head by hunting the hunters may earn the Prince's respect. Finally, he is straightforward in his desires; he often seeks people out, tests them, and rewards those who prove themselves worthy with a boon.

As the progenitor of [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md), Hircine is recognized as **the Father of Manbeasts**. He serves as the guardian of were-creatures, which are often referred to as his "children". The condition is called Hircine's Gift by those that view it as a blessing, and Hircine's Curse to those that consider it an abomination. Hircine is quite prideful of lycanthropy, and resents those that received it willingly but have come to regret it; he's known to have ingrates slain to rid them of their misery. Regardless of whether they worshipped him or not, Hircine will typically claim the souls of skinshifters, and they will spend eternity in the Hunting Grounds.

The [Demiprince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demiprince) [Dearola](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dearola) is Hircine's daughter. There is a belief that Hircine is allied with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), though [Lyranth the Foolkiller](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyranth) claims that if such an arrangement exists, it would be obscure outside their own circle. Hircine has [Daedra Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Lord) that manage some of his sub-spheres and report to him, such as the Lord of Fishing. He is also associated with the Daedra Lord [Hollowjack](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hollowjack), whose realm of [Detritus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Detritus) is a "carving" from the shell of the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md). [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm) is an enemy of Hircine. Hircine's [summoning day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hircine%27s_Summoning_Day) is the 5<sup>th</sup> of Mid Year.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Hircine.md).

## Ithelia
[![ON-trailer-Ithelia 02.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/ON-trailer-Ithelia_02.jpg/150px-ON-trailer-Ithelia_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Ithelia_02.jpg) [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia), known as the **Prince of Paths**, the **Mistress of the Untraveled Road**, the **Unseen**, the **Fate-Changer**, the **Dark Reflection**, the **Last Tomorrow**, the **Unweaver**, and **She Who Saw and Wept**, is the Daedric Prince of Paths. Some believe her will has some kind of relation to [unbound Prisoners](Lore_Hero.md). She supposedly had the ability to shape destiny, alter [fate](Lore_Fate.md) and rearrange its strands according to her whim. Considering this power a threat to the fabric of reality, [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), in alliance with [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), erased all traces of her memory from existence in order to preserve reality. Ithelia left behind her loyal servant, [Torvesard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torvesard), as a contingency plan in her absence.

As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she was unknown to the academic community, though some scholars had uncovered evidence of forgotten [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) deities. Torvesard, in an alliance with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) and [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina), was able to recover his memory of his Prince and began his efforts in finding and freeing her in earnest. Eventually, Ithelia's memory was restored, with her then setting out to reclaim all her power. However, with the help of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the support of Hermaeus Mora, she saw the destruction her path would result in, even for herself, and accepted being exiled, seemingly with no possibility of return, to a world without magic, thus ending the danger to reality. The connection to the other reality was then severed using [Abolisher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abolisher). Hermaeus Mora then once again erased all memory of Ithelia’s existence from every being, save for the Vestige and himself.

For more information, see the [main lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia).

## Jyggalag
[![ON-concept-Prince-Jyggalag-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/ON-concept-Prince-Jyggalag-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Jyggalag-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Jyggalag-emblem.png) [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag), the **Prince of Order**, also known as **Gray Prince of Order**, is a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) whose sphere represents logical order and deduction; his great library once contained a logical prediction of every detail of the world and of every action that would ever take place on [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) or [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), long before they actually happened. He was cursed in antiquity by the rest of the Daedric Princes to become the Prince of Madness, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). After the events of the last [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md), Sheogorath and Jyggalag are no longer the same Daedric Prince; however, they are still connected in other ways.

Jyggalag commands his own Daedra, the [Knights of Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_Order), which are spawned from obelisks summoned by his followers, the [Priests of Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Priests_of_Order). Together, they constitute the [Forces of Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forces_of_Order). He is viewed as stern, bleak, and colorless, with him and his soldiers lacking originality.

For more information, see the [main lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag).

## Malacath
[![ON-concept-Prince-Malacath-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/ON-concept-Prince-Malacath-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Malacath-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Malacath-emblem.png)
[Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath), the **Daedra Prince of Lies, Deception, and Hypocrisy**, the **God of Curses**, **Daedric Prince of the Bloody Oath**, **Lord of Ash and Bone**, **Lord of Monsters**, **Oathbreaker**, **Creator of Curses**, **He Who Speaks Sideways**, the **Furious One**, the **Keeper of the Bloody Curse**, the **Daedric Prince of Vengeance** the **Defender of the Betrayed**, also known as **Mauloch** (or **Malauch**) to the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), **Orkey** in [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) tradition, **Orkha** to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), **Malooc** to the [Giant Goblins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Giant_Goblin) and [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), **Malak** to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), and **Muluk** the **Blue God** of the [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) and [Ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre), is a Daedric Prince whose sphere is "the patronage of the spurned and ostracized, the keeper of the Sworn Oath, and the Bloody Curse", as well as conflict, battle, broken promises, and anguish. Malacath has been described as a "weak but vengeful" [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) by some, and he fittingly (given his sphere) is allegedly not recognized as a Daedra Lord by his peers;. Despite this, however, [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) considers him among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as he was one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md). In addition, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) includes Malacath among his "siblings", the Daedric Princes, and has described Malacath's [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md), [Volendrung](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volendrung), as a "true Daedric artifact".

The Prince rules over a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) known as the [Ashpit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashpit), and he counts the oversized but dull-witted [Ogrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogrim) as his servants.

[Goblin-Ken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Goblin-ken) are often thought to have origins tied to Malacath, and he is worshipped by them in some of his aspects. Malacath himself has been known to take patronage over the Goblin-ken races, and is known to protect and guide them.

Malacath is considered the first of the Four Corners of the [House of Troubles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Troubles) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), though other sources say he is an ally of [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), and an enemy of [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm). His [summoning day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath%27s_Summoning_Day) is the 8<sup>th</sup> of Frost Fall. Troll fat is a consistent element in rituals intended to summon Malacath.

For more information, see the [main lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath).

By his own account Malacath loves very few things, vengeance being one of them. The Prince has described destructive vengeance as a beautiful act, the beauty of which the perpetrator shouldn't lessen by attempting to claim its consequences were unintended. According to Malacath himself, he has no love for any of the other Daedric Princes. Despite some of his titles, Malacath is said by some sources to have historically exhibited a straightforward mentality, of which indirect and subversive action is unbecoming.

## Mehrunes Dagon
[![ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes Dagon-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes_Dagon-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes_Dagon-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Mehrunes_Dagon-emblem.png) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) (formally, **the Exalted and Most Puissant Lord, Gerent of Dagon, Mehrunes**), also called **the great Daedra Lord**, **the Black Daedra Lord**, **Lord of Change**, **Prince of Ambition**, **Prince of Disaster and Destruction**, **Master of Razors**, **Sovereign of Destruction**, the **Flame Tyrant**, the **Father of Cataclysm**, the **Golden Flame**, the **Flame-Father**, and the **Mer-Pride's End**, is the **[Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of Darkness and Destruction**, whose sphere encompasses destruction, change, revolution, energy, and ambition. The **Lord of Fire and Flood** is associated with natural dangers like fires or earthquakes. Flash floods, thunderstorms, and other natural disasters have been linked to communions between him and his cultists. The Prince is also said to be the author of the [*Mysterium Xarxes*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysterium_Xarxes). Mehrunes Dagon has an insatiable hunger for destruction.

Dagon's destruction is not without purpose, as it is believed to be a means of change and violent rebellion. Though Dagon is best known for his domain of destruction, it is said that that is actually just a portion of his sphere of influence. In actuality, all of his aspects are rooted in Change, one of the [Eleven Forces](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eleven_Forces), thus that force flows very strongly within his realm.

[Xivilai Moath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivilai_Moath) and [Faydra Shardai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Faydra_Shardai) are Dagon's adoptive children. At one point, the [Dark Seducer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Seducer) Si'ess was his paramour and his bodyguard. There is a belief that Mehrunes Dagon is allied with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), though [Lyranth the Foolkiller](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyranth) claims that if such an arrangement exists, it would be obscure outside their own circle, and that the relationship between Daedric Princes cannot be captured by "cheap mortal dualisms" of "Friend and Foe". Nonetheless Dagon and Hircine do sometimes pursue mutual interests that make them act in concert. Enemies of the Prince of Destruction include [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm), [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), with whom Dagon shares a bitter rivalry—the two have been known to meddle in each other's schemes when the opportunity arises. It is also rumored that the Prince hates [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) considers Dagon among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as he was one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md), and claims he "makes art of destruction". Dagon's [protonymic](Lore_Nymic.md) is **Lehkelogah** (listen Link ⏵) and his neonymic is **Djehkeleho-dehbe-effehezepeh**. (listen Link ⏵) The Prince's summoning day coincides with the [Warriors Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Warriors_Festival), the 20<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Dusk.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).

## Mephala
[![ON-concept-Prince-Mephala-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-concept-Prince-Mephala-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Mephala-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Mephala-emblem.png) [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) is a Daedric Prince who is associated with murder, lies, deception, [sex](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sex), and secrets, and is always weaving a web of intrigue and terror. Though it is stated that her sphere is obscured to mortals. Mephala is also known as the **Webspinner**, **Plot-Weaver**, **Androgyne**, the **Spinner**, the **Spider**, the **Teacher of the Secret Arts**, the **Queen of the Eight Shadows of Murder**, the **Silver Spider**, the **Spider Queen**, the **Spider God**, the **Dark One**, the **Whispering Lady**, the **Lady of Whispers**, and the **Queen of Oblivion**. She is also referred to as the princess of secrets and she who weaves the sordid web of human affairs. Long, thin silver hair is a trait associated with Mephala's champions.

Mephala interferes in the affairs of mortals for her own amusement, and does nothing without purpose. Mephala thinks of the affairs of mortals as a weave; pull but one thread and the whole thing unravels. To her, the "web" of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) is an interconnected system of action and consequence, and she spins new threads to influence outcomes. In an essay analyzing the Daedric Prince, [Divayth Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divayth_Fyr) posits that Mephala likely doesn't see mortals as mere playthings, unlike some of her fellow Princes.

The Webspinner deals in secrets and hidden lore. She and her worshippers value secrets and mysteries above all else. Those who stumble upon one of Mephala's shrines or a place where an artifact of hers is hidden may find Mephala whispering forbidden secrets in their ears. [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the "Demon of Knowledge", is sometimes referred to as Mephala's sibling. Hermaeus Mora collects knowledge of all sorts, but Mephala cares only about knowledge that is obscure and undisclosed. Not only is she secretive, Mephala also cares very little for the safety and well-being of mortals. The Spider takes great pleasure in strife. A ruined marriage, two peaceful factions erupting into bloody warfare over falsified evidence of treachery ... she finds all of these amusing.

Mephala is purportedly one of the "strongest of the recognizable spirits" that emerged soon after Akatosh formed and time began. At one point prior to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Mephala's enemies included [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm) and [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), and she considered [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) an ally. The ancient Khajiit considered her an ally to [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah), [Boethra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boethra), and [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj). They also believed that she was Boethra's lover. Her summoning day coincides with the [Witches Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witches_Festival), which falls on the 13<sup>th</sup> of Frostfall.

## Meridia
[![ON-concept-Prince-Meridia-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-concept-Prince-Meridia-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Meridia-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Meridia-emblem.png) [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), **Lady of Infinite Energies**, the **Bright Lady**, the **Strict but Fair Arbiter of Inner Luminance**, the **Sunfire**, the **Radiant One**, the **Keeper**, the **Lady of Light**, the **Red Star**, the **Harbinger of Dawn**, **Goddess Who Holds**, **Mirror of the Lie** and the **Prince of Life** is the Daedric Prince "of Life and Light", associated with the energies of living things. It is stated that her sphere is obscured to mortals. The Sunburst is her symbol. She has a great and everlasting hatred for the undead and will greatly reward any who eliminate them from the world. With fervor, she reveals purity within chaos, aiming to abolish false-life so that the Mundus may be reforged with the fire of new light. As such, Meridia has a reputation for benevolence, and is one of the few Daedric Princes who is usually not considered to be wholly evil. Her day of summoning is the [13<sup>th</sup> of Morningstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia%27s_Summoning_Day).

In spite of this, she is referred to as the **Glister Witch** or the **Lady of Greed** by those who view her less favorably, with a habit of collecting live [human specimens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Purified) to satiate said greed. To her enemies, Meridia is known for her violent loathing of disorder, as well as her obsessiveness, self-righteousness, irrational anger towards entities she deems impure, and hatred of mortal free will and defiance. She believes that free will should be surrendered to passion, "destiny is fulfilled for the vessel".

As with most Princes, Meridia sees mortals as "assets" primarily for use as a means to an end. Meridia can bestow upon mortals immortality, at the cost of their will, becoming those known as the [Purified](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridian_Purified); slaves to her command, cleansed of disease, death, and free will. People who join the ranks of the Purified are both those that willingly pledge to serve Meridia and the unwilling, such as those that defy her. Meridia also granted immortality to her champion, [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril_the_Unfeathered), the ancient [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) enemy of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md). Her Aurorans and Umaril returned during the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) to seek vengeance on the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).

Her sphere of energy and life often brings her into direct conflict with many of the other Princes of Oblivion. The most notable example of these feuds is her eternal battle with the Daedric Prince of Domination, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). During the waning days of the Ayleid Empire, Meridia would be responsible for the destruction of the Mola Gbal -worshipping [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) city of [Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abagarlas), commanding her knights in the Meridia-worshipping Ayleid city of [Delodiil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Delodiil) to invade after discovering a great and terrible [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) known as the [Mortuum Vivicus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mortuum_Vivicus) was gifted to Abagarlas by the Lord of Domination himself. This feud would continue well into the Second Era, where Meridia's assistance was instrumental in stopping Molag Bal's [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). Denizens of Molag Bal's realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) refer to Meridia as the **Shining Bitch**, and her worshipers are often locked away and tortured within the [Lightless Oubliette](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lightless_Oubliette). Her other notable enemies include [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), who once invaded Meridia's realm in her quest to rewrite reality, and [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm), the God of War who dislikes most Daedric Princes.

For more information, see the [main lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia).

## Molag Bal
[![ON-concept-Prince-Molag Bal-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-concept-Prince-Molag_Bal-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Molag_Bal-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Molag_Bal-emblem.png) [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) (Stone-Fire, or literally the "Fire Stone")<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Stone Fire)</sup> is the Daedric Prince whose sphere is the domination and enslavement of mortals. His ultimate desire is to harvest the souls of all mortals, bringing them within his sway by spreading seeds of strife and discord in the mortal realms. To this end, he obsessively collects soul gems, and has even dragged pieces of Nirn into his realm to satisfy his insatiable desire for conquest. The more souls he collects, the more he wants.

Molag Bal values patience and cunning. He often deceives those he deals with, and has no trouble waiting exceedingly long periods of time for his plans to come to fruition. He also takes great pleasure in the suffering of mortals, and often has them tortured for his amusement. Those who fall into his clutches are forced to labor ceaselessly for his benefit. The Prince has a penchant for necromancy, often employing the use of the risen dead and forcing his followers to serve beyond the grave. He is capable of preserving live mortals far beyond their normal lifespan to prolong their suffering.

Molag Bal is acknowledged as the father of several children, though it is unclear what those relationships mean to him. He has a son, [Ozozzachar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ozozzachar), who is a [Daedric Titan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Titan). He also has a daughter named [Molag Grunda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Grunda), a [Winged Twilight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winged_Twilight) who took the lowly [Frost Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Atronach) Nomeg Gwai as her consort. Molag Bal did not approve of the relationship and had them both banished to Oblivion so he could punish them for eternity. A persistent legend about [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md) claims he is the progeny of Molag Bal and a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) woman.

Molag Bal's enemies are [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), [Lamae Bal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamae_Bal) and [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay). He was at one point allied with [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura). His summoning day is during [Chil'a](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chil%27a), on the 20th of Evening Star. He appears before his cultists in the guise of a mortal when invoked on other dates.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).

## Namira
[![ON-concept-Prince-Namira-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/ON-concept-Prince-Namira-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Namira-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Namira-emblem.png) [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) is a Daedric Prince whose sphere is the ancient [Darkness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void). She is also known as the **Daedric Prince of Hunger**, the **Mistress of Decay** or **Lady of Decay**, the **Spirit Daedra**, and the **Goddess of the Dark**. Namira is the ruler of sundry dark and shadowy spirits, and is often associated with [slugs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Slug), [spiders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider), and other things that inspire mortals with instinctive revulsion. The symbol of a [bat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bat) is featured on her emblem. The Khajiit believe that all creatures who feed on rotten flesh are her spies. She is the Daedric Prince of spirits and shadows, as well as the patron of vermin and squalor. Namira also appears to be associated with beggars and the beggaring gifts of disease, pity, and disregard. Namira and her shadowy endeavors are often recognized to bear some association with eternity. Somewhat in line with this, the Khajiit believe that she is a spirit of infinite realms. Namira sees herself as the rightful ruler of all spirits. In some culture's creation stories, she is responsible for corrupting [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), or making a deal with him during his endeavor to create [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).

Mortals who become ensnared by this spirit are tortured until they forget who they were and know only Namira. Ancient Khajiit knew her as an **[Ur-dra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ur-dra)**, a term also used to describe [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). It implies that the owner of the title is the "eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes". [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) is similarly called the Ur-Daedra who is "elder than Ada". Namira's followers and the priesthood of [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) have clashed in the past. Namira counts [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah), the [Twilight Cantors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Twilight_Cantors), [Khenarthi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi) and [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm) among her enemies. Her summoning day is [Marukh's Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukh%27s_Day), the 9<sup>th</sup> of Second Seed.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Namira.md).

## Nocturnal
[![ON-concept-Prince-Nocturnal-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/ON-concept-Prince-Nocturnal-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Nocturnal-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Nocturnal-emblem.png) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) is a Daedric Prince, or "Daedric Princess", whose sphere is the night, darkness, and mystery. She is also known as the **Lady of the Night**, the **Mistress of Shadows**, the **Unfathomable Mistress of Shadows**, **the Shadow Thief**, **the Unfathomable**, the **Empress of Murk**, the **Shadow Queen**, the **Shadowy Hag** (while donning her [cowl](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md)), the **Lady of the Twilight**, the **Daughter of Twilight**, the **Lady of Shadows**, the **Mistress of Mystery**, the **Mother of Shadows**, **Lady Luck**, the **Night Mistress**, the **Prince of Night and Darkness**, and the **Saint of Suspicion**.

The most prestigious title Nocturnal goes by is the title of **[Ur-dra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ur-dra)**, which implies that she is the "eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes", and is a title which the [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) claims nearly all the Royalty of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) recognize, and which has also been used to address Nocturnal by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). The title was also used by ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) to describe [Namiira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Namiira), the Eldest Spirit. While this belief conflicts with Nocturnal's claim over the title, it does complement her claim that she is part of the original void, in that she came from the [black blood](Lore_Ebony.md) of the [Dark Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart) of [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj), which is a piece of "primal Void". Nocturnal herself claims that "before Oblivion, there was Nocturnal", and her followers believe she predates the current [reality](Lore_Aurbis.md) entirely. [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) is similarly called an Ur-Daedra who is "elder than Ada".

Nocturnal is said to be capable of shaping the night. She is associated with, and often depicted alongside, jet-black [ravens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Raven) and [crows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow), which are said to possess the ability to speak. Nocturnal's summoning day is the 3rd of Hearth Fire, although she may be summoned at any time at a shrine dedicated to her. Nocturnal's enemies are [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm), [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura).

## Peryite
[![ON-concept-Prince-Peryite-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/73/ON-concept-Prince-Peryite-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Peryite-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Peryite-emblem.png) [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) (listen Link ⏵) is one of the Daedric Princes that dwell within the realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). As the **Daedric Prince of Pestilence**, his sphere of influence includes tasks, natural order (not to be confused with the perfect order of [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag)), contamination, and pestilence. Peryite's sphere is described as "ordering the lowest orders of Oblivion". He is also known as the **Daedra of Pestilence and Plague**, the **Taskmaster**, the **Blighted Lord**, **Figuring father**, the **Lord of Infection and Pestilence**, the **Bringer of Disease and Pestilence**, the **Lord of the Natural Order**, the **Master of Tasks**, and the **Lord of Abundant Pus and Bountiful Vomit**. Peryite "blesses" his worshipers with [diseases](Lore_Disease.md), and is considered one of the more destructive Princes. He is typically depicted as a green four-legged [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), and often takes on the form of one, with the likeness to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) seen as some primordial and curious jest. Peryite occasionally appears as ghostly apparitions of vermin, such as [skeevers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skeever). The coiling, snake-like dragon and the skeever are two of Peryite's most sacred symbols. The [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) is said to give Peryite the spirits of skeevers when they die. According to some of his followers, Peryite abhors heterogeny, thus offerings of disease given to him should always be of the same strain.

Peryite is considered one of the weakest of the Princes.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Peryite.md).

## Sanguine
[![ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1b/ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Sanguine-emblem.png) [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine), known to his worshippers as the **Lord of Revelry**, **Blood-Made-Pleasure**, and he **who tastes the Shaven Fruit**, is a Daedric Prince whose sphere is hedonistic revelry, debauchery, wild orgies, and passionate indulgences of darker natures. As the **Daedra Prince of Lust, Perversity, and Unnatural Sexual Relations**, anything in excess is his motto. Sanguine is depicted as a portly yet muscular crimson-skinned man with a demonic head with horns, tusks, usually with a [bottle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alcohol) in his hand or a [whore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prostitution) under his thumb. He is thought to control thousands of small realms called the [Myriad Realms of Revelry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Revelry).

For more information, see the [main lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine).

## Sheogorath
[![ON-concept-Prince-Sheogorath-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/ON-concept-Prince-Sheogorath-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Sheogorath-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Sheogorath-emblem.png) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) is the Daedric Prince of [Madness](Lore_Madness_(disease).md), **Fourth Corner of the [House of Troubles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Troubles)**, the **Skooma Cat**, **Lord of the Never-There**, **Raver**, **Comforter of Men**, **The Trickster**,and **Sovereign of [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md)**, **Lord of the Creative**, **Lord of the Deranged**. His sphere is madness and creativity, and his motives are said to be unknowable. He is sometimes referred to as the **[Mad Star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Stars)**, the **Mad Lord**, the **Mad One**, the **Mad God** (or **Dam Dog**), and **Uncle Sheo** among other things. His realm, best known as the Shivering Isles, has also been called the Madhouse. It's believed that those who go there lose their sanity forever.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb_2)</sup> Of course, only the Mad God himself may decide who has the privilege to enter. The [Golden Saints](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aureal), or Aureals, and [Dark Seducers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mazken), or Mazken, are his servants. The Mad God typically manifests on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) as a seemingly harmless, well-dressed man often carrying a cane, a guise so prevalent it has actually been coined "**Gentleman With a Cane**". "Fearful obeisance" of Sheogorath is widespread in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and he plays an important part in [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) religious practice. Sheogorath's summoning day falls on [Mad Pelagius day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mad_Pelagius), which is the 2nd of Sun's Dawn. However, there is a chance he can be summoned on another Daedric Prince's summoning day if there is a storm on that day.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Sheogorath.md).

## Vaermina
[![ON-concept-Prince-Vaermina-emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-concept-Prince-Vaermina-emblem.png/150px-ON-concept-Prince-Vaermina-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Vaermina-emblem.png) [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina) (also spelled **Vaernima**), called the **Prince of Dreams and Nightmares**, the **Gifter**, the **Mistress of Nightmares** **and Dark Portents**, the **Lady of Nightmares**, the **Weaver of Dreams**, the **Queen of Nightmares**, **Weaver of the Panoply**, **Daedra Lady of Corruption and Decay**, the **Dreamweaver** and the **Dark Lady** is a [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) whose sphere is the realm of dreams and nightmares, and from whose realm evil omens issue forth, as well as corruption and decay. All mortals come under her sway as they sleep. She is considered one of the more "demonic" Daedra in that she is destructive for the sake of causing destruction, her method being torture. She is disgusted by mortals who maintain a moral compass, instead taking delight in those who commit outrageous sins or are unafraid to kill in pursuit of power. Laymen know her for her reputation as the Daedric Prince of nightmares and evil dreams.

Vaermina's [plane of Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Oblivion) is [Quagmire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quagmire), described by observers as a nightmarish land. It is said Vaermina hungers for the memories of mortals, collecting them from her citadel at the center of the realm, and leaving them with visions of horror and despair. Every few minutes, reality shifts and becomes ever more horrifying. It is not known what Vaermina does with these memories, but it is assumed to be malevolent.

Vaermina is said to be an enemy of [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), and [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), but an ally of [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine). She is also at odds with [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura). Her summoning day coincides with the [Merchants' Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Merchants_Festival), the 10<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Height.

For more information, see the [main lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina).
