# Five Companions

For the Merethic Era band of Atmorans, see [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-concept-achievement-Council_of_the_Five_Companions.jpg/200px-ON-concept-achievement-Council_of_the_Five_Companions.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-achievement-Council_of_the_Five_Companions.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-achievement-Council_of_the_Five_Companions.jpg) Four of the Five Companions; Abnur Tharn, Sai Sahan, Lyris Titanborn, and Varen Aquilarios, c. 2E 582 The Five Companions were a group of adventurers who embarked on a quest to rediscover the lost [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). The Companions were led by Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), who desired to use the Amulet of Kings to rekindle the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) in a ritual aimed at persuading [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to alter his heritage and make him a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md), and thus a legitimately-ordained ruler of the Empire. The other four Companions consisted of some of the greatest fighters and minds of that time. [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md), a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warrior said to have the blood of [giants](Lore_Giant.md), served Varen as his personal bodyguard. [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md), the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) Captain of Varen's [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), was charged with protecting the group. [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), Chancellor of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and Varen's advisor, accompanied Varen in his capacity as [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md). Lastly, there was [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), a legendary necromancer who held considerable influence with Varen and claimed to know how to conduct the Dragonfire ritual.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>

For years after [Varen's conquest of the Empire](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md), the Five Companions traveled the land in search of the elusive artifact. In [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), having successfully located the amulet and returned to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), Varen and the Companions initiated the ritual with the Amulet. It was then that Mannimarco revealed his treachery and corrupted the ritual to break the ancient covenant between Akatosh and mortals, triggering a catastrophe which the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) named the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md). A powerful explosion of magical energy swept across [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), setting the plane adrift in the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCA-2)</sup> and rendering it vulnerable to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s plans to merge Nirn and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) in the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>

Varen vanished in the chaos of the Soulburst, allegedly destroyed in the blast. In the immediate aftermath, Sai Sahan managed to secure the Amulet of Kings and fled from the Imperial City to hide it, taking it to the ruins of the city of [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md), the burial site of the [Reman emperors](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). Mannimarco made Lyris Titanborn his captive, and Abnur Tharn, seeking to preserve his status, pledged his loyalty to Mannimarco and had his daughter, [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), declared Empress Regent in Varen's absence. The Five Companions and Varen's dream of reviving the Reman Empire came to an end, and the world was set on a course for the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>
