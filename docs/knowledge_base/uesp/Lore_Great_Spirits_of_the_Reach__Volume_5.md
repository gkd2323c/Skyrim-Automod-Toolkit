Great Spirits of the Reach: Volume 5 **by [Vashu gra-Morga](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Vashu_gra-Morga), Chief Daedrotheologist at the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)** *Lorkhan in Reach theology* *
---
Scholars often dismiss Reach theology as simple [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship, but the great spirits of the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) extend beyond the [Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Like many [human](Lore_Man.md) cultures, people of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) venerate [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) as well. They know him as [Lorkh](Lore_Lorkh.md), the Spirit of Man, the Mortal Spirit, or the Sower of Flesh.

According to Reach myths, Lorkh convinced the Spirit Queen, [Namira](Lore_Namira.md), to grant him a place in the infinite void where he could create a realm for wayward spirits. Rather than a vibrant paradise, Lorkh created a hard and painful place— [a realm that taught through suffering](Lore_Mundus.md). While some resent Lorkh's cruelty, most praise his wisdom. According to the Reachfolk, those who suffer most know best. Hardship is a means to wisdom and glory, and Lorkh provided hardship in ample supply.

Lorkh supposedly still walks among the mortals of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). He appears only once in a great while, braving the pain and sorrow of the cruel world he created to aid the Reachfolk in times of desperate need. My research indicates that the dreaded [briarheart](Lore_Briarheart.md) ritual may have begun as a reflection of that [immortal sacrifice](Lore_Dark_Heart.md).
