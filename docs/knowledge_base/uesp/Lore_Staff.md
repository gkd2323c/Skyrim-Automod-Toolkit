# Staff

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/SR-menu-Staves.jpg/200px-SR-menu-Staves.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-menu-Staves.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-menu-Staves.jpg) Staves [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/LG-avatar-Dark_Elf_Female_2.png/200px-LG-avatar-Dark_Elf_Female_2.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-avatar-Dark_Elf_Female_2.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-avatar-Dark_Elf_Female_2.png) A mage wielding wands (Legends) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png/200px-LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png) A shock staff (Legends) Staves (or **staffs**) are implements and weapons frequently wielded by practitioners of magic.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GGM-1)</sup> Staves may be imbued with individual enchantments and used regardless of the wielder's magical abilities.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Delmar-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> They may also serve as focal points for casting spells to assist traditional spellcasting, acting as a focus of power rather than its source.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-1SMS-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CM62-6)</sup> Various types of staves can be crafted and enchanted to accommodate different forms of magic. Staves designed for casting spells exist across all schools of magic, including [restoration](Lore_Restoration.md), [destruction](Lore_Destruction.md), [illusion](Lore_Illusion.md), [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md), [alteration](Lore_Alteration.md), [thaumaturgy](Lore_Thaumaturgy.md), or [mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Additionally, they represent a mage's status and hold considerable symbolic significance,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AMS-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Kud-Ei-8)</sup> evoking the qualities of leadership and authority.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CM113-9)</sup>

In addition to staves, other tools serve similar purposes, such as smaller **wands**,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DMI-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RMQ-11)</sup> which can also function as focal points for spells, or be enchanted themselves.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Lena-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-WSD-13)</sup> **Rods** are utilized for divination,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Cont Fork-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Treasures Rod-15)</sup> although they can also serve as tools for training in spellcasting.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AS-16)</sup> They also have more specific uses. Similar to staves and wands, rods can also bear singular enchantments.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lyranth-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Twisted Rod-18)</sup> Some rods are designed to control fire and serve as ceremonial tools.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BOT8-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-LTTA-20)</sup> Additionally, some are used to channel magic in similar manner to staves.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-QV-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Diagna Staff Rod-22)</sup> The tools utilized by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) to command their [animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) are often referred to as rods.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Dwemer Rod-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Skyrim Spider Rod-24)</sup>

Wands and rods are categorized as distinct implements from staves, forming part of the training equipment for [Shad Astula](Lore_Shad_Astula.md) adepts.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AS-16)</sup> Other types of items are also utilized as aids for channeling magic, such as [gauntlets](Lore_Psijic_Gauntlets.md) for members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Psijic Gloves-25)</sup> or tomes commonly used by [Arcanists](Lore_Arcanists.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-WAA-26)</sup>

Staves are also employed in close-quarters combat.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GGM-1)</sup> This is often the case when spellcasters find themselves in need to fight at short range.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-CM111-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CM107-28)</sup> In such scenarios, they function as blunt weapons.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-CM88-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CM105-30)</sup> On occasion, they are employed for defensive purposes.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-CM51-31)</sup> Some staves possess features enabling them to function to some degree as melee weapons. Depending on their design, staves provide a diverse range of uses, doubling as half-pikes, spears, harpoons, pole-axes, or tridents when the situation demands.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> A staff can also double as a barrier when on sentry duty,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CM49-32)</sup> or a pry bar.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TG36-33)</sup> Some staves also serve as crosiers,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-CM101-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MWUvoo-35)</sup> often having ceremonial functions in the societies where they are used.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-CM48-36)</sup> Some staves serve as symbols of office, like the Royal Staff of Anequina, which is used by the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) monarchs of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md),<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-RSOA-37)</sup> or [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md)'s Rod, a royal Redguard staff. Replicas of Fahara'jad's Rod are often given as gifts by [Forebear](Lore_Forebear.md) nobles to their children.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TFFRR-38)</sup>

## Enchantment
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/SR-activity-Staff_Enchanter.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Staff_Enchanter.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Staff_Enchanter.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Staff_Enchanter.jpg) A Dunmeri Staff Enchanter [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/SR-interior-Myrwatch_06.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Myrwatch_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Myrwatch_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Myrwatch_06.jpg) A Staff Enchanter [Enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md) staves is regarded as a challenging endeavor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Delmar-2)</sup> The Staff Enchanter serves as an arcane implement, distinct yet similar to the Arcane Enchanter used in general enchanting practices.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SSE-39)</sup> [Azra Nightwielder](Lore_Azra_Nightwielder.md), the original shadowmage, and [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) were both renowned for their mastery in staff enchanting. Azra Nightwielder pioneered the field of staff enchantment. However, his techniques eventually became regarded as rudimentary by Fourth Era standards, though he remains esteemed as a genius of his time. Neloth of House Telvanni extensively studied Azra's creations.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Neloth-40)</sup> However, this practice is not uncommon among mages both within,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Delmar-2)</sup> and outside the Mages Guild.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-WSD-13)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ESOGlyphs Ench-41)</sup>

Neloth of House Telvanni asserted that he was the creator of the Staff Enchanter, or stated that its creation required grand wizardry,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Neloth-40)</sup> although other variations of staff enchanters exist.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SSE-39)</sup> Additionally, the art of enchanting staves through alternative methods, such as the use of runestones and glyphs, is also practiced.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CM62-6)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ESOGlyphs Ench-41)</sup> [Heart stones](Lore_Heart_Stone.md) are employed in the enchantment method requiring Neloth's Staff Enchanter,<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SSE-39)</sup> yet there are other methods of enchanting staves that do not necessitate them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Delmar-2)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ESOGlyphs Ench-41)</sup>

## Culture
Numerous [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) cultures employ unique methods for crafting and enchanting staves.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-CM-63)</sup> Various cultures utilize different materials for their spellcasting staves, with commonly known options including maple, oak, ash, or elm.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-1SMS-5)</sup>

### Akaviri
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-concept-Dragonguard_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Dragonguard_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dragonguard_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dragonguard_Style.jpg) Dragonguard Staff Akaviri staves hold great value among their mages, often adorned with splendid and lavish decorations. Many featured gold leaf accents and impressive red gems. The hafts, made from a dense and flexible wood not found in Tamriel, are capable of deflecting even the force of a great axe.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-CM18-64)</sup> Some staves are embellished with metal rings affixed to their heads, producing a melodious and fearsome sound as the Akaviri moved—an eerie song that sent chills down one's spine. The purpose of these rings has been questioned, as the Akaviri of old were believed to be too practical to indulge in purely stylistic choices.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-CEAAS-65)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-CM92-66)</sup> Factions inspired by the Akaviri, like the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) and [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), were recognized for their unique style. They continued the tradition of using red gems and intricate decorations on their staves.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-CM76-67)</sup> Some likened these staves to storytellers, portraying a spell as a tale and the staff as the storyteller itself.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-HG-68)</sup> While the Akaviri may no longer be present in Tamriel, their descendants persist, and their cultural legacy endures. Charged and storm staves they left behind can still be found in Tamriel, with some being safeguarded in Dragonguard vaults.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-69)</sup>

Akaviri deities Myn and Zisa were linked to a powerful staff and an elegant wand, respectively.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TOTEAkaviri-43)</sup>

### Altmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/LG-cardart-Rayvat_the_Mage.png/200px-LG-cardart-Rayvat_the_Mage.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Rayvat_the_Mage.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Rayvat_the_Mage.png) Altmeri Staff The Altmer are renowned for their spellcasters,<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Skyrim Race-70)</sup> with two of their deities, Xarxes and Syrabane, often depicted wielding staves.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-XAO-57)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Syrabane Statue-58)</sup>

Sapiarch staves are adorned with upper ends that echo the shape of the symbolic home of the Altmer, the Crystal Tower. Atop these staves are Magicka-flow brackets holding a finial Nirn-globe, symbolizing their global leadership in all arts of scholarship and knowledge. The sphere also acts as a focusing lens for magical projection.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-CMSapiarch-71)</sup> While some accounts in historical fiction suggest that Sapiarchs only carry a staff and a dagger,<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-KEP4-72)</sup> they have been observed utilizing various metal armaments.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-CMSapiarch-71)</sup>

The staves of the Welkynars, the knightly order of Cloudrest, are topped with stylized gryphon wings, resembling the wings of a knight's flying mount during a power dive. This shape also evokes the focus of a spellcaster's will. The long ash-wood haft of these staves typically ends in a sharp ferrule.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Welkynar-73)</sup>

Members of the Divine Prosecution and soldiers of the Aldmeri Dominion are known for their special designs of staves, each reflecting their respective organizations' values and purposes.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-DPS-74)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Covenant Pactand Dominion-75)</sup>

Clan Direnni is famous for its unique armaments, including staves designed by Raven Direnni herself. These staves feature two gracefully curved blades that come to points over a middle prong, giving the impression that the staff itself is ready to take flight. The metal on the staff's end also aids in navigating rough terrain.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Ancestral Alt-76)</sup>

Certain Altmer staves are crafted from wood harvested from Silatar Island.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-77)</sup>

Some Altmeri rods are designed to control fire and are typically used to maintain flames in Altmeri shrines as ceremonial tools.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BOT8-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-LTTA-20)</sup>

### Argonian
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-crown_store-Voriplasm_Staff.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Voriplasm_Staff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Voriplasm_Staff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Voriplasm_Staff.jpg) Voriplasm infused staff During ancient times, staves held a significant role as instruments of the Sithis priesthood. These bronze rods were adorned with icons and symbols of the Great Serpent, the deity to whom the priests offered sacrifices. Some staves even featured razor-sharp, glass teeth designed for bloodshed during various ceremonies. The historical evidence suggests that Sithis played a more prominent role in ancient Argonian worship compared to the present day, especially among most tribes.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Elder-78)</sup> The [Rootmender's Staff](Lore_Rootmender%27s_Staff.md) is an ancient Argonian artifact.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Da DESO-79)</sup>

Modern Argonian staff designs, much like their ancient counterparts, vary based on the tribe. The staff of the [Dead-Water Tribe](Lore_Dead-Water_Tribe.md), for example, is as formidable as their blades. Its amber gem is infused with the [sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md) of the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), absorbing its power. These staves are crafted using the bones of fallen mages, a process believed to transfer their magical essence to the weapon.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Water-80)</sup> Some of these staves were infused with [voriplasm](Lore_Voriplasm.md) essence.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Voriplasm-81)</sup> The staves of the [Sul-Xan](Lore_Sul-Xan.md) tribe feature large spikes that provide additional advantages in melee combat. The heads of these staves are adorned with jade and a disturbing visage, similar to the masks worn by the tribe. There was speculation that these depictions may represent a revered ancestor.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Sul-82)</sup> [Xit-Xaht](Lore_Xit-Xaht.md) of [Mazzatun](Lore_Mazzatun.md) also had their unique staves. Distinguishing between a ceremonial staff and a battle staff among the Mazzatun inhabitants was challenging. They are often crafted from fallen branches of their tribe's Hist tree. The heads of these staves are adorned with animal and other skulls, each chosen for its significance to the wielder.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-Maz-83)</sup>

Staves used by [Keshu](Lore_Keshu_the_Black_Fin.md)'s forces, the Black Fin Legion, showcase an Imperial influence in their design. The challenging terrain and close-quarters nature of skirmishes in the Black Marsh necessitate a dual-purpose approach for every legionnaire, whether archer, footsoldier, or spellcaster. To cater to close combat situations, Black-Fin Legion staves are equipped with lead striking heads and robust brass hafts, ensuring spellcasters are prepared for melee encounters.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-BFL-84)</sup>

It was known for its potent abilities to drain stamina and unleash powerful lightning enchantments.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-SOEE-86)</sup> The Staff of Nenalata is another notable Ayleid staff.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Quest Oblivion Ayleid-87)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Staff_of_Nenalata-88)</sup> Certain ancient Ayleid staves were known to cause their wielders to hear faint, beautiful singing.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-89)</sup>

Any spell channeled through such a weapon carries the full blessing of Z'en. These staves ensured that their wielders repay bloodshed in kind.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Stags-92)</sup> Some Bosmeri staves, referred to as Spiderstaves, were covered in sticky coatings,<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-93)</sup> while others were fashioned to emanate despair.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-94)</sup> [Vinedusk Rangers](Lore_Vinedusk_Rangers.md) also employed staves.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-95)</sup> Certain Bosmeri spell-eaters, including [Celedith](Lore_Celedith.md), were noted as staff wielders during the Imperial invasion of Valenwood in 1E 2712.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-PLP-96)</sup><sup>[[97]](#cite_note-PJJ-97)</sup>

Rindir was a renowned Bosmer staff seller, acclaimed for offering the finest staves in the entire Imperial City during the late Third Era. Many mages vouched for the quality of the staves he sold.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-Rindir-100)</sup>

### Breton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-crown_store-Galenstone_Staff.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Galenstone_Staff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Galenstone_Staff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Galenstone_Staff.jpg) A Galenstone Staff Staves are commonly used by Breton mages throughout High Rock.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-FT-103)</sup> Bretons, regardless of their city,<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-CM114-104)</sup> druidic,<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-YW115-105)</sup> or wyrd origins, commonly utilize staves.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-GWS-106)</sup> Breton weaponry, including staves, draws inspiration from their Elven ancestry. These weapons are characterized by their elegance and beauty, while still possessing undeniable lethality.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Breton5-107)</sup> Early Breton mages crafted their staves from the wood of the [Wyrd Tree](Lore_Wyrd_Tree.md), a legendary plant with roots said to reach into the bones of the world. These staves are noted for their exceptional mana manipulation abilities, surpassing those made by the Sapiarchs.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-93AB-108)</sup>

The details about staves of the Wyrd are rare, with only hints of avian imagery among the staves of the [Glenmoril Wyrd](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md).<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-GWS-106)</sup> However, druids are recognized for utilizing a diverse array of staff styles, each associated with their respective circle. Druids as a whole believe that even after being cut and hewn, the wood they use remains alive and aware, evident in their staves which rhythmically thrum as if still trying to grow, breathe, and draw water from the earth beneath. Some druidic staves were adorned with stone designs, reflecting their connection to nature and the earth.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Galenstone-109)</sup> Specifically, Druids of the Firesong circle favor skarn, carving their heads with the emblem of the ever-watchful gryphon, channeling elemental ferocity against their foes. They take great pride in their staves, considering them exemplary. Further details regarding differences in staves among the other two circles are scarce.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-YW115-105)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-FS117-110)</sup>

City Bretons of Systes, particularly from Gonfalon Bay's Guard, utilize staves with a unique double-pointed design as a practical deterrent against attackers. The double points serve as a menacing feature, amplified by the crackling of lightning or fire for added intimidation.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-CM114-104)</sup> Breton knighthood frequently included staff-wielding knights, known as spellknights, alongside traditional warriors. The Ascendant Order equips its members with sturdy steel rods featuring dark leather grips, suitable for dealing deadly blows in melee combat when needed. Battlemages of the Order of the Silver Rose rely on silvered-steel staves as their primary tool, enabling them to endure melee combat and deliver potent strikes against mortal and Daedra-tainted adversaries. The silhouette of a rosebud on these staves symbolizes the latent magical potential awaiting manifestation. Members of the Oaken Order, known for their preference for magical combat, possess uniquely designed staves tailored to their distinctive style.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-SR106-111)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-CM111-27)</sup> Individual noble houses occasionally developed their own unique designs for staves, as seen with House Mornard.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-HM118-112)</sup> Bretons are known to use Staves of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) to combat undead.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-FL-113)</sup>

### Daedra

### Dunmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-crown_store-Necrom_Armiger_Staff.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Necrom_Armiger_Staff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Necrom_Armiger_Staff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Necrom_Armiger_Staff.jpg) A Necrom Armiger Staff The Dunmer are renowned for their rich magical culture, a tradition that extended back to their predecessors, the Chimer, who also used staves and wands.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-ESOChimer-118)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-Tales OFTamriel-119)</sup> Different groups within Dunmer society, including the various Great Houses,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-CM51-31)</sup><sup>[[120]](#cite_note-CM52-120)</sup> Sotha Sil's disciples, known as the Apostles,<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-APO-121)</sup> members of diverse Dunmeri organizations and orders devoted to religion,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CM49-32)</sup>, knighthood,<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-CM47-122)</sup> and legal assasination,<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-CM30-123)</sup> as well as tribal Ashlanders, each have their own distinct styles when it came to crafting of staves.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-CM48-36)</sup> Dunmeri staves exhibit elegance but are reflective of far more harsh and rigid environments than those of Altmer. Dunmeri artifice draws inspiration from the curved and spiky forms of the carapaces of the giant insects that inhabit Morrowind. While elegant, these staves also evoke a sense of fear and intimidation.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-CM2-124)</sup> Some staves are crafted from bonemold,<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-Bonemold Style-125)</sup> while others utilize remnants of [dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md), malachite, silver, ebony, wood, or steel.<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-Staves Morrowind-126)</sup>

[House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), renowned for their mercantile endeavors, extended their symbolism to their staves as well. A Hlaalu mage's spell staff features a finial shaped like the House's double-scales symbol, proudly showcasing the wielder's affiliation with the world-famous Morrowind mercantile masters. The foot of the staff is sheathed in metal with a heavy ferrule, capable of serving as defensive weaponry when necessary.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-CM51-31)</sup> Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) wielded a staff adorned with an ornate jewel on its headpiece, similar in design to the jewels of [her crown](Lore_Crown_of_Barenziah.md).<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-Barenziah-127)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-Skyrim Barenziah-128)</sup>

While predominantly warriors, [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) also houses mages.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-Morrowind Redoran-129)</sup><sup>[[130]](#cite_note-ESORedoran-130)</sup> Their spell staves sport finials resembling the shalk-mandible horns of their helmets, designed to detach easily during close combat and reattach after battle without damage.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-CM52-120)</sup>

[House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), dedicated to magical research, boasts powerful mages. Their spell staves feature finials adorned with the House symbol crafted in gilded silver, ensuring that Telvanni wizards are unmistakably identified, even when not wearing their uniform.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-CM50-131)</sup> Dunmer of Bal Sunnar, adhering to the guidance of Matriarch Lladi Telvanni, ensured that the staves carried by their mages bore a marking resembling a star breaking through the night, fastened with gold. The head of the staff is split like two arrowheads, symbolizing their Matriarch's departure and her blessed return, united in unwavering commitment.<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-BI119-132)</sup> Silver Staves of Peace serve as the traditional patron's gifts to their protege within House Telvanni, often associated with promotion to the rank of Mouth.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-EFET-133)</sup><sup>[[134]](#cite_note-Morrowind Mouth Quests-134)</sup>

Less is known about the staves of [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md),<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-GIStaff-135)</sup> [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md),<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-Dres Generic-136)</sup> and the Sixth House, [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md), but all three houses had mages who utilized staves.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-Dagoth ESO-137)</sup><sup>[[138]](#cite_note-Zula-138)</sup>

The Ashlanders used spell staves not only as a magical weapons but also as a ceremonial crosiers, laden with symbolism meaningful to their culture. Each staff is adorned with one, two, or even three symbols of the Good Daedra, encircled by a double crescent of chitin and bone. The haft of the staff terminates in a pointed ferrule akin to that of a mace.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-CM48-36)</sup> Ashkan [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md) wielded and utilized Sunna'rah to temporarily drain the divine powers of the Tribunal.<sup>[[139]](#cite_note-Chodala-139)</sup><sup>[[140]](#cite_note-AT-140)</sup>

Staves were considered among the preferred weapons of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md),<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-FOTT-141)</sup> with some, like the [Crosier of St. Llothis](Lore_Crosier_of_St._Llothis.md), being associated with significant figures in the faith.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MWUvoo-35)</sup> Dunmer religious texts occasionally make reference to staves. For instance, *[Sermon 17](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_17.md)* of *[the Thirty-Six Lessons of Vivec](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md)* recounts an instance where Nerevar attempted to fashion a staff from eight spokes. Vivec responded by stating that it was not the appropriate time for such an endeavor and asserted that he himself was the wheel.<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-SOV17-142)</sup> The weaponry of the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md) holds a profound connection to the content of Vivec's Sermons. Inspired by passages from Sermon 17th, their staves are designed to be singular in shaft and to embody contradictions in their very essence.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-CM47-122)</sup> The design of Buoyant Armigers' staves in Necrom differed from those found in Vvardenfell.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-NAS-143)</sup> The [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) also employed staves in their duties. The staff of a Militant Ordinator of the Order of War spell-caster is topped with dual sweeping crests resembling those on their helms, pointed and parallel, guiding their righteous magical onslaughts. They also served as a horizontal barrier to entry when used by sentries guarding doors or portals.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CM49-32)</sup> The Order of Doctrine, known for their distinctive style, arms their militant scholars with staves that both intimidate and silence those who spread false teachings.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-Doctrine-144)</sup> Dunmeri militias were also known to utilize staves in their operations.<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-AMStyle-145)</sup> The [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) was not renowned for its spellcasters, as there were only a handful within the organization. Rarely, the Tong would utilize staves with finials resembling heavy brass Dwarven fish, although little else is known about their designs.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-CM30-123)</sup>

[Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) initially drew inspiration for his [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) technology from the Dwemer, but over time, he developed a more distinctive style. Staves associated with decommissioned [factotums](Lore_Factotum.md) of the D-series were thought to be most effective with a focal finial made from the soul gem bezel setting of a spider construct, be it ancient or modern. Surprisingly, the gripping element from a bread slice carbonizer was found to work nearly as effectively.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-Refabricated-146)</sup> More modern designs, used by the Apostles of Sotha Sil from the 2nd era up until the 4th,<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-GTS-147)</sup> featured a perfect brass sphere as the focus, similar to their maces. However, unlike the Apostle mace, this sphere rotates on a central axle of the staff, enhancing the balance and reflecting the perfection of Lord Seht. Additionally, it was noted that spinning it provides hours of pleasant idle distraction for children and pets.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-APO-121)</sup>

Crafted from a close-grained material resembling wood but not quite, the haft is encircled by bands of Dwarven metal. The finials, typically circular or fan-shaped, are modest in size.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Dwemer Style15-148)</sup> Although there are no records indicating Dwarves worked with wood, their staves are fashioned from a lightweight yet durable metal alloy. The headpieces often resemble small mechanisms, though their actual function in enhancing Dwarven staves remains a mystery.<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-AAS96-149)</sup> Designs varied depending on the region and settlement, such as those found in Rkindaleft,<sup>[[150]](#cite_note-RDS-150)</sup> Rkugamz, Vault of Mhuvnak,<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-SORStyle-151)</sup> or Arkthzand.<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-AAS96-149)</sup>

### Other

Factions such as alliances during wars like [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), and [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) were recognized for their unique styles and choices of materials for staff making.<sup>[[260]](#cite_note-ABBS-260)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Covenant Pactand Dominion-75)</sup>

A formal initiation into the Mages Guild entails utilizing a staff crafted and enchanted by the guild, serving as a symbol of one's status. The specific enchantment imbued within the staff is determined by the preference of the initiating mage. Additionally, the wood utilized for crafting these staves originates from groves maintained by guild mages, including one located northeast of the Imperial City near [Wellspring Cave](Lore_Wellspring_Cave.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AMS-7)</sup> Staves sometimes bear more symbolic than practical roles, yet they often hold great significance to their owners.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Kud-Ei-8)</sup>

Staves are crafted from the corpses of both [Hadolid](Lore_Hadolid.md) and Dreugh, yet neither of these races are known to use staves or wands themselves.<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-Staves Morrowind-126)</sup><sup>[[262]](#cite_note-EGS-262)</sup>

## Notes
- | [1.](#note_nb1) | Sources from the [second](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[263]](#cite_note-ESO-263)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-1SMS-5)</sup> [third](Lore_Third_Era.md),<sup>[[264]](#cite_note-Battlespire Book-264)</sup> and [fourth eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) all mention staves of restoration.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup> They are often carried into battle as a supplementary tool for healing wounds.<sup>[[265]](#cite_note-RHH-265)</sup> Similarly, staves of destruction are observed throughout the second,<sup>[[263]](#cite_note-ESO-263)</sup> third,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Delmar-2)</sup> and fourth eras.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup> Accounts about illusion staves are more sparse, but they are referenced during the third,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Delmar-2)</sup> and fourth eras.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup> Accounts from the fourth era also mention staves of conjuration and alteration.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup> Third era accounts also make reference to staves associated with the disciplines of mysticism,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Delmar-2)</sup> and thaumaturgy.<sup>[[264]](#cite_note-Battlespire Book-264)</sup> |
| --- | --- |

- | [2.](#note_nb2) | Certain staves with sharp finials were equipped with one or more sharp spikes for combat purposes, a trait particularly notable in Argonian staves used by the Sul-Xan tribe.<sup>[[266]](#cite_note-CM102-266)</sup> Followers of Keshu also adopted this practice.<sup>[[267]](#cite_note-CM103-267)</sup> Certain staves employed by the Aldmeri Dominion military are notable for their resemblance to pole-axes. Many arcane warriors have utilized them as such, particularly in challenging situations.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Covenant Pactand Dominion-75)</sup> Some staves utilized by mercenary spellcasters featured finial ends with one or more sharp spikes, allowing the staff to function as a half-pike when needed.<sup>[[268]](#cite_note-CM19-268)</sup> The True-Sworn mercenaries used staves with heads shaped nearly like tridents, a design choice influenced by pragmatic considerations aligned with their tasks.<sup>[[259]](#cite_note-100TS-259)</sup> A Maormer pirate leader [Taleria](Lore_Taleria.md) wielded a staff that could double as a harpoon during the Second Era. She mandated that any member of her crew with a talent for magic must master a stave similar to her own, serving both for spellcasting and as a harpoon.<sup>[[269]](#cite_note-CM110-269)</sup> The staves utilized by the [Silken Ring](Lore_Silken_Ring.md), a cult dedicated to Mephala, featured spear-like heads, albeit blunt.<sup>[[256]](#cite_note-SR44-256)</sup> Some staves wielded by sailor mages are crafted with the head resembling a three-pronged fish spear. However, certain sources do not advocate for any practical utility with this design.<sup>[[270]](#cite_note-CM116-270)</sup> Staff replicas resembling famous spears like the Spear of Bitter Mercy did exist, but their effectiveness in melee combat remained uncertain.<sup>[[271]](#cite_note-SOBMS-271)</sup> |
