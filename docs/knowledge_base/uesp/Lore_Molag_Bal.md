*Indiscriminate death. Set upon by the gnawing and the rabid. Cyrodiil's candle flickers and is snuffed. Servants and superiors -- no one is spared the sharp tooth, ridged blade, or diseased hook, barb, or beak. And Molag Bal will watch from his palace in Coldharbour where everything stares back at him with his own face, and he will gloat, and mock, and gorge on the souls of mortals.*
— [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) Molag Bal (Stone-Fire,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Meridia-1)</sup> or literally the "Fire Stone")<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Stone Fire)</sup> is the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) whose sphere is the domination and enslavement of mortals.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> His ultimate desire is to harvest the souls of all mortals, bringing them within his sway by spreading seeds of strife and discord in the mortal realms.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> To this end, he obsessively collects soul gems,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OX-7)</sup> and has even dragged pieces of Nirn into his realm to satisfy his insatiable desire for conquest.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The more souls he collects, the more he wants.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EGTColdharbour-9)</sup>

Molag Bal values patience and cunning. He often deceives those he deals with, and has no trouble waiting exceedingly long periods of time for his plans to come to fruition.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mo Viv-12)</sup> He also takes great pleasure in the suffering of mortals, and often has them tortured for his amusement.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TSPC-13)</sup> Those who fall into his clutches are forced to labor ceaselessly for his benefit.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOHOTLoad-14)</sup> The Prince has a penchant for necromancy,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Val Jou-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-UL-16)</sup> often employing the use of the risen dead and forcing his followers to serve beyond the grave.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOPalolel-17)</sup> He is capable of preserving live mortals far beyond their normal lifespan to prolong their suffering.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LFTD-18)</sup>

Molag Bal is acknowledged as the father of several children, though it is unclear what those relationships mean to him.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-GGLST-19)</sup> He has a son, [Ozozzachar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ozozzachar),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MBal General-20)</sup> who is a [Daedric Titan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Titan).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Ozzozachar-21)</sup> He also has a daughter named [Molag Grunda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Grunda), a [Winged Twilight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winged_Twilight) who took the lowly [Frost Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Atronach) Nomeg Gwai as her consort. Molag Bal did not approve of the relationship and had them both banished to Oblivion so he could punish them for eternity.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWVamp-22)</sup> A persistent legend about [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md) claims he is the progeny of Molag Bal and a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) woman.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-DCSG-23)</sup>

Molag Bal's enemies are [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm),<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DFFactions-24)</sup> [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-THOH-25)</sup> [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia),<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TLO-26)</sup> [Lamae Bal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamae_Bal)<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOLamae-27)</sup> and [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay). He was at one point allied with [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DFFactions-24)</sup> His summoning day is during [Chil'a](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chil%27a), on the 20th of Evening Star.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-28)</sup> He appears before his cultists in the guise of a mortal when invoked on other dates.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Invoc Az-29)</sup>

It is rumored among the [kyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) who serve him that his second-favorite type of [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) after the mace is the axe, as it is the tool of the headsman. However, nobody is brave enough to ask him about it.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CM63D-30)</sup> He purportedly has terrible hygiene and smells of rotting flesh.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Irrai-31)</sup>

## Titles
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/ON-statue-Coldharbour_Sculpture.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Coldharbour_Sculpture.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Coldharbour_Sculpture.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Coldharbour_Sculpture.jpg) A sculpture of slaves being tortured in Coldharbour Mortals have often used descriptors and alternate names to refer to the Daedric Prince in conversation. Molag Bal is unique in that he is known by an exceedingly large amount of nicknames, some used by specific groups for different reasons. He is most commonly referred to as the **God of Schemes**,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-COT5C1-32)</sup> the **Harvester of Souls**,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-OBYushi-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-CTOC-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-JOC-35)</sup> the **Lord of Domination**,<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Go SMB-36)</sup> the **Prince of Domination**<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ESOMace Style-37)</sup> and the **Lord of Brutality**.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONAfneer-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Stone Rit Tom-39)</sup> These terms are comparatively neutral, and can be used by anyone to describe him, from normal citizens to his greatest enemies and his most devout worshippers. These titles are descriptive, and utilize aspects of his sphere of influence to identify him as the subject of conversation. The **Dark Lord** is another neutral name, used by fearful slaves in hushed whispers, and uttered by the Prince's enemies in defiance.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESOJurisa-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Tzik TFA-42)</sup> Molag Bal's minions use the name in reverence.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-OCL-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ESOAmbient-44)</sup>

Several other nicknames which the mortals of Tamriel have identified Molag Bal with include the **Harvester**,<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ESOAbnab-45)</sup> the **Lord of Corruption**,<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-46)</sup><sup>:293</sup> the **Corrupter**, the **Tormentor of Men**, the **Corrupter of Souls**,<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-OBAmir-47)</sup> the **Lord of Troubles**,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-OJOGR-48)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-49)</sup> the **Prince of Schemes**,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-50)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Danys TWD-51)</sup> the **God of Brutality**,<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-52)</sup> the **Sower of Strife**<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-53)</sup> and the **Scheming Lord of Coldharbour**.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-54)</sup> These nicknames share a common theme in that they describe Molag Bal's sphere of influence. These titles also draw from his most easily recognizable behaviors. Molag Bal is known to harvest souls, torment mortals for fun and corrupt the minds of good people with promises of power.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-HLBJ-55)</sup> He hatches elaborate schemes to spread his influence across Tamriel. The **Prince of Murder** is a more vague title that has been affixed to the Prince by the servants of his enemy, Meridia.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TG-56)</sup> The title does not frame murder as an aspect of his sphere in the way that it falls under Mephala's divine portfolio; rather, it illustrates the idea that Molag Bal is an individual of an unpleasant nature. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an ally of the Army of Meridia referred to Molag Bal as the **Dark One**.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Tzik TFA-42)</sup>

The **King of Rape**<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-THOT-57)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-LOV12-58)</sup> is a title that is used in the Living God [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md)'s sermons. It draws upon Molag Bal's brutal nature and, at least in *[The House of Troubles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_House_of_Troubles)*, refers to literal rape and his desire to "ruin the Dunmer gene pool".<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-THOT-57)</sup> The **Elder Spirit of Domination and Supreme Law** is a moniker derived from the epistles of [Amun-dro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amun-dro), an ancient Khajiit priest who committed the names of the old Khajiit deities to paper.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TAS-59)</sup> [Carlovac Townway](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Carlovac_Townway) referred to Molag Bal as the **Prince of Rage** in his popular historical fiction novel, *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era)*.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-2920v4-60)</sup> [Sigillah Parate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sigillah_Parate) identified Molag Bal as the **Schemer Princess** in her work, *[Invocation of Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Invocation_of_Azura)*.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Invoc Az-29)</sup>

**Molag the Slave-Lord**<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Ot No C-61)</sup> is a one-off nickname used by [Phrastus of Elinhir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phrastus_of_Elinhir) in his lecture on the nature of Molag Bal's plane of Oblivion, Coldharbour. The name is in reference to Molag Bal's voracious desire to amass unfathomable amounts of souls, and his tendency to enslave those whose souls he comes into possession of. The **Schemer**,<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TCC-62)</sup> the **Prince of Pain** and the **Captain of Corruption** are monikers written in popular bardic performances from the Interregnum. *The Plane Meld* was a song that described the impending doom from the then-ongoing Planemeld. It likens Molag Bal to some sort of demented farmer, tilling the land of Tamriel and sowing corruption as far as he can reach.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ESOBards-63)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/ON-place-Bal_Ur.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bal_Ur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bal_Ur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bal_Ur.jpg) Bal Ur, a temple of great importance to Molag Bal's followers on Vvardenfell The **Dark Father**,<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ESORahaja-64)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-65)</sup> the **Father of Vampires**,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-ESONoryon-66)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ESOFreed-67)</sup> **Lord of the Undead**,<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-CM60-68)</sup> and the **Father of Undeath**<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EGTColdharbour-9)</sup> are nicknames derived from Molag Bal's association with [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and the [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead). Regardless of whether or not they revere him, vampires who recognize Molag Bal as the progenitor of the first vampire may refer to him as the Dark Father. Scholars and laymen who are familiar with Molag Bal's association with vampires or his propensity for raising the dead may refer to him as either of the latter.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ESORahaja-64)</sup> The **Defiler**<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOLamae-27)</sup> is a title of note among the scions of [Lamae Bal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamae_Bal), the first vampire. The vampires of Lamae's coven are encouraged to despise Molag Bal, and they acknowledge him as the one responsible for their matron's state of undeath.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ESORS-69)</sup> The scions of Lamae have also referred to Molag Bal as the **Father of Torment** and the **King of Corruption**<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ESORS-69)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-SRLogrolf-70)</sup> in light of the notion that Molag Bal takes great delight in the suffering of mortals, and actively seeks to corrupt anything he believes will further his goals or spread strife throughout Nirn.

The **Lord of Lies**,<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-NMV-71)</sup> the **Master of Domination**<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-72)</sup> and the **Lord of Darkness**<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-LTDVolcat-73)</sup> are names that have been uttered by the Prince's adherents. The **Dark Master**<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-NMV-71)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ICXiv-74)</sup> and the **Dread Lord**<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-CMX-75)</sup> are titles which have been explicitly used by Molag Bal's cultists and Daedric minions in the context of their service. The Daedra who serve Molag Bal refer to him as "the Dread Lord", "the Dark Lord" and their "Brutal Lord"<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-76)</sup> in their missives. Servants both mortal and otherwise have identified their god with many variations on the words "lord" and "master", denoting the Prince's position of power above the denizens of his Plane.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-NMV-71)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-DDTT-77)</sup> An example of such a name is the **Lord of Schemes**.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-78)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-TEWObserver-79)</sup> The **Lord of Coldharbour** is a simple title that refers to his dominion over Coldharbour.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-ESOSkull-80)</sup>

## Religion and Culture

### Altmer
Alongside Azura and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), the Daedra worshiping [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) revered Molag Bal as one of their primary Daedric patrons. The Corelanyan [High Elves](Lore_Altmer.md) prayed to him for strength when the need to defend their livelihood arose.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Ho CC2-81)</sup> Molag Bal fell out of favor around [1E 2633](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2633), after the Three Queens rose as the Corelanyan's guardian triumvirate.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Ho CC5-82)</sup>

### Ayleid
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg/200px-OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Garlas_Agea.jpg) Ayleid statuette of Mola Gbal Molag Bal was known as **Mola Gbal** to the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md),<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ESOHo SLoad Txt-83)</sup> and was the patron deity of [Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abagarlas).<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-TWo D-84)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-85)</sup>

### Breton
The Bretons of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) share stories about the "Terrible [Torchbug](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torchbug) of Molag Bal" the night before [Waking Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waking_Day). The stories vary, but the one consistent part of every variation of the tale involves a fearsome, savage entity being followed by blue torchbugs.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Blue Torchbug-86)</sup>

### Dunmer
Molag Bal is a Daedric power of much importance in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), where he is always the archenemy of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), the Prince of Plots. He is seen as a primary obstacle to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) people (and their [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) predecessors). In the legends, Molag Bal always tries to upset the bloodlines of Houses or otherwise ruin Dunmeri "purity".<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-VOFIT-87)</sup> He is said to be the parent (along with [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)) of a population of [supermonsters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Monster_Children) that terrorized the [Molag Amur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Amur) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-LOV14-88)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-89)</sup>

He is known to the Dunmer as the third corner of the [House of Troubles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Troubles), and one of the Bad Daedra.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-MWThird Corner-90)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-DD-91)</sup> Dunmeri mythos cites [Bal Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bal_Ur) as the "birthplace of Molag Bal",<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-92)</sup> where the [Doom Drum](Lore_Lorkhan.md) tricked him into becoming mortal.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TSDM-93)</sup> Bal Ur is a place of great importance for Vvardenfell's vampire clans, particularly the [Aundae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aundae_Clan), who believe the ruin is important to their vampire heritage.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-OJOGR-48)</sup> In the days of old Resdayn, Molag Bal was not worshipped so much as he was placated.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-ESOBal Ur-94)</sup>

Molag Bal plays a pivotal role in the narrative of Vivec's famous sermons. The Prince and the Warrior-Poet parleyed in the wilds of Molag Amur,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-LOV12-58)</sup> an event which served as the catalyst for the creation of a variety of monsters which the tribune would gradually slay throughout the rest of the sermons. During their time together, Molag Bal taught the God-Poet the secret syllable of royalty, CHIM, which, in his lessons, Vivec described as essential to becoming a Ruling King of the world. Their meeting ended in treachery as great monsters arose from the earth in the wake of their union, and Vivec vanquished the Daedric Prince in kind.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-LOV14-88)</sup> The Shrine of Inevitable Secrets, a temple devoted to [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) in Morrowind, was dragged beneath the waves by dreugh. Some legends attribute the shrine's disappearance to Vivec and Molag Bal's fury upon learning that the Lord of Secrets had spied on them, removing his shrine from the world and disallowing more to be built as punishment.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-95)</sup>

### Dreugh
Molag Bal purportedly has connections to the [dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh). According to legend, the dreugh used to be far more intelligent and civilized than they are today. The dreugh were said to worship a creature known as the [Ruddy Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruddy_Man). Not much is known about this entity, but it is believed to be a manifestation of Molag Bal.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-CETD-96)</sup> Dunmeri folklore alleges that Molag Bal was the chieftain of the dreugh in a time when they ruled the world. During that time, Molag Bal took on a form that was spiny, armored, and built for life beneath the waves. The Ruddy Man from the teachings of the Tribunal Temple is said to resemble this iteration of Molag Bal, and was supposedly a product of Vivec and Molag Bal's union.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-Vivec28-97)</sup>

### Reachmen
*"Hail Molag Bal, the Prince of Pain
* *Brutal master, lord of troubles
* *Giver of strength to slay and strive
*

*For men are meant to struggle"*
— *From [Hail to the Ancient Spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hail_to_the_Ancient_Spirits), an old Reach song* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/87/ON-concept-Reachfolk_Molag_Bal.png/200px-ON-concept-Reachfolk_Molag_Bal.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Reachfolk_Molag_Bal.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Reachfolk_Molag_Bal.png) Reachfolk emblem for Molag Bal Molag Bal is venerated by some [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) clans. The people of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) recognize the Lord of Lies as a schemer who enjoys his tricks. Known as the **Father of Torment**, the **Defiler** and **He Who Schemes in the Shadows**, Molag Bal is a spirit that represents hardship.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-98)</sup> He's somewhat of a testing god, acknowledged as a spirit who brings trouble, and in doing so, he teaches the people of the Reach to endure.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-Fialyn-99)</sup>

The Reachfolk understand that hardship is inevitable, but instead of bemoaning their sorrows, they embrace the pain as wisdom.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-98)</sup> They look up to those who work hard,<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-ESOHereline-100)</sup> and believe those that are plagued by troubled times are strengthened by their experiences. Molag Bal grants nothing but unexpected pain. Those of the Reach who venerate Molag Bal appreciate the lessons he teaches, growing resilient in his shadow.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-98)</sup>

The [Cinder-Heart Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cinder-Heart_Clan) reveres the Defiler,<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-ONCHCC-101)</sup> and their practices reflect the extreme horrors of Molag Bal's sphere. They are known for their cruel treatment of captives, whom they burn alive. Their briarheart ritual is augmented with the intense pain of opening a subject's chest cavity and filling it with hot coals.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-102)</sup>

Reachmen are sometimes referred to as the Witchmen of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), a name that stems from their use of dark "Reach-magic".<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-103)</sup> Although [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) is believed to be the source of the dark nature magic used by Reach hedge wizards,<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-104)</sup> Molag Bal can grant similar power. The name "gravesinger" is worn by some Reachman necromancers who broker deals with Bal<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ONZK-105)</sup> in exchange for the power to command the loathsome dead.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-TIEGTTB-106)</sup> These gravesingers can command corpses by planting the seeds of foul [vines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flora_B#Bloodthorn_Vines) in their victims' bodies. Molag Bal's "gifts" may leave a gravesinger's body in a state of decay.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ESOAngof-107)</sup>

### Khajiit
Some ancient Khajiit tribes knew him as **Molagh**, one of the twelve Demon Kings and the Elder Spirit of Domination and Supreme Law. In some forms of Khajiiti mythos, Molagh has a wife who freed Merrunz to weaponize his destructive nature. Molagh was the first to attempt an assault on the Lunar Lattice with intent alongside Merrunz and Merid-Nunda. Boethra fought him to a standstill before the Lattice, and Azurah brought him down.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TAS-59)</sup> A pre-ri'Datta text known as *The Bladesongs of Boethra* describes the battle at the Lattice in greater detail, alleging that Molagh taunted Boethra with the severed head of [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj) to lure her away from guarding the Lunar Lattice, allowing Dagon and Merid-Nunda to break through the moonlight barrier.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-108)</sup> Amun-dro's writings say that he is to be faced along the Path and overcome by the Will Against Rule, a concept associated with Boethra. Molag Bal is among those the Epistles of Amun-dro refer to as the Adversarial Spirits.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TAS-59)</sup>

### Maormer
Little is known about [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) religion, but Keel Cutters, Maormer slave traders, were known to invoke the name of Molag Bal.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-CVST-109)</sup>

### Imperials
Daedra worship was not prohibited by law in Cyrodiil, but public opinion and the religious establishment were so staunchly opposed to the notion, that many who worshipped Daedra did so in secret.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-MH-110)</sup> Molag Bal has a shrine in Cyrodiil's [Great Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Forest),<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-OBMolag Bal Quest-111)</sup> and is listed as one of the 16 Acceptable Blasphemes in the first edition of the *Pocket Guide to the Empire*.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-PGE1EI-112)</sup>

### Philosophy
The Lord of Lies is the center of a longstanding philosophical debate known as the Molag Bal Paradox. Revolving around the notion that Molag Bal consistently defies the will of others (often suppressing their will by imposing his own upon them), the paradox poses the following question: "If everyone would express the belief that [Molag Bal] exists, would he therefore cease to do so because his nature demands he oppose the wishes of others?" Assuming that Molag Bal is subject to the metaphysical rules of the Aurbis, philosophers have argued whether or not the Prince "truly" embodies the concept of domination.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-Isayas-135)</sup> The Molag Bal Paradox is considered a classic puzzle, and scholars have engaged with it since at least the Second Era.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-Agmund-136)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/ON-place-The_Lost_Fleet.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Lost_Fleet.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Lost_Fleet.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Lost_Fleet.jpg) The remains of the All Flags Navy The ancient Daedric temple-city of Mor Naril, built sometime in early history on the island of [Solstice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Solstice), was dedicated to Molag Bal. His servants built it to harvest souls departing the mortal plane.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-ESOArvoril-137)</sup> The temple predates the Argonians' arrival to the island, who were called over across the sea to settle on Solstice before the event known as [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-138)</sup><sup>[[138]](#cite_note-TAECS-139)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-140)</sup> These Argonians considered the Daedra on the island their enemies, and constructed stone guardians known as [Voskrona](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voskrona_Guardian) to wage war on them.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-TCWTM-141)</sup>

Kinlady [Iniel Corelanya](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iniel_Corelanya) learned necromantic magics from Mor Naril during her ill-fated campaign to conquer Solstice in [1E 723](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_723). She later called upon Molag Bal during the Corelanyans' war against the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) in [1E 853](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_853).<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Ho CC2-81)</sup> Her necromantic exploits caused a schism in her clan betweeen those who rejected the Daedra and those unafraid to petition them for aid. The Corelanyans eventually came to reside on Solstice, coexisting peacefully with the native Argonians after several centuries of conflict. The [Peace of Xor-Hist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peace_of_Xor-Hist) ushered in an era of calm and prosperity that lasted until [1E 2631](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2631), when the tyrant Kinlord [Istamil Corelanya](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Istamil_Corelanya) rose to power. He smothered dissent with impunity, and was largely unpopular, inciting a civil war. Desperate to maintain power, Istamil sought Molag Bal's assistance in silencing the opposition at the temple of Mor Naril. The Lord of Brutality bestowed legions of Daedra and great necromantic power unto Kinlord Istamil, who used these gifts to slaughter his enemies wholesale. Molag Bal's power ran unchecked until Istamil's cousin [Vinutilmo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vinutilmo_Corelanya) rallied beneath the Three Queens, Meridia, Azura, and Nocturnal, to push back against the kinlord and his Daedric patron. Istamil was slain, and Mor Naril was ultimately sealed.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Ho CC5-82)</sup>

Sometime in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Molag Bal made a pact with [King Styriche](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Styriche) of [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Hammerfell).md), a powerful vampire and the leader of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-MBSTE-142)</sup> The terms of their agreement eventually brought thirteen of the Gray Host's exarchs into Coldharbour, where they were tortured for eternity by the Daedric Prince.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-MBSTE-142)</sup> [Rada al-Saran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rada_al-Saran), the last known surviving leader of the Gray Host, sought to free his kin from Coldharbour in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) with the help of the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md).<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-GHOESO-143)</sup> At some point, a Molag Bal-worshipping Ayleid clan that lived in [Greenshade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenshade) ceased making offerings to the Prince at Moonhenge. Their city was consumed in blue fire and transported to Coldharbour.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-144)</sup>

In [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260), the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) sailed to [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) and defeated the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md). The Sloads' Coral Tower was destroyed during the siege, causing it to plunge into the sea.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-JOTEi-145)</sup> The Maelstrom of Bal<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-TDO-146)</sup> opened up in the wake of the tower's fall, and a large chunk of the fleet was pulled beneath the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) and into Coldharbour. Molag Bal appeared before the wreckage and struck a deal with the leader of the fallen fleet, giving the admiral complete control over his sailors with a relic known as the Crown of Bones.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-JOTEi-145)</sup><sup>[[146]](#cite_note-BBABone-147)</sup><sup>[[147]](#cite_note-CCESO-148)</sup>

Molag Bal was responsible for the destruction of the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) settlement of [Gil-Var-Delle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gil-Var-Delle) at the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when he was summoned by King [Dro'Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro%27Zel) of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md).<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Ho SAO-149)</sup> Molag Bal consumed the souls of all mortals present that day, leaving nothing more than the charred remains of the town's once mighty [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md).<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-ESOGil-150)</sup> His corrupting influence lingered into the second era.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Danys TWD-51)</sup><sup>[[150]](#cite_note-151)</sup> The disaster at Gil-Var-Delle was the catalyst that sparked the conception of the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md). Molag Bal was present at the meeting where [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) sealed his famed bargain with the powers of Oblivion, and agreed to the terms the Living God set.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TCC-62)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-load-Black_Gem_Foundry.png/200px-ON-load-Black_Gem_Foundry.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Black_Gem_Foundry.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Black_Gem_Foundry.png) Black soul gems in Coldharbour Molag Bal is rumored to be responsible for the creation of the first soul gems.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-152)</sup> This theory appears to have some grain of truth to it: Mannimarco and his cohorts, believing the Harvester held the secret to trapping souls, stole their first black soul gems from Coldharbour some time after the King of Worms' expulsion from [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md).<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-153)</sup>

He is credited with the creation of two species of Daedra, both involved in his experiments in a facility known as the [Vile Laboratory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vile_Laboratory).<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-LFAYQ-154)</sup> The [Xivkyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivkyn)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OX-7)</sup> and the [Daedric Titan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Titan) are the products of his fascination with vestigial hybridization and the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md)' dominion over mortalkind respectively. Both of these species were released upon the people of Tamriel for the first time during Molag Bal's invasion of Nirn.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-DDTT-77)</sup>

In the 582nd year of the Second Era, Molag Bal attempted to merge Nirn with his plane of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) in an event known as the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). However, this campaign was halted by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[153]](#cite_note-ESOMQEvents-155)</sup> A last-ditch effort to claim the Mundus for his own occurred not long after, while his forces occupied the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-Nar Jou-156)</sup><sup>[[155]](#cite_note-ESOICEvents-157)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-158)</sup> Led by the King of Worms, the [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm) and an offshoot of [Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) traitors dubbed [Legion Zero](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Legion_Zero) served beneath Molag Bal's Daedric minions to invade and maintain control over the city.<sup>[[156]](#cite_note-159)</sup> Molag Bal's minions had converted the centrata, the central hub of the City's sewers beneath the White-Gold Tower, into a terminal for portals to Coldharbour.<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-Nar Jou-156)</sup> Molag Bal sought to locate and destroy the Sublime Brazier so the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) could never be lit again.<sup>[[157]](#cite_note-160)</sup><sup>[[158]](#cite_note-161)</sup> This plot, along with a ritual known as the Planemeld Obverse which involved an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md), were also foiled.<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-ESOICEvents-157)</sup><sup>[[159]](#cite_note-ESOWGT-162)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/ON-quest-Planemeld_Obverse_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Planemeld_Obverse_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Planemeld_Obverse_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Planemeld_Obverse_04.jpg) The Planemeld Obverse is set into motion Molag Bal also arranged to have biological weapons produced in the [Imperial Prison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Prison). The City's citizens were to be brought to the prison and delivered to the prison's [flesh sculptor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flesh_Sculpture), a powerful necromancer who held mastery over flesh. The sculptor would turn mortal skin and organs into carriers for disease and pollution, creating flesh devices that would spew noxious clouds to contaminate the earth around them. Molag Bal's generals planned to deploy a thousand of these siege engines throughout the City, ending the Alliance War and turning the tide in Molag Bal's favor.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-TN-163)</sup><sup>[[161]](#cite_note-NJ-164)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-place-Nobles_District_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Nobles_District_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nobles_District_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nobles_District_02.jpg) The Imperial City under siege Sometime prior to House [Gardner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gardner)'s fall, Princess Violetta of House Gardner used Birdsong Tower as her garden-spot. It was a pleasant place just west of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), where flowers bloomed and birds sang. Molag Bal was offended by the tower's joyous environment, and sent a plague of undeath to profane it. Birdsong Tower became known as [Soulshriven Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soulshriven_Tower).<sup>[[162]](#cite_note-165)</sup> A hero curbed the tower's undead infestation in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-166)</sup> Molag Bal was also responsible for cursing the town of [Westtry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Westtry) at some point between [2E 220](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_220) and [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). The town was invaded by [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) raiders, and a priest of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) sought aid from his god. He was answered by Molag Bal instead, and begged the Prince to save Westtry. Molag Bal subsequently cursed Westtry with undeath.<sup>[[164]](#cite_note-ESOLeon-167)</sup> The curse was lifted by the Vestige in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-ESOMemento Mori-168)</sup> That same year, Abagandra—the Lightless Remnant of the [Doomcrag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Doomcrag) —was given to Molag Bal and transported to his realm to prevent it from devastating [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire) with a plague of vampirism.<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-169)</sup>

During Mannimarco's service to Molag Bal, the King of Worms schemed to assume Molag Bal's place as the God of Brutality. Using the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), the most powerful soul gem the world has ever known, Mannimarco intended to trap Molag Bal's essence and use it to ascend to godhood.<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-Mann So ST-170)</sup> This plot was foiled by the Vestige at [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor) prior to the end of the Planemeld, and the God of Schemes dragged Mannimarco's soul to Coldharbour to administer punishment.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-171)</sup>

Some time after Molag Bal's defeat, the Worm Cult began sacrificing souls to the Lord of Brutality in earnest with the help of magical engines known as soul reapers, seeking to curry the Daedric Prince's favor.<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-172)</sup> To this end, the cult slew Guildmaster [Merric at-Aswala](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Merric_at-Aswala) of the Fighters Guild and kidnapped [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md).<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-173)</sup><sup>[[171]](#cite_note-174)</sup> The cult gathered on the eastern half of the island of Solstice, erecting a barrier of soulstuff to bisect the island so they could work in secret. On their half of the wall, the cult prepared a "reverse Planemeld": while the original Planemeld sought to drag Nirn into Coldharbour, this new ritual sought to bring Coldharbour to the mortal realm.<sup>[[172]](#cite_note-175)</sup>

## Appearance
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/ON-creature-Molag_Bal_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Molag_Bal_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Molag_Bal_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Molag_Bal_02.jpg) The face that inspires Molag Bal's religious iconography Molag Bal has been depicted several different ways by the sculptors who construct shrines dedicated to him. The only truly consistent trait across all portrayals is the presence of a tail, horns and/or frills, and a muscular frame with bestial legs. Some artists interpret him as a bipedal creature with the head of a reptile, long claws, three digits on each hand, and digitigrade legs on paws shaped like that of a lion<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-OBApp-178)</sup> or a large reptile.<sup>[[176]](#cite_note-MWApp-179)</sup> These depictions possess aquatic frills, and occasionally horns.<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-OBApp-178)</sup> Molag Bal once manifested before an agent of the Blades as a green-skinned humanoid with red eyes, elven ears, the legs of a goat, a long, thin tail, a goat-like face, and curved black horns.<sup>[[177]](#cite_note-180)</sup> Acclaimed author Carlovac Townway described the destruction of Gilverdale in *2920, The Last Year of the First Era*: by his account, bright red mist poured over the town in a wave, engulfing building after building in its wake, with Molag Bal arriving as a grinning, red-skinned giant.<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-2920v2-181)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/a/a7/ON-concept-Prince-Molag_Bal-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Molag_Bal-emblem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Molag_Bal-emblem.png) An emblem representing Molag Bal His most iconic appearance is depicted stylistically on his eponymous Mace: a face with ridged horns, aquatic frills, and sharp fangs. During the Planemeld, Molag Bal appeared in the form that inspired the Mace's rendering of his face. Molag Bal possesses a broad, sinewy, muscular frame carried by digitigrade legs with hips crowned by jagged protrusions. A row of spikes ran from the top of his head down the length of his back, and a bony tail extended from the base of his spine, ending in a tri-tipped axe-shaped structure. Ridges jutted up from his sternum, lining the space where the base of his neck and his shoulders met to frame a horned head with frills on each side.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-ESOApp-182)</sup>

His face was reminiscent of a bare skull: a sunken nose, dark circles around light blue eyes that were overseen by pronounced eyebrows, angled cheeks, and rows of lengthy pointed teeth. Small spikes lined his chin. His flesh was blue-grey in coloration, and his horns were black. His extremities grew darker in color as they extended further away from his torso: while his shoulders were blue-grey, his hands were almost dark enough to match his black claws.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-ESOApp-182)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-banner-Coldharbour_Elite_Guard.jpg/200px-ON-banner-Coldharbour_Elite_Guard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Coldharbour_Elite_Guard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Coldharbour_Elite_Guard.jpg) The heraldry of the Coldharbour Elite Guard Molag Bal's face is quite literally plastered all over Coldharbour. Decorative seals on fortress walls and floors depict a threatening fanged grimace.<sup>[[180]](#cite_note-183)</sup> Sconces lit with cold fire look like stylized, simplified sculptures of his head,<sup>[[181]](#cite_note-184)</sup> as do the accents on some braziers<sup>[[182]](#cite_note-185)</sup> and bookshelves,<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-186)</sup> as well as the spouts on fountains.<sup>[[184]](#cite_note-187)</sup><sup>[[185]](#cite_note-188)</sup> Flaccus Terentius describes this show of hubris rather succinctly in his account of Molag Bal's palace, a place where "everything stares back at [Molag Bal] with his own face".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EGTColdharbour-9)</sup> This iconography has made its way onto the tapestries woven by his followers,<sup>[[186]](#cite_note-189)</sup> as well as the banners carried by his soldiers into battle.<sup>[[187]](#cite_note-190)</sup> Molag Bal's face is a common symbol in his worship.<sup>[[188]](#cite_note-191)</sup><sup>[[189]](#cite_note-192)</sup>

Molag Bal is often depicted as scarce in the way of clothing by stonemasons: during the Planemeld, he opted for a loincloth adorned with skulls, tusks, and bits of metal and bone.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-ESOApp-182)</sup> If one were to take a blade and cut into his physical form, a [viscous blue substance](Lore_Azure_Plasm.md) would leak from the wound.<sup>[[190]](#cite_note-193)</sup>

## Behavior
*Molag Bal is a master of all forms of domination over mortals, including the arts of illusion. As a matter of course the Lord of Brutality prefers lies to the truth—except when the truth is more cruel.*<sup>[[191]](#cite_note-194)</sup> Molag Bal is arrogant, calculating, and cares little for the welfare of mortalkind.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Go SMB-36)</sup><sup>[[192]](#cite_note-On Ob-195)</sup> He views pacifism as a form of weakness.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-OBMolag Bal Quest-111)</sup> He is also fond of ostentatious and macabre displays of power, occasionally going out of his way to hold elaborate ceremonies. An example of such an event was the "first Daedric Titan unveiling and Soul Shriven impalement celebration".<sup>[[193]](#cite_note-Cad-196)</sup>

The God of Brutality has little patience for failure. He scorns the Daedra beneath him for falling in battle, considering their "weakness" a blemish on their reputation and deserving of recourse.<sup>[[194]](#cite_note-197)</sup> Independent thought is among that which Molag Bal's servants consider "weak": they believe absolute obedience to the Prince is a virtue, and indeed a point of strength.<sup>[[195]](#cite_note-198)</sup> Weakness is, of course, punished by pain.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ICXiv-74)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/ON-npc-Inmate.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Inmate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Inmate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Inmate.jpg) A Mind-Shriven thrall Molag Bal is quite keen on depriving mortals of free will. To this end, Molag Bal wields a form of [mind magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mind_Magic): he intrudes upon the minds of mortals as a form of torture. It is an invasive, agonizing experience.<sup>[[196]](#cite_note-ESOTPDOG-199)</sup> By forcing his way into a mortal's mind, he can show them horrific visions.<sup>[[197]](#cite_note-Varen Council-200)</sup> He also does this to Daedra, particularly magically-gifted Xivkyn. These augurs share a mental link with Molag Bal, acting as vessels to carry out his dark whims.<sup>[[198]](#cite_note-ESOXiv Aug-201)</sup> This practice has unintended risks, as mortals who have had a mental connection to the Prince forced upon them can see through his eyes if they concentrate hard enough. Molag Bal can feel the presence of one who accomplishes such a feat, and he doesn't appreciate the trespass. During the Planemeld, [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) did just that to distract the Daedric Prince, allowing the Vestige to traverse Coldharbour unmolested. Molag Bal was enraged by Varen's presence, leaving him blind to the hero's deeds in his realm while he dealt with the former Emperor.<sup>[[196]](#cite_note-ESOTPDOG-199)</sup>

This mode of mental binding is not limited to the torment of living mortals: Molag Bal's minions have bound unfortunate souls to mundane objects with the intent to place them near his enemies on Tamriel, enabling the Prince to spy on those who oppose him from the mortal realm. Being forced to serve as a looking glass for Molag Bal is unbearably painful for the bound spirit.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-ESOSkull-80)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/ON-skill-Soul_Burst_06.jpg/200px-ON-skill-Soul_Burst_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Soul_Burst_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Soul_Burst_06.jpg) Molag Bal wields souls as ammunition Some mortals have had their force of will completely sapped as a result of Molag Bal tampering with their mind.<sup>[[199]](#cite_note-ESOCBL-202)</sup> Such was the case with the infamous pirate, Captain Blackheart, whose entire crew was transformed into undead slaves.<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-ESOJump Ship-203)</sup><sup>[[201]](#cite_note-204)</sup> He was cursed when a relic the crew had plundered spoke to him, claiming to be Molag Bal. The Prince uttered a spell, transforming the crew and making them unable to disobey their Captain's orders:<sup>[[202]](#cite_note-ESOShifty Tom-205)</sup> the Captain also fell utterly under Molag Bal's control.<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-ESOMartha Jou-206)</sup> Captain Blackheart never killed the inhabitants of the ships he plundered—following the crew's enslavement,<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-ESOMartha Jou-206)</sup> Molag Bal proceeded to slaughter hundreds of innocents with Captain Blackheart's hands, tormenting him with fleeting moments of independent thought.<sup>[[199]](#cite_note-ESOCBL-202)</sup>

The practice of stealing the minds of mortals is not restricted to spellcraft, as Molag Bal also accomplishes this through [alchemy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy). The [Mind-Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mind-Shriven) are created when mortals are forced to imbibe a poison called the Blood of Coldharbour. These individuals have their force of will stolen by the Daedric Prince,<sup>[[204]](#cite_note-207)</sup> and become unnaturally aggressive as a result of their transformation.<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-ESORegilus-208)</sup>

As is expected of a Daedric Prince, Molag Bal can summon his Daedric minions at will. Unfortunately for those who oppose him, this means he can summon a horde of Daedric Titans.<sup>[[206]](#cite_note-ESOGOS-209)</sup> Molag Bal can [weaponize souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Magic) and cold fire to assault those who wrong him.<sup>[[207]](#cite_note-TFAFire-210)</sup><sup>[[208]](#cite_note-ESOBal Combat-211)</sup> Those who offend Molag Bal may find themselves suddenly missing important features, such as their skin.<sup>[[209]](#cite_note-GOSBS-212)</sup> Renowned Daedrologist Endemel Githendas posits that it is plausible to assume that Molag Bal may be capable of altering a victim's bone structure in pursuit of his eternal desire to inflict agony upon others.<sup>[[210]](#cite_note-AOCS-213)</sup> He is not above turning people into ravenous undead monsters,<sup>[[209]](#cite_note-GOSBS-212)</sup> and [manipulates shadows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Magic) to imprison powerful servants of Meridia.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LFTD-18)</sup>

## Relations with Other Entities
The politics of Oblivion are complex. Alliances come and go, and what each Daedric Prince thinks of one another is largely unknown. However, Molag Bal has a few clearly defined relationships with other Princes, particularly Meridia. The two aren't very fond of each other. Molag Bal revels in undeath and destruction,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Val Jou-15)</sup> while Meridia believes that the sanctity of life energies is not to be perverted.<sup>[[211]](#cite_note-SRLoading-214)</sup> Though they both share a strong distaste for free will,<sup>[[212]](#cite_note-215)</sup> these fundamental beliefs are the foundation for their bitter rivalry.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg) A confrontation between gods The two have actively antagonized each other throughout the course of Tamrielic history: Meridia's role in halting the Planemeld is a prominent example of this. Meridia sent a city under her protection<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-TWo D-84)</sup> into Coldharbour to host an invasion force of her own making,<sup>[[213]](#cite_note-216)</sup><sup>[[214]](#cite_note-217)</sup> wrested control of the Fighters Guild to turn its attention toward interfering with the Planemeld,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mo Viv-12)</sup> and personally appeared in the heart of Molag Bal's stronghold to destroy the Planar Vortex. Meridia plotted to oppose the Planemeld for years in advance.<sup>[[215]](#cite_note-ESOTFA-218)</sup>

During the same time period, Meridia foiled Molag Bal's efforts to use the Mortuum Vivicus, a relic that siphons mortal souls and is capable of unleashing a storm of undeath.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mo Viv-12)</sup> This would be the second time Meridia has thwarted Bal's attempts to release the Vivicus upon Tamriel, the first occurring in the First Era during the time of [Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abagarlas) and [Delodiil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Delodiil). Molag Bal's worshippers plotted to harvest the souls of Meridia's faithful and unleash the Mortuum Vivicus' necromantic potential,<sup>[[216]](#cite_note-BTMM-219)</sup> and the Lady of Light destroyed the Abagarlan's city in response.<sup>[[217]](#cite_note-220)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_17.jpg/200px-ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_17.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_17.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_17.jpg) Meridia exchanges words with Molag Bal Molag Bal became so familiar with Meridia's methods during their years of conflict that he had a facility constructed specifically to imprison her servants.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TLO-26)</sup> Some ancient Khajiiti tales suggest that Meridia and Molag Bal once fought for a common goal during Bal's assault on the Lunar Lattice,<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TAS-59)</sup> but for the most part, their history is rife with conflict.

Boethiah is another Prince with whom Molag Bal isn't necessarily "on good terms". This is another case of a difference in fundamental beliefs: Molag Bal expects those who worship him to obey without question.<sup>[[218]](#cite_note-DA-221)</sup> On the other hand, Boethiah expects her followers to actively question and oppose authority, and despises mortals who attempt to appeal to her with bent knees and words of adoration.<sup>[[219]](#cite_note-Boe Pro-222)</sup> Molag Bal seeks strength for himself, while Boethiah looks for strength in others.<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-TDBAYQ-223)</sup> Their differences aren't the only thing setting them apart; the two Princes have some record of antagonizing one another. In the past, Boethiah has sent her followers to desecrate Molag Bal's holy sites, and Molag Bal has retaliated in kind.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-THOH-25)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura.jpg) Molag Bal exacts vengeance upon a priestess of Azura Molag Bal is apparently bitter rivals with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and the two will thwart each other at every given opportunity.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-FAAYQ-122)</sup> The [Order of the Waking Flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Waking_Flame) alleges that Mehrunes Dagon once personally freed a group of his faithful from imprisonment in Coldharbour by sending a projection of himself to do battle against Molag Bal while the faithful were transported to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md).<sup>[[221]](#cite_note-Eo MD2-224)</sup> [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) views Molag Bal's desire for adulation as unnecessary, interpreting it as a weakness.<sup>[[222]](#cite_note-ESOMalacath-225)</sup> The servants of Molag Bal and Azura have also thwarted each other in the past. The most noteworthy of such instances revolved around a priestess of Azura who brokered peace between two groups in Valenwood whom Molag Bal's minions were sent to divide, foiling his plan.<sup>[[223]](#cite_note-ESOEOA-226)</sup><sup>[[224]](#cite_note-227)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-JOC-35)</sup>

Molag Bal doesn't just have conflict with other Princes, but the Divines as well. Molag Bal has a particular distaste for Arkay, who is invoked when the souls and bodies of deceased mortals are consecrated for burial. Molag Bal delights in the art of necromancy, and Arkay's opposition to the undead contradicts Molag Bal's belief that corpses are tools for him to use to further his goals.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-VOFIT-87)</sup> In the legend of the first vampire's creation, Molag Bal invents a new form of undeath to spite Arkay's sway over the cycle of life and death.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ESORahaja-64)</sup><sup>[[113]](#cite_note-OLBTM-113)</sup><sup>[[225]](#cite_note-228)</sup> While Arkay and Molag Bal never engage each other personally, their servants are likely to find themselves at odds with one another. The [Order of Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Arkay) is an organization that seeks to protect the autonomy of mortal souls and prevent their misuse by necromancers and Daedra. They oppose the necromantic energies wielded by Bal's cultists and the very existence of vampires.<sup>[[226]](#cite_note-229)</sup> Additionally, Arkay's blessing is capable of preventing mortal souls from being perverted by Molag Bal's dark influence.<sup>[[227]](#cite_note-ESOWill Broken-230)</sup><sup>[[228]](#cite_note-ESOMallius-231)</sup>

The followers of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) have also opposed the God of Schemes. Cardinal Belforte of the Order of Stendarr devoted his life to ridding Tamriel of Daedric corruption, and he was especially thorough in his persecution of Molag Bal's faithful. He acquired numerous tracts from the cults his order extinguished, which furthered scholars' understanding of the Daedric Prince's motivations.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Ot No C-61)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Crown of Bones
The Crown of Bones was created by Molag Bal for the Admiral of the Lost Fleet.<sup>[[229]](#cite_note-232)</sup><sup>[[230]](#cite_note-233)</sup> It was a crown that allowed the wearer to control the skeletons of sailors whose ships were dragged into the Maelstrom of Bal.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-JOTEi-145)</sup>

### Dolorous Cista
The [Dolorous Cista](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dolorous_Cista) was a vessel that harvested mortal suffering, converting it to magical energy.<sup>[[231]](#cite_note-CLG_Ossein-234)</sup><sup>[[232]](#cite_note-Skork-235)</sup> Physical pain,<sup>[[233]](#cite_note-236)</sup> fear,<sup>[[234]](#cite_note-Kiruk-237)</sup> and frustration are but a few examples of valid sensations that the cista was capable of feeding on.<sup>[[235]](#cite_note-LBME-238)</sup> The relic could be filled with the suffering of both Daedra and mortals.<sup>[[236]](#cite_note-239)</sup> The cista showed great potential as a weapon of mass destruction: during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), its Daedric handlers were beset by infighting over the subject of who was truly "worthy" to wield its power.<sup>[[231]](#cite_note-CLG_Ossein-234)</sup><sup>[[232]](#cite_note-Skork-235)</sup> One of the Daedric torturers laboring within the Cage speculated that the Lord of Brutality himself had plans to wield it.<sup>[[232]](#cite_note-Skork-235)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg/150px-SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg) The Mace of Molag Bal (*Skyrim*)
### Mace of Molag Bal
The [Mace of Molag Bal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mace_of_Molag_Bal), also known as the **Vampire's Mace**, is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) attributed to Molag Bal, the Lord of Domination and Enslavement. Its enchantment drains the stamina and magicka of its victims and transfers them to the bearer. It also has been known to have the ability to transfer an enemy's strength to its wielder or trap their soul. It has been said to be a good weapon of choice for vanquishing wizards.<sup>[[237]](#cite_note-TL-240)</sup><sup>[[238]](#cite_note-SR-241)</sup>

Many legends surround the mace, but its origins can be traced back to when Molag Bal deceived an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) blacksmith and enslaved him in pits of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). The blacksmith was turned into a [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven) and forced to forge the mace.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Schick-242)</sup><sup>[[239]](#cite_note-RM14D-243)</sup>

Due to Molag Bal's penchant for meddling in mortal affairs, he grants the mace to those he deems worthy but has also been quite free with his artifact. Molag Bal assumes that when such a powerful artifact falls into mortal hands, a trail of death and destruction is sure to follow.<sup>[[237]](#cite_note-TL-240)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Schick-242)</sup> Some believe the mace to be an object of Daedra worship.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-MH-110)</sup> Molag Bal's followers refer to it as **the Master's Mace**,<sup>[[240]](#cite_note-Bt MM-244)</sup><sup>[[241]](#cite_note-ESOJavad-245)</sup> and often swear by it.<sup>[[242]](#cite_note-246)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/eb/ON-quest-Will_of_the_Council.jpg/150px-ON-quest-Will_of_the_Council.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Will_of_the_Council.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Will_of_the_Council.jpg) The Mortuum Vivicus
### Mortuum Vivicus
The [Mortuum Vivicus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mortuum_Vivicus) was a powerful [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) created by the Daedric Prince Molag Bal. It was a massive spell that was capable of holding and harnessing thousands of souls, and could destroy all of Tamriel if it were released.<sup>[[243]](#cite_note-SACPC-247)</sup> It had no physical form, instead appearing as a large orb of cold light. It was originally given as a gift to the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) King [Anumaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril) of [Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abagarlas), a Daedraphile settlement on [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md) that was dedicated to Bal.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/ON-place-The_Orchard_03.jpg/150px-ON-place-The_Orchard_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Orchard_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Orchard_03.jpg) One of the Harvest Hearts (ESO)
### Harvest Hearts
Harvest Hearts are artifacts that Molag Bal uses to enslave large communities and empower specific parts of his divine portfolio.<sup>[[244]](#cite_note-Vanus Harvest Heart-248)</sup>

During the Planemeld, the Vestige destroyed a Heart within a portion of Coldharbour known as the Orchard, a large graveyard full of vampires. That particular heart greatly increased the rate at which vampirism spreads, and turned a few of the Coldharbour invasion force's own into bloodfiends. While that Heart was intact, the vampires in the Orchard were completely under Molag Bal's control, unable to disobey their orders to protect the area.<sup>[[245]](#cite_note-Virgar-249)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/SR-quest-Rings_of_Blood_Magic.jpg/150px-SR-quest-Rings_of_Blood_Magic.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Rings_of_Blood_Magic.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Rings_of_Blood_Magic.jpg) The Rings of Blood Magic
### Rings of Blood Magic
The [Rings of Blood Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rings_of_Blood_Magic) are a set of two ancient [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) rings which enhance the wearer's [Blood Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blood_Magic) and [vampiric](Lore_Vampirism.md) powers.<sup>[[246]](#cite_note-SRSadri-250)</sup> The **Ring of the Erudite** fortifies the wearer's magicka,<sup>[[247]](#cite_note-SRErudite-251)</sup> while the **Ring of the Beast** fortifies the wearer's health and increases the power of a [Vampire Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord)'s claws.<sup>[[248]](#cite_note-SRBeast-252)</sup><sup>[[249]](#cite_note-LGAsia-253)</sup> Both rings are triple-banded with two gemstone insets; the Ring of the Erudite is brassy and possesses green gems, while the Ring of the Beast is golden with red gems.<sup>[[247]](#cite_note-SRErudite-251)</sup><sup>[[248]](#cite_note-SRBeast-252)</sup><sup>[[249]](#cite_note-LGAsia-253)</sup>

According to myth, Molag Bal himself gave these rings to ancient and powerful Vampire Lords.<sup>[[246]](#cite_note-SRSadri-250)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/ON-item-Stone_of_Ashalmawia.jpg/150px-ON-item-Stone_of_Ashalmawia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Stone_of_Ashalmawia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Stone_of_Ashalmawia.jpg) One of the Stones of Cold Fire
### Stones of Cold Fire
The seven Stones of Cold Fire are artifacts that were gifted directly from Molag Bal and distributed throughout Daedric ruins on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) that were dedicated to the Lord of Lies. These stones hold the power to summon and compel Daedra. They were used during the Interregnum by [Mistress Dratha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mistress_Dratha) to make a deal with a Dremora named Xykenaz to prolong her life. The stones were located at [Ashalmawia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashalmawia), [Bal Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bal_Ur), Esutanamus, Kushtashpi, Ramimilk, Yansirramus, and Tusenend.<sup>[[250]](#cite_note-ESOAAC-254)</sup><sup>[[251]](#cite_note-255)</sup>

The stones have a polished black surface and are cold to the touch. Blue flames swirl within them.<sup>[[252]](#cite_note-256)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/ON-item-Vampiric_Shard.jpg/150px-ON-item-Vampiric_Shard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Vampiric_Shard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Vampiric_Shard.jpg) A vampiric shard
### Vampiric Shards
Vampiric Shards are foul relics that corrupt whatever they come in contact with. When the Saxhleel village of Haj Uxith was brought to Coldharbour, Molag Bal gave the Argonians one of these crystals to keep their [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) alive; it was the only thing sustaining the Hist in Coldharbour's inhospitable soil. Over time, the shard poisoned the Hist. The Hist was dying, but the shard kept it from passing, forcing it to endure constant torment.<sup>[[253]](#cite_note-257)</sup>

## Coldharbour
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f8/ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief_02.jpg) Heart's Grief's vestibule, where the most wretched of failures are tormented [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by Molag Bal, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Domination. As befits his sphere, Bal's realm is a desolate, apocalyptic image of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), which is achieved by both mocking imitation and outright theft of parts of the mortal realm. It is inhabited by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and the eternally-tormented husks of mortals called [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven).<sup>[[254]](#cite_note-OTNOC-258)</sup>

Mortals who made deals with Molag Bal can also be found living in Coldharbour.<sup>[[255]](#cite_note-Uxith-259)</sup><sup>[[256]](#cite_note-Faraniel-260)</sup> The souls of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) are sent to Coldharbour when they die.<sup>[[257]](#cite_note-ESOYushiha-261)</sup><sup>[[258]](#cite_note-ADP-262)</sup><sup>[[259]](#cite_note-ESOGwendi-263)</sup> Coldharbour has been described as a manifestation of fear and exploitation.<sup>[[260]](#cite_note-DPOT-264)</sup> Descriptions of the plane vary widely, but all accounts agree that Coldharbour is a dismal, cold, and largely lifeless realm.<sup>[[254]](#cite_note-OTNOC-258)</sup>

Coldharbour is full of innumerable facilities, all built to further Molag Bal's dark schemes. Among these facilities is the Library of Dusk, a library filled with propaganda that serves as a trap for scholars.<sup>[[261]](#cite_note-265)</sup> It is available for use by Molag Bal's minions.<sup>[[262]](#cite_note-266)</sup> There is another library in Coldharbour that is slightly less accessible. It is Molag Bal's personal collection, located in his stronghold. The shelves in Molag Bal's library span entire cliffs and run deep into a chasm. Unsurprisingly, plenty of the books on his shelves are very disturbing.<sup>[[263]](#cite_note-267)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/ON-place-The_Endless_Stair.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Endless_Stair.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Endless_Stair.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Endless_Stair.jpg) Molag Bal's citadel The realm possesses several qualities that function as security measures. Things that teleport into Coldharbour will find themselves scattered, ending up in locations they did not intend to land.<sup>[[264]](#cite_note-ESOMalkur-268)</sup><sup>[[265]](#cite_note-269)</sup> Magical constructs known as [Coldharbour Sentinels](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coldharbour_Sentinel) are used by Molag Bal to guide his vision in Coldharbour. With their aid, he can see into whatever prison they're anchored in. These Sentinels are magically connected, so destroying one will blind the other Sentinels it was bound to.<sup>[[266]](#cite_note-270)</sup>

Coldharbour mostly consists of prisons, but other facilities include those where research is conducted. He is fascinated by the metamagical technology of Soul Gems, and often has a mystical research project or two in the works to develop new and more efficient methods of stealing and imprisoning the souls of mortals.<sup>[[267]](#cite_note-271)</sup> Not every part of Coldharbour serves a practical use; there are many graveyards in Molag Bal's plane of Oblivion for no other reason than because he likes them.<sup>[[268]](#cite_note-272)</sup> The giant skeletal structures in the Ossein Cage region are intended to instill a sense of horror and dread in those who walk its fractured valleys.<sup>[[210]](#cite_note-AOCS-213)</sup>

### Servants
Molag Bal maintains a strong military within his realm. He expects complete loyalty from his servants, and does not tolerate weakness among his ranks.<sup>[[269]](#cite_note-273)</sup><sup>[[270]](#cite_note-274)</sup><sup>[[218]](#cite_note-DA-221)</sup> Daedra most commonly seen in his service include [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora), [Clannfear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clannfear), [Daedroth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedroth),<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-DD-91)</sup> [Grievous Twilights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grievous_Twilight), [Daedric Titans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Titan), [Cold-Flame Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cold-Flame_Atronach), [Banekin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Banekin) and [Harvesters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Harvester). [Ogrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogrim), [Scamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp), [Watchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher), [Winged Twilights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winged_Twilight), [Xivilai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivilai), [Spider Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider_Daedra) and [Dark Seducers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Seducer) are also found in his service. The [Xivkyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivkyn) are a Daedric race that is exclusively loyal to Molag Bal, and serve as his personal guard.<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-Nar Jou-156)</sup> They behave much like their master; they constantly scheme against one another and they obsessively collect soul gems.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OX-7)</sup><sup>[[271]](#cite_note-ESOCEG-275)</sup> The hooved [Maldrith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maldrith) are also counted among his ranks.<sup>[[272]](#cite_note-Maldrith ESO-276)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-AS-277)</sup> They serve Molag Bal as roaming inspectors and inquisitors, and are feared by other Daedra.<sup>[[273]](#cite_note-MCo C-278)</sup>

Molag Bal forbade ordinary [Flame Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flame_Atronach) from being summoned in his realm after a subordinate conjured one in his palace and damaged his favorite ice sculpture. Their presence within Coldharbour was eventually replaced by [Cold-Flame Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cold-Flame_Atronach).<sup>[[274]](#cite_note-DDCFA-279)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/ON-npc-Duchess_of_Anguish_04.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Duchess_of_Anguish_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Duchess_of_Anguish_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Duchess_of_Anguish_04.jpg) One of the Xivkyn The Dremora within his service are divided into several clans, the most prominent and powerful being the [Deathbringers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deathbringer_Clan) and the [Foolkillers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Foolkillers_Clan), who struggle against each other for prominence within Molag Bal's ranks.<sup>[[275]](#cite_note-OOADC-280)</sup> During the Interregnum, the Deathbringers were at the forefront of this struggle.<sup>[[276]](#cite_note-ESOTCMF-281)</sup> Their Valkynaz was a trusted servant of Molag Bal who was tasked with guarding the key to the Prince's inner sanctum and had access to the Daedric Prince himself.<sup>[[277]](#cite_note-ESORCVanus-282)</sup> Three other Dremora are known to have been part of Molag Bal's inner council.<sup>[[278]](#cite_note-FAESO-283)</sup> Aside from playing a part in Coldharbour's politics, they fill the ranks as soldiers, couriers, guards, and torturers, among other roles.<sup>[[279]](#cite_note-IWSBAM-284)</sup><sup>[[280]](#cite_note-ESOWailing-285)</sup> The Darkbinders are a low-ranking Dremora clan that serve as jailers within Coldharbour. Darkbinders are occasionally assigned to administer punishment to soul shriven who are of particular interest to Molag Bal.<sup>[[281]](#cite_note-286)</sup>

It is a fairly simple task to discern a minion of Molag Bal from those in service to another Prince: the easiest way to tell is by looking at their eyes. The eyes of Daedra within Molag Bal's service tend to be icy blue, just like their masters', and many of them have blue or blue-grey skin.<sup>[[282]](#cite_note-KCAHC-287)</sup><sup>[[283]](#cite_note-ESODaedra-288)</sup> It is believed to be because Molag Bal likes to twist things into his own image.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-AS-277)</sup> Stonefire Scamps are particularly easy to discern as servants of Molag Bal, as their flesh and fur are a rich blue-grey, their eyes are the same tone as their master's and they have deep black horns.<sup>[[284]](#cite_note-Stonefire Scamp-289)</sup> [Daedrats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedrat) and [Fiendroths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fiendroth) can be seen scuttling around in Coldharbour and in places on Mundus where Molag Bal's influence is pervasive.<sup>[[285]](#cite_note-290)</sup>

Slate-Skinned Daedrats are a breed whose distinct markings and blue eyes stem from their diet of corpses that have been touched by the Lord of Brutality. While not brazen minions of Molag Bal, they bear characteristics common in his servants, and are often studied by those interested in researching or thwarting the Prince.<sup>[[286]](#cite_note-Slate Daedrat-291)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-creature-Dread_Watcher.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Dread_Watcher.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Dread_Watcher.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Dread_Watcher.jpg) A Watcher that serves Molag Bal The rules that govern Molag Bal's minions are outlined in two known doctrines: the *Seven-Hundred-and-One Edicts* is the primary set of rules, and the *Mandatory Codicils* cover special cases. Breaking one of these rules lands the offending party a sentence to a facility known as the Scathe-Rings, where those imprisoned can be sentenced to many excruciating punishments.<sup>[[287]](#cite_note-ESOLCLoad-292)</sup> One such punishment is "Second-Degree Gradual Discorporation".<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TLO-26)</sup> Strict uniform codes are among the guidelines that govern Bal's servants.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CM63D-30)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-CMX-75)</sup> Those whose service is exceptional (i.e.: exceeding quotas in certain realms of employment) may earn themselves time in the Bliss-Cells.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TSPC-13)</sup>

Daedra are not the only ones rewarded in Coldharbour's work culture. Soul-Shriven slaves who inform on their compatriots can be rewarded with shifts of lighter labor or a visit to the Grotto of Depravity,<sup>[[288]](#cite_note-293)</sup> a cave that serves as the site of an eternal party. This doesn't mean the shriven get to have fun: there's a high probability that the Daedra who are actually enjoying themselves will torment the soulless husks in some manner, and shriven suffering is often mandated.<sup>[[289]](#cite_note-Par The-294)</sup> Despite this, the shriven assigned to the grotto are permitted brief reprieve, even fishing in the plasm pools under constant Daedric supervision.<sup>[[290]](#cite_note-295)</sup>

If one of Molag Bal's servants is "put on trial" for their transgressions, they are tried in a facility known as the Court of Contempt, where they are entitled to a counsel of [Scamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp) outfitted in puffy wigs. Scamps are not allowed to speak in the Court of Contempt, and the Court has a one-hundred-percent conviction rate of defendants, otherwise known as "the Guilty". These trials are mostly for show and never end fairly.<sup>[[291]](#cite_note-PCC-296)</sup><sup>[[292]](#cite_note-297)</sup> One notable instance of a minion incurring the Daedric Prince's wrath is an incident known as "The Seat of Tyranny Rude Cushion Incident", wherein a Dremora named Xykenaz was imprisoned in a nanoplane for seven epochs for putting a whoopie-cushion on Molag Bal's throne.<sup>[[293]](#cite_note-298)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-skill-Daedroth%27s_Heat.jpg/200px-ON-skill-Daedroth%27s_Heat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Daedroth%27s_Heat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Daedroth%27s_Heat.jpg) A Daedroth general in service to Bal Molag Bal has appointed quite a few powerful servants to serve as generals or other high-ranking officers. Among these servants are spies,<sup>[[294]](#cite_note-299)</sup> [agents of chaos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Menta_Na), [brutal prison wardens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lord_Warden_Dusk), and [gifted mages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Kena).<sup>[[218]](#cite_note-DA-221)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MBal General-20)</sup> Molag Bal values cunning as well as strength, and seems to grant power to those he finds interesting or strong... even if their claim to fame is upsetting the balance of power among the other Daedra within his service. Though he would prefer his military hierarchy remains stable, he has rewarded servants of his for displays of guile in the past.<sup>[[218]](#cite_note-DA-221)</sup><sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ESOLord Warden-121)</sup><sup>[[295]](#cite_note-300)</sup> Molag Bal has also employed a scribe named [Denogorath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Denogorath), whose full title is "Dread Archivist and Promulgator of Mandatory Doctrine".<sup>[[296]](#cite_note-ESODenogorath-301)</sup>

Mortals also serve the Daedric Prince in his realm. Apart from the cultists that worship him on Nirn, Molag Bal has the [Soul-Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul-Shriven) and Mind-Shriven as slaves, with the Dremora as their common overseers.<sup>[[297]](#cite_note-302)</sup> Vampires may also be found serving the Daedric Prince.<sup>[[298]](#cite_note-303)</sup><sup>[[299]](#cite_note-Lan Jour-304)</sup>

The extent that the *Seven-Hundred-and-One Edicts* applies to mortals is not known, but there are consequences for mortals who displease Molag Bal nonetheless. Agents who disappoint the Daedric Prince harshly enough are pitted against each other in an endless war at the Cliffs of Failure. The basic premise of the "game" at the Cliffs is for the participants to fight to win back the Dark Lord's favor.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-TEWObserver-79)</sup> However, there is no end to the game, and these individuals are doomed to fight for Molag Bal's amusement forever.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ESOAngof-107)</sup> Mortals and Daedra who anger Molag Bal severely are tormented in the vestibule of Heart's Grief.<sup>[[300]](#cite_note-305)</sup> These individuals have usually failed the Daedric Prince<sup>[[301]](#cite_note-GOSAelif-306)</sup> or committed some other egregious infraction.<sup>[[302]](#cite_note-GOSMannimarco-307)</sup>
