# Second Era Wars

The following is a list of all known [wars and conflicts](Lore_Wars.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Most of these took place during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) (2E 430 – 2E 854).

## Yashnag's Chiefdom vs. Western Skyrim
c. 2E 435 — [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467)

There was a conflict between [Yashnag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yashnag_gro-Yazgu)'s Chiefdom (an Orcish state in western Falkreath Hold) and the Nordic kingdom of Western Skyrim from c. 2E 435 to [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467). The conflict has no official name, and it is unclear when it started, but it began 'more than 30 years' before 2E 467 and must have started after the Sack of Orsinium and the Skyrim Schism in 2E 431.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

## [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/LG-cardart-Emeric%2C_Covenant_King.png/200px-LG-cardart-Emeric%2C_Covenant_King.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Emeric,_Covenant_King.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Emeric,_Covenant_King.png) King Emeric [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566) - [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TFOKR-11)</sup>

King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) had been courting Rayelle, daughter of King [Ranser](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ranser) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), when he met and abruptly married Princess [Maraya](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maraya), daughter of the Redguard King [Fahara'jad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fahara%27jad) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). Ranser was deeply offended, and withdrew his diplomats from Wayrest, surreptitiously raising levies and hiring mercenaries in [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire) over the course of a year.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TATOAM-3)</sup> Finally, in [Last Seed](Lore_Calendar.md) of 2E 566, he swept down from the northern mountains, taking Emeric by surprise and nearly razing Wayrest immediately. Only the efforts of the king's personal guard saved the city, and forced a protracted siege.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TFOKR-11)</sup> Ranser's forces were insufficient to take the fortified city by storm, and so the siege dragged on as the tide of the war turned against him. Ranser's aggression was unpopular even among his own nobles, and several prominent houses of [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire) withdrew their military support and sued for peace.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HTARH-12)</sup> While internal stresses took their toll, the original [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) paid dividends, as [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), and [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) were compelled to come to Wayrest's aid.

Meanwhile, Emeric was cannily leveraging his diplomatic connections. His recent marriage ties with the Redguards summoned a fleet of fierce warriors across the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), while an offer to restore [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) brought the Orcs into the fray.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TFOKR-11)</sup> Under [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog_gro-Bagrakh), the old enemies of Shornhelm burned Ranser's holdings and were waiting to prevent the troops' retreat from the advancing Breton and Redguard forces. Caught between the hammer and anvil at Markwasten Moor, Ranser's army was utterly destroyed. Only a select few were able to escape with the king, who retreated to a tor overlooking the smoking ruins of Shornhelm, hoping to make a last stand.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-KRT-13)</sup> Driven to desperation, Ranser turned to dark sorcery, but was murdered during the ritual by one of his own generals.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-14)</sup> The Crown of Shornhelm went missing during the chaos, and that evil place has henceforth bore the name Traitor's Tor.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-15)</sup>

## Second Akaviri Invasion
[2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572)<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GTTEP-16)</sup>

Also called the **Liberation War** by the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> A fleet originating from the [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) nation of [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(place).md), under the command of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal), invaded northern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the Second Era. United by a common enemy, the Nords of Eastern Skyrim, the Dunmer of Morrowind and the Argonians of Black Marsh jointly defeated the Akaviri invaders and formed an unusual alliance, the Ebonheart Pact.

### Major Battles
- **Battle of Windhelm** or **Sack of Windhelm**, 7 Sun's Height – 7 Last Seed 2E 572:<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SIR-18)</sup> For reasons unknown, the Akaviri fleet, although originating from the east, bypassed the eastern shores of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), instead landing near [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), in the north of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSAI-19)</sup> Windhelm was taken by surprise and utterly sacked, resulting in the death of Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and her heir, Princess [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md), who both fell leading their troops,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSAI-19)</sup> known as the Blood Claws.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SIR-18)</sup> Nurnhilde's younger brothers reached Windhelm during the battle, but too late to prevent its sack:<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-20)</sup> [Fildgor Strong-Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fildgor) at the head of his Stormfist Brigade,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TSC-21)</sup> [Jorunn the Skald-Prince](Lore_Jorunn.md) leading the "Pack of Bards".<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-JTSK-22)</sup> The twin-brothers joined forces to repel the Akaviri invaders.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TSC-21)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg/500px-ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg) [Heita-Meen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heita-Meen), [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md), and [Tanval Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indoril) at the [Battle of Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Vivec%27s_Antlers) near [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart), which led to the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md). - **[Battle of Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Vivec%27s_Antlers)**, 2E 572: Jorunn then sought the aid of the reclusive [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), who summoned the mythical Ash-King, [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-JTSK-22)</sup> With the aid of Wulfharth, Jorunn rallied his troops and fortified [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), expecting an imminent assault.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSAI-19)</sup> However, the invaders simply bypassed the city, passing into [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls) in western [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSAI-19)</sup> They were opposed by Dark Elf forces commanded by general Tanval of [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril), who staged a fighting retreat as the Akaviri slowly advanced towards eastern Stonefalls.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSAI-19)</sup>

There, on a beach of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) just to the west of [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart) that would later be named [Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec%27s_Antlers),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESO-23)</sup> [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) troops under the command of the living god [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) met and held them, while Jorunn and Wulfharth (and allegedly the Stormfirst Brigade<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TSC-21)</sup>) pursued the Akaviri force into a surprise pincer attack.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSAI-19)</sup> The fighting was fierce, but the Nord army was overextended, and victory seemed uncertain until an elite Argonian phalanx of Shellbacks led by [Heita-Meen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heita-Meen)<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> reinforced the Dunmeri army, turning a stalemate into a slaughter.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-UA-25)</sup> With the aid of a tidal wave summoned by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md), the Kamal were obliterated by the three unlikely allies.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESO-23)</sup>
This conflict united three races who had held great animosity towards each other for centuries, leading to the formation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-UA-25)</sup> The Dunmer freed the Argonian slaves in recognition of their crucial aid.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GTTEP-16)</sup> However, the Nords of Western Skyrim did not join the Ebonheart Pact, as they had not been affected by the Second Akaviri Invasion and thus saw no need for an alliance, were skeptical that a Nordic–Dunmeri–Argonian alliance could work given their historic emnity, and had also been at odds with their fellow Nords of Eastern Skyrim for a long time.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSAI-19)</sup> - **Brothers' War**, 2E 572: As Jorunn the Skald-Prince returned home to Windhelm, a succession dispute arose between him and his twin-brother [Fildgor Strong-Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fildgor), who had jointly defeated the Akaviri invaders after the Sack of Windhelm in which their elder sister Queen Nurnhilde fell.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TSC-21)</sup> The brothers disagreed on who should succeed their sister and how to rule the land going forward, leading Jorunn to challenge Fildgor to single combat.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TSC-21)</sup> This long and fierce duel, known as the [Brothers' War](Lore_The_Brothers%27_War.md), was won by Jorunn, who exiled Fildgor and was crowned the Skald-King of Eastern Skyrim,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Brothers.27_War-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TSC-21)</sup> three weeks after the Battle of Vivec's Antlers.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-JTSK-22)</sup>

## Colovian Revolt
[2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576) - [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577)

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg) Leovic, slain by Varen The [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md), also known as **Varen's Rebellion**, was a rebellion against Emperor [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic) of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) by Duke [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol). Following Leovic's legalization of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship throughout the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), Varen Aquilarios marshaled the military forces of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) to depose him, triggering a widespread rebellion in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576). Varen based his rebellion out of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), where he coordinated his troops as they liberated Cyrodiil's other cities to further weaken Leovic's control. Despite his desire to fight on the frontlines, Varen instated his nephew [Carolus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Carolus_Aquilarios) to defend Kvatch, who repelled attacks from Longhouse-allied forces from [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and elsewhere. Meric Renmus pledged allegiance to Varen and was given the rank of general for his loyalty.

The rebellion claimed victory in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), when Varen's forces stormed the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and confronted Leovic's last legion in a bloody battle that razed the city's Market District. Varen personally dispatched Leovic at the foot of the Ruby Throne before proclaiming himself emperor.

[Euraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxia_Tharn) entered [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) as the Empire's envoy following the start of [Varen's Rebellion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen%27s_Rebellion). Received at [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) as an emissary of the new emperor at the head of a large contingent of [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) mercenaries, Euraxia turned on her hosts and deposed [King Hemakar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hemakar) of Rimmen in the **Frostfall Coup**, slaughtering the royal family and establishing herself as queen.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TFC-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOKhamira-27)</sup> From Rimmen Euraxia consolidated an iron grip over much of northern Elsweyr,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TFC-26)</sup> training siege weapons on her own capital to quell any signs of revolt from the local [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Abnur Elsweyr-28)</sup> Unbeknownst to Euraxia, however, Hemakar's daughter [Khamira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khamira) survived the Frostfall Coup, and would ensure her eventual overthrow after the Usurper Queen's attempt to ally with [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid) and his rage of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Two Queens-29)</sup>

## Three Banners War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/LO-misc-Three_Banners_War_Header_cropped.png/200px-LO-misc-Three_Banners_War_Header_cropped.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Three_Banners_War_Header_cropped.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Three_Banners_War_Header_cropped.png) Three Banners War The [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), also called the **Alliance War** and the **Great War**, or the **Usurper War** by Imperials, was a major conflict involving numerous [races](Lore_Races.md) and [factions](Lore_Factions.md) that took place across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).

The years leading up to the war saw a divided Tamriel witness a series of conflicts and crises that inspired the different nations of the land to band together into three distinct alliances: the greater [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), and the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580).

The competing ideologies of these alliances set them on a course for war, which began in earnest sometime in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580). Tensions between the alliances were further exacerbated by the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) and subsequent military aggression of the Empire of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) under [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)'s puppet, Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn). Each alliance saw the opportunity to overthrow the corrupt Imperial regime by capturing the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and installing their own emperor on the [Ruby Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne), but also had to deal with the other two alliances. The three central leaders of these three competing alliances were [Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) of the Aldmeri Dominion, [High King Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of the Daggerfall Covenant, and [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) of the Ebonheart Pact.

The armies of the Covenant, Pact, and Dominion each invaded Cyrodiil, which served as the main theater of war, however they also launched attacks against each other's lands. The war caused enormous damage to Cyrodiil's towns and cities, and countless civilians were displaced and resettled throughout Tamriel. The war was ongoing as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), despite numerous attempts at [peace talks](#The_Three_Banners_Truce). The war's ultimate outcome is unknown, but all three alliances, as well as the Empire of Cyrodiil, had ceased to exist by the ninth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

The conflict of the Three Banners War is often symbolically represented by a three-headed [ouroboros](Lore_Ouroboros.md), with each head symbolizing one of the alliances: the eagle for the Aldmeri Dominion, the lion for the Daggerfall Covenant, and the dragon for the Ebonheart Pact.

## The Tiber War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png/200px-LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_New_Era.png) Tiber Septim [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852) - [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896)

The [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) (sometimes **Talosian conquest**) was a series of conflicts spanning through the second half of the last century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), started by the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) warlord [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) and continued by his general [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). The results of the wars were manifold, most important of them being the end of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) with the founding of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) with Septim as its first Emperor, the proclamation of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), and the unification of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for the first and only time in its history.

### Major Battles
- **Battle of Old Hroldan**, [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852). [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md)'s general [Hjalti Early-Beard](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) successfully led his king's [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) troops and [their allies](Lore_Nord.md)' berserkers into breaking the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) lines, forcing them to fortify themselves at [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md). The next day Hjalti approached the gates of the besieged city, protected by the winds created by a storm following his trail. Reports say that the ancient Nord art of [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) was used to shout down the walls of Old Hroldan, after which Hjalti's army took the city with relative ease by force. As a result, he was nicknamed Talos, meaning Stormcrown, by his Nordic troops.
- **Sack of Sancre Tor**, 2E 852. While his army was distracting the majority of the defenders, Talos made his way inside the fortress of [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor), capturing the Nord and Breton leaders and making them order their troops to surrender. St. [Osla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Osla) of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) has a statue dedicated to her for her actions in the battle, where she healed the warriors that had fallen in battle. Sancre Tor was abandoned and Talos recovered the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).
- **Battle of Five Bridges**. After the initial success of his foes, Tiber Septim commanded his reserves to victory, attacking only after his foes were completely victorious, as advised by his Battlemage Zurin Arctus. Having been lulled into a false sense of security by their victory, Tiber took the enemy army by surprise, routing an army twice the size of his own.
- **Taking of the Imperial City**, [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854). After a siege of [the capital](Lore_Imperial_City.md), the battlemages of the [Eastern Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands) surrendered before Cuhlecain's armies. Before he could crown himself Emperor, he was assassinated, and Talos was crowned in his stead as Tiber Septim.
- **Moth Rebellion**, [2E 857](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_857).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Moth)</sup> An uprising in the Imperial City. The Third Legion, mostly made out of newly-recruited Colovian peasant boys, was sent from nearby [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) to quell it. There the Legion earned its sobriquet "The Faithful" while besieged in the Hesod Barracks by the [Cultists](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md). After the rebellion ended, Tiber Septim triumphantly reentered the capital.
- **Battle of the Bjoulsae**. Under the command of [Amiel Richton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Richton), an Imperial squadron crushed [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md)'s naval power in a surprise dawn attack.
- **Sack of Senchal**. After the besieged [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) refused to surrender, General Pottreid ordered the Third Legion to attack the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), with free reins. Due to heavy civilian casualties, including children, the battle was omitted from officially-sanctioned Imperial history.
- **Battle of Black Rocks**, [2E 863](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_863).<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Blackrocks)</sup> The forces of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) caught the Third Legion by surprise and almost completely destroyed it. The remnants of it made the core of the newly created Ruby Legion stationed in [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley).
- **Battle of Stros M'Kai**, [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Also known as [Battle of Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Hunding_Bay) by Imperials. [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif/300px-LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif) Battle of Stros M'Kai, the penultimate battle of the Crowns Fleeing from the capital of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), Prince [A'tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A%27tor) rallied the remaining [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) for one final battle. The navies of both sides clashed near the isle of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in the [Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hunding_Bay). Leading the Imperial New West Navy was Lord Admiral Amiel Richton, who brought in a dragon called [Nafaalilargus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nafaalilargus). The turning point of the battle was Prince A'tor's death by a poisonous arrow from Dram, a Dunmer assassin. [Archmage Voa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voa) tried to save the prince, but due to the strength of the poison he was forced to place his soul in a soul gem and to put his body in a magically induced stasis before being burned by the dragon. After their defeat, the remaining Crowns fled to Stros M'Kai, most of them to the Old Quarter of the city, which was promptly burned to the ground by the dragon, killing them all as a result. Some of the survivors took refuge at the local temple of [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay). The priests there gave them asylum, hiding the body of prince A'tor in the temple and healing the wounded, only to be forced to surrender the latter to the Imperials. The result of the battle was that the Redguard forces, aside from the Crown-allegiant [Restless League](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restless_League), were either completely annihilated or subdued. Following the crowning achievements of his career, Richton was made the Provisional Governor of Stros M'Kai, reigning over the city with terror for the next three months.
- **Stros M'Kai Uprising**, 2E 864. Led by siblings [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) and [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), the Restless League came out of hiding and stormed the Palace, with Cyrus himself cornering the Provisional Governor Amiel Richton and the Imperial Emissary Dram aboard a [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) [dirigible](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Airships). Cyrus' sword, enchanted with the soul of Prince A'tor, then killed them both. This act was allegedly the third incarnation of [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding).
- **Rebellion of Sentinel**, 2E 864. After hearing of the Crown rebellion in Stros M'Kai, Baron Volag, leader of the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), came out of hiding and retook Sentinel from the Provisional Governor [Senecus Goddkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senecus_Goddkey). As a result of these two rebellions, the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](#First_Treaty_of_Stros_M.27Kai) was signed.
- **Sack of Mournhold**. One of the several battles that occurred after Septim's legions invaded [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and before the [Armistice](#Armistice) was signed. The city was razed and the royal family of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) was wiped out, except for the five-year-old heiress [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah).<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_Barenziah)</sup>
- **Surrender of Alinor**, [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896). Given to the Empire as a part of the terms of the Armistice by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), Numidium besieged the [capital](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) of [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), its fall happening within an hour. Alinor's fall marked the end of the Tiber War, finally unifying [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), in turn leading to the proclamation of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). The great Altmer battlemage [Areldur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Areldur) died during Tiber Septim's conquest of the Summerset Isles.

### Peace Treaties
- **Forebear Truce**. After the Crown forces regrouped at Stros M'Kai and even got close to retaking the capital of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), the Forebears, desperate at the prospect of losing the civil war, asked Tiber Septim for aid. The Empire was to lend its armies to the Forebear cause in return for a swath of holding titles along the southeast border of Hammerfell. After the Crowns were crushed and the Forebear leader [Baron Volag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volag) went into hiding not a day later, rumored to be dead, the treaty was revised, and Imperial garrisons were stationed across Hammerfell *"until such a time as the native rulership can see fit to reinstate itself safely on its rightful throne."*
- **First Treaty of Stros M'Kai**, [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Following the [double](#Stros_M.27Kai_Uprising) [rebellion](#Rebellion_of_Sentinel) of both most prominent Redguard factions, the Empire was forced to draw a treaty with the rebels, finally legitimizing their rule. It was signed by Tiber Septim, [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), and Baron Volag, representing the Empire, the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), respectively. While Hammerfell's status as a province remained, a remark from almost two hundred years later stated that the Redguards felt that they *"are part of the Empire, but not a subject."*
- **Armistice**. After the Imperials amassed their forces at Morrowind's western borders and had several skirmishes with Tribunal-led Dunmeri forces, [Lord Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) himself proclaimed that he would initiate peace talks with Septim's Empire. The terms of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) were culturally very favorable to Morrowind, such as the freedom of [religion](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and perseverance of the ancient Dunmeri right to own and trade slaves. Strategically, the Armistice was very favorable to the Empire, most importantly the access to Morrowind's rich [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) mines and to the [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) Brass God [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).
