I thought it best I handle introducing today's correspondent as he will likely only refer to himself as a humble priest.

I first knew of Kuzam-jo only by reputation; stories of a priest dedicated to acts of charity and celebrations of love. I sent letters across the continent in search of this priest, only to learn that he had been returning my letters all along.

Quote, "I had not realized I was the wise and venerable priest you were searching for. After all, many of my colleagues fit that description just as well." This is who Kuzam-jo is. Kind, humble, and above all, uplifting.

I spent some time in [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md) with Kuzam-jo as he made final preparations for his [Hearts Week](Lore_Heart%27s_Week.md) celebrations, a new festival looking to promote love and light across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

I hope you find his answers to questions of romance, friendship, and all things Mother Mild as enlightening as I did.

***

Greetings everyone, I am Kuzam-jo. A humble priest of [Mara](Lore_Mara.md). Now let us get to your questions, yes?

*I find myself thinking of [wedding ceremonies](Lore_Marriage.md) across Tamriel. We know a bit about everyone's celebrations … except for the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). What might their ceremonies look like?*

*—Mazkeen al- [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md)*

The Maormer are protective of their customs and traditions, so I cannot speak with certainty. But, I can share one ceremony I was fortunate enough to witness. During my pilgrimage across the southern shores of Tamriel, I came upon a binding ceremony between the captain of a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) merchant vessel and a Maormer pirate.

I watched as their hands, clasped in shared prayer, were bound together by a [sea serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md) hatchling. New love bound to new life. And oh how it lashed and bit at them. But they did not flinch. They stood firmly in the surf and exchanged vows through song and so the serpent was calmed. The sea and its roiling chaos stood no chance of splitting the couple. And so they continued to sing until the tide rose to swallow them.

As we waiting *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]*, watching the waves, the wizened Maormer who stood beside me shared a simple truth. If they had failed to convince the serpent of their love it would have kept them bound beneath the surf, until the sea claimed them. But before dread could set in the lovers broke the surface. And returned to us hand in hand.

*I am sure you are getting plenty of questions about your so-called "civilized" traditions, but tell me this; What do you know about the "primitive" folks and how they express their love?*

*—"[Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) Tim"*

Have I witnessed an [Imga](Lore_Imga.md) say "I love you?" No, not in so many words. But, of all the traits shared between the "civilized" and "primitive," I believe love to be the most potent. The closest to the source of light which sparks warmth and empathy. I have witnessed Goblins guide lost children back to their village, [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) unblock streams clogged by storm-felled trees, and [Lamia](Lore_Lamia.md) protect clutches of eggs they themselves did not lay.

It is these expressions of empathy that prove to me that love is present in all the myriad peoples that populate Tamriel.

*My question is about a more sombre matter. Of finality. My wife is an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of the shores of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md). Me? Just a humble [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) from the [Druadachs](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md). Do you believe that we will find each other on the other side of the river when the time comes?*

*—Kaenag of [Clan](Lore_Reachfolk_Clans.md) Firewolf*

First, I must congratulate you Kaenag. To have a love so potent that it questions the bounds of life and [death](Lore_Death.md) is a treasure.

I do believe that love survives this final passage. And for those readers who also harbor these fears, I will say this. Do not impose the physical rules of mortal life on the infinity of what comes next. Death is one passage of many you have already walked and will walk. The difference will only be that on this next journey, you will not be alone.

*While we approach the sacred duty of embodying Mara's divine love from different directions, I write to inquire how best to include those among us who do not express or experience what we term romantic love in ways that most people would recognize.*

*—Euryale Kyr'Eris*

Mara teaches us that all relationships, romantic or otherwise, can blossom given warmth and kindness. I will share with you that I myself have always found romantic love, of the intimate sort, to be a curiosity. Perhaps this is strange to hear from a Priest of Mara? But I offer this so you know I speak from personal experience.

These holidays. These ceremonies. They can, despite our best efforts, exclude those who love differently. To that I say, be curious. Be kind. Be present. Invite friends and lovers in equal measure and be not afraid to say I love you to those you care for. Too often we reserve such words for only those we are betrothed to. Know that to a friend, a comrade, a passing stranger, these words can be a boon.

*The Altmer revere Mara, yet their marriages are arranged in favor of "lineage and heritage." What part does love and Mara play in Altmeri unions?*

*—Lady Nerevar*

First, I do not know for certain how Mara views arranged marriages, but stories abound of unexpected love blossoming even in the most stone-hearted of unions. The Divine Mother is a patient gardener. Second *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]*, while I cannot say that all affairs are equal in Mara's eyes, I do ask that those who partake in these secretive trysts ask themselves whose garden they are watering.

*I deeply love a Khajiit, and throughout our travels, I have been considering proposing, but as an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md), I am unfamiliar with Khajiit marriage customs. How should I propose? If she agrees, what preparations should I make for the wedding?*

*—Faustus Bloodskal*

I did not have a traditional Khajiiti upbringing, few orphaned [furstocks](Lore_Furstock.md) do, but my foster parents did their best.

They had an idea in their heads of what I wanted, what I needed. And so, they sang [songs](Lore_Songs.md) with [words](Lore_Ta%27agra.md) they could not pronounce and took me to celebrations where they were perceived as tourists. I will always appreciate their efforts, but as I grew older, I came to understand the unique eccentricities of our little life together. The rambunctious Khajiit and his [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) parents.

Mara teaches us that all proof of love comes from within us. Traditions are but one form of expression no better or worse than simple words spoken from the heart.

As traveling companions, no doubt you have heard your fair share of nostalgic tales and campfire yearnings, yes? Trust that you already know more than you realize and do not be afraid to follow your instincts. Now, to offer some pragmatic advice. If there is a mother still in the picture, be sure to butter her up. Second, it is never too early to save your [coin](Lore_Currency.md). Khajiiti marriages are, very often, weeklong affairs.

*What [crops](Lore_Produce.md) are eaten during Hearts Week festivities? Other than [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md), of course. Var Var. This one is hoping to make a special [treat](Lore_Cuisine.md) for a faithful companion.*

*—Purrscha, Priestess of [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md)*

Priestess of Rajhin indeed! My mouth waters at this question and it will be some time before I can sate these cravings. Var var var, I will try not to hold it against you.

Brindled Pudding comes to mind. A [rice](Lore_Rice.md) dish made with [carrots](Lore_Carrot.md), [ginger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ginger), cardamom and crushed [pistachios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pistachio). Then there is a dish where soft balls of [cheese](Lore_Cheese.md) soak in sweetened [milk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Milk) spiced with saffron. Its name varies, but I know it as Spilled Moons. Then of course there is gold fudge, a dessert made with so much clarified [butter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Butter) that it is best you don't think about it.

I'm certain by now you've noticed two things. One, we Khajiit enjoy [our sweets](Lore_Khajiit_Cuisine.md), and two, many of our celebratory deserts *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* are made with pantry staples, reminders of a time when the deserts of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) tested our people. This way we can remember to enjoy the comforts of today.

*How do those afflicted with [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) express love? Do [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) feel or show love toward others in a unique way?*

*—Lone Werewolf Erickson*

Forgive me for using this question to scratch a particular, nagging itch of mine. You see, in my time as a Priest of Mara, I have found a great many folks who are dismissive of cursed lovers. I have always found this to be strange. Some say, "Kuzam-jo, aren't [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and werewolves ravenous beasts? Would their bloodthirst not corrupt notions of love?" To which I ask, "is greedy love unique to the cursed?" Certainly not. Yes, there are those in our lives who will mistake hunger for love, but many of these individuals are not cursed. And while they are misguided, it is also not your responsibility to set them on the right path. We must all make the most of the time we have.

Which is to say, yes. Werewolves, and all other cursed individuals, mortal or otherwise, can still carry the spark of love. You need only listen to the [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) which howl at the [moons](Lore_Moons.md). Are their songs without yearning? Without passion? No. I think not. Now imagine how many ways a werewolf can howl, "I love you."

*Since Heart's Day originated as [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md)'s summoning day, is the [rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rose) [Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md)'s presence still felt in the present celebrations? Rose petals guide you!*

*—Lady Lunetta Gleamblossom*

It is true that this [holiday](Lore_Holidays.md)'s roots date back to the Prince's summoning day, but I am proud to say that its meaning has grown far beyond his influence. If his presence is felt at all, I hope that it is only in the way we should all love with unabashed revelry. Take that flickering spark of love, or friendship, or comradery, and nourish it without reservation. Celebrate the gift it is to love and be loved.
