# Divine Prosecution

The Divine Prosecution are a state-level law enforcement organization responsible for enforcing both secular and religious law, ensuring [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) social and cultural rules are adhered to.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSuriwen-1)</sup> Under the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), they were a division of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> and were the highest authority of law and order on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), second only to the Queen and her Royal Court.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> They target what they perceive to be heresy, including [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Dt Oy Ei S-4)</sup> The Divine Prosecution are wary of outsiders, but were known to have deputized skilled adventurers to handle dangerous threats after the island of Summerset was opened to outsiders by Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

The Divine Prosecution is made up of jurisreeves, justiciars and bailiffs. Jurisreeves oversee investigations, normatively in groups of three. Each such group, known as an examiner's quorum, consists of a revelator-naganwe or "death seer"<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSoravil-6)</sup> to conduct magical inquiries; an admanen—literally "listening eye"—to act as a fulcrum and balance competing theories about the case; and a thalmilan to judge the evidence and render a verdict.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTarnamir-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONLorne-8)</sup> The rule of three relates to an Altmer superstition that holds the number two to be inauspicious,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONLorne-8)</sup> but also serves the utilitarian purpose of ensuring that each case is examined from multiple perspectives.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTarnamir-7)</sup>

One of the units of the Divine Prosecution is the Hindalatus wing, which is led by a jurisreeve titled the Chief Inquisitor and charged with the investigation of "adoctrinal crimes".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTarnamir-7)</sup> Before [Queen Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md)'s decree opened Summerset to foreigners and the rise of the [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md), the Prosecution primarily busied itself with settling petty disputes and policing seditious speech.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONLindafwe-9)</sup>
