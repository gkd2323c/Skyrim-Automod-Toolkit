[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/SR-symbol-Falkreath.png/200px-SR-symbol-Falkreath.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-symbol-Falkreath.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-symbol-Falkreath.png) The symbol of Falkreath [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg/200px-SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath_Hold.jpg) Pine forests in Falkreath Hold Falkreath Hold (or simply called **Falkreath**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:573</sup> is one of the main [holds](Lore_Hold.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), known for its lush pine forest. [Lake Ilinalta](Lore_Lake_Ilinalta.md), the province's largest body of freshwater, constitutes a significant portion of the hold. The people of the hold are generally prosperous farmers and loggers, thanks to the Ilinalta.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:573</sup> Falkreath has an ancient association with battle and [death](Lore_Nord_Death_Beliefs.md). Like the hold, the capital city is called [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), but it is also known as the "heroes' graveyard". It contains one of the largest cemeteries in the province.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRNenya-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-COSRF-3)</sup> Falkreath's alignment has sometimes shifted between the Kingdom of Skyrim and the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md), the autonomous government of western [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RA-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAH-5)</sup>

The region connects the southern holds of Skyrim with the northern reaches of west-central [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), making it a major thoroughfare.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:573</sup> It is historically neighbored by the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) to the south, [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to the northwest, [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) to the east, and [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md) to the north.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRMap-6)</sup> From across the provincial border to the south, Falkreath is connected to northern Cyrodiil near [County Bruma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:County_Bruma) and [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) in northeast [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMap-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BLFalkreath-8)</sup>

## Society and Ecosystem
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/SR-book-Wolfsbane.png/80px-SR-book-Wolfsbane.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Wolfsbane.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Wolfsbane.png) Artist's depiction of a nightshade [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg) The Ancestor Glade Falkreath is predominantly inhabited by [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Skyrim, but many races have at one point inhabited or laid claim to the region, including [Colovians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Colovian), <sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RA-4)</sup> the [Keptu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keptu), [Minotaurs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minotaur),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EOBS-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MTCDTBH-10)</sup> and the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OOS-11)</sup> Many in the capital city, from shops to families, draw inspiration from battle and death for their names. The region's signature [cheese](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheese) dish, [Barrowost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nord_Cuisine#Barrowost), resembles common eidar cheese but is exclusively aged in Nordic barrows. The cheese acquires an intense earthy sweetness and is punctuated with sharp notes. The Nords practice an ancient tradition with Barrowost called "grave curd" in which fresh cheese is interred atop of the coffin of the recently deceased. On every anniversary, the bereaved eat a fifth of the cheese until it is finished.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-COSRF-3)</sup>

Falkreath has at times been an independent state.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RA-4)</sup> A merchant family from the late Second Era, the Far Falkreath Estate, had strong ties to the Holds of Skyrim.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-RGTobias-12)</sup> The [Glenmoril Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd), more specifically the Hagfeather Coven, has lived in western Falkreath since at least the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The Hagfeathers were known to worship the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Namira](Lore_Namira.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TGW-13)</sup> but at some point, a coven in Falkreath worshipped [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). It was the Hircine-worshipping coven which bestowed [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) on the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md), starting with the Harbinger [Terrfyg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Terrfyg).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRKodlak-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KJ-15)</sup>

The region's main feature, aptly named **Pine Forest**,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Astrid-16)</sup> stretches between the borders of the Reach and Whiterun, as well as the snow-laden country between the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) and the Jerall Mountains. It is dense with coniferous trees and a wide variety of flora, including [mora tapinella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mora_Tapinella),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SRMora-17)</sup> all four varieties of [mountain flower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mountain_Flower), [nightshade](Lore_Nightshade.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SRNightshade-18)</sup> [nirnroot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirnroot), and [thistles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thistle_Branch).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SRThistle-19)</sup> There are also many [snowberries](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snowberry_Bush) throughout the eastern hills and the path toward Cyrodiil.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SRSnowberry-20)</sup>

Falkreath hosts a wide variety of wildlife. The temperate water of the enormous Lake Ilinalta hosts many different types of fish. The more common include [Abecean longfin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abecean_Longfin), [Cyrodilic spadetail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrodilic_Spadetail), [histcarp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Histcarp), [river betty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:River_Betty), [salmon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salmon), [silverside perch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silverside_Perch), and [slaughterfish](Lore_Slaughterfish.md). Others are [catfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Catfish), [pearlfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pearlfish), [pygmy sunfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pygmy_Sunfish), and the [spadefish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spadefish).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FM2-21)</sup>

In the forest can be found several species of [wolf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolf) and [bear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear), as well as [frostbite spiders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostbite_Spider) and [spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan). The local Nords were able to tame [sabre cats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sabre_Cat) and train them as mounts.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONSabre-22)</sup> A cave known as Bloated Man's Grotto protects some wildlife within an idyllic subterranean ecosystem. Several types of [butterfly](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Butterfly) live in the grotto, such as blue butterflies, the monarch butterfly, and even the [luna moth](Lore_Luna_Moth.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SRBloated-23)</sup> The [pine thrush](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pine_Thrush) is a bird commonly found in southern Skyrim and Falkreath.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SRThrush-24)</sup>

In the east, a road connects [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md) to the Rift through a mountain pass called the [Lion's Den](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lion%27s_Den).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ITLD-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONLion-26)</sup> Southeast of Falkreath's capital is one of the fabled [Ancestor Glades](Lore_Ancestor_Glade.md), hidden groves across Tamriel where the [Ritual of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Ritual_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) can be performed. The ritual, used by the [Moth Priests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moth_Priests) to read [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), involves placing a "draw knife" in the glade's central grotto to harvest the [canticle tree](Lore_Canticle_Tree.md) and attract [ancestor moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SRDexion-27)</sup>

## History

### Ancient background
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg) Bjarfrud Skjoralmor [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg/200px-SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg) Bleak Falls Barrow Falkreath's ancient history is intertwined with the many cultures and people of the surrounding area. Though the name is of [elvish](Lore_Elvish.md) origin,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:574</sup> the city was founded by [Bjarfrud Skjoralmor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor), an ancient Nord warrior who repeatedly drove a "beastly host" from their "heathen wood". As the host kept returning, he eventually decided to chop down the woods and create a town.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EOBS-9)</sup> These woods had evidently been the ancestral home of a group of Minotaurs.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MTCDTBH-10)</sup> The [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) may have lived in Falkreath Hold, as well, claiming it was their ancient right to return to the land in the early Second Era.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OOS-11)</sup> One of Falkreath's oldest ruins is the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md), an important sanctum for [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), the Daedric Prince of Darkness. Deep in its halls is the Ebonmere, a portal to Nocturnal's plane of Oblivion, [Evergloam](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Evergloam). It has existed since long before recorded history.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SRKarliah-28)</sup>

Presumably, ancient Falkreath was home to a [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) tribe called the [men-of-'kreath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Men-of-kreath), one of several which were enslaved by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) of the Cyrodilic [heartland](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartland) and taken to [Sardavar Leed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sardavar_Leed).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TA-29)</sup> Some no doubt participated in the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), which overthrew the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md). Those elves who tried to escape north into Skyrim during the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md) were slaughtered by High King [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md) and his forces.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASIV-30)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/SR-place-Twilight_Sepulcher.jpg/200px-SR-place-Twilight_Sepulcher.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Twilight_Sepulcher.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Twilight_Sepulcher.jpg) The Twilight Sepulcher Unlike other parts of Skyrim, the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) has only one known temple: [Bleak Falls Barrow](Lore_Bleak_Falls_Barrow.md), in the mountains west of Riverwood, close to the border with Whiterun Hold. Deep in its interior, the Keeper of the [Dragonstone](Lore_Dragonstone.md) was buried and given the eternal mission to guard their namesake, a stone slab with a map of [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) burials all over Skyrim. When the hold was formally established is unknown, but it was sometime in early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), or perhaps even in the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), just after the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-SRFarengar-31)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Granite)</sup>

### Early History
In the early First Era, Falkreath Hold took part in the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md), a roughly half-a-century-long war for the High Kingship after the death of [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md). Falkreath would eventually pledge allegiance to Jarl [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-KOV-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PGE1E-33)</sup> Roughly two hundred years later, Falkreath Hold come into major conflict again, this time with the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order). The [First Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Empire) had at one point acquired southern Skyrim, only to lose it to the Nords ruled by High King [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Sometime around [1E 478](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_478), eight months before [Rislav Larich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rislav_Larich) became King of [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad), the High King died at the [Battle of Sungard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Sungard)<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-RTR-34)</sup> while defending Falkreath city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:574</sup> As the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) was deciding on who should succeed him, the Empire reclaimed Falkreath Hold. The Pact of Chieftains decided on Kjoric's son, [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md), as the new High King. While accounts claim Hoag died in the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors),<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-FSOKW-35)</sup> a legend persists that he died on the same spot his father did when he reclaimed Falkreath for the last time years later.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:574</sup>

By the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Falkreath and its environs were part of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RA-4)</sup> In [1E 2331](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2331), both the estates and their enemies, the former Alessian-controlled Cyrodiil, were destitute thanks to the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SH4-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-37)</sup> For the next four hundred years, the Colovian kings were desperate and greedy for wealth, or as the *[Remanada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remanada)* describes it, they "became through pride and habit as like thief-barons and forgot covenant".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RA-4)</sup> In [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) of distant [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) landed on the northern shores of Skyrim and Morrowind. They cut a swathe south until they reached Falkreath's side of the [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), en route to Cyrodiil.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-LOTD-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-PGE3EOL-39)</sup> [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) assembled the disparate Colovians and the [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) to confront them at the mountain pass. The Imperials emerged victorious, and Reman went on to become the first Emperor of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-37)</sup>

### The Kingdom of Western Skyrim
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg) Hakkvild's hall built after his victory [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/ON-render-Mother_Ciannait.jpg/200px-ON-render-Mother_Ciannait.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Mother_Ciannait.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Mother_Ciannait.jpg) Mother Ciannait of the Icereach Coven After the death of High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), contenders for the High Kingship came down to [Jarl Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) and Logrolf's daughter, [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md). In the end, two separate kingdoms were established between the east and the west. Falkreath Hold joined High King Svartr to create the Kingdom of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TCOF-40)</sup> Roughly around that time, [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) was destroyed by the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), and many Orcish refugees flocked elsewhere. Chieftain [Yashnag gro-Yazgu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yashnag_gro-Yazgu) brought many of his people east to build a chiefdom in western Falkreath, which would become one of the largest examples of Orcish dominion to ever rise in Skyrim.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OOS-11)</sup>

Yashnag's chiefdom was a bane to the Nords of Falkreath Hold for thirty years. At some point, the chieftain killed the Jarl in battle. His son, Hakkvild, inherited the throne to a decrepit hold largely occupied by the Orcs. In [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467), Hakkvild challenged Yashnag and his champions in an obscure Orcish ritual trial by combat and single-handedly won each encounter. How he came to learn of this ritual is unknown, but Yashnag's followers fled Skyrim for the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains) and [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OOS-11)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONLoad Forsaken-41)</sup> Hakkvild burned the chiefdom down to the ground and earned the sobriquet "Yashnag-Slayer".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OOS-11)</sup> In [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), Chief [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog_gro-Bagrakh) assembled a force of Orcish warriors to reclaim Yashnag's former land in Falkreath, but they were eventually pushed out by the Nords.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TCOKK4-42)</sup>

After the fall of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Falkreath Hold was caught in a minor but destructive conflict between the [Second Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Second_Legion) and the former advisors of the Emperors, the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md). The legionnaires chased the witches' coven north over the Jerall Mountains and confronted them north and west of Falkreath's capital. After the slaughter of legion soldiers, the coven took shelter in the graves west of the city. The witches managed to escape them when their matriarch, Mother [Ciannait](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ciannait), resurrected a horde of undead Nords and used [illusion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion) magic to strike fear into the soldiers. While the Second Legion retreated back to Cyrodiil, the Icereach Coven fled north to their [home](Lore_Icereach.md) on the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-MTCCM-43)</sup>

### The Siege of Falkreath Hold
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned_02.jpg) Domihaus storms the Jarl's keep [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fa/ON-npc-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg) Thane Eerika Skjoralmor In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), citizens of Falkreath's capital were kidnapped by beasts at night. Jarl [Hjurgol Skjoralmor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hjurgol_Skjoralmor) dismissed these claims as mere rumors.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-IRTCR-44)</sup> At that same time, [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) raided villages across the countryside and refugees poured into Falkreath.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ULTM-45)</sup> Led by the man-bull warrior, Domihaus the Bloody-Horned, an allied clan of Reachmen and Minotaurs called the [Dreadhorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreadhorn_Clan) would lay siege to the city for weeks.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ULTM-45)</sup>

While Jarl Hjurgol Skjoralmor was held up in his keep, his daughter, Thane [Eerika Skjoralmor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eerika_Skjoralmor), led the defense of the city.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONDemise-46)</sup> A runner just barely made it out of the city and brought word of the assault to the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted), who sent [warriors](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to help break the siege. These warriors arrived from the eastern outskirts of town and fought their way through to Eerika and together they killed Domihaus. But before they could, Domihaus managed to breach the Jarl's keep, and in the chaos, the Jarl was killed. Consequently, Eerika inherited the throne.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONDemise-46)</sup>

It is unknown if Falkreath Hold continued its association with the Kingdom of Western Skyrim. Both entities saw a drastic change in leadership in the same year, with Eerika inheriting the throne from her father and later Princess [Svana](Lore_Svana.md) taking the throne after the death of her father, High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOArt Greymoor-47)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ONWolf-48)</sup>

### Falkreath in the Third Empire
By the late Second Era, Falkreath was again part of the Colovian Estates. Their ruler, [King Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md), wanted to unify the Estates, but in order to do so, he needed to secure his borders to the north with the Reach, where the Nords and local Reachmen had warred for centuries. In an attempt to solidify his hold, he allied with Skyrim and chose his general, [Hjalti Early-Beard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hjalti_Early-Beard), to lead a small band of Colovian warriors and Nord berserkers. The [Battle of Old Hroldan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Old_Hroldan) proved instrumental in Cuhlecain's efforts to unify the west, which came to fruition within a year.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAH-5)</sup>

Holds such as Falkreath were kingdoms under the Third Empire,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PGE3E-49)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-AR-50)</sup> and like the rest of Skyrim, they supported Queen [Potema Septim](Lore_Potema.md) in the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-51)</sup> When the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) broke out in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Skyrim was under siege and faced severe casualties, specifically in the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md), and it was said everything between Falkreath and [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) was laid to waste by the [daedric hordes](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-OBGate-52)</sup>

### Falkreath in the Skyrim Civil War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/SR-npc-Siddgeir.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Siddgeir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Siddgeir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Siddgeir.jpg) Jarl Siddgeir of Falkreath [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png/200px-LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png) Alduin's destruction of Helgen Sometime after the Pale Pass was re-discovered in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433),<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-OBLifting-53)</sup> an inter-provincial road was established between Cyrodiil and Skyrim. The Pale Pass entered Skyrim through Falkreath Hold, specifically south of Helgen, and Fort Neugrad was there to guard the road between them.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-IM-54)</sup> By roughly [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), the Empire had a strong military presence in the region, especially through the town of Helgen.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BLFalkreath-8)</sup>

After the death of High King [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the province fell into the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) between the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), based in western Skyrim, and the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), who rallied the Old Holds together for their common cause. Jarl [Dengeir of Stuhn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dengeir_of_Stuhn) was a forthright Stormcloak supporter, but vacated the hold's throne and was replaced by his nephew, [Siddgeir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Siddgeir), a supporter to the Empire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRNenya-2)</sup> The Legion based in Falkreath was spearheaded by Legate Skulnar, and [Fort Neugrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Neugrad) was a vital point of contention for control over the region.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-SRCourt-55)</sup>

In that same year, the Imperial Legion captured Ulfric Stormcloak after a skirmish near [Darkwater Crossing](Lore_Darkwater_Crossing.md). The Jarl, Stormcloak soldiers, and other criminals were taken to Helgen to be publicly executed. In attendance was General [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md), the Military Governor of Skyrim, and [Elenwen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elenwen), the First Emissary of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-SRUnbound-56)</sup> Before the Jarl could be executed, however, a black [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) descended onto the town and razed it to the ground. In the ensuing destruction, Ulfric escaped town and fled back to Windhelm. Meanwhile, a doom-driven hero escaped through Helgen's underbelly: the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who later scaled the northern mountains to reach Bleak Falls Barrow and retrieve the Dragonstone.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-SRUnbound-56)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SRBleak-57)</sup>

It was also around this time that the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md) tracked down a [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) sanctuary built at the ancient site of a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) in Falkreath Hold and destroyed it. It is unknown if the Brotherhood survived the assault or died out completely in the province.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-SRDeath-58)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-SRDestroy-59)</sup>
