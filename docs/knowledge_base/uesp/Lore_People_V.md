#### Silvenar Varbarenth (fl. [3E 80](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_80))
[Varbarenth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varbarenth) was [the Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Silvenar) of the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) people. Not much is known about him, except that he was the son of Varbaril. The timing of his assumption of the Silvenar role and the identity of his [Green Lady](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Lady) counterpart is unknown. However, it is confirmed that he held the office as of [3E 80](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_80).

#### Vaia (fl. before 2E 582)
[Vaia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaia) was an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) hero held in high regard by the Orc branch of the Cult of [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac). She features in the Orsimer religious scripture *The Book of the Warrior God* and has a passage titled *Vaia's Courage* devoted to her and her deeds. In this passage, she is described as a valiant defender, who managed to shatter the enchanted hammer of [Rhogar the Destroyer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rhogar_the_Destroyer) with her small wooden shield.

Vaia's Golden [Ash](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash) symbolizes the strength, heritage, and unity of the Orsimer people. The symbol originates from the ash tree, from which she carved out the shield that repelled the blow of Rhogar the Destroyer. Her deeds influenced the metalworking of the Trinimac Cult faithful. Those who follow the way of Trinimac often emboss their axes and shields with Vaia's Golden Ash.

#### Kinlady Valarmil Corela (fl. Dawn Era or Late Middle Merethic Era)
**Kinlady** [Valarmil Corela](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valarmil_Corela) was an [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) noblewoman and the earliest [Corelanyan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clan_Corelanya) in recorded history. She was a follower of [Saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tribunal_Saints) [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) who chose not to join his exodus.

#### Valaste (fl. 2E 582)
[Valaste](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valaste) was the Master of Incunabula of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), responsible for maintaining the guild's libraries, arcanaea, and seeking out new and interesting works to add to the collection.

#### Valdar (fl. late Merethic Era)
[Valdar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valdar) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warrior who resided within [Valthume](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valthume) in ancient times. When the tyrannical [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) [Hevnoraak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hevnoraak) died, Valdar swore to ensure that the [lich](Lore_Lich.md) never rose from his grave. That oath persisted for countless generations, beyond Valdar's own death.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) entered Valthume and encountered the spirit of Valdar, whose strength was fading. The warrior would not be able to keep Hevnoraak at bay much longer, and asked the Dragonborn to bring him Hevnoraak's vessels so they could weaken the lich before he awoke. The Dragonborn collected the vessels and emptied them, preventing Hevnoraak from reawakening at full power as he'd hoped. The pair performed a ritual to awaken Hevnoraak in a weakened state, and the Hero defeated the Dragon Priest. With Hevnoraak vanquished, Valdar's duty was no longer necessary, and his spirit was finally able to rest.

#### Jarl Valdimar (fl. 4E 122)
**Valdimar** was the Jarl of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) during the [Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md). He lost family during the catastrophe, and some of his council insisted that the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) was to blame. Arch-Mage [Deneth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deneth) wrote to him soon after the collapse denying the collage's involvement and urging calm. At the conclusion of his letter to Valdimar, Deneth urged him to not allow the belief that the college was responsible for the collapse to take root among the people of Winterhold.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTGC-1)</sup> He lost his home in the collapse, so one of old Winterhold's buildings was repurposed as the Jarl's Longhouse.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>:478</sup> Nearly eighty years after the Great Collapse, many of the inhabitants of Winterhold were hostile toward the college and distrusted it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>:477</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Korir-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thaena-4)</sup>

#### Valdur (fl. late Merethic Era)
[Valdur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valdur) was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior from the late- [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). In his lifetime, he was one of the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) who died during the conflict against [Sinmur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sinmur), the [half-giant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Half-Giant) chieftain. Valdur and his shield-sister, Hakra were buried in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md)'s western corners and bound to the region as its caretakers, to forever protect it from threats. He is buried in an underground barrow called Taarengrav, located south of [Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md) and southwest of [Nimalten](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nimalten).

#### Valentis (fl. First Era)
[Valentis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valentis) was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) prince and the brother of Sorcerer-King [Celemaril](Lore_Celemaril.md). He revolted against his brother and founded the town of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) at the site of his brother's tomb.

#### Valindor (fl. 4E 201)
[Valindor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valindor) is a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) living in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). Being born and raised in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), as a child he played in the towering trees, living among his brethren. He later apprenticed in the holy city [Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silvenar) under a master armorer. He evidently was a great student as his master urged him to take over his business on his deathbed but Valindor instead chose to leave Valenwood and wander [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

He would eventually settle in the [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) city of [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), where he would take employment at the city's [fishery](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fish) and [meadery](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black-Briar_Mead). Valindor lived a humble life and was a pleasant member of the community, staying busy between his two jobs, giving to those in need, and encouraging [addicts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drugs) to become sober. Valindor however, occasionally expressed regrets over the simple life and routine he had become accustomed to and wondered if he should move on, little did he know a massive shakeup to his life was coming.

Valindor turned out to be a blood descendant of [Lord Harkon](Lore_Harkon.md), the [Vampire Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord) ruler of [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md). Only the descendants of Harkon could be conferred his blood to become a [Vampire](Lore_Vampire.md), and only under a certain alignment of the [stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars). The Volkihar identified Valindor as one of these such descendants along with the right aligning of the stars. The clan sent an individual to Riften to turn Valindor as they wished to have an informant in his area of the province. After receiving the unholy blood, Valindor was grateful for his transformation and considered it a gift.

#### Valsirenn (fl. 2E 582)
[Valsirenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valsirenn) was high ranking [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) monk and member of the [Conclave of the Eleven Forces](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conclave_of_the_Eleven_Forces). She was instrumental in stopping the [Daedric invasion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Triad) of [Summerset](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Summerset) and dispatching the Abyssal Cabal. Following the death of [Iachesis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iachesis) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) she took the position of interim Ritemaster and became the temporary leader of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), a long-standing monastic group of mages dedicated to studying the [Old Ways](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism).

#### Grandmaster Vashu-Pir (fl. 1E 2734 - 2758)
[Vashu-Pir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vashu-Pir) was the second leader of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), who, together with [Ja'darri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ja%27darri) was responsible for the defeat of the Black Beast, Laatvulon. He was remembered as one of the four greatest Grandmasters the Dragonguard have ever known. He served as the Grandmaster from [1E 2734](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2734) to [1E 2758](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2758).

#### Vastarie (fl. early 2E - 2E 582)
[Vastarie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vastarie), also known as **the Witch of Azurah**, is an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) [Lich](Lore_Lich.md) notable for her study of souls, the results which were a great boon to the dark arts of [Necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy). Prior to becoming a necromancer, she studied on [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) alongside other notable [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) Monks, such as [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) and [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md). She is believed to be the first to discover how to use [Sigil Geodes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sigil_Geode) to bind spirits, and personally brought the first [black soul gems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Soul_Gem) to Tamriel after opening a portal to [Coldharbour.](Lore_Coldharbour.md)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_A)</sup>

When Mannimarco first left Artaeum to form the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult), she and her future husband Telacar initially followed suit, eager to research soul magic more in-depth and support their friend. Soon however, Vastarie fled the Worm Cult when she realized the extent of Mannimarco's obsession with power. Eventually, she was forced to leave her husband as well, after the death of their son led him down a similar path.

Vastarie is a fervent worshipper of the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura). Sometime after her falling out with Vanus, she achieved lichdom and dedicated herself to serving Azura.

#### Valerica (fl. 4E 201)
[Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md) was the castigated wife of Lord [Harkon](Lore_Harkon.md), leader of the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md) clan. After Harkon made a deal with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to become [Vampire Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord), Valerica gained the honorary title of Blood Matron of [Castle Volkihar](Lore_Castle_Volkihar.md). Valerica, Harkon and their daughter [Serana](Lore_Serana.md) had a falling out as Harkon became determined to blot out the sun forever. He required the blood of a true [Daughter of Coldharbour](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daughters_of_Coldharbour) to power [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md), and he had the option of using either Valerica or Serana's blood. To stop him, Valerica sealed herself and the Elder Scroll inside the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md), and hid away Serana, as well, so Harkon could never complete his ritual with their blood. While Lord Harkon still somewhat cared for Serana, he has no remaining respect for his estranged wife. Upon Harkon's death, Valerica was finally able to leave the Soul Cairn and returned to Castle Volkihar.

#### Valus Odiil (fl. 3E 433)
**Valus Odiil** was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) adventurer who wandered [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He was an advanced user of heavy armor, having been taught by a Redguard soldier named Pranal in Hammerfell. He was once the wielder of the artifact [Chillrend](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chillrend). Valus eventually settled down and had two sons, Antus and Rallus. He began working his ancestral farm just outside the city of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and also owned a shack within the city. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), too old to fight, he and his sons were driven from their farm by raiding [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) from the [Great Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Forest). Unable to persuade the city guard to help, Valus enlisted the help of the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to aid his sons in reclaiming the farm.

#### Vanus Galerion (fl. 2E)
[Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) (sometimes **Galerion Vanus**) (born **Trechtus**), called the **Great Mage**, was the founder and first Archmagister of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and one of the most famous users of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) in the history of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). By some accounts Galerion was the "quintessential mortal mage", his magical ability acknowledged even by the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), who believed his power could be used to complete the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in a matter of mere hours. He himself claimed to have been the greatest mage of his time. [Divayth Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divayth_Fyr) regarded him as a gifted magical talent who, in his view, squandered that potential by devoting himself to the pursuits of the Mages Guild. Vanus trained with the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) along with fellow student [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md). He was a staunch opponent of Mannimarco's practice of [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy), and ultimately led an army of mages against Mannimarco's legions of undead. Though he succeeded in halting Mannimarco's advance, he was slain in the battle.

#### Emperor Varen Aquilarios ([2E 536](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_536) - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) (also called **the Betrayer** by [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic)) was an Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). In [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), Emperor Leovic of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) issued a decree that legalized [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), which caused Varen, the Duke of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) and Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), to initiate a [rebellion](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) against the Emperor. In [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Varen and his forces stormed the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), where he personally slew Leovic in the throne room of the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md). He then declared himself emperor and married Leovic's widow, [Clivia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn), to consolidate his rule.

Varen subsequently led a group called the [Five Companions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Five_Companions) on a two-year quest to recover the lost [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), which they eventually recovered. Heeding the advice of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), Varen planned to use the amulet in a ritual to persuade [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to make him [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) and therefore a legitimate emperor. In [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), as the ritual was attempted, Mannimarco corrupted it, which triggered the event known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md). Varen vanished in the chaos, and Clivia assumed the Imperial Throne as empress regent in his absence, setting [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) on a course for the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).

Following the Soulburst, Varen, who was believed dead, ended up at an abbey of the [Moth Priests](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), where he eventually became known as **the Prophet** after reading the abbey's [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). Word of Varen's [prophetic predictions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) from studying the Elder Scrolls spread far and wide, eventually reaching Mannimarco, who immediately arrested Varen for rumor-mongering and treason. Varen was imprisoned in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and later assisted [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in their efforts to defeat [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and stop the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).

#### Admiral Vasi Hadrach (fl. 2E 9th century)
[Vasi Hadrach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vasi_Hadrach) was the Admiral of the [New West Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) during the ninth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and one of the subordinates of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). He was assassinated by a rebel [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) in a brothel of [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) at some point before [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), when the title of the Admiral of the New West Navy was already held by his successor, [Amiel Richton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Richton).

#### Duke Vedam Dren (fl. 3E 427)
[Vedam Dren](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vedam_Dren) was Duke of the Imperial District of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Of noble [descent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dren), Dren was Hlaalu Grandmaster of the Vvardenfell district before he inherited his title. He ruled from the Grand Council Chambers in [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castle_Ebonheart), surrounded by Dunmer and Imperial Counselors.

Vedam Dren was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) [knight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights) but also thought of himself as a noble, a leader and a diplomat. Vedam is the older brother of [Camonna Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camonna_Tong) head [Orvas Dren](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orvas_Dren), who ran the [Dren Plantation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dren_Plantation), and the father of Ilmeni Dren, the leader of the abolitionist group [Twin Lamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Twin_Lamps).

After losing over half the Tong's shipments in rapid succession, Orvas toyed with the idea of having Vedam assassinated, so that he might consolidate his power and succeed Vedam as Duke of Vvardenfell and Grandmaster of House Hlaalu.

For more information see the full [Lore article](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vedam_Dren).

#### Veesk-Olan (fl. 2E 582)

#### Saint Veloth the Pilgrim (fl. Dawn Era or Late Middle Merethic Era)
[Saint Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md), also known as **Veloth the Pilgrim** or **Veloth the Prophet**, was a [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) (or [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md))<sup>[†](#intnote_race)</sup> mystic hailing from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), either during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) or Late Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). He is best known as the father of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) race, when he and his followers split from their Aldmeri roots and migrated en masse to their new homeland called Resdayn, now known as [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). After his passing, Veloth became arguably one of the most revered figures in Dunmer history, and became known as the [patron saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_Saints) of outcasts and seekers of spiritual knowledge.

#### Count Verandis Ravenwatch (fl. 1E - 2E 582)
[Verandis Ravenwatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Verandis_Ravenwatch) was a pureblood [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) who had been turned directly by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). He was the founder of [House Ravenwatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Ravenwatch) and once a member of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). For a vampire, Verandis has a pure heart and was unwilling to give into his predatory instincts. He attempted to sway vampires away from their bloodthirsty natures, for his desired goal was peace with mortals. Verandis was known for his restraint. He only fed on the willing, notable for seeing mortals as equals. He expected other members of his House to follow suit with his code of conduct.

#### Empress Veraxia Tharn (fl. 2E 534 - 542)
[Veraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veraxia_Tharn) was a member of the prominent Nibenese [House Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tharn) of [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

During her lifetime the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) led by [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) the Black Drake conquered the [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and founded the [Longhouse Dynasty](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) in [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533).

One year after the conquest, in [2E 534](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_534) she married the Emperor [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach). The new Emperor decided to marry her for political gain, in an attempt to merge Reach sensibilities with Imperial culture in order to reinforce his connection and commitment to the Empire.

Her fate following the death of her husband in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541) or [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542), and the fall of the Longhouse Dynasty in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577) is unknown.

#### Potentate Versidue-Shaie (?b - [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324))
A prominent [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) [Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) of the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). #### Shadowscale Veezara (fl. [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Veezara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veezara) was an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) assassin and member of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). Veezara was one of the last known [Shadowscales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowscale), as such he was trained by the Dark Brotherhood since the day of his birth. For a time he served as an assassin in service to the King of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Eventually he was honorably discharged from service, and went on to wander [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) before he eventually made his way to the Dark Brotherhood Sanctuary in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The Sanctuary was the last remaining one on Tamriel, in the outskirts of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), and was active in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). There he become a loyal servant [Astrid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Astrid), the leader of the Sanctuary.

Veezara was introduced to a [new member](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) of his Dark Brotherhood family, saw the arrival of [Cicero](Lore_Cicero.md) and the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md), and assisted his new family member with their escape after assassinating [Vittoria Vici](Lore_Vittoria_Vici.md) at her wedding, only to later be murdered by the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md) during the massacre of the Falkreath Sanctuary. However, conflicting reports suggest that the latter events never happened, except for his murder during the massacre. In any case, with Veezara's death it is possible that the Shadowscales have become extinct.

#### Viridian Sentinel
The [Viridian Sentinel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Viridian_Sentinel) protects the human inhabitants of northern [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai) from the beasts and witches that live in the forest. According to the legend of the Viridian Sentinel, long ago the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) forged a covenant with the [Earth Bones](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) to protect their settlements located throughout all of High Rock from the wild. The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), who displaced the Direnni, had no such arrangement, and as a result their farms in Bangkorai were gradually overrun by the wilderness. The people were pushed to the brink of starvation, causing a young boy called [Greenward](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenward) to pray to [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) for salvation. Stendarr taught him to forge a new pact with the forest, giving him the power to restrain the natural creatures within. He thus became the first Viridian Sentinel.

The Viridian Sentinel has great power over the forest, and can use this power to perform potent feats of [magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Magic). When the [Glenmoril Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd) became bound to the forest, they also gave some deference to the Sentinel due to that connection (though they had not been a party to the original pact). Although Viridian Sentinels were known for their longevity, over time, the Sentinel ages like other mortals, and must pass on their power and title to a new mortal before death. Their eyes age at a normal rate, however, and therefore a Sentinel usually requires a monocle when reading to counteract deteriorating eyesight.

#### Viriya (fl. 4E 201)
[Viriya](Lore_Viriya.md) is a retired [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) adventuress and well-known master [fisherman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fish), who worked at the [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) fishery. There she attempted to breed [mudcrabs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mudcrab) in the hope of selling their meat on the town's market. She was seen as somewhat strange by her peers for her love of the creatures. Her primary work at the fishery involved facilitating special fishing requests and bounties from across the province. A responsibility she shared with fellow master fisherman, [Swims-In-Deep-Water](Lore_Swims-In-Deep-Water.md), up until her eventual retirement from the fishery. After which the duty of handling the distribution of the fishing requests fell solely on Swims-In-Deep-Water.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) ventured to the Riften docks to meet Viriya in the hopes of honing their fishing skills. Viriya tasked the Dragonborn with various errands, including catching a juvenile mudcrab for her to keep as a pet, which she named Snippy.

One of the many errands she assigned the Dragonborn was dealing with an infestation of mudcrabs that were threatening fishermen on the shore west of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md). She gifted the Dragonborn an [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) fishing rod that belonged to her father after it was resolved. Later on, Viriya sent an urgent message to the Dragonborn when the mudcrabs returned. Viriya and the Dragonborn soon joined forces with an army of guards to defeat a giant [Guardian Spirit](Lore_Guardian_Spirit.md) in the form of an [Emperor Crab](Lore_Emperor_Crab.md) that threatened Dawnstar.

Afterward, Viriya decided to retire from the fishing business. She tasked the Dragonborn with helping her collect supplies for a crab-based stall in Riften's Grand Plaza and gifted them a special amulet as a reward.

#### Vithrelnaak (Dawn Era - ?d)

[Voice of Ouze](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voice_of_Ouze) is a [Changeling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Changeling), a rare forest spirit found in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The Voice served as an envoy between the Oathbreakers and the Y'ffre's faithful ever since the Changelings were imprisoned in [Ouze](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ouze). Together with the guardian [Celonron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Celonron), they protected other Oathbreaker spirits.

#### Volrina Quarra (fl. 2E 582 - 3E 427)
[Volrina Quarra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volrina_Quarra) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) and the matriarch of the [Quarra Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quarra_Clan) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).

#### High Priest Vonos (?b - 4E 201)
[Vonos](Lore_Vonos.md) was the leader of the reformed [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). A devout worshipper of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), he would lead a successful plot to reopen an Oblivion Gate, allowing Dagon to once again have a foothold in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

#### Prince Voragiel Septim (fl. 3E 390s - 3E 405)
[Voragiel Septim](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md#cite_note-HEIRSDF-68) was a possible<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Heirs)</sup> son of [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md), and therefore a [Dragonborn Prince](Lore_Dragonborn.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Background Game-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TBOTD-18)</sup>

#### Captain Voruse Bethrimo (?b - 3E 427)
[Voruse Bethrimo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voruse_Bethrimo) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) captain of the [Buoyant Armigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Buoyant_Armigers) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He was also the wielder of [Crosier of St. Llothis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crosier_of_St._Llothis) and an Adept of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md).

#### Saint Vorys the Immolant (fl. late 1E)
[Saint Vorys the Immolant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vorys) (also known as the **Fiery One** to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md)) is a saint of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Vorys was the patron saint of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md). He had fallen out of reverence by [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) due to being "a bit unsavory", and [Roris the Martyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saint_Roris_the_Martyr)'s death during the [Arnesian War](Lore_Arnesian_War.md) elevated him to saintly status in his place by that point.

#### Voslaarum (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Voslaarum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voslaarum) was a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name meant "Claw-Water-Twin" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

Voslaarum and [Naaslaarum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naaslaarum) were revered twin dragons, who lived beneath the Frozen Lake within the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md). They were most likely killed at some point and resurrected by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) upon his return, although it is possible that they survived until [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).

At some point, in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), when the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) ventured to the Forgotten Vale, Voslaarum and Naaslaarum were resting beneath the ice of the lake and guarding a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md). They fought against the hero but were ultimately defeated and slain.

Having had his soul devoured by another dovah, Voslaarum was killed permanently and cannot be raised again.

#### King Vrage (fl. 1E 222 - 240)
[King Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md) (also called **Vrage the Gifted** or **Vrage the Butcher**) was the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the fifteenth monarch descended from [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md). He is widely known for founding and expanding the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) across the northern reaches of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), growing his domain to encompass parts of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and the [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay.md). Along with his predecessor [Harald](Lore_Harald.md), King Vrage can be credited with leading [man](Lore_Man.md)'s rise to dominance over [mer](Lore_Mer.md) in what would become the provinces of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and High Rock.

#### Vraseth (fl. Merethic Era)
A [Nede](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) from ancient [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), Vraseth was a member of the nomadic tribe who came across [Lamae Bal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamae_Bal) after she had been infected with [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md). He was involved in her attempted cremation, and was brutally raped when she awoke on the pyre. The [Vraseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vraseth) vampire bloodline was named after him, implying he was infected with vampirism.

#### Vuljotnaak (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Vuljotnaak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vuljotnaak) was a green [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Dark Maw Eat" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

The circumstances surrounding his death are contradictory and not well understood. The [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) dated his death to during or just after the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), according to ancient documents recovered from the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md). However, engravings found on an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) burial mask depict Vuljotnaak being "devoured" by a hissing serpent (likely a [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md)), which would place his death sometime after [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703). Despite what this depiction would imply, his soul was not devoured by a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md). Instead, like many dragons, his remains were retrieved by members of the Dragon Cult, and he was buried in a dragon mound near the settlement of [Granite Hill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Granite_Hill) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The location of his grave was later documented by the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) in [2E 373](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_373).

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Vuljotnaak was returned to life by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). He was later killed by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) and his soul was consumed, preventing him from ever again being resurrected.

#### Vulthuryol (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Vulthuryol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vulthuryol) was a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name meant "Dark-Overlord-Fire" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

It is unknown when or how Vulthuryol accessed [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md), but temporal disturbances were a method known to teleport massive creatures from all over Tamriel into the caverns. As of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Mzark Cavern system acted as his lair. Vulthuryol was not recorded in the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md)'s *[Atlas of Dragons](Lore_Atlas_of_Dragons.md)*.

In 4E 201, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md#Last_Dragonborn) ventured to Blackreach. Through use of the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), they awoke Vulthuryol. The two did battle in the Mzark Cavern, and the Dragonborn absorbed the dragon's soul. Having had his soul devoured by another dovah, Vulthuryol was killed permanently and cannot be raised again.

#### Vund the Stone-Shaper (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
**Warlord** [Vund](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vund) **the Stone-Shaper** was a skilled alchemist and the leader of a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md) marauder group active in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He led them against the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) settlement of [Dragon Bridge](Lore_Dragon_Bridge.md).

#### Vychamp (fl. late 3E)
Self-styled leader of the [Scenarist Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scenarist_Guild). He emasculated the children of [Nezmyt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nesmyt) due to jealousy over his helpmate. This hindered Nesmyt when he first joined the Scenarist Guild, as he needed to "pledge fealty" to Vychamp if he hoped to rise in rank, but would not do so because of the incursions Vychamp had committed against him.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TDC-19)</sup>

#### Arch-Curate Vyrthur (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Vyrthur](Lore_Vyrthur.md) was a [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) and the Arch-Curate of [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El) who for millennia sought vengeance against his one-time patron for allowing him to become a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md). He was the brother of Knight-Paladin [Gelebor](Lore_Gelebor.md), the only other uncorrupted Snow Elf known to have survived beyond the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). The entire [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) was once ruled by the Arch-Curate Vyrthur, assisted by his Prelates and Paladins.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Gelebor met the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) and requested that the hero kill Vyrthur in exchange for [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md), which was in Vyrthur's possession. Gelebor unknowingly playing right into his brother's hands, for the Last Dragonborn unwittingly brought the Arch-Curate exactly what he wanted: [Serana](Lore_Serana.md), a [Daughter of Coldharbour](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daughters_of_Coldharbour), a being of the purest evil in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Vyrthur sought revenge on Auri-El through the sundering of the sun, which serves as the connection between [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), the plane of the gods, and [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), which would thus eliminate Auri-El's influence on Nirn. This was to be achieved through the corruption of Auri-El's bow, accomplished by soaking it in the blood of a Daughter of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). He had long ago publicized a prophecy called the [Tyranny of the Sun](Lore_Tyranny_of_the_Sun.md), that was later revealed within the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), with the intent of drawing such a person to him. Fortunately, Serana and her companion were able to defeat and kill Vyrthur before he could bring his vengeance to fruition.

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Heirs) | In *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, the player character is given a personal [Background History](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Background_History#Notes) which they may read in their character menu, determined by their chosen class. In certain scenarios—such as those for the Bard or Rogue—the player character encounters a male heir of Emperor Uriel VII. There are 6 possible names assigned to this prince, chosen from a unique pre-designed list specifically created for this single use in the Background History: Pelagius, Cephorus, Uriel, Cassynder, Voragiel, and Trabbatus (in that order). In the Ranger background, the prince you met mentions that he has brothers that are also on the run; the plural use of "brothers" indicates the existence of multiple other princes. The number of possible names could logically indicate 6 in total. These figures precede the later introduction of Geldall, Enman, and Ebel, the Emperor's sons coined in *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*. Ultimately, no information on the other names for princes given in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* is available, as they are not referenced in subsequent games.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-20)</sup> |
