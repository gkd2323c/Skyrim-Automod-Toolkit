[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/SR-place-Windhelm.jpg/200px-SR-place-Windhelm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Windhelm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Windhelm.jpg) Windhelm circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Skyrim_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) A map of Skyrim showing Windhelm in the northeast Windhelm, the **City of Kings**, also known as "Ysgramor's city",<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Sot R-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRSerana-2)</sup> is a city in northeastern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), close to the border with [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and is the only substantial city in the [Hold](Lore_Hold.md) of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md).

It is not only the oldest extant city in Skyrim<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim Loading-3)</sup> (the oldest city being [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md)),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-5)</sup> but it does hold the title of being the oldest continuously inhabited human settlement in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLoading-6)</sup> Windhelm was once the capital of the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) and the old palace of the [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) dynasty, the [Palace of the Kings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Palace_of_the_Kings), still stands in the center of the city.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup>

[Morvunskar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Morvunskar) is located on the strategic high ground southwest of Windhelm, overlooking the main roads leading to the city. Like most Nord cities, Windhelm has a [Hall of the Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hall_of_the_Dead) where bodies are interred, overseen by a Priest of [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) who ensures that corpses are properly consecrated and cared for.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLoading-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup> The city's arena is known as the Hall of Trials.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLoading-6)</sup>

## History

### Merethic Era
Windhelm was founded by the legendary [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) and the crew of his ship, the Ylgermet, following the [Battle of the Moesring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_the_Moesring) in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). According to Nordic tradition, after the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) disbanded, the Ylgermet's crew stumbled upon the barrow of [Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol), Ysgramor's son. When Ysgramor looked south and saw where the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) flowed into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), he decreed that a great city should be built there to serve as a monument to the glories of mankind, a place from where he could gaze upon the hill of [Yngol's barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol_Barrow), and a gateway to guard Skyrim's interior against incursion via the river. It was built by [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) slaves of the early Nords.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Sot R-1)</sup>

Captive elves were forced to build the city in the architectural style of their captors, and it is alleged that as many elves died during Windhelm's construction as were killed by the Ylgermet's crew before they had arrived at the site. The city's great bridge was designed to allow a vigilant watch on the White River for elven attacks, and Ysgramor's palace, the Palace of the Kings, towered over the city to show man's dominion over the very winds that had caused Yngol's death on the voyage from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). An elaborate tomb was built in the catacombs beneath Windhelm to serve as Ysgramor's final resting place upon his death, but was never used because he chose to be buried [by the sea](Skyrim_Ysgramor%27s_Tomb.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Sot R-1)</sup>

### First Era
Ysgramor's descendants would rule the burgeoning territory of the Nords from Windhelm. When [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) assumed the mantle of High King in [1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143), he relinquished his holdings in Atmora and established Skyrim as an independent kingdom with Windhelm as its capital.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FC-8)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> During the time of [Vrage the Gifted](Lore_Vrage.md), the city became the capital of not just Skyrim, but the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) that also encompassed [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> When the First Empire of the Nords later collapsed amidst the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) that raged from [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369) to [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), the city was sacked.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/ON-place-Windhelm.jpg/200px-ON-place-Windhelm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Windhelm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Windhelm.jpg) Windhelm circa 2E 582 In [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), after the onset of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) was assassinated, precipitating a succession dispute between his daughter, [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md), and Jarl [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). While Freydis was accepted as High Queen by the Crown of Verity in Windhelm, a partial [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) convened in Solitude and declared Svartr to be High King. From then on, Skyrim was divided into two kingdoms, with Windhelm serving as the capital of [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) and the seat of Freydis and her line.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCOF-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-10)</sup>

Windhelm was sacked a second time in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572) by the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) army of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal), and then-High Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) was slain in battle along with her daughter, [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md), who briefly succeeded her.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-JTSK-11)</sup> The Palace of the Kings was one of the few of the city's original structures that survived these events, however it was badly damaged. The palace court was temporarily displaced as High King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md), who succeeded Nurnhilde, elected to repair the rest of city first, and work on the palace was not completed until [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Jorunn-12)</sup>

At some point circa 2E 582, Windhelm was raided by a group of [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), including the terrifying [Pale Fingers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pale_Fingers),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SRGazetteer-13)</sup><sup>:34</sup> an agent of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) mage [Deslandra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deslandra).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-14)</sup><sup>:60</sup> The raid was one of many in Eastern Skyrim, as Pale Fingers was wreaking havoc on the surface to draw attention away from Deslandra's plans underground in [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-14)</sup><sup>:60</sup> The Outer Watch learned of this and provided relief to the city.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SRGazetteer-13)</sup><sup>:34</sup>

At some point during the Second Era, the city also suffered under the disastrous reign of Jarl [Elgryr the Unminded](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elgryr_the_Unminded), who taxed his citizens to the point of destitution and eventually drove them to revolt.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWL-15)</sup> In the ninth century of the Second Era, during the formation of the [Third Empire of Tamriel](Lore_Empire.md), Windhelm served as a base for Imperial troops guarding the [Dunmeth Pass](Lore_Dunmeth_Pass.md) into Morrowind prior to that region's incorporation into the Empire.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup>

### Third Era
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Windhelm was an active settlement, being ruled by King Asgan. It had a rivalry with [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) and [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Arena-16)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Amol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amol) to the west, [Dragon Wood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon_Wood) to the south, [Laintar Dale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laintar_Dale) to the southwest, and Morrowind to the east, towards [Cormar View](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cormar_View).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Map Arena-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> When the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) broke out in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Skyrim was under siege and faced severe casualties. It was said that everything between [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) and Windhelm was attacked by the [Daedric hordes](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-OBGate-19)</sup>

### Fourth Era
By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Windhelm was the center of the [Stormcloak](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) [Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) against the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). Jarl [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), leader of the rebellion, sought to liberate Skyrim from Imperial control to protect the traditional Nord way of life and restore the right to worship [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), which had been banned Empire-wide by the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md). In open defiance of the ban, Windhelm's Temple of Talos was kept open throughout the conflict with the Empire. The city had also come to house a substantial population of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) refugees who had fled Morrowind in the aftermath of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md). The elves were relegated to the city's run-down Snow Quarter, later named the Gray Quarter after the Dunmer refugees, and faced constant suspicion from the xenophobic local Nords.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup>

## Notes
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Windhelm's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Paladins".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-20)</sup> This was finally used in *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)*, where the Windhelm Paladins arena team competed in an event known as the Dragon Games.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-21)</sup>
