[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg/200px-SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) High Hrothgar circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) High Hrothgar (named for the region of [Hrothgar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrothgar))<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1Map-1)</sup> is a secluded monastery, perched atop the highest peak in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3S-2)</sup> Here reside the few members of the ancient order of the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), who endeavor to become more attuned to the [voice of the sky](Lore_Thu%27um.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup> [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md), after his defeat at Red Mountain and subsequent meditation, built his home at High Hrothgar and founded the order to study the Thu'um in peace.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRHrothgar-4)</sup> The Nords believe that they were created by [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) when the sky breathed onto the peak of this mountain, and many journey up the seven thousand steps on pilgrimages.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-COTS-5)</sup> Few are granted access to its halls as they must be deemed worthy by the Greybeards first (notable examples including [Grundwulf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grundwulf), [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-JTSK-6)</sup> [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md)). Most who are accepted are then trained in the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md). Some can also receive the [Clever Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clever_Men) training at Hrothgar, linking the arts and philosophy of Greybeards with clever craft.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-HHTM-7)</sup>

High Hrothgar is also home to [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md), a reformed dragon who surreptitiously leads the Greybeards. He resides upon the inaccessible uppermost peak of the mountain, where Alduin was sent forwards in time at the end of the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) by ancient Nord heroes using an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).
