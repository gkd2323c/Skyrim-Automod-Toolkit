# Thrassian Plague


The Thrassian Plague (called **slug famine** by some ancient texts<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-R-1)</sup>) was an artificially-created pandemic of mass proportions that occurred in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-1TTP-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-C-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWR-4)</sup> It is estimated that half of the population of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AHo D-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NWo B-6)</sup> and even [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) as a whole,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-C-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWR-4)</sup> perished because of it. It was created by the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) and received its name from their homeland, the coral kingdom of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-OL-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3-E-8)</sup> The Sload spread the plague through the use of infected sea beasts that were sent onto the coastlines. The Sload would then use the chaos of the Thrassian plague to sack several cities along the coasts while also enslaving those who lived there.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tendil-9)</sup>

## History
While the reasons for its creation and the methods used to discover the culprits are uncertain, it has been established that the slugfolk of Thras released this calamity on Tamriel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-OL-7)</sup> Appearing in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200) (some sources state it was as late as [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260)),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLoading-10)</sup> it washed upon the shores of western Tamriel closest to Thras, starting at islands in the [Abecean](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), [Hew's Bane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hew%27s_Bane), [Southpoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Southpoint), [Malabal Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malabal_Tor), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall), [Cape Shira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cape_Shira), and the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Hot S-11)</sup> It soon spread further across the mainland (going as far as [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)), destroying cultures and decimating populations as it went.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3-Ot Eo M-12)</sup> In [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) the disease wrought devastation on the city of [Corgrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corgrad), which was flooded by its ruler [Nemfarion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nemfarion) in an effort to prevent the Plague from spreading, drowning most of its inhabitants.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESONemfarion-13)</sup> In the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), the nobilities of [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) all escaped to the [Isle of Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Isle_of_Balfiera).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TDC-14)</sup> The Corgrad Wastes were reputed to bear a taint of corruption well into the Second Era.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOFarowel-15)</sup> The Archmage [Syrabane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syrabane), using [his powerful ring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Warlock%27s_Ring), is said to have saved many people from perishing from the disease,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-16)</sup> possibly even finding a cure.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-FAo T-17)</sup>

While it's unknown when the disease fully subsided, cases of the Thrassian Plague were still being reported near [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) over four hundred years after its initial outbreak.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-WJot B-18)</sup> Bearers of the Plague still remained in the Corgrad Wastes over a millennium later in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), using blood sorcery to suppress the symptoms of the disease.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESONemfarion-13)</sup>

### Aftermath
The Thrassian Plague had major political and cultural repercussions on Tamriel, the most notable one being the assembling of the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md), tasked with the invasion of Thras. Forces were sent from almost every nation of western Tamriel, ancient enemies putting aside their differences to exact revenge on the Sload.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3-H-19)</sup> Assisted by Syrabane<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-16)</sup> and led by [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo), its Baron-Admiral and the king of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), the All Flags Navy "sailed to Thras, slaughtered all the Sload they could find, and, with great unknown magicks, sunk their coral kingdoms into the sea."<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWR-4)</sup> It would take many years before Thras was restored to its former glory, and its population never attempted any similar attack on Tamriel.

Other consequences of this temporary alliance were evident decades later. The [western parts of Cyrodiil](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) began to overshadow the more advanced and religiously ardent [east](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Basin), which eventually led to the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) that ended the thousand-year rule of the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-C-3)</sup> which in turn shifted the focus of power in the Iliac Bay from [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) to [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE1-HR-20)</sup>

Another dire consequence of the Thrassian Plague was the destruction of the equilibrium of the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) people due to heavy loss of life. The disease forced the survivors into roles they did not choose, polarizing their culture and forever changing their region from sixteen peaceful and co-existing states that resisted all foreign invasions into two very culturally opposed and politically divided states, [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) and [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3-E-8)</sup>

## Spread and Symptoms
In [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200) the Thrassian Plague began to spread through Tamriel, released into the continent through the use of infected sea beasts sent onto the coastlines,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tendil-9)</sup> the disease spread at practically supernatural speed leaving hundreds of thousands of misshapen corpses in its wake and afflicting the populace with symptoms such as boils, brittle bones, seeping mucous from the eyes and ears, and an unquenchable thirst that drove folk to madness.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Sys-21)</sup> According to the account of an Altmer Kinlord who experienced the events, at its height the Plague was ever penetrating, seeping into every person, every creature and every drop of water, driving the afflicted to drink uncontrollably until their very bones cracked under the weight and bearing an intent of its own to spread misery.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESONemfarion-13)</sup> Soon over half of the population of the continent was dead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-C-3)</sup>

The Plague proved resistant to attempts at a cure, a number of unsung heroes laid down their lives in search of one in vain, managing only to confirm the suspicion that the disease was unnatural and had originated from the Sload of Thras.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Sys-21)</sup>

The influence of the disease was so pervasive that certain locations such as the Corgrad Wastes were reputed to bear a taint of corruption even well into the Second Era.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOFarowel-15)</sup>
