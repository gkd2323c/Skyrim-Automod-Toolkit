—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Dusk is one of the eight major cities in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and is located on the southeast corner of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). The city acquired its name because the cliffs behind it leave it in shadows as the [sun](Lore_Sun.md) sets in the west.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOLive SF-1)</sup> Before it was settled, Dusk was the site of a lonely tower called **Dusk Keep** (also called the **Tower of Dusk**),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONPlume-2)</sup> whose origins are a complete mystery.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOLive SF-1)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-crown_store-Pale-Plume_Fledgling_Gryphon.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Pale-Plume_Fledgling_Gryphon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Pale-Plume_Fledgling_Gryphon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Pale-Plume_Fledgling_Gryphon.jpg) The Pale-Plume Gryphon [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/ON-place-Colossal_Aldmeri_Grotto.jpg/200px-ON-place-Colossal_Aldmeri_Grotto.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Colossal_Aldmeri_Grotto.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Colossal_Aldmeri_Grotto.jpg) Colossal Aldmeri Grotto What the layout of Dusk city is like is currently unknown, but it does contain a port.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CM1-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRMap-4)</sup> As stated earlier, it is located on the southeast corner of Summerset Isle and next to steep, nameless bluffs. This area is the home of the [Pale-Plume Gryphon](Lore_Pale-Plume_Gryphon.md), an otherwise uncommon variant of gryphon that can be identified by their white feather heads and their black feather bodies.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONPlume-2)</sup>

A large, hidden grotto to the west of Dusk contains ancient Altmer ruins and ruined shipwrecks.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLoad Colossal-5)</sup> Dusk is neighbored by several settlements, including [Archen Grangrove](Lore_Archen_Grangrove.md) to the west, [Silver Wood](Lore_Silver_Wood.md) to the northeast, and [Thorheim Guard](Lore_Thorheim_Guard.md) to the northwest. However, no road connects the two places.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ARMap-6)</sup>

Dusk Keep was hidden from the mainland and mostly inaccessible by foot. Southeast of [Sil-Var-Woad](Lore_Sil-Var-Woad.md) is an island dense with a forest, and a beach on its east flank that one can easily trek through to access the bay. Between it and the mainland is a short river valley that leads directly to Dusk Keep's shore. While the base of the tower is lower on the cliff, the actual entrance was accessible high above the water and across a bridge. The path to the entrance was a scaleable trail that zig-zags up the cliffside and ends directly across the bridge.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONDusk-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/ON-place-Dusk_Keep.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dusk_Keep.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dusk_Keep.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dusk_Keep.jpg) Dusk Keep ca. 2E 582 The Dusk Keep was designed by Lady [Ahnora](Lore_Ahnora_of_Rellenthil.md) of [Rellenthil](Lore_Rellenthil.md), who sought to emphasize its role as an overlook. She envisioned it as a forward-facing structure that faces the future boldly, while also protecting the past. It was a duty she believed every Altmer should strive to fulfill.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONDusk-7)</sup>

The bay and its surrounding areas have been visited before by other people. The [Atmoran](Lore_Atmoran.md) explorer, [Gjadil the Keel-Hauler](Lore_Gjadil_the_Keel-Hauler.md) buried her treasure after she raided King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md)'s palace in distant [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md). It was re-discovered years later by her descendants in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONIgeke-9)</sup> Colossal Aldmeri Grotto, a large cavern inside the cliffs and west of Dusk has been used by the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) navy as a watering stop and now contains ancient ruins.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLoad Colossal-5)</sup>

Even though the settlement was built years later, references to Dusk appeared in the mid-Second Era. Doctor [Alfidia Lupus](Lore_Alfidia_Lupus.md) writes in her series of [racial motifs](Lore_Crafting_Motifs.md) that Professor [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) was the only member of the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) at the time to visit the Summerset Isles, namely [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) with a brief stop in Dusk.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CM1-3)</sup> Pluribel of Dusk was a scholar that wrote the magisterial, the *Collapse of the Ayleids*, which was referenced in a report commissioned by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) Committee of Alliance Relations.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASIV-10)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the Dusk Keep served as the headquarters for [Ceythalmor](Lore_Ceythalmor.md) during their plot to assassinate Proxy Queen [Alwinarwe](Lore_Alwinarwe.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Wisteria-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TSOA-12)</sup> The scheme was investigated by agents of the Garland Ring, who ultimately foiled the attempt.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OCAF-14)</sup>

Dusk Keep stood alone in the bav for many years until the late Second Era, when the Second [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) built a port there. By the Third Era, Dusk had grown into a town and a fully-fledged city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOLive SF-1)</sup> Dusk became a primary stop in the [East Empire Trading Company](Lore_East_Empire_Trading_Company.md)'s shipping lines and connected to other cities like [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md), [Lillandril](Lore_Lillandril.md), [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md), and [Woodhearth](Lore_Woodhearth.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRMap-4)</sup>

In [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), the kings of the Summerset Isles formed a unified alliance to combat the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-15)</sup> in their last-known invasion of the province, the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-16)</sup> Despite having assistance from Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) and the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md), the Maormer were defeated because of a supernatural storm conjured accredited to the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWQ5-17)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Dusk was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Saura and had a rivalry with [Shimmerene](Lore_Shimmerene.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AR-8)</sup>

### Known Rulers
The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) have traditionally served as counselors to the rulers of the Summerset Isles since long before Dusk was established. Upon their return sometime in the Third Era, they continued this service for the many kingdoms of Summerset including Dusk<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FOA-18)</sup> until their island [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md)'s second disappearance sometime in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SRTolfdir-19)</sup>

## Notes
- In early cut drafts for ESO's south Summerset zone of Varyllis, Dusk was home to expert [smiths](Lore_Smithing.md) (or "Master Metalwrights") known as the Scintillants, and the beaches of the city were subject to a [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) invasion. The Maormer had also banished [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) from his place in the sky, so High Kinlady Astraela of Dusk helped in forging [Phynaster's Ring](Lore_Ring_of_Phynaster.md) from pieces of the Shattered Crown, which was spread throughout crypts in the city, to return the deity to his rightful place.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Cut Content-21)</sup> - The Tower of Dusk is based off the Umbral Tower, found on a lone island immediately south of the Scintilla Kinhouse in the bay to the east. Talrien the Archivist (who ended up becoming [Telenger the Artificer](Lore_Telenger_the_Artificer.md)) would be studying in the deep underground chambers of the Tower and help free the trapped Ascendant spirits from their reliquaries in the Umbral Tower by touching the Pyxides of Ashes.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Cut Content-21)</sup>
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Dusk's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Executioners".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-22)</sup>
