—Dinahan-Al-Rihad<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DAR-1)</sup> **Prince** Dinahan-Al-Rihad (also written as **Dinahan-al-Rihad**) was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) noble of the House of [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md), the rightful heir to its throne and an [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) loyalist, living in exile in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DAR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Noura-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/BL-quest-Tournament-_Dinahan-Al-Rihad.jpg/200px-BL-quest-Tournament-_Dinahan-Al-Rihad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-quest-Tournament-_Dinahan-Al-Rihad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-quest-Tournament-_Dinahan-Al-Rihad.jpg) Dinahan after his defeat in Heroes Hall Little is known of Dinahan's life before his departure from his homeland. Around 4E 180, during his exile in Cyrodiil, he was active in the province's gladiatorial arena circuits. He maintained that participating in these battles was a way to prepare himself to reclaim the throne he believed was rightfully his, explaining that although he had been granted asylum, he grew restless and needed to remain at his peak for the day he would return to claim his crown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DAR-1)</sup>

He was often described as a clever showman of great skill whose theatrics concealed real skill. Dinahan played up his status as a prince-in-exile, boasting about political ties and plans to reclaim his throne, all to make opponents mistake him for a pampered dilettante rather than a dangerous combatant. Some warned that this was precisely his tactic: to be underestimated. Some doubted his royal pedigree, suspecting the persona was merely a crowd-drawing act, yet his wealth and the occasional presence of influential spectators at his matches kept the rumors alive. Whether he was genuinely a displaced prince or merely adept at cultivating such an image, there was broad agreement on one point: he remained a formidable combatant and an admirable sportsman.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Noura-2)</sup>

[The Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) challenged him during the tournament in Heroes Hall and ultimately emerged victorious.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Quest-3)</sup> Their prowess impressed Dinahan enough for him to remark that he would make them his war minister should he one day reclaim his throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DAR-1)</sup>

## Notes
- His name does not follow customary Redguard naming conventions, which would typically render it as *Dinahan al-Rihad*.
- Although the exact reasons for his departure from Rihad were unclear, it is known that the cities across southern Hammerfell had fallen to the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in the aftermath of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), with only [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) to the west left standing. The entire southern region was left in shambles by the end of the war, even when the Redguards re-claimed the entire region, it was still in a state of ruin. When the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) was signed in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), Hammerfell had officially ceded from the Empire,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup> though some Imperial loyalists continued to be active.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DAR-1)</sup>
