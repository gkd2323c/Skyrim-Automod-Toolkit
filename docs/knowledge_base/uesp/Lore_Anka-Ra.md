# Anka-Ra

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/ON-npc-Anka-Ra_Sentinel.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Anka-Ra_Sentinel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Anka-Ra_Sentinel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Anka-Ra_Sentinel.jpg) An undead Anka-Ra warrior The Anka-Ra (roughly translated as "former warrior" or "old warrior")<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EJ-1)</sup> were one of the most fearsome warrior bands of the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), or "warrior wave", ancestors of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) who invaded western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) from their homeland of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Wot RG-3)</sup> They conquered the region of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) and their leader [Tarish-Zi](Lore_Tarish-Zi.md) proclaimed himself emperor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup>

Due to the oaths they made in life,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOPoly-5)</sup> the Anka-Ra can rise from their tombs as a unique form of stone [undead](Lore_Undead.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOPoly-5)</sup> The secret knowledge needed to bind them for eternity was granted to Tarish-Zi by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSA-6)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/ON-place-Anka-Ra_Burial_Site.jpg/200px-ON-place-Anka-Ra_Burial_Site.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anka-Ra_Burial_Site.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anka-Ra_Burial_Site.jpg) An Anka-Ra burial site The Anka-Ra arrived in southern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) (then known as the [Deathlands](Lore_Deathlands.md)) as part of the sencond warrior wave, under the command of Tarish-Zi.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ca Cv3-7)</sup> They cut a bloody swath across the province, marching towards the region of Craglorn.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Wot RG-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

The Anka-Ra subdued and conquered Craglorn, which was previously inhabited by [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BUM-9)</sup> They attacked the Nedic kingdoms and beseiged [Skyreach](Lore_Skyreach.md), the seat of the Nedic royal family.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> In his search for Nedic treature, Tarish-Zi searched for the lost Nedic ruins of [Reinhold's Retreat](Lore_Reinhold%27s_Retreat.md), and subsequently ordered the complex to be buried when he did not find what he was looking for within its walls.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup>

Having conquered the region, the invaders constructed the great [Hel Ra Citadel](Lore_Hel_Ra_Citadel.md) to protect a nearby Yokudan city, the name of which was lost to time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ca Cv3-7)</sup> Hel Ra was used as a training grounds to study the way of the [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md), a martial philosophy on blade mastery, with the ultimate goal of realizing a [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md), a "spirit sword" formed from sheer force of will.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EJ-1)</sup> Of note are a band of prominent Ansei known as "the Four". According to legend, their prowess with the Shehai was unmatched and they single-handedly captured Nedic fortifications, breached walls, and cut down entire armies.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TTPOLB-11)</sup>

The invasion of the Ra Gada corresponds with the disappearance of the Nedes from the historical record as a distinct culture; it is believed that Nedic civilization was already fading by the time of the Anka-Ra's conquest.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Although Tarish-Zi ruled over his new kingdom justly for a time, he was a cruel man, and he soon began to turn his armies against his allies, styling himself as an emperor rather than a king.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

In time, Tarish-Zi and his Anka-Ra warriors died and were buried in great tombs across the landscape of Craglorn.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BUM-9)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/ON-quest-The_Warrior%27s_Call_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Warrior%27s_Call_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Warrior%27s_Call_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Warrior%27s_Call_02.jpg) Titus Valerius confronts Emperor Tarish-Zi In life, Tarish-Zi had a connection to the [constellation](Lore_Astronomy.md) of [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md) due to his martial skill, and this connection was exploited circa [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Arnure-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SOE-15)</sup> when the constellations fell from the sky, landing in Craglorn as mortal beings known as the [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md). The Celestial Warrior was corrupted by [Malazar](Lore_Malazar.md), the Celestial [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md), who compelled him to summon Tarish-Zi forward in time.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOWarrior-16)</sup> Under the Serpent's control, Tarish-Zi invoked the oath of eternal loyalty sworn to him by his Anka-Ra, and commanded them to rise from their tombs as undead stone warriors.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ARBS-18)</sup>

However, before he could be fully corrupted by the Serpent's influence, the Celestial Warrior summoned his champion, Titus Valerius, from across time. Titus served in the Anka-Ra army but was against Tarish-Zi and his brutal style of rulership. The Warrior's call pulled Titus across time, depositing him stark naked in the middle of a melee at the [Dragonstar Arena](Lore_Dragonstar_Arena.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTitus-4)</sup> He was subsequently deemed to be insane and was arrested, but was freed by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWC-19)</sup>

The Celestial [Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) managed to escape the Serpent's corruption by hiding in the town of [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md), where she posed as a Redguard named Hara. She joined the [Star-Gazers](Lore_Star-Gazers.md) and led the group in their efforts to combat the celestial threat posed by the Serpent and his armies. As part of this effort, Star-Gazer priests journeyed to the ancient Anka-Ra burial sites scattered throughout western Craglorn to consecrate the tombs and temporarily prevent any more undead Anka-Ra from rising.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ARBS-18)</sup>

With the aid of Titus Valerius, the Star-Gazers were able to learn of the threat posed by the Anka-Ra and identify the burial place of Tarish-Zi. Titus and the Vestige entered the Tombs of the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md) and found Tarish-Zi within his tomb. Together, they confronted the "false emperor" and killed him, putting an end to the Serpent's plans for an Anka-Ra army under his command.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWC-19)</sup>

Even with Tarish-Zi defeated, the Celestial Warrior remained under the Serpent's influence. He retreated to Hel Ra Citadel, which had been sealed for many centuries.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EJ-1)</sup> Hearing rumors of scholars disappearing within the unsealed fortress, the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) arrived in Craglorn to combat whatever threat had awakened inside.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> A group of Undaunted assaulted the citadel, fighting through hordes of undead Anka-Ra to reach the Warrior's chamber and defeat him. This defeat allowed the Celestial Thief to appear and cleanse the Warrior of the Serpent's corruption.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> With the aid of the Vestige, the Warrior subsequently returned Titus Valerius to his own time period once the Serpent's plans were foiled.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

Although the threat of an Anka-Ra army was ended, they continued to plague the region.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ARBS-18)</sup> [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) seemingly pulled many Anka-Ra into her realm to fight as combatants in the Celestial Ring, one of several rounds within the Dragonstar Arena.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Some Anka-Ra [Sandcamels](Lore_Camel.md) and [Senche-Lions](Lore_Senche-Lion.md) were tamed and used as mounts; these creatures appeared to be made from the sand of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md), which would mystically amalgamate into the shimmering form of a mount when summoned.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>
