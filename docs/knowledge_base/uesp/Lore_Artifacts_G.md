## Gambolpuddy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/MW-item-Gambolpuddy.jpg/200px-MW-item-Gambolpuddy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Gambolpuddy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Gambolpuddy.jpg) Gambolpuddy [Gambolpuddy](Lore_Gambolpuddy.md), also known as the **Ambiguous Glove**, is an extravagant right glove belonging to [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), the Daedric Prince of madness and one of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) seen as the [Bad Daedra](Lore_Bad_Daedra.md) by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). It fortifies abilities (Agility, Intelligence, Personality, Luck) at the cost of draining others (Endurance, Speed, Strength, Willpower). It also has the ability to trick wearers of the glove into believing they have different identities, usually that of the previous wielder. Its main role is ceremonial, and is to be used as a sacrificial offering to Sheogorath's shrine in [Ald Daedroth](Lore_Ald_Daedroth.md), in order renew a pact with Sheogorath to limit his influence on the Dunmeri people. Gambolpuddy is not limited to the confines of Ald Daedroth, and is known to be a recurring artifact.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Gambolpuddy)

## Gauldur Amulet
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/SR-item-The_Gauldur_Amulet.jpg/200px-SR-item-The_Gauldur_Amulet.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-The_Gauldur_Amulet.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-The_Gauldur_Amulet.jpg) Gauldur Amulet The [Gauldur Amulet](Lore_Gauldur_Amulet.md) was originally owned by the powerful wizard Archmage [Gauldur](Lore_Gauldur.md) during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). His three sons Jyrik, Sigdis and Mikrul grew resentful of their father's power and murdered him in his sleep sometime during the reign of High King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) ([1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143) - [1E 221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221)). They divided his amulet between them and laid waste to the surrounding villages until the High King sent Archmage [Geirmund](Lore_Geirmund.md) and a company of battlemages to stop them.

After the three brothers were defeated, they were sealed away with the pieces of the amulet. The High King ordered that the whole affair be stricken from history, fearful of the terrible power of Gauldur's amulet, but the "Forbidden Legend" managed to live on through the ages.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_The_Gauldur_Amulet.md)

## Glamoril
[Glamoril](Lore_Glamoril.md) (also spelled **Glamorill**, and meaning "secret of life" in [Elvish](Lore_Elvish.md)) was a powerful artifact created by Archmagus [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md). The great and mysterious treasure was said to have been stolen from [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) himself by Archmagus Shalidor. The massive maze [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md) was built in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) around the ancient city of Bromjunaar to guard Glamoril, according to some stories. While stories have persisted since the First Era and none have ever been able to confirm the existence of Glamoril or its purpose or function, Shalidor considered it one of his finest achievements and called it a key to the inner workings of life itself. When he began work on the artifact, he reached a critical juncture where even his knowledge and power failed him, and needing more he turned to [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md).

The [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) hypothesized that this secret of life he possessed, whatever it was, may have been the catalyst for the great number of writings he produced on a vast array of topics. Other theories by the College include that it perhaps allowed him to live multiple lifetimes in a short span of time, or increased his intelligence and knowledge in ways unfathomable to others. The College was always searching for more of Shalidor's writings in an attempt to understand both their knowledge of the man, and of magic in general. During his time at the College, [Urag gro-Shub](Lore_Urag_gro-Shub.md) conducted lectures on the secrets of Glamoril.

## Glass Bow of the Stag Prince
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-weapon-Glass_Bow.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-weapon-Glass_Bow.png) Glass Bow of the Stag Prince The [Glass Bow of the Stag Prince](Lore_Glass_Bow_of_the_Stag_Prince.md) is a bow made of [Malachite](Lore_Malachite.md). Its exact origins are unknown. The [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) is imbued with an [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) that bestows the Blessing of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) the Stag Prince upon the wielder. The boon it grants increases the physical attributes of the wielder, namely their vitality and stamina. The powers granted by the Blessing of Stag Prince grow in potency the more game is hunted with the bow.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Glass_Bow_of_the_Stag_Prince.md)

## Goldbrand
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/OB-item-Goldbrand.jpg/200px-OB-item-Goldbrand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Goldbrand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Goldbrand.jpg) Goldbrand The [Goldbrand](Lore_Goldbrand.md) is an ancient [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) of the [North](Lore_Skyrim.md) which takes the form of a golden katana. According to legends, they first bestowed the blade upon the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) warrior [Sivdur](Lore_Sivdur.md), (ancestor to the modern [Battle-Born](Lore_Battle-Born.md) clan) who was sworn to protect them. It embodies the power of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), and burns those that it strikes.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Goldbrand), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Goldbrand), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Goldbrand_(item).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Goldbrand.md)</sup>, [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Goldbrand)

## Golden Scab
The [Golden Scab](Lore_Golden_Scab.md) is a curio associated with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md).

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), it was last known to be in the possession of Gazmod the Collector, a [watcher](Lore_Watcher.md) residing in [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), alongside the [Toxicruciform](Lore_Toxicruciform.md) and [Bilious Censer](Lore_Bilious_Censer.md). Eorda, a worshipper of Peryite, believed that she could gain Peryite's favor by somehow recovering these artifacts for her lord.

**Found in:** - [Lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Eorda)

## Gray Cowl of Nocturnal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/SR-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg/200px-SR-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg) Gray Cowl of Nocturnal The [Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md) (or the **Shadow Queen's Cowl**) is a daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that once belonged to [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). It takes the form of a dark leather cowl, which obscures the face of the wearer. Nocturnal is revered as a god by thieves across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Her reputation as the Mistress of Shadows has sometimes led thieves to attempt to steal an item from her to prove their greatness. As Nocturnal is usually depicted wearing a cloak and a cowl, it is around these two items that legends have arisen. The story of the theft of Nocturnal's cloak is probably fiction, as is at least one story of the theft of the cowl, but the cowl is known to have left her possession. It appears to have been stolen by [Emer Dareloth](Lore_Emer_Dareloth.md), a guildmaster of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), although a curse bestowed upon the cowl by Nocturnal meant that the name of cowl's owner is lost to mortal memory. *"Whosoever wears it shall be lost in the shadows. His true nature shall be unknown to all who meet him. His identity shall be struck from all records and histories. Memory will hide in the shadows, refusing to record the name of the owner to any who meet him. He shall be known by the cowl and only by the cowl."* This curse was broken through the use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md), causing history to be rewritten as a result, as such was the strength of the curse lifted that lifting it [altered time](Lore_Time_Magic.md).

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_The_Gray_Cowl_Returns!.md)</sup>

## Groundsplitters
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-concept-Groundsplitters.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Groundsplitters.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Groundsplitters.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Groundsplitters.jpg) Groundsplitters The [Groundsplitters](Lore_Groundsplitters.md) are a pair of sabatons associated with the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon. Their enchantment reflects their creator's obsession with destruction: this artifact causes the wearer's strides to be comparable to that of a [Giant](Lore_Giant.md), despite a lack of significant weight when not worn. These sollerets are essentially a weapon capable of turning those it kicks into paste. On a larger scale, a forceful stomp is capable of creating fissures; should the bearer of this artifact be careless, they can end up a statistic in a catastrophe of their own making.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:A_Rose_of_Many_Thorns)
