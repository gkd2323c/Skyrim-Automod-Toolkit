# The Mage

*This one sees ... the arcane. Yes, Magrus dances on light blue paws—tiny flames and the scent of incense ....*
*Ah, yes ... this one sees the flaming palm, the starry robe.*
*It is the Mage that guides you. A fortuitous sign. As surely as Magrus sheds the sun's white light upon the hidden, if you seek out wonders ... you shall find them.*
—Baandari fortune-telling for those born under the Mage<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HK-1)</sup> The Mage (also known as the **Wizard**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGErasmo-2)</sup>, the **Sage**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-C-3)</sup> the **Mechanist** to the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGErasmo-2)</sup> and the **Witch in the Stars** or simply the **Witch** to the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md))<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arana ESO-4)</sup> is a [constellation](Lore_Constellations.md) of twenty-seven stars and the planet [Julianos](Lore_Julianos_(planet).md), which is in the night sky during the month of [Rain's Hand](Lore_Rain%27s_Hand.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-C-3)</sup> Along with [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md) and [the Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md), it is a Guardian constellation, and its charges are the [Apprentice](Lore_The_Apprentice.md), the [Atronach](Lore_Atronach.md), and the [Ritual](Lore_The_Ritual.md). Those born under the sign of the Mage are thought to have more magicka and a talent for spellcasting. They are also thought to be arrogant and absent-minded.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

[Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) who followed the [Cult of the Stars](Lore_Cult_of_the_Stars.md) modeled their weapons and armor after the constellations. Each part of their armaments was crafted in honor of a different constellation. [Staves](Lore_Staff.md) were linked to the Mage, of Rain's Hand "who wieldeth ye staff as the mightiest of armaments" and "whose hand raineth magicka by rod and by staff". Their staves emulated the Mage's qualities and were known for star-disk at their finials for "faster stellar spellcaster".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CM41-6)</sup> The fabrics used to craft the ancient Nedic ornate clothing often referenced the Mage.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOCR-7)</sup>

The Reachmen believe that the Witch shines her light on covens that protect the Reach from outsiders and others who mean to do Reachfolk harm and that her good eye sees through everything—rock, water, and flesh—to find the hard truths. According to them she never lies. Together with the Hunter and the Headsman they are called Reach Guardians and that they protect lesser stars from the Snake.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arana ESO-4)</sup>

The constellations are present in ancient [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) and [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) poetry.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWC-8)</sup> In one of their songs The Mage "orders through the night."<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Coyle-9)</sup>

The Dwemer associate the Mechanist with the letter f.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

According to the [Baandari](Lore_Baandari.md) fortune tellers, the Mage is linked to [Magrus](Lore_Magrus.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HK-1)</sup> The Mage was also believed to "light one's way on the paths of glory".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Uriel-11)</sup>

## The Celestial Mage
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mage Quest-12)</sup> the [constellations](Lore_Astronomy.md) fell from the skies and became known as the [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md). The mortal incarnation of the Mage was known to the [Firstmages](Lore_Firstmages.md) as the "**Learned One**".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-COTLO1-13)</sup> [The Mage's Staff](Lore_The_Mage%27s_Staff.md) fell from the skies as well, forming the [Spellscar](Lore_Spellscar.md) in [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CM-14)</sup>
