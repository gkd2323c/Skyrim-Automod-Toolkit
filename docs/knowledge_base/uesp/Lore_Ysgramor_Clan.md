# Ysgramor Dynasty

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg/200px-SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) Ysgramor's palace in Windhelm The Ysgramor Dynasty (also called the **Ysgramor Clan**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3S-1)</sup> is the name given to the series of [kings](Lore_Empires.md) that ruled [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) after the time of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) in the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). It pre-dates and overlaps with the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) by centuries. Initially, Ysgramor established his kingdom after his victory against the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elves.md). In the aftermath of [The Return](Lore_The_Return.md), Ysgramor laid the foundations of the first human Empire by building the city of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and the [Palace of the Kings](Lore_Palace_of_the_Kings.md) which dominated the center of the Old City. [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), and [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), collectively known as the "Old Holds", were also established.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3S-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup>

After Ysgramor died, his descendants went on to rule his kingdom for centuries after. However, it was also around this time the authority of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) took hold and [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) ruled the land with an iron fist. The relationship between the cult and Ysgramor's Dynasty in respect to ruling is unknown, but some sources speculate that Ysgramor could have been a Dragon Priest himself (or at least associated with them) given his stature at the time.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Also, weaponry and heirlooms bearing dragon-headed mofits were known to be a mark of the royal line.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

The Dragon Cult was eventually overthrown after the Dragon War and the line of kings from Ysgramor's Dynasty continued in the following centuries. While many of the kings that came after Ysgramor remain unknown, one prominent ruler stands out. King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) was the thirteenth in Ysgramor's line and is remembered for being the first king to relinquish all holdings in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). Establishing the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) as a separate people, he was responsible for the consolidation of Skyrim as a centralized kingdom.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FC-6)</sup>

The Jagged Crown dates back to around Harald's reign and served as the symbol of office for the High King. The period of its usage is remembered as a time when the High King reigned by right of arms and collective adulation.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-7)</sup> Around [1E 184](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_184), Harald established the first [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) - a gathering of representatives from each Hold to choose the next King from qualified members of the royal family. This continued as the system of succession for centuries.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONGirdle Anti-8)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup>

Ancient records indicate that Ysgramor's heirs battled a mysterious figure named Aumriel for decades until it was sealed away in [Frostmere Crypt](Lore_Frostmere_Crypt.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LL-9)</sup> The Nordic goal of conquering the vast new land of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) was fully realized under Harald's son, [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md). King Vrage initiated multiple military expansions in [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240) that pushed past the borders of Ysgramor's kingdom and into [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and heralded a greater empire known as the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md). While it is unknown exactly how many rulers were part of the dynasty, the Ysgramor's lineage ended [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369) with the death of [King Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1S-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3S-1)</sup>
