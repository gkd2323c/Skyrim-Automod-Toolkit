# Ayleid Diaspora


During the Ayleid Diaspora, the Wild Elves fled in all directions, but not all would be successful.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AU4TET-7)</sup> Those that fled into [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) were slaughtered by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), and those that attempted to enter [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) were rejected by the [Barsaebic Ayleids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barsaebic_Ayleids). Most Ayleids fled across the [Strid River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strid_River) into [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ASIV-5)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3V-8)</sup> The last king of the Ayleids, [Laloriaran Dynar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar) of [Nenalata](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nenalata), commanded the last vassal state.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-1)</sup> In the years [1E 372](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_372) - [374](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_374), King Dynar was given an ultimatum by the Alessian [Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ami-El). He and his people were eventually forced to abandon Nenalata and flee to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in order to escape the Ayleid Pogrom.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-1)</sup> It's speculated that the exodus strengthened the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni.md) in High Rock - in which case, the Ayleids had their revenge, as the war with the Direnni eventually crippled the Alessian Empire and brought about its fall.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DRD-9)</sup>

Ayleids fleeing Cyrodiil were granted refuge by the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and allowed to settle in Valenwood on the condition that they adopted aspects of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). Having little choice, the Ayleids agreed and refrained from harming the forest, which probably contributed to the dilution of their culture.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ASIV-5)</sup>
