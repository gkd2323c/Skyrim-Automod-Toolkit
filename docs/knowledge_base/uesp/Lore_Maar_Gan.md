[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg/200px-MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Maar_Gan.jpg) Maar Gan ca. 3E 427 Maar Gan (also called **Markgran Forest**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AR-1)</sup> was a [kwama](Lore_Kwama.md) -mining village in the ash wastes of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), found within the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). According to [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) mythology, the town was the site of a confrontation between [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), where the warrior-poet taunted the [daedric prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) so he would throw a rock intended for the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) at them instead. Since then, a shrine was built around the **Magic Rock of Maar Gan** and the town became a popular pilgrimage stop.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWPilgrim-2)</sup>

## History
*"Here Mehrunes Dagon held this rock high above the Dunmer. Vivec taunted Mehrines * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * Dagon so that Dagon threw the rock at Vivec instead of the people."*
—Description found on the Magic Rock of Maar Gan<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MS-3)</sup> When the incident took place is unknown. Even though the settlement did not exist in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VTAN-4)</sup> a location of the same name was a regular stop used by [silt strider](Lore_Silt_Strider.md) caravaneers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SSCL-5)</sup> As the story goes, Mehrunes Dagon threatened the Dunmer with a large rock but Vivec was able to convince him to redirect it to them. The site where it landed is where the Maar Gan Shrine is built. The [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md), [Anhaedra](Lore_Anhaedra.md) was bound to the rock and pilgrims traveled to the shrine to re-enact the event by hurling insults at him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWPilgrim-2)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Markgran Forest was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Nisain and had a rivalry with [Verarchen Hall](Lore_Verarchen_Hall.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AR-1)</sup> Markgran Forest was neighbored by several settlements, one of which included [Stoneforest](Lore_Stoneforest.md) to the south.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ARMap-6)</sup>

After Vvardenfell was opened to the rest of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) began to covet the land and captured much of northwestern Vvardenfell.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ASHOM-7)</sup> Maar Gan's main industry was eggmining and the closest mine to the town was the Hairat-Vassamsi Egg Mine found northwest of it. At the time, the town was often harassed by monsters coming over from the [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md). In response, monster hunters traveled to the town and volunteered to protect it.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWGeneric-8)</sup> Maar Gan had a close call to destruction in [3E 424](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_424) when a swarm of [blighted](Lore_The_Blight.md) creatures nearly destroyed it. The [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) was called to defeat the monsters and were able to save the town. Councilor [Athyn Sarethi](Lore_Athyn_Sarethi.md) even awarded one member named Percius Mercius with [glass armor](Lore_Malachite.md) for their efforts.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWPercius-9)</sup> When the Blight infected the kwama in Hairat-Vassamsi, an adventurer named Ravila Neryon attempted to remove it but died in the process and a memorial was dedicated to her in Maar Gan's outpost.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RM-10)</sup>

Ever since the Redoran Council moved to [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md), Maar Gan was governed by Councilor Remas Morvayn until his death at the hands of blight monsters. Since at least [3E 426](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_426), his position was filled by his wife, [Brara Morvayn](Lore_Brara_Morvayn.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RB427-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWBrara-12)</sup> The ranking Redoran official in town was Kinsman Alds Baro, who also worked as the local [blacksmith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blacksmithing) in the outpost.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MWAlds-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWSpecific-14)</sup>

The exact fate of the town during the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) is unknown but all of Vvardenfell was caught in the destruction brought about by [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md)'s eruption. A Redoran councilor began a relief effort to support outlying settlements that were hit the hardest.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRY2-15)</sup> How Maar Gan's holy shrine fared when the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md) replaced the former one is also unknown as the New Temple denounces Tribunal teachings as misguided.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TR-16)</sup>
