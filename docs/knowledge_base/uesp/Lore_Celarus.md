Celarus was leader of the [Psijics](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) for at least 290 years as of [3E 400](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_400).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FOA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDOO-2)</sup> [Waughin Jarth](Lore_Waughin_Jarth.md) records him as the leader of the Order as far back as at least [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110). Celarus was Loremaster of the Order under [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) and later [Ritemaster Valsirenn](Lore_Valsirenn.md) by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONCelarus-3)</sup> He was the author of *[The Old Ways](Lore_The_Old_Ways.md)*.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TOW-4)</sup> He was considered a wise scholar and thoughtful if remote sage who sought balance in all things. He was responsible for seeking and containing items and forces that could disrupt the Aurbic forces of Tamriel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HIP-5)</sup>

One of the pieces of the [Tales of Tribute](Lore_Tales_of_Tribute.md), a popular card game originating from the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md) played continent-wide during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSOT-6)</sup> bore the image of Loremaster Celarus. A deck themed after him and the Psijic Order was part of the game.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TOT1-7)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HIP-5)</sup>

*[Response to Bero's Speech](Lore_Response_to_Bero%27s_Speech.md)* mentions a sage by the name of Celarus, though it's unclear if they're the same person.
