# Animal Hides

**Animal Hides** and pelts can be harvested from dead [animals](Skyrim_Animals.md). [Tanning](Skyrim_Tanning.md) converts the unprocessed hides into processed Leather and Leather Strips, which can then be used for creating items at [forges](Skyrim_Forge.md) and improving them at [grindstones](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) and [workbenches](Skyrim_Workbench.md). Hides are only one of several categories of items that can be obtained by hunting animals; the others are detailed elsewhere:

- [Animal Parts](Skyrim_Animal_Parts.md) for assorted less-useful animal parts
- [Raw Food](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Raw_Food) for meat that comes from animals
- [Ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md) for various alchemy ingredients that come from animals

Animal hides, including leather and leather strips, are traded by blacksmiths (including quartermasters) and by hunters encountered in the wilderness. [Giants](Skyrim_Giant.md) are also a good source of hides -- they will drop up to 3 random, level-dependent hides.

## Other
Several other items are categorized as "Animal Hides" by merchants, meaning that blacksmiths and hunters will buy them. Most of these are detailed elsewhere:

- All Fur armor: [Fur Armor](Skyrim_Fur_Armor.md), [Fur Boots](Skyrim_Fur_Boots.md), [Fur Bracers](Skyrim_Fur_Bracers.md), [Fur Helmet](Skyrim_Fur_Helmet.md), and [Fur Shoes](Skyrim_Fur_Shoes.md) - These items are also categorized as armor, so blacksmiths would buy them even if they weren't in the animal hides category.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's fur bracers, a new bracer variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with an 8 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

## Leather Smithing
Several varieties of low-quality light armor can be [crafted](Skyrim_Forge.md) either exclusively or primarily out of leather. These items can be forged from the start of the game, without the need to unlock any [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) perks. This also means that only normal-level tempering of these armor types is possible (there is no perk that provides the ability to "improve them twice as much"). [Fur Armor](Skyrim_Fur_Armor.md) is not listed here because it cannot be crafted by the player.

These derived armor pieces are no longer categorized as animal hides by merchants, but are instead categorized as armor (the only implication being that [Hunters](Skyrim_Hunter.md) will not buy them).

### Hide Armor
Hide is the lowest-quality light armor in the game, providing even less protection than [Fur Armor](Skyrim_Fur_Armor.md), and appears in leveled lists at the start of the game. All items can be [tempered](Skyrim_Workbench.md) using 1 [Leather](Skyrim_Leather.md). There is little reason to craft any of these armor pieces, because with the exact same materials you can instead craft better-quality Leather Armor, with the exception of the shield (for which there is no leather equivalent).

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's hide bracers, a new bracer variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 10 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

### Studded Armor
Studded armor is only available in a cuirass, which provides slightly more protection than the Hide armor equivalent. It appears in leveled lists at the start of the game, and can be [tempered](Skyrim_Workbench.md) using 1 [Iron Ingot](Skyrim_Iron_Ingot.md). Crafting Studded Armor uses the exact same amount of leather as either Hide Armor or Leather Armor, but also requires one Iron Ingot. Given that Leather Armor is better quality and requires fewer materials, there is little reason to craft Studded Armor.

### Leather Armor
Leather Armor is better than Hide, Fur, or Studded armors, but is still a relatively low-quality type of light armor. It appears in leveled lists starting at level 6 (enchanted varieties at level 7). All items can be tempered using 1 [Leather](Skyrim_Leather.md). Since Leather Armor uses only Leather and [Leather Strips](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips), this may be a good light armor set to craft for yourself early on.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's leather bracers, a new bracer variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 10 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

### Leather Scout Armor<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Leather.md)</sup>
Leather Scout Armor is added by the [Alternative Armors - Leather](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Leather.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md). A full enchanted set can be recovered through the quest [Double-Edged](Skyrim_Double-Edged.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Leather.md)</sup>, while unenchanted pieces can be crafted and also appear in random loot.

Each piece has a slightly higher armor rating and higher value than regular leather. This puts the set on par with [Elven armor](Skyrim_Elven_Armor.md) when it comes to armor rating, though it's still cheaper and heavier in comparison, while also lacking a shield.
