[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/ON-place-Annihilarch%27s_Summit.jpg/200px-ON-place-Annihilarch%27s_Summit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Annihilarch%27s_Summit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Annihilarch%27s_Summit.jpg) Annihilarch's Summit Annihilarch's Summit is a high peak in the Sever, a storm-ravaged region of the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). The peak is inhospitable to mortals and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) alike due to the constant lightning and wind shear.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lyranth-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Rynkyus-2)</sup> A single path leads from the base, passing through cave tunnels to reach the summit. However, the way is blocked by a wind-gate, and mortals are unlikely to survive the ascent without magical protection due to the violent weather that batters the peak.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Rynkyus-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## History
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md) established a permanent portal to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) atop Annihilarch's Summit.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DUL-5)</sup> The location was most likely suggested by Valkynaz Nokvroz, leader of the allied Ruinblood Clan.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Rynkyus-2)</sup> The portal led to [Fort Grief](Lore_Fort_Grief.md), a ruined fort in [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md). Here, [Sister Celdina](Lore_Celdina.md) built the Riven Cataclyst, a machine capable of transforming [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) into manifestations of destruction known as [Incarnates](Lore_Incarnate.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) began using the portal at Annihilarch's Summit to transport captured [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) from unaligned clans to Fort Grief for the purposes of creating an army of Incarnates. Many of those captured were members of the Bladebearer Clan.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DUL-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOBorn-7)</sup>

After her defeat at Burning Gyre Keep, Sister Celdina and her minions fled to Fort Grief. However, she was followed by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), whose Daedric and mortal allies assembled at the base of Annihilarch's Summit to attempt the climb. Rynkyus, leader of the Bladebearers, led the march, providing magical protection during the ascent.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Rynkyus-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> In addition to natural hazards, the summit was also guarded by Dagon's forces, requiring the group to battle their way to the top.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOBorn-7)</sup> Expecting to find a Dagonist temple at the peak,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lyranth-1)</sup> the group instead found and stepped through the portal to Fort Grief. The Vestige succeeded in destroying the Riven Cataclyst and rescuing the remaining Dremora prisoners, who were free to return to the Deadlands.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOBorn-7)</sup>

Annihilarch's Summit was visited that same year by the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md), who numbered only three following the death of Destron Brolus. Corrupted by his readings of the *[Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md)*, Sombren lured his fellow Ambitions here to serve Mehrunes Dagon. When he failed to convince them to relinquish their power to Dagon, he forcibly stole it from Destron's twin, Calia Brolus. Having realized Sombren's betrayal, the Vestige arrived at Annihilarch's Summit to stop him.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOLast-9)</sup> Instead, Sombren summoned an echo of Destron to fight for him.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The group mangaged to defeat this echo, and [the Anchorite](Lore_Mairead.md) used her power to push Sombren through a portal in an effort to protect Calia.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOLast-9)</sup>

## Note
- "Annihilarch" is Daedric title associated with the forces of Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> More specifically, the Annihilarchs of Dagon are elite warbands of Dremora associated with Valkynaz Nokvroz and the Ruinblood Clan.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>
