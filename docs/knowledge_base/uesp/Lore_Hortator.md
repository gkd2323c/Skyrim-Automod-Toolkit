# Hortator

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png/200px-MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png) [Daedric Face of Terror](Lore_Daedric_Faces.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFo T-1)</sup> representing Nerevar, specifically "his more terrible aspect as Hortator and Padomaic champion."<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-2)</sup> *Hortator is more a chief hero than a general. The councils plan and approve strategy. It's a terrible system. The Legions aren't like that. The generals ignore the politicians. And win the wars. But a Hortator leads by doing. The champion who wins every battle and inspires by example. The hero who challenges and defeats the opposing hero in single combat. Doesn't have to be polite or smart. Or even a nice person. Just has to win. And make others want to win.*
— [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) Councilman describing the Hortator's Duty<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGen Hort-3)</sup> A hortator<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is a war leader appointed within all the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) during a rare time of conflict or emergency, who holds "wide-sweeping authority" within the house political structure for as long as the conflict lasts. Each Great House appoints its own hortator, but under historical precedence, such as the threat of a greater enemy, it is possible for one to be named hortator of many or all of the Great Houses, or to be named War Leader by the [Grand Council](Lore_Grand_Council.md) of Morrowind Province.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGen Hort-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ra To HT-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRB5-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWMQ-7)</sup> These appointments are "usually singular" in nature (which could indicate a few instances of more than one hortator for a Great House being appointed at the same time historically).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ra To HT-5)</sup>

The title of "the Hortator" when capitalized as such is often used to refer to [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Indoril_Nerevar.md), the beloved leader of the ancient Chimer.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOV-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Vo F-9)</sup>

## History

### First Era
The title of "hortator" was introduced only after "High Velothi" culture of the oldest Chimer, seemingly in the time of the Great House culture becoming prevalent.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Indoril Nerevar, who was active throughout 1E 400s into the 1E 600s, is one of the most famous of the hortators in Morrowind's history; Nerevar served as the hortator of the Great Houses during the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) and the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TBORM-11)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Vo F-9)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOV-8)</sup>

Sometime in the First Era long after the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) of the 1E 400s the princely grandsons of [High King Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md) of Morrowind, being twins and thus joint heirs, divided the family's royal state in half, with the Dunmeri mining colonies of Ebonheart and Mournhold each arising as their own separate city-states ruled under each grandson. This was "about the same time" that the office of the High King of Morrowind (referred to instead as the "high chief of the clans" at that time) was vacated in favor of a "temporary War Leader"—to be appointed by a [council](Lore_Grand_Council.md) in times of "provincial emergency."<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRB5-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-KRP-12)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup>

### Second Era
By the [Age of Heroes](Lore_Age_of_Heroes.md) and before, [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) had historically appointed hortators within its ranks.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ra To HT-5)</sup> Ordinators of the Tribunal Temple had even served as the hortator of House Telvanni in the past.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ra To HT-5)</sup> House Dres also had an appointed hortator at this time.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HSDH-13)</sup> And [Shad Astula](Lore_Shad_Astula.md) (an academy founded by the Great Houses in the [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) region) had a "class hortator" selected from its students.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SAESO-14)</sup>

### Third Era
A hero called [the Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) served as the hortator in the war against [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), uniting the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), defeating the [Sixth House](Lore_Sixth_House.md), and saving the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) from [the Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWMQ-7)</sup>

## Regalia
Different Great Houses maintained distinct regalia associated with the office of the hortator. The Redoran Hortator bore an enchanted signet that fortified health to signify the office,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ROTH-15)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Redoran_Dialogue-16)</sup> the Telvanni Hortator was granted a special robe that fortified magicka and was considered an ancient artifact long unused by the time it was donned by the Nerevarine,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ROTH2-17)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Aryon-18)</sup> and the Hlaalu Hortator wore a belt that likewise fortified magicka.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BOTH-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Hlaalu-20)</sup> It is unknown what regalia, if any, were associated with the Indoril and Dres Hortators.

## Known Hortators
The following list is logically non-exhaustive, as there has seemingly been many historical hortators beyond what is known.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ra To HT-5)</sup>

- Indoril Nerevar<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOV-8)</sup>
- Hortators of House Telvanni<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ra To HT-5)</sup>
- Hortator of House Dres<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HSDH-13)</sup>
- Class Hotators of Shad Astula<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SAESO-14)</sup>
- The Nerevarine<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWMQ-7)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^1.](#note_nb1) | "Hortator" is a real world term from Latin, meaning "inciter, encourager, exhorter" and is derived from the word "hortor" ("to encourage, exhort, urge") + "-tor" (used in Latin to form a masculine agent noun).<sup>[[LANG 1]](#cite_note-wiktionary-21)</sup> Historically, hortators were the officers on board a Roman board ship, who gave out the chant for rowers to keep the stroke at a proper pace.<sup>[[LANG 2]](#cite_note-perseus-22)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^2.](#note_nb2) | This "temporary War Leader" appointed may or may not describe Indoril Nerevar's appointment to role of hortator, who was often described as the "Great Ashkhan" or "King" of the Chimeri people.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ZN-23)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Vo F-9)</sup> However, what happened when in this context above is unavoidably confused as [The Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md) was written for *Daggerfall*, long before the lore about the hortator featured in *Morrowind* was created—which was somewhat incongruent with the previous context; therefore, it also possible that the appointment of this "War Leader" and the dismantling of the office of High King came *after* Nerevar's lifetime (which would indicate a different conflict where a "temporary War Leader" was needed) |
| --- | --- |

- The chain of events as presented in the poetic [36 Lessons of Vivec](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md) indicates that the leaders of [House Mora](Lore_House_Mora.md) and its [Clan R'aathim](Lore_Ra%27athim.md) were all murdered by the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) in the time before the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOV-8)</sup> however we know from the lore that the R'aathim do actually live on beyond that time (including King Moraelyn, Queen Lian and her twin sons, the R'aathim King of Ebonheart installed by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), [Duchess Katariah R'aathim](Lore_Katariah.md), [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), and [King Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md)).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRB5-6)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-24)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-IWTB-25)</sup>
- The [Deshaan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Deshaan) and/or [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Stonefalls) zones in ESO originally featured two hortators named: "Hortator Invil" of the [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) and "Hortator Valyne" of the [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), but these characters and their storylines were cut from the final game. They both would have been slain by the cut "Orlishkaarn Tribe" of Ashlanders during a conflict around the Iliath Temple.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-MCut-26)</sup>
