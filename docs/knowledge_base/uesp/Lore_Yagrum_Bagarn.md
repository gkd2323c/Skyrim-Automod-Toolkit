*You wonder where the Dwemer have gone? Perhaps better to wonder why one remains. Even Gods dislike the absolute, for it stinks of something larger than themselves.*
— [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)<sup>[[nb1]](#intnote_Last_Words)</sup> Yagrum Bagarn is the last known living [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). Bloated and sick with [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md) disease, he was under the care of the ancient [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) wizard [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md), in [Tel Fyr](Lore_Tel_Fyr.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDF-1)</sup>

## Biography

### Early History
In Yagrum's prime, he was considered a wise scholar.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SSLW-2)</sup> Before the disappearance of the [Dwarves](Lore_Dwemer.md), Bagarn was a Master Crafter in the service of the chief Tonal Architect, Lord [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWYB-3)</sup> Yagrum survived the disappearance because he was in another dimension at the time, an undescribed "Outer Realm". Yagrum Bagarn would later return to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and has been looking for his people ever since with no success.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWYB-3)</sup>

Yagrum is mentioned by [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) in the book *[Where Were You ... Dragon Broke](Lore_Where_Were_You_..._Dragon_Broke.md)*, as one of the people that experienced the [Middle Dawn](Lore_Middle_Dawn.md), a phenomenon where linear time broke and became non-linear. As to the question where Yagrum himself was when this event happened, Mannimarco stated "The Last Dwarf would talk, if they would let him".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WWYDB-4)</sup>

At one point, Yagrum befriended Divayth Fyr.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWYB-3)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing artifacts such as [Chrysamere](Lore_Chrysamere.md), the [Bow of Shadows](Lore_Bow_of_Shadows.md), and the [Staff of Hasedoki](Lore_Staff_of_Hasedoki.md) among various items of "unimaginable significance".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TL-6)</sup> By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TA-7)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWFR-8)</sup> At this point of time, it went under the name *[Tamrielic Artifacts](Lore_Tamrielic_Artifacts.md)*.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TA-7)</sup>

### Third Era
As Yagrum learned of more artifacts, he catalogued them in his research and made revisions in his book. Three more artifacts, the [Bloodworm Helm](Lore_Bloodworm_Helm.md), the [Daedric Crescent](Lore_Daedric_Crescent.md), and the [Dragonbone Cuirass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonbone_Cuirass), were added to his catalogue of artifacts. The book itself went through various name changes, such as *[Famed Artifacts in Tamriel](Lore_Famed_Artifacts_of_Tamriel.md)*,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> and what finally stuck was the name *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TL-6)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TLR-10)</sup>

Yagrum Bargarn would at one point in time contract Corprus, causing him to fall into a state of insanity. Divayth Fyr took him in and cured much of his dementia, as well as trying out various harmless spells and potions to cure him, though none of them worked.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWYB-3)</sup> He also assisted Fyr in effectively curing the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) of their Corprus during [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) crisis in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Divayth noted that while Yagrum still showed signs of corprus, his memory seems to slowly be coming back from time to time.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SSLW-2)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), reports of Bagarn's survival were known by mages across the continent.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Marcurio-12)</sup>

Two-hundred years old and still suckling at her uncle Gothren's teat. Pathetic."*
