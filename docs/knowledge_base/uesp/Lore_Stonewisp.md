[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/ON-render-Stonewisp_of_Truth_and_Law.jpg/200px-ON-render-Stonewisp_of_Truth_and_Law.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Stonewisp_of_Truth_and_Law.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Stonewisp_of_Truth_and_Law.jpg) A Stonewisp of Truth and Law Stonewisps are [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) created by the clergy of [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTL-1)</sup> They should not be confused with [wisps](Lore_Wisp.md), which are entities linked to Wispmothers that are believed to be [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md), [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), or [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EGT-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TW-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ASpell-4)</sup> The mage followers of Julianos create these unique creatures as a form of devotional art. The wisp symbolizes orderly aesthetics, balance, and focused energy, while also providing a beautiful tone to aid in meditation.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTL-1)</sup>

Among the items used to craft them were axiomatic runestones—a complete set of runestones etched with sigils of truth and law, as outlined in the scriptures of Julianos,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AR-5)</sup> a glyph of law—a flawlessly inked, illuminated representation of the symbol associated with the god of wisdom and logic,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GL-6)</sup> and powdered remains of an actual wisp—the luminous dust left behind by the death of a common wisp, sanctified for use by the priests of Julianos.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PWR-7)</sup>

These constructs and the means to create them were among the many otherworldly items in the stock of [the Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md), an eccentric traveling merchant.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TI-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LATH-9)</sup>
