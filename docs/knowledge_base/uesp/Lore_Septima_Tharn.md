*Clear communication is the key to effective delivery of your point of view. A demand for surrender will be more effective if carried to the enemy by a headless corpse of one of their own tied to a horse."*
— [Septima Tharn's Leadership Maxims](Lore_Septima_Tharn%27s_Leadership_Maxims.md) Septima Tharn of [House Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) was Magus-General<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTo N-1)</sup> of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and a powerful [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was a relative of Empress [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), and like her other relatives, she venerated [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Septima-2)</sup> As commander of the Imperial Seventh Legion, Magus-General Septima proved to be a very ruthless tactician. She was willing to take and kill hostages, as well as enslave the populace of conquered cities.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OFA-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CO-4)</sup> Her critics claimed she had no military accomplishments beyond "bullying freeholders for their back taxes".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BSOHR-5)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Septima abducted King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and then slew him in the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) in order to destabilize the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). By slaying him in the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) crypt the [Hall of Heroes](Lore_Hall_of_Heroes.md), she was also able to physically follow his soul to the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md). A soldier of the Covenant was able to follow and slay Tharn in turn. As a reward for this heroic deed, [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) allowed the soldier and King Emeric to return to life in [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ban Quest-6)</sup>

## Notes
- The details of her relation to Clivia are inconsistent; most sources (including Abnur himself) claim that she is a cousin to the Tharns,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BSOHR-5)</sup> being his grand-niece/third cousin,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-7)</sup> while others state that she is a daughter of [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>
