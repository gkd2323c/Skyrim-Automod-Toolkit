The Unveiled Azadiyeh Answers Your Questions **by [The Unveiled Azadiyeh](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#The_Unveiled_Azadiyeh)** *The Unveiled Azadiyeh answers questions on lore*
---
The article was published alongside two books authored by the Unveiled Azadiyeh and a following preface: *The Unveiled Azadiyeh, Redguard historian and cultural scholar, shares new books.*

Today, we reach into the archives to explore Hammerfell's Redguards. The Unveiled Azadiyeh is a noted historian and scholar of the Crown Redguards, traditionalists who staunchly preserve their Yokudan roots. She answers your questions and presents two new lore books.

---
**I was reading the book [Glinting Talons](Lore_Glinting_Talons_(book).md), but the numerous references to light and the sun seemed out of place, given what we know about [Tava](Lore_Tava.md) being the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) version of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md). I was reminded of a discussion that mentioned a possible relationship between [Merida](Lore_Meridia.md) and [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md). Due to Meridia's position among the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) and their cultural identification with birds and feathers, I was wondering if the book Glinting Talons purposefully drew parallels between these deities, thus making Tava the 'missing link,' so to speak. – Phil W.**

The Unveiled [Azadiyeh](Lore_Azadiyeh.md) says: "Be not misled, O Phil, by the confused misunderstandings of the Tamrielics, whose minds are clouded by blood and darkness. Tava, as spirit of the air and goddess of weather, has dominion over all elements of sun and storm, save only for the stars, which belong to [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md) (may praises ever follow his name). Kyne and Kynareth are but her shadows, images seen dimly through the dust of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the snow of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Of course, it is praiseworthy for the [Nedelings](Lore_Nede.md) and [Nordlings](Lore_Nord.md) to worship the [gods](Lore_Gods.md) in any manner, even if their understanding is faulty and misguided. We can but hope that they come to the True Way before [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md) returns to take us all.

As for Meridia, speak not the name of a daemon in consanguinity with that of a god. She is an intruder from beyond [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), and no part of our world. Go, O Phil, contemplate your mistake, and step no more upon the Path of Error."

**I read somewhere that the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) ruler King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) is said to be 'the Serpent God of the Satakal.' What does this mean for Yokudan mythology and history? We know the Maormer are obsessed with serpents, and [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) are, too. Maybe there's a link between the [Left-Handed Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md) and the Maormer? – Marelo Ryan**

The Unveiled Azadiyeh says: "It may be as you say, Marelo at-Ryan, for your words waft the aroma of wisdom, but as to their Full-Truth, I am unable to aver it. We speak no more of the Left-Handed Elves (may curses follow them into the Eight Abysses), for to recall their abominations but darkens our days—and who can say how many each of us shall have before [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) beckons us, save that they will be too few?

"As for the Maormer, whatever their burden of association with the Sinistral Mer, they come not north of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), for our fearless sea-warriors have taught them to keep well clear of the shores of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Orgnum, their so-called 'Immortal Monarch,' will find he is not so deathless at the coming of the true Satakal (may it be long from today, inevitable though it is)."

**Are there any other somewhat isolated Redguard communities like the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md)? I also have another question, what is the official name of the nomads of [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md)? Are they just called the Alik'r?" – Wayward Swordsman**

The Unveiled Azadiyeh says: "The Ash'abah, though an unclean tribe of pariahs, nonetheless perform a function blessed by Tu'whacca, and they have sibling-tribes in southern Hammerfell as well. I have heard tell of a wayward tribe of Redguards far in the chilly north known only as the [Horsemen](Lore_Silverhoof_Horsemen.md), but this may be merely legend. Then, of course, there are the [Exiled Sun-Eaters](Lore_Exiled_Sun-Eaters.md) of Numaneh, but to tell their tale is forbidden."
