[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/SI-place-Vitharn.jpg/200px-SI-place-Vitharn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Vitharn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Vitharn.jpg) Vitharn during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) Vitharn is a ruin in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). The ruin was once a thriving city and fortress, founded by fanatics in southern [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md). Four generations of Counts ruled over the city, planning to usurp the Isles from the Madgod, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fo V-1)</sup> Ultimately, the incompetence of Vitharn's rulers and the [Manic](Lore_Mania.md) origin of the mother of the last count, Cirion, caused the Vassal Fanatics to invade the city and slaughter all therein, breaking their sworn allegiance to Vitharn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fo V-1)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Sheogorath's [Champion](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) put to rest the ghosts of Vitharn who had been cursed to relive the night of their defeat over and over again.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Go V-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/SI-place-Vitharn_02.jpg/200px-SI-place-Vitharn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Vitharn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Vitharn_02.jpg) Statue of Sheogorath [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/LG-cardart-Ghost_Fanatic.png/200px-LG-cardart-Ghost_Fanatic.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ghost_Fanatic.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ghost_Fanatic.png) A ghostly Fanatic haunting the ruins of Vitharn The city of Vitharn was founded by Count Vitraen in southern Dementia.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fo V-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lit SI-3)</sup> The Count gathered those who would pledge fealty to him and set up his county. His wife, Mawean, bore him two children: Csaran and Nweala. The Count and Countess believed that, with proper political influence, Csaran could usurp Sheogorath. When Vitraen died his son succeeded him. The Madgod, amused by Csaran's belief that he could usurp his throne, allowed his marriage to Sheen-in-Glade, the daughter of an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) midwife. Csaran's reign is remembered for his infidelity. It was his adultery that drove his wife mad, and brought him to the bed of his sister Nweala. The two had a son named Cesrien who contributed in his own way to the fall of Vitharn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fo V-1)</sup>

Count Cesrien was born with legs which seemed uneven in length, he breathed in a terrible rasp, was dull, and was often sick and under the care of numerous midwives and nurses. Cesrien was quick tempered and had violent tendencies. He seemed to take pleasure in killing, as the early years of his reign were marked by his extermination of all beasts, men, and mer in sight of Vitharn. During these years, the lands of southern Dementia were unsafe to travel in, with the roads being littered with corpses of trespassers and others staked to trees as markers of Vitharn's territory. All the while, however, Cesrien grew more and more ill. Eventually, Cesrien became introverted, keeping only a select few courtiers in his presence at any given time, although he did continue to appear in public to rally Vassal Fanatics for raids on the countryside. Toward the end of his short reign, Cesrien chose a vibrant Heretic woman from Mania named Jideen as his bride. Tensions began to arise between Vitharn and its Vassal Fanatics, and continued to do so after Cesrien's health failed and his son, Cirion, ascended the throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fo V-1)</sup>

When Cirion assumed the throne, some say that he still bore bruises from his father's hand. The young Count was known to be easily frightened, and even the shadow of a passing bird scared him noticeably. His mother, Jideen, tried for years to ease the tensions between Vitharn and the Vassal Fanatics, but their hatred of the Countess' heritage was too deep-seated to be undone by diplomacy. The Fanatics finally snapped, besieging Vitharn and taking the city in one day. Sheogorath, who was already displeased with the city for sins of pride, cursed its people to relive their final moments of cowardice for eternity.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fo V-1)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Sheogorath's Champion entered the ruins and spoke with the spirit of Count Cirion. The Champion assisted the ghostly defenders who failed to stop the Fanatics, taking on the role of the Count and finally defeating the attacking Fanatics, lifting the curse. With the curse broken, the ghosts were finally put to rest.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Go V-2)</sup>
