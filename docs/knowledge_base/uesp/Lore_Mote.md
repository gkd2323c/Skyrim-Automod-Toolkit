# Mote

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-creature-Malevolent_Mote.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Malevolent_Mote.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Malevolent_Mote.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Malevolent_Mote.jpg) A mote (ESO) Motes, also known as **Spellmotes**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SNS-1)</sup> are entities that take on the shape of orbs composed of [magical](Lore_Magic.md) energies.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MAM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MEM-3)</sup> They were thought to possess varying degrees of autonomy, with some being described as bolder than others. Motes can exhibit heightened aggression when specific magics disrupt them or the spirit who commands them.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ember-4)</sup> Motes are attracted to locations linked with magical energies, like [Tor Draioch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tor_Draioch).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MAM-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ember-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFOF-5)</sup> Certain motes possess the capability to change the appearance of individuals and animals using [illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) magic,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ember-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFOF-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LM-6)</sup> while others can actively control minds and change their actual shapes.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EP-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AHTB-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Animals-9)</sup> In certain stories, motes are described as remnants of ancient spirits.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SOTW-10)</sup>

Certain [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md), such as [Windsorrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Windsorrow) and [the Chronicle](Lore_The_Chronicle.md), held specific control over motes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ember-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFOF-5)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TC-11)</sup> The Chronicle controlled spirit motes that could transform individuals into [Forest Wraiths](Lore_Forest_Wraith.md) and command animals. It bestowed blessings through these ethereal particles, often releasing them from the muse-mouth plants to be inhaled by the [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) of the [Eldertide Circle](Lore_Eldertide_Circle.md), granting visions of stories. In times of danger, injury, or fear, it the motes could cause animals to become frenzied, or envelop the land in a dense, disorienting fog.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EP-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AHTB-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Animals-9)</sup>

Certain motes, known as Meridian Motes, are linked to the Daedric Prince Meridia. These motes possess a golden hue and emit a radiant light.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MEM-3)</sup> [Dagonic](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) [Morpholiths](Lore_Morpholith.md) were also commonly designated as motes.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-UM-12)</sup> The term "mote" is also employed to describe magical particles utilized in certain spells, such as the [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md) spell of magelight.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Magelight-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SM-14)</sup> The Mote of Cleansing Fire was another magical effect, cataloged within the *Codex Arcana* of [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BA-15)</sup> Motes can also serve the purpose of guiding individuals in specific directions.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RD-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Phantasmal_Mote-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SR-18)</sup> They are occasionally generated through the utilization of magical devices,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RD-16)</sup> such as [memory stones](Lore_Memory_Stone.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MEMMO-19)</sup> Specific motes were employed by the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) in [summoning](Lore_Conjuration.md) rituals,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DKK-20)</sup> and for accessing memories from the past.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-EA-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Frii-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TDOK-23)</sup>

The term "mote" was additionally employed to signify a quantity of mana. With each expenditure of a mote of mana, the eternity of [Ideal Masters](Lore_Ideal_Masters.md) diminishes.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

It is possible to attract and ensnare a mote using specifically crafted silver traps, designed for the purpose of capturing these magical particles.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ember-4)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-HIQI-25)</sup>
