# Dark Elf Script

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/RG-comic-Page_14-Dark_Elvish.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_14-Dark_Elvish.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_14-Dark_Elvish.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_14-Dark_Elvish.jpg) A Dres overseer yelling in Dark Elvish<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> This script<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> has been used by the [Dark Elves](Lore_Dark_Elves.md) in the closing days of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) in their [language](Lore_Dark_Elvish.md) —specifically, by retainers of [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) adjacent to their city of [Tear](Lore_Tear.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Origin-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> and in the architecture of the city-state of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONNecrom-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vorys-3)</sup> The Dark Elven culture before and after this time widely utilized the alternative [Daedric Alphabet](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) by and large for their writing, signage, and symbology.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ON-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Morrowind-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Tribunal-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-7)</sup> The script has also been used when rendering depictions of [St. Vorys](Lore_Vorys.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vorys-3)</sup> and [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Boethiah-8)</sup> Ultimately, the prominence of this script in relation to the Daedric Script is unknown.

The script is presented in a mirrored fashion, with sentences sometimes facing right instead of left, or left instead of right, depending.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Origin-1)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^1.](#note_nb1) | The name of this script is unknown. |
| --- | --- |
- | [^2.](#note_nb2) | The script is seen in the speech balloons of *[The Origin of Cyrus!](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Origin_of_Cyrus!)* comic book, and is implicitly meant to convey that the Dark Elves are using an indiscernible foreign language. |
| --- | --- |
- This script premiered in *[The Origin of Cyrus!](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Origin_of_Cyrus!)* comic book included with *[The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)*. The dark elves' typical script was switched to the [Daedric Script](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) previously used in [*Battlespire*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire).
