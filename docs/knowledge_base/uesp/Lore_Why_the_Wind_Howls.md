The oldest known legend surrounding the lost [xanmeer](Lore_Xanmeer.md) of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md). Transcribed by Seekxilts.

Sit down little one and listen to the wind as it rushes through the gorge. Hear its story and sense the tale it tells you as it whips past your scales. Stay awhile and listen. Listen to the sound of death.

However bad you imagine your past to be, know that the truth is worse. Place yourself in the [long-ago darkness](Lore_Duskfall.md) when the unmaker raged across the land. This is the time when your ancestors were scaleless and soft. See how the tallest trees were uprooted. See how the deepest riverbeds dried to nothing. See the great beasts growing thinner and leaner before they dropped to the dirt without a sound. This is the time of Naj-Caldeesh.

But why does the wind tell this story? Why does it howl? The wind and the mountain grew together. Like good friends they pushed each other, each one rising above the other until they both touched the sky. The wind danced, happy to have a partner who could hold them aloft and give them such glorious rocks to move between. The mountain loved to feel the wind flit around its peaks. It loved the sound of the wind's laughter. And so it was for a long time.

Neither the mountain nor the wind noticed when the ancients arrived. It wasn't until the mountain's voice grew weak and its glorious peaks began to crumble that the wind halted its dance. By that time, it was too late. The ancients carved into the mountain. They hollowed it out. The ancients carved their beds into the mountain's bones. They chipped away at the mountain's flesh and hollowed it out to make room for themselves. The ancestors dug so deeply into the mountain that they found its heart and they took it for themselves.

What was the wind to do? It watched as the mountain stopped growing. It tried to dance the same steps that brough *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* it such joy, but the joy was hollow. It tried to touch the sky again, but without the reach of its friend, the wind could not. And so it did the only thing the wind could do. It began with a keening cry, the soft whisper of grief flowing through the air. And then it grew. Louder and more powerful. Until the grief turned to anger. Until the sadness was gone.

Do you hear it? The distant scream? The howling of the wind against the mountainside? It is trying to find what was lost and buried. It is trying to find Naj-Caldeesh. And once it finds the entryway? Who knows? Maybe the screaming will stop. But it might continue.

It might grow louder.
