[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg/200px-OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg) Miscarcand circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Miscarcand is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) found amidst the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The largely-intact exterior of the ruin is one the biggest in Cyrodiil. It has two deeper subsections named *Sel Vanua* and *Morimath* which translate to "Deep Halls"<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> and "Darkened Home" respectively.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

Miscarcand was once the seat of power for a powerful Ayleid kingdom, but it has since fallen into disrepair following the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). It is said to be haunted by their last king, [Morimath](Lore_Morimath.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Beragon-3)</sup> a vengeful spirit who guarded one of the few remaining [Great Welkynd Stones](Lore_Great_Welkynd_Stone.md) that continued to shine in its halls for many years. Many would die in vain attempting to claim the Great Stone of Miscarcand.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## History
At its height, Miscarcand was one of the most powerful city-states in the western [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md). Miscarcand recognized [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) as Empress following the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), and it was powerful enough to defend itself from the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) following her death. However, the Ayleids of Miscarcand began to depart [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) in small bands, fleeing across the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md) to nearby [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). In time, King Morimath was the last Ayleid left at Miscarcand.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Beragon-3)</sup>

Miscarcand's location was rediscovered circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). [Nantharion](Lore_Nantharion.md) of [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md) had led a group of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) to the newly-grown Dawnwood, where he founded the city of Vashabar and proclaimed himself king. However, King Nantharion was secretly the leader of the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), a cult dedicated to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md). In truth, the Recollection were responsible for the growth of the Dawnwood, and Nantharion had founded Vashabar atop the forgotten ruins of Miscarcand. When he was exposed, Nantharion brought Ithelia to Vashabar, allowing her to open an entrance to Miscarcand and reclaim her divine power.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins of Miscarcand were occupied by the [Bitterfish Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bitterfish_Tribe) of [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> There was a great attack planned for the city of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), by the Daedric Hordes of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and in order to dispel the Great Gate of Oblivion, [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) had required various artifacts. One such artifact was the Great Welkynd Stone, which was essentially one of a kind at this time. Martin had sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to Miscarcand to acquire the stone, as the ruins were their only choice. Successfully, the Hero of Kvatch was able to obtain the Great Welkynd Stone, and avert destruction towards Bruma.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
