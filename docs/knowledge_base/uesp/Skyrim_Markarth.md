[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/SR-place-Markarth.jpg/200px-SR-place-Markarth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Markarth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Markarth.jpg) Markarth, viewed from outside the city gates [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/SR-place-Markarth_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Markarth_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Markarth_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Markarth_02.jpg) Inside Markarth **Markarth** is a major city in the southwest of Skyrim near the border of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), and the capital of [the Reach](Skyrim_The_Reach.md). It is housed in an ancient [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city, making its architecture noticeably different from that of most cities in Skyrim, with much of the city carved into the surrounding rock. [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) [Igmund](Skyrim_Igmund.md), the initial ruler of the Reach, is having issues with a group known as the [Forsworn](Skyrim_Forsworn.md), who infest the nearby countryside and attack travelers on sight. Though many races populate the city, the people of Markarth are not fond of outsiders, and the guards treat them with hostility and suspicion.

Half the city is owned by the Silver-Blood family. The [Silver-Blood Inn](Skyrim_Silver-Blood_Inn.md) near the entrance to the city is a good place for brawling, picking up bounty letters, hiring a follower, or renting a room. The Silver-Bloods also own [Cidhna Mine](Skyrim_Cidhna_Mine.md), which is inside the city walls and used as a prison, said to be the most secure prison in Skyrim. Most of the poor of Markarth live in extreme poverty in an area dug into the cliff called [The Warrens](Skyrim_The_Warrens.md). Many in the Warrens work in the mine and at the smelters in the area. Several shops are in an open-air marketplace near the city entrance. The guards are quartered in a Dwemer dormitory carved out of the stone beneath the imposing [Guard Tower](Skyrim_Guard_Tower.md) at the center of the city. Markarth also houses [Understone Keep](Skyrim_Understone_Keep.md), the ancient Dwemer castle and seat of government carved into the mountain at the west end of the city.

For more information on Markarth, see the [lore article](Lore_Markarth.md).

## Notes
- [Vlindrel Hall](Skyrim_Vlindrel_Hall.md) is a [house](Skyrim_Houses.md) available for purchase after completion of certain quests for the jarl. It may be purchased from the steward for 8,000 gold.
- Evidence of the former Dwemer presence in Markarth is found in the deserted ruins of a Dwemer city. The ruins still contain several operational (and hostile) Dwemer machines.
- Markarth is a unique city in that many citizens may be killed depending on the choices that you make during quests, making the phrase that only "Blood and Silver" runs through Markarth true. See the notes to the [table of NPCs above](Skyrim_Markarth.md#Markarth_People) for details.
- Investing in the various shops here also adds the investment bonuses to your spouse's gold for their shop.
- Markarth also appears in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* as [Markarth Side](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Markarth_Side), and reappears in [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Markarth).

## Bugs
- There is a dialogue bug with [Markarth guards](Skyrim_Markarth_City_Guard.md) that occurs after the two Forsworn quests. They will attempt to arrest you, and if you reply with any option other than resist arrest, the dialogue will repeat endlessly. <sup>**?**</sup> - The simplest way of resolving this is to go back to the Temple of Talos and speak with the guards inside. Choose the option to go to jail, then walk outside and speak to another guard to pay your bounty, persuade, etc. successfully.
- For various other methods on how to resolve this, see the [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:talk_Markarth).
- The steward [Raerek](Skyrim_Raerek.md) may refuse to speak with you, or will not offer to sell you [Vlindrel Hall](Skyrim_Vlindrel_Hall.md). See [this section](Skyrim_Vlindrel_Hall.md#Bugs) for details. <sup>**?**</sup>
