*Here in his shrine, that they have forgotten. Here do we toil, that we might remember. By night we reclaim, what by day was stolen. Far from ourselves, he grows ever near to us. Our eyes once were blinded, now through him do we see. Our hands once were idle, now through them does he speak. And when the world shall listen, and when the world shall see, and when the world remembers, that world will cease to be.*
—Miraak's Chant<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup> Miraak (meaning *Allegiance Guide* in the [dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md)), also known as the **First Servant**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> was originally a [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). He is the **First [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md)**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup> although that term would not become used until after the time of [St. Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), the first Dragonborn recorded in history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBOTD-3)</sup> He ruled over the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), where a large temple was dedicated to him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup>

## Biography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/MER-art-Sahrotaar_And_Miraak.webp/150px-MER-art-Sahrotaar_And_Miraak.webp)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Sahrotaar_And_Miraak.webp) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Sahrotaar_And_Miraak.webp) Sahrotaar and Miraak [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/LG-cardart-32.png/200px-LG-cardart-32.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-32.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-32.png) Cultist of Miraak [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/SR-place-Temple_of_Miraak.jpg/200px-SR-place-Temple_of_Miraak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Temple_of_Miraak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Temple_of_Miraak.jpg) The Temple of Miraak under construction on Solstheim Miraak served as a leader of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Firstblade-4)</sup> and possessed a [Dragon Priest Mask](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon_Priest_Masks), also called [Miraak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon_Priest_Masks#Miraak), given to him by the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md). At some point, he sought out [the teachings](Lore_Black_Books.md) of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the Demon of Knowledge, and became his champion. Hermaeus Mora taught him the power to bend the will of dragons.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mora-5)</sup> Using this knowledge and his innate ability to devour the souls of dragonkind, he turned on his winged overlords and killed a great number of them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup> Three Dragon Priests, [Ahzidal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ahzidal), Dukaan, and Zahkriisos, joined him and became his three most powerful servants, called the "Acolyte Priests".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

During the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), [Hakon One-Eye](Lore_Hakon_One-Eye.md) and others sought Miraak out, desperate for a strong ally in their fight against [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). Though apparently confident that he could defeat Alduin if he so chose, he refused to help, leaving mankind to their own fate.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Miraak-7)</sup>

[Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) legend remembers Miraak as "the Traitor", and recounts how he plotted against the dragons with Hermaeus Mora. Another Dragon Priest, Vahlok the Jailor (known as "the Guardian" to the native Skaal), uncovered this deception, and the two engaged in a duel so vicious and destructive that the modern island of Solstheim was allegedly ripped from the mainland of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TGATT-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tharstan-9)</sup> Vahlok defeated Miraak, but just as he prepared to deliver the killing blow, Hermaeus Mora snatched away and saved his champion.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TGATT-8)</sup> Following this, the dragons razed his temple to ashes,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Frea-10)</sup> and tasked Vahlok the duty of watching Solstheim for signs of Miraak's return.

Miraak was not seen or heard from again for many ages;<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TGATT-8)</sup> he evidently spent all this time in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), Hermaeus Mora's realm. Though he essentially became imprisoned there, Miraak spent his time gathering knowledge and power with the goal of freeing himself from Hermaeus Mora's service.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Miraak-7)</sup>

Some time during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Miraak tricked one of Hermaeus Mora's mortal servants, a Cipher of the Eye, to smuggle cursed scrolls into the scriptorium of Apocrypha, Quires Wind. The scrolls caused many of the Ciphers to turn into Hushed, and made the scriptorium's Daedric guardians go mad and feral.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CSA-11)</sup>

It wasn't until [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) that Miraak was able to use arcane [magical](Lore_Magic.md) powers to overtly influence the people of Solstheim, making them unknowingly work for him around the island, such as by rebuilding his temple, while they slept. They also worked on building arches around the [Stones of Power](Lore_Standing_Stones.md) on the island, which were apparently meant to assist in his return by harnessing the energy of the stones. Others came to worship Miraak more consciously, and formed a cult dedicated to him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup>

Miraak's plan became threatened by the advent of the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md), and cultists were sent to track down and kill this "False Dragonborn".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CO-12)</sup> The Last Dragonborn responded to this antagonism by journeying to Solstheim, where the hero sought out and used the hidden knowledge of Hermaeus Mora to thwart Miraak's efforts on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), journey into Apocrypha, and confront him in battle. When it was clear that Miraak would lose, Mora dealt him a fatal blow, killing the First Dragonborn and allowing the Last Dragonborn to claim his soul.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-1)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Mask
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/SR-item-Miraak.jpg/150px-SR-item-Miraak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak.jpg) Miraak's mask [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/ON-hat-Tusked_Dragon_Priest_Mask.jpg/150px-ON-hat-Tusked_Dragon_Priest_Mask.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-hat-Tusked_Dragon_Priest_Mask.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-hat-Tusked_Dragon_Priest_Mask.jpg) Tusked Dragon Priest Mask Prior to his betrayal of his Dragon overlords, they had granted Miraak a [mask](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon_Priest_Masks#Miraak) that had the ability to significantly fortify the wearer's magicka. The mask bears a striking resemblence to the so-called "Tusked Dragon Priest Mask", though with the addition of tentacle-like motifs.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MM-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TDPM-14)</sup>

### Firstblade
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg/150px-SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg) Firstblade [Firstblade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Firstblade), also known as **Miraak's Sword**, is a powerful sword linked to [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). It appears as a primal green blade with writhing sclerotic [watcher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) tentacles and [Seeker](Lore_Seeker.md) -sinew Threads wrapped around it, which extend to attack targets and drain their stamina during combat. When it is swung it takes the shape of a tentacle and grows in length, and even when not being swung, the sword writhes with tentacles around it, with them parting around the base of the sword to expose a green eye. It can be tempered with the use of [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and [daedra hearts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Heart), much like other [Daedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment) weapons.

It is unknown if Firstblade was seen after the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), but by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) of Solstheim knew it as a strange sword that belonged to a renegade Dragon Priest named Miraak far in the distant past. The sword was in possession of the renegade [dragon priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) Miraak, perhaps dating back to the time before the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), and was in his possession during his time in Apocrypha. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), he used the sword while battling the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) at the summit of Apocrypha, who killed him and took possession of the blade.

### Miraak's Staff
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/SR-item-Miraak%27s_Staff.jpg/150px-SR-item-Miraak%27s_Staff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak%27s_Staff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak%27s_Staff.jpg) Miraak's Staff [Miraak's Staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miraak%27s_Staff) is a powerful [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) [staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staves). It has the power to unleash Mora's Agony, summoning writhing apocryphal tentacles that stagger and poison any they come into contact with.
