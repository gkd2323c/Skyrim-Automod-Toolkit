Nhad-hatta, also known as **the Cat Lord**, was the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) around [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), during [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s conquest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). By the time Tiber Septim entered the fray to claim the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), the [Rim-Men](Lore_Rim-Men.md) returned to Elsweyr, where they paid tribute to the Mane for protection.

He and his tribe resided in [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), where they lived in luxurious palaces built from massive timbers of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) oak. He was often seen in day-long meditations atop his palanquin, held up by his inexhaustible [Cathay-raht](Lore_Cathay-raht.md) servants. He was known to refuse an Imperial diplomat a meeting at his private palace even though he had been promised an audience. The envoy was cast out from the area by one of the Warrior Guards because humans had been traditionally forbidden to trespass on these estates.

During his rule, his province potentially made a treaty with the [Second Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PG2-1)</sup>

## Notes
- In response to a fan claiming Nhad-hatta's [*Pocket Guide*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_The_Elsweyr_Confederacy) portrait suggested the stature of a [Cathay](Lore_Cathay.md), developer [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) described how he had imagined him as "a cat sitting on a tall, wheeled platform hidden by the countless hair-locks," implying an appearance characteristic of an [Alfiq](Lore_Alfiq.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-2)</sup>
- Nhad-hatta's appearance, and that of Manes in general, was noted to be "absolutely outlandish and unique."<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-2)</sup>
