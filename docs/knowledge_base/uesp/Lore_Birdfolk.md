# Birdfolk

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1b/OB-concept-Imperial_City_Map.jpg/200px-OB-concept-Imperial_City_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-concept-Imperial_City_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-concept-Imperial_City_Map.jpg) Map of the Eight Islands, site of the Imperial City Birdfolk (also known as the **Bird Men**, **Cyrodilic bird-people**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAmalien-1)</sup> "**feathered men**", or "**bird creatures**")<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Fot N-2)</sup> were a [race](Lore_Races.md) of [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) who supposedly inhabited what became the [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the Early-to-Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGECyro-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Fot N-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AAFCS-4)</sup>

## History
During the early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the aboriginal beastpeoples of Tamriel lived in preliterate communities.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BTAOM-5)</sup> The name they gave themselves, if any, is lost to history. Only oral histories and the fragmented ballad of [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md), an epic [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md) poem called *[Udhendra Nibenu](Lore_Udhendra_Nibenu.md)* (translated as *[Father of the Niben](Lore_Father_Of_The_Niben.md)* by [Florin Jaliil](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Florin_Jaliil), who thought it might not be entirely factual), offer glimpses of the ancient beast races that inhabited the land of Cyrod, but they are "shadowy, mist-drawn portraits of time before reckoning".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGECyro-3)</sup> The ballad describes the adventures of the legendary [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorer Topal the Pilot, who charted the coasts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and discovered the [Niben River](Lore_Niben_River.md) and [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). In the poem, while Topal and his crew strove to return home to [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), they traveled up the Niben River and the [Niben Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md) to [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md), where they encountered "brilliant flightful creatures of glorious colors" with "taloned feet". At first the "feathered men" were speaking Aldmeri, but the crew soon figured out they were only repeating what they had heard, not understanding the meaning to any of the words. Topal's crew taught the Bird Men their language as well as how to write with their feet, and in return the Bird Men made Topal their lord and granted him the "eight islands" that would later make up City Isle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Fot N-2)</sup> Other histories say he purchased the Eight Islands from the beastfolk natives in exchange for the secret of literacy, rather than being given as a gift.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBDecentius Opsius-6)</sup>

After Topal's departure, the "extraordinary bird creatures" are never mentioned again in recorded history. There are varying accounts of what happened to them: based on Topal's poem, Florin Jaliil believed that the bird creatures met their extinction at the hands of the "cat demons" (likely the ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md)) long before the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) migrated to Cyrodiil.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Fot N-2)</sup> However, it is also said that their newfound skills of reading and writing were in vain, as it soon made them better slaves to the Ayleids.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBDecentius Opsius-6)</sup> There are also records of "savage beast peoples" who learned the crafts of war and magic from their Ayleid masters,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ga L-7)</sup> but it is vague as to which betmeri this refers to.

Traders from [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) continued to spread stories of feathered folk, described as avian men and women with talons for feet.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AAFCS-4)</sup> By the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the prophecies of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) continued to make reference to the Birdfolk, although to a lesser extent than in the past.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) allegedly came into possession of a bird-person skull circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), though its veracity was disputed by claims that it was in fact a [terror bird](Lore_Terror_Bird.md) skull.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAmalien-1)</sup>

## Notes
- The Bird Men are not to be confused with [harpies](Lore_Harpy.md), an all-female species of birdlike women which were created sometime after [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808) in the city of [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ghraewaj-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGEOL-10)</sup>
