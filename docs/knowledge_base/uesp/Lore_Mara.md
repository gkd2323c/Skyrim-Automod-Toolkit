# Mara

*Come to me, Mara, for without you, I might forget the ways of our fathers, and preening by the light of latest fashion, my words might tremble like the thin reeds of novelty in the tempest of enthusiasms.*
— *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition.md)* [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-heraldry-Deities_Mara.png) Mara, also known as **Mother Mara**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-APAYQ-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATG-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> **Mother Mild**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> and the **Divine Mother**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-APAYQ-1)</sup> is the **Goddess of Love, Fertility, Agriculture,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOFN-6)</sup> and Compassion**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> She is also acclaimed as the **Mother-Goddess**,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOFE-8)</sup> and is one of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

In the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), Mara was originally worshipped simply as a fertility goddess,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VOFB-10)</sup> but the sphere of the Divine later extended to agriculture and love. Nir, the female principle of the cosmos credited as the mother of creation, is occasionally associated with Mara as a result of her role as the goddess of fertility and agriculture.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOFN-6)</sup> As a result, priests of Mara credit her with giving birth to creation.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Maramal-11)</sup> Mara is considered a near universal deity as the Divine is featured in the pantheons of all the races of the continent of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) save the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOFE-8)</sup>

## Mythology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/OB-Window-Mara.jpg/150px-OB-Window-Mara.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Window-Mara.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Window-Mara.jpg) Mara [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-quest-The_Oldest_Orc_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Oldest_Orc_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Oldest_Orc_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Oldest_Orc_02.jpg) Nuralanya and Gurlak reuniting in afterlife by the grace of Mara *The Goddess Mara recognized their true love and wept at their loss. Not having power over death, she could do nothing to save Shandar, but she knew that she could not let their love die. She reached down from the heavens and picked up Mara and Shandar in her arms, and placed them high in the heavens. They could be together always, and provide light in the dark night to others so that they may be safe from the evils in the world.*
— *[Mara's Tear](Lore_Mara%27s_Tear.md)*<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MT-12)</sup> According to her clergy, it was Mara who first gave birth to Creation. She then pledged to watch over mortals as her children. They also believe mortals learned to love each other from her love for all of them.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Maramal-11)</sup> According to the creation myth presented in the *[Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)*, Mara and the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) (gods) were born from the mingled blood of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md), the good and evil primal forces, respectively, and therefore have a capacity for both good and evil, in contrast to the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), who were born from the blood of Padomay, and thus are only evil.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

The creation myth of the Khajiit acclaims Mara as the Mother Cat and describes the Divine as the daughter of Ahnurr (Anu) and Fadomai (Padomay).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/SR-quest-The_Book_of_Love_07.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Book_of_Love_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Book_of_Love_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Book_of_Love_07.jpg) Fenrig and Ruki reuniting in afterlife thanks to the actions of Agent of Mara The formation of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), the Dragon God of Time, from the mingled blood of the brothers Anu and Padomay facilitated the formation of Mara and the gods as they learnt to structure themselves.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> *Shezarr's Song*, a Cyrodilic creation myth, acclaims the creation of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) to the sacrifice of Mara and the gods whose sacrifices were embodied as eponymous planets.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AAD-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Orrery-17)</sup>

Mara and the gods were subsequently bound to the Earth Bones as a result.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AAD-16)</sup> The moons of [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md) are claimed to be the embodiment of the lovers, a mortal woman named Mara and [Shandar](Lore_Shandar.md), respectively, as created by the Divine.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MT-12)</sup> When [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) created his daughter [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md), he drew inspiration from other divinities and bestowed upon her the smile of Mara.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TNC-18)</sup>

Different cultures attribute different relationships between Mara and the other gods. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Skyrim consider Mara a handmaiden of [Kyne](Lore_Kynareth.md) and concubine of [Shor](Lore_Lorkhan.md) (Lorkhan).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOFN-6)</sup> However, the Bretons, Altmer, and Bosmer consider Mara to be the wife of Akatosh or Auriel to the Elves.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VOFB-10)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-VOFA-20)</sup> Other faiths acclaim Mara as the wife of Lorkhan or concubine of Akatosh, or, in some cases, concubine of both.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOFE-8)</sup> In *Ark'ay The God*, Mymophonus the scribe claims that Mara deified Arkay, the God of Birth and Death, so to allow him to teach mortals the necessity of life and death.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATG-2)</sup> In the First Era, the Divine punished [Sai](Lore_Sai.md), God of Luck, and deprived him of his body for his failure to uphold his pact with [Ebonarm](Lore_Reymon_Ebonarm.md), God of War, to spread luck throughout Tamriel in the gods' absence.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> The [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), also known as the Miracle of Peace, in 3E 417 is acclaimed as the work of Mara, Akatosh, and Stendarr.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> It is unknown if Mara successfully destroyed the [Skull of Corruption](Lore_Skull_of_Corruption.md), a daedric artifact, in 4E 201.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-WN-23)</sup>

On occasions, she would act through intermediaries to ensure that the spirits of the deceased would be united together also in death.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TOL-24)</sup> There are also documented cases of Mara intervening more directly, enabling the departed to briefly return as ghosts to share final moments with their loved ones and guide them beyond the mortal realm. This intervention extended even to situations involving individuals from different races with distinct beliefs about the afterlife. Notably, Mara facilitated the reunion of Nuralanya, a [Corelanya](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), and an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) named Gurlak in the afterlife.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Nuralanya-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TOO-26)</sup> The clergy of Mara supported this belief, teaching that even couples from different backgrounds, such as an Altmer and a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md), would not enter death alone.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LAHDMM-27)</sup>

## Teachings
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3b/LG-cardart-Amulet_of_Mara.png/200px-LG-cardart-Amulet_of_Mara.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Amulet_of_Mara.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Amulet_of_Mara.png) Amulet of Mara [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-crown_store-Pledge_of_Mara.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Pledge_of_Mara.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Pledge_of_Mara.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Pledge_of_Mara.jpg) A shrine to Mara with [rings of Mara](Lore_Rings_of_Mara.md) As Goddess of Love, Mara is the bearer of love,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dinya-28)</sup> and shines the light of love on all mortals.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Briehl-29)</sup> Mara gave life to mortals so that they may love.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Heart-30)</sup> The gift of love was bestowed upon mortals by the Divine so to honor Mara,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Heart-30)</sup> and allow them to understand the true nature of the Divines.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dinya-28)</sup> Mara teaches that love provides nourishment and life to all mortals.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dinya-28)</sup> The Divine commands mortals to "live soberly and peacefully. Honor your parents, and preserve the peace and security of home and family".<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> Mara's Benevolence is eternal and those who offer their love to the Divine will never be forsaken.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TWM-32)</sup> The Warmth of Mara shapes the destiny of mortals and can be felt across Tamriel.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TWM-32)</sup> Priests of Mara teach that the Divine is omnipresent and her love and affection can be found in the heart of all mortals, thus linking them to Mara.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-THOL-33)</sup> The holy truth declares that love creates life and therefore those who love create more life.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Heart-30)</sup>

Mara is also the patron of [marriage](Lore_Marriage.md),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dinya-28)</sup> and the act of union is carried out at her shrines and temples.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ROM-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TBOM-35)</sup> The Divine teaches that the spark of union between two mortals comes from within and is not based on appearance.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dinya-28)</sup> The oaths and vows of marriage create life from love and are respected in the name of Mara.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Heart-30)</sup> The act of union is confirmed upon taking the Pledge of Mara and Mara does not place any restrictions on marriage, thus all may marry, irrespective of gender and race, and unite their souls in the holy union.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-APAYQ-1)</sup> Mara makes her will known to her priests through visions,<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Erandur-36)</sup> and commands them to aid those whose love needs guidance.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TOL-24)</sup> The priesthood of Mara strive to unite all mortals and are intolerant of those who are intolerant of others.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DFBenevolence-37)</sup> Mara's Prayer from the Doomed is a prayer recited in desperate situations.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-38)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/ON-render-Heart%27s_Week_Companions.jpg/200px-ON-render-Heart%27s_Week_Companions.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Heart%27s_Week_Companions.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Heart%27s_Week_Companions.jpg) Heart's Week celebrations in Daggerfall [Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md) is a holiday celebrated throughout Tamriel on the 16th of [Sun's Dawn](Lore_Sun%27s_Dawn.md) and serves as the central day of the week-long Heart’s Week festival, honoring love wherever it is found, among friends, lovers, and even passing strangers.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONRL-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Event Announcement-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-DF-41)</sup> Ironically, Heart's Day originated as the Summoning Day dedicated to [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Debauchery.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-DF-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LATH-42)</sup> As newer religions rose to prominence, they appropriated the holiday and sanitized Sanguine’s influence in order to strengthen devotion to their own traditions.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LATH-42)</sup> Since then, it has been predominantly associated with Mara.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-MPP-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-BHD-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-CLAL-45)</sup>

### Worship in Black Marsh
The majority of the population of the province of Black Marsh venerate the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), and, in the Second Era, worship among the Argonians of Black Marsh of Mara and the gods was limited to a small minority of those who had assimilated to the culture of the Empire.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-46)</sup> Imperial missionary efforts in the province in the Third Era were unsuccessful as missionaries died from local diseases, such as at Umpholo, where a Temple of Mara was constructed.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Argonian-47)</sup> Mara is known to have been worshipped as a member of the pantheon of the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), the indigenous humans of Black Marsh, and was considered one of their three "mothers in the Around-Us", as well as [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) and [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TWGIT-48)</sup>

### Worship in Hammerfell
The worship of Mara in the province of Hammerfell is divided between the Crowns, who represent the conservatives, and Forebears, who represent the cosmopolitan Redguards.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Crowns-58)</sup> Mara is known to both Crowns and Forebears as [Morwha](Lore_Morwha.md), a fertility goddess, however, the names Morwha and Mara are used interchangeably among the Forebears.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Forebears-59)</sup> Morwha is considered the favorite wife of Akatosh or Ruptga, to the Forebears and Crowns, respectively.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Forebears-59)</sup> The goddess is always depicted with four arms so that she may "grab more husbands".<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Crowns-58)</sup> Mara is worshipped throughout Hammerfell, including the Island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md),<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Forebears-59)</sup> and is the patron deity of the regions of [Antiphyllos](Lore_Antiphyllos.md),<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Antiphyllos-60)</sup> [Ayasofya](Lore_Ayasofya.md),<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Ayasofya-61)</sup> [Ephesus](Lore_Ephesus.md),<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Ephesus-62)</sup> and [Ykalon](Lore_Ykalon.md).<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Ykalon-63)</sup> In the late Third Era, similarly to High Rock, the Benevolence of Mara was responsible for the administration of temples dedicated to Mara and the Maran Knights was also active in the province at this time.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DFBenevolence-37)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Maran_Knights-64)</sup> A Conservatorium of Morwha was located in the city of [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) in the late First Era.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-65)</sup>

### Worship in High Rock
[![](https://images.uesp.net/d/df/DF-sign-Benevolence_of_Mara.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-sign-Benevolence_of_Mara.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-sign-Benevolence_of_Mara.png) Symbols of the Benevolences of Mara in the Iliac Bay region Mara, a member of the pantheon of the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOFE-8)</sup> is the patron deity of several regions of High Rock, such as [Anticlere](Lore_Anticlere.md), [Betony](Lore_Betony.md), [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), and [Northmoor](Lore_Northmoor.md).<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-66)</sup> It is said that villages which have clement weather year-round are protected by the Mother Goddess.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TPThe Blue Dawn-67)</sup> In Anticlere, the 21st of Last Seed was known as [Appreciation Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Holidays#Last_Seed), an ancient holiday of thanksgiving for a bountiful harvest. It was considered a holy and contemplative day devoted to Mara, the goddess-protector of the region.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-DFAppreciation-68)</sup>

Nedic sculptures depicted coupled idols of an Elven Mara embracing a Mannish Lorkhan, seen as early evidence of Aldmeri culture being welcomed with open arms by the [Manmer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Manmeri).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Manmer-69)</sup> In the late Third Era, temples dedicated to the Divine were administered by the [Benevolence of Mara](Lore_Benevolence_of_Mara.md), a religious organization dedicated to Mara and her teachings, and could be found in major urban centers throughout the province. The Benevolence of Mara was led by a patriarch and was allied to the [Temple of Stendarr](Lore_Temple_of_Stendarr.md), a religious organization dedicated to [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) and his teachings.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DFBenevolence-37)</sup> The [Maran Knights](Lore_Maran_Knights.md) were a controversial knightly order dedicated to Mara and the protection of her temples. Some within the priesthood of Mara called for the disbandment of the order, while others argued the order was necessary to create peace.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Maran_Knights-64)</sup>

### Worship in Morrowind
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/MW-banner-Mara.png/100px-MW-banner-Mara.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Mara.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Mara.png) Banner of Mara in Morrowind The Dunmer of Morrowind largely neglect worship of Mara and the Divines, however, in the late Third Era, worship of Mara and the Divines was practiced by a small minority of Dunmer on the Island of Vvardenfell. Worship of the Divine in Vvardenfell in the late Third Era was largely confined to its non-Dunmer population who prayed at shrines operated by the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), a missionary group, which combined worship of the Divines and provided services for all the Divines at shrines found at Imperial forts.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ICMOR-70)</sup> A shrine to Mara and the Divines was also located in the Royal Palace of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) during this time.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TRRP-71)</sup> Following the collapse of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) in 3E 427, worshippers of Mara and the Divines formed the majority of [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) and Vvardenfell districts.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-72)</sup> In the Second Era, during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), shrines of Mara were located in the cities of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md) and Mournhold.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ROM-34)</sup>

### Worship in the Reach
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/ON-creature-Mara_%28wolf%29.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Mara_%28wolf%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Mara_(wolf).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Mara_(wolf).jpg) A direwolf of the Shrine of the Hunt-Father, named after Mara [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cf/ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg/150px-ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Reachfolk_Mara_Altar.jpg) An altar bearing Mara's seal in a sacred Reachman site During the Interregnum, it was customary to decorate the steets of Markarth for the celebrations of the [Heart's Week](Lore_Heart%27s_Week.md). The decoartions included banners, blue floral patterns and hanging Amulets of Mara, along with pink and red flowers associated with Dibella.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Markarth Mara-73)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), two [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachman.md) clans, [Eagleseers](Lore_Eagleseer_Clan.md) and [Six-Fords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Six-Fords) were to hold a handfasting upon an altar of Mara at [Hroldan Ring](Lore_Hroldan_Ring.md) to unite the two clans.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ONVAO-74)</sup> One of the direwolves kept in the [Shrine of the Hunt-Father](Lore_Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.md)'s grounds was named after Mara.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Mara-75)</sup> Some Reacholk marriage traditions share similarities to Mara worshipping Bretons and [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elves.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TW-76)</sup>

After imperialization and the loss of independence, some [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Mara, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Belchimac-77)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Cedran-78)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Degaine-79)</sup> As of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) several Reachfolk accept marriage under Mara.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TBOM-35)</sup> Spirits of [Briarhearts](Lore_Briarheart.md), whose original hearts were defiled are unable to pass to the afterlife,<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-BWN-80)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-COTHD-81)</sup> unless the original heart of the deceased Briarheart is purified at the shrine of Mara and properly buried.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-SKCOTH-82)</sup>

### Worship in Skyrim
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/87/SR-concept-Wolf_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Wolf_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Wolf_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Wolf_Mural.jpg) Mara as the Wolf carved in stone [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/LG-cardart-Priest_of_Mara.png/200px-LG-cardart-Priest_of_Mara.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Priest_of_Mara.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Priest_of_Mara.png) A priestess of Mara in Skyrim The Divine is also worshipped as a member of the Nordic pantheon.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOFE-8)</sup> The Sacred Wolf was one of the most revered Atmoran animal totems, associated by later Nords with the goddess Mara,<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Frostbane_Wolf-83)</sup> who was known as **Mother Wolf**.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-TWASC-84)</sup><sup>[[82]](#cite_note-OM-85)</sup> In the Second Era, Mara was associated with the [Ice Wolves](Lore_Ice_Wolf.md) that inhabited the mouth of the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) in the northeast of the province.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-White_River_Ice_Wolf_Pup-86)</sup> Wooden figures of Mara depict a naked woman partially clothed in wolf pelts who bears fangs of her own, similar to Atmoran stone carvings.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-WMF-87)</sup>

Worship of Mara in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) was widespread in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) and shrines dedicated to the Divine could be found both in the wilderness and in urban centers, such as at the Temple of the Divines in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md),<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-SRTOTD-88)</sup> and at the Temple of Mara in [Riften](Lore_Riften.md).<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-SRTOM-89)</sup> Shrines dedicated to Mara usually have flower-spouting fertility statuettes.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-MFS-90)</sup> A pond in the hold of Eastmarch was named after Mara.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-MEP-91)</sup> Some argued the return of the dragons in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) was a sign from Mara signaling her displeasure with the people of Skyrim.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Maramal-11)</sup>

To Nordic traditionalists, Mara takes a lesser role in the pantheon, considered to be Kyne's handmaiden, and one of Shor's concubines.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOFN-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-TTWA-92)</sup> Her worship tends to be more emphasized in areas with a large Imperial influence. Amulets of Mara are frequently worn to indicate that someone is looking to marry, as life is hard and full of hardship in Skyrim courtships tend to be very short. Marriages are often held in Riften's Temple of Mara.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Maramal-11)</sup>

### Worship in the Summerset Isles
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/LO-people-Mara.jpg/150px-LO-people-Mara.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Mara.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Mara.jpg) Altmeri depiction of Mara Mara is worshipped in the High Elven pantheon.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-VOFA-20)</sup> An Altmer legend tells that Nenamil, the Archon of Song, once embarked on a pilgrimage to see Mara's beauty in person, believing that the greatest love he could feel was love for the mother of creation. His journey eventually took him to what is now known as [Archon's Grove](Lore_Archon%27s_Grove.md), where he found a flower of surpassing beauty, the so-called Rose-of-Archon. Upon seeing the flower, he realized that Mara was more than just a being: she was beauty incarnate, found in every exquisite thing. From then on, Nenamil would preach Mara's beauty and the Rose-of-Archon would honor and represent her. Roses-of-Archon are collected by the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) for the Divine Reverence to Mara, a ceremony that lasts for at least fifty-eight days.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-93)</sup>

## Artifacts
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Chalice_of_Mara.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Chalice_of_Mara.png) Chalice of Mara During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), a Temple of Mara located in the town of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md), Cyrodiil, was home to several holy relics of Mara, including a chalice, phylactery, dagger, and circlet. The Chalice of Mara was alleged to have been a wedding gift from Mara to St. [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), founder of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), at her wedding to [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md).<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ONTMA-94)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Sphinxmoth Bandits, a group of bandits operating from the neighboring [Fort Sphinxmoth](Lore_Fort_Sphinxmoth.md), looted the temple of its valuables, including the relics. The bandits were defeated by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the relics were subsequently returned to the temple.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-ONAffront-95)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), on the [Island of Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), the Divine appeared before the Nerevarine as an apparition in the form of a Breton woman named **Ama Nin** and gifted the Nerevarine her blouse and skirt.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-96)</sup> Some claim the [Everflow Ewer](Lore_Everflow_Ewer.md) was created by Mara.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-97)</sup>

Of the [Crusader's Relics](Lore_Crusader%27s_Relics.md), it is unknown whether Mara created the Cuirass or Greaves of the Crusader. The Crusader's Relics are a set of artifacts created by the Divines in the early First Era for [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), the Divine Crusader, to allow him to defeat and banish [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md), an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) Sorcerer King. Regardless, Pelinal, despite his victory over Umaril, was slain and the Crusader's Relics were scattered and lost for thousands of years.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-KOTNBook-98)</sup> The Cuirass of the Crusader was held by the Wyrm of Elynglenn in Cyrodiil until recovered by Sir Amiel Lannus, a member of the chivalric order the Knights of the Nine, in 3E 111.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-99)</sup> The cuirass was stored in the undercroft of the Priory of the Nine, where it lay until recovered by the Hero of Kvatch in 3E 433.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-KOTNPOTN-100)</sup> The Greaves of the Crusader were recovered by Sir Berich Vlindrel and were buried with him in the [Vlindrel](Lore_Vlindrel.md) family tomb, but was later recovered by Sir Roderic of Wayrest and his squire Lathon, who gifted the greaves to the Hero of Kvatch.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-KOTNTFS-101)</sup>

### Mara's Blouse
[Mara's Blouse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara%27s_Blouse) is a piece of clothing associated with the Goddess of Love, Mara. It is a red blouse imbued with magical properties that protect the wearer from the effects of hostile spells.

### Mara's Skirt
[Mara's Skirt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara%27s_Skirt) is a piece of clothing associated with the Goddess of Love, Mara. It is a red skirt imbued with magical properties that improve the vitality and health of the wearer.

### Mother Mild's Sickle of Absolution
[Mother Mild's Sickle of Absolution](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mother_Mild%27s_Sickle_of_Absolution) is a lesser known [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with goddess Mara. Little is known of the sickle or of the practices it was tied to, though it was said to have been associated with blood rites long before such rituals became linked with sinister or macabre connotations. According to [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md), Mara did not always harvest crops alone.

## Planet
The planet [Mara](Lore_Mara_(planet).md), sometimes called **MHARA**, is one of the Planets in the skies of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), and according to the Warrior-Poet, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), is one of the eight worlds known to the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). Mara is [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar_(planet).md)'s satellite and is orbiting around it, while [Dibella](Lore_Dibella_(planet).md) is orbiting around Mara. As with all astral bodies seen in the skies above [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and their corresponding deities, the planet Mara is believed to be the plane of the goddess Mara as well as the goddess herself, as seen from the mortal plane. It is said to actually be a different [plane of existence](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) in its own right, infinite in size and mass, with its appearance as a sphere being only a visual phenomenon caused by mortal mental stress. According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).
