Logrolf was the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the final years of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md). At some point during his reign, Logrolf traveled to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to pay his respects to Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak). In the Imperial City, a reception was held for Logrolf in the city's Skyrim Embassy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CMN-1)</sup> In [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), following the collapse of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), Logrolf was assassinated, leaving his daughter, [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md), as his heir presumptive.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCOF-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lyris-3)</sup> After Logrolf's assassination, Jarl [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) claimed that Freydis was illegitimate, ultimately causing a schism over the high kingship that resulted in the division of Skyrim between [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md), ruled by Freydis and her heirs, and [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md), ruled by Svartr and his successors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCOF-2)</sup> Logrolf was the last high king of a united Skyrim for at least the next one hundred fifty years.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lyris-3)</sup> Following the start of the schism, many of the reminders of Logrolf's reign in Solitude were destroyed.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONTreasures L-4)</sup>
