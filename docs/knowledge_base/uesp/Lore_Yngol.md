Yngol was the son of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) and older brother of [Ylgar](Lore_Ylgar.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup> He was a brave strategist who defeated enemies before they even knew the battle had begun.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup> He was also purportedly the greatest [blacksmith](Lore_Smithing.md) the [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) had ever known.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sot R5-2)</sup>

After the human city of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md) was sacked by the [Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md), in an event known as the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md), Yngol fled back to Atmora with his father and brother.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup> According to [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) myth, he forged [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md), Ysgramor's legendary battleaxe, on the deck of the fleeing ship the same night and presented it to his father.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sot R5-2)</sup>

After returning to Atmora, Ysgramor sent Yngol and his brother to recruit the bravest warriors of the land and form the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). During the Return, he commanded a ship called the Harakk, but it was lost during the Storm of Separation just before they arrived at [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup> It was eventually found along the shore, but Yngol was dead.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-YATSG-3)</sup> It was a devastating loss to the Five Hundred Companions.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NOYB-4)</sup> It is said that Ysgramor wept in his grief and slew a dozen beasts, burning them in honor of his fallen son.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-YATSG-3)</sup> A [barrow-hill](Lore_Yngol_Barrow.md) was dug in the [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) tradition, and Yngol was laid to rest with rites and honors among his clansmen far below the rocky face of Hsaarik Head, the first of the Children of the Sky to perish in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-YATSG-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR2-1)</sup> Yngol's resting place ultimately determined the placement of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and the Palace of the Kings, as Ysgramor wanted to be able to view his son's resting place from the windows of his palace.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SOTR19-5)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) explored his tomb and encountered the ghostly shade of Yngol. After defeating the spirit, Yngol's ancient enchanted helmet was retrieved from his skeletal remains.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Skyrim-6)</sup>
