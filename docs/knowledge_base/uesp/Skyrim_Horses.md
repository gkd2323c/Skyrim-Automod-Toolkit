[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/SR-creature-Horse.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Horse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horse.jpg) Horse **Horses** are available as mounts in Skyrim, and improve your [movement](Skyrim_Movement.md) speed. They are somewhat slower than horses in [Cyrodiil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Horse) but are better at combat and have more endurance. If you fast travel to a city, then an owned horse you are riding will move to the stables outside the city; an unowned horse will return to its starting point. Only an owned horse will stay where you left it when you dismount. Horses you do not own will begin to travel back to their normal locations if you dismount them.

## Finding a Horse
Horses can be purchased from [stables](Skyrim_Stables.md), located outside each major [city](Skyrim_Cities.md). All horses have the same speed, stamina, and health, varying only in color. The only two exceptions to this are [Shadowmere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmere) and [Frost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost).

Stealing horses is considered to be a [crime](Skyrim_Crime.md). To steal a horse, simply mount it and start riding. Stealing a horse incurs a 50 gold [bounty](Skyrim_Crime.md#Bounties) in that [hold](Skyrim_Holds.md) which stacks every time a guard notices (or is told about) you mounting a stolen horse (even if you dismount and then mount the same stolen horse). However, stealing a horse while hidden in sneak mode allows you to ride the horse without incurring a bounty. It is still considered a stolen horse, so dismounting and then mounting the horse in plain sight will incur a bounty. Note that most horses you encounter are likely to be owned by someone, even if you find the horse in the middle of the wilderness. When dismounted, owned horses will stay in the spot you left them, but stolen and unowned horses will run home.

Several horses can be freely borrowed from their owners once you befriend the stable owners. Although you are free to ride these horses, as soon as you dismount the horse will start to return home (occasionally sliding/moving so quickly that it is impossible to catch up with the horse). They may reappear after you fast travel. Locations include:

- [Riften Stables](Skyrim_Riften_Stables.md): A horse can be borrowed by [winning a brawl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bloody_Nose) with [Hofgrir](Skyrim_Hofgrir_Horse-Crusher.md), or by erasing [Shadr](Skyrim_Shadr.md)'s [debt](Skyrim_Stabilized.md) with [Sapphire](Skyrim_Sapphire_(person).md).
- [Katla's Farm](Skyrim_Katla%27s_Farm.md) ([Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md)): Horses can be borrowed by helping [Katla](Skyrim_Katla.md) [harvest her crops](Skyrim_Gather_Wheat.md).

If the owner of a horse, such as a [hunter](Skyrim_Hunter.md), dies by a hand other than yours, their horse becomes a free horse and you can use it without it being considered a crime.<sup>[*verification needed*]</sup> The horse will still not be owned by you and will not follow you or stay put after dismounting. The same holds true for horses stabled at forts captured during the civil war quests. There is a guaranteed unowned horse at an unmarked [hunter camp](Skyrim_Unmarked_Places.md#Hunter.2C_Fishermen_and_Other_Camps) on the northern coast of Winterhold near [Bleakcoast Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bleakcoast_Cave). There is also a [random encounter](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Bandit_Killed_With_Horse_Left_Behind) which consists of a dead bandit on the ground and a horse which is free to ride. Also, most of the military camps have two horses at them; some of those horses are free to ride as well, but only if you are aligned with the camp's particular faction (Stormcloaks or Imperial Legion). Even then, there is a chance that these horses aren't free to ride, even if you are a part of that particular faction; the reasons for this are unknown. Finally, most of the other unowned horses follow a walking route, with a spawning point and a final destination. When you fast travel, all unowned horses are reset to their original spawning positions; this means that if you fast travel to a location where a horse spawns, you can reliably mount that horse if you do so quickly, before the horse leaves on its route.

### Unique Horses
There are three unique horses available to you:

- [Shadowmere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmere) has substantially more health and somewhat more stamina than a normal horse, extremely high health regeneration and a unique look. He also returns from the dead at the place where he was first obtained ten days later, if killed. He is obtained during the [Dark Brotherhood](Skyrim_Dark_Brotherhood.md) quest [The Cure for Madness](Skyrim_The_Cure_for_Madness.md). Note that to obtain this horse you must participate in the Dark Brotherhood questline—if you [destroy them](Skyrim_Destroy_the_Dark_Brotherhood!.md) instead, this horse will never be available.
- [Frost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost) has more health and stamina than a regular horse, but is otherwise not unique. He can be obtained during the quest [Promises to Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Promises_to_Keep) started in [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md).
- [Arvak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Arvak)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> is a spectral undead horse added with the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md). He can be summoned for 60 seconds, but will remain with you until you dismount even if past the time limit. You must complete the [Soul Cairn Horse Quest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Cairn_Horse_Quest) in order to obtain the [Summon Arvak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summon_Arvak) spell.
- A fourth unique horse, [Karinda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Karinda), is available on PC via the Console, but does not otherwise appear in-game. She was intended to appear in the beta version of "[The Whispering Door](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Whispering_Door)" and has unlimited health and stamina.
- 3 unique horses, [Caravan A Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unused_Creatures#Caravan_A_Horse), [Caravan B Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unused_Creatures#Caravan_B_Horse), and [Caravan C Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unused_Creatures#Caravan_C_Horse), were intended to be assigned to each of the [Khajiit Caravans](Skyrim_Khajiit_Traders.md) (one horse per Caravan), but are never seen at them.
- The [Headless Horseman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Headless_Horseman) rides a unique, unobtainable Ghost horse.

### Tamable Horses<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Wild_Horses.md)</sup>
The [Wild Horses](Skyrim_Wild_Horses.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the ability to tame [wild horses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wild_Horse). Wild horses have more health and stamina than purchasable horses, and are [essential](Skyrim_Essential_NPCs.md). When you encounter one, you will have to chase it down and mount it, prompting the following message:

*Wild horses will buck until tamed. Stay mounted until they submit. If you are thrown, try again. Once tamed, you can add a saddle and rename them at any stable.* *When changing horses, tamed horses will return to the location where they were found.* You will need to remain mounted on the horse until it is successfully tamed. It has a chance to ragdoll you when bucking (or, more accurately, rearing), which will force you to chase down the fleeing horse once you get back on your feet. Once tamed, the horse is yours. Most wild horses will return to the original location they were found, but the [Unicorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unicorn) will instead return to the [College of Winterhold](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(place).md).

There are eight wild horses in total. All except the Unicorn are named "Wild Horse", but are distinguished here based on their miscellaneous objective name:

- **[Horse Whisperer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Horse_Whisperer)** - [Red Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Find_and_tame_the_Red_Horse)
- [Dapple Brown Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Find_and_tame_the_Dapple_Brown_Horse)
- [White Spotted Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Find_and_tame_the_White_Spotted_Horse)
- [Pale Mare](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Find_and_tame_the_Pale_Mare)
- [Grey Spotted Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Find_and_tame_the_Grey_Spotted_Horse)
- [Black Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Find_and_tame_the_Black_Horse)
- [Chestnut Horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Find_and_tame_the_Chestnut_Horse)
- **[Creature of Legend](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Creature_of_Legend)** - [Unicorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unicorn)

## Horse Abilities
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/SR-activity-Mounted_Combat.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mounted_Combat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mounted_Combat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mounted_Combat.jpg) Mounted combat - When mounted, allows for faster speed, except when fast-traveling.
- Carry weight that is independent of yours, allowing for sprinting and fast travel even when you are overencumbered.
- Can distract and attack enemies during combat.
- Allows for climbing extremely steep slopes such as mountains, and surviving some falls that would normally kill you, but instead kill your horse.
- Will fast travel with you to places if lost.
- Will count as a witness for crimes you commit in its presence.
- Pressing the sprint button while on a horse will allow the horse to run faster for a short time without depleting your stamina— horses have their own stamina pool.
- If your stamina is currently depleted and it doesn't regenerate (such as with being a vampire in sunlight) before getting on your horse, your horse will have 0 stamina and cannot sprint until you replenish your stamina.
- [Mounted combat](Skyrim_Combat.md#Horse-Mounted_Combat) is enabled as of patch 1.6; you can use melee or ranged attacks on horseback, but not magic.

### Horse Naming<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Wild_Horses.md)</sup>
The option to rename your horses is added by the Wild Horses Creation. You can only rename wild horses. ### Saddles<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Wild_Horses.md)</sup>
With the Wild Horses Creation, you can speak to any stablemaster about changing your active mount's saddle by selecting the dialogue option: *"I'd like to change my horse's saddle."* This will allow you to select from a number of visual options for your horse:

- Standard saddle (100 gold)
- White saddle (100 gold)
- Imperial saddle (100 gold)
- Stormcloak saddle (100 gold)
- Dark Brotherhood saddle (100 gold)
- Remove the saddle (free)

These saddle options are available to wild horses as well as base game horses like Shadowmere.

#### Notes
- The Dark Brotherhood Saddle isn't available unless the "[With Friends Like These...](Skyrim_With_Friends_Like_These....md)" quest is completed.
- Saddles cannot be worn at the same time as horse armor. It will remove the horse armor.
- Every time the saddle is changed regardless if it's a saddle already paid for it will still cost 100 gold.
- The Reindeer and Dwarven horse cannot wear saddles.

## Notes
- The disguised bandits at [Nilheim](Skyrim_Nilheim.md) will attack and even kill your horse without provocation. See [the place page](Skyrim_Nilheim.md#Bugs) for details.
- If your [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md) accidentally hits your horse in combat (or vice versa), they can become aggressive towards each other.
- You can mount a horse that is hostile to you. The horse will stop attacking you entirely while you are riding him and function normally. Once you dismount, though, the horse will go back to being hostile until you mount him again. Fast traveling may cause the horse to become friendly to you.<sup>[*verification needed — see [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:talk_Horses#Fast_traveling_on_unfriendly_horses)*]</sup>
- A horse you own will wait for you where you dismount. If you leave your mount outside of a dungeon with more than one entrance, it will still be by the exit that you entered the dungeon but if you leave from an alternate exit fast-traveling will reunite you and your horse.
- If your horse is killed while you are mounted upon it, you will be thrown to the ground. This results in you being unable to attack or defend until you regain your feet, similar to the effects of being ragdolled by an [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) shout.
- Only the horse that you last rode will travel with you. Horses you bought will return to where they were purchased as soon as you mount a different horse, while horses obtained as quest rewards will stay where you leave them. If you wish to not have the horse you are riding follow you around anymore, there are a few options: - If you have Dawnguard installed and have learned the spell Summon Arvak, you can summon him and ride him around for at least a minute, then when you dismount him there will no longer be a horse following you.
- You can find an unowned horse or steal one (there are several unowned horses available on the map, and every major city has a stable where you can find a horse to steal) and fast travel with it into a major city; when the horse "goes to the stables" it actually returns to its original location.
- You can kill the horse you last rode (if it's an unowned horse on the map, it will respawn).
- Horses can catch [diseases](Skyrim_Disease.md). When a horse is in combat with an animal, there is a chance that your horse will catch a disease (your horse will exude a whitish glow similar to when you catch a disease) from whatever animal he was fighting. The disease does affect the horse, although some (such as Witbane) will not have a noticeable effect, some others can be especially detrimental in [Survival Mode](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md), such as Crippling Rattles which permanently disable the horse's Stamina regen, as there is no way to cure them. Disabling Survival Mode doesn't seem to remove the disease from horses/followers.
- Be careful when fast-traveling to the [College of Winterhold](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(place).md) on horseback, as you will spawn on a narrow section of the bridge, making it easy to fall off and die if you are not careful.
- The horse from Whiterun stables, according to Skulvar, is called Queen Alfsigr. He also calls her Allie, for short.
- The [Headless Horseman's horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Headless_Horseman#Horse) is a spectral horse which [roams around Skyrim](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Headless_Horseman) at night together with its rider. It cannot be interacted with in any way.
- You will always mount your horse from the left side. Even if you activate the "ride" prompt from any other direction or angle, you will always mount it from the left. This also means that if you are too close to something on the left side while mounted on a horse (such as a low wall, boulder, or other blocking object), you can't dismount, either.
- Horses rear back if you press the jump button while stationary. In order to jump while riding a horse, you have to be moving: the horse gallops a bit (even if you hadn't pressed the sprint command) and then jumps. Even then, horse jumping is highly different than the jumps that you can perform, as it is more of a leap forward than an actual jump. Because of the slight delay between the command and its performance, you have to time horse leaping and what you want to jump over.
- Most horses tend to have very little courage in battle. If you don't engage in mounted combat and instead dismount to fight hostiles, expect your horse to run far away from the scene of the attack.
- If you are rag-dolled off of a wild horse while taming it, you can die even on level ground. There is no documented best way to tame a wild horse.
- While unmounted horses are capable of backing up, mounted horses cannot. Using the controls to move backward has the horse turn around to face that direction.
- There is a rare animation while seated on a horse where you pet their neck.
- The Imperial saddle existed in the game before the Creation Club add-on. It could only be seen in the introduction quest, [Unbound](Skyrim_Unbound.md), where the horses ridden by the Imperial soldiers (including [General Tullius's](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md) mount) use this saddle. Standard saddles found without the add-on are made from brown fur and have horns (reminiscent of those found on the Iron Helmet) attached. Depending on the size of your character (such as a female Khajiit, which is the smallest), it can also seem like your character is holding the horns of the saddle, rather than the reins. "Phantom" reins are held with saddle-less horses (including Arvak), although there isn't anything where your character's hand is placed.
- Attempting to ride a horse through a cave opening will cause this warning to appear at the top left of the screen: "Dismount Horse before going through door". Approaching closely also causes this warning, even if you did not attempt it.
- If you are dual-wielding weapons and engage in mounted combat, you will always use the right weapon for every attack, even if you use the command for attacking with the left hand; you will lean over in your saddle and attack on the left with your right weapon. You cannot use shields while on horseback, either, though they will remain equipped. Same goes for all staves. However, torches can be equipped while mounted on horseback: they don't give off as much light as when on foot and the torch is placed awkwardly (as it is in the hand resting on the saddle).
- Certain spell effects will remain on you even after mounting a horse, such as cloak spells (like [flame cloak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Flame_Cloak)), armor spells from the school of [Alteration](Skyrim_Alteration.md), and the [candlelight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Candlelight) spell.
