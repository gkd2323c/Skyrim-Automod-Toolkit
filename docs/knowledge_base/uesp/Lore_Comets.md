# Comets

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-crown_store-Comet%2C_Aetherial.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Comet%2C_Aetherial.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Comet,_Aetherial.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Comet,_Aetherial.jpg) An Aetherial Comet (ESO) Comets are celestial bodies characterized by their small size, composition of ice and dust,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-S33-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TV-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skills-3)</sup> and the distinctive trails known as a comet's tail.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSEBOA-4)</sup> They move through the heavens,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CA-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCD-6)</sup> and are often linked to significant events and frequently hold symbolic importance across various cultures and religions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCD-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EG-7)</sup> They also are present in some magical practices.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-IDOF-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Skills2-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skills-3)</sup>

## Cultural Interpretations
During the Interregnum, vials of comet dust were commonly found across Tamriel. Some of these small vials contained dust that allegedly fell from the sky when a starry comet streaked over the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TV-2)</sup> Some celestial animals were named after the comet.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Mount-10)</sup> Comet-Wine is an [alcoholic beverage](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md) named after the comet.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TTBL-11)</sup> [Magic](Lore_Magic.md) involving the summoning of comets from constellations existed.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-IDOF-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Skills2-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skills-3)</sup>

### Altmer
Comets are a present theme in Altmeri [musical](Lore_Music.md) and poetic traditions. One [song](Lore_Songs.md), *[The Star-Eyed Bride of Alinor](Lore_The_Star-Eyed_Bride_of_Alinor.md)*, uses the imagery of a comet's tail to evoke the beauty of an Altmeri lady's hair.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSEBOA-4)</sup>

### Ayleid
Ayleids were known to recreate the effect of a shooting [star](Lore_Stars.md) passing through the night sky using magic. This practice was later adopted by mages from other cultures for decorative purposes, such as an individual known as Uryacil.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CA-5)</sup>

### Dunmer
One of the origin stories of [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) claims that it was a frozen comet that Vivec suspended in the air above the city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-S33-1)</sup>

An esoteric text of Velothi origins, *[From Exile to Exodus](Lore_From_Exile_to_Exodus.md)*, describes [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) to be as bright as eightfold comets.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FETE-12)</sup>

### Khajiit
The [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) believe that the sight of a falling star is a sign from [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) that someone is in great need.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Zerith-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Krinze-14)</sup>

[Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md), an ancient Khajiiti hero, was famously known for taming a comet according to one of the tales, although this is believed to be an exaggeration.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AT-15)</sup>

### Reachfolk
According to the hagiography of [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md), a great comet blazed across the sky on the night of his birth, and a flock of silver [swans](Lore_Swan.md) flew over his mother's humble shelter at the hour he came into the world.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCD-6)</sup>

### Redguard
An [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) account by [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) states that King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) gained support for his alliance with King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) and stirred religious fervor after a starry comet appeared in the northern skies, which he believed was a sign from [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding), the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) Make Way [God](Lore_Gods.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EG-7)</sup>

## Notes
- One source claims that Lorkhan's Heart has often been described as a falling star or comet, similar to other imaginative descriptions like fires thrown by Sheogorath or the burning corpses of racial heroes such as Baan-Dar, and giant rocks like the Ministry of Truth. It argues that these are fanciful tales rather than accurate accounts, favoring the theory that comets and "falling stars" are natural and necessary transfers of Aetheric energies into Mundus.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SV-16)</sup>
