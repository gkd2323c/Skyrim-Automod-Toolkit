# Flesh Sculpture

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/db/LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png/200px-LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png) Flesh Sculpture (Legends) Flesh sculpture is a style of [flesh magic](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md) based around the manipulation of flesh of living beings, be it for healing or for cosmetic purposes such as changing one's face.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Galathil-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESONecromancer-2)</sup>

While seemingly obscure, flesh sculpting isn't something reserved to the underworld of Tamriel. The "art" of flesh-sculpture can be traced back to the [Daedraphiles](Lore_Daedraphiles.md), [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city-states who adopted widespread [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era). The Ayleids subjected their [slaves](Lore_Slavery.md) to the practice to honor the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) they worshipped.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DWTA-3)</sup> Legend speaks of the Wailing Wheels of [Vindasel](Lore_Vindasel.md), where the Ayleids derived strange pleasure by subjecting [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) slaves to the "art-torture" of using their skin for flesh sculptures.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAyleid N-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TA-5)</sup> The exiled [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) brought this horrific art form with them to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), where they enslaved local [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) for the color and texture of their skin.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAyleid N-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Captured Time-6)</sup> [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) contains a Faculty of Chirurgeons where masters of flesh sculpture teach their art. [Nohotogrha](Lore_Nohotogrha.md), an oasis found in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), is also home to flesh sculptors known as the Hollow-Faced Men. One known practitioner of this art, [Galathil](Lore_Galathil.md), claims to have learned her art in both of these places circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Galathil-1)</sup> According to Galathil, flesh sculpting cannot be used on the dead, which includes those afflicted with [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Galathil-1)</sup> Despite its magical nature, changing one's appearance requires conventional surgical tools such as knives.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Galathil-1)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), some [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) used flesh sculpting as a means of healing themselves and others.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESONecromancer-2)</sup>
