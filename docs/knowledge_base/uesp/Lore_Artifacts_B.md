## Barilzar's Mazed Band
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/LG-misc-Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.jpg/200px-LG-misc-Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-misc-Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-misc-Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.jpg) Barilzar's Mazed Band [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md) was an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of immense power created by the former [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md), [Barilzar](Lore_Barilzar.md), in the middle of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). It was capable of teleporting multiple people at once across dimensions. The ring's powers far exceeded Barilzar's intentions, as it was able to open rifts in time and space, and could even open the gates of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), releasing nightmarish creatures upon the land. The ring was cursed and stripped of its power, so that only a [god](Lore_Gods.md) could use it without being destroyed. This curse is thought to have resulted in the death of Barilzar, although the mage lived on as an undead [lich](Lore_Lich.md), and was sealed within an abandoned crypt in [Old Mournhold](Lore_Old_Mournhold.md) along with the ring.

**Found in:** - [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band_(item)), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Return_to_Clockwork_City)

[Enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) by [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) himself, it was given to his [Ash Vampire](Lore_Ash_Vampire.md) brother, [Dagoth Uthol](Lore_Ash_Vampire.md#Dagoth_Uthol) of [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md). ## Bilious Censer
The [Bilious Censer](Lore_Bilious_Censer.md) is a curio associated with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), found in the form of a thurible. Judging by its name, it appears to be capable of inducing nausea.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), it was last known to be in the possession of Gazmod the Collector, a [watcher](Lore_Watcher.md) residing in [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), alongside the [Toxicruciform](Lore_Toxicruciform.md) and [Golden Scab](Lore_Golden_Scab.md). Eorda, a worshipper of Peryite, believed that she could gain Peryite's favor by somehow recovering these artifacts for her lord.

**Found in:** - [Lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Eorda)

## Bi Polar Blade
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/TR-weapon-Bi Polar_Blade.png/200px-TR-weapon-Bi Polar_Blade.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-weapon-Bi Polar_Blade.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-weapon-Bi Polar_Blade.png) The Bi Polar Blade The [Bi Polar Blade](Lore_BiPolar_Blade.md) is a strange [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of some renown. According to legend, a nobleman sank his fortune into creating a longsword of supreme power. Two smiths on opposite ends of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) created each half of the blade, unaware of the other, with the hope that no one man, save the owner of the blade, would know its true power. Unfortunately, the smiths were not given enough instruction, and created halves with enchantments that completely negated each other. One half of the blade is [golden](Lore_Gold.md), and the other half is made of a brown metal. The sword is enchanted with the ability to both magically calm and frenzy a humanoid. These conflicting enchantments make the blade practically useless, although the calming effect is dominant.

**Found in:** - [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Bi Polar_Blade)

## Black Books
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/SR-quest-Black_Book_The_Winds_of_Change.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Black_Book_The_Winds_of_Change.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Black_Book_The_Winds_of_Change.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Black_Book_The_Winds_of_Change.jpg) A Black Book [Black Books](Lore_Black_Books.md) are [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Fate and Knowledge. Each book contains some manner of forbidden knowledge. [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) claims that no one actually knows where they came from, though the materials for their creation are prepared within a bindery in Apocrypha known as Quires Wind, before being presented to the prince to pen and complete. Some books are from the ancient past; some are from the future, as time appears to be more malleable for the Daedric Prince of fate and destiny.

The thick tomes are bound in black covers, with a symbol representing Mora on the front, and emit a black mist. The books are written with [Seeker](Lore_Seeker.md) ink that takes ten years to brew, on paper pressed from trees that have never seen the sun. The covers are derived from [Lurker](Lore_Lurker.md) leather, and the threads used to bind the tomes together come from silk originating from the [Spiral Skein](Lore_Spiral_Skein.md). All Black Books were regarded as [Tracts Perilous](Lore_Tracts_Perilous.md) due to the hazards they could impose on their readers.

A number of Black Books were spread across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by Mora, and are usually located in the depths of ancient dungeons. The exact number of Black Books is unknown to everyone except for the Daedric Prince of Knowledge himself. Within every Black Book lies a forbidden secret, serving as a tantalizing temptation or reward for mortals whom Hermaeus Mora, the Great Eye, favors.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Black_Book_(book).md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>

## Blade of Woe
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/SR-item-Blade_of_Woe.jpg/200px-SR-item-Blade_of_Woe.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Blade_of_Woe.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Blade_of_Woe.jpg) Blade of Woe The [Blade of Woe](Lore_Blade_of_Woe.md), also known as the **Night Mother's Kiss**, is a ritualistic dagger associated with [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) and the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). It is said that anyone slain with the dagger has their soul sent straight to the [Void](Lore_Void.md). It is named after the dagger the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) used to slay her own children. It takes the form of an [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) or [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Equipment.md) dagger which can, at its most lethal, instantly kill a target that is unaware of its user, shroud the wielder from view when going for the killing blow, as well as temporarily increasing its wielder's movement speed to make an escape after killing with the blade. It is also known to damage or drain health, deplete magicka and willpower, and demoralize anyone struck by it. The blood spilled by the blade may become tainted.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Blade_of_Woe), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Blade_of_Woe.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Blade_of_Woe), [Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Blade_of_Woe)

## Bloodskal Blade
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/SR-item-Bloodskal_Blade.jpg/200px-SR-item-Bloodskal_Blade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Bloodskal_Blade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Bloodskal_Blade.jpg) The Bloodskal Blade The [Bloodskal Blade](Lore_Bloodskal_Blade.md) (also known simply as **Bloodskal**) is an ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). In appearance it is an enchanted [silver](Lore_Silver.md) sword, either a long blade or a greatsword, sometimes decorated with red markings. It has the power to either cause magical frost damage to those that it strikes, or release a ribbon of mystical energy, a red energy blast which can hit an out-of-reach target.

The blade was created by the Bloodskal clan, a clan of Nords on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). It came to rest in Bloodskal Barrow, guarded by [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md). The energy blasts released by the blade were necessary to open a strange door within the ruins.

**Found in:** - [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Bloodskal), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Bloodskal_Blade.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>

## Bloodthirst
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/SR-item-Bloodthirst.jpg/200px-SR-item-Bloodthirst.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Bloodthirst.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Bloodthirst.jpg) Bloodthirst [Bloodthirst](Lore_Bloodthirst.md) is an [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) "blood drinker" longsword, the other known example being the transforming blade, [Dawnfang and Duskfang](Lore_Dawnfang_and_Duskfang.md). Another enchanted longsword, [Rimelink](Lore_Rimelink.md), is crafted in the same style as these weapons.

Like Dawn/Duskfang, its crossguard and pommel are adorned with elegant jewels, its pronged blade resembles the gaping maw of a beast, and it has a snakeskin pattern that envelops the areas around the crossguard, handle, and pommel. It differs from both in that its jewel is infused with swirling red blood. Its sinister appearance is known to make people feel uneasy. It earns its name thanks to its unquenchable thirst for blood, and it fortifies its wielder and increases their swing speed.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Bloodthirst.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Dawnfang_%26_Duskfang.md)</sup>, [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Bloodthirst)

## Bloodworm Helm

## Bow of the Fox
[Bow of the Fox](Lore_Bow_of_the_Fox.md), also known as **Bow of Shor**, is a legendary weapon linked to [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) wielded by Njor the Brave, celebrated in the legends and songs of the [Haefriding](Lore_Haefriding.md) [Nords](Lore_Nords.md). According to the legend it manifested from a dream, transforming from the visage of the [fox](Lore_Fox.md) into a bow and a set of arrows.
