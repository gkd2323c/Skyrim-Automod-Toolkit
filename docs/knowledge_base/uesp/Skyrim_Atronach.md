# Daedra

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-npc-Conjurer.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Conjurer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Conjurer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Conjurer.jpg) An atronach under the command of a conjurer **Daedra** are creatures and sentient beings that can be found across the vast expanses of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Only two of these types of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) appear in [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md), however: the atronachs and Dremora. Despite the lasting ramifications of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), conjurers across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) could not resist the allure and power associated with summoning Daedra and attempting to command them. Many of these summonings are successful and those Daedra serve their masters without fail, but even more summonings go wrong and result in unbound beings turning on their masters.

There are far fewer unbound "wild" Daedra in Skyrim than there were in the previous two Elder Scrolls games, Morrowind and Oblivion. Narratively this makes sense—Daedra-worship was always part of the Dunmer culture and was thus comparatively common in Morrowind, even during the reign of the Tribunal, and there were still many Daedric ruins there where unbound Daedra of all levels from Scamp to Golden Saint could be found, but there are no such ruins and no such cults in Skyrim at all. And since the Oblivion Crisis, the world has largely—and probably justifiably—turned away from Daedra-worship, with the exception of those still revered by the Dunmer. The Orcish cult of Malacath still persists in their strongholds, though not so much among Orcs who live in the wider world. Worship of the other Daedric Princes is mostly restricted to secretive cults.

Even summoned Daedra are not what they used to be: you can no longer soul trap your own summoned Atronachs or Dremora to fill soul gems, although you can soul trap the summoned beings of another caster.

## Daedra Types
Atronachs are intelligent [elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) that can be called forth from Oblivion to serve a [conjurer](Skyrim_Conjurer.md) in battle against his or her foes. Atronachs are all immune to [paralysis](Skyrim_Paralysis.md), cannot be [reanimated](Skyrim_Reanimate.md), and cannot be made [invisible](Skyrim_Invisibility_(effect).md) by any means. [Dremora](Skyrim_Dremora.md) are manlike beings that are treated as NPCs in-game rather than as creatures, and therefore have stats, resistances, and perks more suited to that, as detailed on their page.

### Flame Atronach
Flame Atronachs appear as female humanoids engulfed in flame, wearing intricate, black, metal armor. Though they are the weakest type of atronach encountered in Skyrim, they are extremely agile and reasonably proficient in close combat. Flame Atronachs prefer to use their flame-based magic at a distance and will actively try to avoid melee combat if at all possible. While typically found in the employ of enemy spellcasters, the player can also conjure them and use them in battle. As these atronachs levitate, they therefore possess the innate ability to [walk on water](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Waterwalking). When slain, unbound Flame Atronachs will drop [Fire Salts](Skyrim_Fire_Salts.md), an [alchemical](Skyrim_Alchemy.md) [ingredient](Skyrim_Ingredients.md).

### Frost Atronach
Frost Atronachs are Daedric constructs of solid ice that resemble oversized humanoids. They have a long melee reach and their attacks usually stagger targets, but they are entirely lacking in ranged options. Still, once caught in melee range of them, their chilling cloak slows targets and prevents easy escape. They can be summoned by you, and are occasionally used against you by enemy conjurers. All Frost Atronachs have the innate capability of [breathing underwater](Skyrim_Waterbreathing_(effect).md), which is relevant because they are also the only variant of atronach present in Skyrim that does not hover above the ground. Upon death, unbound Frost Atronachs will have [Frost Salts](Skyrim_Frost_Salts.md) on their remains that can be looted.

### Storm Atronach
Storm Atronachs are Daedra of elemental shock. They appear as a shattered statue, the cluster of its remains quickly revolving around an unseen, electrified center of mass. Their arms are only seen when they are in combat, and their legs are only witnessed when they are performing certain melee attacks. Storm Atronachs can be encountered as a hostile enemy, usually summoned by an enemy caster, or you can summon them to aid in combat. Like Flame Atronachs, the Storm Atronach hovers and thus has the [Waterwalking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Waterwalking) ability. In the destroyed remains of unbound Storm Atronachs, you will find [Void Salts](Skyrim_Void_Salts.md) that you can take.

It is worth noting when considering Storm Atronachs for summons that storm thralls lose the ability to cast the powerful [Chain Lightning](Skyrim_Chain_Lightning.md) spell when compared to the Storm Atronachs themselves. In this case, it may be wiser to simply recast [Conjure Storm Atronach](Skyrim_Conjure_Storm_Atronach.md) as needed for the more powerful summon.

### Dremora
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg) A Dremora lord Dremora, like atronachs, hail from the [planes of Oblivion](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md), but unlike atronachs they possess a malicious sentience and directly serve the [Daedric princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) in their pursuits. These ruthless beings are obsessed with destruction and have their own variants and ranks. They can wear [Daedric armor](Skyrim_Daedric.md), have powerful [fire spells](Skyrim_Destruction_Spells.md), wield blazing weapons, and be among the most difficult of foes to defeat. Dremora can be summoned by you with the [Conjure Dremora Lord](Skyrim_Conjure_Dremora_Lord.md) spell, and are a component in many quests. All specific information related to Dremora, including their ranks, roles, and stats, is captured in greater detail on the [Dremora page](Skyrim_Dremora.md).

### Lurker <sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/SR-creature-Lurker.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Lurker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Lurker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Lurker.jpg) A Lurker **Lurkers** are Daedric creatures that come from the murky waters of [Hermaeus Mora](Skyrim_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s realm of [Apocrypha](Skyrim_Apocrypha.md). They appear as giant amphibious humanoids, and serve their Prince by guarding forbidden knowledge. Lurkers attack with swipes of their huge hands, and use their long legs for stomping attacks, which have a 100 point [stagger](Skyrim_Stagger.md) effect within 16 feet. They can also attack from afar by spewing tentacles from their mouths. They are very dangerous opponents, and should be fought one at a time. They also have the ability to regenerate health.

### Seeker <sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/SR-creature-Seeker.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Seeker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Seeker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Seeker.jpg) A Seeker **Seekers** are grotesque tentacled Daedra. They are servants of [Hermaeus Mora](Skyrim_Hermaeus_Mora.md), usually guarding tomes of forbidden knowledge, and are rarely seen outside [Apocrypha](Skyrim_Apocrypha.md). [Miraak](Skyrim_Miraak_(person).md) gained control of a few Seekers while in the realm of Apocrypha, which he forces you to deal with during the quests [The Gardener of Men](Skyrim_The_Gardener_of_Men.md) and [At the Summit of Apocrypha](Skyrim_At_the_Summit_of_Apocrypha.md). When idle, Seekers usually conjure a book from thin air and begin to read. While you are undetected, they may become mostly invisible with only a smoky shadow giving away their location. Some varieties seem to, while in this invisible state, flit about the area very quickly. This can be especially surprising to sneaky characters, because very soon after they are shot with an arrow, they can appear right in front of you. Also surprising for sneak-oriented characters, Seekers' magical attacks seem to automatically strike you while they are searching for you, even if you are still undetected. These abilities make the Seeker a formidable and nerve-racking opponent for those that prefer a stealth approach.

All varieties of Seeker will summon a somewhat weaker clone to aid them in battle. This clone has less health and will disintegrate when its parent Seeker has been destroyed.

You can loot Seeker remains for various books including spell tomes but not unread skill books (from the [base game](Skyrim_Books.md) and [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Books.md)) and [scrolls](Skyrim_Scrolls.md) (only from the base game) when they die.

### Golden Saint <sup>[CC](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/SR-creature-Golden_Saint_Warrior.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Golden_Saint_Warrior.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Golden_Saint_Warrior.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Golden_Saint_Warrior.jpg) A Golden Saint Golden Saints are humanoid golden-hued Daedra that use [golden](Skyrim_Golden.md) avian-themed armor and weapons. They are primarily aligned with the Daedric Prince [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), and are very intelligent and generally hostile. Despite their angelic figure, they are a proud, arrogant race who are quick to anger and cruel in their punishment. All specific information related to Golden Saints is captured in greater detail on the [Golden Saint page](Skyrim_Golden_Saint.md).

### Dark Seducer <sup>[CC](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/SR-creature-Dark_Seducer_Warrior.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dark_Seducer_Warrior.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dark_Seducer_Warrior.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dark_Seducer_Warrior.jpg) A Dark Seducer Dark Seducers are scantily-clad, humanoid Daedra that use [dark](Skyrim_Dark.md) serpentine-themed armor and weapons. They are aligned with the Daedric Prince Sheogorath, although their allegiances are known to be flimsy. Unlike their counterparts, the Golden Saints, Dark Seducers are generally polite and patient when interacting with mortals. All specific information related to Dark Seducers is captured in greater detail on the [Dark Seducer page](Skyrim_Dark_Seducer.md).
