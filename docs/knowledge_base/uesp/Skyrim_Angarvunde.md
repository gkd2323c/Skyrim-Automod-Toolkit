[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/SR-place-Angarvunde.jpg/200px-SR-place-Angarvunde.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Angarvunde.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Angarvunde.jpg) Angarvunde **Angarvunde** is a medium-sized [Nordic ruin](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) west of [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md) containing [draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md). It contains three zones: *[Angarvunde](#Angarvunde)*, *[Angarvunde Catacombs](#Angarvunde_Catacombs)*, and *[Angarvunde Ruins](#Angarvunde_Ruins)*.

Its [word wall](Skyrim_Word_Wall.md) teaches part of the [Animal Allegiance](Skyrim_Animal_Allegiance.md) [dragon shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md).

## Related Quests
- **[Angarvunde](Skyrim_Angarvunde_(quest).md)**: Discover the treasure of Angarvunde.

## Walkthrough

### Exterior
The path leading from the road to the entrance is the natural habitat for several leveled [animals](Skyrim_Animals.md). The entrance is little more than a tunnel leading into a cave, and is marked by broken arches and fallen masonry. Outside of the entrance is a campsite, complete with a [cooking pot](Skyrim_Cooking_Pot.md) hanging over the blazing campfire, two unowned bedrolls next to the fire, and a barrel of [food](Skyrim_Food.md) with a lantern on top beside them. There is a wooden platform behind the fire, with a third unowned bedroll under a lean-to, and a square wooden table and chair beside it. On the table is a copy of the [Speech](Skyrim_Speech.md) [skill book](Skyrim_Skill_Books.md) *[A Dance in Fire, v7](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Dance_in_Fire,_v7)* and [Medresi's notes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Medresi%27s_Notes), a journal that foreshadows the [related quest](Skyrim_Angarvunde_(quest).md) awaiting inside the ruins.

### Angarvunde
The entrance leads directly into a narrow passage descending to the southwest. As you begin to explore, you will overhear [Medresi Dran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Medresi_Dran) complaining, presumably about a mining crew she was to hire, mentioned in her journal, being cowards. You will encounter her at the top of some wooden scaffolding with ramps leading down to a wider passage. She will initiate a conversation with you, offering to split the treasure with you in return for clearing out the undead infesting the place. She carries a [key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Angarvunde_Key) that is required to completely explore the ruins. Although it can be [pickpocketed](Skyrim_Pickpocket.md) with a high enough skill, this can lead to difficulties (see [bugs](#Bugs)). There are numerous [burial urns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Burial_Urn) and [urns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Urn) found throughout the ruins.

From the bottom of the scaffolding, follow the passage as it takes a couple turns, before eventually leading to a room containing a broken sarcophagus and many large urns, most of which are broken. A door in the southern corner leads directly into the main chamber, where three leveled [draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md) roam, including a magic-user. There are raised platforms on either side as you enter the chamber, and broken masonry and furniture all around the room. The only item of worth in this room is a single burial urn behind the first pillar on the left. There are three further exits from the room: a sequential pair of gates in the middle of the southwest wall that lead to your ultimate goal, a broken walkway on the southeast side leading up to a balcony and an iron door to *[Angarvunde Catacombs](#Angarvunde_Catacombs)*, and opposite, on the northwest side, a passage with stairs leading up to an iron door to *[Angarvunde Ruins](#Angarvunde_Ruins)*. On the raised sections at the rear are passages blocked by spears and windows looking through to shortcut exits from each of the side zones.

Dispatching the three enemies in the first room will result in Medresi catching up with you and offering the key. The key opens the doors to both zones. Levers that can only be accessed by traversing each side zone must be activated, raising the two gates to the southwest and giving access to the final chamber. Each lever opens one of the gates, and also lowers the spears to allow access back into the main chamber from the respective side area. You can explore the side zones in either order.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/79/SR-word_wall-Angarvunde.jpg/200px-SR-word_wall-Angarvunde.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-word_wall-Angarvunde.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-word_wall-Angarvunde.jpg) The word wall Medresi waits for you to clear out both side zones in front of the two gates to the southwest. As soon as you use the second lever, Medresi rushes ahead and meets an untimely end. Her death is scripted; you can save her from the trap by sprinting to catch up and using the [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) shout, but she dies anyway. The trap she triggers is a circular section of the floor that rises and meets a section of stake-studded ceiling that lowers, killing her instantly. The chest she was rushing toward is wide open, and contains only a small [coin purse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse) and the [broken steel sword handle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Broken_Steel_Sword_Handle). The section of floor stays in the raised position, but you can use some fallen masonry to access and loot her corpse for the few [potions of minor healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing) and other items she carried. Once that's done, inside the raised section is a flight of spiral stone stairs leading down to the true treasure room, containing the word wall, which teaches a word for the Animal Allegiance shout. In front of the wall is an unlocked boss [chest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chest) and a random enchanted weapon. When you're done, backtrack to the entrance, as there isn't a shortcut.

### Angarvunde Catacombs
Through the door are stairs descending to a short wide passageway with a cave-in at either end, and a narrower passage leading off to the southeast with a large urn in front. This turns southwest and enters a cave containing a [dead draugr](Skyrim_Containers.md#Draugr) with a [banded iron shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Banded_Iron_Shield) by the top of a stairwell in the middle of the room, with spiral wooden stairs leading down. Below you can hear another draugr patrolling. The stairs stop a short distance above the ground, requiring you to drop and find an alternative route back out. [Followers](Skyrim_Followers.md) may refuse to accompany you, although they will be waiting by the spears in the main chamber.

Once you drop down, you will be faced with a leveled draugr who is patrolling up and down more stairs to the northwest. The room under the stairwell contains little of value except a burial urn by a damaged throne. At the bottom of the stairs is a second leveled draugr, who is likely to be alerted to any fighting with the first. You are actually on the other side of one of the cave-ins passed in the previous wider passage. The passage continues to the northeast, passing a short flight of stairs on the left with an apprentice-trapped chest at the top, and another cave-in behind. Opening the chest without disarming the trap will trigger [poison darts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dart_Trap) to shoot from the side walls in front of the chest. The main passage turns a short distance after the stairs, and passes a set of shelves holding a burial urn and some [ruined books](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ruined_Book). A magic-using draugr patrols this section of passageway.

After another turn, you can see the start of catacombs ahead, and a leveled draugr slumbering in an upright alcove. There is also another leveled draugr patrolling just out of sight, who is likely to be alerted to any fighting. In an alcove to the right of the first draugr is a burial urn with three loose [septims](Skyrim_Gold.md) on the ground in front. Around the corner, stairs descend to the northwest. At the bottom, you'll find another draugr in an alcove on the right. Opposite is a broken sideboard with a burial urn in front. The passage continues through to the next area and another draugr, in an alcove behind a novice-locked chest on your left. Take care, as opposite this alcove is an open doorway into a room where another leveled draugr is patrolling. To the right of the doorway is an alcove containing a random healing, magicka, or stamina potion.

The next room contains four huge [canis roots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Canis_Root), one in each corner, an unlocked chest behind a broken pillar, and a [corundum ore vein](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ore_Vein) on the southeast wall. The exit is to the northwest, and leads back into catacombs. Opposite is an alcove containing a random healing, magicka, or stamina potion. There are stairs leading up to the northeast, with a [pressure plate](Skyrim_Pressure_Plate.md) partway up that triggers poison darts from the floor. There is a burial urn by a turn with more stairs, and another pressure plate and more poison darts from the floor. The traps can be used to your advantage, as the room at the top contains three leveled draugr, which at higher levels can all be deathlords and present a challenging fight. The room is full of vegetation, but holds nothing of interest, and climbs to the rear, before stairs lead up to wider catacombs with horizontal alcoves along both sides. Some of the alcoves hold dead draugr that can be looted for random [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md) or minor loot, some hold burial urns, and some hold slumbering draugr. Around the first corner, an alcove on the left above one of the slumbering draugr contains an empty [petty soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Petty_Soul_Gem). Work your way through these catacombs, dealing with the draugr as you go, until you reach an iron door at the top. There is a large urn to the right, and a sideboard to the left, with a copy of *[Wraith's Wedding Dowry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wraith%27s_Wedding_Dowry)*, a random [dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger), and a random potion on top.

The door leads back to *Angarvunde*, to the room behind the spears and seen through the window. The room contains two whole large urns, an unlocked chest, and a lever that both lowers the spears allowing access back to the main chamber and also raises one of the gates to the final chamber to the southwest.

### Angarvunde Ruins
Through the door is a narrow passage that takes several turns, descending flights of stairs and passing a large urn before you reach the first of several pressure plates in the floor, each of which triggers a row of [swinging blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Swinging_Blades) to swing once in each direction. The passage leads to a room containing a draugr. There is a stone table in the middle of the room with a copy of the [Illusion](Skyrim_Illusion.md) skill book *[Before the Ages of Man](Skyrim_Before_the_Ages_of_Man.md)* and a burial urn on top, and behind the table, against the wall, is a novice-locked chest. Three large urns are also found in the room. There are two sets of spears blocking two passages from this room to the northeast and southwest, an iron door to the northeast to the right of the blocked passage, and an open doorway to the northwest. Behind the iron door is a cave-in with two burial urns among the rubble.

To the northwest is a short passage leading to a split-level room, with a leveled draugr on a walkway opposite from where you enter. There are wooden stairs leading down in front of you to the lower level of the room. At the bottom of the room is another leveled draugr patrolling in and out of a passage to the southwest. Staying on the upper level and following it around to the right leads to the walkway the first draugr stands on. This crosses first to a pillar, then originally to a balcony on the far side of the room, but part of the walkway has collapsed; the balcony can easily be reached by jumping, however. On the balcony is a pedestal with a leveled [poison](Skyrim_Poison.md) on a [weight sensitive plate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Weight_Sensitive_Plate) that triggers [spears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spear_Trap) to shoot from above and in front of you; to avoid damage, stand to the side, use [Telekinesis](Skyrim_Telekinesis.md), or knock it off with a ranged spell or [weapon](Skyrim_Weapons.md). If the poison is accidentally knocked off, it can be difficult to find, due to the lower level being filled with vegetation, but there is nothing else of interest in the lower level.

Through the doorway to the southwest is a wide flight of stairs with a magic-using draugr patrolling backwards and forwards along the passage at the top. This passage takes a couple turns before climbing a flight of stairs up to a room behind one of the sets of spears blocking access in the first room of this area. There is a lever opposite, between two sets of spears. The lever lowers both sets here and the other set on the far side of the room. Behind the set on the left is a novice-locked chest, while the second set gives access back to the first room, and the third set back in the first room gives access to the passage to the northeast. On your right when passing back into the first room of this area is a large urn.

Beyond the spears to the northeast are three burial urns by a flight of stairs leading down. The passage turns back on itself, then turns again before opening into a wider passage. On your left is a cave-in, while to the right is an urn and a set of shelves, holding only clutter. Once past the shelves, the passage turns again, and you'll encounter a draugr patrolling the next section. By the corner is a pressure plate that triggers a [battering ram](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Battering_Ram), which can be used to your advantage. At the far end, the passage staggers to the right, where a leveled draugr is lurking just out of sight. This draugr is normally alerted to fighting the previous draugr.

At the end is another cave-in and a doorway into a large chamber with two levels of walkways above. On the lower of these is a draugr magic-user. There are two upright sarcophagi against the left-hand wall. Upon entering, both sarcophagi will burst open and leveled draugr will emerge. There is a sideboard between the sarcophagi holding two random [potions](Skyrim_Potions.md) of [healing](Skyrim_Fortify_Health.md), [magicka](Skyrim_Fortify_Magicka.md), or [stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Stamina), another random potion, and a dagger. In the southeastern corner is a burial urn, an urn, and an unlocked chest, while against the northeast wall, wooden stairs lead up to a balcony and the first walkway. This crosses the room before a series of stairs leads up to the higher walkway. This leads across to a room containing a set of shelves holding a random [helmet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Helmet), a large coin purse, and two random potions of healing, magicka, or stamina. There are also three burial urns in the room, and stairs descending to the southeast.

At the bottom is a wide passage with several opened sarcophagi lining the walls patrolled by a leveled draugr. At the other end, around the corner to the right, are several statues with a random two-handed weapon, a medium coin purse, a [ruby](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ruby), and nineteen loose septims in front of them. To the left of the statues are stairs leading up to the southwest. At the top by a lit brazier is one last burial urn, and on the left is an iron door.

The door leads back to *Angarvunde*, similar to the end of the Catacombs zone, to the room behind the spears. The room contains a broken sideboard with another two random potions of healing, magicka, or stamina, and two burial urns. Continuing into the room, you will find a large urn, an unlocked chest, and the lever that lowers the two sets of spears granting access back into the main chamber and raises one of the gates to the southwest.

## Notes
- This location is marked as cleared when the related quest is completed. Clearing the location is not triggered by killing its boss enemy, unlike most other dungeons, but rather by discovering the word wall. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.2, addresses this issue. It adds a draugr boss near the word wall with the scripts to mark the location as cleared.
- You can also kill Medresi as soon as you enter, which will still allow you to progress the miscellaneous objective.
- A room in *Angarvunde Catacombs* contains four giant canis roots. Most canis roots are roughly one foot tall, while these four are the size of trees and taller than you.
- There is a [silver ore vein](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Ore_Vein) southeast of the camp.
- There is a small storage area with a [strongbox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Strongbox), [lockpicks](Skyrim_Lockpick.md), and potions farther up the mountain south-southeast of the camp.
- The Prima guide identifies Angarvunde as a temple.
- A [dragon mound of the same name](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Angarvunde_Mound) appears in [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online).

## Bugs
- Clearing this location does not increment the number of "Dungeons Cleared" displayed in your journal, and therefore does not contribute to the [Delver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Delver) achievement. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.2, fixes this bug.
- If you pickpocket the key from Medresi instead of talking to her, she can get stuck: you can open the gates, but Medresi will refuse to go through them. In this case, you must kill her to progress. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.1, fixes this bug.
- When chasing Medresi into the last room, the floor trap might open, immediately close again, and then remain closed, so the final room with the word wall cannot be accessed. - If you retreat to the main chamber and reenter the final room a second time, the floor trap may open for you.
- The room boundings for Angarvunde near the word wall may be slightly too small to prevent portions of that part of the dungeon from disappearing from the screen. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.2.3, fixes this bug.
- The barrel and camp may respawn despite the dungeon being classed as a "No Reset Zone". <sup>**?**</sup>
- In *Angarvunde Catacombs*, past the canis root and when you get to the resting draugr, the sound effect for the stone steps in the area seems to be the sound effect for wooden steps. <sup>**?**</sup>
