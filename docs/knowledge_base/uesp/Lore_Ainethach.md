Ainethach was a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) landowner. He owned [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md) and its two mines, Sanuarach Mine and Fenn's Gulch Mine, during the early years of the third century of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AINE-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LGD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mena-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/SR-quest-Sanuarach_Mine_%28quest%29.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Sanuarach_Mine_%28quest%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Sanuarach_Mine_(quest).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Sanuarach_Mine_(quest).jpg) Ainethach confronting mercenaries of the Silver-Blood family Ainethach's family owned Karthwasten and its two mines, Sanuarach Mine and Fenn's Gulch Mine, for generations. As of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Ainethach was one of the last remaining Reachfolk landowners. He had issues with both the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and the Reachman [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md), as the Nords believed he was working for the Forsworn, while the Forsworn thought he was working for the Nords.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AINE-1)</sup> However, some natives and Nords held him in high regard.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ragnar-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Enmon-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mena-3)</sup> He employed Reachmen, Nords, and [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LGD-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Belchimac-6)</sup> His right-hand man was a Nord named Ragnar who helped him supervise the mines.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ragnar-4)</sup>

In 4E 201, Karthwasten was targeted by the Silver-Blood family of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), who wanted to acquire Sanuarach Mine from him. After the Forsworn attacked the mines, the Silver-Blood mercenaries arrived to clean things up, but the sellswords remained in the area and halted the mining operations. The Silver-Bloods wanted the land as a form of payment for their services.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ATAR-7)</sup> The arrival of the mercenaries was not seen as an altruistic action and was considered another ploy to force natives to sell their land.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AINE-1)</sup> Whether or not Ainethach decided to sell Sanuarach Mine to the Silver-Bloods is unknown, but it remained active regardless.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SM-8)</sup>

Karthwasten was also attacked by a group of Forsworn who kidnapped the future Sybil of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), [Fjotra](Lore_Fjotra.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Enmon-5)</sup> An [emissary](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) of the [House of Dibella](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md) sought information about the missing girl, and Ainethach directed them to Fjotra's father, Enmon.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AINE-1)</sup> Fjotra was eventually found and rescued.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-THOD-9)</sup>

He fathered two children, Voada and Rondach. As of 4E 201, they were adults, lived in Markarth, and worked in [Understone Keep](Lore_Understone_Keep.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Rondach-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Voada-11)</sup>
