[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4d/ON-place-Ashalmawia.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ashalmawia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ashalmawia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ashalmawia.jpg) Ashalmawia ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/MW-place-Ashalmawia.jpg/200px-MW-place-Ashalmawia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ashalmawia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ashalmawia.jpg) Ashalmawia (Morrowind) Ashalmawia is a ruined [daedric temple](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) found on the seaside hills of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Situated on the bleak wealds of the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md), it is an old temple dedicated to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Domination, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), one of the Four Corners of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md). The Sunken Vaults of the temple have been used by the Prince's countless worshippers, including the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) to bring about his wrath and influence.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSavile-1)</sup>

## History
During High King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md)'s reign in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), a representative from Ashalmawia traveled to the vast temple of [Assurnabitashpi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Assurnabitashpi), in light of [Mordrin Hanin](Lore_Mordrin_Hanin.md)'s passing. On the ninth day of mourning, the cup of passage was mixed with a lethal concoction and passed to every guest who attended. The drink killed most of the attendees and provided Hanin with companions for his trip to the next world. It is unknown if the diplomat from Ashalmawia was one of the victims.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HW-2)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) wreaked havoc throughout Tamriel and uprooted many different locations; however, Vvardenfell remained largely untouched because of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md)'s protection. However, the Worm Cult managed to establish a conclave in Ashalmawia's sunken vaults, and it soon became infested with several daedra.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSavile-1)</sup> To deal with this threat, the [Temple Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) sent an [Outlander](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to disrupt their rituals. The outlander stopped several rituals, which summoned all sorts of enemies, such as a [Harvester](Lore_Harvester.md) and a [Watcher](Lore_Watcher.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONDaedric-3)</sup>

At around the same time, Mistress [Dratha](Lore_Dratha.md) sent an outlander to retrieve seven of the Stones of Cold Fire. One of them was located deep in Ashalmawia and it was guarded by the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) soldier, Zylara.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCost-4)</sup> Numani-Rasi of the [Urshilaku Tribe](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md) sent various warriors to recover historical artifacts from several daedric ruins, one of these sites was the temple, Ashalmawia.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONRelics-5)</sup>

In the late Third Era, Ashalmawia was frequently occupied by rogue magic users who often harassed nearby locals. The [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) rooted out a witch's coven from the temple, but one witch named Thelsa Dral escaped and hid in the nearby Asha-Ahhe Eggmine.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWKaye-6)</sup> During the [Vvardenfell Crisis](Lore_Vvardenfell_Crisis.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Molag Bal cultists took over Ashalmawia. They were led by the battlemage, Gordol, who was targeted by [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) for terrorizing nearby [Ald Velothi](Lore_Ald_Velothi.md). He was promptly dealt with by a Redoran [House-Brother](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWGordol-7)</sup> At around the same time, the [Twin Lamps](Lore_Twin_Lamps.md) sent one of their own to rescue a runaway slave that hid in Ashalmawia, with several slavers on her tail. The slave was saved and escorted out of Vvardenfell from a ferry in Ald Velothi.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWLamps-8)</sup>
