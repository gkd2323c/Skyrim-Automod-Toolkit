# Boots of the Apostle

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/MW-item-Boots_of_the_Apostle.jpg/200px-MW-item-Boots_of_the_Apostle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Boots_of_the_Apostle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Boots_of_the_Apostle.jpg) The Boots of the Apostle (Morrowind) The Boots of the Apostle are very mysterious. The wearer of the boots is rumored to be able to levitate, though sightings of them in use have been extremely rare.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> The [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) supposedly gave [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) the boots after he had learned their secrets from them, as a reward for his "cunning craft and riddling". They are now considered relics of Tiber Septim and are legendary treasures of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-2)</sup> **

## History
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWYB-3)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-4)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Boots of the Apostle among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TA-5)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the boots and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWFR-6)</sup>

After learning from the Greybeards, Tiber Septim (known back then as Talos Stormcrown) is said to have descended from the mountain wearing the boots, while "riding the clouds, striding through the air". After his reign as the [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) ended, the boots were lost for many years.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-2)</sup>

The boots would later come into the possession of a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) hero, who perished with them in the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) stronghold of Berandas. Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) reportedly recovered the artifact from the hero's corpse on behalf of the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) from the stronghold. They were guarded by a pair of [Winged Twilights](Lore_Winged_Twilight.md), who had also kidnapped a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) healer named Ama Nin, who may have been the manifestation of the goddess [Mara](Lore_Mara.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BOTA-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-2)</sup> The boots were allowed to be kept by the Nerevarine to use them in service of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-2)</sup> but they sold them to the [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) Museum of Artifacts later that year.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
