[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/MW-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg/200px-MW-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg) The ruins at Ald Sotha circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Ald Sotha (meaning "Old Sotha" in Dark Elvish) was an ancient, ruined settlement located northeast of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), in the [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). In the time of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md), prior to the formation of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), Ald Sotha was originally a town with many towers that was home to [House Sotha](Lore_House_Sotha.md). It was later destroyed by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), with [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) saving [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) from Dagon's armies, leaving him as its only survivor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Saryoni-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arox-2)</sup> By the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Ald Sotha had become a temple to Mehrunes Dagon, though it was visited by members of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) during the Pilgrimages of the Four Corners.

[Roland's Tear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Roland%27s_Tear), a rare variation of the common [gold kanet](Lore_Gold_Kanet.md) flower, is known to grow in the ruins. It was sacred to the Tribunal,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DS-3)</sup> and is prized by alchemists for its wondrous powers when used in potion making.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/ON-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald_Sotha.jpg) Ald Sotha circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Originally, Ald Sotha was the seat of [House Sotha](Lore_House_Sotha.md), a minor house of the settled [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). It was the birthplace of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), who would later achieve godhood through the power of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) and become a member of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), as well as his sister [Sotha Nall](Lore_Sotha_Nall.md). The town of was recounted as a tranquil place, with a fish pond, flower garden with many birds, artisanal kitchens, and many sunny days with activities involving pottery wheels, catching torchbugs in jars, and eating scrib jelly on rainy days.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAmbient-5)</sup> The town was destroyed by the armies of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) early in the First Era; Sotha Sil was the sole survivor, having been saved by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Saryoni-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ABHo AS-6)</sup> The attack occurred at some point after he and his sister snuck out of their home on a rainy morning. His family woke up to find their homes and tapestries already burning, and screamed in agony in their final moments.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAmbient-5)</sup> [Arox the Mutilator](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arox) was present at the attack and recounted his talons rending flesh, the screams of the damned, and the towers of Ald Sotha tumbling down.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arox-2)</sup>

Prior to its destruction, sometime during the reign of [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), a representative from Ald Sotha traveled to the vast temple of [Assurnabitashpi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Assurnabitashpi) in light of the death of [Mordrin Hanin](Lore_Mordrin_Hanin.md). On the ninth day of mourning, everyone drank from the cup of passage, and a great many of them were killed by the deadly concoction, supplying Hanin with companions to the next world. It is unknown if the Ald Sotha diplomat was among these perished souls.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HW-7)</sup>

A shrine to Mehrunes Dagon was later built at the location, likely in remembrance of the Prince's destruction of the original city. The shrine fell into ruin, although it continued to be populated by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). The [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) occasionally made pilgrimages to Dagon's shrine deep within the ruins,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Saryoni-1)</sup> and the [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) were charged with keeping away Daedra worshippers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

Mehrunes Dagon's sentiments towards Sotha Sil's threats for not upholding the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md) were of indifference, with Ald Sotha used a reference for how easily he can destroy things that he pleases.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONTWMD-9)</sup> After Dagon violated the pact by attacking [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), the Clockwork God was finally able to enact revenge for Ald Sotha, and banished the oathbreaker with the aid of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) to the Deadlands.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TTIS8-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-2920v11-11)</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) had taken over the ruins, and it became the headquarters of the Brotherhood in Vvardenfell. This attempt at expansion was viciously repelled by the native [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), the ancient enemies of the Brotherhood. The Tong infiltrated the ruins and executed the local [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md), [Severa Magia](Lore_Severa_Magia.md), which helped to put an end to the Dark Brotherhood's activities on Vvardenfell.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The Tong also liberated several [Threads of the Webspinner](Lore_Threads_of_the_Webspinner.md) from the ruins, artifacts created by [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md) which rightfully belonged to their patron, [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The Tribunal Temple also made a pilgrimage to Dagon's shrine that year, and recovered the [shoes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shoes_of_St._Rilms) of [St. Rilms](Lore_Rilms.md) from the ruins.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

The ruins were likely destroyed in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), when [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) crashed into Vivec City. This impact devastated the surrounding area, creating [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md), a large crater filled with boiling water from the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
