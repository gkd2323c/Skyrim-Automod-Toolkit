Darloc Brae was a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) warlord active during the fifth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTR-2)</sup> He was known as the "**Golden Beast of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md)**",<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-3)</sup> also shortened to the "**Golden Beast**"<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM73-4)</sup> and the "**Beast of Anequina**".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTR-2)</sup> His conquests spanned across the savannahs and badlands between the [Larsius River](Lore_Larsius_River.md) and the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOLG-5)</sup> Anequina's martial traditions began with Darloc Brae's reign.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM73-4)</sup> Despite his worship of the Fire-Cat, [Merrunz](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), he made great efforts to preserve [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s ancient history.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLDM-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-3)</sup>

## Biography
Darloc Brae was born in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when Elsweyr was ruled by a single [Moon-Emperor](Lore_Moon-Emperor.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aa PAI-7)</sup> The Moon-Emperor gave rise to Darloc Brae in [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aa PAI-7)</sup> In 1E 461, he attended the coronation of Emperor [Gorieus](Lore_Gorieus.md) of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), along with other important contemporary figures such as [Dumac Dwarfking](Lore_Dumac.md) and Lord [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Indoril_Nerevar.md), both of [Resdayn](Lore_Morrowind.md), and High King [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md) of the Kingdom of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTR-2)</sup> Darloc Brae worshipped the Fire-Cat, Merrunz, the Khajiiti aspect of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLDM-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOLN-8)</sup>

By 1E 461, Darloc Brae had begun the [Anequine Conquests](Lore_Anequine_Conquests.md), during which he conquered all of Anequina, parts of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), and parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTR-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aa PAI-7)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOLG-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESODWC-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TGt L-10)</sup> The stronghold of Khaj Rawlith and the Do'Krin Monastery, located near the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md) and [Crescent River](Lore_Crescent_River.md), marked the westernmost points of his domain, and [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) and the [Larsius River](Lore_Larsius_River.md) marked the eastern border.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOLG-5)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESODKM-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOM-12)</sup> Darloc Brae conquered [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) during the conquests, and was under Khajiiti control for more than twenty years as a result.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TGt L-10)</sup>

Darloc Brae's reign is said to have been glorious,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOLKD-13)</sup> but accounts describe life under him as brutal and terrible.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM73-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLDM-6)</sup> He excavated mines such as Darkpool Mine,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOLDM-6)</sup> and erected temples and strongholds.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOLG-5)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESODKM-11)</sup> His crowning architectural achievement was the Do'Krin Monastery in the region of [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESODKM-11)</sup> Places such as [Desert Wind Adeptorium](Lore_Desert_Wind_Adeptorium.md) were built as a refuge for those fleeing the conquests of the Golden Beast.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESODWC-9)</sup>

One of Darloc Brae's most important contributions to the culture of Elsweyr were his efforts to preserve the province's history from the period of the sixteen kingdoms.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-3)</sup> In [1E 463](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_463), cultists of Merrunz burned the Grand Archive of [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md), which contained most of the province's records from the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-3)</sup> By the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Khajiiti history from the Merethic Era was murky at best, but Darloc Brae's efforts to preserve the remaining records by placing them in vaults and smaller archives across Elsweyr allowed the early history of Elsweyr and the Khajiit to not be completely lost.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-3)</sup>

It is unknown when the Golden Beast died, but many of his soldiers were buried in the Rimmen Necropolis.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOLN-8)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an aspect of Darloc Brae reanimated his ancient forces in the Rimmen Necropolis and dueled with the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) forces of a champion of Mehrunnez (Merrunz).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Ot Tot FM-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESORNGE-15)</sup> Both sides were eventually defeated by [wandering travelers](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESORNGE-15)</sup>

### Legacy
While Darloc Brae's reign is notorious, his endeavors had a great and long-lasting influence on Elsweyr. The martial traditions of Anequina are said to have begun with Darloc Brae, as are the Anequine styles of arms and armor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM73-4)</sup> His preservation of the Khajiiti records from the Merethic Era allowed historians to gain insight into the period of the sixteen kingdoms, such as the rise and fall of the Hunt-Lord's aristocracy.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-3)</sup> His angry and feral demeanor was replicated in the beast masks that are used in the Khajiiti Havoc Dance.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DBBM-16)</sup> His eyes were replicated for those with a fiery spirit and peerless martial skills comparable to those of the Golden Beast of Anequina.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DGEs-17)</sup> He is remembered as a mighty conqueror and some Khajiit hold him in esteem.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aa PAI-7)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Ot Tot FM-14)</sup> Circa 2E 582, artifacts dating back to the Anequine Conquests were considered valuable across the [Tenmar Forest](Lore_Tenmar_Forest.md), between [Torval](Lore_Torval.md) and [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOLKD-13)</sup>
