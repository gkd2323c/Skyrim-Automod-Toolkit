Tullius was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) general of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and the Military Governor of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Tullius was originally from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), but Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) sent him to Skyrim to serve as the province's military governor following the killing of High King [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) and the subsequent outbreak of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Tullius was sent to Skyrim to suppress the rebellion and restore the peace.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup> As military governor, Tullius commanded all legions stationed in Skyrim from Castle Dour in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), and the [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) Legate [Rikke](Lore_Rikke.md) served as his second-in-command.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Caesennius-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-THo S-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:420</sup>

Although [Elisif the Fair](Lore_Elisif_the_Fair.md) was the Jarl of Solitude during his tenure as military governor, many regarded Tullius as the real power in the city.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:421</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Odar-5)</sup> Tullius was a taciturn and serious man who had little interest in or understanding of Nordic culture, viewing much of it as nonsense, but he eventually came to respect it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup>

Many supporters of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) regarded Tullius as their best hope for victory.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-rikke-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Hadvar-7)</sup> Tullius is credited with turning things around for the Empire in its efforts to suppress the Stormcloak Rebellion.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Hadvar-7)</sup> However, Tullius' efforts to quell the rebellion were hindered by the Emperor's unwillingness to send legionaries to Skyrim, as doing so would weaken Cyrodiil's defenses.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup> As a result, the Imperial Legion in Skyrim received very few reinforcements and was forced to recruit locally.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Caesennius-2)</sup> On 15 Last Seed, 4E 201, Titus II sent a letter to Tullius, requesting that several Imperial relics be placed back into the service of the Empire and ordering him to recover the [Lord's Mail](Lore_Lord%27s_Mail.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Lt GT-8)</sup>

Although the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) forced Tullius to tolerate the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), he personally despised them, even remarking that they almost made him want to join the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md). Tullius believed the Thalmor stirred up trouble in Skyrim to force the Empire to waste precious resources and soldiers on quelling the Stormcloak Rebellion. He also believed the rebellion was a mere interlude to a renewed war with the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup>

After the beginning of the war, the Imperial Legion made several attempts to capture [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), but he managed to repeatedly elude them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Hadvar-7)</sup> To finally capture Ulfric, Tullius prepared an ambush outside [Darkwater Crossing](Lore_Darkwater_Crossing.md) in southern [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md). On 15 Last Seed, 4E 201, Ulfric and a small group of his men walked into the legion's ambush and surrendered without much of a fight. After capturing Ulfric, he was gagged to prevent him from using the thu'um and carted with other prisoners for two days until they arrived in [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md) for execution, stopping short of their planned destination, Cyrodiil, on Tullius' orders.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Hadvar-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ralof-9)</sup> [Elenwen](Lore_Elenwen.md), the Thalmor Ambassador to Skyrim, attempted to intervene and prevent Ulfric's execution, as the Thalmor wanted the civil war to continue,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDUS-10)</sup> but Tullius dismissed her, and Ulfric was lined up for the chopping block.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-unbound-11)</sup>

Before Ulfric could be executed, the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) attacked Helgen, prompting Tullius to order the guards to get the townspeople to safety.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-unbound-11)</sup> Tullius then had the imperial battlemages attack Alduin, but he quickly ordered the remaining imperial troops to retreat to Helgen's keep.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-unbound-11)</sup> In the chaos, Ulfric was able to escape and return to [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) to continue his rebellion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:552-553</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Delphine-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Orgnar-13)</sup> Tullius managed to survive Alduin's attack and make his way back to Solitude.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup>

Rikke eventually persuaded Tullius to dispatch legionaries to recover the [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md) to prevent Ulfric from using it to legitimize his claim to the High Kingship of Skyrim.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-rikke-6)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRTJC-14)</sup> However, it is unknown if the legion was successful in recovering the Jagged Crown from the ruins of [Korvanjund](Lore_Korvanjund.md). During the rebellion, Tullius made numerous requests to [Balgruuf the Greater](Lore_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md), the neutral Jarl of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md), to garrison imperial soldiers in his city, but the Jarl repeatedly denied these requests.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tullius-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-rikke-6)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRJBt Gd-15)</sup> Eventually, Balgruuf had to end his neutrality after Ulfric issued him an ultimatum that forced him to pick a side in the war. Balgruuf, after consulting the members of his court, decided to side with the Empire, reluctantly allowing General Tullius to garrison troops in his city. Shortly after Balgruuf's decision, the Stormcloaks arrived, initiating the Battle for Whiterun.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-message-16)</sup> The outcome of the battle is unknown. At some point, Tullius sent one of his agents named Margret to [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) to investigate the powerful Silver-Blood family.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Margret-17)</sup>
