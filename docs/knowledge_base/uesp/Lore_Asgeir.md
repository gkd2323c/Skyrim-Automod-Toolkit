*It was in this state of carousing that they were approached by a young and breathless messenger of their sister crew from the Kaal Kaaz. The boy (Asgeir, as his name is now sung) had ran unimaginable distance at breakneck speed from the blood-stained fields of the Clouded Sun, to deliver the news to all who would hear. When he reached their camp, he bellowed a great sob before relieving his heart with the news that the mighty Ysgramor had breathed his last.*
— *[Songs of the Return, Vol 56](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return,_Vol_56.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR56-1)</sup> Asgeir was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior and a member of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). In his youth, he served as a messenger, and his name was eventually immortalized in legend and song.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR56-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/SR-place-Saarthal.jpg/200px-SR-place-Saarthal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Saarthal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Saarthal.jpg) Saarthal [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) Ysgramor Little is known about Asgeir's life beyond what is recorded in the *Songs of the Return*.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR56-1)</sup> As one of the original Five Hundred Companions, he hailed from Atmora, swore the Oath of the Companions, and answered [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s call to venture south.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOTR7-2)</sup>

He was a member of the crew of the Kaal Kaaz. Following the retaking of Saarthal, he ran from the blood-stained fields of the Clouded Sun to the camp of the crew of the Chrion, led by Captain Rhorlak. At the time he was still very young, referred to as a boy in the *Songs of the Return*. Arriving breathless after covering an immense distance, he delivered the news that Ysgramor had died. After announcing the harbinger's death, Asgeir continued his run to inform other Companion crews scattered across the land, leaving the crew of the Chrion in deep mourning.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR56-1)</sup>

After the final battle against the [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) at the barren pass, the crew of the Kaal Kaaz,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SOTR19-3)</sup> which included Asgeir,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR56-1)</sup> as well as the crews of the Sadon Reyth and the Ylgermet, parted ways and never again joined shields.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SOTR19-3)</sup>

He was remembered for his deeds, and the name Asgeir came to be sung in songs and legends.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOTR56-1)</sup> [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) men continued to bear the name Asgeir as late as [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), though it is uncertain whether this was in honor of the legendary Asgeir himself.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Asgeir-4)</sup>
