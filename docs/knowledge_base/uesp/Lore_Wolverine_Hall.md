[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/MW-place-Wolverine_Hall.jpg/200px-MW-place-Wolverine_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Wolverine_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Wolverine_Hall.jpg) The entrance to Wolverine Hall Wolverine Hall is a settlement situated just south of [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md). It was an attempt on the part of the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) to project power in a region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) where they were relatively powerless. The [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) tolerated the presence of the fort mostly because nobody cared enough to knock it down. Wolverine Hall housed the services not permitted in the town itself, such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) shrine. These were concealed within the fort itself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTSM-1)</sup> The Mages Guild and Fighters Guild chapter in Sadrith Mora was established in Wolverine Hall under lease from the Telvanni Council.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MGC-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FGC-3)</sup>

Between the Hall and the town itself was Dirty Muriel's Cornerclub, a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) -style building that was home to the local [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DMC-4)</sup> While they typically didn't engage in business with the Mages Guild, the thieves of the Cornerclub occasionally collaborated with the mages of Wolverine Hall. They would request items like recipes for a potion of dispel magic or hire a wizard for protection against the Camonna Tong thugs at their guildhouse.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BH-5)</sup>
