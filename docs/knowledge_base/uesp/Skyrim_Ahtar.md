[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/SR-npc-Ahtar.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ahtar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ahtar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ahtar.jpg) Ahtar by his desk in the jail [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/SR-quest-Return_to_Grace.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Return_to_Grace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Return_to_Grace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Return_to_Grace.jpg) Roggvir's execution **Ahtar** is a [Redguard](Skyrim_Redguard.md) [warrior](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Combat Warrior2H) in [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md) where he works as public executioner and warden of the [Castle Dour Dungeon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Castle_Dour_Dungeon). He recently let [one of his prisoners](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Hargar) escape and will [task you](Skyrim_Kill_the_Bandit_Leader.md) with killing the escapee. In return, Ahtar will not only reward you, he will also become available as a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md).

When first entering Solitude, you will witness Ahtar carrying out his duties as executioner when he decapitates [Roggvir](Skyrim_Roggvir.md) while numerous citizens cheer. After the execution, Ahtar will return to his job in the depths of Castle Dour dungeon where he wanders around from 8am to 8pm. He spends the evening inside the [Winking Skeever](Skyrim_The_Winking_Skeever.md). When the clock strikes midnight he heads down to the [house](Skyrim_Jala%27s_House.md) of local merchant [Jala](Skyrim_Jala.md) for eight hours of sleep.

Ahtar is equipped with the rare [headsman's axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Headsman%27s_Axe) and wears the unobtainable [executioner's robes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Executioner%27s_Robes), [boots](Skyrim_Unobtainable_Items.md#000cf8b0), and [gloves](Skyrim_Unobtainable_Items.md#Gloves). He carries an [Orcish sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Sword), the [key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Jala%27s_House) to Jala's house, the [Castle Dour dungeon key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Castle_Dour_Dungeon_Key), a [belted tunic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Belted_Tunic), and a selection of [middle-class items and gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Common).

## Quest-Related Events

### Kill the Bandit Leader
If you speak to him directly, he will ask:

*"You wouldn't be a sellsword, would you? I have a little problem you could solve."* **What's this problem you have?** *"I may have accidentally let a prisoner escape. The leader of one of those bandit groups. I told everyone he died during questioning, and I need you to track him down and make sure he doesn't show up to disprove that."* **I'll do it.** *"Good. I'll have a sack of coin waiting for you when you get back."* **Forget it.** *"Suit yourself."* If spoken to again before the bandit is dead:

*"Remember. I need that bandit dead. Not alive."* When you return from [Broken Oar Grotto](Skyrim_Broken_Oar_Grotto.md) after killing Captain Hargar, he will be grateful and say:

*"That's a load off my mind. Here's your pay."*

## Notes
- Ahtar is marked internally as "courting" Jala, and the two are marked as allies (not actually lovers). He also sleeps in her house but the two will never acknowledge each other or speak of their relationship to you. Neither will anyone else in town. - Ahtar is also marked as a friend of [Legate Rikke](Skyrim_Legate_Rikke.md), another relationship that never gets adressed by anyone in-game.
- If you have already killed [Captain Hargar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Captain_Hargar), either during the quest [Lights Out!](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lights_Out!) from [Jaree-Ra](Skyrim_Jaree-Ra.md) or by clearing out the grotto without a specific quest, Ahtar will no longer give you the quest [Kill the Bandit Leader](Skyrim_Kill_the_Bandit_Leader.md), and as such, can not become a follower through these means. You will then have to increase his disposition in another way (such as through a radiant event) in order to have him become a follower.

## Bugs
- Ahtar's AI schedule gets broken due to his eat package checking for Roggvir's execution still being active and because his jail marker was placed incorrectly. As a result, he'd be stuck inside Jala's house unable to exit once the execution was over and he went home for the first time. This could result in you being unable to talk to him to finish the 'Kill the Bandit Leader' quest. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.6, fixes this bug.

- If you break into Jala's house while he is supposed to be there (i.e. at night) and then leave, this sometimes fixes his AI and allows him to leave the house normally. You may need to be detected by him and have him order you to leave the house. Also, this fix may sometimes be temporary.

- During his daily routine, headsman Ahtar was supposed to engage in conversation with the imprisoned [Bjartur](Skyrim_Bjartur.md). However, the two conversations will never be heard due to several developer oversights: - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.4, fixes this bug.
