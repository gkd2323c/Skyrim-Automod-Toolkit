## *[Introduction](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Introduction)*
Submitted for consideration, care of the esteemed [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) and their august review board.

The works included herein represent the latest in my ongoing series attempting to catalog the cultural contributions of the many and varied peoples of the [continent](Lore_Tamriel.md). My treatise, "We Are All [Earth Bones](Lore_Earth_Bones.md) Under the Skin" is in consideration for the Gwylim prize, and I hope you find these works stand as worthy corollaries to that body of evidence.

Each volume will consider a beast. And not just any beast, but the beasts (large or small, thick or thin) that are utilized as riding mounts. I leave no subject untouched: from the origins of the common [horse](Lore_Horse.md), to the [bizarre bugs](Lore_Nix-Ox.md) of [Vardenfell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vardenfell), to the rare [constructs](Lore_Construct.md) left behind by the [Dwarves](Lore_Dwemer.md). I appreciate your time and attention, and hope that these volumes are as entertaining to read as they were to compile.

May the [Father of Equations](Lore_Julianos.md) keep you safe in these trying times,

Fadus Falto of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md)

## *[Volume 1, Horses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_1,_Horses)*
Common? Perhaps. Ubiquitous, yes. But boring? No, I say to you. The [horse](Lore_Horse.md) is everywhere, used in a myriad of applications and by essentially every culture on the [continent](Lore_Tamriel.md). From sturdy riding mounts to draft horses, these beasts fill the streets of our towns and cities so thick as to pass without notice. But why? Why is the horse so common?

The answer lies, as ever, in our [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) past. When the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) arrived on mainland Tamriel from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) (a subject I discuss at length in my treatise), they brought with them a sturdy stock of horses already well integrated into their culture. These shaggy beasts were well adapted to the cold climes of that distant continent, but it's worth noting that careful study has revealed there was already an indigenous population of horses on the mainland as well. Undomesticated, totally, by the Elvish cultures that lived across its length and breadth. It was not until well after the Nedes spread across the continent that we see evidence of horse husbandry in [Elvish](Lore_Mer.md) settlements.

What seems, at first glance, like a strange gap in Elvish ingenuity is in fact a function of well-developed cultural structures. Elves domesticated beasts like the [hippogriff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hippogriff), the [elk](Lore_Elk.md), and the esoteric [indrik](Lore_Indrik.md) so far back in their distant past that they themselves have only a dim understanding of when those creatures became companion animals to their people. As well, we must consider the widespread acceptance of [magical study](Lore_Magic.md) at almost every level of [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) society. Alongside the benefits to taming whatever creature catches your cultural fancy, the ability to move earth and stone to construct buildings with the force of your will makes the common draft horse seem somewhat tame in comparison.

Whereas the peoples coming from the frozen north looked upon their shaggy steeds and saw salvation. Warmth from the cold, [milk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Milk) for the young, skins for armoring their hides, and of course sturdy transportation and cartage across vast distances. The shaggy steeds from the north and the indigenous horses of the mainland were wholly compatible from a breeder's perspective (a fascinating note worth exploring in a volume all its own), and we see evidence of form and function diversification in many settlements as the newly arrived people moved across the continent.

Elvish settlements, especially those without ready access to the resources of major cities, quickly adopted these beasts as "work horses." And by the time of the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) [diaspora](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) horse usage across mainland Tamriel was well established.

## *[Volume 2, Wolves and Foxes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_2,_Wolves_and_Foxes)*
The perspective of the city dweller holds that the [dog](Lore_Dog.md) is a loyal pet, a faithful companion sitting by the fire. Their wild relatives, meanwhile, are [chicken](Lore_Chicken.md) -eating nuisances. Perhaps even blood-thirsty beasts, if the winter gets long and cruel.

But to the resilient folk who live beyond city walls and travel long distances through the rugged frontier of the north, the largest dogs, [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md), and [foxes](Lore_Fox.md) make for boon companions and sturdy mounts. Is there any wonder? These beasts make for sources of warmth in even the cruelest blizzard, fiercely loyal protectors, and impressive hunters when food is scarce. I once met a mount breeder from [Bleackrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md) who proudly showed me his beautiful hounds, carefully tended over generations till they reached the size of a [Wood Elf](Lore_Wood_Elf.md)!

I had the pleasure of riding one across the icy beaches of their home isle and it was a moving, if freezing, experience!

## *[Volume 3, Nix-Oxen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_3,_Nix-Oxen)*
I sat conversing with the dynamic and thoughtful Kylia Thando at the Abbey of Saint Delyn, an inn in the stony heart of [Vivic City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), as she related to me the fascinating history of the [nix-ox](Lore_Nix-Ox.md) and its domestication. [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and the [ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) are a cauldron of unique creatures of all kinds, and this enormous insect plays a myriad of roles across the region: caravan beast of burden, plow-pulling farm laborer, and of course sturdy riding mount.

The history of the [Dark Elf](Lore_Dark_Elf.md) [houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) hold that these creatures did not exist prior to the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)'s arrival from distant shores. Or at least, they did not exist at the prodigious size and strength they do now. The volcanic lands in which that culture found itself following revered [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) to this new part of the world was lacking in all the beasts and birds and comforts they had known in the idyllic [Summerset isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Myth holds that the nix-oxen were a gift to the Prophet from [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) herself. The remaking of a tiny insect into a creature capable of carrying her favored daughters and sons into the future she saw for them all.

## *[Volume 4, Big Cats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_4,_Big_Cats)*
I cannot stand [cats](Lore_Cat.md). Not [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), mind you. I have many friends in academia throughout [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). But actual cats, from Big-Eared Mousers to the great [Senche-Lions](Lore_Senche-Lion.md) prowling the Savannah *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* of [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md). Their silent stares are judgmental, their demeanor arrogant. Even their languorous yawns imply a certain boredom with life that I find distasteful.

Imagine then, what it was like for me to ride firsthand one of the great tamed beasts from the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) hills and find the experience emotionally moving. The nimble path the beast picked out as we scaled a hill, its powerful muscles moving beneath its beautiful coat. I would be a poor researcher indeed if I did not admit that I can be mistaken, that new experiences cannot shift my thinking.

Perhaps the brave souls that husband these great beasts know what they are about. I still wholeheartedly believe every cat I meet is silently planning how to make a meal of me.

## *[Volume 5, Kagouti and Guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_5,_Kagouti_and_Guar)*
I had the pleasure of discussing the stewardship of the strange bipedal creatures of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) at a lavishly appointed cornerclub near [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md). My host, a well-spoken [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) magister, took great pleasure in discussing the many and varied ways these lizard-beasts are husbanded across [the region](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md). She personally prefers the sturdy [kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md) over the more prosaic work-a-day [guar](Lore_Guar.md). More recently domesticated, more future potential, even potentially magically more potent as some recent experiments have proven.

I demurred from offering an opinion, a visitor to these lands, happy to sip my Kaveh and listen rather than speak. I find the bipedal nature of the kagouti and the guar their most fascinating attributes, and when my host wound down and I was able to ask after a few details I found her responses oddly cold and evasive. Where as just moments ago she'd been excited, even overjoyed to share her thoughts, asking after this fundamental descriptor caused her to grow distant.

The only thing I could get from her is that the magically adaptable nature of the guar and kagouti is, to use her words, "not an accident." I've asked for access the esteemed [Magister Varkenel](Lore_Varkenel.md)'s notes on the development of the [Vvardvark](Lore_Vvardvark.md). Purely for academic reasons, you understand! But I was soundly rejected based on "secrets vital to the House."

Make of that what you will.

## *[Volume 6, Camels](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_6,_Camels)*
I can report firsthand that the renowned musk of the [camel](Lore_Camel.md) is real. It is pungent, and it is fierce. I found myself in the wastes near [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset.md) for more than a week, adopted by an [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) caravan. These kind travelers taught me all they could about these famously ill-tempered beasts. Indeed, the creatures were not easy to approach at first. But the steady husbandry and the long relationships these people had with their mounts overcame any reservations I, or the camels, had about the situation.

I discovered, when I was able to sit astride a handsome [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) beast halfway through the week, that their loping gait is actually most agreeable once you set aside your expectations. In the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md), their thickly padded feet make short work of vast distances. Their reputation as ships of the desert, sailing along the sands, is well earned and poetically true.

I cannot stress enough, however, the need for quick reflexes around a camel that has yet to accept you. Whether it is their teeth or their spit, a surly camel will let you know how it feels in no uncertain terms.

## *[Volume 7, Durzogs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_7,_Durzogs)*
Allow me to set the scene. A group of academics seated around a tavern table, a nice bottle of tawny port open for all to enjoy. It's late and the subject turns, as it often does among such a group, to history's eccentricities. Inevitably some wit will propose, with mock seriousness, an origin for the [durzog](Lore_Durzog.md). The game then is to add to the proposed origin in a farcically grand fashion without contradicting its basic premise. Every notion I've heard from these drunken pedagogs, truthfully, are just as valid as the serious scholarly papers I've read about these bizarre creatures.

I frame this as a joke, because there is humor to be found here, but in truth I see this lack of clarity as a slow-motion tragedy. From the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) to the shores of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md), you can find durzogs happily living and hunting alongside [Goblinkin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Goblinkin). The durzog, in turn, represents a deep divide between the ancient cultures of the continent and [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s current stewards.

Researchers far more accomplished than I have tied [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) to some of the earliest inhabited sites on the continent. Possibly predating the earliest arrival of [Men](Lore_Man.md) from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) or [Elves](Lore_Mer.md) from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), and implying a once-much more grand and complex culture for this diminutive people. Consider the protean shape of the durzog. The complexity of their shape, and the perfection of their hunting form.

These beasts deserve more respect, academically as well as culturally. Their fangs and grim visage may hide a secret well worth exploring.

## *[Volume 8, Elk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_8,_Elk)*
I was fascinated, before beginning this survey, with the idea of sitting astride an [elk](Lore_Elk.md). These beautiful creatures are cultivated far from my home in [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md), and until I found my way to the azure waters and verdant shores of [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md) I had never ridden a cervine beast for any length of time. My [druid](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md) host, a large and enthusiastic fellow from Stonelore Grove, helped me to understand the art of elk-raising across the continent. The majority of these beasts are reared through one of two traditions: [druidic](Lore_Druids.md) or [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).

Druids, I'm told, find the creatures the perfect compliment to their magical tradition. Other mounts are skittish during druidcrafted rituals and the fundamental forces they gather in their spellcasting. Elk and their relatives, deeply tied to the wilds as they are, find that kind of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) no more upsetting than a late-summer rainstorm. The [Wood Elves](Lore_Wood_Elf.md) of Valenwood, meanwhile, have a long tradition of rearing elk and [deer](Lore_Deer.md) as mounts simply because of need and availability.

## *[Volume 9, Senche-raht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_9,_Senche-raht)*
I am no more qualified to speak on the culture and preferences of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) than I am to teach sailing to a [Sea Elf](Lore_Maormer.md) corsair. Luckily for this scholar I was able to reach out through the prolific [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) scribe Chirrzari to find an able guide to this most unique of mounts: the incredible [Senche-raht](Lore_Senche-raht.md). Saharrzag, a well-spoken Khajiit of great humor and considerable patience, asked that I start this entry with the simple reminder that these mighty felines are as intelligent as you or I. Unlike the beasts that populate the rest of this series, Khajiit that choose to carry riders into the fray do so of their own volition. If you haven't already, I encourage you to read Saharrzag's notable pamphlet, "[Not Just Mounts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-rahts__Not_Just_Mounts)."

Senche-raht have been battle steeds for their brethren since the earliest days of the [Sixteen Kingdoms](Lore_Sixteen_Kingdoms.md). Saharrzag recounted tales of revered cavalry battalions made up entirely of Senche-raht and their riding partners. These fearsome mounted armies would act as the spear-point for Khajiiti footsoldiers on the march, softening up resistance and scattering defenses prior to a massed assault. These groups were highly organized and subdivided into lodges of fraternal soldiers, each lodge organizing around their own specialized customs, rituals, and war oaths.

## *[Volume 10, Rams](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_10,_Rams)*
Of all the beasts I rode in research for this series, it was the stout [ram](Lore_Ram.md) that surprised me most of all. I have sat astride snarling wolves, mighty bears, even mouldering [constructs](Lore_Construct.md) from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s distant past. But the [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) river ram a kind [skald](Lore_Skald.md) allowed me to canter up and down a cobbled [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) street absolutely terrified me.

At first the mount was all gentle movements and sure steps. But halfway up the street it turned its head to regard me with one of those flat, strange eyes. Whatever it saw in my countenance it did not like, for it took off like an arrow into the heart of the bustling market. My companion was left far behind as the beast crashed through produce carts, seamstress stalls, and the [wares](Lore_Honey.md) of a beekeeper who hissed curses in the [tongue](Lore_Jel.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) as I left crushed wares and sticky glass in my wake.

Talking with my friend later that evening, wearing fresh clothes and lighter a tidy stack of [coins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coins) to repay for the damages, he related that this is apparently an all too common experience. Rams, unlike many other creatures in this study, are domesticated in only the loosest sense. So if you ever find yourself astride a well-behaved ram, consider yourself fortunate. You ride at their pleasure.

## *[Volume 11, Bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_11,_Bears)*
The mighty [bear](Lore_Bear.md) as a mount is a study in contrasts. [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [skalds](Lore_Skald.md) tell tales of bloodthirsty bears, rampaging through a camp in search of a scrap of meat. But when husbanded by a person with a stout heart and a fearless demeanor, the legendary protective nature of a mother bear can be turned to the aide *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* of a rider. Once bonded, these beast *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* make fearsome mounts, walking unflaggingly over even the most rugged terrain and rising to defense of their rider even in the face of the greatest of beasts.

An outrider who patrols the far flung reaches of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) swore to me in a tavern by the [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) trade road that his [snow bear](Lore_Snow_Bear.md) mount stood up to the likes of a [giant](Lore_Giant.md) that had wandered into his path. Said the great beast rose up on its hind legs, the rider clinging desperately to its back, and let loose a terrifying roar that sent the giant running for the hills. When the danger had past the beast, who he affectionately calls Snowpelt, nuzzled into him like a mother tending to her cub. Incredible!

## *[Volume 12, Constructs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_12,_Constructs)*
The remnants left behind by the long-departed [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) continue to delight and confound researchers to this day. Not a year passes where some aging [construct](Lore_Construct.md) is unearthed, gifting the wider academic community another tantalizing glimpse of their ancient brilliance. Meanwhile the study of constructs that act as mounts, "[vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md)" in the original language, is a tantalizingly active subject to research. While most of Dwarven culture moulders beneath the earth, there's a very active community of enthusiasts unearthing, revitalizing, even modifying constructs to new aesthetic and functional specifications.

Do not mistake me: the core concepts that drive Dwemer mechanisms remain understood only by the likes of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and a rare few. But while the core mechanisms underlying [horse](Lore_Horse.md), [senche](Lore_Senche-Cat.md), or [spider](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md) constructs remain untouchable the artifice surrounding them have seen a robust explosion in creativity in recent years.

To better understand this craft, I spoke by correspondence with the artificer known as "The Spidersmith." This prominent yet enigmatic figure in the secondary vamidium modification trade is well respected in elite arcanomagical research circles. He spoke at length of the joy they derive from seeking an understanding of these ancient mechanisms. Taking them apart, re-assembling them, even restoring long-rusted wrecks to functioning status. Their appreciation for Dwemercraft has earned them some heady commissions from individuals and organizations across the [continent](Lore_Tamriel.md).

He stated to me, exclusively for this text, that he's most proud of the work he's done with the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) of [Sotha Sil's City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). That such a group would allow an outsider to study and confer among their ranks is a rare thing indeed. Together he and several gifted artisans studied an Ebon Horse construct recently discovered in an ancient Dwemer holdfast, a collaboration he considers a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]*
