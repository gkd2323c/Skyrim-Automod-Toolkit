# Zenithar

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/ON-item-Zeal_of_Zenithar_Decoration.jpg/200px-ON-item-Zeal_of_Zenithar_Decoration.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Zeal_of_Zenithar_Decoration.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Zeal_of_Zenithar_Decoration.jpg) Religious decorations associated with Zenithar featuring the star sigil [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/BL-misc-Zenithar.png/150px-BL-misc-Zenithar.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Zenithar.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Zenithar.png) Depiction of Zenithar *Zenithar says: Work hard, and you will be rewarded. Spend wisely, and you will be comfortable. Never steal, or you will be punished.*
— *[Ten Commands: Nine Divines](Lore_Ten_Commands__Nine_Divines.md)* Zenithar, the **God of Work and Commerce**, the **Provider of our Ease**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> is one of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FMGAE-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CK1-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1I-4)</sup> The Trader God is frequently seen as the same deity as the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) god [Z'en](Lore_Z%27en.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup> In the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), however, Zenithar is considered a more cultivated god of merchants and middle nobility, being the deity of wealth, labor, commerce, communication,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GTC-6)</sup> and community.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MZP-7)</sup>

He has strong ties to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup> The introduction of the worship of Zenithar was largely tolerated in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) due to his similarities to a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) agricultural deity.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-8)</sup> His worshippers say that, despite his mysterious origins, Zenithar is the god 'that will always win'.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup> His priests teach that the path to peace and prosperity is through earnest work and honest profit, not through war, bloodshed, or theft.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TCND-9)</sup> Zenithar is seen as a warrior god, but one who is restrained and reserved in times of peace.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-KE10-11)</sup>

He is thought to be associated with [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup> as well as a large blue star sometimes seen in the skies of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-KE8-13)</sup> It's also said that he is most in touch with the mortal world, and that he created the Mace of the Crusader for [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) to help him defeat [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-KOTN-14)</sup>

Zenithar appeared to the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) during their service to the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), as a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) named Jon Hawker, and after he had been freed, the Nerevarine received his gauntlets, Zenithar's Warning and Zenithar's Wiles.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Chapel_of_Zenithar.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Chapel_of_Zenithar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Chapel_of_Zenithar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Chapel_of_Zenithar.jpg) Chapel of Zenithar [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/ON-place-Cropsford_10.jpg/200px-ON-place-Cropsford_10.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cropsford_10.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cropsford_10.jpg) A shrine to Zenithar in [Cropsford](Lore_Cropsford.md) Temples devoted to him are sometimes called [Resolutions](Lore_Resolution_of_Z%27en.md). Worship of Zenithar is typically more prevalent in some geographic areas, such as [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), [Totambu](Lore_Totambu.md), and [Kambria](Lore_Kambria.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CK1-3)</sup>

A [blacksmith's](Lore_Smithing.md) anvil is his symbol, representing labor and production. The [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) [Merchant's Guild](Lore_Merchants_Guild.md) is known to award small golden anvils in Zenithar's name to upstanding members.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Worshippers of Zenithar often keep small devotional anvils in their homes and pewter scale weights engraved with a blue star in the coin purses for financial luck in trying times.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Contraband Z1-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Contraband Z2-18)</sup>

One form of an offering to Zenithar is to burn crops at a shrine of him to offer thanks for prosperity.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Antias-19)</sup> Worshippers also use special prayer beads to call forth good luck and success in all types of negotiations.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Contraband Z3-20)</sup> During special celebrations dedicated to Zenithar, many cities all around Tamriel were decorated with imagery of the blue star, coins and crops.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ZOZ-21)</sup>

Zentihar is also venerated on the isle of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice.md), the seat of [Clan Corelanya](Lore_Clan_Corelanya.md). His worship was introduced to [Sunport](Lore_Sunport.md) by Imperial traders from Leyawiin. As the patron of commerce and craftsmanship, he has gained a devoted following among Sunport's merchants and traders.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-VOFS-22)</sup>

During the Interregnum, the free city of Markarth was one of many cities decorated with Zenithar images and devotional objects during special celebrations dedicated to the god.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Markarth-23)</sup> After Imperialization and the loss of independence, some [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Zenithar, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Belchimac-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Cedran-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Degaine-26)</sup> As of then a shrine of Zenithar was found close to the [Four Skull Lookout](Lore_Four_Skull_Lookout.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-FSL-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SOZ-28)</sup>

Some [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) followers of the Eight Divines considered the sacred toil, fortitude, and triumph in the face of hardship spoken of by the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) as virtues best embodied by Zenithar.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-UV-29)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Golden Anvil
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/ON-place-Zenithar%27s_Abbey_06.jpg/200px-ON-place-Zenithar%27s_Abbey_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Zenithar%27s_Abbey_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Zenithar%27s_Abbey_06.jpg) Golden Anvil A holy relic of Zenithar, that was made of unremarkable iron, by a pious smith, Zenithar honored the smiths work by turning the anvil into gold. The anvil would then fall under the care of the priests of Zenithar's Abbey, which later fell into ruins by avarice.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONDaluion-30)</sup> The [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) resolute, Daluion traveled to the ruined [Zenithar's Abbey](Lore_Zenithar%27s_Abbey.md) to find the long-lost Golden Anvil and learn the story of the abbey's arbitrator, Gavos Douar. With the help of an [adventurer](Lore_The_Vestige.md), they were able to bypass the monsters that inhabited the ruin and recover the sigil keys to the adytum on the east side of the abbey.

They found the decrepit and corroded Golden Anvil on the temple's main altar as well as an elderly Gavos Douar, who tried to preserve the anvil for many years. Before Zenithar's faith was forever lost in the abbey and the anvil, Daluion blessed it with the adventurer's toil and it brought holy light back to the Golden Anvil.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONAnvil-31)</sup> Shortly after its discovery, Daluion returned to the temple in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) and informed the Primate of the anvil's location. The Knights of Iron were deployed to recover it and cleanse the abbey.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONDaluion-30)</sup>

### Mace of the Crusader
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/OB-item-Mace_of_the_Crusader.jpg/150px-OB-item-Mace_of_the_Crusader.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Mace_of_the_Crusader.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Mace_of_the_Crusader.jpg) The Mace of the Crusader The [Mace of the Crusader](Lore_Mace_of_the_Crusader.md) (also known as the Mace of Zenithar) was created by Zenithar. Those it strikes are burnt by holy flames, and it has the ability to turn the undead. According to legend, when Pelinal Whitestrake was slain, a messenger carried the mace to the settlement of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). Centuries later, the master craftsman Saint Kaladas built the Great Chapel of Zenithar to honor the legend. When Kaladas died and was entombed in the Chapel, those who prayed to him began to receive visions of the mace. In the vision, the mace can be seen in the distance, separated by a void. The void symbolizes doubt, and an inscription on the tomb says to "walk in the faith". Sir Ralvas of the original Knights attempted hundreds of times to reach the mace, but failed each time. The pilgrim later discovered that the Boots of the Crusader are needed to cross the void; Zenithar has close ties to Kynareth, as craftsmen need to take resources from the natural world. With the boots, the path of faith was illuminated, and the pilgrim recovered the mace.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-32)</sup>

### Zenithar's Blessed Awl
[Zenithar's Blessed Awl](Lore_Zenithar%27s_Blessed_Awl.md) is a tool for weavers of thread, reed, and wood. Believers in the god of commerce Zenithar claim that "through the awl, all designs are made possible, threads hold strong, and work progresses easily."

### Zenithar's Bleeding Whetstone
[Zenithar's Bleeding Whetstone](Lore_Zenithar%27s_Bleeding_Whetstone.md) is a lesser known [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with god Zenithar. Little is known of the sickle or of the practices it was tied to, though it was said to have been associated with blood rites long before such rituals became linked with sinister or macabre connotations. According to [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md) it was quickly forgotten.

### Zenithar's Warning and Zenithar's Wiles
[Zenithar's Warning and Zenithar's Wiles](Lore_Zenithar%27s_Warning_and_Zenithar%27s_Wiles.md) are a set of gloves associated with the God of Commerce Zenithar. They were imbued with magical properties and effects such as reducing an adversary's ability and willingness to fight, and increasing people's disposition towards the wearer.

## Planet
The planet [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar_(planet).md) is one of the planets found in the skies of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). [Mara](Lore_Mara_(planet).md) is Zenithar's satellite and is orbiting around it. As with all astral bodies seen in the skies above [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and their corresponding deities, the planet Zenithar is believed to be the plane of the god Zenithar as well as the god himself, as seen from the mortal plane. It is said to actually be a different [plane of existence](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) in its own right, infinite in size and mass, with its appearance as a sphere being only a visual phenomenon caused by mortal mental stress. According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).
