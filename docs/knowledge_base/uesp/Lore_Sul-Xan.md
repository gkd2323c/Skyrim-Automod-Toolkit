# Sul-Xan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-render-Sul-Xan_Fleshripper.jpg/200px-ON-render-Sul-Xan_Fleshripper.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Sul-Xan_Fleshripper.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Sul-Xan_Fleshripper.jpg) Sul-Xan raiders The Sul-Xan is a tribe or cult of [Nagas](Lore_Naga.md) once led by Flame-Herald Bahsei,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONMalosza-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> who worship chaos and [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) as the Razor Prince<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> and the True Egg-Child of [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive1-4)</sup> Their homes are known as the legendary [Xi-Tsei](Lore_Xi-Tsei.md) xanmeer, the conquered [Rockgrove](Lore_Rockgrove.md) and Silent Halls, and they raid the surrounding region of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) and are greatly feared by the Argonians of the surrounding villages. They perform ritual sacrifices and create wide soul snares with the intent to summon powerful spirits of destruction.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONJela-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONDust-6)</sup>

They have a one-sided view of [Argonian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Argonian) religion, embracing only the darkest, cruelest beliefs, and scorning the rest as weakness.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONBS-7)</sup> Thus, they sacrifice outsiders and other Argonians alike in hopes of one day summoning Mehrunes Dagon to bring blood and destruction across Black Marsh,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSXN-8)</sup> and eventually the entire world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONJela-5)</sup>

The Sul-Xan traditionally wear death masks made of jade to battle,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CMSX-9)</sup> and tattoo their faces and bodies with green patterns as a raider oathtaking ceremony.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

Sul-Xan warlords with a romantic interest in someone may resort to drastic measures to propose their union, such as kidnapping the object of their affection and offering the heads of that person's neighbors as a dowry.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup>
