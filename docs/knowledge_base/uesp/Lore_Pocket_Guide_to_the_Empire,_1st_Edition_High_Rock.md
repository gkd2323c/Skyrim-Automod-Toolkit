need the most forthright of the Emperor's men* [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) encompasses [Greater Bretony](Lore_Greater_Bretony.md), the [Dellese Isles](Lore_Dellese_Isles.md), the [Bjoulsae River tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bjoulsae_River_Tribes), and, by tradition, the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). Its various peoples are called [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) for the sake of convenience only, as the endless multitude of city-states, principalities, baronies, duchies, and kingdoms that make up High Rock has, until recently, resisted all attempts at centralization into a single culture or government. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of the [First Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) never conquered the whole of High Rock; the Cyrodiils ruled it, but failed to stamp out its virulent sectarianism, which sprang up again with renewed fury during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). It is only now, under the guidance of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), that High Rock is finally tasting the fruits of peace and unity, although a few Bretons still chafe under [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s firm hand. Aside from [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) rule, Bretons are connected only in their language, geographic location, and the ancient rift that separated them from their Nordic progenitors, the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md). [Khosey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khosey), in his '[Tamrilean Tractates](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tamrilean_Tractates),' transcribes a firsthand account of the "discovery" of the Bretons by a Nordic hunting party. The Bretons, in ten generations of Elven intermingling and slavery, had become scarcely recognizable as humans. Indeed, the hunting party attacked them thinking they were some new strain of [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md), halting their slaughter only when one of the oldest began to wail for his life, a shrieking plea that was spoken in broken Nordic. When word of this reached [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), the Nords reasoned that the "Manmeri" beyond [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) were, in fact, descended from human slaves taken during the Elven destruction of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md). King [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md) made the first priority of his Empire the liberation of his long-tormented kinsmen in High Rock. His initial onslaught took him as far as the [Bjoulsae](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md), but beyond that the First Empire never established a lasting presence; the crafty Elves were too strong in their [magic](Lore_Magic.md), and many of the Bretons aided the Elves against their would-be liberators. Ironically enough, it took the tyranny of the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) to finally free High Rock from Elven dominion. Although the Alessians were crushed at the [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md), this costly victory so weakened Aldmeri power that the Elves could no longer challenge the emerging nobility of Greater Bretony, who seized power throughout most of High Rock within two decades of the Alessian defeat. This rebellion was not a coordinated effort, however, and while most of High Rock was freed from Elven tyranny by [1E500](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498), parts of the province remained under Elven rule for much longer. The Western Reach, paradoxically, was one of the last bastions of the Aldmeri in High Rock, the legacy of which is still apparent today (see below). Bretons have fought on both sides of most of the great conflicts of [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history, including [Glenumbria Moors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenumbra_Moors); the memories of these victories and defeats continues to taint relations between the many factions of this divided people. The burghers of [Anticlere](Lore_Anticlere_(city).md), for instance, still noisily commemorate the [Battle of Duncreigh Bridge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Duncreigh_Bridge), the "famous victory" of their Duke over the neighboring hamlet of Sensford in [1E 1427](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1427) (a battle which apparently achieved nothing, as each village continues to boast its own ruling family of antique lineage), by marching each year down Sensford's main street, a progress that results in numerous injuries on both sides even when it does not provoke a brief war between the "[knightly orders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders)" of the two villages. Today, the social structure of the Bretons has divided itself into a poor middle class and destitute peasantry, a magical elite separate from their squalor, and an often incoherent jumble of nobility and ruling families above them all. It is beyond the small ambition of this pamphlet to address the latter in any better terms, for even the natives have difficulty distinguishing their leaders from one another. Indeed, it is an old joke among the Bretons: "find a new hill, become a king," and many have taken it to heart. Youths of all professions and trades in High Rock spend their free time in knightly pursuits, real and imagined, performing good deeds and the like for all and sundry, in oft-vain efforts to achieve, one day, a noble status. This "quest-obsession," more than anything, has served as High Rock's sense of national identity, a peculiar form of altruism and mutual reliance that binds its people together. The geography of High Rock is as varied as its people. The forested peaks of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains), occupied only by herders and the occasional dismal hamlet, divide the Western Reach from more heavily settled west of High Rock. The only true cities lie along the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), where several small kingdoms prospered from the trade that flowed through the Bay to the Bjoulsae River. Inland, the land rises to the windswept plateau of North [Kambria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kambria), with many small towns tucked into the folds and valleys that wind their way down to the northern coast. This bucolic landscape is marred by the grim fortifications that perch atop every hill and crag, a reminder of the constant warfare that has been the scourge of the province. In the past, each petty lord, secure in his castle, enriched himself with tribute from all who traversed his domain, a circumstance clearly incompatible with the free flow of commerce. Now Tiber Septim has begun a program of demolition of these myriad fortresses, a wise policy that should facilitate prosperity while removing a potential refuge for subversives. Although the Bretons are divided into numerous mutually antagonistic factions, to the outsider a singular uniformity in dress, architecture, and customs prevails throughout the land. Bretons are not an imaginative people, a legacy of the Elves, perhaps, and traditional ways are not lightly abandoned. Their villages are pleasant collections of half-timbered structures of one or two stories, with the rustic inn, a shop or two, and perhaps a lordly manor completing the picture. The traveler need not visit more than a handful of Breton communities before satisfying himself that he has captured the flavor of the whole. The people, too, despite their cherished particularism, are remarkably similar in name, accent, and dress throughout the province. It may be that this unacknowledged homogeneity bodes well for the future harmony of High Rock. Most Bretons share an affinity for magic wrought, no doubt, by their lamentable intermingling with the Elves. This talent manifests itself in High Rock's pocket cultures in various ways. In the richer, more urban centers of the Iliac Bay, it has been systematically organized along the hierarchical lines of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md). Children are tested for their magical potential at an early age, and those who pass enter apprenticeship programs funded by the Guild itself, or through independent sponsorship. In more remote regions, such as [Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md) and the Wrothgarian Mountains, witches and medicine men, barely distinguishable from [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) shamans, hold sway over the superstitious peasants with feats of untutored, but often impressive, magical ability.
