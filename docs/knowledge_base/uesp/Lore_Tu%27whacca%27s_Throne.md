[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/ON-place-Tu%27whacca%27s_Throne.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tu%27whacca%27s_Throne.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tu%27whacca%27s_Throne.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tu%27whacca%27s_Throne.jpg) Tu'whacca's Throne ca. 2E 582 Tu'whacca's Throne is a large necropolis built on a vast plateau overlooking the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md), in the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). As its name suggests, it is dedicated to [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) god of [souls](Lore_Souls.md) and caretaker of the afterlife, the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md). This site, in particular, is a monument to the many [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) who perished in the continent's sinking and the burial site for many [heroes](Lore_Hero.md) and honored ancestors in Redguardic history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RFTD-1)</sup>

## General Information
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg) A bas-relief depicting Tu'whacca [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/ON-npc-Throne_Keeper_Farvad.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Throne_Keeper_Farvad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Throne_Keeper_Farvad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Throne_Keeper_Farvad.jpg) Throne Keeper Farvad Tu'whacca's Throne lies on the geographical region of [Myrkwasa](Lore_Myrkwasa.md), on a high rise plateau overlooking the desert landscape. On the south side are large stairs carved into the stone.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RFTD-1)</sup> The Goat's Head Oasis is located at the base of the plateau, near the stairs and its [wayshrine](Lore_Wayshrines.md) is built at the bottom of the staircase.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONThrone-2)</sup>

Because of the Redguards' large adversion to [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md), the dead interred in Tu'whacca's Throne are consecrated to make sure they cannot rise.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONFarvad-3)</sup> The Redguards build vast monuments to honor their dead and propel their spirits to the afterlife, with Tu'whacca's Throne being the most stark and finest example of this. Many pilgrims across Hammerfell come to Tu'whacca's Throne to pay respects to the fallen Yokudans that fled lost [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), and the many historical Redguard kings interred in its labyrinthine necropolis.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RFTD-1)</sup>

The [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholar, [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) wrote that Tu'whacca's Throne is *"more than a simple sepulcher; it is a terrifyingly vast monument to the dead."* He believed that it housed enough buried dead to summon an entire city's population of undead.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGT-4)</sup> Many notable figures are buried here, including the evil usurper-king Ra Boshek, the Alik'ri queen [Afsar](Lore_Afsar.md), the [Diagnan](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md) armiger [Rhina](Lore_Rhina.md), and the founder of the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), Yokeda [Razul](Lore_Razul.md). The latter three were very powerful in life, changing the course of Hammerfell's history in major ways.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONRamati-5)</sup>

The necropolis is overseen by a group of Tu'whaccan priests known as Throne Keepers,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RFTD-1)</sup> with their leader being the High Throne Keeper. Each Throne Keeper is assigned a quadrant of the necropolis and its upkeep.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NTTKF-6)</sup> The Throne Keeper Coif is a helmet with several thin [copper](Lore_Copper.md) medallions inserted into the coif.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONCoif-7)</sup> The nomadic [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) tribe is duty-bound to work with the Throne Keepers of Tu'whacca's Throne. They travel to the temple to perform the annual Rite of Royal Rest, where the many Redguard kings and queens are brought back to their tombs and, in theory, kept happy in the afterlife so that they are not tempted to return and rule from beyond death. The Throne Keepers retire as the Ash'abah perform their ritual for 36 hours. Outsiders are also forbidden from witnessing it and are forced to wear blindfolds.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGT-4)</sup>

The tombs have magical safeguards in their Statues of Ward and Warning at their entrances. In an instance where the dead is resurrected and defeated by the Ash'abah, the body goes through a Rite of Re-Interment.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NTTKF-6)</sup> Tu'whacca's Breath is a powerful magical defense used by the Throne Keepers to cleanse the temple and return its dead to rest. By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there was no record of its use until then. First the four braziers, one for each cardinal direction, and then a final one need to be lit for the spell to activate.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONFarvad-3)</sup> Once a Throne Keeper calls upon Tu'whacca, they are enveloped in a storm cloud and unleash a thunderous clap.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONUwafa-8)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-npc-Yokeda_Razul.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Yokeda_Razul.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Yokeda_Razul.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Yokeda_Razul.jpg) Yokeda Razul Before Tu'whacca's Throne was built, heroes such as [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) and [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md) were kept in the [Hall of Heroes](Lore_Hall_of_Heroes.md), located in southern [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). But the Keepers decided that it now belonged to the gods and that no mortal is ever truly welcomed. Tu'whacca's Throne and the Motalion Necropolis in [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) became the next place for peoples' interment.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONNadira-9)</sup> Perhaps one of Tu'whacca's Throne's oldest inhabitants is Yokeda Razul, the former companion of [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md) during his legendary conquest of Alik'r.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONRamati-5)</sup>

In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Imperial scholar Flaccus Terentius was traveling all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to document the provinces and their people for *[The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel](Lore_The_Improved_Emperor%27s_Guide_to_Tamriel.md)*. The Ash'abah brought him from [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md) to Tu'whacca's Throne, where they began their annual Rite of Royal Rest. As he was not permitted to see, he spent hours contemplating and drank [pomegranate](Lore_Pomegranate.md) wine. Once inebriated, he removed his blindfold and began wandering the necropolis, drawing its funerary structures and taking notes. He then stumbled into a tomb and awoken Ra Boshek. After surviving its initial attack, the [mummy](Lore_Mummy.md) left for [Leki's Blade](Lore_Leki%27s_Blade.md) and the Ash'abah were left furious with Flaccus.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGT-4)</sup>

The Ash'abah defeated Ra Boshek and returned whatever remained of him to Tu'whacca's Throne.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGT-4)</sup> But for a time, his remains were not kept in his mausoleum and only returned after the Rite of Re-Interment was completed in [Midyear](Lore_Midyear.md), circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NTTKF-6)</sup> In that same year, the Withered Hand, led by the brothers [Uwafa](Lore_Uwafa.md) and Alasan stole the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md) from [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md). Uwafa took the ward of Ansei Radan and resurrected many undead in Tu'whacca's Throne, including the heroes, Armiger Rhina, Queen Afsar, and Yokeda Razul. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) arrived with the Ash'abah to assist Throne Keeper Farvad in cleansing the temple. By activating Tu'whacca's Breath, the Vestige breached the temple and defeated Uwafa, recovering the Ansei Ward.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONUwafa-8)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONBreath-10)</sup>
