# River-Elk Clan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-npc-Anslettar.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Anslettar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Anslettar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Anslettar.jpg) Member of the River-Elk Clan ca. 2E 582 The River-Elk Clan was a prominent [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) that lived in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), a region in western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The River-Elks were strong in number and inhabited a multitude of semi-permanent camps throughout the [Karth](Lore_Karth_River.md) Valley. Although they were distrustful of the foreign way, they willingly engaged in trade with foreigners, provided they proven themselves to be friends to the clan.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Anslettar-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief-of-Chiefs_Cannear.jpg) Chief-of-Chiefs Cannear ca. 2E 582 During the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), River-Elks were led by Chief [Cannear](Lore_Cannear.md). She eventually retired and the position of leadership of the clan was given to her successor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POTR-3)</sup>

When [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of the [Blackdrake Clan](Lore_Blackdrake_Clan.md) became the Ard of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) in [2E 559](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_559), his power had only extended to the confines of his city while in the Reach proper, the strongest tribes at the time, such as the [Eagleseers](Lore_Eagleseer_Clan.md), the [Six-Fords](Lore_Six-Ford_Clan.md), the River-Elks, and the [Thornroots](Lore_Thornroot_Clan.md) had the most power. To the clans, the Ard was just another chieftain despite the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md)'s authority over the Reach.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POTR-3)</sup>

As of [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568) they were still considered one of the more prominent clans in the region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

In the years leading up to [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), Cannear, a chief emeritus of the River-Elk Clan attained the title of [Chief-of-Chiefs](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md#The_Chief-of-Chiefs), a position of great authority in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POTR-3)</sup> and held it for at least four years. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) she resolved the longstanding rivalry between the [Eagleseer](Lore_Eagleseer_Clan.md) and the [Six-Ford](Lore_Six-Ford_Clan.md) clans.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONVows-4)</sup>

In the same year, some members of the clan moved to the city of Markarth in search of prosperity and safety from the incursion of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). One such clansman, a Reachfolk known as Anslettar, wanted to prove himself to Ard Caddach and join the elite unit of [Stonehands](Lore_Stonehands.md). It is unknown whether he succeeded or not.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Anslettar-2)</sup>
