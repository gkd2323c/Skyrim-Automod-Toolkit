[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/SR-npc-Birna.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Birna.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Birna.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Birna.jpg) Birna tending her shop **Birna** is a [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) [pawnbroker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pawnbroker) who runs [Birna's Oddments](Skyrim_Birna%27s_Oddments.md) in [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold.md) and feels depressed about how little custom the shop has; her only regular customer is the [housecarl](Skyrim_Housecarl.md), [Thaena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Thaena), who isn't the most friendly person. Her brother, [Ranmir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ranmir), has a tendency to drink away their earnings.

Birna wears a [belted tunic](Skyrim_Belted_Tunic.md) and a pair of [boots](Skyrim_Boots.md#0001be1b). She is equipped with an [iron dagger](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md), and carries a [key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Ranmir%27s_House) to [Ranmir's house](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ranmir%27s_House) as well as a selection of [common items and gold](Skyrim_Leveled_Lists.md#Common).

Since Ranmir spends much of his time drinking inside the [Frozen Hearth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Frozen_Hearth), Birna must dedicate most of her time to running the store in order to support both herself and her brother. She wakes up at 7am and unlocks the door to the store. She will then stand behind the counter in the store for the next thirteen hours, offering her services between 8:30am and 8pm. After a busy day of running the shop, she will leave at 8pm to head to the Frozen Hearth. She will find a seat and eat and drink with the other regulars for the next three hours. At 11pm, she will make her way back home. She will stay up for another hour, until heading upstairs to sleep for the next seven hours.

## Related Quests
- **[Yngol's Barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol%27s_Barrow)**: Find the treasure unlocked by the [Coral Dragon Claw](Skyrim_Coral_Dragon_Claw.md).

## Quest-Related Events

### Yngol's Barrow
If you ask her if she has ever had a bad deal after asking about her business, she will explain how she came to possess a seemingly useless [dragon claw](Skyrim_Coral_Dragon_Claw.md):

- **You said something about a bad deal?**
- *"Yeah, it was stupid of me. I shouldn't have believed the story and just refused the trade. But I didn't, and now I'm stuck with this worthless junk."*
- **What was the story?**
- *"Some line about this claw thing and [Yngol Barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Yngol%27s_Barrow). He said it was worth more than its weight in gold if I took it back there. Something about placing it back in Yngol's chamber, I don't know. What was I thinking? Even if it were true, I'm not setting foot in some ancient tomb, filled with who knows what."*
- **I could look into it for you.**
- *"I don't even care any more. You want to check it out? Fine. I'll sell you the thing for fifty gold, and then it's your problem."*
- **I'll take that claw for 50 gold.**

If you try to buy it from her without enough money, she will say:

- *"Oh, I'd gladly sell it to you if you had the money. But you don't, do you?"*

If you do have the required amount of gold, she will give you the dragon claw:

- *"Fine, here you go. You get killed on account of this thing, it's not my fault. Got it? But, I suppose, if it does actually turn out to be worth something, let me know, will you?"*

Despite this request, you cannot inform her of the [true treasure](Skyrim_Helm_of_Yngol.md) the claw protects.

## Dialogue
Approaching Birna will have her inform you that almost everything she has is for sale and information about her brother:

- *"I'll sell just about anything, if it's worth my time. Keep that in mind, will you?"*
- *"I do what's necessary so we can get by. Have to, since I can't count on my brother for anything."*
- *"If you're looking for Ranmir, he's either passed out in bed, or working on winding up that way."*
- *"My brother's a good-for-nothing drunk. We'd have lost the house by now if it were up to him."*
- *"Just because we live in the middle of nowhere doesn't give Ranmir the right to drink himself into oblivion."*
- *"Ranmir has no problem drinking himself under the table, so I have to be the one to make ends meet."*

If you assist Ranmir in stopping himself from [drowning his sorrows](Skyrim_Drowned_Sorrows.md), she will instead make a series of comments about how her life has been improved now that her brother isn't drinking her profits away:

- *"Things are easier now that I don't have to worry about Ranmir anymore."*
- *"I still can't believe you were able to help Ranmir pull himself together."*
- *"Without all our money going to drink, I can afford to expand my selection a bit."*

When speaking to Birna directly she can be asked questions:

- **Why set up shop here?**
- *"Wasn't by choice, I can tell you that. Options were in short supply. Ranmir and I grew up here, and nearly any money I make, he drinks away. Without the coin to pack up and leave, I decided to take to trading."*

- **Do you get much business way up here?**
- *"Very funny. I'm sure you've noticed that Winterhold isn't exactly overflowing with people. So I'll sell whatever I can. If it's worth putting a price on, you can bet I'll do it. I've been burned a few times, made a few bad deals, but mostly I come out on top."*

If you choose to invest in her business:

- **I'd like to make an investment in your business.**
- *"You want to give me money? Just like that? Fine, but I intend to earn that investment. I don't need handouts."*
- **Let's see what you can do with this.**
- *"I'll work hard to justify your faith in me."*

## Unused Dialogue
- Birna's dialogue after the end of [Drowned Sorrows](Skyrim_Drowned_Sorrows.md) doesn't change to mention that [Ranmir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ranmir) is gone. She was supposed to expand upon her previous response if asked about why she set up shop in Winterhold after the quest was completed, but there was nothing put in the scripting to allow the updated line to be said:

- *"Wasn't by choice, I can tell you that. Options were in short supply. Ranmir and I grew up here, and his drinking soaked up whatever coin we had. So I opened a shop to try and get by. Now, though... Have to admit, I'm attached to the place. Suppose that makes me crazy, doesn't it?"* - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.0, fixes this bug.

Should she meet with Ranmir inside The Frozen Hearth, she will either angrily ask about their money or desperately try to get him to return home:

## Notes
- Birna *technically* isn't related to the quest Yngol's Barrow at all. All of her dialogue is stored in a different quest, [A Bad Trade](Skyrim_A_Bad_Trade.md), internally known as `Freeform Winterhold A`, and is internally defined as belonging to this quest as well. However, the quest ultimately goes unused in the final game, despite the fact that purchasing the coral claw advances the quest to stage 20. No other scripts update it. The dev notes attached to the quest stages list the purchased item as "the jar" but are otherwise in-line with the events that transpire in the final game. This "jar" would've actually been a [burial urn](Skyrim_Unobtainable_Items.md#Other_Unobtainable_Items) which still exists in the files, and gets checked for by a trigger associated with the quest. - A dev note attached to stage 10 of Yngol's Barrow still references the freeform quest, reading: *"if the player enters Yngol barrow before getting the related FF quest in Winterhold, just never have that appear."*
- If you talk to Birna enough times in rapid succession, she may say phrases said by Winterhold Guards.
