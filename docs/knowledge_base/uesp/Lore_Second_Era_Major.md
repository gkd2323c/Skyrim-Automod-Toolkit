**Note:** The Second Era of Tamriel is also commonly referred to as the *Common Era, CE.* or the *Second Age*.

## First Century

2E 1 — Beginning of the Second Era.

- By decree of Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md), year one of the Second Era begins on the first of Morning Star. The marking of the new era commemorates the demise of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) and Versidue-Shaie's official assumption of stewardship of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).

## Second Century

## Third Century

2E 230 — The first [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) is formed.

- Sickened by the foolishness of his superiors in the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) founds the new guild in [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md).
- At around this time, a triad of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) — [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) —make a deal with the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sload.md) K'Tora to acquire the Heart of Transparent Law—the Stone of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md). K'Tora uses mind magic to make [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md), the leader of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), steal the Heart. Trying to foil K'Tora's plan, Iachesis makes a deal with Nocturnal to learn a spell with which to hide the Heart in a language that could only be used by mortals. Unbeknownst to all, Iachesis hides the Heart within himself—but soon forgets these events, due to K'Tora's magic. Shortly thereafter, the island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) —home of the Psijic Order—is removed from Nirn by the Order and goes missing.

2E 283 — Potentate Versidue-Shaie declares martial law at the Council of Bardmont.

- Following the destruction of his fortress at [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), Versidue-Shaie convenes the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and declares martial law throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), warning the rebellious leaders of his vassal kingdoms to disband their private armies or face his wrath. This sparks 37 years of intense conflict, at the end of which Versidue-Shaie's Imperial legions stand victorious as the sole army in the land.

2E 299 — Potentate Versidue-Shaie attempts to remove [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md)'s bridges

- The Potentate's plan was to allow large sailing vessels to pass from [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) to the [River Niben](Lore_River_Niben.md). Leyawiin's Chamber of Legates, fearing that the additional merchant traffic would simply sail through Leyawiin without stopping, managed to stall the project until it was abandoned.

## Fourth Century

2E 301 — The kingdom of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) is founded.
2E 309 — The [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr.md) is founded.

- [Keirgo](Lore_Keirgo.md) and [Eshita](Lore_Eshita.md), rulers of the minor kingdoms [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) and [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md), marry. The kingdoms are combined to create the province of Elsweyr, sparking a great class war which almost results in outside intervention.

2E 312 — The [Seamount Orcs](Lore_Seamount_Clan.md) take over [Betony](Lore_Betony.md) (approximate date).

- The invasion is said to have taken place nine generations before the Alliance War. Under Orcish rule, the island would be known as *Betnikh*.

2E 320 — [Dinieras-Ves, "The Iron"](Lore_Dinieras-Ves.md) founded [The Syffim](Lore_The_Syffim.md), the forerunner of the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md).

- Since Potentate Versidue-Shaie had destroyed all armies but his own, the Syffim were intended to act as a hired army to suppress brigandage in the provinces.

2E 324 — Potentate Versidue-Shaie is murdered.

- The Potentate is murdered in his palace in [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) by the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md).

2E 324 — [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) inherits the title of Potentate.

- Potentate Savirien-Chorak succeeds his father, Versidue-Shaie. Although noted to be a poor administrator who was prone to mishandling the crises that occurred during his reign, Savirien-Chorak is credited with reversing the ban on forming local armies, and accepting [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) into the Empire.

2E 326 — Fall of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md).

- Anequinan Tribal Rebels take the capital of Ne Quin-Al, killing most of the royal family. The [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) intervenes and instates a rotational system where control shifts between the desert tribes and city nobility based on the Moons.

2E 358 — Earliest known written record of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md)

- A note was found beneath the floorboards of an abandoned house in the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) village of Jallenheim. Depicting a war between two shadowy organizations, scholars speculate that this is a rare piece of evidence of the war between the Morag Tong and its hated offshoot, the Dark Brotherhood. The Brotherhood was probably in existence before this date, but its secrecy shrouds its true origins, though it was most likely formed by several ex-members of the Morag Tong.

## Fifth Century

2E 428 — The [Order of the Silver Dawn](Lore_Order_of_the_Silver_Dawn.md) is founded.

- In response to the "Curse of the Crimson Moon" werewolf crisis in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), Adeliza Silver-Axe establishes the order after her beloved son is inflicted with [Lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md).

2E 430 — Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) is assassinated, ending the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).

- Savirien-Chorak and all of his heirs are murdered, ending the years of the Akaviri Potentate and the Second Empire of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Some historians date this event to 2E 431.
- Orsinium loses the status of an Imperial territory as a result of the death of the last Potentate and the dissolution of the Second Empire.

2E 431 — [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) splits into Eastern and Western kingdoms.

- After High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) is assassinated, Jarl [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) challenges the legitimacy of Logrolf's heir, [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md), and a [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) is convened. Although Freydis is accepted by the Crown of Verity and named High Queen in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), a partial Moot held in Solitude declares Svartr to be High King. From then on, Skyrim is divided into an Eastern kingdom, ruled by the successors of Freydis, and a Western kingdom, ruled by the successors of Svartr.

2E 431 — [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) is sacked by [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) forces.

- After losing its status as an Imperial territory, Orsinium is destroyed for the second time. Some sources claim it happened the following year.

2E 432 — The [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) is re-established in [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md).

- [Cavor Merula](Lore_Cavor_Merula.md), a warrior-priest of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), reforms the ancient order in response to the collapse of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).

2E 435 — The [Lamia](Lore_Lamia.md) Invasion

- The invasion is preceded by a storm that is believed to have originated at [the Island of Storms](Lore_Tempest_Island.md) off the northwestern coast of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Orcish remnants flee west and establish Forsaken Stronghold in Bangkorai.

2E 480 — Baz Swordbreaker's brigand army attacks Kvatch

- The charismatic [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) marauder leads his army to Kvatch, but is defeated by the Order of the Hour. The Primate of Kvatch thereafter grants the Order to form into an army during times of religious emergencies.

## Sixth Century

2E 522 — King [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) is crowned.

- King Hurlburt of House Branquette, who led Breton forces at the Battle of Granden Tor, ascends to the throne.

2E 532 — Prince [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md), son of King Hurlburt, is born.
2E 529 — [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) the Black Drake marches on Cyrodiil.

- Having subdued rival clans and taken control of the Reach, Durcorach marches his army south and invades Cyrodiil.

2E 533 — [Durcorach the Black Drake](Lore_Durcorach.md) conquers Cyrodiil and makes himself Emperor.
2E 536 — [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) is born.
2E 541 — The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) invade High Rock.

- [Durcorach the Black Drake](Lore_Durcorach.md), Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and progenitor of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), invades the land of the Bretons.

2E 541 — [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) is sacked.

- After the death of King [Heseph](Lore_Heseph.md) of House Moile, the city falls after only three days' siege by [Durcorach the Black Drake](Lore_Durcorach.md). It was retaken by Duke [Blaise Guimard](Lore_Blaise_Guimard.md), who was later elected to the throne. Some accounts place this event in the following year.

2E 542 — [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) invaders attack [Alcaire Castle](Lore_Alcaire_Castle.md).

- They were driven back by [Sir Byric](Lore_Byric.md) after he set fire to the fields surrounding Alcaire Castle. The Alcaire Knights were renamed [Knights of the Flame](Lore_Knights_of_the_Flame.md) thereafter to honor his bold action and determination.

2E 542 — The First [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) is formed.

- After [Hallin's Stand](Lore_Hallin%27s_Stand.md) and [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) fell, Durcorach lays siege to [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) for fifty-seven days and nights, but is ultimately slain by [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md). The kings of Daggerfall, Camlorn, Shornhelm, Evermore, and Wayrest sign the first Daggerfall Covenant after the victory.

2E 546 — King [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md) of Shornhelm dies, ending his reign.

- [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) supported the claim of Hulburt's legitimate son, Prince Phylgeon, to the throne of Shornhelm. [Houses Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) supported Phylgeon's illegitimate elder half-brother, Prince [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md).

2E 546 — Prince [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) ascends to the throne of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).

- The Council of the North meets to consider the claimants to the throne. Through various political machinations, King Hurlburt's illegitimate son wins by a narrow victory.

2E 559 — The War of Two Houses.

- [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) engage in a bloody conflict south of [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md). Hlaalu emerges victorious due to the heroics of Purilla Falen.

2E 560 — The [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) originates in [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md).

- A strain of the Knahaten Flu hits northern- [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) causing many deaths. The [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) were strangely immune to the flu which has led some to believe that they were responsible for it in some way. The flu continued until around 2E 603.

2E 563 — [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) is crowned King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md).

- Earl Emeric is elevated to the throne and House Cumberland becomes the second royal dynasty of Wayrest after the Knahaten Flu kills the entire [Gardner](Lore_Gardner.md) royal family.

2E 566 — [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) erupts.

- Offended that King Emeric refused the hand of his daughter in marriage, King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) launches a surprise siege on Wayrest in Last Seed.

2E 567 — House Dufort is granted control of the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md)

- In response to House Mornard's betrayal during Ranser's War, King Emeric strips Duke Avrippe Mornard of his title and exiled him to Cyrodiil. Additionally, he seized a full two-thirds of his house's holdings and granted them to the Mornard's eternal foe, House Dufort.

2E 567 — The Greater [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) is formed.

- Following the events of Ranser's War, the provinces of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) sign the Second Daggerfall Covenant with the Orcs of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md). King Emeric grants the Orcs the right to reestablish Orsinium.

2E 568 — King [Blaise Guimard](Lore_Blaise_Guimard.md) of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) dies.

- He is succeeded by his son, King [Eamond](Lore_Eamond.md).

2E 572 — The [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md)

- [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md)'s army lands in northeast Skyrim and captures [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), killing Queen [Mabjaarn](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md). En route to [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), Dir-Kamal learned it was protected by forces under Jorunn the Skald-King and [Wulfharth the Ash-King](Lore_Wulfharth.md), and bypassed that city in favor of attacking [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). Dir-Kamal was then trapped between Jorunn's forces and a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) army led by [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md); with the timely aid of a phalanx of Argonian troops, they drove the Akaviri invaders into the sea to drown. Another account states that the Akaviri invaders of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) were wiped out when [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md) flooded the land.

2E 572 — Jorunn the Skald-King is crowned High King of Skyrim.

- Having defeated the Akaviri invaders, Jorunn returns to Windhelm and is crowned High King of Skyrim in the throne room of the Palace of the Kings.

2E 572 — The [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) is formed.

- Following the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md), the provinces of Morrowind, Skyrim and Black Marsh sign a mutual defensive pact in the city of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md).

2E 576 — [Varen's Rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md) begins.

- [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), the last of a dynasty of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) known as the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), legalizes Daedra worship throughout the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), immediately sparking a rebellion. [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), the Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), marshals the armies of the Colovian Estates against the emperor.

2E 576 — Conflict between [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) erupts.

- Shortly after [Carolus Aquilarios](Lore_Carolus_Aquilarios.md) assumed temporary command of Kvatch, Anvil's Imperial prefect [Ephrem Benirus](Lore_Ephrem_Benirus.md) attempts to seize Varen Aquilarios's property and holdings. Carolus repels several attacks on Kvatch, and severely weakens Anvil's defenses.

2E 577 — [Fortunata ap Dugal](Lore_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.md) declares herself governor of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md).

- Captain Fortunata takes advantage of the weakened city and deposes the Imperial Prefect with her pirate army.

2E 577 — The Nordic Hold of [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md) is established.

- High King Svargrim creates the Hold to guard Western Skyrim's southern border from incursions by the hostile Reachmen. The town of Karthwatch is also established shortly after.

2E 577 — Varen's Rebellion ends.

- After a bitter war, Varen Aquilarios's forces storm the Imperial City and defeat Emperor Leovic's last remaining legion. Varen personally kills Leovic in the Imperial Throne Room and proclaims himself emperor.

2E 577 — Emperor Varen Aquilarios leads the [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md) on a quest to retrieve the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).

- Seeking to legitimize his rule, Varen and his allies [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md), [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md), [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), and [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) embark on a quest for the lost Amulet of Kings, which Mannimarco asserts can be used in a ritual to persuade [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to make Varen a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md). After two years of searching, the Five Companions finally recovered the Amulet.

2E 578/579 — The [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) occurs.

- Mannimarco betrays Varen Aquilarios during his attempt to use the Amulet of Kings to become Dragonborn, corrupting the ritual and breaking the ancient covenant with Akatosh that sealed Nirn from Oblivion. An explosion of arcane energy, the Soulburst, causes Varen to vanish, and its mystical aftershocks sweep across Nirn, causing mages to die or go mad, Daedra to appear in greater numbers, and the constellation of the Serpent to dominate the night sky. In the ensuing chaos, the Daedric Prince [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) deploys Dark Anchors to begin pulling Nirn into his realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) to merge the two together, a process known as the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).

2E 579 — [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md) becomes Empress Regent following the disappearance of Varen Aquilarios.

- In her husband's absence, Clivia Tharn assumes regency over the Imperial Throne. Clivia's reign reportedly ushers in a time of "madness, murder, and decay".

2E 580 — Ayrenn is crowned Queen of [Alinor](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).

- After disappearing in 2E 573, Ayrenn was assumed lost and her eldest brother Prince Naemon was named the next heir to the throne. When King Hidellith died and Naemon prepared to assume the throne, Ayrenn returned to Summerset Isle, asserted her prior claim, and was crowned.

2E 580 — The First [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) is formed.

- In need of allies following their own internal troubles, the provinces of Elsweyr and Valenwood petition Summerset for aid, and the nations join together to become the Aldmeri Dominion.

2E 580 — The [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) begins.

- The Aldmeri Dominion, Ebonheart Pact and Daggerfall Covenant clash to wrest control of the Ruby Throne from the Empire of Cyrodiil and defend themselves against the Imperials under Molag Bal's influence. The Dominion seeks to reestablish Elven dominance over Tamriel to protect it from the carelessness of the younger races. The Covenant intends to restore the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) and return peace and stability to Tamriel. The Pact desires to defeat the Imperials in order to preserve the independence of their homelands.

2E 582 — The [Mortuum Vivicus](Lore_Mortuum_Vivicus.md) is destroyed.

- The [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) successfully destroys the Mortuum Vivicus with the help of their newest Champion.

2E 582 — The Mages Guild sanctuary of [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md) returns to Tamriel.

- After nearly a thousand years of being in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), the sanctuary of Eyevea is returned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by a Mages Guild Adept with the help of [Arch-Mage Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md).

2E 582 — The [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) ends.

- The three alliance leaders meet on the island of [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) in neutral territory to discuss an invasion of Coldharbour. After they fail to come to an agreement, they are attacked by the forces of Molag Bal, after which they give their blessings to the Mages and Fighters Guilds spearheading an assault into the Daedric realm. The combined forces of the Guilds charge into Coldharbour led by the Vestige. There, they destroy the Great Shackle, and seal the Planar Vortex, thus ending the Planemeld.

2E 582 — Daedric plots in the Imperial City foiled.

- In a final attempt to restart the Planemeld, Molag Bal focused on the occupation of the Imperial City. A Dragonguard agent called the [Drake of Blades](Lore_Drake_of_Blades.md), with the help of the Vestige, stopped Molag Bal's plot to destroy the [Sublime Brazier](Lore_Sublime_Brazier.md), the key to rekindling the Dragonfires. The attempt at a second Planemeld, the "Planemeld Obverse", is also stopped with the slaying of [Molag Kena](Lore_Molag_Kena.md).

2E 582 — Molag Bal is banished

- [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) banishes Molag Bal within his own realm, thus finally stopping his bid for merging [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) into one nightmarish hellscape. With it, the souls stolen by him are released from his clutches.

2E 582 — The [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) of [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) is restored to its former glory.

- After a failed heist and the resulting persecution from [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md), the Thieves Guild of Abah's Landing is restored to its prime and begins to spread its influence throughout Tamriel.

2E 582 — Count [Carolus Aquilarios](Lore_Carolus_Aquilarios.md) of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) emerges as the dominant power on the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md).

- Shielded from the ongoing Alliance War by [Varen's Wall](Lore_Varen%27s_Wall.md), a *de facto* independent Gold Coast sees the end of a power struggle between [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), Kvatch, and the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md). Aquilarios has Governor [Fortunata ap Dugal](Lore_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.md) of Anvil assassinated by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md); facing persecution from the Order of the Hour, the Brotherhood subsequently kills Primate [Artorius Ponticus](Lore_Artorius_Ponticus.md), stopping him from raising an army against Aquilarios.

2E 582 — [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) is saved from the machinations of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) and [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md).

- Seeking to gain entry to [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), the Daedric Prince and his minion steal the staff Sunna'rah, a tool Sotha Sil had created for experimenting on his power. The staff ends up in the hands of the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md), who aspired to the title of [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md). Chodala's use of the staff drains away the power of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md), which weakens the Warrior-Poet's hold over the moonlet [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) and endangers all of Vvardenfell. Fortunately, the [Champion of Azura](Lore_Seryn.md), a [clockwork mage](Lore_Barilzar.md), and an [Outlander](Lore_The_Vestige.md) come to Vivec's aid. Chodala is discredited and defeated, Clavicus Vile's plot is thwarted, and Vivec's power is restored.

2E 582 — A plot by [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) to take over the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) is foiled.

- [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s Shadow, serving Nocturnal, is summoned and replaces him as ruler of the Clockwork City. At the same time, the [Blackfeather Court](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md) enters the city and steals the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md), which has been in the Clockwork God's possession. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), assisted by [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) and the Clockwork Apostles, unveils the plot; but is unable to retake the Skeleton Key before it is taken by Sotha Sil's Shadow. Using the key, Nocturnal and the Shadow attempt to take over the Clockwork City; but are stopped by the Vestige, Divayth Fyr and a Clockwork Apostle, who save Sotha Sil by defeating his Shadow. Sotha Sil gives the Skeleton Key to Divayth Fyr for safekeeping.

2E 582 — The [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) is saved from a triad of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md)

- The three Daedric Princes [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) attempt to execute their plan to take control of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) using the Heart of Transparent Law, which they had made [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) steal around [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230) —who had also hidden it from them. After they trick Iachesis to reveal the Heart's location, that being within himself, they kill him; but Nocturnal betrays Clavicus Vile and Mephala by taking the Heart for herself. With it she takes control of the Crystal Tower, hoping to use its properties to achieve omnipresence in the entire [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). However, her plans are foiled by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the Psijic Order, assisted by the two betrayed Princes and a champion of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), and the Heart is returned to the Crystal Tower.

2E 582 — The [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md) is recovered and the Root-Whisper tribe is restored

- [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) aids Cyrodilic Collections in recovering a powerful artifact that contains the spirits of a lost Argonian tribe in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).

2E 582 — [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) unleashes various Daedric artifacts onto Cyrodiil

- In order to relieve his own boredom at the Three Banners War, the Daedric Prince Sheogorath temporarily claims several artifacts belonging to the other Daedric Princes. He then offers them to the combatants in Cyrodiil to bring upheaval to the ongoing conflict.

2E 582 — A [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) threat is averted in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)

- The Imperial battlemage [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) accidentally unseals the legendary [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md) using the [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md), believing it to contain a powerful weapon. This results in a rage of Dragons—sealed away by the Khajiiti hero [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md) in the Merethic Era—being unleashed upon Elsweyr.
- The Dragons form an alliance with [Euraxia Tharn](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md), the ruler of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md). The alliance is short-lived, as [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) slays Euraxia, bringing Rimmen back under the control of the Khajiit.
- The Dragons plot to obtain lunar power of [Jone](Lore_Jone.md) and [Jode](Lore_Jode.md) using the [Moon Gate](Lore_Moon_Gate.md) of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md). They are stopped after the Vestige steps into the Plane of Jode, banishing the Dragon leader, [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md).
- [Khamira](Lore_Khamira.md), the daughter of King Hemakar, is crowned queen of Rimmen.

2E 582 — The [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) is reformed in [Southern Elsweyr](Lore_Pelletine.md)

- The ex-members of the [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md), [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), assemble a new Dragonguard—largely consisting of ex-pirates and [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) soldiers—to defend [Pelletine](Lore_Pelletine.md) from the Dragon threat. With the help of the Dragon [Nahfahlaar](Lore_Nahfahlaar.md), they manage to acquire the powerful artifact [Mask of Alkosh](Lore_Mask_of_Alkosh.md) and defeat the Dragon [Laatvulon](Lore_Laatvulon.md).
- [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md) returns to Elsweyr after having been banished, seeking to use the [Aeonstone](Lore_Aeonstone.md) found on the island [Dragonhold](Lore_Dragonhold.md) to gain the power of a god, becoming the prophesized [Dark Aeon](Lore_Dark_Aeon.md). The new-founded Dragonguard are aided by Nahfahlaar, [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) and Queen [Khamira](Lore_Khamira.md) in destroying the Aeonstone and defeating Kaalgrontiid. However, Kaalgrontiid is able to absorb a great amount of power before being killed, which risks destroying all of Elsweyr if not contained. Nahfahlaar and Abnur Tharn use their combined power to contain it, resulting in the disappearance and possible death of the latter.

2E 582 — Death of High King Svargrim

- [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) agent Fennorian, representative of King Jorunn Lyris Titanborn, Princess of Western Skyrim Svana and the Vestige join forces to unveil the vampiric threat looming over Skyrim.
- [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md) personally approaches High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), offering him incredible power in exchange for his servitude. The High King agrees and becomes a Vampire Lord. The [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md), together with the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md), create [harrowstorms](Lore_Harrowstorm.md), magical weather anomalies to pull their comrades out of Coldharbour, and then using the Stone Husks, to fully restore their corporeal forms allowing them to live once more, killing hundreds of inhabitants of Skyrim in the process.
- The Gray Host takes control over Castle Dour and unleashes a powerful harrowstorm inside the city. After a great battle, High King Svargrim is slain and the Gray Host defeated and retreating from Western Skyrim.
- Relations between Western and Eastern Skyrim improve as King Jorunn offers aid to the victims of the vampire attack. Princess Svana becomes Jarl of Solitude.

2E 582 — The Gray Host is destroyed in The Reach

- [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) consolidates more power over the Reachfolk clans. A faction of [rebels](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md) led by [Arana](Lore_Arana.md) is formed in opposition to the power of the Ard.
- The Gray Host unleashes an attack on the citizens of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) as a sacrifice for the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md).
- [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md) assisted by the Vestige, defeats Rada al-Saran, releases the souls harnessed by the Gray Host and resurrects the Reachfolk who died during the Gray Host assault on Markarth. The rebels and loyalists of the Ard join forces.
- Peace treaty between the Reach, Western Skyrim and Eastern Skyrim is signed.

2E 582 — Search for the Four Ambitions

- Sombren, Calia, and Destron, three out of the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md), have been released.
- Assassins have been targeting members of the Elder Council in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). Councilors Velan Sophus, Jirich, and Abor have been successfully assassinated.
- It is revealed that Councilor [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md) was the High Priest of Mehrunes Dagon. His forces surround Gideon, and even Dagon himself makes an appearance, but they are ultimately defeated at Fort Redmane.

2E 582 — Mehrunes Dagon banished to Oblivion

- The Fourth Ambition, who is actually [the Anchorite](Lore_Mairead.md) of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), is revealed as the illegitimate daughter of Emperor Moricar and Sister [Celdina](Lore_Celdina.md).
- Mehrunes Dagon materializes within [Fort Sundercliff](Lore_Sundercliff.md), but his egonymic is employed to banish him back.
- [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) absconds with the powers of the Four Ambitions, vanishing to employ them for undisclosed purposes.

2E 582 — Machinations of the Ascendant Order unveils on High Isle

- Lord [Bacaro Volorus](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md), heading the [Society of the Steadfast](Lore_Society_of_the_Steadfast.md), takes charge of organizing peace negotiations on the High Isle, aiming to halt the ongoing Three Banners War.
- The Ascendant Order, a clandestine pan-Tamrielic society opposed to monarchies, grows in power and gains prominence.
- Prince Irnskar, Queen Ayrenn, and High King Emeric face assassination attempts.
- A fierce battle unfolds on All-Flags islet between the forces of the Ascendant Order and opposing factions, resulting in the demise of the Ascendant Magus.

2E 582 — Prophecy of [Kasorayn](Lore_Kasorayn.md) comes to fruition

- Frii is awakened.
- Archdruid Orlaith of the [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md) collaborates with the Dreadsails and lays siege to [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md), aiming to proclaim herself as the new Druid King. However, her attempt proves unsuccessful. Count Leonard Mornard tragically passes away during the siege, and his son Stefan Mornard assumes leadership over Galen.
- Lord Bacarro Volorus, is unveiled as the leader of the Ascendant Order and the descendant of [House Merchad](Lore_House_Merchad.md), the lineage of the Druid King Kasorayn. He steals the regalia of the Druid King and endeavors to declare himself as the new Druid King. This reckless act triggers the eruption of the [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md) volcano. In a heroic act, Frii sacrifices themselves to protect others.
- Peace talks resume following these tumultuous events, but the ultimate outcome remains uncertain and unknown.

2E 582 — The Prince of Paths is remembered by the world.

- [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) forms a contract with the Vestige to deal with the foreseen crisis.
- Through the efforts of [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md) and his Hidden Kindred allies, the Daedric Prince [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md) is remembered by the world.
- Followers of Ithelia begin claiming relics such as the Staff of Paths and consilidating power.
- The prison of Ithelia is discovered by Torvesard and the Vestige but she already escaped to parts unknown.

2E 582 — The rise of the Shardborn and the discovery of Ithelia.

- In [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) the Dawnwood grows overnight, destroying towns and displacing people. The Bosmer settlement of Vashabar is built with the new jungle.
- The scholar-mage Leramil the Wise on the behalf of Mora works with the Vestige to investigate disturbances in the Weald. Tribune Alea Idolus and the former adventurer Beragon also become involved in this investigation. It is found that two factions are responsible for these disturbances, and both are associated with Ithelia, the mortal [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), and the Daedric [Shardborn Clan](Lore_Shardborn.md) who are led by Shardmarshal Vargas.
- It is discovered that the Bosmer leader of Vashabar, [King Nantharion](Lore_Nantharion.md) is the leader of the Recollection and aims to restore Ithelia to her proper state.
- Ithelia is encountered at the Outcast Inn, though she is intially has a spotty memory and has lost much control over her power. A temporary alliance with the Vestige and jaunt into the Many Paths results in her power stabilizing.
- Relics belonging to the Princes that originally trapped Ithelia are recovered, with the aim of using them to find and stop the Prince. They lead to Ayleid ruins of [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md) beneath Vashabar, while the Vestige and their allies are able to slay Nantharion, Vargas and Torvesard are able to restore Ithelia's powers and the trio disappear from the ruins.
- The relics are used to track and follow Ithelia to the ruins of [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md) and her [Loom of the Untraveled Road](Lore_Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road.md), a device that would allow her to alter reality and reweave everyone's fates. The Vestige and their allies confront Ithelia at the Loom, Vargas is slain in the battle and the Loom is destroyed. A furious Ithelia is filled with the power of the Many Paths and leaves with Torvesard for parts unknown.
- Talks between Greenspeaker Darolith and [Count Calantius](Lore_Count_Calantius.md) to work out a treaty between the Dawnwood and the rest of the Weald begin.

2E 582 — Ithelia is permanently banished to another reality

- Soon after the destruction of the Loom of the Untraveled Road, tears in reality begin appearing around Tamriel, and are linked to Ithelia's growing instability. Investigation of the shattered Loom reveals that Ithelia plans to defeat Hermaeus Mora and rebuild Mirrormoor on the ruins of Apocrypha. The [Mirror of Truth](Lore_Mirror_of_Truth.md) relic is constructed by Leramil at the suggestion of the Vestige as a way to snap Ithelia out of her destructive spiral.
- Ithelia and Torvesard are tracked down in the Mythos of Apocrypha, where they were draining Mora's power. The Vestige fights Ithelia until they are able to activate the Mirror, which shows Ithelia the truth. As she was being sealed away ages ago, she realized that Mora was correct about her being a danger to reality. While Ithelia was prepared to stop, Torvesard was not and claimed both Prince's powers with the aim of completing Ithelia's original goals. The Vestige and their allies work together to stop Torvesard.
- Hermaeus Mora and Ithelia take council from the Vestige about what should be done with the latter to prevent her from breaking reality. The Vestige suggests that Ithelia leaves for another reality where there is no Daedra or magic to prevent her power from coming back to her. Both Princes agree and work together to find such a reality and open a portal to it, Ithelia then leaves for good.
- Rituals are performed around Tamriel to begin work on repairing the reality tears. Afterward Hermaeus Mora uses his powers to ensure everyone forgets Ithelia once more as a precaution. The sole exception to this being himself and the Vestige, before formally ending their contract.

ca. 2E 582 — Reemergence of the Worm Cult

- Several Fighters Guildhalls are attacked and Guildmaster Merric investigates, discovering the remmergence of the Worm Cult, but is sadly slain in Rivenspire while destroying one of their devices. Vanus Galerion is abducted while investigating a Worm Cult Nest and [Prince Azah](Lore_Azah.md) and [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md) are nominated as interim leaders during this crisis.
- Soul Reaper devices are reported appearing around Tamriel, stealing the souls of anyone who is killed near them. Alliance ambassadors gather on [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) and are convinced by Prince Azah to lend aid to the Guilds to combat the Worm Cult. Vanus Galerion manages to make contact from his prison and the Stirk Fellowship is formed.
- The Writhing Wall is activated by the Worm Cult on the southern island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), splitting it in two. Their leader [Wormblood](Lore_Wormblood.md) begins his search for the Gift of Death.
- The Stirk Fellowship arrives on Solstice, allying with the local factions and investigating why the Worm Cult came to the island. They discover Wormblood's plan to resurrect Mannimarco using his recovered sarcophagus and the Gift of Death.
- The King of Worms, Mannimarco, is resurrected in the Colored Rooms using Wormblood's body rather than his own. This comes at the cost of the dying [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md) to use the Gift on herself to deny the Worm Cult and to bring back Darien Gautier.

ca. 2E 582 — Battle for the Writhing Wall

- Fellowship forces around Tamriel work together to bring down the Writhing Wall by empowering the Meridian Lens device to punch a hole through the barrier. The device eventually reveals the Writhing Fortress, a structure built in the liminal space between Coldharbour and Solstice. The Stirk Fellowship fight their way through the fortress and destroy the Soul Spire empowering the barrier, and the destruction of the Writhing Wall follows soon after.

ca. 2E 582 — Mannimarco is defeated a second time in Mor Naril

- Stirk Fellowship forces make their way into Eastern Solstice, with the two main goals of rescuing Vanus Galerion and discovering Mannimarco's plans. Contact is made with the Stone-Nest tribes who have been under attack by the Worm Cult ever since the Writhing Wall appeared. Improved Soul Reaper devices known as Soul Flayers are discovered.
- Vanus Galerion is rescued from Xul-Haj and the Worm Cult is found to be amassing at the ancient Daedric temple of Mor Naril in northeastern Solstice. Mannimarco is discovered to be summoning the Wraithforge from Coldharbor to the temple to use its powers.
- Investigation of the Gates of Mor Naril reveal the remains of Voskrona Colossals, stone constructs immune to necrotic magic that were created by the ancient Argonians who fought daedra forces the last time Mor Naril was active. A way to reactivate the colossals is searched for.
- The Stirk Fellowship and their Solstice allies infiltrate Mor Naril and attack the Worm Cult forces. The Vestige and Darien Gautier enter the Wraithforge and slay Mannimarco a second time.
- Regent Karinwe Corelanya begins to work with the Stone-Nest to guard the ruins of Mor Naril.

2E 830 — The Second [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) is formed.

2E 852 — The [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) begins.

- Forces led by General Talos, later known as Tiber Septim, begin a series of conflicts that result in the unification of Tamriel under the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and mark the final years of the Second Era.
- The stronghold of [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) is captured by Nord-Breton invaders. In the dead of winter, General Talos leads a daring assault against overwhelming odds, retakes the stronghold, and recovers the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) from the Tomb of [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md) below Sancre Tor.

2E 854 — [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) is assassinated.

- The King of the Colovian Estates is assassinated by a [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) nightblade, just before his coronation as the Emperor. The assassin also burns the Imperial Palace to the ground and attempts to kill General Talos, who is crowned in Cuchlecain's stead as [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).

2E 862 — [Thassad II](Lore_Thassad_II.md) dies.

- The High King of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), Thassad II, dies from natural causes, being considerably ill and old at the time of his death. After his death, the leader of the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), Baron [Volag](Lore_Volag.md), starts a revolution to reinstate the republic.

2E 864 — The [Battle of Hunding Bay](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md).

- Crown Prince [A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md), son of Thassad II, heir to Hammerfell and leader of the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), leads his forces in continual victory over the Forebears. Desperate, the Forebears ask Tiber Septim for help, who sends his forces led by Lord Admiral [Amiel Richton](Lore_Amiel_Richton.md), who destroys the Crown forces at the harbor of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md).

2E 864 — The [Stros M'Kai Uprising](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai_Uprising.md).

- The date of the Redguard rebellion on the island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) off the coast of Hammerfell. The [Restless League](Lore_Restless_League.md), led by siblings Cyrus and [Iszara](Lore_Iszara.md), destroys the Imperial fleet in the harbor of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and defeats the small contingent of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) stationed on the island.
- Lord Amiel Richton, Provisional Governor of Stros M'Kai, dies at the hands of Cyrus, wielding a sword enchanted with the soul of Crown Prince A'tor.
- Baron Volag comes out of hiding and retakes the Forebear city of Sentinel. Following the two uprisings, Emperor Tiber Septim is forced to sign a [peace treaty](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md) on terms more favorable to Hammerfell.

2E 882 — [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) reawakens.

- Dagoth Ur and his Ash Vampires awaken underneath the [Red Mountain,](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) where they bind themselves to the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) and cause the mountain to erupt. They ambush the [Tribunes](Lore_Tribunal.md) during their annual ritual bathing in the Heart's power, and drove them off. From then on, the Tribunal was cut off from the Heart chamber, and would launch intermittent campaigns to attempt to force their way inside.
- The event led to Sotha Sil to begin development on the [Mechanical Heart](Lore_Mechanical_Heart.md), a synthetic replication of the Heart of Lorkhan.

2E 896 — Tiber Septim conquers Tamriel.

- Tiber Septim finishes conquering most of the lands of Tamriel with the aid of the Dwarven artifact [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) and the battlemage [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md), finally uniting Tamriel under the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). The time of Tiber's reign is considered by many to have been a just and glorious one, among the best of Tamriel's history.
- The beginning of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) is pronounced by Tiber Septim at the end of the year.
