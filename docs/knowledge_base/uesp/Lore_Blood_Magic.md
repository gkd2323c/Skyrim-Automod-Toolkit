# Blood Magic

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png/200px-LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png) A Blood Sorceress Blood Magic is a an ancient form of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) believed to be associated with dark practices. It is most often used by [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) belonging to powerful bloodlines, who can use it to drain life, reanimate the dead, telekinetically lift others from a distance, summon [gargoyles](Lore_Gargoyle.md), and paralyze others.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Harkon-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Aundae Sorcerer-2)</sup> Vampire Lords can only perform blood magic while levitating and can use their life drain effect to slay mortal men and enhance their blood magical power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Harkon-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vampire Lord-3)</sup> Practitioners of blood magic are referred to as sanguimancers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sanguimancer-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ADP-5)</sup> [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md) is also associated with blood rites.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SG-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWS-7)</sup>

To a lesser extent, non-vampires can also use blood magic in combat to siphon health from enemies.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOSkills-8)</sup> [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) are said to be naturally competent at blood magic.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Blood magic can be also used to drain memories.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Khazasha-10)</sup>

Though often requiring sacrifices, some blood magic can be performed with the use of animal blood.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONCalawende-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONAshaka-12)</sup>

## Uses
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/OB-misc-Blood_Fountain.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Blood_Fountain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Blood_Fountain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Blood_Fountain.jpg) A blood fountain (Oblivion) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_03.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Lord_Harkon_03.jpg) A blood altar to Molag Bal Blood Magic rituals are performed to complete feats of great power. An elven arrow tainted with the blood of a pure-blooded [Daughter of Coldharbour](Lore_Daughters_of_Coldharbour.md) becomes a Bloodcursed elven arrow. If fired at the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md) with [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md), it is capable of blotting out the Sun for an entire day.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-13)</sup> Lord [Harkon](Lore_Harkon.md) planned to use this method to becloud out the sun for eternity upon discovering the Tyranny of the Sun prophecy, so that he and his vampire kin would free to hunt at whatever time they pleased.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Harkon-1)</sup>

According to legend, the Great [Sea Serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md) terrorizing Serpent's Grotto was formed from the blood of a fish that pleaded for its life to the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), but was not spared. It came to terrorize the Bosmer until the clan decided to seal it away through the use of blood magic, sacrificing three of the clan's volunteers into blood wells.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-St GS-14)</sup> A Blood Rune could be used to repair the seals on the wells if they ever came undone.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Sendel-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSL-16)</sup>

Several monsters can be created or summoned through blood magic, such as [coagulants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coagulant), [hemopteras](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hemoptera), and [hemonculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hemonculous).

### Artifacts
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9f/ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bloodroot_Forge_03.jpg) The Bloodroot Forge One of the more common applications of Blood Magic is in the creation of blood-forges and artifacts enhancing blood magic power. The [Morkul Clan](Lore_Morkul_Clan.md) of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) once housed the Hand of Morkul, a terrible device that fused living captives into weapons and armor, as the centerpiece of their bloodforge within their clan holding of Morkuldin.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONAshaka-12)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Handof Morkul-17)</sup> They revered [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), and were capable of creating gargoyles and living statues through blood magic to serve as guardians.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Handof Morkul-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-GJ-18)</sup> The Blood of Mauloch is a blood magic spring of red water and mud used by the original Orcish inhabitants of [Abamath](Lore_Abamath.md) as war paint, believed by them to imbue them with strength.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Arithiel-19)</sup> All Orc wise women learn to use blood magic in their art to power healing spells after brutal combat.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

The [Keptu](Lore_Keptu.md) constructed the [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md) over a source of [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) before their decline.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-NNo RO-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-NNot F-22)</sup> The [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) later used unholy rites to combine blood, nirncrux, and iron at the Forge to imbue their soldiers with blood-forged power.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Blood Forged-23)</sup>

Blood magic is not inherently associated with dark or macabre practices, and such beliefs are often merely a superstition. Aedric worshippers, including the [Druid](Lore_Druids.md) followers of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) and [Clever Folk](Lore_Clever_Folk.md) honoring [Mother Wolf](Lore_Mara.md) were known to practice forms of blood magic,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LADCG-24)</sup> and rituals involving blood.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-OM-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Havil-26)</sup> Additionally, certain artifacts associated with the Aedra, such as [Mother Mild's Sickle of Absolution](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mother_Mild%27s_Sickle_of_Absolution) and [Zenithar's Bleeding Whetstone‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zenithar%27s_Bleeding_Whetstone) have been linked to ritual uses involving blood.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SG-6)</sup>

The bloodspring of Redwater Den and the Bloodstone Chalice could improve vampiric blood powers.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-13)</sup> The [Rings of Blood Magic](Lore_Rings_of_Blood_Magic.md) are a set of bone rings that increase vampiric power, and are said to have been given to ancient powerful Vampire Lords by Molag Bal himself.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Feran Sadri-27)</sup>

### Seals and Locks
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/SR-quest-Discerning_the_Transmundane.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Discerning_the_Transmundane.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Discerning_the_Transmundane.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Discerning_the_Transmundane.jpg) Dwemer lockbox that requires Dwemer blood to open [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/SR-npc-Delphine_25.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Delphine_25.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Delphine_25.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Delphine_25.jpg) Akaviri blood seal Lockboxes containing valuables can be enchanted so that only that only relatives can open them. This is done by tying the enchantment to the blood of the original owner, ensuring only those who share the same blood can open it, even after the owner's demise. The [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) Noellaume family did this with a box containing their signet rings and the rings of the families who joined them through marriage, passing down the lockbox through a maternal line from mother to daughter.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-LEN-28)</sup>

The [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) are known to have 'blood seals' which are triggered by specific blood. The blood seal in [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md), which requires the blood of a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) to open, is one example of this.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) are also known to have used their own blood to unlock lockboxes. An example of this was the lockbox in Septimus Signus's Outpost in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), which required Dwemer blood to open. Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) used a mixture of the blood of the Elven races to trick the box into opening.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>
