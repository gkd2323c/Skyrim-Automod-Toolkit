# Levitation Act


The knowledge of levitation magic was known to be obscure by [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48). However, there were still those who practiced or utilized it, including a [Synod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Synod) mage named Lazarum, who apparently discovered a levitation spell.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TIC-5)</sup><sup>:39</sup> In 4E 48, a Breton named Annaïg Hoïnart learned how to brew levitation potions, which allowed her and Mere-Glim to travel to the floating city of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md). Synod mages used levitation magic on Imperial legionaries in a battle with Umbriel.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LOS-6)</sup><sup>:250-252, 254</sup>

Some [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) Telvanni towers, such as [Tel Mithryn](Lore_Tel_Mithryn.md) on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), had magical runes fitted at the base of the stem which granted anyone access;<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DB-7)</sup> however, it is unknown if this was related to the Levitation Act as levitation pads were used by the Telvanni as far back as the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## Notes
- The need for a Levitation Act seems to stem from a gameplay design perspective outside lore. At least in the case of *[The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)*, it [was stated](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Cartogriffi%27s_Posts) that the idea for including the [Boots of the Apostle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boots_of_the_Apostle) as a [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) was rejected due to levitation potentially breaking quests.
