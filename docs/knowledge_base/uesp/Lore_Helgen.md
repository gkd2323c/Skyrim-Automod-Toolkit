[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/SR-place-Helgen.jpg/200px-SR-place-Helgen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Helgen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Helgen.jpg) Helgen, [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Helgen, referred to as the "Gateway to the North" due to the many travelers who visited while coming from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to the south,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGt S-1)</sup> was a fortified hamlet located east-northeast of the city of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), south of [Riverwood](Lore_Riverwood.md), and north of [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>:118</sup>

The rather small "backwater hovel" was guarded by a wooden wall and the slopes of the mountains it was nestled in,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGt S-1)</sup> and surrounded a large fort known as Helgen Keep. The Keep held a barracks for Imperial troops, a torture room, and led into a series of caves that traveled north into the wilderness.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Helgen-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HK-4)</sup>

## History
The [Mede](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md) [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) maintained a strong military presence throughout [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md), particularly in Helgen, as far back as [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Blades-5)</sup>

On the 17th of Last Seed, [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), an Imperial convoy carrying [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), several of [his soldiers](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), a horse thief, and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) arrived at Helgen for an execution, only to be interrupted by the surprise [return](Lore_Return_of_the_Dragons.md) of, and attack on the village by, [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). Ulfric, Tullius, and [Elenwen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elenwen) all managed to escape alive, but every citizen of Helgen died during the attack, leaving only a young boy named Haming as the sole known survivor of the village.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Unbound-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Hamingand Froki-7)</sup> Helgen was left in a state of ruin, which bandits later took residence of.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Helgen-3)</sup> Though Vilod perished in the attack, his mead made using the native juniper berry and red mountain flowers lived on in Helgen and beyond, truly embodying the spirit of Skyrim.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TESOCBT-8)</sup>

## Notes
- A location known as [North Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:North_Keep) appeared in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:North_Keep)* west of the location of Helgen (on a different road), sharing a similar name to the town's aliases of the "Gateway to the North" and "Helgen Keep". The town of [Oakwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oakwood) in Arena was also found closer to Helgen's location, on the same road between [Fort Neugrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Neugrad) and [Riverwood](Lore_Riverwood.md). It is unclear that either of them are intended to be the same town as Helgen.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Arena-9)</sup> According to the unofficial text, [Skyrim In the New Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Skyrim_In_the_New_Era), Helgen has gone by several names, and has been inhabited by refugees from Oakwood and Neugrad after those settlements were destroyed.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SINE-10)</sup>
