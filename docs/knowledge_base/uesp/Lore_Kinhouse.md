# Kinhouse

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/ON-interior-Forgotten_Wastes.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Forgotten_Wastes.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Forgotten_Wastes.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Forgotten_Wastes.jpg) Kogoruhn, last clanstead of House Dagoth<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWHasphat-1)</sup> **Kinhouses** are a feature within the elven societies of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HK-3)</sup> and the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Talviah-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOF-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Homestead Int-6)</sup> Definitionally, the term kinhouse can be used in place of the terms 'clan' or 'house' when referring to a noble elven political faction or family (sometimes called a "Kinship").<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Marostaale-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Cut Content-8)</sup> A kinhouse may also refer to the central clan building(s) of an elven clan, whether the kinhouse is used for residence, administration, gathering place, or some mixture of the three.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOF-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-NV-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HK-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DQI-10)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Homestead Int-6)</sup> The term kinhouse can simultaneously refer to both the building and the family that resides within it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DM-11)</sup> In the Dunmer culture, the term **clanstead** is used in the same context.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWHasphat-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Po M-12)</sup> Sometimes Altmer refer to a kingdom as a **Kinhold**.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Lo F-13)</sup>

Among the Altmer, the leaders of Kinhouses are referred to as Kinlords and Kinladies,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Kinlords-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Cut Content-8)</sup> collectively referred to as the Kinrulers.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TOTK-15)</sup> In certain city-states, these rulers may hold the elevated titles of High Kinlords and High Kinladies.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-High Kinladies-16)</sup> The offspring of Kinlords and Kinladies are called Kinheirs. Other similar titles were also in use. [Sundavar Corelanya](Lore_Sundavar_Corelanya.md) of [Salas En](Lore_Salas_En.md), for instance, assumed the title of First Kinlord upon establishing his kingdom. However, this particular title was not adopted by his [Corelanyan](Lore_Clan_Corelanya.md) successors, who instead used the more traditional titles of Kinlord and Kinlady. This continued until the family eventually abandoned these titles altogether, choosing instead to designate their leaders as Regents, a term reflecting humility, signifying one who ruled not by personal authority but as a steward of the power entrusted to them.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-HOCC-17)</sup>

Some smaller Altmeri kinhouses were respected and influential despite their lack of prominence. Marriages between members of different kinhouses could lead to their unification into a single house.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-AS-18)</sup>

Among the Dunmer, ranks within the kinhouses are often displayed through tassels hung from clothing, indicating a Dark Elf's rank within their particular kinhouse.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TK1-19)</sup> Kinhouse colognes are spiced perfumes created by the alchemists of [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), each featuring unique fragrances specific to each house.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TK2-20)</sup>

## Notes
- Calan is another term used to refer to Altmeri and Aldmeri families.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TPOP-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MB-22)</sup>
- During the development of base game *ESO*, [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) was going to be included as a zone called Varyllis, which was meant to contain the southlands of the isle, from [Alinor](Lore_Alinor.md) to [Dusk](Lore_Dusk.md). That zone was meant to feature a number of Altmeri noble kinhouses across its landscape (such as the Felballin, Isquer, Scintilla, Syldarim, and Teriskor Kinhouses) each headed by a kinlord or kinlady.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Cut Content-8)</sup>
- *[The Elder Scrolls Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends)* originally featured more connections between Altmer cards and kinhouses, such as the [High Elf Soldier](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:High_Elf_Soldier) card originally being named a "Summerset Kinholder" per the card's art file name (`summerset_kinholder`), which may indicate that a "Kinholder" was originally intended to be the cultural title for soldiers in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) (note: "Clanholder" is a title within the tribal ranks of the [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) of Morrowind),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MWR-23)</sup> and the Naarifin's Elite originally being named `kinship_trainee`, suggesting it was a novice member of a "Kinship". The [Moment of Clarity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Moment_of_Clarity) was originally called `syldarim_astrologer` indicating a more explicit connection to the Syldarim's roles as astronomy experts with the power to return planets to the sky.
