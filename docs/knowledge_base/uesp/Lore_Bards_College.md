[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/SR-place-Bards_College.jpg/200px-SR-place-Bards_College.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bards_College.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bards_College.jpg) The Bards College circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) The Bards College is an institute found in the city of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), within the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The Bards College is well known throughout the province as a prominent institute of music and history for the aspiring bard, or the *Skald* as they are known in Skyrim. Students of the Bards College are known to be well-trained and successful in their business. Graduates of the Bards College are often employed in various noble households; some have even entertained the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup> The college is marked by the *Flame of Callisos*, which is found at the entrance. As long as the flame remains lit, the College will stand.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Wt WXI-2)</sup>

## Further Information
The Bards College has two known departments: History and Lute (Music). The Bards College, along with many poets and historians, write poems and stories in the [Poetic Edda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Poetic_Edda), a collection of endless poems that depict Skyrim's extended history.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRGiraud-3)</sup> The [Ateia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ateia) family is a recurring contributor to the Bards College, since their earliest members, Parteron Ateia, who donated his last flute to the Bards College,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AF-4)</sup> all the way to Pantea Ateia, who was a dean at the college during the Skyrim Civil War.

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-place-Bards_College.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bards_College.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bards_College.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bards_College.jpg) The Bards College circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/SR-quest-Tending_the_Flames_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Tending_the_Flames_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Tending_the_Flames_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Tending_the_Flames_03.jpg) The Burning of King Olaf circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) The Bards College was founded when Skyrim was a part of the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), roughly during the twilight years of Ysgramor's dynasty, and the years following.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup> At one point, Solitude was sacked by [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) in the late years of the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md), and sometime later, Solitude and the Bards College would make a yearly tradition based on Olaf's exploits. The College's students flood the markets to celebrate for a week and end it with the *Burning of King Olaf*, where everyone gathers at the college courtyard to burn an effigy that depicts King Olaf One-Eye.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KOV-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRTending-6)</sup> This tradition would continue for many centuries, to celebrate Olaf's victory over [Numinex](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Numinex) and his ascension to the throne. More and more famous bards and scholars would come from the Bards College, such as [Callisos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Callisos) and [Morachellis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morachellis).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup> The now famous Flame of Callisos was named after the alumni.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Wt WXI-2)</sup>

Many years later, during the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Bards College was administrated by Headmaster [Viarmo](Lore_Viarmo.md), who chose to keep the politics of the civil war away from his institute.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRViarmo-7)</sup> At this time, Jarl [Elisif the Fair](Lore_Elisif_the_Fair.md) had canceled the annual Burning of King Olaf, as she considered it in poor taste after the assassination of her husband, High King [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md). To persuade her to change her mind, Viarmo had sent a [student](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) of the college to find a lost verse of the Poetic Edda, [King Olaf's Verse](Lore_King_Olaf%27s_Verse.md), written by the First Era skald, [Svaknir](Lore_Svaknir.md). The student found themselves at [Dead Man's Respite](Lore_Dead_Man%27s_Respite.md), the burial place of High King Olaf One-Eye. The student had brought back King Olaf's Verse and presented it to Jarl Elisif. The burning of the effigy would commence that year, and continue to do so.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRTending-6)</sup>
