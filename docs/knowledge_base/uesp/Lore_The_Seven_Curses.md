The Seven Curses **Annotated by [Gilvas Barelo](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Gilvas_Barelo)** *Extract from the Apographa of the Dissident Priests alluding to the trials the Nerevarine must face*
---
[from the Apographa of the [Dissident Priests](Lore_Dissident_Priests.md), annotated by [Gilvas Barelo](Lore_Gilvas_Barelo.md), Abbot of [Holamayan](Lore_Holamayan.md)]

*...through the doors of the unmourned house*
*where scoffers scoff and schemers scheme*
*from the halls of the oath-breaking house*
*rings seven curses of gods blasphemed*

*first curse, Curse-of-Fire*
*second curse, Curse-of-Ash*
*third curse, Curse-of-Flesh*
*fourth curse, Curse-of-Ghosts*
*fifth curse, Curse-of-Seed*
*sixth curse, Curse-of-Despair*
*seventh curse, Curse-of-Dreams...*

#### Notes:
Lines 1-3: Ambiguous. May refer to the impiety of the god-mocking House [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), or the treacherous diplomacy of the subtle [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md), or both. House Dagoth, however, was reviled as oath-breakers for their treachery at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). It may, however, refer to unspecified broken oaths of peace between Lord [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) and Lord [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md), founders of the [Grand Council](Lore_First_Council.md). Nerevar and Dumac were loyal friends until the disagreements between the Dwemer and the other [Great Houses](Lore_Great_House.md) broke out in open conflict.

Line 4: The Dwemer were the mockers and profaners of the divine.

Lines 5-6: The curses of fire and ash would come from Red Mountain where [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur_(god).md) rules. These were the earliest reported threats from Red Mountain.

Line 7: Curse of flesh suggest blight diseases, especially corprus. The fire and ash storms preceded the threats of blight and corprus.

Line 8-10: Obscure. May refer to as-yet-unrecognized threats from Dagoth Ur.

Line 11: Recent reports of soul sickness and disturbed dreams come from townfolk and [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) alike. That the seventh and final curse has begun suggests the threat presented is about to reach a crisis.
