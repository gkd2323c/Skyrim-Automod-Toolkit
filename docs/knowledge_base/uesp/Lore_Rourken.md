# Rourken


## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/RG-misc-Dwarven_Ruins_Banner.png/130px-RG-misc-Dwarven_Ruins_Banner.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Dwarven_Ruins_Banner.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Dwarven_Ruins_Banner.png) Dwemeri Banner<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RG-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg) The Hall of Volendrung, where the hammer landed *Each moment so turned becomes an island in your path. For example, the Dwemer, united, had their way in Dwemeris.
But one clan—Rourken, the wayward clan—struck anchor and sailed across the sky. Why do this? Mired in a sea, they sought the sky.*
— [Spinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spinners) [Girnalin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Girnalin)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Girnalin-5)</sup> When the [First Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Council) was founded in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), the Rourken Clan self-exiled from [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) because they did not want to make peace with the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md). The Rourken Chieftain lifted his hammer, Volendrung and threw it to the far west across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), promising to lead his clan to "wherever it should fall". Their trek through the continent has been the subject of legend, often depicted in Hammerfell [dwemer ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Ruins) as a mass exodus of golden-clad dwemer and trudging through the forests of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Once they reached the desert, Volendrung landed head-strong from the clouds like a falling star in the night sky before it landed.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1E-6)</sup> The Rourken Clan initially settled throughout the province's southern coast, where they established an easy trading agreement with the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) to the south, and the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) to the north. Near the Gilane region, they built their capital city, Volenfell, on the site where his hammer fell.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup>

The Rourken discovered that the land was rich in resources and untapped potential was within their grasp, so they expanded beyond their capital and built many deep mines and busy workshops.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSan Load-7)</sup> The Rourken had expanded all over the Alik'r Desert and beyond, as far south as [Bthzark](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthzark) in [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DJP-8)</sup> and some that stayed east in the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains) like the colony, Rkundzelft.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONRkund Load-9)</sup> The Dwemer scholar, Thelwe Ghelein believed that other clans splintered themselves from the main clan to settle in far more remote places. One such example was the ruins of [Zthenganaz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zthenganaz) in [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md)'s snow frontier,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLoad Zthen-10)</sup> and another possible example was the ruins of [Irkngthand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Irkngthand), in [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DI2-11)</sup> It has even been theorized that the underwater Dwemer facility at Graven Deep in the middle of the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) was constructed by the Rourken Clan.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Ghelein suggested that there may be even more undisturbed archaeological sites as far south as Cyrodiil and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DI1-13)</sup>

Sometime prior to their disappearance, the Rourken Clan was engaged in a large-scale conflict with the Arch-Mage, [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) aptly known as the Battle of Rourken-Shalidor. On the side of the Dwemer was King [Rourken](Lore_Rourken_(person).md), one of the last dwarven kings. In battle, he wielded the [Spellbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spellbreaker), a magical tower shield that was fashioned specifically for him against Shalidor's magic. It was lost to time after the battle,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ARSpell-14)</sup> but its historical importance was well known and documented.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TL-15)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Flint-Tooth_Warchief.jpg) The ghost of a giant Goblin warchief (ESO) The Dwarves of Hammerfell eventually a faced an otherworldly invasion from a new breed of [giant goblins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Giant_Goblin), who invaded via a rent in space and time known as the [Goblin gate](Lore_Goblin_Gate.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RGH-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-17)</sup> These Goblins were generally described as taller and less "cowardly" than Goblins of the later Eras.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TKF-18)</sup> They possessed a huge army of fighters, and appeared in the middle of the province, where they would pillage and raze towns to the ground, driving the Dwarves out of the province, on exodus to parts unknown.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RGH-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-17)</sup> The victorious Goblins were led by and worshipped the god [Malooc](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malooc).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-VOF-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-T26OFSIU-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-EGE-22)</sup> In time, Volendrung, the Great Hammer of these Rourken Dwemer, ironically came to embody the power of Malacath, who had become known as one of the Dwemer's most bitter foes.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OBMartin-23)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WIV-3)</sup>

The Rourken Clan vanished with the rest of their race sometime around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), in concurrence with the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md). Their cities were heavily plundered by the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) and [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) pirates that roamed the coast, and the streets were prowled by desert predators until the sandstorms had gradually swallowed them. By the time the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) arrived in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808), much of their civilization had been lost,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup> though some of their ruins are still accessible, albeit very buried. With that being said, Volenfell's existence had been questioned by historians and archaeologists for the longest time, leaving many to question whether it ever existed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WIV-3)</sup>

### Post-Disappearance and Legacy
With Clan Rourken's self-exile from Resdayn, many other clans followed suit and settled in other regions beyond their traditional hold. Clan [Kragen](Lore_Kragen.md) is one such example, who followed the Rourken's path out of Resdayn and settled in what is now [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). There, they built their new city, [Arkngthamz](Lore_Arkngthamz.md) and formed a four-way alliance between other city-states. However, as time went on and their alliance started to wane, the Kragen expanded further west into the [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md), where they built [Fang Lair](Lore_Fang_Lair.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-AP-24)</sup>

Two of the Clan's most well-known artifacts are Volendrung and Spellbreaker, both of which are also related to [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), both [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) and [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) respectively. The writer, Gurour claims that the former is one of the most notorious mythological artifacts in history, not only for its ties to both the Dwemer and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), but that every detail about it has been heavily questioned by scholars, such as Chief Rourken's ability to throw it cross-continent. A persistent theory from skeptics such as Gurour is that Volendrung's flight is poetic imagery of the clan's exodus from Resdayn.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WIV-3)</sup>

After many years of being lost in the desert, Volenfell was re-discovered in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), by the explorer duo, Tharayya, Quintus Verres, and their band of adventurers in the northeastern Alik'r region in [Tigonus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tigonus). Their expedition team was able to locate it when they received rumors of the changing desert winds unearthing a Dwemer ruin. Their mission was to locate the [Guardian's Eye](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guardian%27s_Eye), an artifact of immense power,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TJ-25)</sup> but when Tharayya was betrayed by Verres and his mercenaries, the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) stepped forward to defeat Verres and retrieve the artifact, but a failsafe in the ruins buried the Guardian's Eye in the sands.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONBlood-26)</sup> Since their discovery, there has been a race between scholars, treasure-hunters, and even the [Seventh Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seventh_Legion),<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SCO-27)</sup> to search the ruins and its storied depths.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONLoad Volen-28)</sup> But even with the collapse and years since its discovery, by the late-Third Era, Volenfell and its ruins were still intacted, but they were reportedly near Gilane, according to the [Imperial Geographic Society](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Geographic_Society).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup>

The [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) tend to reference the Rourken Clan in their exclamations, even though they arrived on Tamriel after the Dwemer's disappearance.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3E-2)</sup> Some common Redguard exclamations include, *"by Rourken*", *"by the Dwarf-King"*, and *"by the beard of the Dwarf-King"*.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DFRaces-29)</sup>
