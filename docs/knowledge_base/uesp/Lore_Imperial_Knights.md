# Imperial Legion

[![MW-banner-Imperial.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/MW-banner-Imperial.png/100px-MW-banner-Imperial.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Imperial.png) *Upon my honor I do swear undying loyalty to the Emperor… and unwavering obedience to the officers of his great Empire. May those above judge me, and those below take me, if I fail in my duty. Long live the Emperor! Long live the Empire!*
—Imperial Legion oath The Imperial Legion, also known as the **Imperial Army**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWQ-1)</sup> **Dragon-Tribe of the Emperor**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> the **Red Legions**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Heimskr-3)</sup> and the **Ruby Ranks**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup> is the main fighting force of the [Empire of Tamriel](Lore_Empire.md). It is often pluralized as the Imperial Legions. Headquartered in the [Imperial City Prison](Lore_Imperial_City_Prison.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLoad Prison-5)</sup> they operate under the auspices and authority of the Emperor himself. With its vast numbers, quality training, and rigid discipline, the Legion is considered one of the best armies ever assembled in history. The primary mission of the Imperial Legion is to preserve the peace and rule of law in the Empire.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OL-6)</sup> Those who protect the Emperor and the Imperial Province are sometimes called the **Imperial Guard** and the **Imperial Watch**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ASS.21-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GFMOM-8)</sup> A Legion Centurion is typically in command of the [Palace Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) of the [White Gold Tower](Lore_White_Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PBI-9)</sup> The Legion's administration is known as the **Imperial War Office**.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOS-10)</sup><sup>:Part 3, Chapter 4</sup>

In peacetime, the Legion serves primarily as a garrison force — manning forts, patrolling roads, and providing guardsmen for towns, cities, counties, and nobles.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Oblivion-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Morrowind-12)</sup> They are empowered to arrest criminals and seize their property, among other things.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PN-13)</sup> In wartime, the Legion's responsibilities and powers are greatly increased. During conflicts, the Legion serves as an invading and occupying force, overwhelming opposition with numerical superiority and strict economy of force. Legionnaires serve under an Imperial contract and, for its duration, they can be reassigned to different postings or even subcontracted to serve in local guard forces, at the discretion of the superior officers who have the authority to reassign them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBTaking Care Lex-14)</sup> The aquatic equivalent of the Imperial Legion is the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md), with the two often overlapping.

## Overview
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/LG-cardart-Tullius%27_Conscription.png/200px-LG-cardart-Tullius%27_Conscription.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Tullius%27_Conscription.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Tullius%27_Conscription.png) An Imperial Legion [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/OB-place-Imperial_Legion_Offices.jpg/200px-OB-place-Imperial_Legion_Offices.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_Legion_Offices.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_Legion_Offices.jpg) Imperial Legion Offices in the Imperial City The Legion is divided into structured and disciplined military units; the primary, and largest, of which is the Legion, which is further divided into Cohorts.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AALL-15)</sup> Legions are numbered for identification, and each possesses a proud history and sub-culture.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup> The [Imperial Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) are a rich tradition of war mages trained in the support of the Legions. All facets of warfare are accounted for in Legionary operations, and the Legion employs large numbers of highly-specialized troops in conjunction with one another to achieve victory, among them archers, light and heavy cavalry, battlemages, and healers.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-RQOR-17)</sup> Imperial Battlemages are often mixed with other legions, and it is standard for each legion cohort to include a cadre of spellcasters,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-CATLA2-18)</sup> but also form cohorts under a Battlemage Palatine, who acts as the commander of the cohort.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Imperial organizations, such as the Imperial Society, requisition legionnaire support as needed for their endeavors.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TROK-20)</sup> A special Legion platoon comprises the guard of any Duke in the province of Morrowind, the Duke of Vvardenfell, for instance. These soldiers are among the best the Legion has to offer; the most accomplished soldiers of the Duke's guard have the honor of serving as personal Palace guard to the King of Morrowind.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> The Legion was also known to have an expeditionary force, that was known to have put down tribal uprisings in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

The Legion's versatility is tied to its non-discriminatory recruitment. The Legion recruits individuals of all races and creeds into its ranks and benefits from the diversity of skills and backgrounds they bring (though Colovians form the majority). The Legion's strict standards for recruitment prioritise endurance, soldierly virtue, and trustworthiness, among other qualities. [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) were known to be a large part of the Imperial garrison of [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> In the Second Era, Lyris Titanborn, a half- [giant](Lore_Giant.md), was a member of the Imperial Legion and despite experiencing discrimination from some soldiers she rose quickly through the ranks due to her strength and height, eventually serving Emperor Varen Aquilarios personally.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> The Legion is also one of the few groups to treat the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) well<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>, with them forming a large number of soldiers in the Legion due to their natural strength and aptitude for smithing.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Disaffected [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) also joined the Legion to resist the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) in the Fourth Era.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-LF-28)</sup> [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) such as General [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md), who was one of Tiber Septim's closest subbordinates, also have played a major role in the Imperial Legion<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>, the assassin [Dram](Lore_Dram.md) is another example of a high ranking Dunmer in the Legion.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-RGArchive-30)</sup> [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) form a large part of the Legion too, with the Skyrim Legions being mostly made up of Nords, with the Nords benefiting from the Empire's protection and prosperous trade. In exchange, the people of Cyrodiil sleep peacefully, knowing their northern border is guarded by some of the fiercest warriors in Tamriel.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> The Legion has also counted [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), such as [Dragonne Papré](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonne_Papr%C3%A9) and [Nahfahlaar](Lore_Nahfahlaar.md), within its ranks, the latter of these being employed to devastating effects in wars and the former being stationed in the [Imperial Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup>

Service in the Legion is considered a model of Imperial citizenship, and as such requires a high standard of excellence. Influential Imperial military families would often send their children to be trained in legionary academies, such as the [Topal Legionary Academy](Lore_Topal_Legionary_Academy.md), where they would be trained to be elite soldiers.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup> Legionaries are typically expected to be at the peak of physical fitness in order to weather the rigors of melee combat and long marches in heavy equipment, demonstrate mastery of blades, polearms, and blunt weaponry, and have great skill in utilizing heavy armor as well as hand-shields.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OL-6)</sup>

Legions are known to have their own marching songs,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup> and also have skalds (war bards) that train in the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup> [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) is the Patron of the Imperial Legion.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-MWImperial-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-37)</sup>

## History
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-icon-banner-Guild_Banner_32.png)
### The Alessian Legions
The first incarnation of the Imperial Legion arose under the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when it was known as the Alessian Legion.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-PAPAHS-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-39)</sup> Legends speak of the legions led by [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) when the man-bull took the White Gold Tower.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-CHOA-40)</sup> The Legion sprang from the hardy rugged stock of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and the sophisticated battlemages of [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md). United, the warriors of Cyrodiil were a force to be reckoned with.

The Alessian army spent most of the third century of the First Era securing Alessian control over [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), slowly overcoming [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) holdouts despite their magical prowess and often ingenious tactics.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-DOTN-41)</sup> The Alessians fought many [Wars](Lore_Wars.md) throughout the First Era; many cultures have legends speaking of the seemingly unstoppable legions of Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) circa [1E 1030](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1030).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TLORE-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-LL-43)</sup> Nevertheless, the Legion's fortunes have always waxed and waned along with the Empire. When the leadership of the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) succumbed to internal squabbling, the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) began,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup> a decade-long civil war which no doubt pitted legionnaire against legionnaire.

### Reman Era Legions
Once the province was reunited again under the god-hero [Reman I](Lore_Reman.md) circa [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), his legions spent the last two centuries of the First Era reestablishing an Empire of Man in Tamriel.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-2920L-45)</sup> The Imperial Legion as it is known in modern times first appeared under the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), thanks to the influence of the [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). Their weaponry, methods, and rigid discipline turned the Imperial Legion into the unparalleled fighting force which was able to conquer every region of mainland Tamriel (save for Morrowind). By the end of the era, the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) was the only modern-day province which had no Legion presence.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup> A known member of a Reman era Legion was [Luciana Pullo](Lore_Luciana_Pullo.md), a battlemage and lieutenant during the Akaviri troubles and Valenwood annexation.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-46)</sup>

In [1E 2707](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2707), construction of Fort Redmane began under General Nemenius Hestor (commander of the Tenth Legion). General Hestor's original plans called for a powerful keep to be called Fort Niben, but construction proceeded fitfully. In the early years of Emperor Reman's reign, Cyrodiil's resources were sorely strained by the need to subdue threats all across Tamriel and so Khajiit brigandage in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) simply did not rise to the same level as other threats. Frustrated by the parsimony of his superiors, General Hestor schemed to bring the threat posed by Anequinan raiders to their attention. He settled upon a fierce but formerly obscure Khajiit clan-chief named Hunzur-ri, and built him up as the leader of a wild horde of "beast-men" slavering for human blood. The general went so far as to name Hunzur-ri "the Red Mane," and describe him as a fanatical religious leader who had declared a holy crusade to drive men out of the Trans-Niben region altogether. General Hestor's plan worked and exaggerating the threat of the Red Mane caused gold to flood in to build the keep Hestor demanded to secure the border. In the meantime, Hunzur-ri found himself harassed by his fellow hunt-barons for "provoking" the Imperials. Infuriated by the fabrications flowing from General Hestor's reports, Hunzur-ri gathered a small band of loyal followers and crossed the Niben to seek out his foe—a dozen warriors in a single stealthy raid, not the rampaging horde he was said to command. Not an hour's march from the fort, Hunzur-ri and his warriors ambushed General Hestor. The general was killed by the very Khajiit he had vilified, but Hunzur-ri in turn was cut down by Hestor's soldiers. The Imperial Legion chose to describe Nemenius Hestor's death as a heroic last stand, in which the general bravely gave his own life to end the threat of "the Red Mane." A few years later, Fort Niben was renamed Fort Redmane in honor of the Legion's fierce adversary. Ironically, by the time the fort was completed, the troublesome raids that had impelled General Hestor to fortify the area in the first place had come to an end as the Khajiit kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine were already under Imperial control.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-47)</sup>

#### Blackwater War
The [Blackwater War](Lore_Blackwater_War.md) occurred between [1E 2811](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2811) - [1E 2837](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2837). A military expansion ordered by [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md), which seized the northern and eastern marshes of Argonia, resulting in the formation of the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) Province of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). While the Imperial forces won several decisive battles in the early stages, their progress quickly stagnated due to the brutal terrain, endless raids, disease, and mysterious disappearances. After several failed campaigns, each employing wildly differing tactics, the conflict declined into a protracted and complex guerrilla war with no clear victor in sight. Ultimately, tribal infighting on the part of the Argonians finally brought the war to a close. Rather than an official armistice, the war simply ended when the Argonians abruptly buried their weapons and went back to their day-to-day lives.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TBW-48)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-PGE3A-49)</sup>

- **Battle of Argonia**, 1E 2811. A one-sided affair with [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) suffering significant casualties. The survivors retreated to [Helstrom](Lore_Helstrom.md), the impenetrable center of the Province.
- **Xi-tsei Massacre**, Last Seed 14, 1E 2820. The remnants of General Bucco's fourth legion were annihilated by a massive Argonian war party at the foot of an ancient Argonian pyramid.
- **Great Burn**, Rain's Hand, 1E 2828. In an attempt to neutralize the inhospitable terrain, Elissia Mallicius ordered a unit of sappers to set fire to a peat bog outside of [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md). Unbeknownst to the Legion, the fire spread through a vast underground network and within months the entire province was ablaze. Hundreds of species of unique flora and fauna were rendered extinct and entire tribes of Argonians were lost. The Legion suffered significant casualties, this marked the end of their second campaign.

#### Four-Score War
The [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) took place between [1E 2840](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2840) - [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920) and was centered around the border regions of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), with the armies of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) facing the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) -led forces of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). The war was initiated by [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md) after he had successfully taken over [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Reman II died fighting the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) in [1E 2843](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2843) and the war was continued by his successors [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md) and [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md).

The war ended when [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) signed the Cervant Truce with [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md). As a result, Morrowind remained the only Tamrielic nation independent of the Second Empire until its collapse in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430).

- **Battle of Bodrum**, 20th of First Seed, [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920). Forces led by Vivec ambushed the Cyrodilic army and almost completely destroyed it.
- **Battle of Ald Marak**, 16th of Mid Year, [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920). A heavily armored army led by Imperial Crown Prince [Juilek](Lore_Juilek.md) took the Dunmer by surprise due to faulty intelligence on their part. Commanding the mystics in the Legion to cast water-breathing spells, his army passed through Lake Coronati, with the majority of the Dunmer army commanded by Vivec being unable to respond to the attack in time.
- **Battle of Black Gate**, Sun's Height, [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920). While a truce between the warring parties had been called, the Legion sacked the fortress of [Black Gate](Lore_Black_Gate.md).

Under the [Akaviri Potentates](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) at the beginning of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Imperial Legion achieved something unthinkable: they became the only fighting force in the Empire. In [2E 283](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_283), when faced with widespread rebellion, Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) declared martial law, demanding that all of his vassals dissolve their armies. After thirty-seven years and enormous sacrifice, the Imperial Legion had quelled all who did not obey, wiping out any organized resistance to them within the Empire. They were the only army in the land.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-HOTFG-50)</sup>

### Puppets of Pretenders
During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the Second Era, after the dissolution of the Second Empire, the Imperial Legion became the instrument of various warlords in Cyrodiil, notably the infamous [Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) family. Many disparaged the Legion around Tamriel during this time, claiming that it no longer exemplified the superb qualities it had in the past.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-51)</sup> At the beginning of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), all Legions were recalled to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md),<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-IRO-52)</sup> but many couldn't make it to [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md) and instead set up isolated camps throughout the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) as the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) raged around them.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESO-53)</sup> Legionaries who were in the Imperial City when the Anchors began to drop were given the option to swear fealty to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) by drinking the "[Blood of Coldharbour](Lore_Blood_of_Coldharbour.md)", a poison that would slowly turn the imbibers into [Mind-Shriven](Lore_Mind-Shriven.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-54)</sup> Many instead chose death, but those who turned traitor formed a new regiment called [Legion Zero](Lore_Legion_Zero.md).<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-55)</sup> Legion Zero cooperated with the [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) occupiers, and transformed the Elven Gardens District into a workshop for constructing siege equipment for the Daedric forces.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-DFI-56)</sup>

A group of loyalist legion soldiers led by Captain [Anatolius Caudex](Lore_Anatolius_Caudex.md), known as the [Immortal Eight](Lore_Immortal_Eight.md), created an improvised garrison in the middle of the [Nobles District](Lore_Nobles_District.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), where they fought against the forces of Molag Bal and against Legion Zero.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-57)</sup> They also played a part in stopping Bal's plans to snuff out the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md).<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-58)</sup> Captain Caudex had previously defended Fort Warden from the three Alliances, being the last man to leave the fort, stating that the *"Rebels must learn that the Empire is invincible. So we must be invincible."*<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-59)</sup>

Elsewhere in Tamriel, Legion troops under the command of the Tharns became engaged in relatively minor campaigns to annex surrounding regions, with little long-term success. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) troops under [Javad Tharn](Lore_Javad_Tharn.md) entered [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) territory in a failed attempt to annex the [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md) region south of Colovia.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESO-53)</sup> Under Magus-General [Septima Tharn](Lore_Septima_Tharn.md), the [Seventh Legion](Lore_Seventh_Legion.md) became engaged in operations across much of Tamriel, often with very little support.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-60)</sup> An example of this was when the Seventh Legion entered [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) territory in a failed attempt to annex [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESO-53)</sup> The Legion's leadership was encouraged to act ruthlessly, and they often engaged in abductions, as well as summary executions of disloyal soldiers, disobedient prisoners, or recalcitrant conquered civilians.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-61)</sup>

In [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), during the [Frostfall Coup](Lore_The_Frostfall_Coup.md) of [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), the [Cygnus Irregulars](Lore_Cygnus_Irregulars.md) feigned loyalty to [Euraxia Tharn](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md) due to being outnumbered by her Nibenese mercenaries.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-calo-62)</sup> However, they often sabotaged her plans as well as being outspoken on the invasion of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md). Later in [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581), Prefect Calo anticipated that the Irregulars would eventually work alongside Lord Gharesh-ri against the Euraxians and so had been training them in preparation for joining their forces.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-letter-63)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Cygnus Irregulars were pardoned by Lord Gharesh-ri and formed an alliance with Queen Khamira against Euraxia Tharn. Following this, the Irregulars helped at the Dov-Vahl Shrine, standing guard against any Euraxian reinforcements while the [Dragonhorn](Lore_Dragonhorn.md) was recovered. The Irregulars assisted the Northern Elsweyr Defense Force at the Moon Gate of Anequina, holding off the Euraxians and [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) from Jode's Core.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-quest-64)</sup> After the defeat of the Euraxians and dragons in [Northern Elsweyr](Lore_Northern_Elsweyr.md), the surviving Irregulars and Prefect Calo attended the royal coronation of Queen Khamira. She accepted the assistance of the Cygnus Irregulars to help Elsweyr as engineers and aide workers.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-calo-62)</sup>

A group of all-female legionaries known as the Diamond's Daughters intended to take part in the [Dragonstar Arena](Lore_Dragonstar_Arena.md) in order to bring Imperial law and order back to Craglorn.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-65)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-66)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/LG-cardart-Septim_Guardsman.png/200px-LG-cardart-Septim_Guardsman.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Septim_Guardsman.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Septim_Guardsman.png) A Septim Empire legion guard In the region of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) in Cyrodiil, hundreds of former Imperial legionaries stationed in Blackwood had received neither pay nor orders from Cyrodiil in months, with their units having been effectively disbanded. These legionaries were recruited by the Council of Legates in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) to become part of the Ivory Brigade by offering to make good on their back pay and confirming their role as protectors of the province. These legionaries acted as the core of the Ivory Brigade, with native Blackwooders forming a militia around them, these Blackwooders provided knowledge of the terrain, people, and conditions of Blackwood, while the veteran legionaries taught the militia military discipline and Imperial fighting skills.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-67)</sup>

### The Tiber War
In [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852), the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) began, and nothing would ever be the same. In the dead of winter, General [Talos](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) led a small army from Cyrodiil to take the mighty fortress of [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) from the allied [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) - [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) forces which had occupied it. The Cyrodilic army was small, poorly trained and outfitted, short on rations and unprepared for winter campaigning. And yet, the man who would become known as Tiber Septim led them to victory.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-BOST-68)</sup>

The Battle of Sancre Tor was just the beginning of a renaissance for the Imperial Legion. With his invincible General Talos, King [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) was able to unite the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) in a year, and soon thereafter in [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854) took the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), taking the title of Emperor. He was soon killed, and Emperor Tiber Septim took his place. His ever-loyal soldiers solidified his hold on Cyrodiil and began invasions on Skyrim and High Rock,<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TAH-69)</sup> after which young men in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) began to flock to Septim's armies.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-70)</sup> With Septim's rule in High Rock, Skyrim, and Cyrodiil solidified, Septim attacked [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-RGAttrebus-71)</sup>

The Legion suffered its share of tribulations while forging the Empire of Tamriel. In the Battle of Black Rocks in [2E 863](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_863), the Aldmeri Dominion almost destroyed the Third Legion<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-RGAttrebus-71)</sup> and the initially smooth annexation of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) the following year was disrupted by the [Stros M'Kai Uprising](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai_Uprising.md).<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TAH-69)</sup> For a brief time during the Moth Rebellion, Tiber Septim was driven out of the Imperial City and the Legion within the city was besieged.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-RGAttrebus-71)</sup> And Tiber's generals positively dreaded a campaign against the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of Morrowind (though they were spared from attempting to conquer the entire province thanks to the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md)).<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-OM-72)</sup>

One by one, the provinces were brought into the fold. The [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) had never been successfully invaded by the Legion, though they were reportedly more than capable of repulsing elven incursions.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-73)</sup> But finally, in [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), Septim's use of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) brought the homeland of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) under his control, making him the first true Emperor of Tamriel.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TAH-69)</sup>

### The Third Empire
Under the Septim Empire, the Imperial Legion regained its reputation as a model military force.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-MUT-74)</sup> From Morrowind to the Summerset Isles, the Legion helped keep the peace. It had to manage a great deal of insurrection, the many major wars of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md),<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-BHOTE-75)</sup> and piracy,<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-CDJ-76)</sup> not to mention the disastrous invasion of Akavir circa [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup> They developed and maintained a system of garrisons throughout Tamriel, manning and supplying each in accordance with the threats they faced (though some questioned the significance of these threats given the exorbitant cost of maintaining this system).<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-TEP-77)</sup> During this time, they were known for their close association with the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), which frequently provided services in their forts.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-FMGAE-78)</sup> The Legion maintained a strong presence in Morrowind throughout the Third Era, and it is believed that [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), before his defeat at the hands of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), was preparing for an extended campaign against them utilizing assassination and terror.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-DUP-79)</sup>

At some point in the Third Era, the Imperial Watch came into possession of a section of pillar carved by the Ayleids and infused with significant [destructive](Lore_Destruction.md) power. The Imperials attempted to harness some degree of this magical power by bringing the stone to the summit of [Cloud Top](Lore_Cloud_Top.md) in the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md). Wizards from the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) were brought in to tune the stone to react to shock magic, and the Imperials achieved some measure of success at harnessing this power. The site was ultimately abandoned due to the danger posed by the stone's reaction.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-EN-80)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-81)</sup>

#### The Great War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/LG-quest-The_Siege_of_the_Imperial_City_03.jpg/200px-LG-quest-The_Siege_of_the_Imperial_City_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Siege_of_the_Imperial_City_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Siege_of_the_Imperial_City_03.jpg) Siege of the Imperial City [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png/200px-LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png) Battle of the Red Ring The [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) rose to power again in the Summerset Isle, and the Legion could not stop them from reforming the Aldmeri Dominion. The Thalmor eventually delivered an ultimatum to [Emperor Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) demanding a series of enormous concessions. When the Emperor refused, the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) began.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup> The Aldmeri Dominion initially invaded deep into the heart of Cyrodiil, capturing [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) and [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) rapidly before the Legion could mobilize an effective defense. More Dominion armies invaded into Hammerfell, bypassing western Cyrodiil entirely. When they realized the weakness of the Legion, however, the Aldmeri Dominion grew bolder and mobilized an offensive against Cyrodiil as a whole, intending to bring the entire Empire under their control. Quickly, [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) and the Imperial City were the only cities that remained under Legion control in Cyrodiil. Meanwhile, the legions in Hammerfell were pressed hard and forced to retreat, leaving the entire southern half of Hammerfell to fall to the Dominion. The Imperial City itself came under siege. Though relieved by several legions of hardened Nord legionaries, the Emperor ultimately gave the order to break the siege and abandon the city. An entire Legion sacrificed itself to defend the Emperor's rear.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup>

At this time, the Imperial Palace was sacked, plundered, and burned with the Aldmeri Dominion seeking an Imperial surrender. While the Emperor strung them along, he recalled all the legions defending Hammerfell and rearmed and refreshed the Skyrim and Cyrodilic legions. However, General Decianus allowed a great number of "invalids" to be discharged from the Legions before they marched east in an effort to drive the Altmeri back from [Skaven](Lore_Skaven.md). These veterans formed the core of the army that eventually drove the Altmeri General [Arannelya's](Lore_Arannelya.md) forces back across the Alik'r late in 174, with the Dominion forces taking heavy losses on their retreat from harassing attacks by the Alik'r warriors.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup> This successfully duped the Thalmor into believing the entire army was still fighting them there.

Martius Silius was a commander in the Imperial Legion during the war, however he and his men got separated from their legion. They ended up deep in Aldmeri-controlled territory and decided to make as much trouble there as they could. During the winter they raided camps at night, and attacked caravans bringing supplies to the front. This went on for weeks, until they rejoined the Imperial forces just in time for the Battle of the [Red Ring](Lore_Red_Ring_Road.md). They were just a dozen left at that point, but they fought valiantly and helped retake the Imperial City.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-106)</sup> During the Battle of the Red Ring the Emperor stormed south with his refreshed legions as the Hammerfell legions came in from the west. The Aldmeri Dominion, caught by surprise, was attacked by the Hammerfell legions from the west, while the Skyrim legions swept east and south and the Emperor, along with his personal Cyrodilic legions, assaulted the Imperial City, surrounding it on three sides and fending off Dominions piecemeal reinforcements and blocking their retreat from the Imperial City. They retook the Imperial City and all of Cyrodiil.

In the end, the Legion was victorious, though exhausted, no Legion had more than half its forces fit for duty, and Two Legions of soldiers had been effectively annihilated, not including the loss of the Eighth Legion in the retreat from the Imperial City the previous year. But the Emperor believed that with this victory was the best time to negotiate peace with the Dominion and put an official end to the war, resulting in the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) being signed, which essentially gave in to all the Thalmor's initial demands.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup> Hammerfell, however, was unwilling to concede defeat and the loss of so much of their territory, and so, in order to preserve the peace with the Dominion, the Emperor officially renounced Hammerfell as part of the Empire.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup><sup>[[100]](#cite_note-TIC-100)</sup>

#### Skyrim's Civil War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-A_Land_Divided_02.jpg) A battle between the Legion and the Stormcloaks In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Jarl [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) killed [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md), the High King of Skyrim, in a duel, leading to the outbreak of a [civil war](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in the province.

The Imperial Legion's efforts to quell the rebellion were hindered by the Emperor's unwillingness to send legionaries to Skyrim, as doing so would weaken Cyrodiil's defenses. As a result, the Imperial Legion in Skyrim received very few reinforcements and was forced to recruit locally. General [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md) was sent to Skyrim to quell the rebellion and managed to turn things around for the Empire in the war. On the 15th of Last Seed,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> less than a year after the start of the conflict,<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> Ulfric and his men were ambushed by the Legion outside [Darkwater Crossing](Lore_Darkwater_Crossing.md) in southern Eastmarch. The legion captured Ulfric and his men along with a horse thief from Rorikstead and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who was apprehended attempting to cross the border with Cyrodiil. The prisoners were then carted for two days until they arrived in [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md) for execution.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-SRRalof-107)</sup>

Before Ulfric could be executed, the dragon [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) attacked Helgen, and Ulfric escaped, signaling the return of dragons to Skyrim. The Empire, Stormcloaks, and Thalmor were all unprepared for the return of the dragons. The ongoing civil war meant many cities and settlements were unable to commit resources to defending against the dragons.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-Jouane-108)</sup> Afterward, the war escalated, with [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) across Skyrim solidifying their allegiance to either the Empire or the Stormcloaks.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Skyrim-109)</sup>

## Armor, Arms and Transport
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/LG-cardart-Imperial_Siege_Engine.png/175px-LG-cardart-Imperial_Siege_Engine.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Imperial_Siege_Engine.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Imperial_Siege_Engine.png) Imperial siege engine [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/LG-cardart-Imperial_Armor.png/200px-LG-cardart-Imperial_Armor.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Imperial_Armor.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Imperial_Armor.png) One of the many variants of legion armor <center>*The Akaviri's style of armorsmithing changed the Imperial legions irrevocably. It took a great deal of time, of course, but no one can dispute their influence.*
— [Verita Numida](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Verita_Numida)</center> Legionaries are specially outfitted depending on a large number of factors, including climate, location, task, and expectations. They employed armors of chain, scale, plate mail, and gender-neutral skirts in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), heavy steel plate overlying chain mail in the capital province of Cyrodiil during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), and lighter leather, chainmail, and steel plate armors in Skyrim during the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md). Battlemages tend to wear hoods, healers wear light padded armor, guardsmen on loan to nobles wear the vestments of their town, city, county, or hold, and rangers and foresters tend to wear fur or leather apparel in addition to their Legionary armor. Dragonscale armor and shields are also used, with the craft coming from Akavir and being implemented since the Reman Empire.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup> In addition, the uniform may vary depending on the culture of the local area or province.

Legion foot soldiers mainly use Imperial swords, steel swords, and Imperial bows.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-110)</sup> However, other weapons are often used as well, such as broadswords, shortswords, spears, pikes, claymores, axes, daggers, warhammers, maces, and [staffs](Lore_Staff.md).<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-111)</sup><sup>[[112]](#cite_note-112)</sup><sup>[[113]](#cite_note-113)</sup> The Legion mass-produces crossbows that are used by its missile troopers alongside short and long bows.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-114)</sup>

The Imperial Legion also employs siege machines such as catapults, battering rams, trebuchets, and ballistae.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-115)</sup><sup>[[116]](#cite_note-116)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-DFI-56)</sup>

The [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) based their armor designs on Reman Era Legion armor, with their chest pieces as an example.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-117)</sup> The [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) also based their armor design on that of the Second Era legions.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-118)</sup>

The Imperial Legion also utilizes different types of cavalry, with the main type being horses. Many breeds of horses are used by the Imperial Legion, such as the Bay horse.<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-119)</sup>. However, other animals have been mounted by Legion cavalrymen throughout history as well. The bear cavalry first came into use by 10th Legion in [2E 23](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_23).<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-APBear-120)</sup> [Camels](Lore_Camel.md) have been ridden in Hammerfell, [guar](Lore_Guar.md) in Morrowind,<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-121)</sup> sabre cats in Skyrim,<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-122)</sup> and wolves in High Rock.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-123)</sup> When Empress Hestra led her Legions in harrying the Vampire King Styriche and his Gray Host into western Hammerfell, it's said she did so while riding a great flame atronach bear.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-Flame Bear-124)</sup> In the event of a crisis behind enemy lines, a legionnaire may sell excess material to non-combatants. Such material must have no significant military application. Gold acquired in this manner must be used to secure passage back to the legion.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-Gratas-125)</sup>

## Known Legions
- **[Legion Zero](Lore_Legion_Zero.md)** — A traitorous Imperial Legion that swore fealty to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) during his invasion of the Imperial City, becoming [Mind-Shriven](Lore_Mind-Shriven.md).<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-126)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/ON-crown_store-Second_Legion_Arms_Pack.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Second_Legion_Arms_Pack.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Second_Legion_Arms_Pack.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Second_Legion_Arms_Pack.jpg) Second Legion style weapons [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/LG-cardart-Healing_Hands.png/150px-LG-cardart-Healing_Hands.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Healing_Hands.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Healing_Hands.png) Legion healer [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/LG-cardart-Moonmoth_Castellan.png/200px-LG-cardart-Moonmoth_Castellan.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Moonmoth_Castellan.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Moonmoth_Castellan.png) Moonmoth Legion Castellan - **[Second Legion](Lore_Second_Legion.md)** — Led by General Nipia, the Second Legion accompanied Varen Aquilarios during the Colovian Revolt, and were headquartered in the vicinity of Bruma during peacetime. After Leovic was overthrown in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), the Second Legion pursued the Icereach Coven of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), who had worked alongside Leovic, into [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) where many legionaries fled due to the effects of Mother [Ciannait](Lore_Ciannait.md)'s magic. The Optio in charge of this particular regiment was prepared to continue the chase if the High Command of the Second Legion wished for it, but advised the Legate to allow legionaries to return to Cyrodiil. <sup>[[127]](#cite_note-MTCMC-127)</sup> The Imperial War Mastiff is their mascot and symbol.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-128)</sup>

- **Third Legion** — Also known as the **Faithful Third**. It helped put down the Moth Rebellion in the Imperial City in [2E 857](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_857) alongside [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s honor guard. In [2E 863](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_863), the forces of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) caught the Third Legion by surprise and almost destroyed it. The remnants of it formed the core of the newly created Ruby Legion stationed in the [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md). The [Sack of Senchal](Lore_Tiber_Wars.md#Sack_of_Senchal) was carried out by the Third Legion. [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(soldier).md) was a known member.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-RGAttrebus-71)</sup>

- **Fourth Legion** — Led by General Augurius Bucco in [1E 2811](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2811), it participated in the [Blackwater War](Lore_The_Blackwater_War.md). The Fourth Legion was completely decimated on 14 Last Seed, [1E 2820](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2820). Under General Regulus Sardecus, the Fourth Legion reformed circa [1E 2823](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2823), regaining all the territory that had been lost in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-129)</sup> A deserter from this legion worked against the forces of Molag Bal during his invasion of the Imperial City in 2E 582, actively destroying the enemy's siege weapons.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-QR-130)</sup>

- **[Fifth Legion](Lore_Fifth_Legion.md)** — Led by Captain Lampronius circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), who trained recruits. The Fifth Legion was recreated in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) and participated in the Invasion of Akavir as one of the initial landing legions. Fifth Legion Porter, an alcoholic beverage, is named after them.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup>

- **[Sixth Legion](Lore_Sixth_Legion.md)** — Little is known of the Sixth Legion, with only one known member, Antonia Gratas, who was stranded in [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) and followed Legion protocol by selling any surplus equipment to secure passage back to her Legion.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-Gratas-125)</sup>

- **[Seventh Legion](Lore_Seventh_Legion.md)** — Briefly took control of Southern Bangkorai during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Later participated in the Invasion of Akavir in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) as one of the initial landing legions.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup> In the Fourth Era, the Seventh Legion and Fifteenth Legion defended Orsinium.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOS-10)</sup>

- **Eighth Legion** — Fought a desperate and doomed rearguard action on the walls of the Imperial City in [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174), while Titus II broke out of the city to the north with his main army.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup>

- **[Ninth Legion](Lore_Ninth_Legion.md)** — Part of the first group of Imperial troops sent to Black Marsh, the legion was lost in Tsofeer Cavern in Murkmire.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-LON-131)</sup> The Ninth Legion was recreated sometime before [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) and participated in the Invasion of Akavir as reinforcements.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup>

- **[Tenth Legion](Lore_Tenth_Legion.md)** – Riders of the first Imperial bear cavalry in [2E 23](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_23), who would parade their bear mounts Leyawiin's streets each week.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-APBear-120)</sup><sup>[[132]](#cite_note-APBear Cub-132)</sup> Participated in the Invasion of Akavir in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) as one of the initial landing legions. Uriel V and the Tenth Legion made a final stand at the Battle of [Ionith](Lore_Ionith.md) in 3E 290, allowing remnants of the Fourteenth Legion to reach [Septimia](Lore_Septimia.md). Two survivors of the Tenth Legion reported that they saw the death of Uriel V.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup> The Tenth Legion was recreated sometime before the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) with Legate Justianus Quintius serving as their commanding officer; they fought in both [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup>

- **Twelfth Legion** – The Twelfth Legion was involved in the defense of the Imperial City during the Umbriel Crisis.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOS-10)</sup><sup>:339</sup>

- **[Thirteenth Legion](Lore_Thirteenth_Legion.md)** – Led by General Meric Renmus, [Emperor Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) sent the Thirteenth Legion to [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) shortly after becoming emperor to help restore order after the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) had ravaged the area. The legion defended the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) as they rebuilt and formed a city council alongside the Khajiit. The legion became known as the "[Shields of Senchal](Lore_Shields_of_Senchal.md)".<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-133)</sup>

- **Fourteenth Legion** – Participated in the Invasion of Akavir in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) as one of the initial landing legions.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup>

- **Fifteenth Legion** – A Legion involved with defending [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOS-10)</sup>

- **Seventeenth Legion** – Participated in the Invasion of Akavir in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) as a reinforcement legion.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RDAI-16)</sup>

- **Eighteenth Legion** – Captain Evernal was a known member of the Eighteenth Legion, with him having a badge displaying this fact. Evernal was later a part of a group of Imperial Regulators outside of the city of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), where they searched visitors for contraband such as Moon Sugar.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-TIC-100)</sup>

- **[Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md)** – Referred to as a "shadow legion", the Imperial Guard served as the personal protectors of the Emperor. [General Warhaft](Lore_Warhaft.md) was a known member.

- **[Imperial Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md)** - Well-trained war mages serving in the Imperial Legion.

- **[Cygnus Irregulars](Lore_Cygnus_Irregulars.md)** – A cohort of the Imperial Legion that mostly consisted of military engineers, scouts, and trainers.

- **Legion of Chorrol** – A Legion based in Chorrol circa [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576) that participated in [Varen's Rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md) and helped to overthrow [Emperor Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md).<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-134)</sup>

- **Legion of the West Weald** – An Imperial Legion from [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), led by General Lavinia in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). In 2E 582, the legion invaded the [South Weald](Lore_Northern_Woods.md) to annex the region and captured [Arenthia](Lore_Arenthia.md).<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-YFTT-135)</sup> This Legion included remnants of the Third Cohort that remained stationed in Skingrad following the Soulburst.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-136)</sup>

- **Deathshead Legion** – An Imperial Legion based in [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), led by General Darius. It consisted primarily of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-137)</sup>

- **Hawkmoth Legion** – An Imperial Legion based in [Castle Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), led by Knight Protector Frald the White.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-138)</sup>

- **Moonmoth Legion** - An Imperial Legion based in Fort Moonmoth in [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), led by Radd Hard-Heart.<sup>[[139]](#cite_note-139)</sup>

- **Diamond Marines** – A division that participated in the [Blackwater War](Lore_The_Blackwater_War.md).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TBW-48)</sup>

- **Longhouse Legions** – The name given to the legions that remained loyal to the Longhouse Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) that fought against the forces of [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) during Varen's Rebellion.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-LLBF-140)</sup>

- **Scavenger Legion** – An Imperial Legion based in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Hat Scav Leg-141)</sup>

## Imperial Legion Ranks

### General Officers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/SR-quest-Joining_the_Legion_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Joining_the_Legion_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Joining_the_Legion_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Joining_the_Legion_02.jpg) Imperial General - **Grand Marshal**<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-LTTGMOTIL-142)</sup><sup>[[143]](#cite_note-143)</sup>
- **General**, commanders of one or multiple legions.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-Darius-144)</sup><sup>[[145]](#cite_note-Tullius-145)</sup><sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TGW-105)</sup> One source notes that one general attends meetings of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) to forward any of the council's decisions to the other generals and the Legionary's Council, where all military matters are discussed.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-146)</sup> Magical projections are used to quickly pass orders around if no battlemages are available to do so. - **Aide–de–Camp**, acts as a secretary to an Imperial general, in charge of recruiting and spreading information and orders.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-YFTT-135)</sup>
- **Commander**, described as the immediate superiors of legion captains.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-147)</sup><sup>[[148]](#cite_note-148)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GFMOM-8)</sup> They have the authority to relieve serving legion captains of their duties and reassign them to different postings, which includes sub-contracting them to other local guard forces for the remainder of their legion contract.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GFMOM-8)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBTaking Care Lex-14)</sup>
- **Legate**, general officers often associated with politics. Legates are below the rank of general and often command legions.<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-149)</sup><sup>[[150]](#cite_note-150)</sup>

- **Tribune**, serve in each legion below the rank of legate.<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-152)</sup><sup>[[153]](#cite_note-153)</sup>

Prefect Calo led his own cohort known as the [Cygnus Irregulars](Lore_Cygnus_Irregulars.md).<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-calo-62)</sup> [Ephrem Benirus](Lore_Ephrem_Benirus.md) was a known governing prefect of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md).<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-154)</sup>

- **Commandant**<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-160)</sup> - **Chapter Steward**, known to deal with recruitment

## Notes
- The Imperial Legion was inspired by the Roman Legions who were the supreme military power in Europe during classical antiquity.
- A 33rd cohort is mentioned in ESO, while Roman Legions were known to only have 10 in a single Legion. If there were 33 cohorts, the number of men would equal ~16,000 in a single Legion, instead of the standard ~5000 men in the Roman Empire.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AALL-15)</sup> - Battlemages of the "33<sup>rd</sup>" supposedly helped to banish Mehrunes Dagon at some point in Kvatch.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-182)</sup> However, it is unclear if this refers to a Thirty-third Legion, a Thirty-third Cohort that is part of any Legion, or something else entirely.
- The Tribunal God [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) referred to the Legion as "moths".<sup>[[182]](#cite_note-183)</sup>
- Officers of the Imperial Legion and knights of the various Imperial military orders were privileged to wear Templar Heavy Armor.<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-Codex-184)</sup><sup>[[184]](#cite_note-MW-185)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-UOL2-186)</sup>
- The [Red Templars](Lore_Red_Templars.md) (also known as the Red Dome Templars) were unstable crusaders who drank the blood of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) in order to obtain short-term martial shouting powers. They were hated by the rest of the Legion and a significant number of the members of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) wanted them dispersed. Due to this, they were mainly sent off to remote places like [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[185]](#cite_note-Po M-187)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-UOL4-188)</sup>
- | [^](#note_nb1) | *[TES V: Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)* is always hard-set to start on the 17th of Last Seed, 4E 201. After escaping Helgen with Ralof, he will explain to his sister, Gerdur, that the Stormcloaks were ambushed "two days ago". |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_nb2) | After escaping Helgen with Hadvar, if the [player-character](Skyrim_Prisoner.md) asks who the other prisoners were at the beheading, he will say that Ulfric Stormcloak "founded the Stormcloaks years ago" and that he murdered the High King "a few months ago". Several pieces of dialogue with other NPCs will refer to Torygg's death as "recent". |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_nb3) | Sometime between Torygg's death and the events of *Skyrim*, Jarl [Elisif the Fair](Lore_Elisif_the_Fair.md) has canceled the "Burning of King [Olaf](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md)" festival, held annually in Solitude, believing it would be in poor taste. This is presumably the first time the festival was to be celebrated since her husband [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md)'s death, and since his death, which occurred sometime after the previous festival, marked the start of the rebellion, the rebellion had started less than a year before Ulfric was captured and the events of the game begin (less than a year between both festivals). |
