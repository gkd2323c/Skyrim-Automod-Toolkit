# Mymophonus

Mymophonus was a scribe from the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), roughly three hundred years after Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md)'s reign.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAC-1)</sup> He was a lore writer, however, his work is criticized for not being entirely reliable to the source material. He often took real characters and situations, and distorted them in a way that they lost all meaning.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPP-2)</sup> Sometimes he credits himself as **Mymophonus the scribe**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATG-3)</sup>

One of Mymophonus' earliest books was *[Ark'ay The God](Lore_Ark%27ay_The_God.md)*, a story of how the [deity](Lore_Arkay.md) himself ascended into godhood.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATG-3)</sup> This book, in particular, was made an example of Mymophonus' credibility as a writer, with some calling it "invalidated".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPP-2)</sup> Sometime later, he wrote *[The Armorer's Challenge](Lore_The_Armorer%27s_Challenge.md)*, which tells the story of Empress Katariah's chosen armorsmith [Hazadir](Lore_Hazadir.md), who bested fellow craftsmen, [Sirollus Saccus](Lore_Sirollus_Saccus.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAC-1)</sup> One of his next books was the *[Myths of Sheogorath](Lore_Myths_of_Sheogorath.md)*, which was a collection of tales pertaining to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Madness, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MOS-4)</sup>

Many books from the Third and [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) such as the *[Ruminations on the Elder Scrolls](Lore_Ruminations_on_the_Elder_Scrolls.md)* written by [Septimus Signus](Lore_Septimus_Signus.md) and *[Redguards, Their History and Their Heroes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Redguards,_History_and_Heroes)* written by [Destri Melarg](Lore_Destri_Melarg.md) were transported back to the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) because of the [Gandranen Ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gandranen_Ruins)' magic in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Gandranen-5)</sup> Mymophonus' *Myths of Sheogorath* was one of these books<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MOS-4)</sup> but another book also penned by him called *the Athedorix Conundrums* became such a rare text that it was recovered and placed in a collection of other such books in the Library of Dusk, a compound in the daedric plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). It was a book of nine questions of ill-omen that would drive readers crazy with their answers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TLODRB-6)</sup>
