Clivia Tharn was the ruler of the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). A member of the prominent [Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) family, born to [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) and his seventh wife, [Pulasia](Lore_Pulasia_Tharn.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HTON-2)</sup> and Abnur's sixteenth daughter overall.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-3)</sup> She was also the widow of former Emperors [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) and [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md). Abnur had plans for her first child to inherit the throne,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mt C:CR-4)</sup> but by the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) she was "comfortably lodged in Mannimarco's pocket",<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-1)</sup> and had disappeared from the public eye months before the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONCouncilor Itinia-5)</sup>

## Biography
When she was 7-years-old, Clivia played a game of Imperial War-Chess with her younger relative [Euraxia](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md) and played an illegal move. Outraged, Euraxia taunted her by saying that she would one day be queen of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), but Clivia remained unimpressed, stating she would be the Empress. Her sister [Septima](Lore_Septima_Tharn.md) noted that while she lacked instinctive prowess in the magical and political arts, Abnur groomed her for the Ruby Throne because she had an innate ability to command others, and they would have no choice but to follow.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt C:CT-6)</sup> She married [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), and served as his consort. When Leovic was overthrown and killed in a violent [rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md) for legalizing [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), his usurper, Duke [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md), wed Clivia to consolidate his rule.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-3)</sup> When the [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md) embarked on a journey to locate the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), Abnur sent Clivia a letter instructing her to oversee the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) in his absence and saying that if [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)'s plan to relight the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) worked, her first child would inherit the whole of Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mt C:CR-4)</sup>

In [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), Varen mysteriously disappeared during a calamity known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md), and Clivia was quickly declared Empress Regent of the Empire in his stead.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TATOAM-7)</sup> This aroused widespread suspicions that Clivia and her father were responsible for Varen's disappearance, though it unbeknownst to even Clivia it was Mannimarco's doing. Mannimarco, who had orchestrated the Soulburst, became the power behind the Ruby Throne, turning Clivia to worship [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and manipulating her into expelling the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) so that his [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) could operate freely.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AL-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESO-9)</sup> When the three alliances invaded [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), they quickly conquered much of Cyrodiil and confined the Empire to only the Imperial City and [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md)'s environs.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCC-10)</sup> The alliances were unable to take the Imperial City, and so Clivia continued to rule, but despite this, whenever one of the alliances gained supremacy over the other two alliances, they would choose an Emperor from their own ranks, although these Emperors were usually short-lived. This lasted until a [dark anchor](Lore_Dark_Anchor.md) opened over the Imperial City, heralding a Daedric invasion which quickly overran the Imperial forces, and resulting in the deaths and capture of many of its citizens who were unable to escape.

While absent from the Elder Council meeting at the start of the invasion,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MEC-11)</sup> she was in charge of commanding troop movements, though her nonsensical orders for General Velarius suggest she was being impersonated even at the time.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONDialogue-12)</sup> Elder Councilor [Itinia](Lore_Itinia.md) had not seen Empress Clivia for months even before the anchors fell, and had not seen her after.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONCouncilor Itinia-5)</sup> As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Clivia's fate is unknown; she is not among the known survivors of the Daedric invasion of the Imperial City, and one of Molag Bal's servants, [Molag Kena](Lore_Molag_Kena.md), impersonated her to gain access to the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in order to carry out the "[Planemeld Obverse](Lore_Planemeld.md)" ritual.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESO-9)</sup> It is unclear how long Kena had been impersonating Clivia.

The alliance spy networks tried to discover the true fate of Clivia Tharn.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LATSFAS-13)</sup> At some point after the defeat of the Molag Kena, Dhulef, a court mage of the [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) inquired his old associate, the leader of the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md), a [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) spy network, [Copper Dariah](Lore_Copper_Dariah.md) about the whereabouts of the Empress Regent. She responded in a cryptic manner: "Stop asking about the Tharns. They're graveyard dead, buried in the ground. We have enough unquiet corpses in the world. Why go making more?"<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LATD-14)</sup>

## Notes
- Though Septima says Clivia was the sister of both her and Euraxia,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt C:CT-6)</sup> Abnur states that Septima is his grand-niece/third cousin and Euraxia is his half-sister.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-1)</sup>
- One source described Clivia Tharn as "a descendant of the savage [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md)".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TATOAM-7)</sup>
- [Anatolius Caudex](Lore_Anatolius_Caudex.md) experienced a prophetic dream the night before the Imperial City fell. He saw the Empress Regent Clivia Tharn and her generals take Molag Bal's hand, heard creaking chains and smelled charred flesh.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CC-15)</sup>
