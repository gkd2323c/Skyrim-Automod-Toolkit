# Mycoturgy

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg/200px-SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg) A Telvanni tower Mycoturgy is a branch of magic involving control and manipulation of fungi. Practitioners of this arcane art are known as **Mycoturges**, **Fungimancers**, **Shroomtenders**, and **Sporecrafters**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FPB-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-NAch-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Spore Savant-4)</sup>

The Mycoturges are a group of specialized mages within the ranks of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), known for their abilities to grow giant mushrooms into any shape they desire, including towers,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kelesan.27ruhn-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ZOSCorrespondence-6)</sup> and creatures made of spores and fungi.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LA-1)</sup> Telvanni architecture is dominated by their wizards' tower, a fantastic organic form grown and sculpted from stems, caps, and root-like holdfasts of the native mushrooms. Telvanni villages are comprised of smaller mushroom pods hollowed out for their craftsmen and commoners.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GHOM-7)</sup> [Soul Gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) can be used to help the tower grow faster and to strengthen its foundations. The process can take several days, and is often assisted by the builders and overseen by the foremen.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MS-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LH-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GGM-10)</sup>

When creating creatures, Telvanni magisters use only the hardiest of the fire-hued fungi when magically coercing them into a new form.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SMSS-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SMSP-12)</sup> Archmagister [Nelos Otheri](Lore_Nelos_Otheri.md) was a known mycoturge, and a patron of his coterie of mycoturges, known to be the best in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). He was known to outline the specifications of the [Horse](Lore_Horse.md) -shaped fungi creature to his coterie, and was known for the creation of the Sadrith Mora Spore Steeds.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LA-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SMSS-11)</sup>

Though they kept their methods secret,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LS-13)</sup> practice of fungi-based magic was not limited to the members of the House Telvanni. Fungimancer's Prayer-Beads are magical beads, which, when invoked, can be used to form a circle of glowing mushrooms around the wielder.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FPB-2)</sup> Mycoturges often display intricate mushroom markings on their bodies using self-made mycelial inks.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MFM-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MBM-15)</sup> Achieving mastery grants them the honor of wearing bloodtooth toadstool fascinators as symbols of their skill and expertise in this unique art form.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-MF-16)</sup> Mycoturge retreats also inspired paintings.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MRPW-17)</sup>

[Ahzidal](Lore_Ahzidal.md), a great [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md) was also known to create fungal creatures, such as the Fungiflare Wolf, an ideal wolf mount for dangerous forests and caverns with a vicious temperament by design and nature—fungiflare mushrooms are lethal.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONFungiflare Wolf-18)</sup> Ahzidal made the Shadowcap Cave-Senche's azure glow the centerpiece of the unsettling feline mount, becoming prized by spelunkers as its bioluminescence can light up the darkest caverns without making a sound.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONShadowcap Cave Senche-19)</sup> Seeking to meld fire with fungi, Ahzidal created the first Embermould Warhorse, formed from char morels often used in stews, the mount can light a rider's way on the darkest winter nights.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONEmbermould Warhorse-20)</sup>

Integrating research left behind by Ahzidal, Mage [Naleldil](Lore_Naleldil.md) created animals to be used as pets from spores some time around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONGloomspore Bear Cub-21)</sup> Naledil was also a fungimancer and was known for a wide variety of creations. Her [Gloomspore](Lore_Gloomspore_Creature.md) [horses](Lore_Horse.md) were made with the usage of the giant lichen.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Gloom H-22)</sup> Addition of manic morels from [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) was known to make her creations more animated.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Gloom P-23)</sup> Naledil used [bleeding crowns](Lore_Bleeding_Crown.md) and spores known for their calming properties to create fungi [guars](Lore_Guar.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Gloom G-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Gloom GC-25)</sup> [mora tapinella](Lore_Mora_Tapinella.md) to create [bears](Lore_Bear.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Gloomspore_Bear-26)</sup> and [scaly pholiota](Lore_Scaly_Pholiota.md) to create [camels](Lore_Camel.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Gloomspore_Camel-27)</sup> Gloomspore Wolves were created from [hypha facia](Lore_Hypha_Facia.md), and some of their pups had oxblood fungus spores added to instill fierce guardianship in them.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Gloomspore_Wolf_.28mount.29-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Gloomspore_Wolf_Pup-29)</sup> She also created Gloomspore [Senches](Lore_Senche.md), and utilized thunder fungi in the process of their creation.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Gloomspore_Senche_Cub-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Gloomspore_Senche_.28mount.29-31)</sup> Some of them were inspired by the cult of [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Nighthaunt_Gloomspore_Senche-32)</sup> Gloomspore creatures also occur naturally in the caverns of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-GC-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Blackreach-34)</sup> Mycoturgy can be used to create Gloomspore Weapons.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Gloomspore_Style-35)</sup>

The [Wardens](Lore_Wardens.md) were also known for their ability to manipulate fungi with their magical practices.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOWarden-36)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-FG-37)</sup> Certain [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [Shamans](Lore_Reachfolk_Shamans.md) were known to study magical properties of fungi.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Bralthahawn-38)</sup>

## Notes
- Mycoturges should not be confused with mycologists, [alchemists](Lore_Alchemy.md) who specialise in mushrooms and fungi. Mycologists do not use sorcery, but may be employed to nurture and maintain existing Telvanni mushroom towers.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-40)</sup>
