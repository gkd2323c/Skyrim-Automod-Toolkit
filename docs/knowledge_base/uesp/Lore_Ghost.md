**Ghosts**, also known as **spirits**, are the disembodied [souls](Lore_Soul.md) of the deceased that for one reason or another are unable to move on to their respective afterlife. They are classified as being a form of [undead](Lore_Undead.md) known as the Returned. Most often, they remain on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) because they had some form of unfinished business they wanted to see completed before they moved on.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup> The deeds of a spirit in life can sometimes cloud their path to the afterlife. Such spirits, held back by guilt and unresolved affairs, might refuse entry into the afterlife even if they have been accepted into it, holding themselves back as long as their unresolved affairs remain. Being summoned to provide aid to the living, through lending their strength or inhabiting and animating bodies willingly, is said to be one of the ways through which spirits can resolve the issues that keep them from the afterlife, once employed by certain branches of necromancy such as that used by the Order of the Hidden Moon. In some cases these powerful unresolved emotions might cause the soul itself to crack and be wounded, allowing for a [Void](Lore_Void.md) to be created within it. Forgiving one's self can heal the Void-wound, allowing that spirit to proceed to the afterlife beyond.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cantor-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Notes-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Zerith-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLi De-5)</sup> Ghosts can also come about when prevented from entering the afterlife. One of the known methods of this is through the use of curses, magical rituals can also be performed to summon and bind them to Nirn. One of the most prolific methods of bringing back souls as ghosts is through the use of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-UL-6)</sup>

Ghosts come in many shapes and forms—some are so degraded they appear to only have their upper bodies, while others may appear skeletal, or whatever shape their body was in death. Most often, they will appear exactly how they did in life.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GAsi ES-7)</sup> During combat, ghosts can see invisible enemies and often have a chilling effect on those they attack, being also able to drain the magicka of their enemies from a distance. They can even be hard to hit, often hiding in the shadows where they are effectively invisible.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ARGhost-8)</sup>

Similar to other supernatural creatures, ghosts are often the target of professional hunters dedicated in dealing with them, with some of those hunters encountering various strange spirits and unusual entities during their hunts.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCTEA-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Spirithunter-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Claudina-11)</sup> Though hunted, it is said that ghosts never truly die even when slain, merely losing the energy which they'd built up that allowed them to manifest within the world.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTheyo-12)</sup>

## History

### First Era
Before the rise of [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman_Cyrodiil.md) as emperor of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), legends state that [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) bled with ghosts thanks to the chaos of the period when the Imperial Province lacked an emperor or the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Remanada-13)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) The Soulburst At some point, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) made a pact with a priest of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) that caused a curse to befall the Breton village of [Westtry](Lore_Westtry.md), causing the inhabitants of the entire village to become ghosts and relive their invasion in the hands of Nords over and over again as.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOTGW-14)</sup> The curse of the town was later broken thanks to the aid of the Vestige, using a magical gem in order to free the spirits of the townspeople, allowing the people of the village to finally find some rest.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOMM-15)</sup>

#### The Planemeld
In [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578) or [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579) the infamous necromancer [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), convinced the Cyrodilic Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) that, despite not being [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md), if they slightly modified the coronation ritual, they would be able to both relight the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) and convince [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to gift Varen with the blood of a dragon.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Cot FC-16)</sup> However, Mannimarco corrupted the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), and, by performing the ritual, Varen broke the divine covenant of Akatosh and [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) which protected Nirn from the forces of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). This caused what was known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md), which not only tore the veil between Mundus and Oblivion but also tore the veil between [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) and Nirn. This tearing increased the number of hauntings to record levels, even average mortals were able to visibly and audibly hear ghosts, and [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md) they normally would have never been able to see or hear. The tearing in the veil also allowed necromancers to raise many forms of undead on an unprecedented scale. Its unclear if staving off the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) allowed for these restless spirits to return to Aetherius.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Gabrielle-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) an army of [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) spirits led by General Radrathren, returned to haunt the ancient battlefield of the Starved Plain, near the city of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md) in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md). The ghostly army was not only just a threat to invading [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) soldiers, who were invading Daven's Watch at the time but also a threat to the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) armies repelling the Daggerfall Covenant army. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) was tasked with finding a solution to the threat. One mage suggested enslaving the ghosts to fight for the Pact, and help defend against the Covenant, while the other mage suggested the ghosts should be set free and returned back to their natural rest. It is unknown if the Vestige decided to free the army or instead took the suggestion of enslaving the army to help defend Davon's Watch.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Tt A-19)</sup>

During the same year, the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) violated the tomb of Warcaller Targoth and his warriors in [Betnikh](Lore_Betony.md), dragging their spirits back into the mortal realm as ghosts, with the commander of the cult using the magical war horn of Targoth in order to attract he and his warriors.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOTargoth-20)</sup> The ghost of Targoth then asked the Vestige for help, with the hero later freeing the warcaller, although it is unclear if they freed his warriors as well by returning the magical horn or if they remained as spirits if the horn was kept.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> If the horn was kept, the army of ghosts would have been present during an attack against the Bloodthorn Cult by the local orcs of Betony of the [Seamount Clan](Lore_Seamount_Clan.md), giving their living kin a valuable reinforcement during the battle, with Targoth himself recognizing the reason to keep the horn as he and his warriors were able to help their living kin even in a ghostly form.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOCD-22)</sup>

The same Bloodthorn Cult would later attack a burial mound in the Glenumbra region of High Rock, where they similarly began to enslave the spirits of the dead to do their biding. Once again the Vestige interfered, killing the cultists who were attempting to bind the ghosts and also defeating the spirit of one of the great heroes who had been successfully enslaved, freeing the ghosts in the process.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOSAK-23)</sup>

Meanwhile in [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md), a group of ghosts of bandits began to threaten the surrounding area of the cave where they had been originally starved to death, with many spectral archers, brigands and rogues at their side.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOEdain-24)</sup> The Vestige later aided the ghost of the knight that attempted to kill the bandits in life, defeating the spirits of the criminals and trapping them inside the sword of the knight, fulfilling the vow of the soldier and allowing him to rest as well as ending a threat to the region.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOLot T-25)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 253](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_253), the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) sent the [Nightmare Host](Lore_Nightmare_Host.md) to take control of the Barony of [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TTET-26)</sup> According to legend, it comprised of a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) ruler and its army of undead, including ghosts. These monsters were defeated by [Othrok](Lore_Othrok.md) and his holy army of men and animals. Othrok went on to become the first Baron of Dwynnen<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TFOTU-27)</sup> and his army later became the modern-day [Order of the Raven](Lore_Order_of_the_Raven.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DFRaven-28)</sup>

#### Imperial Simulacrum and Warp in the West
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png/200px-DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png) The ghost of King Lysandus during the late Third Era King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) and his legions of ghosts haunted the streets of the city of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) at night circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), calling for "vengeance". It was even recommended that people should not wander the streets of the city at night as any travelers would be certainly attacked.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DFCurse-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-DFLysandus-30)</sup>

The hauntings would only end thanks to an [Agent](Lore_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) who interfered, with the hero being able to soothe the rage of the ghost after sprinkling a special dust onto Lysandus' corpse, allowing the two to communicate. Lysandus informed the hero about his wish for vengeance against Lord [Woodborne](Lore_Woodborne.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DFRevelation-31)</sup> With the death of Woodborne and Lysandus vengeance complete, the spirit of the king was able to return to the afterlife alongside his legions of ghosts, leaving Daggerfall safe.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DFRevenge-32)</sup>

Other hauntings often happened in houses in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) done by ghosts that said that they built the houses that were haunted, with temples of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) often asking adventurers to deal with the issue in return for appreciation of the temple that contracted the adventurer in question.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-DFSpook-33)</sup> Similar quests were also offered to the members of those temples as well.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-DFHaunted-34)</sup> Members of the Benevolence of Mara were also sometimes asked to deal with a haunting that happened in their sacred shrines in the region and if the member failed, Maran Knights would be sent instead, causing the ghost in question to rest in peace regardless.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-DFPowerful-35)</sup>

Similarly, Knightly Orders of the Iliac Bay often had to deal with exorcisms of ghosts as well, as the spirits often haunted abandoned properties that nobles resided, with the nobles in question contracting the Orders for the job. If the job was refused by a knight, the Order might have turned the job to the temples of the Divines instead.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-DFExorcism-36)</sup>

#### Sightings in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427)
By the time of the appearance of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), many ghosts were found across [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and in the underground ruins of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), particularly in the ancestral tombs spread across the island.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Morrowind-37)</sup> Some ghosts of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) were also found haunting the ruins of their cities at the time, both in Vvardenfell but also in the underground portions of Mournhold.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MWDwarven Ghost-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-MWDahrk-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-MWRadac-40)</sup>

One ghost was even seen in a section of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md)<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-MWVivec Ghost-41)</sup> while another haunted an inn in the [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) city of [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md), ruining the local business of the establishment and scaring customers, with this special ghost being able to be killed again but always returning. Investigations eventually pointed out that a conjuration expert was to blame, with the conjurer saying they were using the haunting as a joke.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-MWGG-42)</sup> Members of the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) of Vvardenfell sometimes had to deal with similar hauntings that caused problems, such as one that happened beneath a house that was built on top of a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) ancestral tomb.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-MWTH-43)</sup>

#### Oblivion Crisis
Later, many ghosts were found across the Imperial Province of Cyrodiil by the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), usually being found in [Ayleid ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md), some caves or even in the undercrofts of chapels.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-OB-44)</sup> Notable occurrences of ghosts during this period happened after an entire family was murdered by a member of the Dark Brotherhood, causing the matriarch to attempt to attack the assassin in a ghostly form and another happened in [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) where a manor was being haunted and the curse on the location had to be lifted by the one who bought it.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-OBPerennia-45)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-OBLD-46)</sup>

There was a ghost that haunted the [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md) that people have dubbed the "Forlorn Watchman". He would always appear at 8pm and sadly overlook the water from the ruins of Fort Irony. The people claimed that the ghost died in a horrible shipwreck but the truth had eventually come out.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-OBForlorn-47)</sup> The ghost was originally the sailor, Grantham Blakeley, who was thrown overboard during a mutiny in [3E 421](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_421).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-LOTEM-48)</sup> An adventurer caught wind of the legend and tracked down the ghost in his usual haunt. Blakeley pointed them to the shipwreck on the [Mouth of the Panther](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mouth_of_the_Panther) and released his soul from the mortal world by destroying his killer, the mutineer and [gloom wraith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gloom_Wraith), Gable the Traitor.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-OBForlorn-47)</sup>

By this time, several ghosts of members of the Blades were found in the ruins of [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) after being freed from a curse that kept them as undead by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-OBBD-49)</sup> Another haunting event was seen in a ship of Anvil known as *The Serpent's Wake*, with the ghosts of the crew remaining after being murdered, with the Hero of Kvatch being asked to retrieve a crystal ball found inside the ship, likely causing the hero to attack the spirits in the process.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-OBTGSA-50)</sup> The Hero would later put an end to the curse [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) placed upon the ghosts inhabiting [Vitharn](Lore_Vitharn.md), a curse that for many years forced them to relive the events of their defeat at the hand of Vassal Fanatics. When the curse was lifted the spirits were finally able to move on to their rest.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Go V-51)</sup>

### Fourth Era
By the year [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), several cases of ghosts and hauntings were seen in the region of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), with them being seen in ruins, the wilds and sometimes even in populated areas.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Skyrim-52)</sup> A notable case of ghosts happened after a necromancer amassed a legion of enslaved ghostly followers who were eventually asked by the necromancer to gather other people in order for their numbers to be further expanded, allowing for a potential threat to Skyrim as this army was built up.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-AJ-53)</sup>

Several ghostly visions of members of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) were also seen by this time by a member of the College while the person was delving in the Nordic ruin of Labyrinthian, with most of the ghosts appearing to relive their final moments. Two of the spectres were holding the Dragon Priest [Morokei](Lore_Morokei.md) with magic, having remained in that state thanks to the actions of [Savos Aren](Lore_Savos_Aren.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-SKTSOM-54)</sup>

Ghosts of members of the Dark Brotherhood defended their sanctuary in the city of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) and might have been encountered by a member of the organization of assassins while pursuing a traitor.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-SKTCFM-55)</sup>

## Ghosts of Animals
Ghosts of animals are sometimes used as mounts both by other ghosts and the living, such as is the case for [horses](Lore_Horse.md) returned from the grave.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-56)</sup> Other animals, such as [cats](Lore_Cat.md), can also appear as ghosts, with Nibenese legends stating that a cat can haunt the house where its owner was murdered, with the cat being sent by Lord [Hollowjack](Lore_Hollowjack.md) to torment the killer with shame and guilt.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Ghostly Housecat-57)</sup> The [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Azurah](Lore_Azura.md) is known to use the spirits of cats as her messengers to the world of the living, sent to guide them when their need is great. In some cases Azurah might send the spirit of one's own feline companion into the world to guide them, said to be a sign of her mischief.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Zerith-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cantor-2)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Ja.27kh-58)</sup> One of the favored feline companions of Telvanni wizards is a type of cat from the city of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md) said to be capable of seeing spirits.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Necrom Ghostgazer-59)</sup> Other cats are also said to have the same ability and can even keep company to ghosts that are haunting houses.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Haunted HCat-60)</sup>

Ghosts of [chickens](Lore_Chicken.md) sometimes have mystery behind their existence, with it being suggested that they might appear after killing those animals in places that are already haunted.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Spectral Chicken-61)</sup>

According to the [Augur of the Obscure](Lore_Augur_of_the_Obscure.md), the ghosts left behind by [coral](Lore_Coral.md) are horrifying to behold.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-62)</sup>

## Characteristics
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-npc-Fillia.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Fillia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Fillia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Fillia.jpg) Filia, a spirit which can only be perceived by those with unnaturally keen senses
### Manifestation
As the disembodied souls of the dead, ghosts are normally insubstantial and incorporeal,<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-So Skyrim-63)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-On Skeletons-64)</sup> able to neither be touched nor be seen.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONArondil-65)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-ONDusant-66)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ONFillia-67)</sup> Manifesting in a tangible and visible form requires energy, which not all ghosts have, as a result, though present throughout Tamriel, a spirit will often not appear until it comes into contact with necromantic energy.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Mf NM-68)</sup> Spirits which have some of the energy required to manifest in a tangible form but not enough of it to fully do so might manifest in indistinct half formed shapes without any visible identifying characteristics.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ESOIndistinct-69)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-LATH-70)</sup> Spirits can accumulate magicka over time.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-ESOFaindor-71)</sup>

Ghosts are innately audible and visible to certain mortals with unnaturally keen perception, as well as during phenomena such as the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) which both increase the frequency of hauntings and allow even the average mortal to perceive spirits,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Gabrielle-17)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ONFillia-67)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-TIC-72)</sup> surviving a near-death experience is also said to make communication with spirits easier.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTheyo-12)</sup> Ghosts can sometimes manifest in a form that's visually identical to their living selves, with their true nature as spirits only becoming apparent once they let their true nature become known.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-ONAncalin-73)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ONWeeper-74)</sup> Places where a lot of suffering has occurred are said to be infused with the necromantic energies a soul feeling such emotions produces, this miasma can form a trap which prevents souls from moving on to the afterlife and turns them into ghosts, a phenomenon commonly encountered in battlefields.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Stain-75)</sup>

### Influence and Possession
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) Moon-Singer Talbira, a spirit who reincarnated herself into a living body In addition to manifesting in a spectral form, a ghost can make its presence known through other means such as influencing its environment through speaking or moving objects,<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ONShelreni-76)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ONMavos-77)</sup> possessing the living,<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ONJanne-78)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-ONGalga-79)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-ONUldor-80)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-ESORun-81)</sup> possessing and reanimating the dead,<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ONGhora-82)</sup> or possessing and animating one or more inanimate objects such as statues or [Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md).<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ONYokudan-83)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-ONCenturion-84)</sup> In some cases a ghost might attach itself to a living host and remain with them over a period of time, seeking to enjoy a pale likeness of the life they lived, pursue sensations they craved in life, or take their host's life for themselves. Such spirits fall into three categories, Lurking-type Spirits who wait in lonely places for a host and settle in the recesses of their mind undetected, Possession-type spirits who seek to avoid detection so they can infiltrate their host's mind and gradually consume their thoughts until they've no identity left, and Traveler-type spirits who attach themselves to those who seek sensations like adventure but might influence their host towards recklessness and consequently death. The presence of such spirits can be sensed by ghost hunters as a form of spiritual malaise, allowing for the use of cleansing rituals to banish the intruding presence.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Spirithunter-10)</sup> There have been cases where the spirit of a dead mortal has entered the body of a living being and integrated itself with the living soul residing there. Though ghosts have the ability to possess and animate both living and dead bodies as well as inanimate objects by entering them, such phenomena are said to not be the same thing as this spiritual integration. Whereas in a possession the soul of the spirit remains distinct and it exerts control over the host body, with the host soul sometimes being expelled outright,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ESOFarwen-85)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-ESOAwa-86)</sup> the integration phenomenon results instead in the soul of the spirit merging with the soul of the living host without exerting control, thus the spirit is effectively reborn or [reincarnated](Lore_Reincarnation.md) into the living host, who will than start to experience the memories of that spirit as if they'd lived through them, and manifesting the knowledge and abilities the soul reincarnated within them possessed and feeling its emotions as their own. The phenomenon has also been described as a spirit being resurrected as the "twin soul" of its host. The initial entry of the soul into the living body is accompanied by a brief bout of intense pain, after the rebirth, other spirits who knew the reincarnated soul in life can sense its presence within its reincarnated form and realize the host soul and the reincarnated soul as now being the same. Though such spiritual rebirth is possible, it is said that it is relatively rare for spirits to behave in that manner.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Zerith-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cantor-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Notes-3)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Journal-87)</sup>

### Experienced Sensations
As they no longer have bodies, ghosts do not normally feel sensations like pain or exhaustion, and will eventually start to forget them, they also require no sleep or sustenance. Exceptions to this lack of sensation exist in the form of necromancy, as necromantic bindings or having part of their bodies bound to a flesh construct is described as painful for the spirit, of things which the ghost is vulnerable to in combat, which can also inflict pain, and of the sensation they felt while dying which a ghost is said to continue to feel even after death such as for example remaining perpetually tired if they died from exhaustion.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-ONCynric-88)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ONMeln-89)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ESOGette-90)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-ESOGuzguk-91)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-BLTower-92)</sup> If a ghost has part of its body incorporated into an undead construct such as a [Flesh Atronach](Lore_Flesh_Atronach.md), the spirit will be able to feel both the construct's drive to kill and feed, and any physical blows the construct might receive, though the ghost itself can also use the connection to influence the construct to perform certain actions. The spirits that have body parts bound to the same undead construct can sense one another.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ESOGette-90)</sup><sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ESOSurille-93)</sup> Spirits can sense strong emotions associated with an object lingering around it.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-ESOVanisande-94)</sup>

### Methods to Vanquish and Defend Against Spirits
Because ghosts are already dead "slaying" a ghost won't always end the haunting,in such cases the spirit is said to only sleep for a time until it awakens and returns once more, indeed it is said that spirits never truly die even when slain instead merely losing the energy which they'd built up that allowed them to manifest within the world, <sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTheyo-12)</sup><sup>[[95]](#cite_note-ONNavid-95)</sup> though methods like trapping the spirit in an enchanted object and than destroying the item can prevent its return,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOEdain-24)</sup>, tethering the spirit to its body through magic can imprison it,<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-ESOLauriel-96)</sup> and certain rites such as those employed by the [Keepers of the Dead](Lore_Keepers_of_the_Dead.md) can also usher a ghost's passage into the afterlife.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ONShelreni-76)</sup> In some cases a powerful vanquished ghost will leave behind part of the energy it used to manifest in a tangible form, this ghostly essence can be gathered and used in rituals to force the vanquished spirit to remanifest and bind it to one's will.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTheyo-12)</sup> In some cases a spirit's discorporation can desecrate the surrounding ground making it harmful for the living.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-ONFrost Mage-97)</sup><sup>[[98]](#cite_note-ONStorm Mage-98)</sup> Even when its soul was sundered by powerful magic, it is said a ghost can eventually recoalesce in the same location, given enough time. Indeed, the fragments of a spirit shattered by magic need to be bound by spells to prevent them from seeking each other out and reforming into the whole, and will proceed to recoalesce once the spell keeping them apart is lifted.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-ESOAlanwe-99)</sup>. Magic can be used to bind a spirit from moving on to the afterlife, but it is said even such souls can find a way to move on eventually.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-ESOAnconath-100)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-ESOSombren-101)</sup> There are certain tones of sound which are painful to immortal creatures, including ghosts and [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), spirit hunters have been known to use special whistles which emit this tone to repel such beings.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Claudina-11)</sup> During times of increased spiritual activity and large scale hauntings such as those caused by a cursed relic or by a [Solar](Lore_Sun.md) [Eclipse](Lore_Astronomy.md), Tamrielic kings have been known to sometimes use [time altering](Lore_Time_Magic.md) magical items such as Arcane Hourglasses to fast forward time and undo the haunting or [celestial phenomenon](Lore_Eclipse.md) causing the increased spirit activity.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-102)</sup><sup>[[103]](#cite_note-Castles Items-103)</sup>

### Connection to Bodies and Vitality
The more recent the death, the stronger the lingering connection a spirit has to its original body and the harder it will be to control that body via necromancy,<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-On Necromancy-104)</sup>, because of this link spirits instinctively know which bones are their own.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ESOSurille-93)</sup> Some ghosts have also been shown to retain a connection to their descendants, communicating with them through dreams and transmitted feelings in order to drive them to finish what unresolved affairs are keeping them in the mortal world and lay their spirits to rest, and guiding them with illusory projections of the events of their lives along the way.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ESOAlwyn-105)</sup> The spirits of the dead, even those that have otherwise been long dormant and inactive, can be roused when given a taste of the vitality of the living. Once given a taste of the life they now lack these roused spirits become thirsty for more and cannot return to slumber, thus the spilling of blood near places where the dead rest is a method used by necromancers who wish to awaken them for their purposes. Both blood taken from an unwilling target and blood given willingly can call to the dead, but it is ill advised for a necromancer to offer their own blood for this process. The dead hunger for more of the vitality of the one whose blood they were given, and so them developing a taste for the necromancer's own lifeforce can prove highly dangerous.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-TTD-106)</sup>

### Additional Forms of Manifestation
A ghost's form is dressed in the same clothes it last wore in life,<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ONMeln-89)</sup> spirits will also sometimes manifest alongside replicas of objects they were associated with while living or of objects of emotional importance such as a gravestone, one captain was even known to still command a spectral version of her ship in death.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ONAmbre-107)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ONWeeper-74)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Proconsul_Beloren-Kaie.jpg) An Akaviri spirit, returned from the afterlife to pass judgment on the living during the Proving Festival A ghost which becomes consumed by its negative emotions will transform into a [Wraith](Lore_Wraith.md).<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Mf NM-68)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-ONPraxin-108)</sup> Some ghosts can also take on the form of [Wispmothers](Lore_Wisp.md).<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-ONLost-109)</sup> Many reports from across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) describe a phenomenon where the souls of the dead adopt a variety of forms to return and speak with the living. Animals, plants, or even fearsome visions, are all reported manifestations. It has been theorized that the [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md), immortal entities of pure magic, might also be such beings, Ascended Ancestors returned in a different form.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-LAS-110)</sup> Some sources claim that ghosts grow stronger if well-honored by the living, gaining the ability to cross from the afterlife into the living world more easily, take on different forms such as that of a black bladed golden warrior that is "swift as the wind", grant great fortune or misfortune, commune through and alter dreams, and even drag one to the afterlife if angered.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Clever-111)</sup> It is said that when spirits commune with the living time passes differently for the one experiencing the event.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cantor-2)</sup>

It is sometimes possible for someone to manifest as a ghost while they are soul trapped and their spirit's container is held elsewhere.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-ONLisondor-112)</sup><sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ONGasteau-113)</sup> The soul of an individual on the precipice of death can manifest in a spectral form, similar to a ghost. A soul burdened by strong emotions such as regret can splinter, its parts manifesting simultaneously in multiple locations tied to those emotions.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Jahones-114)</sup> Such souls, caught between life and death, can be prevented from moving on to the afterlife through the use of powerful magic. However, even the power of a [Luminary](Lore_Luminary.md) can't prevent a soul from passing on to Aetherius indefinitely, and powerful [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md) might eventually arrive to force the transition into Aetherius.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-Netch-115)</sup><sup>[[116]](#cite_note-End-116)</sup> A soul can sense the afterlife calling to them when its time for them to move on,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Notes-3)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-Eifa-117)</sup> being able to feel the ties that bind them to the mortal realm falling away,<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-ESOVanisande-94)</sup> One Argonian spirit claimed to be able to hear the Void beckoning when it was time to move on.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-Akech-118)</sup>

### Communication with Spirits
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/ON-npc-Septima_Tharn_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Septima_Tharn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Septima_Tharn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Septima_Tharn_02.jpg) An Ancestor Spirit returned from Aetherius to avenge a past grievance [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg/200px-CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg) A solar eclipse, a phenomenon during which spirit activity is said to increase During certain times such as the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) Proving Festival, the living have the ability to communicate with the spirits of the dead by invoking their names, while the dead have the ability to gaze upon the living world and cross over to pass judgment on their descendants or those who've invoked them should they so desire. It is said spirits will give their living descendants either bad luck or good fortune depending on whether the're pleased or angered, but will only cross over if truly enraged. Thus one should honor their ancestors as they can influence the fortunes of their descendants.<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-ONProconsul-119)</sup><sup>[[120]](#cite_note-Proving-120)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Clever-111)</sup> A similar phenomenon occurs during the [Old Life Festival](Lore_Old_Life_Festival.md), a holiday celebrated throughout the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) when the living reach out to the departed by leaving messages at shrines, and the departed sometimes return from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) in response.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ONPetronius-121)</sup> According to some sources all waterways connect the realms of the living and the dead, being the means by which spirits cross over into the living world.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-ONDomitia-122)</sup> Spirits, like vampires and other undead, are said to be encountered more frequently during phenomena such as a solar eclipse. Inhabitants of Tamriel can sometimes receive warning of an impending eclipse in the form of [prophetic](Lore_Sight.md) nightmares revolving around it and the subsequent surge of spirits and undead.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-102)</sup>

Upon death, some of the accumulated experiences of mortal existence which the soul is vessel for, the [Fjyrons](Lore_Fjyron.md), the "will to peace", or memories of the dead,<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-TA-123)</sup> dissipate into the living world. This essence retains a connection to both the grand fabric of [creation](Lore_Aurbis.md) and to all aspects of the soul in all their [destinations](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md). Thus, because all aspects of a soul remain connected, tapping into the Fjyrons allows one who knows how to receive the erudition of the ancestors. Knowledge and guidance from beyond mortality, from the existence beyond the present and the past and the known world, where time is irrelevant, allowing one to glimpse the cosmic tapestry and its threads and representing a connection to the Aedra and the "endless Aedric coil of mortal souls" through the experiences of every mortal who has ever lived. Because of this some groups such as the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) make use of this connection to receive the wisdom of the ancestral spirits in the afterlife, using it to read and interpret the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-TDS-124)</sup><sup>[[125]](#cite_note-POTM-125)</sup><sup>[[126]](#cite_note-LAESMP-126)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/LG-cardart-Sovngarde_Hero.png/200px-LG-cardart-Sovngarde_Hero.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Sovngarde_Hero.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Sovngarde_Hero.png) A spirit of Sovngarde returned to aid a living champion Certain [Shouts](Lore_Thu%27um.md) such as the Call of Valor are said to function by issuing a call for aid to Aetherial spirits, who respond to the call and "journey beyond time and space" to give their aid.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-Call-127)</sup> There exist magical runes which can call a spirit back into the living world from Aetherius, Archmage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) placed such runes on his tomb to call his soul back to Nirn during times when magic in Tamriel was under threat.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-Shalidor-128)</sup>

## Demeanor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg/200px-ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg) The Emissary of Z'en, a spirit emissary of the god Z'en Spirits are said to not like to visit the mortal world, with it being claimed they do so only out of duty and obligation. Some spirits claim that their otherworld is more pleasant, or at least more comfortable for spirits than the mortal realm, which is cold, bitter, and full of pain and loss. As such, spirits that are forced to remain in the mortal plane, whether by their own unfinished business or by external magic, often go mad.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-ATD-129)</sup>

Some ghosts are shown to still heed the allegiances or cultural traditions they held to in life, with a captain's crew or the army of a general or king returning collectively when called,<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ONAmbre-107)</sup><sup>[[130]](#cite_note-Warcaller-130)</sup><sup>[[131]](#cite_note-Orator-131)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DFRevenge-32)</sup><sup>[[132]](#cite_note-Right-132)</sup> and even being conjured directly to the side of the spirit they've allegiance to.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-ONFounder-133)</sup><sup>[[134]](#cite_note-ONStorm-134)</sup><sup>[[135]](#cite_note-ONForest King-135)</sup> This phenomenon can even be encountered with spirits already in the afterlife.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-Fildgor-136)</sup>

Some [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ghosts are said to mark the living who've somehow managed to gain their favor, making glowing marks resembling the [Blessed Life-Tree](Lore_Blessed_Life-Tree.md) appear on their bodies that can potentially also gain favor from any other ghosts lingering in Ayleid ruins.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-BLTBody-137)</sup>

Some spirits can come to serve specific functions on the behalf of [gods](Lore_Gods.md), becoming their emissaries to the world of the living. [Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) deities such as [Z'en](Lore_Z%27en.md) have been known to choose spirits to act as their heralds, tasking them with duties such as assigning and collecting the debts of the faithful in regards to his sphere of payment in kind. Such spirits might appear to the living to offer guidance, or pass judgment, and are revered by the faithful as instruments of divine will.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-Emissary-138)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-Mathragor-139)</sup>

## Powers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-skill-Haunting_Spectre.jpg/200px-ON-skill-Haunting_Spectre.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Haunting_Spectre.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Haunting_Spectre.jpg) A ghost attacking an enemy through a conjured spectre [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-creature-Harrowing_Haunter_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Harrowing_Haunter_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Harrowing_Haunter_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Harrowing_Haunter_02.jpg) A ghost draining the soul of the living The nature of ghosts gives many of them special capabilities when it comes to their manifestations, with some of them being able to fly and use the shadows to turn invisible.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ARGhost-8)</sup> Other ghosts have spells that are capable of dealing considerable damage at a short range and are only able to be damaged by weapons made out of [silver](Lore_Silver.md) or better materials, therefore being completely invulnerable to unprepared adventurers.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-DFGhost-140)</sup>

Ancestor ghosts seen in the region of Morrowind were also immune to weapons made out of normal materials, with some of them being able to cast curses on their enemies, reducing various capabilities while also causing damage.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-MWAncestor-141)</sup> At least one of the ghosts in Vvardenfell was even able to give the disease of Brown Rot to the ones that they attacked.<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-MWSul-Senipul-142)</sup>

Various ghosts can also use frost spells natural to them in order to combat their enemies, with some of those ghosts even being immune to effects of silence, causing them to be dangerous and problematic even to prepared mages. Some of the ghosts with those capabilities of attack and defense have been seen to not be affected by normal weapons, but are able to be hit if the weapon in question is enchanted.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-OBGhost Skill-143)</sup>

While many ghosts have special offensive abilities such as conjuring spectral apparitions, reanimating the dead,<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ONGhora-82)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ONWeeper-74)</sup>, or opening portals,<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-ONProconsul-119)</sup> there are also those that merely retain and use what they had in life, such as archers that can use their own bow and arrows, even if in spectral form, as a way of offense against their enemies. They are still capable of disappearing without leaving a trace behind if they need to move somewhere else though, showing access to supernatural powers.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-SKKatria-144)</sup> Other ghosts can be seen wearing a variety of weapons other than bows such as swords and battleaxes, sometimes using a shield for defensive purposes,<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-SKComp Ghost-145)</sup> or a stave for the use of magic.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-ESOCaster-146)</sup> Some spirits have shown the ability to cause living beings to instantly combust,<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-Emissary-138)</sup> or create additional copies of their own spectral form to aid them in combat.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-Shadow King-147)</sup><sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Shadow Aspect-148)</sup>

The powers of the ghosts are not limited to only combat, they can also be valuable with information, exhibiting perception of things which occur in remote locations or within the confines of the area their haunting is tied to,<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ONMeln-89)</sup><sup>[[149]](#cite_note-ONNurnhilde-149)</sup>, being able to sense curses, whether someone has a soul, or when a life expires,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOEdain-24)</sup><sup>[[150]](#cite_note-ESOAyleid-150)</sup><sup>[[149]](#cite_note-ONNurnhilde-149)</sup> and sometimes holding knowledge that mortals are not aware of and that can be precious even to a god, therefore being able to help with advice in various ways.<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-151)</sup> It is said they can "know and affect the future, speak with other spirits and work great magic", and they can allow for the living to communicate with other ghosts.<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-Ancestor-152)</sup><sup>[[153]](#cite_note-Leramil-153)</sup> In addition the abilities of spirits can sometimes be of use with providing protection through the lifting of curses,<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-OBValdemar-154)</sup>, through granting blessings that shield from dangerous magic or poison,<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-ONVeloth-155)</sup><sup>[[156]](#cite_note-ONTrue Path-156)</sup> or granting blessings that give other advantages to the living.<sup>[[157]](#cite_note-Ancestor Shrines-157)</sup><sup>[[158]](#cite_note-Repose-158)</sup> Such rewards can also take the form of granting the living the ability to use certain [spells](Lore_Spells.md), such as reanimating the dead.<sup>[[159]](#cite_note-Shivering-159)</sup> Their abilities can also be used to heal the living, with mender spirits sometimes being invoked by necromancers for aid. Being summoned to provide such help to the living, either by lending their strength or to inhabit and animate bodies willingly, is said to be one of the ways through which spirits can resolve the issue that keep them from the afterlife, once employed by the Order of the Hidden Moon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLi De-5)</sup><sup>[[160]](#cite_note-ONZerithabilities-160)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Notes-3)</sup> Ghosts can also perform various duties even in their spectral form, although sometimes they might not be aware that the area that they are acting for is not abandoned, with the god [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) being known as capable of having his followers in such a state.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-161)</sup> In one case the spirit of a banker tasked with delivering letters to inform recipients of coffers they were to receive simply kept performing the task after death, tracking down the recipients and making the deliveries.<sup>[[162]](#cite_note-162)</sup> Ghosts are also one of the many creatures that are capable of escaping physical barriers naturally, forcing mages to create spells with a high cost of magicka in order to contain them, although objects like binding stones can also be used.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-TBS-163)</sup>

Some ghosts are also capable of opening portals to their last moments of life, allowing the one that steps through the portal to see a vision of what happened, with the vision being able to show events as they happened even in the eyes of the enemies of the ghost that opened the portal.<sup>[[164]](#cite_note-ESOUt P-164)</sup> Others simply project illusory recreations of moments from their lives.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ESOAlwyn-105)</sup> There have been cases of spirits reaching out to the living via altering their dreams and subsequently using said dreams as a medium to [alter time](Lore_Time_Magic.md) in a form of ultimately fatal curse. The living target of the curse would initially experience strange dreams relating to the ghost's past, eventually realizing that they were being made to travel to different eras of history in their sleep when the people in the dream noticed the intruding presence and reacted to it, resulting in them being immediately moved to a different time upon being seen, and thus revealing the intent behind the magic. The experience would repeat nightly until burns started appearing on the dreamer's body, culminating in them magically burning to ash.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Journal Olaf-165)</sup>

There exist dangerous ghosts that will steal the souls of the living,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOEdain-24)</sup> or even enthrall those whose souls they take to their service causing them to return as ghosts as well. Among such hostile spirits those who expel living souls to steal their bodies are known as Body Shifters and are considered very dangerous.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ESOFarwen-85)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-ESOAwa-86)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-ESORun-81)</sup>

Certain spirits are shown to be able to travel to other planes, manifesting there to take vengeance on those who've wronged them,<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-ESOSchemes-166)</sup> or returning to the world of the living from the afterlife to bestow blessings to those who've aided them before departing again,<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-Ulfgar-167)</sup> to act on the behalf of a deity that they serve as emissaries,<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-Emissary-138)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-Mathragor-139)</sup> to give their approval for great deeds,<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-Ysgramor-168)</sup><sup>[[169]](#cite_note-Yngol-169)</sup><sup>[[170]](#cite_note-Ylgar-170)</sup> or to impose trials on their living descendants.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-ONDomitia-122)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Clever-111)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-ONProconsul-119)</sup> while others can create magical barriers,<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-OBPriory-171)</sup>, dispel them,<sup>[[172]](#cite_note-Flames-172)</sup>, or provide fortifying blessings.<sup>[[173]](#cite_note-OBUnfeathered-173)</sup> Some ghosts have been shown to be able to curse the living to remain within the area of their haunting, rendering them incapable of leaving that region.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-Cirion-174)</sup>

The spirits of certain mages who created a spell or curse in life have been shown to be able to continue powering and maintaining their creations even in death, with their spirits needing to be vanquished and bound for the curse to be lifted.<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-Vildras-175)</sup> A spirit's essence can be given fully in order to cast especially powerful magic, sacrificing the spirit's independent spectral existence in the process. Such an event occurred when the spirit of the mage Alanwe sacrificed herself to banish the fully powered [Ebony Blade](Lore_Ebony_Blade.md) from Tamriel for a long time and cursed it to never stay with any one wielder for long, despite the will of its creator [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and the Blade's magic attempting to resist.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-ESOAlanwe-99)</sup>

## Cultural Significance

### Dunmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Conundrum.jpg) A Dunmer summoning his ancestor According to Dunmer ancestor worship, the spirits of the dead continue to exist in the world, being able to "know and affect the future, speak with other spirits and work great magic". The Dunmer believe that recently deceased spirits are weaker but close to the living in spirit, quick to guide and protect, whereas older spirits are stronger but stranger and more distant, with the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) being the oldest and greatest but also strangest of such spirits. Malevolent spirits are considered "daemons and "devils" from whom benevolent " Hero" spirits protect.<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-Ancestor-152)</sup>

For the Dunmer, the proximity with the ghosts of the deceased is one much closer if compared to other races, with the ghosts being generally able to be interacted and honored. Ghosts can even be found defending the hearth, teaching wisdom to future generations and allowing some to know better about the future, in return they are guarded and protected by the Dunmer, who keep a close proximity with them.<sup>[[176]](#cite_note-TDOTS-176)</sup>

The bond between a Dunmer and the ghostly ancestors is partially blood, ritual and volitional, with a member being brought into a House thanks to [marriage](Lore_Marriage.md) being able to bind themselves through ritual to another House and therefore gain access to communicate and benefit from the ancestors of another family. That bond of marriage is weaker than the one of their offspring, and they still maintain access to their own ancestors who can be contacted and worshipped in a family shrine.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-ATD-129)</sup>

This relationship with the ancestors and their ghosts is extremely intimate and personal, but also quite serious. Dunmer also feel that all that they think and do is under the watchful eye of the ancestor spirits, causing a special interaction with those ghosts.<sup>[[177]](#cite_note-ROCW-177)</sup> [Ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) is also practiced by the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribes and was not exclusive to the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) Dunmer when the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) were an active force as gods.<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-POT-178)</sup>

Due to those beliefs, both the Tribes and the Temple believed that forcefully summoning the ghosts of the ancestors was a shameful and sinful act as it not only made them slaves but also took the spirits out of their own world. The Dunmer did often consider it cruel to take the spirits even if it was done out of their own will in service and love to family and their race, but it was seen as much worse if it was forceful necromancy, with the action not being tolerated in Morrowind in general.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-BR-179)</sup>

### Bretons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg/200px-ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Moors.jpg) Glenumbra Moors There are many legends and spooky stories in High Rock involving ghosts and their hauntings, with many ruined castles and other ruins that dot the countryside, with each likely having a story of ghosts behind it. The ruins of Baelborne Rock and Dresan Keep in particular had many legends surrounding them while other areas were completely suggested that they should be avoided both thanks to the hauntings and the danger that the undead posed.<sup>[[180]](#cite_note-GOG-180)</sup> [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) as a whole is known for being haunted; it is said that everyone living there has some story to tell about a haunting. While the woods of [Daenia](Lore_Daenia.md) are believed to be haunted, they are actually inhabited by [spirits of nature](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md). The most haunted area within Glenumbra is the [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), the area where the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md) took place in the First Era. Another famous haunted location within Glenumbra is the cemetery of [Cath Bedraud](Lore_Cath_Bedraud.md), where many of those who died at the Battle of Glenumbra Moors were buried. During times of heavy fog, ghostly lights and moans have been reported to be seen in the vicinity of the cemetery.<sup>[[180]](#cite_note-GOG-180)</sup>

Another area with many ghost stories is the [Cambray Hills](Lore_Cambray_Hills.md) where [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) is located, as that part of High Rock is known for being haunted as well, with any given member of the populace being believed to know to usually have a unique ghost story or two.<sup>[[181]](#cite_note-GP-181)</sup>

### Bosmer
It was speculated that some talismans made by [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) were created and worn in order to ward off ghosts or to keep spirits from crawling back out of tar-pits where the Bosmer that disobeyed the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) were buried, sometimes alive.<sup>[[182]](#cite_note-ONPTWS-182)</sup>

Bosmer [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md) claim that the spirits of the dead experience a change upon reaching Aetherius, with many souls of mortals becoming spirits of a different sort in that place.<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-ONGirnalin-183)</sup>

### Nord
An ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) tale, known as [Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts](Lore_Yngol_and_the_Sea-Ghosts.md) refers to the death of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s eldest son, [Yngol](Lore_Yngol.md), at the hands of vengeful "*Sea-Ghosts"*.<sup>[[184]](#cite_note-Yat SG-184)</sup> An odd custom observed in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), involves placing a bowl of sea water inside the doorway to a house. This is believed to capture any wandering spirits that may come from the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md).<sup>[[185]](#cite_note-Nelos-185)</sup>

### Orc
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/ON-npc-Bolg_of_Wicked_Barbs.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Bolg_of_Wicked_Barbs.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Bolg_of_Wicked_Barbs.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Bolg_of_Wicked_Barbs.jpg) Bolg of Wicked Barbs, an Orcish spirit, testing the living [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/ON-npc-Grubduthag_Many-Fates.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Grubduthag_Many-Fates.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Grubduthag_Many-Fates.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Grubduthag_Many-Fates.jpg) Grubduthag Many-Fates, an Orcish spirit, testing the living [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) that pass onto the [Ashpit](Lore_Ashpit.md) as their afterlife are believed to be "thrust into [Malacath's](Lore_Malacath.md) coals", honed, tempered and made whole in the cleansing fires of Malacath through a process called the ritual of tempering, which all Orcish [souls](Lore_Souls.md) bound for the Ashpit must undergo when entering the realm. The grudges and fury of these Orcs are said to be heated and melted within the flames, and are eventually forged into the next generation of mortal Orcs. With their grudges returned to the living world, the immortal Orcs of the Ashpit are thus freed to create new grudges. Some of the most memorable of these new grievances are folded and hardened on the Anvil of Ash to create weapons and armor of legendary stature.<sup>[[186]](#cite_note-Ot A-186)</sup><sup>[[187]](#cite_note-ONThazeg-187)</sup> There is said to be no place for a gentle soul in the Ashen Forge, such souls are instead believed to be given to Trinimac, who watches over all those who have fallen and guides them to their final reward.<sup>[[188]](#cite_note-ONBazrag-188)</sup> Orcish spirit-legions can be found in the Ashpit with [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) of the [Doomdriven Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Doomdriven_Clan) serving in these spirit-legions as marshals and commanders.<sup>[[189]](#cite_note-ODC-189)</sup> Some Orc spirits that serve Malacath have been known to manifest in the world of the living in order to test the living followers of Malacath, with some among them even summoning [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) from the Ashpit such as [Flame Atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) to aid them, as was the case in the training grounds of the Oathsworn Pit.<sup>[[190]](#cite_note-Bolg-190)</sup><sup>[[191]](#cite_note-ONGruth-191)</sup><sup>[[192]](#cite_note-ONSluth-192)</sup>

### Reachmen
Some clans of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachman.md) are believed to worship the ghosts of the dead, often sacrificing [goats](Lore_Goat.md) to ancient heroes.<sup>[[193]](#cite_note-GSR-193)</sup> Some ghosts of the Reachfolk remain on Nirn to provide guidance to their descendants. One of such ghosts was the legendary [Vateshran](Lore_Vateshrans.md) [Aydolan](Lore_Aydolan.md).<sup>[[194]](#cite_note-Aydolan-194)</sup>

### Tsaesci
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/OBR-npc-Akaviri_Commander_Mishaxhi.jpg/200px-OBR-npc-Akaviri_Commander_Mishaxhi.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-Akaviri_Commander_Mishaxhi.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-Akaviri_Commander_Mishaxhi.jpg) Commander Mishaxhi, a Tsaesci spirit [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-npc-Shade_of_the_Founder.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Shade_of_the_Founder.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Shade_of_the_Founder.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Shade_of_the_Founder.jpg) The Shade of the Founder, an Akaviri spirit who crossed over during the Proving Festival to protect his legend The [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci.md) religion is one of ancestor worship. They believe that all waterways connect the worlds of the living and the dead and from them the spirits of their ancestors gaze upon their living descendants and their deeds,<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-ONDomitia-122)</sup> thought to grant either good fortune or misfortune depending on whether the actions of their progeny have pleased them. One's ancestors are invoked by calling their name during the Proving Festival, a date on which it is said it is possible for spirits to cross over from the afterlife into the living world. Ancestor spirits are said to grow more powerful the more they are revered, thus a well-honored ancestral spirit will be able to cross from the afterlife into the living world more easily, take on different forms such as that of a black bladed golden warrior that is "swift as the wind", grant great fortune or misfortune, commune through and alter dreams, and even drag one to the afterlife if angered. Thus ancestor spirits are viewed as something to be appeased, able to render significant aid if properly honored but also dangerous if offended, known to deliver potentially fatal trials for what they consider insulting behavior such as verbal boasts of greatness that disrespect the dead.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Clever-111)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-ONProconsul-119)</sup><sup>[[120]](#cite_note-Proving-120)</sup> Spirits might also return to the living world for other reasons, such as taking action to maintain their own legacy, whether based on truth or not.

### Psijic Order
The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) maintains that souls that cross over into the afterlife can grow to gain great power and rule there, with a particularly malevolent tyrant for example, feeding into the power of Daedra like [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) through their acts in life, and going on to "rule in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md)" after death. The Psijics believe that the "Daedra and gods to whom the common people turn" are themselves "no more than the spirits of superior men and women whose power and passion granted them great influence in the afterworld" and that while living they too were "bewildered" by their own ancestors going back to the original progenitors, the [Acharyai](Lore_Acharyai.md).<sup>[[195]](#cite_note-Old-195)</sup>

### Other Beliefs
Many reports from across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) describe a phenomenon where the souls of the dead adopt a variety of forms to return and speak with the living. Animals, plants, or even fearsome visions, are all reported manifestations. It is theorized that the [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md), immortal beings of pure magic, might have originated as mortal souls, being the spirits of Ascended Ancestors that transformed into their current state after death due to this described phenomenon. As the Luminaries themselves can't recall their origins, this theory remains unproven.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-LAS-110)</sup><sup>[[196]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-196)</sup> Whatever the case, however, a Luminary can indeed be created out of the essence of a mortal soul, as was the case with the Crow Luminary, who was created through a ritual devised by [Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild.md) out of a portion of her own essence. Though the process is straining and the restoration of the spirit takes time, a soul that was used in such a manner will eventually recover.<sup>[[197]](#cite_note-Crow-197)</sup>

## Notes
- Sometimes necromancers are suggested to use their servants sparingly and to allow no one to see them in order to cause others to eventually forget the undead. This was one of the practices done to combat [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) and his influence, as the blessings of such a divine prevents those buried with proper rituals from being raised, which could likely affect the presence of ghosts as well.<sup>[[198]](#cite_note-ATE-198)</sup>
- Despite being somewhat common across Tamriel, there are those that show that they do not believe in ghosts.<sup>[[199]](#cite_note-RJ-199)</sup>
- Superstitious sailors are known to tell ghost stories, which include the presence of entire ghost ships as well.<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-LCO-200)</sup>
- Certain [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and [Nature Spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) have been known to manifest in ghostly forms, however these creatures can never truly die, so these aren't truly ghosts.
