# Elder Scrolls


The exact number of Scrolls in the world is unknowable. They "do not exist in countable form", and they may disappear and reappear from moment to moment and place to place.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAOTS-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dexion-6)</sup> There exist [books](Lore_The_Elder_Scrolls_(books).md) that seemingly transcribe portions of the Elder Scrolls.

## Reading an Elder Scroll
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_05.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_05.jpg)](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_05.jpg.md) [](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_05.jpg.md) A Moth Priest reading an Elder Scroll *The clothes of the broken map are worn only by fools and heretics. The map is an exit for laziness. It is the dusty tongue, which is to say the given chart that most take as a story that is complete. No word is true until it is eaten.*
— [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Sermon27-7)</sup> Elder Scrolls contain [writing in an unknown script](Lore_Elder_Alphabet.md) and what seem to be star charts depicting [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md). According to one scholar, for an individual with "no training in the history or knowledge of the Scrolls", there is nothing revelatory about them and there are no adverse side affects from reading them.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EOTES-8)</sup>

Without preparation, a Scroll will take the sight or sanity of a reader who understands its importance. They will, however, get a glimpse of the Scroll's contents, which may include insight into the future, the past, or the nature of reality.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dexion-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EOTES-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Urag-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Septimus-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DTES-11)</sup> When the Scrolls are attuned to the timeless time of a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) in an attempt to see what happened during the Middle Dawn, their glyphs always disappear.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-WWY-12)</sup>

Due to the risks involved, the task of storing, cataloging and reading the Elder Scrolls has traditionally fallen on the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md). The Cult had devised a method of reading them, known as the [Ritual of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Ritual_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), that used the aid of the sacred [Ancestor Moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md) themselves to dampen the Scrolls' effect on the Moth Priests' sight and mind. Reading a Scroll still blinds them temporarily. This blindness lasts longer and longer with each reading,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-POTAM-13)</sup> and their returned eyesight degrades until it is gone for good.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EOTES-8)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Terran-14)</sup> [Urag gro-Shub](Lore_Urag_gro-Shub.md), an experienced scholar of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), believed that the blindness afflicted by the Scrolls was a "price" taken by the Divines in return for their knowledge.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Urag-9)</sup> Though blindness is a common outcome of reading the Elder Scrolls, there are those within the cult who believe it's not inevitable but rather the result of a lack of patience, as Moth Priests that wait months between readings have been known to retain their sight for decades.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAESMP-15)</sup> Those who read Elder Scrolls regularly begin to see the inscrutable script within as the letters of their own first language.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MSTAAYQ-16)</sup> If an Elder Scroll was blank, a Moth Priest could also cut themselves and dribble their blood onto the Scroll, causing a prophecy to be revealed. The more blood a Scroll was given, the more words and letters it would spell out.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPFrozen Tears-17)</sup>

Certain individuals such as the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) have managed to read an Elder Scroll without permanent blindness or madness, despite knowing their importance, gaining knowledge from them and having little to no training on how to avoid their side effects. Some believe that the Scrolls themselves allow these individuals to read them.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Skyrim-18)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dexion-6)</sup> The lich Corelian claimed he could read the Elder Scroll in his possession, though doing so was difficult.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONCD-19)</sup>

The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) invented a machine which could transcribe an Elder Scroll, and the transcription could be read safely.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Septimus-10)</sup> It is unclear whether this transcription reveals all of the Scroll's revelatory content, or only a fraction of it.

[Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) are unable to read Elder Scrolls, as ancestor moths refuse to communicate with them.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAESMP-15)</sup>

If the knowledge contained within an Elder Scroll is of the future, it may change from reader to reader, and from reading to reading. Until a prophecy is fulfilled, the true contents of an Elder Scroll are hazy, malleable, and uncertain, reflecting the changing nature of [reality](Lore_Aurbis.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MSTAAYQ-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-20)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Chana-21)</sup> Once the events within come to pass, its contents become fixed, identical to all who read it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LHOT-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Terran-14)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MSTAAYQ-16)</sup> Through the actions of a [Hero](Lore_Hero.md), the contents of a Scroll are made history and become "true". For this reason, a Hero can be considered the scribe of the next Elder Scroll, the one in which the prophecy has been fulfilled into a fixed point, negating its precursor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBLight-22)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-20)</sup>

## Origin & Nature of the Scrolls
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/Elder_Scroll.jpg/125px-Elder_Scroll.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Elder_Scroll.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Elder_Scroll.jpg) Contents of an Elder Scroll, as seen in *[TES IV: Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* The Scrolls are fragments of creation from outside time and space.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-23)</sup> They have been variously described as "bits of the divine made substance" with no author,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Urag-9)</sup> "gods-built/created",<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-2)</sup> "blasphemies" which "the gods would fear to tamper with",<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Arngeir-24)</sup> and as points of light in a knowledgeless void.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Rot ES-25)</sup> They are also said to be indestructible.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dexion-6)</sup> While many Scrolls that have been collected are small enough to be held by two hands, it is suggested that the Scrolls can also reach enormous sizes, with the smooth cylindrical metal core of the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) itself said to be a container for a massive Elder Scroll.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-26)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-20)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-mkirc-27)</sup>

The term "Aedric Prophecies" is disputed by some scholars,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LHOT-3)</sup> and the Moth Sister [Terran Arminus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Sister_Terran_Arminus) asserts that "prophecy" is an inaccurate word for what amounts to a side-effect of the Scrolls' intrinsic nature. According to her, the Scrolls are "woven into" the future, "as well as the present, and the past, and every other aspect of this reality we call the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md)".<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MSTAAYQ-16)</sup> Like the Scrolls themselves, the prophecies within are not created, but rather have always been and will always be. <sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAESMP-15)</sup> While any connection the Scrolls have to the Aedra are still unclear, the Scrolls are in no way fragments of [Aka](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-mkirc-27)</sup>

While it is impossible for magic to affect the contents of an Elder Scroll that has come to pass,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LHOT-3)</sup> the Ayleid king [Valentis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Valentis) developed an incantation which binds his [brother](Lore_Celemaril.md) to a specific future described in an Elder Scroll. It is uncertain whether this incantation causes the future in the scroll to come to pass immediately, or if the scroll's contents and Celemaril truly become bound together in some way.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Blades-28)</sup> The dragon Shulkunaak explains that the Scrolls can *affect* all possible futures.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Shulkunaak-29)</sup> The scholar [Urag gro-Shub](Lore_Urag_gro-Shub.md) claimed that the Scrolls are a reflection of all possible futures and pasts, and that even falsehoods one might read within them are true.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Urag-9)</sup>

According to the dragon [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md), the Scrolls do not exist, yet always have existed. He believed that the Last Dragonborn's carrying of an Elder Scroll was irrefutable proof that the Last Dragonborn was [Doom-Driven](Lore_Hero.md), blessed by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), and had the "very [bones of the earth"](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) at their disposal.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-2)</sup>

The Cult of the Ancestor Moth believes that the contents of the Elder Scrolls "exceed even the gods, both aedra and daedra." The Cult implies some connection between the Scrolls and the "dissipated" [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of mortals, claiming that both exist in a state where time is irrelevant.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-POTAM-13)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TDS-30)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MSTAAYQ-16)</sup> The Moth Priest [Dexion Evicus](Skyrim_Dexion_Evicus.md) insists that the Scrolls "have a mind of their own", and can appear, disappear, and allow themselves to be read at will.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dexion-6)</sup> The Scrolls cannot be reliably counted at all, because their number is not fixed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAOTS-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-31)</sup>

The scholar [Septimus Signus](Lore_Septimus_Signus.md), who is claimed to have been driven mad by the Scrolls, argues to the contrary that the knowledge Scrolls grant "is a passing awareness when compared to the encompassing mind of divinity." He similarly claims that the Scrolls see "left and right in the stream of time. The future and past are as one: Sometimes they even look up. What do they see then? What if they dive in? Then the madness begins."<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Septimus-10)</sup> He also was the author of a book titled [Ruminations on the Elder Scrolls](Lore_Ruminations_on_the_Elder_Scrolls.md), a "philosophical view" on their role.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Rot ES-25)</sup>

Some texts accredit [Mnemo-Li](Lore_Mnemo-Li.md) with collecting "all the [songs](Lore_Music.md)" made by mortals and the keeping of the Elder Scrolls.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-mkirc-27)</sup>

## History
According to *Herma-Mora and the Moth*, [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Forbidden Knowledge, long ago sought the secrets of ancestor moths and their connections to the [fjyrons](Lore_Fjyron.md) of mortal souls, so that he may access the secrets of the Elder Scrolls. He encountered a lone moth who refused to give away any confidential information but managed to bargain with the Great Eye a deal. To showcase that the Daedric Prince was wise and merciful despite greatly wanting to obtain the secrets behind the Elder Scrolls, ancestor moths from then on would wear his eyes on their wings, branded on them by the Prince himself.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAESMP-15)</sup>

In the [Udhendra Nibenu](Lore_Udhendra_Nibenu.md) it is said that, when [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) encountered the "[Two-legged reptiles](Lore_Argonian.md)", he was gifted an Elder Scroll which he placed in his ship's cargo hold. When that one Scroll became many, the ship's [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md) realized the gift's importance and instructed the crew to bring aboard all the Scrolls they could find. This event resulted in the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) becoming the largest known repository of Elder Scrolls.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAESMP-15)</sup>

An Elder Scroll was used by [Felldir the Old](Lore_Felldir_the_Old.md) to cast [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) forward in time, ending his reign in that age. This use of the Elder Scroll shattered time at the spot where Alduin was banished, creating a [Time Wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md) on the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Skyrim AB-32)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-2)</sup>

Some [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) texts suggest the methods used by the Cult of the Ancestor Moth to read the Scrolls might've originated from the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md). The Nedic slaves were tasked to tend to the reading rooms within the White Gold-Tower, where the elves made use of the Ancestor Moths and [Canticle Trees](Lore_Canticle_Tree.md) to learn how to master the world from the Elder Scrolls in their possession. When the Ayleids realized their slaves could also hear the song of the moths, they decided to teach them the secrets of how to read the Scrolls, so as to have them perform the readings and bear the burden in their stead. In secret these Nedic moth tenders spirited away the components of the reading ritual and established their own sacred glades, beginning the tradition that gave rise to the Cult of the Ancestor Moth. There are those within the Cult that believe the secrets of reading the Scrolls were taught by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), or that the moths were first stolen from the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md).The Tradition of naming the Elder Scrolls is said to have also started with the Ayleids, though some within the Cult of the Ancestor Moth are skeptical of this claim.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAESMP-15)</sup>

Long ago, the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) devised a means to extract knowledge from the Scrolls without requiring someone to sacrifice their sight. Complex machinery interfaces with the Scroll and draws out information, inscribing it onto a metal Lexicon which can then be read by those with the requisite knowledge.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Septimus-10)</sup> It is unknown how the quality or quantity of the information gained this way compares to that when read directly from a Scroll.

### Second Era
Hermaeus Mora was known to possess a number of Elder Scrolls circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). They can sometimes be seen floating within vats in his infinite realm of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESApoc-33)</sup>

Numerous Elder Scrolls were stored at the White-Gold Tower within a chamber known variously as the Imperial Library, the Hall of Records, and the Elder Library.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAOTS-5)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TWQ-34)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Oblivion-35)</sup> During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Imperial City fell to hordes of Daedra. To protect the scrolls, the Cult of the Ancestor Moth hid several of them around the grounds of the [Temple of the Ancestor Moths](Lore_Temple_of_the_Ancestor_Moths.md) in northeastern Cyrodiil. Eventually, troops from each alliance found the scrolls and stole them from the moth priests.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONSA-36)</sup> Across Cyrodiil, each alliance built vast holy temples to house the scrolls they had stolen. The temples were built close enough to the battlefields to bestow the scrolls' blessing onto the troops. It is theorized by Moth Priest Euclidius, that the real reason the Scrolls bestowed "blessings" on the troops possessing them was that, in holding them, the troops performed a collective osmotic absorption of the prophecies therein, and were unwittingly influenced to make the decisions that would coincide with the probability of events that would lead them to victory. Unknowingly influenced to make the choices that would help them triumph by the prophecies of the Scrolls.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONPA-37)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-GWD-38)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Arminus-39)</sup> After the war ended, many scrolls were re-housed within the Imperial Library.

During this time the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) minions of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) used an Elder Scroll housed within the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in an attempt to enact the [Planemeld Obverse](Lore_Planemeld.md), a process which would result in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) being pulled into [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), bringing the mortal world under the control of Molag Bal. This plan was ultimately foiled by [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-40)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Terran-14)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Kena-41)</sup> The same year, the warlord [Orpheon](Lore_Orpheon_the_Tactician.md) and his army attacked the monastery of Lep Seclusa, believing the Scroll inside held a prophecy which would force the alliances to bend the knee to him, but he was slain before he could achieve his goal.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-UE-42)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Chana-21)</sup>

### Third Era
While the Cult of the Ancestor Moth would report information to the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), [Ocato](Lore_Ocato.md) had stated that neither the Elder Scrolls nor the ancestor moths had ever provided them with much "practical information".<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-NMI-43)</sup> It is alleged that the rise of Emperor [Pelagius Septim III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) was foretold within the Elder Scrolls.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TWQ-34)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late Third Era, many organizations across the provinces attempted to decipher an Elder Scroll with an artifact, usually a tablet that was key in the process. These included the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md)<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ARThelen-44)</sup> and [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ARTuramane-45)</sup> The [Brotherhood of Seth](Lore_Brotherhood_of_Seth.md) used a code from an ancient map to locate the [Crypt of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md) on an Elder Scroll, so that the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) could acquire a piece of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ARHalfas-46)</sup> Queen Blubamka of [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) tasked the Champion with retrieving a special parchment that was stolen band of [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) led by [Golthog the Dark](Lore_Golthog_the_Dark.md). The parchment was able to decipher an Elder Scroll that would in turn reveal [Fang Lair](Lore_Fang_Lair.md)'s location. The Eternal Champion returned with the parchment from the goblins in [Stonekeep](Lore_Stonekeep.md) and traveled north into Fang Lair, where they found their first piece of the Staff.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ARFang-47)</sup>

Circa [3E 422](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_422),<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-OBGray Fox-48)</sup> Count [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md) wished to retire from his position as leader of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) and return to his wife, Countess Millona Umbranox of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md). However, he had been cursed upon donning the [Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md): his previous identity had been erased from the minds of others, forcing him to assume the identity of the infamous [Gray Fox](Lore_Gray_Fox.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-49)</sup> This curse had been placed upon the Cowl by [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) herself when it was stolen from her by the first Gray Fox, [Emer Dareloth](Lore_Emer_Dareloth.md). Umbranox found himself unable to reveal his true identity to anyone, instead simply appearing to them as a stranger.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-OBGray Fox-48)</sup> Undeterred, Umbranox spent the next eleven years planning a heist to steal a particular Elder Scroll from the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md) in order to break the curse. Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), he enlisted the aid of the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to plan and execute the heist. With the Elder Scroll in his possession, Umbranox invoked its powers to rewrite history as if Dareloth had never stolen the Cowl from Nocturnal, thereby breaking the curse, and allowing the Thieves Guild to benefit from an altered history where their first Guild Master was still able to operate in the normal world of business and society.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TUH-50)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-OBGray Fox-48)</sup>

Following the Palace break-in, rumors circulated that a Scroll had been stolen by a master thief.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-51)</sup> In response to these rumors, an Imperial Librarian attempted to take a complete inventory of the Scrolls, but the effort proved fruitless as their numbers and placement seemed to fluctuate for no discernible reason.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAOTS-5)</sup>

During that same year, the Hero of Kvatch was recruited by [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) priest [Ilav Dralgoner](Lore_Ilav_Dralgoner.md) of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) to investigate [Beldaburo](Lore_Beldaburo.md), believing it was the source of disappearances in the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md). When the Hero discovered a powerful magical barrier there, the priest concluded that such a barrier could only be the result of a Time Wound. Ilav surmised that a Time Would was actively being opened at Beldaburo through the use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) that had been stolen from the Order just years prior, and provided the Hero with a blessing that would allow them to bypass the barrier. The culprit was revealed to be the [lich](Lore_Lich.md) Corelian, who had been experimenting on the kidnap victims using their newfound power. However, Corelian had gradually gone mad from their unnatural existence, and sought to use the Elder Scrolls to travel back in time and prevent themselves from ever becoming a lich. The Hero of Kvatch slew the lich before the Time Wound could be fully opened. This destroyed the Time Wound and restored the flow of time, allowing it to move in accordance with Akatosh's rules once more and thereby saving reality from collapsing. With the threat ended, the Hero was made a Paladin of the Hour by the grateful priest, who could sense time had indeed been healed.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-52)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-53)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONCD-19)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-54)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Around [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Elder Scrolls mysteriously vanished from the Library, and were scattered across Tamriel.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dexion-6)</sup> Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), a previously undiscovered Elder Scroll was found within the statue of the founder of the town of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md), Cyrodiil. As an Elder Scroll can affect all possible futures, the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md) used this Elder Scroll under the guidance of the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) Shulkunaak, to bind the immortal sorcerer-king [Celemaril](Lore_Celemaril.md) to the one future where he failed to escape the spell that was used to imprison him anew, ensuring the end of his threat to the world despite his unkillable nature.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Shulkunaak-29)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Blades-28)</sup> At some point following the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), the Moth Priest [Kellen](Lore_Kellen.md) secured an Elder Scroll amidst an unspecified conflict, and told the story of the Forgotten Hero to his comrades Isha and Nagh to inspire them to follow in the Hero's footsteps.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Forgotten-55)</sup>

The year [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) saw the discovery of three Elder Scrolls. Two Elder Scrolls were discovered in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) itself, one hidden in the Dwemer city [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md) and the other in the ancient ruin of Dimhollow Crypt. The third was located in the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md). One of these Scrolls was used by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to cast himself back to the moment of the creation of the Throat of the World Time Wound and learn the [Shout](Lore_Thu%27um.md) Dragonrend. The three Scrolls were also used by the Dragonborn to divine the location of [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Skyrim-18)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-2)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-56)</sup>

## Notes
- The Elder Scrolls are retroactively the namesake of *[The Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)* franchise. When [*Arena*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena) transitioned from an action combat game into an RPG, "*The Elder Scrolls: Chapter I*" was added to the title, not only to better advertise that it was an RPG (which was a prerogative), but also to evoke a sort of tome like novel, something that new *Chapters* could be written into down the line. The team eventually picked "The Elder Scrolls", which took its name from Tamriel's mystical tomes of knowledge that told of its past, present, and future.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Vijay Interview-57)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-10th Ani-58)</sup> In *Arena*, the Elder Scrolls were established as artifacts that could be used to locate lost locations. Certain messages would also speak of the Elder Scrolls foretelling the hero's adventure begins in the Imperial Dungeons, their fate will be written in the Elder Scrolls, and upon the conclusion of the main quest that the adventure "was but a chapter in the story of the Elder Scrolls". In *Daggerfall*, it is further expanded that the Elder Scrolls have predicted major events.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-DFUG-59)</sup> For many years the scrolls had only been mentioned and described, and the first physical appearance of an Elder Scroll in the games was in *Oblivion*.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TUH-50)</sup>
- In the late 1990s, Bethesda had a plan to make a website with a lot of content about where they had "really" got the *Elder Scrolls* stories, involving fictional accounts of finding them in the three physical Elder Scroll replicas in the basement (two perfectly preserved, one broken, the latter of which was never used). The developers would get to "play" with the Scrolls and mull over the stories within, such as how they "found" the tale of Cyrus and "adapted" it into *[The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)*. The developer diaries would contain "conversations" of how they would need to change the stories found within the Scrolls to make it them playable products, ultimately "compromising" to streamline the esoteric stories into easily digestible video games. It would have also given the developers a way of saying that they knew all future games were going to have discrepancies.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-mkirc-27)</sup> The website ultimately never came to fruition, though [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) referenced this plan by jokingly saying all the Elder Scrolls seen in the games are copies of the "real" Elder Scrolls, found in the form of three giant cylinders kept in the vaults beneath [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-20)</sup>
- [Runes](Lore_Elder_Alphabet.md) written on the Elder Scrolls and their covers seem to be the same kind of runes that are found on the amulets of the Elder Council, which seem to be made of a metal similar to that of the Elder Scrolls' covers and crimped with a similar purple gem.
- The names of the Elder Scrolls that were fought over during the Three Banners War (Alma Ruma, Altadoon, Chim, Ghartok, Mnem, Ni-Mohk) are all [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md) words mentioned in [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md)'s *[Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md)*.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-MDC-60)</sup>
- According to some legends, the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) were in possession of an Elder Scroll, and followed its guidance to arrive to their new home on [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md).<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-SHV1-61)</sup>
- A Moth Priest's interpretation of the contents of an Elder Scroll can be seen in [Terran's Notes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Terran%27s_Notes).
- It is said that prophecies revolving around [Man](Lore_Man.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) tend to be the most common among accounts in the Elder Scrolls but, according to the elders of the Cult of the Ancestor Moth, this was not always so. [Sload](Lore_Sload.md), [Imga](Lore_Imga.md), [Fauns](Lore_Faun.md), and [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) have all been referenced in the prophecies of the Scrolls.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAESMP-15)</sup>
