# Root

[![Root Tunnel concept art](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/SI-concept-RDung01.jpg/200px-SI-concept-RDung01.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-concept-RDung01.jpg) The Root (or **Root System**) is a theoretical living organism that connects all plant life in [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). It encompasses the hollow **Root Tunnels** that grow beneath the realm's surface, as well as the giant [Mushroom Trees](Lore_Mushroom_Tree.md) native to the Isles.

The tunnels, commonly called "root dungeons", grow at an incredibly slow rate, although it can vary from a few feet every month in places to only a few inches every few decades. Over the millennia, the system grew to cover the entire realm, and it is theorized that the tunnels act as the veins of the realm, connecting every plant. The tectonic forces exerted on the Root results in the vast number of [amber](Lore_Amber.md) deposits found in these tunnels, which is used by the mortal inhabitants of the realm to craft high quality weapons and armor. The [Gnarl](Lore_Gnarl.md) are said to be symbiotic caretakers of the Root, guarding the tunnels and cleaning up amber until they eventually fuse with the walls after growing too large.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ba S-1)</sup> The tunnels often serve as homes for the various species of flora and fauna, and are regularly explored by adventurers. Entry is possible by opening the thin membranes at the base of giant Mushroom Trees.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SI-2)</sup>

Although [heretical](Lore_Heretics.md), it has been speculated that the Root acts as a conduit for the madness of the realm by transporting color from somber [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) to vibrant [Mania](Lore_Mania.md), and thus ecologically resulting in the mental illnesses of each region's inhabitants. Further, it is thought that destroying the Root and its Gnarl protectors would cure the realm's inhabitants and dethrone [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ba S-1)</sup> This may have some semblance of truth, as the lifeblood of the Isles collects in the Pools of Madness in a root tunnel known as [the Fountainhead](Lore_The_Fountainhead.md), beneath the [Palace of Sheogorath](Lore_Palace_of_Sheogorath.md). These pools feed the Font of Madness, which would traditionally be corrupted by Order right before [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md)'s arrival to take the palace at the end of each [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SI-2)</sup>

## Tamrielic Root Systems
Mushroom Trees and their saplings are capable of living on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) seemingly disconnected from the Root, as can be seen on [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md) and the floating island in [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EESO-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SI-2)</sup>

On the 2nd of First Seed, [4E 200](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_200), a [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) mage named [Thoron](Lore_Thoron.md) came into contact with a lump of Sheogorath-shaped amber, granting him a vision of the lands of Mania.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> As he subsequently descended into manic insanity, Thoron began collecting items related to the Shivering Isles from all over [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), hoping to use their connection with the realm to once again reach Mania.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-T2-5)</sup> By the 15th of Midyear, [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Thoron had come into possession of the [Sword of Jyggalag](Lore_Sword_of_Jyggalag.md), which began to reveal to him a method by which the barriers between Tamriel and the Shivering Isles could be broken. He had taken occupation of the sewers beneath the city of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), which the Mad God had a history with,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> where a Root tunnel had taken hold.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-T2-5)</sup> He began to use the Sword to magically cut through to the Shivering Isles, allowing flora, fauna, and objects to cross over into Tamriel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TJv3-7)</sup> Over time, these brief openings allowed the Root tunnels to grow and expand out from beneath Solitude. These tunnels were seemingly maintained by corrupted [Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md), which began to take on Gnarl characteristics.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CC-8)</sup> The Sword ultimately manipulated Thoron into helping it break through into the Shivering Isles,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TJv3-7)</sup> but this ritual was stopped circa 4E 201 by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who killed Thoron and removed the Sword from the heart of the burgeoning Root system.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

## Notes
- The Root is similar in nature to the natural caves of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), which originally served as the capillaries of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), pulsing with the divine essence of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
- The Mushroom Trees are similar in appearance to the giant [Emperor Parasol](Lore_Emperor_Parasol.md) mushrooms of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).
