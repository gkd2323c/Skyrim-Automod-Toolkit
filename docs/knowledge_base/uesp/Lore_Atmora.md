# Atmora

| --- | --- |
| Type | Continent |
| Continent | Atmora |
| Demonym(s) | Atmoran, Altmoran |

Atmora, or **Altmora** in [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md), (meaning *"Elder Wood"*),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BAM-3)</sup> and referred to as "the land of truth" by Atmorans,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NOYB-4)</sup> is a [continent](Lore_Places.md) north of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), where tradition has it the first [humans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Human) came from in [ancient days](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1Pro-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-6)</sup> It has been claimed to be the ancestral land for all [Nedic peoples](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BAM-3)</sup> the ancestors of the [Cyrodilians](Lore_Imperial.md), [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), and [Nords](Lore_Nord.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FC-7)</sup>

The continent was once teeming with life, but has since been frozen over in an event dubbed "the [Frostfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostfall)", smothering it with frost, placing it in a permanent winter,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSI-8)</sup> and potentially even freezing it in [time](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Time).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-9)</sup> The cause of the event is uncertain, though one tale attributes it as revenge by the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) for the genocide of their people at the hands of the Atmorans.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSI-8)</sup> The closest known group to inhabit areas north of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) are the [Sea Giants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Giant), who [sail the waters](Lore_Sea_Transportation.md) of the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SGP-10)</sup>

## History
What little is known of Atmora is derived mainly from ancient myths. The [Anuad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuad) creation myth from the [Mythic Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mythic_Era) claims the land of Atmora was once part of [Old Ehlnofey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Ehlnofey) (which later became Tamriel), and became a separate continent as a result of a [war](Lore_Wars.md) between the Wandering and Old [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) that reshaped the face of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), with the Wandering of Atmora becoming the Nords.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TAA-11)</sup> [The Monomyth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Monomyth) speculates that after [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) created Men, they chased the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) out of Altmora and he shattered the land into many.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> According to Nordic beliefs, after men were formed on the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) when [the sky](Lore_Kyne.md) breathed onto [the land](Lore_Skyrim.md), they crossed the seas north to Atmora.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-12)</sup> The [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) faith teaches that [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El) led the original Aldmer against the armies of Lorkhan in mythic times, vanquishing him and establishing the first [elven](Lore_Mer.md) [kingdoms](Lore_Empires.md) of the Aldmer, Altmora and Old Ehlnofey. Atmoran myths hold that the Nords were lead by [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor), [Stuhn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stuhn), and [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md) to victory over their Aldmeri oppressors time and again.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOF-13)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) Ysgramor in the [Hall of Valor](Lore_Sovngarde.md) Regardless of their origins, it was a not a single invasion that the first humans came to Tamriel from Atmora, but a series of them over hundreds of years, creating many different Nedic cultures, the new-arrived Atmorans always clashing with the generations that had already established themselves.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-6)</sup> For centuries, Tamriel had served as a "safety valve" for Atmorans who, for whatever reason, could not stay in Atmora. In the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), while civil war raged in Atmora, a fleet of Atmorans migrated and settled in [northern Tamriel](Lore_Skyrim.md). Among them were [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) and his two sons, [Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol) and [Ylgar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ylgar).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BAM-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FC-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/SR-place-Saarthal.jpg/200px-SR-place-Saarthal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Saarthal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Saarthal.jpg) The ruins of Saarthal, an Atmoran city Not long after founding the city of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md) in the [Ice Fields](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ice_Fields) of the [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md) region, the Atmoran settlers were massacred by the Snow Elves in what became known as the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BAM-3)</sup> Ysgramor and his sons escaped and fled back to Atmora, where they assembled the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) and led [the Return](Lore_The_Return.md) to seek vengeance upon the elves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BAM-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-12)</sup> The massive shipyards of the Atmoran port of [Jylkurfyk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jylkurfyk) were commissioned to build a fleet that would carry the Companions across the Sea of Ghosts, including [Darumzu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darumzu), [Harakk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Harakk),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SOTR2-14)</sup>, and [Jorrvaskr](Lore_Jorrvaskr.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SOTR7-15)</sup> When Ysgramor and his Companions finally landed at [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md) on Tamriel's northern coast, they drove the elves from northern Tamriel and then went on to defeat them on the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-FOTSP-16)</sup> The territory the Companions conquered formed the precursor of modern-day [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

Under the reign of Ysgramor's descendant [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) in the second and third centuries of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Skyrim was consolidated as an independent kingdom. Harald relinquished all of his holdings in Atmora, and Atmoran mercenaries left Skyrim and purportedly returned to their homeland.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FC-7)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-12)</sup> Atmorans would continue to travel to Skyrim over the following centuries, notably including [Ysmir Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), but in declining numbers.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-FSOKW-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-RTR-18)</sup> The last Atmoran "invasion" of Tamriel was recorded in [1E 68](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_68), when two Atmoran ships laden with corpses begged to make port in Tamriel.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-19)</sup> The last purported emigrant from Atmora known to history is [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) in the ninth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md); legends relate that he sailed to Skyrim and spent his youth among the Nords.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE1C-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TAH-21)</sup>

It was around the time of Ysgramor's migration that Atmora began to undergo an inexplicable shift in climate, the harbinger of the continent's demise.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SOTR7-15)</sup> Once known as a green, albeit colder land, Atmora was gradually overcome by "the frost fall" and rendered largely uninhabitable,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1Sky-12)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-19)</sup> becoming a "frozen continent".<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PBE-22)</sup> There is some suggestion that, as of the early [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Atmora was still inhabited,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWQ4-23)</sup> but expeditions there in the mid- to late-Third Era only found a place of permanent winter with little life and no sign of human habitation. It is believed that Atmora no longer supports any civilized life, and those people who did not leave it for Tamriel ultimately succumbed to the ever-worsening climate.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-19)</sup> The freezing gales coming from Atmora could be felt as far as Skyrim.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SSC-24)</sup>

## Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-crown_store-Frostbane_Dragon_Priest.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Frostbane_Dragon_Priest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Frostbane_Dragon_Priest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Frostbane_Dragon_Priest.jpg) Atmoran dragon priest garb designed to withstand extreme cold Early Nordic society is reflective of Atmoran society. Many Nordic customs were inherited from Atmora, and the continent's culture has been highly influential on Tamriel.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-19)</sup> Atmorans were a sea-faring people, though they purportedly had no knowledge of agriculture and survived off of hunting. The Atmorans were purportedly engaged in ceaseless warfare.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FC-7)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-19)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SOTR19-25)</sup> Atmorans were also a pre-literate society and lacked a formal writing system of their own; Ysgramor is credited with developing a runic transcription of Nord speech based on Elvish principles, and is consequently considered the first human historian.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BAM-3)</sup> While Atmorans were considered one race, it is implied that there were regionally-distinct racial groups, such as the "sinewy long folk" whose "ruddy skin matched the dawn" that inhabited the eastern edge of Atmora.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SOTR24-26)</sup>

Atmoran settlers of Tamriel brought with them traditions such as naming ceremonies and their religion of animal worship.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SOTR2-14)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-THOTR-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TDW-28)</sup> They deified the hawk, wolf, snake, moth, owl, whale, bear, and fox, but preeminent among all animals were the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TDW-28)</sup> At the height of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md)'s influence, before the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), the [dragon priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) held as much power as kings, ruling in the stead of the aloof dragons they worshipped. In Atmora, the priests demanded tribute and set down laws and codes of living that kept peace between dragons and men. In Tamriel, however, they reigned as tyrants and made virtual slaves of the rest of the population.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TDW-28)</sup> Atmorans also passed down myths involving demons, especially [Herma-Mora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Herma-Mora), "the Woodland Man", who would become a part of the Nordic pantheon.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOF-13)</sup>

## Geography
Though a cold continent, Atmora had marshes in the east<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TOo TO-29)</sup> and forests with running streams.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-FAHM-30)</sup>

## Fauna
[Giants](Lore_Giant.md) are suggested to have also originated from Atmora,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-G:AD-35)</sup> supported by when Ysgramor met Giant-Wives there<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-FAHM-30)</sup> as well as tales of ancient giants and Atmoran titans.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LTTO-36)</sup> The animals that provided the basis for Atmoran talismans, such as bears, wolves, owls, moths, hawks, whales, and snakes, are believed to have lived on the continent. Deer, hares and foxes also lived there.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-FAHM-30)</sup> The [Chub Loon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chub_Loon) is theorized to be native to Atmora. According to Nord myth, Atmoran Chub Loons were much larger, standing as tall as two warriors and with a squawk that could start avalanches. Modern Atmoran Chub Loons are much more diminutive in size, comparable to their Tamrielic brethren, but with distinct white plumage and a blue beak.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ACL-34)</sup> Atmoran [Bantam Guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bantam_Guar) are fur-less, cold-blooded reptiles with icy blue eyes suspected to be from there, though this is considered dubious at best.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ABG-37)</sup> According to Nord legend, bears originated on the continent of Atmora, leading some to suspect that the [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) monks went there to find the original model of their Psijic Bear Exemplar.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PBE-22)</sup> The fiercely noble Atmoran [Snow Bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Bear) have friendly demeanors while they are young,<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ASBC-38)</sup> and are said to have been brought from Atmora by Ysgramor himself, who deemed them the "only worthy avatars" of the ancient Nord Bear totem.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ASB-39)</sup>
