# Warlock's Ring

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/SR-item-Warlock%27s_Ring.jpg/200px-SR-item-Warlock%27s_Ring.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Warlock%27s_Ring.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Warlock%27s_Ring.jpg) Warlock's Ring The Warlock's Ring (also known as the **Ring of the Warlock**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup> is one of the most popular [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of myth and fable originally owned by the Arch Mage [Syrabane](Lore_Syrabane.md), the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) god-ancestor of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) and patron of the mages. In [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s ancient history, Syrabane reportedly saved all of the continent by judicious use of this ring, and ever since, it has helped adventurers with less lofty goals. In appearance, it is a ring with a large gemstone, either red or green in color. It is best known for its ability to reflect spells cast at its wearer and to fortify the wearer's speed and health, though it may have additional powers. It is said no adventurer can wear the Warlock's Ring for long, for it is said that the ring is Syrabane's alone to command.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-2)</sup>

## History

### First Era
In the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Syrabane judiciously used the ring to save many from the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup> Because of this, he is attributed with helping to save the entire continent of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The ring has passed hands many times, helping adventurers to reach their goals. No one may possess the ring for long, as it is said that the ring is Syrabane's alone to command.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-2)</sup>

### Second Era
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWYB-4)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Warlock's Ring among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TA-6)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Warlock's Ring and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWFR-7)</sup> As of then "by Syrabane's ring" was one of Altmeri exclamations.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SEA-8)</sup>

### Third Era
The Warlock's Ring was uncovered by the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Hero.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in an unknown province.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup>

In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), an adventurer obtained the Warlock's Ring after doing a task for their knightly order. The agent defeated an [arch-lich](Lore_Lich.md) in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) area that was raising an army of undead. After putting a stop to the undead menace, the agent discovered a clue to the ring's whereabouts. Following the note's instructions, the agent followed the trail to a secluded wizard, who claimed that the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) stole the artifact from his great grandfather. After putting the spirit of the wizard's great grandfather to rest, the ghost revealed the location of the Mages Guild branch that stole the artifact from him. The agent ventured there and took the ring by brute force. However, there are conflicting accounts that the artifact centered around this story was actually a different one altogether, with [Chrysamere](Lore_Chrysamere.md) (the most prominent), the [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md), and the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md) taking its place in the story.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DFTLA-9)</sup> A fake copy of the Warlock's Ring also appeared in the region before the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) occurred; it was created by a mage in an attempt to bribe an agent of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) who was sent to assassinate them. The fake ring functioned identically to the true artifact, but after several days it crumbled to dust. This account is also shaky, as some accounts claim [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) or the Staff of Magnus was offered in its place.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Another source claims the ring was in the possession of [Lady Brisienna Magnessen](Lore_Brisienna.md), a [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) operative. Brisienna offered the ring as payment in return for the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), which would give the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) control of [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_DF)</sup>

The ring resurfaced several years later in the possession of Vindamea Drethan, a powerful sorceress who lived in a cave on an island off the shore of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), along [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md). News of this reached the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Drethan was killed by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), who then claimed the ring.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Later that year, the Nerevarine sold the ring to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md), who put it on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

### Fourth Era
The Warlock's Ring re-emerged around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), when [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) came across it during their journey. The ring is thought to have been looted from an extravagant chest.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

The ring later came into the possession of an elderly mage named Brutius who lived in [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md). An avid fisherman, Brutius would lure victims into the marsh under the pretense of holding a fishing competition. Once far enough out of town, he would ambush his victims with help from his gang of vagrant bandit mages. Brutius eventually lost the Warlock's Ring when defending himself against a hammer blow from one of his victims. The ring came off and fell into the river east of Morthal, just northwest of Myrwatch.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Brutius attempted to ambush the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), resulting in the deaths of him and his fellow bandits. The Dragonborn learned of the location of the Warlock's Ring from his journal and managed to fish it out of the river.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_DF) | In *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, [Gortwog](Lore_Gortwog.md) originally offered the same artifact as Lady Brisienna, the Warlock's Ring, in exchange for the Totem of Tiber Septim. Gortwog's reward was changed in patch v1.07.213 so he can offer Chrysamere instead. This patch was unsuccessful and stripped him of offering anything at all, requiring a [third-party fix](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall_Mod:DFQFIX) to correct it. |
| --- | --- |
- Previously in the [Myrwatch](Skyrim_Myrwatch.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md), a guild of rogue mages from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) was mentioned to have found the Warlock's Ring on their journeys in Morrowind. It was brought to their headquarters at Myrwatch and stored there, to be held for use in the coming "great war" foreseen by their augurs.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-17)</sup> The guild intended to assemble a great number of legendary artifacts in preparation for this conflict, but failed to obtain any others. It transpired that this great war was in fact the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), which the mages were ill-prepared to face.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> However, the artifact wasn't featured in this Creation and the text was later changed in a patch to remove this detail.
