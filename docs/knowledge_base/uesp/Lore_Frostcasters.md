# Frostcasters

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/ON-crown_store-Frostcaster_Style.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Frostcaster_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Frostcaster_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Frostcaster_Style.jpg) Frostcasters working with Stalhrim The Frostcasters are [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) shamans of [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md) known for their mastery of Frost [Magic](Lore_Magic.md). Not all Skaal shamans are Frostcasters; the title is reserved for those who specialize in Frost Magic. They were known to skillfully integrate [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) into their arms and armor, creating decorative pieces.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Style-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Style2-2)</sup>

King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) had a collection of Frostcaster clothing, documented by the renowned Imperial Ethnographer Doctor [Alfidia Lupus](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Alfidia_Lupus) during her visit to his Embassy in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Style-1)</sup> Apart from the Frostcaster garbs, stalhrim armors of a distinctive style known as Stalhrim Frostguard were also documented by Doctor Alfidia Lupus.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SF-3)</sup>

The Skaal tribe's shamans domesticated Snow Leopard [Sabre Cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md) as mounts, intending to keep them exclusive. However, it is unknown if there were Frostcasters among them.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SLSC-4)</sup> While information about Frostcasters is confined to the Second Era, the shamanistic practices and shamans of the Skaal continued to hold a role in Skaal culture into the [Third](Lore_Third_Era.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Morrowind-5)</sup> and [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). However, none of these shamans were referred to as Frostcasters.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Skyrim-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim2-7)</sup>

While there are no known named Frostcasters, notable individuals like Skaal Shaman Rik,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Rik-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Garb-9)</sup> Korst Wind-Eye,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Morrowind-5)</sup> Storn Crag-Strider, and his daughter Frea were recognized as shamans within the Skaal tribe.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Skyrim-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim2-7)</sup> There were also priests, some attempting to spread the faith of the All-Maker on the mainland,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Greymoor-10)</sup> and [witches](Lore_Witch.md) known as [Windcallers](Lore_Windcallers.md) dedicated to the All-Maker.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Garb-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Face-11)</sup>
