# Alkosh

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/ON-item-Alkosh%27s_Hourglass%2C_Replica.png/200px-ON-item-Alkosh%27s_Hourglass%2C_Replica.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Alkosh%27s_Hourglass,_Replica.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Alkosh%27s_Hourglass,_Replica.png) Alkosh depicted alongside his Hourglass Alkosh is a pre- [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) Dynasty [Anaquinine](Lore_Anequina.md) deity, also known as the **Dragon King of Cats**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWS-2)</sup> the **First Cat**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WOCMA-3)</sup> the **Highmane**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWS-2)</sup> and the **Great Cat King of Time**.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTOD-4)</sup> He is depicted as a fearsome dragon, swift and graceful but also powerful and clever, and radiant as the sun, which the Khajiiti say "is just a real big cat".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-5)</sup> He is the god of Time,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WOCMA-3)</sup> and the king of [dragons](Lore_Dragons.md)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Hats-6)</sup>, a variation on the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), and thus an "[Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) -as-culture-hero" for the earliest [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md). Alkosh's worship was co-opted during the establishment of the [Riddle'Thar](Lore_Riddle%27Thar.md), but the Dragon King remains popular in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s wastelands.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup>

Alkosh is said to be at once the weaver of the tapestry of time and the incarnation of the tapestry itself, the threads that comprise it unspool from the tip of his tail and, should the thread end, there will be nothing, as all exists within time's domain. The Dragon King of Time, like [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md), can see across all the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) of [Fate](Lore_Fate.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bladesongs-8)</sup>

## Mythology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg/200px-ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg) Aspect of Alkosh in a deep slumber [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/LG-cardart-Descendant_of_Alkosh.png/200px-LG-cardart-Descendant_of_Alkosh.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Descendant_of_Alkosh.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Descendant_of_Alkosh.png) Descendant of Alkosh (Legends) The Khajiit believe the [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) phenomenon occurs when Alkosh has been broken.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WWYDB-9)</sup> In some pre-ri'Datta beliefs, Alkosh is said to have appeared after the disappearance of [Akha](Lore_Akha.md), taking over the role and crown of the First Cat, and inheriting rule over the myriad kingdoms of Akha along the Many Paths. While he ruled over the children of Akha, he was soon [overthrown](Lore_Middle_Dawn.md) and his body scattered on the West Wind. It was [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md) who flew along the Many Paths and put Alkosh back together, and now they both "safeguard the Many Paths from the [wayward children](Lore_Dragons.md) of Akha" from his unions with the [Winged Serpent of the East](Lore_Winged_Serpent_of_the_East.md), the [Dune Queen of the West](Lore_Dune_Queen_of_the_West.md), and the [Mother Mammoth of the North](Lore_Mother_Mammoth_of_the_North.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWS-2)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

Some [Moon-Singer](Lore_Moon-Singers.md) songs link Alkosh to the origins of the Khajiiti [demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) and hero [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md). In the song Alkosh, perpetually grim due to the challenges of guarding time, refuses to be cheered up by either [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) or [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md), dismissing the words of the former and dispatching the latter with a roar. The task is left to [Sheggorath](Lore_Sheggorath.md), who finally manages to make Alkosh laugh by telling a joke about [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). Alkosh laughs for thirty days and nights, shaking the sky and making the moons dance until, finally, a single tear of joy falls rolls down his cheek and lands onto Elsweyr where Khunzar-ri emerges from its depths, fully grown and ready for adventure.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOKRO-10)</sup>

It is said Alkosh repelled an elven pogrom of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) during the mythic times of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by descending from the heavens as a shooting star and shouting the invader off the summit of [Sunspire](Lore_Sunspire.md) and out of Elsweyr,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup> leaving behind a "wound" in the sky.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-5)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) this [Time Wound](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) would make Sunspire the target of the dragon Nahviintaas, who used his own [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to start tearing the wound wider, in an attempt to rewrite time and restore draconic rule.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-5)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nahv-11)</sup>

The Khajiit believe that Alkosh is responsible for preserving the stability of the world, he is said to maintain the steady movement of the moons, [Jode](Lore_Masser.md) and [Jone](Lore_Secunda.md), it is believed that without him they would freeze in place, allowing terrible things to slip through the Lunar Lattice.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-5)</sup> It is said that all time flows from Alkosh and that, without him, the world would either sit lifeless or churn like a boiling pot. Because of this, staving off both total chaos and stagnation, his role is considered the most important among all the gods.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Natrada-12)</sup> It is said that Alkosh watches over time's tapestry, whenever a "snag" appears to threaten it, Alkosh realigns the threads with his claws and the aid of the Pride of Alkosh.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Pride-13)</sup>

In pre-ri'Datta Khajiiti belief, the Khajiiti followers of Alkosh are urged to pray to him not for his strength or his mighty roar, but rather for his sense of duty and purpose.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWS-2)</sup> The Khajiit acknowledge both [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) as aspects of Alkosh.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Natrada-12)</sup> One pre-ri'Datta Khajiit account describes how, during the Middle-Dawn, [Boethra](Lore_Boethiah.md) heard the [Selectives](Lore_Marukhati_Selective.md) speaking lies in a way that made them true, drawing runes that were attempting to make it so that none of Akha, Alkosh, Alkhan or any of Akha's children ever existed. It was only through her intervention, *"cutting concepts at strange angles"*, that the world began to spin again in proper time.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bladesongs-8)</sup>

It is common among the Khajiit to fashion wooden religious fetishes that depict Alkosh as a reclining cat with its jaws open wide.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Contraband A-14)</sup>.

Ardent worshipers of Alkosh use ritual implements associated with the deity such as sacred time-lost sand that causes whoever touches it to experience memories of the distant past and moments from their future yet to be experienced, sanctified white-gold ingots, and engraved glass-blowing tools, to craft sacred hourglasses capable of altering local perception of [time](Lore_Time_Magic.md), allowing them to experience the different times of day at will and making it seem as if time's standing still during those times.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Soulfire-15)</sup>

## Pride of Alkosh
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/ON-place-Shrine_to_Alkosh.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shrine_to_Alkosh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_to_Alkosh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_to_Alkosh.jpg) Shrine to Alkosh [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/ON-place-Pridehome_Temple.jpg/200px-ON-place-Pridehome_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Pridehome_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Pridehome_Temple.jpg) Pridehome Temple The Pride of Alkosh is an order of Khajiiti warriors dedicated to Alkosh, comprised of the [Forgotten Manes](Lore_Mane.md), would be Manes who were born under the eclipse, making them more attuned to the influence of the moons, but also to the call of the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md) and [Dark Moon](Lore_Dark_Moon.md), and so to becoming [Dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md). As cubs they are taken to [Pridehome](Lore_Pridehome.md), where they are trained as warriors and are taught Alkosh's wisdom, becoming the "secret defenders" who serve as the Dragon King's "claws" that help catch and pull the dangling threads of time's tapestry.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Pride-13)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Hizuni-16)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Za.27ji-17)</sup>

The Pride of Alkosh and [Pridehome](Lore_Pridehome.md) were founded by [Ja'darri the Endless](Lore_Ja%27darri.md), a member of [Reman](Lore_Reman.md)'s [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), ally to the dragon [Nahfahlaar](Lore_Nahfahlaar.md)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESODK-19)</sup> and Forgotten Mane. After nearly succumbing to the call of the Dark Moon, Khenarthi appeared and transported Ja'darri to the darkness within her own heart where, now armed with a lantern and blade that shone with Alkosh's light, she overcame its dark influence.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup> Upon her victory Ja'darri was presented with the [Mask of Alkosh](Lore_Mask_of_Alkosh.md) and tasked with defeating the dragon [Laatvulon](Lore_Laatvulon.md) whose actions threatened time's tapestry. The prideful Nahfahlaar refused to awaken the Mask's power, a requirement for using its might set by Alkosh who "looks at mortals with a weary eye", leading to Ja'darri's death, after which Khenarthi raised her to a [place](Lore_Spilled_Sand.md) beyond even the [Sands Behind the Stars](Lore_Aetherius.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Nuziwa-20)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-18)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup>

Just like Ja'darri, upon death members of the Pride are raised to a place beyond even the Sands Behind the Stars and all that is known by Khenarthi.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Za.27ji-17)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup> These champions of Alkosh, like Pridehome, are said to then exist outside the tapestry of time and linear progression. It is said that in some sense they both have always existed and will always exist, and that Pridehome and the Pride were simply woven into the tapestry by Alkosh, allowing them to also interact with those within linear time. Because of this extratemporal nature, each Clan Mother of the Pride of Alkosh is simultaneously both the first and the last to occupy the position. This extratemporal nature is said to also apply in some sense to dragons, allowing them to return even when seemingly vanquished, and to the "Doom To Come", the time when dragons return to bring imbalance to the world. As both the Doom and the Pride will always exist, the champions of Alkosh prepare for the time when dragons will return and bring disorder to the world again.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Pridehome-21)</sup>

The members of the Pride are said to be entrusted with Alkosh's secrets. Clan Mothers of the Pride are said to be able to see into the tapestry of time to discern coming events. <sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Tadali-22)</sup>

Alkosh is said to sometimes bestow his champions with a feline companion, a lion made of pure light with which they'll ride to fulfill their mission.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Blessed-23)</sup>

A song about the trials of a Khajiit seeking to brave the trials of the Halls of the Highmane, claim the Mask of Alkosh, and join the Pride to stand before Ja'darri herself in the end, circulated during the mid [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Song-24)</sup>

The Tale of Dro'Zira, a Khajiit account of the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) describes how [Ra'Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), who had been granted the "roar of Lorkhaj", spoke to the moons and commanded them to "move to their fullness in the sky" to turn a band of warriors from the Pride of Alkosh, who had heard the roar of the Great Cat King of Time and raced to meet his [Voice](Lore_Thu%27um.md), crossing all of Tamriel in three days for that is how fast the Pride's warriors were back then, into [senche](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) and strip them of all reason, having them fight at his side, as he could not bring himself to kill them. When Ra'Wulfharth later returned to [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) he called his people to him, but only one of these changed Khajiit, Dro'Zira, remembered and answered the summons. Dro'Zira is said to have carried the Ash King up the Red Mountain on his back, so he could strike at the heart of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) forces, and to have saved him by pouncing on [Dumalacath](Lore_Dumac.md) the Dwarf-Orc, who held his blade to Wulfharth's throat. For this service Lorkhaj is said to have "returned Dro'Zira from the lands of [Sheggorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)", though the other changed Khajiit grew smaller and lost their cunning altogether.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTOD-4)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Mask of Alkosh
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-item-Mask_of_Alkosh.jpg/200px-ON-item-Mask_of_Alkosh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Mask_of_Alkosh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Mask_of_Alkosh.jpg) The Mask of Alkosh The [Mask of Alkosh](Lore_Mask_of_Alkosh.md) is a holy relic of the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) -Cat Alkosh, his light willing it into existence to help mend the tapestry of time, and is worn by the champions of the Pride of Alkosh. It was first worn by the [Ja'darri](Lore_Ja%27darri.md) the Endless, the first champion of Alkosh and the founder of the Pride of Alkosh. A Dragon must awaken the dormant power within the mask before it can be used. When the mask is empowered, it imbues its wielder, known as a Mask-Bearer, with extraordinary power.

## Realm
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg/200px-ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg) The Spilled Sand (Aspect of Alkosh slumbering in the background) The [Spilled Sand](Lore_Spilled_Sand.md) is a strange plane of existence that is associated with Alkosh. It consists of an endless expanse of sand interspersed with an oasis of golden trees, and a colossal slumbering golden dragon with scales of pure gold. The twin moons, Jode and Jone, can be seen in the sky alongside a series of [stars](Lore_Stars.md). The realm is described as not a place but rather a "story that has and will be told time and time again" and "myth made manifest", it is said to exist at once within and outside the tapestry of time, and beyond even the Sands Behind the Stars.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-18)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup>

When questioned on whether the Spilled Sand is the realm of Alkosh itself, Ja'darri would not outright confirm it, answering instead that Alkosh is both weaver and embodiment of time's tapestry and all always exist within time's realm regardless of where they are.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-7)</sup> The Mask of Alkosh allows for one to journey to this realm when awoken via the blood of a dragon.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Dragonguard-25)</sup>
