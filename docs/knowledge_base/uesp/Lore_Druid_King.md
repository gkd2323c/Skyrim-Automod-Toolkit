The Druid King (or **Druid Queen** and **Archdruid**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LADCG-1)</sup> is more accurately described as a title rather than a person. It is a position for the highest authority among the [druids](Lore_Druid.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LABHI-2)</sup> and a philosophical figure that is revered in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARExclamations-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NWD-4)</sup> The role of Druid King is not inherited, as titles among the druids are earned.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONGTSC-5)</sup>

The Druid King has the authority to bind and command the nature spirits<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> safely,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODL-7)</sup> entities that are as unpredictable as nature itself.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONS-8)</sup> The Druid King has dominion over nature in that they can bring prosperity or make nature run amok.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODL-7)</sup>

## History
Druidic history is primarily oral history,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LADCG-1)</sup> for many records have been lost, and druidkind has been nearly wiped out several times throughout history.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODL-7)</sup> One of the many myths for the origins of druidism say a Nedic figure known as Druid Queen [Galen](Lore_Galen_(person).md) was one of the first to channel the songs of Y'ffre, and became the first Archdruid.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LADCG-1)</sup> Since then, druids have been ruled over by a line of Druid Kings up to a point.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LABHI-2)</sup>

If Man or Mer came to High Rock first is debated in scholarly circles. Some believe that Nedic civilization and their Druid King were already established in the province when the Aldmer arrived,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MDBMM-9)</sup> while others claim that the Nedes arrived in High Rock afterwards, and encountered the elves gradually with a variety of reactions and outcomes.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FC-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3High-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LSP:OAC-12)</sup>

After the fracture of the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) into druidic circles (the [Eldertide](Lore_Eldertide_Circle.md), [Firesong](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md), and [Stonelore](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md) Circles), the Druid King's rule was replaced by the Draoife, a ruling council of influential druids from each circle.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESODR-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Draoife_Storystone-14)</sup> Archdruid became a title for a high ranked druid that oversee lesser ranked druid's training in the True Way, and are advised by elder druids.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> There are many archdruids per circle.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESODR-13)</sup>

The last Druid King was murdered at the hands of the heretical Allwither Order, who possibly wanted the Ivy Throne to enact revenge on their former Elven oppressors.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONSHADG-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESODruid King-17)</sup> Druids hold on to the [Dream of Kasorayn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Dream_of_Kasorayn), a prophecy detailing two possible futures. When the druidic circles are reunited and elect a new Druid King, they may bring the Green Renewal (also known as the Green Reward), in which they would teach everyone to live in harmony with nature. The other possibility was the Green Scourge, in which nature would run amok among the tyranny of the druids.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODL-7)</sup>

The Firesong Circle wish to bring about the revival of the Druids of Galen by reuniting the druid circles<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-18)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-ESOYoutube Firesong Tour-19)</sup> and crowning a new Druid King. Circa 2E 582, they assassinated the Draoife when they refused to elect a new Druid King, and made moves to obtain the other circle's seeds. The leader of the Firesong was disposed of, but the puppeteer and leader of the Ascendant Order, Lord Bacaro Volorus, took matters to his own hands and forcebly ascended as the new Druid King. Bacaro's reign was swiftly put to an end by the [Vestige](Lore_Vestige.md), and thus the Green Scourge was prevented.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

"By the Arch Druid" is a common exclamation by modern-day Bretons.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARExclamations-3)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DFExclamations-21)</sup>

### Alternative Fate
The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) had the means to scry secrets from fate lines beyond the bounds of Tamriel. Among these secrets were tomes that spoke of [alternative timelines and realities](Lore_Many_Paths.md), where certain key events transpired differently.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONTTT-22)</sup> In one of these alternate realities, Bacaro defeated his other enemies and ruled over Tamriel from atop his Ivy Throne in Mount Firesong in a new [druidic](Lore_Druid.md) reign. With his victory, Tamriel was engulfed in firestorms targeting "false kings and queens", and those displaced by them were assisted by the Ascendant Order, Society of the Steadfast, and reformed Druids of Galen. Dark and cold days filled with turmoil were the norm during this transition, as volcanoes continued to "belch forth their retribution" covering the skies, but according to Bacaro's subordinates, the hardships would pass and the Ascendant Lord would uplift Tamriel's citizens.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-CATT-23)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Druid King's Regalia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/ON-misc-Druid_King_Vestments.png/150px-ON-misc-Druid_King_Vestments.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Druid_King_Vestments.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Druid_King_Vestments.png) Druid King's Regalia The [Druid King's Regalia](Lore_Druid_King%27s_Regalia.md) consists of a crown, a gauntlet, and a staff which were created by the god Y'ffre. These gifts were worn with wisdom and mercy by the line of Druid Kings,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONEV-24)</sup> and serve to legitimize one's ascension to the Ivy Throne.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOOrlaith-25)</sup> After the death of Kasorayn, the last Druid King, they remained dormant within sacred seeds entrusted to the three main Druidic Circles.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESODruid King-17)</sup> The regalia supposedly has no innate power of their own,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESODR-13)</sup> but after a [pretender](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md) stole the Druid King's Regalia, they almost brought upon the Green Scourge which would have called forth the wrath of nature upon Tamriel but they were slain before being able to do so.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODL-7)</sup>

The Regalia serves to crown one as Druid King, said to be the only one who can safely invoke and command even the greater spirits of nature.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PIT-26)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODL-7)</sup>. A wielder of the Regalia can also command a nature spirit to merge with them in order to gain its power. <sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TIThrone-27)</sup> The Regalia can also be used to allow a nature spirit to bind itself to an area and take on a new role, replacing that region's prior spirit, such an event occurred when the spirit Frii became the new spirit of [Mount Firesong](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md) in order to prevent the volcano from erupting and destroying the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-28)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODL-7)</sup>
