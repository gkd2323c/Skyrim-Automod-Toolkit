[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-load-Gorne.png/200px-ON-load-Gorne.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Gorne.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Gorne.png) Gorne during the Second Era [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/ON-crown_store-Gorne_Striped_Wolf.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Gorne_Striped_Wolf.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Gorne_Striped_Wolf.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Gorne_Striped_Wolf.jpg) Gorne Striped Wolf Gorne is a forested [Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) -ruled island<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PSI-1)</sup> off the eastern coast of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It was first charted by [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOTN-2)</sup> Gorne lies directly to the north of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md). The island is home to a giant monstrous skull formation,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-3)</sup> which aligns with the anatomy of the supposed rib-like protrusions of the creature [Gulga Mor Jil](Lore_Gulga_Mor_Jil.md) that makes up [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md)'s foundations,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ESO2023Global Reveal-4)</sup> and the Great Arm formation to the city's west.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Many strange creatures found nowhere else in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) inhabit Gorne. Among them is the [Gorne Striped Wolf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorne_Striped_Wolf), a breed of a giant [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) that terrorized the island's settlers until they were suppressed in an event called the Troubled House Hunt. These wolves are used as mounts due to their large size.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Another species said to have inhabited the isle are giant creatures known as [bat lizards](Lore_Bat_Lizard.md), a supposed ancestor to [cliff racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md) that could grow to the size of ships.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOTN-2)</sup> Although [nymphs](Lore_Nymph.md) are not unique to Gorne, they can also be found there.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PSI-1)</sup>

Gorne is one of the main settings for a series of fiction books called *[Poison Song](Lore_Poison_Song.md)*, written by Bristin Xel. The book features a small village also called Gorne, ruled by the Indoril nobles of Sandil House. It is described with wide cupolas, twisted cobbled streets, and thatched roofs.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PSI-1)</sup> Two of the books' main characters grew up on the island. Tay, an adopted member of House Indoril who discovers his heritage as the heir of [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md), and his cousin, Baynarah, the niece of the famous general, Indoril-Triffith, a veteran of the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md). After Tay returns to Gorne and learns about past, his sister, Dagoth-Acra, massacres the servants in Sandil House and escapes with him to [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PSV-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PSVI-8)</sup>

By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Gorne was considered to be part of the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-9)</sup> but remained under Indoril stewardship as an asylum for mentally unstable and magically insane [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) mages. It suffered a Daedric incursion from the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) that year.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Givyn-10)</sup> One of the earliest battles in the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) was a coordinated attack on several Imperial garrisons, located on islands off Morrowind's eastern mainland coast in [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-11)</sup> Whether Gorne was caught in the conflict is unknown.

## Notes
- The *[Poison Song](Lore_Poison_Song.md)* series was the only reference to Gorne before *[Father of the Niben](Lore_Father_Of_The_Niben.md)* and the island's appearance in [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online). *Poison Song* places Gorne in the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), roughly north of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PSIV-12)</sup> Future references to Gorne have instead put it east of mainland Morrowind, in the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md) near Necrom.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOTN-2)</sup>
- The [Diodata](Lore_Diodata.md) vampire clan, who have the power to see through the eyes of others, inhabit the island to evade the [Korgari](Lore_Korgari.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-13)</sup>
