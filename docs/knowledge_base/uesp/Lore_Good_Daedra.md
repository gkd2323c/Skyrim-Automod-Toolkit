# Good Daedra

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/ON-furnishing-Tri-Angled_Truth_Altar.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Tri-Angled_Truth_Altar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tri-Angled_Truth_Altar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Tri-Angled_Truth_Altar.jpg) Chimeri Tri-Angled Truth Altar to the Good Daedra The Three Good Daedra is the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) (and before that, [Velothi](Lore_Velothi.md)) term for three of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md): [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). These Princes are worshipped by the Dunmer as their ancestors. They are contrasted with the [Bad Daedra](Lore_Bad_Daedra.md), who are believed to test the Dunmer people's resolve and faith.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> To their worshippers, they were known as the **Three Sisters** and the **Triangular Gate**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ADAW-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FEE-4)</sup>

Mephala gave the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) the [Codes of Mephala](Lore_Codes_of_Mephala.md), taught them the ways of evasion and assassination, also founded the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md). Boethiah and Mephala together organized the structure that would end up as the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of the Dunmer. Boethiah taught the Dunmer philosophy, magic, and ["Responsible" Architecture](Lore_Responsible_Architecture.md) of their daedric shrines.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VF-5)</sup> According to one myth, Boethiah asked the Chimer to be the color of bruise, turning them into Dunmer,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LV32-6)</sup> however Boethiah sometimes gets attributed with the more conventional teachings of Azura,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VF-5)</sup> and in traditional retellings it is Azura who curses the Chimer with ashen skin.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-7)</sup> Azura is credited with teaching the Chimer the mysteries needed to be different than the Altmer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VF-5)</sup>

The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) replaced worship of the Good Daedra among the settled Dunmer following their apotheosis at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), and the three Good Daedra were thereafter known to the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) as the **Anticipations** of the three Tribunes.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) tribes refused to worship the Tribunal, rejecting them as 'false gods', remaining true to their worship of the Good Daedra. [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) still honored the Good Daedra.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-UHI-9)</sup> The [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) however despised them for their fear of wisdom and order, calling them the **False Princes** and "the Anti-Gears that turn counter to the Nameless Will".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TIS-10)</sup>

Following the deaths and disappearance of the Tribunes and the rise of the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md), worship of the Good Daedra was restored and they became known as the **Reclamations**.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> They are now also called the **True Tribunal** or **House of Reclamations**, having reclaimed their place in the hearts of the Dunmer people from the "false Tribunal" that used their status to shield the True Tribunal from the public's minds. The name "Anticipations" is no longer used for them, as they have reclaimed their identity and no longer represent the patronage of the false Tribunal.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DBElder Othreloth-12)</sup> Not all settled Dunmer turned away from Tribunal worship, although those few hidden sects who continued to worship the Tribunes as gods were persecuted by the New Temple for their heretical beliefs.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

The Halls of Veneration is a subterranean temple under Eidolon's Hollow and is considered one of the holiest places of worship for the Good Daedra. It was abandoned when the Tribunal replaced the Good Daedra as the prime Dunmer faith.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONLoad Eidolon-14)</sup>
