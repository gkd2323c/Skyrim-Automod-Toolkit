# Great Hunt


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/ON-wallpaper-Wolfhunter_02-2880x1620.jpg/200px-ON-wallpaper-Wolfhunter_02-2880x1620.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Wolfhunter_02-2880x1620.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Wolfhunter_02-2880x1620.jpg) Hircine overseeing a Great Hunt within the March of Sacrifices The Great Hunt, also known as the **Wild Hunt**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMKR-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_A)</sup> the **Sacred Hunt**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSFD-2)</sup> the **Ritual of the Innocent Quarry**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TPOTH-3)</sup> and the **Hunter's Game**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KWEBM-4)</sup> is the name given to the spectacular hunts usually conducted by the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), who holds the title of Master of the Great Hunt.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHGBT-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-THJ-6)</sup> Due to his heavy involvement, it is also known as **Hircine's Hunt**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BMHH-7)</sup>

Within Hircine's realm of the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md), Great Hunts are a constant occurrence.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOHDW-8)</sup> Outside of his realm, Great Hunts are rare and are rumored to happen once per era.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KWEBM-4)</sup> Great Hunts are called upon in Nirn for Hircine's own amusement,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BMHunt-9)</sup> or to punish those that defy him.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SRLS-10)</sup> Typically, the hunted party, or "Hare" is chosen by Hircine himself, while the hunters compete to be granted a boon by the Daedric Prince. These events can be anywhere from apocalyptic in nature to a gathering within his [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md). Though it can take place anywhere the Prince of the Hunt chooses, one can determine the next location by dissecting the stomach of a fresh wolf corpse.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCH-11)</sup>

When the Great Hunt starts, [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md) turns red, becoming the [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodmoon), and the hunt is believed to end when the Bloodmoon goes away. The Bloodmoon is said to symbolize the blood of the Hunter's Prey, and those wishing to join the Great Hunt craft rounded bone pendants that are stained with blood to symbolize it. <sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KWEBM-4)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FPI-12)</sup>

The Great Hunt is also another name for Hircine's Hunting Grounds.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOT-13)</sup>

## History
*The Bloodmoon will rise again, and my hounds will walk the lands. Let this world enjoy its brief respite, for Hircine will hunt again!*
— [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)
### Second Era

#### March of Sacrifices
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/ON-quest-The_Great_Hunt_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Great_Hunt_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Great_Hunt_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Great_Hunt_02.jpg) Hircine appearing before participants of the Great Hunt in 2E 582 Mortal inhabitants of Nirn were allowed to participate in a Great Hunt that was to be held within the March of Sacrifices region of the Hunting Grounds. During this time, the werewolf lord [Vykosa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vykosa) was amassing the largest werewolf pack that [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) has ever seen.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RJ-14)</sup> She plotted to use her [Moon Hunter Pack](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Hunter_Pack) to take over [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), exact revenge on [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) who had cursed her, and take down anyone that stood in her way.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-THICC-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lt M-16)</sup> Vykosa sent her lieutenant Balorgh along with a portion of her pack to infiltrate the hunting grounds, and to report back to her once they have found a way inside.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LTB-17)</sup> With the Great Hunt looming in, a portal manifested on Nirn within the Greenshade region of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), serving as a invitation to the hunt. This was of a great convenience to the Moon Hunter Pack, who opted to participate to earn Hircine's boon for the pack's benefit.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-BPESO-18)</sup>

Various hunters had come to compete to earn Hircine's boon, aside from Vykosa's pack, a rival Werebear group, sisters of the [Glenmoril Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd), and a tribe of Valenwood [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) attended. The rules were simple, hunters were to compete in retrieving the heart of a Great [Indrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indrik) known as Tarcyr, a creature that has been imbued with special powers from Hircine himself. As this hunt took place in the Hunting Grounds, those who died during this hunt would be damned to remain within it.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOHDW-8)</sup>

A group of [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) assisted Hanu, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of the [Zainab](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zainab) tribe of [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders), in completing the hunt in hopes of gaining Hircine's boon, so that she could free the soul of her father, who was infected with lycanthropy shortly before his death. The Undaunted competed with other hunting parties, but were successful in completing Hircine's task of retrieving Tarcyr's heart. To steal the heart, the rival Moon Hunter Pack then swooped in and dispatched Hanu, thus forcing Hanu's spirit to remain within the realm. Hircine declined to grant his boon to the Pack as they did not slay the hare, but in the spirit of the hunt, Hircine offered them a chance of redemption if they were to instead bring the hearts of the Undaunted. The two groups clashed, and Balorgh revealed his Werewolf Behemoth form—an experimental form of lycanthropy that towers over regular werewolves.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Despite this, Balorgh was defeated, and the Undaunted earned Hircine's boon. The departed Hanu was given the choice of freeing her father from the Hunting Grounds, or herself at the request of her father. It is unknown which of the two escaped.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOHDW-8)</sup>

### Third Era

#### The Ritual of the Innocent Quarry
The [Chimera of Desolation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chimera_of_Desolation) is the setting for the Ritual of the Innocent Quarry, a site where Great Hunts were held. It was an ancient Daedric rite that drew energy from the powerful magicka stream engulfing the plane. The creators and times of the rituals are lost to history, but when followed properly the rite brought great power and prestige to an individual dubbed the "Huntsman". The ritual pit this all-powerful Huntsman and their Greater and Lesser Dogs (all [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md)) against a mortal known as the Innocent Quarry, or simply, the Hare. In short, the Huntsman's aim was to corner and kill the Hare, while philosophically reflecting on the nature of their places in the Hunt.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TPOTH-3)</sup> Many Daedra considered being the Huntsman a great honor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BSLTFD-20)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/SR-item-Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.jpg/200px-SR-item-Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.jpg) Spear of Bitter Mercy (Skyrim) The Ritual begins at the Chapel of the Innocent Quarry, a large green crystal edifice, and the only structure erected by the Daedra on the island.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMKR-1)</sup> The Lesser Dogs assemble outside, while inside the Huntsmen, the Greater Dogs, and the Master of the Hunt perform rites to initiate and sanctify the Huntsmen, the Hunt itself, and the Innocent Quarry. Afterward, the Select Huntsman emerges, displays the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy), and recites the Offices of the Hunt. The Offices explains the four stages of the Hunt: the Drag, the Chase, the Call, and the View to the Kill. In the Drag, the Lesser Dogs flushed the Hare out into the open. The Chase is conducted by the Greater Hounds, who pursued the Hare. They then corner the Hare during the Call, and summon the Huntsman for the kill. Finally in the View, the Select Huntsman kills the Hare with the ritual Spear of Bitter Mercy, then calls upon the Master of the Hunt to view the kill by ringing the town bell of Trybador. After the Hunt is concluded, the Master of the Hunt then bestows the Bounty upon the Select Huntman, awarding them the title of Huntsman Bold. The Huntsman Bold is then allowed to name the next Hare for the next Hunt, though is forbidden from participation in that Hunt themselves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TPOTH-3)</sup>

The Huntsmen, Master, and Hounds are solemnly sworn to honor the Offices, or Law, of the Hunt. The Law strictly defined all details of the Hunt, such as how many Hounds of each sort could participate, how the Spear of Bitter Mercy could be wielded, and so on. Of particular importance, the Law stated that the Hare must have a genuine chance to escape, no matter how slim. This generally took the form of six keys, which if collected would allow the Hare to open a portal in the Temple of Daedric Rites and escape the Chimera of Desolation. The Daedra considered escape to be effectively impossible, but considered failure to provide the opportunity to be a "shameful and unforgivable betrayal of the Law of the Hunt".

Additionally, the Ritual of the Hunt granted the Huntsmen protection from all forms of attack, including virtually all weapons and magical effects. The only exception was a Huntsman's own Spear; a single touch of the Spear of Bitter Mercy meant instant and certain death, whether the victim was Hare or Huntsman. It was believed that only the most powerful Daedra were immune to the deadly effects of the Spear.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TPOTH-3)</sup>

The details this hunt are noted in the book *[The Posting of the Hunt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Posting_of_the_Hunt)*, and its edicts are also followed by worshipers of Hircine.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

Hircine is believed to have hosted one of these ritual hunts<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Noonan)</sup> in [3E 398](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_398), when [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s forces attacked and seized control of the Battlespire with the blessing of Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), who was [impersonating](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BSPaxti-22)</sup> With the portal back to Tamriel blocked, an [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md#The_Apprentice) trapped within the Battlespire made it to the Chimera and became the Hare in a Great Hunt instigated by Dagon's [Herne](Lore_Herne_(creature).md) minions. However, the apprentice was then aided by [Chimere Graegyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chimere_Graegyn), an inhabitant of the Chimera of Desolation and someone who had previously defied Dagon.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Battlespire-23)</sup>

In this Great Hunt, [Frost Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Daedra) served as the Lesser Dogs, [Fire Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fire_Daedra) as Greater Hounds, Hernes as the Huntsmen, and the [Dark Seducer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Seducer) Si'ess as the Master of the Hunt.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSFD-2)</sup> One of the Huntsmen was a Herne named Egahirn; it was his first Hunt, and he was given the honor of making the Kill, being given the title of Select Huntsman.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-24)</sup> However, in order to assure his victory he kept one of the six gatekeys needed for the Hare to escape on his person. This was a direct violation of the Law of the Hunt, and it did not sit well with many of the other participants (as Egahirn was protected against all weapons and magics except the Spear of Bitter Mercy).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BSLFB-25)</sup> It also presented an opportunity; when he tricked Mehrunes Dagon, Chimere wore the Armor of the [Saviour's Hide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saviour%27s_Hide), which protected the wearer from attacks by oathbreakers—which Egahirn had became by his actions.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-BSChimere-26)</sup> The Apprentice retrieved the Spear of Bitter Mercy, wore the Saviour's Hide, and was able to slay Egahirn and escape the realm.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Battlespire-23)</sup>

#### The Hunter's Game
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt.jpg/200px-BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt.jpg) The Bloodmoon, a sign of the Great Hunt The Great Hunt of the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) is referred to as the Hunter's Game. According to the Skaal, once per era Hircine brings his hunt to Nirn itself, and walks the land with his Hounds in search of prey.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KWEBM-4)</sup> Fortunately, the arrival of Hircine on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) can be reliably predicted by their own Bloodmoon Prophecy. When it occurs on Solstheim, the Hunt is preceded by four signs. The first is the coming of the "Hounds" when [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) appear on the island. The second is the "Fire from the Eye of Glass", a pillar of fire that appears on the surface of the frozen [Lake Fjalding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Fjalding). The third is the "Tide of Woe", a [horker](Lore_Horker.md) massacre, the bodies of which then wash up all along the island's northern coast. The final sign is the namesake of the prophecy: the "Bloodmoon", when [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md) turns crimson from the blood of the Hunter's Prey. After the four signs begins the Hunter's Game. After the hunt, Hircine returns to his realm for another era.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KWEBM-4)</sup>

One such Bloodmoon came to fruition in the year [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). Hircine summoned the island's Four champions to take part in the hunt as Hares: [Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Karstaag) the [Frost Giant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Giant); Captain [Falx Carius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Falx_Carius) of [Fort Frostmoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Frostmoth); and Tharsten Heart-Fang, leader of the Skaal and wielder of [Hircine's Ring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hircine%27s_Ring). It is unknown if the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) took part in the Hunt as a Hound or as a simple mortal. The interior of [Mortrag Glacier](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mortrag_Glacier) was used as a hunting ground, and within its interior lay a maze filled with Hircine's hounds, which had to be navigated to reach the Hunter. The Nerevarine made it through, but in order to escape the Hunt, they were required to fight an aspect of Hircine (Strength, Speed, or Guile). The Nerevarine succeeded in defeating the Hunter, causing the glacier to collapse and ending the Hunt of Hircine.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BMHunt-9)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Skaal's Hunter's Game was seemingly a thing of the past. The werewolves of Solstheim had been reduced to only a few members of the Frostmoon Pack. Their alpha, Majni, still held the remaining blessed rings from their past hunts.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MDB-27)</sup> The island also saw a rise in the number of [werebears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Werebear), who were laying claim to werewolf territories.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-HDB-28)</sup>

### Fourth Era

#### Spirit of the Hunt
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/BL-event-Spirit_of_the_Hunt.png/200px-BL-event-Spirit_of_the_Hunt.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-event-Spirit_of_the_Hunt.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-event-Spirit_of_the_Hunt.png) The Combantants of the Great Hunt of 4E 180 Sometime in [Rain's Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rain%27s_Hand) of [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md#The_Warrior) heeded Hircine's call and went through a cursed portal that led to a strange land cloaked in green mists, with an obscured red sky overhead. There, they defeated the [wispmother](Lore_Wispmother.md) known as the Wintry Widow, the [spriggan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan) Aenoxia Venomblood, a [bear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear) known as the Beast, and the humanoid Night Ranger. The Great Hunt came to a close when Hircine's Apostle was tracked down and slain.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Blades-29)</sup>

#### Bloated Man's Grotto
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-quest-Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.jpg) The Bloodmoon dominating the skyline of Bloated Man's Grotto 4E 201 A murder occurred in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the victim being a young girl from [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md). The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) visited the imprisoned culprit, Sinding, who revealed he was a werewolf. Sinding had taken the [Ring of Hircine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_Hircine) to help control his condition—this evidently upset the Prince, who cursed the ring to cause the wearer to transform at random. The girl was merely an innocent bystander.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SRMoonlight-30)</sup>

Sinding had come to Falkreath to hunt the majestic White Stag, as Hircine would commune with anyone who slayed it, and wished to beg the Prince's forgiveness. The Dragonborn offered to take the ring to Hircine in Sinding's place, and the werewolf gratefully accepted before escaping his cell. The Dragonborn tracked down and slew the White Stag, and an Aspect of Hircine appeared. The Huntsman agreed to "consider" lifting the ring's curse, but initiated a Great Hunt within Skyrim<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-SRIMBMR-31)</sup> as he demanded an offering—Sinding's hide. The werewolf was holed up within Bloated Man's Grotto where the Bloodmoon had appeared, and by the time the Dragonborn arrived, one group of hunters had already been slain. Sinding made one last plea, asking the Dragonborn to defy Hircine and fight alongside him. It is unknown if the Dragonborn joined the Hunt and lost the ring, saved Sinding from the hunters and had Hircine remove the curse, or betrayed Sinding by eliminating the hunters before turning on him and claiming both artifacts.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SRMoonlight-30)</sup>
