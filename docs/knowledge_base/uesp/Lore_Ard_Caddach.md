*An ard offers food, freedom, and protection with his right hand. With his left, he promises cruelty and punishment. Those that covet the right get the left. Simple as that.*
—Caddach<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup> Caddach of the [Blackdrake Clan](Lore_Blackdrake_Clan.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup> (also known as the **Despot of Markarth**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> was a [Reachman](Lore_Reachfolk.md) who was the Imperial Governor of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and later the Ard of Markarth during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During the reigns of his kinsmen, Emperors [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md) and [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), Caddach served as the Imperial Governor of Markarth.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> After Leovic was overthrown in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Caddach set aside his imperial title and took instead the old [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) title of ard, thereafter ruling Markarth as an independent city-state.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> As ard, Caddach consolidated power and worked to unite the [Reachman clans](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) under his authority.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> In 2E 582, he played a role in fighting the reemerged [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) and contributed to its defeat.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Reach-4)</sup>

## History

### Governor of Markarth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-npc-Lady_Belain.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Lady_Belain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lady_Belain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lady_Belain.jpg) Lady Belain of the Nighthollow Clan *The Despot's true genius, however, lies in his political calculation. To the Reachmen, he speaks of a free and independent Reach, holding true to Reach tradition and scorning the weak ways of outlanders. (His words, not mine.) But underneath that Reach-for-the-Reachfolk bluster, Caddach uses the trappings and systems of Imperial authority to transform Markarth into a functional state for the first time in the Reach's long and bloody history.*
—Nilene Devierin<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> Caddach was a member of the [Blackdrake Clan](Lore_Blackdrake_Clan.md) and a cousin of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup> When he was a child, Caddach's grandmother shared stories of the [Vateshran](Lore_Vateshrans.md) with him, including tales of the Night Lords.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup> As a young man, he journeyed south to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to serve in the Reach guard of his kinsman Emperor [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md) and proved himself a warrior wise beyond his years. During his time in Cyrodiil, Caddach received an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) education and military training.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> He married at least three times.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup>

In [2E 559](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_559), Moricar saw the need to establish some degree of Imperial authority over the increasingly restless clans of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), so he chose to appoint a trusted kinsman to the task.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> He selected Caddach and sent him to the City of Stone to serve as the Imperial Governor of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> To reassure the clan chiefs that he did not intend to impose Imperial law in its entirety on the free [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), Moricar limited Caddach's writ to Markarth and the lands immediately surrounding the city.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> Caddach's role was to both rule the city and maintain peace between the [Reachfolk clans](Lore_Reachfolk_Clans.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> Therefore, the clan chiefs were satisfied that Caddach's role was limited to maintaining order in Markarth while leaving the clans to govern themselves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup>

Caddach administered Markarth effectively for the rest of Moricar's reign and retained his position through the troubled years of Moricar's son, Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md). During this period, Caddach ruled in the Reach manner. His judgments were swift and brutal, earning him the epithet the "Despot of Markarth".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup>

At some point during his governorship, Caddach took Lady Belain, who was secretly the matriarch of the [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) [Nighthollow Clan](Lore_Nighthollow_Clan.md), as his chief advisor. Belain advised Caddach on both arcane and diplomatic matters, and she gained a reputation for her wily political instincts, using soft words and subtlety to temper his sternness.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CBLB-5)</sup> Circa [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568), Leovic sent Consul [Cardea](Lore_Cardea.md) from her post in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to aid Caddach as an administrator and to provide a conduit between Cyrodiil and the Reach.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Pot R-6)</sup> Caddach came to appreciate Cardea's administrative skills.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cardea-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AIi M-8)</sup>

At some point prior to [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), the group known as the [Stonehands](Lore_Stonehands.md) pledged their loyalty to Caddach.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gwenyfe-9)</sup> The Stonehands mainly served as his personal guard and occasionally as his spies and counselors.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Gwenyfe-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Hroltar-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTC-11)</sup>

### Ard of Markarth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/ON-npc-Consul_Cardea.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Consul_Cardea.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Consul_Cardea.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Consul_Cardea.jpg) Consul Cardea [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/ON-npc-Ambassador_Goronwy.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ambassador_Goronwy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ambassador_Goronwy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ambassador_Goronwy.jpg) Ambassador Goronwy *Markarth is more than Dwarven stones and high walls. It's strength.*
—Caddach<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup> In 2E 577, Leovic was overthrown by [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) in the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SHot LE-12)</sup> When he learned of Leovic's overthrow, Caddach set aside his Imperial title and took instead the old Reach title of ard (meaning king of the fort).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> After Leovic's overthrow, the remaining Reachmen retreated from the [Imperial Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SHot LE-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Mt CHKS-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Arvnir-14)</sup> The members of the Blackdrake Clan soon began squabbling among themselves, and they eventually attempted to take Markarth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup> In response, Caddach had the surviving Blackdrakes captured and then bound by their ankles and thrown headfirst from the top of Markarth's walls onto the rocks below.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTC-11)</sup> Any Blackdrakes whom Caddach did not execute were exiled.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> By purging his clan, Caddach secured his hold on the city against his rivals.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup>

Caddach's eighteen-year governorship allowed him to build the undisciplined warriors under his command into a loyal army that was ready to support him as ard. The Reachman clans, in some cases grudgingly, accepted his control over Markarth—no single clan or feasible alliance of clans was powerful enough to oppose Caddach's army. As long as the clans accepted no other king and answered Caddach's requests for warriors when necessary, he did not attempt to govern them or interfere in their internal affairs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> To the various clan chiefs, Caddach was simply one more chief, deserving no more deference than any of their peers.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Pot R-6)</sup> He was careful not to force a clan to act against its will unless he was certain of his strength and advantage. He did not hesitate to use all available resources to subdue an unruly clan if he was certain of his strength and advantage.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup>

During the five years after he claimed the Mournful Throne, Caddach slowly consolidated his power in Markarth, transforming the fortress into a city-state and establishing it as the capital of a free and independent Reach. To the Reachmen, Caddach spoke of a free and independent Reach, honoring tradition and scorning the ways of outlanders, but beneath the rhetoric, he used the trappings and systems of imperial authority to transform Markarth into a functional state for the first time in the Reach's history. He imposed laws (of a sort) in Markarth and ordered the keeping of written records in [Understone Keep](Lore_Understone_Keep.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup> His laws made trade in the City of Stone easier and more efficient,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HS-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Nolovan-16)</sup> and he was firmly opposed to the skooma trade.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-J.27ghamar-17)</sup> After Leovic's overthrow, the Ard requested that Cardea remain in Markarth as an administrator, a request that she accepted.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cardea-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AIi M-8)</sup>

Prior to the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), Caddach permitted [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) and his [Worm Cult](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) to take shelter in the ruins of [Nchuand-Zel](Lore_Nchuand-Zel.md), located in Markarth, in return for Mannimarco facilitating Varen Aquilarios's downfall.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lyris-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGTB-19)</sup> Caddach eventually agreed to provide Mannimarco with a portion of land in the hills near Markarth for experimentation in merging it with [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). Mannimarco's experiments to merge the provided land with Coldharbour failed, and it was sundered from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Mannimarco then departed Markarth, and Caddach never saw him again. Following Mannimarco's departure, Caddach sealed off Nchuand-Zel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup>

Months after the disappearance of Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), Councilor [Itinia](Lore_Itinia.md) sent Helvar Tarperian to Caddach with a proposal to enthrone him as emperor because he was the closest living relative of the Longhouse Emperors. Caddach rejected Itinia's proposal, as he did not want to be the Cyrodilic nobility's puppet, and he told Tarperian that if Caddach ever returned to Cyrodiil, it would be at the head of another Reachman horde.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTC-11)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) dispatched Lady [Nilene Devierin](Lore_Nilene_Devierin.md) as an envoy to the Reach to attempt to persuade Caddach to rein in the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) and Dark Witness Clan, who at the time were attacking [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Although Caddach met with Devieren, he dismissed her concerns because he had no interest in provoking the Reachfolk clans to defy his power for the sake of peace with High Rock, and he wished to avoid appearing subservient to Emeric.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CBLB-5)</sup> In 2E 582, the Ard sent one of his diplomats named [Goronwy](Lore_Goronwy.md) to Cyrodiil to serve as the ambassador to the newly founded [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) settlement of Vashabar.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AG-20)</sup> He also authorized a diplomatic mission to [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md), successfully persuading Count [Calantius](Lore_Calantius_of_Skingrad.md) to oversee the return of Reachfolk cultural artifacts from Varen's Watch.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-CS-21)</sup>

Caddach granted High Shaman [Glynroch](Lore_Glynroch.md) the use of a building atop Markarth's central crag for the purpose of erecting a new shrine to [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), which became the [Shrine of the Hunt-Father](Lore_Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Glynroch-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Lt AG-23)</sup> At one point, Caddach's protégé attempted to usurp his position, resorting to whispers, lies, and manipulating people against one another. Caddach uncovered the scheme and dealt harshly with the would-be usurper.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-VP-24)</sup>

### The Gray Host
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/ON-npc-Arana.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Arana.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arana.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arana.jpg) Arana of the Wayward Guardians [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/ON-interior-Understone_Keep_10.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Understone_Keep_10.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Understone_Keep_10.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Understone_Keep_10.jpg) The moot during which the peace between Reachfolk and Nords was signed *I cannot think of a time or a tale where a Reachfolk and Nord extended a hand to one another unless each was holding a dagger. This may be the first time such a thing's occurred.
May it not be the last.*
—Caddach<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup> In 2E 582, the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) invaded the Reach to reawaken the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md), which was located in the section of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md) beneath the region.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Verandis-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Rada-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Lt PD-27)</sup> Caddach and the Gray Host soon agreed to a peace deal that was brokered by Belain, with the terms being that the Gray Host would spare Markarth's inhabitants, and in return the Reachmen would not obstruct their search for a Dwarven relic.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Belain-28)</sup> The peace deal was, in actuality, a ploy by Belain and the Gray Host to gather mortals in Markarth to sacrifice them to fuel the reawakening of the Dark Heart.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Verandis-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Rada-26)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Belain-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-GHID-29)</sup> After agreeing to the deal, Caddach summoned the Reachman clans to Markarth, although some clans refused to abandon their territories.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Lt AG-23)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Lt KE-30)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Belain-28)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Fynvagh-31)</sup> [Arana](Lore_Arana.md) of the [Ghostsong Clan](Lore_Ghostsong_Clan.md) opposed Caddach's peace with the Gray Host, so she formed a faction called the [witch-rebels](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md) to fight him and the Gray Host.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Belain-28)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Arana-32)</sup>

After Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md) and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) arrived in the Reach, Caddach met with the latter and instructed them to track down Arana. After meeting the rebels and investigating some Gray Host camps, the Vestige returned to Caddach and informed him that they had learned that Belain was allied with the Gray Host.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TDo M-33)</sup> However, instead of taking the fight to the Gray Host, Caddach believed that the best course of action was to wait and trust in the strength of Markarth's defenses, despite recognizing that the peace deal was suspect.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup>

Later, Arana and the witch-rebels arrived to help defend Markarth, and soon afterward, the Gray Host attacked the city.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Bradan-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Gwendis-35)</sup> During the attack, Belain tried to kill Caddach, but Arana saved him and drove her off into Nchuand-Zel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup> Following that, Caddach and the other Reachmen defended Markarth against the attack until the Vestige killed Belain, which led the Gray Host to call off their assault on the city and retreat.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Bradan-34)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AFo S-36)</sup> Arana died during the fighting, but before her death she had made peace with Caddach, and the witch-rebels, now known as the Wayward Guardians, agreed to honor it.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Bradan-34)</sup>

As part of the efforts to defeat the Gray Host and its leader [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md), Caddach granted the Vestige and [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md) access to Nchuand-Zel to investigate the land that Mannimarco had sundered from Tamriel.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TEo E-37)</sup> When the Vestige and Verandis Ravenwatch killed Rada al-Saran, the count was able to resurrect all the souls who had been used to reawaken the Dark Heart, including Arana, an event known as the Miracle at Markarth.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Fynvagh-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Arana-32)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Kynaer-38)</sup> After Arana was resurrected, Caddach requested that she become his new counselor, and she accepted the position.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Arana-32)</sup>

Following the defeat of the Gray Host, High King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) of [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) and Jarl [Svana](Lore_Svana.md) of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) traveled to the Reach.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SC-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Jorunn-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Svana-42)</sup> Caddach then hosted a meeting in Understone Keep in which he, Jorunn, and Svana reached a peace agreement between Eastern Skyrim, Western Skyrim, and the Reach.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Jorunn-40)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Svana-42)</sup> He intended to expand Markarth, regarding it as a worthy holdfast but not yet a true grand city. His plans focused on putting the surrounding land to work, repairing its stone foundations, and expanding trade, hoping to harness the skills of Nords who would offer their craft and labor for the right price.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Caddach-1)</sup>
