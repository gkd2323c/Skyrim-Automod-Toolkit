## Special Glyphs
In addition to the small glyphs that make up the background text on the scroll, there are 26 larger glyphs that appear on top of everything. Each of these is unique and most do not appear to match any of the smaller glyphs. 17 of them are circled, while the remaining 9 are not. They are listed in top-to-bottom/left-to-right order below.

## Notes
- The writing is present on the robes of [Moth Priests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moth_Priests).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Prophets Robes-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>
- The [Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md) artifact features the script throughout its body<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SREye-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Elder Scroll-1)</sup> (and is also said to have a similar origin to the Elder Scrolls themselves).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFCN-5)</sup><sup>:160</sup>
- It is difficult to even transcribe the characters or to attempt translation based on the sources given. The fact that there are more characters than in the Latin alphabet means that a simple cipher is unlikely to be of much use.
- Glyphs from this alphabet were also featured on the [Runes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Runes) and [Playmats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Playmats) in *[The Elder Scrolls: Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)*. This was due to the game's original UI design being Elder Scrolls-themed, with the play mat being the [surface of a Scroll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Miscellaneous_Art#Play_Mats) and the game's [narrator](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Kellen) being a moth priest.
