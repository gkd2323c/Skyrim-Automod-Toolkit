Modir the Far was an ancient [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [Tongue](Lore_Tongues.md),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> and according to some sources a [Greybeard](Lore_Greybeards.md),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-V2-1)</sup> who erected a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) at [Shearpoint](Lore_Shearpoint.md) in honor of his brother Oskar's memory. He belonged to a clan known for its powerful voice and strong affinity for the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), an aptitude his brother, dubbed the Fool, lacked.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-V1-2)</sup>

## History
*Modir the Far raised (this) stone
(in his) brother's memory, Oskar
the Fool, whose voice was weak and
not (the) mighty Shout of his clan.*
—Shearpoint Word Wall Inscription<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-V1-2)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/SR-place-Shearpoint.jpg/200px-SR-place-Shearpoint.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Shearpoint.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Shearpoint.jpg) Shearpoint Word Wall erected by Modir the Far Little is known of Modir's past. He lived in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md),<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> and was a brother of Oskar the Fool, and a member of a clan known for its powerful voice.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-V1-2)</sup> He eventually became a Greybeard,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-V2-1)</sup> and as such resided in [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md) and followed the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE-3)</sup> His brother Oskar, known for his weak voice eventually died, and Modir erected a Word Wall in his memory's honor at Shearpoint.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-V1-2)</sup>

Little is known about Modir's later fate, but the site of his brother's memorial was also the burial ground of the [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), [Krosis](Lore_Krosis.md). It later became a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) lair.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SR-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Krosis-5)</sup> The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) visited the Word Wall built by Modir to further their mastery of the Voice.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SR-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWOP-6)</sup>
