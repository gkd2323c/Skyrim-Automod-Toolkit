Sabre Cats, also known as **Sabertooths**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFCN-1)</sup><sup>:120</sup> or **Rhojiit**, are large [cats](Lore_Cat.md) that primarily inhabit [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), but have also been found in parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOSC-2)</sup> They are powerful and fast predators that evolved two distinctive and dangerous sharp front teeth.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Co S-3)</sup> They are very dangerous and will attack wildlife and people without hesitation, often ignoring the former in favor of the latter.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRSC-4)</sup> They are known to carry the [disease](Lore_Diseases.md) [Witbane](Lore_Witbane.md).

According to a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) [story](Lore_The_Tale_of_Dro%27Zira.md) recorded by Sonia Vette, who overheard the story being told to a child, sabre cats were originally Khajiiti warriors in the Pride of Alkosh. They were called to Skyrim by [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md), but their devotion would earn them the ire of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhan.md), who would share a "roar" with [Ra'Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) to change the moon phases of [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md). Ra'Wulfharth used this ability to change the Khajiit warriors into the [Senche](Lore_Senche_(furstock).md) furstock, a very large quadruped form of Khajiit, and Lorkhaj "stripped from them all reason". Overtime these Rhojiit "grew small and lost their cunning altogether", resulting in the modern sabre cats that roam the land today.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-To D-5)</sup> Potentially contradicting this story however, are accounts of sabre cats existing during the lifetime of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Many variants of sabre cat share physical similarities with the known forms of [Senche-Cat](Lore_Senche-Cat.md), with the addition of the distinctive sabre teeth.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOSenche Cats-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOSabre Cats-8)</sup> Like Khajiit,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOCougar-9)</sup> they are considered cousins of Senche-Cats.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOSCM-10)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Sabre_Cat_Tooth.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Sabre_Cat_Tooth.png) Sabre Cat Tooth (Skyrim) [Teeth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sabre_Cat_Tooth) from sabre cats have been harvested for [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) purposes, as well as their [eyes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eye_of_Sabre_Cat).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SCTSR-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SCESR-12)</sup> Their pelts are used in various applications ranging from decoration to leatherworking.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SCPSR-13)</sup> However, the author Aldetuile discourages consumption of their meat, as it is "tasteless and not fit to eat".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Co S-3)</sup>

There are many recorded instances of successfully tamed sabre cats, often as mounts, although the vast majority of sabre cat are wild.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOSC-2)</sup> The cute appearance of their cubs have resulted in a few being taken in as pets, although they are noted to be dangerous even in their youth.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOSCC-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOSSCC-15)</sup> Sabre cats are also captured and left in their wild state, to act as a form of entertainment by pitting them against other animals or people in combat.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LKHSR-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TPSR-17)</sup>

Sabre cats have some association with the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), who is known as the "Mother of Men and beasts". Facing a [guardian spirit](Lore_Guardian_Spirit.md) in the form of a sabre cat is one part of the goddess' sacred trials.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-KST-18)</sup> Bards have claimed that the Vale Sabre Cat variant's glowing fur was a blessing granted to them by Kyne. However scholars in the [College of Sapiarchs](Lore_College_of_Sapiarchs.md) refute this.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOVSCG-19)</sup>

## Variants

### Death Mask Sabre Cat
Death Mask Sabre Cats are a breed of sabre cat bred to have a macabre appearance by [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESODMSC-20)</sup>

- **Found in:** [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Death_Mask_Sabre_Cat)

- [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/ON-crown_store-Death_Mask_Sabre_Cat.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Death_Mask_Sabre_Cat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Death_Mask_Sabre_Cat.jpg) Death Mask Sabre Cat (ESO)

### Sabertooth Tiger
Sabertooth tigers are a type of striped sabre cat commonly found around the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region. They attack with their very long incisors and front paws.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DFSaber-21)</sup> Nobility sometimes train them as house pets.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-DFRP-22)</sup>

- **Found in:** [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Sabertooth_Tiger)

- [![](https://images.uesp.net/9/90/DF-creature-Tiger.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-creature-Tiger.gif) Sabertooth Tiger (Daggerfall)

### Snowy Sabre Cat
The Snowy Sabre Cat primarily makes its home in the coldest stretches of Tamriel, with the adaption of a paler coat helping them blend in with the snow where they live.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Co S-3)</sup> No other feline creature in Tamriel is as adapted to the cold as they are.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOSSCM-23)</sup>

A breed spotted variety are known as Snow Leopard Sabre Cats, and were domesticated by the shamans of the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) tribe as mounts.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

- **Found in:** [Skyrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Snowy_Sabre_Cat), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Snowy_Sabre_Cat), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Snowy Sabre Cat), [Skyrim Very Special Edition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Enemies#Snowy_Sabre_Cat), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call_to_Arms:Models), [Skyrim - The Adventure Game](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Dungeons#Encounters)

### Vale Sabre Cat
The Vale Sabre Cat originates from the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md). Its most notable difference from other breeds is its glowing fur, a trait it shares with other animals of the Vale. The origin of this glowing fur is a matter of a scholarly debate.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOVSCG-19)</sup>

- **Found in:** [Skyrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vale_Sabre_Cat), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Vale_Sabre_Cat), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Vale_Sabre_Cat)
