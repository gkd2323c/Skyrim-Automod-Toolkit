# Dwarven Automatons

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/73/SR-creature-Dwarven_Automatons.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Automatons.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Automatons.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Automatons.jpg) A group of automatons advancing on their target **Dwarven Automatons** (also known as **Dwemer Constructs** and **[Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md)**) are various ancient, robot-like machines encountered in [Dwarven Ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md) across [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim_(place).md) and [Solstheim](Skyrim_Dragonborn_Places.md). Despite the disappearance of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) after the events of the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), their creations remain functional in long lost cities beneath the ground, performing whatever operation their masters tasked them with.

## Automaton Types
Dwarven automatons vary in shape and size, but they all function in a similar way. They are non-living constructs that cannot be detected with [Detect Life](Skyrim_Detect_Life_(effect).md) or [Detect Undead](Skyrim_Detect_Undead.md), but can be detected with the [Aura Whisper](Skyrim_Aura_Whisper.md) [shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) or the [Vampire Lord](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md)'s [Detect All Creatures](Skyrim_Detect_All_Creatures.md) ability. For purposes of powers like [Agent of Dibella](Skyrim_Agent_of_Dibella.md), all automatons are treated as male. Automatons are immune to [paralysis](Skyrim_Paralyze_(effect).md), cannot be ragdolled (except for Dwarven spiders), cannot be [reanimated](Skyrim_Reanimate.md), cannot be [soul trapped](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md), and have a permanent [Waterbreathing](Skyrim_Waterbreathing_(effect).md) ability. Note that while they cannot be soul trapped, they do recharge enchanted weapons when killed by an actor with the [Soul Siphon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Siphon) perk. Furthermore, contrary to what one might expect from reading *[Ruins of Kemel-Ze](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ruins_of_Kemel-Ze)*, Dwarven automatons are not immune to [shock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shock_Damage), which actually causes them normal damage. Instead, they are immune to [frost](Skyrim_Frost_Damage.md) and [poison](Skyrim_Poison.md) damage, and boast a 25% resistance to [magic](Skyrim_Magic_Resistance.md).

### Dwarven Spider
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/63/SR-creature-Dwarven_Spider.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Spider.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Spider.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Spider.jpg) A Dwarven spider Dwarven spiders are small, arachnid-like automatons, and are very common in [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md). They tend to be found mining or working on building or maintaining various objects that are found in their ruins. They can also be found hiding inside of pipe ports along the walls and ceilings of these ruins where they will lay in wait until they notice something walk by, after which they will leap out and attempt to engage whatever alerted them. They are very agile in combat and will attack with a variety of leg stabs, and can also perform a leap attack that will both deal greater damage and close the distance between them and their target. Variants of the dwarven spider higher than level 7 have access to short-range electric jolts that deal [shock damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shock_Damage), and will explode a moment after being killed to deal further shock damage. These variants are easily distinguished from workers both by name and by the electrical special effects that emanate from their core. Dwarven spiders can be looted for [Dwarven scraps](Skyrim_Ores_and_Ingots.md#Dwemer_Metal_.28Smeltable.29), a very small chance at precious [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md), [metal ore](Skyrim_Ores_and_Ingots.md), [Dwarven Oil](Skyrim_Dwarven_Oil.md), and [soul gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md) once killed. They are also the only kind of automaton that can be ragdolled with [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md).

There are two variants of Dwarven spiders both named **Broken Dwarven Spider** that can be randomly created by the [Aetherial Staff](Skyrim_Aetherial_Staff.md), but spawn dead. The first variant ([xx](Skyrim_Form_ID.md) 0142bc) simply dies and does nothing else, but the second variant ([xx](Skyrim_Form_ID.md) 0121a9) spawns a randomized number of Dwarven scraps at its position when it dies.

### Dwarven Sphere
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Sphere.jpg) A Dwarven sphere Dwarven spheres are constructs that, unlike Dwarven spiders, were intentionally designed for combat. These spheres patrol Dwarven ruins in a condensed spherical form until they encounter a threat, at which point they rise into their semi-humanoid form and attack. Like Dwarven spiders, they can hide in Dwarven pipes and exit through ports to attack unsuspecting foes that wander too close. Basic Dwarven spheres utilize only melee attacks, but higher level variants use their arm-mounted [crossbows](Skyrim_Crossbow.md) to shoot powerful darts at targets with whom they aren't engaged in melee combat. Because their mode of locomotion is the sphere, their attacks involve a number of very speedy lunges that can close the distance between them and a target rapidly and are very hard to sidestep. When you kill a Dwarven sphere, you will find a number of items on its remains including [Dwarven metal scraps](Skyrim_Ores_and_Ingots.md#Dwemer_Metal_.28Smeltable.29), a small chance at precious [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md), [Dwarven Oil](Skyrim_Dwarven_Oil.md), a number of [Dwarven bolts](Skyrim_Dwarven_Bolt.md) if *[Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)* is installed, and [soul gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md).

There is a variant of Dwarven sphere named **Broken Dwarven Sphere** ([xx](Skyrim_Form_ID.md) 0142bd) that can be randomly created by the [Aetherial Staff](Skyrim_Aetherial_Staff.md), but spawns dead. Unlike the similar Broken Dwarven Spider automatons, there is no variant of the Broken Dwarven Sphere that spawns items on death.

### Dwarven Centurion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Centurion.jpg) A Dwarven centurion Dwarven centurions are the largest and strongest creations the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) left behind after their disappearance. Designed in the form of a massively armored warrior with a hammer on one arm and a spring-loaded battleaxe on the other, they are formidable enemies that typically serve as the boss of their [dungeon](Skyrim_Dungeons.md). In addition to their incredibly strong melee attacks which can be on par with those of a [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md), they can unleash a powerful steam breath attack that functions like a [shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md). Combining these features with the set of resistances that nearly all Dwarven automatons have and their very high health stats makes them an adversary not to be trifled with.

Usually centurions are not found simply wandering around a Dwarven ruin, but instead are found immobile within their charging gantries until they are activated either by proximity, lever, or being attacked at range. This can oftentimes give ranged attackers a chance to get a few shots in with either their [bow](Skyrim_Bow.md) or a [spell](Skyrim_Spells.md) before the automaton can fully activate and engage in melee range. In some cases these great constructs are even used as traps to prevent any would-be treasure hunter from plundering the ruins they protect.

If you are a low-level melee character, a surprisingly effective strategy is to get as close to them as possible, to the extent that your character is physically touching the centurion's legs. This is effective because centurions cannot lower their arms or tilt their heads to attack enemies beneath their shoulders, thus enabling you to encircle them and avoid their attacks with relative ease. Another great strategy is to get them stuck outside of a small hallway and get out of range of their steam attack, allowing you to hit it with ranged attacks. It is worth noting when facing them that they are immune to nearly all shouts except for [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md), which causes them to stagger, and [Marked for Death](Skyrim_Marked_for_Death.md). Sometimes when using Unrelenting Force on a centurion before it has activated from within its gantry, it can be rendered inert and it will not activate immediately, enabling you to strike it at will for a time. When defeated, a centurion can be looted for a guaranteed [Centurion Dynamo Core](Skyrim_Centurion_Dynamo_Core.md), a [Grand Soul Gem](Skyrim_Grand_Soul_Gem.md), a number of [Dwarven scraps](Skyrim_Ores_and_Ingots.md#Dwemer_Metal_.28Smeltable.29), leveled [arrows](Skyrim_Arrows.md), and a few precious [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md).

### Dwarven Ballista<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/SR-creature-Dwarven_Ballista.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dwarven_Ballista.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Ballista.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dwarven_Ballista.jpg) A Dwarven ballista Dwarven ballistae are automatons that can be encountered on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Dragonborn_Places.md) in the [Dwarven ruins](Skyrim_Dwarven_Ruins.md) located there. The *[Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)* [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md) also adds them to the leveled lists used in some Dwarven ruins in Skyrim, meaning you can encounter them there as well. Ballistae are ranged attackers that will make every effort to stay as far away from their targets as possible and assail them with high-power ballista bolts from a distance. Their attacks are particularly powerful, will stagger those they hit, and completely ignore the armor of their targets, enhancing their strength further. For this reason, they are high priority targets to remove as soon as possible whenever they are encountered. An effective strategy for combating them is to stay at range and evade their attack, and then rush them and attempt to defeat them before they have a chance to reload their ballista bolt. Much like other automatons, the ballista's inventory when destroyed includes [Dwarven metal bits](Skyrim_Ores_and_Ingots.md#Dwemer_Metal_.28Smeltable.29), [Dwarven Oil](Skyrim_Dwarven_Oil.md), [soul gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md), and a decent chance at precious [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md).

## Notes
- The lightning bolt that certain leveled Spiders shoot is referred to the game as "Shock Spit" (which is seen in the Active Effects submenu when you're hit).
- [Bthardamz](Skyrim_Bthardamz.md) is the only ruin in Skyrim where you can find a wandering Dwarven Centurion, as all others are stationed in charging stations before activating. It's located in the hallways before you enter the chamber with [Orchendor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orchendor). If left alone (it doesn't detect you and you don't engage it), it will walk down the hall, then the crumbled stairs and circle the column with the waterfall inside it, then go back the way it came, repeating this patrol.

## Bugs
- If you manage to stagger a Centurion before it finishes its activation cycle, it may become stuck in place and unable to move.
- Killing a Dwemer Ballista can sometimes crash the game. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.1130.0, fixes this bug.
- Lightning-based magic can cause defeated automatons to turn into ash if the *Disintegrate* perk is active. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.1, fixes this bug.
- [Bloodthorn](Skyrim_Bloodthorn.md) and [Halldir's Staff](Skyrim_Halldir%27s_Staff.md) can soul trap Dwarven automatons despite the fact that they don't have souls in the first place. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.6, addresses this issue. The keywords to keep them from working on Dwemer constructs were missing.
- Centurions may approach but not attack, either with melee or their steam breath. Instead, they will only stand very close (sometimes strafing around you), but still appear as a hostile red dot on the compass. If you run away, they will follow, but still not attack when they catch up to you. If this bug happens, they still can be attacked and killed. <sup>**?**</sup>
