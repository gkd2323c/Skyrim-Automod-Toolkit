[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/ON-place-Village_of_the_Lost.jpg/200px-ON-place-Village_of_the_Lost.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Village_of_the_Lost.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Village_of_the_Lost.jpg) The Village of the Lost The Village of the Lost is a border realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and ruled by Overseer Grexev. It formed during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) when Molag Bal attempted to fuse Coldharbour and [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). Although not part of Coldharbour proper, three villages were pulled from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and landed in the realm after being fooled by deals with the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) Grexev. These included a small township in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) named [Silver Run](Lore_Silver_Run.md), along with the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) village of Khaj'yahai and the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) stronghold of Dushnikh's Shadow.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HITW-1)</sup> These villages were then scattered among islands of floating rock, and the villagers' souls were stolen. As it materialized, the realm was entirely covered in [Azure Plasm](Lore_Azure_Plasm.md), unformed chaotic creatia common in Coldharbour, from which all Oblivion matter is formed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

The leaders of the three villages— Suronii, Zirani and Ugorz— had survived the initial merging and remained as [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md). [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and undead also flocked to the realm in great number to protect it. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a Tamrielic invasion force entered Coldharbour in an attempt to halt the Planemeld. A servant of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) named Mezha-dro came to the realm to seal tears in Oblivion and prevent any more parts of Tamriel from slipping into the realm.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HITW-1)</sup>

The land that was torn from Tamriel during the Planemeld was restored after the defeat of Molag Bal at the hands of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOArd-3)</sup>
