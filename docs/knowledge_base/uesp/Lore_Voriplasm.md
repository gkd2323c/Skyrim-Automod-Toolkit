A Voriplasm (a.k.a. an **Ooze** or **Slime**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-pet-2)</sup> is an amorphous blob of sentient green mucus,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Clog-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Survival G-4)</sup> mostly found in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Known for having an insatiable hunger, these creatures can strip down their victims to the bone within moments,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAA4-5)</sup> or can form spikes to pierce their target.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Survival G-4)</sup> Voriplasms can then envelop and puppeteer the remaining skeletal structure of their victims as a "voriplasmic corpse".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OV-6)</sup> The word for their name in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md) is unknown, but it translates to "death-mud".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONLCL-7)</sup>

It is believed that their ability to split into smaller entities is how these slimes reproduce. One Argonian theorizes that perhaps all voriplasms originate from one singular "mother-beast".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Survival G-4)</sup> Leading credence to this theory is the "Wuju-Ka", a gargantuan slime that was sealed away in Tsofeer Caverns in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md). This Wuju-Ka was essentially a spawning pool, churning out smaller versions of itself, and had a higher degree of control over them than the common voriplasm. Indeed, it utilized them as its "eyes, ears, and appendages", and through these extensions is how it interacted with the world outside its containment.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

Voriplasms are considered highly metamorphic and are able to adapt to their surroundings with unbelievable specificity.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Voriplasms, or at least organisms very similar to them, seem to be capable of forming from unclean locations or diseased creatures, even outside of Black Marsh.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Voriplasms can even be found in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), primarily around [Shipwright's Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shipwright%27s_Regret), where they are known as **Swamp Rot**.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

## Uses
Argonian weaponry is sometimes infused with voriplasm essence.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Voriplasm-13)</sup> [Alchemists](Lore_Alchemy.md) are known to have interest in studying the creatures, even to the commitment of importing them from Black Marsh.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Clog-3)</sup> A mage from [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md) found a way to tentatively "tame" small voriplasms. While these pets follow their owners around, they are not particularly docile, and will attempt to engulf their owners if the opportunity arises.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-pet-2)</sup> The [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) uses voriplasms as a method for spreading disease.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Certain Daedra are known to take forms resembling voriplasms.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
