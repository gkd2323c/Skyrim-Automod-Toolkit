# Imperialized Reachfolk

Imperialized Reachfolk are [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) who have assimilated into [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) lands.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tisfira-1)</sup> During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), especially during the reign of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), a larger number of Reachfolk than ever ventured out in search of their fortunes.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup> Some of them were found in service of the Emperor [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), and in his [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arloakh-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARANA-4)</sup> Moricar's son, [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) was regarded as the most Imperialized among the Reachfolk Emperors.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SHOTLE-5)</sup> Entire clans could be found within [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Medresi-6)</sup> While they were eventually expelled from Cyrodiil during the reign of [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Arvnir-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MTCMC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-COT5C-9)</sup> by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), some could still be encountered in the former Imperial territories, including the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TR-10)</sup> [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Trano-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gellsoane-12)</sup> and the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-West Weald Reachfolk-13)</sup> Some of them expressed longing for the times of the Longhouse Emperors.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Isolelme-14)</sup> Many of them eventually made their way back to the [Reach](Lore_Reach.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARANA-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arloakh-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Senan-15)</sup> Reachfolk could also be found in various other territories, traveled abroad to the lands of [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md),<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Bastheladir-16)</sup> and could also be encountered in foreign lands,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-GSC-17)</sup> affiliating themselves with various foreign factions,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AAR-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Hroltar-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Sibellec-20)</sup> and some even delving into the realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), such as [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Kelaer-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Iglytha-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Anchorite-23)</sup> the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md),<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Caruggan-24)</sup> and [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Cipher_Cianrella-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Cipher_Devkeln-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Cipher_Bernollach-27)</sup> Reachfolk were present abroad and engaged in political and diplomatic missions on behalf of their nation in Cyrodiil and Valenwood.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CS-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-AG-29)</sup>

After the imperialization and the loss of independence, some [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and surrounding areas embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Belchimac-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Cedran-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Degaine-32)</sup>

The Reachfolk of Markarth, known as the **Freehands of Markarth**,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-AC-33)</sup> while still of the Reach, were exposed to more foreign influences than the [Wilders](Lore_Wilders.md), and were known to import foreign goods.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-VB-34)</sup> They had a higher literacy rate,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arloakh-3)</sup> and access to more commodities,<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Bretull-35)</sup> and differed culturally to some degree from the Wilders.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ansvyn-36)</sup> Clans new to Markarth were known for different cultural behaviors compared to those accustomed to city life.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Beirrulla-37)</sup>
