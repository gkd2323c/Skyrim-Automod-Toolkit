[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/db/LO-map-Elsweyr_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Elsweyr_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Elsweyr_(Anthology).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Elsweyr_(Anthology).jpg.md) **Elsweyr** [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/LO-race-Khajiit.png/200px-LO-race-Khajiit.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Khajiit.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Khajiit.png) Illustration of a typical Khajiit male Elsweyr is a region that lies on the southern coast of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and is home to the feline [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). It is divided into two major climates: savannahs, badlands and dry plains in the north, and fertile lands of jungle and rainforests in the south.

Originally, sixteen Khajiiti clans roamed the province, and were often simplified as belonging to a region or realm. With a combination of outside pressures and mastery of areas of expertise, it became more accurate to associate a clan with an area. Eventually, sixteen tribes became [sixteen kingdoms](Lore_Empires.md#Sixteen_Kingdoms_of_Elsweyr): [Ne-Quin-Al](Lore_Ne-Quin-Al.md), [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md), [Dune](Lore_Dune.md), [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md), [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Elsweyr).md), [Meirvale](Lore_Meirvale.md), and [Helkarn](Lore_Helkarn.md) in the north; [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md), [Bruk'ra](Lore_Bruk%27ra.md), [Pa'alatiin](Lore_Pa%27alatiin.md), [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md), [Tenmar](Lore_Tenmar.md), [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), [Khenarthia](Lore_Khenarthia.md), and [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) in the south.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOSongof Kingdoms-2)</sup> The lullaby known as the Hasa Zha'ja explains how these clans got their name, while the poem known as Zhan Zhab tells of the transition from clan names being used to refer to the names of regions. Due to the Thrassian Plague, these sixteen clans changed their tribal functions or they may have died due to starvation and other hardships. These clans essentially became two and were separated ideologically and geographically as what we would come to know as the two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOAPAI-3)</sup> These two kingdoms would eventually be united into the [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md), and once more separated in two kingdoms in the [Third Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

## Geography
Elsweyr is bordered by [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to the west and by [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to the north and east, with access to [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) and the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md) to the south. [Lake Vread](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Vread) is a body of water located somewhere in the eastern portion of Elsweyr.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FSOBDE-7)</sup>

Elsweyr is divided into two main regions: [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) in the north and [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) in the south. The island of [Khenarthi's Roost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi%27s_Roost) in the Southern Sea is also included in the province of Elsweyr. The Saimisil Steppes is another region found within the province.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AT-8)</sup>

### Anequina
[Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) encompasses the northern half of Elsweyr. The region is dominated by harsh badlands and dry plains,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> which gave rise to a hardened warrior culture among the local Khajiit that viewed their neighbors to the south as decadent and depraved.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/ON-place-Weeping_Scar_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Weeping_Scar_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Weeping_Scar_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Weeping_Scar_02.jpg) The Scar
#### The Scar
[The Scar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Scar) (also known as the **Scar of Anequina** or simply the **Anequina Desert**)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOLoad Meirvale-9)</sup> is a large desert that runs at the center of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md).

The Scar is also the name of the main feature of the region: a large canyon that crosses the entire desert from the southwest to the northeast. It is a landscape of harsh wastelands, desolate mesas and treacherous cliffs. Smaller canyons like the Ashen Scar connect to the main chasm.

The largest settlement, is the city of [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md) in the north. Built on top of a mesa, in the middle of the chasm, it is only accessible via narrow stone bridges. At the other end of the canyon, The Stitches is a shanty town crippled by criminal activities.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-place-Anequina_Aqueduct.jpg/200px-ON-place-Anequina_Aqueduct.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anequina_Aqueduct.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anequina_Aqueduct.jpg) The Rim Territories
#### The Rim Territories
The [Rim Territories](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rim_Territories) (or simply known as **the Rim**)<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSK-10)</sup> is composed of the eastern borderlands of Anequina, around the regional capital of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md). It Includes the hills and badlands surrounding the City and the southern valley that leads to [Hakoshae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hakoshae).

The region is the namesake of the [Rim-Men](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rim-Men), a group of [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) that descend from the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> The Rim is irrigated by the Anequina Aqueduct, providing water to its inhabitants and allowing the culture of Moonsugar cane.

The Tenmar Borderlands in the south are attached to the Rim Territories. It consists of a fertile valley between the desert of the Scar and the jungle of Pelitine, stretching all the way to [Grahtwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahtwood). It includes the settlement of Merryvale and the Moon Gate of Anequina.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/ON-place-Riverhold_04.jpg/200px-ON-place-Riverhold_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Riverhold_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Riverhold_04.jpg) The High Savannah
#### The High Savannah
The [High Savannah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Savannah) is an area of arid savannah and dry grassland that stretches along the northern border of Elsweyr.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GTNE-11)</sup> Its largest settlement is the city of [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESO-12)</sup> There are twisted trees found around the region,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RRR-13)</sup> and stiff savannah breezes blow through [The Rim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Rim) from the savannah.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOLain-14)</sup>

It is home to a variety of fauna, particularly felines<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AAIT-15)</sup> domesticated by the native Khajiit such as the Midnight Fennec Fox<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DFF-16)</sup> and Pride-King Lion, the latter of which are the top predators of the area.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PKL-17)</sup> "Savannah Grassware" consisting of light but sturdy woven bowls are fashioned out of the native plants.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SG-18)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/ON-place-Dawnmead.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dawnmead.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dawnmead.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dawnmead.jpg) Reaper's March
#### Reaper's March
[Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March) is a region connecting the jungles of northeastern [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) with the rolling plains of northwestern Elsweyr.

Only the eastern half of Reaper's March is considered a part of Elsweyr: the area called [Dawnmead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dawnmead) surrounding the city of [Dune](Lore_Dune.md) and a fraction of [Jodewood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jodewood) in the south, including the city of [Rawl'kha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawl%27kha).

Despite the dry conditions of Reaper's March, the Khajiit have managed to farm and harvest [moon sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar) in the area for generations through extensive irrigation projects.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MSITM-19)</sup>

Khajiiti temples in the region are noted for their awe-inspiring beauty, though there are many other monuments and grand structures which are decayed and ruined.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TME-20)</sup>

### Pellitine
[Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) encompasses the southern half of Elsweyr. The region is considerably more fertile than the arid north, and is replete with jungles, rainforests, and river basins.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> The Khajiit of Pellitine were consequently wealthier than their neighbors to the north, whom they derided as uncouth barbarians.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> The seat of the Mane, [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), is located here.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/ON-place-Senchal.jpg/200px-ON-place-Senchal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Senchal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Senchal.jpg) Quin'rawl Peninsula
#### Quin'rawl Peninsula
The [Quin'rawl Peninsula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quin%27rawl_Peninsula) (sometimes simply called **Quin'rawl**<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ADi M-21)</sup>) is a region found in the southeast of Elsweyr between the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md) and the [Topal Sea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Topal_Sea). It is a region of perilous highlands across the north, the sandy beaches in the southeast, and the marshes of the south.

Quin'rawl is heavily centered around [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), a major city in Elsweyr that also serves as Pellitine's capital since the upheavals of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ho SAO-22)</sup> Several islands are found off the region's coast, such as [Tideholm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tideholm) and [Topal Island](Lore_Topal_Island.md) to the east, and both [Khenarthi's Roost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi%27s_Roost) and [Dragonhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonhold) to the south.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/ON-place-Tenmar_Forest.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tenmar_Forest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tenmar_Forest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tenmar_Forest.jpg) Tenmar Forest
#### Tenmar Forest
The [Tenmar Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tenmar_Forest), also known as the **Tenmar Jungle**<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>, in southern Elsweyr is a large region including the cities of [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), the seat of the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md), and [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md). Primarily known for the production and export of [moon sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar), its sugarcane groves are venerated and diligently guarded by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-place-Temple_of_the_Crescent_Moons.jpg/200px-ON-place-Temple_of_the_Crescent_Moons.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Temple_of_the_Crescent_Moons.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Temple_of_the_Crescent_Moons.jpg) Khenarthi's Roost
### Khenarthi's Roost
[Khenarthi's Roost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi%27s_Roost), the ** Isle of Three Temples**, is a small island off the coast of Elsweyr inhabited by both [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) and [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), named for the Khajiiti goddess [Khenarthi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi). The island is dotted with [moon sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar) plantations and is a location of both active Khajiiti worship and Maormer magic, as the Khajiit signed a peace treaty with the Maormer which allowed them to settle on the island. The port city of [Mistral](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mistral) functions as the capital of the island and the center of commerce. Khenarthi's Roost once contained the ancient Kingdom of **Khenarthia**, one of the original sixteen kingdoms of Elsweyr, known for their sugar production and expert fishermen.

## History

### Since the Beginning of Time
The origins of the region of today's Elsweyr and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) lies far back in the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), when [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) was only one large continent. As the war among the wandering [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) and Old Ehlnofey broke out, the continent broke apart into four.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TAA-24)</sup> The later lands of Elsweyr remained on what is now known as [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The Khajiiti creation myth explains the hot deserts of northern Elsweyr and the wet and poisonous [Tenmar Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tenmar_Forest) in the south to be made by [Nirni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirni) as revenge for being tricked by [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-WOCMA-25)</sup>

Early records and encounters reveal the Khajiit to have lived far beyond today's borders of Elsweyr—reaching north into the [Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands), close to today's [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md)<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-FOTN-26)</sup>, and west into [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) —making them the dominant culture of southern Tamriel. It was already highly developed as [Men](Lore_Man.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) just arrived on the continent and fought for dominance in the north.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> The ancient Khajiit in the Heartlands also may be responsible for the extinction of the [Bird Men](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird_Men) home to [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-FOTN-26)</sup> In some of Tamriel's oldest history, the province of Elsweyr was ruled by the [Moon-Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon-Emperor). Gracious architecture that shows veneration towards the moons derives from this, as seen across modern-day [Dune](Lore_Dune.md)'s high walls.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

### The Sixteen Kingdoms
Sometime in the Merethic Era, when Elsweyr was composed of sixteen kingdoms, Kaalgrontiid and his horde of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) came to dominate the land and above all, consume the Moon's lunar power. Kaalgrontiid is believed by some to originate from Alduin's horde in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), having been driven to split off from the bulk of his kin in the north in order to sate his own pride and ambition. This behavior is considered normal for dragons, who are naturally driven to seek mastery.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-LADragons-28)</sup> Kaalgrontiid in particular hoped that with the Moon's power, he would become [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)'s equal.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOKaalgrontiid-29)</sup> To stop them, The Khajiit hero [Khunzar-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khunzar-ri) recruited four companions, [Nurarion the Perfect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nurarion_the_Perfect), [Flinthild Demon-Hunter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flinthild_Demon-Hunter), [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anequina_Sharp-Tongue), and [Cadwell the Betrayer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cadwell).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Kat D-30)</sup> When fighting them proved to be futile, Cadwell suggested that it may be best to convince the dragons that they were now a willing part of their plans. In preparation, Anequina attuned to Shadow Dance Temple, incited the moons to align to form an eclipse, allowing the Moon Gate of Anequina to open a portal the Plane of [Jode](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode), where the object the dragons desired, [Jode's Core](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode%27s_Core), rested. The dragon's ambitions would be their undoing, as the dragons accessed the [Plane of Jode](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Plane_of_Jode), but Khunzar-ri convinced them to not simply absorb the power of the Core like they originally intended, but instead store their energy within it in the guise that they would combine the lunar power with their own.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOCadwell-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOKhamira-32)</sup> The gamble worked, and the dragons were weakened enough for them to retreat back into the wilderness.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Kat D-30)</sup> To finish the job, Khunzar-ri once again convinced the dragons to play a part of his scheme. He managed to lure them into the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus), where he managed to seal them away. The Dragons would be obscured as "demons" in legends, and become the fabled "Demon Weapon". Their power would be coveted by many people.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Kat D-30)</sup>

By the beginning of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the sixteen realms in the region of today's Elsweyr still existed.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Pacrooti-33)</sup> These were not comparable to the kingdoms of Men and Mer, as those Khajiiti realms were not fighting each other for power or land. Instead they were existing in harmony and complementary. Each such kingdom was specialized on providing goods or services and shared it with their neighbors.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-EGTE-34)</sup> [Ne Quin-al](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ne_Quin-al), also known as *[Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md)* in Cyrodilic, for example provided their excellent trained fighters of the [Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Two-Moons_Dance) to [Torval](Lore_Torval.md) in exchange for fish and other goods from the sea. Influence and power shifted constantly, and the dominance of the regions cycled through the kingdoms depending on the phases of [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md). For example, Ne Quin-al was in power at full moons, while Torval was when both moons were half, or [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) when they were both new.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

In all this time, the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) seemed to have shown little activity in the regions controlled by the Khajiit.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Burri-35)</sup> Only after [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243), when [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) begun to persecute the Ayleids, the clan of [Atatar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atatar) tried to find refugee in the Khajiiti lands. However, most of them died on the ill-fated expedition.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ASIV-36)</sup>

At similar time [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) has been slaying many Khajiit, having them mistaken for another strain of [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md). [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh) is being attributed to have repelled this attack<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-VOFTK-37)</sup>, although it also is said Pelinal had realized his error himself.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/eb/ON-place-Do%27Krin_Monastery.jpg/200px-ON-place-Do%27Krin_Monastery.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Do%27Krin_Monastery.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Do%27Krin_Monastery.jpg) Do'Krin Monastery was built with the loot of the Anequine Conquests In the [fifth century of the First Era](Lore_First_Era.md#Fifth_Century)<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-RTR-38)</sup> the Golden Beast of Anequina, [Darloc Brae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darloc_Brae), expanded the territory of Ne Quin-al in the process of the [Anequine Conquests](Lore_Anequine_Conquests.md) until it ranged from Khaj Rawlith in the west, over [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia), up to the north at [Larsius River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Larsius_River), and [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) in the east. The loot of the Conquests he used to build Do'Krin Monastery<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LSDKM-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-LSKR-40)</sup> He was also invited to the coronation of the [Emperor Gorieus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorieus) in [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-RTR-38)</sup>

With the year of [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668) came [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md), an event caused by the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE1M-41)</sup>

Over many centuries there are no further records of the happenings in the kingdoms of the Khajiit. Only the [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) from [1E 1200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1200) until [1E 2208](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2208)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-42)</sup> was mentioned to be observed by the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md), using the constancy of the [Ja-Kha'jay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ja-Kha%27jay) to record it.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-WWYDB-43)</sup>

The political balance in the sixteen Khajiiti kingdoms proved stable enough for thousands of years. Neither the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) nor the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) of Valenwood dared to extend their borders too far into the lands of the Khajiit, but caused increasing animosity between the Khajiiti realms.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-EGTE-34)</sup>

### The Two Kingdoms
After two millennia, the political landscape of the Khajiiti territory changed drastically with the emerging [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) of [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-EGTE-34)</sup> It expanded along the trading routes deep into the realm of the Khajiit and caused mass mortality. The balance between the sixteen kingdoms was finally destroyed as the survivors were forced into undesired roles; initial consternation turned to distrust and violence.

The remains of the original sixteen realms were two realms with opposing cultures: The northern [Ne Quin-al](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ne_Quin-al) with its many warrior clans, and the wealthy southern [Pa'alatiin](Lore_Pa%27alatiin.md), also known as *[Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md)* in Cyrodilic. Both kingdoms fought for centuries, but neither could ever gain the upper hand in Elsweyr.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-EGTE-34)</sup>

#### In the Second Empire
With the rise of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), the newfound [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) began to expand. Also, the two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine could not resist the Empire's power and were conquered, alongside a propaganda campaign to justify the invasion.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-MBHAYQ-44)</sup> Later, in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), Queen Naghea of [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) lead the left flank for the Empire on in the [Battle of Bodrum](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-2920FS-45)</sup> In the same year, the King of Senchal, [Dro'Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro%27Zel), became appointed advisor to the court of [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-2920SD-46)</sup>

As long as Anequina and Pellitine were part of the Empire, they found security in it, but also never stopped rebelling against it at the same time.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> The Empire never could bring an end to the fights between the two Khajiit kingdoms.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-EGTE-34)</sup>

### The Formation of the Elsweyr Confederacy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-concept-Elsweyr_Map.png/200px-ON-concept-Elsweyr_Map.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Elsweyr_Map.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Elsweyr_Map.png) Elsweyr around the Second Era The ongoing strife between Anequin and Pellitine was finally resolved by the Khajiit themselves. [Keirgo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keirgo) of Anequina and [Eshita](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eshita) of Pellitine married in [2E 309](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_309) and combined the two conflicting kingdoms, aware of the political impact, into one: The [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md). The reason for the name is a matter of debate; either a reference to the old Khajiiti proverb "A perfect society is always found elsewhere", or to the [Sands Behind the Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sands_Behind_the_Stars), in [Ta'agra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ta%27agra) called *Llesw'er*.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-EGTE-34)</sup> The latter however is a paradise promised by the [Riddle'Thar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> two years after the Elsweyr Confederacy had already formed.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONLSRT-47)</sup>

#### The Rebellion
The formation of the new kingdom did not bring the expected peace. The cleft between tribal culture of the north and nobility of the south was still present. The power between the two former kingdoms, each had their own central government and allied tribes, shifted to the nobility. The chieftains of the north felt betrayed by both their ruling classes and forgot about their vendettas only to sign treaties of their own. Soon the cities of former Anequina were under constant attack, leaving King Keirgo no choice than to petition to the Empire for help. But the Empire itself was in similar unrest, as they lost their own ruler, [Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie), assassinated in his palace in Senchal, in [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TBOD-48)</sup> As [Ne Quin-al](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ne_Quin-al), the old capital, fell to the rebels, the young Confederation seemed to soon break apart.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg/200px-ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rawl%27kha_Temple.jpg) Rawl'kha temple in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) As the rebellion kept going on, [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta) —the spiritual leader of the Khajiit—revealed the [Riddle'Thar Epiphany](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar) in the [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance) of [Rawl'kha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawl%27kha) in [2E 311](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_311).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONLSRT-47)</sup> The edicts from this key event in Khajiiti theology however were not accepted by everyone at this time<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Shazah-49)</sup>, leading to those exiled and their temples deconsecrated.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ONLSCS-50)</sup> Rid-Thar-ri'Datta was since then known as the First Mane, although there have been at least two Manes before him.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Shazah-49)</sup> The Mane, usually nonpartisan, used Riddle'Thar to proclaim a new system of dividing the powers in Elsweyr. Bound to the dance of the two moons—their different phases—the power in a region shifted between the nobility of the cities and the chieftains of the deserts. The Mane itself was overseeing this shift of powers, and as this system was implemented it finally brought peace to Elsweyr some time around or after [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324) —lasting under many more Manes.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

Some time after the last Potentate, [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak), of the Second Empire was killed in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430)<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TBOD-48)</sup>, warlord [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(warlord).md) persecuted the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The Khajiit granted the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) and others asylum in and around Rimmen in northern Elsweyr.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> They later built the Tonenaka in the city.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>:164</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-CMT-52)</sup>

#### Outbreak of the Knahaten Flu
Two hundred years after the rebellion—that followed the formation of the Elsweyr Confederacy—the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) ravaged the land of the Khajiit just as unprepared as the Thrassian Plague before.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TEATC-53)</sup> The flu, reported the first time in [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold) in [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560)<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-PGE3A-54)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE1W-55)</sup>, appeared in Elsweyr the first time on Sweet Street in the [Black Keirgo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Keirgo) slums of Senchal. First dismissed as a toxin in the goods by the city's elders, it spread over to Dagi's Pride, Squint-Eye, and the docks of [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md). The expansion of the flu accelerated, soon to be in [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md) in [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565)<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Shazah-49)</sup>, [Dune](Lore_Dune.md), [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md), and everywhere between.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TEATC-53)</sup>

As the Khajiit were focused on fighting their own extinction, [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) armies, in the time of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), invaded the northern part of Elsweyr and annexed it. Their invasion went as far as [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) and Orcrest, until they were fought back there in [2E 562](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_562) or a few years later.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Khali-56)</sup>

In the meantime, Clan Mother Mizaba-ko of Corinthe identified the transfer of the flu, and Rathuni-la Dawnwhisker distilled a sorghum-tea that mitigated the worst of the symptoms. Lord Gharesh-ri, Speaker for the Mane, organized the remnants of the [Mane's Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mane%27s_Legion) to maintain order and use the newfound knowledge. However, all this did not prevent further spreading of the flu and many more dead. When hope was almost lost, unexpected help came from the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), bringing direly needed physicians, healers, and supplies. This aid gradually defeated the Knahaten Flu and saving the Khajiit. Most of them were however suspicious about the Altmer's help, as it was given to preserve the Khajiit as possible alliance partners in times of need.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TEATC-53)</sup>

In [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576) [Euraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxia_Tharn) became Queen of Rimmen after the [Frostfall Coup](Lore_Frostfall_Coup.md).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-HTON-57)</sup>

#### In the First Aldmeri Dominion
With the death of King [Hidellith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hidellith) of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) took up the throne.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ATUQ-58)</sup> As around this time invading armies moved towards southwest Tamriel, she summoned the Khajiit to stand by her side, as compensation for helping them defeating the Knahaten Flu. The signing of the [Elden Accord](Lore_Elden_Accord.md) by Queen Ayrenn, [Lord Gharesh-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Lord_Gharesh-ri) and King [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran) formed the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in which the Khajiit thus entered.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Gharesh-ri-59)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TEATC-53)</sup> Under the united banner of the Dominion, they now also moved into the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TCC-60)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-WTNAI-61)</sup>

##### Attack on Reapers March
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/ON-concept-Reapers_March.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Reapers_March.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Reapers_March.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Reapers_March.jpg) Reapers March in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) also hit [Reapers March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reapers_March) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) from the [realm](Lore_Coldharbour.md) of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), with the help of the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult), appeared all over the region in an attempt to suck it into Coldharbour.

Also in this year, the Colovians once again invaded northern Elsweyr. [Javad Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Javad_Tharn) worked together with the Stonefire Cult, and they were able to corrupt the current Mane [Akkhuz-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Akkhuz-ri) as he was on a travel to Valenwood—to show condolence to the death of [Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silvenar) [Edhelorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Edhelorn) in the same year.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Bodring-62)</sup> The Mane was henceforth possessed by the Dark Mane —a [Dro-m'Athra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro-m%27Athra) —and worked together with Javad Tharn.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Khali-56)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Kazirra-63)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-The Colovian Occupation-64)</sup> On the way to the Handfasting of the new Silvenar [Indaenir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indaenir) and [Green Lady](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Lady) [Finoriell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Finoriell), Akkhuz-ri attacked his followers and fled to Reaper's March.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Gharesh-ri-59)</sup>

On his way, Akkhuz-ri freed the Dro-m'Athra in the long forgotten temple of Khaj Rawlith, below Fort Grimwatch, to aid Colovian siege there. The [Moon Hallowed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soulless_One) and both Lunar Champions, Shazah and Khali, fought the Colovians and Dro-m'Athra back with the aid of the spirit of Rid-Thar-ri'Datta.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Grim Situation-65)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Grimmer Still-66)</sup> In Arenthia, the Moon Hallowed helped the local militia to end the Colovian occupation, destroy a Dark Anchor, and drive the Dark Mane out of Akkhuz-ri.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-The Colovian Occupation-64)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Stonefire Machinations-67)</sup>

The Mane resigned due the dark influence that had befallen him<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Akkhuz Ri-68)</sup>, leading the two Lunar Champions on the way to become his successors. In the temple of Rawl'kha, Shazah and Khali faced the Trial Path successfully. Before this point there never have been two Lunar Champions at the same time to contest for the position of the Mane before in recorded history.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Kauzanabi-jo-69)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-The First Step-70)</sup>

[Martialis Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Martialis_Tharn), Javad Tharn and the Stonefire Cult also sought to acquire the Golden Claw<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Rid Thar Ri Datta-71)</sup> —a powerful relic from the era of Mane [Tullar-dra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tullar-dra)<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Azahrr-72)</sup> —in Do'Krin Monastery, but were fought back by the Moon Hallowed and Rid-Thar-ri'Datta's spirit.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Motes In The Moonlight-73)</sup>

After this, Shazah, Khali and the Moon Hallowed fought the Dark Mane and by him and Javad Tharn released Dro-m'Athra in [Moonmont](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonmont).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Rid Thar Ri Datta-71)</sup> One of the two Lunar Champions sacrificed herself to contain the Dark Mane, leaving only one Lunar Champion left.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-The Golden Claw-74)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/ON-place-Dune.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dune.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dune.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dune.jpg) Dune in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Later the Moon Hallowed and remaining Lunar Champion arrived in [Dune](Lore_Dune.md). At this time, the city was under an attack of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), summoned up by Bayya and Javad Tharn.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Bayya-75)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Esan-76)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Dagila-77)</sup> The city was liberated, the by the Stonefire Cult befouled temples<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Shazah-49)</sup> cleansed, and a Dark Anchor at the Shrine to Lorkhaj in the temple district destroyed.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-The Fires Of Dune-78)</sup> In presence of the Dominion's leaders and other important figures at the Two Moons Path temple, the Moon Hallowed and Lunar Champion walked the last path—the Two Moons Path. There they ultimately defeated Javad Tharn and the Dark Mane. The other Lunar Champion that sacrificed herself before was set free by this, able to reach [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Rid Thar Ri Datta-71)</sup> Returned from the Path, the remaining Lunar Champion rose as new Mane of the Khajiit.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-The Den Of Lorkhaj-79)</sup>

With the destruction of the Great Shackle and the end of the Planemeld within the same year— [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) —also the Dark Anchors no longer threatened Reapers March.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Breaking The Shackle-80)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-The Final Assault-81)</sup>

##### Dragons over Elsweyr
After the retrieval of the Wrathstone around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Imperial battlemage [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) inadvertently unleashed the Dragons from their containment from the Halls of Colossus, thus causing them to continue in their quest for the Moon's Lunar Power. The Dragons allied themselves with the forces of the Queen of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), [Euraxia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Euraxia_Tharn), who were lead to believe that the dragons were willing to be used as tools. The Dragons were quick to cross Euraxia when she was no longer a use to them, and was left without their aid against the forces of Elsweyr Defense Force as well as the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md). The turn of events resulted in her death and the return of Rimmen to Khajiit control. Euraxia's forces now without a leader, allied with the Dragons to avoid their wrath.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-ESOTFO-82)</sup> The Dragons were put to a halt when the Vestige stepped into the Plane of Jode and defeated Kaalgrontiid and his kin, preventing the lunar power from being completely absorbed.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ESOJC-83)</sup>

The dragon Laatvulon sought to continue his master Kaalgrontiid's plan of bringing on the Dark Aeon, intending for Kaalgrontiid to become Akatosh's equal. To this end, Laatvulon created a Khajiiti dragon cult, the New Moon Cult, whose ranks were bolstered by many of the vulnerable Khajiit from the region, such as those who were affected by the Knahaten Flu. The red dragon [Nahfahlaar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nahfahlaar), who was Laatvulon's ancient rival, dueled Laatvulon near the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) in southern Elsweyr, but was overpowered due to Laatvulon's newfound power. Afterwards, Taking refuge in [Tideholm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tideholm), Nahfahlaar was encountered by the Dragonguard, which been recently reformed by the Redguard Blademaster [Sai Sahan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai_Sahan). Seeing that Nahfahlaar meant no harm, an alliance was forged once again with former foes/allies, one in which Nahfahlaar served as a valuable asset. This alliance led to victory, and Laatvulon, the Demon from the East, was slain.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-84)</sup>

Nahfahlaar subsequently aided the Dragonguard in defeating Kaalgrontiid and the remnants of the New Moon Cult. Kaalgrontiid returned and planned to siphon the energy he collected from Jode's Core and become empowered by a giant cluster of [Aeonstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeonstone). To prevent anyone from disrupting his plans, he utilized the energy of the Aeonstone to create an aegis, a massive and impenetrable ward, around the ancient fortress of Dragonhold. Through the combined efforts of Nahfahlaar, the Dragonguard, and Queen [Khamira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khamira)'s forces, Kaalgrontiid was slain. However, the Dark Aeon became unstable as a result of Kaalgrontiid's machinations and threatened to destroy the entire continent of Tamriel. It required the combined power of Abnur Tharn and Nahfahlaar to prevent the Aeonstone from affecting the area outside of Dragonhold. Nahfahlaar and Abnur were successful, but only at the cost of Abnur's life. With Tharn's sacrifice, the rage of Dragons was stopped.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-84)</sup>

##### After the Dragons
The Akaviri of Rimmen later joined the cause to restore the Second Empire, when remnants of the [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) army of the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) of [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572) tried seizing the Throne from Attrebus' successors. This attempt did not succeed, and the Khajiit used the chaos of these events to reclaim lost land in a series of border wars.

Rimmen seceded from Elsweyr in [2E 812](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_812), forming the Kingdom of Rimmen. They however still were paying taxes to the Mane in return for protection of their independence.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup>

#### In the Third Empire
Towards the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the ascending [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) conquered parts of Anequina and cast out all Khajiit. There, in the Halls of Colossus, he reconstructed and reactivated [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-SMIWDOT-85)</sup> Not long after, Senchal was besieged by [Imperial](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) troops under General Pottreid. The population did not surrender and was slain entirely.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Attrebus-86)</sup> Ultimately, Elsweyr was annexed to become a province of Tiber Septim's [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), but as under the Second Empire, there have been rebellions against the Empire.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

Under the reign of Emperor [Uriel Septim II](Lore_Uriel_II.md) a rebellion broke out in Torval. It proved too strong and forced the Emperor to lend money from King [Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantiarco) to pay additional mercenaries next to the Imperial Legions to strike it down successfully. In return he had to promise him the hand of his daughter, Galana.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TWQ1-87)</sup>

##### The Five Year War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Valenwood at the end of the Third Era During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) —from [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) to [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399) —a conflict between Valenwood and Elsweyr escalated:<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> In [3E 395](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_395), Bosmer invaded Torval, slaying thousands of citizens as retaliation for, as they claimed, Khajiiti bandit attacks. This [Slaughter of Torval](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Slaughter_of_Torval) marked the beginning of the [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) for the Khajiit.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-MUT-88)</sup> The Bosmer date its beginning back to [3E 394](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_394).<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-CHOA-89)</sup> After a lost battles at Zelinin, the Khajiit won the upper hand in all following: the [Heart of Anequina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Anequina), [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396) at [Fort Sphinxmoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Sphinxmoth), Athay<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-ADIF3-90)</sup> and Vindis.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ADIF4-91)</sup> The Khajiit occupied both banks of [Xylo River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xylo_River) in [Malabal Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malabal_Tor).<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-CHOA-89)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> At the end of the Imperial Simulacrum, with Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md)<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ALOUS-92)</sup> back on the throne, the Empire tried to give back the land to the Bosmer. But Khajiit had already settled down in the area and refused to leave, leaving the Empire to leave the situation, possibly convinced that the land rightly belonged to the Khajiit by ancient treaty, and not to cause another conflict.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

##### Loss of Territory to Leyawiin
At or before [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432) there has been another border conflict; Between Elsweyr and the County of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). A long lasting dispute was resolved by an agreement between the Mane and [Marius Caro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marius_Caro), Count of Leyawiin, leading to a loss of territory for Elsweyr comparable to the win of the previous Five Year War. This agreement however is not accepted by all Khajiit, and so the [Renrijra Krin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Renrijra_Krin) tried to take back the land.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

The last two decades of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) in Elsweyr were marked by the flourishing of [Moon Sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar) trade. Under this influence, the formerly dangerous slum Senchal rose up to a resort for wealthy and powerful Khajiit. The sugar trader Ya'Tirrje, called Gold Cat by his wealth, had a rumored villa in the port city, and paid the guards to keep it crime-free, while continuing his business in Torval, Corinthe and Rimmen.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

### Dissolution

#### After the Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/27/Mfull_Sfull.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) Masser and Secunda vanished for two years in the Void Nights After the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) and the following assassination of Potentate [Ocato of Firsthold](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md), the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Elder_Council.md) begun.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-RT4-93)</sup> In this time, Elsweyr once again found itself independent of the Empire, but was granted no peace. In or around [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48) the Mane was assassinated, sinking the south of Elsweyr into chaos. During this time, an unknown proclaimed himself Potentate of Rimmen and surrounding areas.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>:179</sup> While the city was rich of Moon Sugar, distribution was tightly controlled, and entirely forbidden to Khajiit clans refusing to work in the city.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>:179, 186</sup>

#### Void Nights and Great War
In [4E 98](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_98), Masser and Secunda vanished from the sky for two entire years. The so-called [Void Nights](Lore_Void_Nights.md) ended in 4E 100 with the return of the moons, while the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) took credit for bringing them back. Whether or not they were actually responsible for this turnaround remains unresolved. Nevertheless, as the Khajiit people and culture are tied inexorably close to the moons, the Thalmor were able to win great influence over them as their apparent saviors. Within the following fifteen years, until [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115), the influence of the Empire dwindled so much it was unable to stop the coup which dissolved the Elsweyr Confederacy and recreated the original kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine as client states of the [Third Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup>

In [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171), Aldmeri armies invaded Hammerfell and Cyrodiil simultaneously. A strong force commanded by the Thalmor general Lord [Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md) attacked Cyrodiil from the south, marching out of hidden camps in northern Elsweyr and flanking the Imperial defenses along the Valenwood border.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup>

## Other Information

### Cuisine
*→ See the main article: **[Khajiit Cuisine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khajiit_Cuisine)*** The cuisine of Elsweyr is invariably sweet. Meals such as candies, cakes, puddings, sugarmeats and honey stews makes the staples of the Khajiit diet. Non-Khajiiti are often advised against eating these dishes as most of them contain moon sugar, a dangerous substance to which the Khajiit have both a natural predisposition and a resistance developed from a lifelong consumption of the substance. On such occasions, merely touching the food with lips is considered a polite form of declining.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> Elsweyr Fondue is a dish containing ale, moon sugar and Eidar cheese, which is a prime example of the cuisine.

### Flora
Olives, palms, and tamarisk grow along streams in northern Elsweyr.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>:176</sup>

### Fauna
[Greems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greem) are a type of animal native to Elsweyr that travel in flocks.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup> [Slarjei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Slarjei) are some sort of mount well suited for the desert.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>:149</sup> Red [cattle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cow) graze near Riverhold.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>:144</sup> The hills between Elsweyr and Cyrodiil are filled with [trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TIC-51)</sup><sup>:143</sup>
