*There are no known examples of dragon eggs or dragonlings. The Iliac Bay area has stories of such things, but so far all have proven false. The eggs turned out to be eggs of other reptiles. The small dragons were merely oversized lizards and no relation to true dragons.*
— *[There Be Dragons](Lore_There_Be_Dragons.md)* Dragonlings (also called **Fairy Dragons**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FDS-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTextures-2)</sup> are small, intelligent reptilian creatures that are [draconic](Lore_Dragon.md) in appearance and native to the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region and [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragonling-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FE-4)</sup> Those who speak their language, [Dragonish](Lore_Dragonish.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Dragonish-5)</sup> say that beneath a veneer of maddening riddles and non sequiturs are minds of great wisdom.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFUG-6)</sup> They are known to be aggressive to those that cannot speak their language.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragonling-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Dragonish-5)</sup>

Dragonlings can breathe fire and pose a fair challenge in combat. It is claimed they are even capable of [spellcasting](Lore_Spells.md). As such, a Dragonling is considered a worthy opponent for any adventurer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFUG-6)</sup> They are damaged by the magical protections that safeguard temples and other holy places.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragonling-3)</sup> The [Knightly Orders](Lore_Knightly_Orders.md) of the Iliac Bay region occasionally send their members to slay Dragonlings.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Slay-7)</sup>

Dragonlings are known to reproduce by laying eggs.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Slay-7)</sup> Their droppings are said to have strange, almost magical properties. One account alleged that their excrement can explode if handled too roughly, can change between liquid and solid states, and even sneak away when not being observed.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-The Menagerie-8)</sup>

## Relation to Dragons
Despite all their merits, Dragonlings are seemingly less grand than their lineage would suggest. In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), when many were unaware if the "great Dragons" of legend still lived or had ever existed, the Dragonling's relation to these great Dragons of legends was unknown,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFUG-6)</sup> with some believing them to be younglings<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Slay-7)</sup> or at least kin of true Dragons.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-9)</sup> Many would outright refer to Dragonlings as small Dragons or simply Dragons during this time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-9)</sup> Certain scholars considered it common knowledge that Dragons were incapable of reproduction.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LADit SE-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), scholars came to the conclusion that the Dragonlings of the Iliac Bay region were nothing more than merely "oversized [lizards](Lore_Lizard.md)", with no relation to true Dragons.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TBD-12)</sup> Despite this, some sources still refer to them as Great Wyrms.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The relation, if any, of their language to the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) is unknown.

## Uses
Dragonling scales, referred to as "[Fairy dragon's scales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fairy_dragon%27s_scales)", are used as [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) reagents for their properties of spell reflection.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FDS-1)</sup> The Dragonling Game is said to be one of the most well known sports of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), where opposing teams try to knock a ball through a series of loops on the ground while mounted on a [horse](Lore_Horse.md), each player keeping their roped Dragonling from being captured or killed by the other team.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Games-14)</sup> [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) allegedly used swarms of [undead](Lore_Undead.md) Dragonlings as scouts.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-TPWormgod-15)</sup>

Their language, Dragonish, was taught by the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), the [Akatosh Chantry](Lore_Akatosh_Chantry.md), and the [Temple of Kynareth](Lore_Temple_of_Kynareth.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Dragonish-5)</sup>
