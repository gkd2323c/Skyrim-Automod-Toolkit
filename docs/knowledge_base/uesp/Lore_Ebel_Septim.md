[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png/160px-RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-texture-Red_Diamond_Shield_03.png) [Red Diamond heraldry](Lore_Red_Diamond.md), badge of the Septim Dynasty Ebel of the [Septim House](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) was the third born son of [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) and [Empress Caula Voria](Lore_Caula_Voria.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWRumors1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-A-2)</sup> and therefore a [Dragonborn Prince](Lore_Dragonborn.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBOTD-3)</sup> Ebel was described as "affable and reasonably handsome for a man of middle age," but not nearly as handsome as his brother [Enman](Lore_Enman_Septim.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Loranna-4)</sup> Some close to the family described Ebel and his brothers as "preening fools", and Uriel VII's biographer described the children as being "victims of their parents' unhappy marriage."<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lo USVII-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-CCVTU-6)</sup> Ebel and his brother Enman never married, leaving them open for political alliance should the need arise.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TPWormgod-7)</sup>

## History

### Background
Ebel was born right around [3E 380](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_380), and would've been around nine years of age by the time the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) began, and about nineteen by the time it concluded.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-A-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFC-8)</sup> According to the author of *[The Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md)*, Emperor Uriel VII's children were sent off to various prisons disguised as schools by the usurper [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Morrowind Real Barry-9)</sup>

### Unrest in Cyrodiil City
In the time following the Restoration of the rightful Emperor in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399) and on into [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), rumors swelled from wizards that Ebel and his brothers were actually [doppelgangers](Lore_Doppelganger.md) placed in the household by Jagar Tharn during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), leading to riots in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) calling for the removal of the false heirs, resulting in many deaths.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWRumors1-1)</sup> At the same time, there was some controversy in the city over the royal succession; while the eldest heir [Geldall](Lore_Geldall_Septim.md) was considered the Crown Prince, Prince Ebel and Prince Enman were the two "declared heirs" for the throne.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWRumors2-10)</sup> Ebel was "well-regarded" and seen as a viable candidate for the [Dragon Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Loranna-4)</sup>

### Conflict in Morrowind
When the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) were infighting with each other circa [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBRumors-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Loranna-4)</sup> an envoy from [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) was sent to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to treat with Ebel. When the envoy arrived, Ebel was practicing archery in a practice yard before two Imperial ladies: Marchioness Mnaeta of Thios Major and Princess Kolympa of Riyleel. They moved the meeting into the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md), and Ebel suggested that the Empire might support the Indoril side, should the Imperial holdings on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) be returned, as well as the creation of new Imperial holdings on the mainland.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Loranna-4)</sup>

### Assassination of the Heirs
Ebel was later murdered by [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) cultists at the age of 53, in a coordinated assassination of the Emperor and all his immediate heirs.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-A-2)</sup>
