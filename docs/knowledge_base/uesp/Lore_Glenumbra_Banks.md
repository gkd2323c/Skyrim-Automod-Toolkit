# Glenumbra Banks

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/ON-place-Glenumbra_Banks.png/200px-ON-place-Glenumbra_Banks.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Banks.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Glenumbra_Banks.png) Islets seen from the Glenumbra Banks The Glenumbra Banks are a series of shifting sand bars and narrow islets off the northwest coast of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). They are littered with rotting spars and twisted planks of shipwrecks, the remains of ships that attempted to cross the Banks' treacherous and ever-changing channels. Guiding ships wend merchant vessels through the channels and are well paid for the job.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Bt GB-1)</sup>

Even after calm, stormless nights, the sands shift and the Banks change. The morphous nature of the Banks cannot be attributed to the far-off [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) Current, nor the westward [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) Zephyr. An explanation, offered by a veteran navigator of the Banks, is that the legendary, immortal leviathan [Ithguleoir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithguleoir) causes the changes. Supposedly, the monster's oily back can be seen breaching the water's surface as he digs devious traps in the Banks.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Bt GB-1)</sup>

## Notes
- In *[The Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*, two fairly large landmasses can be viewed over the horizon of the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) near [Farwatch Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Farwatch_Tower) in [Glenumbra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Glenumbra).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Glenumbra-2)</sup> According to then-Lead Loremaster [Lawrence Schick](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Lawrence_Schick), this is part of the Glenumbra Banks.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-3)</sup> There used to be five more islands along the coast, but they were removed sometime around the 2017 game updates.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-4)</sup>
