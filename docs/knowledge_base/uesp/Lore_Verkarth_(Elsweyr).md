Verkarth was one of the original sixteen kingdoms of Elsweyr, known for their fierce warriors and soldiers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSK-2)</sup> In that time, those soldiers were known as the do-radhin.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rajhin-3)</sup>

## History
From the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) to the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Verkarth stood as one of the sixteen kingdoms of Elsweyr, known for their skilled and fierce warriors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSK-2)</sup> In the late years of the Merethic Era, the feudal hunt-lords of [Helkarn](Lore_Helkarn.md) and [Meirvale](Lore_Meirvale.md) were in constant conflict against each other, to the point where both drafted their peasants. Three years later, these peasant armies rebelled against their rulers, and inspired various others to rebel, causing commotion in other kingdoms, including Verkarth. The regal caste system across Elsweyr had been abolished.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Oot KMT-4)</sup> But like many other kingdoms, Verkarth fell into disrepair, following the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260). Verkarth was subsequently absorbed into the Kingdom of Anequina.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3E-5)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Verkarth Hills was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Ab'bil and it had a rivalry with Cori Darglade.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AR-1)</sup> Verkarth Hills was neighbored by several settlements, one of them being [Dune](Lore_Dune.md) to the north.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Map Arena-6)</sup>
