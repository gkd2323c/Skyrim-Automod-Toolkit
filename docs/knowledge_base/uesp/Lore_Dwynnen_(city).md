[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/DF-Location-14974-Automap.png/200px-DF-Location-14974-Automap.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-Location-14974-Automap.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-Location-14974-Automap.png) Map of Dwynnen Dwynnen is a walled city<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFDwynnen-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920V9-2)</sup> but its precise location is unclear.<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_location)</sup> Some sources suggest that the city is located inland in deep woodland country, near the border toward [Urvaius](Lore_Urvaius.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFDwynnen-1)</sup> but others place it on the Iliac Bay coast.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TR-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-2920V10-6)</sup> The land is typically verdant all year long, that is until the winter comes along and brings snowfall<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TR-5)</sup> and sleet.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-2920V10-6)</sup> Even though it is the barony's knightly order, the [Order of the Raven](Lore_Order_of_the_Raven.md) did not have a garrison in Dwynnen city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFDwynnen-1)</sup>

## History
When or where Dwynnen was founded is unclear,<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_location)</sup> though its appearance in the historical fiction, *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)* suggests it was active at this point. One of its most well-known residents was [Cassyr Whitley](Lore_Cassyr_Whitley.md), who is remembered for his service in the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) for [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md). In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), Lady [Corda](Lore_Corda.md), a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) initiate from [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) was brought across the Iliac Bay to Dwynnen, where people witnessed her procession before she left for the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920V9-2)</sup>

In [3E 253](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_253), a date largely agreed upon by archivists,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFOTU-8)</sup> the [Nightmare Host](Lore_Nightmare_Host.md), an [undead](Lore_Undead.md) army in service of the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) traveled north to the Iliac Bay and conquered Dwynnen.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-9)</sup> The [lich](Lore_Lich.md) that led them took residence in Castle Wightmoor and ruled over Dwynnen's people. According to legend, on the 5th of [Sun's Dawn](Lore_Sun%27s_Dawn.md), [Othrok](Lore_Othrok.md) emerged from the wilderness with an army of men and animals, blessed by the [gods](Lore_Divines.md), and defeated the Nightmare Host in the [Battle of Wightmoor](Lore_Battle_of_Wightmoor.md), thus liberating Dwynnen.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFOTU-8)</sup>

Since then, the barony expanded south to the Bay coast and went to all-out war with the Camoran Usurper. The invader's armies had finally reached the Iliac Bay in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267) and the [Battle of Firewaves](Lore_Battle_of_Firewaves.md) commenced.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFOTU-8)</sup> The capital of Dwynnen was directly affected in the battle. Refugees from as far south as southern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) traveled north and sought safety in the city. As the city was besieged, hazardous smoke and fire filled the streets, and the seaport was set ablaze. Othrok's guards and warriors searched the city for survivors and guided them to Castle Wightmoor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TR-5)</sup> Othrok and his allied regions were able to defeat the Camoran Usurper that year. Othrok's liberation of Dwynnen is celebrated on the anniversary as [Othroktide](Lore_Othroktide.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TFOTU-8)</sup>

By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Barony of Dwynnen hosted the [School of Julianos](Lore_School_of_Julianos.md). In [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), when the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Miracle_of_Peace.md) occurred, the Dwynnen region was annexed by the Kingdom of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), at the time ruled jointly by King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) and Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWITW-10)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_location) | Dwynnen's founding and location are both inconsistent with established lore. *[The Fall of the Usurper](Lore_The_Fall_of_the_Usurper.md)* suggests that the Dwynnen region did not extend south to the coast until after the Camoran Usurper's downfall, however both *[The Refugees](Lore_The_Refugees.md)* and *2920, The Last Year of the First Era* puts the city on the coast and accompany it with a port. By some, the latter 2920 series is highly regarded as a well-researched story<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FAD-11)</sup> but others like the Imperial historian, Mercator Mannix considered it largely fictional but it bears some seeds of truth.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AOTGS-12)</sup> |
