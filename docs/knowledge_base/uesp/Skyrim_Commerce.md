# Commerce


## Items
**Items** are carried in your inventory, and along with **gold** are the basis for commerce in Skyrim. For the purposes of commerce, the most important property of an item is its worth.

### Base Price
Every item has a **base price**, which is used to determine how much it is worth in transactions with [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md). Whenever a value for an item is shown on the site, it refers to the base price of that item.

### Price Adjustments
Merchants will never sell items to you below the base price, and will never buy items from you above the base price. They will buy and sell at a specific factor of the base price, determined by your [Speech](Skyrim_Speech.md) skill, relevant [Speech skill perks](Skyrim_Speech.md#Skill_Perks), and by effects from such as [Fortify Persuasion](Skyrim_Fortify_Persuasion.md) and [Fortify Barter](Skyrim_Fortify_Barter.md).

The starting baseline for selling items to merchants (the 'sell value') is approximately one-third the base value (≈ 33%), and for buying items from merchants, the 'buy cost' is approximately three times (≈ 300%) the base value. (See the [Speech skill article](Skyrim_Speech.md#Prices) for details.) Note that Speech Perks have a much larger effect on prices than the character's Speech skill, especially near the beginning of the game.

#### Non-Adjusted Prices
Several types of good or service transactions are fixed and not affected by price adjustments. These include:

- The price of [houses](Skyrim_Houses.md) and furniture
- The price of [skill training](Skyrim_Trainers.md)
- The rate for a room or bed at an inn
- The price at which certain quest givers will buy [firewood](Skyrim_Chop_Wood.md), [ore](Skyrim_Mine_Ore.md), and [crops](Skyrim_Gather_Wheat.md).

## Merchants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/SR-quest-Lisbet%27s_Missing_Shipment.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Lisbet%27s_Missing_Shipment.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Lisbet%27s_Missing_Shipment.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Lisbet%27s_Missing_Shipment.jpg) [Lisbet](Skyrim_Lisbet.md), a typical merchant **[Merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md)** are specialized [NPCs](Skyrim_NPCs.md) in Skyrim that buy and sell goods with you. There are different types of merchant, such as blacksmiths, apothecaries, or general goods, and each will only buy and sell certain types of goods, unless you have the [Merchant](Skyrim_Merchant_(perk).md) perk. All merchants will refuse to buy [stolen items](Skyrim_Crime.md#Stolen_Items), unless that merchant is a designated [fence](Skyrim_Fence_(merchant).md), or you have the [Fence](Skyrim_Fence_(perk).md) perk and have invested in that merchant. You are also unable to sell any item marked as a [quest item](Skyrim_Quest_Items.md) under any circumstances as long as that item is marked as a quest item.

### Merchants' Gold
Merchants in Skyrim have only a limited amount of **available gold** at any given time. Each merchant starts with a certain base amount of gold in their inventory. Most merchants, with you having no perks and not having invested in the merchant, will have about 750 gold to barter with. However, some merchants (typically stall vendors) will have a much smaller amount of gold, averaging about 25 gold. When you buy an item the merchant gains more gold, and when you sell an item to them they lose gold. Therefore, you can never gain more gold than was in the merchant's inventory to begin with. The perks [Investor](Skyrim_Investor.md) and [Master Trader](Skyrim_Master_Trader.md) can increase the base amount of gold merchants have to barter with, allowing for greater profits. Merchants' gold is periodically reset to its base amount along with their inventory after a certain amount of time has passed in-game.

- Generally, you will see merchants' gold refilled if you wait for two in-game days.
- Alternatively, most merchants' inventories will reset if you save or autosave, force them into combat, and reload.
