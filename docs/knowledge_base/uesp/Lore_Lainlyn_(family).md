# Lainlyn (family)

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg) Tava's Blessing [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/DF-map-Lainlyn.png/200px-DF-map-Lainlyn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Lainlyn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Lainlyn.png) The Barony of Lainlyn The Lainlyn family is a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) clan and the namesake of the eponymous [city-state](Lore_Lainlyn.md) and later [barony](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AUAL-1)</sup> While their political affiliation is never stated, the Lainlyn settlement is historically associated with the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AUADC1-2)</sup>

In his time, Jamshald Lainlyn was a talented artist and in [2E 102](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_102), he created the Ansei Shrine, a set of three statues that depict the [Anseis](Lore_Ansei.md); Halelah, Maja, and Radan, who sacrificed themselves to become the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md). It was commemorated to Ansei Maja, who was born in Tava's Blessing back in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AS-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EOTAW-4)</sup>

Around the time King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) took the throne in [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), Tava's Blessing's master shipwright was Curly Lainlyn. The King ordered the shipyards to build all vessels to a consistent standard, and so Curly Lainlyn wrote the book, *"[Strakes and Futtocks](Lore_Strakes_and_Futtocks.md)"* to detail the process for shipwrights.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SAF-5)</sup> By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Curly Lainlyn was replaced by Sulim.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSulim-6)</sup>
