| During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), a seated statue of the Night Mother stood inside the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md)'s [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) sanctuary. Five skulls were placed on her lap, representing her five murdered children.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Appears ESO-1)</sup> | The motif of her murdered five children is again represented in bas-reliefs found on the Black Doors of Dark Brotherhood sanctuaries in the Gold Coast and [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). Here it shows the Night Mother wielding a blade over the heads of her children, whom she later murdered in a dark ritual with [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) who is also represented by a large skull.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DBESO-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Izkul-3)</sup> <br> <br> During the Third and Fourth Eras, Dark Brotherhood sanctuary doors in Cyrodiil and Skyrim similarly depicted the Night Mother's murders.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DBOB-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DBSR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRDawnstar-6)</sup> | | |
| The statue in the center of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) was known as the Lucky Old Lady, but unbeknownst to the city folk it was actually a shrine to the Night Mother herself. It was here the Listener of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) would prays to the Night Mother each night, who would in turn inform the Listener about targets for assassination. Beneath the statue was the secret crypt of the Night Mother, where the bodily remains of her and her five children were interred. <br> <br> During the late- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) after the [Purification](Lore_Purification.md) of the [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) Sanctuary, the Night Mother summoned the Black Hand to her crypt where it was revealed [Mathieu Bellamont](Lore_Mathieu_Bellamont.md) was a traitor who sought to destroy the Brotherhood.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBHonor-7)</sup> On the 1st of Sun's Dusk in [4E 188](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_188), the statue was destroyed after Bravil erupted in violence over a war of control being Cyrodiil's two largest [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) traffickers. Listener [Alisanne Dupre](Lore_Alisanne_Dupre.md) attempted to guard the crypt of the Night Mother, but was burned alive and the crypt was raided.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cicero2-8)</sup> | [![OB-quest-Whispers of Death.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/7/7c/OB-quest-Whispers_of_Death.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Whispers_of_Death.jpg) Oblivion | | |

| [![ON-statue-Sithis Statue Xul-Thuxis.png](https://images.uesp.net/8/89/ON-statue-Sithis_Statue_Xul-Thuxis.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Sithis_Statue_Xul-Thuxis.png) ESO | [![LO-misc-Sithis Statue.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/4/4c/LO-misc-Sithis_Statue.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Sithis_Statue.jpg) ESO | [![OB-interior-Deepscorn Statue.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/5/59/OB-interior-Deepscorn_Statue.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Deepscorn_Statue.jpg) Oblivion |
| [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) are one of the only cultures in Tamriel to openly revere Sithis, and as a result, numerous forms of statuary depicting him can be found across ancient temples in Black Marsh.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Xode-9)</sup> One notable example was a vast statue of Sithis was built in the temple of Xul-Thuxis in Murkmire, before it was abandoned. The name of the temple translates to "Snake-Means-Death City" - an appropriate name for a temple dedicated to the Dread Father.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONloading Xul Thuxis-10)</sup> | Another large statue could be found as the centerpiece in the ancient Argonian temple of Xith-Izkul, located deep in the heart of Blackwood. The temple later became a sanctuary for the Dark Brotherhood before it [purified](Lore_Purification.md) by the Black Hand and abandoned.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONGhost-11)</sup> | A skeletal statue of Sithis with a gaping hole in his chest could be found in the ancient ruins of [Deepscorn Hollow](Lore_Deepscorn_Hollow.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the late- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Believed to have been a [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) temple devoted to the Dread Father, its subterranean halls eventually fell into ruin and sank into the boggy southern coast of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) and various dark cults were known to have taken shelter over the years, such as the [Unmakers](Lore_Unmakers.md)<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Thadarond-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOGreywyn-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> |
| [![ON-furnishing-Murkmire Shrine, Sithis Relief.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/f/f4/ON-furnishing-Murkmire_Shrine%2C_Sithis_Relief.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Murkmire_Shrine,_Sithis_Relief.jpg) ESO | [![ON-furnishing-Shrine, Sithis Looming Anointed.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/1/1e/ON-furnishing-Shrine%2C_Sithis_Looming_Anointed.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Shrine,_Sithis_Looming_Anointed.jpg) ESO | [![ON-statue-Murkmire Statue 10.png](https://images.uesp.net/7/71/ON-statue-Murkmire_Statue_10.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Murkmire_Statue_10.png) <br> ESO |
| Ancient Argonians often associated Sithis with serpent or skeletal imagery, and this could be seen in the numerous shrines and idols across Black Marsh.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Contra Sithis1-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Contra Sithis2-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Sithis Coiled-17)</sup> The likeness of Sithis was also inscribed on [brass](Lore_Brass.md) medallion carried by the [Shadowscales](Lore_Shadowscales.md) as a means to identify fellow members.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Contra Shadowscale Medallion-18)</sup> | | |
