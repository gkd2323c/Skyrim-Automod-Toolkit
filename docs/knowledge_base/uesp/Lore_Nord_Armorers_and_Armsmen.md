Nord Armorers and Armsmen *Notes on Nordic blacksmithing culture and techniques*
---
A [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [blacksmith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blacksmithing) feels a particular affinity with his anvil, bellows, hammer, and tongs. For the Nord, the creation of fine (if inelegant) [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) and armor is as important as proficiency with a blade, axe, or hammer. Such skills are learned from youth and are almost mandatory. As the Nord armorer and weaponsmith perfect their techniques, the forge becomes a second home. Close by is the tanning rack, where the hides of every beast of [the north](Lore_Skyrim.md) have been measured for their levels of durability and flexibility. Layered on top is [iron](Lore_Iron.md), [steel](Lore_Steel.md), and [corundum](Lore_Corundum.md) alloys. The result is a steel that holds tighter and bites sharper than weapons from other realms.

When Nords refer to their blades as "stinging," they mean more than its cutting power: superstitious Nord smiths are said to add a drop of wild [bee](Lore_Bee.md) [honey](Lore_Honey.md) into everything they create. The whys and wherefores are misplaced in long-forgotten lore, but the practice is widespread. To this day, no Nord armorer would work a forge without first crumbling [honeycomb](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Honeycomb) into his quenching tub.
