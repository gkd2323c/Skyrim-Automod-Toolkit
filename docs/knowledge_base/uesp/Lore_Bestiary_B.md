## [Baliwog](Lore_Baliwog.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/LG-cardart-Baliwog.png/200px-LG-cardart-Baliwog.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Baliwog.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Baliwog.png) A Baliwog **Baliwogs** (or **Wogs**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SB-2)</sup> are said to be the larval stage of the Grummite,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FFTM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LG-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Utter-Leyton-5)</sup> although this relationship has never been proven.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SILoading-6)</sup> They frequent the lakes, rivers and bogs of the Shivering Isles. They have four limbs, a powerful tail, razor-sharp teeth, and a set of long spines along their back.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SIB-7)</sup> They possess the uncanny ability to regenerate when immersed in water.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SB-2)</sup> They are less dangerous when young, but over the course of two years a Baliwog will increase in size, growing to be larger than a man in both length and weight,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FFTM-3)</sup> and developing a large dorsal fin on its back. The most dangerous Baliwogs are known to be venomous, although a bite from any Baliwog has the risk of transmitting [Swamp Fever](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Fever), a [disease](Lore_Disease.md) which they often carry. A single [pearl](Lore_Pearl.md) can sometimes be harvested from the body of a dead Baliwog.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SIB-7)</sup> Their meat is a common local dish for residents of the Shivering Isles, who often [smoke Baliwog legs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Smoked_Baliwog_Leg).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

Eventually, the adult Baliwog will feel the urge to seek out deep water and bury itself in the mud. It hibernates there for many months, gestating into a Grummite and emerging from the mud fully formed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FFTM-3)</sup> It is believed that Baliwogs can also grow up to be Scalons.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SIRumor-10)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SB-2)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Baliwog), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Baliwog)

## [Banekin](Lore_Banekin.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-creature-Banekin.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Banekin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Banekin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Banekin.jpg) A Banekin [Banekin](Lore_Banekin.md) are a small lesser [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) resembling a winged humanoid with mottled gray skin, a tail full of ridged spikes, pointed ears and horns on their heads. Banekin are associated with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and use powerful shock spells in combat. They can speak Tamrielic, and are sometimes used as [familiars](Lore_Familiar.md) by powerful wizards, though they are unreliable due to their forgetfulness, as well as their inclination for cruelty and destruction. Banekin are agile daedra. Aside from being able to use their claws and tails full of ridged spines, they can use spells to weaken their foes as well as make them nauseous.

They are not well treated by larger daedra, such as [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md), who torture loose banekin, and [Daedroths](Lore_Daedroth.md), who are known to eat them alive. There has been one documented case of a Dremora in [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md) keeping a banekin in a crib, akin to a baby.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Banekin), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Bantam Guar](Lore_Bantam_Guar.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/ON-pet-Bantam_Guar.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Bantam_Guar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Bantam_Guar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Bantam_Guar.jpg) A Bantam Guar Bantam Guar are small, docile creatures native to Morrowind, that are part of the [scuttler](Lore_Scuttler.md) family. They are also commonly called "ugly [chickens](Lore_Chicken.md)" due to their appearance and behavior, and like chickens, are raised for eggs and meat. Despite the name, they are not related to [guar](Lore_Guar.md). They are, however, related to [cliff racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md) and [cliff darters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cliff_Darter). Unlike their cousins, bantam guar cannot fly.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MFPO-17)</sup>

## Basilisk

Basilisk Powder is a type of explosive powder capable of poisoning those caught in its range,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOBHClass-22)</sup> though it is unknown if it is derived from or merely named after basilisks. ## [Bat](Lore_Bat.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-creature-Bat.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Bat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Bat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Bat.jpg) A bat [Bats](Lore_Bat.md) are flying mammals often found in caves and dungeons, traveling in groups. ## Bat Lizard
Bat lizards are said to be a species of flying creature encountered by [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). They were found on a forested island theorized to be [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md). They possessed leathered wings and grew to be the size of ships. While some think the creatures to have been [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), it is theorized that they are a now-extinct ancestor to the [cliff racer](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_Father_Of_The_Niben.md)

## [Bloodfiend](Lore_Bloodfiend.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-npc-Bloodfiend.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Bloodfiend.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Bloodfiend.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Bloodfiend.jpg) A bloodfiend [Bloodfiends](Lore_Bloodfiend.md) are feral [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md). The process of becoming a bloodfiend typically begins when a vampire starves, loses their sanity and turns feral. However, the term "bloodfiend" also refers to other vampiric abominations that do not experience an infection with an incubation period as most vampires do.

Feral vampires are vampires who have lost their minds over the years, until they eventually become deranged with hunger. A vampire can become a feral vampire when they are unable to feed, refuse to feed, or go for long without blood. The most notable example of a vampire that has gone feral in this manner is [Lord Lovidicus](Lore_Lovidicus.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/RG-creature-Bull.jpg/200px-RG-creature-Bull.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-creature-Bull.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-creature-Bull.jpg) A bull Bulls are male [cattle](Lore_Cattle.md), herbivorous animals often raised for meat. A bull that is raised as a beast of burden is known as an ox. In Tamrielic folklore traditions, a bull's ossified member is considered as a charm for good fortune and can be found across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ONPetrified-52)</sup> To the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), the symbol of a winged bull was considered to be important enough to placed in their tombs.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONBull Statue-53)</sup> The aforementioned symbol is also seen in *[the Adabal-a](Lore_The_Adabal-a.md)*, where [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) is referred to as a winged bull. Among the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), the [Bull-Heart Warriors](Lore_Bull-Heart_Warriors.md) use their skulls in rituals,<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ONBull Hat-54)</sup> while the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) considered the bull to be their emblem after beginning their alliance with the [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md) and embellished their belongings with bull symbolism.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ONDreadhorn-55)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Bull), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Bull)

## [Butterfly](Lore_Butterfly.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/SI-place-Door_to_Cyrodiil_02.jpg/200px-SI-place-Door_to_Cyrodiil_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Door_to_Cyrodiil_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Door_to_Cyrodiil_02.jpg) Butterflies come in many varieties [Butterflies](Lore_Butterfly.md) are diurnal insects with colorful wings. Their larvae are known as [caterpillars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caterpillar). Nets are sometimes used to catch them. Migratory monarch butterflies are a common sight across all of southern Tamriel, and are seen in the northern provinces during summer. In contrast to the [Ancestor Moth](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md), which represents the past, monarch butterflies are said to represent the souls of the present. Blue butterflies are common in Skyrim. Butterflies are often associated with [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), and serve as a sign of his influence. The buildings of his [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) are essentially sentient as they are actually made out of butterflies. [Pyandonean](Lore_Pyandonea.md) swallowtails once swept through and sung in the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md).
