# Bow of Shadows

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg) The Bow of Shadows The Bow of Shadows is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that, according to legend, was forged by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> The legendary ranger, Raerlas Ghile, was granted the Bow for a secret mission that failed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> The Bow was lost, though Raerlas is said to have used it to take scores of his foes down with him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> The Bow is said to grant the user invisibility and increased speed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup>

[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWYB-2)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-3)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Bow of Shadows among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TA-4)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Bow of Shadows and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWFR-5)</sup>

Many sightings of the Bow of Shadows have been reported; it is said that the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) assassin [Dram](Lore_Dram.md) once wielded it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> Dram likely used the Bow during the [Battle of Hunding Bay](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md), when he shot [Prince A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md) with the poisoned arrow that eventually killed him.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Dram also made use of the bow during the rebellion of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), led by the [Restless League](Lore_Restless_League.md), most notably in battle against [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

Near the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Bow came into the possession of a [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) named [Goris the Maggot King](Lore_Goris_the_Maggot_King.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) killed Goris and his accomplice Luven in the Venim Ancestral Tomb, a tomb in the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) which Goris used as a lair. The Nerevarine then retrieved the Bow.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Morrowind-8)</sup> Later that year, the Bow was sold to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md) and put on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tribunal-9)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the bow had ended up in the hands of a mysterious Dunmer assassin who sought to assassinate the Jarl of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md). The Jarl's steward caught wind of this plan, and enlisted the help of the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to investigate. The Dragonborn eventually uncovered the assassin's plan and killed the assassin at their hiding place in Dragonsreach, claiming the bow for themselves.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

## Notes
- The Bow of Shadows appeared in [*Morrowind*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind). Dram appeared wielding a bow in [*Redguard*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard), although it wasn't initially identified as the Bow of Shadows. It later appeared in *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)* with the [Bow of Shadows](Skyrim_Bow_of_Shadows.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md).
