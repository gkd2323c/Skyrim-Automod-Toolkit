Goris the Maggot King was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) operating on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He appeared to be brutish, being responsible for summoning many undead and sending them to extinguish his perceived adversaries to whom he simply owed payment.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> At some point he came into possession of the [Bow of Shadows](Lore_Bow_of_Shadows.md), an artifact of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) which has historically been in Dunmer hands; notably, the infamous assassin [Dram](Lore_Dram.md) once possessed the Bow.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup> Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) plundered Goris' base of operations in Venim Ancestral Tomb in pursuit of the Bow. It is believed Goris did not survive the encounter.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Morrowind-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## Notes
- In addition to his necromantic capabilities, Goris was versed in [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) magic as well.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Gam-6)</sup>
- He has a very similar title to fellow necromancer, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) the King of Worms.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MKOW-7)</sup>
