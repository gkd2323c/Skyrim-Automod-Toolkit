## Notes
- The randomly encountered treasure hunters in Skyrim will always spawn dead. However, they might spawn standing upright and then fall over when you approach close enough.
- The note that the Skyrim treasure hunters carry name the boss of a location. This can be a generic enemy, such as a [Draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md) or [bandit](Skyrim_Bandit.md), or a named enemy, like [Volsung](Skyrim_Volsung_(creature).md) or [Isobel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Isobel). If it is a named enemy, the note becomes grammatically incorrect, stating that the treasure in question is "guarded by a Volsung".
- It is possible to read the note while it is on the body of the treasure hunter to get the information on where the loot is. Picking up the note and reading it causes a miscellaneous quest to be stuck in your log; it will also turn the note into a quest item, which you can't remove from your inventory until you go to the location and get the item. This can also block getting "retrieval" quests from NPCs, as you can only have one of these types of quests active at a time. The notes also only reference enchanted weapons; the notes will never point towards unenchanted gear.
