Bonnie Spriggan Logbook **by [Captain One-Eye](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Captain_One-Eye)** *A logbook for a ship of the Lost Fleet from Coldharbour*
---
Scrounged up some paper and managed to make a ship's log. I don't rightly know what day it is and haven't for some time. No sense marking the days, but figure maintaining a record might keep me level.

We're about done cobbling together our ship. Decided the Bonnie Spriggan suits just fine as a name. Most of her salvaged pieces are from that wreck anyhow, after the Era burnt down. Should have left more to guard her, I suppose, but circumstances couldn't be helped.

Crew didn't much like that ship anyway, on account of it belonging to the admiral. Trying not to let the loss eat me up inside. Wood's not natural to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), so naming her the Bonnie Spriggan seems aspirational in a way.

* * *
Haven't even set sail, and we already found a stowaway. A wrinkled [soul-shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md) tucked up among our supply crates. Been living there for some time by the looks of it. Calls herself Argenta and she speaks like we've just woken her from a nightmare. Going on about some Wailing Prison, about needing to escape. Don't know if she can hold herself together enough to even take orders, but she's part of the crew. Any with aims to escape this wretched plane are welcome aboard the Bonnie Spriggan. All the better if you can pull your weight, but I've got a legion of Lost Fleet sailors behind me who can fill in for those that can't.

* * *
Found others seeking escape. Surprised at how many were absconded here or simply left behind. Even more surprised that they managed to survive this long. One, a strapping [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) called Khag, brought warning of some pirate king sent to retrieve the [Crown of Bones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown_of_Bones) from me.

That got a fire lit under us, I'll tell you. We made final repairs and cast off on the azure sea. Strange thing to sail on, but I've just about got a handle on it. The Spriggan's unwieldy, but she'll hold. I've got my shaman's tricks to make sure of it.

* * *
Turns out Argie, Argenta, can cook. Crew says the food's not bad, either. Argie gets a bit stabby with the still-wriggling ingredients we manage to fish up from the [plasm](Lore_Azure_Plasm.md), but we keep an eye on her. Didn't much think about how to feed a mortal crew, I'll admit. Glad we decided to keep her.

* * *
Keep picking up stragglers, mortals trapped in Coldharbour who became caught in its web one way or another. Not an honest-born pirate among the lot of them, but they're learning to sail all the same. Learning to outwit first and only fight as a last resort. That's how we stay alive.

* * *
Finally, this supposed pirate captain Khag warned us about. [Captain Velehk Sain](Lore_Velehk_Sain.md). Caught up to us at long last.

Lost a good number of our crew. Lost Fleet skeletons, mostly. But not all. Managed to outfox him with some [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) [magic](Lore_Magic.md) at a crucial moment. That made him mad.

Blame myself for this. For underestimating this captain hunting us. Lost him for now, but I can see he's sly enough and brutal in spades. He'll find his way back to us. [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) always do.

* * *
Came in a circle, back to our makeshift dockyard and scavenged up some poor sap called Kuxali. He'd put a crew together to come looking for me. Me! Got caught in the same whirlpool that led me to this [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) -damned fate. Well, his crew are all dead now. So that's one thing we have in common.

Fool says he looked up to me as a young hatchling. Not something I asked for, so not something I need or want to feel guilty about. But want and will sometimes pull against each other.

Been a miserable stretch as we try to keep ahead of Captain Sain, and I guess Kuxalis's lost his shine on me.

So there's another thing we have in common.

* * *
Another brush with Captain Sain. That Harvester of his managed to slither on the deck of the Spriggan on what we thought was a quiet night. Crewman Salvus rang the bell as warning, and it had some strange effect on her. Looked closer and found ancient runes engraved on the inside. Some sort of enchantment from the original owners among the Lost Fleet, I figure. Managed to drive her off in all her frenzy, and she only dragged two into the plasm. Thank Sithis for that.

When next our ships met, Khag made some daring maneuvers and managed to break aboard the Caterwaul to steal supplies. And found a [cat](Lore_Cat.md)! A cat aboard the Caterwaul. Damn his soft heart.

* * *
Turning the tide where Captain Sain is concerned. Losing fewer each brush with him. Din learning to steer and avoid hits from those wretched Teeth.

Cat turned out to be a sly little [Alfiq](Lore_Alfiq.md) named Suraj. Gets along well with everyone, even surly Murph, even better than Din. So that time, we even gained one for the two we lost.

* * *
Two among our crew came to see me independently about customs of captains performing [marriages](Lore_Marriage.md) at sea. Not a custom I'd heard of in my corner of Black Marsh, said I, but I'd do as they liked.

Still not sure if they were talking about each other, but I'll keep it to meself until they work it out.

* * *
Crew's stronger than ever. Battened down into some real pirates. But I don't know how long we can keep limping along like this. So few of our Lost Fleet remain after taking hits for our mortal crew. The hits we take after this feel like they'll count for more.

Been praying to Sithis to send something our way. Anything from a rogue wave to a bottomless plummet off a cliff would be welcome at this point.

Thought this log had more pages. Strange. Wonder where they went.
