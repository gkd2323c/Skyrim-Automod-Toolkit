— [Peakstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peakstar) House Dagoth (also called **Clan Dagoth**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> the **Sixth House**, **Shadow House**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MDGH-2)</sup> **Sleeping House**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dw S-3)</sup> **Lost House**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TN-4)</sup> **Traitor House**, **House of Lord Dagoth**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Generic Dialogue S-5)</sup> the **Tribe Unmourned**<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Peakstar-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Nibani-7)</sup> or "unmourned house")<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TLP-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-T7C-9)</sup> is the defunct sixth [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) led by [Voryn Dagoth](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), later known as Dagoth Ur. The lost clan's ancient homeland was swallowed up by what is now [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-10)</sup> being the only Great House to have their capital, [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md), located in the region.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FSo KW-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SM-12)</sup> In the [First Age](Lore_First_Era.md), House Dagoth betrayed the other Great Houses and allied with the Dwemer during the War of the First Council (though some sources claim they fought against the Dwemer), being branded a traitor house and was destroyed for their treason.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Generic Dialogue S-5)</sup> All records of the house's existence were destroyed following the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) and its members were either killed or absorbed into the remaining houses.

Unbeknownst to all, Lord Dagoth had survived and spent centuries slowly building his strength and influence from within [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). This culminated with Dagoth's attempt to re-create the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) god-construct [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) in order to overthrow the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) and conquer [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DUP-13)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Dagoth Ur was slain and his plans halted with the destruction of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MW-14)</sup>

## Early Years
House Dagoth was founded in ancient times, predating the [War of the First Council](Lore_The_War_of_the_First_Council.md). They were one of the founding clans of the Grand Council, who collectively control the politics and trade of Morrowind with the other Great Houses.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> Prior to the War, thirty of the most influential and revered Chimer Clans worked together to build the fabled Library of Andule, a grand repository containing the genealogical records related to the earliest [Velothi](Lore_Veloth.md) settlers and other assorted knowledge about the earliest history of the Great Houses.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bradyn-15)</sup> Three of the thirty Revered Families hailed from House Dagoth; the Favel, Ginith, and [Rethandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rethandus) Clans.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Dagoth Tombs-16)</sup> The locations of the tombs were presumed lost in the aftermath of the War of the First Council, along with the Library of Andule and the records contained within.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bradyn-15)</sup> The six tombs were rediscovered in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), alongside the other twenty-seven families and the Library of Andule itself.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Library Of Andule Quests-17)</sup>

## The War of the First Council
The first mentions of House Dagoth are recorded in connection to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) between the Chimer and the Dwemer. Imperial sources claim that House Dagoth stood with the Dwemer (and identifies both as Chimer clans).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Wot FC-18)</sup> However, others with more direct knowledge of the actual events, such as the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) tradition and that of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) depicts the war as first being prosecuted against the Dwemer by an alliance of all the Great Houses, including Dagoth. By this account, Voryn Dagoth had discovered that the Dwemer, whose king [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md) had been a close friend and ally of the Chimer Hortator [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Indoril_Nerevar.md), were secretly using the Heart of Lorkhan to build a [mechanical god](Lore_Numidium.md), which was an affront to the Chimer faith. After counseling with [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), who confirmed Dagoth's story, Nerevar came before Dumac to force the issue. Dumac, however, seemingly had no knowledge of this project undertaken by [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md) and his tonal architects and responded with hostility to Nerevar's accusations which resulted in war. Disputes over religion divided the Council, with House Dwemer and House Dagoth on one side and all the other Houses on the other. Dumac and Dagoth invited Orc and Nord clans as allies, and held northwest Morrowind, while Nerevar mustered the other Houses and nomad tribes and marched to meet the Dwemer-Dagoth-Westerner forces.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue N-19)</sup>

The specifics of what happened after the climactic [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) are lost to history. By the Ashlander account, Voryn Dagoth wished for the destruction of [Kagrenac's Tools](Lore_Kagrenac%27s_Tools.md), while Nerevar sought counsel with his advisers -- [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md). The three coveted the power of the Heart and so conspired to obtain the tools by any means, even going so far as to murder Nerevar because he would not go along with their plan. When the three came to Lord Dagoth without Nerevar he refused to hand over the tools, citing his oath to Nerevar to guard them, but was defeated and driven underneath Red Mountain, beneath even the Heart Chamber, and the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) took the tools. The version of the story told by Vivec privately to his priests is somewhat different. Nerevar returned to the mountain with his advisers after deciding that Kagrenac's Tools could be used for good, but Dagoth had already experimented with them and the Heart. He fought against the Tribunal, believing only he could be trusted with the tools. Though this version does not mention how Nerevar died, it is somewhat supported by Dagoth Ur himself who, in a message to the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) states that it was Nerevar himself who "struck me down."<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Na RM-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TBo RM-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MDU-22)</sup>

Since Dagoth Ur had disappeared, and the rest of his house had been soundly crushed in battle, Great House Dagoth sank into obscurity until the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Efforts to resurrect the House were made around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), but were seemingly unsuccessful.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOMW-23)</sup>

## The Re-awakening
In the year [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882), at the close of the Second Era, Dagoth Ur and his remaining subjects became active again within Red Mountain. Having somehow been kept alive by the power of the Heart, he had spent centuries dreaming and planning. When he arose, he took possession of the Heart Chamber and with unknown rituals, bound himself to the Heart, becoming a heartwight. He also made his nobles into the first of the [Ash Vampires](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Ash_Vampire), powerful beings who were also bound to the Heart, though not as strongly, who would serve as generals in his coming conquest. In his long sleep, he had adopted the goals of Kagrenac and set about finishing the Dwemer's ill-fated project, hoping to create a new god called [Akulakhan](Lore_Akulakhan.md) for whom his House would serve as the priesthood. In the long term, House Dagoth and its new, immortal masters would conquer all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and worship of Akulakhan would become its sole religion.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DUP-13)</sup>

The first blow was struck that same year when the [Almsivi](Lore_Almsivi.md) came to Red Mountain for the annual ritual to tap the Heart and renew their powers. Dagoth Ur and his minions ambushed them and drove them from the Heart Chamber. This would prove to be a pivotal event in the history of Morrowind, as the forces of the Temple would never again control the region around Red Mountain, nor would the Tribunal ever regain their full powers. With the initiative in his hands, Dagoth Ur set about expanding the number and influence of his House. To this end, he spread [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md), terrible ash storms spawned at the center of Red Mountain that carried with them the dreaded [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md) disease which twisted its victims into monsters.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Called the "Divine Disease" by the some of the Temple faithful, Corprus is in fact the effect of Dagoth Ur's power on mortal flesh. Those deemed unworthy to the cause it turns into mindless husks, but those with potential to serve it made into [Ash Creatures](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Creatures), beings whose bodies and minds are progressively warped by the alien designs of Dagoth Ur. These creatures would go on to promote the interests of the House throughout Vvardenfell, notably by influencing the dreams of some susceptible Dunmer. These "Sleepers" would go into a trance-like state and act as conduits for the thoughts and message of Dagoth Ur, and even act as assassins.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

The Sixth House demonstrated a tremendous amount of foresight, and constantly looked towards its long-term goals. While House Dagoth was ideologically expansionist, it was also very slow and methodical in its movements. In more than 400 years of conflict with the Tribunal, House Dagoth hardly expanded from Red Mountain—only to some ancient Dunmer [strongholds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Dunmer_Strongholds) and smugglers' [caves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Caves). This was largely due to Dagoth Ur's apprehension of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). He knew that a premature conflict would result in the inevitable defeat of his House, and so shunned open conflict, at least until Akulakhan could be completed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DUP-13)</sup>

## House Dagoth and the Tribunal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Ascended_Sleeper.jpg) An [Ascended Sleeper](Lore_Ascended_Sleeper.md) For the following four hundred years (roughly the first four hundred years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md)), Almsivi sent annual raiding parties of [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) and [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md) led by the Tribunal in an attempt to capture the Heart Chamber, but they were always unsuccessful and eventually suspended because of heavy losses to the Temple forces. Blight storms became progressively stronger and affected more and more of Vvardenfell, posing a dire threat to its people. In an attempt to contain the Blight, the Almsivi constructed the [Great Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md). Maintaining it required a significant amount of power which was increasingly in short supply, as the Tribunal had not had access to the Heart for centuries.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DUP-13)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Generic Dialogue G-26)</sup>

Rather than breach the Ghostfence directly, House Dagoth made use of smuggler bases and ships along the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) to spread its influence among the populace. With these, they exported idols called Ash Statues, which agents of the House sold and spread around populated areas. These statues seemingly contained some modicum of Dagoth Ur's power, and would corrupt those who were exposed to them consistently.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

In 3E 400, [Dagoth Uthol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Dagoth_Uthol) took control of the old Dunmer fortress of [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md) and created an underground tunnel leading under the Ghostfence, into Red Mountain, creating an additional avenue for forces and supplies to pass into Vvardenfell.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DUP-13)</sup>

Several years later in 3E 417, disaster struck the Tribunal when the artifacts Sunder and Keening were lost in an ill-fated expedition to Red Mountain by Almalexia and Sotha Sil. Only the intervention of Vivec saved his two companions, but the tools were lost. Dagoth Odros and Vemyn would hold Sunder and Keening in their respective Dwemer fortresses in the Ashlands for safekeeping.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> After this defeat, the Tribunal retired to their capitals and there were no more serious offensives against Dagoth's holdings until the coming of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DUP-13)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PDU-29)</sup>

Dagoth Ur, though he now held Sunder and Keening, lacked [Wraithguard](Lore_Wraithguard.md) to bind them all together. The best he could have done would be to study Sunder and Keening's enchantments and attempt to duplicate them himself. In 3E 426-7, there was a sudden spike in Sixth House cult activity, including the assassinations of several high-profile Imperial sympathizers as well as foreign Imperial citizens living in Vvardenfell.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DUP-13)</sup> This may have been intended as a challenge to the authority of the Tribunal, or an attempt to further sour relations between the Dunmer and their Imperial suzerain. It's not known whether this had any bearing on [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md)'s decision to send the would-be Nerevarine to Vvardenfell that same year. Misshapen monsters and diseases called 'blight disease' also continued spreading from Red Mountain, a volcano where the Devil Dagoth Ur and his ash vampires lived.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Generic Dialogue C-30)</sup>

## Culture
Due to the purge of records associated with House Dagoth, very little is known about its culture and character. One of the few extant documents describes them as "subtle" and having a penchant for diplomacy and subterfuge.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-T7C-9)</sup> This may go some way to explain Dagoth Ur's willingness to take up the failed project of his race's ancient enemy, as well as his seemingly conciliatory attitude towards the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MDU-22)</sup> The symbol of House Dagoth is the [Third-Eye](Lore_Third_Eye.md),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-31)</sup> on banners it resembles two beetles with marking representing three eyes on their backs.

The risen House Dagoth displayed a pronounced affinity for sound and [music](Lore_Music.md), though it's difficult to know if this was part of its makeup before its fall. Nearly every shrine encountered in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) featured a set of large bells or chimes, which seemingly played an important role in its rituals. This could be a reference to the source of Dagoth Ur's power, which utilised Dwemer tonal architecture to exploit the Heart of Lorkhan. Some of the more advanced forms of [ash creature](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Creatures) had long, perforated, trunk-like snouts which may have been used as musical instruments.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-32)</sup> The fictional epic *[Poison Song](Lore_Poison_Song.md)* depicts the house having a strong connection to music to the point that its descendants can somehow "hear" its songs still,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PS-33)</sup> though it's unclear what basis, if any, this has in reality. Recent excavations of certain Dwemeri sites have also produced artifacts bearing the clan markings of Dagoth, indicating that Chimer and Dwemer once mingled within the same communities.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-10)</sup>
