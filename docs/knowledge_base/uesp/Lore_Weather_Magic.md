# Weather Magic

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg) A summoned Hurricane Weather Magic (also known as **Storm Magic**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mt CM-1)</sup> and **Air Magic**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-STOD-2)</sup>) is magic that involves manipulating the weather. There are quite a few recorded instances of mortals altering the weather using magic.

Weather magic has never been an exact science because of the temperamental nature of what it seeks to control. Minor spells to conjure gusts of wind or forks of lightning are common, but manipulation of a region's climate is much more difficult to achieve. Maormer war wizards have longed for the ability to lower catastrophic hailstorms onto enemy borders as a preamble to invasion, or to halt a blizzard to make an unexpected march through inclement weather.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WW-3)</sup>

Although weather magic is sometimes attributed exclusively to certain races such as Maormer, everyone can learn how to manipulate weather.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Quartermaster-4)</sup>

## Argonian
On the western shore of the [Oliis Bay](Lore_Oliis_Bay.md) is an ancient calendar called the [Xinchei-Konu Monument](Lore_Xinchei-Konu_Monument.md). It is a device from the Days of Stone-Nest that manipulates the weather for the benefit of the people; at least, that is one of its many abilities. The [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) call it the "ku-vastei", which translates from [Jel](Lore_Jel.md) into the "catalyst of needed change". The tablets that make its calendar function depict each of the twelve months<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONVozei-5)</sup> and the components for the ritual are brackish mud, [deathly bloom](Lore_Deathly_Bloom.md), and wood from the Tree that Crumbles Stone.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONParaxeeh-6)</sup>

Pilgrims to [Hissmir](Lore_Hissmir.md) sometimes participate in a martial ritual known as the Trial of the Rains. Those who pass this trial are said to gain a certain level of control over the rain, allowing them to prevent flooding.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-7)</sup><sup>:11</sup>

## Ayleids
The Ayleids along the coast of their [empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) used an artifact called the Sphere of Storms, which controlled the weather and manipulated the waves for a multitude of reasons. They mostly used it to protect the shoreline, water the local crops, and speed the waves for transport. While most of them have long since been forgotten, one in the ruins of [Ilayas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ilayas_Ruins) remains and is protected by the Stormwardens, an order of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) lightning mages that protect the [Strident Coast](Lore_Strid_River.md) on [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONCirnean-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONMariel-9)</sup>

## Druids
The [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) possess the ability to manipulate the weather using [nature magic](Lore_Nature_Magic.md). The [Gloomlantern](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gloomlantern) is an example of this; created by the [Deep Druids](Lore_Deep_Druids.md), this artifact is infamous among pirates for its ability to shroud a ship in a thick veil of fog.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Ascendant Order](Lore_Ascendant_Order.md) used a druidic ritual to summon an unnatural storm targeting the three [Alliance](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) leaders as they approached [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md) for peace talks.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

## Dwemer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/TR-place-Bamz-Amschend%2C_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg/200px-TR-place-Bamz-Amschend%2C_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Bamz-Amschend,_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Bamz-Amschend,_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg) The Weather Witch in [Bamz-Amschend](Lore_Bamz-Amschend.md) The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) created machines called [Weather Witches](Lore_Weather_Witch.md) that are capable of manipulating the weather. They were created by the Dwemer at the height of their civilization.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Almalexia-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-FSv1-13)</sup>

## Enchantment
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-crown_store-Prismatic_Banner_Ribbon.jpeg/200px-ON-crown_store-Prismatic_Banner_Ribbon.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Prismatic_Banner_Ribbon.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Prismatic_Banner_Ribbon.jpeg) A Prismatic Banner Ribbon [Enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) items sometimes affect the weather. Examples include ribbons that create localized rainbows,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PBR-14)</sup> minor blizzard-creating mementos,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BG-15)</sup> and Clan [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) devices that generate artificial [aurora](Lore_Aurora.md) effects.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AAP-16)</sup>

## Harrowcraft
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d9/ON-concept-Harrowstorms.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Harrowstorms.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Harrowstorms.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Harrowstorms.jpg) A Harrowstorm [Harrowstorms](Lore_Harrowstorms.md) are arcane weather events created with a combination of the magic of the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) and ancient [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) that appeared without too much warning, leaving death and destruction behind. The energies created by those storms were capable of instantly killing many people or transforming them into near-mindless shells known as [harrowed](Lore_Harrowed.md), although others become [feral vampires](Lore_Bloodfiend.md) known as harrowfiends, but some who were in the vicinity of the storms were not always affected. The use of the ritual fetishes and alchemy needed to summon harrowstorms is known as **harrowcraft**.

## Kynareth
The [Ring of Kynareth](Lore_Ring_of_Kynareth.md) is an artifact that could cause rain to fall.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ROK-17)</sup> The clergy of Kynareth were known to be able to bless other items to provide similar results.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ff-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LFH-19)</sup>

## Maormer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-12.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-12.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-12.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-12.jpg) A man being sacrificed to power a Maormeri hurricane [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/ON-npc-Valaran_Stormcaller_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Valaran_Stormcaller_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Valaran_Stormcaller_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Valaran_Stormcaller_02.jpg) A Conduit stone and "sudden storm"The Maormer have many methods of using storm magic to obliterate their foes. One Stormreeve in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) was able to unleash powerful gusts of wind and create twisters that could electrocute enemies on contact.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SRN-20)</sup> The Maormer can whip up hurricanes with the intent to destroy entire fleets. They can do so with a ritual using serpent-shaped storm totems to siphon life energy from sacrifices marked with runes. Blood sacrifices such as these are only utilized when a summoner needs to rapidly accrue and release a lot of energy. The energy from the totems can be used to form powerful hurricanes,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOMastengwe-21)</sup> or it can be funneled into a storm atronach, which can only hold so much energy before it discorporates. Maormer mages can use wind energies to bind the creature until it performs its intended function. When it explodes, it releases a wave of energy that kills everything it touches, except for creatures with an affinity for storm energy, such as [Thunderbugs](Lore_Thunderbug.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOEalcil-22)</sup>

## Psijic Order
Members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) have been long-rumored to possess spells cast in the Old Way of magic that can bend the elements to the user's desire. There are recorded instances of sudden lightning and flash rain turning to small-scale blizzards along the coast of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md). Ancient Maormer scholars believed it possible that instructional texts on the usage of such spells exist.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WW-3)</sup> The Psijic Order also created the powerful storm that annihilated Orgnum's fleet in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110) during the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3-23)</sup>

## Reachfolk
The [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [Skyweaver Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skyweaver_Clan) is known for their weatherworking skills.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONDeanbroogha-24)</sup> The *Tale of the Sister Who Turned Her Skin Red*, a Reachfolk legend, mentions the witch Tancia, who wielded powerful Air Magic. According to the tale, she was capable of feats, such as conjuring a whirlwind powerful enough to move a cart, making the clan's hunters as light as air, letting them walk into the sky and back down as easily as if climbing or descending a set of stairs, and even boiling the air to such temperatures to roast people alive.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-STOD-2)</sup>

## Redguard
[Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) are known to be able to harness the powers of the air and sand associated with deserts. Magic wielded by [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) wizards is focused into reflection and purpose. Shifting sands and air are one of the key components not only of the desert, but of its natural magics.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-WAS-25)</sup> The [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) mage [Hassavir](Lore_Hassavir.md) was noted for his ability to manipulate Alik'r's desert winds and conjure storms on the hottest of days.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

## Sheogorath
It is known that [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) possesses a spell capable of changing the weather. However, this spell changes the weather according to his mood; the spell is unpredictable and it functions at Sheogorath's whim, no matter who casts it—meaning it functions entirely randomly. The [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) has previously taught the spell to mortals in his favor, allowing them to alter the climate of an entire region. There are stories of his followers trying to stymie flashfloods but summoning torrential rain instead, or trying to put out brush fires and feeding the flames with unwanted lightning storms, to Sheogorath's delight.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WW-3)</sup>

## Sotha Sil
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-sky-Celestiodrome_Day.jpg/200px-ON-sky-Celestiodrome_Day.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-sky-Celestiodrome_Day.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-sky-Celestiodrome_Day.jpg) The Celestiodrome during the day [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) created simplified simulations of the weather to emulate the climate of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) inside his [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). The systems and mechanisms employed to create these simulations are enormously complex.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-HOR-27)</sup>

The Clockwork City's celestiodrome is a glass sphere around the structure that functions as the realm's sky.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESOPVA-28)</sup> It is capable of mimicking the day–night cycle. Sotha Sil achieved the proper humidity needed for a breathable atmosphere for his realm<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-MPStars-29)</sup> with the creation of the Halls of Regulation, which maintain the City's water cycle, breathable air, humidity, temperature, and wind currents, as well as creating drinkable water.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TCCAG-30)</sup>

## Thu'um
[Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) can summon storms with their [shouts](Lore_Thu%27um.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mt CM-1)</sup> [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) Tongues could also wield the Thu'um, allowing them some mastery over the weather. Accounts of such abilities displayed by Tongues during the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) include the power to diminish fogs, mists, and clouds with the sheer bravado of their shouts. According to Negil's "Dragons at Windhelm", an army of the Tongues was capable of foiling an airborne sneak attack by Dragons who sought to strike under the cover of storm clouds.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WW-3)</sup> Another use of the Thu'um allows for the creation of powerful tornadoes.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup>

## Tsaesci
The [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) are believed to wield powerful arcane forces that can control the weather across vast regions. Numerous feats were accredited to the Tasaesci's magical aptitude. During [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md)'s failed invasion of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) in [2E 288](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_288), the Tsaesci altered the weather during the summer season and created the hot, dry wind that prevent rain from falling. This created a drought that caused the crops of their [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) enemies to fail and dried up an entire river.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DAI-32)</sup>

## Wyrd
[Wyrd](Lore_Wyrd.md) elemental plumes were used to invoke elements of wind and air into swirling vortexes.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-WEP-33)</sup>
