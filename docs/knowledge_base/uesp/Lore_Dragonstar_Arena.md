[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/ON-place-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg) Dragonstar Arena Dragonstar Arena (known in the past by many other names, including **Skyrim Arena** and **Valenwood Arena**) is an inter- [planar](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) arena complex devoted to [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md). Although the entrance is located in a fixed position in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), portals within transport combatants to all manner of environments before ultimately ending up within Boethiah's [realm](Lore_Realm_of_Boethia.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> The arena is an ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruin, but the entrance regularly changes location whenever competition in the area begins to falter.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TTA-2)</sup>

It is unknown how long the arena has been in existence for, although based on the small number of recorded champions it seems to have been established in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). To become a champion of the arena, teams needed to reach the tenth round and defeat the previous champion. The first champion was Faithful Adrasa, who was bested by Biiri the Beautiful when the complex was known as the Valenwood Arena. The arena entrance later moved from [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where it was dominated by the fourth champion, Holgstad the Horrible.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BCOC-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TTA-2)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) it moved again, from Skyrim to the Upper [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) region of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), high in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md). It was renamed the Dragonstar Arena, after the city of [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md) to the south. The arena's true nature was not made public, and combatants, spectators, and merchants all flocked to the ruins. Notable participants include [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) members, Nords from Skyrim, [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md) [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) [Dragonclaws](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonclaw_Clan), [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) slavers, [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md), and [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md). An [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) even entered into the arena, with plans to neutralize the region's strongest warriors so that the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) could retake Craglorn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> The Mages Guild team was enslaved after falling in battle, and forced to fight alongside [Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md). All manner of beasts were similarly employed in the arena, including undead [Anka-Ra](Lore_Anka-Ra.md) warriors and victims of [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md). Boethiah's [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) servants also took part, such as [Bloodwraith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodwraith_Clan) [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) and [Daedric Titans](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> The sixth champion of the arena, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named Hiath the Battlemaster,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BCOC-3)</sup> was defeated by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup>

Steel-dipped spines of the fallen competitors' corpses were often presented to next of kin as a consolation prize or sold on the black market.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DACP-4)</sup> Audience members often bought souvenirs such as gaudy drinking vessels bearing the symbols of the arena.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DAT-5)</sup> At some point the Dragonstar Arena stopped accepting tokens. As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Dragonstar Arena tokens were considered a collector's item.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DAT2-6)</sup>
