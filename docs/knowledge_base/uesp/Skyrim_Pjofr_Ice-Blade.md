[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/SR-npc-Stranger.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Stranger.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Stranger.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Stranger.jpg) The Stranger [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/SR-npc-Pjofr_Ice-Blade.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Pjofr_Ice-Blade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Pjofr_Ice-Blade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Pjofr_Ice-Blade.jpg) Pjofr Ice-Blade unmasked **Pjofr Ice-Blade** is one of the many people who have taken up the mantle of the legendary thief known as [the Gray Fox](Lore_The_Gray_Fox.md).

When first meeting him, his name is simply **Stranger**. After he gives the cowl to you, his name is revealed. This is a reference to the Gray Cowl's ability to magically conceal identities.

## Related Quests
- **[The Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Skyrim_The_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md)**<sup>[CC](Skyrim_The_Gray_Cowl_Returns!.md)</sup>: Investigate the legend of the Gray Fox.

## Notes
- His name is likely derived from the Old Norse word *þjófr*, meaning "thief', except the creator seems to have mistakenly identified the letter *þ* (known as "thorn" and pronounced as the "th" in "thorn") as a "p". A more correct spelling would thus have been *Thjofr*.
- Pjofr and his nephew will walk out of Riften and disappear from the game after passing the city gates once the quest is completed. - [![Nintendo Switch Logo.svg](https://images.uesp.net/5/5d/Nintendo_Switch_Logo.svg)](Skyrim_Switch.md) This does not appear to be the case in the Switch version, where Pjofr and his nephew will walk north to [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), then west all the way to [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), where they will then seemingly despawn.<sup>[*verification needed — is this the same on other platforms?*]</sup>
