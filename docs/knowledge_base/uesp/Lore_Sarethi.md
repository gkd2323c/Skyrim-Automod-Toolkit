# Sarethi

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/MW-place-Sarethi_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg/200px-MW-place-Sarethi_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Sarethi_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Sarethi_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) Sarethi Ancestral Tomb (Morrowind) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/MW-npc-Athyn_Sarethi.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Athyn_Sarethi.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Athyn_Sarethi.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Athyn_Sarethi.jpg) Councilor Athyn Sarethi The Sarethi family is a clan of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) from the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They are a noble family associated with [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) as both Councilor [Athyn Sarethi](Lore_Athyn_Sarethi.md), the so-called Hope of the Redoran, and his father served as councilors in the Redoran Council.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RB426-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-THOTR-2)</sup> The **Sarethi Ancestral Tomb** is located at the end of a small peninsula on the largest island of [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md), northwest of [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md) in northernmost [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWSarethi-3)</sup> **Sarethi Farm** is a family-owned property located in southeast [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), east of [Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRSarethi-4)</sup>

## General Information
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c9/SR-place-Sarethi_Farm.jpg/200px-SR-place-Sarethi_Farm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sarethi_Farm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sarethi_Farm.jpg) Sarethi Farm (Skyrim) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/MW-npc-Salyn_Sarethi.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Salyn_Sarethi.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Salyn_Sarethi.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Salyn_Sarethi.jpg) Salyn Sarethi In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Thiile Sarethi was a clothier who lived in [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONThiile-5)</sup> Adras Sarethi was a mage employed in the [Shad Astula](Lore_Shad_Astula.md) academy and maintained the school's collection of manuscripts and books.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONAdras-6)</sup> At some point in time, the tradition of maintaining an ancestral tomb fell out of favor in Morrowind, especially since the signing of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) and the then created policy to place ancestral remains to the [Great Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AATD-7)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the Sarethi Tomb was occupied by a coven of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) from the [Aundae](Lore_Aundae_Clan.md) bloodline.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWSarethi-3)</sup>

The Sarethi family held high positions in House Redoran. Sometime in the late Third Era, a Sarethi noble held the council seat but eventually passed away. His seat was highly contested between his son, Athyn, and his nephew, Andas Sarethi. Athyn had little ambition for the council seat, but Andas was a prodigy and brought up to succeed. He was initially known as the "Hope of the Redoran" after his mother chanted an omen during his difficult birth. He possessed high intelligence, strength, and extraordinary health, but developed an impertinence and often antagonized Athyn. One of their main grudges came when Andas sent Athyn to [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) to complete his training in his place. But in doing so, Athyn learned many fighting techniques with a club.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-THOTR-2)</sup>

When Athyn's father died, he challenged Andas for his council seat, and in an unexpected turn of events, Athyn defeated Andas and secured a position among the Redoran Council. Since then, the "Hope of the Redoran" moniker passed onto him, seeing as how, without Andas' ambitions for the council seat, Athyn never would have been where he was now.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-THOTR-2)</sup> Athyn at some point married Domesea Sarethi and had a child named Varvur. Like the rest of the Redoran Council, he moved to the city of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) on Vvardenfell.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWAthyn-8)</sup> There, his family lived in Sarethi Manor in Ald'ruhn-Under-Skar, and he reigned over the South Gash region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RB426-1)</sup>

Salyn Sarethi was a respected member of the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md)<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_House)</sup> and was stationed in [Ghostgate](Lore_Ghostgate.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWSalyn-9)</sup> He was considered one of the order's best warriors,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWFrald-10)</sup> so much so that he was gifted Enamor, a [glass](Lore_Malachite.md) dagger by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) himself. He was also a well-known poet, valuing honor and wit.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWSalyn-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWAllding-11)</sup> He condemned the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) for having no courtesy, honor, poetry, or wit. So much so that Knight-Protector Frald the White needed someone to challenge him in a battle of wits.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWCourtesy-12)</sup>

Avrusa Sarethi was an expert in botanical [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and intially lived in Vvardenfell in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). At a young age, she was sent to [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) to study under [Sinderion](Lore_Sinderion.md), a master alchemist known for his studies on [nirnroot](Lore_Nirnroot.md). Sometime later, she lived in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), where she ran an apothecary. However, due to the events of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), she and her sister, Aduri, were forced to leave. With nothing else, they traveled to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and lived on Sarethi Farm, a small, previously bought family estate in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md). While Arvusa was content living on the farm, Aduri was a free spirit.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SRAvrusa-13)</sup> She yearned to become a painter and explore the world.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRAduri-14)</sup>

Master Sadren Sarethi was a scholar who once conducted extensive research on [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) and the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) culture of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). He once posited a theory that [wispmothers](Lore_Wispmother.md) are actually an obscure form of [lichdom](Lore_Lich.md) created by a long-forgotten [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) culture. Thus suggesting that they were not [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), but come from a cult of powerful [sorceresses](Lore_Sorcerers.md) that achieved eternal life through undeath. He further theorized that the [wisps](Lore_Wisp.md) that surround them were merely emanations or [conjurations](Lore_Conjuration.md) of the wispmothers.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TW-15)</sup>
