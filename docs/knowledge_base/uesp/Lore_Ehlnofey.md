# Ehlnofey


The concept of the Ehlnofey is of paramount importance within the beliefs of the [elves](Lore_Mer.md), particularly the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), where the term is used to represent their cultural understanding of the Aedra as ancestor spirits. According to elven myth, the Ehlnofey are considered to be the Aedric spirits who stayed in Nirn after [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus) and the [Magna Ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge) departed in order to keep working on the new world so it'd remain viable. Among those spirits, some, typically referred to from that point on as "Earthbones", are thought to have followed the example of Y'ffre, giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law as the "bones of the earth", eternal laws of nature. While others, typically referred to from that point on as "Ehlnofey", are thought to have chosen not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, thus becoming the progenitors to mortal life, which arose from their lines and took on its current form due to a phenomenon of gradual diminishment, of each consecutive generation becoming weaker and more removed than its progenitors in stature and might.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mystery-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAA-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-5)</sup> This understanding forms the basis of the originally elvish term "Aedra" or "ancestor", denoting those spirits which they perceive as being part of their mythic genealogy, including figures such as [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), who most modern Altmer and Bosmer claim direct descent from, [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) the "greatest knight of the Ehlnofey", [Syrabane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syrabane), and [Phynaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phynaster).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-8)</sup> Thus elven religious iconography depicts the Ehlnofey as vaguely elven in shape, but featureless, reflecting the fading memory of the Ehlnofey.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EES-9)</sup>

The Aedra or Ehlnofey are thought to have remained within the world until they vanished.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Last-10)</sup> They are thought to primarily reside in [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Serana-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GU-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOVairabrian-13)</sup> having been bound to the Earth Bones<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AAD-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TOA-15)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup> as the cornerstones of the Mundus.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3Arena-16)</sup> The terms "spokes", "eight components of [chaos](Lore_Aurbis.md)", "lent bones of the Aedra", "Bones of the Wheel", "et'Ada-gears" and ["Eight gift-limbs"](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planets) have also been used to refer to them in this capacity, after having transformed into the framework of reality.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOV6-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LL-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TTIS-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MDC3-20)</sup> Some sources present a more negative view of the circumstances of these spirits and their residence in Aetherius, naming the Mundus as their "cemetery", calling them "spent ghosts", alleging them to have died as part of the creation of the world, having "died in pursuit of an impossible goal", or to have burial crypts.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Lyranth2-21)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GU-12)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ARLords Mail-22)</sup> Thus the Aedra are sometimes called the "dead gods" or the "mortal gods", sometimes disparagingly, owing to them having become bound to the laws of mortality.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Vastarie-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Prophet-24)</sup>

Different peoples harbor different understandings of Ehlnofey's relation to Aedra. In some accounts, both "Ehlnofey" and "Earth Bones" are described as referring exclusively to the early offspring of the Aedra rather than to the Aedra themselves, who were told to populate the world and live on through their children, leading to the gradual emergence of the various races of man and mer.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mystery-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-5)</sup> The concept of descent from the ancestor spirits is generally viewed as "elven conceit" by the religions of [Men](Lore_Men.md) who, with the exception of the [Redguards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Redguards), view themselves as creations of those spirits rather than their descendants.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAA-7)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TAX-25)</sup> *[The Annotated Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)* describes how the Aedra originated from the intermingling blood of [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) and [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay), and how the Ehlnofey come from a world shattered by Padomay before Anu and Padomay's second "long and furious" battle began, suggesting, per said account, the Ehlnofey may have a separate origin predating the Aedra.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAA-7)</sup>

Perhaps because of their association with the land itself, "Old Ehlnofey" became another name for [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), the mystical homeland of the elves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-8)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3All-26)</sup> The language or languages of the Ehlnofey became known as Ehlnofex.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> It has been found that the "Earth Bones", the laws of nature, can be manipulated to some extent.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MWBaladas-27)</sup>

For more information on the Aedra and their role in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s religions more broadly, see the entry on [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra).

## Origin
The exact nature of the Ehlnofey is a matter shrouded in much ambiguity; some sources describe them as great beings which once walked Nirn whose true origins none can truly say.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Last-10)</sup> There are two major origins that are presented: one that describes the Ehlnofey as survivors of a previous world that settled Nirn after its creation, and another claiming them to be Aedric spirits who were party to Nirn's construction and chose to remain in the world they'd crafted to stabilize it after the departure of Magnus and the Magna-Ge.

### Survivors of the Twelve Worlds
According to the [Anuad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuad), when Anu formed Nirn following the shattering of the Twelve Worlds by Padomay, two surviving races from what Padomay had sundered were brought to the new world alongside the fragments of their realms which had been used to form it, the Ehlnofey and the Hist.

A group of Ehlnofey, those that would eventually become the progenitors of the Mer, arrived in the new world together alongside a relatively intact fragment of their former home, and attempted to live on as they had before, in their land which would come to be known as Old Ehlnofey. Another group, those who would become the progenitors to Men, were scattered across the new world, and had to wander and find each other over time.

When the wandering Ehlnofey came across Old Ehlnofey, they were overjoyed to find their kin living among the splendor of ages past, but rather than welcoming them, the inhabitants of Old Ehlnofey viewed them as degenerates, fallen and corrupted from their experiences on Nirn. In the end and for whatever reason, war broke out between those of Old Ehlnofey, mightier but fewer, and those that wandered, weaker but more numerous and hardened by survival. This destructive war would reshape the face of Nirn, sinking much of the land beneath new oceans, destroying much of the original realm of the Hist and creating the continents of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Yokuda.](Lore_Yokuda.md)

The remains of Old Ehlnofey would become Tamriel while the wandering Ehlnofey would scatter to the newly formed continents. Eventually, over time and the passage of generations, the descendants of the two factions of Ehlnofey would gradually give rise to the various groups of Men and Mer. At some point prior to their disappearance, the Ehlnofey are said to have taken on "brilliant students". These students have been theorized to be the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), who were thus given the sobriquet "Dwarves" by their titanic masters. The events of this tutelage under the Ehlnofey are described in more detail in the full text of the Anuad myth.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-28)</sup>

While this struggle occurred, the "gods and demons" of the world—the Aedra, [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and Magna-Ge—would form out of Anu and Padomay's spilled blood, as distinct groups from the Ehlnofey and from each other.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAA-7)</sup>

### Aedric Progenitors
According to the Altmeri creation myth, when Magnus departed the Mundus, the et'Ada that took part in its creation broke into groups, most would follow the flight of the God of Magic to become the Magna-Ge, but some of those present chose instead to stay after he departed and keep working on the new world in order to keep it viable.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup>

Among those et'Ada who stayed, some would follow the example of Y'ffre, giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law, and would typically be referred to as "Earthbones" or "Earth Bones". Others, who would come to be known as Aedra, would choose not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, having to "make children just to last". This group of spirits would from that point typically be referred to as the "Ehlnofey".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-5)</sup> The offspring of the Aedra would exhibit a gradual change however, each consecutive generation was weaker than the one prior, more removed from their progenitors in stature and might.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

Through this generational phenomenon of diminishment, the offspring of the Ehlnofey would also differentiate. The Ehlnofey would eventually give away and the first [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) would arise, while the "weakest souls" would be formed by [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) into armies he named "Men".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

Altmeri myth holds that [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El) and Lorkhan and their respective followers would then war with one another, leading to the shattering of [Altmora](Lore_Atmora.md), the "Elder Wood" and one of the first kingdoms established by Auri-El alongside Old Ehlnofey, at Lorkhan's hands, and culminating in Lorkhan's defeat and the removal of his [Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan). During this conflict and prior to Lorkhan's defeat, Auri-El begged Anu to take them back, but Anu refused, as he'd already created something to replace them. The more merciful [Anui-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anui-El) created [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) and [Auriel's Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel%27s_Shield) to be used by Auri-El against Lorkhan's hordes.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup>

Following the defeat of Lorkhan, the Aedra would convene at the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) to decide the fate of the new world. Lorkhan's Heart would be removed and the laws and roles of all spirits in the new world would be determined. After this meeting, the Convention, the Aedra would depart the world in their excess for its own sake, as their direct presence rendered both it and the timeless continuity of existence itself unstable.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-NMI-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AU4TET-30)</sup>

At some point following the events of the war, the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) descendants of the Ehlnofey, described as the strongest of the lesser spirits that remained in the world after Convention, are said to have left their doomed and ruined continent of Aldmeris and colonized southwest Tamriel in the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3All-26)</sup> only to spend many centuries fruitlessly searching for their old homeland.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-FOTN-31)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-NMI-29)</sup>

## Legacy
*We are the echoes of old voices, remnants of a time long ago. Still, a few of us remain.
We were the Y'ffre. Then we became the Ehlnofey, the Earth Bones. … *
—Guardian of the Earth The fracturing of the Aldmer along cultural and racial lines is called the Sundering of Aldmeris, an event during which the Aldmer splintered into the groups that would become the Dwemer (Deep Ones), the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) (Changed Ones), the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) (Green or Forest Ones), the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) (Snow Ones), the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) (Sea or Tropical Ones) of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md), the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) (Elder or High Ones), and the [Sinistral Mer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lefthanded_Elves) (Left-handed Ones) of Yokuda. The progeny of the Wanderers (Wandering Ehlnofey) became the races of men, eventually splitting into many different racial groups, including the [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede), the [Atmorans](Lore_Nord.md) of Atmora, the [Yokudans](Lore_Redguard.md) of Yokuda, and possibly the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), and other aboriginal groups ([Proto-Bretons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Proto-Breton)).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAA-7)</sup>

Though the protean race is apparently gone, said by some sources to have faded and vanished after journeying to the lost corners of the world,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Last-10)</sup> the Ehlnofey are not entirely forgotten. They are thought to have taken on students before they disappeared, passing their knowledge on to emerging races such as the Dwemer, or the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), to whom they are said to have taught the Elder Way.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-28)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Peterson Interview-32)</sup> Knowledge of most individuals has been lost to time, but prominent members still appear in some form in the [pantheons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) of the various races. The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) are said to have preserved the Ehlnofey's Dawn Era magics and language to a greater extent than other Mer.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup> The theories of the Dwemer High Craftlord [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac) involved using sacred tones on the Heart of Lorkhan and bending the Earth Bones, commanding the "obedience" of the Ehlnofey.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MWBaladas-27)</sup> According to legend, the [Sun Birds of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun_Birds_of_Alinor), a mythical order of Aldmeri explorers from the Merethic Era, managed to pierce the veil between the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and Aetherius and journey to the Immortal Plane by using the raw magic of the Ehlnofey.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Furn Sunbird-33)</sup>

Nirn is said to be imbued with a wellspring of primordial energy which flows in a network via lines within the ground, described as the manner in which [Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids) visualize the Earthbones, the laws of nature, places where these "[bonelines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bonelines)" intersect are said to possess great power and have an effect on both magic and prayer performed there.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONSWC-34)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONPE-35)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-VGTYC-36)</sup> According to some sources, the reason Nirn is filled with natural caverns is that in primordial times they formed a network that functioned like nodes or capillaries for the divine essence of the Aedra that created it.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Wind-37)</sup>

The laws that govern Nirn and the Mundus and determine the role of all spirits within them, are believed to have been set in place at and through the Adamantine Tower by its creators, defining the roles and nature of the Earthbones within the confines of the "[Dragon's](Lore_Akatosh.md) timebound tale" and thus the framework of the natural world. The Adamantine Tower itself is described as a "sleek silver vessel", which became attached to the changing earth at one of the joint-points where the Earthbones on which the physical flesh on Nirn was hung met, with each such point radiating a "palpable reality".<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-38)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AU4TET-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-NMI-29)</sup>

According to some Daedric accounts, the Mundus is defined by a degree of malleability which generally doesn't exist within the realms of Oblivion. Whereas Oblivion realms are fundamentally shaped and curated by the will of their creators, the Mundus and its constituent realms submit simultaneously to the ordered influence of the Earthbones and the stubborn caprice of Lorkhan. Thus the Mundus is "doughy", things within it can be destroyed, but they never remain gone and instances of them inevitably spring up again.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Lyranth2-21)</sup>

The earliest martial discipline of Nirn was the "Prismatic Vector Dance of the Ehlnofey", which was recorded and studied by the Demiprince [Fa-Nuit-Hen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Maelstrom-39)</sup>

## Lingering Ehlnofey

### The "Old Bones"
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/ON-npc-Dringoth_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Dringoth_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dringoth_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dringoth_02.jpg) Dringoth, a supposed Ehlnofey possessing the skeletal corpse of a Bosmer (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-npc-Goradir_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Goradir_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Goradir_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Goradir_02.jpg) Resting place of Goradir, a supposed Ehlnofey (ESO) In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), what seemed to be lingering Ehlnofey spirits were still present in the Bone Orchard of southern [Grahtwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahtwood).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Dringoth-40)</sup> Originally a group of "Old Bones" that wandered the lands, the travels of these gigantic beings unintentionally caused untold suffering in the lands they'd travel through, making them decide to seek a true death by being completely forgotten since, for as long as they were remembered by the world, they could not truly die and leave it.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Olphras-41)</sup> The last of this group, Dringoth, would continue to wander by himself, and so became feared by the Bosmer as a terrible destroyer who would absentmindedly trample their cities and hills under his feet while wandering through, his approach felt through the ground quaking days in advance of his arrival. It is said homes burst and hills turned to slurry under his gait. The fearful Bosmer tricked and magically sealed Dringoth in slumber and, worried the restless remains of the other Old Bones might rise to wander again, brought them to the Bone Orchard, where the magic that ensnared Dringoth would imprison them as well.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Last-10)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Olphras-41)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Research-42)</sup> As the site became a graveyard and shrine to Y'ffre tended by the [Spinners](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spinners) , the Old Bones interred there could not be forgotten and so could not truly die. Some of the Orchard spirits would inhabit elven [skeletons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skeleton) to move around, while others preferred to stay resting in their own massive and monstrous appearing bones. The Bone Orchard itself was constructed around the "hill" that was Dringoth's skull, and which the great Barrowbough tree that sealed him was planted on.This group would help foil an attempt by the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult) to reanimate them into an army of giants by possessing the bones of the Bosmer buried in the Orchard and fighting alongside the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), who used the power of a piece of Dringoth's rib to control or destroy the cult's undead, thus preventing the threat their reanimation would have posed to Tamriel. During these events the Orchard Ehlnofey decided on whether to allow Dringoth to keep residing within the Bosmer skeleton he was possessing, or to destroy his vessel and forcibly bind his soul to his own bones so, with his body still sealed by the Barrowbough, he'd never wander again.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONKOB-43)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Olphras-41)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Dringoth-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Rib-44)</sup>

### The Guardians

They venerate the Breton interpretation of Y'ffre, Jephre.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-WCo NHR-45)</sup> They are guided by [elemental spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) known as the Guardians,<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOWI-46)</sup> which are among the extinct Ehlnofey who claim they were once Y'ffre.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ESOGE-47)</sup> The Guardians are said to have been present since the dawn of Tamriel,<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOWI-46)</sup> nurturing and protecting the land and serving as guides to the Wyrd. They are linked to the [Wyrd Tree](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wyrd_Tree), a nature spirit older than [Men](Lore_Men.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) that takes the form of a gargantuan oak tree that emits an unnatural glow.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-WCo NHR-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ESOWH-48)</sup> It gives life to the land and strength to the Wyrd, and conceals secrets from when time began, its roots stretching all across Nirn and touching everything, even the "bones of the world".<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-49)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOWJ-50)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ONWTV-51)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-CBSABS-52)</sup> The Guardians aided the Vestige in cleansing the corruption of [Angof the Gravesinger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Angof) from the Wyrd Tree, using their power to provide protection from the power of the elemental [wraiths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wraith) called forth by Angof, whose magic would have otherwise been fatal. They repudiated Angof and his [Bloodthorn Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodthorn_Cult) for their pacts with the Daedra, claiming that their deeds "mocked the Aedra, the Ehlnofey" and polluted the land with dark magic.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ESOGE-47)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESOGW-53)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOGA-54)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Corruption-55)</sup>

### Other
Some sources speak of sea-dwelling Ehlnofey with hearts made of [pearl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pearl) residing in old places of primordial energy, rivers and reefs where the Earthbones never quieted down.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONSWC-34)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONPE-35)</sup>

Some claim that the Dwemer and those who would become Bosmer learned how to defy natural laws, the Earthbones, by witnessing Ehlnofey control laws of nature, such as that governing decay or the [passage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Portals) between [realms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence). Through observing the passing of Y'ffre, who was the first of the Ehlnofey to give of themselves to the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) fully and become an Earthbone, Bosmer learned to defy the Earthbone he established to govern the physical form of things. It is said that this is the knowledge modern Bosmer invoke to enact a [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md), reverting to what life truly is beneath Y'ffre's imposed law. The reason for the seemingly undying nature of such Hunt creatures is said to be that in severing themselves from the control of one Earthbone, others also lose their power to impose their own laws on them, thus leading to the Earthbone governing lifespan also losing its power.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-5)</sup> Some sources claim that the Psijics were not the original practicioners of the Elder Way, but instead learned it from the original inhabitants of Nirn, the Ehlnofey.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Peterson Interview-32)</sup>

## Characteristics
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather.jpg) A Prismatic Sunbird Feather, said to channel the raw magic of the Ehlnofey [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Pearls_of_Ehlnofey.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Pearls_of_Ehlnofey.png) Pearls of Ehlnofey, an Aldmer amulet incorporating materials of alleged Ehlnofey origin According to descriptions by seeming Ehlnofey, beings like them can't die and depart from the world unless they are completely forgotten. Fearful that, because of this, the colossal "Old Bones"would rise to wander the lands again, and inflict untold suffering in their wake, the Bosmer used the magic of the Barrowbough to seal them into eternal slumber.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Olphras-41)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Dringoth-40)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Last-10)</sup>

Bones originally belonging to or possessed by the Ehlnofey are said to glow with great [Magicka](Lore_Magic.md). Despite appearances, such remains have no traces of [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy), and even exhibit a degree of resistance to necromantic interference, making it impossible for necromancers to dispel Ehlnofey possession of bodies.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Research-42)</sup> Legends surrounding the Bone Orchard describe the beings interred there as having had bodies "made entirely of bone", even while they still wandered the world prior to their confinement by the Bosmer. Indeed the "Old Bones" themselves describe their physical forms during their travels as composed of bone, or as having always been skeletal in nature as far back as they can remember. This might mean being comprised of bone is simply an aspect of their physical form as opposed to their bodies having changed to that state.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Last-10)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Olphras-41)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Dringoth-40)</sup> Other sources describe aquatic Ehlnofey with hearts made of pearl residing in bodies of water filled with primordial energy.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONPE-35)</sup>

Ehlnofey spirits have exhibited the ability to possess and reanimate other bodies, and to return and bind souls to their original vessels. Fragments of bone from their bodies possess a power to control or destroy the undead.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONKOB-43)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Olphras-41)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Dringoth-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Rib-44)</sup> It is said that the Ehlnofey possess the power to control natural laws such as those governing decay or the passage between realms, and that the Dwemer learned how to defy the hold of such natural laws, the Earthbones, through observing the Ehlnofey.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-5)</sup>

The moods of the Ehlnofey appear to have an effect on the earth around them. In the Bone Orchard, whenever an Ehlnofey residing within its own bones speaks in anger, the ground will quake in response, and the Old Bone Goradir claimed that his mirth upon learning that the Bosmer body Dringoth was possessing had been taken apart shifted the earth of his barrow. The way into the Heart of the Barrowbough could only be opened by Nirenan, one of the Old Bones, who could be invoked through the use of a horn crafted from her own massive tooth.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONKOB-43)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Goradir-56)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Dringoth-40)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Amariel-57)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Horn-58)</sup>

The ancient Aldmer incorporated magically potent [coral](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coral), pearl, and all manner of watery stones in their arcane talismans. Such materials can only be found in places of wild and primordial magic where the Earthbones never quieted down and are collectively termed as "Pearls of the Ehlnofey". Pearls from the Nilumin River in particular are known as "Tears of the Ehlnofey" and were sought by Aldmer divers, who considered them valuable in both healing and enhancing physical and magical ability, incorporating them in both talismans and [alchemy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy). Legend ties the origin of such materials to sea-dwelling Ehlnofey with hearts of pearl, it is theorized that they have a special connection to physical laws, granting them real Aurbic power, and that the Ehlnofey themselves favor them. Whatever the case, Aldmeri talismans incorporating these "Pearls of Ehlnofey" are said to be far more powerful than their modern successors crafted by modern Altmer.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONPE-35)</sup> Myth claims that Aldmer explorers, the Sunbirds of Alinor, also used such raw Ehlnofic magic to pierce the veil between the Mundus and Aetherius and journey to the Immortal Plane.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Furn Sunbird-33)</sup>

While slumbering, Ehlnofey are said to be able to dream; the Spinners overseeing the Bone Orchard would tell the Old Bones stories of the Bosmer whose bodies were also buried there to give them something new to dream about.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Amariel-57)</sup>

Some sources describe the Altmer as having the "blood of Ehlnofey", also called the "Old Blood", which is believed to be valuable in [Blood Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blood_Magic).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Gharakul-59)</sup>

## Behavior and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-npc-Olphras_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Olphras_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Olphras_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Olphras_02.jpg) The remains of Olphras, who acted as leader of the supposed Ehlnofey at the Bone Orchard [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/ON-quest-Keeper_of_Bones.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Keeper_of_Bones.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Keeper_of_Bones.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Keeper_of_Bones.jpg) The "Old Bones", supposed Ehlnofey, possessing Bosmer skeletons to stand in judgment of Dringoth The "Old Bones" consider each other distant "family" due to so few of them still remaining within the world.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Dringoth-40)</sup> They refer to others as "mortals" and, of those encountered, most view their inability to die as long as they're remembered in a negative manner, describing it as "the doom of endlessness". All but one of these beings are said to have journeyed far to remote places of the world where they could be fully forgotten and so could die, prior to becoming trapped as a result of Dringoth's actions.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Olphras-41)</sup> This comports with Bosmer myth, which claims the Ehlnofey generally faded and vanished after journeying to the lost corners of the world.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Last-10)</sup>

The language of the Ehlnofey, [Ehlnofex](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ehlnofex_Languages), has been described as a dangerous "many-headed" language with characters that constantly distort as one looks at them.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Thief-60)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-NMI-29)</sup> The Ayleids are said to have preserved the Dawn Era magics and runes of the Ehlnofey to a greater extent than other elves, suggesting comparatively greater similarity in their magical tradition.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup>

## Cultural Significance
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/LO-people-Jephre.jpg/200px-LO-people-Jephre.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Jephre.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Jephre.jpg) Jephre, as depicted by the Altmer Altmer view themselves as "true children of the et'Ada", descendants of the Aldmer and the Ehlnofey, the Aedra or "Divines", that preceded them, in an "unbroken line" dating back to Nirn's creation.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Oghma-61)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-62)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Rejection-63)</sup> This belief was central to the ancestor worship of the Aldmer that eventually gave way to the Altmeri pantheon when the religion shifted from the worship of one's own ancestors to the worship of the ancestors of one's "betters", the most influential in their society.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-8)</sup> Most Altmer and Bosmer claim direct descent from Auri-El himself.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup> Altmer also count deities like [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), [Syrabane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syrabane) and [Phynaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phynaster) among their ancestors.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-8)</sup> Due to this perceived origin, the Altmer believe that within each of them is housed the "divine spark", and thus they've an obligation to keep an Oghma, an "everscriven scroll" in which they memorialize the events of their lives in honor of their divine ancestors.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Oghma-61)</sup> The Altmer use the eagle both in association with Auri-El,<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Cont Driftwood-64)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Cont Winged Auriel-65)</sup> and as a symbol representing the Aedra, their ancestors, who descended from Aetherius and became trapped in physical form by the creation of Nirn, as a whole.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-ESOKB-66)</sup> This belief is also reflected in their stylistic motifs in which circles, arcs, and tendrils, abound, symbolizing the elven ancestors, the Aedra, being constrained by the creation of the Mundus.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Ancient Elf-67)</sup> Altmeri prayers reference Auri-El as the Fixed Center of existence who always envelops all people and invoke him to grant his followers stability.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-COTF-68)</sup> Their prayers often emphasize their believed ancestral connection with the Aedra, urging the faithful to give praise to the Divines and thus to their own ancestral lines, and describing the Altmer as always having the gods in their lives. It is said that the body of the divine wills the Altmer into being, and that they are one with the gods, their ancestors, and so should venerate them as one.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Praise-69)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Mirnor-70)</sup> Owing to their perceived divine lineage, the Altmer believe it is possible for them to achieve apotheosis under certain conditions, and so they hope to one day rejoin their divine ancestors in the stars.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOVairabrian-13)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TAX-25)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-misc-Wild_Hunt_Transform_Cropped.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Wild_Hunt_Transform_Cropped.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Wild_Hunt_Transform_Cropped.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Wild_Hunt_Transform_Cropped.jpg) A Wild Hunt transformation (ESO) Bosmer are known to worship Y'ffre. A vital part of the Bosmeri pantheon, Y'ffre's Ehlnofey, or "Earthbones," were created from his corporeal form to establish safety and the laws of nature. These laws took the shape of stories, and there is great debate between tribes competing to interpret Y'ffre's ways and knowledge of the times of chaos, before Y'ffre arrived on the mortal plane.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-GTEV-71)</sup> Some legends claim that the Boiche Elves, originating from the Earthbones that followed Jephre, grew the first great [Graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) the out of the Perchance Acorn, which would become [Tower of Green-Sap](Lore_The_Towers.md#Green-Sap). Due to the nature of the Acorn being, perchance, elsewhere, many different Green-Saps sprung in many different forms, forming the walking Graht-Oak forests of the Valenwood. Each one told its own stories of the Green, the forest home of the "Boiche-become-Bosmer".<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AU4TET-30)</sup> Some of the Bosmeri [Spinners](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spinners) are connected to the Earthbones, although the exact nature of this connection is unknown.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-LES-72)</sup>

Altmer and Bosmer myth describes how during the Dawn Era, life and even the land itself were formless and constantly shifted between shapes until Y'ffre's Naming gave all things in Nirn an enduring form.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TOAF-73)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ONWild Hunt Crate-74)</sup> This event, the establishment of shape, is considered a core element of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). In certain circumstances, such as a Wild Hunt, which is enacted in accordance with what the Pact allows, creatures can be made to "forget their Y'ffre taught shapes", causing the bonds of the Naming to unravel, and return to this original formless state.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ONWild Hunt Crate-74)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-ONWild Hunt Horse-75)</sup> Within Bosmer belief, Y'ffre created day, night, and the places between through the manner in which he interpreted the laws of time established by [Anui-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anui-El) and applied them to Nirn. He is also believed to be responsible for the movements of the stars, which dance and sway to his compelling song.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-GESOD-76)</sup> [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith also acknowledges Y'ffre as the deity responsible for the movements of the stars.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Jephre-77)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-WCo NHR-45)</sup>

Ancient Aldmer were known to utilize magic associated with Ehlnofey in their arcane craftsmanship.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONPE-35)</sup> According to the legend of the [Viridian Sentinel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Viridian_Sentinel), long ago the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) forged a covenant with the Earth Bones to protect their settlements from the wild. The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), who displaced the Direnni, had no such arrangement, and as a result, their farms in [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai) were gradually overrun by the wilderness. The people were pushed to the brink of starvation, causing a young boy [Greenward](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenward) to pray to [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) for salvation. Stendarr taught him to forge a new pact with the forest, giving him the power to restrain the natural creatures within. He thus became the first Viridian Sentinel.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TVS-78)</sup>

[Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md) honor a diverse array of [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirits), incarnations of nature birthed from [the Green](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green), the lifeforce of Nirn's natural world, created in the Dawn Era by the sacrifice of Y'ffre and the Earthbones.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Frii-79)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Ooze-80)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Gauntlets-81)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Audrine-82)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Laurel-83)</sup> The spirits honored by the Reachfolk can have distinct characteristics, such as the spirits associated with springs, trees or sacred animals.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-GSOTER2-84)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Malug-85)</sup> Their [shamans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachman_Shamans) occasionally utilize calming spirits that may inhabit their [staves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staves).<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-SOTSW-86)</sup> Expressing gratitude to nature spirits, they make it a practice to thank them before felling trees and replace them by planting new ones.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Malug-85)</sup>

Y'ffre and the Earthbones play a central role in the beliefs of both [Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids) and [Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wyrd).<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-Wyrd-87)</sup>.The Druids commune with the voices of Y'ffre and the Earthbones for guidance and tap into their power through their magic.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Audrine-82)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Legacy-88)</sup> The Wyresses believe that during Y'ffre's Naming, the act which gave all life on Nirn its shape,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ESOWHC-89)</sup> the deity tasked the Earthbones to choose those who would become the guardians of nature,<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Wyresses-90)</sup> a task ultimately given to the Wyresses, the Name-Daughters, who are descendants of the Ehlnofey themselves.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-WCo NHR-45)</sup> Like the Druids, the Wyresses draw upon the power of the Earthbones through their magic.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Galerion Veil-91)</sup>

The "Old Ways" of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) are said to combine [mysticism](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism), the Eleven Forces and the divine laws that define reality into a philosophy and way of life which boasts a deep understanding of the Ehlnofey, from whose lines the races of Man and Mer arose.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mystery-4)</sup> Indeed, some sources claim that the Psijics were originally taught the "Old Ways" by the Ehlnofey themselves.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Peterson Interview-32)</sup>

According to the [Order of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), mortal [souls](Lore_Souls.md) are the Aedric essence at the core of every mortal's existence.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-TDS-92)</sup> Souls have also been described as the piece of every mortal which comes from Aetherius, the realm of pure light and magic, and a "tiny [star"](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars) which flares into a ["sun"](Lore_Sun.md) when magic is invoked.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-LOS-93)</sup><sup>:52</sup>

The existence and nature of the spirits involved in the creation of the Mundus has been subject of commentary by some of the Daedra, though even among them various views exist. The [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) [Lyranth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyranth) described the Mundus as the "cemetery" of the Aedra, claiming that their original hopes for their mortal descendants, and their world, were ultimately not achieved due to the mortal's foolishness. Nonetheless, though the creators of the Mundus are said to have "died in pursuit of an impossible goal", they are praised for their immense conviction and admired for having wrought order out of chaos, a "brutal coercion" which even Daedra must deem impressive. By contrast, Magnus and the Magna-Ge are viewed as cowards, no other spirits being as low, who fled when things turned dire, thus making it impossible to ever know what could've been achieved had they chosen to stay and keep helping to the end.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Lyranth2-21)</sup> Contrasting Lyranth's view, the [Mazken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mazken) information broker Madam Whim claimed that no living being existed which could explain the true relationship between the Aedra and the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), and that no information she'd ever come across in her long life indicated the Aedra were anything more than a story mortals told themselves to feel safer in their beds at night.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Whim-94)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg) Tzirzhalir - The war between the Ehlnofey described in *[The Annotated Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)* bears many striking parallels with the war between Lorkhan and [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel) described in *[The Monomyth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Monomyth)*, and the texts are likely summarizing the same events from different historiographical perspectives.
- The Ehlnofex war has been described by some sources as being the same event as [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) told in a different manner.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-5)</sup>
- [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) has been described as the *"greatest knight of the Ehlnofey, champion of the Dragon of Time"* in certain [Cyrodiilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) folktales.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-LOS-93)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Whim-94)</sup>
- The common practice of Dunmer and Altmer to dress in a manner resembling that of the ancient Aldmer, or that of other cultures that resemble the Aldmeri style, such as the ancient Ayleids and Chimer, elves of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), which is often viewed by the Mer as their golden age, is called "draping Ehlnofic". Bosmer, who like [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) prefer to live in the "Aurbic Now", are said to generally not share the practice.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Ancient Elf-67)</sup>
- The "Old Bones" being ancient gargantuan creatures of bone bares some resemblance to another gigantic skeletal being that existed since ancient times known as [Tzirzhalir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tzirzhalir).<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Tzirzhalir-95)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-KRN-96)</sup> A similar looking creature to Tzirhalir, called Mzrelnir, was found in the Lucent Citadel in [Fargrave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fargrave).<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-LC-97)</sup> Some similarity also existed between the names of these beings, and those of some of the "Old Bones" such as Goradir or Fildenir.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-ONGoradir-98)</sup><sup>[[95]](#cite_note-ONFildenir-99)</sup>
