[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/SR-place-Sea_of_Ghosts.jpg/200px-SR-place-Sea_of_Ghosts.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sea_of_Ghosts.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sea_of_Ghosts.jpg) The Sea of Ghosts along the northern coast of Skyrim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/MER-art-Arena_Anthology_Game_Map.jpg/200px-MER-art-Arena_Anthology_Game_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Arena_Anthology_Game_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Arena_Anthology_Game_Map.jpg) The Sea of Ghosts is found on Tamriel's north coast. The Sea of Ghosts (also known as the **Ghost Sea**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRLoading-1)</sup> or the **Northern Sea**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> is the body of water that consists of the [northern coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northern_Coast) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Gt V-3)</sup> the eastern coast of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Gener G-4)</sup> and the southern coast of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3EOL-5)</sup> It is a part of the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md), which consists of the north and east seas of Tamriel. The Sea of Ghosts is not easy to navigate. It took some time for the people of Tamriel to explore these misty waters, which are often riddled with glaciers and icebergs.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1ESR-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3ESR-7)</sup> The Sea of Ghosts acquired its name from the ancient tale of Skyrim, known as *[Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol_and_the_Sea-Ghosts)* which refers to the death of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s eldest son, [Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol), at the hands of the "*Sea-Ghosts"*.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Yat SG-8)</sup> The Sea of Ghosts host its own variety of animals that are not commonly found in other parts of the continent, specifically [horkers](Lore_Horker.md) and [chub loons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chub_Loon).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mot CL-9)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg/200px-SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) Yngol Barrow (Skyrim) In the mid- [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorers sailed across Tamriel, charting the continent's waterways and sea lanes. The most well-known was [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md), who explored all of Tamriel's coast, but most notably the [Niben River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_River).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FOTN-10)</sup> Aldmeri explorers mapped the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), building several wizards towers along the coast, specifically in [Ald Redaynia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ald_Redaynia), [Bal Fell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bal_Fell), [Tel Aruhn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tel_Aruhn), and [Tel Mora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tel_Mora). But these towers were eventually abandoned as the High Velothi Culture, and the early [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) began to establish the [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) and the [Ashlander Tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-11)</sup>

Migration of Men from Atmora to Skyrim occurred over the centuries, with the founding of several settlements the largest of which was [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md). In the late Merethic Ega, Ysgramor himself was one such settler alongside his two sons fleeing Civil War in Atmora. However the [Snow Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Elves) of Skyrim destroyed Saarthal in the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-11)</sup> leaving only Ysgramor and his sons alive, who came to Atmora and gathered the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) to return to Skyrim to wreak vengeance against the Elves. Ysgramor the Harbinger and the Atmorans sailed across the Sea of Ghosts from the port city of [Jylkurfyk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jylkurfyk) and landed on [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md). However, their fleet was caught in the Storm of Separation and the Harbinger's eldest son, Yngol, was killed in the raging storm in the Atmoran Strait.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOFallowstone-12)</sup> In legend, it is said that Yngol was claimed by the *"Sea-Ghosts"*.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Yat SG-8)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Sot RV2-13)</sup> Yngol's body was recovered by Ysgramor and buried in a traditional Atmoran barrow on the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) estuary, where it meets the Sea of Ghosts. Ysgramor looked to the south and decided to have a great city built near the estuary. This city of kings, [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), was made to have [Yngol Barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol_Barrow) viewable from the castle.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Sot RV19-14)</sup> As time went on, more [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) cities were built along the coastline, such as [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), and [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md).

The cities of [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) and Solitude maintained a summer route, with no waypoint between each location. That was when the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) entrepreneur, [Yric Flowdys](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yric_Flowdys) discovered shores for an ideal harbor at the base of Dore Elard in the 9th century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). The deep waters were able to accommodate large merchant ships, and on the northernmost point, Flowdys built the town of Northpoint. As time went on, Yric Flowdys adopted the name of the mountain as his family name, [House Dorell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dorell). They later attained the title of Barony from Empress [Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra) of the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), and became a prominent power in the Kingdom of [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire). They have extensive ties with the Court of Solitude.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-NAA-15)</sup>

An army of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and [lycanthropes](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) known as the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) once occupied a castle on the remote island of [Grayhome](Lore_Grayhome.md) in the Sea of Ghosts. The island was left abandoned following the defeat of the Gray Host circa [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BSHR-17)</sup>

There have been two instances where the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) of the eponymous [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) have invaded Tamriel, both of which arrived on the continent's shores the same way. The [First Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) landed on the north coast of both Skyrim and Morrowind, cutting a bloody swathe all the way to the [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md) in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). By [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), General [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) defeated the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) and established the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lot D-18)</sup> In case of another threat, Reman I built strongholds across Skyrim's north coast as his bulwark.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Ia C-19)</sup> In any case, the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) rarely took place in Skyrim's coast. This invasion led by [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal) traveled across the Sea of Ghosts, bypassing [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), the [Telvanni Peninsula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telvanni_Peninsula), and [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), only to land on the shores of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). Here, Dir-Kamal's forces sacked the city, killing the High Queen of East Skyrim, [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md), and her shortly-lived successor, [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md). From there, his armies traveled southeast into the region of [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls) in Morrowind. As history knows it, the Akaviri were defeated and gave rise to the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TSAI-20)</sup>

In [3E 271](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_271), Emperor [Uriel Septim V](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_V) explored the Sea of Ghosts and conquered the island of [Roscrea](Lore_Roscrea.md), as of his several campaigns to expand the Empire.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BHot Ev3-21)</sup> Roscrea is found off the coast of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) and it was converted into an Imperial fiefdom. The history of the territory is unknown, however, it was later claimed by the Kingdom of Solitude in the late years of the Third Era, in [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), to further expand their power. After marrying the Queen [Macalla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Macalla) of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), King [Thian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thian) held a majority of control of Skyrim's northern coast. Under his reign, the island had been used to further chart the sea's mysterious waters.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3ESR-7)</sup>

## Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/BM-npc-Reaver_Group.jpg/200px-BM-npc-Reaver_Group.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-npc-Reaver_Group.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-npc-Reaver_Group.jpg) A reaver raiding party on Solstheim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg) A Sea Giant longship The people who inhabit the Sea of Ghosts are known as out-islanders. They spend most of their time at sea, journeying from the far north on their ships to raid the coasts of northern Tamriel. The Nords refer to these raiders as [reavers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaver). They generally target one-hut settlements and hunting camps, dropping off small raiding parties rather than beaching their ships for fear that their vessels would be stolen or burned.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Raiders are known to make base on remote frozen islands in the Sea of Ghosts; for example, the Frostbite Raiders once inhabited an icy island off the [Frozen Coast](Lore_Frozen_Coast.md) of northeastern [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

In addition to reavers and other raiders, the Sea of Ghosts is home to the elusive [Sea Giants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Giant), a species of [giant](Lore_Giant.md) rarely seen on Tamriel. They allegedly only make landfall during the coldest winters and never stay for long. They slaughter and pillage, then vanish into the ice and fog.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOOgvar-24)</sup> Sightings of their longships have been reported over the centuries, but generally sailors who witness the Sea Giants do not live to tell the tale.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LTTO-25)</sup> Sea Giants sometimes come into conflict with raiders and invade their territory.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> They are also known to interbreed with the people of Tamriel, producing offspring known as [Half-Giants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Half-Giant) who accompany them on their raids.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

The Sea of Ghosts was once home to the infamous [Vampire Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord) [Falgravn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Falgravn). Said to have once been a pirate, Falgravn traveled the Sea of Ghosts since the reign of King [Ysmir](Lore_Wulfharth.md), disappearing for hundreds of years at a time before being sighted again.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOOgvar-24)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Lord Falgravn mounted an invasion of [Kyne's Aegis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kyne%27s_Aegis) with an army of vampires and Sea Giants, but he was killed by the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) and the island liberated.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TFFKA-28)</sup>

For the peoples of northern Tamriel, diving naked into the Sea of Ghosts is a rite of passage.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> The Nords of Windhelm participate in the Snow Bear Plunge, a [New Life](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival) tradition involving such dives.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

## High Rock
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/ON-place-Northpoint_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Northpoint_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Northpoint_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Northpoint_02.jpg) Northpoint (ESO) At some point, the Sea of Ghosts meets the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) to the west, as the town of [Eagle's Brook](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eagle%27s_Brook) is considered [Glenumbra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenumbra)'s gateway to the Eltheric Ocean.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-GTa C-31)</sup> The coast of [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire) and [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) are at least considered part of the Sea of Ghosts. The port-cities of [Fharun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fharun), [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md), and [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) are the largest settlements on the northern coast. Although, there are some small harbors across the coast, such as the [Morkul Stronghold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morkul_Stronghold), home to the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) clan of smiths of the same name. Formerly, the once great city of [Erokii](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Erokii) was found on the shores, hidden from the rest of Rivenspire by the [Kurallian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurallian_Mountains). It is now known as The Doomcrag. From the east to the west, the Sea of Ghosts becomes more hospitable as glaciers are less common. Where the regions of Rivenspire and Wrothgar meet on the mainland is the [Great Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Bay), a water basin with several shipwrecks and stones jutting out. Further northeast into the frozen north, traversing is much more difficult. The Frozen Fleet is a ship graveyard held together in an icy wasteland. Wrothgar's coast is notably populated by the chub loon, mysterious flightless birds that came from Atmora.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mot CL-9)</sup> In the seas surrounding Rivenspire and [Glenumbra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenumbra) is a notorious creature known as the Ithguleoir, a leviathan that preys on the people of the coast.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Ithguleoir-32)</sup> The royal [House Dorell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dorell) are the ancestral rulers of Northpoint and are known for their maritime endeavors. They founded the settlement as a waypoint on the summer route between [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) and Solitude. The shores around the city are notably deep, which made the location all the more favorable for a checkpoint.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-NAA-15)</sup>

## Morrowind
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/MW-place-Khuul.jpg/200px-MW-place-Khuul.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Khuul.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Khuul.jpg) Khuul (Morrowind) Morrowind's coast is shared between the Great Houses of [Redoran](Lore_Redoran.md) and [Telvanni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telvanni). The cities of [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md) and [Firewatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Firewatch) are found on the coast, as well as many minor settlements. These include [Ald Velothi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ald_Velothi), [Dagon Fel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagon_Fel), [Gnisis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnisis), and [Khuul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khuul). The [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) empties into the Sea of Ghosts, separating the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) from the rest of mainland Morrowind. It is believed that the First Era eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), known as [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md), caused the Inner Sea to form and separate the region.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Ro KZ-33)</sup> [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) mythology indicates that the Inner Sea was created by the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), the Brass-God of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-36Lo Vs36-34)</sup> Bypassing Firewatch leads into the rest of the Padomaic Ocean, all the way to distant [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). The coast of Vvardenfell is ridged and volcanic, and both the [Foyada Bani-Dad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Foyada_Bani-Dad) and the [Valley of the Wind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valley_of_the_Wind) empty into the Sea of Ghosts. The archipelago of [Sheogorad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheogorad) is found off the coast of the Black Isle. These scattered islands surround the large island of Sheogorad, from which the archipelago gets its name. These islands have only one known settlement, the town of [Dagon Fel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagon_Fel). The remote fishing village of [Ald Redaynia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ald_Redaynia) no longer exists, but it was found on the westernmost point of the Sheogorad region, on the second-largest island. There is an uninhabited island between Sheogorad and the coast, west of the ancient stronghold of [Rotheran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rotheran) known as [Shuran Island](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shuran_Island). The villages along the Morrowind coast are typically built on wooden foundations and are often controlled by House Redoran. Morrowind's eastern coast is also bounded by the Sea of Ghosts,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Gener G-4)</sup> containing the rugged [northeast archipelago](Lore_Northeast_Archipelago.md) and lush island of [Gorne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorne), with the [Telvanni Peninsula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telvanni_Peninsula) bordering it further south.

## Skyrim
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/SR-place-Solitude_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Solitude_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Solitude_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Solitude_02.jpg) The Great Arch of Solitude Skyrim's northern coast consists of the cities of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), and [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), as well as their respective holds ([The Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), and [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md)). Each of them are prominent cities that are often utilized by the East Empire Trading Company. The latter two, Solitude and Windhelm are built where two great rivers empty into the Sea of Ghosts. The [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md) runs across Haafingar as well as [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), while the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) starts from [Lake Ilinalta](Lore_Lake_Ilinalta.md) in southern Skyrim and descends northeast into Eastmarch. However, the city of Winterhold is not a port city. It is built atop of the [Broken Cape](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Broken_Cape), where it overlooks the entire sea from the highest bluffs. A large chunk of the city fell into the sea after the Great Collapse.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Ot GC-35)</sup> Beyond the cities, there have been little to no settlements on the coast of Skyrim. The only known example is the small town of [Dunbarrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dunbarrow).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-CDJP1-36)</sup> It is not uncommon to find wrecked ships across the northern coast. The most infamous case of this is the [Pilgrim's Trench](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pilgrim%27s_Trench), a graveyard of ships off the coast of Winterhold. Islands off the coast are sparsely populated, much less so than in Morrowind, but there are still people, nonetheless. The [Volkihar Clan](Lore_Volkihar.md) of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) live in an ancient castle on an island north of Haafingar. East of Winterhold is Serpentstone Isle, which contains the eponymous Serpent Stone, one of the [Standing Stones](Lore_Standing_Stones.md) of Skyrim. The Sea of Ghosts transitions into the region of [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md) through the [Drajkmyr Marsh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drajkmyr_Marsh), which takes a large portion of Hjaalmarch and ends toward the towns of [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) and [Fort Snowhawk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Snowhawk). Along the coast can be found various ruined forts built during the time of Emperor [Reman I](Lore_Reman.md) of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). As a defense measure for another invasion from [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), he built several strongholds across Skyrim's northern coast.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Ia C-19)</sup> Further from the coast, the [Merringar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Merringar) is a rare fish that is hunted in schools for food. [Dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh) are also found here and are attracted to the Merringar's blood.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TRKR-37)</sup>

## Islands
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg/200px-Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Solstheim_map_Bloodmoon.jpg) Map of Solstheim (ca. 3E 427) - [Blackbone Isle](Lore_Blackbone_Isle.md): A haunted island that houses the treasure and wealth amassed by Cyrus the Restless
- [Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md): A small island between Windhelm and Solstheim far from major trading routes
- [Castle Volkihar](Lore_Castle_Volkihar.md): An island north of the border between Skyrim and High Rock, the ancient home of the Volkihar Clan
- [The Chill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Chill): A glacial island north of Winterhold, with an ice cave that serves as the city's jail
- [Giant's Tooth](Lore_Giant%27s_Tooth.md): An island inhabited by Giants northeast of Winterhold that contains the last remaining Ironwood trees in Skyrim
- [Gorne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorne): A lush island off the coast of Necrom that is home to several exotic species
- [Grayhome](Lore_Grayhome.md): An island far to the north of Northpoint, formerly the home of the Gray Host
- [Icereach](Lore_Icereach.md): An island north of Haafingar, home to the Icereach witch coven
- [Kyne's Aegis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kyne%27s_Aegis): A medium-sized island northwest of Solitude, where many ships pass before reaching the city
- [Northeast Archipelago](Lore_Northeast_Archipelago.md): A sparsely populated, rugged archipelago located northeast of Morrowind
- [Olenveld](Lore_Olenveld.md): An island-city roughly a summer's day sail away from Winterhold that was abandoned in the Second Era
- [Rockjoint Island](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Japhet%27s_Folly): A frozen island northeast of Winterhold, considered inhospitable for a permanent settlement
- [Roscrea](Lore_Roscrea.md): An island fiefdom somewhere north of Solitude that was originally conquered by Uriel V
- [Serpentstone Isle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Serpentstone_Isle): An icy island northeast of Winterhold, named after the Serpent Stone at its peak
- [Sheogorad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheogorad): A rocky archipelago off the north coast of Vvardenfell that is mostly uninhabited, save for Dagon Fel
