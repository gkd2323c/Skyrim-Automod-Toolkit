## Neb-Crescen
[Neb-Crescen](Lore_Neb-Crescen.md) is a cursed sword created by [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) that causes its wielder to be filled with reckless abandon, compelling them to kill everyone in a fit of rage when they hold it. The *[16 Accords of Madness](Lore_16_Accords_of_Madness.md)* describes it as being a seductive wide "arc of honed steel" that is intricately decorated, such as being adorned with jewels, having a beautifully decorated handle, and even having a practicality in its elaborate pommel, which is designed to balance its considerably hefty weight. It is most known for being used against [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) to trick him into murdering his own son.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_16_Accords_of_Madness.md)

## Necromancer's Amulet
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg) The Necromancer's Amulet The [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md) (also known as the **Necromancer Amulet**) is a legendary [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md). In appearance it is an exquisite amulet, often with a [jade](Lore_Jade.md) skull emblazoned upon it, or sometimes carved into the shape of a tiny skull with glowing eyes. It grants the wearer magical protection equivalent to plate armor, as well as the ability to regenerate from injury, resist mundane weapons, and absorb magicka and life force. The wearer also becomes wise beyond his years and far more capable in the School of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md). This comes at a price, as the amulet can drain the wearer's strength, endurance, health and stamina. The artifact is popular among both thieves and mages. The amulet is unstable in this world, and is forever doomed to fade in and out of existence, reappearing at locations distant from that of its disappearance.

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Necromancer%27s_Amulet), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Necromancer%27s_Amulet), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Necromancer%27s_Amulet), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Necromancer%27s_Amulet), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Necromancer_Amulet.md), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Necromancer%27s_Amulet)

## Nerveshatter
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/SR-item-Nerveshatter.jpg/200px-SR-item-Nerveshatter.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Nerveshatter.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Nerveshatter.jpg) Nerveshatter [Nerveshatter](Lore_Nerveshatter.md) is a warhammer associated with the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) Lady [Syl](Lore_Syl.md), Duchess of [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md). It is made from [madness ore](Lore_Madness_Ore.md) and jagged shards of [amber](Lore_Amber.md), and is enchanted to deal shock damage to those it strikes.

When threatened, the eternally paranoid Lady Syl would retreat to the ruined tunnels of Xirethard that run beneath [New Sheoth](Lore_New_Sheoth.md) and would equip herself with [Dark Seducer](Lore_Dark_Seducer.md) armor and Nerveshatter. She claimed to have defeated many while wielding the hammer. Syl was killed by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) during the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), although it is unknown if she defected to the [Forces of Order](Lore_Forces_of_Order.md) or if her death was part of the Ritual of Accession. In any case, Nerveshatter was claimed by the Hero.

**Found in:** - [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Nerveshatter), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Nerveshatter.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Saints_%26_Seducers.md)</sup>, [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Syl,_Duchess_of_Dementia)

## Nettlebane
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/SR-item-Nettlebane.jpg/200px-SR-item-Nettlebane.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Nettlebane.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Nettlebane.jpg) Nettlebane The [Nettlebane](Lore_Nettlebane.md) is a crudely-engraved [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) dagger that was crafted by [Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), built with the intention to harm the natural world and thus perform sacrifices on [Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md). It has the ability to damage ancient flora older than metal, such as the [Eldergleam](Lore_Eldergleam.md), one of the oldest living things in Skyrim.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Nettlebane.md)

## Nightwielder's Tome‎‎
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/ON-item-Nightwielder%27s_Tome.jpg/200px-ON-item-Nightwielder%27s_Tome.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Nightwielder%27s_Tome.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Nightwielder%27s_Tome.jpg) Nightwielder's Tome‎‎ The [Nightwielder's Tome](Lore_Nightwielder%27s_Tome.md) is a book written by [Azra Nightwielder](Lore_Azra_Nightwielder.md) in runes that cannot be read by mortal eyes. Attempting to study the contents of the freezing pages will cause your skin to crawl and your vision to worsen. It was theorized that Azra managed to write the tome by being able to see with more than just his eyes.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Blackwood_Quest_Items#Nightwielder.27s_Tome)

## Nuzava's Anvil
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-item-Nuzava%27s_Anvil_%28museum%29.jpg/200px-ON-item-Nuzava%27s_Anvil_%28museum%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Nuzava%27s_Anvil_(museum).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Nuzava%27s_Anvil_(museum).jpg) Nuzava's Anvil [Nuzava's Anvil](Lore_Nuzava%27s_Anvil_(item).md) was a remarkable travel anvil that belonged to the legendary [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) smith, Forge-Wife [Nuzava](Lore_Nuzava.md) of the [Morkul Clan](Lore_Morkul_Clan.md). It was said that the anvil was adorned with intricate carvings that some suspected to be arcane runes, although Nuzava denied this claim.

Despite her insistence that her skills were enough, rumors persisted that any smith who tried to use the anvil would suffer accidents, such as broken hammers, shattered metal, and smashed fingers. The truth behind these claims remains uncertain, but the anvil's reputation was well known among the Orcs.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:House_of_Orsimer_Glories#Nuzava.27s_Anvil)
