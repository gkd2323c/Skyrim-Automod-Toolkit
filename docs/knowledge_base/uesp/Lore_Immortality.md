**Immortality** refers to an individual’s ability to live indefinitely, free from aging or natural death. While many beings in the Elder Scrolls universe may possess this trait, they can still be killed under certain circumstances. This form of immortality pertains to biological longevity, where the individual does not age or succumb to disease, but is not immune to harm or destruction. Most beings on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), however, are mortal and subject to the natural cycle of life and death.

## Mortals
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg/200px-BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg) A Scroll of Revival [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/LG-cardart-Yagrum_Bagarn_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Yagrum_Bagarn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Yagrum_Bagarn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Yagrum_Bagarn_02.jpg) Yagrum Bagarn, made immortal by the Corprus disease There are many ways for mortals to avoid death, although usually at a price. Most forms of [undead](Lore_Undead.md) are simple revenants, such as mindless [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md) or [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md). Restless spirits often remain bound to Tamriel as [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md) or [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md).

The most powerful mages have been known to live far beyond a normal mortal lifespan.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> The most famous examples are the Wizard-Lords of [Great House Telvanni](Lore_Great_House_Telvanni.md), who have been known to live for centuries.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) were able to extend their lives through the continued use of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md); the hearts power also allowed [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) to resurrect himself from the dead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBORM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Nibani-4)</sup> Necromancers often attempt to use their knowledge to cheat death, although only the most powerful of necromancers manage to ascend to [lichdom](Lore_Lich.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> which often becomes a curse in very short order for those who succeed.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vastarie-6)</sup> Another way mortals avoid a natural death is contracting vampirism. [Lord Harkon](Lore_Harkon.md) sacrificed a thousand innocents in Molag Bal's name so he would be given the "gift".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRHarkon-7)</sup>

The [corprus disease](Lore_Corprus.md) gives those afflicted with it immortality, but it also destroys the victims minds, and deforms their bodies. Only two people have ever really benefited from this type of immortality without losing their minds to the disease. The last living Dwemer [Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), who was grotesquely deformed by the disease, but had the benefit of having most of his insanity caused by Corprus removed do to treatments used by Divayth Fyr, allowing for him to remain lucid, and the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) who was partially cured of corprus disease, while also retaining all the positive effects of the disease.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Yagrum-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Divayth-9)</sup>

During the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) kept the Nord population enthralled and obedient by means of a widespread [dragon-worshipping cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md), and many people of this era became the undead [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) that still prowl the crypts of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), Atmora, and anywhere else the dragon priests "kept peace between dragons and men".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDW-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ATD-11)</sup>

Some mortals have been known to achieve a form of life beyond death by having their soul replaced by a Daedric [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CCTAP-12)</sup> Molag Bal preserves mortals who have been sacrificed to him in such a manner.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CCTAP-12)</sup> Additionally, he has often been known to bring mortals back from the dead.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> On rare occasions, mortals have become immortal by achieving divinity, such as the god [Talos](Lore_Talos.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-VOF-15)</sup>

The [Purified](Lore_Purified.md) are beings that have been lustrated for purification by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). For mortals, this usually involves being stripped of their will and bestowed with the gift of immortality for the purpose of serving her for all eternity. Beings who join the ranks of the Purified include those who willingly pledge themselves to Meridia's service,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONMeridian Cub-16)</sup> as well the unwilling<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-17)</sup>, such as those who defy her.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOKing Narilmor-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONMeridian Purified-19)</sup>

There are other, simpler ways to avoid death, such as through the use of an enchanted scroll of revival at the moment of death.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>
