Hlaalu Athyn Llethan was the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) King of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), following the abdication of the longtime Queen, [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BOB3-2)</sup> In [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), he issued his most well-known decree when he revoked the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md)'s ban on trade and settlement in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), ushering in a flood of Imperial and [House Dunmer](Lore_Great_Houses.md) colonists that expanded old settlements and built new ones.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASHOM-3)</sup> As the King of Morrowind, he was known under several titles, such as the Defender of the People and Law, Duke of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) and Hlaalu Province, Grace of All Gods, High Councilor and Lord of Morrowind, and Loyal Servant of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TN-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HP-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RB426-6)</sup>

## History
*May his spirit rest among his ancestors. But he was king in name only. The Dunmer do not, and never have, submitted to kings like the Westerners, regardless of what the Empire says.*
—Mournhold citizens on King Llethan's death<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRGen King-7)</sup> Athyn Llethan's birthdate is unknown, but he came from a prestigious lineage as a nobleman of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup> He was a descendant of [Hlaalu Brevur](Lore_Hlaalu_Brevur.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KRP-8)</sup> At some point, he married a Dunmer named Ravani, but they never had any children.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRGen King-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TRRavani-9)</sup> His nephew was [Talen Vandas](Lore_Talen_Vandas.md), whom he treated like a son,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TRPM-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRGD-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-12)</sup> and his niece was Barenziah,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup> daughter of Mournhold's rulers at the time of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s invasion<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRB1-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BOB1-14)</sup> and Queen of Morrowind for most of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), Morrowind suffered great upheaval, largely from the [Arnesian War](Lore_Arnesian_War.md) with [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). In [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391),<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Date)</sup> a peasant uprising, organized by a few disgruntled nobles, broke out in Mournhold, demanding that Barenziah's husband, [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md), step down. While Barenziah and her children were away in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), Symmachus was slain in the revolt, and the rebels took over Mournhold.<sup>[[Note 2]](#intnote_Leader)</sup> Barenziah, who feared for her life back home, fled with her children to the Kingdom of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), where she married King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) and became the Queen of Wayrest. With Barenziah in exile, Llethan became the King of Morrowind and took up residence in the Royal Palace in central Mournhold.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BOB3-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TRB5-15)</sup>

Unlike Barenziah, who was beloved by her people,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRB4-16)</sup> Llethan and his court were despised by native Dunmer, as they never recognized a figurehead ruler like the Empire did.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRGen King-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KRP-8)</sup> Although Llethan was considered a dignified king, many did not take him seriously.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TREffe-17)</sup> There was an uneasy truce between the king, the Tribunal Temple, and the Great Houses.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup> In 3E 414, Llethan abolished Vvardenfell's status as a Temple preserve, as it had been since the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), and allowed people from across Morrowind and the Empire to settle it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASHOM-3)</sup>

In [3E 418](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_418), Eadwyre died, and Barenziah's son [Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) lost the subsequent power struggle for Wayrest's throne to his stepsister, [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md), so he and Barenziah returned to Morrowind in exile.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AGAD-18)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LRPTESP-19)</sup> Llethan welcomed them kindly, and Barenziah had no interest in resuming her reign, instead seeking to retire to her family estates.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AGAD-18)</sup> Llethan was old like Barenziah, and he presumably intended for his nephew, Talen Vandas, to succeed him after his death.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-12)</sup> Until then, he continued his role as king. In [3E 426](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_426), he granted relief to merchants by imposing favorable tariffs on imported [alcoholic beverages](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md), such as [flin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flin), a [Cyrodiilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) whiskey, but tariffs on native drinks like [greef](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greef), [shein](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shein), and [sujamma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sujamma) remained high.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RB426-6)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-YB426-20)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), King Llethan passed away, and because Vandas had predeceased him, the throne of Mournhold passed to his great-nephew, Helseth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRGen King-7)</sup> Helseth had lingered around Llethan's court since his return and rumors circulated about his ambitions for the throne.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AGAD-18)</sup> Vandas died sometime between Helseth's return and his accession, with the official statement being that Vandas was killed during a hunting trip.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TRPM-10)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRHelseth-21)</sup> The official cause of death for Llethan was old age, but many people, including Ravani Llethan, believed that Helseth was involved in his death.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRGen King-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TRRavani-9)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TRFedris-22)</sup> Even a newspaper called *the Common Tongue* hinted at this idea by presenting Helseth's history of corruption and subterfuge.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRGen King-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TRRavani-9)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TCT-23)</sup> Barenziah claimed that Llethan lived a rich and rewarding life.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TRBarenziah-24)</sup>

Fedris Hler, the steward of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and leader of the [High Ordinators](Lore_High_Ordinators.md), believed that most Dunmer in Mournhold knew that Helseth had the King killed, but did not speak up out of fear.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TRFedris-22)</sup> Although the public paid some respect to Llethan's passing, they were generally indifferent to it, but many were still suspicious about both his and Vandas's deaths occurring around the time of Helseth's arrival in Mournhold.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRGen King-7)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TRGen Helseth-25)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Date) | The 3rd Edition of the *[Pocket Guide to the Empire](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire.md)* ties the revolt in Mournhold and Symmachus' death with the Arnesian War, which occurred in [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TTET-26)</sup> But the given year for Symmachus' death is [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391), five years earlier.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TDC-27)</sup><sup>:15</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_Leader) | Sources such as the *[Biography of Queen Barenziah](Lore_Biography_of_Queen_Barenziah.md)* and the 3rd Edition of the *Pocket Guide to the Empire* establish that Athyn Llethan took over after Symmachus' death and Barenziah abdicated the throne, however, *[the Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md)* claims that a rebel leader took control instead. But while the *Biography of Queen Barenziah* attests to this by saying rebels took over Mournhold, it also establishes a regent was named to rule in her absence. Though no other sources identify this rebel leader or mentions the rebellion, the leader of Mournhold in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* is known as King **Zirik** and their connection to Barenziah or Llethan is unknown.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-AR-28)</sup> |
