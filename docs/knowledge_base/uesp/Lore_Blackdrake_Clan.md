# Blackdrake Clan

The Blackdrake Clan was a prominent [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) that lived in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) during the reign of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md). They were composed of friends and close relatives of the Longhouse Emperors.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-npc-Ard_Caddach.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ard_Caddach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ard_Caddach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ard_Caddach.jpg) Caddach of the Blackdrake Clan ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Durcorach.jpg) Emperor Durcorach, the Black Drake The Blackdrakes rose under [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) the Black Drake. The clan was formed by a coalition of the closest relatives and friends of the newly crowned [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) soon after Durcorach's army conquered [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) in [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup> The clan took his famous appellation for their own clan name. Due to their relationship with the imperial line, they were given more respect than clans of such small numbers would usually receive.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

In [2E 559](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_559), Durcorach's son, Emperor [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), appointed a member of the Blackdrake Clan and one of his cousins, [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), as the Imperial Governor of Markarth. His role was to both rule the city and to maintain peace between the local Reach Clans.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup>

As of [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568), during the reign of the grandson of Durcorach, Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), the Blackdrakes were considered one of the most prominent clans in the region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

After the fall of the Longhouse Emperors in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), a great number of Reachmen departed Cyrodiil. When the last remaining members of the Blackdrake Clan attempted to take the city of Markarth, Caddach executed them and solidified his rule. Caddach purged his clan, executing or exiling all remaining Blackdrakes who could challenge him for leadership. According to his own words, he killed all the relatives he could find. Caddach executed his kin by having them thrown from the highest walls of the city, painting the ramparts with their blood.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MTC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCaddach-4)</sup> Caddach abandoned his Imperial title and took instead the old Reach title of ard.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCaddach-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOMASIS-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-5)</sup>

The Blackdrake Clan was no more. Although individuals associated with the Longhouse Emperors, such as Leovic's advisor, known as [the Rat](Lore_The_Rat.md), and Moricar's daughter, [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md), were alive as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TA-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TR-7)</sup> the clan was virtually extinct. Caddach claimed that "he had no clan but the Reach."<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-5)</sup> He was referred to as the last of the Blackdrakes by some, but he was not fond of this moniker.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MTC-3)</sup>

## Notes
- When Durorach allied with the [Tagh Droiloch](Lore_Tagh_Droiloch.md) coven in [2E 527](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_527), warriors loyal to Durcorach, who were progenitors of the Blackdrake Clan were known to apply the paint made of asp oil and soot to enter a battle frenzy.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TBDFW-8)</sup>
- [Camhain Tharn](Lore_Camhain_Tharn.md) was a nephew of [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md), but whether or not he was a member of the Blackdrake Clan is unclear.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ARTG-9)</sup>
