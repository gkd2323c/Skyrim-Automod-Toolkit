# Witches

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg) A Witchmother Witches and **Witch-men** are practitioners (typically female)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HCP:TTD-1)</sup> of hedge-magic known as **witchcraft**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Clans-2)</sup> They tend to gather in covens that vary in faith and practices (see below). Witchcraft is often seen in a negative light, and practitioners are sometimes persecuted by witchhunters, individuals that specialize on rooting out and destroying dark cults and profane sorcery.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

There are a variety of ways to become a witch, and traditions vary across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Some witches receive the Secret Mark of Power in an arcane ritual, while others seek the patronage of a [Daedric Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lords.md). Witchcraft is said to involve opening oneself to the invisible world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Witches were granted special permission by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) to summon daedric princes as per the terms to the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md) establised in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920V4-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TCC-7)</sup>

The [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md) is an annual holiday that falls on the 13th of [Frostfall](Lore_Frostfall.md), coinciding with [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md)'s summoning day. During the festival, the denizens of Tamriel defy their superstitious fears. [Ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), demons, and evil spirits are mocked and celebrated by both occult occurrences and outrageous costumes.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WFR-8)</sup> Sinister types gather in the wilderness outside cities. The creatures they create or summon may plague Tamriel for eons.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WFDF-9)</sup> The Witches Festival earns its name as the most sacred holiday for witches.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OPS-10)</sup>

## Racial Differences

### Bretons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg/200px-ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg) A Glenmoril Wyress In High Rock, the [Wyrd Covens](Lore_Wyrd_Covens.md) are a Bretonic subculture of nature-witches, the [Beldama](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md) and [Glenmoril Wyrd](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md) being two major ones. Some myths place the creation of the Wyrd in the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), when [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)'s Naming (which gave all creatures of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) their shape)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOWHC-11)</sup> and his transformation to the first of the [Earthbones](Lore_Earthbones.md) occurred. The Wyrd were to be wardens of nature, descended from the Ehlnofey themselves.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOWTND-12)</sup> In the modern day, beliefs among the various Wyrd Covens vary. The Beldama Coven for example still reveres Jephre and their Ehlnofey heritage,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-WCONHR-13)</sup> while the various branches of the Glenmoril Wyrd lean towards worshipping the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TGW-14)</sup>

They stem from the same traditions as the [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) ancestors of the Bretons, the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md),<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESODhulef-15)</sup> but a religious schism brought about the Wyrd's separation.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONWD-16)</sup> Repulsed by [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) and the civilization-embracing druids they referred to as "barons in ivy robes", these witches retreated into isolation, and sought out the Earthbones in their efforts to prostrate themselves before unmarred nature.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONWD-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LABHI-17)</sup> Once a singular entity, the Wyrd split into subgroups by the time of the last [Druid King](Lore_Druid_King.md)'s voyage to [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOWM-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONSDK-19)</sup> These sisterhoods remained behind on the mainland to safeguard the forests of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESODR-20)</sup> with some of the dozen or so covens even spreading throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-WCONHR-13)</sup> The Wyrd's way is to turn away from civilization altogether, and to live in nature as beasts do.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONWD-16)</sup>

In remote regions of High Rock, such as [Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md) and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), witches and medicine men are held in high regard by the superstitious country-folk because of their impressive magical feats.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-21)</sup> Many witches' coven dedicated to nature worship can be found in [Daenia](Lore_Daenia.md)'s forests.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GP-22)</sup>

The Breton tradition of cremating the dead dates back to the days of High Rock's witch kings, who ruled over the Bretons at some point in their history.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-CP-23)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DGP-24)</sup> [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) easily conquered the squabbling Breton kingdoms, but would find it impossible to truly squash the sectarian conflicts between them that hampered trade and prosperity.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-PGE1High-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3High-26)</sup> Septim opted to entrust a governor of his choosing to end the feud between High Rock's witch-kings, which was a successful endeavor.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

### Dunmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/MW-npc-Zennammu.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Zennammu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Zennammu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Zennammu.jpg) A Mabrigash In Morrowind, witches, similarly to [warlocks](Lore_Warlocks.md), are typically bound to the service of a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) in hopes of receiving power. They are inclined to worship the [Bad Daedra](Lore_Bad_Daedra.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MWGDM-28)</sup> Witches are drawn towards the [Daedric Ruins](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) that dot the province, which are rumored to have been erected by the Princes themselves. However, the Tribunal Temple is known to keep the ruins clear from any Daedric cults that may hope to spring around them.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

The [Mabrigash](Lore_Mabrigash.md) are an [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) tribe primarily made up of renegade wise women who have forsaken the established rules of behavior for an Ashlander woman, so that they may master the dark arts and become a witch-warrior.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-MWM-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Lt B-31)</sup> Mabrigashes are outcasts among the Ashlanders.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Lt B-31)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-MWAC-32)</sup>

One of the most significant witch-warriors in Ashlander history was Sanit-Kil, who in turn worked in the service of the legendary wise-woman, Kinitu-Nila. Sanit-Kil's amulet, the Heart of Fire is a token acknowledged by [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md)'s four tribes and it has been passed down among the [Erabenimsun](Lore_Erabenimsun_Tribe.md)'s people for years.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-MWManirai-33)</sup>

In the [King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md) series, one of the main characters, [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md) is referred to as the "Witch-King" by [Edward](Lore_Edward.md) in the first book.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-KE1-34)</sup>

### Goblins
[Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) tribes are known to include female Goblin witches within their ranks.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-OBGoblin Witch-35)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-36)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-37)</sup> Goblin tribes usually possess at least one shaman, typically a female magic user who leads the tribe.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-38)</sup> These shamans are sometimes referred to as witches.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-OBGoblin Witch-35)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

### Imperials
One of the former Emperors during the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md), [Thules the Gibbering](Lore_Thules_the_Gibbering.md), was a [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md) witch-warrior who, despite his heritage, was not well-liked.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LOS-40)</sup><sup>:96</sup>

One myth regarding the Lucky Old Lady statue of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) explains that an Imperial prince, who was taken in by the Lady, was kidnapped and cursed by a band of witches to be rude and horrible.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DOTN-41)</sup>

Sea witches are religious figures or magic leaders among the [Sea Elves](Lore_Maormer.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Umindior-42)</sup>

### Nords
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/BM-npc-Fryse_Hag_1.jpg/200px-BM-npc-Fryse_Hag_1.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-npc-Fryse_Hag_1.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-npc-Fryse_Hag_1.jpg) Fryse Hag The Nords are said to have legendary witch-warriors, whose weapons were engraved with runes. Nordic smiths attempt to replicate this with their secret metalcrafts for their steel weaponry.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-MWGaro-43)</sup>

The Direfrost Clan were rulers of northern Eastmarch, and were well known for their persecution of witches, which they found their divine calling. The covens were numerous in the area, erecting hovels to the Daedric Princes, and would murder innocents and children.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Clans-2)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-44)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-45)</sup>

The [Fryse Hags](Lore_Fryse_Hags.md) are an order of savage [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) witches dedicated to the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), the widow of the god [Shor](Lore_Shor.md). Each hag is a powerful ice mage in her own right, although they have also been known to consort with the undead or use enchanted [silver](Lore_Silver.md) weapons. The hags wander the wilds of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), living outside civilized society and indiscriminately killing any traveler they come across, viewing outsiders as a despised threat to their beliefs. They are known to congregate in ice caves.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-46)</sup> [Windcallers](Lore_Windcallers.md) are [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) witches dedicated to the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md) known for their mastery of [wind magic](Lore_Weather_Magic.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Costume-47)</sup>

[Clever Men](Lore_Clever_Men.md) were occasionally equated to witches.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-LN-48)</sup>

In the twilight years of the Third Era, a local witches' coven near [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) elevated in power when their leader, [Jsashe](Lore_Jsashe.md) assumed control of the [county](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md) and even took the title of "Witch-Queen". This was after a series of disasters struck the region and the people blamed and later exalted her. She was a self-proclaimed priestess of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) and had effective control over the county by [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PGE3-49)</sup>

### Reachfolk
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hagraven.jpg) A Hagraven [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg) A Reachfolk witch Reachmen are often referred to as the Witchmen of High Rock, a name given to them due to their use of dark "Reach-magic". It is believed that they learned much of this magic through trading with the [Orsimer](Lore_Orsimer.md), their neighbors who also occupy the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-21)</sup> Hircine is believed to be the source of where Reach-magic is drawn from,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-50)</sup> though the magic crosses into [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s domain when the cruel magic of the Gravesingers (Reachmen [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md)) is involved.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONZK-51)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-52)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ESOAngof-53)</sup> Reach-magic has been outlawed within the Mages Guild, though that has not stopped the art from being widely studied.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-21)</sup>

[Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md) were once witches, but have undergone a ritual, trading their humanity for access to powerful magic. The transformations they undergo infuse their entire beings with some element of that power.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-HGTS-54)</sup> Some witches of the Reach source their magic from hagravens, performing "ecstatic ceremonial heres[ies]" such as sacrifices to acquire dark nature magic.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOEGTB-52)</sup> Although primarily a Reachfolk tradition, other covens and cultures have been known to produce hagravens.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-55)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-56)</sup>

Reachfolk witches respect the ancient witch [Naenra Waerr](Lore_Naenra_Waerr.md). To honor her, Reach witches who pass the final test of wisdom and power set to them by a clan elder gain the privilege to craft a mask that resembles the [Witchwise Headdress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witchwise_Headdress), a mask Naenra wore while creating the [Umbra Sword](Lore_Umbra.md).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Witchwise Headdress-57)</sup>

Many young Reachfolk witches perform taming rituals on [sabre cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md) hoping to gain their loyalty.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-KRSC-58)</sup> They are also renowned for their Bloodrage tools, which are inlaid with numerous enchantments used by the Reachfolk to harvest runestones more effectively.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Reach_Witch_Removal-59)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-BD-60)</sup>

The [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) and the [Tagh Droiloch](Lore_Tagh_Droiloch.md) were prominent covens composed primarily of Reachfolk.

Among the Reachfolk, titles and groups like "[witch-knights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witch_Knights)" and "witch-lords" were recognized. The [Witch Knight Charger](Lore_Horse.md#Witch_Knight_Charger) was a variety of horse bred by the Reachfolk.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-WLK-61)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-WKC-62)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-WKD-63)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-creature-Hag.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Hag.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Hag.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Hag.jpg) A hag The [Witchmother coven](Lore_Witchmothers.md) is the name for a loose coven of powerful witches who have been transformed into [hag](Lore_Hag.md) -like creatures known as Witchmothers.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Vanima-64)</sup> They venerate [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) and carry out her bidding. The number of coven members is unknowable and ever-changing,<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Taerma-65)</sup> and they have been known to take on new members from all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Vanima-64)</sup> The ashes of venerated witches are used to sanctify arcane "wicker-steeds".<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-BWSteed-66)</sup>

The [Witch Tree](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witch_Tree) is a mysterious sentient tree found in Ghast's Pass, a mountain pass in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) region of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), north of [Azra's Crossing](Lore_Azra%27s_Crossing.md). It can speak cryptically through the rustling of leaves, and can also move its roots to communicate. It grew around one of the seven [Star Teeth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md), a treasure which it provided to the [Soul of Conflict](Lore_Soul_of_Conflict.md) for their confrontation with the [Umbra' Keth](Lore_Umbra%27_Keth.md).<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-67)</sup>

Witch covens of unknown origins exist in High Rock:

- The Daggerfall Witches: The Daggerfall Witches were active in [Shalgora](Lore_Shalgora.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Daughters of Wroth: The Daughters of Wroth were active in the Wrothgarian Mountains circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Sisters of The Bluff: The Sisters of The Bluff were active in the [kingdom of Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- [Skeffington Coven](Lore_Skeffington_Coven.md): A notable group of witches that live in the Barony of [Phrygias](Lore_Phrygias.md), within the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).
- The Tamarilyn Witches: The Tamarilyn Witches were active in the [county of Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Witches of Alcaire: The Witches of Alcaire were active in [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>

Some witch covens are also active in Hammerfell:

- The Dust Witches: The Coven of the Dust was active in [Antiphyllos](Lore_Antiphyllos.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Mountain Witches: The Coven of the Peaks was active in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Sisters of Kykos: The Kykos Coven was active in [Dak'fron](Lore_Dak%27fron.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Tide Witches: The Coven of the Tide was active in [Myrkwasa](Lore_Myrkwasa.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Witches of Devilrock: The Devilrock Coven was active in Dragontail Mountains circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
- The Witches of the Marsh: The Coven in the Marsh was active in the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DWC-68)</sup>
