Subtropical Cyrodiil: A Speculation **by [Lady Cinnabar](Lore_Lady_Cinnabar.md) of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md)** *Thoughts on the climate of Cyrodiil and its relationship to the White-Gold Tower* *
---
According to "[The Heartland of Cyrodiil](Lore_The_Heartland_of_Cyrodiil.md)," by that old fraud [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md), the [Nibenese valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md) and the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) hills have always enjoyed the temperate climate they have today, and early references to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) as a subtropical jungle were merely errors on the part of one of the Heimskrs.

Really? What, then, of the "waving fronds" of [Vahtacen](Lore_Vahtacen.md) mentioned in the Hosiric Lays? What of [Khosey](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Khosey)'s "dense-jungl'd shore of [Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md)" in the [Tamrilean Tractates](Lore_Tamrilean_Tractates.md)? Are these, as well, the mistakes of errant copyists?

No, I think it more likely that three millennia ago Cyrodiil's climate was warmer and wetter than it is today. The environment of the [Heartland](Lore_Heartland.md) has changed. Which begs the question: how?

I've given a great deal of thought to this question, and would like to propose a hypothesis. However, I am not a scholar of deep mythohistory like [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) or [Beredalmo the Signifier](Lore_Beredalmo.md), so let's just call the following … a speculation.

[Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) is the center of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md); Cyrodiil is the center of Tamriel; and at the center of Cyrodiil stands that greatest of mortal-made structures, the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) —which was patterned in open emulation of the [Adamantine](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) (or Direnni) Tower, the oldest structure in Tamriel, said to have been erected by the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) themselves.

This was no mere homage, whim, or coincidence: White-Gold was built in the semblance of Adamantine in order to echo the first Tower's undeniable mystical properties. And not just to echo them, but, due to its central location, to amplify them.

What are these mystical properties? This leads us to the domain of [Tower Lore](Lore_The_Towers.md), a realm fraught with scholarly conflict, but I will try to give a simple, and uncontroversial, summary.

When the Aedra were persuaded—or hoodwinked—by [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) into creation of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), the physical flesh of Nirn was hung on a skeleton of joints, each of which radiated a palpable reality— [the bones of the world](Lore_Earthbones.md), as it were.

At one of these mystical joint-points the Aedra erected a great structure, the Adamantine Tower, where they held a conclave to decide the fate of Lorkhan and the Mundus. In later times [mortal mages](Lore_Direnni.md) discovered the Tower, and deduced its reality-affirming properties. The [Merethic Elves](Lore_Aldmer.md) then imitated it, erecting the White-Gold and [Crystal Towers](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) at other joint-points.

In doing this, what did the Ur-Elves hope to achieve? I would posit that, through their collective "possession" of such Towers in their realms, over time the Elves actually amended their local reality to conform to their desires.

Thus the [Summerset archipelago](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), in the sphere of the Crystal Tower, is a warm and paradisiacal domain perfectly adapted to the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md). And Cyrodiil, in the sphere of the even-more-powerful White-Gold Tower, became a warm and subtropical jungle—which suited the ease-loving [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md).

But then the slaves of the Heartland High Elves rose up against their masters, conquered the valley of the Nibenay, and the Ayleids ruled no more. Thereafter, White-Gold Tower was the center of a human empire, peopled by [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) and [Cyro-Nords](Lore_Imperial.md) who originated in cooler, northern climes. And so the Tower of Cyrodiil responded to the desires of its new masters.

And that, I believe, is the answer to how the Heartland changed from subtropical to temperate: because once Men ruled in Cyrodiil, the local reality changed to meet their needs and wishes. Changed slowly, perhaps, almost imperceptibly, but inexorably—until Cyrodiil became the realm of temperate forests and fields we now know.

So, is that the truth of the matter? Have I deduced the answer to the mystery? I cannot be sure: I'm only a humble scholar, residing in the Tower of the Fifth Doctrine, which is neither White-Gold nor Adamantine. The only thing I'm quite sure of is that any theory propounded by Phrastus of Elinhir is almost certain to be wrong.
