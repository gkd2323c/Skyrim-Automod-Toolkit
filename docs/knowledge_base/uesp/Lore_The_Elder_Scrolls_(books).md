# The Elder Scrolls (books)


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/RG-misc-The_Elder_Scrolls.png/200px-RG-misc-The_Elder_Scrolls.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-The_Elder_Scrolls.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-The_Elder_Scrolls.png) *The Elder Scrolls*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Redguard Trailer-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGIS-2)</sup> *The Elder Scrolls* is a multi-volume book of chapters, with each volume or chapter of the books covering a [Hero](Lore_Hero.md) and an [Event](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Event) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGIS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind Intro Movie-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vijay Interview-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Julian Indigo-5)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Their title is derived from the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) of Tamriel,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARIS-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vijay Interview-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Redguard Trailer-1)</sup> and and they are stored in the tower laboratory of the [Imperial Library](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Library)<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> where the Elder Scrolls themselves were housed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aetherius Journey-7)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGIS-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-bookoftime-8)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

The individual chapters have powers of [divination](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight), allowing the reader to glimpse future events with astonishing accuracy and witness "fate as it will recorded upon the Elder Scrolls."<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aetherius Journey-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> *The Elder Scrolls Chapter I: The Arena* was witnessed by the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) in a dream state where they witnessed [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) betraying Uriel Septim VII and stealing his throne.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARIS-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-CGRTAb BS-9)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> *The Elder Scrolls Chapter II: Daggerfall*, referred to by the sorceress [Nulfaga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nulfaga) as the *Book of the Time*, allowed the [the Agent](Lore_Hero.md#The_Agent) to witness the turmoil taking place in Tamriel's near future and the outcome of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Df IS-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aetherius Journey-7)</sup> Adjacent books called *The Elder Scrolls Adventures* organized by alphabetical volume were also stored in the labratory.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGIS-2)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_nb1) | Series creator [Vijay Lakshman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Vijay_Lakshman) recalls pitching the name *The Elder Scrolls* for the series as a way to describe a book of "Chapters", where each entry would represent a new software release—i.e. a new chapter in the book of The Elder Scrolls.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vijay Interview-4)</sup> This intention is seen in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*'s intro sequence, which zooms in upon the tomelike chapter and has an unraveling scroll showing certain events, which then becomes the main menu.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARIS-6)</sup> This motif was repeated for the intro and outro videos for *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Df IS-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Aetherius Journey-7)</sup> According to series artist [Mark Jones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Mark_Jones), the intent at the time was for the tower laboratory that held the chapters to appear in the introductions of all the *[Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)* games as a connecting visual.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-bookoftime-8)</sup> *[Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)* later showed all the individual chapters of *The Elder Scrolls* book on a shelf in the tower, as well as volumes on another shelf making up *The Elder Scrolls Adventures*<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGIS-2)</sup> (the same tower previously appeared in the [*Daggerfall* Winter CES advertisement](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Daggerfall_CES_Cinematic)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-12)</sup>). But ultimately, the intended book and labratory motif was quietly retired after *Redguard*. |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_nb2) | *Daggerfall*'s manual introduction narrative refers to the Scrollkeepers of the Imperial City.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DUG-13)</sup><sup>:5</sup> Monastic orders founded by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and dedicated to [Julianos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Julianos) are later described as the keepers of the Elder Scrolls.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue J-14)</sup> And even later lore expands upon that with the introduction of the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) and their private library in the [Imperial City's Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE1C-15)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBUltimate-16)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_nb3) | A cut scroll page from the [*Arena* introduction scene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Main_Quest#Introduction_Scene) describes the Eternal Champion seeing the intro events as an in-world dream in the time before they unfold, when out-of-world they're reading the context from the intro movie book.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Arena History-17)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_nb4) | Despite the fifth book on the shelf in the *Redguard* introduction sequence being called *The Elder Scrolls: Romanelli* there, the fifth *Elder Scrolls* video game was *Skyrim* rather than *Romanelli*.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGIS-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-18)</sup> [Romanelli](https://www.wisdomlib.org/names/romanelli) is a real world Italian surname, meaning "little Roman" or "young Roman". |
| --- | --- |
- The video games themselves are the chapters of *The Elder Scrolls*.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vijay Interview-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-MK-19)</sup> On related note, scroll visuals are featured in the stylized user interfaces of *Arena*, *Daggerfall*, *Redguard*, *Morrowind*, *Oblivion*, and *Legends*. In *Arena*'s case, the main menus appears as an unraveling scroll or Elder Scroll despite it being shown at first as a tome in the introduction sequence.
