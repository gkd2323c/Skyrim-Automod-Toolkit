# Stars

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/RG-constellation-Empty.jpg/200px-RG-constellation-Empty.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Empty.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Empty.jpg) Stars from the [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) observatory Stars are astronomical objects believed by many to be tears in the veil of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that lead to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). There are a great many stars in the sky, some of which form the [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md).

[Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) myths claim that stars were created by the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), [Anuic](Lore_Anu.md) spirits that fled Creation after the departure of its architect, the god [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EOMN-1)</sup> who made the world.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WOT-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Girnalin-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIC-4)</sup> Magnus himself left the greatest tear in the firmament: the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md), sometimes named Magnus.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-36Lo V33-7)</sup> The stars are therefore sometimes referred to as the "children of Magnus".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> According to one [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) myth and the creation tale of the Anuad, the stars were instead created from the blood of Anu. Some believe that stars are "fragments" of Magnus.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIC-4)</sup><sup>:136</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TAA-9)</sup>

Within [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) belief [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) is believed to have created day, night, and the places between through the manner in which he interpreted the laws of time established by [Anui-El](Lore_Anui-El.md) and applied them to Nirn, he is also believed to be responsible for the movements of the stars, as to this day the realms they're windows to sway and wink to his song.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Girnalin-3)</sup> [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith also acknowledges Y'ffre in this capacity of being the deity responsible for the movements of the stars, which still sway to the beautiful song he sang when he walked [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) before the first day.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Jephre-10)</sup>

The ancient [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) believed the most sublime form of light was star light. They left behind many artifacts made from meteoric iron or meteoric glass, which are Aetherial fragments or commonly known as shooting stars. <sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mft S-11)</sup>

[Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) knew the power of the stars, building orreries and observatories to watch the constellations and who they protected<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Erasmo-12)</sup>, they had a particular interest in the [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) constellation. Dwemer runes were also used to represent constellations.

Some stars are named after or associated with [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) or the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) in general.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DBI-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> According to [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md), the Daedra were born before the stars.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

[Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) believe that the stars were created by [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), who was so big he could place the stars themselves in the sky, as a way to guide spirits to the [far shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md). Their myths also describe how, after his death, the hunger of [Sep](Lore_Sep.md), the Second Serpent, took form in the sky as a hungry void that chases and tries to devour the stars.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-5)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Monomyth-16)</sup> Some sources claim that, before deceiving the spirits, Sep stole one of the stars which Ruptga held in his hand and was using to form the [Great Map](Lore_Astronomy.md) and put it in his pocket. When Sep was killed, it is this star that fell out of his mouth in the form of a pearl. The peal fell to the land and was covered by sand, and so came to belong to [Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md). It is said that this Pearl is the "Way and the Guide", the "light to the Far Shores and back again" and, when it is at last discovered again, [water](Lore_Water_Magic.md) will flow over all the previous Worldskins and the time of Sep's Hunger will be over, the Hungry Stomach of [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md) at last sated. It is also said that the Hunger is what answers the call of the worlds to save them, and that the only real world is that of Satakal, as the stars can't be devoured.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

[Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) believe that the stars guide your destiny from birth to death and beyond.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

The ancient [Nedes](Lore_Nedes.md) believed the stars were set in their courses by the [Great Dragon of Time](Lore_Akatosh.md), who also appointed [the guardians](Lore_Astronomy.md#Constellations) to watch over them.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Abba Arl-19)</sup>

The [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md) are powerful ethereal beings formed by the power of the stars, the most powerful of which represent the [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md). It is unknown if these beings are from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) or if they are mortals who have achieved a state of ascension.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MV-20)</sup> Whatever the case, the Celestials that represent the constellations are linked to them, as in [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Arnure-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SOE-22)</sup> the corresponding stars temporarily vanished from the sky while the Celestials took form in [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), and are said to be part of the world's natural order.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Thief-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Hara-24)</sup>

## Falling Stars
Falling stars or "shooting stars" can be attributed to **[comets](Lore_Comets.md)**, or [Aetherial fragments](Lore_Aetherial_Fragments.md) falling from Aetherius.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-CA-27)</sup> It has been suggested that these are a natural and necessary process which correctively transfers Aetheric energies to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SV-28)</sup>

## History
The ancient [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) people of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) followed a religion known as the [Cult of the Stars](Lore_Cult_of_the_Stars.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Erold-29)</sup>

The ships built for [Yngol](Lore_Yngol.md) and [Ylgar](Lore_Ylgar.md) are named after two favored stars in the [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) sky; the Darumzu, and the Harakk.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-SOTR2-30)</sup>

In the area of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) the three stars of Locanda glowing in the night stand for unholy alliances.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TVR-31)</sup>

Some [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [witches](Lore_Witches.md) can read the stars and see the future and hidden truths in their light.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ARANA-32)</sup> Similar practices could be observed among the inhabitants of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DO-33)</sup>

According to her faithful, [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) "lights the sky of our world" and "is the stars of our world". They believed that "her thousand truths are a thousand lights in the night sky".<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-IDWA-34)</sup>

In [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578) or [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) occurred, as a result, the constellation of the Serpent became so large in the night sky it seemed to threaten every other star sign at once. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the year of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) which the Soulburst set in motion, the Serpent Celestial enacted his own scheme to ensnare the other constellation celestials and remake the mortal world in his image, but was stopped by [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ILESO-35)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Prophet-36)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Thief-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Hara-24)</sup>
