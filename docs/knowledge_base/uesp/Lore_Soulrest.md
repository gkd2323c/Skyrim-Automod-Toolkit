The city is a quiet port-town that leaves the crashing sound of the sea behind its gates,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AR-2)</sup> and its coastal access brings it a vast influx of foreign goods and aesthetics.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAnti Ritual-3)</sup> It was the capital of Black Marsh during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWMap-4)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
Soulrest is located in the southwest corner of Black Marsh, on a peninsula that extends into the west wrapping around Topal Bay, but this is erroneously ommitted by some maps. It lies at a point where the eastern edge of the Topal Bay meets the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md). Just south of the city is the start of river that drains out into the sea and travels northeast into the interior.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWMap-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ARTamriel-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Map-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MCMap-8)</sup> There are also a number of islands found south of Soulrest and the mainland.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONMap-9)</sup> Soulrest was neighbored by several settlements, one of them being Chasepoint to the east.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup>

## History
[Argonians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Argonians) have lived in the Soulrest region since at least ancient times, back when the [wraxu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wraxu) birds were still alive and their likeness was etched in ancient [xanmeers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xanmeer).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONAnti Wraxu-10)</sup> But the settlement has existed since at least [1E 1033](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1033), back when the notorious pirate-lord, [Red Bramman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Bramman) was active. After every skirmish with [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), Red Bramman would escape via a narrow, winding river near Soulrest, encased by a dense thicket of mangroves. The [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) eventually found the path and followed Bramman down the river until they reached the provincial heart and his bandit kingdom near [Blackrose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackrose).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3E-11)</sup>

Many years later, the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) made their attempt to conquer Argonia and regions such as south-coastal Black Marsh were caught in the fray. General Regulus Sardecus led the second campaign over Black Marsh in the [Blackwater War](Lore_Blackwater_War.md) and in a controversial move, one of the General's trusted legates, Elissia Mallicius ordered her unit of sappers to burn a peat bog near [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold) on [Rain's Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rain%27s_Hand), [1E 2828](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2828). But unknown to them, the bog was connected to a vast underground network that stretches across the western portion of Black Marsh, from Stormhold to Soulrest and Gideon to the south. The toxicity of the Great Burn led to many severe casualties, not just from the Argonians or the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TBW6-12)</sup> but especially for wildlife like the wraxu, who went extinct.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONAnti Wraxu-10)</sup> At some point in time, there was a struggle for regional hegemony in Soulrest.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAnti Ritual-3)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Soulrest was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Heciana and had a rivalry with [Gideon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gideon).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AR-2)</sup> Sometime in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the tribes of Black Marsh started a series of uprisings that destroyed plantations and mines in Soulrest, all the while halting exports of [gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold) and timber. The Expeditionary Force was sent in by the Empire to quell these tribes.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MWGen-13)</sup> At this time, Soulrest was accepted to be the capital of Black Marsh.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWMap-4)</sup>
