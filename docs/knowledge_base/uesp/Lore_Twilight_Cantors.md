[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-quest-Chiaroscuro_Crossroads_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Chiaroscuro_Crossroads_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Chiaroscuro_Crossroads_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Chiaroscuro_Crossroads_03.jpg) A Twilight Cantor performing an exorcism The Twilight Cantors are a [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) order of wandering priests dedicated to [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md). They live a life of wine and prayer―full of dozing and bathing and joyful music. They spend a great deal of the day sleeping when they are not on the road. The Twilight Cantors do not consider themselves "professional musicians", and do not charge for their work.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCTEA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Adara.27hai ESO-2)</sup> Sometimes, they'll sing at joyful events, such as weddings and namings.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Adara.27hai ESO-2)</sup> The leader of the Twilight Cantors is known as the Chief Crier.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Krinze-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Patch Notes2.3.5-4)</sup>

Despite their peaceful and idyllic day-to-day activities, the Twilight Cantors serve a very important purpose in Khajiiti culture. Wherever the [dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md) appear, the Twilight Cantors soon follow. They are called upon to perform exorcisms and expel [Namiira's](Lore_Namira.md) children from the Mundus through means such as prayer, chanting, and singing.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Adara.27hai ESO-2)</sup> They are treated by the common folk with reverence and fear. It is commonly believed that a cantor's presence is a sign of imminent misfortune. Thus, when the Twilight Cantors come to visit, the locals are quick to give them offerings. <sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCTEA-1)</sup> The Twilight Cantors have little need for worldly possessions, and when traveling, generally carry only the necessities and tools that assist them in their duties. An example of these tools are ankle chimes, whose music aids in the expulsion of dro-m'Athra.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCTEA-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MBAC-5)</sup>

## History
The Twilight Cantors were called to the Temple of Seven Riddles in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by [Elsweyr's](Lore_Elsweyr.md) Moon Bishops in the wake of a disaster. The temple's abbot desecrated the ancient seals keeping the dro-m'Athra from invading the temple, allowing the dark spirits to flood the premises.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Patch Notes2.3.5-4)</sup> [Adara'hai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adara%27hai) of the Twilight Cantors managed to restore the seals and vanquish the dro-m'Athra with the help of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md).

## Known Leaders
The title of High Cantor, along with the hierarchical structure of the Twilight Cantors, was an enforced construct created by Torval Curiata as a means of controlling the order.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Viti-6)</sup> In 2E 582, the position was held by High Cantor Viti, who served as the acting leader of the Twilight Cantors in the absence of Chief Crier Adara'hai. Adara'hai had temporarily withdrawn on a holy sabbatical following her actions at the Temple of Seven Riddles and the Black Heights.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Krinze-3)</sup>

- Chief Crier [Adara'hai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adara%27hai) *(fl. 2E 582)* - High Cantor Viti *(2E 582 - 2E 582)*
