The Crow, also known more specifically as the **Luminary Crow**, was one of the [Luminaries of Magic](Lore_Luminary.md), mysterious immortal beings of pure [magic](Lore_Magic.md), spirits likened to beams of sunlight from [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) himself,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragon-2)</sup> her chosen form was that of a [Crow](Lore_Crow.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragon-2)</sup> The Crow was the youngest of the Luminaries by far,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup> and she played an important role in the rediscovery of Ulfsild's creation, Scribing, a "metamagical theoryset"<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Largesse-3)</sup> which was the precursor to the modern [spellcrafting](Lore_Spellcrafting.md) system.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ON2024-4)</sup>

Unlike the other Luminaries, the Crow was an intentional creation of [Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md). Partly inspired by [the Gryphon's](Lore_The_Gryphon.md) beliefs regarding his own origins,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-5)</sup> she used part of her own [soul](Lore_Souls.md) in a ritual that combined her spirit and a copy of her mind from that moment in time, flashing them upon the great wellspring of arcane power she'd gathered in the Scholarium's Scribing Altar to create a new Luminary of Magic, a ritual described as the ultimate act of Scribing. As the looming threat of the loss of [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md) left little time to plan the ritual to Ulfsild's normally exacting standards, the process was not fully refined, causing the Crow to have a chaotic arcane presence. The ritual to create the Crow took an enormous toll upon Ulfsild's spirit, siphoning a considerable amount of her soul, and required years of recovery.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Fot C-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ulfsild-7)</sup> The Crow described itself as Ulfsild's [familiar](Lore_Familiar.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup>

The Crow viewed herself as the piece of Ulfsild left behind to ensure that Scribing would pass to Ulfsild's inheritor and to the world. She described herself as a being born in sorrow but filled with hope, the shadow of a person, who'd soared on the winds of magic for centuries to ensure Scribing did not become lost to the world. The Crow saw life like as a great maze, legacies as blessings, and thought that the burdens of both should not be endured alone. She viewed the continuation of Ulfsild's work, the Luminaries being safe and happy, and the Scholarium becoming filled with life and laughter once more, as the destiny she was made to realize, and desired to see the world Ulfsild remembered after it was achieved. After spending centuries in the shape of a crow, she preferred her feathers to her previous form.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup>

The Luminaries saw the world and magic in a way that was different from mortals.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAS-8)</sup> The Crow claimed she, like all Luminaries, possessed a mastery of magic beyond mortal understanding, and that power, freely given, would allow one to access arcane mysteries most could only dream of. Indeed, Scribing itself was made possible through channeling their power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TI-9)</sup>

## History

### Creation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ulfsild%27s_Echo_of_Introduction.jpg) Ulfsild the Evergreen, creator and spiritual source of the Luminary Crow [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-place-The_Scholarium.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Scholarium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Scholarium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Scholarium.jpg) The Scholarium beneath Eyevea [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/ON-place-Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg/200px-ON-place-Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg) The door to the Wing of the Crow Ulfsild lived circa [1E 2150](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2150),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAS-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MGC-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ULTI-11)</sup> and built the Scholarium beneath the island of Eyevea. Some time later, the island was transported to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) due to a bargain [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) made with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) in exchange for the [Folium Discognitum](Lore_Folium_Discognitum.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Shalidor-12)</sup>

Knowing that nothing she or the Luminaries could do would outpace the greed of a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md), Ulfsild first froze the [Luminary Gryphon](Lore_The_Gryphon.md) in a tempest of ice, the magic of which she tied to a ward she left behind. This prevented the enraged Luminary from confronting Sheogorath, a being against whom even he was no match, while also preventing the Prince from discovering the Scholarium and Luminaries, which would've been disastrous.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gryphon-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWOTG-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Fox-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Echo-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Echo Loyalty-17)</sup>

Ulfsild quickly enacted a final plan meant to assure that Scribing would not become lost to the world. She used part of her own soul in a ritual that combined her spirit and a copy of her mind from that moment in time, flashing them upon the great wellspring of arcane power she'd gathered in the Scholarium's Scribing Altar, to create a new Luminary of Magic through a ritual described as the ultimate act of Scribing.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Fot C-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ulfsild-7)</sup> Thus the Lum Inary Crow was born. She chose the shape of her physical form, and her personal [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md), the [Wing of the Crow](Lore_Wing_of_the_Crow.md), was shaped and hewn into existence in the same manner. It became connected to the Scholarium via its own door.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTC-18)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TI-9)</sup>

Before Sheogorath pulled Eyevea out of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), Ulfsild recorded the secrets on how to scribe within the Scholarium's library in an effort to preserve her life's work.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONHTS-19)</sup> In the event that the Scholarium were to be recovered, she left whoever would don the role as her successor, and emphasized they seek out the Luminaries that would continue guarding the place.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONULYDMF-20)</sup>

With the doors closed, the altar became dormant. Without that beating heart, the mastery of Scribing was believed to stagnate. The Scholarium itself was not discovered, however, for it had been warded against mages, Princes, and powers long ago, and those defenses held strong even in the Shivering Isles. The Crow did not have much time to speak with Ulfsild, her other self, before the doors were sealed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup>

Ulfsild left numerous messages for potential future inheritors of Scribing by creating Echoes—magical projections of herself with within the Scholarium and Sunnamere. These instructional illusions scattered throughout the library were meant to guide the inheritor through the process of contacting the Luminaries.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup> Among these visages of her were the Flickering Image, Ulfsild's Echoes of Introduction, the Lens, Regret, the Dragon, Loyalty, Mystery, Charity, the Final Trial, and the Netch.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Ulfsild_Projections-21)</sup>

### Rediscovery of Scribing
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/ON-quest-The_Second_Era_of_Scribing_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Second_Era_of_Scribing_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Second_Era_of_Scribing_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Second_Era_of_Scribing_03.jpg) The unstable Scribing Altar In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), around the time Eyevea was returned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by Shalidor and the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), the Scholarium was rediscovered by a group of [knights](Lore_Knights.md) from the [Order of the Lamp](Lore_Order_of_the_Lamp.md). During their arrival, the once dormant altar became unstable,, necessitating the sealing of the doors to prevent catastrophe. The disaster was prevented due to the intervention of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), who replaced the unstable focusing crystal. This potentially also prevented the discovery of the Scholarium by Sheogorath, which would've likewise been catastrophic.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SCOS-22)</sup> After the magic was calmed, the Scholarium's caretaker, the Luminary Crow, directed the Vestige to seek out the other Luminaries and gain their support. To inherit Ulfsild's legacy and advance spellcraft itself, their strength would have to be returned to the altar.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup>

The [Inheritor](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Scholarium was guided by Ulfsild's echoes and the True Sight Lens, which she'd created to allow others to see magicka and Ley Lines, as she could. Retracing her footsteps, the Inheritor discovered fables and keys, broke the wards, and entered the Wings of the Indrik, the Dragon, the Gryphon, and the Netch to reconnect the Scholarium with them. The Luminaries proffered a series of challenges for the Inheritor to prove themselves. Once completed, the Luminaries agreed to lend their power.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragon-2)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Netch-23)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gryphon-13)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TI-9)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/ON-creature-Aut%27arioth%2C_Inheritor%27s_Bane.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Aut%27arioth%2C_Inheritor%27s_Bane.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Aut%27arioth,_Inheritor%27s_Bane.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Aut%27arioth,_Inheritor%27s_Bane.jpg) Aut'arioth, the Inheritor's Bane [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Crow.jpg) The Luminaries of Magic With their trials completed, the Luminaries granted their Sigils to the Inheritor. Placed at the altar, the Sigils restored the connection between the realms of the Luminaries and the Scholarium, and their strength flowed into the altar once more.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragon-2)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TWOTD-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TWOTI-25)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWOTG-14)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TWOTN-26)</sup> With their strength fueling it, the altar could once more "shape the very heart of magic itself" and translate its very essence into mortal hands, rewriting both the magic and the mortal as the practitioner scrawled their soul upon the page.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TI-9)</sup> Reconnecting the great power of the Luminaries caused new Ley Line activity, thus making Affix Scripts, Signature Scripts and Luminous Ink appear across Tamriel.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TI-9)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Sigil Dragon-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SOTIA-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Sigil Gryphon-29)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Sigil Netch-30)</sup>

With the support of the other Luminaries secured, the Crow revealed the truth: she was the final Luminary, created by Ulfsild herself, and the last who could lend her power to the Scribing Altar. The chaotic magic that had originally led to the rediscovery of the Scholarium was a result of the Crow's own presence, as Ulfsild's ritual had never been fully refined due to the urgency of the situation.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup>

In the [Wing of the Crow](Lore_Wing_of_the_Crow.md) the Vestige underwent the final trial to be declared the Inheritor. Within the Luminary Crow's replica of [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md), the Inheritor navigated the invisible path through the maze with the aid of [the Fox](Lore_The_Fox.md), who had been tasked to help by Ulfsild herself.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Fox-15)</sup> Once the wards were broken and the [Chimera](Lore_Chimera.md) Aut'arioth bested, the Crow's final challenge was complete. The Dragon and the other Luminaries then appeared and acknowledged the victorious Inheritor. The Crow guided the Inheritor to retrieve her own Sigil, which Ulfsild had taken with her when she departed, so it too could be added to the Scribing Altar, amplifying its power further.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dragon-2)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTC-18)</sup> Thus Scribing returned to Tamriel. Knowing the Inheritor had other matters to address, the Crow chose [Knight of the Lamp](Lore_Order_of_the_Lamp.md) Votary Nahlia as the Scholarium's new caretaker.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Crow-1)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SCOS-22)</sup>

[Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) was particularly enthusiastic of the possibilities Scribing would open up, imagining altars being installed in every guild hall and perhaps that Scribing would one day be taught in the wilds.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAS-8)</sup> The legacy of Ulfsild's lived on, with Scribing eventually evolving into modern spellcrafting.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ON2024-4)</sup>

## Fable of the Crow
The *[Fable of the Crow](Lore_Fable_of_the_Crow.md)* is an allegorical retelling of the origins of the Luminary Crow. In the tale, the [Crow](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md) met with the [Raven](Lore_Shalidor.md) who, wise and solitary from his many years, was the only one who could match her wits. The two built a [grand nest](Lore_Eyevea.md) and all the magic of the forest was theirs.

The Crow filled the nest with shiny gifts from her [friends in the woods](Lore_Luminary.md). Though she explained the gifts were meant to protect her and strengthen their friendship and were given in compassion, the Raven was skeptical, as he viewed them as "an awful lot of unexpected charity". The Raven soon lost interest in the gifts, but complained no further, as he saw they made the Crow happy.

One day the Raven returned, eyes glittering with a longing never before seen in him. He explained that he'd met a [Stranger](Lore_Sheogorath.md) who'd promised he'd give him a [star](Lore_Folium_Discognitum.md) in exchange for the nest. The Raven spoke of what wondrous things he could do with the star in his hands. The Crow was distraught, and wondered if something had changed in the Raven, or if he'd always been this way. The Raven set his deal in motion, and there was no going back.

Realizing he'd done wrong, the Raven offered to help move what he could from her cache in the nest to safety before the Stranger took it. But the Crow refused. If the things in her cache were incomplete or, worse, if the Stranger found them, it would spell disaster.

Thus the Crow decided to leave the nest behind under secret guard. She plucked her own dark feathers, enough to practically make another Crow and, without realizing she'd done it, Feather-Crow was born. A being made in her image, not unlike her friends of the forest. Crow told Feather-Crow to hide among the leaves and gifts and guard the nest. Crow's own feathers would one day grow back, and she'd build a new nest. Then her fledglings would come to reclaim the nest, fed on stories they'd see the dark leaves and recognize their mother's feathers.

But Feather-Crow had come from Crow, and knew that, for all the talk of adding others, the new nest would remain barren. Thus Feather-Crow made its vow. The new fledglings could come from wherever, and so long as they embodied the virtues of her friends in the forest, their gifts and magic would be shared with them in turn.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Fot C-6)</sup>

## Wing of the Crow
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/ON-place-The_Crow%27s_Labyrinthian_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Crow%27s_Labyrinthian_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Crow%27s_Labyrinthian_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Crow%27s_Labyrinthian_03.jpg) Wing of the Crow
The [Wing of the Crow](Lore_Wing_of_the_Crow.md) is a [pocket realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) belonging to the Luminary Crow. It was shaped and hewn into being in the same way she chose her form. The reason for the realm's formation was unknown, though the [Luminary Indrik](Lore_The_Indrik.md) theorized that the realms of the [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md) had formed due to the wisdom of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), which did not wish for the Luminaries to be both alone and wandering.
