# Ebony Blade

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg/300px-ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg) The Ebony Blade *Only a daedra most foul could have concocted such a malevolent and twisted weapon. But it appears that all who wield it end up with the crazed eyes of those wild men who roam the hills chattering with rabbits.*
— *[Admonition Against Ebony](Lore_Admonition_Against_Ebony.md)* The Ebony Blade, also known as the **Vampire** or **the Leech**, is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). It resembles an [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) katana, and is considered to be an artifact of great evil, having the ability to absorb the life essence of those that it strikes. Part of the damage inflicted flows into the wielder as raw power, draining the victim of their health and sometimes stamina. It has also been known to fortify the wielder's skill at using long blades, magically silence opponents to prevent them from casting spells, and deceive victims. Khajiiti myths do not mention the blade by name, but instead refer to it as 'the killing word of the Spider' and 'the black edge of shadow'.

The Blade has corrupted and perverted the desires of great men and women, and is infamous due to the many lives it has taken at the hands of its dark owners. It is popularly believed that all who wield the blade are driven insane, and some even believe that the souls of its wielders are consigned to Oblivion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a charm was cast on it by a mage so it would not remain with any one individual for long, in an attempt to save the souls of any too infatuated by the Blade.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOCOH-2)</sup>

## History
According to Khajiit mythology, sometime before the Khajiiti people recognized a [Mane](Lore_Mane.md), and before the city of Rim'kha would become known as [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rajhin Shadow-3)</sup> the thief god [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md)'s apotheosis began when he stole the Ebony Blade and the [Ring of Khajiit](Lore_Ring_of_Khajiiti.md) from [Mafala](Lore_Mephala.md) while the Prince was sleeping, though eventually both artifacts would be returned.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-4)</sup>

### Second Era
In the early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) named Nerien'eth uncovered the Ebony Blade while scouring [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for artifacts to study. He brought it to the [Agea Relle](Lore_Agea_Relle.md) for study, believing it to be an ancient [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) blade.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Mephala's whispers through the Blade slowly began to corrupt Nerien'eth's mind, until eventually he snapped and ensnared the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of his students and fellow researchers. He summoned [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to torture their souls for eternity, and shattered the soul of his wife Alanwe so that she would suffer the most. The Agea Relle became known as the [Crypt of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md), and was avoided by the people of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) for many years. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a group of [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) entered the Crypt of Hearts and cleared it of undead and Daedra, freeing the captured souls and restoring Alanwe's spirit.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOCOH-2)</sup>

Instead of departing for the afterlife, Alanwe stayed behind to protect the vault from Nerien'eth, who returned later that year. Using the Ebony Blade, Nerien'eth summoned minions of Mephala, powerful Daedra who aided him in recapturing the lost souls. The group of Undaunted returned, and once again aided Alanwe by investigating Nerien'eth's return. The group located the Ebony Blade in an underground ritual site connected to the Crypt of Hearts and destroyed Nerien'eth when he wielded it in battle. Alanwe's spirit then communed with Mephala, who attempted to corrupt her by promising to help destroy the Ebony Blade, but Alanwe refused to listen to the Prince. Instead, she used her own soul to place a charm on the Blade so that it would never remain with an individual for long, and then banished the Blade to an unknown location. According to Mephala, however, the Blade was fully empowered at the time and fought against Alanwe's will, and it yearned to return. However, Alanwe's sacrifice did deny Mephala the use of the Ebony Blade during a subsequent great war between the forces of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOCOH-2)</sup>

The significance of Nerien'eth led to his documentation within the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md) in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). There, copies of various entities were subsequently created as [maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), creatures composed of vivified ink manifested by stories from books, and served as mindless extensions of the [Daedric Lord](Lore_Daedric_Lord.md) [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md). Maligraphic copies of both Nerien'eth and the Ebony Blade were formed, which were later destroyed by adventurers aided by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAIA-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MM-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Enemies-8)</sup>

In the same year, [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) ventured to the Oblivion plane of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), where he discovered that someone intended to auction the Ebony Blade. Fyr, with the assistance of an adventurer, discovered that the item was a replica of the real artifact and punished the person responsible for the forgery.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WOL-9)</sup>

Following the construction of the Redoran Canton in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), the assassin Rivis of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) came into possession of the Ebony Blade. Knowing the danger it posed, he hid it away, keeping its existence a secret even from his fellow assassins. However, word of the Blade eventually reached Orndras, another member of the Tong, allegedly through whispers from Mephala herself. Though he submitted a detailed report of the incident, Orndras ultimately betrayed the organization, massacring all but one of his handlers and vanishing with the artifact. In response, the Morag Tong launched a mission to recover the Ebony Blade and capture Orndras, dead or alive.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

### Third Era
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) (circa [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) - [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399)), rumors of the Ebony Blade's return to Tamriel began to circulate throughout the provinces. Many believed these rumors to be reflective of a plunging moral climate and a sign of the times during these ten years, as the Ebony Blade was considered to be a vile - though admittedly powerful - weapon. The [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) eventually traced one of these rumors to a man attempting to sell information about the Ebony Blade in a tavern. This in itself was seen to be a significant indicator of how poorly Tamriel was faring during the Imperial Simulacrum, as many considered information as valuable as the Ebony Blade's whereabouts worthy of the richest rulers in Tamriel, yet the man was reduced to trying to sell his information in taverns. Upon meeting the Eternal Champion, the man warned them of the Ebony Blade's dire reputation before agreeing to sell them the location of the map leading to the Blade, a dungeon in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The Eternal Champion traveled to Morrowind and explored the lowest reaches of the dungeon in order to uncover the map, which revealed the location of the Ebony Blade itself: another dungeon, but outside of Morrowind. The Eternal Champion then traveled to this second dungeon and recovered the Blade.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup>

In the events leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) (circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405) – [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417)), an [agent](Lore_Hero.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) summoned Mephala and was tasked with assassinating a noble in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) area. In return, a worshipper of Mephala rewarded the agent with the Ebony Blade.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFMQ-11)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) summoned Mephala and was tasked with starting a feud between two prominent families, the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) Ulfgar and [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) Dalvilu, in the settlement of [Bleaker's Way](Lore_Bleaker%27s_Way.md). The Hero murdered the leaders of the two families and planted evidence which implicated the other. The settlement descended into chaos, and the two families fought until only one remained. Mephala then rewarded the Hero with the Blade.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBM-12)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Accounts of [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md)'s involvement differ, but it is said that in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176) she offered her assistance in killing her former minion Moivva Karnai, a Khajiit in service to the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) who she grew tired of. She is rumored to have offered to weaken her minion, or she may have granted the Ebony Blade or her own amulet to complete the assassination.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

Sometime after the end of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md) came across the Ebony Blade in their journey. The sword is thought to have been looted from an extravagant chest.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

Some time in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the Ebony Blade came into the possession of the Jarl of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md), who attempted to destroy it by placing it within the [Skyforge](Lore_Skyforge.md). Not even the hottest fires could melt it, and in fact the Blade seemed to cool the coals when it was placed within. As a last resort, the Blade was hidden away deep within Dragonsreach, with access granted only to the Jarl of Whiterun and his trusted court wizard.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AAE-15)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Nelkir, the young son of Jarl [Balgruuf the Greater](Lore_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md), stumbled upon the locked door to the chamber where the Blade was kept. Mephala spoke to the child through her artifact, whispering secrets through the door. Word spread through the city of the jarl's "dark child", and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) was tasked with helping the child. When questioned, Nelkir spoke of the "Whispering Lady." The Last Dragonborn investigated the "Whispering Door," and was contacted by Mephala. Under the guidance of Nelkir, the Last Dragonborn obtained a key to the door, and retrieved the Ebony Blade. However, having been locked away for so long, the weapon had lost much of its power. Mephala instructed the Last Dragonborn to nourish the Blade with the "blood of deceit" by murdering trusting friends.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SRTWD-16)</sup>
