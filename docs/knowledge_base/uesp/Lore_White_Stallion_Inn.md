## History
In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the inn was run by a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) innkeeper named Grelaith, who provided lodging and provisions to travelers along the Green Road.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONGrelaith-4)</sup> The most notable feature of the inn at this time was the large number of pets kept on the premises by the innkeeper.

By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the stable had disappeared for unknown reasons, and the inn itself had been converted to the White Stallion Lodge,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBWSL-2)</sup> a shelter and safehouse for the [Knights of the White Stallion](Lore_Knights_of_the_White_Stallion.md), a chivalric order operating in and around Leyawiin and chartered by the Count of Leyawiin.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBKot WS-3)</sup> In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), it became the residence of Mazoga the Orc, a former bandit turned knight<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBMt Oq-5)</sup> who was inducted into the Knights of the White Stallion by [Count Marius Caro](Lore_Count_Marius_Caro.md) after defeating Black Brugo, the notorious leader of the Black Bow Bandit gang, with the help of the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBKot WSq-6)</sup> Mazoga used the lodge as a base of operations for continued raids on the remaining members of the Black Bow Bandits.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBBBBq-7)</sup>
