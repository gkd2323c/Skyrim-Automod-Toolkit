[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Greymoor Keep is a large [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) castle located in [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md), the legendary subterranean realm of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It has stood over the underground since ancient history, long before the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) built their cities here. Some legends even claim that the first vampires built it and that great power resides in its stone.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLoad Greymoor-1)</sup> The keep is one of Blackreach's largest structures. It reaches to its ceiling and is even built directly underneath [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONFennorian-2)</sup> Not only is it the namesake of the [Greymoor Caverns](Lore_Greymoor_Caverns.md), the western section of Blackreach that runs under [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), and [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md), but also its northern region also called **Greymoor Cavern**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBlackreach-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3b/ON-furnishing-Greymoor_Keep_Banner%2C_Hanging.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Greymoor_Keep_Banner%2C_Hanging.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Greymoor_Keep_Banner,_Hanging.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Greymoor_Keep_Banner,_Hanging.jpg) Greymoor Keep's banner Greymoor's origins reside in myth and superstition. It is said that the first vampires laid down its stonework.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLoad Greymoor-1)</sup> According to legend, the first vampires are descendants of the original vampire, [Lamae Beolfag](Lore_Lamae_Beolfag.md), a [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) woman<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OLBTM-4)</sup> from the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), when her people roamed the modern northern provinces.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FC-5)</sup> She is also the progenitor of the vampiric strain, [Noxiphilic Sanguivoria](Lore_Noxiphilic_Sanguivoria_(disease).md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONVampirism-6)</sup> Whether this information applied to Greymoor Keep's supposed founders is unknown.

The keep predates the Dwemer's arrival into Skyrim,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLoad Greymoor-1)</sup> which was sometime after the [Rourken Clan](Lore_Rourken.md)'s flight from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AP-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1-8)</sup> Throughout history, Greymoor Keep has remained hidden in the most legendary place in Skyrim. It has even become a fable on its own.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONBanner-9)</sup> It took several years for [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md), who investigates anything related to vampires, to know about it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONGwendis-10)</sup> But based on their subsequent research, they learned that no one person actually owns the castle and that powerful individuals claim it from time to time.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONFennorian-2)</sup>

For the longest time, it was ruled by Lady [Essenia](Lore_Essenia.md), a master alchemist, and the keep's high chamberlain.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONGwendis-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RNCV-11)</sup> Under her reign, Greymoor Keep avoided schemes and politics from other vampire clans, like the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md), much to House Ravenwatch's surprise.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONGwendis-10)</sup> This was until she collaborated with the leader of the then revived [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md), [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she was recruited into his plans for the Gray Host and gave up her crown as Greymoor Keep's ruler. While she faced opposition from her elder kin, she preferred to study and pursue scientific endeavors.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RNCV-11)</sup>

Preparations were made after this change in leadership. Essenia wrote a dictate of renewal calling for Greymoor Keep's repair. One of these requests was to remove passable stone alcoves that were previously helpful during a [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) siege.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DOR-12)</sup> Using her influence, Essenia called every vampire in Skyrim to join the Gray Host in Greymoor<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONGwendis-10)</sup> while the Gray Host set up the keep for a powerful [harrowstorm](Lore_Harrowstorm.md), including having a field of [Stone Husks](Lore_Stone_Husk.md) to resurrect old Gray Host members.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONFennorian-2)</sup>

Rumors of harrowstorms and underground castles eventually reached House Ravenwatch, and two agents, Gwendis and Adusa-daro traveled to [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) to learn more. However, Adusa was captured and Lady Essenia kept her imprisoned for her unique bloodline related to [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md). Gwendis enlisted help from an [adventurer](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to enter Greymoor Keep and recover Adusa-daro. The two encountered Essenia in the keep's Cathedral of Blood and killed her after she turned into a shadowy [Harrowing Reaper](Lore_Harrowing_Reaper.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONLady-13)</sup>

Rada al-Saran's plans continued and culminated in a final harrowstorm targeted at Solitude, with Greymoor Keep as the witch's pike and High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md) as the one to control it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONFennorian-2)</sup> The Gray Host invaded the city through Greymoor Keep, directly under it. Four heroes; the Vestige, Princess [Svana](Lore_Svana.md), Fennorian of House Ravenwatch, and [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md) entered Greymoor Keep, climbing to the top and entered Castle Dour through the Temple of the Divines. From there, they defeated Svargrim at the eye of the harrowstorm and saved Western Skyrim.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONRising-14)</sup> What became of Greymoor Keep after that is unknown, but the Gray Host in Western Skyrim was defeated.
