## Tasker
The **Taskers**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IC-2)</sup><sup>:Part 2, Chapter 1</sup> or **Wormies** as they were unaffectionately called by the Imperial soldier Mazgar gra Yagash,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 6</sup> were the [reanimated](Lore_Undead.md) corpse warriors of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md), a floating city severed from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). They were individuals whose [souls](Lore_Soul.md) were stolen by [Soul-spinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul-spinner) lines, then animated by their ethereal flying larvae intergrating into their bodies. Due to this, reports from the [College of Whispers](Lore_College_of_Whispers.md) likened them more to [flesh atronachs](Lore_Flesh_Atronach.md) then more conventional reanimated undead like [bonewalkers](Lore_Bonewalker.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 2, Chapter 6</sup> In addition to killing inviduals to obtain more souls to power Umbriel, they were tasked with gathering raw materials and creatures to be used in Umbriel's kitchens. As such they can be summoned back to Umbriel using special incantations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IC-2)</sup><sup>:Part 2, Chapter 8</sup>

They were primarily composed of freshly dead corpses riddled with [maggots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maggot), hence the name.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 6</sup> They began to rise around Umbriel and follow its trajectory from its starting point of [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md) in southern [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), to the [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md) in north-central [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) in central [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 3</sup> Many of the wormies comprised of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) but the army consisted of anything that came in Umbriel's path. Even people who died while fighting them can be instantly reanimated into a wormy.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 1</sup>

Unlike the mindless zombies some would assume, they showed signs of intelligence and willful, malicious intent.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 1</sup> They were capable of speaking, albeit in a raspy tone. They also had no problem surviving underwater, whether it was in the Scathing Bay or [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 3, Chapter 3</sup> As they encroached on [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md), they successfully ambushed a group of legionnaires escorting people from Mountain Watch and intentionally stayed out of range from the city's siege weapons.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-3)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 6</sup>

**Found in:** - [The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Creatures#Notes), [Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Creatures#Notes)

## [Terror Bird](Lore_Terror_Bird.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/ON-creature-Terror_Bird.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Terror_Bird.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Terror_Bird.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Terror_Bird.jpg) A Topaz Terror Bird [Terror Birds](Lore_Terror_Bird.md), also known as **Khrasaat** in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md), are large, carnivorous and flightless [birds](Lore_Bird.md) found in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and parts of the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) (typically close to the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) border). They are very aggressive towards anyone who approaches them or their territory. Terror Birds have a communal nature when it comes to looking after their young and eggs with pack-like behavior, and will attack anything that comes near the nest without hesitation. Terror Birds are also extremely strong and are seen as a challenge by [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) warriors, in which the warriors die almost as often as they win against the beast, they have also been recorded taking down a [Senche-Cat](Lore_Senche-Cat.md).

Their diet consists of meat, from animals such as [antelope](Lore_Antelope.md) and sometimes men and mer, though the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) mistakenly seem to think that a Terror Bird's diet consists of only [moon-sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md) plants.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Terror_Bird), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Wily Kee'va)

## [Tharriker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tharriker)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-creature-Tharriker.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Tharriker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tharriker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tharriker.jpg) A Tharriker [Tharrikers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tharriker) (meaning **Forest Raiders** or **Forest Hunters** in [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md#Language)) are large, unnatural, and highly adaptable [senche](Lore_Senche.md) -like carnivores with four ears, feathery front legs, and [scorpion](Lore_Scorpion.md) -like tails. They were created by the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md)'s use of corrupted [Greenspeaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenspeaking) [magic](Lore_Magic.md), and sired in their "grow pits". Despite their origins they are capable of [reproduction](Lore_Sex.md) by laying eggs.

Tharrikers were first unleashed in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), appearing around the same time the Dawnwood forest did. They were often used to aid members of the Recollection in combat, with many also being affected by corruption from the [Wildburn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn). Tharrikers would quickly spread to other parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), some making it to the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). The wild magic that created them allowed for rapid adaptation to the snowy slopes of their new home, as such as a white-furred subspecies arose in this region.

A few were rescued from the Recollection's grow pits by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). These rescued tharrikers were then trained to be used as mounts and kept in [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md)'s stables. [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) of the [Rock Bone Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rock_Bone_Tribe) were also capable of training tharrikers. The white-furred subspecies of the Jerall Mountains would be domesticated by the war deserters that hid in the region.

- See also: [Chimera](Lore_Chimera.md) and [Mantikora](Lore_Mantikora.md)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Tharriker)

## Thunderbug
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-creature-Thunderbug.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Thunderbug.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Thunderbug.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Thunderbug.jpg) A thunderbug Thunderbugs are large [beetles](Lore_Beetle.md)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup> that are imbued with electricity, which they can use to defend themselves and their nests. They congregate in packs to feed off of their fellows' elemental energy.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-YH-11)</sup> Thunderbugs get their name from the shock they deliver when they bite. They can also spit tiny shock balls and call thunder from the sky to attack. They usually keep to themselves, and nest in soft dirt near trees and under ridges. They are fiercely protective of their eggs, which are sweet and feel tingly when swallowed.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BGIB-12)</sup> Thunderbugs hatch as maggots, which are essential in eliminating the more dangerous poisons in Rotmeth during the brewing process. Thunderbug hearts are also used to brew Rotmeth, as they give the brew a signature tang.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FEAS-13)</sup> The thunderbug's carapace, mandibles and chitin can be used to make a variety of goods.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Contraband-14)</sup>

## [Tiger](Lore_Tiger.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/56/LG-cardart-Senche-Tiger.png/200px-LG-cardart-Senche-Tiger.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Senche-Tiger.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Senche-Tiger.png) A Senche-Tiger [Tigers](Lore_Tiger.md) are large, predatory [felines](Lore_Cat.md) found mainly in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), as well as on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), and historically in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). They are known for their distinctive stripes. A female tiger is known as a **Tigress**.

Many of the cats known as tigers in southern Tamriel are actually a type of [Senche-Cat](Lore_Senche-Cat.md), known as a **Senche-Tiger** or **Senche-Tigress**, differentiated by their larger size. The two species may often be confused however due to their similar appearance. Senche-Tiger as a term has also been used to generally refer to any type of Senche-Cat, or to the [Senche](Lore_Senche_(furstock).md) or [Senche-raht](Lore_Senche-raht.md) furstocks of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).

Many Khajiit have tiger-like fur patterns, with [Pahmar](Lore_Pahmar.md) and [Pahmar-raht](Lore_Pahmar-raht.md) being known to particularly ressemble tigers and senche-tigers. The [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md) are described as a tiger-like race of cat-folk. Some [Sabre Cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md) resemble tigers so may be known as [sabertooth tigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sabertooth_Tiger).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Senche-Tiger). [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Senche-Tiger)

## [Troll](Lore_Troll.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-creature-Garden-Gulp.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Garden-Gulp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Garden-Gulp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Garden-Gulp.jpg) A troll [Trolls](Lore_Troll.md) (known as **Trohls** in ancient text) are large, [ape](Lore_Ape.md) -like, bestial humanoids endowed with three eyes. Known as formidable monsters throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), they have a powerful regeneration ability that makes them almost unkillable, but they also have a crippling weakness to [fire](Lore_Elements.md#Fire). Trolls are found all over the continent, mostly in caves and forested areas. They are typically reclusive creatures; their lairs are usually far from commonly traversed paths. They are also truly ancient creatures, having roamed Nirn since the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md). Despite this, the origin of trolls is completely unknown to scholars, and much about their [reproductive](Lore_Sex.md) biology is not well understood.

## [Turtle](Lore_Turtle.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/ON-furnishing-Seed_Doll%2C_Turtle.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Seed_Doll%2C_Turtle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Seed_Doll,_Turtle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Seed_Doll,_Turtle.jpg) A turtle doll [Turtles](Lore_Turtle.md) are shelled reptiles found in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and [Thras](Lore_Thras.md). They are able to swim and can float up to the surface of water. Newborn turtles are considered quite meek. The [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) slaughter and revive various sea creatures such as turtles and [crabs](Lore_Crab.md) to keep as pets. Turtle stew is a slow roasted dish.

Certain types of turtles are known as **tortoises**. Certain people have summoned giant sea turtles to aid them in sailing. [Haj Mota](Lore_Haj_Mota.md) and the [Mock Turtle](Lore_Mock_Turtle.md) are considered to be species of turtles.

[Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) have many sayings related to turtles, such as that one with a "turtle-heart" is "Hard on the outside, but soft on the inside". Another phrase is "The turtle walks slowly and wears armor on her back. Her soul is cautious, just as her body is cautious." [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) likened the [Psijic's](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) policies to a "bemused turtle" that lumbers along quietly, only occasionally poking its head above the grass to check for danger.

**Found in:** - Lore

## [Tzirzhalir](Lore_Tzirzhalir.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tzirzhalir.jpg) Tzirzhalir [Tzirzhalir](Lore_Tzirzhalir.md), the **nightmare behemoth**, was a powerful seemingly [undead](Lore_Undead.md) [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) entity that went by many names, and resembled a gargantuan [bone colossus](Lore_Bone_Colossus.md). The creature was described as profoundly evil. He was confined to the Pit of Sorrows at the heart of the Unhallowed Grave in [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). Aside from his massive size, he could unleash a wide cloud of freezing air with his breath and hurl flaming boulders.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Tzirzhalir)
