*The best thing about yaghra? We have many, many more.*
— K'Tora of the Abyssal Cabal Yaghra are a [race](Lore_Race.md) of deep-sea creatures, said to have been created from [crustacean](Lore_Crustacean.md) -like bottom feeders, that serve and revere the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESODS-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:219</sup> They live in [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)'s many seas and oceans, and are only rarely seen on land, having no names known to surface-dwellers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOCaptain-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:219</sup> They have varying morphologies and are said to be followers of an unspoken social hierarchy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:219</sup> The Sea Sload address their yaghra servants through a combination of the guttural [Sload language](Lore_Sload_(language).md) and [mind magic](Lore_Mind_Magic.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IJA-4)</sup> They are even capable of being taught magic themselves by their masters.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Alt-5)</sup>

Yaghra are fear-inducing to the point that they are alleged to make [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) "seem like purring [kittens](Lore_Cat.md)" by comparison.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOCaptain-3)</sup> Their strange appearance often garners comparisons to [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), though they are unrelated.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESODS-1)</sup>

## History

### Origins
The first Yaghra Larva were created from a species of crustacean-like bottom-feeders that had a symbiotic relationship with other sea life.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:219</sup> When they were created exactly is unknown, as the inhabitants of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) were largely unfamiliar with the creatures.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESODS-1)</sup> However [Ritemaster Iachsesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) recalled encountering the creatures in the service of the Sea Sloads sometime around [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis-6)</sup>

### Summerset Invasion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a0/ON-prerelease-Abyssal_Geyser_02.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Abyssal_Geyser_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Abyssal_Geyser_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Abyssal_Geyser_02.jpg) A group of Yaghra at an Abyssal Geyser Circa 2E 582, the Abyssal Cabal used the Yaghra as soldiers during their attack on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). They were brought to Summerset from deep beneath the waves with the aid of Abyssal Pearls. These objects were used in ritual sites known as Abyssal Geysers as part of the Cabal's attempts to sink the island.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IJA-4)</sup> Attracted by the Abyssal Pearls, Yaghra were summoned and wreaked havoc on the shores of Summerset.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PRN-7)</sup> One member of the Cabal, the Sea Sload K'Tora, oversaw these Geysers until he was killed by the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). The [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) subsequently assumed control of the Geysers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOBM-8)</sup> During this time, Yaghra could be found on the shores of Summerset, particularly near the [Abyssal Geysers](Lore_Abyssal_Geysers.md), as they were drawn to the power of the Pearls that were located at the ritual sites.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PRN-7)</sup>

Other members of the Cabal would utilize yaghra in unique ways. The alchemist [B'Korgen](Lore_B%27Korgen.md), attempted to use alchemy to empower the other members of the Abyssal Cabal and transform the yaghra into even more fearsome creatures. The primary ingredient of his concoctions was a form of corrupted [coral](Lore_Coral.md). His plans were thwarted when he was killed by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JF-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TAA-10)</sup> During Z'Maja's incursion of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md), the Sea Sload used shadow magic to summon umbral Yaghra to defend herself.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-WOTW-11)</sup>

### Alternative Outcome
The Psijic Order had the means to scry secrets from [fate](Lore_Fate.md) lines beyond the bounds of Tamriel. Among these secrets were tomes that spoke of [alternative timelines and realities](Lore_Many_Paths.md), where certain key events transpired differently.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

In one of these alternate realities, the Abyssal Cabal succeeded in conquering the Summerset Isles. B'Korgen was said to have taken Yaghra apprentices, with them toiling alongside him everyday, learning the art of [fleshcrafting](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Alt-5)</sup>

## Social Hierarchy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/ON-creature-Yaghra_Monstrosity.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Yaghra_Monstrosity.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Yaghra_Monstrosity.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Yaghra_Monstrosity.jpg) Yaghra Monstrosity (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-creature-Yaghra_Spewer_04.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Yaghra_Spewer_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Yaghra_Spewer_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Yaghra_Spewer_04.jpg) Yaghra Spewer (ESO) There are several varieties of yaghra. **Yaghra Larva** are similar in appearance to [spiders](Lore_Spider.md), and are about the size of [Mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md). They are, of course, the larval stage of the creature.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> They have (or had) a symbiotic relationship with other sea life. Larvae serve as workers, peons and minions, and are low in the Yaghra social hierarchy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:219</sup>

**Yaghra Monstrosities** are large Yaghra that are similar in body structure and shape to the larvae, but have hard exoskeletons, [crab](Lore_Crab.md) -like claws, and carry large shells on their backs like [hermit crabs](Lore_Hermit_Crab.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Monstrosities are steadfast commanders. They produce Larval Yaghra, and overwhelm the battlefield through rapid reproduction. They have an almost unnatural yet commanding bond with their lesser brethren.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:220</sup>

The **Yaghra Strider** has a spined exoskeleton and attacks with crab-like claws.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Striders are extremely aggressive. They show an impressive amount of loyalty to the greater Monstrosities, whom they view as their leaders. They also appear to revere the Sea Sload as their gods. Striders act as soldiers, defending the Yaghra nests.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:219</sup>

The last known variety is the **Yaghra Spewer**, which have bioluminescent shells and possess poison sacks in their necks, which they use to spew a corrosive substance. It is this ability for which they are named.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOSpewer-16)</sup> Spewers share a similar social standing with Striders, but exhibit a more conservative ranged combat style. They appear to call out targets for Striders to focus on, and are intent on creating dangerous areas on the battlefield for their enemies to avoid.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOSCESG-2)</sup><sup>:220</sup> They do this by spewing their poison, which pools on the ground and is harmful to stand in.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOSpewer-16)</sup>
