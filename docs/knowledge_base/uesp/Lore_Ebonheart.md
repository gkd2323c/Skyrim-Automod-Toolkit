—Partial in-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Ebonheart (also called **Old Ebonheart**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONContraband-1)</sup> is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup> and is located in the center of [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONEbonheart-3)</sup> The city is considered the eldest city-state in the province and is more commonly known as the ancestral seat of the [Ra'athim Clan](Lore_Ra%27athim.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup> described by the potentially fictional *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)* book series as an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) miners.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KEVII-5)</sup> Some members are well-known across Tamriel, such as [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md), called the "Champion of Tamriel" in the *King Edward* book series,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KEII-6)</sup> and [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) Empress-Regent of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TMOP-7)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/ON-place-Hlaalu_House_%28town_hall%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hlaalu_House_%28town_hall%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hlaalu_House_(town_hall).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hlaalu_House_(town_hall).jpg) The Ebonheart Town Hall [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/ON-place-Ebonheart_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ebonheart_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ebonheart_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ebonheart_02.jpg) The Argonian Quarter of Ebonheart Ebonheart is found on the north-central coast of Stonefalls, in a strategic location on the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONEbonheart-3)</sup> The city was the seat of power of a country led by the Ra'athim Clan, one that spanned many mining fiefdoms and encompassed what became modern-day Mournhold. Historically, the Ra'athim Clan's parent faction, [House Mora](Lore_House_Mora.md) was absorbed into the greater [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), which places it in Great House's district.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup> But in the mid-Second Era, the city was affiliated with [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), whose leadership was centralized in the Stonefalls region via [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONEbonheart-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIEGM-8)</sup> Ebonheart is neighbored by several settlements, including [Darnim Watch](Lore_Darnim_Watch.md) to the east, [Eagle Moor](Lore_Eagle_Moor.md) to the southeast, [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) to the east-southeast, Old Keep to the far southeast, [Riverbridge](Lore_Riverbridge.md) to the southwest, and [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls_(settlement).md) to the northwest.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup>

Beyond the walls lies the ash wastes and the ridged hills of the mountains, where only the local [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) and mining prospects flourish. The majority of the city proper is built on an island, nestled in the large river basin next to the inactive volcanic spire, [Ash Mountain](Lore_Ash_Mountain.md), and the aforementioned river, which comes from the south.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONEbonheart-3)</sup> While some maps show the river going as far south as south-central Morrowind, near [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Thir)</sup> others indicate that it starts in the mountain valley called the [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(place).md) and descends downward into the river basin.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONMap-9)</sup> For the longest, [Dreughs](Lore_Dreugh.md) were common around Ebonheart, [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and the surrounding waters but have been driven out over centuries and now live in small, isolated pockets.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-NOTD-10)</sup>

The city is surrounded by large stone walls and its exterior is open land where people tend to set up encampments. At the center of the city is the seat of power, Castle Ebonheart, which sits on a stone foundation that overlooks the waterfront and the local Argonian Quarter. The south side of the castle contains the local city plaza and parkland, right in the middle of the main street that is anchored by the local Temple to the west and the local cornerclub to the east. Around the castle are two steep staircases that lead into the Argonian Quarters, lowland with wooden planks and ashreeds on the side of the road. At the center of it all lies their Hist Tree.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONEbonheart-3)</sup> According to the historical-fiction, *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)*, the Tribune of Sorcery, [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) has a hidden laboratory in the smoky catacombs underneath the city, where he "forged the future with his arcane [clockwork apparatus](Lore_Clockwork_City.md)."<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-2920V12-11)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-place-Charred_Ridge_%28Ebonheart%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Charred_Ridge_%28Ebonheart%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Charred_Ridge_(Ebonheart).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Charred_Ridge_(Ebonheart).jpg) Vision of Ebonheart from the early First Era (ESO) Ebonheart is considered the eldest city-state in Morrowind and its rivalry with [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) to the south has existed since the dawn of the province's history. Before they were separate settlements, they were part of one fief led by the Ra'athim Clan. In these early days, the clan was associated with [House Mora](Lore_House_Mora.md), a minor house that eventually became part of House Hlaalu.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup> When the [Conquest of Morrowind](Lore_Conquest_of_Morrowind.md) took place in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), many places such as Ebonheart were taken over by the Nords. In the potentially fictional *King Edward* book series, [Kronin](Lore_Kronin.md) and his sons [Cruethys](Lore_Cruethys.md), [S'ephen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:S%27ephen), and [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md) became raiders and took up guerrilla warfare to fight against their oppressors.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KEX-15)</sup> The Nords held the city for a century and a half<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KEXII-16)</sup> but its original rulers returned to their post after Resdayn had been liberated in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KEX-15)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3-17)</sup>

According *King Edward*, sometime after the conflict, Ebonheart was ruled by King Cruethys, who later died when the council chambers collapsed onto him.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KEXII-16)</sup> His brother, Moraelyn took up his role afterward, and in his possession were the [Horn of Summoning](Lore_Horn_of_Summoning.md) and the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md); items of immense power. He placed these artifacts under Ephen's protection and it was hence bound to his tomb underneath Mournhold. Moraelyn's daughter, Lian took his place later and her sons were left as joint heirs to the throne. The schism left both Ebonheart and Mournhold as separate states.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup>

### Ebonheart in the Second Era
By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Ebonheart was nominally ruled by [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONDrathus-18)</sup> while other houses had a significant presence in town, such as House Hlaalu and [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md). In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), after the [Battle of Vivec's Antlers](Lore_Battle_of_Vivec%27s_Antlers.md), which ended the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md), leaders of the defending [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md), Dunmer, and Nord forces gathered in Ebonheart to formulate their alliance now known as the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-UA-19)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) invaded the Stonefalls region and landed in Vivec's Antlers to the west of the city, where they planned their invasion of the city led by Alexandra Conele. At the time, three factions were present in Ebonheart, the Dark Elves, the Argonians, and the Nords visiting from [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) but each of them was unwilling to fight together and defend the city. Drathus Othral decided to employ an [Agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Pact to reconcile these factions and help them with whatever problem ails them. Over the course of their quest, they learned that a Dark Elf named Rhavil Urano was responsible for their troubles and discovered their allegiance to the Daggerfall Covenant. With the threat neutralized, Ebonheart was able to band together for an impending invasion.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONOrder-20)</sup>

### Ebonheart in the Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png/105px-AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Staff_of_Chaos.png) Staff of Chaos [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/AR-item-Hammer_of_Gharen.png/200px-AR-item-Hammer_of_Gharen.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Hammer_of_Gharen.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Hammer_of_Gharen.png) The Hammer of Gharen After the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) had been signed, the old noble families of Morrowind were gone, and new people, some of whom were upstarts like [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) attained power over the land. While [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) took the [Horn of Summoning](Lore_Horn_of_Summoning.md) from Ephen's tomb, he left the Staff of Chaos in Morrowind's protection. Ebonheart argued against this decision by using their status as the ruling house to get the staff. But Mournhold reminded them it was Moraelyn, who left it in Ephen's protection and Mournhold was undeniably his birthplace.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup>

Symmachus, with help from a rag-tail end of the Ra'athim Clan, hid the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) in Ephen's tomb, where it remained for several years. For that service, he was granted the kingship of Ebonheart and continued to rule there for many years.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRB4-12)</sup> Over time, however, the kingdom became jealous that the staff was kept in Mournhold instead of with them. According to the historical fiction, *"The Real Barenziah"*, the councilors in Ebonheart picked at the city's bones and sucked them dry, something that Symmachus wanted to prevent for Mournhold.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup>

Over the course of her career, Queen [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude_(kingdom).md) gathered allies across the northern tier of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), from High Rock to northern Morrowind,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWQ6-22)</sup> which included Ebonheart. When the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) commenced, the city rallied its arms and fought the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). It was a poorly-kept secret at the time that they supported Potema and her son, [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_III.md). Since then, the Empire slowly healed as Emperor [Magnus Septim](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) spent much of his reign mending diplomatic wounds created by the war. Scholars believe that, as a means to cement relations with Ebonheart, he proposed the marriage between his son, [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_III.md) and the heiress to the Ra'athim Clan, [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TMOP-7)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Ebonheart was an active settlement. It was ruled by King [Casik](Lore_Casik.md), who acquired a reputation for his extreme ruthlessness at the time. Ebonheart had a rivalry with several other cities, namely [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md), [Firewatch](Lore_Firewatch.md), [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md), and [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-AR-23)</sup> In the latter years of this decade, the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) traveled to Morrowind to seek out the lost mines of [Dagoth-Ur](Lore_Dagoth-Ur_(place).md), where the last piece of the Staff of Chaos was located. Local rumors eventually brought them before King Casik, who offered the dungeon's whereabouts in exchange for a quest.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ARDagoth-24)</sup>

If they were to retrieve the lost [Hammer of Gharen](Lore_Hammer_of_Gharen.md) from [Black Gate](Lore_Black_Gate.md), then he could strike on the [Anvil of Mithas](Lore_Anvil_of_Mithas.md) and thus have it reveal Dagoth-Ur's location. Just as he predicted, Casik was able to procure the dungeon's location and with it, the Eternal Champion was able to find the last piece of the Staff.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ARDagoth-24)</sup> With knowledge of the mine's location, King Casik hoped to harvest raw metals from the mountain and unearth the lost art of Dwarven [smithcraft](Lore_Smithing.md) for his people to create the greatest weapons in the realm.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ARCasik-25)</sup> Ever since the Eternal Champion defeated [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), many subjects of the Empire partake in a pilgrimage across the continent, retracing their steps from the quest. Loyalist pilgrims make their way through Ebonheart.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-AMM-26)</sup>

### Known Rulers
*"first among equals"*
—A quote oft-expressed by Ebonheart's rulers<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup> For many years, Ebonheart was ruled by the Ra'athim Clan, a family that once held the role of High Kingship in the province.<sup>[[Note 2]](#intnote_High_King)</sup> They were affiliated with the minor House of Mora, which was eventually absorbed into the Great [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRB4-12)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-IWTB-27)</sup> Over the course of history, other people had control over the city. For a century and a half, the Nords of Skyrim held Ebonheart during the Conquest of Morrowind. Who led at this time is currently unknown, but according to the *King Edward* book series, the Ra'athim Clan retook it<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KEX-15)</sup> after the Nords were defeated in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3-17)</sup>

Their territory encompassed Ebonheart and Mournhold until joint heirs were appointed to each respective city and since then they have been separate entities. At the time they ruled, the High Kingship was also vacant when a temporary war leader was elected.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup> In the mid-Second Era, House Dres governed the city from the manor in the center of town<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONDrathus-18)</sup> but by the Third Era, the Ra'athim Clan returned to the throne.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TMOP-7)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRB4-12)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-quest-Lava_Foot_Stomp_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Lava_Foot_Stomp_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Lava_Foot_Stomp_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Lava_Foot_Stomp_04.jpg) The Lava Foot Stomp [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/ON-place-Shrine_of_Vivec.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shrine_of_Vivec.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_of_Vivec.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_of_Vivec.jpg) The Shrine of Vivec
- *For more information, see the [Culture](Lore_Dunmer.md#Culture) and [Society](Lore_Dunmer.md#Society) sections on the Dunmer page.*

Ebonheart is known by a couple of other names. Colloquially it is called the **Black City**, which mirrors one of Vvardenfell's names, the Black Isle.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-AR-23)</sup> [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) also referred to the city as the **One True Ebonheart**.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ROAFS-31)</sup> A local dish in Ebonheart are blackened [snake](Lore_Snake.md) hearts.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONElam-32)</sup> Unlike most other cities, Ebonheart is open-minded when it comes to interring dead outlanders, though it is more likely to find a tomb with multiple names on a plaque than a private grave.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LAHT-33)</sup>

One fateful day, Vivec visited Ebonheart in the mid-Second Era and became enthralled by its architecture, wandering its streets for hours and studying every bit of it. He developed a desire to have it moved or built in a spot south of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) in Vvardenfell. The Archcanon at the time, [Tarvus](Lore_Tarvus.md) wrote to the Tribunal Temple and House Hlaalu engineers about Vivec's inquiries about a new project.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-OME-34)</sup> While the outcome of this is unknown, the Third Empire would build their own fort-town west of Vivec's Palace also called [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md) after the trade and settlement ban on Vvardenfell was uplifted.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ASHOM-35)</sup>

The mainland city has reverence for the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md). The three gods had previously gone through Ebonheart on their annual pilgrimage to Vvardenfell.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ONSildras-36)</sup> Shrines to each of the gods anchor the city in three points, with Vivec and [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md)'s shrines on the southwest and east corners respectively, while [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s shrine marks the northernmost point of the city.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONNight-37)</sup> Castle Ebonheart at the center of town is inspired by the Tribunal as the three-spired structure is emblematic of this.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ATGTC-13)</sup>

For a short time, the court of Ebonheart declared dancing to be illegal and the locals protested by inventing the Lava Foot Stomp, which they called a necessity if their boots ever catch flame. They would practice it in taverns while wearing a dazzling pin. Since then, it has become a [New Life](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md) tradition and practiced across Stonefalls, from Ebonheart to [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONBreda-38)</sup> *"The Real Barenziah"* associates the [Nightingale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nightingale) with Ebonheart and the Ra'athim Clan.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB5-4)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Thir) | The river that comes north toward Ebonheart and empties into the Inner Sea is called the River Thir in the concept map for the [TES III: Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind). But since the name does not appear in any official capacity, it cannot be included in the main text of the lore page. The river was much like its appearance in Arena however, where it extended south until it reached the vicinity of Narsis, past Riverbridge.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-39)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_High_King) | While *"[the Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md)"* claims that the Ra'athim Clan held the High Kingship so early in Morrowind's history, dialogue in regards to King [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md)'s death suggest that the idea of Kings is a western creation and that the Dunmer have never really acknowledged one.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-MWGen Llethan-40)</sup> The Empire revived the title of King for Athyn Llethan from early Chimer traditions of a "high chief of the clans", like the High Kingship of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-KRP-41)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Ebonheart's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Reavers".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-42)</sup> *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)* brought back many of these arena teams to compete in an event known as the Dragon Games, but renamed Ebonheart's arena team into the "Ebonheart Serpents".<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-43)</sup>
