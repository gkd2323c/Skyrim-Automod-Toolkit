## [Palla](Lore_Palla_(creature).md)
[Palla](Lore_Palla_(creature).md) is the name of a misshapen humanoid beast, who was said to have escaped from a mad wizard's laboratory and went on to menace [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). In appearance it is described as having taloned hands, and as being large, misshapen, and monstrous. It is apparently intelligent and capable of speech. It was slain by the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) warrior Xarlys, who herself died in the struggle against it. Xarlys would become well-known in [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) for slaying the beast.

Palla was accidentally resurrected by a member of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), who had become infatuated with Xarlys after viewing a statue commemorating her battle with Palla. The mage had been confused and thought that the warrior's name was Palla, and had used a strange [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) artifact to try and revive her, constantly whispering the name in affection. When the mage realized his mistake, Palla found him, also appearing to have affection for him. The fate of the two is unknown.

All that is known of Palla is written in a [published tale](Lore_Palla.md) of the same name, authored by [Vojne Mierstyyd](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Vojne_Mierstyyd), who may be the mage from the story.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_Palla.md)

## [Pangrit](Lore_Pangrit.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/ON-creature-Pangrit.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Pangrit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Pangrit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Pangrit.jpg) A pangrit [Pangrits](Lore_Pangrit.md) are bipedal marine creatures, described as resembling a cross between a [guar](Lore_Guar.md) and a [shark](Lore_Shark.md). They are native to islands of the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md), being found primarily in the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md) and the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md). They are known to posses chitinous scales on parts of their body.

The origins of Pangrits is unknown. Some believe pangrit were creations of the [Hadolids](Lore_Hadolid.md), who are by far the most prolific race to tame and train them; often keeping their company and using them as beasts of warfare and burden. Others refute this idea, citing the animal's lack of meaningful intelligence as proof they are naturally occurring, or at least were simply found by hadolids.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Pangrit)

## Pariah Scamp
Pariah Scamps are tiny [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) from [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md)'s [Ashpit](Lore_Ashpit.md), described as small, black, foul-smelling [homonculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Homonculus). They possess a unique power called Pariah's Vision Diversion, which causes mortals not to notice them, as mortals look everywhere except where the scamps are. A mortal can temporarily gain this power by eating a pariah scamp.

The [magic](Lore_Magic.md) used to [summon](Lore_Conjuration.md) them is fairly unreliable. Scrolls prepared for the summons must be used within a day of inscription. The binding is also volatile and subject to Aetheric decay. The scamps can apparently be summoned in a dormant state, which is useful for those looking to store them until they need to eat one. Pariah scamps should be stored deep underground and far away from each other as a precaution to prevent their proximity to one another from awakening them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

- See also: [Scamp](Lore_Scamp.md)

**Found in:** - [Lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naryu%27s_Journal_Wrothgar)

## Parrapton

Riders powder their hands and rub them together to signal to their parrapton, which depending on context, will signal when they are to fly or land. A parrapton is no longer a drone when the queen selects a mate.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPThe Blue Dawn-4)</sup>

## [Pixie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pixie)
[Pixies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pixie) (known as **Whilloki** to [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md)) are a type of [faerie](Lore_Faerie.md) described as appearing as glimmering sparks with the personality of whimsical pranksters. Ahrtabazus included them in his controversial "Faerie Chain" theory from [2E 456](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_456), which connected all the types of fay in a hierarchal chain. The chain began with simple pixies and ended with the faeries considered to be "god-like". Most controverisally, he included Elves as part of this chain, placing just above above whilloki and below [nephrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nephrine). Many Elves found it offensive to be placed only slightly above whimsical pranksters such as whillwoki.

Several small or flame-related creatures have been compared to pixies. Flame pixies are a tiny form of [Flame Atronach](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md). The Magma Queen was a giant [Nixad](Lore_Nixad.md), another form of fay, that served as a champion of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) and was referred to as a pixie. The burrowing pixie owl is a rare species of [owl](Lore_Owl.md) native to western Tamriel.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_The_Faerie.md)

## [Plankton](Lore_Plankton.md)
[Plankton](Lore_Plankton.md) are tiny organisms that float in water, classified as either [animals](Lore_Bestiary.md) or [plants](Lore_Flora.md).

[Septimus Signus](Lore_Septimus_Signus.md) referenced plankton metaphorically in his *[Ruminations on the Elder Scrolls](Lore_Ruminations_on_the_Elder_Scrolls.md)*. He sought to illustrate one of the origins of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) by imagining a life beneath the waves, endowed with sharp vision and surrounded by beautiful fabric. By placing the fabric over one's gills, one would begin to breathe in and absorb its threads. While the plant fibers nourish the [soul](Lore_Souls.md), "the wretched plankton would pollute the cloth until it stank to heavens of [prophecy](Lore_Sight.md)". He posed the question of whether one is the sea, the breather, or the fabric, or if one is the breath itself.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_Ruminations_on_the_Elder_Scrolls.md)

[Hollowjack](Lore_Hollowjack.md) is a Pumpkin Spectre and a [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) who rules over Detritus. Those seen in Detritus display very disturbing behavior, such as playing kick-ups with skulls, dancing maniacally, and cannibalism. They are not inherently hostile to visitors, showing a propensity to spook them with sudden scares and shushing them when witnessing their devious acts.

Hollowjack sometimes summons these creatures to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the Witches Festival to torment mortals. Visions of these nightmarish beings are also summoned by hedge wizards during the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md), a holiday that takes place on the 13th of [Frostfall](Lore_Frostfall.md) each year.

## [Purified](Lore_Purified.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg/200px-ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Narilmor.jpg) King Narilmor, an ancient Ayleid willingly purified by Meridia The [Purified](Lore_Purified.md) are beings that have been lustrated for purification by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). For mortals, this usually involves being stripped of their free will and bestowed with the gift of immortality for the purpose of serving her for all eternity. The Purified are imbued with a golden radiance within their bodies that causes them to emit a glow from beneath their skin. Beings who join the ranks of the Purified include those who willingly pledge themselves to Meridia's service, as well the unwilling, such as those who defy her. They are also referred to as **Lustrants** by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) minions, though this is sometimes used as a catch-all term to refer to Meridia's followers.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:DLC#Wrathstone)

## [Pygmy](Lore_Pygmy.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/SI-item-Pygmy_Skull.jpg/200px-SI-item-Pygmy_Skull.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-item-Pygmy_Skull.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-item-Pygmy_Skull.jpg) A supposed pygmy skull [Pygmies](Lore_Pygmy.md) are a diminutive humanoid group, some of which supposedly inhabited the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Though the pygmies of the Isles are said to have all been eaten by the [Grummites](Lore_Grummite.md). Pygmies are known to the inhabitants of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region, and referred to in direct contrast with [giants](Lore_Giant.md).

The skeletal remains of pygmies can be found scattered throughout the Shivering Isles. The skull of a pygmy was one of the objects on display at the Museum of Oddities in [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). The word pygmy is used in Tamriel to describe comparatively small creatures, such as with the [pygmy sunfish](Lore_Sunfish.md) and [weeping pygmy shark](Lore_Shark.md).

**Found in:** - [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Pygmy_Skull)
