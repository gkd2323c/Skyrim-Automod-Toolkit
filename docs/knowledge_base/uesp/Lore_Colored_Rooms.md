[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/ON-place-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg) The Colored Rooms The Colored Rooms are a many-faceted realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), known as the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Infinite Energies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCR-1)</sup> From what little has been seen of the Colored Rooms, they seem to resemble a cross between a [coral reef](Lore_Coral.md) and a vast field of floating stones, strewn with colorful trails of dust or cloud. The "ground" between the stones looks like luminescent water, but is solid enough to walk on. The skies are described as floating effluvium oceans of crimson.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Online-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ETLR-3)</sup> It has been suggested that the "colors" of the realms are a reference to the different schools of [magic](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GT-4)</sup>

[Aurorans](Lore_Auroran.md) and their warhorses are the spawn of Meridia that reside in the Colored Rooms, being made from pure light and have skin tones of virtually any hue.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONCostumes-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONWarhorse-6)</sup> [Golden Saints](Lore_Aureal.md) are also purported to reside in the realm. The Colored Rooms have been described by those souls who have been there as "pretty boring" aside from Meridia's lectures (which are described as "pretty boring" as well).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONDarien-7)</sup>

## History
The Colored Rooms were formed when Meridia fled [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) with her fellow [Magna-Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md). However, instead of fleeing to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), Meridia went into the [Void](Lore_Void.md), where she was chided by the Daedric Princes for her trespass. Bending the light of [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md), Meridia then carved her own sphere from the chaos of the Void by an act of "sheer divine will", creating a new [plane of Oblivion](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCR-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EOMN-8)</sup>

The half-Ayleid king [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md) was defeated by [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and was cast adrift in the "waters of Oblivion", having made an alliance with Meridia granting him immortality. He spent many thousands of years reforming in the Colored Rooms before he could return to Mundus.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-KOTN-9)</sup>

A rift to the Colored Rooms opened within Scrivener's Hall on 1st Hearthfire, [2E 570](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_570), and closed a year later on 1st Hearthfire, [2E 571](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_571).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MRT-10)</sup>

During an earlier confrontation with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) in the Planar Vortex, [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md) was caught up in the magical spell of a [Light of Meridia](Lore_Light_of_Meridia.md) and was transported to the Colored Rooms.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONDarien-7)</sup> When the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) defeated [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Meridia briefly brought them to the Colored Rooms to reclaim their soul, where Molag Bal addressed them and stated the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) was over.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Online-2)</sup> Darien later returned to Tamriel as Meridia's "Golden Knight", a vessel of her power,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONDarien-7)</sup> and after he was abducted by [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), [The Triad](Lore_The_Triad.md) invaded the Colored Rooms.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONMeridia-11)</sup> When the invasion was over, enemy combatants were rounded up and [purified](Lore_Purified.md), with some being banished to Nirn.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Lus Dae-12)</sup> [King Narilmor](Lore_Narilmor.md), reincarnated as the Symphony of Blades, fought off a group of Undaunted in the realm but was defeated, resulting in the retrieval of the right half of the [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

## Notes
- In a speech to the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) incorrectly names Meridia's realm as [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), which is actually the realm of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Oblivion-14)</sup> The name of the realm first appeared in the *[Imperial Census of Daedra Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Imperial_Census_of_Daedra_Lords)*.
