# Ascendant Order

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/ON-prerelease-Ascendant_Order.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Ascendant_Order.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Ascendant_Order.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Ascendant_Order.jpg) The Ascendant Order The Ascendant Order was a pan-Tamrielic movement<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOGR-1)</sup> turned terrorist organization that attempted to assassinate the major players responsible for the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Online-2)</sup> This war caused common folk across the continent whose lives were ruined by the destruction it caused to flock to the order.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTAP-3)</sup> They were drawn in by leaders of the faction, the Ascendant Lord and his Ascendant Magus, who promised them that they would bring down Tamriel's tyrants who grow richer and powerful at the expense of misery and bloodshed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MTCALM-4)</sup> [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) -influenced, the Ascendant Order likened themselves to a [Knightly Order](Lore_Breton_Knightly_Orders.md), though most legitimate Orders thought of them as nothing more than well-armed fanatics.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOGR-1)</sup> Members of the organization concealed their identities and united under a share goal of toppling Tamriel's corrupt governments. They saw themselves not as bandits, but as knights that strike at tyranny from the shadows.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM111-5)</sup> The Order had been generally non-hostile until the peace talks between [the](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) [three](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) [alliances](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) at [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), where they attempted to slay the faction leaders.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Online-2)</sup> By this point, they had infiltrated nearly every level of Breton society,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONU34-6)</sup> and had established the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres.md) as their main base of operations.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Online-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-trailer-Ascendant_Order.png/200px-ON-trailer-Ascendant_Order.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Ascendant_Order.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Ascendant_Order.png) A crowd of Ascendant Order Followers The Ascendant Order was created by [Lord Bacaro Volorus](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md), who lost his partner and children during the conflict of the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md). He found some footing during his grief when he turned to philanthropy, and donated from his fortune to charities. He founded the [Society of the Steadfast](Lore_Society_of_the_Steadfast.md), a humanitarian organization as a response to the Three Banners War, which offered aid to the common folk affected by the continent-wide war.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-So ST-7)</sup> In truth, Lord Bacaro had planned to leverage that goodwill he had built overtime to lure the major players responsible for the Three Banners War to their deaths.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

Bacaro's identity as the Ascendant Lord was unmasked by [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md). His ultimate goal was to become the new [Druid King](Lore_Druid_King.md) and bring an era of destruction to Tamriel, replacing the fallen [Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) with his own "Ivy Throne". The Vestige confronted him at [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md), defeating Volorus and saving the archipelago from destruction.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-THLord-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIThrone-9)</sup>

Although the peace talks were initially a trap set by Lord Bacaro, the Steadfast Society were kept in the dark about his plans and had no involvement with the Ascendant Order. Even with Lord Bacaro gone, they continued his original vision of helping those suffering from war and disaster. As Lord Bacaro had set up a trust many years ago, the society was self-sufficient, and they continued to sponsor the peace talks and take care of all the expenses.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

The significance of the Ascendant Order allowed them to be documented within the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md) in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). There, copies of them were subsequently created as [maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), creatures composed of vivified ink manifested by stories from books, and serve as mindless extensions of the [Daedric Lord](Lore_Daedric_Lord.md) [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LAIA-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MM-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Enemies-13)</sup>

### Alternative Fate
The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) had the means to scry secrets from fate lines beyond the bounds of Tamriel. Among these secrets were tomes that spoke of [alternative timelines and realities](Lore_Many_Paths.md), where certain key events transpired differently.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> In one of these alternate realities, Bacaro defeated his other enemies and ruled over Tamriel from atop his Ivy Throne in Mount Firesong in a new [druidic](Lore_Druid.md) reign. With his victory, Tamriel was engulfed in firestorms targeting "false kings and queens", and those displaced by them were assisted by the Ascendant Order, Society of the Steadfast, and reformed Druids of Galen. Dark and cold days filled with turmoil were the norm during this transition, as volcanoes continued to "belch forth their retribution" covering the skies, but according to Bacaro's subordinates, the hardships would pass and the Ascendant Lord would uplift Tamriel's citizens.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
