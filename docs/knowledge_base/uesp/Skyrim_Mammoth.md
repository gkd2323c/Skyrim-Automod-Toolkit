[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Mammoth.jpg) A wild Mammoth **Mammoths** are hulking creatures found roaming the plains around [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) in Skyrim, where their bones frequently decorate the landscape. They are enormous beasts, with shaggy coats of fur and long tusks. They are generally found near [giant camps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Giant_Camps), or traveling under the protection of [giants](Skyrim_Giant.md) in the wilderness. Wild mammoths and tame mammoths can be distinguished by their tusks; those of tame mammoths bear engravings, while wild mammoths have unadorned tusks.

If a tame mammoth is attacked, its herding giant will rush to defend his stock, provided that the attack occurs within his detection range. Conversely, if a giant that owns a mammoth is attacked, his herd will turn hostile (but only until the giant is killed, at which point the mammoth will turn passive again). Mammoths will not enter water or cross a stream in pursuit (provided the water is deep enough), and may get stuck behind trees or rocks, which can prove useful for those wishing to evade them or kill them from a distance.

Tame mammoths in giant camps will eventually turn hostile if you linger near them for too long and ignore their warnings to leave (trumpeting and/or kicking the ground while trumpeting), but tame mammoths that patrol the wilderness with their giant will not turn hostile no matter how long you are around them, unless you attack them first. Wild mammoths will always remain peaceful unless attacked. You also might [encounter](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md) a wild mammoth fighting two [sabre cats](Skyrim_Sabre_Cat.md). Unlike other wild mammoths, this one will attack if you stay near it for too long, and will also turn hostile if you assist it in killing the cats, even if you never hit it (intentionally or unintentionally). A mammoth can also be found in another random encounter with two giants, being attacked by a group of bandits. This mammoth behaves like other tamed mammoths owned by giants.

## Notes
- [Enchanters](Skyrim_Enchanting.md) will find mammoths especially useful, as they are the most ready and reliable source of grand [souls](Skyrim_Souls.md) in the game.
- Mammoths will slowly regain a small amount of health (about 30 points) when their health bar nears zero.
- Regardless of shouts used, perks, or level, you can only stagger a mammoth. It is impossible to actually make a mammoth ragdoll. They are some of the only characters in the game that have a fixed death animation.
- Besides their other resistances, mammoths are also resistant to Ice Form and Vampiric Grip.
- Mammoths cannot be reanimated by any [Reanimate](Skyrim_Reanimate.md) spells but can be reanimated by the [Ritual Stone](Skyrim_The_Ritual_Stone.md) power and the [Soul Tear](Skyrim_Soul_Tear.md) shout.
- Leading a pursuing predator (bear, wolf, dragon, etc.) near a mammoth or tending giant will cause them to attack and possibly kill the predator. Be careful not to assist the giant and/or mammoth, as that can turn them hostile, even if you never hit them with friendly fire.
- Several dead mammoths can be found in the game. One is on the frozen coast of the north, near [Alftand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Alftand); this mammoth was slain in ancient times by Dwarves, as it is part of an iceberg and has Dwarven arrows and spears riddling its corpse. The second is at the bottom of [Halted Stream Camp](Skyrim_Halted_Stream_Camp.md), being cut up by a group of bandits. The third is in the volcanic tundra, being watched over by his former owner. The fourth is at the entrance of [Cronvangr Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Cronvangr_Cave).
