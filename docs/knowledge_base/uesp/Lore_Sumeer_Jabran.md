*You are fit only to speak with the half-witted beasts who serve me. Speak with them, if you please, but they speak only in the language of pain... a language I command, but do not understand.*
—Sumeer Jabran to the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) (listen Link ⏵) High Lord Sumeer Jabran is a feared being in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), who with his two comrades, [Dremora Methats](Lore_Methats.md) and [Wonshala Keriayn](Lore_Wonshala_Keriayn.md), have joined him in filling high-ranking positions to the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) that they have served.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Anchor-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BSDSW-2)</sup> Jabran was a **High Lord of the Clans** under the banner of [Clan Dagon](Lore_Clan_Dagon.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSSJ-3)</sup>

## History
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), Sumeer Jabran was the [Nightblade](Lore_Nightblades.md) of the trio known as the Dremora Travelers, who were considered the most fearless of their kind in [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s service,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Anchor-1)</sup> and served as generals to his invading forces.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Anchor G-4)</sup> They were tasked with protecting Dark Anchors but were slain by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), and the Anchor was destroyed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Anchor-1)</sup> A [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) staff which they looted during their conquests was recovered from the Dark Anchor's ruins after their defeat, though it later found its way to a Frost Atronach named Nomeg Rine who terrorized [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

By [3E 398](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_398), Sumeer Jabran and his comrades had changed their allegiance to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), with him joining Clan [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) and serving as a lieutenant and a high lord of the clans. During this time, he was one of four major players in the occupation of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), and was in charge of the looting.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSBA-6)</sup><sup>:102-103</sup> He outranked Methats, who he sent to guard the Weir Gate.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSBA-6)</sup><sup>:95-96</sup> Sumeer was determined to reassemble the voidguide and commanded the Specialists, a group of magical Dremora, as well as [Rishaal Tamir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rishaal_Tamir), Wonshala Keriayn, and Zenaida Nacarra in this goal.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSBA-6)</sup> Rishaal hand-delivered a missing piece of the voidguide to Sumeer after obtaining it from the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSBA-6)</sup><sup>:112</sup> It is rumored that he requested the aid of the Apprentice in recovering the voidguide, though they may have gone through other means to escape into the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BSSJ-3)</sup>

## Notes
- The character artist of *[Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire)*, [Mark Jones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Mark_Jones), had this to say about the creation of Sumeer Jabran's model: "I was pleased with the way he came out as he was very close to the original sketch which gave him the attitude, I thought, of a [genie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Djinn). If you look closely at his clothing you can just make out Daedric symbols."<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
