[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9f/SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg/200px-SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg) Thirsk Mead Hall Agnar the Unwavering was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) chieftain of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) in the fifth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Originally from the mainland, Agnar came to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) for some much-needed rest and found it in the mead-soaked halls of Thirsk. He became Chieftain of Thirsk after the previous Chieftain, the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), reportedly journeyed to [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BMDialogue-1)</sup> He accepted [Svenja Snow-Song](Lore_Svenja_Snow-Song.md) as his advisor and later married her.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AJ-2)</sup>

Svenja was haunted by the Udyrfrykte's massacre of Thirsk in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), which she was one of the only survivors of. To prevent any more destruction, Agnar and Svenja traveled across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in hopes of finding and slaying the [Uderfrykte Matron](Lore_Uderfrykte_Matron.md), the progenitor of the Udyrfrykte. Around [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a shepherd spotted it in the remote highlands of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and he and Svenja then tracked it across the border into [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). In Cyrodiil, a hunter told them about the Horror of Dive Rock, which Agnar and Svenja concluded was the matron. They established a camp on the overlook at [Dive Rock](Lore_Dive_Rock.md), and after three days of watching, they spotted the matron. Once they confronted the matron, Svenja was quickly struck down and devoured, forcing Agnar to retreat to the camp. The chieftain made final preparations to attack the beast, but he was killed as well.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AJ-2)</sup> The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) later stumbled upon the camp, discovered Agnar's body and journal, and slew the Uderfrykte Matron.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

The names of Agnar and the succeeding chieftains of Thirsk are not recorded in *Thirsk, a Revised History*, with the written record only resuming after a period of around one hundred fifty years. The Nerevarine and Agnar are the only known chieftains of Thirsk from that period of unrecorded history.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAH-4)</sup>
