# Imperial Cult

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/ff/OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Talos.jpg/200px-OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Talos.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Talos.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Talos.jpg) The Great Chapel of Talos in Bruma (Oblivion) *The missionary arm of the great faiths, the Imperial Cult brings divine inspiration and consolation to the [Empire's](Lore_Empire.md) remote provinces. The cults combine the worship of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md): the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), [Mara](Lore_Mara.md), [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), and [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), and the [Talos Cult](Lore_Talos_Cult.md), veneration of the divine god-hero [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), founder and patron of the Empire. Imperial Cult priests provide worship and services for all these gods at the Imperial Cult shrines in settlements throughout Vvardenfell.*
— *[For my Gods and Emperor](Lore_For_my_Gods_and_Emperor.md)* The main religion of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) can trace its origins back [Saint Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s institution of worship of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) as a synthesis between the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) gods and the [Nordic pantheon](Lore_Nord.md#The_Nordic_Pantheon).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Although known as the Imperial Cult in the provinces, strictly speaking this term refers the missionary arm of the religion, present in provinces such as [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FMGAE-2)</sup> As the majority religion in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), it is more commonly known in that province simply as **the Church**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> or **the Chapel**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> After the apotheosis of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) began to spread belief in the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md), incorporating worship of the Empire's god-hero [Talos](Lore_Talos.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FMGAE-2)</sup> However, the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) was forced to sign the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), which outlawed Talos worship.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## Hierarchy
There are different positions in the Cult; for example, lay healers gather ingredients for health and healing potions, and minister to the sick and hurt in poor and isolated communities. Almoners gather alms from fellow members, and people with good mercantile skills often fall into this line of work. Shrine sergeants help keep order at shrines and often act as couriers; in some cases they will be called upon to escort priests and fight for the faith against groups like witches.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Advancement within the cult often favors those who are proficient with [Restoration](Lore_Restoration.md), [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md), [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md), and blunt weaponry so as to avoid bloodshed. Skills in [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md) and speech are also valued by the cult.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FMGAE-2)</sup>

The usual ranks within the cult are, in ascending order of seniority: Layman, Novice, Initiate, Acolyte, Adept, Disciple, Oracle, Invoker, Theurgist, and Primate.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MW-10)</sup> Laypeople can also be granted honorary membership of the Church as a Pilgrim if they are called to carry out a holy pilgrimage.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Chapels may employ a local city guard to serve as Chapel Guard, a lay position within the Church.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The Church also venerates [Living Saints](Lore_Living_Saints.md), individuals who have received a calling to live by the commands of their chosen Divine as an example to others.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Certain [saints](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) continue to be venerated long after death,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ralvas-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Gt C-17)</sup> and devotions are often made collectively to the [Communion of Saints](Lore_Communion_of_Saints.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-GTTIC-18)</sup>

## Organization
The cult is based out of Cyrodiil, and its ministries across the Empire are handled by the **Council of the Nine**, consisting of the Primates of Cyrodiil's various Great Chapels.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Chana-19)</sup> In Cyrodiil, the Church is divided into eight parishes, each dedicated to a divine. [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) Parish is devoted to Mara,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Chana-19)</sup> [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) to Akatosh, [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) to Dibella, [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) to Stendarr, [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) to Arkay, [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) to Julianos, [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) to Talos, and [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) to Zenithar.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Each of these cities possesses a Great Chapel devoted to the worship of their parish patron. Kynareth does not possess a Great Chapel, as the Church considers the Chapel of Kynareth to be the natural world; her center of worship is therefore a shrine located deep in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) west of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> The Imperial City itself houses the [Temple of the One](Lore_Temple_of_the_One.md), a center of worship that dominates the city's [Temple District](Lore_Temple_District.md). The [Dragonfire Cathedral](Lore_Dragonfire_Cathedral.md) is a secret subterranean vault and temple hidden beneath the city. Both are tied to the ritual of the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TSub Braz-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Lt D-23)</sup>

During the Imperial colonization of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Imperial Chapels in [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md) served as the headquarters of the cult in Vvardenfell District. The cult offered services at shrines in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) forts established throughout the region.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MW-10)</sup> The cult at that time maintained relatively good relations with the other guilds of Morrowind, being allies with the Imperial Legion, [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), and [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and on friendly terms with the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), and [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FMGAE-2)</sup>

There is an institute called the **Imperial Seminary**, which focuses on the study of religion and also prepares people for the role of clergy. Based on speculation that certain spirits on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) can use the deeds of a mortal to elevate themselves to godlike status, a notable member of the seminary, Motusuo, suggested that these spirits could be the remnants of lost and nigh-forgotten deities, who upon losing their following reverted to their most basic form.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-OOGAW-24)</sup> The seminary archived an abridged version of *Mythic [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md)*, a theory crafted by [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) apologists for Emperor [Uriel Septim V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md). The summary was featured in *[The Monomyth](Lore_The_Monomyth.md)*, a book containing other creation myths.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TM-25)</sup>
