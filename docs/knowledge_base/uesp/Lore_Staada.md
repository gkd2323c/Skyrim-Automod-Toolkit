Staada is a [Golden Saint](Lore_Golden_Saint.md) commander in service to [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) that is often entrusted with critical missions.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-THASI-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRSQ-4)</sup> She notably wears the Mad God's signet ring.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Like her fellow Aureal, Staada is linked to the [amber](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amber) that grows in the [Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Root) system of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), which is sometimes used in rituals to summon or even bind Golden Saints. However, the usage of rarely-occurring Sheogorath-shaped amber is required to summon her.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RAFECC-6)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Staada was summoned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), where she guarded a lesser summoning circle deep in the [Alik'r Desert](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alik%27r_Desert) southeast of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md). She maintained a mysterious portal there until she was banished by a group of adventurers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> She carried a lead on the whereabouts of a cameo depicting a Golden Saint, which the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)'s Antiquarian Circle excavated, studied, and combined with several other jewelry pieces, completing the Saint and the Seducer necklace.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCodex-7)</sup>

The significance of Staada allowed her to be documented within the [Infinite Archive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Archive) in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). There, copies of various entities were subsequently created as [maligraphies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maligraphy), creatures composed of vivified ink manifested by stories from books, and served as mindless extensions of the [Daedric Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Lord) [Tho'at Replicanum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tho%27at_Replicanum). A maligraphic copy of Staada was formed, which was destroyed by mortal adventurers aided by augmentations directly from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LAIA-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MM-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Enemies-10)</sup>

### Third Era
In the fourth century of the Third Era, Sheogorath made a wager with [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) to see if a person in isolation for a century would be able to prevent themselves from going mad through meditation. In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Sheogorath sought to interfere with the wager by disturbing the seclusion of Azura's priestess, Rayna Drolan, and tasked Staada and various minions to invade the Isle of the Daedra<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-11)</sup><sup>:29</sup> in the [Sheogorad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheogorad) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Azura charged the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) with banishing the Daedric invaders and bringing back proof of the Mad God's meddling. The Nerevarine defeated Staada and took Sheogorath's Signet Ring from her corpse as proof.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Staada was serving as the commander of [Brellach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brellach) in the Shivering Isles when the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) began. The forces of Order invaded Brellach to cripple the realm's army, and Staada was taken prisoner.<sup>[[nb1]](#intnote_A)</sup> She was freed by Sheogorath's [Champion](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) and aided in the liberation of the stronghold. Although she perished with the other Golden Saints when the Wellspring of the Aureal was frozen by Order, they were revived when the Champion destroyed the crystals stopping the flow of [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md). Staada would then lead the Aureal to remove any remaining remnants of Order from their home, allowing them to resume their normal duties.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-THASI-3)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Staada manifested in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) to protect the Shivering Isles from encroachment by a manic mage named [Thoron](Lore_Thoron.md), who had begun breaking down the barriers between that realm and Tamriel using the [Sword of Jyggalag](Lore_Sword_of_Jyggalag.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CC-12)</sup> After the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) put an end to this threat by killing Thoron, Staada wrote to the Dragonborn to arrange a meeting.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> She requested the item Thoron used as the focus of his rituals, although the details of this proposed exchange are unknown. Indeed, it is unknown if the Dragonborn made any trade at all with Staada, or whether they instead chose to bind and summon her using powerful [Conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) magic.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRSQ-4)</sup>

## Notes
- [<sup>nb1</sup>](#note_A) This event only occurs if the player chooses to become the Duke/Duchess of Dementia during the [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles) main quest. This will lead Duke [Thadon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thadon) to betray Sheogorath for the death of [Syl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syl). His betrayal led the Aureal to be caught off-guard, allowing for the invasion of Brellach. If the player instead chooses to become the Duke/Duchess of Mania, Syl will survive and attack the Mazken's home of [Pinnacle Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pinnacle_Rock) in retaliation for Thadon's death.
- Staada's five appearances make her tied with [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) for the most recurring character in the series, barring [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md).
