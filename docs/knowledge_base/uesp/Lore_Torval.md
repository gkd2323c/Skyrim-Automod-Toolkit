—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Torval is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup> and is situated in the dense jungles of the [Tenmar Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tenmar_Forest). Torval is the seat of power for the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md), the spiritual and temporal leader of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Their tribe lives with them in exotic palaces and symmetrical [moon sugarcane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar) gardens.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup> The city has a long history associated with the Mane, going back to its time as one of the sixteen kingdoms.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup> In modern-day, Torval is known for its southern wines.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AR-5)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/ON-flora-Moon_Sugar_Cane.jpg/200px-ON-flora-Moon_Sugar_Cane.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Moon_Sugar_Cane.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Moon_Sugar_Cane.jpg) Moon Sugarcanes Torval is located on the southwestern corner of Elsweyr,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LOMap EL-6)</sup> near the Valenwood border, which is only a few hundred miles away.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup> It is part of the Kingdom of [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LOMap EL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CM74-7)</sup> the southern region of Elsweyr that is defined by its jungles and rainforests.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup> The Torval region has a damp climate<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CM74-7)</sup> and the earth around the city contains [ochre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ochre), a natural clay earth pigment. Torval Royal Ochre, in particular, is a rare and valuable dye.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONTreasures Ochre-8)</sup> The Mane and their tribe live in large stately palaces made from massive timber from Valenwood [oak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oak). Around these palaces are symmetrical sugarcane gardens, where the Mane often meditates in his palanquin.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup> Torval is neighbored by several settlements, including Darvulk Haven to the far southeast, Portneu View to the northeast, and Valenwood to the west,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup> toward Cormeir Spring and [Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haven).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ARMap VW-9)</sup>

## History

### Early History
Like the other sixteen kingdoms of ancient Elsweyr, Torval started as one of the original clans of nomadic Khajiit, all of whom excelled in a trade or discipline and swore fealty to the [Moon-Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon-Emperor).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AAPAI-13)</sup> Torval was defined by its worship of the Mane.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SOK-14)</sup> As these clans mastered their roles, they pushed themselves into defined geographical areas and the concept of tribal regions was established from there. This is how the idea of sixteen kingdoms came to fruition.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AAPAI-13)</sup> Before they were full-fledged kingdoms, however, they were petty fiefdoms ruled by a cruel hunter-aristocracy helmed by the Hunt-Lords.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OOTKMT-15)</sup>

The [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) was a tumultuous time for the Khajiit as their society was at the heel of these Hunt-Lords. The [Temples of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance) were the only ones to check them as Khajiiti adepts and Torval clergies held significant cultural influence. Despite the Hunt-Lords' attempt to oppress the people, their efforts were proven futile as the Khajiit developed the modern-day [Claw-Dances](Lore_Claw-Dances.md) and rebelled. Over the course of a thousand years, the hunter's caste system was abolished all over ancient Elsweyr and these fiefdoms became kingdoms.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OOTKMT-15)</sup> Throughout history, these people lived in harmony among each other and their dominance was determined by the phases of the moons. When the moons were both halves, Torval was at its peak in power.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3-16)</sup>

In [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SH3-17)</sup> the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) led to the dissolution of the sixteen kingdoms, including Torval.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AAPAI-13)</sup> The clans of the south gathered in [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) to the east, where the Khajiit were already able to adapt and the moon-priests of the Pa'alatiin Clan guiding them through the hardship. With Senchal as their capital, southern Elsweyr became known as the Kingdom of [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) and distinguished themselves from the nomads of the [north](Lore_Anequina.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-HOPAO-18)</sup>

### Torval in the Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-npc-Lord_Gharesh-ri_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Lord_Gharesh-ri_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lord_Gharesh-ri_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Lord_Gharesh-ri_02.jpg) Lord Gharesh-ri [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/ON-npc-Mane_Akkhuz-ri_03.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Mane_Akkhuz-ri_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mane_Akkhuz-ri_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mane_Akkhuz-ri_03.jpg) Akkhuz-ri, the Mane In the mid-Second Era, the Mane was Akkhuz-ri and his advisor, the Speaker of the Mane was Lord Gharesh-ri, both of whom had known each other since childhood.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONGharesh-19)</sup> In [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) had broken out from Black Marsh<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TEATC-20)</sup> and five years later, had reached Elsweyr, specifically Senchal.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HOSAO-21)</sup>

It did not take long for the flu to spread all over Elsweyr, including Torval. As people across the province slowly found ways to mitigate the disease, Lord Gharesh-ri assembled what was left of the [Mane's Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mane%27s_Legion) to maintain order and use their knowledge to the people's benefit. Queen Ayrenn offered assistance to the Khajiit and in order to fight a common enemy, both factions decided to come together.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TEATC-20)</sup>

Akkhuz-ri and Gharesh-ri were present in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580) when the signing of the [Elden Accord](Lore_Elden_Accord.md) took place between leaders of the First [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-22)</sup> Gharesh-ri signed the Elden Accord and thus established Elsweyr as a member of the trans-provincial alliance.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONGharesh-19)</sup> He became a member of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) Inner Council; advisors that Queen Ayrenn had the utmost trust in.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-AOSAYQ-23)</sup> Meanwhile, other members of the Thalmor were figureheads,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Zeni Max Interview-24)</sup> such as Lady Radurra-dra of Torval, who represented southwestern Elsweyr.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-KH-25)</sup>

Two years later in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Akkhuz-ri became possessed by the Dark Mane and rampaged across [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March) to the north. When the two were separated in [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia), Akkhuz-ri relinquished his title as Mane and left it between the twins, Khali and Shazah. One of the twins became the new Mane in the Temple of the Dance in [Dune](Lore_Dune.md), while the other took on the immortal task of bearing the Dark Mane's mantle and sealed away in [Moonmont](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonmont).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONReaper-26)</sup> In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the Mane was [Nhad-hatta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nhad-hatta), who ruled during [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s conquests of the late Second Era. An Imperial diplomat arranged a meeting with the Mane in his palace in Torval but was denied entry by the guards.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup>

### Torval in the Third Era
By around [3E 80](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_80), Torval underwent a rebellion,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TWQ1-27)</sup> one of many that plagued Emperor [Uriel Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_II)'s reign.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-28)</sup> He brokered a deal with the King of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) at the time, [Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantiarco) who donated millions of gold in exchange for a bride, thus establishing an alliance with the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md). The Emperor agreed and the money was used to quell the rebellion in Torval.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TWQ1-27)</sup> Later in time, Uriel II's great-grandson, [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) was moved across Tamriel to different healing institutions and asylums to relieve him of his [madness](Lore_Madness_(disease).md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-29)</sup> The Emperor's visit to the asylum in Torval was adapted into a story written by the famed author [Waughin Jarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waughin_Jarth) called *"[The Asylum Ball](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Asylum_Ball)"*. Pelagius III decides to hold a lavish ball in the asylum, and the old compound became a beautifully decorated ballroom with an orchestra in the pit. After the event, Pelagius III was moved to another location in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and reportedly died three months later<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TAB-10)</sup> in [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-29)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Torval was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Mojtabe and had a rivalry with [Corinth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corinth).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AR-5)</sup> These ten years were marked with conflicts between the provinces, and Elsweyr was caught amidst the [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) against Valenwood to the west. How this event started is up to debate but the Khajiit felt it began with the so-called [Slaughter of Torval](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Slaughter_of_Torval), where the Bosmer invaded the city and killed thousands, only to fall back after Khajiiti reinforcements intervened.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-MUT-30)</sup> In a map of capital cities across the provinces circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Torval is listed among them in Elsweyr.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-MCMap-31)</sup>

### Known Rulers
*The King and His Court come down the road
The Sacred Mane of Torval
Servants of hirsute majesty
The loyal guards of Torval*

For the longest time, Torval was ruled by a religious figure known as the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup> While [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta) is acknowledged as the first Mane,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONShazah-32)</sup> that title has long been associated with Torval since its time as one of the sixteen kingdoms.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup> In the traditional Khajiiti child's chant, *[The Sixteen Kingdoms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Sixteen_Kingdoms)*, Torval's ruler is referred to as the Sacred Mane of Torval.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup> By the Third Era, Torval was ruled by a King/Queen.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AR-5)</sup>

The Mane employs an advisor and political representative called the Speaker of the Mane, who governs in their stead.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONShazah-32)</sup> The Mane is also guarded by an elite force of Khajiiti warriors called the [Bi-Lunar Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bi-Lunar_Guard), who mount themselves on Black Mane [lions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lion)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONMane-11)</sup> and heavily guard the main premises.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/ON-place-Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance.jpg/200px-ON-place-Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance.jpg) One of the Temples of Two-Moons Dance [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-crown_store-Black_Mane_Lion.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Black_Mane_Lion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Black_Mane_Lion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Black_Mane_Lion.jpg) The Black Mane Lion *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Khajiit.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Khajiit.md#Society)** sections on the Khajiit page.* The Temple of Two-Moons Dance in Torval is considered the best place in Tamriel to train in hand-to-hand combat, and its masters have taught people from all over the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). They teach everything from ancient to modern techniques, and their students have become famous. One such example was Taren Omothan,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MZT-12)</sup> a monk and master trainer who took residence in [Holamayan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Holamayan) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MWTaren-34)</sup> When the time came for students to graduate and move on from the temple, a grand festival of celebration commences, in which debates and competitions are held.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MZT-12)</sup>

While the Temple of Two-Moons Dance are led by the lunar clergy, the [Torval Curiata](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torval_Curiata) are a sub-faction that enforces the epiphanies of [Riddle'Thar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar) and fight heretics that oppose it.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONSabannar-35)</sup> In Torval, the people use ritual incense burners made out of [bronze](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bronze) and with moon-sugar canes.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ONTreasures Censer-36)</sup> Although Torval is known for wines,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AR-5)</sup> other brands of [alcohol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alcoholic_Beverages) are exported from the city, such as brandy,<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ONBrandy-37)</sup> and [mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mead).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-F2-1)</sup> There is an active market in Torval and nearby Senchal for relics from the [Anequine Conquests](Lore_Anequine_Conquests.md), especially artifacts from [Darloc Brae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darloc_Brae)'s time.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONLoad Kuna-38)</sup> By the waning years of the Third Era, the Gold Cat of Senchal, Ya'Tirrje had a drug-smuggling business in Torval, among other noteworthy cities.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3-16)</sup>

## Notes
- The name "Torval" is an alternate name for [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md),<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Opsius-39)</sup> the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorer who fully charted the sea lanes of Tamriel. He was active in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), when he first documented the "cat demons" which are speculated to be the ancient Khajiit.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-FOTN-40)</sup> It is possible that the city of Torval was named after Topal the Pilot.
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Torval's gladiatorial team would have been called "[the Firedrakes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fire_Drakes)".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-41)</sup>
