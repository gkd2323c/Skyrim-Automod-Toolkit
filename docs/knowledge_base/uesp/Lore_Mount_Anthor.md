[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/SR-place-Mount_Anthor.jpg/200px-SR-place-Mount_Anthor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mount_Anthor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mount_Anthor.jpg) Mount Anthor circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/LG-location-Mount_Anthor.png/200px-LG-location-Mount_Anthor.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-location-Mount_Anthor.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-location-Mount_Anthor.png) Mount Anthor (Legends) Mount Anthor is a mountain found southwest of the city of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), in the [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md) region of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The **Winterhold Mountains** stretch from [Hsaarik Head](Lore_Hsaarik_Head.md) in the northeast,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:454</sup> traveling around the [Winterhold Glaciers](Lore_Winterhold_Glaciers.md) to Nightcaller Temple, and make up the border with [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) to the south and east, meeting with [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md)'s [Lake Yorgrim](Lore_Lake_Yorgrim.md) and [Giant's Run](Lore_Giant%27s_Run.md) in the southeast. Due to the grandness of the peak, the mountains are often referred to as the **Mount Anthor Range**, and include the Wayward Pass, named for the summit of **Wayward Peak**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup> Ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) built a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) at the summit of the mountain.

The mountain is most famous as the site of the battle between [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) and the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Numinex](Lore_Numinex.md) during the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> According to [Taleon Mythmaker](Lore_Taleon_Mythmaker.md), the Crown of the Shadow of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) was forged in the shadow of Mount Anthor and was named after it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASC-3)</sup> The mountaintop once again became a dragon lair after the return of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), displacing the band of warlocks who had previously occupied it. The jarl of Winterhold put out several bounties in an attempt to drive off the dragons.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-4)</sup>

Sometime circa 4E 201, the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) and a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) sorceress named Laaneth killed a dragon at Mount Anthor and retrieved its skull at the request of [Alfe Fyr](Lore_Alfe_Fyr.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
