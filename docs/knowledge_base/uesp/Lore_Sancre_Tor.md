[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg/200px-OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) Sancre Tor circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Sancre Tor (or the **Golden Hill**), also known as the **Tor**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> was an ancient fortified city in [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), located in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) north of the city of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) in what is now [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> It is said to be the mythical birthplace and final resting place of [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman.md) and his successors.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-R-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TLST-4)</sup> It is notable for the [Fort Runestones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Runestones) placed throughout the complex during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-misc-Sancre Tor Frieze.png/600px-ON-misc-Sancre Tor Frieze.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Sancre Tor Frieze.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Sancre Tor Frieze.png) Frieze found at Sancre Tor The founding stories of Sancre Tor are conflicting and shrouded in myth. It is written that [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) received the divine inspiration for her [slave rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) at the site of Sancre Tor, and afterwards founded her holy city there.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSai-6)</sup> However, this conflicts with the story of Alessia's youth, where she was raised as a slave in the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city of [Sard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sard).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Apocryphal accounts describe Sancre Tor as an Ayleid holding which was taken by Alessia; the loss involved eight horns known as the Hallowed Clarions.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

Another origin myth claims that King [Hrol](Lore_Hrol.md) gave his life by copulating with the spirit of Saint Alessia on the site of Sancre Tor, seemingly impregnating the earth. The small mound of mud left behind by their union soon grew into a small mountain (the "Golden Hill", also known as Hrol's Hillock), attracting a community of believers to settle there. After nine months, the shepherdess [Sed-Yenna](Lore_Sed-Yenna.md) heard a cry from the top of the mountain, and climbed it to find the newborn Reman Cyrodiil, wearing the [Chim-el Adabal](Lore_Chim-el_Adabal.md) on his forehead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-R-3)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/LO-misc-Colovian.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Colovian.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Colovian.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Colovian.jpg) A Colovian from Sancre Tor Sancre Tor fell into ruin following the fall of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). Many heroes and noblemen were buried in the crypts, including the Remans and their [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) protectors. Within the main chamber of the catacombs are five tombs bearing epitaphs dedicated to Reman I, II and III, along with two other unmarked rulers (although it is implied these tombs belong to [Kastav](Lore_Kastav.md)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> and [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md)).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tombs-9)</sup>

Priestesses of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) around Sancre Tor painted their ankles in the same fashion as the extinct [Keptu](Lore_Keptu.md) circa [1E 2762](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2762).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSe-10)</sup>

After its abandonment, Sancre Tor became a dark place, haunted by vast numbers of undead. Some time after the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) of [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) and Kasura hid the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) in the ruins of Sancre Tor before being captured by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the reformed [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md) entered the ruins in an attempt to retrieve it. [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) swiftly followed, raising more undead from their graves in an attempt to stop them. However, he was defeated in battle by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) after revealing his plans to usurp Bal's godhood. He was then pulled into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) for his treachery, allowing the Companions to escape with the Amulet.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ON-11)</sup>

By the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Sancre Tor had once again become inhabited. In [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852), the city was captured by invading forces from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Bo ST-12)</sup> General [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), who would later become [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), recaptured the city that winter.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Bo ST-12)</sup> However, the city was in a strategically inconvenient position, and Talos abandoned it to again fall into ruins.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TLST-4)</sup> At some point the mercenaries [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) and [Tobias](Lore_Tobias.md) had an encounter with the mercenary lord [Duadeen](Lore_Duadeen.md) at Sancre Tor.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Redguard T-13)</sup>

### Third Era
Many decades later, Tiber Septim was fatally wounded at another battle at Sancre Tor, where he died and ascended to become one of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The Blades built a shrine in the catacombs of Sancre Tor, on the spot where he had received the blessing of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBJauffre-15)</sup> It then served as a place of pilgrimage for the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) for many years.

The old fortress of Sancre Tor was later corrupted by the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md), prompting it to be sealed shut by the Grandmaster of the Blades in the thirty-sixth year of the reign of Tiber Septim.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup> The four mightiest Blades of the time, Alain, Valdemar, Rielus, and Casnar, never returned from the ruins and were cursed to undeath.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBJauffre-15)</sup> In the years after Tiber Septim's reign, it was gradually forgotten, decaying into a haunted ruin. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) visited the ruins in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) to recover the Armor of Tiber Septim, unsealing the ruins with the approval of the Blades. After freeing the four ancient Blades from undeath, their spirits aided the Hero in entering the Reman crypt and recovering the armor.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OBBOTD-17)</sup> The Hero was also aided by a mysterious guardian known as Baynham.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
