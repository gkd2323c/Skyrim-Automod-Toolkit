Reman III was the fifth and last [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) and [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). His forty-three-year reign was marked by the long and bloody conflict known as the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) against [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), Reman III negotiated a treaty with [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), one of the god-kings of Morrowind, to end the war; however, the Emperor was assassinated by the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) before it could be signed and ratified.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920SDv11-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Fa D-3)</sup> His death marked the end of the Reman Dynasty, and only sixteen days after his assassination, his potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) proclaimed the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the beginning of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-2)</sup>

## Biography
Reman was born in [1E 2863](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2863).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TEot V-4)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_grandfather)</sup> In [1E 2877](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2877), Emperor [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md) suddenly died, so Reman was crowned emperor at the age of fourteen.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TEot V-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-R3-5)</sup> Brazollus' estate, known as Villa Doria, had served as the Imperial Court for the twenty years preceding Reman III's reign, but shortly after his accession, Reman returned the Imperial Court to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) so that he could exercise power in his own name.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TEot V-4)</sup>

At some point, he married [Tavia](Lore_Tavia.md), who came from a wealthy background and had powerful allies in [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920LSv8-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGt G-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup> With Tavia, he had a son, [Juilek](Lore_Juilek.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-2920MSv1-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3C-10)</sup> In [1E 2899](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2899), the Emperor had Tavia imprisoned in the city of [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md) for purportedly plotting against him.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3A-8)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920LSv8-6)</sup> For the next fourteen years, the Empress was moved from castle to castle, until she was finally imprisoned at Castle Giovesse in [1E 2913](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2913).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGt G-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-2920MSv1-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Aot GS-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-12)</sup> At some point, he took a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) mistress named Rijja.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-2920SDv2-13)</sup>

At some point during his reign, Reman III hunted down and slew [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) with his [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) bodyguards.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOCR-14)</sup>

By the time of Reman III's accession, the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) with [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) had been ongoing for thirty-seven years, and the conflict continued for the remainder of his reign.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3M-15)</sup> The only battle of the Four-Score War in which Reman III is known to have personally participated was the Battle of [Bodrum](Lore_Bodrum.md) on 20 First Seed, [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), where he commanded the rearguard of the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). The day before the battle, the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) army dammed the [Pryai River](Lore_Pryai_River.md), flooding the plain near Bodrum and forcing the Imperial Army to trudge through mud into battle. The next morning, while the Imperial Army was trudging through the mud, the commander of the Dunmer, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), ordered his battlemages to cast spells at the dam on the Pryai River, destroying it, washing away the Imperial vanguard, and forcing the Emperor to order the remains of the army to retreat.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-2920FSv3-16)</sup>

In Sun's Height 1E 2920, after Vivec agreed to the terms of a peace treaty that Juilek negotiated, Reman III ordered the Imperial Army to attack and capture the strategically located fortress of [Black Gate](Lore_Black_Gate.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920LSv8-6)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-2920SHv7-17)</sup> On 31 Last Seed, Juilek was killed.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-2920LSv8-6)</sup> In Frostfall, the Emperor met Vivec at Bodrum to negotiate a new peace treaty after breaking Juilek's treaty.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920SDv11-1)</sup> On 6 Sun's Dusk, he and Vivec agreed on the final terms of the treaty and planned to sign it one month later at the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920SDv11-1)</sup>

On 6 Evening Star, Reman III was assassinated by the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) in the audience chamber of the Imperial Palace.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Fa D-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-R3-5)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3At Eo M-18)</sup> It is believed that his potentate, [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md), ordered his assassination.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-2)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-APC-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOAd-20)</sup> On 8 Evening Star, Versidue-Shaie and Vivec agreed to the [Cervant Truce](Lore_Cervant_Truce.md), ending the Four-Score War.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-2)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-21)</sup> Reman III's death left the [Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) with no heirs, so on 22 Evening Star, Versidue-Shaie proclaimed that Reman's line was dead and that he would be taking over rulership of the Empire but retaining his title of potentate.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-2)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3At Eo M-18)</sup> The Potentate also proclaimed the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the beginning of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-2)</sup> After this, the Second Empire entered the period known as the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3C-10)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1C-22)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-APC-19)</sup>

He was entombed with the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) in the innermost chamber of the Tomb of the Reman Emperors in [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Tot RE-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Bo ST-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TLST-25)</sup> [Talos Stormcrown](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) reputedly recovered the Amulet of Kings from the tomb of Reman III during the Sack of Sancre Tor in [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TLST-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Ob LS-26)</sup>

## Notes
- King [Dro'Zel](Lore_Dro%27Zel.md) of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) served as an advisor to Reman III.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920SDv11-1)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Ho SAO-27)</sup>
- | [^](#note_grandfather) | It is never directly stated who Reman III's father is. In *[2920, Sun's Dawn](Lore_2920,_Sun%27s_Dawn_(v2).md)*, Reman III is sullen after the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) Battlechief of [Soulrest](Lore_Soulrest.md) stepped on his foot, and declares, "My father would have flogged him. My grandfather would have had him killed," as punishment for offending royalty. This implies that Reman III's father and grandfather were of the same royal standing as Reman III himself, and were therefore emperors. As Reman III's two immediate predecessors were Emperor Brazollus Dor and Emperor Reman II, it is reasonable to conclude that Brazollus Dor was Reman III's father, and Reman II was his grandfather. |
