## [Maligraphy](Lore_Maligraphy.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/ON-object-Roiling_Ink.jpg/200px-ON-object-Roiling_Ink.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-object-Roiling_Ink.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-object-Roiling_Ink.jpg) A Maligraphy in base form [Maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md) are [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) creatures composed of vivified ink. They are described as being myths from books come to life, and as such can take on virtually any form. These forms can include mortals of different races and affiliations, other daedric entities, [animals](Lore_Bestiary.md), [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md), [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), [shades](Lore_Shade.md), [celestials](Lore_Celestial.md), [undead](Lore_Undead.md), and even [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) or [clockwork](Lore_Factotum.md) [constructs](Lore_Construct.md). They can replicate not only the physical appearance of the forms they copy but also the accompanying equipment, including powerful [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md). They were summoned by [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md) in the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md). Each maligraphy's strength depended on the page they were created from, translating the power in the contents of the page to physical prowess.

Though classified as Daedra nominally, they are truly pieces of Tho'at without individuality. They were created from fragments of her [nymic](Lore_Nymic.md) combined with the ink of the archive's tomes via Daedric [magic](Lore_Magic.md). Due to the nature of the archive, a potentially infinite amount of maligraphies may have formed, though they are normally unable to sustain themselves outside of the archive, some have found a way to escape its confines.

Commonly, a variety of maligraphies are found alongside their specific stronger subtype, the Fabled. Another type of very powerful maligraphy was known as the Marauder.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Infinite_Archive)

## [Man-Beast](Lore_Lycanthropy.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-npc-Jailer_Melitus.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Jailer_Melitus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jailer_Melitus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jailer_Melitus.jpg) A Man-Beast Man-Beasts are individuals afflicted with a kind of [Lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) of which very little is known. ## [Mantikora](Lore_Mantikora.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/LG-cardart-Mantikora.png/200px-LG-cardart-Mantikora.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Mantikora.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Mantikora.png) A Mantikora [Mantikoras](Lore_Mantikora.md) are unnatural composite creatures, artificially created to serve as soldiers by the [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md) circa [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572). They were created via their experiments with [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md), [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md), and ritualistic [magics](Lore_Magic.md). They combine the traits of numerous predators, such as [crocodiles](Lore_Crocodile.md), [welwas](Lore_Welwa.md), [scorpions](Lore_Giant_Scorpion.md), and specially bred [wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md). Additionally, captured Men and Mer were also utilized to give the creatures their sapience. Mantikoras were created to be used in [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md)'s plot to revert [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) back to the chaos of the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md). One was even possessed by him to battle his enemies.

Mantikoras are large, powerful, and exceptionally intelligent - and as a result extremely dangerous. They are intelligent enough to communicate and barter with other races. They are aggressive creatures that like the thrill of the hunt, and they never kill their victim quickly to enjoy their fun. They typically wield huge javelins in combat, though they are also capable of producing a deadly venom. Their venom can cook their victims from the inside, leaving them as venom-charred husks. The venom of a specific Mantikora, Tapupezu Azzida, was used as a supplement in an illegal [narcotic](Lore_Drugs.md) known as [Daze](Lore_Drugs.md#Daze) in [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md). While Daedra could survive it, it is lethal to mortals, though regular Mantikora venom is devasting even to Daedra.

Only certain Mantikoras, known as progenitors, are created with the ability to [breed](Lore_Sex.md).

- See also: [Chimera](Lore_Chimera.md) and [Tharriker](Lore_Tharriker.md)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Mantikora), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Mantikora)

## [Marsh Giant](Lore_Marsh_Giant.md)
[Marsh Giants](Lore_Marsh_Giant.md) are a [giant](Lore_Giant.md) -like [race](Lore_Race.md) native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). These manifestations of nature are a form of [woodland spirit](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md) considered "cousins" to [spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md). They are not related to the common giants of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), but they do share their size and pastoral activities. Their exact level of intelligence is unknown due to limited interactions. However, the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) apparently have a long and acrimonious history with the Marsh Giants, and urge the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to avoid the creatures if they can.

They play an important role in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md)'s ecosystem by knocking down dead trees, turning the soil and making room for younger vegetation to grow. This encourages the growth of mosses and suppresses the development of invasive fungi. Marsh giants also hinder loggers, contribute to [avian](Lore_Birds.md) diversity and ward off certain predators, such as feral [cats](Lore_Cat.md).

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_On_Marsh_Giants__A_Study.md)

## [Mind-Shriven](Lore_Mind-Shriven.md)
Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mind-Shriven_Spellfiend.jpg) A Mind-Shriven [Mind-Shriven](Lore_Mind-Shriven.md) are mortals or [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who have had their will and mentality stolen by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), the Daedric Prince of Domination and Enslavement. They are not to be confused with [Soul-Shriven](Lore_Soul-Shriven.md), dead mortals whose [souls](Lore_Soul.md) have been stolen by Bal and replaced by a Daedric [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md). Unlike these "[vestiges](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md)", Mind-Shriven are not dead and have retained their mortal bodies.

Becoming a Mind-Shriven involves drinking a poison known as the [Blood of Coldharbour](Lore_Azure_Plasm.md), which will slowly begin to rob the imbiber of their willpower. This process can be delayed through alchemy, but not halted. Mind-Shriven can be identified by the wispy blue veins of cold fire that emanate from their veins, and their glowing blue eyes. Once the transformation is complete, the victim takes on a lifeless, pallid grey tone and becomes mindlessly aggressive. Some of the afflicted experience side effects apart from the intended effect, such as their vision being tinted blue as their eyes change. The process is not just effective on the mortal [races](Lore_Races.md), as [horses](Lore_Horse.md) and [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) have been transformed into Mind-Shriven. Even lesser Daedra can become Mind-Shriven, as in the case of Mind-Shriven [Scamps](Lore_Scamp.md), unusual specimens that are often found in the company of other Mind-Shriven creatures.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Mind-Shriven)

## [Minotaur](Lore_Minotaur.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-creature-Minotaur.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Minotaur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Minotaur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Minotaur.jpg) A minotaur [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md), also called **man-bulls** or **bull-men**, are a species of [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) native to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) with the body of a [man](Lore_Human.md) and the head of a [bull](Lore_Bull.md). During the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), they were said to have symbolized the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), but in later eras became more commonly perceived as savage monsters by the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

They have thick grey or sometimes brown or red hide and fur, and large curved horns. Some Minotaurs clothe themselves in a simple loincloth and shawl or wear nose-rings. Others wear intricate leather armor, and some prefer to be completely naked. They are most commonly found in the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md), [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) and the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) regions of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), as well as [The Scar](Lore_The_Scar.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), the [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), and in ancient times across [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Minotaur.md).

## [Miregaunt](Lore_Miregaunt.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-creature-Miregaunt.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Miregaunt.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Miregaunt.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Miregaunt.jpg) A miregaunt [Miregaunts](Lore_Miregaunt.md) are large tree-like creatures that can be found across [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), primarily in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md). Though they also can be found on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md). According to [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) folklore, they have existed as long as the Argonians themselves. Some believe them to be the "wrath of Murkmire". Most Argonians believe them to be related to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) in some way. Some tribes believe that the Hist trees call forth a portion of the swamp to accomplish a specific task, either guarding an area, defending a place, or taking vengeance on someone or something that hurt or hampered the Hist in some way. Others seem to indicate that a miregaunt is an unintended by-product of some unknown Hist activity, called forth by accident and set loose with no specific purpose in mind.

Although they consist mainly of plant substance, they sometimes incorporate other materials into their shape, including mud, stone, vines, and even pieces of ancient architecture. They bear some small resemblance to [lurchers](Lore_Lurcher.md) and similar creatures found in other parts of Tamriel, but in all other respects they appear totally unique creatures.

Every miregaunt has a large cavity in its mid-section. Sometimes an encountered miregaunt's cavity is empty. Other times a random piece of stone or other detritus fills the empty space. In rare cases, something valuable rests within the miregaunt, such as a precious stone, an ancient relic, or even a living creature. The local Argonians believe that these valuable objects have been taken into the miregaunt to purposely protect or imprison them. Miregaunts are part of the land: when one is struck down, it will inevitably come back later.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Miregaunt), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Mudcrab](Lore_Mudcrab.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/93/SR-creature-Mudcrab.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Mudcrab.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Mudcrab.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Mudcrab.jpg) A mudcrab The [Mudcrab](Lore_Mudcrab.md) (or **Mud Crab**) is a common variety of [crab](Lore_Crab.md) capable of camouflaging itself as a small rock. They can be found along every shore and waterway of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and are also a common sight underground. They are not typically aggressive unless cornered or provoked. However, if a mudcrab ever gets a taste of meat it becomes a hunter, and may be a threat to livestock. Mudcrabs are usually not dangerous unless they swarm, as they rely solely on their pincers and burrowing abilities in combat. Mudcrabs are capable of stripping a corpse down to its bones in days, which usually takes weeks for lesser crabs. Some mudcrabs can grow to enormous sizes, and rare albino variants are known to exist.

Although they are inquisitive by nature and can be kept as pets, most citizens of Tamriel regard mudcrabs as disgusting vermin. Mudcrabs have been characterized by certain [fish](Lore_Fish.md) enthusiasts as unhappy creatures, "dour and sunless like the soil in which they sleep". Mudcrabs are prized for their sweet crab meat and legs, and their chitin is sometimes used in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).

## [Mummy](Lore_Mummy.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/ON-npc-Mummy.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Mummy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mummy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mummy.jpg) A mummy [Mummies](Lore_Mummy.md) are a powerful form of [undead](Lore_Undead.md). The practice of mummification of the honored dead is common in many cultures across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). This often involves covering the preserved corpse in distinctive cloth wraps; such [mummy wrappings](Lore_Mummy_wrappings.md) can be used in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md). Sometimes the body is also fitted with a death mask. Bodies preserved in such a manner are liable to rise from the dead, either due to an inability to pass on or due to the actions of a [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md). The process of mummification is believed to protect a corpse's ligaments and muscle from the detrimental effects of rot, thereby greatly increasing its durability, stability, and mobility once reanimated.

The first step to creating a mummy is to soak the decaying corpse in a bath of [salt](Lore_Salt.md) or natron for at least one month to halt decay and remove unpleasant odors. More salts may need to be applied in moist climates such as [Argonia](Lore_Argonia.md) or [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) if the salt bath becomes saturated. A wide variety of embalming tools can be used to remove the vital organs before or after this process, although some necromancers skip this step as there is little practical reason to do so. Any organs removed during the embalming process are stored separately in crypt jars by many cultures on Tamriel. The next step is to wrap the body in cloth or linen to preserve and protect it. The wrappings and the corpse's desiccation results in a greater stiffness of the body in comparison to [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md), requiring proper necromantic rituals to imbue the mummy with enough strength to move itself. However, the result is a much stronger undead servant who can follow commands with more independence and understanding. Mummification is sometimes carried out on living victims instead of corpses.

Undead mummies require a [silver](Lore_Silver.md) or better [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) to be harmed, and often carry horrible [diseases](Lore_Disease.md). Those who take a body part from a mummy can be subjected to a "mummy's curse", and will receive nightly hauntings until the mummy is destroyed.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Mummy), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Mummy), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Mummy)
