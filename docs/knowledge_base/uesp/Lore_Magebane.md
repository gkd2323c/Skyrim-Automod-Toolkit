## Mace of Aevar Stone-Singer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/BM-item-Mace_of_Aevar_Stone-Singer.jpg/200px-BM-item-Mace_of_Aevar_Stone-Singer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-item-Mace_of_Aevar_Stone-Singer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-item-Mace_of_Aevar_Stone-Singer.jpg) Mace of Aevar Stone-Singer The [Mace of Aevar Stone-Singer](Lore_Mace_of_Aevar_Stone-Singer.md) (also called the **Mace of Aevar Stonesinger**) is a prized [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) artifact. According to legend, the eponymous hero used the mace to amplify the Song of the Earth, returning the Gift of the Earth and making the land rich again for the Skaal.

It is traditionally awarded to anyone who completes the Ritual of the Gifts, a sort of pilgrimage around [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) practiced by the Skaal. Though a magnificent and lethal [stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) weapon which inflicts frost damage on foes, the mace is also extraordinarily heavy.

**Found in:** - [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Mace_of_Aevar_Stone-Singer)

## Mace of Molag Bal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg/200px-SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mace_of_Molag_Bal.jpg) Mace of Molag Bal The [Mace of Molag Bal](Lore_Mace_of_Molag_Bal.md), also known as the **Vampire's Mace**, is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) attributed to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), the Lord of Domination and Enslavement. Its enchantment drains the stamina and magicka of its victims and transfers them to the bearer. It also has been known to have the ability to transfer an enemy's strength to its wielder or trap their soul. It has been said to be a good weapon of choice for vanquishing wizards.

Many legends surround the mace, but its origins can be traced back to when Molag Bal deceived an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) blacksmith and enslaved him in pits of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). The blacksmith was turned into a [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md) and forced to forge the mace.

Due to Molag Bal's penchant for meddling in mortal affairs, he grants the mace to those he deems worthy but has also been quite free with his artifact. Molag Bal assumes that when such a powerful artifact falls into mortal hands, a trail of death and destruction is sure to follow. Some believe the mace to be an object of Daedra worship. Molag Bal's followers refer to it as **the Master's Mace**, and often swear by it.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Mace_of_Molag_Bal), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Mace_of_Molag_Bal), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mace_of_Molag_Bal), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Mace_of_Molag_Bal.md), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Mace_of_Molag_Bal)

## Mad God's Dancing Shoes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/ON-item-Mad_God%27s_Dancing_Shoes.jpg/200px-ON-item-Mad_God%27s_Dancing_Shoes.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Mad_God%27s_Dancing_Shoes.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Mad_God%27s_Dancing_Shoes.jpg) Mad God's Dancing Shoes [Mad God's Dancing Shoes](Lore_Mad_God%27s_Dancing_Shoes.md) are a pair of jester-like footwear associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Madness [Sheogroath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). These effects could include [conjuring wheels](Lore_Cheesemancy.md) of [cheese](Lore_Cheese.md), inflicting various forms of damage upon the wearer's foes, enhancing the wearer's speed, restoring stamina, or even reducing the magicka cost of spells, among other possibilities.

## Majeel's Scroll
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-item-Majeel%27s_Scroll.jpg/200px-ON-item-Majeel%27s_Scroll.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Majeel%27s_Scroll.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Majeel%27s_Scroll.jpg) Majeel's Scroll The [Majeel's Scroll](Lore_Majeel%27s_Scroll.md) is a sacred ornament that dates back to the time of the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md). It is an item of importance to the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) culture and religion. Together with [Sword of Shabnam](Lore_Sword_of_Shabnam.md) and [Lamp of the Satakalaam](Lore_Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam.md), they represent the aspects of the Redguard in body, mind, and spirit. The scroll represented the mind.

Together with two other relics, it was dedicated centuries before the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), back when the High Temple of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) was built. Since then, they have been the sacred tokens of Satakalaam's High Temple. Although all temples had relics to honor the gods, the Majeel's Scroll was specific to this temple.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Alik%27r_Desert_Quest_Items#Majeel.27s_Scroll)

## Mask of Sotha Sil
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg/200px-SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg) The Mask of Sotha Sil The [Mask of Sotha Sil](Lore_Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.md) is the **blessed mask** (also known as the **killing mask**, or **warmaker helm**) of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md). It embodies his idea for a *Anuvanna'si* (Tamriel Final), whose one of many goals was where life would undergo a synthesis of organic and non-organic nature (akin to the [Fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md)). Indeed, overtime, Sotha Sil's body would become more and more synthetic, and his mask also changed to undergo this transformation. His followers, the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md), mimic this synthesis with modifying their bodies, as well as craft their headwear in the Clockwork God's image.

It is one of the [Masks of the Tribunal](Lore_Masks_of_the_Tribunal.md), the other two being [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md)'s [War Mask](Lore_Mask_of_Almalexia.md), and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s [Ash Mask](Lore_Vivec%27s_Ash_Mask.md).

## Masque of Clavicus Vile
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/OBR-item-Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg) The Masque of Clavicus Vile The [Masque of Clavicus Vile](Lore_Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile.md) (also known as **The Masque of Clavicus** or **Mask of Clavicus Vile**) is a helm which as its name suggests, is associated with the Daedric Prince [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), and is enchanted with the ability to make the wearer more popular wherever they might go by making them seem charming and trustworthy. Much like other Daedra and their artifacts, Clavicus seems to retain ultimate control of the Masque and may recall it without warning.

The best known story of the Masque tells the tale of Avalea, a noblewoman of some renown. As a young girl, she was grossly disfigured by a spiteful servant. Avalea made a dark deal with Clavicus Vile and received the Masque in return. Though the Masque did not change her looks, suddenly she had the respect and admiration of everyone. A year and a day after her marriage to a well connected baron, Clavicus Vile reclaimed the Masque. Although pregnant with his child, Avalea was banished from the Baron's household. Twenty-one years and one day later, Avalea's daughter claimed her vengeance by slaying the Baron.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Masque_of_Clavicus), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Masque_of_Clavicus_Vile.md)

## Mehrunes Dagon's Axe
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/ON-trailer-Gates_of_Oblivion-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Gates_of_Oblivion-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Gates_of_Oblivion-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Gates_of_Oblivion-Mehrunes_Dagon.jpg) Mehrunes Dagon's Axe [Mehrunes Dagon's Axe](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon%27s_Axe.md) is an artifact associated with Daedric Prince of Destruction Mehrunes Dagon. It is a large and fiery double-bitted axe. It is said that no mortal has earned enough favor to be entrusted with Dagon's own axe, or to know its name.

Mehrunes Dagon was known to wield his axe on numerous occasions, including his attempts to invade Nirn in 1E 2920, 2E 582, and 3E 433, as well as during his assault on the Lunar Lattice in the ancient past. Numerous statues also depict him wielding the weapon, including—but not limited to—those found in the Daedric ruins of Ald Sotha, Ebernanit, and [Ularradallaku](Lore_Ularradallaku.md) on the isle of Vvardenfell, as well as in the mountains of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) in the province of Skyrim. The statue in [Plaza Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom.md) in the southern part of the city of Mournhold, commemorating Almalexia's victory over Mehrunes Dagon, also depicts him wielding an axe during their battle.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Mehrunes_Dagon%27s_Quest), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mehrunes_Dagon), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Mehrunes_Dagon)

The Mnemic Egg takes the form of a seed infused with [Hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md), which visibly flows and pulsates throughout the Egg.

When a Hist tree dies, the death is described as the tree being "born backwards." That is to say, the death of a Hist creates a Mnemic Egg. The memories of that Hist and its Argonians are all contained within the Mnemic Egg. The Hist share a collective consciousness; they have the ability to communicate with one another, and to feel what another Hist is feeling. As a result of the Hist's shared connection, and the fact that the Mnemic Egg is a physical vessel for all of the memories of the dead Hist that birthed it, the Mnemic Egg can be used as a gateway into the collective consciousness of all Hist Trees. This also means that in the wrong hands, a Mnemic Egg can be used to sever the Argonian connection to the Hist.

## Muatra
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-item-Muatra.jpg/200px-ON-item-Muatra.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Muatra.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Muatra.jpg) Muatra [Muatra](Lore_Muatra.md), meaning **Milk Taker**, is a spear wielded by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), one of the living gods of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md). In his younger years, Vivec had a chance encounter with the Hortator of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), telling Nerevar that he would make a legend of the [netch](Lore_Netch.md) longhook in his possession and that it would be his weapon of choice in Nerevar's guard. The longhook was in his possession from the days of his youth where he bonded with his father Irdri who was a netchiman by trade.

During the event known as the Pomegranate Banquet in which Vivec and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) made an unholy union, Vivec noticed his body was tenderly used and pointed this out; Molag Bal replied, stating that his love was accidentally shaped like a spear. Vivec bit new words onto Molag's spear so it could give more than ruin. Disaster was struck when the Chimer, Demons, and Monsters who bore witness to this took out their spears and imitated this biting which caused the earth to crack, spawning a new terrible race that was born of the biters and caused destruction. Vivec, however, had learned a new secret from Molag Bal when he had bitten him, and thus was able to change his own spear into a more terrible thing.

Vivec named his new spear Muatra. The spear in this new form had the ability to turn anyone Vivec touched with it barren and wither them to bones. Vivec used Muatra to destroy many things, including: the biters and their progeny; his own progeny he created with Molag Bal, including eight particularly powerful children who escaped his initial wrath; the last of the [N'chorbal](Lore_N%27chorbal.md), which were terrible rock-skin bugs native to [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md); and, according to some legends, even Nerevar himself. Muatra, along with [Vivec's Lyre](Lore_Vivec%27s_Lyre.md), is often depicted in shrines and tapestries as the harp and spear are symbols of Vivec.

## Mysterium Xarxes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg/200px-OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg) Page 1 of the Mysterium Xarxes The *[Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md)* was [a tome](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes_(book).md) written by Mehrunes Dagon, who scribed it in "the deserts of rust and wounds". It was an artifact of great—and evil—power. The *Xarxes* was said to have a vile corrupting influence; reading from the book required magical protection from its powers, even merely handling it was said to be dangerous. Because of the hazards it poses to its reader, this book was classified as a [Tract Perilous](Lore_Tracts_Perilous.md).

In 2E 582, the Xarxes was stolen from the retreat of Emperor Leovic by Eveli Sharp-Arrow. Calia, one of the Four Ambitions of Mehrunes Dagon, would eventually use the book to learn how to use her power to drain Daedric energies, an ability which would aid in the defeat of high priest of the Waking Flame Egonymic in the process of performing the ritual to create the Ambitions. After Vandacia was defeated and his attempt to merge Nirn and the Deadlands using the power of the Ambition Destron was foiled, the book was given to the Ambition Sombren, who intended to study it to learn more about the Four Ambitions. However, the power of the book took its toll. Influenced by the artifact, Sombren joined Dagon and stole the power of two of the other Ambitions, which he used to start a merging of the Deadlands and Nirn. The crisis was ended when fourth Ambition, Mankar Camoran. After studying the tome, Mankar wrote the *Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes* (also known as the *Mythic Dawn Commentaries*). Inspired by the prophecies and promises within the book, Mankar founded the Mythic Dawn, a Daedric cult devoted to Mehrunes Dagon. The *Xarxes* acted as the cult's holy book, and was stored in the Mythic Dawn's hidden shrine in the caverns beneath [Lake Arrius](Lore_Lake_Arrius.md) in Cyrodiil. Using the power of the book, Mankar created Gaiar Alata, or "Paradise", an alternate realm where the souls of Mythic Dawn cultists went in death, to be resurrected as 'Ascended Immortals', who were revived continuously whenever slain.

In 3E 433, following the Mythic Dawn's assassination of Emperor Uriel Septim VII and all of his legitimate heirs, the Blades infiltrated the cult and stole the *Mysterium Xarxes* from their shrine to Mehrunes Dagon. It was taken to [Cloud Ruler Temple](Lore_Cloud_Ruler_Temple.md), where Martin Septim, the illegitimate son of Uriel and the heir to the throne, translated the *Xarxes* and discovered a way to create a portal to Camoran's Paradise. The ritual required a Great Welkynd Stone, a [Great Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md), a Daedric artifact, and an Aedric artifact. The Hero of Kvatch entered Gaiar Alata and defeated Camoran, retrieving the Amulet of Kings. The *Mysterium Xarxes* was destroyed in the process.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mysterium_Xarxes), [Oblivion Mobile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/OBMobile:Oblivion_Mobile)
