# Nede

— *[Frontier, Conquest](Lore_Frontier,_Conquest.md)* The Nedes (also known as the **Nedic peoples** or rarely **Nedelings**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSOP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AE4-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LA:TUA-3)</sup> were a [human](Lore_Man.md) race (or a group of races) of unknown extraction, who inhabited much of Tamriel during the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and [First Eras](Lore_First_Era.md). The Duraki society of the Nedes who lived in Hammerfell (then called the "Deathlands")<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WLJ-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BUM-5)</sup> largely followed the [Cult of the Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cult_of_the_Stars) which revolved around the worship and study of the [constellations](Lore_Astronomy.md#Constellations), and beings known as the [Celestials](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Celestial). Nedic culture experienced a sharp decline over several centuries as they began to assimilate into other cultures, before being completely exterminated by the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) invasion of Hammerfell.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DNP-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NOTD-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-NPF-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MMS-9)</sup>

## History

### Origins

### The Deathlands of Volenfell and the Beastfolk
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png/200px-ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Celestial_tapestry.png) A Nedic tapestry depicting the Celestials *To the heavens above, we raise our eyes, our hands, and our hearts.*
—Sample from a Nedic prayer<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DNP-6)</sup> While Nedes inhabited several of the modern provinces, it was in the sun-baked wastelands of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) which they knew as the Deathlands that Nedic civilization truly thrived. The Duraki Nedes faced numerous foreign invasions, including both the Ayleids and the Dwemer, who dismissed the Duraki as backward savages to justify subjugating them. These Nedes also frequently clashed with their neighbors, the primitive stone-worshipping [Iron Orcs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Orc),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NOTD-7)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OOT-27)</sup> although there is evidence of harmonic periods between the two peoples.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Nedic Hex Totem-28)</sup> Nedic texts often reference an event called the "Autumn of Snakes", where according to the histories hundreds of mammoth-sized snakes emerged from the ground and devoured whole towns before finally being subdued by the Nede spear-maiden, Ranev the Coal-Eyed Wanderer. Nedic scholars describe the Autumn in meticulous and near-identical detail, though that the event is now known as completely apocryphal.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TUSI-29)</sup>

Despite these frequent conflicts, Duraki culture prospered for centuries in the Deathlands. The Nedes were known to be skilled in masonry, with their bustling cities constructed largely from stone. However, little of this stonework remains, as what wasn't completely destroyed by the invading [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) was often appropriated and modified beyond recognition.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-HRC-30)</sup> While the Duraki were known to be devout star worshippers, during the Yokudan invasion their prayers went unanswered, and many looked to other sources for salvation. This did them little good and the Nedes quickly fell to the Yokudan horde, vanishing from the Deathlands forever.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NOTD-7)</sup>

Nedic settlement within provinces inhabited by [Beast races](Lore_Beastfolk.md) is known to have occurred but is not well-documented. During the Merethic Era, a Nedic fort was known to have been only a short distance from a [Dune](Lore_Dune.md) adeptorium in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-K:T1-31)</sup> Many early human tribes are known to have once inhabited [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), among them being the Horwalli, [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), [Orma](Lore_Orma.md), and [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PGE3BM-32)</sup> though they are never referred to as Nedes and survived well into at least the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The Kothringi are said to be descended from the Nedic humans, and lived in Black Marsh as far back as records go.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONLod-33)</sup>

### Under the Elves
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/ON-place-Skyreach_Pinnacle.jpg/200px-ON-place-Skyreach_Pinnacle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Skyreach_Pinnacle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Skyreach_Pinnacle.jpg) One of the ruined Nedic towers of Skyreach [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/MER-art-The_High_Priestess_%28crop%29.jpg/200px-MER-art-The_High_Priestess_%28crop%29.jpg)](File_MER-art-The_High_Priestess_(crop).jpg.md) [](File_MER-art-The_High_Priestess_(crop).jpg.md) Alessia, leader of the Slave Rebellion In many lands the Nedes existed as a minority, and had no choice but to co-exist relatively peacefully alongside [elves](Lore_Mer.md), oftentimes in a subordinate position. There are records of self-serving Nedic knights going as far back as the Merethic Era, such as [Sir Cadwell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cadwell), the hero of [Cyrod](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and champion of the Third Nedic Massacre who was once a member of [Khunzar-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khunzar-ri)'s Kra'jun.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Ct B-34)</sup>

The proto-Bretons<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-35)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-LADCG-36)</sup> were the Nedes who settled in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). They are known by the retroactive nomenclature, the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md),<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-MDBMM-37)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-RDBA-38)</sup> a moniker later adopted by their Breton descendants.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LATD-39)</sup> Guided by a line of druidic kings, these Nedes survived in ancient High Rock by tapping into, nurturing, and terraforming nature.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONWD-40)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LABHI-41)</sup> If Man or Mer came to High Rock first is debated in scholarly circles. Some believe that Nedic civilization and their Druid King were already established in the province when the Aldmer arrived,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-MDBMM-37)</sup> while others claim that the Nedes arrived in High Rock afterwards, and encountered the elves gradually with a variety of reactions and outcomes.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-15)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-PGE3High-42)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-LSP:OAC-23)</sup> The Direnni established a feudal hierarchy where the Nedes lived at the bottom, while the elven nobles positioned on top. The Nedes of High Rock may have also clashed with the orcs in that region.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Nedic Hex Totem-28)</sup> The Direnni enjoyed many privileges over their human subjects including the 'Perquisite of Coition', the right to engage in recreational intercourse with any Nede of their choice.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TBMOP-43)</sup>

Many Direnni took advantage of this and took on Nedic concubines, which often resulted in the birth of mixed-race "[manmer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Manmer)" offspring who would become the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md). While the Bretons were not officially recognized as members of the noble houses they descended from, they often occupied social positions above those of the Nedic peasantry. Eventually this arrangement was formalized with the creation of a new caste between the Elves and the Nedes, in which the Bretons enjoyed a comfortable position in society, but were forbidden from marrying Elves in an attempt to preserve the Elven race. Eventually after generations of interbreeding with their Nedic underlings the Bretons were completely assimilated, birthing a new culture with those of the most elven blood on top. Even during the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md), they ruled largely through human clients, who later became the Breton nobility.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-LSP:OAC-23)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/15/ON-quest-Captured_Time.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Captured_Time.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Captured_Time.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Captured_Time.jpg) An Alessian knight rebels against his Ayleid oppressor While relations between the Nedes and the Direnni were cordial if rather unbalanced, the Nedes suffered greatly under the tyranny of the Daedra-worshipping [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md). The Ayleids would commonly stage raids on Nedic lands, enslaving those whom they captured.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NOTD-7)</sup> Once in Ayleid lands the Nedes were held in bondage alongside members of various other human races, forced to work in a variety of menial jobs, such as agriculture and maintaining infrastructure. In Ayleid kingdoms under the grip of more malevolent Daedric princes, human slaves were subjected to horrific cruelties.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TA-44)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSOP-1)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-PGE3C-45)</sup>

In the Middle Merethic Era, the Nedes, subjugated by the Ayleids, began to resist with help from a mysterious figure, [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr). Nedic folklore often depicts Shezarr as a teacher and unifier rather than a warrior. Duraki legend credits him with teaching stoneworking and command over nirncrux while the Perena claim he introduced soul magic. In Cyrod, Shezarr supposedly revealed Ayleid battle-magic secrets to humans. A stone tablet from Sedor depicts him as "the [Shezzarine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezzarine), Shor-Who-Lives, Teacher of Men." These tales speak of Shezarr as a source of inspiration to the Nedic resistance against Ayleid oppression, sustaining their spirit until Saint Alessia's rebellion.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TFOS-46)</sup>

In the year [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242), the Slave Queen [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) led a rebellion against the Ayleid slave masters, resulting in the emancipation of all slaves within the Ayleidic empire. While the slaves were freed, centuries of Ayleidic suppression and intermingling with other enslaved groups had taken their toll on Nedic culture in Cyrodiil and all that remained were their mixed race descendants, the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TA-44)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSOP-1)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-PGE3C-45)</sup>

Nedic presence within [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) is unknown, though the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) have been recorded in early history as "soiling Time's line" by taking Mannish wives.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TM-16)</sup>

The Nedes also maintained a presence in Skyrim, but little is known of their relations with the native [Falmer](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) or the [Atmorans](Lore_Nord.md) who replaced them. The Nedes of Skyrim were believed to be a more primitive people than their cousins in other lands, however their skill in metallurgy was known to be quite advanced.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-NDS-47)</sup> They were generally nomads but still had some form of a religious congregation (worshipping [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) instead of the Atmoran [Orkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orkey)).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Lamae Bal-48)</sup> The Nedic peoples of the late Merethic era hunted the lion population of a western valley in [The Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) for its furs.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-It LD-49)</sup>

The Nedes spread across northern Tamriel, and once had a presence in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) during the Merethic,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-15)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Wot P-50)</sup> with tribes that marched and plundered across the land.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Vunal-51)</sup> It is recorded in Dunmeri texts that these Nedes waged at least one bloody war against the ancient [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) in the early First Era, the [Rontha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rontha) being the largest of the tribes to invade [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls). causing mass death and destruction and refusing diplomatic resolutions with the Chimer. After a few short years they controlled most of the modern-day Stonefalls and cut off reinforcements from the north, before being stopped by the so-called [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(creature).md), but at a terrible cost for the Chimer.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TBo S-52)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Vunal-51)</sup> These Nedes were accomplished leatherworkers, attributed to their nomadic nature.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Nedic Wineskin-53)</sup>

### Disappearance and Legacy
*If we will die, we will die together.*
—Fragment from a Nede's journal<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-WJ-54)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-quest-The_Reason_We_Fight.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Reason_We_Fight.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Reason_We_Fight.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Reason_We_Fight.jpg) A Nedic general makes a desperate pact with a nereid to defend Shada's Tear, one of the last standing Nedic cities, from the Yokudans Over the centuries, various events contributed to the dwindling of Tamriel's Nedic population. They had suffered a history of defeat and enslavement at the hands of various peoples including Dwemer, Ayleids, Yokudans, and Direnni. Cyro-Nords captured Nedic ports along the [Strident Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strident_Coast) (a somewhat unknown region presumably around the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) of Cyrodiil and the southern coastline of Hammerfell).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-PGE1C-55)</sup> In the west, The Nedes who inhabited the isle of [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) had long struggled against the elves prior to the Ra Gada, and though they fought a courageous battle under their Warchief Haakon, they fell before the Yokudan onslaught.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Farvad-56)</sup> Everywhere, Indigenous populations were left broken and scattered, absorbing the cultures of conquering people, or migrating to be subsumed in new lands.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NOTD-7)</sup> By the ninth century of the First Era, Nedic culture was confined to the deserts of [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md).

In Cyrod, early Imperial culture had formed during the time of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), reflected in relics containing a mix of Ayleid and early Imperial influences.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FCCR-57)</sup> Inhabitants of Colovia had begun using present-day Imperial names, even while Nedes in nearby Hammerfell maintained their strong cultural presence.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Titus-58)</sup> In the year [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808)<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-HOTIB-59)</sup> the "warrior wave" of the Yokudans, the Ra Gada, made landfall in Tamriel. They quickly swarmed the province, killing or enslaving all that they crossed. Soon they had displaced the Nedes everywhere but in the remote region of [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn). There, Nedes embittered by the decline of their culture began to turn to sources of spiritual guidance other than the stars.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DNP-6)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-WJ-54)</sup> This did them little good when the Anka-Ra, one particularly brutal and conquest-hungry band of Ra Gada led by the sadistic Emperor [Tarish-Zi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tarish-Zi), reached Craglorn and dispatched the Nedes as quickly as they had elsewhere. After years of struggle and decline, Nedic culture had finally met its end.

Though the Nedes no longer exist as a distinct people, much of their culture survives in the societies of their descendants. Even in Hammerfell much of the Nedic religion, custom, and language was adopted by their annihilators, the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-60)</sup> In [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) the great old Nedic traditions were maintained, remaining as one of the people true to the original Nedic culture. The Nibenese people are said to be the closest thing to a pure-bred Nede still remaining on Tamriel.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Abnur Tharn-24)</sup> Ownership of Nedic possessions (particularly those with inscriptions) came with great prestige.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Lt SBX-61)</sup>

## Tribes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/ON-npc-High_King_Durac_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-High_King_Durac_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_King_Durac_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_King_Durac_02.jpg) High King Durac, who presided over the council of Nedic kings from other tribes The Nedic tribes were far from homogeneous. Each tribe came with its own culture, and their cultures were further mixed and admixed after arrival in Tamriel. Some were as different from each other as [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) and [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Abnur Tharn-24)</sup> Though they are said to have come from Atmora, Nords are sometimes considered Nede as well.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FC-15)</sup>

The catch-all term "Nede" is applied so broadly to [Merethic-Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) humans as to be almost worthless.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-21)</sup> The term "Nedic Tribes" actually covers a wide panoply of different human cultures of disputed origin, with a variety of traditions and practices.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Abnur Tharn-24)</sup> Tribes were led by kings and warchiefs, ruling different parts of the Nedic realm, and were known to meet in councils with other Nedic rulers.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SE-62)</sup> They were markedly divergent in appearance, such as those in High Rock mixing with elves to the point of resembling them and the Kothringi of Black Marsh developing silver-colored skin. The known names given to groups of Merethic Era humans are as follows, with their status as solely being Nedes considered to be very general:

- In Black Marsh: - The [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) are said to be descended from the Nedic humans, and lived in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) as far back as records go.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONLod-33)</sup> The [Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septim_Empire) speculated that they may have been sent there as refugees or prisoners, or settled along the coastal waterways and adapted to the environment.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PGE3BM-32)</sup> As tribes of Men such as Kothringi, [Horwalli](Lore_Horwalli.md), [Orma](Lore_Orma.md), and [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md) survived well into the modern age, they are not considered Nedes, and are likely descendants of Nedes in the same way Imperials are.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PGE3BM-32)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TLYFE:2920-63)</sup>
- In Cyrodiil: - The **[Al-Gemha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Al-Gemha)**, **[Al-Hared](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Al-Hared)**, **[Men-of-ket](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Men-of-ket)**, and **Kothri** were among the men herded as slaves by the Ayleids from across all the [Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben) in [Cyrod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrod).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TA-44)</sup>
- The **[Men-of-ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Men-of-ge)** were among the men herded as slaves from across the Niben by the Ayleids, who were eventually "destroyed" when the Flower King [Nilichi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nilichi) made great sacrifice to a long-forgotten [insect god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Insect_God).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TA-44)</sup>
- The **[Perena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Perena)** were a Nedic tribe found in Cyrodiil<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-21)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Nedic Armor Pack-64)</sup> and Hammerfell.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Watcher Ritual Stole-65)</sup> They practiced the observation of the stars in the night sky, according to ancient texts such as the Perenaal Fragment.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SE-62)</sup> Three members of the Perena sect called the Eternal Watchers were responsible for locking away [Virmaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Virmaril) the Betrayer.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Watcher Ritual Stole-65)</sup>
- The **[Thousand-Strong of Sedor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thousand-Strong_of_Sedor)** were an ancient tribe of men of [Sedor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sedor). They were enslaved by Ayleids and later liberated by [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSOP-1)</sup>
- In Hammerfell: - The **[Duraki](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Duraki)** were a tribe that resided in [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) and had their seat of power at Skyreach.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SE-62)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Nedic Armor Pack-64)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-SD-66)</sup> Their warriors followed the ancient Nedic High King Durac against the invading [Anka-Ra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anka-Ra), but met their downfall after [Virmaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Virmaril) the Betrayer slew the council of nedic kings and the tribe fell to the Yokudan onslaught.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-VJ-67)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-WSJ-68)</sup>
- The **[Keptu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keptu)** or **Ket Keptu** were a tribe who settled in [central](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunforge) [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md)<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-CM12:B-69)</sup> and the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) bordering Cyrodiil.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-21)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-NNo RO-70)</sup> They are inconsistently described as being Nedes<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Nedic Armor Pack-64)</sup> and separate from Nedes,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSOP-1)</sup> likely a repercussion of the usage of "Nede" as a catch-all term and oversight of their differences from the Perena.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-21)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TA-44)</sup> The [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) [Dreadhorn Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreadhorn_Clan) are descendants of the Keptu.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Mt C-71)</sup>
- In Northern Tamriel (High Rock, Morrowind, Skyrim): - The **[proto-Bretons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Proto-Breton)**, the Nedic ancestors of the modern Bretons, inhabited High Rock.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-MDBMM-37)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LATD-39)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-RDBA-38)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-WAD-72)</sup>
- The **[Men-of-kreath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Men-of-kreath)** were imported from the north (presumably [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md)) by the Ayleids as slave labor.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TA-44)</sup> King [Kestic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kestic), who ruled a Nedic tribe from a forested region near Skyreach,<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-SD-66)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-VJ-67)</sup> may have been a king of the Men-of-kreath.
- The **[Rontha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rontha)** were a nomadic, particularly vicious clan among those who invaded the [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls) region of Morrowind. They were known for branding both people and items captured in battle.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Nedic Wineskin-53)</sup>
