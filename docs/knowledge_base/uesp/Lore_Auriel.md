# Auri-El

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/ON-statue-Auriel.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Auriel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auriel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auriel.jpg) A High Elf statue of Auri-El *Anuiel, who was the soul [of Anu and] of all things, therefore became many things, and this interplay was and is the Aurbis. At first the Aurbis was turbulent and confusing, as Anuiel's ruminations went on without design. Aspects of the Aurbis then asked for a schedule to follow or procedures whereby they might enjoy themselves a little longer outside of perfect knowledge. So that he might know himself this way, too, Anu created Auriel, the soul of his soul. Auriel bled through the Aurbis as a new force, called time.*
— *[The Monomyth](Lore_The_Monomyth.md)*, "The Heart of the World"Auri-El (or **Auriel**),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AR.2FDF-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> also known as the **King of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md)**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> and the **Fixed Center**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-COTF-5)</sup> is the [elven](Lore_Mer.md) analogue to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAAD-6)</sup> and the chief deity of most Aldmeri pantheons.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> He is the soul of [Anui-El](Lore_Anuiel.md), who is the soul of [Anu the Everything](Lore_Anu.md), and it is said time began with his existence.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup>

Most [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) claim to be direct descendants of Auri-El. In [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), he is known as **Auri-El Time Dragon**, king of the gods.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) and [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) also worshipped Auri-El.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRGelebor-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> In the early days of Aldmeri society, which was highly stratified, Auri-El was regarded as an ancestor spirit of the upper castes. However, his worship gradually spread among the lower castes, who sought to emulate their social superiors. Over time, Auri-El came to be revered as the common progenitor of Aldmeri society as a whole.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-8)</sup> While generally represented as a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), most Altmeri societies also depict him as or alongside an [eagle](Lore_Eagle.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Cont Driftwood-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Cont Winged Auriel-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-COTF-5)</sup>

While generally being considered one of the [original spirits](Lore_Et%27Ada.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> some sources such as the [Codex Scientia](Lore_Codex_Scientia.md) portray Auriel as a "great Elvish [demi-god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demi-God)" or a "quasi-mythical Elvish deity," downplaying his divinity.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AR.2FDF-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ARShield-11)</sup> In contrast, [Bretic](Lore_Breton.md) myth describes the rise of the new **[Aurielian gods](Lore_Altmer.md#Religion_and_Cosmology)**, who usurped Akatosh and the race of dragons in Tamriel’s earliest eras.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-KE-12)</sup> The [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) unsuccessfully attempted to sever the association between Auri-El and Akatosh, and in doing so, were held responsible for the sullied events of the [Middle Dawn](Lore_Middle_Dawn.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SOT-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Bladesongs-14)</sup> All but the most dogmatic of theologians tend to agree that the Imperial Akatosh and the Elven Auri-El are one and the same.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AYQ-15)</sup>

## Mythology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auri-El.jpg) An Ayleid statue of Auri-El [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_%28Vale%29.jpg/200px-SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_%28Vale%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_(Vale).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Inner_Sanctum_(Vale).jpg) A Snow Elf statue of Auri-El
### Altmer-Heart of the World
*[E]ven Auriel, when told he would become the king of the new world, agreed to help Lorkhan.*
—The Heart of the World The Altmer believe that existence began with Anu the Everything, who encompassed and encompasses all things. Seeking to know himself Anu created his soul, Anui-El, who, also given to self-reflection, went on to create [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) as the sum of all limitation via which he'd differentiate between his myriad attributes and ponder himself. An interplay which is the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

The primordial Aurbis was a place of chaos, as Anui-El ruminated without design. Seeking an escape, the [Aspects of Aurbis](Lore_Gods.md) asked Anu for a schedule they could follow to exist outside perfect knowledge. In response Anu created Auri-El, the soul of Anui-El, who spread through Aurbis as the force called Time, allowing the Aurbic aspects to understand themselves and take names.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

One of the aspects was [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), more of a limit than a nature, he wandered Aurbis planting new ideas based on restriction and outlined a plan to create a new world where even the aspects of aspects would be allowed to self-reflect, the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). Many chose to follow, and even Auri-El was swayed by the promise of becoming king of this new world.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> But Lorkhan had tricked the other spirits, as Mundus was a place composed almost wholly of limitation. Many of the [et'Ada](Lore_Gods.md) vanished completely, most of those left followed the flight of [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), becoming the [Magna-Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), but some of those present chose instead to stay after he departed keep working in the new world in order to keep it viable.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Btao M-16)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> Among those Et'Ada who stayed, some would follow the example of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law, and would typically be referred to from that point on as "Earthbones" or "Earth Bones". Others would choose not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, having to "make children just to last", these spirits would from that point typically be referred to as the ["Ehlnofey"](Lore_Ehlnofey.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-17)</sup> The offspring of the Aedra would exhibit a gradual change however, each consecutive generation was weaker than the one prior, more removed from their progenitors in stature and might.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Mystery-18)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

Through this generational phenomenon of diminishment the offspring of the Ehlnofey would would eventually give away and the first [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) would arise, while the "weakest souls" that resulted from it would be formed by [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) into armies he named "Men".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

This participation, an event the Altmer would name the Sundering,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Folly-19)</sup> would be considered Auri-El's only known moment of weakness, an act which forever sundered the elves from the spirit worlds of eternity.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> Auri-El and the other gods of the Aldmer were disgusted by their enfeebled selves and what they had created, and insisted that everything was permanently spoiled, and all they would be able to do would be to teach the elves to suffer with dignity.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

To make up for his error, Auri-El led the original Aldmer against the armies of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) in mythic times, vanquishing him and establishing the first kingdoms of the Aldmer, [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Old Ehlnofey](Lore_Old_Ehlnofey.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> During this conflict Auri-El begged Anu to take them back but Anu refused as he'd already created something to replace them. The more merciful Anui-El created [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) and [Auriel's Shield](Lore_Auriel%27s_Shield.md) to be used by Auri-El against Lorkhan's hordes. Though Atmora was retaken by men and Old Ehlnofey was sundered, Auri-El's greatest [knight](Lore_Knight.md), [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) (who, in some places, was even more popular than Auri-El<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TTNOO-20)</sup>), succeeded in defeating Lorkhan, and Auri-El hid his [Heart](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) by attaching it to an arrow and sending it far into the sea.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>. After [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) was held by the victors and the laws which would govern the mortal world were decided on,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Enigma-21)</sup>, the Aedra departed the world as their direct presence destabilized reality and time.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-NMI-22)</sup> Auri-El ascended to heaven in full observance of his followers so that they might learn the steps needed to escape the mortal plane.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> With this event the gods and magic in the mythic sense exited the world and history, finally linear, began.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Btao M-16)</sup>

### Cyrodiil-Shezarr's Song
*These Aedra, the Gods of the Aldmer, led by Auri-El, were disgusted by their enfeebled selves, and by what they had created. 'Everything is spoiled, for now, and for all time, and the most we can do is teach the Elven Races to suffer nobly, with dignity, and chastise ourselves for our folly, and avenge ourselves upon Shezarr and his allies.*
—Shezarr's Song According to the Cyrodilic Creation Myth, "Shezarr's Song", Shezarr came to the Aedra with a new idea: to become mothers and fathers, and make great sacrifices with no guarantee of success. Shezarr spoke beautifully to them, and moved them beyond mystery and tears. In some legends, [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) is first to agree to Shezarr's proposal, and provided the space for this creation in the void.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> Thus the Aedra gave free birth to the world, the beasts, and the beings, making these things from parts of themselves. This free birth was very painful, and afterwards the Aedra were no longer young, and strong, and powerful, as they had been from the beginning of days.

Some Aedra were disappointed, bitter, and angry with Shezarr, and with all creation, for they felt Shezarr had lied and tricked them. These Aldmeri gods, led by Auri-El, resolved to teach the elves to suffer with dignity, to chastise themselves for their folly, and to seek vengeance on Shezarr and his allies for reducing them to their enfeebled selves. But the gods of men and beast folk, led by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), were pleased with creation despite their great sacrifices, and resolved to teach the mortal races to live well, to cherish beauty and honor, and to love one another as the gods love them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

Auri-El is believed to currently reside in [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) alongside the other elven gods.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRSerana-23)</sup>

## Worship
*Penitent, give thanks and praise to the soul of Anu the Everything, father to us all. The scales and fangs and flame of the creator envelop all of the people. Always.*

*To complete your venerations here, intone: "By the Fixed Center and his hand in our lives, we are all made safe. Auri-El, grant me the stability of the Divine. Be always at my side."*
—Coils of the Father Many Snow Elves had one great desire: to become one with Auri-El. They built the [Chantry of Auri-El](Lore_Chantry_of_Auri-El.md) in the western mountains of modern-day [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) so that they could pursue a pilgrimage of spiritual discovery and ascend into the heavens.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TTS-24)</sup> They would recite the mantras of Auri-El at several wayshrines, meditating on their lessons, before journeying to the Inner Sanctum to ostensibly become one with their god, purportedly vanishing with a look of bliss on their face upon their meeting with the Arch-Prelate.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-25)</sup>

The Altmer use the eagle both in association with Auri-El himself,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Cont Driftwood-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Cont Winged Auriel-10)</sup> and as a symbol representing the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md), their ancestors, who descended from Aetherius and became trapped in physical form by the creation of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOKB-26)</sup> Altmeri prayers reference Auri-El as the Fixed Center of existence who always envelops all people with his "scales and fangs and flame", and invoke him to grant his followers stability.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-COTF-5)</sup>

Bosmer perform acts of veneration, primarily at the beginning of each year, which involve charitable work overlooked in the previous months. However, they hold little affection for him.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TIGTT-27)</sup> There have been cases of Bosmer undertaking [knighthood](Lore_Knight.md) as a form of devotion to Auri-El.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-OBBrellin-28)</sup>

The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) acknowledge both Auri-El and Akatosh as aspects of [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Natrada-29)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Auriel's Bow
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg/200px-SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg) Auriel's Bow [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md), (also spelled **Auri-El's Bow**) is an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) used by the elven god [Auriel](Lore_Auriel.md). Although it commonly takes the form of a modest elven [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) bow, it has also appeared made of [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md). Either way, it is one of the most powerful weapons in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

The bow draws its power from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) itself, channeling it through the [sun](Lore_Sun.md). It has the ability to turn any arrow into a "missile of death", although its enchantment effects vary: it has been known to cause magical fire or shock damage, drain an opponent's stamina and magicka reserves, or harness the power of the sun. It can also make the wielder immune to lesser attacks, and is especially devastating when used against the undead. Occasionally it can be seemingly unenchanted. Without Auriel's power behind it, however, the bow uses its own store of energy for its power. Once exhausted of this energy, the bow will vanish, abandoning its owner to reappear elsewhere.

### Auriel's Shield
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/SR-item-Auriel%27s_Shield.jpg/200px-SR-item-Auriel%27s_Shield.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Shield.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Shield.jpg) Auriel's Shield [Auriel's Shield](Lore_Auriel%27s_Shield.md) is an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) used by the elven god [Auriel](Lore_Auriel.md), the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), Dragon God of Time. It takes the form of a small shield, sometimes rounded, made from either [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) or [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md). It has several magical abilities that help to make its wielder nigh invulnerable. Its known enchantments include resistance to fire, shielding from either magical or physical attacks, healing properties, magical armor enhancement, and spell reflection. Sometimes it also appears to be seemingly unenchanted.

The shield has the ability to absorb the power of blocked attacks; this energy can then be released by bashing an opponent with the shield, delivering a devastating physical blow. Its shielding ability is supposedly unsurpassed. The shield is twinned with [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md).

Like many great artifacts, the shield has a life and personality of its own, and does not feel bound to its user. A popular fable tells of it abandoning its owner in her greatest hour of need, but this story is thought to be apocryphal. Legend says it was created by [Anuiel](Lore_Anuiel.md) at Auriel's request so that the latter could use it in his campaign against the forces of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).
