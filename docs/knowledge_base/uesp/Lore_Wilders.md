# Wilders

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Arana_02.jpg) Arana, leader of the Witch-rebels turned Wayward Guardians (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/ON-render-Arana_02.jpg/200px-ON-render-Arana_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arana_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arana_02.jpg) Witch-rebels, a faction of wilder Reachfolk Wilders,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arllonal-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCA-2)</sup> or **wilder clans**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AC-3)</sup> is a term used by the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) to describe Reachmen and [clans](Lore_Reachfolk_Clans.md) not directly affiliated with [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). These clans inhabit the rocky crags of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Anafira-4)</sup> and do not swear fealty to the Ard.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AC-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SB-5)</sup> This does not apply to all clans in the Reach, as some clans inhabited Markarth, and were loyal and subject to the Ard;<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Linwuuruben-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Gwenyfe-7)</sup> for instance, the [Stonehands](Lore_Stonehands.md) were a clan, which served as Ard's personal guard.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Gwenyfe-7)</sup> Entire clans, such as the [Starsinger Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Starsinger_Clan), were also found in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the rule of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Medresi_Guvron-8)</sup> Some of those [Imperialized Reachfolk](Lore_Imperialized_Reachfolk.md) returned to the Reach, and some of them joined the wilder clans afterward.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Senan-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Arana-10)</sup>

Although Wilders are typically attuned to nature, even they sometimes forget that dark things tend to happen when the land is angry.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arllonal-1)</sup>

The [River-Elks](Lore_River-Elk_Clan.md) were a large wilder clan, often referred to as a "clan of many tents" by other wilders.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Anslettar-11)</sup>

According to Reach customs, Markarth should serve as a neutral territory, accessible to any wilder wishing to visit and trade.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-POTR-12)</sup>

Members of the [Thornroot Clan](Lore_Thornroot_Clan.md) preyed upon innocent Wilders, attempting to transform them into their army of [briarhearts](Lore_Briarheart.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TD-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Medone-14)</sup>

The [Witch Rebels](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md), led by [Arana](Lore_Arana.md) was a faction of the Wilders that opposed the rule of Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the leader of the city Reachfolk of Markarth.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AC-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SB-5)</sup> They eventually reconciled with him, leader of Markarth, and the city Reachfolk, and became known as the Wayward Guardians.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AC-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ardanir-15)</sup>
