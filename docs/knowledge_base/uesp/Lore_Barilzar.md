Barilzar was a powerful [Dark Elf](Lore_Dark_Elf.md) mage who lived in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOBarilzar-1)</sup> who later became a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) lord after creating an infamous artifact known as [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md).

## History
Barilzar was a powerful wizard mostly interested in portal magic and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) mysteries. At some point in his life he was a respected mage of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> For a time, he was a [Clockwork Apostle](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) and studied with [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) in his [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). There he would learn many things and was even entrusted with a device created by Sotha Sil called the [Seht's Affect Inducer](Lore_Seht%27s_Affect_Inducer.md), a magical device that can manipulate a person's emotions. Afterwards, he moved out of the Clockwork City to an ancient [Velothi](Lore_Velothi.md) tower called Odirniran in the [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), where he continued his research and performed his experiments.

### Crisis on Vvardenfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-npc-Barilzar.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Barilzar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Barilzar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Barilzar.jpg) Barilzar around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Barilzar played a prominent role in thwarting [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s plot to gain access to the Clockwork City around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). When [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) began to lose his divine energy, he allied himself with [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and sent them to Barilzar's tower to obtain information. Barilzar suggested that the staff which Sotha Sil created as part of his research of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md)'s divine powers, [Sunna'rah](Lore_Sunna%27rah.md), might be responsible for Vivec's loss of energy.

Later, when the Vestige learned that Sunna'rah was in the hands of the young [Urshilaku](Lore_Urshilaku.md) Ashkhan [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md), Barilzar provided a way to defeat him: he invented and built a tonal inverter, a device capable of disrupting the staff's power, from the parts recovered by the Vestige from the Dwemer ruins of Galom Daeus, [Nchuleft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nchuleft), and Arkngthunch-Sturdumz.

The Vestige and Chodala's sister Seryn defeated Chodala and retrieved Sunna'rah. When they returned to Vivec and used it to return his divine powers to him, it instead drained most of his remaining power, transferring it to the Clockwork City. This almost caused [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) to fall from the sky, as well as made the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) rain fire onto the [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_(city).md). This turn of events was revealed to be a ruse orchestrated by [Archcanon Tarvus](Lore_Tarvus_Indoril.md) —who in reality was [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md), [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s loyal servant, who took the form of the late archcanon.

The sudden transfer of most of Vivec's divine energy to the Clockwork City "lit it up", which allowed Barbas to discover its location. He traveled there to make final preparations for Clavicus Vile's arrival. Barilzar and the Vestige soon followed. Barilzar took the Vestige to Seht's Vault beneath [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), where the City – in reality a tiny clockwork structure about the size of a large crate – was located.

Barilzar and the Vestige entered the City, then fought their way through the fabricants guarding it to finally reach Barbas. The Vestige defeated Barbas before he could allow Clavicus Vile to enter the Clockwork City. Afterwards, Barilzar and the Vestige returned to Vivec and gave him back his divine energy. With Vvardenfell safe, Vivec rewarded everyone who took part in stopping Barbas and Clavicus Vile, including the Vestige and Barilzar. Balrizar was rewarded with gemstones containing tiny fractions of divine energy. Barilzar intended to use these gems to improve portal magic, and subsequently returned to his tower to continue his research on it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOMW-3)</sup>

### Almalexia's plot
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/TR-creature-Barilzar.jpg/200px-TR-creature-Barilzar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-creature-Barilzar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-creature-Barilzar.jpg) Barilzar as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Sometime in the middle Second Era when his hair had turned gray, Barilzar created a powerful artifact, the [Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRGD-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:316</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TESLCard-6)</sup> It was primarily a device used for teleportation, but it also had the ability to "destroy the hearts and minds of mortals",<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRB-7)</sup> as well as to open gates to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md)<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TRTO-8)</sup> and summon "hellish creatures best left in a nightmare". It was considered an evil and cursed artifact, during an unknown event it was said to be stripped of its power so only a god or one of divine birth could take advantage of its full power and not be destroyed.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TRPM-9)</sup> Barilzar was transformed into a [lich](Lore_Lich.md), but whether it was intentional or not was unknown. Journals written by Barilzar in the Second Era imply he was looking into the dark arts to extend his life.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Some also believed that the Band itself did it to him,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TRTO-8)</sup> and Barilzar himself claimed he hated his existence as a lich.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRB-7)</sup> Barilzar and the ring then disappeared from history.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:316</sup>

When [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) sent the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) to retrieve the Mazed Band for her in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Barilzar was discovered in an abandoned crypt beneath Mournhold, guarding the ring for all eternity in his undeath. The Nerevarine fought Barilzar in the crypt and defeated him, ending his life for good.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRBarilzar-12)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRB-7)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRBMB-13)</sup>

## Notes
- In *[Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Tribunal)* Barilzar's unique non-player character model file is `liche_king.nif`, which bears a unique crown (as well as recycled pieces of [Royal Guard Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Royal_Guard_Armor) assets).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Liche King Nif-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> In the final game Barilzar is referred to as an "ancient lich lord" in the player quest journal, but the "lich king" description from the data files may or may not apply to the actual character—though the fact that the model contains a crown seems indicative of that intent.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRBMB-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Liche King Nif-14)</sup> - The stakes of Barilzar's crown visually resembles the [fleur-de-lis](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fleur-de-lis) symbol.
- Barilzar in *Tribunal* uses a spell entirely unique to him called "[Harthoon's Heavy Eyes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Harthoon%27s_Heavy_Eyes)".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRBarilzar-12)</sup> It is unknown who or what Harthoon is. - Most unique spells in *Tribunal* are associated with the character that casts them, for example there's [Barenziah's Fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Barenziah%27s_Fire) or [Gedna's Barrier](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Gedna%27s_Barrier)/[Gedna's Refill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Gedna%27s_Refill). Which may or may not mean "Harthoon" was the original name of Barilzar during development.
