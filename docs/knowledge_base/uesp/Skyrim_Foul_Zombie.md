# Zombie

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/SR-npc-Zombie.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Zombie.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Zombie.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Zombie.jpg) A zombie [bandit](Skyrim_Bandit.md), followed by his [puppetmaster](Skyrim_Necromancer.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/SR-npc-Nord_%28NPC%29.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Nord_%28NPC%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Nord_(NPC).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Nord_(NPC).jpg) The moment of resurrection **Zombies** are any [creature](Skyrim_Creatures.md) or [person](Skyrim_NPCs.md) that has died and been temporarily resurrected. Zombies are still capable of warfare despite no longer being able to think for themselves; thus, they are often used as extra bodies in a fight. Zombies are most typically created by [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md) and [necromancers](Skyrim_Necromancer.md) for use in combat; however, you may also create your own. Zombies are typically resurrected through the use of a temporary [reanimation](Skyrim_Reanimate.md) spell or enchantment. For more details about the use of zombies as allies, see [this page](Skyrim_Reanimate.md).

Most NPC zombies will groan while animated and may say *"Must...kill,"*, *"You...must...die,"* and *"Kill,"* while in combat. They will occasionally make comments indicating gratefulness for release from their undeath upon defeat. Zombies disintegrate into lootable ash piles at death or once the reanimation spell effect wears off, unless they were reanimated by [Dead Thrall](Skyrim_Dead_Thrall.md), [The Ritual Stone](Skyrim_The_Ritual_Stone.md) power, or with staves. They cannot be [soul trapped](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md) because they've already died before.

## Plague of the Dead<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Plague_of_the_Dead.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/SR-creature-Zombie.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Zombie.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Zombie.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Zombie.jpg) A zombie [Plague of the Dead](Skyrim_Plague_of_the_Dead.md) adds a new **Zombie** creature. Similar in appearance to [Ash Spawn](Skyrim_Ash_Spawn.md), these zombies wield generic weapons and drop an ingredient called [Mort Flesh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mort_Flesh). Zombie hordes can be encountered randomly as a [world interaction](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md) or as part of the related quest, [The Rising Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Rising_Dead). They can also be [summoned](Skyrim_Conjure_Zombie.md) and encountered at set spawn points in the world.

### Variants
All zombies have [frost resistance](Skyrim_Resist_Frost.md), [magic resistance](Skyrim_Resist_Magic.md), [poison immunity](Skyrim_Resist_Poison.md), and [waterbreathing](Skyrim_Waterbreathing_(effect).md). Most zombies have a [weakness to fire](Skyrim_Weakness_to_Fire.md), except for Dread Zombies, which also have a natural [health regeneration](Skyrim_Regenerate_Health.md) ability during combat. Zombies can't cast spells however, as they have no magicka.

In general, aside from abilities, zombies have stats similar (if not identical) to [Draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md), with each version having a counterpart. The generic Zombie is equivalent to Draugr, Foul Zombie to Restless Draugr, Putrid Zombie to Draugr Wight, Malignant Zombie to Draugr Scourge, Relentless Zombie to Draugr Deathlord, and Dread Zombie to Draugr Death Overlord.

Zombies wield a leveled one-handed or two-handed melee weapon. They are initially of [iron](Skyrim_Iron.md) or [steel](Skyrim_Steel.md) quality, while [Orcish](Skyrim_Orcish.md) start to appear at level 9, [Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md) at level 15, and [elven](Skyrim_Elven.md) at level 22, though iron and steel are four times more likely to appear than the other types.
