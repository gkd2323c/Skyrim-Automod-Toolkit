# Ammonite

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/BS-sprite-1WAL01.png/200px-BS-sprite-1WAL01.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-sprite-1WAL01.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-sprite-1WAL01.png) Ammonite and trilobite fossils (*Battlespire*) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-stolen-Topaz.png) Ammonites (also known as **Eltheric Ammonites**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mot EA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AEG-2)</sup> are extinct aquatic [mollusks](Lore_Mollusk.md) once native to the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) and known only for the beautiful shells they left behind. Although the true origin of these shells is unknown, the consensus among naturalists is that ammonites predate even the oldest of [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) ruins.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mot EA-1)</sup> Fossilized ammonites, along with [trilobites](Lore_Trilobite.md), could also be found in the stone walls of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Ammonites are most commonly harvested for use by mages and [druids](Lore_Druids.md) due to the fact that they contain stores of raw [magicka](Lore_Magic.md). The capacity varies depending on the shell, with some only holding trace amounts of magicka and others holding nearly as much as a lesser-grade [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gems.md). However, ammonites cannot be recharged and suffer from arcane decay, losing potency within a month of being harvested. Nevertheless, they are an important source of magicka for magic users on [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), and they are an important trade good for the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mot EA-1)</sup> Untapped ammonites can sometimes be identified by the faint, shimmering glow they emit.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Depleted shells become dull and do not glow or shine.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

As a possession of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), the Systres became a center for ammonite export. Ammonite mines were opened across the islands, and excavation exploded for a time in the [1E 2800 s](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2800), driven by [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md) warlock-fashion. Under the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), prisoners continued to be sent to [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md) to work the island's ammonite and [larimar](Lore_Larimar.md) mines.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) oversaw ammonite mining activities at Brokerock Mine in eastern Amenos.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Various complex guides and diagrams depicting the process of drawing arcane energy from ammonites were relatively common as of then.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AEG-2)</sup>

The [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) [Dark Elf](Lore_Dark_Elf.md) mage-philosopher Dothasi Salvi believed that ammonites are a natural resource and a more moral way to obtain extra magicka in comparison to [soul-trapping](Lore_Soul_Trap.md). The [Wood Elf](Lore_Wood_Elf.md) mage-philosopher Lagrenthir of [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) contended that the use of ammonites should be shunned due to their native range's proximity to [Thras](Lore_Thras.md), and the possibility that their existence might therefore be the result of some form of [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) magic.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mot EA-1)</sup>

It has been theorized that [copper](Lore_Copper.md) may be used to detect the presence of ammonites.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
