The Dreugh ([/d r ɛ ɡ/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) listen Link ⏵)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOLive Episode2-1)</sup> are an ancient species of aquatic, [octopus](Lore_Octopus.md) -like [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> commonly hunted by Dreughers for their hide and the [wax](Lore_Dreugh_Wax.md) scraped from their shells.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> They are sometimes known as **water dreugh**<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CETD-6)</sup> or **cepholomer**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-PGE2H-7)</sup> with a known variant being the **dreugh man o'war**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BGN-8)</sup> The dreugh live on land and in water during different stages of their lives.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup>

Dreugh are known to be immune to paralysis, poison, and disease, and resistant to magic, but have a low tolerance to frost. They can regenerate from wounds in darkness and in water, but are damaged by the magical protections that safeguard temples and other holy places.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Their infamous shock attacks are unique to land dreugh, and those in the water must rely on melee attacks.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-11)</sup>

According to various legends, the dreugh once possessed a great civilization.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DUG-12)</sup> Having ruled the whole world as [kings](Lore_Trident-Kings.md), in their mysterious domain of [Lyg](Lore_Lyg.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MDC4-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV28-14)</sup>

## History and Mythology

### Rulers of Lyg
*And when the whole of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) was a tidal ocean, with left behind ideas, there was a tribe unwilling...*
—Unknown<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-15)</sup> The origins of the dreugh are clouded in myth. According to various legends, the dreugh once ruled the world as tyrants, and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) had served as their chief, having a form closely resembling that of his followers.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV28-14)</sup> According to the teachings of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), the tyrannical dreugh-kings ruled over all of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and enslaved the oceans in their land of Lyg, which was said to have been destroyed in the former [kalpa](Lore_Kalpa.md) by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MDC4-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TNC-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PGE2Con-17)</sup> Similar accounts are mentioned in ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) myth, which states the Demon Cat Dagon (also called Merrunz) and the Demon King Molagh fought in ancient conflict.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

In the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region, the dreugh are rumored to be the "cursed" remnants of a once proud prehistoric civilization from long before the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DUG-12)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-DFPreview-19)</sup> The esoteric *[Tsaesci Creation Myth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Tsaesci_Creation_Myth)* makes reference to "dreugh-waters" coming into being during "the Shedding".<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

### Ancient Morrowind and Tribunal Mythology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/MW-concept-15.jpg/200px-MW-concept-15.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-15.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-15.jpg) The Ruddy Man in battle with Vivec
(Morrowind concept art) The ancient [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) once had dealings with the dreugh; [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) teachings speak of the treacherous "[Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of the sea" and their castles of glass and coral.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOV1-21)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV28-14)</sup> It is said they even took the forms of dreugh during the first of the "[Altmeri formwars](Lore_War_of_Manifest_Metaphors.md)", during which they are claimed to have sieged the [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) [meatmerchants](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md).<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-How Beautiful Among Us-22)</sup>

In the mythical account of the living god [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md)'s birth presented in the *[36 Lessons of Vivec](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md)*, Vivec's mother was approached by [Ayem](Lore_Almalexia.md) and informed an image of Vivec resided within her before being tossed in the sea by Ayem to the dreugh who changed her sex so she could give birth to the image as an egg.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOV1-21)</sup> These dreugh were said to have taught Vivec how to use his "Water Face" before he was born.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LOV16-23)</sup> Later in these accounts of his life, Vivec had a sexual union with Molag Bal, the former ruler of the dreugh, known as the Pomegranate Banquet.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LOV12-24)</sup> Legends circulated that the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Knowledge, [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), spied on Vivec and Bal during the Pomegranate Banquet, which angered the two. In retaliation, an army of dreugh was sent after Mora's shrine of worship located in [Anudnabia](Lore_Anudnabia.md). The shrine was destroyed and dragged beneath the sea and no more shrines to Mora were allowed to be built in Vvardenfell.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MMI-25)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TIS-26)</sup> Though other sources indicate Mora's shrine being consumed by the sea was more likely an act of [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

During the banquet, Vivec gave birth to many monstrous offspring,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LOV12-24)</sup> one of them was known as the [Ruddy Man](Lore_Ruddy_Man.md). The Ruddy Man took the form of a dead carapace that was the old image of Molag Bal from when he was the king of the dreugh. When this dead carapace was dropped into the world, a Chimer child from [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md) wore it to scare his village, but instead became the Ruddy Man. Vivec did battle with the Ruddy Man in Koal Cave, and their fighting created the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md) region of Vvardenfell. Vivec was victorious, and went to give the carapace to the dreughs who modified his mother. However their leader, the Queen of Dreughs (whose name is not easy to spell), was in a period of self-incubation. Her wardens took the carapace from Vivec in her stead and promised to safeguard it, but this is the first recorded account of the dreughs being liars. Instead, the dreugh imbued the living armor with mythic inflexibility. The Ruddy Man appeared again ten years later at [Tear](Lore_Tear.md), this time worn by a wayward shaman of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md). After defeating him a second time, Vivec gave the carapace to the devout and loyal mystics of the Number Room, who made it into "a philosopher's armor".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV28-14)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TPP-28)</sup>

After their betrayal, Vivec no longer trusted the dreugh.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV28-14)</sup> Tribunal mythology claims he created [nix-hounds](Lore_Nix-Hound.md) to hunt the dreugh during a time-lost campaign against them.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-15)</sup> The site of the Ruddy Man and Vivec's first battle, Koal Cave, would become the site of the Shrine of Valor, which was set up by the Tribunal Temple and served as part of the Pilgrimage of the Seven Graces. Pilgrims were told that the Ruddy Man was the father of all dreughs, and that Vivec spared his life on the condition that he and his children agreed to give up their tough hides as armor for the Dunmer.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TPP-28)</sup> The dreughs' protracted conflict with Dunmer hunters is blamed for their devolved intelligence and the further destruction of their civilization.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CETD-6)</sup>

### Later History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-creature-Ruddy_Broodmother.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Ruddy_Broodmother.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ruddy_Broodmother.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ruddy_Broodmother.jpg) The Ruddy Broodmother One obscure account mentions dreugh aiding in the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) invasion of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200), attacking the [Coral Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coral_Tower) itself.<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-29)</sup> In [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-30)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-PGE3M-31)</sup> Vivec unexpectedly formed a brief alliance with the [Trident-Kings](Lore_Trident-Kings.md) of the dreugh who would fight together with the Dunmer of Morrowind against the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci.md) invasion.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-CNC-32)</sup> Despite this brief alliance, in the centuries leading to [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) most of the dreugh of Morrowind were driven out, leading to many of them only living in isolated pockets.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup>

Circa 2E 582, a dreugh known as the Ruddy Broodmother began terrorizing the roads around Molag Mar, killing pilgrim and Buoyant Armiger alike. The Ruddy Broodmother made its lair within the Dreudurai Glass Mine, which the [Erabenimsun](Lore_Erabenimsun_Tribe.md) knew as ancient dreugh spawning grounds.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOIbaal-33)</sup> The Vestige, with the help of a group of Ashlanders, drew it out of hiding and slew it. A pilgrim who survived the incident and fled into an ancestral tomb believed the creature to be the mythical Ruddy Man, though Ashlanders denied its existence, claiming that the Broodmother was simply a powerful and cunning dreugh. The Buoyant Armigers considered the thought of the beast being the Ruddy Man borderline heresy. The captain of the Buoyant Armigers at the time suggested that if the Ruddy Broodmother truly was related to the creature of legend, that it might be a lesser creature "cut from the same cloth" as the Ruddy Man.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESONaros-34)</sup>

Dubious [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) records speak of civilized Dreugh living around the [Dellese Isles](Lore_Dellese_Isles.md) in the early [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-PGE2H-7)</sup> Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) a Dreugh warlord lived in the waters of Koal Cave, but was slain by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) as part of the Pilgrimage of the Seven Graces.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Warlord-35)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-36)</sup>

When the floating city of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) arrived over [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) in [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), the dreugh that lived in the surrounding waters knew to flee from the sight of it.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Lo S-37)</sup>

## Behavior and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/LG-cardart-Dark_Harvester.png/200px-LG-cardart-Dark_Harvester.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dark_Harvester.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dark_Harvester.png) A land dreugh preparing fish
(Legends) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/MW-concept-Dreugh.jpg/200px-MW-concept-Dreugh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Dreugh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Dreugh.jpg) A water dreugh wielding a bident
(Morrowind concept art) Water dreugh are mostly mild-mannered, and usually scavenge for food.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> Occasionally, dreugh will cut fishermens' nets while scavenging to steal the fish inside.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> Dreugh are omnivores, and have been seen eating [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) flesh.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> When hunting, they prefer dark, dank places from which to snatch unsuspecting prey.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOIbaal-33)</sup>

Dreugh are troglophiles, and can often be found living in caves and underwater grottos.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-38)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-39)</sup> Dreugh are found mostly in northern, western and eastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md),<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-40)</sup> but can usually be found anywhere coastal that isn't in frigid waters.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Legionnaire Interview-41)</sup> They are known to inhabit the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) and the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), migrating into the waterways of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> Land dreugh can be found as far inland as [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CETD-6)</sup>

Despite being far from their more lofty origins,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CETD-6)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DUG-12)</sup> the dreugh still possess some civilization.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Warlord-35)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-PGE2H-7)</sup> They were historically ruled by the Trident-Kings as late as the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-CNC-32)</sup> and they are rumored to have underwater colonies or cities.<sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-GN-42)</sup> Legends tell of civilized land dreugh colonies which raised herds of [mudcrabs](Lore_Mudcrab.md) as food stock. These colonies purportedly built stone cairn houses and structures. While it is claimed no evidence of such advanced behavior exists today,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> the colony of dreugh that inhabited Fungal Grotto in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) were known to have command over mudcrabs, and had a king.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-KS-43)</sup> Land dreugh are dexterous enough to use knives to prepare fish for consumption.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-44)</sup>

While it is said dreugh have no known [language](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Languages) or communication skills,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> many in the Iliac Bay region do apparently understand [Impish](Lore_Imp.md), and will even cease hostilities towards those that speak it.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DFImpish-45)</sup> Dreugh are known to have historically followed Molag Bal,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV28-14)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MMI-25)</sup> and later worshipped his manifestation, the Ruddy Man,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CETD-6)</sup> whom they considered the god of their people.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-KCS-46)</sup>

### Life Cycle
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/ON-creature-Dreugh.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Dreugh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Dreugh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Dreugh.jpg) A land dreugh (ESO) For one year of their life, dreugh undergo *karvinasim* and emerge onto land as aggressive crustaceans to [breed](Lore_Sex.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> During this period they are known as **land dreugh** or **Billies**.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-47)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-48)</sup> Land dreugh carapaces are very sturdy, and can deflect most attacks from common weaponry.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> They favor shoreline marshes and rivers close to the open water.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> Dreugh are capable of channeling some form of shock energy through their bodies, and usually do this when defending their nests from invaders.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> Most scholars agree this is a natural adaptation, and not magical in nature.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> There is said to be a reverse process of karvinasim called *akarvina*, where land creatures instead become creatures of the sea.<sup>[[UOL 9]](#cite_note-TPMorrowind Moonlight-49)</sup>

Land dreugh lay eggs which are surrounded in a protective jelly,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-48)</sup> then encase their eggs in mud.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> It is thought that karvinasim heightens the dreugh's martial instincts, making them more aggressive.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> Hatchlings are closely guarded, and broodmothers are extremely territorial.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> Unlike their aquatic counterparts, the land dreugh show no signs of their usual intelligence and kill indiscriminately,<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-BAJ-50)</sup> even going so far as to cocoon living victims in mud to feed their young.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ESOTDHA-51)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-52)</sup> Not much else is known about their nesting habits.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup> Dreugh are very protective of their eggs, and are unafraid of invading mortal settlements or destroying ships to find them.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TDT-53)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TOTSA-54)</sup> If their eggs have gone missing, they can sense where the missing eggs are.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TDT-53)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TOTSA-54)</sup> Fisherfolk occasionally harvest eggs from land dreugh mud nests, though this is a dangerous task.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NOTD-5)</sup>

After their year on land, dreugh undergo *meff*, where they digest their unneeded skin and organs and vomit the congealed remains as small fibrous balls called "grom".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> Grom is foul-smelling, and can make those with a weak stomach queasy.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-9)</sup> There is no known alchemical use for grom, but boiling the substance allows it to be shaped to create potion flasks that don't break when dropped.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-FEJ-55)</sup>

Despite their life cycle being well-documented, some scholars argue that the land dreugh is actually a distant relative of the true dreugh from further back on the evolutionary timeline, perhaps even related to [Spider Daedra](Lore_Spider_Daedra.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-BAJ-50)</sup>

## Uses
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Dreugh_Wax.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Dreugh_Wax.png) Dreugh Wax (Skyrim Creation Club) The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) use dreugh wax in alchemy for its modest magical properties,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-MWWax-56)</sup> and their hide to make traditional armor and weapons.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-57)</sup> Waterstones are calcified deposits found in dreugh organs. They absorb fresh water, and may be swallowed to stave off dehydration.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ESOWaterstone-58)</sup> Dreugh are sometimes hunted for their meat, which can be more easily extracted from the shell via the use of specialized tools.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESOCorer-59)</sup> Dreugh meat apparently smells like [lobster](Lore_Lobster.md).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Maj-60)</sup>

Dreugh wax can be used to make candles,<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ESOCandle-61)</sup> boot polish,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESOPolish-62)</sup> and wax stoppers used to close open bottles.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ESOCorker-63)</sup> Dreugh wax corkers are slightly controversial; some people who use them say they make drinks more flavorful, and others say it leaves a waxy aftertaste on the lip of the bottle.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ESOCorker-63)</sup> Dreugh wax can also be used to make ladles whose only purpose is to spoon [scrib jelly](Lore_Scrib_Jelly.md) when cooking, as the jelly won't stick to the wax.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ESOLadle-64)</sup> Dreugh wax ladles are useless in all other culinary applications.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ESOLadle-64)</sup> Their wax is also used to treat netch leather armor, to keep it in fighting shape.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-STD-65)</sup> Dreugh claws can be used to make knives and ornamental objects, such as candle holders.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ESOKnife-66)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-ESOCandle2-67)</sup> Grom is occasionally preserved in vinegar and stored in sealed glass decanters by the Dunmer.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-ESODecanter-68)</sup> Bright blue ink can be brewed from dreugh ichor.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ESOInk-69)</sup> Open-bottomed spoons made of [Bogmother](Lore_Bogmother.md) dreugh-shell are used in the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) ritual of the Empty Feast of Nullity.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-ESOSpoon-70)</sup> Dreugh-shell is also suitable for making decorative plaques with which to mount trophies and other objects.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-ESOPlaque-71)</sup> Rich, dark red pigment can be extracted from dreugh shell and used for a variety of artistic and cosmetic purposes, such as lip stain.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ESOPaint-72)</sup> When mixed with mushrooms, dreugh excrement can be used to make repellant that drives dreugh (or anything that can smell) away from fishermen.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ESORepellant-73)</sup>

## Notes
- The dreugh were one of the last character models designed for *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*. It had been decided that there was not enough variety in underwater creatures, so the dreugh was invented to "pep things up".<sup>[[UOL 10]](#cite_note-DFTDE-74)</sup>
- [Dreugh Shrimp](Lore_Bestiary_S.md#Shrimp) and [Dreughfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreughfish) are both named after the species.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ESOShrimp-75)</sup>
