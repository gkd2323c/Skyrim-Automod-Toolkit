[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg/200px-SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Cracked_Tusk_Keep.jpg) Gularz Khazgur, a ruined Orcish keep in Falkreath Yashnag gro-Yazgu was an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) chieftain best known for establishing a chiefdom in [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) during the early- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Despite resistance from the High King, he was successful in claiming territory for the Orcs in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and ruled for over thirty years. He was eventually killed in battle by Jarl [Hakkvild](Lore_Hakkvild_Yashnag-Slayer.md), whose father had previously been killed by Yashnag, and the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) who viewed Yashnag's Kingdom as a nuisance celebrated the chief's demise. To the Orcs however, Yashnag is venerated for his might in establishing an Orc kingdom in Skyrim in spite of the ignorant Nords.

## Biography
After [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) was sacked by [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) forces in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), Yashnag and his clan fled east to reclaim lands in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) they felt were theirs by ancient right. [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md), the king of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md), was unsuccessful in preventing Yashnag claiming territory, and eventually his chiefdom became a bane upon Western Falkreath for more than thirty years. During this time, Yashnag sacked various forts, such as Faldar's Tooth, and killed the Jarl of Falkreath on the field of battle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OOS-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONloadfaldar-2)</sup>

Yashnag wielded an axe in battle, which is sometimes sworn upon by Orcs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dragon Dialogue-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-i FWyou Heavy-4)</sup> [King Thunder](Lore_King_Thunder.md), an Orcish drum renowned for both its bass tone and impressive volume, was hidden during the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md) and eventually made its way to Yashnag gro-Yazgu's stronghold in Falkreath.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KT-5)</sup>

Yashnag was later challenged in a ritual trial by combat by [Hakkvild](Lore_Hakkvild_Yashnag-Slayer.md), the Jarl's son who had inherited the crumbling hold. One-by-one, Hakkvild defeated all of Yashnag's champions and finally killed the chieftain himself, eradicating the Orc chiefdom of Western Falkreath in [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467). With the destruction of Yashnag's chiefdom, the Orcs scattered further into Skyrim or back into the mountains of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OOS-1)</sup> Some of the exiled Orsimer went east to [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth.md) and established Forsaken Stronghold, though the last Orcs in the stronghold died in [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563) after they were visited by a merchant infected with [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONload FS-6)</sup> Even after his death, Yashnag's memory lived on. During the mid-Second Era, [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](Lore_Kurog.md) sought to follow in his footsteps and rebuild his kingdom in Falkreath.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TCOKK-7)</sup> Various oil paintings of the chieftain were also in circulation.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cont Yashnag-8)</sup>
