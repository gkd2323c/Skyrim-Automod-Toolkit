***Lord of Souls*** is the second of two [novels](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels) written by [Greg Keyes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Greg_Keyes) that follow the story of *[The Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)* between the events of *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* and *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)*. The book was published by [Del Rey Books](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Del_Rey_Books), part of the [Random House Publishing Group](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Random_House), on September 27, 2011.

The shadow of the Infernal City, [Umbriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels_Umbriel), moves across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), killing those that pass beneath its and raising the dead to power itself.

Reeling from a devastating discovery, [Prince Attrebus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels_Prince_Attrebus_Mede) continues on his seemingly doomed quest to obtain a magic sword that holds the key to destroying the deadly invaders. Meanwhile, in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), the spy [Colin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels_Colin_Vineben) finds evidence of betrayal at the heart of the empire—though his own heart may betray him first. And [Annaïg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels_Anna%C3%AFg_Ho%C3%AFnart), trapped in Umbriel itself, has become a slave to its dark lord and his insatiable hunger for souls. How can these three unlikely heroes save [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) when they cannot even save themselves?
