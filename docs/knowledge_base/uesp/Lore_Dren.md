# Dren

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/MW-place-Dren_Plantation.jpg/200px-MW-place-Dren_Plantation.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Dren_Plantation.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Dren_Plantation.jpg) The Dren Plantation (Morrowind) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.png/200px-LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.png) Duke Vedam Dren The Dren family are a noble and prominent clan of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) in the [Great House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWGen Vedam-1)</sup> Its members have held high positions across [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and even across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Two of its most well-known members include [Vedam Dren](Lore_Vedam_Dren.md), the Duke of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the late Third Era and [Narsis Dren](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Narsis_Dren), the famed explorer from the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). The family historically owned the **Dren Plantation**, a [netch](Lore_Netch.md) farm on the strip of land between [Lakes Amaya](Lore_Lake_Amaya_(Vvardenfell).md) and [Hairan](Lore_Lake_Hairan.md), in the [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWPlantation-2)</sup>

## History
*When you hear something unbelievable, don't believe it.*
—Vedam and Orvas Dren's father<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWVedam-3)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Mistress Rilasi Dren was the proprietor of the Dren Plantation and was associated with the Magistrate of [Suran](Lore_Suran.md), Hlaren.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONRilasi-4)</sup> Most notable of all was Narsis Dren, a self-proclaimed famous explorer that traveled all over the continent and wrote stories detailing his adventures, such as *Narsis Dren and the Draugr Dilemma*.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONNarsis-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONElfbetta-6)</sup> He is named after the city of [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md), the ancient capital of House Hlaalu.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-UHH-7)</sup>

There were several other Drens active in 2E 582, including Adavos Dren of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), who led an excavation of [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md)'s roots to collect samples,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONAdavos-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ROS-9)</sup> Baladas Dren, a Temple pilgrim,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONBaladas-10)</sup> Beldan Dren of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), Irna Dren, who owned the Dren Family Market in [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONIrna-11)</sup> Orini Dren, a pilgrim of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), and Urynnar Dren and his wife in [Serkamora](Lore_Serkamora.md) during the [Llodos Plague](Lore_Llodos_Plague.md)'s outbreak.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONUrynnar-12)</sup> Enar Dren was [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md)'s Master of Records and wrote a treatise on the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) for Councilor Eris Releth.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HWOE-13)</sup>

Before the Drens, [Katariah R'Aathim](Lore_Katariah.md) ruled as Duchess of Vvardenfell, before leaving Morrowind in [3E 141](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_141) to marry [Pelagius Septim](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TWQ8-14)</sup> In [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), Vvardenfell was reorganized into an Imperial District and made open for colonization for the first time in history, after an agreement was struck between Morrowind's Imperial King and the Emperor of Tamriel.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Lo USVII-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GTV-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ASHOM-17)</sup> By this same time Vedam Dren was the Grandmaster of House Hlaalu and Duke of Vvardenfell, and was the direct representative of Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md). As Duke, he governed Vvardenfell District from the township of [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md), a castle within the Ascadian Isles located west of Vivec City.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GTV-16)</sup> The [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) and Tribunal Temple administered their own territories in the District, but all under the advice and guidance of Duke Vedam Dren.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ASHOM-17)</sup> Vedam's younger brother, [Orvas Dren](Lore_Orvas_Dren.md) was the leader of the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md), Morrowind's traditional guild of thieves and criminals.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWVedam-3)</sup> He was also the proprietor of Dren Plantation and ran a lucrative smuggling operation from there.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWPlantation-2)</sup> Vedam Dren's daughter was Ilmeni Dren, leader of an underground group of abolitionists called the [Twin Lamps](Lore_Twin_Lamps.md). Because of their conflicting ideals and professions, Ilmeni and Orvas were at odds with each other.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWIlmeni-18)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) appeared on Vvardenfell and met with the councilors of House Hlaalu so that they may name them Hortator. However, because Nevena Ules of Suran and Velanda Omani of [Elmas Island](Lore_Elmas_Island.md) were in Orvas' pockets, they needed to convince him. Orvas, however, allied himself with the [Sixth House](Lore_Sixth_House.md) and initially rejected the Nerevarine. It is unknown if they began to fight, leading to Orvas' death or if he was convinced to support them. But with him out of the way, the Nerevarine became House Hlaalu's Hortator.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MWHortator-19)</sup> In the same time, a Hlaalu councilman desiring the Grandmaster's seat needed to take control of the Camonna Tong from Orvas Dren. How this ended is also unknown but Vedam Dren named them the new Grandmaster.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MWDealing-20)</sup>

Other Drens lived on Vvardenfell in 3E 427, such as Avoni Dren, a [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) hireling in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MWAvoni-21)</sup> Daynali Dren, a wealthy merchant based in [Tel Mora](Lore_Tel_Mora.md),<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWDaynali-22)</sup> Galmis Dren, a battlemage leader of smugglers that possessed a prized [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) artifact called the [Ring of the Wind](Lore_Ring_of_the_Wind.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MWGalmis-23)</sup> and Marayn Dren, an apprentice of the Balmora [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MWMarayn-24)</sup> Following King [Hlaalu Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md)'s abolishment of slavery, revolts broke out across Morrowind. The Dren Plantation was the site of a large riot, with many deaths between both opposing sides.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LRPPH-25)</sup> Tredayn Dren was an archivist in the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) and recounted a tale originally transcribed by Clarisse Vien from the [oral traditions](Lore_Vateshrans.md) of the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) called *[the Legend of Red Eagle](Lore_The_Legend_of_Red_Eagle.md)*.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TLORE-26)</sup>
