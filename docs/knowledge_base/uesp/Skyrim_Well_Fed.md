# Hunger

**Hunger** is an attribute added in [Survival Mode](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md). Over time, you will become hungry, which will impact your total [stamina](Skyrim_Stamina.md), as well as your ability to wield most [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md). As you become hungrier, your total available stamina will decrease, represented by a dark region inside the stamina bar, and there is a chance you will become [weakened](Skyrim_Disease.md#Weakened). Eat [food](Skyrim_Food.md) to restore your Hunger. If you eat enough, you will receive the Well Fed bonus. Cooked food restores more hunger than uncooked food. If you eat [raw meat](Skyrim_Food.md#Raw_Meat), you may contract [Food Poisoning](Skyrim_Disease.md#Food_Poisoning), which can prevent you from receiving health benefits from food until the effect wears off. [Hot soups](Skyrim_Food.md#Hot_Soups) can now be cooked by using [fire salts](Skyrim_Fire_Salts.md), which restore your cold and also grant a bonus to your warmth rating.

Different foods have different weights of replenishment lowering the Hunger meter by set amounts. Very small replenishes 2, small replenishes 18, medium replenishes 220 and large replenishes 380.

Hunger increases by 7.5 every 0.24 in-game hours (43.2 seconds in real time) that pass. For [Orcs](Skyrim_Orc.md) it is instead 6.375 every 0.24 in-game hours.

Orcs become hungry 15% slower. [Khajiit](Skyrim_Khajiit.md) may eat raw meat as they do not get [Food Poisoning](Skyrim_Disease.md#Food_Poisoning); the same goes for [Argonians](Skyrim_Argonian.md).

The player's maximum stamina after the penalty from Hunger is (Base Stamina)*(1000-Hunger)/841.

The stamina penalty from Hunger begins once the player becomes Peckish(160 Hunger).

## Effects
| Hunger <br> Value | Level | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0–79 | Well Fed | Stamina regenerates 10% faster. |
| 80–159 | Satisfied | No effect. |
| 160–339 | Peckish | Total stamina is reduced. Attacks with weapons are 10% slower. You are 30% less effective with using shields and sneaking. |
| 340–519 | Hungry | Total stamina is reduced. Attacks with weapons are 20% slower. You are 50% less effective with using shields and sneaking. |
| 520–769 | Famished | Total stamina is reduced. Attacks with weapons are 30% slower. You are 70% less effective with using shields and sneaking. |
| 770–1000 | Starving | Total stamina is reduced. Attacks with weapons are 40% slower. You are 90% less effective with using shields and sneaking. |

## Notes
- The [Ring of Namira](Skyrim_Ring_of_Namira.md) can be used to feed, both from deceased humanoid enemies as well as from conjured [Dremora Lords](Skyrim_Conjure_Dremora_Lord.md).
- The [Elemental Fury](Skyrim_Elemental_Fury.md) shout can be used to negate the weapon attack speed penalty at higher Hunger levels.
- While in the [Deadlands](Skyrim_Deadlands.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_The_Cause.md)</sup>, you will be immune to the effects of Hunger.
- If you choose to become a vampire in [Bloodline](Skyrim_Bloodline.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>, feeding on the [Vampire Cattle](Skyrim_Vampire_Cattle.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> at [Volkihar Keep](Skyrim_Volkihar_Keep.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> can help you keep your Hunger under control.
- With version 1.6 of the [Official Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), the [Potion of Blood](Skyrim_Potion_of_Blood.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> can be used to restore Hunger if you are a vampire.

## Bugs
- Seared Slaughterfish and Charred Skeever Meat may restore less Hunger than intended. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6, fixes this bug.
