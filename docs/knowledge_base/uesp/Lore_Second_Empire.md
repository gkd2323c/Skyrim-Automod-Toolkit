The Second Empire, also known as the **Cyrodilic Empire**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Origin-2)</sup> the **Empire of Cyrodiil**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HOTFG-3)</sup> the **Empire of Tamriel**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-2920LSv8-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TOAM3-5)</sup> the **Tamrielic Empire**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-6)</sup><sup>:6</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-THOTE-7)</sup> the **Reman Empire**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-THOTE-7)</sup> **Reman's Imperium**,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> and the **Second Empire of Men**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> was a human-led empire that, at its height, controlled every region of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) with the exception of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> The history of the Second Empire is divided into two main periods: the reign of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) that witnessed the final two centuries of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and the subsequent [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) during the first four hundred thirty years of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

## Reman Dynasty
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/CT-Events-Imperial_Generic_%28Large%29.png/200px-CT-Events-Imperial_Generic_%28Large%29.png)](File_CT-Events-Imperial_Generic_(Large).png.md) [](File_CT-Events-Imperial_Generic_(Large).png.md) Reman-era Imperial The Second Empire and the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) were founded by [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman.md) after he defeated the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) at the Battle of [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703). Until that point, [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) had been divided into several small kingdoms, but once Reman had brought the armies of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and the [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) together to defeat the invasion, he was able to unite all of Tamriel's human inhabitants in the face of possible aggression from the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup>

The Battle of Pale Pass established Reman I as the leading power in Tamriel, but many parts of the continent refused to acknowledge his rule. Valenwood was not subdued until [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3V-9)</sup> In [1E 2804](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2804), [Reman II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II) deposed Emperor [Kastav](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kastav), marking the beginning of what many consider the golden age of the Second Empire.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RIITLo A-10)</sup> In [1E 2811](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2811), the Empire under Reman II invaded [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) quickly won a great and decisive victory in the Battle of Argonia, as well as in several other clashes, inflicting significant casualties on the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TBW-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3A-12)</sup> The conflict, known as the [Blackwater War](Lore_Blackwater_War.md), continued until [1E 2837](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2837), when the Argonians abruptly buried their weapons and went back to farming, fishing, and weaving, and Reman II declared the official annexation of Black Marsh.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TBW-11)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-13)</sup>

In [1E 2840](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2840), the Empire invaded [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), the only province outside of Imperial control, in what became known as the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3M-14)</sup> It was during the war in [1E 2843](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2843) that Reman II was killed in battle against the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RIITLo A-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-R2-15)</sup> Reman II was succeeded by his illegitimate son [Brazollus Dor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brazollus_Dor), who continued the war until his death in [1E 2877](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2877).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TEot V-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-17)</sup> Brazollus Dor's successor, [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III), continued the war, but progress was slow. The three living gods of Morrowind's [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) — [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia), and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) —proved to be capable leaders. By [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), both sides were nearing exhaustion after the Empire captured the fortress of Ald Marak; thus, the two sides agreed to a truce to bring an end to the war.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TLYOTFE-18)</sup>

Before the truce could be formally agreed on, Reman III was assassinated,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3C-19)</sup> bringing an end to the Reman Dynasty and instigating the period of the Second Empire known as the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TLYOTFE-18)</sup>

## Akaviri Potentate

### First Era
After the assassination of Reman III, Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie), who is believed to have ordered the Emperor's assassination, and Vivec agreed to the [Cervant Truce](Lore_Cervant_Truce.md), ending the Four-Score War. On 22 Evening Star, 1E 2920, Versidue-Shaie proclaimed that he was assuming leadership of the Empire but retaining his title of Potentate. He also declared the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the start of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-2920ES-20)</sup>

### Second Era
After becoming the ruler of the Empire, Versidue-Shaie began persecuting those who opposed his rule and remained loyal to the Reman Dynasty, a period known as the Reman Purges.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Systres-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONBlackmarrow-22)</sup> By [2E 283](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_283), Versidue-Shaie was faced with a disintegrating empire. The vassal kingdoms throughout Tamriel had reached a new height of rebellion, openly challenging Imperial rule by refusing to pay taxes and leading sorties against Imperial garrisons. To suppress the rebellious armies, Versidue-Shaie declared martial law in 2E 283, initiating the War of Subjugation, which finally ended in Imperial victory in [2E 320](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_320).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Hot FG-23)</sup> The immense cost of the War of Subjugation resulted in the impoverishment of both the Empire and its constituent kingdoms. To maintain order without incurring the expense of a standing army, Versidue-Shaie agreed in [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321) to a proposal from his kinsman Dinieras-Ves, which established what would later be known as the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-FGC-24)</sup>

In [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324), Versidue-Shaie was assassinated by the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) in the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md)<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Bo D-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-FAD-26)</sup> and succeeded by his son, [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Bo D-25)</sup> The Morag Tong were outlawed across the continent and reformed secretly as the Dark Brotherhood.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-6)</sup><sup>:6</sup> In [2E 400](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_400), Savirien-Chorak held a celebration to commemorate four centuries of potentate rule.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PCo V-27)</sup> Savirien-Chorak granted Orsinium provincial status during his reign.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-13)</sup> Savirien-Chorak and all his heirs were assassinated by the Dark Brotherhood on a night in the month of Sun's Dawn [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430) —within a fortnight the Akaviri Potentate crumbled.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Bo D-25)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KRP-28)</sup> By [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), civil wars and insurrection had dissolved the Empire, ushering in the [Imperial Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-6)</sup><sup>:6</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Bo D-25)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup>

During the early years of the Interregnum, the Empire survived as the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), a rump state ruled by non- [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) emperors that was mostly confined to the [Imperial Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

## Administrative Titles
The exact hierarchy of ranks, the nuances between them, and the trajectory of advancement has not been spelled out, but can generally be understood as categorized below.

- **[Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md)**, the ruler of the Empire.

- **[Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md)**, leader of the Akaviri faction on the Elder Council and Chief Advisor to the Emperor.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-17)</sup>

- **[High Chancellor](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md)** (sometimes Imperial Battlemage), the head of the Elder Council. - **Councilor**, a member of the Elder Council.

- **Provincial Governor**, A Governor of an Imperial Province.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-PGE1p-29)</sup>

- **Governor**, A Governor of a region within an Imperial Province.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TFo S-30)</sup>

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-Remanada_31-0) The *[Remanada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remanada)* contains a mythical story of Reman emerging from [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor) as a child, but this should not be taken as literal truth.
2. [^](#cite_ref-Reman_II_36-0) The exact year of Reman II's accession to the throne is unclear and requires some deduction. Reman II's epitaph states that he was crowned emperor in 1E 2812 and reigned for 39 years, but according to *[Reman II: The Limits of Ambition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II__The_Limits_of_Ambition)*, Reman II died in 1E 2843. *[Emperor Kastav's Epitaph](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Emperor_Kastav%27s_Epitaph)* states that Kastav died in 1E 2812. *[Annals of the Dragonguard](Lore_Annals_of_the_Dragonguard.md)* states that Kastav was deposed sometime between 1E 2804 and 2806. With all the information combined, the most likely explanation is that Kastav was deposed in 1E 2804, and Reman II was only officially crowned emperor after Kastav's death in 1E 2812.
3. [^](#cite_ref-SCd_40-0) Most sources such as *[The Brothers of Darkness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Brothers_of_Darkness)*, *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition)*, and *[Systres History: Volume 7](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_History__Volume_7)* state that Savirien-Chorak was assassinated in 2E 430, but other sources such as the *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition)* and the *[Crafting Motifs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crafting_Motifs)* series state that Savirien-Chorak was assassinated in 2E 431. Since more sources state that Savirien-Chorak was assassinated in 2E 430, this year is presumably the correct year of the assassination.
4. [^](#cite_ref-SCMTo DB_41-0) It is unclear which assassin organization assassinated Savirien-Chorak. *The Brothers of Darkness* states that the Dark Brotherhood assassinated Savirien-Chorak, while *[Sacred Witness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sacred_Witness)* states that the Morag Tong were responsible for the assassination.
