*To create from nothingness is…poetry.*
—Nurelion<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup> Curalmil was a legendary [alchemist](Lore_Alchemy.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CTA-2)</sup> and a [Tongue](Lore_Tongue.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Curalmil-3)</sup> who lived in an early age of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and is known for the creation of the [White Phial](Lore_White_Phial.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/SR-item-The_White_Phial.jpg/200px-SR-item-The_White_Phial.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-The_White_Phial.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-The_White_Phial.jpg) The White Phial Little is known about Curalmil's life. He lived prior to the end of the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-QN-4)</sup> of [1E 140](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_140).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SSSJ-5)</sup> He was an alchemist of a great renown and skill, who lived in an early days of Skyrim, when the province was only beginning its slow turn to ice. At some point he created the White Phial, a self-replenishing alchemical [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) considered one of the greatest masterpieces of its kind. The Phial was created from magically infused snow said to have fallen during the first snowfall upon the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup> He used ancient magical techniques, employing a form of old magic that has largely been lost since the Dragon War. This magic bore similarities to the [Briarheart](Lore_Briarheart.md) rituals practiced by the modern [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-QN-4)</sup>

Even in death, Curalmil's reputation for craftiness endured. He was buried alongside his creation in a long-abandoned cave west of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup> beside a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FC-6)</sup> and took deliberate measures to safeguard the White Phial. The artifact was sealed in such a way that only a master alchemist could access it, requiring a specially prepared alchemical concoction to activate the pedestal that opened the chamber containing the treasure buried alongside him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWP1-7)</sup>

For years his legacy was studied by scholar and alchemist Nurelion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup> Eventually, shortly before his death circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), he requested [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to ventrue to Curalmil's tomb, find the White Phial and retrieve it. They also fought the undead alchemist who rose from dead as the tomb was ransacked.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWP1-7)</sup> The artifact was later repaired,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-QN-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RTP-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWPI-9)</sup> allowing Nurelion to witness its power on his deathbed.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RTP-8)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nurelion-1)</sup>
