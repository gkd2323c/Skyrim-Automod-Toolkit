*We Nords are a people unafraid of getting our hands dirty*
—Tailor-Thane Jork<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHD-1)</sup> Jork was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) craftsman and thane, specifically a Tailor-Thane of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). He was celebrated as a distinguished clothier during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEAYQ-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ad/ON-concept-Nobles%27_Fashionable_Attire_06.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Nobles%27_Fashionable_Attire_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Nobles%27_Fashionable_Attire_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Nobles%27_Fashionable_Attire_06.jpg) Nordic formal wear designed by Jork [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/ON-place-Windhelm.jpg/200px-ON-place-Windhelm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Windhelm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Windhelm.jpg) Windhelm Little is known of Jork's early life, aside from the fact that he hailed from Windhelm and was granted the title of Tailor-Thane in the years leading up to [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SSJ-3)</sup>

Jork designed a variety of Nordic-style jerkins, including the Rawhide-Laced Riften Jerkin, inspired by the brief summers of Riften and crafted as a patterned sleeveless piece with rugged rawhide laces;<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RLRJ-4)</sup> the Solitude Stevedore's Jerkin, reflecting the practical, traveler-influenced style of dockworkers of Solitude;<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SSJ-3)</sup> and the Skald Damask Jerkin, a popular damask twill favored by skalds across Skyrim and paired with a warm tunic underneath.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SDJ-5)</sup>

He also designed several dresses, including the Bleakrock Housecarl Dress, which reflects the practical yet stylish spirit of Nord life and is suited to both duty and daily wear;<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHD-1)</sup> the Bardic Tavern-Singer's Dress, a free-swinging layered gown perfect for performing songs or evening dancing in taverns;<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTSD-6)</sup> and the Jarl's Justice Formal Frock, created for the rare formal moments in Skyrim when a Jarl presides over legal matters and tradition calls for proper, dignified attire.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-JJFF-7)</sup>

Jork also created the Jarl's Deluxe Moot Regalia, conceived in anticipation of the reunification of East and West Skyrim and the grand Moot where the Jarls would gather to choose a High King, though he unveiled the outfit long before such an event came to pass.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-JDMR-8)</sup> Tailor-Thane also designed the Nordic Knot Doublet and Breeches, an upscale ensemble inspired by traditional Nordic knotwork honoring the heritage of the people of Skyrim while remaining stylish on anyone who wears it.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NKD-9)</sup> He also designed a Dwarven-themed conical wizard hat with matching goggles, claiming that if the Dwemer were still around and had wizards, this is exactly what those wizards would wear.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DSMH-10)</sup>

Lady Eloisse, Fifth Countess [Manteau](Lore_House_Manteau.md), noted that pockets now common in men's clothing across High Rock were originally a Nordic invention. She highlighted Jork's designs that included hip flasks, avoiding vulnerable external holsters. His clever interior pockets let men carry flasks on their hip or lapel without any visible bulge.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEAYQ-2)</sup>
