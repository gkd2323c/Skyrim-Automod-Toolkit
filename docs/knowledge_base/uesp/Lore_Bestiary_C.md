## [Caenlorn Wolf](Lore_Caenlorn_Wolf.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/BM-quest-The_Skaal_Test_of_Wisdom_04.jpg/200px-BM-quest-The_Skaal_Test_of_Wisdom_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-quest-The_Skaal_Test_of_Wisdom_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-quest-The_Skaal_Test_of_Wisdom_04.jpg) The Caenlorn Wolves returning to the world of the spirit The [Caenlorn Wolves](Lore_Caenlorn_Wolf.md) (or simply the **Caenlorn**) are the sacred ceremonial [spirit](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) of the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md), a tribe of [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) native to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) who are known for their worship of the island's wolves and [bears](Lore_Bear.md). These wolves are held in high regard by the tribe's shaman. Usually the Caenlorn Wolves reside in the world of the spirit with the [All-Father](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:All-Father); they are only summoned by the Skaal for certain ceremonies.

According to the traditions of the Skaal, crimes such as theft or bearing false witness can result in a sentence of either exile or death. Of the two, death is considered the more honorable punishment for a warrior, as it involves a ritual sacrifice to the wolves. In such cases, the Caenlorn Wolves will be summoned so that the accused may be fed to them. The Skaal view this as the preferred way to die, as they believe that the warrior's spirit lives on and is purified by allowing the wolves to consume their flesh. The wolves will then return the spirit to the cycle of life so that the warrior may be [reincarnated](Lore_Reincarnation.md) in a different form. However, some members of the Skaal consider the practice to be barbaric due to the horror and pain involved.

**Found in:** - [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Caenlorn_Wolf)

## [Canah](Lore_Canah.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/RG-creature-Canah.jpg/200px-RG-creature-Canah.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-creature-Canah.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-creature-Canah.jpg) A canah The **[Canah Bird](Lore_Canah.md)**, or simply **Canah**, is a colorful exotic [bird](Lore_Bird.md) species of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) that are bred for their beauty. Their multicolored feathers are thought of as both beautiful and highly useful, as it is said the [goddess](Lore_Gods.md) [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md)'s light shines bright for them. Kynareth has been known to bless individuals with the ability to temporarily transform into a canah if an appropriate offering is given at her shrines on [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), namely one of their feathers.

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the [Altmer](Lore_High_Elf.md) trader Gerrick sold canah feathers to [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md). Cyrus was able to quickly fly between shrines of Kynareth across the island by using the feathers to temporarily transform into a canah.

## Caterpillar
Caterpillars are minuscule, many-legged<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Ogzar-10)</sup> larval insects found throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) which eat leaves.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ATo K-11)</sup> They can sport a variety of colors and patterns, including green,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ATo K-11)</sup> orange and purple stripes,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ACA-12)</sup> yellow, and red.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-No Eb-13)</sup> Types of caterpillar are often incredibly diverse from one another, with some possessing furry backs<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-No Eb-13)</sup> and others having up to sixteen eyes.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Olith-14)</sup> In nature, they are preyed upon by [robins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Robin),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ATo K-11)</sup> while [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) are known to prepare them in [their cuisine](Lore_Argonian_Cuisine.md)<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Ogzar-10)</sup> and [beverages](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-No Eb-13)</sup> [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) [smoke](Lore_Drugs.md) caterpillars through bone pipes.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-15)</sup> [Inferniums](Lore_Infernium.md) closely resemble large caterpillars.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OI-16)</sup>

At some point in a caterpillar's life, they develop a cocoon,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Record-17)</sup> from which a [butterfly](Lore_Butterfly.md) or [moth](Lore_Moth.md) may emerge.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ACA-12)</sup> Caterpillar farms can be found in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), where they are raised until they become moths and butterflies, at which point they are eaten.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ACA-12)</sup> Silks may also be harvested from caterpillar farms, which are spun into rough linens.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Silk-18)</sup>

Habanoo caterpillars are a rare species found in [Betnikh](Lore_Betnikh.md) that can have sixteen eyes.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Olith-14)</sup> Frondfire caterpillar cocoons are sometimes given as donations at the temple of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Record-17)</sup>

**Found in:** - [The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Creatures#Notes)

## [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/ON-npc-The_Mage.jpg/200px-ON-npc-The_Mage.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Mage.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Mage.jpg) A Celestial The [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md) are powerful ethereal beings formed by the power of the [stars](Lore_Stars.md). The most powerful Celestials represent the [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md). It is unknown if these beings are from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) or if they are mortals who have achieved a state of ascension. When asked if she really was [the Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) constellation, the Thief Celestial answered she was, in a way, just as one might look at a picture of the constellation in the stars and identify it from the image, she was the Thief, or "one way of seeing" it.

The major Celestials are said to be part of the natural order of the world, and thus even those that cause chaos like [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) can only be contained, as their death would cause even greater peril. Major Celestials are said to possess the power to remake the world, and have been theorized to be capable of [altering time](Lore_Time_Magic.md) in a manner likened to ["hiding behind a broken Dragon"](Lore_Dragon_Break.md), traveling the "line" of time and than cutting it to wrap themselves in a "context where none can ever follow". Because of this immense power, they can only safely manifest on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) through the use of Apex Stones, it is said that should an Apex Stone be destroyed the corresponding Celestial's full power would be unleashed upon the mortal plane, a force [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) could not withstand.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Celestial)

## [Chimera](Lore_Chimera.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/ON-creature-Chimera.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Chimera.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Chimera.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Chimera.jpg) Chimera A [Chimera](Lore_Chimera.md) (also spelled **Khimera**) is a dangerous fantastical, ever shifting and changing creature "patched together" from different sources. In ancient times, they were dark forces in the wilderness around [Tyrigel](Lore_Tyrigel.md) that the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) knew to avoid. The [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) created chimeras to function as guardian beasts. They were given form through [Blood Magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md) in honor and sacrifice of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md). All chimera are claimed to have been created by the Druid King himself. Physically, these chimeras have the heads of a [gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md), a [lion](Lore_Lion.md), and a [snake](Lore_Snake.md) or [wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md). After their exodus to the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md), the druids created chimeras to safeguard their newfound home, influenced by the trials that they had already faced. The purpose of the Chimeras was to guard and preserve ancient druidic relics and sacred locations, ensuring their utmost safety and sanctity. ## [Cliff Strider](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/ON-creature-Cliff_Strider.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Cliff_Strider.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Cliff_Strider.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Cliff_Strider.jpg) Cliff Strider [Cliff Striders](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md) (also spelled as **Cliffstriders** or **Cliff-Striders**) are a species of large, winged [reptilian](Lore_Reptile.md) creatures native to parts of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), such as the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md) and formerly [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Unlike Cliff Racers, Cliff Striders aren't the strongest fliers, and spend much more time on the ground. Despite this they are still powerful predators that prey on much of the local wildlife, including [man](Lore_Man.md) and [mer](Lore_Mer.md). Their young are known as Cliff Skippers and are hatched from large nest mounds built from dung. After hatching, the young enjoy some measure of protection from at least one of their parents. When threatened, they have been known to projectile-vomit the contents of their stomach into a stinging, acidic spray. It is said the older a Cliff Strider gets, the more crazaed and dangerous it becomes.

It was recorded that the physiology and behavior of the Cliff Strider is similar to the [hackwings](Lore_Hackwing.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and it is possible that they shared ancestry. Some historians theorize this possible divergence resulted from [Dark Elves](Lore_Dunmer.md) raiding Black Marsh for thousands of years to bring back things they deemed valuable, such as slave labor, livestock, and critters. By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Cliff Strider population of Vvardenfell had been exterminated by the [Ash Blight](Lore_Ash_Blight.md), along with several other unique Vvardenfell species. Though Cliff Striders could also be found on the mainland.

## [Coldharbour Sentinel](Lore_Coldharbour_Sentinel.md)

Coldharbour Sentinels are not simply independent entities but are interconnected through a complex system of magical bonding. They possessed varying roles or purposes, as there were at least four types of Sentinels in Coldharbour: the Sentinels of Debilitation, who are capable of inhibiting movement speed of those they behold, the Sentinels of Dissipation, who can drain life force out of those they gaze at, the Sentinels of Enfeeblement, who are capable of reducing battle prowess of those they see, and the Sentinels of Vulnerability, who make those in their line of sight more susceptible to physical and magical harm. Smaller, but mobile eyes of Molag Bal were known as **Coldharbour Ocular Observers**. Some of them had a reputation for being more curious and less vigilant than Molag Bal would have like.

They moderately resemble [lobsters](Lore_Lobster.md) and [shrimp](Lore_Shrimp.md).<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-61)</sup> The Gold Coast Crayfish can be found in the sunny waters of the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md). The Pearlescent Crayfish can be found on [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md). The Shalk-Brother Crayfish can be found in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The Karth Crayfish can be found in lakes around [The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). The Effigy Crayfish can be found in the lakes of western [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), while the Ghost Crayfish can be found in the lakes of eastern Solstice.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-ESOFish-62)</sup>

## [Crocodile](Lore_Crocodile.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/ON-creature-Crocodile.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Crocodile.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Crocodile.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Crocodile.jpg) A crocodile [Crocodiles](Lore_Crocodile.md), are large, semi-aquatic, carnivorous [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md) with thick hides that can be found in swamps and near rivers throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Crocodiles are described as having "ancient skin", with one Argonian claiming they roamed [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) before his people did. Similar reptiles to them include [alligators](Lore_Alligator.md) and [caimans](Lore_Caiman.md).

In the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) tongue of [Jel](Lore_Jel.md), they are known as **rormasu**, which translates to "big-mouthed reptile that blinks above water". Redguards colloquially know them as **marsh [dunerippers](Lore_Duneripper.md)**, while some [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) apparently known them as **four-clawed [daedroths](Lore_Daedroth.md)**, though the humor in this title is thought to be lost in the translation from [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md).

- See also: [Werecrocodile](Lore_Werecrocodile.md)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Crocodile), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Stalking Crocodile), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Crosswing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crosswing)
Crosswings are [insects](Lore_Insect.md) that inhabit [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). They are believed to have a fierce bitterness when they die afraid. The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) use spells to attract them as part of the process for brewing [beer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beer) from meat.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_On_the_Brewing_of_Dark_Meat_Beer.md)

## Crow
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg) A crow Crows are black [birds](Lore_Bird.md) that can be found all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). A group of crows is called a "murder". They are commonly associated with the Daedric Prince [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-68)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-69)</sup> They should not be confused with their [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) counterparts, [Crow Daedra](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md). Crows are sometimes cooked and eaten, and some [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) platters exist to serve them in three ways: stuffed, roasted and boiled.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-70)</sup> Scarecrows are kept in fields to keep crows away from crops.

- See also: [Blackbird](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackbird), [Magpie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magpie), and [Raven](Lore_Raven.md)

## [Crow Daedra](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-creature-Duke_of_Crows_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Duke_of_Crows_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Duke_of_Crows_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Duke_of_Crows_02.jpg) A Crow Daedra [Crow Daedra](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md), also known as **Night's Messengers**, are one of many [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) species that mimic those found on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). They are associated with the Daedric Prince [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). They differ from real [crows](Lore_Crow.md) in that they can talk and they have unnatural glowing purple eyes. They have their own feudal courts, the most notable of which are the [Blackfeather Court](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md) and their rivals, the Exarchs of Dross. They do not consider themselves Daedra and instead think themselves to be real crows. They enjoy eating fleshy bits of mortal bodies, such as eyes. They also have a tendency to collect shiny objects. Crow Daedra procreate and lay eggs in order to hatch more of their kind.

- See also: [Glow Raven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glow_Raven)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Blackfeather_Court), Betrayal of the Second Era

## Crystaljack
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/ON-pet-Crystaljack.png/200px-ON-pet-Crystaljack.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Crystaljack.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Crystaljack.png) A Crystaljack Crystaljacks are venomous [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) insects that resemble large [dragonflies](Lore_Dragonfly.md), though they are often referred to as [wasps](Lore_Wasp.md).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-71)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-BASD-72)</sup> They are native to [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md), a lost Daedric realm. Unlike some of the other denizens of that realm, Crystaljacks appear to be organic in nature, with shimmering skin that simply mimics the appearance of mirrored surfaces.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-CJp-73)</sup> They are known to emerge from cocoons.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-74)</sup> They are creations of the forgotten [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), and can generally be found anywhere she or the forces of Mirrormoor have exerted their influence. Crystaljacks could be found throughout the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) during Mirrormoor's attempted invasion.

The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) had interest in studying their crystallized venom.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-75)</sup> Mortals are capable of training the creatures and keeping them as pets.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-76)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-BASD-72)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-CJp-73)</sup>

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Crystaljack)
