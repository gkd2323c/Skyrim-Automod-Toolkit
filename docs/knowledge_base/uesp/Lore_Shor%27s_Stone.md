[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/SR-place-Shor%27s_Stone.jpg/200px-SR-place-Shor%27s_Stone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Shor%27s_Stone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Shor%27s_Stone.jpg) Shor's Stone circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Shor's Stone, named after the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) god [Shor](Lore_Shor.md), is a small [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) mining village in the pass between [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) and [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), north-northwest of [Riften](Lore_Riften.md). Shor's Stone has a watchtower that overlooks the village, aptly named Shor's Watchtower.

Shor's Stone Mine, sometimes called Redbelly Mine due to the red mist that fills it, provides ebony, [Quicksilver](Lore_Quicksilver.md), and [iron](Lore_Iron.md) and serves as the foundation of the local economy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> For a period of time, the settlement served as the home of the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md), based out of [Fallowstone Hall](Lore_Fallowstone_Hall.md). The Fallowstone Vaults served as a museum and mortuary for the Companions as well as the resting place of [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md), and shrines to the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) surrounded the hall.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

## History
In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an alliance of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) known as the Three Tribes attacked the settlement on the orders of the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md). They took control of the mine and Fallowstone Hall in order to steal Wuuthrad and resurrect the [giant](Lore_Giant.md) [Sinmur](Lore_Sinmur.md). With the aid of the surviving Companions, the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) liberated the settlement, but not before Wuuthrad was lost.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

At some point, the Companions abandoned Fallowstone Hall, and [Jorrvaskr](Lore_Jorrvaskr.md) in [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) once again became their headquarters. The master thief Drayven Indoril, believed by some to be the infamous Nightingale of legend, is said to have retired to this quiet village.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TN-4)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the town was in danger of disappearing entirely when invading [frostbite spiders](Lore_Frostbite_Spider.md) forced the mine to close temporarily, however it was cleared out by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
