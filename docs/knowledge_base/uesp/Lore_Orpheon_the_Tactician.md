Orpheon the Tactician was an [arcanist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arcanists) warlord and [battlefield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battlefield_Emperors) [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FBPLS-1)</sup> Little is known of Orpheon's early life, but some sources suggest that he had [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) blood in his lineage.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LTO-2)</sup> After the outbreak of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), he broke his allegiance to his alliance and recruited despondent deserters from every other army in Tamriel to form the Army of Orpheon.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FBPLS-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCN-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-P1035-4)</sup> Orpheon's army was supplemented by [dunerippers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Duneripper), [haj motas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haj_Mota), [Argonian behemoths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xal-Krona), [Senche-rahts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-raht), and [gryphons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gryphon).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FBPLS-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

During the month of [Rain's Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rain%27s_Hand), at some point during the Three Banners War, he led his army in a march from [Cropsford](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cropsford) to [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md). At Bruma, Orpheon and his army laid siege to the city for a fortnight with the siege engines of his siegemaster, Malthoras. A few days after the siege engines brought down the city walls and the first of the flags appeared asking for a parley, Orpheon sent one of his officers, Flamedancer Ajim-Rei, to burn Bruma, resulting in the city being set alight. The chronicler of this campaign, Lewin Frey, regarded the campaign as the start of Orpheon's reign.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSo B-6)</sup> Eventually, Orpheon and his army took control of every keep outside the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), and he proclaimed himself emperor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCN-3)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Orpheon lost control of the [Ruby Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LTO-2)</sup> After losing control of the throne, Orpheon learned of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), which he believed contained a prophecy that could end the Three Banners War and legitimize his claim to the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LTO-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FBPLS-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCN-3)</sup> In 2E 582, he ordered one of his trackers, Garvin, to locate the Elder Scroll, which he tracked to the island of Lep Seclusa.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FBPLS-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Kha-7)</sup> After learning of the Elder Scroll's location, Orpheon and his army invaded Lep Seclusa and assaulted the [moth priests](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) and Hel Netu monks who defended the scroll.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCN-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-P1035-4)</sup> However, before he could secure the Elder Scroll, the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) defeated his army and killed him.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOBW-8)</sup>
