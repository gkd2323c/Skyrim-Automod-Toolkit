[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/93/MW-item-Volendrung.jpg/200px-MW-item-Volendrung.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Volendrung.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Volendrung.jpg) Volendrung, the Hammer of King Rourken King Rourken was a [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) chieftain and later the king of the Dwemer clan that bore his name— [Clan Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md). When [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) Hortator [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) and Dwemer King [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md) aligned to form the [First Council](Lore_First_Council.md), Rourken and his people resisted this alliance.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WIV-1)</sup> From [Resdayn](Lore_Morrowind.md), Chieftain Rourken threw his hammer, [Volendrung](Lore_Volendrung.md), across Tamriel and vowed to lead his clan to wherever it landed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1E-2)</sup> The hammer landed in a location which became known as [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md), in the province of modern day [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1E-2)</sup> which is named after the dwarven city.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3H-3)</sup> There, Rourken became a king among his people.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AAQS-4)</sup>

Later in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Rourken and his clan found themselves embroiled in a war with the legendary wizard [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md). Rourken had the [Spellbreaker](Lore_Spellbreaker.md) shield created to aid his side in the conflict, although the artifact was lost in the final battle between the two sides.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AAQS-4)</sup> Rourken's high honor guard were also outfitted in special gauntlets imbued with tonal magic derived from Spellbreaker, which were likewise believed to have been used in the battle with Shalidor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RSO-5)</sup>

Further tribulations faced the Rourken Clan as their territory came to be invaded by a race of [giant Goblins](Lore_Giant_Goblin.md), who descended to the middle of the province via the [Goblin gate](Lore_Goblin_Gate.md), a "rent in space time" leading to the invaders' home world.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NFRH-6)</sup> Some accounts attest to the Rourken being driven from Hammerfell by the goblins,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-KE1-7)</sup> though others attest to the clan continuing to occupy their strongholds in the province until the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3H-3)</sup>

Rourken's people were not spared from the fate of all Dwemer following the cataclysm of the Battle of Red Mountain, as they too disappeared.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3H-3)</sup> Following the disappearance of the dwarves, Rourken's artifacts became associated with [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md): Spellbreaker with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DFPeryite-8)</sup> and Volendrung with [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), who ironically was an enemy of the clan.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WIV-1)</sup> [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) who inhabited Hammerfell in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) paid tribute to the Dwarven King with various exclamations such as "Beard of the Dwarf-King," "By the Great Hammer," and "By Rourken."<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DFRaces-9)</sup>

## Notes
- Rourken has been called "last of the Dwarven Kings",<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AAQS-4)</sup> but the significance of this title is unknown, as his clan vanished like all others following the Battle of Red Mountain.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3H-3)</sup>
