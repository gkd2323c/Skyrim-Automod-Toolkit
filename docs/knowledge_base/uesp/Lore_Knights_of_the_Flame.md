# Knights of the Flame

[![ON-banner-Knights of the Flame.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-banner-Knights_of_the_Flame.png/90px-ON-banner-Knights_of_the_Flame.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Knights_of_the_Flame.png) *Protectors and warriors for the town and royal family of Anticlere. They are sworn to guard the Flytes, though they are curious why they are not permitted to stop highwaymen outside the Anticlere limits, but to keep their activities to the palace and town of Anticlere. Several of the better-looking, dimmer knights are personal guards of Lady Flyte, and enjoy great privileges. Everyone is pretty loyal and dedicated, as grateful as they are to the Flytes for rescuing the town formerly known as Reich Gradkeep.*
— [The Daggerfall Chronicles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles) The Knights of the Flame are a [knightly order](Lore_Knights.md) from [Anticlere](Lore_Anticlere.md). During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) they were sworn to High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) and his brother, Duke Nathaniel at [Alcaire Castle](Lore_Alcaire_Castle.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONDame Dabienne-1)</sup> In the Third Era, their allegiance was to [Lord Auberon](Lore_Auberon_Flyte.md) and Lady Doryanna Flyte, who rescued them from the chaos following the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) in [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403). Only those known for their loyalty to Anticlere and their blessed patrons are judged worthy to join the order. In return for their allegiance, the knights are treated as veritable gods in Anticlere where the guilds are mainly found.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-2)</sup> Their garrisons can be found in many cities and towns in Anticlere, such as Charborne Wood and Chestermarket.

## History
Their origins can be traced back to [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542), when Sir [Byric](Lore_Byric.md) of the Flame set fire to the fields surrounding the [Alcaire Castle](Lore_Alcaire_Castle.md) in order to hold back invaders from [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). To honor his bold action and determination, the [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md) Knights (as they were previously known) were renamed the Knights of the Flame. During that battle, the Knights of the Flame fought alongside their loyal dog companions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-So SB-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Pet-4)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the knights were led by Sir Hughes, with their main base being Firebrand Keep. Sir Hughes had gone mad due to being possessed by the Omen of Fear, a [Watcher](Lore_Watcher.md) in service of [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md). This caused him to order the Knights to attack the Redguard encampment at Alcaire Castle, an act which threatened the fabric of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md); however, not all the Knights agreed with him and a large number were exiled, such as Sir Edmund, forming a resistance faction. This resistance recruited fellow Knights still in Firebrand Keep and eventually mustered enough strength to attack the Keep and kill Sir Croix. This led to Sir Edmund assuming command of Firebrand Keep, and the Knights of the Flame didn't proceed with the attack on the Redguards.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

By the fifth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Knights of the Flame had been affiliated with the Flyte family of Anticlere for generations.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFGen Flame-7)</sup> The [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) left the knighthood in disarray but they were guided back to restoration by Lord [Auberon Flyte](Lore_Auberon_Flyte.md), who rose to power as the new Lord of Anticlere<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DFGen Join-8)</sup> and named them the region's new knightly order.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFGen Flame-7)</sup> Members of the order were loyal to the ruling family and the people of Anticlere, and were thus celebrated in return.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DFGen Join-8)</sup> Some members were appointed as Lady [Doryanna Flyte](Lore_Doryanna_Flyte.md)'s personal guard and enjoyed great privileges. For a time, the order was kept in the cities and restricted from patrolling the region's highways.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TDC-9)</sup><sup>:100</sup>
