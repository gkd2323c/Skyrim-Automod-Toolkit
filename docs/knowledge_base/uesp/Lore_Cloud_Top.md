These ruins include a section of pillar carved by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) and infused with significant power.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EN-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/OBR-place-Cloud_Top.jpg/200px-OBR-place-Cloud_Top.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Cloud_Top.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Cloud_Top.jpg) Cloud Top circa 3E 433 *(Oblivion Remastered)* The Ayleid stone was transported here by the [Imperial Watch](Lore_Imperial_Watch.md) at some point in the Third Era in an attempt to harness some degree of the Ayleid's magical power. Wizards from the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) were brought in to focus the stone's power and it was eventually tuned to react to [shock](Lore_Shock.md) magic, although several guild mages were severely injured in the process. Through the use of a [Welkynd stone](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md), the Imperials were marginally successful in harnessing the stone's power.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EN-3)</sup> By casting a shock spell on the pillar while holding a Welkynd stone, the caster would be granted command of powerful [destructive](Lore_Destruction.md) magic, namely a shock spell called "Finger of the Mountain".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Knowledge of the events at Cloud Top were recorded in a book entitled *Fingers of the Mountain*, which was given to the keepers of the Imperial Watch.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EN-3)</sup> This book was lost after it was taken back to Cloud Top by an unknown individual, who was then killed by lightning while interacting with the stone.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a former guild mage named Earana arrived in Chorrol seeking to locate the book. Teekeeus, the local guild leader and her former nemesis, instructed the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to reach the book first and secure it for the Mages Guild for safekeeping. Teekeeus sought guidance from the [Council of Mages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Council_of_Mages) to determine what to do with the book, while Earana attempted to enlist the Hero's aid in return for the knowledge needed to harness the stone's destructive powers. The book was successfully retrieved from the charred corpse of its previous owner at Cloud Top, although it is unknown who the Hero agreed to help.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
