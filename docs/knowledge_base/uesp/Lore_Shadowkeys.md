# Shadowkeys

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg/200px-SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg) A Shadowkey<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Shadowkey Poster-1)</sup> *DOUBT IS THE KE(Y) TO KNOWLEDGE*
—A quote in [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) letting, engraved at the very bottom of the Shadowkey<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Shadowkey Poster-1)</sup> Shadowkeys are ancient artifacts associated with [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md), which are used "unlock specific patterns in shadow."<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azra Night-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Porliss Caith-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Tanyin Aldwyr-4)</sup> The Shadowkeys are not unsmall objects.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Shadowkeys-5)</sup> Each one depicts a [golden dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) (its wings extended out and shaped around a purple gem, forming the bow of key; a long serpentine torso mounted at the top, with its head and tongue before the shaft as if the dragon were swallowing it; and a tail that wraps around the shaft, flowing into and forming the key's blade near the bottom).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Shadowkeys-5)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Shadowkey Poster-1)</sup>

The Shadowkeys are used for the opening of **Shadow Gates** —magical barriers of Shadow Magic often used to protect treasure.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Shadowkeys-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Shadow Gates-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Events Shadow-7)</sup> These Shadow Gates (sometimes called transport gates) can have a variety of appearances, from portal-like or door-like to outright invisible. Typically these feature a force of shadows slowly swirling and shifting slightly in shape, waiting to be unlocked by the power of a Shadowkey.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Shadow Gates-6)</sup>

## History

### Background
The exact origins of the Shadowkeys is entirely unknown. [Azra Nightwielder](Lore_Azra_Nightwielder.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) was familiar with the Shadowkeys.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azra Night-2)</sup>

### Third Era
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md)'s [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) around [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), eleven Shadowkeys were cherished by various notable figures on all sides of the conflict in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). A hero called the [Soul of Conflict](Lore_Soul_of_Conflict.md) collected all eleven keys and harnessed their power, using them to open the various Shadow Gates across the world–most of which held a [powerful guerdon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Shadow_Gate) in their midst (such as the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md), [Magicka Edge Axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Magicka_Edge_Axe), and [Geldwyr Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Geldwyr_Shield)) or a powerful enhancement which can "unlock possibilities in the shadows", calling upon shadows to bolster the subject's mind or body.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Events Shadow-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Shadow Gates-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DEGate-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DWGate-9)</sup>

The keys also have other unique uses. One Shadowkey was used by the Soul of Conflict to resurrect the merchant [Volstok Violet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Volstok_Violet), restoring him to life from a [zombified](Lore_Zombie.md) state.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Another Shadowkey was used by the Soul of Conflict to free the original shadowmage Azra Nightwielder from his entrapment in a crystal of ice.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Events Shadow-7)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azra Night-2)</sup>

## Notes
- Originally Shadowkeys were going to be the main [Mac Guffins](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mac Guffin) of *Shadowkey* (as can be read on the early official game descriptions),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> but this was changed during development to the [Star Teeth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md) (as can be read on final official game descriptions).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Web-12)</sup> This was done because all interaction with the [N-Gage Arena](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/N-Gage_(service)) feature—used to obtain special downloadable content (like levels, weapons, spells, and powerups)—was cut during the latter months of development, and so the game producers opted to make all the special content unlockable in the game itself: reworking the design around Shadowkeys and Shadow Gates to have places in the game where players can obtain the special content.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-shadfaq-13)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
- Dragon iconography is a common theme across *[Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Shadowkey)* (being featured both in the Shadowkeys themselves and the user-interface/heads-up-display), likely in reference to the story centering around the city-state of [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Events Shadow-7)</sup>
- The bottom word on in Daedric lettering on the Shadowkey spells out "JONES" in reference to [Mark Jones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Mark_Jones), the author of the Shadowkey artwork.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Shadowkey Poster-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-SKTDE-15)</sup>
