# Cherim

Cherim was a famous [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) tapestry maker who lived in [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md) during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). At the height of his success he had four factories creating replicas of his works and his originals were sold for extraordinary prices. *[Cherim's Heart of Anequina](Lore_Cherim%27s_Heart_of_Anequina.md)* records his interview with [Livillus Perus](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Livillus_Perus), where he discussed one of his first tapestries, the Heart of Anequina, depicting Cherim's involvement in the [Heart of Anequina](Lore_Heart_of_Anequina.md) during the [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) which took place between [3E 394](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_394) and [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399). Cherim was known to have a brown and orange striped tail, as seen in his small self-portrait on the Heart of Anequina.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CHOA-1)</sup>

Cherim's tapestries usually depicted historical moments from all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). These included [Convention](Lore_Convention.md), the Fall of White-Gold Tower, and the Velothi Exodus. His works usually used muted colors, contrasting with his subjects' luminous skin tones. This itself contrasts with older, more traditional styles of tapestry. Emperor [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) owned at least ten tapestries, while at the time of Livillus Perus' interview, his representatives were negotiating the purchase of five more. Cherim believed that all Khajiit stereotypes were unfair, but was compelled to acknowledge them every so often.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CHOA-1)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/MW-npc-Jobasha.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Jobasha.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Jobasha.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Jobasha.jpg) Jobasha - *Sermon Zero* features a hidden message that also contains Cherim's name.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-S0-2)</sup>
- [Jobasha](Lore_Jobasha.md), the bookseller from Morrowind, was a good friend of Cherim. He once cryptically revealed that many sermons hide their secrets and mentioned the idea of studying Sermon Zero, as well as Cherim's exquisite tapestries. When asked if the Cherim who makes tapestries and the Cherim of Sermon Zero were the same, Jobasha did not deny, but also said that it's "a common misunderstanding".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-DGP-3)</sup> *Cherim's Heart of Anequina* was among the books sold by Jobasha.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jobasha-4)</sup>
- "White Gold" is one of his finest pieces, though it's rarely seen. It depicts the White Tower, with a dragon spiraling around it and a moth priest at the summit—a famous moment, though few recall it.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-DGP-3)</sup> Another of his works was a large tapestry depicting Empress Alessia breaking her chains of slavery.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TPWormgod-5)</sup>
- The words *[Joden](Lore_Jode.md) [jone](Lore_Jone.md) sacrificed themselves for duty, for love, for "significant form"* were attributed to him.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-TS0A-6)</sup>
- An "obscure and irrelevant Khajiit who hid some kind of secret message in tapestries" was mentioned in *[Minutes of the Lusty Argonian Historical Society](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Minutes_of_the_Lusty_Argonian_Historical_Society)*. The meaning of his concealed messages was unclear, though he wove fanciful connections between the constellations, the moons, and towers.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MOTLAHS-7)</sup>
