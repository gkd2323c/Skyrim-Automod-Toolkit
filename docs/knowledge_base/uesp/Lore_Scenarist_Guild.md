# Scenarist Guild

[![DF-misc-Scenarist Guild.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/DF-misc-Scenarist_Guild.png/400px-DF-misc-Scenarist_Guild.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Scenarist_Guild.png) The Scenarist Guild<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is a centuries-old guild of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) with a presence across all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The Scenarists are Tamriel's troubadours, bards, and minstrels whose notes and observations have been compiled into a thousand impressive volumes over hundreds of years.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup> A neutral guild member not associated with any geographic entity acts as an observer, keeping others' accounts honest and free from inborn or regional bias.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup>

Their principal facility is the Scenarist Guild citadel nestled in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), which was surrounded by a magical barrier to prevent trespassers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup>

## History

### Third Era
After a rather tortured path to membership, a man named [Nesmyt](Lore_Nesmyt.md) had finally been entered into the ranks of the Scenarist Guild as an acolyte. However his first few weeks in the guild were rough, due to higher ranking members bullying him and sneering at his low station in the guild. And advancement proved upward proved to be impossible, as Acolyte Nesmyt did not get along with the self-styled leader of the Mercenary Guild,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> [Vychamp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vychamp), and refused to ever pledge fealty to him due to the fact that Vychamp had emasculated Nesmyt's children in a fit of "jealous rage" over Nesymt's helpmate. This series of unfortunate events caused Nezmyt to hallucinate powerful images of decadence, destruction, and wantonness—interrupting any chance of his being able to sleep during this period.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup> However through whatever means Nezmyt had overcome the ordeal with Vychamp because by the mid-to-late [fourth century](Lore_Third_Era.md#Fourth_Century) Nezmyt himself had become the master of the Scenarist Guild—which he ruled with "an iron hand."<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup>

Beginning in [3E 370](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_370), guildmaster Nezmyt created a sect of Imperial scribes charged with creating the *[Codex Scientia II](Lore_Codex_Scientia.md)* (a "Book of Knowledge" designed for heroes, and a second *Codex Scientia* tome first written about by ancient prophets).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CD-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFCD-5)</sup> After the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) period concluded in the year [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), the book was focused on recording all historical events beginning with the defeat of the usurper [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup> to the conclusion of the [Wars of the Iliac Bay](Lore_Fractious_Wars.md) —and the book became known as *[The Daggerfall Chronicles](Lore_The_Daggerfall_Chronicles.md)* after the events that took place in that [city-state](Lore_Daggerfall.md) during the wars.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup> It also described in detail how to traverse the [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) landscape from a physical, mystical, and historical perspective and was considered a must-have survivor's guide for anyone adventuring in the western kingdoms the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup> The guild maintained a Monster Watch Grid, or bestiary, which they documented in the *Chronicles* as a way of informing the reader of the various monsters that inhabit the Iliac Bay, including the thousands of dark minions released by the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md) in the events leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup> However, the book was smuggled out of the Scenarists' citadel by anonymous renegade guild member and was subsequently hidden in a shrine of the [Oracle of Hammerfell](Lore_The_Oracle.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup> It is not known what ultimately became of the book.

## Notes
- | [^](#note_nb1) | In the text, the Scenarist Guild is also referred as a "Mages Guild", and in another instance Vychamp of the "Mercenary Guild" is indicated as having power over the Scenarists' organization. While the former can be deduced as a simple case of clunky capitalization and/or word use given the context, its harder to pin down the latter issue with the Mercenary Guild—which could contextually serve as just another odd synonym of the Scenarist Guild or possibly a distinct faction unto itself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- There is another guild that employs minstrels, appropriately called the [Minstrels Guild](Lore_Minstrels_Guild.md), which was sanctioned by the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) in [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HOTFG-6)</sup>
