Great [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)!

It is my worshipful pleasure to welcome you back to the world of the living! Preparations continue apace on our great work in eastern [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md). The native population here consists primarily of Stone-Nest [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), who differ from their mainland counterparts in that they appear indifferent to [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) trees and continue to use [xanmeers](Lore_Xanmeer.md) as temples and burial sites. They are a minor inconvenience to our activities and to our work, but make excellent fodder for experiments and slave labor.

Most of these Argonians are dispersed among small settlements, with the village of Xor-Hist being the largest. While elders called stone-shapers appear to demonstrate some level of leadership and authority, the Stone-Nest have no standing army and only a handful of true warriors scattered throughout the villages. One minor group among them that I almost hate to mention, since there are so few of them, are the Void zealots that call themselves death-dancers. They seem to be some combination of warrior and priest who wander from village to village to deal with problems and offer guidance as warranted. They appear to consider themselves responsible for the souls of their fellow Argonians, and if any of these Stone-Nest people are going to cause us problems, it will be this small number of death-dancers.

Indeed, even as we have thus far dominated this side of the island, using the village populations as both workforce and test subjects, what appears to be no more than two or three death-dancers have caused us quite a few setbacks and problems. Most notably is the death-dancer who calls herself Jeetra. Obstinate and stubborn even for an Argonian, the old lizard has actively resisted us every step of the way since we put the Writhing Wall in place. Her obduracy seems to be even-handed, though, as she demonstrates contempt for not only the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md), but disdain for any mainlander—and even for most of the non-Argonian inhabitants of Solstice. What started as speeches to her people warning of the dangers presented by the “corpse-stealers” (yes, that’s how she refers to us) has developed into an outright resistance. She’s actually begun gathering warriors from among the scattered tribes and seems to be attempting to forge them into some sort of army. It’s almost laughable!

Still, she has proven to be a thorn in our side. Supply shipments leave one location and never reach their destination, captives are set free from work camps, and patrols are ambushed. Her knowledge of the region and her close ties with her kinspeople have made it difficult to track her down and dispose of her. She remains a ghost in many ways, and those captives we question describe her as hard-bitten, opinionated, unpleasant, old, and extremely capable.

However, her own acrid personality and distrust limits her efficacy, in that she neither seeks nor accepts help from anyone outside other Stone-Nest villagers. She remains a bit of grit in the wheels of our great work, but I have no doubt we will soon find and dispose of her. I have also ordered some of our best researchers to learn all they can about these so-called death-dancers. Some information suggests they are simply caretakers for the sick and dying, intent on making the passage from this life to the next as easy as possible. But other reports indicate they are some sort of class of warrior-monk, which certainly better explains the amount of trouble their extremely small numbers have already caused us. As you have always taught us, information is the stone that sharpens our blades.

In the meantime, we will remain vigilant and keep her and her followers at bay. Jeetra seems to have a connection to the village that lies in the shadow of the Xul-Haj xanmeer. Perhaps we can use that to draw her out and finally get our hands on her. Your approval for such an operation is requested. Regardless, Jeetra and these other death-dancers will not trouble us much longer

All hail Mannimarco! All hail the Order of the Black Worm!

Commander Kor-Zakul, Gristmung Hold
