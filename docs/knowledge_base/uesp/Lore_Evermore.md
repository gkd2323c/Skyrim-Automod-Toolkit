[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-place-Evermore.jpg/200px-ON-place-Evermore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Evermore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Evermore.jpg) Evermore, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Evermore, sometimes spelled **Evermor**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>, is a city on the banks of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) in eastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located near the border with [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

Its impressive palace was assembled from slabs of [granite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Granite) quarried from nearby [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-2)</sup> Local traditions include the worship of [Saint Pelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saint_Pelin), a hero of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) who once served as a clergyman at [Bangkorai Garrison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Garrison).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Historically, the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of Evermore ruled over the region of [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai). These lands were subject to frequent attacks from the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) of the mountains, who asserted their birthright to the city and aimed to reclaim their ancestral homeland. Southern Bangkorai included the [Fallen Wastes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fallen_Wastes) region of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), leading Evermore's rulership to often be contested by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO-4)</sup> Evermore's position along trade routes spanning the Tamriel allowed it to become an important trading hub. Locally, it linked eastern High Rock, the southern and central regions of Hammerfell, [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), and western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), while more distant trade routes to the southeast connected it with [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-2)</sup>

## History
According to some Reachmen, the lands of northern Bangkorai, encompassing the place where the city of Evermore was built and the areas extending northward, were once a part of the Reachfolk [homeland](Lore_Western_Reach.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MZ-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Brinarch-6)</sup> The city itself was founded in [1E 983](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_983) by the [Bjoulsae River Tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bjoulsae_River_Tribes) after the fall of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) opened the Bjoulsae for trade and transport. According to the Decretal of Fiefdoms by Empress [Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra), the city's earliest leader was Barron Guy Marde of ye Rivver Banke. Their descendants would go on to form [House Guimard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Guimard), the ruling house of Evermore during the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRHo KE-7)</sup>

In [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542) Evermore was sacked by a horde of Reachmen led by [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) the Black Drake after only a three day siege. It was retaken by Duke [Blaise Guimard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blaise_Guimard), who was later elected to the throne of Evermore.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRHo KE-7)</sup> Evermore along with Daggerfall, [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) signed the first [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) following the defeat of the Reachmen.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ta Toa M-8)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Evermore and its lands were once again attacked by Reachmen, this time led by Uela. The [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) attempted to infiltrate the city in preparation for the arrival of the [Seventh Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seventh_Legion); however, Covenant agents were able to prevent the fall of the city with the help of Uela, who instead sought to take Evermore for herself, though she was ultimately killed before ever reaching the city.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO-4)</sup>

By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) the lands of Evermore had become fragmented and the Fallen Wastes region was possessed by Hammerfell. During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) the city-state of Evermore was ruled by King Tristynak. It had a rivalry with [North Point](Lore_Northpoint.md) and the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AR-9)</sup> Evermore was neighbored by several settlements, including Black Wastes to the west and Dunkarn Haven to the east.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Map Arena-10)</sup> Before the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) it was one of eight kingdoms in High Rock<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>, though the aftermath of the Warp would see Evermore as one of only five remaining Kingdoms in High Rock.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>
