# Kalpa


## Cultural Beliefs
*And the awful fighting ended again.*
— *[Shor Son of Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Shor_Son_of_Shor)* It is said that both Men and Mer know Tamriel is where the "nexus of creation" took place, as well as where the Last War will happen.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MA-7)</sup>

The dragon Paarthurnax referred to kalpas as being "the egg of the next world", and considered [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md)'s defeat at the hands of the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) enough to stop (either permanently or temporarily) the end of the world.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PTHNX-2)</sup>

According to [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) (Or potentially, [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr)),<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Xan Timeline-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-MKP-9)</sup> the end of the Kalpa is an echo of [Convention](Lore_Convention.md), in which he and Akatosh eat each other in madness.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOP8-10)</sup>

According to some sources, the destruction of [Lyg](Lore_Lyg.md) took place in the prior kalpa.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-9C-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-11)</sup> It is also said that the "half-Elf" [Umaril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril_the_Unfeathered)'s divine father hailed from the "world-river" of the previous kalpa.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSOP3-12)</sup>

### Ayleids
According to the Ayleid Creation Myth, the [Anuad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuad), the current world was proceeded by twelve, which were destroyed by the great battle between Anu and Padomay.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TAA-13)</sup>

### Dunmer
According to the teachings of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) God [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), the law of time is "static change", which he compares to a [reptile coiled in wheel shape](Lore_Ouroboros.md). Vivec explains that "cosmic time is repeated" through echoes of the primordial dichotomy of [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) and [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay), called the [Enantiomorph](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Enantiomorph). Those who reenact that dichotomy are said to cause catastrophes that end the universe. In the process, the actors themselves are said to enter the [Underworld](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), subsequently revising their own histories in retrospect as an [age of gold](Lore_Dawn_Era.md). Following the demise of the old universe, these revisions transform into "[the structure](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Earthbones) of [future nostalgia](Lore_Mantling.md)", allowing for further modification and reenactment. <sup>[[10]](#cite_note-36L2-14)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-36L21-15)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-36L11-16)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-36L35-17)</sup>

### Nords
The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) believe that the "Dead Gods", such as [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor), died to bring the current kalpa, which itself is a byproduct of its predecessor's destruction at the hands of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) the World-Eater, and that this world shall also be destroyed to create a new one.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VOF-18)</sup> What precedes it is the Final War,<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-GTNTR-19)</sup> the final doom,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRHS-20)</sup> the final battle,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SSCSS-21)</sup> where the Nords, led by the new gods of the current kalpa known as "Twilight Gods", "will show their final, best worth". Thus far the only Twilight God besides Alduin, is [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), the Dragonborn totem.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-GTNTR-19)</sup>

### Redguards
The [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) also believe in a cyclical world that is, or at least was, periodically consumed by the serpent god [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VOF-18)</sup> described as eating himself and being reborn similar to how a river empties itself into the sea yet is reborn at its source.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-KS-22)</sup> Satakal was depicted by the [Na-Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Na-Totambu) as an [ouroboros](Lore_Ouroboros.md), a snake eating its own tail.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOCo S-23)</sup> The [Crown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown) scholar and historian The Unveiled [Azadiyeh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azadiyeh) wrote of Satakal pulling [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) beneath the waves due to the "Hunger of [Sep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sep)" filling the hearts of mortals, bringing evil to Yokuda. She described it as a "Time of Ending", with their voyage to Hammerfell being described as a "Time of Beginning".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-THo S-24)</sup> Azadiyeh also wrote a plea to the people of Hammerfell to return to reverence of their traditional gods and traditions, believing that if they did not honor their ancestors, it would bring about a new "Ending Time", worse again than those before.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Pf OE-25)</sup>

One Redguard proverb spoke of the world as being Satakal's egg, which he will later devour,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-KS-22)</sup> similar to the way the dragon Paarthurnax described the cycle of kalpas.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PTHNX-2)</sup>

Some Yokudan myths describe the Second Serpent, [Sep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sep), creating Nirn out of previous dead worldskins, rather than Satakal the First Serpent.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup> Some Redguard pilgrims describe the cycle of worldskins as Satakal eating himself and Sep repeatedly tricking the spirits to make Nirn each cycle, as well as stealing a Pearl from Ruptga. When the pearl is uncovered, water runs from it over the deadskins of Satakal, and the Hungry Stomach of both Sep and Satakal is satisfied, only for the cycle to then repeat. <sup>[[21]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

Sep is regarded to be a [hungry void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent) in the sky,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup> while Satakal is regarded to rise from the starry depths of the ocean.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-THo S-24)</sup> To some Redguards, this symbolizes their dichotomy; "Does not the serpent made of sky above reflect the serpent made of the sea below? Yea, it is so."

### Argonians
The Argonians of the [Azdi-Kostleel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adzi-Kostleel) tribe believe in a cyclical world comparable to the one described in the Yokudan creation myth, with [Atakota](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atakota) having similarities to Satakal, periodically consuming and recreating the world.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-COR-27)</sup> The Argonians of the [Nisswo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nisswo) tribe also acknowledge Atakota and [his shadow](Lore_Lorkhan.md). The Shadow eventually devours the broken halves of Atakota before the World can end, to save his children and keep them within his secrets.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-COR-27)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOWUS-28)</sup> Tales about an explorer deep in Black Marsh describe a golden Argonian whose face changed twelve times. Soon after the explorer witnessed a vision of many wheels and their towers, that began to break, before quickly the Shadow of Atakota came to devour them.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LTOTFE4-29)</sup>

### Khajiit
It is said that the spirits of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) whose souls have not [been corrupted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro-m%27Athra) are taken by [Khenarthi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi) to the [Sands Behind the Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sands_Behind_the_Stars), where they await for the "Next Pounce".<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOLMD-30)</sup>

Pre- [ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta) myths state that at the end of time, Khenarthi will summon the united spirits of the Khajiiti people to defend creation.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TSS-31)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-32)</sup> It is also said that [Tojay-raht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tojay-raht) fighters train for the "War at the End".<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AOPF-33)</sup>

Certain tales about [Boethra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boethra), describe a cyclical battle that has happened repeatedly at the Dawn of the world. These tales also describe Boethra seeking the truth the [Marukhati Selectives](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukhati_Selective) sought, and coming upon twelve worlds, finding among them two serpents in battle.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TBOB-34)</sup>

### Reachfolk
The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) believe that at the "end of all days", [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) will fight alongside them,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONHSG-35)</sup> and the [Dark Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart) will beat again, reawakened by feeding on the deaths of mortals, and its darkness will spread from its depths to consume everything, sparing only [Namira](Lore_Namira.md)'s faithful.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONA-36)</sup>

### Tsaesci
The Tsaesci refer to the birth of time as "the Striking",<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-36L21-15)</sup> which split the [Egg](Lore_Aurbis.md) into twelve worlds. Each world was said to be a serpent with a name, that coiled unto themselves and became more eggs.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-37)</sup>

*And the awful fighting began again.*
— *[Shor Son of Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Shor_Son_of_Shor)*
