# Wildspear Clan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp.jpg/200px-ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp.jpg) Wildspear Clan Camp ca. 2E 582 The Wildspear Clan was a prominent [clan](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) that lived in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), a region in western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They were a settled clan. Their holdings were located near Markarth,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup> to the northeast from the capital.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WCC-2)</sup>

The Reachfolk of the Wildspear Clan dedicated themselves to one of the [Great Spirits of the Reach](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md), the Master of the Chase [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). They were devout followers of the Hunt-Father and honored him through ritual hunts. They believed that [humans](Lore_Man.md), especially strong and clever enemies of the clan were the best prey for these sacrifices.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

They erected a shrine to Hircine, known as the Wildspear Ritual Stone in the easternmost part of their camp. They were also in possession of copies of the text of *[Reach Hunting Hymn](Lore_Reach_Hunting_Hymn.md)*.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WCC-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/ON-npc-Wildspear_Archer.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Wildspear_Archer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Wildspear_Archer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Wildspear_Archer.jpg) A hunter of the Wildspear Clan ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wildspear_Clan_Camp_02.jpg) Members of the Wildspear Clan praying to Hircine ca. 2E 582 The Wildspear Clan was considered one of the more prominent clans of the Reach as of [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OTCOTR-1)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) High Shaman [Glynroch](Lore_Glynroch.md) of the [Shrine of the Hunt-Father](Lore_Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.md) requested Apprentice Gwerina, one of the shamans active in the Karth Valley to deliver the [symbol of Hrokkibeg](Lore_Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang.md) and join him in the temple in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LTAG-3)</sup> She died while carrying the task out, near to the camp of the Wildspear Clan.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AG-4)</sup> The symbol of Hrokkibeg was found and then returned to the shrine by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOH-5)</sup>

Wildspear Clan was hostile to both outsiders and their Reachfolk neighbors. In the same year, hunting in their territories was considered dangerous by the Reachmen of the city of Markarth due to their ritualistic hunting traditions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Travregholan-6)</sup>

In the same year, several members of the [Thornroot Clan](Lore_Thornroot_Clan.md) attacked a few other clans of the Reach. They also intended to attack the Wildspears, but it is unknown whether the clans fought or not.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONAD-7)</sup>

Despite their hostile ways, at least some members of the clan joined the [Wayward Guardians](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md) led by [Arana](Lore_Arana.md). Although they were mostly looking for plunder, they contributed to the fall of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Desola-8)</sup>
