*The further north you go into Skyrim, the more powerful and elemental the people become, and the less they require dwellings and shelters.*
— *[Children of the Sky](Lore_Children_of_the_Sky.md)* The Ice Tribes (also referred to as **glacier tribes**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-promo-1)</sup> are blue-skinned, tribal humanoids known to be encountered in the northern, frozen regions of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DSvarieties-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SKvarieties-3)</sup> Their physiques are typically quite muscular and some can be seen with two blue spike formations upon their shoulders.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DSvarieties-2)</sup> They are known to make allies with creatures like [trolls](Lore_Troll.md), flying [rays](Lore_Ray.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SKglacier-4)</sup> and the fearsome [Gehenoth](Lore_Gehenoth.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DSevents-5)</sup> They are also capable of negotiations and making deals with [men](Lore_Man.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/3/3b/DS-creature-Gehenoth_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DS-creature-Gehenoth_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DS-creature-Gehenoth_02.png) The Gehenoth Thriceborn In [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), Ice Tribes inhabited [Glacier Crawl](Lore_Glacier_Crawl.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) at the time when the [Soul of Conflict](Lore_Soul_of_Conflict.md) ventured through it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SKglacier-4)</sup> The Hero defeated the Ice Tribes there, using the keys they carried to unlock access to the frozen-over [airship](Lore_Air_Transportation.md#Airships) that crashed into the glacier long ago. There, the Hero slew the Ice Tribes' Glacier Chieftain and retrieved the [Star Tooth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-AG-9)</sup> the Ice Tribes inhabiting the [areas](Lore_The_Pale.md) outside of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DSplaces-10)</sup> had raided the settlement. One of Vinticae's four champions had secretly set the Ice Tribes upon the town.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-promo-1)</sup> Not knowing which of his champions was the traitor, he banished them all. Vinticae sent for the [Hero of Dawnstar](Lore_Hero_of_Dawnstar.md), and with the help of the [oracle](Lore_Sight.md) healer [Eustacia](Lore_Eustacia.md), the Hero interrogated the champions. Once the Hero exposed the true traitor, the Ice Tribes and their allies began their final assault upon Dawnstar. Despite the city's outer defenses, sheer numbers allowed some invaders to get past the walls. With the aid of the three loyal champions and the power of [Starfrost](Lore_Starfrost.md), the Hero slayed the Ice Tribes, concluding the battle with the defeat of the Gehenoth Thriceborn.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DSevents-5)</sup>

## Society
Beyond Dawnstar, the Ice Tribes had their own locations called the Ice Tribe Haven and Ice Council.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DSplaces-10)</sup> The Ice Tribes give themselves ranks like Scout, Warrior, Bowman, Raider, Leader, Captain, and Chieftain. Their ranks are always preceded by another word, including Ice, Hungry, Winter, Snow, Blizzard, Dawn, Elemental, Elite, Glacier, and Thriceborn. They are known to wield pole weapons, axes, daggers,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> and bows. They can be seen using armors of cloth and [metal](Lore_Metals.md), shields, and jewelry.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DSvarieties-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SKvarieties-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

It is unknown if their strongest warriors being known as Thriceborn has any relation to [reincarnation](Lore_Reincarnation.md), a belief held by many Tamrielic cultures that a [soul](Lore_Soul.md) can be reborn.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Co S-13)</sup>

## Notes
- The Ice Tribes in *Shadowkey* were never explicitly called "Ice Tribes". However, due to the similarities in appearance, locale, and tribal militancy, as well as both *Shadowkey* and *Dawnstar* being *[Travels](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/TES_Travels)* games and the shared "Ice Scout" variant, it is almost certain that they are indeed Ice Tribes.
- In *Shadowkey*, the Ice Tribes reuse the same models as [Orcish NPCs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Shadowkey-Orc) but without the tusks.
- Given the mystery and obscurity surrounding the Ice Tribes, many fan theories have attempted to connect them to a different races such as the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md), [Riekr](Lore_Riekr.md), or [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md). However, there has been no confirmation of any one of these theories.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-AG-9)</sup>
