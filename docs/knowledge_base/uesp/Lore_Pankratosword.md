# Shehai

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/ON-tribute-hunding-Shehai_Summoning.png/150px-ON-tribute-hunding-Shehai_Summoning.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Shehai_Summoning.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Shehai_Summoning.png) Shehai Summoning A **Shehai** or **Spirit Sword** is an ability used by the [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) to manifest a sword from their very [soul](Lore_Soul.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHTH-1)</sup> By sheer force of will, an Ansei warrior could create this ethereal sword of pure thought and it would take shape in their hand,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EJ-2)</sup> This form of the Way became known as "Shehai Shen She Ru" or Way of the Spirit Sword,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DTS-3)</sup> an art known as **sword magic**.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HRC-4)</sup> and all sword-singers trained to learn this form through intense training and meditation in the Way of the Sword, but not every swordsman was successful in becoming an Ansei.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHTH-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup> Those who could were said to be more than mortal.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-COTFC6-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-7)</sup> According to Master Assistant Materials Acquirer Pacrooti, the Ansei had mastered their sword techniques so thoroughly that they could access abilities similar to a mage's spells.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Pacrooti-8)</sup> Conversely, there have been certain gifted individuals who could form a Shehai from a very young age or with little or no training.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup> Other times, a Shehai may be formed in times of great stress.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DTS-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup>

To become an Ansei, a sword-singer had to demonstrate an ability to form the Shehai.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup> These Shehai were typically described as pale, misty, insubstantial, and as if it was made of light. Sometimes its shape wouldn't even be particularly sword-like, and would be of no use as an actual [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHTH-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NFRH-9)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup>

Ansei of the second-rank and above were known to have the ability to not only form the Shehai, but wield it as a weapon in battle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup> Their Shehai shined brighter and was much deadlier. Described as an "unstoppable weapon of great might" that could cut down foes like "a scythe through wheat", disarming such an Ansei could only be done by severing their head or taking their mind.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-7)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHTH-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup> There was some evidence, however, that suggested the Shehai of an Ansei could be shattered, leaving behind only its essence.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Cont Shehai-10)</sup> Ansei of the highest virtue wandered the countryside engaging in battle, righting wrongs, and seeking to end the strife.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RTHTH-1)</sup> To them, manifesting the Shehai was said to have come as naturally as breathing.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TDOA-11)</sup>

Forbidden techniques involving the Shehai are reputedly capable of great devastation to a continent, such as that used by the rebel band of Ansei called the Hiradirge.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-12)</sup> Indeed, the [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) Warrior corroborated this legend when he claimed the Shehai of a first-rank Ansei is what sank Yokuda.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWarrior-13)</sup> Further, some accounts claim that this forbidden sword-stroke was called the "Pankratosword"—a maneuver that could destroy the very laws of nature.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TIL-14)</sup>

## Decline
*We chose the blades and destroyed the beasts. But as a result, the Shehai—our spirit swords—were forever destroyed. Centuries of wondrous tradition was snuffed out. I must bear that weight always.*
— [Derik Hallin](Lore_Derik_Hallin.md) After conquering Hammerfell, the Yokudans found themselves in a time of relative peace. Many warriors put down their swords in order to build their new nation.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TTTTOH-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONNan-16)</sup> Ansei warriors became very rare, and although many training halls were established throughout Hammerfell, very few produced masters of the Shehai.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FTMS-5)</sup>

When Hammerfell came under attack from a race of giant [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) from a rent in space time,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NFRH-9)</sup> Hallin scoured the land to find the five mighty swords (a mix of Shehai and [smithing](Lore_Smithing.md)) that ended a similar goblin threat during the time of [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md). Although their use was forbidden, Hallin and his companions wielded the blades and hurled the swords together into the void, defeated the goblin army by sealing them in the void between their world and [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). As a result, their Shehai was forever destroyed. Hallin saved the people and eventually restored the teachings of Frandar Hunding to Hammerfell.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NFRH-9)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-STDH-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONDH-18)</sup> Later in his life, when threatend by a surviving giant goblin warlord, the Queen of [Ojwambu](Lore_Ojwambu.md) pleaded with [Derik Hallin](Lore_Derik_Hallin.md), the "Last of the Ansei". According to legend, Hallin called forth the spirits of long-dead Ansei to aid him in his defense of the city. Numerous Ansei warriors appeared and fought alongside Hallin until they were victorious.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TTTTOH-15)</sup> It is believed that the Ansei's ability to summon the Shehai was lost when the Ansei died out in the First Era.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Au A-19)</sup>

By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there were no known Ansei warriors, with native [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) swordsmen believing the existence of Shehai to be absurd<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Hireling Abnab-20)</sup> and some foreign scholars dismissed the Shehai as "superstitious nonsense".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-7)</sup> For unexplained reasons, the Ansei simply died out.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LFWS-21)</sup> By the [Third](Lore_Third_Era.md) and [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), although there were some who followed the old ways within the ancient Halls of Virtues of War, very few Redguards had ever seen a Shehai, let alone called forth one.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NFRH-9)</sup>

### Modern Occurrences
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg) A Shehai In the mid- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the Yokudan sword-saint [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md) became a [Vampire Lord](Lore_Vampire_Lord.md) and, at some point, lost his ability to manifest his Shehai. He journeyed to [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), where he met with the legendary [Red Eagle](Lore_Red_Eagle.md), a [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) hero named Faolan who was known for wielding a flaming sword called [Red Eagle's Fury](Lore_Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESORed Eagle-22)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Faolan taught Rada how to reclaim his Shehai by focusing on what he loved most: his [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md), an army of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) to whom Rada wished to grant salvation.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESORed Eagle-22)</sup> Rada would later claim that his Shehai had been "reforged in bitter blood," and it thereafter took the form of a black sword that glowed a bloody red.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

When [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md) constellation temporarily took a [mortal form](Lore_Celestial.md) in the mid-Second Era, he wielded an extremely powerful Shehai that was capable of producing shockwaves and magical storms.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONAssault-25)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONMoves-26)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Redguard swordmaster [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) achieved his lifelong ambition of summoning his Spirit Sword. He did this by following the ancient teachings of Red Eagle while on an expedition to the Reach, following in the footsteps of Rada al-Saran by putting aside his devotion to duty, and focusing on what he loved: his companion, [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESORed Eagle-22)</sup> With Rada's death that same year, Sai mourned for his devotion to restoring the monstrous Gray Host rather than following the honorable tradition of the Ansei.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-27)</sup>
