Bonewalkers (or **bone-walkers**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Sermons-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MDC2-2)</sup> are [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) [zombie](Lore_Zombie.md) -like creatures<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> covered in gaping wounds and sharp protuberances of bone and metal employed in the rituals that bind them to the mortal plane.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lot D-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MW-5)</sup> All Bonewalkers are considered to be malevolent and dangerous.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lot D-4)</sup> These revenants are used to guard the ancestral tombs throughout [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MW-5)</sup>

Though not as powerful as other undead, Bonewalkers are feared for the terrible curses they can bestow upon their foes. Bonewalkers are resistant to frost, poison, and shock attacks. They are known to carry the disease [Brown Rot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brown_Rot). They can also be [summoned](Lore_Conjuration.md) by magic users.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MW-5)</sup>

## Creation
They are created by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) through sacred ritual, considered by most foreigners to be a form of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Sharn-6)</sup> However, the practice of necromancy is outlawed in Morrowind.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The Dunmer do not consider the creation of Bonewalkers and other such revenants to be necromancy, instead associating it with traditional [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Aat D-8)</sup> The Dunmer themselves consider the summoning of Bonewalkers through profane ritual to be blasphemy and necromancy; they draw a distinction between [ancestral spirits](Lore_Ancestor_Ghost.md) who perform their sacred duty, and a sorcerer's thrall, bound to service.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Those from outside Morrowind refer to these flesh revenants as simply a form of zombie.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lot D-4)</sup>

## Variants
There are several varieties of Bonewalker that can be encountered, such as Greater Bonewalkers and Lesser Bonewalkers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MW-5)</sup> Greater Bonewalkers are larger and more powerful than the more common Lesser Bonewalkers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lot D-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MW-5)</sup> Some Bonewalkers have three precious stones set in their lower jaw, in accordance with ancient magical practice.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Sermons-1)</sup>

## History and Mythology
According to the teachings of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) encountered a Bonewalker in the ancestor alcove of [House Mora](Lore_House_Mora.md) when he came to recover the [Treasure Wood Sword](Lore_Treasure_Wood_Sword.md). The revenant emerged from a wall and declared that the Sword would not be handed over due to bargains that House Mora had made with [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), but Vivec banished it and subsequently recovered the Sword.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Sermons-1)</sup> The [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) [Hortator](Lore_Hortator.md) [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) was supposedly transformed into a Bonewalker after his death at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md). Even though this information is lost to the annals of most Tribune histories, he is still depicted in his Bonewalker form on his Temple shrines.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-10)</sup>
