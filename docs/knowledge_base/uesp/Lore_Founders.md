# Tales of Tribute

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-item-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg/200px-ON-item-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg) Tales of Tribute board *Tales of Tribute is many things to many different people. For some […] it is simply a means to revelry. But for the dreamers and truth-seekers, it provides so much more. Each deck tells a story. A timeless fable.*" —The Author<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> Tales of Tribute (or **Tribute** for short) is a two-player card game that draws inspiration from various playing card traditions and incorporates aspects from other card games. It originated in Gonfalon Bay and has gained popularity throughout Tamriel during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The game involves players "telling a tale" to compete against each other and collect cards that improve their ability to control the game and earn Prestige points.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBG-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup>

The **Roister's Club** was an organization responsible for management of all aspects of Tales of Tribute, such as organizing tournaments, producing new rules, sponsoring chapters, and manufacturing cards. In the beginning, the Roister's Club was a small group of only a few dozen people who created the game. Although originally only the game's creators were members of the club, it has grown to the point that almost everyone who played the game identified as a Roister.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup>

As time passed, the game's original creators, who were mostly scholars, artisans, and explorers, began to be referred to as the **Founders**. While they were known for valuing their anonymity, they still played an active role in promoting the game.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup>

## Origins and Overview
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-load-Tales_of_Tribute.png/200px-ON-load-Tales_of_Tribute.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Tales_of_Tribute.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Tales_of_Tribute.png) The Roisters [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/ON-place-Gonfalon_Gaming_Hall.jpg/200px-ON-place-Gonfalon_Gaming_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Gonfalon_Gaming_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Gonfalon_Gaming_Hall.jpg) The Gonfalon Gaming Hall Tales of Tribute originated in [Gonfalon Bay](Lore_Gonfalon_Bay.md) and has become popular throughout entire [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Initially, only the creators of the game belonged to the Roister's Club, but eventually, practically everyone who played the game was referred to as a Roister. The original club was a small group of game designers, consisting of only a few dozen people, who later came to call themselves Founders. Despite being mostly scholars, artisans, and explorers, little was known about them as they preferred to remain anonymous. Despite their secrecy, the Founders continued to work on the game, sponsoring gaming chapters, organizing tournaments, and rewarding excellent players. It was speculated that they could reveal themselves in the future and receive recognition for their contributions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup>

The exact number of Founders is not widely known, but at least five of them were known. The Author, an enigmatic [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) woman, resided in the [Hollow City](Lore_Hollow_City.md) within [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and viewed the game of Tales of Tribute as having a higher purpose beyond mere entertainment.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup> Linyia, an [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) explorer and scholar of ancient games, had a passion for breathing new life into long-forgotten games. Her quest for knowledge took her to the ancient ruins of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md). She was known for her expertise in strategy and deck composition, and her contributions helped shape the game into its modern iteration.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-L-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Gelekh-6)</sup> Murzaga gra-Ghorn, an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) from [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md), was known to assist the Roister's Club with their procurement needs, such as ink, stock, and other related items.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MGG-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup> Nhorhim was a blind [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) who resided with the [Ternion Monks](Lore_Ternion_Monks.md) of [Mistwatch](Lore_Mistwatch.md). He was one of the creators of the game and had an exceptional ability to recognize every card. Nhorhim could identify the cut, weight, and even the scent of each card.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-N-9)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Gelekh-6)</sup> Fillia was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who excelled at various games, including cards, dice, and parlor games. She played a crucial role in the creation of Tales of Tribute, but her winning streak came to an abrupt end when she was murdered by a drunken opponent who couldn't handle losing. Her spirit lingered in a cottage in Grahtwood, where she awaited a player skilled enough to defeat her in Tales of Tribute and release her from her ethereal prison.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-F-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SG-11)</sup>

Eventually, the club expanded and gained more recognition throughout Tamriel. The Roister's Club became an organization that oversees all aspects of Tales of Tribute. They were responsible for arranging tournaments, releasing updated rules, supporting chapters, and most notably, the production of the game's cards. Players of the Roister's Club were sponsored by game-barons, who provided financial support for the club's activities, led local chapters, and helped to promote the popularity of Tales of Tribute throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup> Tales of Tribute have left their mark on daily expressions. For instance, when someone speaks of power, wealth, and recognition, one could say, "It sounded like a game of Tribute.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Federo-12)</sup> There are magical tricks associated with the Tales of Tribute game, including card summoning.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Card_Conjurer-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HPTC-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Card_Conjuring-15)</sup> Among the known players of the Tribute was [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md), a Mother of Blades of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Naryu-16)</sup>

The Roister's Club had chapters operating in many cities and towns throughout Tamriel. Some of these locations included [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), [Shimmerene](Lore_Shimmerene.md), [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), [Gonfalon Bay](Lore_Gonfalon_Bay.md), [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md), [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md), [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md), [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md), [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), [Rawl'kha](Lore_Rawl%27kha.md), [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md), [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md), [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md), and even places such as Stonelore Grove, Amenos Station and [Baandari Trading Post](Lore_Baandari_Trading_Post.md) along the [Xylo River](Lore_Xylo_River.md) in [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Roiser CC-17)</sup> Little is known of the history of each chapter but the Gonfalon Bay's chapter's headquarters, the Gonfalon Gaming Hall on the [Harbor Island](Lore_Harbor_Island.md) was originally a [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) villa. After [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) took it over during the duchy transition, they eventually sold it to game-baron Razhamad, who purchased it in circa [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581). Since that time, the card players in that location were able to sustain a respectable livelihood by taking advantage of tourists who were willing to part with their gold.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-VGTHI-18)</sup> As of that time Mournhold Chapter was considered to be very prestigious.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup>

Various chapters of the club advertised themselves,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-RCT-19)</sup> and offered game tutorials to those interested in learning how to play.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-STP-20)</sup> The patrons of the Roister's Club gaming halls could access official rules guides for the game.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Gelekh-6)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SG-11)</sup> The Roister's Club encouraged their members to stay sharp, and spread the game's popularity by performing various challenges, such as defeating both common folk of Tamriel and card-playing adventurers.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Kishka-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Marunji-22)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SG-11)</sup> They were rewarded with various benefits, including cards and clues, in exchange for their efforts.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-CATC-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DT-24)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup> Some Roisters traveled around to search for potential new players, handling all the registration fees and membership dues.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SG-11)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg/200px-ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute.jpg) Roisters of the Roister's Club [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/ON-npc-The_Author.jpg/200px-ON-npc-The_Author.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Author.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Author.jpg) The Author, one of the Founders As the club and the card game gained popularity, mugs and steins associated with them started to become more commonly found.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-RCC-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MRCS-26)</sup> Clubs hosted events and celebrated [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md) with banquets. Ornamental carving knives were used during those festivities.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-RCCK-27)</sup>

Tales of Tribute is known to incorporate aspects from other card games. The game's mechanics draw inspiration from various playing card traditions, such as the hexagonal shape taken from [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) fortune-telling decks and the symbol arrangement borrowed from an ancient [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) card game played in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Despite these influences, Tales of Tribute goes beyond being just an amalgamation of ideas. The decks achieve a cohesive aesthetic through the framing of the cards and the design of the card backs, which feature Bretonic elements and the distinctive winged crown that unifies the identity of each card.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup> Banners representing the game are characterized by a color scheme of red, gold, and blue.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TOTB-28)</sup> The Tales of Tribute sigil consists of three cards. This tri-card symbol is not limited to game-related items only, as it could also be found on various other objects such as steins used in Roister's Clubs.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MRCS-26)</sup>

The production of the cards themselves occurs in Gonfalon Bay, although their aesthetic inspiration draws from various locations throughout the continent. The cards are typically made from pasteboard, a durable material that is common in the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md). Printers and painters use various pigments from across the mainland, and [squid](Lore_Squid.md) ink is utilized to write numerical information, which is a key factor in identifying forgeries. Roister's presses run persistently to produce more cards for the game,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup> which are made with heavy stock. Members of the Roister's Club then mark them as belonging to a specific deck. Blank, unfinished cards could be found in circulation throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BTC-29)</sup>

Originally, the tokens were crafted from [larimar](Lore_Larimar.md), a precious stone mined from nearby [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md). However, by the mid-Second Era, coins were made using more common materials like [iron](Lore_Iron.md), [limestone](Lore_Limestone.md), and [sandstone](Lore_Sandstone.md) to reduce costs. The use of [Horker](Lore_Horker.md) [ivory](Lore_Ivory.md) tokens became increasingly popular as well, especially in the northern regions of Tamriel.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/ON-npc-Nhorhim.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Nhorhim.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nhorhim.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nhorhim.jpg) Founder Nhorhim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-npc-Fillia.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Fillia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Fillia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Fillia.jpg) Founder Fillia The game's popularity spurred the creation of various items designed to enhance its accessibility and convenience. Clockwork Card Shufflers are elaborate mechanisms designed specifically for shuffling Tribute decks during official tournaments.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CCS-30)</sup> Various methods were used to keep track of the prestige score count, including the use of Tribute dices, often made of [jade](Lore_Jade.md), [gold](Lore_Gold.md) -flecked Prestige beads, and Prestige counters with etched dials made of [cherry](Lore_Cherry_Blossom_Tree.md) wood. Dedicated containers, such as lacquered cases or velvet cloth bags, were often used to store some of these implements.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-JTD-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-GFPB-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-CWPC-33)</sup> Display cases and travel kits were produced,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TOTDD-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TOTKT-35)</sup> as well as tables specifically tailored for gaming rooms.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TOTTD-36)</sup> Other such example were card frames, made of metals such as [pewter](Lore_Pewter.md) designed to add weight and protection to the cards.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PTCF-37)</sup> Players of the game had access to various other accessories, including embossed lacquered boxes to store play decks and woven hempen play mats adorned with fanciful representations of Tribute Patrons painted onto the reeds.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-LETDB-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-HTPM-39)</sup>

In the Roister's Club, players are ranked based on their level of experience, which determines the opponents they can challenge. Novice players can only compete against other novices and are not allowed to challenge higher-ranked players. However, by playing and winning games, players can climb the ranks and earn the ability to challenge higher-tier opponents.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SG-11)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup> A portion of players deemed the ranking system an unnecessary addition and instead chose to engage in the game purely for enjoyment.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-STK-40)</sup>

While for some, Tales of Tribute was merely a diverting way to pass the time,<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Mizzik-41)</sup> it was more than just a game of entertainment and chance. For some it was more than just a game, it was a way of life, even compared to a religion.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup> It was also addictive,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Gelekh-6)</sup> and prone to being exploited by crime lords who owned illegal gambling parlors. One such establishment was Yves Grandvache's Gambling Parlor, located within the outlaw's refuge in [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-FS-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-RD-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Eye-44)</sup> Certain people suffered significant financial losses by betting their money on the game,<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-VJ-45)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Gelekh-6)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-GLarocque-46)</sup> Typically, gambling wasn't a significant part of the game, though it was far more prevalent during unofficial matches. Some players were even at risk of losing their freedom due to their accumulated debts.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup> Sometimes animals were put up as stakes in a game of Tribute.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-CL-47)</sup>

In 2E 582, a Tales of Tribute tournament was organized, attracting contestants from different parts of Tamriel, including members of the Roister's Club's various chapters such as the Gonfalon Bay Chapter, Wayrest Chapter, Elden Root Chapter, and Mournhold Chapter.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TTB-48)</sup> The tournament took place in various locations, including Daggerfall,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-FN-49)</sup> Shornhelm,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-CN-50)</sup> Wayrest,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TTB-48)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-VB-51)</sup> Skywatch,<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Lentondil-52)</sup> Marbruk,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Malilen-53)</sup> Elden Root,<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-COTP-54)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Raenobi-55)</sup> Davon's Watch,<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-IK-56)</sup> Stormhold,<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Dralcia K-57)</sup> Gonfalon Bay, and Mournhold,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TFR-58)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TF-59)</sup> where players battled it out in intense games of Tales of Tribute. Eventually, a new member of the Gonfalon Bay Chapter<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ETOTQ-60)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-ANV-61)</sup> emerged as the champion, defeating all opponents,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TFR-58)</sup> including the chief novice of the Wayrest Chapter, Varnauld Broch,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TTB-48)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-VB-51)</sup> adjutant game-baron of the Elden Root Chapter Raenobi,<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-COTP-54)</sup> and champion of the Mournhold Chapter Teliv Fels,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TFR-58)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TF-59)</sup> earning the recognition, the title of Tribute Champion,<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-GBCM-62)</sup> and the grand prize - a significant sum of money, thanks to contributions from the Roister's Club and city governments.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup>

Masters of Tales of Tribute tournaments were rewarded with trophies made of [orichalcum](Lore_Orichalcum.md),<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TTO-63)</sup> [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md),<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-TTE-64)</sup> [quicksilver](Lore_Quicksilver.md),<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-TTQ-65)</sup> [voidsteel](Lore_Voidsteel.md),<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-TTV-66)</sup> and [rubedite](Lore_Rubedite.md),<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TTR-67)</sup> with rubedite being the most prestigious and orichalcum being the least.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TOT-68)</sup> Golden medallions are also among the trophies awarded to the winners of the Tribute tournaments.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Quen-69)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Umindior-70)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-GM-71)</sup> In addition, new decks were often given out as prizes in tournaments, among other rewards.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup>

## Rules
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e4/ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_04.jpg/200px-ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_04.jpg) Tales of Tribute match [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-npc-Linyia.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Linyia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Linyia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Linyia.jpg) Founder Linyia Tales of Tribute is a two-player card game where each player competes by "telling a tale". Players can increase their chances of winning by collecting cards, which improve their ability to control the game's action and earn Prestige points. To win a match, players must achieve one of the following:

- Amass at least 40 Prestige points on their turn and maintain lead until the end of their opponent's turn,
- Be the first to reach 80 Prestige points (immediate victory),
- Secure the support of all four Patrons (immediate victory).

At the start of a match, each player selects two Patron Decks that they own, which determine their starting cards, the Tavern's available cards, and the Patrons present for the game. In addition, the Treasury deck plays a vital role in the game. As a result, each match is played with a total of five decks, which includes one Treasury deck and four themed decks. On each turn, players draw up to five cards and play them in any order, with some cards granting Gold and others granting Power or Prestige. Gold is used to buy cards from the Tavern, as each card has a numerical cost, while Power activates Patrons and damages enemy agents. Unused Gold is lost at the end of the turn, but unused Power is converted into Prestige.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_Player.jpg/200px-ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_Player.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_Player.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_Player.jpg) A match between players of Imperial and Reachfolk origins Players can enhance their decks by purchasing cards from the Tavern and adding them to their Cooldown Pile. Cards can be divided into two categories: Actions and Agents. The effect of an Action card is resolved right after it is played, while an Agent card can be placed on the game board and has long-term effects. Their health can be diminished by spending power or removed from the board using specific Action cards or Patron abilities. Additionally, Contract cards are another type of card in the game that can be either an Action or Agent card. When players purchase Contract cards from the Tavern, they are played immediately instead. Lastly, there is another category of cards in the game known as Curse cards. These cards must be played before non-curse cards. The only known Curse Card is added to the opponent's deck through the usage of the Rajhin Patron ability. Once a player's Draw Pile is empty, they must shuffle their Cooldown Pile to create a new Draw Pile. Most cards have a Suit that corresponds to one of the four Patrons in the game, which triggers Combos that enhance certain cards' potency.

Patrons provide additional options for play and victory and start in Neutral alignment. Players typically can interact with one Patron per turn, and a Patron that Favors your opponent will return to Neutral alignment. Some Patrons have usage requirements, so players should carefully consider each one before committing.

Early on, players should add cards that increase their Gold gain per turn, limit Suit variety, and remove starting cards from their deck to increase their chances of drawing more powerful cards from the Tavern. Completing trials and following leads to special locations allows players to discover new decks and upgrade cards of existing ones. The game provides an opportunity to learn about new cards and find clues on acquiring new decks.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBG-2)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Brahgas TD-72)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TOT-68)</sup>

## Decks
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_03.jpg/200px-ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_03.jpg) Tales of Tribute cards [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_02.jpg/200px-ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Tales_of_Tribute_02.jpg) Tales of Tribute is played in inns all across Tamriel Tales of Tribute card decks are designed with themes based on legendary and historical figures from different cultures of Tamriel and beyond.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TOT1-73)</sup> In addition to these themed decks, there is also a universal Treasury deck that plays a vital role in the game.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Treasury-74)</sup> Each deck of Tales of Tribute represents a unique story and requires a different strategy to play.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup> New decks were regularly introduced.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSOOT-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Brahgas-8)</sup>

Series One introduced the eight decks for tournament play.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TOT1-73)</sup> These included the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) heroic figure [Saint Pelin](Lore_Pelin.md),<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-SP-75)</sup> ancient [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) king [Faolan the Red Eagle](Lore_Faolan.md),<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-RE-76)</sup> legendary [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) of the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md),<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-AFH-77)</sup> Grandmaster [Delmene](Lore_Delmene_Hlaalu.md) of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) who ushered in the golden era for this [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) [house](Lore_Great_Houses.md),<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-GDH-78)</sup> Immortal Sorcerer King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md), the leader of the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md),<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-SKO-79)</sup> [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md), the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) deity of thieves,<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-R-80)</sup> a Duke of Crows who led the [Blackfeather Court](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md) of the [Crow Daedra](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md),<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-DOC-81)</sup> and the enigmatic [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) Loremaster [Celarus](Lore_Celarus.md).<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-C-82)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TOT1-73)</sup>

Other notable figures include [Druid King](Lore_Druid_King.md) [Kasorayn](Lore_Kasorayn.md) who led the [druid](Lore_Druid.md) exodus to the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md),<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-DB-83)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-DK-84)</sup> and [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), a living Dunmeri god, and a member of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md).<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Almalexia-85)</sup>

Various custom and homemade Tribute patron tokens and decks were common and spread across Tamriel during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-TUTD-86)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-HTP-87)</sup> Certain founders and tribute masters were also known to use their own personalized decks, typically named after their creators.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-DB-83)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MGG-7)</sup>

### Treasury
The Treasury Deck is a Tales of Tribute deck that includes a patron and a set of unique standard cards. The deck comprises of six copies of one starter card and twenty contract cards, eight of which are unique, with varying numbers of duplicates. Unlike other patrons, the Treasury Deck patron always remains neutral and has no favor system. Its patron ability is to replace any card in play with a Writ of Coin card. At the start of the game, six copies of the Gold starter card are added to each player's deck. The standard cards in the deck always consist of one Ambush card, four Barterer cards, three Black Sacrament cards, two Blackmail cards, three Harvest Season cards, three Imprisonment cards, two Ragpicker cards, and two Tithe cards. Additionally, Writ of Coin cards can be added to the game through the patron's ability.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Treasury-74)</sup> Some players deemed cards like Black Sacrament too violent in themes for a card game.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Mizzik-41)</sup>

### Almalexia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-npc-Armiger_Bolm%2C_The_Paramount.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Armiger_Bolm%2C_The_Paramount.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Armiger_Bolm,_The_Paramount.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Armiger_Bolm,_The_Paramount.jpg) Tribute Grand Master Armiger Bolm, the Paramount The Almalexia Deck is a Tales of Tribute deck that includes a patron and a set of unique standard cards. It is themed after Almalexia, known as the Mother Morrowind, a member of the Tribunal. The deck comprises of two copies of one starter card and twenty standard cards, eight of which are unique, four of which are upgrades of other unique cards, with varying numbers of duplicates. At the start of the game, one copy of the Collection Plate starter card is added to each player's deck. The patron's power allows the player to look at the top cards of the opponent's deck and move one to their cooldown pile.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Almalexia-85)</sup>

The deck consists of three copies of Bardic Veneration (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Festival of Forbearance), three copies of Devotional Gaoler (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Hand of Almalexia), two copies of Mother's Mercy (one of which can be replaced with the upgraded card ALMSIVI's Charity), two copies of Mournhold Clergy, three copies of Philanthropy, two copies of Temple Arbiter, three copies of The Lesson of Ayem, and two copies of Tribunal Sentinel (one of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Mercymother Elite).<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Almalexia-85)</sup>

Around 2E 582, a member of the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md), Bolm, also known as the Paramount, arrived in the southern [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md) seeking a thrilling and elegant challenge. He sought out opponents for a game of Tales of Tribute, a contest of wit, daring, and grace. He asserted that his card deck had been blessed by Almalexia herself.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-COTP2-88)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ABTP-89)</sup> Players could upgrade specific cards in the deck by discovering clues, completing trials, and following leads to unique locations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBG-2)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-MEC-90)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-HOA-91)</sup> There were available tapestries with themes that matched the cards in this deck.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-TAP8-92)</sup><sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TAP5-93)</sup>

### Ansei Frandar Hunding
The Ansei Frandar Hunding Deck is a Tales of Tribute deck that includes a patron and a set of unique standard cards. It is themed after Frandar Hunding. The deck comprises of two copies of one starter card and twenty standard cards, eleven of which are unique, four of which are upgrades of other unique cards, with varying numbers of duplicates. At the start of the game, one copy of the Way of the Sword starter card is added to each player's deck. The patron's power allows the player to receive one extra coin during turns in which they begin in favored status.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-AFH-77)</sup>

The deck consists of two copies of Ansei Assault (one of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Ansei's Victory), three copies of Battle Meditation (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Grand Oratory), three copies of Conquest, three copies of March on Hattu (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Hira's End), three copies of No Shira Poet (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Hel Shira Herald), three copies of Shehai Summoning, and three copies of Warrior Wave.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-AFH-77)</sup>

It is one of the eight decks that were introduced to the tournament play by the Series One.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TOT1-73)</sup> Players could upgrade specific cards in the deck by discovering clues, completing trials, and following leads to unique locations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBG-2)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-AVC-94)</sup><sup>[[95]](#cite_note-GOC-95)</sup>

### Druid King
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/53/ON-npc-Druid_Braigh.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Druid_Braigh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Druid_Braigh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Druid_Braigh.jpg) Tribute Grand Master Braigh who introduced Tales of Tribute to Druids The Druid King Deck is a Tales of Tribute deck that includes a patron and a set of unique standard cards. It is themed after Kasorayn, the last Druid King. The deck comprises of two copies of one starter card and twenty standard cards, twelve of which are unique, four of which are upgrades of other unique cards, with varying numbers of duplicates. At the start of the game, one copy of the Ritual Herbs starter card is added to each player's deck. The patron's power allows the player to spend power to replace cards in the Tavern. Additionally, the patron's power allows the player to add a powerful card, The Chimera, to their Cooldown Pile when certain conditions are met.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-DK-84)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-FP-96)</sup>

The deck consists of two copies of Deepwoods Ritual, two copies of Eldertide Fenwitch (one of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Draoife Ritecaller), three copies of Firesong Haruspex, two copies of Forest Wraith, three copies of Runes of the Draoife (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Druid King Vestments), three copies of Stonelore Rockseer (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Envoy of the Draoife), two copies of Whispers of the Grove, and three copies of Wispcaller Totem (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Wispheart Totem).<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-DK-84)</sup>

It was created by the Druid Braigh, a man who introduced Tales of Tribute to the Druid community of [Glimmertarn](Lore_Glimmertarn.md) on Galen. He boasted numerous titles, including Tribute Grand Master, the Ivy Prodigy of Galen, the Ivy Savant, the Deck-Breaker, and the Streak-Breaker. The deck he created eventually gained recognition from the Roister's Club.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-DB-83)</sup><sup>[[97]](#cite_note-TNFB-97)</sup> Players could upgrade specific cards in the deck by discovering clues, completing trials, and following leads to unique locations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBG-2)</sup><sup>[[98]](#cite_note-WTC-98)</sup><sup>[[99]](#cite_note-DKVC-99)</sup> There were available tapestries with themes that matched the cards in this deck.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-TAP2-100)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-TAP3-101)</sup>

### Saint Pelin
The Saint Pelin Deck is a Tales of Tribute deck that includes a patron and a set of unique standard cards. It is themed after Saint Pelin. The deck comprises of two copies of one starter card and twenty standard cards, twelve of which are unique, four of which are upgrades of other unique cards, with varying numbers of duplicates. At the start of the game, one copy of the Fortify starter card is added to each player's deck. The deck consists of four copies of Archer's Volley (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Siege Weapon Volley), two copies of Bangkorai Sentries (one of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Knights of Saint Pelin), two copies of Banneret (one of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Knight Commander), two copies of Rally, three copies of Reinforcements (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Legion's Arrival), two copies of Shield Bearer, two copies of The Armory, and two copies of The Portcullis.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-SP-75)</sup>

It is one of the eight decks that were introduced to the tournament play by the Series One.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TOT1-73)</sup> Players could upgrade specific cards in the deck by discovering clues, completing trials, and following leads to unique locations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBG-2)</sup><sup>[[118]](#cite_note-KCC-118)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-KOSPC-119)</sup><sup>[[120]](#cite_note-LAC-120)</sup> There were available tapestries with themes that matched the cards in this deck.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-TAP7-121)</sup>

### Sorcerer-King Orgnum
The Sorcerer-King Orgnum Deck is a Tales of Tribute deck that includes a patron and a set of unique standard cards. It is themed after King Orgnum of Pyandonea. The deck comprises of two copies of one starter card and twenty standard cards, twelve of which are unique, four of which are upgrades of other unique cards, with varying numbers of duplicates. At the start of the game, one copy of the Sea Elf Raid starter card is added to each player's deck. The patron's power allows the player to exchange gold for a fixed amount of power or an amount dependent on the number of cards in their possession, and to add copies of Summerset Sacking card in cooldown pile.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-SKO-79)</sup>

The deck consists of three copies of Ghostscale Sea Serpent (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Sea Serpent Colossus), two copies of King Orgnum's Command, three copies of Maormer Boarding Party (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Summerset Sacking), two copies of Maormer Cutter, two copies of Sea Raider's Glory, three copies of Serpentprow Schooner (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Pyandonean War Fleet), three copies of Storm Shark Wavecaller (up to two of which can be replaced with the upgraded card Serpentguard Rider), and two copies of Snakeskin Freebooter.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-SKO-79)</sup>

It is one of the eight decks that were introduced to the tournament play by the Series One.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TOT1-73)</sup> Players could upgrade specific cards in the deck by discovering clues, completing trials, and following leads to unique locations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBG-2)</sup><sup>[[122]](#cite_note-SRC-122)</sup><sup>[[123]](#cite_note-SSC-123)</sup> There were available tapestries with themes that matched the cards in this deck.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-TAP10-124)</sup><sup>[[125]](#cite_note-TAP11-125)</sup>
