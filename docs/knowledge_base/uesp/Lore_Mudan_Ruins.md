[3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) The Mudan Ruins (also known as the **Mudan Grotto**) are a sunken [Dwarven ruin](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) amidst the [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), within the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).

## History
Mudan was once the site of a [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) settlement, but it was later abandoned, much like other settlements. The Mudan Ruins were more on the surface than they were sometime later. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Mudan Ruins were explored by the former grandmaster of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), [Rythe Verano](Lore_Rythe_Verano.md), on his quest for the Simulacrum Rubric. Verano was intercepted by [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md) and her cohort, [Boldekh](Lore_Boldekh.md), a powerful [Warden](Lore_Warden.md). Their conflict was interrupted by a swarm of [Dwarven Animunculi](Lore_Dwarven_Automatons.md), later involving a [Dwarven Colossus](Lore_Dwarven_Colossus.md). Verano did not survive the Colossus' attack, but both Virian and Boldekh left unscathed, as Naryu began her hunt against the Seven Secretives.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NJVB-1)</sup>

Many years later, during [the Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) outbreak in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the Mudan Ruins were flooded and became colloquially known as the Mudan Grotto. The local water fauna largely inhabited it. An adventurer had attempted to loot the Mudan Grotto but had died in vain. Deep in the ruins were more Dwarven Animunculi and the artifact known as the [Dragonbone Mail](Lore_Dragonbone_Mail.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWMudan-2)</sup> a rare cuirass blessed by the Imperial Battlemage, [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup>
