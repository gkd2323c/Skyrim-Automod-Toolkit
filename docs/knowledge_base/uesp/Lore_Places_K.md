## Ka Po' Tun
[Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(place).md) (meaning **Tiger-Dragon's Empire**), originally called **Po Tun**, is a nation of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). It is inhabited by a race of cat-folk also called the [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md). They are ruled by [Tosh Raka](Lore_Tosh_Raka.md), the divine Tiger-Dragon who bears similarities to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). Ka Po' Tun has become a very great empire, stronger than [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(place).md) in all but naval battle. When the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) attempted to enslave the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), the black dragons fled to Po' Tun. A great war was waged, which left both the cat-folk and the snakes weak, and resulted in the death of the dragons. Since that time, the cat-folk have tried to become the dragons. Tosh Raka was the first to succeed, and renamed the nation to Ka Po' Tun. Ka Po' Tun plans to kill the Tsaesci and then invade [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

## Kagrenzel
[Kagrenzel](Lore_Kagrenzel.md) (translated as *"Music City"* in [Dwemeris](Lore_Dwemeris.md)) is a small [dwarven ruin](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) found in the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It is found in the region of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md). Kagrenzel was a holdout shrouded in mystery, a *"death trap"* reminiscent of Yokudan tombs; the journal of a seeker after a Dwemer artifact opined that Kagrenzel's *"entire purpose is to kill those who would search it."* A vast pitfall leads into a giant chasm, which flows into **Stony Creek Cave**. At one point, the stronghold contained the left side of the [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md).

## Kairou
[Kairou](Lore_Kairou.md) is a region on the western coast of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region. It is bordered by [Pothago](Lore_Pothago.md) to the north, [Myrkwasa](Lore_Myrkwasa_(fiefdom).md) and the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) to the east, and [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md) to the south. Its main city is the city of Kairou.

On the 20th of [Evening Star](Lore_Evening_Star.md), Kairou celebrates a special event called [Chil'a](Lore_Chil%27a.md). It is the blessing of the new year in the region and is both a sacred day and a festival. The archpriest and the baroness each consecrate the ashes of the old year in a solemn ceremony, then street parades, balls, and tournaments conclude the event.

The regional deity of Kairou is [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), and the dominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline of the region is the [Selenu](Lore_Selenu.md). After the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Kairou became a part of the kingdom of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md).

## Kamal
[Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(place).md) (meaning **Snow Hell**) is a nation in northern [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). It is inhabited by armies of snow demons (also known as [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md)), who thaw out every summer and attempt to invade [Tang Mo](Lore_Tang_Mo_(place).md), but are always successfully repelled by the [monkey-folk](Lore_Tang_Mo_(race).md). [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md), a king among the demons, once invaded [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), but was defeated circa [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), when [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), warrior-poet god of the Dunmer, flooded the entirety of Morrowind, successfully drowning the invasion.

Kamal is known to be icy and snow-covered, with deep snows and northern forests.

## Kambria
[Kambria](Lore_Kambria.md) was a [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) march in central [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) located in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region. It shared borders with the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) to the north, [Koegria](Lore_Koegria.md) to the east, [Bhoriane](Lore_Bhoriane.md) to the south, and [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md) to the west. The provincial seat was the city of Kambria. The province rose from lowlands in the south to a windswept plateau in the north, where towns were situated in valleys for shelter. The tops of the hills in the area were once covered with forts, but these were demolished by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) in order to reduce potential points of resistance. The regional deity was [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), and the dominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline in the region was [Vraseth](Lore_Vraseth.md).

The ruler of Kambria was allied with the Baron of Dwynnen during the war against the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) and helped turn the tide of the war against the Usurper. The city of Kambria is known to possess several libraries, and has been described as picturesque.

## Kanesh
[Kanesh](Lore_Kanesh.md) is a large island in the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) archipelago, between the [Azurian Sea](Lore_Azurian_Sea.md) and the [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md), and the closest of the group to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Located due east of [Akos Kasaz](Lore_Akos_Kasaz.md), it is a harsh volcanic island. To its south are the islands of [Irranhu](Lore_Irranhu.md) and [Samara](Lore_Samara.md).

In the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there was a settlement named Lumut on the east side of the island.

## Kardesh

## Karndar Watch
[Karndar Watch](Lore_Karndar_Watch.md) (or **Kardnar Watch**) is an ancient village located in the Diren Valley region of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), within the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Karndar Watch is a small but historic settlement, as it is the origin of the Laws of Karndar Watch, created in [ME 1180](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#ME_1180). The laws are ancient and very comparable to the ones established by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) in the [northeast](Lore_Morrowind.md).

By the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Karndar Watch had become a derelict ruined settlement. Though one of the main roads on the island led up to it, it received little contact from the populace outside of worshipers who'd set up shrines at the ruins and the occasional traveling merchant. The grounds were overrun by [imps](Lore_Imp.md) and an [indrik](Lore_Indrik.md) inhabited the area. The settlement was still marked on maps during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md), but it is unknown if it was re-inhabited by that time.

By the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Karndar Watch was once again an active settlement, being ruled by Lady Zenosara. It had a rivalry with [Silsailen Point](Lore_Silsailen_Point.md). It was neighbored by several settlements, one of them being [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) in the west, and was located across the sea from [Belport Run](Lore_Belport_Run.md).

## Karnwasten
[Karnwasten](Lore_Karnwasten.md) is a large natural cove on the northern face of [Eton Nir](Lore_Eton_Nir.md), the largest mountain in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). For the longest time, this hidden port has served as a central hub for smuggling on the archipelago's [main island](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). Many foreign ships come here since so few items from the mainland are allowed and nobles across the province pay good money for it. By the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there was a settlement present in the area of the same name. By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), it was known as **Karnwasten Moor**, but previously it was called **Karnstern Moor**.

## Karthald
[Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md) is a historical [hold](Lore_Hold.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) that existed during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). It was established as a defensive border against [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) after being carved from its northern territory of the upper Karth Valley named **Reachwater Pass**, along with parts of the [Karth River Forest](Lore_Karth_River_Forest.md). The Reach was at the time an independent [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) kingdom ruled by the Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md). Karthald was established in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577) by the kingdom of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md), although [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) clans had inhabited the land for generations alongside the Reachfolk. The fortified village of [Karthwatch](Lore_Karthwatch.md) was established in as the new hold's capital. Karthald's borders also encompassed the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) stronghold of [Mor Khazgur](Lore_Mor_Khazgur.md), though the Orcs were keen to insist that Mor Khazgur was a sovereign outpost of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md). While the roads of Karthald were safe enough for travelers, Karthwatch was a spartan settlement with little hospitality to offer.

Karthald ceased to exist as a separate hold after the destruction of Karthwatch at the hands of the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) circa 2E 582. In retaliation for the prior massacre of a local Reachfolk clan by the Nords and the alleged kidnapping of the crucial clan heir, [Bjora](Lore_Bjora.md), they launched an assault. The Reachmen created a [Harrowstorm](Lore_Harrowstorm.md) above the town in order to drain the life energy of its inhabitants, using their [souls](Lore_Soul.md) to fill ancient reliquaries containing the mortal remains of members of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). Many of the residents of Karthwatch were transformed into [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) monstrosities known as [Harrowfiends](Lore_Harrowfiend.md), including Jarl Olfwenn herself. Others were taken hostage by the witches, although a rescue was mounted by the town's survivors. The sole survivor of the jarl's court was the court wizard, Magister Fridelja, who went on to represent the hold politically in the turmoil that followed. She and the other survivors sought refuge in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). However, the rest of the hold was similarly laid to waste, its people left dead or scattered. With the Nords driven out and the hold's capital destroyed, Karthald was no more, allowing the territory to be reclaimed by the Reach.

In [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852), the Reach was conquered by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and annexed by Skyrim as a Nord hold. By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), no traces of Karthald's time as a hold remained, and a ruined fortress called [Broken Tower Redoubt](Lore_Broken_Tower_Redoubt.md) sat on the approximate site of Karthwatch.

## Karthmad
[Karthmad](Lore_Karthmad.md) is a mountain in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) that stands at the head of the valley of the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md). It is better known as the site of the city of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), or [Nchuand-Zel](Lore_Nchuand-Zel.md) as it was called by its original builders, the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). The stronghold of Nchuand-Zel was carved by the Dwemer into the subterranean heart of Karthmad and then expanded upwards until its structures penetrated above the surface.

## Karth River
The [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md) is a large and important river that starts in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and empties into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) near the port-city of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). The Karth is well known for its violent rapids and the dangerous terrain, such as jutted sharp stones and the crashing torrents on the river valley. But despite this, the Karth River provides a wealth of resource and life to the local people, whether they are [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) or [Nords of Karth](Lore_Nord.md). The people who live also understand that the Karth River can take as much as it gives. The river has been known to sweep people away and destroy everything from food stores to entire camps. The Karth demands respect above all else.

The river is known to contain a rare and shimmering rock called the [Bright River-Shale](Lore_Shale.md) which is used by the Reachfolk in a ritual to commune with the Spirits of the Reach. The river's water is sacred to many Reachfolk clans, who believe it possesses magical and cleansing properties.

## Karthwasten
[Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md) (also called **Karthwasten Hall**) is a long-standing settlement located in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) near the banks of the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md), from which it gets its name.

Control of the settlement has changed hands between [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) multiple times, usually due to violent conflict. The town was built around the ancient Sanuarach mine, which the Nords prized greatly for its silver. The Reachmen of the Karth, by contrast, traditionally held that mining was an affront to nature, and so would often ignore or even destroy the mine during their tenure.

Eventually, the fighting between the two factions would die down. By the Fourth Era, Karthwasten was known as a mining town with a mixed population. Despite being owned by a Reachman at this time, the town not only mined the Sanuarach, but the newer Fenn's Gulch Mine near the settlement as well.

## Karthwatch
[Karthwatch](Lore_Karthwatch.md) was a fortified [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) town and the capital of [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md), a historical [hold](Lore_Hold.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) that existed during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).Karthwatch was established to defend the border against [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) after Karthald was carved from its northern territory. The Reach was at the time an independent [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) kingdom ruled by Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the so-called Despot of Markarth. Karthald was established in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577) by the kingdom of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md), although [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) clans had inhabited the land for generations. The location for Karthwatch was decided due to its proximity to the ancient Nordic ruins of [Bleakridge Barrow](Lore_Bleakridge_Barrow.md), which was thought would make a good defensible position in the event of a Reachman invasion. Karthwatch was considered a spartan settlement with little hospitality to offer for travelers. The banner of Karthwatch featured a stylized tower symbol on a steel blue background.

Karthwatch was destroyed circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) and their [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) allies. The Reachmen created a [Harrowstorm](Lore_Harrowstorm.md) above the town in order to drain the life energy of its inhabitants, using their [souls](Lore_Soul.md) to fill ancient reliquaries containing the mortal remains of members of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). Many of the residents of Karthwatch were transformed into [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) monstrosities known as [Harrowfiends](Lore_Harrowfiend.md), including Jarl Olfwenn herself. Others were taken hostage by the witches, although a rescue was mounted by the town's survivors. The sole survivor of the jarl's court was the court wizard, Magister Fridelja, who went on to represent the hold politically in the turmoil that followed.

She and the other survivors sought refuge in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). However, the rest of the hold was similarly laid to waste, its people left dead or scattered. With the Nords driven out and the hold's capital destroyed, Karthald was no more, allowing the territory to be reclaimed by the Reach. The settlement would eventually transform into the city of **[Snowhawk](Lore_Snowhawk.md)** by the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), with Karthald's former location on the plateau later becoming the site of a fort.

By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), a ruined fortress called **Broken Tower Redoubt** sat on the approximate former site of Karthwatch. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Sibyl of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) had passed away, and a new one was needed for the [House of Dibella](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md) in the city. The priesthood had discovered the young girl, [Fjotra](Lore_Fjotra.md) in Karthwasten, but a group of Reachmen known as the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md) had taken her to Broken Tower Redoubt, where a shrine to [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) was located. From there, the young girl was freed by [someone](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) sent by the Temple of Dibella, and taken back to Markarth, where she would serve as the Dibellan Sybil of Markarth.

## Kemel-Ze
[Kemel-Ze](Lore_Kemel-Ze.md) (translated as *"Cliff City"* from [Dwemeris](Lore_Dwemeris.md)) is a [Dwarven ruin](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) found along the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) in the coastlines of the [Fungal Lowlands](Lore_Fungal_Lowlands.md). The village of Marog is found nearby.

In the First Era, before the Tribunal ascended to the godhood, Nerevar Indoril brokered an alliance with the Dwemer. Dumac Dwarfking gifted Sotha Sil a wing of Kemel-Ze as a sign of that alliance. Sotha Sil used this facility to do preliminary work on the Clockwork City. There he studied the flora and fauna of Nirn to learn how to replicate their functions with metal and energy. He built an early factotum, he named Protus.

He used Mnemonic Fragments to store some of his memories. He sought to use the memories to create an artificial animus. But all he was able to achieve was the appearance of a fully developed soul,a convincing lie—artificial projection of himself. In this laboratory, he also created the [Clockwork Probabilis](Lore_Clockwork_Probabilis.md) to manipulate the Intrinsic forces that govern events on Nirn. Some of those forces are constant—like the force which compels objects to fall to the ground, while others defy prediction and categorization. The Clockwork Probabilis is a prototype, a precursor to the Throne Aligned, one of Sotha Sil's great works.

It was abandoned in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) when its original [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) inhabitants mysteriously disappeared. Sotha Sil's wing was also eventually abandoned as his attention shifted to other pursuits. Nonetheless, it remained a sacred site for his followers. When he moved to the Clockwork City, Sotha Sil put Protus in charge of overseeing the laboratory and his creations.

A human [Marukhati](Lore_Marukhati_Selective.md) named Xal visited the ruin in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

## Khenarthi's Roost
[Khenarthi's Roost](Lore_Khenarthi%27s_Roost.md), the ** Isle of Three Temples**, is a small island off the coast of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) inhabited by both [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) and [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), named for the Khajiiti goddess [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md). The island is dotted with [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md) plantations and is a location of both active Khajiiti worship and Maormer magic, as the Khajiit signed a peace treaty with the Maormer which allowed them to settle on the island. The port city of [Mistral](Lore_Mistral.md) functions as the capital of the island and the center of commerce. Khenarthi's Roost once contained the ancient Kingdom of **Khenarthia**, one of the original sixteen kingdoms of Elsweyr, known for their sugar production and expert fishermen.

## Khuul
[Khuul](Lore_Khuul.md) was a small fishing village in the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), on the island's northern coast. Aside from a single [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) -style shell-house, which served as a Tradehouse, the buildings were all rustic huts made of logs, rough planks, and thatch. There was a boat to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) in the village if one had any reason to go.

The only other legitimate businesses near Khuul were two egg mines–the Panud Egg Mine, just outside town, and the Asha-Ahhe Egg Mine, far to the southwest. There was also a [silt strider](Lore_Silt_Strider.md) port just south of town, by the Panud Egg Mine.

The date of Khuul's founding is uncertain. While an area known as Khuul was a stop for [Silt Strider](Lore_Silt_Strider.md) caravaneers around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), there was no settlement there besides the nearby [Urshilaku Camp](Lore_Urshilaku_Camp.md). Khuul had always been rumored to be a smuggling town, but the residents never confirmed nor denied this. They were suspicious of strangers asking a lot of questions and didn't like people poking around.

## Kilkreath Temple
[Kilkreath Temple](Lore_Kilkreath_Temple.md) is an old nordic ruin found on the mountains of [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The temple was once a place of worship for the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Infinite Energies, [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), and in its hayday, it attracted many pilgrims from all corners of the world and its activity was openly known. Travelers that pass by the temple are greeted with warm hospitality and even resources like bedding. At some point, the temple became inactive and were later known as the **Kilkreath Ruins**. The temple is marked by the **Statue of Meridia**, which looms over the great expanse of northwest Skyrim.

## King's Haven Pass
[King's Haven Pass](Lore_King%27s_Haven_Pass.md) (or simply **King's Haven**) is a valley path that runs through the northern mountain, [Eton Nir](Lore_Eton_Nir.md), found in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). This mountain pass was once a prominent trade route that connected the cities of [Corgrad](Lore_Corgrad.md) and [Shimmerene](Lore_Shimmerene.md), both found on two sides of the island, but it was later abandoned and contain the region's population of [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), who escaped to these highlands following the Elven incursion of the islands. The village of **Kings Haven** (also spelled **Kingshaven**) was active throughout the reign of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).

## King's Guard
[King's Guard](Lore_King%27s_Guard.md) (or **Kings Guard**) is a region of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in northern [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), to the north of [Cambray Hills](Lore_Cambray_Hills.md). Due to the trade that regularly passes through the area, the region is a melting pot of the dress, food, and culture of the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) city-states.

The region was named after the mountain range separating Glenumbra, [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) and [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md). It is home to many crypts and burial grounds, such as the great cemetery of [Cath Bedraud](Lore_Cath_Bedraud.md) and [Balefire Island](Lore_Balefire_Island.md). The only settlement of note is the mining town of Crosswych. The mountain pass linking Glenumbra with Stormhaven is also located here. During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), a town called Kings Guard was present in the region. It was ruled by Baron Rodoryan, and had a rivalry with the towns of [Ilessen Hills](Lore_Ilessen_Hills.md) and [Meir Darguard](Lore_Meir_Darguard.md). It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Eagle Brook](Lore_Eagle_Brook.md) in the north, [Moonguard](Lore_Moonguard.md) in the southwest, and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), in the southeast.

The Red Rook Bandits, a large bandit clan from the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) period, originated in the mountain passes of the region. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the region suffered heavily from the necromantic activities of the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md), requiring military intervention from the [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md).

## Knifepoint Hollow
[Knifepoint Hollow](Lore_Knifepoint_Hollow.md) is a subterranean ruin located northwest of [New Sheoth](Lore_New_Sheoth.md), on the foothills of northern [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). It was once the site of the **Great Library of Jyggalag**, which once contained all the knowledge in creation prior to [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md)'s transformation into [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). When Sheogorath discovered the existence of the library, he had it burned, and converted it into a prison for [Dyus](Lore_Dyus.md), the keeper of the great library.

Like all ancient ruins found in the Isles, it is theorized that the complex at Knifepoint is a relic of the [Predecessors](Lore_Predecessors.md), the mortal inhabitants of the realm whose civilization was wiped out in a previous [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md). By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), [root tunnels](Lore_Root.md) had broken through into its ancient subterranean halls, and the only means of accessing the ruins from the surface was by means of a root tunnel.

The provincial seat was the city of Koegria. The dominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline was [Lyrezi](Lore_Lyrezi.md), and the regional deity was [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md).

## Kragenmoor
[Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md) is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), located in the ash wastes of [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md). The city is the great nexus of the region's farming industry and it has historically been occupied by the Great [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md). While some accounts described Kragenmoor as wretched and downtrodden, others such as the one from [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) noted the miles of [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) plantations that stretched across the fertile valley. For a time in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Kragenmoor was the capital of House Dres and was the home of the Dres Grandmaster.

## Kurallian Mountains
The [Kurallian Mountains](Lore_Kurallian_Mountains.md) are a mountain range in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), specifically in the region of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md). ## Kvatch
[Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) is a Colovian city in the hinterland of the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), located between [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md). It is built atop a large bluff, raising the city above the surrounding forests. Its coat of arms is a black [Dire Wolf](Lore_Dire_Wolf.md) on a grey background. The city is home to an arena, the imposing Kvatch Castle, and the Great Chapel of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). County Kvatch extends far to the northwest, up to the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md) at the border of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). The people of the city wear turban-like helmets with chainmail.

## Kyne's Aegis
[Kyne's Aegis](Lore_Kyne%27s_Aegis.md) is an island found in the icy waters of the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It is the coastal bulwark of [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), since the seas are treacherous and riddled with ship graveyards, any that tries to enter the mainland from the west must go through Kyne's Aegis as a checkpoint. On the island's southern coast lies the large fortified-village of **Kyne's Perch**, while the hills on the north contains an old ruin from the days of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md). The hills beyond Kyne's Perch is known as **the Span**, which consists of several mining outposts that date back to the days of the island's [gold](Lore_Gold.md) rush.

## Kynesgrove
[Kynesgrove](Lore_Kynesgrove.md), a simplification of the original **Kyne's Grove**, and also known as **Dragonwood** is a small village south of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). The village survives off a [Malachite](Lore_Malachite.md) mine and some farming. Although there are an abundant number of trees in the area, they are considered sacred to [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) and are not allowed to be harmed.

The village also has an arena that is among the oldest ones still in service. Legend says the great First Era warrior [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md) was a regular fighter there before he went on to found the Imperial City arena.
