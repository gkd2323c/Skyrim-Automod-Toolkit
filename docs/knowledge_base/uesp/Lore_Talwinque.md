[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/OB-place-Talwinque.jpg/200px-OB-place-Talwinque.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Talwinque.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Talwinque.jpg) Talwinque circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/ON-place-Eaglerock_Ruins.jpg/200px-ON-place-Eaglerock_Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Eaglerock_Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Eaglerock_Ruins.jpg) Talwinque circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Talwinque, known in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) as **Eaglerock Ruins**, is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located in the Colovian Highlands, northeast of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and northwest of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBMap-1)</sup> The ruin is noted for the large statues found inside its halls, as opposed to on the surface like many other ruins. Its two subsections of *Talwinque Ceysel* and *Aransel* translate to "Hall of Shadow" and "Hall of the King" respectively in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).

Following the fall of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) at the end of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the clan of Talwinque escaped largely intact as they fled south beneath the canopy of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). They are presumed to have settled there and adopted the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) under the terms of the native [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), eventually being absorbed into their local population.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup> The burgeoning [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) toppled the remaining Ayleid cities of Cyrodiil one by one, and Talwinque subsequently fell into ruin.

Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), as part of the plot by the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md) to take over western Cyrodiil and return the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md) to power, a jungle called Dawnwood suddenly appeared overnight and covered parts of the Colovian Highlands and the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md). The overgrown areas included the site of Talwinque.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONEaglerock Ruins-3)</sup>

By the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the halls of the ruin had become home to a group of bandits. Many skeletons were found inside, although the origin of these unfortunate individuals is unknown.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup>
