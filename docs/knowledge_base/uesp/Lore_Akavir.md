[![](https://images.uesp.net/c/c3/MW-banner-Akaviri.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Akaviri.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Akaviri.jpg) An Akavir-inspired Banner Akavir, also known as **Dragon Land**, is a continental landmass east of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and home to the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) races. The two continents have a history of animosity towards each other, with Akavir invading Tamriel several times in the past and [Tamriel invading Akavir](Lore_Invasion_of_Akavir.md) at least once.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RDAI-1)</sup> Much of what is known about it in Tamriel is acknowledged to be incomplete or inaccurate.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup>

## Inhabitants
There are four major nations on Akavir, each with its own race.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MA-3)</sup> Of special note are the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md), with one source claiming that they are one of the races of Men, while another claims that the Men that did live in Akavir were all "eaten"<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Eaten)</sup> long ago by the Tsaesci.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAA-4)</sup> No [mer](Lore_Mer.md) are known to have ever lived there.

### Dragons
[Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) (also known as **drakes**, **wyrms** or **worms**, **dov** or **dovah** in [their native language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), **drah-gkon** or **dov-rha** to the ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), or **Aka** in [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md)) are a rarely-seen [race](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Race) of large [reptilian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile) beasts of [Aedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) nature. They are rumored to be from Akavir (which literally means "Dragon Land"), though there are ancient tales of Dragons also coming from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). They are large, scaled creatures, easily several times larger than a human or elf. They have long, slender extremities, with thin bat-like wings and ridges of spikes along their back. Dragons have three sharp talons and one vestigial digit known as a dewclaw on each of their feet, as evidenced by the way their [alphabet](Lore_Dragon_Alphabet.md) is written. There are several varieties of Dragon that come in a range of colors.

They are regularly said to breathe fire, but more accurately, they speak it. They speak words in their language a certain way, known as a [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) or "Shout", to cause many effects, including elemental breaths such as fire or frost.

Dragons are well-known for their affinity for [magic](Lore_Magic.md). It is unknown how they possess the ability to speak and fly despite their lack of lips or the fact that their wings are not naturally strong enough to support such heavy torsos. As the immortal children of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), they are specially attuned to the flow of time, and they feel an innate urge to dominate others that is near-impossible to overcome. Some sources suggest both dragons and [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) are fragments of Akatosh's soul.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Dragon.md).

### Kamal
The [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) (or **Kamali**) are a race of "snow demons" who live in Akavir. The word means "Snow Hell". According to *[Mysterious Akavir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysterious_Akavir)*, the snow demons spend the winter frozen solid, but, when summer comes, they thaw out and attack [Tang Mo](Lore_Tang_Mo_(place).md), always unsuccessfully. In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), the Kamal invaded [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) under their king, [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal), reportedly searching for a person or object they called the "[Ordained Receptacle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordained_Receptacle)". The Kamal fleet bypassed [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) entirely and landed near [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) in Eastern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), catching the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) off-guard and sacking the city. Oddly, the Kamal army then headed south but ignored [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), marching into the [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls) region of Morrowind. There, on a beach just to the west of [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart) that would later be named [Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec%27s_Antlers), they were surrounded by a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) army led by [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) and a Nord army led by Prince [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md). The Kamal fought ferociously, hoping to last long enough for their fleet to arrive and evacuate them, but, at the last moment, a phalanx of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) joined the fray. With the aid of a tidal wave summoned by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md), the Kamal were obliterated by the three unlikely allies.

This was not the last Tamriel would see of the Kamal, however. Sometime before [2E 812](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_812), remnants of the Kamal invasion force who had somehow survived the slaughter at Vivec's Antlers resurfaced in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), seizing the Ruby Throne from the successors of the warlord [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(warlord).md). The Kamal were soon joined by the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), who had previously fled to [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) to avoid Attrebus' persecution. The Kamal and Rimmen Akaviri attempted to rebuild the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), but the effort was doomed to failure.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Kamal_(race).md).

### Ka Po' Tun
The [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md) (or **tiger-dragons**) are a race of [tiger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tiger) -like cat-folk who live on the continent of Akavir. It is unknown to what extent they are similar or related to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Although all of the races have fought each other at various times, the Ka Po' Tun consider the Tsaesci to be their greatest enemies. They have recently allied themselves with the Tang Mo.

The leader of the Ka Po' Tun is [Tosh Raka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tosh_Raka). He is rumored to be a divine being who was the first of his race to succeed at becoming a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md). Although some believe the story is metaphorical, *[Mysterious Akavir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysterious_Akavir)* claims that the change was indeed physical and that Tosh Raka has all the characteristics of a dragon, with a tiger-like coloring. Under the rulership of Tosh Raka, the former Po Tun were renamed to Ka Po' Tun and became a rival to the supremacy of Tsaesci. After the ultimate defeat of the Tsaesci, they plan to invade [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md).

### Tang Mo
The [Tang Mo](Lore_Tang_Mo_(race).md) are a race described as a generous and kind [monkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Monkey) -folk. Tang Mo translates as "the [Thousand Monkey Isles](Lore_Tang_Mo_(place).md)", which refers to the area where they live. There are various breeds of Tang Mo. They are very brave and simple, and many are also believed to be insane. They can raise armies when they must, for all of the other [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) nations have, at one time or another, tried to enslave them. So far the Tang Mo have always succeeded in defending their lands. Recently they have shrewdly allied themselves with their former enemies, the [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Tang_Mo_(race).md).

### Tsaesci
The [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) also known as the [serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snake) -folk or snake men, are a race originating from the continent of Akavir, east of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). While the racial status of the Tsaesci is ambiguous, with some sources claiming them as a [human](Lore_Man.md) race while others claim them as a race of [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md), all known encounters with Tsaesci show they are at least a race of humanoids, with some looking indistinguishable from humans. However, the possibility of Tsaesci being shapeshifters further increases the ambiguity of their status.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md).

### Other
Early documented encounters between the Akaviri and the earliest inhabitants of Tamriel suggest that there may be Akaviri people with rat-like features, and others who are canine in appearance. This suggests Akaviri cultures that have yet to be discovered and perhaps extinct.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/SR-book-Sky_Haven_Temple.jpg/200px-SR-book-Sky_Haven_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Sky_Haven_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Sky_Haven_Temple.jpg) An Akaviri map of Skyrim highlighting Sky Haven Temple Most Akaviri history is only known so far as it affects Tamriel, which means it is a story of conflict and violence, betrayal and intrigue. During time periods where they show no interest in Tamriel, they are thought to be fighting among themselves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MA-3)</sup>

The first appearances of the Akaviri in Tamriel are that of pirates which are now assumed to have been scouts for eventual invasions on the continent.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> They held a hostile presence in High Rock's First Era, where pirates and raiders established themselves to the islands to the west of Wayrest around [1E 800](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_800) and were counted as enemies to the Bretons at the time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WJOTB-5)</sup> Perhaps one of the earliest narratives concerning Akavir is [The Story of Lyrisius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Story_of_Lyrisius), in which the eponymous General had his own army destroyed before going on to destroy an Akaviri slavetrader army through other means.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSOL-6)</sup> Thus, even the oldest tales from Akavir are about war.

Legions of Akaviri raiders invaded northern Tamriel around [1E 2700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2700),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOTD-7)</sup> and were among the reasons the population of Wayrest moved into the Gardner's walls for protection in [1E 2702](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2702).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WJOTB-5)</sup> High Rock was weakened by the Akaviri invasion, with the population of the Iliac Bay being halved a third time. It is said that if Daggerfall had not been hardened and fortified through wars in its first thousand years of existence, the Iliac Bay would have been Akavirian in the present day.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AHOD-8)</sup>

The most famous and important interaction between the two continents concerns the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) of Tamrielic emperors and their successors, the [Akaviri Potentates](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md). In [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-2920V6-9)</sup> Tsaesci forces of Akavir invaded Tamriel and were decisively defeated at [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md) by the army of [Reman I](Lore_Reman.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> With the Akaviri's number and their tactics boosting his ranks, Reman continued on to pacify the rest of Tamriel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOTD-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE1ECY-10)</sup> In an effort to make Cyrodiil a cosmopolitan land, Reman I blended elements of Akaviri culture with that of High Rock, Colovia, and Nibeney into a common whole.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3C-11)</sup> As [Reman](Lore_Reman.md)'s empire was also under threat from the elves, he agreed to allow the survivors of the disaster to remain in Tamriel and strengthen his army.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> They eventually rose to positions of power and in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the last year of the First Era, [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie) brought about the deaths of [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III) and his heir [Prince Juilek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Juilek), then took the throne as Potentate.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-2920V8-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-2920V12-13)</sup> After his assassination by the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) in [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324), the empire passed to his son [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak), who was in turn assassinated along with his heirs in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3C-11)</sup>

A second, short-lived Akaviri invasion of Tamriel occurred in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572). An army of Kamal led by [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal) landed in northeast [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and besieged [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) before marching south towards [Riften](Lore_Riften.md). Upon learning that Riften was defended by forces under the command of [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) and [Wulfharth the Ash-King](Lore_Wulfharth.md), Ada'Soom decided to bypass that city and attack [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) instead, unwittingly leading his army into a trap. A Dunmer army led by [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) halted the Kamal advance, and Jorunn's pursuing Nord army cut off their retreat. With the timely aid of a phalanx of Argonian troops, the combined Tamrielic force drove the Akaviri invaders into the sea to drown.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Jt SK-14)</sup> According to another source, the Akaviri army was destroyed not in a battle, but when [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md) intervened and flooded the land.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Vo F-15)</sup> Regardless of how the Kamal were defeated, it is noted that some of their number survived and later resurfaced in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where they seized control of the Imperial Throne from the successors of the warlord [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(warlord).md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE1-E-16)</sup>

Centuries later, Tamriel would finally retaliate against Akavir. At the height of his power in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) and after twenty years of strategic preparation, [Uriel Septim V](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_V) launched an invasion of the continent and captured two cities, which he dubbed [Septimia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septimia) and [Ionith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ionith).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RDAI-1)</sup> The enterprise ultimately came to utter ruin at the [Battle of Ionith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Ionith) in [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RDAI-1)</sup> Since then, the two continents have had little or no contact. The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) is thought to have gone on an expedition to Akavir, but, as of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), had not been heard from since.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OBRumors-17)</sup>

Around the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), an enigmatic individual known as [Mohi-Titona](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mohi-Titona) was described as the supposed Queen of Akavir, although it is unknown whether or not she is a real figure.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

## Influences on Tamriel

### Warriorhood
The martial ability of the Akaviri soldiers had a strong influence on the military hierarchies of Tamriel. The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) benefited from the superior organization and tactical knowledge of the Akaviri, and the political and legal systems of the Empire were both improved as well.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> The Akaviri Potentate's peculiar armorsmithing practices was said to have changed the Imperial legions forever.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONSS-19)</sup> It was an Akaviri, Dinieras-Ves, who first proposed what would become the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) to his kinsman, the Potentate Versidue-Shaie.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-HOTFG-20)</sup> The Empire in general benefited from the influence of Akavir.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup>

The Akaviri who served under Reman Cyrodiil were dubbed the Dragonguard, as the Dragonborn Emperor's new shock troops were renowned dragon-slayers.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TBOTD-21)</sup> They were masters of fire magic and alchemy.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-So AM-22)</sup> This Dragonguard was the direct predecessor of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), who adopted Akaviri style for their weaponry, armor and heraldry.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TBOTD-21)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-23)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE1ECY-10)</sup> Their headquarters, [Cloud Ruler Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloud_Ruler_Temple), is also built in the Akaviri style. The Red Dragons that have come to represent the Empire and the Imperial City were originally Akaviri war mounts.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE1ECY-10)</sup> A class of warriors known as [Dragonknights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonknights) emerged during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), similarly based on the martial traditions of the dispersed Akaviri Dragonguard.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> These warriors made use of unique magical abilities<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AFDOE-25)</sup> said to be based on the powers of dragons.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MBRP-26)</sup>

### Culture
Akaviri surnames are rare and prized possessions among the Cyrodilic citizenry of today, and there are trace facial features of the Akaviri in many distinguished Cyrodilic families. Some colonies of "true Akaviri", such as [Hakoshae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hakoshae), still exist in both the Empire and its border regions, but they are named so for their practices and customs, rather than for the purity of their blood.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE1ECY-10)</sup>

### Weaponry
As a result of the original Akaviri invasion and their subsequent induction into Tamrielic society, the traditional weaponry of the east has been gradually integrated into common weapon design across Tamriel. Among the arsenal of the invaders was the dai-katana, katana, wakizashi, and tanto.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MWMuzgub-27)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Akavir Notes-28)</sup>

The tanto is a short blade that [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) wizards like to use as a matrix for their [enchantments](Lore_Enchanting.md). Their mercenaries often carry enchanted tantos gifted by their mage-lord patrons. The wakizashi is another short blade, but unlike the common western Tamrielic double-edged longsword, it is single-edged and more refined. These swords are rare and very few blacksmiths can craft them. The katana is a highly prized weapon that is too expensive and sophisticated to be popular with the Imperial Legion and [hero-adventurers](Lore_Hero.md), but nobles, collectors, and swordmasters value these blades for their superior balance and effectiveness. The dai-katana, a two-handed longsword, is not common and they are generally unpopular. The throwing star is another example of Akaviri weaponry used in Tamriel.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MWMuzgub-27)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/LG-card-Summerset_Orrery_02.png/200px-LG-card-Summerset_Orrery_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-card-Summerset_Orrery_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-card-Summerset_Orrery_02.png) Pre-release map from *Legends* [1.](#note_Eaten) The meaning of term "eaten" as given from what is known of the land of Akavir is not clear. Although description of the "vampiric" Tsaesci might suggest literal meaning, it could be a metaphor for being subdued. In *[Mysterious Akavir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysterious_Akavir)* by anonymous writer, the Tsaesci are told to have "tried to eat all the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md). They managed to enslave the Red Dragons, but the black ones had fled to (then) Po Tun". Also, the *[Akaviri Diary Translation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akaviri_Diary_Translation)* of an Akaviri messenger states that during his journey to deliver orders to [Fort Pale Pass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Fort_Pale_Pass) he met another Akaviri messenger, stating that his leg had been badly hurt by wolves. As the Tsaesci are described as beings with humanoid upper body and snake lower bodies, to being entirely snake-like, this statement would suggest that men indeed continued to live in Akavir, even if they were enslaved by the Tsaesci. The Akaviri-made [Alduin's Wall](Lore_Alduin%27s_Wall.md) shows members of the Dragonguard appearing to be human-looking. - A fan rendition of the continent of Akavir was originally depicted in pre-release images of the [Summerset Orrery](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Summerset Orrery) card in [*The Elder Scrolls: Legends*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends). However, this artwork never made it into the final game.
