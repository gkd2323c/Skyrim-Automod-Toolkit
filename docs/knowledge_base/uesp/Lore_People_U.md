#### Warlord Uchuiran (fl. 2E 582)
[Uchuiran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uchuiran) was one of the thirteen [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachman.md) warlords of the [Winterborn Clan](Lore_Winterborn.md). He lived during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) period, around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), and fought against the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) for his ancestral homeland, [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md).

#### Admiral Uhlchesis (fl. before 2E 582)
**Admiral** [Uhlchesis](Lore_Uhlchesis.md) was a [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) cultist of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) and a famous sailor known as the scourge of the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md).

#### Warchief Ulaqth (fl. 1E 2920)
**Warchief ** [Ulaqth](Lore_Ulaqth.md) was an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) ruler of the city of [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md) during the reign of [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Reman Cyrodiil III](Lore_Reman_III.md), in the last years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He was known for his distrust towards the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), due to the slave-raids on his lands organized by the Duke of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).

He took part in the [Four Score War](Lore_Four_Score_War.md) in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920) and commanded the right flank of the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) army during the Battle of Bodrum on the 20th of First Seed, when Queen Naghea of [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) led the left flank and Lord Storig of [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md) charged with the vanguard. During that battle, the forces led by the member of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) ambushed the Cyrodilic army and almost completely destroyed it. Ulaqth's fate is unknown, and whether or not he perished in this battle is unclear.

#### Uldor (fl. before 2E 582)
[Uldor](Lore_Uldor.md) was a powerful [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) wizard who used to rule [Khenarthi's Roost](Lore_Khenarthi%27s_Roost.md) with the reign of terror. He learned how to separate his spirit from his body, even in death. He was able to possess both alive people and corpses. He was not limited to commanding one body and could "wear" hundreds of mindless forms at a time, even in his undeath.

#### Witch Ulecia (fl. before [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577))
[Ulecia](Lore_Ulecia.md) was a legendary figure from the oral tradition of the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md). She was a [witch](Lore_Witches.md) of [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) origins known for her remarkable prowess in [water magic](Lore_Water_Magic.md) and her ill-fated rivalry with her sister [Tancia](Lore_Tancia.md), an [air mage](Lore_Weather_Magic.md) of comparable power. Tancia and her sister were once revered as two of the greatest Reach witches, but their unquenchable desire for power ultimately drove them toward a perilous and destructive journey. The story of *The Sister Who Turned Her Skin Red,* named after Tancia's sister, served as a cautionary tale illustrating the dangers of an insatiable hunger for power.

#### Ulfgar the Unending (?b - [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427))
[Ulfgar the Unending](Lore_Ulfgar_the_Unending.md) (or, alternatively, **the Unyielding**) was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior from the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) or possibly even the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). By his own personal account he and four close companions fought alongside the Atmoran legend [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) as members of the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). Many centuries later he and his four companions embarked on a quest to find [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md). However, his companions [Nikulas](Lore_Nikulas.md), [Erlendr](Lore_Erlendr.md), and [Hunroor](Lore_Hunroor.md) were betrayed by the wizard [Grimkell](Lore_Grimkell.md), who turned them into stone pillars at Brodir Grove on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). They were avenged by Ulfgar, who killed the wizard. Five hundred years later, Ulfgar was somehow still alive, and still searching for Sovngarde. In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), he met the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), who agreed to help him find the Nordic paradise. Upon investigating, the Nerevarine informed the ancient Nord that the only way to reach Sovngarde is to die an honorable death in combat. Ulfgar challenged the Nerevarine, hoping to meet such a fate. The Nerevarine accepted his challenge, and the two engaged in mortal combat. In the end, a fourth pillar appeared at Brodir Grove, and Ulfgar met his old friends in Sovngarde. For more information, see the [lore article](Lore_Ulfgar_the_Unending.md).

#### Ulfgi Anvil-Hand (fl. Merethic Era)
[Ulfgi Anvil-Hand](Lore_Ulfgi_Anvil-Hand.md) was one of the greatest [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warriors among the participants of the [Battle of the Moesring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Moesring.md).

Ulfgi, together with [Heimdall the Frenzied](Lore_Heimdall_the_Frenzied.md), [Strom the White](Lore_Strom_the_White.md), and [Freida Oaken-Wand](Lore_Freida_Oaken-Wand.md), stood among the mightiest warriors of their group, yet all were defeated by the [Snow Prince](Lore_Snow_Prince.md), his [spear](Lore_Spear_of_the_Snow_Prince.md), and his mastery of ice and snow [magic](Lore_Magic.md). They fell in battle and lay dead at the foot of the Moesring Mountains. Though victorious, the Prince too met his end that day, slain by Finna, daughter of Jofrior, whom the elf had previously killed.

#### Jarl Ulfric Stormcloak (fl. c. 4E 161 - 201)
[Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) of the [Stormcloak Clan](Lore_Stormcloak_Clan.md) was the Jarl of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and the leader of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md). Ulfric first rose to prominence due to his involvement in the Markarth Incident, which began when he and his militia successfully retook the city. For retaking [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), Ulfric and his militia were initially permitted to worship [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) by Jarl [Hrolfdir](Lore_Hrolfdir.md) and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md); however, they were soon arrested to appease the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md). Following his release from prison, Ulfric and his militia known as the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) engaged in skirmishes with the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), although they failed to gain much support from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s populace. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Ulfric killed the High King of Skyrim, [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md), in a duel, beginning a full-scale civil war against the Empire. Ulfric was an incredibly divisive and controversial figure; while many of his supporters viewed him as a hero, many of his detractors viewed him as a usurper and opportunist. In battle, he was known to wield heirlooms of his clan, such as his sword, and a battleaxe known as Bjornblad.

#### Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen (fl. circa. 1E 2150)
**Archmage [Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md)** (also called **Ulfie** as a nickname by her ex-husband [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md)) was a gifted [witch](Lore_Witch.md), healer, and priestess of [mixed](Lore_Multiraciality.md) [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) and [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) heritage. She had a unique ability, thought to have been gifted from [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), which allowed her to see the flow of magicka on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). As she learned more about magic, her ability to see it is said to have granted her an unparalleled perspective of magecraft. This ability led her to the creatures she called [Luminaries of Magic](Lore_Luminaries_of_Magic.md), immortal beings of pure magic she likened to beams of sunlight from Magnus himself. Collaborating with these entities, she developed the "metamagical theoryset" of Scribing, the precursor to the modern [spellcrafting](Lore_Spellcrafting.md) system. Although she was famous in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), her admirers may not have realized that the stories about magical heroines such as the **Warrior-Witch of [Kyne's Aegis](Lore_Kyne%27s_Aegis.md)** or the **Clever-Woman of the [Druadach](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md)** were about one woman, and many of her greatest accomplishments remained secret until they were discovered by the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

#### Ulliceta gra-Kogg (fl. c. 2E 532 - 582)
[Ulliceta gra-Kogg](Lore_Ulliceta_gra-Kogg.md) was an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) mage that lived during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

Ulliceta was from the Kogg Clan of Orcs, but was later inducted into the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) around [2E 232](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_232). She was forced to abandon her previous life in favor of the Old Ways, which is the central focus of the Psijic Order. Ulliceta gra-Kogg would study at the Psijic Order for many years, gaining a reputation among her teachers, being called *Adma'na*, which is "poor listener" in [High Elvish](Lore_High_Elvish.md). Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Ulliceta had uncovered the Eye of Rememberance and decided to traverse the Traitor's Vault, which was formerly [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)'s laboratory during his time on [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md). Ulliceta and an [associate](Lore_Soulless_One.md) of the Psijic Order explored the ruins, uncovering Mannimarco and [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md)'s early history and rivalry. However, Ulliceta was caught by the Relicmaster, Glenadir. This resulted in Ulliceta's banishment from the Psijic Order. She would later relocate to the newly-built second [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), where she remained at the Temple of Ire.

Ulliceta gra-Kogg quickly joined the local [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and eventually became the Magister of the Mages Guild at [Nova Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md#Nova_Orsinium) as of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Ulliceta was at the forefront of defending the use of [Necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) against Voth Karlyss, the Magister of [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md). In the end, Arch-Mage [Hannibal Traven](Lore_Hannibal_Traven.md) banned guild mages from practicing the Black Arts at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). It was revealed that not only was Ulliceta an apologist of Necromancy, but a practitioner; when the [Order of the Lamp](Lore_Order_of_the_Lamp.md) attempted to apprehend her, she was able to escape their clutches. Ulliceta's argument in favor of Necromancy was documented in *[The Black Arts On Trial](Lore_The_Black_Arts_On_Trial.md)*, as Traven felt it was worthy of inclusion. Her ultimate fate is unknown.

#### Umaril the Unfeathered (?b - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril.md) (sometimes **Umar-Il**) remembered by his enemies as **Umaril the Accursed** and **Umaril Godsbane**, was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) sorcerer-king of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) and liege-lord of the other Ayleid sorcerer-kings, a right he was bestowed thanks to his incredible power and status as the most favored champion of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). He ruled from the throne room of the [Temple of the Ancestors](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md); as the holder of the Cyrodilic throne, he was known as the **Cyrodiil**. His control over the White-Gold Tower also earned him the appellation **Dread Castellan**. Umaril was of such might that he was said to be able to slay the greatest of warriors with a single blow. He had an appearance akin to a golden [Angel](Lore_Angel.md), which was represented by his helm's visage and his signature unfeathered wings. He wielded a golden-hued sword artifact that was known as the Unfeathered Greatsword, or more simply as Umaril's Sword. He was ultimately defeated by the [Divine Crusader](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) before his plans could come to fruition.

#### Umbacano (?b - 3E 433)
[Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) mage and suspected [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [Revivalist](Lore_Ayleid_Revivalists.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), he was working at the Yassu [glass](Lore_Malachite.md) mine in the dangerous [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), along with a fellow Altmer Ayleid enthusiast named Sorcalin. By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Umbacano had migrated to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), become considerably wealthy, and had begun employing adventurers to seek out Ayleid artifacts across [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for his private collection.

In 3E 433, Umbacano enlisted the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to search the province's Ayleid ruins for ten ancient statues known as the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md). He also tasked the Hero with recovering an ancient carving from the ruins of [Malada](Lore_Malada.md), as well as the [Crown](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md) of [Laloriaran Dynar](Lore_Laloriaran_Dynar.md), the king of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md). It is not known if the Hero recovered the crown or accepted rival collector [Herminia Cinna](Lore_Herminia_Cinna.md)'s proposal to trick Umbacano into accepting the crown of the rival city of [Lindai](Lore_Lindai.md).

What is known is that Umbacano took one of the crowns to the throne room of Nenalata and attempted to use it in order to restore Nenalata to its former glory. There are conflicting accounts of what happened next; some say that Umbacano used the crown of Nenalata in a ritual to have himself recognized as the new King of Nenalata, taking on a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) -like appearance as a consequence and resulting in his death at the hands of the Hero of Kvatch. Others say that he invoked the crown of Lindai and inadvertently killed himself, destroying the crown in the process. Regardless of the specifics, Umbacano is known to have died inside Nenalata.

#### The Underking (?b - [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417))
The [Underking](Lore_Underking.md) (sometimes **Under King**), or the **Evil One**, was a mysterious figure of myth and legend, known throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) (and by some accounts, even earlier). His legions of living and undead followers were called his Agents, and he was known to be opposed to [Mannimarco, the King of Worms](Lore_Mannimarco.md) and [his cult of necromancers](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md). The Underking was one of seven factions in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region that played a major role in events preceding and during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417).

The Underking was the "old bugbear" of fairy tales dating back to the earliest years of the [Septim dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md). Some tales described him as a very old, very powerful [lich](Lore_Lich.md) —or at least, something resembling one. Others claimed he was the most powerful mage who ever lived. Somehow, this mage "lost his heart" and his very being was transformed. The mage's heart "disappeared", and without it, the Underking could not truly live or die.

The stories were largely true, though the Underking's identity remains a matter of dispute. Most sources (notably, the Underking himself) claim he was once [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md), Battlemage of Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). Zurin also went by his [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) name Arnand the Fox. As the Underking, Arctus has been associated with [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md), the god of magic, and was claimed to be the most powerful mage to ever live. However, according to *[The Arcturian Heresy](Lore_The_Arcturian_Heresy.md)* the Underking was previously [Ysmir Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), a supernatural being said to have lived many lives, and who was an ally of Tiber Septim. Among Wulfharth's numerous purported titles was "Underking". The King of Worms was also rumored to be the Underking.

For more information, see the [main Lore article](Lore_Underking.md).

#### Emperor Uriel Septim I (?b - [3E 64](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_64))

#### Emperor Uriel Septim II (?b - [3E 82](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_82))
[Uriel Septim II](Lore_Uriel_II.md) was the fifth emperor of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for eighteen years, from [3E 64](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_64) to [3E 82](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_82). His reign was beset by blights, plagues, and insurrections, and his poor management of the Empire's financial policies left it bankrupt and in debt. He was preceded by his father, [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_I.md), and was succeeded by his son, [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md).

#### Emperor Uriel Septim III (Uriel Mantiarco) (3E 97- [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127))
[Uriel Septim III](Lore_Uriel_III.md) (born **Uriel Mantiarco**) was the ninth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for six years, from [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121) to [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127). In [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120), he and his mother [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) asserted that the heir apparent [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) was a bastard, and they proposed that Uriel take the throne instead of Kintyra due to the alleged uncertainty of her parentage. After hearing of this accusation, Kintyra banished them from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), and they returned to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), where they began planning the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md). In 3E 121, his forces captured and deposed Kintyra II; he then assumed the throne as Uriel Septim III. However, only half of the provinces supported Uriel III, while the rest supported his uncles, [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), who allied to oppose him. In 3E 127, Uriel III was captured at the Battle of Ichidag and placed in a cart for transport to stand trial in the Imperial City, but a mob burned him to death while en route. He was preceded by his first cousin, Kintyra II, and was succeeded by his uncle, Cephorus I.

#### Emperor Uriel Septim IV (Uriel Lariat) (?b - [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247))
[Uriel Septim IV](Lore_Uriel_IV.md) (born **Uriel [Lariat](Lore_Lariat.md)**) was the fifteenth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for forty-five years, from [3E 202](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_202) to [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247). He was preceded by his older brother, [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md), and was succeeded by [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md).

#### Emperor Uriel Septim V (?b - [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290))
[Uriel Septim V](Lore_Uriel_V.md) was the seventeenth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for twenty-two years, from [3E 268](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_268) to [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290). During his reign, Uriel V conquered [Roscrea](Lore_Roscrea.md), [Cathnoquey](Lore_Cathnoquey.md), [Yneslea](Lore_Yneslea.md), and [Esroniet](Lore_Esroniet.md). In [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288), Uriel V embarked on his most ambitious enterprise when he invaded [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), but he was killed during the invasion at the Battle of [Ionith](Lore_Ionith.md) in 3E 290. Despite his death in Akavir, Uriel V is considered a military genius and second only to [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) as one of the greatest warrior emperors of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He was preceded by his father, [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md), and was succeeded by his son, [Uriel VI](Lore_Uriel_VI.md).

#### Emperor Uriel Septim VI ([3E 285](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_285) - [3E 313](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_313))
[Uriel Septim VI](Lore_Uriel_VI.md) was the eighteenth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for thirty years, from [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290) to [3E 320](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_320). He was preceded by his father, [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_V.md), and was succeeded by his sister, [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md).

#### Emperor Uriel Septim VII ([3E 346](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_346) - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/LO-people-Uriel_Septim_VII.png/200px-LO-people-Uriel_Septim_VII.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Uriel_Septim_VII.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Uriel_Septim_VII.png) An engraving of Uriel Septim VII from the [Pocket Guide to the Empire, 3rd Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition.md) [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) ([/ˈ j ʊər i ə l ˈ s ɛ p t ɪ m/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ***yer** -iel septm* Link ⏵) was the twenty-first [Dragonborn Emperor](Lore_Dragonborn.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), though some described him as the twenty-fourth of the Septim line.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Placement)</sup> He succeeded to the throne in [3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368) after the passing of his father, [Pelagius Septim IV](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_IV.md). Uriel VII's 65-year reign was interrupted for a decade by the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), during which he was trapped in another [plane of existence](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) while the impostor [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) ruled in his name. After being restored to the throne, his reign saw the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Miracle_of_Peace.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and the fulfillment of the [Nerevarine Prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Uriel VII was assassinated in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), ushering in the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).

#### Mad Urkazbur (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Urkazbur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Urkazbur), otherwise known as **Mad Urkazbur**, was a powerful and intelligent [Snow Ogre](Lore_Ogre.md) shaman, known as the "Ice Elder of the Ogres" or the "mad Ogre wizard". Urkazbur resided in the wilds of northern [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), where he had gathered a small army of fellow Ogres. Urkazbur was considered unusually intelligent for his kind, even seemingly knowing how to read [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md). He was a master of powerful totemic frost [magic](Lore_Magic.md), his impressive abilities included the power to create enchanted [duplicates](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ice_Golem) of himself made entirely of ice. These effigies started out small, about the size of a child's doll, and would grow large enough to fight at his side. Urkazbur was known to regularly practice his ice spells.

#### Usunok (fl. before 2E 582)
[Usunok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Usunok) was an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) hero held in high regard by the Orc branch of the Cult of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md). He features in the Orsimer religious scripture *The Book of the Warrior God* and has a passage titled *The Rage of Usunok* devoted to him and his deeds. In this passage, he is described as a great hunter and warrior, known for his fury, charging only with his fists and the blessing of Trinimac, delivering a single, fatal blow to his foe. His deeds influenced the metalworking of the Trinimac Cult faithful. Those who follow the way of Trinimac call their rounded maces "fists of Usunok" in honor of his deeds. His strength was considered legendary, he could fall down a full-sized [mammoth](Lore_Mammoth.md) with a single punch.

#### King Uuril (fl. 1E)
[Uuril](Lore_Uuril.md) was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) king who ruled from [Silaseli](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silaseli) in northern [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). Uuril had twenty-three children. His youngest child was Urenenya, who was renowned for her beauty.

An astute and ambitious ruler, Uuril secured his family's power for generations by marrying twenty-two of his children to both rivals and allies. Many suitors traveled from far and near lands to marry Uuril's last unwed child, Urenenya, hoping to become connected to the king. However, Urenenya refused to be wed, defying Uuril. Urenenya refused to be married off because she was in love with a [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) engineer named Klathzgar. With great reluctance, Uuril confined Urenenya to a tower at Silaseli, intending to release her only if she agreed to an arranged marriage.

After Urenenya fell gravely ill, Uuril had his healers attempt to restore her health, but they could not cure her maladies. Taking pity on his dying daughter, Uuril allowed Urenenya to leave her tower. Soon afterward, Urenenya disappeared, and Uuril never learned her fate. Uuril later died and was buried at Silaseli.

#### Uwafa (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Uwafa](Lore_Uwafa.md) was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) who lived in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He was born in the city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) to a powerful family of [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md). His father was Suturah, and he had two siblings: his brother Alasan, and his younger sister Shiri.

#### Uzdabikh (fl. 1E 10th century)
[Uzdabikh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uzdabikh) was an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) hero who emerged from obscurity to become the celebrated figure of the [Battle of Fharun Pass](Lore_Wars.md#Battle_of_Fharun_Pass). Leading a warband tasked with defending a tunnel, Uzdabikh faced an unexpected onslaught from the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) army, resulting in the heroic defense of the pass. Armed with bow, axe, knife, and unmatched ferocity, Uzdabikh stood alone as her warband fell one by one. She fought relentlessly, and the tunnel became a bloody battlefield where one-thousand-and-one Redguards met their demise. Legends speak of a blessed helmet that shielded Uzdabikh from every attack, leaving her standing among the Redguard corpses when reinforcements arrived. Her helm disappeared during the ancient battle of Fharun Pass.
