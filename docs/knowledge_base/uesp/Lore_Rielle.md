[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-place-Rielle.jpg/200px-ON-place-Rielle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rielle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Rielle.jpg) Rielle circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Rielle, meaning "Beauty" in [Ayleidoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleidoon), is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Ruins) on the steps of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md), northwest of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBMap-1)</sup> It holds a large crypt in its lowest levels.

During the ongoing [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the adventurers Calm-Water, Doralon, and Filibert Morfin had come to the ruins in hopes of entering it and making a profit from their journey. To their dismay, the entrance was sealed off by an iron gate, and they departed to make up the money for their fruitless endeavor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dialogue-3)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins had opened and the subterranean passages were swarming with [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead) and [rats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rat).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) attempted to steal the [Great Welkynd Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Welkynd_Stone) from Rielle's inner crypt to reopen an [Oblivion Gate](Lore_Oblivion_Gate.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The Mythic Dawn excavated a passage that tunnelled under the Jeralls from Varlais Cavern in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) and broke through into Rielle's subterranean complex. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) was made aware of the plot and took the Great Welkynd Stone to keep it out of the wrong hands, defeating the stone's [lich](Lore_Lich.md) guardian, Norion the Undying, in the process. The exit to Cyrodiil had collapsed in the intervening years, though the rest of the ruin was mostly intact.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
