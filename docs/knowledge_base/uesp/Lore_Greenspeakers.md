# Greenspeakers

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f8/ON-place-Snugpod.jpg/200px-ON-place-Snugpod.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Snugpod.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Snugpod.jpg) Tree-Pods created by Greenspeakers [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/ON-npc-Nethrin.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Nethrin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nethrin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nethrin.jpg) The Greenspeaker, Nethrin The Greenspeakers (also known as **Greenshapers**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONBowenas-1)</sup> **Green-Singers**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-2)</sup> **Home Singers**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLoad Snug-3)</sup> **Pod-Singers**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONPod-4)</sup> and **Tree Shapers**)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONBranch-5)</sup> are [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) practitioners of [nature magic](Lore_Nature_Magic.md) who work in tandem with the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) to shape the environment into settlements such as villages and even cities.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONDorinlas-6)</sup> Their practices are sometimes referred to as **Pact Magic**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PME-7)</sup>

They enhance the growth of [Graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) trees and branches to form furniture, [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md), and even housing called Pod-Homes.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONDorinlas-6)</sup> They also craft it into bridges and platforms,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONOutside-8)</sup> as well as gates<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONValeguard-9)</sup> and large temples.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONTemple-10)</sup> They also create items of everyday use with the usage of the magic, such as flatware.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Cont W-11)</sup>

According to the accounts of the followers of the [Dawnway](Lore_Dawnway.md) from the period of [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), greenspeaking emerged early in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). An ancestor of Nedolir, hailing from the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md) supposedly developed techniques that would later lead to the widespread cultivation of seedpod houses throughout Valenwood.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VIVAB-12)</sup> However, the Bosmer inhabited graht-oak shaped settlements long before that period, as cities such as Elden Root had already stood and been inhabited well before the arrival of the Ayleids.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AE4TET-13)</sup>

Greenspeakers use [greenspring seeds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenspring_Seeds) to grow new forests and turn them into settlements and enclosures.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONDarolith-14)</sup> As they shape the trees into homes, the wood leaves behind wilted and useless samples of [heartwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartwood), which are then re-purposed into decorations by the locals.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONRanneth-15)</sup> The Graht-oaks are grown from a similar item called a Perchance Acorn. These acorns are the [Green-Sap](Lore_Green-Sap.md)'s equivalent to the stones of the legendary [Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AE4TET-13)</sup> A greenspring seed corrupted with [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) magic is called a [wildburn seed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn_Seeds), and not only does it grow a forest at a faster rate than a normal seed, it leaves a devastated outer region. Dawnwood in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) was grown with a wildburn seed, with its outer area called the Wildburn.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONDarolith-14)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONBeragon-16)</sup>

Some of the mages employed by the [Vinedusk Rangers](Lore_Vinedusk_Rangers.md) are also accomplished greenspeakers. They are frequently used whenever the Graht-oak of Vinedusk Village is destroyed and they need to move their location. They plant the seeds and the growing process takes a few days until it is completely built.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONGlooredel-17)</sup> The Falinesti were considered the greatest greenspeakers in history and their end goal was to create the perfect fusion of city and forest, which led to the creation of modern-day [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md), the capital of Valenwood.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONAndewen-18)</sup>

[Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) claims that the Greenshapers and [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md) are not mutually exclusive but if one wanted to become a Greenshaper, they do not need to become a Priest of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) like a spinner.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-2)</sup> Greenspeakers don formal garbs embellished with antlers for rare formal ceremonies.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-GG-19)</sup>
