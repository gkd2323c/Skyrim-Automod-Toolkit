# Kyne

Warrior-Wife, who sanctifies every axe-edge
Goddess of the Storm, who gifted us with voice
Bless us, Kyne; may we serve you with every word*
— [Shrine inscription](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shrine_inscription) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg) Stone Depiction of the Hawk Kyne, or **Kaan** in the [dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), is the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) goddess of the Storm, widow of [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor), the Blessed Warrior-Wife,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:354</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KC-2)</sup> and a favored god of warriors.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Froki-4)</sup> She is one of the Hearth Gods, and is considered the chief deity of the Nordic pantheon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DATN-5)</sup> As the goddess of the Storm, she is strongly associated with wind and rain, and is thought to control both forces, even when they bring misery.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSOP-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SOTR19-7)</sup>

She is often called the **Mother of Men and beasts**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Froki-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:354</sup> as Nords believe the sky exhaled onto the land at the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) to form them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Froki-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-COTS-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1S-9)</sup> She is also called the **Kiss at the End**, because Nords generally believe that she leads their dead to [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-GOSMON-10)</sup> Ancient Nords were also known to call her **Sister Hawk**.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

### Mother of Nords
Nords still refer to themselves as "the Sons and Daughters of Kyne".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Decree-12)</sup> Some consider her to be the leader of the Nordic pantheon of [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-GOSMON-10)</sup> Kyne and [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) are considered by followers of the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines) to be one and the same, though more ardent supporters of the Old Ways among the Nords dismiss Kynareth as a "pale shadow".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Froki-4)</sup> While Nords also acknowledge [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara), the "Mother-Goddess" of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), they view her only as Kyne's handmaiden.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup> The trees near [Kynesgrove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kynesgrove) (or Kyne's Grove) are thought to be sacred to her.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-events-13)</sup> The ancient Nords believed servants of Kyne found eternal rest in the [Forest of Dreams](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forest_of_Dreams), such as Hela, a "friend to all beasts".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRKyne-14)</sup> [Kyne's Aegis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kyne%27s_Aegis) is an island found in the icy waters of the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md).

### Goddess of Storm
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/ON-concept-Kyne_shrine.png/200px-ON-concept-Kyne_shrine.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Kyne_shrine.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Kyne_shrine.png) Shrine to Kyne [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-crown_store-Fountain%2C_Kyne%27s_Radiance.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Fountain%2C_Kyne%27s_Radiance.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Fountain,_Kyne%27s_Radiance.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Fountain,_Kyne%27s_Radiance.jpg) Kyne's Radiance Fountain in Markarth Nords believe she first taught them how to wield the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), or Storm Voice, though some sources claim she did so through her "daughters",<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:330</sup> while others claim she did so in tandem with [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Hrothgar-15)</sup> Appropriately, "Kaan" is a Word of Power, which can be used to soothe wild beasts, who are also believed to be her creation.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Froki-4)</sup> Many great Tongues are considered to be blessed by Kyne, such as [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), the Storm of Kyne, and [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus), the "Bull of Kyne".<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TAH-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-FSOKW-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Both have been called sons of Kyne and the Breath of Kyne.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSOP-6)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-FSOKW-17)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SATD-19)</sup> Some ancient legends speak of the goddess's personal intervention at important moments in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s history.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-FSOKW-17)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SOTR24-20)</sup> Others suggest she was involved in granting the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) to [St. Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSOP-6)</sup> On one occasion Kyne intervened and cleansed and forgave Keeper Ormi who turned into a [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md), after her son Sahun revealed himself to still be alive and prayed for her.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOFKH-21)</sup>

The ancient Nords associated her with the [hawk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hawk).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Felldir-22)</sup> Bas-reliefs and totemic representations depicting Kyne can be found in various Nordic ruins throughout [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TDW-23)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-events-13)</sup> [Kyne's Tears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flora_K#Kyne.27s_Tears) are a rare plant whose delicate flowers are said to have magical properties, particularly involving purification.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-HT-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> The plant is said to grow where the tears of the goddess Kyne fall to the earth.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-KT-26)</sup>

The [Fryse Hags](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fryse_Hags) of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) are known to venerate Kyne,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Reinhardt-27)</sup> and the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) claim to act in her name on the rare occasions that they speak.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> The [Keepers of the Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keepers_of_the_Grove) are part of a holy order dedicated to Kyne, serving as the tenders of the Shrine of Kynesgrove in the eponymous city.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-KOTG-29)</sup>

[Auroras](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aurora) are sometimes referred to as "Kyne's lights".<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-30)</sup> Fountains imbued to emit magical auroras were dedicated to the goddess.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Crown-31)</sup>

The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) [Dreadhorn Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreadhorn_Clan) is known for their use of shamanistic Kyne magic.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive FALK-32)</sup>
