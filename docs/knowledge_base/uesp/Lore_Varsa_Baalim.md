[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Unearthing_Mehrunes_Razor_13.jpg) Ruins of Varsa Baalim Varsa Baalim was an [Ayleidic](Lore_Ayleid.md) city located in the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), close to [Lake Canulus](Lore_Lake_Canulus.md) on the foothills of the [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md). It was a great, ringed, labyrinthine city constructed during the height of Ayleid rule to guard the Nefarivigum, a foul construct of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) used to test pilgrims seeking [Mehrunes Razor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mehrunes_Razor).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-To AC-1)</sup>

For years, the elves drove back the many pilgrims who came for the evil artifact. However, in the early days of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) managed to slip into the city unnoticed and turn the populace. Varsa Baalim was thrown into a gathering storm of madness and ruin, until it seemed that none were left to prevent the Razor from being recovered. Suddenly, the city was gone, when the mountains of the Eastern Niben swallowed Varsa Baalim and the Nefarivigum with it. Whether it was caused by a final fallback plan of the Ayleids, a natural cataclysm, or the touch of the [Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) is unknown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-To AC-1)</sup>

For centuries the city remained hidden, entombed deep within the mountains, inhabited by the undead vampiric remnants of its populace. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a rogue [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) arch-mage named [Frathen Drothan](Lore_Frathen_Drothan.md) set out to use the Nefarivigum to recover Mehrunes Razor, which he could then use to overthrow the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). He gathered many mercenaries to his cause and formed the [Drothmeri Army](Lore_Drothmeri_Army.md), which marched into Cyrodiil and captured [Sundercliff Watch](Lore_Sundercliff_Watch.md), an abandoned fort built during the rule of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LS-2)</sup> The fort had deep [iron](Lore_Iron.md) mines that extended far underground, and Drothan used these to excavate the entrance to Varsa Baalim. Once it was uncovered, he ventured into the ruins of the city with a large detachment of mercenaries and magically sealed the entrance to deter assassins.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The vampires quickly fell upon the band, and Drothan fled, making his way to the Nefarivigum and leaving the scattered mercenaries to fight back against the vampires.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md), hearing rumors of Drothan's rebel army, entered Sundercliff Watch, fought through the Drothmeri Army, and broke the magical barrier preventing any from entering Varsa Baalim. The Hero traversed the ruins, where the battle between the Drothmeri and the vampires still raged, and entered the Nefarivigum to defeat Drothan and claim the Razor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Drothmeri failure was briefly documented in the book *[The Keepers of the Razor](Lore_The_Keepers_of_the_Razor.md)*.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TKOTR-6)</sup>

## Notes
- Mehrunes Dagon was summoned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and attempted to merge [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) with his plane of the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) using the power of the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md). He manifested in the village of [Sundercliff](Lore_Sundercliff.md) above Varsa Baalim, choosing it as the place to begin this merging of the realms. Dagon was banished before this ritual could be completed, but his arrival resulted in the destruction of Fort Sundercliff and the surrounding village.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
