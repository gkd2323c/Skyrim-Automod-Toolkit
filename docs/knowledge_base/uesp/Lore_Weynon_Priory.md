## History
In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the priory was dedicated to the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) and served as a retreat for the acolytes of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). It was also home to a small village at the time. The priory was known for sending missionaries to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) to convert the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) away from worship of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), which was a perilous endeavor at the time.

During the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the priory remained neutral but vulnerable to the marching armies. It was located in the territory initially controlled by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Cyr-1)</sup> As the war raged on, the priory was targeted by bandits and [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AB-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BDS-3)</sup> The Black Dagger bandit gang went so far as to steal the Claw of Akatosh, although it was quickly returned.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COA-4)</sup> The acolytes attempted to combat the evils being committed by a [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) cult called the [Shadowed Path](Lore_Shadowed_Path.md), and kept a close eye on the ruins of [Lindai](Lore_Lindai.md) while it was occupied by the cult.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ETL-5)</sup> The priory received substantial aid from passing soldiers.

Following the apotheosis of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), Weynon Priory was rededicated to the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md) and became a chapter house of the [Order of Talos](Lore_Order_of_Talos.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBBaurus-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBPrior Maborel-7)</sup> The order was responsible for spreading the teachings and worship of the divine [Talos](Lore_Talos.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBPrior Maborel-7)</sup> and had close ties to the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBBaurus-6)</sup> Sometime in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the monks at Weynon Priory took in and raised the orphan Reynald Jemane after his family home, [Weatherleah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Weatherleah) was attacked by [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBReynald-8)</sup> By the late Third Era, [Jauffre](Lore_Jauffre.md), the Grandmaster of the Blades, took up residence as a monk in the priory.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBBaurus-6)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), right before his assassination, [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) sent an escaped prisoner (the future [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md)) on a mission to deliver the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) to Jauffre.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBTut-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBDt A-10)</sup> Jauffre hid the Amulet within the priory and sent the Hero to [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) to fetch Uriel's illegitimate son, [Martin](Lore_Martin.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBFt A-11)</sup> However, Kvatch had been sacked by the Daedric forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), thus starting the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBBt So K-12)</sup> When the Hero returned to Weynon Priory with Martin, it was under attack from the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) cult, who successfully stole the Amulet before being driven off.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBWPq-13)</sup>

With the signing of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Blades were formally disbanded and the worship of Talos was banned in the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The priory's status following these events is unknown.
