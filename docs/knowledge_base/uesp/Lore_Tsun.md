# Tsun

—Tsun Tsun is the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) god of trials against adversity. He died defending [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor) from foreign gods,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup> with his death remembered as bringing about the current world.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dat N-2)</sup> Tsun and his brother [Stuhn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stuhn) were both Shield-thanes of [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRTsun-3)</sup> Dead gods such as Shor and Tsun don't need temples,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:330</sup> for they have the biggest one of all; a vast hall of drinking and revelry known as the Hall of Valor (Shor's Hall).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:150</sup> He currently guards the Whalebone Bridge to the Hall of Valor of [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:631</sup> At Shor's bidding, he has taken on the role of the master of trials, asking new arrivals to the utopia to prove their strength in combat against him before they can enter the Hall, where he fights using his axe.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRTsun-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONMerenfire-5)</sup> The **Bear** was also one of the ancient [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) animal totems, associated later with the Nord god Tsun.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dat N-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Frostbane Bear-6)</sup>

Tsun held great respect for [magic](Lore_Magic.md), which he called the "[Clever Craft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clever_Craft)", and wielded the power of the [Voice](Lore_Thu%27um.md). He and others desired to face [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) when the Time-Eater set his soul-snare in Sovngarde, but for reasons unknown, Shor held them back,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRTsun-3)</sup> though Alduin's grip on Sovngarde eventually was broken by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Skyrim-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Dragonslayer-8)</sup> Only Tsun can transport the living from Sovngarde by summoning Shor's Might to return them to the Throat of the World.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:236</sup>

The spirits of clawthanes, wolves who've been accepted into Sovngarde, are said to often stand watch over the Whalebone Bridge alongside Tsun.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCW-9)</sup>

In the myth recounted in [Shor Son of Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Shor_Son_of_Shor), Tsun takes on the form of [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) at certain points in the tale while the two remain, at least partly, distinct beings, noted as being "akin" to one another, at the same time.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Tsun's Armor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/SR-item-Tsun%27s_Armor.jpg/150px-SR-item-Tsun%27s_Armor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Tsun%27s_Armor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Tsun%27s_Armor.jpg) Tsun's Armor [Tsun's Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tsun%27s_Armor) is an artifact associated with Tsun. His attire consists of armored boots, a torc, reinforced bracers, and a wide belt with a large engraved metal buckle worn over a layered kilt with hip-protecting metal plates. It kept his chest bare and exposed.

It was donned by Tsun as he fought those seeking to cross the Whalebone Bridge in [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), though it could also be found beyond [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) and worn by mortals. When worn, it restored their magicka and mended their wounds, though it also exposed them to danger.

### Tsun's Battle Axe
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg/150px-SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg) Tsun's Battle Axe [Tsun's Battle Axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe), (also written as **Tsun's Battleaxe**) is a weapon artifact associated with Tsun.

It was wielded by Tsun as he fought those seeking to cross the Whalebone Bridge in [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), though it could also be found beyond [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) and used by mortals. When wielded, it made them more likely to successfully make use of their skills.

## Notes
- A ghostly figure called the Watcher at the Bridge guarded the portal during the Interregnum, but their exact relation to Tsun was unknown.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONWat B-11)</sup>
- Some sources claim that he was hailed as a hero of the ancient days. He was described as a brother to [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), shield-thane to Shor, and a warrior of unmatched might. Among the Nords, he was revered as the greatest warrior to have ever lived.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Prima-12)</sup><sup>:152</sup>
- Tsun's Judgement was a weapon named after Tsun.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TJ-13)</sup>
