Saint Olms the Just is the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) patron [saint](Lore_Temple_Saints.md) of Chandlers and Clerks and the patron saint of [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Olms was the founder of the [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md), and was responsible for the conceiving and articulating the Inquisitorial principles of testing, ordeal, and forced repentance.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWdialogue-3)</sup> Olms was canonized by the Tribunal, and shrines to him could be found in Tribunal temples.

While they were alive, saints [Llothis](Lore_Llothis.md) and Olms were known to ride through the [Brass Fortress](Lore_Brass_Fortress.md) during the parades on holy days on [fabricant](Lore_Fabricant.md) [Nix-Ox](Lore_Nix-Ox.md) mounts.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NX-4)</sup>

When close to death, [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) placed Olms's soul into a modified [black soul gem](Lore_Souls.md#Soul_Gems) which was then brought to the Asylum Sanctorium in the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) and installed into a [Clockwork](Lore_Clockwork_Titan.md) [Titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md) to make him immortal.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SM-5)</sup> Unfortunately, the process eventually drove him to madness, which manifested as paranoia, an obsessive desire for freedom, and a persecution complex as he viewed his position as an unjust punishment or test from the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> His soul was released from the Clockwork Titan in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), and kept safe in the black soul gem while he rested.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AL-7)</sup>

All [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) shrine offerings grant cures of common diseases, of blight diseases, and of afflictions of poison. Offerings made to the shrine of Saint Olms additionally grant Olms' Benediction, for resistance to common disease.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-mwshrine-9)</sup>

One of the cantons of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md) was named in his honor, and a statue of Olms was erected on the upper outer levels.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The same was done for [Delyn](Lore_Delyn.md), and together they were called the Twin Saints.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LSD-13)</sup>
