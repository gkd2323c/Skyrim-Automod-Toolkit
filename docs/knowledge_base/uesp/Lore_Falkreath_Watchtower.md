—A local saying referring to Falkreath<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Falkreath (also called **Falcrenth**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AR-2)</sup> or **Falcreath**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KEVII-3)</sup> is one of the nine major cities in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRMap-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARMap-5)</sup> serving as the capital of [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) and the boreal forest. Its name derives from [Elvish](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md), though its meaning is unknown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:573</sup> The city is known as the "heroes' graveyard"; many great battles took place here for centuries, and countless warriors were buried here, leaving behind graves and monuments to them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRNenya-6)</sup> Because of the nature of the city, the merchants of Falkreath are known to reflect the melancholic themes of [death](Lore_Death.md#Nord_Beliefs_and_Customs) in their business.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRLoad-7)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
Falkreath lies in the southern forests of Skyrim, on a strategic crossroad in the middle of its respective hold and the provincial border towards [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md)'s wilder country, the [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KEVII-3)</sup> It is built in the forest basin between the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) and the highlands that contain the Shriekwind Bastion, where a small lake forms at the base, suited for a mill in this lumber-town. It is a city that is heavily reliant to the lumber industry, due to the abundance of resources around them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRLoad-7)</sup> In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Falkreath was much larger in scope. The jarl's longhouse once overlooked the town from the hills in the south, while the original Hall of the Dead was kept underneath the mountains. Nowadays, the current hall was a single home for the priest, as they tended to the expansive graveyard in the city's northern section. The jarl's longhouse is stationed in the dead center of the city, on the main road that runs through Falkreath. The land outside of the city is largely unpopulated, but beyond the western gate, the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) sanctuary was found just off the road.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRFalkreath-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONHold-9)</sup> The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Falkreath are known to master the ability to tame [Sabre Cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md), who are considered much more feral and wild than the [Senche-Cat](Lore_Senche-Cat.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOCM-10)</sup> Falkreath is neighbored by several settlements, including [Neugrad Watch](Lore_Neugrad_Watch.md) to the northeast, [North Keep](Lore_North_Keep.md) to the far north, and Hammerfell to the west towards [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARMap-5)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ARMap HF-11)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/63/SR-npc-Knight-Paladin_Gelebor.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Knight-Paladin_Gelebor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Knight-Paladin_Gelebor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Knight-Paladin_Gelebor.jpg) Falkreath's name originates from the elves
### Proto-History and the Men-of-'Kreath
The city loans its name from the [Elvish language](Lore_Elvish.md), but whatever connection the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) had to the area in ancient times is unknown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:573</sup> The earliest allusions to Falkreath come from two poetic works of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md): *[The Adabal-a](Lore_The_Adabal-a.md)*, attributed to [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md), and *[The Song of Pelinal](Lore_The_Song_of_Pelinal.md)*, written by various unknown authors. The former work mentions a tribe called the "men-of-'kreath", who were "imported from the North" as slaves to the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md). According to the latter work, [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) achieved the liberation and empowerment of the men-of-'kreath after orchestrating a massacre of elves in the "northern holdings" (presumably southern Skyrim and northern Cyrodiil). As they are not described as being Nords, the men-of-'kreath seem to have been a distinct group of [humans](Lore_Man.md) that were indigenous to the region.

### Foundation and Early Nordic History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Deathlord_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.jpg) Deathlord Bjarfrud Skjoralmor Falkreath was founded by ancient [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warrior [Bjarfrud Skjoralmor](Lore_Bjarfrud_Skjoralmor.md), who drove out "beastly" woodland inhabitants and cut down the great forest to build the settlement.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Eo BS-13)</sup> Many battles occurred near Falkreath for countless centuries, and many graves and monuments were built to commemorate the fallen. The town essentially grew around its cemeteries.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRNenya-6)</sup>

According to the *[Poetic Edda](Lore_Poetic_Edda.md)*, Falkreath was one of the places that submitted to [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) as High King of Skyrim.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-KOV-14)</sup> A statue once stood commemorating the spot where [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md) fell in battle defending Falkreath from the First Empire; local legend has it that his son, [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md), fell in exactly the same spot when he retook Falkreath years later,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PRIMA-15)</sup><sup>:901</sup> however, he was known to have actually fallen in the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md).

Later in the First Era, the [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) king [Kestic](Lore_Kestic.md) may have held sway over the Falkreath area, as evidenced by his epithet, "the Forest King". He was the last to hold this title, as the Nedic civilization of the [Deathlands](Lore_Hammerfell.md) fell with the arrival of the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Not D-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SD-17)</sup>

Falkreath's history is intertwined with that of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), as control of Falkreath often shifted between the Colovian estates and the holds of Skyrim. The earliest account of this was during an interregnum between the [First](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) and [Second Empires](Lore_Second_Empire.md) when the Colovian nations had split apart from each other and their leaders became thief-barons and petty-kings. That was until the arrival of [Reman I](Lore_Reman_I.md) and the formation of his Empire, although Falkreath's role during this time is unknown.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-R-18)</sup>

### Falkreath in the Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hakkvild%27s_High_Hall.jpg) Hakkvild's High Hall With the death of High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), Skyrim split into eastern and western parts. The Western Kingdom, which included Falkreath, followed High King [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md), who ruled from [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TCo F-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-20)</sup> The Skjoralmor Clan (descended from Bjarfrud Skjoralmor) ruled as jarls of Falkreath during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), and were aligned with the Western Kingdom.

When the Imperial Province of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) was sacked in 2E 431, it displaced many [Orsimer](Lore_Orsimer.md), who in turn fled to Skyrim and formed a large chiefdom under [Yashnag gro-Yazgu](Lore_Yashnag_gro-Yazgu.md). The chiefdom was considered the height of Orcish power in Skyrim and it was stationed in western Falkreath Hold, often harassing the hold capital. High King Svartr was unable to stop the threat it imposed, which ultimately led to the death of the Jarl of Falkreath. His son [Hakkvild](Lore_Hakkvild_Yashnag-Slayer.md) took his place and challenged Yashnag in a ritual of combat. In the end, Hakkvild had slain the Orcish chief and garnered the title "Yashnag-Slayer". His victory led to the dissolution of the chiefdom and their return to the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) in [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Oo S-21)</sup>

During [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](Lore_Kurog.md)'s consolidation of power, he planned to reestablish the Orcish kingdom in Falkreath and create an Orcish city to rival [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). During this period, Kurog's forces were accumulating in the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md), ready to take Falkreath, however, the Nords held them back. Kurog gave this plan up after becoming allies with King Emeric.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

#### Icereach Witches and Imperial Intervention
Circa [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), after [Varen's Rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md), Reachfolk were driven out of Cyrodiil by Imperial forces. The Icereach Coven, as former advisors and teachers of the overthrown [Emperor Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), were among them.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SHOLE-23)</sup> They were led by a powerful witch known as Mother [Ciannait](Lore_Ciannait.md), and during their escape from Cyrodiil they wound up in the area west of Falkreath. They had taken refuge among the graves west of the town and raised numerous dead Nords from the graveyard to defend themselves from Imperial forces. Ultimately the Imperials ceased to continue their pursuit after suffering great losses and left the matter of the Reachfolk incursion to the local Nords.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MTCMC-24)</sup> The Nords eventually managed to cast the Reach witches out, driving them back to their ancient base on the island of [Icereach](Lore_Icereach.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Arvnir-25)</sup>

#### Dreadhorn Clan's Siege
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c5/ON-render-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg/200px-ON-render-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Eerika_Skjoralmor.jpg) Eerika Skjoralmor [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Domihaus_the_Bloody-Horned.jpg) Domihaus the Bloody-Horned Under the command of [Domihaus the Bloody-Horned](Lore_Domihaus.md), the Dreadhorn Clan planned to wrest control of their ancestral lands in Falkreath Hold from the Nords,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Mt C-26)</sup> greatly emboldened by their armaments from the [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md).

In the years prior to the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Falkreath was ruled by Jarl [Hjurgol Skjoralmor](Lore_Hjurgol_Skjoralmor.md), who did not act justly in the days leading to the sacking of Falkreath.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-IRt CR-27)</sup> The Jarl took to hiding in his longhouse, while his daughter and thane, [Eerika Skjoralmor](Lore_Eerika_Skjoralmor.md), led the remnants of the resistance.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Bo F-28)</sup> She held the town for some time until the intervention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), who delved through the battlefield and aided the Thane in their efforts to save the city. The group was able to defeat Domihaus at the Jarl's Keep and witness the coronation of Jarl Eerika.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOFalkreath-29)</sup>

### Falkreath in the Age of the Septims
In the latter years of the Interregnum, Falkreath was once again part of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md), who were also split into petty-kings and warlords, while the divided kingdoms of Skyrim had dissolved. King [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) of Falkreath sought to unify the estates but in order to move forward, he needed to secure his northern border, towards [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). His prized general, [Hjalti Early-Beard](Lore_Hjalti_Early-Beard.md), led his forces at the Battle of [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md), but as the siege proved that breaking the walls was impossible, the next morning he used his [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to obliterate it and win the battle. With this newfound power, King Cuhlecain unified the west and conquered the east, preparing to proclaim himself Emperor. But he was later assassinated and Hjalti, now known as [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), was named the Emperor. What followed was the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TAH-30)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Falkreath was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Bjeld, and it had a rivalry with [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AR-2)</sup> When the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) broke out in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Skyrim was under siege and faced severe casualties, where it was said everything between Falkreath and [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) was laid waste by the [daedric hordes](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-OBGate-31)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/SR-npc-Sinding_02.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Sinding_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Sinding_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Sinding_02.jpg) Sinding escapes from Falkreath's jail [Dengeir of Stuhn](Lore_Dengeir_of_Stuhn.md) served as the Jarl of Falkreath in the years that preceded the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md). He was encouraged by many people to step down as the Jarl when he supported the rebellious [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), as opposed to the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-SRDengeir-32)</sup> The position of leader of Falkreath was then given to his nephew, [Siddgeir](Lore_Siddgeir.md), who was known to be arrogant yet loyal to the Empire, although it was his steward Nenya that fulfilled the role as leader much more than him.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRNenya-6)</sup> Legate Skulnar commanded the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) throughout Falkreath Hold, and the stronghold of [Fort Neugrad](Lore_Fort_Neugrad.md) was a vital position for Falkreath's allegiance in the civil war.

## Notes
- The Nords of Falkreath practice an old Nordic custom with their [dead](Lore_Death.md#Nord_Beliefs_and_Customs) called *grave curd,* in which a fresh piece of [cheese](Lore_Cheese.md) is placed on top of a loved one's coffin, and every year on the anniversary, a fifth of the cheese is consumed by the family.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-COSRF-33)</sup>
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Falkreath's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Dark Shadows".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-34)</sup> *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)* brought back many of these arena teams to compete in an event known as the Dragon Games, but renamed Falkreath's arena team into the "Falkreath Fighters".<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-35)</sup>
