Makela Leki, also known was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warrior or "Sword Saint", a known worshipper of the God of War [Reymon Ebonarm](Lore_Reymon_Ebonarm.md), and one of the most famed heroes in [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) history. She is best known for her sacrifice at the Battle of [Bangkorai Pass](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), where she and her five companions were responsible for routing an invading [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) army led by [King Joile](Lore_Joile.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-1)</sup>

## Early Life
Makela grew up in a noble Redguard family, with three brothers and a sister. Her father was a swordmaster and Ansei warrior many years before he settled down to raise a family. Much like her father, Makela possessed the unique qualities that allowed her to form a [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md) - a mystical blade made of pure thought. Indeed, her natural affinity towards the Shahai was such that it's said she could form the Shehai as an infant.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-1)</sup>

At the age of eleven Makela was enrolled into the Hall of the Virtues of War to become a [Sword Singer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword_Singers), and joined a small band of Maidens of the Spirit Blade. Although she had a natural ability towards the Shehai, seldom was it substantial enough to be used as a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md). Despite this, Makela was still the first student from her local hall to have the ability to call forth the Shehai in two generations, thus was named as an Ansei warrior.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-1)</sup>

As the years passed, Makela and her band of five maidens mastered the Way of the Sword and completed their training, eventually returning to their hall to serve as instructors. Makela also married a man named Raliph and started a family of her own.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-1)</sup>

## The Breton Betrayal
In [1E 950](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_950), the joint forces of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) led by King [Joile](Lore_Joile.md) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) launched an attack on [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) that resulted in a thirty year siege of the city. Although King [Joile](Lore_Joile.md) was allied with the Redguards, he betrayed them during the campaign in [1E 973](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_973) and killed [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md), a famed swordsmaster and contemporary of Makela. Joile then executed his plan of invading Hammerfell through the region of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WJot B-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3O-3)</sup>

During the night of the Midyear Festival, Makela received word of the betrayal and the invading Breton army from the north. Makela and her band of maidens rushed to the [Bangkorai Pass](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md) where Joile's forces had slain the stationed Redguard forces and were about to breach the mountain pass. Makela and her band defended the pass against his army, but eventually all of them fell, except Makela. In desperation after her father's sword broke in her hand, Makela was able to summon her spirit sword in earnest, which allowed her to single-handedly defeat the Breton army and even slay King Joile himself. Although Makela was successful in repelling the invasion, she was gravely wounded.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-1)</sup>

Before death, she recorded her thoughts and memories of her life and the final battle into a magical [memory stone](Lore_Memory_Stone.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-1)</sup> Although details of the battle were preserved for later generations, Makela deeply regretted not preserving the memory of her immediate family, resulting in all of them being lost to history.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONML-4)</sup>

## Legacy
Shortly after her death, Makela's memory stone was found in the Bangkorai Pass. Its contents secured Makela's status as a legend, and her victory over the Joile was preserved and was celebrated by Redguards thereafter.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONML-4)</sup> It is said that because of her selfless sacrifice, the Breton armies never reached Sentinel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-St ML-5)</sup> Her body was laid to rest in the sacred [Hall of Heroes](Lore_Hall_of_Heroes.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONTrial-6)</sup> However, some scholars believe her final resting place to be in a nondescript cave in western [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Lt HS-7)</sup>

Centuries after her death, Makela's memory stone was stolen by an exiled [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) warrior named Ulbazar Thief-Lord.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> He fled [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and sought refuge in [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), only to die after being imprisoned in the city's crypts. In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), as a favor to King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md), High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) sent a detachment to retrieve Makela's stone from the crypts and return it to the Impervious Vault in [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONKings-9)</sup>

In the mid-Second Era, [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) encountered the spirit of Makela in the Hall of Heroes, where she granted them her blessing and entry into the Chamber of Passage.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONTrial-6)</sup>
