# Altmer


The Altmer are renowned as one of the most intelligent races, and their exceptional innate aptitude for magic makes them the most gifted in the [arcane arts](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LGDesc-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sk Altmer Description-8)</sup> This magical affinity is further enhanced by their naturally long [lifespans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lifespan), which range from several centuries to as much as a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Variety Pack4-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRB2-10)</sup> Some of the most ancient and powerful Altmer mages have extended their lives far beyond this natural span, in some cases enduring for millennia through the application of highly advanced magical disciplines.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PMB-12)</sup> In addition to their magical prowess, Altmer possess natural resistances to [disease](Lore_Disease.md) and [paralysis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Paralysis), though they are noted to be somewhat vulnerable to elemental forces.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DFAltmer Stats-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWAltmer Stats-14)</sup>

[Altmeri society](Lore_Altmer.md#Social_Structure) has been shaped by millennia of scholarly refinement and is deeply hierarchical and tradition-bound. The Altmer have long pursued an ideal of cultural perfection, venerating their lineage and striving to preserve the traditions, knowledge, and practices of their ancient ancestors.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOOlnewil-15)</sup> This ideal continues to shape all aspects of Altmeri life, from education and governance to art and daily conduct.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TPo P-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONFamily Shrine-17)</sup> The [Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapiarchs), a prestigious council of scholars and sages, are entrusted with preserving knowledge, overseeing education, and maintaining cultural continuity.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot Co S-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sot CT-19)</sup>

High Elves are often perceived by other races as proud, aloof, or even arrogant.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-NOEA-20)</sup> These perceptions are reinforced by centuries of cultural insularity and the perceived superiority of their customs, ancestry, and intellectual traditions. The Altmer, for their part, consider themselves Tamriel's most civilized people: the continent's common tongue is derived from [Altmeri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Elvish) speech and writing, and much of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md)'s art, law, architecture, and scholarship can trace its roots back to Altmeri tradition.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AODL-21)</sup>

## Appearance
[![High Elf male](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/51/LG-arena-Highelf_4.png/170px-LG-arena-Highelf_4.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-arena-Highelf_4.png) The Altmer are typically relatively tall, often standing a full head taller than the average [men](Lore_Men.md) or [mer](Lore_Mer.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-DFAltmer Description-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-23)</sup> They possess slender, graceful builds with long limbs and refined features,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> and their skin tones are typically various shades of gold; a trait that commonly sets them apart from most other elven races.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-23)</sup> Their hair is usually fair-blond, ginger, white, or silver, though darker shades such as brown or black are also fairly common.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOChar Create-24)</sup> Eye colors range from blue, green, and brown to gold and amber, often luminous or piercing.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOChar Create-24)</sup> Their facial features are angular, with high cheekbones, narrow chins, and long, sharply pointed ears.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOChar Create-24)</sup> In both posture and expression, Altmer often project an air of superiority or aloofness, reinforced by their cultural upbringing.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup>

Altmer fashion reflects their fixation with beauty, status, and arcane mastery. Jewelry and personal grooming are highly valued, and signs of very advanced aging are rarely visible—thanks to both natural longevity and magical upkeep.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-VOFHE-25)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRB2-10)</sup> The common practice of Altmer to dress in a manner resembling that of the ancient Aldmer, or that of other cultures that resemble the Aldmeri style, such as the ancient Ayleids and [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), elves of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), which is often viewed by the Mer as their golden age, is called "draping Ehlnofic". Clothes in this style often exhibit stylistic motifs in which circles, arcs, and tendrils abound, symbolizing the elven ancestors, the Aedra, being constrained by the creation of Mundus.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Ancient Elf-26)</sup>

## History

### Origin
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg) Artistic representation of Aldmeris The Altmer trace their ancestry and spiritual identity to the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), a mythic and nonlinear age when the first spirits—the [Et'Ada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Et%27Ada) —emerged from the primordial interplay between [Anuiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuiel), the soul of [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) and the force of stasis and ordered reflection, and [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis), the shadowed echo of Anuiel, embodying change and primordial chaos. From the Altmeri perspective, these spirits are not merely gods but literal ancestors—an idea that deeply influences their worldview and social hierarchy.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

A central tenet of Altmeri theology, laid out in works such as *[The Monomyth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Monomyth)*, is the belief that mortality is not a divine gift, but a curse. The mortal plane of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) was created through deception by [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), the so-called Missing God, who tricked many spirits into sacrificing their essence.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> While humans—who, with the exception of the [Redguards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Redguards), see themselves as creations of the gods—interpret this act as a gift of life through divine sacrifice, the Altmer regard it as a catastrophic betrayal that severed them from the immortality of their ancestors.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TAX-27)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> According to the creation myth, when [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus), the architect of the plans for the mortal world, decided to terminate the project, the gods decided to convene at the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) to decide on what to do. Most of the spirits departed with Magnus, becoming the [Magna Ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge). But some Aedric spirits chose to stay on Nirn after Magnus and the Magna Ge departed in order to keep working on the new world so it'd remain viable. Among those spirits, some, typically referred to from that point on as ["Earthbones"](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), are thought to have followed the example of [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre), giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law as the "bones of the earth", eternal laws of nature. While others, typically referred to from that point on as ["Ehlnofey"](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), are thought to have chosen not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, thus becoming the progenitors to mortal life, which arose from their lines and took on its current form due to a phenomenon of gradual diminishment, of each consecutive generation becoming weaker and more removed than its progenitors in stature and might. It was due to this phenomenon that the first [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), forebears of modern Altmer, are thought to have eventually emerged.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BTAOM-28)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Mystery-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Anuad-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-31)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-NMI-32)</sup>

An alternate creation myth that explains Altmeri origin is put forward in the *[Anuad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuad)*, attributed to the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-MR-33)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Mono2-34)</sup> It describes the cosmic struggle between Anu and Padomay, the death of the world-mother Nir, and the creation of Nirn from the remnants of twelve shattered worlds. The [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) —ancestors of elves and men—survived, but a war between their factions reshaped the world, sinking much of the land beneath new oceans and destroying most of the realm of the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) who were also survivors of the original Twelve Worlds. As this account tells it, the Elves, including the Altmer, are descended from the 'Old Ehlnofey,' a group who remained in their ancestral homeland and preserved much of their original power and knowledge while remaining fewer in number. <sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Anuad-30)</sup>

#### Early Schisms
The Altmer are thought to be the most direct, unaltered descendants of the original Aldmer elves,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> and the transition from one to the other is not entirely clear. When the Aldmer left their homeland of [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) —a place regarded by some sources as allegorical rather than literal—they settled primarily in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), arriving first on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) and later spreading westward.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BTAOM-28)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3All-6)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/LO-misc-Velothi.png/200px-LO-misc-Velothi.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Velothi.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Velothi.png) Depiction of [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) leading the Chimer to Morrowind Following the [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) at the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md), where the gods set the laws of reality and cast out [Lorkhan's Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) before departing from the affairs of Nirn,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BTAOM-28)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Enigma-35)</sup> the Aldmer organized into powerful arcane societies on the Summerset Isles. Much of their early history is lost or obscured, but it is known that many ruins and structures—most notably the [Ceporah Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceporah_Tower) —predate Aldmeri settlement by centuries.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> During this period, the Aldmer likely warred with the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) and other unknown beings for dominance over Summerset.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PGE3O-36)</sup> Eventually, they unified and constructed the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) as a monument to their collective spirit and a pinnacle of magical achievement, unsurpassed until the rise of the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), which served as the headquarters of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) founded by [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-t Cot T-37)</sup> The Crystal Tower was considered their greatest architectural and metaphysical achievement, a monument to unity, arcane mastery, and the metaphysical Tower principle of Crystal-Like-Law.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-t Cot T-37)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-t Cot T-37)</sup> It served as a beacon of Aldmeri civilization and the foundation of Altmeri identity.

Yet ideological schisms fractured Aldmeri unity. The most prominent of these was the [Velothi dissident movement](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Velothi), which rejected Altmeri orthodoxy and led the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) eastward to what would become [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ASHOM-38)</sup> Other groups, such as the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), and [Orsimer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orsimer), emerged independently in response to distinct cultural, spiritual, or metaphysical divergences—each embracing different interpretations of the divine.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TCO-39)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE1S-40)</sup> Many traditions attribute the divergence of elven subraces to their differing relationships with the gods,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TTNOO-41)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TA-42)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-VAM-43)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-FOTN-44)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-THOT-45)</sup> or to the realization that different Towers could tell different "stories" and thus shape different domains, leading to elven unity breaking due to different groups aligning themselves with alternative Towers and destinies.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Enigma-35)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-NMI-32)</sup> The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) were once considered a splinter group from Summerset, but tapestries in the Crystal Tower suggest their separation occurred before the Aldmer left Aldmeris. This view was accepted by both Altmer and Maormer sources by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TMo P-46)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TCPo A-47)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFRot M-48)</sup> The Altmer who remained in Summerset came to define themselves as the truest heirs of the Aldmer. Their society emphasizes purity of form, ancestral memory, and strict continuity of tradition. Songs are still composed in the [Aldmeri language](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-49)</sup> Their arcane, political, and religious institutions all reflect a reverence for the past and a desire to emulate the perfection of their divine ancestors in hopes to one day rejoin them in the stars.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOVairabrian-50)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOOlnewil-15)</sup>

### First Era
Two prominent Altmeri institutions emerged during the First Era, shaping not only the Isles but also the wider development of [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) civilization. The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), based on the isle of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), became the foremost authority on mystical philosophy and arcane theory. Claiming descent from the oldest traditions of Aldmeri magic, the Order played a formative role in early conceptions of the metaphysical structure of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) and the practice of the Elder Way.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PSIJIC-51)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup> In contrast, the Direnni Clan embraced a more worldly approach. Originally one of the noble lineages of Summerset, the Direnni relocated to [Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balfiera) in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) and gradually expanded their influence across [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). During the early to mid-First Era, they established a quasi-imperial dominion over large swaths of western Tamriel, including human populations.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-DRD-52)</sup> Their rule culminated in the enforcement of the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md), a legal and arcane regime that laid early groundwork for structured governance across the region.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Before The Ages-53)</sup>

#### The Psijic Order and the Old Ways
[![Banner of the Order](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/ON-banner-Psijic_Order.png/100px-ON-banner-Psijic_Order.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Psijic_Order.png) The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) is the oldest known magical and philosophical organizations in Tamriel, predating even the establishment of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) by many centuries. Its first known mention in recorded history dates to [1E 20](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_20), when the Breton sage [Voernet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voernet) chronicled his journey to the isle of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) to confer with the Psijic Ritemaster, [Iachesis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iachesis).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup> However, the Order's true origins likely reach back to the earliest days of Aldmeri colonization of the Summerset Isles during the late Merethic Era.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-PGE3SI-54)</sup>

According to tradition, the Psijic Order was formed in response to a theological and social transformation among the early Aldmer. As Aldmeri society began to shift from the general veneration of all ancestors to the more hierarchical worship of ancestor-heroes—those claimed as divine progenitors by the ruling elites—the foundations of the Altmeri pantheon began to coalesce around the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra). In opposition, a faction of conservative Aldmeri mystics rejected this selective reverence, favoring the original, more universal worship of their ancestors.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-PGE3SI-54)</sup> These dissenters withdrew from mainstream society and settled on Artaeum, where they formalized their esoteric beliefs into what became known as the Elder Way.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-M-55)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup> The Elder Way, central to Psijic philosophy, emphasized introspection, meditation, and mastery of the self through magical discipline—especially through the school of [Mysticism](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism), which they viewed as a bridge between mortal perception and the divine architecture of the Aurbis. The Psijics taught that through disciplined spiritual and magical study, one could come to perceive and influence the hidden currents of reality.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PSIJIC-51)</sup>

Though the Psijic Order generally discouraged direct intervention in worldly politics, it did not wholly isolate itself. The doctrine of seliffrnsae—“grave and faithful counsel”—governed their rare engagements with secular leaders.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PSIJIC-51)</sup> The Psijics offered guidance only to those rulers they deemed wise or receptive to their teachings, often through cryptic visions, subtle manipulation of fate, or timely prophecy. The Ritemaster and the [Council of Artaeum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Council_of_Artaeum) collectively oversaw such decisions, and their influence could be felt from the courts of the Altmeri kings to the battlemages of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PSIJIC-51)</sup>

At the center of Psijic mysticism stood the legendary Ceporah Tower, which housed the [Dreaming Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreaming_Cave) —a site of immense arcane significance. This ancient structure allowed initiates to peer beyond the veil of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), accessing the different planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) or beyond.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis-56)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-2920TLYFR-57)</sup>

Although Artaeum and the Order itself would mysteriously vanish from the world for centuries at a time—most notably in the late Second Era—the Psijics' legacy endured in the institutions and magical philosophies they inspired, including the later formation of the Mages Guild by the Altmer [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md), a former Psijic himself.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Mages Guild-58)</sup>

#### The Direnni Hegemony
The [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) are an aristocratic merchant clan of Altmer whose members laid the foundation for modern [alchemy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy), [conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration), and [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TBMOP-59)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-60)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Once-61)</sup> In stark contrast to the Psijic Order’s inward-looking mysticism, the [Direnni Clan](Lore_Direnni.md) pursued a deliberate campaign of expansion and domination beyond the shores of Summerset. Descended from noble Altmeri lineages, the Direnni departed [Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alinor) during the Middle Merethic Era and established their power base on the [Isle of Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Isle_of_Balfiera), where they seized and repurposed the ancient [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md). Revered as the oldest structure in Tamriel and the mythic site of the Convention, control of the Tower imbued the Direnni with both symbolic legitimacy and metaphysical authority, reinforcing their claim as rightful stewards of Merethic heritage.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-DRD-52)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/DF-banner-Direnni_Crest.png/200px-DF-banner-Direnni_Crest.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-banner-Direnni_Crest.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-banner-Direnni_Crest.png) The [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) Banner of the Third Era Over the following centuries, the Direnni hegemony stretched from all of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), into large parts of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) constructing a formidable aristocracy upheld by magical dominance, bureaucratic centralization, and carefully orchestrated intermarriage.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-62)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-TDCP11-63)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Once-61)</sup> The Direnni are known more for their legendary warriors and monarchs, and less for their spellcasters, but magic is still an important tradition to them.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-DRD-52)</sup>

The Direnni played a pivotal role in the ethnogenesis of the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md). During their dominion over High Rock, they engaged in widespread breeding with the subjugated [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nedes) population, who were often used as concubines or sexual slaves. This Aldmeri and Nedic interbreeding<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3All-6)</sup> occurred for centuries as far back as the Merethic Era.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-MDBMM-64)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-LATD-65)</sup> The resulting mixed-race offspring, known as [Manmer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Manmer), formed a distinct racial group that gradually evolved into the Bretons over successive generations.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Bretons-66)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-67)</sup> These individuals possessed partial Elven ancestry and a natural aptitude for magic. Although initially marginalized, the Bretons gradually rose in prominence within the Direnni administration and came to occupy more formalized roles, serving as retainers, scribes, battlemages, and even minor nobles within the Direnni courts. Over time, they developed a distinct cultural identity that synthesized Elven and human traditions.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Bretons-66)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-67)</sup>

The apogee of Direnni power came in [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482), when the battle-mage [Aiden Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aiden_Direnni) achieve an important victory over the Alessian Order at the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors). This decisive triumph checked Alessian expansionism and secured Direnni dominance in High Rock. However, the momentum proved short-lived. By [1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498), they were weakened after the war, and a combination of factors—including overextension, waning central authority, and the growing autonomy of Breton nobility—forced the Direnni to withdraw from the mainland, retreating to Balfiera.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-TLKot A-68)</sup> One account claims that the Direnni had already completed their withdrawal to Balfiera by the time the Redguards arrived in Tamriel.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Website Lore-69)</sup> Another contradictory source places the final stages of the Direnni’s flight from High Rock sometime around or after the ninth century of the First Era.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ROTPO-70)</sup> Yet another source claims that the Direnni remained dominant in High Rock until the middle of the First Era.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-PGE3High-71)</sup>

While no other Altmeri family would ever rival the Direnni in scope or longevity, smaller and unrelated efforts to establish mainland strongholds did occur. Among these, the [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) is the most notable. In the sixth century of the First Era, the Corelanya settled the partially abandoned Ayleid city of [Salas En](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salas_En) in southern Hammerfell. There, they revitalized its ancient port and established maritime trade routes with [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and the Topal Bay archipelagos. Unlike their orthodox cousins in Summerset, the Corelanya were known practitioners of Daedra-worship and were rumored to maintain covert ties with the seafaring [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), further alienating them from mainstream Altmeri society.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TSEE-72)</sup> This southern enclave was ultimately destroyed in [1E 853](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_853) by Ra Gada forces under King [Xakhwan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xakhwan), who launched a campaign to purge foreign settlements from the Hammerfell coast.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ESOHaqmir-73)</sup> Corelanyan Elves located outside the Ra Gada's reach regrouped at Summerset and attempted to convince High King Naurnindon to help them reclaim their territory. Frustrated from years of failed diplomacy, they attempted a coup to install Kinlady Arlinwe of Firsthold as High Queen. In [1E 909](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_909), Naurnindon gave the Corelanya a choice: surrender their nobility and assets or face exile. They chose to exile themselves to the island of [Solstice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Solstice) for its ample space for growth, and distance from any territory under the High King of Summerset’s rule.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Ho CC3-74)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Ho CC4-75)</sup>

Despite their retreat, the Direnni retained control of the Isle of Balfiera and demonstrated their continued martial strength by repelling a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) naval assault during the Ra Gada invasion in [1E 907](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_907).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Bretons-66)</sup> Nevertheless, by the Second Era, the clan had faded into a largely ceremonial role. What endured was their legacy: preserved in ancient texts, in the arcane traditions of their descendants, and in the enduring presence of the Adamantine Tower—silent and immutable, as the world changed around it.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-ESOLauriels Lament-76)</sup>

### Second Era
The Second Era was a tumultuous period for Tamriel, yet the Altmer of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) remained relatively insulated from much of the early chaos that followed the fall of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) in [2E 1](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_1) and the ensuing rise of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md). While the continent fractured into local powers during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) (beginning in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430)), the Altmer remained committed to their insular traditions and isolationist policies.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup> The Altmer continually fended off incursions from the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), including a disastrous naval expedition near [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) in [2E 486](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_486) in which the majority of the Altmeri fleet was lost.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TMOP-77)</sup>

#### The Aldmeri Dominions and Imperial Rule
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) The sigil of the First Aldmeri Dominion While mainland Tamriel experienced many problems and sagas during the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the sea insulated the Altmer of Summerset from their problems, though they had their own limited internal conflicts as well as repeated, large-scale invasions from the Sload and Maormer to deal with.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> In [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580),<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-TUQ-78)</sup> however, amid the chaos of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) that followed the fall of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), [Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) of Summerset allied with the Bosmer of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) in the Elden Accord to form the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ESOAyrenn-79)</sup> The Dominion's propaganda decried the carelessness of Men and promoted the establishment of Elven rule across Tamriel, viewing the Aldmeri races as the only peoples with the "wisdom and restraint" to be its proper stewards. To that end, the Dominion fought the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) against the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) for control of mainland Tamriel.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Welcome-80)</sup> The war's ultimate outcome is unknown, but all three alliances, as well as the Empire of Cyrodiil, had ceased to exist by the ninth century of the Second Era.

#### First Dominion
*I have no hatred for the races of Man, but they are young. Like all children, they are driven by emotion. They lack the wisdom that comes with age. I would sooner place an Altmer infant on the Ruby Throne than surrender Tamriel to their capricious whims.
The Altmer, the Bosmer and the Khajiit share the common traits of intelligence, patience and reason. We do not seek riches or plunder. Domination is not our goal, nor is the acclamation of power for its own sake.
Today we make our stand. Today we take back the Ruby Throne, which is ours by ancient right and the blessings of the Divines.
Stand with us.*
—Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri A major turning point came with the birth and later return of [Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn), who vanished in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573) and was presumed dead. Upon her dramatic reappearance in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Ayrenn claimed the throne of Alinor just as her brother, Prince Naemon, prepared to ascend. Her coronation marked the beginning of a new, outward-facing era for the Altmer. Ayrenn's experiences traveling Tamriel shaped her progressive vision, which challenged traditionalist values and prioritized strategic cooperation with neighboring provinces.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-AUQ-81)</sup> Under Ayrenn’s rule, the [Elden Accord](Lore_Elden_Accord.md) was signed, leading to the formation of the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) between the Altmer, [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Though publicly framed as a partnership of mutual aid, the Dominion's ideology promoted Altmeri supremacy, emphasizing the Elves’ perceived divine mandate to steward Tamriel.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-WTNAI-82)</sup> The Dominion quickly mobilized its military might, incorporating Altmeri battlemages, naval forces, and elite agents like the [Eyes of the Queen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eyes_of_the_Queen) into its war efforts. It joined the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) (circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582)), contesting control of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) against the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) and the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md). The Dominion's invasion route through [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March) into [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) was marked by strategic offensives and magical superiority, though the war itself devolved into protracted stalemate.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-ESO-83)</sup>

Although Ayrenn’s progressive vision won admiration among younger Altmer and allied races, it sparked fierce opposition from conservative factions within Summerset, such as the Kinlady dynasties of [Cloudrest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloudrest) and the increasingly powerful Sapiarchs of Alinor. Dissent intensified as the war dragged on and losses mounted. Prince Naemon, having reluctantly accepted a secondary role under Ayrenn, was later discovered consorting with the [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance)<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Lit ES-84)</sup><sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Tof ER-85)</sup>

#### Second Dominion
A Second Dominion was allegedly established in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830) following Altmer intervention in a dynastic dispute in Valenwood. The Second Dominion once again allied the Altmer with the Bosmer and Khajiit, but also with the Maormer — their hereditary enemies, who claim that the Altmer are a mongrel race and that the Sea Elves (Maormer) are the true descendants of the “purest strain” of Aldmeri ancestors, though this claim is highly dubious.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TCPo A-47)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-60)</sup> This expanded alliance could not stand against [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), who crushed the Dominion with the help of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) and finally brought the Summerset Isle into the Imperial fold in [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896).<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-OM-86)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-87)</sup> The Altmer remembered the conquest with shame and horror, and likely contributed to social unrest among their youth later in the Third Era.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup>

### Third Era
A notable moment of collaboration between the wider Empire and Summerset was [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md) in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), during the reign of [Emperor Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md). The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) of Pyandonea, led by [King Orgnum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgnum), launched a massive invasion of the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-87)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TDC-88)</sup> In spite of the longstanding rivalries among the Altmeri kingdoms, the threat prompted unprecedented unity. Despite this combined strength, the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) and the royal navies of Summerset could not withstand Orgnum’s fleet.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Wot I-89)</sup> Victory seemed certain for the Maormer when, off the coast of Artaeum, a freak storm arose and utterly annihilated their ships. Legends attribute the storm to the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), and this has since been widely accepted.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-87)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TWQ4-90)</sup>

Not long after the war, the Psijic Order vanished along with the Isle of Artaeum, withdrawing from worldly affairs for nearly 500 years.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup>

#### The Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/LG-cardart-Oblivion_Gate.png/200px-LG-cardart-Oblivion_Gate.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Oblivion_Gate.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Oblivion_Gate.png) An open Oblivion Gate The [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) proved an even more traumatic moment for the Altmer. As Daedra overran Tamriel, the unnatural turmoil of the seas killed many of the Altmer fleeing the mainland to Summerset, and wrecked the ports that would have received them. The Crisis devastated Tamriel and inflicted an grievous wound on the Altmer. The Crystal Tower briefly served as a stronghold for the surviving refugees in Summerset until it fell to powerful Daedric forces.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-RTI-91)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-RT-92)</sup> Its fall during the Daedric incursion not only caused widespread chaos but dealt a psychological blow to the very foundation of Altmeri identity.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-TD-93)</sup> Although Martin’s sacrifice finished the crisis, the Empire, already weakened by the Crisis and the death of Emperor [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), was unable to effectively respond on time.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-RT-92)</sup>

In the immediate aftermath of the Oblivion Crisis, power in the Isles shifted dramatically. The [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), originally an obscure bureau for the preservation of Altmeri heritage, had played an important role in the governments of the First and Second Dominions.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Aicantar-94)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-23)</sup> Following the chaos of the Crisis, an extremist cabal of Altmer supremacists now assumed this name and claimed credit for saving the Altmer from extinction using "deep and subtle magicks".<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-RTII-95)</sup> Many Altmer, disillusioned by Imperial failure and desperate for leadership, embraced Thalmor rhetoric. The faction swiftly consolidated influence through both propaganda and pragmatic crisis management, portraying themselves as the true inheritors of Altmeri destiny.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/LG-quest-Fugitives_02.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Fugitives_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Fugitives_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Fugitives_02.jpg) Soldiers of the Third Aldmeri Dominion during the Great War In [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), the re-formed Thalmor seized power in Summerset outright and deposed the Altmer monarch, renaming the province Alinor.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TGW-96)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-RTIV-97)</sup> Soon afterwards, in [4E 29](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_29), a faction friendly to the Thalmor took over Valenwood and formed a new union with the Altmer, establishing the Third Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TGW-96)</sup>

The Third Dominion proceeded to break off all contact with the Empire, and the Altmer lands remained silent to the outside world for some seventy years as the Thalmor consolidated their power.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TGW-96)</sup> A grim demonstration of the ruthlessness of the Thalmor in this pursuit – and the continuing resistance to their rule among some Altmer – was the [Night of Green Fire](Lore_Night_of_Green_Fire.md) in [4E 42](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_42), in which a group of Altmer dissidents were massacred by Thalmor mages at [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-SFasendil-98)</sup> At any rate, the Thalmor's sway continued to expand. After Nirn's moons vanished in the [Void Nights](Lore_Void_Nights.md) of [4E 98](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_98) – [100](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_100), they claimed credit for their restoration and persuaded the Khajiit of Elsweyr to ally once more with the Aldmeri Dominion in [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115). Finally, the Thalmor turned their sights on the Empire itself. From [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171) to [175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175) the Dominion fought the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) against the Empire, inflicting a series of punishing defeats that led to the signing of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md), which ceded large parts of southern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) to the Dominion, disbanded the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), and banned the worship of the man-god [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) across the Empire.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TGW-96)</sup>

With the entire province of Hammerfell having gained independence from the Empire, and the Empire itself in turmoil from a civil war in Skyrim – abetted by the Thalmor and the possible assassination of Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md)<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-Dossier-99)</sup><sup>[[97]](#cite_note-SRKill Empire-100)</sup> – the Altmer had attained a position of substantial power in Tamriel by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), though much remained unclear about the situation in Alinor itself.

The apprentice of [Balagog gro-Nolob](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balagog_gro-Nolob), Urzhag gro-Larak, visited the Summerset Isles several times around the end of the Second Century of the Fourth Era during his travels across Tamriel which saw him learn from a variety of races to improve his cooking skills. During one of these visits, he got permission with two other foreign chefs to research under some limitations in the College of Sapiarchs. Though the Isles were opened up, many of the native inhabitants were still hesitant to outsiders.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-101)</sup>

## Culture
Among the Altmer, the arts serve not merely as entertainment or expression, but as mechanisms for cultural continuity, philosophical discipline, and the formal demonstration of refinement. The ideal Altmeri artist is not an innovator but a refiner—one who improves upon ancestral works in accordance with established praxic standards.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-ESOLive Summerset-102)</sup>

### Language
The Altmer language [High Elvish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Elvish) (alternatively Altmeri,<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-Beast Galen-103)</sup> High Elven,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TDC-88)</sup><sup>:6</sup> or Elvish),<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-ESOSummerfall-104)</sup> is a direct descendant of [Aldmeris language](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md), preserved through rigorous scholarship and formal oratory.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-23)</sup> Though few beyond the borders of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) speak modern Altmeri with fluency, the continent’s common tongue, the official language of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) and the most widely spoken across modern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), known as [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md), is derived from Altmeri speech and script. Many formal, magical, and legal terms in Tamrielic are either direct borrowings or derived calques.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

Public address, legal declarations, and even private correspondence are expected to conform to syntactic traditions. The [Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Sapiarchs) are regarded as the ultimate custodians of both spoken and written purity.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot Co S-18)</sup>

### Literary Arts
Altmeri literature is dominated by philosophical treatises, genealogical chronicles, ceremonial scripts, and didactic histories. The Scrolls of Praxis, maintained by the College of Sapiarchs, serve as both a moral code and a textual anchor for all ceremonial behavior.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sot CT-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot Co S-18)</sup> Altmeri prose tends toward elevated registers and strict structural conventions. Even political or ideological writings such as [*An End to Isolation*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:An_End_to_Isolation) or [*A Rejection of Open Borders*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_Rejection_of_Open_Borders) follow the same rhetorical frameworks as religious or historical texts.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-AEt I-105)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-ARo OB-106)</sup> Subversive literature—particularly works authored by apraxics—has historically been censored or banned.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-ESOTanorian-107)</sup><sup>[[103]](#cite_note-ESOLanarie-108)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/ON-prerelease-Rellenthil.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Rellenthil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Rellenthil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Rellenthil.jpg) Amphitheatre of Rellenthil The city of [Rellenthil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rellenthil), located in central Summerset northeast of Alinor, is widely regarded as the cultural heart of Altmeri theatrical arts.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Rellenthil-109)</sup> It is best known as the home of the [House of Reveries](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Reveries), a prestigious and enigmatic troupe of performers whose influence spans the entire isle.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Ho R-110)</sup> Founded on the Altmeri ideal that artistry must transcend ego and identity, members of the House of Reveries are required to adopt masks and stage names, never revealing their birth names or personal affiliations to the public—even to each other.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Ho R-110)</sup> The House performs elaborate stage plays, which combine dance, illusion magic, lyrical narration, and thematic symbolism. These productions often center on abstract Altmeri concerns such as self-discipline, transcendence, and the tension between duty and desire.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Ho R-110)</sup>

The troupe's annual performances are considered major events in Altmer cultural life, drawing nobles, scholars, and artisans from across [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Rellenthil-109)</sup> The House also provides occasional commentary on political or philosophical trends through symbolic characters and veiled dialogue—one of the few forms of sanctioned cultural critique in an otherwise rigidly formal society.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Ho R-110)</sup><sup>[[103]](#cite_note-ESOLanarie-108)</sup> Despite their use of [illusion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion) and anonymity, Reveries are not considered subversive by Altmeri authorities. On the contrary, their adherence to tradition and their use of symbolic performance make them an exemplary model of controlled artistic expression.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Ho R-110)</sup>

Altmeri poetry is ritualized, metrically complex, and almost exclusively public in function. It is composed and recited to mark rites of passage, funerals, ascensions, and diplomatic events. Deviation from traditional meters is discouraged and often viewed as a violation of ceremonial form.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TPo P-16)</sup> Court bards and orators are not entertainers but custodians of cultural memory. They recite ancestral genealogies, mythic sequences, and didactic parables drawn from canon. Improvisation is rare and discouraged in favor of perfect recitation.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TPo P-16)</sup>

### Architecture
Altmeri cities and towns reflects their pursuit of beauty, order, symmetry perfection, aligning with the [Path to Alaxon](#Path_to_Alaxon). Structures are typically constructed from pale stone such as alabaster or coral limestone, and are characterized by elegant spires, high arches, domed ceilings, and gilded ornamentation.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup> Buildings often feature open-air courtyards, garden terraces, and magical lighting that adjusts with the time of day.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup> Imperial critics are divided on their opinions of Altmeri architecture. Some would call it "soaring and graceful" while others would say it is "repetitive and static". They have curved gables and strong, pointed steeples that emphasize height, and ceilings far higher than most buildings. The historian, Cantaber Congonius makes note of discernable similarities with Ayleid architecture, though subtle changes are less obvious.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-NOEA-20)</sup>

Architectural design is a form of praxis in itself, with each generation of Altmeri builders refining the work of their ancestors through a process of idealization and inherited aesthetic standards.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-ESOLive Summerset-102)</sup> They are resistant to large-scale changes and innovation, and are content to tinker on what is established. The end result is highly sophisticated precision, harmony, and the repeated use of orthodox compositions.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-NOEA-20)</sup> The finest structures are said to echo the divine geometries of Aetherius, and many cities are arranged according to numerological principles derived from ancestral records.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sot CT-19)</sup> Notable examples of Altmer architecture include the towers of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md), the College of Sapiarchs, and the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md).<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-RTIV-97)</sup>

### Military
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/LG-cardart-Battlereeve_of_Dusk_03.png/200px-LG-cardart-Battlereeve_of_Dusk_03.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Battlereeve_of_Dusk_03.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Battlereeve_of_Dusk_03.png) A Battlereeve of [Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dusk) commanding Altmeri forces. Altmeri [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) and armor exemplify refined craftsmanship and aesthetic philosophy, favoring graceful, organic designs inspired by birds, flowers, and seashells. Their craftsmanship is elegant and visually appealing, but still powerful.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Arms And Armor-111)</sup> Armor is often embossed with motifs resembling feathers or scales, while helms and cuirasses may be stylized with winged or beaked features.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

A particularly illustrative account of Altmeri martial culture is recorded during a mainland fighters' gathering, where the aloof, golden-skinned representative of the Isles was persuaded—through the diplomatic efforts of the Khajiiti champion, Zadabal-ra—to attend. He arrived not alone but with a caravan of gleaming shields, winged helms, and spears consecrated to [Phynaster the Guardian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phynaster). While the Altmer offered extensive commentary on the martial deficiencies of the mainlanders—provoking the ire of a Nord champion—tempers were ultimately eased by mead and rigorous sparring. It was in this combat that the soaring, swan-necked elegance of Altmeri weaponry was put to the test.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Arms And Armor-111)</sup>

Forged from rare minerals and exotic ores sourced from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), their weapons reflect the same intricacy found in Altmeri speech. Even the most humble Altmer disdains the use of iron or steel, viewing such materials as crude and provincial. Instead, they favor complex forging processes involving refined crystalline elements.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Arms And Armor-111)</sup>

Among these, volcanic glass, formed from resins and mineral deposits, is especially prized. Weapons made from this glass are well-balanced, milky green in hue, and unexpectedly durable. Despite their apparent fragility, such blades are effective in battle, though they require frequent and careful sharpening to maintain their edge. The Altmer further enhance their arms through embellishments and enchantments. One might encounter a long blade inlaid with shimmering glass, crowned with a golden pommel, and etched with eagle wings or avian heads. Their bows, noted for their elegant winged curves, have even been likened in shape to a maiden's thigh.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Arms And Armor-111)</sup> The long bow, in particular, is historically associated with Altmer aristocrats.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-MWGarothmuk-112)</sup> Rather than use leather from wild beasts, the Altmer breed [guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guar) specifically for their pliable hides, ensuring each component of their weaponry adheres to the standards of controlled refinement.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Arms And Armor-111)</sup> Altmeri weapons often reflect the physical and cultural characteristics of their wielders—elongated, barbed, and richly gilded. The result is a striking combination of delicate beauty and deadly function.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Arms And Armor-111)</sup>

In addition to their traditional forces, the Altmer have at times employed unconventional auxiliaries. During certain periods, goblins were trained under Altmeri direction to support their armies.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-Fedris-113)</sup>

Naval warfare has long been a domain of Altmeri excellence.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TMo P-46)</sup> Faced with repeated [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) incursions during the First and Second Eras, the Altmer constructed a formidable fleet. Although their maritime dominance had waned by the time of the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md), necessitating support from the [Psijics](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), their naval tradition remained deeply respected. Altmeri ships, like their blades, are often described as slender and swan-necked—embodying the same principles of elegance, precision, and lethality.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Arms And Armor-111)</sup> The southern city of [Sunhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunhold) holds the largest fleet in the province and has a reputation of being a capable fortress-city.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-ONHelenaere-114)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-OBIAG-115)</sup> King [Orgnum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgnum) of Pyandonea, who had long sought after the Summerset Isles, had his eyes particularly on the city.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-TKOVC17-116)</sup> Under the First Aldmeri Dominion, Sunhold was their navy's home port.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-ONLoad Sunhold-117)</sup>

### Dueling
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/OB-concept-04.jpg/200px-OB-concept-04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-concept-04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-concept-04.jpg) Elven weaponry Altmer who find themselves in disagreement typically prefer to resolve such matters through dialogue rather than violence. Extended conversation, contemplation, and the sharing of wine and fellowship are culturally favored methods of dispute resolution. However, if discussion fails to yield a mutually satisfactory conclusion, the matter may be settled through a formal duel.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-Guide Altmer Cult-118)</sup>

#### Rules of Dueling
The traditional rules of Altmeri dueling are as follows:

- Combatants may only arm themselves with a traditional Altmeri dueling foil. The use of [longswords](Lore_Weapons.md#Blades), [axes](Lore_Weapons.md#Axes), [flails](Lore_Weapons.md#Blunt_Weapons), or similar weapons is strictly prohibited. The foil must be washed in mineral water, thoroughly dried, and polished to a fine sheen.

- Before the duel begins, each participant must recite the full name of their opponent, optionally referencing relevant past misdeeds. If a combatant mispronounces or stumbles over the name, they are required to pause and repeat it in full two more times.

- A neutral witness must be present to observe the duel. This individual is honor-bound to intervene should either participant commit a violation of dueling etiquette, such as fleeing the field, using dishonorable tactics (e.g., throwing dirt), or harassing the opponent beyond the bounds of the engagement.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-Guide Altmer Cult-118)</sup>

The victor of the duel is expected to show humility and courtesy toward the defeated, providing medical aid, comfort, and any necessary support for recovery. In return, the defeated must formally acknowledge their error and request forgiveness. Through this mutual exchange, both parties preserve their honor—a concept regarded by the Altmer as the highest ideal on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-Guide Altmer Cult-118)</sup>

### Symbolism
Altmeri visual art emphasizes symmetry, divine geometry, and cosmological motifs. Civic and sacred buildings are adorned with murals and mosaics depicting scenes from Altmeri myth, particularly the ascension of [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), the construction of the Crystal Tower, and key moments in Aldmeri history. Art is not an individual endeavor: many major commissions—especially in temples or public spaces—must be approved by guilds or Sapiarchic oversight. Common symbols include latticework patterns representing the Aurbis or cosmic order; starlight and rays indicating alignment with Aetherius; mirrored forms expressing balance and ancestral symmetry.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot Co S-18)</sup>

The Altmer use the eagle both in association with Auri-El himself,<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Cont Driftwood-119)</sup><sup>[[115]](#cite_note-Cont Winged Auriel-120)</sup> and as a symbol representing the Aedra, their ancestors, who descended from [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) and became trapped in physical form by the creation of Nirn.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-ESOKB-121)</sup>. Altmeri prayers reference Auri-El as the Fixed Center of existence who always envelops all people with his "scales and fangs and flame", and invoke him to grant his followers stability.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-COTF-122)</sup>

These symbols are also echoed in [architecture](#Architecture) and religious iconography, reinforcing the unity of Altmeri aesthetics across disciplines.

### Food
→ *For more detailed information, see: [Altmer Cuisine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Altmer_Cuisine)* Being isolated in the Summerset Isles has allowed the Altmer to perfect their own cuisine without much interference from the outside races. When they do open their shores to the other races, they will embrace their culinary contributions. Among themselves, Altmer culinary tastes may differ depending on where they are from as those from one area may not agree with those from another on who has the best take on their cuisine.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-CCV1-123)</sup>

The Altmer have strict rules when it comes to table manners and deportment<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-TESOC-124)</sup> with several details and rules about how to behave at the dinner table. Some of these rules include refilling the glass of the diner to your left and never refilling your own, never resting utensils on the edge of any vessel, not eating more than the most prestigious attendee at any function, always allowing the head of your table to lead the discussion, never watching another while they chew, and never using the wrong utensil as it is considered barbaric. They believe it is better to watch and follow the example of the other guests than to make such a mistake.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-PPS-125)</sup> Rules around food and drink in Summerset can be similarly strict as well. Altmeri beverages must all be brewed according to the "rules of conformance"<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-126)</sup> and being caught with any drinks that don't follow the laws can cause one to run afoul of the [Divine Prosecution](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divine_Prosecution) and lead to possible imprisonment.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-127)</sup>

Seafood is commonplace on Summerset Isle, which is surrounded by sparkling seas. There is no aversion to raw meat and several Altmeri dishes contain raw [fish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fish), however Altmer will never consume anything that is alive and there are certain animals that are not to be eaten such as rodents.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-CCV1-123)</sup>

## Society

### Social Structure and Hierarchy
[![ON-statue-Alinor Kinlord Statue 02.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/ON-statue-Alinor_Kinlord_Statue_02.png/180px-ON-statue-Alinor_Kinlord_Statue_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Alinor_Kinlord_Statue_02.png) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/OB-concept-04.jpg/300px-OB-concept-04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-concept-04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-concept-04.jpg) Elven weaponry At the time the Crystal Tower was built in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the Elves of Summerset had a relatively egalitarian society, though social stratification slowly developed into a rigid hierarchy of classes over the millennia.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> The Wise (teachers and priests) became the highest echelon, followed by Artists, Princes, Warriors, Landowners, Merchants, Workers, and enslaved peoples such as [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> Early literature by man painted the High Elves in poor light, though the impartiality of these accounts is questionable. It was suggested that the Altmer practiced eugenic infanticide, putting to death nine in ten of their newborns due to impurities, that [Altmeri names](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Altmer_Names) are simply combinations of numbers in their own language, and that Altmer culture revolves around displaying social status.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-60)</sup> Some of this has basis in fact; Altmer born with deformities or impurities are often – though not always<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Tuttle Amalien-139)</sup> – abandoned by their parents as "hulkynd" (sometimes to be raised by priests of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md)),<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-ONManacar-140)</sup> and the full name of an Altmer is relatively extensive, detailing their patrilineal and matrilineal histories in addition to the history of their clan.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONFamily Shrine-17)</sup>

##### Praxis
At an Altmer's coming of age, they are granted a calian, a sphere of [aetherquartz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherquartz) crystal representing their honor and social status.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TPo P-16)</sup><sup>[[135]](#cite_note-141)</sup> The aetherquartz in calian spheres is polished using ground pumice and ocean sand.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-142)</sup> Calians are considered to be "praxic talismans".<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-ESOMo R-143)</sup> Mer who have committed some particularly grievous crime against Altmer law are termed "ousters" and "apraxics". While ousters are simply banished from their homes,<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-144)</sup> apraxics are treated more severely: their calians are ceremonially shattered by a curate and it is wholly forbidden for Altmer in good standing to speak to them.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-ESOLanarie-108)</sup><sup>[[137]](#cite_note-ESOMo R-143)</sup> Some apraxics labor for years to restore their status, as part of which they must painstakingly repair their own calian,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TPo P-16)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-ESODecanter-145)</sup> while others choose to remain in exile. Nonetheless, in the middle of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) subversive literature written by aprax mer gained wide currency among young Altmer on Summerset Isle,<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-ESOLanarie-108)</sup> and provoked some of them to engage in revolutionary activity.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-ESOMo R-143)</sup> This questioning of Altmer customs coincided with reforms introduced by Queen Ayrenn after the founding of the First Aldmeri Dominion, including the end of Summerset's longstanding isolation and the opening of its borders to other races and cultures, which provoked heated debate and political maneuvering.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-AEt I-105)</sup><sup>[[140]](#cite_note-ACf OB-146)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-ARo OB-106)</sup>

##### Alaxon
One of the dominant philosophies of the Altmer of Summerset is the "Path to Alaxon". Alaxon is the state of perfection that every High Elf strives for. The Path to Alaxon represents the approach Altmer take to achieve that state. Practice of the Path requires intense dedication and concentrated effort.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOOlnewil-15)</sup> Those who are judged to have attained a degree of perfection are granted an alaxon sphere, which, like calians, may be destroyed if they are judged to have fallen from it.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Cot DP-147)</sup> The Path to Alaxon is reflected in Summerset's characteristic architecture, which builds on the aesthetic practice of past architects in a sequential process of refinement.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-twitch1-148)</sup>

### Law and Government
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fc/ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg/200px-ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg) The College of Sapiarchs Altmer society was highly stratified by the middle of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The monarch of Summerset ruled as sovereign with the advice of the [College of Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Sapiarchs), an elite and highly selective scholarly institute,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot Co S-18)</sup> and was sometimes represented by a viceroy titled the Proxy Queen or King.<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-ESOValsirenn-149)</sup> The monarch descended from an almost continuous line of royalty extending back to the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-ESOPalace-150)</sup> Their behavior was regulated by a set of written precedents known as the Scrolls of Praxis, whose chief interpreters were the Sapiarchs: these formed part of a broader system of inherited norms termed the Ceremoniarchy.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sot CT-19)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-TUQ-78)</sup> The heir to the throne was required to study the Ceremoniarchy for 3,555 days in the Sapiarchs' Labyrinth before they could take the crown,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ot Co S-18)</sup> and royal decrees were issued in its name.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-AEt I-105)</sup> Hereditary nobles termed kinlords and kinladies held many of the Altmer lands in fief. Altmer royalty were assisted by lieutenants titled vicereeves,<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-ESOPelidil-151)</sup> while cities that served as county seats were administered by canonreeves, civil officials equivalent to mayors in other cultures.<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-ESOElquisa-152)</sup>

The [Divine Prosecution](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divine_Prosecution) administers secular and religious justice,<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-TDP-153)</sup> and is made up of jurisreeves overseeing investigations and rank-and-file justiciars. Jurisreeves travel and deliberate in groups of three, each with distinct responsibilities: a revelator-naganwe to conduct magical inquiries, an admanen to serve as an inspector, and a thalmilan who would weigh the evidence and render a verdict.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-ESOLorne-154)</sup> The jurisreeves may have originated as a religious order.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-ESOEye-155)</sup> Under [Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn)'s [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), executive responsibilities were increasingly assumed by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), originally a bureau for the protection of Altmeri heritage.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Aicantar-94)</sup> The Thalmor incorporated the Divine Prosecution as a division at that time,<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-ESOCarawen-156)</sup> but the Prosecution existed long before the Thalmor's rise to power.<sup>[[150]](#cite_note-ESODaiyanni-157)</sup>

The Altmer stringently regulate traffic into Summerset, and they have banned the import of such foreign goods as books, foods and perfumes in the past.<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-ESORenzir-158)</sup> At the time of the First Aldmeri Dominion, the roster of forbidden books included mundane foreign writings judged to be "coarse", but the Divine Prosecution devoted particular attention to the suppression of literature promoting Daedra worship.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-ESOTanorian-107)</sup>

Altmer politics was transformed in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) by the return of the Thalmor as a militant Elven supremacist group following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). In the wake of the chaos caused by the Crisis and the fall of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), the Thalmor overthrew the Summerset monarchy in a coup d'état.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-RTIV-97)</sup> Since then, officers of the Thalmor have taken direct charge of government functions such as diplomacy and espionage.<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-Skyrim DI-159)</sup>

### Dissidence and Reform
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1b/LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Thalmor_Justiciar_02.jpg) A Fourth Era Thalmor justiciar As mentioned, whenever a substantial minority had some sort of socio-political or theological disagreement with the majority in Summerset in the Dawn Era, the dissidents would leave and usually go on to become other races of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md). However, some time in the Merethic Era, one early group of dissidents who opposed the evolution to the traditional Altmer pantheon left the mainland of Summerset but remained quintessentially Altmer. The [Psijics](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) decided to follow the mystical Old Ways of Aldmeris and broke away to settle [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), the third-largest island of the Isle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-87)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup> Though they have often refused requests for dialogue from Alinor during their periods of seclusion from Tamriel,<sup>[[153]](#cite_note-TMo A-160)</sup> the Psijics are not hostile to the other Altmer, and have offered their magic to protect Summerset in the past.<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-ESOTS-161)</sup> A leader of the Psijics, [Ritemaster Iachesis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iachesis), led them through the entire First Era and most of the Second, a span of some 3,500 years, giving some indication of the potential longevity of the Altmer.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FOA-11)</sup><sup>[[155]](#cite_note-2920L-162)</sup><sup>[[156]](#cite_note-ESOLi T-163)</sup>

A subsequent bout of dissent occurred late in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), as younger Altmer again began pushing for social reform. These episodes were unusual in a society used to dissidents simply leaving.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> But there were few if any places left to go at that point. Some change had taken place by the end of the Third Era: the Altmer once more allowed foreigners to come to their shores; some non-Altmer were even allowed to rise to the ranks of nobility in Summerset.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> Many of these young rebellious Altmer turned to the Psijics for guidance, somewhat ironically consulting their distant past for a path to their future, while some militant radicals turned to terrorism.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup>

These tentative reforms were substantially reversed at the turn of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) by the rise of the Thalmor, who instituted a policy of persecution and even open slaughter of dissidents and non-Elven races.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-RTIV-97)</sup> Numerous surviving Altmer dissidents were forced to flee to other parts of Tamriel, where they were frequently targeted by Thalmor assassins.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-SFasendil-98)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-RTIV-97)</sup> While many Altmer continue to disagree with the Thalmor in private, few venture to oppose them publicly.<sup>[[157]](#cite_note-BTelarendil-164)</sup>

## Subcultures
Altmer culture, has different subcultures throughout the regions of Tamriel.<sup>[[158]](#cite_note-Ho CC-165)</sup>

### Ancients
The [Ancients](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancients) are the ancestors of the Altmer of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), the second largest island in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) archipelago. They are responsible for the various limestone ruins that dot the island. The term is used by the natives to refer to both the early [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) and more recent Altmer ancestors.

The ruins of the Ancients are similar in style to the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Ruins) found on mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). However, Altmer architecture is cleaner and more direct, with less baroque ornamentation than that of the Ayleids. Aside from their shared ancestry, the reason for this similarity is due to Altmeri propensity for refinement over innovation, and a resistance to large-scale change. As well as [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) [Welkynd Stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welkynd_Stones), these ruins also contain [Malondo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malondo_Stone) and [Culanda Stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Culanda_Stone), modern inventions of the Altmer which were not utilized by the Ayleids. The Ancients are also known to have harnessed the power of [Meteoric Iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meteoric_Iron) to create Aetherial Wells, magical relics similar to the Ayleid Wells found on the continent.

The ruins of Torinaan are named after High Lord [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan_(person).md), an early Aldmer settler from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), and are an apparent example of Aldmer ruins. [Tanzelwil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tanzelwil) and [Castle Rilis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castle_Rilis), on the other hand, serve as mausoleums for modern Altmer. Other ruins on the island include Ezduiin, the [Buraniim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Buraniim) sea-complex, Quendeluun, and the Eye of the Ancients.

The term "Ancients" is also sometimes used to refer to the Ayleids on mainland Tamriel.

### Corelanyans
→ *For more detailed information, see: [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md)* The [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) (also known as **House Corelanya**, **Corelanyan High Elves**, **Corelanyan Elves**, or simply the **Corelanyas** or **Corelanyans**) is a group of Altmer who reside on the island of [Solstice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Solstice), primarily in the city of [Sunport](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunport), located south of mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They follow a divergent form of [Velothi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Velothi) traditions, having chosen to remain behind during [Saint Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) ’s exodus from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) rather than join him. Eventually, the Corelanyans were exiled and relocated to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) around the [sixth century of the First Era](Lore_First_Era.md#Sixth_Century). There, they founded coastal settlements, often repurposing and expanding upon structures originally built by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md). However, around [1E 853](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_853), the invading [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) forced them to abandon the mainland. They then settled on the island the native [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) called Oztet-Ta (“Sprouting Root” in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md)); while this name persisted among the Argonians, the Corelanyans came to call it Solstice, with one scholarly interpretation claiming that it reflects the their connection to the Three Queens, with [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia) as Day, [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) as Night, and [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) as the Dawn and Dusk that bridge them.

### Direnni
→ *For more detailed information, see: [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md)* *Few families in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) can boast so many famous figures, wielding so much power over the fate of so many. Our warriors and kings are stuff of legend, and it is not to dismiss their honor and their achievements to say you have heard quite enough about them.*
—Vorian Direnni, [De Rerum Dirennis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:De_Rerum_Dirennis) The [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) (also known as **Clan Direnni** and **House Direnni**) are an aristocratic merchant clan of Altmer<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> (formerly [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md)) whose members laid the foundation for modern [alchemy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy), [conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration), and [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md). The Direnni are most credited as being the [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) ancestors of the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), a race produced by their intermingling with the [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) for centuries during the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). The Direnni established themselves as a powerful sovereignty referred to as the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md) which rose to prominence around [1E 355](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_355), but some consider their rule in the years leading up to its establishment as part of the Direnni Hegemony as well. The Hegemony fractured around [1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498), with one source claiming that the Direnni remained dominant until the middle of the First Era, during which the whole of High Rock was commonly called the Direnni Hegemony. At its peak, this hegemony stretched from all of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) into large parts of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

## Religion and Cosmology
The religious worldview of the Altmer is deeply rooted in their belief that they are the direct descendants of the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra), or "ancestor spirits"—divine beings who gave their essence to shape the mortal world.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AD-4)</sup> According to Altmeri myth, the cosmos began before the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), when the primordial force [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu), representing stasis and order, brought forth [Anuiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuiel), the soul of all things. Anuiel then created [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis), the personification of change and limitation, so that it might know itself.<sup>[[159]](#cite_note-S-166)</sup> The interplay of Anuiel and Sithis gave rise to the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md), the chaotic void in which the Original Spirits-that would come to be known as Aedra and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) —emerged.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup>

*[E]ven Auriel, when told he would become the king of the new world, agreed to help Lorkhan.*
— [The Heart of the World](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Monomyth) The Altmer interpret the creation of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) as a cosmic catastrophe initiated by [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), the "Trickster God," who persuaded some spirits to participate in creating a flawed, mortal plane. This act is seen as a spiritual fall from the divine to the temporal, severing the [Elves](Lore_Aldmer.md) from their ancestral eternity.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> The emergence of the elves is described in the Altmeri creation myth, according to which some of the Aedra that stayed to work on Nirn after [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus) departed, known also as the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-31)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Mystery-29)</sup> chose to have offspring. The ensuing offspring grew progressively weaker than their parents with each generation, a phenomenon of generational diminishment that soon led to the emergence of the first Aldmer.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), the chief deity of the Altmeri pantheon and the soul of Anuiel, from whom most modern Altmer claim direct descent, is said to have opposed Lorkhan, led the faithful Aedra against him, and eventually ascended to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), becoming a model of apotheosis for his followers.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOVairabrian-50)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/ON-map-Summerset_%28physical%29.jpg/200px-ON-map-Summerset_%28physical%29.jpg)](File_ON-map-Summerset_(physical).jpg.md) [](File_ON-map-Summerset_(physical).jpg.md) An Altmeri depiction of Summerset watched over (from left to right) by Xarxes, Auri-El, Mara, and Y'ffre/Jephre, capped by the Crystal Tower Over time, social stratification influenced religious practice. The lower classes increasingly adopted the ancestor spirits claimed by their social superiors over their own, contributing to the consolidation of a more centralized pantheon, comprised of figures such as Auri-El, Trinimac, Syrabane, and Phynaster, who were among those ancestor spirits that came to be considered gods. A shift in religion opposed by some of the Aldmeri elders, who broke off to form the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) in protest.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> Despite this convergence, debates persist over which beings are truly worthy of veneration, and dissenters—especially those sympathetic to [Men](Lore_Men.md) or heterodox figures like [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) —are often marginalized or persecuted.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TGW-96)</sup> Altmer view themselves as "true children of the Et'Ada", descendants of the Aedric Divines in an unbroken line dating back to Nirn's creation, a divine lineage which is thought to allow them to achieve [Apotheosis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lifespan) in certain circumstances.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-OATO-168)</sup><sup>[[162]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-169)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TAX-27)</sup> The ultimate spiritual aspiration of the Altmer is thus to transcend mortality and return to the divine state of their ancestors, following the example of [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El) and the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra). In this, their religion serves not only as a cultural anchor but as a metaphysical roadmap back to the [stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOVairabrian-50)</sup> The principal Altmeri pantheon consists of [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus), [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), [Y’ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre) (also called [Jephre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jephre)), [Xarxes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xarxes), [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara), [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), and [Syrabane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syrabane).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-VOFHE-25)</sup> Additional cults also revere deities such as [Phynaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phynaster), [Oghma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma) and, controversially, Lorkhan.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-VOFHE-25)</sup> However, limitations to eight or nine deities are considered an [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) imposition and are generally rejected by traditionalist Altmer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> The Corelanya Clan has its own unique pantheon as well.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup>

### The Eight

#### [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/ON-statue-Auriel.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Auriel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auriel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Auriel.jpg) An Altmeri statue of Auri-El (ESO) **Auri-El**, also known as the King of the Aldmer<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup>, is the chief deity<sup>[[164]](#cite_note-Auri Chief CB-171)</sup> of the Altmeri pantheon. He is said to be the soul of Anui-El and time began with his existence.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> Altmer creation myth says that in his only ever moment of weakness, he took part in creation. Out of regret, he rallied the Aldmer against the armies of men and Lorkhan to victory.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> Most Altmer claim descent from the King of Aldmer<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> and worship of the god takes many forms, including various trinkets and altars which are often gilded or themed after birds.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Auri CB-172)</sup> Veneration statuettes are similarly used<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-Auri Statuette-173)</sup>. Other knick-knacks like time keeping devices or children games are similarly themed after the chief deity.<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-Auri Misc CB-174)</sup> Altmeri prayers invoke him as the "Fixed Center", beckoning him for Divine stability in their lives,<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-COTF-122)</sup> and the Hand of Auri-El guides them through life and from harm.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-Mo Auriel-175)</sup> In Balfiera, worshippers of the god see [aurielic quasigriffs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quasigriff) as sacred.<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-AQuasi-176)</sup> Songs devoted to the Divines describe the god as the "wise, noble, and tall" forefather of all.<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-PB-177)</sup>

#### [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus)
The God of Sorcery, **Magnus**, is credited the Altmer for the blueprints and diagrams needed to create the mortal plane. His escape from creation, at the very last moment, cost him and what remained of him on Nirn is wielded by mortals as magic.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup><sup>[[171]](#cite_note-TGo Mag-178)</sup> Venerations to Magnus invoke his [Eye](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md) and the [Empyrean Light](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) as a request for his wisdom and patience.<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-TGo Mag-178)</sup> His Eye is similarly called upon to watch over spells High Elves cast.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-Mo Auriel-175)</sup> Altmer invoke him in sign-offs.<sup>[[172]](#cite_note-Letters MG-179)</sup>

#### [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac)
**Trinimac**, otherwise known as the Paragon<sup>[[173]](#cite_note-Trini Statuette-180)</sup>, is the champion of Auri-El and the Altmeri pantheon.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TA-42)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TTNOO-41)</sup><sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> Creation myth places him at the front lines of the conflict with Lorkhan, and as the one who ripped out the Heart of the trickster god.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> Religious sayings mention the Arm of Trinimac protecting the Altmer in their darkest hours.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-Mo Auriel-175)</sup> Worshippers of the god use veneration idols like statuettes and golden masks.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-Trini CB-181)</sup>

#### [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre)
The God of the Forest, **Y'ffre** is recognized by the Altmer as the one who became the first Ehlnofey, creating the laws of nature and allowing mortals safety through understanding of the world.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup><sup>[[175]](#cite_note-BLto Ald-182)</sup> Prayers to Y'ffre praise the god as the one who showed the Altmer people the way, and whose Bones give them "food, shelter, warmth, and companionship"<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-PB-177)</sup><sup>[[168]](#cite_note-Mo Auriel-175)</sup> Altmer see [Indriks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indrik) as divine beings woven by the god<sup>[[176]](#cite_note-YSparkle-183)</sup>, and have formed a group of [paladins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jephrine_Paladins) modeled after the creatures.<sup>[[177]](#cite_note-ECEBR-184)</sup> Knowledge of the god extends to cultural splits of the Altmer, such as Clan Corelanya, who show interest in the archipelago's ways.<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-Yffre CB-185)</sup> Some [House of Reveries](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Reveries) members wear masks inspired by Y'ffre, symbolizing his tales- be it nature and growth, or his shaping of creation.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-FVMask-186)</sup><sup>[[180]](#cite_note-BYMask-187)</sup>

#### [Xarxes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xarxes)
**Xarxes** is the God of Ancestry and Secret Knowledge. He was Auri-El's scribe and keeps track of all Aldmeri feats since the start of time. This extends to not just individual stories, but all connections and lineage that bind the Altmer people together. It is this way that Xarxes has a steadfast role in defining Summerset society.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TAX-27)</sup><sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> It is said the Mouth of Xarxes whispers in the ears of Altmer, encouraging them to always seek, learn and discover.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-Mo Auriel-175)</sup> Prayer songs to him praise him as the "scriver" and "archivist of glory" for transcribing Altmeri story.<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-PB-177)</sup> He is said to have created his wife [Oghma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma) from his favorite moments in history.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup><sup>[[181]](#cite_note-Xand O-188)</sup> Worshippers show their devotion through idols such as statuettes.<sup>[[182]](#cite_note-Xarxes Statuette-189)</sup> He is also credited with authoring the Aldmeriad, the Elven origin epic, and instructing the Altmer to create [Oghmas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma) —everscriven scrolls that memorialize their lives as sacred duty in honor of their inherited divine spark.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-OATO-168)</sup>

#### [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara)
**Mara** is the Goddess of Love and Fertility and the wife of Auri-El. She has been associated with [Nir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods_N#Nir).<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> Her Heart is said to beat within each mer, connecting them all by blood and spirit.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-Mo Auriel-175)</sup><sup>[[175]](#cite_note-BLto Ald-182)</sup> Songs in her name pray to her to be blessed with children, and refer to her as the mother above.<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-PB-177)</sup> The Sapiarchs hold a ceremony in her name, the Divine Reverence to Mara, which lasts for at least fifty-eight days. This tradition started due to a pilgrimage to [Archon's Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Archon%27s_Grove) where the flowers Rose-of-Archon were discovered. This lead the pilgrim to a realization about Mara, that all she is the beauty found in all beings.<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-Ro Archon.5D.5D-190)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/LO-people-Mara.jpg/200px-LO-people-Mara.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Mara.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Mara.jpg) An Altmeri depiction of Mara (ESO)
#### [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md)
The God of Righteous Rule and Lord of Compassion, **Stendarr** is also known as the apologist of Men. He's praised for his compassion towards "lesser ken".<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup><sup>[[170]](#cite_note-PB-177)</sup> In the city of [Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lillandril), a cult to the god known as the [Sect of Harmonious Masters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sect_of_Harmonious_Masters) was formed. They believed that in all of Stendarr's mercy his gifts should be shared with all, especially those "less fortunate in heritage". Despite cults like this, some Altmer disdained the god still, due to his title as the apologist of Men.<sup>[[184]](#cite_note-Rituals Master-191)</sup>

#### [Syrabane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syrabane)
**Syrabane**, otherwise known as the Warlock's God, was an Aldmeri god-ancestor of magic who participated in the [Fall of the Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fall_of_the_Sload) and saved countless from the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md). He, sometimes She<sup>[[185]](#cite_note-Xarxes Gender-192)</sup>, became revered as a God as Altmer society developed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-5)</sup> Some Altmer claim them as a student of Magnus, whilst others see him as a facet of the god entirely.<sup>[[186]](#cite_note-ILSA-193)</sup> Altmer songs about Syrabane speak of his role in stopping the Thrassian Plague, and refer to them as the youngest of the Eight Divines.<sup>[[187]](#cite_note-Tale Syra-194)</sup> He's claimed by the Altmer as the one to have taught their people how to thread silver for magical jewelry,<sup>[[188]](#cite_note-Silver Strand-195)</sup><sup>[[189]](#cite_note-Syra Ward-196)</sup> and he's credited with the discovery of [Frog-Metal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frog-Metal).<sup>[[190]](#cite_note-ONStranglers-197)</sup> A holy relic to him was stored in Torinaan.<sup>[[191]](#cite_note-SSM-198)</sup><sup>[[192]](#cite_note-Relic_Rescue-199)</sup><sup>[[193]](#cite_note-MN-200)</sup> Worshippers show their devotion through idols such as statuettes.<sup>[[194]](#cite_note-Syra Statuette-201)</sup>

### Other Gods

#### [Phynaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phynaster)
**Phynaster**, also known as the Guardian, Preserver and the Wayfarer<sup>[[195]](#cite_note-Aa Aot ACF-202)</sup><sup>[[196]](#cite_note-Treasure PD-203)</sup><sup>[[197]](#cite_note-Treasure WLB-204)</sup> is the hero-god of the Altmer, who taught them how to prolong their lives by shortening their stride.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup> Phynaster's followers are known to take often times dangerous pilgrimages in honor of the god, and his cult petitions adventurers and [justiciars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divine_Prosecution) to recover articles of faith from fallen pilgrims.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-ESOTanorian-107)</sup> Devotional trinkets to the Preserver often take the form of charms, boots and dolls- the latter of which invoke his likeness to keep children safe when away from home.<sup>[[198]](#cite_note-CBPhynaster-205)</sup> His name is also invoked by sailors and marines for guidance and protection.<sup>[[199]](#cite_note-ASGTSE-206)</sup><sup>[[200]](#cite_note-ESOLandare-207)</sup> Similarly, those starting a new journey beckon him for guidance as well.<sup>[[201]](#cite_note-ESOTalomar-208)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-item-Shrine_to_Phynaster.jpg/200px-ON-item-Shrine_to_Phynaster.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shrine_to_Phynaster.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shrine_to_Phynaster.jpg) An Altmeri idol of Phynaster (ESO)
#### [Oghma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma)
**Oghma** is the wife of Xarxes, created from his favourite moments in history<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup><sup>[[181]](#cite_note-Xand O-188)</sup>, and is associated with the Altmer tradition of keeping records of their lives.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-OATO-168)</sup> Her name is also used to describe a type of journals known as the everscriven scrolls.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-OATO-168)</sup><sup>[[202]](#cite_note-CBOghma-209)</sup>

#### [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)
This Creator-Trickster-Tester deity is in every [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) mythic tradition. His most popular name is the Aldmeri **Lorkhan**, or Doom Drum. He convinced or contrived the [Original Spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods#Types_of_Gods) to bring about the creation of the mortal plane, upsetting the status quo—much like his father [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) had introduced instability into the universe in the [Beginning Place](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beginning_Place). After the world is materialized, Lorkhan is separated from his [divine center](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), sometimes involuntarily, and wanders the creation of the Et'Ada. He and his metaphysical placement in the "scheme of things" is interpreted a variety of ways. To the High Elves, he is the most unholy of all higher powers, as he forever broke their connection to the spirit plane. In the legends, he is almost always an enemy of the Aldmer and, therefore, a hero of early Mankind.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-VOF-167)</sup>

### Clan Corelanya
The Corelanyas have a unique religious tradition, being descended from Altmer who embraced the dissident teachings of the prophet Veloth. While most of the Elves who followed Veloth eventually came to venerate the living gods of the Tribunal, the Corelanyas (like the Ashlanders of Morrowind) continue to revere Daedric powers. While the Corelanyas of old had dealings with a number of Daedric powers, during their long exile on Solstice their faith coalesced around three particular Daedra: [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura), and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). The Elves of Solstice refer to them as the Queen of Light, the Queen of Dusk, and the Queen of Darkness. These Three Queens reign over all aspects of life, both the good and the bad.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup> They have a cyclical faith, believing that the nature of their clan is the circle of history itself. Dawn, Dusk and rinse and repeat- it's a cycle they see as a beautiful truth in a difficult world. They believe their Three Queens are allied in support of their clan, though their Daedric allegiences between one another is a subject of debate in the religion.<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-Solstice LA-210)</sup> The Corelanyas have various worship trinkets and idols for the Three Queens, such as ivory carved icons, prayer songs and ornate washbasins.<sup>[[204]](#cite_note-CBThree Queen-211)</sup>

Besides the gods listed below, many others are worshipped- The open nature of Clan Corelanya shows itself in freedom of religion, as there are little restrictions or prohibitions on what one can worship enforced by the Regents.<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-ESOLucilla Grantho-212)</sup> Some of these other gods include [Zenithar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zenithar) who was introduced by Imperial traders, and found popularity among the merchants of [Sunport](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunport), whilst [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor) and [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) found reverence among Nord settlers- although the worship of Shor is considered somewhat uncomfortable by the Corelanyas.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup> The Corelanyas, particularly the Collegium Praxis, also find interest in studying the native [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) religions of [The Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Stone) and [The Tide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Tide)<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup>, as well as Summerset religious practices.<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-Yffre CB-185)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cathedral_of_the_Three_Queens_02.jpg) An shrine to the Three Queens (ESO)
#### [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia)
**Meridia** is the Queen of Light, and is said to grant each mortal potential and purpose. As the goddess of light, life and order- she is seen as the arbiter of all things as they are meant to be. The Corelanyas Elves strive to live up to her perfection, even in the face of certain failure.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup> Despite this adoration, even High Priests can sometimes doubt her judgement, with Meridia's [purification](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Purified) recently seen as controversial.<sup>[[206]](#cite_note-ESOEraamine-213)</sup>

#### [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)
**Nocturnal** is the Queen of Darkness, and gathers mortals into her embrace at the end of their lives. Judging them in the afterlife, she presides of death and oversees final justice. She's not loved by the Corelanyas, but respected for her sterness.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup>

#### [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura)
**Azura** is the Queen of Dusk, and is known to comfort and guide mortals through life. She's a intermediary between the other Queens and their mortal followers. The Corelanyas see her as a goddess of beauty, fortune and love.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup>

#### [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine)
**Sanguine** is the Prince of the Revelry, and due to the laid back and easy-going attitudes of the Corelanyas has found great acceptance among the people.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Vo FS-170)</sup> His worship was seemingly endorsed at some points, with his Everlasting Fair featured in official visitor's guides to the island commissioned by the Sunport regency.<sup>[[207]](#cite_note-VGTSolstice-214)</sup>

#### [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)

### Religious Culture & History

#### The Divine Body
The Altmer view their gods as parts of a "divine body", often articulated in religious teachings as follows: the Hand of Auri-El, Eye of Magnus, Arm of Trinimac, Bones of Y’ffre, Mouth of Xarxes, and Heart of Mara. Altmer theology holds that through devotion, they remain spiritually united with these divine aspects.<sup>[[209]](#cite_note-ESOUurkar-216)</sup>

#### The Sacred Numbers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/53/ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg/200px-ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg) The Garden of the Sacred Numbers(ESO) Sacred Numbers in Altmeri numerology include [three](Lore_Numerology.md), five, and eight, each reflecting metaphysical ideals of harmony and balance. Three represents the "Prime Celestials", the [sun](Lore_Sun.md) and the [moons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons). Five the number of the [Elements](Lore_Elements.md): Earth, Air, Water, Fire and Aether. Eight for the eight [Planets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planets) as well as the sum of three and five. These "Good Numbers" all summed together are [Sixteen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince), which is considered a very powerful number. Finally, Two is the "Bad Number" because it represents duality, which Altmer consider impossible. Due to this, the Altmer rarely do anything in twos aside from romantic couplings. It is also seen as a metaphysical force of Man, due to the common Mannish worldview of opposing forces, leading further to the belief of Two being a Bad and inauspicious number. Contrarily, Three is seen as a fulcrum, an essential and central force, which makes it an auspicious number. Because of this, certain Altmeri government roles are only done in groups of three.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-ESOLorne-154)</sup><sup>[[210]](#cite_note-Tot SN-217)</sup> In Summerset, there's an elven garden known as the Garden of Sacred Numbers. In [another reality](Lore_Many_Paths.md), the power of the Sacred Numbers was called upon to fight a threat from beyond [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), casting endless nets of pure magicka- though even they failed in the end.<sup>[[211]](#cite_note-GSNumbers AU-218)</sup>

#### Religion in Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/ON-load-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg/200px-ON-load-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg) An Altmeri monastery. (ESO) Altmeri religious authority is governed by a priestly hierarchy. Aldarchs lead monastic communities dedicated to peace and meditation,<sup>[[212]](#cite_note-ESOFirinore-219)</sup> while temple priests instruct acolytes and officiate public rites.<sup>[[209]](#cite_note-ESOUurkar-216)</sup> Ascendant Curates manage the conferral, refinement, and ceremonial destruction of [calians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calian), which are talismanic tokens bestowed upon young Altmer at the age of majority, signifying their entrance into adult society.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TPo P-16)</sup><sup>[[213]](#cite_note-ESOPrayer Beads-220)</sup>

[Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship is officially outlawed and culturally taboo, though some Altmer, particularly those drawn to power and arcane mastery form clandestine cults to the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md).<sup>[[214]](#cite_note-ESOSeekers-221)</sup><sup>[[215]](#cite_note-ESOBanished-222)</sup> Civil authorities prosecute these practices harshly, and the living gods of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) are viewed as seditious heresies.<sup>[[216]](#cite_note-ESOSum Sotha-223)</sup> Some Altmer are venerated posthumously as saints, such as [Saint Ohmandil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ohmandil) and [Saint Terilde](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Terilde). Their relics, including the Circlet of Saint Ohmandil and Saint Terilde's Fingerbone, are preserved and honored in temples and monasteries.<sup>[[217]](#cite_note-COSO-224)</sup><sup>[[218]](#cite_note-STF-225)</sup><sup>[[193]](#cite_note-MN-200)</sup>

With the coming of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), many Altmer resented the apotheosis of [Talos](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), bitterly recalling Tiber Septim’s brutal conquest of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-RTIV-97)</sup> However, large-scale religious controversy did not erupt until the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) seized power in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). The Thalmor declared that no human like Tiber Septim, could achieve godhood,<sup>[[219]](#cite_note-SKOndolemar-226)</sup> and outlawed the worship of Talos within their territories.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-RTIV-97)</sup> In the lead-up to the [Great War](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Great_War), the ban on Talos worship was formalized as one of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) ’s demands to the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) —demands largely seen as a pretext for an invasion that was already planned.<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-227)</sup> [Emperor Titus Mede II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Emperor_Titus_Mede_II) conceded to the ban in the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md), a peace treaty that ended the conflict.<sup>[[221]](#cite_note-Skyrim WGC-228)</sup> Afterward, incidents such as the [Markarth Incident](Lore_Markarth_Incident.md) saw the Thalmor demand the right to enforce the ban on Talos worship within Imperial territory, claiming the authority to uphold the treaty—despite using it to justify further espionage and suppression in preparation for renewed conflict.<sup>[[222]](#cite_note-Skyrim Markarth-229)</sup><sup>[[223]](#cite_note-Skyrim Thalmor-230)</sup>

## Altmeri Institutions

### Auridon Paladins
[Auridon Paladins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auridon_Paladins) were an Altmeri militant order known for their elite warriors and mages. These [paladins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Paladins) were known for their high-quality training, amazing willpower, fighting skills, and reliability. The main objective of the Auridon Paladins was to defend the isle of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), the gateway in and out of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) from the threat of the ambitious outsiders and to stop the rebel factions of the native High Elves from disturbing the order and peace.

### College of Sapiarchs
The [College of Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Sapiarchs) (or **College of the Sapiarchs**) is an institution located on an island west of the city of [Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lillandril) on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). It is home to the **Sapiarchy**, a sagely order considered by many to be Summerset's foremost scholars and finest magical minds. The Sapiarchs reputation is renowned, and some of the greatest mages in history can be counted among their numbers. These scholars of magical and esoteric knowledge are each devoted to a specific and singular topic of study, but also serve as instructors of the heirs to the royal throne of Summerset and possess considerable political leverage. The Sapiarchs generally see the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md)'s understanding of magic as limited and see them as beneath them. Thus, the Sapiarchs hindered their efforts in Lillandril. Members of the local Mages Guild feel it's based on feelings of racial superiority of Altmer being the only true mages, and the influence they have gained.

### Divine Prosecution
The [Divine Prosecution](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divine_Prosecution) are a state-level law enforcement organization responsible for enforcing both secular and religious law, ensuring Altmeri social and cultural rules are adhered to. Under the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), they were a division of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> and were the highest authority of law and order on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), second only to the Queen and her Royal Court. They target what they perceive to be heresy, including [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship. The Divine Prosecution are wary of outsiders, but were known to have deputized skilled adventurers to handle dangerous threats after the island of Summerset was opened to outsiders by Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

### Eyes of the Queen
The [Eyes of the Queen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eyes_of_the_Queen), or simply the **Eyes**, were a personal guard and intelligence organization that served [Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) of the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Though the Eyes were formally established in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580) upon Ayrenn's accession to the throne, they existed as an underground agency working to secure Ayrenn's rise to power for many years beforehand. The Eyes served as an elite corps responsible directly to the Queen, and played a central role in suppressing the [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance), an Altmeri supremacist group that attempted to depose Ayrenn in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Operatives from the Eyes of the Queen often acted independently, doing things that they believed would be best for [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

### House of Reveries
[House of Reveries](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Reveries) are a well-known group of performers and artists known for providing entertainment to Summerset. They are recognizable by their distinctive painted masks and stage names, which they always use. Apart from aiding their performance, the masks also fulfill a crucial role for the actors and actresses themselves: they offer a vital service by allowing them to perform without the worry of facing the overly strict standards of decorum of the Altmeri society. The House of Reveries was regarded as the heirs to the Altmeri long legacy of cultural arts, with its membership being highly exclusive.

Their base is in the village of Rellenthil, where they have an amphitheater for performances and auditions for new members. Most of Rellenthil has some affiliation with the House of Reveries, but membership in the troupe is exclusive to performers. Those seeking to join are referred to as Hopefuls. The symbol of the house is a double horn. The banner of the House prominently features this [instrument](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Instruments) against a white backdrop, adorned with green embellishments.

### Paladins of Jephre
[Jephrine Paladins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jephrine_Paladins), also known as the **[Paladins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Paladins) of Jephre** are an Altmeri militant order of followers of [Jephre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jephre). Their attire is inspired by [Indriks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indrik), creatures that bear a relation to their patron deity. They are known to be the protectors of the wild. It is believed that whenever a paladin of Jephre performs an act the God of Song and Forest finds worthy, an infant autumnal Indrik takes its shaky steps into the world.

### Psijic Order
The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) (rarely spelled **Psijiic Order** and **the Order of Psijics**) or the **Order of PSJJJJ** is the oldest monastic group of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), devoted to the study and practice of [Mysticism](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism), which they call the "Old Ways" or "Elder Way". Though these terms are also used to refer to the religion and customs of the order, related to ideas of meditation and achievable higher states of being, and described as the combination of Mysticism, the Eleven Forces, the divine laws that bind and define the nature of reality, and a deep understanding of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), those [Aedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) spirits which established the foundations of life and the world, into a philosophy and way of life. They make their home on the Isle of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). They have often been criticized for hoarding their knowledge and in turn mocking other scholars for not seeing their way. Some sources claim that the Psijics were not the original practicioners of the Elder Way, but instead learned it from the original inhabitants of Nirn, the Ehlnofey.

### Sect of Harmonious Masters
The [Sect of Harmonious Masters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sect_of_Harmonious_Masters) was a religious order devoted to the worship of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) and mastering [Restoration Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restoration). Its members dedicated themselves to adapting the magic of Stendarr's Light into spells, rituals, and ceremonies of cleansing and healing that can be employed by all of the mortal races. The sect originated from Altmeri city of [Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lillandril), as a group of Altmer who rejected the common Altmeri disinterest with Stendarr, the Apologist of Man, and embraced his mercy. Despite its Altmeri roots Harmonious Masters are open for all races, not even excepting the [Beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md). They believed that as Stendarr, in his boundless mercy, has given all mortals the potential ability to employ his magical gifts, they should feel honor-bound to provide knowledge of these spells to all the peoples of Nirn freely and without hindrance. Their cause was to improve the general welfare of all folk in every culture. They eschewed all forms of violence and turned Stendarr's glowing gift into a tool for healing. They were known to spread their knowledge freely to everyone, even to the militant adherents. One of the known members of the Harmonious Masters was the author [Aritanwe of Lillandril](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Aritanwe_of_Lillandril).

### Sun Birds of Alinor
The [Sun Birds of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun_Birds_of_Alinor) were a mythical order of [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorers from the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). According to legend, they managed to pierce the veil between [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) using raw magic of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md). Their expeditions to Aetherius are said to be among the most famous attempts in history, alongside similar endeavors undertaken by the [Royal Imperial Mananauts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Royal_Imperial_Mananauts) of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) during the time of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md), but some scholars believe that actual proof of a successful journey is nonexistent.

The order was eventually dissolved due to the vast expenditures of magicka required to travel. Few remaining legacies exist of these fabled travelers. Possibly the most notable example is the [Great Orrery](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Orrery) at [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), whose spheres are made up of genuine celestial mineral gathered by travelers during the Merethic Era.

Lastly, during the mid-Second Era, a prismatic Sun Bird feather was unearthed by the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)'s Antiquarian Circle. While its exact function remains unknown, scholars speculated it may have been an orienteering tool of some sort.

Their [vessels](Lore_Air_Transportation.md#Sunbirds) have been described as "[birds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird) made out of the [sun](Lore_Sun.md)".

### Thalmor
The [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) refers to an Altmeri governmental organization that has served each of the three [Aldmeri Dominions](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in varying capacities. It is also a general term for the members and paramilitary enforcers of that organization.

### Trebbite Monk
The [Trebbite Monks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trebbite_Monks) were a monastic order based in the isle of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Their credo has ever been for pure Altmer bloodlines on Summerset, and among the royal families most of all. They were also known for their charity, and for helping the Altmeri peasants, as they value tradition above every other virtue.

During the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) the Trebbite Monks, represented by Friar Lylim worked with Lady Gialene, daughter of the King of [Skywatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skywatch), to start a revolution against the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) Queen of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), [Morgiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morgiah). Trebbite Monks were the faction that disliked the Dunmer influence on the Altmeri court the most as their power and influence was greatly reduced by the efforts of queen Morgiah. They were willing to aid Lady Gialene in her endeavors and gathered an army to oppose the Dunmer monarch. Despite the growing resentment in the countryside from peasants who called Morgiah the "Black Queen", they stopped short of insurrection, and the revolutionary army was primarily composed of the monks themselves and a horde of mercenaries, among which there were rejected members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). The revolt ended in a panicked, unorganized retreat, and after Lady Gialene was tortured and confessed her treachery, she was sent back with an escort to her father's court of Skywatch.

### Veiled Heritance
The [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance) was a group of Altmeri supremacists that formed in response to Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn)'s accession and the subsequent formation of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580). Vehemently opposed to Ayrenn and her [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) allies, they sought to depose her, destroy the Aldmeri Dominion, and expel all non-Altmer from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). High Kinlady [Estre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Estre), who was Prince [Naemon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naemon)'s wife and Ayrenn's sister-in-law, was the group's first leader as the Veiled Queen. It was planned that Estre or Naemon would succeed Ayrenn as monarch.

The Veiled Heritance was made up of disgruntled Altmer from throughout the Dominion. The group primarily operated on Estre's home island of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) and later in the region of [Greenshade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenshade) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Their primary base of operations was a ruin on [Errinorne Isle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Errinorne_Isle) known as the Veiled Keep, which housed the group's leadership council, the Veiled Council.

During Ayrenn's sojourn in Auridon in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Heritance attempted to assassinate her, but they were ultimately defeated, and Estre was killed. After their defeat in Auridon and the death of Naemon, the prince's advisor and attendant, Vicereeve [Pelidil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelidil), absconded with his body and assumed control of the remnants of the Heritance in Greenshade, where they resumed their conflict with the Aldmeri Dominion. Pelidil was eventually killed in a battle in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), but before his death, the vicereeve performed a ritual that raised Naemon from the dead as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md), who then assumed control of the final remnants of the Heritance. The Dominion subsequently destroyed Naemon's lich and defeated the Heritance in a battle at [Hectahame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hectahame). After the repeated defeats, the Heritance appears to have largely collapsed.

### Welkynars
[Welkynars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welkynars), also known as **Welkynar Gryphon Knights** and **Welkynars of Eton Nir**, were a prestigious order of elite Altmer [knights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights) who dedicated their lives to protecting the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Based in the city of [Cloudrest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloudrest) atop [Eton Nir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eton_Nir), the Welkynars remained vigilant to answer any urgent calls for aid. This need for quick mobility is what led to the first Welkynars to tame the native [gryphons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gryphon) and use them as their mounts. Given the city's central location on [the island](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), and the help of these flying creatures, the Welkynars were able to quickly render aid across the entire realm. While any member of Summerset's governance or military could petition the Wekynars for aid, accepting the petition was at the order's discretion. Between assignments, Welkynars patrolled Summerset for signs of danger, often stopping in cities and collecting information on any potential threats.

## Artifacts

#### [Flask of Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flask_of_Lillandril)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png/200px-RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) The Flask of Lillandril The [Flask of Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flask_of_Lillandril) is a magical flask that absorbs and reflects magic of all kinds. Its [anti-magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anti-Magicka) property also prevents mages from detecting its location. The flask was discovered around the year [1E 470](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_470) by a group of elves living in the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) city of [Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lillandril). The flask itself was not of Elvish make, but its creator is unknown. The elves spread news of their discovery, enough to make it an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of song and legend. It became the city's symbol and was displayed on their banners.

The elves used the flask to perform favors for the barons of Summerset Isle by keeping in check the magical power of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). One legend says that a stable boy defeated the [High Wizard of Valenwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Wizard_of_Valenwood) using only the flask. After some time, the elves who discovered the flask feared that Bosmer assassins would kill them, so they fled for [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall), hoping to buy safety from the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). The elves shipwrecked off the southern coast of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), killing all of them according to legend, and the Flask of Lillandril was lost.

#### [Heart of Transparent Law](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Transparent_Law)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e9/ON-furnishing-Replica_of_The_Heart_of_Transparent_Law.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Replica_of_The_Heart_of_Transparent_Law.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_of_The_Heart_of_Transparent_Law.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_of_The_Heart_of_Transparent_Law.jpg) Heart of Transparent Law The Heart of Transparent Law is a part of the Stone of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), Transparent Law. The Heart was taken from the Tower shortly after the founding of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in 2E 230 by [Ritemaster Iachesis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iachesis) of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), who was under the control of [K'Tora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:K%27Tora) of the Abyssal Cabal through the use of mind magic. In a desperate attempt to keep the [Sea Sloads](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Sload) from using the Heart to sink Summerset, Iachesis enlisted the help of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) to hide the Heart inside his own body. During the Interregnum, Nocturnal tried to take control of the Crystal Tower. Using a technique she stole from Sotha Sil utilizing life energy as a power source, she planned to use the life energy of Nirn, and then the energy of the other Princes afterwards, to amplify the Crystal Tower to make her power limitless. Nocturnal's plan was foiled by the Psijic Order and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), with the assistance of Mephala and Clavicus Vile, who had been betrayed by Nocturnal, and the Golden Knight, who was the vessel of [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia). Using the divine power of Meridia's [Dawnbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dawnbreaker), the Vestige defeated Nocturnal and restored Transparent Law using the Heart. The Heart of Transparent Law was said to awaken a dark urge to seize it for themselves in those who looked upon it.

#### [Obscuros](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Obscuros)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-quest-Of_Knives_and_Long_Shadows_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Of_Knives_and_Long_Shadows_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Of_Knives_and_Long_Shadows_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Of_Knives_and_Long_Shadows_02.jpg) The Obscuros The [Obscuros](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Obscuros) is a scrying artifact that enhances the user's [Sight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) and projects their visions in the form of holographic constructs for anyone to see. It is said to be the most powerful scrying device on Tamriel. It was used and protected by the [ancient](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancients) [Psijics](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) before being deactivated and hidden to prevent its misuse, being relocated to the island of [Dranil Kir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dranil_Kir) and locked away with magical traps. The artifacts' presence on the island made it infamously plagued by severe weather.

#### [Ring of Phynaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_Phynaster)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/SR-item-Ring_of_Phynaster.jpg/200px-SR-item-Ring_of_Phynaster.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_Phynaster.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_Phynaster.jpg) Ring of Phynaster The [Ring of Phynaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_Phynaster) is an artifact created by the [eponymous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phynaster) Hero-God of the Altmer. The Ring improves its wearer's overall resistance to poison, magicka, and shock. The ring added much needed security for Phynaster to survive his adventurous lifestyle. Because of this, he lived for hundreds of years, and it has since been passed from person to person. The ring has become synonymous with Phynaster, so much so that it will not stay with anyone, other than the Hero-God himself. When it is kept long enough, it will disappear to another resting place, for the next adventurer.

#### [Unraveling Staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unraveling_Staff)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/ON-item-Unraveling_Staff.jpg/200px-ON-item-Unraveling_Staff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Unraveling_Staff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Unraveling_Staff.jpg) The Unraveling Staff The [Unraveling Staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unraveling_Staff), occasionally called a wand, is a powerful [staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff) artifact renowned for its ability to unravel any material. It was crafted in [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) by Anillyon. It is extremely dangerous, capable of dismantling buildings, trees, and even flesh of living beings.

## Notes
- The Altmer are a version of the modern fantasy "[High Elf](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noldor)" archetype, shaped by genre conventions that became especially prominent after [J. R. R. Tolkien](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien)'s [legendarium](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tolkien%27s_legendarium). - The similarities are mostly broad and thematic, such as the portrayal of an ancient, long-lived, culturally conservative people, and the social prestige attached to lineage and language.
- The noted resemblance between [Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alinor) (used for Summerset Isles and its capital in different periods) and [Valinor](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valinor) (Tolkien's far-western, blissful realm across the sea, where many Elves live) can also be understood in terms of their similar written form, and in the way both places evoke idyllic, luminous western homelands associated with beauty, cultural memory, and a sense of elven antiquity.
