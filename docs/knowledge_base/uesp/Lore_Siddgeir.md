Siddgeir was the Jarl of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He had two uncles, Jarl [Dengeir](Lore_Dengeir_of_Stuhn.md) of Falkreath and Thadgeir.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dengeir-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Thadgeir-3)</sup> Siddgeir owned two businesses in [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), Deadwood Lumber Mill and Gray Pine Goods.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574, 575</sup>

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the local nobles encouraged Dengeir to step down as jarl, and he ultimately agreed to abdicate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dengeir-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Dengeir was granted the title of [thane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thanes), and Siddgeir succeeded him as Jarl of Falkreath, making him one of the youngest jarls in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dengeir-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Nenya-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Sr LS-6)</sup> According to Siddgeir, Dengeir stepped down because of his old age and failing health.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup> However, Dengeir contested the official narrative that he stepped down as a result of his old age and declining health. Dengeir, who was a supporter of the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), believed that the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) had bribed the nobles to encourage him to abdicate in order to replace him with a pro-imperial jarl, such as Siddgeir.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dengeir-2)</sup>

Siddgeir had no interest in ruling Falkreath, so he entrusted matters of governance to his steward, Nenya.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Nenya-5)</sup> With Nenya administering Falkreath, Siddgeir spent his time hunting and feasting.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup> He used Falkreath's funds to buy himself fine clothes and expensive mead.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dengeir-2)</sup> Siddgeir had dealings with a group of bandits in Falkreath Hold, which gave him a cut of their loot; however, they eventually stopped paying him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Dengeir-2)</sup> Siddgeir's main reason for supporting the Empire was the wealth and power that it provided.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Unlike most jarls, Siddgeir viewed the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) favorably, as they richly rewarded their friends. He also did not oppose the Thalmor's persecution of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worshippers, believing they deserved it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup>
