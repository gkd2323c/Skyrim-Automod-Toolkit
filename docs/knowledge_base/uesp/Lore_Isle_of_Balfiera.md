[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/DF-map-Isle_of_Balfiera.png/200px-DF-map-Isle_of_Balfiera.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Isle_of_Balfiera.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Isle_of_Balfiera.png) The isles of Balfiera The Isle of Balfiera (also called **Balfiera Island**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup> **Balfiera Isle**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBMOP-3)</sup> or simply **Balfiera**)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> is an island region within the northern portion of [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). There are at least eight islands in the chain. In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the largest settlement was the town of Upvale, the dominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline was [Lyrezi](Lore_Lyrezi.md), and the regional deity was [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IOBDAG-5)</sup>

Balfiera was known as a neutral meeting place for the kingdoms of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), who used it to facilitate negotiations and treaties.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1High-6)</sup> However, the isle is most renowned as the site of the [Direnni Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md), formerly the Adamantine Tower, the oldest known structure in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup> Legends say it was constructed on the "little island" by the gods themselves so they could hold [their own negotiations](Lore_Convention.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The title for the ruler of the isle is the Castellan of Balfiera, derived from the title originally used for the commander of the tower.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1High-6)</sup> The isle was an independent, unconquered territory before the events of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), after which it fell under the dominion of one of the four surviving kingdoms of the Iliac Bay region.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWITW-8)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/DF-place-Upvale.png/200px-DF-place-Upvale.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Upvale.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Upvale.png) Upvale, the largest settlement on Balfiera [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-load-Balfiera.png/200px-ON-load-Balfiera.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Balfiera.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Balfiera.png) Part of Balfiera Archaeological study of the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) on the Isle of Balfiera has dated its construction to around [ME 2500](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#ME_2500), the earliest known date in Aldmeri and Nordic history, making it the oldest known structure in Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1High-6)</sup>

According to Beredalmo the Signifier, [Direnni Cygnus](Lore_Direnni_Cygnus.md), was the progenitor to Clan Direnni as she discovered the Isle of Balfiera and decreed that her descendants should carry her name.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Once-9)</sup> In the Middle Merethic Era, Clan Direnni rediscovered and captured the Adamantine Tower.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Once-9)</sup> Some believe the Direnni were following their destiny to rule from Balfiera, while others believed that the clan was exiled.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOBE-10)</sup>

The Direnni's claim over the Isle of Balfiera was strengthened when it was purchased by [Ryain Direnni](Lore_Ryain_Direnni.md) in [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RTR-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-12)</sup> Certain [Druidic](Lore_Druid.md) artifacts like music boxes playing *Blessings of Stone* had some ties to the isle. Its melody was reminiscent of tunes linked to *Ballads from Balfiera*, a music typical among the lower-class, deeply rooted in [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) traditions. It also resembled ancient Altmeri music.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MBBOS-13)</sup>

After the downfall of the Direnni Hegmony around [1E 500](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_500), the Direnni retreated to the isle.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1High-6)</sup> During the early history of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), around [1E 800](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_800), the islands west of the city were crowded by raiders and [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) pirates.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-WJot B-14)</sup> In [1E 907](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_907), [Peregrine Direnni‎](Lore_Peregrine_Direnni.md) repelled an attempted invasion and drove an entire [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) flotilla back to [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) by using her sorcery to manipulate the waves of the Iliac Bay.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBMOP-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-O-15)</sup>

In [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200) in the midst of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md), the nobility of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) all escaped to the Isle of Balfiera.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TDC-16)</sup>

The [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) was established in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703) after [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman_I.md) suppressed the Akaviri invasion of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) at the battle of [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1C-17)</sup> With the Akaviri's number and their tactics boosting his ranks, Reman continued on to pacify the rest of Tamriel.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LOTD-18)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1C-17)</sup> High Rock was already weakened by the Akaviri invasion,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-AHOD-19)</sup> and an alliance known as the [League of Unrest](Lore_League_of_Unrest.md) was created between Daggerfall, Wayrest, and Balfiera, to stop Reman's forces.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-RGMaiko-20)</sup> They were defeated however, and Reman formed modern-day Cyrodiil using the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md), [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md), and parts of High Rock.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1C-17)</sup>

[Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Queen_Ayrenn) studied at the Isle of Balfiera after having run away from home in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TRo QA-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-A:TUQ-22)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Balfiera was invaded by a [Harvester](Lore_Harvester.md) known as Shyazzel the Traveler. A representative of the [Sinderill](Lore_Sinderill.md), Norianwe, was assisted by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in defending the isle from the daedric intrusion. Once peace was restored to the isle, Norianwe brought the hero into a chamber with inactive portals and the arcane helix. The arrival of the Vestige activated the chamber's portals, connecting the island to numerous locations across Tamriel, places which Norianwe suspected desperately needed saving. According to her, the Vestige was chosen by the Gallery, or perhaps even the Adamantine Tower itself, to aid the citizens of the Tamriel in need.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TGOA-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Norianwe-24)</sup>

As of [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), Balfiera accommodated several settlements, with Upvale being the largest among them. Additionally, there were smaller settlements like Warwych, Blackhead, Gallomarket, Singbrugh, and Singbury.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IOBDAG-5)</sup> It was considered an 'unconquered territory' among the several independent kingdoms of the Iliac Bay until the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), when it became part of one of the four emergent kingdoms of the region.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TWit W-25)</sup> In the same year, [Baltham Greyman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baltham_Greyman) visited the island, seeking to steal magical relics from the Direnni Tower.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-FS2-26)</sup>

[Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) agents were harassing and possibly murdering refugees in Balfiera and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) around [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48); the victims were all of mixed blood or had associations considered by the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) to be "unclean".<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TIC-27)</sup>

### Historic Figures
The island came into the hands of the [Direnni Clan](Lore_Direnni.md) in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) when [Ryain Direnni](Lore_Ryain_Direnni.md) bought the Isle of Balfiera.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RTR-11)</sup> The mother of [Pelagius the Mad](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md), [Utheilla](Lore_Utheilla_Direnni.md), was of the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) blood and brought Pelagius and his siblings to the island as she fled from the assault brought upon Wayrest by [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md) and Queen [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-MOP-28)</sup> Shortly before the Warp in the West, the island was ruled by [Medora Direnni](Lore_Medora_Direnni.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-FM-29)</sup>
