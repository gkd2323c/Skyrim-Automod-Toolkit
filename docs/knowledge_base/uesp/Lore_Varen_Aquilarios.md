Varen Aquilarios (also called **the Betrayer** by [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md))<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DID-1)</sup> was an Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). In [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), Emperor Leovic of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) issued a decree that legalized [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), which caused Varen, the Duke of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) and Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SVR1-2)</sup> to initiate a [rebellion](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) against the Emperor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ef EV-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SHot LE-4)</sup> In [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Varen and his forces stormed the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), where he personally slew Leovic in the throne room of the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SHot LE-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-5)</sup> He then declared himself emperor and married Leovic's widow, [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), to consolidate his rule.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-5)</sup>

Varen subsequently led a group called the [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md) on a two-year quest to recover the lost [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), which they eventually recovered. Heeding the advice of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), Varen planned to use the amulet in a ritual to persuade [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to make him [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) and therefore a legitimate emperor. In [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), as the ritual was attempted, Mannimarco corrupted it, which triggered the event known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md). Varen vanished in the chaos, and Clivia assumed the Imperial Throne as empress regent in his absence, setting [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) on a course for the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).

Following the Soulburst, Varen, who was believed dead, ended up at an abbey of the [Moth Priests](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), where he eventually became known as **the Prophet** after reading the abbey's [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VA-7)</sup> Word of Varen's [prophetic predictions](Lore_Sight.md) from studying the Elder Scrolls spread far and wide, eventually reaching Mannimarco, who immediately arrested Varen for rumor-mongering and treason.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup> Varen was imprisoned in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and later assisted [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in their efforts to defeat [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and stop the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Lyris SSIC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOMQ-9)</sup>

## Biography
Varen Aquilarios was born to a [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) duke in [2E 536](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_536).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-10)</sup> Varen later became the Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VW-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TWat D-12)</sup> and Duke of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup> His nephew from an unnamed sibling, [Carolus Aquilarios](Lore_Carolus_Aquilarios.md), viewed him as a role model.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TWat D-12)</sup> In [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), the third emperor of the [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) dynasty known as the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), issued a decree that legalized [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md). This outraged much of the population, and prompted Varen to initiate a rebellion.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ef EV-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SHot LE-4)</sup>

Varen convinced Carolus to stay behind and protect the home front, who, although disappointed not to be able to fight alongside Varen, nevertheless vowed to keep Kvatch and its people safe. Varen then departed the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) with the [Second Legion](Lore_Second_Legion.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TWat D-12)</sup> Before his departure, he constructed a massive wall that separated the Gold Coast from the rest of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), later called "[Varen's Wall](Lore_Varen%27s_Wall_(place).md)", to protect the region from Leovic's forces.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VW-11)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ett W-13)</sup>

### The Colovian Revolt
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg/200px-ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg) Varen's Wall [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Deadlands_and_Damnation-Leovic.jpg) Emperor Leovic, slain by Varen Varen secretly contacted High Chancellor [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) and asked for his assistance in defeating the Reachmen, who agreed to assist him.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-5)</sup> Varen was initially defeated by Leovic's forces in a battle at [Fort Ash](Lore_Fort_Ash.md), but Varen then took command of the Legion of Chorrol and pushed the Emperor's forces into the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ef EV-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SVR2-14)</sup> Following Varen's victory, the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovia.md) joined the rebellion, with the exception of Anvil, where the count, [Ephrem Benirus](Lore_Ephrem_Benirus.md), remained loyal to Leovic.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ef EV-3)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-10)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TWat D-12)</sup> When the revolt broke out, Leovic was broadly unpopular, and Varen found a number of allies among Heartland and [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) families as well.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-10)</sup> Varen and his forces captured the city of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), which became the rebellion's primary base.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ef EV-3)</sup>

In [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Varen and his army stormed the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), where in the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) he drove his sword into Leovic's heart at the foot of the Ruby Throne, killing him, and Varen immediately declared himself emperor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SHot LE-4)</sup> For Tharn's loyalty and assistance in the rebellion, Varen agreed to take the Chancellor's daughter and Leovic's widow, [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), as his bride.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ef EV-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-5)</sup> Due to the large amount of casualties during the uprising, Varen converted the [Market District](Lore_Market_District_(Imperial_City).md) of the Imperial City into the Memorial District, filling it with mass graves.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

### Forming The Five Companions
Varen's rule was largely stable and without problems, but the thought of him being an illegitimate ruler bothered him. Varen Aquilarios was not a "true emperor", as he was not [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md). This fact made him insecure.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup>

Believing that only a Dragonborn could rightfully take the Ruby Throne, in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), he formed the Five Companions to find the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) after the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) sorcerer Mannimarco convinced him that he knew a ritual that would allow Varen to become Dragonborn. After hearing of this, he set out on a two-year quest to find the Amulet with his Five Companions: Varen himself, the half-giant warrior [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md), the sorcerer [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md), a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) blademaster and the captain of the Emperor's [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), and Abnur Tharn.

### The Soulburst
The Five were able to recover the Amulet by [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579) and prepared to perform the ritual to make Varen Dragonborn. They gathered at the [Temple of the One](Lore_Temple_of_the_One.md) in the Imperial City and began to perform the ritual. While Tharn held the Amulet, Varen asked [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to rightfully grant his claim as emperor by making him a Dragonborn. At that moment, Mannimarco immobilized everyone present and revealed his true intentions; he was an agent of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), and the ritual did not reignite the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md), instead tricking them into destroying the ancient barriers shielding Mundus from the forces of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and thus allowing Molag Bal to begin the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), a forceful attempt to drag Nirn out of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and into his own realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). This event was known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md), and it shook every corner of Tamriel.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Harborage-16)</sup>

Subsequently, Lyris was captured and brought into Coldharbour. Sai Sahan fled with the Amulet and managed to hide it in [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) before being captured and held in the Halls of Torment. Mannimarco summoned his [Worm Cult](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) and turned the Temple of the One into a sacrificial ground where dozens of souls were harvested and brought to Coldharbour, and Abnur joined him in order to gain his trust and spy on him. Varen, however, disappeared,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Harborage-16)</sup> officially declared and believed to be dead following the event.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ef EV-3)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TCC-17)</sup>

### Time with the Moth Priests
After the Soulburst, Varen woke up weak and near death at the [Abbey of the Moth Priests](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), suffering from amnesia and no longer remembering his role as Emperor. The Moth priests took pity on him, assuming he was a lost vagabond, and gave him shelter within their establishment.

It was there where he set his eyes on the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), mysterious artifacts of immense knowledge and power. He began to study and read the scrolls in order to learn the events of Tamriel which have happened and have yet to happen, including his life as Emperor and his destiny with the Vestige. The priests were shocked to discover how quickly he managed to read the scrolls, believing it to be a prophetic sign from the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md). As a result of continuously reading the scrolls, Varen gradually lost his eyesight. He carved himself a staff which he also used as a walking stick<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup> called the Emperor's Blessing. It was engraved with runes that speak to his abilities as a mage and [seer](Lore_Sight.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONStaff-18)</sup> Though blind, Varen still continued to study the scrolls in his mind's eye, and foresaw the events to come in Tamriel's future.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup>

Word of Varen's prophecies began spreading across the land, eventually reaching the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). When Mannimarco discovered this, he had the Prophet arrested under the grounds of rumor-mongering and treason. The King of Worms had the abbey and its contents burned to the ground, and transferred Varen into the custody of his Daedric master, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Bal then unleashed the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) upon [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), and had Varen imprisoned in The Wailing Prison.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-6)</sup> Varen was kept in a specialized cell: one that would not release the prisoner held within unless a living soul took their place.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Lyris SSIC-8)</sup> During his imprisonment, Molag Bal frequently invaded Varen's mind, causing his captive immense pain. By forcing his way into Varen's head, Molag Bal opened his own thoughts to the prisoner; Varen could see through Molag Bal's eyes if he concentrated hard enough. Molag Bal could feel Varen's presence whenever he did this, and it enraged the Prince.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Proph DOG-19)</sup>

### Rescue From Coldharbour
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/ON-npc-The_Prophet_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-The_Prophet_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Prophet_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Prophet_02.jpg) Varen as the Prophet, aged unnaturally beyond his years A hero that would become known as [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) was sacrificed to Molag Bal by Mannimarco in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Their soul was damned to Coldharbour, where they joined forces with Lyris Titanborn. The pair fought their way through the realm to rescue Varen, who Lyris called "the Prophet". Lyris and the Vestige were unaware of the Prophet's true identity, the former not recognizing him as the harrowing experience he went through aged and diminished him, leaving him blind and almost feeble.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Harborage LS-20)</sup> Varen could not be released from his cell unless a living mortal took his place, so Lyris gave herself to free him. Varen and the Vestige proceeded to escape Coldharbour through a [Dark Anchor](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

Varen and the Vestige ended up in different parts of the same province, but eventually regrouped at a sanctuary which the Prophet called the Harborage. There, the Vestige learned of the Soulburst, and the two hatched a plan to free Lyris Titanborn from her imprisonment in Coldharbour. The Daedra moved her to the Foundry of Woe after Varen's escape. There, she was forced to make weapons for Molag Bal's soldiers. The Prophet learned this due to his mental link to Molag Bal,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Proph DOG-19)</sup> which he exploited to distract the Prince long enough for the Vestige to enter Coldharbour and retrieve Lyris Titanborn from her prison. During the mission, Lyris and the Vestige learned that Sai Sahan was still alive.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Lyris and Varen set out to find leads on Sai Sahan's whereabouts. Lyris heard rumors of Worm Cult activity in a nearby settlement, which the Vestige investigated. The Vestige entered a Worm Cultist's hideout and found an orb which the cult used to communicate with none other than Abnur Tharn.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

Varen used the orb the Vestige retrieved to communicate with Abnur Tharn, who offered to give the group Sai Sahan's location in exchange for Tharn's extraction from Coldharbour. Tharn was being held in the [Castle of the Worm](Lore_Castle_of_the_Worm.md), Mannimarco's stronghold in the realm. Abnur realized he was becoming obsolete to Mannimarco; he would soon be disposed of if he wasn't rescued. Lyris and the Vestige entered Coldharbour and rescued Abnur Tharn, who explained that Mannimarco intended to use the Amulet of Kings to absorb Molag Bal's essence and ascend to godhood. Tharn only revealed that he didn't know where Sai Sahan was being kept after they made it back to the Harborage. It was then that the Prophet revealed his true identity as Varen Aquilarios to the Vestige.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VA-7)</sup>

### Finding the Amulet of Kings
Abnur and Varen continued the search for Sai Sahan, and Tharn found a lead at a Daedric lair in an undisclosed ruin. There, Mannimarco kept his most valuable documents. Tharn and the Vestige traveled together and learned that Sai Sahan was being kept at the Halls of Torment, where Mannimarco had him tortured in an attempt to extract the location of the Amulet of Kings. Sai's torturer was the Duchess of Anguish, one of Molag Bal's most skilled torturers.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> With this information, Varen opened a portal to the prison, enabling the Vestige (accompanied by either Tharn or Titanborn) to rescue Sai Sahan.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Sai Sahan hid the Amulet of Kings after he fled the Imperial City, and revealed that it was hidden in a sacred location and protected by magic wards. He needed to retrieve the key to bring down the ward protecting the artifact from the [Valley of Blades](Lore_Valley_of_Blades.md), a hidden refuge in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) where an old friend of his trained skilled swordsmen. Varen sent Abnur, Lyris, Sai and the Vestige to the Valley. The group encountered Mannimarco, retrieved the Ring of Stendarr's Mercy and the sword of Sai's father, then returned to the Harborage.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup>

The Vestige, Sai Sahan, Lyris Titanborn and Abnur Tharn soon set out to [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) to retrieve the Amulet of Kings. Varen Aquilarios opened a portal to get them there, and waited at the Harborage for their safe return. The group returned with the Amulet of Kings and news of Mannimarco's demise at the Vestige's hand. Mannimarco who was dragged to Coldharbour by Molag Bal to face an eternity of torment for his treachery.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> Molag Bal forced visions of Mannimarco's torture upon Varen; a portent for the fate of Nirn should he and the Companions fail to stop the Daedric Prince.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

### The Final Battle
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/ON-npc-Varen_Aquilarios_%28Old_Life_Observance%29.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Varen_Aquilarios_%28Old_Life_Observance%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Varen_Aquilarios_(Old_Life_Observance).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Varen_Aquilarios_(Old_Life_Observance).jpg) The spirit of Varen Aquilarios
- *"I cannot aid you in the coming battle. My time in this world is at an end. I must take my place in the Immortal Plane. Farewell, old friend. We will not speak again."*

Varen called the Vestige to the Harborage once more, as Sai wished to reforge the Five Companions. At the meeting, the group discussed how the Vestige was to retrieve their soul—after the Planemeld was stopped, the Vestige was to use the Amulet of Kings to imbue themselves with Divine power enough to defeat Molag Bal. However, this would require a willing mortal sacrifice: one of the Five Companions would have to give their life so the ritual could be performed. Abnur Tharn was the only one capable of performing the ritual; Varen, Sai or Lyris would have to sacrifice themselves for the good of Tamriel.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup>

The [Mages](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and [Fighters Guilds](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) staged an invasion of Coldharbour in a bid to stop the Planemeld once and for all.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup> The Vestige joined the invasion and was instrumental in its success. The Planemeld was ultimately stopped with the aid of Molag Bal's enemy, the Daedric Prince [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup> However, Molag Bal still held the Vestige's soul and immense power; he would have recovered if the Companions stalled, so they entered [his fortress](Lore_Heart%27s_Grief.md) to confront the God of Schemes. One of the Five was sacrificed to allow the Vestige to be imbued with the righteous fury of Akatosh, which the Hero used to vanquish Molag Bal, forcing the Prince to relinquish his grasp on their soul.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup> Regardless of whether or not he was sacrificed, Varen Aquilarios believed his time in the world was at an end and his soul moved onto Aetherius soon after the Planemeld was stopped.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36)</sup>
