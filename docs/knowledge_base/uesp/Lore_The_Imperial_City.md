—Refayj, [Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition.md) *<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup>* The Imperial City (also known as **Cyrodiil**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LOMap-2)</sup> **Cyrodiil City**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> the **Imperial Capital**, the **Capital**, and the **City of a Thousand Cults**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>) is the capital city of [Cyrodiil Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the [Tamrielic Empire](Lore_Empire.md), the city belongs to a political territory known as the **Imperium**<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBLoad-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lex Dia-6)</sup> or **County Imperium**.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Imperium Demo-7)</sup> Culturally Nibenese, the Imperial City is described as the heart of Tamriel.

A thriving city, its flooded lower dwellings are sailed by gondolas and riverboats. [Moth Priests](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) walk the streets in clouds of ancestor moths. House guards bearing exceptionally long daikatanas keep watch over the streets. The city proper is located on [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md), which lies in the center of [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md), but continues across the lake and merges into ruins left by the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) and the villages of the southern [Niben River](Lore_Niben_River.md). The city stains the mouth of the Niben red with the tinmi soil of Rumare's shores.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup>

## History

### Merethic Era And First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png/200px-LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Before_the_Ages_of_Man.png) A depiction of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) with the White-Gold Tower in the Merethic Era Before the founding of the city, when [Beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) dominated Tamriel, the native [Bird Men](Lore_Bird_Men.md) lived on the eight islands. In the middle Merethic era [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md), in search of Aldmeris, came upon the islands and the native Bird Men. Topal lived among them for some time and, in exchange for the secrets of literacy, was gifted the eight islands for the Aldmer.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Fot N-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBDecentius Opsius-9)</sup> The history of the Imperial City itself began with the construction of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) by the earliest [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) migrants to Cyrodiil in the middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and was the center of the region occupied by their [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) descendants. The tower was originally called the Temple of the Ancestors, and was home to the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md), a set of Ayleid statues made from [meteoric iron](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md) and [glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano C-10)</sup> By [1E 0](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_0), due to the long lines of communication between the high kings in the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) and the tributary kings in White-Gold, the latter had become an independent state.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg/200px-OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) Pelinal battles Umaril atop the White-Gold Tower circa [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) In [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243), one year into the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the Alessian forces advanced on the city. During the siege, the Ten Ancestors were removed from the City and dispersed among other Ayleid cities.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano C-10)</sup> Once Pelinal breached the White-Gold Tower, the Ayleid king [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md) challenged him to a duel. Outraged by the hesitation of the other rebel leaders to assault the Tower, Pelinal confronted Umaril alone. Though wounded by the Ayleid king, he prevailed, but was immediately killed and cut into eight pieces by the other Ayleid kings. The next morning [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) shook the White-Gold Tower by smashing into it; the humans of the rebellion had finally claimed the City for Alessia.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup> While official histories of the First Empire state that when Alessia's forces captured the Tower, all the Ayleids' relics of arcane power were taken and destroyed, others insisted that a king had escaped with the chief artifacts.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

On the 23rd of Sun's Dawn [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461), Emperor Gorieus was crowned in the Imperial City without any entertainment in accordance with Marukh's doctrine.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-rtr-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-misc-The_Heartlands_Cropped.jpg/200px-ON-misc-The_Heartlands_Cropped.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Heartlands_Cropped.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Heartlands_Cropped.jpg) A painting of the Imperial City contemporaneous with the Alessian Order In [1E 478](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_478), the Imperial City survived a plague that swept the Colovian West unscathed and killed King Mhorus Larich of Skingrad, which led his eldest surviving son Dorald, an Alessian priest in the Imperial City, to immediately cede the kingdom to the Empire.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-rtr-14)</sup>

In [1E 946](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_946), construction of the Imperial Arena began under [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBbasin-15)</sup>

According to the *Remanada*, [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) was then found at [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) with the Amulet of Kings embedded in his forehead by a shepherdess who carried the infant to the White-Gold Tower and placed him on the throne uncontested at an unknown date in the First Era.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

Imperial painters resumed painting the Imperial City in its entirety late into the [thirtieth century](Lore_First_Era.md#Thirtieth_Century) of the First Era, after severe Alessian restrictions on artists which grew into cultural taboos fell.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

Throughout [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the Imperial City saw many great events. On the 19th of Mid Year the Potentate arrived in the Imperial City amid great fanfare, the streets lined with men and women cheering him as the symbol of the taking of [Ald Marak](Lore_Ald_Marak.md). During Sun's Dusk, preparations were made for the arrival of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md) in the Imperial Palace, an event attended by prominent nobles such as King Dro'Zel of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md). The audience chamber of the Palace dangled silver ribbons from the rafters, burning cauldrons of sweet herbs simmered in every corner, and Pyandonean swallowtails swept through the air, singing their songs. On the 6th of Evening Star Emperor Reman III was assassinated by the Morag Tong on behalf of Versidue-Shaie on his own throne. On the 22nd of Evening Star Versidue-Shaie announced from the Speaker's Balcony of the Imperial Palace that the Cyrodiil line was dead and that he would assume the role of Emperor but keep the title of Potentate, with his first act being the declaration of the beginning of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

### Second Era
In [2E 400](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_400), Potentate Savirien-Chorak held a celebration to commemorate four centuries of [potentate rule](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md). Festivities in the Imperial City included many games, revelries and a poetry competition.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PCo V-19)</sup>

#### Longhouse Emperors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg/200px-ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg) Artistic representation of Durcorach's conquest of Cyrodiil Sometime before [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), the [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) warlord [Durcorach the Black Drake](Lore_Durcorach.md) conquered the Imperial City, and was proclaimed Emperor of Cyrodiil shortly after. Durcorach married [Veraxia Tharn](Lore_Veraxia_Tharn.md) to legitimize his rule, and the so-called [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) would go on to rule over the Imperial City and Cyrodiil longer than most previous conquerors.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-COTFC4-20)</sup> His son, Emperor [Moricar the Middling](Lore_Moricar.md), preferred to stay in the Imperial City rather than accompanying his armies.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TWo S-21)</sup> After his own son, Prince [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), returned from his campaign against border raiders, he was received in the Great Hall of White-Gold, where he chose [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md) as his wife;<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Mt CCT-22)</sup> he had inherited the Ruby Throne by [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GCGP1-23)</sup>

#### Varen's Rebellion and the Soulburst
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) The Soulburst [Varen's Rebellion](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md) ended in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577) when Varen Aquilarios's forces stormed the Imperial City and confronted Leovic's last legion in a bloody battle that razed the Market District. Varen personally dispatched Leovic at the foot of the Ruby Throne before proclaiming himself emperor.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-GL-24)</sup>

The [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) of [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578) (or [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579)) was a massive explosion of arcane energy which sundered the veil between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and disrupted the connection between the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of the denizens of Nirn and their mortal hosts. The [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) themselves named this event after it was set into motion.

The necromancer [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) had convinced the Cyrodilic Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) that, despite not being [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md), as all true emperors must be, if they slightly modified the coronation ritual, they would be able to both relight the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) and convince [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to gift Varen with the blood of a dragon. However, Mannimarco had corrupted the coronation ritual of the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), having spent weeks inscribing glyphs and incantations that would weaken the ancient barriers protecting Nirn and tear the veil between worlds , setting the mortal realm adrift in Oblivion. Thus by performing the ritual, Varen broke the divine covenant of Akatosh and [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) which protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion.

As the veil between worlds shattered, a wave of magical energy radiated outwards from the Imperial City, and Emperor Aquilarios was lost in the explosion. Mystical aftershocks propagated all across the planet. Mages died or went mad, Red Mountain trembled, earthquakes shook Skyrim, heavy storms devastated the coastlines of Elsweyr and Valenwood, and Nirn came "unmoored from the fabric of the [multiverse](Lore_Aurbis.md)". The event caused numerous mages in the vicinity of Imperial City to die or go mad while mages in the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) suffered injuries. [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) was significantly affected when earthquakes ruptured the ground in a cataclysm dubbed by locals as the "Sundering", splitting the town and leaving a gaping fissure infested with [flame atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) that killed a number of the town's civilians. Wooden walkways were erected over the chasm but the noxious fumes caused many to fall to their deaths while crossing. The event was sensed by the magically sensitive across Tamriel, and the constellation of [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) grew so large it dominated the night sky and seemed to threaten every other star sign at once. Nirn became vulnerable to the forces of Oblivion, and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) began appearing in greater numbers than ever before. [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), the Daedric Prince of Domination and Mannimarco's master, seized the opportunity to begin disconnecting the souls of Nirn from their hosts to fuel the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), an attempt to merge his realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) with Nirn. The Soulburst also frayed the veil between Mundus and [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), causing a rise in the number of [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), revenants, and [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md) across Tamriel which became audible and visible to even average mortals. The weakening of the veil also made it easier for necromancers to raise corpses on an unprecedented scale.

At some point after the Soulburst, mysterious slate-skinned figures, known as Red Diamond Guardians, periodically appeared in the Imperial City to fight the Daedra. Each one had the Red Diamond encrusted upon their torso. No one knew where they came from or where they went after each fray.

The Imperial City [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) was blamed for the explosion and expelled from the city by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), with control of the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) given over to the "Fellowship of Anchorites" (a pseudonym for Mannimarco's [Worm Cult](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md)).

#### Planemeld and Planemeld Obverse
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-concept-22.jpg/200px-ON-concept-22.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-22.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-22.jpg) The three alliances fight for control of the City [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg) Dark Anchors over the Imperial City and Isle [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-concept-10.jpg/200px-ON-concept-10.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-10.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-10.jpg) Daedric Titans in the City During the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), the Imperial City was invaded by the Daedric forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), who were helped by the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) and the traitorous Legion Zero. [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchor.md) opened above the city, from which Daedra flooded out, killing and enslaving the citizens of the City. A giant Dark Anchor was then opened above the White-Gold Tower. The [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), and [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) invaded the City and extensively fought among themselves and against the legions of Daedra and Worm Cultists during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), in an effort to claim the Imperial City for themselves.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOCyrodiil-25)</sup>

In a final attempt to restart the Planemeld,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-26)</sup> Bal sent a [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) called [Molag Kena](Lore_Molag_Kena.md) to pose as the seemingly-deceased Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md) and "retake" the White-Gold Tower. In truth, Kena sought an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) within the Tower and planned to use its power to initiate the "Planemeld Obverse" and begin simultaneously pulling Coldharbour into Tamriel. However, a [moth priestess](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) called [Terran Arminus](Lore_Terran_Arminus.md) had read the scroll before the Daedric occupation of the Imperial City, and had learned that she would need aid in order to save the scroll. This aid arrived in the form of a group of [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), and the prophesied Soulless One, who helped Clivia's entourage ascend the shattered reality of the Tower's upper levels before being betrayed by the imposter. These heroes defeated Molag Kena atop the Tower before she could fully complete the ritual, and in the process destroyed the Anchor above the city.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-27)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-28)</sup>

Coinciding with the events at White-Gold Tower, Bal's forces began to search the city for a relic called the [Sublime Brazier](Lore_Sublime_Brazier.md). Housed in the hidden [Dragonfire Cathedral](Lore_Dragonfire_Cathedral.md) in the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins beneath the city, this brazier was key to relighting the Dragonfires.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-29)</sup> If it was destroyed, Akatosh's Covenant would be broken and Mundus would be eternally vulnerable to Daedric invasion. This plot was uncovered by the [Drake of Blades](Lore_Drake_of_Blades.md), a mysterious [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) agent still operating within the city. With the help of the Vestige and other allies, the Drake entered Dragonfire Cathedral and performed a ritual to become the brazier's immortal guardian. The Daedric siege of the cathedral was lifted in the process, and the leader of Bal's forces, Xivkyn General Malivus, was banished.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-IC-30)</sup> The Drake would serve as guardian for all time, effectively foiling Molag Bal's plot.

#### Tiber War
[Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) captured the Imperial City in [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854). Before he was crowned, however, he was assassinated, and [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md) then crowned [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) the new Emperor of all Cyrodiil.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TAH-31)</sup>

In [2E 857](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_857), the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) rose up in the Moth Rebellion. The Third Legion, mostly comprised of newly-recruited Colovian peasant boys, was sent from nearby Anvil to quell it. There the Legion earned its sobriquet "The Faithful" while besieged in the Hesod Barracks by the Cultists. After the rebellion ended, Tiber Septim triumphantly reentered the capital.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Attrebus-32)</sup>

### Third Era
[Destri Melarg](Lore_Destri_Melarg.md), famed Redguard historian and translator began his study and writings in the Imperial City in [3E 39](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_39), where he took the last name of Melarg due to the Breton, Nordic, and Dark Elf cultures he encountered in the City.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-NFRG-33)</sup>

Under [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md), the city was as infamously decadent as its Emperor. The result of this was the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), in which the Imperial City was captured in only a fortnight by [Uriel Septim III](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md) in [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121) as two other attacks drew Imperial forces away from the capital.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-34)</sup>

Circa [3E 145](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_145) - [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153), the Mad Emperor Pelagius Septim III had the Imperial Palace undergo frequent early-morning scrubdowns due to his obsession with cleanliness.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TMOP-35)</sup>

#### Imperial Simulacrum
In [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391), Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) accompanied her [daughter](Lore_Morgiah.md) and [son](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) on a journey to the Imperial City. Barenziah was there to plead with the emperor for aid, and hoped the presence of the children would soften his heart. However the [false emperor](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) remained unmoved, and when word came that evening that her husband Symmachus was killed in a revolt, the surviving royals were forced into exile.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-BOB3-36)</sup>

Circa [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), a [shadowmage](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md) named [Skelos Undriel](Lore_Skelos_Undriel.md) was driven from the Imperial City by Tharn's agents, and Undriel fled to the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md), a region at the time engulfed in the War of the Bend'r-mahk.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TESTS-37)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Sk SUd-38)</sup>

In [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) entered the Imperial City and descended into the depths of the Imperial Palace to face the imposter Jagar Tharn. Tharn was killed by the Champion, which freed Uriel Septim VII and [General Warhaft](Lore_Warhaft.md), putting an end to the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-39)</sup> Following the Restoration, a grand state memorial service was held for Symmachus at the Imperial City, befitting the man who had served the Septim Dynasty for so long and so well.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-BOB3-36)</sup>

Even years after the Imperial Simulacrum, the people were paranoid and unrest developed in the Imperial City. Wizards claimed that the Emperor's Sons; [Geldall](Lore_Geldall_Septim.md), [Ebel](Lore_Ebel_Septim.md), and [Enman](Lore_Enman_Septim.md) were actually [doppelgangers](Lore_Doppelganger.md) placed in the Imperial Palace by Jagar Tharn from when he was still the Imperial Battlemage. A mob developed calling for the death of these "false" heirs and [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) retaliated. Many people died in the ensuing conflict.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MWGen Unrest-40)</sup> The unrest became so drastic that members of the Imperial Legion in Morrowind believed they would be recalled to protect the Emperor.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-MWGen Recall-41)</sup>

#### Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/OB-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Light_the_Dragonfires.jpg) Dagon's invasion of the city during the Oblivion Crisis (Oblivion) The [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) began and ended in the Imperial City during [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), with the assassination of Uriel Septim VII by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md). Before he died, Septim gave the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) to a [prisoner](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) who had accompanied him and his Blades.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-OBtut-42)</sup> The prisoner then fled through the Imperial Prison's sewer system to [Weynon Priory](Lore_Weynon_Priory.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-43)</sup> After the Defense of Bruma and the death of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), the Hero of Kvatch and [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) rushed to the Imperial City to meet High Chancellor Ocato. Just as Ocato accepted Martin's claim to the throne they were alerted that Oblivion gates had begun to open all over the City. High Chancellor Ocato, Martin Septim, his Blades and Imperial Legionary escorts fought their way through a besieged Imperial City. The Daedric Prince [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) himself appeared in the city, marking the fall of the barriers between Oblivion and Nirn. The battle ended when Martin Septim, in a desperate last-ditch effort to light the Dragonfires, shattered the Amulet of Kings and became the Avatar of Akatosh. The Avatar banished Dagon back to Oblivion, ending the Crisis. The Avatar turned to stone, killing Martin, but also leaving a permanent reminder of his sacrifice in the form of a statue.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-OBLt D-44)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg/200px-BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg) Umbriel at the Imperial City Around [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede.md) captured the Imperial City with a thousand men and seized control of the Empire from [Thules the Gibbering](Lore_Thules_the_Gibbering.md), marking the start of the [Mede Dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md).

#### Umbriel Crisis
[Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) invaded [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), first appearing far off the coast of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), south of [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md). It was summoned there by the rogue [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) of Lilmoth, which was able to communicate to the similar [trees](Lore_Hist.md#The_Trees_of_Umbriel) native to Umbriel.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TIC-45)</sup><sup>:53</sup> It traveled north to [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md) and then west to the Imperial City in an attempt to free itself from Vile's realm using metaphysical power of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-LOS-46)</sup>

#### Great War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png/200px-LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png) The Forgotten Hero at the Battle of the Red Ring In [4E 172](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_172), during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), Aldmeri armies advanced deeper into Cyrodiil and by the end of the year, the Dominion general Lord Naarifin had advanced to the very walls of the Imperial City. There were fierce naval clashes in Lake Rumare and along the Niben as the Imperial forces attempted to hold the eastern bank.

In [4E 173](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_173), fresh legions from Skyrim bolstered the Emperor's main army in the Imperial City, but the Aldmeri forced the crossing of the Niben and began advancing in force up the eastern bank. By the end of the year, the Imperial City was surrounded on three sides with only the northern supply route to Bruma remaining open.

During the spring of [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174), Aldmeri reinforcements gathered in southern Cyrodiil and on 12th of [Second Seed](Lore_Second_Seed.md) they launched a massive assault on the Imperial City itself. One army of the Dominion drove north to completely surround the city, while Lord Naarifin's main force attacked the walls from the south, east, and west. The Eighth Legion fought a desperate rearguard action on the walls of the city, while Titus Mede II broke out of the city to the north with his main army, smashing through the surrounding Aldmeri forces and linking up with reinforcements marching south from Skyrim under General Jonna. Meanwhile, however, the capital fell to the invaders and the infamous Sack of the Imperial City began. The Imperial Palace was burned, the White-Gold Tower itself looted, and the elves perpetrated atrocities against the populace.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-47)</sup>

On the 30th of [Rain's Hand](Lore_Rain%27s_Hand.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Battle of the Red Ring occurred, named after the [road](Lore_Red_Ring_Road.md) that encircles the Imperial City, where Titus II split his remaining forces into three hosts that surrounded the occupied Imperial City. Titus himself was injured by assassins and unable to fight; however, unknown to his troops, his place was taken by the mysterious [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md), who donned his armor and led the Legion to victory. An attempt by the Aldmeri to break out of the city to the south was blocked by the unbreakable shieldwall of General Jonna's battered legions.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Lt Sot IC-48)</sup>

After a long, bloody battle, the Dominion army in Cyrodiil was utterly destroyed and the Imperial City recaptured. Lord Naarifin was hung from the top of White-Gold Tower and kept alive for thirty-three days.

The [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md#The_Warrior) of Rivercrest was known to have traveled to the Imperial City at some point before the Great War where they became a [Blade](Lore_Blades.md) and lived there until they were forced to flee due to the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-49)</sup>

## Layout & Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/OB-place-Imperial_City_Intro.png/200px-OB-place-Imperial_City_Intro.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City_Intro.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City_Intro.png) Imperial City districts At the center of the City is the Green Emperor Way, in which lies the White-Gold Tower at its own center. Beneath the city is an extensive underground whose sewers connect to various substructures which in turn break into the many natural caverns of the island. The White-Gold Tower in particular has a vast basement system, in which [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) and his servants took refuge, and which leads to an Ayleid ruin known as the Hall of Epochs. The underground is thus a haven for those who don't wish to be found: bandits, goblins and vampires dwell beneath, occasionally rising to endanger the city's inhabitants. Conversely, the Blades used secret passages such as the Old Way to move around the city, though using them can pose serious risks: Emperor Uriel Septim VII was assassinated by the Mythic Dawn in one of these passages beneath the Prison District.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-OBtut-42)</sup>

Through the Red Ring Road and the other main roads that span across the province, it is connected to a myriad of outer provincial settlements (i.e, Arenthia to the southwest in Valenwood, Gideon to the southeast in Black Marsh, Kragenmoor to the northeast in Morrowind, Riverhold to the south in Elsweyr, Riverwood to the north in Skyrim, and Stonedale to the northwest in Hammerfell).

### Arboretum District
The **Arboretum District** is the southeast district of the Imperial City. It consists of botanical gardens and statues to each of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md), with a statue of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) being in the center.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-GTTIC-58)</sup> The Arboretum contains an archival institution known as the Imperial Academy of Records and Histories in its east section that had millennia of scrupulous record-keeping as of the Second Era mostly regarding lineage, writs of stewardship, and letters patent. Its proximity to the institution makes it a popular haunt for the city's scholars.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Arboretum was being used to burn these archives by the invading [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). ### Arena District
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/LG-misc-Versus_Arena.jpg/200px-LG-misc-Versus_Arena.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-misc-Versus_Arena.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-misc-Versus_Arena.jpg) The Imperial Arena
- *"The best techniques are passed on by the survivors."* —Motto of the Imperial Arena

The **Arena District** is home to the Imperial Arena, also known as the **Bloody Jewel of the Imperial City**. It is located in the northeast of the Imperial City and contains little of note other than the Imperial Arena, which serves as a proving ground for warriors and as a popular attraction and gambling opportunity for citizen and tourist alike. Some citizens, however, believe that the fighting is faked, claiming the blood to be "berry syrup" and the fighting "ham-acting". Others say the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) runs the Arena to amuse the masses.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-62)</sup>

The Arena is also used for other events such as musical symphonies,<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-63)</sup> troupes of acrobats,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MS-51)</sup> and even mock naval battles.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-64)</sup>

The sewers that run beneath the district are known for being gory, such as the section known as Beneath the Bloodworks which previously served as a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) lair.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-65)</sup> The Harena Hypogeum, also beneath the Arena District, was held by the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-66)</sup>

Two statues are present in the district, the statue of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) in the south and the statue of [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) in the north. Alessia is depicted in her role as the first ruler of the Cyrodiilic Empire and the founder of the Eight Divines, while Morihaus is depicted as a pit fighter who led Alessia's armies.

#### History
The Imperial Arena was founded by [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md) between [1E 946](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_946) and [1E 947](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_947) under the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-OBLoading-67)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-68)</sup>

In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), a fight between Reman II's son Prince Juilek and the Potentate Versidue-Shaie's son Savirien-Chorak occurred here to see which of the Akaviri and Cyrodiilic fighting styles was better.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Molag Bal's forces took over the Imperial City and used the Arena for entertainment by forcing captured citizens to fight against [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) creatures such as Clannfear and Ogrim. The [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) participated in the Arena against such creatures and against the [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) Dredaza the Ringmaster, who acted as the Arena's announcer. When Dredaza was defeated, a [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) known as 'The Rat', a former advisor to [Emperor Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), gave the Vestige information regarding the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) and Molag Bal.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-69)</sup> During this period, members of all three alliances helped an [Imperial Legionary](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) named Valga Atrius in rescuing Imperial citizens from the Arena District.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-70)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Owyn was the blademaster of the Imperial Arena, and [Agronak gro-Malog](Lore_Agronak_gro-Malog.md), widely known as the "Gray Prince" was the Grand Champion of the Arena. During this period, the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) participated in the Arena, and eventually reached the title of Champion. The Hero found evidence that Agronak was half-vampire and presented it to Agronak, causing him to lose his will to fight. Agronak allowed the Hero to kill him in the Arena without resistance. This victory made the Hero of Kvatch the new Grand Champion of the Arena.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-71)</sup>

### Dragonfire Cathedral

The Cathedral's architecture is Ayleid, but it was repurposed in the early days of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) to house the Sublime Brazier, a relic essential to the lighting of the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-72)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Molag Bal sought to destroy the Sublime Brazier to prevent the Dragonfires from ever being relit, sending a Xivkyn General known as Malivus to spearhead an attack on the temple. While the attack was repelled, Father Egnatius, a priest of Akatosh, performed a ritual on the Brazier. ### Elven Gardens District
The **Elven Gardens District** is a residential district in the northwest of the Imperial City.

At some point before the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), the Elder Council had implemented policies concerning animal waste which had improved conditions in the Elven Gardens District.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-74)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the district had been taken over by [Legion Zero](Lore_Legion_Zero.md). The Legion Zero traitor Captain Regilus operated out of this district to assist the Vestige and the Drake of Blades. He was forced to drink the Blood of Coldharbour to maintain his cover, and fought off its influence long enough to steal a key to the Dragonfire Cathedral from the Daedra.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-75)</sup> At the same time, Quintia Rullus of the Fourth Legion was assisted by soldiers of the three alliances in destroying Legion Zero's siege engines and forge in an effort to prevent the destruction of the Imperial City.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-76)</sup>

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the rival Ayleid relic collectors [Herminia Cinna](Lore_Herminia_Cinna.md) and [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) had taken up residence in the Imperial City. The former had come into possession of the [Crown of Nenalata](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md), a very valuable Ayleid artifact, and a key to the sealed royal burial chamber of [Lindai](Lore_Lindai.md). Her rival collector, Umbacano, attempted to purchase the crown from her, hoping to use it to declare himself King of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md) and lead a rebirth of Ayleid civilization in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Fearing Umbacano's ulterior motives, she refused, and Umbacano sent the Hero of Kvatch to steal it from her house in the Elven Gardens District. Cinna instead offered the key, which would have allowed the Hero to retrieve the Crown of Lindai in order to trick Umbacano. It is unknown if the Hero went ahead with this plan or not, but in either case Umbacano died in Nenalata.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-77)</sup>

### Talos Plaza District
The **Talos Plaza District** (previously the **Nobles District**) is the western district of the Imperial City. The wealthy primarily live in this exclusive district, and it houses the Tiber Septim hotel, the most expensive and luxurious hotel in the Imperial City.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-78)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a group of Imperial loyalists known as the [Immortal Eight](Lore_Immortal_Eight.md) held a garrison in the middle of the Nobles District against the forces of Molag Bal and Legion Zero. The name of this group came from the fact that they could not permanently die: when they did, they simply woke up again. Each of these eight may well have been physical manifestations of the Eight Divines, as each was closely associated with one. The Vestige assisted them in holding off the forces of Molag Bal and discovered how they were immortal.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-79)</sup> Their Captain, Anatolius Caudex, also assisted in protecting the Sublime Brazier.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-TSub Braz-73)</sup>

At the same time a Redguard hunter named Brihana made a trade of setting traps for Ogrim in the District, selling their parts to alchemists and Khajiiti traders. ### Temple District
The **Temple District** (also called the **Cathedral District**)<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-CM1-81)</sup> is located in the southwest of the Imperial City and is home to The Temple of the One, the largest religious structure in Cyrodiil. This great structure, a staple of ceremony and official worship since the days of Reman I and the institution of the Dragonfire ritual, is dedicated to the ineffable deity of the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md). They called this spirit 'the One', identifying it as [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), the 'Supreme Spirit'. Although the Remans were not known to follow Alessian teachings, they regularly used the temple to worship, and Tiber Septim restored the building and its associated cult when he took the Dragon Throne. The District is also home to other temples dedicated to the Divines, such as the [Benevolence of Mara](Lore_Benevolence_of_Mara.md)'s cathedral.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MS-51)</sup> There are thousands of cults operating in the Temple District and the Imperial City at large, such as the priests of St. [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), the [Cult of Heroes](Lore_Cult_of_Heroes.md), the [Cult of Emperor Zero](Lore_Cult_of_Emperor_Zero.md), the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), and the cult of [Shezarr](Lore_Shezarr.md), to name but a few.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-82)</sup>

It is said that Empress Alessia was buried on the site of the Temple of the One; but this is decidedly false, and the true location of her body is unknown.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-83)</sup> Some notable people lived in the Temple District, such as [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) in the late years of Savirien-Chorak's reign<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-CM1-81)</sup> and Symmachus, who had a house near the Temple of the One, two blocks away from the Imperial Palace.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TRB3-84)</sup>

#### History
The [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) occurred here in [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), when [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) deliberately corrupted the coronation ritual of [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md).<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-85)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Vestige saved members of the Imperial City's clergy in the temple from a [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) known as Xotos Vel. Among those saved was Father Egnatius, who played a crucial role in saving the Dragonfires. Soldiers of all three alliances also helped a Khajiit priestess known as Sister J'Reeza protect the temple's sacred artifacts. They did so by placing wards on the artifacts which would cause any Daedra who touched them a great deal of pain.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-86)</sup>

In [3E 41](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_41), [Pelagius Septim](Lore_Pelagius_Septim.md) was murdered by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) while praying in the Temple of the One.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-34)</sup>

The Temple was the scene of the fiery resolution of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), where Martin Septim's sacrifice caused the banishment of Dagon from Mundus and created the famous statue of Akatosh.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-OBLt D-44)</sup>

### Waterfront District
The **Waterfront District** is found in the southwest of the Imperial City. It serves as the main harbor for the City and as a residential area for the poorer residents. Ships from throughout Tamriel come here for trading, transporting goods, and other business.

The harbor part of the District is full of traders, privateers, sailors, the destitute and is home to the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md). The Imperial Trading Company has its warehouses here alongside an office. The Waterfront lighthouse is connected to the harbor and lights the way for ships at night, it is controlled by the Imperial Legion.

The residential parts of the District are home to hundreds of shacks, shanties, and lean-tos that crowd between the wall and the water, and many are built raised up from the water on stilts. The District is home to many different neighborhoods of different races, such as the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) neighborhood extant in the Second Era, with its tavern named the Tipsy Torchbug.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-87)</sup> While Argonians are rarely found residing within the walls of the Imperial City, large numbers are known to dwell in the Waterfront District. 'The Xanmeer', a large building found in the district is clear evidence of their impact there.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-88)</sup> Gondolas and river-ships sail along the watery avenues of the district's flooded lower dwellings.

#### History
During the Planemeld in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Molag Kena](Lore_Molag_Kena.md) (under the guise of Empress Clivia Tharn) ordered troops to be sent to defend the Waterfront District. General Velarius told her that as the District held no strategic value, and those men would be sent to die for nothing, refusing her orders and accusing her of stepping out of line for "playing general", for which she lacked the expertise. Kena threatened exposing extra postings and his "questionable command decisions" which he had ordered to protect his daughter. This failed to faze Velarius, confident in the Legion's continued loyalty to him.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-ESOGV-89)</sup>

Since [3E 380](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_380) the poor here had not been taxed despite Imperial law decrees that all citizens of the City must pay taxes, but in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Imperial Watch Captain Hieronymous Lex reinstated taxes on the poor as punishment for them being complicit in assisting the Gray Fox. This money was taken back by the Thieves Guild to send the Imperial Guard a message that it was unacceptable.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-90)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-91)</sup>

[Emer Dareloth](Lore_Emer_Dareloth.md), the first [Gray Fox](Lore_Gray_Fox.md), originally lived on the Waterfront. However, a curse was placed upon him and his successors by [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), stripping them of their identity so that they would only be known as the Gray Fox. This caused the Thieves Guild to fall on hard times, and Dareloth's house fell into dereliction after his death. When Nocturnal's curse was broken by [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md) using the power of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), time was retroactively changed, undoing the years of hardship suffered by the guild. As a result, the ruins of Dareloth had instead become a fully-fledged guildhall for the Thieves Guild.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-92)</sup>

The first time [Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) ever went to the Waterfront shantytown in [4E 41](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_41) was when he was fifteen. He was attracted by its reputation of being the poorest and most dangerous part of the Imperial City. In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), the [Umbriel Crisis](Lore_Umbriel_Crisis.md) was reaching the Heartlands. The [floating city](Lore_Umbriel.md)'s leader: Vuhon enlisted help from the Emperor's Minister, [Hierem](Lore_Hierem.md) and gave him undead thralls to stalk the wilderness and hide in Lake Rumare to keep people from the Imperial City. These "[wormies](Lore_Wormies.md)" overran the Waterfront District, leaving it empty and silent.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Lo S-93)</sup><sup>:Part 3, Chapter 3/5</sup>

Attrebus Mede, carrying his unconscious companion, Sul reached the Waterfront District at night via a boat. He was, however, attacked by wormies from underneath him and taken into custody by a man, who recognized him as the prince. They in turn brought him to Hierem, who tortured and kept them imprisoned until members of the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md), Colin Vineben, and Letine Arese rescued them.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Lo S-93)</sup><sup>:Part 3, Chapter 3/5</sup>
