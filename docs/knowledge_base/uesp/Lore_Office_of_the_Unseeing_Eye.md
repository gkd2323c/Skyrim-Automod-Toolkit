## Oaken Order
The [Oaken Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oaken_Order) (or the **Oaken Knights**) was a [Breton Knightly Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders) active in the [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Because the Oaken Order prioritized magic over martial combat, members of the Oaken Order were typically battlemages, conjurers, and support spellcasters. Their order was speculated to have been created by [druids](Lore_Druid.md), though membership also extended to outsiders (even non-mages) willing to learn [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre)'s teachings. They maintained their druidic connections, often citing them as their foundation for their high level of magical understanding. Some members of their order have overshadowed [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) members in the art of [Destruction](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Destruction) and [Conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) magic. The guild's conjurers were especially threatened by the Oaken Order's skills, downplaying them as practitioners of hedge magic. The [Order of the Iron Knot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Iron_Knot) saw the Oaken Order as lacking chivalry and respect for combat in the way a traditional knight would.

The Sapphire Tournament, hosted by [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) at Castle Navire, serves as a proving ground for squires seeking entry into the Systrean knightly orders. The Oaken Order is one of the three orders that take part, with the other two being the [Order of the Albatross](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Albatross) and the [Order of the Iron Knot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Iron_Knot). Each sets its own distinctive trial. Participation earns favor, though each order selects only one or two aspirants for knighthood. Most contenders attempt a single challenge, but those who succeed in all three are granted a powerful boon.

## Office of the Unseeing Eye
The **Office of the Unseeing Eye** seems to be an Imperial faction operating alongside the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), and has dealt with intelligence gathering on a Daedric artifact: when the [Gray Cowl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gray_Cowl) was supposedly stolen from the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), the Office investigated rumors of the incident and directed a triad of Moth priests to determine the validity of the tale, its current owner, and how the curse associated with the Cowl can be lifted, so that the Moths priests may wield the Cowl without being affected by the curse.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IGC-1)</sup>

## Order of the Albatross
The [Order of the Albatross](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Albatross) was a [Breton Knightly Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders) active in the [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). These swashbuckling warriors were often singers or bards but they also tended to engage in piracy. Fontenot Leila founded the order to give those children of nobility who could not look forward to their parent's inheritance a means of securing their own legacies. Their numbers, both in active knights and percentage of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) explored, greatly exceeded that of other knightly orders based in the archipelago. This was made possible by the order's focus on adventure, discovery, and daring feats, which drew initiates from the mainland..

The Sapphire Tournament, hosted by [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) at Castle Navire, serves as a proving ground for squires seeking entry into the Systrean knightly orders. The Order of the Albatross is one of the three orders that take part, with the other two being the [Order of the Iron Knot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Iron_Knot) and the [Oaken Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oaken_Order). Each sets its own distinctive trial. Participation earns favor, though each order selects only one or two aspirants for knighthood. Most contenders attempt a single challenge, but those who succeed in all three are granted a powerful boon.

## Order of Arkay
The [Order of Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Arkay) (also called the **Temple of Arkay**) is the temple dedicated to [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) the Mortals' God of Birth and Death. They are generally the place of worship for him. The order reveres the neutrality of Arkay; it is absolute to them and, because of this, they will not offer strangers their blessings. To the order, to give favor or curse to another person will upset the eternal balance. Their expansive collection of libraries and repertoire of sages is considered some of the finest in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), however, the order is rather private and does not take applications lightly. The [Knights of the Circle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Circle) is their militant arm and the [House of Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Dibella) is their enemy. In the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), the order has many chapels, some of which include the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and the [Ilessan Hills](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ilessan_Hills) and [Shalgora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shalgora) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).

## Order of Diagna
The [Order of Diagna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Diagna) was a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) order of [Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ansei) that would go on to become one of the many knightly orders in the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) area. It is devoted to the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) deity [Diagna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Diagna), "Orichalc God of the Sideways Blade". Initiates wishing to join the Order must play the part of the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) in their annual recreation of the Siege of Orsinium. The order was mentioned as being extinct by the Third Era.

The Order was chiefly involved in the invasion of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), which lasted for thirty years. Fighters from the Order of Diagna kept totems of Diagna for luck during the siege. The leader of the order, [Gaiden Shinji](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gaiden_Shinji), was killed during the siege. His aide was Windwalker Tamahl, a member of the Order. Armiger [Rhina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rhina) was an Armiger in the Order of Diagna who died in the final days of the Siege of Orsinium. Born in the siege camp there, she spent her entire twenty-six years fighting the Orcs, where her sacrifice was said to have brought the Redguards victory.

## Order of Flame and Shadow
The [Order of Flame and Shadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Flame_and_Shadow) was a [Breton Knightly Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders) active in the [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago) at some point before the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). It was a mysterious Knightly Order, and as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) it was disbanded. According to some, it was nothing more than a legend. Tomes and histories indicate this order was comprised of a sole knight, known as the **Knight of Flame and Shadow**, who passes the mantle to a protege after an extensive and secretive training regiment. The Knight of Flame and Shadow had been sighted on a few occasions over the centuries, both working alongside the nobility and also actively entreating an uprising among the common folk. Little is known about the identity, motivations, and whereabouts of this line of knights. As of 2E 582, the Knight of Flame and Shadow hasn't been heard from for a long period of time. It was speculated that the last holder of the title perished before they were able to pass on the legacy.

## Order of Saint Pelin
The [Order of Saint Pelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Saint_Pelin) or **Knights of Saint Pelin** is a [knightly order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights) of the kingdom of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The order is named after [Saint Pelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saint_Pelin), who sacrificed himself to defeat the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) as they assaulted the [Bangkorai Pass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Pass) in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). They are responsible for maintaining a library of obscure historical scrolls at [Bangkorai Garrison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Garrison) and have been called upon for the defense of the capital city on occasion. They are responsible for holding the Bangkorai Pass and protect the weak, in hopes that they can be as brave and humble as Pelin.

## Order of Talos
The [Order of Talos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Talos) was a religious order dedicated to spreading the teachings and worship of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md). They worshipped Talos as both a [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) and a [patron saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Saints). They had close ties to the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), and many members of the Blades retired to positions in the order. [Weynon Priory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Weynon_Priory) was at one time run by the order, since as early as [3E 360](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_360). The most notable member of the order is, perhaps, Grandmaster [Jauffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jauffre), who lived at the priory for a time before returning to [Cloud Ruler Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloud_Ruler_Temple) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). Following the signing of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md), the Blades were formally disbanded and the worship of Talos was banned in the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). As such, it could be assumed that the Order of Talos is no longer in existence.

## Order of the Black Worm
The [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm) is the cult led by [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) which is devoted to [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy). Those outside the Order sometimes call it the **Cult of the Black Worm** or the **Worm Cult**, although the organization was officially known as the **Fellowship of Anchorites** during its brief Imperial charter. Being the most infamous practitioners of necromancy, they are colloquially known as **the Necromancers** or the **Necromancer's Cult**. While [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) formed his [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in the early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Mannimarco secretly formed the Order in its shadows, appointing himself archnecromancer. Their many dark accomplishments include aiding the forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), the great battle where Galerion was killed, and numerous plots against the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), including an attempt during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) which resulted in the apparent death of Mannimarco. Members and servants of the cult are often branded with a black pentagram, to symbolize the damnation of their souls.

## Order of the Braided Vine
The [Order of the Braided Vine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Braided_Vine) was a [Breton Knightly Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders) active in the [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). It was a minor Knightly Order founded and primarily composed of the [Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids) of the [Stonelore Circle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonelore_Circle). Members of the Order were dedicated to the protection of the landmarks and places sacred to the Druids, such as certain groves and various island locations. It is unknown if this order was created to pique interest in druidism, or if it was a genuine attempt to meld their lifestyle into modern [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) culture.

## Order of the Candle
The [Order of the Candle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Candle), which replaced [Knights of the Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Moon) as the official protectors and warriors of the Kingdom of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), was a knightly order founded early in the Fifth Century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). The Order was charged with protecting the royal family and kingdom of Sentinel. The Order was treated with high respect in Sentinel, and only skilled men and women with high loyalty to the royal family of Sentinel were able to join the Order. It was initially led by Lord Vhosek and Lord [K'avar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:K%27avar). They were known for their affinity for espionage and worn numerous different uniforms whenever they needed to go into combat incognito.

## Order of the Crypt
The **Order of the Crypt** is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [knightly order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders) of spell-knights that originally served as keepers of the [Crypt of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md) and protectors within the kingdom of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md). The Order had taken a vow to not just guard the Crypt of Hearts, but to guard the people against those who might seek to use their hearts in briarheart rituals. They eventually broadened their goals, becoming legendary crusaders in the process.

## Order of the Cup
The [Order of the Cup](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Cup) was a [Breton Knightly Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders) active in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Among their duties was serving as the protectors of the [Plessington Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Plessington_Clan). As of [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), they were led by Lord Plessington of [Ykalon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ykalon). As of then they were neither strong in numbers nor very powerful, but they were very eager to help in all the affairs of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Lord Plessington was known to send his knights off to right various wrongs, seemingly without ulterior motives. Knights often returned from those quests with a number of interesting stories.

The events of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) led to the incorporation of the fiefdom of Ykalon into the [Kingdom of Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md). It also caused most of the forests in Ykalon to burn down. It is unknown how this affected the members of the Plessington Clan, and knights of the Order of the Cup, however.

## Order of the Dragon
The [Order of the Dragon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Dragon) is an order of [knights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights) originally founded by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). ## Order of the Eye
The [Order of the Eye](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Eye) is a sub-faction of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) that specializes in the art of [sight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) and use their abilities to scour tombs and ruins for extraplanar threats like the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They also seek out [daedric artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) and rifts created from the veil that separates [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Members of the order are called Scryers and are normally taught by the Docent.

The Order of the Eye utilizes a myriad of tools in their profession, such as a scrying-bowl filled with [water](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Water_Magic), a gazing mirror, and spheres that are sometimes made from metal like [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md). They also practice augury and use flocks of birds to receive their sight.

## Order of the Hour
The [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) (also called the **Hour Order**) is one of the oldest [knightly orders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights) in the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire.md), serving as the militant arm of the [Akatosh Chantry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akatosh_Chantry). Their sole purpose is to enact the will of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) the Great Dragon, as well as preserve the stability and permanence the God of Time represents. The Order has scattered and re-organized on several occasions throughout history, but their mission has remained unchanged.

## Order of the Lily
The [House of Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Dibella) (archaically known as the **Diblashuut**) is the temple dedicated to [Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella) the Blessed Lady, Goddess of Beauty and Love. They are generally the place of worship for Dibella. The priests and priestesses within are epicureans and orgiasts who aspire for the beauty of the form and mind. Their establishments are known to teach healing. The Order of the Lily is the House of Dibella's militant arm, and the [Benevolence of Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Benevolence_of_Mara) is considered their allies. The House of Dibella is considered enemies of the [Akatosh Chantry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akatosh_Chantry), the [Order of Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Arkay), and the cult of [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine). ## Order of the Raven
The [Order of the Raven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Raven) (also called the **Knights of the Raven**) is a [knightly order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders) found in the [Barony of Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen). They are a legendary order that fought behind Baron [Othrok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Othrok) in his battle against the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) and the [Nightmare Host](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nightmare_Host) that plagued the region in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267). They have a high standard for recruits, as they only accept the greatest warriors that can remain loyal to the Barony.

## Order of the Scarab
The [Knights of the Scarab](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Scarab) are [Gallants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gallant) serving as the official guard for the royal family of [Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Totambu) in Northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). ## Order of the Waking Flame
The [Order of the Waking Flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Waking_Flame) (or simply the **Waking Flame**) was a secret cult devoted to the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of Destruction, [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). The Waking Flame operated during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and worked with the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) to create the [Four Ambitions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Four_Ambitions). ## Ordinators
The [Ordinators](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordinators) (sometimes called Temple Ordinators) were the holy guards and priest-soldiers of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) led by the [Alma Rula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alma_Rula). They are divided into four distinct orders. The Order of the Watch provided guards for temples and shrines, and additionally acted as town guards in Temple-owned settlements such as [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City). They were responsible for enforcing Temple law, along with [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) law following the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md). The Order of War (also known as the Militant Ordinators) fought the enemies of the Temple, with most War Ordinators traditionally stationed near [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to combat the forces of the [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House). The Order of the Inquisition identifies and suppresses heresy among priests and laymen within the Temple. The Order of Doctrine and Ordination are militant scholars who opposed the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) Prophecies and other dissident teachings.

The [Oreyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oreyn) family is an [Elvish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elf) bloodline that consisted of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) that at one point in time ruled a kingdom in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).

## Orgak Clan
The [Orgak Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgak_Clan) were one of the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) clans of the northern [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md). They were founded by Kloth, and were known for their Scepter of the Riekr King-Chief.
