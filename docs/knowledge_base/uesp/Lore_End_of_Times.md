# End of Times

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/TR-npc-Eno_Romari.jpg/200px-TR-npc-Eno_Romari.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-Eno_Romari.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-Eno_Romari.jpg) Eno Romari The End of Times was a popular suicide cult created in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) just before the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), active in the palace-city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) and led by a young Dunmer named Eno Romari. Romari claimed the End of Times to be a peaceful group devoted to relieving suffering and helping the troubled to find enlightenment through understanding of what is happening in the world and what is to come in the next.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tribunal Eno Romari-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRAlmalexia-2)</sup> The cult prophesied that the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) would lose their divine powers and that the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) would pass through the gates of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and invade [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Tribunal-3)</sup> The cult's belief in a coming calamity by the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) was proven when the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) came to pass at the closing of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Ob MQ-4)</sup>

## History

### Origins
How Eno Romari developed his beliefs is unknown, but his cult became quite popular in the palace-city of Mournhold in the closing days of the Third Era.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRASP-5)</sup>

### Beliefs
The followers of the End of Times believed that the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md)'s power was fleeting, and that the cosmic fall of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) would cause ripples throughout the lands, heralding the end of the age and a time a many changes: with the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) invading [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) with their hordes of Daedra, flooding from the gates of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), roaming the world freely, and washing over the land to destroy all that man and mer have built over thousands of years.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tribunal Eno Romari-1)</sup>

The End of Times denied the Daedra as ancestors, and declared that they are not allies to mortals despite what some will claim.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> To combat this calamity, the End of Times preached about a "glorious sacrifice" called the Cleansing, wherein a follower would cleanse themselves of all worldly possessions and burns on earth, and "send themselves" to the "true [Ancestors](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md#Dunmer)" in a suicide ritual to spread word amongst the Ancestors and watch over or protect mortal kind in preparation for when "all shall join them" in the fight against the Daedric hordes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tribunal Eno Romari-1)</sup>

### Intervention of Almalexia
The land-god [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) sent the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) to investigate the cult, and then had the Nerevarine manufacture a storm on her behalf using the [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) [Weather Witch](Lore_Weather_Witch.md) machine as a show of "her" power against those that would doubt her divinity and follow the End of Times. Subsequently, Almalexia sent her [ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) to deal with the cult and its leader.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRAlmalexia-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRASP-5)</sup> It's unknown what the ultimately became of the cult following these events.
