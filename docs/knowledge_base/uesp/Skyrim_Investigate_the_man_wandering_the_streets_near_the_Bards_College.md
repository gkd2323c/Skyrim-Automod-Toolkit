## Quick Walkthrough
1. Investigate a man called [Dervenin](Skyrim_Dervenin.md).
2. Gain access to the [Pelagius Wing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_Wing) of the [Blue Palace](Skyrim_Blue_Palace.md).
3. Search for [Dervenin's master](Skyrim_Sheogorath.md) in the Pelagius Wing.
4. Complete the First Trial.
5. Complete the Second Trial.
6. Complete the Third Trial.
7. Escape from [Pelagius Wing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_Wing).

## Detailed Walkthrough

### Solitude
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/SR-npc-Dervenin.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Dervenin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dervenin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dervenin.jpg) *"You'll help me! You help people, right? That's what you do?"* In [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), you may hear a rumor from innkeepers that a mad beggar is wandering around the streets of the city, distressed over the absence of his master, saying things like: *"Please, take pity on an old madman!"* and *"You! You'll help me! You help people, right? That's what you do?"*

The beggar, named [Dervenin](Skyrim_Dervenin.md), will plead you to find his master: *"My master has abandoned me! Abandoned his people. And nothing I say can change his mind. Now he refuses to even see me. He says I interrupt his vacation! It's been so many years... Won't you please help?"* He will tell you that his master was "visiting a friend in the [Blue Palace](Skyrim_Blue_Palace.md)" and further detail that his master "went into the [forbidden wing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_Wing) of the palace, to speak with an old friend". He will give you [Pelagius' Hip Bone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius%27_Hip_Bone), required to enter the forbidden wing. You also need to retrieve the key to the wing, either from one of the [cleaning](Skyrim_Erdi.md) [staff](Skyrim_Una.md) or from [Falk Firebeard](Skyrim_Falk_Firebeard.md), the steward.

### Blue Palace
Enter the Blue Palace and locate one of the cleaning staff. The key can be obtained from [Erdi](Skyrim_Erdi.md) or [Una](Skyrim_Una.md) by discussing the Pelagius Wing with her and telling her that Falk asked you to "look into it". You must pass an easy level, invisible [Speech check](Skyrim_Speech.md#Persuasion_Options) to persuade her to give you the key. Alternatively, you can convince [the castle steward](Skyrim_Falk_Firebeard.md) to let you in if you have completed the quest [The Man Who Cried Wolf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Man_Who_Cried_Wolf).

If you lack the respective requirement, the final dialogue option will not appear; the NPC will simply exit dialogue.

Exploring briefly in the wing will show it in a state of disrepair. Further travel will require you to walk through some spider webs before you suddenly find yourself transported into a [strange realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_a_Madman).

### Pelagius' Mind
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg/145px-SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wabbajack.jpg)"It's all you've got."You will find yourself suddenly wearing a full set of Fine Clothes with your inventory inaccessible and your abilities removed. The scene before you appears to be an extravagant dinner set in the middle of a misty wood and surrounded by stone archways. Two people are sitting at the table, one wearing a distinctive outfit and sitting on a throne, and the other a depressed man. The latter is [Pelagius III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_the_Mad) who is complaining of various woes, although he sounds strangely sane, which delights the man across from him. Pelagius will leave after a short conversation during which the other man tries to cheer him up.

Upon further investigation, it then transpires that the other individual is none other than [Sheogorath](Skyrim_Sheogorath.md) himself. Tell him that you have a message for him and that he needs to come back from vacation. Sheogorath says he will leave if you can make your way out of Pelagius' mind. He will give you the [Wabbajack](Skyrim_Wabbajack.md) to help you. There are three arches which lead to three separate trials, all of which must be completed. To the southeast is the Arch of Confidence, to the northeast is the Arch of Night Terrors and to the northwest is the Arch of Paranoia. These trials can be completed in any order.

### Pelagius' Confidence
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_02.jpg) Pelagius' Anger beating up Pelagius' Confidence In this scene you will see Pelagius' [Anger](Skyrim_Anger.md) beating up Pelagius' [Confidence](Skyrim_Confidence_(NPC).md). Confidence doesn't look like he is doing too well, since he is tiny and Anger is huge. Sheogorath wants you to tip the scales. Use the Wabbajack on Anger (casting twice) until he is tiny, and then on Confidence (again casting twice) until he is as big as Anger was. You must act quickly, as Confidence will shrink every time Anger hits him. During the battle, two spectral figures labeled [Self Doubt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Self_Doubt) will appear and begin to spar with each other. They do not seem to serve any purpose other than to get in your way as you attempt to complete the trial.

### Pelagius' Night Terrors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_03.jpg) Pelagius with his converted night terrors in the background You will see a bed in the middle of the forest with Pelagius sleeping on it. Sheogorath tells you that Pelagius has been suffering from night terrors since he was a child, and you are tasked with getting rid of them. Use the Wabbajack on [Pelagius the Tormented](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_the_Tormented) to start the sequence, and then use it on each terror in turn as they arrive. After a successful attack with the Wabbajack each will turn into something more innocuous. Do not attack the innocuous creatures; instead, use the Wabbajack on Pelagius again to call the next terror. Repeat this process of zapping Pelagius and then the creatures that appear until he wakes up. Don't take too long in zapping the creatures after they appear, or you will have to start over. In order, the creatures or enemies that appear are:

1. A [wolf](Skyrim_Wolf.md) will appear (to be turned rather humiliatingly into a goat).
2. A [bandit chief](Skyrim_Bandit.md) will then appear, to be turned into a younger version of Pelagius.
3. A [hagraven](Skyrim_Hagraven.md) will appear. After use of the Wabbajack, it will be turned into one of Pelagius' [sultry maidens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sultry_Maiden), from his early days.
4. A [flame atronach](Skyrim_Flame_Atronach.md) will appear. It turns into a bonfire after use of the staff.
5. A [dragon priest](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md) will appear, to be turned into an inaccessible chest behind the bonfire. (The Dragon Priest may summon an atronach as well, but Wabbajack has no effect on it, so you should ignore it.)

After all of the terrors have been converted, everyone will convene around the bonfire, and Sheogorath will communicate with you and ask you to progress to the next area.

### Pelagius' Paranoia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6d/SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_04.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Mind_of_Madness_04.jpg) Pelagius' Paranoia Through the Arch of Paranoia to the northwest is a small fort-like structure with steps leading up to the edge. In the middle of this structure there is an arena occupied by two fighting Storm Atronachs. Supposedly, one is yours and one is your [opponent's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Pelagius_the_Suspicious), who is sitting opposite you with two other men. You are instructed to use Wabbajack to defeat your enemy. Don't bother zapping the atronachs themselves, as one will respond to the changes the other goes through, making the fight endless. Instead, aim for one of the two [bodyguards](Skyrim_Bodyguard_(Pelagius).md) sitting next to your opponent and Wabbajack them. Both of the men will turn into wolves and attack your opponent, killing him.

### Your Reward
Go back to Sheogorath when you've finished his tasks and tell him you've fixed Pelagius' mind. He will summon Dervenin and return to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), telling you to keep Wabbajack. He mentions his chamberlain, [Haskill](Lore_Haskill.md), as he prepares to depart, saying, *"Well, I suppose it's back to the Shivering Isles. The trouble Haskill can get into while I'm gone simply boggles the mind..."* You will then be transported back to the Pelagius Wing with all the items and powers you had when you started, as well as your new staff and clothes. Before leaving, move further down the hall and down the stairs to find a copy of the [Lockpicking](Skyrim_Lockpicking.md) [skill book](Skyrim_Skill_Books.md) *[Surfeit of Thieves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Surfeit_of_Thieves).*

## Notes
- This quest contains several references to events in [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion) and the [Shivering Isles expansion.](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles) See [here](Skyrim_Elder_Scrolls_Historical_References.md#Oblivion) for more information.
- The Items, Magic, Map, and Skills keys are disabled during the quest, but the Map can be opened by first going to Quest, then pressing M to show on map, then pressing L to show local. Consequently, it is also impossible to open the chest in the Night Terrors scene. The Favorites menu and hotkeys are also disabled. - If you open the menu using the method above (or by pressing X on a highlighted quest, if playing on Xbox 360, or by pressing Square on a highlighted quest, if playing on PS3) you can fast travel out of Pelagius's mind. However, you will be unable to open menus, favorites, or change equipment. You will still be wearing the same outfit as in Pelagius's mind and be holding the Wabbajack.
- It is also possible to access your inventory while in Pelagius' mind; at the Night Terrors scene after the wolf is turned into a goat, other terrors can kill it. Its body can be "looted", rendering you able to take off your current equipment.
- During the quest, your Wabbajack will have infinite charges.
- The items on Sheogorath's dining table are unowned, and therefore, free to take.
- Once the quest is complete, the Wabbajack and fine clothes set will remain equipped on the Dragonborn. Therefore, you'll have to re-equip your weapons and armor manually.
- You cannot pickpocket Sheogorath or Pelagius the Mad, but you can pickpocket Pelagius' Confidence (once Pelagius' Anger is defeated) and Dervenin (once he is summoned).
- When Sheogorath sends Dervenin back to the Shivering Isles, Dervenin's beard remains intact. This doesn't make sense, as beards were banned there.
- The enemies meant to attack Pelagius in the Night Terrors section may attack you, instead.
- Right before you enter Pelagius' mind, you may appear naked for a brief moment.
- Anything you pick up while in Pelagius' mind will transfer with you to the real world once the quest is complete.
- If you open your map via your journal, you will be placed north of [Bleakwind Basin](Skyrim_Bleakwind_Basin.md).
- Punching or pickpocketing Dervenin after he is summoned by Sheogorath constitutes a crime and will net you a bounty in [Haafingar](Skyrim_Haafingar.md).
- This quest can be done as early as level 1. If your Speech skill is too low to convince the maids to let you into the Pelagius Wing, you can increase the skill with [Fortify Persuasion](Skyrim_Fortify_Persuasion.md) effects. You can get [The Gift of Charity](Skyrim_The_Gift_of_Charity.md) bonus from Dervenin by giving him 1 gold before asking him about anything else, and you can get the [Blessing of Dibella](Skyrim_Blessing_of_Dibella.md) from the [Temple of the Divines](Skyrim_Temple_of_the_Divines.md) in Solitude. If both are done, your Speech skill will temporarily be at least 35, and that will be enough to convince the maids to let you into the Pelagius Wing.

## Bugs
- If you enter Pelagius's Mind while you have the [Stray Dog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stray_Dog) in your company, the dog won't be in the Wing when you return—other followers such as [Lydia](Skyrim_Lydia.md) (human) or [Barbas](Skyrim_Barbas.md) (dog) are unaffected. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.1, fixes this bug.
- The game may freeze on transition into Pelagius's Mind. This is probably just a "normal" transition freeze, but it may be caused by the quest-specific behavior that occurs at this point, and may not allow you to continue with the game. It is possible the freeze is caused by having more than 999 items of one kind - likely to be one or more types of arrows - in which case the freeze can be avoided by dumping enough of the offending item(s) to reduce the count to 999 or less, before reaching the transition point to Pelagius's Mind.
- The Wabbajack may not auto-equip itself on you once Sheogorath gives you it. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.4, fixes this bug.
- Pelagius's Mind is technically an outdoor area and can spawn dragons. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.9.26.0.8, fixes this bug.
- Exiting the Pelagius Wing during and after the quest can cause the game to crash on PS5 and Xbox Series X. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.342, fixes this bug.
- When you complete all the tasks for Sheogorath, he will bid you farewell but you will not actually leave Pelagius' mind. - You must exit the dialogue with Sheogorath to trigger a scene that will lead to your leaving.
- Loading from an earlier save usually fixes this.
- If you have a [Dead Thrall](Skyrim_Dead_Thrall.md) active when you enter Pelagius' Mind, the thrall may be missing when you return. If this happens, check your active effects, and if the thrall is still assigned to you, fast travel may return it to your location. <sup>**?**</sup>
- One can gain access to his menu by pickpocketing Confidence, read a Black Book, exiting the pickpocket menu, then access the quick menu (Down button in PS4), selecting an item and then exiting. You will then be able to access your inventory permanently.
