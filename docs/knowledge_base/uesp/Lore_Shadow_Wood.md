[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/ON-place-Driladan_Pass_%28Shadow_Wood%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Driladan_Pass_%28Shadow_Wood%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Driladan_Pass_(Shadow_Wood).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Driladan_Pass_(Shadow_Wood).jpg) The Shadow Wood The Shadow Wood was a parallel version of the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), a realm of darkness and evil. It was a mirror image of the Valenwood, but lay in perpetual darkness, inhabited by [shades](Lore_Shadow.md) and corrupted [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Indaenir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Rt P-2)</sup>

Following the death of Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), his corpse was stolen by the remnants of the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md) led by Vicereeve [Pelidil](Lore_Pelidil.md). Using the power of the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), Pelidil returned Naemon from the dead as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) to lead armies of undead and unleash corruption in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Sat H-3)</sup> The Shade of Naemon set about creating a new realm, the Shadow Wood, by corrupting the forests of Greenshade. He planned to transform all of Valenwood in this manner, supplanting it with the Shadow Wood.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skull-4)</sup> Naemon envisioned that the Shadow Wood would ultimately consume all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Naemon allied himself with the bound spirits of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who were executed for violating the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md); many agreed to side with him, believing that they would be restored to life to inhabit the Shadow Wood.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Skull-4)</sup> However, many spirits became unwillingly trapped within the Shadow Wood.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

The future [Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) [Indaenir](Lore_Indaenir.md) began investigating Naemon's corrupting influence following the latter's attack on the city of [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md). Sensing that [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md) was calling out for vengeance, Indaenir investigated several sites in the [Green's Marrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green%27s_Marrow) region of Greenshade. Using his connection to the Green, Indaenir cleansed these sites with the aid of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md). He entered sections of the Shadow Wood on several occasions, revealing Naemon's plan for the Valenwood and destroying these pockets in the process. Indaenir ultimately determined that the only way to destroy the Shadow Wood was to confront the Shade of Naemon and destroy him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Indaenir-1)</sup>

Together with his [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) allies, Indaenir confronted Naemon at the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins of [Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md). There, it was revealed that Naemon had used the Staff of Magnus to corrupt the [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md), a tree found deep within the ruins and through which the metaphorical lifeblood of Valenwood flows.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Sat H-3)</sup> With the help of the Vestige, Indaenir defeated the lich, but the Heart had already been corrupted and was spreading its darkness throughout Valenwood. In a desperate attempt to reverse the damage, Indaenir invoked the power of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) to cleanse the Heart and save the Valenwood, losing his life in the process. However, the Green reached out to him in death and restored him to life as the new Silvenar, chosen by Y'ffre.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Sat H-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Indaenir-1)</sup>
