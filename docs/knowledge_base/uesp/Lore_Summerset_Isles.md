[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/LO-map-Summerset_Isle_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Summerset_Isle_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Summerset_Isle_(Anthology).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Summerset_Isle_(Anthology).jpg.md) **Summerset Isle** The Summerset Isles (sometimes spelled **Sumurset**, **Summurset** or **Sumerset**),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> officially named **Alinor** since [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), is a province in southwest [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) consisting of fourteen islands of varying size.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset Isles-1)</sup> The three largest and most significant islands are [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), and [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), the latter of which is apt to disappear for sizable lengths of time without warning. It was settled and ruled under the [Kingdom of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) in ancient times, but in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) seized power and renamed the province after its capital, [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md), though many outside the province still refer to it as Summerset. The current inhabitants of the province are the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), though [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) were there when the elves first arrived.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONToothmaul-2)</sup> It is believed that the first inhabitants were the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) before they were driven away by the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), whom the Altmer claim to be directly descended from.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3S-3)</sup>

## Geography
[![The landscape near the Crystal Tower](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/ON-trailer-summerset-Summerset_Isle.png/300px-ON-trailer-summerset-Summerset_Isle.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-summerset-Summerset_Isle.png) The Summerset archipelago lies within the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md), its shores washed by the [Sea of Pearls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_of_Pearls) to the west, the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) to the north, the [Blue Divide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Divide) to the east, and the open waters of the Eltheric Ocean to the south.

Cloudrest, atop Eton Nir, the highest mountain in Summerset, is an odd mixture of architectural styles, with buildings like strangler vines, built on top of older structures. The oldest, and most isolated, of all ruins there are made of coral, which must have been carried many, many, miles away from the sea.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3S-3)</sup> This may suggest original occupation by the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md), who lay claim to the archipelago and have long coveted it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOValsirenn-4)</sup>

### Islands
[Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) The largest island of the Summerset archipelago. [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) The second-largest island, located closest to mainland Tamriel. [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) The third-largest island, historically located off the southwest coast of Summerset Isle. It is the sanctuary of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), and has a tendency to vanish for centuries at a time. [Belport Run](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Belport_Run) A small island and settlement northeast of Summerset Isle, in the [Auridon Strait](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auridon_Strait). [Buraniim Isle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Buraniim_Isle) A small island south of the port of Skywatch and east of the town of Mathiisen, the site of a hidden [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) ruin. [Calluis Lar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calluis_Lar) An island off the coast of Auridon, near the city-state of Firsthold in the Abecean Sea. [College of Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Sapiarchs) An unnamed island within sight of Lillandril is dominated by the College of Sapiarchs, the academic heart of the Summerset Isles and school to the royalty of Alinor. [Coral Aerie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coral_Aerie) An uninhabited islet just off the northern coast of Summerset. [Dranil Kir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dranil_Kir) An island off the coast of Auridon formerly occupied by the Psijic Order that houses the [Obscuros](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Obscuros), a powerful scrying device. [Errinorne Isle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Errinorne_Isle) A small island to the east of Skywatch. It was used by the Aldmer as a mercantile hub, and later served as a Sload beachhead for invading Auridon before the [All-Flags Navy](Lore_All-Flags_Navy.md) purged them. [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md) An island north of Summerset, purchased by Arch-Mage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) in the early First Era and subsequently lost in [The Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) until the mid-Second Era. [Isle of Contemplation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Isle_of_Contemplation) A small recursive island within Auridon known for being beautifully serene. [Silatar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silatar) A small, secluded island. [Wasten Coridale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wasten_Coridale) An island off the coast of Alinor noted for its hanging gardens
## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/LO-race-Altmer.png/200px-LO-race-Altmer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Altmer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Altmer.png) Illustration of a typical Altmer male. Altmeri tradition holds that their ancestors, the revered [Ancients](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancients), first made landfall at the Summerset Isles on the northernmost shore of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), at a site called Nine-Prow Landing in the middle of the Early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AECXII-5)</sup> The legendary High Lord [Torinaan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torinaan) was the very first Aldmer to disembark from the foremost of their nine ships. Torinaan claimed the island for his people, gave Auridon its name, and swore a blood-oath that he would never leave it. As the Aldmer began to explore the island, they encountered a number of fearsome native creatures known as the [Gheatus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gheatus), [Ilyadi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ilyadi), and [Welwa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welwa). While some suggested they flee for safer lands, Torinaan refused and battled with the monsters, slaying them with powers drawn from the teachings of his ancestors. Free of danger, the Aldmer resumed taming the land, using magic to sculpt and tend it to their liking.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLOF-6)</sup> [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), the "First among Holds",<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OTA-7)</sup> was the first city they built, not far from the original landing site.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLOF-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FHA-8)</sup>

Afterwards, the Aldmer spread throughout Auridon in earnest, eventually inhabiting the rest of the Summerset Isles and colonizing mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AECXII-5)</sup> They built numerous cities and wonders such as the vaunted [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md). Over time, the Aldmer of Summerset became the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), and the society of the Isles became divided into different social classes. At the top were the Wise, who served as teachers and priests, followed by the Artists, Princes, Warriors, Landowners, Merchants, and Workers. At the bottom were the tamed beasts, such as the [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) who were native to Summerset, which were enslaved.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3S-3)</sup> Once the city of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) was established, it became the capital of the Isles and seat of the Altmeri monarchy.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-9)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/ON-place-Firsthold.jpg/200px-ON-place-Firsthold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Firsthold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Firsthold.jpg) The city of Firsthold Despite the idyllic way of life the Altmer established for themselves, they were never free of conflict. The Sload frequently used their necromantic powers to attack and invade the Isles, such as in the Sack of Skywatch in [1E 1301](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1301) and the War of the Uvichil between [1E 2911](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2911) and [1E 2917](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2917).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3S-3)</sup> The Altmer's envious cousins, the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), also constantly endeavored to wrest the Isles away, believing themselves to be the true heirs of Aldmeris and therefore the rightful owners of Summerset.

Although the precise circumstances are unclear, the Summerset Isles became incorporated into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), which at its zenith controlled all of Tamriel with the exception of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), in the late First Era. This concession was apparently arranged to the Altmer's advantage: all outsiders aside from traders and Imperial ambassadors were prohibited from traveling to the Isles, and these privileged few could only conduct their business within the capital of Alinor. [Eric of Guis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eric_of_Guis), the personal emissary of Emperor [Reman II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II) to the Altmer, noted in his journals that while the Altmer reliably paid their tithes to the Empire, they did so purely to minimize contact with non-Altmer rather than out of a desire to avoid war with the Empire.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-9)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the Summerset Isles were a part of both the first and second [Aldmeri Dominions](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). The First Aldmeri Dominion was founded by the storied Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) of Alinor, who joined the Isles with Valenwood and Elsweyr in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580) to oppose the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) in the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md). However, many Altmer opposed the Dominion—which they saw as a threat to the purity of their isolated Isles—and groups like the [Veiled Heritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Veiled_Heritance) were formed to undermine the alliance.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-10)</sup> As Queen Ayrenn was often away from Summerset, dealing with issues in other parts of the Dominion, she appointed her cousin [Alwinarwe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Proxy_Queen_Alwinarwe) Proxy Queen of Alinor, to handle affairs on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) in her place.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOSummerset-11)</sup> The Second Aldmeri Dominion arose centuries later in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830), when the Summerset Isles conquered Valenwood over a dynastic dispute. Both incarnations of the Dominion were attempts by the Altmer to reassert Elven dominance over Tamriel in the vacuum created by the fall of the Second Empire, and both ultimately failed.

The Summerset Isles was the last nation in Tamriel to be brought into the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) of the late Second Era. In [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) wielded the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) against Alinor, forcing the city's surrender within minutes. Summerset Isle was then absorbed as a province of the Empire. During its time as part of the Empire, Summerset was considered the "weakest link in the Imperial chain".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DFC-12)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), Summerset Isle was beset by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). The [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) was toppled by the daedra hordes. The [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), once a minority voice, were given greater power and authority in order to deal with the crisis. They were successful and came to be seen as the saviors of Summerset Isle.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RT2-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TGW-14)</sup> In 4E 22, the Thalmor overthrew the ancient Altmeri monarchy,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RT4-15)</sup> taking total control of the Summerset Isles and renaming them Alinor, after the capital city.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TGW-14)</sup> Valenwood was brought under the Thalmor's control in [4E 29](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_29) following a coup with Thalmor-aligned Bosmer, establishing the Third [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). Later, the former Confederacy of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) joined the Dominion as two separate client states.

The apprentice of [Balagog gro-Nolob](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balagog_gro-Nolob), [Urzhag gro-Larak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Urzhag_gro-Larak), visited the Summerset Isles several times around the end of the Second Century of the Fourth Era during his travels across Tamriel which saw him learn from a variety of races to improve his cooking skills. During one of these visits, he got permission with two other outsiders to research with some limitations in the College of Sapiarchs. Though the Isles were opened up, many of the native inhabitants were still hesitant to outsiders.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-16)</sup>

It could become a dangerous situation."*
- A set of islands are shown off the southwest coast of Summerset in many maps. According to datamined ESO files, they were called "The Pearls", but did not end up appearing in the accessible parts of the zone in the game. An unnamed island off the northeast coast of Summerset in ESO also serves as the location of the town of [Belport Run](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Belport_Run) in Arena.
