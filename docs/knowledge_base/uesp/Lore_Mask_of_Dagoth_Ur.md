# Mask of Dagoth Ur

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/SR-item-Mask_of_Dagoth_Ur.jpg/200px-SR-item-Mask_of_Dagoth_Ur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mask_of_Dagoth_Ur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mask_of_Dagoth_Ur.jpg) Mask of Dagoth Ur (Skyrim) *It was Dagoth Ur who opened the gate, and beckoned me forward. And when I wear his face, I speak his truth, and from his words I spawn the cure that will spread across the world.*
—Erden Relvel<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ERN-1)</sup> The Mask of Dagoth Ur is a full-face mask of [gold](Lore_Gold.md) and [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HZ-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MODU-3)</sup> adorned with a [third eye](Lore_Third_Eye.md) in its forehead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MODU-3)</sup> The mask was associated with and worn by [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) after his resurrection by the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DU-4)</sup> It was also informally known as **his face**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ERN-1)</sup> and could project Dagoth Ur's influence.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ERN-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PAN-5)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Little is known about the exact origins of the Mask of Dagoth Ur, or when it was first donned.

## History

### Dagoth Ur's Resurrection and Defeat
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/LG-quest-The_Heart%2C_Revealed_02.jpg/200px-LG-quest-The_Heart%2C_Revealed_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Heart,_Revealed_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Heart,_Revealed_02.jpg) Dagoth Ur renewed by the Heart of Lorkhan's power, wearing his mask *Perhaps it means nothing. It is peculiar, the tall figure in the golden mask. It puts me in mind of the golden helm of the Ordinator, but that does not seem to fit the... spirit of the dream. My people take careful account of dreams, but only a wise woman can tease from them their meanings.*
—Hassour Zainsubani<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HZ-2)</sup> The first confirmed sighting of the mask occurred in [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DUP-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRM-7)</sup> That year, Dagoth Ur awoke refreshed from his dream, having formed a unique connection with the Heart of Lorkhan,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DUP-6)</sup> and become the False Dreamer.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-36L-8)</sup> Despite his death,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nibani-9)</sup> the Sharmat dreamt that he lived, and thus he and his seven kin were revived. Later that same year,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DUP-6)</sup> the Tribunal set out on their annual pilgrimage to the Heart of Lorkhan to renew their powers, but this time they were ambushed by Dagoth Ur and the [Ash Vampires](Lore_Ash_Vampire.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DUP-6)</sup> They escaped only through a cunning ruse. This encounter was also the earliest known instance of Dagoth Ur wearing his mask.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRM-7)</sup>

Dagoth Ur wore the mask both in flesh,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DU-4)</sup> and in the dreams of the people of Morrowind he invaded.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HZ-2)</sup> Dagoth Ur wore the mask in his final moments when the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) confronted him in his citadel and the Heart Chamber in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DU-4)</sup>

### The Mask's Influence
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/SR-npc-Erden_Relvel.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Erden_Relvel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Erden_Relvel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Erden_Relvel.jpg) Erden Relvel wearing the Mask of Dagoth Ur *The artifact speaks to us. I feel its heartbeat rattling in my head like a loose rock. Rattle, rattle, rattle. A curious song. Not a heart of blood, but stone.*
—Priest Arthamis<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PAN-5)</sup> Following the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), worship of the Tribunal largely collapsed as the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md) restored devotion to the [Reclamations](Lore_Reclamations.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> [Tribunal cults](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) nonetheless persisted into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) and were persecuted as heretical.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> One such Almalexia cult built a hidden shrine on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) called Ashfall's Tear, near [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md), where they safeguarded Tribunal relics, including the Mask of Dagoth Ur. The mask had earlier been entrusted to Matriarch Drevlan, who was warned to never open the chest it was stored in by her predecessor. She sealed it within the temple armory behind a portcullis requiring the masks of all three Tribunes to open.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MDN-12)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PAN-5)</sup> Some of Dagoth Ur's influence lingered within the mask, and several temple priests were corrupted by the Sharmat's voice.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PAN-5)</sup>

Around [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Diviner Erden Relvel reached the artifact when Dagoth Ur raised the portcullis, allowing him to be able to take the mask. Seduced by his master's words, Erden became a priest of Dagoth Ur and learned how to create [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md), which he used to create [Ash Zombies](Lore_Ash_Zombie.md) from his fellow priests and assault the temple at Ashfall's Tear, seeking to spread the Sharmat's "cure" to the world.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ERN-1)</sup> Knowing the cult would soon realize the mask was gone, Erden left a note challenging whoever bore the three Tribunal masks to face him, hoping to reenact Dagoth Ur's confrontation with the Nerevarine.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ERN-1)</sup> Wearing the Mask of Dagoth Ur and the [Robe of the Lich](Lore_Robe_of_the_Lich.md), he awaited this champion near [Tel Mithryn](Lore_Tel_Mithryn.md), commanding Ash Zombies. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) ultimately answered the challenge, defeated Erden, and claimed the artifacts.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>
