Thonar Silver-Blood was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) member of the Silver-Blood family who was a powerful figure in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) during the late second and early third centuries of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He was the younger brother of Thongvor, the head of the Silver-Blood family.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:504</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MJ-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rhiada-3)</sup> Thonar's wife was Betrid, who was a former devotee of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) and shamelessly admitted that she only married him for the money.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rhiada-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Betrid-5)</sup>

## History
In [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174), the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), led by [Madanach](Lore_Madanach.md), took advantage of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md)'s preoccupation with the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) to overthrow [Hrolfdir](Lore_Hrolfdir.md), Jarl of Markarth, and declare the Reach to be an independent kingdom.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:501</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBo M-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Sr JId-7)</sup> King Madanach ruled the Reach as an independent kingdom for two years until [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) and his Nord militia, who had been hired by Hrolfdir, drove the Reachman rebels from Markarth and retook the city in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:501</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBo M-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Sr JId-7)</sup> Madanach was captured, quickly tried, and sentenced to death, but Thonar intervened and offered him a stay of execution.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Madanach-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TJ-9)</sup> Thonar offered Madanach a deal: he would be spared and allowed to lead his remaining followers, who had fled into the hills and become the Forsworn, in their [rebellion](Lore_Forsworn_Rebellion.md) while he was imprisoned in Cidhna Mine, as long as the Forsworn dealt with the Silver-Bloods' enemies.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Madanach-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TJ-9)</sup> Madanach accepted the deal, and although he found it humiliating at first, he knew Thonar would come to believe that he was under control and lower his defenses eventually.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Madanach-8)</sup>

Although Thongvor was the head of the family, he primarily concerned himself with politics and spent most of his time at Jarl [Igmund](Lore_Igmund.md)'s court in [Understone Keep](Lore_Understone_Keep.md), leaving Thonar to handle all the Silver-Bloods' business.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:504</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rhiada-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup> Thonar made all the deals, intimidated local landowners into selling to him, and managed the Silver-Bloods' businesses, including the Treasury House, Silver-Blood Inn, and Cidhna Mine, Markarth's prison, which produced a significant portion of the silver in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rhiada-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MJ-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Betrid-5)</sup> The Silver-bloods' mercenaries guarded Cidhna Mine, and they used the prisoners as forced labor to mine silver ore.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCo S-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Urzoga-11)</sup> Thonar and the Silver-Bloods virtually owned all of Markarth and half the Reach.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rhiada-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Betrid-5)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mulush-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ainethach-13)</sup> Thonar also wielded significant influence over Markarth by bribing the city guards to answer to him.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MCG-14)</sup> The Reachman Nepos the Nose, who was secretly a high-ranking Forsworn agent, administered Cidhna Mine and the smelter, handling all the paperwork for Thonar.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:502</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mulush-12)</sup>

Despite his immense power in Markarth, Betrid questioned Thonar's seeming lack of desire to be the formal head of the Silver-Blood family.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Betrid-5)</sup> Betrid felt Thonar was not ambitious enough, leading her to frequently acquire potions from Markarth's alchemy shop to fuel his ambitions.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Betrid-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Bothela-15)</sup> Thonar responded to his wife's criticism by stating that someone had to keep the silver production running, and he would take control of the family on his own time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), General [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md), the Military Governor of Skyrim, sent one of his agents named Margret to Markarth to investigate the Silver-Blood family.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Margret-16)</sup> Thonar eventually learned that Margret was an imperial agent, prompting him to order Madanach to have the Forsworn deal with her to prevent her from interfering with the Silver-Bloods' business.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thonar-4)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Margret-16)</sup> Madanach passed the orders to Nepos the Nose, who then sent a letter to a smelter worker named Weylin ordering him to kill Margret.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-1)</sup><sup>:348</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Nepos-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-WN-18)</sup>

It is unknown if Weylin succeeded in killing Margret, but his attack on her drew the attention of a local named Eltrys and a [visitor](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to Markarth, whom Eltrys subsequently recruited to investigate why the guards did nothing. After the visitor did some investigating, Thonar had one of the city guards threaten them to cease their investigation, but this failed to end it. The visitor eventually learned of Thonar's agreement with Madanach, so he had the city guard kill Eltrys, frame the visitor for the murder, and imprison them in Cidhna Mine.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TFC-19)</sup> The visitor managed to escape Cidhna Mine not long after their imprisonment, but Thonar's fate following this is unknown.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-NOECM-20)</sup>
