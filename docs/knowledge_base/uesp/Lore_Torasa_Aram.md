Torasa Aram was a wealthy [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) noblewoman who lived during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). She used her wealth to establish the Museum of Artifacts, a museum in the Godsreach district of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). By offering large sums of money to adventurers, she was able to collect a significant number of [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md), items of great and often divine power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup>

Her museum's collection was expanded greatly in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) due to the patronage of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), who sold many valuable artifacts to Torasa. In return for several donations made by the Nerevarine, Torasa parted with an ancient [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) Battle Shield from the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md). This shield contained a piece of [Trueflame](Lore_Trueflame.md), the fractured blade of [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) which was later reforged by the Nerevarine.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBON-3)</sup>

As is the nature of great artifacts, many of the items from the museum's collection were not content to stay with one owner for long.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TA-4)</sup> Even by the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), several artifacts had been reclaimed by their respective [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), or had vanished to reappear elsewhere on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
