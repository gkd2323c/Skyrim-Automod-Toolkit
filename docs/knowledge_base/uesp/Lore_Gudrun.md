## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/ON-place-Temple_of_the_Ancestor_Moths.jpg/200px-ON-place-Temple_of_the_Ancestor_Moths.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Temple_of_the_Ancestor_Moths.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Temple_of_the_Ancestor_Moths.jpg) Temple of the Ancestor Moths ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/ON-npc-Bellona_Novatian.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Bellona_Novatian.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Bellona_Novatian.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Bellona_Novatian.jpg) A Moth Priestess and a practitioner of the Way of the Peaceful Fist The exact dates of Gudrun's life is unclear. Sources from the final days of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) mention that she lived many generations ago.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POTAM-1)</sup> By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Way of the Peaceful Fist was already in practice among the Moth Priests, indicating that she was active before the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bellona-2)</sup>

Before 2E 582,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bellona-2)</sup> Gudrun arrived at the Temple of the Ancestor Moths in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). Newly blinded by prophetic visions of what was to come, she brought with her a new form of teaching. The ancestors' visions revealed the necessity for the monks to learn self-defense. Though little else was known about her, the practice of the Way of the Peaceful Fist endured for generations. The monks dedicated themselves to training in Gudrun's teachings, striving tirelessly to master the sword of no sword and the axe of no axe. They began their journey as novitiates by following her teachings.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POTAM-1)</sup>

The arts Gudrun taught the monks proved valuable during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). While the Way of the Peaceful Fist was not enough to overcome the hordes that stole the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), it was effective in defending the priests against wayfarers who sought to desecrate their sanctuary.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bellona-2)</sup>

## Notes
- In the original drafts for ESO's main quest, there was Ancestor Moth sanctuary called "Gudrun's Retreat". It filled a similar role to [the Harborage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:The_Harborage_(place)), and some moth priests would carry recall stones that would teleport them to the sanctuary in times of danger.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOMain Quest-3)</sup>
