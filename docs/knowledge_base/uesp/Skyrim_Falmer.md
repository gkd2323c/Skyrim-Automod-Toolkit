# Falmer


The *[Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)* [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md) adds further depth to the history of the Falmer. [Knight-Paladin Gelebor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Knight-Paladin_Gelebor), one of two known surviving Snow Elves, recounts how the Snow Elves sought protection from their Nord enemies long ago. The Dwemer agreed to shelter them, but at a high cost: the Snow Elves were forced to eat a toxic fungus, which rendered them completely blind. This was an extremely unsavory choice in the eyes of many of the Snow Elves, but those who sought alternatives to the deal were either killed (by the Dwemer or others), relented, or simply vanished. In time, the now-blinded Falmer became slaves to their Dwemer protectors. In light of their sad fate, Gelebor refers to the monstrous Falmer in contemporary Skyrim as "the Betrayed". He also claims that the toxin the Dwemer used to blind them is not responsible for the sorry state of contemporary Falmer, and hints that the rest of the transformation can be explained by thousands of years of evolution.

## Falmer Types
Falmer utilize a variety of fighting styles, including melee, ranged, and magic attacks. The specific variety you will meet is level-dependent, and you will continue to meet low-level versions even at high levels. Boss-level Falmer, however, will always be at the highest possible level. The *Dawnguard* add-on adds the Falmer Warmonger, which is one of the few creatures to have a grand soul.

All Falmer are blind (80 pts) and have a [Fortify Sneak](Skyrim_Fortify_Sneak.md) ability (30 pts). Most Falmer you will encounter are male; in fact, the Falmer listed in the tables below are all male in the base game. If *Dawnguard* is installed, however, the Falmer shaman will all be female and wear a special female Falmer outfit (their gender is still marked as male in the game data, however; only the Falmer Warmonger shaman is actually marked as being female).

Any Falmer that uses martial attacks (all types except shaman) also has a leveled "Falmer Poison" perk, which allows them to apply a leveled poison to their weapons. This poison is actually a spell that lasts for 3-4 seconds and causes a set amount of damage to health on hit (rather than damage per second over time).

### Melee Fighters
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/SR-creature-Falmer_Melee.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Falmer_Melee.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Melee.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Melee.jpg) A Falmer fighter carrying a Falmer sword These Falmer prefer to fight up close, wielding a fearsome weapon in one hand and a shield in the other. In addition to a [Falmer Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Shield), they carry either a [Falmer Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Sword), a [Honed Falmer Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Honed_Falmer_Sword), a [Falmer War Axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_War_Axe), or a [Honed Falmer War Axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Honed_Falmer_War_Axe). Falmer of level 22 or higher (except Warmongers) will also be equipped with a [Falmer Helmet](Skyrim_Falmer_Helmet.md).

In addition to the gear listed above, melee Falmer loot includes the following items:

- 50% chance to carry leveled amount of gold
- 75% chance to carry [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md), [ore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ore), Dwemer metal objects, [ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md) (common, uncommon, or rare), a shovel or pickaxe, or [damage health](Skyrim_Damage_Health.md) [poisons](Skyrim_Poisons.md)
- May also drop [Falmer ears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Ear) (one at a 50% chance, one at a 25% chance)
- Falmer of level 15 or higher have a 50% chance of having a healing potion (Falmer Shadowmasters and Warmongers are guaranteed to carry one)

If *Dawnguard* is installed, Falmer Skulkers, Gloomlurkers, Nightprowlers, and Shadowmasters may spawn either with the default gear listed above, or with the same heavy armor outfit as the Falmer Warmongers. These "heavy armor" alternatives are identified by the "[xx](Skyrim_Form_ID.md)" at the beginning of the IDs listed in the table below. Falmer Warmongers are equipped with the following armor:

- **Boots**: 25% chance to have either [Falmer Hardened Boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Hardened_Boots) or [Falmer Heavy Boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Heavy_Boots)
- **Cuirass**: 75% chance to have either [Falmer Hardened Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Hardened_Armor) or [Falmer Heavy Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Heavy_Armor)
- **Gauntlets**: 25% chance to have either [Falmer Hardened Gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Hardened_Gauntlets) or [Falmer Heavy Gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Heavy_Gauntlets)
- **Helmet**: either a [Falmer Hardened Helm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Hardened_Helm) or a [Falmer Heavy Helm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Heavy_Helm)

### Archers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/SR-creature-Falmer_Archer.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Falmer_Archer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Archer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Archer.jpg) A Falmer archer stalking toward his enemy Some Falmer prefer to stay at a distance and assault you with missile attacks. These archers wield a [Falmer Bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Bow) and carry 12 [Falmer Arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Falmer_Arrow), and have a chance to carry a small number of other arrows (of any quality from [iron](Skyrim_Iron.md) to [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric.md)). Level 1 archers also carry a Falmer Sword, and any archer of level 22 or higher is equipped with a Falmer Helmet.

In addition to the gear listed above, Falmer archer loot includes the following items:

- 50% chance to carry leveled amount of gold
- 75% chance to carry gems, ore, Dwemer metal objects, ingredients (common, uncommon, or rare), a shovel or pickaxe, or damage health poisons
- May also drop Falmer Ears (one at a 50% chance, one at a 25% chance)

If *Dawnguard* is installed, Falmer Warmonger archers will appear starting at level 48. These heavily-armored Falmer have the following equipment in addition to the gear listed above:

- **Boots**: 25% chance to have either Falmer Hardened Boots or Falmer Heavy Boots
- **Cuirass**: 75% chance to have either Falmer Hardened Armor or Falmer Heavy Armor
- **Gauntlets**: 25% chance to have either Falmer Hardened Gauntlets or Falmer Heavy Gauntlets
- **Helmet**: either a Falmer Hardened Helm or a Falmer Heavy Helm

### Shaman
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/SR-creature-Falmer_Skulker.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Falmer_Skulker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Skulker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Skulker.jpg) A Falmer Shaman wielding her bound sword [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/SR-creature-Falmer_Shadowmaster.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Falmer_Shadowmaster.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Shadowmaster.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Shadowmaster.jpg) A Shadowmaster shaman using a staff of Lightning Bolts Falmer shaman are skilled spellcasters, relying primarily on the use of offensive spells in combat. Although they do not know the [Bound Sword](Skyrim_Bound_Sword.md) spell, all shaman carry a bound sword with the following enchantments: Banish Summoned (an effect that functions like the [Oblivion Binding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Oblivion_Binding) perk), [Soul Trap](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md) (5 seconds), and Turn Raised Undead (essentially a [Turn Undead](Skyrim_Turn_Undead_(effect).md) effect). Falmer shaman do not wear armor.

In addition to the gear listed above, Falmer shaman loot includes the following items:

- 50% chance to carry leveled amount of gold
- 75% chance to carry a selection of ore, ingredients (common, uncommon, or rare), damage health poisons, or [soul gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md)
- May also drop Falmer Ears (one at a 50% chance, one at a 25% chance)

If *Dawnguard* is installed, Falmer Warmonger shaman will appear starting at level 48. They do not wear heavy armor like their martial counterparts, but they do have a chance to carry either a Bound Sword or a staff of [Firebolts](Skyrim_Firebolt.md), [Ice Spikes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ice_Spike), or [Lightning Bolts](Skyrim_Lightning_Bolt.md).

### Spellswords
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/SR-creature-Falmer_Spellsword.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Falmer_Spellsword.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Spellsword.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer_Spellsword.jpg) A Falmer Spellsword chasing down his enemy Falmer spellswords mix the martial attacks of their melee cousins and the magical attacks of the Falmer shaman. They wield either a Falmer Sword, a Honed Falmer Sword, a Falmer War Axe, or a Honed Falmer War Axe, and they do not carry a shield, preferring instead to keep a hand free for spells. Falmer of level 22 or higher (except Warmongers) will also be equipped with a Falmer Helmet.

In addition to the gear listed above, Falmer spellsword loot includes the following items:

- 50% chance to carry leveled amount of gold
- 75% chance to carry gems, ore, Dwemer metal objects, ingredients (common, uncommon, or rare), a shovel or pickaxe, or damage health poisons
- May also drop Falmer Ears (one at a 50% chance, one at a 25% chance)

If *Dawnguard* is installed, Falmer Warmonger spellswords will appear starting at level 48. These heavily-armored Falmer have the following equipment in addition to the gear listed above:

- **Boots**: 25% chance to have either Falmer Hardened Boots or Falmer Heavy Boots
- **Cuirass**: 75% chance to have either Falmer Hardened Armor or Falmer Heavy Armor
- **Gauntlets**: 25% chance to have either Falmer Hardened Gauntlets or Falmer Heavy Gauntlets
- **Helmet**: either a Falmer Hardened Helm or a Falmer Heavy Helm

Frozen Falmer come in two varieties: Frozen Vampire Falmer and Frozen Falmer Shaman.

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/SR-place-Falmer_Tent_Scorpion.jpg/200px-SR-place-Falmer_Tent_Scorpion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falmer_Tent_Scorpion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falmer_Tent_Scorpion.jpg) Falmer tent with scorpion - In some ruins, such as [Nchuand-Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nchuand-Zel), you might see small, two-foot-tall versions of Falmer.
- In *Dawnguard* locations (such as the caves leading to the [Forgotten Vale](Skyrim_Forgotten_Vale.md)), some of the Falmer tents are crafted with an [unknown scorpion creature](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unused_Creatures#Scorpion).
- Frozen Vampire Falmer do not actually use vampire abilities (invisibility, the Drain Life spell, etc.) but have a unique look: they have Vampire Lord lookalike wings on their backs, though they cannot use them. All Frozen Falmer (Vampire ones and others) shatter upon death, leaving behind no corpse to raise (such as with [Dread Zombie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dread_Zombie)), though they can be harvested for a soul with the appropriate [spell](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(spell).md) or enchanted weapon.
- All Feral Falmer (and their "Frozen" counterparts) do not spawn with weapons, but if there is one nearby, they will pick it up and use it. If a Frozen Falmer/Frozen Vampire Falmer picks up a weapon, it will be lost when they are killed, as there is no corpse left behind to loot. If you drop weapons in the Chantry to avoid being overencumbered (a very real possibility in the vast Forgotten Vale), make sure they aren't anything you ever want to use again.
- The weaponry for Melee and Spellsword Falmer is [leveled](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists). Only Falmer of level 18 or higher can spawn with "Honed Falmer" weapons. Falmer of level 21 are just as likely to spawn with a "Honed Falmer" weapon as a regular one, and are twice as likely to spawn with one at level 28 or higher.

- If you unlock a Falmer-style chest, the prompt to unlock it may still appear, even though you already unlocked it. - Press your activate key again, and you should now be able to search the chest properly.

The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch), version 2.0.2, fixes this bug.
- The Falmer shield doesn't have the DLC1Armor Materiel Falmer Heavy Original and Armor Heavy keywords set like the other pieces in the set.
