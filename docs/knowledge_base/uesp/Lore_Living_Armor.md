Living Armors are [animated armor](Lore_Golem.md#Armor) [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) of [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) origins,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DL-1)</sup> associated with cults of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ALDA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WE-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AKLA-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TH-5)</sup> Living Armors exist in various forms, ranging from humanoid-shaped suits of armor,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WE-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TH-5)</sup> to large, [feline](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) -like mounts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DL-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ALDA-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AKLA-4)</sup>

They were forged by master Nord [smiths](Lore_Smithing.md), designed to offer both swiftness and warmth. Their overlapping plates moved with tireless grace, animated by the fire burning within.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DL-1)</sup> The religious text *Dedication Ceremony* describes armor consecrated to Akatosh as allowing one's inner flame to shine brightly through the forge's creation, spreading light and guiding others toward wisdom.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AKLA-4)</sup> In contrast, armor dedicated to Alduin is said to channel the fury in the World-Eater's belly and the rage in his visage, allowing the creature's wrath to be felt by all who stand against them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ALDA-2)</sup>

Magic that allowed mortals to assume forms resembling the humanoid-shaped Living Armors existed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WE-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TH-5)</sup> The Akatosh armor, Time's Herald Living Armor, was described as a golden-scaled suit, wreathed in righteous flames and untouched by the passage of time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TH-5)</sup> In contrast, Alduin's form, World-Eater's Living Armor, evoked the razor-sharp hunger of the World-Eater himself, its shadowy scales inspired dread, and its presence stirred a scorching, apocalyptic rage.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WE-3)</sup>
