This article is about the General of the Imperial Guard. For other characters with the same name, see [Talin](Lore_Talin.md).

Talin Warhaft (b. [3E 355](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_355))<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> served as General of the [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) during the reign of Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-English Manual Arena-2)</sup>

Talin had a wife, along with a daughter named Laretha Warhaft, who would go on to become a Commander within the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) in adulthood,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena Character Creator-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPWarofthe Wormgod-4)</sup> and another child, who would become known as the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena Character Creator-3)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life
When Talin Warhaft was just beginning his career as a spellsword and had just celebrated his 16th spring, he met another young spellsword, a human who called himself Marten the Blade. They became friends very quickly. Despite their youth, the two dreamed of fame and fortune.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup> During an ambush by bandits, Warhaft became wounded, Marten utilized a spell that changed his outward appearance into that of the bandit leader, then, he took advantage of the immediate confusion the bandits were facing to lure them away from Talin's wounded body.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup> Marten and Talin traveled together for many years, but some time in Marten had a son. Not wanting his boy to grow up without seeing his father, Marten abandoned his travels and took the mother as a wife.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup>

In one memorable tale from an unknown period of Talin's youth, Warhaft visited a primitive island where a young child was sacrificed once a year to appease [Arius](Lore_Gods_A.md#Arius), the God of Fire. Whenever the natives neglected the sacrifice, the island volcano would erupt, killing hundreds of villagers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena Character Creator-3)</sup>

### Imperial Guard
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/BS-misc-Battlespire_Cinematic_Render.jpg/200px-BS-misc-Battlespire_Cinematic_Render.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-misc-Battlespire_Cinematic_Render.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-misc-Battlespire_Cinematic_Render.jpg) The Battlespire Later in life, Talin Warhaft won a position within the Imperial Guard, an elite unit of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Morrowind Imperial Guard Topic-6)</sup> who are trained in the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Battlespire MBNET-9)</sup> Only those who could prove themselves by successfully mastering the challenges of the Battlespire were chosen for the Emperor's Imperial Guard.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Battlespire MBNET-9)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-8)</sup> Eventually, Talin Warhaft would be appointed leader of the entire Imperial Guard under the title of General.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-English Manual Arena-2)</sup> Warhaft formed a close-knit team, supposedly capable of annihilating any threat to the Emperor.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup>

Warhaft once fought at the Battle of [Borim Valley](Lore_Borim_Valley.md), where his men had routed the enemy. They would have captured all their opponents if not for the brave sacrifice of a young sergeant on the opposing side, who held a pass long enough for his men to retreat to safety.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena Character Creator-3)</sup>

In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), General Warhaft attended [Mid Year's festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mid_Year_Celebration)<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> in the Emperor's banquet hall (two and half months before the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) would begin). The ghost of Elder [Ria Silmane](Lore_Ria_Silmane.md) would retroactively declare that this event was where evil took form in her master:<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-English Manual Arena-2)</sup> [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md#Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel), [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md); which holds at least some truth, it was here where General Warhaft would regale an anecdote to the Crown Princess [Ariella Septim](Lore_Ariella_Septim.md), Talin recounted the tale from his youth of when his friend, Marten the Blade, used a transmorph spell to change his own appearance into that of another, so that Marten may trick a group of bandits into thinking he was their leader. It was this anecdote that inspired a disgruntled Jagar Tharn to steal the appearance of his Emperor, and nab Septim's place on the [Dragon Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md).<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup>

### Imperial Simulacrum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/0/00/AR-scene-Talin_Warhaft_Sprite.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Talin_Warhaft_Sprite.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-scene-Talin_Warhaft_Sprite.jpg) Talin Warhaft Then came the 1st of [Hearthfire](Lore_Hearthfire.md) [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389). It was in the Imperial Training Hall, the place where Talin and the Guards worked on their combat tactics, that an ornate seal appeared on the floor, cast by Tharn. Tharn then summoned the Emperor and his Guard, declaring he had found something strange and heard rumors of treachery. When the Emperor arrived, Tharn cast the spell. The seal flashed once, then surrounded the Emperor and his Guard with a wall of flames. They tried in vain to break the magic barrier. Imprisoned by the seal, they could only watch helplessly as Tharn transformed into the Emperor's appearance. Tharn then summoned demonic beings, who also transformed themselves to take the form of Talin and the other guards. An astonished Emperor and his Guard stared out, as their look-alikes stood there before them like a reflection in a mirror. Tharn explained to the Emperor that time passed more slowly in the dimension where he would dispatch them. By the time they would eventually die several generations would have grown up on Tamriel, and for them the glorious reign of Uriel Septim would be centuries old. Even if the magic of the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) alerted the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), they could not know for whom the bell tolled. Nevertheless, Tharn assured the Emperor that he would take care of everything. In a few moments, the Emperor and his Imperial Guards would be sucked out of this world and propelled into another dimension, chosen by the Imperial Battlemage.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-5)</sup>

Warhaft's experience within the dimensional prison after his banishment to Oblivion alongside Uriel VII is entirely unknown. But information of this may be extrapolated from Uriel's public account.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion Uriel Book-10)</sup>

*Little is known of Uriel's experience while trapped in Oblivion. He says he remembers nothing but an endless sequence of waking and sleeping nightmares. He says he believed himself to be dreaming, and had no notion of passage of time. Publically [sic], he long claimed to have no memory of the dreams and nightmares of his imprisonment, but from time to time, during the interviews with the Emperor that form the basis of this biography, he would relate details of nightmares he had, and would describe them as similar to the nightmares he had when he was imprisoned in Oblivion. He seemed not so much unwilling as incapable of describing the experience.*<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion Uriel Book-10)</sup> The Emperor and Talin were eventually rescued in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399) by [Talin's child](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md). Talin told them that they had earned his deepest respect, and stepped aside to allow the Emperor to name them [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) of the Empire.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Arena Imperial Palace Quest-11)</sup>

### Later Life
Sometime after the Imperial Simulacrum, Lady [Allena Benoch](Lore_Allena_Benoch.md) was appointed head of the Emperor's Imperial Guard.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Morrowind Benoch Book-12)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), it was said that some generals in the Legions had one eye on Emperor Uriel Septim, who was supposed to be dying of age and illness, and one eye on the throne.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Morrowind Sjoring-13)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Manuals of [Arms](Lore_Manual_of_Arms.md) and [Armor](Lore_Manual_of_Armor.md), which General Warhaft commissioned the writing and publication of to serve as standard guides to all officers of the Imperial Legion regarding their standard equipment, had circulated throughout Cyrodiil.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-14)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

However, he still appears and gets mentioned in the game's opening and character creator scenarios, so his disappearance from the plot during the endgame confuses the narrative.
