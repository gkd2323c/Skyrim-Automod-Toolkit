# Spiritblood Clan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg) Vateshran Aydolan [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png/200px-ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png) Vateshtan Hollows *Our clan uses the Rites to determine which of our children shall ascend into adulthood. If they survive the challenge, they come out as full Reach warriors. They carry all the privileges and responsibilities that the clan offers.*
—Angair<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Angair-1)</sup> Spiritblood Clan was one of numerous [clans](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md) of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) that lived in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) since the ancient times, during the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They were known for their fierce warriors and rites of passage set in [Vateshran Hollows](Lore_Vateshran_Hollows.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VH-3)</sup>

## History
*I watched as Carinar the Sharp slew his father, sundering the Quicktalon clan in twain. When Carinar chose to move his people south, I joined him. As we marched, I sang of his triumph and strength. In return, he named me vateshran.*
—Vateshran Aydolan<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/ON-npc-Spiritblood_Champion.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Spiritblood_Champion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Spiritblood_Champion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Spiritblood_Champion.jpg) Spiritblood Champion [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/ON-npc-Rites_Matron.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Rites_Matron.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Rites_Matron.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Rites_Matron.jpg) Rites Matron The Spiritblood Clan was founded by [Carinar the Sharp](Lore_Carinar.md) long before [1E 2502](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2502),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup> before the times "anyone can remember".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Preview-4)</sup>

He was born in the Reach as a son of the chief of the [Quicktalon Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quicktalon_Clan). He eventually plotted against his father and killed him. His actions led to the sundering of the Quicktalon Clan, which split into two factions - Spiritblood Clan, which supported Carinar, and the other, loyal to the deceased chieftain. Carinar decided to move south. Among the first members of the Spiritblood Clan was the legendary [Aydolan](Lore_Aydolan.md), the storyteller who was singing tales of the triumph and strength of Carinar during this travel. Carinar bestowed him the title of the first [Vateshran](Lore_Vateshrans.md) of the Spiritblood Clan.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup>

The famed Vateshran Aydolan created Vateshran's Rites, a set of trials held in the ancient caves of the Vateshran Hollows, where the veil between the planes of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md) was thin. They were prepared for the Spiritblood Clan members as a rite of passage<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup> as well as the trial for many chieftains of the clans of the Reach.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Preview-4)</sup> Everyone who survives the Rites earns a vateshran song that will last forever and the title of Spritblood Champion. Those who passed the trial were considered adult and fully-fledged members of the Spiritblood Clan.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESORites_Matron-5)</sup>

Eventually, Aydolan died, but he decided to remain on the Nirn as an ancestral spirit to guide the next generations of the Reachfolk,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup> a practice that was not unheard of among certain clans of the Reach.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GSOTR2-6)</sup> He was not the only Spiritblood clan member to continue to oversee the Rites after the death. A group of Reachfolk spirits maintained parts of the trial located in the Hunting Grounds.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESORC-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-statue-Totem_of_the_Brave.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Totem_of_the_Brave.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Totem_of_the_Brave.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Totem_of_the_Brave.jpg) One of the Totems of the Brave in the Vateshran Hollows Over a thousand years after the foundation of the clan, in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the members of Spiritblood Clan still inhabited southern parts of the Reach.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-A_Challenge_of_Worth-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Vateshran.27s_Rites-9)</sup> They were famed for their valiant and powerful warriors, who were described to show brutality in the battle that scares foreigners and other Reachfolk alike. It was speculated whether their combat prowess stems from their ancestry or their style of combat training. Warriors of the clan were believed to be brave and unyielding, not ceasing to fight until they die.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VH-3)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) accepted to participate in the Vateshran's Rites and accepted the challenge to travel through the realms of the Oblivion. After Vestige completed all the challenges but the last one Aydolan greeted them in the part of the trial known as the Champion's Circle where he revealed that the final test is to best his spirit in combat.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup> The trial was interrupted by [Maebroogha the Void Lich](Lore_Maebroogha.md), who consumed Aydolan's soul and unmade his very being in order to be able to manifest in this realm.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MTVL-10)</sup> Vestige slew the lich but spirit of the first vateshran of the Spiritblood Clan was absorbed into the [Void](Lore_Void.md) and no more.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Vateshran.27s_Rites-9)</sup>

Despite the death of the First Vateshran, the practice of the Vateshran's Rites remained alive for generations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup> Even after his soul was destroyed the trials remained active and were maintained by three sisters of the Spiritblood Clan - Binder in the Dark, Lamenter of the Slain, and [Rites Matron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rites_Matron).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESORites_Matron-5)</sup>

## Culture
*Try not to cross paths with members of the Spiritblood Clan. They show a brutality in battle that even scares other Reachfolk. Whether a gift of their blood or their style of combat training, their warriors do not cease until they lay dead.*
—Valencia Pitio, Imperial Commandant<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VH-3)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/ON-creature-Binder_in_the_Dark.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Binder_in_the_Dark.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Binder_in_the_Dark.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Binder_in_the_Dark.jpg) Binder in the Dark [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3b/ON-creature-Lamenter_of_the_Slain.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Lamenter_of_the_Slain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Lamenter_of_the_Slain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Lamenter_of_the_Slain.jpg) Lamenter of the Slain The Spiritblood Clan have their children undergo dangerous trials (called the Vateshran's Rites) as a rite of passage. Those who emerge become fully-fledged members of the clan, and earn a vateshran song that will last forever.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Angair-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESORites_Matron-5)</sup> Usually Reachfolk train their entire lives for that day. They enter knowing full well that it may mean their death.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Caddgarn-11)</sup>

The Lamenter of the Slain, one of the proctors of the trial grounds, performs rites on behalf of all who have died during their Vateshran's Rites. Warriors of the Spiritblood who felt defending the ways of their ancestors are honored by having Totems of the Brave erected in their honor.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Aydolan, one of the ancestor spirits personally guides prospective challengers.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Vateshran.27s_Rites-9)</sup> Binder in the Dark is responsible for keeping the balance as the power of Oblivion pushes, presses, and leaks out into the caverns.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BITD-13)</sup>. She specializes in controlling otherworldly intrusions and restrains the bleed between Vateshran Hollows and the Oblivion. Without her and the other sisters, the great spirits would overrun the Hollows.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup>

Even though they did not accept outsiders into their ranks,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESORites_Matron-5)</sup> they were not hostile to the foreigners and were not opposed to trading goods with them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Aydsil-14)</sup>

They were using crossbows and grappling bows since as early as [1E 2502](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2502).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAydolan-2)</sup>
