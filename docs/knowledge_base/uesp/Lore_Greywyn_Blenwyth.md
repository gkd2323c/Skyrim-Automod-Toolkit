Greywyn Blenwyth was an infamous [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) and the founder of the [Crimson Scars](Lore_Crimson_Scars.md), a splinter faction within the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). He sought to eventually infect the entire Brotherhood with vampirism, but he was betrayed and the Crimson Scars were destroyed. Greywyn went into hiding, but was eventually tracked down by the Dark Brotherhood and assassinated. He was devoted to the worship of [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), and was likely a member of the [Cyrodiil Vampyrum Order](Lore_Cyrodiil_Vampyrum_Order.md). Before his death, Greywyn gave ownership of his lair, [Deepscorn Hollow](Lore_Deepscorn_Hollow.md), to the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md), a long-lost relative.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBQuest-1)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
Greywyn Blenwyth was trained as an assassin by an unknown master. These shadow teachings made him highly proficient at sneaking, and he possessed uncanny dodging abilities that allowed him to remain light on his feet. He was proficient in the use of bows and short swords.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBPrima-2)</sup><sup>:15</sup>

Greywyn became a vampire in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), around the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOGreywyn-3)</sup>

### Imprisonment
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Greywyn journeyed to a cave known as Deepscorn Hollow, located beneath a small offshore island along the coast of [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md), in the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The ruins of an ancient [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) temple to [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) could be found within, and Greywyn entered the cave hoping the temple would hold answers about his vampiric condition. However, Deepscorn was occupied at that time by the [Unmaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unmakers) cult, led by the "Daughter of Sithis", an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) woman named Lalais. Lalais discovered Greywyn's vampirism when he was caught feeding on one of her followers and was intrigued, but she had him locked away in a prison cell upon discovering that he had not yet mastered his vampiric powers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOGreywyn-3)</sup>

While imprisoned, Greywyn encountered [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in the company of the mercenary Tenarei Vels. Tenarei had been hired to track down Quistley Silvelle, a noble from [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) who had fallen in with the cult. Greywyn aided the party in tracking down Quistley and Lalais within the ruins, and asked to be freed from his cell in return. However, it is unknown if he was released.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

### The Crimson Scars
Greywyn joined the Dark Brotherhood circa [3E 421](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_421) with the express intent of converting its members to vampirism, thinking that it was the desire of Sithis for vampires to act as his Hand.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-desc-5)</sup> He and his kin infiltrated the Brotherhood and began to convert others to their ways. However, he realized that existing members may be angered or new recruits deterred from joining if vampirism became a requirement. By carefully forging an alliance with several key members of the Brotherhood, he was convinced that he could persuade or dispatch any who opposed the silent takeover. He dubbed his splinter faction the Crimson Scars, a name derived from the unusually vicious neck lacerations left by his assassins and their jagged blades.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-desc-5)</sup>

At some point, Greywyn entered an unknown [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) to clear out the bandits encamped within. He was injured by a bandit's arrow when an ancient dart trap revealed his position. He prayed to the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) as the bandits descended,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBPrima-2)</sup><sup>:15</sup> and evidently he survived the encounter.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-journal-6)</sup>

The turning point of Greywyn's plans called for the swift execution of the current upper echelon of the Dark Brotherhood in one massive, cautiously executed strike. However, the Scars were betrayed by one of their own members, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named Silarian, who informed the [Black Hand](Lore_Black_Hand.md) of their plans. The second [Purification](Lore_Purification.md) in the Dark Brotherhood's history was carried out when assassins bearing silver weapons fell upon the Scars as they slept, killing most of the members.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-desc-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-journal-6)</sup> Greywyn's creation of the Scars did not escape the notice of historians, and the act was one of the notable events of the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), with his name being infamous enough to be mentioned directly in later timelines of the period.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TET-7)</sup>

### Deepscorn Hollow
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/OB-interior-Deepscorn_Hollow_08.jpg/200px-OB-interior-Deepscorn_Hollow_08.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Deepscorn_Hollow_08.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-interior-Deepscorn_Hollow_08.jpg) The Shrine of Sithis in Deepscorn Hollow Greywyn somehow escaped the carnage and retreated to Deepscorn Hollow, which he had previously used as a hideaway when he was a novice.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-journal-6)</sup> Here he attempted to reform the Crimson Scars, using the cave as a base of operations. He sent a message across Cyrodiil, seeking those who would join his cause or any surviving Scars, but it was to no avail.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-desc-5)</sup> He succeeded only in contacting [Rowley Eardwulf](Lore_Rowley_Eardwulf.md), a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) member of the Crimson Scars who had also survived. Rowley agreed to provide Greywyn with the tools necessary to improve his lair and return Deepscorn Hollow to its former glory.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-journal-6)</sup>

Following the Purification, all of Greywyn's prayers to Sithis had went unanswered, despite many sacrifices. His vampiric nature and the betrayal of his Brothers had displeased Sithis. When his Dark Master eventually did speak, the god agreed to approve of the Crimson Scars, but at a price. As punishment, Greywyn was ordered to renounce his vampiric ways. In his search for a cure, Greywyn came across a musty tome in a ruined fort that bore the answer: [Purgeblood Salts](Lore_Purgeblood_Salts.md), an alchemical ingredient that can be bathed in to cure vampirism.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-desc-5)</sup>

He spent months gathering the ingredient necessary to refine the salts. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), almost twelve years after having found the cure, Greywyn sensed his impending death. He tracked down the Hero of Kvatch, a long-lost relative of his and the heir to Deepscorn Hollow.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBQuest-1)</sup> For a time Greywyn watched the Hero of Kvatch from afar, and eventually delivered a note making himself known. He provided the Hero with a map to Deepscorn Hollow, requesting only that the Hero honor Sithis with the darkest of deeds.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> With all his preparations for the cure complete, Greywyn approached his shrine to Sithis for one last prayer, when he was stabbed in the back by an assassin from the Dark Brotherhood, which had finally tracked him down.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-desc-5)</sup> The Hero of Kvatch subsequently arrived in Deepscorn Hollow and found Greywyn's [journal](Lore_Greywyn%27s_Journal.md). The hero then contacted Rowley Eardwulf, who agreed to renovate the lair.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBQuest-1)</sup>

## Notes
- The [official *obliviondownloads.com* description](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Vile_Lair#Background) states that Greywyn was assassinated by the Dark Brotherhood before being able to use the Purgeblood Salts, which had only taken months to prepare. [*Greywyn's Journal*](Lore_Greywyn%27s_Journal.md) states that preparations began in [3E 421](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_421). However, the [*Bloodstained Note*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Bloodstained_Note) states that Greywyn was still alive in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), almost twelve years later, a slight discrepancy. - The *obliviondownloads.com* description also states that Greywyn had discovered and named Deepscorn Hollow personally. This is contradicted by the cave's [appearance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Deepscorn_Hollow) in ESO.
- Greywyn may also be related to [Taris Rendil](Lore_Taris_Rendil.md), another long-lost relative of the Hero of Kvatch.
- In *[Oblivion Remastered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion_Remastered)*, the Hero of Kvatch does not inherit Deepscorn Hollow from a long-lost relative. Instead, the Hero claims it after following rumors of Greywyn's attempt at resurrecting the Crimson Scars there.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
