# Huntsman's Warmask

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4d/ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask.jpg/200px-ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask.jpg) Huntsman's Warmask *What Aspect will he show you? The flank of the unicorn? The skull of the wolf? Bloody claws or bloody hooves? Aye, or even the paw print of the fox? Whatever face you see, so Uraccanach says, you are blessed in the seeing.*
— [Uraccanach the Witchman](Lore_Uraccanach.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HW-1)</sup> [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Huntsman%27s_Warmask.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Huntsman%27s_Warmask.png) Huntsman's Warmask Huntsman's Warmask is a powerful relic associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of the Hunt, [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), and connected to both [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) and [Ket Keptu](Lore_Ket_Keptu.md) cultures. Those who wore the mask were empowered and enraged while hunting their chosen prey, enabling them to brand the quarry with the Mark of Hircine in the likeness of the [Bloodmoon](Lore_Bloodmoon.md), though this boon came at the cost of diminished combat effectiveness against other foes. The mask also revealed various aspects of Hircine to its wielders. Upon completing a successful hunt, the wearer was also rejuvenated.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HW-1)</sup>

## History
*The five are all, the five are one. Five are true and death-in-the-woods.*
—Engravings on the Huntsman's Warmask as interpreted by Verita Numida<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOBM-2)</sup> The exact origins of the Huntsman's Warmask were uncertain. The Fangs of Bitter Mercy, one of its key components, were believed to be of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) origin, their engravings forming a chant to [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) in his aspect as Alrabeg, the Hunter: *The five are all, the five are one. Five are true and death-in-the-woods.* Scholar Verita Numida argued for a Reachfolk origins of the Fangs, while Ugron gro-Thumog proposed that similar motifs could be found on war-masks belonging to great chiefs of the Ket Keptu. He also noted that the fangs represent the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md), also called Spear with Five Points. Reginus Buca agreed that the Ket Keptu, known for their skilled hunters and warrior-smiths would consider wielding the Spear of Bitter Mercy a great honor, concluding that any who bore this war-mask would have been one favored by Hircine himself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOBM-2)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask_02.jpg/200px-ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Huntsman%27s_Warmask_02.jpg) Hunstman's Warmask's boon [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-effect-Mark_of_Hircine.jpg/200px-ON-effect-Mark_of_Hircine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-effect-Mark_of_Hircine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-effect-Mark_of_Hircine.jpg) The quarry branded with the Mark of Hircine The Mighty Bear's Snarl was another component of the artifact, a carved wooden face-mask shaped like the snarling snout of a beast. It closely resembled several Reachfolk relics found within the [Shrine of the Hunt-Father](Lore_Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.md) in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). According to High Shaman [Glynroch](Lore_Glynroch.md) and Verita Numida, it represented one of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s aspects, Hrokkibeg, the Mighty Bear. The similarity, however, was not limited to Reachfolk craftsmanship. Ancient [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) heroes of the Ket Keptu were also known to bear emblems and totems depicting bears. Ugron gro-Thumog theorized that the war-mask incorporating this snout may once have belonged to a prominent war-chief or hunt-leader, a champion of Hircine. While scholars debated on whether the Fangs and the Snarl came from the Ket Keptu or the Reachfolk,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBS-3)</sup> the two cultures were known to have exchanged traditions and shared notable stylistic similarities in their ceremonial headdresses.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOKeptu Skull-4)</sup> Furthermore, some Reachfolk clans, such as the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md), descended directly from older Nedic groups of the Keptu,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MTCD-5)</sup> and were themselves renowned for their smithing prowess.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CM54Bloodforge-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Store-7)</sup>

The Foxshadow Headwrap, another component of the Huntsman's Warmask, was more universally believed to be of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) origin. Crafted from the fur of a fox, the headwrap was thought to have once been part of a ceremonial Reachfolk mask, possibly worn by a shaman or chieftain beneath the war-mask itself. Despite its great age, the cloth portion of the artifact has not decayed as most textiles of its era would have. Due to the materials used, scholars believe it to represent Gulibeg, the Quick Fox, one of Hircine's aspects associated with cunning, speed, and trickery.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FH-8)</sup> Similarly to the headwrap, the Skinshifter's Cowl was attributed to the Reachfolk. Scholars identified it as resembling the cowls worn by the [Reachfolk shamans](Lore_Reachfolk_Shamans.md) in contemporary times as beast-themed coverings were often incorporated into ceremonial headdresses or war-masks. Initially, researchers struggled to identify the type of leather used, though they agreed on its considerable age. [Amalien](Lore_Amalien.md) later theorized that the hide had been blessed for [shape-changing](Lore_Lycanthropy.md), suggesting it once belonged to a powerful Reachfolk shaman and follower of Hircine. Buca referenced Uraccanach's writings, associating this component with yet another aspect of Hircine, Storihbeg, the Manbeast.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SC-9)</sup> The mask was anointed with Dark Stag Oil, a sacred substance to the Reachfolk, dedicated to yet another aspect of Hircine, Uricanbeg, the Great Stag. It was believed that warriors donning the warmask imbued with this oil could drive their enemies away in terror.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DSO-10)</sup>

Uraccanach was known to comment on the Huntsman's Warmask, a powerful relic associated with Hircine tied to both his own Reachfolk culture and that of the [Ket Keptu](Lore_Ket_Keptu.md). He spoke of the various aspects Hircine could reveal to those who wield the mask, mentioning forms such as the flank of the [unicorn](Lore_Unicorn.md), the skull of the [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md), bloody claws, bloody hooves, or even the paw print of the [fox](Lore_Fox.md). According to Uraccanach, whichever visage appeared, the wearer would be blessed in witnessing it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HW-1)</sup> Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a member of the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) at the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) followed several leads to locate multiple parts of the mask. After unearthing and reassembling them, the artifact was sent to the Circle's scholars for study.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HW-1)</sup>
