[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg/200px-SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Druadach_Mountains.jpg) The Druadach Mountains [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg/200px-MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg) Skyrim The Druadach Mountains are a mountain range which separate western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and eastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). The misty peaks tower over [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md) in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), though they gradually diminish closer to [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md) and [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). The range curves around the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md), and nearly stretches into [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md). It is part of the greater [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-1)</sup> bordering its other subranges such as the [Wrothgarian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains) range in the High Rock regions of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) and [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai) to the east. It makes up the north border of [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn), while the south border is defined by the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> The [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md), an enormous glacial valley, is located deep within the Druadach Mountains, on the border between Skyrim and High Rock.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DG-3)</sup>

Since the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) have been taking advantage of the cliffs, caves, and rugged terrain of the Druadach Mountains in guerrilla warfare campaigns against invading armies.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TLORE-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TMOTR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1C-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1High-7)</sup> They are home to many ancient ruins, fortresses, and [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) strongholds.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SR-8)</sup>

## Notes
- Back in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), when Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) ruled over Markarth, a piece of land in the Druadachs was loaned to the King of Worms, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) and it was, in turn, ripped from the face of Tamriel.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONCaddach-9)</sup> It was cast into the depths of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) where it became known as [Grayhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grayhaven) and the remnants of that land were later called the Sundered Land.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONVerandis-10)</sup> A Dwemer lift in [Nchuand-Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nchuand-Zel) goes directly to the area.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONCaddach-9)</sup>
