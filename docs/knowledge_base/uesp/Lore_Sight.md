# Sight

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg) An Oracle of the Imperial Cult The Sight,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DSEustacia-2)</sup> sometimes referred to as **divination** and **scrying**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OOTED-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLilatha-4)</sup> is the ability possessed by some individuals that allows them to divine information through visions and prophecy. Such users may be called **Oracles**, **Seers** (or **Seeresses**),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Volgo-5)</sup> **Farseers**, **Soothsayers**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vanus TAVD-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Rhea-7)</sup> **Prognosticators**, or (parasynonymously) **Prophets**. The manner of visions attained by Sight vary. Such visions can include foretelling potential futures, seeing into other realms, revealing hidden things, finding lost places, and even communicating at a distance.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vanus TAVD-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OV-8)</sup>

A vision is not always simply a visual perception, conveying non-visual senses like sound, smell, touch, and temperature, as well as information decoupled from any of the senses.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Rhea-7)</sup> However, visions are not absolute, and may provide an incomplete story, information that is subject to change, or wisdom that is often vague, abstract, and interpretive.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Marieve TAVD-9)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Rhea-7)</sup> Using Sight can work better when the seer is feeling their best, and using their Sight can sap their energy for a time.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The clarity of such visions can imply an increase in its validity.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Rhea-7)</sup> Visions can also occur in ones dreams.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Vandoril-11)</sup>

While some are born with the Sight or a natural affinity for it, devotion to the [gods](Lore_Gods.md) can grant the Sight.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Lalatia-13)</sup> It is also suggested that lineage plays some factor in one's predisposition to be born with the Sight.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DSEustacia-2)</sup> Most [Alfiq](Lore_Alfiq.md) are blessed with Sight.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Some seers claim that their Sight manifests through dialogue with inanimate objects.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Others may receive visions involuntarily, which can be troubling. Seer's Stones are amulets known to help stifle one's oracular senses.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> [Azura](Lore_Azura.md)'s associations with mystery, fate, and prophecy<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> make her more connected to the Sight (which her followers call the gift of **Crystal Vision**) and mortal seers than most gods.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWNibani-18)</sup> Azura believes that prophecy must occur, and guides her seers that can't always endure "knowledge of the truth".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-5PS-19)</sup> [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) have also been known to possess the gift of Sight on occasion.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Volgo-5)</sup>

The ability to predict the future through examination of animal entrails was not an uncommon ability among [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [Shamans](Lore_Reachfolk_Shamans.md) of [Namira](Lore_Namira.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-WB-20)</sup> and [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) haruspices among the [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) of [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FH-21)</sup> The [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Dhalmora](Lore_Dhalmora.md) perform this practice on [guar](Lore_Guar.md) intestines.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESODhalmora-22)</sup> A similar ability, an ancient scrying technique that required usage of human entrails instead, was known as **Visceromancy**. It was practiced by the denizens of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SA-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TPAS-24)</sup> Some individuals possess the ability to derive readings from the pattern of tea leaves left at the bottom of a cup.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESODhalmora-22)</sup>

Some [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) are known to possess the gift of Sight. It was said that the dragon blood possessed by members of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) allowed them to see more than lesser men.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) was troubled all his life by dreams that showed him visions of the future, including his assassination and the start of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) that followed.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Uriel-26)</sup>

A [Neesak of Scrying](Lore_Neesak_of_Scrying.md) is a crystal that is believed to possess divinatory properties and is used for fortune-telling or divination purposes.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-NOS-27)</sup>

While the gods<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Kot NProphet-28)</sup> and [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md)<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> are known to grant prophecies, this does not necessarily confer the power of Sight. However, such individuals are still referred to as Prophets.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Kot NProphet-28)</sup>

## Notable Seers
- The Prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md) — Also known as Marukh the Seer; a prophet of the First Era.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>
- [Velothi](Lore_Ashlanders.md) Wise Women<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> — Spiritual leaders whose prophetic visions help their tribe prepare for dark days.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup> They affirm that "the star is the key, and the dream is the door", meaning that dreams are the door to prophecy, and that Azura unlocks that path.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWNibani-18)</sup>
- [The Oracle](Lore_The_Oracle.md) — Foresaw [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md)'s defeat in [The War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md), though her counsel was disregarded by [King Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md), arguably leading to his demise.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup>
- [Eustacia](Lore_Eustacia.md) — Assisted the [Hero of Dawnstar](Lore_Hero_of_Dawnstar.md) in singling out [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md)'s traitor by providing crucial oracular intelligence.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup>
- Heliand — A soothsayer and troubadour who found Vanus Galerion - then named Trechtus - after his escape from [Sollicich-on-Ker](Lore_Sollicich-on-Ker.md).
- Oracle Marieve — A witch of the [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md) who predicted the deaths of Kings [Folbert](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Folbert) and [Tamlien](Lore_Tamlien.md) and regularly communicated with the [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vanus TAVD-6)</sup> In the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) abducted her in an attempt to extract the location of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) from her in an act by the [Daedric Triad](Lore_Daedric_Triad.md) to further their influence on [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Marieve TAVD-9)</sup> However, she did not know its location, as her Sight only allowed her simple communication. She escaped from [The Spiral Skein](Lore_Spiral_Skein.md) with help from [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) and [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).
- Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) — Known for his prophetic dreams.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Uriel-26)</sup>
- [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) Oracles — Are granted visions by the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md). Some of these visions depict [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) of divine make that have fallen into unworthy hands.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Lalatia-13)</sup>
- The [Imperial Saints](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) and the [Drake of Blades](Lore_Drake_of_Blades.md) — Said to have been blessed with precognitive powers by the Divines.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup>
- [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) — Known to have prophetic predictions and has constructed [factotums](Lore_Factotum.md) like The Grand Prognosticator that are capable of some semblance of foresight. However, it is unclear if he or his machines possess the Sight, if the predictions are educated guesses founded on statistical data analysis, or something inbetween.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36)</sup>
- [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) — Was known for her various prophecies such as those related to the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-37)</sup> Despite prophetic tendencies from both Vivec and Sotha Sil, Ayem claimed that they were not seers and that only she was privy of what would come to pass.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TRAlmalexia-38)</sup>
- [Druid King Kasorayn](Lore_Kasorayn.md) — Most well known seer in [druidic](Lore_Druid.md) history.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LADCG-39)</sup>
- [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) — Foresaw the events of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).
- Oracles of Azurah — A faction of seers in service to Azura who operate in Pariah Abbey of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Shrike](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md) Dyzera began kidnapping the oracles in service to the Daedric Triad. With the help of seer Rhea Opacarius, the Vestige freed them from Dyzera's realm.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-40)</sup>
- Ravencrone Family — Jarl [Idgrod](Lore_Idgrod_Ravencrone.md) of [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) and her children, although they don't recognize it, are manipulating Magicka subconsciously in order to achieve their visions. Idgrod the Younger is much more adept at separating the visions from reality.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-41)</sup><sup>:436</sup>
- [Rynandor the Bold](Lore_Rynandor_the_Bold.md) — A seer-mage from Summerset who met a violent fate, likely at the hands of the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup>
- Temples of the Divines — Known to have seers.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-43)</sup>
- The art of scrying is prominently used in the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md). The Master of the Scrye is a high-ranking member of the Guild that serves as counsel to the Master of Incunabula.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-OOTMG-44)</sup> The [Order of the Eye](Lore_Order_of_the_Eye.md) is a sub-faction of the Mages Guild that uses scrying in their activities.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OOTED-3)</sup>
- The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) practice the art of divination and have kept it to themselves for many years, away from the public and the Mages Guild.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-THOTR-45)</sup> That also use an artifact called the [Obscuros](Lore_Obscuros.md), a scrying device that intensifies the user's perception and projects their visions outward. It was stored away on the remote island of [Dranil Kir](Lore_Dranil_Kir.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLilatha-4)</sup>
- The [Witchmother coven](Lore_Witchmothers.md) scry for disturbances in the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) by peering into their cauldrons, looking for signs of approaching woe.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Taerma-46)</sup>
- [Dro'Jizad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro%27Jizad) was a prophet from [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), whose prophesies have yet to be revealed to the public, even in the late Third Era.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-THOTR-45)</sup>
- [Sibellec](Lore_Sibellec.md) was a seer from [The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) who aided the [Wyrd](Lore_Wyrd.md) against Mother Murk in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-WAC-47)</sup>
- The [Fate-Bearers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fate-Bearers) are a group of prophetic assassins based at their shrine of the changing fates on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). They use the rare gift of sight to see what the future holds, and to eliminate targets that have the potential to cause unmitigated disasters.
