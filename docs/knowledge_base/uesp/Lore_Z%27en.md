# Z'en

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg) A Z'en idol Z'en is a God of Toil, vengeance, agriculture, and payment in kind worshipped by [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md). Although he is the god of agriculture, he is rumored to have a larger realm of influence than just crops and cattle. Studies indicates his mythology originates from both [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) and [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) cultures.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup> Like [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), Z'en is a god of [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONAllanwen-2)</sup> The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) recognize an et'Ada known as [Xen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xen),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> who is sometimes recognized as the Altmeri spelling of Z'en.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LSP:OAC-4)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-crown_store-Z%27en%27s_Vengeance_Kiss-Me-Not_Cap.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Z%27en%27s_Vengeance_Kiss-Me-Not_Cap.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Z%27en%27s_Vengeance_Kiss-Me-Not_Cap.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Z%27en%27s_Vengeance_Kiss-Me-Not_Cap.jpg) Z'en Vengeance Kiss-Me-Not Cap Some Bosmer would wear a special cap in honor of Z'en, to show their devotion to fulfilling a desire for vengeance, even to the point of renouncing love while the cap was worn. Sometimes the cap was seemingly worn only as a joke.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ZVKMNC-5)</sup>

Z'en was supposedly the chief deity of the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOChirah-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Chirah Shovel-7)</sup> and scholars speculate that it was introduced into [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) by Kothringi sailors.

Z'en is often compared to the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) god of commerce [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), with Zenithar considered the more refined of the two.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup> Z'en is used as a name for Zenithar as the patron deity of the [Resolution of Z'en](Lore_Resolution_of_Z%27en.md), temples erected to worship and praise Zenithar.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DUG-8)</sup>

Z'en is believed to be an entity of much higher cosmic order than simply agriculture. This line of thought is reinforced by the Emissary of Z'en, who claims Z'en wishes for balance, as "one must discard what makes the scales tip".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOEmissary Zen-9)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg/200px-ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Emissary_of_Z%27en.jpg) The Emissary of Z'en [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-item-Shaman_Chirah%27s_Devotion_Shovel.jpg/200px-ON-item-Shaman_Chirah%27s_Devotion_Shovel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shaman_Chirah%27s_Devotion_Shovel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shaman_Chirah%27s_Devotion_Shovel.jpg) Kothringi Shaman Devotion Shovel dedicated to Z'en His shrines once spanned the whole of the Valenwood before the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) decimated his faithful, believed to be the work of [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) [Shadowscales](Lore_Shadowscales.md) infiltrating his worshippers and spreading the disease.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mathragor-10)</sup> The last of his dedicated followers were a clan of Bosmer in the Bloodtoil Valley, who had long been at odds with the [Wood Orc](Lore_Wood_Orc.md) [Drublog Clan](Lore_Drublog_Clan.md), who revered [Mauloch](Lore_Mauloch.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFOB-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DSJ-12)</sup> A war soon broke out among them in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) with casualties on both sides, and Z'en, growing impatient the world, sent his emissary to deal with his follower Mathragor, a Bosmer who threatened to unleash a curse upon the Drublog, as the curse would only feed Mauloch's fury.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOEmissary Zen-9)</sup> [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) may have persuaded him to not unleash the curse, but it is unknown what course of action he took. In the end, Z'en turned away from the world until the time that the scales would tip once more.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Payment In Kind-13)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOEmissary Zen-9)</sup>

The Stags of Z'en were a Bosmeri force who exacted punishment upon the wicked in the name of the Green. They revered Z'en as the god of toil and vengeance.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Circa 2E 582, the Stags were sent by [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) to face the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Maarselok](Lore_Maarselok.md) in the [Tenmar Valley](Lore_Tenmar.md), but they were infected with the dragon's [Azure Blight](Lore_Azure_Blight.md) and had to be rescued by the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AZQ-16)</sup>

By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the use of Z'en (or Zen) as a short name for Zenithar<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DFRumors-17)</sup> had developed around the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), where the [Resolution of Z'en](Lore_Resolution_of_Z%27en.md) temples worshipped Zenithar.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

## Notes
- The [Ghost of the Green](Lore_The_Ghost_of_the_Green.md) is a folkloric assassin that is sometimes called "Z'en's avenging champion", and was believed to have operated in [Russafeld](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Russafeld) around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Old Wounds-19)</sup>
- The [Z'en's Whitefish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Z%27en%27s_Whitefish) is a fish found in [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md) that is named after him.
- Some sources describe Z'en as having some sort of connection or similarity to the god [Mauloch](Lore_Malacath.md). It is said that both gods demand a 'blood price' and 'payment in kind' and thus both demand retribution against the other. It is also said that Zen's presence fades from the world as that of Mauloch grows stronger.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONSpinner-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONZ.27en-21)</sup>
