# Daedric Faces

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/LO-misc-Daedric_Helms.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Daedric_Helms.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Daedric_Helms.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Daedric_Helms.jpg) Daedric Faces Daedric Faces,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFOT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFOI-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFOG-3)</sup> also known more simply as **Daedric helms** or **Daedric helmets**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup> are ceremonial [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) full-face [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Equipment.md) masks that depicted various figures from Dunmer culture.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFOT-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup> They should not be confused with the unique [Masks of the Tribunal](Lore_Masks_of_the_Tribunal.md) worn by the members of the [ALMSIVI](Lore_Tribunal.md): the [Mask of Almalexia](Lore_Mask_of_Almalexia.md), the [Mask of Sotha Sil](Lore_Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.md), and [Vivec's Ash Mask](Lore_Vivec%27s_Ash_Mask.md), or the [Mask of Dagoth Ur](Lore_Mask_of_Dagoth_Ur.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MOTS-5)</sup>

## Types of Daedric Faces

### Daedric Face of Terror
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png/150px-MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Terror.png) Face of Terror **Daedric Face of Terror**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFOT-1)</sup> also known as the **Terrifying Face** was a type of mask associated with [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Indoril_Nerevar.md). Only the members of [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) were permitted to don them.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup> They represented Nervar's more terrible aspect as [Hortator](Lore_Hortator.md) and [Padomaic](Lore_Padomay.md) champion, possibly as a [bonewalker](Lore_Bonewalker.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup> As of [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the locations of two Faces of Terror were known. One was in the possession of [Enar Dralor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Enar_Dralor), an [Ordinator](Lore_Ordinator.md) [Knight](Lore_Knights.md) Commander, while the other was hidden deep within [Anudnabia](Lore_Anudnabia.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ED-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Anudnabia-8)</sup> The [Golden Mask of Devotion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golden_Mask_of_Devotion) was another type of Indoril mask worn by the Ordinators.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AGTLV-9)</sup> It is said to depict Nerevar's true visage.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>

### Daedric Face of Inspiration
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Inspiration.jpg/150px-MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Inspiration.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Inspiration.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_Inspiration.jpg) Face of Inspiration **Daedric Face of Inspiration**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFOI-2)</sup> also known as the **The Face of the Inspiring Fountain** was a type of mask associated with the Prince of Plots [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md). Only the members of [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) were permitted to don them.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup> The twin horns of the Face of Inspiration floated in the proximity of the helmet.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFOI-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup> As of [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the locations of three Faces of Inspiration were known. One was in the possession of [Galdal Omayn](Lore_Galdal_Omayn.md), the Grand Marshal of the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md). Other two were found in the Shrine of Pitted Dreams within the caverns of Mamaea and inside Ibar-Dad's chambers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mamaea-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ibar-Dad-11)</sup>

### Daedric Face of God
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_God.jpg/150px-MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_God.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_God.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Face_of_God.jpg) Face of God **Daedric Face of God**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFOG-3)</sup> or simply **The Face of God** was a type of mask associated with [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md). Only Vivecian priests were permitted to don them.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup> As of [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the only known Daedric Face of God was stored within the resting place of [Olmgerd the Outlaw](Lore_Olmgerd.md) in Tukushapal, a ruin connected to the Marvani Ancestral Tomb.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tukushapal-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MAT-13)</sup>

### The Comforting Face of Tendrils
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/LO-misc-Sheo_Mask.jpg/150px-LO-misc-Sheo_Mask.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Sheo_Mask.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Sheo_Mask.jpg) The Comforting Face of Tendrils **The Comforting Face of Tendrils** was a type of mask associated with the Fourth Corner of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md), [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). It was illegal to wear unless the bearer was insane.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup>

### The Face of the Forbidden Tickle
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/LO-misc-Molag_Bal_Mask.jpg/150px-LO-misc-Molag_Bal_Mask.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Molag_Bal_Mask.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Molag_Bal_Mask.jpg) The Face of the Forbidden Tickle **The Face of the Forbidden Tickle** was a type of mask associated with the Third Corner of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). It was illegal to wear.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup>

### The Face of the Husband of Fire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/LO-misc-Dagon_Mask.jpg/150px-LO-misc-Dagon_Mask.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Dagon_Mask.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Dagon_Mask.jpg) The Face of the Husband of Fire **The Face of the Husband of Fire** was a type of mask associated with the Second Corner of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). It was illegal to wear.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFTDE-4)</sup>

## Notes
- Despite [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) being the First Corner of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TS-14)</sup> it is unknown whether he is represented with a traditional Daedric Face.
- Daedric Faces were originally intended to extend below the wearer's waist, effectively serving as torso armor as well, though at an estimated weight of five hundred pounds, they would be utterly unwearable without immense strength.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- Various mundane masks depicting members of the Almsivi, such as the Mercymother's Crown, the Chimer Half-Mask, and the Sotha Sil Novelty Mask were also worn on different occasions by various groups. However, these masks were not Daedric in nature.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOMAsks-15)</sup>
- [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) used masks inscribed with [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) inscriptions in to heal. Some of these inscriptions could be translated as *stasis* or *return*, *wither* or *deflate*, and *barren* or *dry out*, leading some to speculate that the masks were used to cure victims of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TNLM-16)</sup>
- [Dunmeri funerary practices](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Death_Dunmer) also involved death masks crafted from other materials, such as [clay](Lore_Clay.md) or [gold](Lore_Gold.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-NDM-17)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-IDM-18)</sup>
