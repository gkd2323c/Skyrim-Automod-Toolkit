Brazollus Dor was the fourth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) and [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). He was the illegitimate son of [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md) and a common-born maid named Irinia Dor. Due to the illegitimacy of his birth and the expectation that Reman II would produce a legitimate heir, it was unlikely he would become emperor. However, the unprepared Brazollus assumed the throne after Reman II's death during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md). At the beginning of his reign, the ill-prepared Brazollus did his best to fulfill his responsibilities. As his reign continued, he became uninterested in matters of state, so he delegated nearly all issues of policy and politics to his potentate, [Sidri-Ashak](Lore_Sidri-Ashak.md), and he spent the rest of his reign partaking in libertine activities, games, and revels.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life
Brazollus Dor was born in [1E 2815](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2815), and he was the illegitimate son of Emperor [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md) and a common-born maid named Irinia Dor. Reman II provided a comfortable estate for Irinia and Brazollus, allowing them to live well. Brazollus was described as powerful and handsome in his youth, and he strongly resembled his illustrious forefather, [Reman I](Lore_Reman.md). Since it was expected that Reman II would produce a legitimate heir, Brazollus settled into the life of a minor lord, not an Imperial scion. He spent his days looking after his vineyard, hunting, and entertaining friends.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup>

### Emperor of Tamriel
In [1E 2843](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2843), Reman II was killed in battle against the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), so Brazollus succeeded his father as emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RIITLo A-3)</sup> He decided to keep his mother's surname when he ascended the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup> At the beginning of his reign, Brazollus did his best to fulfill his new responsibilities.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup> He resumed his father's campaign in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), fighting valiantly enough and even personally leading the vanguard into battle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-2920FS-4)</sup> However, Brazollus was less successful in handling his administrative and diplomatic duties at the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), which he proved ill-suited to deal with.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup>

Unhappy in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), he soon retreated to his estate in the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) countryside, and he began spending more and more time away from the capital.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup> Eventually, the Emperor became uninterested in matters of state, so he delegated nearly all issues of policy and politics to his potentate, [Sidri-Ashak](Lore_Sidri-Ashak.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSC1-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAHS-6)</sup> This was Brazollus' one wise decision during his reign, for Sidri-Ashak proved to be both a skilled administrator and a man of integrity.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup>

With Sidri-Ashak administering the [Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), he busied himself with the construction of Villa Doria, a palatial estate in the hills west of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup> Brazollus preferred to spend most of his time at his estate, and he returned to the White-Gold Tower less and less frequently as his reign progressed, until he stopped bothering to return at all.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup> From [1E 2857](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2857) onward, Villa Doria served as the Imperial Court, although little official business was actually conducted there. Instead, it was a place of entertainment and diversions.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup>

Freed from his burden of duty, Brazollus was credited with advancing the arts of feasting, idleness, and debauchery to a level unrivaled until the reign of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSC1-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAHS-6)</sup> Brazollus spent his time partaking in libertine activities, games, and revels, many of which turned into orgies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSC1-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAHS-6)</sup> Brazollus loved hunting in the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md), so he officially declared a large section of the region's plains to be his personal game reserve, which became known as the [Imperial Reserve](Lore_Imperial_Reserve.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AHJIITIR-7)</sup>

One notable event of his reign was the extravagant Feast of Saint [Coellicia](Lore_Coellicia.md) to honor a minor martyr of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), which was held at the Emperor's autumnal estate in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). Saint Coellicia is traditionally honored in the closing days of Last Seed with a fast, but to try and rekindle the adoration of his subjects, Brazollus instead sought to turn the fasting day into a feasting day. Brazollus' feast was composed of forty dishes, arranged in eight courses of five dishes each.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSC1-5)</sup>

### Death
In [1E 2877](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2877), Brazollus died suddenly, reportedly after engaging in unspecified intense debaucheries, although some people suspected foul play.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAHS-6)</sup> It was rumored that factions unhappy with Sidri-Ashak's growing power decided to neutralize the potentate by bringing a vigorous new emperor to the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup> Brazollus was succeeded by [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup> Reman III quickly moved to return the Imperial Court to the Imperial City and exercise power in his own name.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup> Despite not being a particularly capable ruler or much interested in matters of state, the Empire flourished during Brazollus' reign.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup>

After Brazollus' death, Villa Doria was all but abandoned and later fell into ruin during the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TEot V-1)</sup>

## Notes
- Brazollus had a brother-in-law named Anwentende, who was captured by pirates and castrated shortly before Brazollus held his Feast of Saint Coellicia.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FSC3-8)</sup> Brazollus had a paramour named Lady Tusks-Not-Seen.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FSC2-9)</sup>
