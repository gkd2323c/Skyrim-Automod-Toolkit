## Daedra Count
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/BS-creature-Daedra_Count_02.png/200px-BS-creature-Daedra_Count_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Daedra_Count_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Daedra_Count_02.png) A Daedra Count A Daedra Count is a formidable, and highly intelligent greater [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) being. Described as middle nobility of the Daedra,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-1)</sup><sup>:63</sup> they have command over many [Lesser Daedra](Lore_Lesser_Daedra.md). They are known to have a strong sense of honor, and even show respect to mortals. They can cast a variety of spells, but also are proficient in close quarters combat. Many of these high-ranking Daedra are known to serve [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Daedra_Count)

## [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/d/db/BS-creature-Daedra_Lord.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Daedra_Lord.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Daedra_Lord.gif) A Daedra Lord A [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) (also spelled **Daedric Lord** or **Daedroth Lord**)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Daedric Lord)</sup> is a greater [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) being who occupies a rarefied space near the top of Daedric society, just one rung down from the Daedric Princes themselves, maintaining great power and hierarchy over [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that is matched only by the [Demiprinces](Lore_Demiprince.md), and exceeded only by the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). The powers of the Daedra Lords are varied in scope, with some embodying their own individual spheres, and others being servants of the Daedric Princes who help maintain sub-spheres. Many of the more powerful Lords also rule over their own realms and demi-planes within the infinite swirling void of Oblivion.

Daedra Lords are one of the most powerful Daedra to be commonly seen in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and are to be feared by all but the most experienced adventurers. They are known to be powerful fighters and spell casters, and can only be harmed by [mithril](Lore_Mithril.md) or better [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md). [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) has many Daedra Lords in his service.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daedra_Lord), [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Daedra_Lord), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Crow_Mother), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Daedric Titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/ON-creature-Daedric_Titan.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Daedric_Titan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Daedric_Titan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Daedric_Titan.jpg) A Daedric Titan [Daedric Titans](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md), commonly referred to simply as **Titans**, are giant winged [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) that serve [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) as war-slaves. They were first created in imitation of the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) when Bal's interrogation of [Boziikkodstrun](Lore_Boziikkodstrun.md) proved fruitless. After modifying Boziikkodstrun's skeleton and substituting his [soul](Lore_Souls.md) for a Daedric [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md), Bal created the first Titan. Daedric Titans are anatomically similar to Dragons, although with dark black skin and prehensile frontal limbs. They have the ability to speak a spell of flaming essence-drain that can debilitate an opponent with a single word, similar to the power of the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md). They are notoriously difficult to kill. Some Titans are much larger than others, requiring the use of [siege weapons](Lore_Siege_Weapons.md).

They serve Molag Bal as the guardians of souls, and therefore the keepers of black soul gems. However, they are also posted to guard other strategic resources. According to some sources, Titans were released upon [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for the first time during the attempted [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Specifically, Molag Bal released them into the skies over the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) when a portal to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) was opened above the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

Daedric Titans eventually came to serve other [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), such as [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), who had them summoned to fight mortals for her amusement. [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) has **Ash Titans** in his army. Unlike their blue cousins, Ash Titans have red skin, and breathe regular flames rather than blue cold-fire.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Daedric_Titan), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Daedric Titan), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Daedroth](Lore_Daedroth.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/OBR-creature-Daedroth.jpg/200px-OBR-creature-Daedroth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-creature-Daedroth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-creature-Daedroth.jpg) A Daedroth [Daedroths](Lore_Daedroth.md) are [crocodile](Lore_Crocodile.md) -headed bipedal [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), associated with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). They are a strong, animalistic type of Daedra, but still a dangerous foe with powerful clawed arms and moderate magical ability. Though some have been known to use weapons and wear simple armor, most attack with bare claws. Some are man-sized and spit poison or shock magic, while others tend to be larger and belch firebolts instead. These larger Daedroths initiate battle by shaking themselves, invoking a protective shield.

Linguists will point out that "Daedroth" is also the proper singular form of "Daedra", but for these creatures the common usage is Daedroth (singular) and Daedroths (plural), with Daedra (both singular and plural) being used as the generic term. The more proper, specific name for them is "Daedralings". The term "Lesser Daedra" is sometimes used (again, both singular and plural), though this appellation technically refers to Daedra of all types beneath the Daedric Princes.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daedroth), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Daedroth), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Daedroth), [Oblivion Mobile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/OBMobile:Daedroth), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Daedroth), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Blood-Crazed Daedroth)

## [Death Hopper](Lore_Death_Hopper.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-creature-Death_Hopper.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Death_Hopper.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Death_Hopper.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Death_Hopper.jpg) A Death Hopper [Death Hoppers](Lore_Death_Hopper.md) are giant, carnivorous [frogs](Lore_Frog.md) that can be found in the swamps of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) and the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md). They are typically green, but can also be brown. Death hoppers are generally found in packs. They hunt by lurking beneath the surface of water, waiting for their prey to wander above them. They can swallow large prey whole. Death hoppers are extremely poisonous, and can spit globs of toxic matter over great distances. They are known to spawn in the foul waters of the West Weald: their tadpoles are referred to as nymphs.

Plague Hoppers are a [diseased](Lore_Disease.md) brown variety of Death Hoppers which are born in the [Pits](Lore_Pits.md). They could also be found in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) as a result of [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md)'s infiltration of the realm. A tale about the Viridescent [Dragon Frog](Lore_Dragon_Frog.md) says that the green frogs once grew as large as [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), somewhat similar to Death Hoppers, but envied the Dragons their wings. Upon making a pact with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), they gained their wings but lost their size, becoming dragon frogs.

The [Sul-Xan](Lore_Argonian.md#Tribes) tribe is known to use death hopper vocal sacs when making armor.

- See also: [Bull-Horned Magma Frog](Lore_Bull-Horned_Magma_Frog.md)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Death_Hopper)

## Deer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/OB-creature-Deer.jpg/200px-OB-creature-Deer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Deer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Deer.jpg) A doe Deer are herbivorous mammals seen in the forests of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They are often hunted for their meat and pelts. They are very skittish and will quickly flee potential danger.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> A male deer is called a stag, and a female deer is a doe. Stags have antlers, and does lack them.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> A baby deer is called a fawn.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Vale deer are a species of deer that are only found in the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) and in some areas of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md). In life, these deer have a unique greenish glow in their eyes and hide, but it fades upon death. Other more distinct species of deer include the [elk](Lore_Elk.md), [moose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moose), and [reindeer](Lore_Reindeer.md). [Antelopes](Lore_Antelope.md) are a similar type of animal, but are not deer.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Deer), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Deer.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Deer), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Lowland_Troll), [Skyrim VSE](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Dungeons)

## Dire Wolf

The Blackwood dire wolf carries some features of the extinct long-legged swamp wolf due to possible cross-breeding.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Antiquity WTS-14)</sup>

## [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/ON-creature-Mulaamnir.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Mulaamnir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Mulaamnir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Mulaamnir.jpg) A dragon [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) (also known as **drakes**, **wyrms** or **worms**, **dov** or **dovah** in [their native language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), **drah-gkon** or **dov-rha** to the ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), or **Aka** in [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md)) are a rarely-seen [race](Lore_Race.md) of large [reptilian](Lore_Reptile.md) beasts of [Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) nature. They are rumored to be from [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) (which literally means "Dragon Land"), though there are ancient tales of Dragons also coming from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). They are large, scaled creatures, easily several times larger than a human or elf. They have long, slender extremities, with thin bat-like wings and ridges of spikes along their back. Dragons have three sharp talons and one vestigial digit known as a dewclaw on each of their feet, as evidenced by the way their [alphabet](Lore_Dragon_Alphabet.md) is written. There are several varieties of Dragon that come in a range of colors.

They are regularly said to breathe fire, but more accurately, they speak it. They speak words in their language a certain way, known as a [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) or "Shout", to cause many effects, including elemental breaths such as fire or frost.

Dragons are well-known for their affinity for [magic](Lore_Magic.md). It is unknown how they possess the ability to speak and fly despite their lack of lips or the fact that their wings are not naturally strong enough to support such heavy torsos. As the immortal children of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), they are specially attuned to the flow of time, and they feel an innate urge to dominate others that is near-impossible to overcome. Some sources suggest both dragons and [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) are fragments of Akatosh's soul.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Dragon.md).

Ducks are [waterfowl](Lore_Bird.md) who typically reside in lakes among the reeds.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-AGo S-41)</sup> Their young are known as 'ducklings', and have yellow feet.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Augur-42)</sup> They are common game birds, with their feathers being used for pillows<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Pillow-43)</sup> and, in conjunction with their leather, high-end coats.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Lathdanvir-44)</sup> They are also hunted for food, with duck breast, tongue, and eyes being high class meals in [Imperial cuisine](Lore_Imperial_Cuisine.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TFo SC-27)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Nobleman-45)</sup> One method of hunting involves the usage of duck calls, whistles made to emulate the sounds of ducks.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Call-46)</sup>
