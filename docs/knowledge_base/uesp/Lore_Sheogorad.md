[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/MW-place-Sheogorad.jpg/200px-MW-place-Sheogorad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Sheogorad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Sheogorad.jpg) A typical view of the Sheogorad region The Sheogorad region is a wild maritime province consisting of about 28 islands extending into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) off the northern coast of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWSavant-1)</sup> The high pillar-like menhirs characteristic of the area also result in hundreds of rocks, either just exposed, or lying just below the surface, which makes marine navigation almost impossible.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGeo-2)</sup> The only accessible port, [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md), is on the main island of the archipelago, reached by skirting the region and coming in from the north. The region is largely hostile and uninhabited,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWSavant-1)</sup> and has a more northern climate compared to the rest of the island.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-3)</sup>

## History

### First Era
Ancient [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) maps show that the coastline of Vvardenfell extended much further north during the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when the island was part of the mainland, suggesting Sheogorad was once much larger than its current scope.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KKP-4)</sup>

The settlement of [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md) traces its origins to the [Conquest of Morrowind](Lore_Conquest_of_Morrowind.md) in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). At this time, High King [Vrage the Butcher](Lore_Vrage.md) ruled over the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), and invaded surrounding territories such as Resdaynia and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FC-5)</sup> But when both the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) had driven out the Nords in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416), the Dwarves built their own city on Dagon Fel's ruins, known as Mzuleft. But like many other Dwemer settlements, Mzuleft was later abandoned.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWDagon Fel-6)</sup>

### Second Era
Sometime in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Dagon Fel was a settlement with an active port,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONGravius-7)</sup> known for its [Nix-Oxen](Lore_Nix-Ox.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFNO-8)</sup> Additionally the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) necromancer, a [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) called [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md), is described as having had a wizard's tower in the Sheogorad region, which was seemingly located just to the east of the [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md) village<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Artisa-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Sarnir-10)</sup> —this would have had to have transpired before N'Gasta's settlement (before the year [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862)) and subsequent death (circa [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864)) in [Hammerfell Province](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RGNGasta-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Rescue Iszara-12)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), the terms of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) which restricted Vvardenfell as a Temple reserve were lifted, allowing that subcontinent and its surrounding islands like Sheogorad to be opened up to settlement. Nordic fishermen returned to the area and the village of Dagon Fel. Because of its location, Imperial scholars and adventurers arrived in the area to explore the nearby Daedric and Dwemer ruins, of which the region is riddled.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWDagon Fel-6)</sup> By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Sheogorad was seemingly non-aligned in the political conflicts of Morrowind's [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup> However, the wilds were home to plenty of smugglers,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> bandits,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup> vampires,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> and necromancers.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> There are many ancient Velothi buildings, [Dwemer ruins](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md), and [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) shrines throughout the region that provide ideal lairs for those who seek to skirt the eyes of the law,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGeo-2)</sup> and the perilous nature of the uninhabited islands<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWSavant-1)</sup> serves as a buffer against those who would seek to drive them out. The [Aundae Clan](Lore_Aundae_Clan.md), who seem to have vague ties to [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AGa D-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DGGM-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-21)</sup><sup>:510</sup> are a group of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) lairing in [Ashmelech](Lore_Ashmelech.md) in the southeast part.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MWDA-22)</sup>

## Landmarks
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/ON-place-Ald_Daedroth.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ald_Daedroth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald_Daedroth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald_Daedroth.jpg) Ald Daedroth circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The port town of Dagon Fel is the only real settlement visited by ships in the region.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup> It is a fishing village whose population skews more heavily Nord than most of Vvardenfell. The village was founded by Nords thousands of years ago, and was inhabited by them until the Dunmer drove the Nords from Morrowind.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup> The local ruins extend into the town itself. The main body of the Dwemer ruin of Mzuleft is south of the town, separated by walls and a steep range of hills, but the Dwemer towers and ruins are virtually part of the eastern town and extend to the island to the north.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

On the far south of the main island, also called Sheogorad,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWSavant-1)</sup> is the ancient stronghold [Rotheran](Lore_Rotheran.md). The small [Shuran Island](Lore_Shuran_Island.md) can be found off the south coast of the main island, west of Rotheran. Shuran island lies between Vvardenfell and the island of Sheogorad.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup> The region is home to two very large Daedric shrines: [Ald Daedroth](Lore_Ald_Daedroth.md) to the far east (sometimes considered part of [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md)), and Assurdirapal on a long island in the west of the chain, which also holds the Velothi ruin of [Ald Redaynia](Lore_Ald_Redaynia.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-27)</sup> One of the northern islands due west of Dagon Fel is home to the Sanctus Shrine, the site where Archcanon Saryoni did his meditations and wrote his famous [sermons](Lore_Saryoni%27s_Sermons.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Positioned mid-north to the rest of the region is the Isle of the Daedra,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-29)</sup><sup>:29</sup> which is where a wager was carried between [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) and [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), on if a person in isolation for a century would be able to prevent themselves from going mad through meditation.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-MWAQ-30)</sup>

One of Morrowind Province's major dungeons laid in the westernmost Sheogorad during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ARMDMap-31)</sup>

### Islands

Sheogorad
- The eponymous largest island of the Sheogorad archipelago
Shuran
- Tucked into the southwest corner along the main island west of Rotheran
Sargon
- A long island which stretched from Ald Redaynia to Assurdirapal
Ashmelech
- The isolated home of the Aundae vampire clan
Isle of the Daedra
- An island once home to a priestess of Azura, who remained isolated there for a century as part of a wager between Sheogorath and Azura

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/AR-map-Morrowind_Dungeon_Map.png/200px-AR-map-Morrowind_Dungeon_Map.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Morrowind_Dungeon_Map.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Morrowind_Dungeon_Map.png) *Arena*'s Map of Morrowind, with its dungeons - In *[The Elder Scrolls: Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, the region that would later be named Sheogorad was directly attached as a peninsula to the Black Isle of Vvardenfell.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ARMDMap-31)</sup> On concept art maps made of Vvardenfell for *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*, a water-based region is highlighted around Sheogorad with the same exact shape as the original peninsula from *Arena* can be seen.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-MWVMap-32)</sup> Seemingly the land bridge area between Sheogorad and Vvardenfell from *Arena* was intentionally retconned into a [strait](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait) -style subregion for *Morrowind*. - An in-world character post written from the perspective of [Hasphat Antabolis](Lore_Hasphat_Antabolis.md) may be a reference this dichotomy, chalking up the differences to old maps from the First Era when the topography of Vvardenfell was different and the northern coastline of the island extended much further north.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KKP-4)</sup>
- One of the major dungeons of Morrowind in *Arena* was located on the westernmost tip of of the peninsula, which later became the Sheogorad region of *Morrowind*.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ARMDMap-31)</sup> The names of dungeons in *Arena* were randomized, but had this naming scheme: Catacombs of [Monster], Cavern of [Monster], Cellar of [Monster], Crypt of [Monster], Dungeon of [Monster], Fortress of [Monster], Labyrinth of [Monster], and Mines of [Monster].<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ARNames-33)</sup>
