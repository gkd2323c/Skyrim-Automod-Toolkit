Saint Pelin the Martyr was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) priest of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) who was canonized after sacrificing himself in defense of the [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Garrison.md) in [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029), which in turn, brought [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) into the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOSP-1)</sup> He became a hero to the Bretons of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) and [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md), memorialized by the chapel of St. Pelin in Evermore, the [Order of Saint Pelin](Lore_Order_of_Saint_Pelin.md), the knightly order of the Kingdom of Evermore, and the great cemetery called Pelin Graveyard.

## History

### Blood Benediction
Pelin was born in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) when the world was more untamed and the surrounding region was riddled with [minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md) and [centaurs](Lore_Centaur.md). At some point, Pelin was indoctrinated into the [Temple of Stendarr](Lore_Temple_of_Stendarr.md), at their chapel in the Bangkorai Garrison. He was a beadle and tended to the spiritual needs of the soldiers and the guards, but he would also bring water to the sentries. In 1E 1029, the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md), led by King [Styriche](Lore_Styriche.md) of [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Hammerfell).md) invaded the garrison. As the enemy besieged the stone walls, Pelin ran into the chapel and prayed to the [divines](Lore_Divines.md) and the saints. Not long after, he was tending to the wounded and learned that soldiers lost their blood from [bat-men](Lore_Werebat.md). He ran up the garrison and offered his blood to the horde, who drained him dry. Crippled on the floor, Pelin prayed to Stendarr for an ocean of blood to flow out of him so that the monsters can be swept away. It was at that moment, blood came out of his decrepit body like a great flood, providing the horde a great feast and the soldiers a perfect distraction. The horde was nearly defeated, until the armies of Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) came to finish the fight. Pelin has since been remembered as a lesser-known saint.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOSP-1)</sup>

### Legacy
Saint Pelin's deeds are celebrated in Evermore. Every year at the Bangkorai Garrison, the people host the Red Parade, which has a re-enactment of Pelin's sacrifice.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGHAH1-2)</sup> His portrait is put on display throughout the stronghold,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONContraband Portrait-3)</sup> and even a great tapestry is draped, depicting his heroic sacrifice.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONContraband Tapestry-4)</sup> Some depictions of Saint Pelin portray him praying to [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONContraband Figurine-5)</sup> Some of his holy relics can be found in the St. Pelin Chapel, located in the main square of Evermore. One of the most illusive crimelords of Evermore, Splendid-Paws was said to have interest breaking into the chapel.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FTDSSP-6)</sup> St. Pelin's Clods is a crude gemstone that is carried by the Order of Saint Pelin and even bears their insignia. It is made from Pelin's blood and the soil of Bangkorai.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONClod Anti-7)</sup> Several of them are converted into necklaces, which can be recognized by the Pyre Watch.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGHAH1-2)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), one of the rarest items in circulation was a crystal vial of St. Pelin's blood. Even if it was emptied the blood always [magically](Lore_Blood_Magic.md) comes back.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONContraband Blood-8)</sup>

Pelin's sacrifice is discredited by some as being fanciful.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOGwendi-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SC-10)</sup> The true history of what actually happened may never be known as the true account was lost to history, only fragments of what happened during that time are known to scholars. What is known is the combined might of the Bangkorai Garrison and the Alessian Empire played a pivotal role in the Gray Host's defeat. Magic also played a role in the events that transpired, but its unknown if the magic involved came from actual Divine invention as the stories of Saint Pelin claim, or if it was Alessian Sorcery.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SHelaria-11)</sup> However, Pelin's blood has magical properties as stated above, giving credence to his power.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONContraband Blood-8)</sup>

One of the pieces of the [Tales of Tribute](Lore_Tales_of_Tribute.md), a popular card game originating from the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md) played continent-wide during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md)<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TSOT-12)</sup> bore the image of St Pelin. A deck themed after him and Knights of Saint Pelin was part of the game.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TOT1-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HIP-14)</sup>
