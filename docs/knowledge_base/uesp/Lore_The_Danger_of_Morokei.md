The Danger of Morokei **by [Keldnyr](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Keldnyr)** *A priest of Kyne's notes on his lifelong vigil*
---
The [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) embody a memory from a dark time in [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) history, when proud folk cowered and [knelt](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) before the mighty [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md). They begged for scraps of the Dragons' glory, to the ruin and misery of their own kin.

Know that across [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), many of these Dragon Priests were entombed after the end of the [ancient wars](Lore_Dragon_War.md), but they did not rest easy. Many would not stay dead. Among these was [Morokei](Lore_Morokei.md).

When Morokei lived, he dwelt in the ruin we know as [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md). Once, many lifetimes ago, it was known as the city of [Bromjunaar](Lore_Bromjunaar.md) and was the seat of the Dragon Priests' power. After Morokei's defeat, the full extent of the power gifted to him by the Dragons became clear — he could not be killed save by the [voice](Lore_Thu%27um.md) of a Dragon itself.

I, and other priests of [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), keep the restless priests within their tombs by way of four sacred fires. By keeping these braziers lit, we seal the tomb with warding magic. This is our secret and holy purpose. If any fool seeking the power of Morokei were to intrude upon this place and break the seal, the Dragon Priest's vengeance upon Skyrim would be swift and terrible.

But I grow old, and the vigor of my heart and bones fades with each passing year. One day, soon, I must ask my granddaughter, Yrsild, to take up this mission in my stead. She is devout in the ways of Kyne and will understand the dire importance of this sacred duty.
