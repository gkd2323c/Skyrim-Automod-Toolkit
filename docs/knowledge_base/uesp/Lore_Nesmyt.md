Nesmyt (also spelled **Nezmyt**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup>) was the leader of the [Scenarist Guild](Lore_Scenarist_Guild.md), which he ruled with "an iron hand".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup>

## History

### Third Era
After a rather tortured path to membership, Nesmyt had finally been entered into the ranks of the Scenarist Guild as an acolyte—it was unknown to him, that the guild had existed for hundreds of years before his joining. Nesmyt's first few weeks in the guild were rough, due to higher ranking members bullying him and sneering at his lowly station in the guild. And advancement proved upward proved to be impossible, as Acolyte Nesmyt did not get along with the leader of the guild, [Vychamp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vychamp), and refused to ever pledge fealty to him due to the fact that Vychamp had emasculated Nesmyt's children in a fit of "jealous rage" over Nesymt's helpmate. This series of unfortunate events caused Nezmyt to hallucinate powerful images of decadence, destruction, and wantonness—interrupting any chance of his being able to sleep during this period.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup> However through whatever means Nezmyt had overcome the ordeal with Vychamp, and by the mid-to-late [fourth century](Lore_Third_Era.md#Fourth_Century) Nezmyt had become the master of the Scenarist Guild.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup>

Beginning in [3E 370](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_370), guildmaster Nezmyt created a sect of Imperial scribes charged with creating a second *[Codex Scientia](Lore_Codex_Scientia.md)* (a "Book of Knowledge" designed for heroes, which was first written about by ancient prophets).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TDC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CD-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFCD-5)</sup> After the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) period concluded in the year [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), the book was focused on recording all historical events beginning with the defeat of the usurper [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup> to the conclusion of the [Wars of the Iliac Bay](Lore_Fractious_Wars.md) —and the book became known as *[The Daggerfall Chronicles](Lore_The_Daggerfall_Chronicles.md)* after the events that took place in that [city-state](Lore_Daggerfall.md) during the wars.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:180</sup> However, the book was smuggled out of the Scenarists' citadel by anonymous renegade guild member and was subsequently hidden in a shrine of the [Oracle of Hammerfell](Lore_The_Oracle.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dag Hint-1)</sup> It is not known what ultimately became of the book.

## Notes
- Nesmyt is probably the namesake of [Bruce Nesmith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Bruce_Nesmith), who provided additional material for [*The Daggerfall Chronicles*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles).
