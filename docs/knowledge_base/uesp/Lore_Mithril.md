# Mithril

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/OB-item-Mithril_Armor.jpg/200px-OB-item-Mithril_Armor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Mithril_Armor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Mithril_Armor.jpg) Mithril Armor (Oblivion) *Pacrooti has heard tell of this metal, a material so rare, it no longer even exists! Once smiths in Tamriel could use it to create special arms and armor, but then one day, poof! All gone! Life is indeed strange, no? Is it not humorous to live in a world where even history can change? Even history of future! It makes Pacrooti's head hurt sometimes.*
—Pacrooti<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MAMAPAYQ-1)</sup> Mithril (sometimes spelled **Mythril**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBMD-2)</sup> is an elusive and relatively scarce [metallic material](Lore_Metals.md), known for being light and rather valuable, likely due to its rarity.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KEVII-3)</sup> It comes in two different colors; in places such as [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) it comes in a shiny silver color,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBArmor-4)</sup> while in the provinces of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), it is seen in navy-blue.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFArmor-5)</sup> When broken, it can easily be melded together when heated up.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAR-6)</sup> The only known veins of mithril can be found in the [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md), where the [Kragen Clan](Lore_Kragen.md) harvested the material as one of their main resources, but whether it has been exhausted is unknown.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KEVII-3)</sup> There is another variant of mithril called **Elvish Mithril**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ARElden-7)</sup>

The two distinctions of mithril go way beyond their color and location. In Cyrodiil, it is made into light armor and is considered stronger than typical leather and chainmail, but weaker than [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) (the material of elven armor) and [malachite](Lore_Malachite.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBArmor-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mo Armor-8)</sup> Whereas the navy-blue variant is stronger than [dwarven metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md) and moonstone, but weaker than [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and Orcish Armor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFArmor-5)</sup> Mithril armor is as strong and heavy as [adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md), but is less resilient.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFArmor-5)</sup> The [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) used mithril armor that is as strong as the latter variant, although it is considered medium armor instead.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BSArmor-9)</sup>

In ancient times, mithril was in constant use by smithies across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to craft special arms and armor, but all traces of the metal had reportedly vanished by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MAMAPAYQ-1)</sup> However, it resurfaced some time later, and by the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) it appeared in abundance in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region, in Cyrodiil, and in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBArmor-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFArmor-5)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The [Lord's Mail](Lore_Lord%27s_Mail.md), the plate cuirass of [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md), is known to be partially made of mithril.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFMail-11)</sup>

## Notes
- "Mithril" (or alternatively "Mithriil") is a common name for [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ARNames-12)</sup>
- [Mithril](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mithril) is a fictional metal that originates from [J. R. R. Tolkien](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien)'s [legendarium](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tolkien%27s_legendarium). Tolkien is known for his works such as *[The Hobbit](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hobbit)* and *[The Lord of the Rings](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Lord_of_the_Rings)*. It is a silver metal that is lighter and stronger than typical steel. An example of the material in his writings is the mithril-coat, a sheet of mail that was worn by [Bilbo](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilbo_Baggins) and [Frodo Baggins](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frodo_Baggins) in their adventures.
- Mithril was omitted from *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)* as a material type, which [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) acknowledged as being due to his distaste for it being featured in *[The Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)*.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-13)</sup>
