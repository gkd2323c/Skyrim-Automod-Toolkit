# Multiraciality

*They were always questioning who I was. Was I Imperial? Was I a Redguard? They wanted to put me in a neat little box, but I didn't quite fit.*
—Aeliah Renmus<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Aeliah-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_%28Deluxe%29.png/200px-AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_%28Deluxe%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_(Deluxe).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_(Deluxe).png) [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), who has been described as a "mongrel elf" and is known to have Imperial ancestry Many different mortal and immortal [races](Lore_Races.md) inhabit the realms of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). The mortals of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) can typically be categorized into one of these racial groups. However, **multiracial** people do exist and can exhibit the traits of two or more races. They are sometimes described as **hybrids**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Cot DC-2)</sup> or derogatorily as **half-breeds**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DUG-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Orcs Amogus-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Lot B-5)</sup> and **mongrels**.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBMOP-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRBIX-7)</sup>

One view on [multiracial reproduction](Lore_Sex.md#Racial_Compatibility_and_Reproduction) is set out in *[Notes on Racial Phylogeny](Lore_Notes_on_Racial_Phylogeny.md)*, a text published by the [Imperial University](Lore_Imperial_University.md)'s Council of Healers. Generally, the offspring of such a union will bear the racial traits of the mother, though some traces of the father's race may also be present.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Some take an absolutist stance on this view, stating that all children inherit their mother's race.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Generic MW-9)</sup> Others have stated that if a pair of different human races have children, the offspring will bear features of both races, instead of only the maternal side.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tuttle Posts2021-10)</sup> Children born to the same parents may be of different races.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Galiel-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Fadiya-12)</sup> For more information on racial compatibility, see the article on **[sex](Lore_Sex.md)**.

## Interracial Couples in Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/CT-Events-Baby_Boom_%28Large%29.png/200px-CT-Events-Baby_Boom_%28Large%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-Events-Baby_Boom_(Large).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-Events-Baby_Boom_(Large).png) An interracial couple with their child (Castles) *Many of these milk-drinkers consider it heresy for a Dark Elf to marry one of another race. As if the Three care who we love and live for!*
—Hrogar, a Nord married to a Dunmer<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Hrogar-13)</sup> Interracial relationships are somewhat common, but they may be looked upon poorly in some cultures. Some [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) consider it heresy to marry outside of their race.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Hrogar-13)</sup> The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) consider interracial relationships to be the worst possible offense given their stance on racial purity, and have gone to the extreme to punish those involved in such relationships. For example, an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) named Gurlak was in a relationship with his owner and slave master Nuralanya. When the relationship was found out, Gurlak was bound to a magical circle which extended his [lifespan](Lore_Lifespan.md). He outlived his lover who was also imprisoned nearby and went on to live for thousands of years in this state of imprisonment.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) were responsible for a series of murders against refugees of mixed blood in [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) and [Balfiera](Lore_Balfiera.md) during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lo S-15)</sup><sup>:41</sup> "Half-elf" has been used as an [insult](Lore_Profanity.md) within Altmeri culture against Bosmer, aspersing their height.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Firthaedal-16)</sup> In [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), Human- [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) marriages are generally frowned upon.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> The Bosmer have been recorded in early history as "soiling Time's line" by taking Mannish wives.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TM-18)</sup>

## Akaviri × Tamrielic
- There exists trace [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) ancestry in many distinguished [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) families, who sometimes even possess trace facial features of their ancestors. - The [Rim-Men](Lore_Rim-Men.md) are a group of Imperials who resided in [Hakoshae](Lore_Hakoshae.md) that are known for their [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) ancestry, which dates back to the time of the fading [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TNShrine-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Magnate Feina-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1E-22)</sup>

### Ayleid-Bosmer
- Ayleid refugees fleeing from the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) were known to intermingle with the Bosmer. ## Human × Elf

### Manmer (Aldmer-Nede)
The most famous example of multiraciality are the Bretons of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Historically known as [Manmer](Lore_Manmer.md), the Nedic population of High Rock was forced to interbreed with the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) ([Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) in modern times, but Aldmer back then)<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-MDBMM-91)</sup> of [Clan Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). Over many generations, this resulted in a magically-inclined distinct race.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBMOP-6)</sup> Whether Bretons themselves are humans or elves is a topic of debate,<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-MDBMM-91)</sup> with most people believing that they are the former.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-PGE3A-92)</sup> For more information on the racial history of Bretons, see the **[main article](Lore_Breton.md)**.

- [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/LO-race-Breton.png/118px-LO-race-Breton.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Breton.png) Illustration of a typical Breton

### Altmer-Breton
- The [Dumonte](Lore_Dumonte.md) Breton family was known for its considerable familial connection with the Altmeri Clan Direnni (previously of the Aldmer race).<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-AVAYQ-93)</sup>

### Ayleid-Nede
- During the early [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md), the [Nede](Lore_Nede.md) [Nunex Faleria](Lore_Nunex_Faleria.md) had three children with an unnamed female [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md). Their offspring were described as "Elven children". When their son fathered a child with an unnamed female Imperial, the result was a child with enough elven features for the Falerias to be executed by the Empire.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-TLFF-94)</sup>

### Bosmer-Nord
- [Valindor](Lore_Valindor.md) is a Bosmer who had ancient Nordic ancestry.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Vingalmo-86)</sup>

### Breton-Dunmer
- Emperor [Cassynder Septim](Lore_Cassynder_Septim.md) was the son of Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) (a Dunmer) and Emperor [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_III.md) (a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md)). Although Cassynder was half- [elven](Lore_Elf.md), he aged like a Breton, so by the time he ascended to the throne he was already middle-aged.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-95)</sup> His half-brother, Emperor [Uriel Septim IV](Lore_Uriel_IV.md), was also half-Dunmer half-Breton, being the son of Katariah and [Gallivere Lariat](Lore_Gallivere_Lariat.md).<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-95)</sup>

### Chimer-Nord
- [Mith](Lore_Mith.md) was a half Chimer-half Nord, being born to a Chimer mother and Nordic father, taking on mostly the elven appearance of his mother although he did have his father's red hair.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-KEIV-96)</sup>

## Hybrid Races
The following races have been described as "half-breed" or "hybrid" races, either literally or metaphorically:

- [Harpies](Lore_Harpy.md), being an all-female race, are said to reproduce by kidnapping human males. They are also said to be "deranged half-breed" creatures, but it is unclear how literal this is due to their cursed origins.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DUG-3)</sup><sup>[[102]](#cite_note-Ghraewaj-108)</sup>
- [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md) are a bull-like beastfolk, and it is said the [demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) gave rise to the race through his dalliances with the Nedic Slave-Queen [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and the birth of her son, [Belharza the Man-Bull](Lore_Belharza.md). This origin would paint them as a cross between Nedes and a demigod in [bull](Lore_Bull.md) form.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-On Mino-109)</sup>
- [Proto-Bretons](Lore_Proto-Breton.md) or "Half-Elves" were the first Bretons from the early First Era and were more elven in appearance than their modern counterparts.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-49)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBMOP-6)</sup>
- [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) have been theorized to be the result of interbreeding between [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) and the indigenous bestial tribes of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md).<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-ACo V-110)</sup> The tall, bestial and hunchbacked [Maormer Leviathans](Lore_Maormer_Leviathans.md) are described as "the spawn of some unholy and sorcerous coupling of Sea Elf and [Sea Serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md)".<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-TSE-111)</sup>
- [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) were "bred" by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to be his personal guard, made as a hybrid race from both [Xivilai](Lore_Xivilai.md) and [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md).<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Ot X-112)</sup> However, their creation was the result of a [vestigial](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) hybridization process; Xivilai and Dremora are immortal Daedric beings, and do not reproduce.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-113)</sup>

## Notes
- *[The Elder Scrolls: Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)* does not restrict the creation of babies based on race or gender. Due to game limitations however, offspring can only belong to the race of one parent and will not bear any mixed traits.
- In *The Real Barenziah Part IX*, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) is described by [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) as a "mongrel elf", allegedly being part Wood Elf, part Dark Elf, and part "only the gods know what", with dubious human ancestry. Jagar himself apparently claimed to be born in Valenwood to a Wood Elf mother. Symmachus was supposedly prejudiced against other elves (with Altmer and Bosmer being singled out in the biography as folk Symmachus abhorred for being "effete intellectuals" and "lazy thieves" respectively), instead admiring humans (particularly Bretons) for their intelligence and pragmatism. Tharn's alleged lack of human ancestry according to Symmachus was noted by the author as a perceived flaw rather than indicative of the truth.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRBIX-7)</sup>
