# Third Eye


## Associations and Beliefs

### Almsivi
An account of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s youth before their ascension to godhood speaks of an encounter with [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indoril_Nerevar). In this encounter Vivec, claiming to have "the third-eyes fire", offered to perform a reading of Nerevar's future.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-WMBTM-2)</sup> A common saying in [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) culture under the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) was "Three Hands, three Hearts, three Eyes."<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

### House Dagoth
[House Dagoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dagoth) is heavily associated with the third eye, as they took it as their symbol.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Wound-7)</sup> Their sigil was that of a [beetle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beetle) with three circles on its back. [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) wore a [mask](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mask_of_Dagoth_Ur) after his resurrection that featured a prominent third eye.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWDU-8)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-9)</sup> His seven [Ash Vampire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Vampire) brethren also each possessed a third eye.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AVamp-10)</sup> Dagoth Ur's followers regularly commented on his or their own "discerning eye",<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWDE-11)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWDOK-13)</sup> and were known to have the third eye symbol on their palms.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Wound-7)</sup> [Akulakhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akulakhan) was built with a groove in its forehead that seeped [blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Blight),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Concept-15)</sup> though the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) was said to actually be its third eye.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Concept-15)</sup>

### Magnus
In ancient [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) belief, [Magrus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magrus) was commonly known as the "Third Eye of [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah)", with Azurah having been said to have taken or received one of his [eyes](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md), which she used to make the "[Aether Prism](Lore_Sun.md)".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-16)</sup> In a text authored by Star-Queen Varalias, the fragmentary phrase "until Azura opens the Third Eye" is included in its section on the [Star-Orphan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge) [Sheza-Rana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheza-Rana).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-9C-17)</sup> The "[Eye of Shalidor](Lore_Aurbic_Eye.md)" or "Eye of Magnus" is a symbol sometimes used to represent a third eye. It is worn on circlets and hoods as a third eye,<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Magnus Art-18)</sup> and those who pass the final test during the trials of joining the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) find [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md)'s eye marked across their brow.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Eo SFM-19)</sup>

### Three-Eyed Creatures
Notably, [trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll) are one of the few creatures that possess a third eye, nestled in their forehead.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-20)</sup> [Morachellis Hag-Husband](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morachellis_Hag-Husband), a legendary skald, once posited the idea that a troll's third eye might be attuned to the ethereal realm of [spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spirit) parallel to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), rather than the mundane world. However, it's important to note that his views on the subject were not considered particularly reliable, as he made many other dubious claims.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFG-3)</sup> The third [eyes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahl_Eyeball) of [ice trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahl) are sometimes mummified and repurposed as tough, leathery pouches for carrying small items.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-GEGP-21)</sup>

A cultural leader among the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md), referred to as an "Elder Distended One", was described as having three eyes that emerged upon its belly that each "opened again as a toothless mouth, disgorging [unreadable] that the attendants eagerly consumed."<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

The [Mountain Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md)<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-File G-23)</sup> found in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) are known to have three eyes.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

## Notes
- [Ash Zombies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Zombie) had gaping holes in their foreheads where a third eye otherwise could have been,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AZombie-25)</sup> with this being because their heads are said to have burst from "enlightenment".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Wound-7)</sup> In real-life, the opening of a third eye represents enlightenment.
