Aedra and Daedra *Overview of the basic differences between Gods, Demons, Aedra, and Daedra*
---
The designations of Gods, Demons, [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md), and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), are universally confusing to the layman. They are often used interchangeably.

"Aedra" and "Daedra" are not relative terms. They are [Elvish](Lore_Elvish.md) and exact. [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) is a Daedra both in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). "Aedra" is usually translated as "ancestor," which is as close as [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodilic.md) can come to this Elven concept. "Daedra" means, roughly, "not our ancestors." This distinction was crucial to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), whose fundamental split in ideology is represented in their mythical genealogy.

Aedra are associated with stasis. Daedra represent change.

Aedra created the [mortal world](Lore_Mundus.md) and are bound to the [Earth Bones](Lore_Ehlnofey.md). Daedra, who cannot create, have the power to change.

As part of the divine contract of creation, the Aedra can be killed. Witness [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) and the moons.

The protean Daedra, for whom the rules do not apply, can only be banished.
