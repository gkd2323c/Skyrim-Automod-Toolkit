[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Sutch was a major [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) city located in the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), near the edges of the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) and the border with [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Map-1)</sup> From the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) to the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Sutch was a notable cultural hub noted for its artists, artisans, and performers that attracted visitors seeking artistic and philosophical pursuits.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jt SK-2)</sup> County Sutch extended north towards the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md), an area which is now considered part of County [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md). Sutch was connected to the [Gold Road](Lore_Gold_Road.md) north of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early Map-4)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
Sutch is found on the on the northwestern edges of the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) south of the [Brena](Lore_Brena_River.md), where it meets the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md). In the center of Sutch lies the town square, where a number of large buildings surround the local well, with paths winding out from the center to the rest of the city and its houses. The city is built in a clearing, surrounded by a number of boulders and trees.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO-5)</sup> A number of landmarks could be found nearby in the Second Era, including Black Drake Villa, Hiddenspring Cottage, and the Sutch Gatehouse to the south, the grove of Frontier's Cradle and fort of Varen's Watch to the northwest, [Niryastare](Lore_Niryastare.md) to the north, along with [Linchal Grand Manor](Lore_Fort_Linchal.md) and [Trumbe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trumbe) to the west.

## History
Long before the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), Sutch was home to the wealthy Baron Ignace, whose castle was noted for its tall and unscalable walls. The book *[Thief of Virtue](Lore_Thief_of_Virtue.md)* tells the tale of Ravius Terinus, a master thief of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), who attempted to rob the coin collection of Baron Ignace from the latter's castle in Sutch. Ravius failed to steal the coins, but instead slept with Baroness Veronique in order to hide from the guards. The book states that Sutch was a city in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md); it is unknown if this is an error or if Sutch was once indeed considered part of nearby Hammerfell. The author also believes the Thieves Guild to have been wiped out 450 years prior to the book's writing,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-To V-7)</sup> but as various branches of "thieves' guilds" have always existed and the book's publication date is unknown, it is unclear which guild this was referring to.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LA-8)</sup>

By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), Sutch had become a notable town and trade center.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FGRT-9)</sup> [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) visited Sutch in his youth, sometime between [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546) and [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), for its artistic values.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Jt SK-2)</sup> [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) visited the marketplace of the city around the time of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) and met a hunter who had trapped a [river troll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:River_Troll),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FGRT-9)</sup> and two towers near Sutch could be seen behind [Varen's Wall](Lore_Varen%27s_Wall_(place).md) around this time.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESODB-10)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a gang of vampires led by Balandrus, the Terror of the Weald, attacked the city. Even though Balandrus was killed, the population of Sutch was decimated during this event.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Around this time, a [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md) Mosaic had also been opened just west of Sutch by the Recollection, and an incursion from it was repelled by the West Weald Legion.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MI-12)</sup>

Sea shanties from Sutch were popular among sailors around the time of the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Vivecmeeting-13)</sup> The city was still marked on maps in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Map-1)</sup> and [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), nothing remained of the settlement but the ruins of Fort Sutch Abbey. The fort was home to a band of mercenaries until their ailing warlord, Roderick, was poisoned by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md), although it was made to appear that he had died from his illness.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The fort was attacked by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) when an [Oblivion Gate](Lore_Oblivion_Gate.md) to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) opened outside its walls. The Gate was destroyed by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), with the help of the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

## Notes
- The author [Charonius of Sutch](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Charonius_of_Sutch) presumably hailed from Sutch, sometime before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-16)</sup> [Thendaramur Death-Blossom](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Thendaramur_Death-Blossom), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md)'s Conduit who oversaw dozens of Gauntlets, also came from the city.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-17)</sup>
- The city of Sutch was originally set to appear in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* but was removed before release, as can be seen in the terrain surrounding Fort Sutch, which is unusually flat and sparse compared to the rest of the Gold Coast. A few references to the city as still existing were mistakenly left in the final game as well.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early Map-4)</sup> - As she is the only Divine lacking one in the final game, it's presumed that a Chapel of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) was also meant to be in Sutch, but as it was scrapped before release, her communion site ultimately became a nearby [shrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Shrine_of_Kynareth), making her the only deity without a chapel in *Oblivion*.
- Because the [Imperial Palace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:The_Imperial_Palace) in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* serves as the final dungeon in the game, Palace quests in the [Imperial Province](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Imperial_Province) are inaccessible. However, fourteen random quest dungeon icons are present through a toggle but cannot be accessed. One of these dungeons happens to be located right where Sutch is located south of the Brena River near Hammerfell's border.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ARMap-18)</sup>
