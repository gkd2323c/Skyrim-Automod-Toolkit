## Red Dragon Crown
The [Red Dragon Crown](Lore_Red_Dragon_Crown.md) (also called the **Imperial Diadem**, **Imperial Crown**, and **Cyrodilic Crown**) is the main symbol of office worn by the [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). By the time of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), it was thought to be the main symbol of power in the Empire. However, it is simply a mundane piece of jewelry; in contrast, the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) was also a sacred symbol of the Empire which possessed real power.

## Red Eagle's Fury
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/SR-item-Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.jpg/200px-SR-item-Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.jpg) Red Eagle's Fury [Red Eagle's Fury](Lore_Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.md), also known as **Red Eagle's Bane** was the legendary sword wielded by [Faolan](Lore_Faolan.md) during his defense of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) against the invasion of the Imperial forces of Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) which started in 1E 1028 until his last stand against [Empress Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md)'s forces in 1E 1030.

Red Eagle's Fury was described as magnificent, and powerful flaming blade. According to the tales of the Vateshrans, when it was wielded by Faolan, "no ring or steel could turn his blade." The sword was likened to the [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md) of the [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md), though any direct connection between them is unclear; however, in rare circumstances, Shehai have been known to persist after the bearer's death.

According to the legends, when the Reach is free, his blade should be returned to him that he might rise and lead them again. The legend turned out to be true when Faolan's corpse was presented with the Red Eagle's Fury and the ancient king rose from his grave.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.md)

## Remnant of Argon
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/ON-item-Remnant_of_Argon.jpg/200px-ON-item-Remnant_of_Argon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Remnant_of_Argon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Remnant_of_Argon.jpg) The Remnant of Argon as seen during a vision The [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md) is an artifact created by the Argonian [Root-Whisper Tribe](Lore_Root-Whisper_Tribe.md) in a last ditch effort to save both themselves and their [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) from the invading [Barsaebic](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md). The invading Ayleids had been harvesting the souls of their tribe at an alarming rate, and so the Root-Whisper Hist sacrificed itself to create the Remnant of Argon, an amber artifact that could contain the souls of the entire tribe until the Ayleid incursion had passed. The Barsaebic Ayleids attempted to stop its creation, but were held back long enough for the Remnant to be completed.

Though the Remnant of Argon was meant to only be a temporary measure, it was slowly lost to time. The Ayleid occupation lasted far longer than anticipated thanks to their leader Myndhal's efforts. With the Root-Whisper Hist cut off from the rest of its kind and the Root-Whisper tribe all but dead, there was no clear way for them to pass along the Remnant's location. Eventually, all knowledge of the Remnant of Argon faded into obscurity, only remembered by the Hist and the Argonians of Murkmire as something that invoked immense sadness.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Deep-Root)

## Resonant Sphere
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/ON-quest-Family_Feud.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Family_Feud.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Family_Feud.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Family_Feud.jpg) The Resonant Sphere The [Resonant Sphere](Lore_Resonant_Sphere.md) was a large [brass](Lore_Brass.md) spherical object located in the [Radius](Lore_Radius.md) of the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). It was regarded as one of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s minor marvels, and was known for its [tonal](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) properties.

It was characterized by precise symmetry and flawless geometry. Perfectly round in form, the Resonant Sphere was designed to respond audibly when acted upon by [magic](Lore_Magic.md), causing aural response and emitting characteristic tones. These sounds closely resembled the chimes that echoed through the Brass Fortress, particularly those heard from the central spire during the city's night cycle.

According to Raynor Vanos, a [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) scholar of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), the Sphere's behavior suggested a deeper purpose. He speculated that Sotha Sil may have employed specific sound frequencies as a precise mechanism through which to conduct his divine or arcane workings and processes.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Family_Feud)

## Rimelink
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/BL-artifact-Rimelink.jpg/200px-BL-artifact-Rimelink.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-artifact-Rimelink.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-artifact-Rimelink.jpg) Rimelink [Rimelink](Lore_Rimelink.md) is an enchanted blade made in the style of the known [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) "blood drinker" longswords: the transforming blade known as [Dawnfang and Duskfang](Lore_Dawnfang_and_Duskfang.md) and the vampiric sword named [Bloodthirst](Lore_Bloodthirst.md).

Like these other longswords, its crossguard and pommel are adorned with elegant jewels, its pronged blade resembles the gaping maw of a beast, and it has a snakeskin pattern that envelops the areas around the crossguard, handle, and pommel. Like Dawnfang, it has a golden snakeskin pattern, but it differs from both Dawnfang and Duskfang in that its jewel appears to be infused with a blizzard. Like Duskfang, its attacks freeze the wielders enemy, but Rimelink grants its user mastery over the power of frost, and makes enemy frost attacks laughable against Rimelink's user. The blade amplifies the power of frost attacks, draining away a target's stamina when it strikes, and its power is further magnified when the wielder is in mortal peril.

**Found in:** - [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Rimelink)

## Ring of Hircine
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/SR-item-Ring_of_Hircine.jpg/200px-SR-item-Ring_of_Hircine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_Hircine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ring_of_Hircine.jpg) Ring of Hircine The [Ring of Hircine](Lore_Ring_of_Hircine.md) (also known as **Hircine's Ring**, or **The Hircine Ring**) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). In appearance, it is usually an engraved metallic ring showing the head of a wolf, although it has also been known to appear as a spiked leather buckler. The ring can temporarily give the gift of [Lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) to the wearer, and allows lycanthropes to control their transformations. Lycanthropes who possess the ring are not affected by the moons or by bloodlust, and can change form at will. Hircine punishes those that did not legitimately earn the ring, stripping the ring of its ability to control transformations and instead making transformations unpredictable. For non-lycanthropes, the ring is often useless, although it has been known to extend the wearer's life and allow for transformation into a [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md). The ring is known to affect werewolves and [wereboars](Lore_Wereboar.md), and likely works on other lycanthropes as well.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Hircine%27s_Ring), [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Hircine%27s_Ring), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Ring_of_Hircine.md)

## Ring of Khajiiti
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg/200px-OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg) Ring of Khajiiti The [Ring of Khajiiti](Lore_Ring_of_Khajiiti.md) (sometimes just **Ring of Khajiit** or **Ring of the Khajiiti**) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with two [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), who have both been known to offer the Ring in return for service. It is said that the ring makes the wearer invisible, silent, and quick.

The Ring was originally owned by Mephala, until it was stolen off her arm by [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md), the renowned burglar who later became revered as the Thief God of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Using the Ring's powers, Rajhin became the most successful burglar in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s history. It is even said that Rajhin stole a tattoo from the neck of [Empress Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_I.md) as she slept. According to an account, he utilized the Ring's abilities to pilfer the [Book that Knows](Lore_Oghma_Infinium.md) from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s library beneath the sea. Eventually, after making use of its power too freely, Rajhin was abandoned by the Ring and left exposed to his enemies.

## Ring of the Pale Order
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_the_Pale_Order.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_the_Pale_Order.png) Ring of the Pale Order The [Ring of the Pale Order](Lore_Ring_of_the_Pale_Order.md) is a signet ring once belonging to a prominent member of the now-defunct [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) Knights of the Pale Order, who were notorious for their persecution of the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) during the final stages of their flight to Balfiera, around or after the ninth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[nb 7]](#intnote_Pale)</sup> Its enchantments shape the wearer’s behavior, driving them toward isolation and ritualized violence to draw out the ring’s full power. It is said to radiate a cold surge of energy and a deep, nagging paranoia, invigorating the wearer as they inflict damage in combat. The enchantment, however, weakens in the presence of allies, becoming less potent the more companions accompany the wearer. Consequently, members of the Pale Order were known for keeping their own company, and rumors persist that they and their associates were [vampires](Lore_Vampires.md) or practitioners of dark magics. It was not uncommon to see knights of the Pale Order drenched in blood, their strength growing with each passing battle.

The artifact’s power derives from its intricate design. The band itself is broad, heavy, and golden, forming the ring’s sturdy base. A groove along the interior bears an elegy for all those slain by the wearer in unassisted triumphs, along with the engraving, “Killed at my hand,” reflecting the deeply personal and ritualized nature of violence within the Order. The gallery rail, a style dating to the fifth century of the First Era, carries the inscription, “Vigor through Blood,” suggesting that physical strength, endurance, or even vitality itself was believed to increase through killing. Polished onyx stones flank the ring’s signet, engraved with “Austerity, Autonomy, and Ferocity,” a behavioral mandate that explains why the knights neither [married](Lore_Marriage.md), traveled together, nor maintained close social bonds. The ring thus reinforced isolation and self-surveillance, turning its wearer inward and away from society. Though it is unknown whether the original owner of the Ring of the Pale Order was a vampire, the signet identifies them as a member of [House Aurmine](Lore_House_Aurmine.md), vassals to the despotic king of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), Festrien Spenard I, who is believed to have been a vampire posing as all six rulers of the Spenard dynasty.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Ring_of_the_Pale_Order)

## Rings of Lordship

One of the rings may also sometimes be silver and spiked in appearance, with yellow gemstones.

## Runestone of Malacath
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Runestone_01.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Runestone_01.png) Runestone of Malacath The [Runestone of Malacath](Lore_Runestone_of_Malacath.md) was a runestone named after the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), and said to pulse with dark energy. It was the focus of a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) ritual called the Ritual of Unbinding, said to be capable of severing an entity's ties, mystical and magical, to release them from all links to mortal and immortal realms.

Once invoked, the runestone would channel energy into one of the binding stones used for the ritual. For most beings, completion of the ritual required two binding stones, more powerful beings required three, and especially powerful beings required four. The binding stones were named for Essence, Magicka, Spirit, and Memory. The Runestone could also be invoked to create a key that could than be used on the binding stones to revoke the energies within and break the connection. Though the rite was normally fatal for the bound being, it was possible for a willing sacrifice to offer up their life to energize the key instead through the Ritual of the Proxy, thus energizing the key with their own soul, and allowing for the bound being's survival. If the ritual was interrupted, the severed links could be restored. Conversely, if the binding stones were energized once more, the ritual could resume.

The Ritual of Unbinding was used by the [Drublog Clan](Lore_Drublog_Clan.md) of [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), in an attempt to sever the mystical ties between the [Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) and the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), as part of the plans of the [Hound](Lore_Ulthorn.md). However, the timely intervention of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the sacrifice of a willing [soul](Lore_Souls.md) through the Ritual of the Proxy, allowed for the Silvenar to be released and his links to the Bosmer to be restored.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:The_Prisoner_of_Jathsogur)
