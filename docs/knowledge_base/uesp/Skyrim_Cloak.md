# Cloak

| Cloak | |
| --- | --- |
| School | [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) |
| Type | Offensive |

**For 60 seconds, opponents in melee range take/have <effect>.**

**Cloak** spells are offensive spells belonging to the [Destruction school](Skyrim_Destruction.md) that are cast on the player and inflict negative effects to all nearby enemies.

When cast, an aura appears around the player, unique to the spell being cast. Any enemies who comes into range while a cloak spell is active will suffer its effect. Cloak spells are "smart", they will not damage followers or friendly NPCs, only ones who are hostile to you. You may only have one cloak spell active at any given time, casting a second will cancel the first.

Increasing the magnitude of the spell, via [potions](Skyrim_Fortify_Destruction.md) or dual casting, will increase its range, not the damage or duration.

- ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch), version 3.0.2, addresses this issue. ([details](https://afktrack.afkmods.com/index.php?a=issues&i=19898)) Dual casting will increase duration instead

Cloak spells will remain in effect even if you enter a state that doesn't allow you to cast spells, such as turning into a [Vampire Lord](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md) or going underwater. This can be particularly useful when fighting [slaughterfish](Skyrim_Slaughterfish.md), as you cannot use other attacks while underwater.

One strategy for using cloak spells is to cast it and then simply run in tight circles around your enemies. They may be unable to keep up and get a hit in, and the damage will slowly take them out.

Casting a cloak spell while [summoning spells](Skyrim_Conjure.md) are in effect will despawn your summons.
