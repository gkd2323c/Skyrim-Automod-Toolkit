#### N'Gasta (?b- [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864))
[N'Gasta the Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:N%27Gasta) (or simply **N'gasta**) was a legendary [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) [necromancer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) and a famed servant of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) throughout the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He lived in the old wizard's tower on his [eponymous Isle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Isle_of_N%27Gasta) west of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and terrorized the island with his Soul Snare until he was killed in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) by [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md), who wielded the [Flask of Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flask_of_Lillandril). Clavicus Vile viewed him as "a character of remarkable charm and complexity." N'Gasta is remembered by [necromancers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm) as one of the greats.

#### Lord Naarifin (? – [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175))
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/LG-quest-The_Showdown.jpg/150px-LG-quest-The_Showdown.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Showdown.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Showdown.jpg) Naarifin during the Siege of the Imperial City **Lord Naarifin** was a [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) general who led the assault on [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md). After the success of his initial assault on Cyrodiil, he attacked and captured the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). It was said that he was a [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) worshipper who intended to bring about a prophecy known as "the Culling", the implementation of which involved mass Daedric summoning. He was defeated in battle by either [Emperor Titus Mede II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Emperor_Titus_Mede_II), or, in lesser-known stories, by the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) masquerading as the Emperor. He was then hanged from the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) for thirty-three days. One source claims he was carried away by a winged [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) on the thirty-fourth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESL-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup>

#### Naaslaarum (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Naaslaarum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naaslaarum) was a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name meant "Tooth-Water-Twin" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

Naaslaarum and [Voslaarum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voslaarum) were revered twin dragons, who lived beneath the Frozen Lake within the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md). They were most likely killed at some point and resurrected by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) upon his return, although it is possible that they survived until [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).

At some point, in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), when the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) ventured to the Forgotten Vale, Naaslaarum and Voslaarum were resting beneath the ice of the lake and guarding a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md). They fought against the hero but were ultimately defeated and slain.

Having had his soul devoured by another dovah, Naaslaarum was killed permanently and cannot be raised again.

#### Prince Naemon (? - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Naemon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naemon) was a prince of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) who was the heir to the Throne of Alinor for seven years. Naemon became the heir to the Throne of Alinor following the disappearance of his older sister, [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn), in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573), but when Ayrenn returned in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), she was crowned Queen of Alinor over Naemon. Naemon outwardly took Ayrenn's return well, but in actuality he was resentful. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), during the ratification ceremony at the Orrery of [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root), Naemon betrayed Ayrenn, but the Orrery transformed him into an [Ogrim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogrim), and he was killed by an agent of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).

Following the events in Elden Root, Naemon's devoted advisor and attendant, Vicereeve [Pelidil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelidil), absconded with his body, stole the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), and resurrected the prince as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md). Naemon then took command of an undead army and attempted to corrupt the [Heart of Valenwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Valenwood) by using the Staff of Magnus, but his lich was ultimately destroyed by the Aldmeri Dominion in the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin of [Hectahame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hectahame).

After his death, Naemon's [soul](Lore_Soul.md) was claimed by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and imprisoned in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) before he was later resurrected once again, this time by the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult) on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md).

#### Witch Naenra Waerr (fl. before 2E 582)
**Witch** [Naenra Waerr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naenra_Waerr) was an ancient and legendary [witch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witches) and [crafter](Lore_Smithing.md). She was a legendary figure within [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) legends and traditions, and was responsible for the creation, or, according to other sources, [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) of [Umbra](Lore_Umbra.md), a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) designed with the sole purpose of the entrapment of souls. It was created at the request of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), who wanted the sword as a tool of mischief which would send him souls from the mortal realm.

According to some versions of the tale Naenra was believed to be [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) in disguise.

#### Nahfahlaar (?b - [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864))
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/ON-creature-Nahfahlaar_02.jpg/150px-ON-creature-Nahfahlaar_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nahfahlaar_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nahfahlaar_02.jpg) Nahfahlaar on the floating island of Dragonhold [Nahfahlaar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nahfahlaar) (meaning *Fury-For-Water* in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md)), also known as **Nafaalilargus** (sometimes spelled **Nafalilargus** or **N'falilaargas**), was a red [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) who often allied with mortals for his own protection. He acknowledged that dragons are bound by Fenjuntiid, the will of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), meaning that it is in a dragon's nature to pursue domination. However, he was willing to ally himself with select mortals in order to bide time and ensure his presence for events that may require his intervention. In more ancient times, he allied with the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) and Pride of Alkosh to defeat his archenemy, [Laatvulon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laatvulon). He took a liking to [Ja'darri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ja%27darri), a member of the Pride, being remembered in the legends of the moon priests as the **Red Beast**. He would later go on to serve as a powerful soldier of the Empire under Tiber Septim, before his physical body was eventually killed at the hands of [Cyrus the Restless](Lore_Cyrus.md). Due to the immortal nature of dragon [souls](Lore_Souls.md#Dragon_Souls), Nahfahlaar isn't truly dead and could be raised again.

Nahfahlaar repeatedly allied himself with mortal protectors, which had prevented the Dragonguard from eliminating him. The earliest recorded alliance was with King [Casimir II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Casimir_II) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), which the Dragonguard successfully ended in [2E 369](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_369).

#### Nahagliiv (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Nahagliiv](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nahagliiv) is a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Fury Burn Wither" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

Nahagliiv was among the dragons to die during the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) era, with his remains being gathered by the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) and buried in a dragon mound west of [Rorikstead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rorikstead). By [2E 373](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_373), local tales still named him as the dragon buried near the town, and the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) recorded him in their atlas of dragons as being deceased by report.

Upon his return, Alduin began the process of resurrecting the many dead dragons buried throughout [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in ancient times by the Dragon Cult. Nahagliiv made a reappearance approximately before the reformed Blades unsealed [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md), and attempted to terrorize the town of Rorikstead. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) slew Nahagliiv and absorbed his soul. Having had his soul devoured by another dovah, Nahagliiv was killed permanently and cannot be raised again.

#### Tree-Minder Na-Kesh (?b - ([2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Na-Kesh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Na-Kesh) was the Tree-Minder of the [Xit-Xaht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xit-Xaht) tribe and a skilled [alchemist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy). She was obsessed with returning [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) to the pre- [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) way of life, and made use of her mad [Hist](Lore_Hist.md)'s tainted [sap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hist_Sap) to this end.

The psychotic Hist of Mazzatun, [Tsono-Xuhil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tsono-Xuhil), does not possess ordinary sap. It instead weeps an ominous substance known colloquially as [amber plasm](Lore_Amber_Plasm.md) from its bark, which drives those who ingest or bathe in it mad. Na-Kesh performed cruel alchemical experiments with the amber plasm, and it was through these experiments that she was able to harness the power of the substance. She manipulated the plasm in just the right way to enhance a Saxhleel's speed and strength, and did so to herself.

She planned on distributing a brew made of the mad Hist's sap to the whole Xit-Xaht tribe so they could ascend, conquer Black Marsh, and eventually take all of Tamriel. She was defeated in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted), just after the Xit-Xaht raided the village of the nearby Su-Zahleel tribe and enslaved its inhabitants.

#### Mage Naleldil‎‎ (fl. before 2E 582)
**Mage** [Naleldil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naleldil) was a [mycoturge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mycoturgy), and a renowned master of arcane husbandry who created unique and exotic mounts and pets infused with fungal spores known as the [Gloomspore creatures](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gloomspore_Creature). Her creations were sought after by scholars, adventurers, and anyone in need of a reliable and sturdy companion. Using her extensive knowledge of [alchemy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy) and mycoturgy, Mage Naleldil crafted a range of mounts with distinct features and properties. She integrated research left behind by [Ahzidal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ahzidal), a grand [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md) in her work.

#### King Nantharion Rayn (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Nantharion Rayn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nantharion) of [Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silvenar) was the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) king of Vashabar. #### King Narilmor (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
King [Narilmor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Narilmor) was the ruler of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city of [Garlas Malatar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Garlas_Malatar). Under his rule, his city was regarded as the beacon of the west within the Ayleid Empire. In life, he was highly devoted to the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia). He converted his city to her veneration, and even went as far as giving up his will to her to become an immortal [Purified](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Purified) so that he could serve her for all eternity. Narilmor was considered a favorite of Meridia, second only to [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril_the_Unfeathered).

#### Countess Narina Carvain (fl. 3E 433)
Member of the [Carvain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Carvain) family and [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) ruler of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). The [Nibenean Heartlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands) had a reputation for being a devout chapel goer, a respected ruler, and a cunning and ruthless negotiator, though she was also known for sharp-dealing and treachery.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GTB-5)</sup> Not that she wasn't popular; her people seemed to think she was tough, cold, and cranky, but also believed that Bruma was in good hands. She was a supporter of High Chancellor [Ocato](Lore_Ocato.md) during the Crisis. She was also something of a scholar and an avid collector of [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) artifacts.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Oblivion-6)</sup> The Countess funded an expedition which rediscovered the Akaviri fort at [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), which had been covered by a rockslide.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PPD-7)</sup> Together with the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) and [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), she orchestrated the Battle of Bruma, one of the most epic and crucial victories against the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) during the Crisis.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Oblivion-6)</sup>

#### Archmagister Nelos Otheri (fl. 2E 582)
[Nelos Otheri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nelos_Otheri) was Archmagister of the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) chapter of the [Telvanni Council](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), based in [Sadrith Mora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sadrith_Mora). He was also renowned for his mastery of [mycoturgy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mycoturgy), and served as a patron to a group of esteemed mycoturges acknowledged as the finest in the entire [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In addition, he extended his support to Magister [Varkenel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varkenel)'s endeavors in experimenting with the creation of new animal species, particularly the [Vvardvarks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vvardvark). As the leader of House Telvanni, he was the one who officially rejected the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).

#### Master Neloth (fl. 3E 427 - 4E 201)
[Master Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) is a Mage Lord and Councilor of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), he was Master of [Sadrith Mora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sadrith_Mora), and resided there in the tower of [Tel Naga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tel_Naga) where his Mouth was Arara Uvulas. Neloth is at least seven hundred and seventy five years old. He sported a collection of rare treasures, which at one point included [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md), although these were often stolen by other Telvanni. He also enjoyed kidnapping the daughters of [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) Councilors, an interest he shared with another prominent Telvanni, [Divayth Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divayth_Fyr). A gifted enchanter, he studied and collected [staffs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azra%27s_Staffs) made by [Azra Nightwielder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azra_Nightwielder) and showed a fondness for [canis root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Canis_Root) tea. Along with the other Telvanni councilors, he recognized the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) as Hortator.

Neloth has difficulty with the concept that other people matter, and judges their health and well-being solely based on his personal comfort and needs. Despite his stubbornness, he is no fool, as he recognizes power and that power in others must be respected.

#### Kinlord Nemfarion (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
Founder and ruler of [Corgrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corgrad_Wastes) on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) at the time of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) c. He drowned the city in an effort to stop the disease from spreading, then used blood sacrifices from the city's survivors to prolong his and his family's lives by magical means in a desiccated condition while he searched for a permanent cure. To these survivors and their descendants, who lived in caverns underground, Nemfarion and his kin became known as "the Saviors", since his blood magic suppressed the symptoms of the Plague. He attempted to claim the life of a distant descendant of his own clan named Faidur as part of a ritual to revive himself fully, but his servants were defeated by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and Nemfarion himself crumbled into dust when the ritual was broken.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESONemfarion-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-WAQuest-11)</sup>

#### Hortator Indoril Nerevar (?b - c. [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700))
[Lord Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar), also called **Saint Nerevar Moon-and-Star**, the **Champion of Azura**, the **Hero of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md)**, and **Godkiller**, was the **[Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) King** of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) (modern-day [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)). As the Hortator, or Great Ashkhan, of the united Chimer people, he forged an alliance with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), and he was one of the founders of the [First Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Council), uniting all the people of Resdayn for the first time in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416). Nerevar then took on the title of "First Councilor". The alliance eventually broke down, leading to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md). At the climactic [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), Nerevar led the Chimer to victory, though he died shortly after the battle under disputed circumstances.

Under the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), he was known as the **Herald of the [Triune](Lore_Tribunal.md) Way**. [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) [prophecies](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) of his "rebirth" as the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) and heroic return to Morrowind fueled the creation of the [Nerevarine Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevarine_Cult). St. **Nerevar the Captain**, patron of Warriors and Statesmen, became one of the foremost saints of the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith, along with his wife, [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) the Warden, Lord [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) the Magus, Lord [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) the Poet, and Saint [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) the Pilgrim. Nerevar became the patron saint of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md).

#### Nerevarine (fl. 3E 427)
The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), the prophesied **Incarnate**, is the alleged<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> [reincarnation](Lore_Reincarnation.md) of [Chimeri](Lore_Chimer.md) warlord [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar). The Nerevarine arrived in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) by the order of [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), having been sent to [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) as a prisoner of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). Their arrival was directly linked to [a prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) which dictated that Lord Indoril Nerevar would be reincarnated and return to Morrowind to defeat [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur_(god).md) and restore the glory of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md). The [hero](Lore_Hero.md) fulfilled the prophecy, ending [the Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight). In recognition of this, they were also known as **Nerevar-Born-Again**, **Nerevar Reborn**, **Starkborn**, **Moon-and-Star Reborn**, **Hortator**, **Mourner of the Tribe Unmourned**, **Redeemer of the False Gods**, and **Blodskaal**.

As destiny unfolded, they battled against [living gods](Lore_Tribunal.md), and then, in the frozen wastes of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), against the champions of the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). Rumors swirled at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) that the Nerevarine went on an expedition to [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) and has not been heard from since.

#### The Netch
[The Netch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Netch), also known more specifically as the **Luminary Netch**, was one of the [Luminaries of Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Luminary), mysterious immortal beings of pure [magic](Lore_Magic.md) likened to beams of sunlight from [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus) himself. Its chosen form was that of a [Netch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Netch). The Netch was younger than either the [Luminary Gryphon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Gryphon) or the [Luminary Indrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Indrik), and possessed a somewhat unusual nature, but was considered to be just as powerful as the other Luminaries. It played an important role in the life of [Archmage Ulfsild the Evergreen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulfsild_the_Evergreen), collaborating with her alongside the other Luminaries to develop the system of Scribing, a "metamagical theoryset" that was the precursor to the modern [spellcrafting](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spellcrafting) system.

As with all Luminaries, the Netch was said to possess a mastery of magic that made the average archmage look like a neophyte. It was not particularly fond of mages, and would only lend them its power if its challenges were completed. As beings of pure magic, the Luminaries were said to have understood magic in a way mortals never could and so, their power, freely given, allowed one to access arcane mysteries most could only dream of. Indeed, Scribing is said to have been somehow destined to channel the power of the Luminaries from the start.

#### Countess Nevenia Caro (fl. 2E 569 - 582)
[Nevenia Caro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nevenia_Caro) was the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) Countess of Leyawiin in the Province of Blackwood during the turbulent times of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Although she was officially the leader of the region, she largely relinquished her practical authority to the Chamber of Legates.

#### Never-Gives-Up (fl. 2E 582 or earlier)
[Never-Gives-Up](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Never-Gives-Up) was an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) war hero of some renown, who perished at some point before or during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

Little is known about Never-Gives-Up's life, but as of 2E 582 several mounted, [mummified](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mummy) Argonian tails with attached engraved plaques could be found all across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Engravings on each of those plaques claimed that the tail they were attached to was real and once belonged to this war hero. They were among various oddities sought by the collectors.

#### Nesmyt (fl. 3E 370)
[Nesmyt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nesmyt) (also spelled **Nezmyt**) was the leader of the [Scenarist Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scenarist_Guild), which he ruled with "an iron hand".

#### Mane Nhad-hatta (fl. 2E 864)
[Nhad-hatta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nhad-hatta), also known as **the Cat Lord**, was the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) around [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), during [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s conquest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). By the time Tiber Septim entered the fray to claim the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), the [Rim-Men](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rim-Men) returned to Elsweyr, where they paid tribute to the Mane for protection.

He and his tribe resided in [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), where they lived in luxurious palaces built from massive timbers of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) oak. He was often seen in day-long meditations atop his palanquin, held up by his inexhaustible [Cathay-raht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cathay-raht) servants. He was known to refuse an Imperial diplomat a meeting at his private palace even though he had been promised an audience. The envoy was cast out from the area by one of the Warrior Guards because humans had been traditionally forbidden to trespass on these estates.

During his rule, his province potentially made a treaty with the [Second Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).

#### Night Mother (?b - ?d)
At the head of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) hierarchy is the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md), an immortal spirit who is revered and worshipped by the Brotherhood. She is only ever met face-to-face in very exceptional circumstances and is seen as a phantom-like form. When a person prays for the death of another, the Night Mother hears and passes on the details of the prayer to her Listener. At this point, the Listener informs a Speaker, who then goes to meet the hiring party in order to draw up a contract. According to Dark Brotherhood beliefs, she is the wife of [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis) and bore five children.

It is unclear who the "true" Night Mother is exactly, as there have been several women known to have used the title. In the book *[Fire and Darkness: The Brotherhoods of Death](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fire_and_Darkness)*, the Night Mother is identified as the Daedra Prince [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). In another book entitled *[Sacred Witness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sacred_Witness)*, the Night Mother is identified as a former [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) member. And in the well known [2920](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era) series of books she is seen as head of the Morag Tong, because the Dark Brotherhood only split well into the Second Era. Another woman claimed to be the Night Mother is likely to have lived during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md): although the woman represented by [the statue in Bravil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Statues_Other_Deities#Night_Mother) lived in the First Era, which suggests the two are not the same person.

#### Nikulas (fl. 2E)
[Nikulas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nikulas) was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior from the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He and four companions embarked on a quest to find [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md). However, Nikulas, [Erlendr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Erlendr), and [Hunroor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hunroor) were betrayed by the wizard [Grimkell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grimkell), who turned them into stone pillars at Brodir Grove on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). They were avenged by [Ulfgar the Unending](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulfgar_the_Unending), who killed the wizard. All of the friends eventually found their way to Sovngarde.

#### Nilene Devierin (fl. 2E 582)
**Lady** [Nilene Devierin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nilene_Devierin) of [Stormhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhaven), (also spelled **Devieren**), was a diplomat of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), serving High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) as the **Royal Envoy to the Reach**. She also authored reports to High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), detailing her activities in the court of Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) and providing a description of Ard Caddach himself.

#### Flower King Nilichi (fl. early 1E)
[Flower King Nilichi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nilichi) was one of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) kings active prior to or during the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). He was responsible for the eradication of a [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) tribe, the [Men-of-ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Men-of-ge), through a great sacrifice to an [Insect God](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Insect_God) whose name was lost to history.

He is considered an infamous Ayleid King, compared to figures such as [Fire King Hadhuul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hadhuul), [Haromir of Copper and Tea](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haromir), [Gordhaur the Shaper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gordhaur_the_Shaper), and [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril).

#### Chief Noldan (fl. c. 1E 1030)
**Chief** [Noldan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Noldan) was a [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) clan leader during the [Empress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tamrielic_Emperors) [Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra)'s conquest of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) in circa [1E 1030](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1030). At that time, during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the region of the Reach was ruled by ten Reach kings. When Hestra's forces were taking over the region they eventually encountered Noldan's clan. According to the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) accounts, he eventually surrendered and submitted to the forces of the Empire. In a cunning act of treachery he gifted the commander of the Imperial forces his flint spear purportedly dipped in blood. It turned out that the spear was in fact covered in lethal and very potent poison rather than blood. Those who handled the item before the deception was discovered suffered miserable deaths.

#### Nolovan (fl. 2E 582)
[Nolovan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nolovan) was a renowned [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) craftsman and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [metal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Metal) dealer active during the times of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He possessed considerable expertise in Deep Folk and their machinery, establishing him as one of the most knowledgeable individuals on the subject in the city of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) during his times.

#### Queen Nulfaga (3E 336 - ?d)
[Queen Nulfaga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nulfaga) (later titled **Dowager Queen**, **Queen Mother**, and variations thereof, also known as the **Mad Witch of the Wrothgarian Mountains**) was the wife of King [Arslan II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arslan_II) and mother of King [Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus), both of the Kingdom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md). Nulfaga was a renowned mystic, and kept a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) as her [familiar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Familiar), [Skakmat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skakmat). In [3E 401](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_401), Nulfaga located the [Mantella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantella), the heart of the ancient [golem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem) [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), in her astral travels.

During the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) in [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403), Nulfaga foresaw her son's doom and withdrew to her castle, [Shedungent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shedungent), in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains). When word came that Lysandus had died during the [Battle of Cryngaine Field](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Cryngaine_Field), slain by an arrow to the throat, the news drove her mad with grief. In reality, King Lysandus had been assassinated before the battle, and when his ghost began haunting the streets of [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) at night, Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) sent an [agent](Lore_Hero.md#The_Agent) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) to investigate. This agent found Nulfaga alive (but still mad). Nulfaga regained her senses after the agent unmasked and took down Lysandus' murderer, allowing his spirit to rest at last. Nulfaga later sent the agent to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) to retrieve the Mantella, an act which led to the phenomenon known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).

#### Queen Numara (?b - 2E 576)
[Numara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Numara) was the Queen of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), the northern kingdom of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). She was a [Moon-Singer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon-Singers). She was an owner of the amulet of [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anequina_Sharp-Tongue), which served as the key of the Moon-Gate, and Numara was possibly her direct descendant. During her queenship, the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) and [Varen's Rebellion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen%27s_Rebellion) took place.

#### Numinex (?b - ?d)
[Numinex](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Numinex) was a fearsome [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) famous for his battle with [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), which occurred some time before [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) when Olaf became High King and ended the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md).

#### Lord Nunex Faleria (fl. c. 1E 361)
[Nunex Faleria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nunex_Faleria) was a [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) lord and member of the regency council that oversaw the Kingdom of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) circa [1E 361](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_361).

#### Nurarion the Perfect (fl. Merethic Era)
[Nurarion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nurarion) **the Perfect**, also known as **the Voiceless** was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) legendary hero and a member of the [Sunhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunhold) royal family and of [Khunzar-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khunzar-ri)'s Kra'jun, who made a deal with [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) for [great powers](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to fight off [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) invasion led by King [Orgnum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgnum).

#### Queen Nurnhilde (?b - 2E 572)
Daughter of Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). She was the elder sister of twins [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) and [Fildgor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fildgor), and thus fated to take the throne.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TBW-12)</sup> During the [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) Invasion of [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), Queen Mabjaarn went down defending Windhelm, so Nurnhilde briefly assumed rulership before she was also killed during the invasion.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SIR-13)</sup> For this, she earned the title "The Brief Queen". She was succeeded by Jorunn,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TBW-12)</sup> and buried in the catacombs of Fort [Morvunskar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morvunskar) with the [Crown of Freydis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown_of_Freydis).

During the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Fildgor stole Nurnhilde's body along with the Crown of Freydis. He planned to summon her soul and fuse it with his, forcing the crown to recognize him as the rightful ruler of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Her spirit appeared to warn soldiers of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) of her brother's plans.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESO-14)</sup>

#### Nuulion (fl. 2E 5th - 7th centuries)
[Nuulion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nuulion) was a master [smith](Lore_Smithing.md) of the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) of [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) origins, and a known keeper of the secret of the arts of crafting Elven armor from [moonstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonstone) and [quicksilver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quicksilver). Little else is known about him, but he was active between the [fifth century](Lore_Second_Era.md#Fifth_Century) and the [seventh century](Lore_Second_Era.md#Seventh_Century) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Although some sources claim that slavery (of other races than [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md)) was illegal in [Summerset](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Summerset) as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Nuulion was a known slave owner, both before and after that time. One of his slaves, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named Ulvul Llaren, worked the bellows for him for three centuries.

Ulvul managed to escape his captivity and returned to mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) at some point during the seventh century of the Second Era. To punish his previous, cruel owner, he revealed all the secrets of the Altmer armor and weapon smithing he knew to the world, such as the key information regarding moonstone as a key ingredient, and the instruction specyfing that salt water must be used to quench the hot metal.

#### Forge-Wife Nuzava (fl. before 2E 582)
**Forge-Wife** [Nuzava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nuzava) was a highly respected [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) [smith](Lore_Smithing.md). She played a significant role in the [Morkul Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morkul_Clan) as one of its prominent members, responsible for establishing their distinct style of crafting arms and armor. Nuzava was one of the most revered metalworkers of her time.

#### Poet-Champion Nynelle Dumaris (fl. 2E 400)
**Poet of Galen** [Nynelle Dumaris](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nynelle_Dumaris) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) poet active during the times of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md). She was the author of numerous poems, including famous sonnets *[A Lissome Sprite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_Lissome_Sprite)* and *[Fearless as the Sun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fearless_as_the_Sun)*. She was the victor of the poet tournament held in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and was acknowledged by the Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak) in [2E 400](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_400) who named her the **Poet-Champion**.
