## Ul'vor Kus
**Ul'vor Kus** (or **Ul'vor-Kus**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-K.27tora-1)</sup> is the undersea kingdom ruled by the [Sea Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Sload). Very little is known about the mysterious kingdom but it is believed to be located somewhere in the [Sea of Pearls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_of_Pearls).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMot SL-2)</sup> As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) it was regarded as an ancient place by the Ritemaster of the Psijic Order [Iachesis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iachesis).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Iachesis-3)</sup> His successor [Valsirenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valsirenn) specified it is very distant from [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Valsirenn-4)</sup>

The [Ul'Vor Staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ul%27Vor_Staff) is a mysterious magical staff associated with the kingdom.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONBM-5)</sup> [Haj Mota](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haj_Mota) Reefhammer was known as the bane of Ul'vor-Kus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-K.27tora-1)</sup>

## Ularradallaku
[Ularradallaku](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ularradallaku) (also known as **Ularra**) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) ruin located on the slopes of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) in the island district of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), situated in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In its time, Ularradallaku was a temple dedicated to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), the Daedric Prince of Revolution and Destruction. It also contained a statue to [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), the Huntsman Prince. It subsequently fell into ruin. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Ularradallaku was used by the [Circle of Champions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Circle_of_Champions) as a site for bloodsport. Fighters from all three sides of the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) came to the Ularra battleground to participate in the games held by the gladiators.

Eventually, the ruins once again fell into disuse. The [Ordinators](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordinators) were charged with keeping cultists from restoring old Daedric shrines such as Ularradallaku, as worship of the [Bad Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bad_Daedra) was forbidden by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md). However, by [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) the Ordinators had begun to neglect this duty, as they were occupied with suppressing of [dissidents](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dissident_Priests). With the Ordinators' attention turned elsewhere, worship at many of these temples was revived. Ularradallaku was one such site, being reclaimed by worshippers of Mehrunes Dagon. That same year, the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) attempted to gain a foothold within the island of Vvardenfell, and negotiated with Mehrunes Dagon's cultists. The cultists promised the Brotherhood shelter in Dagon's shrines across Vvardenfell. Eno Hlaalu, grandmaster of the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong), took notice and ordered an assassin to wipe out the Brotherhood's strongholds. One of Ularra's worshippers wielded the Glove of Sanguine Safekeeping, one of the [Threads of the Webspinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Threads_of_the_Webspinner) considered sacred by the Morag Tong. The gloves were reclaimed by an agent of the Tong that same year.

## Ultherus Swamp
[Ultherus Swamp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ultherus_Swamp), also known as **Utherus Swamp** is a region in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Little is known about this region of Argonia. The beautiful, but deadly [Dragon's-Tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon%27s_Tongue_Plant) plants are especially common around the area of the swamp.

## Umbriel
[Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) was a floating island contained within a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). It was split from the [Fields of Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fields_of_Regret), [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s realm, by [Umbra](Lore_Umbra.md).

It had buildings jutting from rock and castles hanging from cables, and towers on the rim of the island that appeared to be made of spun glass.

## Underpall Cave
[Underpall Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Underpall_Cave) is a natural cave network found along the [Orange Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orange_Road) between [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) and [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), in the Autumn Range region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Great Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Forest). It contains a subterranean fort named Underpall Keep, as well as a large underground lake in a cavern known as the Reflecting Chamber.

Like all natural caves, the tunnels that make up Underpall Cave are said to have once served as the capillaries of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), pulsing with the divine essence of the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra). In [2E 512](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_512), a tunnel collapse sealed away the ruined fortifications of Underpall Keep, preventing any miner from re-entering that section for at least several decades. The keep was still buried circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when the cave network was taken over by a group of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) known as the Bloodborn. Led by Raelynne Ashham, the Bloodborn took control of Underpall in order to raise an army of vampires and undead. Her brother, Gaston Ashham, was simultaneously raising his own in the ruins of [Lipsand Tarn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lipsand_Tarn) some distance to the west, and the two planned to meet in the Twilight Woods before sweeping all of northern Cyrodiil with their combined armies. A [nereid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nereid) known as Emelin the Returned also appeared in Underpall at this time. Both Raelynne and Emelin were ultimately killed by passing [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) soldiers.

By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the ruins of Underpall Keep had again become accessible, and the cave was used as a family tomb by the [Vlindrels](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vlindrel), an old and powerful [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) family. It became the final resting place of Lord Berich Vlindrel, a tyrannical ruler and ex- [Knight of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Nine). Lord Vlindrel was buried with the [Greaves of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greaves_of_the_Crusader) and the [Sword of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword_of_the_Crusader), two legendary relics of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) which Lord Vlindrel had stolen from his former order when he went to fight in the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) circa [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120). However, the evil which had overcome Lord Vlindrel in life ensnared his [soul](Lore_Soul.md) in death, and his restless [ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) began to haunt the Orange Road, wielding the corrupted Sword of the Crusader which had been dragged down into evil along with him. This curse prevented him from joining his fellow Knights of the Nine in their eternal ghostly vigil at the [Priory of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Priory_of_the_Nine).

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), heeding the call of the Prophet of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), a knight named [Sir Roderic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Roderic) performed a Pilgrimage to the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines) and received a troubling vision from the gods, instructing him to put Lord Vlindrel's spirit to rest and recover the Relics of the Crusader from his tomb. Sir Roderic traveled all over [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) asking about the Vlindrels, and eventually located their family crypt in Underpall Cave. Along with his faithful squire Lathon, Sir Roderic entered the cave complex, which was crawling with undead, vampires, and necromancers. He confronted Lord Vlindrel's spirit and was struck down, but Lathon managed to flee with the Greaves. He brought them to the Priory of the Nine, the headquarters of the reformed Knights of the Nine led by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md). The Hero then traveled to Underpall Cave to recover the Sword of the Crusader and reconsecrate it at the Great Chapel of [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) in [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal). By restoring the weapon to its holy purpose, the Hero redeemed Lord Vlindrel's soul, allowing him to join his fellow Knights in undeath and once again serve the Nine for all eternity as Sir Berich. After hundreds of years, the spirit of Lord Vlindrel would no longer haunt the Orange Road at night.

## Urshilaku Camp
The [Urshilaku Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Urshilaku_Tribe) is one of the four distinct tribes of the [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlander) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The Urshilaku are known as lore-keepers and mystics, they are highly-respected, and are in fact, the second-most populated tribe in the region. They commonly live along the northern coast, in the **Urshilaku Camp** where the [West Gash](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:West_Gash) meets the [Ashlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlands). The Urshilaku Tribe are staunch believers in that the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) would return to unite the people. A sub-sect of the Urshilaku, known as the [Nerevarine Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevarine_Cult) are the ones that enforce this belief and worshipped [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indoril_Nerevar). The Urshilaku Tribe are known to keep [Guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guar) bones, because it is believed that the soul of the guar lingers in the bones and that they can continue to guide them, even after death. The **Urshilaku Burial Caverns** are found just east of [Bthungthumz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthungthumz).

## Urvaius
[Urvaius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Urvaius) is a county in south-central [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) region. It has existed since at least the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). After the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), it became a vassal of the [Kingdom of Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md).

It is bordered by [Ykalon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ykalon) and [Phrygias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phrygias) to the north, [Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen) to the east, [Anticlere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anticlere) to the south, and [Daenia](Lore_Daenia_(duchy).md) to the west. Its capital is the city of Urvaius. The [Thrafey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thrafey) clan is the predominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline, and the regional deity is [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md).

## Ustengrav
[Ustengrav](Lore_Ustengrav.md) is an ancient Nordic barrow found in the wilderlands of [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Located on the northeast edge of the [Drajkmyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drajkmyr_Marsh) marshlands, it is the site of a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md), and the burial place of [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md), the founder of the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) way back in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).
