# Music


## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-item-Shrine_to_Jephre.jpg/200px-ON-item-Shrine_to_Jephre.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shrine_to_Jephre.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shrine_to_Jephre.jpg) Shrine to Jephre resembling a musical instrument
### Origin
The god [Jephre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jephre) the Singer is credited with inventing the music of nature, teaching birds to sing songs and streams of their tinkling tune. Jephre is credited with inspiring the first great ballads of the elves.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Of J-2)</sup>

The anthology of tales known as the *[Myths of Sheogorath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Myths_of_Sheogorath)* has a story dubbed "Sheogorath Invents Music", which instead tells of the mad god inventing music. A young woman he encountered commented on how beautiful the birds' songs were. The Mad God agreed, but noted that mortals could not do the same. And so Sheogorath invented the first musical instruments from the body parts of that woman. From her tendons he made lutes, her skull and arm bones he made a drum and from her bones he made flutes. He presented these gifts to the mortals, allowing them to make music.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MOS-3)</sup>

The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) believe that the most ancient sounds were gifted to the world by [Khenarthi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi), and so they honor her with music and song.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## Culture

### Altmer
The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) perform songs that were written by the Aldmer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) musical notation is notoriously difficult to read.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

### Argonian
The [Dead-Water Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dead-Water_Tribe) of [Naga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naga) use throat songs such as battle hymns, marching songs, and challenge chants.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

### Breton
The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors_(barony).md) celebrate the holiday of Broken Diamonds by singing the Sephavre, a song in Old Bretic that is the anthem for the murder of Kintyra II and the sorrow that still pervades Glenumbra Moors.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> There is a minor deity in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) named [Jhim Sei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jhim_Sei), whose sphere of influence may revolve around music as they are compared to the likes of Jephre.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-KEII-9)</sup>

### Dunmer
The [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House) and [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) displayed a pronounced affinity for sound and music, though it's difficult to know if this was part of its makeup before its fall. Nearly every shrine encountered in Vvardenfell featured a set of large bells or chimes, which seemingly played an important role in the House's rituals. Some of the more advanced forms of Ash Creature have long, perforated, trunk-like snouts which may be used as musical instruments.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Ash Concept-10)</sup> The fictional epic [Poison Song](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Poison_Song) depicts the house having a strong connection to music to the point that its descendants can somehow "hear" its songs still. Dagoth Ur also proclaims he is "OLDER THAN MUSIC".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

### Imperial
[Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) have musical symphonies and opera houses to create and enjoy music.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) also has marching songs.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The Empire has two officially sanctioned guilds that employ minstrels and bards called the [Scenarist Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scenarist_Guild)<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TDC-15)</sup> and the [Minstrels Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minstrels_Guild).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HOTFG-16)</sup> The former is an ancient guild that is present in every significant city in the provinces, but is headquartered in their citadel in the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TDC-15)</sup> The latter was official recognized by the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) with the approval of the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md) in [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HOTFG-16)</sup>

[Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella) is called the Lady of Music.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AVAYQ-17)</sup>

### Khajiit
The [Dro-m'Athra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro-m%27Athra) are linked to music. Khajiit can be transformed into Dro-m'Athra by being exposed to the "Bent Dance", which is a dance without music, only following the rhythm of the beating Heart of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhan.md). If a Khajiit is being corrupted by the Bent Dance, the "Chase Away" song (*S'virash Sahaala*) can be sung to break it if the corruption isn't too strong. The "Chase Away" song is the only song that can break the Heart's tempo; the notes dance up and down the scale, confusing the Darks and making the Heart sluggish and weak. The tempo eventually breaks, and the corruption will pass. The "Chase Away" song is slippery and unpredictable, as [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah) is. The Khajiit hold that it must not be used unless the need is great. In ancient times, it was used to treat diseases and ward off [cane-locusts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cane-Locust). Singing near Dro-m'Athra gives them a chance to twist the song to their tune and worm their way into a normal Khajiit's heart.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TRAH-18)</sup> Songs, chanting, music, and relics such as Khenarthic Bells, which are rung in reverence to [Khenarthi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi), can also be used to drive back the Darkness.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOCC-19)</sup>

### Kothringi
Music was very important in [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) society. They were known to make instruments out of a variety of different materials, such as turning hollow logs into [wamasu-sized](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wamasu) drums, plucking sinew from [cliffstriders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cliff_Strider) to make low humming bow harps and most notable of all, their [chimes](Lore_Music.md#Bells_And_Chimes) known to be the Kothringi's most loved instruments. A Kothringi by the name of Baelah molded hundreds of chimes from [tin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tin) and [copper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Copper), hanging them from branches and striking them for their sounds. The Kothringi sometimes invited the [Saxhleel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saxhleel) for feasts and celebrations where they played their instruments for the Argonian people.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-COS-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-21)</sup> The Kothringi people also created bugles from horns of [leviathan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Leviathan).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-KLH-22)</sup> Glass beads were also worn, serving as wearable instruments.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-21)</sup>

### Nord
The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Skyrim have skalds who write and recite songs and poems. These skalds are known to join the Imperial Legion as war bards.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SRJorn-23)</sup> "Skald" is an old Nord word meaning "bard".<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-24)</sup> [Skald's Retreat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skald%27s_Retreat) on [Lake Honrich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Honrich)'s [Isle of Gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Isle_of_Gold) is a place where Skyrim's skalds can study their trade and spend time among others with similar talents and interests.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-JTSK-25)</sup> The [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) is another facility where bards can study music, poetry and other similar arts. The College and the Skalds also keep records of the [Poetic Edda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Poetic_Edda), an ancient collection of poems, stories, and legends that depict the extensive history of Skyrim to which all bards are said to contribute to in their time.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SRViarmo-26)</sup> Skyrim's bards can elect to celebrate important occasions by putting on a salskap, a gathering of bards featuring song, poetry, and drink.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOLeiborn-27)</sup> In Skyrim, exceptional singing voices are said to be a "blessing from Kyne".<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-24)</sup>

### Other Uses
Music can be used as a means to cast spells or carry out rituals. The practitioners of the art of using music to cast spells are known as [Thaumavocalists](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thaumavocalists).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MBFP-28)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina) compelled a skald to sing a tune to words found in a specific tome held in Skald's Retreat's library.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESOEngling-29)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOOdana-30)</sup> The song this bard sang plunged Skald's Retreat into a space between [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and [Quagmire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quagmire).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOOdana-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SOA-31)</sup> A performance of *The Song of Sinmur*, one of the old songs of the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) was enough to wrench Skald's Retreat from Vaermina's grasp.
