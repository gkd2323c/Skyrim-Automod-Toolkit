Mounts of Tamriel: Volume 6, Camels **by [Fadus Falto](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Fadus_Falto) of Skingrad** *An account of riding a camel through the Alik'r* *
---
I can report firsthand that the renowned musk of the [camel](Lore_Camel.md) is real. It is pungent, and it is fierce. I found myself in the wastes near [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset.md) for more than a week, adopted by an [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) caravan. These kind travelers taught me all they could about these famously ill-tempered beasts. Indeed, the creatures were not easy to approach at first. But the steady husbandry and the long relationships these people had with their mounts overcame any reservations I, or the camels, had about the situation.

I discovered, when I was able to sit astride a handsome [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) beast halfway through the week, that their loping gait is actually most agreeable once you set aside your expectations. In the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md), their thickly padded feet make short work of vast distances. Their reputation as ships of the desert, sailing along the sands, is well earned and poetically true.

I cannot stress enough, however, the need for quick reflexes around a camel that has yet to accept you. Whether it is their teeth or their spit, a surly camel will let you know how it feels in no uncertain terms.
