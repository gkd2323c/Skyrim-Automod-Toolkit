#### Sage Taheritae (fl. before 2E 582)
[Taheritae](Lore_Taheritae.md) was a great sage, and a member of the [Psijiic Order](Lore_Psijiic_Order.md) known for the publication of *Oegnithr*. They were a proponent of counterbalancing the bad changes by any means necessary.

#### General Takar (fl. [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48))
[Takar](Lore_Takar.md) was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) warrior from the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He was a general of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and one of Emperor [Titus Mede I](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md)'s most trusted generals. Before he joined the Emperor, he fought against the Empire. It was rumored that Titus Mede won him over through personal combat.

#### Talen Vandas (?b - c. [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427))
**Talen Vandas** was the nephew and presumed successor of [King Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Llethan had no children and thought of Vandas as a son.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRGen King-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TRGD-3)</sup> He was popular among the people of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) and known as smart and charismatic.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRBarenziah-4)</sup> Vandas was also considered a powerful warrior.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRPM-5)</sup> He was purportedly killed in a hunting accident around the time of Llethan's death; however, some suspected that [Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) was involved in both of their deaths.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRGen King-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRGen Helseth-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRPM-5)</sup> Many citizens of Mournhold were saddened by his death, as they believed he could have been an excellent king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-1)</sup>

#### Taleon Mythmaker (fl. [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572))
[Taleon Mythmaker](Lore_Taleon_Mythmaker.md) was a famous [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) scholar, author and historian of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He was known for his various tales related to Nord history, particularly those concerning [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold.md), and the [Crown of Freydis](Lore_Crown_of_Freydis.md), as well as his insights into [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) culture.

#### Fleet Queen Taleria (?b - c. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
**Fleet Queen** [Taleria](Lore_Taleria.md) (also known as the ** Dreadsail Queen** and **Pirate Queen**) was a [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) leader of the Dreadsail pirates. She was a [Sea Witch](Lore_Sea_Witches.md), and a Sea Mage, known for her powerful magical abilities, particularly her control over the sea and her ability to conjure storms capable of sinking of numerous ships. She was believed to be the lynchpin holding the Dreadsail pirates together and one of the most powerful conjurers among her people. Taleria's fleet was known for its perfect coordination, and some suspected that Taleria was behind this, using her powerful magic to guide their ships over great distances.

#### Tamika (fl. before 3E 399 - at least 3E 433)

#### King Tamlien (fl. before 2E 580)

#### Witch Tancia (fl. before [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577))
[Tancia](Lore_Tancia.md), also known as **The Sister Who Turned Her Skin Red** was a legendary figure from the oral tradition of the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md). She was a [witch](Lore_Witches.md) of [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) origins known for her remarkable prowess in [air magic](Lore_Weather_Magic.md) and her ill-fated rivalry with her sister [Ulecia](Lore_Ulecia.md), a [water mage](Lore_Water_Magic.md) of comparable power. Tancia and her sister were once revered as two of the greatest Reach witches, but their unquenchable desire for power ultimately drove them toward a perilous and destructive journey. The tale of "The Sister Who Turned Her Skin Red," named after Tancia, tale served as a cautionary tale about the perilous pursuit of power and the devastating consequences consorting with the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) can unleash.

#### Singer Tansal (fl. before 1E 973)
[Tansal](Lore_Tansal.md) was a master [swordsmith](Lore_Smithing.md) and [sword-singer](Lore_Sword-singers.md) from the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He forged a sword first wielded by [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md)'s father, which bore a [serpent](Lore_Snake.md)'s crest. That sword would then pass on to his daughter, Makela, who became one of history's most well-known [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md). The sword broke at the battle of [Bangkorai Pass](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md) while she fought King [Joile](Lore_Joile.md)'s armies.

#### Arch-Mage Taris Rendil (?b - c. [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Taris Rendil](Lore_Taris_Rendil.md) was an Arch-Mage of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) who constructed [Frostcrag Spire](Lore_Frostcrag_Spire.md). Rendil was dissatisfied with living in the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), so he constructed the Spire in the lofty [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to escape his colleagues' prying eyes. He was the creator of the Spire's Atronach Altar, and also collaborated with [Sinderion](Lore_Sinderion.md) of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) to develop the Frostcrag Apparatus Table. He was in some way associated with Lennasaan and Rindsey, two great adventurers who were buried in an ice cave that served as the underground vault of the Spire.

When age began to overwhelm Rendil, he entrusted many of his belongings to Aurelinwae of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). A long-lost relative of Rendil's, the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), was his sole heir and successor and inherited the Spire in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). Rendil wrote the *[Frostcrag Spire Memoirs](Lore_Frostcrag_Spire_Memoirs.md)* on his deathbed, leaving instructions on how to restore the Spire to its former glory. His dying wish was granted by the Hero of Kvatch, who compensated Aurelinwae and returned Rendil's possessions to the tower.

#### Tarnian Lovidicus (fl. [2E 534](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_534) - 582)
[Tarnian Lovidicus](Lore_Tarnian_Lovidicus.md) was the chief councilor of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During his nearly fifty-year tenure as chief councilor, Lovidicus served under Emperors [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md), [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), and Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md). After the collapse of the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) and the dissolution of the Elder Council, Lovidicus fled to the city of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), and he was selected by Countess [Nevenia Caro](Lore_Nevenia_Caro.md) to serve on the city's governing body, the Chamber of Legates, eventually becoming its de facto leader. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Dagonist cult known as the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md) targeted Lovidicus and other former members of the Elder Council to discover the locations of the Daedric weapons known as the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md). Lovidicus played a part in investigating the assassinations of his old colleagues and revealing former councilor [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md) as the high priest of the Waking Flame.

#### Taron Dreth (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Taron Dreth](Lore_Taron_Dreth.md) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) mage who claimed to be the "world's foremost expert" in [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) metallurgy and forging techniques. He was supposedly the author of *[The Aetherium Wars](Lore_The_Aetherium_Wars.md)*, but he actually plagiarized the work from the master under whom he studied, a secret he was willing to kill to protect. At last report, he was traveling in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), most likely looking for evidence to support his stolen theories on [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md). He was killed in the wilderness in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).

#### Tarvus Indoril ([2E 362](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_362) - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Tarvus Indoril](Lore_Tarvus_Indoril.md) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) priest who served as the Archcanon of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Born into [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), Tarvus was highly devoted to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and became the youngest Archcanon in history. His peers described him as a good man and an excellent priest. He played an important role in the foundation of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), but at some point he had died in his sleep. Seeing an opportunity, he was subsequently impersonated by [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md) in the events leading up to [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s plot to drain Vivec's divine energy. Following the end of the crisis, he was succeeded by his pupil [Llevule Andrano](Lore_Llevule_Andrano.md) as the Archcanon of the High Fane. Tarvus was subsequently revived by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and became a Proctor of the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). He took on much of the same duties he had in his previous role under Vivec, redirecting them in service of Sotha Sil.

#### Tarvyn Othren (fl. before 2E 582)
[Tarvyn Othren](Lore_Tarvyn_Othren.md) was a pioneer [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) mage. He is accredited with the summoning of the first [Iron Atronach](Lore_Iron_Atronach.md) [guar](Lore_Guar.md) in history.

At some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), on a drunken dare, he decided to perform the conjuration. The summoned guar immediately killed his companions. Consumed by grief, Othren sought redemption for his heinous act. Ever since then, he only rode on that guar as a reminder of his mistake.

Despite the morbid origins of his discovery, by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the art of conjuring Iron Atronachs shaped like guar was popularized among conjuration mages.

#### Archmage Telenger the Artificer (fl. 2E 578 - 2E 582)
[Telenger the Artificer](Lore_Telenger_the_Artificer.md) was a celebrated genius, writer, explorer, and scholar on topics of anthropology and [magic](Lore_Magic.md), as well as an archmage, a canonreeve, a powerful [conjurer](Lore_Conjuration.md) and magical engineer. He was in service to both the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). #### Thadon, Duke of Mania (?b - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Thadon](Lore_Thadon.md) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) madman and Duke of [Mania](Lore_Mania.md) at the beginning of the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). He resided in the House of Mania in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Thadon was known for his extreme debauchery and self-indulgence. This hedonistic lifestyle eventually led to a deterioration of his mental faculties, resulting in the Duke spending much of his time in a drug-fuelled haze. He is associated with the Diadem of Euphoria, an enchanted crown.

At some point, Thadon had a forbidden affair with [Syl](Lore_Syl.md), the Duchess of [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md). When this affair came to an end, Syl stole the Chalice of Reversal from Thadon as an act of revenge and hid it in Dunroot Burrow; without the Chalice, Thadon could not negate the harmful effects of [Felldew](Lore_Felldew.md), a cripplingly addictive drug. In 3E 433, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to Thadon's court to become a courtier of the House of Mania. Thadon tasked the Hero with retrieving the Chalice from Dunroot Burrow, a [root tunnel](Lore_Root.md) which cannot be entered without first ingesting Felldew.

The Hero later began the Ritual of Accession to become a Duke or Duchess of Mania or Dementia, although it is unknown if the Hero chose to replace Syl or Thadon. Regardless, the surviving ruler promptly defected to [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md) and became a [Priest of Order](Lore_Priest_of_Order.md), betraying the Army of Sheogorath and ultimately perishing within the tunnels of [the Fountainhead](Lore_The_Fountainhead.md).

#### Tharanus Ye Redde-Hand (fl. 1E 3rd century)
[Tharanus Ye Redde-Hand](Lore_Tharanus_Ye_Redde-Hand.md) was a contemporary of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) and [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) and one of the earliest known [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) ancestors of the modern noble [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) family, [House Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md).

Tharanus Ye Redde-Hand was mentioned in the [Tamrilean Tractates](Lore_Tamrilean_Tractates.md) by [Khosey](Lore_Khosey.md). He was described as a slave overseer employed by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) of [Fanacas](Lore_Fanacas.md), the mining hold in the hills north of modern-day Cheydinhal. According to House Tharn historians, he was probably literate and employed in some clerical capacity, due to the fact that Ayleids were known to have kept their business records in red ink.

According to Lady Euphemia Glaber's theory Tharanus and notorious [Tharhan](Lore_Tharhan.md), the Mutilant of the Gradual Massacre in [1E 227](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_227) were the same person. This theory was disproved by the text of the "Scroll of Precursor [Saints](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md)" discovered in the vaults below the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) by Chancellor [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541). According to Chancellor Abnur the theory was coined by dishonest scholars who distorted the few texts that remained from the times of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) for slanderous political purposes. The rare texts found by Abnur revealed that Tharanus was a critical supporter of Alessia's cause. Tharanus was responsible for the disruption of supply routes through forged logistical orders and even leading his own battalion into some of the war's bloodiest conflicts.

#### Emperor Tiber Septim ([2E 828](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_828) - [3E 38](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_38))
[Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) (sometimes enumerated as **Tiber Septim I** or **Septim I**) (also known as **Talos Stormcrown**, **Hjalti Early-Beard**, and **Tiberius Imperator**, among [many other titles](#Titulature)) was a military leader who became one of the most famous figures in [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history, reigning as Emperor Tiber Septim from [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854) to [3E 38](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_38). He declared the onset of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) at the end of the same year.

Tiber Septim ruled for eighty-one years and is considered by many to be the greatest emperor in history. He gave his name to the lineage of Cyrodilic Emperors known as the [Septims](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md). In the centuries following his reign, Tiber became revered as a [god](Lore_Gods.md) and [saint](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md), and is worshipped as one of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md) under the name of **[Talos](Lore_Talos.md)** (meaning **Stormcrown** in the [language](Lore_Ehlnofex.md) of the ancient [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md)).

#### Topal the Pilot (fl. Merethic Era)
[Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) (also known as **Topal the Explorer** and **Torval the Pilot**) was an [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorer and poet. He was the first known Aldmer to explore Tamriel, charting Tamriel's sea lanes in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) on his ship the *Niben*, after which the [Niben River](Lore_Niben_River.md) was named. Topal sailed into [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) (which was subsequently named after him) and up through the Niben River until he was deep in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Here he taught the native [Bird Men](Lore_Bird_Men.md) how to read and write in exchange for the [Eight Islands](Lore_Eight_Islands.md), the future site of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).

He made wide-ranging voyages, penetrating even into hostile environments such as Black Marsh, where he made some of the first recorded observations of Argonians: "manlike reptiles, fleet of foot and running the length of this great mire". The *[Udhendra Nibenu](Lore_Udhendra_Nibenu.md)* ([Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md) for *"Father of the Niben"*) is an epic describing Topal's voyages, but only fragments of the poem have survived. He supposedly also kept a record of his voyages named *The Southern Coast as Far as the Eastern Sea*, which were later kept in the Library of Dusk in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) he was recognized as the [patron saint](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), where [a statue of him](Lore_Statues_Notable_Figures.md#Topal_the_Pilot) could be found in the southwest corner of town.

[Tosh Raka](Lore_Tosh_Raka.md) is the emperor of [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(place).md) (or the "Tiger-Dragon Empire"). Through unknown methods, he purportedly transformed himself into a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md). This transformative process is also said to have turned him into a god, with one source going as far as to state he could be an Akaviri avatar of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and possibly holds a grudge against his "mirror brother". He is allegedly the largest Dragon in the world, and may resemble a hundred foot tall [tiger](Lore_Tiger.md) with [bat](Lore_Bat.md) -like wings.

Since at least the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it has been known Tosh Raka plans to conquer [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). If he is successful, he plans to set his sights on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Much of what is recorded in the book [*Mysterious Akavir*](Lore_Mysterious_Akavir.md) supposedly came from the scrolls and works of Tosh Raka.

#### Vateshran Tosmorn (fl. after [1E 1030](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1030), before [2E 302](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_302))
**Vateshran** [Tosmorn](Lore_Tosmorn.md) was a legendary [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [storyteller, historian](Lore_Vateshrans.md), and author of numerous Reachfolk manuscripts, written in ancient Reachfolk script, such as *[The Love of Isolen](Lore_The_Translated_Works_of_Tosmorn,_II.md)*, *[The Song of Gwyna](Lore_The_Translated_Works_of_Tosmorn,_III.md)*, and *[The Death of Faolan](Lore_The_Translated_Works_of_Tosmorn,_IV.md)*.

#### Arch-Mage Trebonius Artorius (fl. 3E 427)
[Trebonius Artorius](Lore_Trebonius_Artorius.md) was the Arch-Mage of the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) branch of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). As Arch-Mage, he resided in the guild hall in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), which served as the Vvardenfell branch's headquarters. It was believed he was promoted to the position to remove him from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where he originally served the guild.

#### Tsono-Xuhil (fl. Merethic Era - 2E 582)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/ON-flora-Tsono-Xuhil.jpg/150px-ON-flora-Tsono-Xuhil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Tsono-Xuhil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-flora-Tsono-Xuhil.jpg) Tsono-Xuhil [Tsono-Xuhil](Lore_Tsono-Xuhil.md) was the mad [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) of [Mazzatun](Lore_Mazzatun.md), kin to the [Xit-Xaht](Lore_Xit-Xaht.md) tribe.

Tsono-Xuhil was obsessed with maintaining the pre- [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) way of life. The Hist and its last known tree-minder, [Na-Kesh](Lore_Tree-Minder_Na-Kesh.md), were not keen on the lifestyle shown to the [Saxhleel](Lore_Argonian.md) by [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md). While the rest of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) accepted the idea that change is inevitable and altered their practices to adapt accordingly, Tsono-Xuhil sought order in the city of Mazzatun. Tree-Minder Na-Kesh viewed the modern Argonian way of life with disgust, regarding the use of impermanent materials like reeds, mud, and wood in construction and craft as "savage". Believing that their people were above such things, the tree-minder, guided by her Hist, conspired to impose its will upon all of Black Marsh.

#### Kinlady Tuinden (fl. 2E 555 - 580)
**Kinlady** [Tuinden](Lore_Tuinden.md) was the queen consort of King [Hidellith](Lore_Hidellith.md) of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor.md). She was the mother of Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) and Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md).

#### Mane Tullar-dra (?b - before 2E 582)
[Tullar-dra](Lore_Tullar-dra.md) was the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) of the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) people. The exact period of his rule is unknown but it was before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Little is known about Tullar-dra's life.

He died at some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). His remains are preserved in the Tomb of the Manes in [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) Necropolis, a huge tomb complex located southwest of the Rimmen itself. He was buried together with other prominent Manes: [Zebiden-jo](Lore_Zebiden-jo.md) and [Jinninji-ri](Lore_Jinninji-ri.md). His spirit's rest was briefly interrupted in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) when [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) escorted the remains of the ancient Khajiiti ruler Jora-ri to the burial chamber in the Tomb of the Manes.

#### General Tullius (fl. 4E 201)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/SR-npc-General_Tullius.jpg/150px-SR-npc-General_Tullius.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-General_Tullius.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-General_Tullius.jpg) General Tullius (Skyrim) [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) general of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and the Military Governor of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Tullius was originally from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), but Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) sent him to Skyrim to serve as the province's military governor following the killing of High King [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) and the subsequent outbreak of the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Tullius was sent to Skyrim to suppress the rebellion and restore the peace. As military governor, Tullius commanded all legions stationed in Skyrim from Castle Dour in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), and the [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) Legate [Rikke](Lore_Rikke.md) served as his second-in-command.

#### Harbinger Tulvar the Unmentioned (fl. 1E)
[Tulvar the Unmentioned](Lore_Tulvar_the_Unmentioned.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) Harbinger of the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Tulvar was mentioned briefly by [Swyk the Long-Sighted](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Swyk_the_Long-Sighted) in his record of the most notable Harbingers, but was not considered notable enough for a separate entry. Tulvar granted the title of the honorary Companion to [Cirroc the Lofty](Lore_Cirroc_the_Lofty.md), who later became the first Harbinger of other heritage than [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md).

#### Turala (fl. 1E 2920)
[Turala](Lore_Turala.md) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) woman from the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). She was originally a member of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) and the mistress of [Indoril Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Brindisi_Dorom.md), the Duke of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), but after being pregnant with the Duke's child, she was forced into exile. Turala traveled to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), where she was brought in by the [Skeffington Coven](Lore_Skeffington_Coven.md) of [witches](Lore_Witch.md) and went by her new name, **Turala Skeffington**. She is most well-known as one of the central characters of *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)*, a famous historical fiction written by [Carlovac Townway](Lore_Carlovac_Townway.md).

#### Captain Turpis "Volte-Face" Tharn (fl. 1E 2321 - 2331)
**General** [Turpis Tharn](Lore_Turpis_Tharn.md), also known as **Turpis "Volte-Face" Tharn** was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) member of the royal [House Tharn](Lore_Tharn.md) of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md), ancestor and descendant of numerous famous figures. Turpis took part in the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md), which took place in years [1E 2321](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2321) - [1E 2331](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2331).

Members of the Tharn family participated in the war on both sides, acting as captains and leading mercenary companies. Following the end of the war, he was in possession of the lands around the Cheydinhal that became the home of the House. He became the **Earl of Outer Cheydinhal** and married a niece of Admiral [Bendu Olo](Lore_Bendu_Olo.md) and became the ancestor of numerous descendants.

#### Turuk Redclaws (fl. 2E 582)
[Turuk Redclaws](Lore_Turuk_Redclaws.md) was a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) and a founding member of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), a group of adventurers who sought out great challenges for gold and glory.

#### Shield-Brother Tysnal (fl. Merethic Era)
**Shield-Brother** [Tysnal](Lore_Tysnal.md) **Who Was Twice-Named** was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior and a member of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). Along with his twin brother [Terr](Lore_Terr.md), they were part of the crew of [Jorrvaskr](Lore_Jorrvaskr.md) and loyal followers of [Jeek of the River](Lore_Jeek_of_the_River.md). He was one of many grand warriors recorded in the *Songs of the Return*.

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Heirs) | In *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, the player character is given a personal [Background History](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Background_History#Notes) which they may read in their character menu, determined by their chosen class. In certain scenarios—such as those for the Bard or Rogue—the player character encounters a male heir of Emperor Uriel VII. There are 6 possible names assigned to this prince, chosen from a unique pre-designed list specifically created for this single use in the Background History: Pelagius, Cephorus, Uriel, Cassynder, Voragiel, and Trabbatus (in that order). In the Ranger background, the prince you met mentions that he has brothers that are also on the run; the plural use of "brothers" indicates the existence of multiple other princes. The number of possible names could logically indicate 6 in total. These figures precede the later introduction of Geldall, Enman, and Ebel, the Emperor's sons coined in *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*. Ultimately, no information on the other names for princes given in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* is available, as they are not referenced in subsequent games.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Histpry DF-20)</sup> |
