[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-place-Stormhaven.jpg/200px-ON-place-Stormhaven.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhaven.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhaven.jpg) Stormhaven circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Stormhaven is the south-central region of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It is a prosperous place rich in fishing, farming, hunting, mining, and trading opportunities. The [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) flows into the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) along the coast, from which the [Isle of Balfiera](Lore_Isle_of_Balfiera.md) and its famous [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) can be seen in the distance. The [King's Guard](Lore_King%27s_Guard.md) mountain range to the west and north separate it from [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) and [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) form its border to the north and east. The western region [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md) is home to the city of Alcaire (also called [Alcaire Castle](Lore_Alcaire_Castle.md)) as well as other settlements, like the port [Koeglin Village](Lore_Koeglin_Village.md). The central portion of Stormhaven, [Menevia](Lore_Menevia.md), is home to the merchant city of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). Wayrest forms part of Menevia's border to [Gavaudon](Lore_Gavaudon.md), the eastern region of Stormhaven. Gavaudon has no major cities of its own, only small towns like [Wind Keep](Lore_Wind_Keep.md) and Shinji's Scarp, though a bridge over the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) in the east leads directly to the city of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) in [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md).

The region is mostly populated by [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), along with a minority of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) (much to the chagrin of many of the Bretons). [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) also inhabit the region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> The cities of Alcaire and Wayrest, along with numerous smaller towns and keeps, make Stormhaven one of the two most populous regions of High Rock (the other being Glenumbra). While there is quite a bit of traffic between these two,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTAC-2)</sup> Stormhaven also has significant trade and strong ties with [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) to the north.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup>

## History

### Early History
Stormhaven's known history begins with the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md), who discovered the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) in the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and claimed it and the surrounding area. For a time, they ruled all of modern-day High Rock,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BTAOM-4)</sup> and at least part of Stormhaven was presumably part of their dominion for virtually their entire hegemony.

The Direnni interbred with [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) subjects as a matter of entertainment, and did so frequently, so frequently that the manmer which inevitably resulted from these unions were so numerous that they formed a new race, the Bretons. The Direnni were few in number, and established a hierarchical, feudal system in order to control their vast domain. The Bretons became a privileged middle class within this society. The Direnni ruled for untold generations, but following the [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md) circa [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482), they lost their grip on High Rock and eventually retreated back to their tower. The Bretons merely replaced them with their own noble families and carried on, eventually joining the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) in [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TBMOP-5)</sup> When High Rock decided to secede in [1E 2305](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2305), Alessian forces were stalled and turned away in Bangkorai, sparing Stormhaven from their retribution.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BSOHR-6)</sup> Centuries later, they joined the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) and continued to prosper.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TBMOP-5)</sup>

### Second Era

#### Reachmen invasion and formation of the Daggerfall Covenant
In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), the [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) [Durcorach the Black Drake](Lore_Durcorach.md), who recently conquered [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and declared himself emperor, invaded the region from the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). Eventually, the Black Drake reached Alcaire, which resulted in people evacuating to the city of Wayrest, hoping for protection from the Reachmen. The Siege of Wayrest began, which occurred for 57 days. The crowded conditions in Wayrest were a small price to pay for the protection of Wayrest. Because the Reachmen lacked siege weapons or battleships, the Siege of Wayrest ended in a stalemate, forcing the Reachmen to advance westward to the city of Daggerfall. Wayrest and the citizens of Alcaire were saved, and the Reachmen were defeated at the city gates of Daggerfall. <sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TOAM-7)</sup> The Black Drake himself was struck down by Emeric during this battle and his forces were scattered.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SHot LE-8)</sup>

After the Reachmen were defeated, Wayrest, along with Daggerfall, [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) and [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), signed the first [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TATOAM-9)</sup>

#### Ranser's War
In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) swept through High Rock, killing King [Gardner](Lore_Gardner.md) and the entire royal family of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md). Succession fell to Emeric, who ascended to the throne, beginning the Cumberland Dynasty. There was reportedly a "Halo of Gold" around the sun on the day of his coronation.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TATOAM-9)</sup>

In [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566)<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFOKR-10)</sup> King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) had been courting Rayelle, daughter of King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), when he met and abruptly married Princess [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md), daughter of the Redguard King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). Ranser was deeply offended, and withdrew his diplomats from Wayrest, surreptitiously raising levies and hiring mercenaries in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) over the course of a year.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TATOAM-9)</sup> Finally, in [Last Seed](Lore_Calendar.md) of 2E 566, he swept down from the northern mountains, taking Emeric by surprise and nearly razing Wayrest immediately. Only the efforts of the king's personal guard saved the city, and forced a protracted siege.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFOKR-10)</sup> Ranser's forces were insufficient to take the fortified city by storm, and so the siege dragged on as the tide of the war turned against him. Ranser's aggression was unpopular even among his own nobles, and several prominent houses of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) withdrew their military support and sued for peace.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup> While internal stresses took their toll, the original [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) paid dividends, as [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), and [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) were compelled to come to Wayrest's aid.

Meanwhile, Emeric was cannily leveraging his diplomatic connections. His recent marriage ties with the Redguards summoned a fleet of fierce warriors across the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), while an offer to restore [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) brought the Orcs into the fray.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFOKR-10)</sup> Under [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](Lore_Kurog_gro-Bagrakh.md), the old enemies of Shornhelm burned Ranser's holdings and were waiting to prevent the troops' retreat from the advancing Breton and Redguard forces. Caught between the hammer and anvil at Markwasten Moor, Ranser's army was utterly destroyed.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-KRT-11)</sup>

In the aftermath of the war, Emeric helped expand the Daggerfall Covenant, including [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) and [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) within it. Emeric was a strong advocate for an [invasion of Cyrodiil](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in order to restore "the true flame of the Empire of Man".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TATOAM-9)</sup>

#### Nightmares over Stormhaven
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), while still recovering from the devastation of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFOKR-10)</sup> Stormhaven was plagued by a group of [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) worshippers called the [Supernal Dreamers](Lore_Supernal_Dreamers.md). What started in Alcaire soon spread across Stormhaven: raids, kidnappings, and pacts with [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) sent fear through the populace.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LFDN-12)</sup> The Dreamers sought to spread their influence even further and eventually converge their forces on Wayrest, and thereby plunge the entire [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) into a waking nightmare.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LTS-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-IDWA-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DOTHC-15)</sup>

At the time, Stormhaven was also home to the Spirit Wardens, an order of [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) worshippers founded by an Orc named Durak who claimed to have received a vision of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). Azura had told him to prepare for a time when the people of Stormhaven would be plunged into a madness of nightmares, and to protect Stormhaven when the time came.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-FOTSW-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OCOP-17)</sup>

#### Birth of Tiber Septim
It is said that one Hjalti Early-Beard was born within the borders of the [Kingdom of Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md) in [2E 828](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_828), and that Hjalti would go on to become [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TAH-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SROld-19)</sup> Despite this, it is also said that the Emperor never returned once to his beloved birthplace.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-20)</sup>

Since then, Alcaire celebrates [Tibedetha](Lore_Tibedetha.md) (middle Tamrielic for "Tiber's Day") on the 24th of Mid Year, honoring its most famous son, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), with a great party<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-HOTIB-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-20)</sup>

### Third Era
Emperor [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md) and his half-brother [Uriel Septim IV](Lore_Uriel_Septim_IV.md) had been kings of Wayrest before they took the throne of Empire.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BHot E-22)</sup>

By [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), the kingdoms of [Koegria](Lore_Koegria.md), [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md), [Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) and [Gavaudon](Lore_Gavaudon_(fiefdom).md) approximatively covered the region.

After the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), the whole region along with the northeastern part of the [Glenumbra Peninsula](Lore_Glenumbra.md) belonged to the [kingdom of Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md)<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TWITW-23)</sup> During that time, it was ruled by King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DF-24)</sup>

After Eadwyre died, his daughter [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md) and stepson [Helseth](Lore_Helseth.md) briefly fought over the throne, but eventually Elysana won and banished Helseth and his mother, [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md). <sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AGAD-25)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In [4E 188](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_188) the kingdom of Wayrest was sieged by corsairs. A [spy](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) who was infiltrating the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) was given the task of assassinating King Barynia. Fearing for his life and knowing about the conspiracy against him, he made a deal with the pirates and would betray his own kingdom by leaving the city gates open for them, so they could come in and destroy the conspirators who plotted to assassinate him. The Forgotten Hero would aid the city defenders in driving off the corsairs from the city gates. It's unknown if the Forgotten Hero spared the life of King Barynia and allowed him to sail away with the pirates, or if the hero went through with the contract and ended the king's life.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CG-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-CWTR-27)</sup>

## Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/ON-crown_store-Wayrest_Canto_Chapeau.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Wayrest_Canto_Chapeau.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Wayrest_Canto_Chapeau.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Wayrest_Canto_Chapeau.jpg) Wayrest Canto Chapeau Alcaire is known for its farmers and fishing villages, who often given thanks to Zenithar for their bountiful harvests. Alcaire celebrates its region as the birthplace of Tiber Septim.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DFT-28)</sup> Menevia is dominated by the city of Wayrest, which has contributed immensely to High Rock's commerce. Gavaudon's inclement climate and threats from vampires and Orcish raids attracts only the hardy frontiersmen. They tend to the region's rich alluvial soil, a boon from the Bjoulsae River's flood plains.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-29)</sup> Stormhaven has a population of merchant lords.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>
