— *[Nord Cuisine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nord_Cuisine__Savory_Edition)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NCSE-1)</sup> Nord cuisine reflects the harsh climate and rugged landscape that has shaped their warrior culture. Farming is difficult, but wild game supplies a good deal of their nutrition. These meats are preserved through smoking, drying, pickling, or salting. Fish are also a prominent feature in Nord cooking, such as the salmon endemic to the White River. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) are reputed for their great, warm, hearty feasts. Large roasts of [mammoth](Lore_Mammoth.md), [horker](Lore_Horker.md), and assorted game meats are cooked over a spit in the center of the hall, while rustic desserts made with juniper or snowberries are baked in cob ovens. The fruits available in [Skyrim's](Lore_Skyrim.md) scattered bushes have also made their way into the mead that is much-loved by this rugged race, which has developed a reputation abroad for being sweet and strong.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESOC-2)</sup><sup>:10</sup> Skyrim's cold climate may make farming difficult, but Nords aren't without their fair share of greens and fruits such as apples,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> leeks,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>, and potatoes<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> among other crops. The cold waters of Skyrim provide a bountiful source of seafood for the hardy Nords. They also keep [cows](Lore_Cattle.md), [pigs](Lore_Pig.md) and [chickens](Lore_Chicken.md) for their meat. The Nords enjoy many soups and stews and basic hearty meat stews may contain red<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> or white meat.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

[Mead](Lore_Mead.md) is a large part of Nordic culture and is very common and popular among them with many meaderies operating and having operated across Skyrim including Blackbriar, Honningbrew, and Voljar meaderies among others. Nord mead is generally made from fermented honey or molasses and water with some brewers adding a grain mash. A successful brewer can make a name for themselves and drunken tavern fights have been known to break out over who the best mead makers are. Every Nord brewmaster uses their own combination of spices, fruit, and occasionally hops although many don't enjoy the bitterness that hops add to a mead. There are tales of meads that have been mixed with the blood of heroes.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMM-8)</sup>

## Utensils and Tableware
Nordic utensils and tableware consist of items that employ Skyrim's natural bounty and beauty. Sturdy travel steins are used by Nords on the move.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Different native woods like [ironwood](Lore_Ironwood.md) and [rosewood](Lore_Rosewood.md) are used to make cups and goblets<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> while others are crafted from simple pewter.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Hollowed out [Horker](Lore_Horker.md) tusks are used to make sturdy drinking horns.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> Mead decanters are made from earthenware<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> and fermenting jugs are decorated with native animals or scenes of famous Nord battles.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Larger taverns have created collectable mugs for customers to keep.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

[Pinewood](Lore_Pine_Tree.md) bowls are found in prominent Nord households where they're used to hold morsels for snacking.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Uniquely, Nord helmets have sometimes been refashioned into serving bowls.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Large, rectangular platters are crafted from pinewood.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Some wood platters are carved with detailed decorations.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Wealthy Nord clans may use elegant [crystal](Lore_Crystals.md) plates.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Some Nords see it as tradition to only eat soup with a silver spoon.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

Nord tables are decorated with some different adornments. In [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md), place mats woven from swamp reeds are common as they are tough enough for daily use but delicate enough not to scratch finished wood.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Decorated trivets made from wrought [iron](Lore_Iron.md) are used on dining tables.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Quality napkins woven from [troll](Lore_Troll.md) mane are highly durable and absorbent making them perfect for feasts.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> Mammoth tusk is used to make spice shakers.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

The Nords use a few specialty tools in cooking. The blubber masher possesses a fine mesh made of tightly woven steel wires and is used to break up thick sea mammal blubber for cooking.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Metal cheese choppers are used to slice chunks from large cheese wheels.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Ivory handled gravy whisks are common in Nord kitchens<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> as are gravy boats crafted from ceramic.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup> Those who enjoy [chaurus](Lore_Chaurus.md) use large tools to crack them open and extract the meat.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup>

This mixture is boiled in a wide, shallow pot over the course of many hours. | **Barrowost** | **Barrowost** or commonly, **barrow-cheese**, much resembles the common eidar cheese found throughout the holds of Skyrim. Whereas eidar cheese is aged in cellars or caverns, however, barrowost is exclusively aged in Nordic barrows. Be it from the stagnant air, the endlessly sweating stone, or the magic within the barrow walls, the cheese gains an intense earthy sweetness, punctuated with sharp notes. It is one of Falkreath's most reputed cheeses. Some citizens of Falkreath follow an ancient Nord tradition of "grave curd." According to the practice, a fresh farmer's cheese is interred atop a loved one's coffin. Every year, on the anniversary of the departed's death, the grave curd is exhumed and a fifth of it is consumed by the bereaved.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-cheeses-34)</sup> | |

| **Greenedge** | **Greenedge** is a festive cheese of Hjaalmarch. The name of the cheese, derived from the "baskets" of rushes plucked from nearby bogs and woven together, speaks to both its visual appeal and the process of its creation. The cheese is brined and pressed within these baskets, with the latter process imbuing the rind of the normally cream-colored cheese with a verdant hue. The consumption of Greenedge, customarily served at the end of feasts, is something of a spectacle. Young hunters make a game of holding the rush-encased cheese over a flaming torch until its casing burns away and the cheese transforms into a near-molten state. Observers laud veterans of this ordeal for the showmanship they add to this feat, dancing the highly combustible cheese along the torch flame without pausing too long, lest the cheese blacken or their fingers burn. <br> <br> Thus prepared, they flip the hot cheese onto a table and guests immediately plunge bread—or, occasionally, sliced apples—into the melted greenedge. Children find particular joy in the activity, for at this point the cheese's secret contents are revealed—dried fruits and berries, and in wealthier households a single amber plum. The lucky eater that spears the plum before all others is named king of the feast.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-cheeses-34)</sup> | |
| **Mammoth Cheese** | Mammoth Cheese is not produced by Nords and is taken from the camps of [giants](Lore_Giant.md). It is sometimes found on the tables of Nords and used in Nordic recipes. | **Riftwash** | **Riftwash** is a goat cheese with a glassy, violet-black exterior, which belies a pallid and crumbly core. It is pressed to remove much of the water within the cheese, producing a dry cheese, the cheese is washed several times in Riften's famous blackberry mead before being shrouded in wax which has been dyed with blackberry must, a by-product of the city's burgeoning meadworks.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-cheeses-34)</sup> | |
| **Solitude Eidar** | **Solitude Eidar** is an extremely valued and rare cheese produced in Solitude. Only cows that belonged to the herd of Jarl Svartr, first king of Western Skyrim, can provide milk for the cheese. The royal herd, descended from these cows, is maintained by loyal retainers. Milk arrives regularly in Solitude, where it undergoes inspection by a royal cheesemonger—a hereditary office within the Blue Palace. This connoisseur of dairy has exacting standards for the milk he inspects and only one out of every dozen barrels is deemed fit for the making of Solitude Eidar. The milk curdles in a precise fashion, using a series of hourglasses custom-made for the purpose. Lastly, a crumb of cheese from the previous batch is added into the next one, thus creating an unbroken chain of eidar dating back generations.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-cheeses-34)</sup> | |

## Notes
- The Nordic practice of drying unsalted fish to make [Stockfish](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stockfish) has real-world roots: it is common in Norway, where wooden racks called "hjell" are used to dry fish on the foreshore (a part of the shore that is above water when the tide is low and submerged underwater when the tide is high).
