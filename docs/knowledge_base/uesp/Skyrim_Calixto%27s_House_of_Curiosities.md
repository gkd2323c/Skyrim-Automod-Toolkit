## Calixto's House of Curiosities
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities.jpg) The interior of the House of Curiosities [Calixto Corrium](Skyrim_Calixto_Corrium.md) and his sister Lucilla inherited a considerable fortune from their parents, and decided to travel across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) years ago. During their travels, they collected a number of curious and, some might say, legendary items. When his sister died, he settled in Windhelm and opened his collection for public viewing.

While Calixto is awake between 5am and 12am, his door is only unlocked from 9am until 8pm, the same as a regular store. Outside of these hours, it is locked with a leveled lock. During the opening hours, you can enter the house and look around, or pay Calixto 2 [septims](Skyrim_Gold.md) for a guided tour, on which he points out the most intriguing items in his collection and proceeds to give a little background story on each of them.

Upon entering, in the northwest corner, under the stairs that lead up to a small loft area, is a table holding an [iron dagger](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md), an [iron mace](Skyrim_Iron_Mace.md), and a copy of *[Nightingales: Fact or Fiction?](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nightingales__Fact_or_Fiction%3F)*. Against the north wall are two sets of shelves holding a bowl of [void salts](Skyrim_Void_Salts.md), a [salt pile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile), two bowls of [troll fat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Troll_Fat), two bowls of [frost salts](Skyrim_Frost_Salts.md), a collection of Ancient Nord [Embalming Tools](Skyrim_Embalming_Tool.md) (the first item shown by Calixto during his guided tour), a bowl of [fire salts](Skyrim_Fire_Salts.md), a bowl of [bone meal](Skyrim_Bone_Meal.md), some [slaughterfish scales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Scales), seven [slaughterfish eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Slaughterfish_Egg), three [spider eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spider_Egg), three [chaurus eggs](Skyrim_Chaurus_Eggs.md), and a bowl of [mammoth cheese](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mammoth_Cheese_Bowl), with a bottle of [Frostbite venom](Skyrim_Frostbite_Venom.md) on top of the left-hand shelving unit.

Against the east wall is a third set of shelves holding three [iron ingots](Skyrim_Iron_Ingot.md), an empty [lesser soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lesser_Soul_Gem), two empty [petty soul gems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Petty_Soul_Gem), a [potion of minor stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina), a sample of [hagraven feathers](Skyrim_Hagraven_Feathers.md), and [The Book of Fate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Book_of_Fate). The Book of Fate is a unique book in which each reader sees their own destiny, unless it is blank, which is suggested by Calixto to be caused either by the reader having no predetermined destiny, or by imminent death. This is the second item shown on the guided tour. To the right of these shelves, in the southeast corner of this part of the shop, is a round wooden table and chair, with two [giant's toes](Skyrim_Giant%27s_Toe.md) and some bones on the table. Beside it is a wooden support, with three [garlic](Skyrim_Garlic.md) braids hanging from it.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Calixto%27s_House_of_Curiosities_02.jpg) Calixto's bedroom Around the corner, against the east wall, is another set of shelves holding a bunch of [lavender](Skyrim_Lavender.md), a collection of Dwemer items including [cups](Skyrim_Dwemer_Cup.md), [plates](Skyrim_Dwemer_Plate.md), and a [dish](Skyrim_Dwemer_Dish.md), and [Ysgramor's Soup Spoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ysgramor%27s_Soup_Spoon). The spoon is the third item on the guided tour, and appears to actually be a fork and not a spoon. To the right is a long wooden table holding [The Dancer's Flute](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Dancer%27s_Flute) among some skulls and bones. The flute is the fourth and final item on the guided tour. Under the table is an unlocked respawning chest, which is empty. To the right, against the south wall to the left of the doorway into Calixto's bedroom, is another set of shelves holding a [woodcutter's axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Woodcutter%27s_Axe) and a respawning [knapsack](Skyrim_Knapsack.md) which contains leveled items, with a barrel containing foodstuffs beside it.

Through the doorway is his bedroom, which contains an owned single bed along the east wall, which remains owned even after his death. Against the north wall is a tall wardrobe containing [clothes](Skyrim_Clothes.md), and opposite, against the south wall, is a square wooden table holding a bottle of [Nord mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead). Against the west wall is a [cooking pot](Skyrim_Cooking_Pot.md) on a stand. Climbing the stairs to the loft area in the main room, there is a locked non-respawning chest tagged Calixto's Chest, which contains the [Butcher Journal](Skyrim_Butcher_Journal.md), a [steel dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger), and some embalming tools. There is a second loft that is only reachable using the Whirlwind Sprint shout; however, it only contains a large stack of crates and four food barrels.
