# Sap-Speakers

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/ON-npc-Sap-Speaker_Kuzei.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Sap-Speaker_Kuzei.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sap-Speaker_Kuzei.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sap-Speaker_Kuzei.jpg) Sap-Speaker Kuzei of the Root-Whisper Tribe *There was a sap-speaker there, yes? A Saxhleel blessed by the Hist with second-eyes and second-ears.*
—Xukas<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xukas-1)</sup> [Sap-Speakers](Lore_Sap-Speaker.md) are [Argonians](Lore_Argonians.md) that act as the direct intermediaries of the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). Their specific role and methods of communion with the Hist can vary greatly from tribe to tribe: some tribes practice unique forms of interaction,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TOMM-2)</sup> while others do not engage in communion at all.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Keshu-3)</sup> Sap-speakers were revered for their wisdom,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Jee-Lar-4)</sup> and are key figures among their tribes alongside the likes of village elders and tree-minders.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KGTW1-5)</sup> They provide aid to those chosen by the elder Hists to carry out sacred tasks. This aid can come in the form of offering words, goods, or distance when needed.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HT-6)</sup> Some Argonians believed they were destined for that position.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-KGTW2-7)</sup>

## Culture

### Gee-Rusleel
The Gee-Rusleel, also known as the Miredancer tribe, known for their deep reverence of the Hist, maintained the role of sap-speaker for countless generations. Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), this sacred position was held by an individual called Thumarz. The sap-speaker bore a solemn spiritual duty that involved long periods of seclusion, during which they sought direct communion with the Hist. Their isolation took them either deep below the tree's roots or high into its branches. While in contact with the Hist, the sap-speaker received visions and other forms of communication that were often difficult to comprehend: to this end, they studied books, scrolls, and tablets left by their predecessors to reflect on the signs delivered to them by the Hist. Only after this stage of contemplation did they emerge to share the Hist's will with the tribe. Throughout these periods, the sap-speaker survived solely on what the Hist provided, including the sap, leaves, and the otherwise forbidden fruit of the tree. This communion came at a cost, as the consumption of large amounts of [Hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md) often resulted in sap-poisoning. Even tribal elders did not fully understand the nature of the sap-speaker's experience.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TOMM-2)</sup>

### Izta-Ahuak
Tree-minders and sap-speaker are key roles in Argonian tribes that answer to a living Hist. Since the Itza-Ahuak, the Tide-Born have no Hist, they have no sap-speakers: instead, their tide-readers fulfill similar roles.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Keshu-3)</sup>

### Root-Whisper Tribe
The sap-speaker of the Root-Whisper tribe was known to lead the sacred clutch.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RKD-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ritualist-9)</sup> At one point, this position was held by Kuzei.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SSK-10)</sup> She and her followers created the [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md) to protect the tribe's future from the invading forces of the Barsaebic [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md). The [Rootmender's Staff](Lore_Rootmender%27s_Staff.md), once known as the Sap-Speaker's Staff used to belong to her, and later it was entrusted to the Rootmender.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SSK-10)</sup>

### Seekhat-Xol
The inhabitants of Seekhat-Xol's village were known to rely on sap-speakers for their communion with the Hist.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-KGTW2-7)</sup>

### Wasseek-haleel
Wasseek-haleel, (also known as the Bright-Throats) were known to listen to their Hist through music produced by wind chimes. Despite their lack of a sap-speaker, the Bright-Troats recognized the role of the sap-speakers, considering them blessed by the Hist.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xukas-1)</sup>

## Notes
- Individuals known as "Hist-Speakers" were authority figures in [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) era Argonian temples.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FNOJ-11)</sup>
- Dolls crafted to resemble sap-speakers are a rare sight outside Murkmire.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SSMD-12)</sup>
