[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b6/SR-npc-Skald.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Skald.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Skald.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Skald.jpg) Jarl Skald on his throne [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/SR-npc-Skald_04.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Skald_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Skald_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Skald_04.jpg) Skald arguing with [Brina Merilis](Skyrim_Brina_Merilis.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/SR-npc-Skald_05.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Skald_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Skald_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Skald_05.jpg) Skald in exile in the [Palace of the Kings](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md) **Skald** is an [elderly](Skyrim_Elder.md) [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) and the initial [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) of [Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md) in [the Pale](Skyrim_The_Pale.md). A militant supporter of the [Stormcloak rebellion](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md) and fiercely loyal to its leader, [Ulfric Stormcloak](Skyrim_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), he will be overthrown if the [Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md) prevails, and replaced by [Brina Merilis](Skyrim_Brina_Merilis.md).

He has been Jarl for 35 years and is generally regarded as too arrogant and overzealous to the Stormcloak cause for the position. The majority of the townsfolk find his political fanaticism foolish and his personality unpleasant, including members of his court. Many would much prefer [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) supporter Brina Merilis as jarl.

His attire reflects his status and personality and includes a full set of [noble clothes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Noble_Clothes) with [fur-lined boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fur-lined_Boots) and a [silver and moonstone circlet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_and_Moonstone_Circlet). He carries a [steel sword](Skyrim_Steel_Sword.md) and a selection of [upper-class items and gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Rich).

Due to his political beliefs, Skald has surrounded himself with veterans from the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), including court wizard [Madena](Skyrim_Madena.md) and housecarl [Jod](Skyrim_Jod.md), although both of them disagree with his stance. As a result, Skald spends the entire day—from when he wakes up at 8am until his bedtime at midnight—sitting on his throne and arguing with his staff about political matters, at times belittling his life-long servant [Bulfrek](Skyrim_Bulfrek.md). In the event that the Imperial Legion [regains the Pale](Skyrim_Reunification_of_Skyrim.md) in the civil war, Jarl Skald will move to the [Palace of the Kings](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md) and live in exile. The only exception to his daily routine in Dawnstar occurs when you visit the city for the first time; he will then be engaged in a heated debate with his rival Brina Merilis just outside the entrance to the [White Hall](Skyrim_The_White_Hall.md).

## Dialogue
His greetings:

*"I've been in charge of Dawnstar for over 35 years."* *"No Empire is going to take the Jarlship away from this Son of Skyrim."* *"The Pale will throw those Imperial backbiters up against the wall when they come for us."* *"Skyrim will not bow to a false king any longer. Ulfric is the true king."* When asked how Dawnstar stands in the civil war, he will be quite confident:

**How is Dawnstar faring in the war?** *"The Stormcloaks are destined to win this fight. [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) is with us, and when you fight under a Divine's blessing, victory is assured. Dawnstar has a history of being the site of battles that have crippled the fate of the Empire. I hope this remains true."* **Is Dawnstar prepared for dragon attacks?** *"The only reason dragons are attacking is because Talos is angry with us for turning our backs on him. As soon as we drive the Empire out of Skyrim, the dragons will vanish. Mark my words."* When spoken to in exile, he'll make it clear that he holds a grudge:

*"Damn Imperials. They won't hold Dawnstar forever."* *"Why are you speaking to me, Imperial?"* When exiting dialogue:

*"Don't bother me again unless it's important."*

### Waking Nightmare
If you have yet to start the quest [Waking Nightmare](Skyrim_Waking_Nightmare.md), an annoyed Skald will greet you with:

*"Unless you're here to solve this nightmare problem, I don't need you."* **Nightmare problem?** *"That's right. Dawnstar. My Dawnstar is plagued with nightmares. I haven't slept properly in days. That priest of Mara who came here before you says the Divines will cure us. Well, until they do, I don't have any business to discuss with outsiders."* Once the quest has been completed:

*"The nightmares in Dawnstar have lifted, and I hear you helped that happen. You have my thanks."* He now has confidence enough to hand you a few smaller tasks.

### Kill the Giant
**I'm looking for work.** *"Giants are a common problem in the Pale. The Empire always demanded I leave them be, but the Empire isn't here now. Go and slay one of the beasts. It should let them know that Dawnstar is not to be trifled with."* **I don't have time for that.** *"You don't have time? Fine."* **It will be done.** *"I'm counting on you"* His goodbye:

*"Good luck with the giant. Don't get stepped on."* When you return with:

**The giant in (Giant Camp) is dead.** *"You've shown those monsters that they will no longer be tolerated in the Pale. Good work. This ends Skald's small string of quests.

## Rumors
In Dawnstar, several citizens will tell you about the current Jarl. Mine owner [Leigelf](Skyrim_Leigelf.md) has the following to say about Dawnstar's jarl: *"That would be Skald the Elder. He's been running Dawnstar ever since he was a boy, and he never lost that brat's attitude. Turned on the Empire first chance he got. Now we're all part of this rebellion."* [Beitild](Skyrim_Beitild.md) will also share her opinion: *"That would be Jarl Skald the Elder. He's over in the White Hall, probably talking about Ulfric like he's the second coming of Talos."* The aging [Frida](Skyrim_Frida.md) can remember Skald from way back, but still thinks him as a fool and prefers Brina Merilis: *"Pfft. I'm the oldest woman in Dawnstar. Was here when the Skald the Elder was Skald the Younger. He's a fool, if you haven't met him already. Thinks Ulfric Stormcloak is invincible and spits dragon fire. The people here look to Brina Merilis when they need things settled. Real firebrand going up. Wasn't surprised when she joined the Legion."* Lastly, Brina Merilis herself will comment briefly on the argument you visited in front of the White Hall: *"The Jarl and I have our disagreements. Sorry if you had to overhear one of them"* and *"Skald supports Ulfric's rebellion, and he doesn't like having a couple of old Legionnaires living in his town. He's a fool. A dragon has attacked Helgen. Dawnstar needs the Empire now more than ever."*

## Notes
- In the *[Prima Official Game Guide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V__Skyrim__Prima_Official_Game_Guide)*, the summary for him reads:

*"Skald has ruled Dawnstar since his father's death on the battlefield 35 years ago. He came into his rule in his teenage years and has never really lost the arrogance and sense of invincibility that comes with that age. He is quick to judge and doesn't change his mind once he has decided something."* - The court of Dawnstar, whether under the control of Skald or Brina Merilis, is the only one that doesn't employ the services of a steward.
- Skald doesn't use any title, despite mostly being referred to as **Skald the Elder**. The *Prima Official Game Guide*, as well as internal data also refer to him under said name. Apparently, he also used the title **the Younger** in the past. In addition to these titles, [Bulfrek](Skyrim_Bulfrek.md) refers to him as **Skald Felgeif**.
- Skald claims to have been in charge of Dawnstar for over 35 years. Therefore, he must have become Jarl by 4E 166.
- According to Frida, Skald had a son at one point in his life, but it is unknown what became of him or how his relationship with his father is, as he is never mentioned elsewhere.
- Skald will always refer to you as "Imperial" when he's in exile, even if you haven't joined the Imperials, and the city's turnover has been caused by [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md). This is due to the dialogue checking whether or not he's in exile, and not the cause of said exile, as specific dialogue for different causes doesn't exist.
