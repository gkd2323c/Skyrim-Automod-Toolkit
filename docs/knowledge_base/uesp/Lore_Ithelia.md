# Ithelia

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/ON-concept-Ithelia.png/200px-ON-concept-Ithelia.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Ithelia.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Ithelia.png) Ithelia *There was another Daedric Prince! My Prince! A Prince that saved me and preserved just enough of my memory so that I could one day save them in return. A Prince whose jealous and craven siblings cast down and wiped from the annals of history..*
— [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md) Ithelia, known as the **Prince of Paths**, the **Mistress of the Untraveled Road**, the **Unseen**, the **Fate-Changer**, the **Dark Reflection**, the **Last Tomorrow**, the **Unweaver**, and **She Who Saw and Wept**, is the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Paths.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mora Co F-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Torv Prologue-2)</sup> Some believe her will has some kind of relation to [unbound Prisoners](Lore_Hero.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TNC-3)</sup> She supposedly had the ability to shape destiny, alter [fate](Lore_Fate.md) and rearrange its strands according to her whim. Considering this power a threat to the fabric of reality, [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), in alliance with [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), erased all traces of her memory from existence in order to preserve reality.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup> Ithelia left behind her loyal servant, [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md), as a contingency plan in her absence.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup>

As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she was unknown to the academic community,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONAzandar AC-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup> though some scholars had uncovered evidence of forgotten [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) deities.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOJ-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gandrinar-8)</sup> Torvesard, in an alliance with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) and [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md), was able to recover his memory of his Prince and began his efforts in finding and freeing her in earnest. Eventually, Ithelia's memory was restored, with her then setting out to reclaim all her power. However, with the help of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the support of Hermaeus Mora, she saw the destruction her path would result in, even for herself, and accepted being exiled, seemingly with no possibility of return, to a world without magic, thus ending the danger to reality. The connection to the other reality was then severed using [Abolisher](Lore_Abolisher.md). Hermaeus Mora then once again erased all memory of Ithelia’s existence from every being, save for the Vestige and himself.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Fate Forgotten Prince-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup>

Ithelia's realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) is [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-11)</sup>

## History

### Star Orphan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/ON-place-The_Whispered_Path.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Whispered_Path.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Whispered_Path.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Whispered_Path.jpg) A statue of Ithelia Originally the eldest member of the [Star-Orphans](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), Magna Ge that followed the parabolas that led away from [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), Ithelia was known as the **White Star**, the **Prime Archon** and the **Queen of Fates Unwritten**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TNC-3)</sup>

When Ithelia was shown the [Infinite Abyss](Lore_Apocrypha.md) and the Indeterminate Sea (Mora Obscura), she discovered the Indefinite Limits of [Lorkh-Apeiron](Lore_Void.md). It is said she bears connection to the harmony within duality that exists within the dichotomy of [Anui-El](Lore_Anuiel.md) and [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), and the unity of opposites that contained [starlight](Lore_Varliance.md) and [endless possibility](Lore_Many_Paths.md) beyond [cosmic interplay](Lore_Aurbis.md). Within texts discussing her, it is said she bears some connection to the "madness of the Time God" and the "first challenge of his shadow", who in nothingness saw those endless possibilities first. She also has unknown connection to the establishment of linear time, as layered upon endless possibility by the afixing of [Ada-Mantia](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) and its Zero Stone, a stable spire afixed by a stone of nothing possible,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-NMI-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BTAOM-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AU4TET-14)</sup> Texts discussing her also speak on how [Aka](Lore_Akatosh.md) disappeared in the South, and [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) learned why Aka's insanity is all that is and could be. From this lesson, [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) and the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) was fixed to cleave a path through the everything to reach Numancia.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> As a result, echoing the endeavor of the Prime Archon, Ithelia, her Ayleid followers began to use violence against Man to bring forth a [Numinous](Lore_Hero.md) Paravant, whose metaphysical nature would give them unbound hands and the ability to achieve Ithelia's goal, to break the shackles of [fate](Lore_Fate.md) and give every being the world and history they desired.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TNC-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

Ithelia found worshippers in the Mythic Age. Her Ayleid followers prayed and offered sacrifices to her before embarking on a journey.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ESOTG-17)</sup> She had a significant amount of ties to the region known today as the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-11)</sup>

### Imprisonment
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg) The spire within the Mythos that served as Ithelia's Prison [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png/200px-ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png) Ithelia's Prison At some point in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md),<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> after Ithelia altered [fate](Lore_Fate.md) to avert a coming catastrophe, conflict arose when Mora examined her fate threads and realized Ithelia's power had already caused irreparable damage to the fabric of fate and that her continued existence endangered all reality. Mora's fears intensified when Ithelia created her [Loom of the Untraveled Road](Lore_Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road.md), a great fate altering engine via which she intended to tap into the power of the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) and give every being their own desired history, breaking the shackles of fate. Perceiving that the Many Paths were splintering under Ithelia's "unnatural manipulations" and foreseeing that reality would shatter in the attempt to achieve what Ithelia desired if the Loom was used, Mora took action.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vargas-18)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Untraveled-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-20)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg) The submerged shrine of Hermaeus Mora that serves as a portal to Apocrypha, within the Inner Sea [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg) Hermaeus Mora discussing the threat of Ithelia Eventually, an alliance formed between Hermaeus Mora, [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). Before they acted, Mora warned Ithelia to abandon her plan to use the Loom and alter fate at the city of [Niryastare](Lore_Niryastare.md), but she refused.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Relics-21)</sup> Each of the three Princes allied with Mora took possession of their chosen mortal champions and met at a submerged shrine of Hermaeus Mora in the ruined city of [Anudnabia](Lore_Anudnabia.md), which had been dragged beneath the waves by Ithelia's fateweaving. There, they entered a hidden [Demiplane](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) imperceptible to her powers, where they could plan their attack in secrecy. Each Prince crafted a weapon to counteract Ithelia’s fate-manipulating abilities. To track her, Mephala forged the Skein of Secrets, enabling her to locate Ithelia and her Unseen by tracing the remnants of their power, normally invisible to magical observation.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup> To perceive her, Azura created the [Lamp of Clarity](Lore_Lamp_of_Clarity.md), illuminating Ithelia, her Unseen, and any open portals to the Path that could reach her.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup> To reach her, Boethiah forged [Abolisher](Lore_Abolisher.md), a blade capable of opening, closing, and severing connections between Paths.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Alea-23)</sup> Finally, Mora created the [Echonir](Lore_Echonir.md), a weapon capable of tracking and erasing any lingering memory of Ithelia.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Examination-24)</sup>

Thus prepared, the allied Princes and their armies attacked. Ithelia was ultimately cornered within the Ayleid city of [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md), which was a great shrine to her.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Gambit-25)</sup> She was defeated through the combined efforts of the other Princes, drained of her powers to the extent of having no more strength than a mortal,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup> and imprisoned within the Mythos, the core of Apocrypha, a place her own powers couldn't perceive. However, before she was sealed away, two things occurred. The first was that Ithelia enacted a failsafe within the mind of her scion, [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md), that would allow him to one day recall events associated with her imprisonment in the form of dreams and lead him to the path of freeing her.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TJ-26)</sup> The second was that, through her own powers to perceive fate and the Many Paths, Ithelia saw that Hermaeus Mora was correct about the threat she represented, a discovery she would forget due to her imprisonment.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup> With Ithelia imprisoned, the four Princes designed a plan to erase the very memory of her existence from reality, so that the danger would never return.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Dandera-27)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Dugikh-28)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Adept-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Po Fate-30)</sup>

Two of the other Princes whose support Hermaeus Mora sought in this matter were [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) and [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md). However, Mora's attempt to secure their approval for his plans proved futile. Despite Mora's insistence that Ithelia represented a grave danger to reality, Vaermina and Peryite argued that she shouldn't be punished solely because of something she might do, and that the spheres of the Princes were sacrosanct. Believing that Hermaeus Mora was overstepping his boundaries, the two Princes refused to support him. It was then that Mora revealed he was going to proceed with his plan regardless. Mora shattered the statue of Vaermina and cast his spell, removing the memory of Ithelia from every mind across all reality.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Hidden-31)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Vaermina-32)</sup> Ithelia's imprisonment was complete, and would become Mora's most guarded secret, the only recollection of the event in existence split between three Glyphics within [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mora Co F-1)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Go LM-33)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup>

### Prelude to Return
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/ON-render-The_Blind.jpg/200px-ON-render-The_Blind.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-The_Blind.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-The_Blind.jpg) The Blind within the Bedlam Veil [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg/200px-ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg) The Recollection within the Oathsworn Pit In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Daedric Prince Ithelia returned to [Aurbic](Lore_Aurbis.md) affairs. Vaermina and Peryite invaded and nearly destroyed Apocrypha via a realm corroding plague unleashed by the Taskmaster, in their efforts to retrieve the Glyphics that held the last recollection of Ithelia. Though the two Princes were foiled with the aid of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), in the chaos, the Dremora Torvesard managed to recover the final Glyphic and regain his lost memories of Ithelia.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Necrom Events-34)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Calamity-35)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Unhealthy-36)</sup> With Torvesard's memories restored, Hermaeus Mora's spell started to weaken, causing other servants of Ithelia to start recovering some of their lost knowledge. These servants of the Prince of Paths started looking for artifacts which would help with the release and restoration of their Prince.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Po Fate-30)</sup> Concurrently, the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) cult of [Ayleid revivalists](Lore_Ayleid_Revivalists.md) dedicated to Ithelia, formed from the descendants of her ancient worshippers that had fled to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), under the leadership of Valenwood nobleman [Nantharion Rayn](Lore_Nantharion.md). Plagued by the feeling of an "aching hole" in the world, an "emptiness where something should be but nothing is", the Recollection formed with the goal of restoring their Prince and lost realm, an event dubbed the "Day of Remembering".<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-FN-37)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-NJ-38)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-39)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-40)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-41)</sup>

The Blind, a powerful Daedric servant of Ithelia aided by her cultists, the Blind Path, attacked the [Maelstrom Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maelstrom_Arena)'s Bedlam Veil in an effort to retrieve the Echonir.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-vault-42)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ILS-43)</sup> The Recollection attacked the Oathsworn Pit, training grounds of the [Oathsworn](Lore_Oathsworn.md), an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) warrior sect dedicated to [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), in an effort to retrieve the [Staff of Many Paths](Lore_Staff_of_Many_Paths.md),<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Dulzush-44)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Bazrag-45)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Strife-46)</sup> and Torvesard himself sought out the relics of the three Princes that had delivered Ithelia's defeat, the Lamp of Clarity, the sword Abolisher, and the Skein of Secrets.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Po Fate-30)</sup> Though the Recollection and Blind Path were repelled with the aid of the Vestige and the Echonir wasn't seized,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-vault-42)</sup> the Staff of Many Paths was taken by the Recollection.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Strife-46)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-quest-Prisoner_of_Fate.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Prisoner_of_Fate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Prisoner_of_Fate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Prisoner_of_Fate.jpg) Torvesard using Abolisher to cut open the path to Ithelia Investigating the threads of fate and witnessing the memories of Ithelia’s imprisonment, Leramil the Wise and the Vestige pursued Torvesard after he stole Abolisher from the hidden shrine of Boethiah where it was kept. Passing through the submerged shrine of Anudnabia, Torvesard used the blade to enter the Mythos and attempt to free his Prince. He summoned Daedra allied with Ithelia to delay the Vestige and carved a path into the spire believed to be her prison, only to find it empty. Ithelia was already gone. In remembering her, Torvesard restored fragments of her lost power, weakening her bonds until she broke free. He fled through a portal to seek her out, convinced she would be drawn to places tied to her fragmented memories. Hermaeus Mora then revealed the truth. The spire was never the prison. Forgetting was. Once Ithelia was remembered, her shackles failed. Unable to foresee what followed, Mora sent Leramil and the Vestige away to await a new path to open.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Po Fate-30)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/ON-render-Infinite_Archive_08.png/200px-ON-render-Infinite_Archive_08.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Infinite_Archive_08.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Infinite_Archive_08.png) Tho'at Replicanum's invasion of the Infinite Archive Around the same time, [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md), a [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) associated with Ithelia<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-47)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-LAIA-48)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-11)</sup> who had taken on the form of "vivified ink", invaded the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md), an Adjunct Realm of Apocrypha, in search of something. Prior to this event many fateline connections between the Index, the archival mechanism that restored things within the realm that were destroyed, and its contents broke. Tho'at splintered her [nymic](Lore_Nymic.md) and imbued it into the tomes of the Archive, allowing her to create a potentially infinite number of [Maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), Daedric facsimiles of vivified ink that replicated the creatures and objects described in the texts that spawned them, extensions of Tho'at herself.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOMM-49)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Malkhest Archive-50)</sup>

In response to the invasion Master Malkhest, caretaker of the Archive, used [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md) to recruit adventurers to combat the Maligraphies.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-51)</sup> With the aid of the Vestige, Malkhest gathered enough [paleonymic](Lore_Paleonymic.md) fragments from the vanquished Maligraphies to summon Tho'at Replicanum and banish her.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ESOJun-52)</sup> However, in addition to creating Maligraphic copies of the creatures in the tomes, Tho'at had already replicated herself hundreds of times across the Archive by creating [Maligraphic](Lore_Maligraphy.md) copies of herself. Though replicas, each Tho'at Replicanum copy had a slightly different nymic, so they were all separate beings to one another, and different nymics had to be gathered to banish each. The Vestige and Malkhest would continue working to banish the spreading presence of Tho'at and the Maligraphies from the Infinite Archive.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOMM-49)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESOMMR-53)</sup>

### Return and Restoration
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/ON-npc-Ithelia.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ithelia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia.jpg) Ithelia in Elenglynn In the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), the Recollection used the Staff of Many Paths to imbue [Greenspring Seeds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenspring_Seeds) with [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) energies. These seeds are normally used by [Greenspeakers](Lore_Greenspeakers.md) to grow new sections of forest, but the corruption strengthened and twisted their [magic](Lore_Magic.md), creating Wildburn Seeds. The Recollection used these to cause a thick jungle to cover much of the West Weald overnight, and in doing so created the [Wildburn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn) on its outskirts, an area of magical land corruption that mutated living beings.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Darolith-54)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Greenspeaker Sorilen-55)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Seeds Suspicion-56)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Nantharion-57)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribune-58)</sup> Nantharion Rayn then led Bosmer settlers from [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md) into this forest, named it the Dawnwood, and founded Vashabar atop the Ayleid ruins of Miscarcand, crowning himself its king. There, he and many of his followers abandoned the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) in favor of a new philosophy known as the [Dawnway](Lore_Dawnway.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Nantharion-57)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Mt CN-59)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Gambit-25)</sup> At the same time, mystical events called Mirrormoor Incursions spread throughout the Weald as Ithelia’s realm bled into Nirn and her Daedra searched for their lost Prince.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Incursions-60)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-npc-Shardmarshal_Vargas.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Shardmarshal_Vargas.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Shardmarshal_Vargas.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Shardmarshal_Vargas.jpg) Shardmarshal Vargas with Abolisher in the Feldagard Keep Stripped of her memories and power, Ithelia wandered the West Weald until she was drawn to the ruins of Elenglynn, once an Ayleid citadel devoted to her worship,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Paths Chaos-61)</sup> where the Vestige and Leramil encountered her while following disturbances in fate. There, Ithelia briefly relived a past confrontation with Hermaeus Mora over her manipulation of destiny before vanishing once more.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Paths Chaos-61)</sup> The crisis deepened at Feldagard Keep, where Ithelia’s lieutenant Shardmarshal Vargas led the Recollection and [Shardborn](Lore_Shardborn.md) using the artifact Abolisher to open portals that bypassed the keep’s defenses. With the aid of Tribune Alea Idolus, the Vestige repelled the attack, pursued Vargas into Mirrormoor, and seized the artifact.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Paths Chaos-61)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Cohort-62)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Hold-63)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vargas-18)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribune-58)</sup> Fate’s ripples then led the Vestige and Beragon Sharp-Dagger near Vashabar, where they uncovered King Nantharion’s alliance with the Recollection and their role in the Wildburn, and where his attempt to create a new Wildburn Seed using the Staff of Many Paths was interrupted, unleashing its power prematurely and destroying the Recollection’s living fortress of [Hoperoot](Lore_Hoperoot.md).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Darolith-54)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Greenspeaker Sorilen-55)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Seeds Suspicion-56)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Nantharion-57)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribune-58)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/ON-npc-King_Nantharion_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-King_Nantharion_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Nantharion_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Nantharion_02.jpg) King Nantharion before the Great Well, wielding the Staff of Many Paths After a memory of Ithelia was revealed by the Echonir at Hoperoot, the Vestige traveled to the Outcast Inn. ### Ithelia's True Plan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) The Loom of the Untraveled Road Though the Shardborn continued their incursions against the West Weald, Beragon surmised that their attacks were only meant to serve as a distraction to prevent the discovery of Ithelia's true plan. At Beragon's behest, the Vestige used the Skein of Secrets to track Ithelia's presence to the Ayleid ruins of [Wendir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wendir). In the ruins, the Vestige used Abolisher to tear open a planar disturbance that Leramil sensed, a portal that had been closed, which the group, consisting of the Vestige, Beragon, Leramil, and Alea, crossed to find themselves in an unknown section of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md). The group found themselves in a realm that was gradually transforming, with crystals growing from every surface. Leramil realized that Fargrave had once been part of Mirrormoor, and was simply responding to the return of its Prince.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Untraveled-19)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup>

The group was confronted by Shardmarshal Vargas, who banished Beragon and Alea to a dark and timeless mirror-reflection of reality, while Leramil barely shielded herself and the Vestige from this fate. Using the Echonir, they reached an inactive magical gate and restored it through the Lamp of Clarity by accessing a reality where it still functioned. The gate led to a sub-plane housing the Loom of the Untraveled Road, but a dead end forced the Vestige to use the Skein of Secrets, only for a trap that Ithelia had set in place to drain it, preventing the three Princes’ relics from being used against her within her own realm. With no options left, Leramil attempted to portal directly to the Loom, but Vargas diverted the spell, sending them to Ithelia’s former prison. There, Leramil realized the surrounding mirrors were actually windows into other Paths. The Vestige used the Lamp of Clarity to reopen them, restoring Beragon and Alea at the cost of the Lamp’s power. The group then used Abolisher to open a portal back to the Loom.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Untraveled-19)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vargas-18)</sup>

In the Loom, Ithelia's plan was revealed. It was a great fate-altering engine, a series of portals within portals leading to the Many Paths that would allow Ithelia to access alternate histories from within the Paths and weave them into reality. Ithelia intended to use the Loom to rewrite history so that her conflict with the Princes had never occurred, and ultimately to break the shackles of fate by giving each and every being their own desired history. Leramil explained that the Loom had been the threat Mora foresaw, as its use would not produce Ithelia's intended outcome, but rather cause reality itself to shatter in its attempt to reshape itself as Ithelia desired. The group confronted Shardmarshal Vargas while Ithelia drew in power from the Many Paths through the Loom and Torvesard helped his Prince. The Shardmarshal was defeated, and the Vestige used Abolisher to sunder the portals making up the Loom, destroying it, but also depleting Abolisher's energies in the process. An enraged Ithelia, now filled with so much power from the Many Paths it overwhelmed even her control, vanished.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Untraveled-19)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Beragon-22)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vargas-18)</sup>

Soon after, the [Watchling](Lore_Watcher.md) fate-auditor of Hermaeus Mora, Scruut, appeared, warning of terrifying disturbances in fate that would soon unravel all reality. Tracking the disturbance, the Vestige, Scruut, and Leramil, discovered a disturbing truth. Ithelia's power was causing distortions of fate so severe they were developing into reality tears, holes in the fabric of reality itself, the result of a piece of existence itself having been destroyed due to instabilities in fate. Visible as rifts in space filled with nothingness, they would shred and destroy fate threads surrounding them, and erase from existence anything unfortunate enough to touch them. The rifts would keep manifesting more and more across various locations and planes of existence, causing reality to crumble a bit further with each passing moment, multiplying and growing until all existence itself was undone, akin to pulling on a loose thread until one's whole tunic unravels.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Scruut-65)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-20)</sup>

The memory revealed Ithelia's final plan, if Hermaeus Mora wouldn't allow her to reclaim her realm of Mirrormoor, then she would simply take Apocrypha from him and rebuild Mirrormoor upon its ruins. The Vestige then left for [Ceyond](Lore_Ceyond.md), to retrieve a shard from one of the first Daedra Ithelia had made, the Primordial Shattered Titan. The Vestige retrieved the shard but, in the process, they were confronted by Torvesard, who used a reality tear in an attempt to erase them from existence. The Vestige was pushed out of the way by Curate Gadayn, who was erased instead.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Scruut-65)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-20)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup>

### Forgotten Legacy
With Ithelia gone, the tears in reality were repaired through use of a spell originating from Hermaeus Mora himself. The spell was cast at nexus points in reality, locations which naturally resonate throughout the realms, allowing the energies opposite to the force that created the tears to be inserted into the fabric of reality and resonate across Aurbis, beginning the restoration of all damage to fate, though completion of the process would take some time. The energy that counteracted the damage came from the relics of the three Princes, Abolisher, the Lamp of Clarity, and the Skein of Secrets, which permanently lost their Daedric power and were rendered mundane objects.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Scruut-65)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-In Memory Of Quest-66)</sup>

Following Ithelia's departure, Hermaeus Mora cast his spell, and Ithelia's memory was removed from every mind across all reality once more, replaced only by recollections of Torvesard's scheme with Vaermina and Peryite, and the Recollection cult. Mirrormoor itself would be remembered, but only as a shattered ruin, whose inhabitants were masterless. Hermaeus Mora and the Vestige, who had earned the Prince's trust, would be the only ones to remember the truth, the last reminder of the Prince of Paths and of a world that might've been. However, though Ithelia was gone, seemingly forever, when questioned on the matter, Mora conceded that even he could not always see the final path the future would take.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-In Memory Of-67)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-In Memory Of Quest-66)</sup>

## Worship

### Ayleids
The Ayleids of Essondul and [Elenglynn](Lore_Elenglynn.md) worshipped Ithelia as the Prince of Paths.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-WTGS-68)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup> The Ayleids of the city of [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md) also revered Ithelia, and it was in that city that Ithelia and her defenders made their last stand against the other Princes.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Tribune-58)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Nantharion-57)</sup> Ayleid worshippers of Ithelia believed that they had to enact violence against [Man](Lore_Man.md), so that their actions would usher in a [Numinous](Lore_Hero.md) [Paravant](Lore_Alessia.md) that would usher in Ithelia's goal of cleaving through everything to break the shackles of fate and reach Numancia.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TNC-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup> The Temple of [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md) in [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) was built atop the ruins of an ancient Ayleid temple, which was originally dedicated to another divinity. Scholars have speculated that the divinity in question might have been an unknown Daedric Prince, as the symbols and iconography found at the site did not match any of the known Daedric Princes.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOJ-7)</sup>

Some Bosmeri scholars of the Second Era speculated that the Ayleids, may have worshipped another deity forgotten to the history. Vague and frantic inscriptions and imagery, are associated with her, conveying a sense of urgency and confusion. They suggested that the Ayleids were grappling to remember a forgotten deity or perhaps an individual central to their worship. One inscription alluded to the idea of setting someone free—possibly a slave or the deity themselves—but the details remain unclear. Some theorize that this figure was once venerated by the Ayleids before being taken away, lost to both time and the world.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gandrinar-8)</sup>

### Bosmer
The [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md) was a Bosmer cult dedicated to Ithelia. They felt a certain emptiness plaguing Nirn, an "aching hole" where something should be, but nothing is.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-FN-37)</sup> They sought to fix it by recovering "what was forgotten" and restoring Ithelia.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-NJ-38)</sup>

### Blind Path
The Blind Path were a cult that served the Blind, a powerful Daedric servant of Ithelia. After undergoing the trials for membership, all members would don blindfolds and never remove them.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-vault-42)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ILS-43)</sup>

Places that were part of [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md) are said to be fundamentally connected to the Loom, and [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) used in such locations will have their power drained by the Loom as it gains power. Through the Loom Ithelia can also draw immense amounts of power from the Many Paths, though the untold amounts of energy ultimately overwhelm her, transforming her into the Last Tomorrow, the threat Hermaeus Mora foresaw. Even after being destroyed through the use of [Boethiah's](Lore_Boethiah.md) Blade, [Abolisher](Lore_Abolisher.md), the Loom still radiates power, preventing approach through [portal magic](Lore_Portals.md) and making the area around the Loom itself unstable, threatening anyone lingering there with the possibility of becoming lost in the Many Paths.

### The Staff of Many Paths
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/ON-trailer-Gold_Road.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Gold_Road.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Gold_Road.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Gold_Road.jpg) The Staff of Many Paths with fate threads swirling around the top The [Staff of Many Paths](Lore_Staff_of_Many_Paths.md) was a powerful [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) named for the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Ithelia and filled with her power. It had the ability to drain, hold, and imbue beings and objects with energy.

The Staff was long guarded by the Oathsworn, followers of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) within their training ground of the Oathsworn Pit, until it was stolen by the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), a [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) cult that revered Ithelia. It is said that around the Staff strange things would often happen, weapons going missing, or initiates somehow thrashing a veteran trainer during their first lessons, causing the relic to be locked away. The Recollection would use the Staff to imbue the [Greenspring Seeds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenspring_Seeds) which [Greenspeakers](Lore_Greenspeakers.md) normally use to grow new sections of forest with [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) energies that strengthened and corrupted the [magic](Lore_Magic.md), creating Wildburn Seeds which they used to cause a thick jungle to cover much of the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) overnight, and to create the [Wildburn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn), a magical corruption of the land that mutated living beings. The leader of the Recollection, King [Nantharion](Lore_Nantharion.md), attempted to use the Staff to create a new Wildburn Seed, but the interruption of the ritual by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) caused the Seed's energies to be released prematurely, destroying the Recollection's living fortress of [Hoperoot](Lore_Hoperoot.md).

At [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md) Nantharion attempted to use the Staff to drain the energies of the great [Ayleid Well](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md) in order to restore Ithelia to her ancient power and create a Wildburn that'd cover all of the West Weald. Though Nantharion was slain, the servants of Ithelia retrieved the Staff and channeled the Well's power into her, restoring her ancient might. The Staff was last seen during Ithelia's attempted takeover of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), wielded by [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md), who used the artifact to drain the power of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) and imbue Ithelia with it. After Ithelia was granted clarity by the Mirror of Truth and decided to put an end to her plans, a desperate Torvesard turned the Staff on his Prince, absorbing the combined power of both Ithelia and Hermaeus Mora into himself and imprisoning both. Torvesard used the Staff to channel his power in battle. After Torvesard's defeat the Staff vanished in a flash of light and the stolen powers were returned to their respective Princes, the artifact's fate is not known.

## Ithelia and Fate
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Face_Marking.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Face_Marking.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Face_Marking.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Face_Marking.jpg) Markings resembling those of Ithelia, found on those touched by Mirrormoor's power over fate's threads As Mistress of the Untraveled Road, Ithelia wields the power to unravel fate and rearrange it to her will, shaping destiny itself. Even her mere presence can tangle fate’s threads or draw echoes of other realities into her own, trapping them between worlds. When fully in command of her power, existence and fate can be bent to accommodate her choices. From her seat in Mirrormoor, when utilizing the Loom of the Untraveled Road, Ithelia can rewrite history, undo mistakes, unlock unrealized possibilities, replace reality with alternate timelines drawn from the Many Paths, or potentially even grant every being a history shaped entirely by their desires. This power is dangerous, however. Its use can create anomalies such as fate knots, or become even worse if they transform into reality tears, which can threaten existence itself. If sufficiently enraged, Ithelia can unravel reality around her, shredding existence until nothing remains. Though reality strives to conform to her will, it may shatter in the attempt to do so. For this potential to endanger reality, either willingly or as a side effect, Ithelia was given the title the Last Tomorrow, a prophesied destroyer of reality and a threat to the pillars of causality as foretold by Hermaeus Mora. Due to the nature of her power, Ithelia's presence is said to untether all consequences, causing fate's threads in her immediate surroundings to seemingly vanish and so effectively disabling the fate sight of those who can perceive them.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup>

As a Star-Orphan of the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) before becoming a Daedric Prince, Ithelia was the White Star and Queen of Fates Unwritten. Additionally, she is said to will that a [Prisoner Unbound](Lore_Hero.md), beings that can forge their own fate, always exist.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TNC-3)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Body_Marking.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Body_Marking.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Body_Marking.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Memory_Body_Marking.jpg) Markings resembling those of Ithelia, found on those touched by Mirrormoor's power over fate's threads As a Daedric Prince, after Ithelia used her power to alter fate to avert a coming catastrophe, Mora examined her fate threads and perceived that Ithelia's power had already caused irreparable damage to the fabric of fate and that, if allowed to continue, her very existence endangered all of reality. Mora's fears intensified when Ithelia created her [Loom of the Untraveled Road](Lore_Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road.md), a great fate altering engine via which she intended to tap into the power of the Many Paths and give every being their own desired history, breaking the shackles of fate. Believing that Ithelia's plans would result in the end of reality, Mora repeatedly confronted her to demand she stop, but she refused to listen. Because of this, Mora, Azura, Boethiah, and Mephala, warred against Ithelia and imprisoned her within the Mythos, the core of Apocrypha, after which Mora erased all memory of her existence from reality.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Rakzzin-jo-69)</sup>

The [Staff of Many Paths](Lore_Staff_of_Many_Paths.md) was a powerful artifact associated with Ithelia, capable of causing strange phenomena such as a novice soundly defeating an expert warrior or objects to disappear to take place around it. The Staff also possessed the ability to drain, hold, and imbue beings and objects with immense amounts of energy. After Torvesard, its last known wielder, was defeated, the artifact vanished in a flash of light and its fate is unknown.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vargas-18)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Seeds Suspicion-56)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Gambit-25)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Fate Forgotten Prince-10)</sup>

## Ithelia and the Many Paths
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-npc-Ithelia_03.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ithelia_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_03.jpg) An alternate Ithelia [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/ON-npc-Ithelia_04.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ithelia_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_04.jpg) A different alternate Ithelia As the Prince of Paths, Ithelia can perceive all the Many Paths at once. Though she can't outright control the Many Paths, she can travel between them, or follow their threads and reach out to alter their fates. All alternate Ithelias across the Many Paths are connected to one another, a connection strengthened when Ithelia imbibes the Sage's Dream, a brew formed from the essence of many of her "reflections" from different Paths, which allows her to incorporate knowledge and power from them. Ithelia can also share her perception of the Many Paths with others, allowing them to briefly view the Many Paths as she herself does.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup>

Through her power to influence the Many Paths, Ithelia is said to be able to reshape the fates. Using her Loom of the Untraveled Road, a series of portals within portals leading to the Many Paths, it is said Ithelia can access alternate fates and histories and weave them into reality to replace the history that exists. Ithelia's ultimate goal prior to her imprisonment was to use the Loom to give each being their own desired history and so break fate's shackles entirely.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vargas-18)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup> Fearing that Ithelia's unnatural manipulations, which had already caused the Many Paths to splinter, would lead to the end of reality, Hermaeus Mora and three allied Daedric Princes took action to imprison her.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup>

## Mirrormoor
*I find peace and soul's release when casting on the 'moor. With oiled skin, reflections spin, mirror's grace implored. A shimmer here, a glimmer there, my form reflects the light. Forever lost, forever found, mirror's fated knight.*
— *Lost in Mirrormoor*<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Metamorphosis-70)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) The Loom of the Untraveled Road in Mirrormoor [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md) was the realm of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Ithelia, Mistress of the Untraveled Road. [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md) is a fragment of this forgotten realm.

Mirrormoor is a large desert wasteland filled with gargantuan skeletons and surrounded by an auroral sky. While Ithelia is active, it is filled by shattered mirrors and crystalline structures and the sky is fractured by cracks. Those elements were not present in Fargrave before Ithelia's return. Some stories have described a city of crystal buildings and many-hued courts.

After Ithelia was exiled to a different reality, Mirrormoor and its denizens remained known, but Ithelia was erased from everyone's memory once more by Hermaeus Mora, her plane of Mirrormoor remaining as a shattered ruin, whose inhabitants are masterless. <sup>[[64]](#cite_note-In Memory Of-67)</sup> Despite her banishment, the power of Ithelia and Mirrormoor continued to emanate across the threads of fate, marking some with its forbidden knowledge and leaving some among those who visited the realm changed. It is said that those touched in this manner could not recall who or what had changed them, retaining only dim memories of the event, like shards of glass scattered on the ground.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Memory Body-71)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Memory Face-72)</sup>

## Servants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/ON-concept-Shardborn_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Shardborn_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Shardborn_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Shardborn_Style.jpg) Clan Shardborn Armor and Weapons [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/ON-concept-Lucent_Sentinel_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Lucent_Sentinel_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Lucent_Sentinel_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Lucent_Sentinel_Style.jpg) Lucent Sentinel Armor and Weapons The [Shardborn](Lore_Shardborn.md) are the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) servants of Ithelia.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-11)</sup> For milennia, the Dremora [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md) was Ithelia's last remaining servant, having had just enough of his memory preserved to come and save her.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup> Ithelia has many glass-like crystalline servants in her service, they often took more familiar forms such as dragons, mantikoras, mind terrors, and hollow sentinels.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-11)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-TFV-73)</sup> Among her more unique servants are [Crystal Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crystal_Atronach), [Crystaljacks](Lore_Crystaljack.md), [Fractured Remnants](Lore_Fractured_Remnant.md), [Mirrorplasms](Lore_Mirrorplasm.md), [Shattered Shards](Lore_Shattered_Shard.md), and [Skeletal Shards](Lore_Skeletal_Shard.md).

[Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md) is a [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) composed of living glass like these servants, who was responsible for invading the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-LAIA-48)</sup> The Blind was a glass-like large Dremora servant of Ithelia who invaded the Bedlam Veil.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ILS-43)</sup>

Ithelia's Daedric servants, the Unseen, such as the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) Torvesard, also possess a degree of ability to manipulate the strands of fate. This allows them to accomplish tasks such as entering the [Eyes of Mora](Lore_Knower%27s_Eye.md) without the corresponding key and even to alter the memories of the Prince contained there, thereby rewriting the event depicted itself. In addition this ability to influence fate has been used against enemies, be it to banish them to the Many Paths, casting them through both physical distance and realities, or to use reality tears to erase them from existence. They are not visible to the gaze of other Daedric Princes, even that of Hermaeus Mora, which is why they're called the Unseen, but their presence can be felt indirectly through the distortion they cause in fate's threads.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mora-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Leramil-9)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Vargas-18)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTorvesard-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup>

Because of the ability they and their Prince possess to influence fate, the Daedra of Mirrormoor believe that there is no fate that can't be altered. To reflect this belief, they often wear designs of glimmering threads upon their skin, to remind themselves that one's fate and future are one's own making.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Face-74)</sup> Mirrormoor Daedra believed themselves to be the owners of the [Arcane Knot](Lore_Arcane_Knot.md), a dangerous mystical object said to possess the power to connect the different realities, the Many Paths, to one another, treating them as if they'd never been separate to begin with, and even allowing one to cross over from one to the next. The Knot was long held within the Lucent Citadel in [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), until a group of adventurers retrieved it for the Scribes of Mora, having defeated the Mirrormoor Daedra attempting to reclaim it.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Keshargo-75)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Neletai-76)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Scribe Nairoline-77)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Knot Awaits-78)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^1](#note_nb1) | *[The Nine Coruscations](Lore_The_Nine_Coruscations.md)*, a religious text of Ayleid origins, mentions the "Numinous Paravant", a being with "unbound hands", and thus the potential to achieve the goal of Ithelia to reach Numancia, breaking the shackles of fate and giving everyone their own desired history. Ithelia's Ayleid worshippers seemed to believe they could somehow cause the Numinous Paravant to appear by committing violence against Man.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TNC-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup> The Numinous Paravant might've been [Saint Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), who rebelled against the Ayleids, and one of whose titles is "Paravant".<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TA-79)</sup> |

- [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) referred to Fargrave as "a shattered ruin of reflection and sorrow", making it possible that he might have known some elements of the realm's past.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-LA-82)</sup>
- In *[Betrayal of the Second Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Betrayal:Betrayal_of_the_Second_Era)*, the Bosmer [Deslandra](Lore_Deslandra.md) attempted to enact an event known as the Great Nullification, which would forever sever the flow of [magicka](Lore_Magic.md) and its influences on [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-83)</sup><sup>:52</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-84)</sup><sup>:50</sup><sup>[[82]](#cite_note-VWGazetteer-85)</sup><sup>:38</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-86)</sup><sup>:19</sup> In some playthroughs where the heroes are defeated, she can succeed, leaving one of those failed playthroughs as a potential reality Ithelia could have exiled herself to.
- The ultimate goal of Ithelia, a former Magna Ge, is described in the [The Nine Coruscations](Lore_Nine_Coruscations.md), an Ayleid religious text, as cleaving through everything to reach "Numancia", the breaking of the shackles of [fate](Lore_Fate.md) and the limitations of linear time imposed by [Ada-Mantia](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md), to give every being their own desired history.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TNC-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ithelia-16)</sup> In the [Mythic Dawn Commentaries](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md), the term "Nu-Mantia" is used by [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) as an exclamation associated with "liberty" and the "promise of paradise", the goal of the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md). As Mehrunes Dagon, the Mythic Dawn's patron deity, is said in the same text to have been created by the Magna Ge, the two concepts might be related.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-MDC-87)</sup>
- [Wandering Spirits](Lore_The_Wandering_Spirits.md) recounts the tale of [Akha](Lore_Akha.md), who vanished in the South, with [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md), who has himself been described as both weaver and embodiment of time's all-encompassing tapestry,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-88)</sup> appearing in his place, and bearing his crown and rule of his kingdoms across the Many Paths. This event might be the same as the disappearance of Aka linked to the afixing of Ada-Mantia that Ithelia is said to have witnessed, from a different cultural perspective.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-TWS-89)</sup>
- Ithelia's claim that Daedra are "unchanging" flies in the face of [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s claim that *"[e]verything changes. Even Daedric Princes. Especially Daedric Princes."*<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-SISheogorath-90)</sup> Given Sheogorath's madness, it is impossible to tell whether this was a statement of fact or just a reference to his transformation into [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md).
