## History
During the time of the imperatum saliache, unconfirmed legends associate dark Garlas Agea with the worship of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-3)</sup> which is corroborated by the statuettes of him found in the ruins.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup> The meaning behind its name alludes to the dark, horrific, and perhaps better forgotten secrets of the Ayleids.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loading Screen-1)</sup> It later fell into ruin after the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), as the burgeoning [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) toppled the remaining Ayleid cities of Cyrodiil one by one.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/ff/ON-load-Garlas_Agea.jpg/200px-ON-load-Garlas_Agea.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Garlas_Agea.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Garlas_Agea.jpg) Ruins of Garlas Agea In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Gold Coast Trading Company](Lore_Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.md) set up covert operations within the ruins for various questionable activities. It did not prove to be an easy task as the ruin was booby-trapped with giant swinging [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) blades and gas traps, which killed many of the workers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GACL-5)</sup> They began using the halls as a place to house dangerous and exotic creatures smuggled from [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), such as [welwas](Lore_Welwa.md) and [duneracers](Lore_Duneracer.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCG-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Laronen-7)</sup> One of the smuggled creatures, an armored [troll](Lore_Troll.md) named Ironfang, was the target of a bounty set up by Laronen, a member of a rival trading guild.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCG-6)</sup> The company was transporting the greatest looted treasures of the fallen [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) out of the ruins through the western entrance. The [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) merchant running it was assassinated by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). This was done in relation to [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) diplomat Dathus Ildram, who was posing as a high-ranking Hlaalu negotiator and was later tracked down and killed by [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-NJ:GC-8)</sup>

Laronen hired an adventurer to kill Ironfang and obtain the Gold Coast Company's ledgers as leverage.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCG-6)</sup> Captain Jean Apinia, a former member of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) known for her war crimes in [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md), hid out in the ruins with the valuables she and her soldiers stole.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Raz-9)</sup> She was tracked down and killed by [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md) with the help of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Do W-10)</sup> [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md) were also known to congregate around the exterior of the ruin's two entrances, one of which had been gated off by the company for use of cargo transport.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruin had fallen further into disrepair, with many of its arches broken and its statue of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) crumbling. A group of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) came to inhabit the ruins, and there were recently placed offerings to the shrine of Molag Bal inside.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup>
