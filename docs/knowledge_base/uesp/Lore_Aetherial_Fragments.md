# Aetherial Fragments

This article is about the material. For the book, see [Aetherial Fragments (book)](Lore_Aetherial_Fragments_(book).md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/OB-misc-Meteoric_Glass.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Meteoric_Glass.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Meteoric_Glass.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Meteoric_Glass.jpg) Samples of Meteoric Glass Aetherial Fragments are pieces of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) which become dislodged and fall to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) from the heavens. These are commonly observed by the people of Tamriel as 'shooting stars'. These otherworldly materials are known for their [magical](Lore_Magic.md) power, and have often been utilized to great effect by those who manage to recover these fragments. Most notably, the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) made significant use of Aetherial fragments due to their reverence for starlight.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

Crystalline residue found on ancient [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) prayer lamps seems to indicate that worshippers of [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md) used some kind of [meteoric](Lore_Meteors.md) fuel to generate light resembling starlight.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

## Aetherium
[Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md) is an incredibly rare, luminescent blue crystal found deep underground. The crystal has a strong magical aura, but it is alchemically inert and harmonically volatile, and no known conventional process can enchant, smelt, mold, bind, or break it. It was discovered by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in their deepest mines some time in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and is the only known location where it is found. Based on its name, the crystal may have some connection to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) and the Aetherial Fragments which sometimes fall to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), such as the [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md) harnessed by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md).

The Aetherium is embedded in [Gold](Lore_Gold.md) and [Dwarven Metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md) to create Aetherial artifacts. Other materials such as [Malachite](Lore_Malachite.md), [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and [Sapphire](Lore_Sapphire.md) are also used. Crowns, shields and [staffs](Lore_Staff.md) can all be crafted with Aetherium. The artifacts have unique and powerful enchantments, such as the ability to turn enemies ethereal, summon [Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md), or harness the power of the [Standing Stones](Lore_Standing_Stones.md). An aetheric lodestone is a perfectly round orb that is highly magnetic and threaded with veins of glowing blue Aetherium.. Certain [Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md#Architon) are imbued with Aetherium crystals.

## Culanda Stone
[Culanda Stones](Lore_Culanda_Stone.md) are luminescent golden crystals used by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and usually only found on the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). The stones are pieces of cut and enchanted [Meteoric Glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md), a rare form of Aetherial Fragment. Alongside [Malondo Stones](Lore_Malondo_Stone.md), they are an attempt at creating magical crystals similar to the [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) and [Varla Stones](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) produced by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md).

Culanda Stones provide a bright golden light and are therefore mostly used as a light source. They can also trigger magical effects or store magicka, but are always destroyed upon depletion, similar to Ayleid stones. Some scholars insist that the Culanda Stone improves upon Ayleid methods and can be cultivated like the Welkynd Stones once were, although others believe the Altmer simply discovered a more reliable method of recharging the original Ayleid stones. They are commonly seen on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), in the ruins built by the [Ancients](Lore_Ancients.md). Culanda Lacquer is used to create armor and clothing for the [College of Sapiarchs](Lore_College_of_Sapiarchs.md).

## Jewel of Fire
The [Jewel of Fire](Lore_Jewel_of_Fire.md) was a rare gem that was said to be a [fallen star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherial_Fragment) which still glowed with an inner flame. The Jewel played a large role in the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) used the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) to imprison [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), as well as killing his apprentice [Ria Silmane](Lore_Ria_Silmane.md) so word of his betrayal would not reach the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md). To ensure no one could free the Emperor, Tharn attempted to destroy the staff. However, as the Staff of Chaos was indestructible, it was instead split into eight pieces which he then hid in dangerous locations throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). As a final failsafe in the event that the staff was reassembled, Tharn rendered it useless by draining the magic energy of the Staff into the Jewel of Fire, which was in turn hidden beneath the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md).

## Magna-Geode
[Magna-Geodes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna-Geode) are glowing, roughly oval-shaped crystals of varied sizes with a polyhedral structure, typically golden in color. They bear a resemblance in appearance to fragments of [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md).

They are commonly found in places of worship dedicated to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of [Light](Lore_Elements.md#Light), the fallen [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). One such location where multiple Magna-Geodes have been found was the [ruined](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) settlement of [Garlas Malatar](Lore_Garlas_Malatar.md). Another notable location was the [Kilkreath Temple](Lore_Kilkreath_Temple.md), located on [Mount Kilkreath](Lore_Mount_Kilkreath.md) and built in the ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) architectural style. It housed a particularly special magna-geode.

Two of Meridia's artifacts, her [Beacon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia%27s_Beacon), and [Brilliance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia%27s_Brilliance), are shaped like magna-geodes. The Starry Globe was a Magna-Geode stored in the realm of [Deadlight](Lore_Deadlight.md), and it revealed a memory where [Celdina](Lore_Celdina.md) shared her philosophy on [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) with her daughter [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md).

## Malondo Stone
[Malondo Stones](Lore_Malondo_Stone.md) are golden crystals used by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and usually only found on the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). The stones are pieces of cut and enchanted [Meteoric Glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md), a rare form of Aetherial Fragment. Alongside [Culanda Stones](Lore_Culanda_Stone.md), they are an attempt at creating magical crystals similar to the [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) and [Varla Stones](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) produced by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md). Unlike the Ayleid stones, which crumble to dust when harnessed by modern mages, Malondo Stones can be recharged and can be tapped by spellcasters as a source of magicka replenishment or to restore charge to enchanted items. Some scholars insist that the Malondo Stone improves upon Ayleid methods and can be cultivated like the Welkynd Stones once were, although others believe the Altmer simply discovered a more reliable method of recharging the original Ayleid stones. Vitrified Malondo is used to create armor and clothing for the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). Altmeri artisans were known to craft cameos with likeness of people carved into the glowing malondo stones. One of known people portrayed that way was Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md). They were a royal gift for the High Elf youth. Royal Battlereeve's Command Batons were crafted from yellow sunwood and crowned with a radiant Malondo Stone.

## Meteoric Glass
[Meteoric Glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md) (also called **Aetherial Crystals**) is a variety of [Aetherial Fragment](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherial_Fragment). Compared to the more common [meteoric iron](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md), it rarely falls to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). The glass is crystalline and comes in various colors. It is prized for being magical in nature and highly enchantable.

The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), known for harnessing Aetherial Fragments, used the glass to create artifacts like [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) and [Varla Stones](Lore_Varla_Stone.md). The crystals were also used as a source of light in Ayleid settlements, likely due to their abhorrence of fire, which they saw as an impure and primal form of light. [Skyshards](Lore_Skyshard.md) were believed to be shards of Aetherial magicka that carry the essence of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). They are sometimes linked to [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) or [Anu](Lore_Anu.md). [Vakka Stones](Lore_Vakka_Stone.md) are a type of meteoric glass found in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md), while [Culanda](Lore_Culanda_Stone.md) and [Malondo Stones](Lore_Malondo_Stone.md) are a variety of meteoric glass used by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md). The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) use beads of meteoric glass in their jewelry crafting, harnessing the arcane potential of the glass using an unknown crafting technique.

When properly cultivated, meteoric glass crystals are able to grow and multiply. The Ayleids were only beginning to cultivate the material when they were wiped out by the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). Naturally occurring meteoric glass is a common sight in Ayleid ruins, and can often be seen growing on the crumbling limestone walls of these underground structures. Enchanted pieces of meteoric glass, such as Welkynd Stones, can grow in the same way.

The seven [Star Teeth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md) are similar in nature to meteoric glass, but it is unknown if they are made of the material. The origins of the [Jewel of Fire](Lore_Jewel_of_Fire.md) also suggest a link to the glass.

## Meteoric Iron
[Meteoric Iron](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md) is a variety of Aetherial Fragment. It is the most common fragment to fall to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). All known samples of the metal are a rusty brown in color. It is prized by armorers and enchanters for its properties in the forging of enchanted weapons and armors. The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), known for harnessing Aetherial Fragments, incorporated the metal into their architecture. As such, it is a common sight in Ayleid ruins. Meteoric Iron is resistant to most elements, and it's believed to have been used by the Akaviri [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) to forge bulwarks against the Dragons' elemental breath.

## Skyshard
[Skyshards](Lore_Skyshard.md) and **Sky Prisms** are a rare type of [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md). Sometimes called "Shards of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md)", skyshards are shards of Aetherial magicka that carry the essence of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). They are sometimes linked to [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) or [Anu](Lore_Anu.md).

Like other aetherial fragments, Sky Prisms fall to Nirn from Aetherius through the stars, although only during specific lunar alignments. As they fall, the Prisms can be seen splitting into three shards. If three skyshards are subsequently brought together, they reform into a silvery prism and confer the magical energy to any nearby being. As well as allowing mortals to become more powerful through the creation of prisms, absorbing the power of even a single skyshard allows a [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md) to be re-attuned to Anuic magic and thus return to Nirn from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).

Although usually found outdoors, skyshards are often discovered and brought underground by hoarders. The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) were known to harness skyshards in some way. They can also be summoned through ritual, even in Oblivion. Realms such as [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) have a significant number of inexplicable fallen skyshards. It is believed that Breton warriors used to incorporate skyshard slivers in the hilts of their swords.

[Space Cores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Space_Core), also known as **Space Spheres** or **Spheriphem**, are [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) native to [space](Lore_Astronomy.md), the void above [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). They are capable of speech and possess a single eye. Similar to Aetherial fragments, Space Cores occasionally fall to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), although the experience is apparently unsettling for them. Fallen Spheriphem will therefore attempt to cajole passing adventurers into helping them return to the comforting void. Space Cores can be used in [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) rituals at the Atronach Forge in place of a [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md).

## Star Teeth
[Star Teeth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md) is a term usually used to refer to a group of seven magical prismatic crystals taken from the skies by an [airship](Lore_Airships.md). The term is also used to refer to the material they are made from, which even in small shards is considered valuable. These shards have been used in things such as armor. The denizens of western [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) use star teeth decoratively to create extremely valuable studded clothing such as belts.

## Vakka Stone
[Vakka Stones](Lore_Vakka_Stone.md) (literally "Sun Stones" in the [Jel](Lore_Jel.md) language) are bright yellow crystals used by the ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to store the raw [magicka](Lore_Magicka.md) that falls from the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md). They are only known to be found in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md). The stones are pieces of cut and enchanted [Meteoric Glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md), a rare form of Aetherial Fragment, glowing with arcane power. They are similar to the [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) and [Varla Stones](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) that were used by the Ayleids, and rival that of the Kajin-Jat Crystal. Prior to [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md), some tribes of ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) were directed by their [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree to worship the [sun](Lore_Magnus.md) or even to collect its power. Known as the Sun-Blessed, they were eventually betrayed by their [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) -worshipping brethren, and the practice faded into obscurity.

## Varla Stone
A [Varla Stone](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) (Aldmeris for "star stone") is an enchanted shard of cut [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md), a rare type of Aetherial Fragment. The luminescent crystals of white or blue hue are remarkably powerful, enabling untrained users to restore magical energy to any number of enchanted items. The stones were most notably used by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), but are believed to date further back to the mid- [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) when High Lord [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md) used them drive off the native beasts of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md). Because of their great value and utility, many of these artifacts were looted from [Ayleid ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) after the [Ayleid diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md). As such, intact Varla Stones are also extremely rare, but since they are small and easily concealed, diligent explorers may still occasionally come across them. Defective variants are known as "clouded" Varla Stones, and can occur if the meteoric glass forming the stone had some flaw. Over centuries, magicka ebbing and flowing through the stone heightens the flaw, causing the glass to cloud and its magicka to radiate irregularly.

When used, a Varla Stone will crumble to dust; there is no known way of recharging or preserving the stone, although scholars believe that their magical power may have originally been maintained by the [Great Welkynd Stone](Lore_Great_Welkynd_Stone.md) of an Ayleid city. Varla Stones are usually found atop pedestals in Ayleid ruins, sometimes protected behind a remotely-opened cage. Flawed Varla Stones can be used to contain a trapped [soul](Lore_Soul.md), and can thus be used to recharge enchantments, although to a much lesser extent than the unflawed stones, roughly equivalent to that of a common [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gem.md). When used this way, they do not crumble to dust like usual.

## Welkynd Stone
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Welkynd_Stone.png) [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) (Aldmeris for "sky stone/heaven stone"; literally, "sky child") are luminescent aquamarine crystals created by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md). The stones are pieces of cut and enchanted [Meteoric Glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md), a rare form of Aetherial Fragment. The stones contain the concentrated power of Mundus and were used as a means of storing magicka. The Ayleids also used the stones as keys to magically open locks in their cities.

Magically talented individuals can draw the power from the stone to restore their own magicka. The means of restoring power to a drained stone was lost with the death of the Ayleids; without this knowledge, the stones simply crumble to dust after being used. Welkynd Stones are prized by mages, and many remain to be looted from Ayleid ruins, where they are often placed atop pedestals for illumination.

Like other types of meteoric glass, Welkynd Stones have the ability to grow and multiply if correctly cultivated. The Ayleids mastered the art of creating the crystalline structures and were beginning to cultivate them outside of their underground communities when they disappeared from history. Uncut Welkynd Stone crystals can occasionally be seen growing on the stone walls of subterranean Ayleid ruins. [Lithnilian](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Lithnilian), an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) scholar, was the first to discover the crystals growing in a natural cave. Bramblepoint Cave was located in the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md). The discovery proved that, with the proper materials, magic, and research, the Welkynd Stone could adapt to any environment.
