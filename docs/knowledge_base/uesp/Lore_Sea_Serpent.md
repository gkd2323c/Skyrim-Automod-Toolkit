[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg/200px-ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg) Sea Serpent attacking a ship in the Abecean (ESO) Sea Serpents are large [reptilian](Lore_Reptile.md) sea monsters native to [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md), capable of reaching 64 feet above the water in height.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGArchive-1)</sup> These [snakes](Lore_Snake.md) are said to be large enough to capsize warships.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASGSE-2)</sup> One story mentions a sea serpent twice the size of a war galleon coiling around such a ship and dragging its broken hull beneath the sea.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSCW-3)</sup> They are described by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) explorer [Ja'dasha](Lore_Ja%27dasha.md) as among the most horrible and dangerous creatures of the sea, along with [krakens](Lore_Kraken.md) and [Sloads](Lore_Sload.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Maps depicted serpents in the waters south of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Map-5)</sup> and the skeleton of a serpent was found in the sands of the island circa [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Redguard-6)</sup> [Reef Vipers](Lore_Reef_Viper.md), another aquatic species native to Pyandonea that are trained by the Maormer,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTanorian-7)</sup> may be related.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASGSE-2)</sup>

Sea serpents are known for their distinctive head frills.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CSL-8)</sup> They are said to twist around each other when they mate.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Serpent Veil Orgnum-9)</sup>

## Religion and Culture
Sea serpents are tamed and ridden by the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) for use as steeds and warbeasts to support their navy, and can be summoned by them during combat. It is said that this is possible through the powerful [snake magic](Lore_Snake_Magic.md) of their king, [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Fot SV-11)</sup> Maormer ships are decorated with sea serpent designs and are capable of passing through the thick kelp around Pyandonea, which serves as a well-camouflaged home for the sea serpents that are Orgnum's guards and occasional mounts.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-12)</sup> Their hide and teeth are also used by Maormer to craft their gear.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CM64P-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONSea Serpent Weapon-14)</sup> The Maormer often use serpent iconography in their weapons, armor,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CM64P-13)</sup> banners,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LCO-15)</sup> and most notably their totems.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONMaormer Totem-16)</sup> Sea serpents were used by the Maormer during the Battle of [Seaside Sanctuary](Lore_Seaside_Sanctuary.md) against the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#First_Dominion),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PE-17)</sup> and they attempted to break the wards binding a century old sea serpent in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TGS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSL-19)</sup>

It is said that sea serpents are sacred to the Maormer.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CM64P-13)</sup> The Maormer are known to have made shrines to a serpent god, with some theorizing these to be representations of Orgnum,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MSS-20)</sup> who is believed by some to be the [Serpent God](Lore_Satakal.md) worshipped by the [Satakal](Lore_Satakals.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-10)</sup> and who fanatical Maormer such as Serpent Caller [Vinsha](Lore_Vinsha.md) had seen in visions.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DFO-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SCV-22)</sup> There are rumors among the people of Tamriel that [Maormer Leviathans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maormer_Leviathan) are the spawn of some unholy and sorcerous coupling of Sea Elf and Sea Serpent.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TSE-23)</sup>

In the writings of [Azadiyeh](Lore_Azadiyeh.md), the Yokudan god Satakal is described as rising from the "starry depths" to pull Yokuda beneath the waves.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-THo S-24)</sup> Satakal is elsewhere in Yokudan mythology said to cyclically eat the world only for it to be reborn again.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-KS-25)</sup> A traditional Redguard maxim speaks of a "serpent made of the sea below".<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-KS-25)</sup> A statue in Hews Bane of a woman with a serpent wrapped around her was theorized to be the Yokudan queen [Hazadiyya Sea-Queen](Lore_Hazadiyya_Sea-Queen.md) surrounded by a sea serpent,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-LICQuen-26)</sup> and was also theorized to be a depiction of the water and rain goddess [Zeqqi](Lore_Zeqqi.md), daughter of the Yokudan god [Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LICWalks-27)</sup>

In writings associated with the Atmoran religious group the People of the Serpent, the Serpent is described variously as a god of the sea and as the sea itself.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TACW-28)</sup> Multiple scholars have theorized that the Cyrodiilic god Arkay was a fusion between the elven Xarxes and the Atmoran death god [Orkey](Lore_Orkey.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TAX-29)</sup> and devotees of the later Nordic pantheon revered Orkey as a snake.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TSo G-30)</sup> Whether his associations with the oceans survived is unknown. The Nords of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), the [Haefriding](Lore_Haefriding.md), believe that during their voyage to the island, their ship became trapped in an unnaturally long night, caused by a giant snake named Sumodah<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Pft14-31)</sup> blocking out the sun. It is said that the hero Njor the Brave dreamed of a fox, whose tail curved back into [a bow](Lore_Bow_of_the_Fox.md) and its whiskers lengthened to arrows, and Njor fired a shot from Sovngarde into the snake's heart. When the rest of his crew came to look for him, Njor was gone.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TFo SS-32)</sup>

The followers of Syrabane fashioned bows in the shape of sea serpents.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-cm Syrabane-33)</sup>
