# Transport


## Walking, Running, Strafing and Sprinting
**Walking** is the slowest speed at which you will normally move. To walk, use the [movement control](Skyrim_Controls.md#Movement) appropriate for your platform. You cannot move faster than at a walking pace while you are [overencumbered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Encumbrance).

You will generally spend most of your time moving at a moderate **run**; running uses no [stamina](Skyrim_Stamina.md). You can also **strafe** or sidestep using the left or right "move" commands.

**Sprint** by holding the sprint control key (or toggle on/off for the [Special Edition](Skyrim_Special_Edition.md)). Sprinting is significantly faster than running. As it consumes stamina, you can only sprint for a limited distance. You will generally exhaust your stamina more quickly when sprinting in heavy armor or when wielding a heavy weapon.

Walking or running backwards is slower than going forwards, and you cannot sprint backwards or sideways.

Movement speed is decreased by a percentage equal to the total weight of currently equipped weapons and gear multiplied by 3/23, with a maximum penalty of 15% at or above 115 weight equipped. This penalty can be mitigated by taking the [Steed Stone](Skyrim_The_Steed_Stone.md), the Conditioning perk from [Heavy Armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md), or the Unhindered perk from [Light Armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md).

## Dragon Riding<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
As part of the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) DLC, you can ride a [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md) once you have acquired all three words of the [Bend Will](Skyrim_Bend_Will.md) shout. For more information see [Dragon Riding](Skyrim_Dragon_Riding.md).

As you cannot steer a dragon while riding it, the main functionality of this method of transportation is for [fast travel](#Fast_Travel).

## Horse Riding
You can travel much faster on horseback than on foot. You can also ride a horse while overencumbered for normal or fast travel. For complete information see [Horses](Skyrim_Horses.md).

## Carriage
Carriages will carry you between the nine capital cities of each [hold](Skyrim_Holds.md) in Skyrim for 20–50 gold. They are the fastest means to get to cities which you have not yet discovered - although in the plain game, they are only available in the five major hold capitals: the four smaller hold capitals can be the destination but not the origin of a carriage journey.

The fare to the five larger hold capitals of [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md), [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md), and [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) costs 20 gold, whereas going to the four smaller hold capitals of [Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md), [Falkreath](Skyrim_Falkreath.md), [Morthal](Skyrim_Morthal.md), and [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold.md) costs 50 gold.

With the [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md), homestead carriages also provide transport from the three homesteads to [Darkwater Crossing](Skyrim_Darkwater_Crossing.md), [Dragon Bridge](Skyrim_Dragon_Bridge.md), [Ivarstead](Skyrim_Ivarstead.md), [Karthwasten](Skyrim_Karthwasten.md), [Kynesgrove](Skyrim_Kynesgrove.md), [Old Hroldan Inn](Skyrim_Old_Hroldan_Inn.md), [Riverwood](Skyrim_Riverwood.md), [Rorikstead](Skyrim_Rorikstead.md), [Shor's Stone](Skyrim_Shor%27s_Stone.md), and [Stonehills](Skyrim_Stonehills.md). There is no charge for these services beyond the initial cost of hiring the carriage driver.

You will always use the seat on the left side of the carriage when boarding, no matter which side you board from.

There are none to be found on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>.

When first arriving at one of the five major hold capitals, you can speak with the carriage operator for some local information.

## Ferrymen<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>
Three ferrymen are added by the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md) and can transport you between three coastal cities for 50 gold, or to [Icewater Jetty](Skyrim_Icewater_Jetty.md) for 500 gold. You can also take a self-service ferry between Icewater Jetty and [Castle Volkihar](Skyrim_Castle_Volkihar.md) for free.

With the [Bittercup](Skyrim_Bittercup.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md), you may have to ask Harlaug to bring you to the island of [Giant's Tooth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Giant%27s_Tooth). He will continue to offer transport to the remote island for 50 gold.

The ferry drivers are:

- [Gort](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gort) ([Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md))
- [Harlaug](Skyrim_Harlaug.md) ([Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md))
- [Jolf](Skyrim_Jolf.md) ([Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md))

### Dead Man's Dread<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Dead_Man%27s_Dread.md)</sup>
With the [Dead Man's Dread](Skyrim_Dead_Man%27s_Dread.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md), you will have the opportunity to acquire the *[Dead Man's Dread](Skyrim_Dead_Man%27s_Dread_(place).md)*, an ancient pirate ship that serves as a [player home](Skyrim_Houses.md). This mode of travel is one-way.

## Fast Travel
You can fast travel from any exterior location to any location marked on your in-game [map](Skyrim_Maps.md) and which you have discovered (visited). Discovered locations are indicated on your map or compass with light-colored markers. Dark-colored markers, on the other hand, are places you have had marked on your map but have not yet discovered yourself, and therefore are not available for fast travel. Although fast travel transports you "instantly", a more "realistic" amount of game time will have passed upon arrival; however, such elapsed travel times are the same whether on foot or horseback. The further the distance, the more time that will pass before you arrive. You can also fast travel via the "Show On Map" function via your quest journal.

The forest section of [Frostmere Crypt](Skyrim_Frostmere_Crypt.md) is considered outdoors, therefore you can fast travel from there.

With the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) expansion, you can fast travel between Skyrim and [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md) after your first visit to the island. Fast traveling while [riding a dragon](Skyrim_Dragon_Riding.md) transports you almost instantly in game time.

You cannot fast travel when near any hostile enemies, when overencumbered (unless you are on a horse), nor when falling, jumping, or during the animation of mounting a horse. You also cannot fast travel from [Giant's Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Giant%27s_Grove) or [Skuldafn](Skyrim_Skuldafn.md), despite them being outdoor places. Fast travel is totally disabled when playing in [Survival Mode](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md), except while riding a dragon.

- Fast traveling from a snowy location can cause it to snow in areas that don't normally experience snowy weather conditions. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Rarely, fast traveling can cause the world to appear and be stuck in an underwater-like state. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.1130, fixes this bug.
