# Slavery

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/LG-cardart-Dres_Tormentor.png/200px-LG-cardart-Dres_Tormentor.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dres_Tormentor.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dres_Tormentor.png) A slaver lashes an Argonian slave on a Dres plantation *It is the right and duty of all sentient beings to be masters of their own fate, to live or die according to their own wishes. Slavery is a foul practice that takes this choice from those bound under its terrible yoke, transferring their decision-making capacity to the owner.
This is neither fair nor right, and should be resisted as violently as possible, everywhere and at all times. Anyone who stands idly by and lets another be enslaved is no better than the one who cracks the whip.*
— *[A Free Argonian's Manifesto](Lore_A_Free_Argonian%27s_Manifesto.md)* Slavery is the practice of owning an individual as property. It was once widespread throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), ranging from the practice of using defeated enemies as slaves, [selling one's own children](Lore_The_Argonian_Account,_Book_4.md) to combat hunger, or invading land specifically to capture slaves, as the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) long did to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The enslavement of [men](Lore_Men.md), [Orcs](Lore_Orsimer.md), and [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) by [elven](Lore_Mer.md) [races](Lore_Races.md) was traditionally permitted.

## Origins
Slavery has existed on Tamriel since the [Early Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Early_Merethic_Era), when the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) arrived on the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and enslaved the native [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AE12-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONToothmaul-2)</sup> The slave trade has existed since at least the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) grandmaster [Thalthil Dres](Lore_Thalthil_Dres.md)' raid on [Thorn](Lore_Thorn.md) secured the trade for his house.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Thorn-4)</sup> [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) slavetraders plagued Tamriel by the hundreds in the First Era, but they were destroyed by the machinations of folk-hero [Lyrisius](Lore_Lyrisius.md) and have not threatened Tamriel since.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSo L-5)</sup>

## Slavery by Practitioning Race

### Altmer
The [High Elves](Lore_Altmer.md) are known for enslaving goblins,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> using them for menial tasks such as mucking out dung pits and cleaning floors. Often being beaten by their elven owners, this has been known to give the goblins forms of mental trauma with escaped goblins still sweeping and being frightened of others.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-byz-7)</sup> Goblins have also been used traditionally by the Altmer to supplement their armies.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FH-8)</sup> They use other [goblin-ken](Lore_Goblin-ken.md) as slaves as well, such as [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) and [gremlins](Lore_Gremlin.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1:WR-9)</sup> The High Elves have also been noted to enslave members of other elven races, such as Orcs and the Dunmer Ulvul Llaren.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1:WR-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

### Argonian
The [Archeins](Lore_Archein.md) are a tribe of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) infamous for their kleptocracy.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TAA4-11)</sup> They were often embezzling money and power to themselves at the expense of their race. Some of these examples include selling other Argonians into slavery for the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md)<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FP-12)</sup> or exploiting their land for foreign interest, such as the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE3E-13)</sup>

To help with the building of their city, [Mazzatun](Lore_Mazzatun.md) the Argonian tribe of [Xit-Xaht](Lore_Xit-Xaht.md) often captured people for months. The Xit-Xaht rarely talked next to those prisoners, but the members of the tribe were noticed to never stop moving, as they pointed and whipped as if they were mad, with one of the captives believing that the tribe would turn feral without the guidance of their leader.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CJ-14)</sup>

### Ayleid
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/OBR-statue-Alessia.jpg/200px-OBR-statue-Alessia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-statue-Alessia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-statue-Alessia.jpg) A statue of Alessia the Slave Queen in her shackles [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/LO-misc-Ayleid_Pedestal.png/200px-LO-misc-Ayleid_Pedestal.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Ayleid_Pedestal.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Ayleid_Pedestal.png) A pedestal carving depicting Nedic slavery *And this thing I have thought of, I have named it, and I call it freedom.*
—Alessia while speaking to [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TSOP2-15)</sup> The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) started their long history with slavery when they conquered the [Eight Islands](Lore_City_Isle.md), taking the native [bird men](Lore_Bird_Men.md)<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-FOTN-16)</sup> as slaves and making use of their literacy.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Decentius-17)</sup> According to legend, after [Shezarr](Lore_Shezarr.md) mysteriously vanished from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the Merethic Era, the Ayleids were able to conquer and enslave the local [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Sat D-18)</sup> At first, their capture was occasional, but it grew systematic and widespread.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DWTA-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Adabal-20)</sup> In [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) captured Argonians as slaves, prizing their resilience.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Scholars-21)</sup> The Ayleids also took [Duraki](Lore_Duraki.md) slaves in [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), but the specifics of the period are unknown, with later Duraki architecture displaying little to no Ayleid trace.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Not D-22)</sup>

Ayleid slavery was cruel and reviled. To honor the [Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphiles.md) clans tortured the Nedes, with the practice of [flesh-sculpture](Lore_Flesh_Sculpture.md) and gut-gardening becoming a particularly widespread form of "art".<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Adabal-20)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DWTA-19)</sup> Specific implements of art-torture, used upon slaves for strange pleasures, included the wailing wheels of [Vindasel](Lore_Vindasel.md) and the gut-gardens of Sercen. In the city-state of [Fire King Hadhuul](Lore_Hadhuul.md), slave children were incinerated for sport, and others were forced to inhale [drugs](Lore_Drugs.md), giving them visions of unseen torment. Some tribes of Nedes were completely wiped out by these religious practices, such as the [men-of-ge](Lore_Men-of-ge.md), who were sacrificed by [Flower King Nilichi](Lore_Nilichi.md) to an [insect god](Lore_Insect_God.md). Slaves were often dehumanized, unable to keep family or choose a name.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Adabal-20)</sup>

Led by [Slave-Queen Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), the enslaved Nedes rose against the Ayleids in the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) circa [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Sat D-18)</sup> Some city-states of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) opposed slavery and assisted the Alessians in their revolt, although the resulting economic transition to a slave-less economy was wrenching.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Dynar-24)</sup> The rebellion was ultimately successful, toppling the Ayleid Empire one year later in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243). Though the now-free Alessians led the new [empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), it is inconclusive whether slavery ever ended under their rule. The cultural perception of the Ayleids as brutal slavemasters persisted for centuries after their extinction.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TLKot A-25)</sup> Indeed, the revulsion for Daedra worship that pervades most human cultures in Tamriel probably originated from the treatment of Nedic slaves by the Daedraphiles in the Late Merethic.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DWTA-19)</sup>

### Bosmer
Tribal [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) have the custom of the "Mourning War". After the war-time death of a tribe member, the clan responsible for their death is raided for captives. Typically only one, but if the deceased was of notable importance multiple hostages may be taken. These captives serve as "replacements" for the slain Bosmer and are physically tortured for an extensive period of time. When finally mentally broken, the hostages are instead happily welcomed into the clan and mentally bewildered by this sudden change of behavior, submitting to their torturers. Typically, they're also given the possessions, position and family of the Wood Elf they're replacing, though in modern times this is less common.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

### Breton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-npc-Enslaved_Miner.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Enslaved_Miner.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Enslaved_Miner.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Enslaved_Miner.jpg) A miner enslaved by the Red Rooks [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) have typically been on the receiving end of slavery, and had their origins as a race by being progenated by generations of Elven intermingling and slavery.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-27)</sup> Despite this, during the era of the High Rock [coin-barons](Lore_Coin-barons.md), slavery was a fact of life for Bretons.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> The island of [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md) was a stronghold for slavers from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), though by the time of [High King Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) this was no longer the case.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-MT-30)</sup> However, some Bretons still practiced slavery during his reign, such as House Velmont in Hew's Bane,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-WS-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-AV-32)</sup> and bandit groups such as the Red Rooks.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup> At least one vizier in High Rock purchased an entire tribe of Goblins for use as slaves from House Dres.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-AD-34)</sup>

### Dragon
After migrating to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) became increasingly strict.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TDW-35)</sup> Those who resisted the influence of the cult became the slaves of the iron-fisted [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md),<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Yvara-36)</sup> the underlings of the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) themselves. In [Valthume](Lore_Valthume.md), the domain of [Hevnoraak](Lore_Hevnoraak.md), citizens would be tortured and humiliated.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Valdar-37)</sup> Thousands died in the [rebellion](Lore_Dragon_War.md) against the Dragon Cult, in which some Dragons betrayed [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) after seeing his tyranny.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-38)</sup>

### Dreugh
According to the teachings of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), "the [Mundex Terrene](Lore_Mundex_Terrene.md) was once ruled over solely by the tyrant [dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) -kings, each to their own dominion, and borderwars fought between their slave oceans... No one that lived did so outside of the sufferance of the dreughs." This lasted until [Lyg](Lore_Lyg.md) was destroyed by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and he declared "each of the nineteen and nine and nine oceans Free."<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

### Dunmer
*After that, there was magic sleep, I think. When it wore off, I was in the belly of a stinking Dark Elf boat.*
—Jhaka, former slave<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-40)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg/300px-RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg) A House Dres slaver camp near [Tear](Lore_Tear.md) Most slaves in Morrowind were beastfolk, such as Khajiit, [Imga](Lore_Imga.md), and Orcs, with Argonians being the most commonly kept slaves. The Dunmer historically considered all beastpeoples and [humans](Lore_Men.md) to be mere "animals", and as such "no more than domesticated chattels".<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Po M-41)</sup> Since the advent of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), slavery was illegal throughout the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), but protected by law in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). This exception was granted by Tiber Septim in the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) of [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896) as a concession to the Dunmer, who saw the use of slaves as their ancient right. Recent developments have seen the rise of abolitionists such as the [Twin Lamps](Lore_Twin_Lamps.md), often with support from members of the leading families on Vvardenfell. Slavery remained legal in Morrowind throughout the lifespan of the Third Empire, provoking strong reactions on both sides.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup> It was only by Imperial times that the Dunmer reluctantly acknowledged humans as people, with many Dunmer at the end of the Third Era avoiding keeping human slaves of any nation so as to not offend humans, although slaves with [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) features were common the province's north.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Po M-41)</sup>

- *"We are disappointed that the Imperial Cult does not speak out against the inhumane practices of slavery in Morrowind. But perhaps you are right. It is by demonstrating our superior commitment to civilization and humanity that we best disprove the Dunmer's barbaric insistence that we are no more than animals."* — [Im-Kilaya](Lore_Im-Kilaya.md), Head of the Argonian Mission in Ebonheart

[House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) thrived on mass abduction of Argonians and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) into slavery, often with the support of local rival warlords selling their own kind.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TAA-45)</sup> Dres families worked Argonian slaves on large [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) plantations on the southeast coast of Morrowind, while others profited from the slave trade itself.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup> They held thousands in their infamous slave-pens of [Tear](Lore_Tear.md), to be shipped by smugglers to cave holding pens on the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md). The plantations of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) nobles made widespread use of slaves as farm labor. In comparison, slavery was uncommon among the members of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), though some of their warriors used slaves, pitting them against beasts for entertainment.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-46)</sup><sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact) - source for Redoran involvement?]</sup> [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) used slaves as servants for housework and as labor in mines. Towns like [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md), [Suran](Lore_Suran.md) and [Tel Aruhn](Lore_Tel_Aruhn.md) had regular slave markets.

In the aftermath of the [Vvardenfell Crisis](Lore_Vvardenfell_Crisis.md), there were a few rebellious landowners who attempted to eliminate slavery in their lands. Some were murdered by their slaves, some were sabotaged and ruined by their House, and others were driven into poverty until they had no option but to sell their land to their neighbors who still kept slaves.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Ted Peterson Posts HHM-47)</sup> In a shrewd political maneuver, King [Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md), representing his own house of Hlaalu, formed an alliance with House Dres. Subsequently, he renounced the slave trade in [3E 430](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_430),<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-48)</sup> setting the remaining Houses of Indoril and Redoran against him in a bloody civil war.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup> Helseth emerged from the war battered, but without the loss of his essential powers, leaving the Morrowind slave trade generally destroyed.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-PGE3M-49)</sup>

### Dwemer
After their defeat at the hands of the invading Atmorans during [The Return](Lore_Merethic_Era_Wars.md#The_Return), particularly at the [Battle of the Moesring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Moesring.md),<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-The_Betrayed-50)</sup> many [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) across Skyrim were forced to accept an offer of sanctuary from the Dwemer. As per the terms of their stay, the elves consumed a toxic fungus that rendered them blind. At first, they were servants, but eventually, the Snow Elves became slaves.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TF:AS-51)</sup> Additionally, the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) Clan of Dwemer conquered Craglorn after their exodus of Resdayn, enslaving the local Duraki, using the Nedes' labor in their settlements. The Duraki seemed to respect the Dwarves' mastery of stonework, requesting burial amidst their ruins<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Erold-52)</sup> and utilizing their techniques in a crude way for their own structures.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Not D-22)</sup>

The Snow Elves were herded into slave pens,<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Irk-53)</sup> where they were subjected to torture at the hands of the Dwemer.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Karliah-54)</sup> In [Irkngthand](Lore_Irkngthand.md), the enslaved mer were forced to build their Great Statue in secret, tricking their masters into believing they had confiscated the Eyes of the Falmer.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-TLW-55)</sup> After generations of enslavement, the Snow Elves rebelled against the Dwarves, managing to temporarily overthrow them and take refuge in [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md)'s deepest caverns. The ensuing [War of the Crag](Lore_War_of_the_Crag.md) raged bitterly for years, until [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700),<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-DW1-56)</sup> when the Dwemer race vanished from Tamriel.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TF:AS-51)</sup> Over time, the once-proud race degraded both mentally and physically, becoming known as the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md).<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Gelebor-57)</sup>

### Falmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/SR-npc-Falmer_Servant.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Falmer_Servant.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Falmer_Servant.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Falmer_Servant.jpg) A Falmer servant According to legend, the Falmer emerge from their caverns in the dead of night to capture infants.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TF:AS-51)</sup> They have been known to take captives from the surface.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-58)</sup> Within the Silent City of Blackreach, the Falmer own slaves who act as guards and lookouts.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-59)</sup> They also use slaves for mining work.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-60)</sup>

### Imperial
During and after [Queen Euraxia Tharn](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md)'s invasion of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) purchased slaves from [Sea Elf](Lore_Maormer.md) privateers. Queen Euraxia used clever loopholes to avoid the workers being considered slaves by law, but the truth was clear.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Abnur-61)</sup> The slaves were forced to mine for new ores in the desert.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Jahhouz-62)</sup> After the [Battle of](Lore_Wars.md#Battle_of_Pale_Pass) [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) kept [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) slaves which played a significant part in establishing the administrative structures of the Empire, as well as in the training of its military.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-PGE1C-63)</sup> [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s army sold captive Bretons into slavery after the [Battle of](Lore_Wars.md#Battle_of_Sancre_Tor) [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md).<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-64)</sup> In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), using [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) for slave labor was legal in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-65)</sup>

When the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) took over [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), they turned its people into slaves and forced them to work solely to feed them, while leaving little else for themselves.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONVyctoria-66)</sup> Young men were forcefully extracted from their homes and recruited into their ranks. When one commoner named [Donus Falto](Lore_Donus_Falto.md) fled the town, it sparked the rebellion between the Imperials and the people.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONVyctoria-66)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONFalto-67)</sup>

### Khajiit
The Thazahrr Cartel is a slavery organization founded by the Khajiit Thazahrr-ra. It is based in [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) in southern Hammerfell and handles the slave industry in the region. Only a few Khajiit make up its ranks as the group is also comprised of Imperials, Bretons, and Redguards. By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Thazahrr Goldfang was the sixth person to take the name and rank of Merchant Lord in the cartel. When the Shadow Conglomerate emerged in power, the cartel lost prominence, something that Thazahrr Goldfang hoped to reclaim and in turn, acquire the same honorific as her ancestor.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ALML4-69)</sup> In the same year, Khajiiti pirates raided [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) for Argonian slaves.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TRV-70)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TSSM-71)</sup>

### Maormer
The Maormer are no strangers to piracy and slavery, selling slaves to the Imperials<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Jahhouz-62)</sup> and using distinctive manacles unique to their race.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-72)</sup> They were reported to use [Snake Magic](Lore_Snake_Magic.md) to influence the minds of their captives and ensorcelled locks when capturing slaves to ensure that they remained docile and compliant.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-CS-73)</sup>

### Reachfolk
As a fractured society, each of the [Reachfolk Clans](Lore_Reachfolk_Clans.md) has its own set of rules and policies. Certain clans,<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-General-78)</sup> such as [Crow-Wives](Lore_Crow-Wife_Clan.md),<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-AB1-79)</sup> [Dreadhorns](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md),<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Filand-80)</sup> and [Thornroots](Lore_Thornroot_Clan.md) were known for keeping slaves and captives. This was not a practice accepted by many other clans, such as [Hillhunters](Lore_Hillhunter_Clan.md).<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-81)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Bralthahawn-82)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Medone-83)</sup> Freedom was considered to be of great importance in the Reach.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Arana-84)</sup> Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of Markarth offered food, freedom, and protection to all his subjects,<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-AC-85)</sup> former captives of the other clans were free in Markarth.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Kjaggus-86)</sup> [Wayward Guardians](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md) were known to detest captivity and slavery, "not wishing a fate like that on anyone."<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Ardanir-87)</sup>

### Redguard
The waves of [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) who fled the sinking of Yokuda enslaved many of the native beastfolk and Nedes of Hammerfell.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-88)</sup> The Duraki, the Nedes of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), were conquered and enslaved by the [Anka-Ra](Lore_Anka-Ra.md), who deemed them a backward people deserving of nothing more.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Not D-22)</sup>

### Sload
[Sload](Lore_Sload.md) merchants are known to purchase large quantities of slaves from Tear. It is suspected that they bring them to their native [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) for usage in [Necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md).<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-89)</sup>

### Snow Elf
According to Nordic account, during the snow elven raid on [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md), the elves took slaves from the human population. They reasoned that the Bretons, after generations of intermingling with elves, were the descendants of these slaves,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-27)</sup> but this is disputed.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-TB:Mo P-90)</sup>

### Tsaesci
The Tsaesci have attempted, at one point, to enslave the Tang Mo. Additionally, they successfully enslaved the goblins of their nearby islands, using them for labor and for blood, which they consume for their [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) satiation. They similarly enslaved the Red [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) after an attempt to "consume" them,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Myst Aka-68)</sup> going on to use them as [war mounts](Lore_Air_Transportation.md#Dragons).<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-PGE1C-63)</sup>
