# Dawnguard Equipment

## Dawnguard Weapons
Dawnguard weapons gain an additional 5 points of damage against vampires. This includes the [Dawnguard Rune Axe](Skyrim_Dawnguard_Rune_Axe.md) but not the [Dawnguard Rune Hammer](Skyrim_Dawnguard_Rune_Hammer.md), due to a bug.

## Dawnguard Armor
When wearing a full set of armor (body armor, boots, gauntlets, and helmet), Dawnguard armors reduce incoming damage from vampires by 25% and the magnitude of hostile spells cast by vampires is reduced by 25%. Mixing the light and heavy sets will not confer the bonus, however.<sup>[*verification needed — Looks like it should still work in the CK*]</sup> Additionally, the bonus is "hidden" as it does not appear in the active effects menu. Light armor benefits from the [Matching Set](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md#Matching_Set) perk, but heavy armor does not due to a bug.

The heavy armor protects as well as [Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md) armor does, for less weight (even less than [Blades Armor](Skyrim_Blades_Armor.md)), but still offers less protection for more weight than [Steel Plate Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate_Armor); the big selling point is when you're up against vampires. The light armor offers protection between [Elven](Skyrim_Elven.md) and [Scaled](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Scaled), for weight also between them, but is less protection for more weight than [Elven Gilded Armor](Skyrim_Elven_Gilded_Armor.md) or [Chitin](Skyrim_Chitin.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> - again, the compelling interest here is specifically for fighting vampires, particularly the protection against spells, which armor normally provides no protection at all against.
