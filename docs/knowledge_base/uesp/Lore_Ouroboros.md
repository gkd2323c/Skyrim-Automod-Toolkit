# Ouroboros


## Culture

### Dunmer
*[Sermon 21](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_21)* by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) speaks of a "[reptile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile) wheel, coiled potential, ever-preamble to the never-action."<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-S21-6)</sup>

### Redguard
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg/150px-ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Coil_of_Satakal.jpg) Satakal ouroboros depicted on a Na-Totambu ceremonial gong In [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) culture, the symbol of a snake consuming itself in an endless cycle of rebirth was a motif revered as a symbol of their god [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Satakal-1)</sup>

### Solstice Argonians
In the culture of the [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis) is known to have been depicted as a twisting serpent devouring its own tail.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Sithis Wicker-7)</sup>

### Stirk Fellowship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-place-South_Siege_Camp_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-South_Siege_Camp_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-South_Siege_Camp_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-South_Siege_Camp_02.jpg) A symbol of ouroboros on the Stirk Fellowship's tents The ourobors symbol was employed by the Stirk Fellowship.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WW-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LASolsice-5)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Lady [Karinwe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Karinwe_Corelanya), the Regent of the [Clan Corelanya](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clan_Corelanya), likened the symbolism of the Three Queens to the ouroboros found in the sigil of the Stirk Fellowship: a cycle of Dawn, Dusk, and renewal, repeating endlessly. She considered it "a beautiful truth for a complicated world".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LASolsice-5)</sup>

### Three Banners War
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Icon-Transparent_Logo.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Icon-Transparent_Logo.png) Alliance War Ouroboros During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the ouroboros symbol became associated with the ongoing conflict of the Three Banners War. This variation features heads representing each alliance: the eagle for the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), the lion for the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and the dragon for the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Legends-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-AUA-3)</sup> It was intended to evoke the essence of the three warring alliances.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-AUA-3)</sup>

This emblem was prominently featured on various decorations, including [holiday](Lore_Holidays.md) items for celebrations such as the [New Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival) and the [Witches Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witches_Festival),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Lantern-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Wreath-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-NLCAA-11)</sup> as well as other celebratory occasions.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Cake-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Container-13)</sup> The imagery was incorporated into Chef [Donolon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Donolon)'s renowned cake collection, adorning desserts known as Jubilee Cakes.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Cake-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CD-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-AFC-15)</sup>
