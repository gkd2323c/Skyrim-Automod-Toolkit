# Planes of Existence

Within the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) exist a multitude of different planes of existence, from the well-known mortal realm of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and the planes of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), to the mysterious [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) pocket realms and the magical planes of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). The term **Outer Realms** usually refers to Aetherius and Oblivion together,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3AS-1)</sup> but may also include a number of "other, less structured forms" in the Aurbis.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> It is said that there also exist planes beyond Aetherius, and harder to travel to than it, where even powerful [magic](Lore_Magic.md) such as that of the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) can't reach, and the location of which is unknown to even the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONMarieve-3)</sup> The plane to which the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) transport their island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) when it is not on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is said to be one such realm.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLil-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOValsirenn-6)</sup> The very "sinews" and "viscera" of the planes are said to be comprised of [magicka](Lore_Magic.md), the strings of magic holding the realms and threading all existence together.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Dreynis-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Dreynis Notes-8)</sup>

Several terms may be used to classify realms. **[Slipstream Realms](Lore_Slipstream_Realm.md)** are points within the Aurbis at which transliminal forces balance. They operate as transition zones where the conceptual truths of different realms can coexist.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nuit1-9)</sup> **Oblivious Vortexes** are realms between planes that gather drifting fragments of [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tutor-10)</sup> **Inchoate Realms** are regions of incomplete and half-finished demi-planes that were for some reason abandoned by their projectors. They are highly unstable and prone to volatile realm-rips. **Collective Realms** are planes that exist as collective extensions of their numerous, less-powerful inhabitants. **Sundered Realms** are pocket realities that were shattered by interplanar war or Princely expungement.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nuit1-9)</sup> **[Adjacent Places](Lore_Adjacent_Place.md)** exist past the normal non-cardinal points<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Sermon26-11)</sup> and exist as parallel reflections of the realms they are 'adjacent' of.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKAMA-12)</sup>

**Pocket Realms** (alternatively Pocket Realities,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nuit1-9)</sup> Pocket Planes,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tamien-13)</sup> or Pocket Dimensions)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Wit IP-14)</sup> are self-contained realities exempt from the laws of nature, often lying beyond mortal comprehension.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Isles-15)</sup> There are a nearly infinite number of them, located between planes in gathered chaotic creatia.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nuit1-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tutor-10)</sup> Stretches of Oblivion such as the Ur-Mora Clarion region contain tens of thousands.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nuit1-9)</sup> They are typically smaller and weaker than typical planes, prone to collapsing at significant magical disruption.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tamien-13)</sup> Despite this, some Pocket Realms have been described as having more extensive or even infinite scale, and not conforming to the rules of reality mortals know.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Panopticon-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Working-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Leramil-18)</sup>. Pocket realms are defined by their minor nature in comparison to the greater planes, and are often under the jurisdiction of a Daedric Prince, though they might not lie within the same time and space as that Prince's main realm.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Nuit2-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LAIA-20)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Leramil-18)</sup> Some pocket realms are only accessible from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Azura-21)</sup>

Pocket Realms can also be pieces of a greater plane.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Taerma-22)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Leramil-18)</sup> These are also referred to as **Subrealms** or **Adjunct Realms**, referring to their connection to a greater plane.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LAIA-20)</sup> Other planar designations exist, such as **Nanoplanes**<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Nano-23)</sup> and **Demiplanes**,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Demi-24)</sup> but their exact parameters are unclear. The designation **Chaos Realms** seems to refer both to the sixteen Planes of Oblivion<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Eo MN-25)</sup> and a type of minor reality similar to pocket realms.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

The realms comprising Oblivion are described as being part of a different universe or a different dimension in relation to Mundus.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Atronite-27)</sup> Oblivion realms are an extension of their masters, formed out of [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Fox-28)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LSP-29)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nuit1-9)</sup> and maintained either by greater Daedra or the collective will of many lesser Daedra, although some are ruled by mortals who have ascended to immortality—like the [Ideal Masters](Lore_Ideal_Masters.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-LMA_FNH-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-EGRN-31)</sup> Accounts from the Daedra describe the minor realms in Oblivion as overall being infinite in both number and complexity.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Lyranth2-32)</sup> It is said that Oblivion realms can be infinite in both size and shape, and that all proper Oblivion realms are infinite in scale.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Leramil-18)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-TESONRWRL-33)</sup> The realms of Oblivion have been described as "infinite in their variation", just like the Daedra that inhabit them, thus it is said they share little in common, being "the very definition of change and variation, manifesting all possibilities, and validating all understanding and misunderstanding".<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Jackdaw-34)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-LA:Lt F-35)</sup> Indeed, it is said that what the mortal senses perceive while in an Oblivion realm is crafted from illusion and metaphor, the result of their mind attempting to make sense of the surrounding chaos. Because of this phenomenon things like the "grass" one might perceive while in an Oblivion realm, are actually just the memory of that thing as interpreted by the mortal mind.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Telbaril-36)</sup>

According to some accounts, the mortal [realms](Lore_Mundus.md) were also formed out of Creatia by the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Co S-37)</sup>

The planes of Aurbis are made up of different [reality layers](Lore_Layers_of_Reality.md), spatially overlapping sections which can be accessed via the use of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) which shifts beings and objects between them, things shifted to a different layer generally can't be perceived or interacted with by those who lack the necessary arcane means.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Leramil Hidden-38)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Meln-39)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Hidden-40)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Blightcrown-41)</sup>

Beyond and outside the Aurbis and all its constituent planes exist the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Ithelia-42)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Leramil-18)</sup>

## Daedric Princes' Realms
[Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) contains all the dimensional planes which are home to the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). The sixteen realms of the Daedric Princes, referred to by the general term **Planes of Oblivion**, can be very extensive, Molag Bal's plane is described as being an apocalyptic replica of all of Nirn, and Hermaeus Mora's is said to be infinite in size. Indeed, according to [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), were a Prince to simultaneously invade the realms of the other Princes to seize the power within through the use of something like the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), they'd gain infinite power through the infinite connections made.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-43)</sup> It has been said that, just like Apocrypha, all proper Oblivion realms are infinite in size.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Leramil-18)</sup>

Each prince's plane may have a more specific name, such as Apocrypha for [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s, Coldharbour for [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s, Moonshadow for [Azura](Lore_Azura.md)'s, and Quagmire for [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md)'s. Each plane is supposedly very different in appearance, depending upon the nature of the prince who rules it, as the realms of the Princes are extensions of those Princes.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-EGRN-31)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Whim-44)</sup> They can range from beautiful to desolate; some may not even resemble worlds as we think of them, containing nothing but dust and smoke, or changing constantly.

### Apocrypha
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-trailer-Shadow_Over_Morrowind-Apocrypha.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-Shadow_Over_Morrowind-Apocrypha.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Shadow_Over_Morrowind-Apocrypha.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Shadow_Over_Morrowind-Apocrypha.jpg) Apocrypha [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Knowledge and [Fate](Lore_Fate.md). The geography of the realm varies, with some areas being open with fields containing odd flora, other areas are an endless library consisting of untitled books with black covers, where all forbidden knowledge can be found, and the crackling towers of learning mingle with archways of despair and confusion. The realm is haunted by the ghosts of mortals forever searching for knowledge. Stacks of books form mazes and spiraling pillars that can reach as high as the illuminating green sky. Intricate ruins and monstrous carvings can be found among the stacks. The plane is covered in a sea of ink and roiling tentacles, while Hermaeus Mora is omnipresent over all, taking glee in tormenting mortals from his realm. Some areas of the realm are consumed by darkness which can kill any who enter it. Discarded pages and floating tomes fill the fetid air, as if pulled by invisible string. Storms of notorious strength purportedly rage over Apocrypha.

Apart from magical teleportation, Apocrypha is most easily entered by mortals through the reading of [Black Books](Lore_Black_Books.md), tomes of forbidden knowledge displaced from time and scattered across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). An apparition of the reader's body remains in [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), tethering their life force. Most mortals who gain access to the secrets within Apocrypha are driven insane by unending revelations. Such was the fate of the mage [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md), who famously disappeared in Apocrypha during his voyages through Oblivion. However, those who successfully journey through Apocrypha are known to discover powerful knowledge. The laws of the mortal world hold no power in this realm.

### Ashpit
[Ashpit](Lore_Ashpit.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) ruled over by [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Outcasts. The exact manner and time in which the realm came to be, and whether and how it might be tied to the commonly believed transformation of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) into Malacath, are not known. Orcish spellwrights call for boons from the Ashpit and rarely any other realms. That includes conjuring [Flame](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) [Atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md). The Ashpit is hard to access, as the pathways to his domain take on a characteristic level of concealment to protect the disenfranchised and cast-out.

The realm mostly consists only of dust, palaces of smoke, and vaporous creatures; anguish, betrayal, and broken promises like ash fill the bitter air. Few mortals manage to reach the realm, where levitation and magical breathing are necessary to survive. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) have been known to bottle the realm's thick, roiling vapor.

The Spine of Ashpit is a surprisingly light skeletal spine found in the realm. It is made from a grey dust, and fragments of bone have been known to be taken from it and brought to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) claims that the spine is the metaphorical "backbone" of the realm, which he looks down upon.

However, some areas of the realm are safe for mortals. In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), Malacath chose to bring [Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Sul) and [Prince Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) to the Ashpit, where they found a garden of slender trees, and "vines festooned with lilylike flowers" wound about the trunks; a "multitude of spheres moved, deep in the colorless sky, as distant and pale as moons". Occasionally the vines "collapsed and formed into a cloud of black moths that swarmed about them". This garden seems to have some emotional significance to Malacath, who describes it as a "shadow of a garden", and an "echo of something that once was".

### Attribution's Share
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-concept-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dragonstar_Arena.jpg) Attribution's Share [Attribution's Share](Lore_Attribution%27s_Share.md) is the plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Deceit. The Realm of Boethia is described as a country of labyrinthine policy and betrayals, with maze gardens and twisted towers. It was formerly known as **Snake Mount**.

### Coldharbour
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/ON-load-Coldharbour_%28old%29.jpg/200px-ON-load-Coldharbour_%28old%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Coldharbour_(old).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Coldharbour_(old).jpg) Coldharbour [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Domination. As befits his sphere, Bal's realm is a desolate, apocalyptic image of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), which is achieved by both mocking imitation and outright theft of parts of the mortal realm. It is inhabited by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and the eternally-tormented husks of mortals called [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md).

Mortals who made deals with Molag Bal can also be found living in Coldharbour. The souls of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) are sent to Coldharbour when they die. Coldharbour has been described as a manifestation of fear and exploitation. Descriptions of the plane vary widely, but all accounts agree that Coldharbour is a dismal, cold, and largely lifeless realm.

### Colored Rooms
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/ON-load-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg/200px-ON-load-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Colored_Rooms.jpg) Colored Rooms The [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md) are a many-faceted realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), known as the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Infinite Energies. From what little has been seen of the Colored Rooms, they seem to resemble a cross between a [coral reef](Lore_Coral.md) and a vast field of floating stones, strewn with colorful trails of dust or cloud. The "ground" between the stones looks like luminescent water, but is solid enough to walk on. The skies are described as floating effluvium oceans of crimson. It has been suggested that the "colors" of the realms are a reference to the different schools of [magic](Lore_Magic.md).

[Aurorans](Lore_Auroran.md) and their warhorses are the spawn of Meridia that reside in the Colored Rooms, being made from pure light and have skin tones of virtually any hue. [Golden Saints](Lore_Aureal.md) are also purported to reside in the realm. The Colored Rooms have been described by those souls who have been there as "pretty boring" aside from Meridia's lectures (which are described as "pretty boring" as well).

### Deadlands
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-load-The_Refuge_of_Dread.jpg/200px-ON-load-The_Refuge_of_Dread.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Refuge_of_Dread.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Refuge_of_Dread.jpg) Deadlands The [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) are a Daedric realm of Oblivion created and ruled over by the Daedric Prince of Destruction, Mehrunes Dagon. The embodiment of its creator's sphere, the dimension is fraught with natural disasters and destructive change. The area known as the Burn is covered by an ocean of lava, scattered with scorched volcanic islands and ruined structures. Many lesser Daedra roam the realm freely, but Dremora dominate the hierarchy. The Sever is a region of the Deadlands with light soil covering jagged rock with sulfur pools throughout, fierce winds, and constant lightning storms. Destructive tornadoes and extremes in temperature characterize the region. The capital of the Deadlands is known as Destruction's Solace.

Although mortals have attempted to map the Deadlands, the realm's many rivers of fire reverse course every few hundred years, rendering these maps useless. The realm's unformed chaotic creatia appears as quivering pools of lava.

The force of Change, one of the Eleven Forces, flows strongly in the Deadlands, as all aspects of Dagon's sphere are rooted in Change. The currents flow so strongly that one who attempts to tap into change within the realm runs the risk of being swept away, and entering a synchroseisiac state, becoming out of sync with reality and being scattered across time and space. Thus the realm is especially suited for those wishing to study that force. Some Daedra that reside in the Deadlands have thus learned how to read the flow of Change, granting them such clarity that they are practically [precognitive](Lore_Sight.md).

### Evergloam
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/44/ON-interior-Crow%27s_Wood.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Crow%27s_Wood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Crow%27s_Wood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Crow%27s_Wood.jpg) Evergloam The [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Shadow. It is a realm of perpetual twilight, and the "cradle of shadow". The Evergloam exists adjacent to all other realms of reality simultaneously; it can almost be glimpsed when one turns and looks quickly while in the dark. It consists of a primary plane and several pocket realms, including the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md) and the [Crow's Wood](Lore_Crow%27s_Wood.md). These realms are thought to be constantly shifting, and are perceived differently by different mortals. It is filled with forests, whose trees cast long shadows over a purple landscape. The land is dotted with crumbling buildings and sunken crypts.

### Fields of Regret
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg) Fields of Regret The [Fields of Regret](Lore_Fields_of_Regret.md) are a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Wishes. The realm appears as a tranquil countryside, dotted with majestic cities of glass and ornate buildings and populated by [Skaafin](Lore_Skaafin.md). It contains dense forests, lakes, and mountains, with floating golden metals and stone spirals filling the landscape. The city of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) was once a part of the realm, but was eventually severed and came to rest in the [Realm of the Hist](Lore_Realm_of_the_Hist.md).

According to some sources, the Fields of Regret are not a place in the same sense that a place on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is, rather the realm is a "state of mind", specifically the state of mind of Clavicus Vile, which reflects on the realm and causes everything there to be exactly as Vile wishes it to be. Thus its form is said to represent Vile's mindset, a green, sunny and pleasant land interspersed with tranquil ponds under an expanse of clear blue sky, reflecting Vile's view of himself as a playful and fun-loving god who basks alongside his companion, Barbas, the pleasant appearance concealing Vile's true temperament which is likened to that of a child cruelly playing with insects.

### Hunting Grounds
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c9/ON-load-Hunter%27s_Glade.jpg/200px-ON-load-Hunter%27s_Glade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hunter%27s_Glade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hunter%27s_Glade.jpg) Hunting Grounds The [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md), also known as the **Great Hunt**, is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of the Hunt. It has been described as a realm of dense woodland and vast grasslands populated by [werebears](Lore_Werebear.md), wild [cattle](Lore_Cow.md) and [unicorns](Lore_Unicorn.md). It is a savage land inhabited by vicious creatures such as [bears](Lore_Bear.md), [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md), werecreatures, and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). These creatures are generally much larger than their counterparts in the mortal realm. The spirits of animals from the mortal plane are a common sight within the Hunting Grounds, as Hircine brings them in to please his followers. Some Daedra from the realm take form of sapient animals. All who die within the Hunting Grounds are doomed to remain within the realm after death. Inhabitants of the realm may take on the shapes of various animals to participate in the eternal hunt.

Followers of Hircine are promised an afterlife within the Hunting Grounds, where they can indulge in spectacular hunts, as well as experience an endless cycle of violence as well as death and rebirth, reborn endlessly in bodies that never fall ill. Its inhabitants reside in the many hunting lodges dotting the landscape. The forests of the Hunting Grounds are teeming with powerful beasts, which allow Hircine's followers to engage in the most spectacular hunts. For the devoted, it is a paradise. The souls of [lycanthropes](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) are claimed by Hircine and spend eternity experiencing the thrill of the hunt in his Hunting Grounds. However, it is possible for a soul to transfer to the proper afterlife (such as [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md)) if their lycanthropy is cured posthumously, provided their soul is yet to have been taken to the Hunting Grounds. During the daylight hours, [werebears](Lore_Werebear.md) and fierce [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) stalk the land in their continual Hunt. When the sun sinks below the horizon, Hircine announces himself with a pack of werewolves to take his turn in the cycle.

### Mirrormoor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) Mirrormoor [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md) was the realm of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), Mistress of the Untraveled Road. [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md) is a fragment of this forgotten realm.

Mirrormoor is a large desert wasteland filled with gargantuan skeletons and surrounded by an auroral sky. While Ithelia is active, it is filled by shattered mirrors and crystalline structures and the sky is fractured by cracks. Those elements were not present in Fargrave before Ithelia's return. Some stories have described a city of crystal buildings and many-hued courts.

### Moonshadow
[Moonshadow](Lore_Moonshadow.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), Queen of Dawn and Dusk. The realm is said to hold "too much beauty", so much so it can render mortal visitors "half-blind". It contains flowers, waterfalls, pink trees, and a city of silver. Azura herself resides in a rose palace, and is welcoming to mortal travelers.

To the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), Moonshadow is where [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) tends the Gates of the Crossing—the bridge between Nirni and the afterlife beyond. It is there where they are guided to the [Sands Behind the Stars](Lore_Sands_Behind_the_Stars.md) if they are worthy.

According to myth, as Azurah wept in the [Great Darkness](Lore_Void.md) after the death of her [mother](Lore_Fadomai.md), the light from her tears chased away the Darkness and eventually left a "place of moonlight and shadow". Khajiiti mythology also writes that when [Magrus](Lore_Magrus.md) fled from [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md) and [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md) after the former had plucked his eye out, he fell into the Moonshadow. There, Azurah judged him as too fearful to rule a sphere, and tore out his other eye. The blinded Magrus fled to the [heavens](Lore_Aetherius.md), and Azurah made of his eye a "stone to reflect the [Varliance Gate](Lore_Varliance_Gate.md)".

### Scuttling Void

### Quagmire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/ON-place-Quagmire.jpg/200px-ON-place-Quagmire.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Quagmire.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Quagmire.jpg) Quagmire [Quagmire](Lore_Quagmire.md), or the **Dreamstride**, is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Nightmare, [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md). It is a realm of horrors, where reality shifts upon itself in seemingly impossible ways. Every few minutes, lightning flashes and the realm morphs into a terrifying scene, each one more frightening than the last. Mortal sleepers often slip into this realm without any help at all.

From her citadel in the center of the realm, Vaermina reaches out into [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and collects the memories of sleeping mortals, leaving nothing but visions of horror and despair. These experiences, known as nightmares, involve the mind of a mortal traveling to Quagmire. Through magical teleportation, it is possible for a mortal to enter Quagmire while awake, as was done by the mage [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md). It is inhabited by [Omens](Lore_Omen.md), daedric servants of Vaermina, who sends them to haunt the inhabitants of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). Wasps inhabiting the realm weave their nests from nightmares.

## Other Realms of Oblivion
There are over 37,000 pocket realms of Oblivion with drastically varying purposes. Some are created by Daedric Princes as a prison or purgatory, while others are the homes of certain lesser Daedra.

### Abyss
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/BL-misc-Abyss.jpg/200px-BL-misc-Abyss.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Abyss.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Abyss.jpg) Portal to the Abyss The [Abyss](Lore_Abyss.md) is a mysterious realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) consisting of a never-ending series of rooms and corridors, both real and unreal at the same time, which together form an endless dungeon. Few have survived exploring the infinite reaches of the Abyss, and none have conquered it. It is said to be linked to the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [lich](Lore_Lich.md) king, [Celemaril Light-Bringer](Lore_Celemaril_Light-Bringer.md). However, Celemaril is not responsible for its creation.

### Arum-Khal's Realm
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/ON-place-Arum-Khal%27s_Realm.jpg/200px-ON-place-Arum-Khal%27s_Realm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arum-Khal%27s_Realm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arum-Khal%27s_Realm.jpg) Arum-Khal's Realm [Arum-Khal's Realm](Lore_Arum-Khal%27s_Realm.md) was a dark plane of existence created by the [dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md) [lich](Lore_Lich.md) [Arum-Khal](Lore_Arum-Khal.md) where only dark spirits could thrive. It resembled floating islands with glowing [corals](Lore_Coral.md) and ruined [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) architecture hanging in a void of eternal twilight, reminiscent to that of the [Dark Behind the World](Lore_Dark_Behind_the_World.md). It was destroyed circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

### Chimera of Desolation
The [Chimera of Desolation](Lore_Chimera_of_Desolation.md) is a small realm of Oblivion created by the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon.

The Chimera was once Caecilly Island, a small island off the coast of [Northmoor](Lore_Northmoor.md) (or [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), according to some sources) in High Rock. The entire island was cast into the void during the Third Era as an act of revenge against Chimere Graegyn, a mortal conjurer living there. The abandoned towns of Trybador and Granvellusa still stand on the desolate island.

The plane was once accessible via the Battlespire, but this link was severed following Dagon's invasion of the edifice during the Imperial Simulacrum. Upon Dagon's banishment during the Oblivion Crisis, Caecilly was returned back to Tamriel.

### Deadlight
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/ON-place-Deadlight.jpg/200px-ON-place-Deadlight.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Deadlight.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Deadlight.jpg) Deadlight [Deadlight](Lore_Deadlight.md) is a shattered plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), said to have been destroyed in the ancient past by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).

According to legend, the realm was once the domain of a forgotten [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) who earned Dagon's wrath. In response, Dagon made war upon the Prince, destroying their home. Nothing remained of the realm but a shattered fragment, a place illuminated only by a green, ghostly [aurora](Lore_Aurora.md). The realm possess a ruined keep, which stood beneath a starless sky. The plane itself is devoid of life and energy, which is unusual for a realm of Oblivion. The surrounding landscape consists of jagged stone peaks, dotted with lightning rods and flora typical of the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) such as [bloodgrass](Lore_Bloodgrass.md).

The only known entrance to Deadlight is a crumbling [portal dais](Lore_Portal_Dais.md) called the Western Gate, located in a region of the Deadlands known as the Burn.

### Desolation's End
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/ON-quest-Cutting_Off_the_Source.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Cutting_Off_the_Source.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cutting_Off_the_Source.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cutting_Off_the_Source.jpg) Desolation's End [Desolation's End](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Desolation%27s_End) was a small pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by a member of the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) called Lord Velian.

It was created through [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) ritual after the villagers of [Eagle's Brook](Lore_Eagle%27s_Brook.md), in the [Cambray Hills](Lore_Cambray_Hills.md) region of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), were turned into [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md). The realm resembled a nightmarish [Daedric ruin](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) covered in glowing red glyphs and surrounded by lava. The ruin's only inhabitants were [banekin](Lore_Banekin.md) and Velian's shade.

The local mage, Merien Sellan, learned of Velian's ritual and entered the pocket realm to stop him. He allowed himself to be willingly captured in order to collapse the plane, killing himself in the process. His son Tamien asked [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to enter the realm and kill Velian, allowing Merien to escape before the collapse but also allowing the cultists to leave with the zombie army by ship. It is unknown if Merien was rescued, although the realm was collapsed regardless.

### Detritus
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/ON-place-Seat_of_Detritus.jpg/200px-ON-place-Seat_of_Detritus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Seat_of_Detritus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Seat_of_Detritus.jpg) Detritus [Detritus](Lore_Detritus.md) is a ghastly demi-plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) ruled over by [Hollowjack](Lore_Hollowjack.md), the Lord of Mortal Fears. It has also been described as a "half-world", and is a realm filled with untold horrors. Every year, during the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md) on the 13<sup>th</sup> of Frost Fall, the portals between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and Detritus open and gruesome items from this realm flood the markets of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). According to an enigmatic and immortal entity known as [the Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md), Detritus is a "carving" from the shell of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md).

Hollowjack derives his supernatural power from metaphysically feeding on the fear of mortals, and his realm is dedicated to this singular purpose. Detritus is described as a claustrophobic series of small spaces cluttered and jammed with piles of mortals' lost and broken personal items. Hollowjack frivols there among his collection of shattered memories, categorizing and classing and replaying them to find new vulnerabilities in the mortal mind. There is no outside on Detritus, only room after room of recurring nightmares and internal torments. According to Hollowjack, this is because "mortals fear most what is inside themselves".

### Fargrave
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/ON-interior-Waking_Flame_Base_03.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Waking_Flame_Base_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Waking_Flame_Base_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Waking_Flame_Base_03.jpg) Fargrave [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), also known as the **Celestial Palanquin**, is an obscure demiplane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), functioning as a crossroads where the paths through Oblivion become easier to navigate. With no [Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), the accords that make it harder for other realms to connect to Nirn do not apply, making it a convenient way station between Nirn and the rest of Oblivion. In appearance, it is a large desert wasteland filled with [gargantuan skeletons](Lore_Bearers_of_Fargrave.md) and surrounded by an auroral sky.

[Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and mortals alike can be found here, engaging in trade on a planar level. The inhabitants prefer their privacy, and as such not many in the mortal world are aware of its existence. Through the use of relics such as portal keys, one can move through Fargrave with impunity to reach Nirn and Oblivion. According to some of the Daedric inhabitants, without a Prince to set the laws and manipulate Fargrave's reality, the local Daedra are afforded a great deal of freedom, allowing them to shape their dwelling as they choose.

### Fourth Sinus of Takubar
The [Fourth Sinus of Takubar](Lore_Fourth_Sinus_of_Takubar.md) (also known as **DOP 9497.15**, called **Takubar** for short) is a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and the home of the [Cold-Flame Atronachs](Lore_Cold-Flame_Atronach.md). It is a realm of extreme cold, where the material bonds of the bedrock slide apart to flow like lava. It is the inversion of [Infernace](Lore_Infernace.md), a realm of extreme heat. Takubar was discovered by the denizens of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) after [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) banished common [Flame Atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) from his realm. [Frost Mares](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Mare) are theorized to originate from this plane. [Corvus Direnni](Lore_Corvus_Direnni.md) theorized that the Frost Senche-Panther may be a form of atronach summoned from Fourth Sinus of Takubar.

It can be assumed that Takubar, like Infernace, is a 'collective realm', a highly organized realm of Oblivion created not by a single Daedra, but as a collective extension of its numerous, less-powerful inhabitants. This type of realm is considered relatively exceptional, although they're also rather mundane and uniform.

### Grayhaven
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/ON-place-Grayhaven.jpg/200px-ON-place-Grayhaven.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grayhaven.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grayhaven.jpg) Grayhaven [Grayhaven](Lore_Grayhaven.md) is a lost realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> located deep in the [Void](Lore_Void.md), beyond the reach of the [Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Oblivion as well as the inhabitants of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). ### Havoc Wellhead

### Gorge
[The Gorge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorge) is a thin, vestigial realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) which acts as a divide between the realms of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md). It is a realm where need and hunger are one, described as a pit of famine and desperation where brutal [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) spend eternity devouring anything they can, even each other, to feed a boundless hunger that will never be satiated. Pinnacle among them is [the Insatiable](Lore_The_Insatiable_(creature).md), a [Hunger](Lore_Hunger.md) of great cunning and power.

The Gorge clings to the skin of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), gnawing in futility to break through. As such, it resembles a horrifying mirror image of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), almost identical save for the fleshy growths and Daedric beasts. It is known to be home to hungers, [scamps](Lore_Scamp.md), [ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md), [daedrats](Lore_Daedrat.md), [fiendroths](Lore_Fiendroth.md), red [torchbugs](Lore_Torchbug.md), and [watchers](Lore_Watcher.md).

The Insatiable can be summoned to Nirn through Daedric ritual. Once summoned, it will tirelessly feast on life, using the essence it devours to slip freely between the Gorge and Nirn in order to ambush its prey. Mortal sorcerers willing to brave the horrors of the Gorge can similarly move between these realms by creating an anchor using their own vital essence at a place where the barrier between Nirn and Oblivion is thinnest. When complete, the anchor will allow permanent passage between the realms until the sigils are snuffed out. This ability can act as a powerful means of passing undetected in the mortal world.

### Infernace
[Infernace](Lore_Infernace.md) (also known as **DOP 6**) is a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). It is a realm of extreme heat and molten rock, and is the home of the [Flame Atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md). It is well known to all [conjurers](Lore_Conjuration.md) due to the ability to summon subservient atronachs from the realm. The [Fourth Sinus of Takubar](Lore_Fourth_Sinus_of_Takubar.md), a realm of extreme cold, is an inversion of Infernace.

Despite being considered a highly-organized and physical realm, the reality of Infernace is not a projection of the mind of any particular [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Instead, it was created as an extension of its Flame Atronach inhabitants. Realms such as Infernace are termed 'collective realms', and are considered relatively exceptional. However, collective realms are also rather mundane and uniform, and the endless magma and fumaroles of Infernace are no exception.

The clan structure of Infernace is unknown, and no hierarchy has been observed due to the difficulties involved in distinguishing individual Flame Atronachs. The realm was once visited by the [Demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](Lore_Fa-Nuit-Hen.md), who is said to have worn the [Flame Monarch's Crown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flame_Monarch%27s_Crown).

### Levinace
[Levinace](Lore_Levinace.md) is a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). It is the home of the [Storm Atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md).

It might be assumed that Levinace is a 'collective realm' similar to [Infernace](Lore_Infernace.md), created as an extension of its Storm Atronach inhabitants. If so, the realm would be rather mundane and uniform in appearance.

Levinace is well known to all [conjurers](Lore_Conjuration.md) due to the ability to summon subservient atronachs from the realm. Summoned Storm Atronachs are usually compelled to take a roughly humanoid form, often for combat purposes. Sufficiently skilled conjurers can compel atronachs from this realm to assume any number of exotic forms, such as for use as summoned mounts. [Corvus Direnni](Lore_Corvus_Direnni.md) is famed as a pioneer in this field.

### Maelstrom
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/ON-load-Maelstrom_Arena.jpg/200px-ON-load-Maelstrom_Arena.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Maelstrom_Arena.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Maelstrom_Arena.jpg) Maelstrom [Maelstrom](Lore_Maelstrom.md) is the realm of [Demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](Lore_Fa-Nuit-Hen.md), the Multiplier of Motions Known. It appears in the style of an arena where mortal challengers can battle the demiprince's Barons of Move Like This in rounds reminiscent of other Daedric or Tamrielic locales. In addition to the various arenas, the realm itself resembles a desert at dusk, with a collection of [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) -style buildings in its center; this is the area where [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) spectators gather between each round.

### Nightflame
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-interior-Elden_Hollow_II.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Elden_Hollow_II.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Elden_Hollow_II.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Elden_Hollow_II.jpg) Nightflame [Nightflame](Lore_Nightflame.md) is a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) specializing in [shadow](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md) and [fire](Lore_Elements.md#Fire) -based magic. It served as the seat of power of a powerful [Daedric Titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md), Bogdan the Nightflame. The realm consisted of Daedric ruins enclosed entirely by stone, with few holes in the walls allowing for a minute amount of [sunlight](Lore_Sun.md) to shine through. The ruins are laid out and sometimes built upside-down onto surfaces in such a way that did not seem to apply to the laws of gravity, being filled with countless bone remains that were frequently found in the realm, and even incorporated into the structures. The creatures under Bogdan's command were said to be so powerful and great in number that they could provide a whole army capable of ending the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). The realm would later collapse when a group of [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) slew Bogdan and the [Guild mage](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) Bakkhara destroyed the final altar, although Bogdan himself managed to possess Bakkhara's brother Kargand in the process and survive.

### Noweyr
[Noweyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Noweyr) (also known as the **Realm of Noweyr**) is a plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). A tale intertwining Noweyr and the thief- [god](Lore_Gods.md) [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md) exists. It is said that Rajhin pilfered enchanted purple horses and ponies from somewhere within the realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Whenever inquired about the exact origin of these magical creatures, his worshippers were known to playfully respond with a cryptic and whimsical answer, saying, "Noweyr!".

### Prison of Xykenaz

### Soul Cairn

The Ideal Masters describe the realm as an otherworldly refuge dedicated to peace, love, eternal rest, and harmony. Mortal souls sent to the Soul Cairn eventually lose all memory of their former lives, with only newcomers to the realm retaining wisps of their former memories. The realm was once accessible from the [voidgate](Lore_Magic_Transportation.md) of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), but the link was severed after the citadel was partially destroyed. Valerica once created a portal to the Soul Cairn within the ruined portion of Castle Volkihar. ## Realms of Aetherius
[Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) is the Immortal Plane of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md), and the source of all magic in the world. Realms of Aetherius are often associated with different cultures' concepts of an afterlife.

### Beginning Place
The **[Beginning Place](Lore_Beginning_Place.md)** is a location of cosmic and religious importance within [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Origin)</sup> It is the site at which the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) was formed from the interplay of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md)'s stasis and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md)'s instability. Later, it was where the [et'Ada](Lore_Gods.md) crystalized into existence. [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) God of Time, came into being first. Following his example, the most powerful spirits formed, including [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), and [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md).

In a paraphrased retelling of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) myth *[The Anuad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Anuad)*, there exists the **Limbo Rift of Void**. Anu and Padomay are said to have [crossed from it](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) when they first arrived in the [Void](Lore_Void.md). Due to their interplay, [Nir](Lore_Nir.md) formed, drawing the love of both entities. However, Nir professed she only loved Anu, causing Padomay to beat her in anger. For this, Anu fought and defeated Padomay, banishing him back into the Limbo Rift. Shortly after, Nir gave birth to creation serving the role of origin and thus as a personification of the Beginning Place for the [twelve worlds](Lore_Kalpa.md) of [creation](Lore_Aetherius.md). After many ages, the alignment of the twelve worlds of Creation borne from Nir allowed Padomay to open the rift and return. Hating Creation, he shattered the twelve [planets](Lore_Planets.md), drawing Anu's attention. They entered combat once more, Anu again emerging the victor. However, as Anu combined the remnants of the twelve worlds into [one](Lore_Nirn.md), Padomay attacked him by surprise. In response, Anu dragged him through the Limbo Rift, sealing it behind them both, forever.

According to the teachings of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), mighty, net-like [fires](Lore_Elements.md#Fire) exist in the Beginning Place. [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) used them to bind [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) during their [marriage](Lore_Marriage.md). Later, Vivec, [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) gathered them to contain the [ash](Lore_Ash_Blight.md) of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) after its eruption.

### Far Shores
The [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md), also called the **Walkabout**<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> and the **Far Dunes**, is the afterlife sought by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). According to [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) myth, [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), the serpentine God of Everything, eats itself over and over, periodically consuming all of creation. By "moving at strange angles" to stride between "worldskins", a process known as the Walkabout, the strongest of the spirits learned to bypass this cycle of destruction. Thanks to [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md)'s guidance, many weaker spirits were able to find their way as well, and the practice became so easy that it became a place—the Far Shores. Here, the spirits can safely wait until Satakal has passed and a new skin has emerged. There is no hunger or thirst in the Far Shores, but there are ample martial challenges to keep Redguard warrior-spirits engaged for eternity.

### Mantellan Crux
The [Mantellan Crux](Lore_Mantellan_Crux.md) was a small, self-contained plane located in [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) that was designed as a puzzle to test those who would seek the heart of [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md). Its name was derived from the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md), a large soul gem that was once used to power the Numidium. The plane served as the resting place and prison of the Mantella after it was blasted from [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) at the dawn of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) by [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md).

### Sands Behind the Stars
The [Sands Behind the Stars](Lore_Sands_Behind_the_Stars.md), also known as **Llesw'er**, is the realm to which the souls of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) travel at the end of their days. Described as a paradise promised to the Khajiit by the [Riddle'Thar](Lore_Riddle%27Thar.md), souls are carried there by [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md)'s embrace, as long as they have followed the true path of the moons. It is said that the realm is filled with dunes formed of [sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md), and a "warmth without end".<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Ta T-46)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOLMD-47)</sup> Legends state that the name "Llesw'er" may have been the inspiration for name of the Khajiiti homeland, [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-PGE1E-48)</sup> It is said that Khajiiti spirits await here until the [Next Pounce](Lore_Kalpa.md),<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOLMD-47)</sup> where Khenarthi will call upon their combined might to fight for creation at the end of time.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TSS-49)</sup> It is also believed that it is in the [Moonshadow](Lore_Moonshadow.md) where [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) tends the Gates of the Crossing—the bridge between Nirni and the afterlife beyond.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONMM-50)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ONSR-51)</sup> It is at this place where Khajiit souls initially go, and if deemed worthy, they are then guided to the Sands Behind the Stars.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Book-52)</sup><sup>:152</sup>

### Sovngarde
[Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md) is the storied afterlife of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), a part of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) where the greatest Nord heroes go when they die to enjoy an eternity of feasting and merriment, and where the [gods](Lore_Gods.md) [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) and [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md) reside after having died to bring about the current [world](Lore_Mundus.md). It is ruled by Shor, created by him long ago through his [Clever Magic](Lore_Clever_Craft.md), and contains the Hall of Valor, a supposedly gigantic feasting hall where the mead "flows like a waterfall". It is said that in Sovngarde even the tedium of immortality is unknown, as spectral foes wait in the surrounding shadows, to do battle with any who would test their mettle. At least one [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) was found within the realm, and many more spoke of it.

Those who cross the threshold of the living and enter the afterlife are greeted below the red glow of the eternal sunset, following the pathway down between the mammoth tusks and the monolithic cowled statues and into the valley of mists—the Shadowed Vale. Tsun currently guards the mythical Whalebone Bridge past the Vale, which leads to the Hall of Valor. At Shor's bidding, he has taken on the role of the master of trials, asking new arrivals to the utopia to prove their strength in combat against him before they can enter Shor's Hall. Clawthanes, the spirits of wolves who have reached Sovngarde, aid Tsun in this duty, keeping watch alongside him. It is said that, within Shor's Hall of Valor, protected by the power of the god himself, no gloom can grip the heart for long, and there are no shadows. However it is also said that, as it is a realm of the dead, Sovngarde is a place where the living shouldn't linger. Most Nords agree that [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) leads the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of the dead into Sovngarde, which is why she is known as the *Kiss at the End*.

Some people regarded Sovngarde as a myth by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

### Necromancer's Moon
The [Necromancer's Moon](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md), also known as **the Revenant**, is a [moon](Lore_Moons.md) and the divine body of the God of Worms, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md). ### Nirn
[Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) (which means '[Gray Maybe](Lore_Aurbis.md)' in [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex_Languages.md)),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> or the **mortal Aurbis**, is the Mortal Plane, the planet upon which Tamriel and the mortal races reside. Some sources use the terms *Nirn* and *Mundus* synonymously, though others describe Mundus as a more expansive structure which extends past Nirn itself and encompasses additional regions and planes.. The origin of Nirn is a matter shrouded in much ambiguity, and different creation myths propose different events leading to its creation. It is commonly thought to have been created during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) by the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) and [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), based on [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s inspiration and, per some sources, [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md)' designs. Though other sources propose different origins, which include Nirn being a direct creation of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md), or being a [deity](Lore_Gods.md), the goddess Nirni. Some sources claim that the existence of Nirn and Mundus, normally impossible, was allowed to come into being through the power of the [Magna-Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md), the Green Star, the embodiment of the feminine power of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) that exists everywhere and nowhere at once.

## Parallel Realms
These realms exist in parallel with the physical realm of Mundus.

### Nature Realm
The [Nature Realm](Lore_Nature_Realm.md) is a realm of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) which exists in parallel with the physical realm. It is a realm of spirits. The Nature Realm is a mirror image of the physical world, but tinged green. The spirits of physical beings can be seen in the Nature Realm, but cannot be interacted with.

The [Glenmoril Wyrd](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md) possess the ability to enter the Nature Realm as [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) spirits, and can use this to bypass obstacles in the physical realm.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) named Uela and her Dark Witnesses occupied Jackdaw Cove, a forest in the [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth.md) region of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Using dark [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) magic, Uela corrupted the forest, weakening the local Glenmoril wyresses in the process. Uela specialized in utilizing [crows](Lore_Crow.md) as spies and messengers, magically possessing their physical bodies. These crow spirits were helpless and could do nothing but watch from the Nature Realm as she controlled their physical forms. In order to reach Uela and kill her, a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) wyress named Rashan guided [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) into the Nature Realm in order to commune with the crow spirits and bypass her defenses.

### Shadow Wood
The [Shadow Wood](Lore_Shadow_Wood.md) was a parallel version of the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), a realm of darkness and evil. It was a mirror image of the Valenwood, but lay in perpetual darkness, inhabited by [shades](Lore_Shadow.md) and corrupted [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md).

Following the death of Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), his corpse was stolen by the remnants of the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md) led by Vicereeve [Pelidil](Lore_Pelidil.md). Using the power of the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), Pelidil returned Naemon from the dead as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) to lead armies of undead and unleash corruption in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md). The Shade of Naemon set about creating a new realm, the Shadow Wood, by corrupting the forests of Greenshade. He planned to transform all of Valenwood in this manner, supplanting it with the Shadow Wood. Naemon envisioned that the Shadow Wood would ultimately consume all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

It has a tendency to vanish from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), being placed in a pocket realm for centuries at a time by the magic of the Psijics. It is said that this plane exists in a place harder to reach than even [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) or even realms "beyond" it, where even the magic of the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) can't reach, thus allowing for the Order to research the truths of [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) without interruption. The island is protected by the Psijic Order's warding magic and only those that the Psijics have attuned to the wards can enter. Permanent portals leading to Artaeum, wherever it might be, exist in certain Psijic ruins in Summerset such as the Keep of the Eleven Forces.

The Isle of Artaeum once again disappeared around 100 years before the [civil war](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and has not been seen since.

Directly beneath the island's [Ceporah Tower](Lore_Ceporah_Tower.md) lies the Dreaming Cave, which houses a portal that can be used to travel to any realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and beyond, and is described as the place where all the [magicka](Lore_Magicka.md) and other energies in Artaeum pool, like water dripping from a chain.

The Isle of Artaeum was home to [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), the leader of [Necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

### Dreamsleeve
The [Dreamsleeve](Lore_Dreamsleeve.md) is an obscure and rarely mentioned concept or construct. Disparate functions have been ascribed to it, though they are not necessarily mutually exclusive.

Dreamsleeve "transmission" has been mentioned as a method of communication which has been used to contact those across vast distances. The Dreamsleeve has been described as a conduit for sending special transmissions, used by magicians and Imperial clerks. To reach it, one must have at least part of their brain constantly meditating, as one aspect of the conduit is its ability to carry images of concepts not yet "real". It is utilized as a rapid means of magical communication, related to [telepathy](Lore_Telepathy.md) and "Memospores". In order to interpret the prophetic dream of a mortal, [Tutor Riparius](Lore_Tutor_Riparius.md) had to "sleevestroke" the mortal's "concept-organ".

As a result, [Nocturnal Lieutenant](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md) [Jaciel Morgen](Lore_Jaciel_Morgen.md) fell into a deep despair and drove her own spirit into the Dreamsleeve. This left the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) physically present but mostly unresponsive, as if in a deep sleep. Only the self-sacrifice of her servant Deyanira Katrece was enough to awaken her.

According to the teachings of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), "mortals leave the dreaming-sleeve of birth the same, unmantled save for the symbiosis with our mothers". It is through the abandonment of the mother that mortals therefore enter the domain of Lord Dagon, the Prince of Destruction.

### Wing of the Indrik‎‎
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg) Wing of the Indrik
The [Wing of the Indrik](Lore_Wing_of_the_Indrik.md) is a pocket realm belonging to the [Luminary Indrik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Luminary_Indrik). It was shaped and hewn into being in the same way he chose his form. He considers it a pleasant respite from the larger world, a place to find peace and clear his mind of stray thoughts. He believed that realms of other [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md) could serve different purposes. Like other Luminaries, he formed his own pocket realm at birth, though the reason remains unknown.The Indrik felt that [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), in its wisdom, did not wish the Luminaries to be both alone and wandering.

### Wing of the Netch
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/ON-interior-Wing_of_the_Netch.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Wing_of_the_Netch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Wing_of_the_Netch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Wing_of_the_Netch.jpg) Wing of the Netch
The [Wing of the Netch](Lore_Wing_of_the_Netch.md) is a pocket realm belonging to the [Luminary Netch](Lore_The_Netch.md). It was shaped and hewn into being in the same way it chose its form. The reason for the realm's formation was unknown, though the [Luminary Indrik](Lore_The_Indrik.md) theorized that the realms of the [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md) had formed due to the wisdom of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), which did not wish for the Luminaries to be both alone and wandering.
