# High Desert

| High Desert | |
| --- | --- |
| Type | Province |
| Continent | [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) |

High Desert is a province of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RHH-1)</sup> known for its towering mountains rich in [onyx](Lore_Onyx.md) deposits,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LAB-2)</sup> and vast deserts. One of its more remote desert regions was called [Hunding](Lore_Hunding_(region).md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RHH-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/1/13/LO-people-Frandar_Hunding.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Frandar_Hunding.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Frandar_Hunding.jpg) A depiction of Frandar Hunding Little is known of the history of High Desert. [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md), who hailed from the province was named after the region of Hunding as his full name was Frandar do Hunding Hel Ansei No Shira. His family had deep roots in the province, with their lineage stretching back to the dawn of recorded history in High Desert, where they were known as artisans and mystics. Hunding's grandfather served as a retainer to the Elden Yokeda [Mansel Sesnit](Lore_Mansel_Sesnit.md) and played a key role in the unification battles before Elden Yokeda's assassination.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RHH-1)</sup>

Another of the notable landmarks of the region was [Hattu Mountain](Lore_Hattu_Mountain.md) where Frandar Hunding was developing his philosophy of the [Way of the Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Way_of_the_Sword), which he later recorded in the *[Book of Circles](Lore_Book_of_Circles.md)* while living as a hermit in a cave within the mountains of High Desert.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RHH-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-3)</sup>

During the [War of the Singers](Lore_War_of_the_Singers.md), Emperor [Hira](Lore_Hira.md) attempted to invade the province of High Desert but was defeated by the united forces of the [Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword_Singers), led by Frandar Hunding and supported by his son, [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DTS-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGT-5)</sup> After the war, Frandar Hunding led his followers to the great port city of Arch in the province of [Seawind](Lore_Seawind.md). From there, they ventured eastward, crossing into Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RHH-1)</sup>

Although most of the continent of Yokuda sank in [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-6)</sup> some parts of the province, particularly around the Hattu Mountains, remained inhabited well into the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) by Hunding Zealots.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LAB-2)</sup> Texts by the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) scholar [Destri Melarg](Lore_Destri_Melarg.md) speak of High Desert, offering insights into its history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RHH-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DTS-4)</sup>
