[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/SR-npc-Ahlam.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ahlam.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ahlam.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ahlam.jpg) Ahlam **Ahlam**, a [Redguard](Skyrim_Redguard.md) [priestess](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Priest), is a healer at the [Temple of Kynareth](Skyrim_Temple_of_Kynareth.md) in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md). She is the wife of [Nazeem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nazeem), who owns [Chillfurrow Farm](Skyrim_Chillfurrow_Farm.md). If [Arcadia](Skyrim_Arcadia.md) is killed, Ahlam will take over running [Arcadia's Cauldron](Skyrim_Arcadia%27s_Cauldron.md).

The couple never sets foot on the farm, but are permanent residents of the [Drunken Huntsman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Drunken_Huntsman) and Ahlam sleeps there every night between midnight and 9am. While her self-satisfied husband spends all of his time belittling the citizens of Whiterun, Ahlam uses her healing skills in the Temple of Kynareth from 9am to 9pm, interrupted only by a breath of fresh air near the Gildergreen tree between noon and 4pm. She spends her evenings in the upstairs area of the Huntsman, looking at her lazy husband while he sleeps.

While her husband is obviously wealthy, Ahlam makes a more humble appearance and wears farm [clothes](Skyrim_Clothes.md#000209a6) and [boots](Skyrim_Boots.md#000209a5). She carries an [iron dagger](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md), along with a selection of [common items and gold](Skyrim_Leveled_Lists.md#Common). She also carries the [key to Wintersand Manor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Key_to_Wintersand_Manor).

## Dialogue
When spoken to she can potentially say:

- *"Looking for my husband Nazeem? Check the Jarl's backside. That's usually where he stuffs himself these days."*
- *"If you run into my husband Nazeem, remind him that he's not married to himself."*
- *"Men are all alike, from Skyrim to Hammerfell. They care only for war and politics, and treat their women like cattle."*
- *"Are you married? You look the type. Make sure you treat your wife with the respect she deserves."* (Only if you are male)
- *"Here you stand, talking to a married woman. Why? Am I that interesting? Or perhaps there are other things on your mind."*

If Nazeem is dead, she expresses self-pity about her status as a widow with:

- *"Here you stand, talking to a still grieving widow. Why? Am I that interesting? Or perhaps there are other things on your mind."*

## Notes
- Nazeem and Ahlam were originally meant to live in their own house, Wintersand Manor, rather than bunk at The Drunken Huntsman. A related faction, a key, location data, and locklist a still exist for the house in the game's data. - In a [design document](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Skyrim_Cities%27_Design_Excerpts), two large houses, presumably manors, were shown to be located next to [Dragonsreach](Skyrim_Dragonsreach.md) as part of a "Nobles District". In the final game, only Dragonsreach exists at the very top layer of the city, being the only building actually part of the Cloud District.
- The *[Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)* DLC adds a duplicate of Ahlam to the files. This duplicate is internally known as `DLC1Test Angry`, and is almost completely identical to Ahlam. However, the duplicate doesn't have any dialogue, and her mood is set to "Angry", rather than "Neutral". It's unknown for what purpose this test NPC was made exactly.
