Cliff Racers (also spelled as **Cliffracers** or **Cliff-Racers**), known to consist of species such as **Long-tailed Cliff Racers** and **Girdle-tailed Cliff Racers**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gen-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HJ-2)</sup> are large flying [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md) found throughout [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They possess long tails and large vertical sails along their spines,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gen-1)</sup> and do not have legs. They are closely related to [Cliff Striders](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md) and [Cliff Darters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cliff_Darter).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCL-4)</sup> They are very aggressive and will attack anyone in sight. Cliff Racers have good vision range, and will often detect and follow travelers without being noticed. This can result in swarms of them descending upon the unwary adventurer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/LG-cardart-Supreme_Dragon.png/200px-LG-cardart-Supreme_Dragon.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Supreme_Dragon.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Supreme_Dragon.png) A Dragon in Morrowind There are several differing theories as to their origins. Their alleged ancestors, known as [Bat Lizards](Lore_Bat_Lizard.md) were said to have been encountered by [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). They were native to a forested island theorized to have been [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Some historians theorize that Cliff Striders, which are closely related to Cliff Racers, have a shared ancestry with [Hackwings](Lore_Hackwing.md). This possible divergence may have resulted from [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) raiding [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) for thousands of years to bring back things they deemed valuable, such as slave labor, livestock, and critters.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAMQA-7)</sup> One account states Cliff Racers migrated to the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) from parts unknown where, thanks to their numbers, they overwhelmed the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) population that lived there and drove them out.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GN-8)</sup> Though another claims that the [Skylamps](Lore_Skylamp.md) ate all the Dragons of Morrowind.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Po M-9)</sup>

Vvardenfell locals in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) used to claim that a visit to Vvardenfell wasn't a true visit unless you had Cliff Racers diving at you.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Though Cliff Racers of that time were smaller and not as actively dangerous as those of later eras.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

Due to their pest-like nature, in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) Cliff Racers were driven from Vvardenfell by [Saint Jiub the Eradicator](Lore_Jiub.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRJiub-12)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCL-4)</sup> although they have been known to exist in mainland Morrowind well into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRMjoll-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/BM-interior-The_Greathall_02.jpg/200px-BM-interior-The_Greathall_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-interior-The_Greathall_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-interior-The_Greathall_02.jpg) A taxidermied Cliff Racer in the Greathall of Skaal Village circa 3E 427 [Racer Plumes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Racer_Plumes) are used locally and throughout the Empire as decorations for garments and household goods.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gen-1)</sup> Pillows are known to be stuffed with racer plumes.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> These plumes are also used to make levitation potions.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

Cliff Racers are sometimes taxidermied and used in decoration.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Their light and hollow bones are used as cost-effective quills.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

Most Cliff Racer meat is inedible or extremely tough but it can be stewed to make a ragout.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-RFV-20)</sup> Cliff Racer tails are fatty and tolerable. Dunmer prepare them by boiling [trama root](Lore_Trama_Root.md) for a whole day until the roots are soft, the tails are then added until browned. The roots are then placed onto a plate and the tails over the top of the roots. A little water is then added, finally [bittergreen](Lore_Bittergreen.md) is sprinkled onto the dish and it is served.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-RFV-20)</sup>

Cliff Racers are the subject of an old Vvardenfell folk [song](Lore_Songs.md#Dunmer_Songs).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-21)</sup>
