## History

### Early Life
Aubk-I was the second child and only daughter of King [Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md) and Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). In her youth, the princess was described as athletic, beautiful, and charming; the favorite child of the royal couple.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DFMissing-4)</sup>

In [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402), the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) erupted between Sentinel and [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md). The following year, Daggerfall won a decisive victory at the [Battle of Cryngaine Field](Lore_Battle_of_Cryngaine_Field.md), in which both Camaron and King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of Daggerfall were slain.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFC-5)</sup> Sentinel surrendered, and to help restore friendship between the kingdoms, Aubk-i was wed to the new king of Daggerfall, [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFC-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NWOB-6)</sup> However, she was popularly considered in Sentinel to be a prisoner of war.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NWOB-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FWOB-7)</sup>

### The Warp in the West
In [3E 401](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_401), Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) had sent a letter of some importance to [Mynisera](Lore_Mynisera.md), the Queen of Daggerfall at the time.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFC-5)</sup> Due to the War of Betony, the courier was delayed and when it arrived, Aubk-i was queen.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFC-5)</sup> According to a witness, when she read the letter her jaw dropped and eyes widened in amazement,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DFCyndassa-8)</sup> and she became quite nervous of her mother-in-law and the Emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10th-9)</sup>. According to the emperor, the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md) had been found.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup> According to legend, whoever bore the Totem could control [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), an ancient [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) used by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to conquer [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup>.

Aubk-i promised to deliver the letter to Mynisera,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DFCyndassa-8)</sup> but the Dowager Queen never got it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DFMynisera-10)</sup> Some say the letter was stolen by the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) and sold to [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog.md), chief of the [Minat](Lore_Minat.md) Orcs and leader of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md);<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFMorgiah-11)</sup> others say it was Queen Aubk-i herself who sold it to the Orc chieftain.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFC-5)</sup> Another source posits that Aubk-i's lady in waiting, [Cyndassa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Cyndassa), understood that the information in the letter was valuable even if she did not understand its content, and sold it to the highest bidder.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10th-9)</sup> Regardless, Gortwog sold copies of the letter to the major powers in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region, setting off a power struggle for possession of the Totem and Numidium.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-1)</sup>

The event is now known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWITW-3)</sup> [Reality was distorted](Lore_Dragon_Break.md); seven factions simultaneously activated Numidium to their own ends.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWITW-3)</sup> The resulting death and devastation has been described as "tragic, and beyond mortal comprehension",<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWITW-3)</sup> but when the dust had settled the region had become more unified than at any point in modern history, leading some to dub the events the "Miracle of Peace".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3High-12)</sup>

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Aubk-i and Gothryd had four children and were beloved by the realm.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWITW-3)</sup> Their son [Camaron](Lore_Camaron_(prince).md)'s marriage to Lady Kelmena of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), suggested peaceful relations would continue for some time.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3High-12)</sup>
