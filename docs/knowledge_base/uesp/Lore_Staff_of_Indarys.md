# Staff of Indarys

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/OBR-item-Staff_of_Indarys.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Staff_of_Indarys.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Staff_of_Indarys.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Staff_of_Indarys.jpg) Staff of Indarys The Staff of Indarys was a family heirloom of the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) [Indarys](Lore_Indarys.md) family.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CAI-1)</sup> In appearance, it was a gnarly, thorny wooden [staff](Lore_Staff.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOI-2)</sup> Its motif of thorny vines matched that of [Thornblade](Lore_Thornblade.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Thornblade-3)</sup> the other heirloom and featured symbols of the County [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) during the Indarys rule, as well as emblems of the local order of the [Knights of the Thorn](Lore_Knights_of_the_Thorn.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBShield-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBBanner-5)</sup> It was imbued with a potent [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) that commanded lightning and weakened the strength of those struck by its magic.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOI-2)</sup>

## History
Little is known about the past of the Staff of Indarys. Alongside with Thornblade it was regarded as an heirloom of the Indarys family. According to Count [Andel Indarys](Lore_Andel_Indarys.md) it was held in Castle Cheydinhal for several generations.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CAI-1)</sup> However, the Count himself was an immigrant and the first in his family to hold the title and inhabit this castle.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBMN-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GTC-7)</sup> It is possible that the staff was awarded to the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) after they rescued Andel's son, Farwil, from the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). However, it is unknown whether the staff left the possession of the Indarys lineage, as it was one of the heirlooms, alongside Thornblade, that were offered as a reward for the rescue.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CAI-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWK-8)</sup>
