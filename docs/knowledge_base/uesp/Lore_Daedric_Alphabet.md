# Daedric Alphabet

The **Daedric alphabet** is a symbolic typeface used in replacement of the standard English alphabet. The alphabet was designed by Mark Jackson<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> for [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire) and was called "Daedric" because of its use by the invading [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) forces. The release of [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind) revealed that both the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and the Daedra use the Daedric alphabet extensively. Most scholars agree that the Dunmeri use of the Daedric alphabet is a remnant of when they worshipped Daedra before the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md).

The inscription of Daedric letters often serves an arcane purpose, such as in the creation of [Sigils](Lore_Sigil.md).

## Xayah and Yahkem
[![](https://images.uesp.net/4/46/MW-banner-Tel_Fyr.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Tel_Fyr.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Tel_Fyr.png) An example of a Yahkem on a Dunmeri flag [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/MW-item-Daedric_Axe_Edge.jpg/150px-MW-item-Daedric_Axe_Edge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Axe_Edge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Axe_Edge.jpg) Lettering on the Daedric Axe - from top to bottom: EFCPHEQX - A rare example of a Xayah. The Daedric letters Xayah (X) and Yahkem (Y) are often omitted from the Daedric alphabet in scrolls and books, but sometimes one of these letters show up in signs and banners. The most well known appearance of one of these two letters is of the Daedric letter Yahkem, which appears on the banner for [Tel Fyr](Lore_Tel_Fyr.md) as seen in the picture on the right where the larger letter on the upper right is the letter Yahkem. The letter Xayah can be seen inscribed on a [Daedric Battle Axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Daedric_Battle_Axe) in Morrowind.<sup>[nb 1](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Alternate forms
Aside from the Xayah and Yahkem, five other letters have alternate forms, as seen in the chart. The Doht (D) and Jeb (J) characters are often mirror-imaged from their default orientations, and the Hekem (H) and Yoodt (U) characters also have noticeably differing variants, Hekem's alternate form lacking a prominent vertical stroke, and Yoodt's being shown with a rounded bottom instead of flat. Ayem (A) can also be seen in mirrored form at the end of the Arena canton [banners](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Arena.png) and signs in Vivec.

## Clustering
Another irregularity with Daedric text is that it does not have to be written from left-to-right. For decorative purposes, each word in a phrase is often clustered together with the first letter being significantly larger and colored differently than the rest. The following is an example of a sign hanging over an archway in [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md), a city in [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind), as well as an annotated version of the banner with the words separated:

- [![The banner of Gnisis](https://images.uesp.net/0/0f/MW-banner-Gnisis.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Gnisis.png) [![Annotated banner](https://images.uesp.net/b/be/LO-lettering-Daedric_Clusters.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-lettering-Daedric_Clusters.jpg)

Notice how each of the major words in the phrase above has its first letter much larger than the rest and in a different color. It is common for the larger letter to be colored similarly to the backdrop, as with the two letter Sehts (S) in this example.

The clusters in the example above can be arranged in the left-to-right format as follows:

- The Thrice Sealed House Withstands the Storm

Thus, when the Daedric letters are transliterated into English letters, the message reads:

- **THE THRICE SEALED HOUSE WITHSTANDS THE STORM**

## Examples of use

### *An Elder Scrolls Legend: Battlespire*
The daedra and their alphabet are a constant theme throughout *[Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire)*.

Every level features the following three:

- [Sigil Wards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Sigils#Wardings_of_Entry): A blue floating Daedric letter which damages the player on touch.
- [Sigils of Entry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Sigils#Sigil_of_Entry): Letter allowing the player to pass the matching Sigil Ward.
- [Sigils](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Sigils#Sigil): Badges of military rank used by the Daedric invaders. On use, allows the player to become ethereal for a short time.

[Protonymics & Neonymics](Lore_Nymic.md):

- Protonymic of Mehrunes Dagon: Lehkelogah : LKLG
- Neonymic of Mehrunes Dagon: Djehkeleho-dehbe-effehezepeh : JKLO-DB-FEZP
- Neonymic of Xivilai Molath: Wegerohseh-chehkohieu : WGRS-CKU
- Neonymic of Faydra Shardai: Nepehkweh-kodo : NPK-KD

Notes:

- [Roht = Rishaal = Peytifar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Peytifar_Note)
- [Meht = Gatanas = Memasgiat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Memasgiat_Note)
- [Zyr = Zenaide = Berkul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Berkul_Note)

Other:

- The stoles of the [Daedra Counts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Daedra_Count), [Daedra Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Daedra_Lord), [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Dremora), [Sumeer Jabran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Sumeer_Jabran), and [Clarentavious Valisious](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Clarentavious_Valisious) all appear to read ABMNOPI.
- The [Daedric Crescent](Lore_Daedric_Crescent.md) seen in various places spells out ABMNOPRHVIM. - This also applies to the *Morrowind* version of the Crescent.
- The sacred code DUSK must be entered in Daedric letters to restore the flow of Nocturnal mana in [Shade Perilous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Level_4).
- A gate in [Shade Perilous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Level_4) opens to Djemekweh : JMK (BK4_030)
- In the unpacked Sigils.txt, the Daedric alphabet is numbered 51-76 rather than 1-26.
- X and Y did not exist. Q was not yet labeled Quam.

### *Redguard*
Usage of the alphabet is largely limited to N'Gasta and Clavicus Vile.

- [N'Gasta's Spellbook](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:N%27Gasta%27s_Spellbook) is written in the alphabet.
- The Oht sigil is used for the doors behind the [Guardians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Guardian).

### *Morrowind*
*[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)* made extensive use of the Daedric alphabet.

- All [magical scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Scrolls) have an inscription written in Daedric (Most often "WOE UPON (Y)OU", but some have more interesting messages).
- The spell icons make use of the characters, though only as design elements, not readable text. To see examples of this, see [Spell Icon Letters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Spell_Icon_Letters).
- Many of the cities in Morrowind feature banners displaying the city name in Daedric.
- The city of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md) has various religious platitudes written in Daedric on [banners](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Vivec_Banners) throughout the city.
- The letters are used as textural elements of various items, such as [Bonemold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Bonemold) armor.
- The [box art for Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-cover-Morrowind_Box_Art.jpg) and both expansions has the names of the six Dunmer Great Houses inscribed around the sides. The game launcher incorporates this feature as well.
- Some permanent [Paper Lanterns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Paper_Lantern) have the message "FORGE DARKNESS INTO LIGHT" on their panels.
- Certain clothing items bear Daedric text, an example being one type of expensive robe, where the messages "LEARN B(Y) SERVING" and "FAITH IS ONL(Y) LAW " can be read on the yellow sash. ([image 1](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Mehra_Drora.jpg), [image 2](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Danso_Indules.jpg))
- The [belt buckle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Bonelord.jpg) worn by [Bonelords](Lore_Bonelord.md) reads "UOS".

[![](https://images.uesp.net/8/8d/TR-banner-SAV.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-banner-SAV.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-banner-SAV.png) s Av - we're in Almalexia's city - Small chests have "ASV", an abbreviation for "Almsivi", repeated on their lids. In Mournhold, Almalexia gets top billing - there are many tiles with the letters "SAV" with the "A" much larger than the other letters.
- The opening to Morrowind is almost entirely Daedric, save for the end which changes into English, saying "Many fall, but one remains."
- Outside of many of the towns in Vvardenfell are obelisks which read "WELCOME" in Daedric lettering.

### *Shadowkey*
The key image used in [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Shadowkey) has the text "DOUBT IS THE KE(Y) TO KNOWLEDGE" along its stem, and "JONES" right at the bottom, the surname of the artist who drew the image ([Mark Jones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Mark_Jones)). This can only be seen on [large versions](http://www.imperial-library.info/content/shadowkey-poster-1) of the image, however.

### *Oblivion*
*[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* used the alphabet in text far less often than in Morrowind, but it does appear on textures, mostly in the realm of Oblivion itself or other locations associated with the Daedra. For example, these three inscriptions are in Mehrunes Dagon's plane: "HERE IS NO, THERE IS NO", "OBLIVION AWAITS", "MEHRUNES DAGON BECKONS" (last one is in every [Sigillum Sanguis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Sigillum_Sanguis)). Every [Oblivion Gate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:A_Gate_to_Oblivion) is in the shape of an Oht. You can also find this text in some books. For example, the *[Bible of the Deep Ones](Lore_Bible_of_the_Deep_Ones.md)* (found in the [Hackdirt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Hackdirt) chapel in Oblivion) contains almost all Daedric lettering. In addition, the border of the [Oblivion box](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-cover-Oblivion_Box_Art.jpg) contains the phrase "FOR LORD DAGON FOREVER REBORN IN BLOOD AND FIRE FROM THE WATERS OF OBLIVION" repeated several times in Daedric text; a phrase that also appears inside the letters of the word "Oblivion" on the same box art and in the *[Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md)*. Similarly, the Shivering Isles box cover reads "THERE IS NO GENIUS WITHOUT A TOUCH OF MADNESS", a phrase taken from the *Epistles* of [Seneca the Younger](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seneca_the_Younger).

### *Skyrim*
In *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)*, Daedric appears in several places, including:

- the [fire](Skyrim_Fire_Rune.md), [frost](Skyrim_Frost_Rune.md), [lightning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lightning_Rune), [ash](Skyrim_Ash_Rune.md), [frenzy](Skyrim_Frenzy_Rune.md), [poison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Poison_Rune), and [paralysis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Paralysis_Rune) runes,
- on the ground, spiralling around the pillar at the [Sacellum of Boethiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sacellum_of_Boethiah),
- a short inscription on the tomb of [Jurgen Windcaller](Skyrim_Jurgen_Windcaller.md), which is simply "WINDCALLER".
- various documents contain recipes written in Daedric for items that can be created at the Atronach Forge.
- on the base of altars in [Apocrypha](Skyrim_Apocrypha.md) in the Dragonborn add-on, that reads "AS FOR THE REST THE WEAK SHALL BE WINNOWED THE TIMID SHALL BE CAST DOWN THE MIGHTY SHALL TREMBLE AT MY FEET AND PRAY FOR PARDON". This text is also featured in the [*Mysterium Xarxes*](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md).
- the [steel plate helmet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate_Armor) has "Nord Plate Nord Plate" written on the brow.

### ESO
The Daedric alphabet appears in a number of locations in *[The Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)* as well. The format here is less uniform than in previous games, as letters, words, or complete phrases may be written backwards, upside down, or mirrored. In addition to specific instances, oftentimes singular letters are used within circles.

#### Combat Abilities & Runes
- The [Sorcerer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Sorcerer) ability [Daedric Curse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Daedric_Curse) reads "CONJURE", "SHOCK" and "ALTER"
- The [Fire Rune](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Fire_Rune) ability reads "FIRESONG"
- The [Nightblade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Nightblade) ability [Soul Shred](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Soul_Shred) reads "ILLUSION" and "STEALTH"

- Summoned [Storm Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Storm_Atronach) bear shackles reading "BOUND IN SERVITUDE"
- Necromancer ritual circles read "HAIL KING OF WORMS"
- Coldharbour rune circles read "MOLAG BAL RULES COLD HARBOR". Note the alternate spelling of Coldharbour.
- The effect of the [Fighters Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Fighters_Guild) skill [Circle of Protection](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Circle_of_Protection) reads "FOR GLORY"
- Rune circles in [Wailing Prison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:The_Wailing_Prison) read "DEATH" and "FEAR"

## Resources

### Daedric Font
This font set was created by Dongle, and includes the "official" X and Y. This is the font used by all articles using Daedric characters on this site, so you should install it in order to view these pages correctly.

- [Obliviontt.zip](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Obliviontt.zip) 135 kb
- For Windows: Unzip and copy the Oblivion.ttf file into the WINDOWS\FONTS (95, 98/ME, XP, Vista and 7) or WINNT\FONTS (NT, 2000) directory.
- For Mac: Unzip (Stuffit Expander should work), and copy the Oblivion.TTF file into your /Library/Fonts folder.
- For Linux: Open the TTF files and click on "Install Font".

### Daedric Translation
If using the Daedric font above, text can be translated by simply pasting into Notepad or any text-editing software and changing to a regular English font.

Otherwise, Daedric can be approximated using standard [Unicode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicode) characters with this [Daedric Unicode translator](https://lingojam.com/Unicode Daedric Converter-).

If translating from an image or anything not written using a proper font, the Daedric characters can be typed individually into this [Daedric reverse translator](http://www.seajays.org.uk/morrowind/daedric.htm).
