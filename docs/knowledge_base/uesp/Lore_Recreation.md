## Board Games
- Altmer Heritage - A stacking puzzle game which uses stacked pairs of pieces representing ancestors where breeding mismatches result in tumbles<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Atmoran Axe Throwing - A board game in which players presumably throw axes at the board<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Angles and Arcs - A game in which players compete to calculate the most complex shapes in the shortest time played on a blackboard with a mathematical compass<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Chess<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Cross Stones - A popular [High Elf](Lore_High_Elf.md) board game<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Crystal-Column - An expensive tower-stacking game enjoyed by High Elves played with crystal blocks that tend to break on their first tumble<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Cut It Until It Dies - A deadly [Iron Orc](Lore_Iron_Orc.md) game<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Dematah - A traditional [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) token-based game, with a notable feature of the game being some or all of your tokens consisting of slaves; a variant called Traitor's Dematah contains an extra token, allowing one to replace an opponent's slave token with one's own<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>
- Diamond Duel - An ancient [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) board game adapted from a long-forgotten [Aldmeric](Lore_Aldmer.md) ritual enjoyed by [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) nobles<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Dragon's Duel - A complex strategy game played with a board and pieces in which [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) cults vie for power<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Hammergammon - A board game played with pieces and a multiplier die<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Honeycomb Checkers - A simple board game of hopping marbles over each other<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Nine-Shells - A game played in Murkmire<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-To MM-3)</sup>
- Nine-holes - A game played with pieces and a base<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>
- Payback - An [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) game played with 32 pawns: two sets 16 of a color<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Pearls of Pyandonea - An incredibly complex High Elf board game played with hundreds of tiny pieces<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Perchance Acorn - A classic [Wood Elf](Lore_Wood_Elf.md) acorn-hiding puzzle game played with an acorn, cups, and a board<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Shells and Stones - A game played on a reed mat in Murkmire<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-To MM-3)</sup>
- Shellback-gammon - A board game played with tiles and a board<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Silver Nine-Shells Set - An antique board game<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Snips and Tails - A less deadly version of Cut It Until It Dies<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Spoils of War - A popular game played with pieces and a board with a hexagonal grid<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Strife - An Imperial game played on a board with a series of triangular patterns<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Tabula - A faster version of backgammon, and the Imperial version lacks a doubling cube.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Triangle Chess - a game similar to chess but has three players.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Veloth's Conundrum - A traditional Dark Elf puzzle game<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>
- Where's Falinesti? - A casual board game played with pieces and a board<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOgames-1)</sup>

## Music
[Music](Lore_Music.md) (**Emeratu** in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md)) is a major part of most cultures across [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) with music and poetry commonly being heard in taverns and cities, and dedicated schools and institutions for the musical arts existing across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). There are a wide variety of [musical instruments](Lore_Instruments.md) found across all races and cultures, and music is also heavily featured in Tamrielic religions.

## Drugs
[Drugs](Lore_Drugs.md) are addictive, illicit, and often illegal substances with hallucinogenic or mood-altering effects which are ingested for recreational or ritualistic purposes. Many different drugs exist in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and beyond, and abusing them is generally disapproved of, with some exceptions.

Many of these drugs are just variants of [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md), such as [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) and the rarer potion known as Balmora Blue. Other notable drugs are [greenmote](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenmote) and [felldew](Lore_Felldew.md). Of course, Tamriel is also filled with many popular [alcoholic beverages](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md), such as Jagga, fermented pig's milk which is popular in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Various alchemical ingredients could be construed to have drug-like effects, but most are covered separately in the [Alchemy section](Lore_Alchemy.md).

## Sex
[Sex](Lore_Sex.md) is intimate physical contact between individuals, typically for the purposes of reproduction or pleasure. This article documents not just the biology, but also the various customs, stories and reputations the different cultures, religions and races of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) (and [beyond](Lore_Oblivion.md)) have regarding sex and reproduction. This article also touches on the reproduction of animals and on sexual dimorphism found both in fauna and in the various races.
