# Passive Creatures


## Aquatic Creatures
Fish can be found in most large bodies of water. Look for insects flying above the water to find fish below. Several species can be farmed with [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) by using the [Fish Hatchery](Skyrim_Fish_Hatchery.md) at [Windstad Manor](Skyrim_Windstad_Manor.md). The following fish yield items of the same name unless otherwise noted.

### Salmon
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/SR-creature-Salmon.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Salmon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Salmon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Salmon.jpg) A leaping Salmon Salmon are found swimming in the rivers and seas of Skyrim and are often seen jumping out of the water to ascend waterfalls and rapids. Catching salmon yields [salmon meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Meat) which is food, not an alchemical ingredient, as all other species of fish are. While the other species of fish tend to swim near the surface of bodies of water, salmon may be seen either near the surface or in the depths. Salmon may be shot with an arrow (or killed with various shouts) to make them easier to catch, then collected by using the action key. However, the prompt to do so may not come up. With the [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) add-on installed, [salmon roe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salmon_Roe) may be harvested from leaping salmon found in streams, or swimming salmon the player kills, but not from pre-existing [dead salmon](#Salmon_.28dead.29).

The [Fishing](Skyrim_Fishing.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation.md) adds [salmon](Skyrim_Salmon_(item).md) as an item, allowing them to be transported alive if caught.

## Insects
Despite their small size, insects are capable of pushing you and other NPCs around. Harvesting is also possible by using melee attacks, ranged attacks, or [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) on the target insect. Ingredients must then be collected from the ground.

### Hawk
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/SR-creature-Hawk.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Hawk.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hawk.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hawk.jpg) A Hawk [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e4/SR-creature-Hawk_Nest.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Hawk_Nest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hawk_Nest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Hawk_Nest.jpg) A Hawk in its nest Hawks are birds seen flying around Skyrim. They can be shot down, yielding [hawk beaks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hawk_Beak) and [hawk feathers](Skyrim_Hawk_Feathers.md); those pages provide details on where hawks can be found.

They can be killed with magic (including non-damaging spells like [Courage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Courage)), a few shouts (e.g. [Animal Allegiance](Skyrim_Animal_Allegiance.md), [Soul Tear](Skyrim_Soul_Tear.md), and [Kyne's Peace](Skyrim_Kyne%27s_Peace.md)), or shot with an arrow, although this may fling out of sight, as they are very light. If the body flies too far, the arrow can detach from the body and instead fly up into the sky.

Hawks will have a nest nearby that they return to from time to time. A good strategy is to attack hawks when they are in their nests using a low power spell such as [Flames](Skyrim_Flames.md), rather than [Fireballs](Skyrim_Fireball.md) or arrows, to keep their bodies from being flung out of sight.

At one point Hawks could be killed only once. Once they respawned, they became invincible. As of current patches the Hawks activators will respawn on the same standard 10 day cycle as everything else.<sup>[*verification needed — see [talk page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:talk_Passive_Creatures)*]</sup>

In Solitude, a hawk passes very close to the middle of the bridge between the towers that lead to the Emperor's guest quarters. If you wait about 30 seconds, its circling flight pattern will bring it closer, whereupon it can be killed with a close range weapon like a sword.

### Whispering Spirit<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/SR-creature-Whispering_Spirit.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Whispering_Spirit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Whispering_Spirit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Whispering_Spirit.jpg) A Whispering Spirit Whispering Spirits are invulnerable wisp-like creatures you can find within the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md). Some follow set routes, and some seem to just fly around randomly. You are unable to interact with them yourself in any way, as any attack has no effect on them. Despite this, [Bonemen](Skyrim_Boneman.md), [Wrathmen](Skyrim_Wrathman.md), and [Mistmen](Skyrim_Mistman.md) will all give chase to these creatures, either by running after and trying to attack them, or using ranged attacks. This can provide both a humorous sight, and a helpful distraction if you come across already present enemies. They only chase the spirits before detecting you, after which they will continue after the spirit for a short time before turning their attention to you.

Note that unlike other passive creatures listed here, whispering spirits are technically true creatures, as opposed to animated activators. They are similar to [wisps](Skyrim_Wisp.md), hostile ethereal monsters which accompany [wispmothers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wispmother). The name "Whispering Spirit" is taken from the game's data files, as the creature's name is never seen in game.

## Notes
- When harvesting passive creatures with shouts (fish, insects) for food/alchemy ingredients, note that insects (or their parts) fall when killed, but fish (of all types) float. This can hamper ingredient gathering over bodies of water, as the insects will sink to the bottom and be difficult to find, due to their tiny sizes.
- Salmon killed either by archery or shouts will not have any activation prompt to gather them, though you will still be able to when hovering the crosshairs over them and pressing the activation button.
- Despite their tiny sizes, insects can shove the player character around, if they happen to bump into them. You can also "ride" moths and butterflies if you can jump on one as it lands, and before it takes off: you will float off into the sky a few feet before falling back to the ground.
- There are at most 69 hawks in the base game, as 69 different entities link to the container that holds a hawk's death drops (hawk feathers, hawk beak) in the Creation Kit.
- Base game hawk nests will disappear once its hawk is killed, while the two nests added by *Hearthfire* remain after all hawks in the vicinity are slain. Base game hawk nests also lack loot of any sort, and are merely an area for the hawk to land, while the hawk nests added by the add-on yield [hawk's eggs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hawk%27s_Egg) when activated.
