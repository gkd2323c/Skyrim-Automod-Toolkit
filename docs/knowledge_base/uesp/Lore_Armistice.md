# Armistice


The terms of the Armistice were intended to make Morrowind a part of the Empire, while also allowing it a large degree of autonomy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Artisa-2)</sup> To succeed in this, Tiber Septim knew that he would have to severely limit Morrowind's military power. Accordingly, he successfully demanded Morrowind's surrender of [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) to the Empire and its acceptance of a substantial Imperial presence in terms of Legion soldiers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAH-1)</sup> In exchange, Morrowind was permitted to keep its autonomy, maintaining the power of the Tribunal Temple and the Great Houses,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OM-3)</sup> while being supervised by Imperial representatives in the capital, [Almalexia City](Lore_Almalexia_(city).md), and in each of the six newly-created administrative districts.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASHOM-10)</sup> Additionally, and perhaps most importantly in the minds of Dunmer elites, the practice of slavery in Morrowind was protected by the Empire.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BB-11)</sup> Dunmer religious practices were similarly protected.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OM-3)</sup>

The Armistice was supported most enthusiastically by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), with [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) grudgingly following Vivec's lead, while [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) did so only with the strong provisions protecting slavery.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OM-3)</sup> [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) remained neutral and isolationist throughout the proceedings, until the support of Hlaalu and Redoran was officially established, when they joined in on the support of the treaty.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OM-3)</sup> [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril) bowed to the will of the Tribunal, but remained hostile to the Empire; in fact, many Indoril nobles chose ritual suicide rather than to rule an occupied nation.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OM-3)</sup>

Following the Armistice, the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) became a Temple preserve with some small settlements maintained by the Great Houses as well as scattered [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Ashlanders) tribes. However, the island was not opened to large-scale settlement by civilians or exploitation by the East Empire Company until [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASHOM-10)</sup>
