# Padomaic Ocean

| --- | --- |
| Type | Body of Water |
| Appears in | Morrowind, [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Topal_Bay), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), ESO |

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/ON-place-Padomaic_Ocean.jpg/200px-ON-place-Padomaic_Ocean.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Padomaic_Ocean.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Padomaic_Ocean.jpg) The Padomaic Ocean, as seen from Morrowind's coast (ESO) The Padomaic Ocean is a vast body of water that surrounds most of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Surround)</sup> The Padomaic meets the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) at two locations within the Eltheric: off [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) in the north<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOContraband-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Bt GB-2)</sup> and at the [Blue Divide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Divide) in the south.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Blue Divide)</sup> Its easternmost known reach is the Eastern Sea, which borders [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Sermon17-3)</sup> The ocean's waters meet at least three continents, Tamriel to the west, Akavir to the east, and [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) to the north, with several smaller islands scattered between them.

The ocean lends its name to the [Padomaic Crest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomaic_Crest), a treacherous cliff region along the [Telvanni Peninsula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telvanni_Peninsula)'s eastern coast; the crest's name, in turn, derives from [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay), the primordial [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) of change.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Monomyth-4)</sup> The exact nature of its connection to the ocean remains unknown.

## History

### Origins
According to myth, during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), shortly after the creation of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) had interspersed seas, but no oceans. After the war between the Wandering and Old [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), the land was reshaped, sinking much of the land beneath new oceans, forming the Padomaic. Most of the domain of the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) was sunk into its waters, leaving only a small corner left standing, later known as [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), on Tamriel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAA-5)</sup>

After the Ehlnofey war, circa [ME 2500](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#ME_2500), the [et'Ada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) [convened](Lore_Convention.md) at the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) to decide the punishment of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), a key figure in the war.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Monomyth-4)</sup> He was killed by [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), but when both he and [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel) failed to destroy his [heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), Auriel fastened it to an arrow and shot it into the Padomaic,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Monomyth-4)</sup> forming [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

### Exploration
The Padomaic Ocean was first charted by [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) explorer [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) in the [Middle Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Middle_Merethic_Era).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FOTN-8)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup> In his unsuccessful search for [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), he mapped out the coasts of Tamriel, such as [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)'s [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) and, later named in his honor, [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). He returned to [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) with treasures, sparking wonder in the faraway land<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FOTN-8)</sup> and prompting other Aldmeri sailors to follow in his footsteps, further charting the coastline of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup>

In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Emperor [Uriel Septim V](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_V) explored the Padomaic and conquered several islands as part of his [invasion of Akavir](Lore_Third_Era_Wars.md#The_Invasion_of_Akavir): [Roscrea](Lore_Roscrea.md) in [3E 271](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_271), [Cathnoquey](Lore_Cathnoquey.md) in [3E 276](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_276), [Yneslea](Lore_Yneslea.md) in [3E 279](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_279), and [Esroniet](Lore_Esroniet.md) in [3E 284](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_284).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BHot Ev3-9)</sup> Uriel's Far East Fleet later discovered numerous islands and sandbars off the coast of Akavir, whose sheer quantity prevented their ships from making port.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RDa I-10)</sup>

Roscrea was later made a fiefdom and claimed by the [Kingdom of](Lore_Haafingar.md) [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) in [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), to further expand their power. After marrying the Queen [Macalla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Macalla) of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), King [Thian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thian) held a majority of control of the [Northern Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northern_Coast). Under his reign, the island was used to further chart the Sea of Ghosts' mysterious waters.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3ESR-11)</sup> Expeditions into the sea reached even to Atmora, with reports describing a place of permanent winter, with little life and no sign of human habitation.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-12)</sup>

## Composition
The waters of the Padomaic range from icy and treacherous in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> to placid in [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE1C-14)</sup>

### Creatures of Legend
The [Isle of Red Mist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Isle_of_Red_Mist)'s cannibal cult practiced graphic rituals that summoned ancient titans from the Sea of Ghosts. Taking the form of two giant female [turtles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Turtle), the ancient matrons were capable of pushing treasure-laden ships at speeds faster than the winds.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Saga3-25)</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), an [Emperor Crab](Lore_Emperor_Crab.md) [Guardian Spirit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit#Guardian_Spirit) awoke and caused havoc, driving off a force of guards from nearby [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> Eventually, with the help of an [adventurer](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) and usage of flaming catapult assaults, the crab's spirit was defeated.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Sea-ghosts are vengeful beasts who inhabit the eponymous Sea of Ghosts. During the onset of the Return, they massacred the crew of Harakk, the ship captained by Ysgramor's son, [Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Yat SG-28)</sup> amidst the Storm of Separation.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Songs2-29)</sup>
