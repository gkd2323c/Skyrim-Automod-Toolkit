# Holidays


## Morning Star

### [New Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival)
- Morning Star 1<sup>st</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg) A New Life Festival get-together The [New Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival) (also shortened to **New Life**) is a [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) -wide event that celebrates the birth of a new year and the death of the old year. In contrast to the [Old Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Life_Festival), New Life takes place at the beginning of [Morning Star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morning_Star). It was originally the celebration of the sun god, [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md), but over time it changed into a celebration of the gift of New Life, heralded by the sun. The New Life Festival also marks the start of the [Winter Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md#Winter_Solstice), when Magnus the Sun begins his return, and the days start to grow longer. Almost all major races in Tamriel celebrate the New Life Festival in their own culturally unique ways, and the celebrations themselves have been known to change over time. Many are symbolic of historical events or carry deeper meanings. Festivities include races, feasts, games, and dancing.

Many of the New Life Festival’s traditions have been influenced by the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) holiday of [Saturalia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saturalia), which over time has become closely associated with the festival and is sometimes regarded as almost synonymous with it. Most notably, this includes the decoration of evergreen trees topped with a shining light ornament, which are then called Saturalia Trees to reflect their Breton origins. Evergreen trees are valued for their symbolism of resilience and rebirth, and placing gifts beneath Saturalia Trees is a customary part of the celebration. During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) ’s role as a major trade hub helped Saturalia-inspired trees, decorations, and other seasonal goods spread widely, creating a booming market and establishing them as a familiar part of New Life Festival observances even among cultures with their own long-standing traditions. This adoption began during the time of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and the influence of these Breton traditions continues to be felt in New Life Festival celebrations into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

[Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s Summoning Day coincides with the New Life Festival, the 1st of Morning Star.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

Scour Day is a celebration held in most [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) villages on the day after [New Life](#New_Life_Festival). It was once the day one cleans up after New Life, but has changed into a party of its own.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:69</sup>

Ovank'a is the day the people of the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) offer prayers to [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) in the hopes of a mild and merciful year. It is considered very holy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:69</sup>

### Meridia's Summoning Day
- Morning Star 13<sup>th</sup>

In Daggerfall, this is the Summoning Day for [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### South Wind's Prayer
- Morning Star 14<sup>th</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>/15<sup>th</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CS-4)</sup><sup>:146</sup>

The 15<sup>th</sup> of Morning Star is a major holiday taken very seriously in Tamriel. The South Wind's Prayer is a plea by all the [religions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Religions) of Tamriel for a good planting season. Citizens flock to services in the cities' temples in the hopes of receiving free healing. Only some will be judged worthy of this service, but few can afford the temples' usual price.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CS-4)</sup><sup>:146</sup>

### Day of Lights
- Morning Star 16<sup>th</sup>

The Day of Lights is celebrated as a holy day by most villages in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) on the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay). It is a solemn day of prayer for a good farming and fishing year.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFC-2)</sup><sup>:69</sup>

A possibly unrelated Festival of Lights is a tradition in the [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) city of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md). Little candies are given out to celebrate.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

### Waking Day
- Morning Star 18<sup>th</sup>

The people in [Yeorth Burrowland](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yeorth_Burrowland) invented Waking Day in prehistoric times to wake the spirits of nature after a long, cold winter. It has evolved into a sort of orgiastic celebration of the end of winter.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HOTIB-6)</sup>

### [Heart's Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart%27s_Day)
- Sun's Dawn 16<sup>th</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9f/ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg) Heart's Day furnishing decorations [Heart's Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart%27s_Day) (also called **Lovers' Day**) is a holiday celebrated throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) on the 16th of [Sun's Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun%27s_Dawn) and serves as the central day of the week-long **Heart’s Week** festival, honoring love wherever it is found, among friends, lovers, and even passing strangers. In almost every house, the [Legend of the Lovers](Lore_Legend_of_the_Lovers.md) is sung for the younger generation. In honor of these lovers, Polydor and Eloisa, inns in cities across Tamriel offer a free room for visitors. It is said that if such kindness had been shown to these Lovers, it would always be springtime in the world.

Ironically, Heart's Day originated as the Summoning Day dedicated to [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine), the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of Debauchery. As newer religions rose to prominence, they appropriated the holiday and sanitized Sanguine’s influence in order to strengthen devotion to their own traditions. Since then, it has been predominantly associated with [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara), though some customs tied to [Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella) were not unheard of. Since its reinvention, Heart's Day has been commercialized to the point where some consider the holiday to have been invented by merchants as a way to make money.

To properly set the mood in the bedroom in honor of Heart's Day, flower petals, various sweets, and much more are brought in. Common recommended foods to share with a lover are [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) fondues and [sweetrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sweetroll), while Elves often have [snowberry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snowberry_Bush) cordials for their preferred beverage. Each item helps those so inclined to fulfill on the day's delightful promise with their partners in the adventure called life. Flowers associated with Dibella are picked for the occasion.

## First Seed

### Hermaeus Mora's Summoning Day
- First Seed 5<sup>th</sup>

In [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), this is the Summoning Day for [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### First Planting
- First Seed 7<sup>th</sup>

On the 7<sup>th</sup> of First Seed every year, the people of Tamriel celebrate First Planting, symbolically sowing the seeds for the autumn *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* harvest. It is a festival of fresh beginnings, both for the crops and the people. Neighbors are reconciled in their disputes, resolutions are formed, bad habits dropped, the diseased cured. The clerics at the temples run free clinic all day to cure people of the various ailments found in Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CS-4)</sup><sup>:147</sup>

### Day of Waiting
- First Seed 9<sup>th</sup>

The Day of Waiting is a very old holy day among certain settlements in the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains). Every year at that time, a dragon is supposed to come out of the desert and devour the wicked, so everyone locks themselves up inside.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Hogithum
- First Seed 21<sup>st</sup>

In [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), this is the Summoning Day for [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Flower Day
- First Seed 25<sup>th</sup>

**Flower Day** is a holiday held on the 25th of [First Seed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Seed) and is a joyous celebration of the springtide after the past winter.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup> It is arguably one of the oldest holidays in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), along with [Waking Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waking_Day) from the [Yeorth Burrowland](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yeorth_Burrowland). It is observed all over High Rock, originally from the smaller villages.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HOTIB-6)</sup>

Children go out and pick the new, blooming flowers while the older folk come out to dance and sing.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup> The celebration in [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) is especially vibrant, colorful, and exciting; compared to the other cities of High Rock.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TIHR-10)</sup> Similarily, Camlorn's people fashion tophats with flowers and shamrocks for the arrival of spring.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONHat Lucky-11)</sup>

### [Festival of Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Festival_of_Blades)
- First Seed 26<sup>th</sup>

The [Festival of Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Festival_of_Blades) is a holiday held on the 26th of [First Seed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Seed). It is created in honor of the first [Redguard's](Lore_Redguard.md) victory over [Malooc](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malooc)'s Horde, an army of giant [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) that lived in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Although modern scholars believe that the story is a myth, the holiday is still popular across the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) of the southern [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay).

### [Jester's Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jester%27s_Festival)
- Rain's Hand 28<sup>th</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/ON-event-Jester%27s_Festival_2017.jpg/200px-ON-event-Jester%27s_Festival_2017.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-event-Jester%27s_Festival_2017.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-event-Jester%27s_Festival_2017.jpg) Jester's Festival participant stealing a pig "princess"The [Jester's Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jester%27s_Festival), also known as **Jester's Day**, and the **Festival of Fools**, is an annual holiday that falls on the 28th of [Rain's Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rain%27s_Hand). During the event, troupes of jesters and fools encourage the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) of all walks of life to celebrate the foolish and absurd. Performers roam the streets and openly mock the powerful, towns celebrate with festive pranks, and silly games are held with joke prizes rewarded as an incentive. During this time, the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) takes advantage of the insanity and strike people's pockets.

The origins of the Jester's Festival are speculated, with some stating that it originated with the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) once turning the whole world mad. Indeed, there appears to have some association with Sheogorath, as his fellow Prince [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine) once manifested under the alias of a Breton named "Samuel Gourone" during the celebration to spice it up in honor of his daedric sibling. Others believe that it sprung from [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) emulating a caricature of [Men](Lore_Men.md). Other theories tie it closely to the invention of alcoholic beverages, or [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) teaching Mer humor.

## Second Seed

### Second Planting
- Second Seed 7<sup>th</sup>

The celebration of Second Planting is in full glory this day. It is a holiday with traditions similar to First Planting, improvements on the first seeding symbolically to suggest improvements on the [soul](Lore_Souls.md). The free clinics of the temples are open for the second and last time this year, offering cures for those suffering from any kind of disease or affliction. Because peace and not conflict is stressed at this time, battle injuries are healed only at full price.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CS-4)</sup><sup>:148</sup> It is considered a time for realizing one's limitations.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

### Marukh's Day
- Second Seed 9<sup>th</sup>

Marukh's Day is only observed by certain communities in Skeffington Wood. By comparing themselves to the virtuous prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md), the people of Skeffington Wood pray for the strength to resist temptation. In [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), this is the Summoning Day for [Namira](Lore_Namira.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### [Koomu Alezer'i](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Koomu_Alezer%27i)
- Second Seed 17th<sup>th</sup>

The [Koomu Alezer'i Yaghoub](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Koomu_Alezer%27i) ([Yoku](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yoku) for *"We Acknowledge Yaghoub"*), also simply called **Koomu Alezer'i** is a holiday held on the 17th of [Second Seed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Second_Seed) and observed by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). It is a day made in remembrance of the [city](Lore_Sentinel.md)'s founder, [Yaghoub](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yaghoub) the Seafarer, who discovered the city's harborage on that day in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by following [the Steed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Steed) constellation and named it after the watchman who first saw it.

### Fire Festival
- Second Seed 20<sup>th</sup>

The Fire Festival in [Northmoor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northmoor) is one of the most attended celebrations in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It began as a pompous display of magic and military strength in ancient days and has become quite a festival.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Fishing Day
- Second Seed 30<sup>th</sup>

Fishing Day is a big celebration for the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) who live off the bounty of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay). They are not usually a flamboyant people, but on Fishing Day, they make so much noise, fish have been scared away for weeks.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup> The holiday was also adopted in Skyrim and in other parts of Tamriel where fishing is both a livelihood and a way of life.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TESTOC-13)</sup><sup>:16</sup>

## Mid Year

### Pelinal's Midyear Massacre
- Unknown Date

Pelinal's Midyear Massacre is an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) celebration that dates back to the fall of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md). Celebrants remember [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md)'s slaughter of the Ayleid forces at Heldon Bridge, a key battle to capturing the Imperial City. During the celebrations, warriors are anointed with a red blood-like substance in honor and mimicry of Pelinal, whose face and hair was said to be covered in the blood of "righteously slaughtered Ayleids". This ritual is said to invigorate warriors and imbued them with righteous power or even grant visions of St. Alessia herself. The celebrations are presided over by the priests of St. Alessia. They are charged with delivering sermons and offering blessings to combatants, who are said to become renowned for their brave deeds in days thereafter.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONMaera-14)</sup>

### Drigh R'Zimb
- Mid Year 1<sup>st</sup>

The festival of Drigh R'Zimb, held in the hottest time of year in [Abibon-Gora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abibon-Gora), is a jubilation held for the sun Daibethe itself. Scholars do not know how long Drigh R'Zimb has been held, but it is possible the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) brought the festival with them when they came in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Hircine's Summoning Day
- Mid Year 5<sup>th</sup>

In [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), this is the Summoning Day for [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Mid Year Celebration
- Mid Year 16<sup>th</sup>

Today is the 16<sup>th</sup> of Mid Year, the traditional day for the Mid Year Celebration. Perhaps to alleviate the annual news of the Emperor's latest tax increase, the cities' temples offer blessings for only half the donation they usually suggest. Many so blessed feel confident enough to enter the (dangerous) dungeons when they are not fully prepared, so this joyous festival has often been known to turn suddenly into a day of defeat and tragedy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Dancing Day
- Mid Year 23<sup>rd</sup>

Dancing Day is a time-honored holiday in [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall). Who started it is questionable, but the Red Prince Atryck popularized it in the second era. It is an occasion of great pomp and merriment for all the people of Daggerfall, from the nobles down.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Tibedetha
- Mid Year 24<sup>th</sup>

Tibedetha is [middle Tamrielic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Middle_Tamrielic) for "Tibers Day." It is not surprising that the lorddom of [Alcaire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alcaire) celebrates its most famous native with a great party. Historically, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) never returned once to his beloved birthplace. It is also his birthday.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

## Sun's Height

### Merchants' Festival
- Sun's Height 10<sup>th</sup>

The bargain shoppers of the known world are out in force today and it is little wonder, for the 10<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Height is a holiday called the Merchants' Festival. Every marketplace and equipment store has dropped their prices to at least half. The only shop not being patronized today is the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), where prices are as exorbitant as usual. Most citizens in need of a magical item are waiting two months for the celebration of Tales and Tallows when prices will be more reasonable... In [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), this is the Summoning day for [Vaernima](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaernima).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

This is the busiest shopping day of the year, bar none, with some recommending spending every coin you've saved.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

### Divad Etep't
- Sun's Height 12<sup>th</sup>

During Divat Etep't, the people of [Antiphyllos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Antiphyllos) mourn the death of one of the greatest of the early [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) heroes, [Divat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divad_Hunding), son of [Frandar of the Hel Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frandar_Hunding). His deeds are questioned by historians, but his tomb in Antiphyllos is almost certainly genuine.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### Sun's Rest
- Sun's Height 20<sup>th</sup>

You will have to wait until tomorrow if you are planning on making any equipment purchases, for all stores are closed in observance of Sun's Rest. The temples, taverns, and Mages Guild in the celebrating are still open their regular hours, but most citizens choose to devote this day to relaxation, not commerce or prayer. This is not always convenient, but the [Merchants' Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Merchants_Guild) heavily fines any shop that stays open, so everyone complies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

Thoses who really need to buy things take a trip "out to the country" if they want to buy something.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

### Fiery Night
- Sun's Height 29<sup>th</sup>

Few besides the natives of the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) would venture out on the hottest day of the year, Fiery Night. It's a lively celebration with a meaning lost in antiquity.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-1)</sup>

### [Witches Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witches_Festival)

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg) Wearing costumes and giving or receiving treats is a norm during the Witches Festival The [Witches Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witches_Festival) (sometimes spelled as **Witch's Festival** or **Witch's Fest**) is an annual holiday that falls on the 13th of [Frostfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostfall), coinciding with [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md)'s Summoning Day. Festival foods are ghastly-themed, with names like Frosted Brains, Bowls of "Peeled Eyeballs," and Bewitched Sugar Skulls. Cities across Tamriel embrace the festival, adorned in styles reminiscent of [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) wickerwork.

### [Saturalia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saturalia)
- Evening Star 25<sup>th</sup>

[Saturalia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saturalia) is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) celebration that heralds the [New Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival), and is held on the 25th of Evening Star. Originally a holiday for a "long forgotten god of debauchery", it has become a time of gift giving, parties, and parading. Visitors are encouraged to participate. [Reindeer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reindeer) are traditionally associated with the holiday.

A tale covers a miraculous rescue by [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine). During a winter storm, a lost and abandoned mercenary prayed for rescue, and Sanguine answered it by [creating](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem) a frozen steed for her, which allowed her to take vengeance against the lord that betrayed her on Saturalia morning. Saturalia Bitter Mulled Wine is an alcoholic drink enjoyed during this day.

Saturalia shares some elements with the cross-continental New Life Festival, with it managing to become connected (or at some points synonymous with) the festival. This is thanks to the efforts of merchants from the trade hub that is [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) capitalizing on the holiday through consumer goods. Widespread adoption of Saturalia-themed trees, decorations, and more across Tamriel for usage in the New Life Festival make for a booming market during this time of year, touching even Tamrielans that grew up with their own New Life traditions. This convergence of these Breton-influenced aesthetics and practices were started during the time of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and has carried onto the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

### [Old Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Life_Festival)
- Evening Star 30<sup>th</sup>/31<sup>st</sup>

The [Old Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Life_Festival), sometimes simply called **Old Life**, is primarily an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) holiday but it is also celebrated throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by other cultures in their own unique ways due to the reach and influence of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). It falls at the last day of the year, and is a time people write messages of remembrance for their dead loved ones, and may occasionally receive an answer from Aetherius. Old Life pilgrimage shrines can be found throughout Tamriel, from the west in [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md)'s [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), all the way to the east in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)'s [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls). People who are unable to travel to the pilgrimage sites can have a proxy deliver their messages, and if they receive a response, they will know.

During the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) in the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), the holiday was celebrated by going to the temple to reflect on their past. Some would go for more than this, for it is rumored that priests will as the last act of the year perform resurrections on beloved friends and family members free of the usual charge. Worshippers know better than to expect this philanthropy, but they arrive in a macabre procession with the recently deceased nevertheless.

The Old Life Festival complements the [New Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival), and both are considered two sides of the same coin. The Old Life Festival being a day of rest before the New Life Festival, which is one big party. Summarized neatly as "the day we ask forgiveness for old sins, before committing new ones", even unpopular temples in are packed with people. By acknowledging those that have passed, one can begin to move forward, as the pilgrimage shrines serve as a vessel for letting the departed know the things people held to themselves while they were alive. Those who receive a response from a spirit may feel it is a life-changing experience.

### Sovereignty Day
Sovereignty Day is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) holiday that celebrates [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md)'s independence from the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Pa P-42)</sup> The day of the holiday is the day when Baroness Falinne Guimard lead a defense of [Bangkorai Pass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Pass) against the [Alessian Legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) in [1E 2305](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2305).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-43)</sup>

### Wintertide Festival
The Wintertide Festival is a yearly [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) holiday occurring during snowfall, where wealthy households save snow in sealed jars.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup>
