[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/ON-load-Angof%27s_Sanctum.jpg/200px-ON-load-Angof%27s_Sanctum.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Angof%27s_Sanctum.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Angof%27s_Sanctum.jpg) Angof in his sanctum Angof **the Gravesinger**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Angof1-1)</sup> also known as **Angof the Undying**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Angof2-2)</sup> **Angof of the Reach**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TO-3)</sup> and **the Reachmage**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SOTR-4)</sup> was a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachman.md) leader of the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md), a [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) faction devoted to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). He was a powerful [gravesinger](Lore_Reachfolk_Shamans.md) and [spellcaster](Lore_Magic.md) and was responsible for the cult's attacks in [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SOTR-4)</sup>

## History

### The Reach
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-place-The_Reach.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Reach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Reach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Reach.jpg) The Reach, Angof's homeland My father told me tales of Angof. He was a terror, even back then, and now he's here in Glenumbra. Every Breton bully has joined his Bloodthorn cult, but I get the blame. *
—Giraud Scraeg, a half Reachman loyalist of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GS-5)</sup>* Angof's exact origins are unknown, though he is known to have hailed from the Reach. The precise date of his birth is also unclear. In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541) or [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TOAM3-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRHKE-7)</sup> by the time of [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md)'s attack on [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), Angof was already feared among the population of the Reach and known for his terror. Even by around [2E 552](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_552), tales of his dark deeds continued to be known and told by the Reachfolk. Angof was viewed with disdain by many of his kinsmen,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GS-5)</sup> including the powerful [seer](Lore_Sight.md) [Sibellec the Old](Lore_Sibellec.md), who claimed he was a harbinger of evil.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-STO-8)</sup> As a Gravesinger, Angof wielded tremendous power to command the loathsome dead.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-9)</sup> This title was bestowed upon him because he made a pact with Molag Bal.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ZGK-10)</sup>

Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the Reachfolk leader of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), instructed High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), through High King's Royal Envoy to the Reach, Lady [Nilene Devierin](Lore_Nilene_Devierin.md), to strike against invading factions like Angof's cult, aiming to weaken them and enhance Caddach's authority in the region, showing no interest in providing any support to Angof. He emphasized to Emeric that unifying the Reach under one king, rather than allowing a multitude of stubborn clans and factions, would also serve the best interests of High Rock.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CBLB-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-12)</sup>

### High Rock
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/ON-concept-Cath_Bedraud.png/200px-ON-concept-Cath_Bedraud.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Cath_Bedraud.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Cath_Bedraud.png) Cath Bedraud [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-npc-Vision_of_Angof.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Vision_of_Angof.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vision_of_Angof.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vision_of_Angof.jpg) Vision of Angof *Angof seeks to cause as much chaos and mayhem as possible. Beyond that, I'm not sure of his ultimate motives. Has he come to Glenumbra to conquer us or destroy us? And in the end, does that distinction even matter? All I know is, we have to find a way to stop him. We have to!*
— *[Schemes of the Reachmage](Lore_Schemes_of_the_Reachmage.md)* by [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SOTR-4)</sup> Angof's primary objective was to conquer the [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) region in High Rock.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SOG-13)</sup> He established the cult's headquarters at the ancient cemetery of [Cath Bedraud](Lore_Cath_Bedraud.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LJLW-14)</sup> The precise timing of this is unclear, though some evidence of his presence and corrupted [Bloodthorn Vines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodthorn_Vines) were seen as early as [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-EGT-15)</sup> He has defiled the most sacred vaults of Cath Bedraud with writhing vines and dark necromancy, usurping the crypts of the ancient Breton kings for his plans.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CB-16)</sup> However, the cult's invasion didn't begin until the onset of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). The cultists initially seized control of numerous burial sites in the [King's Guard](Lore_King%27s_Guard.md) region, raising the dead to serve as minions. Angof himself traveled to the ancient battlefield at [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), resurrecting the long-dead [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) general [Faolchu](Lore_Faolchu.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lo To C-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-VOAF-18)</sup> Faolchu, a powerful werewolf in life, was forced to serve Angof by spreading lycanthropy throughout Glenumbra, eventually overrunning the city of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) and infecting the Breton nobility.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Rally Cry-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-APFV-20)</sup> Acting on his behalf, [Vardan](Lore_Vardan.md) expanded the cult's activities to the isle of [Betnikh](Lore_Betnikh.md). While Angof offered him support, the initiates he recruited were largely inexperienced. Ultimately, Vardan's schemes failed.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-VD-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Vardan-22)</sup> Another of his subordinates, Grivier Bloodcaller, infiltrated [Balefire Island](Lore_Balefire_Island.md), which had once served as a burial ground, with the aim of supplying the cult's forces, hoping to please Angof.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-WORM-23)</sup>

At an unknown point of time Angof supposedly taught a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) sorceress secrets of life after death.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Card-24)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_%28person%29.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_%28person%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_(person).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_(person).jpg) Angof the Gravesinger in Cath Bedraud Angof plotted the assassination of King Cassimir of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-VN-25)</sup> but the attempt failed when his assassin, Verrik, was stopped before he could complete the mission. Only after this incident did the king turn his attention to Angof’s activities and initiate a proper investigation.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-KCV-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-BA-27)</sup> Although Angof was a Reachman, many Bretons joined his cult.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GS-5)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-APFV-20)</sup> Some believed he seemed like the strong leader that Glenumbra desperately needed, finding him to be very persuasive.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-HV-28)</sup> Despite the probable danger, some citizens of Glenumbra began collecting clippings of Angof's vines to display in their homes.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-AAV-29)</sup>

Anogof's body was pale and covered in thorns,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Angof1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Angof2-2)</sup> leading some to speculate whether he was already undead at that point. At that time, he was already described as old.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-GB-30)</sup> Later in his life, he described this state as monstrous and expressed a hope to return to his mortal form.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Angof2-2)</sup>

Angof's cultists formed an alliance with the Red Rook Bandits,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-RRJ-31)</sup> and launched an effort to corrupt the [Wyrd Tree](Lore_Wyrd_Tree.md). To achieve this, they created [lurchers](Lore_Lurcher.md),<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-WI-32)</sup> and captured the Guardians of the [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md), ancient beings revered by the local [witches](Lore_Witches.md). Angof's dark magic was employed to corrupt both the guardians and the land, ultimately leading to their imprisonment.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-WI-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Guardians-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Corruptions-34)</sup> He also struck a deal with the Hag Fen coven of Mother Murk to distract the Beldama Wyrd further, keeping them occupied.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-BSC-35)</sup> However, ultimately, they were defeated thanks to the efforts of an adventurer and Sibellec, a Reachwoman seer who stood against Angof.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-STO-8)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-WAC-36)</sup> The Guardians were later saved when a representative from the Covenant intervened, ending the corruption and freeing the spirits.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Guardians-33)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Glenumbra-37)</sup> The activities of his cult and allies were addressed in various locations across Glenumbra, resulting in the liberation of Camlorn.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-CM-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TFOF-39)</sup> Despite that, Angof continued to pose a threat,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-GG-40)</sup> which ultimately led to the arrival of Daggerfall Covenant forces in the northern parts of Glenumbra,<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-TTFTTE-41)</sup> in the region of the King's Guard, where members of the [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md),<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-CMA-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-BC-43)</sup> and [Knights of the Dragon](Lore_Knights_of_the_Dragon.md),<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Dragon-44)</sup> established a redoubt.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TLGS-45)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-MTT-46)</sup> Shortly after, they confronted him at his headquarters in Cath Bedraud.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-MOC-47)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TL-48)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ATGS-49)</sup> The Breton forces were numerous, and the renowned [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md) was among them.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-DG-50)</sup> Additionally, they received support from a contingent of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) led by Gabrielle Benele,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ATGS-49)</sup> who procured the Midnight Talisman, which was instrumental in Angof's defeat.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-AD-51)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-GBJ-52)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-GB-30)</sup> The ancient graveyard was filled with traps,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-SPG-53)</sup> and his inner sanctum was secured with a triple lock,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-DG-50)</sup> which was unsealed using shard-keys obtained with the help of one of the spirits Angof had resurrected against their will.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-STK-54)</sup> Ultimately, the Breton forces defeated Angof and killed him.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ATGS-49)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-CM-38)</sup>

### Coldharbour
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_04.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_04.jpg) Angof in the Coldharbour [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Undying_02.jpg) Angof tending to Breton mage's wounds *I made mistakes. I became a … monster. I don't want Molag Bal's favor, but if I can win this cursed game I might be able to regain my mortal existence. Before I became a monster. Or at least I can hope to earn a true and final death.*
—Angof<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Angof2-2)</sup> In death, Angof and some members of the Bloodthorn Cult were taken to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), with their [souls](Lore_Souls.md) claimed by Molag Bal due to their pact. As punishment for their failure, Bal condemned them to the Cliffs of Failure to engage in endless battles. Angof, seeking redemption and a cure for his disfigured form, became repentant. He questioned the choices he had made, and regret engulfed him, driving him to desperate actions. Under the supervision of a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) known as the Observer, the cultists were forced to combat the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) led by Thallik Wormfather and the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md) led by High Kinlady [Estre](Lore_Estre.md). Later that year, a [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) invasion force entered Coldharbour to stop the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), and several [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) members became trapped on the Cliffs of Failure.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TO-3)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-TEW-55)</sup>

The Vestige joined the games to rescue them, with the Overseer offering the option to save Angof, Thallik, or Estre. Angof promised to assist in a final assault on the Endless Stair, though it remains unknown who was ultimately saved.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-TEW-55)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ATIYS-56)</sup> Juline Ginis, a member of the Mages Guild who sided with Angof at the Cliffs of Failure, was initially cautious and deemed him arrogant. However, her attitude changed after he showed compassion and offered her his help. She claimed that he had changed and remembered what it was like to be human, deeming him a mage of great caliber and power.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-JG-57)</sup> Another of Angof's subordinates, Paldrin the Wise, also worked with Juline at the Cliffs of Failure.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-PTW-58)</sup> If Angof managed to redeem himself, he took residence in the Hollow City and played a crucial role in the final battle against Molag Bal's forces. Using his nature magic and necromancy, Angof fought against his former master's minions, aiding the Tamrielic forces in their efforts to thwart the Planemeld and bring an end to Molag Bal's schemes.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Angof2-2)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TFA-59)</sup>
