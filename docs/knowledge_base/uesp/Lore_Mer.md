# Mer

[![OB-misc-Topal Statue.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/OB-misc-Topal Statue.png/250px-OB-misc-Topal Statue.png)](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) **Mer** ([/m ɛ r/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English), meaning *Folk*),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Mer)</sup> or **Elves**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> are the "Elder Races" of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), such as: the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), and [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) —all of which are descendants of the primordial [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) race.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> The [Orsimer](Lore_Orc.md) are also commonly believed to share this ancestry.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTNOO-3)</sup> The term Mer is also commonly used for non-elven peoples, like [Betmer](Lore_Betmer.md) which refers to Beastfolk, and Nemer which seemingly refers to anyone who is not an elf.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWNTH-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWDialogue Betmeri-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Tendil-6)</sup> The terms "elven" and "merish" are adjectives meaning "of or relating to the Mer".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Elves are said to consider themselves the only "truly human race", as they are descended directly from the gods. Some elves regard Mannish and Beast races as nothing more than intelligent animals.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Ger H-10)</sup>

The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), often called Manmer, are the result of interbreeding between Aldmer and [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md);<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1High-11)</sup> they are most commonly considered men, and not mer.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3All-12)</sup> A couple races of mer have disappeared from the world or fallen from prominence, including the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TAA-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE1S-14)</sup> The Maormer either have a different origin entirely or diverged from their brethren before the Aldmer inhabited [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-15)</sup>

What sets the mer apart from the other races of Tamriel is their [long lifespans](Lore_Lifespan.md); aside from the Orsimer, Aldmeri lifespans typically range from several centuries to as much as a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors like disease or violence.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRB2-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-IWADE-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Elven longevity has been described as a kind of “godhood” that was “granted by the gods”.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-KEXII-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRB2-16)</sup> Lifespans in the thousands are typically seen among powerful mages or nobles. It is said Elven nobles live “a lot longer” than common folk due to “selective breeding” and a “luxurious lifestyle” (among other reasons).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPP-20)</sup> Some of the most ancient and powerful Elven mages have extended their lives far beyond the natural span, in some cases enduring for millennia through the use of potent magical practices.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FOA-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PMB-22)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

According to one source the average elven woman can conceive at least twice, but no more than four times—and typically only around the time she turns sixteen.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TRB3-24)</sup> However, the same sources describe a [particular Dark Elven woman](Lore_Barenziah.md) giving birth up to four times within a two decade period when she was in her 300s.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TRB3-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TDD-26)</sup> There are also several known Dunmer families with over six children.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONPKI-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONME-28)</sup> Regardless, the rule does not apply to Bosmer, who are known for their numerous offspring.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONEVE-29)</sup>

### Other Ethnonyms
Before the "-mer" suffix rose to prominence, the archaic "-che" suffix was used in its place to refer to the Elder Races (around the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md))<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TSOP-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TA-31)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Nantharion-32)</sup>, in terms such as: the [Boiche](Lore_Bosmer.md),<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TWEDF-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-AU4TET-34)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ACf R-35)</sup> [Moriche](Lore_Dunmer.md)<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TWEDF-33)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Po M-36)</sup> (or [Maliache](Lore_Chimer.md)),<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-RPTrial Of Vehk-37)</sup> Noiché (or Noiché'el),<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Po M-36)</sup> and [Salache](Lore_Altmer.md)<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TWEDF-33)</sup> (or [Saliache](Lore_Ayleids.md)).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TSOP-30)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TOot O-38)</sup> The ruling government of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) was referred to as the [Imperatum Saliache](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md)<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TA-31)</sup> —and though the term "Ayleid" seems to be the accepted modern name for the people used in common parlance,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TWEDF-33)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONLaloriaran-39)</sup> the term "Saliache" is considered the older endonymic name.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Nantharion-32)</sup>

The "-iil" particle is an ancient elven suffix describing the "folk" of a region,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Cyrus Comic-40)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Land War Poetry-41)</sup> seen in terms like [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), [Delodiil](Lore_Delodiil.md), [Resdayniil](Lore_Resdayn.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Cyrus Comic-40)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TWOD-42)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Land War Poetry-41)</sup> In the case of Cyrodiil, that term stood for the capital city, land, the people, and the figurehead of that region—which is derived from an ancient the-king-is-the-land tradition dating back to when the Ayleids ruled there.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Cyrus Comic-40)</sup>

Additionally the older version of [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) seems to be [Alcharyai](Lore_Alcharyai.md),<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Po M-36)</sup> and the name of the [Left-Handed Elves](Lore_Left-Handed_Elves.md) of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) is [Kanuryai](Lore_Left-Handed_Elves.md),<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CFEM-43)</sup> indicating that "-yai" is an archaic or older suffix used by Aldmeri cultures.

### Elvendom
"Elvendom" is a term that refers broadly to the Elven world or collective body—the selves and souls—of all the Elven [races](Lore_Races.md), and their continued existence in the world. A band of great heroes from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), [Moraelyn's Companions](Lore_Moraelyn%27s_Companions.md), hid the legendary [Horn of Summoning](Lore_Horn_of_Summoning.md) artifact away in a secret location, so that it may be used "at [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)'s last need" and if "all of Elvendom" faced dire peril.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TRB4-44)</sup>

Amongst some Elves, there was an belief that all the Elven races must one day unify. During the rise of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), [¥R](Lore_%C2%A5R.md) —a minor member of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) —expressed this belief, claiming that "the Mer must unite *at last* or be consumed one by one".<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-PGE1PR-45)</sup>

## Aldmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg/200px-ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg) [High Lord Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md) leads his Aldmer ships to [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fc/OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg/200px-OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg) Statue of [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) The [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) (meaning "First Folk" or "Elder Folk"), also known as **Alcharyai**, were the original proto- mer of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), who first settled the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) before gradually spreading across much of mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). According to legend, the Aldmer originated from a mythical lost continent known as [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md); however, some sources, including allegedly the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) themselves, suggest this place is allegorical.

Their skin was said to possess a lustrous bronze hue, and modern [Altmer (High Elves)](Lore_Altmer.md) are widely regarded as the closest living representation of the original Aldmer, both in physical appearance and in cultural tradition. [Altmer society](Lore_Altmer.md#Society_and_Religion) highly reveres Aldmeri ancestry, often striving to emulate their customs and ideals. Some sources even suggest that they engage in selective breeding to preserve Aldmer-like features. Certain High Elf songs are still composed in [Old Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md), the ancient language of their forebears. The Aldmer are commonly regarded as the first technologically advanced civilization to emerge on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), though some argue that this distinction belongs to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). Beastfolk ancestors such as the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) also inhabited Tamriel during this era, but their societies are generally described by historians as aboriginal and preliterate. Whatever the case, these populations were quickly displaced by the seemingly more sophisticated Aldmer.

Few individual Aldmer are remembered by name, but a handful of notable figures endure in both legend and recorded history. Among them are [High Lord Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md), the first to settle the Summerset Isles; [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md), who charted much of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md); and [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md), a former Aldmer who became the undying sorcerer-king of the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). The infamous necromancer [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), known as the King of Worms, once claimed he was not an Altmer, but an Aldmer.

The Aldmer are said to have possessed a refined and chivalric high culture, and are known to have developed their own system of musical notation—so intricate and esoteric that it remains difficult to interpret even in modern times. As the Aldmer spread across Tamriel, they gradually diverged, culturally and physiologically, into the various elven races known today. Some sources attribute the splintering of the Aldmer to their realization that different [Towers](Lore_Towers.md) with their own Stones could tell different "stories", to alter reality and shape their own domains. Thus the elves are said to have "self-refracted, each to their own creation", with every splinter group following their own Tower, the Bosmer following [Towers](Lore_Green-Sap.md), the Chimer following [Red-Heart](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), the Altmer [Crystal-Like-Law](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), et alia. Those who remained in the Summerset Isles became the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md). The earliest migrants to the mainland settled in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), giving rise to the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md). [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) was colonized by both the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and, later, the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), precursors of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). A group claimed by some sources to have split off in Summerset and by others in Aldmeris itself, became the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). High Rock was also home to the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), a race of mixed heritage born from interbreeding between native [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) and Aldmeri settlers, such as the [Clan Direnni](Lore_Clan_Direnni.md). A number of myths also claim that the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) originated from followers of the elven ancestor spirit [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), who were transformed alongside their deity due to the intervention of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), while trying to halt the Velothi dissident movement.

The term *Aldmeri* is sometimes used more broadly to refer to all elves, and it is also used in reference to members of the modern [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Aldmer.md).

## Altmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/8/81/LO-race-Altmer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Altmer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Altmer.png) Illustration of a typical Altmer male The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), also known as **High Elves** by the denizens of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), by the archaic term **Salache**, or as the self-titled "**Cultured People**", are a tall, golden-skinned elven race hailing from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). They regard themselves as the direct descendants of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) and as the purest, most unaltered heirs of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), the ancient elven race who first arrived on Tamriel from the mythical lost continent of [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md).

The Altmer are renowned as one of the most intelligent races, and their exceptional innate aptitude for magic makes them the most gifted in the [arcane arts](Lore_Magic.md). This magical affinity is further enhanced by their naturally long [lifespans](Lore_Lifespan.md), which range from several centuries to as much as a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors. Some of the most ancient and powerful Altmer mages have extended their lives far beyond this natural span, in some cases enduring for millennia through the application of highly advanced magical disciplines. In addition to their magical prowess, Altmer possess natural resistances to [disease](Lore_Disease.md) and [paralysis](Lore_Paralysis.md), though they are noted to be somewhat vulnerable to elemental forces.

[Altmeri society](Lore_Altmer.md#Social_Structure) has been shaped by millennia of scholarly refinement and is deeply hierarchical and tradition-bound. The Altmer have long pursued an ideal of cultural perfection, venerating their lineage and striving to preserve the traditions, knowledge, and practices of their ancient ancestors. This ideal continues to shape all aspects of Altmeri life, from education and governance to art and daily conduct. The [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md), a prestigious council of scholars and sages, are entrusted with preserving knowledge, overseeing education, and maintaining cultural continuity.

High Elves are often perceived by other races as proud, aloof, or even arrogant. These perceptions are reinforced by centuries of cultural insularity and the perceived superiority of their customs, ancestry, and intellectual traditions. The Altmer, for their part, consider themselves Tamriel's most civilized people: the continent's common tongue is derived from [Altmeri](Lore_High_Elvish.md) speech and writing, and much of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md)'s art, law, architecture, and scholarship can trace its roots back to Altmeri tradition.

## Ayleid
The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), also known as the **Heartland High Elves**, **Wild Elves**, or **Saliache**, were the first race to establish an empire in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and had ruled modern-day [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for countless years dating back to before recorded history. They were thin and lean with pointed ears and angular facial features. Their complexion was lighter than that of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), but they were darker than [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md); their skin tone being a light tan to a dark bronze in color and their eyes ranging from white to turquoise.

[Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md), their language, is similar to [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), the ancient language of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md). The Ayleids were the original founders of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). They constructed the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), which they called the "Temple of the Ancestors", in emulation of the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md). The [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) collapsed in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) when it was overthrown by the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). While it's believed that the Ayleids remain in the wilds of every province on Tamriel, sightings have been few. There have been no known sightings in the [Third](Lore_Third_Era.md) or [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

## Bosmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/c/c8/LO-race-Bosmer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Bosmer.png) Illustration of a typical Bosmer male The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) (formerly the **Boiche**) are the [Elven](Lore_Elf.md) clan-folk of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), a forested province in southwestern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). In the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), they are often referred to as **Wood Elves**, but they typically call themselves Bosmer and the **Tree-Sap people**. Bosmer rejected the stiff, formal traditions of [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) high culture, preferring a more romantic, simple existence in harmony with the land. They are relatively nimble and quick in body compared to their more "civilized" [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) cousins (who often look down upon the Bosmer as unruly and naive). Their agility makes them well-suited as scouts and thieves. However, they are also a quick-witted folk, and many pursue successful careers in scholarly pursuits or trading.

Bosmer possess a natural lifespan ranging from several centuries up to a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors. Though they are considered less influential than some of their Elven brethren, the Bosmer are also relatively prolific, with 12 children in a family considered average. As a result, they outnumber all other mer on Tamriel.

The best archers in all of Tamriel, the Bosmer snatch and release arrows in one continuous motion; they are even rumored to have invented the bow. They have many natural and unique abilities; notably, they can command simple-minded creatures and have a nearly chameleon-like ability to hide in forested areas, as well as natural resistance to poison and disease. **Nature's Grace** grants Bosmer greater affinity with [healing magics](Lore_Restoration.md) and the capability to recover from harm quickly. Many in the forests of Valenwood follow the tenets of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). These "Green Pact Bosmer" are religiously carnivorous and cannibalistic, and do not harm the vegetation of Valenwood, though they are not averse to using wooden or plant-derived products created by others.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Bosmer.md).

## Chimer
The [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) (literally, the **People of the North**), also called the **Changed Folk**, **Velothi**, **Changed Ones**, or the archaic **Maliache** were the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) tribes of dissidents who abandoned the ways of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) and embarked on a great exodus to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They followed the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md), who spoke to them on behalf of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), Prince of Plots, and two other "[Good Daedra](Lore_Good_Daedra.md)". The teachings of Veloth and the Daedra spawned a new culture on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and a new people, sparking the period of the High Velothi culture. They were dynamic, ambitious, long-lived Elven clans devoted to fundamentalist ancestor worship. They closely resembled the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), though their skin bore a paler golden hue. [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) apparently maintained her appearance as a Chimer (as did [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), partially) despite the Curse which changed the Chimer into the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Chimer.md).

## Dunmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/7/77/LO-race-Dunmer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Dunmer.png) Illustration of a typical Dunmer male [![ON-misc-Dunmeri Tapestry.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-misc-Dunmeri Tapestry.png/175px-ON-misc-Dunmeri Tapestry.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Dunmeri Tapestry.png) The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), also known as **Dark Elves**, **Ash-Elves** by the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), "**Pureblooded Folk**" by themselves, or the archaic **Moriche**, are the grey-skinned, typically red-eyed elven peoples of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), "Dark Elf" is the common usage, but they and their [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) brethren prefer the term "Dunmer". "Dark" is variously interpreted as meaning "dark-skinned", "gloomy", and "ill-favored by fate", with the Dunmer and their national identity embracing these various connotations with enthusiasm.

Their combination of powerful intellects with strong and agile physiques often produce great warriors and sorcerers. On the battlefield, Dunmer are noted for their skill with a balanced integration of the sword, the bow and destruction magic. Dunmer possess a natural lifespan ranging from several centuries up to a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors. Some of the most ancient and powerful Dunmer mages have extended their lives far beyond this natural span, in some cases enduring for several thousands of years through the use of potent magical practices. In character, they are generally grim, aloof, and reserved.

Dunmer distrust and are treated distrustfully by other races. They are often called proud, clannish, ruthless, and cruel, from an outsider's point of view, but greatly value loyalty and family. They also apparently have a reputation for quick tempers. Despite their powerful skills and strengths, the Dunmer's vengeful nature, age-old conflicts, betrayals, and ill-reputation prevent them from gaining more influence. Those born in their homeland of Morrowind are reputed to be considerably less friendly than those who grew up in the Imperial tradition.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Dunmer.md).

## Dwemer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/MW-concept-Dwemer.jpg/200px-MW-concept-Dwemer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Dwemer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Dwemer.jpg) two male Dwemer The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), (literally '**the Deep**, or **Deep-Counseled** or **Secretive People**, **people of the deep**), are a fabled "Lost Race" of Mer from Dwemereth, which mostly consisted of modern-day [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), where they are believed to have been the most prolific, though they also had a strong presence in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md).

The races of Mer use the term "Dwemer", which translates to **Deep-Elves** or **Deep Folk** and connoting variously with **deep-delving,** **profound,** and **close-counseled**; Imperial usage is **Dwarven,** derived in fable from the affectionate regard of the Giant races for their 'little' Dwemer friends. These Giants have been speculated to be [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) or the more common [Giants](Lore_Giant.md). They were also called the **Children of Brass** by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md).

The early history of the Dwemer is still clouded in mystery. There is no known story of their dissociation from the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), which must have occurred very early in [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history, as their society bore few correlations with that of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) besides some similar legal principles. According to some legends, the Dwemer originated from the same group as the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), and were treated as another tribe, and later, as a secular [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md). Other sources however claim they already occupied the northeast of Tamriel when [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) and his people arrived there.

The Dwemer built elaborate underground cities near and beneath mountain ranges, including the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) and [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), and in the mountains of the isle [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md). Many misconceptions about them have abounded for centuries: scholars long thought that most [Dwemer ruins](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) which dotted Tamriel outside Morrowind were mere outposts and that there were few significant Dwemer settlements elsewhere until [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420). The presentation of the Dwemer in fictitious but popular novels like the *[Ancient Tales of the Dwemer](Lore_Ancient_Tales_of_the_Dwemer.md)* has also lodged an inaccurate impression of them in the popular consciousness, painting them as familiar, comfortable characters who were very similar to humans. In reality, they are better described as having been fearsome, unfathomable, and even cruel, though also careful, intelligent, and industrious. Their society consisted of free-thinking yet reclusive clans devoted to the secrets of science, engineering, and the arcane until they mysteriously disappeared around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Dwemer.md).

## Lefthanded Elves
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/ON-texture-Warrior_Wave_Illustrated_Tome_01.png/200px-ON-texture-Warrior_Wave_Illustrated_Tome_01.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-texture-Warrior_Wave_Illustrated_Tome_01.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-texture-Warrior_Wave_Illustrated_Tome_01.png) A depiction of the Ra Gada facing pointy-eared enemies The [Lefthanded Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md) (also **Left-Handed Elves**, or simply **Lefthanders**), also known as ** Kanuryai** in their native tongue and **[Sinistral](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/sinistral#Adjective) Mer** (or **Sinestral Elves**), were a race of [elves](Lore_Elf.md) from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). They were the enemies of the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) and fought long and bitter conflicts with them for a thousand years, with the [Orichalc Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md#Orichalc) being supposedly involved in their wargames. It is said the [Lefthand Empire](Lore_Lefthand_Empire.md) was four times the size of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) under [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), which itself accounted for the entirety of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

## Maormer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/Orgnum_clean.png/200px-Orgnum_clean.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Orgnum_clean.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Orgnum_clean.png) Second Era depiction of King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), also known as **Sea Elves**, **Tropical Elves**, **Pyandoneans** (or derogatorily as **Fish Elves**), are a race of Mer that reside on the island kingdom of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md), far south of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). They possess many traits strange for elves, and they are theorized to be the result of interbreeding between [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) and the indigenous [bestial tribes](Lore_Beastfolk.md) of Pyandonea. Similar to their [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) cousins in build, their skin can range in tone from pearlescent white or grey to blue, and [Maormer Leviathans](Lore_Maormer_Leviathans.md) can have scales. Some Maormer have strange, chameleon-like skin that is entirely colorless, as if their flesh were made of some white limpid jelly. Some Maormer ears are broader than those of other mer, and are flared along the outer ear like a clip point blade. Their ears are shaped in such a way that they resemble fins. Maormer eyes can be pale blue, with a faint distinction between the sclera and the iris. Their eyes can also be blank. They also may have gills, though it is unclear if this claim was literal. Their tattoos are elaborate, and they tend to display a vicious demeanor towards the denizens of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Maormer are acclimated to life on the waves, and are steady on their feet in even the roughest waters. Maormer tongues can filter saltwater and make it potable. It is believed that Maormer bones are naturally springy.

Among the Maormer, the leviathans are the most feared. They have been described by one scholar as "the spawn of some unholy and sorcerous [coupling](Lore_Sex.md#Racial_Compatibility_and_Reproduction) of Sea Elf and [Sea Serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md)". They are scaly and massive, standing a head and a half over the tallest Nords when they draw themselves to their full height, which they don't often do because they carry themselves in a beastly posture. Maormer leviathans are usually hunched over, their backs bent and arced like a snake ready to strike. When they walk, their movements are akin to slithering in an uneven predatory lope. The Leviathans appear unused to using their legs on land. They move deftly in the water, and can climb effortlessly up the slippery sides of ships. They are capable of stretching their jaws out of place to swallow their prey.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Maormer.md).

## Orsimer
[Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), also called **Orsimer** (*[Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md): Cursed* or *Pariah Folk*) in ancient times (as well as in modern times by followers of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md)), and **Ornim** in their [native tongue](Lore_Old_Orcish.md), are the people of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), northern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) (literally translated as "Orc-Town"). They are noted for their unshakable courage in war and their unflinching endurance of hardships. Orcs have elven blood, but are sometimes grouped into [Beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md), specifically [Goblin-ken](Lore_Goblin-ken.md). Some people in High Rock refer to Orcish men as "stallion Orcs" and Orcish women as "mare Orcs" (who are described as being "seldom weaker" than stallion Orcs).

In the past, Orcs were widely feared and hated by the other nations and races of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and were seen as nothing more than uncivilized beasts, savage barbarians. The Orcs are as civilized as any of the other races, having worked hard to be seen as actual people.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> They have slowly won acceptance in the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), in particular for their distinguished service in the Emperor's [Legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). Many Orcs in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) have actually received an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) education as a benefit of serving the Imperial Legion. Orc armorers are prized for their craftsmanship, and Orc warriors in heavy armor are among the finest front-line troops in the Empire, and are fearsome when using their berserker rage.

Orcs have a lifespan similar to that of humans, possibly because according to the Nords, [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) passed a curse [Orkey](Lore_Orkey.md) had placed on the Nords onto the Orcs. [Orc's blood](Lore_Orc%27s_blood.md) has [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) properties, and is also known as the "Rising Sun". While many Imperial citizens regard Orc society as rough and cruel, the Orcs consider themselves to be stern, fair, and just. Orcs have their own language known as [Orcish](Lore_Orcish.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Orc.md).

## Snow Elves
The [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md), also known as the **Ancient** **[Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md)** or **Ice Elves**, are a proud race of mer with an unusually great aptitude for magic. They tend towards cold, remote regions, and inhabited most of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and some sites in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), before being displaced by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Before that, they had a civilization which rivaled even the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). They resemble the Altmer in build, being white-haired, pale-skinned, and have very long lifespans, as well as a strong resistance to frost, to the point where they were immune to cold and could live comfortably in ice caverns.

Much as "High Elf" and "Wood Elf" are interchangeable with "Altmer" and "[Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md)" respectively, "Falmer" is really just another term for "Snow Elf". However, in this case, time eventually provided distinct meanings for the two terms. Many of the ancient Snow Elves were betrayed in a deal for asylum they struck with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). The Dwemer forced their wards to subsist only on toxic fungi which twisted them into sightless slaves. It is the hideous descendants of these slaves who have come to represent the Falmer to many of the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). People often refer to the grand ancient race of Skyrim elves as Snow Elves, and to their barbaric remnants as Falmer.

Few know that an unknown number of civilized Snow Elves rejected the Dwemer's deal. Even fewer know that some have survived, uncorrupted, into modern times. [Knight-Paladin Gelebor](Lore_Gelebor.md) of the [Chantry of Auri-El](Lore_Chantry_of_Auri-El.md), the only known and potentially last Snow Elf still alive today, embraced the distinction between "Falmer" and "Snow Elf" because of the negative connotations "Falmer" had accumulated, though he preferred calling the corrupted Falmer "the Betrayed". Gelebor believes it is possible that there are some other isolated conclaves of surviving snow elves nestled elsewhere on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Snow_Elf.md).
