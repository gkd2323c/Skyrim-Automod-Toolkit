Fahara'jad was the High King of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and King of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) in the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), he assisted his son-in-law King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), ultimately helping Emeric emerge victorious in the conflict.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOKR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTTDC-2)</sup> In the aftermath of Ranser's War, he helped form the Second [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) and emerged as one of its primary leaders alongside High King Emeric and King [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](Lore_Kurog_gro-Bagrakh.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTTDC-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM10-3)</sup> He later helped lead the Covenant during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).

## History

### Background
Fahara'jad was the only child of Grandee [Makala](Lore_Makala.md) and Grandeya Fanesh of [Antiphyllos](Lore_Antiphyllos.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRLOS-4)</sup> Fahara'jad was a [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md), but he was a descendant of the [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md) [Phyllocid Dynasty](Lore_Phyllocid_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRLOS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LHa LAYQ-5)</sup> He was related to High King [Ar-Azal](Lore_Ar-Azal.md) and King [Ja-Fr](Lore_Ja-Fr.md). Fahara'jad married [Za-Rifah](Lore_Za-Rifah.md), the Flower of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md). With Za-Rifah, he had three children: [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md), the Jewel of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md); Lakana, the Star of the [Almandine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almandine); and Crown Prince [Azah](Lore_Azah.md), the Lion of Antiphyllos. Za-Rifah died while giving birth to Azah.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRLOS-4)</sup> After Za-Rifah's death, Fahara'jad grew close to her younger sister, Magnifica Falorah.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Zeira-6)</sup>

As a young prince, Fahara'jad was told various tales of [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) history, including *[The True-Told Tale of Hallin](Lore_The_True-Told_Tale_of_Hallin.md)* and *[The Worthy Ar-Azal, His Deeds](Lore_The_Worthy_Ar-Azal,_His_Deeds.md)*. These tales were later compiled and published as *The History of Histories, As Told to Young Prince Fahara'jad*.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWAAHD-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTTTH1-8)</sup>

According to the biography *[The All-Beneficent King Fahara'jad](Lore_The_All-Beneficent_King_Fahara%27jad.md)*, when Fahara'jad was a prince of Antiphyllos, he hunted birds in the Garden of the Grandees. After he killed a [crow](Lore_Crow.md), [Onsi](Lore_Onsi.md) himself descended from the heavens to save the prince from a vengeful [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md). The biography says Onsi intervened because Fahara'jad was prophesied to lead his people during the "Years of Peril" (the only witness to this encounter was Fahara'jad himself).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TABKF-9)</sup>

### King of Sentinel and High King of Hammerfell
Around [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), after the outbreak of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Ramzi the Distrait](Lore_Ramzi_the_Distrait.md), the King of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), sealed himself in the [Samuruik](Lore_Samuruik.md) Palace and slowly starved to death because of his fear of contracting the virus, leaving the kingdom in chaos. Amid the chaos, Fahara'jad journeyed from [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md) to [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) and declared himself King of Sentinel. After Fahara'jad declared himself king, the rulers of southern Hammerfell acted with vehement repudiation, but the Forebear viziers and northern chieftains ratified his accession. Many Crowns considered Fahara'jad to be a usurper, a false ruler waiting for punishment at the hands of [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md) the Worldskin.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTH-10)</sup>

During Fahara'jad's reign, one of his viziers, Suturah, slew all of the king's advisers and resurrected them as part of a failed assassination attempt on him. Suturah fled and raised an [undead](Lore_Undead.md) army to attack Sentinel. In response to the undead threat, Fahara'jad turned to the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) for help. This was a controversial move because the Ash'abah were considered pariahs in Redguard society for destroying the [Ra-Netu](Lore_Ra-Netu.md), an act that is taboo among Redguards. The Ash'abah headman, Marimah, agreed to help. In an ambush, the Ash'abah destroyed the army of Ra-Netu and killed Suturah. Fahara'jad was careful to stay quiet about his appeal to the Ash'abah and publicly attributed the Ra-Netu's destruction to intervention by the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SAMITA-11)</sup>

Around [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), traveled to Sentinel for an official visit with Fahara'jad.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TOAM6-12)</sup> Emeric was expected to wed [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md), the daughter of King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), but he suddenly decided that he wished to marry Maraya, and Fahara'jad offered Emeric her hand in marriage, which he accepted.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOKR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTTDC-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TOAM6-12)</sup> Emeric and Maraya married in the spring of 2E 566, and her dowry brought a trade agreement between [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and Hammerfell that strengthened trade between the two provinces.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOKR-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TOAM6-12)</sup> Lakana later married Emeric's brother Duke Nathaniel of [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md), further strengthening Fahara'jad's alliances.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTH-10)</sup>

Emeric's decision to marry Maraya instead of Rayelle infuriated Ranser, resulting in him initiating what later became called [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in Last Seed 2E 566. Ranser and his army besieged [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), and Fahara'jad sent battalions of elite Redguard warriors from Sentinel to defend the city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOKR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTTDC-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TOAM6-12)</sup> The Redguard warriors assisted the forces of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), and [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) in breaking the siege and driving Ranser's troops from [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md) in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TFOKR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTTDC-2)</sup> After Ranser's defeat and the end of Ranser's War in 2E 567, northern Hammerfell under Fahara'jad, the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) kingdoms, and the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) came together to form the second or greater [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTTDC-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM10-3)</sup>

After allying with the Daggerfall Covenant, Fahara'jad proclaimed himself High King of Hammerfell.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTH-10)</sup> Although Fahara'jad proclaimed himself High King of Hammerfell, his authority was not universally recognized across the province.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTH-10)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-13)</sup> The Crowns of southern Hammerfell were vexed by Fahara'jad's effrontery in proclaiming himself high king, but they found his claims too difficult to challenge.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTH-10)</sup> Emeric granted him nominal rulership over the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md), the southern region of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ARE-14)</sup>

### Three Banners War
Fahara'jad issued a decree placing all merchant ships and crews loyal to the Daggerfall Covenant under his protection and revoking all previously granted letters of marque against Breton and Orc ships. He directed allied ships to focus on vessels of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), offering a great bounty for their ships, cargo, and crew members.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BDOTWAPKF-15)</sup> He called on all "loyal sons and daughters of [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md)" to defend the Covenant.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AEPL-16)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Lakana was nearly poisoned, so Fahara'jad dispatched Envoy Darima and a regiment of soldiers led by his chief military advisor, General Thoda, to ensure her safety at [Alcaire Castle](Lore_Alcaire_Castle.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lakana-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Darima-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONThoda-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SHJ-20)</sup> The arrival of the Redguard soldiers at Alcaire Castle caused significant tension with the [Knights of the Flame](Lore_Knights_of_the_Flame.md), who protected Duke Nathaniel and Duchess Lakana, as they feared that the Redguards planned to attack.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Gerieux-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Falhut-22)</sup> Lakana was ultimately murdered by the leader of the Knights of the Flame, Sir Hughes, who was manipulated by the [Omen of Fear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Omen_of_Fear), a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) in service to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SHJ-20)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SHF-23)</sup> Lakana's remains were returned to Sentinel.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Darima-18)</sup>

That same year, a group of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) known as the Withered Hand, which was led by [Uwafa](Lore_Uwafa.md), Alasan, and Shiri, the children of Suturah, emerged to take revenge on Fahara'jad.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Fahara.27jad-24)</sup> To raise an undead army to battle Fahara'jad, the Withered Hand needed to remove the protection provided by the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md), which prevented consecrated dead from being raised by necromancy.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Fahara.27jad-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Farvad-25)</sup> For weeks, Fahara'jad ignored the Ash'abah's warnings about the Withered Hand.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONPA-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Talia-27)</sup> The Withered Hand kidnapped Crown Prince Azah as a distraction and then tunneled into the Impervious Vault beneath Sentinel, where they stole the Ansei Wards.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONPA-26)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TIV-28)</sup>

After the theft of the Ansei Wards, Fahara'jad sent an agent of the Covenant to contact the Ash'abah for assistance in combating the Whitered Hand's undead army and recovering the wards.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ISot A-29)</sup> Ultimately, the Withered Hand was defeated, and the Ansei Wards were returned to the Impervious Vault.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ARw U-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-AP-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-At H-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Rt AW-33)</sup>

Circa 2E 582, Fahara'jad requested that Emeric perform a favor for him by returning the Memory Stone of [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md). Emeric asked King [Eamond](Lore_Eamond.md) of Evermore for assistance in recovering the memory stone, who then ordered the [Knights of Saint Pelin](Lore_Knights_of_Saint_Pelin.md) to retrieve it from the Crypt of the Exiles in Bangkorai.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Ofa KO-34)</sup> Dame Frederique, the knight sent to recover the memory stone, was killed in the Crypt of the Exiles, but an adventurer managed to retrieve it and bring it to a Redguard scholar named Madaima, who was tasked with returning it to the Impervious Vault.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Ofa KO-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-AFBK-35)</sup>

After the Crisis of the Withered Hand and the Planemeld, Fahara'jad decided that his son Azah needed to understand the wider world to prepare for the throne. He wrote to Merric at-Aswala, Azah's former mentor and now leader of the Fighters Guild requesting his son's admission. Azah was to join not as a prince, but as a common man, to gain true experience in war, diplomacy, and leadership. Fahara'jad believed that through the Guild's neutrality, Azah could travel Tamriel, meet foreign leaders, and grow without royal privilege under the tutelage of Merric himself.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONPA-26)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-MTC-36)</sup> He also requested that his son compile a dossier on [Bahtra at-Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bahtra_at-Hunding) with the assistance of the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md). He also received a letter about her from Talia at-Marimah of the Ash'abah.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-37)</sup>
