The site later lent its name to the town of **Thorheim Guard**, a butchered Tamrielic version of the original name.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arena-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-THKL-3)</sup>

## History
Tor-Hame-Khard was originally an estate that belonged to the Prime Battlereeve Fiirfarya in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). But on one fateful morning, the entire populace was found slaughtered, which resulted in the settlement's abandonment.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-THK-1)</sup> Since then the site was inhabited by various dangerous parties.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-JT-4)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the ruins of Tor-Hame-Khard were used as the base of the [Sagacians](Lore_Sagacians.md), a rogue group of scholars that were paid to acquire an [Abyssal Pearl](Lore_Abyssal_Pearl.md), playing on a Justiciar's love and concern for her [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) -addicted sister. While the Pearl was bound for the [College of Sapiarchs](Lore_College_of_Sapiarchs.md), Justiciar Avanaire had stolen it, stowing it away deep within Tor-Hame-Khard. The [Divine Prosecution](Lore_Divine_Prosecution.md) was sent to recover the artifact but were swiftly defeated by the Sagacians. Their plot was foiled when the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) was able to defeat Avanaire before [Yaghra](Lore_Yaghra.md) and other dangerous creatures summoned by the pearl could spread from the ruins.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pearls_Before_Traitors-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The [Divine Prosecution](Lore_Divine_Prosecution.md) also sent deputized newcomers to the ruins to recover stolen relics from the Sagacians and to obtain information on the group's plans.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

Family shrine of the line of Nivulirel was located inside Tor-Hame-Khard.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LFN-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Roguzog-10)</sup>

Sometime later during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Thorheim Guard was thriving, under the rule of Lady Zenosha. It had a rivalry with [Silver Wood](Lore_Silver_Wood.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Arena-2)</sup>
