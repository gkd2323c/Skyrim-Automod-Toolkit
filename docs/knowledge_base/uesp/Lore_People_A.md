#### Master Abelle Chriditte (fl. [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427))
[Abelle Chriditte](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abelle_Chriditte) was a renowned [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) master [alchemist](Lore_Alchemy.md) from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). She was also a [witch](Lore_Witches.md), who specialized in [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) and [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md), and practiced [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md).

#### Abnur Tharn ([2E 418](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_418) - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) was the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [Grand Chancellor](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and overlord of [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) during the middle of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Much like his ancestors, who were known for holding positions of power throughout [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) since the days of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), Tharn served as advisor to various [emperors](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) and kings for over 117 years. During the turbulent rise and fall of Cyrodiilic rulers during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), Tharn was the sole constant, providing continuity and consistency to the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md). Fiercely loyal to the Empire, Tharn became Chancellor of Elder Council in [2E 465](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_465) and has served as the [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) ever since. Tharn is also the [author](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Lord_Abnur_Tharn) of various texts.

#### Chief-Crier Adara'hai (fl. 2E 582)
**Chief-Crier** [Adara'hai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adara%27hai) was a leading figure of the [Twilight Cantors](Lore_Twilight_Cantors.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was responsible for several high-profile instances of [dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md) vanquishing around the time of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).

#### Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal (2E ?b - 2E 572)
[Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md), also spelled **Ada'Soon Dir-Kamal**, was the King of [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(place).md), a nation of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md).

In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), he led an army of his people in an invasion of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), reportedly in search of a person or object roughly translated as the "[Ordained Receptacle](Lore_Ordained_Receptacle.md)". Dir-Kamal's army landed in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) near the city of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), which they subsequently besieged and sacked. [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md), was summoned to help repel the invasion of the "Akaviri demon" (sources differ on who summoned him). Dir-Kamal decided to bypass [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) and retreat from Skyrim, instead setting his sights on [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) refused to let him get away and pursued him into [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), where his army was crushed between them, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) army led by [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), and a third force of [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) Shellbacks. Remnants of Dir-Kamal's army survived this battle and resurfaced years later in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where they seized the Imperial Throne from the successors of the Emperor [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(warlord).md).

#### Adrimk (fl. Merethic Era)
[Adrimk](Lore_Adrimk.md) was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior and a member of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). Adrimk was one of many ancient heroes recounted in the *[Songs of the Return](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return.md)*. She was known for her wisdom and remembered as the **war-teacher**.

#### Adonia of the Heart's Ache (fl. First Era)
[Adonia of the Heart's Ache](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adonia_of_the_Heart%27s_Ache) was an [Arcanist](Lore_Arcanist.md), artist, and artisan from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), recognized for her association with the Girvayne family, a renowned [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) lineage of [instrument](Lore_Instruments.md) makers.

#### Aegilief (3E ?b - [3E 393](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_393))
[Aegilief](Lore_Aegilief.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) chieftain of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the fourth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). She became Chieftain of Thirsk after slaying Oddny the Unfaithful and presenting her head as a battle trophy. She was chieftain for eight years.

#### Aela the Huntress (fl. [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Aela the Huntress](Lore_Aela_the_Huntress.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) member of the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). Aela was one of the highest-ranking members, and as such she was a member of the [Circle](Lore_The_Circle.md).

Aela's family has a long and proud history with the Companions. Her mother was a Companion, as was her grandmother, and every woman in her line, all the way back to [Hrotti Blackblade](Lore_Hrotti_Blackblade.md), who stood among the original Five Hundred. Aela remained in the wilderness with her father until she was old enough to undertake the Trial. Though her mother did not live to see her join the ranks, Aela fought to honor her memory and the legacy of all her Shield-Sisters throughout the ages.

#### Aer, the Conjurer Magnificent (fl. 1E 2150)
[Aer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aer), **the Conjurer Magnificent** was a pioneer [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) mage. She is accredited with the summoning of the first [Iron Atronach](Lore_Iron_Atronach.md) [bear](Lore_Bear.md) in history.

Little is known of Aer's history. She lived in the late years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and was a conjurer of some renown, earning her title of the Conjurer Magnificent. In [1E 2150](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2150), she performed the first summoning of Iron Atronach Bear.

By the [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the art of conjuring Iron Atronachs shaped like bears was popularized among the conjuration mages. Aer's notes survived to that time, and, according to them, the lumbering, ursine frame matched the atronach's temperament perfectly, making the calling-ritual far easier than traditional summoning.

#### Aerin (fl. 3E 427)
[Aerin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aerin) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) thief and a master of light armor in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). She was a trainer in the use of light armor and was an expert on the various styles. She was also highly proficient at sneaking and acrobatics and was known to offer training in these skills as well.

#### Aetherion
[Aetherion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherion) is an experimental [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) created by the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) of [Skyreach](Lore_Skyreach.md) while they were researching the power of [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md). It took the form of an ethereal [Daedric Titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md), a Daedric perversion of the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) that was created by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Aetherion was close to immortal, and would simply condense back into a core of [Aetherial](Lore_Aetherius.md) energy when its corporeal form was destroyed. It was imprisoned within Skyreach Pinnacle in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) along with the lesser Celestials some time after [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808), when [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) invaders sealed the Nedic wards.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md) unsealed the ruins in their attempt to understand the Nedes' usage of nirncrux. This freed the dangerous Celestials within, forcing the cultists to retreat. The expedition leader, Mederic Vyger, subsequently left the Court and attempted to set things right. He enlisted the aid of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and re-entered the ruins to fight back against the Celestials and restore the wards. Aetherion appeared at the very end of the ritual in an attempt to stop Vyger, but the intruders destroyed his core and the wards were successfully restored.

Following the restoration of the ancient wards in the Pinnacle and the defeat of Aetherion, he was imprisoned again in Skyreach.

#### Queen Afsar (fl. Late First Era)
**Queen** [Afsar](Lore_Afsar.md) was a powerful [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) monarch who ruled [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) in the Late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). She was remembered as one of the people who changed the course of Redguard history.

#### Agnar the Unwavering (3E ? - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Agnar the Unwavering](Lore_Agnar_the_Unwavering.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) chieftain of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) in the fifth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Originally from the mainland, Agnar came to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) for some much-needed rest and found it in the mead-soaked halls of Thirsk. He became Chieftain of Thirsk after the previous Chieftain, the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), reportedly journeyed to [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). He accepted [Svenja Snow-Song](Lore_Svenja_Snow-Song.md) as his advisor and later married her.

Svenja was haunted by the Udyrfrykte's massacre of Thirsk in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), which she was one of the only survivors of. To prevent any more destruction, Agnar and Svenja traveled across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in hopes of finding and slaying the [Uderfrykte Matron](Lore_Uderfrykte_Matron.md), the progenitor of the Udyrfrykte. Around [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a shepherd spotted it in the remote highlands of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and he and Svenja then tracked it across the border into [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). In Cyrodiil, a hunter told them about the Horror of Dive Rock, which Agnar and Svenja concluded was the matron. They established a camp on the overlook at [Dive Rock](Lore_Dive_Rock.md), and after three days of watching, they spotted the matron. Once they confronted the matron, Svenja was quickly struck down and devoured, forcing Agnar to retreat to the camp. The chieftain made final preparations to attack the beast, but he was killed as well. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) later stumbled upon the camp, discovered Agnar's body and journal, and slew the Uderfrykte Matron.

The names of Agnar and the succeeding chieftains of Thirsk are not recorded in *Thirsk, a Revised History*, with the written record only resuming after a period of around one hundred fifty years. The Nerevarine and Agnar are the only known chieftains of Thirsk from that period of unrecorded history.

#### Agnorith (fl. 2E 9th Century)
[Agnorith](Lore_Agnorith.md) was the brother of [Emperor Tiber Septim I](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). Agnorith had a daughter named [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_I.md), who was the Queen of [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md), and became Empress Kintyra Septim I after her [first cousin once removed](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_I.md) was assasinated at the [Temple of the One](Lore_Temple_of_the_One.md) by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). Thereafter, Agnorith's descendants continued the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), ushering in the beloved line of [Emperors Uriel](Lore_Emperors_Uriel.md).

#### Ahbiilok (?b - ?d)
[Ahbiilok](Lore_Ahbiilok.md) is a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Hunter-Blue-Sky" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

During the early years of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), at some point after the founding of the organization in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), he was seen throughout the northern parts of the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). There were numerous recorded attempts to kill him, but not a single one was successful. He was recorded as an active dragon by the chronicler of the Dragonguard, [Brother Mathnan](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Brother_Mathnan), in the *[Atlas of Dragons](Lore_Atlas_of_Dragons.md)*, and as of [2E 373](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_373), he was believed to be lairing somewhere in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).

According to *[Twin Secrets](Lore_Twin_Secrets.md)*, a renowned [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md) [Brarilu Theran](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Brarilu_Theran) found a dragon sheltered in the smoking ruins of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and defeated it in combat. Instead of killing the Dragon, he parlayed with it. He asked the Dragon for its name, but the Dragon refused and instead offered to teach the [mer](Lore_Mer.md) how to defy the Law of Firsts and enchant items with multiple effects.

#### Ahzidal (fl. late Merethic Era)
[Ahzidal](Lore_Ahzidal.md) (meaning "the Embittered Destroyer" or literally "Bitter Destroyer" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md)) was the first great [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) enchanter, and possibly the first human to master the Elven methods of enchanting. As a [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), he was a powerful mage, and was also able to wield the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) in battle.

#### Sapiarch Aicantar of Shimmerene (fl. 2E 582)
[Aicantar of Shimmerene](Lore_Aicantar_of_Shimmerene.md) was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) [author](Lore_Books_by_Author.md) from [Shimmerene](Lore_Shimmerene.md), and the [Sapiarch](Lore_College_of_Sapiarchs.md) of Indoctrination active around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), whose works mainly consisted of propaganda for the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).

He was responsible for several works of propaganda relating to the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), namely *[Crimes of the Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Crimes_of_the_Daggerfall_Covenant.md)*, *[Regarding the Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Regarding_the_Ebonheart_Pact.md)*, *[An Unusual Alliance](Lore_An_Unusual_Alliance.md)*, and *[Welcome to New Aldmeri Irregulars](Lore_Welcome_to_New_Aldmeri_Irregulars.md)*. He is also the author of several official [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) documents, such as [a handbill](Lore_Thalmor_Handbill.md) regarding Dominion etiquette and [responses](Lore_Response_to_Citizen_Inquiries.md) to inquiries submitted to the Presidium of Aldmeri Cultural Illumination and Dissemination. Arguably Aicantar's most notable work is *[Before the Ages of Man](Lore_Before_the_Ages_of_Man.md)*, a chronicle of the [Dawn](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) and [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) Eras which survived in popular literature well into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He also wrote guidelines for the apparel, armor, and equipment of the Aldmeri Dominion. Additionally, he engaged in correspondence explaining various noteworthy topics related to the affairs of the Aldmeri Dominion.

Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Aicantar was the subject of an assassination plot in Shimmerene. The [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md) recruited Apraxic Mer—outcasts forced to live in the city's sewers—whom they attempted to persuade into murdering Aicantar, hoping to cause discord and turmoil in Shimmerene. The plot was uncovered by a [newcomer](Lore_Soulless_One.md), however, who killed the Court of Bedlam's agent in Shimmerene, putting an end to their scheme.

#### Prince Aiden Direnni (fl. 1E 480 - 482)
[Prince Aiden Direnni](Lore_Aiden_Direnni.md) was a member of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) Clan. An Altmer folk-hero, he appears in myths as the last king of a heroic line.

Often spoken of alongside his cousins [Ryain Direnni](Lore_Ryain_Direnni.md) and [Raven Direnni](Lore_Raven_Direnni.md), Aiden won many battles against the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) in [1E 480](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_480). During this time he reluctantly invoked the [Rights Charter](Lore_Rights_Charter.md), allowing him to conscript his [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) subjects into the war. His crowning achievement was leading Direnni forces to victory against the Alessians in the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md) in [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482), ending their ambitions of conquering [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The [Day of Release](Lore_Day_of_Release.md) was celebrated thereafter by the people of [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) to commemorate his victory.

Over three thousand years later, around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), his broken sword was recovered by the [Merethic Society](Lore_Merethic_Society.md) to be displayed in their museum in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md).

#### Queen Akorithi ([3E 369](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_369) - ?d)
[Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md) (sometimes **Akorith-i** or **Akorithi dir Abibon**) was Queen of the Kingdom of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) and wife of King [Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md), and became the Queen Regent upon her husband's death at the [Battle of Cryngaine Field](Lore_Battle_of_Cryngaine_Field.md) in [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403). Akorithi and Camaron were the parents of [Arthago](Lore_Arthago.md), [Aubk-i](Lore_Aubk-i.md), [Greklith](Lore_Greklith.md), and [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md).

As the ruler of Sentinel, Akorithi played a major role in the events that culminated in the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417). She and Greklith both died of a plague sometime near the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), and the queen was succeeded by her youngest son, Lhotun.

#### Alandro Sul (fl. 1E 7th Century)
[Alandro Sul](Lore_Alandro_Sul.md) (or **Alandro-Sul**) was the shield-companion of [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) during the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md). It is said he is the immortal son of [Azura](Lore_Azura.md). According to an ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) account of the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), he wore the Wraith Mail during the battle, and fought the Ash King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md). He defeated Wulfharth, but was struck blind during the fight. After the battle, he lived with the [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md), and is credited with spreading the belief among them that the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) murdered Nerevar.

#### Baron Alard Dorell (fl. 2E 567 - 582)
[Alard Dorell](Lore_Alard_Dorell.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman who reigned as the Baron of [House Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Following [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), Dorell served alongside the leaders of [Houses Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) as a member of the triumvirate Council of the North, which was formed to govern [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) until the selection of a new King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), House Montclair's representative on the Council of the North, initiated a civil war in Rivenspire so he could become King of Shornhelm. During the civil war, Alard and House Dorell allied with Houses Tamrith and [Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) to prevent House Montclair from conquering Rivenspire. Ultimately, Baron Dorell played a crucial role in defeating House Montclair and killing Baron Montclair at the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md).

#### Empress Alessia (?b - [1E 266](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_266))
[Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), also known as **Queen Alessia**, **Saint Alessia**, the **Lady of Heaven**, **Queen-ut-Cyrod**, **Queen of Mortals**, **Slave Queen of Cyrodiil**, **Mother of the Empire**, **Queen of Stars**, and **El-Estia**, was the first Empress of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the founder of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), the first of three Cyrodilic empires. She led the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) against the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) in the third century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), freeing the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) of [Cyrod](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) from slavery and founding the Alessian Empire. As the first Empress of Cyrodiil, she established a new religion, a fusion of the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) and [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) pantheons known as the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md). On her deathbed in [1E 266](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_266), she was canonized by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) or [Shezarr](Lore_Shezarr.md), and her soul placed in the central stone of the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), thus forging Akatosh's covenant with men to protect [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) from the forces of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and starting the [Oversoul](Lore_Oversoul.md) of emperors. She has also been stated to have achieved [apotheosis](Lore_Apotheosis.md) in death, and was described as becoming wife to [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) and [Auri-el](Lore_Auri-El.md), in addition to her marriage with Morihaus. Among worshippers of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), Alessia is the [patron saint](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) of humanity. The Empress is associated with [The Thief Constellation](Lore_The_Thief.md).

Alessia is not her birth name, but rather a corruption of a title, **Al-Esh** ("High Highness"), that was given to her by her followers. Another title bestowed on her was **Paravant** ("The First"). From corruptions of these two titles come countless other names by which she is known to various peoples, including **Perrif**, **Paraval**, **Pevesh**, **Perrethu**, **Paravania**, **Esha**, and **Aleshut**. The [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) was so-called because she purportedly appeared to the prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md) in a vision and bestowed her teachings upon him, for which she was known by the Order as the "**Enlightened One**". She married the hereditary Count of Chorrol, [Charus Valga](Lore_Charus_Valga.md), and they had one daughter, [Alessia](Lore_Alessia_Caro.md). Count Valga was killed in battle against [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) clansmen of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and his death was greatly lamented by the Countess and the people of Chorrol. After Charus' death, Arriana became the ruling Countess of Chorrol. The Count's death greatly affected her, as on many a lonely night she would speak to the portrait of him that hung in her bedchamber.

During her reign, Valga was regarded as a dignified, righteous, and reliable ruler. She was known as a devout follower of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and frequently attended the [Chapel of Stendarr](Lore_Temple_of_Stendarr.md) in Chorrol. Around [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), Alessia married the Count of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), [Marius Caro](Lore_Marius_Caro.md), and she dutifully visited Arriana every month.

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Chorrol's court mage, Chanel, stole the portrait of Count Valga from the Countess' bedchamber. Valga was devastated by the loss of the portrait and launched an investigation into the theft. It is unknown if the painting was ever recovered. During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), Valga, unlike the other counts and countesses of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), did not have a strong opinion of High Chancellor [Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md)'s handling of it. After an Oblivion Gate opened outside of Chorrol's south gate, she warned the people of Chorrol to stay inside the walls, and she put the city guard on full alert. However, some of Chorrol's residents felt that the Countess was more worried about finding the portrait of Count Valga than the security of the city. When the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) closed the Oblivion Gate outside of Chorrol, Valga provided military aid for the defense of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md).

As a Dragon Priest, he was a powerful mage, also able to wield the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) in battle.

#### Queen Arzhela (fl. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))

At the parley, Arzhela and her guards were attacked by the Seventh Legion, but the Queen managed to escape safely. #### Atrozu
**Atrozu** was the [Totem Spirit](Lore_Totem_Spirit.md) of the Kalmur Clan. He was an embodiment of the clan as a whole. Memories, hopes and dreams, and even souls of every clan member all came together to form Atrozu. The clan totem took on the shape of a ghostly baby bear. It was formed from the shared kinship and familial love of the clan members. The strength of unity and family is what manifests and makes the existence of Atrozu possible. This rare practice among the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) didn't go unnoticed as the concept drew the attention of the hungry Ravener, a [Dremnaken](Lore_Dremnaken.md) in the service of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), who plotted to steal and feast on this familial bond and take possession of Atrozu for himself in order to learn and understand the concept of mortal family.

Unlike how it is the case among traditional Orcish clans where the wise woman is the mother of the Chieftain, the Kalmur tie this role with Atrozu. Instead, Atrozu is responsible for choosing each wise woman for the clan. Part of the training for the position of the wise woman involved learning how to speak with and summoning Atrozu.

However, during an ambitious, unapproved magical experiment in the Midden, he lost his corporeal form and wound up fused to the magical energies that flow through the College. He thereafter appeared instead as a large sphere of blue-white light, described as an incorporeal "maelstrom of writhing oddness and flame". He has sequestered himself in the Midden, the catacombs beneath the College, ever since, but keeps tabs on everything going on in the College. He possesses a unique wisdom which the College has relied on in times of need.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), he guided the College through the potentially disastrous crisis they faced following their excavation of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md) and the discovery of the [Eye of Magnus](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md).

#### Sybil Augustine Viliane (fl. 2E 582)
**Lady** [Augustine Viliane](Lore_Augustine_Viliane.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [Sibyl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sibyl) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md)'s chapter of the [House of Dibella](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md). Aside from her role as a high-ranking member of the clergy, she also authored books and letters addressing topics of faith, including *[Dibella's Mysteries and Revelations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella%27s_Mysteries_and_Revelations)*.

#### Avalian (fl. before 2E 582)
[Avalian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Avalian) was a hero held in high regard by the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) branch of the Cult of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), according to which he was a blessed child of Trinimac. He features in the Orsimer religious scripture *The Book of the Warrior God* and has a passage titled *Avalian's Flight* devoted to him and his deeds. In this passage, he is described as charging the volcanic mountain and fighting the fiery god of lava and stone during its eruption, and driving his blade into the heart of the beast. His deeds influenced the metalworking of the Trinimac Cult faithful. They notch the blades of their axes to evoke Avalian's axe, which was damaged when it cracked the heart of the fire mountain, and emboss them with [Vaia](Lore_Vaia.md)'s Golden Ash, the symbol of strength and unity of the Orcs.

#### [Avenger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Avenger)
The [Avenger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Avenger) is an entity who has been evoked by the likes of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) King [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-KEII-12)</sup> Nothing is known of them beyond their mere existence other than the fact that the context in which they were evoked was one of anger.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-KEII-12)</sup>

#### Duke Avrippe Mornard (2E? b - [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568))
[Avrippe Mornard](Lore_Avrippe_Mornard.md) was the Duke of the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) and head of [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), Avrippe supported King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) against his liege lord, King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md). After Ranser's defeat in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), Emeric stripped Avrippe of his dukedom, granted it to House Mornard's rival, [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md), and reduced the Mornards to the title of count. Emeric then exiled him to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where he died in [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568).

#### Vateshran Aydolan (fl. before 1E 2502)
**Vateshran** [Aydolan](Lore_Aydolan.md) **of the [Spiritblood Clan](Lore_Spiritblood_Clan.md)**, the **Spiritblood Champion** was a [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) storyteller and historian who followed the [Carinar the Sharp](Lore_Carinar.md) after the [Quicktalon Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quicktalon_Clan) was divided by the patricide. He was the creator, and one of the first champions of the [Vateshran's Rites](Lore_Vateshran%27s_Rites.md) performed in the ancient caves of the [Vateshran Hollows](Lore_Vateshran_Hollows.md), which later served as the clan's coming of age trials, as well as the trials for the chieftains. Following his death he remained on the [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) as ancestral spirit to oversee the trials and guide the generations of Spritblood.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/ON-npc-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Queen_Ayrenn.jpg) Queen Ayrenn of Alinor
#### Queen Ayrenn of Alinor ([2E 555](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_555) - ?d)
[Ayrenn Arana Aldmeri](Lore_Ayrenn.md) (also known as **Queen Ayrenn the First** and known by titles such as the **Eagle Primarch of the Aldmeri Dominion** and **Monarch of the Throne of Alinor**) was the High Queen of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), the Queen of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md), and one of the major faction leaders of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She founded the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) during the Second Era, in the midst of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), which she formed by allying with the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Her adventures prior to her ascension opened her eyes to the corruption brewing at the very heart of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). She competed against High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) and [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) for the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md). She sought to conquer [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to usher in a new age of Elven rule across Tamriel to protect it from the reckless tendencies of the younger races, particularly those of [men](Lore_Men.md), who she saw as shortsighted due to their brief lives.

#### The Unveiled Azadiyeh (fl. 2E 582)
**The Unveiled** [Azadiyeh](Lore_Azadiyeh.md), the **Songbird of Satakalaam** was a renowned and well-known [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) historian and cultural scholar of the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), traditionalists dedicated to preserving their [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) roots. She authored numerous texts of a scholarly, religious, artistic, agitative, and didactic nature, detailed various locales of historical significance to her people, and critiqued the politics of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), High King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) and [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). She was also a [spellbinder](Lore_Magic.md).

#### Crown Prince Azah (fl. 2E 582)
**Crown Prince** [Azah](Lore_Azah.md), **the Lion of [Antiphyllos](Lore_Antiphyllos.md)**, was the third child and only son of King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), and Queen [Za-Rifah](Lore_Za-Rifah.md) the Flower of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md). He was the younger brother of Queen [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md) and Duchess Lakana. He was instrumental in the fall of the Withered Hand, and formation of the Stirk Fellowship.

#### Azandar al-Cybiades (fl. 2E 582)
[Azandar al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md) was a renowned [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [arcanist](Lore_Arcanist.md), known for his exceptional expertise in the realm of magical theory and practice. His unique approach to harnessing the power of magic set him apart as a brilliant theoretician in the field. His world-class talent, power and knowledge was compared to likes of [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) of the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md).

#### Azra Nightwielder (?b - ?d)
[Azra Nightwielder](Lore_Azra_Nightwielder.md) was the original Shadowmage, and a pioneer of [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md). He was the first to discover that shadows were not merely an absence of light, but the reflection of possible worlds created by forces in conflict.

References to Azra are unreliable, as even texts in the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md)'s archives on [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) have been tampered with Shadow Magic. However, according to them, Azra was born somewhere in the early Second Era in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md), which lies between [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md), and [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). Despite the Order's tampered estimate of this date of birth, shadow mages have been seen dating back to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).
