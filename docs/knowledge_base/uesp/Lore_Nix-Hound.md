Nix-hounds (often shortened as **nixes**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> are bloodsucking insectoid pack-hunters akin to [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) that are native to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IEGT-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BGN-3)</sup> as well as having migrated to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) as a result of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Creation-4)</sup>

In [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) mythology, nix-hounds were created by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) to hunt the [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) during a time-lost campaign against them.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-5)</sup> However, they are more commonly considered distant relatives of [nix-oxen](Lore_Nix-Ox.md) and [kwama](Lore_Kwama.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> and are considered to be in a family known as [nix creatures](Lore_Nix_Creature.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Nix-7)</sup>

## Biology

### Behavior and Diet
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/SR-creature-Nix-Hounds.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Nix-Hounds.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Nix-Hounds.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Nix-Hounds.jpg) Nix-Hounds on Solstheim
(Skyrim Creation Club) Nix-hounds sustain themselves by using their proboscis to drain their prey of bodily fluids, leaving behind a 'meat-husk' that other creatures such as [alits](Lore_Alit.md) will eat.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> Though sometimes impatient alits will fight with nix-hounds over a fresh kill.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Common prey include baby [guars](Lore_Guar.md), [goats](Lore_Goat.md), kwama pupae and [ash hoppers](Lore_Ash_Hopper.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> In rare cases, large nix-hounds may prey on other, weaker nix-hounds.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSorim Nakar-9)</sup>

When young nix-hounds emerge from their chrysalis, they can immediately drain up to four similar sized prey a day. They have been noted as being very opportunistic while searching for food, easily killing small animals and children even at this early development stage.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> While primarily carnivorus, newborn nix-hounds have also been known to occasionally graze on plants such as [marshmerrow](Lore_Marshmerrow.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MHWN-10)</sup> Adult wild nix-hounds are aggressive pack-hunters, with many [men](Lore_Men.md) and [mer](Lore_Mer.md) meeting their end from an unsuspected ambush. They are said to be as fast as wolves, but are too simple minded to flee a losing battle.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-KCAHC-11)</sup>

*Always remember to face your nix when feeding it.* *Avoid direct eye contact while your nix is relieving itself.*

*Make sure your friends and neighbors don't leave their pets and children unattended around your nix.*

*Never shout at your nix.*

*Never whisper to your nix.*

*Never show another nix excessive attention or affection.*

*Avoid contact with the nix's saliva, as it is corrosive and may form a permanent bond with the skin.*

*Never display any signs of physical weakness around your nix.*
— *[Nix-Hounds: A Manual for New Owners](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nix-Hounds__A_Manual_for_New_Owners)*
### Cleaning and Diseases
Nix-hounds are known to shed.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The average nix-hound can spend hours a day self-cleaning, they use their nimble pedipalps to scrape and polish their facial chitin and antennae. However, they can struggle to reach their thorax and parts of their abdomen. This can lead to them suffering from pests such as [ash mites](Lore_Mite.md), which can cause even a tame nix-hound to become extremely aggressive.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> They are also subject to several other parasites and diseases, including [dampworm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dampworm), [rabies](Lore_Rabies.md), [rattles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rattles), [rockjoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rockjoint), and [black-heart blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black-Heart_Blight), the latter making them stronger and more dangerous to travelers.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IEGT-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

### Variants
The Coldspring Nix-Hound is a breed of nix-hound with striking blue and purple coloration, bred by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) to survive in environments outside their native [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Rogue nix-hounds are a larger and much more dangerous breed of the common nix-hound.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BGN-3)</sup>

Primitive nix-hounds were known to exist in the distant past. These creatures were darker in coloration than their more modern counterparts.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) of Bal Sunnar once used [time magic](Lore_Time_Magic.md) to summon primitive nix-hounds and nix-oxen from the past for use as guardians.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/MW-ing-Hound_Meat.jpg/200px-MW-ing-Hound_Meat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-ing-Hound_Meat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-ing-Hound_Meat.jpg) Nix-Hound Meat (Morrowind) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/SR-quest-My_Pet_Nix-Hound.jpg/200px-SR-quest-My_Pet_Nix-Hound.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-My_Pet_Nix-Hound.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-My_Pet_Nix-Hound.jpg) A trainer with his tamed Nix-Hound
(Skyrim Creation Club) While usually vicious predators, nix-hounds are well-known to be affectionate, attentive, and faithful pets that will follow their owners around.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Creation-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Manual-1)</sup> Shamans in particular, were known for their ability to train the creatures.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-IWADE-18)</sup> "Nixie" is an affectionate name for pet nix-hounds.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Nix-hounds can be trained to scrounge for specific resources, such as sea shells and [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) ingredients.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOBBL-20)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Creation-4)</sup> They are also commonly used as trackers, watch-hounds, and [netch](Lore_Netch.md) -deflators, particularly by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of [Bal Foyen](Lore_Bal_Foyen.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Crown-21)</sup> Nix-hounds were used as war dogs in the [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md) chap'thil.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FSo KW-22)</sup> According to legend, one of [Ysgramor's](Lore_Ysgramor.md) pets of renown that were among his [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) was "Rackety-Nix the Nix-hound".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-FHMC-23)</sup>

Nix-hound meat is said to be sweet and tender, and it has modest magical properties.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BGN-3)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MHM-24)</sup> It is said to taste good with fresh strudel.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-25)</sup> [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) commonly use nix-hound meat to make chili.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-26)</sup> They also use nix-hound parts in a variety of their tribal customs.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSorim Nakar-9)</sup> Nix-hound eyes are considered quite valuable.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Alchemists are known to render nix-hound flesh to make [drugs](Lore_Drugs.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ACTB-28)</sup>

Nix-hounds are a regular subject of Dunmeri cultural works. Examples include an appearance in "The Friendly Alit", as one of the many creatures frightened by the titular Alit.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BAFf M-29)</sup> In the *36 Lessons of Vivec*, the Warrior-Poet describes [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) as being "clawed like a nix-hound".<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-36LS6-30)</sup> Felt toys of sleeping nix-hounds are sometimes given to Dunmer children.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOCodex-31)</sup>
