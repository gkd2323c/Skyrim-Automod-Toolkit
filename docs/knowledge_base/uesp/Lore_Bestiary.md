# Bestiary

—The scholar Pim, discussing the importance of education about Tamriel's creature<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> **The Bestiary** of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and other realms are filled with many diverse creatures, both dangerous and benign. Many examples of creatures such as the skittish [deer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deer) or the opportunistic [nix-hound](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nix-Hound), [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) entities such as the destructive [ruinach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruinach) or the puny [fiendroth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fiendroth), and the damned [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead) — including the accursed [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) and the powerful [lich](Lore_Lich.md) — are included in this guide; this clearly demonstrates the extent and variety of the creatures and **fauna** that inhabit the universe.

This overview serves to describe how these animals, Daedra, undead, and other beings affect the world, how the environments they live in have affected them, and their roles in the world in general.

## Creatures in General

### Variety
[Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and certain other realms are home to many non-intelligent mortal creatures, known as animals or beasts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> These creatures are typically believed to be the creation of a nature [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:God), though which one depends on the culture.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Kyne C-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BMSkaal-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ius-6)</sup> Animals themselves are divided into many categories based on their traits or morphology, such as vertebrates or invertebrates,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Wpet-7)</sup> with further differentiation being classes like [mammals](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mammal),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OArg-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ga E-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Eot O-10)</sup> [reptiles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OArg-8)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Eot O-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ga E-9)</sup> [birds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> [fish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fish),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Eot O-10)</sup> [amphibians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Lore-Creatures-Amphibians),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Eot O-10)</sup> [arthropods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arthropod),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> [echinoids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Echinoid),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> or [mollusks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mollusk).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> These classifications can also be divided into further subcategories or families, for example within the classification of "mammal" there are marsupials,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ius-6)</sup> canines,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> felines,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONSenche Cougar-16)</sup> rodents,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-GMT-17)</sup> and many others. Intelligent mortal creatures are typically considered [races](Lore_Races.md), though races that are considered "monsters" by the "civilized" races of Tamriel are typically included on this Bestiary list. Monster is a broad term that applies to most any violent or strange creature, as such it is practically useless as an actual classification,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> though this has not stopped the inhabitants of Tamriel from using it as such.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

There exists many types of creatures besides mortal animals. The [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) is home to many spirit creatures, such as the [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) of Nirn, and the Daedra of the myriad realms of Oblivion.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-So S-21)</sup> Living creatures can be unnaturally revived through [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) to become [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead) creatures.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-GMT-17)</sup> Non-living materials can be given life as [golems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem), with more advanced varieties like [animunculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) and [factotums](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Factotum) doing this through machinery. [Fabricants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fabricant) are artificially grown mortal creatures that combine technology with flesh.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PLJ-22)</sup> Some creatures are created through magical modifications or curses such as the [lycanthropes](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), or the [ash creatures](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Creature) of [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur). Others are created specifically through the combination of other creatures, with composite creatures such as [chimeras](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chimera), [vvardvarks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vvardvark), and [mantikoras](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantikora) being examples of this.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Lives-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TBOG-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LA-25)</sup>

Certain creatures are believed to only exist in myths and fiction, such as the [rhinoceros](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rhinoceros).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> Though the inhabitants of Tamriel have been mistaken on such things, as the very real [unicorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unicorn) is believed by many to only be a myth.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

### Uses
Creatures serve many uses to the intelligent races throughout Mundus and Oblivion. While a creature may serve one primary purpose, it does not mean its role is set in stone. Some animals can have multiple purposes, such as the [horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horse) and the [guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guar), which can serve as beasts of burden for carrying people and cargo, or be used as mounts for war. Uses can depend on culture as well, as in a few, horses are instead used as a source of food.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-EDAYQ-28)</sup>

It is common for animals and monsters to be hunted as food or for their fur, bones, or alchemical properties. In some cases they are even used as building supplies, most notably in the case of the [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) city of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) where the Redoran Council maintained a Manor District under the shell of an [Emperor crab](Lore_Emperor_Crab.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-NDM-29)</sup> Many species are domesticated and farmed so that their meat or produce can be harvested with ease. A notable example being the [kwama](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama) whose eggs are harvested for food in underground mines.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

Many animals can also serve as pets or companions, with common examples being [dogs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dog) and [cats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cat), and more exotic examples including [mudcrabs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mudcrab) and [salamanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salamander). In some cases creatures are used as a work force; many intelligent creatures such as [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) and [Ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre) are enslaved and made to live lives of forced labor.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE1-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup> Daedra such as [clannfear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clannfear) are often used as summoned [familiars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Familiar) by mages, or as soldiers in Daedric armies.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Kyne C-4)</sup><sup>:159</sup> Death doesn't always mean the end of usefulness for a mortal creature, as they are sometimes raised by necromancers to serve as guards or as experimental subjects.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ALC-33)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-GMT-17)</sup>

### Extinction
Due to external factors many animal species on Nirn can and have been driven to extinction. This has often been the result of disasters such as the [Ash Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Blight) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) or the Great Burn of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-VTAN-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-PWF-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Antiquity WTS-36)</sup> Occasionally extinctions are caused due to overhunting or consumption by the mortal races, whether intentionally or otherwise.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-CM52-37)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TFOSC-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Rt W-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Cont Mount-40)</sup>

Many extinctions are only regional, one example being with [cliff racers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cliff_Racer), who were driven to extinction on Vvardenfell,<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-SRJiub-41)</sup> but individuals continued to live elsewhere.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-SRMjoll-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-43)</sup> These regional extinctions can sometimes be undone, such as the case with the [welwa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welwa), who were hunted to extinction on [Summerset](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Summerset) but were eventually reintroduced due to the efforts of conservationists.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Rt W-39)</sup>
