# Walking Ways

— *[36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 6](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_6)* The Walking Ways are six interrelated means of achieving [Apotheosis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Apotheosis) (or attaining a [mantle](Lore_Mantling.md)),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36L6-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Girnalin-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MDC-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> allowing for a state of transcendence and freedom from all known laws and mortal limitations of [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) created by the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra), as well as the corruptions of the black sea of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> They are described as the formulae by which mortal individuals can return to the first brush of [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) and [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) and experience the pure possibility of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) first-hand, allowing them a state comparable to the divine power the Gods possessed prior to their Convention at the end of the Dawn Era.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36L6-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-36L18-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WWYDB-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Trial-7)</sup> The process of ascension by any Walking Way may destabilize both the Mundus and the Aurbis in relation to it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MDC-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WWYDB-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-ESOYoutube Guild Summit-8)</sup> However, the result of this transcendence is uniquely free of the disaster that troubled the Gods, allowing the individuals who achieve it to remain in the Mundus and exercise their power without threatening the physical make-up of the mortal realm or destabilizing its linear chronology with their presence.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-9)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup>

All Walking Ways are said to be protean values or models possessing an innate and constant aspect of transformation. As such, they all fall under the [Psijic Endeavor](Lore_Psijic_Endeavor.md),<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> each of them representing one of the six fundamental ways that Change is wrought on Nirn.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Girnalin-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> The first goal of the Psijic Endeavor (and thus the purpose of all Walking Ways) is to attain the [Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md), which refers to attaining Divinity on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). The second goal is what to do [after](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amaranth), once transcendence is reached.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-36L35-10)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-36L37-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SONGOM-12)</sup> In ancient times, the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) learned the rules of the Endeavor from [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TCO-14)</sup> These Daedra charged them with transcending the Aedra that created them. In Morrowind, the Psijic Endeavor is seen as being related to [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), the Missing God.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Vo F-15)</sup>

## First Walking Way - The Numidium
*Ah. I will tell you the truth, because you will believe none of it. The Brass God is [Anumidum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumidium), the Prime Gestalt. He is also called the divine skin. He was meant to be used many times by our kind to transcend the [Gray Maybe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gray_Maybe).*
—Xal, a human [Maruhkati](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maruhkati_Selective)<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-13)</sup> The First Walking Way is described by Vivec as the "Wrong Walking Path" followed by [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-36L15-16)</sup> It is tied to [the Divine Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Blight) (associated with the ash of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md)), its distributors Akulakhan and Numidium, and by extension the source of their Divine power, the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-36L15-16)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-36L36-17)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-NARM-18)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Concept-19)</sup> Any mortal may attain Apotheosis by these means. [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) is one infamous example of such, as he successfully used a facsimile of the Heart, the [Mantella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantella), and the 'Second Numidian Effect' it produced to join the ranks of the Gods as [the Revenant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancer%27s_Moon).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOTE-20)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ko WDF-21)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WWYDB-6)</sup>

Various heretical cults of the Empire, including remnants of the Marukhati Selectives, are especially reverent of this Walking Way. The Marukhati view the Numidium as a great means of transcendence, used again and again to surpass the limitations of the [Gray Maybe](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-13)</sup> According to some sources, [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) attained Apotheosis by this Walking Way.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AH-22)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Second Walking Way - The Endeavor
*I give you an ancient road tempered by the second walking way. Your hands must be huge to wield any sword the size of an ancient road, and yet he who is of right stature may irritate the sun with only a stick*
— *[36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 23](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_23)* The Second Walking Way is described by Vivec<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> as a "sword the size of an ancient road", which can be thought of as the long journey an enemy puts before you, yet which you walk anyway.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-36L23-23)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MKP-24)</sup> It is tied to the trials and adventures of a [Hero](Lore_Hero.md), which test their character and cut them into better shapes, allowing them to attain Apotheosis.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-36L23-23)</sup> According to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) and, by extension, [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) himself are a direct demonstration of this Walking Way.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-36L11-25)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-36L23-23)</sup>

This Walking Way is held in special esteem by [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), harkening back to the Exodus by Veloth and his establishment of the Psijic Endeavor.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-36L23-23)</sup> It is said [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) showed Veloth and his followers the right way to Walk to achieve an Exodus through pain.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FETE-26)</sup> According to some sources, [Hjalti Early-Beard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hjalti_Early-Beard) attained Apotheosis by this Walking Way.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Third Walking Way - The Prolix Tower
*To sing a law, and then Speak into the heart of that law, convincing it of a subtle error and how it must change its own Self. That is how Nature's course—its own Sea—is shaped and reshaped over time. Such changes can affect the whole of Mundus.*
—Spinner [Girnalin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Girnalin)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Girnalin-2)</sup> ** The Third Walking Way is described as Word of Action, exploring hysteria without fear by means of speech and writing.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-36L27-27)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Girnalin-2)</sup> It is tied to manipulation of the [Earthbones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Earthbones) that dictate the natural laws and rules of the Aurbis by various magical forms of speech and writing,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Wa P-28)</sup> including [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), [Spinning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spinners), [the Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md), and [Tonal Architecture](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tonal_Architecture).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-36L27-27)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Girnalin-2)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-AE4TET-29)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> Those who attain Apotheosis this way are said to be capable of substituting natural laws for one another without any ill-effect, even if those laws conflict with each other.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-36L27-27)</sup>

The tenets of this Way are held in particular esteem by the Spinners of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Girnalin-2)</sup> According to some sources, [Ysmir](Lore_Ysmir.md) attained Apotheosis by this Walking Way.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> This may refer to the fact that the title of Ysmir has historically been given by the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), practitioners of the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md), who name Ysmir while they withstand their voices.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Vo F-15)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Arngeir-30)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AH-22)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-31)</sup>

## Fourth Walking Way - CHIM
*CHIM. Those who know it can reshape the land. Witness [the home](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) of the [Red King](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) [Once Jungled](Lore_Cyrodiil.md#Home_of_the_Red_King_Once_Jungled).*
— *[Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes, Book Three](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries_3)* The Fourth Walking Way is called [CHIM](Lore_CHIM.md), or the steps of the dead,<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Nu Hat-32)</sup> and is described as a proper comprehension of the virtues of mortality through willingly submitting oneself to pain.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-36L12-33)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-36L31-34)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-36L32-35)</sup> It is characterized by Vivec as a painful and violent process, likened to the trauma inflicted by a mace or a hammer,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-36L31-34)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-36L32-35)</sup> which entails reaching an understanding about the truth of the universe and one's place within it in a manner similar to a hostage savoring the wound received "as a punishment for being mortal".<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-36L32-35)</sup> The subsequent release from torment leads to a simultaneous comprehension of the full scope of existence, allowing one to achieve Apotheosis, though at risk of losing their individuality to the pleasure of annihilation.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-36L31-34)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-36L32-35)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup>

This truth is encapsulated in the [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md) sigil of CHIM, described as the secret syllable of royalty and a representation of the Tower.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-36L12-33)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> One who learns CHIM this way is said to not only observe the Tower, but reside within it, "writing with black hands" in a manner reminiscent of Mephala, the origin of sex and murder who wields both theory and terror in her hands.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-36L11-25)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-36L31-34)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> The Warrior-Poet [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) claims to have learned the secret syllable from [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-36L12-33)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-36L32-35)</sup> although some sources claim that the revelation leading to his divinity came from interacting with the Heart of Lorkhan itself, where his hands were "burnt black" by its divine energies.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-36L31-34)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Trial-7)</sup>

This Way is held in high esteem within certain Daedric Cults, such as the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MDC-3)</sup> or the followers of Mephala and Boethiah in Morrowind.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-36L11-25)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-36L32-35)</sup> A lesser Daedra by the name of Ezhkel sought out what they believed was CHIM as a way of achieving transcendence.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-36)</sup> According to some sources, [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) attained Apotheosis by this Walking Way.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MDC-3)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Heimskr-37)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-TMHT-38)</sup><sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Nu Hat-32)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Fifth Walking Way - The Enantiomorph
*And Talos said to the Arctus, "Let us join as one to fortify this throne, this land, these people, each one glorious under heaven!"*
— [The Prophet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Prophet)<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Prophet-39)</sup> The Fifth Walking Way is described as the lover who is bereft of a double, which Vivec states is originally proven by Anu and his double, [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-36L35-10)</sup> An Enantiomorph is an existence wherein two individuals (The King or Rebel), due to a catalyst coveted by both (The Lover), become parts of a merged dichotomy, and thus are metaphysically interchangeable.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-36L11-25)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-36L35-10)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MKP-24)</sup><sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-PGE2Con-40)</sup> Any Enantiomorph as a whole is based upon the original framework of Anu and Padomay, as established within the [Anuad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuad) and contemporary theologies.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-36L35-10)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TAA-41)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MKP-24)</sup> The victor of the Enantiomorph reaches Apotheosis, and is decided by a Witness who is blinded or maimed as part of the ensuing clash.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-36L11-25)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MKP-24)</sup><sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-PGE2Con-40)</sup> Vivec claims the Nerevarine and Dagoth Ur were an Enantiomorph,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-36L11-25)</sup> of which the Nerevarine was eventually decided the victor, with Vivec serving as the witness.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-MWEnd Quest-42)</sup> Vivec also claims to have been the victor within his own Enantiomorph with Mephala, wherein his Mother was the blinded Witness.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-36L2-43)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36L29-44)</sup>

The Enantiomorph is held in esteem by various cults within Cyrodiil, including remnants of the Marukhati Selectives and the teachings of a man only known as "the Prophet". The most famous example of the Enantiomorph is Talos and [Zurin Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zurin_Arctus).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-36L19-45)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-13)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Prophet-39)</sup> According to a member of the Marukhati, their [oversoul](Lore_Oversoul.md) was known to the world as "Tiber Septim".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-13)</sup> Notably, Zurin also had a shared title with Wulfharth, the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AH-22)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-DFWho-46)</sup> According to some sources, the ultimate victor of the Enantiomorph was Zurin Arctus,<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-UDF-47)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AH-22)</sup> who attained Apotheosis by this Walking Way.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Sixth Walking Way - The Scarab
*Each of the aspects of the ALMSIVI then rose up together, combining as one, and showed the world the sixth path. Ayem took from the star its fire, Seht took from it its mystery, and Vehk took from it its feet, which had been constructed before the gift of Molag Bal and destroyed in the manner of truth: by a great hammering. When the soul of the Dwemer could walk no more, they were removed from this world.*
— *[36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 36](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_36)* The Sixth Walking Way has been referred to as the [Scarab](Lore_Scarab.md),<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-36L30-48)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36L29-44)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> and is of special interest to contemporary astrolothurges (those who study the meaning of stars).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-36L30-48)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Observatory-49)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-VT-4)</sup> It has been tied to a demonstration by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), who joined as one to destroy the [soul of the Dwemer](Lore_Numidium.md), by stealing the traits of the Walking Star: its fire, its mystery, and its feet.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-36L36-17)</sup> In an account of the legend of Boethiah and [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) and his [followers](Lore_Orc.md) were balled into a singular sphere as "the scarab gathers dung".<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FETE-26)</sup> The Scarab as a name and symbol has been directly ascribed to Lorkhan.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-36L10-50)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MDC-3)</sup>

Scarab symbology had much prominence in the cultures of the Dwemer and Dunmer in particular.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-51)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Observatory-49)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-CM52R-52)</sup><sup>[[UOL 9]](#cite_note-Concept Dw-53)</sup> According to some sources, Septim, referring to either the [Emperor](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) or the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), or all of the previous, attained Apotheosis by this Walking Way.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Given the demonstration by the Tribunal of many joining as one,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-36L36-17)</sup> this may refer to the Oversoul of Emperors, of which Tiber Septim embodied.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po M-13)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WWYDB-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 10]](#cite_note-54)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MKP-24)</sup>

- *[Terran's Notes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Terran%27s_Notes)* describe a vision in which a ritual taking place at [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) causes it to break into "the Number of Walking Ways". Terran then wonders why there are Eight. The description closely resembles what was said to have occurred during the ritual performed by the Marukhati Selectives, when the [Staff of Towers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff_of_Towers) was split into eight fragments. As the number eight correlates with the Wheel, which possesses eight 'spokes' in the form of the Towers, it is possible that Sister Terran was seeing a vision of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) preventing an event similar to the Middle Dawn.
- The unofficial text *"[Loveletter From the Fifth Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Loveletter_From_the_Fifth_Era)"*, written by Michael Kirkbride, describes the Walking Ways plainly as such: "The Numidium. The Endeavor. The Prolix Tower. CHIM. The Enantiomorph. The Scarab that Transforms into the New Man." While not typically considered a valid source, this instance provides important context for the implications present in the series itself.
