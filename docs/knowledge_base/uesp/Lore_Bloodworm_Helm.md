# Bloodworm Helm

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg) The Bloodworm Helm (Oblivion Remastered) The Bloodworm Helm is the [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) crown of the famed [lich](Lore_Lich.md) [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> who magically constructed it out of [trollbone](Lore_Troll.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRHOBB-2)</sup> Although beautiful to behold, many find the [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) repulsive due to its morbid nature.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TRTA-3)</sup> The helm grants the wearer greater skill at [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBBWM-4)</sup> as well as the ability to turn the undead,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWBWM-5)</sup> drain the essence of enemies, and summon a [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) minion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> If the wearer is undead, their attacks are also made more powerful.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRBWM-6)</sup>

Mannimarco left the helm behind when he [ascended to godhood](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md) during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TRTA-3)</sup>

## History
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWYB-7)</sup>. At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-8)</sup><sup>:241</sup> which listed the Bloodworm Helm among artifacts of "unimaginable significance." However, during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the book was titled *[Tamrielic Artifacts](Lore_Tamrielic_Artifacts.md)* and did not include the Helm,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TA-9)</sup> which was added in later editions.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> Fyr himself hoped to utilize Yagrum's findings in his search for the Helm and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWFR-10)</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) the helm had come into the possession of a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) necromancer named Crazy Batou, who had established a lair in the Maren Ancestral Tomb in the [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Batou was killed by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), who then sold the helm to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md). Torasa put the helm on display in the [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) Museum of Artifacts.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TRTM-12)</sup> It was later obtained by the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and transported to the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for safekeeping.

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Mannimarco returned to the affairs of mortals and attempted to destroy the Mages Guild. The [Council of Mages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Council_of_Mages) dissolved in the ensuing attacks on the guild, and Master-Wizard [Irlav Jarol](Lore_Irlav_Jarol.md) took the helm to the abandoned Fort Teleman in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) with a group of researchers to study the artifact in an effort to better understand how Mannimarco could be stopped. The expedition went against the advice of Arch-Mage [Hannibal Traven](Lore_Hannibal_Traven.md), who feared for the group's safety. Traven was proven correct when Irlav was killed by an alliance between [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBPG-13)</sup><sup>:162</sup> A member of the Mages Guild was sent by Traven to recover the helm, who then took the helm from Irlav's corpse. It was then brought back to the University and returned to a secure place in the Arch-Mage's Tower.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBTBH-14)</sup>

Sometime in the Fourth Era, a necromancer was collecting items for his lair that ranged from trinkets to artifacts that fit the morbid and macabre. He had become fixated with obtaining the artifacts of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md); the Bloodworm Helm and the [Staff of Worms](Lore_Staff_of_Worms.md). An apprentice was tasked with retrieving the former, to which they brought the [Helm of Oreyn Bearclaw](Lore_Helm_of_Oreyn_Bearclaw.md), confusing one skull helm for another. He recruited a new apprentice named Naara after this ordeal, who then attempted to slay him and take the artifacts he had found for her own ends. This would be her downfall, for the apprentice was trapped within the room which had been turned into a puzzle. Should Naara want to take the artifacts, she would have to earn them by using them to figure out the puzzle to be freed from the trap.

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) located a necromancer's lair at Gallows Hall and completed the puzzles set out by the place's former owner, thus earning the right to claim Mannimarco's artifacts and the lair itself.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRDD-15)</sup>
