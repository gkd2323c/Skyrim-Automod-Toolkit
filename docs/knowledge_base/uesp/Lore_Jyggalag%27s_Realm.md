[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/LG-quest-The_Warrior_03.jpg/200px-LG-quest-The_Warrior_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Warrior_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-The_Warrior_03.jpg) Jyggalag and Dyus in the realm Jyggalag's realm was the realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) ruled over by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md). It was said to be a realm of perfect Order.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-1)</sup> It later became known as the **[Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md)**, the domain of the Mad God [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LGDyus-2)</sup>

The realm was home to the **Great Library of Jyggalag**, a vast collection of knowledge on all things mortal and [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) alike.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SIDyus-3)</sup> The knowledge was the result of a formulae of logical deductions that Jyggalag implemented.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SIDyus-3)</sup> [Dyus of Mytheria](Lore_Dyus.md) served as the keeper of the Great Library, eventually having his role expanded to the chamberlain of the realm, which involved performing all the mundane tasks that came with running a realm or those that Jyggalag found distasteful. Dyus' title suggests he originated from a place called **Mytheria**, though due to his loss of memory, he is unsure whether Mytheria is even a real place or not.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SIDyus-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IWTDSI-4)</sup>

## History
Jyggalag's realm expanded throughout Oblivion further with each passing era, increasing the Prince of Order's influence.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LGDyus-2)</sup> The other Daedric Princes, fearful of his power, cursed Jyggalag to live as the thing he despised most, [Madness](Lore_Sheogorath.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LGDyus-2)</sup> Just as Jyggalag himself changed, so too did his realm: it became a realm of madness, known as the Shivering Isles.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LGDyus-2)</sup>

A cycle known as the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) allowed Jyggalag to reassume his true form once an era and retake his realm but at the conclusion of the Greymarch, he was always transformed back into Sheogorath.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-1)</sup> This cycle was finally broken by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) when they mantled Sheogorath and defeated Jyggalag in direct combat.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SIEnd-5)</sup> This freed Jyggalag to wander the voids of Oblivion again.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIJyggalag-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IWTDSI-4)</sup>

## Notes
- In a forum post from 2006, [Douglas Goodall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Douglas_Goodall) briefly mentioned Jyggalag's realm while discussing [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md)'s claims that [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) was a Daedric realm. After doubting the accuracy of Mankar's beliefs, Goodall ended his discussion on that topic with the line, *"Besides, everyone knows that Nirn is really Jyggalag's realm..."*<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DGP-6)</sup> This comment was likely made in jest, as Dyus claims to have never visited [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IWTDSI-4)</sup>
