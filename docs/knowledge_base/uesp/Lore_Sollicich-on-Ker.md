## History

### Second Era
Much of Sollicich-on-Ker's history is known from *[Galerion the Mystic](Lore_Galerion_the_Mystic.md)*, a biography of Archmagister [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) written by [Asgrim Kolsgreg](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Asgrim_Kolsgreg). Sollicich-on-Ker was an estate owned by a minor nobleman, [Lord Gyrnasse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gyrnasse), who owned various serfs. Among these servants was Trechtus, who would one day be known more commonly as Vanus Galerion, founder of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Galerion Mystic-1)</sup>

Lord Gyrnasse was active during the early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He was very strict and forbade his serfs from learning, claiming that literate serfs were an abomination and a danger to everyone around them. When the smugglers that brought Trechtus books were found, they were promptly executed and Trechtus' father was kept hanging for weeks amidst the hottest summer Sollicich-on-Ker had seen in years. Three months later, Trechtus escaped Lord Gyrnasse's Keep and made it to Alinor, which was on the other side of the island. It was there that he met Heliand, who eventually took him to the island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) and the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Galerion Mystic-1)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_nb1) | Sollicich-on-Ker seems to have been inspired by English naming custom, seen on real world town names such as [Burton-on-Trent](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burton-on-Trent) (the name of a town upon the [River Trent](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_Trent) in [England](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/England)), [Southwick-on-Wear](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwick,_Sunderland), or [Newcastle-on-Clun](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newcastle,_Shropshire). [Bodrum-on-Prystasus](Lore_Bodrum.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) shares this trait, with Bodrum being a village on the [Prytasus River](Lore_Pryai_River.md). This would indicate that Sollicich-on-Ker is located on a **Ker River**. |
