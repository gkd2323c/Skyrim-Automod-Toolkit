[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/SR-race-Wood_Elf.jpg/200px-SR-race-Wood_Elf.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-race-Wood_Elf.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-race-Wood_Elf.jpg) A compilation of the preset Wood Elf faces available in Skyrim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/SR-concept-6877590997.jpg/200px-SR-concept-6877590997.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877590997.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877590997.jpg) Female concept art [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/SR-concept-6877591181.jpg/200px-SR-concept-6877591181.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877591181.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877591181.jpg) Male concept art The ** Wood Elves** are the Elven clan-folk of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), a forested province in southwestern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). In the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), they are often referred to as Wood Elves, but **Bosmer**, **Boiche**, or **the Tree-Sap people** is what they call themselves. Wood Elves rejected the stiff, formal traditions of [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) high culture, preferring a more romantic, simple existence in harmony with the land and its wild beauty and creatures. They are relatively nimble and quick in body compared to their more "civilized" [Altmeri](Lore_High_Elf.md) cousins (who often look down upon the Wood Elves as unruly and naive). Their agility makes them well-suited as scouts and thieves. However, they are also a quick-witted folk, and many pursue successful careers in scholarly pursuits or trading. Wood Elves live two to three times as long as humans; with a 200-year-old Wood Elf being old and a 300-year-old Wood Elf being very, very old. Though they are considered less influential than some of their Elven brethren, the Wood Elves are also relatively prone to producing offspring. As a result, they outnumber all other [mer](Lore_Mer.md) on Tamriel.

The best archers in all of Tamriel, the Wood Elves snatch and fire arrows in one continuous motion; they are even rumored to have invented the bow. They have many natural and unique abilities; notably, they can command simple-minded creatures and have a nearly chameleon-like ability to hide in forested areas. Many in the forests of Valenwood follow the tenets of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). These "Green Pact Bosmer" are religiously carnivorous and cannibalistic, and do not harm the vegetation of Valenwood, though they are not averse to using wooden or plant-derived products created by others.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Wood_Elf.md).

## In-Game Description
*The clanfolk of the Western [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) forests, also known as "Bosmer." Wood elves make good scouts and thieves, and there are no finer archers in all of Tamriel. They have natural resistances to both poisons and diseases. They can Command Animals to fight for them.*

## Traits
- *Resist Disease and Poison* ability (Form ID `000aa025`): Your Wood Elf blood gives you 50% resistance to [poison](Skyrim_Resist_Poison.md) and [disease](Skyrim_Resist_Disease.md), constant
- *Command Animal* greater power (Form ID `000e40cf`): Make an animal an ally for 60 seconds, once per day - This power has magnitude 99 pts and area of effect 75 ft
- *Resist Fatigue*<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md)</sup> (Form ID `FE00088f`: Your Wood Elf blood gives you 25% resistance to [fatigue](Skyrim_Fatigue.md).
- Height: 0.98 (M), 1 (F)

## Gameplay
The nimble Wood Elves are strongly suited for thief characters and archers. Their talent for alchemy allows them to make poisons to help their archery even more and to create potions that could help their thievery.

In combat, Wood Elves are heavily focused on archery, in which they are generally understood to be the single best among the Tamriel races. They start out with a nice bonus in Archery and are well-suited to use it. They are good in sneaking, so they can often get a sneak attack bonus.

Particularly in the early levels, the animal ally ability can be somewhat useful. Low combat skills can render some creatures (particularly bears) extremely dangerous to low level characters, especially melee focused players. This ability also overrides a Spriggan's ability to control animals, which can potentially turn a fight in the player's favor. Additionally, having an animal to distract dragons early on can be particularly helpful.

This benefit does diminish as the game goes on, but remains extremely useful in some cases, most notably controlling Mammoths against Giants, and controlling Chaurus against Falmer.

In combat, Wood Elves may be taunted with "*Should have stayed in your precious forests, Elf!*"

## Notes
