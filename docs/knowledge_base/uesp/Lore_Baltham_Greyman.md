*The old women who run the Mages Guild have no sense of adventure, take no risks, dare nothing, and learn nothing. I have never lifted a finger against them, except to repudiate their control. Yet, simply because I refuse to abide by their rules, the Mages Guild would slay me! Why do you give them your blind obedience, killing on command like a hired assassin? Join me and we will defy them all!*
—Baltham Greyman<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FS1-1)</sup> Baltham Greyman was an influential [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) scholar and former member of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BG-2)</sup> known for his unsanctioned experiments and open disdain for the institution. He led a merry band of Guild defiers,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FS1-1)</sup> carried out a daring theft from the [Direnni Tower](Lore_Direnni_Tower.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FS2-3)</sup> and was noted for his work in the fields of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FS2-3)</sup> and [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md) magic.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Spell-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/DF-quest-Baltham_Greyman.png/200px-DF-quest-Baltham_Greyman.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-Baltham_Greyman.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-Baltham_Greyman.png) Baltham Greyman Little is known of Baltham Greyman's past. He was a pupil of a guildmagister from one of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) chapters of the Mages Guild. Although highly talented and full of promise, he lacked loyalty to the Guild's ancient principles, a failing that ultimately led to his expulsion. He believed the institution was fundamentally flawed, lacking a sense of adventure, unwilling to take risks, daring nothing, and learning nothing. Baltham persisted in his unsanctioned research, which provoked the anger of his former master and led to an order for his execution sometime between [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405) and [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417). When [an agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) hired by the Guild was sent to kill him, the agent instead betrayed the Guild and joined Greyman's band of Guild defiers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FS1-1)</sup>

Afterward, Baltham contacted his newfound ally first by [raven](Lore_Raven.md) and later through an [atronach](Lore_Atronach.md) servant, proposing an expedition to the [Direnni Tower](Lore_Direnni_Tower.md) on the [Isle of Balfiera](Lore_Isle_of_Balfiera.md), a site he believed to be rich in arcane wonders. Together they explored the tower and seized the relic he sought there.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FS2-3)</sup> Later that same year, Greyman orchestrated a raid on a Mages Guild chapter to obtain another artifact required for his experiments. Despite his ban, he retained sympathizers within the Guild, enabling him to arrange the theft. With the item secured, he resumed his forbidden research.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FS3-5)</sup>

Other reports suggest that he perished before he could set his plans into motion,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FS1-1)</sup> or that he was ultimately betrayed by his fellow conspirator.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FS2-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FS3-5)</sup>

Baltham's Insight, a [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md) [spell](Lore_Spells.md) designed to detect life was named after him. Tomes detailing its use were widespread by [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Spell-4)</sup>
