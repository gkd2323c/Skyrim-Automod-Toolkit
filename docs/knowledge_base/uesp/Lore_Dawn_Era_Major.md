**Note:** The [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), also known as the **Dawn Age**, the **Beginning Times**, or the **Chaos Times**, was a period during which time followed an incomprehensible nonlinear path and the very laws of nature remained unset, making a timeline an artificial fabrication. A conflict was simultaneously a mere ideological difference of opinion and a manifest war. As noted in *[The Lunar Lorkhan](Lore_The_Lunar_Lorkhan.md)*, this "War of Manifest Metaphors" renders the era mostly incapable of supporting a narrative. Since fixed dates during this time are unknowable, there is no specific nomenclature for years within the Dawn Era. Further, some events, such as the [Velothi](Lore_Veloth.md) dissident movement, are attributed to the Dawn Era by some sources and to the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) by others. In later eras, in various areas and over varying periods of time, people have experienced a refrain of the chaos of the Dawn, which is known as a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md).

The Beginning

- Though the details vary with each culture and religion, all legends of the beginning of existence which have been passed down speak of two entities who appeared in the [Void](Lore_Void.md) and soon found themselves in conflict. [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) legend says Satak, the Snake who came Before, eventually merged with Akel, its "Hungry Stomach", to create [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), the Yokudan God of Everything. Satakal is considered a fusion of the concepts of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md), [elven](Lore_Mer.md) personifications of the primordial forces of stasis and change respectively. Other dichotomies include Ak-El, [Anuiel](Lore_Anuiel.md) - [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), and [Ahnurr](Lore_Ahnurr.md) - [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md). Even the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md) worshipped by ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) faced a twin force, [the Adversary](Lore_The_Adversary.md). Regardless of the nomenclature, all seem to represent the dichotomy between existence and nothingness, order and chaos, light and dark.

Creation

- The [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md), the Original Spirits, crystallize in the Beginning Place, springing forth from the two primordial forces. Legend speaks of twelve worlds birthed into existence where life flourished. The interplay of the light and the dark becomes creation - known variously as [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md), Gray Maybe, Arena Supermundus, and Satakal. When the "First God" [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) forms, time begins, and it becomes easier for some spirits to achieve structure and become aware of themselves as beings with a past and a future. Their constant flux and interplay create more spirits. The strongest of the recognizable spirits crystallize: [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md), [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), etc. Others remain as concepts, ideas, or emotions, their personalities slowly congealing.
- [The Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md), one of the creation myths, speaks of a third primordial force, a female principle known as [Nir](Lore_Nir.md), which first gave birth to the cosmos before expiring. The [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) creation myth similarly associates the beginning of creation with maternal death.

[Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s Plan

- A [Padomaic](Lore_Padomay.md) being, a barely formed urge known as Lorkhan (among many other names), enters every aspect of [Anuiel](Lore_Anuiel.md) and outlines his vision for the creation of a mortal realm, [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), the hub of the Wheel, the center of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). Its stated purpose is to be the soul of the Aurbis, a place where aspects of aspects could [self-reflect](Lore_CHIM.md) and thereby reach immortality, equaling or even surpassing those who made them.
- By some accounts, [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) is the first to agree to Lorkhan's plan, and provides space for its creation in the [Void](Lore_Void.md).
- To the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), "Khenarthi" is instead the one who requests the birth of "Lorkhaj", and it is Lorkhaj who "makes a place for children" to be born.
- In the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) tradition, [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md) creates [Sep](Lore_Sep.md), the Second Serpent, from leftover scraps of [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md)'s skins in order to help him save lesser spirits. Driven insane by [hunger](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) and fed up with helping, Sep tricks many spirits into pursuing a shortcut to the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md).

The Great Construction

- Many [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md), the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md), join together to achieve [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s vision by contributing their own power. [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md) serves as the new world's architect. When told he will be the king of this realm, even [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) joins the project. A collection of prominent [Padomaic](Lore_Padomay.md) spirits reject the idea and decide to create [worlds within themselves](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md), using only their own divinities, over which they could have total control. They are now known as [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md).

Worlds collide — [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is conceived

- As the ancient forces continued their struggle, the twelve worlds are eventually shattered and then combined into one - Nirn. The only survivors are the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) (the ancestors of [mer](Lore_Mer.md) and [man](Lore_Man.md)) and the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). Some stories attribute the creation of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to this time.

[Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) regrets

- Most of the creators of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) die or are crippled by their sacrifices. The mortal plane is highly magical and dangerous. As the Aedra walked, the physical make-up of the mortal plane and even the timeless continuity of existence itself became unstable. They exist as shadows of their former selves. The new land is a chaotic place where time follows no clear path and decay is constant. Hatred and resentment of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) grows.
- [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) begs [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) to take them back, but Anu had filled their places with something else. [Anuiel](Lore_Anuiel.md) grants Auri-El his [bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) and [shield](Lore_Auriel%27s_Shield.md) to save the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) from Lorkhan's hordes of [men](Lore_Man.md). Auri-El establishes the first kingdoms of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), [Altmora](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Old Ehlnofey](Lore_Old_Ehlnofey.md).
- In the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiiti.md) tradition, "Lorkhaj" tricks his siblings into entering the place for children he had ostensibly made for the children of "[Nirni](Lore_Nirn.md)" and traps them there.
- In the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) tradition, [Sep](Lore_Sep.md) has spirits assemble and inhabit a ball of [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md)'s dead past skins, but the spirits there begin to die, as the ball is too far away from the real world of Satakal and the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md) are too far away to reach anymore.

Escape — [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md) leaves [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md)

- Magnus and those who would follow him, the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) (the majority of the [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md)), abandon [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). They flee, ripping holes in the fabric of the universe in their departure to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). The new sun and stars allow an influx of [magic](Lore_Magic.md). By some accounts, Magnus decided to terminate the project.

Battles of Alduin and Lorkhan

- According to [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) legend, [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) and [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) are believed to have fought at the beginning of time.
- According to a pre-ri'Datta text of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), Alkhan the Scaled Prince was slain by [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md) and his companions. Though, due to the immortal nature of Alkhan, he will return in time.

The Shattering of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)

- Lorkhan is separated from his [divine center](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md). Stories differ on whether this is voluntary. [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), the greatest knight of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), apparently knocks Lorkhan down in front of his army, reaches in "with more than hands", and takes his Heart, undoing the God of Mortals.
- In the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) tradition, [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md) squashes [Sep](Lore_Sep.md) with a big stick as punishment for leading so many spirits astray, causing his hunger to fall out.

The First Secret

- According to [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) legend, "[Azurah](Lore_Azura.md)" speaks the First Secret to [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md), and they part, allowing her to transform the suffering forest people into Khajiit. "[Y'ffer](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)" hears the First Secret and uses it to transform his own group of forest people into [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md).

The Sundering of [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md)

- [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmeri.md) society begins to fracture. Some, like the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), listen to [tainted et'Ada](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), and others, like the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), soil "Time's line" by taking [Mannish](Lore_Man.md) wives. Then the hordes of Men under [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) (or [Shor](Lore_Shor.md)) rebel. They take over [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), chase the forces of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) south to [Old Ehlnofey](Lore_Old_Ehlnofey.md), and shatter that land into many.

War of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md)

- [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), once one large landmass with interlocking seas, is sundered by war between [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), or Old Ehlnofey, and "the Wanderers". Old Ehlnofey is ruined, and the remnants of the Wanderers are left in the other landmasses - modern day [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), and [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). Most of the lands of the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) are destroyed, with only modern-day [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) remaining.

The [Velothi](Lore_Veloth.md) dissident movement — The Transformation of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md)

- In the final days of the Dawn, the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) deems the culture of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) to be decadent and attempts to lead fellow [mer](Lore_Mer.md) to a new land. Trinimac and his followers attempt to halt this schism. Trinimac is then consumed by [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of deceit, only to return after the Dawn as [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). Veloth's people become the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and begin their migration east to the region known in later eras as [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Trinimac's followers become the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and leave Summerset for the northern region of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), where the original 13 clans establish their home.

[Convention](Lore_Convention.md) — The end of the Dawn

- When [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md) departs, and as the world congeals into reality, the [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md) make a great Tower to discuss how best to proceed with [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). This is the Ur-Tower, the first unassailable spike of reality in the Dawn and one of the axes of creation itself (it is now known as the [Direnni Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md), or Adamantine Tower, on the isle of [Balfiera](Lore_Balfiera.md) in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md)). At this Convention, the physical, temporal, spiritual, and magical elements of Nirn are set.
- [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) is either condemned to exile in the mortal realms, or killed in battle leading the armies of Men against Auriel and the Aldmer. His [Heart](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md), impervious to destruction, is cast from the Tower or fastened to an arrow and shot into [the sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), forming a [volcano](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) where it lands, and Lorkhan is left to wander the creation of the et'Ada. As the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) say, he "slinks around in a dead skin".
- The development of mortals now follows slow, comprehensible paths. The progeny of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) slowly diversify and find their way back to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), which for many of the earliest years of creation is thought to have only been inhabited by the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). The people of Tamriel become the mer (elves): The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) (Deep Ones), the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) (Changed Ones), the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) (Green or Forest Ones), and the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) (Elder or High Ones). The fracturing of the Aldmer along cultural and racial lines is the Sundering of [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md). The continent no longer exists in any recognizable form, assuming it ever did. The other Ehlnofey become the beasts and the [humans](Lore_Man.md) of Nirn, who will eventually split into racial groups, including the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md), [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), and Tamrielic Aboriginals, as well as the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md).
- The Tower remains behind even as some of the gods disappear back into [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). The [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) sacrifice themselves into other forms so that they can stay in and salvage [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). The substance of the world forms from their transfigurations, and parent spirits give way to the succeeding mortal races. Some, like [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), ascend in full observance of their followers so that they might learn the steps to escape [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). [Magic](Lore_Magic.md) ebbs along with this divine exodus, and the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) stabilizes.
- [Linear elven history begins](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).
