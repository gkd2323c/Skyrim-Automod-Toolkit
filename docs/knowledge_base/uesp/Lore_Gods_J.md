#### Jhim Sei
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/DF-place-Shrines.png/200px-DF-place-Shrines.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Shrines.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Shrines.png) Shrines (Daggerfall) Jhim Sei is an unknown deity that has a presence in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), primarily in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The nature of Jhim Sei is unknown, although their sphere of influence may revolve around [music](Lore_Music.md), since he is compared to [Jephre](Lore_Jephre.md), the Elven God of Song and Forest in the historical-fiction, [King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KEII-1)</sup> By [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), there are shrines dedicated to Jhim Sei across High Rock and even in remote places like the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFShrines-2)</sup>

#### Jhunal, Rune God
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/ON-card-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.png/200px-ON-card-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-card-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-card-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.png) Jhunal's owl mask, depicting Wise-Wing The [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) god of hermetic orders and knowledge, represented by an owl.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Contra-3)</sup> After falling out of favor with the rest of that pantheon, he became [Julianos](#Julianos) of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md). He is absent in modern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) mythology.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> [Wise-Wing](Lore_Wise-Wing.md) the Owl is associated in northern Tamriel with Jhunal, and is said to warn the unwary away from graveyards and witches' covens during the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CSS-5)</sup> [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) mentioned "JHUNAL" as one of the eight known worlds.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-36L3-6)</sup>

#### Jode, Big Moon God
[Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) god of the Big Moon. Also called [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) or [Mara](Lore_Mara.md)'s Tear. In [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) religion, Jode is only one aspect of the Lunar Lattice, or ja-Kha'jay.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup>

#### Jone, Little Moon God
[Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) god of the Little Moon. Also called [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md) or [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md)'s Sorrow. In [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) religion, Jone is only one aspect of the Lunar Lattice, or ja-Kha'jay.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup>

#### Julianos, God of Wisdom and Logic
[Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), [Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) God of Wisdom and Logic, is one of the [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) [Divines](Lore_Divines.md), governing the realms of literature, law, history, and contradiction. He is usually associated with [magic](Lore_Magic.md), and thus is often revered by wizards. [Jhunal](Lore_Jhunal.md), the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) father of [language](Lore_Ehlnofex.md) and mathematics, fell out of favor in the Nordic pantheon, after which he became Julianos of the Divines. Monastic orders founded by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and dedicated to Julianos are the [keepers](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). He is particularly venerated among many [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) and [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), but he also sees worship among the [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). As a Divine, he represents the virtue of learning, scholarship, and justice. His domains include sorcery, [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md). He is said to "incant the Damned Equation". His symbol is a triangle.

#### Jyggalag
[Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md), the **Prince of Order**, also known as **Gray Prince of Order**, is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) whose sphere represents logical order and deduction; his great library once contained a logical prediction of every detail of the world and of every action that would ever take place on [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) or [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), long before they actually happened. He was cursed in antiquity by the rest of the Daedric Princes to become the Prince of Madness, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). After the events of the last [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md), Sheogorath and Jyggalag are no longer the same Daedric Prince; however, they are still connected in other ways.

Jyggalag commands his own Daedra, the [Knights of Order](Lore_Knights_of_Order.md), which are spawned from obelisks summoned by his followers, the [Priests of Order](Lore_Priests_of_Order.md). Together, they constitute the [Forces of Order](Lore_Forces_of_Order.md). He is viewed as stern, bleak, and colorless, with him and his soldiers lacking originality.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Jyggalag.md).
