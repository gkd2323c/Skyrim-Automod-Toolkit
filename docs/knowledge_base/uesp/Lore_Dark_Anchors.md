# Dark Anchors

— [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) Dark Anchors were metaphysical devices which were used to weaken the barrier between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), allowing the two [realms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) to be merged. They were created in the [Black Forge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Forge) using an interstitial alloy known as [Tyranite Calx](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tyranite_Calx), and further infused with [soul gem fragments](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Gems) and trace other components.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RJ-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PDA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The chains were comprised of ebony-alloy cold-iron.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Dark Anchors were created for use in the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s attempted conquest of Nirn which occurred after the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Singular Dark Anchors are capable of pulling portions of Nirn into Coldharbour,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vanus BTS-6)</sup> and serve as [portals](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Portals) allowing [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to cross into Nirn,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODark Anchor-7)</sup> each weakens the barrier between worlds further.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PDA-2)</sup>

Around each Dark Anchor the [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) of Coldharbour seep into Nirn and, responding to the will of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), gradually transform the infested land to mirror Coldharbour itself.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GBAYQ-8)</sup> Objects in the process of being pulled in by a Dark Anchor can form into a distorted space known as a Shattered Void.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Terran-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Shattered Void-10)</sup>

## The Planemeld
[Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) ordered the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult) to place Dark Anchors throughout Tamriel, intending to utilize them to initiate the Planemeld.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:22</sup><sup>:38</sup><sup>:42</sup><sup>:43</sup> The Bosmer mage, [Deslandra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deslandra), wishing to free Tamriel from the tragedies caused by magic and by extension, Daedra, infiltrated the Worm Cult to uncover their plans for the Dark Anchors.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-12)</sup><sup>:19</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:22</sup> This led to her epiphany; the moment that Mannimarco would launch his Planemeld, Deslandra hoped to disrupt it by breaking the chains of that which linked all Dark Anchors, resulting in a massive shockwave that would forever sever the connection between Nirn and its sources of Magicka. However, Deslandra's plans to sever the flow of magicka and its influences on Mundus could have unintended consequences like cutting off communication with deities, but she felt the benefits outweighed the potential fallout.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-12)</sup><sup>:19</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-VWGazetteer-13)</sup><sup>:38</sup> Fearing this, she was slain by the Hero of the Outer Watch, inadvertently allowing Mannimarco to begin the Planemeld.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:48,50,52,54</sup>

### The Great Shackle
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-interior-The_Mooring.jpg/200px-ON-interior-The_Mooring.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Mooring.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Mooring.jpg) The Great Shackle The Great Shackle is the ultimate Dark Anchor; a massive device capable of completing the Planemeld on its own accord. It functions by casting metaphysical chains across the planar chaos to grip onto Nirn and pull it into Coldharbour.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vanus BTS-6)</sup> The Great Shackle is said to exist in multiple places at once, thus giving it the ability to bind disparate realms together.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Great Shackle-14)</sup> The flames used to forge the Great Shackle were fueled by a substance called the Charcoal of Remorse. Once cast, the device was quenched in the Blood of a Thousand Innocents.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

In addition to its main function of completing the Planemeld itself, the Great Shackle could also generate a Planar Vortex, likened to an "Oblivion straw" where the planes churn and meld together. The Vortex would continue to pull in Nirn until that world and its "very essence" were absorbed into Coldharbour, even in the event of the Great Shackle's destruction.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vanus BTS-6)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSo P-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOMeridia TFS-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Cadwell-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Laloriaran Dynar-19)</sup> The Planar Vortex and the Endless Stair from which it could be accessed and around which the Planemeld was centered, are said to have been designed for Molag Bal by the [Mad Architect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mad_Architect).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Endless Stair-20)</sup>

### Powering the Planemeld
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/ON-place-White-Gold_Tower.jpg/200px-ON-place-White-Gold_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-White-Gold_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-White-Gold_Tower.jpg) The Dark Anchor above the White-Gold Tower [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/ON-interior-White-Gold_Tower_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-White-Gold_Tower_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-White-Gold_Tower_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-White-Gold_Tower_02.jpg) The Shattered Void, the distorted space which formed within the White-Gold Tower due to the influence of the Dark Anchor It takes considerable power to form a connection to Nirn across the planar chaos of Oblivion. Due to the considerable power required, [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven) are useless. Powerful mortals, especially magic-users, provide the most effective and clean source of energy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PDA-2)</sup>

The [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) overseers of the Black Forge considered Vanus Galerion, the "quintessential mortal mage" as an incredibly effective power source, and believed that with the "power raging inside him", they could've finished the forging of the Great Shackle and powered the Shackle itself, thus completing the Planemeld in mere hours instead of months.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSo P-16)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Galerion Health-21)</sup>

Due to lacking access to a power source like Galerion, most of the power for Dark Anchors came from elemental [Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atronach). [Flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flame_Atronach) and [Frost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Atronach) Atronachs provided enough power, but volatile [Storm Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Storm_Atronach) provided the most, and thus a great number were used to launch the Dark Anchors and the Great Shackle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PDA-2)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSo P-16)</sup>

Creatures used to power the Planemeld through the Great Shackle were entrapped within a prism. Magicka conduits were used to drain the life force of imprisoned organisms. This energy was volatile; reversing the flow of energy through the magicka conduits would break the control locks and overload the prism, and could affect the creature within as well, on one occasion transforming a [Frost Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Atronach) into a [Flame Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flame_Atronach).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSo P-16)</sup> This destroyed the source of power, ultimately depriving the Planemeld of precious fuel.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vanus BTS-6)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOBTS-22)</sup>

The largest Dark Anchor opened above the skies of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), intent on pulling the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), a metaphysical focus so important it had the potential to pull all of Tamriel in with it once taken,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Tower-23)</sup> into Coldharbour.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Dolmen construction was interrupted in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains), as the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) of the newly-built [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) used the Black Worm Clan's ancient power stones to construct the city. The stones were stolen before the anchors even began to fall, forcing the cultists to start anew.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-OCL-25)</sup>

### Ending the Planemeld
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/ON-load-The_Mooring.jpg/200px-ON-load-The_Mooring.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Mooring.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Mooring.jpg) Galerion stands before the Great Shackle [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-interior-The_Planar_Vortex.jpg/200px-ON-interior-The_Planar_Vortex.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Planar_Vortex.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Planar_Vortex.jpg) The Planar Vortex As the Alliance War dragged on, Molag Bal's power over Tamriel continued to grow. Together with the Fighters Guild, the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) under Vanus Galerion and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) finally arranged for a meeting of the three alliance leaders on the island of [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) to discuss a potential counter-invasion of Coldharbour. Before an agreement could be reached, Molag Bal sent Daedra to the island to kill the alliance leaders. Although the attackers were repelled, the leaders quickly consented to a guild-led invasion, having seen Bal's influence firsthand.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

Though initially scattered across Coldharbour by the realm's defenses, the invasion force was gradually reassembled within the [Hollow City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hollow_City), a sanctuary of Meridia within Coldharbour which, encased by the power of the [Light of Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Light_of_Meridia) and pushed into Molag Bal's realm long ago by its Prince, served as a piece of Meridia's realm within the domain of the God of Schemes, and so could not be touched.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Groundskeeper-27)</sup> The last [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) king, [Laloriaran Dynar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar), who had been brought to Coldharbour alongside the Hollow City and been kept alive for centuries to be tortured by the Daedra, was also rescued from his prison in the Lightless Oubliette through the use of one of the Lights of Meridia, and went on to assume leadership of the force assembled in the Hollow City.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Dynar-28)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Laloriaran Dynar-19)</sup>

During the invasion circumstances that threatened to accelerate the completion of the Planemeld emerged. Vanus Galerion, who had been teleported outside the Black Forge of Coldharbour alone by the realm's defenses,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Groundskeeper-27)</sup> attempted to disable it to help stop the Planemeld. He was soon captured by the Daedra, who intended to use him as a power source, believing that as the "quintessential mortal mage", the power raging inside Galerion would allow them to complete the forging of the Great Shackle and power it with Galerion's magic, thus allowing them to complete the Planemeld in mere hours instead of months. To prevent this outcome Galerion separated his being into its constituent [essences](Lore_Souls.md) of magicka, health and stamina, which the Daedra could not use to complete the Planemeld individually. Despite this, the overseers of the Black Forge imprisoned the separated essences and used them to power the Black Forge itself, using them to craft Dark Anchors and components of the Great Shackle, to power their [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven) slaves so they'd keep working tirelessly, and to feed their [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) servants. The Vestige freed him, restored and reunited his essence, and journeyed with him, when he personally destroyed the Great Shackle that was intended to bind Coldharbour and Nirn together by sending metaphysical chains across the planar chaos to latch onto the world. With this task accomplished, Galerion also joined the force assembled in the Hollow City.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSo P-16)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Galerion Magicka-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Galerion Stamina-30)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Galerion Health-21)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Galerion-31)</sup>

Though the Great Shackle had been destroyed, it had generated a Planar Vortex prior to its deactivation. Likened to an "Oblivion straw" that would pull Nirn and "its very essence" into Coldharbour by itself if not dispersed.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSo P-16)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Galerion-31)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Cadwell-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Laloriaran Dynar-19)</sup>

The guild forces, the "Army of Meridia", crossed the Chasm of Coldharbour but were met by the vampires of the Orchard which were controlled by one of Molag Bal's Harvest Hearts. Vanus proposed enacting a ritual that would destroy the Heart and target the [blood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blood_Magic) of all vampires connected to it, rendering them mindless and allowing the guilds to pass through. The vampire leader Virgar proposed using a method that would free the vampires from the Heart instead, allowing them to become allied to the guild forces.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Galerion-31)</sup> It is unknown what the Vestige ultimately chose, but the vampires were severed from Molag Bal's control, allowing the guilds to enter the Reaver Citadel, which they captured to gain access to the Endless Stair, to which they were granted access by the restored [Foolkillers Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Foolkillers_Clan).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Galerion-31)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Harvest-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Citadel-34)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Final_Assault_02.jpg) Molag Bal battles Meridia in the Planar Vortex [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b3/ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_09.jpg/200px-ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_09.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_09.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-God_of_Schemes_09.jpg) The Vestige, imbued with the power of the Divines, fights Molag Bal Thus a small group entered the Planar Vortex with Meridia. Their goal was to unleash the Light of Meridia at the focal point of the Vortex thus dispersing it. However, before the Light of Meridia could be unleashed, the group needed to destroy the Dark Ancors that Molag Bal was using to bind the Vortex together as, otherwise, the resulting backlash would "destroy Nirn as surely as the Planemeld would". Meridia, whose power was greater in the in-between realm than it had been in Coldharbour, aided in this task by distracting Molag Bal and defending the group from him. After the destruction of the Anchors, the Light, magically prepared by Vanus Galerion and [Gabrielle Benele](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gabrielle_Benele), destroyed the vortex entirely, setting back all progress Bal had made on the Planemeld. The victory, however, would cost the life of Laloriaran Dynar, who had stayed outside the protective barrier Meridia had created when the Light was unleashed in order to deal with the portals Molag Bal's forces were using, pushed towards the shield at the last moment, he was teleported away with the rest, but it was too late for him to be saved and he was fatally scorched as a result.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOMeridia TFS-17)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOTFS-35)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Galerion-31)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Laloriaran Dynar Final-36)</sup>

Thinking themselves victorious, the invasion force returned to Tamriel. However, Molag Bal had simply retired to his palace at [Heart's Grief](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart%27s_Grief), and planned to attempt a second Planemeld when he eventually recovered his strength.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-37)</sup>

Though the immediate danger had been quelled the powr Molag Bal had gained over Nirn was still prodigious. In order to stop any future attempts at another Planemeld and restore protection to Nirn, the Five Companions journeyed to Heart's Grief and confronted Bal using the Amulet of Kings. Mantling the power of Akatosh via the Amulet, made possible through the sacrifice of one of the Companions and a ritual performed by Abnur Tharn, the Vestige faced the Prince in single combat and banished him to the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void), freeing the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) and restoring the land he had captured, and ensuring the erasure of what remained of Molag Bal's hold over Nirn.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ESOGodof Schemes-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ESOAbnur-39)</sup> The event gravely wounded Molag Bal, whose power would take some time to recover, and expended the energies of the Amulet of Kings for a generation or more.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESOMeridia-40)</sup>

## Notes
- Wailing Shackler [Wolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolf) are beasts created from the pieces and lingering auxiliary magic of Dark Anchors. They were initially designed as [Senche](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche) but instead formed as wolves due to a mistaken rune.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Wailing Wolf-41)</sup>
- Anchorborn [Welwa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welwa) are beasts born in the blasted wastes of Coldharbour. It is said only the chains of Dark Anchors can hold them back once they've scented their quarry.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Anchorborn Welwa-42)</sup>
- Anchorborn Ponies are ponies that were fitted with Dark Anchor debris, transforming them into creatures that bear some visual resemblance to [Mind-Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mind-Shriven).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Anchorborn Pony-43)</sup>
- The forges which are used to forge the Dark Anchors are also used to forge the Black Forge Iron Senche. These metallic Senche burst in a soul-shearing light when called. It is said that those who look upon them feel as if they'll never feel warm again, though some riders claim they feel nothing while mounted.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Black Forge Senche-44)</sup>
- Tyranite Calx, an alloy used in the construction of Dark Anchors, can be formed into metal bands which weaken the will of the wearer its attached to via a [Soul Magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Magic) ritual.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RJ-1)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-SJ-45)</sup>
