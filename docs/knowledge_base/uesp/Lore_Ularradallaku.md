## History
In its time, Ularradallaku was a temple dedicated to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), the Daedric Prince of Revolution and Destruction. It also contained a statue to [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), the Huntsman Prince.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONUlarra-2)</sup> It subsequently fell into ruin. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Ularradallaku was used by the [Circle of Champions](Lore_Circle_of_Champions.md) as a site for bloodsport. Fighters from all three sides of the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) came to the Ularra battleground to participate in the games held by the gladiators.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Eventually, the ruins once again fell into disuse. The [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) were charged with keeping cultists from restoring old Daedric shrines such as Ularradallaku, as worship of the [Bad Daedra](Lore_Bad_Daedra.md) was forbidden by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md). However, by [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) the Ordinators had begun to neglect this duty, as they were occupied with suppressing of [dissidents](Lore_Dissident_Priests.md). With the Ordinators' attention turned elsewhere, worship at many of these temples was revived.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Ularradallaku was one such site, being reclaimed by worshippers of Mehrunes Dagon. That same year, the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) attempted to gain a foothold within the island of Vvardenfell, and negotiated with Mehrunes Dagon's cultists. The cultists promised the Brotherhood shelter in Dagon's shrines across Vvardenfell. Eno Hlaalu, grandmaster of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), took notice and ordered an assassin to wipe out the Brotherhood's strongholds. One of Ularra's worshippers wielded the Glove of Sanguine Safekeeping, one of the [Threads of the Webspinner](Lore_Threads_of_the_Webspinner.md) considered sacred by the Morag Tong. The gloves were reclaimed by an agent of the Tong that same year.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
