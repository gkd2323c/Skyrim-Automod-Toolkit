*Devotedly built and dispassionately destroyed. A clean cut, as if from an artisan's knife.*
—Verita Numida<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Head-1)</sup> Skivoxo the Dread Sculptor was a prominent and renowned [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) [sculptor](Lore_Statues.md), formerly under the patronage of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), known for his unusual and extraordinary talents.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Head-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DMBE-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arm-3)</sup>

## History

He hailed from [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), the realm of his patron Prince.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Head-1)</sup> Though immortal by nature, the mortal accounts indicate he was primarily active during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DMBE-2)</sup> His work was easily identifiable by its distinctive style,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Head-1)</sup> and he was renowned for his profound understanding of anatomy, though, as a Dremora, this knowledge was believed to originate from more violent experiences.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arm-3)</sup>

Several of Skivoxo's destroyed and defaced creations were uncovered on the [isle of Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by a member of the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md). They were later examined by other scholars of the Circle, including Ugron gro-Thumog, Verita Numida, Reginus Buca, and [Amalien](Lore_Amalien.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Head-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arm-3)</sup> Among these sculptures was a defaced effigy depicting the head of Molag Bal,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DMBE-2)</sup> as well as a carved arm and a severed head. The qualitsy of the carvings suggested they had once been part of large, significant works. The head of one statue appeared to have been cleanly removed from a larger sculpture, a cut consistent with an artisan's blade. This gave rise to rumors and speculation that Skivoxo himself had severed it, deliberately destroying what he had once devotedly created. His whereabouts remained unknown, and some believed he may have experienced a falling out with his Daedric patron.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Head-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arm-3)</sup> Some sources claim that the statue's arm had been preserved by the most devoted members of the cult, only to be destroyed by heroes seeking to bring an end to the Harvester of Souls.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arm-3)</sup>

## Notes
- Molag Bal was known for his fondness for statues, including Dilogene's *Ice Fangs No. 4*, which was said to be one of the Prince's favorites.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DDCFA-4)</sup>
