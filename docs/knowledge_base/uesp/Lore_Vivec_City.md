— *[36 Lessons of Vivec](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_24.md)* Vivec City (often shortened to simply **Vivec**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> was the largest city on the island-district of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In its heyday, it was one of the largest cities in eastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> and the holy seat of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) theology on Vvardenfell.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NFTA-3)</sup> It was named after [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) the Warrior-Poet, one of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god-kings that ruled Morrowind for most of its modern history and the one who made their home here.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> The city was known as the **City of Swords**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36LOVS24-4)</sup> as in the laws that cut people into better shape.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-36LOVS23-5)</sup>

After the [Ministry of Truth](Lore_Ministry_of_Truth.md) crashed onto the city, it caused [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to erupt and destroyed most of Vvardenfell. Those chain of events are remembered as the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), and while some settlements were later on the path toward rebuilding, Vivec City's fate is unknown.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRYII-6)</sup> By the mid-first century of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), what remained of it was a smoldering and charred ruin in the center of the boiling [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TIC-7)</sup><sup>:Pt. 3, Ch. 9</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/MW-place-Arena_Canton.jpg/200px-MW-place-Arena_Canton.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Arena_Canton.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Arena_Canton.jpg) The Arena Canton [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/MW-book-Vivec_Region.jpg/200px-MW-book-Vivec_Region.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Vivec_Region.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Vivec_Region.jpg) Map of the Vivec Region ca. 3E 427 Vivec City was located on the south-central coast of Vvardenfell, in the [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md) region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONVivec-9)</sup> A land characterized by its temperate climate, lush, well-watered lowlands, and myriad lakes and rivers.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GTVV-10)</sup> Much of the surrounding region was populated by a series of plantations, farmhouses, and the occassional smugglers' hideout.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup> The city was specifically built in a body of water called the [Norvayn Bay](Lore_Norvayn_Bay.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWElone-11)</sup> between [Elmas Island](Lore_Elmas_Island.md) to the east and the [Systata Lagoon](Lore_Systata_Lagoon.md) to the west.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWGen S-12)</sup> Here, it had direct access to the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup>

The city was built in the Dunmer Metropolitan style and served as a unique example of high Dunmer architecture. It was evenly divided eight-ways by ginormous, multi-tiered blocks called cantons, each the size of a complete town. These cantons had wide, open streets on the outside of each tier, each accessible by a large incline, while the inside was where people congregated and commerced.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup> When these areas were initially built, a blessing stone, which contained small fractions of Vivec's divine power was placed in its foundation to sanctify them.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTarvus-13)</sup> The city was famous for its steam-parks, its monuments, and the canals that separate the cantons.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-POM-14)</sup>

The domed top layer was the plaza, a courtyard where noble manorhouses and fine shops were built into corridor walls. Below that was the waistworks, a two story section for several stores, cornerclubs, and temples. This area was the central section of a canton as it contained the most places and people, but below that on a canton's base floor was the canalworks, named so for how close they were to the canals. On the inside, it was only accessible through the downstairs waistworks or trapdoors. Here were tombs, small shops, storage rooms, and even local paupers. Finally, its last section was the underworks, essentially, the cantons' sewers. Water is drained from here and to the canals.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup>

Vivec City's northernmost and largest canton was the Foreign Quarter, a three-tiered structure, while its east and westmost cantons were the Telvanni and Hlaalu Compounds respectively. The city's main entrances were through these cantons via bridges to the Ascadian Isles. Most of the main roads in the region all ended in Vivec City. The Hlaalu footbridge lied on the [Castle Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md) - [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md) road, the Foreign Quarter footbridge traveled north into farmland, and the Telvanni footbridge was useful for travelers toward [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md). To the center south of these outer cantons was a two-by-two parameter of parallel cantons, the Redoran and Arena cantons to the north, connecting to the Foreign, Hlaalu and Telvanni cantons, and the St. Delyn and St. Olms cantons to the south, further connected to the Temple District in the south. The city's shape is similar to a [cross](Lore_Cross.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup>

The Great House cantons held active administrative services, like the Hall of Records, the Treasury, holding cells, and several services only available to house members. Councilors and house officials took residence here, but to varying degrees. In the city's late years, at least one Hlaalu councilor named [Crassius Curio](Lore_Crassius_Curio.md) lived in the Hlaalu canton and Mage-Lord Mavon Drenim was the ranking Telvanni official in the Telvanni canton. Because outlanders were originally allowed access to the Foreign Quarter, several Imperial guilds like the [Fighters](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) in Vivec City, and several independent services and craftspeople operated there.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> Vivec City's port-of-call was known as North Landing and contained a single dock and silt strider port.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWGen S-12)</sup>

South of the Redoran and Arena compounds are St. Delyn and St. Olms, twin residential and trade cantons where many apartments and shops strewn its outer streets and inner corridors.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWGen S-12)</sup> Connected by two bridges to the south is Vivec City's Temple District and its main structure, the High Fane, the largest Tribunal temple on Vvardenfell. Underneath the main temple were the Halls of Wisdom and Justice, the Temple's administrative, executive, judicial, and martial offices. Hanging above it was the Ministry of Truth, a moonlet held by Vivec's power, base for the region's Ordinators, and maximum security prison. The south end of Vivec City was the Palace of Vivec, the eponymous god's home and seat of power. It was a large ziggurat with canals stacked over each other for several layers and a tall staircase to its entrance.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/ON-prerelease-Vivec%27s_Palace.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Vivec%27s_Palace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Vivec%27s_Palace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Vivec%27s_Palace.jpg) The Palace of Vivec [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/MW-creature-Vivec.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Vivec.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Vivec.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Vivec.jpg) Vivec the Warrior-Poet When Vivec City was built is unknown and its origins are deep rooted in the Tribunal Temple's mythology. Vivec's [36 lessons](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md) claimed it was founded in the days of [Resdaynia](Lore_Resdayn.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36LOVS24-4)</sup> referring to the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)'s ancient name for Morrowind. The province received its modern name sometime after the first eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) in [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE1-16)</sup> The events that lead to the city's founding in the [24th sermon](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_24.md) take place after the [Skyrim Conquests](Lore_Skyrim_Conquests.md) in the third century, as established in previous sermons.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-36LOVS9-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-18)</sup> The historical-fiction series *[Poison Song](Lore_Poison_Song.md)* dates Vivec City's initial construction in the aftermath of the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) and [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PSVI-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PSVII-20)</sup> which in the series' given timeline is roughly [1E 685](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_685).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PSIII-21)</sup><sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Dates)</sup>

As the 24th sermon taught its disciples and pilgrims, after Vivec retrieved the [Treasure Wood Sword](Lore_Treasure_Wood_Sword.md) from the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), they returned to the Provisional House and looked down into the middle world to see the third of his strongest [monster children](Lore_Monster_Children.md), [Horde Mountain](Lore_Horde_Mountain.md). Before he could approached it, he found it trapped by three minor [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) houses. Vivec smiled as they cheered on his arrival and he commenced a grand celebration of love and duty around the captured monster. Vivec combined the three houses to create the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md) and pierced Horde Mountain with his spear, [Muatra](Lore_Muatra.md), turning it into a big bag of bones.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36LOVS24-4)</sup>

He threw the bag northeast and the bones fell down, turning into foundation stones for a city while the net became bridges interconnecting them. Its segments were touched by his holy wisdom, creating the most perfect city streets in the known worlds. Vivec named this new city after his own [sigil](Lore_Vehk_(letter).md) and Chimer traveled great distances to see it, filling it with laughter and love, and the strength of the tree-shaped enemy children. Vivec's fellow Tribunal, [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) came to give their blessings. Almalexia blessed it with the holy protection of [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), while Sotha Sil blessed it with a [spell](Lore_Spells.md), one that protected it from [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s dark corner and from straying far from the [ALMSIVI](Lore_Almsivi.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-36LOVS24-4)</sup>

Whether there is any truth in Vivec's lessons or not, especially with the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md)'s rejection of Tribunal worship and their teachings,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TR-22)</sup> it is generally understood that Vivec's Palace has sat in Norvayn Bay for as long as Vivec decreed it. Pilgrims came far and wide to look in awe, but as more people came, the Temple Canton was built to receive them. With more visitations came the desire to trade, and with this desire came the need for construction and expansion. The Twin Saints cantons, named after Saint [Delyn the Wise](Lore_Delyn.md) and Saint [Olms the Just](Lore_Olms.md), were built, and the Great Houses, Hlaalu and Redoran came to provide their arms and wealth to enrich it.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LSP-23)</sup>

#### The Legends of Lie Rock
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg/200px-MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg) The Ministry of Truth A key landmark in Vivec City was the Ministry of Truth, known in legend as [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md). It origins are varied, between the Temple itself and many of Vivec City's people. The Temple had two origins, one in general myth was that [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), the Mad Star and the Fourth Corner of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md), rebelled against the Tribunal by tricking Baar Dau to change its course in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) toward the city, which he called a mockery to the heavens.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup>

In Vivec's 33rd sermon, it was the seventh of his strongest monster children. After its conception, it hide in the [Scaled Blanket](Lore_The_Serpent.md) to access the Hidden Heaven, and haggled with the [Void Ghost](Lore_Lorkhan.md) for *"a power that no divinity will dare disobey."* Vivec sent [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) to destroy Baar Dau. In the heavens, he made peace with the Dominion Planets, the [north](Lore_Akatosh_(planet).md), [south](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md), and [third-pole-stars](Lore_Julianos_(planet).md), who allowed him to wander among their charges and gave him the red sight to find the moonlet. As Nerevar spoke with Lorkhan, however, Baar Dau left in the confusion to launch their attack on Vivec City.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-36LOVS33-24)</sup>

Vivec City's people panicked as they saw the shooting star barrel towards them but Vivec stopped it from falling by suspending it with his own magic. In the general Tribunal myth, Baar Dau was so overwhelmed by Vivec's courage and daring, it swore eternal service to the Tribunal.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup> But in Vivec's 33rd sermon, he stabbed it with Muatra and kept it in place with its original intentions intact. That way if his people's love and faith for him and his city ever went away, then Baar Dau would resume its fall onto the land.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-36LOVS33-24)</sup> The meteor was hollowed out and it became the city's headquarters for its Ordinators.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup>

### Trials and Tribulations in the Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-place-Vivec_City_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Vivec_City_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec_City_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec_City_02.jpg) Vivec City under construction [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg) Baar Dau falling in the sky By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there was a desire in Vivec City to expand, resulting in a new project that would build the future Arena, Hlaalu, and Redoran Cantons, with the Telvanni Canton later down the line.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONVivec-9)</sup> In that time, Vivec appointed Canon [Tarvus Indoril](Lore_Tarvus_Indoril.md) as his Archcanon.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MTCAT-25)</sup> But while the city had always been administered by the Temple, its governance during this time was shared with House Redoran, who at that point, controlled Vvardenfell's southern and southwestern coasts.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOMIFOV-26)</sup>

House Redoran also brokered an agreement with House Hlaalu, where they would provide the labor and resources for the construction of their settlements for a very low cost.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-HCS-27)</sup> Whether the same could have been said about Vivec City is unclear, but while it maintained its Dunmer Metropolitan style, other Redoran towns like [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) and [Suran](Lore_Suran.md) were made in House Hlaalu's signature style.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-HCS-27)</sup> A [granite](Lore_Granite.md) quarry built west of [Lake Nabia](Lore_Lake_Nabia.md) near [Foyada Mamaea](Lore_Foyada_Mamaea.md) became Vivec City's main supply of raw materials,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONLoad Foyada-28)</sup> while a woman named Shiralas became the project's overseer.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTarvus-13)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Vivec's power waned and he began an investigation. Using one of his blessing stones from the construction, he confirmed through a divination ritual of strange happenings on Vvardenfell and sent [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to discover the truth of his behalf.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONConundrum-29)</sup> What they found was the former Clockwork mage named [Barilzar](Lore_Barilzar.md) and his research on the transfer of divine energy, that an [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) stole a [mystical staff](Lore_Sunna%27rah.md) from the [Nycotic Cult](Lore_Nycotic_Cult.md) of daedra, and the self-proclaimed [Incarnate](Lore_Failed_Incarnates.md) of Nerevar, [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md) and his meeting with a renegade Ashlander group called the [Red Exiles](Lore_Red_Exiles.md). As the situation escalated, however, Vivec sent away all of his priests, with only the outlander, Archcanon Tarvus, and Canon Llevule Andrano allowed to see him in his weaker state.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONInquiries-30)</sup>

What they learned was that Chodala had the Red Exiles steal the staff known as [Sunna'rah](Lore_Sunna%27rah.md) so that he could use it to steal Vivec's powers and further his claims as Nerevar Incarnate.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONInquiries-30)</sup> But even when he was proven to be a false incarnate, Chodala persisted and continued to drain the god's power. As it continued to leave him, Red Mountain grew more active, but Vivec had enough energy left to hold Baar Dau.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONDelusions-31)</sup> The Vestige and their Ashlander companion, [Seryn](Lore_Seryn.md) defeated Chodala in the ruins of Kaushtarari, using Barilzar's Tonal Inverter to counteract Sunna'rah. The outlander returned with Sunna'rah to Vivec's Palace and gave it to the Archcanon, who used it to further drain Vivec's power. At that point, he revealed himself to be [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md), the daedric hound of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), who needed Vivec's divine magic to locate Sotha Sil's [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONIntervention-32)</sup>

With some of the last vestiges of Vivec's power taken, the magic holding Baar Dau was gone and it resumed its fall onto the city. Canon Llevule devised a plan using one of the blessing stones and restored some of Vivec's energy to keep him alive a little longer.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONDisaster-33)</sup> With Barilzar's help, the outlander entered the Clockwork City and defeated Barbas. The three heroes, Barilzar, Seryn, and the Vestige returned Vivec his power, who then used it to restore Vvardenfell from the chaos of its near destruction. Vivec realized that Archcanon Tarvus died weeks before Barbas took his identity. After the recent events, he named Llevule Andrano his new Archcanon.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONRestoration-34)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONBlessings-35)</sup> In that same time, Bradyn of the Library of Vivec began a project to model all of Vvardenfell and locate the legendary [Library of Andule](Lore_Library_of_Andule.md), the secret repository of knowledge to the Great Houses. In time, the project was complete and thus began a great deal of research into the ancestry of the Great Houses.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONLibrary-36)</sup>

### Vivec City and the Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/MW-place-Vivec_Foreign.jpg/200px-MW-place-Vivec_Foreign.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Vivec_Foreign.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Vivec_Foreign.jpg) The Foreign Quarter By the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and his new [Cyrodilic Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) began expanding into the other provinces after a series of conflicts known as the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). After their successful conquest for [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), the Empire focused their efforts on Morrowind. Despite their attempts to retaliate, their war ended after Vivec appeared before his people in Vivec City, announcing his negotiation of a treaty. The [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) was signed with Tiber Septim and it formally incorporated Morrowind as an Imperial Province.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-OM-37)</sup>

Per the terms of the Armistice, Vvardenfell was deemed a Temple preserve and was uninhabited, save for a few sanctioned Great House settlements and the pre-established Ashlander settlements.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ASHOM-38)</sup> Only its major cities, such as Vivec City were made accessible to the Empire.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-KPR-39)</sup> However, foreigners were denied access to most of the city so its final district, the Foreign Quarter was built on the north end of Norvayn Bay to accommodate them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> Before she was Empress, [Katariah Ra'athim](Lore_Katariah_Ra%27athim.md) knew the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) master armorer [Hazadir](Lore_Hazadir.md) when he was just a [slave](Lore_Slave.md) in Vivec City.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TAC-40)</sup> The [pirate](Lore_Pirates.md) captain Kaladas ran an active [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) route between Vivec and [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), sometime before the [Camoran Usurper's Invasion](Lore_Camoran_Usurper%27s_Invasion.md) in the mid- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-CDJ1-41)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-CDJ2-42)</sup>

In [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), the King of Morrowind, [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md) revoked Vvardenfell's status as a preserve and the ban on trade and settlement of the island. As a result, a flood of Imperial colonists and Great House Dunmer came to the island to expand on old settlements.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ASHOM-38)</sup> Four years earlier, however, the slowly re-surfacing [Sixth House](Lore_Sixth_House.md) established cells in Vivec City's lower-class waterfronts, carefully sowing the seeds of their eventual return.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DUP-43)</sup> By the city's late years, outlanders were allowed access beyond the Foreign Quarter and to the rest of the city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup>

Another byproduct of the revoked ban was the arrival of the neighboring Great Houses, Hlaalu, Redoran, and Telvanni, and the new distribution of territory on Vvardenfell. Vivec City, sacred Temple sites, and practically any unclaimed territory was part of the Temple District.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ASHOM-38)</sup> Each of the Great Houses had a presence in Vivec City, in at least their eponymous cantons.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> But House Hlaalu owned more land across the Ascadian Isles and some councilors lived in and around the city, like Crassius Curio and Dram Bero in the Hlaalu Compound, Yngling Half-Troll in the St. Olms Canton, and Velanda Omani in Elmas Island, in the East Vivec region.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-YB426-44)</sup>

### Vivec City and Moon-and-Star Reborn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/MW-npc-Mehra_Milo.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Mehra_Milo.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Mehra_Milo.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Mehra_Milo.jpg) Mehra Milo [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/MW-npc-Tholer_Saryoni.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Tholer_Saryoni.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Tholer_Saryoni.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Tholer_Saryoni.jpg) Archcanon Tholer Saryoni In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), amid the [Vvardenfell Crisis](Lore_Vvardenfell_Crisis.md), a [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) agent was sent by their spymaster, [Caius Cosades](Lore_Caius_Cosades.md) to gather intel on the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), their [associated cult](Lore_Nerevarine_Cult.md), and the Sixth House cult in Vivec City. These informants included a Thieves Guild smuggler, a Morag Tong thrall, and a Temple Mehra named Milo.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-MWInformants-45)</sup> Having been watched by the Ordinators, however, she was taken captive to the Ministry of Truth. The Agent, now on the path to becoming the Nerevarine, rescued her and escaped with [scrolls](Lore_Scrolls.md) of Divine Intervention to [Castle Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md). From there, they fled to the [Holamayan Monastery](Lore_Holamayan.md), home to the [Dissident Priests](Lore_Dissident_Priests.md).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-MWMilo-46)</sup>

The Nerevarine, from then on, continued their path toward fulfilling the [Nerevarine Prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) by completing its trials, and becoming recognized by the Ashlander tribes and the Great Houses as the Nerevarine and Hortator respectively. Their quest frequently brought them to Vivec City, like when they dueled the Redoran Archmaster [Bolvyn Venim](Lore_Bolvyn_Venim.md) in the Vivec City Arena,<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-MWRedoran-47)</sup> or found Hlaalu Councilor Dram Bero in the "haunted" house of the St. Olms Plaza.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-MWHlaalu-48)</sup>

However, the Tribunal Temple in the High Fane, through the Order of the Inquisition began their own investigations on them, considering its heretical and contradictory nature to the temple. Archordinator [Berel Sala](Lore_Berel_Sala.md) discovered their close ties to the Blades and their association to the [Emperor](Lore_Uriel_VII.md)'s close interest, further questioning their validity and motives. Archcanon Tholer Saryoni arranged a formal, yet secret meeting with the Nerevarine to determine the truth.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NFTA-3)</sup> Having acknowledged their claims as both Hortator and Nerevarine, and with the threat of [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) and the Sixth House reaching its peak, Tholer Saryoni acknowledged a needed change in the Temple's policies and allowed them to met with Vivec privately in his palace, where they would discuss plans to defeat the Sharmat.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-MWNerevarine-49)</sup>

In that same time, several events took place in and around Vivec City. A string of murders occurred across the city with seven confirmed victims, five outlanders, and two Ordinators. Elam Andas of the Order of the Watch began an investigation and learned that each of the outlanders had only been in Vvardenfell for less than a week. Three were found in the Foreign Quarter, one in the Hlaalu Compound, and another in the water by the Arena. The first theory was that it was an anti-Imperial fanatic, but two Ordinators were later killed. The victims had their throats slit and neither of the Ordinators had their possessions taken. Following a lead about one outlander being threatened by a Dunmer woman, an adventurer hired by Elam Andas found the killer, a Sixth House Dreamer Prophet and ending her spree.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-MWKillings-50)</sup>

With the Sixth House rampant at this time, Berel Sara, leader of the Ordinators in Vivec City and Temple fanatic used his influence to fight back, superceding control over the Archcanon, ignoring Temple policy and causing issues with Duke [Vedam Dren](Lore_Vedam_Dren.md) and House Hlaalu. Berel Sala and Tholer Saryoni came to an understanding after a House Father of House Hlaalu recovered the lost [Robe of St. Roris](Lore_Robe_of_St._Roris.md) and the Archcanon presented it during reconciliation.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-MWControl-51)</sup>

The Guildmaster of the Vvardenfell Fighters Guild, Sjoring Hard-Heart was killed in the Vivec City chapter but the circumstances are unclear. Secretly, he was connected to the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md) and used his influence to corrupt the Fighters Guild and target the burgeoning Thieves Guild.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MWPercius-52)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-MWStacey-53)</sup> Whether his death came from someone within the Fighters Guild or someone in the Thieves Guild is unknown, but the Camonna Tong lost any influence in the Fighters Guild and the Thieves Guild survived potential destruction had Sjoring attacked their guild chapters.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-MWHeart FG-54)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-MWHeart TG-55)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/MW-npc-Dalse_Adren.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Dalse_Adren.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Dalse_Adren.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Dalse_Adren.jpg) A Vivec City Gondolier [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/MW-interior-Arena_Canton.jpg/200px-MW-interior-Arena_Canton.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-interior-Arena_Canton.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-interior-Arena_Canton.jpg) The Vivec City Arena
- *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Dunmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Dunmer.md#Society)** sections on the Dunmer page.*

As previously one of the largest cities in eastern Tamriel, Vivec City boasted a dense population, as did its sister city to the west, Castle Ebonheart. Hundreds of tourists and pilgrims flocked to the city daily to see the High Fane and the Ministry of Truth, as well as offer prayers and thanks at the Palace of Vivec. Only the most devout were allowed to see Vivec in person at his palace.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> Perhaps one of the last people to see him before his disappearance was [Jiub](Lore_Jiub.md), who had slain seventy-six [cliff racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md) and attained sainthood by Vivec in front of his throne.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-SJO-65)</sup>

The Saint Delyn and Olms Cantons were Vivec City's centers of population and trade. Commoners and paupers lived in apartments strewn around its outer streets while craftspeople ran their shops in spaces inside the canton. These shops, which included the Brewers and Fishmongers, Farmers and Laborers, Glassworkers, Potters, Tailors and Dyers, and Tanners and Miners Halls, are where Vvardenfell's crafts and light industries were based.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> It was the craftsmer of Vivec that created rust funnelcap paint, in a process of grinding dried funnelcap mushrooms into paste that is as simple as it is potent.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-ONRust Anti-66)</sup>

The [Sweeps](Lore_Sweeps.md) were a guild of broom-handlers who made good money cleaning large city streets like in Vivec of volcanic ash from Red Mountain.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-IWADE-67)</sup> The city's main form of transportation were its gondolas, who shuttled passengers through the canals for a low-price.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> While its port-of-call for larger ships and silt striders was North Landing.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWGen S-12)</sup> Vivec City's Arena was famous in its heyday and a popular tourist site. Many shows from mock battles to real gladiatorial combat, and even theater were held there. The Arena could hold hundreds of spectators, while the waistworks were used by entertainers and combatants between performances.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> The city even had its own gladiatorial team called the Vivec City Cliff Racers, who previously fought in a pan-Tamrielic event called the Dragon Games.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-CSGlory-68)</sup>

The Great House cantons were significant centers of commerce for their respective factions, the Hlaalu, Redoran, and Telvanni. Each of the districts contained an active administrative center including a local treasury, hall of records, and holding cells. In the Redoran canton were two noble clans, the Dralor and Saren families, who lived in manor houses in the plaza. House Telvanni only rented their compound from the Temple, which is why they lived in Velothi towers rather than their preferred mushroom towers. Telvanni services were available in the top layers while [slaves](Lore_Slave.md) and monsters were held together in its lowest quarters.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup>

In the Foreign Quarter and Hlaalu Compound canalworks were the Foreign Quarter Tomb and Hlaalu Ancestral Vaults respectively.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup> Cosmopolitan cities of Morrowind like [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md), [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) and Vivec City were more open-minded with interring deceased outlanders, unlike smaller, tight-knit communities. But still, it was unlikely for someone to find more than a handful of foreign names in their crypts.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-LAHT-69)</sup> Vivec City, on the other hand, had the Foreign Quarter Tomb, presumingly for the burial of foreign races and it was fairly expansive.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWVivec-8)</sup> The Hlaalu Ancestral Vaults' purpose was unknown, but the Hlaalu family had another ancestral tomb on Vvardenfell, the Hlaalu Ancestral Tomb south of [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md) and east of Zaintiraris.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-MWTomb-70)</sup>

### Temple Worship and Legend
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/MW-place-Ministry_of_Truth.jpg/200px-MW-place-Ministry_of_Truth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ministry_of_Truth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ministry_of_Truth.jpg) The Ministry of Truth suspended above the Temple Canton *I raise lanterns to light my hollows, lend wax to the thousands the candlesticks that bear my name again and again, the name innumerable, shutting in, mantra and priest, god-city, filling every corner with the naming name, wheeled, circling, running river language giggling with footfalls mating, selling, stealing, searching, and worry not ye who walk with me.*

*This is the flowering scheme of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). This is the promise of the [PSJJJJ](Lore_PSJJJJ.md): egg, image, man, god, city, state. I serve and am served. I am made of wire and string and mortar and I accede my own precedent, world without am.*
—Excerpt from the [Scripture of the City](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_25.md) Being the city of a god, sometimes called the **City of the Warrior-Poet**,<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-CTV-71)</sup> Vivec City was associated with the many practices and stories of the Tribunal Temple. The Scripture of the City is the 25th sermon of Vivec's 36 lessons. In the beginning he calls it, **God's City** and describes it as an extension of himself, or as he puts it *"rebuilding myself"*. Vivec ends the scripture by claiming it was the flowering scheme of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) and the promise of the [PSJJJJ](Lore_PSJJJJ.md).<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-36LOVS25-72)</sup>

Pilgrims of the temple traveled all over Vvardenfell to visit the Shrines of the Seven Graces, a plaque or three-sided triolith with an inscription of its associated story. Three of these shrines were located in Vivec City, the Shrines of Generosity, Courtesy, and Daring.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup> The Shrine of Generosity sat at the top of the stairs to Vivec's Palace and was there to celebrate the heroes that died in the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), as well as the common people that also suffered and died. A donation of 100 gold was left there as a sign of good fortune.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup>

The Shrine of Courtesy lied at the heart of the Puzzle Canal, the labyrinth of waterways under Vivec's Palace. It was dedicated to a time when Vivec battled Mehrunes Dagon and handed him his own [weapon](Lore_Sword_Not_Held.md) so that they could fight on equal terms. This act of chivalry moved the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md), Mehrunes Dagon's most honorable and chivalrous servants, that they developed a bond of respect and courtesy with the Tribunal's followers.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup> For much of Vivec City's history, the Puzzle Canal became a place of worship to test heroes who hoped to earn Vivec's favor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTV-2)</sup> A [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) named Krazzt stood guard at the shrine, awaiting a pilgrim to present them with a plain [silver](Lore_Silver.md) longsword.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup>

The Shrine of Daring was dedicated to when Vivec stopped Baar Dau's fall with his magic. A potion of rising force was customary as a offering to the shrine.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup> Baar Dau's legend is known in Tribunal literature but the city's people to the Temple itself have different ideas of its origins. While the Temple and Vivec have differing accounts in *[The Pilgrim's Path](Lore_The_Pilgrim%27s_Path.md)* and the *36 Lessons of Vivec*,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TPP-15)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-36LOVS33-24)</sup> the common folk gave their own theories, as published by the Imperial chronicler, Minerva Calo. The names of each citizen was changed to avoid Temple reprisal. A Dunmer merchant named Tolvasa recounts when her mother told many bedtime stories about Baar Dau, her favorite being *The Tale of the Lonely [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md)*, in which the former Star-Orphan comforts [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) as kindred spirits in the lonely expanse of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TOBD-73)</sup>

Instead of interpreting it as one of Vivec's monster children, the Dunmer scholar Tiram believed it was a chrysalis for the last monster child, and that once it hatched, Vivec would appear from his palace and battle it for 47 days until it broke into 36 pieces. An [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) named Narkhozug called it *"a giant hunk of dung"* created by [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) after Vivec bad mouthed [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md). Some people had less fantastical ideas, like the Nord miner Hodstag, who believed it was a gold mine rife with precious metals or the longshoreman Donoven, who just dismissed it as a rock. The Order of the Inquisition, when presented with the question, suggested to leave such matters with the Temple and that its precise nature is a mystery.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TOBD-73)</sup>

In Vivec's thirtieth sermon, he fights the sixth of his strongest monster child, who was born as [Ha-Note](Lore_Ha-Note.md). Having avoided Vivec's wrath, Ha-Note traveled sideways to the [Adjacent Place](Lore_Adjacent_Place.md) and was brought in by the [Grabbers](Lore_Grabbers.md). The Grabbers, who had never built their own city, was jealous of Vivec City and decided to build their "tower-hope" on Ha-Note's face. Vivec eventually faced the new Ha-Note known as [City-Face](Lore_City-Face.md) and killed it by stabbing its heart with the [Ethos Knife](Lore_Ethos_Knife.md).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-36LOVS30-74)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Dates) | The timeline established throughout *Poison Song* is widely inaccurate to other established dates. The series references House Dagoth and the Dwemer in the past tense, however the climax of the series takes place roughly around 1E 685, fifteen years before the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md). One of its main characters, Indoril-Tay is also supposed to be a House Dagoth orphan and is eighteen by the end of the story. The series also references the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md), an event that would not happen until almost three hundred years later. |
| --- | --- |
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams,<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-75)</sup> but the names for many of these teams were brought back in *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)*. As Vivec City didn't exist the time, the "Vivec City Cliff Racers" arena team was invented for them, and they competed in an event known as the Dragon Games.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-CSGlory-68)</sup>
- In design maps from 1996, this city was originally called **Vivane City**. Vivec himself was later [renamed](Lore_Vivec.md#Notes) for a number of reasons.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-MKP-76)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-Concept Map-77)</sup>
