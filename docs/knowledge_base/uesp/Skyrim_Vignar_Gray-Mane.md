[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane.jpg) Vignar Gray-Mane **Vignar Gray-Mane** is an [aging](Skyrim_Elder.md) [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) and former [Companion](Skyrim_Companions.md), living in [Jorrvaskr](Skyrim_Jorrvaskr.md) in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md). He is the patron of Clan [Gray-Mane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gray-Mane), one of the two oldest families in Whiterun, the other being Clan [Battle-Born](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Battle-Born). He is the brother of [Eorlund Gray-Mane](Skyrim_Eorlund_Gray-Mane.md) and uncle to his children. He is a staunch supporter of the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md), and will become the new [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) should they [conquer](Skyrim_Battle_for_Whiterun_(Stormcloaks).md) the city.

He is obviously wealthy and wears the attire to show it, a set of [fine clothes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fine_Clothes#000cee80) with matching [boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Boots#000cee82). He carries a [Skyforge steel sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skyforge_Steel_Sword), a spare [belted tunic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Belted_Tunic), and a selection of [upper-class items and gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Rich).

He is frequently accompanied by his servant [Brill](Skyrim_Brill.md), whom he allegedly helped to rehabilitate from a self-destructive lifestyle. As Vignar is still an honorary member of the [Companions](Skyrim_Companions.md), he shares a room with Brill on the ground floor of [Jorrvaskr](Skyrim_Jorrvaskr.md) and sleeps there every night between 1am and 8am. He spends most of the day wandering around the famous mead hall, at times taking random meals or talking to the other Companions. At 3pm he heads outside and patrols Whiterun, keeping a steady eye on his rival [Olfrid Battle-Born](Skyrim_Olfrid_Battle-Born.md). At 7pm he enters the [Drunken Huntsman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Drunken_Huntsman) where he stays until his bedtime at 1am. There are two small exceptions to this daily routine. The first is when you first enter Whiterun and Jorrvaskr, where you will find Vignar as part of the crowd cheering at the sparring session between [Njada Stonearm](Skyrim_Njada_Stonearm.md) and [Athis](Skyrim_Athis.md). The second is when [Kodlak Whitemane](Skyrim_Kodlak_Whitemane.md) dies; Vignar will participate in the funeral near the [Skyforge](Skyrim_Skyforge.md).

If the Stormcloaks capture Whiterun he will now have appointed Brill as [steward](Skyrim_Steward.md) and [Olfina](Skyrim_Olfina_Gray-Mane.md) as [housecarl](Skyrim_Housecarl.md), Vignar will take the seat of [Balgruuf the Greater](Skyrim_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md) and hold court in [Dragonsreach](Skyrim_Dragonsreach.md) every day between 8am and 8pm. He then heads up to his quarters and goes straight to bed, sleeping until 4am. He then leaves Dragonsreach and pays a late visit to Jorrvaskr where he strolls around until it is time for his duties as Jarl at 8am. Vignar will then also take over all of Balgruuf's quests and tasks, including purchasing [property](Skyrim_Breezehome.md) in Whiterun. If you ask him if there's anything else he needs, he will offer you to become [Thane of Whiterun](Skyrim_Thane_of_Whiterun.md) if you help five people in Whiterun hold (which you may well have already done, but Balgruuf did not have this requirement when you became Thane at the end of [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md). He will take over as the [steward](Skyrim_Steward.md) if [Brill](Skyrim_Brill.md) dies. Additionally, Vignar will play Balgruuf's part in the quests [The Fallen](Skyrim_The_Fallen.md) and [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md):

## Dialogue
The ongoing feud between the [Gray-Manes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gray-Mane) and [Battle-Borns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Battle-Born) obviously started with the [civil war](Skyrim_Civil_War.md). When approached, Vignar will angrily tell you about his political beliefs:

*"When the Empire surrendered to the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), they shamed us all!"* *"For thirty years, I served the Legion as a commander. Fought in the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md)."* *"We Gray-Manes have been feuding with the Battle-Borns for years. There's no more bitter enemy than an old friend."* *"You're lookin' at a real warrior! At least I used to be."* *"Look at you! Betcha think you're something, huh?"* When spoken to:

**Tell me about the Gray-Manes.** *"We've got an old saying -- the Gray-Manes have lived in Whiterun since the first snows fell in Skyrim. Well, maybe that's not entirely true, but our clan's got a long an' proud history in this hold. We respect the old ways, the Nord ways, an' a Gray-Mane has worked the [Skyforge](Skyrim_Skyforge.md) since before the time of my grandfather's father."* **What about your rivals, the Battle-Borns?** *"Aye, they're an old clan like ours, but they cast their lot with the Empire when the uprising began. Olfrid Battle-Born made his money from farming, an' got himself some new friends among the Empire's nobles. Now he's in the Emperor's pocket like the rest of 'em, with no regard for honor an' tradition."* **Your clans used to be friends?** *"Aye, we used to be fast friends, though it was long ago. We were two of the city's oldest an' most-respected clans, an' we could trace our histories all the way back to [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s companions. But then Olfrid got rich an' decided he an his kin were too good for us simple, backwards Nord folk. When the troubles began, he took the Empire's side. We haven't spoken much since then."* **Why did you join the Companions?** *"When you get to be my age, you don't remember why you did much of anything. Only that it happened."* **Who are the Companions?** *"They used to be something. Nowadays they just squabble amongst themselves. Take my advice and keep away. You look too smart to get caught up with this bunch."* **What does it mean to be a Companion?** *"My advice? Don't overthink it. Fight well and the rest takes care of itself."* When he sits around in the mead hall watching the sparring session between Njada and Athis he will spontaneously yell:

*"Chop chop!"* *"Get him!"* *"Get in there and push!"* *"Look at the young ones scrap!"* *"Twelve septims on the girl! Look at that speed."* *"Always good to see 'em go."* Additionally, when [Skjor](Skyrim_Skjor.md) dies, he will provide his opinion on the tragedy:

**Skjor has fallen to the [Silver Hand](Skyrim_Silver_Hand.md).** *"Tragic, of course. But, well. Fires go out. Others ignite. Skjor inspired many with his life. Perhaps his death can continue to inspire."*

## Quest-Related Events

### Battle for Whiterun
After [fighting](Skyrim_Battle_for_Whiterun_(Stormcloaks).md) your way through the streets of Whiterun for the Stormcloaks, it is finally time to conquer [Dragonsreach](Skyrim_Dragonsreach.md). Ulfric's second-in-command [Galmar Stone-Fist](Skyrim_Galmar_Stone-Fist.md) and [Ralof](Skyrim_Ralof.md) will help you fight [Balgruuf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Balgruuf) and [Irileth](Skyrim_Irileth.md). When the Jarl is on his knees, he will finally surrender and Irileth will sheathe her sword. Moments after, Vignar will come rushing through the front door, yelling:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_05.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_05.jpg) *"You have no place in Whiterun anymore."* **Vignar**: *"Balgruuf!"*
**Balgruuf**: *"Vignar Gray-Mane! Your family was noticeably absent from the walls. Now I know why. Wouldn't a dagger in the back have sufficed?"*
**Vignar**: *"You think this is personal? The Empire has no place in Skyrim... not any more. And you? You have no place in Whiterun anymore."*
**Balgruuf**: *"A convenient position to hold now. But mark my words, old man, in the days to come, Ulfric will spread his rebellion thin. And what then? We need the Empire, as much as it needs us. We Nords are the Empire! Our blood built it. Our blood sustains it! You of all people should know that."*
**Vignar**: *"If this was my Empire, I'd be able to worship whoever I damned well pleased. You wish to see an Empire without [Talos](Lore_Talos.md)? Without its soul? We should be fighting those witch-elves, not bending knee to them. The Emperor is nothing more than a puppet of the [Thalmor](Skyrim_Thalmor.md). Skyrim needs a High King who will fight for her, and Whiterun needs a Jarl who will do the same."*
**Balgruuf**: *"Tell me, Vignar. Was all this worth it? How many of those corpses lining our streets wear the faces of men who once called you friend? What about their families?"*
**Galmar**: *"Enough! Both of you! There is a burning city out there that needs a government."*
**Vignar**: *"He's right. Galmar, come, let us restore order."*
**Balgruuf**: *"This isn't over. You hear me you old fool! This isn't over! And you. A Stormcloak? I'd thought better of you. You'll all come to regret this day."*

After the heated debate with Balgruuf, Vignar will sit down at his new throne, saying nothing but a victorious:

*"They will sing stories of this day."*
### The Fallen
With Balgruuf out of the way, an unsuspecting Vignar will have to deal with the return of the [dragons](Skyrim_Dragon.md) and the rising threat from [Alduin](Skyrim_Alduin.md), and decide whether or not he will be part of capturing a dragon within Dragonsreach. Regardless of your progress for the Stormcloaks, you will have to present him with the idea:

**I need your help. I need to trap a dragon in your palace.** *"I must have misheard you. I thought you asked me to help you trap a dragon in my palace."* You can choose to persuade him; if your [Speech](Skyrim_Speech.md) skill is too low, he will respond: *"I'm sorry, but I can't do it. We'll just have to keep fighting the dragons as best we can."* If you have the right Speech skill, he will instead ask: *"Of course. You already [saved](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md) Whiterun from that dragon. I owe you a great deal. But I don't understand. Why let a dragon into the heart of my city when we've been working so hard to keep them out?"* Alternatively, you can ask him nicely and get: *"What you're asking for is insane. Impossible! Why let a dragon into the heart of my city when we've been working so hard to keep them out?"* You can then tell him that it is the only way to stop the dragon attacks: *"There must be another way. The risk is too great."* You will have no choice but to tell him that Alduin has returned: *"Alduin? The World-Eater himself? But... how can we fight him? Doesn't his return mean it's the end times?"* Three optional responses are now available:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/SR-place-Dragonsreach.jpg/200px-SR-place-Dragonsreach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Dragonsreach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Dragonsreach.jpg) *"Now, what's this nonsense about trapping a dragon in my palace?"* **It's only hopeless if we give up.**
*"I didn't say anything about giving up. Now, what's this nonsense about trapping a dragon in my palace?"*

**I'm Dragonborn. It's my destiny to stop him.**
*"I don't know about such things, but I heard the Greybeards summon you. That's good enough for me. Now, what's this nonsense about trapping a dragon in my palace?"*

**Maybe so, but I plan to go down fighting. What about you?**
*"Spoken like a true Nord. I'll stand beside you, Dragonborn. Now, what's this nonsense about trapping a dragon in my palace?"*

Due to his skepticism, you will have no choice but to tell him that it is up to him to stop Alduin. His response will depend on your progress in the civil war quest line. If you have yet to complete it, he will be quite hesitant, as Whiterun will be vulnerable to enemy attacks: *"I want to help you, Dragonborn. And I will. But I need your help first. What do you think the Imperials would be doing while this dragon is busy slaughtering my men? No. I can't risk weakening the city while we are under the threat of enemy attack. I'm sorry."* You will then have to come up with a plan to make both the Imperials and Stormcloaks agree to a temporary truce, starting the quest [Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md): *"Then I would be glad to help you with your mad dragon-trapping scheme. But getting both sides to agree to a truce will be difficult at this point. The bitterness has gone too deep. Maybe... hmm... what of the Greybeards? They are respected by all Nords. High Hrothgar is neutral territory. If the Greybeards were willing to host a peace council... then maybe Ulfric and Tullius would have to listen."* When you promise to convince the [Greybeards](Skyrim_Greybeards.md), he will bid you farewell with: *"Aye, Dragonborn. Maybe you can stop the dragons - and this war into the bargain."* When you ask him if Dragonsreach was actually built to capture a dragon he will refer to the skull above the throne and tell the tale: *"According to [legend](Lore_Olaf_and_the_Dragon.md). Although I never thought to put the tale to the test. [Jarl Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) it was, who later became High King. They say he Shouted it into submission in single combat atop Mount Anthor and brought it back to Whiterun. Numinex was the dragon's name. That's his skull decorating the main hall."* Also, if you try to make him set up the trap before the peace council, he will remind you: *"My men will be ready when you are, Dragonborn. You have a truce to arrange first, eh?"*

#### Season Unending
[Season Unending](Skyrim_Season_Unending.md) is the truce negotiation held at [High Hrothgar](Skyrim_High_Hrothgar.md); if Vignar is Jarl of Whiterun, he will appear in the meeting room, standing next to [Ulfric Stormcloak](Skyrim_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) and [Galmar Stone-Fist](Skyrim_Galmar_Stone-Fist.md). Before the meeting he will comment:

*"If you can arrange an end to the fighting, Dragonsreach is at your disposal."* *"We should get started."* He will then take a seat right next to you, and will participate in the negotiations. When [General Tullius](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md) makes his first [demand](Skyrim_Riften.md), Vignar will angrily break in:

**Tullius**: *"Riften must be returned to Imperial control. That's our price for agreeing to a truce."*
**Vignar**: *"Elisif, are you really going to be a party to this foolishness? Do you really think Ulfric will just turn Riften over because some Imperial general barked an order?"*
**Ulfric**: *"That's quite an opening demand, Tullius."*
**Vignar**: *"Ulfric, you can't be taking this demand seriously? We can hold Riften against anything the Empire can throw at it. Besides, Jarl Laila will never agree to..."*
**Ulfric**: *"Vignar. We will do whatever I decide is in the best interests of Skyrim. Are we clear?"*
**Vignar**: *"Yes, my lord."*

If the negotiations favor the Stormcloaks, Tullius and his allies will leave the table and Vignar will make a comment to you depending on the outcome:

*"Giving up Riften is a heavy price to pay for this truce, Dragonborn. I hope it was worth it."* *"This is quite an achievement, Dragonborn. And Jarl Ulfric. Markarth is ours!"* *"I admit, I really didn't expect these talks to succeed. Getting the Empire to agree to anything at all is quite an achievement."* At the end of the negotiation, Arngeir will ask Vignar directly if he knows the consequences of the plan:

**Arngeir**: *"Jarl Vignar, I assume you are familiar with the Dragonborn's plan?"*
**Vignar**: *"Yes, I'm ready to do my part. Just say the word, and my men will help you spring this trap."*

After the lengthy explanation by [Esbern](Skyrim_Esbern.md), Vignar will leave the table and High Hrothgar, walking all the way back to Whiterun, determined to keep his word: : *"When I return to Whiterun, I'll get my men ready to help trap this dragon."*

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_04.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vignar_Gray-Mane_04.jpg) *"Steady now! We need to bring it down!"* After the negotiations in [High Hrothgar](Skyrim_High_Hrothgar.md), or If the civil war is already over, he will only think twice before agreeing:

*"Then... Whiterun will stand with you, Dragonborn. We will take the risk, and gain the glory, whether it be in victory or defeat! So what's the plan, then? How do you intend to lure a dragon into the trap?"* **I don't know yet. But I know some people who can probably help.** *"Oh... well, I'll leave that to you. I trust you know your business. Probably for the best. It will give me time to see if that old trap still works - and break the news to the men."* Asking him again will net you: *"We'll be ready, don't worry. You do have a plan for actually luring a dragon into this trap, don't you?"* When you have learned the shout to call [Odahviing](Skyrim_Odahviing.md) he will say: *"As I promised, my men stand ready. The great chains are oiled. We wait on your word."* When you give the order he will respond, *"My men know what to do. Make sure you do your part. I'm putting my city in your hands."* and walk all the way up to the [great porch](Skyrim_Dragonsreach.md#Dragonsreach_Great_Porch) and position himself on the balcony, waiting for your next move: *"Let's do this. Summon this dragon of yours."* and *"We're ready when you are."*

When Odahviing finally appears, a [hunting bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow) and twelve [steel arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Arrow) will be added to Vignar's inventory and he will do his best to help get the dragon to land, while barking out orders:

*"Hold your fire until he's close!"* *"Steady now! We need to bring it down!"* *"Keep under cover until it's down!"* When the dragon lands, he will shout:

*"Get back! Get back! We need to trap it, not kill it!"* *"That's it! Now, wait until he's well inside!"* When the trap is released, a victorious Vignar will yell and position himself near the entrance, waiting for you to interrogate Odahviing:

*"Got him!"*
### The World-Eater's Eyrie
When the quest ends and [The World-Eater's Eyrie](Skyrim_The_World-Eater%27s_Eyrie.md) begins, an impressed Vignar will greet you with:

*"I'm still amazed that your plan worked."* *"You'll be the toast of Whiterun for this!"* He will, however, be very annoyed when court wizard [Farengar Secret-Fire](Skyrim_Farengar_Secret-Fire.md) appears and tries to take samples from the trapped dragon:

*"Farengar! Enough, fool!"* When you ask the guard to release the dragon, he will back you up:

*"Carry on, soldier. This is all part of the Dragonborn's plan."* Lastly, before you take off, he will carefully approach the dragon. As you fly towards [Skuldafn](Skyrim_Skuldafn.md) he will shout:

*"May Kynareth guard you while you pass through her realm!"*

## Notes
- He is also referred to as **Vignar the Revered** by the Companions and some of the citizens of Whiterun. The location overview of Whiterun in the *[Prima Official Game Guide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Elder_Scrolls_V__Skyrim__Prima_Official_Game_Guide)* also lists Vignar under this name.
- Vignar has a package that would exile him to [Viola Giordano's house](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Viola_Giordano%27s_House) in Windhelm should the Empire retake Whiterun. Brill, Olfina and her brother [Avulstein](Skyrim_Avulstein_Gray-Mane.md) also have this package. However, since it is impossible for this to happen, the package is never used.
- If Vignar reinstates your Thaneship after Whiterun is taken for the Stormcloaks, he will reward you with a sword whereas Balgruuf gave you an axe. - This is changed with version 2.1.3 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch).
