# Elven

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg/200px-SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg) Moonstone Ore [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/SR-item-Refined_Moonstone.jpg/200px-SR-item-Refined_Moonstone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Refined_Moonstone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Refined_Moonstone.jpg) Refined Moonstone [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/SR-activity-Mining_%28Moonstone%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Moonstone%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Moonstone).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Moonstone).jpg) Moonstone Vein **Elven** [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) and light [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md) are medium-quality items that are made using the mineral **moonstone**. The weapons and some of the armor require [quicksilver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ingot) as well as moonstone. Elven forging techniques were developed by the [High Elves](Skyrim_High_Elf.md) and were originally a closely guarded secret (see *[Light Armor Forging](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Light_Armor_Forging)*).

- Three moonstone ores are obtained by [mining](Skyrim_Mining.md) moonstone ore veins.
- One refined moonstone is obtained by [smelting](Skyrim_Smelter.md) two moonstone ores.
- Elven Smithing requires 30 or greater [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill.
- Elven armor is level 12 [light armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor_(item).md), made from refined moonstone, [iron ingots](Skyrim_Iron_Ingot.md), [leather](Skyrim_Leather.md), and [leather strips](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips).
- Elven weapons are level 19 weapons, made from refined moonstone, [quicksilver ingots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ingot), iron ingots, and leather strips.
- Refined moonstone is also used when forging [glass](Skyrim_Malachite.md) weapons and armor.

## Mineral Sources
In addition to the following guaranteed sources, the best random locations are:

- Refined moonstone may be sold by blacksmiths starting at level 6 and general goods [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md) starting at level 4.
- Moonstone ore may be sold by blacksmiths and general goods merchants starting at level 10.
- Moonstone ore may be randomly found in [Falmer](Skyrim_Falmer.md) loot starting at level 7.

There are a total of 44 veins, 6 ores, and 9 ingots at guaranteed locations. Some individual veins that are located outside and are not near any landmarks have been left out of the following list. ## Elven Smithing
Elven Smithing is the first perk in the Light Armor branch of the [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill's perk tree. Unlocking it requires a Smithing skill of at least 30, and the [Steel Smithing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Smithing) perk must have already been unlocked. Elven Smithing allows elven armor, elven gilded armor, and elven weapons to be crafted.

The Elven Smithing perk also causes tempering of elven weapons and armor (including elven gilded armor and elven light armor) to be twice as effective. This perk applies to both unenchanted elven gear and enchanted gear ([generic armor](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md), [generic weapons](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md), or [custom](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked. Tempering of several other items is also improved by Elven Smithing, namely: [Borvir's Dagger](Skyrim_Borvir%27s_Dagger.md), [Firiniel's End](Skyrim_Firiniel%27s_End.md), [Herebane's Courage](Skyrim_Herebane%27s_Courage.md), [Herebane's Fortress](Skyrim_Herebane%27s_Fortress.md), and [Auriel's Bow](Skyrim_Auriel%27s_Bow.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>.

### Elven Armor
Elven armor is a medium-quality armor with several variants.

- Standard elven armor is better quality than [leather](Skyrim_Leather.md#Leather_Armor), but lower quality than [scaled](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Scaled). However, it is also the lightest set of armor in the game. It appears in leveled lists starting at level 12 (enchanted varieties at level 13).
- Elven gilded armor is an improved version of the cuirass that is also unlocked by the Elven Smithing perk, but is created using [quicksilver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ingot) in place of leather. Its armor rating is 6 points higher, making it better than scaled armor, but has the same weight as standard. However, a full set of elven armor when equipped with the elven gilded armor has the same total armor rating as a full set of scaled armor. It appears in leveled lists starting at level 27 (enchanted varieties at level 28).
- [Elven light armor](Skyrim_Elven_Light_Armor.md) is a variant worn by the [Thalmor](Skyrim_Thalmor.md) that includes boots, cuirass, gauntlets, and helmet, but no shield. It cannot be crafted by the player, and does not appear in leveled lists. Despite its name, it does not weigh any less than standard elven armor, but is of lower overall quality (with the same armor rating as leather armor).

All versions of elven armor can be tempered using one [refined moonstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Refined_Moonstone), with the exception of elven gilded armor, which is tempered using one [quicksilver ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ingot). Having the Elven Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement.

- In terms of armor rating-to-weight ratio (i.e. the number of armor points per unit of weight), the Elven Gilded Armor has the highest ratio in the game.

Any combination of elven and elven light armor qualifies for the [Matching Set](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md#Matching_Set) perk. However, elven gilded armor does *not* match any of the other pieces. Therefore, for characters who have unlocked the Matching Set bonus, the elven gilded armor cuirass is effectively of lower quality than elven armor cuirass.

- This was fixed by the [Official Skyrim Patch v1.9](Skyrim_Patch.md#Version_1.9). Any combination of elven, elven light, and gilded armor now benefits from the Matching Set perk.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's elven gauntlets, a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 12 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

### Elven Hunter Armor <sup>[CC](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md)</sup>
Elven Hunter armor is a variant added by the [Alternative Armors - Elven Hunter](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Elven_Hunter.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md). It can be crafted once the quest [Once A Hunter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Once_A_Hunter)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Elven_Hunter.md)</sup> is completed.

The set lacks a helmet and shield, though the regular pieces can be used to get the matching set perk. The set pieces' stats are identical to the regular pieces, except for each piece having two more points in armor rating. As the gilded armor is still superior, it can be combined with the hunter boots and gauntlets, and the regular helmet and shield to get a total armor rating of 89. This combination exceeds the quality of [scaled armor](Skyrim_Scaled_Armor.md), while also remaining the lightest armor in the game. In terms of armor rating-to-weight ratio (i.e. the number of armor points per unit of weight), the boots and gauntlets have the highest ratio in the game, tying with the [Dark](Skyrim_Dark.md) equivalents.

### Elven Weapons
Elven weapons are medium-quality weapons that are better than the weapons associated with medium-quality heavy armor, ([Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md) and [Orcish](Skyrim_Orcish.md)), but not as good as [glass](Skyrim_Malachite.md) or [ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md). They appear in leveled lists starting at level 19 (enchanted varieties at level 20). All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using one refined moonstone, and having the Elven Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement. Arrows can only be smithed if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed. Elven Crossbows are only available with the [Elite Crossbows](Skyrim_Elite_Crossbows.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

## Notes
- Elven armor can be a better overall choice than light armors made from higher quality materials: with sufficient [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) and [Light Armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) skill levels and perks, elven armor can be improved well past the [Armor Cap](Skyrim_Armor.md#Armor_Cap) of 567, at which point there is no advantage to upgrading to scaled, glass, or dragonscale armors. In fact, there would be a disadvantage due to the increased weight of those armor types, unless you have perks negating the weight of worn armor.
- Elven Gilded Armor was originally rumored to be pointing to the wrong model data. It was temporarily fixed with version 2.0.4 [Unofficial Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), but it later turned out to be correct the whole time, and was reversed with version 2.0.9.
- If you are carrying an elven sword, guards may ask, *"Why the Elven blade, hmm? Nord steel not good enough for you?"* If wearing an elven armor, guards may comment with *"Hah, Elven Armor eh? What's wrong with good old Nord Steel?"*
- Moonstone jewelry can be found in random loot (i.e., [Copper and Moonstone Circlet](Skyrim_Copper_and_Moonstone_Circlet.md) and [Silver and Moonstone Circlet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_and_Moonstone_Circlet)) but cannot be crafted by you.
- Moonstone (ore or ingot) is one of four items needed at the [Atronach Forge](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md) to create a [Staff of the Frost Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_the_Frost_Atronach).
- Moonstone ore can be sold to [Perth](Skyrim_Perth.md) for the quest [Mine Ore](Skyrim_Mine_Ore.md).
- Ore veins (all types) sometimes do not respawn normally. See [Mining (Bugs)](Skyrim_Mining.md#Bugs) and elsewhere on that article and its talk page.
- Temper recipes are found in the CK for elven gilded boots, gauntlets, helm and shield, meaning they may have been planned at some point during development.
- With the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on, you can convert elven arrows into [Bloodcursed](Skyrim_Bloodcursed_Elven_Arrow.md) and [Sunhallowed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sunhallowed_Elven_Arrow) varieties.
- With the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) add-on, [Redoran Guards](Skyrim_Redoran_Guard.md) in [Raven Rock](Skyrim_Raven_Rock.md) will have elven weapons equipped.

## Bugs
- Elven war axes receive no benefits from the first rank of the [Hack and Slash](Skyrim_Hack_and_Slash.md) perk, just from rank 2 and 3. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.0, fixes this bug.
- Elven gauntlets may appear considerably darker than the rest of the armor set. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.3, fixes this bug.
- Elven battleaxes use the sheathing/unsheathing sounds of a one-handed sword rather than those of a battleaxe. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Sunhallowed and Bloodcursed Elven arrows lack the material keyword designating them as Elven arrows. <sup>**?**</sup>
