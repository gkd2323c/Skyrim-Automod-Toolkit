# Instruments

Instruments are objects used to produce sounds for use in [music](Lore_Music.md).

## Accordion
[Arenthian](Lore_Arenthia.md) "squeeze boxes" are popular among [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) travelers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

The Orcs of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) also commonly play accordions which are jokingly called "Orc-cordians".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

## Bagpipes
Bagpipes were found in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) among the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), and [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) among the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CENB-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIGalen-4)</sup> In Morrowind, they were often crafted from [netch](Lore_Netch.md) bladders. The adoption of netch bladders in the crafting of bagpipes gained popularity through the efforts of the inventor Serana [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CENB-3)</sup> Bagpipes in Galen were made from a [hoarvor](Lore_Hoarvor.md)'s bloat-sac and proboscis, often adorned with [parrot](Lore_Parrot.md) feathers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIGalen-4)</sup>

Wind chimes are often made with a variety of materials such as fox bones<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>, driftwood<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>, brass, silver, scrap metal, seashells, fragments of [scrib](Lore_Scrib.md)<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>, and [ogre](Lore_Ogre.md) bones to name a few.

Reachfolk use bells and chimes in their rituals. They believe that their sound draws the attention of the spirits.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOBEL-9)</sup>

## Drum
Drums are a common instrument throughout Tamriel and are commonly used by bards, armies, and tribes.

They can be made from a wide variety of materials, such as the back-teeth of bull [alits](Lore_Alit.md) which make acoustically impressive drum bases<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>, guarskin drumheads<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>, wooden bases, [elk](Lore_Elk.md) skin drumheads<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> and [giant snake](Lore_Giant_Snake.md) skin drumheads<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> to just name a few.

The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) celebrate the life of revered [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md) by creating a drum out of their skin.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

War drums are used by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), and the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

Orcs are known for their penchant for percussion instruments in general.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-WPD-16)</sup> Iron Orcs utilized drums during executions.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Contraband Iron Orc-17)</sup>

Reachfolk witches use bristle-pine drums to wake the spirits during magical rituals.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Contraband Reach_Ritual_Drum-18)</sup>

[Totem of the Brotherhood](Lore_Totems_of_Hircine.md) is a drum artifact related to [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONAO-19)</sup>

The [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) are known to have pellet drums.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

## Flute and Fife
Flutes and fifes are common instruments, with there being many variants such as slide-flutes<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>, kazoos<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup>, and nose-flutes<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> for example. People that play these instruments are known as flautists and people that create them are known as [flutesmiths](Lore_Smithing.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-AF-24)</sup>

They can be made from a variety of materials such as [silver](Lore_Silver.md)<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup>, [moon-sugar cane](Lore_Flora_M.md#Moon_Sugar_Cane)<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>, armbones<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> and hand-carved kwama limbs.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup>

The [Ateia](Lore_Ateia.md) family of [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) are master flautists with a history with the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). Parteron Ateia was a master flutesmith whose creations are both rare and highly coveted. The Ateia family themselves or collectors across Tamriel keep them away from the public but the Ateian Fife, Parteron's last creation was specifically donated by him to the Bards College so it can be enjoyed and preserved for future generations.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-AF-24)</sup>

The ancient [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md) had instruments similar to modern flutes made of gold but the knowledge of how to play these has been lost.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

The Key to [Velyn Harbor](Lore_Velyn_Harbor.md) is a special fife that resembles a hollow [brass](Lore_Brass.md) key.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONKey-30)</sup>

## Horn
[Dragonhorns](Lore_Dragonhorn.md) are legendary [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) crafted primarily by the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), used as weapons against the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md). They are magical warhorns carved out of the horns of dragons, inscribed with various arcane runes, and are capable of producing a tonal sound that incapacitates dragons.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-32)</sup>

[Old Orsinium](Lore_Old_Orsinium.md) possessed a horn called the Horn of Beasts which had been crafted by Zbulgat the Wild-Walker. This horn summoned the creatures of the Wrothgarian Mountains. Bears, echatere, and hundreds of other wild creatures rallied to defend the city walls when the horn was blown, however King [Golkarr](Lore_Golkarr.md) blew the horn three times during a single moon cycle, and on the third blare the horn cracked. This horn was later recovered from Old Orsinium by the House of Orsimer Glories museum.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup>

## Jaw Harp
**Jaw Harps** (also known as mouth-harps) are a variety of musical instrument found across various cultures in Tamriel. They are played by placing the instrument in the mouth and plucking the strings.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Scrib_Mouth-Harp-34)</sup> They can be made with a variety of materials, including steel<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Burnished_Steel_Jaw-Harp-35)</sup>, wood and scrib antennae<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Scrib_Mouth-Harp-34)</sup> and Maormer bone. With the latter material, it is said to be naturally springy and is highly sought after by unscrupulous bards.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Maormer Jaw-36)</sup>

Since then, eight course lutes have become commonplace across Tamriel. Anyone that strummed it was cursed to never let it go until they played the perfect melody.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONSticky-39)</sup>

## Music Box
