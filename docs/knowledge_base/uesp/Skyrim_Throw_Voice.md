[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/SR-creature-Krosis.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Krosis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Krosis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Krosis.jpg) [Krosis](Skyrim_Krosis_(creature).md), the Dragon Priest guarding the Word Wall **Throw Voice** is a [dragon shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) that causes the shout to be heard from the point on a surface the player is targeting. Enemies will often go investigate the sound, allowing it to be used as a distraction. The shout has a limited range, and only fools those who have not spotted you.

All three levels of the shout are identical in effect. Using more words allows a shorter recharge time. Even though all three words are learned together, each word requires its own dragon soul to unlock it.

## Notes
- [Shearpoint](Skyrim_Shearpoint.md) is guarded by both a [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md) (post- [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md)) and the [Dragon Priest](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md) [Krosis](Skyrim_Krosis_(creature).md), making it difficult to obtain this shout. The only other shout that has all its word wall locations guarded by a dragon priest is Storm Call.
- The insult thrown is random each time, but the things you can say include:

1. "Hey, slug-breath!"
2. "Hey, melon-nose!"
3. "Hey, cheese brain!"
4. "Hey, ugly!"
5. "Hey, troll bait!"
6. "Hey, skeever butt!"

- Using this shout while aiming at a neutral creature (such as a [Giant](Skyrim_Giant.md) or [Mammoth](Skyrim_Mammoth.md)) turns the creature hostile.
- This shout can be used to drop hung clay firepots (a trap commonly seen in Draugr tombs) from a safe distance by aiming onto the pot itself.
- This shout is one of two that have a very short cooldown (the other is [Dragon Aspect](Skyrim_Dragon_Aspect.md)). Without [Shout fortifying](Skyrim_Fortify_Shouts.md) equipment and blessings, the cooldown period for using all three words is just 5 seconds.
- Throw Voice is the only shout in the game where using more words means less cooldown; almost all others (aside from Dragon Aspect and [Call of Valor](Skyrim_Call_of_Valor.md)) increase the amount of cooldown the more words that are used.
- The voice that insults is very similar (if not outright identical) to the "Female Even Toned" voicetype.
- Although the first word of this shout supposedly is **Zul**, the pronunciation from the voice acting and the subtitle note found in the game file suggest that the 1st word should actually be **Vun**, which translates to *Tongue*.

## Bugs
- This shout is capable of triggering guards to confront you about using shouts in cities even though its nature should make that impossible. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.6, addresses this issue. It has been removed from formlist WICast Dragon Born Shouts.
- Argonians, Elves, female Orcs, or female Khajiit do not have the taunting lines when you use this shout since they don't have the recorded line for them. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 3.0.1, addresses this issue. They now have a custom "Hey" line when they use this shout.
- The voice may sometimes play talking to any NPC.
