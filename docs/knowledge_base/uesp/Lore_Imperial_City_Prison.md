[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/OB-place-Imperial_City_Prison_District_02.jpg/200px-OB-place-Imperial_City_Prison_District_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City_Prison_District_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City_Prison_District_02.jpg) The Imperial City Prison (Oblivion) The Imperial City Prison (also called the **Imperial Prison** or the **Imperial Dungeons**) is located in the **Prison District** of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), connected by bridge to the [Market District](Lore_Market_District_(Imperial_City).md). It is infamous for its impregnability, although several secret escape tunnels connect up to the ancient subterrane and the prison sewers beneath. It is the headquarters of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup>

The Prison District is a circular walled enclosure located on the northeastern peninsula of [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md), outside the walls of the Imperial City proper. A gated wall splits the district in two, with offices and the armory on the public-facing side and a prison yard beyond the gates. In the center lies the Bastion, a tall tower that leads to the prisons below.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/ON-place-Imperial_City_Prison.jpg/200px-ON-place-Imperial_City_Prison.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Imperial_City_Prison.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Imperial_City_Prison.jpg) The Prison under Daedric occupation (ESO) In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) began his attempted [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) by opening a portal to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) above the skies of the Imperial City. The city quickly fell, with [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) raining down from above and the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) and Imperial Legion turning traitor from within. At the time, the prison was under the command of Captain Ululmalit, who refused to turn traitor. The Daedra quickly overran the prison complex, imprisoning Ululmalit and killing most other legionnaires.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Command of the prison was then given to [Dusk](Lore_Lord_Warden_Dusk.md), a [Grievous Twilight](Lore_Grievous_Twilight.md) who declared himself "Lord Warden". Dusk and his council of [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) retreated deep into the distorted caverns that had formed beneath the prison, maintaining control of the complex through the deployment of [watchers](Lore_Watcher.md). The Daedra then proceeded to conduct heinous experiments on mortal captives from the Imperial City, turning them into [Mind-Shriven](Lore_Mind-Shriven.md) or conducting [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) "flesh sculpting" with their corpses.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

As the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) raged on, provincial forces entered the city to liberate it from the Daedra, and the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) arrived at the Imperial City Prison to free the countless mortal prisoners within. The [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) offered to guide them through the complex so that they could defeat Lord Warden Dusk and free her clan, the [Foolkillers](Lore_Foolkillers_Clan.md), from their eternal entrapment. The Undaunted fought through the Daedric forces and banished Dusk, whose [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) was soultrapped by the vengeful Lyranth. However, she then ordered the mortals to leave and took control of the now-empty prison for her lord Molag Bal. Lyranth would later restore the Foolkillers to power in Coldharbour,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> and after the end of the Planemeld the prison was eventually restored to Imperial control.

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/AR-quest-Escape_from_Prison.jpg/200px-AR-quest-Escape_from_Prison.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Escape_from_Prison.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Escape_from_Prison.jpg) A Shift Gate in the Imperial Dungeons (Arena) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/OB-quest-Tutorial.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Tutorial.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Tutorial.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Tutorial.jpg) A cell in the prison (Oblivion) The Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) used the passages of the Imperial City Sewers to hide treasures he had stolen from the Emperor's coffers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Later, at the start of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), Tharn had the future [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) thrown into a cell in the sewers and left to die. With guidance from the spirit of [Ria Silmane](Lore_Ria_Silmane.md), the Champion escaped his cell and fought his way to freedom by traveling through a Shift Gate.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> For over forty years following the Champion's departure, not a single prisoner escaped from the Imperial City Prison.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) was held in the Imperial City Prison for a time, before being pardoned by Emperor Uriel VII and sent by ship to [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) together with a fellow prisoner named [Jiub](Lore_Jiub.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWJiub-9)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the future [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) was imprisoned inside the same cell that once housed the Eternal Champion before being pardoned by Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) as he was passing through the hero's cell along a secret escape route guarded by his [Blades](Lore_Blades.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Brief History GI-10)</sup> The Emperor was fleeing the city while being pursued by assassins from the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md). However, the cultists knew of the existence of the prison's escape route and succeeded in killing the Emperor, triggering the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). Before his death, Emperor Uriel gave the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) to the Hero, delaying the cult long enough for the Blades to rescue his illegitimate son [Martin](Lore_Martin.md). Following the assassination, the prison sewers and subterrane were sealed and occupied by the Imperial Legion, who conducted an investigation into how the events had been allowed to transpire. Despite being heavily guarded, the secret route was exploited that same year by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) in order to reach a mark within the prison; this was the first time the Imperial City Prison had been infiltrated by a Dark Brotherhood assassin in over 300 years.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), [wormies](Lore_Wormies.md) under the control of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) began besieging the Prison District as the floating city approached [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md). They actively attempted to break through the gate into the district, and swarmed City Isle in such numbers that the Legion was unable to push them back from the city walls. As Umbriel approached the city from the northeast, it seemed likely that it would pass over the prison and turn all those within into undead wormies. However, it was banished from the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) before this could happen, killing the besieging army.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

## Notes
- The Prison had at least 20,252 prisoners by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It also has a solitary confinement wing.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
- In *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*, the cell for this location is referred to as the Imperial Legion Headquarters and the dialogue refers to it as the Imperial Legion Compound/Headquarters, while the map marker and worldspace call it the Imperial City Prison District. This is because the Legion Headquarters and Imperial City Prison were initially intended to be separate districts, found on separate islands albeit connected together by a bridge (as seen on [early concept art](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-concept-Imperial_City_Map.jpg)), before being consolidating into a single district later on in development.
