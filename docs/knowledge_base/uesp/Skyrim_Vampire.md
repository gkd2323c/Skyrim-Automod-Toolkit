# Vampire


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/SR-npc-Vampire_13.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Vampire_13.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vampire_13.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vampire_13.jpg) Vampires **Vampires** are [NPCs](Skyrim_NPCs.md) in [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md) afflicted with *[Sanguinare Vampiris](Skyrim_Sanguinare_Vampiris.md)*. The disease causes them to physically change, giving them supernatural powers and making them immune to poison, but vulnerable to [sunlight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sun_Damage) and [fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage). Vampires are [undead](Skyrim_Undead.md) and therefore susceptible to magical effects aimed at the undead but unaffected by [Illusion](Skyrim_Illusion.md) spells unless the perk 'Master of the Mind' has been unlocked. Since they are NPCs rather than creatures, all vampires have black [souls](Skyrim_Souls.md).

Vampires usually make their [lairs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Vampire_Lairs) in caves and other dark places, and may also be found gathered around ritual sites in the wilderness. They are often accompanied by mortals called [Vampire's Thralls](#Vampire.27s_Thrall). Vampires usually attack on sight, even if you are also a vampire. If struck by a vampire's [Vampiric Drain](Skyrim_Vampiric_Drain.md) spell, you can be infected with *Sanguinare Vampiris*, which, if not cured within three days, will transform you into a [vampire](Skyrim_Vampirism.md).

While most vampires encountered will have the tell-tale signs of vampirism, such as black and red eyes, some have no outward displays of vampirism and can only be discovered with use of [Detect Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Dead) - or through lack of aura from [Detect Life](Skyrim_Detect_Life_(effect).md) (like [Sybille Stentor](Skyrim_Sybille_Stentor.md) and [Alva](Skyrim_Alva.md), for example). All generic vampires are female; only boss-level vampires are male. The only exception to this is the vampire that appears in the "[Vampire Trick](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#Vampire_Trick)" world interaction: this vampire uses the same data as the generic vampires below, but is always male instead of female.

When the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md) is installed, in addition to several new vampires being added, changes to the physical appearance of vampires from the base game will be seen. Most vampires will have glowing yellow eyes and distorted faces, with bat-like noses, a red line from nose to chin, and ridges above their eyes (similar to [Dark Elves](Skyrim_Dark_Elf.md)). During the events of the Dawnguard [quests](Skyrim_Dawnguard_Quests.md), vampires will attack some towns or cities. Dawnguard also introduces the powerful [Vampire Lords](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md) and the [Volkihar vampire clan](Skyrim_Volkihar_Vampire_Clan.md) as a fully fleshed out [faction](Skyrim_Factions.md).

## Vampire Types
All vampires have a Drain Life spell which can [drain health](Skyrim_Drain_Health.md), [stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Drain_Stamina), and [magicka](Skyrim_Drain_Magicka.md) at a rate of a few points per second. Low-level vampires can drain only health, mid-level vampires can drain both health and stamina, and the highest-level vampires can drain health, stamina, and magicka.

Vampires also have an ability called "Chill Touch," which creates an ice hazard that deals 10 points of [frost damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Frost_Damage) on forward power attacks. All vampires of level 12 or higher have a leveled ability called "Vampire Touch", which grants a constant [Fortify Unarmed Damage](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) bonus. The "Vampiric Invisibility" spell allows vampires to become invisible for 20 seconds.

### Vampires
Although they rely primarily on magic in battle, all vampires carry one of the following [one-handed weapons](Skyrim_One-Handed_Weapons.md) as backup:

- **Sword**: [iron sword](Skyrim_Iron_Sword.md), [steel sword](Skyrim_Steel_Sword.md), [orcish sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Sword), [dwarven sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Sword), or [elven sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Sword)
- **War Axe**: [iron war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_War_Axe), [steel war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_War_Axe), [orcish war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_War_Axe), [dwarven war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_War_Axe), or [elven war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_War_Axe)

Vampires wear a pair of [boots](Skyrim_Clothing.md#000c36e8) and one of the following outfits, depending on the vampire's level:

- [Black mage robes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Mage_Robes)
- [Leather armor](Skyrim_Leather_Armor.md) and [boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Boots)
- Enchanted robes ([Fortify Destruction](Skyrim_Fortify_Destruction.md), [Fortify Conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Conjuration), or [Fortify Magicka Rate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fortify_Magicka_Rate))
- [Elven armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Armor) and [boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Boots)
- [Orcish armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Armor) and [boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Boots)
- [Glass armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Armor) and [boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Boots)

If Dawnguard is installed, vampires wear either [vampire armor](Skyrim_Vampire_Armor.md) or [vampire robes](Skyrim_Vampire_Robes.md) along with a pair of [vampire boots](Skyrim_Vampire_Boots.md).

Vampires may carry miscellaneous items, including [lockpicks](Skyrim_Lockpick.md), [potions](Skyrim_Potions.md), [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md), [jewelry](Skyrim_Jewelry.md), and [books](Skyrim_Books.md), and have a 50% chance to carry a small amount of gold. On death, vampires drop [vampire dust](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire_Dust) and have a 15% chance to drop a leveled potion or [poison](Skyrim_Poisons.md).

The male vampire that appears during the "Vampire Trick" world interaction carries a leveled dagger ([steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger), [orcish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Dagger), [dwarven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Dagger). or [elven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Dagger)) and may also carry some gold and a random [scroll](Skyrim_Scrolls.md).

### Vampire's Thrall
Vampire's Thralls are often encountered along with vampires, though they are not actually vampires themselves. All of the thralls are based off the data used for bandits ([one-handed melee fighters](Skyrim_Bandit.md#One-Handed_Melee), [two-handed melee fighters](Skyrim_Bandit.md#Two-Handed_Melee), [archers](Skyrim_Bandit.md#Archers), and [mages](Skyrim_Bandit.md#Mages)), so they use many different combat styles and can vary greatly in terms of strength, level, equipment, and known spells. They have their own faction and are considered friends to vampires. Note that with exception of [Dexion Evicus](Skyrim_Dexion_Evicus.md) and [Hroggar](Skyrim_Hroggar.md), thralls can never be "freed". Killing all of the vampires in the area will not make their thralls become non-hostile. However, if you are a [werewolf](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md) and you encounter a vampire and its thrall while you are in beast form, you can kill the vampire, change back to human form, then draw and sheathe your weapon as if you were surrendering. This will cause the surviving thrall to cease hostility.<sup>[*verification needed — Is this true, or even notable?*]</sup>

## Notes
- Regular vampires have a morality of "Any Crime", which means they're willing to commit any crime, but boss-level vampires have a morality of "No Crime," which means they are unwilling to commit crimes of any sort. Because of their aggression, however, they will still attack you on sight.
- Not only vampires' forward power attacks inflict the chill touch effect, but they also do 3x the damage of a normal attack, instead of 2x.
- Vampires' Drain Life spell is listed in the Active Effects menu as "Drain Health" with the effect being listed as "Drain Life: Absorb 7 points of health per second from the target. Only equipped in the left hand." with a duration of 1 second.
- Vampire NPCs are not limited to once-a-day use with their invisibility spell, as you are when a vampire.
- The [Voice of the Sky](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Voice_of_the_Sky) blessing also applies to vampires. Because of their aggression, however, vampire thralls and boss-level vampires will still attack you on sight.
