#### Iana-Lor, the Violet Star
[Iana-Lor](Lore_Iana-Lor.md), **the Violet Star**, **the Soul Matron**, **the Silent Mother** is one of the lesser-known [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), one of the Star-Orphans and an [Anuic](Lore_Anu.md) ur-entity. She was one of [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md)'s daughters and the sister of [Merid-Nunda](Lore_Merid-Nunda.md), [Mnemo-Li](Lore_Mnemo-Li.md), [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md), [Xero-Lyg](Lore_Xero-Lyg.md), the eldest [Prime Archon](Lore_Ithelia.md), [Sheza-Rana](Lore_Sheza-Rana.md), [Unala-Se](Lore_Unala-Se.md), and [Valia-Sha](Lore_Valia-Sha.md). Alongside her sisters, she was part of the Nine Coruscations, who followed the parabolas that led away from Magnus and separated from him when he withdrew from the creation of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). Similarly to other Magna Ge she is now synonymous with the [star](Lore_Stars.md), which she created. Iana-Lor was not yet gone, not while the [Moons](Lore_Moons.md) remained aloft and maintained in their Motions.

In the text authored by Star-Queen Varalias, she is associated with the [element of light](Lore_Elements.md#Light). Perched upon a throne, she disperses mortal souls along countless pathways. Within this, fate finds its opportunity to manifest, dictated by the Prime Archon's decree. Iana-Lor salvages souls with no predetermined destination. Their essence fuels the magicka essential to the Aurbis. She is a celestial entity capable of truly dispassionate love. What is required shall come to pass, as ordained. Under her influence, the cosmic disks persist in their rotation.

#### Insect God
A deity whose name was lost to history, known only as an [Insect God](Lore_Insect_God.md), was venerated by Flower King [Nilichi](Lore_Nilichi.md). The [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) [Men-of-ge](Lore_Men-of-ge.md) were eventually "destroyed" when the Flower King made a great sacrifice to this long-forgotten insect god.

#### Ithelia
[Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), known as the **Prince of Paths**, the **Mistress of the Untraveled Road**, the **Unseen**, the **Fate-Changer**, the **Dark Reflection**, the **Last Tomorrow**, the **Unweaver**, and **She Who Saw and Wept** is the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Paths. She supposedly had the ability to shape destiny, alter fate and rearrange its strands to her whims.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONHerma Mora-3)</sup>

#### Ius, God of Animals
[Ius the Extremely Agitated](Lore_Ius.md) is the **God of Animals** in some folklore, who has [statues](Lore_Statues.md) throughout [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and parts of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). These statues depict Ius as a little misshapen humanoid carrying a rod with scales on each end.

The tale behind the [rod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rod) he carries states that one day an evil farmer decided to kill all of his animals and have a big party. As the story unfolds, animal after animal is killed and prepared for a big meal. Lastly, the farmer comes to the [ox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ox) and prepares to slit its throat. The ox, not wishing to be anybody's dinner, prayed very vocally (in the form of a moo) to Ius. Then, Ius appeared carrying a rather large set of balance weights and, without explanation, ate the farmer and vanished. Ever since that day, Ius has always been portrayed as carrying a large set of scales with him. Local Ius worshipers are said to neither know nor care about the reason behind the scales.
