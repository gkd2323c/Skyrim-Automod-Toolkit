[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/ON-place-Elinhir.jpg/200px-ON-place-Elinhir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Elinhir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Elinhir.jpg) Elinhir at its height in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Hammerfell_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Hammerfell_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Hammerfell Elinhir, historically known as the "**City of Mages**", is a town in eastern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), close to the border with both [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It is located in the [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) region, close to the Skyrim town of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md). The city is noted for its massive Apex Towers of [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) design. Elinhir reached its zenith in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), when it was considered the center of civilization in Craglorn. As of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Elinhir was known for its use of magical techniques to make everyday tasks easier.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MA-2)</sup>

## History
Elinhir is the oldest surviving city in Craglorn. It was originally a [Nede](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) settlement, as evidenced by the city's tall Apex Towers. According to some legends, it was built by the "Nameless Founder" at the impact site of a great skystone that fell from the sky.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The Apex Stone of [the Mage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Mage), a powerful remnant of the Nedic religion, is located beneath the city.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOAD-4)</sup> It was conquered by [Ryain Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ryain_Direnni) and his legions sometime during the Alessian Reforms.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Once-5)</sup>

The city was overseen by Governor Pomptinus from [1E 2820](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2820) to [1E 2852](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2852) as part of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), during which time the city sewerworks were constructed.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOAD-4)</sup> Before the beginning of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Elinhir was similar to the rest of Craglorn in that it was settled mainly by those unfit for life inside the Empire, and it was constantly preyed upon by bandits and [Iron Orcs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Orc).

Early in the Second Era, a group of rogue mages led by Felix "Blackcaster" Augustus arrived at the city after departing from the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and attempted to occupy the ruins of the Apex Tower. The mayor refused them, and in retaliation the mages seemingly made a deal with the Iron Orcs so that the latter would besiege the city. In a show of might, the mages drove the Orcs away, and subsequently signed the [Apex Accord](Lore_Apex_Accord.md) with the town's leadership. Under this agreement, the mages would protect the town for as long as the people of Elinhir provided support for the operation of their mages' academy.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

From these rogue mages grew the [Blackcaster Mages Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackcaster_Mages_Guild), who eventually came to rule over the city. Elinhir still served as a haven for outcasts, but was "civilized" under the strict rule of the mages. Despite being labeled as renegades by the Mages Guild, Elinhir prospered under the mages as their academy grew. Between [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546) and [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), Elinhir was a cultural hub noted for its artists, artisans, and performers, and [Prince Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) visited the city out of artistic and philosophical pursuits.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Jt SK-7)</sup> This prosperity changed circa [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Arnure-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SOE-9)</sup> when the constellations fell from the skies above Craglorn. An agent of the [Scaled Court](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scaled_Court) made a deal with the mages, offering to unlock the true power of the Nedic devices in their towers in return for the location of the Mage's Apex Stone. [The Serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent) subsequently corrupted the Stone and gained control of the Mage, who caused chaos in the surrounding region. [Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elemental_Daedra) guardians, who had been summoned to protect Craglorn from [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md), turned rogue, and people were transformed into mindless spellfiends by the massive amounts of magicka permeating the region.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-10)</sup>

The Blackcaster mages were similarly corrupted when they attempted to cast the Nedic spell. The results were horrendous, and the Blackcasters began to hunt down every mage in Elinhir to steal their magicka, killing many civilians in the process. At their nearby complex of Balamath, the guild also created [Air Monarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Air_Atronach) from the rogue elementals in an attempt to assemble a Daedric army for the Serpent. The guild's leader, Arch-Mage Yamanu-ko, was driven insane by her lust for power, and Elinhir's surviving population were forced to flee or take refuge in the city sewers. The city's problems were ultimately resolved with the death of Yamanu-ko and the cleansing of the Mage's Apex Stone.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-10)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/AR-place-Elinhir.jpg/200px-AR-place-Elinhir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Elinhir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Elinhir.jpg) Elinhir during the Imperial Simulacrum According to an edition of the [Pocket Guide to the Empire](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Hammerfell.md) written in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of Elinhir had adopted [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) fashion and taste.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Elinhir was an active settlement. At the time, it was known as the *"Guardian of the West."* It was ruled by Queen Fhimke-i, and it had a rivalry with [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Arena-12)</sup> Elinhir was neighbored by several settlements, including Nimbel Moor to the northwest, Stonedale to the south, and [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) to the northeast.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Map Arena-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Elinhir was caught up in the [War of Bend'r-mahk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:War_of_Bend%27r-mahk), having been claimed by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Skyrim. Although resistance in Elinhir was feeble at best, there was much more conflict on the border regions in the countryside.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-15)</sup> At around the same time, the High Prophet of Elinhir, Ayaan-si, assembled the True Crowns of the north and started a rebellion against King [Lhotun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lhotun) and the [Kingdom of Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) to the west. Ayaan-si's efforts had started to pay off when he managed to liberate [Totambu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Totambu) from Sentinel.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3EH-16)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), butchers in Elinhir used magical techniques of crushing up ice wraith teeth to preserve meat for long periods. Marise Aravel referred to it as a small town near Skyrim's border.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MA-2)</sup>

## Notes
- The city is spelled "Blinhir" on the *Elder Scrolls Anthology* map, though this is likely a misspelling.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Anthology Map-17)</sup>
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Elinhir's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Blackcasters".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-18)</sup> It was later reintroduced as the name of a group of mages in the city.
- During [Saturalia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saturalia), the youth of the gentry give each other licentious presents that are intended to dare the recipients into lascivious behavior, though the giver's name is never revealed—it's up to each recipient to guess who their 'Secret [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine)' was.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-NLFI-19)</sup>
