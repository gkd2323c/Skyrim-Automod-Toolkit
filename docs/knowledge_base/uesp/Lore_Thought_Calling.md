# Telepathy

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Spiked_Miter.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Spiked_Miter.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Spiked_Miter.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Spiked_Miter.jpg) Dwarven Spiked Miter Telepathy is a [magical](Lore_Magic.md) ability allowing individuals to establish a strong mental connection with others, enabling them to communicate over great distances through thought alone. There are some with a gift for the skill, though telepathy takes some practice to use.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPEvening Star-1)</sup> Masters of this skill like the monks of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), and the [Sea Sloads](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) are particularly good at it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Prima-2)</sup>

[Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) and [Seif-ij Hidja](Lore_Seif-ij_Hidja.md) are notable examples of individuals who developed a mutual telepathic link.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDOO-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-NODOS-4)</sup> [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) links the caster's mind with the summoned, forming a delicate connection primarily for control and dismissal, but in skilled hands, it can become more formidable.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDOO-3)</sup>

## Practitioners
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Master_Miter.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Master_Miter.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Master_Miter.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Dwarven_Master_Miter.jpg) Dwarven Master Miter [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/ON-crown_store-Sagacious_Psijic_Senche.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Sagacious_Psijic_Senche.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Sagacious_Psijic_Senche.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Sagacious_Psijic_Senche.jpg) Sagacious Psijic Senche [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/ON-creature-K%27Tora_03.jpg/200px-ON-creature-K%27Tora_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-K%27Tora_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-K%27Tora_03.jpg) K'Tora casting a Mind Blast
### Daedra
The [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) are known to telepathically speak, sometimes to multiple people at once,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Tyranus-5)</sup> though this ability is limited to those near one of their shrines if they are weakened.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Barbas-6)</sup>

Some other [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) were known to employ tools like magical compasses to establish telepathic connections,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Plokun-7)</sup> while others had the ability to communicate without such devices.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FTJODV-8)</sup>

### Dwemer
The Dwemer were known practitioners of telepathy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDOO-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CHIM-9)</sup> They had a mastery over [Tonal architecture](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) and were able to achieve this through technological means. The Dwemer had headgear known as Dwarven Miters,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONDwarven Visor Miter-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Neramo-11)</sup> which gave them the ability that was known as "The Calling", which seems to have been similar to telepathy.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CHIM-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup> It allowed the Dwemer to communicate with each other over great distances. Three known varieties of miters exist, with the master miter being worn by the "Tuathumz" or "central pylon" of their Thought Calling system. It contains three finials thought to be broadcast-foci.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONDwarven Master Miter-13)</sup> The spiked miter has spikes believed they actually functioned as "etheric antenna," helping with long-distance Thought Calls.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONDwarven Spiked Miter-14)</sup> The common visor miter lacks these features. It has been suggested that, in the last moments before they disappeared, Lord Kagrenac used The Calling to summon all of the Dwemer people and rally them to carry out his plans to transcend the [Mortal Plane](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CHIM-9)</sup>

### Psijic Order
The Psijics are known practitioners of telepathy, and are also said to share the secret to performing "The Calling" as practiced by the Dwemer.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CHIM-9)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDOO-3)</sup> They are known to selectively breed [Senches](Lore_Senche.md), selecting only the brightest among the litters to sire the next generation. Psijic monks who choose these creatures as companions often establish telepathic connections with them, valuing their remarkable conversational skills.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SPS-15)</sup>

### Sload
The [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) were known to use telepathy through [mind magic](Lore_Mind_Magic.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Prima-2)</sup> They could connect to multiple minds at once, namely their [Yaghra](Lore_Yaghra.md) minions. The magic appeared to operate in an area of effect, allowing anyone in the proper vicinity to hear the telepathic words.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-IJA-16)</sup>

### Third Empire
At the onset of the [Invasion of Akavir](Lore_Third_Era_Wars.md#The_Invasion_of_Akavir), the Battlemage Corps of [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) maintained telepathic communication with [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) from across the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md). However, [Uriel Septim V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md)'s battlemages were subsequently plagued by magical interference by the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) and struggled to connect with their telepathic compatriots at the War College in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-R:Da I-17)</sup> Members of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) were known to confirm the completion of quests via telepathy.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-If O-18)</sup>

In the Third Era, a group of people called **Telepaths** began to be employed by certain City-State kings, and were rumored to delve into a court follower's mind on the king's behalf to ascertain the truthfulness of their statements.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Arena CQ-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Daggerfall CQ-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-QC-21)</sup> The Psijic Order are notable telepaths, and were known to send advisors out to the rulers of Tamriel where they provide "grave and faithful counsel" to their beneficiaries.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TOW-22)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-npc-Ealcil_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ealcil_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ealcil_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ealcil_02.jpg) A mage using Psijic projection [![](https://images.uesp.net/f/fa/DF-item-Totem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-item-Totem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-item-Totem.png) The Totem of Tiber Septim Certain mages had the capability to establish direct contact with another person's mind. Spells like Psijic projection,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ealcil-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Indaenir-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Rurelion-25)</sup> also known as aural projection,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-UGK-26)</sup> were used for long-distance communication. Despite their name, these spells were not exclusive to the Psijic Order, even though they were named after them.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ealcil-23)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Seeks-the-Night-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MTM-28)</sup> Some people have natural affinity towards that kind of magic.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-LF-29)</sup>

Sending stones are mysterious devices that enable long-distance communication, and while their origins are unclear, some have been found in [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) crypts located in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). They were employed by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), and had Aldmeri and Psijic etchings.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-KAH-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-BQI-31)</sup>

Several [gods](Lore_Gods.md) can reportedly communicate telepathically, such as [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md),<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Sithis-32)</sup> [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md),<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Tu.27whacca-33)</sup> and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Arkay-34)</sup> Some godly [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md), such as [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md) and [Meridia's Beacon](Lore_Meridia%27s_Beacon.md), are capable of telepathy.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-DFTotem Dialogue-35)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Beacon-36)</sup>

The Listener of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) can hear the voice of the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md), a figure of reverence whose physical form is only a corpse.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Cicero-37)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-NMother-38)</sup>

[Faolan](Lore_Faolan.md), entombed in his resting place, was said to possess the ability to establish telepathic connections with those who wielded his blade, [Red Eagle's Fury](Lore_Red_Eagle%27s_Fury.md), in close proximity to [his tomb](Lore_Rebel%27s_Cairn.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TRC-39)</sup>

Golden Eye [Guars](Lore_Guar.md) exhibit remarkably rapid responses to commands, often likened to almost possessing a telepathic connection with their riders.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-GEG-40)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/ON-npc-Magister_Gothren_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Magister_Gothren_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Magister_Gothren_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Magister_Gothren_02.jpg) Telvanni Mouth's projection Those whose [souls](Lore_Soul.md) are bound can communicate telepathically with whomever has the object they are bound to on their person.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Meln-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Dusk-42)</sup> Conversely, those who are bound can also hear those who possess their item.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Nelacar-43)</sup>

It is possible to use [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md) to establish a telepathic connection. This is achieved by connecting the shadows of beings, enabling communication across vast distances. However, the procedure for doing so is accompanied by pain. [Azra Nightwielder](Lore_Azra_Nightwielder.md) termed this phenomenon an "umbric connection."<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-AN-44)</sup>

[Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) Magisters often relied on the aid of Mouths, assistants who acted on their behalf. On certain occasions, Mouths also served as conduits for channeled projection of their masters, manifesting projections of their superiors originating from their mouths.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-AS-45)</sup>
