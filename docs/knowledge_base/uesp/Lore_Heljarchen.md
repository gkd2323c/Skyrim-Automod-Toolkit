[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/AR-place-Helarchen_Creek.jpg/200px-AR-place-Helarchen_Creek.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Helarchen_Creek.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Helarchen_Creek.jpg) Helarchen Creek during the Imperial Simulacrum [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-place-Heljarchen_Hall.jpg/200px-SR-place-Heljarchen_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Heljarchen_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Heljarchen_Hall.jpg) Heljarchen Hall Heljarchen (also called **Helarchen Creek**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup> is a historical settlement located in the region known as [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), within the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It is found on the edge of the Pale, where the mountains descend into the great plains of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md). By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) the original settlement had been abandoned and a private homestead known as **Heljarchen Hall** was constructed on the land.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Helarchen Creek was an active settlement, being ruled by Lady Ingte. It had a rivalry with [Laintar Dale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laintar_Dale) and Dunpar Wall.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) to the northwest, Dunpar Wall to the southwest, and [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) to the northeast.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Map Arena-3)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the area had become entirely abandoned. The jarl of Dawnstar offered to sell the land to the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) as a private estate for the purpose of constructing a homestead and [wheat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wheat) mill known as Heljarchen Hall.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## Notes
- Cut files from *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)* suggest that the Nightgate Inn of [Lake Yorgrim](Lore_Lake_Yorgrim.md) was meant to be part of a village known as Heljarchen, which would have contained an apothecary, a blacksmith, and a few farmhouses. However, only [Nightgate Inn](Skyrim_Nightgate_Inn.md) made it into the final game, and the rest of the settlement was cut.
