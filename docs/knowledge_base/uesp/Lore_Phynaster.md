# Phynaster

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-furnishing-Alinor_Shrine%2C_Phynaster.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Alinor_Shrine%2C_Phynaster.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Alinor_Shrine,_Phynaster.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Alinor_Shrine,_Phynaster.jpg) A shrine to Phynaster, symbolizing his walking stride Phynaster the Guardian<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Aa Aot ACF-1)</sup>, the **Preserver**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Treasure PD-2)</sup>, the **Wayfarer**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Treasure WLB-3)</sup>, is the hero-god of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). While he is not part of the main Aldmeri Pantheon, his worship persists through significant cults throughout the province. He taught the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) to extend their lives a hundred more years, simply by shortening their stride.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vo FTHE-4)</sup> Phynaster is associated with the [Ring of Phynaster](Lore_Ring_of_Phynaster.md), that comes and goes throughout time between different warriors. It is said to be the source of his longevity,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TL-5)</sup> but it may have also been his undoing. It was rumored that the [Wharf Rats](Lore_Wharf_Rats.md) killed Phynaster for his ring and when he was buried at the [Forest of Elborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forest_of_Elborn), they took it for themselves.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AD-6)</sup>

In the early years of [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) colonization on the Summerset Isles, they shifted the focus of their religion from their [ancestors](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) to the ancestor spirits, the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md). Among the many spirits were Phynaster, as well as [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) and [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md). The elders that opposed this change went on to become the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-7)</sup> Many of Phynaster's followers take a pilgrimage through dangerous areas across the province, such as the [King's Haven Pass](Lore_King%27s_Haven_Pass.md) as tests of faith, but several perish in the attempt yearly. It is customary for the [Divine Prosecution](Lore_Divine_Prosecution.md) to be petitioned to recover objects from followers who died as part of the pilgrimage, so that the priests may *"bless the fallen pilgrim's souls on their new journey."*, as a way to bring *"proper closure, for the departed and the survived."*<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTanorian-8)</sup>

In addition to ceremonies dedicated to the heavenly journey of the departed, Phynaster's likeness is also given to young children so that Phynaster may watch over them *"during their adventures away from their parents"*,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Treasure PD-2)</sup> and replicas of his boots are kept by travelers to bring them luck.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Treasure WLB-3)</sup> His name is also invoked by sailors wishing Phynaster to guide them on their journey at sea,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ASGTSE-9)</sup> by Marines asking Phynaster to guide the path of messengers sent on a mission,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOLandare-10)</sup> and by people about to start a new journey.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOTalomar-11)</sup> An Altmer beggar of [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) believed that Phynaster would come to take her back home to Summerset.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOVairaame-12)</sup>

While Phynaster is worshipped by the Altmer, he is also venerated by the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SRGelebor-13)</sup> and the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), the latter of which is a carry-over from their days as retainers of [Clan Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md), who saw the hero-god as their teacher and patron deity. He is mainly worshipped by Breton mages that emphasize their elven heritage.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Vo FTB-14)</sup> The worship of Phynaster was considered heretical in [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) society and the worship of the deity was prohibited to Imperials during the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-15)</sup>

## Notes
- Some symbols of Phynaster include [copper](Lore_Copper.md) boots fashioned in his canonical style and charms with *"blessings of safe passage"* carved into sharp [Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) teeth.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Treasure GTT-16)</sup>
- Phynaster was said to be the same race as the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> However, some sources claim that this Phynaster was a mortal named after the god.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TP-18)</sup>
- Phynastic Scroll Cases used by [Aiden Direnni](Lore_Aiden_Direnni.md) were associated with teleportation.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Antiq-19)</sup>
- During the Second Era, there was an inn named "Phynaster's Stride" in the port-city of [Haven](Lore_Haven.md) in Valenwood.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOPS-20)</sup>
- Ted Peterson, in response to the question whether [Phen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phen) is related to Phynaster in any way, said that he is "Not sure, but it works well."<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TP-18)</sup>
