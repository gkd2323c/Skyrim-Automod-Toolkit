# Cattle

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/ff/ON-creature-Cow.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Cow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Cow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Cow.jpg) A Cow [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-creature-Bull.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Bull.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Bull.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Bull.jpg) A Bull Cattle are large, docile, domesticated animals common throughout [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), especially on farms, where they primarily eat hay. Females are known as **cows** while males are known as **bulls**. A bull that is raised as a beast of burden is known as an **ox**. Cows from Skyrim are also called **highland aurochs**, and are used for their meat,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gwinor-1)</sup> dairy,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Denskar-2)</sup> and hide.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> In Skyrim, they are sometimes given to [Giants](Lore_Giant.md) by local farmers as an offering.

## Cattle in Culture
Bulls in particular hold a lot of cultural importance. In Tamrielic folklore traditions, a bull's ossified member is considered as a charm for good fortune and can be found across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONPetrified-4)</sup> To the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), the symbol of a winged bull was considered to be important enough to placed in their tombs.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONBull Statue-5)</sup> The aforementioned symbol is also seen in *[the Adabal-a](Lore_The_Adabal-a.md)*, where [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) is referred to as a winged bull. Among the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), the [Bull-Heart Warriors](Lore_Bull-Heart_Warriors.md) use their skulls in rituals,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONBull Hat-6)</sup> while the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) considered the bull to be their emblem after beginning their alliance with the [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md) and embellished their belongings with bull symbolism.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONDreadhorn-7)</sup> The story of *[The Ox and the Evil Farmer](Lore_Ius,_Animal_God.md)* references a prayer an ox made to [Ius](Lore_Ius.md), the god of animals, to save him from a farmer that would make him dinner. Ius instantly appeared and ate the farmer, saving the ox, though the ox eventually became meat for the local inn's pantry anyways.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ius-8)</sup> The [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md)'s emblem was a horned bull in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MFDoom-9)</sup>
