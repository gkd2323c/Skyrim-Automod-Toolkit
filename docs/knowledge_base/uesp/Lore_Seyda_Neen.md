## History

### Origins
"May We Seek Forever the Seyda Neen"
— [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) mantra<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup> The Seyda Neen was originally the flagship of a fleet that [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) had sent to sea at the behest of a Saint, "to see the face of [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md)". The mariners on this voyage intended on sending back for the rest of their House, when they found whatever this "face" was." But an unnatural storm destroyed the fleet, with the jetsam and flotsam coming back to shore; the [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) then used these materials to construct the Grand Pharos Lighthouse, so that any of their countrymer that may have survived the storm could find their way back. It became a tradition of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) nobles to embed glass gems from their ancient flagships in their foreheads, in memory.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-3)</sup> [Hlaalu Brevur](Lore_Hlaalu_Brevur.md), who was a historical figure of some significance to the Hlaalu bloodline, was directly affiliated with the Seyda Neen.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Po M-2)</sup>

### Port Village
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/LG-cardart-Prison_Ship.png/200px-LG-cardart-Prison_Ship.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Prison_Ship.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Prison_Ship.png) Prison ship at Seyda Neen Over time, a village sprouted around the lighthouse. The Imperial-style structures were built by the [Gold Coast Trading Company](Lore_Gold_Coast_Trading_Company.md) in the years prior to the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-4)</sup> while the village was administered and protected by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup> For much of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Seyda Neen was little more than a simple fishing village, occupied mainly by [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-AFFresh-6)</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), House Hlaalu still governed Seyda Neen,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TN-7)</sup> but the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) guarded the seaport.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Morrowind-8)</sup> Its native population was small, though it was a common stop for Imperial travelers going to and from Vvardenfell. The Grand Pharos was a unique lighthouse at the harbor mouth renowned for guiding mariners throughout the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md). Most visitors were processed through the [Census and Excise Office](Lore_Census_and_Excise.md), part of the Empire's Coastguard station at the harbor. Imperial cutters used the dock as a restocking point to control smugglers and pirates running the waters off the Bitter Coast.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GTV-9)</sup> [Silt striders](Lore_Silt_Strider.md) provided convenient travel to [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md), [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md), and [Suran](Lore_Suran.md). Wildlife in the surrounding area included [cliff racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md), [scribs](Lore_Scrib.md), [rats](Lore_Rat.md), [netch](Lore_Netch.md) and [slaughterfish](Lore_Slaughterfish.md). There were also [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) not far off shore.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Morrowind-8)</sup>

### Fate
Following the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5) that devastated Vvardenfell and much of mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), nothing is known of Seyda Neen's fate. As it was located near [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), which received the full brunt of [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md)'s impact, and settlements across the island were destroyed by the resulting eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-10)</sup> it is unlikely Seyda Neen survived the calamity intact.
