[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-npc-M%27aiq_the_Liar.jpg/200px-SR-npc-M%27aiq_the_Liar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-M%27aiq_the_Liar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-M%27aiq_the_Liar.jpg) M'aiq the Liar **M'aiq the Liar** is a [Khajiit](Skyrim_Khajiit.md) well-known for his often humorous comments on the *[Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)* series, as he (or an ancestor of the same name) has also appeared in *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:M%27Aiq_the_Liar)*, *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:M%27aiq_the_Liar)*, and [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:M%27aiq_the_Liar). He often breaks the [fourth wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fourth_wall), making witty comments about the game's strong points, smart remarks to common complaints about the game, or just general references to different aspects of the game or lore.

A wanderer, M'aiq may be found in a number of [random areas](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md#M.27aiq_the_Liar), either around cities or roaming the wilderness. When encountered, he may say a multitude of different things. After he utters three of the phrases listed below, if you try to speak with him again, he will only say *"M'aiq is tired now. Go bother somebody else."*, and then, *"M'aiq is done talking."*

M'aiq wears [hooded monk robes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hooded_Monk_Robes) and [boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Boots#000bacd7), and carries three bottles of [skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skooma).

## Dialogue
**"M'aiq wishes you well."**

A common greeting when you first meet him or enter conversation. **"M'aiq knows many things, no?"**

A hint at M'aiq's vast knowledge. **"M'aiq knows much, and tells some. M'aiq knows many things others do not."**

A recurring [quote](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:M%27aiq_the_Liar), referencing the one in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*. **"What does this mean, to combine [magic](Skyrim_Magic.md)? Magic plus magic is still magic."**

This and the next line reference changes to the magic interface, such as custom spells available in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*, and *Oblivion*. This refers to pre-release talks that the new dual-wielding system might also include the ability to combine spells, a feature that is not present in the final game. **"Too much magic can be dangerous. M'aiq once had two spells and burned his [sweetroll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sweet_Roll)."**

This refers to '[Dual Casting](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dual-Casting)', which overcharges double-cast spells. Also, the "sweetroll" is a running gag occurring in many [Bethesda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Bethesda) games, being mentioned in both *[Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)* and *[Fallout](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Fallout)* games. **"Much snow in Skyrim. Enough snow. M'aiq does not want any more."**

Referencing the fact that *Skyrim* was originally promised to have dynamic snow that built on surfaces. This is present in the final game to a small degree, but really only changes textures to snowy textures instead of accumulating a physical pile of snow. **"Skyrim was once the land of many butterflies. Now, not so much."**

This might refer to the fact that you can catch butterflies, or it could be a reference to an apparent bug from early in *Skyrim*'s development where butterflies would not despawn and would quickly flood the game world. **"Snow falls. Why worry where it goes? M'aiq thinks the snowflakes are pretty."**

Referencing how the new physics behavior system works. Alternatively, it could be referring to the fact that snow (and rain) clips through most objects so it can be snowing underneath a shelter. **"M'aiq carries two weapons, to be safe. What if one breaks? That would be most unlucky."**

Referencing the dual-wielding system introduced in *Skyrim*. Additionally, he may also be referring to the fact that *Skyrim* removed the durability mechanic used for weapons and armor in previous games. **"M'aiq is always in search of calipers, yet finds none. Where could they have gone?"**

A callback to [*Oblivion*'s M'aiq](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:M%27aiq_the_Liar), who would stop whatever he was doing at noon each day to spend five hours looking for [calipers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Calipers). **"M'aiq hears many stories of war... yet few of them are true."**

A possible self-criticism by Bethesda, whose system of creating characters made scripted 'major' battles unrealistically small. This was a major complaint for the [main quest of *Oblivion*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Main_Quest), as well as for the Civil War quests of *Skyrim*. **"M'aiq has heard that the people of Skyrim are better looking than the ones in Cyrodiil. He has no opinion on the matter. All people are beautiful to him."**

Referencing the completely revamped and improved character designs in *Skyrim*. **"M'aiq saw a mudcrab the other day. Horrible creatures."**

This is common line of dialog used as small talk between NPCs in *Oblivion*. It addresses criticisms that the NPCs in *Oblivion* would often have inane conversations and repetitive dialog. **"M'aiq does not understand what is so impressive about shouting. M'aiq can shout whenever he wants."**

A reference to [dragon shouts](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md), which are unique to *Skyrim*. **"Why do soldiers bother with target practice? One learns best by hitting real people."**

Making fun of the various soldiers who can be seen "practicing" Archery by shooting at inanimate an target. This references the skill leveling system, in which you must hit valid targets to gain experience in combat skills. **"Something strange happens to [Khajiit](Skyrim_Khajiit.md) when they arrive in Skyrim."**

This may perhaps refer to the drastic redesigns of the beast races for *Skyrim*. **"[Werebears](Skyrim_Werebear.md)? Where? [Bears](Skyrim_Bear.md)? Men that are bears?"**

A reference to one of M'aiq's lines of dialogue in *Oblivion* that referenced werewolves. This is also a reference to pre-existing lore that alluded to the presence of werebears in Skyrim, which led some fans to the hope that players might be able to become werebears in *Skyrim*. Hostile werebears, as well as the ability to summon a werebear as an ally, were later added by the *[Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)* expansion. **"Dragons were never gone. They were just invisible and very, very quiet."**

A reference to his line in *Morrowind* saying that you have to fly high to see dragons because "the ones near the ground are very hard to see, being invisible". Or possibly a reference to how *Skyrim* initially handles dragons: they are technically present in the game data, but you cannot encounter them normally in the game world until the [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md) quest is complete. **"M'aiq once walked to [High Hrothgar](Skyrim_High_Hrothgar.md). So many steps, he lost count."**

A reference to the legendary 7,000-step mountain path that leads from [Ivarstead](Skyrim_Ivarstead.md) to High Hrothgar, and possibly a further reference to [Todd Howard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Todd_Howard)'s claim that he would take the time to count every individual step during playtests. **"M'aiq does not remember his childhood. Perhaps he never had one."**

Referencing the lack of children in previous games, as well as *Skyrim*'s lack of children from non-human races, such as Khajiit. **"M'aiq is very practical. He has no need for mysticism."**

Referencing the fact that *Oblivion*'s [Mysticism](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mysticism) spells were reassigned to other magic trees. **"[Nords](Skyrim_Nord.md)' armor has lots of fur. This sometimes makes M'aiq nervous."**

Simply a suggestion that, as a Khajiit, M'aiq is afraid that the Nords might hunt him for his fur. **"Some like taking friends on adventures. M'aiq thinks being alone is better. Less arguing about splitting treasure."**

The newest iteration of a classic quip by M'aiq, in which he criticizes the popular fan complaints about the lack of multiplayer and cooperative play modes in the *Elder Scrolls* games. **"Some say [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) is [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). Some say M'aiq is a Liar. Don't you believe either of those things."**

Likely a reference to how *Skyrim* has changed the lore concerning Alduin. Before *Skyrim*, it had been established that Alduin was just the Nordic variation on Akatosh, a different view on the same deity; *Skyrim* reinterpreted this to be a stereotype by non-Nords that Nords do not agree with, as shown in *[Alduin is Real](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Alduin_is_Real)* and *[The Alduin/Akatosh Dichotomy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Alduin_Akatosh_Dichotomy),* and gave strong evidence (such as [Paarthurnax](Skyrim_Paarthurnax_(dragon).md)'s dialogue) that the two are actually distinct beings. **"It does not matter to M'aiq how strong or smart one is. It only matters what one can do."**

References the fact that *Skyrim* characters do not have Attributes, such as Strength and Intelligence, that were present in previous games. **"M'aiq was soul trapped once. Not very pleasant. You should think about that once in a while."**

Referencing the [soul trap spell](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(spell).md) that you can use to trap a dead enemy's soul in a [soul gem](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md), which is useful for [enchanting](Skyrim_Enchanting.md). This also implies that souls trapped in gems can eventually return to their bodies--or that M'aiq has been reincarnated. **"Nords are so serious about beards. So many beards. M'aiq thinks they wish they had glorious manes like Khajiit."**

Reference to the fact that facial hair appears on many NPCs in *Skyrim* as well as being an option in the character creator. *Oblivion*, on the other hand, did not feature any bearded characters at all except for [Sheogorath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Sheogorath) from the *[Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles)* expansion; *Shivering Isles* also had [an easter egg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Sheogorath%27s_Punished) that lampoons the lack of bearded characters. **"M'aiq loves the people of Skyrim. Many interesting things they say to each other."**

Reference to how *Skyrim*'s larger voice cast and more natural dialogue system marked a significant improvement over *Oblivion*'s lack of different voice actors and diverse and fluent conversations, a major complaint of the community and critics of *Oblivion*. **"M'aiq can travel fast across the land. Some lazy types take carriages. It is all the same to M'aiq."**

Referencing the new [carriage](Skyrim_Carriage.md) system for travel in *Skyrim*, which was at some point [intended](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Developer_Mods_Kurt_Kuhlmann) to transport you in real time, but ended up very similar to the fast-travel system that was also present in *Oblivion*. Perhaps also a reference to *Oblivion*, where after talking to you he would run away at high speed. **"Once M'aiq got in trouble in [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md), and fled to [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md). It is good that nobody there cared."**

Refers to crime being tracked separately for each area of Skyrim; [guards](Skyrim_Guards.md) no longer try to arrest you for a crime you committed in another hold. **"The people of Skyrim are more open minded *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* about certain things than people in other places."**

Possibly refers either to the tolerance of [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Necromancy) and [skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skooma) in Skyrim compared to other provinces, or to the fact that *Skyrim*'s new [marriage](Skyrim_Marriage.md) system allows for both opposite-sex and same-sex marriages. **"M'aiq has heard it is dangerous to be your friend."**

Reference to the ability to recruit people as [followers](Skyrim_Followers.md) whom you've befriended, who will help you in battles and adventures—a very dangerous activity, given the frequent encounters with bandits, undead, dragons and many other life-threatening things. Also may be reference to the fact that there are a few instances in the game where you are encouraged to sacrifice your followers, such as [Boethiah's Calling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Boethiah%27s_Calling). **"M'aiq knows why [Falmer](Skyrim_Falmer.md) are blind. It has nothing to do with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) disappearing. Really."**

Refers to lore added in *Skyrim* which suggests that Falmer living in the lands beneath Skyrim were poisoned and enslaved by the Dwemer, leading the crippled Falmer to rebel and fight the Dwemer until the latter's disappearance. **"M'aiq's father was also called M'aiq. As was M'aiq's father's father. At least, that's what his father said."**

Referring to the fact that "M'aiq the Liar" appears in the previous two *Elder Scrolls* games which took place more than 200 years before *Skyrim*. Khajiit are not known to live this long, so this could imply that this version of M'aiq (and possibly others) is a descendant or possibly even reincarnation of a previous M'aiq. **"How does one know there was a city of [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold.md)? M'aiq did not see it with his eyes. Did you?"**

Likely refers to the "[Great Collapse](Lore_Great_Collapse.md)", which led to a majority of Winterhold falling into the [Sea of Ghosts](Skyrim_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), but may also be a reference to *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, in which Winterhold was still a major city, and in which M'aiq made no appearance. Alternatively, it could be a reference to the fact that there is not the slightest hint of the old city, such as ruins. It could also be a reference to how cities in *Skyrim* have to be discovered for fast travel, whereas they were already discovered from the start of the game in *Oblivion*. **"Don't try blocking if you have two weapons. You will only get confused. Much better to hit twice anyway."**

Reference to the fact that dual-wielding makes it impossible to [block](Skyrim_Block.md).
## Notes
- M'aiq can see you even when you are invisible.
- Unlike most NPCs, M'aiq will not become hostile to you when you are at stage four [vampirism](Skyrim_Vampirism.md). He will also still comment to you when you are in [werewolf form](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md) or in vampire lord form, and transforming in front of him does not trigger a bounty or cause him to become hostile.
- [Assaulting](Skyrim_Crime.md) M'aiq does not incur a bounty. This and his essential status combined with his pacifism make him an excellent target for gaining XP in combat and magical skills.
- When M'aiq isn't around Skyrim, he is located in a small holding cell called "Elsweyr", inaccessible to you.
- M'aiq has become a [running gag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Running_Gags). A M'aiq the Liar also appears in *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:M%27Aiq_the_Liar)*, *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:M%27aiq_the_Liar)*, and [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:M%27aiq_the_Liar). Please see [this page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:M%27aiq_the_Liar) for more information.

## Bugs
- Standing next to M'aiq but not initiating conservation can technically allow you to hear an unlimited amount of comments. This is rather difficult to do, though, as M'aiq is constantly on the move and won't say more if you exhaust his allotted quotes before exiting conversation. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.9, fixes this bug.
