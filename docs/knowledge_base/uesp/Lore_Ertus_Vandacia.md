Ertus Vandacia was a member of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and the High Priest of the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Vandacia rose to prominence when he assisted [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) in becoming emperor, which resulted in his appointment to the Elder Council as a reward for his service.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDRt EC-1)</sup> As the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame, Vandacia was one of the few who knew about the deal Durcorach struck with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), which involved creating the Daedric weapons known as the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJOEL-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CAJ-3)</sup> During the reign of Durcorach's son [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), Vandacia performed the Ritual of Empowerment, which created the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Wo D-4)</sup>

Moricar's son and successor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) was killed in the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577); however, prior to his death, he moved the Ambitions and did not tell Vandacia where he moved them to prevent them from falling into the hands of the revolt's leader, [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJOEL-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup> Following this, Vandacia ordered his Waking Flame cultists to find the Four Ambitions so that he could sacrifice them and initiate the merging of the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) and [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Po A-6)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Vandacia managed to sacrifice Destron, one of the Ambitions, which initiated the merging of the Deadlands and Nirn.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Po A-6)</sup> However, shortly afterward, Vandacia was killed by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and two of the other Ambitions, Sombren and Calia.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Po A-6)</sup>

## Biography

### Background
Ertus Vandacia was a member of the distinguished noble family, House Vandacia.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGt G-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup> House Vandacia owned extensive estates in [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md) and the borderlands of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and had deep roots in the region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGt G-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CEVd-9)</sup> Circa [2E 534](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_534), Vandacia was conferred the title of Imperial Governor of Gideon because he was one of the leading nobles of the region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGt G-7)</sup>

At some point, Vandacia became the High Priest of the Dagonic cult known as the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup> Vandacia was one of the sympathetic nobles and secret [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worshipers within the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) who helped the [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) warlord [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) strike a deal with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) to have him crowned Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup> In return for Mehrunes Dagon's assistance, Durcorach would oversee the rituals and preparations needed to create the Daedric weapons known as the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ADi S-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-XBo C-12)</sup> In [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533), Durcorach conquered Cyrodiil and was crowned emperor, and he rewarded Vandacia for his service and assistance by appointing him to the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) on 1 Sun's Dawn, 2E 534.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IDRt EC-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CEVd-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup>

### Elder Council and Creation of the Four Ambitions
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg/200px-LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-The_Elder_Council.jpg) Vandacia (second from the right) with other members of the Elder Council During his nearly fifty-year tenure as a member of the Elder Council, Vandacia helped guide the Empire through the reign of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md), and his record was considered impeccable.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CEVd-9)</sup>

After Durcorach's death, his son and successor, [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), inherited responsibility for the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-XBo C-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SMI-13)</sup> After Moricar inherited responsibility, the [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) Xynaa used the [Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md) to instruct Vandacia on how to perform the Ritual of Empowerment, which would enable the creation of the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-XBo C-12)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOSom-14)</sup> Five of Vandacia's fellow councilors worked on different aspects of the Four Ambitions, but as the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame, Vandacia was one of the only individuals who knew the entire truth about them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJOEL-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CAJ-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CTLd-15)</sup> During Moricar's reign, Vandacia instructed the Emperor on how to create the Four Ambitions, and members of the Waking Flame conducted the required preparations and rituals.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-EMd-16)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Weapons_of_Destruction_08.jpg) Vandacia performing the Ritual of Empowerment on Calia and Destron In [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561), Moricar, his son [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), and the Waking Flame prepared the final rituals and sacrifices to Mehrunes Dagon that would see the creation of the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup> In the same year, Vandacia, accompanied by the Emperor, performed the Ritual of Empowerment on the twins Calia and Destron.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Wo D-4)</sup> To complete the ritual, Vandacia sacrificed the parents of Calia and Destron, Ramin and Lestra Brolus, imbuing the twins with parts of Mehrunes Dagon's [egonymic](Lore_Egonymic.md) and transforming them into Ambitions.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Wo D-4)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-EMd-16)</sup> Around this time, Vandacia performed the Ritual of Empowerment on Sombren and [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md), transforming them into the two other Ambitions.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOXd-17)</sup> While performing the Ritual of Empowerment, Vandacia took a little of Dagon's egonymic for himself, granting him great power.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOSom-14)</sup>

In [2E 564](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_564), Moricar died, and his son Leovic succeeded him as emperor, inheriting responsibility for the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SMI-13)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DID-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ELd D-19)</sup> In [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), Leovic issued a [decree](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic%27s_Great_Spirits_Proclamation) that legalized Daedra worship in the Empire, which triggered a [revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) led by [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), Duke of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) and Count of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CTLd-15)</sup> Leovic moved the Ambitions to new locations in response to the revolt's advance toward the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), but he did not tell Vandacia where he moved them to prevent them from falling into Varen's hands.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJOEL-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ELd D-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-CJR-20)</sup> Leovic informed his steward, Farrul Lupus, of every detail of the Four Ambitions and placed documents in hidden caches so that Vandacia and the Waking Flame could continue the plan in the event of his death.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TJOEL-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DID-18)</sup> In [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Varen and his forces stormed the Imperial City and killed Leovic in the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), and Varen immediately declared himself emperor.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHot LE-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CTLd-15)</sup>

Vandacia continued to serve on the Elder Council during Varen's reign and [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md)'s regency.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Mot EC-21)</sup> Vandacia did not attend the Elder Council meeting that was held near the beginning of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) on 16 Second Seed.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Mot EC-21)</sup> Instead, he sent a letter to the council saying that he had elected to remain in Gideon, where he believed he could be more effective in helping manage the [iron](Lore_Iron.md) crisis in the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Mot EC-21)</sup> Following the outbreak of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580) and the fall of the Imperial City to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s Daedric forces circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Empire collapsed, and the Elder Council dissolved.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CATAYQ2-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-23)</sup>

### Search for the Four Ambitions
After the collapse of the Empire and the dissolution of the Elder Council, Vandacia returned to his holdings in Gideon and began serving as an advisor and bureaucrat to the city's governor, [Keshu the Black Fin](Lore_Keshu_the_Black_Fin.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CEVd-9)</sup> Keshu regarded him as a trusted advisor and appreciated his willingness to share knowledge.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOKt BFd-24)</sup> By 2E 582, Vandacia wielded an unprecedented level of power in the city.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Mt Cs TEC-8)</sup>

Before his death, Leovic did not transfer responsibility for the Four Ambitions to an heir, thereby activating the codicils described in the Mysterium Xarxes, which stipulated that whoever sacrificed the Four Ambitions could claim the reward previously promised to the Longhouse Emperors.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-XBo C-12)</sup> Sacrificing the Ambitions would enable Mehrunes Dagon to conquer and merge [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) with the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), and Dagon would reward whoever sacrificed them by allowing them to rule [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), which was the reward formerly promised to the Longhouse Emperors.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SMI-13)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-EMd-16)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOEVd-25)</sup> Therefore, after Leovic's death, Vandacia and the Waking Flame began searching for the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOLd-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESODEd-27)</sup>

To locate and preserve the secret of the Four Ambitions, he ordered his Waking Flame cultists to kill anybody with knowledge of them—that being Leovic's former steward, Farrul Lupus, and Councilors Abor, Faleria, [Itinia](Lore_Itinia.md), Jirich, and Velan Sophus—and then retrieve their documents regarding the Ambitions.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup> The high priest also ordered the cultists to find Leovic's hidden caches.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-AMT-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TES-29)</sup> Finally, Vandacia dispatched his personal guard, a group known as the True-Sworn, to Durcorach's abandoned estate on the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md), Black Drake Villa, with orders to retrieve the Mysterium Xarxes and destroy any documents regarding the Four Ambitions.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESOPEd-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-CMTSS-31)</sup>

The True-Sworn burned much of Black Drake Villa, but before they could recover the Mysterium Xarxes, they were defeated by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md); Eveli then retrieved the book.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-BS-32)</sup> The Waking Flame killed Lupus, Abor, Jirich, and Sophus; however, they were unable to kill Faleria and Itinia.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TES-29)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ADS-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-DZJ-34)</sup> Vandacia ordered the cultists to frame the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) for the murders to deflect suspicion from the Order of the Waking Flame for as long as possible.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-HPO-35)</sup> Vandacia also staged an attack on himself by having his personal guard capture the Dark Brotherhood assassin Elam Drals.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Pt S-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CEVd-9)</sup> After capturing Drals, Vandacia and his guards took him to Keshu and the Black Fin Legion at the Gideon Courthouse, where they imprisoned the assassin.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CEVd-9)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOKt BFd-24)</sup>

The Vestige then arrived at the courthouse and informed Vandacia that [Tarnian Lovidicus](Lore_Tarnian_Lovidicus.md), the former chief councilor of the Elder Council, wanted him to go to [Castle Leyawiin](Lore_Castle_Leyawiin.md) because of the assassinations of their former Elder Council colleagues and the potential threat to his life. Vandacia initially refused to travel [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), but he eventually agreed.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ADS-33)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-An_Unexpected_Adversary_04.jpg) Vandacia strangling Lovidicus with magic After Vandacia traveled to Castle Leyawiin, the Vestige and Eveli Sharp-Arrow went to [Bloodrun Cave](Lore_Bloodrun_Cave.md) to deal with the Brotherhood sanctuary there.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AUA-36)</sup> After the Vestige and Eveli departed, the high priest commanded the Waking Flame members at the [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) of [Welke](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welke) to follow them to Bloodrun Cave, kill them and the Brotherhood assassins, recover the cache Leovic hid in the cave, and retrieve the Mysterium Xarxes from Eveli.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-HPO-35)</sup> The cultists attacked the Brotherhood sanctuary, where they recovered Leovic's cache, which provided them with the information they needed to locate the first Ambition. However, they failed to kill the Vestige, Eveli, and all the assassins.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AUA-36)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-DZJ-34)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DZASc-37)</sup> Afterward, the Waking Flame attacked Castle Leyawiin. In the chaos, Vandacia took the other elder councilors' encoded documents related to the Four Ambitions from the scribe Beragon, who was in the process of decoding them.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-DZJ-34)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AUA-36)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-23)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ESOBd-38)</sup> Vandacia then killed Faleria and attempted to strangle Lovidicus with magic in the castle courtyard, but when the Vestige and Eveli arrived, the high priest summoned the Disciple of Aspiration and teleported away.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AUA-36)</sup>

### Attempts to Secure the Four Ambitions
After Vandacia and the Waking Flame decoded the documents to learn the locations of the Four Ambitions, cultists were sent to Doomvault Capraxus, which housed two of the Ambitions, Calia and Destron. The Vestige, Eveli, and [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md) reached Calia and Destron in Doomvault Capraxus before the Waking Flame, and they, along with the twins, killed the cultists. Following that, Eveli took Calia and Destron to the [Temple of Dibella](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md) in Gideon to protect them from the Waking Flame.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-AHV-39)</sup> The Waking Flame did manage to capture the Ambition, Sombren, when they ambushed him after he returned to his vault.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOSom-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-quest-A_Mysterious_Event_13.jpg/200px-ON-quest-A_Mysterious_Event_13.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Mysterious_Event_13.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-A_Mysterious_Event_13.jpg) Vandacia preparing to sacrifice Sombren After Sombren's capture, Vandacia took him to his keep in the Deadlands, which Mehrunes Dagon had awarded him. Before Vandacia could sacrifice Sombren, the Vestige and Eveli interrupted, freed Sombren, and took him to the Temple of Dibella in Gideon to protect him.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-AME-40)</sup>

Eventually, Vandacia learned that the Ambitions were located in Gideon. As a result, Vandacia began assembling his cultists in preparation for an attack on Gideon, with the aim of capturing the Ambitions. The high priest assembled a large force for the assault, including guards from Gideon's garrison who were secretly loyal to him.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOKt BFd-24)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-23)</sup>

### Battle at Fort Redmane and Death
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-npc-Councilor_Vandacia_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Councilor_Vandacia_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Councilor_Vandacia_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Councilor_Vandacia_02.jpg) Vandacia at Fort Redmane Due to Keshu's concerns about treachery in Gideon and the poor condition of the city walls, the Ambitions relocated to [Fort Redmane](Lore_Fort_Redmane.md), which would be easier to defend against Vandacia's forces.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOCRLd-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOKt BFd-24)</sup> This did not deter the Waking Flame, who used portals to launch an assault on the fort. Vandacia found the Ambitions in the fort's inner bailey, where he sacrificed Destron, initiating the merging of the Deadlands and Nirn and enabling Mehrunes Dagon to participate in the assault. After they regrouped, the Vestige, Sombren, and Calia confronted Vandacia in the fort's inner courtyard, where they fought and killed the high priest, stopping the merge of the Deadlands and Nirn.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Po A-6)</sup>

## Legacy
The significance of Vandacia allowed him to be documented within the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md) in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). There, copies of various entities were subsequently created as [maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), creatures composed of vivified ink manifested by stories from books, and served as mindless extensions of the [Daedric Lord](Lore_Daedric_Lord.md) [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md). A maligraphy copy of Vandacia was formed, which was destroyed by an adventurer or two who were aided by augmentations directly from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-LAIA-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-MM-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-CVIA-43)</sup>
