# Dawnway

—King [Nantharion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nantharion)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nantharion-1)</sup> The Dawnway is a philosophy and way of life that emerged among the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). It stood in contrast to the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) as an alternate way of life for followers of [the Green](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green). The Dawnway is primarily associated with the Dawnwood, a jungled region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) which followers of the Dawnway regarded as their historic homeland.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Darolith-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/ON-npc-Nothelas.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Nothelas.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nothelas.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Nothelas.jpg) Nothelas, a Dawnway priest The Dawnway can trace its origins to the tradition of dawnwalking, which had been maintained by Bosmeri families who could trace their lineages back to the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vi Va B-3)</sup> The philosophy emerged circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) when the forgotten [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia) escaped from her imprisonment in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), resulting in the denizens of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) remembering her existence for the first time since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). A cult dedicated to Ithelia emerged as a result, and began calling themselves the [Recollection](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Recollection).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Members of the Recollection were primarily Bosmer who placed great importance in their ancestral links to the Ayleid Diaspora; many Bosmer can trace their ancestry back to Ayleid refugees who fled Cyrodiil following the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). Over the centuries, the diaspora in Valenwood were required to adopt the Green Pact and were gradually absorbed into the native Bosmer population.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> The Recollection were secretly led by [Nantharion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nantharion) of [Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silvenar), who persuaded a group of Bosmer to migrate to the newly-grown Dawnwood in Cyrodiil. In truth, the Recollection were responsible for the growth of the Dawnwood through the use of corrupted greenspring rituals. Nantharion founded the city of Vashabar and proclaimed himself king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nantharion-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONantharions Gambit-6)</sup>

Unlike Green Pact Bosmer, followers of the Dawnway had no qualms about cultivating crops or harvesting wood from the forest.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Darolith-2)</sup> They would make use of deadwood in place of traditional Bosmeri crafting materials when required.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> These differences would sometimes bring Dawnway Bosmer into conflict with followers of the Green Pact, who were otherwise welcomed to Dawnwood as neighbors.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOVoice Village-8)</sup> In some cases, fervent Green Pact followers were even banished from Vashabar by the Dawnway Bosmer.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Phythia-9)</sup> However, Dawnway priests still revered [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre) and the Green,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> and followers of the philosophy still made use of [Greenspeakers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenspeakers) in the construction of tree-pod dwellings.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vi Va B-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Some Green Pact followers, while recognizing that the Dawnway also revered the Green, considered their focus on Ayleid heritage foolish and unimportant, disapproving of it taking precedence over honoring the Green.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Phythia-9)</sup> Dawnway Bosmer would still defer to ancient Bosmeri rites and customs when invoked, such as the [Rite of Propagation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rite_of_Propagation).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOVoice Village-8)</sup> Some gods from the mainstream Bosmeri pantheon, like [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara), were also worshipped in Dawnway settlements such as Vashabar, though their worship took on a distinct form that differed from how these deities were revered elsewhere.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-GN-13)</sup> The Dawnway followers of Vashabar maintained Daedric religious idols of the Ayleids.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Gandrinar-14)</sup>

King Nantharion and the Recollection planned to expand the Greenway to cover the entirety of the [West Weald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:West_Weald) and attempted to found a fortified settlement at [Hoperoot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoperoot). When his treachery was revealed, Nantharion brought Ithelia to Vashabar, where she opened an entrance to the lost Ayleid ruins of [Miscarcand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miscarcand) below. Nantharion was killed within the ruins, ending the threat posed by the Recollection.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONantharions Gambit-6)</sup>

The Dawnway Bosmer also established another settlement at [Strid River Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strid_River_Valley), built on the site of an older Imperial village. However, it was short-lived, as it was decimated by the Daedric crisis that followed.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRV2-15)</sup>

In the wake of Nantharion's death, leadership of the Bosmer population in Vashabar was given to Greenspeaker Darolith, a follower of the Green Pact. Darolith expressed hoped that the Dawnway was not inherently flawed as a philosophy in the absence of Ithelia's continued influence.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Darolith-2)</sup> All knowledge of Ithelia's existence was subsequently wiped by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Despite this, remnants of "Nantharion's movement" continued to linger in some corners of Valenwood. In her role as [Green Lady](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Lady), [Gwaering](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gwaering) warned King [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran) that the spirit of the Bosmer people remained divided.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Dawnwood no longer existed within the borders of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> It is unknown if any Bosmer continued to follow the Dawnway.

## Notes
- The Dawnway and dawnwalking are likely named for the dawn-magic of the Ayleids. As a culture, the Ayleids of Cyrodiil preserved the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) magics and language of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) that other elves forgot.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>
- Dawnwood Tenders are followers of the Dawnway who have covered their bodies in a binding mark to signify their resolve in following their Ayleid ancestor heritage. These markings resemble blue, full-body tattoos depicting the [Blessed Life-Tree](Lore_Blessed_Life-Tree.md), a common Ayleid symbol.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ARBLT-22)</sup>
