**Marked for Death** is a [shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) that temporarily reduces an enemy's health and [armor rating](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Armor_Rating), making them more vulnerable to physical damage. Each word further decreases the target's armor rating by 25 points, which means they will take 3% extra physical damage per word. All three ranks have a duration of 60 seconds.

This shout's effects can be mitigated or amplified by the target's [magic resistance](Skyrim_Resist_Magic.md).

## Related Quests
- **[Destroy the Dark Brotherhood!](Skyrim_Destroy_the_Dark_Brotherhood!.md)**: Finish the [Dark Brotherhood](Skyrim_Dark_Brotherhood.md) and rid Skyrim of their kind.

## Notes
- This shout can cause the target's armor rating to become negative, which results in them taking increased damage from all physical sources.
- As with other shouts, [Companion's Insight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Companion%27s_Insight) will protect followers from the effects of Marked for Death.

## Bugs
- The voice acting for this shout is incorrect; the character will say "Lun Lun" for the second rank and "Lun Lun Aus" for the third rank. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.0, fixes this bug.
- The word wall of "Krii" has some of the text cut off. Both the "R" in "ROKei" and the "M" in "MOROKei," though still legible, are only half letters. Based on its translation, it is probable that "ROKei" is actually "MOROKei" and the line that begins with "MOROKei" is actually "KO MOROKei."
- The word wall in Forsaken Cave has been known to go through all cinematics, with the player getting a message saying they have learned the word. However the word does not become available for the player to unlock. Leaving the cave and returning does not allow the cinematic to re-run.
- This shout may fail to stack with itself properly. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.629, fixes this bug.
- The target's armor rating does not recover after the shout ends, and is damaged by 75 *per second,* rather than 75 total. This allows you to stack the shout multiple times on the same enemy to deal increasingly higher damage. Conversely, if the shout hits your follower or other NPCs, they will permanently take increased damage from physical attacks. **Mod Notes**: The damage armor effect does not have the "Recover" flag. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.629.0.8, fixes this bug.

- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) This bug is fixed by version 1.1 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch).
- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) This can be fixed by opening the [console](Skyrim_Console.md), selecting the affected actor, and using the command `restoreav damageresist 9999999`.
- Learning [Drain Vitality](Skyrim_Drain_Vitality.md) before learning all three words of Marked for Death may cause Marked for Death to become glitched so no newer words may be learned. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch), version 1.1.2, fixes this bug.
- Learning a word of Marked for Death after installing Dawnguard causes a glitched shout to appear in the Shouts menu. It is named Drain Vitality, but has all three words of Marked for Death. The recharge is 12,12,12 and the bugged description says "Drains 1 points of Health per second for 20 seconds. Drains 1 points of Magicka per second for 20 seconds. Drains 1 points of". Using the shout causes the effect listed, although no words are actually shouted by the character. See the bug section of the page [Drain Vitality](Skyrim_Drain_Vitality.md) for fixes. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch), version 1.1.2, fixes this bug.
