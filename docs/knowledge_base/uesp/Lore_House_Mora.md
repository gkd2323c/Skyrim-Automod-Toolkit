[![](https://images.uesp.net/e/e2/MW-banner-House_Hlaalu.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-House_Hlaalu.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-House_Hlaalu.png) Banner of House Hlaalu House Mora was a [prominent house](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) active as late as the mid-early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-1)</sup> until it was absorbed by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRB4-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-IWTB-3)</sup> Its most well-known members were the [Ra'athim](Lore_Ra%27athim.md) clan, who held lucrative mining fiefs and the High Kingship of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_King)</sup> House Mora's lineage contained traces of [human](Lore_Man.md) blood, but to what degree is unclear.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRB4-2)</sup>

## History
When House Mora was founded is unknown, however, the modern houses of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) evolved from the nomadic Velothi tribes of the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BTAOM-4)</sup> House Mora was present for the [Conquest of Morrowind](Lore_Conquest_of_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-1)</sup> the [Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md)'s legendary invasion of the east.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-5)</sup> A member of House Mora, a [rose-worn prince of garlands](Lore_Moraelyn.md), was a warhero for their efforts in the conflict.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-1)</sup>

The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) historically had ties with the Dark Elves through a trade deal with Clan R'aathim and the Kings of High Rock, where the retainers of King Moraelyn of Ebonheart were allowed to mine in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) for [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and start a colony for their people in High Rock in exchange for providing ebony weapons to the Breton lords and teaching the Bretons how to smith ebony themselves.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KE-6)</sup> House Mora of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) and its Clan R'aathim notably had human blood in its family tree, before that house fell and was adopted into the House Hlaalu.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRB5-7)</sup> There are some sources that suggest [King Edward](Lore_Edward.md) of Daggerfall was the son of the Breton [Queen Aliera](Lore_Aliera.md) of Wayrest and King Moraelyn.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Jindra-8)</sup>

As told in his [36 lessons](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md), in the years following the [Skyrim Conquests](Lore_Skyrim_Conquests.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) traveled across Morrowind to hunt down the seven strongest of his [monster children](Lore_Monster_Children.md), born from his unholy union with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-36LOVS12-9)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-36LOVS18-10)</sup> One of these monsters, the [Treasure Wood Sword](Lore_Treasure_Wood_Sword.md) became a "lessoning tune" to the lower Velothi houses who preached its power. By the time Vivec found it, it was in House Mora's possession and so he appeared as a visitation in their ancestor alcove. There a [bonewalker](Lore_Bonewalker.md) appeared before them and said the Treasure Wood Sword will not leave with them as they made a bargain with [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-1)</sup>

Unpleased by this, Vivec left and employed the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) to commit a mass killing spree against House Mora, taking place across the Ra'athim clan's holdings.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-1)</sup> At the height of their power, their influence extended between [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) and [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRB4-2)</sup> In time, the King of Assassins presented Vivec with the Treasure Wood Sword after killing the prince of House Mora. As quoted from Vivec's 22nd sermon, he states *"The prince of House Mora is now fond of you, as well. I placed him in the Corner of [Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). His eyes I set into a fire prayer for the wicked. His mouth I stuffed with birds."*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-1)</sup>

House Mora's history after this is unknown. [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) expressed interest in a notable [enchantress](Lore_Enchanting.md) of their line, but whether they survived the Morag Tong's mass execution is also unknown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-36LOVS22-1)</sup> They ultimately went extinct after it was absorbed by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRB4-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-IWTB-3)</sup> but the Ra'athim clan continued to live on throughout the eras and ruled Ebonheart as late as the second century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TMOP-11)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_King) | The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) never acknowledged a king like the [Empires](Lore_Empire.md) of the west.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRDeath-12)</sup> However, the Imperial title of the King of Morrowind stems from an archaic title in early Chimer traditions of a "high chief of the clans".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-KRP-13)</sup> Holders of the Imperial title, [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md), and [Helseth](Lore_Helseth.md) trace their lineages all the way to the Ra'athim clan.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-IWTB-3)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- The word, *Mora*, in most variations of the elven language, including the [Dunmer language](Lore_Dunmer_Language.md), translates into either *Forest* or *Wood*.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWSul-14)</sup> In some instances of [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md), the word can also mean *[Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md)*.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-NSP-15)</sup>
- In a forum post, developer Douglas Goodall once gave [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md)'s full and formal name as "Serjo Indoril Nerevar Mora", indicating a possible association with House Mora.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-FPgoodall-16)</sup>
