The Snow Prince was a [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) of great fighting prowess, decisiveness, and elegance who lived on the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). His kin had great respect for him, and hailed him as their savior in the [Battle of the Moesring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Moesring.md). The Snow Prince killed many [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) in the battle with his ice magic and his spear, but was struck down by Finna, a twelve-year-old girl, who threw her mother Jofrior's sword at him after she had seen the Prince slay her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fot SP-1)</sup>

The Nords won the battle, but out of respect and awe they did not burn the body of the Snow Prince with the rest of his kin. Instead, they buried him in a freshly dug barrow, which later became known as Jolgeirr Barrow, along with his steel armor and spear. His body was not encased in [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) as Nordic tradition dictated, but the tomb was lined with treasures worthy of royalty and guards were assigned to protect it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Fot SP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OWN-2)</sup> News of his death spread across the land, reaching even the isolated and scattered tribes of Snow Elves across Skyrim.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-JOMA-3)</sup> Due to the Atmorans destroying many Falmeri documents, next to nothing is known about the Snow Elves' governmental structure and what duties the Prince had.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sot SS-4)</sup>

The Snow Prince ordered the creation of a stone throne designed with simple lines, leading some scholars to believe this was reflective of his personality. It was excavated by the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sot SS-4)</sup>

Sometime before the tomb of Jolgeirr Barrow was abandoned, the guardians became aware of Angria "and her rabble" planning to steal the Snow Prince's steel armor. Heinlen the Heavy's men drove the graverobbers away, although they made off with the ancient helmet. Heinlen tried to warn Winter-Fist,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OWN-2)</sup> but ended up at the bottom of [Lake Fjalding](Lore_Lake_Fjalding.md), likely murdered by the thieves.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Angria's corpse was found in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), hanging from the ceiling of her hideout, the Frykte ice cave.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> In the same year, Jolgeirr Barrow was rediscovered and looted. *[Fall of the Snow Prince](Lore_Fall_of_the_Snow_Prince.md)*, an ancient account of the Battle of the Moesring taken by [Lokheim](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Lokheim), was uncovered within and came into the possession of Athellor, an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) scholar of the thought-to-be-extinct Snow Elves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Bloodmoon-7)</sup> The document was later published and had become popular literature in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) by [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Fot SPOblivion-8)</sup> and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Fot SPSkyrim-9)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Spear of the Snow Prince
The [Spear of the Snow Prince](Lore_Spear_of_the_Snow_Prince.md) is a powerful steel weapon once wielded by the legendary [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elves.md) Snow Prince. The spear was imbued with a potent [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) that both inflicted frost damage and left those it struck more vulnerable to the chill, while simultaneously corroding and disintegrating their armor. [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) accounts describe both the Snow Prince's spear and his armor as bearing a radiant, fearsome glow of unknown magicka, causing him to appear more wight than warrior.

## Notes
- Prior to the release of *[Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)*, strings pulled from *Skyrim*'s [Patch 1.5.26](Skyrim_Patch.md#Version_1.5.26.0) indicated that a Snow Elf Prince would appear as a character. As development continued, the sideplot evolved into [Gelebor](Lore_Gelebor.md) and [Vyrthur](Lore_Vyrthur.md)'s stories as priests of Auri-El rather than the Prince appearing, though remnants of the character remained in the form of Vyrthur's throne and the [Ancient Falmer Crown](Skyrim_Ancient_Falmer_Crown.md).
