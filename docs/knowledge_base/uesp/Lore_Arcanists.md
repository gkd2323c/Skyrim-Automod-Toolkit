# Arcanists

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-render-Arcanist.jpg/200px-ON-render-Arcanist.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arcanist.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arcanist.jpg) An arcanist with their tome Arcanists are [spellcasters](Lore_Magic.md) who wield runic spellforms learned from a tome touched by the forces of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), the [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) realm of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). This tome is both the source of knowledge from which the arcanist learns of the spells they wield and the vector through which these spells are cast. In other words, their power is derived from the book itself: knowledge alone is one thing, but arcanists are unique in that the tome they wield is integral to their spellcasting. All mages use runes of some kind, but the runework wielded by arcanists is extremely elaborate on top of producing unique feats of spellwork.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WA-1)</sup> Some arcanists have been known to use their power to [scry](Lore_Sight.md) or manipulate the very [threads of fate](Lore_Fate.md). <sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Leramil-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup> Scruut, a [Watchling](Lore_Watcher.md) fate auditor in the service of Hermaeus Mora, believed that influencing fate in this manner was a power not meant for mortals, and that it could potentially result in the undoing of the mortal that did so.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Scruut-5)</sup>

## The Arcanist's Tome
Every arcanist's tome is unique.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Twit Archive-6)</sup> The knowledge in each tome is suited to the mind and soul of each individual arcanist: if one arcanist were to attempt to read another's book, they would find it impossible to learn from. Just like the tomes themselves, the process of becoming an arcanist is equally unique—no arcanist discovers their tome the same way as another, and the bond formed between a tome and its caster is wholly unique.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup> Some hypothesize that these tomes pieces or debris of Apocrypha itself, and that this bond is a piece of Oblivion finding him in Nirn, and in a mortal's mind.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WA-1)</sup> As well, the tomes seemingly have a mind of their own: they communicate with their wielders. For at least one Arcanist, this communication wasn't done through audible words but not all tomes "behave" the same way.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup>

The contents and context within the tomes often shifts, showing arcanists things they should and shouldn't know respectively. The tomes can also travel of their own accord, occasionally teleporting to different locations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AANote4-7)</sup> Arcanist tomes are not [Black Books](Lore_Black_Books.md),<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ESO2023Global Reveal-8)</sup> though Hermaeus Mora himself has compared the two: both are forms of knowledge that he allows into [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ETHMR-9)</sup> The Prince of Knowledge is capable of communicating with arcanists through these tomes.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AANote5-10)</sup>

### The Arcanist's Arsenal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/ON-concept-Arcanist_Symbols.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Arcanist_Symbols.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Arcanist_Symbols.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Arcanist_Symbols.jpg) Arcanist's runic symbols Runes and [language](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Languages) are the foundation from which an arcanist's power is derived. The arcane spellforms learned from and driven by these tomes enable an arcanist to command runeforms, project beams that scar the world in front of them, conjure nigh impenetrable spell armor matrices and command restorative magics deemed to be inventive by mortal standards. Arcanists who specialize in defensive runeforms are known to refer to themselves as "Soldiers of Apocrypha", whilst restorative and supportive runecrafting is known as "Curative Runeforms". However, most of all, they're best known for their beams of eldritch power.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Bahtra Hun-11)</sup> These powers may be used in various and distinctive ways by each arcanist but it appears that the notion of etching a rune on reality to modify its form seems to be universal among them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WA-1)</sup> Arcanists carved runes representing specific beings to manifest unique spells.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CHak Abil-12)</sup>

The runework of an arcanist is believed to be a universal language between the spellcasters, however, this is untrue: an arcanist's runework is wholly unique to that individual despite the shared symbols. They don't have specific, exact meanings like [enchantment runestones](Lore_Enchanting.md#Runestones_and_Glyphs) but instead are symbols for the metamagical subconscious of the arcanist. A sigil that to one arcanist might mean "fire", to another could mean something entirely different, despite using the same rune.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup> Arcanists who specialize in runes are sometimes referred to as "rune-carvers".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Tentacles.png/200px-ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Tentacles.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Tentacles.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Tentacles.png) An arcanist summoning tentacles Runes are not all an arcanist wields, as tentacles are just as much of a common sight in their arcane repertoire due to their connection with Mora's realm. These creations are summoned not just for self-defense but also to lash out at foes. They're considered both awe-inspiring and disturbing by some due to their eldritch appearance and memorable auditory and olfactory qualities.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WA-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ASShields-13)</sup> This kind of spellcasting is sometimes designated as the "tentacular school of thought".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup>

The Abyssal Sea of Mora's realm, with endless secrets drowned in its depths is a powerful source of the eldritch for arcanists, who channel and bend its waters to their will. Those who wield these depths are sometimes derogatorily referred to as "tiresome paddlers"<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup> The very concept of fate itself is a precise tool in an arcanist's spellbook, and some have reported to seeing them manipulate possibility around them in unique and creative ways, extending even to the fate-manipulation of an arcanist's very soul. This skill is considered a faint echo of the "fate sight" believed to be held by Mora themself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WA-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Arc Abilities Fate-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Crux.png/200px-ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Crux.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Crux.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Arcanist_Crux.png) An arcanist's "crux" floating around them For the arcanist, spellcasting consists of a good deal of hand-waving. Renowned arcanist Azandar al-Cybiades surmised that the reason arcanists tend to favor sweeping hand-motions is due to the nature of Apocrypha itself. As al-Cybiades described it, magicka flows through an arcanist's mind while spellcasting with a robustness not experienced quite as regularly by other mages. All the extra potentiality generated by the arcanist's eldritch power needs to be directed somewhere: as a result, an arcanist may find themselves inadvertently summoning tentacles if the motions they make to cast a less taxing spell (such as conjuring flame to heat water for a cup of tea) are a little too forceful.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup>

When compared to the schools of magic, the arcanist's spellcraft would fall under the schools of [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md) and [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/ON-trailer-Arcanist_Tome.jpeg/200px-ON-trailer-Arcanist_Tome.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Arcanist_Tome.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-Arcanist_Tome.jpeg) An arcanist's tome One becomes an arcanist when an eldritch tome somehow finds its way to Nirn, ultimately seeking out and binding itself to a mortal [soul](Lore_Soul.md) which perfectly matches its arcane resonance.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup> At least one known arcanist has "accidentally" walked this path when an arcanist's tome "bit" him.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup> Those who become arcanists are said to align with Apocrypha as easily as one learns to breathe or speak.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ETHMR-9)</sup> Despite their innate connection with Hermaeus Mora, not all arcanists are devout followers of the Prince: some arcanists outright hate or distrust him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WA-1)</sup>

Arcanists are diverse and unique people with different methods to their path. Some view it as a new way of life, others in a more methodical and scholarly manner. Others still crave knowledge as deeply as the Prince whose realm their power stems from, and some take up an isolated life of scholarship.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup> Many see this magic as a means to an end, a way of defending themselves or achieving unique goals, while others pledge themselves fully to the One-Who-Knows in mind, body and soul, resulting in blatant Daedric corruption.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup>

Being an arcanist can be a troubling experience at times: they may occasionally experience vivid sensory phenomena, such as feeling the waters of the abyssal sea rushing through their head. Regardless, most arcanists find that the price they pay for eldritch knowledge is worth it for the power they wield.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup> It is customary for arcanists to establish sanctums to practice their craft.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Azandar Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Azandar Sanctum-16)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Martina Arcanist Sanctum-17)</sup>

## History
Arcanists gained prominence in the academic world during the Interregnum. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), more arcanists than ever before utilized the craft in the public sphere, but they are believed to have existed for much longer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WA-1)</sup> Acclaimed scholar and arcanist Azandar al-Cybiades theorized that a change in the fabric of reality caused this influx of new arcanists, with the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) being cited as a potential catalyst for the sheer number of tomes that traveled to Nirn in such short time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup> Arcanists in this time were known to style their hair in an uncomplicated crest.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ACrest-18)</sup> Arcanists sometimes used ebony dice sets representing various concepts, with the point of tentacles representing good or bad luck being one of contention.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ADice Set-19)</sup>

[Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md) had an influence on the arcanist's craft in the Second Era, with his obscure magics making their way into a common arcanist's repertoire.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-20)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ASZED-21)</sup> Other figures such as the [Blind Man‎‎](Lore_Blind_Man.md), [Fate Crone](Lore_Fate_Crone.md), and [Baron of Breakers](Lore_Baron_of_Breakers.md) can be invoked and contacted by arcanists seeking power and knowledge.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CHak Abil-12)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-AANote6-22)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AANote7-23)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AANote8-24)</sup> At some point in history, the [Rune Knights of the Purgatory Disquisition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rune_Knights_of_the_Purgatory_Disquisition) wielded and perfected a variety of runic spellcraft which found itself in the skillset of arcanists during the Interregnum.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arc LA-4)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-RSWAbil-25)</sup>
