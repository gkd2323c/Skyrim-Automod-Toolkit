# Fighters Guild

We of the Guild will always fight.
For good or evil, day or night,
We of the Guild will always fight.
… Just so long as the price is right!*
—Fighters Guild creed<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Atavar-1)</sup> [![OB-banner-Fighters Guild.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/OB-banner-Fighters_Guild.png/200px-OB-banner-Fighters_Guild.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Fighters_Guild.png) The Fighters Guild, present across most of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), provides a common and, more importantly, public place of training, study, and employment for those of a martial persuasion. The guild is a professional organization chartered by the Emperor to regulate the hiring and training of mercenaries, protect commerce, capture or drive away beasts, and similar security duties.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FGC-2)</sup> Guild halls can take on a contract from any citizen, provided it does not conflict with the laws or customs of the region.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBLoading-3)</sup>

The Fighters Guild is highly organized in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and is run by a ruling family, the Dontons. They have had numerous rivals over the years, including the dubious [Blackwood Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackwood_Company),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTL-4)</sup> and outmaneuvering them has kept the Guild performing at its best. In the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), the Fighters Guild were allies with the [Citadel of Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Citadel_of_Ebonarm) and in northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), some Fighters Guild chapters are dedicated holy places to [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm) called Fighter Trainers Guildhalls.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFEbonarm-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFGuild-6)</sup> The Fighters Guild previously had a presence in Skyrim, at least in the Interregnum,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONGuild-7)</sup> but by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), there were no active chapters. Instead, mercenaries-for-hire were found with the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRLoad-8)</sup>

The Guild gained greater prominence at the beginning of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) under [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_I.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-9)</sup> Guild Stewards are entrusted with receiving and dispensing contracts to Guild members, hearing complaints about the servicing of said contracts, promotions, and determining the eligibility for re-admittance into the Guild of former members who had stolen from or assaulted other members.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FGC-2)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/ON-banner-Fighters_Guild_02.png/200px-ON-banner-Fighters_Guild_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Fighters_Guild_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Fighters_Guild_02.png) Fighter's Guild Banner (ESO) The Fighters Guild was conceived in [2E 320](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_320) by [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie) at the suggestion of [Dinieras-Ves, "The Iron"](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dinieras-Ves) to provide for public order without having to resort to standing armies.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-HOTFG-10)</sup> At first, it was called [The Syffim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Syffim) ('soldiers' in [Tsaesci](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ehlnofex_Languages)). Although it began as an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) -only organization, it eventually opened its doors to all races.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-HOTFG-10)</sup>

During the time of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), the Fighters Guild was contracted to destroy [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) sent to Tamriel by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). The guild master at the time had ties to the daedric prince [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), and is reflected with the highest ranking members, who were given the power to summon [Dawnbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dawnbreaker) in the midst of battle to aid them in fulfilling this contract.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESO-11)</sup>

### Third Era

#### Hammerfell
In the years prior to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), [Queen Akorithi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akorithi) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) was saved from an assassination attempt by [Allena Benoch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Allena_Benoch), a mercenary of the Fighters Guild.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-WAP-12)</sup> In the same period, [Lord K'avar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:K%27avar) allied himself with the [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm), and plotted to overthrow Queen Akorithi and usurp the throne of Sentinel. K'avar went into hiding once the Fighters Guild got involved, although he was later arrested and imprisoned in the dungeons of Castle Sentinel. Akorithi ordered his execution, but it is unknown if this was carried out.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DFK.27avar1-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DFK.27avar2-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DFK.27avar3-15)</sup>

#### Morrowind
In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), in Morrowind, after the previous guild master left his position, the new guild master along with many in the upper ranks were enticed into supporting the [Camonna Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camonna_Tong). During the corruption, the Guild provided some of the only concrete evidence of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), as well as it being masterminded by a man called [Gentleman Jim Stacey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gentleman_Jim_Stacey). Reports vary, but some claim that after the Morrowind's Thieves Guild came into conflict with the Camonna Tong, the Camonna Tong used the Fighters Guild to take down the Thieves Guild.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MOM-16)</sup> Others state that the former guild master slayed the corrupt members, and others believed that the Thieves Guild helped in killing the corrupt members.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Morrowind-17)</sup>

#### Cyrodiil
Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a wizard named Argoth employed the Fighters Guild to invade the Ayleid ruin of [Arpenia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arpenia), and recover [Sinweaver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sinweaver) from the scholar Azani Blackheart. A large group of warriors were sent to the ruins, but were successfully repelled by Blackheart. Fifteen guild members died, including the son of Guildmaster Vilena Donton, and many more were injured. Argoth turned to the [Blackwood Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackwood_Company) to complete the job. However, the Company double-crossed Argoth and seemingly made a deal with Blackheart: if he gave them Sinweaver and departed from Arpenia, the Blackwood Company would pay him off and reveal Argoth's location. Blackheart agreed, and subsequently killed the wizard, retrieving his weapon. He then moved his base to [Atatar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atatar), another Ayleid ruin not far from Arpenia.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Modryn-18)</sup> Seeking to defame the rival Blackwood Company, Fighters Guild second-in-command Modryn Oreyn, along with a [new recruit](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), investigated the abandoned ruins of Arpenia. Oreyn's suspicions proved correct when he found the place free from any signs of battle. The two then journeyed to nearby Atatar, which was being guarded by Blackheart's bandit allies. After fighting their way into the depths of the ruins, Oreyn and the recruit confronted Blackheart and killed him, avenging their fallen comrades.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

## Council of Province Generals
The Council of Province Generals is a council of the Fighters Guild's highest ranks. Each general represents a province of the Empire, managing the guild's business there on behalf of the guildmaster.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the number of delegates was lessened. Only five former provinces were represented in the Council: Cyrodiil, Elsweyr, High Rock, Skyrim, and Valenwood.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-COPG-21)</sup>
