[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Hammerfell_(Anthology).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Hammerfell_(Anthology).jpg) **Hammerfell** [![](https://images.uesp.net/8/80/LO-race-Redguard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Redguard.png) Illustration of a typical Redguard male Hammerfell, once known as **Hegathe**, the **Deathland** (or **Deathlands**), and **Volenfell**, is a region in the west of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It has been described as a province, kingdom, or republic throughout its history. Situated between [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3H-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MCMap-2)</sup> Hammerfell features diverse landscapes including beaches, jungles, grasslands, mountains, swamps, and the vast [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) desert. It is inhabited by the human race of [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), who migrated to Tamriel following the partial sinking of their homeland, [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). While predominantly urban and maritime, with bustling port cities, the interior of Hammerfell is sparsely populated with small farms and nomadic tribes.

All the old cities of lost Dwarves are now the cities of modern Hammerfell.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGH-3)</sup>

## Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/DF-map-Iliac_Bay_%28labeled%29.png/350px-DF-map-Iliac_Bay_%28labeled%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_(labeled).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_(labeled).png) A map of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region in the Third Era showing the forested mountains and coastal jungle regions of Hammerfell east of the Alik'r Desert Hammerfell is located in the western part of Tamriel. It borders [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) to the north, [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) to the northeast, and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to the southeast. The border with High Rock is defined by the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-m WT-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> though the border was formerly further south at the [Bangkorai Pass](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md) in the second Era,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ON-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSo HR-8)</sup> and the fiefdom of [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth_(fiefdom).md) located south of the river was controlled by [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) for some time after the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWITW-9)</sup> The Cyrodilic border is defined by the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md), which rises in the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) and separates Hammerfell from the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md). It has extensive access to the sea, with the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) to the northwest, the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) to the west and the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) to the south. Craglorn and the Western Reach make up the borders along Skyrim's Druadach Mountains.

Hammerfell has a variety of environments, ranging from temperate to tropical climates. The coasts in particular are vibrant with plantlife, and biomes vary from open grassland, subtropical beaches, humid swamps around [Tigonus](Lore_Tigonus.md), temperate woodland mountains around the [Dragontails](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), forests around [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-10)</sup> and thick jungles such as the [Bantha](Lore_Bantha.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DUG-11)</sup> The [Alik'r desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) is found in the northwest, with the barren wastelands of [Dak'fron](Lore_Dak%27fron.md) found towards the southeast of the desert.

There is a notable level of thermal activity in the province, particularly around the Dragontail Mountains, as evidenced by lava harvested by facilities at the [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md), Aldunz, and Mzeneldt.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SAC-12)</sup> A volcano connected to Molavar is found near [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-EPA-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DFVolcano Map-14)</sup> and there is at least one volcanic crater in the province.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TIC-15)</sup>

Hammerfell is divided into several regions, among them the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md), southern [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md), [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), [Khefrem](Lore_Khefrem.md), and [Sunforge](Lore_Sunforge.md). Many isles in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) and [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) are under Hammerfell's control, including [the Chain](Lore_The_Chain.md), [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ON-7)</sup> [Cespar](Lore_Cespar.md) and [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md). The [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) and [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) have at certain times also considered a part of Hammerfell.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MCMap-2)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

### [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/DF-map-Abibon-Gora.png/200px-DF-map-Abibon-Gora.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Abibon-Gora.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Abibon-Gora.png) Partial map of Abibon-Gora [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md) (also known as **Albion-Gora**) is a region that rests on the western coast of Hammerfell, in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region. It is a humid subtropical region with beaches and grasslands on its coastlines, and forests throughout its interior. It shared borders with [Kairou](Lore_Kairou.md) to the north and the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) to the east when it was a barony in the Third Era. It is unknown how far south it goes, but the entire region from Abibon-Gora down to the peninsula of [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) is a lush plant-filled rainforest. [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) predominate the region, with [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md) as the favorite deity. Sophists and mystics at his chapel engage in friendly debates during gatherings. The capital city is [the city of Abibon-gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md), and the locality is protected by the [Knights of the Wheel](Lore_Knights_of_the_Wheel.md).

### [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md)
[![ON-concept-08.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3b/ON-concept-08.jpg/200px-ON-concept-08.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-08.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-08.jpg) The [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md), best known as the **Alik'r Desert**, is a large region which forms the heart of Hammerfell.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-17)</sup> In truth, the distinction between the Alik'r's shifting sands and the kingdoms of Hammerfell is hazy; travelers in the vast desert report never being entirely sure which kingdom they may be in. An enormous red mist seems to rise up from the sand in the mornings, though it disappears again well before noon.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TA-18)</sup> Major cities of the region include [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) in the northwest, the port city of [Tava's Blessing](Lore_Tava%27s_Blessing.md) in the north, [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) and [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset.md) in the east, as well as [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md) in the south.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ON-7)</sup>

Many [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruins lie under its sand,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3H-1)</sup> as does a stronghold of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), Wind Scour Temple.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-19)</sup> The Lost City of the Na-Totambu is also hidden somewhere in the wastes.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ON-7)</sup> During the Warp in the West, there were reports of a large, spontaneous sandstorm, and the region ultimately came under the rule of Sentinel.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWITW-9)</sup> At the beginning of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171), the [Imperial legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) in southern Hammerfell, overwhelmed by the sudden onslaught of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), were forced to retreat across the Alik'r in the now-famous March of Thirst.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TGW-20)</sup>

### Antiphyllos
[![DF-map-Antiphyllos.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/DF-map-Antiphyllos.png/200px-DF-map-Antiphyllos.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Antiphyllos.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Antiphyllos.png) [Antiphyllos](Lore_Antiphyllos.md) is a region in northern Hammerfell and is part of the greater [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) area. The region's monarchy was once connected to the [Phyllocid Dynasty](Lore_Phyllocid_Dynasty.md), the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) family that ruled the province during its time under the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). Some of their last known descendants were Grandee [Zizzeen](Lore_Zizzeen.md) of Antiphyllos and his relative, High King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). The local [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of the region have the utmost respect for [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md), one of the great [sword-singers](Lore_Sword-singers.md) of Redguard history. They observe the holiday, [Divad Etep't](Lore_Divad_Etep%27t.md) on the 12th of [Sun's Height](Lore_Sun%27s_Height.md), which is a day of mourning dedicated to the warrior. The [Anthotis](Lore_Anthotis.md) clan of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and the [Dust Witches](Lore_Witches.md) are active in the region.

### Bantha
The [Bantha](Lore_Bantha.md), or the **Banthan Jungle,** is a lush tropical jungle region in the province of Hammerfell.

The precise location of the Bantha is unknown; however, it is considered part of the Iliac Bay. The people of the Bantha are known to celebrate two holidays. [Aduros Nau](Lore_Aduros_Nau.md) is celebrated on the 28th of [Sun's Dawn](Lore_Calendar.md#Sun.27s_Dawn) to commemorate the transition from the winter, and the [Feast of the Tiger](Lore_Feast_of_the_Tiger.md) is observed on the 14th of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md). Though the Feast of the Tiger presumably originated as a means of venerating an obscure [Tiger God](Lore_Tiger_God.md), it is now a thanksgiving.

### Cape Shira
[Cape Shira](Lore_Cape_Shira.md) is an area comprising the western end of mainland Hammerfell. It has a lush tropical climate, though the northern coast is rocky. [Salas En](Lore_Salas_En.md) is found here. [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md) sailed northwest to round the Cape, becoming the first of the Ra Gada to discover the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).

Cape Shira roughly corresponds to the location of the regions of [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md), [Kairou](Lore_Kairou.md), and [Pothago](Lore_Pothago.md) in the Iliac Bay.

### Cespar
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/LO-map-Herne_and_Cespar.png/200px-LO-map-Herne_and_Cespar.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Herne_and_Cespar.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Herne_and_Cespar.png) Cespar (Bottom) [Cespar](Lore_Cespar.md) (also spelled **Caspar**) is a large island off the western coast of Hammerfell, south of [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md). Sometime in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the former noblewoman, Lady Benwyne Thronebane was exiled to the island of Cespar and lived there since at least [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). When the Painted Eye took over the citadel [Borderwatch](Lore_Borderwatch.md) in southern [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), they made a demand to free Lady Thronebane but it was later revealed that all of their demands were a ruse. The island was considered by the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) to be part of Hammerfell's territory in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the island appeared to be unaffiliated with any province on certain maps.

Tralan the Two-Blade, a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) swordsman who became one of Emperor [Titus Mede I](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md)'s most valued men, hailed from Cespar.

### The Chain
[![LO-map-The Chain.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/LO-map-The_Chain.jpg/200px-LO-map-The_Chain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-The_Chain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-The_Chain.jpg) [The Chain](Lore_The_Chain.md) is a group of at least three islands in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), south of [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) in Hammerfell and west of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), near the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md). The two smaller islands trail off to the west. The island was considered by the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) to be Hammerfell's territory in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

The [coral](Lore_Coral.md) kingdoms of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) are described as a set of islands southwest of the Chain.

### Craglorn
[![ON-load-Craglorn.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/ON-load-Craglorn.jpg/200px-ON-load-Craglorn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Craglorn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Craglorn.jpg) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/15/ON-concept-Craglorn_Live_02.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Craglorn_Live_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Craglorn_Live_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Craglorn_Live_02.jpg) Varying biomes of Craglorn [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) is a region in northeastern Hammerfell, at the foot of the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md). It is situated between [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

The southern part of the region, called Lower Craglorn, consists mainly of dry wastelands, with some green areas near the bodies of water. A gate in the western mountains connects the region with [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). The main settlement is [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md), a city heavily influenced by the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) culture, located in the easternmost part of Lower Craglorn. Elinhir lies very close to the Skyrim town of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), which is just across the border to the northeast. [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md) is located within the south-central portion of Craglorn.

The northern part of the region is called Upper Craglorn and is part of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). Its landscape is rugged, mountainous, and very similar to [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), which is just across the mountains to the north. The main city, [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md), lies in the northwest corner of the region. The east part of this region is called the Valley of Scars, while the west is known as [Skyreach](Lore_Skyreach.md).

Craglorn was home to many different civilizations in the past. These include [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md), and their ruins are common in the region. It was also the last bastion of the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) and the remnants of their civilization can be found here. A Nedic [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md) can be found near the city of Elinhir. The [Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md) are the oldest remaining civilization in the region. Old [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruins are located in the northernmost mountains of Upper Craglorn.

### [Cybiades](Lore_Cybiades.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/DF-map-Cybiades.png/200px-DF-map-Cybiades.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Cybiades.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Cybiades.png) Cybiades [Cybiades](Lore_Cybiades.md) is an island off the northern coast of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), within the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). It has a tropical coastal climate with a vibrant variety of plantlife. The people of Cybiades create an oil condiment from hot peppers.

By [the fifth century of the Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md#Fifth_Century), the only notable structure on the island was the ruins of Cosh Hall, where armed [human](Lore_Human.md) squatters had taken up residence. Although otherwise uninhabited, Cybiades was considered a separate political entity until the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), when it was most likely subsumed by the massive expansion of the Kingdom of Sentinel.

### Dak'fron
[![DF-map-Dak'fron.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/DF-map-Dak%27fron.png/200px-DF-map-Dak%27fron.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Dak%27fron.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Dak%27fron.png) [Dak'fron](Lore_Dak%27fron.md) is a dry region within north-central Hammerfell in the greater [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region, dominated by [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). It is one of the most barren wastelands in the province, and [giant scorpions](Lore_Scorpion.md) migrate from the "blighted Dak'fron" to the marginally lusher Alik'r Desert as an invasive species. Dak'fron borders the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) to the northwest, [Santaki](Lore_Santaki_(county).md) to the north, and the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) to the northeast.

The Sisters of Kykos are found in Dak'fron and the dominant vampire bloodline is the [Anthotis](Lore_Anthotis.md). Dak'fron is known for its fine [amethysts](Lore_Amethyst.md).

After the events of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Dak'fron became part of the [Kingdom of Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Ayaan-si, High Prophet of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md), had called upon the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) of the north to rebel against [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md), and had financed a number of forays against the borders in Dak'fron.

### Dragontail Mountains
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg) Nedic ruins in the Dragontail Mountains [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg/200px-ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg) Hel Ra Citadel in the Dragontail Mountains The [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), (called the **Dragon's-Tail** in the First Era by Redguards) are a mountain range in northern Hammerfell. They contain the Upper Craglorn region and then curve to the southwest, towards the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) and the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). Politically, the mountains are surrounded by several regions to the east and west. On its western flanks was the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md), [Dak'fron](Lore_Dak%27fron.md), [Tigonus](Lore_Tigonus_(fiefdom).md), and [Santaki](Lore_Santaki_(county).md). To the east is [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md), [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth_(fiefdom).md), [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset_(barony).md), and [Totambu](Lore_Totambu.md). The march of [Ephesus](Lore_Ephesus_(march).md) is located in the middle of the Dragontail Mountains. Regions in the area include the Valley of Blades, Bazidir Valley, Lemdazi Valley, and Ngonta Valley, and named peaks in the range include the volcano of Mount Dakobo, Mount Ewado near [Scourg Barrow](Lore_Scourg_Barrow.md), Mount Nombaje, and [Heldorn Mount](Lore_Heldorn_Mount.md). Other locations of note include [Drear](Lore_Drear.md) and Ganada Pass.

### Ephesus
[![DF-map-Ephesus.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/DF-map-Ephesus.png/200px-DF-map-Ephesus.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Ephesus.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Ephesus.png) Not to be confused with [Ephesus](Lore_Ephesus.md) the region of High Rock bearing the same name in the 2nd Era.

[Ephesus](Lore_Ephesus_(march).md) is an landlocked polity in the wider [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region, in the province of Hammerfell. It is a remote territory high in the western [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), located in a general area with known volcanic activity.

### Fallen Wastes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/ON-load-Bangkorai.jpg/200px-ON-load-Bangkorai.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Bangkorai.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Bangkorai.jpg) Fallen Wastes The **Fallen Wastes** is the southernmost region of the greater region of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). The entirety of the Fallen Wastes is considered geographically to be on the Hammerfell side of the border, although around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) it was controlled by the [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) city-state of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) and considered a part of High Rock by the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md). The capital of the region is the city-state of [Hallin's Stand](Lore_Hallin%27s_Stand.md), located on the northern end of the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), just before the [Bjoulsae Estuary](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md). The ancient ruins of the [Hall of Heroes](Lore_Hall_of_Heroes.md) is the gateway to the Redguard afterlife, the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md). Many Redguard heroes were buried in the Hall of Heroes, including [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) and [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md). Among other large ruins in the region are Nilata Ruins and Razak's Wheel, which are Ayleid and Dwemer respectively. The [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Garrison.md) marks the northern end of the region and connects both provinces. The eastern gates lead into the region of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md).

### [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md)

## History

### Merethic Era

#### Early inhabitants
[Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) peoples in Craglorn are known to have existed since at least the [late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era), where they regularly engaged in conflict with the local [Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> Due to the harsh environments of Hammerfell, tribes of Desert Orcs and Giant Goblins controlled most of the province, far away from the 'civilized' races.

#### Ayleids
During the late merethic and early first era, the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) from Cyrodiil created several settlements in what was to become Hammerfell. Some were close to their territory of origin such as Molavar and Balamath in eastern Craglorn or [Varlaisvea](Lore_Varlaisvea.md) just across the Brena river, but a few were far away such as Nilata in the Fallen Wastes or even [Salas En](Lore_Salas_En.md) on the western reaches of the [Alik'r desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) close to the rocky coast of [Cape Shira](Lore_Cape_Shira.md).

### First Era

#### Dwemer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_03.jpg) The Hall of Volendrung in Volenfell, the site where the hammer landed Hammerfell was originally called Hegathe by the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) and the Deathland by the early [Nedic people](Lore_Nede.md), both terms roughly meaning the same thing. In [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [Rourken Clan](Lore_Rourken.md) settled the region after opposing the creation of the joint Dwemer- [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) state of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md). It is said that the [leader](Lore_Rourken_(person).md) of the clan threw his mighty hammer, [Volendrung](Lore_Volendrung.md), across the continent, and followed it west to their new home. The region eventually became known as [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md), or "City of the Hammer", after the Dwemer capital built near the location of modern-day [Gilane](Lore_Gilane.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3H-1)</sup> The Dwemer were eventually driven from the province by an otherworldly invasion of giant goblins.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGH-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Early-26)</sup> Any remaining Dwemer of Volenfell vanished in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) along with the rest of the Dwemer race, and their empty cities were looted by [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) and [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) pirates before being reclaimed by nature.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3H-1)</sup>

#### Warrior Waves
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/LO-misc-Warrior_Wave_Crop.png/200px-LO-misc-Warrior_Wave_Crop.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Warrior_Wave_Crop.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Warrior_Wave_Crop.png) Warrior Waves Over a century passed before civilization would return to Volenfell, and when it did it took the form of "warrior waves" of migration from Yokuda, known as the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md). This mass colonization occurred in four successive waves during the eighth and ninth centuries of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and spanned approximately twenty eight years.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CM28RG-27)</sup>

The first recorded wave of migration took place around [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780). After winning a war against Emperor [Hira](Lore_Hira.md), [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) and his army of renown [sword-singers](Lore_Sword-singer.md) left their homeland and sailed to Hammerfell.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-RTHATH-28)</sup> Hunding's flotilla of ships landed in Hammerfell and discovered vast numbers of giant [goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) strongholds littering the sands. Frandar refused to co-exist with the [Flint-Tooth](Lore_Flint-Tooth_Tribe.md) tribe on their new homeland, and rallied his army to slay the beasts. Frandar was killed in one of the early conflicts, but his son [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md) pushed onward and forged five mighty [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md), which allowed him to drive out the giant goblins and conquer the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONTKF-29)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-STDH-30)</sup>

The next wave is believed to have occurred around the time of Yokuda's sinking in [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-31)</sup> This wave consisted of ships that departed from both the continent and its islands, including [Yath](Lore_Yath.md) and [Akos Kasaz](Lore_Akos_Kasaz.md), the largest island of Yokuda and home to its capital. The bulk of the refugees, including the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md) ruling class, were put ashore on the island of [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) while the Ra Gada sailed on to mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-17)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-31)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Hot IB-24)</sup> The fleet first landed at [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) on the southwest coast of Hammerfell. From Hegathe they spread along both southern and western coasts, while others continued north and east into Hammerfell itself.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-STJOA-32)</sup> The fifty-six ships of the second wave, known as the "Tavan wave", sailed southeast towards [Hunding Bay](Lore_Hunding_Bay.md) and the southern coast of Hammerfell.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-33)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CM28RG-27)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Cont Plat-34)</sup> The island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) was one of the first landfalls settled by the Yokudans.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONload Stros-35)</sup> They discovered the peninsula of [Khefrem's Boot](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md). Led by Prince [Hubalajad](Lore_Hubalajad.md), he established the city of [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) and built a citadel named No Shira.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-IDOPH-36)</sup>

To the north went a group of warrior-sailors under the leadership of Grandee [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md), in ships they had brought from Akos Kasaz. Yaghoub was the first of the Ra Gada to discover the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) as he rounded [Cape Shira](Lore_Cape_Shira.md). Yaghoub, following the constellation of [the Steed](Lore_The_Steed.md), decided to make his home on the northern coast of Hammerfell and established the city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-STJOA-32)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-LOTYC-37)</sup> Many of the Ra Gada kings were wealthy corsairs, and raided Breton coastal cities and merchant vessels along the southern Iliac Bay.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONYokudan-38)</sup> Ra Gada colonists from the island of Yath clashed with an [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) clan known as the [Corelanya](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md), and attacked the Conrenlaya inhabitants of [Salas En](Lore_Salas_En.md), and established their own settlement within the ruins.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TSEE-39)</sup>

They quickly established settlements along the coasts of Cape Shira, and built a grand palace in a valley known as the Ash'abah Pass. The Yokudans pushed farther inland into [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) and discovered the ruins of Nilata, which were also inhabited by [Elves](Lore_Mer.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONload Nilata-40)</sup> The most ruthless group of Ra Gada was arguably the [Anka-Ra](Lore_Anka-Ra.md). Led by the self proclaimed emperor [Tarish-Zi](Lore_Tarish-Zi.md), they cut a bloody swath across Hammerfell and all the way into [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) in their search for a new home.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-WOTRG-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONload Helra-42)</sup> In their wake, the Nedes as a distinctive people disappeared from the historical record shortly after their arrival, although some accounts claim Nedic culture was already fading prior to the Ra-Gada.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-NOTDL-43)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TTPOLB-44)</sup> The Anka-Ra established [Hel Ra Citadel](Lore_Hel_Ra_Citadel.md), a vast complex believed to be used as a training ground for their [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-CAC-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-EJ-46)</sup>

#### The Goblin Warlord
During the First Era, [1E 874](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_874), when Warlord Thulgeg's army of Orcs and Goblins was driven from Hammerfell by the Redguards, the Bretons of [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md) denied them passage through the Pass and forced them to flee northeast, trudging all the way through the Dragontail Mountains before they finally reached Orsinium. Not a single goblin made its way through the pickets into [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-47)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TM-48)</sup>

Sometime after the foundation of the Redguard city of [Ojwambu](Lore_Ojwambu.md), a giant goblin warlord by the name of [Mahgzoor Rockhand](Lore_Mahgzoor_Rockhand.md), became warlord of all the goblin tribes in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md). Mahgzoor rose his great blade 'Bone-Hewer' and roared in a mighty voice like an earthquake, and declared that the day of vengeance, at last, was at hand. He led his "Endwise army" down the Dragontail Mountains and into Hammerfell, and the people of the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) fled from this goblin army to Ojwambu, where the city became full of refugees. As the people started to leave the city to travel to the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md), goblin scouts saw that the Redguards had a considerable number of [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warriors, and the smallest goblin scout was forced to return to Mahgzoor by the other goblin scouts. Once this scout returned, Mahgzoor struck his head off and then told the goblins to destroy the crop fields and block up the rivers so that nobody in the city could eat or drink, unaware that only Hallin and his fellow Ansei were in the city. After a long time of waiting, Mahgzoor became suspicious as the city should have starved at that point, so he called forth his goblin shamans, asking them if the Redguard warriors were just "shadows". To see if this was the case, the shamans sacrificed twin infants and sent a scullion to the East Gate, and this scullion was killed by a spear from Hallin. The shamans returned to Mahgzoor and told him that they were real warriors, and in response to this, Mahgzoor was enraged and killed the shamans. He then ordered his army to get ready to assault the city. This assault lasted for 17 days, where no Ansei survived except for Hallin and at the end of this assault, Hallin and Mahgzoor clashed swords for a whole day. Eventually, both were mortally wounded, with Hallin striking off the head of the Warlord Mahzgoor. They both died, but Hallin was smiling knowing he had won. The Redguards who had been using this time to train with the Book of Circles then marched to retake the lands captured by the goblins, renaming Ojwambu to Hallin's Stand.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-49)</sup>

#### The Siege of Orsinium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg) Statue of Gaiden Shinji and Baloth Bloodtusk In [1E 948](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_948), [King Joile](Lore_Joile.md) of Daggerfall sent a letter to [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md) of the [Order of Diagna](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md), which proposed a joint attack on Orsinium. Agreeing to provide aid, an alliance was formed between the Order of Diagna, the armies of Daggerfall (High Rock), and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) (Hammerfell). The Bretons of the [Bjoulsae River Tribes](Lore_Bjoulsae_River_Tribes.md) also agreed to lend their aid.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ONABS-50)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-51)</sup> The armies amassed and began a campaign for the destruction of Orsinium.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-WJot B-52)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-PGE3O-53)</sup>

During the 30-year campaign, which became widely known as the "Siege of Orsinium", the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) armies of Daggerfall, the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) soldiers of Sentinel, and the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) [Order of Diagna](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md) invaded the Orc homeland. The Orcs gave much resistance and proved themselves to be fearsome and brutal warriors, just as they did in the past. However, King [Joile](Lore_Joile.md) of Daggerfall already planned to betray the Redguards and invade Hammerfell after having destroyed Orsinium. He and his [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) general, [Mercedene](Lore_Mercedene.md) of the [Winterborn](Lore_Winterborn.md), tricked the Orcish hero [Baloth Bloodtusk](Lore_Baloth_Bloodtusk.md) and his Savage Sons, who initially did not take part in the war, to join King Golkarr of Orsinium. Afterwards, he manipulated the leader of the Order of Diagna, [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md), into having a duel with Bloodtusk over Orsinium's fate. During the duel, Mercedene ordered Joile's archers to kill both combatants, effectively removing the most powerful warriors of both Orsinium and the Order of Diagna for Joile.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ON-7)</sup> In [1E 973](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_973), with Orsinium's defeat still years away, King Joile launched his invasion of Hammerfell, but he was defeated and killed at the [Bangkorai Pass](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md) by the [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warrior [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Ft MSo ML-54)</sup>

The Siege of Orsinium finally ended in [1E 980](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_980) when the allied forces breached the city's defenses, overran it, and razed it to the ground. Despite this loss, it did not mark the end of either the Orcs or the dream of Orsinium.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-WJot B-52)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-PGE3O-53)</sup> The city was rebuilt in the same location and razed again at least two more times over the following centuries.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ONOld Ors-55)</sup>

#### Thrassian Plague
In [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200), the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) broke out and it first appeared on mainland Tamrielic soil through major port cities on the southwest coast. Hegathe was the disease's point of entry in Hammerfell and it caused a significant decrease in the population.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-1TTP-56)</sup> Many people at the time searched for a cure in vain and unsung heroes like Mazazim al-Hegathe died on their quest.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SH3-57)</sup>

When [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md) erupted over in [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md) in [1E 2484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2484), Hegathe experienced vibrations from the seismic force of the volcano.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-SH4-58)</sup>

#### Knahaten Flu
One of the most famous stories from the Knahaten Flu is that of the Crimson Ship. Filled with Kothringi refugees, it left the shores of Black Marsh on the 9th of First Seed, [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), only to be turned back at every port. After a year on the sea, they reached Hammerfell, and with no one to shelter them, they sailed west<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TDC-59)</sup> to the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). The book, [*Pirates of the Abecean*](Lore_Pirates_of_the_Abecean.md) tells a tale of the discovery of the ship, found adrift by a group of pirates, its entire crew dead.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-60)</sup> To memorialize this event, the people of Hammerfell honor the 20th of Rain's Hand as the [Day of Shame](Lore_Day_of_Shame.md). Nobody leaves their house, for it is told that the Crimson Ship will return on this day.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Hi D-61)</sup>

#### The [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md) Fall to Craglorn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-npc-Celestials.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Celestials.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Celestials.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Celestials.jpg) The three Guardian Celestials: the Warrior, the Thief, and the Mage The [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md) fell from the sky to Craglorn circa [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578) and took on [mortal form](Lore_Celestial.md).<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Arnure-70)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-SOE-71)</sup> As is his nature, [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) used this opportunity to attack the other Celestials by corrupting the Guardian Constellations through their Nedic Apex Stones. Quickly establishing a cult known as the [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md), the Serpent gained control of [the Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md) beneath the city of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md) in the Ruins of Kardala. Under his influence, the Mage took control of an army of [atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) that had been summoned to the region recently to repel [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), and created the powerful new [Air Monarch](Lore_Air_Atronach.md) and [Foundation Stone Atronachs](Lore_Foundation_Stone_Atronach.md), before being sundered into many aspects scattered throughout Craglorn.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-TTd EA-72)</sup> The Warrior was forced to summon Emperor [Tarish-Zi](Lore_Tarish-Zi.md) from the past so that he could raise an undead army of [Anka-Ra](Lore_Anka-Ra.md) warriors from the many Yokudan tombs in the region, but not before he summoned his own champion, a contemporary Nedic warrior named Titus Valerius.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-ESOWarrior-73)</sup> A great celestial obelisk known as [the Mage's Staff](Lore_The_Mage%27s_Staff.md) also crashed to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) at that time.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-SB-74)</sup>

As the only guardian remaining, [the Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) hid her Apex Stone in the ruins of [Reinhold's Retreat](Lore_Reinhold%27s_Retreat.md) beneath the city of [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md). She then assumed the guise of a blind [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) woman named Hara, and took control of the [Star-Gazers](Lore_Star-Gazers.md).<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TTd MA-75)</sup> Recruiting scholars and warriors from across Tamriel, Hara sent the Star-Gazers to search for the missing Guardians and prevent more Anka-Ra from being summoned. With the help of Kelmen Locke, the only surviving summoner of the atronach army, the Star-Gazers managed to locate the uncorrupted aspects of the Mage so that they could overpower the Serpent; the restored Mage subsequently fled to the [Aetherian Archive](Lore_Aetherian_Archive.md) in an attempt to escape Time. Meanwhile, Titus Valerius helped the Star-Gazers return Tarish-Zi to his grave.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-TWC-76)</sup> However, the Serpent soon learned of the Thief's location and attempted to corrupt her, but was prevented by Locke, Valerius and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TMG-77)</sup>

After being forced to reveal her true identity, rumors of the Thief's appearance in Belkarth drew the attention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md). Groups of Undaunted launched an assault on the Aetherian Archive and [Hel Ra Citadel](Lore_Hel_Ra_Citadel.md) to free the Mage and the Warrior from the Serpent's influence. Regardless of this, the Serpent continued his attempt to raise armies, planning to return [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) to a primal state. However, the Scaled Court was betrayed from within by [Cassipia](Lore_Cassipia.md), the Regent of Serpentine Stratagems. Along with her companion Little Leaf, Cassipia secretly worked alongside the Vestige in stopping the Court's creation of [mantikora](Lore_Mantikora.md) and dismantling the [Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md)' attempt at raising an army of primal, nirncrux-infused [trolls](Lore_Troll.md). Cassipia's true motives were revealed when she subsequently declared herself [the Exalted Viper](Lore_The_Exalted_Viper.md) and sought to usurp the Serpent's place in the sky. She briefly succeeded in becoming a Celestial, but was killed in battle against the Thief and the Vestige.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-DOt EV-78)</sup> The Undaunted later entered the Scaled Court's stronghold of Sanctum Ophidia and defeated the Serpent.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Sanct O-79)</sup>

The wards at Skyreach Pinnacle were also broken by the Scaled Court that year, but were quickly resealed before the Celestials could escape.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-UC-80)</sup> Other minor Celestials were known to have appeared on Tamriel around this time. Celestial Rams became notorious for wandering the rocky terrain of Craglorn, as they sometimes entered gardens and ate everything that had been planted.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-81)</sup> Nebulous Star-Born Steeds were Celestial [horses](Lore_Horse.md) that originated in [Spellscar](Lore_Spellscar.md),<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-82)</sup> a magically polluted area created when the Mage's Staff fell to Tamriel.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-SB-74)</sup> Starchaser Senches also appeared at this time; these creatures resembled [senche-cats](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) composed of floating onyx shards similar to stone found at Spellscar.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-83)</sup>

The fallen Constellations later reassumed their place in the sky.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ESO-84)</sup>

#### Thieves Guild of Abah's Landing
By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), royal power in Abah's Landing had been entirely supplanted by a group of criminal cartels known collectively as the "Merchant Lords". The hinterlands of Hew's Bane were populated primarily by smuggler crews and [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) pirates, with whom the Lords actively did business.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-MFDL-85)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-ALMLv1-86)</sup> The city-state of Abah's Landing thus developed a deep rivalry with the kingdom of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) to the north, whose traders usually bore the brunt of this thievery they harbored.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-MFDL-85)</sup>

A local criminal gang known as the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) was founded in Abah's Landing in the Second Era, and ultimately grew to become a Tamriel-wide organization.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Zeira-87)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-88)</sup>

#### Tiber War
[![LO-misc-Redguard History.gif](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif/200px-LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Redguard_History.gif) The best-understood episode of the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) was the conquest of the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [Kingdom of Hammerfell](Lore_Kingdom_of_Hammerfell.md). Despite observing the might of the quickly expanding Empire, the already aged and ailing High King of Hammerfell, [Thassad II](Lore_Thassad_II.md), resisted all forms of Imperial invasion.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-RGIntroduction-89)</sup> It was only after his death in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862)<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-17)</sup> that Hammerfell began to weaken; not from an external invasion, but rather from an internal revolution. It was a civil war between the two most prominent factions: the rebellious [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) and the loyalist [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), the latter of which supported Thassad's heir, [A'Tor](Lore_A%27tor.md).

After initial defeats, the Forebears signed a [pact](#Forebear_Truce) with the Emperor, granting him minor territory concessions in exchange for aiding them in the civil war. The already weakened Crowns still provided heavy resistance to invading Imperial forces, until at last they were cornered at [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). The [Battle of Stros M'Kai](#Battle_of_Stros_M.27Kai) was won thanks to Admiral Richton's shrewd tactics and the aid of Septim's dragon vassal, [Nafaalilargus](Lore_Nahfahlaar.md). The Empire swiftly took control of Hammerfell, imposing several Provisional Governors to rule different regions.

Later that same year, an uprising led by siblings [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) and Iszara saw the Restless League come out of hiding. They stormed the Palace, and Cyrus himself cornered Provisional Governor Amiel Richton and Imperial Emissary Dram aboard a [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) dirigible. Cyrus killed them both with the Soul Sword, which was enchanted with the soul of Prince A'tor.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-RGRaze Palace-90)</sup> This act was allegedly the third incarnation of the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding).<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Vo Fit E-91)</sup> Thereafter, Tiber Septim came to Stros M'Kai personally to sign a treaty that held more favorable terms for the Redguards. Iszara represented Hammerfell, and was guided by the wisdom of Prince A'Tor via the Soul Sword.

It is later reflected that the Redguards become a "people without a past" because of the Tiber War and the Redguards' being "stalwart guardians of Hammerfell" during the conflict.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-RDBA-92)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-93)</sup><sup>:9</sup> Having held on to the ground for which its people "shed tears of blood", land and rock itself became under the Redguards' command.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-RDBA-92)</sup>

#### War of the Bend'r-mahk (3E 397)
[![SK-narrative-Start1.png](https://images.uesp.net/a/a3/SK-narrative-Start1.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-narrative-Start1.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-narrative-Start1.png) The [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) was a conflict that started in [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) period between the provinces of Hammerfell, [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-SKmap-97)</sup> High Rock and Hammerfell fought as allies in the war against invading Skyrim forces.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-OBLathon-98)</sup>

During the war [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) defeated the forces of Hammerfell and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and claimed many miles of the [eastern parts of those provinces](Lore_Western_Reach.md), including the cities of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md).<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-99)</sup> Some of these territories were claimed to have been lost in previous wars at the expense of Skyrim.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-PGE3High-100)</sup>

The most peculiar situation in Hammerfell's territory in the war arose in the city of [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md), where the city was divided into western (Hammerfell) and eastern (Skyrim) sections of the city, walled off from each other, with each section having its own government and a desire to take control of the entire city. even resorting to acts of terrorism.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-99)</sup> One such act was members of the Mages Guild of Dragonstar using noxious enchantments on Skyrim soldiers.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TMG-77)</sup> The Thieves' Guild of Dragonstar were also known to be aligned with Hammerfell despite being in Skyrim's section of the city.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-101)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-102)</sup> The national divide in Dragonstar persisted for thirty odd years after the conflicts conclusion.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-99)</sup>

The war resulted in a deeper division between the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) factions of [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md), as the Forebear cities showed no inclination to help the Crown cities assaulted and occupied by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), which in turn was a response to the lack of help by Crowns to the Forebears during [the Camoran Usurper's invasion](Lore_Camoran_Usurper%27s_Invasion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3H-1)</sup>

The Redguards harbor a fierce grudge against [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md), and yearn for a chance to take back land that was seized from them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3H-1)</sup> After peace was established, a commission was created to repair the damage done by the war.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-103)</sup>

#### War of Betony (3E 402 – 3E 403)
[![LO-misc-Daggerfall History.gif](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif/200px-LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif) The [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) (sometimes the **Siege of Betony**) was a military conflict between the kingdoms of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), fought amid the wider unrest of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) and, more specifically, the Wars of the Iliac Bays. The conflict centered on [Betony](Lore_Betony.md), a strategically important island at the mouth of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), which was claimed by both kingdoms. According to the most common accounts, Lord Mogref of Betony sought Daggerfall's protection against pirates and brigands, while Sentinel maintained that an older agreement placed the island under its authority.

Hostilities culminated at [Cryngaine Field](Lore_Cryngaine_Field.md), where King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) was killed and his son [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md) assumed command; King [Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md) of Sentinel also died before the battle's end. The war concluded with Betony under Daggerfall's control, and the subsequent peace settlement was sealed by the marriage of King Gothryd to Camaron's daughter, [Princess Aubk-i](Lore_Aubk-i.md).

In the aftermath, Betony remained a possession of Daggerfall. Gothryd succeeded his father in Daggerfall, while in Sentinel Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md) ruled as regent until her children reached their majority. Though some accounts emphasize the war's heavy cost and uneasy aftermath, late Third Era sources present it as the turning point after which Daggerfall became "the mightiest" of the Iliac Bay's three principal kingdoms, a position it retained for some fourteen years until the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), when [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) emerged as the bay's dominant power.

The most primary documentation of the conflict comes from two books, both called *The War of Betony*. [Vulper Newgate](Lore_The_War_of_Betony_(Newgate).md)'s version contains the official history, as written by the victors; [Fav'te](Lore_The_War_of_Betony_(Fav%27te).md)'s book tells the story from the viewpoint of a citizen of Sentinel. *[The Daggerfall Chronicles](Lore_The_Daggerfall_Chronicles.md)*, authored by the [Scenarist Guild](Lore_Scenarist_Guild.md), also provides an account of the events.

## Background
[![DF-banner-Daggerfall Sentinel Wayrest.png](https://images.uesp.net/4/40/DF-banner-Daggerfall_Sentinel_Wayrest.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-banner-Daggerfall_Sentinel_Wayrest.png) The [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) was a fractious and conflict-ridden region primarily divided between the powers of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md). Daggerfall and Sentinel would often find themselves in conflict for dominance as the premier military power of the region, and Daggerfall and Wayrest would similarly compete for control of trade. A fourth power would spend most of the history of the Bay being suppressed and attacked by the other powers, [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).

Daggerfall's monarch during this period was King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md). Unusual for a ruler at the time, Lysandus was noted to hold sympathies for the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) population, likely inspired by watching a campaign of extermination against them in his youth. Prominent figures of his court included his mother [Nulfaga](Lore_Nulfaga.md), his wife Queen [Mynisera](Lore_Mynisera.md), and his court sorceress and lover [Medora Direnni](Lore_Medora_Direnni.md). All three were noted for their magical prowess. His issue consisted of Prince [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md), the sole child he would have with his wife.

The Court of Sentinel was ruled by King [Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md). His wife was Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md), a union that would prove more productive and result in the children [Arthago](Lore_Arthago.md), [Aubk-i](Lore_Aubk-i.md), [Greklith](Lore_Greklith.md), and [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md). Out of disgust for the scholarly nature of his eldest son Arthago and to secure the succession for his preferred son Greklith, Camaron would have his son abducted and left to die in [3E 400](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_400).

Wayrest, similar to the role it would play in this conflict, featured the most complicated court. The elderly King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) had outlived his wife Queen [Carolyna](Lore_Carolyna.md), who was also survived by their daughter Princess [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md). In a move that would prove to be tumultuous, Eadwyre remarried. Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) brought her children from her previous marriage to General [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md), Prince [Helseth](Lore_Helseth.md) and Princess [Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md), with her to Wayrest. Helseth and Elysana would immediately begin competing, plotting, and scheming to become the true heir to Wayrest. Elysana was betrothed to a rising noble known as Lord [Woodborne](Lore_Woodborne.md), who was secretly plotting with Prince Gothryd to usurp the throne of Wayrest for himself. Although he had reached out to King Lysandus with the same plot, the King of Daggerfall proved to be unwilling to engage in that form of skullduggery.

[Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), while lacking the critical formal recognition needed to protect its population, was working to achieve that status. The King of Orsinium was [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md), who won the land through enforcing a claim via a duel against a Wayrest noble. As previously noted, their cause had found an unlikely ally in King Lysandus.

Ruling over all of the temporal powers was the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Empire.md), which would wax and wane in influence over the centuries of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). By the opening years of the fifth century of the Third Era, the Empire was in a period of waning influence. Unbeknownst to most of the people of Tamriel, Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) was secretly replaced by the Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md). Although the usurper took Uriel's place, he refused to continue his policies to further solidify Imperial power, and seemingly adopted policies that would solely bring ruin and conflict to Tamriel. By the end of his ten year reign, the Empire was greatly weakened. Despite Uriel VII's efforts to correct the situation, he would prove incapable of correcting the situation to put a stop to this infighting in his empire.

## Prelude to War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/DF-map-Betony.png/200px-DF-map-Betony.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Betony.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Betony.png) The Island of Betony As its name suggests, the coming conflict centered around the island of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md). Although closer to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md)'s [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) peninsula, prior to the coming conflict the island shared closer ties with the Hammerfell kingdom Sentinel according to Fav'te's account. Newgate instead described the island as being independent, and Sentinel's claim as a potentially illegal 200 year old contract declaring Betony to be a "traditional holding" of the Kingdom of Sentinel. The sparsely populated fishing island's true value lay with its strategic position, which would prove to be a major asset to whichever power that controlled the island.

Circa [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402), Betony, little more than a village, was being constantly plagued by attacks from pirates. Fav'te claimed that these pirates raids were in fact being sponsored by King Lysandus, who desired to conquer the island and was waging a silent war to gain it. Lord Mogref of Betony saw his continued de facto independence as untenable. He formally requested for vassalization to the Kingdom of Daggerfall, in return for the protection of the crown and a monetary reward for himself. Sentinel's preferred account instead claimed that Lysandus illegally invaded and occupied the island.

Sentinel diplomatically protested this, citing a 200 year old treaty. These objections would fail to move Daggerfall. As both kingdoms prepared for war, conflict began within their courts. Fav'te would claim that almost all of Lysandus' advisors would try to convince him to end the conflict, including his court sorceress Medora and his mother Nulfaga. Instead of heeding their counsel, all opposing voices would be expelled from his court. This would prove to be untrue for Medora and Nulfaga, who would truly leave his court later on and for different reasons. Newgate instead claims that most of Sentinel's court was for the war, with the notable opposition from Camaron's chief advisor [The Oracle](Lore_The_Oracle.md), who reportedly could see the future. The Oracle claimed to see only death and defeat in the war, earning her banishment.

With neither side willing to cede Betony without a fight, war became inevitable. During the year [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402) King Camaron the White Moon of Sentinel declared war on King Lysandus the Dragon of Daggerfall.

## The War of Betony
[![](https://images.uesp.net/f/f4/DF-npc-Lord_Bridwell_%28face%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lord_Bridwell_(face).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lord_Bridwell_(face).png) Lord Bridwell The course of the military conflict known as the War of Betony shows one side as having a clear advantage, Daggerfall. Every single major battle of the war would be won by Daggerfall. Fav'te attributes the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) edge to magic and espionage, which Camaron and his fellow [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) refused to use over more conventional and "honest" tactics. Still, this would not prove to be enough to bring an end to the war, and the minor powers of the Iliac Bay began to be drawn into the conflict.

The first major battle of the war would also be the first battle altogether. The Battle of the Bluffs, a naval battle, would take place off the coast of Daggerfall shortly after the war began. Daggerfall's forces were put under the command of Lord [Bridwell](Lore_People_B.md#Bridwell), the leader of the [Knights of the Dragon](Lore_Knights_of_the_Dragon.md).Both sides first sent main infantry squares to attack each other in traditional combat, followed by night raid with Nightblade force on each other's flanks and rear. As general,<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_General)</sup> Bridwell proved to very capable, and secured victory thanks to his "bravery and leadership".

Sentinel's navy lie defeated, but not utterly destroyed. They would go on to threaten the [Isle of Craghold](Lore_Isle_of_Craghold.md) and put the garrison under siege for several days. Lord Bridwell would once again prove victorious when he went to relieve the defenders.

Although a few minor engagements would see Sentinel succeed, their loss records saw their next major entry at the Battle of Glenpoint Hills. During this battle the leader of the [Order of the Candle](Lore_Order_of_the_Candle.md), Lord [K'avar](Lore_K%27avar.md) would suffer a disfiguring injury by a fireball thrown by a [Dark Elf](Lore_Dark_Elf.md) mercenary.

The poor performance of the Kingdom of Sentinel for such a martially-inclined culture left a notable impression on the memories of the war. Numerous jokes were created about Sentinel's forces after the conclusion of the war.

### A Court in Crisis
Although Lysandus, Gothryd, and Bridwell were winning the war for Daggerfall, the court did not fare well in their absence. While away, Queen Mynisera uncovered evidence of her husband's affair with Medora, and promptly banished her. She would also place a curse on the court sorceress, trapping her in [Direnni Tower](Lore_Direnni_Tower.md). Medora would leave, still desperately hoping her lover Lysandus would end the war before his death. Nulfaga would also repeatedly beg her son to end the conflict, as her magical abilities foresaw he would die if the war continued. Nulfaga, dejected and surrounded by opposing voices, ultimately decided to leave Daggerfall for her castle [Shedungent](Lore_Shedungent.md) rather than watch her son die, leaving behind her [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) familiar [Skakmat](Lore_Skakmat.md) to observe the court in her place.

His mother's leaving and the banishment of his lover proved to be what it took to convince Lysandus to begin peace negotiations.

### The Treaty of Gradkeep
[Reich Gradkeep](Lore_Reich_Gradkeep.md) (now known as Anticlere) was chosen as the site to negotiate a peace between Daggerfall and Sentinel. The neutral High Rock fiefdom would see King Lysandus, King Camaron, Lord Bridwell, a Daggerfall Priest of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) named Vanech, and soldiers from both sides arrive for the negotiations, with Lord Graddock, Lady Graddock, and their heir Mara Graddock acting as hosts.

By all accounts, the negotiations started off very well. All parties acted amicably, and it seems genuine compromise and terms were achieved. The ensuing breakdown in negotiations is similarly universally attributed to the written treaty not being reflective of the previous agreements. A summary of what these terms were, and what caused the breakdown according to each major account follows:

#### [Fav'te's Version](Lore_The_War_of_Betony_(Fav%27te).md)
- Lord Graddock had appealed to both parties and was responsible for beginning the peace negotiations.
- King Camaron was "excessively generous" and offered to give up some of his rights to Betony.
- The written treaty was purposely miswritten by a Daggerfall scribe, which was immediately noticed by King Camaron.
- The room erupted into conflict shortly thereafter, with no mention of who started the fighting. Reich Gradkeep would be massacred, and Daggerfall and Sentinel would encamp at [Ravennian Forest](Lore_Ravennian_Forest.md) and [Yeorth Burrowland](Lore_Yeorth_Burrowland.md) respectively.

#### [Newgate's Version](Lore_The_War_of_Betony_(Newgate).md)
- Lysandus began peace negotiations by reaching out to Lord Graddock to facilitate them.
- King Camaron, at a disadvantage in the war, was initially courteous.
- King Camaron flew into a rage after realizing the treaty would include a formal agreement to share Betony.
- King Camaron ordered his troops to begin rioting, slaughtering the neutral occupants of Gradkeep's palace and city, with Daggerfall's forces bringing order to the chaos and routing Sentinel's army. Sentinel fled to [Yeorth Burrowland](Lore_Yeorth_Burrowland.md), and Daggerfall chased until [Ravennian Forest](Lore_Ravennian_Forest.md) where they made camp.

#### [Scenarist Guild's Version](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles/Narrative)
- Lysandus decided to seek peace, with Reich Gradkeep chosen as a meeting place for him and Camaron.
- The meeting was conducted civilly by both parties.
- The Priest Vanech deliberately sabotaged the meeting, seeing Betony as a holy land of Kynareth. He produced a fake treaty that was designed to offend Lysandus, although how is not specified.
- "According to legend" Lord Bridwell started the riot by shattering the negotiating table with a battleaxe. Camaron realized what Vanech had done and personally killed him. Meanwhile the Graddock's tried to restore peace, but would fail. Soldiers and civilians from Daggerfall, Sentinel, and Reich Gradkeep began fighting, leaving the palace "soaked in blood".

### The Battle of Cryngaine Field
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d9/DF-prerelease-Army.gif/200px-DF-prerelease-Army.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-prerelease-Army.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-prerelease-Army.gif) The Battle of Cryngaine Field *"After the days of [Tiber](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and the traitor [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), when the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) has ruled the land of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for four hundred and five winter moons, then the White Moon, [Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md), King of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), will shine dark on his mortal enemy, King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md), the Dragon of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md). Though the Dragon shall combat with the Moon, there will be no victory at [Cryngaine Field](Lore_Cryngaine_Field.md). For it is written that dark sorcery, war, and forbidden acts shall bring terror to the errants in the courtroom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md)."* The conclusion of the War of Betony would take place in [Cryngaine Field](Lore_Cryngaine_Field.md) during the year [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403). Over the course of the week that followed the breakdown in negotiations, the Daggerfall and Sentinel camps received reinforcements to prepare for their final engagement. And it was during this week that King Lysandus plotted to fake his death.

Like all of the other major battles of the War of Betony, Daggerfall once again proved victorious at Cryngaine Field. The armies broke camp and clashed in the field, only for a strange mist to descend, causing chaos for both sides. During the chaos, King Lysandus was struck with an arrow from a Sentinel archer, either in his heart or his throat, killing him.

Gothryd was crowned mid-battle, not far from the battle lines. Inspired by their new king in full regalia urging them on, the battle turned decisively in the favor of Daggerfall's forces. King Camaron met his demise shortly after Lysandus, although all three primary accounts are in dispute as to how. Fav'te claims that Gothryd challenged Camaron, who was exhausted from fighting the battle for the full length of the engagement, to a duel. Unable to even defeat the physically spent king honorably, used "dirty trick and black magic" to backstab him before their duel even began. Newgate instead claims that Sentinel's forces began to rout as Daggerfall's was inspired to push on by their new king, and Gothryd engaged and slew Camaron mid-battle. *The Daggerfall Chronicles* is similar to Newgate's account, except it attributes the death of Camaron to Lord Bridwell instead of King Gothryd.

### The Truth of the Battle
[![](https://images.uesp.net/6/6f/DF-npc-Lord_Woodborne_%28face%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lord_Woodborne_(face).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lord_Woodborne_(face).png) Lord Woodborne However, not everything was exactly as it seemed to be. Lysandus, heartbroken over his lover being banished for his court, and generally uninterested in ruling, approached his son Gothryd to form a plan for him to secretly leave his crown while still living. The plan that they formulated consisted of Lysandus being replaced by a lookalike to be supplied by Lord [Woodborne](Lore_Woodborne.md). During the battle the fake Lysandus would feign death, all the while the real Lysandus would be escaping his life, with the help of Lord Woodborne to live with Medora. Gothryd, "who wanted his father to be happy", agreed to this scheme.

Meanwhile, Lysandus' mother Nulfaga, still very worried about his fate, ordered the dragon Skakmat to intervene in the battle by creating a cloud of fog to blind the fighters. It was during this time that Gothryd shot his father's impersonator. Although the plan was for him to just feign death, the arrow proved to be lethal and the impostor king was buried in Lysandus' place.

*The Daggerfall Chronicles* then claims that Lysandus was betrayed mid-journey by Lord Woodborne. Fearing that the king would prove to still have influence over his son after his "death", Woodborne ordered the [Minat](Lore_Minat.md) Orc Tribe led by Gortwog to kill him just before he was to set sail to join Medora. Lord Woodborne then kept the desecrated corpse of Lysandus as a potential blackmail tool if King Gothryd proved troublesome. Although it points to the right culprit, Woodborne, Lysandus truly died before the Battle of Cryngaine Field. Lord Woodborne assassinated King Lysandus during a meeting between Lysandus and a Wayrest peace envoy.

The potential duplicity of Wayrest against King Lysandus is not entirely clear. Lysandus was a noted sympathizer of the cause to form a province for the Orcish people, earning the natural enmity of Wayrest, who stood to lose land if Gortwog was successful in his efforts. He was also a supporter of the Empire, a position that was not shared by his Sentinel and Wayrest counterparts. Although the exact level of involvement the Crown of Wayrest had in Lysandus' death cannot be said, it is clear they were directly aware of the truth of it.

King Gortwog, wishing to prevent the death of one of the rare royal sympathizers to his cause, sent forces to ambush to Wayrest envoy, but they failed to kill the assassin among their ranks. He also attempted to alert Medora Direnni to this plot, but the letter failed to reach her.

It is unclear if Gothryd betrayed his father, or if he was even aware his father was truly killed.<sup>[[Note 2]](#intnote_Gothryd)</sup>

## Aftermath
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png/200px-DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-Lysandus_Ghost.png) The Shade of Lysandus *"Daggerfall, Sentinel, and Wayrest have weathered the storms of contention, plague, famine, and pestilence. The recent War of Betony was typical of Iliac Bay warfare: sincere, frighteningly violent, and peaceably resolved."* — *[A History of Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:A_History_of_Daggerfall)* With King Camaron dead and their forces routing, Lord Oresme of Sentinel formally surrendered. Sentinel formally ceded all claims to Betony, and at the same time informally ceded their dispute against Daggerfall as the primary military power of the Iliac Bay. Lord Oresme committed suicide while traveling back to Sentinel, with Newgate claiming it was while aboard a ship and the [Scenarist Guild](Lore_Scenarist_Guild.md) instead stating it was while still on the march. The peace terms included a marriage between the newly crowned King Gothryd and Princess [Princess Aubk-i](Lore_Aubk-i.md). Although this term was intended to help maintain peace between the kingdoms, it was a popular view in Sentinel that the princess was essentially a hostage. Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md) would act as Queen Regent until her youngest son, [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md), could ultimately take the throne.

King Gothryd enjoyed a very brief period of popularity as the victor of the War of Betony. However, rumors quickly began to circulate that he was involved in the death of his father after his shade began haunting Daggerfall. Still, with a technical alliance with his former adversaries in Sentinel and his secret alliance with Lord Woodborne, as well as his possession of a [major artifact](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), there was no serious direct challenge to his position after the war. The death of Lysandus and marriage of Gothryd caused there to be a change in the identity of the Queen of Daggerfall. The chaos of the war and this change among royalty would lead to some very critical confusion to a courier with a letter from the Emperor.

Wayrest was not threatened by Daggerfall's control of Betony as far as trade goes, thanks to their monopoly over the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md). With Lysandus dead and their armies completely spared from any fighting, Wayrest remained prepared to continue to challenge the Orcish attempts to seize control of the northern region of their kingdom. Unfortunately their temporary stalling of this threat came at the cost of creating one they did not even realize; Woodborne, who was only one heartbeat away from seizing control of the kingdom for himself, and potentially [much more](Lore_Numidium.md).

[Nulfaga](Lore_Nulfaga.md), having failed to save her son, fell into madness with her grief, isolation, and experiments. Mynisera became the Dowager Queen of Daggerfall, and isolated herself from the rest of the court. Seemingly still loyal to her late husband, she began destroying documents that would shine a poor light on his reputation, particularly his love letters to Medora. Medora, trapped by Dowager Queen Mynisera in her ancestral home, tried to repeatedly summon her lost lover, but repeatedly failed.

The pirates who acted as a catalyst for the conflict with their attacks on Betony were forced to disperse, but not by the army of Daggerfall or Sentinel. The buccaneers based their operations out of the isle of [Balfiera](Lore_Balfiera.md), which was now haunted by the undead thanks to [Medora Direnni](Lore_Medora_Direnni.md). In her failed efforts to summon Lysandus' spirit, she managed to raise undead all over the island, making it too dangerous to continue operating from.

#### Night of Green Fire
In [4E 42](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_42), Altmer dissidents who had fled to Sentinel from the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) in [Alinor](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) were attacked by Thalmor operatives in what became known as the [Night of Green Fire](Lore_Night_of_Green_Fire.md). Their efforts to fight back against the Thalmor were in vain and, by the time [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) soldiers arrived, the entire refugee district of the city had been destroyed. According to Legate Fasendil, an Altmer stationed in Sentinel at the time as a member of the Imperial Legion, the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) was not content with killing dissidents at home, so they went to Hammerfell to finish the job.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LF-109)</sup> Thalmor agents were also harassing and possibly murdering refugees in [Balfiera](Lore_Balfiera.md) and Sentinel around [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48); the victims were all of mixed blood or had associations considered by the Dominion to be "unclean."<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TIC-110)</sup>

#### Great War
Over a century later, the Thalmor of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) would attempt to seize control of Hammerfell from the Third Empire. Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md)'s refusal to concede to Thalmor demands triggered the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171). Aldmeri forces crossed western Cyrodiil and invaded Hammerfell's southern coast, easily sweeping through the scattered resistance put up by the disunited Redguards. Initially only seeking to conquer Hammerfell, the Dominion launched a full-scale invasion of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) when it realized the Empire was weaker than previously believed. Imperial fortunes took a turn for the better in Hammerfell two years later, when a Forebear army from Sentinel broke the siege of the Crown city of Hegathe, leading to the reconciliation of the two factions. After the Empire's hard-won victory in the Battle of the Red Ring in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Empire and Dominion signed the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) and ended the war. Outraged that the terms of the treaty called for a large portion of southern Hammerfell to be turned over to the Dominion, however, the Redguards soundly rejected it. Titus II was forced to officially renounce Hammerfell as an Imperial province to preserve the peace, and fighting between the Aldmeri and Redguards continued. In [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), the Dominion agreed to the [Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_Second_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and finally withdrew from the region, leaving Hammerfell an independent albeit devastated nation.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TGW-111)</sup>

## Politics
Redguards are divided between the cosmopolitan, open minded Forebears and the traditionalist, isolationist Crowns. The Forebears are the descendants of the Yokudan military class, the Ra Gada, and are generally open to outside cultures such as the Imperials and Bretons; the Crowns are the descendants of the Yokudan noble class and still prefer to shun outsiders, vying to preserve the customs and culture of their ancestors.

### Political Factions

#### Forebears
The [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) is the name given to a social and political group of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). The Warrior Wave of [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), or first invasions of [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) to reach Tamriel, are now known as the Forebears. They cleared the way for their rulers, the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md), and their faction, which is now known as the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md). When the Na-Totambu and the Yokudan nobility came later, [Razul](Lore_Razul.md) made himself Yokeda to cement the power of the Ra Gada, with some calling Yokeda Razul the first Forebear. Forebears tend to be more nomadic in nature, as many of them work as traders and merchants throughout Hammerfell, which gives them the most experience when dealing with foreigners. Their symbol of depicts three swords whose blades are crossed and tied together with a golden knot, with a small cape attached to them, while their uniform colors are red and yellow. Some of the major Forebear strongholds include [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md), and [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md).

One of the major differences between the Forebears, as compared to the Crowns, is that they worship the same pantheon as the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) and [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) and take a more liberal approach to tradition and religion. This is one of the biggest factors in why the two don't get along, as the Crowns believe the Forebears engage in what is considered "outrageous, non-traditional practices." For example, the Forebears are believed to secretly support the pariah [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) tribe in their efforts to cleanse the stench of necromancy and undeath, and will even call upon them for aid. The Crowns view this as a major affront to their religious practices, as those who strike down an ancestor are considered unclean and cursed. The Forebears worship of Imperial deities [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), rather than the traditional gods of [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), and [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), plus their willingness to engage with Tamrielic cultures outside their own, is heavily despised by the Crowns faction. This contributes to the hatred the Crowns and Forebears have for one another.

The Crowns and Forebears represented the two halves of a highly stratified Hammerfell and fought a bitter civil war during the [Common Era](Lore_Common_Era.md). With the Crowns nearing victory, the Forebears made an alliance with the Empire, allowing them to capture the province; but when it became apparent they were being used, the Forebears joined the Crowns in launching a double rebellion against Imperial leadership, resulting in the signing of the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md). During the Third Era, the Forebears were the most cosmopolitan of the Redguard factions and happily embraced the Empire of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). Near the close of the Third Era, a third faction, the [Lhotunics](Lore_Lhotunics.md), emerged, representing a middle ground. By the Fourth Era, they were at peace with each other, though divisions remain.

#### Crowns
The [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) are what is left of the old ruling class of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), the **Na-Totambu**. They can be found mostly in southern and inland Hammerfell and are known for still honoring the ancient [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) ways. Generally speaking, the Crowns dominate the southern region of Hammerfell and continue to adhere to their [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) background. Some Crown families in [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md) during the mid-Second Era were supposedly descended directly from the ancient Yokudan nobility.

#### Lhotunics
The [Lhotunics](Lore_Lhotunics.md) (the name Lhotun meaning **Second Boy** in [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md)) are the third faction in [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) society that appeared after the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). During the Warp, the [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) became the dominant power in most of northern Hammerfell. However, most of Sentinel's new lands were [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md). To keep his kingdom together, [King Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md) was forced to create what some consider a third party, one with reverence for the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) past but respect for the Imperial ways. The moderate Lhotunics have attracted nothing but contempt from both the Forebears and the Crowns.

## Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/ON-place-Hallin%27s_Stand.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hallin%27s_Stand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hallin%27s_Stand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hallin%27s_Stand.jpg) [Hallin's Stand](Lore_Hallin%27s_Stand.md), an example of the Redguard architecture Redguard buildings vary from simple tents to the sturdy domed structures of the cities which incorporate many Yokudan advancements. The latter are renowned for their rigidity, flexibility, heat transference, and general ornate beauty. They are much easier to repair than most Imperial stonemasonry. While much Imperial construction requires demolition and rebuilding after conflict, many of the Yokudan accent pieces, such as the flying staircases or ornamental domes, are designed to crumble after an attack or an earthquake, which allows them to be easily replaced in sections afterward. However, these structures are still solidly built; they stand tall and firm against the harsh environment of Hammerfell.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-121)</sup> They are characterized by thickly-built walls to help insulate against the heat of the desert, as well as a carefully-planned system of ventilation ducts. These ducts are built alongside windows high on the walls, and are covered by horizontal slats which keep out the sun while permitting the wind to enter. Each building is designed to channel even the slightest breeze from these ducts down to the floor.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Craft Motif6Redguard-125)</sup>

Redguard stables have been recognized for their lavishness and the ingenious design which makes them perfect for the environmental hazards the Redguards face. Their enormous mausoleums are thought to be the purest, undiluted representations of Yokudan architecture in their society. The most famous example, [Tu'whacca's Throne](Lore_Tu%27whacca%27s_Throne.md) in the Alik'r, is also a monument to the many Yokudans who died when the continent was destroyed, and it receives many pilgrims seeking to pay their respects.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-121)</sup>

The buildings of the Redguards are notable for being heavily ornamented, with engravings and golden domes. However, some of the cities in Hammerfell are built in different styles: for example, Dragonstar consists of Nordic buildings, whereas the Colovian style can be seen in Elinhir.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ON-126)</sup>

Since Hammerfell was home to many civilizations in the past, many different types of ruins can be found throughout the province, among them [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md), [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) ruins. In Craglorn, there are also [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) and [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruins.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ON-126)</sup>

In [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md), where [bees](Lore_Bee.md) pollinate them,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BBS-132)</sup> and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), where pomegranates, [figs](Lore_Fig.md), and [olives](Lore_Olive.md) grow across the green shoreline. In honor of the city's founder, Grandee [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md), the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of the [kingdom](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) celebrate [Koomu Alezer'i](Lore_Koomu_Alezer%27i.md) by eating pomegranates.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-STJOA-133)</sup> Pomegranate wine is the most popular type of wine in the Alik'r Desert<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONTreasures P-134)</sup> and it is an ingredient in [seared venomsteaks](Lore_Redguard_Cuisine.md#Seared_Venomsteaks), a dish native to the desert region.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-RFDD-135)</sup>

- Poppy - Both black and white varieties can be found growing wild in the mountains of Hammerfell. Their succulent pods are often the only nourishment for adventurers who find themselves in the wilderness without rations. It is said that black and white poppies imbibed together have magical properties. When they are crushed and mixed with the milk of the agile-footed mountain goat, the resulting potion allows the user to glide safely aboveground.

## Fauna
The hot and dry lands of Hammerfell are home to many fierce creatures. The Alik'r Desert is inhabited by [dunerippers](Lore_Duneripper.md), [assassin beetles](Lore_Assassin_Beetle.md), [giant scorpions](Lore_Giant_Scorpion.md), [crocodiles](Lore_Crocodile.md), [jackals](Lore_Jackal.md) and [harpies](Lore_Harpy.md), and the local people insist that their vast plain of sand is home to a multitude of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DUG-136)</sup> [Giant wasps](Lore_Wasp.md), [lamias](Lore_Lamia.md) and [lions](Lore_Lion.md) can be found in Bangkorai. [Sep adders](Lore_Sep_Adder.md),<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Tot SA-137)</sup> [dragon frogs](Lore_Dragon_Frog.md), [haj mota](Lore_Haj_Mota.md), [herons](Lore_Heron.md), jackals, and [kotu gava](Lore_Kotu_Gava.md) can be found in [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md). In somewhat cooler Craglorn, one can find [Welwa](Lore_Welwa.md), [duneracers](Lore_Duneracer.md), or more peaceful [camels](Lore_Camel.md), [daggerbacks](Lore_Daggerback.md), [fellrunners](Lore_Fellrunner.md), [sandroaches](Lore_Sandroach.md), and [fennec foxes](Lore_Fennec_Fox.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ON-126)</sup> The [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) is home to several reptilian creatures, such as the [wickeder](Lore_Wickeder.md), the [bounder](Lore_Bounder.md), the [clawrunner](Lore_Clawrunner.md), and the [wormmouth](Lore_Wormmouth.md). [Wolves](Lore_Wolves.md) and [floaters](Lore_Floater.md) also reside in the region.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Shadowkeycreatures-138)</sup>
