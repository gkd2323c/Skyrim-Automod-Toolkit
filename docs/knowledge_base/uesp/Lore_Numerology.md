# Numerology

— [Swims-In-Deep-Water](Lore_Swims-In-Deep-Water.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> **Numerology** is the study or veneration of the significance of specific numbers, and is a subject of interest in several cultures.

## Culture

### Altmer
*You see, the number two is considered … inauspicious.* [...] *Two implies duality—two simple forces in opposition. People don't like to talk about it in polite company, but the real reason we distrust it is that it's the number of Man.* [...] *Man sees the world as a seesaw—full of opposing forces. Day and night, good and evil, life and death. But this is a defective worldview. Without a third force, a fulcrum, a seesaw is just a flat board.*
— [Jurisreeve Lorne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Jurisreeve_Lorne)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-JL-2)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/53/ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg/200px-ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garden_of_the_Sacred_Numbers.jpg) Garden of the Sacred Numbers The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) are known for their reflections on sacred numbers,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOSN-3)</sup> and for establishing dedicated spaces, such as the Garden of Sacred Numbers, for their contemplation.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GOTSN-4)</sup> The Garden of the Sacred Numbers is said to be laid out in a way that makes numerological sense to the Altmer, for whom the numbers have certain mystical properties.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOSummerfall-5)</sup>

The Altmer are known to revere auspicious numbers and distrust inauspicious numbers, for instance the number eight is considered auspicious<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup> and thus revered,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOSN-3)</sup> while the number two is considered inauspicious and thus distrusted.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-JL-2)</sup> During the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the Altmer were known to distrust the number two both because it is an inauspicious number, but because it is also considered the number of [Man](Lore_Man.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-JL-2)</sup> Because the number two is considered inauspicious, Altmer Jurisreeves always travel in groups of three. Though this has the practical consequence of investigations carried out by Jurisreeves being more thorough, some Jurisreeves noted that the tradition was based more in superstition than utility.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-JL-2)</sup> The [Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapiarch) were known to swear on the Seven Sacred Texts.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PMA-7)</sup>

One such Altmeri philosopher pondered in this garden and ultimately consolidated his thoughts in his work *[Thoughts on the Sacred Numbers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thoughts_on_the_Sacred_Numbers)*. In this anonymous book, the author argued that certain Sacred Numbers are considered *auspicious,* as they are integral to the very essence of the universe. According to this author:

- Three symbolizes the Number of the Prime Celestials, represented by the sun and the two moons.
- Five signifies the Number of the Elements, as reality is made up of Earth, Air, Water, Fire, and Aether.
- Eight represents the Number of the Planets and is also the sum of three plus five.
- Three, Five, and Eight are the Good Numbers, and their total, Sixteen, is believed to be a number of great power.
- The Bad Number, Two, lacks vision and attempts to manifest duality, which is, as they believe, impossible.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOSN-3)</sup>

The Altmer alphabet was known to be twenty-four letters.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-M.26C-8)</sup> Twenty-four was known to be eight times three,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-M.26C-8)</sup> both of which are considered auspicious numbers by the writer of *Thoughts on the Sacred Numbers*.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOSN-3)</sup>

This author's ruminations on Three, Five, and Eight are corroborated in documents related to the Psijic Order. The Psijic Order had access to books from [alternative timelines and realities](Lore_Many_Paths.md), where certain key events transpired differently.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-9)</sup> One of those tomes spoke of a reality where a group of unknown entities from the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) attempted to invade Nirn. The Psijic Order of this reality attempted to harnass the sacred strength of the numbers three, five and eight to contain these entities, but it was ultimately unsuccessful. In the end only the numbers remained, but eventually even they failed and the reality was seemingly destroyed.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Go SNTT-10)</sup>

The phrase *"fivefold venerations"* was a common greeting among Altmer around the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup> and later.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Some sources connect the phrase to the Altmeri worship of [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-16)</sup> while others connect the phrase with their veneration of the Divines as a whole.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

Some sources claim that the Altmer do not take on personal names but instead only combinations of numbers,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-18)</sup> however this is demonstrably false and the source that claims it to be true- the Pocket Guide To The Empire, Volume 1- is noted by the Altmer known as '[¥R](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:%C2%A5R)' to be exceptionally propagandistic.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1Preface-19)</sup> In his notes about the Pocket Guide, however, ¥R makes mention of his father's *"numeric mystery,"* granting credence to the theory that some Altmer do, in fact, take on numbers as their personal names.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1Preface-19)</sup>

Some sources make mention of an "82 Crodo," seemingly related to the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) leader [Andel Crodo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Andel_Crodo) of the [Second Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), an Altmeri scholar who only rarely *"stoops from his rarefied perch in the archives of Alinor to dabble in the mundane."*<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-20)</sup> 82 Crodo may be one such Altmer who took on a number as his personal name whilst keeping his family name.

Some sources suggest the Altmer regard Talos' placement as Ninth Divine to be Lorkhanic nonsense, that is, convienent for man.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MKPosts-21)</sup>

### Dunmer
The number three was considered a lucky number associated with the Tribunal.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-35)</sup>

The number seven appears multiple times surrounding the [Nerevarine Prophecies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevarine_Prophecies). The [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlander) oral prophecy called "[The Stranger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Stranger)" spoke of the Nerevarine appearing when *"sleepers serve the seven curses,"*<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-36)</sup> which may have been in reference to the seven [Ash Vampires](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Vampire) in service to [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) and their [Ascended Sleeper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascended_Sleeper) servants. A different prophecy recorded by the [Dissident Priests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dissident_Priests), called "[The Seven Curses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Seven_Curses)", spoke of seven literal curses that the Nerevarine would suffer.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-37)</sup> Another Ashlander oral tradition, called "[Seven Visions of Seven Trials of the Incarnate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Seven_Visions)," spoke of the Nerevarine going through seven trials to prove their worthiness.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-38)</sup>

The seemingly widespread sermons of Deldrise Morvayn, [Clockwork Apostle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clockwork_Apostle) to Sotha Sil, speak of a "Furnace of Forgotten Numbers," seemingly a place of punishment or destruction to which the Daedric Princes and all of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) will be damned to after the creation of Tamriel Final.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-39)</sup> Texts from the same author also noted that the mathematics of the Dwemer were "ugly," and stated that those who pursue these ugly maths shall pay a great price in blood.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ti S5-40)</sup>

A text of unclear veracity claiming to be written by Vivec spoke of there being equations that last, and equations that pass unto the West. The text instructed the reader to choose the equations that last over those that pass into the West, degrading the West as wicked and "thought-slendered."<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-HBYA-41)</sup>

#### The 36 Lessons of Vivec
*I've read all the texts, the 36 Lessons, seen the patterns in the numbers. They aren't three loving gods. They are one giant monster from beyond the Void, and they're going to harvest souls for their true master.*
—Disordinator<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-42)</sup> Sermon 28 of the 36 Lessons of Vivec mentions several numbers in a mystical capacity. After defeating the [Ruddy Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruddy_Man) a second time, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) gave the monster's carapace to the mystics of the Number Room. They wrapped it in their shells, described as *"a series of flourishes by two supra numerates, one hormonally tall and the other just under his arms."* They then applied a resin from the carcasses of the now useless numbers between twelve and thirteen.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-36LOVS28-43)</sup>

In Sermon 6 of the 36 Lessons of Vivec, Vivec states that he is *"unto three, six, nine, and the rest that come after."*<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Sermon6-44)</sup> Also in Sermon 6, Vivec states that he is *"your protector and the protector of Red Mountain until the end of days, which are numbered 3333.*"<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Sermon6-44)</sup> Sermon 6 also says that *"There is a fourth kind of philosophy that uses nothing but disbelief,"* though neither this philosophy nor the previous three are explicitly identified within the 36 Lessons themselves. Could you ever tell if they switched places? I can and that is why you will need me."*<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Sermon11-45)</sup>

In Sermon 2, the world is said to have five corners.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Sermon2-46)</sup> This is repeated again in Sermon 28,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-36LOVS28-43)</sup> in Sermon 29,<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-36LV29-47)</sup> and in Sermon 35.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Sermon35-48)</sup>

### Khajiit
*Kier-Jo makes it simple simple for you. Eight phases for every moon. Eight logics between yes, no, and might be. Two of each common Khajiit and eight more. Even the Elves hid it in their precious twenty-four alphabet.*
— [Kier-Jo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kier-Jo_Chorvak)<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-M.26C-8)</sup> Pre- [Riddle'Thar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar) Khajiit religious texts spoke of [Mafala](Lore_Mephala.md) having two numbers, eight and sixteen, said to be two of her keys.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-56)</sup>

According to some sources, the Khajiit scholar [Kier-Jo Chorvak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kier-Jo_Chorvak) placed a heavy emphasis on the number eight and its connection with the number Khajiit [furstocks](Lore_Khajiit.md#Furstocks), which he placed at twenty-four.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-M.26C-8)</sup> Other sources have historically placed the number of Khajiit furstocks at sixteen "regular" furstocks and one Mane, for a total of seventeen,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-57)</sup> though other sources have stated that there are over twenty.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-58)</sup> Kier-Jo's counting implies that different Manes can be born as different furstocks, such as a [Cathay](Lore_Khajiit.md#Cathay) Mane, or a [Dagi](Lore_Khajiit.md#Dagi) Mane, bringing the total possible number of furstocks to twenty-four while the total number of "active" furstocks remains seventeen.

In that story, the reason that [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) are no longer commonly found across Tamriel is that they lost a war against the [Aurielian gods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel) and fled to a hidden village of dragons, which the village's founder, [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), named *"Section 22."* When confronted about the "stupidity" of that name, Akatosh claimed he found it *"very meaningful, and most appropriate,"* while sarcastically suggesting that mortals would see no difference between Section 22 and Section 16. Akatosh claimed that the number 22 in particular appealed to him aesthetically and possessed some sense, which he refused to reveal to his mortal companions.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-KEXI-60)</sup>

The [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md) associated the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) with the number five, with *"The five are all, the five are one. Five are true and death-in-the-woods."* being a known chant to Hircine's aspect of Alrabeg the Hunter.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-61)</sup> A spell invoking several gods of the Reachfolk pantheon could be found in [Crow's Wood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow%27s_Wood), a pocket realm of [Evergloam](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Evergloam) ruled over by the [Hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) [Daedra Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Lord) [Crow Mother](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Crow_Mother), which invoked Hircine and [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) to *"bind this place three times seven times."*<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-62)</sup>

[Breton](Lore_Breton.md) culture around the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) was said to be "fascinated" with triads and the number three, with some scholars relating it to the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) reverence for three as an auspicious number.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-CM93Breton-63)</sup> Several pieces of Breton equipment and furniture around the time had the [triquetra](Lore_Triquetra.md) symbol emblazoned on them,<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-64)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-CM93Breton-63)</sup> said to be a reminder that all three [druidic circles](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) share the same goal.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-65)</sup>

Paleonumerology is considered a subject of research in the Empire. In *[The Dragon Break Re-Examined](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Dragon_Break_Re-Examined)* paleonumerology is mentioned to have aided in disproving the existence of the Middle Dawn during the late 3rd Era.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TDBRE-66)</sup> The author of *[Shezarr and the Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr_and_the_Divines)*, Faustillus Junius, includes his title of the *Subcurator of Ancient Theology and Paleonumerology for the Imperial Library* in the book.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-San D-67)</sup>

During the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the number seven was generally considered to be good luck.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-68)</sup>

Some sources spoke of "[Julianos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Julianos) Cubes," supposedly numerological texts capable of containing prophecies and secrets of the universe. Numerologists studying such texts believed that the numbers 1 and 0 represented Anu and Padomay.<sup>[[UOL 10]](#cite_note-69)</sup>

The number seventeen is associated in several places with the phenomenon known as the "Hurling Disk." In Sermon 18 of the 36 Lessons of Vivec, Vivec states that *"In this world and others EIGHTEEN less one (the victor) is the magical disk, hurled to reach heaven by violence."*<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-36LV18-70)</sup> Likewise, in Sermon 29, the number 17 is said to be associated with the Hurling Disk.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-36LV29-47)</sup> The Hurling Disk is also associated with the number seventeen in the text [On the Detachment of the Sheath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:On_the_Detachment_of_the_Sheath), a mystical [Marukhati](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukhati_Selective) text associated with the [Middle Dawn](Lore_Dragon_Break.md#The_Middle_Dawn).<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-71)</sup> In the [Remanada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remanada), the number of questing knights who accompany [King Hrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrol) on his quest are specifically noted as numbering "eighteen less one," the same phrasing used in [Sermon 18](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_18).<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-72)</sup> The founder of the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md), was renowned to have defeated 17 disputants when establishing his new philosophy, the [way of the voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Hrothgar-73)</sup>

The Daedric Prince, [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) and his servants are associated with the number seven. In his realm, the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), his [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) legions are housed in seven hundred and seventy seven strongholds, with [Ardent Hope](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ardent_Hope) being his strongest.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-AGTTD-74)</sup> In Vivec's seventh sermon, [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) was approached by [Kh-Utta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kh-Utta), the Duke of [Scamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp) and a servant of Dagon. He brought Nerevar the seven pennants of his legions.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-36LOVS7-75)</sup>

## Eight and One
The [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines) were sometimes referred to as the "Eight and One" (not to be confused with [the One](Lore_The_One.md)) after [Talos](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s ascension into the pantheon.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-prophet-76)</sup> This "Eight and One" motif appears in several other places across Tamrielic lore, with eight objects (usually deities) being shown alongside a ninth, somehow separate or set apart, object or deity. The near-universal appearance of "Eight and One" also extends to the numerals 8 and 1 in various different forms.

In Sermon 18 of the 36 Lessons of Vivec—notably, a number made up of the numerals 1 and 8—Vivec sets out to kill his nine monster children, but discovers that one of them was already killed simply by his decision to write the Book of Hours- that child being himself.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-36LV18-70)</sup>

[Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) ruled for 81 years, having been was crowned as leader of [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md)'s forces in [2E854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E854) and ruling as Emperor until his death in [3E38](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E38).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-77)</sup> Tiber Septim died at the age of 108.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TAH-78)</sup> Some sources state that King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md), first king of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), ruled from [1E 113](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_113) to [221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221), 108 years,<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-79)</sup> though other sources claim his reign began in [1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-80)</sup> Other sources claim that Harald died at the age of 108.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-PGE1Skyrim-81)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-82)</sup>

The [Middle Dawn](Lore_Dragon_Break.md#Middle_Dawn) was said to have lasted 1008 years, tracked by the Khajiit observing the passage of the moons from within the Break.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-83)</sup> Some sources, which [the Prophet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Prophet) of Anvil had knowledge of,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-prophet-76)</sup> spoke of the *"one thousand and eight Cyrodiilic weapons of rapture."*<sup>[[UOL 11]](#cite_note-84)</sup>

One Bretonordic tale about the creation of [Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) spoke of Dagon pleading for mercy from Alduin during the latter's eating of the world. It is said that Dagon pleaded "one hundred thousand and eight" times, but that this took so long he did not realize that both the world around him and he himself had been eaten.<sup>[[UOL 12]](#cite_note-85)</sup>

The [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) was made up of one large gem, the Red Diamond, surrounded by eight smaller gems representing the Eight Divines.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-86)</sup>

### The Missing God
*9. The Missing. 11*<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-36LV29-47)</sup> [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) is sometimes referred to as the "Missing Ninth" to the "Eight Limbs" of the Aedra,<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MKPosts-21)</sup> and as [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr) he has been called the "missing sibling" of the Eight Divines,<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Monomyth-87)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-88)</sup> or "Shezarr Who Goes Missing."<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-89)</sup>

Though Lorkhan's status as "the missing god" is present in nearly every Tamrielic mythic tradition,<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Vo F-90)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Monomyth-87)</sup> the Aedric pantheon is not the only Tamrielic pantheon to be made up of "Eight and One" deities. Some groups of [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), such as those associated with the Star-Queen Varalias, worshipped a group of [Star Orphans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge) called the [Nine Coruscations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Nine_Coruscations). Like the more common Eight Divines pantheon, the Nine Coruscations consisted of eight "regular" deities and one "missing" deity,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-T9C-34)</sup> that being [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia), who was erased from memory and reality by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-FOTFP-91)</sup>

The number of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) is usually counted as sixteen,<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-36LV21-92)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-PGE1Invocation-93)</sup> which is recognized as eight times two.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-36LV21-92)</sup> In [Sermon 21](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_21), Vivec speaks of the number of the Daedric Princes as the spaces between the [eight spokes](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) of [the Wheel](Lore_Aurbis.md), stating, *"The spaces between the gift-limbs number sixteen, the signal shapes of the Demon Princedoms."* This remains [mostly](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deadlight) [true](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fargrave) in regards to the [realms of the Daedric Princes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence#Daedric_Princes.27_Realms), as there are still exactly sixteen realms directly ruled over by Daedric Princes.

Sixteen as being the number of Daedric Princes in existence has proven to be false, with Princes such as [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag) being included among their ranks,<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-94)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-95)</sup> as well as the Daedric Prince [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia), who Vivec would have had no knowledge of.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-FOTFP-91)</sup> However, both Jyggalag and Ithelia can in some way be considered separate from the others, or in some way "lost"- Jyggalag because of his curse transforming him into [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), which even when he was freed from still resulted in a connection between the two Princes,<sup>[[UOL 13]](#cite_note-96)</sup> and Ithelia because she was erased first from all memory<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-97)</sup> and second from this reality.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-FOTFP-91)</sup> This brings the total number of Daedric Princes not just to eighteen, but also but to sixteen "regular" Princes and two "missing," twice the eight and one of the Aedra.

[The First](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) created the [Twelve](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md), and [the Second](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) created the Twenty-Two, adding up to a total of Thirty-Six. The Thirty-Six are said to be hollow and unworthy of worship, but they still live on as the Eight and One.<sup>[[UOL 16]](#cite_note-100)</sup>

This is a reference to the birthday of [Kurt Kuhlmann](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Kurt_Kuhlmann), September 11, which is 9/11 in American calendar notation.
