## Quick Walkthrough
1. Speak with [Ainethach](Skyrim_Ainethach.md) about the troubles with [Sanuarach Mine](Skyrim_Sanuarach_Mine.md).
2. Talk to [Atar](Skyrim_Atar.md) about why he shut the mine down.
3. Convince Ainethach to sell the mine, or convince Atar to leave.

## Detailed Walkthrough

### Miner Extortion
When you first enter [Karthwasten](Skyrim_Karthwasten.md), you will see the townsfolk squaring off against a group of [mercenaries](Skyrim_Silver-Blood_Mercenary.md) sent by the [Silver-Blood](Skyrim_Thonar_Silver-Blood.md) family in [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md) to secure the mine after a [Forsworn](Skyrim_Forsworn.md) incident. [Ainethach](Skyrim_Ainethach.md), the owner of the mine, is arguing with [Atar](Skyrim_Atar.md), the leader of the mercenary band:

**Ainethach**: *"I want you sellswords out of my mine."*
**Atar**: *"Watch your tongue, native. We'll leave when we're sure there's no Forsworn here."*
**Ainethach**: *"Oh, and when would that be, I wonder? When I sell my land to the Silver-Bloods?"*
**Atar**: *"The Silver-Bloods have made you a very generous offer for this pile of dirt. I suggest you take it."*

The Silver-Bloods are demanding that the mine be signed over to them in return for the protection they provided in the last attack. The mercenaries have been instructed to prevent anyone from returning to work in the Sauranach Mine until the rights to it are signed over to the Silver-Blood family.

### Deciding to Help Ainethach
Ainethach should be standing on the porch of his house during the day, and once approached he will agree to let you help him, and will ask you to convince Atar to leave. Unless you catch up to the band before they go into the mine, you will need to enter the mine to find Atar in the far end, standing on a raised platform.

#### Talking to Atar
After agreeing to help Ainethach enter the mine and speak with Atar. When you ask him to leave you will be given three options.

| Option | Dialogue | Reaction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| This whole town wants you out. Leave. **(Persuade)** | **Passed**: Dealing with a mob wouldn't be good for my coin purse. Fine. We're leaving. <br> **Failed**: Let's see them make us. | **Passed**: Return to Ainethach <br> **Failed**: Nothing |
| You'll listen to gold, I take it? **(Bribe)** | **Passed**: That I will, stranger. This is more than the Silver-Bloods are paying us. We'll clear out. <br> **Failed**: Don't think I'm being paid already? Get lost. | **Passed**: Return to Ainethach <br> **Failed**: Nothing |
| I think I'll just kill you. **(Intimidate)** | **Passed**: Whoa. Wait a second. I was just kidding. We'll leave. We'll leave! <br> **Failed**: I thought today was going to be boring. | **Passed**: Return to Ainethach <br> **Failed**: Attack |
| On second thought, I'll be leaving. | Milk drinker. | Nothing |

After successfully convincing him, you will have to run back to Ainethach to tell him the good news, whereupon he will give you your cut.

Note that even after successfully persuading, bribing, or intimidating, the mercenaries will claim that the mine is theirs, and will warn against sudden movements. There is no bounty for killing the mercenaries even after they agree to leave.

#### Strong Arm Tactics
An alternative option is to not talk to Atar at all, but to simply kill all the mercenaries. There are no mages or archers in the group, just weak melee fighters and the somewhat more formidable Atar, who is equipped as a bandit chief with leveled heavy armor, heavy shield, and one-handed weapon. The mercenary outside the mine will still greet you with pleasantries even after taking this option. There is no bounty for killing the mercenaries inside the mine, even if you attack first.

### Selling Out
You can also remedy the situation by speaking to Atar who will offer you a cut of the purchase price if you can convince Ainethach to sell. You have to talk to Ainethach, and will be given the usual persuade, bribe, intimidate options if you tell him "I think you should sell the mine".

| Option | Dialogue | Reaction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| They won't stop until you do. **(Persuade)** | **Passed**: You're right. They always get what they want, somehow. Fine. Tell Atar I'll sell. <br> **Failed**: Someone has to stand up to them. I won't sell. | **Passed**: Return to Atar <br> **Failed**: Nothing |
| Look. I'll give you a fair price. **(Bribe)** | **Passed**: That's more gold than they're offering. Fine. I'll sell. <br> **Failed**: This isn't about the money. | **Passed**: Return to Atar <br> **Failed**: Nothing |
| You do or you get hurt. **(Intimidate)** | **Passed**: Easy. I'll sell. Just don't hurt anyone. | **Passed**: Return to Atar |
| Forget it, then. | Let's do that. | Nothing |

After successfully convincing him, you will have to run back to Atar to tell him the good news, whereupon he will give you your cut.

### Reward
The reward is the same regardless of which path you choose to take, a medium amount of leveled gold. Don't miss the seven [silver ore veins](Skyrim_Silver.md) in the mine and the leveled shield that Atar carries (if you decide to kill the mercenaries).

| Levels | Reward |
| --- | --- |
| 1–9 | 400 |
| 10–19 | 600 |
| 20–29 | 800 |
| 30–39 | 1,000 |
| 40+ | 1,200 |

## Notes
- Siding with Ainethach will cause Ainethach's, [Lash gra-Dushnikh](Skyrim_Lash_gra-Dushnikh.md)'s and [Ragnar](Skyrim_Ragnar.md)'s dispositions towards you to rise to 1. - Siding with Atar will cause Ainethach's, Lash gra-Dushnikh's, and Ragnar's dispositions towards you to fall to -1.

## Bugs
- When leaving Sanuarach Mine, a [hagraven](Skyrim_Hagraven.md) corpse may randomly appear in front of the mine left of the smelter. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.7, addresses this issue. The corpse will no longer appear in front of the mine.
- Ainethach's dialogue at the end of the quest does not exit with a goodbye flag. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch), version 3.0.9, fixes this bug.
- If you make Ainethach sell the mine by brawl, there's no further dialog with him and you have no other choice but to help Ainethach.
