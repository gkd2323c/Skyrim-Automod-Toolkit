# Elder Council

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/OBR-interior-Imperial_Palace%2C_Elder_Council_Chambers.jpg/200px-OBR-interior-Imperial_Palace%2C_Elder_Council_Chambers.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-interior-Imperial_Palace,_Elder_Council_Chambers.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-interior-Imperial_Palace,_Elder_Council_Chambers.jpg) The Elder Council Chambers in 3E 433 The Elder Council (sometimes referred to as the **Imperial Council**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOTFG-2)</sup> the **Imperial Elder Council**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CTEC-3)</sup> the **Imperial Council of Elders**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CTEC-3)</sup> or the **Elders of the Imperial Council**)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Background Game-4)</sup> is the premier administrative body of the [Cyrodilic](Lore_Third_Empire.md) government.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RT-5)</sup> The Council's actions are governed by the rules and procedures outlined in the Elder Council Charter.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RT-5)</sup> It typically convenes at the Elder Council Chamber in the lower floors of the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GTTIC-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRDexion-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Oblivion-8)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The leader of the Elder Council holds the title of High Chancellor, and a Chief Councilor is designated as his deputy.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CTEC-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RT-5)</sup> Just above the Elder Council in the hierarchy is the **Inner Council**, headed by the High Chancellor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HCO-11)</sup> The [Imperial Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) are often found in leadership positions on the Elder Council,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AT2-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-14)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Df UGH-15)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-16)</sup> and the core [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay.md) at [Cyrodiil City](Lore_Cyrodiil_City.md) is described the home of an "age-old aristocracy of battlemages," which has existed since the early [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE1C-17)</sup>

Though it theoretically merely helps the [Emperor](Lore_Emperor.md) govern by dealing with various details,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Oblivion-8)</sup> the Council has proven capable of wrestling a substantial amount of authority away from the [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) throne, and there is often a power struggle between the two. The Council has legislative authority, and may vote on and enact new laws they deem proper. However, the reigning Emperor retains the power of veto, a right famously exploited by [Uriel Septim VI](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VI.md) to restore the power of the Emperor when faced with an unruly Council.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

When the Council is not in some sort of disagreement with an Emperor, its members are often looking for ways to curry favor with the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GTTIC-6)</sup> Each member receives an amulet worth a small fortune which is specially crafted as a symbol of their office.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRDelvin-19)</sup> As a prominent family in Cyrodiil garners more and more power and repute, people eventually start thinking it is only a matter of time before they obtain a representative on the Council,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Oblivion-8)</sup> though it is not clear exactly how new Council members are appointed. Appointment is seen as both an honor and burden, though some nobles view it only as a means to obtain more power and influence.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Imp P-20)</sup>

## Duties
[![SR-concept-Elder Council Amulet.jpeg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/SR-concept-Elder_Council_Amulet.jpeg/200px-SR-concept-Elder_Council_Amulet.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Elder_Council_Amulet.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Elder_Council_Amulet.jpeg)
### Governance
While its importance cannot be overstated, much of the Elder Council's responsibilities consist of the intricate but dull problems of running an Empire,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SR-21)</sup> such as administering Imperial taxes on vassal kingdoms.<sup>[[nb 4]](#cite_note-23)</sup><sup>[[nb 5]](#cite_note-24)</sup> The Council is typically concerned with taxation, infrastructure, and minor legislation (of which the Emperor holds veto power).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Imp P-20)</sup>

The leaders of the Elder Council may personally command Imperial Legions during times of war.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWQ6-25)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup> During extreme emergencies the wider Elder Council may return to the provinces to deal with local matters, but the Inner Council continues to govern.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HCO-11)</sup>

### Kingmakers, Regents, and Emergency Powers
The Council only tends to get noticed when its members are dealing with some emergency or power struggle related to the Emperor. The Council is responsible for recognizing and crowning the next ruler upon the death of the Emperor, and they are tasked with running the government in the interim under the leadership of the High Chancellor, the de facto leader of the whole Empire during such periods.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBArriana-26)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ASS.21-27)</sup>

The Council may also recognize an Emperor or Empress Regent to function as the Empire's ruler should the rightful Emperor be incompetent (if, for example, the Emperor is a minor or mentally deranged). Choosing the rightful Emperor theoretically is only a matter of birthright, but the Council has demonstrated some discretion in judging whether an individual's genealogical claims are accurate and whether that individual holds the closest relation to the original imperial bloodline (and, inevitably, various other considerations no doubt influence these decisions).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWQ-28)</sup> Numerous times throughout history, though, the members of the Council have recognized an Emperor not because of that person's birthright, but because that person had become Emperor-in-fact by seizing the capital, along with the Council.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWQ-28)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TIC-29)</sup>

In the extraordinary event that the Elder Council is forced to assume a longer-term regency over the Empire (if, say, something should happen to all known heirs to the Imperial throne), the Council asserts complete control over the military forces of the Empire.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ASS.21-27)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LTIC-30)</sup> This regency remains until such time as the Council recognizes an individual's claim to be Emperor. If an individual cannot be decided upon, the Elder Council Charter authorizes the Council to appoint the High Chancellor as Potentate (a sort of steward who takes on the role of Emperor in all but name).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RT-5)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-2920L12-31)</sup>

## History

### Alessian Empire
The Elder Council's history stretches back to the founding of the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) by [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Oblivion LS-32)</sup> Their earliest known act was in [1E 266](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_266), when the Council elected [Belharza the Man-Bull](Lore_Belharza.md) as the second Emperor of Cyrodiil following Alessia's passing into the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-33)</sup>

### Akaviri Potentate
Each Emperor of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) was advised by the leader of the Akaviri faction on the Elder Council, who bore the title Akaviri Potentate.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-34)</sup>

In [2E 282](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_282), Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) called together the Council in a meeting which has since been called the Council of Bardmont, where he announced universal martial law and the dissolution of all armies in the Empire other than his own. This ushered in one of the bloodiest periods of conflict in the history of Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOTFG-2)</sup>

### Advisors to the Longhouse Emperors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/ON-misc-Abnur Tharn.png/200px-ON-misc-Abnur Tharn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Abnur Tharn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Abnur Tharn.png) Grand Chancellor Abnur Tharn At the height of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), in [2E 534](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_534), Emperor [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) dynasty reconstituted the Elder Council.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SHot LE-35)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-IDRt EC-36)</sup> Durcorach was a [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) chieftain who had conquered the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) the previous year, and although he was unfamiliar with Cyrodilic culture, he nonetheless attempted to embrace it to reinforce his connection and commitment to the Empire.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SHot LE-35)</sup> To this end, he purged the Elder Council of dissenters while retaining or appointing experienced Councilors whom he deemed trustworthy.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-IDRt EC-36)</sup> The Elder Council continued to serve Durcorach's heirs, the Emperors [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md) and [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md), and also remained intact under the brief reigns of Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) and Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md). When the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) began in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the chaos that ensued scattered the Councilors, and the Elder Council was rendered defunct for an indeterminate period.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-MOTEC-37)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CTEC-3)</sup>

### Under the Septims
According to the disputed events in *[The Arcturian Heresy](Lore_The_Arcturian_Heresy.md)*, it was the Council who came upon the aftermath of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s successful ambush of the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md), which he then lied to them about, claiming that his Imperial Battlemage [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md) had killed his guards and tried to assassinate him.

Emperor [Pelagius Septim II](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_II.md), who inherited a debt-ridden Empire when he ascended to the throne in [3E 82](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_82), dismissed the entire Elder Council and allowed only those willing to pay great sums to resume their seats. He encouraged similar acts among his vassals, and though he returned prosperity to Tamriel, his critics cite consequences. First and foremost, wise but poor individuals were reportedly lost in the process.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWQ-28)</sup> The inexperience this introduced may have led to some of the troubles his son [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) faced when he in turn became Emperor.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup> Not only that, it may have cost Pelagius II his life, as it was rumored that a former Council member was responsible for poisoning and killing him.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWQ-28)</sup> But the most long-term effect of selling off seats on the Council was, obviously, that it became composed of the wealthiest people in the Empire. What Pelagius II may not have anticipated is that the collective power these individuals wielded would often prove equal or greater to that of the Emperor, and vicious power struggles emerged as a result.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup>

Twenty-two years after the death of Pelagius II,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TTET-38)</sup> the Empress [Kintyra II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) was captured at the beginning of the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) in [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121), and the Council was outwitted by the forces of the Wolf Queen [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) and her son, [Uriel Mantiarco](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md). Their attacks made the Council send the [army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) off to western [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and eastern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), leaving Cyrodiil itself weak. After a siege of the Imperial City which lasted only a fortnight, the Council recognized Potema's son as Emperor Uriel Septim III. In [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127), after Uriel III was captured and killed, they then recognized the next contender who laid claim to the Imperial City, Uriel III's uncle [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup> However, the emperors following the War of the Red Diamond yielded to the authority of the Council in order to keep some semblance of order in the land.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-PGE3All-39)</sup>

The throne passed to Cephorus' brother [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), who then died in [3E 145](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_145), and the Elder Council went on to suffer through the reign of Emperor [Pelagius Septim III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md), also known as "Pelagius the Mad".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup> It is said Pelagius III never really ruled Tamriel, and it was the Council, along with the Emperor's wife [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md), who made all the decisions.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TMOP-40)</sup> In [3E 147](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_147), Pelagius III reportedly declared himself incompetent to reign,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TDC-41)</sup> and the Emperor of Tamriel entered a private asylum. The Council made Katariah the Empress Regent of Tamriel, and after Pelagius III's death in [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153), she was formally recognized as Empress.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TMOP-40)</sup>

### Katariah and the Council
The disputes between Empress Katariah and the Elder Council are the stuff of legend. As a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) woman with no blood claim to the throne, she was vulnerable to all sorts of prejudices and suspicions, and some even considered her ascendancy to the throne to be the beginning of the end of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup> One of her disagreements with members of the Council famously resulted in a duel which her champion won. This victory, recounted in *[The Armorer's Challenge](Lore_The_Armorer%27s_Challenge.md)*, earned the Empress some respect in the Council.

Nevertheless, Katariah was uncomfortable in the Imperial City and traveled extensively across the Empire, leaving the Elder Council to keep the Empire running on their own. The Council assumed a great deal of power during the reigns of Katariah and her children, and evidently took a great liking to it. [Uriel Septim IV](Lore_Uriel_Septim_IV.md), in particular, found it impossible to command their fealty. He consistently found himself at odds with the Council, and consistently lost the battles. Their last victory over Uriel IV was posthumous: upon his death, they voted to disinherit his children, and instead recognized a cousin more closely related to the original Septim line as Emperor [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md). This led to nine years of fighting with Uriel IV's heir, as well as other insurrections around Tamriel which would drag on for a century.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup><sup>[[nb 6]](#cite_note-43)</sup>

### Uriel VI's struggles
Emperor Uriel Septim VI was crowned in [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290), but he was still a boy at the time. Though his mother was given a restricted Regency of the Empire, the Elder Council retained the real power, and its members enjoyed unrestricted, unlimited freedom to promulgate laws and profit from said laws. Uriel VI found it very difficult to retake the authority of the Emperor. He was given greater responsibility over the course of several years, but wasn't allowed full license to rule until he was already twenty-two years old, and by that time, the Council had stripped the office of the Emperor of almost all its real power, save the Emperor's right to veto. After six years of vetoing, bullying, and generally strong-arming the Council, Uriel VI could finally claim to truly rule Tamriel, and a sage soon coined the saying "[Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md) conquered [Esroniet](Lore_Esroniet.md), but Uriel VI conquered the Elder Council".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup>

When Uriel VI died, he left behind a strong Imperial throne for his sister [Morihatha](Lore_Morihatha.md). It is known that the Council frequently became frustrated with the slow pace of Morihatha's military campaigns despite their remarkable success, and it is commonly believed that a summarily tried and executed Councilman was the person who hired assassins to kill her in [3E 339](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_339). The succeeding Emperors of the Third Era maintained the dominance Uriel VI had established over the Council.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup>

### The Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/OBR-npc-High_Chancellor_Ocato_02.jpg/200px-OBR-npc-High_Chancellor_Ocato_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-High_Chancellor_Ocato_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-High_Chancellor_Ocato_02.jpg) High Chancellor Ocato However, around the onset of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), it was rumored that power had shifted back to the Council under High Chancellor [Ocato of Firsthold](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) fifteen years beforehand, during the rule of Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-LTM-44)</sup> The Crisis began with the assassination of Uriel VII and his heirs, and it would not end until the Council found a new Emperor to light the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md). Ocato ultimately recognized [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md)'s claim to the throne on the Council's behalf, but there was no opportunity to formally crown him before he perished at the [Temple of the One](Lore_Temple_of_the_One.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TOC-45)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-OBLight-46)</sup>

### The Stormcrown Interregnum
Following the Oblivion Crisis, the Elder Council could not find a suitable heir to the throne, so High Chancellor Ocato reluctantly agreed to become Potentate.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RT-5)</sup> The Elder Council was not as strong in leadership as they would have liked, and made efforts to consolidate and conserve the Empire and its Provinces, though with perhaps little effect. Skyrim appeared to be on the brink of secession, and the other provinces each had their own internal and external problems to deal with.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TPFrom The Ashes-47)</sup> Ocato's assassination circa [4E 15](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_15) precipitated the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RT-5)</sup> The Elder Council fractured, leading to years of ruthless in-fighting, plots and backstabbing. Many tried to claim the Ruby Throne. Violent, unnatural storms reportedly lashed the Imperial City several times during this anarchy, always with the eye of the storm looking directly down upon White Gold Tower.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RT-5)</sup> This chaos did not end until the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) warlord [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md) captured the Imperial City circa [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22) and deposed the Empire's ruler, Thules the Gibbering. Thules the Gibbering was not a well-liked ruler, but because he was Nibenese, many on the Council favored him over a Colovian usurper.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LOS-48)</sup><sup>:96</sup> [Hierem](Lore_Hierem.md), who was from an old and well-connected Nibenese family and a minister to Thules, helped smooth over the conquest by convincing the Elder Council to accept Titus as a liberator rather than a conqueror.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LOS-48)</sup><sup>:96</sup>

## Roles
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-nobilityfactionicon.jpg) The exact hierarchy of ranks and the nuances between them has not been spelled out, but can generally be understood as categorized below.

- **High Chancellor** (also **Grand Vizier**<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE1A-49)</sup> or **Grand Chancellor**),<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ESOMT-50)</sup> the foremost leader of the Elder Council.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-tes4o-51)</sup> This title and role is often held by the Empire's [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-14)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HCO-11)</sup>
- **Chief Councilor**, the secondary leader of the Elder Council.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CTEC-3)</sup>
- **Councilor** (also **Elder**<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Conc Of Eld-52)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Conc Of Eld2-53)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Background Game-4)</sup> or **Councilman**),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE-13)</sup> a member of the Elder Council.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CTEC-3)</sup> Dukes of Cyrodiil seemingly have seats on the Council.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAC-1)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Official Fan Ob Int4-54)</sup>

#### Imperial Battlemage
The **[Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md)** (also stylized as the **Imperial Battlemage of [Tamriel](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md)**, the **Imperial Battlemage of the Elder Council**, the **Imperial Battlemage of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md)**, or the **Grand Battlemage**) serves as the chief advisor to the [Tamrielic Emperors](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) on varying matters (such as: governance, warfare, battle magic, occult sciences, et al). When the role is extant, the Imperial Battlemage typically heads the Imperial Elder Council. The [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md) government of [Cyrodiil City](Lore_Cyrodiil_City.md) has featured an "age-old aristocracy of battlemages" since the early [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), but the Imperial Battlemage as the designated title for battlemage advisors didn't arrive in its current form until the [Reman Empire](Lore_Reman_Empire.md).

During the reign of the [Septim Emperors](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), it was customary for the future Imperial Battlemage to be chosen from the [Spire Council](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) (a elite clique of five powerful battlemages who administer and maintain the [Imperial Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) —an elite Mages Guild and Imperial War College). A strong relationship of some sort seems to exist between the Imperial Battlemage and the [Imperial Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), with the Imperial Battlemage having the direct power to promote or dismiss members of the guild. Another example of this relationship can be seen in the case of a regional Archmage of the Mages Guild seeking to undergo the Battlespire Test to become a member of the [Tamrielian](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md) elite. Finally, [Imperial Battlemage Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) of the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Empire.md) was himself a "Master-Wizard" of the Mages Guild, and also Ocato's battlemage bodyguard, Evangeline Beanique, was a "Warlock" of the Mages Guild.

#### Imperial Mananauts
The **[Royal Imperial Mananauts of the Elder Council](Lore_Royal_Imperial_Mananauts.md)** (or simply Imperial Mananauts) were a scientifically advanced faction that served under the Imperial Elder Council, founded in the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) under the [Reman Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). Though their original [expeditionary](Lore_Air_Transportation.md#Aetherial_Vessels) purpose to explore [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) was eventually deprecated, the Mananauts remained active as an [Imperial organization](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md) into the [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md) and [Third Eras](Lore_Third_Era.md).

## Notes
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* and *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* dialogue indicates that other city-states of Tamriel (like [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md)) have their own local Elder Council (also called "Council of Elders" or "Circle of Elders"), of which each member is singularly called as an elder.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Elysana Robe-67)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Conc Of Eld-52)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Conc Of Eld2-53)</sup> There are also references in the series to city aldermen.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-68)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-69)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Po N-70)</sup> - [Lord Castellian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Lord_Castellian) is the head of the Wayrest Elder Council.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Elysana Robe-67)</sup> The Council was debating whether or not to eliminate the law of succession, which they seemingly had the power to decide.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Elysana Robe-67)</sup>

### Annotations
1. [^](#cite_ref-9) *[Rising Threat](Lore_Rising_Threat.md)* notes that High Chancellor Ocato convened the "full" Elder Council following the Oblivion Crisis, suggesting that the Council may sometimes convene and act as a quorum.
2. [^](#cite_ref-10) It is never stated precisely how many members compose the Elder Council, though the [Elder Council Chambers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Imperial_Palace#Elder_Council_Chambers) as seen in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)* seated thirty.
3. [^](#cite_ref-18) *[How Orsinium Passed to the Orcs](Lore_How_Orsinium_Passed_to_the_Orcs.md)* suggests the Council might have some judicial authority, as well (or at least, may give their advice on how to interpret laws to judicial officers within the Empire).
4. [^](#cite_ref-23) The Council collected direct taxes from [Nova Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) and treated its leader as a recognized king, even though officially, the Orc homeland remained a county of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), not a recognized kingdom.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TWITW-22)</sup>
5. [^](#cite_ref-24) The Council chartered and funded the Black Horse Courier, a newsletter distributed for free to the citizens of Cyrodiil during the late Third Era.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Oblivion-8)</sup>
6. [^](#cite_ref-43) In [3E 246](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_246), during the reign of Uriel IV, the Council decreed that any man without a liege who occupied a castle for more than three months would be granted the rights and titles of that estate. This was meant to discourage absent and foreign land ownership, as there were some tricky property rights dilemmas in Skyrim at the time.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TRG-42)</sup>
7. [^](#cite_ref-66) Amaund Motierre's status as a Council member is inferred from the fact that he possessed an Elder Council amulet in [*Skyrim*](Skyrim_Skyrim.md).
