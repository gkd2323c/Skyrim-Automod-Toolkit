## Rabbit
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/ON-creature-Conjured_Apparition.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Conjured_Apparition.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Conjured_Apparition.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Conjured_Apparition.jpg) A rabbit **Rabbits** (also known as **bunnies** or **coneys**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> are small, harmless herbivorous [mammals](Lore_Mammal.md) which are sometimes hunted by [foxes](Lore_Fox.md) and other predators. Their only defenses are their small profile and their speed,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRRabbit-2)</sup> they often hide in underground dens known as warrens.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Not OS-3)</sup> [Hares](Lore_Hare.md) are a similar species of long-eared mammals,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> as such they are often conflated.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Rabbits are members of the Skeevera order, a classification for rodents and rodent-like creatures in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Other known members of this category include [skeevers](Lore_Skeever.md) and the common [rat](Lore_Rat.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Not OS-3)</sup>

Rabbits are very commonly hunted by the humanoid races for their meat or hide,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> with them even being the subject of hunting competitions.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Rabbit meat is found in many forms of [cuisine](Lore_Cuisine.md) such as stews.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Not OS-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Rabbits are occasionally tamed as pets,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Not OS-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> and can be trained to seek out [plants](Lore_Flora.md) for use in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The concept of a carnivorus rabbit is considered absurd.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Rabbit.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Rabbit), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Bog_Lurcher), [Skyrim VSE](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Dungeons)

## [Ra-Netu](Lore_Ra-Netu.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f8/ON-npc-Ra-Netu_%28female%29.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ra-Netu_%28female%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ra-Netu_(female).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ra-Netu_(female).jpg) A Ra-Netu [Ra-Netu](Lore_Ra-Netu.md) is a [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md) term used by [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) to describe the [risen dead](Lore_Undead.md), specifically [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md) raised from the bodies of their ancestors.

According to Redguard religious belief, striking the dead carries with it a terrible curse and is considered an act of dishonor. Because of this, fighting back against Ra-Netu and other forms of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) is difficult for the people of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). The [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md) provide protection over the dead of [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md); as long as their bodies are consecrated in the name of [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), they cannot be raised by even the most powerful necromancy. As a result, most Ra-Netu are raised using unconsecrated corpses, such as those lost at sea or bodies taken from forgotten graveyards.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Ra-Netu)

## [Rat](Lore_Rat.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/OB-creature-Rat.jpg/200px-OB-creature-Rat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Rat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Rat.jpg) A rat Rats are rodents common to all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They can be found both in city sewers and the wilderness, and are known to carry a variety of [diseases](Lore_Disease.md). They also can vary in size greatly. Rats are often kept by [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) for food.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OBPrima-15)</sup><sup>:53</sup> Pack rats can be trained to carry or drag items. [Skeevers](Lore_Skeever.md) are similar large rodents found commonly in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

There is an obscure [Khajitti](Lore_Khajiit.md) deity known as the [Rat-god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rat-god).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

- Variations: Giant Rat, Azra Rat, Assault Rat, Bog Rat, Umbric Rat, Telvanni Sewer Rat, Game Rat, Cave Rat, Sewer Rat

## Ray
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/SK-animation-Cavern_Stinger_Idle.gif/200px-SK-animation-Cavern_Stinger_Idle.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-animation-Cavern_Stinger_Idle.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-animation-Cavern_Stinger_Idle.gif) A Cavern Stinger Rays are creatures that live within [Glacier Crawl](Lore_Glacier_Crawl.md), an icy region in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) of eastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), near the border of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). They resemble aquatic [rays](Lore_Fish_R.md#Ray), but fly through the air instead of swimming. When attacking or defending themselves they will use the barbs at the end of their tails, with which they lunge at their target, akin to a spear thrust.

There are three known varieties; Cavern Stingers, Shadowrays, and Snowrays. Cavern Stingers, which have gray-blue skin and dark spots, are the weakest. The Shadowray variant has a brown coloration and gloomy physical appearance. However, the Shadowray also has the ability to blind their opponents, which is unique among the Ray subtypes. The strongest of the known varieties is the Snowray, the most vibrant variant with bright green skin adorned with orange spots. A substance known as [Snowray Powder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snowray_Powder) can be harvested from their bodies, which is considered a valuable [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) ingredient.

**Found in:** - [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Ray)

## [Riekling](Lore_Riekling.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/SR-creature-Riekling.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Riekling.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Riekling.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Riekling.jpg) A riekling [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md) are a race of blue-skinned ice [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), related to the other [Goblin-ken](Lore_Goblin-ken.md). The most common Rieklings are small in stature and found on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), but larger Rieklings were once present on mainland [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They are aggressive, and can be dangerous in groups. Despite being considered little more than beasts by the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), Rieklings are naturally cunning. Rieklings speak the goblin language, and can be conversed with by expert linguists. Some speak [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodilic.md), although they rarely say anything intelligible. Apart from this, they seemingly have little culture of their own. They scavenge what few relics they can find from the other races, and form strange attachments to these treasures.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Riekling.md).

## [Riekr](Lore_Riekr.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/BT-concept-Riekr.png/200px-BT-concept-Riekr.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Riekr.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Riekr.png) A riekr [Riekrs](Lore_Riekr.md) (or **Riekr Goblins**) are a race of [magically](Lore_Magic.md) gifted ice [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) from the province of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), related to other [Goblin-ken](Lore_Goblin-ken.md). They are sometimes described as [crossbreeds](Lore_Multiraciality.md) between standard Goblins and [Rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md), or as just a larger variety of Riekling. The Riekr are social creatures; each goblin is expected to be able to pull their own weight within their tribe, and those who can't are not treated kindly.

Their skin color varies between shades of white and blue. Many of them have long white hair, as well as prominent beards or mustaches.

The Riekr have a unique initiation system for those who show magical capabilities where they are exiled and have to buy their way back. The mage must then wait a certain period before they are allowed to buy their way back into the tribe with a food offering such as a freshly slain [deer](Lore_Deer.md), [bear](Lore_Bear.md), or [echatere](Lore_Echatere.md). Once the mage buys their way back into the tribe, they quickly rise in status to become a revered member of the tribe, gaining influence with the chief.

Riekr chiefs own objects that indicate their position of power in the tribe. The Riekr known as King-Chief Edu stole and used an ancient scepter created by the Orgak Orc clan, as a symbol of his power. Another Riekr chief who was notably a spellcaster had a totem as his symbol of power.

Riekr are known to work closely with the [Snow Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) that also inhabit Wrothgar. They are said to provide their Ogre allies with weapons and armor they wouldn't normally have access to. Riekr have a mostly antagonistic relationship with the more "civilized" peoples that reside in Wrothgar, sometimes organizing raiding parties to kidnap hunters. However they have been known to welcome outsiders in their ranks as long as they smell like a Goblin. Riekr have also been known to display attraction towards Orcish women.

Like other Goblins, Riekr tame wild beasts such as feathery [durzogs](Lore_Durzog.md). Also like other Goblins, Riekr are capable of speaking [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md), though often require training to do so. Some Riekr are tamed and raised to be servants, doing tasks such as setting tables.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Riekr), Betrayal of the Second Era

## Rootworm
Rootworms are strange translucent eight-foot long [worms](Lore_Worm.md) native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) that are commonly referred to as the "Underground Express", because of their ability to swallow travelers whole and carry them with the current.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-AA-44)</sup> They travel quite rapidly through tunnels formed by the roots of [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) trees.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-45)</sup> The digestive systems of rootworms are apparently quite slow, and travelers "could live in a rootworm's belly for months".<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-AA-44)</sup>

## [Ruinach](Lore_Ruinach.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/ON-creature-Ruinach.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Ruinach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ruinach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ruinach.jpg) A ruinach [Ruinachs](Lore_Ruinach.md) are a race of four-armed [Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Daedra) that serve as [Mehrunes Dagon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Mehrunes_Dagon)'s elite marauders, and have come to refer to themselves as the **Sword**, **Voice**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive1-46)</sup> and the **Fifth Hand of Mehrunes Dagon**.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-BPTMR-47)</sup> They are fanatical followers of their lord, and believe themselves an extension of his will. Indeed, as the physical manifestation of Dagon's fiery malevolence,<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-BPTMR-47)</sup> they share their lord's destructive tendencies. And although they are unintelligent, they make up for it in brutish strength.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-48)</sup>

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Ruinach)

## Rustbite Ringtail
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/ON-pet-Rustbite_Ringtail.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Rustbite_Ringtail.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Rustbite_Ringtail.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Rustbite_Ringtail.jpg) A Rustbite Ringtail Rustbite Ringtails are small, red-furred mammals that can be kept as pets. They are commonly perceived as adorable.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-49)</sup> Despite their name, they are not the same as the similarly named [Alinor Ringtails](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alinor_Ringtail).
