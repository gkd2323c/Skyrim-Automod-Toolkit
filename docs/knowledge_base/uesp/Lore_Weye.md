It can be found at the end of the main bridge connecting [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md) (and therefore the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md)) to the mainland.

## History
A settlement existed at Weye even before the construction of the bridge, as far back as the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Weye was initially located within the territory held by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), although the area changed hands many times over the course of the war. Weye's two [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) residents remained in the settlement during the war.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESO-1)</sup> A sewer grate near the settlement was used to access the Imperial City during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). This was done primarily by the Covenant army, which established a base within the sewers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

The bridge connecting to the Imperial City was built sometime later, although it seemingly did nothing to increase the size of the small settlement. By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Weye consisted of the Wawnet Inn and a fisherman's hut.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The inn's publican, Nerussa, was a collector of wines, and with the help of the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) amassed a large collection of [Shadowbanish Wine](Lore_Shadowbanish_Wine.md), which was quite ancient and rare by that point.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The Wawnet Inn was also home to [Rowley Eardwulf](Lore_Rowley_Eardwulf.md), a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) and a member of the [Crimson Scars](Lore_Crimson_Scars.md), who had come to Weye after he escaped a [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) [Purification](Lore_Purification.md) in [3E 421](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_421).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
