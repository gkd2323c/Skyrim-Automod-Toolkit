### County Chorrol
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg/200px-OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) Sancre Tor **County Chorrol** is a region centered around the eponymous city in western Cyrodiil and is ruled by the Count of Chorrol. Like other counties in the province, its size is comparable to the [holds](Lore_Hold.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-THOS-9)</sup> but their borders have yet to be explicitly defined on any map. The north-northwest corner of Cyrodiil, northwest of the [Orange Road](Lore_Orange_Road.md) in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) is confirmed part of the county. Some notable locations in this area include [Cloud Top](Lore_Cloud_Top.md), [Lipsand Tarn](Lore_Lipsand_Tarn.md), [Ninendava](Lore_Ninendava.md), and [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md). Chorrol is also located where the Great Forest meets the Colovian Highlands in the north.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBMap-7)</sup>

The County is known for its plentiful mines and timber. They also export wool and mutton. They are self-described as hard-working, tough and independent mountain people,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBArriana-10)</sup> as well as god-fearing people that adhere to the Ten Commands.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBChanel-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBSelvilo-12)</sup>

## History

### Merethic and First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/ON-place-Chorrol_02.jpeg/200px-ON-place-Chorrol_02.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Chorrol_02.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Chorrol_02.jpeg) The Great Oak circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) While not much is known about the town's early history, the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) knight [Cadwell](Lore_Cadwell.md) hailed from Chorrol,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LSD-13)</sup> and Chorrol has housed a chapel to [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) since at least the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). The position of Archbishop of Chorrol was filled by one Vincius Imbrex by no earlier than [1E 1051](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1051).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFA-14)</sup> The [Academy of Chorrol](Lore_Academy_of_Chorrol.md) was a scholarly institution which was based in the city at some point, but very little is known about it.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Ao H-15)</sup> The first Count of Chorrol was [Kantus Jeril](Lore_Kantus_Jeril.md), who was appointed for his heroism at the Battle of Pale Pass.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/ON-place-Chorrol.jpg/200px-ON-place-Chorrol.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Chorrol.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Chorrol.jpg) Chorrol circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The city had become a focal point for merchants in the shade of an oak tree since the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) when it was known as "Little Oak Place".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TCC-3)</sup>

[Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) was the Duke of Chorrol before [Emperor Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md)'s legalization of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship caused him to take up arms against the Emperor in the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Cot FC1-17)</sup> After being defeated at [Fort Ash](Lore_Fort_Ash.md), Varen took control of the Legion of Chorrol, enabling him to defeat Leovic's troops and force them to retreat into the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md), paving the way for the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovia.md) to unite and fight under his banner.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Ef EV-18)</sup> During the Colovian Revolt, Varen drew his strongest support from the Colovian Estates, including the old nobility of County Chorrol.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-19)</sup>

The powerful [Motierre](Lore_Motierre.md) family have also been firmly established in Chorrol since at least [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TCC-3)</sup> Chorrol was significantly affected by the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579) when earthquakes ruptured the ground, splitting the town and leaving a gaping fissure infested with [flame atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) that killed Doran, the town's prefect. Wooden walkways were erected over the chasm but the noxious fumes caused many to fall to their deaths while crossing.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TCC-3)</sup> Many buildings, including the Great Chapel, were damaged or destroyed by the constant earthquakes, with the Oak and Crosier inn resorting to nailing furniture to the floor.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Gil-20)</sup>. During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the town was initially located within the territory held by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), although it changed hands many times over the course of the war. However, the real power over the roads around Chorrol was determined by control over nearby Fort Rayles.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

At some point following the [Sack of Sancre Tor](Lore_Sack_of_Sancre_Tor.md) in [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Bo ST-22)</sup> a statue dedicated to [Saint Osla](Lore_Osla.md) entitled "The Touch of the Healer's Hand" was erected just inside the city gates.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OBPrima-23)</sup><sup>:361</sup> Known locally as "the fountain",<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup> this statue depicts Saint Osla ministering to the fallen at the Sack of Sancre Tor.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OBPrima-23)</sup><sup>:361</sup> It was erected in memory of all who died in that great battle. The district near the town's main gate came to be known as Fountain Gate following its construction. At some point, the statue seemingly was indeed a fountain and was surrounded by a pool, but by [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) it had dried up.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Gt C-4)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/OB-place-Chorrol.jpg/200px-OB-place-Chorrol.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol.jpg) The Great Oak of Chorrol [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg/200px-OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg) The statue of [St. Osla](Lore_Osla.md) at Fountain Gate At the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Chorrol was ruled by Countess [Arriana Valga](Lore_Arriana_Valga.md), widow of the hereditary Count [Charus Valga](Lore_Charus_Valga.md) of Chorrol.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Gt C-4)</sup> Circa [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), a political marriage was arranged between Lady [Alessia](Lore_Alessia_Caro.md) of Chorrol and Count [Marius Caro](Lore_Marius_Caro.md) of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup> In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Chorrol came under siege by the forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) when an [Oblivion Gate](Lore_Oblivion_Gate.md) opened outside the city walls during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the [Crimson Dirks](Lore_Crimson_Dirks.md) raided Colovian furs from the city.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Members of the Dirks would sometimes gather at The Grey Mare.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup>
