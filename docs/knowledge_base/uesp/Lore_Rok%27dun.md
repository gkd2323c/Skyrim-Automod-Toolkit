*Unleash our Ra Gada against the intruders! Crush them as we did the Nedes of old!*
—Rok'dun<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YR-1)</sup> **Yokeda** Rok'dun **the Flame** was a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) of the first rank. He held a distinguished position among The Four, an esteemed group of elite warriors distinguished during that period, and took a notable role in the early Yokudan invasion of the [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) homelands.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTP-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-place-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg) Hel Ra Citadel Little is known of Rok'dun's life. He hailed from the continent of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) and was a master of the art of sword-singing.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTP-3)</sup> He played a major role in the early Yokudan campaigns against the Nedic homelands of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He fought alongside the other members of the Four: Mamireh, Akamon, and Roshadon. Some sources associated these events with the First Yokudan invasion,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CA-2)</sup> while others claimed that, as an [Anka-Ra](Lore_Anka-Ra.md), Rok'dun instead served among the forces of [Tarish-Zi's](Lore_Tarish-Zi.md) incursion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TV-4)</sup>

At some point, he ascended to the esteemed role of Ansei of the highest rank. Before undergoing initiation, he was required to relinquish his most cherished weapon.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CA-2)</sup> His prowess was legendary, with myths recounting feats of unparalleled skill in battle. It is believed that he and the other members of the Four were favored by the Warrior Stars, granting them unparalleled abilities. Despite their formidable reputation, the fate of The Four remains shrouded in mystery. Scholars speculate on their demise, but their graves have never been discovered. Some even questioned their existence altogether,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TTP-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATC-5)</sup> yet they played a pivotal role in the conquest of [Hel Ra Citadel](Lore_Hel_Ra_Citadel.md) from the Nedes.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Yokeda_Rok.27dun-6)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), with the approval of [Kailstig the Axe](Lore_Kailstig_the_Axe.md), a band of [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) entered the halls and defeated the Anka-Ra within, among them Rok'dun the Flame,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YR-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATC-5)</sup> before confronting the [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) [Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md). The Warrior was ultimately overcome by the Undaunted and released from the Serpent's dominion. Thereafter, the Warrior joined the Celestial Thief in the effort to defeat the Serpent.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATC-5)</sup>
