*And it was the shepherdess Sed-Yenna who dared climb the hill when she heard his first cry, and at its peak she saw what it had yielded, an infant she named Reman, which is "Light of Man."*
—⁠ *[Remanada](Lore_Remanada.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Remanada-1)</sup> Sed-Yenna was a humble shepherdess who ascended [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md), where she discovered the infant [Reman](Lore_Reman.md). She named him and played a crucial role in securing his enthronement as the [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Remanada-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg/200px-OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) Sancre Tor The exact origins of Sed-Yenna are largely unknown, but she was remembered as a humble shepherdess and dedicated follower of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), and had a younger sister named Shonni-Et.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Remanada-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-2)</sup> According to the *[Remanada](Lore_Remanada.md)*, in the years preceding, but leading up to [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), when Reman had already reached adulthood,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE-3)</sup> King [Hrol](Lore_Hrol.md) encountered the spirit of Empress [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), who held the [Dragonfire Covenant](Lore_Dragonfires.md) in one hand and the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) in the other. In what may have been a divinely inspired, yet seemingly mad, attempt to restore the unity of the Empire, Hrol made a union with a hillock he believed housed Alessia’s spirit. He perished soon after, but nine months later, the mound of earth began to grow into a small mountain, stirring rumors among the local shepherds and [bulls](Lore_Cattle.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Remanada-1)</sup>

As this hill, which would later be called the Golden Hill, or Sancre Tor, continued to churn, a small community of believers gathered. It was Sed-Yenna, daring to ascend the hill after hearing a mysterious cry, who discovered at its peak an infant she named [Reman](Lore_Reman.md), meaning "Light of Man." In the child’s forehead was the Amulet of Kings, the Chim-el Adabal, burning with the dragonfires of ancient promise. None dared stop Sed-Yenna as she carried the newborn to the steps of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md). Upon the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md), the infant spoke as an adult, declaring, "I AM CYRODIIL COME."<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Remanada-1)</sup> Thereafter, it became the duty of Sed-Yenna and Shonni-Et to become the midwives of Reman, and to raise him in the Dibellan fashion of the [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-2)</sup> He later rose to become a grand ruler, known as the "Son of the West," uniting many disparate factions under his leadership during the [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) Invasion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE-3)</sup>

## Notes
- [Out-of-game texts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride%27s_Texts) such as *[Fireside Chat - Reman and the Shonni-etta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Fireside_Chat_-_Reman_and_the_Shonni-etta)* and *[The Shonni-etta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Shonni-etta)* provide additional details about Sed-Yenna and her family, offering further insights into her story and background as a vestal of the [Diblashuut](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FC-4)</sup>
- The character of Sed-Yenna is an homage to a character created by user Loranna Pyrel, named Sen-Yedda.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Loranna-5)</sup>
