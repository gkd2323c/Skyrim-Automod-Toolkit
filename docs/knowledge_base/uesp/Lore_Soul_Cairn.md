# Soul Cairn

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg/200px-SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg) The Soul Cairn The Soul Cairn is a [realm of Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Oblivion) filled with the trapped [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of the dead. Rather than being ruled over by any [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince), it is controlled by mysterious beings that call themselves the [Ideal Masters](Lore_Ideal_Masters.md). Necromancers sometimes enter a covenant with these malevolent masters, sending them souls in exchange for various powers. The vampire [Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md) theorized that when souls trapped in black [soul gems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Gems) are used to power an enchantment, the remnants are transported to the Soul Cairn to roam for eternity.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Serana-1)</sup> The Ideal Masters believe that by freeing mortals from life and dooming them to eternal undeath, they are being saved from meaningless hardship and gifted with peace. The Ideal Masters describe the realm as an otherworldly refuge dedicated to peace, love, eternal rest, and harmony.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IM-2)</sup> Mortal souls sent to the Soul Cairn eventually lose all memory of their former lives, with only newcomers to the realm retaining wisps of their former memories. Those who stumble across them will often hear blessings, curses and ghostly warnings.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BA-3)</sup><sup>:115</sup>

The realm was once accessible from the [voidgate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magic_Transportation) of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), but the link was severed after the citadel was partially destroyed.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Valerica once created a portal to the Soul Cairn within the ruined portion of Castle Volkihar. Her silver-lined portal vessel required four ingredients to activate: finely ground bone meal, purified void salts, soul gem shards, and the blood of herself or a close relative.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> This portal rejects the living, as only the undead (including vampires) and partially soul-trapped living beings are capable of entering.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Serana-1)</sup>

According to some sources the Ideal Masters are actually "disembodied entities living in a distant Platonic ideal" with their "only connection" to the realm being "small magical gems which provide a channel of communication and sensation with those bound in form to the Soul Cairn."<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BAAT-6)</sup>

## Geography and Inhabitants
*Once they struggled, hammered, cried
Fought for justice, honor, pride.
Now from time and tide released
They guard and serve in silent peace*
—Inscription from the Soul Cairn<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SCSGWN-7)</sup> The Soul Cairn is filled with all manner of undead. Some forms are unique to the realm, such as [Bonemen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boneman), [Mistmen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mistman), and [Wrathmen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrathman). These creatures are seen as blessed by the Ideal Masters and are mentioned in the *[Book of Life and Service](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Book_of_Life_and_Service)*, with there being a hierarchy of the Ideal Masters, Wrathmen, Mistmen, and then Bonemen.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Powerful mages can summon undead minions from the realm using [conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) magic. In their hunger for souls, the Ideal Masters often strike deals with [necromancers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy). In return for souls, the necromancers are given the ability to summon armies of undead from the realm, although they often fall prey to the Masters and end up trapped in the realm themselves.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Serana-1)</sup>

The Soul Cairn's landscape is desolate, bleak, barren, and grey, marked by blackened, dead ruins, bones, gravestones and "soul fissures". Nothing grows in the realm, apart from dead trees and a type of fungus called [Soul Husks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Husk). The realm contains many structures associated with burial, such as the Coffin Chapel and the Boneyard. The Chapel of Love is a building that contains the Emerald Gates, a portal that can be used to teleport to any of the outer realms, provided the user knows the name of the realm. The Emerald Gates are powered by the Mana Font, which is controlled by three rods, the Rod of the Pilot, the Rod of the Pillar, and the Rod of the Passage, collectively known as the Fingers of Life.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IM-2)</sup> Lightning strikes the ground constantly, and lightning attractors can harness this power to create Black Soul Gems.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IM-2)</sup> At least one [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) was found within the realm.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SC-10)</sup>

The Ideal Masters hoard many treasures, which are guarded by their undead minions. They have many powerful servants, such as the [Boneyard Keepers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keeper) and the [Reaper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper). Crystals throughout the realm serve as conduits for the Ideal Masters to speak to their minions or feed on their victims.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IM-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/SR-concept-Dawnguard_01.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Dawnguard_01.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Dawnguard_01.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Dawnguard_01.jpg) Skyrim concept art The [Vampire Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord) named [Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md) fled to the Soul Cairn through a portal of her own creation with an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). The Ideal Masters, failing to claim her soul, imprisoned her within the Boneyard and assigned the Keepers to maintain a force field around the structure. Sometime after this, the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Durnehviir](Lore_Durnehviir.md), seeking to secure his mastery of the skies through the use of necromancy, made a pact with the Ideal Masters in return for great powers of summoning. The Masters ordered him to guard Valerica until her death but neglected to inform the dragon that she was an immortal vampire. After eras spent living in the Soul Cairn, Durnehviir's body became accustomed to the realm, ensuring he could never leave the Masters' service.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

During [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s invasion of the Battlespire during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) invasion force entered the realm for use as a waystation between their own realms and their newly conquered domains. This displeased the Ideal Masters, as the Daedra were destroying their undead servants and plundering their treasure. Several [Morphoid Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morphoid_Daedra) were ordered to remain in the Soul Cairn by [Xivilai Moath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivilai_Moath).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-L3-14)</sup>

Many mortal captives from the Battlespire were brought to this realm and murdered by the Daedric invaders, including [Paxti Bittor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Paxti_Bittor) and Kirel Aman, trapping their souls eternally unless freed. An [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md) from the Battlespire passed through the realm on a quest to save a fellow apprentice from Dagon. This hero freed many trapped spirits, and was aided by the Ideal Masters in escaping the realm through the Emerald Gates into the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md). The apprentice was eventually successful in halting the flow of Daedra through the Soul Cairn by forcing them to relinquish their hold on the newly-conquered realms.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-L3-14)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) soul-trapped [Saint Jiub](Lore_Jiub.md) during a Daedric attack on the city of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md). His soul was sent to the Soul Cairn.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) and [Serana](Lore_Serana.md) entered the Soul Cairn in search of Valerica and her Elder Scroll. They defeated many of the Ideal Masters' servants, retrieving the scroll and rescuing Valerica.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
