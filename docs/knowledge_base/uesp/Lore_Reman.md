*"I AM CYRODIIL COME"*
—Reman<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBProphet-1)</sup> Reman Cyrodiil (sometimes enumerated as **Reman I**),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920:TLY-2)</sup> called the **Worldly God**, **Reman Born-a-Man**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-3)</sup> and **Old Reman**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBProphet-1)</sup> was a cultural god-hero of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), founder of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md), and the greatest hero of the [Akaviri Trouble](Lore_First_Era_Wars.md#First_Akaviri_Invasion) of [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBNarina-6)</sup> His name, Reman, means "Light of Man".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-R-7)</sup> Contrary to popular belief, the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) was not named after him; rather, he adopted the ancient [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) name for the province, Cyrod, as his surname.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Origin QA-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSOP3-9)</sup>

The formation of Cyrodiil as it is known today is credited to Reman, who incorporated aspects of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), the [Nibenay region](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley), and the sophisticated if strange culture of the defeated Akaviri into a common whole.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3C-10)</sup> He instituted the rites of becoming Emperor, which included the ritual geas to the Amulet of Kings.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBNarina-6)</sup> During his reign, records indicate [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine) resided in the White-Gold Tower where he is said to have helped Reman with the dubious draftsmanship of the Crendali Festivals. The vulgarities found within these festivals did nothing but hinder Imperial expansion efforts within the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) during Reman's tenure.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ICo DL-11)</sup>

## History

### Prolepsis
During a duel which is believed to have taken place at [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor) over 2,500 years before Reman's birth, the [Demigod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demigod) [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) purportedly screamed out the future leader's name.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSOP3-9)</sup>

### Birth and Youth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/GEN-misc-Reman_Cyrodiil.jpg/200px-GEN-misc-Reman_Cyrodiil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-Reman_Cyrodiil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-Reman_Cyrodiil.jpg) Sketch of Reman Little is known for certain about Reman's early life and ancestry. *[Remanada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remanada)* describes Reman as the product of a union between King [Hrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrol) (who was killed in the union) and the spirit of St. [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) (who was considered to be the land itself), and some other sources seem to corroborate this, though skeptics suspect that this heritage was a fabrication intended to legitimize his rule.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBProphet-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TBOTD-12)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-R-7)</sup> The text goes on to say that Sancre Tor means "Golden Hill", and that it is the place where Reman was conceived and born. He was reportedly found there by the shepherdess [Sed-Yenna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sed-Yenna) with the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), a soul gem of immense power, embedded in his forehead. She carried the infant directly to the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and placed him on the throne without contest.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-R-7)</sup> Further mythical accounts describe Reman being raised by priestesses of [Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella) and having many wives, to whom he was known as the "**Boy Crown Reman**" and "**Child Ut Cyrod**". [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) once manifested to proclaim that Reman was an "immortal fire" that bound heaven to the mundane, and blessed his wives with eternal youth and beauty.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-3)</sup>

### Akaviri Invasion
The invasion of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by forces from the continent of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) is what first allowed Reman, called a "son of the West", to unite many disparate factions under his leadership.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup> A large [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) armada landed on the northern coast and marched through [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) to the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md), defeating all in their path.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LOTD-13)</sup> However, by landing in Morrowind, the invaders had arisen the anger of the god [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), a member of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) that ruled the province. Realizing that they had a common enemy, Vivec and Reman reached an agreement to join forces to defeat the invaders completely.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3M-14)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBNarina-6)</sup> Many of the old forts and castles in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) are remnants of the network of fortifications Reman had built to defend against Akavir.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-IAC-15)</sup> The combined forces of Morrowind and the Army of Reman were successful in defeating the Tsaesci.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3M-14)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBNarina-6)</sup> Reman not only stopped the foreign invaders, he convinced them to help him build his own empire, by which he later conquered all of Tamriel except for [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBNarina-6)</sup> The Akaviri were the first to acknowledge him as [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md). In fact, it was the Akaviri who did the most to promote his standing as Emperor (although Reman himself never took that title in his lifetime).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TBOTD-12)</sup> They had apparently been searching for Dragonborn, and when they heard Reman's voice during the climactic battle at [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), they realized what he was, knelt, and swore fealty to him resulting in the formation of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-16)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CMAAS-17)</sup>

### Later Actions
After thwarting the Akaviri invasion in [1E 2704](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2704), he turned his attention to the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk). He divided the geographical [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) between the Imperial provinces of High Rock and Skyrim to reduce the power or incomes of any individual ruler, and the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) has existed as a separate entity ever since. Despite this, the Reach failed to be tamed, and not a decade passed without troops from either [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) or [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) marching into the Reach in another vain attempt to bring the Reachmen to heel.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-18)</sup> Emperor Reman's [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) also built [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md) in the mountains of the Reach to support their crusade against [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AOTD-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AOD-20)</sup>

Despite the alliance that Reman and Vivec had formed to repel the Akaviri, the relationship between Morrowind and the Second Empire did not remain cordial as mistrust between the two sides began to brew.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3M-14)</sup> Reman died in [1E 2762](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2762),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-OBSancre-21)</sup> by some accounts at the hands of the Morrowind guild of assassins, the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong), possibly under the order of Vivec.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PGE2-22)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-23)</sup> He was subsequently buried in Sancre Tor, which had enjoyed a resurgence of wealth and culture under his rule.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TLST-24)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-OBSancre-21)</sup> After two hundred years of rule, his dynasty ended with the assassination of his last heir, [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III) (also purportedly at the hands of the Morag Tong), bringing about the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1C-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup>
