# Eagle

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fc/ON-creature-Frost_Eagle.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Frost_Eagle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Frost_Eagle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Frost_Eagle.jpg) A frost eagle from Wrothgar [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/ff/SR-item-Eagle_Stone.jpg/200px-SR-item-Eagle_Stone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Eagle_Stone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Eagle_Stone.jpg) The eagle stone in Nordic ruins Eagles are [birds](Lore_Bird.md) of prey that can be found across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and carry cultural significance across the major races. Frost Eagles live in snowy regions like [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONFrost-1)</sup>

The Eagle carries a lot of significance in [elven](Lore_Mer.md) cultures.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ACTB-2)</sup> The **Aldmeri Eagle** (also called the **Golden Eagle**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONTreasures D-3)</sup> and the **Eagle of Summerset**)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM25-4)</sup> represents many things. Firstly, as a totem of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) religion<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONPlume-5)</sup> the eagle represents their ancestors, the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md), who descended from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) and became trapped on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOKB-6)</sup> The eagle is also associated with [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CM89-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONTreasure Idol-8)</sup> the chief divine of the Aldmeri pantheon.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONTreasure Winged-9)</sup> Though they are mostly [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) worshippers, the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) also built statues of Auri-El with the eagle behind him.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONTreasure Winged-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONGarlas-10)</sup>

The eagle also represents the First [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONRegal-11)</sup> who are sometimes called the eagle,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TEATC-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-FOTSV-13)</sup> and the royal Aldmeri family from [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONRegal-11)</sup> They say that on the night Princess [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) disappeared and began her travels across Tamriel, an eaglet landed on [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md)'s statue, among other auspicious signs.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ATUQ-14)</sup> As a symbol of the Aldmeri Dominion it also features in a variant of an [ouroboros](Lore_Ouroboros.md), a symbol associated with the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Legends-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-AUA-16)</sup>

Present in [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) myth is the great eagle [Zylvara](Lore_Zylvara.md), [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md)'s companion mount who brought him to battle against the man-demon, [Quarvish](Lore_Quarvish.md). Light armor pauldrons crafted by the Trinimac Cult have a winged motif meant to represent Zylvara.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CM22-17)</sup> [Sapiarch](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) axes have an eagle claw motif across the blade and inspired its three-pointed edge.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-CM62-18)</sup>

The town of Eagle Vale is located between the cities of [Haven](Lore_Haven.md) and [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md) on the southeastern coast in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ARVale-19)</sup> In [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), there was a town southeast of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) called [Eagle Moor](Lore_Eagle_Moor.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ARMoor-20)</sup> The village of [Eagle's Brook](Lore_Eagle%27s_Brook.md) is located in western [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) near [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONBrook-21)</sup> The eagle is present in the ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) totem religion,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONEagle Medallion-22)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-NOYB-23)</sup> though unlike most other animals, it does not correspond to a specific divine. The [hawk](Lore_Hawk.md) on the other hand, a visually similar totem, represents [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TSOG-24)</sup> Some Nords were known to practice the "blood eagle" on their foes.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-BB-25)</sup>

The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) who live in northwest Tamriel in the eponymous [region](Lore_The_Reach.md) venerate the legendary [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) king of the Reach, [Faolan](Lore_Faolan.md), whose name translates to "Red Eagle" in the [Reach-tongue](Lore_Reach-tongue.md). He was named as such for the screeching bird call that greeted his birth.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TLORE-26)</sup> A similar legend exists for the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) warlord that became Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md). Propaganda from that time explained that during Durcorach's early youth, he gained wisdom from surviving in the wilderness. The eagle taught him to hear tidings from faraway lands and to see into a man's heart with a single glance.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TCD-27)</sup> The [Eagleseer Clan](Lore_Eagleseer_Clan.md) are an ancient and powerful tribe in the Reach that is named after the animal.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONEagleseer-28)</sup>

The Eagle's Gaze is a type of [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) that enhances the wielder's vision.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONMonocle-29)</sup> A similar [alchemic](Lore_Alchemy.md) trait is used in a potent potion rumored to be used by Altmer archers.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-AAA-30)</sup>
