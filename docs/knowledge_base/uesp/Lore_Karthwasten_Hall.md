[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/SR-place-Karthwasten.jpg/200px-SR-place-Karthwasten.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Karthwasten.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Karthwasten.jpg) Karthwasten circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/AR-place-Karthwasten_Hall.jpg/200px-AR-place-Karthwasten_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Karthwasten_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Karthwasten_Hall.jpg) Karthwasten Hall during the Imperial Simulacrum Karthwasten (also called **Karthwasten Hall**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup> is a long-standing settlement located in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) near the banks of the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md), from which it gets its name.

Control of the settlement has changed hands between [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) multiple times, usually due to violent conflict.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Guide2Karth-2)</sup> The town was built around the ancient Sanuarach mine, which the Nords prized greatly for its silver. The Reachmen of the Karth, by contrast, traditionally held that mining was an affront to nature, and so would often ignore or even destroy the mine during their tenure.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Fialyn-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAfter The Storm-4)</sup>

Eventually, the fighting between the two factions would die down. By the Fourth Era, Karthwasten was known as a mining town with a mixed population. Despite being owned by a Reachman at this time, the town not only mined the Sanuarach, but the newer Fenn's Gulch Mine near the settlement as well.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRAinethach-7)</sup>

## History
The area surrounding the Karth River was historically the site of continuous conflict between Reachmen and the Nords of Western Skyrim, with the territory around Karthwasten in particular changing hands multiple times through the Eras.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Guide2Karth-2)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-STOTD-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FNP1-9)</sup> It was an active settlement during the rule of king [Hylfrald](Lore_Hylfrald.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), but it is unknown who controlled it at that time.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FNP1-9)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/ON-crown_store-Karthwasten_Silver_Boar.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Karthwasten_Silver_Boar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Karthwasten_Silver_Boar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Karthwasten_Silver_Boar.jpg) Karthwasten Silver Boar Karthwasten is mentioned briefly in *[The Crowned Dragon](Lore_The_Crowned_Dragon.md)*, a hagiography of [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCD-10)</sup> It asserts that around [2E 490](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_490),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SHOTLE-11)</sup> at just five years of age, he rescued his village from starvation during a harsh winter by hunting a herd of one hundred [elk](Lore_Elk.md) from Karthwasten to the high peaks of the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCD-10)</sup>

Numerous stories of woe and treachery were told about the settlement over the years. The most prominent one was [*The Seeping Sickness of Karthwasten*](Lore_Scary_Tales_of_the_Druadach,_Book_2.md). It involved the witch [Raven's Hand](Lore_Raven%27s_Hand.md) and Ulgar Stonebeard. In this story, a Reachfolk clan reclaimed the settlement from the Nords, and roughly a year later, they were obliterated by a scheme orchestrated by Raven's Hand, with the involvement of Ulgar and his men. The exact date of these events is unclear, but they can be approximately placed during the Sixth Century of the Second Era.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-STOTD-8)</sup>

By the mid-500s of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the aforementioned conflict had reached a stalemate. Although the Nords had successfully seized control of the territory north of Karthwasten, Karthwasten itself remained in Reachman hands, under the leadership of the [Crow-Eye Clan](Lore_Crow-Eye_Clan.md), led by its spiritual leader [Shaman](Lore_Reachfolk_Shamans.md) [Ulvoch](Lore_Ulvoch.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Guide2Karth-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Fialyn-3)</sup>

At some point prior to [2E 558](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_558) the village was visited by [Camhain Tharn](Lore_Camhain_Tharn.md) and described in his *[A Reach Travel Guide](Lore_A_Reach_Travel_Guide.md)*. He stated that its local Reachfolk population was willing to trade with outsiders, accepting both empire's gold and goods for barter.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ARTG-12)</sup>

In 2E 577, [Jarl Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md) declared that the seized land would now be known as a new hold, [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md), so that it, and its newly-built capital [Karthwatch](Lore_Karthwatch.md), could serve as a bulwark against further Reachman incursions from Karthwasten.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Guide2Karth-2)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Icereach Coven began performing Harrowstorm rituals throughout Western Skyrim; stealing the souls of its residents to resurrect the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). Notably, both of these Karth settlements were chosen as targets by the Coven. Karthwatch was utterly destroyed in their attack.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TBOOK-13)</sup> However, Karthwasten survived, largely thanks to the defensive wards the Crow-Eye Clan maintained, but numerous inhabitants of the village were still affected by the [harrowstorms](Lore_Harrowstorms.md) and turned into harrowed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Fialyn-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAfter The Storm-4)</sup>

In the same year, the village was visited by [an outsider](Lore_The_Vestige.md) who was willing to aid locals with their problems related to the Gray Host activity in the region. Local shaman accepted their help and requested them to help his apprentice with the renewal of the wards. They collected materials with a connection to Karthwasten and the surrounding countryside and offered them to the spirits. However, the final part of the ritual was disturbed by the [Netherroot](Lore_Netherroot.md), a plant that was cultivated by the Gray Host in the Sanuarach Mine. After the mines were cleared, and Gray Host defeated the ritual was finished successfully and the wards have been restored.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Fialyn-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAfter The Storm-4)</sup> Damaged buildings were not restored, however, as they were seen as "echoes left over from those who did not belong".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SU-14)</sup>

The village throve once again and the outsider was named a Crow-Eye in spirit and a friend to the Clan.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Fialyn-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SU-14)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAfter The Storm-4)</sup> Shaman Ulvoch attended the moot during which regional leaders Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of the Reach, and Jarl [Svana](Lore_Svana.md) and King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) of Skyrim signed the peace treaty between the Reach, Eastern Skyrim, and Western Skyrim.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SC-15)</sup>

During the Second Era, Karthwasten was known for its domesticated [Silver Boars](Lore_Boar.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KSB-16)</sup>

### Third Era
By the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the town had come under imperial control and was now known as Karthwasten Hall. Ruled by Duke Alarke, they maintained a rivalry with nearby [Granitehall](Lore_Granitehall.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arena-1)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Amber Guard](Lore_Amber_Guard.md) in the northeast, and [Granitehall](Lore_Granitehall.md) in the southeast.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Map Arena-17)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Sometime during the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) the town of Karthwasten was the site of a massacre allegedly committed by the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), who disputed their responsibility for the bloodshed. The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) would later demand compensation for the massacre from the Stormcloaks as a condition for a temporary truce during negotiations at [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

By this time, Karthwasten was one of the last pieces of land in Skyrim that was owned by a Reachman, in this case, [Ainethach](Lore_Ainethach.md). His family had owned Karthwasten for generations. Ainethach was disliked by both the Nords and the local Reachmen, who saw him as a race-traitor. During his stewardship, Karthwasten was targeted by the Silver-Blood family of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). Whether or not the mining town was ultimately claimed by the family is unknown, but it remained active regardless.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRAinethach-7)</sup>

At around the same time, the Sibyl of Markarth had passed away, and a new one was needed for the [House of Dibella](Lore_House_of_Dibella.md) in the city. The priesthood had discovered the young girl, [Fjotra](Lore_Fjotra.md) in Karthwasten, but a group of Reachmen known as the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md) had taken her to [Broken Tower Redoubt](Lore_Broken_Tower_Redoubt.md). From there, the young girl was freed by [someone](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) sent by the Temple of Dibella, and taken back to Markarth, where she would serve as the Dibellan Sybil of Markarth.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

## Notes
- Some sources claim that after the fall of [Snowhawk](Lore_Snowhawk.md) to [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) and [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) forces during the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md), Karthwasten rose to become one of the most influential settlements in the region. Many survivors of Snowhawk resettled elsewhere, with a number choosing to make their homes in Karthwasten. Although Nordic and Reachfolk clans fueled by Imperial meddling continued to feud, peace was maintained for a time. However, in [3E 425](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_425) the tensions escalated beyond control, culminating in open conflict between the Stone-Fists of Markarth and the Silver-Bloods of Karthwasten. When a fierce blizzard struck during the fighting, each faction discovered the other's city left largely undefended and seized it with minimal bloodshed. Years later, the incident became known as Helvar's Mistake or Svarra's Mistake, depending on which side recounted the tale. When the storm finally cleared three days later, both sides realized they had not achieved a true conquest so much as an exchange of cities. Minor skirmishes persisted for years as each faction attempted to reclaim its original seat of power, until High Queen Una marched on both cities and compelled them into peace. To preserve family traditions and legal claims, a compromise was reached: the names of the towns were formally swapped. As a result both ruling families continued to claim that they have governed their respective cities for generations.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SITE-20)</sup>
