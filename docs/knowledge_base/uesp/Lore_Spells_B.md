## Banish
Banish is a spell of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) school. It is used to damage, repel and send conjured entities back to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POSOM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim Banish-3)</sup> Some sources attribute banishing spells to the school of [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md) as well.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Mysticism Spells-4)</sup> Known variants of this spell include:

- Banish Daedra<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim Banish-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Mysticism Spells-4)</sup>
- Expel Daedra<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim Banish-3)</sup>
- Banish Undead<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim Banish-3)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Mysticism_Spells#Banish_Daedra), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Banish.md)

**Spell schools:** [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md), [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md)

## Blind

## Bound Weapons and Armor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png/200px-LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png) A Breton wielding Bound Weapon [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-icon-effect-Bound_Battle_Axe.jpg) [Bound items](Lore_Bound_Items.md), predominantly **bound armors**, and **bound weapons**, also known as **bound armaments**, are conjured ephemeral objects created from [daedric entities](Lore_Daedra.md). The practice of conjuring bound items falls within the domain of the [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) school of magic, specifically referred to as **Armamancy**. Practitioners of this art were known as armamancers, and sometimes as arcane warriors. Swords formed through this method are sometimes called **summoned blades**. Axes, on the other hand, were referred to as **conjured axes**.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Bound_Items), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Bound_Weapon), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Bound_Weapon.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Bound_Armor), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Glenumbra_Sorceress)

**Spell schools:** [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md)

## Buoyancy
Buoyancy is an [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md) spell that causes target to weigh less and allows them to breathe underwater.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oblivion Spells-5)</sup> Some variations of this spell increased target's speed in water, and were associated with the element of the frost and the school of [Thaumaturgy](Lore_Thaumaturgy.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Thaumaturgy Spells-12)</sup> Buoyancy was sometimes considered a variant of the [Swift Swim](Lore_Spells_S.md#Swift_Swim) spell.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Morrowind Buoyancy-13)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Thaumaturgy_Spells#Buoyancy), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Alteration_Spells#Buoyancy), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Spells#Buoyancy)

**Spell schools:** [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md), [Thaumaturgy](Lore_Thaumaturgy.md)
## Burden
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-icon-Burden.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-icon-Burden.png) Symbol of Burden [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-icon-effect-Burden.jpg) Burden is an [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md) spell that amplifies the target's encumbrance, often rendering them immobile and preventing movement.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Burden Morrowind-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Burden Oblivion-15)</sup>

Known variants of this spell include:

- Burden<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Burdening Touch<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oblivion Spells-5)</sup>
- Burden of Sin<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Crushing Burden<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Crushing Burden of Sin<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Crushing Burden Touch<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Cruel Weary<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Dire Weary<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Encumbering Touch<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oblivion Spells-5)</sup>
- Great Burden of Sin<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Heavy Burden<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Heavy Burden Touch<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Hindering Touch<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oblivion Spells-5)</sup>
- Oppressing Grasp<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oblivion Spells-5)</sup>
- Weary<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Morrowind Alteration Spells-16)</sup>
- Weight of the World<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Oblivion Spells-5)</sup>

There are other, more complex spells incorporating the Burden effect such as Harthoon's Heavy Eyes, Trespasser's Bane, Hand of Odros, and Wrath of Odros.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Morrowind Unique Spells-1)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Burden), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Burden)

**Spell schools:** [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md)
