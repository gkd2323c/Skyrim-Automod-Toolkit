# Barsaebic Ayleids

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_10.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_10.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_10.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_10.jpg) Barsaebic Ayleids The Barsaebic Ayleids (or simply the **Barsaebics**) were a [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) subculture that emerged from the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) -worshipping Ayleids and were subsequently driven into exile following the [Scouring of Wendelbek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scouring_of_Wendelbek) in [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198). They occupied northeastern [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and potentially parts of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ASi V-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

## History
Barsaebic culture emerged as a result of the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md), a civil conflict that spanned the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era) to [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198) between the conservative Aedra-worshipping Ayleid clans and the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphiles.md) clans. The conflict was ended in 1E 198, when King Glinferen of [Atatar](Lore_Atatar.md) led a combined force of Daedraphile warriors against the traditionalist Barsaebic forces of [Wendelbek](Lore_Wendelbek.md), driving the Aedra worshippers from the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and into Black Marsh.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ASi V-1)</sup>

The Barsaebics went on to establish many [settlements](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md#Black_Marsh) in Black Marsh, particularly in the regions of [Arnesia](Lore_Arnesia.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3A-4)</sup> [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), and [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> They were responsible for founding a number of major cities, including [Silyanorn](Lore_Silyanorn.md) (modern-day [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md)),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> [Twyllbek](Lore_Twyllbek.md) (modern-day [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md)),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONTwyllbek-8)</sup> [Archon](Lore_Archon.md), and [Thorn](Lore_Thorn.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3A-4)</sup> They came into conflict with the native [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), [Orma](Lore_Orma.md), [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md), [Horwalli](Lore_Horwalli.md), and [Lilmothiit](Lore_Lilmothiit.md) in an attempt to carve out a place in the swamplands of Black Marsh.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3A-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FMJ3-10)</sup> One such conflict involved a Barsaebic attempt to locate the [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md), a relic of the [Root-Whisper Tribe](Lore_Root-Whisper_Tribe.md) of Argonians native to [Deepmire](Lore_Deepmire.md). The Remnant was created by the tribe's [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree to protect the Argonians' [souls](Lore_Souls.md) from being stolen by the Barsaebic invaders.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FMJ3-10)</sup> The hunt for this relic resulted in the creation of [Eld Angavar](Lore_Eld_Angavar.md), a realm of Ayleid pathways and Nexus Gates. A group of Barsaebics ultimately invaded Root-Whisper Village but were unsuccessful in obtaining the Remnant.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESORemnant-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> An enormous battle between the Barsaebics and local Argonians is said to have taken place for control of the land where Silyanorn was eventually built.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The Barsaebics were also known to have enslaved local Argonians for use as laborers and [flesh sculptures](Lore_Flesh_Sculpture.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Captured Time-14)</sup>

In addition to their worship of the Aedra, the Barsaebic Ayleids venerated the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md), a set of statues traditionally held in the [Temple of the Ancestors](Lore_Temple_of_the_Ancestors.md) on [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-15)</sup> Loriasel in Shadowfen was built by the Barsaebics as a temple to venerate the Ten Ancestors.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The Ayleids of Loriasel were responsible for the creation of [keystones](Lore_Keystones.md), relics of power that were created through the torment and fear of Argonian captives.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

When King Glinferen sent an envoy to Gideon seeking aid during the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) circa [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242), the Barsaebics bluntly refused to help. This is accredited with hastening the downfall of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

The Barsaebics eventually faded from history, leaving behind nothing but haunted ruins.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOZahra-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> According to legend, the Ayleids of Stormhold were conquered by the Hist.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONDiina-21)</sup> Although it is believed that the Barsaebic Ayleids were so far removed from the Heartlands that they were spared from [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s uprising,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3A-4)</sup> some Barsaebic settlements were attacked by Alessian forces. For example, the [Temple of Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Sul) in Shadowfen was sacked by the Alessians.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Captured Time-14)</sup>

Some Barsaebic necromancer kings survived by becoming undead [mummies](Lore_Mummy.md). Known as [Fenlords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fenlords), these revenants are said to have once ruled Black Marsh. When their slaves rose against them, the Fenlords had them executed and revived as undead. They eventually disappeared, possibly as a result of Alessian persecution. Some instead believe that the Fenlords used the [Crown of Ascension](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown_of_Ascension) to leave [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) forever.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOZahra-19)</sup> In truth, some Fenlords continue to dwell in the depths of Barsaebic ruins, ruling over the undead servants that still guard their tombs.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOFenlord-22)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOZahra-19)</sup>
