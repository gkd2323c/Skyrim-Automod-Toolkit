# Crown of Nenalata

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/OBR-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg) Ayleid Crown of Nenalata circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) The Ayleid Crown of Nenalata is an ancient [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). Its appearance varies, sometimes appearing as a lightweight [Moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) helm, sometimes as a simple crown. It can be distinguished from other Ayleid crowns by a royal glyph emblazoned upon it. Superficial enchantments grant the wearer spell reflection and increased skill at [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md) and [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) magic, although the true potential of the crown is seemingly much greater. It is an immensely valuable treasure.

## History
The crown was traditionally worn by the king of the Ayleid city-state of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md), a [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphile.md) settlement at the mouth of the [Silverfish River](Lore_Silverfish_River.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The city-state is famed for being the longest-lasting Ayleid outpost within Cyrodiil's borders, due largely to their allegiance to the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md). The crown's final bearer was [Laloriaran Dynar](Lore_Laloriaran_Dynar.md), the so-called Last King of the Ayleids, who inherited it from his father some time before [1E 331](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_331). It had seemingly left his possession by the time he was imprisoned in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) in [1E 484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_484).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the crown had come into the possession of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). However, the crown was stolen by a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) man who was apparently practicing the Rite of Theft. He traded it back to the Dominion for a song, confounding the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md). The crown was brought to a secure vault in the newly-built city of [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) for safekeeping, alongside various other Ayleid artifacts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the crown was purchased by an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholar of the Ayleids named [Herminia Cinna](Lore_Herminia_Cinna.md). An Altmer collector named [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) desired to buy the crown from her, planning to use it in the throne room of Nenalata to transform himself into the King of Nenalata and lead a rebirth of Ayleid civilization. Rightly fearing Umbacano's ulterior motives, Cinna refused to sell, instead hoping to trick him into accepting the Crown of Lindai. Umbacano sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to steal the crown from Cinna's house in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), although it is unknown if the Hero went through with this or recovered the other crown instead. Regardless, Umbacano was killed later that year during an expedition to Nenalata.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBSecrets-3)</sup>
