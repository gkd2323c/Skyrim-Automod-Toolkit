*Tamrielic history is positively bursting with unexplained oddities!*
—Amalien<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Amalien-1)</sup> Amalien was a prominent [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) scholar and antiquarian active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was a member of the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md), and an expert in subjects related to historical enigmas and arcane phenomena.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Amalien-1)</sup>

## History
*The Cult cares nothing for creation. For life. They care only about power. They care only about the foul contracts they've scribed. Atrocities that wrest control of Tamriel from its people. Dark machinations on behalf of inscrutable, distant powers that do not care if we live. Only what we can do for them.
We reject their cruelty. We reject their dispassionate destruction. We create, in spite of them, because that is what life is. It is an act of creation.*
—Amalien<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATSF-2)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e4/ON-wallpaper-Celebrate_10_Years_of_Amazing_Stories_and_Characters_with_this_New_Wallpaper-7680x4320.jpg/200px-ON-wallpaper-Celebrate_10_Years_of_Amazing_Stories_and_Characters_with_this_New_Wallpaper-7680x4320.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Celebrate_10_Years_of_Amazing_Stories_and_Characters_with_this_New_Wallpaper-7680x4320.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Celebrate_10_Years_of_Amazing_Stories_and_Characters_with_this_New_Wallpaper-7680x4320.jpg) Amalien sitting beside Kireth and Raynor Vanos (center, background) Amalien hailed form the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Amalien-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Summerset-3)</sup> She was born with a twisted spine, a condition that made walking impossible.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Amalien-1)</sup> However, she was never branded a hulkynd, as her parents rejected the cultural obsession with perfection and instead raised her with love and support, despite the social challenges they faced.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LTP-4)</sup> Eventually, she left Summerset, feeling that there was little left for her to learn there. She found the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md), the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), and the [Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) stifling, too many rigid rules and narrow minds, with no room for risk or innovation. Seeking adventure, she joined the Antiquarian Circle at the University of Gwylim to explore the world. She was also a skilled engineer and designer, responsible for creating the plans for her wheelchair, which greatly improved her mobility.

By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she was residing at the University's Solitude branch in Western Skyrim, where she jestingly referred to herself as the "resident eccentric." Ugron gro-Thumog, a colleague from the Circle, contributed to her move to Solitude's Gwylim Annex. She primarily focused on history related to unconfirmed historical mysteries and bizarre, arcane phenomena. Her favored topics included the disappearance of the Dwemer, the secrets of the Hist, the mysteries of the Zero Stone, and other similar enigmatic subjects.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Amalien-1)</sup> As of then, she also discovered parts of a [vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md), a mechanical mount.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Verita-5)</sup> While in Skyrim, she worked on a theory regarding the Snow Elves, Dragons, Dwemer, Magnus the Divine, a group of Atmoran skalds and six-legged mammoth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Amalien-1)</sup> She collaborated with several other antiquarians, including [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md), Reginus Buca, Ugron gro-Thumog, Verita Numida, and Muzah-Tei. Amalien was notably young, especially for her race, as she was considered a junior compared to Reginus.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Solstice-6)</sup>

At some point in 2E 582, she conducted the interview with [the Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md) for the University of Gwylim, as Gabrielle Benele, the usual host, was called away on important business and entrusted the series to her. The interview focused on Tamrielic [holidays](Lore_Holidays.md) and the traditions associated with them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LATH1-7)</sup> The host continued the series by speaking with Sister Chana Nirine of the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), discussing the role of the Order and the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) entrusted to them. Sister Chana caught Amalien's attention following a recent incident off the southern coast of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LATH2-8)</sup> involving [Orpheon the Tactician](Lore_Orpheon_the_Tactician.md)'s schemes,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCN-9)</sup> although she emerged unharmed thanks to the intervention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md). During the conversation, Sister Chana shared an excerpt from *The Pilot's Purchase*, which Amalien speculated could be an unknown fragment of [Udhendra Nibenu](Lore_Udhendra_Nibenu.md). She had sent several letters to Sister Chana and her Order seeking clarification on this matter but had not received a response at the time. Additionally, Amalien inquired about the nature of the [ancestor moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md). She was also responsible for editing the interview text and expressed her belief that by the time the next installment of the series was published, Gabrielle Benele would have returned from her urgent matters.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LATH2-8)</sup>

However, that was not the case, as Gabrielle perished while serving as the acting Guildmaster of the Mages Guild during the war against the Worm Cult. Amalien extended her deepest condolences to Gabrielle's sister, [Sara](Lore_Sara_Benele.md), and the entire Benele family, representing the University of Gwylim and the Antiquarian Circle. She continued to act as the editor for the series of interviews in Gabrielle's memory. She established correspondence with Prince [Azah](Lore_Azah.md) and Regent [Karinwe Corelanya](Lore_Karinwe_Corelanya.md) during the first of these interviews, which focused on the Stirk Fellowship and the broader history and culture of the isle of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATSF-2)</sup> After those events, she continued the series. The next installment focused on the worship of [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) and the celebrations of [Heart's Week](Lore_Heart%27s_Week.md). Determined to learn more, she sought out priest Kuzam-jo and sent letters across the continent in search of the elusive priest, only to discover that he had been the one returning her correspondence all along. Before Heart's Week began in [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md), she briefly visited Kuzam-jo in person. She remained in Belkarth while he made his final preparations for the festival, an ambitious new celebration intended to promote love and light across Tamriel. During this time, she observed his work closely and later served as editor for the interview.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LAHDMM-10)</sup>

Many prominent figures active in the decade following the onset of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) were immortalized in a painting commemorating their adventures. Among them was Amalien, depicted sitting at a table alongside fellow scholars Kireth and Raynor Vanos, Tharayya, and Clarisse Laurant, all observing the [Augur of the Obscure](Lore_Augur_of_the_Obscure.md) held by the latter.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

## Research
As a member of the Antiquarian Circle at the University of Gwylim, Amalien worked with a wide variety of relics and historically significant items, contributing her expertise to the study of numerous artifacts from across Tamriel and beyond.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Amalien-1)</sup>

### Elsweyr
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/ON-furnishing-Shrine_of_Boethra.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Shrine_of_Boethra.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Shrine_of_Boethra.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Shrine_of_Boethra.jpg) Shrine of Boethra In Khenarthi's Roost, Amalien studied ancient Altmeri manuscripts about their apparel, a Khajiit skooma pipe, an ancient Khajiiti fishing rod, and a piece of enchanted vampire apparel.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Khenarthi-12)</sup> Amalien also examined the Altmeri apparel notes discovered in mainland Elsweyr, along with Khajiiti religious censers, architectural arches, and ornamental mirrors depicting the moons.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Reaper-13)</sup> She also studied the Dragonguard's weapons and ancient lures she believed were designed for dragons, Khajiiti wells, and ancient depictions of Boethra.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SElsweyr-14)</sup> She also examined item she theorized to be the killing claw of Vashrjo the Killer, a prayer cloth she suspected belonged to Queen [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](Lore_Anequina_Sharp-Tongue.md), Khajiiti water vessels, and [Moth Priest](Lore_Moth_Priests.md) cleansing bowls.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-NElsweyr-15)</sup> She also studied fragments of the Rakkhat's Voidmantle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Solstice-6)</sup>

### Black Marsh
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-furnishing-Painted_Stone_Frog.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Painted_Stone_Frog.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Painted_Stone_Frog.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Painted_Stone_Frog.jpg) Painted Stone Frog Amalien studied ancient Ayleid maps of the Shadowfen, old Argonian toys, a Kothriningi relic linked to Clavicus Vile, and a frog statue.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Shadowfen-16)</sup> In Murkmire, she studied Naga traps, a bowl she theorized was used by ancient Argonian tribes to collect sunlight, and a fragment of the Ring of the Wild Hunt.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Murkmire-17)</sup> In Blackwood, she studied parts of the Argonian relic Gaze of Sithis as well as enchanted Kothringi garments.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Blackwood-18)</sup>

She also analyzed Argonian cultural relics from the isle of Solstice, including necklaces incorporating conch shells from the Tide-Born tribe, skeletons of large crocodiles, ancient stonecarving tools, totems and stonework from the Stone-Nest tribe and fragments of a bismuth Argonian music box. She also studied an ornate enchanted piece of jewelry named after the Monomyth. Amalien also examined vitrified soul crystals linked to the Writhing Wall.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Solstice-6)</sup>

### Summerset Isles
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Echoes_of_Aldmeris_03.jpg) Echoes of Aldmeris [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png/200px-ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png) Prismatic Sunbird Feather Among the items Amalien researched in her home province of the Summerset Isles were armor designs of the ancestral Altmer, maps of Auridon dating back to the formation of the Mages Guild, and tokens commemorating the Skywatch celebrations of freedom from the Sload. She also studied embellishments from the era of the Aldmer, including prayer beads made of aetherquartz that may predate the Altmer, an artistic rendition of Aldmeris, the ancient homeland of the Aldmeri, inert anemone inlays linked to the Sload, a particularly ornate Calian aetherquartz sphere, which she theorized once belonged to a Grand Athelan or another important religious figure, and a set of Nilumin River Pearls, highly prized by the Ancient Aldmer for use in alchemy and magic.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Auridon-19)</sup>

On the main island of the archipelago, among the items discovered were an apraxic decanter made from shattered aetherquartz calian, a map of the region, a badge she speculated was linked to the Jurisreeves, an altar, a music box, an ancient family tree document related to an Altmeri family dating back to the first landing, a Prismatic Sunbird Feather used by the [Sun Birds of Alinor](Lore_Sun_Birds_of_Alinor.md), and even researched the Alinor Allemande dance.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Summerset-3)</sup>

### Pyandonea
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/41/ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg) Maormeri Serpent Shrine Among the findings she studied related to Pyandonea was a statue of a sea serpent, which Amalien theorized might represent Orgnum himself, along with a pipe she believed to be of Maormeri origin—both of which were discovered in Auridon.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Auridon-19)</sup>

### High Rock, Wrothgar and the Systres Archipelago
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/ON-hat-Nighthunter%27s_Cowl.jpg/200px-ON-hat-Nighthunter%27s_Cowl.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-hat-Nighthunter%27s_Cowl.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-hat-Nighthunter%27s_Cowl.jpg) Nighthunter's Cowl She studied an ancient map of [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), a figurine she speculated depicted [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) and [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), a Band of Water, a piece of vampiric armor, and the fragment of the [Ring of the Pale Order](Lore_Ring_of_the_Pale_Order.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Glenumbra-20)</sup> She studied a seal of [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md), a throne of [House Spenard](Lore_House_Spenard.md), a relief depicting the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md), and a grand [pearl](Lore_Pearl.md) from the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Rivenspire-21)</sup> She also studied a painting depicting the [Direnni](Lore_Clan_Direnni.md) royal family and an artistic representation of the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Stormhaven-22)</sup> She also examined a signet bearing the marks of [House Aurmine](Lore_House_Aurmine.md), and a figurine of [Wyrd](Lore_Wyrd.md) origins, which she speculated was a totemic representation of a Divine.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Bangkorai-23)</sup>

When it came to the region of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), Amalien's research was confined to studying one of the Malacath Brutal Bands of [Malacath's Brutal Breed](Lore_Malacath%27s_Brutal_Breed.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Wrothgar-24)</sup> On the isle of [Betnikh](Lore_Betnikh.md), she studied an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) eye-shaped stone dedicated to [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Betnikh-25)</sup>

She also studied relics from the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres.md). Items of note that were unearthed on the isle of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) included a [Thrassian](Lore_Thras.md) crystal shard, [druidic](Lore_Druids.md) runes, a [vulk'esh](Lore_Vulk%27esh.md)'s petrified egg, a music box inspired by druidic culture, a piece of [Faun](Lore_Faun.md)'s Lark Cladding, druidic magical attire, and Syrabane's Ward, a belt linked to [Syrabane](Lore_Syrabane.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Galen-26)</sup> On the main isle of the archipelago, the [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), she examined maps from the First Era, the Nighthunter's Cowl, a piece of armor associated with the [Dov-rha Axethanes](Lore_Dov-rha_Axethanes.md), a piece of armor linked to the [Kanuryai](Lore_Kanuryai.md), a powerful druidic ring, and a magical [Maormeri](Lore_Maormer.md) necklace.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-High_Isle-27)</sup>

### Valenwood
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-furnishing-Branch_of_Falinesti.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Branch_of_Falinesti.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Branch_of_Falinesti.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Branch_of_Falinesti.jpg) Branch of Falinesti Amalien's research into Valenwood included various significant artifacts: maps of the region, ancestral Altmeri armor designs, bones employed by the Spinners, Bosmeri windchimes, a scepter once wielded by Silvenars, sap stones, and fragments of the Ring of the Wild Hunt.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Malabal-28)</sup> She also examined warding sigils associated with Ouze, a pair of shoes tied to the legend of the Eldest, a Bosmeri ritual cauldron, and a throne originating from Falinesti.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Greenshade-29)</sup> Furthermore, she studied an engraved hunting horn, coral clasps similar to those worn by Aldmer wizards such as Maelor the Twin and Surillion Ocean-King, and a fragment of an ancient Altmeri music box mechanism.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Grahtwood-30)</sup> Her research also extended to religious statuettes and fertility totems once used by the Ayleids of Valenwood.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Greenshade-29)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Grahtwood-30)</sup>

### Hammerfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-furnishing-Dwemer_Star_Chart.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Dwemer_Star_Chart.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Dwemer_Star_Chart.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Dwemer_Star_Chart.jpg) Dwemer Star Chart Among the Hammerfell relics Amalien had the opportunity to study were a Na-Totambu ceremonial gong bearing the image of Satakal, a fragment of the Ring of the Pale Order.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Alik.27r-31)</sup> Amalien also examined a Dwemeri trade bar from [Arkngthamz-Phng](Lore_Arkngthamz-Phng.md) and a piece of Dwarven siege weaponry.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Bangkorai-23)</sup> She studied ancient maps of Hew's Bane, wigs made from lion's mane, and Yokudan scabbards.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Hew.27s_Bane-32)</sup>

Among the relics from the isle of Stros M'Kai that Amalien examined were a Goblin steam mill, a fragment of an ancient Dwarven vamidium, a Dwemer puzzle box, astrological and star charts, as well as fragments of frog-metal.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Stros-33)</sup> From the region of Craglorn, Amalien had the opportunity to examine a shard from the [Spellscar](Lore_Spellscar.md), a piece of an Aetherquartz Dwemer mechanism, and Aetherial lenses of Dwemeri origin.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Craglorn-34)</sup>

She also examined Torc Strand of Song and the Torc Strand of Lore, two parts of the Dwemeri artifact known as the Torc of Tonal Constancy,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Alik.27r-31)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Bangkorai-23)</sup> as well as throat guards paired with them.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Craglorn-34)</sup>

### Skyrim, the Reach and Blackreach
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/ON-furnishing-Seat_of_the_Snow_Prince.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Seat_of_the_Snow_Prince.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Seat_of_the_Snow_Prince.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Seat_of_the_Snow_Prince.jpg) Seat of the Snow Prince On Bleakrock Isle, she examined a mask associated with the Dragon Cult, ancient Snow Elven metal carvings featuring Auri-El imagery, and a brazier crafted from Stalhrim.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Bleakrock-35)</sup>

On the mainland, in Eastmarch, she studied a decorative Nordic shield boss from the First Era, a brooch adorned with wolf imagery, Dwemeri ebony mechanisms, a chalice she believed to be linked to Ysgramor, a whale totem of Stuhn that she thought was more artistic than devotional, and a piece of Snow Elf clothing.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Eastmarch-36)</sup> In the Rift, Amalien studied a map of the hold, bones used for augury, and a crown she theorized belonged to a Giant chieftain, speculating it could have once belonged to Sinmur. She also examined a piece of petrified Snow-Cedar associated with the Snow Elves.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Rift-37)</sup> In the Western Holds, Amalien studied both Dwemeri and Nordic tattoo traditions, ancient drinking horns, Dwemeri mechanisms, and a Snow Elven throne.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-WSkyrim-38)</sup> In the region of the Reach, Amalien studied maps from the period of Hestra's conquest, Dwemeri extraction tools, fragments of an ancient Dwarven mechanical scarab, enchanted windows of vampiric origin, and Aldmeri silver threads.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Reach-39)</sup> She also studied the [Huntsman's Warmask](Lore_Huntsman%27s_Warmask.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Solstice-6)</sup>

### Morrowind
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar%2C_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar%2C_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar,_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar,_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg) St. Nerevar, Moon-and-Star On the mainland of Morrowind, Amalien studied a map of Deshaan from the period of The Sun's Death, candlesticks from the Ashlander Mabrigash tribe, and an Ashlander mask made of netch leather, which she speculated was used for healing victims of the Thrassian Plague. She also examined a map of the pilgrimage route of Saint Veloth, a ceremonial Dunmeri altar, and pieces of Dwemeri vamidium.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Deshaan-40)</sup> In the Bal Foyen region, she studied a well-preserved set of the Traitor's Dematah board game, a chalice linked to the origins of House Dres, a guar statue, a Chimeri tri-angled altar devoted to the Good Daedra, and the core of one of Malacath's Brutal Bands.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Bal_Foyen-41)</sup> She also studied a map of Stonefalls from the First Empire of the Nords' era, a gate used by ancient Chimer summoners, the military insignia of General Balreth, and ancient Dwemer siege weaponry.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Stonefalls-42)</sup> In the Telvanni Peninsula region, she had the opportunity to study a Chimeri vessel used for ceremonies and offerings, a map of the area of unclear origins, a Cliff Strider skeleton stand, an alchemy work station of House Telvanni, several Dunmeri tattooing traditions, vestments of the Dunmeri priesthood, Dwemeri defensive greaves, and a Velothi Ur-Mage's Amulet.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Telvanni-43)</sup>

She also studied items tied to the island of Vvardenfell, including its maps, Dwarven rods from the isle, a grapnel she theorized was used to mount giant crabs, and a bust of Saint Nerevar.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Vvardenfell-44)</sup>

### Cyrodiil
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/ON-furnishing-Fresco%2C_Colovian_Lady.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Fresco%2C_Colovian_Lady.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Fresco,_Colovian_Lady.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Fresco,_Colovian_Lady.jpg) Fresco, Colovian Lady She also studied relics linked to the region of Cyrodiil. In Blackwood, she examined the clasp from an Ayleid cloak, a tapestry depicting the region, a ring associated with Sanguine, the Death Dealer's Fete, and the Shapeshifter's Chain.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Blackwood-18)</sup> Amalien studied a map of the Gold Coast, an ornate minotaur nose ring, an ancient bust from Anvil, a shrine to Dibella, and a golden idol depicting Morihaus.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Gold_Coast-45)</sup> Within the region of the West Weald, she also studied items such as a mirror dedicated to Ithelia worship (though she failed to discern links to the specific goddess), a map of the region drawn by the Redguards, various Colovian tapestries and frescoes, ancient Ayleid artifacts like statues and windows, music boxes, and forge equipment. She also examined Dwarven gloves from Clan Rourken, the Shadow Queen's Cowl tied to Nocturnal, and a necklace associated with the Aureal and Mazken Daedra.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-West_Weald-46)</sup>

### Realms Beyond

#### Apocrypha
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/ON-furnishing-Music_Box%2C_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Music_Box%2C_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Music_Box,_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Music_Box,_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg) Music Box, Glyphic Secrets Amalien had researched items of Apocrypha, the realm of the Daedric Prince Hermaeus Mora. Among the topics she explored were antique garments of the Ciphers of the Eye, scrying braziers, petrified watchers, strange sources of light, an actual Watchful Light specimen, structural pieces of Apocrypha shaped like tentacles, an apocryphal loom, engravings dedicated to Hermaeus Mora, elements of an Infinite Tome, and fragments of the Torc of the Last Ayleid King.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Apocrypha-47)</sup> She also studied a fragment of a ceremonial well dedicated to Hermaeus Mora, a Trifold Mirror of Alternatives, a map of some regions of Apocrypha, multiple apocryphal fossils, and a music box crafted by [Adonia of the Heart's Ache](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adonia_of_the_Heart%27s_Ache).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Telvanni-43)</sup>

#### Artaeum
Amalien had the opportunity to study items from the lost island of Artaeum, including a folio associated with the secretive Psijic Order and a tool she believed was used by them for scrying.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Artaeum-48)</sup>

#### Eyevea
Eyevea, an island with a history tied to Shalidor, the Mages Guild, and Sheogorath, was also a place where Amalien had the opportunity to study several relics. Among them were a Grummite pearl, clues she associated with Sheogorath (though her fellow scholars had other theories), and leather used in Sload craftsmanship.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Eyevea-49)</sup>

#### Coldharbour
She studied a map of Coldharbour, torture tools, manacles, and other relics from the realm, along with some Dwemeri and Falmeri artifacts that had somehow ended up there. She also examined a piece of vampiric armor and void crystals,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Coldharbour-50)</sup> Daedric blacksmithing implements, and fragments of [Denogorath](Lore_Denogorath.md)'s statue of Molag Bal, as well as the Daedric Gate used to bring Prince [Naeomon](Lore_Naemon.md) back from the Coldharbour.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Solstice-6)</sup>

#### Clockwork City
Amalien studied Animo Cores, a religious scourge associated with the Clockwork Apostles, as well as a piece of Sotha Sil's machinery, including unknown wall mechanisms and a Mnemonic Star-Sphere.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Clockwork-51)</sup>

#### Deadlands
Amalien studied various specialized and ancient Daedric equipment from the realm, including a map of it, a banner of Clan Mindcleaver, and pieces of Dagonite torture tools. She also examined a shelf filled with black soul gems, a petrified Daedoth horn, and fragments of the Markyn Ring of Majesty and Belharza's Band.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Deadlands-52)</sup>

#### Fargrave
Amalien studied an ancient map of the city of Fargrave, which contained many landmarks not found in modern maps of the realm.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Deadlands-52)</sup>

#### Shivering Isles
Amalien studied [shoes with origins tracing back to the Shivering Isles](Lore_Mad_God%27s_Dancing_Shoes.md), crafted from materials harvested from many of its inhabitants, including Grummites, Gnarls, and Hungers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Solstice-6)</sup>
