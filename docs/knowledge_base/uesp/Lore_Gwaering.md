Gwaering was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) woman who served as the [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was the niece of [Finoriell](Lore_Finoriell.md), the previous Green Lady. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), after the assassination of [Edhelorn](Lore_Edhelorn.md) —who was not only [the Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) but also the husband of Finoriell—and the subsequent death of Finoriell herself, Gwaering was chosen to become the new Green Lady.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TGLG-1)</sup>

Later that year, Gwaering's jealous ex-lover [Ulthorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulthorn_the_Hound) was manipulated by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) into attempting to usurp the Silvenar's position by taking part in the Handfasting ceremony in his stead. Taking on the moniker of "the Hound", Ulthorn and his forces captured the city of [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md) and put Gwaering under Hircine's sway.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESORt S-2)</sup> Ulthorn speculated that the reason Hircine wished for him to usurp the Silvenar was that the Green Lady could be seen as the ultimate prey, being the strongest of the Bosmer, the greatest hunters of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONSOU-3)</sup> The city was retaken when Ulthorn was defeated by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the new Silvenar, [Indaenir](Lore_Indaenir.md). Gwaering was subsequently rescued and performed the Handfasting ceremony, marrying Indaenir.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESORt S-2)</sup>

At some point after her ascension Gwaering had met [Bahtra at-Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bahtra_at-Hunding), a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) studying the ways of the [warden](Lore_Wardens.md) and sought Gwaering out to discuss the intricacies of the Green. Gwaering had noted her sharp intellect, likening it to the thorns of a rose, and recommended King [Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md) to give her time and attention if she ever arrived at [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md). Gwaering had also exchanged correspondence with the King Aeradan regarding the crisis caused by the self-proclaimed King [Nantharion Rayn](Lore_Nantharion.md). In one such letter, she warned him that the spirit of their people remained divided and that Nantharion's influence still lingered in some corners, urging Aeradan to exercise caution.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-4)</sup>
