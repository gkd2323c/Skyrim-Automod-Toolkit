Ebon Stadmont is a location shrouded in mystery, the source of various legends and myths found throughout the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). Ebon Stadmont's tablets and ruins are covered in an unknown and mysterious language, one that is said to be similar to that of the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), with lines of text on structures with statues of [crows](Lore_Crow.md) —a creature associated with [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Forte-1)</sup>

## History
Ebon Stadmont's origins are largely unknown; the text on the various tablets in its ruined halls have long left the people of the Summerset Isles wondering.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Sometime in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the future Grand Maestro, [Forte](Lore_Forte.md) of the [House of Reveries](Lore_House_of_Reveries.md), traveled to Ebon Stadmont with a troupe on an expedition. There, Forte and his entourage studied the language, from which he was able to translate several stanzas of poems.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Forte-1)</sup> But not everyone made it out—one of his associates, Fletch was compelled to go deeper into Ebon Stadmont by a malevolent spirit that inhabits the ruins.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOTES-3)</sup>

Sometime around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Andewen —a member of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) —traveled to the forest, to uncover its mysteries. Andewen and [an adventurer](Lore_Hero.md#Soulless_One) delved deep into Ebon Stadmont; through a series of events, Andewen encountered the so-called Spirit of Ebon Stadmont, who trapped her in the Ebon Sanctum until her waning years, having her study the language. The adventurer who accompanied her, however, used the power of the language to travel in time, giving Andewen's future research to her past-self—before entering the forest—which she published to the Mages Guild, to mostly doubtful reception. Regardless, Ebon Stadmont remained in infamy.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOTES-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Around the same time, the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) made inquiries into the language of the ruins, believing it to be connected to how [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) had hidden the Heart of Transparent Law—the Stone of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) —long ago. In the ruins they encountered the Earl of Nocturnal in the [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md), who revealed that the language could only be used by mortals and that [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) had given Iachesis knowledge of it, allowing him to use an incantation to hide the Heart of Transparent Law. For this reason Nocturnal had been researching the language herself as the "Spirit of the Sanctum"—using mortal agents like Fletch and Andewen—hoping to find a way to claim the Heart for herself.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOLIT-5)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the forest had lent its name to the settlement of Ebon Stadmont, which was under the reign of Lord Lillandllon. It had a rivalry with the village of Marbruk Brook.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including Marbruk Brook in the northwest, Old Falls in the east, and [Riverfield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riverfield) in the southeast.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Map Arena-7)</sup>

## Language
The language of Ebon Stadmont is impossibly ancient. Grand Maestro Forte speculates that it is older than [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and perhaps even the language of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Forte-1)</sup> while the former Psijic Leythen describes Ebon Stadmont's magic as far older than even the Daedric Prince [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), his mistress.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOLeythen-8)</sup> Some fragments of the language are known:<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOTES-3)</sup>

Written:

pa-rah eek-nay
- Past pathway
sel-rah ang-yond
- Eastern temple

Spoken:

baneaweh tendell
gah Norro berrin, vo rah Larow e javeel<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONIachesis-9)</sup>
lainkah faloweh
- Future's door
parah voleisto
- The past revealed
