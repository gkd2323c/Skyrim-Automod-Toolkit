## Eagle's Brook
[Eagle's Brook](Lore_Eagle%27s_Brook.md) (also called **Eagle Brook**) is a village located on the coastal highlands of [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Eagle's Brook is the home of nearby [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md)'s artisans and craftsmen who serve the high nobility of the kingdom. The settlement also serves as a key port to the city-state, one that bridges it to the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) from the north. Because it is a port town, Eagle's Brook sees a regular import of [seafood](Lore_Breton_Cuisine.md#Meats_and_Fish) and prepares it daily.

## Earth Forge
The [Earth Forge](Lore_Earth_Forge.md) is a ruined [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) forge discovered by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and used by their [blacksmiths](Lore_Smithing.md). Its location is a closely guarded secret by the guild, though it was known to some other factions, unbeknownst to them, such as the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md). It lies hidden in the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md) of southwestern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), just north of the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) border, not far away from the city of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). It is accessible from guildhalls across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) via portal magic.

## Earthtear Caverns
[Earthtear Caverns](Lore_Earthtear_Caverns.md) (or **Earthtear Cavern**) is a network of underground tunnels and chasms in the [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) region of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). At least two entrances exist: one just northeast of [Azra's Crossing](Lore_Azra%27s_Crossing.md), and another in the desert far west of [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md).

## Eastmarch
[Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) is the eastern [hold](Lore_Hold.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and one of the four known collectively as the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md). As its name suggests, it lies against the province's eastern border, close to the [Dunmeth Pass](Lore_Dunmeth_Pass.md), the only passage to and from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the area due to the high and rugged [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md).

[Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) is the only sizable city in the otherwise rural province due to the expansive hot springs which dominate the hold. According to legend, these hot springs were created when the hero [Wuunding](Lore_Wuunding.md) fought a mighty Daedra and defeated it with a flaming hammer. The hammer's blows melted the Daedra, leaving behind the numerous hot springs.

[Darkwater River](Lore_Darkwater_River.md) winds north from Lake Geir into this treacherous hot spot, where it meets the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) which then drains into the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) as it passes Windhelm. [River Yorgrim](Lore_River_Yorgrim.md) also joins with the White River before it ends at the Sea of Ghosts. It drains [Lake Yorgrim](Lore_Lake_Yorgrim.md), which lies west in the hold known as [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md).

## Ebon Ro
[Ebon Ro](Lore_Ebon_Ro.md) is a settlement located in the northern area of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), specifically northeast of [Vulkwasten Wood](Lore_Vulkwasten.md) and [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md) and west of [Thormar Keep](Lore_Thormar.md).

## Ebon Stadmont
[Ebon Stadmont](Lore_Ebon_Stadmont.md) is a forest located on the base of [Eton Nir](Lore_Eton_Nir.md), in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Ebon Stadmont is a location shrouded in mystery, the source of various legends and myths found throughout the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). Ebon Stadmont's tablets and ruins are covered in an unknown and mysterious language, one that is said to be similar to that of the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), with lines of text on structures with statues of [crows](Lore_Crow.md) —a creature associated with [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).

## Ebon Wastes
The [Ebon Wastes](Lore_Ebon_Wastes.md) is a settlement found in the region of [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The village was built from the **Ebon Crypt**, the former mausoleum of a noble family called the [Gautiers](Lore_Gautier.md), who hailed from [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md). When it was taken over by a [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md), it was subsequently abandoned.

## Ebonheart
[Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) (also called **Old Ebonheart**) is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and is located in the center of [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md). The city is considered the eldest city-state in the province and is more commonly known as the ancestral seat of the [Ra'athim Clan](Lore_Ra%27athim.md), described by the potentially fictional *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)* book series as an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) miners. Some members are well-known across Tamriel, such as [Moraelyn](Lore_Moraelyn.md), called the "Champion of Tamriel" in the *King Edward* book series, and [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) Empress-Regent of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).

## Ebrocca
[Ebrocca](Lore_Ebrocca.md) was one of the youngest [ruins](Lore_Predecessor_Ruins.md) in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) at the end of the Third Era, when it was almost 1,000 years old. It was located near the settlement of [Highcross](Lore_Highcross.md). The ruins were purged of undead in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) by [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s [Champion](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).

## Echo Cave
[Echo Cave](Lore_Echo_Cave.md) is an icy, partially flooded natural cave network found far west of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), in the [Echo Cliffs](Lore_Echo_Cliffs.md) region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md).

Like all natural caves, the tunnels that make up Echo Cave once served as the capillaries of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), pulsing with the divine essence of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md). It was occupied at some point by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), who left behind [ruined structures](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) in its subterranean passages. The name of the cave has nothing to do with reflected sound, and instead refers to the undead who have long haunted its chilly depths like an unholy echo of the living. It extends deep underground, and includes a subterranean canyon known as the Echo Passages.

## Edward's Mountain
[Edward's Mountain](Lore_Edward%27s_Mountain.md) is a mountain in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) that is located near the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md). It is featured in the [fifth](Lore_Fools%27_Ebony,_Part_the_Fiveth.md) and [sixth](Lore_Fools%27_Ebony,_Part_the_Sixth.md) part of the [Fools' Ebony](Lore_Fools%27_Ebony.md) play.

## Eld Angavar
[Eld Angavar](Lore_Eld_Angavar.md) (meaning **Old Ironwood** in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md); also known as the **Golden Path**) is a realm of Nexus Gates connected by floating [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) architecture. It is an abandoned realm located between worlds, created as a result of Myndhal's search for the [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md). One of the few known entrances to the realm is the still-functioning Gate within the Vakka-Bok [Xanmeer](Lore_Xanmeer.md) in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).

## Elden Grove
The [Elden Grove](Lore_Elden_Grove.md) (also spelled **Eldengrove** or **Eldenwood**) is a legendary forest known among the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) as the birthplace of the sacred First Tree. This First Tree then gave life to all the forests of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Its exact location has been a closely guarded secret, but it is known to be somewhere in western [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). It was one of the places where [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) hid a piece of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md). The grove has been described as "misty" and "labyrinthian". It is alternatively known as the **Grove of the One Great Tree** and the **Grove of the Sages**.

The [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_The_Towers.md#Green-Sap) in **[Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md)** is implied to be the home of the sacred First Tree, as it contains the physical embodiment of the soul of Valenwood. The Ayleids at [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) built their city of Elden Hollow in and around what is called the Elden Tree or the First Tree, believed by many to be the tree that seeded all of Valenwood. The Tree's branches and roots are as hands, reaching at once into the Mundus and Overworld. The city of [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) occupies the boughs and bole of a great walking Elden Tree, possibly the original Elden Tree from which all other [graht-oaks](Lore_Graht-oak.md) are derived. The magic involved in the creation of the tree and the city were from before the dawn of recorded history.

## Elden Root
[Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) (also spelled **Eldenroot**) is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), situated in the southern heart of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md)'s great forests, [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md). Not only is Elden Root the self-proclaimed "center of the Wood Elven nation", but it is also one of Valenwood's six major countries. It is a vast hub of trade and commerce, where many wealthy merchants and officials retain large-scale operations. At the center of it all is one of the legendary [Elden Trees](Lore_Elden_Tree.md), the titanic subspecies of the already enormous [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) trees that laden the southeast.

## Elenglynn
[Elenglynn](Lore_Elenglynn.md) (also called **Elynglenn**) is an [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located in the western edge of the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It is specifically located in middle of the [Imperial Reserve](Lore_Imperial_Reserve.md), between [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) and [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md).

## Elinhir
[Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md), historically known as the "**City of Mages**", is a town in eastern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), close to the border with both [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It is located in the [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) region, close to the Skyrim town of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md). The city is noted for its massive Apex Towers of [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) design. Elinhir reached its zenith in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), when it was considered the center of civilization in Craglorn. As of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Elinhir was known for its use of magical techniques to make everyday tasks easier.

## Elsweyr
[Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) is a region that lies on the southern coast of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and is home to the feline [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). It is divided into two major climates: savannahs, badlands and dry plains in the north, and fertile lands of jungle and rainforests in the south.

Originally, sixteen Khajiiti clans roamed the province, and were often simplified as belonging to a region or realm. With a combination of outside pressures and mastery of areas of expertise, it became more accurate to associate a clan with an area. Eventually, sixteen tribes became [sixteen kingdoms](Lore_Empires.md#Sixteen_Kingdoms_of_Elsweyr): [Ne-Quin-Al](Lore_Ne-Quin-Al.md), [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md), [Dune](Lore_Dune.md), [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md), [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Elsweyr).md), [Meirvale](Lore_Meirvale.md), and [Helkarn](Lore_Helkarn.md) in the north; [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md), [Bruk'ra](Lore_Bruk%27ra.md), [Pa'alatiin](Lore_Pa%27alatiin.md), [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md), [Tenmar](Lore_Tenmar.md), [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), [Khenarthia](Lore_Khenarthia.md), and [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) in the south. The lullaby known as the Hasa Zha'ja explains how these clans got their name, while the poem known as Zhan Zhab tells of the transition from clan names being used to refer to the names of regions. Due to the Thrassian Plague, these sixteen clans changed their tribal functions or they may have died due to starvation and other hardships. These clans essentially became two and were separated ideologically and geographically as what we would come to know as the two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine. These two kingdoms would eventually be united into the [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md), and once more separated in two kingdoms in the [Third Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).

## Eltheric Ocean
The [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) is a vast body of water encompassing the seas west of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It meets the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md) at two points within the Padomaic: the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) in the north and the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md) in the south.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Blue Divide)</sup> Its waters touch at least four continents: [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to the east, [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) to the west, [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md), and, allegedly, [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) to the south.

## Emean Sea
The **[Emean Sea](Lore_Emean_Sea.md)** is the vast body of water that surrounds the entire western half of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). It borders the [Fringe](Lore_Fringe.md) and the Isle of Flame, as well as running the entire length of the Laughing Coast. While the specific point where the Emean Sea ends and the [Enjaen Sea](Lore_Enjaen_Sea.md) begins are not entirely clear, they seem to merge between Saints Watch and the Isle of Flame, and [Heretic's Horn](Lore_Heretic%27s_Horn.md) and Madgod's Boot.

## Enjaen Sea
The **[Enjaen Sea](Lore_Enjaen_Sea.md)**, much like all things in [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s realm, is the second half of a greater whole: the vast ocean that surrounds the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). It encompasses the eastern half of the Isles and is estimated to converge into the [Emean Sea](Lore_Emean_Sea.md) somewhere between the peninsulas of [Heretic's Horn](Lore_Heretic%27s_Horn.md) and Madgod's Boot in the south, and Saints Watch and the Isle of Flame in the north. The Enjaen coast borders the entire run of the Jester's Spine Mountains and is dotted with a few campsites, as well as forming an inlet that neighbors [Deepwallow](Lore_Deepwallow.md) and the capital, [New Sheoth](Lore_New_Sheoth.md).

## Ephesus

It borders the [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth.md) region to the north and the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) to the south. The region contains the Pelin Graveyard and the famous [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md), on the border with Hammerfell. Apart from the Garrison, access to Hammerfell is also possible through the lesser-known Sunken Road. Ephesus is also home to the ruins of Arlimahera's Grip. It was known for is freeborn militia.

## Erabenimsun Camp
The [Erabenimsun Camp](Lore_Erabenimsun_Camp.md) is the home of the Erabenimsun Tribe, one of the four distinct tribes of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The Erabenimsun Camp is commonly found in the region of [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md). Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the camp was found on the base of [Mount Kand](Lore_Mount_Kand.md), and behind the ruined city of [Nchuleftingth](Lore_Nchuleftingth.md). But by [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), it was found near the shores of the [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md).

## Erokii Ruins
[Erokii Ruins](Lore_Erokii_Ruins.md), or simply **Erokii**, is an ancient ruin in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), said to have once been the greatest Ayleid city in northwest Tamriel. The great spire above Erokii, now known as the Doomcrag, has been likened to the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) in construction, and it has been speculated that it may, at one point, have been built as one of [the Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md). It housed the Light of Life, or Lattanya in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md), a relic which helped plants grow and healed illnesses. Pilgrimages to the summit of the Doomcrag would often occur daily. Following a bloody conflict between the Ayleid settlers and the original inhabitants of the area, the relic was tainted, and became Abagandra, the Lightless Remnant, a beacon of death.

## Erolgard
[Erolgard](Lore_Erolgard.md) was a kingdom featured in the fictional novel series *[The Adventures of Eslaf Erol](Lore_The_Adventures_of_Eslaf_Erol.md)*. It was located in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

## Errinorne Isle
[Errinorne Isle](Lore_Errinorne_Isle.md), also called the **Veiled Isle** is an island found off the coast of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Errinorne Isle is named after [House Errinorne](Lore_House_Errinorne.md), a noble house that ruled [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md). Errinorne Island was used as a shipping and warehouse complex for the city of Skywatch, but it was later abandoned after the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) took control of the island. The only landmark is the Veiled Keep, which is found in the center of the island.

## Esroniet
The spice island of [Esroniet](Lore_Esroniet.md) is located between [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). The main port of the island is called [Black Harbor](Lore_Black_Harbor.md). The port was captured in [3E 282](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_282) by [Uriel Septim V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md), and in [3E 284](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_284) the island's ruler, [Prince Bashomon](Lore_Bashomon.md), surrendered the whole kingdom to him. Uriel transformed it into a major base for the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md) and used it as a launching point for the invasion of Akavir in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288).

## Eton Nir
[Eton Nir](Lore_Eton_Nir.md) is the tallest mountain found in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), within the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). Eton Nir consists of an abundance of ruins, as well as expansive tunnel systems that connect each coast. The city-state of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) is found on the highest alps of Eton Nir. The **Eton Nir Grotto** is one of the many underground caverns that are found across the massif.

## Evergloam
The [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Shadow. It is a realm of perpetual twilight, and the "cradle of shadow". The Evergloam exists adjacent to all other [realms of reality](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) simultaneously; it can almost be glimpsed when one turns and looks quickly while in the dark. It consists of a primary plane and several pocket realms, including the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md) and the [Crow's Wood](Lore_Crow%27s_Wood.md). These realms are thought to be constantly shifting, and are perceived differently by different mortals. It is filled with forests, whose trees cast long shadows over a purple landscape. The land is dotted with crumbling buildings and sunken crypts.

## Evermore
[Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), sometimes spelled **Evermor**, is a city on the banks of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) in eastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located near the border with [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

Its impressive palace was assembled from slabs of [granite](Lore_Granite.md) quarried from nearby [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). Local traditions include the worship of [Saint Pelin](Lore_Saint_Pelin.md), a hero of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) who once served as a clergyman at [Bangkorai Garrison](Lore_Bangkorai_Garrison.md).

Historically, the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of Evermore ruled over the region of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md). These lands were subject to frequent attacks from the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) of the mountains, who asserted their birthright to the city and aimed to reclaim their ancestral homeland. Southern Bangkorai included the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) region of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), leading Evermore's rulership to often be contested by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). Evermore's position along trade routes spanning the Tamriel allowed it to become an important trading hub. Locally, it linked eastern High Rock, the southern and central regions of Hammerfell, [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), and western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), while more distant trade routes to the southeast connected it with [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

## Eyevea
[Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md), sometimes known as the **Sanctuary**, is a small island in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), far west of mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Geographically it is part of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) archipelago, found to the northwest of the [main island](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).

Though the island was originally retained by the [High Elven](Lore_Altmer.md) people, the Arch-Mage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) acquired Eyevea in exchange for some minor artifacts he owned. However, after being tricked by the Daedric Prince Sheogorath, Shalidor lost Eyevea, and the island was ripped from Tamriel and brought into the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). During the [Age of Heroes](Lore_Age_of_Heroes.md), the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) collaborated with the ghost of Shalidor to work against Sheogorath and restore the island to Tamriel—as a way of providing the mages of Tamriel a safe haven for study far away from the problems of the world. Subsequently Eyevea became the capital heart and home of the Mages Guild itself.
