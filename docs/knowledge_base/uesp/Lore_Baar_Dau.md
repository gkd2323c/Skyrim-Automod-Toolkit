[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg/200px-MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Mehra_Milo_and_the_Lost_Prophecies_03.jpg) Baar Dau as the Ministry of Truth in 3E 427 Baar Dau<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TPP-1)</sup> (also known as the **Ministry of Truth**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup> **Prison Moon**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWConcept-3)</sup> or **Lie Rock**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOV33-4)</sup>) was a [meteor](Lore_Meteors.md), celestial rock or moonlet which hovered above [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) in southern [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GV-5)</sup> According to the prevailing legends, it was flung from the [Void](Lore_Void.md) by either [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TPP-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATBD-6)</sup> or its own malevolence,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOV33-4)</sup> but was prevented from crashing into the city by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md). Vivec allowed it to remain looming above the city so that if its people ever ceased to love him, the rock would fall and destroy them.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOV33-4)</sup> Other lesser-known tales and theories vary widely as to Baar Dau's origins, with one identifying it as a former [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) named **Una**, another insisting that it was an egg from which would one day emerge the [nightmarish offspring](Lore_Monster_Children.md) of Vivec and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), and yet another claiming that it was [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md)'s dung.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBD-7)</sup>

## Mythology and Origins

### Book of Hours
*[Sermon 33](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_33.md)* from [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s series *[The Thirty-Six Lessons of Vivec](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md)* provides an account of an encounter with Baar Dau, referred to within the story as **Lie Rock**. In the sermon, Lie Rock is one of Vivec's eight [Monster Children](Lore_Monster_Children.md) who initally escaped being slain, the result of the marriage of Vivec and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), the Pomegranate Banquet, in a [previous sermon](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_12.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LOV12-8)</sup> Lie Rock "was born of Vivec's second aperture", meaning it was dung excreted from Vivec.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-9)</sup> Lie Rock was removed from the Pomegranate Banquet from a forgotten guild known as the [Sweeps](Lore_Sweeps.md). Lie Rock escaped the clutches of the Sweeps and ascended to the heavens claiming his divine parentage made him invited into the Hidden Heaven. This Hidden Heaven realm was known as the [Scaled Blanket](Lore_The_Serpent.md), and housed the [Void Ghost](Lore_Lorkhan.md). Vivec sent Nerevar to the heavens with the named axe to shave Lie Rock asunder but the Hortator only encountered the Void Ghost. Lie Rock used this distraction to launch an attack on the city-god Vivec. The inhabitants of Vivec City screamed in terror as they saw the shooting star hurling towards the city out of the sky but Vivec merely raised his hand, froze Lie Rock right above the city and pierced it with Muatra.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOV33-4)</sup>

When Nerevar returned, he asked if Lie Rock should be removed. Vivec responded: *"I would have done so myself if I wanted, silly Hortator. I shall keep it there with its last intention intact, so that if the love of the people of this city for me ever disappear, so shall the power that holds back [their destruction](Lore_Red_Year.md)."*<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOV33-4)</sup>

Nerevar's response to this, *"Love is under your will only"*, pleased Vivec, and he informed Nerevar that he had become a Minister of Truth.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LOV33-4)</sup> Notably, the Ministry of Truth was the name used for the installation built inside Baar Dau.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Vivec_Ministry-10)</sup>

### Alternate Tales
Various other tales for the celestial rock exists such as it being flung from the heavens by the Mad God [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) who was bored and wished to destroy Vivec City as the Daedric Prince felt the city was built in mockery of the heavens. Citizens of Tamriel prayed unanswered prayers to the Divines and Daedric Princes alike to stop the rock's fall but only the prayers to the blessed Living Gods were heard. Vivec raised his hand and stopped the descent of the moonlet. Baar Dau itself pledged eternal servitude to the Tribunal thereafter; it guarded over the palace of Vivec and served as a citadel for the [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinator.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TPP-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATBD-6)</sup>

Another account claimed it was a [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) named **Una**. Una became separated from her kin as they fled to Aetherius and so Una instead chose companionship with [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and chose to nestle in close in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), right above Vivec City where neither Una nor Nirn would feel alone again.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBD-7)</sup>

One [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) tale claims Baar Dau is not the dung of Vivec as the sermons claim, but rather the dung of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). As Vivec had said something about [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) that angered Malacath,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBD-7)</sup> and had also done battle with the Flute-and-Pipe Ogres of the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOV31-11)</sup> the Daedric Prince defecated on the city of the Living God for the disrespect to Malacath's beloved "little brothers".

Yet another scholarly account claimed that Baar Dau was not a rock, but rather an egg. Specifically, the chrysalis from which would one day hatch the final monster of Vivec and Molag Bal's ill-fated union that Vivec would engage in glorious battle with.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBD-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg) Baar Dau in 2E 582, after descending closer to the city Vivec's Palace had existed for milennia before the rest of the city,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OME-12)</sup> attracting pilgrims and wanderers to come visit the poet, including Baar Dau. The display of Baar Dau as a wonder of the city inspired even more pilgrims to come visit.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LSD-13)</sup> Baar Dau originally rested higher in the sky, but plunged dramatically in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when Vivec's power was temporarily drained. The moonlet remained aloft only tenuously, showering Vivec City with burning fragments. After Vivec regained his power, he kept Baar Dau in its new position, just above the High Fane of the Temple Canton.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Some time later, Baar Dau was hollowed out by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and became the Ministry of Truth, where the Temple would imprison [dissident priests](Lore_Dissident_Priests.md) and attempt to reform them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup> The high-security prison was accessible only through levitation, and was directed by a Grand Inquisitor who, per tradition, wore a ring fortified with great enchantments.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The main areas were the Hall of Processing and the Prison Keep. Temple pilgrims offered levitation potions at the Shrine of Daring, which was dedicated to Vivec stopping the moon from destroying the city.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup>

Early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), after the disappearance of Vivec, the magic keeping Baar Dau afloat began to weaken. Two [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), [Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Sul) and [Vuhon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Vuhon), created an [ingenium](Lore_Ingenium.md), a [soul](Lore_Souls.md) -powered device which supported the rock.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TIC-16)</sup> It was created by making a pact with [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), who had a penchant for souls. The Ingenium was used to create a [rift](Lore_Portals.md) to the [Fields of Regret](Lore_Fields_of_Regret.md) through which souls were funneled to Clavicus Vile and the energies of the [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) were leeched to hold the moon in place. The mage Sul, who was one of the designers of Ingenium, theorized that the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) did not care for the stolen energies as he valued the souls he received in exchange more, and that the other architect of the project, Vuhon, might have even made a formal pact with Vile for him to allow the energies to be taken.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-IC-17)</sup> Hundreds of captured souls were needed to run the machine, but eventually living people were used. Only the people with the strongest souls were sacrificed. [Ilzheven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Greg_Keyes_Novels/Ilzheven), Sul's love, was sacrificed by Vuhon, and the ingenium was destroyed when Sul attempted to rescue her. In [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), without anything holding it up, Baar Dau resumed its long-delayed fall, returning to the momentum it had before being halted. This obliterated the city of Vivec, leaving a crater which would become [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md). In turn, the impact created tsunamis which ravaged mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and caused [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to erupt, devastating Vvardenfell.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TIC-16)</sup> The disaster of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) effectively ended the ascendancy of the Tribunal Temple and the mainstream worship of the Tribunal as [gods](Lore_Gods.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TR-18)</sup>
