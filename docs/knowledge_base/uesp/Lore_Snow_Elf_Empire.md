# Snow Elf Empire


When the [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) migrated to their lands in the Late Merethic Era, the two races lived in relative peace for some time, and the Atmorans thrived, summoning more of their kin from Atmora to build the city of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> The peace didn't last and soon the Snow Elves found themselves in constant conflict.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-1)</sup> The catalyst that would lead to the destruction of the Snow Elf Empire was the infamous [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md). Why the elves instigated the massacre of Saarthal is disputed; attributed motives include fear, religious and military provocations, territorial disputes, and a desire to possess [an ancient power](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md) the humans had uncovered.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-NOT-3)</sup>

The elves drove the Atmoran survivors from their shores, but [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) soon returned with his [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Companions.md) and began his onslaught on the elven empire. It is unknown how long the conflict, known as [the Return](Lore_The_Return.md), lasted but by the beginning of the First Era the elves built the [Chantry of Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chantry_of_Auri-El) in the isolated [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) to serve as the epicenter of their religion.<sup>[[nb 1]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Whatever resistance the elves put up was no match for the Atmoran horde and the war continued in their favor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-1)</sup> After wiping out organized elven resistance in Skyrim, the Nordic armies sought their enemy out on the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). The elves made a last stand in the [Battle of the Moesring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_the_Moesring): after an intense battle, their leader, the [Snow Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Prince), was killed, and the empire of the Snow Elves was destroyed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FOTSP-6)</sup> The survivors fled underground to hide and those who remained on the surface were exterminated in the following decades, even as late as [1E 140](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_140).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SSSJ-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DOFA-8)</sup>

Around three centuries passed until the Dwemer arrived in Skyrim sometime after [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DI1-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AP-10)</sup> The Dwemer established their own kingdom [under Skyrim itself](Lore_Blackreach.md), and many Snow Elves turned to their underground brethren for help.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFAS-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-JOMA-12)</sup> The Dwemer offered to provide them a haven, but forced their wards into servitude, only allowing them to consume a toxic fungus which rendered them blind. Over time, the servants became slaves, but despite this the Snow Elves would rise up against their Dwarven Masters waging the unseen War of the Crag, which was at a standstill for centuries until the dissapearance of the dwarves. Unfortunately winning the war against the Dwarves by default did not stop the death knell of the Snow Elves, as they still degenerated into the bestial race known today as the [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFAS-11)</sup>
