## Padomaic Crest‎
The [Padomaic Crest](Lore_Padomaic_Crest.md) (or **Central Highlands**) is a coastal region in east-central [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), presently within the [Indoril District](Lore_House_Indoril.md). It borders the region of [Fungal Lowlands](Lore_Fungal_Lowlands.md) to the west, and the region of [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) to the south. Together with the Fungal Lowlands, it was considered one of the two regions making up the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md). The region is home to the city of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md), as well as the isle of [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md) off the coast. The [Dres](Lore_Dres.md) town of [Alavelis](Lore_Alavelis.md) is located along the south near a volcano, and is home to a [glass](Lore_Malachite.md) mine.

The high uplands and treacherous cliff coastline gives way to craggy rocks along the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md). [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) described seeing large "[bat lizards](Lore_Bat_Lizard.md)" in the area, which are believed to be the ancestors of modern [cliff racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md). Tiny tribes of [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) and [trolls](Lore_Troll.md) also patrol the highlands.

## Padomaic Ocean
The [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md) is a vast body of water that surrounds most of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Surround)</sup> The Padomaic meets the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) at two locations within the Eltheric: off [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) in the north and at the [Blue Divide](Lore_Blue_Divide.md) in the south.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Blue Divide)</sup> Its easternmost known reach is the Eastern Sea, which borders [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). The ocean's waters meet at least three continents, Tamriel to the west, Akavir to the east, and [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) to the north, with several smaller islands scattered between them.

## The Pale
[The Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) (also called the **Pale Hold**) is a large region in north-central [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and one of the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md), the original four territories in the province, where ancient traditions continue to thrive. The region acquired its name from both the pale white tundra and the pigmentation of the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). Because of its distinct shape, some people colloquially call it "the old boot". The hold's population is centralized in its capital city of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md), a seaside town on the [Frozen Coast](Lore_Frozen_Coast.md) and one of the busiest ports in the province. Smaller settlements include [Bardmont](Lore_Bardmont.md), [Dunstad Grove](Lore_Dunstad_Grove.md), [Heljarchen](Lore_Heljarchen.md), and Porhnak.

## Pale Pass
[Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md) is a secluded pass through the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) on the [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) - [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) border. A ruined [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) fort can be found in the pass. The flora is similar to the rest of the mountain range, and a tribe of [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) inhabit the area. A cobbled road winds its way through the pass, and many small frozen lakes culminate in the lowest parts of the valley.

The easiest way to get to and from the pass on the Cyrodiil side is to go through a natural cave system located north of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md), named the Serpent's Trail by the Akaviri. The pass emerges into [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) on the Skyrim side, near the settlements of [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md) and [Neugrad Watch](Lore_Neugrad_Watch.md).

## Pankor
[Pankor](Lore_Pankor.md) is a small island marking the westernmost extent of the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), far to the southwest of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md)'s coast and northwest of [the Systres](Lore_Systres.md). A very small unnamed group of islands lies to the southwest. A bay on Pankor's east coast nearly splits the island into a northern and southern half.

## Panther River
The [Panther River](Lore_Panther_River.md) is a long and serpentine river located throughout the dense woodland of the southern [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Its tributaries run from [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md), and [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md), flowing past the provincial border of the province of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). It empties into the [Lower Niben](Lore_Lower_Niben.md), in a river delta called the **Mouth of the Panther**, and any sailor could tell you that it acquired its name from the sharp jagged rocks that resemble teeth. Sailors stay clear of the river mouth, especially when it is foggy.

## Passwall
[Passwall](Lore_Passwall.md) is the main settlement within [the Fringe](Lore_Fringe.md), a region of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) separated by high walls from the realm proper. Mortals who find themselves in this realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) are usually restricted to the Fringe until they have received Lord [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s "gift" of madness and can enter the realm through the Gates of Madness. Many such individuals take up residence in Passwall while they wait for Sheogorath's blessing to take hold.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Passwall.md).

## Sea of Pearls
The [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md) is a large body of water found west of the continent of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and its western waters, the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). It consists of the western-half of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), the outer reaches of the [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) sea-complex, but it mostly contains other continents beyond Tamriel's grasp, such as the sunken-islands of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). According to some accounts the dreaded archipelago of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md), and the underwater kingdom of [Ul'vor Kus](Lore_Ul%27vor_Kus.md) are within this sea. It is part of the greater [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) and it is neighbored by the Abecean Sea to the east, the [Azurian Sea](Lore_Azurian_Sea.md) in the north, and both the [Cape of the Blue Divide](Lore_Cape_of_the_Blue_Divide.md) and the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md) to the southeast. The Sea of Pearls acquired its name from the myriad shells that wash up on the shores of its islands; they tend to have whorls like [nautilus](Lore_Nautilus.md) shells, which are light-weight and durable. Sailors call the waters around Thras the **Cursed Sea**.

## Pelagiad
[Pelagiad](Lore_Pelagiad.md) was a small settlement in the [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md) region of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a Pelagiad Garrison was maintained by the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), although no town or fort existed by that name in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) at the time.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> The opulent Amaya Lake Lodge was built upon the shores of Lake Amaya during an earlier era, near the future site of Pelagiad. After Vvardenfell was opened by the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) for settlement in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), Fort Pelagiad was built by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) to guard the roads between [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md), [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) and [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md). Legion veterans began to build farms around the fort, and several shops were set up as the village grew. The town was officially chartered, and noted for its Western architecture resembling that of a [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) village. By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the settlement boasted a tavern, tradehouse, blacksmith and [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) services. Following the destruction caused during the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), Pelagiad's fate is uncertain.

## Pell's Gate
[Pell's Gate](Lore_Pell%27s_Gate.md) is a settlement located east of the [White Rose River](Lore_White_Rose_River.md) in the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It can be found where the [Red Ring Road](Lore_Red_Ring_Road.md) joins with the [Green Road](Lore_Green_Road.md), in the region known as the North Weald.

## Pellitine
[Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) ([Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md): *Pa'alatiin*), also known as **Pelletine** or **Southern Elsweyr**, was a kingdom in southern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), which merged with the rival kingdom of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) in [2E 309](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_309) to form the [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md), uniting the region for the first time. Pellitine occupied the southern half of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), a region of dense jungles, rainforests, river basins, tropical grasslands, and pristine white beaches. </noinclude>

## Phrygias
[Phrygias](Lore_Phrygias.md) was a barony on the western coast of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region. It existed in the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), as well as in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). It was surrounded by the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) to the east, [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md) and [Urvaius](Lore_Urvaius.md) to the south, and [Ykalon](Lore_Ykalon.md) to the west.

The [Garlythi](Lore_Garlythi.md) clan was the predominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline, and the regional deity was [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md). The provincial seat was the city of Phrygias. Phrygias was one of the primary members of a coalition that fought and defeated the forces of the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) in the massive [Battle of Firewaves](Lore_Battle_of_Firewaves.md).

## Pillar of Light
The [pillar of light](Lore_Pillar_of_Light.md) is a large waygate that allowed one to travel from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to the [Weir Gate](Lore_Weir_Gate.md) of the [Imperial Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). The pillar itself is round and metallic, and featuring seven large spires and six sizeable metallic objects that face the central teleportal. The pillar's exact location on Tamriel is unknown, but it appears on a shore near a large body of water that seemingly flows through a channel beyond.

Robed humanoid figures are witnessed attending to the pillar of light, and appear there when the [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) is sent off to Battlespire for testing.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Pinepeak Cavern
[Pinepeak Cavern](Lore_Pinepeak_Cavern.md) is a cave next to the path up the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It is a natural cavern with a deep and expansive sub-structure that refugees have often used as a sanctuary in centuries of tumultuous times. While some of the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) claim that it is safe, others refuse to enter the cave because of the fear that it may cave in on them. Unbeknownst to them, Pinepeak Cavern has a tomb deep in the cave system built for one of the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md), Hakra, who died in battle against the [half-giant](Lore_Half-Giant.md) chieftain, [Sinmur](Lore_Sinmur.md).

## Pinnacle Rock
[Pinnacle Rock](Lore_Pinnacle_Rock.md) is the home of the [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md) in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), located in [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) at the southern tip of the Madgod's Boot. This is the Mazken's most sacred place, as it contains the Wellspring of the Mazken. The souls of the fallen are called from the Waters of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) to the Font of Rebirth by use of ceremonial chimes. The stronghold was a gift from Lord [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), and usually no one—not even Sheogorath himself—are permitted inside its sacred halls.

## The Pits
[The Pits](Lore_Pits.md), also known as **Peryite's Pits**, or the **Pits of Pestilence** (sometimes singular **Pit of Pestilence**), is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Pestilence. Here, Peryite guards the lowest orders of Oblivion.

It is unknown what the realm looks like overall. One part of the realm appears as a rocky cave system with occasional skylights filled with a hazy green poisonous fog, floating green embers, and some underground plants and is described as permeated with a noxious presence of miasma. This area of the realm has been home to necromancers and undead. Another part of the realm resembles the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), with lava seas, volcanic islands, and ruined structures. It is also home to many of the same Daedra as the Deadlands. The [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) believes that the realm is usually inaccessible to mortals, but there have been several exceptions.

## Plaza Brindisi Dorom
The [Plaza Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom.md) occupies the southernmost quadrant of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). It is a public garden dominated by [a great statue](Lore_Statues_Tribunal_and_Saints.md#Almalexia) at its center.

The plaza is named after [Indoril Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Brindisi_Dorom.md), the duke of Mournhold who was killed when [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) destroyed the city in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920).

The southwest gate leads to Godsreach, the southeast gate to the Great Bazaar, and the Gates of [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) to the outside world.

## Lake Poppad
[Lake Poppad](Lore_Lake_Poppad.md) is a small, isolated lake in the forested wilderness of the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md) region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It is north of the [Corbolo River](Lore_Corbolo_River.md)'s source, southwest of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md), and far to the east of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and the [Red Ring Road](Lore_Red_Ring_Road.md). A shrine to [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) is on its northeastern shore. [Nirnroot](Lore_Nirnroot.md) has been known to grow on its banks. It originally fed a tributary of the [Reed River](Lore_Reed_River.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), although this stream had seemingly dried up by [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).

## Port Dufort

## Port Hunding
[Port Hunding](Lore_Port_Hunding.md), also called **Stros M'Kai**, is a city on the island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md). Situated north of [Hunding Bay](Lore_Hunding_Bay.md), the city takes up a large portion of the island's area.

## Port Telvannis
[Port Telvannis](Lore_Port_Telvannis.md) (also spelled **Port Telvanis** or **Port Telvanni**) is a city located on an [archipelago](Lore_Northeast_Archipelago.md) in the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md), northeast of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It is described as an island palace under the control of [Great House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), and was their capital during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). The Parliament of Bugs, the High Telvanni wizards who preside over the entire house, are headquartered here.

## Portdun Creek
[Portdun Creek](Lore_Portdun_Creek.md) (formerly known as **Portdun Watch**) is a town found in [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md), a region of the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It was originally an outwork built for Firebrand Keep, the enclave of the [Knights of the Flame](Lore_Knights_of_the_Flame.md). ## Pothago
[Pothago](Lore_Pothago.md) is a region on the western coast of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region. When it was a duchy it was surrounded by [Myrkwasa](Lore_Myrkwasa_(fiefdom).md) to the east and [Kairou](Lore_Kairou.md) to the south. Pothago's main city is the city of Pothago. The [Selenu](Lore_Selenu.md) clan is the predominant [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) bloodline in the region.

On the 18th of Sun's Dusk, the people of Pothago celebrate [Hel Anseilak](Lore_Hel_Anseilak.md), with it being the holiest day for them. The ancient way of the [sword-singers](Lore_Sword-singers.md) and their rich heritage is remembered and honored on this day.

After the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Pothago came under the dominion of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md).

## Pothole Caverns
[Pothole Caverns](Lore_Pothole_Caverns.md) is a natural cave network found deep in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), about halfway along the [Green Road](Lore_Green_Road.md) between [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) and the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).

Like all natural caves, these tunnels once served as the capillaries of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), pulsing with the divine essence of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md). Some say that the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) once used the place as a mausoleum, and the caverns have been associated with the undead ever since. The limestone architecture present around the cave's exterior during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) proves that the Ayleids had some presence here, although by the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) no sign of these structures remained.

During the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Pothole Caverns were occupied by a Circle of the [Shadowed Path](Lore_Shadowed_Path.md), a cult of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) dedicated to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). This Circle was led by Diabolist Vethisa and had been sent to the cave in search of an ancient relic buried beneath the ground, which the cult planned to use to conquer the Imperial City and all of Cyrodiil in the name of Molag Bal. Despite filling the excavated caves with cultists and undead, Vethisa was ultimately killed by passing [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) soldiers.

By the end of the Third Era circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the cave was once again occupied by necromancers, this time the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md). The cultists were well established, having set up traps throughout the tunnels as well as prison cells for their captives and a necromantic hall of worship in the main chamber.

## Pridehome
[Pridehome](Lore_Pridehome.md) is a monastery found deep in the jungle reaches of the [Tenmar Forest](Lore_Tenmar_Forest.md), in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). It is a place of meditation for those that worship the Dragon-King of Cats, [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) and prepare for the end times when [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) return after years of extinction. It is the home of the Pride of Alkosh, a group of warrior-monks that consist of the Forgotten Manes, who dedicate themselves to maintaining peace throughout the province until they pass on.

## Privateer's Hold
[Privateer's Hold](Lore_Privateer%27s_Hold.md) is a dungeon located near the northern border of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), a kingdom in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Specifically, it is north of the town, Gothway Garden, and just south of the river that splits the kingdom from its neighbor, [Tulune](Lore_Tulune.md). Privateer's Hold is a large dungeon under the earth and its signature feature is the large room and the throne high above it.

## Pryai River
The [Pryai River](Lore_Pryai_River.md) (sometimes called the **Prytasus River**)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is a meandering waterway that flows at [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)'s border to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where the flora of Cyrodiil met the flora of Morrowind. Its entire length and prominence across the land is unknown.

## Pyandonea
**[Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md)**, meaning "the Veil of Mist" or "the Mist-Veiled Isles", is an archipelago located to the far south of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It is home to the elusive [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), a race of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) exiled there by the ancient [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md). For all of recorded history, [King Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) has ruled the land. It is said to have metropolises comparable to Tamriel.

The sources differ on the archipelago's location, suggesting it could lie either within the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) or in the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md), which the Maormer are said to rule. Some sources refer to Pyandonea as a continent or as part of a continental system. However, other lists of the known continents of Nirn exclude Pyandonea. Others refer to it as a single island.
