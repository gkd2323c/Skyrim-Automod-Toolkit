# Gallants of Hammerfell

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/ON-npc-Merric_at-Aswala.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Merric_at-Aswala.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Merric_at-Aswala.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Merric_at-Aswala.jpg) Merric at-Aswala, a Redguard Gallant [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/ON-creature-Gray_Yokudan_Charger.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Gray_Yokudan_Charger.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Gray_Yokudan_Charger.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Gray_Yokudan_Charger.jpg) The Yokudan Charger The Gallants of Hammerfell (also called the **Knights of the Desert**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOTYC-1)</sup> or simply **Gallants**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFTF-2)</sup> are expert sword-wielding warriors and horse-riders from the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), specifically the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) region. A gallant's main choice of steed is the [Yokudan Charger](Lore_Yokudan_Charger.md), a breed of [horse](Lore_Horse.md) that can traverse the Alik'r Desert better than other breeds.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOTYC-1)</sup> Gallants have several chapters across the Alik'r Desert and are based in the region's various cities, such as [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONGallant-3)</sup> and [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GOTOT-4)</sup> "Gallants" are the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) equivalent of the Tamrielic [knight](Lore_Knights.md). [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) god of justice, is their favorite deity.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFTF-2)</sup>

## History
It is said that "ancient tradition has predisposed the Redguards to knightly orders". Indeed, the [Order of Diagna](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md) was one of the most ancient among these, dating back to a time when they were a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1:WR-5)</sup> order of [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3:O-6)</sup> People who join a Redguard order must survive dangerous tests of skill to become a gallant.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1HF-7)</sup> During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Knightly Orders of High Rock were different enough from Redguard Gallants to appear alien.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONTKOHR-8)</sup>

Back when the Redguards fought the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), a scout from the Bergama Gallants named Abadaman of the Three Scars traveled east to the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) and took residence in the Old Tower, where he worked as a sentry. Over the course of his time, he was visited by various [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md) and one day during a walk at noon, he found a group of ghosts, Redguard soldiers gathered together for an impending event. The officer ghost, Captain Fayda asked Abadaman to defeat the traitor that took their lives, a Redguard named Amil Red-Hand. However, Abadaman deduced that the events that transpired had yet to occur and they were referring to his son, Amil, who was born with a red mark on his hand. Even though he knew Amil would betray his companions to the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), he refused to help them and left them to their inevitable fate.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GOTOT-4)</sup>

The [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) recruited Redguard Gallants.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONCovenant-9)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when the Withered Hand cult of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) attacked across the Alik'r Desert and the Sentinel Gallants were deployed to assist the relief effort. In Sentinel, Gallants helped clear out the Impervious Vault underneath the city from the cultists and later assisted General Thoda of the Hammerfell Army in the retaking of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) from the [Seventh Legion](Lore_Seventh_Legion.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONGallant-3)</sup>

## Notes
- An undiscovered NPC (or cut dialogue) compare and contrasts Redguard Gallants to [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) Knights. - *"Gallants. Horse-warriors. The Breton call us knights, but we don't fight like their knights. We wear leathers, turn quickly, fire our bows from the saddle. The * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * wear steel and wield steel. Their horses can only move quickly in a straight line."*
- Gallant "knightly orders" are referred to as a Gallantry in another line. - *"Mine and no one else's! The Gallantry of Hallin's Stand, the finest horsemen east of the Abecean Sea!
We came to aid the Garrison. But defending walls is not our way of fighting. I lost many of my fine stallions - and a few horses, too."*
