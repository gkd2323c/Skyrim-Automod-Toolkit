Sidri-Ashak was an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) potentate who served under Emperor [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md). Some regard him as the last strong Akaviri potentate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBLecture-1)</sup> Little is known about Sidri-Ashak prior to the reign of Brazollus Dor. By the time of Brazollus' reign, he was the leader of the Akaviri faction on the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and the chief advisor of the Emperor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup>

Eventually, Brazollus became uninterested in matters of state, so he delegated nearly all issues of policy and politics to Sidri-Ashak.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TEot V-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FSC1-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAHS-5)</sup> This was the Emperor's one wise decision during his reign, for Sidri-Ashak proved to be both a skilled administrator and a man of integrity.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup> He oversaw the last period of [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) roadbuilding in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBLecture-1)</sup> He is the namesake of the [Sidri-Ashak Runestones](Lore_Sidri-Ashak_Runestones.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBLecture-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Oblivion-6)</sup> Sidri-Ashak spent the remainder of Brazollus' reign quietly administering the Empire.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TEot V-3)</sup>

In [1E 2877](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2877), Brazollus died suddenly in his estate known as Villa Doria, reportedly after engaging in unspecified intense debaucheries, although some people suspected foul play.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TEot V-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAHS-5)</sup> It was rumored that factions unhappy with Sidri-Ashak's growing power decided to neutralize the potentate by bringing a vigorous new emperor to the throne. Brazollus' successor, [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md), quickly moved to return the Imperial court to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and exercise power in his own name, presumably removing Sidri-Ashak from power at that time.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TEot V-3)</sup>

Sidri-Ashak wrote a work titled *Recollections of a Humble Potentate*.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-2)</sup>
