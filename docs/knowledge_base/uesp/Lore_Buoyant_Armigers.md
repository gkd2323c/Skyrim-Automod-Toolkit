# Buoyant Armigers

*Vivec's finest: fleet and fit,
Besting heretic by sword
And Ordinator by wit.
Sworn to live a life of noble grace,
Except to laugh in danger's face.*
— [Captain Naros](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Captain_Naros) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/MW-npc-Galdal_Omayn.jpg/200px-MW-npc-Galdal_Omayn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Galdal_Omayn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-npc-Galdal_Omayn.jpg) Galdal Omayn, the Grand Marshal The Buoyant Armigers were a small military order of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), exclusively dedicated to and answering to Lord [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md). The Buoyant Armigers patterned themselves on Vivec's heroic spirit of exploration and adventure, and sought to emulate his mastery of the varied arts of personal combat, chivalric courtesy, and subtle verse. They served the Temple as champions and [knights](Lore_Knights.md) -errant, and were friendly rivals of the more solemn [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md). The majority of members who served in the order hailed from [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), as their ideals were closely related,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BASV-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWGDB-3)</sup> though some members hailed from other Houses,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BASV-1)</sup> and some weren't even [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ME-4)</sup>

The Buoyant Armigers were based in [Ghostgate](Lore_Ghostgate.md) and [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). They were tasked with containing the forces of [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) within the [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md) and tracking down [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) to bring them to justice. They were led by a Grand Marshal and Deputy Marshal, and used traditional chitin, [glass](Lore_Malachite.md) and [Daedric](Lore_Daedric.md) equipment.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MW-5)</sup> They were noted for using spiky glass equipment, an appearance inspired by the lessons in [Vivec's sermons](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BASV-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CMBA-6)</sup> They adopted the [Deep-Moss Ash Hopper](Lore_Ash_Hopper.md) as their mascot bug, due to their admiration of their green carapaces.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONDeep Moss Ash Hopper-7)</sup>

It is unknown if they were related to the [Hidden Armigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hidden_Armigers), the spy network of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Buoyant Armigers and other devotees of Vivec are often given Blank Books of Verse to capture their divine inspirations for prayer readings.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Contraband A2-9)</sup>

According to the founding myth of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), the Buoyant Armigers were formed when three houses "trapped" [Horde Mountain](Lore_Horde_Mountain.md) in a net of doubtful doctrine so that Vivec could pierce the monster with [Muatra](Lore_Muatra.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Medno Oren](Lore_Medno_Oren.md) held the title of Grand Marshal and Exalted Bard of the First Order.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTCS-11)</sup>

After Vvardenfell was opened for settlement in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), some members of the order joined the Imperial [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md). [Galdal Omayn](Lore_Galdal_Omayn.md) was Grand Marshal of the order during the fulfillment of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) Prophecy in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). That year, the Armigers briefly feuded with the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), and several Temple relics were recovered from crusaders who perished within the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) region.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MW-5)</sup> During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Ghostgate was put under siege by the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-12)</sup> and Vivec disappeared.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup> It is unknown what became of the Buoyant Armigers after the rise of the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
