Brynjolf is a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) thief, a member of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) and the [Nightingales](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md) from the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), one day Brynjolf noticed a new face in Riften, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), and approached them, revealing he was a member of the Thieves Guild. He offered them a chance to join his organization. Brynjolf wanted the Dragonborn to plant a merchant's ring on another merchant as a test. Conflicting reports claim whether this operation was successful or not; whichever the case, Brynjolf saw potential in the Dragonborn and sent them to the Thieves Guild's headguarters, the Ragged Flagon in Riften's Ratway.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ACA-1)</sup> He tasked the Dragonborn with collecting debt from different business owners, sent them to Goldenglow Estate, and sent them to Maven Black-Briar, who tasked them with sabotaging one of her business rivals. When the Dragonborn was sent to squeeze information from a smuggler, Gulum-Ei, he advised the Dragonborn to spare him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCB-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SF-5)</sup>

Later on, [Karliah](Lore_Karliah.md), a former guild member and lover of the previous Guild Master, [Gallus Desidenius](Lore_Gallus_Desidenius.md) returned to the Ragged Flagon accompanied by the Dragonborn. She revealed that [Mercer Frey](Lore_Mercer_Frey.md), Gallus' successor, had betrayed and murdered Gallus, framed Karliah, and stolen from the guild for years.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TP-6)</sup> Furious, Brynjolf joined Karliah and the Dragonborn, becoming a Nightingale. They tracked Mercer down to [Irkngthand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Irkngthand), where the Dragonborn defeated him. Brynjolf then elected the Dragonborn as the new Guild Master and was present for the ceremony.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TR-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BS-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-UNM-9)</sup>
