# Daedric

**Daedric** [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) and [heavy armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md) are the best quality standard items available in the base game, with the [Alternative Armors - Daedric Mail](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Daedric_Mail.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adding a [light armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) variant. This page documents standard Daedric armor and weapons; the unique Daedric Artifacts obtained by completing [Daedric Quests](Skyrim_Daedric_Quests.md) are documented on the [Artifacts](Skyrim_Artifacts.md) page.

Daedric items are made out of [ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md), using identical materials to the equivalent ebony items in addition to a [Daedra Heart](Skyrim_Daedra_Heart.md). The heart imbues the items with the essence of the slain [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), as described in *[Heavy Armor Forging](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor_Forging.md)*.

See the [Lore article](Lore_Daedric.md) for general information.

## Daedric Smithing
Daedric Smithing is the fourth perk in the heavy armor branch of the [smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill's perk tree. Unlocking it requires a smithing skill of 90 and the [Ebony Smithing](Skyrim_Ebony_Smithing.md) perk. Although Daedric armor is more effective than Dragonplate, the Daedric Smithing perk can be unlocked before the [Dragon Smithing](Skyrim_Dragon_Smithing.md) perk (which also allows for the creation of Dragonscale light armor).

The Daedric Smithing perk also causes tempering of Daedric weapons and armor to be twice as effective. This perk applies to both unenchanted Daedric gear and enchanted gear ([generic armor](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md), [generic weapons](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md), or [custom gear](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked. Tempering of several other items is also improved by Daedric Smithing, namely all [Dragon Priest Masks](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest_Mask.md) and all [Ancient Nord](Skyrim_Ancient_Nord.md) armor.

Obtaining a Daedra Heart is often the most difficult step in crafting Daedric gear; the [Daedra Heart](Skyrim_Daedra_Heart.md) article provides full details on how they can be obtained.

Daedric weapons and armor can also be created at the [Atronach Forge](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md). Although using the Atronach Forge allows these items to be made without investing in multiple smithing perks, obtaining the necessary materials can be more difficult. In addition to the equivalent ebony item and a Daedra Heart, you need to supply a [centurion dynamo core](Skyrim_Centurion_Dynamo_Core.md) and a [black soul gem](Skyrim_Black_Soul_Gem.md) — as well as having upgraded the forge with a [sigil stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sigil_Stone), which can only be done once your [conjuration](Skyrim_Conjuration.md) skill reaches 90. Also note that the Atronach Forge cannot be used to create unenchanted Daedric Boots (the boots are [bugged](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md#Bugs)), but randomly enchanted boots can be made. Daedric Arrows cannot be made at the Atronach Forge, either. However, the availability of all the ingredients still makes this a viable option for players not wishing to spend valuable skill points on the Daedric Smithing perk. The tempering bonuses, however, means that a player with the perk will see far greater benefits from the equipment.

## Heavy Armor

### Daedric Armor
Daedric Armor is the best [heavy armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md) in the base game, only surpassed by [Creation Club](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) content, such as its daedric plate variant (described [below](Skyrim_Daedric.md#Daedric_Plate_Armor)) and [Madness armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Madness_Armor). It appears in leveled lists starting at level 48 (enchanted varieties at level 49). However, even after level 48, Daedric armor is twenty times less likely to appear than other armor types. All items can be tempered using 1 [Ebony Ingot](Skyrim_Ebony_Ingot.md).

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds a gauntlet variant. Enchanted gauntlets can be bought with an 18 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment. Unenchanted gauntlets can be crafted, which have the same stats as base daedric gauntlets but differ in appearance.

## Light Armor
[](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Daedric_Mail.md)
### Daedric Mail <sup>[CC](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md)</sup>
Daedric mail is a [light armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) variant added by the [Alternative Armors - Daedric Mail](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Daedric_Mail.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md). A set can be obtained by completing [Missing Merchant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Missing_Merchant)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Daedric_Mail.md)</sup>, in addition to being able to craft it.

It's superior to any other light armor piece in the game, though it lacks both a helmet and shield. However, when wearing all three pieces of daedric mail armor, you will gain the benefits of the [Custom Fit](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md#Custom_Fit), [Matching Set](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md#Matching_Set) (light armor version), [Wind Walker](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md#Wind_Walker), and [Deft Movement](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md#Deft_Movement) perks, despite normally needing a helmet. Wearing a heavy daedric helmet will also count as a light armor matching set, though any other combination of daedric and daedric mail armor will only benefit from the heavy armor [Matching Set](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md#Matching_Set) perk. Using any other light armor helmet will prevent the matching set perk bonus.

## Daedric Weapons
Daedric [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) are the best standard weapons available in the original release of the game. The [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on introduces [Dragonbone](Skyrim_Dragonbone.md) weapons, which exceed Daedric in quality. They appear in leveled lists starting at level 46 (enchanted varieties at level 47). However, even after level 46, Daedric weapons are twenty times less likely to appear than other weapon types. All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using 1 ebony ingot. Arrows can only be created if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed. Daedric Crossbows are only available with the [Expanded Crossbow Pack](Skyrim_Expanded_Crossbow_Pack.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

Although most Daedric weapons are superior to their ebony equivalents, the Daedric Mace and Daedric War Axe actually do the same amount of damage as the ebony versions. Given that the Daedric versions weigh more and require a valuable Daedra Heart, some may prefer to use the ebony versions instead.

## Notes
- [Dremora](Skyrim_Dremora.md) also wear a variety of Daedric armor (see [Dremora equipment](Skyrim_Specialty_Gear.md#Dremora_Equipment)) which you can neither loot nor equip.
- Unlike in *Morrowind* and *Oblivion*, you don't need to use [Silver](Skyrim_Silver.md) or Daedric weapons to defeat magical enemies.
- [Bound weapons](Skyrim_Bound_Weapon.md) are similar in appearance to Daedric weapons, but generally have lower base damage values than Daedric weapons (unless you have the [Mystic Binding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mystic_Binding) perk, in which case bound swords have the same damage as the Daedric equivalent and bound bows have a higher damage. The Bound Battleaxe and Bound Dagger<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> still have lower damage than their Daedric equivalents.)
- Daedric equipment is generally not found for sale in merchants' lists, though a couple NPCs can sell it, provided you are at a specific level (40+): [Glover Mallory](Skyrim_Glover_Mallory.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> and the [Dremora Merchant](Skyrim_Dremora_Merchant.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>.
- High leveled [Dragons](Skyrim_Dragon.md) (such as Serpentine or Legendary Dragons) have a decent chance of having either a Daedric weapon or armor in their inventory, though not arrows.
- High level bandits have a small chance of having Daedric arrows in their inventories.

## Bugs
- If you temper a Daedric bow to legendary quality and then enchant it, its base damage may revert to a base Daedric bow.
- At the [Skyforge](Skyrim_Skyforge.md), [ancient Nord](Skyrim_Ancient_Nord.md) armor will also be listed in the Daedric section of the forging menu, and tempering of ancient Nord armor is also improved by the Daedric Smithing perk.
