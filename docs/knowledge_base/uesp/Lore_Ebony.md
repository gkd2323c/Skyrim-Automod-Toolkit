# Ebony


The ore has several alchemical properties<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> and is often smelted into ebony ingots for use in smithing.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Smith-13)</sup> The process of refining the ore takes six months, but the process could be sped up to only a few days with the aid of a convection oven that is stoked by white magical flames. Ebony armor is made by repeatedly folding the ore upon itself to form strips, which are then molded into shape.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TAC-14)</sup> This creates a durable, heavy armor. Ebony [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) are also highly prized. Smiths who can work with ebony are called ebonysmiths.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ARGreetings-15)</sup> The acquisition and use of ebony can lead mortals to transcend their limitations.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFAYQ-11)</sup>

## Cultural Myths
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) The Heart of Lorkhan Legend holds that after the defeat of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) in the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel) could not destroy his [Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), and so lashed it to an arrow and shot it with his [bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) across Tamriel and into the sea.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TM-16)</sup> The blood that poured from the Heart of Lorkhan became ebony.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CM22T-10)</sup>

According to pre- [ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta) [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) myths, after the events of [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) resulted in his organic [heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) being removed, [Namiira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Namiira) filled the hole where [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj)'s heart used to be, and kept him alive as a perverted imitation of his heart. Knowing that Namiira would overcome him, Lorkhaj fled to his sister [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah), who cleansed him of his corruption, and flung the [Dark Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart) into the Void, and Lorkhaj perished within Azurah's embrace.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOFDF-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TDS-18)</sup> Azurah had observed the Dark Heart beating like a drum, with each heartbeat spewing "black blood" (ebony) that tapped the floor to form a rhythm.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOFDF-17)</sup> The spirit [Noctra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Noctra) was later born of this black blood at the steps of the Void Gate.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TDS-18)</sup> The spirit [Boethra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boethra) created the [Ebony Mail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Mail) out of this substance, which functions as the "death-shroud of Lorkhaj", which she wears on nights of the [Ghost Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost_Moon), and "wages war beyond the Lattice".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWS-19)</sup>

According to [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) myth, [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) cried tears of pure ebony as he watched the city [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md) burn. His son [Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol) collected the tears and used them to forge [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md), his legendary battleaxe.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Sot R5-20)</sup>

Orc followers of the Trinimac Cult use ebony to accent their armors and weapons in token of Lorkhan's blood when Trinimac tore out his heart. They believe that Zylvara, the great eagle bore the Golden Champion to his reckoning with the [man](Lore_Man.md) -demon [Quarvish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quarvish).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CM22T-10)</sup>

[Reymon Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reymon_Ebonarm) is the God of War and the companion and protector of all warriors. Ebonarm's name refers to the ebony sword fused to his right arm due to the wounds he suffered in titanic battles of the past, and he is never seen without a full suit of ebony armor. Emblazoned on his ebony tower shield is the symbol of a red rose, a flower known for blooming on battlefields where he appears. According to legend, he appears on the battlefield to reconcile opposing sides and prevent bloodshed.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OH-22)</sup>

The association with Lorkhan's blood reaches various cultures. The term "[Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor)'s blood" is commonly used by Nords to exclaim surprise,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOSB-23)</sup> though the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) equivalent is the phrase "[Sheor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheor)'s blood".<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONLudovic-24)</sup> The town of [Shor's Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor%27s_Stone) is evidently named after Shor, and is known for its ebony mine, where miners harvest the blood of Shor.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> Some Nords believe [Nirncrux](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirncrux) to also be the blood of Shor.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONRH-26)</sup>

## History

### Early History
The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) are said to have learned to forge ebony back in "Boethiah's day",<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-IWADE-7)</sup> with evidence indicating they learned to do so back when they were the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), before the rise of [Great House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_House) culture.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BTAOM-28)</sup> The curves commonly featured in ebony weaponry emulate the Dunmeri style,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DFCN-29)</sup><sup>:161</sup> and it also became common for Dunmer to have their equipment made from lesser materials lacquered with dark tones to appear ebony-like.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CMDE-30)</sup> [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) is said to have once sealed a temple with a seamless plane of ebony.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Vivecmeeting-31)</sup> Historically, the Bretons maintained ties with the Dark Elves through a trade agreement between [Clan R'aathim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra%27athim) of Morrowind and the Kings of High Rock. Under this pact, [King Moraelyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moraelyn) of [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart) 's retainers were granted rights to mine ebony in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains) and establish a colony in High Rock. In return, they supplied the Breton lords with ebony weapons and taught them how to smith ebony themselves.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-KE-32)</sup> The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) used ebony, and [ebony-steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony-steel) alloys for constructing certain [animunculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) and [vamidiums](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vamidium).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-34)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-35)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/ON-item-armor-Ebony.jpg/200px-ON-item-armor-Ebony.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-armor-Ebony.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-armor-Ebony.jpg) The typical "Ebony style" Armor (ESO) Frikkhild Coldheart of Windhelm delved into the origins of the aesthetic most commonly associated with ebony armor and weapons, but her investigation yielded no definitive answers. During her research, she encountered several terms that might be related, though she remained uncertain: Mackkan, Fickledire, and Caldera. Mackkan is known as the wielder of Malacath’s Scourge, while Fickledire refers to the place where the Scourge was forged.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TLS-36)</sup> Additionally, she heard of the term “Glorious Upheaval”, which is the name of a book advocating the worship of Boethiah instead of the Eight Divines.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-GU-37)</sup> Frikkhild suspects that the style represents a fusion of Dunmer, Nord, and even Daedric influences.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-CM37T-38)</sup> [Rolis Hlaalu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rolis_Hlaalu), the Dunmer known as the Mastercraft Mediator, recruited master craftsmen from various trades to complete mysterious and exclusive contracts for high-end clients. These craftsmen were rewarded with "Writ Vouchers", a unique form of [currency](Lore_Currency.md) used to obtain coveted goods and knowledge. Among the high-quality items he offered was a coveted tome that revealed the secrets to craft in the ebony style, which was based off the research of Frikkhild Coldheart.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-39)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-40)</sup>

An ebony mine used to be exploited by the people of [Koeglin Village](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Koeglin_Village) in High Rock, but it was shuttered and became commonly occupied by bandits.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONHallix-42)</sup>

During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) utilized an ebony-steel alloy in their maces.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-43)</sup>

According to various mortal smiths, iron cannot be used to create an alloy with ebony.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-44)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-45)</sup> The [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) used during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) were made of ebony-alloy cold-iron, which were smithed by Dremora in [the Black Forge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Black_Forge).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-LS-46)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TBF-47)</sup>

The Vassir-Didanat Ebony Mine in the [Ascadian Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascadian_Isles) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) is known for its source of ebony, which is mostly lackluster, but just enough to be worth mining. Unsatisfied, one of the previous owners of the mine turned to [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) and erected a shrine within its interior. They made a deal with the Prince of Bargains, requesting a limitless supply of ebony, and Vile provided them a formula for them to do so.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TSF-48)</sup> This substance, dubbed "[False Ebony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:False_Ebony)", looks and feels like real ebony, but can be told apart by a skilled craftsman.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-GJ-49)</sup>

By the mid-Second Era, blacksmiths had achieved a remarkable feat: the creation of materials that surpassed ebony. This breakthrough was driven by the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md), which enabled a wider dissemination of knowledge concerning rare and exotic materials. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), in particular, played a key role by discovering methods to refine magical ores.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MAMA-50)</sup> One such material was [quicksilver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quicksilver);<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-51)</sup> however, by the Fourth Era, quicksilver armor was consistently inferior to ebony, and even to armor crafted from [Chaurus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chaurus) chitin.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-52)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/MW-item-Ebony_Weapons.jpg/200px-MW-item-Ebony_Weapons.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Ebony_Weapons.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Ebony_Weapons.jpg) Ebony weapons (Morrowind) [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) coveted the vast source of Ebony in Morrowind, leading to it being one of the main reasons for his decision to invade it.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TAH-53)</sup> Sometime after the signing of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), it was protected by Imperial law during the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), and could not be mined or exported without an Imperial charter.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Codex-2)</sup> The [East Empire Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:East_Empire_Company) essentially became the sole authority to trade in raw ebony.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-BMCM-54)</sup> The book *The Eastern Provinces Impartially Considered* would later criticize the Empire’s terms within the Armistice, which guarantees Morrowind the right to maintain slavery, punishes those who oppose it, and relies on the Imperial Legion to uphold the practice, all while Imperial-chartered monopolists exploit enslaved labor to extract ebony from the province.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-55)</sup>

After a massacre of [Imperial troops](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) near Armanias in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), [Empress Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) and the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) debated who should design new gear for the legionaries. Katariah supported [Hazadir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hazadir), a clever former slave she had met in [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City), while the Council backed [Sirollus Saccus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sirollus_Saccus), the finest armorer in the Imperial City. To settle the matter, each outfitted a champion for a duel. Saccus equipped Raphalas Eul with exquisite ebony gear, while Hazadir armed [Nandor Beraid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nandor_Beraid) using simpler materials and knowledge he had gained from briefly studying under Saccus. Although Hazadir's equipment was less refined, Nandor triumphed due to Hazadir’s deep understanding of Black Marsh’s harsh terrain, which was simulated in the duel. As a result, Hazadir earned the commission and went on to design the weapons and armor that won the war in Armanias.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-HT-56)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TAC-14)</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Imperial law declared ebony the property of the Emperor, and smuggling it was considered treason, punishable by death.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-57)</sup> Sjorvar Horse-Mouth of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) kept a close watch on the [Camonna Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camonna_Tong) and the smuggler caves scattered along the [Bitter Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bitter_Coast), which were commonly used to transport raw ebony along with other illicit goods.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-58)</sup> That same year, House Redoran struggled to compete with [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md)'s dominance of the ebony trade in Vvardenfell,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Hlaano-59)</sup> with the most obvious conflict centered on the town of [Caldera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caldera), which was founded by the Imperial Caldera Mining Company with the support of House Hlaalu. House Redoran actively worked to find evidence of corruption in Caldera in an effort to shut the mines down.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-60)</sup> Meanwhile, the Hlaalu attempted to negotiate a trade deal with [Zainab Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zainab_Tribe) to have them only sell their ebony supply to House Hlaalu.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-61)</sup> Concurrently, the East Empire Company founded the Ebony mining colony of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) on the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-HORR1-62)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/SR-item-Ebony_Spell_Knight_Armor_Male.jpg/200px-SR-item-Ebony_Spell_Knight_Armor_Male.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ebony_Spell_Knight_Armor_Male.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ebony_Spell_Knight_Armor_Male.jpg) Ebony Bretonic Spell Knight Armor Eventually, any significant East Empire Company presence on Solstheim met its end due to their ventures there being consistently unprofitable.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-63)</sup> Raven Rock would become a House Redoran sovereignty after Solstheim was proffered to the Dunmer in [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-HORR2-64)</sup> When the ebony mine at Raven Rock was closed in [4E 181](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_181),<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-HORR3-65)</sup> House Redoran's neglect of Solstheim increased, as without the mine, the island not only held little economic or strategic importance compared with the other seventy-three outlying settlements of Morrowind.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-HRR-66)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), new ebony deposits were discovered and the mine reopened.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-TFD-67)</sup> The resumed flow of ebony from Raven Rock prompted House Redoran to take a renewed interest in Raven Rock and Solstheim.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-68)</sup>

The mine at Shor's Stone in Skyrim provides ebony and serves as the foundation of its local economy.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-69)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-70)</sup> The denizens of the Orcish stronghold of Narzulbur operate the nearby Gloombound Mine, which contains a significant amount of ebony.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-71)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/MW-item-Daedric_Weapons.jpg/200px-MW-item-Daedric_Weapons.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Weapons.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Daedric_Weapons.jpg) Daedric weapons (Morrowind) [Daedric equipment](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment) is used by the forces of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and is regarded as one of the strongest and rarest types known to Nirn. Mortals have struggled to replicate it, giving rise to various accounts on how to achieve its creation; however, the Dremora [Lyranth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyranth) believes their efforts remain pale imitations of true Daedric craftsmanship, limited by the inferior materials found on Nirn.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-72)</sup> Most mortals agree that forging such items involves binding ebony with Daedric essence<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Earnaana-73)</sup> through a [Daedra heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Heart). Some insist the process must be done at night, under a new or full moon, but never during an eclipse. A red harvest moon is said to be ideal. This, however, is likely myth, as these sinister items have been crafted without those conditions being met.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-HAF-74)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-SRDS-75)</sup> In truth, their creation demands the skill of a master blacksmith and [enchanter](Lore_Enchanting.md). <sup>[[74]](#cite_note-SRDS-75)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Earnaana-73)</sup>

## Artifacts
A number of artifacts are made of ebony, and ebony has been used to improve or temper the quality of various artifacts.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Smith-13)</sup> Sometimes an artifact has appeared as made out of Ebony, but has also appeared at other times made from a different material.

#### [Dragon's Tooth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon%27s_Tooth)
The [Dragon's Tooth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon%27s_Tooth) (or simply **Tooth**) is a mythical ebony dagger with a hilt made from a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md)'s tooth, hence the name. The hilt is intricately carved with intertwining roses, thorns, and leaves, in line with the [black rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_rose) symbology tied with [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and the [Ra'athim Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra%27athim) of [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart). It is also described as having a "wicked point" and a sharp edge you can even shave with. The tooth came from the dragon who roosted in [Fang Lair](Lore_Fang_Lair.md). In the historical-fiction series, *[King Edward](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:King_Edward)*, they were known as [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), the Lord of the Dragons but based on historical information, they were known as [Thurvokun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thurvokun). It is mainly associated with the two legendary heroes, Kings [Moraelyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moraelyn) and [Edward](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Edward), who took in the dragon as one of their [companions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moraelyn%27s_Companions#Akatosh).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg/150px-ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ebony_Blade.jpg) The Ebony Blade
#### [Ebony Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Blade)
The [Ebony Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Blade), also known as the **Vampire** or **the Leech**, is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). It resembles an ebony katana, and is considered to be an artifact of great evil, having the ability to absorb the life essence of those that it strikes. Part of the damage inflicted flows into the wielder as raw power, draining the victim of their health and sometimes stamina. It has also been known to fortify the wielder's skill at using long blades, magically silence opponents to prevent them from casting spells, and deceive victims. Khajiiti myths do not mention the blade by name, but instead refer to it as 'the killing word of the Spider' and 'the black edge of shadow'.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/BL-artifact-Ebony_Mail.jpg/150px-BL-artifact-Ebony_Mail.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-artifact-Ebony_Mail.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-artifact-Ebony_Mail.jpg) The Ebony Mail
#### [Ebony Mail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Mail)
The [Ebony Mail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Mail) is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md). It may appear as an ebony breastplate or occasionally a fuller suit of armor. It grants its wearer resistance to fire and [magic](Lore_Magic.md), as well as magical shielding from physical blows, though in some instances it provides for quieter movements and the ability to poison nearby enemies. According to legend, the artifact was created before recorded history by Boethiah, and it is Boethiah alone who determines who should possess the Mail and for how long a time.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/ON-crown_store-Mehrunes%27_Razor.jpg/150px-ON-crown_store-Mehrunes%27_Razor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mehrunes%27_Razor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mehrunes%27_Razor.jpg) Mehrunes' Razor
#### [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md)
[Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md) (sometimes Mehrunes Razor), also called the Dagger of the Final Wounds, the Bane of the Righteous, and the Kingslayer, is a Daedric artifact created by the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon. This powerful ebony dagger has the ability to kill instantly, as there is a small chance that Dagon will claim the souls of those struck by the Razor. It is Daedric in appearance, emblazoned with Daedric script and seemingly able to drink the light that hits it.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/SR-item-Scourge.jpg/150px-SR-item-Scourge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Scourge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Scourge.jpg) Scourge
#### [Scourge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scourge)
[Scourge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scourge) (also known as **Mackkan's Hammer**, **Bane of Daedra**, the **Daedric Scourge**, or **Scourge, Blessed of Malacath**) is a legendary [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). It was forged from sacred ebony in the Fires (or Fountains) of Fickledire, and is associated with [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath). It is a fierce [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md), and takes the form of a steel or ebony mace. Malacath dedicated it to mortals, and any [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who attempts to invoke its power will be sucked into a voidhole and banished to the [Outer Darkness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) of [Oblivion's](Lore_Oblivion.md) voidstreams. It also has the ability to banish them to Oblivion with a single blow, and can summon Daedra such as [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) and [Scamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp) from Oblivion to do the wielder's bidding. The summons are said to be those that it has previously "killed", and its victims are said to be poisoned. It has been described as a "bold defender of the friendless", which could be related to Malacath's role as the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of outcasts.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/SR-item-Stendarr%27s_Hammer.jpg/150px-SR-item-Stendarr%27s_Hammer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Stendarr%27s_Hammer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Stendarr%27s_Hammer.jpg) Stendarr's Hammer
#### [Stendarr's Hammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stendarr%27s_Hammer)
[Stendarr's Hammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stendarr%27s_Hammer) is a hammer rumored to have once been wielded by [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), the God of Justice. One of his other titles, the God of Righteous Might, is fitting as the hammer is very heavy. It is made of ebony and [sapphire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapphire), which can also be used to further temper it The weapon is enchanted to drain or damage the health of those it strikes, at the cost of the wielder's stamina.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg/150px-SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) Wuuthrad
#### [Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md)
[Wuuthrad](Lore_Wuuthrad.md), meaning "Storm's Tears" in the language of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), was the legendary battleaxe wielded by [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) during his conquest of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). With Wuuthrad in his hands, Ysgramor and his [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) slaughtered an untold number of [elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md), which culminated in the founding of the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md). For the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md), Wuuthrad is a sacred relic which holds a great deal of respect and reverence, and through it they can trace their line straight back to the first Harbinger. It is held to be the greatest of all Nord [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md).

## Notes
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-wood-Heartwood.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-wood-Heartwood.png) Heartwood - The [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) is made from Lorkhan's blood infused with [Magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magicka), and is red unlike ebony's black color.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-76)</sup>
- In real life, [ebony](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebony) is a type of hard, black wood, traditionally used to make piano keys and other such items. This is in no way related to the ebony in *The Elder Scrolls* series, which is a [volcanic glass](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanic_Glass). A closer equivalent in real life would be obsidian, though ebony's use in making armor and weapons suggest that it is far tougher and more malleable than obsidian, which is hard and brittle. The mention of "folding" when smithing it lends evidence that it may be some manner of metallic glass. - Ebony wood also exists in Tamriel.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-EWC-77)</sup> The icon for heartwood appears to depict unprocessed ebony wood.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-HW-78)</sup>
