# Camoran Dynasty

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Valenwood_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Valenwood_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), the realm over which the Camoran Dynasty ruled The Camoran Dynasty, also known as the **Camorian Empire**, was an [aristocratic dynasty](Lore_Empires.md) that ruled [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) for much of its history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> One of the earliest known dynasties in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), its founding by King [Eplear](Lore_Eplear.md) is an event traditionally regarded as the beginning of [Year Zero](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_0) of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup> Eplear's success in uniting the inherently wild [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) has been argued to be one of the greatest military feats in Tamriel's history. The Camorans remained in power for centuries afterwards, even resisting the aggressive expansion of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), until Valenwood was conquered by the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) in [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714) following years of unrelenting warfare and the devastation of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md). The Camoran Dynasty survived, but the Empire broke its influence by granting independence to Valenwood's lesser nobles.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup>

When the Second Empire fell in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), the Camorans unsuccessfully attempted to reestablish their reign, but Valenwood's territories had drifted too far apart culturally to be united and the region descended into conflict.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup> Valenwood would briefly achieve unity under Camoran rule only twice during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), and then only as a member of the first and second [Aldmeri Dominions](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). At the time of the first Aldmeri Dominion in the Sixth Century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md) was crowned king and joined the Dominion to defend his realm against encroaching [Colovians](Lore_Colovian.md) in the north.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AUAAD1-4)</sup> Viewed by some Bosmer as disrespectful of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), Aeradan was forced to fight a civil war against the Blacksap Rebellion led by his cousin Gelthior Camoran, and later suppressed an attempted second insurrection by Gelthior's sons Gorinir and Lorchon in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOGel-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESO-6)</sup> A subsequent dispute over the succession between the Camoran heirs in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830) led to the intervention of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and the crowning of [Camoran Anaxemes](Lore_Camoran_Anaxemes.md), who pledged fealty to the [king of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) and thereby ushered in the second Dominion. Anaxemes was then forced to cede much of his power to the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), a congress of Bosmeri chieftains and Altmeri diplomats who proved modestly effective until [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) swept them away.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> Gorinir family was a cadet branch of the Camoran royal line.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GE-7)</sup>

Under the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the Camorans retained the throne of Valenwood as figurehead monarchs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup> The year [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249) saw another dispute arise over the Camoran succession, a dispute which soon escalated into a massive war that threatened the whole of the Empire. One claimant to the throne, Camoran Kaltos, conquered most of Valenwood in two years with the aid of [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md), also called Haymon Hart-King, another claimant and Kaltos's chief advisor and sorcerer.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TR-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TFOTU-9)</sup> Against Kaltos's wishes, however, Haymon declared war on the people of Colovia and marched his armies north.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TR-8)</sup> Kaltos pleaded with Haymon to return to Valenwood, but Haymon betrayed him and claimed the Camoran throne for himself, thus becoming the Camoran Usurper.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TR-8)</sup> The Usurper's armies ravaged western Tamriel for nearly twenty years, driving through [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) all the way to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) while Kaltos unsuccessfully fought to stop him.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> At last the Principality of [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md) marshaled the other city-states of High Rock and joined forces with Camoran Kaltos to defeat the Camoran Usurper at the [Battle of Firewaves](Lore_Battle_of_Firewaves.md);<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TFOTU-9)</sup> Haymon Hart-King was killed in the battle.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TR-8)</sup>

## Known Camorans
- **Merchant Camoran's mother** — Mother of the eventual wife of Eplear. (possibly apocryphal)
- **[King Eplear](Lore_Eplear.md)** — Founder of the Camoran Dynasty, first king of the whole of Valenwood.
- **Merchant Camoran** — Wife of King Eplear. (possibly apocryphal)
- **Calarthor Camoran** - Member of the Camoran Dynasty who died in battle.
- **[King Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md)** - King of Valenwood during the mid-Second Era and one of the founders of the First Aldmeri Dominion.
- **[Gelthior Camoran](Lore_Gelthior_Camoran.md)** - Cousin of Aeradan Camoran. He led the Blacksap Rebellion in a civil war against Aeradan in an attempt to become king, but he was eventually captured and imprisoned.
- **Gorinir Camoran** - Son of Gelthior Camoran. He became the co-leader of the remnants of the Blacksap Rebellion with his brother, Lorchon, following their father's capture. In 2E 582, he and Lorchon were captured and then imprisoned with their father, effectively ending the Blacksap Rebellion.
- **Lorchon Camoran** - Son of Gelthior Camoran. He became the co-leader of the remnants of the Blacksap Rebellion with his brother, Gorinir, following their father's capture. In 2E 582, he and Gorinir were captured and then imprisoned with their father, effectively ending the Blacksap Rebellion.
- **[King Anaxemes Camoran](Lore_Camoran_Anaxemes.md)** - The Bosmer king of Valenwood during the formation of the Aldmeri Dominion in 2E 830.
- **Kaltos Camoran** - Campaigned for the Camoran throne alongside his sorcerer and advisor, Haymon Camoran.
- **[Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md)** - After betraying Kaltos and taking the Camoran throne for himself, Haymon became known as the Camoran Usurper and proceeded to wage war in western Tamriel before eventually being defeated.
- **[Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md)** - Son of Haymon Camoran and his mistress, Kaalys. He was the founder and leader of the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) cult that sought to bring destruction to Tamriel with the assistance of the Daedric Lord [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). He was defeated by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).
- **[Ruma Camoran](Lore_Ruma_Camoran.md)** - Daughter of Mankar Camoran, joined her father's efforts to usher in a new age. She was defeated by the Hero of Kvatch.
- **[Raven Camoran](Lore_Raven_Camoran.md)** - Son of Mankar Camoran, joined his father's efforts to usher in a new age. He was defeated by the Hero of Kvatch.
- **Celia Camoran** - Notable figure who was impersonated by a thief to help with a heist of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md).

- The Camoran Usurper reportedly fathered an illegitimate son named Mankar who was born just prior to his defeat.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TR-8)</sup> This son shared the same name as [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), an Altmer who led the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) cult, suggesting both are the same individual.
