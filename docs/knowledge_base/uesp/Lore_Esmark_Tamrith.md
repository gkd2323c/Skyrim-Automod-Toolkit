Esmark Tamrith was the Baron of [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland, the future King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), spent a good portion of his youth in the company of Tamrith and Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup>

In [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) declared war on Wayrest after King Emeric married Princess [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) instead of his daughter [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOKR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOAM6-3)</sup> Ranser called Baron Tamrith and House Tamrith, along with the other noble houses of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), to arms, who complied and provided their troops despite their strong ties with Wayrest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-4)</sup> Shortly after the outbreak of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), House Tamrith withdrew its support of him and petitioned for peace, and [House Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) quickly followed suit.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> Following Ranser's death and the conclusion of the war in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), Baron Tamrith and the leaders of Houses Dorell and [Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) pledged their loyalty to Emeric.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NAA-5)</sup>

In the immediate aftermath of Ranser's death, Rivenspire fell into chaos.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-4)</sup> To restore order to Rivenspire, Baron Tamrith, who had emerged as a powerful force for peace and cooperation, proposed the formation of a ruling triumvirate composed of the leaders of Houses Dorell, Montclair, and Tamrith to govern the region in the name of Emeric.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NAA-5)</sup> Because Emeric did not trust any of Rivenspire's nobles to be King of Shornhelm, he approved of the formation of the triumvirate Council of the North to govern the region, but he also promised to eventually select a new king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HKEd-6)</sup> He reportedly disliked the House Montclair representative on the Council of the North, Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CETd-7)</sup>

Esmark married Janece of House Elde, combining their fortunes and creating an even stronger political entity.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> With Janece, Esmark had two daughters, [Eselde](Lore_Eselde_Tamrith.md), who took after her mother, and Janeve, who took after her father.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CVRd-8)</sup> In [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), Eselde left Rivenspire to further her education and religious studies in [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md), while Janeve, against Esmark's wishes, joined the Shornhelm Guard.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Tamrith died of natural causes, and his funeral was held in Shornhelm.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Sd-9)</sup> After his death, Eselde succeeded him as the leader of House Tamrith.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup> He was remembered as a sensible, strong, and pragmatic leader.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CVRd-8)</sup>
