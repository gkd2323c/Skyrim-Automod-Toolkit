# Scourge

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/SR-item-Scourge.jpg/200px-SR-item-Scourge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Scourge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Scourge.jpg) Scourge (*Skyrim* Creation Club) Scourge (also known as **Mackkan's Hammer**, **Bane of Daedra**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBOD-1)</sup> the **Daedric Scourge**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-2)</sup> or **Scourge, Blessed of Malacath**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLS-3)</sup> is a legendary [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). It was forged from sacred [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) in the Fires (or Fountains)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLS-3)</sup> of Fickledire,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-2)</sup> and is associated with [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLS-3)</sup> It is a fierce [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md), and takes the form of a steel or ebony mace. Malacath dedicated it to mortals, and any [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who attempts to invoke its power will be sucked into a voidhole and banished to the [Outer Darkness](Lore_Void.md) of [Oblivion's](Lore_Oblivion.md) voidstreams.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBOD-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WK-5)</sup> It also has the ability to banish them to Oblivion with a single blow,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLS-3)</sup> and can summon Daedra such as [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) and [Scamps](Lore_Scamp.md) from Oblivion to do the wielder's bidding.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWS-6)</sup> The summons are said to be those that it has previously "killed", and its victims are said to be poisoned.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-7)</sup><sup>:241</sup> It has been described as a "bold defender of the friendless",<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLS-3)</sup> which could be related to Malacath's role as the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of outcasts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBOD-1)</sup>

## History
Scourge was once used by Mackkan, who banished many of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s minions with it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLS-3)</sup> The blessing of Scourge was mentioned within the rings allegedly gifted from Malacath to the [Brutal Breed](Lore_Malacath%27s_Brutal_Breed.md) cult after the fall of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) around [1E 1000](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1000).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

### Second Era
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWYB-9)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-7)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing Scourge among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TA-10)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Scourge and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWFR-11)</sup>

### Third Era
The mace eventually came into the possession of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and was hung in the armory of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md). The [Imperial Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) of the Battlespire used it against the Daedra Lords in the name of the Emperor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLS-3)</sup>

In the aftermath of Dagon's invasion of the Battlespire during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the hidden mace was recovered from the Caitiff section of the Battlespire by an [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md) and used to help fight back against the retreating Daedric forces. A Daedra named [Wonshala Keriayn](Lore_Wonshala_Keriayn.md) was interested in obtaining the mace in return for a piece of the Battlespire's shattered voidguide, but when she tried to invoke the weapon's powers with the keyword 'Equality', it banished her to the Outer Darkness.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WK-5)</sup> Scourge survived the ensuing destruction of the Battlespire, and was returned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), where it now wanders the land with adventurers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-2)</sup> Yagrum's Bagarn's book would be updated and go under the name *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*, and would change Scourge's entry to mention the events that transpired in the Battlespire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TL-2)</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the mace once came into the possession of [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md), who kept it in [Tel Fyr](Lore_Tel_Fyr.md), a [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) wizard tower in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)'s [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md). The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) obtained the item while adhering to Divayth's strange hobby of allowing his collection to be pilfered under certain circumstances.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWDivayth-12)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the mace was in the possession of Valkyn Gatanas, a powerful [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) serving as one of Mehrunes Dagon's invasion leaders, who used a pair of magic-negating gauntlets to wield the weapon. After the defeat of the general, Scourge was retrieved by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-The_Cause-13)</sup>

## Notes
- In [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online), Scourge is depicted on Malacath's banners. With its flanges, Scourge resembles an axe at certain angles. Interestingly, Malacath is also depicted with an axe that is reminiscent of Scourge's side profile via the [Orsinium Relief, Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Orsinium_Relief,_Malacath), and the [Malacath's Wrathful Flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Malacath%27s_Wrathful_Flame) item.
