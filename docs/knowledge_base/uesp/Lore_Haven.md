—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Haven is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> located on the stormy waters of the [Long Coast](Lore_Long_Coast.md). Haven is an important trading post and port city that has changed hands constantly over the course of years, from the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) to the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad Aquifer-2)</sup> It is considered the gateway into [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md), the greater region of southeast Valenwood and it is also a key link in the Lane, a major sea route that runs along Tamriel's southwest coast.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASP-3)</sup> Haven acquired its name because it seems to be a safe place in a dangerous world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AR-4)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/ON-place-The_Aquifer.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Aquifer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Aquifer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Aquifer.jpg) The Aquifer [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/41/ON-place-Fighters_Guild_%28Haven%29_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fighters_Guild_%28Haven%29_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fighters_Guild_(Haven)_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fighters_Guild_(Haven)_03.jpg) The Harbor side of the Fort Haven is built on the southeast coast of Valenwood, in a clearing between the shallow marshes and the steep ridges that separates it from the rest of the mainland.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHaven-5)</sup> Haven is at the epicenter of several trade routes. From the sea, it is an in-between stop on the Lane, an active sea route on the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md) between [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), as well as a gateway between the sea and the rest of the mainland.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASP-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONHenriel-6)</sup>

While on land, it is a halfway point between [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md), which lies on the other side of the region to the west, and [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md), the biggest city in the province's southeast quadrant.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHaven-5)</sup> The trail that connects Haven and Southpoint is known as the Haven Trace.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOUnused-7)</sup> The beaches outside of Haven are known to contain well-preserved rare fossils and shells that circulate across the provinces.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONContraband R-8)</sup>

Haven is built in a small crater, at the base of a hill. The city is lowest in the north half, but places such as the Mages Guildhall and the gates are built above the incline and even have stairs leading to them. The south half of Haven is called the Fort, a walled-off sea keep built on a steep hill that looks over the north side and the docks.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHaven-5)</sup> All of the import that goes into Haven has to go through the Fort, but smugglers have always looked for ways around it.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AD-9)</sup> The mound west of the Fort is called Widow's Rock and at its base is the entrance to the Aquifer, an old [Colovian](Lore_Colovian.md) -styled sewer that runs underneath Haven.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASP-3)</sup> Haven is neighbored by several settlements, including Cormeir Spring to the far north-northwest, Heimdar City to the northwest, and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) to the northeast,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> toward [Torval](Lore_Torval.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ARMap EL-10)</sup>

## History

### Early History and the Treethane's Autonomy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/OB-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg/200px-OB-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) The White-Gold Tower [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/ON-interior-The_Aquifer.jpg/200px-ON-interior-The_Aquifer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Aquifer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Aquifer.jpg) The Imperial Sewers under Haven Haven's founding dates back many years ago at an unspecified time in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). It flourished with trade following the construction of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) along with other significant cities at the time, such as [Ceyatatar](Lore_Ceyatatar.md) and [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ACOV-11)</sup> From what can be surmised, Haven was originally a settlement inhabited by Wood Elves, independent from the rest of the region until it was claimed under the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md). It began with King [Eplear](Lore_Eplear.md)'s reign, which started at the beginning of recorded history in [1E 0](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_0) and each of these nations was ruled by a treethane that served under the Camorans.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3-12)</sup>

This era of rule continued for many years until the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) invaded Valenwood and conquered the province in [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714). To disrupt the Camoran monarchy's influence over their people, the Empire granted the treethanes independence, turning local trading hubs into nine, semi-autonomous regions of equal power.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONLoad Underroot-13)</sup> Haven was governed by the Second Empire,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad Aquifer-2)</sup> who built over the settlement's foundation and turned it into a dominant Colovian city. They even built a sewer system called the Aquifer, which extended underneath the city and emptied from the southwest.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASP-3)</sup>

Haven had been the prime trading post in the area for many years since its independence. That was until [1E 2729](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2729), when it had lost much of its business to the newly-built colony of [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFOS-14)</sup> After the Second Empire dissolved in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), the Camoran Empire tried to annex these nations but they had become too culturally different to become one united identity. But without one government to mediate their ambitions, Haven and the other countries went to war with each other, and all fell apart. Sometimes, even outside forces like the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) attacked these nations,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3-12)</sup> such as when Haven and Woodhearth were sacked by the Sea Elves sometime around [2E 455](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_455).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ACOV-11)</sup>

### Haven in the Second and Third Eras
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven.jpg) The Battle of Haven [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Unsafe_Haven_03.jpg) The Necromantic Ritual By the time the High Elves came into Haven, they built over the old Imperial foundation and converted it into their racial architectural style.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONLoad Aquifer-2)</sup> During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Haven had seen little activity from the local natives until it was visited by the [Gold Fleet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold_Fleet), a large fleet of [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) ships that were en-course for [Khenarthi's Roost](Lore_Khenarthi%27s_Roost.md) on an important diplomatic mission with some of the Dominion's highest authorities. Haven saw an intense amount of activity when the first few ships arrived at the port.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AD-9)</sup>

The Jade Dragoons under General [Endare](Lore_Endare.md) were sent to Haven to help the city guard maintain peace and watch out for smugglers. The next day after Haven was practically empty and General Endare left with her militia to seek out Ayleid ruins inland, leaving the city completely defenseless.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AD-9)</sup>

The Jackdaw Pirates, led by Captain Gjarma Raven-Hair used this opportunity to take over and pillage the city. But shortly after, a [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) galleon under Lord Gharesh-ri led the charge to retake Haven. By the time the Prowler had arrived, the city was engulfed in conflict. An [Agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Dominion joined the fray and slowly thwarted their plans. They rendezvoused with Lord Gharesh-ri and his militia by the east gate and gradually defeated each of Gjarma's high-ranked crewmates. The Fort had been sealed away, so the Agent was forced to infiltrate the compound from the Aquifers. They defeated the pirate captain at the Fighters Guildhall, where she was performing a ritual to engulf Haven in a sea of undead.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONUnsafe-15)</sup> Recognized for their actions, the Agent was sent to Elden Root to lend their help to the [King](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md) of the Wood Elves.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONTrouble-16)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Haven was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Silrim and had a rivalry with Southpoint.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AR-4)</sup>

### Known Rulers
For the longest time, the treethanes of Valenwood served the Camoran Dynasty until the Second Empire assumed control of the province. They shifted power to the Camoran treethanes, who then ruled independent powers such as Haven.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3-12)</sup> At some point in time, Haven's leader was known as the Governor.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-FP-17)</sup> By the Third Era, it was ruled by a King/Queen.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AR-4)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-quest-War_Orphan%27s_Sojourn.jpg/200px-ON-quest-War_Orphan%27s_Sojourn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-War_Orphan%27s_Sojourn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-War_Orphan%27s_Sojourn.jpg) The Temple of the Eight [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-place-Fisherman%27s_Isle.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fisherman%27s_Isle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fisherman%27s_Isle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fisherman%27s_Isle.jpg) Fisherman's Isle
- *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Bosmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Bosmer.md#Society)** sections on the Bosmer page.*

As stated previously, Haven is a major port in the Southern Sea. As a stop on the Lane, it finds ships coming from Auridon in the [Blue Divide](Lore_Blue_Divide.md), to Elsweyr and Khenarthi's Roost.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASP-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONHenriel-6)</sup> Smugglers import all sorts of contraband through Haven's docks, such as [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASP-3)</sup> and even [scuttlers](Lore_Scuttler.md) from distant [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MF1-18)</sup>

Fisherman's Isle is a camp on Haven's eastern beach with a magically-embued crafting site,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHaven-5)</sup> one of several found all over Tamriel. As theorized by the Khajiit adventurer, Pacrooti, they are created by master craftworkers, who at the height of their artistry, manifest their abilities and infuse them into their stations, so that any item crafted there would have the same quality as their original works.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MAMAPAYQ-19)</sup>

Local Bosmer had benefitted from the cultural diffusion with the Altmer. Haven and even [Port Velyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Port_Velyn) on the [Strident Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Broken_Coast) supplied the people with a reliable supply of metal weapons and Bosmer swordsmanship had evolved with introduced to Altmer advisors and techniques.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CAOV-20)</sup> When the High Elves arrived in Haven, they introduced a [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md) tradition called the War Orphan's Sojourn. They make a pilgrimage to the [Temple of the Eight](Lore_Divines.md) in the [Tarlain Heights](Lore_Tarlain_Heights.md), where they donate for the children.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONOrphan-21)</sup>
