Pelagius Septim II was the sixth emperor of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for around sixteen years, from [3E 82](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_82) to [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98). When he assumed the throne, the Empire was bankrupt and in debt, so he dismissed every member of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and allowed only those willing to pay great sums of money to sit on the council. Although controversial, this policy ultimately achieved Pelagius' goal of fixing the Empire's financial problems, and by the end of his reign, the Empire had returned to prosperity. He was preceded by his father, [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_II.md), and was succeeded by his son, [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup>

## Biography
Pelagius Septim was the son of [Uriel Septim](Lore_Uriel_II.md) (later Emperor Uriel II) and his wife.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWQ1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BWQ-3)</sup> He had a sister named [Galana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Galana_Septim) and a brother named [Amiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Septim).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWQ1-2)</sup> Circa [3E 52](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_52), Pelagius lost his first wife and remained a widower for eleven years.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWQ1-2)</sup> In the autumn of [3E 63](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_63), Pelagius journeyed to [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) to pay court to the Princess of Camlorn, [Quintilla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quintilla),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Wife)</sup> but when he arrived in the city, he found the kingdom was being terrorized by a werewolf. Working together, Pelagius and Quintilla defeated it and married.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWQ1-2)</sup>

Pelagius had one child with his first wife: Antiochus (b. [3E 51](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_51)), and three children with Quintilla: [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) (b. [3E 67](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_67)), [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) (b. [3E 77](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_77)), and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) (b. [3E 79](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_79)).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWQ1-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup> In [3E 80](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_80), Prince Pelagius and Uriel II arranged the marriage of Potema to [Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantiarco), King of [Solitude](Lore_Haafingar.md), to cement relations with Solitude.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWQ1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BWQ-3)</sup>

In [3E 82](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_82), Uriel II died, so Pelagius was crowned Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TET-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DC-6)</sup> When Pelagius II acceded to the [Ruby Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne), he faced a bankrupt treasury and debt from his father's poor management of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)'s financial policies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ2-7)</sup> To refill the [Imperial Treasury](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Treasury), Pelagius dismissed every member of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and allowed only those willing to pay great sums of money to sit on the council.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BWQ-3)</sup> He encouraged his vassals, the kings of Tamriel, to perform similar actions.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup> Ultimately, these policies refilled the Imperial Treasury, and by the end of Pelagius' reign, Tamriel had returned to prosperity.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup> His critics, however, suggested that the buyback system resulted in the damaging loss of those councilors who were wise but poor in gold.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup>

On 15 Evening Star, [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98), Pelagius II died, and Antiochus succeeded him.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Death)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DC-6)</sup> Many said that Pelagius II was poisoned by a former member of the Elder Council.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ3-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BWQ-3)</sup>
