# Kothringi

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/ON-npc-Sienna.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Sienna.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sienna.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sienna.jpg) A Kothringi The Kothringi (or **Lustrous Folk**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AVAYQ-1)</sup> are a silver-skinned<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MS2920-2)</sup> tribal people indigenous to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) who are believed to be descendants of the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLod-3)</sup> Once considered the only humans native to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), they are now known to have shared the continent with many other tribal groups.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLVR-4)</sup> The race was believed to have been driven to extinction by the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1Wild-5)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/ON-place-Zuuk.jpg/200px-ON-place-Zuuk.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Zuuk.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Zuuk.jpg) Zuuk [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Stillrise_Ritecaller.jpg) Stillrise Ritecallers The presence of the Kothringi in Black Marsh predates the most ancient records. They are believed to descend from the Nedic people.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLod-3)</sup> According to some sources, Kothringi culture started in the early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), or even before that time.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NOS-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOAN-7)</sup> In the ancient past Kothringi and [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of the [Hissmir](Lore_Hissmir.md) warred against each other. Argonians feared that Kothringi could use the power of Zaht stones against them. Little is known of the outcome of this conflict, but Hissmir remained an Argonian settlement.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTB-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Awas-9)</sup>

The Kothringi were thought to have been avid sailors, sailing as far as [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), possibly spreading the Knahaten Flu of [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560) to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and nearby provinces by ship. Kothringi shipbuilding is said to be very idiosyncratic with one example having unusual paddle placement, shallow draft, and a sail. Some vessels were capable of navigating both swamps and coastal waters due to this.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

The Kothringi were thought to be exterminated by the Knahaten Flu,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1Wild-5)</sup> although some of the infected fled on the [Crimson Ship](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md#Kothringi_and_the_Crimson_Ship) westward, away from Tamriel, never to be seen again.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TDC-11)</sup> However, legends tell of those who supposedly saw the ship and never lived to tell the tale.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Pot A-12)</sup>

Despite the widespread belief of the race's extinction, several Kothringi survived the Flu through unnatural means. In [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561), the chief of [Stillrise Village](Lore_Stillrise_Village.md), a Kothringi settlement in [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), entered into a desperate pact with [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) in order to survive the disease.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CSTTO-13)</sup> The [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) kept his end of the bargain, but did so by transforming the populace into immortal, undead [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CSTTO-13)</sup> The villagers maintained appearances through illusion magic, but their secret was discovered in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) when the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) attempted to enslave the undead inhabitants.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CSTTO-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TTO-14)</sup> The villagers successfully repelled the necromancers, but infighting broke out, and it is unknown if they chose to destroy the curse or continue living as skeletons.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TBE-15)</sup> In the nearby town of [Zuuk](Lore_Zuuk_(place).md), a lone Kothringi man named [Drillk](Lore_Drillk.md) became immune to the Flu by absorbing the power of a [keystone](Lore_Keystones.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOMorilye-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESODrillk-17)</sup> He became a person of interest to the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) and the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) in 2E 582, when both sides sought to acquire the keystone.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-KOD-18)</sup> An agent of the Ebonheart Pact rescued Drillk from the Dominion invaders, but the Kothringi revealed that taking the keystone's power would kill him.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESODrillk-17)</sup> It is unknown if the Pact soldier accepted his keystone or not.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ITT-19)</sup>

Some Kothringi are known to have survived the events of Stillrise Village by serving the [Ideal Masters](Lore_Ideal_Masters.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SR-20)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a member of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)'s Antiquarian Circle tracked down and discovered pieces of a magical Kothringi hunter's garment, putting it back together.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Item-21)</sup>

## Society and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Z%27en_Idol.jpg) Kothringi idol dedicated to Z'en [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/ON-item-Harpooner%27s_Wading_Kilt.jpg/200px-ON-item-Harpooner%27s_Wading_Kilt.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Harpooner%27s_Wading_Kilt.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Harpooner%27s_Wading_Kilt.jpg) Harpooner's Wading Kilt Little detail is known about the Kothringi. They generally preferred to go naked, although a few wore clothes when outside their native land.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MS2920-2)</sup> They appear to have worshipped a god named [Z'en](Lore_Z%27en.md) —a God of Toil—<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> as well as [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TGTG-23)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AVAYQ-1)</sup> [Mara](Lore_Mara.md), and [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-24)</sup> It was believed that at some point of the history Kothringi venerated [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), before shifting to Z'en worship.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOAN-7)</sup>

[Music](Lore_Music.md) was very important in Kothringi society. They were known to make instruments out of a variety of different materials, such as turning hollow logs into [wamasu-sized](Lore_Wamasu.md) [drums](Lore_Music.md#Drums), plucking sinew from [cliff striders](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md) to make low humming [bow harps](Lore_Music.md#Harps) and most notable of all, their [chimes](Lore_Music.md#Bells_And_Chimes) known to be the Kothringi's most loved instruments. A Kothringi by the name of Baelah molded hundreds of chimes from [tin](Lore_Tin.md) and [copper](Lore_Copper.md), hanging them from branches and striking them for their sounds. The Kothringi sometimes invited the [Saxhleel](Lore_Saxhleel.md) for feasts and celebrations where they played their instruments for the Argonian people.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-COS-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-26)</sup> It is believed that cultural exchange between Kothringi and Argonians took place.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NOS-6)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) came into possession of a Kothringi bugle created from the horn of a [leviathan](Lore_Swamp_Leviathan.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-KLH-27)</sup> Around the same time, the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) also stole a sacred Kothringi shovel, believed to be a blessed instrument of Z'en, from [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-KHL-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup> Kothringi Humming Dolls were toys that featured a clever arrangement of air bladders and reeds inside and hummed when squeezed.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Contraband Koth-30)</sup>

Some considered the Kothringi peerless crafters,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt-31)</sup> despite their distaste for clothing. They also occasionally crafted pieces of armor.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-32)</sup> They might've been capable of difficult crafting feats such as weaving strands of metal into cord in order to create stronger nautical rope.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-26)</sup> Kothringi spellcraft is considered unusual to the point that an experienced mage may miss its presence. Some Kothringi magic would possibly heighten the target's senses or reflexes.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-26)</sup>

Kothringi utilized [throwing knives](Lore_Throwing_Weapons.md), axes and harpoons in combat.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-26)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTB-8)</sup>

Portable Kothringi abacuses employed varnished sphere-scarabs as slide-beads.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Contrband K1-33)</sup> Kothringi artisans perfected a technique for weaving reeds of different colors and textures into decorative mats.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Contrband K2-34)</sup> Dream catchers crafted by Kothringi could be found around Tamriel during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Contrband K3-35)</sup>

Kothringi folklore mentions "geels-ha butcher eels", massive river predators capable of swallowing [haj mota](Lore_Haj_Mota.md) whole. They would obtain bog-blue jaspers from these eels to use in fetishes.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-26)</sup>

Kothringi knights made up a large part of the imperial garrison of [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md), and Empress [Tavia](Lore_Tavia.md), wife of [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md), the last ruler of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md), had a Kothringi knight named [Zuuk](Lore_Zuuk_(person).md) with whom she conspired to assassinate her husband.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MS2920-2)</sup> After Reman III was assassinated, "Lord" Zuuk returned to Shadowfen and founded a town which was eventually named after him.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TFOZ-36)</sup> The Kothringi buried their dead in cairns, often adorning these burial sites with glass beads. Some glass beads were also worn, serving as wearable instruments.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOWading Kilt Codex-26)</sup> Some residents of Gideon claim that Kothringi spirits linger in the town.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ESODexion-37)</sup> Fortune hunters sometimes poked around Gideon, searching for lost Kothringi treasure.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ESOMadarah-38)</sup>

Kothringi utilized [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) in their artwork and handicrafts, such as their statues.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Contraband Moonstone-39)</sup>
