# Ebony

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/SR-item-Ebony_Ingot.jpg/200px-SR-item-Ebony_Ingot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ebony_Ingot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Ebony_Ingot.jpg) Ebony Ingot [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/SR-activity-Mining_%28Ebony%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Ebony%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Ebony).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Ebony).jpg) Ebony Ore vein **Ebony** is a hard, durable, black glass-like substance that can be forged into high-quality armor and weapons. The primary ebony mine in Skyrim is [Gloombound Mine](Skyrim_Gloombound_Mine.md), in the [Orc Stronghold](Skyrim_Orc_Strongholds.md) of [Narzulbur](Skyrim_Narzulbur.md), while the primary ebony mine in [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> is [Raven Rock Mine](Skyrim_Raven_Rock_Mine.md). See [the lore article](Lore_Ebony.md) for general information.

- Three ebony ores are obtained by [mining](Skyrim_Mining.md) ebony ore veins.
- One ebony ingot is obtained by [smelting](Skyrim_Smelter.md) two ebony ores.
- Ebony Smithing requires 80 [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill.
- Ebony armor is level 32 [heavy armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor_(item).md), made from ebony ingots and [leather strips](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips).
- Ebony weapons are level 36 weapons, made from ebony ingots and leather strips.

## Mineral Sources
In addition to the following guaranteed sources, the best random locations are:

- Ebony ingots may be sold by blacksmiths starting at level 27 and general goods [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md) starting at level 14.
- Ebony ore may be randomly found in [Falmer](Skyrim_Falmer.md) loot starting at level 13.
- Ebony ore may sometimes be found in the ash pile remains of [ash spawn](Skyrim_Ash_Spawn.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> or [gargoyle](Skyrim_Gargoyle.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> corpses.
- Ebony ingots can rarely be found in chests.

These ore vein locations are also listed in the [Ebony Veins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Ebony_Veins) category, and the exterior ore veins are shown on [the interactive map](https://gamemap.uesp.net/sr/?search=Ebony+Ore+Vein).

## Ebony Smithing
Ebony Smithing is the fourth perk in the heavy armor branch of the [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill's perk tree. Unlocking it requires a minimum Smithing skill of 80, and the [Orcish Smithing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Smithing) perk must have already been unlocked. It allows both ebony armor and ebony weapons to be crafted.

The Ebony Smithing perk also causes tempering of ebony weapons and armor to be twice as effective. This perk applies to both unenchanted ebony gear and enchanted gear ([generic armor](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md), [generic weapons](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md), or [custom](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked. Tempering of the [Savior's Hide](Skyrim_Savior%27s_Hide.md) is also improved by Ebony Smithing, even though it does not use an ebony ingot.

With the addition of the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) add-on, Ebony Smithing is required in order to craft [stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md) weapons and armor. ([A New Source of Stalhrim](Skyrim_A_New_Source_of_Stalhrim.md) needs to be finished as well.)

### Ebony Armor
Ebony armor is a high-quality set of heavy armor. It appears in leveled lists starting at level 32 (enchanted varieties at level 33). All items can be [tempered](Skyrim_Workbench.md) using one ebony ingot, and having the Ebony Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's ebony gauntlets, a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 16 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

#### Bugs
- Ebony armor is unaffected by the Heavy Armor perk *[Matching Set](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md#Matching_Set)*. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.9, fixes this bug.
- In third person view, a male wearing ebony armor has an uncovered gap with visible skin around the neck area. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.0, fixes this bug.
- In third person view, the head of a male wearing an ebony helmet will not cast a shadow. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.2, fixes this bug.
- An ebony shield will be invisible if the person wearing it is a vampire. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.1130.0, fixes this bug.

### Ebony Weapons
Ebony [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) are generally the second-best standard weapons in the original version of the game, behind only [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric_Weapons.md) weapons. However, the ebony mace and ebony war axe are actually slightly better than their Daedric equivalents. These two weapons have identical damage statistics to the Daedric versions, but weigh less and do not require the use of a valuable [Daedra Heart](Skyrim_Daedra_Heart.md) to create, making them somewhat better overall, something changed by the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch). The [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on introduces [dragonbone weapons](Skyrim_Dragonbone.md), making ebony the third-best standard of weapon.

Ebony weapons appear in leveled lists starting at level 36 (enchanted varieties at level 37). All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using one ebony ingot, and having the Ebony Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement. Arrows can only be smithed if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed. Ebony Crossbows are only available with the [Elite Crossbows](Skyrim_Elite_Crossbows.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

The [Ghosts of the Tribunal](Skyrim_Ghosts_of_the_Tribunal.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds two new ebony weapons which can be crafted once you've killed the heretic during the [related quest](Skyrim_Ghosts_of_the_Tribunal_(quest).md).

## Notes
- Ebony ore can be sold to [Dushnamub](Skyrim_Dushnamub.md) for the quest [Mine Ore](Skyrim_Mine_Ore.md).
- Ebony ingots are needed to create and temper [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric.md) armor and weapons, and to create a random enchanted armor or weapon at the [Atronach Forge](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md). [Daedra Hearts](Skyrim_Daedra_Heart.md) are needed for creating them as well.
- The Daedric artifact [Ebony Mail](Skyrim_Ebony_Mail.md) Is considered part of the Ebony set after patch 1.9 with matching set perk working on it.
- Several artifacts and unique weapons are tempered using ebony ingots.
- Ore veins (all types) sometimes do not respawn normally. See [Mining (Bugs)](Skyrim_Mining.md#Bugs) and elsewhere on that article and its talk page.
- In real life, ebony is a type of hard, dark wood, traditionally used to make piano keys and other such items. This is in no way related to the ebony in the Elder Scrolls series, which is a volcanic glass. A closer real-world equivalent would be obsidian, though ebony's use in making armor and weapons suggest that it is far less brittle than obsidian.
