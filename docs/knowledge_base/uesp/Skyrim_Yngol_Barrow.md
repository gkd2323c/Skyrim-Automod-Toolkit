[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/29/SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg/200px-SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) Yngol Barrow **Yngol Barrow** is a small [Nordic ruin](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md) northeast of [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) containing the [shade](Skyrim_Yngol%27s_Shade.md) of an ancient Nord warrior. It contains only one zone, *[Yngol Barrow](#Yngol_Barrow)*.

## Related Quests
- **[Dungeon Delving](Skyrim_Dungeon_Delving_(Jarl_-_Caves).md)**: Do a favor for [Jarl Korir](Skyrim_Korir.md) by retrieving the [Helm of Winterhold](Skyrim_Helm_of_Winterhold.md). ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))

- This location is one of many potential targets for [one or more of the Radiant quests](Skyrim_Radiant.md#Quest_Locations).

## Walkthrough

### Exterior
The barrow is on the south bank of the [White River](Skyrim_White_River.md). There are several standing stones in front of the entrance. A short flight of stone steps leads up from the shore and through a squared arch to a statue, with twin piles of stones on either side and a circular stone dish in front holding sprigs of [snowberries](Skyrim_Snowberries.md), [foodstuffs](Skyrim_Food.md), and a copy of *[Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts](Skyrim_Yngol_and_the_Sea-Ghosts.md)*. Larger statues flank the entrance, which is marked by a lit brazier on a stand. An [iron ore vein](Skyrim_Iron_Ore_Vein.md) and a cluster of [slaughterfish eggs](Skyrim_Slaughterfish_Egg.md) are on a small island a few paces to the north.

### Yngol Barrow
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow.jpg) The puzzle room in Yngol Barrow The layout of the barrow is fairly linear: there are twists and turns, but the main path will not split. There are no enemies until you face [Yngol's Shade](Skyrim_Yngol%27s_Shade.md). Bouncing glowing blue orbs found throughout the barrow will follow you around as you explore. At no point do these orbs become hostile, and they disappear when you claim the [Helm of Yngol](Skyrim_Helm_of_Yngol.md).

Upon entering, you are in an ice tunnel descending to the north. At the bottom is a lit [torch](Skyrim_Torch.md) on the left. After a slight turn, you will see the first of the blue orbs. If you have a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md) with you, they will remark about finding an old ruin. Just after seeing the orb, there is an open sarcophagus on the right with a [dead draugr](Skyrim_Containers.md#Draugr) lying on the ground in a small section featuring typical Nordic barrow architecture. Continue following the icy passage as it twists and turns until there is a turn to the north, where ahead you will see two more upright sarcophagi; the sarcophagus to the right has a dead draugr behind the lid, with the passage onward to the right of it. The passage opens into a room with three rotating pillars in alcoves on either side and a closed gate ahead with a lever in front of it. The rotating stone puzzle is solved by reading *[Notes on Yngol Barrow](Skyrim_Notes_on_Yngol_Barrow.md)*, found on the [dead scholar](Skyrim_Dead_Scholar.md) in front of the gate. The notes include a translation describing three animals in their habitats. To your right, as you face the locked gate, you will find an alcove with a throne, and an alcove with running water: the pillar in this alcove must be turned to the **[Whale](Skyrim_Puzzle_Totems.md#Whale)** symbol. On the opposite side, there is an alcove covered in weeds and plants: its pillar must be turned to the **[Snake](Skyrim_Puzzle_Totems.md#Snake)** symbol. The pillar in the last alcove, with sunlight shining through, must be turned to the **[Eagle](Skyrim_Puzzle_Totems.md#Eagle)** symbol. The throne opposite the sunlit pillar requires no action on your part. Pull the lever once you have positioned the pillars correctly to continue.

Beyond the gate are some catacombs, with two large [urns](Skyrim_Urn.md) opposite behind a pedestal that may hold the [coral dragon claw](Skyrim_Coral_Dragon_Claw.md) (see also below). The passage turns to the north and becomes an icy tunnel, which passes through some shallow water before descending and entering a natural cavern with a bridge crossing a pool. Underwater beneath the middle arch of the bridge is an unlocked [chest](Skyrim_Chest.md) and a [burial urn](Skyrim_Burial_Urn.md). Across the bridge is an iron door, with flights of stairs leading down behind. At the bottom is a gate with a lit brazier behind. There are several [hanging moss](Skyrim_Hanging_Moss.md), a dead draugr in an alcove, and two burial urns here. The passage turns to the southeast, then to the northeast. Through another iron door is a wider passage with a passage off to the left. Ahead is an adept-locked gate, behind which is an alcove containing an unlocked wooden chest, three burial urns, a [steel greatsword](Skyrim_Steel_Greatsword.md) (which may be hard to see behind the chest), and a random pair of [boots](Skyrim_Armor.md#Boots). There is also a [salt pile](Skyrim_Salt_Pile.md) in front of the chest that is tricky to interact with, and more hanging moss growing down the alcove's back wall.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/80/SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_02.jpg) Bouncing glowing orbs at the puzzle door Following the passage to the left leads to a small room with a random [greatsword](Skyrim_Greatsword.md) hanging in the air above fifteen sprigs of snowberries. The passage continues to the northeast. Near the end is a [pressure plate](Skyrim_Pressure_Plate.md) that triggers a [spike wall](Skyrim_Spike_Wall.md) to slam down from above in the doorway; moving slowly avoids damage. The doorway is at the top of wide stairs down into a room that has suffered quite a bit of damage. There is an overturned unlocked chest and a dead draugr among the rubble, while opposite are several upright sarcophagi covered with thick cobwebs with a [dessicated corpse](Skyrim_Dessicated_Corpse.md) on the floor in front. There is an open door on the right-hand side of the room leads to a passage with a [puzzle door](Skyrim_Traps.md#Puzzle_Door) at the far end. The combination is found on the coral dragon claw, which you may find on a pedestal earlier in the barrow, or by visiting [Birna](Skyrim_Birna.md) in [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold.md). The correct combination for the puzzle door is (starting from the outer ring): **[Snake—Wolf—Moth](Skyrim_Puzzle_Totems.md#Totem_Symbols)**. There are no negative consequences for incorrect guesses.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_03.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Yngol_Barrow_03.jpg) Yngol's Shade guarding the Helm of Yngol Behind the puzzle door is a tomb with a throne. Yngol's Shade, in the form of a ghost-like draugr, will animate and attack you once you step in the room. Depending on whether the quest [Dungeon Delving](Skyrim_Dungeon_Delving_(Jarl_-_Caves).md) is active (see [notes](#Notes)), this encounter may lead you to obtain either the [Helm of Yngol](Skyrim_Helm_of_Yngol.md) or the [Helm of Winterhold](Skyrim_Helm_of_Winterhold.md). The throne is in the center of the room facing away from you, with an unlocked chest behind it, there's a two-handed skill book *[Song Of Hrormir](Skyrim_Song_Of_Hrormir.md)* in the boss chest. A random two-handed weapon is on the floor to the left. There is a dead draugr in an alcove on the northeastern side of the room. Beyond the throne, there is a gate that rises when you take the helm. Through the doorway is a second unlocked chest at the base of spiral wooden stairs that provide a quick shortcut to an unmarked exit. You exit to the northeast of the entrance, due south of [Wreck of the Winter War](Skyrim_Wreck_of_the_Winter_War.md).

## Notes
- Although Birna is a shopkeeper, she will sell the coral dragon claw for 50 gold through a separate dialogue option.
- If you're in Yngol Barrow and not doing the quest [Yngol's Barrow](Skyrim_Yngol%27s_Barrow.md), you will not be able to get out through the back gate, and will have to retrace your steps (this doesn't seem to apply to *Skyrim Special Edition*). This appears to be intentional. To access the chest, enter the barrow through the alternate exit, which is not marked on your map. Turn right from the main entrance and follow the shoreline north until you come to two logs washed up on shore. Turn right and head toward the cliffs until you reach a small bay with a large rock formation on your right; some of the rocks in this formation have horizontal lines carved on them. Head toward this formation, and you'll find the alternate exit.
- If you're having trouble against the draugr boss, you can cheese the fight by first taking the alternate exit described in the note above. Go down the stairs to find the chest and the locked gate. You will not be able to open the gate from this side, but you can kill the boss through it with ranged attacks, such as arrows or spells, and you'll be safe from his attacks.
- Once you've offered to buy the coral dragon claw from Birna (even if you didn't actually do so), the claw on the pedestal in Yngol Barrow will disappear.
- The button on the pedestal in the throne room does nothing.
- Version 2.1.1 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) assigns Yngol Barrow to [Eastmarch](Skyrim_Eastmarch.md) instead.

## Bugs
- If the puzzle door does not work, check [here](Skyrim_Puzzle_Totems.md#Bugs) for possible fixes.
- The coral dragon claw may be completely missing from the pedestal, and other items may have wedged themselves into the cave's geometry. - Simply go to [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold.md) and buy the other claw from [Birna](Skyrim_Birna.md) if this happens. If you have killed Birna, the quest cannot be completed.
- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) You can open the [console](Skyrim_Console.md) and type `player.additem 000B634C 1` to spawn a copy of the claw in your inventory.
- Entering Yngol Barrow with a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md) may cause a glitch whereby the cave is pitch black and neither the Character Menu nor Journal buttons work. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.1, fixes this bug.

- If this is the case, exit the cave and leave your follower outside.
- Entering Yngol Barrow may cause a glitch whereby the cave is pitch black. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.1, fixes this bug.

- Create a new save inside the cave and reload it.
- Reloading after entering Yngol Barrow can cause the screen to go black. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Even after clearing the dungeon and collecting the helm, the gate to the exit doesn't open. - [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) You can clip through the gate using the console command `tcl` or remove the gate using the console command `"2e22c".disable`.
- If the radiant AI has placed another quest or quest item in the dungeon, even if it is not in your journal (for example, if the Helm of Winterhold or Meridia's Beacon is in the boss chest), a leveled generic (unnamed) draugr may appear rather than Yngol. If so, the Helm of Yngol will be unavailable, and the grated door will not open. - [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) On PC, you may use the console to grant yourself a copy of the Helm of Yngol with the command `player.additem 295f3 1`. You can then return the way you came, bypass the grates by turning off clipping or disabling the gate, as described above.
