[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/SR-place-Morthal.jpg/200px-SR-place-Morthal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Morthal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Morthal.jpg) Morthal circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/ON-place-Morthal.jpg/200px-ON-place-Morthal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Morthal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Morthal.jpg) Morthal circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Morthal, supposedly named after the great Nord hero [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loading Screens-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Banner-2)</sup> is the capital of [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md). It is a fairly small, humble town of little economic or strategic importance besides its small lumber mill.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-THOS-3)</sup> The town's symbol is a white [triskelion](Lore_Triskelion.md) on a field of gray reminiscent of its misty marshes. The triskelion's meaning, whether it represents the harmony of the mind, body, and spirit, or another concept entirely, was a topic of scholarly discussion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Banner-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MBH-4)</sup> Tattoos featuring the Morthal triskelion were reserved solely for individuals who have valiantly defended Morthal and its inhabitants.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MCBM-5)</sup>

Sitting on the southern edge of the [Drajkmyr marsh](Lore_Drajkmyr.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> dry ground can be hard to come by in Morthal. It is often shrouded in a thick fog. Ominous twisted trees grow from the surrounding marsh, giving it a foreboding appearance to approaching travelers. The torches of the town glowing in the fog often give the impression of wisps at a distance. Many choose to steer clear of the place altogether.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

Morthal's small population and relatively remote location have kept it distant from most major conflicts in its history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Loading Screens-1)</sup> The citizens of Morthal are known to be reclusive and cautious of outsiders. Many alchemists travel to Morthal and its surrounding marshes to make use of the plethora of rare and useful ingredients available. Bull Mastiffs from Morthal are fearless and tireless hunters inured to the hardships of the frozen marshes, and are owned as pets by many across the land.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONMorthal Mastiff-8)</sup>

Residents of Morthal have passed down the legend of the Pale Lady for untold years.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LL-9)</sup> They tell of a dangerous ghost woman who prowls the marshes to the north of town seeking her lost daughter, who is thought to be linked to "Aumriel" (mentioned in ancient records as a foe defeated by the descendants of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LL-9)</sup> As the legend goes, the Pale Lady steals daughters because she lost her own. Some believe that if they carve wooden statues beautiful enough, she might return their lost children to them.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Skalferth-10)</sup>
