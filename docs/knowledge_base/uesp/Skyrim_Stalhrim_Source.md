## Stalhrim Source
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-place-Stalhrim_Source_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Stalhrim_Source_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Stalhrim_Source_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Stalhrim_Source_02.jpg) The Stalhrim Source The only relatively even approach to the area is from the west through a gap in the mountains reached by passing over the rocks west of [White Ridge Barrow](Skyrim_White_Ridge_Barrow.md) and proceeding north and east, though you may also approach by descending the mountains from other directions. A stone fence runs along the top of the sunken circular ruin, with a gap in the fence on the west side allowing access to the curved stairs. Descending the stairs reveals ten sarcophagi frozen over with [stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md). Three sarcophagi are to the north, three are to the south, two are to the west partly buried in snow, and two are in the center. The eight outer sarcophagi are upright, while the two in the center are horizontal. After you've mined the stalhrim from each of the sarcophagi, you can loot the uncovered [draugr corpses](Skyrim_Containers.md#Draugr). One sarcophagus is half-buried in the snow and only the draugr head is visible, but you can still loot it. If [No Stone Unturned](Skyrim_No_Stone_Unturned.md) is finished, each draugr will have up to 5 gems, usually of flawless quality.

## Notes
- The map marker for this location does not appear until after you have read the [Stalhrim Source Map](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stalhrim_Source_Map) obtained during the related quest. If you do not read the map before returning it to Baldor, the map marker will be added upon completing the quest.
- If [Deor Woodcutter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Deor_Woodcutter) or [Fanari Strong-Voice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fanari_Strong-Voice) are killed prior to completing the related quest, you will never be able to learn to forge stalhrim yourself. In addition, you will have to discover the Stalhrim Source's location on your own.
- If you attempt to mine the Stalhrim deposits here with only a regular pickaxe in your inventory, a "You need an Ancient Nordic Pickaxe to use this." message will appear on the top left of the screen.
- If you choose to mine the Stalhrim deposits here manually, you can use the [Elemental Fury](Skyrim_Elemental_Fury.md) shout to mine them faster. Don't use an enchanted Ancient Nordic Pickaxe, though, or the shout's speed bonus won't work.

## Bugs
- You may be unable to mine the stalhrim deposits after they respawn. They will appear icy, but will only offer the option to "search draugr". <sup>**?**</sup> - Fast traveling away from the cell and then returning should enable the deposits, allowing you to mine them normally.
- Save, exit to the title screen, and reload. You should be able to mine the deposits again.
- Equip your Ancient Nordic Pickaxe and swing at the deposits manually.
- Fast travel to anywhere on Solstheim, then reload. The deposits should be mineable once again.
- [![On Play Station](https://images.uesp.net/6/64/Playstation.svg)](Skyrim_PlayStation.md) On [PSVR](Skyrim_Skyrim_VR.md), you may need to strike or mine the deposits more often than is necessary in other versions of the game, a few dozen times per ore.
