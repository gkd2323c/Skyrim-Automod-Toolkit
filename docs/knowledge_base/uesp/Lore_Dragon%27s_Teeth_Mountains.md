[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg/200px-AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg) Fang Lair in the Dragon's Teeth Mountains [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/87/ON-interior-Fang_Lair.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Fang_Lair.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Fang_Lair.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Fang_Lair.jpg) Fang Lair's mining caves The Dragon's Teeth Mountains (or simply the **Dragon's Teeth**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARExclamations-1)</sup> is an expansive mountain range that spans a large portion of western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), stretching from the frozen summits of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) all the way to the forests of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), passing through [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). It was said that the spines of the Dragon's Teeth were visible from the top of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) prior to the latter's destruction<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARRia-2)</sup> and [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) was south of it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARFalinesti-3)</sup> The [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md) in Skyrim, the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains) in Hammerfell, and the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) in Cyrodiil appear to all form parts of the Dragon's Teeth.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-4)</sup> "By the Dragon's Teeth" is a common exclamation in Hammerfell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARExclamations-1)</sup>

The Dragon's Teeth Mountains are found near the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) town of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md). The mountains have been described as a wild and tricky country and an overall treacherous and inhospitable landscape, known for winding chasms and hidden valleys. It is known to carry deposits of [mithril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mithril) and [gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold), which were the main resources that the [Kragen](Lore_Kragen.md) clan of [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) harvested through their extravagant mining colony of [Arkngthamz-Phng](Lore_Arkngthamz-Phng.md) (later known as Fang Lair). It is unknown why the Kragen Clan migrated into the Dragon's Teeth Mountains when they had a well-established empire from their research center of [Arkngthamz](Lore_Arkngthamz.md) in Skyrim, where they harvested [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md). They lived in the Arkngthamz-Phng colony for an unspecified amount of years until they were driven out by the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Thurvokun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thurvokun). He rested in the ruins of Fang Lair until his death,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AP-5)</sup> protecting an immeasurable amount of gold, which prompted numerous dungeon delvers to traverse the mountains and seek out the treasure.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KEVII-6)</sup> The area surrounding Fang Lair is completely rife with the beautiful, albeit deadly wildflower, [Dragon's Tongue Plant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon%27s_Tongue_Plant).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TDC-7)</sup><sup>:127</sup>

## Notes
- Unused resource data from *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* includes the **Dragontooth Mountains** rather than the Dragon's Teeth Mountains, perhaps a change meant for uniformity with the nearby [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains). [*The Daggerfall Chronicles*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles) timeline makes mention of the [Battle of Dragontooth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Dragontooth), which may mean this event was intended take place in the Dragon's Teeth.
- In *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, the Dragon's Teeth Mountains originally cut through much of the western part of the Imperial Province. In *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*, this detail had been largely erased from Cyrodiil, with only a few hills and plateaus found in the spot of the mountains' original range. In *[ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*, the mountain range has once again been restored, with much of it making up the boundaries of the [West Weald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:West_Weald) and travelling down to [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-4)</sup>
- The in-game book *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era)* indicates that the Wrothgarian range was intended to be continuous from High Rock down into Cyrodiil, with the hamlet of Wroth Naga lying in Cyrodiil atop the mountains overlooking the lands of High Rock in the west. The out-of-game writer of that book, [Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson), stated that it was certainly his intent for Wroth Naga to be placed in Cyrodiil, but the line about it overlooking High Rock was written in error.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-2920:TLY-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPPosts S-9)</sup>
