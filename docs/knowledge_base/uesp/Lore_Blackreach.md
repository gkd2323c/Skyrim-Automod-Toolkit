[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/SR-place-Blackreach.jpg/200px-SR-place-Blackreach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Blackreach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Blackreach.jpg) Blackreach (Skyrim) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/ON-place-Blackreach_%28The_Coven_Conundrum%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Blackreach_%28The_Coven_Conundrum%29.jpg)](File_ON-place-Blackreach_(The_Coven_Conundrum).jpg.md) [](File_ON-place-Blackreach_(The_Coven_Conundrum).jpg.md) Blackreach (ESO) Blackreach ([Dwemer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemeris): *Fal'Zhardum Din*, "Blackest Kingdom Reaches")<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DI3-1)</sup> is an immense cavern located deep beneath [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), and possibly extending across all Nine Holds within [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Lyris-2)</sup> Many giant glowing mushrooms grow throughout the cavern, providing natural light. The cavern is rich in minerals, including rare geode veins, and a subterranean river flows through it. Originally settled by the first [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) clans and later the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), Blackreach once served as a highway connecting several of the Dwemeri cities found within Skyrim. Various inhabitants can be found within Blackreach, these include [Dwemer Animunculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi), [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), Vampires, [Giants](Lore_Giant.md), [Frost Trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Troll), and [Wispmothers](Lore_Wispmother.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IOBIS.26ESO-3)</sup>

## History

### Early Eras
Sometime between the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FC-4)</sup> and the second century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the first vampire clans would emerge and establish their own kingdoms within Blackreach.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Gwendis TDH-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Eso Verandi-6)</sup> One of these clans was the [Nighthollow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nighthollow_Clan), who discovered an artifact known as the [Dark Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart). They become reliant on its [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) energy for sustenance, and consequently became unable to gain nourishment through blood.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Eso Verandi-6)</sup> They ruled the Reach above from the depths of Blackreach, and were dubbed the Night Lords by the ancestors of modern [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachman.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RBS-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/SR-place-Great_Lift_at_Raldbthar.jpg/200px-SR-place-Great_Lift_at_Raldbthar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Great_Lift_at_Raldbthar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Great_Lift_at_Raldbthar.jpg) Great lift at Raldbthar (Skyrim) When the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) settled in Blackreach, they built Great Lifts to provide quick transport to the surface. [Alftand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alftand), [Mzinchaleft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mzinchaleft) and [Raldbthar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Raldbthar) were all connected thusly to a great Blackreach cavern under Winterhold and the Pale, that also contained the [Tower of Mzark](Skyrim_Tower_of_Mzark.md). [Irkngthand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Irkngthand) may also have contained an entrance, but it was no longer accessible as of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DI3-1)</sup> The Dwemer constructed roads and buildings, culminating in the Silent City in the center of Blackreach. Dwarven Centurions were installed to guard the cavern's roads. The Dwemer farmed mushrooms in the damp environment. The Tower of Mzark, located in Blackreach, contained a device used to transfer the knowledge of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) onto a lexicon. Blackreach also acted as the lair of the dragon [Vulthuryol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vulthuryol).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-8)</sup> Further to the west, there were separate caverns in Blackreach known as Greymoor Cavern and [Arkthzand Cavern](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Blackreach__Arkthzand_Cavern), underneath Haafingar, Hjaalmarch and the Reach, also connected to the surface via Great Lifts and Dwemer citadels (and an entrance apparently discovered or made later from the surface by miners, through the caves of [Gloomreach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Gloomreach).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/ON-place-Arkthzand_Orrery.jpg/200px-ON-place-Arkthzand_Orrery.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arkthzand_Orrery.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arkthzand_Orrery.jpg) Arkthzand Orrery (ESO) Sometime after the death of [King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) in [1E 221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221), the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city-states of [Arkngthamz](Lore_Arkngthamz.md), [Bthar-Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthar-Zel), [Mzulft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mzulft), and [Raldbthar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Raldbthar),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONTAW-9)</sup> (and possibly [Nchuand-Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nchuand-Zel))<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLA-10)</sup> formed an alliance to oversee the extraction, processing, and study of the arcane mineral known as [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONTAW-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLA-10)</sup> This alliance eventually discovered the Dark Heart, and stole it from the Nighthollow Clan.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOIdrisin-11)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Eso Verandi-6)</sup> They created various apparatuses to study and control the heart's power. They opted to create the Library of Arkthzand, a place to share knowledge among the clans,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLA-10)</sup> and built an orrery within it to chart the Void using cosmic forces (aetheric, kinetic, and [tonal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tonal_Architecture)), using the Dark Heart.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Eso Verandi-6)</sup> Eventually, internal disputes would occur over Aetherium, and the precious material would lend its name to the Aetherium Wars, which led to the collapse of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s Dwarven city-states,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLA-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONTAW-9)</sup> and resulted in the Library of Arkthzand's abandonment.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONLA-10)</sup> Although the Dark Heart lay dormant, empty pockets known as void portals would continue to form around the library.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOTLA-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOVP-13)</sup>

In the early First Era, the Dwemer took in the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) that were seeking refuge from the invading [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) after the Snow Prince was defeated. The Dwemer fed the Snow Elves fungi that grew in the caverns which rendered the Snow Elves blind. The Dwemer would later betray the Snow Elves by using them as slaves. The Snow Elves rebelled, overthrowing their Dwarven masters, and fleeing to the darkest corners of Blackreach. The cavern became the stage for the [War of the Crag](Lore_War_of_the_Crag.md), fought between the Dwemer and Falmer for many decades. When the Dwemer disappeared from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), the devolved [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) were free to spread throughout Blackreach.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TFAS-14)</sup> Over the centuries, the Falmer came to inhabit the abandoned halls of their former masters. By taming the [Chaurus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chaurus), they were able to use its chitin to create primitive camps, many of which were set up in Blackreach.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-8)</sup>

Sometime in the First Era, [Rada al-Saran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rada_al-Saran) would come across the Dark Heart beneath the Reach.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Tzinghal-15)</sup> After the defeat of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) at the hands of [Saint Pelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelin), Rada made a deal with Lady Belain, the Matriarch of the Nighthollow Clan, who was seeking to restore the Dark Heart using the Prophecy of the Dark Heart and revitalize her clan. Rada ended up asking her to wait to enact the Prophecy as he first needed to find the means of restoring the Gray Host, and finding the means to access the Arkthzand Orrery.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSOS-16)</sup>

### Second Era
The cavern remained largely unknown to the surface-dwelling people of Tamriel outside of legends<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Lyris-2)</sup> and scholarly texts. During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Crimson Nirnroot](Lore_Crimson_Nirnroot.md) from Blackreach was prized for its rarity and its use in alchemy.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> A [Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#First_Dominion) scout named Vole fell down a shaft and ended up in Blackreach during the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md). He managed to find a chaurus and ate it, but unknowingly consumed a [soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Gems) in the process and gained an innate ability to tinker with machinery, fixing one of the great lifts and leaving the ruins.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONVole-18)</sup> The Icereach Coven would later use Blackreach - a part of the Mzark Cavern that reached as far east as Eastmarch - to experiment on unwilling test subjects by luring them with fliers to the Grinning Horker Inn, though their plot was thwarted by agents of King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TCC-19)</sup>

The existence of Blackreach was somewhat known to some residents of Western Skyrim by the year [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Bitterblade Mining Consortium had set up a small mining settlement known as [Dusktown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dusktown) within the depths of Blackreach.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Urlvar-20)</sup> An area near the Dusktown Caverns, known as Dark Moon Grotto was settled by [werewolf](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) refugees who had escaped the confines of [Greymoor Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greymoor_Keep).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Tranya-21)</sup> Greymoor Keep at the time, was controlled by the Gray Host, the vampire [Essenia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Essenia) who originally controlled Greymoor Keep willingly relinquished her rule of Greymoor Keep to Rada al-Saran, allowing her to focus more on research and experimentations. One of these experiments she conducted involved mixing together various vampiric bloodlines as part of the process of creating her own [bloodknights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodknight).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Gwendis TLOB-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-RNCV-23)</sup> Greymoor Keep would be converted into a massive Witchpike by the Gray Host as part of Rada's efforts of unleashing a massive Harrowstorm on the city of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-FGR-24)</sup>

After being discovered as an ally of Rada by [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and [House Ravenwatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Ravenwatch), Lady Belain hurried to rally the Ghostsong Clan to enact the Prophecy of the Dark Heart. Those who deserted the clan were quickly killed, while the willing were sacrificed to the Dark Heart.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOAD-25)</sup> House Ravenwatch and the Vestige would later assault Nighthollow Keep under Blackreach, and discover the Heart deep within the keep's depths, as well as the ongoing ritual to fully reawaken it. It was too late, and the Heart was reawakened, and resurrecting the Nighthollow clan from its ashes.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOTDJ-26)</sup> Lady Belain later unsealed the doors of Nchuand-Zel after an assault on [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), catching those at Understone Keep off guard and almost killing Ard Caddach. The Vestige followed Arana into the Nchuand-Zel, and recovered the keystone within the ruin's depths to gain access to the Orrery of Arkthzand, which al-Saran required to harvest the Heart's power to its full potential. There, Lady Belain teleported the intruder's to the Dark Heart's chambers, and bathed in its power. Despite this, [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) intervened through Arana, and gave her the strength to press on despite her wounds. Together, the duo slew Belain, striking another blow against Rada, though Arana succumbed to her wounds after the battle.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOAFOS-27)</sup>

The Dark Heart remained a lingering threat, as it was awakened but not at full capacity, and Arkthzand's orrery remained charged with Void energy. With the heart's newfound power quickly draining, time was of the essence for Rada, and he conducted a meeting with some of the other leaders of the Gray Host north of Markarth, near the Druadach Mountains. Although Belain was gone, she managed to buy time for Rada to tie the heart's energy to the Gray Host, further laying down the foundations for his plans.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONGVerandis-28)</sup> After intercepting a letter at a Gray Host meeting place, the Vestige learned of Rada's intent. The letter was written as a final act of desperation, and written on it was Rada's plea for [Verandis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Verandis_Ravenwatch) to rejoin his Gray Host, and House Ravenwatch would come to realize Rada's plan in full. Rada had opened a gateway into his sanctuary realm of Grayhaven, using the Orrery as a map, and the Dark Heart as a power source and gateway for this endeavor. After Rada's defeat at the hands of the Vestige within Grayhaven, Verandis remained behind in Blackreach to safeguard the Dark Heart and to make sure it could never be used again.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONGVerandis-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOTEE-29)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/ON-item-Crimson_Nirnroot.jpg/150px-ON-item-Crimson_Nirnroot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Crimson_Nirnroot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Crimson_Nirnroot.jpg) A Crimson Nirnroot By this time, the Great Lifts and the other known entrances to the Greymoor and Arkthzand Caverns of Blackreach under Haafingar, Hjaalmarch and the Reach had been lost or destroyed: it is not known whether even the caverns themselves still existed. Entrances to the Mzark cavern further to the east still existed: the furthest east entrances through the three Dwemer ruins of Alftand, Raldbthar and Mzinchaleft in the holds of Winterhold and The Pale and the Tower of Mzark itself, and through Great Lifts near each cavern - though all the lifts were either buried in the deepest ends of the Dwemer ruins and required an Attunement Sphere to unlock, or were locked from the inside at surface level by a lever mechanism that was simple enough to use, but could only be accessed by taking the lift upwards from Blackreach.

In [4E 58](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_58), the master alchemist [Sinderion](Lore_Sinderion.md) somehow acquired an attunement sphere, traversed a Dwarven ruin (almost certainly Alftand), and arrived in Blackreach near its Alftand entrance to study the Crimson Nirnroot, having been directed there by an explorer named Obeth Arnesian. The Crimson Nirnroot was a variant of the common [Nirnroot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirnroot) found exclusively in Blackreach, where it grows symbiotically with the cavern's mushrooms.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SFJ-30)</sup> Sinderion set up a field laboratory in one of the abandoned Dwemer houses, where he was eventually killed by one of the still-functional Dwemer animunculi.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the scholar [Septimus Signus](Lore_Septimus_Signus.md) sent the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) to Blackreach on a quest to retrieve the Dwemer's [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup> The Dragonborn also uncovered Sinderion's remains, and by collecting several samples of Crimson Nirnroot, was able to continue with Sinderion's research: which the Dragonborn later passed on to one of Sinderion's more talented apprentices, [Avrusa Sarethi](Skyrim_Avrusa_Sarethi.md), who managed a small farm near Riften. Arethi had learned the technique of farming, cultivating and harvesting Nirnroot. As a result of learning from Sinderion's notes, she was able to apply the same principles to Crimson Nirnroot, and grew the first Crimson Nirnroot on the surface of Tamriel.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup> Through use of the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), the Dragonborn awoke Vulthuryol. The two did battle, and the Dragonborn absorbed the dragon's soul.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup>

## Flora and Fauna
[Netherroot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flora_N#Netherroot) is a plant that can only grow in isolated areas within Blackreach, these netherroots roots are a key alchemical component in the creation of Harrowstorms.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-NTN-35)</sup> Toxic fungi were also once found within Blackreach, these mushrooms were used as part of the process used to blind the Snow Elves by the Dwemer.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TFAS-14)</sup> Various forms of fauna live in Blackreach, these include [vale deer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vale_Deer), chaurus, [frostbite spiders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostbite_Spider), [durzogs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durzog), [trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll), [winter moths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moth), [shroom beetles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shroom_Beetle), [torchbugs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torchbug) [nixads](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nixad) and [cockroaches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Roach). It is the only place [Gloomspore creatures](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bestiary_G#Gloomspore_Creature) such as [bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear), [wolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolf), and [senche](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-Cat) can be found in the wild. Blackreach Jellies, a type of [Swamp Jelly](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Jelly), are also found within the caverns of Blackreach.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Cli BR-36)</sup>
