[3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Indoranyon ([Dark Elvish](Lore_Dark_Elvish.md) for *"Blessed Greenland"*)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMP-1)</sup><sup>:39</sup> is a stronghold located on the coast of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The fort is one of the most notable landmarks on that stretch of the coast.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWThaeril-2)</sup> Indoranyon is mentioned in the historical-fiction book, *[Poison Song](Lore_Poison_Song.md)*, written by Bristin Xel.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PSVI-3)</sup> The ruin's [propylon chamber](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) links to Falensarano and [Rotheran](Lore_Rotheran.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWIndoranyon-4)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Indoranyon was in complete ruin, overgrown with vegetation. The [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) magister, Firuth scouted the ruins as a possible place to build his tower. He rejected Indoranyon for a plot of land outside of [Vos](Lore_Vos.md), in exchange for an endorsement for [Sun-in-Shadow](Lore_Sun-in-Shadow.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONRetainer-5)</sup>

At some point in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the ruins were the haunt of a rogue battlemage named Egroamaro. He was later tracked by the adventurer, Oristian Silverthorn. He fought through the mage's underlings until he finally confronted Egroamaro. Before they fought, Silverthorn used the [illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) spell called Silence to prevent the mage from casting spells and promptly struck him down.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Sil-6)</sup>

During the [Vvardenfell Crisis](Lore_Vvardenfell_Crisis.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Indoranyon was occupied by a cult of [Dagonites](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWSecrets-7)</sup> and mages who have [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) the ruins.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWTelvon-8)</sup> Two of these enchanters were a master, Qorwynn and his apprentice, Erundil. The latter possessed the Ring of Sanguine Unseen Wisdom, one of the twenty-six pieces to the [Threads of the Webspinner](Lore_Threads_of_the_Webspinner.md) that were later collected by the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWWebspinner-9)</sup>
