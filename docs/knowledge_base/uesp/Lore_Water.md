# Elements

The elements are the basic constituents of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). The ancient [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) believed Nirn was composed of four elements: Earth, Water, Air, and Light.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mft S-1)</sup> The element of Fire was not recognized by their culture, although modern [mages](Lore_Magic.md) consider Fire to be one of the five elements of creation.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TOTSN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SIRelmyna-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Practitioners of [Flesh Magic](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md) believe that Flesh is the sixth element.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SIRelmyna-3)</sup>

## Air
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/ON-trap-Tornado.jpg/200px-ON-trap-Tornado.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trap-Tornado.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trap-Tornado.jpg) A small tornado **Air** (known as **Su** in the [Dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md))<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRElemental-5)</sup> is one of the basic elements of creation. [Air Atronachs](Lore_Air_Atronach.md) are a type of [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) formed from pure air. They are believed to possess the combined powers of [Flame Atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md), [Frost Atronachs](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md), and [Storm Atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

The wind is a recurring and fundamental aspect of Nordic tradition and culture. It goes as far back as one of their ancient origin stories when the sky breathed down on the land, and from there, created the [Nords](Lore_Nords.md). Hence why both [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and its [largest mountain](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) near its center are called the "Throat of the World".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-COTS-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1SR-8)</sup> As Nords live closer to the northern wastes, the more powerful and elemental they become. It is said that Nords that live in the far wastes always carry a wind with them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-COTS-7)</sup> The winds are also seen as [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md)'s method of expressing her "celestial mourning" of the death of her lover, [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) and something that mortals benefit from.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WAS-9)</sup>

Similarly, the winds that blow through the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) help spread and guide the magical essence embedded in each grain of sand. For an Alik'ri wizard, the next stage of magical awakening would be scrying the appropriate wind channels and carving novel paths to further diversify the land's memory.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WAS-9)</sup> Wind spirits supposedly used to live deep in the Alik'r Desert and terrorize people in the coastal settlements.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3HF-10)</sup>

The [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md) worship a trapped [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) known as the Guardian of the Air, an ancient being that represents the element in its true form.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> In [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) culture, [Ilni](Lore_Ilni.md) represents the Air element.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tot E-12)</sup> [Unala-Se](Lore_Unala-Se.md) is a [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) associated with Air.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TNC1-13)</sup>

## Earth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Earth.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Earth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Earth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Earth.jpg) An Earth Ehlnofey **Earth** (known as **Gol** in the Dragon language)<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRBend-14)</sup> is one of the basic elements of creation.

In Tsaesci culture, [Nyfa](Lore_Nyfa.md) represents the Earth element.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tot E-12)</sup> [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md) is a Magna Ge associated with Earth.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TNC1-13)</sup> The [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md) worship a trapped [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) known as the Guardian of the Earth, an ancient being that represents the element in its true form.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

## Fire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png/200px-LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png) Fire Magic **Fire** (known as **Yol** in the Dragon language)<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SRFire-16)</sup> is the fifth element of creation. It was not recognized by the ancient Ayleids, who preferred to associate it with the element of Light.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mft S-1)</sup> Practitioners of the School of [Destruction](Lore_Destruction.md) utilize elemental fire magic to create offensive spells.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Certain fires have been known to possess magical connections to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), such as the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> the Direfrost Flame,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> and the Flame of the Harbinger.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> There is a mythical figure in Nordic legend known as Thorgrima, the Keeper of Crimson Fire and the Lord of Magicka.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRWW-21)</sup>

[Fire spirits](Lore_Fire_Spirit.md) supposedly used to live deep in the Alik'r Desert and terrorize people in the coastal settlements.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3HF-10)</sup> The [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) poet [Weltan](Lore_Weltan.md) compared the mysterious red mist of the Alik'r to a sacred flame that rises above the fire. In this case, the fire referred to the hot sand that covers the region. Weltan's writer friend, [Enric Milres](Lore_Enric_Milres.md), explained this further by saying that the fiery sand's power comes from "the beat of the heart of Tamriel itself".<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TA-22)</sup>

In Tsaesci culture, [Myn](Lore_Myn.md) represents the Fire element.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tot E-12)</sup> In certain island cultures, [Arius](Lore_Arius.md) is known as the God of Fire.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Arena-23)</sup> [Valia-Sha](Lore_Valia-Sha.md) is a Magna Ge associated with Fire.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TNC1-13)</sup> The Pandrum Clan of [Orcs](Lore_Orcs.md) (also known as the Fire Clan) supposedly wielded fire as both a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) and as a tool for a variety of uses. They are rumored to consist of primitive shamans and healers. They disappeared into obscurity during the second siege of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-OCAS-24)</sup>

## Flesh
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/SI-quest-Rebuilding_the_Gatekeeper_06.jpg/200px-SI-quest-Rebuilding_the_Gatekeeper_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-quest-Rebuilding_the_Gatekeeper_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-quest-Rebuilding_the_Gatekeeper_06.jpg) A Flesh Magic ritual | - *For more information, see the article on [Flesh Magic](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md).* |
| --- |

Unlike the other five elements, the sixth element of **Flesh** (known as **Slen** in the Dragon language)<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SRForm-25)</sup> is said to have been "hidden" at creation by virtue of its own self-awareness. It remains largely unknown and esoteric among modern societies. The element is formed by binding the Essence of Flesh with the Quintessence of Flesh. The Essence is formed from an amalgamation of Meat, Blood, Bone, and Breath—the essential components of true Flesh. Through many years of intensive labor, these four essential components can be isolated and grown into their mystical forms: Osseous Marrow, Dermis Membrane, Essence of Breath, and Blood Liqueur. The Quintessence of Flesh is the immortal [Soul](Lore_Soul.md), usually represented in Flesh Magic rituals in the form of a bound [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SIRelmyna-3)</sup>

[Xero-Lyg](Lore_Xero-Lyg.md) is a Magna Ge associated with Flesh.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TNC2-26)</sup>

## Light
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/ON-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ayleid_Well.jpg) An Ayleid Well containing starlight | - *For more information, see the article on [Varliance](Lore_Varliance.md).* |
| --- |

**Light** (also called **Aether**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TOTSN-2)</sup> or **Jun** in the Dragon language)<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-27)</sup> is one of the basic elements of creation. It was the most exalted of the four elements recognized by the Ayleids who believed that light was one of the four major elements, with fire being a weaker corruption thereof. They believed the most sublime form of light was starlight due to it emanating from the plane of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), the source of all [magical](Lore_Magic.md) power. The belief that starlight is the most potent of all magical powers led the Ayleids to utilize [Aetherial fragments](Lore_Aetherial_Fragments.md) in their magical practice. This resulted in the creation of [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md), [Varla Stones](Lore_Varla_Stone.md), and [Ayleid Wells](Lore_Ayleid_Wells.md), among other such artifacts associated with starlight.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mft S-1)</sup>

Moonlight is venerated by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). The temple of [Moonmont](Lore_Moonmont.md) in southern [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md) is considered the heart of lunar light.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONRid Thar-28)</sup> All of the moonlight that shimmers on the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) gathers in [Moonlit Cove](Lore_Moonlit_Cove.md), on the southern coast of the [Quin'rawl Peninsula](Lore_Quin%27rawl_Peninsula.md). Here the infused moonlight glows in the temple's natural caves and gives people a place to meditate in peace.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-SWOTSS-29)</sup>

## Water
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg) Water Magic **Water** (known as **Laar** in the Dragon language)<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TIL-30)</sup> is one of the basic elements of creation. On [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), water is composed of memory. When a mortal dies, their memories become water, and in this way all the memories of Tamriel's histories are stored within its waters. This fact is considered to be one of the most dangerous secrets about the natural order of the world, and is therefore closely guarded by the Demon of Knowledge [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> Many cultures utilize some form of [Water Magic](Lore_Water_Magic.md) to manipulate this element.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup>

Water is held in some reverence by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), who use oaths like "by the Blessed Water" or "by the Water of Life".<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ARKhajiit-33)</sup> In Tsaesci culture, [Zisa](Lore_Zisa.md) represents the Water element.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tot E-12)</sup> [Sheza-Rana](Lore_Sheza-Rana.md) is a Magna Ge associated with Water.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TNC1-13)</sup> The [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md) worship a trapped [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) known as the Guardian of the Water, an ancient being that represents the element in its true form.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup> Tide-Born [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) revere all forms of flowing water and refer to it as [the Tide](Lore_The_Tide.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-VOFS-35)</sup>

## Notes
- Although referred to as elemental spells by the School of Destruction, ice, lightning, [disease](Lore_Disease.md), poison, and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) are not considered to be basic elements. - Similarly, [iron](Lore_Iron.md), shadow, [stone](Lore_The_Stone.md), and cold-flame are referred to as elements in the context of Elemental Daedra.
