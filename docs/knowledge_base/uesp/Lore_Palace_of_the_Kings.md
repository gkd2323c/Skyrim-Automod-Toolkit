It is one of the last remaining structures from the days of the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) and is home to the hold's Jarl.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1S-6)</sup>

## History
[Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) built the palace so as to tower over the city, showing man's dominion over the very winds that had caused [Yngol](Lore_Yngol.md)'s death on the voyage from Atmora. He also had the palace built so he could always look upon the hill of his dear son's [resting place](Lore_Yngol_Barrow.md), and feel that his line would know peace in this new home that was never known in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Sot R:V19-7)</sup>

When the city was damaged during the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md), the invading Akaviri burned and looted the Palace of the Kings.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-8)</sup> [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) was crowned High King in the palace three weeks after the Akaviri were defeated,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jt SK-1)</sup> but elected to repair the rest of the city before the palace. The court was temporarily displaced as a result, and by [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581) the city was ruled from Stone-Fist Hall while repairs were ongoing.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Jorunn-9)</sup> A number of stone chunks from the damaged Palace had illegally found their way into Nord homes during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Pot Po Y-4)</sup> The Palace was still closed for the [Konunleikar](Lore_Konunleikar.md) celebrating the tenth year of Jorunn's reign in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The repairs on the Palace were nearly complete when it was ransacked circa 2E 582 by [Greymoor](Lore_Greymoor_Keep.md) vampires working with the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md). The interlopers kidnapped King Jorunn when he was giving a tour of the palace, and proceeded to hold him captive in the palace dungeons while they attempted to turn him into a vampire. This was done on the orders of their patron, [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md), who planned to forcibly make the Skald-King an ally of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). He was rescued by [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md) and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) before this could happen.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

The building remained standing by [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1S-6)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), Windhelm Palace was occupied by King Asgan and [adventurers](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) would seek an audience with him, accepting quests that would take them all over Skyrim.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ARRuler-13)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the palace serves as the headquarters of the Stormcloaks, under the leadership of Jarl [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), who sits upon the ancient Throne of Ysgramor. And so the Palace of the Kings is, as such, the center of the pro-Nord war effort in the Civil War.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-3)</sup><sup>:263</sup>
