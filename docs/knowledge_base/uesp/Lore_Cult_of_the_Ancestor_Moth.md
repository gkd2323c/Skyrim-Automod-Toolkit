# Cult of the Ancestor Moth

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/ON-banner-Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.png/100px-ON-banner-Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.png) The Cult's banner, depicting an Ancestor Moth [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/ON-render-Moth_Priests.jpg/200px-ON-render-Moth_Priests.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Moth_Priests.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Moth_Priests.jpg) Moth Priests *The priests of the Ancestor Moth are devoted to veneration of those who came before us, for the wisdom of the ancestors can sing the future into the present. As such, we alone have the privilege of interpreting the Elder Scrolls.*
—Sister Terran Arminus Before the Cult of the Ancestor Moth (sometime stylized as **Ancestor-Moth**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> **Order of the Ancestor Moth**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POTAM-2)</sup> **Scrollkeepers of the Imperial City**),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFUG-3)</sup> the Cyro-Nords had long exported ancestor-silks, simple but exotic shawls woven with the silks of the gypsy moth and inscribed with the genealogy of the buyer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> For the cult, the ancestor and moth became synonymous. Members of the Order of the Ancestor Moth honor and celebrate their ancestors, whose [spirits](Lore_Ghost.md) are embodied in the [Ancestor Moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md), and possess the knowledge to weave this essence into their creations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POTAM-2)</sup> [Fjyrons](Lore_Fjyron.md) can be sung into the silk produced by the Ancestor Moths. When the silk is spun into cloth and embroidered with the genealogy of the appropriate ancestor, it creates garments of extraordinary power.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POTAM-2)</sup> The singing and hymnal spirits of one's forebears are caught in the special silk-gathering ritual. The swishing of the silk material during movement reproduces the wonderful ancestral chorus contained in the silk. It became a sacred custom among the [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md), which persists to this day. The silk is highly prized throughout the Empire and is exported alongside other valuable goods.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TA-4)</sup> The higher order Monks of the cult are able to forgo the magical ritual needed to enchant the silk and instead merely wear the moths themselves.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup> They are able to [attract the ancestor-moths](Lore_Ritual_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) through the application of ground bark from the moth's favorite tree, and through chanting inaudible mantras which they must constantly maintain to keep the moths in contact with them.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRUnseen-5)</sup>

## The Elder Scrolls and the Cult of the Ancestor Moth
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Ancestor_Glade.jpg) An Ancestor Glade where the Ritual of the Ancestor Moth is performed [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-crown_store-Ancestor_Moth_Swarm.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Ancestor_Moth_Swarm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ancestor_Moth_Swarm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ancestor_Moth_Swarm.jpg) Ancestor Moths, believed to carry Fjyrons which enable one to read the Elder Scrolls The members of the cult are, by tradition, the only people allowed to read the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), a privilege which eventually strikes them blind.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POTAM-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-EOTES-6)</sup> They care for the Imperial Library (also called the Elder Library) within the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBUltimate-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AAOTS-8)</sup>

Some [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) texts suggest the methods used by the Cult of the Ancestor Moth to read the Scrolls might've originated from the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md). The Nedic slaves were tasked to tend to the reading rooms within the White Gold-Tower, where the elves made use of the Ancestor Moths and [Canticle Trees](Lore_Canticle_Tree.md) to learn how to master the world from the Elder Scrolls in their possession. When the Ayleids realized their slaves could also hear the song of the moths, they decided to teach them the secrets of how to read the Scrolls, so as to have them perform the readings and bear the burden in their stead. In secret these Nedic moth tenders spirited away the components of the reading ritual and established their own sacred glades, beginning the tradition that gave rise to the Cult of the Ancestor Moth. There are those within the Cult that believe the secrets of reading the Scrolls were taught by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), or that the moths were first stolen from the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md).The Tradition of naming the Elder Scrolls is said to have also started with the Ayleids, though some within the Cult of the Ancestor Moth are skeptical of this claim.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LAESMP-9)</sup>

According to the Cult of the Ancestor Moth, mortal [souls](Lore_Souls.md) are the [Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) essence at the core of every mortal's existence. They can be likened to the wings of a butterfly, scaled and full of vessels which become filled with the experiences of mortal existence. It is said that upon death a process of dissipation whereby some of these accumulated contents disperse. These essences, the [Fjyrons](Lore_Fjyron.md) or "will to peace", are believed to retain a connection to both the grand fabric of [creation](Lore_Aurbis.md) and to all aspects of the soul in all their [destinations](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md). Thus because, all aspects of a soul remain connected, tapping into the Fjyrons allows one who knows how to receive the erudition of the ancestors. Knowledge and guidance from beyond mortality, from the [existence](Lore_Aetherius.md) beyond the present and the past and the known world, where time is irrelevant, thus allowing one to glimpse the cosmic tapestry and its threads. The [Ancestor Moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md) of the Order are said to imbibe the Fjyrons, described by some as the memories of the dead,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TA-4)</sup> becoming animated by the Fjyrons of the ancestor spirits, shepherding that wisdom and passing it through the generations. Thus the moths represent a connection to the Aedra and the "endless Aedric coil of mortal souls" through the experiences of every mortal who has ever lived, and their trilling forms a sort of primal augur which allows the Order to read and interpret the contents of the Elder Scrolls which contain knowledge said to exceed even the [gods](Lore_Gods.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDS-10)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-POTAM-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AMS-11)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LAESMP-9)</sup> The monks use special cleansing bowls to remove impurities from their fingers before, during, and after they read the Elder Scrolls. This leaves a residue in the bowls that has been suggested to be aetherial residue that could deaden their fingers like the scrolls deaden the eyes.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> They are known to be reclusive and have hidden, or at least out of the way, sanctuaries scattered throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SRDexion-13)</sup>

In [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Elder Scrolls mysteriously disappeared from the Imperial Library and were scattered across Tamriel by forces unknown.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SRDexion-13)</sup> Members of the Cult set out to search for them and return them to the White-Gold Tower.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-14)</sup>

## Notable Moth Priests
- **[Abbot Crassius Viria](Lore_Crassius_Viria.md)** — The abbot of the Temple ca. 2E 582.
- **Dexion Evicus** — A Moth Priest associated with the [Tyranny of the Sun](Lore_Tyranny_of_the_Sun.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SRDexion-13)</sup>
- **Elegian** — A Moth Priest who was responsible for the creation of the [Cataclysts](Lore_Cataclyst.md) and [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md)'s mask.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TDV-15)</sup>
- **[Gudrun](Lore_Gudrun.md)** — A Moth Priestess who introduced the Way of the Peaceful Fist.
- **[Kellen](Lore_Kellen.md)** — A Moth Priest knowledgeable about the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md).
- **[Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md)** — A former Emperor of Cyrodiil known as the Prophet.
- **[Terran Arminus](Lore_Terran_Arminus.md)** — A Moth Priestess who helped to stop the [Planemeld Obverse](Lore_Planemeld_Obverse.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PO-16)</sup>

## Notes
- Monastic orders founded by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and dedicated to [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md) are the keepers of the Elder Scrolls.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue J-17)</sup>
- In early [unofficial lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Unofficial_Lore), some members of the order were known as the **Libromancers**. The Elder Scrolls were blank, but if a Moth Priest libromancer were to cut themselves and dribble their blood onto the Scroll, a prophecy would be revealed. The more blood a Scroll was given, the more words and letters it would spell out.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPFrozen Tears-18)</sup>
