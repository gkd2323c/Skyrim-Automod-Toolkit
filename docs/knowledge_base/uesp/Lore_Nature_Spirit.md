# Nature Spirit

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/ON-creature-Nature_Spirit.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nature_Spirit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nature_Spirit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nature_Spirit.jpg) A summoned Nature Spirit (left) Nature spirits, also known as spirits of the green,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GG-1)</sup> spirits of nature, and spirits of the forest, are spiritual creatures that originate from [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONS-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup> They are described as physical manifestations of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Prima-4)</sup><sup>:216</sup> and are tied to the environment around them. They are as unpredictable as nature itself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONS-2)</sup> By one account, the term "nature spirit" is so vague and broad as to be essentially useless.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONSelene-5)</sup> Habitat-specific terms include **forest spirits**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Lot F-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-STOTDF3-8)</sup> **timber spirits**,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DFWood-9)</sup> **woodland spirits**,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OMGAS-10)</sup> **wood-spirits**,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-EGTVW-11)</sup> **spirits of the wood**,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DFWood-9)</sup> **spirits of the jungle**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SJ-12)</sup> **wind spirits**,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE3H-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Ember-14)</sup> **spirits of the earth**,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DO-15)</sup> **water spirits** or **spirits of water**.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DO-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-INB-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Mot L-17)</sup> Some are known as **tree spirits**,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-BMSkaal-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> **spirits of the trees**,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Malug-20)</sup> or **fire spirits** depending on their appearance.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE3H-13)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-orl-21)</sup> Nature spirits are typically associated with [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) or [Jephre](Lore_Jephre.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONS-2)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-YSS-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Talomar-23)</sup> but some are associated with other gods and goddesses that are affiliated with nature, such as the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-BMSkaal-18)</sup> [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md),<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-FWB-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Kyne C-26)</sup><sup>:59</sup> [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> or even [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-HG-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BLSot H-29)</sup>

## Characteristics
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/ON-crown_store-Cinderstone_Kagouti.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Cinderstone_Kagouti.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Cinderstone_Kagouti.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Cinderstone_Kagouti.jpg) A Cinderstone Kagouti, given form by Druids Nature spirits are described as "dreams of the Green" and "forces of nature in the bosom of Y'ffre",<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Laurel-30)</sup> made real and given form and purpose either naturally or through the will of practicioners of [nature magic](Lore_Nature_Magic.md) such as the [Druids](Lore_Druid.md) and the [Wyrd](Lore_Wyrd.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> Certain greater spirits are said to be a subset of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)'s laws shaped into spirits through [nature magic](Lore_Nature_Magic.md),<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESODA-32)</sup> or to have been bound to a location with a certain task by Y'ffre himself.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PIT-33)</sup> They are said to reside in all things, rocks, roots, leaves, brooks, the wind, a field of flowers, or even a swarm of [Torchbugs](Lore_Torchbug.md), though their will might be difficult to perceive, even for those who know how to listen.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Ryvana-34)</sup> According to the [Augur of the Obscure](Lore_Augur_of_the_Obscure.md), waterfalls house a deluge of water spirits, and each individual stone has its own spirit with its own stories to tell.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Augur_of_the_Obscure-35)</sup>

Nature spirits that haven't yet emerged into the physical world are said to "slumber" within the [Green](Lore_The_Green.md), druids use a concoction named the "Elixir of Y'ffre's Thoughts", said to temporarily turn the imbiber into a dream or memory also, to communicate with them and exchange knowledge while in that state.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup> When a nature spirit "dies", it returns to its slumber within the Green, ending its current incarnation.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Chronicle-36)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PIT-33)</sup> Many vanquished nature spirits are said to reform or reincarnate, eventually.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Laurel-30)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Talomar-23)</sup>

Those spirits that are invoked from the Green to fulfill a purpose can be made to take on a variety of different forms, such as that of a [wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md), [gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md), or a [faun](Lore_Faun.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-orl-21)</sup> In some cases a spirit might choose to manifest itself in a specific form suited to tasks such as protecting local nature from a fire.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Lost-37)</sup> The purpose for which a nature spirit has been invoked might range from guarding a location to recording and passing on stories however, if a spirit is made to wait for a very long time before it can fulfill the purpose it was given, it can forget itself and grow wild and hostile.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup> Due to this, and how unpredictable they can be, invoking a spirit from the Green through magic is something done sparsely even by Druids, only the [Druid King](Lore_Druid_King.md) can safely invoke and command even the greater spirits of nature.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PIT-33)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Laurel-30)</sup>

The emotions and thoughts of mortals are something nature spirits generally can't understand, preventing them from experiencing such things even through the use of magic, but exceptions exist in the form of spirits such as the Chronicle, whose purpose involves passing on the experiences of mortals.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PIT-33)</sup>

Nature spirits can sense and communicate with nature and other nature spirits, something the spirits themselves refer to as hearing their "song". A nature spirit can perceive those things the element of nature it's linked to touches, an earth spirit's "Stonesight" might allow one to perceive all that rests within the rock of the area it's bound to, a root spirit's to hear through the interconnected roots of all plants, and an air spirit's grant knowledge of what is touched by the air. Druids are known to use ritual altars which allow them to commune with nature spirits and share in their senses while doing so.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Laurel-30)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-AD-38)</sup>

When compelled, it is possible for a nature spirit to merge with a mortal vessel, allowing for its power to be drawn upon, this allows for the spirit's "sight", its knowledge of what the element of nature it presides over touches, to be shared by the mortal. Should the vessel's body die, the spirit will be released, freed of prior bonds to a specific location or purpose.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TSD-39)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-AD-38)</sup>

Most spirits are bound to a specific location, and to a purpose and nature that typically don't change, and won't act in way that contradicts them, a peaceful flower spirit won't fight and an unmoving spirit of stone won't help move crops. A spirit's purpose and nature can become temporarily imbalanced through the use of powerful magic or the destruction of the nature they preside over, placing great strain on the spirit and potentially making it lash out in anguish, however, even if such a change occurs, the spirit's original nature will be restored when it is reminded of it, as nothing can truly erase it. Spirits that lost the element of nature they were initially bound to can be rehoused, the spirit of a lost river might find a new river to call home. Exceptions to the general rule exist in the form of spirits who were invoked from the Green to make choices of their own, and so might not be bound by rigid purpose or location the way most spirits are. In addition, a spirit's purpose can become cloudy when their objectives are fulfilled, in which case they may need to seek a new purpose for themselves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Laurel-30)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONS-2)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Lost-37)</sup>

Should the spirit that's bound to a specific area or part of nature depart, it is possible for another nature spirit to bind themselves to it and take the departed spirit's place. This is said to cause the newly bound spirit to transform and grow to match its new purpose. Such an event occurred when the spirit Frii became the new spirit of [Mount Firesong](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md) in order to prevent the volcano from erupting and destroying the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-3)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Laurel-30)</sup>

## Cultural Significance
Nature spirits have a great deal of influence in some of the cultures of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). One of the possible origins for the nature worship of the ancient [proto-Bretons](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) is from the spirit Galen, and they would be later dubbed the retroactive moniker "Druids of Galen". Though the Druids of Galen as a single unifying group are now defunct, modern [druids](Lore_Druids.md) are known to are known to consort with nature spirits.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONS-2)</sup> Friendships can be established with spirits, or they can also be coerced into doing one's bidding,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-40)</sup> but this is frowned upon by some druids.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONS-2)</sup> Druids can also communicate with<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONDJ-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup> and form cordial relationships with animals, who can serve as intermediaries between druids and nature spirits.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-43)</sup> Some nature spirits can take the form of animals.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-KST-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-45)</sup> The [Wyrd Covens](Lore_Wyrd_Covens.md) are also animists who hold nature spirits sacred, as historically druids and the Wyrd were once the same before a religious schism.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONWD-46)</sup> The 18th of [Morning Star](Lore_Morning_Star.md) was known as the [Waking Day](Lore_Waking_Day.md), when the people of the [Yeorth Burrowland](Lore_Yeorth_Burrowland.md) woke the spirits of nature after the winter, somewhat like their more reverential ancestors, in one of the oldest holidays in High Rock before recorded history.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-HOTIB-47)</sup>

The [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) hold reverence for numerous spirits, spanning the spectrum from grand to minor. In reality, the number of faiths within [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) aligns with the multitude of [clans](Lore_Reachfolk_Clans.md) that call it home. Among these clans, some may venerate nature associated spirits such as sacred [elks](Lore_Elk.md) or the spirits of a mountain springs.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-GSOTR-48)</sup> It was customary to thank to nature spirits before felling trees. Subsequently, the Reachfolk often would plant new saplings in the vacant space left by the fallen tree.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Malug-20)</sup>

## Types of Nature Spirits

### Aggression of Root‎
[Aggressions of Root](Lore_Aggression_of_Root.md) are uncommon nature spirits. According to the [Druids](Lore_Druid.md), they speak with the voice of [plants](Lore_Flora.md) and protect nature where it feels threatened. They are known for attracting a wide range of dangerous wildlife to any area they inhabit, creating a hazardous environment for nearby residents and travelers. They can command other nature spirits in battle such as [Forest Wraiths](Lore_Forest_Wraith.md) and [Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md), as well as vegetation such as [Stranglers](Lore_Strangler.md) and creatures such as [Fauns](Lore_Faun.md). They are capable of speech and have a discordant tone.

### Caenlorn Wolf
The [Caenlorn Wolves](Lore_Caenlorn_Wolf.md) (or simply the **Caenlorn**) are the sacred ceremonial spirit [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) of the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md), a tribe of [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) native to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) who are known for their worship of the island's wolves and [bears](Lore_Bear.md). These wolves are held in high regard by the tribe's shaman. Usually the Caenlorn Wolves reside in the world of the spirit with the [All-Father](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:All-Father); they are only summoned by the Skaal for certain ceremonies.

According to the traditions of the Skaal, crimes such as theft or bearing false witness can result in a sentence of either exile or death. Of the two, death is considered the more honorable punishment for a warrior, as it involves a ritual sacrifice to the wolves. In such cases, the Caenlorn Wolves will be summoned so that the accused may be fed to them. The Skaal view this as the preferred way to die, as they believe that the warrior's spirit lives on and is purified by allowing the wolves to consume their flesh. The wolves will then return the spirit to the cycle of life so that the warrior may be [reincarnated](Lore_Reincarnation.md) in a different form. However, some members of the Skaal consider the practice to be barbaric due to the horror and pain involved.

A guardian spirit "haunts" the area it is connected to, and cannot abandon or wander far from the location. Guardian spirits usually take the form of mortal creatures,<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-KST-44)</sup> but despite appearances,<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-58)</sup> these spirits were never mortal.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-So S-57)</sup> Certain guardian spirits were known to be curious or mischievous with mortals, while others are outright hostile.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-So S-57)</sup>

Kyne's Sacred Trials are an old [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) tradition, where the participant has to hunt and defeat several specific guardian spirits, known as Kyne's champions, in order to prove themselves as a true hunter and receive Kyne's blessing. The tradition had fallen into obscurity by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-FWB-24)</sup>

### Ice Wraith
[Ice wraiths](Lore_Ice_Wraith.md) are magical, lucid serpentine nature spirits purely made out of ice.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-HBIW-59)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TW-60)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-KCAHC-61)</sup> They are typically found across [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s snow-blown landscape and mountain ranges but can also be found in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s reclusive mountain country, as well as the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md).<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ONWraith-62)</sup> They blend in the snow and become nigh invisible to the naked eye. They will then lunge at unsuspecting prey with their entire body and bite them to death. Prey can sometimes get infected with [Witbane](Lore_Witbane.md), a disease that dulls a person's intelligence.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-HBIW-59)</sup> Ice Wraiths are among many creatures that are immune to paralysis.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-COWE-63)</sup>

### Marsh Giant
[Marsh giants](Lore_Marsh_Giant.md) are a [giant](Lore_Giant.md) -like race native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). These manifestations of nature are a form of woodland spirit comparable to [spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md). They are not related to the common giants of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), but they do share their size and pastoral activities. Their exact level of intelligence is unknown due to limited interactions. However, the Hist apparently have a long and acrimonious history with the Marsh Giants, and urge the Argonians to avoid the creatures if they can.

They play an important role in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md)'s ecosystem by knocking down dead trees, turning the soil and making room for younger vegetation to grow. This encourages the growth of mosses and suppresses the development of invasive fungi. Marsh giants also hinder loggers, contribute to [avian](Lore_Birds.md) diversity and ward off certain predators, such as feral [cats](Lore_Cat.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OMGAS-10)</sup>

### Nereid
[Nereids](Lore_Nereid.md) (also called **water-sprites** by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), or **water nymphs**)<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-KCAHC-61)</sup> are [mer](Lore_Mer.md) -like, feminine water spirits that live near bodies of water and can often be heard singing an angelic, but transfixing [melody](Lore_Music.md). They are known practitioners of [water magic](Lore_Water_Magic.md), an obscure form of magic, and can either become hostile or tolerant of people's company.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-KCAHC-61)</sup> Generally speaking, the Nereid are an intelligent-folk that more often than not, protect the land they live on and its denizens, even claiming old ruins as their own haven.

The **Leimenid** is a variant of nereid that practices frost magic. They resemble the typical nereid, but appear more ethereal and in a frosty mist. They are typically found in cold climates such as the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), or atop wintry mountains like Scalecaller Peak.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ONPestilience-70)</sup> They have also been used in the [Maelstrom Arena](Lore_Maelstrom.md), the pocket realm of the [demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](Lore_Fa-Nuit-Hen.md).<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ONMaelstrom-71)</sup>

### Spriggan
[Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md), often called **nature's guardians**,<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-OBRumours-72)</sup> are nature spirits<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-FGTS-73)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-OBPrima-74)</sup><sup>:55</sup> that take the form of tree-like female humanoids. They possess moderate intelligence, but despite being revered they are usually hostile towards travelers. Spriggans have their own unique [language](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Spriggan), which can be learned by scholars and used to calm the creatures.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-75)</sup> They are usually found protecting secluded glades and groves all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), often blending in with the plant life and catching trespassers unaware. Spriggans attack with their long, sharp fingers as well as with their teeth, and have the ability to poison enemies. They have incredible regenerative abilities, resulting in adventurers often having to defeat them several times before they remain dead. If threatened, they can call nearby woodland animals to aid them in battle, and some even have the ability to magically summon [black bears](Lore_Black_Bear.md).

The [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) is known to perform perverted rituals that uses Spriggans as a base, twisting the creatures into [lurchers](Lore_Lurcher.md), which are wooden [golems](Lore_Golem.md) of multiple origins. This is said to leave them a shell of their former selves.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESOWI-55)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-76)</sup> In [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md), the tribal bosmer see lurchers as gifts from the [Wilderking](Lore_Wilderking.md), and "embodiments of the raw energy of the forest".<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-ESOFrighten-77)</sup>

The [gnarls](Lore_Gnarl.md) of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) are treelike creatures who seem to have some relation to spriggans,<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-78)</sup> and are sometimes classified with them.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-LG-79)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Utter-Leyton-80)</sup> They are believed to be caretakers of the Shivering Isles' [Root System](Lore_Root.md),<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-81)</sup> and have a truce of sorts with the natural creatures of the Isles.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-82)</sup>

### Wispmother
[Wispmothers](Lore_Wispmother.md) (sometimes **wispfathers**)<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-83)</sup> are magical-energy beings thought to be either ghosts, [wraiths](Lore_Wraith.md) or nature spirits,<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-EGT-84)</sup> and they can be found in the wilds of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Killing a wispmother causes all of its attendant [wisps](Lore_Wisp.md) and shades to die. Lydette Viliane of the [Synod](Lore_Synod.md) has proposed that they are elemental personifications of snow or mist.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TW-60)</sup>

[Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) is known to send wisps to herald the storms of Rain's Hand. To see one is to see the promise of new growth, new life, and a new chapter in nature's cycle.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Galen_Wisp-85)</sup>

### Brackenleaf
[![ON-creature-Brackenleaf 02.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Brackenleaf_02.jpg) [Brackenleaf](Lore_Brackenleaf_(tree).md) is believed to be one of the first trees of Grahtwood. The story goes that when [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) spoke their world-shaping tale, she planted her words in the earth and there grew Brackenleaf. Indeed, Brackenleaf was compared to Elden Root, mentioned as one of many more ancient seeds. Brackenleaf's heart is a sought after relic as legends say the heart holds the power of Y'ffre, the same magic used to create the world. Due to this, it's been under threat since the beginning of time itself, with foes seeking to take its power for themselves. The Outsider is a prominent example of such a foe.

### The Chronicle
[![ON-object-The Chronicle.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/ON-object-The_Chronicle.jpg/200px-ON-object-The_Chronicle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-object-The_Chronicle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-object-The_Chronicle.jpg) [The Chronicle](Lore_The_Chronicle.md), also referred to as the **Spirit of Y'ffre's Path** or simply the **Spirit of the Path**, was a revered nature spirit held in high regard by the [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) of the [Eldertide Circle](Lore_Eldertide_Circle.md). Its primary duty was to preserve their stories and historical accounts. The Chronicle differs from other nature spirits by its emotional depth. It connects with the experiences and emotions of mortals, sharing their feelings through the stories it tells. It was considered among the very powerful Nature spirits, and was well known among the Druids.

### Spirit of Air
[![ON-creature-Spirit of Air.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d9/ON-creature-Spirit_of_Air.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Spirit_of_Air.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Spirit_of_Air.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Spirit_of_Air.jpg) The [Spirit of Air](Lore_Spirit_of_Air.md) is one of three powerful nature spirits residing within the sacred confines of the Earthen Root Enclave. Communing with the Spirit of Air was a transformative experience, granting those who dare to venture its relentless honesty and wisdom. This spirit, loud, and incapable of deceit, was known to bestow the knowledge of all spoken words, a gift that unveils both the beauty and the weight of one's own life narrative. It can repeat anything that was ever spoken aloud. The Spirit of Air challenges individuals to confront their past with unflinching courage, while imparting the invaluable lesson of cherishing each fleeting moment in the tapestry of existence.

### Spirit of Root
[![ON-creature-Corruption of Root.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/ON-creature-Corruption_of_Root.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Corruption_of_Root.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Corruption_of_Root.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Corruption_of_Root.jpg) The [Spirit of Root](Lore_Spirit_of_Root.md) (also temporarily referred to as the **Corruption of Root**) is one of three powerful nature spirits residing within the sacred confines of the Earthen Root Enclave. The Spirit of Root speaks with all the voices of the plants, as plants communicate with each other. The Spirit of Root and the [trellis sentinels](Lore_Trellis_Sentinel.md) share a crucial relationship in the protection and preservation of the Deeproot. Trellis sentinels serve the vital role of providing support to the Deeproot, preventing its collapse and safeguarding the Spirit of Root residing within. Connection with the Spirit of Root served as a poignant reminder that even in the most desolate of places, solitude is an illusion, for the voices of all living beings can be heard anywhere. It allowed druids to hear the realm through interconnected roots of all plants. Spirit of Root could command [Fauns](Lore_Faun.md) and graft-root animals.

### Spirit of Stone

It was associated with the [element](Lore_Elements.md) of [stone](Lore_The_Stone.md) and was known to have the ability to command [Stone Atronachs](Lore_Stone_Atronach.md).
