[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/ON-place-Tarlain_Heights.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tarlain_Heights.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tarlain_Heights.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tarlain_Heights.jpg) Tarlain Heights circa 2E 582 The Tarlain Heights is a region in the [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md) region of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). It is the region's least populated area, with its main population center being the town of Redfur Trading Post, located near the crossroad towards [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) and the [Xylo River Basin](Lore_Xylo_River_Basin.md) in [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md). However, in the past, Tarlain Heights contained significant settlements, namely [Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_Gil-Var-Delle.md), one of the province's [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) cities and [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) during the winter season. Some of Grahtwood's [Wood Orc](Lore_Wood_Orcs.md) clans historically live in the region, such as the Barkbite Clan of [Reman's Bluff](Lore_Reman%27s_Bluff.md) and the Orcs of [Goldfolly](Lore_Goldfolly.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONTarlain-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/ON-place-Redfur_Trading_Post.jpg/200px-ON-place-Redfur_Trading_Post.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Redfur_Trading_Post.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Redfur_Trading_Post.jpg) Redfur Trading Post Some of Tarlain Heights' oldest ruins include the Tomb of [Anahbi](Lore_Anahbi.md) and the [Ayleid ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) of [Falinesti's winter site](Lore_Falinesti_Winter_Site.md) and Wormroot Depths, all of which have some connection to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Anahbi is a mythical Khajiit figure of an unknown age who hailed from [Quin'rawl](Lore_Quin%27rawl.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ADIM-2)</sup> Her tomb was thought to be lost in time until it was discovered in the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONShan-3)</sup> The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) first came to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) after the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) of the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). And through assimilation with the local [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), their culture and society dwindled, and their ruins were abandoned.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ASIV-4)</sup>

Wormroot Depths, an Ayleid outpost near the road to Malabal Tor, contains an ancient Khajiit chamber that holds a triangular piece for deciphering a map on the [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONWormroot-5)</sup> This location was chosen in association with [Nurarion the Perfect](Lore_Nurarion_the_Perfect.md), a companion to the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) hero [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md) who *"communed among the buried roots of the Ayleid worms."*<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KATD-6)</sup> The history of the Ayleid ruins under Falinesti's winter site is unknown. But in the modern age, they are known as Nairume's Prison. Nairume was a First Era [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) noblewoman from Falinesti that caught the affection of Rajhin's shadow of desire. When he was rejected, he bound her for eternity with Rajhin's Mantle and the ice from his vault covered the ruins.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONNairume-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONShadow-8)</sup>

Significant events occurred in Tarlain Heights in the late First Era. During the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md)'s conquest of Valenwood, the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) built the fort [Reman's Bluff](Lore_Reman%27s_Bluff.md) beyond the Kingdom of Grahtwood's borders. After a siege from hostile Wood Orcs, General [Zantonius](Lore_Zantonius.md) left the stronghold to the wilderness and later built [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md) on the [Long Coast](Lore_Long_Coast.md). The Barkbite Clan of Wood Orcs moved in to establish their clanhold. However, they reportedly have no direct connections to the Orcish invaders.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TFARORB-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFOS-10)</sup> Faltonia Salvius, an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) that sought riches and opportunity in Grahtwood decided to build a mining town in central Tarlain Heights and conscripted local Wood Orcs to harvest [gold](Lore_Gold.md) in the mines. The mine dried up within a year and later failed as a quarry mine. The town became known as [Goldfolly](Lore_Goldfolly.md)<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-FP-11)</sup> and by the mid-Second Era, it was abandoned.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONTarlain-1)</sup>

In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the town of [Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_Gil-Var-Delle.md) was destroyed by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Its story has been publicized and dramatized throughout the years, most famously in [Carlovac Townway](Lore_Carlovac_Townway.md)'s historical fiction, *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)*. Although some claim these stories are not confirmed,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TDOG-12)</sup> the incident is attributed to King [Dro'Zel](Lore_Dro%27Zel.md) of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), who did it in response to a Bosmer bard's retelling of the [Legend of the Lovers](Lore_Legend_of_the_Lovers.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-2920V2-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HOSAO-14)</sup> Sometime during the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) period, the city of Falinesti disappeared, having never returned to any of its seasonal sites. Until its return, each of its sites, including the winter one, was taken care of by the Falinesti Faithful.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONAndewen-15)</sup>

By the mid-Second Era, Redfur Trading Post was founded over Anahbi's tomb, and it was the region's main settlement.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONTarlain-1)</sup> [Haven](Lore_Haven.md) on the other side of the region had been rebuilt by the Altmer,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONLoad Aquifer-16)</sup> who also brought the [New Life](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md) tradition called the War Orphan's Sojourn with them. Every year, locals will make a pilgrimage to the Temple of the Eight in Tarlain Heights to donate [money](Lore_Gold_(currency).md) to children in need.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONOrphan-17)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), General [Endare](Lore_Endare.md) of the Jade Dragoons went to the Falinesti Winter Site to retrieve Rajhin's Mantle for the Aldmeri Dominion. In the end, however, the General died in vain attempt to seize power but Nairume was freed from her prison.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONWinter-18)</sup>

## Village of Tarlain Heights
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/AR-place-Tarlain_Heights.png/200px-AR-place-Tarlain_Heights.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Tarlain_Heights.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Tarlain_Heights.png) Tarlain Heights during the Imperial Simulacrum There is a settlement also called **Tarlain Heights** located north of the bay between Greenheart and [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ARMap-19)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Tarlain Heights was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Aracher and had a rivalry with Archen Cormount.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AR-20)</sup> Tarlain Heights was neighbored by several settlements, such as [Archen Cormount](Lore_Archen_Cormount.md) to the north-northeast, [Greenheart](Lore_Greenheart.md) to the far west-southwest, and Stonesquare to the east.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ARMap-19)</sup>
