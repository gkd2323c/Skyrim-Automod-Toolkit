Calantius of Skingrad was the Count of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mt C:Beragon-1)</sup> During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in 2E 582, he issued a decree that was ratified by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), redesignating [Arenthia Vale](Lore_Northern_Woods.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) as the [South Weald](Lore_Northern_Woods.md). He also ordered the [Legion of the West Weald](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) to annex this region.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Yft T-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-3)</sup> Calantius was also the author of *[The Fort Sphinxmoth Ruins](Lore_The_Fort_Sphinxmoth_Ruins.md)*, a comprehensive text detailing the history of [Fort Sphinxmoth](Lore_Fort_Sphinxmoth.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSFR-4)</sup>

## History
Little is known about Calantius' background. He acceded to the throne of Skingrad in [2E 570](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_570).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GI-5)</sup> At some point prior to the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), he penned *The Fort Sphinxmoth Ruins*.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSFR-4)</sup> In addition to his primary residence of Castle Skingrad, he maintained an office in Skingrad's chapel district<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CCO-6)</sup> and owned the Rustwall Estate, located south of the city.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Tegula-7)</sup> Calantius was considered pro-merchant, as he protected them and allowed them to do their jobs with little interference.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Jabariq-8)</sup>

During the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) of [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576) – [577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), Calantius and Skingrad supported [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) against Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md). In 2E 577, after Varen overthrew Leovic and declared himself emperor, Skingrad rejoined the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) as a vassal state, swearing allegiance to the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AAHo S-9)</sup>

After the outbreak of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Calantius issued a proclamation stating that Skingrad and the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) would be held as neutral territory in trust to the Ruby Throne and did not recognize the authority of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Po N-10)</sup> Calantius appointed Tribune Alea Idolus as his marshal and had the [Legion of the West Weald](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) defend Skingrad and the West Weald.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TAI-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mt CTA-12)</sup> Calantius was described as a shrewd opportunist who adeptly shielded his city from the chaos and conflict that engulfed [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-3)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-GRZPWW-13)</sup> However, ruling over a populace with a military mindset, he harbored his own ambitions and seized the opportunity presented by the power vacuum in the south.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-3)</sup> Exploiting the power vacuum, he issued a decree that was ratified by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), redesignating [Arenthia Vale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia_Vale) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) as the [South Weald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:South_Weald), and the Legion of the West Weald, led by General Lavinia Axius, was dispatched to annex the region in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Yft T-2)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CB:T-14)</sup> This annexation attempt ultimately failed.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ON-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SM-16)</sup>

Circa 2E 582, the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), an [Ayleid revivalist](Lore_Ayleid_Revivalists.md) group devoted to the Daedric Prince [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), utilized [Wildburn seeds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn_Seeds) to create a new forest known as the Dawnwood in the [Strid Vale](Lore_Strid_Vale.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Of IR-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Beragon-18)</sup> A group of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), led by [Nantharion](Lore_Nantharion.md), a nobleman from [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md) and the secret leader of the Recollection, subsequently established the settlement of Vashabar in Dawnwood.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-GRZPWW-13)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-VIVa B-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Mt CN-20)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Beragon-18)</sup> Following the establishment of Vashabar, Calantius sent three letters to King [Camoran Aeradan](Lore_Camoran_Aeradan.md), presumably demanding an explanation for the Bosmer's settlement in his territory.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Nt N-21)</sup> Calantius ordered legionaries to investigate both Dawnwood and the [Wildburn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn) —the devastated area that separated the forest from the rest of the West Weald.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CS-22)</sup> Calantius also refused the offers of cooperation from Nantharion and Vashabar.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Nantharion-23)</sup>

After Nantharion was killed and the Recollection was defeated, Calantius agreed to discuss stabilizing relations between Skingrad and Vashabar with the settlement's new leader, Greenspeaker Darolith.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Beragon-18)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Calantius-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Darolith-25)</sup> The Count planned to purchase the abandoned estates of various nobles and landowners in the Dawnwood and Wildburn regions, intending to use the land to secure a treaty with Vashabar and prevent the area from being overrun by monsters and outlaws.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Calantius-24)</sup> Calantius and Darolith eventually reached an understanding and worked to refine the details of a treaty granting Vashabar land rights and trade agreements with Skingrad and the rest of the West Weald.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Calantius-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Darolith-25)</sup> He hoped that by establishing trade between Skingrad and Vashabar and the Bosmer's efforts to heal the devastation caused by the Dawnwood and Wildborn, his people would come to realize that not all Wood Elves were aligned with the Recollection.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Calantius-24)</sup>

In 2E 582, Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) sent a diplomat to Skingrad, who persuaded Calantius to oversee the return of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) cultural artifacts from Varen's Watch.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CS-22)</sup>

In 2E 582, Calantius sponsored a [boar](Lore_Boar.md) hunt for the public. During the hunt, Calantius was nearly killed or seriously injured by a boar, but Albanus Caudex, one of the hunters, saved him. As a reward for saving his life, the Count offered Caudex a [horse](Lore_Horse.md) from his personal stable, as well as a year's supply of salted pork and a monetary prize—for taking down a boar plus more for saving his life.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TCBH-26)</sup> Calantius had a pet [bear](Lore_Bear.md) cub named Lord Svendalmor of the Burnt-Biscuits, who most called Burnbiscuit.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Luther-27)</sup> Burnbiscuit went missing while the Count and his entourage were investigating the devastation caused by the Wildburn, but an adventurer and an imperial legionary named Luther later found him and returned him to Calantius.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Luther-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BB-28)</sup> For returning Burnbiscuit, Calantius paid the adventurer and promoted Luther to sergeant.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BB-28)</sup>
