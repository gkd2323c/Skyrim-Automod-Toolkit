[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Westmark Moor (also called **Markwasten Moor**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WTC-2)</sup> is a rural region in southeastern [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It is specifically found along the [Great Bay](Lore_Great_Bay.md) area where it meets the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) to the south. The region is mostly farmland, with its largest settlements being Hoarfrost Downs and Fell's Run. But southeast toward the coast are large gravesites and burial grounds,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONWestmark-3)</sup> from the [Sanguine Barrows](Lore_Sanguine_Barrows.md) and Tribulation Crypt where Rivenspire's nobility are buried,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBOWM-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLoad Tribulation-5)</sup> to the legendary dungeon called the [Crypts of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONWestmark-3)</sup> The latter is considered part of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SKMap-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Vir2l SK-7)</sup>

## History
Some of the region's oldest ruins and landmarks include the Orc's Finger Ruins, [Ayleid ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) known for a local legend about an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) warrior and his ill-fated fight with a [lich](Lore_Lich.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONLothson-8)</sup> The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) lived in Rivenspire during ancient times,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TROL-9)</sup> presumingly from the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md), when the elves fled [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to High Rock from the [Alessians](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) in [1E 361](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_361).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ASIV-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-11)</sup> Another ruin is the Lorkrata Hills, which were built by King Berthulic, who ruled one of the first free [Breton kingdoms](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md) in the early First Era after the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md)'s decline<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONLoad Lorkrata-12)</sup> in [1E 500](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_500).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1-13)</sup> The Crypt of Hearts was built over a cavern that housed the [Star Teeth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md), artifacts of Ayleid origin and held in high importance by them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ST1-14)</sup>

The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of Markwasten Moor and the Shornhelm region have a long and storied history, from the time of legends with the Trammeling of the [Giants](Lore_Giant.md), to the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when they partook in the Charge of the Montclair Knights in the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md) circa [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482) and the Purge of the [Wyrd-Hags](Lore_Wyrd_Covens.md) in the Year of [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md) ([1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668)).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-1)</sup> The Sanguine Barrows and nearby Tribulation Crypt in Westmark Moor were built to inter Rivenspire's nobles, including members of Houses [Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md), [Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md), and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md). Since the kingdom's houses often clash with each other, the Sanguine Barrows are constantly the site of feuds.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBOWM-4)</sup> One of Westmark Moor's most well-known families are the Edralds, a storied family of some renown.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONFederic-15)</sup>

After the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) landed in [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md), they set to work on building their great fleet to destroy the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) throughout the [1E 2240 s](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2240). While most of Tamriel's shipwrights were on the Systres, several shipyards were also on the mainland. Most notable were the ones in Rivenspire,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SH3-16)</sup> specifically in the settlement now known as Shipwright's Regret. Nowadays, the abandoned harbor is haunted by restless spirits and [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONShipwright-17)</sup>

One example of a feud in the Sanguine Barrows was during [Mid Year](Lore_Mid_Year.md) in [2E 551](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_551), when House Montclair and Tamrith fought for a site intended for family members that died the same day. The Bloody Funeral remains a blight on each houses' history.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBOWM-4)</sup> Markwasten Moor became the site of a pivotal battle during [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) of [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), when the eponymous [King of Shornhelm](Lore_Ranser.md) fought [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) and his early [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). When the Orcs of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) intervened, the Battle of Markwasten Moor ended with Ranser's forces annihilated.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TFOKR-18)</sup> Unknown to historians at the time, Ranser's true last stand was on the site now known as Traitor's Tor in northern Westmark Moor.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TTFOKR-19)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Crypts of Hearts fell under [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s borders during the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SKMap-6)</sup> After the war, Skyrim took several miles of land in High Rock and allowed its counts to take control. Very little territory had changed hands by [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433)<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3-20)</sup> but by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Crypts of Hearts fell in the Kingdom of Shornhelm's borders.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-COTHD-21)</sup>

## Village of Markwasten Moor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/AR-place-Markwasten_Moor.jpg/200px-AR-place-Markwasten_Moor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Markwasten_Moor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Markwasten_Moor.jpg) Markwasten Moor during the Imperial Simulacrum There is a settlement called **Markwasten Moor** in central High Rock, just southeast of Shornhelm and west of the [Great Bay](Lore_Great_Bay.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-AR-22)</sup> It has been active since at least the late Second Era when it was known as **Marnstern Moor**.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-RGMap-23)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Markwasten Moor was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Tristyrick and had a rivalry with Thorkan Park. The only temple in the settlement was the Brotherhood of Temperance.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-AR-22)</sup> Markwasten Moor was neighbored by several settlements, such as [Portdun Creek](Lore_Portdun_Creek.md) to the east and [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) to the northwest.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ARMap-24)</sup>
