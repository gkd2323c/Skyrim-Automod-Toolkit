—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Blackrose is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), situated in a lake in the south-central portion of the province.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> Blackrose is a city surrounded in infamy<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHOBP-2)</sup> and contains many secrets. It is said that many have entered its walls but few ever leave it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AR-3)</sup> From antiquity to the modern-day, Blackrose and its environs hosted many different races, from the [Lilmothiit](Lore_Lilmothiit.md) that initially built the city and presided over the southern coast,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup> to the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) that currently lay claim to it.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGEBM-5)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
Blackrose is located on a large lake in south-central Black Marsh, where three rivers converge at once, from the northeast near [Murkwood](Lore_Murkwood.md), the south toward the [Oliis Bay](Lore_Oliis_Bay.md), and west from presumingly eastern [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> The region once hosted other indigenous races to the province, before their death at the hands of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md). The most well-known was the Lilmothiit, a nomadic race of vulpine [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) that generally lived in the region's southern coast. However more obscurely, the native [human](Lore_Man.md) tribes, such as the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) and the [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md) are confirmed to have once lived in the Blackrose region.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGEBM-5)</sup> Despite the similar names, the infamous [Blackrose Prison](Lore_Blackrose_Prison.md) is not part of the city area and is instead a day's march south of it in northern [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHOBP-2)</sup> Blackrose is neighbored by several settlements, including Longmont to the southwest and Rockspring to the northwest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup>

## History
Blackrose's early history has yet to be detailed but many people have lived in the region throughout the years. As stated before, it was the Lilmothiit who discovered the original community and inhabited the land,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup> but the human tribes, the Kothringi and the Yespest have also lived there.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGEBM-5)</sup> Roughly around [1E 1033](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1033), the region hosted a kingdom of bandits led by the infamous [Red Bramman](Lore_Red_Bramman.md), a scourge of the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) and enemy to both the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) and Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup>

The Imperial province of Black Marsh was established after the [Blackwater War](Lore_Blackwater_War.md) in [1E 2837](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2837),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup> and since then, the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) have commandeered the land and built over it. Cities such as Blackrose are built in the Imperial style of architecture but for sometime,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGEBM-5)</sup> ruins of the previous Lilmothiit community remained throughout the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md)'s reign. Those ruins later served as the foundation for [Blackrose Prison](Lore_Blackrose_Prison.md), a complex built by [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md)'s battlemage, [Pelladil Direnni](Lore_Pelladil_Direnni.md) in the early [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHOBP-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup>

In the wake of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), Lilmothiit tribes in nearby Murkmire fled inland toward Blackrose but their fate is unclear.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAMQA-6)</sup> Rumors around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) suggested that Blackrose was in ruins.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSalora-7)</sup> There was a story involving [Pelagius Septim III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) that when he was visited by an Argonian ambassador from Blackrose, he insisted on speaking in grunts and squeaks as that is how he perceived the [Jel](Lore_Jel.md) language.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMOP-8)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), concurrent to Empress [Morihatha Septim](Lore_Morihatha.md)'s reign, Blackrose rebelled against the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and built a large army in retaliation. In response, the Empress sent her [battlemage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Battlemage), [Welloc](Lore_Welloc.md) to defeat them. The magus used vampiric cloud, a highly skilled spell in the School of [Destruction](Lore_Destruction.md) to displace the rebels' strength and skill to their opponents. The sage [Celarus](Lore_Celarus.md) wrote extensively on the subject. His writing was later sourced by the battlemage, Malviser as an example of Welloc's exemplary knowledge in the school of magic.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-RTBS-9)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Blackrose was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Claudios and had a rivalry with [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AR-3)</sup>
