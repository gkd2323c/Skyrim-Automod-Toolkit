[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/SR-item-Spellbreaker.jpg/200px-SR-item-Spellbreaker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Spellbreaker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Spellbreaker.jpg) The Daedric artifact Spellbreaker The heavy shield, Spellbreaker, is one of the most ancient relics of Tamriel. Aside from its historic importance dating from the Battle of Rourken-Shalidor, the Spellbreaker protects its wielder almost completely from any spellcaster. It is said that the Breaker still searches for its original owner, and will not remain the property of any one else for long. For most, possessing Spellbreaker for any time is power enough.

The shield creates a [Ward](Skyrim_Ward.md) when actively blocking that functions identically to any Ward spell found in the school of [Restoration](Skyrim_Restoration.md). It negates up to 50 points of magical damage, be it inflicted instantly or cumulatively over time (being subjected to a spell like [Frostbite](Skyrim_Frostbite.md) or [Flames](Skyrim_Flames.md) will eventually break the Ward). The Ward is reset every time the shield is raised, and the shield doesn't need to be recharged with [Soul Gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md). It can be [improved](Skyrim_Workbench.md) with [dwarven metal ingots](Skyrim_Dwarven_Metal_Ingot.md).

## Notes
- Spellbreaker has made multiple appearances throughout the series; for more information, see the [lore article](Lore_Spellbreaker.md).
- When walking around with this shield equipped, some NPCs will comment on it. Guards will normally say *"Your shield... Dwarf-make, is it not? But yet it seems so much... more."*
- When attempting to block spells that are more powerful than the shield's Ward can withstand, the Ward will break, and the player will stagger. As such, use with caution against very high-level mages.
- Like other Wards, it is particularly useful against Dragon Breath attacks. See: [Wards](Skyrim_Ward.md#Notes).
- The Shield will level [Blocking](Skyrim_Block.md) normally when used against melee attacks, but has no effect on leveling Restoration when used against spells.
- This shield can be very helpful to mages, as shields do not negate the [Alteration](Skyrim_Alteration.md) perk [Mage Armor](Skyrim_Mage_Armor.md).
- During the [First Lessons](Skyrim_First_Lessons.md) at the [College of Winterhold](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(place).md), Tolfdir will accept a block using the Spellbreaker as though you are using a ward.
- Spellbreaker's ward benefits from both the Ward Absorb perk and [Ahzidal's Gauntlets](Skyrim_Ahzidal%27s_Gauntlets_of_Warding.md), both of which allow your wards to absorb a portion of the magicka cost of the spell that hits the ward. - The Elemental Protection perk from the Block skill tree does not affect the strength of Spellbreaker's ward. However, raising the shield will still provide resistance to any spells that do hit the player.
- [Myrwatch](Skyrim_Myrwatch_(place).md)'s gallery<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Myrwatch.md)</sup> contains a special holder for this item.
- The ward created by this shield is between [Lesser Ward](Skyrim_Ward_(spells).md) (40 points) and Steadfast Ward (60 points) in power. The ward also remains for a moment after you lower the shield, before dissipating.
- All other wards are convex, while the ward made by the Spellbreaker is concave, following the shape of the shield. Despite the size of the ward (being much smaller than the other available wards), it will still protect you from all spells, provided they are not cast at ground around your feet and aren't area-of-effect, like [fireball](Skyrim_Fireball.md), which will still damage you. Like all other wards, Spellbreaker's ward will only protect you from frontal assault; it cannot protect you from the sides or back.

## Bugs
- Tempering Spellbreaker does not benefit from any perk, due to the shield having no material keyword. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.27, addresses this issue. The patch allows the [Dwarven Smithing](Skyrim_Dwarven_Smithing.md) perk to affect its tempering.
