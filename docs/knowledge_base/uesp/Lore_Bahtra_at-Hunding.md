—Bahtra-at Hunding<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BAH-1)</sup> Bahtra at-Hunding was an enigmatic [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) adventurer and teacher of combat skills known for her extensive repertoire and a reputation that drew the attention of numerous heads of state.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup>

## History

### Hammerfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg/200px-ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg) Morwha's Bounty *Like the ways of the Ansei, the Sword Saints of old, each discipline requires focus. Drive. Will. Those who choose to perfect these styles are among the greatest adventurers in the world.*
—Bahtra-at Hunding<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BAH-1)</sup> Bahtra hailed from the line of [Hunding](Lore_Hunding.md) and was descended from a [sword saint](Lore_Ansei.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Little is known of Bahtra's early life, but she eventually rose to prominence as a skilled adventurer. According to some Redguards, she honored her ancestral name. She spent time alongside Talia at-Marimah of the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md), studying methods to combat the [undead](Lore_Undead.md) plague. Although she previously also learned [necromantic arts](Lore_Necromancy.md), she refrained from using them in Morwha's Bounty out of respect for her people's traditions.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup>

### Valenwood
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-npc-Gwaering.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Gwaering.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Gwaering.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Gwaering.jpg) Green Lady Gwaering *One final thing, Camoran dear. I've had something of an apprentice here in the grove with me the last few days. Bahtra at-Hunding, a Redguard of considerable potential. She is studying the ways of the warden and sought me out to discuss the intricacies of the Green from "the deep knowledge of the living wilds." Her words, not mine. She's sharp as rose thorns, that one. Do give her your time if she ends up at Elden Root.*
—Green Lady [Gwaering](Lore_Gwaering.md) in her correspondence to King [Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup> Her travels also brought her to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), where she attracted the attention of several notable figures. The [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md) herself briefly discussed the intricacies of [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md) as she studied the arts of the [wardens](Lore_Wardens.md). Gwaering briefly mentioned her to King [Aeradan Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md) in correspondence otherwise focused on the threat the [Dawnway](Lore_Dawnway.md) movement posed to the unity of the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) people.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup>

### Other Travels
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/ON-npc-Prince_Azah_05.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Prince_Azah_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Prince_Azah_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Prince_Azah_05.jpg) Azah, author of Bahtra's dossier [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/ON-furnishing-Subclassing_Banner.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Subclassing_Banner.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Subclassing_Banner.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Subclassing_Banner.jpg) A banner associated with Bahtra *To choose new disciplines, hold them in your mind. The path to learning these new skills begins with but a single step.*
—Bahtra at-Hunding<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SB-3)</sup> She journeyed across Tamriel, finding herself in various places such as [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [Dune](Lore_Dune.md) in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BAH-1)</sup> The enigmatic Redguard also attracted the attention of [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md) of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) and [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md), leader of the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md). She trained with the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) spymaster and spent time alongside Naryu Virian, demonstrating notable talents in bladework and assassination magic,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup> typical for the [nightblades](Lore_Nightblades.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BAH-1)</sup> Bahtra also studied the [arcanist](Lore_Arcanist.md) arts under [Martina Abor](Lore_Martina_Abor.md) and accompanied them on an expedition to the Underweave in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTC-2)</sup>

Her talents drew the attention and even concern of various influential figures across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). At the request of his father, King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), Prince [Azah](Lore_Azah.md), the acting guildmaster of the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) compiled a dossier on her with the assistance of the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md). In the spirit of fostering peaceful collaboration between the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) for the organization of the Stirk Fellowship, Azah shared this dossier with the leaders of the three alliances: King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), Skald-King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md), and Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md).

By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she had mastered a wide range of combat styles and magical arts, capable of wielding and teaching abilities typical of nightblades, [templars](Lore_Templars.md), wardens, [sorcerers](Lore_Sorcerers.md), [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md), arcanists, and [dragonknights](Lore_Dragonknights.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BAH-1)</sup> During her travels, she may have even provided training to [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), whose renowned reputation had caught her attention.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BAH-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Quest-4)</sup>

## Notes
- | [1.](#note_nb1) | Bahtra's surname, at-Hunding, signifies direct descent from the legendary Hunding.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Zoos Correspondence-5)</sup> Historical records mention two renowned sword saints bearing that name: [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) and his son, [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md). While Frandar was known to have only one son, Divad,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DTS-6)</sup> it is unknown if he had any daughters. Divad in turn may have had a daughter named Cinsel.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Early-7)</sup> As such, while Bahtra certainly descended from Frandar, her exact relation to Divad was unclear.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DTS-6)</sup> |
