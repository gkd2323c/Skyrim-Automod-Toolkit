—Philius Dormier<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup> The Impresario was an eccentric, enigmatic, and immortal traveling exotic goods merchant. She was a figure shrouded in mystery. While she took an appearance of an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), there were various speculations about her true nature. Some believed that she might be an obscure [Demi-Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demi-Prince) and/or a [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) assuming mortal form to interact with humans, while she herself implied that she was not of mortal origins.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup> Others thought of her as a just wealthy eccentric whose riches had driven her mad. There were even rumors of her being a [witch](Lore_Witches.md) [conjuring](Lore_Conjuration.md) strange things like [spider](Lore_Spider.md) legs, [lizard](Lore_Lizard.md) eyes, and bubbling cauldrons. She was exclusively recognized as the Impresario, and any endeavor to ascertain her birth name was generally deemed inadvisable due to the potential for unfavorable consequences.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/ON-render-Nenulaure.jpg/200px-ON-render-Nenulaure.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nenulaure.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nenulaure.jpg) Nenulaure, one of the Impresario's employees *Daedra Lords are like a child with their mother’s sword: all the threat and none of the discipline. I should know!*
—The Impresario<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup> The Impresario's history and origins remained a mystery, but what was clear was her constant travel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup> She confessed that she was not mortal and had traveled the world extensively. In the early years of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md), she witnessed a magnificent [Chimeri](Lore_Chimer.md) celebration of Born Anew. This grand event involved the mass-summoning of the spirits of every woman, man, and child who had died since their arrival in the region. Glimmering spirits stretched as far as the eye could see, marching in a magnificent parade that filled the streets.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup>

She once lived in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), although the exact time and specific location within the city where she resided are unknown. She visited remarkable sites and cities across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), including places such as [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) in [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md) in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md), [Vulkhel Guard](Lore_Vulkhel_Guard.md) on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), [Belkarth](Lore_Belkarth.md) in [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), [Alinor](Lore_Alinor.md) on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) in [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) in [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), [Morkul Stronghold](Lore_Morkul_Stronghold.md) in [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), [Gonfalon Bay](Lore_Gonfalon_Bay.md) on [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md) in the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), and [Sunport](Lore_Sunport.md) on [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), leading a roving expedition. The Impresario's eccentricity was striking, often blurring the lines between reality and fantasy. She spoke of the distant past as if it were recent history and the future as if it had already been written,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup> traits associated with Demi-Princes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LTF-4)</sup> Her primary passion was stories and tales of valor, and she was willing to pay generously for them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup>

To fulfill her desire for stories, the Impresario and her entourage offered challenges to adventurers from all over Tamriel. Those who returned with tales of courage and triumph were rewarded with extraordinarily valuable treasures, fit for royalty. Despite this unconventional business model, she never seemed to run out of her arcane wonders, defying financial logic. With the Impresario, every day was a new adventure.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup> She dealt in an abundance of profoundly exotic and [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) goods. Her business ventures led her to various corners of Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup> Among her notable wares were feathers that held the power to summon [Indriks](Lore_Indrik.md), and she also dealt in actual Indriks.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PD-5)</sup> Instead of accepting money, she preferred to trade in deeds and tales, using [Event Tickets](Lore_Currency.md#Event_Tickets) as a form of [currency](Lore_Currency.md). These pink tickets denominated the value of experiences and could be exchanged at the Impresario's traveling stall. The source of her immense wealth was a subject of much speculation at the time.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ET-6)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-render-Breda.jpg/200px-ON-render-Breda.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Breda.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Breda.jpg) Breda collaborated with the Impresario during the New Life Festivities. [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-render-Philius_Dormier.jpg/200px-ON-render-Philius_Dormier.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Philius_Dormier.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Philius_Dormier.jpg) Philius Dormier, one of the Impresario's employees In or before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Impresario hired an Imperial assistant named Philius Dormier.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MTC-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PD-5)</sup> She also collaborated with other individuals, such as models [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) Cullearn, [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) Nadyashana, and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) Zalun, to assist in presenting her wares.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cullearn-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Nadyashana-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Zalun-9)</sup> She also had a close working relationship with Nenulaure, an [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) trader. Nenulaure was entrusted with the care and breeding of her valuable Indriks.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Nenulaure-10)</sup> Calonir, a [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) author, briefly joined Impresario's entourage, staying at her camp to draw inspiration for his upcoming book about the wilderness of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md), Vvardenfell, [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), and [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Calonir-11)</sup> Chanil-Jei, an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md), was another of the individuals who trailed the Impresario's entourage. Although he wasn't officially employed by her, he had been following her expedition for a while. While she didn't explicitly discourage him, she also didn't actively encourage him. He was on a quest for stories, and during the Impresario's stay in Leyawiin, he scoured the area for tales of adventurers.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BOBB-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Chanil-Jei-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOBQ-14)</sup> Gandrinar, a Bosmer scholar specializing in [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) history, was also be found at the Impresario's camp in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), gathering materials for his upcoming book.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RAR-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Gandrinar-16)</sup>

In addition to working with the people she hired, she also collaborated with other factions. During the celebrations of the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md), she partnered with the [Witchmothers](Lore_Witchmothers.md), referring to their leader as a wonderful person who was very giving. Additionally, she formed partnerships with several individuals, including [Breda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breda), a [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) cleric from [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) who organized numerous festivities associated with the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md). She also worked with Predicant Maera to facilitate celebrations during [Pelinal's Midyear Massacre](Lore_Pelinal%27s_Midyear_Massacre.md). She traded goods themed after these festivals and also conducted trade with goods related to other holidays such as the [Jester's Festival](Lore_Jester%27s_Festival.md), and various other celebrations, including those of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md). She also encouraged adventurers to aid the people of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) and fight the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) for the Khajiit of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PD-5)</sup>

The Impresario aimed to captivate the adventurous, the courageous, and the inquisitive. She urged them to step out of their daily routines and embrace novel experiences, featuring a notable instance where she lured adventurers into exploring the icy peaks of Wrothgar, the perilous sands of Craglorn, shimmering sands and majestic coasts of Elsweyr, and the hazards of the ruined Imperial City. Her objective was to enrich the adventure offerings, making them more enticing for those craving excitement.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Book1-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Book2-18)</sup> Philius Dormier served as her representative and rewarded those who took up the challenge.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PD-5)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BSS-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AVTE-20)</sup>

Around 2E 582, the Imperesario was contacted by [Amalien](Lore_Amalien.md) of the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) (in the absence of [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md), known for conducting similar interviews) to answer several questions in an interview about Tamrielic holidays. She briefly discussed her nature and profession, offering insights into [Hollowjack](Lore_Hollowjack.md), the [Legend of the Lovers](Lore_Legend_of_the_Lovers.md) and its links to [Amun-dro](Lore_Amun-dro.md), Imperial customs related to the [Old Life Festival](Lore_Old_Life_Festival.md), [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [Saturalia](Lore_Saturalia.md), ancient Chimeri traditions of [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md), the correlation between Daedric summoning dates and other festivals, and even [Bantam guars](Lore_Bantam_Guar.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup>
