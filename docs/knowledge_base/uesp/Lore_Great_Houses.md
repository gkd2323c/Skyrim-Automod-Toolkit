# Great Houses

*The Great Houses hate each other -- theft, murder, assassinations, secret raids, standard stuff.*
— [Hasphat Antabolis](Lore_Hasphat_Antabolis.md) The Great House traditions derive from ancient [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) clans and tribes, but now function as political parties.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> In modern times, [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) is ruled by the **Grand Council** of five Great Houses: [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), [House Sadras](Lore_House_Sadras.md), [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), presided over by the Lord High Councilor. [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) previously sat on the Council, but lost its seat to House Sadras in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Each House governs an eponymous district on mainland Morrowind; Redoran in the northwest, Sadras (previously Hlaalu) in the western-center and southwest, Telvanni in the northeast, Indoril in the central heartlands and east, and Dres in the southeast. Three of the Houses also had interests in the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Members of each Great House commonly identify themselves by the traditional colors of their House: red for Redoran, yellow for Hlaalu, blue for Indoril, brown for Telvanni, and gray for Dres.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Dres Color-5)</sup> Following a House War, in addition to their existing colors, the troops of all Houses adopted white robes as the standard of [St. Meris](Lore_Meris.md), and refused to shed the blood of their brethren.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lot S-6)</sup>

## Organization
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/LG-cardart-Unite_the_Houses.png/200px-LG-cardart-Unite_the_Houses.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Unite_the_Houses.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Unite_the_Houses.png) The five Great Houses gathering together Dunmer Great House membership is largely a matter of birth and marriage, but outlanders may also become retainers of a Great House, or may be adopted into a Great House. Those seeking to join a Great House are implored to think carefully before choosing, as once one has joined a House, no other House will ever consider them for membership. Loyalty is very important in the Great Houses. When joining, one becomes family, and, by Dunmer standards, it's a matter of blood and kin, and irreversible. Even if one gets kicked out of one house, no other house will have them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Initially an outlander may gain status in a house as an oath-bonded hireling, pledging exclusive loyalty to a single house and forsaking ambitions with all other houses. Later, after faithful service and advancement in lower ranks, an outlander may seek adoption into a Great House. Adoption and advancement to higher ranks in a Great House requires that a Great House councilor stand as sponsor for the candidate's character and loyalty. Finding a councilor to sponsor an outlander often involves performing a great service for the prospective sponsor and the Great House in question.

In times of extreme danger, house councilors may choose to elect a "hortator" who has the ability to make decisions on behalf of the House as a whole. Election of a hortator requires unanimous consent among all councilors. It has been known for a single dissenting councilor to fight a potential hortator candidate in a duel to the death, thus protecting the honor of the councilor if they win or proving the candidate's ability if they lose.

Smaller houses exist, with virtually every Dunmer family technically being its own house or clan. They will often ally themselves with one of the Great Houses, and are known as "client houses".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-POM-8)</sup>

## History

### Origin
The Great Houses have their origin in the ancient customs of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), a self-exiled [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) sect who followed their prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) across Tamriel from the west. They abandoned their former nomadic lifestyle, and divided themselves into political clans,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3M-9)</sup> which would become the Great Houses Indoril, Redoran, Telvanni, Hlaalu, Dres and [Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md). In [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416), following almost two centuries of suppression by the ruling Nords, the Chimeri hero [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) united the squabbling Great Houses as Hortator, also making alliance with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), their traditional enemies, and succeeded in driving the Nords out of the region. Nerevar and the Dwemer leader [Dumac Dwarfking](Lore_Dumac.md) subsequently created the [First Council](Lore_First_Council.md) of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) to jointly rule the area as an independent nation.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Resdayn flourished for a few hundred years until the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) broke out between the secular Dwemer and the orthodox Chimer Houses Indoril, Redoran, Dres, Hlaalu, and Telvanni.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWot FC-11)</sup> The role of the sixth House, House Dagoth, is not fully understood. Some [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) sources claim that they sided with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) in the war,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWot FC-11)</sup> while other sources, including the Apographa of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), claim that they never betrayed their kin.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Whatever the case, House Dagoth was thought exterminated following their defeat at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md). After Nerevar's death, three of his associates from House Indoril,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1M-13)</sup> [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), succeeded to control of the Council, which they renamed the Grand Council of Morrowind.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWot FC-11)</sup>

### Second Era
House Indoril, due to their association with Nerevar and the Tribunal, remained the dominant party in the Grand Council right through until the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1M-13)</sup> Throughout this time, the Grand Council was responsible for local governance in Morrowind, while the land as a whole was ruled indirectly by the Tribunal.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3M-9)</sup> In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), the [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) invaded Morrowind after ravaging Eastern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). After their defeat in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) with the assistance of the Eastern Nords and two legions of [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) Shellbacks,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TSAI-14)</sup> the Great Houses Redoran, Indoril, Hlaalu, and Dres signed the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) with the Nords of Eastern Skyrim and the Argonians of [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) and [Thornmarsh](Lore_Thornmarsh.md), while House Telvanni abstained from the alliance.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PP:C-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ZOSCorrespondence-16)</sup>

The four Great Houses belonging to the Pact donated troops and resources to the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) a decade later, and vied for control of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) while also warring with the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) and the [Greater Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TTot EP-17)</sup>

### Third Era
In [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) was signed between [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and Vivec which saw Morrowind reorganized as a province of the Empire. Hlaalu eagerly accepted this unexpected turn of events, while Redoran, grateful for a graceful excuse to avoid facing the legions unsupported, were the second to accept the treaty, with House Telvanni joining them shortly thereafter. House Indoril and Dres swore they would resist this to death, and the Lord High Councilor of the Grand Council, himself an Indoril, refused to accept the treaty, and refused to step down. He was assassinated, and replaced by a Hlaalu. A number of other Indoril chose to commit suicide.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OM-18)</sup> House Hlaalu, who had advocated accommodation to the Imperials since the [Legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) started massing on the western border, took this opportunity to take over a number of Indoril councils, while House Dres finally agreed with strong provisions protecting the practice of slavery.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OM-18)</sup> Their enthusiastic support of the Empire cemented the Hlaalu as the leading Great House throughout the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Little is known of the Grand Council through the Third Era, but the role of the figurehead Monarch of Morrowind, personally selected by the Emperor, was first filled by Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), a member of clan R'Aathim and thus House Hlaalu.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-IWTB-19)</sup> She was succeeded by her uncle, Athyn Llethan and later her son, King [Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) of House Hlaalu, whom ruled Morrowind at the end of the Third Era. His reformation of the Grand Council was seen by some as a return to more traditional Dunmer ways, although he also managed to persuade both his own House and House Dres to back the abolition of slavery, and carry that policy through the Council.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3M-9)</sup>

Adril Arano, a Second Councilor of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) in the late [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), claimed that the Empire pulled most of its forces out of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) during the Crisis.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DBArano-20)</sup> However, this contradicts the words of [High Chancellor Ocato](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) himself during the Crisis, who stated that Cyrodiil and cities such as Bruma were virtually defenseless precisely because he was both unable and unwilling to pull any troops out of the other provinces.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Ob Ocato-21)</sup> Indeed, the Imperial Province was left almost entirely to its own devices, and no foreign legions were ever found within Cyrodiil for the entirety of the Crisis or its immediate aftermath.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OB-22)</sup> Still, a history of the Raven Rock colony records that the bulk of the Imperial Guard stationed there was recalled to Cyrodiil from the then-Nordic Solstheim, though the author's source for this matter are the Redoran [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of the settlement.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ho RRv1-23)</sup> While House Redoran succeeded in creating an army, and House Telvanni were able to close some of the Oblivion Gates, the Redoran citadel of Ald'ruhn was destroyed by the Daedric invasion<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-HORRVI-24)</sup>, and House Hlaalu was left powerless without the backing of the Empire.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DBArano-20)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Following the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), which obliterated the settlements on Vvardenfell, Morrowind [was invaded by](Lore_Accession_War.md) the Argonians of Black Marsh. House Hlaalu, due to their close ties with the Imperial administration, became the scapegoat for all of the Dunmer people's suffering. As a result, Hlaalu was stripped of its Great House status and dismissed from the Grand Council.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DBArano-20)</sup> House Telvanni, having lost most of their settlements on Vvardenfell to the eruption of Red Mountain, was almost extirpated in the Argonian invasion as well, with at least one report of its destruction when the last mainland settlement also fell to the Argonians. A few survivors escaped in a ship called "The Pride of Tel Vos", which was later found wrecked in Skyrim. The last child of the Telvanni familial line itself was brought up in Black Marsh under the Argonian name of Brand-Shei <sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LTJ-25)</sup>, although some of the House survived to re-found a colony in Solstheim under the aging Master Neloth. Telvanni, in fact, appears to have rebuilt well enough to hold its seat on the Dunmer council, while the previously unknown House Sadras was selected to fill the space left by Hlaalu.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Morvayn-26)</sup>

House Indoril, meanwhile, had been in decline since the end of the Second Era. The downfall of the Tribunal Temple in the closing days of the Third Era, along with the machinations of King Helseth and his Dres allies,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-OBRumors-27)</sup> saw the Great House suffer greatly, culminating in the sack of their capital of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) by the invading Argonians.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DBArano-20)</sup> While House Indoril still technically exists and holds a seat on the Council, the priesthood of the New Temple are now considered one and the same with House Indoril, and those who become priests are now considered to have "joined Indoril". The political power of the Indoril has thus passed entirely into the hands of the New Temple.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TR-28)</sup> House Dres' modern status is ambiguous, though as the most southerly of the Great Houses and the one most involved in the slave trade, it is likely the Argonian Invasion was devastating. However, the Dres have retained their position on the Grand Council, and are thus known to have survived with a significant portion of their power intact.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Morvayn-26)</sup> House Redoran became the eminent power in Morrowind as a result of their distinguished military and domestic leadership during the Oblivion Crisis, Red Year, and Argonian Invasion, taking the position as leaders of the Grand Council. They moved the capital to the Redoran city of [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md) in the wake of their rise to power and the sacking of Mournhold, where the Grand Council meets within the Rootspire.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

## House Wars
By the immemorial customs of Dunmer society, a House may challenge the honor of another House in the person of one of the House's ranking nobles. The ranking noble of the impugned house is *marked for death* -- in ancient times, actually formally marked with a black banner, but in modern times, served with a formal written public announcement. If within the year of the challenge, the marked noble still lives, the challenging house must publicly forgo any further complaint or scandal on the matter.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-MWSavant-30)</sup>

In pre-Tribunal Morrowind, feuding was carried out with open warfare. This was forbidden by the Tribunal,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1M-13)</sup> and only a ranking challenging noble or a [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) assassin could attack the marked noble. Houses routinely engage the Morag Tong, taking out regulated contracts called *Writs*. This custom permitts Houses to hassle one another on a small scale without threatening public peace and rule of law. Codes of behavior under House Wars are complex and subtle, and the Morag Tong specializes in the honorable and legal prosecution of these factional vendettas. House Wars are expensive, dangerous, and disruptive to all Houses involved. On the island of Vvardenfell alone, a dozen or more Marked Challenges were being prosecuted at any one time.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-MWSavant-30)</sup>

## Current Great Houses

### House Redoran
[![MW-banner-House Redoran.png](https://images.uesp.net/1/1c/MW-banner-House_Redoran.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-House_Redoran.png) [![LG-back-House Redoran.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/LG-back-House_Redoran.png/150px-LG-back-House_Redoran.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-back-House_Redoran.png) [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) (also called **Clan Redoran**, the **Red Party of Redoran** or "Red Hats") is one of the five remaining Great Houses of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). Led by the House Archmaster, it governs the Redoran District of northwestern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from the city of [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md), close to the border with [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). In the District of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), the House's council seat was located in [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) as of [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). In the latter, all councilors lived in the hollowed shell of a colossal prehistoric Emperor Crab, in the district known as Ald'ruhn-under-Skar. Other Redoran-dominated towns on the mainland included [Cormaris View](Lore_Cormaris_View.md) and [Silgrad Tower](Lore_Silgrad_Tower.md), as well as [Khuul](Lore_Khuul.md), [Maar Gan](Lore_Maar_Gan.md) and [Ald Velothi](Lore_Ald_Velothi.md) in Vvardenfell.

In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Redoran also controlled the settlements of Balmora, [Suran](Lore_Suran.md), and [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md). The Redoran also maintained a garrison in [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md) and, in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), maintained a strong military presence in the nascent [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md). In later years, prior to its destruction in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), House Redoran would instead hold an eponymous canton in Vivec City. House Redoran holdings on the mainland included Tal'Deic Fortress, the village of [Serkamora](Lore_Serkamora.md), and the [Darkshade Caverns](Lore_Darkshade_Caverns.md) kwama mines. Despite [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) rule, House Redoran Sentinels patrolled the streets of [Old Ebonheart](Lore_Old_Ebonheart.md) during the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md)'s invasion of [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md).

The main focus of House Redoran is maintaining the traditions of the settled Dunmer and, more specifically, the way of the warrior. As a result, House Redoran has long served as Morrowind's de-facto military. Members of House Redoran often believe themselves to be naturally superior to the other Great Houses. Due to the House's emphasis on tradition and piety, the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) was a natural ally, with the majority of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md) hailing from the House. House Redoran maintained strained relationships with the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribes of Vvardenfell, the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), and [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md). They held respect for [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), and were willing to fight alongside House Dres. During the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)'s reign over Morrowind, the Redoran also held mutual respect for the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). They did not hold other [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) institutions (such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), and the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md)) in such high esteem. Like the other Great Houses, House Redoran loathed the cultists of the [Sixth House](Lore_House_Dagoth.md) and the [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) of Vvardenfell, and disapproved of the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md)'s illegal activities.

### House Telvanni
[![MW-banner-House Telvanni.png](https://images.uesp.net/4/41/MW-banner-House_Telvanni.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-House_Telvanni.png) [![LG-back-House Telvanni.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/LG-back-House_Telvanni.png/150px-LG-back-House_Telvanni.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-back-House_Telvanni.png) [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) (also called **Clan Telvanni**, the **Brown Party of Telvanni** or "brown hats") is one of the Great Houses of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It governs the Telvanni District of northeastern Morrowind, including the eastern portion of the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) District from its council seat in [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md), and the coastal settlements of Tel Mothrivra and [Port Telvannis](Lore_Port_Telvannis.md), the latter of which is home of the Great House's main leadership: the Parliament of Bugs. They once controlled strongholds on the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), but no longer have any properties on the mainland as of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). They value talent, knowledge, and initiative. The house is comprised primarily of wizard-lords who have traditionally isolated themselves and protected commoners in their mushroom-hut villages, pursuing wisdom and mastery in solitude, and generally being quite peaceful. The Telvanni don't care about politics or what others think, staying out of matters beyond their lands as to not trouble anyone, and only want to be left alone in peace to do as they wish and live comfortably. The wizard-lords and their mercenaries keep the people secure, and otherwise leave them alone. By bringing prosperity to their tower, honor to the house, and security to kinsmen and retainers, wizard-lords thereby insure the health, wealth, and security of their house and its people. They are called the most xenophobic of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and shun contact with outsiders, preferring to sell bug-musk and tend their herds of giant insects amid the rocky hills and islands of the extreme far-off ends of the province.

Members of the House can rise through the ranks by eliminating or otherwise 'indisposing' other members, and stealing from other Telvanni and living shows that you deserve it. Most Telvanni lords want little more than to be left alone to their research and ambitions, although many did enjoy the friendly company of their neighbors rather than remaining in isolation. As passionate isolationists, they disdain to claim new holdings. A minority of wizards-lords called "rogue Telvanni" are highly ambitious and egocentric, and were known to compete ruthlessly among themselves for arcane mastery and to control and exploit Vvardenfell's resources. The House believed that these young wizards were expendable, and would be less trouble to the rest of the Telvanni if they were off on Vvardenfell island stirring up trouble with the other colonists. House Telvanni publicly denies supporting the rogue settlements.

They neighbor the Indoril District, and through their kinship with the legendary [Tribunes](Lore_Tribunal.md) the [Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) dominated many of their borders on the mainland for over 3,000 years up until the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), resulting in the Telvanni keeping to themselves on the far-off edges of the province. In general, the Telvanni did not interact with other Houses or Guilds, nor did they have many enemies save for Abolitionists and the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and did not intend to be presided over by any other institutions (particularly the Guild of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) and their monopoly). However, the Telvanni themselves were universally disliked or disapproved of by all other factions found in Vvardenfell as late as [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). On the other hand, the Telvanni did not have the usual hatred of vampires that most other factions held. Their lords and Mouths were willing to speak to [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md); wizards did not fear vampires nearly as much as other folk did.

### House Indoril
[![ON-banner-House Indoril.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-banner-House_Indoril.png/90px-ON-banner-House_Indoril.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-House_Indoril.png) [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) (also called **Clan Indoril**, the **Blue Party of Indoril** or "Blue Hats") is one of the five Great Houses of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). It governs the Indoril District in the heartland of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from their historic ancestral capital city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), also the capital of Morrowind until the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). The Indoril District comprises the east-central lands south and east of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), including large swaths of eastern [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) and [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) in the high uplands and fertile river valleys, as well as parts of the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md) and the eastern coast along the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md). They also control a number of islands in the province, such as the lush isle of [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md). An Indoril settlement of note was the port city of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md), next to [Bal Foyen](Lore_Bal_Foyen.md). House Indoril has never claimed land on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), though ancestral tombs for Indoril families may still be found on the isle, such as those of the Aran, [Ieneth](Lore_Ienith.md), Raviro, Salothran, and Verelnim families.

The Indoril were orthodox and conservative supporters of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) during its millennia-long reign over Morrowind. Claiming kinship with the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) themselves and boasting such ancient heroes as the Hortator, [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), House Indoril has dominated Temple authority since time immemorial and controlled the levers of government for 3,000 years. They used their control of the Tribunal priesthood to dominate the other Houses, which accounted for Indoril's preeminence among the five clans, gaining much territory and becoming the largest one. They had an undisputed hegemony over the province throughout the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second Eras](Lore_Second_Era.md), until their unbroken streak of power was finally overthrown during the signing of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md). They have always held prominent positions within the Temple hierarchy, and throughout history the fates of House Indoril and the Temple have been closely linked. Through the Temple, House Indoril's influence was felt across Morrowind, even beyond their traditional borders. As of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), House Indoril is considered one and the same with the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md).

### House Dres
[![ON-banner-House Dres.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-banner-House_Dres.png/90px-ON-banner-House_Dres.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-House_Dres.png) [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) (also called **Clan Dres**) is one of the Great Houses of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), with presence only on the mainland. It governs the Dres District of southeastern Morrowind from its ancestral capital city of [Tear](Lore_Tear.md), also called 'Dres' and noted for its proximity to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The district also included parts of the fertile [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) plain, the southern areas of the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), and bordered the [Indoril District](Lore_House_Indoril.md), [Hlaalu District](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), and the swamps and marshes of the Black Marsh regions of [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) and [Thornmarsh](Lore_Thornmarsh.md). In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Dres also controlled the cities of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) and [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md) in central Morrowind. At least once, House Dres managed to gain a minor foothold in the Telvanni lands of the eponymous peninsula. House Dres has had no recorded settlements on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Despite this, some Dres families lived on the island in ancient times before relocating to the mainland, and some Dres ancestral tombs may still be found on the isle. Among them is the ancient Dreloth clan, a cadet branch of the Great House, as well as the Maren, Othrelas, Serano, and [Velas](Lore_Velas.md) clans. It was formally established as a Great House before the middle of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by Grandmaster [Thalthil Dres](Lore_Thalthil_Dres.md) after a slave-raid over [Thorn](Lore_Thorn.md).

The Dres have a mainly rural but still very wealthy agrarian agricultural society, maintaining vast [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) plantations on the plains and marshes surrounding Tear. The Dres were also the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) who shipped the majority of slaves to the other Great Houses (on par with the Indoril and Telvanni), keeping thousands of captives, mostly [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) in their infamous slave-pens of Tear and the surrounding plantations. The Dres are also known among the Great Houses for maintaining a persistent tradition of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) [worship](Lore_Good_Daedra.md) and [ancestor reverence](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md), their staunch support of unity between the Great Houses, and their emphasis on inter-House relations and the improvement of Dunmeri society as a whole.

### House Sadras
[House Sadras](Lore_House_Sadras.md) is one of the five Great Houses of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). At the beginning of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) and the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) was stripped of its Great House status and removed from the Grand Council of Morrowind. House Sadras, whose prior status and holdings are unknown, was selected to fill the fifth seat on the Council.

## Former Great Houses

### House Hlaalu
[![MW-banner-House Hlaalu.png](https://images.uesp.net/e/e2/MW-banner-House_Hlaalu.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-House_Hlaalu.png) [![LG-back-House Hlaalu.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/LG-back-House_Hlaalu.png/150px-LG-back-House_Hlaalu.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-back-House_Hlaalu.png) [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) (also called **Clan Hlaalu**, the **Yellow Party of Hlaalu** or "Yellow Hats", and formerly **Great House Hlaalu**) was one of the five traditional Great Houses of the settled [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). It governed the Hlaalu District of western-central [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from its ancient capital of [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md), ruled by a leader known as the High Hlaalu. In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Hlaalu's presence on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) was limited to the port town of [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md). By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) the house had expanded exponentially, with its council seat located in the ex-Redoran city of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), and other Hlaalu-run towns including [Suran](Lore_Suran.md) (also a former Redoran possession), [Hla Oad](Lore_Hla_Oad.md) and [Gnaar Mok](Lore_Gnaar_Mok.md). On the mainland, the formerly Dres cities of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) and [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md) were obtained by Hlaalu. Early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), soon after the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) released Morrowind, anti- [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) sentiment and House Hlaalu's association with the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) led to the stripping of its rank as a Great House, and House Hlaalu was removed from the Grand Council of Morrowind. Their position on the council was filled by [House Sadras](Lore_House_Sadras.md).

House Hlaalu could be characterized as adaptable and opportunistic, and any morals they might have had came second to *business*. The House was largely concerned with business and diplomacy, seeking to strengthen ties with at times unconventional allies such as the Empire, and although profit was often a primary objective, traditional Hlaalu practice recognized the value of a good reputation over money. This was their great strength - they were fast talkers and intelligent traders - and their great liability; most Hlaalu were bribable, either with gold or with other favors, with their leaders as no exception. House Hlaalu retainers were also masters of thievery, sneaking, lock-picking, blackmailing and backstabbing, both literal and metaphorical. While some councilors were honest and fair, others were more under-handed and corrupt. However, House Hlaalu's most distinguishing characteristic was its willingness to live in harmony with the other races, setting it apart from the other, occasionally xenophobic, Dunmer Great Houses.

House Hlaalu maintained oftentimes tumultuous relationships with the other factions of Morrowind, prompting their eventual fall from grace. Though not as favored as [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) or [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) retained an overall positive relationship with House Hlaalu. They had less friendly relationships with the rest of the Great Houses, with animosity - and more rarely, outright hostility - existing between themselves and the Indoril, [Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), and Redoran throughout history; though they were formally allied with the latter on at least one occasion. Like the rest of the Great Houses, Hlaalu relations with the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribes were strained, and loathing existed for [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and [Sixth House cultists](Lore_House_Dagoth.md). Of particular note was their relationship with House Indoril, with the longstanding animosity between the Great Houses being particularly noted by Imperial observers. In the Third Era, the Hlaalu retained a positive relationship with the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md) crime syndicate, and most of the Imperial guilds; including the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), and [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). They were less approving of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md).

House Hlaalu adopted the merchant's scale as their heraldry, referencing the source of their power and their pre-eminence in mercantile affairs. They likewise adopted the color yellow as a designation. Their emblem was commonly referred to as the "Golden Scales", and was frequently present on various items associated with the house. Saint [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) the Pilgrim was the patron saint of House Hlaalu.

### House Dagoth
[![MW-banner-House Dagoth.png](https://images.uesp.net/9/9a/MW-banner-House_Dagoth.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-House_Dagoth.png) [![LG-back-House Dagoth.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/LG-back-House_Dagoth.png/150px-LG-back-House_Dagoth.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-back-House_Dagoth.png)'*Pity Dagoth Ur and the Sixth House. All they do, all they are is foul and evil, but they began in brightness and honor, and the cause of their fall was their loyal service to you, Lord Nerevar.*'
— [Peakstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peakstar) [House Dagoth](Lore_House_Dagoth.md) (also called **Clan Dagoth**, the **Sixth House**, **Shadow House**, **Sleeping House**, **Lost House**, **Traitor House**, **House of Lord Dagoth**, the **Tribe Unmourned** or "unmourned house") is the defunct sixth Great House of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) led by [Voryn Dagoth](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), later known as Dagoth Ur. The lost clan's ancient homeland was swallowed up by what is now [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), being the only Great House to have their capital, [Kogoruhn](Lore_Kogoruhn.md), located in the region. In the [First Age](Lore_First_Era.md), House Dagoth betrayed the other Great Houses and allied with the Dwemer during the War of the First Council (though some sources claim they fought against the Dwemer), being branded a traitor house and was destroyed for their treason. All records of the house's existence were destroyed following the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) and its members were either killed or absorbed into the remaining houses.

Unbeknownst to all, Lord Dagoth had survived and spent centuries slowly building his strength and influence from within [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). This culminated with Dagoth's attempt to re-create the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) god-construct [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) in order to overthrow the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) and conquer [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Dagoth Ur was slain and his plans halted with the destruction of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).

### House Dwemer
Little is known of the faction referred to by some as House Dwemer. During the time of the First Council, Resdayn was united by the Chimer hero [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) and the Dwemer king [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md). According to some Imperial texts and Tribunal Apographa, the Dwemer of Resdayn were considered a Great House.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWot FC-11)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-31)</sup> The [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md), [Kragen](Lore_Kragen.md), and various other clans chose to perform an exodus out of Resdayn, referring to the Dwemer who chose to stay as part of the First Council as their Eastern brethren.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONLoad Zthen-32)</sup> Their recognition as a Great House was discredited when the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) came to power.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-WWt DCa GH-33)</sup>

## Historic and Minor Houses
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/MW-concept-Client_House_Armor.jpg/200px-MW-concept-Client_House_Armor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Client_House_Armor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Client_House_Armor.jpg) Standard Client House armor with various badges
### House Drenim and Retheran
Two obscure houses featured in the classical ballad, *[the Battle of Molag Beran](Lore_The_Battle_of_Molag_Beran.md)*, in which, [House Drenim](Lore_House_Drenim.md) defeats House Retheran on the hills of Molag Beran.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TBOMB-34)</sup>

### House Hleran
House Hleran is an ancient house affiliated with House Redoran. Their ancestor, the Ashlander Dranoth Hleran slew the [Emperor Crab](Lore_Emperor_Crab.md) known as [Skar](Lore_Skar.md), whose carcasses served as the central foundation for the city of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md). His weapon of choice was Calderas, also known as the Spear of House Hleran.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONDrelyth-35)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONSpear-36)</sup>

### House Mora
[House Mora](Lore_House_Mora.md) was a prominent house of [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) active as late as the mid-early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) until it was absorbed by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md). Its most well-known members were the [Ra'athim](Lore_Ra%27athim.md) clan, who held lucrative mining fiefs and the High Kingship of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_King)</sup> House Mora's lineage contained traces of [human](Lore_Man.md) blood, but to what degree is unclear.

### House Rendys
House Rendys was a minor House with holdings in the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md). Its only known member was Meln Rendys, also known as Meln the Mouthless, a magister of House Telvanni.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ONRendys-37)</sup>

### House Sotha
House Sotha was a minor House with holdings in the homestead of [Ald Sotha](Lore_Ald_Sotha.md), considered an unremarkable town belonging to an unremarkable family.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-38)</sup> The only known members were [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), a future member of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), and his older sister [Sotha Nall](Lore_Sotha_Nall.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-39)</sup> It is said that [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) rescued Sotha Sil, the only surviving member of the House, from Ald Sotha when the town was destroyed by Mehrunes Dagon.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-40)</sup>

### House Tirethi
House Tirethi is a prestigious noble house affiliated with House Telvanni and relatives to Mistress [Therana](Lore_Therana.md) of [Tel Branora](Lore_Tel_Branora.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONTiras-41)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONRing-42)</sup>
