# Oghma

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-book-Oghma Infinium.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-book-Oghma Infinium.png) The Oghma Infinium *"I am to be married," she said aloud. "Xarxes, is this what I want? Or just what I must do?"
He rose from her book in silence, measuring her worth.
"Marriage joins two as one," he said. He wrote in the dirt with a dark oak staff, creating a river.
"This is one," Xarxes said. He wrote again. The terrain changed, splitting the river in two as it flowed over a newly-formed cliff. He stepped to its edge and beckoned to her.
"Two become one," he said softly in her ear, pointing over the edge at the twin falls joining together in a pool below.
"Should I marry then?"
"One can travel quite far alone, or together. Which do you prefer?"*
— *[Xarxes and Oghma](Lore_Xarxes_and_Oghma.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xarxes-1)</sup> Oghma, **the Wife of All Enigma**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MDC-2)</sup> is the wife of [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes.md), god of ancestry,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vo F-3)</sup> secrets, hidden knowledge,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Curwe-4)</sup> and death.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAX-5)</sup> They are both present in [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) religion.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOFTHE-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOFTWE-7)</sup> According to the Altmeri and Bosmeri tradition she was created by Xarxes from his favorite moments of history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xarxes-1)</sup> She is associated with the Altmeri tradition of keeping records of one's life.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TOATO-8)</sup> A version of Oghma is featured in [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) myth, where she is referred to as **Ooghama**, shield-wife of the Debris.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-9)</sup>

A tale recorded by [Thandelieth](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Thandelieth) presents the conversation between Oghma and Xarxes before they married. In the tale, Oghma asks Xarxes whether she wants or must marry him. He responded that "Marriage joins two as one" and created a river by writing on the ground with his oak staff. Soon afterward he split the river in two and beckoned Oghma to follow him towards the edge of the cliff. Two newly created rivers were becoming one as the twin falls joined together in a pool below the cliff. Upon witnessing that Oghma asked if she should marry. He gave her freedom of choice and said "One can travel quite far alone, or together. Which do you prefer?" Although this tale does not provide an answer whether she agreed to marry him or not,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Xarxes-1)</sup> it is known that she ultimately agreed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vo F-3)</sup>

## Notes
- In Altmeri culture the term "Oghma" is also used to describe a group of journals also known as "everscriven scrolls". According to an ancient Aldmeri text written by Xarxes called the Aldmeriad, everyone possesses the Divine Spark, and thus the record of thy actions is believed to be a sacred duty.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TOATO-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-IA-10)</sup>
- The name of the artifact [Oghma Infinium](Lore_Oghma_Infinium.md) tome comes from Oghma.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Vo F-3)</sup> - [Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson) recalls the origin for the book as having been based off a D&D campaign, where he created a quest to find a great tome called the *Orcus Infinium* authored by the demon Orcus. As "Orcus" was trademarked, the name was changed to "[Oghma](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oghma)", a Celtic deity of wisdom.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TPP-11)</sup>
- [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride), when asked about the lesser-known Altmeri gods, admitted that he had ideas for Oghma that he never got around to developing.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-9)</sup>
