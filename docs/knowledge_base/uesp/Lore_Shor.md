# Shor

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/SR-concept-Fox_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Fox_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Fox_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Fox_Mural.jpg) Stone depiction of "the Fox"Shor is the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) version of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md). He sided with [men](Lore_Man.md) after the creation of the world. Foreign gods (i.e., [elven](Lore_Mer.md) ones) conspired against him to bring about his defeat, dooming him to the "underworld", with his death remembered as bringing about the current world.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dat N-1)</sup> [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) myths depict him as a bloodthirsty warrior king who repeatedly led the Nords to victory over their [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) oppressors. Before his doom, Shor, sometimes also called the **Children's God**, was chief of the gods.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> Dead gods such as Shor and [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md) don't need temples,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-3)</sup><sup>:330</sup> for they have the biggest one of all; a vast hall of drinking and revelry known as the Hall of Valor (Shor's Hall).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-3)</sup><sup>:150</sup> It is believed Shor can be found at [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), an [Aetherial](Lore_Aetherius.md) utopia he built which is open in the afterlife to all Nords who prove their mettle or die valiantly in battle.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SAR-4)</sup> Though dead, ancient Nordic legends speak of Shor's ghost being "sung" back into the world at momentous occasions in Nordic history.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup> Shor is also associated with the **Fox**, one of the ancient [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) animal totems.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dat N-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FAHM-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Furn Anvil-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg/200px-SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg) Shor's Throne in the Hall of Valor There are many ancient Nordic legends of Shor and his compatriots. [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) was his wife and later his widow, [Stuhn](Lore_Stuhn.md) was one of his Shield-thanes, and [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md) died protecting him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> They say he fought [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) on the spirit plane at the beginning of time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup> They also accept "Lorkhan" as the elven name for Shor; the two are widely regarded as the same entity,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TTOD-8)</sup> as is Shezarr (though the reputation of the "Missing Sibling" of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) was watered-down a bit to be less offensive to elven cultures and those they had influenced).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SATD-9)</sup> Shor's also referred to as an "aspect" of Lorkhan.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-WAS-10)</sup>

The Nords believe the chief of the gods had been killed by elven giants in ancient times, and they ripped out Shor's [Heart](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) and used it as a standard to strike fear into the Nords. This worked until [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) "Shouted Some Sense" and the Nords fought back again. Knowing that they were going to lose eventually, the Elven giants hid the Heart of Shor so that the Nords might never have their god back.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup> According to the Nordic tales Shor created the realm of Sovngarde with his [clever magic](Lore_Clever_Magic.md) in the ancient times, before he faded from their world.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRTS-11)</sup>

### Old Knocker
According to [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholars, the Nords believe their old enemy from Atmora, [Orkey](Lore_Orkey.md), fooled them and used foul magic to reduce the Nordic lifespan to six years. Through unknown means, Shor showed up and removed the curse, throwing most of it onto the nearby [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup>

Nordic legend goes into greater detail. During the reign of High King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Orkey summoned Alduin, and nearly every Nord was "eaten down" to six years old by the Time-Eater. The boy Wulfharth pleaded to Shor for help. Shor's ghost then once again fought Alduin on the spirit plane, and he won. Orkey's folk, the Orcs, were ruined. It was by watching this battle in the sky that Wulfharth learned a new use of the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) which allowed him to save his people.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

### Red Mountain

- *"I do not wreak vengeance on the [Dwarves](Lore_Dwemer.md) for the reasons that the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) might believe I do. Nevertheless, it is true that they will die by my hand, and any whoever should side with them."* — Shor<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

During the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), the Nords learned from the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) that the rediscovered Heart of Shor was at the center of that dispute between the Dwemer and the Chimer. The Tongues sang Shor's ghost into the world again, and he gathered an army as he did of old. Then he sucked in the long-strewn ashes of King Wulfharth and remade him to serve as a general. But the Devil of Dagoth petitioned to be that general, too, emphasizing his service in bringing the Nords the information. So Shor kept them both as generals and marched on [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

The Nordic legends differ on what occurred at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) which followed. One story is that the whole thing was a ruse, and as soon as Shor's army arrived at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), the Chimer and Dwemer joined together to attack them. Their sorcerers lifted the mountain and threw it onto Shor, trapping him underneath Red Mountain until the end of time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

The other, "secret" version of the tale is that Shor's Heart really was at the mountain.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup> At Shor's urging, his generals collected more troops of all kinds, even forging an alliance with [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md). Many Nords could not bring themselves to ally with their traditional enemies, even in the face of Red Mountain, and were close to desertion, but an impassioned speech from Wulfharth about the importance of Shor and this war kept Shor's army together. Shor got his Heart back briefly during the battle, but he needed time, and his opponents set upon him wielding [Kagrenac's Tools](Lore_Kagrenac%27s_Tools.md). In the ensuing melee, Shor was wounded by the Dwemer King [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md), who was killed by Dagoth Ur, who was then struck down by Dumac's rage-filled friend, the Chimer Hortator [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md). Shor mortally wounded Nerevar, but Nerevar's trickery allowed him to cut out Shor's Heart, defeating the chief of the gods once again.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

### Legacy
Many important figures throughout history have been attributed with some relationship with Shor. Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) was called the wife of Shor.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Remanada-12)</sup> When Nords saw [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) during the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), covered with elven blood after the first Pogrom, they said that Shor had returned, but Pelinal just spat at their feet for profaning Shor's name.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TSOP4-13)</sup> The unusual success of High King [Gellir](Lore_Gellir.md) in conquering various Dwemeri city-states in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during the First Era was attributed to the blessings of Shor.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TAW-14)</sup> The zealotry of King Wulfharth earned him the moniker "Shor's Tongue".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-5)</sup>

In the dark days after the fall of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md), Shor was demonized by the elven rulers in [Bretony](Lore_Breton.md), where he became known as [Sheor](Lore_Sheor.md), the Bad Man.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Bow of the Fox
[Bow of the Fox](Lore_Bow_of_the_Fox.md), also known as **Bow of Shor**, is a legendary weapon linked to Shor wielded by Njor the Brave, celebrated in the legends and songs of the [Haefriding](Lore_Haefriding.md) [Nords](Lore_Nords.md). According to the legend it manifested from a dream, transforming from the visage of the [fox](Lore_Fox.md) into a bow and a set of arrows. The tales describe the bow as glowing, and gleaming with power.
