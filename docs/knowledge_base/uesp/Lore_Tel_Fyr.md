— [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) Tel Fyr was a remote [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) Tower, located directly south of [Tel Aruhn](Lore_Tel_Aruhn.md) and southwest of [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md), on an island just off the mainland. Unlike most of the other Telvanni towers on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), most of Tel Fyr was built into a large hill, as opposed to being a free-standing organic growth. It has a dock outside the tower to receive boats and visitors.

Its main resident, [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md), was proof of the Telvanni maxim that rank is insignificant next to the power to do whatever one desires. Divayth was not generally addressed as "Lord Fyr" and was not a member of the Council (despite invitation), but he was nevertheless one of the most significant and powerful of the Telvanni Lords. The tower itself was gifted to Fyr by the Telvanni council.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTrial-1)</sup>

Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), as Divayth was expanding his tower of Tel Fyr into the [Abanabi Caves](Lore_Abanabi_Caves.md), a gateway to a part of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), known as the Halls of Fabrication, was discovered. Divayth Fyr and a group of Telvanni mages tried to make their way to the gate but were stopped by the clockwork god's [fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md). Divayth Fyr therefore enlisted the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) to destroy the fabricants and let him explore the Clockwork City. The plan succeeded, and the gate was likely sealed afterwards.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTrial-1)</sup>

In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Divayth Fyr had turned his back on most of the affairs of men. He converted most of the Abanabi Caves into the [Corprusarium](Lore_Corprusarium.md), a place where he experimented with the [Corprus](Lore_Corprus.md) Disease. Whether the Corprusarium was a compassionate endeavor or simply a good means of gaining experimental subjects, it still was a place of value to all factions and segments of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) society who wanted an alternative to simply killing family or companions who contracted the dreaded disease.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWCorprusarium-2)</sup>

It is unknown how Tel Fyr was affected by the cataclysmic [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) and subsequent [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) invasion of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5).
