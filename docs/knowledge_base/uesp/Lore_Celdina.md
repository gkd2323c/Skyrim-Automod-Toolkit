Celdina was a high-ranking member of the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Originally, Celdina served as a sister at the Priory of the Golden Staff, which was dedicated to [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), the God of Magic; however, during Emperor [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md)'s invasion of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) burned the priory and took her captive.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> After Durcorach's defeat, the Reachmen released Celdina, and she joined the Order of the Waking Flame and devoted herself to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> Circa [2E 556](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_556), Mehrunes Dagon commanded Celdina to become the consort of Durcorach's son, [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), and bear him a child, which resulted in the birth of their daughter, [Mairead](Lore_Mairead.md), one of the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-3)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Celdina began constructing the machines known as [cataclysts](Lore_Cataclyst.md) in order to create an army of [Incarnates](Lore_Incarnate.md) for Mehrunes Dagon, which the Prince of Destruction intended to use to conquer [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md) and [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDSCd-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DRd-5)</sup> After the death of the high priest of the Waking Flame, [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md), in 2E 582, Celdina succeeded him as the order's high priest.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOw Mt CSC-6)</sup> Celdina continued her work on the cataclysts, which resulted in the creation of several Incarnates; however, her work drew the attention of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), who killed her in the Waking Flame's base located in [Deadlight](Lore_Deadlight.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DInc-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bo G-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Dea-9)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life
Celdina was born in [2E 525](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_525).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup> When she was young, her parents abandoned her, and she was taken in by devotees of [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), the God of Magic, at the Priory of the Golden Staff, located near the region of [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> Celdina found happiness at the priory, and its members cared for her and educated her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> Celdina eventually took her vows to become a sister.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> During her education, members of the priory taught Cledina about Magnus' disappointment with the flaws introduced to his creation, [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). An old monk even showed her forbidden texts, revealing that some of the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) once sought a tool to unmake Mundus so it could be remade in accordance with Magnus' plan. The forbidden texts confused her, as she viewed the world as sweet and young, and she did not see how it would be merciful to undo it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup>

When Celdina was sixteen in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), Emperor [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) and his horde of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) invaded [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The Reachmen pillaged and burned the priory, slaughtered Celdina's brothers, and seized her sisters before killing them as well. Celdina managed to survive, but the Reachmen took her captive.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> For months, the Reachmen kept her as a drudge in their camp, during which time, Celdina learned what she believed to be the world's true flaw: suffering is all that awaits.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDSCL-10)</sup> After Durcorach's defeat, Celdina's captors released her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup> When she resurfaced, she renounced her vows and resigned from her order.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup>

### Order of the Waking Flame
Celdina wanted vengeance for what had occurred and recalled the story of the Magna Ge and the tool that could unmake what she perceived as the flawed world: [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup> She sailed from [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) to [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) around [2E 544](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_544) and sought out the Dagonist cult known as the [Order of the Waking Flame](Lore_Order_of_the_Waking_Flame.md), which she eventually joined.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> She found truth in the Waking Flame's teachings and came to believe that only through Mehrunes Dagon would the work of Magnus and the hope of the Magna Ge be brought into being.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDSCd-4)</sup>

Circa [2E 556](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_556), Mehrunes Dagon appeared to Celdina in a dream and commanded her to become the consort of Durcorach's son and successor, [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), and bear a child for him.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-3)</sup> In 2E 556 or [557](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_557), she arrived at the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and presented herself to Moricar, who rewarded her with a minor court appointment.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup> Around [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), Celdina became pregnant with Moricar's child, causing her to leave the Imperial Court.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-3)</sup> Later, she gave birth to their daughter Mairead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-3)</sup>

Soon after Mairead's birth, [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md), the High Priest of the Order of the Waking Flame, imbued her with part of Mehrunes Dagon's [egonymic](Lore_Egonymic.md), transforming her into one of the Daedric weapons known as the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOXd-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-EMd-12)</sup> The Prince of Destruction commanded that no one was to know of Mairead's parentage; however, in [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), Celdina informed Vandacia that the father of her daughter was Moricar.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-3)</sup> In 2E 563, Celdina, who had risen to the rank of disastrix in the Waking Flame, was called to the cult's citadel in [Deadlight](Lore_Deadlight.md) to assume responsibility for the novices there.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ltt HP-3)</sup> In Deadlight, Celdina oversaw the training of select novices of the Waking Flame and authored various testimonies, which many of the recruits were required to read.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDSCL-10)</sup> During her time in Deadlight, she attained the titles of doombringer and, subsequently, sister.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCSC-2)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/ON-quest-Deadlight.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Deadlight.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Deadlight.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Deadlight.jpg) Celdina explaining the Last Hope Mural to Mairead Mairead grew up in isolation to develop her powers, and Celdina regularly visited her to remind her of her duty and responsibility. Although Celdina regularly visited Mairead, she did not know that Celdina was her mother.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MDE3-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MDE346-14)</sup> Around [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Celdina removed Mairead from isolation and brought her to Deadlight. There, she informed Mairead that she was her mother, Moricar was her father, and she would finish her training.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Dea-9)</sup>

Celdina explained to Mairead that she would give Magnus the chance to remake his design and that everything depended on her. She also explained to Mairead that Mehrunes Dagon had the power to shatter and remake [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), but he required a spark to summon his cleansing fire from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and she would be that catalyst.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDSCd-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Dea-9)</sup> However, Mairead did not want to be responsible for the destruction of the mortal realm, so Elegian, a former [moth priest](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) and powerful seer, created a mask for her to control her powers and altered it to make everyone forget her, including herself and Celdina.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TAEc-15)</sup> Afterward, Mairead took a portal to [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), where she became known as the Anchorite.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TAEc-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MTCTA-16)</sup>

### Cataclysts and Incarnates
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/LO-render-Sister_Celdina.jpg/200px-LO-render-Sister_Celdina.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-render-Sister_Celdina.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-render-Sister_Celdina.jpg) Celdina with Valkynaz Nokvroz Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Celdina began constructing the machines known as [cataclysts](Lore_Cataclyst.md) to transform [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) into embodiments of disaster called [Incarnates](Lore_Incarnate.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DRd-5)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOASd-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ICM-18)</sup> Mehrunes Dagon desired an army of Incarnates so that he could use them to conquer Nirn.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOASd-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ICM-18)</sup> The Prince of Destruction sent Valkynaz Nokvroz and his subordinates to assist and oversee the project, although the valkynaz had nothing but contempt for Celdina and the Waking Flame.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DRd-5)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOCLd-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESORd-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OVN-21)</sup> Elegian designed the cataclysts under torture, and Celdina and the Waking Flame used his schematics to create them.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOEd-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-DDd-23)</sup>

In 2E 582, High Priest Vandacia was killed, so Celdina succeeded him as the high priest of the Waking Flame.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOw Mt CSC-6)</sup> Celdina worked to make adjustments and improvements with each version of the cataclyst, eventually leading to the construction of the Fargrave cataclyst.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DRd-5)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOASd-17)</sup> After learning that a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) known as [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md) had recovered a portal key, Celdina ordered Doombringer Rizareen to send Devastator Rullen and some cultists to retrieve the device and incapacitate Lyranth with a special enchanted blade that would render her unable to move.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-WFC-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-WFL-25)</sup> However, Lyranth and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) killed Rizareen, Rullen, and the remaining cultists.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-AAS-26)</sup> They then used the portal key to infiltrate the Waking Flame's base in Fargrave, which was the location of the Fargrave cataclyst.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TKat C-27)</sup> At the Waking Flame's base, the Vestige and Lyranth destroyed the Fargrave cataclyst and then escaped.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TKat C-27)</sup>

After the destruction of the Fargrave cataclyst, Celdina and the Waking Flame constructed the Riven Cataclyst at [Fort Grief](Lore_Fort_Grief.md), which was capable of creating viable Incarnates.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ICM-18)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESODd-28)</sup> Early versions of the cataclyst used a single Daedra and transformed them into Incarnates, but the Riven Cataclyst worked by draining the essence of many Daedra to create Incarnates.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESODd-28)</sup> However, the process of creating Incarnates proved to be slow and difficult, which Celdina considered unacceptable, as Mehrunes Dagon desired an army of them, not a mere handful.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ICM-18)</sup> Therefore, she and the Waking Flame worked to make adjustments and improvements to the cataclyst design to increase the success rate of creating Incarnates while reducing their incubation period.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ICM-18)</sup> The large number of subjects that the Riven Cataclyst required to create Incarnates forced Celdina to use captured members of unaligned Dremora clans.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-SCO-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-DUL-30)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/87/ON-quest-The_Celestial_Palanquin_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Celestial_Palanquin_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Celestial_Palanquin_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Celestial_Palanquin_02.jpg) Celdina performing the ritual to destroy Fargrave's Daedra Once she had the ability to create Incarnates, Celdina began working to conquer Fargrave so Mehrunes Dagon could use its portals to invade Nirn.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDSCd-4)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESODl Ld-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOITd-32)</sup> To accomplish this, Celdina had her cultists place votive runes around Fargrave to steal power from the city's mortals, which she intended to use in a ritual to destroy the city's Daedra.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDSCd-4)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESODl Ld-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOITd-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TCP-33)</sup> After the votive runes collected enough power, Celdina attempted to perform the ritual, but the Vestige, Lyranth, and the Anchorite interrupted and destroyed the runes, stopping the ritual and causing Celdina to teleport away.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TCP-33)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/ON-quest-Born_of_Grief.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Born_of_Grief.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Born_of_Grief.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Born_of_Grief.jpg) Celdina at the Riven Cataclyst Following the creation of the Cyclone Incarnate, Celdina took it to Burning Gyre Keep in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) to demonstrate its power to Nokvroz.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DInc-7)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDSCd-4)</sup> During the demonstration, the Vestige, Lyranth, and the Anchorite once again interrupted and destroyed the Cyclone Incarnate.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DInc-7)</sup> The destruction of the Cyclone Incarnate did not deter Celdina, as she began working to create a legion of Incarnates.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDSCd-4)</sup> Unbeknownst to Celdina, Nokvroz was working to construct his own cataclyst because he feared that mortals, rather than Dremora, would bring about Mehrunes Dagon's triumph.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESORd-20)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-DDd-23)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-VJd-34)</sup>

Once Nokvroz felt confident that he could build the Perfected Cataclyst and create the Perfected Incarnate, he ceased working with Celdina. The Vestige, the Anchorite, and Rynkyus of the Bladebearer Clan eventually attacked Fort Grief and fought and destroyed the Storm Incarnate. They also destroyed the Riven Cataclyst, causing Fort Grief to begin collapsing and forcing Celdina to teleport away.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Bo G-8)</sup>

### Retreat to Deadlight and Death
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/ON-quest-Deadlight_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Deadlight_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Deadlight_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Deadlight_04.jpg) Celdina captures the Anchorite Due to the setbacks and defeats, Celdina ordered the Waking Flame to retreat to their citadel in Deadlight to regroup and prepare the next wave of Incarnates.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-St D-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TI-36)</sup> Celdina ordered the severance of all of Deadlight's portals leading to their bases and other realms, except for one.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-St D-35)</sup> The remaining portal was secured by key and cipher and guarded with a potent ward against Daedric beings.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-St D-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TI-36)</sup> The Vestige managed to acquire a portal key and cipher, which they and the Anchorite then used to enter Deadlight.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Dea-9)</sup>

In Deadlight's citadel, they found a shrine where the Anchorite broke the power of her mask, restoring the memories of her and Celdina.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Dea-9)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TAd-37)</sup> Shortly after, Nokvroz and a large group of Dremora successfully broke through the portal to Deadlight by overwhelming and shattering the ward.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESODl Ld-31)</sup> They then initiated an assault on the Waking Flame cultists in Deadlight. After this, the Anchorite confronted Celdina on Deadlight's summit, and Celdina used magic to bind her. The Vestige then fought and killed Celdina, but when they attempted to release the Anchorite, Nokvroz captured her so he could use her blood to fuel his Perfected Cataclyst.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Dea-9)</sup> Nokvroz later used the Anchorite's blood to turn himself into a Perfected Incarnate, but the Vestige killed him shortly afterward, finally ending the threat of the cataclysts and Incarnates.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-AAH-38)</sup>
