[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/ON-place-Firsthold.jpg/200px-ON-place-Firsthold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Firsthold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Firsthold.jpg) Firsthold Rilis XII was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) noble from the early-mid [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). In life, he was the High Kinlord of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) and ruler of the island, [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md). Early in his reign, he was seen as a hero among his people and an instrument to bring peace to the island.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONCirion-1)</sup> Among his most recognized actions at the time was giving [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) the opportunity to start the first [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in his city.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OOTMG-2)</sup> Later in life however, he developed a thirst for power that would eventually bring about his downfall.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONCirion-1)</sup>

## History

### Life as the High Kinlord
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/LG-cardart-Vanus_Galerion.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Vanus_Galerion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Vanus_Galerion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Vanus_Galerion.jpg) Vanus Galerion; First Mage of Firsthold Rilis XII was born the son of High Kinlord Rilis XI and in his youth, he was revered as a hero who could bring peace to the region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONCirion-1)</sup> By the time Rilis XII became the leader of Firsthold, he was an expert in politics and in time, acquired a thirst for power that drove him toward darker pursuits and [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) pacts.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRATMG-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONCirion-1)</sup>

In the early Second Era, Vanus Galerion, a [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) from [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) and student of the Ritemaster, [Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) collected magicians, students, and artificers from across the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), and established a group of like-minded individuals in Firsthold's urban center.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRATMG-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OOTMG-2)</sup> The people feared what would come from this and petitioned Kinlord Rilis XII to do something about it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRATMG-3)</sup>

In [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230), Rilis XII ordained the Charter Conclave, which brought Vanus Galerion before him, Iachesis, and other notable figures across the province and Psijic Order. The meeting was not recorded, much to the dismay of others, and continues to be the source of much speculation and rumors over the years. The Master of Incunabula in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Valaste](Lore_Valaste.md) believed that Rilis XII saw Galerion's order, at the time known as "Galerion's Folly", as a chance to play mages and the common-folk against each other.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRATMG-3)</sup>

Suffice to say, he approved of the order and it would soon evolve into the Mages Guild.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OOTMG-2)</sup> The [Mages Guild Charter](Lore_Mages_Guild_Charter.md) has stated that the guild was created by Vanus Galerion and Rilis XII<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MGC-4)</sup> but Valaste wrote an [essay](Lore_Kinlord_Rilis_and_the_Mages_Guild.md) that debunks this.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-KRATMG-3)</sup> Sometime in [2E 235](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_235), Rilis XII asked the Varmaster of the [Great Orrery](Lore_Great_Orrery.md) in Firsthold about his future greatness. But when the Varmaster told him the Orrery could not [predict](Lore_Sight.md) the future, he dismissed the Varmaster and ordered the Orrery's deconstruction. At the time, it was a large scandal among scholars across Tamriel. Its pieces remained underneath Firsthold Castle for at least two hundred years.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-5)</sup>

Rilis XII has had at least two children. One would become his successor, Rilis XIII, and the other was his bastard child, Sorondil, whose mother, Lady Elanwe was his mistress.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONElanwe-6)</sup> Rilis XII and his family lived in [Castle Rilis](Lore_Castle_Rilis.md), just northeast of Firsthold.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONRoth-7)</sup> In his employment, he had three trusted captains; Arame, Ralimbar, and Taarulae,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONSorondil-8)</sup> and for entertainment, he hired an arcane, yet mad jester named Falarel.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONLisondor-9)</sup> At some point in time, Rilis XII became attracted to the daedra; he would commune with them and practice their [magic](Lore_Magic.md). From the deepest parts of the castle, he committed terrible acts and according to rumors, he bargained with Vanus Galerion for access to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONElanwe-6)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/ON-npc-High_Kinlord_Rilis_04.jpg/200px-ON-npc-High_Kinlord_Rilis_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Kinlord_Rilis_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Kinlord_Rilis_04.jpg) Rilis XII in his daedric form Lady Elanwe would comment after his [death](Lore_Death.md) that Rilis XIII was less scarred by him than their child, Sorondil. She also claimed that back then, she loved him but was not sure later if it was daedric magic that made her think that way.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONElanwe-6)</sup> In a manifesto written while imprisoned, Rilis XII explains that he heard the voice of an entity he called "his master" and considered his life before this, "a life of blindness". He would eventually present these ideas to other nobles but in doing so, he was outright rejected and Rilis XIII sentenced him to prison in [the Banished Cells](Lore_The_Banished_Cells.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONCirion-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MOKR1-10)</sup>

### Imprisonment and Undeath
As the sole prisoner in the dungeon, Rilis XII was initially guarded by a half-dozen guards created from the kinlord's previous personal guard. In an attempt to escape, as he was escorted down, he unleashed a potent [destruction](Lore_Destruction.md) spell that killed a couple of soldiers and fended against the rest. When the second contingent of guards came, Rilis decided to stay in prison.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MOKR2-11)</sup>

Despite his actions, the nobles that put him away did not want to take his life, even if he begged for it. With nothing else to look forward to, Rilis looked to his master and learned magic that would bring him closer to them.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MOKR3-12)</sup> As the decades went on, guards came and went as the kinlord inched closer to death.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MOKR4-13)</sup> Believing that death would not contain him, Rilis XIII created an order of four warriors called the Keepers; champions of Auridon that were trained in magic to solely keep him contained, even after his passing.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONCirion-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MOKR4-13)</sup> Upon his death, Rilis XIII had his [soul](Lore_Soul.md) sealed within a binding stone and it was kept in the deepest part of the prison.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONCirion-1)</sup>

Sometime in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Rilis XII's former jester took residence in Castle Rilis' crypts and received nightly visits from the spirit of his former master. On the day of [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md)'s return to the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), Falarel brought an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) envoy-scholar visiting the court at the time, [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) to see him. The spirit appeared before them and caused a commotion that collapsed the crypts, killing Falarel in the process.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TIEGSI-14)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), daedra had overrun the Banished Cells and freed Rilis from his prison. The four keepers; Areldur, Cirion, Imiril, and Voranil were separated across the prison, and with their backs to the corner, they received outside help from the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md). One by one, each keeper was saved and eventually, they cornered Rilis' spirit and as a means to subdue him, they split his soul into four binding stones, each kept by the four keepers.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONBanished-15)</sup>

Sometime later, Rilis tempted three of the four warriors, Areldur, Imiril, and Voranil with the same daedric magic he wielded and as a way to compete with his power, they took up the same pacts as he did, only to succumb to his influence. Keeper Cirion enlisted the Undaunted once again to defeat his corrupted compatriots and slay Rilis' soul on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). In order to do this, however, Cirion became the host of Rilis' spirit and through this fusion, the High Kinlord transformed into a daedra. The Undaunted defeated Rilis XII and his soul was banished from the living world. With it gone, the Keeper's Oath had been fulfilled and the last four keepers were able to rest in peace.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONPlan-16)</sup>

The significance of Rilis XII allowed him to be documented within the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md) in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). There, copies of various entities were subsequently created as [maligraphies](Lore_Maligraphy.md), creatures composed of vivified ink manifested by stories from books, and serve as mindless extensions of the [Daedric Lord](Lore_Daedric_Lord.md) [Tho'at Replicanum](Lore_Tho%27at_Replicanum.md). A maligraphy copy of Rilis was formed, which was destroyed by an adventurer or two who were aided by augmentations directly from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). The defeated maligraphy of Rilis faded into a puddle of ink.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LAIA-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MM-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Enemies-19)</sup>
