## Fenlords
[Fenlords](Lore_Fenlords.md), also called **Mummy Lords**, are undead [Barsaebic Ayleid](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) who transformed themselves into [mummies](Lore_Mummy.md). These revenants once ruled over parts of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) as necromancer kings in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).

The Barsaebics were [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) worshippers, driven from the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) into exile in Black Marsh following the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md). The Barsaebics continued their practice of [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md), and the Fenlords were no different in that regard. When their slaves rose against them, the Fenlords had them executed and revived as undead. Even in undeath, the Fenlords mistrusted their servants. To guard against rebellion, they inserted [magical](Lore_Magic.md) control icons into the heads of their undead slaves. These mummified servants can still be found guarding their tombs in the [Ayleid ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md#Black_Marsh) of [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md).

The Fenlords eventually disappeared, possibly as a result of [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) persecution. Some instead believe that the Fenlords used the [Crown of Ascension](Lore_Crown_of_Ascension.md) to leave [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) forever. In truth, some Fenlords continue to dwell in the depths of Barsaebic ruins, ruling over their undead servants.

## Fharun Clan
The [Fharun Clan](Lore_Fharun_Clan.md) is a clan of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) that lives in the [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) region of northeastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Clan Fharun is one of the oldest and self-proclaimed strongest clans in the region, and they have the utmost respect for Orcish customs and devotion to [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). The clan and their clanhold are the original namesake of the present-day kingdom and city-state, [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md).

## Fifth Legion
The [Fifth Legion](Lore_Fifth_Legion.md) is one of the many legions that make up the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), the militant arm of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). Renowned for its participation in the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) campaign in [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), the Fifth Legion played a significant role in the Empire's endeavors in that distant land. Fifth Legion Porter, an [alcoholic beverage](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md), was named after them.

## Fighters Guild
The [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), present across most of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), provides a common and, more importantly, public place of training, study, and employment for those of a martial persuasion. The guild is a professional organization chartered by the Emperor to regulate the hiring and training of mercenaries, protect commerce, capture or drive away beasts, and similar security duties. Guild halls can take on a contract from any citizen, provided it does not conflict with the laws or customs of the region.

The Fighters Guild is highly organized in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and is run by a ruling family, the Dontons. They have had numerous rivals over the years, including the dubious [Blackwood Company](Lore_Blackwood_Company.md), and outmaneuvering them has kept the Guild performing at its best. In the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), the Fighters Guild were allies with the [Citadel of Ebonarm](Lore_Citadel_of_Ebonarm.md) and in northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), some Fighters Guild chapters are dedicated holy places to [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md) called Fighter Trainers Guildhalls. The Fighters Guild previously had a presence in Skyrim, at least in the Interregnum, but by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), there were no active chapters. Instead, mercenaries-for-hire were found with the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md).

The Guild gained greater prominence at the beginning of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) under [Uriel I](Lore_Uriel_I.md). Guild Stewards are entrusted with receiving and dispensing contracts to Guild members, hearing complaints about the servicing of said contracts, promotions, and determining the eligibility for re-admittance into the Guild of former members who had stolen from or assaulted other members.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md).

## Firescourge Clan
The [Firescourge Clan](Lore_Firescourge_Clan.md) is a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) clan in service to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). Although the clan had many members, they were counted among the less distinguished of Dagon's servants. Despite the low-ranking of their clan, they were noted to be loyal foot-soldiers in their service to their Prince.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a member of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)'s Antiquarian Circle [Amalien](Lore_Amalien.md) discovered a Firescourge Clan document, that described the Mindcleaver Clan as a hated secret police, deposed by an alliance of smaller clans in the ancient past. In fact, the Firescourge Clan participated in this deposition and was allied with the Ruinblood Clan, which attempted to destroy all evidence of Mindcleaver Clan's existence.

Markyn Ring of Majesty was a relic composed of five pieces associated with various Dremora clans. A Firescourge Band was one of the parts and was speculated to be worn by one of their leaders to show off their rank to the lower castes and their peers.

## Firstmages
The [Firstmages](Lore_Firstmages.md) were a group of powerful mages that resided in [Aetherian Archive](Lore_Aetherian_Archive.md) in [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md). Said to be unparalleled in the arcane arts, they began to occupy the tower in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and it was here that they found the Great Source, which granted the Firstmages long lives. This allowed them to study indefinitely, and many of their magical experiments took place in the archive's upper floors.

When the [Celestial Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md) arrived during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), her very presence destroyed the Great Source. Many of the Firstmages entered her service as guardians and began to worship her, although a small minority avoided the upper floors in fear of their lives. The [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) were made aware of the opening of the Archive, and flocked to the tower in an attempt to be the first to scale it. The Undaunted ascended the tower and fought through the Mage's guardians before defeating the Celestial herself in battle.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/ON-place-Aetherian_Archive_%28Hero%27s_Return%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Aetherian_Archive_%28Hero%27s_Return%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Aetherian_Archive_(Hero%27s_Return).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Aetherian_Archive_(Hero%27s_Return).jpg) Within the Aetherian Archive At some point, the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) had placed arcane wards around the [Aetherian Archive](Lore_Aetherian_Archive.md), the remnant of the "once-Great Source of knowledge and power", guarded by the fractured fraternity of the Firstmages, in order to prevent its misuse. In 2E 582, Brulo Rlane, a wizard trained by [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) himself, went rogue and broke through the wards and entered the tower, seeking to claim the Great Source for his own. As Brulo's actions had the potential to threaten all of Tamriel, Arnure of the Firstmages reached out to Galerion for aid. In an attempt to delay his former master, Brulo opened planar breaches that unleashed [shadowy](Lore_Shadow.md) [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) beings to slow Galerion’s progress. While Galerion began sealing the rifts, he summoned the Vestige to the Archive to battle the Daedra. As the Vestige fought through the summoned forces, including a powerful [Gloam Knight](Lore_Gloam_Knight.md), Galerion confronted and ultimately defeated Brulo in a magical duel. With the danger passed, Galerion remarked that Nirn would sleep peacefully, unaware of how close it had come to disaster. The two quickly departed from the tower, as the power of the Great Source was too much for either the Vestige or Galerion to linger around long.

## Five Companions
The [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md) were a group of adventurers who embarked on a quest to rediscover the lost [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). The Companions were led by Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), who desired to use the Amulet of Kings to rekindle the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) in a ritual aimed at persuading [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to alter his heritage and make him a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md), and thus a legitimately-ordained ruler of the Empire. The other four Companions consisted of some of the greatest fighters and minds of that time. [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md), a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warrior said to have the blood of [giants](Lore_Giant.md), served Varen as his personal bodyguard. [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md), the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) Captain of Varen's [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), was charged with protecting the group. [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), Chancellor of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and Varen's advisor, accompanied Varen in his capacity as [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md). Lastly, there was [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), a legendary necromancer who held considerable influence with Varen and claimed to know how to conduct the Dragonfire ritual.

For years after [Varen's conquest of the Empire](Lore_Varen%27s_Rebellion.md), the Five Companions traveled the land in search of the elusive artifact. In [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), having successfully located the amulet and returned to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), Varen and the Companions initiated the ritual with the Amulet. It was then that Mannimarco revealed his treachery and corrupted the ritual to break the ancient covenant between Akatosh and mortals, triggering a catastrophe which the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) named the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md). A powerful explosion of magical energy swept across [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), setting the plane adrift in the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and rendering it vulnerable to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s plans to merge Nirn and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) in the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).

Varen vanished in the chaos of the Soulburst, allegedly destroyed in the blast. In the immediate aftermath, Sai Sahan managed to secure the Amulet of Kings and fled from the Imperial City to hide it, taking it to the ruins of the city of [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md), the burial site of the [Reman emperors](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). Mannimarco made Lyris Titanborn his captive, and Abnur Tharn, seeking to preserve his status, pledged his loyalty to Mannimarco and had his daughter, [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md), declared Empress Regent in Varen's absence. The Five Companions and Varen's dream of reviving the Reman Empire came to an end, and the world was set on a course for the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).

## Foolkillers Clan
The [Foolkillers Clan](Lore_Foolkillers_Clan.md) is [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) clan in service to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) based in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md).

The Foolkillers were once betrayed by [Deathbringer Clan](Lore_Deathbringer_Clan.md), who rose to prominence in Coldharbour in their place. The [vestiges](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) of the clan were trapped within a set of armor by the [Grievous Twilight](Lore_Grievous_Twilight.md) [Dusk](Lore_Lord_Warden_Dusk.md). A Foolkiller named [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) avoided that fate and swore vengeance upon the Deathbringers. She was later summoned in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and harvested for her essence by the Aldmeri Dominion alchemist Ruuvitar as part of a plot to defeat the Ebonheart Pact, but she managed to break free and return to Coldharbour. Allying herself with a mortal invasion force there, and abandoning Molag Bal, she first worked with them in the Coldharbour prison the Tower of Lies before recruiting the invasion force to assist her in banishing Deathbringer leader Valkynaz Seris, making her the new Valkynaz of the Reaver Citadel. With his death, the Foolkillers returned to Coldharbour and took control of the Pyramid for themselves.

## Forces of Order
The [Forces of Order](Lore_Forces_of_Order.md) are the minions of [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Order. ## Forebears
The [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) is the name given to a social and political group of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). The Warrior Wave of [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), or first invasions of [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) to reach Tamriel, are now known as the Forebears. They cleared the way for their rulers, the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md), and their faction, which is now known as the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md). When the Na-Totambu and the Yokudan nobility came later, [Razul](Lore_Razul.md) made himself Yokeda to cement the power of the Ra Gada, with some calling Yokeda Razul the first Forebear. Forebears tend to be more nomadic in nature, as many of them work as traders and merchants throughout [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), which gives them the most experience when dealing with foreigners. Their symbol of depicts three swords whose blades are crossed and tied together with a golden knot, with a small cape attached to them, while their uniform colors are red and yellow. Some of the major Forebear strongholds include [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md), and [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md).

One of the major differences between the Forebears, as compared to the Crowns, is that they worship the same pantheon as the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) and [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) and take a more liberal approach to tradition and religion. This is one of the biggest factors in why the two don't get along, as the Crowns believe the Forebears engage in what is considered "outrageous, non-traditional practices." For example, the Forebears are believed to secretly support the pariah [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) tribe in their efforts to cleanse the stench of necromancy and undeath, and will even call upon them for aid. The Crowns view this as a major affront to their religious practices, as those who strike down an ancestor are considered unclean and cursed. The Forebears worship of Imperial deities [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), rather than the traditional gods of [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), and [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), plus their willingness to engage with Tamrielic cultures outside their own, is heavily despised by the Crowns faction. This contributes to the hatred the Crowns and Forebears have for one another.

The Crowns and Forebears represented the two halves of a highly stratified [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and fought a bitter civil war during the [Common Era](Lore_Common_Era.md). With the Crowns nearing victory, the Forebears made an alliance with the Empire, allowing them to capture the province; but when it became apparent they were being used, the Forebears joined the Crowns in launching a double rebellion against Imperial leadership, resulting in the signing of the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md). During the Third Era, the Forebears were the most cosmopolitan of the Redguard factions and happily embraced the Empire of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). Near the close of the Third Era, a third faction, the [Lhotunics](Lore_Lhotunics.md), emerged, representing a middle ground. By the Fourth Era, they were at peace with each other, though divisions remain.

## Forsworn
The [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md), also known as the **Madmen of the Reach**, are a faction of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) formed following the Markarth Incident in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176). The Forsworn claim that they are the true sons and daughters of [The Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), but are often considered "two septims short of a pint of ale".

Despite appearing primitive in nature, the Forsworn have proven to be an aggressive political force, which has an astute grasp of insurgency warfare, and even have agents living among the populace of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) in secret. They directly oppose all other major political groups active in the Reach, including the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md). They wish to drive both [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) out of the Reach and attain independence.

## Frostcasters
The [Frostcasters](Lore_Frostcasters.md) are [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) shamans of [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md) known for their mastery of Frost [Magic](Lore_Magic.md). Not all Skaal shamans are Frostcasters; the title is reserved for those who specialize in Frost Magic. They were known to skillfully integrate [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) into their arms and armor, creating decorative pieces.

## Fryse Hags
The [Fryse Hags](Lore_Fryse_Hags.md) are an order of savage [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) witches dedicated to the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), the widow of the god [Shor](Lore_Shor.md). Each hag is a powerful ice mage in her own right, although they have also been known to consort with the undead or use enchanted [silver](Lore_Silver.md) [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md). The hags wander the wilds of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), living outside civilized society and indiscriminately killing any traveler they come across, viewing outsiders as a despised threat to their beliefs. They are known to congregate in ice caves.

## Furriers Guild
The [Furriers Guild](Lore_Furriers_Guild.md) is an officially sanctioned guild. As their name implies, they employ furriers from across the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).

During the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) in [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321), [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) gave his approval for the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md), which sanctioned businesses such as the Furriers Guild. They were hence protected by the Empire of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), even into the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), but would be required to pay them if they wanted to expand their influence. The Empire had subsequently strengthened since the Guild Act was passed.
