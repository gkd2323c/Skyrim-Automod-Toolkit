[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg/200px-ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg) The Earth Forge The Earth Forge is a ruined [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) forge discovered by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and used by their [blacksmiths](Lore_Smithing.md). Its location is a closely guarded secret by the guild,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FJt M-1)</sup> though it was known to some other factions, unbeknownst to them, such as the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GB-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-STK-3)</sup> It lies hidden in the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md) of southwestern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), just north of the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) border, not far away from the city of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). It is accessible from guildhalls across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) via portal magic.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO-4)</sup>

## History
The Earth Forge was built by the Dwemer before [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) as a subterranean complex to harness the intense heat of molten lava found within. They used this heat to forge exceptional metals.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> After the Dwemer's [disappearance](Lore_Disappearance_of_the_Dwarves.md) following the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), the forge fell into ruin and was lost.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Merric-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE2M-7)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the Second Era, a group of Fighters Guild members, including future guildmaster [Merric at-Aswala](Lore_Merric_at-Aswala.md), discovered the ruins.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Merric-6)</sup> The guild established a permanent presence at the site, utilizing the forge and its facilities to create arms.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FJt M-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> However, they could not fully understand the Dwemer machinery and were unable to make repairs.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The guild's master craftsmen were granted access to the forge, and crafting stations were set up for wood carvers and tailors as well to benefit from the fresh air.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FJt M-1)</sup> The creative skills of the guild crafters imbued many of the Earth Forge's crafting sites with a divine spark, transforming the local reality to give crafted items unique attributes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO-4)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Merric at-Aswala used the Earth Forge to turn the [Prismatic Core](Lore_Prismatic_Core.md) into a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) capable of destroying the [Mortuum Vivicus](Lore_Mortuum_Vivicus.md), a powerful weapon created by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Bal summoned his forces from [Abagarlas](Lore_Abagarlas.md) to invade the forge to stop the process, but Merric succeeded in forging the Prismatic Weapon.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> The weapon was ultimately used by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to destroy the Mortuum Vivicus. A meeting of the guild's Council of Province Generals was later held at the Earth Forge, where Merric was appointed guildmaster.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>
