# Moons

[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/27/Mfull_Sfull.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) Masser and Secunda Moons are astronomical objects within [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). They are said to be small [planets](Lore_Planets.md), though this comparison is only defined by mortal comprehension since, as with all astral bodies seen from Nirn, both the moons and dominion planets, they are truly infinite in size and mass, separate [planes of existence](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) that are only perceived as spheres due to mortal mental stress.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Cosmology-1)</sup> According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Co S-2)</sup> Members of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) described them as islands in the sky.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MF-3)</sup>

[Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is generally thought to have two moons: [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md), also known as Jode and Jone respectively. Masser is the larger of the two, being well over twice Secunda's size.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Orrery-4)</sup> According to the Khajiit, there is a [third moon](Lore_Dark_Moon.md), which they consider the corpse of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOShazah-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TMCD-6)</sup> The [Jonelight Path](Lore_Jonelight_Path.md), a realm described as the place between places where the "liminal forces that bind each here to every there" can be accessed and from where any destination can be reached by those who can walk the path once the point where the "liminal of here touches the liminal of there" is reached , is named for the smaller of Nirn's two moons and is described as full of [stars](Lore_Stars.md) and moonlight <sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOKhamira-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-JP-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOKhun-9)</sup>

Due to the two moons, [solar](Lore_Sun.md) [eclipses](Lore_Eclipse.md) can happen in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) several times a year and are known as [Vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) Days.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-ESOTwitter-10)</sup> During eclipses [spirits](Lore_Ghost.md), [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and other [undead](Lore_Undead.md), are said to be encountered more frequently. The phenomenon is sometimes thought to represent the loss of the favor of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and to be possible to undo with diligent prayer to that deity. Tamrielic kings have also been known to sometimes use [time altering](Lore_Time_Magic.md) magical items such as Arcane Hourglasses to fast forward time and undo the phenomenon. Inhabitants of Tamriel can sometimes receive warning of an impending eclipse in the form of [prophetic](Lore_Sight.md) nightmares revolving around it and the subsequent surge of spirits and undead.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-11)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Castles Items-12)</sup> Those born during a rare eclipse of Tamriel's moons are unusually gifted in the magical arts.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TS-13)</sup> There are some stories about [Daedric armor](Lore_Daedric_Equipment.md) that say that it should always be worked on at night, and never during an eclipse.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HAF-14)</sup>

The [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md) once sought to absorb the energies of [Jode's Core](Lore_Jode%27s_Core.md), the divine core of one of the moons, in the hopes of ascending to the sky as a New Moon and [god](Lore_Gods.md) who would be the equal of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOAbnur-15)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOKaalgrontiid-16)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Kat D-17)</sup>

## Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/DF-banner-Sentinel_Crest.png/200px-DF-banner-Sentinel_Crest.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-banner-Sentinel_Crest.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-banner-Sentinel_Crest.png) Sentinel's crest The origins of the Moons are a divisive subject, and it is said no true origin can exist due to the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md)'s [inability](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) to hold a singular narrative.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TLL-18)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONDOL-19)</sup> Despite this, Tamriel shares many cultural myths about the Moons.

### Altmer
Three is one of the Sacred Numbers, which the Altmer recognize as integral to the universe's existence. Three, in particular, represents the Prime [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md), which are embodied by the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md) and the two moons.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TOTSN-20)</sup> According to [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), the Moons personify a "Cloven-Duality", with which Lorkhan Legends often rail against.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TLL-18)</sup>

### Argonian
[Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) uses the phases of the moon to precisely align a calcinator during the alchemical process. During the full moon, the calcinator faces south and aligns with the Southron pole star, and every night after that, the calcinator rotates clockwise one twenty-eighth of the circle. The device has to be placed in a way where the moonlight shines on half of it. During the new moon, the calcinator should be fully exposed to the light.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CT-21)</sup> There is a town in southern [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) called Moonmarch, which was east of [Soulrest](Lore_Soulrest.md) and southwest of [Blackrose](Lore_Blackrose.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ARMarch-22)</sup>

Argonians believe the origins of Masser and Secunda are tied to the fate of Argonian twins Izzik and Tweer, who wandered too deep into the bowels of an ancient [Xanmeer](Lore_Xanmeer.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TTM-23)</sup>

### Breton
The ancient [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) acknowledged a goddess of the moons named Secunda. Their modern-day descendants in the [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors_(barony).md) observe the [Moon Festival](Lore_Moon_Festival.md) on the 8th of [Sun's Dusk](Lore_Sun%27s_Dusk.md) in remembrance of her.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-HOTIB-24)</sup> East of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) is a village called [Moonguard](Lore_Moonguard.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ARGuard-25)</sup> It was also the birthplace of one of the most legendary mages in Breton history, [the Sage](Lore_Gyron_Vardengroet.md). He was also born under a rare eclipse.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TS-13)</sup> The crescent moon was the symbol of King Casimir of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and the Casimir family.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONKareem-26)</sup>

The sect of Bretons known as [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) showed reverence to the moons, who they referred to as [Mara](Lore_Mara.md)'s Tear and [Shandar](Lore_Shandar.md)'s Sorrow.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESODruid King-27)</sup>

According to one Breton myth, the moons were born from the mortals Shandar and Mara. Having grown up in two different warring villages, the two attempted to run away as they were forbidden to marry. The pair were caught and Shandar was imprisoned to await execution, while Mara was scheduled to marry another. Learning of what was to transpire, Mara tried to escape into the night but soon became lost due to the lack of any light and so was kidnapped by an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md). When the village learned of Mara's plight, they allowed Shandar, who'd begged to be allowed to look for her, to go free. Shandar managed to kill the Orc but was given a mortal wound in the process. In grief, Mara cried out to her namesake, the goddess [Mara](Lore_Mara.md), who took pity and raised the two to the sky in the form of the two moons, who would from then on light up the night to help others remain safe from the evils of the world.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MT-28)</sup>

### Temple Zero
[The Lunar Lorkhan](Lore_The_Lunar_Lorkhan.md) is a theory proposed by the [Temple Zero Society](Lore_Temple_Zero_Society.md) on the origin of the Moons.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Masser_and_Secunda-29)</sup> The theory porposes that the Moons originated as two halves of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s "flesh-divinity", cast within the bounds of Nirn at the time of his destruction. It is even said that [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) claims because of this, the moons are a personification of the "Cloven-Duality", a dichotomy that Lorkhan legends often rail against: ideas of good versus evil, being versus nothingness, and so on. The theory asserts that the Moons were thus purposefully set in the night sky as Lorkhan's constant reminder to his mortal issue of their duty. Temple Zero Society further claims that all other '[Heart Stories](Lore_Convention.md)' are mythical degradations of the true origin of the moons.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TLL-18)</sup>

### Dunmer
The [teachings](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md) of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) describe a civilization of grave [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md) on a moon, known as the Parliament of Craters. They were said to hold resentment toward the kings of Nirn.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Sermon16-30)</sup> Vivec also denounces the alienation of the "Cloven-Duality", with a Hammer, referencing the Artaeum view of the Moons.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Sermon32-31)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TLL-18)</sup>

### Khajiit
For the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), the moons have special significance in their culture. In Khajiiti legend, the motion of the moons constructs something called the "Lunar Lattice" or "Ja-Kha'jay" in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md), which protects Mundus from the rest of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-WOCMA-32)</sup> Khajiit are born in differing forms depending on the current phases of the moons. They will often refer to specific moon phases by the Khajiiti form it is connected to.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Moon Dance-33)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONMazurr-34)</sup> See [Khajiiti Morphology](Lore_Khajiit.md#Morphology) for details. Just as Khajiit are the children of Jone and Jode, the [Drom-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md) are children of the Dark Moon.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Shazah-35)</sup> Their influence is stronger when the moons are new and in tall places close to the moons, and to speak of them outside the moonlight risks their corruption.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MBHAYQ-36)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Adarahai-37)</sup> [Forgotten Manes](Lore_Mane.md), those born under the eclipse, called to become champions of the [Pride of Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) are more closely linked to the moons but also at greater risk of corruption.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ONJa.27darri-38)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TPo A-39)</sup>

The Khajiit believe that [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) is responsible for preserving the stability of the world, as part of this role he is said to maintain the steady movement of the moons, [Jode](Lore_Masser.md) and [Jone](Lore_Secunda.md), it is believed that without him they would freeze in place, allowing terrible things to slip through the Lunar Lattice.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-40)</sup>

Pre- [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) myths link a number of [deities](Lore_Gods.md) to the moons. The moons, Jone and Jode, are considered to be stillborn deities born last in the final litter of [Fadomai](Lore_Padomay.md). After their birth, the goddess [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md) is said to have rocked them in the sky and made their eyes appear bright with two divine lanters, so her mother wouldn't realize they'd died until she herself passed. Despite this, the love of Jone and Jode can still be felt by the Khajiit in the form of moonlight and [Moon Sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md) and the twin moons still dance in the sky. Khenarthi is said to relight the divine lanters of the moons when they burn low, while [Hermorah](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) and [Azurah](Lore_Azura.md) help maintain their motions in the sky.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Spirits-41)</sup>

A number of myths describe a strong link between Azura and the moons. In Khajiiti culture, the First Secret given to Azurah by her mother [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md) gives her dominion over the three moons, Jone, Jode,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-WOCMA-32)</sup> and the [Dark Moon](Lore_Dark_Moon.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Three-42)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESOVastarie-43)</sup> She is said to have formed her own realm of [Moonshadow](Lore_Moonshadow.md) out of a sea of moonlight tears she shed while grieving for her mother, and to have lit the fire the funeral pyre of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md) out of the lanters of love and mercy, of Jode and Jone, before scattering his ashes across the Lattice.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Favored-44)</sup> Other myths speak of how Azurah purred across the stars, coaxing the twin moons to part and make way for a sky-guardian, a third [hidden moon](Lore_Dark_Moon.md) which would serve as the shield of the Lunar Lattice, and shine down upon the Khajiit of purest heart and fervent obedience, who'd be known from that point on as the Litter of the Hidden Moon, and be tasked with redeeming the [Dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Three-42)</sup>

After Lorkhaj's death, Azurah made a funeral pyre before the Varliance gate, and lit it with the Twin Lanterns of Jone and Jode.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Spirits-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Favored-44)</sup> Her tears fell upon the pyre and the ashes scattered across the Lattice.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Favored-44)</sup> As a result, it is said Azurah and Khenarthi can call upon the "true spirit" of Lorkhaj to appear.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Spirits-41)</sup> Sometime after the creation of the Khajiiti people, Azurah, knowing that the false Lorkhaj, the Moon Beast, a twisted shade born of the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md) and the first Dro-m'Athra, would one day covet her children, used the Twin Lanterns of Jone and Jode to summon the true spirit of Lorkhaj to be a sky-guardian.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Spirits-41)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Three-42)</sup> As the third moon, Lorkhaj shined his light upon the Khajiit, choosing the purest of heart to be part of the "Litter of the Hidden Moon", to learn the way of the [Moonlight Blade](Lore_Moonlight_Blade.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Three-42)</sup> And from then on, on nights of the Ghost Moon, Azurah opens the Void Gate, and the Moon Beast will challenge mortals until banished.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Spirits-41)</sup> Furthermore, the spirit [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md) is said to wear the [death-shroud](Lore_Ebony_Mail.md) of Lorkhaj these nights, and "wages war beyond the [Lattice](Lore_Lunar_Lattice.md)".<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Spirits-41)</sup> Modern Khajiiti beliefs on the third moon differ from ancient beliefs, with the birth of the Mane being the main purpose of its existence.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESOVastarie-43)</sup> This belief could have existed since at least 1E 2902, with the earliest known mane.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ONShazah Jin-45)</sup> Furthermore, the reasoning for the Dark Moon's existence is different. Modern Khajiit believe that Lorkhaj's body was hurled to the moons, and forced to follow Jone and Jode forever as punishment.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MBHAYQ-36)</sup> Regardless of beliefs, non Khajiit have difficulty seeing the Dead Moon due to their eyes.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MBHAYQ-36)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TMCD-6)</sup> Though initially a dangerous being, it is said that Lorkhaj was ultimately redeemed by Azurah and became protector of the Lunar Lattice, with the Moon Beast prowling the Lattice to protect [souls](Lore_Souls.md) from the [Void](Lore_Void.md) from than on, thus ending its alignment with Namiira. Thus the Hidden Moon is considered the true spirit of Lorkhaj, freed of darkness.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Zerith-46)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Notes Beasts-47)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MBHAYQ-36)</sup> Black pearls are said to bear the blessing of the Hidden Moon, of Lorkhaj, and can be used to imbue lunar artifacts with power, while [Moon Sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md) water bears the blessing of Azurah. The power of the Hidden Moon can be invoked by acolytes of the Order of the Hidden Moon to aid them. Souls are said to take lunar pathways to reach afterlives such as [Azurah's Crossing](Lore_Moonshadow.md), acolytes of the Order of the Hidden Moon have the ability to usher lingering [spirits](Lore_Ghost.md) through such pathways to reach the afterlife beyond.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Zerith-46)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Zerith Abilities-48)</sup>

Pre-ri'Datta Khajiit myths speak of an alliance of the Adversarial Spirits [Molagh](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Merid-Nunda](Lore_Meridia.md) and [Merrunz](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) attacking the Lunar Lattice, only to be repelled by its defenders, Azurah, [Boethra](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Mafala](Lore_Mephala.md) who also receive the aid of [Noctra](Lore_Nocturnal.md). In such myths the Lattice is described as burning spirits that touch it, causing Molagh to fall into the darkness below when pressed against it. It is said that no spirit had ever managed to damage it until Merrunz, released in those myths to serve as a weapon against the Lattice, cracked it with his axe. Though the Adversarial Spirits are repelled, the myths describe this exact conflict and events having repeated many times in the past, so many even the deities involved don't recall the number.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Spirits-41)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Boethra-49)</sup>

Some [Moon-Singer](Lore_Moon-Singers.md) songs link the moons to the origins of the Khajiiti [demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) and hero [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md). In the song , a young Shazeer Firstclaw, who would go on to become the first Clan Mother of the Barrukit kingdom, was cornered by a pride of hungry [senche-lions](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) while climbing on top of Shimmering Rise. She managed to hold them at bay with a stick and some pebbles until nightfall, when tiredness and hunger began to overtake her. Shazeer called to Jone and Jode for aid, and a moonbeam illuminated the rise. Within it stood a young Khajiit of the same age as Shazeer, who informed her that Jone and Jode had sculpted him out of moonlight, clouds, and her own courageous heart and sent him to rescue her. The Khajiit was Khunzar-ri, who proceeded to do as he'd promised.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOKRO-50)</sup>

There is an ancient legend about a master jumper from [Quin'rawl](Lore_Quin%27rawl.md) named [Anahbi](Lore_Anahbi.md), who leapt from Nirn and almost fell into [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) but was caught by Jone and Jode. Anahbi quickly took the light from Jone and leapt for Nirn again. The two moons followed suit and danced across the skies to grab her. When they caught her again, she stole the light from Jode and tried to escape again, but they were able to restrain her. Anahbi promised to never do it again if they returned her home, and as a reminder of her promise, her brow bore speckles of stardust.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ADIM-51)</sup>

The Tale of Dro'Zira, a Khajiit account of the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) describes how [Ra'Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), who had been granted the "roar of Lorkhaj", spoke to the moons and commanded them to "move to their fullness in the sky" to turn a band of warriors from the [Pride of Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) into [senche](Lore_Senche-Cat.md) and strip them of all reason, having them fight at his side, as he could not bring himself to kill them.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Dro-52)</sup>

The story of [Rajhin and the Stone Maiden](Lore_Rajhin_and_the_Stone_Maiden.md) tells of the [Trickster God](Lore_Rajhin.md) preventing a young maiden from committing suicide. Concerned, Rajhin asked her what would drive her to do such a thing, and she explained that her greedy step-father demanded an insane bride-price, and caused the village he ran to seethe with corruption. Seeking to teach the step-father a lesson, Rajhin aided the maiden in a plan that would allow her to be with her beloved. And so the couple met with the step-father to arrange a deal, and offered something greater than the bride-price. It was a great land that can be seen from their village that has no ruler. One that "shines like a pearl in the darkness", can be claimed, but cannot be reached without their agent. The greedy step-father agreed to the terms, which then prompted Rajhin to manifest behind him. As per their agreement, Rajhin quickly seized him and took him to the moon of [Jode](Lore_Jode.md), far from where he could interfere with the couple's relationship. The villagers were initially shocked, but celebrated that their home was freed of the tyrant, and held a wedding ceremony for the couple. During the night, Rajhin manifested once more, for he traveled too fast which caused the step-father to be separated from his shadow, which threatened the newly-weds. Because they could not see it, from their perspective it appeared like Rajhin was reaching for air, and upon the Thief God's explanation, they were relieved and their laughter echoed across the riverbank.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESORSM-53)</sup>

The ancient Khajiit moon priests practiced a rite known as the [Shadow Dance](Lore_Shadow_Dance.md) or "Dance of Shadows", practiced famously by [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](Lore_Anequina_Sharp-Tongue.md) and described as experiencing moonlight in its distilled form in order to "walk paths that took strange angles" to places "tangential to dreams, but never dreaming". Those attuned to the moons in this manner could "hear the moons sing" and could impact the Lunar Lattice, commanding the moons to move in the sky, open portals to other places and realms such as [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), the [Jonelight Path](Lore_Jonelight_Path.md), or the moons themselves, and manipulate sources of lunar energy to perform deeds such as recharging a [Dragonhorn](Lore_Dragonhorn.md). Descendants of the attuned could become linked to the spirits of their ancestor if they became attuned themselves, allowing them to perceive their memories and emotions and to act as vessels for them to act and communicate through should they desire it. Those who gained better understanding of their attunement learned how to "smell" magic and track and identify it by specific "scent", and gained greater mastery of portals, allowing them to open multiple portals in different locations at once and even imbue the portal magic into their blade and use it as a weapon in combat. Gazing into the eyes of a master of the Shadow Dance attuned to the moons was said to allow one to perceive the movement of their soul reflecting that of the moons and grant knowledge of it, the one transmitting such knowledge had to take care that only the reflection of the Lattice was glimpsed by the recipient, as witnessing the true glory and horror of the Lattice had driven many mad, despite this it is said that even the reflection allowed one to glimpse the bastion of existence, such that could not be found elsewhere. It was said that the reason the Dance could impact something as enormous as the Lattice was that, in truth, the Dance and the Lattice were of the same size, the movements of the attuned soul performing the Shadow Dance were a near perfect existence, a chaotic and beautiful movement "swelling with reverberations" of fumes of moonlight and the "great and small vertices", the moons mirrored and perfected that form, and so were themselves changed for a time. It is said that witnessing the Shadow Dance and the Lattice within the soul of the attuned directly could allow for true understanding of the Shadow Dance, though only those who'd undergone lifetimes of study could be shown this without risking madness. Some masters of the Shadow Dance such as Juha-ri Sage of the White Sand, were known to perform one final Shadow Dance at the end of their lives, causing them to collapse dead as their soul journeyed to the [Sands Behind the Stars](Lore_Sands_Behind_the_Stars.md) and leaving behind their temple acolytes. This last Shadow Dance took place within the vault of the Shadow Dance Temple where Anequina Sharp-Tongue herself danced the Shadow Dance.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOKhamira-7)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOCadwell-54)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOKhun-9)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Chronicles-55)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESOCadwell Betrayer-56)</sup>

Becoming [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) required walking the Two-Moon Path, part of that trial took place within a Demiplane located in Jode, which transformed itself to test those that journeyed there through trials which represented the challenges of their real lives.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Shazah-35)</sup>

The Crimson [Torchbug](Lore_Torchbug.md) is said to be the light of Jode given form. These insects dance around Moon-Sugar Cane to bless the crops.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Red Torchbug-57)</sup>

[Moonmont](Lore_Moonmont.md) in southern [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md) is sacred to the Two Moons and considered the "heart of lunar light" by the First Mane, Rid-Thar-ri'Datta.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ONLoad Moonmont-58)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ONRid Thar-59)</sup>

### Maormer
The Maormer have a tale that relates the origins of Masser and Secunda to Maormer twins Chrysina and Ohmirril, who dove deep into cursed [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) waters.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TTM-23)</sup>

### Nord
Nordic myth ties the moons Masser and Secunda to a pair of twins, Filnrig and Rhainna, who became lost inside of a [magical](Lore_Magic.md) blizzard.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TTM-23)</sup>

Members of the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) refer to someone afflicted with [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) as "moon-born".<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-SRArnbjorn-60)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-SRAela-61)</sup>

In the "[Five Songs of King Wulfharth](Lore_Five_Songs_of_King_Wulfharth.md)", Nerevar's dagger, [Keening](Lore_Keening.md) was described as "made of the sound of the shadow of the moons".<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-FSOKW-62)</sup>

### Reachfolk
Certain [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [clans](Lore_Reachfolk_Clans.md), like the [Crow-Eyes](Lore_Crow-Eye_Clan.md), were known to present stones bathed in moonlight as offerings to their spirits to renew the protective wards surrounding their settlements, like [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md). These moonlit rocks symbolized purity.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Ulvoch-63)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-ATS-64)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-MTS-65)</sup> Additionally, [silver](Lore_Silver.md) is referred to as "moon-kissed ore" by the Reachfolk.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-66)</sup> Furthermore, some clans even derive their names from the moon, as seen with the [Black-Moon Clan](Lore_Black-Moon_Clan.md).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-BM-67)</sup> [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) oversees pacts between Reach clans, approving of the deal if the union is worth committing to. The ritual calling upon Hircine to bless a pact involves several components. On occasion, within these rituals, phrases like "Two great packs seek the same full moon. Make them one, bound by common prey" are intoned.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Arana-68)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-BOTR-69)</sup>

### Redguard
The crescent moon is a symbol of cultural significance in the [Kingdom of Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) as it was the banner of Grandee [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md), the one who first founded the [capital city](Lore_Sentinel.md) in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-STJOA-70)</sup> The moon on the banner is known as the White Moon.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-DFSentinel Ending-71)</sup> King [Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md) was also called the White Moon as a nod to this.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-DFCinematic-72)</sup> Before the [Order of the Candle](Lore_Order_of_the_Candle.md) was the [knightly order](Lore_Knights.md) of Sentinel, it was the [Knights of the Moon](Lore_Knights_of_the_Moon.md).<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

The [Aurbical Abacus](Lore_Aurbical_Abacus.md) was a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) relic designed to track the movements of the moons, among other astronomical objects, and calculated the exact change in seasons. It was an artifact associated with [Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md), the god of agriculture, and kept in a tomb in [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md) before it was later destroyed.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-NJHB-73)</sup>

### Bosmer
The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) regard the moons as spirits of fortune, both good and bad.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Vo FBosmer-74)</sup>

### Vampire
The moondial in [Castle Volkihar](Lore_Castle_Volkihar.md) is perhaps the only one in existence. It was originally a sundial built for the previous owners of the castle, but [Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md) of the [Volkihar Clan](Lore_Volkihar.md) persuaded an [elven](Lore_Mer.md) artisan to change images of the sun to phases of the moons.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-SRSerana-75)</sup>

One of Tamriel's most rare and dangerous artifacts is the [Vampiric Ring](Lore_Vampiric_Ring.md). It was created in ancient times by a cult of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Its rarity comes from the fact it only appears every few hundred moon cycles.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TL-76)</sup>

### Werewolf
Some strains of [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) cause the afflicted to transform into a [werewolf](Lore_Bestiary_W.md#Werewolf) under moonlight,<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-BMWW-77)</sup> or specifically a full moon.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-DFWW-78)</sup> Some of these werewolves worship the moons, but this practice has been considered a parody of true lunar faith.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-LBHAYQ-79)</sup>

### Other
Some sailors believe the moons were enormous [atronachs](Lore_Atronach.md), who wrestled for the amusement of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md), which is what causes thunderstorms.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MF-3)</sup>

## Masser
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/Masser_full.jpg/200px-Masser_full.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Masser_full.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Masser_full.jpg) Masser [Masser](Lore_Masser.md), also known as **Jode**, **Mara's Tear** and **Zennji** in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md), is the greater of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)'s two moons and is acknowledged as one of the attendant spirits of the mortal plane. As such, it is both temporal and subject to the bounds of mortality. As with all astral bodies seen from Nirn, the moons and [dominion planets](Lore_Planets.md), Masser is said to actually be a separate [plane](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) in its own right, infinite in size and mass, with its appearance as a sphere being only a visual phenomenon caused by mortal mental stress. Masser has long since perished and its death has led to mortals perceiving its previously pure white and featureless sphere as having its current texture and reddish hue, the moon's "skin" withering away. According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).

## Secunda
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/Secunda_full.jpg/200px-Secunda_full.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Secunda_full.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Secunda_full.jpg) Secunda [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md), also known as **Jone**, **Shandar's Sorrow** (also spelled **Stendarr's Sorrow**) and **Zennrili** in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md), is the lesser of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)'s two moons and acknowledged as one of the attendant spirits of the mortal plane. As such, is both temporal and subject to the bounds of mortality. As with all astral bodies seen from Nirn, the moons and [dominion planets](Lore_Planets.md), Secunda is said to actually be a separate [plane](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) in its own right, infinite in size and mass, with its appearance as a sphere being only a visual phenomenon caused by mortal mental stress. Secunda has long since perished and its death has led to mortals perceiving its previously pure white and featureless sphere as having its current texture and grayish hue, the moon's "skin" withering away. According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).

The [Jonelight Path](Lore_Jonelight_Path.md), a realm described as the place between places where the "liminal forces that bind each here to every there" can be accessed and from where any destination can be reached by those who can walk the path once the point where the "liminal of here touches the liminal of there" is reached , is named for the smaller of Nirn's two moons and is described as full of [stars](Lore_Stars.md) and moonlight

## Dark Moon
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/Maligned_Saligned.jpg/200px-Maligned_Saligned.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Maligned_Saligned.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Maligned_Saligned.jpg) The alignment of Masser and Secunda The [Dark Moon](Lore_Dark_Moon.md) (or **Dark Moons**), also known as the **Dead Moon**, the **Hidden Moon**, the **Hollow Moon**, the **Ghost Moon**, the **True Spirit of Lorkhaj**, and the **Den of Lorkhaj**, is rumored to be the corpse of the Missing God, [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md). Due to the two moons, solar [eclipses](Lore_Eclipse.md) can happen in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) several times a year and are known as [Vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) Days. During a dark eclipse, however, both moons eclipse the sun, and this alignment reveals the "third moon" (or "missing moon"). This event is significant to the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) people's spiritual duality. On the upside, their spiritual leaders known as [Manes](Lore_Mane.md) are born during this occurrence. However, those born under the dark eclipse are sensitive to the moons, and thus are susceptible to the call of the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md), which could turn them into [dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md). The Bent Cats themselves show reverence to the Dark Moon, and are referred to as Children of the Dark Moon. Their steeds, the Rahd-m'Athra, are thought to hail from the Dark Moons. Khajiit deviants may mark themselves with tattoos reminiscent of the dark moon to show their defiance of social conventions.Though initially a dangerous being, it is said that Lorkhaj was ultimately redeemed by Azurah and became protector of the Lunar Lattice, with the Moon Beast prowling the Lattice to protect souls from the Void from than on, thus ending its alignment with Namiira. Thus the Hidden Moon is considered the true spirit of Lorkhaj, freed of darkness.

## Necromancer's Moon
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg/200px-OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg) The Shade of the Revenant appears when the Necromancer's Moon blocks the light of Arkay The [Necromancer's Moon](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md), also known as **the Revenant**, is a moon and the divine body of the God of Worms, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md). It was created by the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417) when Mannimarco used the power of the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md) to fuel his transfiguration and apotheosis.

The Necromancer's Moon eclipses the planet [Arkay](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md) every eight days, preventing its light from reaching Nirn for 24 hours. This celestial phenomenon is known as the Shade of the Revenant, and casts a purple or red light down on certain locations across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during this period. The purpose of this eclipse is to foil the divine laws of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), allowing Mannimarco to be the only god of life and death. The [Cult of the Black Worm](Lore_Cult_of_the_Black_Worm.md) worship Mannimarco as the God of Worms, and construct altars to him at these locations to harness the Shade of the Revenant for use in creating [black soul gems](Lore_Black_Soul_Gem.md).

## Baar Dau
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg) Baar Dau [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) (also known as the **Ministry of Truth**, **Prison Moon**, or **Lie Rock**) was a [meteor](Lore_Meteors.md), celestial rock or moonlet which hovered above [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) in southern [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). According to the prevailing legends, it was flung from the [Void](Lore_Void.md) by either [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) or its own malevolence, but was prevented from crashing into the city by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md). Vivec allowed it to remain looming above the city so that if its people ever ceased to love him, the rock would fall and destroy them. Other lesser-known tales and theories vary widely as to Baar Dau's origins, with one identifying it as a former [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) named **Una**, another insisting that it was an egg from which would one day emerge the [nightmarish offspring](Lore_Monster_Children.md) of Vivec and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), and yet another claiming that it was [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md)'s dung.
