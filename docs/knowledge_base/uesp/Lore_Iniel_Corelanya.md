*You've already lost! While you charged in, we went behind and killed your king! Ha!*
—Kinlady Iniel<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KI-1)</sup> **Kinlady** Iniel Corelanya was the leader of the [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> She was a skilled warrior, adept in combat and capable of wielding lightning magic, who fought against the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) colonists.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KI-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup> She was also an intrepid explorer and bold conqueror, driven by the pursuit of rich lands for her clan's merchants to exploit.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup>

## History

### Kinheir
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg/200px-ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Western_Solstice.jpg) Solstice Iniel was born in [1E 667](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_667) to Kinlord [Roliondil](Lore_Roliondil_Corelanya.md). She was [Sundavar](Lore_Sundavar_Corelanya.md)'s great-granddaughter and her father's kinheir. She grew up to be an intrepid explorer and bold conqueror, determined to expand the dominions she was destined to inherit. From hidden havens along [Cape Shira](Lore_Cape_Shira.md), Corelanya galleys set sail in search of prosperous lands for their merchants to exploit. Few ventured farther or returned with greater wealth than Iniel herself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup>

In [1E 723](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_723), Iniel landed on the distant island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) and encountered its [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) civilization for the first time. Three years later, in [1E 726](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_726) she returned with a powerful fleet determined to subjugate the island. Iniel was ultimately unsuccessful as the Stone-Nest tribe rallied against her invasion and forced her forces to retreat. However, during her ill-fated campaign, Iniel discovered the dark citadel of Mor Naril, an abandoned [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) temple city, and plundered some of its [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) secrets.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> Iniel's discovery in Mor Naril caused division in House Corelanya. Some rejected the darker Daedric Princes, while others were willing to seek their aid.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HOCC5-4)</sup>

Ultimately, a small Corelanya outpost endured on the island. Although Iniel never succeeded in subduing the native Argonians, she established the foundations of a fortified haven on Solstice. Corelanya merchant ships continued to visit the island from time to time, trading with the cautious and watchful Argonian inhabitants.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HOCC4-5)</sup> [Tuniria](Lore_Tuniria.md) was one of the earliest Corelanyan settlements established on Solstice, though the exact nature of Iniel's connection to it is unclear.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Morlia-6)</sup>

### Kinlady
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/ON-load-Ashaba_Pass.jpg/200px-ON-load-Ashaba_Pass.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Ashaba_Pass.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Ashaba_Pass.jpg) Ash'abah Pass Shortly after returning to Hammerfell, Iniel ascended to the position of kinlady.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> She led the Corelanya Clan during the ninth century of the First Era.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KI-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PH-7)</sup> Iniel's rule was known for its strength and uncompromising nature, but her authoritarian style estranged many of Clan Corelanya's allies. Rumors also circulated regarding her dealings with darker Daedric patrons. In [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808), the Corelanyas faced a formidable new threat at Cape Shira, one that demanded more support than Iniel had.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> The third wave of the Yokudan Ra Gada, led by Grandee [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md), sailed from the Yath and founded the realm of Sentinel, establishing a powerful presence right on the doorstep of [Salas En](Lore_Salas_En.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ASEE-8)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> This warrior wave of [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md), known as the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), landed on the shores of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE-9)</sup> With the wars against the [Lefthanded Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md) in Yokuda still fresh in their minds, they set out to eradicate the Corelanya Elves wherever they found them.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AP-10)</sup> A Yokudan colony from the island of [Yath](Lore_Yath.md) was largely responsible for the attacks on the Corelanya, eventually displacing them from the site.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ASEE-8)</sup>

Iniel showed no willingness to accommodate her new neighbors, just as the Ra Gada were unwilling to overlook a kingdom of Daedra-worshipping mer in lands they sought to claim. For years, the Corelanyas protected the approaches to Salas En through powerful magic and covert raids. Ra Gada forces were hindered by sandstorms, tricked by mirages, and attacked by summoned Daedra. Although the proud warriors of Sentinel came to despise the "accursed city," the Corelanya defenses held strong for many years.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup>

By [1E 853](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_853), the Yokudans were led by King [Xakhwan](Lore_Xakhwan.md), who in a final push to drive the Corelanyas from Hammerfell, sent his two sons to kill their leader.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PH-7)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> Corelanya illusions and stealth could not withstand such a direct and overwhelming assault. In desperation, Kinlady Iniel unleashed her darkest and most feared magic, the necromancy she had discovered in Mor Naril during her youth. She invoked [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s power, raising the fallen warriors to fight repeatedly in her defense.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup>

Princes Haqmir and Sameer led the charge into a valley known as the Ash'abah Pass, and killed Iniel during the battle. However, she tricked them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KI-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup> The Corelanyas deceived the Yokudans into leaving King Xakhwan vulnerable. She had the most powerful of her [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) [Venerien](Lore_Venerien.md) infiltrate the king's palace, kill him,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> and then reanimate his corpse to use against the Yokudans.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VY-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-WV-12)</sup> Yet even this desperate tactic could not save Salas En. Iniel was slain, and her city was laid to waste. Only a few of her compatriots managed to escaped the destruction of Salas En.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup>

### Legacy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/ON-load-Solstice.png/200px-ON-load-Solstice.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Solstice.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Solstice.png) Sunport In the months following Kinlady Iniel's defeat, Ra Gada warbands swept across Hammerfell, hunting down Corelanya havens and slaughtering all who were found. No quarter was asked for or given. Although Iniel left no direct heirs, her cousin [Earlenque Corelanya](Lore_Earlenque_Corelanya.md) rose to assume the mantle of leadership. Stories of Kinlady Iniel's Daedric alliances and blatant use of necromancy followed the Corelanyas back to Summerset, severely damaging their standing with High King [Naurnindon](Lore_Naurnindon.md), which eventually led to their exodus to Solstice, the island Iniel discovered.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HOCC3-13)</sup>

As late as [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), carved [amethyst](Lore_Amethyst.md) intaglios depicting Iniel fighting the sons of Xakhwan could be found on the isle of Solstice.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TK-14)</sup> Iniel features prominently in *[History of Clan Corelanya](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:History_of_Clan_Corelanya)*, authored by Regent [Karinwe](Lore_Karinwe_Corelanya.md), the leader of Clan Corelanya during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOCC-2)</sup> Some say that a pristine sail from the first ship that carried the first Altmer to Solstice remained intact and was preserved even as late as 2E 582.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PCS-15)</sup>

That same year, a vision of Iniel's demise was revealed to [an adventurer](Lore_The_Vestige.md) defending the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) from a necromantic cult.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TI-3)</sup>
