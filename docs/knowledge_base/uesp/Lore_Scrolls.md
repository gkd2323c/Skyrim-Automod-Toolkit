# Scrolls

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg/200px-BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg) Scroll of Revival (Blades) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/ON-item-Scroll_of_Glorious_Battle.jpg/200px-ON-item-Scroll_of_Glorious_Battle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Scroll_of_Glorious_Battle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Scroll_of_Glorious_Battle.jpg) Scroll of Glorious Battle (ESO) Scrolls,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Scroll M-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Scroll O-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Scroll S-3)</sup> also known as **Magic Scrolls**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWEnchanter-4)</sup> and **Magical Scrolls**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWEnchant-5)</sup> are enchanted items that allow usage of magic for people without magical abilities. They are considered among the simplest forms of [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md) and are imbued with a single enchantment and are destroyed with one use. Anyone can use magic scrolls to cast a single spell with perfect reliability.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWEnchant-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWEnchanter-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWMT-6)</sup> Despite typically containing the text in the [Daedric Alphabet](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Scroll M-1)</sup> the ability to read and write is not required to craft magical scrolls, provided the enchanter possess strong force of will. It is implied, however, that this ability is beneficial for the scrolls' quality.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Hroltar-7)</sup> Magic scrolls are occasionally created with the help of the formulas,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SS-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EN-9)</sup> which were occasionally encoded, to protect them from thieves.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PNDNR-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-NTA-11)</sup> Some scrolls are of the Daedric origins.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gavros-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ebony_Epidermis-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LCL-14)</sup>

The [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) used the Scrolls of Glorious Battle to empower their foes with great strength and power<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SOGB-15)</sup> in order to make their deeds even more legendary.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-GTR-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MAR-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-UCB-18)</sup> Those scrolls were devised by [Hroltar the Boaster](Lore_Hroltar.md) at some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Hroltar-7)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SOGB-15)</sup>

Within the school of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md), scrolls of summoning are all based on the Law of Similarity. Typically scrolls used to conjure creatures use parts of the creature, such as their skin for summoning the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). At some point, circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) [Milyn Faram](Lore_Milyn_Faram.md) devised a new method based on a recovered [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [machine](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md), that instead relied on appearance of the conjured being. His scrolls only contained an image of the Daedra, rather than the body part, which worked almost as well as a Daedra skin or heart. Although less effective, his scrolls were available at a fraction of the cost of the scrolls crafted with the usage of the older methods.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MF-19)</sup> Some summoning scrolls are not destroyed after being used, but the creature conjured by them won't obey the user if the invocation is pronounced improperly.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AAS-20)</sup>

Scrolls are occasionally used to test the new spells.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-JZ-21)</sup> They can be used to replicate standard magic effects, such as to cure simple diseases, fly,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWGC-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MWTF-23)</sup> tame animals,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-GTTDW-24)</sup> and, in certain cases even cheat death.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Blades-25)</sup>

For the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), time is said to be relative, and thus her priestesses have been known to be able to craft the scrolls that grant time-altering blessings.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

Scrolls are sold by traders and used for a wide variety of purposes. Common examples include Vigor and Vitality scrolls, which restore stamina and health, respectively. Others such as Almsivi Intervention and Divine Intervention are used for [transportation](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) and are useful for both routine travel and emergencies. Scrolls like Heartwise aid in persuasion, while certain [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) scrolls are also available, including those capable of summoning skeletal minions. Strictly combat oriented scrolls also are sold, such as Drathis' Winter Guest.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Arrille-27)</sup> During the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md), [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) sells magical scrolls at a discounted price.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-WFDF-28)</sup>

Certain magical scrolls operate differently and are not destroyed upon use, and are instead pieces of paper imbued with permanent enchantment.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONContr-29)</sup>

The Never-Ending Scroll was a dangerous relic kept by the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), that could enthrall whoever writes upon it, forcing them to continuously write until they fall dead of exhaustion.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TNES-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ROS-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-PCLODD-32)</sup>

[The Bothdorji Scroll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Bothdorji_Scroll) was a rare scroll protected by the powerful daedra spirits and had the power to summon creatures from the pale of Oblivion.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-BI-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup>

Some scrolls are enchanted with a ward against moisture that protects them from spilling, which makes them incredibly impractical for most conventional forms of note-taking.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONContr-29)</sup>

[Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) are unrelated to the mundane enchanted scrolls. They are scrolls of unknown origin that simultaneously archive both past and future events.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LHOT-35)</sup>

The Scrolls of Baan Dar is a series of vellum scrolls found by a nomad wanderer in a cave somewhere on [Lake Vread](Lore_Lake_Vread.md) in central [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). These scrolls were then painstakingly translated from [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md) to [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md) over the years by many scholars, such as the Second Era scribe [Arkan](Lore_Arkan.md) and his apprentice, Jarvus. No one could verify the authenticity of the First Scroll's translation or the text itself.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TFSOBD-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONFSOBD-37)</sup> The Third Scroll's translation is a rare piece of text that is stored in the Library of Dusk in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). It describes the tale of how Baan Dar stole [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s [thirty-seventh lesson](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_37.md) before they could write it.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TLODRB-38)</sup>
