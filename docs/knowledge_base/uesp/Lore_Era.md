# History


## Eras

### [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg/200px-ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) The Adamantine Tower The **Dawn Era**, also known as the **Beginning Times**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOWH-3)</sup> was a highly magical period during which time followed an incomprehensible, non-linear path and the very laws of nature remained unset, making a timeline an artificial fabrication. The Cosmos were created from the Aurbis and time began. The gods, known as et'Ada, formed and created the mortal plane, Nirn. Conflict was simultaneously a mere ideological difference of opinion and a manifest war. This "War of Manifest Metaphors" renders the era mostly incapable of supporting a narrative. Since fixed dates during this time are unknowable, there is no specific nomenclature for years within the Dawn Era. The Dawn Era ended with the exodus of the gods and magic from Nirn at the Adamantine Tower, at an event known as [Convention](Lore_Convention.md).

### [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md)
**ME 2500 — ME 1** The **Merethic Era**, also known as the **Mythic Era**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAA-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3All-6)</sup> and the **Era of Myths**<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMalana-7)</sup>, was a prehistoric period of time after the exodus of the gods and the subsequent supremacy of Elves in Tamriel. Indeed the term "Merethic" comes from the old Nordic language, literally the "Era of the Elves." The Merethic Era is numbered in reverse order, extending from ME 2500 (the date of construction of the Adamantine Tower) in the distant past to ME 1 (the year before the founding of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md)).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BTAOM-8)</sup>

During the early Merethic Era, the aboriginal beastfolk ancestors of the Khajiit and Argonians lived in preliterate communities throughout Tamriel. The first Elves, known as the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), left their doomed and now-lost continent of [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) and settled in southwestern Tamriel, before spreading across the rest of the continent and splitting into [distinct racial groups](Lore_Mer.md). Some of these Elves later went on to establish [empires](Lore_Empires.md) and create the powerful metaphysical [Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md) that exist across Tamriel.

Humans from Atmora began migrating to Tamriel as early as ME 1000, and built settlements in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). These "[Nedic peoples](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede)" were a minority in a land of Elves, and had no choice but to live peacefully with the various Elven races. Towards the late Merethic Era, the Atmoran hero [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) led a great colonizing fleet to Tamriel and settled in the northern lands, later known as [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Ysgramor didn't submit to the authority of the Elves and ultimately this led to a mass slaughter known as the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md). Ysgramor's retaliation changed the face of Tamriel as he waged war against the Elves, which led to the weakening of their rule.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FC-9)</sup> Eventually Ysgramor exterminated the Elves of Skyrim and established [his kingdom](Lore_Ysgramor_Dynasty.md). Around this time the authority of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) took hold and [Dragons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragons) terrorized Skyrim for an age, before they were eventually overthrown during the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) in the closing years of the Merethic Era.

### [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md)
**[1E 0](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_0) — [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920)** [![ON-misc-Alessian Empire Emblem.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/ON-misc-Alessian_Empire_Emblem.png/200px-ON-misc-Alessian_Empire_Emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Alessian_Empire_Emblem.png) The **First Era**, sometimes referred to as the **First Age**,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWEnnbjof-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HA-11)</sup> can be characterised by the rise and expansion of numerous human [empires](Lore_Empires.md), and the decline of Elven authority across Tamriel. In the early years, the Nords of Skyrim established one of the [first great empires](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) under King Vrage, who initiated multiple military expansions that pushed past the borders of Skyrim and into High Rock and Morrowind.

Around the same time, the human slaves of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) rose up under the leadership of the Slave-Queen [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and rebelled against their Elven overlords. Alongside the heroes Pelinal Whitestrake and Morihaus, Alessia would continue to liberate slaves and fight Daedric armies, culminating in the Ayleid's defeat at the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and the establishment of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TA-12)</sup> While the Ayleid empire fell, the Direnni elves saw a brief rise to power, becoming a formidable economic and military power in High Rock. The [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md) also welcomed many fleeing Ayleids, who migrated to High Rock to escape persecution in an exodus known as the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md). While the Direnni Hegemony managed to conquer High Rock and even some parts of Skyrim and Hammerfell, a war with the Alessian Empire weakened their power in High Rock. In the following years the Direnni could no longer challenge the emerging [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobility, who seized power throughout most of High Rock.

The First Era also saw [conflict erupt](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md) between the once-allied [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) of Morrowind. The war between the two Elven races concluded at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), where upon the entire Dwemer race mysteriously disappeared. These events paved the way for the formation of the Tribunal, which would last for centuries thereafter. After the continent of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) sank into the ocean, the Yokudans survivors migrated to western Tamriel. The fearsome [warrior wave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) drove the elves, [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) and [Nedic](Lore_Nedic.md) peoples from [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and claimed the deserts as their new homeland.

The early-to-middle part of the First Era became known as the "Classical Period".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HR-13)</sup>, and it was after this time a phenomenon known as a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) took place across much of Tamriel for one thousand and eight years. During this time, linear time was broken and became non-linear. When linear time resumed, one of the first recorded events was the destructive [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) that devastated more than half of Tamriel's population. After it was discovered the plague was released by the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md), an armada of ships known as the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) was built and waged war upon the Sload, resulting in the destruction of their homeland.

Following years of internal strife within the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order)'s bloated priesthood and territorial movements to curtail or outlaw the Order's religion, the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) broke out. Western Cyrodiil isolated itself from the Alessian Empire and establishes the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md), and after a decade of conflict, the war concludes and the Alessian Order and Empire are dissolved. The Empire's former territories solidify their independence, and its heartland, Cyrodiil, splits into Eastern and Western halves that continue to diverge culturally over the following centuries.

Eventually, Cyrodiil was reunified when [Reman I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_I) established the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) after defeating an invading [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) army. The Reman Conquests saw Reman expand his empire to conquer almost all of Tamriel, save for [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). His son, Emperor [Reman II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II) attempted to claim Morrowind during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), but died in battle. The war was continued by his successors [Brazollus Dor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brazollus_Dor) and [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III), however Reman III and his son and heir Prince [Juilek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Juilek) were both assassinated by the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920). Akaviri Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie) assumed the Imperial Throne and ratified the [Cervant Truce](Lore_Cervant_Truce.md), which ended the disastrous Four Score War with Morrowind and ushered in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

### [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md)
**[2E 1](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_1) — [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896)** [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/ON-map-Tamriel.jpg/200px-ON-map-Tamriel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Tamriel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Tamriel.jpg) A divided Tamriel during the Interregnum The **Second Era**, sometimes referred to as the **Common Era**,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE1C-14)</sup> was a tumultuous period of time as power shifted multiple times across numerous conflicts. The era began with the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) ruling the vast [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie) sat on the throne until his assassination in the fourth century and was succeeded by his son Savirien-Chorak, who suffered the same fate a century later. The death of the Akaviri Potentate brought about the Second Empire's dissolution and in turn heralded a period of conflict known as the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).

Tamriel's provinces reasserted their independence, and the remnants of the Second Empire nominally survived as the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), a rump state confined to Cyrodiil that was ruled by a succession of warlords. The geopolitical effects of the Interregnum were both far-reaching and varied. For example, [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), an area with an extensive history of factionalism, was more able to deal with the collapse of central power and fared much better than the rest of Tamriel, lapsing back into its usual structure of kingdoms and city states without difficulty.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-16)</sup> In contrast, [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) was divided as never before when it split into two kingdoms, Eastern Skyrim and Western Skyrim, following the assassination of High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) and a succession dispute in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TCOF-17)</sup> and then lost control of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-OOS-18)</sup> One notable Reachman was [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) the Black Drake, who rose to power and conquered Cyrodiil. Naming himself emperor, he established a dynasty known as the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) and reinforced his connection and commitment to the Empire by marrying into the prominent Nibenese [Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tharn) family. The Longhouse Emperors ruled for forty-three years before a Colovian duke named [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) marshaled the armies of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) and led them against Emperor [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic), the grandson of Durcorach, in open rebellion.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SHot LE-20)</sup> In the end, Varen killed Leovic and declared himself Emperor.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-COTFC1-21)</sup>

A [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) of Tamriel occurred around this time, this time under [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal), whose army made landfall in northeast Skyrim and captured [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), killing Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md). Ultimately Dir-Kamal forces were defeated by Nordic, Dunmer and Argonian forces and the Akaviri invaders were driven into the sea. Conflicts such as the invasion led the different nations of Tamriel to band together into three distinct alliances: the greater [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md),<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GTTDC-22)</sup> the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-GTTEP-23)</sup> and the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TROQA-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ATUQ-25)</sup>

The competing ideologies of Tamriel's alliances set them on a course for war. The [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) began in earnest sometime in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580) as tensions between the alliances were further exacerbated by the subsequent military aggression of the Empire of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) - an explosion of arcane energy that occurred in the Imperial City.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TCC-26)</sup> Each alliance saw the opportunity to overthrow the corrupt Imperial regime and install their own emperor on the Ruby Throne<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Carlier-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-barracks-28)</sup> The armies of the Covenant, Pact, and Dominion invaded Cyrodiil, which served as the main theater of war, however they also launched attacks against each other's lands. In the ensuing chaos, the Daedric Prince [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) deployed [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) and began pulling Nirn into his realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) to merge the two together, a process known as the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). Eventually the Planemeld was stopped when the Mages and Fighters Guilds spearheaded an assault into Molag Bal's realm. Following the Planemeld, many of Tamriel's provinces saw further conflict or hardships, including various Daedric plots in Morrowind, Summerset and Black Marsh, the return of [Dragons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragons) in Elsweyr and a vampiric resurgence in Western Skyrim.

In the last days of the Second Era a petty king of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) named [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) came to power alongside the leader of his legions, General Talos. A year later more than half of the Cyrodilic Empire was reclaimed or consolidated, and Cuhlecain captured the Imperial City and proclaimed himself Emperor. Skyrim and High Rock, which bitterly opposed a return to Cyrodilic rule, gathered their armies for a joint invasion of the Colovian West. The Nords that had come to oppose the Empire soon joined the General's forces, for when they heard his [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) they believed him to be a son of Skyrim and the heir to the Empires of Men. Cuhlecain was assassinated soon after and Talos, later named [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), rose to power and conquered all of Tamriel following the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PGE1S-29)</sup>

### [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md)
**[3E 0](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_0) — [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433)** The **Third Era** was a time of consolidation and expansion of the Septim Empire, established by Tiber Septim, also known as Talos, following the Tiber War. Under the rule of the Septim Dynasty, Tamriel was united under a single banner, and the Emperor was considered the divine ruler and protector of the Empire. This era saw the continuing influence of the Septim Empire, with the Emperor ruling from the Imperial City in Cyrodiil. The Septim Emperors maintained their power and influence through the use of the Amulet of Kings, a powerful artifact with ties to the Dragonborn bloodline.

During this time, the mysterious and catastrophic event known as the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Third_Era_Wars.md) occurred. It was a result of a magical upheaval orchestrated by the battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), who imprisoned Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) in an alternate dimension and assumed his identity. Tharn's actions led to a decade of chaos and unrest in the Empire, with multiple false Emperors vying for power. The true Emperor was eventually rescued by the hero known as the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md), bringing an end to the Simulacrum.

One of the most significant events of the Third Era was the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), also known as the Miracle of Peace. It was a result of the activation of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), a powerful Dwemer artifact, by various factions in the Iliac Bay region. It took a hero simply known as the [Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md), later known as The Hero of Daggerfall, to stop it. The activation of the Numidium caused a dragon break, resulting in multiple timelines converging into one. The end result was the unification of several independent kingdoms and city-states in the Iliac Bay region, creating a complex political landscape.

[The Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight) was a devastating mystical plague orchestrated by [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur). This catastrophic disease afflicted both people and the land, spreading fear and despair throughout Morrowind. The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), a prophesied hero, emerged during this era to combat the Blight and confront Dagoth Ur, ultimately bringing an end to his malevolent influence and restoring hope to the Dunmer people.

The [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) was a catastrophic event that marked the end of the Third Era. It was triggered by the invasion of the Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon's forces into Tamriel. The gates to the planes of Oblivion opened across the continent, unleashing hordes of Daedra. The Emperor Uriel Septim VII and his heirs were assassinated, leading to a search for a new Dragonborn to wear the Amulet of Kings. A hero known as the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) ultimately thwarted Mehrunes Dagon's plans.

The Hero of Kvatch, aided by the Blades and the last Septim heir, [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), embarked on a quest to close the Oblivion Gates and stop the Daedric invasion. This culminated in the Battle of Bruma and the sacrifice of Martin Septim to prevent Dagon's victory. With the destruction of the Amulet of Kings, the Septim bloodline came to an end and the Empire entered a period of decline.

The Third Era was marked by significant political, magical, and military events that shaped the fate of Tamriel. It ended with the closing of the Oblivion Gates and the dawn of a new era.

### [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md)
**[4E 1](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_1) — [Ongoing](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201)** The **Fourth Era** was marked by a changing political map across Tamriel. Notably, The Empire fell under the leadership of the [Mede Dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md), after a conflict known as the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md).

The Summerset Isles experienced a seismic shift in power dynamics during this era. The [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), a radical faction within the Altmer society, took control of Valenwood and initiated the secession of the Summerset Isles from the Empire. This marked the beginning of the Third [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). The Thalmor's rise to power reshaped the region's politics and culture, and their influence extended to other provinces, setting the stage for conflicts to come.

The [eruption of Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Year.md) in Morrowind led to significant changes in the region, causing a massive exodus of Dunmer refugees to Skyrim and other provinces. The fall of the Tribunal Temple and the rise of the [New Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Temple) further reshaped Morrowind's religious and political landscape. Morrowind would later cease to be an Imperial territory.

Both Black Marsh and Elsweyr seceded from the Empire and like Valenwood, Elsweyr became a part of the Aldmeri Dominion, after The Thalmor took credit for ending the [Void Nights](Lore_Void_Nights.md).

One of the defining events of the early Fourth Era was the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md). It erupted when the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) launched an invasion of the Empire. The conflict had far-reaching consequences, including the Thalmor's demand for the outlawing of Talos worship, which deeply affected the Nords of Skyrim.

Meanwhile, in Hammerfell, the Redguards successfully gained independence from the Empire after the signing of the [Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_Second_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md). This marked the end of the Empire's control over the province and allowed the Redguards to govern themselves.

The province of Skyrim became a focal point for political and cultural tensions during this era when the death of [High King Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) at the hands of [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) resulted in a [civil war](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) erupting in Skyrim. The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), led by General [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md), clashed with the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), Ulfric's rebel army as they sought Skyrim's independence.

This era saw the [return of the dragons](Lore_Return_of_the_Dragons.md) under the leadership of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), the World-Eater. The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), a legendary hero with the power to absorb dragon souls and shout in the ancient [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), played a pivotal role in countering this threat and ultimately defeating Alduin.
