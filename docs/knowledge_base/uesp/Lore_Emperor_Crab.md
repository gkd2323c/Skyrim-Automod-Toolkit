Emperor Crabs are gigantic semi-aquatic [crabs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crab) who were once native to the [Ashlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlands) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM52-1)</sup> contemporary with the early settled [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md). The species died out long before [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM52-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NDM-2)</sup> with [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) claiming to be the ones who hunted them to extinction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM52-1)</sup> They are described as being as large as cathedrals and some naturalists believe they are related to the [mudcrab](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mudcrab).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NDM-2)</sup> One source claims that they devolved into the modern mudcrab after abandoning divine pursuits in favor of mundane satisfactions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONSLS-3)</sup>

While they were known to be hunted by ancient [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlander), the discovery of massive ash-herder grapnels has caused some scholars to speculate whether the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) or ancient [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) actually tamed these massive beasts.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The arms and armor of House Redoran are inspired by the claws and chitin of the gigantic arthropods.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CM52-1)</sup> They have also famously used the shells of the creatures in architecture.

## Notable Emperor Crabs
- The most famous Emperor Crab was called **Skar**, and its giant shell was incorporated into the city of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md). "Under Skar" was the manor district of the settlement, and housed the leaders of Great House Redoran as well as several merchants.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> The Ashlanders claim that Dranoth Hleran killed Skar with Calderas, a mighty spear.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), ancient rituals were used to awaken Skar and the whole city literally rose up to fight the invaders. However, Ald'ruhn was destroyed anyway by the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) armies of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

- Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a novice [necromancer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) named Seviur established himself at a fishing shack west of the city of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). He conducted what he believed to be a pacification ritual to summon waves of mudcrabs to the shore so that he could practice his ability to resurrect the creatures as undead. However, frenzied mudcrabs continued to come ashore long after Seviur's [illusion magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion) faded, and he was ultimately killed by the creatures.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup> A [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md) fisherwoman named [Viriya](Lore_Viriya.md) sent the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to the shack to deal with the mudcrabs, as the frenzied crabs posed a threat to locals.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Although the attacks subsided, the creatures later reappeared in even greater numbers. Guards were sent from Dawnstar by the Jarl to deal with the creatures, but their ferocity had been underestimated. Dawnstar's forces fled when a [Guardian Spirit](Lore_Guardian_Spirit.md) in the form of an Emperor Crab emerged from the ocean.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Although initially in disbelief at the reports, Viriya summoned the Last Dragonborn to Dawnstar and met up with the city's reinforcements to launch a full attack on the crabs. After several waves of mudcrabs, the spirit Emperor Crab reappeared and attacked the soldiers. It was defeated with the use of flaming catapults, ending the threat.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup>
