[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9f/SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg/200px-SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Thirsk_Mead_Hall.jpg) Thirsk Mead Hall Hrothmund the Red was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) who was the first Chieftain of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) in the fourth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), though some claim he actually lived in the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BMThirsk-1)</sup>

Hrothmund was considered to have been as ugly as a troll, but he was popular with women because he wore the Amulet of Infectious Charm.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Beauchamp-2)</sup> Hrothmund was among the small group who left [Skaal Village](Lore_Skaal_Village.md) to live a warrior-centric life as their ancestors had in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bujold-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kuvar-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Sr LS-6)</sup> Hrothmund and the group traveled south to [Lake Fjalding](Lore_Lake_Fjalding.md), where they found the perfect location for a new settlement. After several months of building, the group completed the mead hall that would serve as their new home and hunting lodge, naming it Thirsk.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>

Tensions quickly arose over who would serve as their leader. Most of the men considered Hrothmund to be their de facto chieftain, as he was the strongest and most capable of the group. However, one warrior, Drengr Bronze-Helm, disagreed. He thought himself most capable of ruling Thirsk and loudly voiced his opposition to Hrothmund. Knowing that conflict and discord would only serve to destroy the new life they had worked so hard to create, Hrothmund exercised his only true option—he beheaded Drengr with his axe. Drengr's slaying proved to the inhabitants of the mead hall that Hrothmund was the most worthy of being Thirsk's chieftain. To ensure that the other Nords would never forget that he had proven his right to lead, Hrothmund placed Drengr's head on a pedestal in the center of Thirsk's main hall for all to see.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>

And so began Thirsk's most time-honored tradition. Any warrior, regardless of race or sex, could claim the chieftainship of the mead hall by displaying the most impressive battle trophy on the great hall's pedestal.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup> Following his death, it became tradition for those seeking to become Chieftain of Thirsk to journey to [Hrothmund's Barrow](Lore_Hrothmund%27s_Barrow.md) to be judged by Hrothmund's spirit in order to receive his blessing.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bujold-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Svenja-7)</sup>

For twenty-one years Hrothmund ruled as Chieftain of Thirsk. With his soft voice and great axe, which was said to be the largest weapon of its kind ever wielded by a Nord, Hrothmund brought peace and prosperity to Thirsk. However, peace caused Hrothmund to grow restless in the warmth and safety of the mead hall. He longed for battle, adventure, and to feel the frost in his veins once more; he could no longer ignore the call of valor. When word spread that a giant and bloodthirsty white wolf known as Ondjage the Fell Wolf was terrorizing travelers in the [Moesring Mountains](Lore_Moesring_Mountains.md), Hrothmund took up his axe and set out to defeat the beast alone. Ondjage was as large as an ox, and the mountain folk said no man or woman alive could bring the wolf down. The words of the mountain folk proved true, for while Hrothmund did chop off one of the Fell Wolf's legs, Ondjage devoured him whole, leaving only his axe. Filled with sorrow and rage, the residents of Thirsk went to the mountains in search of the wolf, who they called Hrothmund's Bane. Together they managed to slay Ondjage, and they feasted on the wolf's roasted flesh as a family.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>

Hrothmund was buried in Hrothmund's Barrow with his axe and the [Amulet of Infectious Charm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amulet_of_Infectious_Charm).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bujold-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Bloodmoon-9)</sup> He was succeeded by [Isgeror White-Wave](Lore_Isgeror_White-Wave.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAH-3)</sup>
