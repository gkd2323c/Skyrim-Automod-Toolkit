—A mysterious voice within Red Mountain speaking to the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) Red Mountain ([Dragon](Lore_Dragon_Language.md): *Sahqo-Strunmah*, "Red-Mountain";<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> [Dwemer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Language): *Vvardenfell*, "City of the Strong Shield")<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup>, also known as **Mount Vvardenfell**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGW-3)</sup> and **Dagoth-Ur** ([/ˈdeɪɡɒθ ˈɜːr/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ***day** -goth UR* or ([/ˈdæɡəθər/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ***dag** -əth-ər* Link ⏵),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is a massive volcano that dominates the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Prior to its most recent [catastrophic eruption](Lore_Red_Year.md), it was the highest mountain in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup> On a clear day, Red Mountain's peak could be seen from the city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), some 250 miles to the south.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup> The mountain is generally considered the most dangerous region in Vvardenfell, and has erupted at least thrice in history: the [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md) event of [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668), the Days of Fire in [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TPP-5)</sup> and the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TNM-6)</sup> (although it was very close to erupting during [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s plot to drain [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md)'s power in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582)).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOM-7)</sup> The Red Year eruption in particular was so devastating that Red Mountain's height was permanently reduced, and the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) claimed the title of Tamriel's highest peak from then on.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1S-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-8)</sup>

The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) settled in the vicinity of Red Mountain as early as the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), giving it the name *Vvardenfell*. In the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) occurred on its slopes, resulting in the outright disappearance of all Dwemer and the rise of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md). This event likely coincided with the volcano's first major eruption, which blanketed the skies of Tamriel with ash for an entire year. Near the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Voryn Dagoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) (believed to have died at the Battle of Red Mountain) awoke within its bowels and challenged the power of the Tribunal, taking on the name "Dagoth Ur", which soon became applied to the whole mountain due to Dagoth's presence. Access to Red Mountain became restricted in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), when the Tribunal built the [Ghostfence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostfence) around the perimeter of the mountain to contain the [Ash Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight) originating from within. The only way inside without the use of magic was through [Ghostgate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostgate) in the south. Due to the proliferation of [disease](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus) -carrying ash storms, few dared to venture past the Ghostfence. It was known, however, that four ancient [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) Citadels were hidden in the region, three of which were residences for Dagoth Ur's most trusted ash vampires; the fourth was the lair of Dagoth Ur himself. Even though this area was extremely dangerous, [malachite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malachite) and [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) mines were set up inside the Ghostfence, under [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) guard or by enterprising [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Ultimately, Dagoth Ur was defeated in his subterranean lair in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), dispelling the supernatural ash storms. However, the disappearance of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) led to the destabilization of the suspended [meteor](Lore_Meteors.md) [Baar Dau](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baar_Dau), which impacted [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) in 4E 5, triggering the most powerful eruption of Red Mountain to date. Known as the Red Year, the eruption caused widespread death and destruction along with a mass exodus of Morrowind's inhabitants.

The geography of Red Mountain is ever-changing. In the wake of an eruption, lava channels form deep ravines called *[foyada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Foyada)*, which, once cooled, are used as roads by the Ashlanders. Bubbling lava pools dot the landscape, fed by magma from deep within the earth. The harsh climate of Red Mountain's slopes makes it accommodating to strange creatures that thrive in volcanic environments, including [ash hoppers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Hopper), [fetcherflies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fetcherfly), and [shalks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shalk), as well as hardy vegetation such as [scathecraw](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scathecraw). [Skylamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skylamp) are known to fly alongside it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TAo M-10)</sup> As the volcano is still active as of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), it is unlikely that any populated settlements still exist in the region.

In the mythology of [the Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md), the Red Mountain was the Red Tower. The Tower's Stone was the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), which was known as the First Stone, a focal point for the Tower's reality altering power, upon which the rules according to which the Tower shaped its domain, were inscribed. The First Stone and its Tower are said to have allowed [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) to exist without the full presence of the divine, and granted it a special kind of divinity, which is called "NIRN", the "consequence of variable [fate](Lore_Fate.md)".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-RMM-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Enigma-13)</sup> As with all Towers, Red Mountain was said to exist beyond the physical and in some sense apart, or detached, from ordinary space and time.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOCelarus-14)</sup>

Red Mountain is said to have been formed at the time of the [Convention](Lore_Convention.md), when [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s [heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) was removed and thrown into the sea; Red Mountain formed where the Heart of Lorkhan landed.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TM-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-THo LMFP-16)</sup> Long after, the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) established themselves in Morrowind, and Red Mountain became a fortress at the heart of the Dwemer kingdom of Vvardenfell. The Dwemer eventually united with the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) to expel the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) from Morrowind in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NARM-17)</sup> The two elven races coexisted peacefully under the banner of the kingdom of Resdayn for a time, until the Chimer general [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indoril_Nerevar) learned that the Dwemer had found the Heart of Lorkhan and sought to use it to empower the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md). This led to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), which culminated in the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-18)</sup> Coinciding with the war, Red Mountain erupted in [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668), reshaping the island of Vvardenfell and blotting out the sun for a full year. This event became known as the [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TNM-6)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup>

### Battle of Red Mountain
The Battle of Red Mountain was the climactic battle of the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), and resulted in the sudden disappearance of the entire [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) race from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TBORM-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NARM-17)</sup> The battle was fought at Red Mountain in [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md) (present-day [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)) around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700)<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DI1-20)</sup> (or possibly earlier),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> between the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) (led by General [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar), the Hortator) and the Dwemer (led by [Dumac Dwarfking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dumac_Dwarfking)),<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NARM-17)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3All-21)</sup> along with armies of [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-18)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FSOKW-22)</sup> The exact truth of what happened cannot be determined due to conflicting accounts,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-POT-23)</sup> but all agree that by the time the dust settled, much of that dust was all that remained of the Dwemer.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NARM-17)</sup> Though Nerevar died, his people claimed victory, and four Chimer were positioned to become living gods.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TBORM-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NARM-17)</sup>

The First Council was held together for hundreds of years thanks to the friendship between Dumac and Nerevar. However, through means lost to history, Lord [Voryn Dagoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) of [House Dagoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dagoth) learned of a Dwemer scheme: deep in the bowels of Red Mountain on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), the Dwemer had found the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), and High Craftlord [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac) planned to harness it and use it to power a giant [mechanical god](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TBORM-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NARM-17)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-KT-24)</sup> Lord Dagoth reported this to the Chimer leadership, and eventually, the Hortator Nerevar confirmed it with his patron [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura). Nerevar confronted his friend Dumac, and they quarreled bitterly (some sources assert Dumac did not know of Kagrenac's plan at the time and thus misunderstood Nerevar's concerns). As a result, Nerevar led the Chimer to war in order to stop this "profane" usurpation.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TBORM-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-NARM-17)</sup>

At the start of the war, Dwemeri forces were concentrated in the northwest of the province, and their campaign initially had great success throughout the north.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-18)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRN-25)</sup> There is speculation that many of the great metal automatons the Dwemer used as troops in the battle were built in the Great Workshops of Nchardak on the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). The coastal Dwemeri city was reputedly able to produce one automaton a day, an apparently impressive rate of production.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-DBNeloth-26)</sup> Many outlanders were at the battle, notably [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), and sources often designate them as allies of the Dwemer.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-18)</sup>

Nerevar, meanwhile, led his united people, the nomadic [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) as well as the [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) ([House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), in particular, likes to trumpet its substantial contribution of warriors to the battle).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-18)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TTNC-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-NMAS-28)</sup> Nerevar did not have full control of the Chimer forces until a series of defeats convinced the Great Houses to relinquish command.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRN-25)</sup> Once he had full command of the Chimer forces, Nerevar carefully orchestrated a confrontation at [Dumac's citadel](Lore_Dagoth-Ur_(place).md), a great Dwemer stronghold within Red Mountain where the Heart was being kept.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-18)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1M-2)</sup>

The bulk of Nerevar's troops left Narsis and headed north at roughly the same time as a horde of westerners entered Morrowind from the west and began crossing the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TBORM-19)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FSOKW-22)</sup> Some sources claim that all who opposed Nerevar's forces in the battle were exterminated.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-18)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRN-25)</sup> Others state that while all enemies of the Chimer forces were decisively defeated, only the Dwemer were wiped out completely.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SN-29)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e4/ON-crown_store-Volcanic_Senche-Panther.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Volcanic_Senche-Panther.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Volcanic_Senche-Panther.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Volcanic_Senche-Panther.jpg) A Volcanic Senche-Panther, said to have emerged from the heart of Red Mountain [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Divine_Disaster.jpg) Baar Dau plummeting towards [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City) as Red Mountain nears eruption, [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Long restive, Red Mountain awoke again briefly in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an apparent consequence of Vivec's power being siphoned away. The culprit behind the Warrior-Poet's debilitating affliction was the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) Clavicus Vile, who needed Tribunal power to find and enter [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). This resulted in Vivec being severely weakened, to the point that [Baar Dau](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baar_Dau) threatened to fall and Red Mountain erupted, endangering Vvardenfell. Fortunately, Vivec's powers were restored through the actions of an [Outlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soulless_One) and disaster was averted.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOM-7)</sup>

In the [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882), known as the Days of Fire<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TPP-5)</sup> to the Dunmer, Dagoth Ur awoke beneath Red Mountain causing it to erupt and unleash [The Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight) and [Corprus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus) across Morrowind. That same year the Tribunal journeyed to Red Mountain to renew their connection to the Heart in a bathing ritual, they encountered Dagoth Ur, reborn to a new incarnation, and with divine power that could more than match their own.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PTDDU-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-PGE3M-31)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DUP-32)</sup> Unable to enter Red Mountain's Heart Chamber, they were forced to retreat. Dagoth Ur had sole control of the Heart from this point on and grew stronger while the Tribunal grew weaker.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DUP-32)</sup>

Following the Days of Fire the Tribunal and their followers would launch seasonal campaigns to Red Mountain in an effort to reclaim the Heart and slay Dagoth Ur and his kin, but they would be repeatedly beaten back and the Heart would always revive them.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PTDDU-30)</sup> When this strategy proved ineffective the Tribunal erected the Ghostfence around Red Mountain to contain the spread of the Blight and the [Corprus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus) monsters.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Vivec-33)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/AR-quest-Dagoth-Ur.jpg/200px-AR-quest-Dagoth-Ur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Dagoth-Ur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Dagoth-Ur.jpg) Dagoth-Ur during the Imperial Simulacrum [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/MW-place-Dagoth_Ur.jpg/200px-MW-place-Dagoth_Ur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Dagoth_Ur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Dagoth_Ur.jpg) The citadel of Dagoth Ur, [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) used the Dwarven mines beneath the mountain as a hiding place for a piece of the shattered [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md). The [Eternal Champion](Lore_Hero.md) discovered the entrance to the mines by striking the [Hammer of Gharen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hammer_of_Gharen) against the [Anvil of Mithas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anvil_of_Mithas) to produce a ringing which would reveal its location. The hero retrieved the piece, completing the Staff, only to discover that Tharn had drained its power into the [Jewel of Fire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jewel_of_Fire).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Arena-34)</sup> After their victory over Tharn, many [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) -loyalists had retraced their steps throughout the adventure, among these places would include the Dagoth-Ur mines.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-AMM-35)</sup>

In [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417) two members of the Tribunal, [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) alongside the [Buoyant Armigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Buoyant_Armiger) would journey to Red Mountain in this attempt to retake the Heart of Lorkhan from Dagoth's forces but ended up losing [Sunder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunder) and [Keening](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keening), two of [Kagrenac's Tools](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac%27s_Tools) in an ambush which forced them to retreat and end the campaigns to Red Mountain.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DUP-32)</sup> From this point onwards almost all of the territory inside of the Ghostfence would be under the control of the Sixth House.

The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) would arrive in Morrowind in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-PGE3M-31)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-MW-36)</sup> The Nerevarine would travel to Red Mountain to confront Dagoth Ur and using the Tools of Kagrenac they would break the enchantments on the Heart of Lorkhan, severing Dagoth Ur's connection with it and ultimately vanquishing him.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TCot SH-37)</sup>

After Dagoth Ur's defeat, the Ghostfence was deactivated and the blight storms came to an end.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TCot SH-37)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg/200px-SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) Red Mountain spewing ash, [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) In [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), recorded by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) as the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), Red Mountain erupted shortly after Baar Dau, since converted into the [Ministry of Truth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ministry_of_Truth), completed its descent and impacted into [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TRY-38)</sup> The eruption produced massive amounts of smoke and toxic ash that smothered much of Morrowind and left Vvardenfell almost entirely uninhabitable.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TR-39)</sup> The portions of Morrowind that survived the initial calamity were not spared for long, as ash from the continually-erupting mountain poisoned the land and sea over the following decades.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TR-39)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DBGjalund-40)</sup>. As of [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the volcano was still experiencing ongoing eruptions with ash reaching as far as [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DBGaryn-41)</sup>

## Notes
- | [1.](#note_nb1) | "Dagoth-Ur" is another name for Red Mountain, and is usually distinguishable from the deity "Dagoth Ur" due to the hyphen, but the use of the distinction is somewhat inconsistent.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ARRia-42)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TPC-43)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Codex-44)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TBA1-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-2920SD-46)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Aa D-47)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TRB5-48)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-MWHNC-49)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-MWGDR-50)</sup> As with the cities of Vivec, Almalexia and Sotha Sil, distinguishing between the place and the person depends on the context. |
| --- | --- |
- While not accessible, Red Mountain is clearly visible from [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md) in the *[Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)* expansion for *[TES V: Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)*, from [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Stonefalls) in *[Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*, and throughout [Vvardenfell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Vvardenfell) in *[Elder Scrolls Online: Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Morrowind)*.
- There was a [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) clan known as Clan Red-Mountain. The figure known as Odrav Anvil-Hand brought great honor to the family and was commemorated with a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md). Whether they are related to Red Mountain itself is unclear.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-SRShouts-51)</sup>
- Red Mountain is alluded to as the **Red Tower** in the *[Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md)*.
