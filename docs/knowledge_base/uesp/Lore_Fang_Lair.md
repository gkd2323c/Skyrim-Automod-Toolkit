[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/ON-place-Fang_Lair.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fang_Lair.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fang_Lair.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fang_Lair.jpg) The entrance to Fang Lair from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Fang Lair, also known by its Dwemeri name **Arkngthamz-Phng**, is a ruin of a [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) city and mine located in [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md), on the border between [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and near the border with [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

## History
Sometime in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) clan [Kragen](Lore_Kragen.md) followed the path of the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) clan and migrated west from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They settled in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) region of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), founding a city called [Arkngthamz](Lore_Arkngthamz.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AP-1)</sup> Thanks to the discovery of [aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md) deposits, the city flourished, and soon allied with the cities of [Bthar-Zel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthar-Zel), [Mzulft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mzulft), and [Raldbthar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Raldbthar), in an attempt to study the wondrous material. For this purpose, the alliance built the Aetherium Forge beneath the ruin of Bthalft in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAW-2)</sup>

At the peak of Arkngthamz's power, Clan Kragen decided to expand their holdings to the west and dug through [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md), eventually founding a sister city, Arkngthamz-Phng, on the border with [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). The city grew rapidly, and eventually replaced the original Arkngthamz as the seat of the clan's power. Entrances to the city existed in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and Hammerfell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AP-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AR-3)</sup> Some aetherium deposits were kept stored in the city.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CN-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg/200px-AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-quest-Fang_Lair.jpg) The Hammerfell entrance circa [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) Eventually, the city was laid to waste by the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Thurvokun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thurvokun), and the Dwemer were driven away. The story of a dragon claiming the city as its lair and the "Phng" suffix in the city's name ultimately led to the adoption of the name "Fang Lair" by the way of folk etymology.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AP-1)</sup> One story tells of a golden dragon inhabiting Fang Lair, who encountered [Moraelyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moraelyn) and allowed him to take an ebony sword after they removed a cursed tooth from his mouth.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KEVII-5)</sup> In the late years of the First Era, some time after Emperor [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) stopped making sport of dragons,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ulfnor-6)</sup> a group of three adventurers delved into Fang Lair and slew Thurvokun, perishing there in the process. Caluurion remained in the ruins as a lich and conducted obsessive studies of the Dragon's remains.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-JOB-7)</sup>

Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the necromancers of the [Blackmarrow Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackmarrow_Cult), led by Orryn the Black, raised the adventurers as undead servants. Orryn managed to briefly reanimate and possess Thurvokun's skeleton, but was defeated by [Yisareh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yisareh) and other members of the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted), assisted by the freed souls of the three adventurers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESO-8)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AR-3)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), one of the pieces of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) was hidden in Fang Lair. After learning the ruin's location from Queen Blubamka of [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md), the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) visited it and retrieved the relic on his quest to defeat [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AR-3)</sup>
