Chrysamere (also called the **Chrysamere Blade**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup> or **the Chrysamere**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFTI-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> is an ancient [adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md) claymore [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) with offensive capabilities only surpassed by its defenses.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AR.2FDF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TL-5)</sup> It has [magical](Lore_Magic.md) properties which, unlike most weapons, are defensive in nature: it heals the wielder, grants them resistance to fire, and reflects hostile spells.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AR.2FDF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TL-5)</sup> It is unaligned, and is not a Daedric artifact.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-D4C-6)</sup><sup>:78</sup> Chrysamere was forged in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) by the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) swordsmith [Asterie Bedel](Lore_Asterie_Bedel.md), who delighted in using the techniques of both [man](Lore_Men.md) and [mer](Lore_Mer.md) in her creations. In Chrysamere's case, this most clearly manifests in the placement of her maker's mark, a "mage's knot" within the heart of the sword that is said to harken back to the earliest days of Bretonic culture. Chrysamere is also said to have been influenced by the magic of Breton society's Elven forebears, and it gained further enchantments over time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAIA-7)</sup> Chrysamere does not typically stay with one swordsman for long, for it chooses not to favor one champion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AR.2FDF-4)</sup> It has earned the title of the **Sword of Heroes**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAIA-7)</sup> but it also goes by other names such as the **Sword of Kings**<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ARCS-8)</sup> and the **Blade of Blades**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup> Chrysamere is considered a holy weapon, and in this context it is referred to as the **Sword of the [Paladin](Lore_Paladins.md)**, or simply the **Paladin's Blade**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TL-5)</sup>

## History

### Merethic and First Era
After Chrysamere's forging in the late Merethic Era, it would subsequently be used in the wars to secure [Clan Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md)'s rule over [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). By this time, Chrysamere had already passed hands several times, each time gaining further enchantments. Its defensive enchantments were further enhanced and retooled during the siege of the Fellthunder Clan of [giants](Lore_Giant.md) in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md), and it would later be seen at the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md) where it played a small yet important role.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAIA-7)</sup>

Chrysamere was also present at the first sacking of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAIA-7)</sup> although it is unknown if this was the sacking supposedly carried out by [Ryain Direnni](Lore_Ryain_Direnni.md) with the help of his Breton legions,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Once-9)</sup> or the sacking of the First Orsinium that took place during the tenth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3O-10)</sup>

### Second Era
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYB-11)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-12)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing Chrysamere among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TA-13)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Chrysamere and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-MWFR-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-item-weapon-Chrysamere_03.jpg/200px-ON-item-weapon-Chrysamere_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-weapon-Chrysamere_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-weapon-Chrysamere_03.jpg) Chrysamere Replica (ESO) Where Chrysamere was during the time of the Interregnum is unknown, but records from the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md) in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) hint that a series of letters penned between a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) researcher from [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) fence from the city-state of [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md) in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) may have contained clues of its whereabouts during that time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LAIA-7)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), both solid gold letter openers in Chrysamere's image<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> and a number of weapon replicas of Chrysamere were seen throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONCS-16)</sup>

### Third Era
During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) (circa [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) - [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399)), a scribe in Alabaster working with the city's archivists learned that Chrysamere had resurfaced somewhere in Tamriel. Further research on the part of the scribe revealed the existence of a tablet, hidden within a dungeon located in Elsweyr itself, which was inscribed with the current location of Chrysamere. Rumors of Chrysamere's return eventually came to the attention of the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md), who followed the rumors to the brother of the scribe. The scribe's brother, who had little desire for adventure, offered to relay the tablet's location in Elsweyr in exchange for gold, which would allow the scribe to complete his studies and his brother to raise a family. After journeying to Elsweyr and discovering the tablet, the Eternal Champion learned Chrysamere was located in a second dungeon outside of Elsweyr, where they were able to acquire the sword.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARTIL-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/DF-quest-The_Lost_Artifact_03.jpg/200px-DF-quest-The_Lost_Artifact_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-The_Lost_Artifact_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-The_Lost_Artifact_03.jpg) The Mages Guild would like to keep their artifact (Daggerfall) Sometime during the events leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) (circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405) – [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417)), [the Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) obtained Chrysamere in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). One story claims it was bestowed to them by a [knightly order](Lore_Knights.md) after putting a stop to an [arch-lich](Lore_Lich.md) that was raising an army of undead.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DFTAD-17)</sup> Another account builds upon this, stating that after putting a stop to the undead menace, the Agent discovered a clue to the blade's whereabouts. This led them to retrieve Chrysamere from a [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) branch, who had stolen it from the great-grandfather of a secluded wizard. However, there are conflicting accounts that the artifact centered around these events was actually a different one altogether, namely the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), the [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md), or the [Warlock's Ring](Lore_Warlock%27s_Ring.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFTLA-18)</sup>

Records also indicate the Agent had at least one unsuccessful attempt in obtaining Chrysamere, during which they were hired to acquire a daedric lodestone in exchange for the artifact. The lodestone's guardian, a [daedra seducer](Lore_Daedra_Seducer.md), revealed that their employer was deceiving them. It is unknown who they chose to believe, but this ultimately led to a violent confrontation in which the Agent slew their employer and confirmed they never had the artifact in their possession. This resulted in the Agent gaining infamy with the imposter's associates, who turned out to be the faction belonging to the King of Orsinium, [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFTI-2)</sup> Nevertheless, the Agent is also thought to have been offered Chrysamere by Gortwog himself at one point in exchange for the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DFWGTT-19)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_DF)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5e/MW-quest-Grandmaster_Duel.jpg/200px-MW-quest-Grandmaster_Duel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Grandmaster_Duel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-quest-Grandmaster_Duel.jpg) Varus Vantinius (Morrowind) Chrysamere would later be in the possession of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md)'s [Order of Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Ebonheart) for many years.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) was tasked by this knightly order with recovering Chrysamere and the [Lord's Mail](Lore_Lord%27s_Mail.md) from some thieves. The Mail was in the possession of an expelled member of the Legion, while the sword had been taken by a treacherous knight. However, the Legion's spies reported that Chrysamere had changed hands to that of Draramu Hloran, a powerful sorceress found in the [Abanabi Caves](Lore_Abanabi_Caves.md) near [Sadrith Mora](Lore_Sadrith_Mora.md). The sword had gained a reputation for being designed for killing spellcasters,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-21)</sup> and Draramu became feared even in Sadrith Mora.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-22)</sup> The Nerevarine dealt with the two threats and returned both artifacts to Varus Vantinius, the Knight of the Imperial Dragon on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWLords-23)</sup> The Nerevarine later challenged Vantinius for the title of Knight of the Imperial Dragon; the two dueled in the [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_(city).md) arena, and Vantinius was slain, allowing the Nerevarine as victor to claim both artifacts from his corpse.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MWGD-24)</sup> Later that year, the cuirass and blade were sold to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md), who put them on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Tribunal-25)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/SR-quest-The_Lost_Paladin.jpg/200px-SR-quest-The_Lost_Paladin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Lost_Paladin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-The_Lost_Paladin.jpg) The Lost Paladin (Skyrim Creation Club) Chrysamere re-emerged around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), when the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md) came across the blade during their journey. The weapon is thought to have been looted from an extravagant chest.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup> Sometime afterwards, the blade was wielded by a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warrior known as the Lost Paladin, who died with the blade at [Forelhost](Lore_Forelhost.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the paladin's aggressive spirit was encountered and laid to rest by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who then claimed Chrysamere.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-cc BGSChrysamere-27)</sup>
