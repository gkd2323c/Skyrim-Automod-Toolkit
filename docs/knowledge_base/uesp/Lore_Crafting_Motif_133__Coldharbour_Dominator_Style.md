[Crafting Motif](Lore_Crafting_Motifs.md) 133: Coldharbour Dominator Style **by [Xivryf the Fingerflayer](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Xivryf_the_Fingerflayer)** *A guide to crafting armor and weapons in the Coldharbour Dominator Style* *
---
A meditation on methods of torture and the myriad ways our simple arms and armaments can be used to exact pain. Endless suffering will be needed to fill the [Dolorous Cista](Lore_Dolorous_Cista.md), my clansfolk. Even if we are low on tools, it does not mean we need be low on creativity. Follow my suggestions below and we will find the Cista brimming with woe in no time.

AXES

I find the axe most useful for denailing. A broad, curved edge can fit under a broad spectrum of mortal fingernails. You'll find you can pry them back with little effort for enormous returns in suffering. Keep a small jar of [salt](Lore_Salt.md) on hand for when the nerves become deadened to this initial assault, your subject will find it most stimulating.

BELTS

Binding, whipping, and strangulation all seem obvious uses, but I have found these methods are ultimately unreliable when it comes to extracting mortal suffering. Keep your belt around your waist where it belongs.

BOOTS

Do not underestimate the power of a good kick. I recommend you start with joints and ribcages. Hard boots against soft tissue may seem both obvious and tempting, but often this leads to a quick death from internal bleeding and ruptured vital organs. Kick smarter, not harder.

BOWS

Do not rely on bowstrings as a reliable source of mortal suffering. Practiced archers should use these weapons in the traditional manner to inflict mental distress. A classic example might be shooting an item off your victim's head, or taking blind shots as they rotate on a torture wheel. Let their anticipation fuel your imagination. The quartermaster can make available barbed arrows which are excruciating to remove, introduce these into your routine as a way to keep your subject off-balance and afraid.

CHESTS

Take caution with rib fractures. It is impossible to tell which mortals have weak bones, at least while they are alive. Such injuries should not be an aim of torture, but rather a mitigated side effect. Disconnected segments of the ribcage can cause other complications that lead to swift death, which of course just denies us a potential source for anguish and fuel.

DAGGERS

Daggers are wonderful tools, primarily for flaying. I see many torturers eager to paint a tapestry of torment on the broad canvas of mortal backs. Instead, I recommend starting with the hands or the soles of the feet. Areas that are sensitive, but not vitally important for survival. As a general rule for any method of torture, it is always better to start distally and move proximally

GLOVES

Mortals find it deeply distressing to be "degloved." Even mere mention of the term can evoke a visceral response. The sight of such disfigurement on the victim's own body also aids in prolonging the mental effects of our work. This is a low-risk method of torture that guarantees a high yield in suffering. I cannot recommend it enough.

HELMETS

A note on the psychology of our work: never reveal that your express purpose in torture is simply to create suffering. Most mortals will either resist your methods tenfold by dissociation, or they will simply give up the will to live. Instead, hide your face and let your victims do the work in imagining your shadowy motivations.

LEG GREAVES

Flighty or jumpy mortals can be subdued by severing one or both hamstring tendons on the back of the upper legs. Take caution: it is easy to accidentally nick veins or arteries using this technique. Take care that they don't bleed out before you have maximized their suffering. Let no well of agony be left untapped.

MACES

Swift application of blunt instruments in the area under the mortal ribcage, just above their soft bellies, triggers a reaction that mortals call "knocking the wind out of you." Doing this once may only offer a mild yield of suffering. However repeated application, spaced out by a short recovery time, gives significant returns. A useful reminder: mortals find it deeply unpleasant when they cannot breathe.

SHIELDS

What marvelously versatile tools shields are. If you have nothing else at hand, many forms of torture covered in this text can be accomplished with a shield alone. I myself like to reserve its use to wring out the final gasps of mortal agony. Slowly crushing a victim's head under the weight of a massive shield offers a vibrant spike of suffering as well as a very pleasant sound.

SHOULDERS

I consider joint dislocation to be a startlingly effective "introductory" torture for mortals. A way to set the scene, as it were. In addition to the psychological effect of the (very satisfying) noise, it sets up a safe torture scene for tortured and torturer. It leaves the victim incapacitated, and in most cases does not break the skin.

STAVES

The use of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) in torture is altogether a far more complex subject than the scope of this text. However if you have a sturdy staff to hand, I have found them tremendous for kneecapping. Just as you can use either end of your staff for such methods, do remember that you can also apply this force to either side of the knee. And mortals usually have two of them.

SWORDS

Do not overthink the sword. They are best for swift dismemberment. If need calls for its use in torture, try to think unconventionally. Prolonged impalement! I once strapped two victims together, back to back, then speared them with a pair of blades in opposing directions. They were pinned together, all while avoiding major organs. Their screams, jostling, and shared agony were a font of suffering.
