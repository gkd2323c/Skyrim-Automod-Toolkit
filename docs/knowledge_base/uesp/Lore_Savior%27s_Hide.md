# Savior's Hide

*You've done well, hunter. And found my favor. That skin will serve you well, child. Look more closely at it. My glories shall protect you from all this world's grievances. Good hunting.*
—Hircine Savior's Hide, also known as **Scourge of the Oathbreaker**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SHN-1)</sup> and **Hircine's Hide**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRTM-2)</sup> is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) commonly associated with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MH-4)</sup> Savior's Hide once referred to a full set of armor, known as the **Armor of the Savior's Hide** (boots, cuirass, gauntlets, greaves, helmet, and pauldrons). Over time, the term Savior's Hide would become predominately used to refer to the **Cuirass of the Savior's Hide**, as the rest of the armor set have not been seen since [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CNATAOFSH-5)</sup> The Cuirass makes the wearer resistant to [magic](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup>

There are three creation stories to the Hide, with two crediting Hircine and the third crediting [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TMKR-6)</sup> The more widely known tale involving Hircine holds that the Daedric Prince rewarded the first mortal to escape his [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md) with his peeled Hide. The mortal then had the Hide tailored into the Cuirass for use in their adventures. This version of the story also claims that the Cuirass gave the wearer a resistance to magic.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup> Another version claims that it was Hircine himself who sewed the Savior's hide from the hide of a [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Blades Saviors Hide-7)</sup>

The third tradition which credits Malacath is lesser known and contains an inaccuracy, stating that it made the wearer *vulnerable* to magic. It claims that the Hide is a "captive servant of Malacath". Despite the conflict between the stories, they agree on the points that the Hide would protect from the blows of an oathbreaker, and would protect the wearer from the sting of the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TMKR-6)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWYB-8)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-9)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Cuirass of the Savior's Hide among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TA-10)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the cuirass and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWFR-11)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), many elaborate sets of armor existed that imitated the style of the Armor of the Savior's Hide .<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

### Third Era
Sometime before [3E 172](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_172), [Chimere Graegyn](Lore_Chimere_Graegyn.md), a retainer of the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) clan, came into possession of the full set of armor. He used it to protect himself when tricking [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) and gaining immortality. Unfortunately for him it only protected him against Dagon's direct physical retaliation; instead Dagon formed an interpretation of the pact that allowed him to destroy Chimere's hometown and cast the town, the island it was built on ([Caecilly Island](Lore_Caecilly_Island.md)), and Chimere into the [Void](Lore_Void.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TMKR-6)</sup> Never wanting to see it again, he split the armor up and hid the pieces all across the island.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CNATAOFSH-5)</sup>

In [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), when Dagon invaded the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), an unnamed [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) of the Battlespire fought their way through many realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and eventually stumbled upon the island, unwittingly becoming Prey in the [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md) which was being held at the time. After talking to Chimere and assembling the six pieces of the Savior's Hide, they obtained the Spear of Bitter Mercy needed to defeat the Huntsman and depart from the realm.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSL5-13)</sup> This apprentice went on to use the Hide to defeat Dagon along with another artifact, the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](Lore_Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LD-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BSL7-15)</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md), a member of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), who was the caretaker of the Corprusarium under [Tel Fyr](Lore_Tel_Fyr.md) and also a collector or artifacts, had acquired the Cuirass. The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) obtained the item while adhering to Divayth's strange hobby of allowing his collection to be pilfered under certain circumstances.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MWDivayth-16)</sup> Later that year, the Nerevarine sold it to a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) noblewomen by the name of [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md), who put it on display in her Museum of Artifacts in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRTM-2)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) obtained the Cuirass after fulfilling Hircine's request to kill the last [unicorn](Lore_Unicorn.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OBHircine-17)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Organized bandit robberies surged in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) around [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176) under the leadership of a self-proclaimed "Bandit Queen", a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) by the name Marita Shuste. The [Sheathed Blades](Lore_Sheathed_Blades.md) came into conflict with the bandits and slew them along with the Bandit Queen, who was the wearer of the Savior's Hide at the time.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

The Cuirass re-emerged around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), when the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md) came across it during their journey. The artifact is thought to have been looted from an extravagant chest.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a werewolf named Sinding stole the [Ring of Hircine](Lore_Ring_of_Hircine.md) in an attempt to control his transformations. However, this plan backfired when Hircine cursed the ring, causing Sinding to transform at random, which led to him killing a child and ending up in jail. Soon after, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md#Last_Dragonborn) met Sinding, who asked the Dragonborn to take the Ring and free it from its curse by hunting the White Stag, before escaping from jail. After slaying the stag, the Dragonborn learned that the Prince would not forgive Sinding's betrayal, and conducted a Great Hunt, where he tasked hunters with killing and skinning him in return for the Savior's Hide. The Dragonborn entered Bloated Man's Grotto where Sinding was hiding, where he was confronting a second time. The Dragonborn was left with the choice to kill him in order to receive Hircine's favor and the Cuirass, or to kill the other hunters trying to get Hircine's favor and the uncursed ring. It is unknown which option the Last Dragonborn chose, only that Hircine was pleased with the results.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SRMoonlight-20)</sup>
