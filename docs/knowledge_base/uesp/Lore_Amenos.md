[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/ON-place-Amenos.jpg/200px-ON-place-Amenos.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Amenos.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Amenos.jpg) Amenos ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/ON-map-High_Isle.jpg/200px-ON-map-High_Isle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-High_Isle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-High_Isle.jpg) Amenos is the second largest island of the Systres archipelago Amenos is the second-largest island in the [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago), an island region in the western part of the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). The island is characterized by a dense, hostile jungle and a hazardous coastline riddled with jagged rocks, unrelenting storms, and dangerous currents. Historically, Amenos has been governed by the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) and it has hosted a prison facility since the early years of the [Common Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Common_Era).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup>

Amenos Station, the main settlement on the island is not only the administrative center of the region but also the only safe place in Amenos. Prisoners are sometimes processed and banished into the jungle, where they fend for themselves against other prisoners and the wildlife. Some sort of magic or alchemy prevents convicts from escaping the island so their only choice is to survive the wilderness or die trying.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONMarcelle-2)</sup> Amenos also has protections in place to discourage [scrying](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) and [portal spells](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md), although it is possible to teleport within the confines of the island with more effort.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-3)</sup> The [Eldertide Circle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eldertide_Circle) of [druids](Lore_Druid.md) live throughout Amenos, in places such as the [Coral Cliffs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coral_Cliffs)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Coral-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONNivathil-5)</sup> and whatever [magic](Lore_Magic.md) the druids used to balance nature in the Systres never quite took off in Amenos.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/ON-interior-Coral_Cliffs_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Coral_Cliffs_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Coral_Cliffs_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Coral_Cliffs_02.jpg) An Eldertide hovel in the Coral Cliffs [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png) Map of Western Tamriel Information about the earliest known settlements on Amenos is very limited but the [Gonfalon Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gonfalon_Bay) Historical and Social Society believes that the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) may have inhabited the island when they first left [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [1E 330](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_330).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-6)</sup> In any case, the Eldertide Circle have typically lived on the island.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONNivathil-5)</sup>

Years later in [1E 660](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_660),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup> the [Lefthanded Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lefthanded_Elves) fled their home in [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) after their empire was toppled by the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md). They later fled across the east<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-6)</sup> and one contingent of elves landed on Amenos with the intent of conquering the Systres. They built their headquarters somewhere on the island and from there, they assaulted the other islands and besieged [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup> The conflict saw the death of hundreds of both elves and druids alike and as the Lefthanders encroached on the heart of druidism, [Mount Firesong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mount_Firesong) erupted in [1E 676](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_676), for the first time in recorded history.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-6)</sup>

Despite that, the elves stood victorious over Systres while the druids went into hiding. Their victory was short-lived however when the rest of the elves were executed by the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) in [1E 785](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_785). These wayfarers lived on the Systres for a short time as they use the archipelago as a stopover for their journey east.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SH2-6)</sup> Even though they liberated the isles from the elves, the druids continued to hide and lived on Amenos' coast and interior.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LABAHI-7)</sup>

### Amenos in the late First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/ON-place-Finimi%27s_Domicile.jpg/200px-ON-place-Finimi%27s_Domicile.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Finimi%27s_Domicile.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Finimi%27s_Domicile.jpg) The home of one of the Systres Sisters [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-place-Brokerock_Mine.jpg/200px-ON-place-Brokerock_Mine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Brokerock_Mine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Brokerock_Mine.jpg) An ammonite mine on Amenos History regarding Amenos and the Systres at large is unknown after this as the next time off-islanders come to the archipelago was in the twenty-third century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), after the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) enraptured Tamriel and killed hundreds of thousands. The [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md), one of the largest naval fleets in history landed on the islands, where they built their warships for the war on [Thras](Lore_Thras.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SH3-8)</sup> [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo) tried to establish diplomacy with a [Hadolid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hadolid) camp in Amenos after their assistance in the [Thrassian War](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thrassian_War), but this ended in failure.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HRJ-9)</sup> For a time, the Systres was claimed by [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) but was later relinquished to the Bretons of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [1E 2327](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2327).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SH4-10)</sup> For a good while, the isles were administrated by [House Guimard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guimard) and for a short time, [House Mantel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Mantel).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SH5-11)</sup>

On the 11th of [Sun's Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun%27s_Dusk), [1E 2704](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2704), emissaries of the newly-formed [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) arrived in Gonfalon Bay and decreed that the Systres were once again the dominion of Cyrodiil. These emissaries came with Breton [coin-barons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coin-barons) who steered the Systres back to its feudal roots before Y'ffelon's eruption. Amenos blossomed under the Second Empire throughout the twenty-ninth century of the First Era, when [ammonite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ammonite) excavation grew sufficiently. This boom was driven by [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) warlock fashion and it gave way for standard mining of [larimar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Larimar) and semi-precious stones. The discovery of [frog-metal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frog-Metal) in vast troves underneath Amenos also shifted the focus of maritime entrepreneurs from fishing to mining.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SH6-12)</sup>

During this time, piracy was at an all-time high and many perpetrators infested Amenos' waters. One of these pirates was Commodore Malfiarre, who terrorized the seas for a decade and damned his fleet with magic created by the [Deep Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deep_Druids).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONVanisande-13)</sup> Perhaps one of the greatest known examples was the Systres Sisters, a trio of three sisters who amassed a grand fortune after raiding High Rock's coast. They chose Amenos as the place to build their hidden vault.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONValentyn-14)</sup> The High Rock Coin-barons were involved in [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md), which was a fact of life of Breton culture of the time. In the Systres, these Coin-barons established a slaver fort on Amenos on the north coast.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONEmeric-16)</sup>

Mount Firesong erupted once again in [1E 2484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2484) and laid waste to the island region. Not only did it destroy the ancient shipyards of the All Flags Navy but obliterated a burgeoning settlement on Amenos.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SH5-11)</sup>

### The Prisons of Amenos and onward
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/ON-place-Amenos_Station.jpg/200px-ON-place-Amenos_Station.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Amenos_Station.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Amenos_Station.jpg) Amenos Station [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-npc-Jailer_Omdil.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Jailer_Omdil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jailer_Omdil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jailer_Omdil.jpg) A jailer in Amenos Station Amenos' main purpose changed when in [2E 11](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_11), the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie) converted it into a penal colony for Reman-aligned political prisoners. Many people who saw the Systres as a symbol of Tamrielic unity were against this decision and voiced it publicly. And many of these critics, such as Lisolda Paquoit were cast to Amenos and died on the island. Although construction of the Potentate's famous jail, [Blackrose Prison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackrose_Prison) was completed, he continued to send people over to the island to work the ammonite and larimar mines.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SH6-12)</sup> While the Systres have changed rulers over the years, Amenos continues to host one of the most dangerous prisons on Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOA-1)</sup>

At some point in time, House Mornard took some control of Amenos' mines but attained even greater power when they were granted dukedom over the isles by the Potentate in [2E 110](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_110). The Duke granted his house exclusive rights to mine Amenos and facilitate the prison complex.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-HOHM-17)</sup> Even after the fall of the Second Empire, House Mornard ruled the Systres and in time, established a rivalry with their associate house, [Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md). Their constant back and forward culminated in [2E 478](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_478) when Duke Ruffe Mornard seized a full third of House Dufort's land and exiled their leaders to Amenos, claiming that they attempted a coup against House Mornard. In the mid-sixth century, however, House Mornard lost power over the Systres. For their involvement in [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), House Mornard was stripped of their dukedom and designated to their holdings on Amenos and [Galen](Lore_Galen.md), while House Dufort became rulers of the duchy.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SH7-18)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Lord [Bacaro Volorus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bacaro_Volorus) invited leaders of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) for a peace summit on High Isle but as they approached the region from the north, their ships were caught in a brutal, albeit uncontrollable storm conjured by a figure known as the [Ascendant Magus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascendant_Magus). The ships were subsequently wrecked on Loch Abhain in High Isle and while their captains were apprehended by the [Ascendant Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascendant_Order), the alliance leaders, Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and Prince [Irnskar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Irnskar) of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) were lost to sea and washed ashore on Amenos' north coast.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONDeadly-19)</sup>

One of Amenos' prison gangs, the Green Serpents, put up a bounty for the capture of these three nobles for the Ascendant Order. And while Ayrenn and Emeric managed to escape their clutches, Prince Irnskar was taken to their hideaway, Old Coin Fort on the north coast. Lady [Arabelle Davaux](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arabelle_Davaux) sent two of her associates, the Breton rogue, Jakarn, and a [mainlander](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to retrieve the nobles. They both infiltrated Amenos Station's stockade to gather intel and the latter delved into the jungle to find the allied leaders. The adventurer found Ayrenn and Emeric in the wilderness but had to fight their way through the fort to rescue the prince. Ayrenn used [weather magic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Weather_Magic) to bypass the island's storms and safely escape the island.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONEscape-20)</sup>
