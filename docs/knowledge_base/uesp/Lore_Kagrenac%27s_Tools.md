# Kagrenac's Tools

This article is about the artifacts. For the book, see [Kagrenac's Tools (book)](Lore_Kagrenac%27s_Tools_(book).md).

Kagrenac's Tools are the legendary [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) crafted by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) Tonal Architect [Lord Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md). Each tool served a specific purpose to siphon the power of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md), via [tonal manipulation](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) of the laws of nature.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWBaladas-2)</sup>

### Wraithguard
The gauntlet **Wraithguard** was created to safely wield the weapon tools, Sunder and Keening. Wielding the weapons without the gauntlet will often lead to the instant death of the wielder. Wraithguard also functions to empower the abilities of Keening and Sunder.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Accounts of its appearance vary, <sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Morrowind-4)</sup> but it typically looks like a gauntlet made of a material comparable to brass. Despite its primary purpose as a means to safely wield the other tools, learning to wield Wraithguard is itself an unpleasant experience.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWVivec-5)</sup> Wraithguard cannot be wielded without first harmonizing your magicka signature with the artifact. After doing so, the first time Wraithguard is equipped, it brings risk of death as the bonding deals near guaranteed fatal damage. If the shock is survived, Wraithguard becomes safe to wield and re-equip from thereon.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-YBW-6)</sup>

### Keening
The short blade **Keening** (also referred to as the **Ethos Knife**)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FSKW-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-36L-8)</sup> was created to flay and focus the power that the hammer Sunder produced.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KT-1)</sup> In song, it is described as a dagger made of the sound of the shadow of the moons.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FSKW-7)</sup>

### Sunder
**Sunder** was created to produce a specific amount of power from the Heart. When struck by Sunder, the Heart would release godlike power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KT-1)</sup> It is described as a hammer of divine mass.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FSKW-7)</sup>

## History

### The Creation of the Tools, The Disappearance of the Dwemer, and the Ascension of Almsivi
At some point by the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Dwemer miners discovered a mysterious, magical stone beneath [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). The Dwemer Tonal Architect Lord Kagrenac determined that this stone was the Heart of Lorkhan, and constructed the tools to harness its powers. His goal was to create a new god for the Dwemer— [Anumidium](Lore_Anumidium.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Morrowind-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KT-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NARM-9)</sup> This god took the form of a colossal brass golem called [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-POT-10)</sup> and was the key to the Dwemeri bid for ascension, be it godhood, immortality, or something greater.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NARM-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWYagrum-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Po M-12)</sup>

By some accounts, Kagrenac constructed Anumidium without the knowledge of the Dwemer King [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md). The [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) learned of it, however, and Dumac's claims of innocence when confronted by his friend Lord [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) sparked the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NARM-9)</sup> The war culminated around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) with the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md); while again accounts vary widely, most agree that Nerevar, [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), and others made their way into the Heart Chamber beneath the Dwarven citadel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FSKW-7)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TBORM-13)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NARM-9)</sup>

In the ensuing battle, Kagrenac (or Nerevar and Dagoth Ur, by [Azura](Lore_Azura.md)'s instruction) used the Tools on the Heart of Lorkhan, which some say was the cause of the Dwemer vanishing at that time from Nirn.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KT-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NARM-9)</sup> Nerevar died (either in battle or by murder).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NARM-9)</sup> Soon after (or years later)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TBORM-13)</sup>, the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) took possession of Kagrenac's tools, and used them to siphon the Heart's power and ascended into living gods.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KT-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NARM-9)</sup> Thereafter, they made an annual pilgrimage to the Heart Chamber to recharge their powers.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DUP-14)</sup>

Although the tools were under the care of the Tribunal, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), alleged fragments of Sunder's haft were circulating as contraband in various markets.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

### Dagoth Ur Awakens
In [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882), [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md) awakened, and ambushed the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) during their annual pilgrimage to [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to renew their divine powers. The event resulted in the Tribunal being cut off from access to the Heart of Lorkhan. The Tribunal then launched intermittent campaigns to assault Red Mountain to force access to the Heart Chamber, and two of the three tools, Keening and Sunder, were lost during a campaign in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DUP-14)</sup> The weapons were then split among two different [Ash Vampires](Lore_Ash_Vampire.md), with Dagoth Odros guarding Keening within the citadel of Odrosal, and Dagoth Vemyn guarding Sunder within the citadel of Vemynal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KT-1)</sup>

### Sotha Sil's Tools
The loss of the Heart led the Tribunal God Sotha Sil to develop his own variant of the Heart of Lorkhan, the [Mechanical Heart](Lore_Mechanical_Heart.md). To siphon the Mechanical Heart's power, Sotha Sil created his own variants of the Tools to control, shape, or even dismantle it if necessary.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SSDTTRTCC-16)</sup> He foresaw his eventual death at the hands of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), and eventually hid his tools as a precaution across Nirn to prevent the Heart's power from being stolen.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-GDTTARTCC-17)</sup> The location of the tools was lost after his demise—though some tales describe a [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) reclaiming them circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rt CC-18)</sup>

### The Heart of Lorkhan Freed
In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), an outlander sent by Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) arrived in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and was believed by many to be the reincarnation of Nerevar—the [Nerevarine](Lore_Hero.md#The_Nerevarine). After many trials, and towards the end of their journey, the Nerevarine was ready to head into Red Mountain. They recovered Wraithguard from the Tribunal God [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md), and assaulted the Sixth House citadels to obtain Keening and Sunder. During their final battle with [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md), all three were used to destroy the enchantments by Kagrenac on the Heart of Lorkhan, after which it seemingly disappeared from the world, destroying Dagoth Ur and Akulakhan in the process.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Morrowind-4)</sup>

### The Tools' Journey to Skyrim
All three tools would eventually leave the collection of the Nerevarine. By [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), Sunder and Wraithguard found their way into the hands of a group of smugglers, who attempted to smuggle them through [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and into [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), using the refugees fleeing the effects of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) as cover. Once out of Morrowind, [Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md) began attacking their caravan. Unknown to them, the Tools had a fail-safe system implemented within them in order to prevent the Tools from ever leaving Morrowind. The fail-safe was a signal indicated by a tuneless and quiet singing that activated a Tamriel-wide network of drones, which sought to retrieve the Tools if they were taken out of Morrowind. After retrieving the Tools, they would then deposit them in a vault until a Tonal Architect could come and retrieve them. Seeking to put a stop to the attacks, some of the caravan captains betrayed their employer and handed Sunder and Wraithguard over to the drones. A scholar working under the same employer oversaw the delivery of the cargo, and learned of this, and managed to track the items in the snowy wastes southwest of Winterhold, to a vault located in Sightless Pit. She became trapped within it and died before an expedition could be organized to find her.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SCCLL-19)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Keening found its way into Skyrim when the mage [Arniel Gane](Lore_Arniel_Gane.md) requested the delivery of it for research. By this time, the blade was no longer dangerous to wield without Wraithguard. Keening was briefly lost along the way when the courier died. Arniel used the blade on a warped soul gem to reproduce the events which led to the disappearance of the Dwemer; unfortunately, the experiment was a success, and Arniel vanished. The blade then passed into the possession of the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md), who had aided Arniel in his endeavor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Skyrim-20)</sup>

During that same year, the Dragonborn learned of the whereabouts of Sunder and Wraithguard's location and traveled to the Sightless Pit. There, they navigated the various traps and battled the vault's guardian, a Dwemer centurion which appeared to be made out of an [ebony alloy](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md). They then retrieved Sunder and Wraithguard, and united all three Tools once again. The Dragonborn discovered that like Keening, Sunder lost its enchantment which killed its wielder without the use of Wraithguard.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SCCLL-19)</sup>
