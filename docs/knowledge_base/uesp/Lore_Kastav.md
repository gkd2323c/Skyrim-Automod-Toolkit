Kastav was the second emperor of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) and [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAHo S-1)</sup> Details of his life and reign are scarce, but sources describe him as an incompetent ruler.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RII-2)</sup>

In [1E 2801](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2801), Kastav ordered the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) of [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) to seize hostages from [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and [Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md) to ensure that the jarls met their conscription quotas. In [1E 2804](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2804), the Winterhold Rebellion broke out, but the Master of Sky Haven Temple refused orders to send the Dragonguard to help suppress it, so the Emperor cut off supplies to the temple. Kastav then sent Kalien, an [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) who had been denied entry into the Dragonguard, to suppress the rebellion. Kalien sacked [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), but this only exacerbated the situation, as the people of Skyrim mistakenly assumed Kalien was a member of the Dragonguard and placed Sky Haven Temple under siege from [1E 2805](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2805) to [1E 2806](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2806) in retaliation for the sacking.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOTD-3)</sup> Kastav was deposed in 1E 2804 and succeeded by [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md), whose first notable endeavor was to negotiate a swift end to the rebellion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RII-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOTD-3)</sup>

After his disposition, he lived for several years in exile alongside his honor guard.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EKE-4)</sup> In [1E 2812](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2812), Kastav died in the [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) region of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and was buried near the future site of [Blackrose Prison](Lore_Blackrose_Prison.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EKE-4)</sup> By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), a fort in the [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md) region of Skyrim bore his name.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FKS-5)</sup>
