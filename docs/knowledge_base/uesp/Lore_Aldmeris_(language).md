# Aldmeris (language)

This article is about the language. For the place, see [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg) Map of Summerset Isle, using Aldmeris script Aldmeris, sometimes called **Ald Aldmeris**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KP-1)</sup> **Old Aldmeris**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Telvanni-2)</sup> **Aldmeric**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ulliceta-3)</sup> **Aldmeri**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FOTN-4)</sup> **Elven**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-On Ob-5)</sup> **Elvish**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Generic A-6)</sup> or **Old Elvish**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aba Loria-7)</sup> is an [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) [language](Lore_Linguistics.md) that originated from the eponymous [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md). It is viewed as a glorious and beautiful tongue compared to [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1TWR-8)</sup> Aldmeris is not limited to words, boasting alternative manuscripts for musical notation<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> and measuring numerology.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Telvanni-2)</sup>

## Usage
In his original exploration of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) taught the [Birdfolk](Lore_Birdfolk.md) of the [Ruby Isles](Lore_City_Isle.md) Aldmeris, in both spoken and written form.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FOTN-4)</sup> The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) wrote many of their documents in Aldmeris.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Hasphat-11)</sup> Until [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) joined the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobility used Aldmeris to signify pride in their Elven origins.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Aldmeris was still taught across the Elven nations of Tamriel and used in Elven [arts](Lore_Recreation.md). Any educated Elf during this time was expected to be able to read and write the language. Some isolated Elven communities spoke it as their first language.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Generic A-6)</sup>

## Language Family and Genetic Relationship
Aldmeris itself is descended from [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md), the language of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-13)</sup> When it came to Tamriel, it branched off into several other languages, including all of the modern Elvish languages such as [High Elvish](Lore_High_Elvish.md), [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md#Language), [Dark Elvish](Lore_Dark_Elvish.md) and [Orcish](Lore_Orcish.md), as well as older or extinct languages like [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md), [Old Orcish](Lore_Old_Orcish.md) and [Velothi](Lore_Dark_Elvish.md#Ald_Chimeris_Dictionary).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Generic A-6)</sup> These offshoot tongues maintain a close resemblance to Aldmeris.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Hasphat-11)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Generic A-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Summerfall-14)</sup> When creating a runic transcription of the oral [Atmoran](Lore_Nordic.md#Atmoran_Dictionary) tongue, [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) used the principles of Aldmeris.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-15)</sup>
