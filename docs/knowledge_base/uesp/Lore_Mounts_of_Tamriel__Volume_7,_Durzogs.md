Mounts of Tamriel: Volume 7, Durzogs **by [Fadus Falto](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Fadus_Falto) of Skingrad** *Speculations on the ancient origins of the Durzog* *
---
Allow me to set the scene. A group of academics seated around a tavern table, a nice bottle of tawny port open for all to enjoy. It's late and the subject turns, as it often does among such a group, to history's eccentricities. Inevitably some wit will propose, with mock seriousness, an origin for the [durzog](Lore_Durzog.md). The game then is to add to the proposed origin in a farcically grand fashion without contradicting its basic premise. Every notion I've heard from these drunken pedagogs, truthfully, are just as valid as the serious scholarly papers I've read about these bizarre creatures.

I frame this as a joke, because there is humor to be found here, but in truth I see this lack of clarity as a slow-motion tragedy. From the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) to the shores of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset.md), you can find durzogs happily living and hunting alongside [Goblinkin](Lore_Goblinkin.md). The durzog, in turn, represents a deep divide between the ancient cultures of the continent and [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s current stewards.

Researchers far more accomplished than I have tied [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) to some of the earliest inhabited sites on the continent. Possibly predating the earliest arrival of [Men](Lore_Man.md) from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) or [Elves](Lore_Mer.md) from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), and implying a once-much more grand and complex culture for this diminutive people. Consider the protean shape of the durzog. The complexity of their shape, and the perfection of their hunting form.

These beasts deserve more respect, academically as well as culturally. Their fangs and grim visage may hide a secret well worth exploring.
