## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/ON-furnishing-Karthwatch_Banner%2C_Hanging.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Karthwatch_Banner%2C_Hanging.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Karthwatch_Banner,_Hanging.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Karthwatch_Banner,_Hanging.jpg) Karthwatch Banner [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/SR-place-Broken_Tower_Redoubt.jpg/200px-SR-place-Broken_Tower_Redoubt.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Broken_Tower_Redoubt.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Broken_Tower_Redoubt.jpg) Broken Tower Redoubt Karthwatch was established to defend the border against [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) after Karthald was carved from its northern territory.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONSvana-4)</sup> The Reach was at the time an independent [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) kingdom ruled by Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the so-called Despot of Markarth. Karthald was established in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONFenrar-5)</sup> by the kingdom of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md), although [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) clans had inhabited the land for generations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Gt WSK-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TAt WH-3)</sup> The location for Karthwatch was decided due to its proximity to the ancient Nordic ruins of [Bleakridge Barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bleakridge_Barrow), which was thought would make a good defensible position in the event of a Reachman invasion.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Karthwatch was considered a spartan settlement with little hospitality to offer for travelers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Gt WSK-2)</sup> The banner of Karthwatch featured a stylized tower symbol on a steel blue background.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Banner-1)</sup>

Karthwatch was destroyed circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) and their [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) allies. The Reachmen created a [Harrowstorm](Lore_Harrowstorm.md) above the town in order to drain the life energy of its inhabitants, using their [souls](Lore_Soul.md) to fill ancient reliquaries containing the mortal remains of members of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). Many of the residents of Karthwatch were transformed into [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) monstrosities known as [Harrowfiends](Lore_Harrowfiend.md), including Jarl Olfwenn herself.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODanger-7)</sup> Others were taken hostage by the witches, although a rescue was mounted by the town's survivors.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOBlood-8)</sup> The sole survivor of the jarl's court was the court wizard, Magister Fridelja, who went on to represent the hold politically in the turmoil that followed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESODanger-7)</sup>

She and the other survivors sought refuge in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). However, the rest of the hold was similarly laid to waste, its people left dead or scattered. With the Nords driven out and the hold's capital destroyed, Karthald was no more, allowing the territory to be reclaimed by the Reach.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESODaughter-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Bjora-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOMF-11)</sup> The settlement would eventually transform into the city of **[Snowhawk](Lore_Snowhawk.md)** by the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), with Karthald's former location on the plateau later becoming the site of a fort.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOLive-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Skyrim-13)</sup>

### Fourth Era
By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), a ruined fortress called **Broken Tower Redoubt** sat on the approximate former site of Karthwatch.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Skyrim-13)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Sibyl of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) had passed away, and a new one was needed for the [House of Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Dibella) in the city. The priesthood had discovered the young girl, [Fjotra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fjotra) in Karthwasten, but a group of Reachmen known as the [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md) had taken her to Broken Tower Redoubt, where a shrine to [Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella) was located. From there, the young girl was freed by [someone](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) sent by the Temple of Dibella, and taken back to Markarth, where she would serve as the Dibellan Sybil of Markarth.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
