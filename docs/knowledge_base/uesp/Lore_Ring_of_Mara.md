# Rings of Mara

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/ON-render-Ritual_of_Mara.jpg/200px-ON-render-Ritual_of_Mara.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Ritual_of_Mara.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Ritual_of_Mara.jpg) A Ritual of Mara [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_Mara.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Ring_of_Mara.png) *Goddess above, shine your light upon our love.* **Rings of Mara**, also known in as **Bonds of Matrimony**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Bond Matrimony-1)</sup> **Wedding Bands**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VWB-2)</sup> and **Wedding Rings**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRW-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WR-4)</sup> are ceremonial adornments worn to represent [Mara](Lore_Mara.md)'s blessing of a [couple's union](Lore_Marriage.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mara Ring-5)</sup> They're exchanged and worn after performing the **Ritual of Mara** with a **Pledge of Mara**, a ceremony in which a couple pledges their commitment to one another on sacred ground. <sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Yisara-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Mara Pledge-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ritual Mara-8)</sup> [Shrines of Mara](Lore_Mara.md) serve as these sacred places, although [Mother Morrowind](Lore_Almalexia.md)'s altars may also be used to perform the blessing.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Mara Pledge-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ritual Mara-8)</sup> Rings of Mara are often [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) with minor blessings, such as improved [healing magic](Lore_Restoration.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Bond Matrimony-1)</sup> or the boon to learn quicker when with your soulmate.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mara Ring-5)</sup>

The custom of using rings to symbolize marriage is widespread throughout Tamriel. It is practiced among many [human](Lore_Man.md) cultures, including the [Bretons](Lore_Bretons.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LE-9)</sup> [Nords](Lore_Nords.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Bond Matrimony-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AWB-10)</sup> [Imperials](Lore_Imperials.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WR-4)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Umbranox-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TROM-12)</sup> [Redguards](Lore_Redguards.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-JJ-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PWR-14)</sup> and [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TTWT-16)</sup> The practice is also observed among the [Mer](Lore_Mer.md), such as [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-RIES-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Nairume-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Fasaran-19)</sup> [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Tirwin-20)</sup> and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Elethien-21)</sup> [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) and [Orcs](Lore_Orcs.md) were also reportedly known to wear them.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Spouses-22)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Ritual Mara-8)</sup> Despite the fact that the traditional Argonians don't follow Mara they adopted the custom of exchanging Rings of Mara.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-LA-23)</sup> Among some [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), a proposer presents a unique [gold](Lore_Gold.md) ring set with three [amethysts](Lore_Amethyst.md): the outer stones represent each partner, and the central gem symbolizes the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TJ-24)</sup>
