# Thieves Guild (faction)

| --- |

[![SR-banner-Thieves Guild.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/SR-banner-Thieves_Guild.png/100px-SR-banner-Thieves_Guild.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-banner-Thieves_Guild.png) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg/200px-SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon.jpg) The Ragged Flagon, headquarters of the Thieves Guild The Thieves Guild is dedicated to the gathering and training of those who are stealthy and shadowy in nature. Although criminal by its very definition, for untold years, local authorities in places throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) have tolerated the existence of the guild for its role as "crime regulator", as it does not tolerate competition or egregious conduct from its members (not to mention the personal financial benefits for authorities who play nice). It is usually considered to be a distinctly [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) entity, though of course other organizations of the Empire do not officially condone their actions.

Like any trade guild, the Thieves Guild is an organization of professionals, except that in this case the professionals are burglars, robbers, pickpockets, smugglers and other enterprising operators. They typically don't have public guild halls but sometimes have safe-houses and members tend to gather at a single location in large towns, such as at a cornerclub, inn or tavern. Typically, members are bound not to rob each other, or kill anyone while thieving. The Thieves Guild usually has the resources to bribe officials, establish a black market of stolen goods and maintain a network of informants. For historical information, see the [Lore article](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md).

Based in the city of [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md), the Thieves Guild's [headquarters](Skyrim_The_Ragged_Flagon.md) can be initially accessed through the [Ratway](Skyrim_The_Ratway.md), a network of tunnels that run under the city.

## Joining
To join the Thieves Guild, travel to [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md) and come into contact with [Brynjolf](Skyrim_Brynjolf.md), who is at the market stalls during the day, or the [Bee and Barb](Skyrim_The_Bee_and_Barb.md) tavern during the night. Brynjolf will approach you and question your wealth before inviting you to help him with a little plot. This begins the first quest, [A Chance Arrangement](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Chance_Arrangement), where you must steal a ring and plant it on another person.

If the quest is failed, Brynjolf will still give you the next quest. Travel down to the canals and enter the [Ratway](Skyrim_The_Ratway.md). Defeat the bandits and follow the tunnels to the [Ragged Flagon](Skyrim_The_Ragged_Flagon.md), headquarters of the Thieves Guild. Here, Brynjolf will give you the next quest, [Taking Care of Business](Skyrim_Taking_Care_of_Business.md), tasking you with roughing up three business owners for debts he needs collected.

You can be expelled from the guild by assaulting/murdering a member, attempting to steal from the guild and getting caught, or murdering someone during a guild quest. You must pay 1,000 gold in [reparations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reparations) to rejoin.

## Advantages
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-The_Ragged_Flagon_02.jpg) Inside the Flagon - Access to the [Thieves Guild headquarters](Skyrim_The_Ragged_Flagon.md).
- A set of [Thieves Guild armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Thieves_Guild_Armor_Set).
- Access to [Thieves Caches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Thief_Cache) spawning loot regularly.
- A suit of [Linwe's Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Linwe%27s_Armor), a unique armor set obtained from a sidequest.
- A set of [Nightingale Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nightingale_Armor), the [Nightingale Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nightingale_Blade), and the [Nightingale Bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nightingale_Bow) after you proceed up the ranks.
- Three interchangeable unique powers once the Nightingale story arc is complete.
- Access to [Chillrend](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chillrend), potentially the deadliest unique sword in the game and the strongest in the original game.
- [Fences](Skyrim_Fence_(merchant).md#Fences) who will buy legally-obtained and stolen goods and will have larger amounts of gold available for trading than normal merchants (1,000 initially, 4,000 after completing all four improvement quests).
- [Tonilia](Skyrim_Tonilia.md), the fence for the Guild, is one of the earliest vendors to regularly sell [Daedric weaponry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Weapons).
- Access to four new merchants at the Ragged Flagon, including a blacksmith and an alchemist, after completing certain sidequests for the Guild.
- Respawning locked chests ranging from novice to master for building up your [Lockpicking](Skyrim_Lockpicking.md) skill (See [bugs](#Bugs)).
- Free gold and lockpicks lying around the headquarters.
- Unlimited thievery radiant quests which reward gold.
- Master-level trainers in Lockpicking ([Vex](Skyrim_Vex.md)), [Archery](Skyrim_Archery.md) ([Niruin](Skyrim_Niruin.md)), [Pickpocket](Skyrim_Pickpocket.md) ([Vipir the Fleet](Skyrim_Vipir_the_Fleet.md)), and [Sneak](Skyrim_Sneak.md) ([Delvin Mallory](Skyrim_Delvin_Mallory.md)).
- A set of [Guild Master's armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Guild_Master%27s_Armor) and the [Amulet of Articulation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Amulet_of_Articulation) can be obtained once the main quest line and the guild improvement quests are completed.
- Dialogue option to avoid conflict with thieves encountered on the roads, later upgraded to such thieves paying you tributes.
- Guards in major holds can be bribed to remove bounties even if you have a low [Speech](Skyrim_Speech.md) skill and no bribe perk.
- The [Skeleton Key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skeleton_Key), an unbreakable lockpick (temporary).
- The only way to learn all three words of the [Disarm](Skyrim_Disarm.md) [shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) is to complete the first six primary quests.
- [Access](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Paid_in_Full) to the contents of [Glover Mallory](Skyrim_Glover_Mallory.md)'s basement, including a full set of [Blackguard's Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Blackguard%27s_Armor).<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>

### Special Job Quests
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/SR-npc-Delvin_Mallory_06.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Delvin_Mallory_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Delvin_Mallory_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Delvin_Mallory_06.jpg) [Delvin](Skyrim_Delvin_Mallory.md) at his usual spot in The Ragged Flagon. Once you join the guild, you can speak to Delvin and Vex to receive radiant Thieves Guild quests. These jobs can be repeated indefinitely, and you can have one active job per quest-giver, but you must complete or quit your current job before you can receive another one from that particular quest-giver. In addition to the monetary reward for completing these quests, they also count towards restoring the guild's status in Skyrim. In order to start the final Thieves Guild quest, [Under New Management](Skyrim_Under_New_Management.md), you must complete five of these jobs in each of the other four main cities in Skyrim ([Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md), and [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md)), at which point Delvin will give you a special "reputation" quest in that city. Completing these reputation quests improves the condition of the Ragged Flagon, and allows you to bribe city [guards](Skyrim_Guards.md) to clear your bounty in that hold. Once you complete [Taking Care of Business](Skyrim_Taking_Care_of_Business.md), you can bribe guards of [the Rift](Skyrim_The_Rift.md).

- **[Delvin](Skyrim_Delvin_Mallory.md)'s radiant quests**: - **[The Numbers Job](Skyrim_The_Numbers_Job.md)**: Alter the business ledger of a store. ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **[The Fishing Job](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Fishing_Job)**: Steal an item out of a target's pocket. ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **[The Bedlam Job](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Bedlam_Job)**: Steal a set amount of gold's worth of items. ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **[Vex](Skyrim_Vex.md)'s radiant quests**: - **[The Burglary Job](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Burglary_Job)**: Steal a special item from a wealthy home. ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))

- **[The Sweep Job](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Sweep_Job)**: Steal three specific valuable items from a wealthy house. ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **[The Heist Job](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Heist_Job)**: Steal a particular item from a store. ([radiant](Skyrim_Radiant.md))
- **Reputation quests**: - **[Silver Lining](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silver_Lining)**: Recover a stolen [silver mold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Curious_Silver_Mold) for a [silversmith](Skyrim_Endon.md). (Markarth) <sup>[†](#intnote_Fence)</sup>

- **[Summerset Shadows](Skyrim_Summerset_Shadows.md)**: Remove a rival guild from the picture. (Windhelm) <sup>[†](#intnote_Fence)</sup>

- **Note**: If you quicksave immediately after the dialogue ends, and before the quest is displayed on screen, you can quickload until you get a quest in the city you want. This can be useful if you want to focus your efforts on cities where you have not yet completed restoring the Guild's status. You can also simply quit the quests, which will be marked as failed in your journal, then select new ones. You can do this an infinite number of times with almost no consequences; the item generated for the Burglary Job will remain permanently, making it hard to do another Burglary in that location, and bugs with the Fishing and Heist Jobs may remove non-quest items from your inventory.
- <sup>([DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md))</sup> You may be sent to [Raven Rock](Skyrim_Raven_Rock.md) for the Bedlam or Fishing quests. The Bedlam Job is bugged, however, so it cannot be completed if you are sent there. The Fishing Job will work as normal, though there is no reputation quest associated with Solstheim.
- If a dragon attacks and kills the mark, such as the owner of the stables, it will fail the quest.

## The Ragged Flagon
The Thieves Guild is headquartered within the [Ragged Flagon](Skyrim_The_Ragged_Flagon.md). This area is divided into a central chamber, featuring a tavern, and the [Ragged Flagon - Cistern](Skyrim_The_Ragged_Flagon_-_Cistern.md) which is only open to members and serves as a living quarters. If you join the Thieves Guild, you will receive a bed, end table, and chest reserved for your own use and located next to the entrance alcove. All containers in the cistern are safe for storage. The cistern also features an [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Lab.md), archery ranges, kitchen, and locked chests that respawn for [Lockpicking](Skyrim_Lockpicking.md) practice. Upon first visiting this area, The Ragged Flagon will be gloomy, empty, and dilapidated, but will gradually be restored as quests are completed.

### Upgrading the Thieves Guild
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/SR-place-Ragged_Flagon_Upgraded.jpg/200px-SR-place-Ragged_Flagon_Upgraded.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Ragged_Flagon_Upgraded.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Ragged_Flagon_Upgraded.jpg) The Ragged Flagon - Cistern improves with the fortunes of the Guild. When doing Delvin or Vex's additional jobs, you need to head out to four different cities: [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md), and [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md). After completing five additional jobs in a single city, you'll be assigned a special job for that city. After completing each special job, the condition of the Ragged Flagon will be improved and a new merchant will move into the main chamber. Additionally, completing a quest will enable you to use the Guild's influence to pay a reduced bounty when confronted by a guard in the corresponding city. Clearing your bounty with this method will also allow you to avoid having any stolen items in your inventory confiscated.

Upon completion of the main Thieves Guild questline, a [Shrine of Nocturnal](Skyrim_Shrines.md) will appear in the Ragged Flagon - Cistern. When all quests are completed, the furniture in the Ragged Flagon will be completely replaced with more upscale variants, along with additional furniture and decorations.

## Fences
Fences are a special kind of [merchant](Skyrim_Merchants.md) that will buy stolen goods from you, removing the stolen tag in the process. Some Thieves Guild quests make available additional fences throughout Skyrim. The [Fence](Skyrim_Fence_(perk).md) [skill perk](Skyrim_Skills.md#Perks) allows you to sell stolen goods to any merchant that you can invest in.

Tonilia is a fence as soon as you join the Guild. The fences near Whiterun (Mallus Maccius), in Solitude (Gulum-Ei) and in Winterhold College (Enthir) are all unlocked during the main quest line. On the other hand, Endon in Markarth, Niranye in Windhelm, and Atahbah, Ma'jhad and Zaynabi in the travelling Khajiit caravans, all require "reputation" sidequests to unlock.

Even with the Fence perk, however, Thieves Guild fences still have the distinction of having the most gold of any merchants in the game (with the exception of [Lucan Valerius](Skyrim_Lucan_Valerius.md) prior to version 1.9 of the [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), though his superior merchant gold is only due to a bug): up to 4,000 gold after completing all of the Special Jobs, and up to 5,000 gold with the Master Trader perk, making them useful even after the Fence perk is unlocked. Enthir is the exception to this; his gold remains at 500 or 1500 with the Master Trader perk.

## Shadowmarks
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-shadowmark-Danger.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-shadowmark-Danger.png) Danger! The Thieves Guild is also responsible for the various symbols or runes, called [Shadowmarks](Skyrim_Shadowmarks_(Thieves_Guild).md), etched or printed all around Skyrim.

These marks are codes that previous thieves have left for future thieves. The legend for the code is outlined in a [book](Skyrim_Shadowmarks_(book).md) of the same name, authored by [Delvin Mallory](Skyrim_Delvin_Mallory.md). There is a copy of this book in [The Ragged Flagon - Cistern](Skyrim_The_Ragged_Flagon_-_Cistern.md).
