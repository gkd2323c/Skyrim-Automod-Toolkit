[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/OB-place-Anvil.jpg/200px-OB-place-Anvil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Anvil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Anvil.jpg) Anvil, circa 3E 433 Quill-Weave was a famous [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) author who lived in [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) in the final years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), and the onset of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). She penned a collection of popular novels such as *[Red Crater](Lore_Mentioned_Texts.md)* and *[The Goblin with the Golden Arm](Lore_Mentioned_Texts.md)* and was known for her belief that the [Doomstones](Lore_Doomstones.md) carried no traces of [magical](Lore_Magic.md) power of any kind, instead linking them to the activities of long-forgotten cults and ancient sky worship of the [Beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) of the [Mythic Era](Lore_Mythic_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NDS-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-QW-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/OB-place-The_Tower_Stone.jpg/200px-OB-place-The_Tower_Stone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-The_Tower_Stone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-The_Tower_Stone.jpg) A Doomstone Little is known of Quill-Weave's past. She was known to spend a lot of her time traveling to gather material and to do research for her books. She was known for her acrobatic skills and was known to offer her services as a trainer.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-QW-2)</sup>

As of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) she was already a famous and accomplished author. At least two of her novels: *[Red Crater](Lore_Mentioned_Texts.md)* and *[The Goblin with the Golden Arm](Lore_Mentioned_Texts.md)* were published as of then.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NDS-1)</sup> Although those novels were known to revolve around the tales of lower classes, thieves, outlaws, murderers, and criminals they were extremely popular, giving Quill-Weave fame both in Cyrodiil and throughout the Empire's provinces.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NDS-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GTA-3)</sup>

During that time Quill-Weave was working on her new series of historical fiction set in the times of early [Reman Empire](Lore_Reman_Empire.md). She planned a cycle of twenty-one books, collectively titled *The Paths of the Doomstones*, with each installment named after a Cyrodilic Doomstone. Unlike her previous works, she aimed to focus on more mature and epic themes to celebrate the noble virtues of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the Empire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NDS-1)</sup> Quill-Weave was noted to be a well-known resident of Anvil, mentioned in *[Guide to Anvil](Lore_Guide_to_Anvil.md)* by the biased author Alessia Ottus, who slandered Quill-Weave in her writing due to her bigotry.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GTA-3)</sup>

During the same year, Quill-Weave was caught leaving rotten meat outside her neighbor Arvena Thelas' home to lure out her pet [rats](Lore_Rat.md) so the local guards could deal with them, causing [mountain lions](Lore_Mountain_Lion.md) to enter the streets of Anvil and harm people. She was unaware of the potential consequences of her actions, and hoped to convince the witness - a [member](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) of the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) to keep this incident a secret. It is unclear whether she faced any consequences for her wrongdoings, but despite that, she continued to reside in Anvil and retained her popularity as an author.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-QW-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AT-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARP-5)</sup>
