# Wardens

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-render-Warden.jpg/200px-ON-render-Warden.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Warden.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Warden.jpg) A Warden using nature magic Wardens are worshippers of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) and defenders of [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md). They are master storytellers whose nature tales become [magical reality](Lore_Nature_Magic.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOWarden-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Warden_Costume-2)</sup> Wardens weave spells to cause plants and fungi to grow rapidly. They can also call animals to come to their aid, and wield frost spells to combat their foes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOWarden-1)</sup> Wardens of all races have been found protecting [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s wilds, and as a whole they do not bow to any specific organized group or guild.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WATW-3)</sup> For centuries they were mostly silent, focused on watching, guiding, and protecting the Green, but they were called to rejoin the world of man during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TESOMWFT-4)</sup> Despite their unique magical practices, the term warden was considered a fancy title with little relevance to some magic educators.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WAA-5)</sup>

The scholar [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) claimed that Wardens are nothing more than a militant group of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md), though this is contested by his rival [Lady Cinnabar of Taneth](Lore_Lady_Cinnabar.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WATW-3)</sup> Both groups may worship Y'ffre, but Wardens can be of any race, while only Bosmer can be Spinners. Additionally, Spinners are typically nonviolent, while Wardens are more than willing to shed blood to varying degrees of those who seek to harm the wilds. Another difference is that Wardens draw upon the environment to shape reality, and cast powerful spells utilizing conjuration or alteration magic, while Spinners specialize in entrancing their audience in stories via illusions. Lastly, Wardens do not follow the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) either (excluding those who are both Warden and Bosmer), as they're willing to make full use of nature's bounty to survive.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WATW-3)</sup> Magic used to corrupt and twist nature can damage the connection a Warden has with the Green.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Amaurel Dia-6)</sup>

A Warden corresponded with Dhulef, the court mage of [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md), requesting information on the warriors of the Green as a part of the interview. After some heated arguments between Druid Laurel of the [Stonelore](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md) and some [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) members (some of which are Wardens themselves), it was speculated that Wardens and [Druids](Lore_Druid.md) are related, but moreso in the heart rather than in the mind. Druids use the True Way to guide their magics, while Wardens feel Nirn calling to them in their gut. Wardens are also possibly being a way for the old world to walk tall in the new, with Wardens being the Green fighting back.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LATD-7)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WAA-5)</sup>

## History
In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), tattoos symbolizing the intertwining of all life forms were popular among those who feel a connection with Nature in Tamriel, including Wardens.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Serpentine_Interlace_Body-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Serpentine_Interlace_Face-9)</sup> Warden's Moss Green is a hue named after the Wardens.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-WDye-10)</sup>

At some point in history before the Three Banners War, a [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) blacksmith began to breed bears in honor of a fallen Warden.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Graysmoke_Bear-11)</sup> The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) created [magical gauntlets](Lore_Psijic_Gauntlets.md) named after them, suited to the usage of Nature-based magic.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PWG-12)</sup> During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) of the Second Era, wardens were employed by the alliances.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-KEW-13)</sup>

Sometime in 2E 582, [Boldekh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boldekh), a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) warrior on Nature's behalf who was sent by [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) to help [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md) assassinate the [grandmaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rythe_Verano) of the Morag Tong.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-NJVb-14)</sup> That same year, [King Camoran](Lore_Aeradan_Camoran.md) was asked to send Wardens to a valley in the Tenmar mountains, for it to be cleansed of [Maarselok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Maarselok)'s taint.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONSN-15)</sup> A student of Second Era Traditions, [Bahtra at-Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bahtra_at-Hunding) discussed the topics of the Green and the ways of Wardens with the [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md) [Gwaering](Lore_Gwaering.md) around that time.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
