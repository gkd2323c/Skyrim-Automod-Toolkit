Saint Meris the Peacemaker (also spelled **Merris**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-spellrest-1)</sup> is the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) patron [saint](Lore_Temple_Saints.md) of Farmers and Laborers. As a little girl, Saint Meris showed healing gifts, and trained as a Healer. She ended a long and bloody House War, intervening on the battlefield in her white robe to heal warriors and wizards on both sides without discrimination. [Great House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) troops adopted white robes as her standard, and disobeyed orders to fight.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Lot S-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWdialogue-3)</sup> Meris was later canonized by the Tribunal, and shrines to her could be found in Tribunal temples.

All [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) shrine offerings grant cures of common diseases, of blight diseases, and of afflictions of poison. Offerings made to the shrine of Saint Meris additionally grant Meris' Warding, for resistance to [corprus disease](Lore_Corprus.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-mwshrine-5)</sup> Additionally, various restoration [spells](Lore_Spells.md) named after Meris were known to be used and sold by healers of Vvardenfell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-spellrest-1)</sup>

In the late Third Era, Saint Meris was believed to be interred in a dedicated tomb within the temple of Tel Dresori. The Swords of St. Meris are five legendary artifacts that will bring despair to the [undead](Lore_Undead.md). If they are used in war against the Great Houses such as [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) or [Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), it would be considered a sacrilege.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-6)</sup>

During the [Ordinator](Lore_Ordinators.md) vestment routine, initiates recite a prayer to St. Meris as they help don the ordinator their cuisses.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
