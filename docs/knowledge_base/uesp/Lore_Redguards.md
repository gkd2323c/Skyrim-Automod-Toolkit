# Redguard

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/80/LO-race-Redguard.png/200px-LO-race-Redguard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Redguard.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Redguard.png) Illustration of a typical Redguard male Redguards are a [human](Lore_Human.md) race that primarily inhabits the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), but have roots that can be traced back to the continent of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCCAR-1)</sup> Known for their unique martial culture, they are reputed as brilliant battlefield tacticians<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONILES-2)</sup> who are quick to adopt new styles of armor and weaponry. They are the most naturally talented warriors in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWGDR-3)</sup> Hammerfell was formerly known as the Deathlands, as it hosts an arid climate with stifling, sandy winds, baking rocks that would brand one's feet, and terrible creatures that dwell in the crags and dunes. Regardless, conquering this hostile environment has become synonymous with Redguard culture.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCCAR-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup> Aside from being physically blessed with hardy constitutions, they also have a resistance to poison and fleetness of foot.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBRedguard-5)</sup> Redguards are known for their love of traveling, adventuring, and sailing the high seas: they can be seen commonly throughout Tamriel as sailors and mercenaries.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWGDH-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup>

Redguards are known for their tall, gaunt frames and finely toned physiques.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> They possess above average human strength and agility, and show a remarkable capacity for surviving in hot, dry climates. Redguards are characterized by relatively dark skin, and wavy or curly hair.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DUG-8)</sup> Tattoos and body piercings are common.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

## History

### The Precursors

- → *For more detailed information, see: [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md)*

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) A map showing [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), the ancient home of the Redguards Redguards hail from the Yokudans, the humans from the western continent of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). The creation myth from *[The Annotated Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)* suggests that Yokuda was once a part of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), but was ripped from the mainland and became a separate landmass after a destructive war among the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md). After this destructive war, the Ehlnofey split into many different racial and aboriginal groups. The group of Ehlnofey that remained on Yokuda eventually became the Yokudans.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TAA-9)</sup>

Yokuda would sink to the sea in ancient times, with the cause attributed either to natural factors (earthquakes, tsunamis, or volcanic eruptions) or to the revenge of a defeated band of [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) called the Hiradirge. Upon the sinking of their homeland around [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792), the Yokudan fleet set sail to the east, eventually arriving in what would later be called Hammerfell.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-10)</sup> The bulk of the refugees remained on the island of [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) while the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), the Warrior Wave, continued on to mainland Hammerfell in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HOTIB-11)</sup> According to residents of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) during the late- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), ships still sailed to Yokuda from Anvil,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Oblivion-12)</sup> and some texts referred to the continent as if it still exists in some form.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1P-13)</sup>

[Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholars concluded that the term "Redguard" originated as a phonetic corruption of "Ra Gada".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> However, Redguard myth considers it a legacy of the [War of the Singers](Lore_War_of_the_Singers.md), a series of seven battles in Yokuda circa [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780), shortly before the continent was destroyed. Their legends say the war was fought between the army of the Last Emperor [Hira](Lore_Hira.md) and the greatly outnumbered Ansei (presumably the Hiradirge), who sought out the leadership of Lord [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) at the onset. Hunding led the Ansei to victory using his "Hammer and the Anvil" strategy, but at great cost. Though they spared the land from Hira's tyranny, they had to leave it afterwards, for when Hunding's strategy reached fruition in the final battle (i.e., when the proverbial hammer fell), more than three hundred thousand people were killed. In the eyes of the citizenry, their guardians were red with blood. So they left Yokuda, sailing across the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md), and took on new names to commemorate their final battle: their new land was Hammerfell, and their new name was the Redguards.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RTHATH-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DTS-15)</sup> As Yokuda sank into the sea, the Yokudans were forced to follow them to this new land or perish.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-10)</sup> For Hunding's role in leading the settlement of the Redguard people in Tamriel, he is sometimes known as the "first Redguard".<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DFHoliday-16)</sup>

These were not the first Redguards to arrive in Tamriel, however. The [Silverhoof Horsemen](Lore_Silverhoof_Horsemen.md) of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), long thought to be a myth, were confirmed to exist by scholars in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They are believed to have migrated there from the Yokudan island of [Akos Kasaz](Lore_Akos_Kasaz.md) sometime in the early sixth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-THFOS-17)</sup> Even earlier Redguards were known to inhabit central Tamriel in the late fifth century of the First Era.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_.28Afareen.29)</sup>

#### The Ra Gada Invasion

- → *For more detailed information, see: [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md)*

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Hammerfell_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Hammerfell_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Hammerfell_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg) Hammerfell, the current home of the Redguards When they arrived, the early Redguards faced a [Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) invasion, but drove them away. Later when more Ra Gada arrived after the sinking of Yokuda, the Ra Gada attacked the settlements of [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-NOTDL-18)</sup> [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), [Mer](Lore_Mer.md),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup> as well as the native beastfolk and creatures.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup> They conscripted those they did not slay as laborers or servants for the "warrior-sailors" of the Ra Gada.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-STJOA-20)</sup> After only a few brutal months, the Ra Gada had established a strong presence along the western shores of Hammerfell, disrupting and replacing the Imperial and [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) raiders who had preyed upon the area for centuries.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE1C-21)</sup> From the footholds along the coast, they continued to launch assault after assault, eventually succeeding in driving off the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and making way for the High King and the Yokudan royalty, known as the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md), to safely arrive in Hammerfell.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup> All the old cities of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) in the region, with a few exceptions, are now the cities of today's modern Hammerfell.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-NFRH-22)</sup>

Despite how long ago they arrived, the Redguards are still the comparative newcomers: every other modern race had already emerged before the Ra Gada arrived at Tamriel's shores.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup> And despite the advanced culture they brought with them, their martial nature and the force with which they established themselves in Tamriel earned them a longstanding reputation as barbarians and cutthroats.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup>

Later in the First Era, the Redguards came under threat from a second, seemingly unstoppable invasion of giant goblins. However, they were thwarted thanks to the leadership of [Derik Hallin](Lore_Hallin.md), who restored the teachings of [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) and his [Book of Circles](#The_Book_of_Circles), which have remained an integral part of Redguard life ever since.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-NFRH-22)</sup>

#### Foreign Relations
*Thy race is a people without a past, for in the Tiber War thou wert stalwart guardians of Hammerfell. As time moved on thy people held onto the ground for which they had shed tears of blood, and now the land, and the strength and endurance of rock itself, is thine own to command...* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Honor%27s_Rest_03.jpg) Baloth Bloodtusk and Gaiden Shinji, assassinated by King Joile's general during the Siege of Orsinium In [1E 948](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_948), [King Joile](Lore_Joile.md) of Daggerfall sent a letter to [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md) of the [Order of Diagna](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md), proposing a joint attack on Orsinium. Joile cited that the Orcs were raiding along the [Bjoulsae](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) and impeding traffic to the heart of the land as reason enough to make war against them. Agreeing to provide aid, an alliance was formed between the Order of Diagna and the armies of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and Sentinel. The armies amassed and began a 30-year long campaign for the destruction of Orc's homeland known as the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-WJOTB-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-PGE3O-24)</sup> In that time, King Joile planned to betray the Redguards and invade Hammerfell. Joile and his Reachman general [Mercedene](Lore_Mercedene.md) enacted a plot to kill Gaiden Shinji and Orcish hero [Baloth Bloodtusk](Lore_Baloth_Bloodtusk.md), which succeeded, despite the efforts of Daggerfall's own [Knights of the Dragon](Lore_Knights_of_the_Dragon.md) to stop the attempt and their defeat of General Mercedene.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> In [1E 973](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_973), Joile's invasion of Hammerfell proceeded, but he was stopped by the Ansei warrior [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md) at the [Bangkorai Pass](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> The Siege of Orsinium finally ended in [1E 980](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_980) when the allied forces breached the city's defenses, overran it, and razed it to the ground.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-PGE3O-24)</sup>

The Redguards initially scorned their neighbors, and didn't even trade with them until after they had proven their worth in the first Siege of Orsinium.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup> Their own native language, Yoku, was almost entirely replaced to help stabilize foreign trade,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> although the language is still spoken.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LOS-27)</sup> [<sup>page number</sup>](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Pages_Missing_Data) They were absorbed into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) sometime during the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-wallpaper-Daggerfall_Covenant-1920x1200.jpg/200px-ON-wallpaper-Daggerfall_Covenant-1920x1200.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Daggerfall_Covenant-1920x1200.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-wallpaper-Daggerfall_Covenant-1920x1200.jpg) Races of the Daggerfall Covenant In [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), king [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md) of Shornhelm [invaded](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) Wayrest, furious with [Emeric of Cumberland](Lore_Emeric.md) for rejecting his daughter's hand to instead wed [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md), the daughter of King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md) of Sentinel. He was defeated at [Markwasten Moor](Lore_Markwasten_Moor.md) by the forces of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) with the help of the Orcs, who were promised to have Orsinium returned for their aid.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TFo KR-28)</sup> The Orcs aiding King Emeric would push Ranser over the edge, and he ordered his Spymaster to round up many Orcs in Rivenspire to be interrogated and executed. The Spymaster would end up murdering hundreds of innocent Orcs throughout the course of war.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Marisette-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Gerard-30)</sup>

These events led to the formation of the Greater Daggerfall Covenant, an alliance between Emeric, the Redguard king Fahara'jad and the Orcish king [Kurog gro-Orsinium](Lore_Kurog_gro-Orsinium.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Toa M-31)</sup> Trade flourished under the alliance, making the Covenant an economic powerhouse.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-THOTE-32)</sup> The Covenant later fought in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) during King Emeric's bid for the throne of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Toa M-31)</sup> The kings of the Daggerfall Covenant looked to [Reman](Lore_Reman_I.md) as a model Emperor, viewing themselves as the spiritual heirs to the Second Empire.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-33)</sup> Focused on restoring the glory of Reman's legacy, the Covenant cited its focus on freedom, worship of the Divines, and primacy of trade as reasons it was meant to be the center of Cyrodiil's power.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-THOTE-32)</sup> The strongest support for the Covenant centered in the city-states of [Hallin's Stand](Lore_Hallin%27s_Stand.md) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), with much of the outer regions of Redguard society remaining distrustful of their new allies.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup>

Sometime during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the fragile administrative republic established in Hammerfell by the Second Empire collapsed, and control of the province reverted back to the traditional, hereditary monarchy of the Na-Totambu. The new High King of Hammerfell moved his seat of power from the old capital of [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) to the more prosperous northern city of Sentinel. This ignited tension between [Forebears and Crowns](#Forebears_and_Crowns), as Sentinel was a Forebear city. Upon the death of High King [Thassad II](Lore_Thassad_II.md) in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862), the people of Sentinel retook their city by force. Crown Prince [A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md) journeyed to the city to seek vengeance, leading to one of the bloodiest massacres in Tamrielic history.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> The bloody civil war that ensued led to Hammerfell being easily absorbed by the [Septim Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), though the Empire soon had to make some significant concessions of power following a successful revolt in [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/MW-race-Redguard_03.jpg/200px-MW-race-Redguard_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-race-Redguard_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-race-Redguard_03.jpg) A Redguard warrior during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) Redguards would become more outgoing under the Septim Empire, and many branched out into other parts of Tamriel. Redguards reportedly served as mercenaries under the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) around [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TFOTU-34)</sup>

[King Camaron](Lore_Camaron.md) of Sentinel would be killed during the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) in [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403), a short but vicious land dispute with [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) over the island of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-NWOB-35)</sup> The aftermath brought a lot of bad blood between the Bretons and Redguards of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-DFC-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-FWOB-37)</sup> Following the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Sentinel (then under [King Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md)) greatly expanded to take over the entire northern coast of Hammerfell.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TWITW-38)</sup> The Redguards of eastern Hammerfell harbor a fierce grudge against [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), yearning for a chance to take back land that was seized during the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) in [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-DFC-36)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) and the destabilization of the Third Empire, the combined forces of Hammerfell and High Rock sacked Orsinium, leading many Orc refugees to flee east into Skyrim.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LOS-27)</sup>

Hammerfell was still part of the Empire when [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) ascended the throne of Cyrodiil in [4E 168](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_168). In [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171), the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) erupted when the Third Aldmeri Dominion demanded, in part, that the new Emperor Titus Mede II cede large tracts of southern Hammerfell to them. The Empire fought back, and High Rock assisted Hammerfell during the Great War. The Dominion took Hammerfell's southern coastline, leaving Hegathe the lone surviving state. Those who survived the March of Thirst joined Legion reinforcements from High Rock in northern Hammerfell in [4E 173](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_173), and the Dominion suffered losses in the province soon after. The Dominion withdrew from the Redguards' home in [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TGW-39)</sup>

Forces comprised of Redguards, Imperials, Nords and Bretons participated in the [Battle of the Red Ring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Red_Ring.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175). It was the climax of the war, and the last major conflict to occur before the signing of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md). Though the rest of the Empire left them to their fate, the Redguards refused to back down and suffered mass devastation all over southern Hammerfell in their long, though successful, repulsion of the Dominion's invasion. Many Redguards remain bitter over the Empire's abandonment, believing that their triumph is proof that the Concordat was a mistake, and the Empire would have succeeded had it fought on.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TGW-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-LJ-40)</sup> However, although the White-Gold Concordat's terms were considered harsh, some accounts maintain that the Imperial armies were in no condition to fight on, and that prolonging the conflict against the Aldmeri Dominion would likely have ended in Imperial defeat.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TGW-39)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup>

## Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/1/13/LO-people-Frandar_Hunding.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Frandar_Hunding.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Frandar_Hunding.jpg) Frandar Hunding, a Sword-Singer, Warrior-Prince, and pioneer of Hammerfell
### Holidays
Many of the existing holidays in modern day Hammerfell appear to originate from before the Redguard's arrival in Tamriel, some which have been changed to fit their new homeland.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HOTIB-11)</sup>

[Ovank'a](Lore_Ovank%27a.md)
- falls on the 12th of Morning Star and is the day the people of the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) offer prayers to Stendarr in the hopes of a mild and merciful year. It is considered very holy.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-45)</sup>
[Day of Lights](Lore_Day_of_Lights.md)
- The Day of Lights is celebrated on the 16th Morning Star as a holy day by most villages in Hammerfell on the Iliac Bay. It is a prayer for a good farming and fishing year, and is taken very seriously.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-46)</sup>
[Aduros Nau](Lore_Aduros_Nau.md)
- On the 28th of Sun's Dawn, the villages in the [Bantha](Lore_Bantha.md) celebrate the baser urges that come with Springtide on Aduros Nau. The traditions vary from village to village, but none of them are for the overly virtuous.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-47)</sup>
[Festival of Blades](Lore_Festival_of_Blades.md)
- Falling on the 26th of [First Seed](Lore_First_Seed.md), it is a holiday created in honor of the first Redguard's victory over [Malooc](Lore_Malooc.md)'s Horde, an army of giant [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) that lived in Hammerfell.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-48)</sup>
[Day of Shame](Lore_Day_of_Shame.md)
- Held on the 20th of Rain's Hand, all along the seaside of Hammerfell, no one leaves their houses on the Day of Shame. It is said that the Crimson Ship, a vessel filled with victims of the Knahaten Plague who were refused refuge hundreds of years ago, will return on this day.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-49)</sup>
[Koomu Alezer'i](Lore_Koomu_Alezer%27i.md)
- It is a holiday held on the 17th of [Second Seed](Lore_Second_Seed.md) and observed by the Redguards of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). It is a day made in remembrance of the city's founder, [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md) the Seafarer, who discovered the city's harborage on that day in the early-mid First Era by following [the Steed](Lore_The_Steed.md) constellation and named it after the watchman who first saw it.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-STJOA-20)</sup>
[Drigh R'Zimb](Lore_Drigh_R%27Zimb.md)
- This festival is held on the 1st of Mid Year, which is the hottest time of year in [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md). It is a jubilation held for the sun Daibethe itself. Scholars do not know how long Drigh R'Zimb has been held, but it is possible the Redguards brought the festival with them when they came in the First Era.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-50)</sup>
[Divad Etep't](Lore_Divad_Etep%27t.md)
- Held on the 12th of Sun's Height, during this holiday the people of [Antiphyllos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Antiphyllos) mourn the death of one of the greatest of the early Redguard heroes, [Divad](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md), son of Frandar of the Hel Ansei. His deeds are questioned by historians, but his tomb in Antiphyllos is almost certainly genuine.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-51)</sup>
[Fiery Night](Lore_Fiery_Night.md)
- Few besides the natives of the Alik'r Desert would venture out on the hottest day of the year, Fiery Night. It's a lively celebration falling on the 29th of Sun's Height, with a meaning lost in antiquity.
[Maiden Katrica](Lore_Maiden_Katrica.md)
- A celebration for Katrica, a warrior-maiden that is associated with the Redguards. They often use the exclamation, *"by Katrica"*, or *"by the Maiden"*.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-DFRaces-52)</sup> Many years ago, the county of [Ayasofya](Lore_Ayasofya.md) was attacked by an unknown threat but was saved Katrica. Ever since then, on the 2nd of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md), the county celebrates her victory and their salvation with the holiday, Maiden Katrica, a large celebration.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-53)</sup>
[Feast of the Tiger](Lore_Feast_of_the_Tiger.md)
- Held on the 14th of Last Seed, the Feast of the Tiger in the Bantha rainforest is like other holidays in praise of a bountiful harvest. It is not, however, a solemn occasion for introspection and thanksgiving, but a great celebration and festival from village to village.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-54)</sup>
[Riglametha](Lore_Riglametha.md)

### Food
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/BK-misc-Official_Cookbook_Redguard_Rice.png/200px-BK-misc-Official_Cookbook_Redguard_Rice.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Official_Cookbook_Redguard_Rice.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Official_Cookbook_Redguard_Rice.png) Redguard Rice
- → *For more detailed information, see: [Redguard Cuisine](Lore_Redguard_Cuisine.md)*

The Redguards live upon the arid land of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md), which makes farming an arduous task. They allow domesticated livestock (such as goats) to graze, and their crops need to be able to resist drought. They often dehydrate or salt their food for storage and trade. Everyday meals, on the other hand, are cooked using methods that don't exacerbate the intense heat of the sun during the day: flatbreads are baked within seconds on searing hot stones instead of ovens, and pots of stew are placed over coals to cook over long periods of time. Their port cities bring in trade that adds variety to their diet, and the hot peppers that grow in their homeland are prized both by them and cooks overseas.

### Language

- → *For more detailed information, see: [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md)*

Yoku, sometimes called Old Redguard, Old Yokudan, or High-Yokudan, was the ancient native language of Yokuda. After the Yokudan migration to Hammerfell around 1E 808, the settlers began to assimilate into the larger Tamrielic theater and the language was almost entirely replaced to help stabilize foreign trade. However, certain groups of Yokudans maintained their language and traditions outside of mainstream Redguard society. By the late Third Era, the language was still spoken, albeit rarely. There is a regional variant of Yoku known as the Banthan dialect. It derives from the eponymous region of Bantha.

Yoku has 48 different versions of the word "honor", which all trace their roots to ugak-ta.

In the desert wastelands of Hammerfell, the most common mount used by the Redguards is the camel, which they count on to survive.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ESOMHC-77)</sup> Camels are used as military mounts, as civil mounts and as livestock, from which locals obtain its meat, hide, and milk.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ESOCLRBF-78)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ESOCHerds-79)</sup> White camels are said to be sacred to Zeht, the Redguard god of food and farming. Redguards have a great affinity for [horses](Lore_Horse.md), and brought many with them to Hammerfell.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-THFOS-17)</sup> The most notable among these steeds are the [Yokudan Chargers](Lore_Yokudan_Charger.md), a breed found in the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md) of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-LOTYC-82)</sup> They are descendants of the gray Yokudan Warhorses brought from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) by the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md).<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ONYokudan-83)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ACTB-84)</sup> These horses are exceptional for their ability to traverse the Alik'r Desert's terrain, unlike other horse breeds. ### Architecture
Redguard buildings vary from simple tents to the sturdy domed structures of the cities which incorporate many Yokudan advancements. The latter are renowned for their rigidity, flexibility, heat transference, and general ornate beauty. They are much easier to repair than most Imperial stonemasonry. While much Imperial construction requires demolition and rebuilding after conflict, many of the Yokudan accent pieces, such as the flying staircases or ornamental domes, are designed to crumble after an attack or an earthquake, which allows them to be easily replaced in sections afterward. However, these structures are still solidly built; they stand tall and firm against the harsh environment of Hammerfell.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup> They are characterized by thickly-built walls to help insulate against the heat of the desert, as well as a carefully-planned system of ventilation ducts. These ducts are built alongside windows high on the walls, and are covered by horizontal slats which keep out the sun while permitting the wind to enter. Each building is designed to channel even the slightest breeze from these ducts down to the floor.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-RM6TR-64)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/ON-concept-Desert_water_catcher.png/200px-ON-concept-Desert_water_catcher.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Desert_water_catcher.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Desert_water_catcher.png) Concept art of a johad, or desert water catcher (ESO) Redguard stables have been recognized for their lavishness and the ingenious design which makes them perfect for the environmental hazards the Redguards face. Their enormous mausoleums are thought to be the purest, undiluted representations of Yokudan architecture in their society. The most famous example, [Tu'whacca's Throne](Lore_Tu%27whacca%27s_Throne.md) in the Alik'r, is also a monument to the many Yokudans who died when the continent was destroyed, and it receives many pilgrims seeking to pay their respects.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup>

## Society
Like all races, Redguards have been shaped by their home. Since their arrival, they have been few in number, relative to the other races of Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup> Their Yokudan heritage left them with advanced seafaring, agricultural, military and even astronomical knowledge that allowed them to thrive in a place where others merely hoped to survive. Redguard society is extremely martial, and nearly everyone is expected to have a grasp of basic weaponry and combat, although only the rulers are generally expected to have detailed knowledge of strategy, formations and tactics. Only the strongest, fastest and smartest Redguards are accepted into the demanding military (which consists mainly of various knightly orders; Redguards don't traditionally have a standing army), and they are expected to prove themselves worthy by facing death.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> Even their entertainment, like snake charming, bears some risk to it.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-MOT1-98)</sup>

They are a highly disciplined and enterprising people, having centuries of experience with warfare in their homeland before arriving on Tamriel's shores. Their warriors are acknowledged as among the best in the world. The Redguards are also known for their naval prowess, and their fleets have proved to be a match for the Empire's best armadas, a fact demonstrated during the Stros M'Kai revolt.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-Redguard-99)</sup> Stros M'Kai and some parts of mainland Hammerfell hold many Dwemer ruins, and some young Redguards seeking to enter military service must brave the dangers of these ruins to prove themselves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> Most of their holidays and traditions seem to revolve around either the celebration of natural phenomena or the commemoration of great battles and warriors.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HOTIB-11)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RTHATH-14)</sup><sup>[[99]](#cite_note-G-100)</sup> The constellation of the [Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md) and its charges seem to be of great importance in their ancient literature.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-TWC-101)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-HTYCTS-102)</sup>

Redguards were known to be able to harness the powers of the air and sand associated with deserts. Magic wielded by Alik'r wizards was focused into reflection and purpose. Shifting sands and air are one of the key components not only of the desert, but of its natural magics.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-WAS-103)</sup> However, the practice of magic has generally been frowned upon since the First Era,<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-ESOShalidor-104)</sup> and outsiders are not tolerated in many parts of Hammerfell.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Cyrus-105)</sup> Some believe that no "true" Redguard warrior would stoop to using magic, considering it a weakness.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ONSN-106)</sup> Certain schools that teach the Way of the Sword forbid students to use magic and enchanted weapons.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-ONIman-107)</sup> In the Third Era, spellcasters were often shunned in Hammerfell, with many believing them to be wicked individuals who steal souls and tamper with minds.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ONTray-108)</sup> [Necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) has always been abhorred in Redguard culture due to their [reverence for the dead](Lore_Redguard.md#Religion). Although the Redguard suspicion of magic is regarded as a hallmark of their culture, it was not always so pronounced: in ancient times, Yokudan war-wizards were part of their armies, albeit rare.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-CM20Y-70)</sup> Sword-singers were said to have forged swords woven with magic, and indeed the [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md) itself is believed to be a form of magic.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RTHATH-14)</sup>

Redguards have engaged in cultural exchange with a variety of other races. [Carlovac Townway](Lore_Carlovac_Townway.md) (author of *[2920, The Last of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)*) once asserted that Redguard culture and their greatest heroes have been heavily influenced by [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) and [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) culture, specifically citing Gaiden Shinji of the [Order of Diagna](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md) as an example of this cultural exchange.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Interview-109)</sup> The Redguards also adopted some of the religion, customs, and language of the Nedes they annihilated.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> Around Redguard cosmopolitan coastal cities, the inhabitants have adopted Breton and Imperial influenced fashion and architecture with a blend of Yokudan flair.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> The two races the Redguards are closest in proximity to, the Bretons and the Orcs, are their historical enemies:<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONILES-2)</sup> however, they have allied with each other at times.

Stuffed lizards are common gifts for children. They're sometimes made to wear little tailored clothes.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-110)</sup>

### Forebears and Crowns
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg) The Forebears kneel before Tiber Septim The social strata of the Redguard has long been fractured.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup> When Hammerfell became a province of the Second Empire and demarcated in the Imperial custom, Redguard society was formally divided into two main socio-political groups: the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> The Crowns are descended from the High King and the Na-Totambu who ruled in Yokuda; they hold Yokudan tradition in high reverence and greatly dislike foreigners. Their influence has been waning since the last High King perished in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862) and his son, the Crown Prince [A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md), reacted by viciously butchering the Forebear citizenry. When Tiber Septim's forces conquered the land two years later, it was perceived as a humanitarian gesture to stop the senseless bloodshed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup> While the intervention did cease open war, the hostility between the various regions of the province remains so great that some have been known to ignore their brethren when they're trying to repulse a foreign invasion.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/af/RG-sign-Crowns.png/200px-RG-sign-Crowns.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-sign-Crowns.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-sign-Crowns.png) Symbol for the Crowns The Forebears are descended from the Ra Gada warrior class which conquered the province, and in so doing were exposed to many Nedic (and later Breton and Imperial) traditions and ideas, while the ancestors of the Crowns were not. The Forebears seek modernity; they are more cosmopolitan than their Crown counterparts and more welcoming of foreigners and different ways of life. They have adopted modified Imperial and Breton styles for their dress, architecture, and [names](Lore_Redguard_Names.md), and many have even reorganized their traditional gods and spirits to fit into the Imperial pantheon of the [Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md). Forebears are generally predominant in coastal cities and other major trade centers in the north, while most Crowns live in more secluded regions in the south, notably the deserts and other inhospitable areas.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), a third faction emerged. Named the [Lhotunics](Lore_Lhotunics.md) after their founder King Lhotun, they espouse the progressive and cosmopolitan values of the Forebears while maintaining a strong respect for their past, though the specific compromises are not known.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup> The movement is largely political: the Forebear kingdom of Sentinel is uniquely well-suited to forge a compromise between the two Redguard factions because it has been a seat of both Forebear and Crown power, and its economic and military strength has made it one of the most powerful and respected kingdoms in all of Tamriel.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup> Since the Miracle of Peace and beyond, the Lhotunic movement has served as a unifying force for Sentinel as it gains dominion over more territory, and since much of that territory is dominated by disgruntled Crowns, the necessity of the Lhotunic movement is apparent. Due to their moderate platform and these expansionist policies, the Lhotunics are generally disliked by the remaining Forebears and Crowns.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-19)</sup> However, the events of the Great War reportedly led to a reconciliation between the two factions.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TGW-39)</sup>

### Lhotunics
The [Lhotunics](Lore_Lhotunics.md) (the name Lhotun meaning **Second Boy** in [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md)) are the third faction in Redguard society that appeared after the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). During the Warp, the [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) became the dominant power in most of northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). However, most of Sentinel's new lands were [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md). To keep his kingdom together, [King Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md) was forced to create what some consider a third party, one with reverence for the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) past but respect for the Imperial ways. The moderate Lhotunics have attracted nothing but contempt from both the Forebears and the Crowns.

### Ash'abah
The [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) (roughly translated as **unclean people**) are an ancient and secretive tribe of Redguards who inhabit the northern wastes of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), roaming the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md). They are shunned by Redguard society due to their "unclean" interactions with the [risen dead](Lore_Necromancy.md), as they disregard religious taboos and destroy the undead, slaying with great skill what might be honored ancestors returned.

Against the widespread distrust of magic, the Ash'abah perform rituals of purification for mausoleums and other places where vengeful spirits might arise, including an annual ceremony at [Tu'whacca's Throne](Lore_Tu%27whacca%27s_Throne.md) meant to ensure the rest of the ranks of Redguard royalty. This activity is regarded as blasphemous, but tolerated due to the necessity of its performance. The Ash'abah considers the body to be sacred. If one ever becomes corrupted by necromancy, they strive to preserve the body while banishing the unholy essence. To achieve this, they have developed highly ritualistic methods of killing that involve uttering pleas of forgiveness and apology.

### Exiled Sun-Eaters
The [Exiled Sun-Eaters](Lore_Exiled_Sun-Eaters.md) of Numaneh were an exiled tribe of Redguards. Little is known about them, and as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), telling their tale was forbidden according to [the Unveiled Azadiyeh](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#The_Unveiled_Azadiyeh), the Songbird of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md), a storyteller, expert in old Redguard lore, and a [follower](Lore_Crown.md) the original [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) pantheon. They were listed alongside the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) and [Silverhoof Horsemen](Lore_Silverhoof_Horsemen.md) in response to the question about various isolated Redguard communities and tribes.

### Silverhoof Horsemen
The [Silverhoof Horsemen](Lore_Silverhoof_Horsemen.md) (also known as the **Horse-Folk of Silverhoof**, or simply **Horsemen**) are a tribe of Redguards abiding in the pastoral basin known as the [Vale of Silverhoof](Lore_Silverhoof_Vale.md). Described as horse nomads, the Horsemen take care of horse herds as their livelihood, and also train steeds for others in exchange for goods. The Horsemen venerate a divine animist spirit they call the Herd Mother.

### Sword-Singers
[Sword-singers](Lore_Sword-singers.md) (or **Sword Singers**) were an order of [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) warriors who followed the "Way of the Sword", a martial philosophy on blade mastery. Singers were capable of astonishing feats of swordsmanship, and were viewed by some to be as much mages as warriors. They sought strength of body, and of mind. Although the tradition of sword-singing originated in Yokuda, it was brought to the shores of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). The sword-singers were instrumental in claiming the entire province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and establishing a new homeland for the Yokudan people. The **Ansei** (or **Sword Saints**) were the greatest among them. The most accomplished sword-singer in history was [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md), whose writings on the subject were revered by the Redguards centuries later.

### Gallants of Hammerfell
The [Gallants of Hammerfell](Lore_Gallants_of_Hammerfell.md) (also called the **Knights of the Desert**, or simply **Gallants**) are expert sword-wielding warriors and horse-riders from the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), specifically the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) region. A gallant's main choice of steed is the [Yokudan Charger](Lore_Yokudan_Charger.md), a breed of [horse](Lore_Horse.md) that can traverse the Alik'r Desert better than other breeds. Gallants have several chapters across the Alik'r Desert and are based in the region's various cities, such as [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) and [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md). "Gallants" are the Redguard equivalent of the Tamrielic [knight](Lore_Knights.md). [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) god of justice, is their favorite deity.

## Religion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-interior-Tu%27whacca%27s_Pillars_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Tu%27whacca%27s_Pillars_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Tu%27whacca%27s_Pillars_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Tu%27whacca%27s_Pillars_02.jpg) A bas-relief depicting Yokudan gods in al-Danobia Tomb Redguards have come to venerate different gods, with the Crowns sticking to the most ancient Yokudan pantheon, while Forebear beliefs have evolved to include many Imperial influences. Many of the Crowns are nomadic tribesman who mainly travel around the Alik'r, and though they adhere to traditional Yokudan beliefs, they have also developed a deep, spiritual attachment to their adopted land. They venerate deities and spirits of all kinds, from agricultural gods to [Satakal the Worldskin](Lore_Satakal.md), the god of everything (a sort of fusion of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md) that bears similarities to the malevolent Nordic god [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md)).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-7)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-TA-111)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-VOF-112)</sup> Occasionally, Satakal will destroy everything to begin anew, and the spirits who manage to find a way to survive are those who can move at "strange angles" to stride between one world and the next, and thus enter the Redguard pantheon of gods.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-TM-113)</sup>

The Silverhoof Horsemen tribe of High Rock have substantially different religious beliefs than their brethren. They venerate a divine animist spirit they call the [Herd Mother](Lore_Herd_Mother.md). Their tradition holds that they left Yokuda in order to freely worship this equine deity.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-THFOS-17)</sup>

### Crown Gods
The Crown pantheon includes Satakal and the chieftain [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), or Tall Papa, the first to survive Satakal's destruction. They also venerate [Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md) (Yokudan God of Farms), Tall Papa's favorite wife [Morwha](Lore_Morwha.md), [Tava](Lore_Tava.md) (considered equivalent to [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md)), [Onsi](Lore_Onsi.md) (a god of war), and [Diagna](Lore_Diagna.md). Tu'whacca was thought to be the god of "Nobody Really Cares" before the creation of the world, when he became a caretaker and protector of souls (similar to [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md)).<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-VOFIT-114)</sup>

#### Satakal
[Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md) **the Worldskin**, also called the **First Serpent**, **Serpent God**, and the **World-Snake**, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) god of everything, and a fusion of the concepts of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md) ([Satak](Lore_Satak.md) and [Akel](Lore_Akel.md)), or the habitable universe resulting of their interaction, also called the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) or "The Gray Maybe". They can be considered analogous to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) in a way, being the first being "born" from the interplay of the original forces. Driven by hunger to eat one world to begin another, Satakal also has much in common with the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). In Yokudan mythology, Satakal has done (and still does) this many times over, a cycle which prompted the birth of spirits that could survive the transition, notably [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), the first who learned how to do so. These spirits ultimately became the Yokudan pantheon.

Satakal is a popular god of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) nomads. Redguard creation myth holds that they are doomed to mortality because they are very far away from the real world of Satakal, and the safe haven of the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md) is too far away for them to jump from the mortal world.

#### Ruptga
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/LO-misc-Tall Papa.jpeg/200px-LO-misc-Tall Papa.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Tall Papa.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Tall Papa.jpeg) Ruptga and his children [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), more commonly **Tall Papa**, is the chief deity of the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) and Redguard pantheon. He was the first god to figure out how to survive the Hunger of [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md).

#### Onsi
[Onsi](Lore_Onsi.md) (also known as the **Boneshaver**), is a notable warrior god of the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) and a part of the Redguard Pantheon. Onsi is said to have taught Mankind how to pull their knives into swords. He is mentioned several times in *[The Book of Circles](Lore_The_Book_of_Circles.md)* by [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md), who wrote that those forging a sword should recite the "Oblation of Onsi" upon completion. A tale of King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md)'s youth claimed that Onsi personally saved him from a vengeful [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md).

#### Diagna
[Diagna](Lore_Diagna.md), (also known as the **Orichalc God of the Sideways Blade**) was an avatar of the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding) (the Yokudan God of Make Way) that achieved permanence as a Redguard sword-god. His "hoary thuggish cult" originated in [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) during the Twenty Seven Snake Folk Slaughter. He was instrumental to the defeat of the [Lefthanded Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md), as he brought [orichalc](Lore_Orichalcum.md) [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) to the Yokudan people to win the fight. According to one myth, Diagna crashed his fist against the Hoon Ding Gong and swords fell from the walls of the Orichalc Tower.

In [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), he led a very tight-knit group of followers, called the [Order of Diagna](Lore_Order_of_Diagna.md), against the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) during the height of their ancient power, but then faded into partial obscurity. He is described as now being little more than a local power spirit of the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) but he is still worshipped as of the [4th Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), being part of the Eight of the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md).

High Kings of Hammerfell are crowned on the Throne of [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) by wearing the [Diadem of Diagna](Lore_Diadem_of_Diagna.md). Warriors of the Order of Diagna carried stone effigies of Diagna during the siege. The [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md)'s alliance of the Orcs and Redguards was alleged by the Unveiled Azadiyeh to have inflamed Diagna's very sword-arm with the Crimson Rash of Betrayal.

### Forebear Gods
The Forebear belief system includes several of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md) recognized by other cultures: [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), and [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md). However, it is often unclear if they have the same understanding of these deities that others possess, as they often use a name of one of the Divines when referring to a Yokudan deity. For instance, they continue to venerate the Bird God Tava, a favorite among many Redguard sailors, but they have been known to call her Kynareth. Similarly, they may refer to Tu'whacca as Arkay, Zeht as [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), and the four-armed fertility goddess Morwha as [Mara](Lore_Mara.md).<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-VOFIT-114)</sup><sup>[[113]](#cite_note-FLPOZ-115)</sup>

#### Akatosh
[Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) is the Dragon God of Time who is present in most religions in Tamriel.

#### Julianos
[Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), [Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) God of Wisdom and Logic, is one of the [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) [Divines](Lore_Divines.md), governing the realms of literature, law, history, and contradiction. He is usually associated with [magic](Lore_Magic.md), and thus is often revered by wizards. [Jhunal](Lore_Jhunal.md), the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) father of [language](Lore_Ehlnofex.md) and mathematics, fell out of favor in the Nordic pantheon, after which he became Julianos of the Divines. Monastic orders founded by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and dedicated to Julianos are the [keepers](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). He is particularly venerated among many [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) and [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), but he also sees worship among the [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) Redguards. As a Divine, he represents the virtue of learning, scholarship, and justice. His domains include sorcery, [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md). He is said to "incant the Damned Equation". His symbol is a triangle.

#### Dibella
[Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), also known as **the Lady**, the **Passion Dancer**, **Our Blessed Lady**, **Lady of Love**, and **Queen of Heaven**, is the **Goddess of Beauty**, Love, Affection, Art, and Music. She is one of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).

#### Stendarr
[Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), also known as **Stendarr the Steadfast**, is the **God of Mercy, Charity, Well-Earned Luck**, and **Justice**. He is also acclaimed as the **God of Compassion**, and **Righteous Rule by Might and Merciful Forbearance**. Stendarr is the **Apologist of [Men](Lore_Man.md)**, patron of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), magistrates, rulers, and knights errant, and one of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).

### Both Pantheons
Both the Crown and the Forebear pantheons can overlap, such as with the gods below.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/ON-statue-Morwha%27s_Blessing.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Morwha%27s_Blessing.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Morwha%27s_Blessing.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Morwha%27s_Blessing.jpg) Statue of Morwha
#### Tava
[Tava](Lore_Tava.md) (the **Bird God**, also known as the **Lady of the Air**) is the spirit of the air and goddess of weather in the Redguard pantheon, and has dominion over all elements of sun and storm, save only for the stars, which belong to [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md). Tava is most famous for leading the Yokudans to the isle of [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) after the destruction of their homeland. She has since become assimilated into the mythology of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md). She is still very popular in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) among sailors, and her shrines can be found in most port cities. In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the city of [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn.md) was called "Tava's Blessing" before being renamed after the Lainlyn family.

#### Tu'whacca
[Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) (or simply **Tuwhacca**), the **Tricky God**, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) **God of Souls** (or **Shepherd of Souls**), and the **Caretaker of the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md)**. Before the creation of the world, he was the god of **Nobody Really Cares**. When [Tall Papa](Lore_Ruptga.md) undertook the creation of the Walkabout, Tu'whacca found a purpose: he became the caretaker of the Far Shores, and continues to help Redguards find their way into the afterlife. Though a soul may have enemies who will try to keep it from reaching the Far Shores, Tu'Whacca is sly and clever, and always outwits them. His cult is sometimes associated with [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) in the more cosmopolitan, [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) regions of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), where the names Tu'whacca and Arkay are basically interchangeable. [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) detest this [Imperialization](Lore_Imperial.md), considering it a betrayal of their heritage. Nevertheless, scholars cannot help but note the clear similarities between them, as well as the similarities they both share with [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes_(god).md) of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), in both the etymology of their names and the divine roles they fill.

#### Morwha
[Morwha](Lore_Morwha.md), (also called the **Teat God**, **Lady Morwha**, and **Desire's Root**) is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) goddess of fertility and love. She is a fundamental deity in the Yokudan pantheon and is the favorite wife of [Tall Papa](Lore_Tall_Papa.md). Morwha is always portrayed as four-armed, so that she can 'grab more husbands'. Her shrines are the shape of a beehive. She was described by potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) as the lusty fertility goddess of the Yokudans, but not as lusty as [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md). She is sometimes depicted alongside her winged guardians, or surrounded by flames and spears.

#### Zeht
[Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md), Yokudan God of Farms, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) god of agriculture, civil law, mathematics, and food, but he still finds a place in the pantheon of the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md). He is similar to [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md), and sometimes worshiped in that name by the Forebears. He renounced his father after the world was created, which is why [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md) makes it so hard to grow food. He is the father of [Zeqqi](Lore_Zeqqi.md). He forsakes the land of [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md), preventing most food from growing there. Occasionally farming utensils of the Zeht's faithful such as digging spades are decorated with semi-precious stones and blessed by a priests of Zeht. The white [camel](Lore_Camel.md) is believed to be sacred to Zeht and pious Redguards tend to bow to passing Cloud Camels.

### Other Gods
There are several other deities with significant Redguard cults among both Crowns and Forebears. [Leki](Lore_Leki.md), the Saint of the Spirit Sword and divine daughter of Tall Papa, is credited with helping the Yokudans before their war against the [Lefthanded Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md) in the [Mythic Era](Lore_Mythic_Era.md). The [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding), the Make Way God, is the Yokudan spirit of perseverance over infidels, and is said to materialize when the Redguard need to "make way" for their people. They believe this spirit appeared twice during the Ra Gada invasion.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-VOFIT-114)</sup>

There is also the "Horde King" [Malooc](Lore_Malooc.md), an enemy of the Ra Gada who led goblins against them in the First Era.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-VOFIT-114)</sup> And finally, [Sep](Lore_Sep.md), believed to be the Yokudan variant of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md). Sep was a "crazy" merchant god who convinced the others to create the mortal world to make it easier for more spirits to survive Satakal's inevitable onslaught. Except it didn't make it easier; rather, the mortal plane acted as a trap which made apotheosis even harder.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-VOF-112)</sup> In this way, the Redguard view of creation has more in common with the Elven tradition than that of other humans, who view the mortal plane as a blessing.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-TM-113)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/ON-interior-Thieves_Den.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Thieves_Den.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Thieves_Den.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Thieves_Den.jpg) Possible statute of Zeqqi
#### Hoon Ding
The [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding), also known as **Hoonding** or **The Make Way God**, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) spirit of 'perseverance over infidels'. The Hoon Ding has historically materialized whenever the Redguards need to 'make way' for their people.

#### Ius
[Ius the Extremely Agitated](Lore_Ius.md), **God of Animals** is represented by statues in certain parts of Hammerfell and has strange stories regarding his origins. He is often depicted holding a large set of scales, but his worshippers have no idea, nor care to know why.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Ius-116)</sup>

#### Leki
[Leki](Lore_Leki.md), also known as the **Saint of the Spirit Sword** is the goddess of aberrant swordsmanship and divine daughter of [Tall Papa](Lore_Ruptga.md), who is said to reside in the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md). She is said to be one of the most popular gods in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

The Na-Totambu of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) warred to a standstill during the mythic era to decide who would lead the charge against the [Lefthanded Elves](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md). Their swordmasters, though, were so skilled in the Best Known Cuts as to be matched evenly. Leki introduced the Ephemeral Feint (a [Sword-Singing](Lore_Sword-singers.md) technique, acording to some stories); afterwards, a victor emerged, and the war with the Elves began.

According to legend, one of Leki's miracles resulted in the founding of a settlement in the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md). [Leki's Blade](Lore_Leki%27s_Blade.md) was established after she dueled with [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md), a great warrior who claimed to match the gods in the way of the sword. [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) came from a long line of Redguard swordmaster nobles, and was "something more than mortal man", descending from Leki the Yokudan Sword-Saint.

#### Malooc
**[Malooc](Lore_Malacath.md#Redguards), the Horde King**, is an enemy god of the Redguards. Some scholars believe Malooc is merely Malacath in disguise, as they appear "similarly boorish" and share many "graceless aspects". These beliefs caused tensions within [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), as some clerics began to question their alliance with the Orcs as part of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).

#### Reymon Ebonarm
[Reymon Ebonarm](Lore_Reymon_Ebonarm.md) is the God of War, a deity of some significance to the Redguards. He was possibly originally a [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) deity or group of deities who may simply be manifestations of the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding) which have subsequently individualized.

#### Sep
[Sep](Lore_Sep.md), also known as the **Second Serpent**, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) and Redguard equivalent of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md). He is said to have been created by [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md).

#### Zeqqi
[Zeqqi](Lore_Zeqqi.md), known as the **Madonna of Tears**, is a water goddess and the daughter of [Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md). As her father forsakes [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md), the water goddess takes pity on the lost souls who call [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) their home. It is theorized that the "Lady in the Cistern" statue depicts her, recognizing the sacrifices she makes to sustain the people of the city, hidden from the gaze of her disapproving father. The Hel Netu swore by Zeqqi's mercy.

### Ancestor Worship
*The Redguards revere their departed ancestors so fervidly that it rivals the devotion many other races have for their gods.*
— *[The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Improved_Emperor%27s_Guide_to_Tamriel_Hammerfell)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/ON-load-Tuwhacca%27s_Throne_%28old%29.jpg/200px-ON-load-Tuwhacca%27s_Throne_%28old%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Tuwhacca%27s_Throne_(old).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Tuwhacca%27s_Throne_(old).jpg) Tu'whacca's Throne, resting place of Frandar Hunding and other Ansei
- → *For more detailed information, see: [Redguard Ancestor Worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md#Redguard)*

The origins of Redguard Ancestor Worship is unclear. Some believe this ancient tradition began after the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md) settled in Hammerfell. They noticed that corpses decomposed very slowly in the desert, and over time the people assigned a spiritual significance to the process and declared that the remains of their ancestors were sacred.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-FJad-117)</sup> Other sources claim Ancestor Worship was the most sacred law and tradition of the ancient Yokudans.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-Yeqdah-118)</sup>

Redguards are famous not just for their many religious rituals surrounding the dead, but also for the elaborate necropolises they build to house them. At these sacred sites, supplicants give prayer not just to their own direct ancestors, but also historical Redguard kings and the untold number of Yokudans who perished when the continent sank.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-IEGTT-119)</sup>

### The Far Shores
The [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md), also called the **Walkabout**<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> and the **Far Dunes**, is the afterlife sought by the Redguards. According to [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) myth, [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), the serpentine God of Everything, eats itself over and over, periodically consuming all of creation. By "moving at strange angles" to stride between "worldskins", a process known as the Walkabout, the strongest of the spirits learned to bypass this cycle of destruction. Thanks to [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md)'s guidance, many weaker spirits were able to find their way as well, and the practice became so easy that it became a place—the Far Shores. Here, the spirits can safely wait until Satakal has passed and a new skin has emerged. There is no hunger or thirst in the Far Shores, but there are ample martial challenges to keep Redguard warrior-spirits engaged for eternity.

### Death

- → *For more detailed information, see: [Redguard Death Beliefs](Lore_Redguard_Death_Beliefs.md)*

When a Redguard dies, priests typically perform a consecration ritual in the name of [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) at their funeral.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-ONTKF-120)</sup> Some also practice mummification of the dead. The body is laid on a table and washed in oils and leaves. Before the head and torso are carefully cut open, the body is drained of blood, and the brain, heart, and other organs are placed into ceremonial jars, followed by a week of chanting prayers, mourning, and swathing the corpse with long ceremonial bandages. The body is then interred in a painted sarcophagus within a subterranean tomb, along with the deceased's favorite weapons, servants, and pets. It's not unusual to find that their crypts and tombs can be even larger and more elaborate than their homes for the living.<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-ONload SC-121)</sup> This is all symptomatic of a Redguard respect for departed relatives, a respect so fervent it is similar to the ancestral worship of some other cultures.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup>

Though the Redguards abhor necromancy, their religion has also led them to abhor any sort of interaction with the undead.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-EOTAW-122)</sup> Tradition bound them not to fight the honored dead, making them particularly vulnerable to necromancers and undead creatures.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-TIOF-123)</sup><sup>[[122]](#cite_note-MNR-124)</sup> Such was the importance of this ideal, that the culture of the Alik'r evolved around it. However in later years many realized that exceptions must be made.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-ONKF-125)</sup> One example is the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md), a tribe of Redguards who have made it their duty to purify mausoleums and destroy the undead wherever they may be found. As a result, they have been shunned from Redguard society, and are exiled to live as nomads in the wastes. Their existence is nevertheless tolerated due to the necessity of their role. Among their other traditions, Ash'abah tribesmen travel to Tu'whacca's Throne annually to perform the 36-hour-long Rite of Royal Rest, which is said to seal departed monarchs in their tombs and keep their spirits happy.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup> Another form of protection against necromancy was the creation of the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md). These ancient relics were forged as part of a covenant with Tu'whacca to ensure that the consecrated dead could not be raised by even the most powerful necromancy.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-ONTKF-120)</sup>

### The Book of Circles

- *Train your opponent to make the wrong response.* — [The Book of Circles](Lore_The_Book_of_Circles,_Loredas_Maxims.md)

The Book of Circles was written by Frandar Hunding, a spiritual leader of the Redguards, in order to pass on his insights. When he was thirty years old (around [1E 750](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_750)), he retired to a cave and began writing what would become the Book of Circles.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DTS-15)</sup> For Redguards, it is an enormous part of their culture and everyday life.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RTHATH-14)</sup><sup>[[124]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-126)</sup><sup>[[125]](#cite_note-DTROHSAI-127)</sup> Reading from it was abandoned sometime later in the First Era until Hallin, the only Ansei at the time, brought back the "old ways" and told each warrior to read the book.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-NFRH-22)</sup> The people of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) celebrate "Dirij Tereur" every year on the 5th of Frost Fall, a holiday honoring Frandar Hunding, and festivities typically include readings from the book.<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-128)</sup>

Each household in Hammerfell has an alcove by its hearth just big enough to hold a copy of the Book of Circles in honor of Hunding.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RTHATH-14)</sup><sup>[[124]](#cite_note-FTMSOML-126)</sup> The book is still highly regarded as a treatise on blade mastery and even other experts at the skill refer to it. It is said to include "thirty-eight grips, seven hundred and fifty offensive and eighteen hundred defensive positions, and nearly nine thousand moves essential to sword mastery".<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-WAP-129)</sup> Though they utilize many other types of arms, a Redguard's sword is considered an extension of his soul and a symbol of honor, and their mastery of blade crafting is unparalleled.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-4)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Aurbical Abacus
The [Aurbical Abacus](Lore_Aurbical_Abacus.md) was an ancient [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) relic created by distilling the knowledge and wisdom of twenty generations of the priest-wizards of the Yokudan god [Zeht](Lore_Zeht.md). It had the physical appearance of having glass gears and ivory pistons, and suffused with the blue glow of star-magic. It was used to track the movements of the moons, constellations, and planets and calculate the exact turn of the seasons. It detected the directional origin of star-magicka, or "[varliance](Lore_Varliance.md)," and stored it as data. It can also predict the weather and even control it on a continental scale. It was brought to [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md) after Yokuda sank, by the Zeht priesthood, where it was buried in the old [Hubalajad](Lore_Hubalajad.md) family tomb known as Bahraha's Gloom.

### Ansei Wards
The [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md) were three ancient Redguard relics that contained the spirits of powerful [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warriors. They were created in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) to combat against the threat of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) in the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md). It is believed that the Ansei forged the wards from their own [shehai](Lore_Shehai.md). According to some scholars, these treasured relics have played an important role throughout the history of the Alik'r. The Wards themselves take the form of large ornate golden scimitars encrusted with gemstones.

### Boneshaver
[Boneshaver](Lore_Boneshaver.md) is a legendary saber named after the warrior god of the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), [Onsi](Lore_Onsi.md) the Boneshaver. Onsi himself is rumored to have gifted the saber to the Ra'gada. Its blade is noted as being sharp enough to be able to cut through any foe.

The saber's physical features are ornate with dragon imagery. Its pommel is shaped like a dragon's head, and has areas on the saber's hilt that resembles scales. The blade itself features a pattern akin to that of flowing water.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/RG-npc-A%27tor_02.jpg/150px-RG-npc-A%27tor_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-npc-A%27tor_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-npc-A%27tor_02.jpg) The Soul Sword
### Soul Sword
The [Soul Sword](Lore_Soul_Sword.md) is a shimmering longsword imbued with the [soul](Lore_Souls.md) of [Prince A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md) following his death in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Aside from being a powerful weapon, the sword can move of its own accord and allows A'tor's spirit to communicate with the wielder. Originally the royal sword of A'tor, it was enchanted with his soul during an attempt to resurrect him, and was wielded by Cyrus the Restless during the [rebellion](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md#Rebellion) against the Imperial forces of Amiel Richton. This unity of A'tor, Cyrus, and the sword is considered to be an occurrence of the [Hoon Ding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoon Ding). It was thereafter put in the possession of [Iszara](Lore_Iszara.md), Queen-Regent of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

## Concept and design
- Redguard culture, traditions, and appearance draw on several real-world influences: - Much of Hammerfell's visual identity draws from the [Middle East](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East) and [North Africa](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa), especially the [Arabian Peninsula](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula) and the [Maghreb](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maghreb), while many aspects of Hammerfell's urban culture and refinement also invite comparison to the [Islamic Golden Age](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Golden_Age). - This is reflected in its desert imagery, curved swords, flowing garments, and [Moorish](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moorish_architecture) architectural elements, seen in the arches, courtyards, ornamentation, and decorative forms of its cities, as well as in broader [Islamic](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_architecture) architectural influences.
- [Caribbean](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean) influence is evident in Hammerfell's seafaring and adventure-oriented culture, particularly its sailors, corsairs, pirates, and free-port settlements. This is especially apparent in coastal locales such as [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) and [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md), and in the broader maritime life of many Hammerfell's cities.
- Influence from [Sub-Saharan Africa](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sub-Saharan_Africa) is most apparent in the physical appearance of many Redguards, particularly their darker complexions and textured hair. According to [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride), his writings on the Redguards were also inspired by the [Black Panthers](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Panther_Party) and by the idea of reverse, or black, imperialism.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-68)</sup>
- Yokudan tradition has influences from [Japanese](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_culture) warrior culture, especially in the central place of swordsmanship in social and spiritual life, the [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) and their "Way of the Sword", and the prominence of the [Book of Circles](Lore_The_Book_of_Circles.md), which recalls the Japanese [Book of Five Rings](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Book_of_Five_Rings). In this respect, the Yokudans and the Ansei, resemble idealized [samurai](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samurai) traditions in many aspects.
