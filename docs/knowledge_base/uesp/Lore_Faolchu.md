Faolchu the Changeling was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [werewolf lord](Lore_Lycanthropy.md#Werewolf_Lord),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOVo A-1)</sup> who served as a general of the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md). Faolchu had a reputation for being nigh invulnerable in battle, earning him the sobriquets of the **Invincible General**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOCJ-2)</sup> and the **Invincible One**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOVo F-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOLD-4)</sup>

Soldiers could bash him with a shield, stick him full of arrows, and cut deep into his flesh, and Faolchu would not falter in his assault.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOCJ-2)</sup> Not even a sword to the heart could kill him. However, Faolchu had a crippling fear of fire. Fire was the only thing that could stop Faolchu in his tracks.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONAlana-5)</sup> Faolchu was one of the generals who commanded the Alessian army against the forces of Clan [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) at the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOLD-4)</sup> He was ultimately killed during a Direnni assault on his headquarters at the Battle of Glenumbra Moors while a fire raged around him.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TNS-6)</sup>

Faolchu was resurrected in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by [Angof the Gravesinger](Lore_Angof.md), who forced the werewolf to serve him and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), becoming known as **Faolchu the Reborn**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOVo F-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOVo A-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOTC-7)</sup> Faolchu wreaked havoc on [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) at Angof's behest, turning many of the region's inhabitants into werewolves.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GG-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOFaolchu-9)</sup> Faolchu turned one of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md)'s citizens, Jonathan Telwin, into a werewolf, who then began spreading his lycanthropy among other residents of the city.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-APf V-10)</sup> When they had enough werewolves inside Camlorn, they opened the gates for Faolchu, his werewolves, and some of Angof's [Bloodthorn](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) cultists, who then massacred much of the city's population.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-APf V-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESODG-11)</sup>

In an effort to discover the means to defeat the ancient general, the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) had [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) travel back in time to see how Faolchu met his demise.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> After experiencing the Battle of Glenumbra Moors first-hand through the eyes of a Breton soldier, the Vestige discovered Faolchu's weakness, giving the [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md) and its allies the information they needed to defeat him once more.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TNS-6)</sup> The Lion Guard and the remaining members of the Camlorn Guard eventually launched their assault to retake the city from Faolchu's forces.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RC-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ALH-14)</sup> By this time, Faolchu was holed up in Camlorn Keep, where he was killed by the Vestige after the Lion Guard archers set fire to the building.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TFOF-15)</sup>

Upon his second death, Faolchu's soul was sent to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), where Molag Bal imprisoned him in [Heart's Grief](Lore_Heart%27s_Grief.md) and tortured him for his failure by burning him to death every day.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOFaolchu-9)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
