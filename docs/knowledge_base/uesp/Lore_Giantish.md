*Oh mighty tundrastrider! How you and your mighty tusked beast silhouette against the great orange expanse. Thundering footsteps herald your herd. Man and beast blazing trail together.*
— *[Ode To The Tundrastriders](Lore_Ode_To_The_Tundrastriders.md)* Giants (sometimes called the **big folk**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AGF-1)</sup> are a [race](Lore_Races.md) of enormous humanoids with colossal strength. They are found in the wilderness of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3V-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Online-5)</sup>

They have thick grey skin, long, powerful limbs, large hands and feet, and thick greyish-brown hair decorated with braids and beads. Additionally, most Giants have decorative scars carved into their chests. They clothe themselves in fur pelts adorned with the bones of animals and people, although many prefer a simple loincloth.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup>

Although they tower above most races, their height can vary. Some stand between 11 and 12 feet tall,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> whereas others can be several times the height of the average person.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Online-5)</sup> Female giants, also called **Giantesses**, are less commonly seen than males by non-giants,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAG-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HHH-7)</sup> but they have been seen in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) and [Whiterun Plains](Lore_Tundra_Plain.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MA-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Mt C:ES-9)</sup> Giant social groups appear to vary, with some being part of clans led by a chieftain,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Sot R27-10)</sup> some being led by powerful matriarchs,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOScalecaller-11)</sup> and others being small families composed of two parents raising children together on isolated mountains.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TSW-12)</sup>

## Origins
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/eb/LG-cardart-Ancient_Giant.png/200px-LG-cardart-Ancient_Giant.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ancient_Giant.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ancient_Giant.png) An Ancient Giant (Legends) The origins of Giants are unclear and sometimes disputed. Although their tapered ears bear a resemblance to that of [mer](Lore_Mer.md), some scholars believe they actually share an ancestral relationship with the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), through the [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> The Atmorans, who were the progenitors of the Nordic race, were said to be originally an intelligent race of ancient giants. The belief is that in ancient times, the Atmorans split into two different [races](Lore_Races.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> The reason for the split is unknown, but some accounts mention an internal conflict that resulted in one group being driven into hiding.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-911Cow-14)</sup>

According to one account, one race became smaller in size, but increased in intelligence and became the Nords. The other race grew larger, lost some of their intelligence, and became the Giants.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> Some Nords even think of the giants as their Atmoran ancestors themselves.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Kyne C-15)</sup><sup>:38</sup> This particular belief thrived in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), at a time when Nordic farmers would offer [cows](Lore_Cow.md) as a sacrifice to Giants in order to avoid attacks on livestock, but both the tradition and the ancestral connection belief is claimed to have partially fallen out of favor in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-EGTS-16)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Kyne C-15)</sup><sup>:38</sup> Though the tradition of farmers gifting them cattle continued,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Kyne C-15)</sup><sup>:38</sup> and would do so all the way into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SRFarmer-17)</sup>

## Culture

### Society and Craft
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/44/LO-book-Giants-A_Discourse.jpg/200px-LO-book-Giants-A_Discourse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Giants-A_Discourse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Giants-A_Discourse.jpg) Depiction of a Giant's camp Despite their primitive appearances, Giants are moderately intelligent, able to fashion tools and clothes for themselves, tend [mammoths](Lore_Mammoth.md), and harness fire. Semi-nomadic, Giants travel with herds of mammoths and set up camps across the lands. Their camps are typically decorated with the skulls and tusks of mammoths, alongside various other bones, some of which are used as tripwire chimes to alert them to any intruders.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> For protection, Giants are known to fashion large, crudely-made clubs of stone, wood and bone and even sets of armor.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-THo CX-18)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> They are sometimes seen wearing the skulls of [trolls](Lore_Troll.md), powerful creatures deadly to many other races, on their belts.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Grok-19)</sup> Despite their rudimentary craftsmanship, they are one of the few races who know of the techniques required to grind the iron-like tusks of mammoths into [powder](Lore_Powdered_Mammoth_Tusk.md), a prized alchemical ingredient.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SRQuintus-20)</sup>

Some Giants belong to a clan, sometimes numbering in the hundreds, which is ruled by a high chieftain.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Sot R27-10)</sup> The clan chieftain's rule is absolute, and in times of conflict, they are known to rally their troops and lead organized attacks. If the chieftain is killed, their followers are often left in disarray.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONGjakil-21)</sup> Giants have also been observed as having matriarchies, where a giantess has the highest authority.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOJorvuld Davaux-22)</sup>

The Giants of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region are known to reside in large strongholds,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GSH-23)</sup> even cohabitating with Orcish tribes or residing within their strongholds.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GSH-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OSH-24)</sup>

### Language and Communication
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/DF-quest-Mephala%27s_Quest_03.jpg/200px-DF-quest-Mephala%27s_Quest_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-Mephala%27s_Quest_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-Mephala%27s_Quest_03.jpg) A Giant Bodyguard (Daggerfall) Giants typically have a deep and guttural speech, sometimes called [Giantish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Giantish).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup> To the average person it sounds like incomprehensible grunts and roars. Giants tend to be non-hostile to speakers of Giantish.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup> Some Giants, however, have learned to speak rudimentary Tamrielic in order to communicate or trade with people,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-26)</sup> and a select few have worked with other races as volunteer gladiators or guards.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Meph Q-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOFo B-28)</sup> Although Giants have no known written language, they seem to have an affinity for spiral motifs. This particular pattern (shared by ancient Nordic craftsmanship) is often depicted as scars on their torsos, carved into their mammoth's tusks, or painted onto various rocks, trees, and walls within their encampments.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> Observers have speculated that this rudimentary art possibly entices females, marks territory, or is simply artistic expression.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAG-6)</sup>

### Relationship with Mammoths
While it's commonly believed that Giants herd mammoths,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SRload-29)</sup> scholars have observed that their relationship is based more on mutual respect.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> They are seemingly able to communicate with mammoths and protect them fiercely. In return, Giants receive companionship and milk, which they process into cheese.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> Their omnivorous diet also consists of cooked [skeever](Lore_Skeever.md) meat, and other animals which they spit-roast over their massive campfires.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> Some accounts claim to have witnessed a Giant eat a mammoth, but the meal was conducted with reverence.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> Some of the giants of the Iliac Bay region are claimed to have eaten members of other races.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Po G-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-31)</sup>

### Death
Giants have been known to gather regularly at a ceremonial site. Here, they come together to trade, mate, and communicate with whatever methods they have developed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> One tale tells of a Giant family residing on Mount [Trolhetta](Lore_Trolhetta.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TSW-12)</sup> Giants also demonstrate intricate beliefs regarding death. They have places set aside as sacred burial grounds, such as [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md)'s Kiss in Skyrim.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-SRGuard-32)</sup> When a Giant is sick or dying, he or she goes to one of these places to die. If a Giant dies elsewhere, other Giants make sure the body gets to the burial ground. They typically don't live near these sacred locations, and they don't guard them. They simply make use of them.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup>

## Variants

### Sea Giants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kyne%27s_Aegis_02.jpg) A Sea Giant longship (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/ON-npc-Chief_Gruthar.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chief_Gruthar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief_Gruthar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chief_Gruthar.jpg) A Sea Giant (ESO) [Sea Giants](Lore_Sea_Giant.md) are a species of Giant with silvery blue skin that are rarely seen on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They're perceived as smarter and more malicious than Giants that dwell on land. Sea Giants are rare enough to be creatures of legend, and have appeared in [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) maritime tales dating back to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Little is known about their culture and behavior. They are capable of speech, and have been witnessed speaking their own language as well as common dialects such as [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md). Sailors have claimed to witness Sea Giant vessels in the frozen wastes of the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). These ships are said to be the size of islands, and are armed with jagged spears. However, these accounts are considered to be wildly exaggerated. They allegedly only make landfall during the coldest winters, and never stay for long. They slaughter and pillage, then vanish in the ice and fog.

### Half-Giants
[Half-Giants](Lore_Half-Giant.md) are the descendants of [Men](Lore_Man.md) and Giants or [Sea Giants](Lore_Sea_Giant.md). They take on the qualities of their [mixed](Lore_Multiraciality.md) heritage, which can be passed down to their descendants. Half-Giants tend to be much taller then the other races, but not nearly as tall as their Giant forebears, with some exceptions.

[Sinmur](Lore_Sinmur.md), also known as Sinmur the Terrible and the Giant of Legend, was a [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) Half-Giant chieftain from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Leading hundreds of his kin, he is perhaps best remembered for his epic conflict against [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) and the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md).

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Half-Giant and Sea Giant raiders led by [Vampire Lord](Lore_Vampire_Lord.md) [Falgravn](Lore_Falgravn.md) stormed the shores of [Kyne's Aegis](Lore_Kyne%27s_Aegis.md), but were defeated by a group of [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md).

### Other "Giants"
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/SR-creature-Frost_Giant.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Frost_Giant.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Frost_Giant.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Frost_Giant.jpg) A Frost Giant (Skyrim) There exist several creatures that are related to giants, or are given the description of giants but are not related.

Legends abound of a race of giants known as Hill Giants<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-33)</sup> from Skyrim that migrated to the present day region of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) in High Rock, bringing along their massive mammoths. Though they are no longer around, giant remains found within the cavern system of Breagha-Fin in the region gives credence to these tales.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-34)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-35)</sup> Legends in the region speak of the "trammeling" of the giants long ago.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-36)</sup> The artifact [Chrysamere](Lore_Chrysamere.md) was used in a protracted siege of the Fellthunder Clan of giants in Rivenspire early in High Rock's history.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LAIA-37)</sup> Sand giants are a type of giant witnessed around the Craglorn region of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-38)</sup>

[Frost Giants](Lore_Frost_Giant.md) are a sentient race called the "even more formidable cousins of Giants",<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-39)</sup> and are classified with them.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-LG-40)</sup> Though it is unknown how closely related they are. Similarly, the [Ilyadi](Lore_Ilyadi.md) were an extinct race of forest-dwelling giants native to the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), known for the multiple eyes which covered their heads,<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-PGE3S-41)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-42)</sup> though they are classified as [fey](Lore_Faerie.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-TF-43)</sup> [Marsh Giants](Lore_Marsh_Giant.md), despite their name and size, are known to be unrelated to Giants, instead being a type of [Nature Spirit](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-OMG:AS-44)</sup> Atmoran Titans are a group that have been mentioned as similar to albeit separate from ancient giants.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Lt TO-45)</sup>

In [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md) was believed to be built in honor of an unspecified race of ancient giants. By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), these giants had not been seen in the land for many years.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ARRia-46)</sup> The [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) were an ancient race of titanic beings. Some scholars believe that the term "Dwarves" originates with a race of Giants, was used in affection towards the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), whom they perceived as unusually small.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-MWHasphat-47)</sup> It is believed these Giants were likely the Ehlnofey.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-48)</sup>

## Relationship with Nords
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/SR-creature-Cow_03.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Cow_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Cow_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Cow_03.jpg) A painted cow (Skyrim) [![ON-concept-Giant.png](https://images.uesp.net/5/55/ON-concept-Giant.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Giant.png) Giants share little civil interaction with humans or elves, but are typically free to wander through the lands they inhabit. Giants are wary of, but used to, the presence of other races. They are mostly harmless and keep to themselves when unprovoked, but are also very territorial and will react aggressively on intruding adventurers or wild animals.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAG-6)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Ft Jo LF-49)</sup> If provoked, they have been known to fling unlucky intruders up to a league away with their clubs, and some Giants keep trophies from slain invaders.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONGjakil-21)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AAG-6)</sup>

Because Giants have been relatively common throughout Skyrim since time immemorial, their relationship with the Nords is the most prominent and varies greatly. The [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) was a particularly violent time, according to the earliest accounts. They tell of a powerful Giant named [Sinmur](Lore_Sinmur.md) who served as the high chieftain of Skyrim's giantish clans.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ONSvari-50)</sup> Ysgramor, in his prolonged military expansions into Skyrim, waged war against Sinmur and the Giant clans. Ysgramor purportedly slaughtered hundreds of Giants, and eventually Sinmur himself.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Sot R27-10)</sup> Despite the early conflicts with Nords, the presence of Giants remained throughout the province in the eras that followed, although both races largely kept to themselves.

Giants have sometimes been characterized by Nordic villagers as "angry hermits" who attack trespassers on sight. This has resulted in them barricading the pathways leading into the Giant's settlements, preventing anyone from accidentally wandering into the area.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-It LD-51)</sup> Some Nords treat the Giants as a nuisance, steal their grazing lands, and even hunt their mammoths for sport.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> During the [Alliance War](Lore_Wars.md#The_Alliance_War) in the Second Era, a clan of Giants went to war with the [Stormfist Clan](Lore_Stormfist_Clan.md) over stolen territory and mammoths.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONSA-52)</sup> During the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in Skyrim, a clan of Giants who were driven out of their lands began to attack settlements as a result.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-SRFarkas-53)</sup>

Both the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) and the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) readily accept contracts to deal with Giants that have been troubling the population.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-SRFH-54)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-DFGK-55)</sup> Similarly, the Jarls of Skyrim have been known to send their forces out to kill a Giant that has wandered too close to a settlement.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-SR-56)</sup> While it may seem the aggressors are almost always the Nords, Giants sometimes play a part in disrupting the peace. Giants can sense weakness and intrude on territory if the opportunity presents itself.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-SRAtub-57)</sup> They sometimes take livestock from farms, or on rare occasions steal valuable items from the populace.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-SRFH-54)</sup> If led by a chieftain, they can be particularly dangerous in large numbers.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ONTFC-58)</sup> Ancient parchments speak of the warrior Rolf the Large being killed fighting giants that waylaid his village and proceeded to play catch with his head.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Sa R-59)</sup>

Conversely, other Nords are content to have Giants living within their borders. Some even consider them as distant cousins that should be respected,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ga D-13)</sup> or write poetry honoring them.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Oth TS-60)</sup> Some Nordic farmers uphold a long-standing, albeit uncommon, tradition of annually offering cows as a sacrifice to nearby Giants in order to avoid attacks on livestock. Traditionally, the cows are painted with swirls Giants are so fond of, so that they recognize them as peace offerings.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SRFarmer-17)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-911Cow-14)</sup> In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Giants were viewed as a serious problem in [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) region of Skyrim, but the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) decreed they be left alone.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-SRSkald-61)</sup> Certain Giants have been given special permission to keep their campsites, but disturbing or trading with them is forbidden.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-OW-62)</sup>

Certain Giant body parts are known to be valuable alchemical ingredients and are much sought after by apothecaries, specifically the [toes](Lore_Giant%27s_Toe.md), thumbs and molars. Because the freshness and condition of the body parts are important, some alchemists have drafted harvesting guidelines for enterprising adventurers who may have recently slain a Giant. The benefits that Giant's body parts provide in both potions and enchantments are numerous. Giant's toes can bolster health and strength, at the cost of weakening stamina. Molars are known to bolster combat abilities, and thumbs can imbue a weapon with a pestilent vapor.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Aa R-63)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> Less common is the consumption of Giant's blood, which results in diminution.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup> A fungus purportedly grows in the Giant's armpits that can be used as a strong [narcotic](Lore_Drugs.md).<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-SRNobleman-64)</sup>

Giants, albeit very rarely, are able to reproduce with humans. One such example of this is [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md), a [half-Giant](Lore_Half-Giant.md) Nord who claims to have Giantish ancestry. It is unknown whether or not they are able to reproduce with mer or even the other races of men.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ONLyris-65)</sup> The erotic charade "The King and the Amorous Giantess" is sometimes enacted with sets of bedroom stilts and large furry mittens.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-AGRE-66)</sup>

## Notes
- According to Nordic myth, Ysgramor was said to have collected the laments of the "Giant-Wives" while in Atmora, and had them twisted into a bowstring for his fabled bow.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-FAHM-74)</sup>
- [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) purportedly traveled to Atmora in the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and met a Giant who was said to be the king of Atmora.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TFt Ko A-75)</sup>
- Giants are, by most accounts, related to Men. One source referring to them as Betmeri incorrectly calls them one of the "smaller Beast races".<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-76)</sup>
- Artist [Jonah Lobe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Jonah_Lobe), who created the model of Giants for *Skyrim*, said this in regards to his design of the Giants: "I modeled the Giants to look like my father. Yes, I'm that guy. I didn't want the player to see them as monstrous, I wanted to look gentle, like oversized shepherds who lived for two hundred plus years, and slept under the stars."<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Jonah Lobe-77)</sup>
- A cut mission from Skyrim's Civil War questline would have had the player bring a Giant named Goldar a Painted Cow as peace offering, an old tradition from when there were more Giants in the world. Goldar is rather cordial in response to this gift, and becomes an ally of the player. Goldar, and the Cow Hand (who gives the player the Painted Cow) implicate that the Giants could be on more peaceful terms with their neighbors if they were not ostracized, and that the Giants were "allowed" before.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-CW08-78)</sup> The Painted Cow concept itself made it into the final game; the player may find farmers in random encounters on journeys to present their Cow to the Giants as a peace offering to cease attacks on their farms, the time between offerings depending on what an individual farmer thinks best.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TES5World Interactions-79)</sup> This worldbuilding item was adopted from [unofficial lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Unofficial_Lore), this being: *[The Seven Fights of Aldudagga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Seven_Fights_of_The_Aldudagga)*, by [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) written in 2005.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Kurt Posts-80)</sup>
