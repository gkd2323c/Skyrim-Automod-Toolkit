# Smithing

| --- |
| [![Smithing](https://images.uesp.net/3/3a/SR-skill-Smithing.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-skill-Smithing.png) |
| Specialization: <br> [Combat](Skyrim_Combat.md) |

**Smithing** is the crafting skill for [combat](Skyrim_Combat.md), providing the ability to create and improve (or temper) [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) and [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md). The higher your skill level, the more you can improve weapons and armor. The Smithing skill tree has a total of 10 perks, requiring a total of 10 perk points to fill.

In-game description: *The art of creating weapons and armor from raw materials, or improving non-magical weapons and armor.*

## Skill Usage
Smithing can only be performed at certain locations such as a Blacksmith's shop. In these designated areas you may find several types of tools:

- [Grindstone](Skyrim_Grindstone.md): Sharpen weapons for extra damage. The higher your Smithing skill, the more the weapon damage is improved.
- [Workbench](Skyrim_Workbench.md): Allows you to improve armor. The higher your smithing skill, the more the armor rating is improved
- [Forge](Skyrim_Forge.md): Create new weapons and armor from raw materials. What you can create is determined by your Smithing skill perks. With [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) installed, you will also be able to create certain [housebuilding materials](Skyrim_Miscellaneous_Items.md#Housebuilding_Items_HF).
- [Anvil](Skyrim_Anvil.md): Same effect as the Forge.

Source materials for smithing are created at other workstations. The following tasks are not directly governed by the Smithing skill, however:

- [Tanning Rack](Skyrim_Tanning.md): Create [leather](Skyrim_Leather.md) and [leather strips](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips) from [hides](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Animal_Hides); hides are most commonly obtained by hunting [animals](Skyrim_Animals.md).
- [Smelter](Skyrim_Smelting.md): Create [ingots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ingots) from raw [ore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ore) or Dwemer scrap metal; raw ore is most commonly obtained by [mining](Skyrim_Mining.md).

While the skill tree might appear to be a circle, it is really two separate paths, both ending at [Dragon Armor](Skyrim_Dragon_Armor.md), which allows creation of the best [light armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) ([Dragonscale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonscale)) and second-best [heavy armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md) ([Dragonplate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dragonplate)). Players may wish to focus exclusively on one of the two branches — either the left-hand branch (primarily light armor), or else the right-hand branch (almost all heavy armor). The left-hand branch is shorter, requiring one fewer perk than the right-hand branch to reach Dragon armor.

Since the left-hand branch includes medium-quality heavy armor ([steel plate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate), via the [Advanced Armors](Skyrim_Advanced_Armors.md) perk), the left-hand branch can also be a good choice for heavy armor specialists. Also note that damage reduction from armor maxes out at 567 displayed armor rating, which can be achieved with nearly any armor in the game given sufficient improvements via tempering and armor skill. Therefore the difference in armor rating between Dragonplate and the best heavy armor, [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric.md), is not necessarily significant. The lighter weight of Dragonplate armor may be of more importance, unless you have either the [Conditioning](Skyrim_Conditioning.md) perk or activate [The Steed Stone](Skyrim_The_Steed_Stone.md), both of which negate the adverse effects of heavy weight.

The available weapons are also an important difference between the left and right branches. Both [ebony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Weapons) and [Daedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Weapons) weapons are better than [glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Weapons) ones, which is the best type offered by the left-hand branch. However, when upgraded to Legendary quality, the damage difference between a glass weapon and a Daedric one will be less than 10% (exact number depends on weapon type). The Dawnguard add-on also adds the ability to create the even stronger Dragonbone weapons, provided the respective perk is purchased.

### Item Quality
Using the Grindstone/Workbench allows the refinement of weapon/armor depending on the character's Smithing level. The levels of item qualities are **Fine, Superior, Exquisite, Flawless, Epic**, and **Legendary**. The Smithing level required to unlock these qualities depends upon whether the character has the Smithing perk of the item's material. Unlike [Enchanting](Skyrim_Enchanting.md), the same improved weapon/armor can be modified again if your Smithing skill increases or by using potions or enchanted apparel that provide a Fortify Smithing effect: this of course costs another ingot of whatever metal or item is involved in improving it.

Each new level increases chest armor ratings by 3.6 and weapon/other armor ratings by 1.8, resulting in an increase of about +0.1 per effective skill point for chest armor, and +0.05 for other items, meaning a smithed "suit" goes up about 0.26 per effective skill point, 0.31 including a smithed shield.

With appropriate supplies, you would improve the Dwarven to Exquisite, but the Elven only to Superior. A plain Iron item would also be limited to Superior because of the lack of an associated perk, even though a Steel item could be made Exquisite (due to Steel Smithing being a prerequisite perk for Dwarven Smithing).

Taken together, these raise the maximum to 1393, the same as 1370: quality 41.
- Version 2.0.0 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) fixes the [Notched Pickaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Notched_Pickaxe) so that it adds 5 to your Smithing skill, as it claims it does. Quality Level The exact rating boost is based on your *effective skill* (accounting for skill level, perks, enchantments, and potions). In detail:

- *effective skill* = (*base skill* - 13.29) × (1 + *perk*) × (1 + Σ *enchantments*) × (1 + *potion*) + 13.29 - *perk* is 1 if you have the relevant smithing perk, otherwise 0
- Σ *enchantments* is the sum of all your Fortify Smithing enchantments (e.g. wearing two +29% Fortify Smithing items → Σ *enchantments* = 0.58)
- *potion* is the effect of the currently active Fortify Smithing potion (e.g. a 20% increase → *potion* = 0.2)
- *quality level* = (*effective skill + 38) × 3 / 103 rounded down to the nearest integer*
- *rating* = (3.6 × *quality level* - 1.6) × *chest armor factor* - *chest armor factor* is 1.0 if the item being enhanced is chest armor, 0.5 otherwise

Note that the final *rating* increase can be a non-integer value. Damage calculations will use the exact (real) rating boost, but you'll see a rounded version of the number in the game menu. Armor ratings are rounded up to the nearest integer, while weapon damage is simply rounded to the nearest integer.

With a base smithing skill of 100, the relevant armor smithing perk, four 29% fortify smithing enchantments, and a 130% fortify smithing potion, the effective skill equals 874.84 (item quality = Legendary 26), which yields a bonus of +93 to chest armor and +47 to other items -- 234 total without a shield. Without the perk, the same yields an effective skill of 444.07 (item quality = Legendary 14), or 49 and 25, respectively - 124 total. Due to the fact that a full set of perks in the relevant armor skill plus 100 skill points gives an approximate multiplier of 4.375 to armor rating, and because a full suit of armor provides an additional 100 pseudo "armor points", with an only slightly better set of enchantments and potion, you'd be able to bring armor with *zero* armor points up to the cap of 667 - and none such exist. This is why it eventually doesn't matter what you're wearing - any armor suit in the game can reach the cap.

Note that perks may not allow you to improve items any better with them as soon as they become available; for example, you can purchase [Steel Smithing](#Steel_Smithing) at skill level 15, but you will not actually be able to *temper* Steel items any better than without the perk until skill level 22.

As a result, the result of proceeding up each "side" of the perk tree (at the fork at Steel Smithing, with Elven to the left and Dwarven to the right) depends on installed DLC and mods; assuming you have the patch, here is how it varies by DLC, listing only the armor you can make when you take the perks, assuming tempering, and ignoring [Ancient Nord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord)/ [Nord Hero](Skyrim_Skyforge.md):

1. No Perk: You can make Iron weapons, Iron Heavy Armor, and Leather Light Armor.
2. **Steel Smithing**: You can make Steel weapons and Steel/Bonemold<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> Heavy Armor; once you can temper things better at skill level 22, a variety of weapons become improvable, including [Hunting Bows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow). If you can manage to acquire the necessary recipe and materials, you can also make *Improved* Bonemold<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> Heavy Armor.
3. **Elven Smithing (Smithing Level 30)**: Assuming you can get the materials, you can now make [Elven Gilded](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Gilded_Armor) light armor, [Elven](Skyrim_Elven.md) weapons (including forgeable bows), and, if you have the DLC, [Chitin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chitin)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> heavy armor. 1. **Advanced Armors (Smithing Level 50)**: No matter what, your Light Armor doesn't actually improve at all; if you have access to [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md), your Heavy Armor is now *better* than Orcish, and your weapons are lighter (but don't hit any harder) than Elven, although your arrows have *not* improved, no matter what.
2. **Glass Smithing (Smithing Level 70)**: Light Armor finally takes a step forward again; [Stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> is better, but only *with* a tempering perk, and Ebony Smithing requires 10 more Smithing Levels than Glass does. All of your weapons get better, including arrows. Your Heavy Armor *does not* get better, for the first and only time on this path if you have [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>. If you have fully completed the [Block](Skyrim_Block.md) skill - 100 levels and all perks that improve Blocking - you can now make the lightest shield in the game capable of maximum blocking without external effects assisting it, such as enchanting or potions.
4. **Dwarven Smithing (Smithing Level 30)**: If you have access to [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md), no benefit at all over Elven - all three forging categories are worse, and Heavy Armor isn't even as good as Steel Smithing, if Improved Bonemold has been unlocked. If you don't, Heavy Armor takes a step forward, as do weapons. 1. **Orcish Smithing (Smithing Level 50)**: No matter what, your weapons don't get any better, nor does your Light Armor. With [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, your heavy armor is *worse* than if you'd taken Elven and Advanced Armors; otherwise, your heavy armor is better than both Dwarven and Advanced can offer.
2. **Ebony Smithing (Smithing Level 80)**: Note the gap; this is *thirty* levels more than Orcish took, so you'll have spent a fair amount of time without an upgrade. Heavy Armor steps forward - *twice* if you have [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, supplanting Dragonplate, and it also lets Light Armor leap to being better than Glass. Weapons permanently commit to being better than on the other branch, until the two join up.
3. **Daedric Smithing (Smithing Level 90)**: Highest Armor Rating Heavy Armor in the game; no improvement for Light Armor. Best weapons in the game unless you have access to [Stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, in which case no benefit over Ebony, and unless you have [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>, in which case only best until the two branches join up.
5. **Dragon Armor**: Best Light Armor in the game, best weapons if you have [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>; *worse* than Daedric or [Stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> for Heavy Armor, so only of benefit there if you came up through Glass.

The "greediest" path for Heavy Armor/Weapons with all DLCs and mods is therefore: Steel Smithing, Elven Smithing, Advanced Armors, Glass (for better weapons), then save up perks until you can buy past Dwarven and Orcish to reach Ebony, then buy Daedric for the Heavy Armor if you need it, and Dragon Armor for the weapons. Light Armor/Weapons is more straightforward: Steel for the weapons, Elven, either buy Advanced for lighter weapons or save up until you can buy past it to Glass, then wait on Dragon Armor; you *can* spend 3 perks buying Ebony Smithing for the Light Armor step between Glass and Dragonscale, as well as the next weapon step, but the difference is relatively insignificant by this point.

### Gaining Skill XP
A few basic tips:

- Crafting items at [forges](Skyrim_Forge.md) gives the largest overall skill increases.
- Improving items at [workbenches](Skyrim_Workbench.md) or [grindstones](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) gives skill increases based on the value added to the item at a rate similar to crafting.
- Smelting items at a [smelter](Skyrim_Smelter.md) does not give skill increases.
- [Tanning](Skyrim_Tanning_Rack.md) hides does not give skill increases.

Animal hides provide plenty of free leather, and are lighter than the leather created from them. Process them into leather just before creating or upgrading items. [Leather bracers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Bracers) provide the most XP per leather.

For the fastest leveling, make sure you have the [Warrior Stone](Skyrim_The_Guardian_Stones.md) active (20% faster), and have [slept](Skyrim_Sleeping.md) in the past 8 hours (+5–15% faster, depending). You should also consider completing the quest [Unfathomable Depths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unfathomable_Depths) (see § [Bugs](#Bugs)).

#### Crafting Items
XP gain for smithing an item follows the following formula:

*XP* = 3 × *item value*<sup>0.65</sup> + 25. Because of the flat 25 XP for each item created, it usually is of greater benefit to craft many cheap items than few valuable items. For example, making 5 [iron daggers](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md) requires 5 [iron ingots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Ingot) and gives (38.4 × 5 =) 192 XP. Making one [Iron Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Armor) uses the same amount of materials but yields only 94.2 XP. In case the crafting recipe produces several items, like [Gold Rings](Skyrim_Gold_Ring.md) or Nails (with [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) installed), *item value* refers to the cumulative value of the produced items.

Making gold or silver jewelry can quickly level up smithing without perk investment. Players can cast [Transmute Mineral Ore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Transmute_Mineral_Ore) to convert iron ore to silver ore and silver ore to gold ore. An iron dagger (10 gold) yields 38.4 XP per ore chunk, whereas 2 [gold rings](Skyrim_Gold_Ring.md) (150 gold total) yield 51.5 XP per ore chunk—34% more efficient Smithing XP gain, along with [Alteration](Skyrim_Alteration.md) XP from the transmuting.<sup>[*verification needed — these values were changed*]</sup> [Halted Stream Camp](Skyrim_Halted_Stream_Camp.md) has a free Transmute Mineral Ore spell tome and lots of iron ore.

Combining gold or silver ingots with [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md) increases the value of the item and thus the skill gain. For example, using an additional flawless diamond to craft a gold diamond necklace (see [Jewelry](Skyrim_Jewelry.md)) boosts XP yield to 163 XP per ore, although flawless diamonds can only be obtained after level 40. Gems can be pickpocketed from rich people or found in dungeons - or in some cases the treasure chests in people's homes: if you bother to complete the Crown of Barenziah and get the Prowler's Profit ability, you will end up with far more gems than you can ever possibly know what to do with. #### Improving Items
XP gain for improving an item approximately follows the following formula:

Δ *XP* = 3.8 × Δ *item value*<sup>0.5</sup> × Δ *Q*<sup>0.5</sup> where Δ *item value* is the amount that the item's gold value increased from the improvement, and *Q* is the quality number of the item (Q = 0, 1, 2, ... is No Improvement, Fine, Superior, ...). Note: the reason that the delta value is used instead of the item value is because calculating the delta of enchanted items using the multipliers doesn't work, and the multipliers are not always accurate on non-enchanted items either.

For example, improving an [Iron Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Armor) from Fine (value = 145) to Exquisite (value = 187) is a value difference of (187 - 145) = 42 and a quality change of 3 - 1 = 2, so the predicted Δ *XP* = 3.8 × (42)<sup>0.5</sup> × (2)<sup>0.5</sup> ≅ 34.8 XP.

With the Dwarven perk and Smithing at or above level 40, improving Dwarven Bows will garner significant XP using readily available (or smelted) Dwarven ingots and easily craftable Dwarven Bows. Once the Ebony perk is unlocked, creating and improving Ebony Bows will garner five times the XP of improving Dwarven Bows.

#### Add-ons
With [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) installed, crafting 10 Nails yields the same XP as an Iron Dagger, but doesn't require leather strips.

With [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) installed, Iron Arrows are a good way to level up Smithing at low levels. A stack of 24 Iron Arrows gives almost as much XP per ore as a Gold Ring, without the necessity of learning Transmute. Dwarven Arrows are even better, if you have the Dwarven Smithing perk. Collect all [Dwemer scrap metal](Skyrim_Ores_and_Ingots.md#Dwemer_Metal_.28Smeltable.29) that you find in your travels. A couple of Dwemer ruins can yield 100 ingots; carefully collecting every bit of available metal in every Dwemer ruin will yield over 1200 ingots, producing over 28,000 arrows which can easily raise your Smithing level by 40 or more. You can wait to sell the arrows (e.g. to vendors with low gold who cannot afford your better loot) since they weigh nothing. Other types of arrow are generally less efficient, since other metals require two ores per stack of arrows.

With Dawnguard, complete the [Lost to the Ages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lost_to_the_Ages) quest and create the [Aetherial Crown](Skyrim_Aetherial_Crown.md). Use the Crown to gain both [The Warrior Stone](Skyrim_The_Warrior_Stone.md) and [The Lover Stone](Skyrim_The_Lover_Stone.md) effects, granting a 35% boost to Smithing XP. Acquiring the [Ancient Knowledge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Knowledge) ability gives you an additional 15% boost. And, finally, sleeping in a bed you own can give you a 10% (15% with a spouse) bonus. This adds up to a potential 65% boost to XP when Smithing. Note that you must store the Lover Stone effect in the Aetherial Crown, and remove it before sleeping to gain both bonuses. Also, the sleeping bonus is prevented if you are infected with [lycanthropy](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md).

- The two may share available money anyway, if you sell to one, save the game, reload that save, then sell to the other.
- Improving or enchanting a stolen item will not remove its stolen status; however, improving or creating items with stolen components does not mark the resulting items as stolen.
- Without the Dawnguard add-on or the use of mods, it is impossible to craft arrows.
- Smithing jewelry can be the most effective way to boost your skill level after completing the quest [No Stone Unturned](Skyrim_No_Stone_Unturned.md).
- The bonus from [Ancient Knowledge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Knowledge) is applied *after* any tempering. In the base game, this means that Dwarven armor will improve less than all other armors, all else being equal. With the Unofficial Patch, Dwarven armor bonuses improve more than all other armors, meaning that it will eclipse even Daedric armor for overall protection with only moderate levels of Smithing.
- It takes 1,858 leather bracers (1,858 leather and 3,716 leather strips) to get from level 15 smithing to level 100 smithing, if done all in a row, with no modifiers to amount of experience earned (no standing stone bonus etc).
- It takes 63 Scout Armor Helmets to reach 15-30 (allowing you to unlock the dwarven smithing perk) with Well rested from an inn or home and the warrior stone perk. (122 leather total, 106 leather and 63 leather strips) It's super easy to farm leather from deer and wolves. You also get the benefit of Venison from the hunt which helps make venison stew. This will require a Skyrim special edition or anniversary addition to have the Scout armor.
- From level 30-100 it will take 465 Dwarven Bows (930 Dwemer Ingots, 465 Iron Ingots) With Well Rested and Warrior Stone.
- It takes 1,486 Gold Rings (743 Gold Bars or 1,486 Gold Ore before smelting) to level from 15-100 with a well rested and warrior stone perk.
- If using the restoration loop glitch enchanted items("Weapons and armor can be improved 68345% better." etc.), the fastest way to train Smithing is improving items, not forging them. It takes about 10 items with the smithing modifier in tens of thousands to get to lvl. 100.

## Bugs
- After improving a weapon, the damage difference with your equipped weapon may not update. - To resolve this, either equip another weapon or unequip your current weapon.
- [Ancient Knowledge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Knowledge) gives a 25% bonus while wearing *any* type of armor (not just Dwarven) and increases effective smithing skill by 15% when tempering weapons and armor instead of giving a smithing leveling bonus of 15%. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.1, fixes this bug.

- This bug is altered, but not fixed, by version 1.9.26.0.8 of the [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), which makes the 25% armor bonus apply to all armor *except* Dwarven (the effective smithing skill portion is unchanged).
- The Steel Smithing perk does not affect Imperial weapons, despite them being made of steel and also despite Imperial armor being affected by the perk. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.7, fixes this bug.
- If you make your Smithing skill [legendary](Skyrim_Skills.md#Legendary_Skills), then raise your skill and reselect the *Arcane Blacksmith* perk, you may be unable to improve enchanted weapons and armor, especially ones that have been given two enchantments.
