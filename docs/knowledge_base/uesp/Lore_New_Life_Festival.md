# New Life Festival

[![The banner of the New Life Festival](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/ON-banner-New_Life_Festival.png/150px-ON-banner-New_Life_Festival.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-New_Life_Festival.png) The New Life Festival (also shortened to **New Life**) is a [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) -wide [event](Lore_Holidays.md) that celebrates the birth of a new year and the death of the old year.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SHCNLF-1)</sup> In contrast to the [Old Life Festival](Lore_Old_Life_Festival.md), New Life takes place at the beginning of [Morning Star](Lore_Morning_Star.md). It was originally the celebration of the sun god, [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md), but over time it changed into a celebration of the gift of New Life, heralded by the sun.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOLTN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup> The New Life Festival also marks the start of the [Winter Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md#Winter_Solstice), when Magnus the Sun begins his return, and the days start to grow longer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SHCNLF-1)</sup> Almost all major races in Tamriel celebrate the New Life Festival in their own culturally unique ways, and the celebrations themselves have been known to change over time. Many are symbolic of historical events or carry deeper meanings. Festivities include races, feasts, games, and dancing.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ON-4)</sup>

Many of the New Life Festival’s traditions have been influenced by the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) holiday of [Saturalia](Lore_Saturalia.md), which over time has become closely associated with the festival and is sometimes regarded as almost synonymous with it.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LATH-5)</sup> Most notably, this includes the decoration of evergreen trees topped with a shining light ornament,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> which are then called Saturalia Trees to reflect their Breton origins. Evergreen trees are valued for their symbolism of resilience and rebirth,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SASL-7)</sup> and placing gifts beneath Saturalia Trees is a customary part of the celebration.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CWCFP-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BLSG-9)</sup> During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) ’s role as a major trade hub helped Saturalia-inspired trees, decorations, and other seasonal goods spread widely, creating a booming market and establishing them as a familiar part of New Life Festival observances even among cultures with their own long-standing traditions. This adoption began during the time of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LATH-5)</sup> and the influence of these Breton traditions continues to be felt in New Life Festival celebrations into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SASL-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Across Tamriel, citizens from all walks of life become New Life Heralds, assisting those who wish to partake in the festivities. Merchants enjoy the resulting prosperity, though not everyone appreciates the revelry. In [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), during the tenure of [Breda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breda) as a New Life Herald, a local politician in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) criticized the revelry as frivolous and hedonistic in times of peril and contrary to the teachings of [Jhunal](Lore_Jhunal.md) and [Stuhn](Lore_Stuhn.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MTYOB-11)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup> In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the reigning Emperor was known to give a New Life Address during this period, and many provinces observed the tradition of free [ale](Lore_Ale.md) in all taverns.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DF-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CS-13)</sup><sup>:146</sup> Vampires, werewolves, and the undead actively oppose the festival, so they dance in darkness to hinder the return of Magnus as the days grow longer,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-NLFI-14)</sup> while the Maormer do not participate in the New Life Festival at all.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup>

[Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md)'s summoning day coincides with the New Life Festival.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DF-12)</sup> The [Serpent](Lore_Astronomy.md#The_Serpent) is also believed to hatch from a [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) egg on this day, dropping its ivory egg tooth down to Tamriel and slithering across the sky for another year.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TSET-15)</sup>

## Celebrations
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-New_Life_Festival.jpg) A New Life Festival get-together Common traditional celebrations across various cultural traditions include food, drink, games, and dance.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BLSG-9)</sup>

### Humans
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/ON-event-New_Life_Festival_2016.jpg/200px-ON-event-New_Life_Festival_2016.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-event-New_Life_Festival_2016.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-event-New_Life_Festival_2016.jpg) The Castle Charm Challenge (ESO) Before celebrating the New Life Festival on the first day of the next year, the Bretons observe Saturalia in Wayrest on the 25th of [Evening Star](Lore_Evening_Star.md), six days earlier, which heralds the New Life Festival.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SDF-16)</sup> The [Alcaire Castle](Lore_Alcaire_Castle.md), after recovering from a bloody war, developed a New Life Festival tradition called the Castle Charm Challenge where participants must entertain the castle's inhabitants with a daring performance. This celebration gave the survivors a chance to heal and laugh once more.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONCCC-17)</sup> Alcaire Festival Sword-Pies are a hearty meat pie enjoyed during the New Life Festival. This Breton meal is served with a miniature bone sword cooked inside. Recovering the sword is said to grant the luck of Magnus.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> The [Eldertide](Lore_Eldertide_Circle.md) [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) of the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres.md) also celebrate the New Life with stargazing sessions and studying the [stars](Lore_Stars.md). They don festive robes for the occasion.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-NLWSR-19)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/ON-render-Snow_Bear_Plunge.jpg/200px-ON-render-Snow_Bear_Plunge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Snow_Bear_Plunge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Snow_Bear_Plunge.jpg) The Snow Bear Plunge (ESO) The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Lower Yorgrim celebrate New Life by doing the Snow Bear Plunge. This involves traveling to remote places, stripping down, and leaping into freezing water, then drying off by a large fire among fellow participants. This is to show that they aren't afraid of what the cold brings, and that at the end of a winter's journey is a warm fire and good company.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSBP-20)</sup> Snow Bear Glow-Wine is a Nordic hearty mulled wine for New Life that is always served piping hot.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Nords also celebrated with snow fights, meant to remind them of the simpler days of their childhood.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-CW-22)</sup> The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) also acknowledge the celebrations. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), one Skaal believed that his brother had been reincarnated as a bear carrying a fragment of the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md), though the community did not share this view. He also believed the bear’s mood during the New Life Festival would shape Tamriel’s fate in the coming year.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TJOG-23)</sup>

[Anseilak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anseilak) is a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) ritual during the New Life period that blesses fallen [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md). The ritual involves collecting seawater that once touched [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md)'s shores and sprinkling it on the resting place of the dead to pray for their peaceful rest. By the Second Era, the perception of magic users in Redguard society had led to the Yokudan mages of old being neglected and ignored when the ritual was being performed. Around 2E 582, Redguard mages traveled to the Motalion Necropolis near [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) in the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) to perform Anseilak for interred Yokudan mages.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Hassavir-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TDATS-25)</sup> The Redguards of [Bergama](Lore_Bergama.md) partake in the Signal Fire Sprint, a race to light several beacons around the city. This is to honor the sacrifice of [an ancestor](Lore_Zoreh_the_Tenacious.md) who saved Bergama from certain doom.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONAubatha-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONSFS-27)</sup> Bergama Warning Fire is a Redguard drink that is served hot during the New Life Festival and said to sharpen the wits. It is made with coffee, yerba mate, [dragonthorn](Lore_Dragonthorn.md), and lotus.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BWF-28)</sup>

The Old Life Festival, primarily an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) tradition, compliments the New Life Festival. These festivals are considered two sides of the same coin based on the idea that to pass into the new year, you must acknowledge the old.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOPG-29)</sup> Dark tales and unsettling songs are told as part of the Old Life traditions to commemorate the shortening of the days. *The Wolf That Craved the Sky* is one of these stories and is considered relevant to the New Life Festival. It involves [Storihbeg](Lore_Storihbeg.md),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-NLFI-14)</sup> [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s Skinchanger aspect.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOLTN-2)</sup> The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) also celebrate the New Life Festival and acknowledge the Skinchanger.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CM31-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-AOTLH-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-NLD-32)</sup> Their version states that with the coming of the New Life Festival, two of Hircine's aspects come to conflict with each other. New Life marks the winter solstice, when the days begin to lengthen due to the return of [the sun](Lore_Magnus.md). Storihbeg the Man-Beast thus pursues the sun in an attempt to eat it and keep the nights longer. To prevent this, Reachmen summon [Hrokkibeg](Lore_Hrokkibeg.md) the Mighty Bear to keep Storihbeg constrained.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-NLFI-14)</sup>

In the Nordic version of the tale, the Skinchanger’s endless hunger once drove it to feast on Morrowind’s volcanoes, creating the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) by draining the Iliac Bay, and to use Valenwood’s trees for oral hygiene. Seeing the moons untroubled, the Skinchanger claimed it could reach them and assume their forms, but the moons warned that the sun would notice. The Skinchanger vowed to devour the sun and stars as well, filling the world with darkness and letting out a triumphant howl. Yet the moons mocked him, saying the sun’s light was too rich a meal. Waiting for the sun’s weakest moment during the Old Life Festival, the Skinchanger struck, only for the sun to flare brightly at the New Life Festival and stop the devourer in pain.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOLTN-2)</sup>

### Elves
The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of [Haven](Lore_Haven.md) celebrate through the simple act of charity. Known as the War Orphan's Sojourn, participants travel to a temple in the heart of [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md) and donate what they can, be it their gold or their time.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONWOS-33)</sup> Old Aldmeri Orphan Gruel is a porridge that is traditionally served during the New Life Festival. This Altmer meal is surprisingly tasty, and it's made with barley, roses, and pumpkins.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup> Altmer give back to their elders by cooking meals for them as thanks for the wisdom they imparted.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ONFFN-35)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/ON-render-Mudball.jpg/200px-ON-render-Mudball.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Mudball.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Mudball.jpg) The Mud Ball Merriment (ESO) [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) immigrants to [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) influenced the Altmer when they introduced the locals of [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md) to the Mud Ball Merriment, a game where balls of mud are thrown at everyone. Each New Life, the Bosmer also agree upon a specific target that they feel needs to be humbled the most.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ONMDM-36)</sup> The Mud Ball Merriment still exists in Skywatch into the Fourth Era.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-37)</sup> Bosmeri communities across [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md) have a variety of traditions. One community travel across Valenwood to sing melodies to the Green. Another community constructs the Mudman, an effigy constructed to bless the new year with fertile growth across all of Tamriel.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONMM-38)</sup> Jagga-Drenched "Mud Ball" is a Bosmer seasonal dessert of sticky consistency traditionally enjoyed during the New Life Festival. It is made with bone flour and senche-passed coffee beans.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) celebrate the New Life with an act of defiance. When dancing was briefly outlawed in the city, the citizens found a way around it by inventing the Lava Foot Stomp. They called the dance a necessity should their boot ever catch fire and performed it in various taverns across town.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONLFS-40)</sup> Lava Foot Soup-and-Saltrice is a seasonal Dunmer meal enjoyed during the New Life Festival. The recipe calls for saltrice, root vegetables, scrib jelly, and flour. A good serving of this soup keeps the scrib on the surface of the broth.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-41)</sup> More devout Dunmer instead celebrate **Triune**, where they visit a temple and learn about the lives and lessons of their [saints](Lore_Temple_Saints.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-IWADE-42)</sup> The [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)'s version of New Life was **Born Anew**, which primarily focused on Ancestral Worship and the veneration of the [Good Daedra](Lore_Good_Daedra.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LATH-5)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/ON-render-New_Life%2C_New_Loot%21_Rewards.jpg/200px-ON-render-New_Life%2C_New_Loot%21_Rewards.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-New_Life,_New_Loot!_Rewards.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-New_Life,_New_Loot!_Rewards.jpg) At the seasonal spawning pools used for the Fish Boon Feast The [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Hissmir](Lore_Hissmir.md) have the Fish Boon Feast, one of [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md)'s oldest New Life celebrations. Those who are able to fish bring back food for those who are unable. This is to remind them that it warms the heart to feed the hungry and help those who are truly in need.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ONFBF-43)</sup> Hissmir Fish-Eye Rye is a foul smelling Argonian concoction typically enjoyed during the New Life Festival. Originating from Hissmir, it consists of rye alcohol, corn flowers, and lemon. Fish is also included in the brewing process, earning the drink its name.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup> [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md)'s Argonians have a practice for New Life of catching the [Dakoi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dakoi), a legendary fish said to bring good fortune and growth for those that consume it. Stormhold's villagers take on the role of the Dakoi itself, creating playful trials that encourage participants to confront their pride, work with others, and embrace change instead of resisting it. It concludes with the Dakoi's symbolic representation sharing fish as a reward for overcoming the challenges.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ONTHD-45)</sup>

During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Seamount](Lore_Seamount_Clan.md) [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) of [Betnikh](Lore_Betnikh.md) celebrated New Life with a constant party known as the Stonetooth Bash. Food, drink, and festivities were at the heart of their New Life celebration.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBreda-3)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONSTB-46)</sup> They partook in Betnikh's Boon, a test of strength where the one who shatters a boulder smallest with a single strike earns a boon of health until the next New Life.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ONB-47)</sup> Although the Orcs eventually lost control of the island and it was repopulated by Bretons,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-48)</sup> a rotisserie [chicken](Lore_Chicken.md) dish known as Stonetooth Bash Chicken became popularized across Tamriel and was still considered a New Life staple circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-49)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-50)</sup> Betnikh Twice-Spiked Ale was an Orcish ale they brewed for the New Life Festival with barley, yeast, rice, and honey.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-51)</sup>

The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Rawl'kha](Lore_Rawl%27kha.md) celebrate the New Life Festival by honoring their trickster god, [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md), with the Trial of the Five-Clawed Guile. The challenge involves stealing coins from either locked boxes or the pockets of a previous winner. This shows the participant has as little regard for barriers as Rajhin did.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ONTumira-52)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ONTFCG-53)</sup> Rajhin's Sugar Claws is a moon sugar heavy pastry enjoyed by Khajiit during the New Life Festival. It is made with flour, honey, and corn.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-54)</sup> Inspired by [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md) ’s legendary bravery, a wealthy Khajiit began a tradition where challengers face a person dressed as a dragon guarding a treasure hoard, retrieve a coin, and receive a prize as his way of giving back to the community.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ONTGD-55)</sup>

The people of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) celebrate the tradition of "Secret [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md)", where the identity of a gift giver is a mystery from the intended recipient. The gifts are promiscuous in nature, and are intended to dare the recipient into naughty acts.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-NLFI-14)</sup> Members of [Akatosh Chantry](Lore_Akatosh_Chantry.md) wear ceremonial stoles made of silk and satin with embroidered hourglasses during ceremonies of the New Life Festival.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Contraband A-56)</sup>
