#### Fire King Hadhuul (?b - [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243))
[The Fire King Hadhuul](Lore_Hadhuul.md) (or **Hadhuul the Fire King**) was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) leader who ruled before and during the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). He is remembered as a particularly cruel slave master; tales from his realm say the slaves were subjected to tormenting [drugs](Lore_Drugs.md), and children were set aflame for nighttime tiger sport. Shortly before the fall of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243), he was slain by [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) on the granite steps of Ceya-Tar (later called [Ceyatatar](Lore_Ceyatatar.md)) in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It was his first and last defeat. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Hadhuul's spirit returned to the ruins of Ceyatatar, along with bound [flame atronachs](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) and the ghosts of his army. The atronachs posed a threat to the nearby town of [Vlastarus](Lore_Vlastarus.md), although the Fire King was banished before he could cause any harm.

#### Haeliaran (?b - ca. [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243))
[Haeliaran](Lore_Haeliaran.md) was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) warlord who died some time around the Fall of [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), which marked the end of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243). He was interred in an Ayleid temple in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) but passed into undeath, becoming a powerful [wight](Lore_Wight.md) and a source of great evil.

At some point, a band of pirates known as the Fellhorn Raiders came from the north and settled in a nearby subterranean grotto. They made a home for themselves here by stealing from the local [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md). However, after several months the raiders uncovered the entrance to the ruined Ayleid temple, releasing the evil within. Haeliaran transformed the pirates into undead [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md) and compelled them to reseal the entrance to the temple.

Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), a group of miners who were camped nearby broke through into the subterranean grotto. The skeletons within slaughtered all but one of the miners that entered; the sole survivor remained within the cavern, onboard the pirates' old ship, confused by Haeliaran's magic and convinced that he was one of the Fellhorn Raiders himself. After a few days without contact, the mining camp above put out a call for help. [The Warrior](Lore_Hero.md#The_Warrior) arrived from the nearby town of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md) and agreed to enter the cave. The Warrior defeated the undead skeletons and succeeded in rescuing the surviving miner, who had come into possession of a key which would unlock the temple entrance. Within, Haeliaran burst from his tomb to confront the Warrior in battle, but he was defeated, and his stone sarcophagus was looted from the temple.

#### Haj-Ja (fl. 2E 582 or earlier)
[Haj-Ja](Lore_Haj-Ja.md) was an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) [smith](Lore_Smithing.md) of some renown. Little is known about them, but as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), they were already considered famous. Although little is known of their achievements, they were known to work with [copper](Lore_Copper.md) and craft flatware. A collection of engraved and burnished copper plates and flatware crafted by Haj-Ja were found all around [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the tumultuous period of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Haj-Ja, similarly to many other smiths of that time, was known to stamp all their products with their mark.

#### Baron Harabant Dufort (fl. early/mid 2E)
[Harabant Dufort](Lore_Harabant_Dufort.md), also known as **Harabant Handless**, was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman who reigned as the first Baron of [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Harabant built the Meadow Fort, a smaller holding in the Oldstone Woods of [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md). It was at the fort that Harabant fought the Battle of Meadow Fort, which was the final battle of an unknown conflict. The battle started at dawn and continued without cessation until sunset.

Harabant's famed sword, Redheart, broke during the last hour of the battle. Despite Redheart breaking, Harabant defeated the dread sorcerer Fellbore by hurling the blade and driving it through the sorcerer's heart. Harabant lost his hand because of wounds sustained in the battle. He subsequently became known as Harabant Handless until he was granted a barony. It was said that he had Redheart's blade melted down and crafted into a hand to replace the one he had lost.

At some point following the Battle of Meadow Fort, officials in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) decided that the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) needed legitimate nobility to truly prosper, so it was decreed that Harabant and House Dufort would be granted a barony. As the first Baron Dufort, many of Harabant's subjects attested to his irritability and were bruised by his hand. He later replaced the Meadow Fort with Castle Navire to the west, which became House Dufort's primary residence.

#### Jarl Hakkvild Yashnag-Slayer (fl. 2E 467)
Jarl of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) in the Second Era. He was young when he inherited the crumbling hold after the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) chief [Yashnag](Lore_Yashnag.md) slew his father in battle. In a ritual trial by combat, he challenged Yashnag and his champions and defeated them in turn, eradicating the Orc chiefdom of Western Falkreath in [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OOS-1)</sup> Jarl Hakkvild later traveled to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) and presented the Orcish war drum, [King Thunder](Lore_King_Thunder.md) to the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) to commemorate his victory.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KT-2)</sup>

#### Haknir Death-Brand (fl. before 3E 427)
An infamous pirate king also known as "The King of Ghosts" who terrorized the northern coasts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He supposedly had an unnaturally long lifespan, although it is unknown to historians how much of the ancient "Haknir Saga" is true. He was said to be a fearless fighter who enjoyed torture and murder for its own sake, and that he had a great wound upon his face which never healed - his eponymous Deathbrand, thought to have been a term in some unknown agreement with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). When he was finally at death's door as an old man, he purportedly placed a curse upon his treasure, including his [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) armor, and had it buried on a rocky shoal off the northern coast of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). He also ordered his boats destroyed, as he did not believe any of his crew were fit to replace him.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Deathbrand-3)</sup> He then sealed himself inside the ruins of [Gyldenhul Barrow](Lore_Gyldenhul_Barrow.md) along with his treasure.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

#### Hakon One-Eye (fl. late Merethic Era)
[Hakon One-Eye](Lore_Hakon_One-Eye.md) was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmoran.md) hero and [Tongue](Lore_Tongue.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). Hakon, [Felldir the Old](Lore_Felldir_the_Old.md), and [Gormlaith Golden-Hilt](Lore_Gormlaith_Golden-Hilt.md) were the leaders of the rebellion against [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). They were among the first mortals to learn how to wield the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), apart from the [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md). They were taught by [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md). During the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), Hakon sought the help of [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md), the First Dragonborn, in defeating Alduin, but Miraak refused to help. Undeterred, Hakon and his companions fought against Alduin atop the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md). Finding that they had no means to achieve a final victory, the companions successfully sent the wyrm forward through time using an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), when the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) entered the realm of [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md) to defeat Alduin, the spirits of Hakon and his compatriots joined the fray in the final battle against their nemesis. He also aided the Last Dragonborn in their other battles, when called to their aid with the Call of Valor.

#### Arch-Mage Hannibal Traven (?b - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Hannibal Traven](Lore_Hannibal_Traven.md) was the Arch-Mage of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He was a staunch opponent of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) and was responsible for several poorly-implemented guild reforms.

Originally, Professor Traven was the head of the [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) Mages Guild, and supposedly ran a "clean hall". He was promoted to the position of Arch-Mage circa [3E 431](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_431). Among his controversial reforms were a tightening on admission to the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) and an outright ban on the practice of necromancy by guild mages. Traven's divisive stance on necromancy had many strong supporters even outside the guild, although it caused a large degree of internal fracturing. Half the Council of Mages resigned, and Magister [Ulliceta gra-Kogg](Lore_Ulliceta_gra-Kogg.md) of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) was forced to flee following her attempted arrest by the [Knights of the Lamp](Lore_Knights_of_the_Lamp.md) for practicing necromancy. Traven would go on to publish [*The Black Arts On Trial*](Lore_The_Black_Arts_On_Trial.md), to document the debate between gra-Kogg and Magister Voth Karlyss of [Corinth](Lore_Corinth.md).

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), coinciding with Traven's policy change, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) returned to the affairs of mortals. With necromancers now shunned from the Mages Guild, many flocked to join the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md). Mannimarco's followers were soon openly attacking the guild, going so far as to burn down the [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) guildhall. As Traven struggled to control the Council of Mages, he turned to the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md#Hero_of_Kvatch) for help in investigating and delaying Mannimarco's plans. Traven had the Hero secure both the [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md) and the [Bloodworm Helm](Lore_Bloodworm_Helm.md), as well as a unique colossal black soul gem the necromancers had created. In a final effort to save the guild, Traven sacrificed himself by trapping his [soul](Lore_Souls.md) within the colossal gem to allow the Hero to confront Mannimarco. With the filled gem, the Hero was shielded from Mannimarco's powerful magic and was able to defeat him in [Echo Cave](Lore_Echo_Cave.md). Traven's final act as Arch-Mage was to appoint the Hero as his successor.

Although Traven's sacrifice saved the Mages Guild from destruction at the hands of the Order of the Black Worm, the institute was not able to survive the strong anti- [magic](Lore_Magic.md) sentiment that arose in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). The [Synod](Lore_Synod.md) and the [College of Whispers](Lore_College_of_Whispers.md) formed following the guild's dissolution. Before his defeat, Mannimarco revealed that he had planned to reanimate Traven as a [Worm Thrall](Lore_Worm_Thrall.md) for study.

#### Harald ([1E 113](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_113) - [1E 221](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_221))
[High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) (also called **Harald Hand-Free** and **Harald of Ysgramoor**) was the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the thirteenth monarch descended from [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md). #### Haras (?b - 2E 582)
[Haras](Lore_Haras.md) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) who led the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) sect of the Hounds of Hircine, a group of legendary hunters who were devoted to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). The Valenwood Hounds were distinguished among the order and recognized as the greatest hunters in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Haras was considered the greatest contributor to this status, a legend known to every Hound across all Tamriel, Haras was considered the best tracker to ever live. Haras personally trained the Valenwood Hounds how to hunt and his methods were the foundation for each training of every Valenwood Hound thereafter.

Eventually Haras set his sights on the ultimate challenge, the hunt of the fabled Pale Senche-Tiger. Haras was unmatched in his devotion to Hircine, and implored the Prince of the Hunt to let him hunt his greatest beast. Hircine granted him this wish, while also making him immune to aging and tiring. Haras' first attempt at capturing the beast ended in near death for himself, and also the death of one of his fellow Hounds of Hircine.

Haras would spend several centuries, beyond the natural lifespan of a Bosmer, endlessly hunting and tracking and the Pale Senche, becoming completely subsumed in the goal. Over time Haras became an increasingly legendary figure among the Hounds, some questioned if he was even real or not, while other hunters claimed to have seen him on occasion, still hunting the elusive beast.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) a [hero](Lore_Hero.md) known as [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) found themselves in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) a region of Valenwood where several Hounds of Hircine, and by chance, Haras were pursuing prey. The Vestige helped several Hounds track and neutralize their prey before finally encountering Haras himself.

Haras was laid out in a state of exhaustion and wounds that could not be seen by the naked eye when the Vestige encountered them, in no condition to hunt any further, Haras confided what information he could of the Pale Senches location. The Vestige managed to track and slay the beast and claim its ivory hide. This caused an aspect of Hircine to appear to the Vestige directly who was pleased with the outcome. Hircine bade the Vestige return to Haras and let him know he may finally rest. Haras was delighted to see the hide of the great beast and died with peace.

#### Lord Harkon (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
A [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [Vampire Lord](Lore_Vampire_Lord.md) and creator of the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md) vampire bloodline. Harkon was a very rich ruler of the lands, but feared mortality. He made a pact with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and sacrificed a thousand innocents to the Prince so he and his family could become vampires. Hidden for many eras in Castle Volkihar, Harkon plotted to blot out the sun so vampires could rule [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). His wife [Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md) tried to deny him this ritual by going into hiding with an Elder Scroll and sealing their daughter Serana away in a crypt. The [Dovahkiin](Lore_Hero.md), armed with [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) and with the help of Harkon's estranged daughter [Serana](Lore_Serana.md), fought Harkon within his castle and slew him.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Dawnguard-8)</sup>

#### Haskill (?b - ?d)
[Haskill](Lore_Haskill.md) is the Chamberlain of [Lord Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), "the one constant in the ever-changing whims of the Madgod". He often takes on the appearance of an elderly [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) man in a melancholy yet flamboyant suit. Just like his colleague [Dyus](Lore_Dyus.md), former Chamberlain of [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md), his exact origins are unknown; but he has claimed to have once been a mortal who [mantled](Lore_Mantling.md) Sheogorath, becoming a [Vestige](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md). On another occasion, however, he has claimed to have been under the service of Sheogorath since the "beginning". He took care of all of the more administrative and mundane duties involved in running the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Despite having once been mortal, he has no desire to visit [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He has a strong dislike of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and finds mortals to be irritating.

#### Haymon Camoran (?b - [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267))
[Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md) (?- [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267)), best known as the **Camoran Usurper** but also as the **Hart-King** and **Haymon Hart-King**, was a claimant to the throne of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), together with Camoran Kaltos, whom he initially served as advisor and sorcerer. After conquering most of Eastern Valenwood, disagreements with Camoran Kaltos over the Usurper's continued campaigning in [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) caused him to turn on Kaltos and take the throne for himself. A period of violent warfare throughout western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) ensued, affecting Valenwood, Colovia, [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and parts of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), before Haymon Camoran's forces were finally defeated at [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md) in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267) by an alliance of High Rock kingdoms in a combined naval and land battle. For most of this war, [Emperor Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md) was simultaneously occupied in a civil war over the Imperial succession, and was not able to effectively counter Camoran's invasion.

#### Heimdall the Frenzied (fl. Merethic Era)

Heimdall, together with [Ulfgi Anvil-Hand](Lore_Ulfgi_Anvil-Hand.md), [Strom the White](Lore_Strom_the_White.md), and [Freida Oaken-Wand](Lore_Freida_Oaken-Wand.md), stood among the mightiest warriors of their group, yet all were defeated by the [Snow Prince](Lore_Snow_Prince.md), his [spear](Lore_Spear_of_the_Snow_Prince.md), and his mastery of ice and snow [magic](Lore_Magic.md). They fell in battle and lay dead at the foot of the Moesring Mountains. #### King Hlaalu Helseth ([3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376) - ?d)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg/200px-TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg) King Helseth [Hlaalu Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md), a male [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), was born to [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) and [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) in [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376), and he is the older brother of [Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md). Before the "[Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md)", he spent most of his time blackmailing, bribing, and even killing nobles in attempts to expand Wayrest's influence. After failing to obtain the throne of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) when his step-father King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) died, he returned to [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) with his mother. Once there, he joined the court of his grand uncle, King [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Llethan.md). After a relatively short period of time, [Talen Vandas](Lore_Talen_Vandas.md), Llethan's chosen heir, died, followed soon after by the King himself, leaving the throne of Morrowind to Helseth. This caused rumors that Helseth had poisoned them, a telling of events that the Queen Dowager, Ravani Llethan, backs up: she also knew of and appears to have been allied to a conspiracy, within [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), against Helseth in favor of a candidate more acceptable to the House. Indeed, despite Helseth being of House Hlaalu himself as well as a nephew of King Llethan, he still was an outlander, not born in Morrowind itself, and a pro-Imperial at that, having his elven ears cropped as to look more Imperial and knowing almost nothing about Dunmer culture. This political stance was certainly shared by his late father, who ended up fighting for the Empire against his own province. The Tribunal Temple also initially conspired against him, but were thrown into disarray by the fall of their living gods. He wore an ancient crown of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), with five points curving inward like blades, supporting one in the middle: what it symbolized in elder days was debatable, but it meant "Five Houses Under One King" in the modern day.

After becoming King, Helseth quickly started to cement his hold on power by using the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) to assassinate a variety of targets, including an attempt to kill the recently reborn [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), though according to Helseth this was a mistake - and one which backfired heavily, since the entire Mournhold chapter of the Brotherhood was wiped out when the Nerevarine pursued the attacks back to their source. The truthfulness of Helseth's apology is suspect, as there was another attempt on the Nerevarine's life while guarding Queen Barenziah for the new King. Strangely, King Helseth tends to play up his habit of assassination, as he was one of the driving forces to publish *[A Game at Dinner](Lore_A_Game_at_Dinner.md)*, a book that accuses him of murder.

In a shrewd political maneuver, King Helseth, representing his own [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), formed an alliance with [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) by marrying Lady Vendama of [Bodrum Mora](Lore_Bodrum.md). Subsequently, he renounced the slave trade, setting the remaining Houses of [Indoril](Lore_Indoril.md) and [Redoran](Lore_Redoran.md) against him through a period of civil unrest. After the successive catastrophes of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) invasion and the break-up of the Empire which had backed him, at some early point during the next 200 years House Hlaalu fell from prominence and Redoran became the new preeminent house, with Hlaalu's place taken by the upstart House [Sadras](Lore_Sadras.md). Neither Helseth nor Barenziah's, nor the Morrowind monarchy's fate after these catastrophies is known, although the former are presumed to have lost their lives before or after the sacking of Mournhold by the invading Argonian hordes.

#### The Hoar Father (?b - ?d)

#### Holgeir (fl. before 4E 201)

However, then Fjori too was bitten by a snake, and she died immediately due to exhaustion from her journey. Holgeir had a great tomb built, and after its completion, he took his own life, hoping to rejoin her in [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md).

#### King Hrol (died before 1E 2703)
**King** [Hrol](Lore_Hrol.md) was said to be a king from lands beyond the lost [Twil](Lore_Twil.md) who along with sixteen western questing knights, set out to heal the borders of his forebears as he had visions of the [Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) that was to come. Hrol and his men marched along sporting banners adorned with [Heartland Brindle Badgers](Lore_Badger.md). At a point within the journey, Hrol and his men came upon the spirit of the Slave Queen, Empress [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) who bore the [Dragonfire Covenant](Lore_Dragonfires.md) in one hand, and the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) in the other. In a perhaps mad, divine driven attempt to restore the Empire unity of old, Hrol made union unto a hillock which he believed Alessia's spirit resided in.

This labor cost him his own life, and that of his shieldthane and the sanity of the rest of his men. Despite this, the legend holds that nine months later that very same mound of mud had become a mountain that would come to be known as the Golden Hill [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md), and atop that mountain lied the babe born of the deeds of Hrol, and Alessia the land, [Reman Cyrodiil I](Lore_Reman.md) who had the Amulet of Kings embedded into his forehead and quickly came to have undisputed rule as the new Emperor of Cyrodiil. This event is occasionally referred to as "the Quest of Sancre Tor". Some vials of the famous soil that Hrol fertilized were bottled up and fenced around Tamriel given their great value. An artifact known as Hrol's Golden Girdle was known to at one time have resided in the [Temple of the One](Lore_Temple_of_the_One.md).

#### Jarl Hrolfdir (4E ?b - c. 4E 176)
[Hrolfdir](Lore_Hrolfdir.md) was the Jarl of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and the father of [Igmund](Lore_Igmund.md). In 4E 174, the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), led by [Madanach](Lore_Madanach.md), took advantage of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md)'s preoccupation with the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) to overthrow Hrolfdir and declare [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to be an independent kingdom. By [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176), Hrolfdir was desperate to retake the Reach, so he turned to a Nord militia led by [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) and promised them that he would permit the free worship of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) in Markarth, which was outlawed under the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) that ended the Great War, in exchange for their help retaking the hold. In a short and bloody war, Ulfric and the Nord militia laid siege to Markarth, and when they breached the city gates, they drove the Reachman rebels from the city. The surviving Reachman rebels fled into the hills and continued to fight the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in the [Forsworn Rebellion](Lore_Forsworn_Rebellion.md).

As a condition to return to Markarth, Hrolfdir, his son Igmund, and his steward Raerek swore oaths to the Empire to abandon the worship of Talos. Following the retaking Markarth, Hrolfdir reportedly ordered the executions of Reachmen with suspected ties to the rebels. After Ulfric and his militia retook Markarth, Hrolfdir allowed them to worship Talos in the city. Shortly after the retaking of Markarth, the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) found out about the promise of legal Talos worship. Faced with the threat of a second war with the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), Hrolfdir helped the Imperial Legion arrest Ulfric Stormcloak and much of the Nord militia in an event called the Markarth Incident.

After the Markarth Incident, Hrolfdir attempted to negotiate peace with the remaining Reachman rebels, who were now calling themselves the Forsworn. While Hrolfdir was attempting to negotiate with the Forsworn, they killed him and took his shield. Eventually, the Forsworn gave his shield to one of their [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) matrons as a trophy. Igmund succeeded him as Jarl of Markarth. He was remembered as an active leader, one who did not sit idly by while evil men took over his lands.

#### Huna (?b - 1E 242 or 1E 243)
[Huna](Lore_Huna.md) was one of the confidants and companions of the legendary [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md). He lived in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and was originally a [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) slave of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) overlords who ruled over their human subjects at the early years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). As a slave, one of Huna's duties was collecting grain for Ayleid masters. At some point, during, or before the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), he was liberated by Pelinal and given freedom. Huna decided to join the rebellion and was trained to become a hoplite.

Eventually, the relationship between Huna and Pelinal transcended the one of a liberator and liberated, to that of lovers. Although Pelinal was by some legends lover of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), the only one he gave non-familial affection to was Huna, who "he had raised up from a grain-slave and loved well."<sup>[†](#intnote_love)</sup>

Huna fought alongside Pelinal and other rebels, until he died by an arrow of Celethelel the Singer in [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242) or [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243). Huna's death was a catalyst for Pelinal's madness. Once Huna died Pelinal would descend into several flights of madness during which he cut vast swathes in mindless fury to avenge him, destroying many Ayleid cities including Narlemae and Celediil, as well as slaughtering the innocent [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) he mistook for Elves, which culminated in him being banished at the [Sunspire](Lore_Sunspire.md) by [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md).

#### Hunroor (fl. 2E)

He and four companions embarked on a quest to find [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md). However, Hunroor, [Erlendr](Lore_Erlendr.md), and [Nikulas](Lore_Nikulas.md) were betrayed by the wizard [Grimkell](Lore_Grimkell.md), who turned them into stone pillars at Brodir Grove on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). They were avenged by [Ulfgar the Unending](Lore_Ulfgar_the_Unending.md), who killed the wizard. All of the friends eventually did find their way to Sovngarde.

#### King Hurlburt (2E ?b - [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546))
[Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md) was the King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), reigning from [2E 522](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_522) until his death in [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546), and the twenty-first Count of [House Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md). Considered the greatest post- [Reman](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) monarch of Shornhelm, Hurlburt led his kingdom's army at the Battle of Granden Tor. In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541) or [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542), [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) and his horde of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) invaded [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and plundered much of the province before finally being defeated in a battle outside [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md). A fortnight after the defeat of Durcorach and his horde, Hurlburt and the kings of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), realizing how weak their realms were individually, signed the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), a pact promising to defend each other's kingdoms and unite as one against all foreign foes.

He wed Countess [Iphilia](Lore_Iphilia_Montclair.md) of [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md), with whom he fathered a son named [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md) in [2E 532](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_532). He also fathered an illegitimate elder son, [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md), with a [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) cousin. In [2E 544](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_544), his queen, Iphilia, died, making twelve-year-old Phylgeon the thirty-eighth Baron of House Montclair.

In 2E 546, Hurlburt died, instigating a succession dispute between Phylgeon and Ranser over the throne of Shornhelm, and Ranser narrowly prevailed by citing a long-lost [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) decree. After Ranser's murder at the Battle of Traitor's Tor in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), a new monarch was not selected, and instead [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) was ruled by the triumvirate Council of the North. Hurlburt's grandson Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md) attempted to claim the crown of Shornhelm, stating that as the son of Phylgeon, he was the only legitimate descendant of King Hurlburt.

#### King Hylfrald (fl. before 2E 587)
**King** [Hylfrald](Lore_Hylfrald.md) was the ruler of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) at some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). He ruled from his seat of power in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). Little is known of his reign, except that as of then [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) marauders were active at the borders of his kingdom, northwest of [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md).
