# Ayleid Ruins

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg/200px-ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg) [Garlas Malatar](Lore_Garlas_Malatar.md), an Ayleid ruin in Cyrodiil *Main article: [Elven Ruins](Lore_Elven_Ruins.md)*

Ayleid ruins are all that remain of the city-states and settlements of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md). They are a common sight in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md)'s seat of power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> Due to migration events such as the [Scouring of Wendelbek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scouring_of_Wendelbek) and the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASIV-3)</sup> ruins can also be found outside of Cyrodiil, in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> with some also present in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ON-2)</sup> Most of these ruins still have functioning [Welkynd](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) and [Varla](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) stones.

Many Ayleid settlements continue to be inhabited even after the disappearance of the Ayleids. They often serve as lairs for outcasts such as bandits and [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md), while some, such as the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md), have been renovated and function as modern settlements.

Ayleid ruins are characterized by the white [limestone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Limestone) used in their construction.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The [Blessed Life-Tree](Lore_Blessed_Life-Tree.md) is a common symbol found on doors in these ruins.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> [Statues](Lore_Statues.md) of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) and other Ayleid figures are also a common sight.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
