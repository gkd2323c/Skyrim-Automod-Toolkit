[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/SR-npc-Erikur.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Erikur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Erikur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Erikur.jpg) Erikur **Erikur**, a [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) [warrior](Skyrim_CombatWarrior1H.md), is a [thane](Skyrim_Thane.md) of [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md). He oversees the local businesses and imports low quality weaponry from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) to sell to the [Imperial army](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md) for a high price. However, his loyalty is flexible and can even be bought by the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md). As an influential and wealthy citizen of Skyrim, he is one of the party guests at the [Thalmor Embassy](Skyrim_Thalmor_Embassy.md) during [Diplomatic Immunity](Skyrim_Diplomatic_Immunity.md), where he may serve as a distraction. He is also the older brother of [Gisli](Skyrim_Gisli.md), who despises him for his pompous and questionable tendencies.

He sleeps from midnight to 8am, then heads to the [Blue Palace](Skyrim_Blue_Palace.md) to [attend court](#Conversations) straight away. At 1pm, he enjoys a two-hour lunch at the palace. He then resumes his duties until 8pm, when he spends the night relaxing at [his house](Skyrim_Erikur%27s_House.md) before going to bed at midnight. However, during Diplomatic Immunity, he will be found at the Thalmor Embassy until the quest ends.

Erikur wears a set of [fine clothes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fine_Clothes#000f8713) along with a pair of [fine boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fine_Boots#00086993). He wields a [steel dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger) and carries the [key](Skyrim_Keys.md#Key_to_Erikur.27s_House) to [his house](Skyrim_Erikur%27s_House.md), a [belted tunic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Belted_Tunic), and a selection of [upper-class items and gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Rich).

## Dialogue
He will often talk about politics and business:

*"The Imperials are good for business, and business is good for Skyrim."* *"I understand what's best for Solitude... and Jarl [Elisif](Skyrim_Elisif_the_Fair.md) knows it."* *"Half of the businesses in town owe me money, and I own most of the rest. I practically run Solitude already."* *"[Bryling](Skyrim_Bryling.md)'s obsession with honor and tradition is quaint, but politically irrelevant."* Should you speak to him directly you can ask him some questions:

**What's your place in the court?** *"I'm a Thane. In the future, you may want to gain that information before talking with someone. Risk of looking like an idiot, and all that."* **Do all the Thanes support Elisif?** *"Oh, most certainly. At least, when Elisif herself asks.
If you're inquiring whether we have concerns about her age and inexperience, well, let me put it this way...
The Thanes have every confidence in [General Tullius](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md)'s leadership."* However, if the [Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md) capture Solitude, his answer will instead be:

*"I don't suppose it matters much anymore.
As long as Ulfric wishes Elisif to remain Jarl, remain Jarl she will. And the Thanes will continue to follow her orders.
As long as those orders came from Ulfric."*

He'll continue sanctimoniously, and end in a more jovial tone; commenting that his work brings in loads of gold.

### [The Dainty Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Dainty_Sload)
If you have completed enough [Thieves Guild](Skyrim_Thieves_Guild_(faction).md) jobs in Solitude, asking [Delvin Mallory](Skyrim_Delvin_Mallory.md) about a special job will have him tell you, *"I need you to make your way to Solitude and talk to Erikur. He's lookin' to run some kind of a shill job. Now, he's not exactly goin' to welcome you with open arms, but keep your cool. Do this job right and we've just made an important friend out there."*

Now approach Erikur and tell him about how Delvin Mallory said he has work for you. If you claim Delvin sent you because you're the best, he'll air a hint of doubt, but if you get straight to the point, he'll be more enthusiastic. Regardless of how you answer him, Erikur will elaborate on his predicament.

**Delvin Mallory said you have work for me.** *"It's about time you got here.
I'm not accustomed to dealing with people who are unreliable. That's why I asked Delvin to send me his best."* **That's why he sent me.** *"Hmph. That remains to be seen."* **Let's just get to work.** *"That's more like it."* *"Nothing raises my ire more than having an agreement broken. It's bad for business and it wastes time.
Captain Volf of the [Dainty Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dainty_Sload) has decided to test my patience on this matter by neglecting to honor a trade agreement we had established."* **I assume this is where I enter the picture.** *"I need you to help me show him the error of his ways by sneaking on board the Sload and planting some contraband."* **What sort of contraband?** *"You'll need to get your hands on some [Balmora Blue](Skyrim_Potions.md#Balmora_Blue) from [Sabine Nyette](Skyrim_Sabine_Nytte.md) down by the docks. She's the first mate on another ship, the [Red Wave](Skyrim_Red_Wave.md).
Once you get your hands on it, I want you to plant it in Captain Volf's footlocker. I'll take care of the rest."* **Consider it done.** *"Captain Volf is ashore right now and I want the authorities waiting for him when he gets back.
Now get going... I don't want to see your face until the job's done."* Before you get to work, you may inquire about the Red Wave and Balmora Blue.

**You have any information on the Red Wave?** *"There's pirates and then there's the crew of the Red Wave. They're in a class by themselves.
They usually make runs along the coast shipping all sorts of contraband to and from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).
Rumor has it they can get you anything for the right price."* **What exactly is Balmora Blue?** *"Not sure. I know that it starts with [Moon Sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Moon_Sugar), but all sorts of other ingredients are added to increase its potency.
Used to be a lucrative underworld commodity when [Balmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balmora) was still standing. Now the stuff is beyond valuable.
It's also very illegal. Anyone caught with Balmora Blue looks forward to rotting in jail for a very long time."* Once you've completed the dirty work for Erikur, give him the good news. He will reward you with a random [spell tome](Skyrim_Spell_Tomes.md).

**I've planted the contraband on the Dainty Sload.** *"Yes, I know. In fact, by now, Captain Volf should be on his way to the prisons.
Our contract is complete. Here's a token of my gratitude for your efforts.
Oh, convey my compliments to Delvin for me. Tell him I'll be happy to reopen whatever doors he needs in Solitude."* Return to Delvin, who will be pleased as well: *"Erikur assures me that Captain Volf will spend pretty much the rest of his life clapped in irons. Best of all, he's also pledged to open doors for us in Solitude and to get things rollin' again."*

## Notes
- During [The Dainty Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Dainty_Sload), his inflection changes, noticeably sounding quite unlike the usual Male Sly Cynical voice.
- He is under the protection of the [Thieves Guild](Skyrim_Thieves_Guild_(faction).md). His house is marked several times with the "protected" [shadowmark](Skyrim_Shadowmarks_(Thieves_Guild).md). Despite this, he may still be the target of a Thieves Guild [special jobs quest](Skyrim_Thieves_Guild_(faction).md#Special_Job_Quests), even if the reputation quest The Dainty Sload has been completed.
- Erikur does not have a unique facial model, he instead uses the default male Nord face. He shares this quirk with numerous other NPCs, including [Briehl](Skyrim_Briehl.md), [Sond](Skyrim_Sond_(bandit).md), [Lynoit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lynoit), [Barknar](Skyrim_Barknar_(Dawnguard).md), and many other generic NPCs.
- A scene with Gisli implies that Erikur owns the building Radiant Raiment is located in, and the sisters pay him rent. However, a scene Bryling has with [Irnskar](Skyrim_Irnskar_Ironhand.md) implies that *she* owns the building and the sisters pay *her* rent, instead.

## Bugs
- When Melaran speaks to Erikur about importing weapons from Black Marsh, his spoken dialogue does not match the subtitles. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.0, fixes this bug.
- Erikur may not appear on the map, making the quest The Dainty Sload impossible. He appears on the main map screen as being situated in the ocean, northeast of the Katariah, however the game directs you from Skyrim to the Blue Palace, and from there no direct path to Erikur can be found. - If this bug occurs, Erikur can be found underneath Solitude at the location of the map marker.
- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) Erikur can be moved from the middle of the ocean during The Dainty Sload if you use the following console commands: `prid 000198A9` and `moveto player` .
