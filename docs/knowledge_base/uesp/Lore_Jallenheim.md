## Jallenheim
**Jallenheim** is a village in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). In [2E 358](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_358), a note pertaining to the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) was discovered in an abandoned house, once owned by a cobbler, who was believed to be a member of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md). By then, the assassin's guild had been outlawed thirty-four years before then.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAD-1)</sup> The settlement is mentioned several times in the fictional book series *[The Adventures of Eslaf Erol](Lore_The_Adventures_of_Eslaf_Erol.md)*. There is apparently a large palace in a park near the town center, owned by a nobleman.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Thief-2)</sup>

## Japhet's Folly
[Japhet's Folly](Lore_Japhet%27s_Folly.md) is a fort on the inhospitable **Rockjoint Island** found northeast of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md), off the coast of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). It is known for its rough climate, lack of habitability, and tales of ghosts by the people of nearby [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md). A sailor by the name of Japhet decided to build a large citadel here, only to pass away from the sheer cold and the isolation, hence the name.

## Jehenna
[Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md) (also called **Jehenna** or **Jelhana**) is one of the nine historical [Breton kingdoms](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md) in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), situated on the icy shores of the [Northern Coast](Lore_Northern_Coast.md). Nestled in the snow-covered forests of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md), it is described as being as beautiful as its name.

## Jerall Mountains
The [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) are a mountain range on the border between [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They meet the [Velothi](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) and [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md) at the [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) border, and the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) at the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) border. The Jeralls are rugged, steep, and inhospitable, with only a few traversable passes. Of particular note is the [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), which was the site of the abrupt end to the [First Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), after which its location was lost, only being rediscovered in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).

## Jode's Light
[Jode's Light](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode%27s_Light) (*Ja'zennji Siir* in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md)) is a ruined temple located on the borderlands of [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md), in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). Named after the larger moon, [Jode](Lore_Jode.md), the temple was founded as a sanctum and haven for every monk and acolyte, no matter their [furstock](Lore_Furstock.md) or background, making it a place where the greatest philosophical minds of ancient Elsweyr gathered.

## Jodewood
[Jodewood](Lore_Jodewood.md) is the southernmost subregion of the greater [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md) region. Jodewood is a border region between the provinces of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), mainly lying within the borders of the former. The [Crescent River](Lore_Crescent_River.md) headwaters are here, flowing north to [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md) at the edge of the March. The capital of the area is the city of [Rawl'kha](Lore_Rawl%27kha.md), a trading post in between the two provinces. The town of Willowgrove is a minor settlement known for its apiary and its brewery, which is used to make honeymead. The Do'Krin Monastery is an ancient [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) temple dating back to the time of [Darloc Brae](Lore_Darloc_Brae.md). The ruins of [Thormar](Lore_Thormar.md) are a known gathering place for worshippers of [Baan Dar](Lore_Baan_Dar.md), who have been known to enter the [Five Finger Dance](Lore_Five_Finger_Dance.md) from here.

## Jonelight Path
The [Jonelight Path](Lore_Jonelight_Path.md) is a [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) used by [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) heroes as a shortcut to travel between the areas of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). It is a place between all places and is named for [Jone](Lore_Jone.md), the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) name for the smaller of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)'s two [moons](Lore_Moons.md). The realm appears to be a collection of floating islands connected by the paths of stars. The realm was described as full of [stars](Lore_Stars.md) and moonlight.

It can be accessed in order to cross vast distances in a short amount of time. It is dangerous without an experienced guide. Prolonged stay in the realm may cause hearts of mortals to burst apart and their souls to be torn apart. Falling through gaps between the stars that act as parts of the bridges between floating isles in the realm is fatal. It is believed that only the swiftest champions of Azurah can survive in the realm for long.

## Jorrvaskr
[Jorrvaskr](Lore_Jorrvaskr.md) is an ancient mead hall in the [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) city of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md). The longhouse was originally constructed from the hull of the *Jorrvaskr*, one of the longships used by the [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) to cross the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). The ship's band decided to settle at the site of the ancient [Skyforge](Lore_Skyforge.md), and the city of Whiterun subsequently grew around the mead hall.

## Jylkurfyk
[Jylkurfyk](Lore_Jylkurfyk.md) was a coastal city in southern [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). It was known for its large shipyard and was active in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).

## Jyggalag's Realm
[Jyggalag's realm](Lore_Jyggalag%27s_Realm.md) was the realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) ruled over by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md). It was said to be a realm of perfect Order. It later became known as the **[Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md)**, the domain of the Mad God [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md).

The realm was home to the **Great Library of Jyggalag**, a vast collection of knowledge on all things mortal and [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) alike. The knowledge was the result of a formulae of logical deductions that Jyggalag implemented. [Dyus of Mytheria](Lore_Dyus.md) served as the keeper of the Great Library, eventually having his role expanded to the chamberlain of the realm, which involved performing all the mundane tasks that came with running a realm or those that Jyggalag found distasteful. Dyus' title suggests he originated from a place called **Mytheria**, though due to his loss of memory, he is unsure whether Mytheria is even a real place or not.
