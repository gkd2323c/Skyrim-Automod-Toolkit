# Man

**Man**, meaning human, is used to refer to any of the human races of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), such as the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) and [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). While the Bretons are the result of interbreeding between [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) and [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1High-1)</sup> they are most commonly considered men, and not mer.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3All-2)</sup> Some races of men have disappeared or otherwise died out, such as the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md). Though it typically connotes the races of men, "human" is still used by various races in reference to the humanoid shape or ability, or to collectively refer to any member of a civilized race, but mainly when it comes to races who can mate with each other and produce offspring. Man, [Elves](Lore_Elves.md) and [Beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) are classified under the definition of both men, and human by Imperial scholars.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ger H-3)</sup> Some consider the races of Men to be less distinct than those of Mer, with more arbitrary boundaries.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LATD-4)</sup>

## Bretons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/LO-race-Breton.png/200px-LO-race-Breton.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Breton.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Breton.png) Illustration of a typical Breton male [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) (from the [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex_Languages.md) *beratu* meaning "half") are a [hybrid](Lore_Multiraciality.md) race of both human and [elven](Lore_Mer.md) ancestry that primarily inhabit their ancestral homeland of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), and the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_The_Systres.md). Bretondom originated from the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) of High Rock and [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) interbreeding for centuries as far back as the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). This mixed ancestry gives Bretons a unique perspective on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)'s oldest feud: the conflict between men and mer. The Bretons' Nedic ancestors are referred to as the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) (or proto-Bretons) by retroactive nomenclature, while [Clan Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) are primarily credited as being their Aldmer ancestors. Bretons are also distantly related to the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md).<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Reachmen)</sup>

The Direnni left a significant mark on the Bretons: the race's obsession with nobility, status, and lineage can be traced back to their days as vassals of Clan Direnni. Bretons possess an inborn and instinctive bond for the [magical arts](Lore_Magic.md), often attributed to their elven blood, and they are known for their intelligence and quick, perceptive grasp of [spellcraft](Lore_Magic.md), [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md), and [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).

Bretons are also noted for having some natural resistance to destructive and magical energies, a trait associated with their race but not tied to their elven lineage. However, their shorter [human lifespans](Lore_Lifespan.md) place them at a disadvantage compared to their elven counterparts, whose naturally long lives grant them far more time to study, refine their craft, and establish lasting reputations. Even so, their potential has not gone unrecognized. The Assistant [Sapiarch](Lore_Sapiarch.md) of Culinary Arts once remarked that Bretons were the second-best sorcerers after the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), believing they had the potential to achieve more than they have demonstrated so far if given equal opportunity. While it is often difficult for an individual Breton to leave a lasting mark on the greater magical community—since the widespread mystical aptitude among their people makes it harder for any one mage to stand out—Bretons have nonetheless produced mages considered among the greatest in Tamriel, with notable figures such as [Voernet](Lore_Voernet.md), [Druid King Kasorayn](Lore_Kasorayn.md), and [Gyron Vardengroet](Lore_Gyron_Vardengroet.md).

Bretons have also retained a connection to their human [druidic](Lore_Druid.md) forebears, and are believed to be the reason for their "shrewd resourcefulness". Druidic spellcraft (which differs from the arcane) is said to be the first truly Breton creation. Bretons have a natural affinity for environmental manipulation, which druids believe should be utilized to enhance or perfect nature, reshaping it just as their god [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) once did. What it means to be a Breton is considered an age-old quandary by their people. To make sense of it all, some may focus on a specific aspect of their culture, or seek to blend it all together. Some Bretons hold their elven heritage in high esteem, which may influence them to worship [Phynaster](Lore_Phynaster.md), the patron deity and "teacher" of the Direnni. Others attempt to seek out the druidic roots of their Nedic ancestors and reconnect with nature.

High Rock is fractious and divided politically, which is seemingly encouraged by the layout of the land itself. Warfare between [kingdoms](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md) accounts for much of Breton history. Despite this, Bretons across the province share many facets of culture, including [language](Lore_Bretic.md), bardic traditions, and heroic tales. Bretons make up the peasantry, soldiery, magical elite, and ruling families of the feudal kingdoms competing for power within High Rock, but they are found across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), often as merchants or mercenaries. [Knighthood](Lore_Knights.md) is pivotal to the Breton identity; its earliest practitioners served under Clan Direnni, but knights are credited with kickstarting High Rock's modern cultural history through tales of liberating the race from their elven oppressors. These knights of old set the example for those that followed by going on grand quests for adventure and glory. In their free time, Breton youths of all walks of life imitate knights through chivalric pursuits in their thoughts or by their actions. Indeed, the profession encapsulates the traditional chivalric principles of Breton society, and the "quest-obsession" that has become a national identity for High Rock.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Breton.md).

## Giant
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg) A male Giant [Giants](Lore_Giant.md) (sometimes called the **big folk**) are a [race](Lore_Races.md) of enormous humanoids with colossal strength. They are found in the wilderness of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).

They have thick grey skin, long, powerful limbs, large hands and feet, and thick greyish-brown hair decorated with braids and beads. Additionally, most Giants have decorative scars carved into their chests. They clothe themselves in fur pelts adorned with the bones of animals and people, although many prefer a simple loincloth.

Although they tower above most races, their height can vary. Some stand between 11 and 12 feet tall, whereas others can be several times the height of the average person. Female giants, also called **Giantesses**, are less commonly seen than males by non-giants, but they have been seen in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) and [Whiterun Plains](Lore_Tundra_Plain.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Giant social groups appear to vary, with some being part of clans led by a chieftain, some being led by powerful matriarchs, and others being small families composed of two parents raising children together on isolated mountains.

## Horwalli
The [Horwalli](Lore_Horwalli.md) are a tribal race of [Men](Lore_Men.md) that lived in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Scholars theorize they were either sent there as refugees or prisoners, or that they settled along the coastal waterways and adapted to the environment. They resided in the region well throughout the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second Eras](Lore_Second_Era.md), but were considered to no longer inhabit the province by the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Horwalli.md).

## Imperials
[![](https://images.uesp.net/b/b3/LO-race-Imperial.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Imperial.png) Illustration of a typical Imperial male The [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), also known as **Cyrodiils**, **Cyrodilics**, and **Imperial Cyrods**, are the well-educated and well-spoken natives of the civilized and cosmopolitan province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Imperials are also known for the discipline and training of their citizen armies, and their respect for the rule of law. Imperials have proved to be shrewd diplomats and traders, and these traits, along with their remarkable skill and training as infantry, have enabled them to colonize various other nations and create the Empire. Their hegemony has waxed and waned throughout the eras, and most historians refer to three distinct Empires, the ends of which each mark a new epoch in [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) history.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Imperial.md).

**Cyrodiil** is a pretty blanket term for almost every element of the Imperial Province: the people are called **the Cyrodiils**, the land is called **Cyrodiil**, the capital city of their province is called **[Cyrodiil](Lore_Imperial_City.md)**, and the ruler of the land no matter their race is called **[The Cyrodiil](Lore_Emperor.md)**. This is derived from a king-is-the-land tradition that reaches back to when the [Elves](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruled the land.

## Kothringi
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-npc-Chieftain_Suhlak.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chieftain_Suhlak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chieftain_Suhlak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chieftain_Suhlak.jpg) A female Kothringi Cheftain The [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) (or **Lustrous Folk**) are a silver-skinned tribal people indigenous to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) who are believed to be descendants of the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md). Once considered the only humans native to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), they are now known to have shared the continent with many other tribal groups. The race was believed to have been driven to extinction by the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Kothringi.md).

## Nedes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-npc-Cadwell_the_Betrayer_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Cadwell_the_Betrayer_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cadwell_the_Betrayer_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cadwell_the_Betrayer_02.jpg) [Cadwell](Lore_Cadwell.md), a Nedic knight of the Merethic Era *...human settlers occupied nearly every corner of Tamriel before Skyrim was even founded. These so-called "Nedic peoples" include the proto-Cyrodilians, the ancestors of the Bretons, the aboriginals of Hammerfell, and perhaps a now-vanished Human population of Morrowind.*
— *[Frontier, Conquest](Lore_Frontier,_Conquest.md)* The [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) (also known as the **Nedic peoples** or rarely **Nedelings**) were a human race (or a group of races) of unknown extraction, who inhabited much of Tamriel during the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and [First Eras](Lore_First_Era.md). The Duraki society of the Nedes who lived in Hammerfell (then called the "Deathlands") largely followed the [Cult of the Stars](Lore_Cult_of_the_Stars.md) which revolved around the worship and study of the [constellations](Lore_Astronomy.md#Constellations), and beings known as the [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md). Nedic culture experienced a sharp decline over several centuries as they began to assimilate into other cultures, before being completely exterminated by the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) invasion of Hammerfell.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Nede.md).

## Nords
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/LO-race-Nord.png/200px-LO-race-Nord.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Nord.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Nord.png) Illustration of a typical Nord male **Nords**, also known as **Northmen**, **Bron** in the [Language of Dragons](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), or the **Children of the Sky**, are a race of tall and fair-haired humans from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) who are known for their incredible resistance to cold and magical frost. They are fierce, strong and enthusiastic warriors, and many become renowned warriors, soldiers and mercenaries all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Eager to augment their martial skills beyond the traditional methods of Skyrim, they excel in all manner of warfare, and are known as a militant people by their neighbors. Nords were known for a time for their nautical prowess when they migrated from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). Seafaring merchants or smugglers are not unheard of, as was the case with [Tobias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Tobias).

When the descendants of the Atmorans transitioned into calling themselves Nords is blurred. The inhabitants of Skyrim during the Dragon War were variously labeled Nords and Atmorans. The truth may lie somewhere in-between; while the two are considered distinct races, the men living in the transition period between the terms' respective usage have been considered 'proto-Nords'. [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) started a trend when he relinquished all holdings in distant and lost Atmora and united the disparate Nordic people to formally create the Kingdom of Skyrim. What followed was the "first Nords" under Harald wished to distinguish themselves from their Atmoran ancestors, but they still felt pride for their ancient heritage. According to *[Great Harbingers](Lore_Great_Harbingers.md)*, these people didn't consider themselves Nords until sometime after the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) arrived to Tamriel. However, some Redguards were known to have inhabited the continent long prior to their mass migration in [1E 800](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_800).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Nord.md).

## Orma
The [Orma](Lore_Orma.md) are a tribe of [Men](Lore_Men.md) native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), along with the indigenous [Horwalli](Lore_Horwalli.md), [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), and [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md). How they arrived in Black Marsh is unknown, with scholars theorizing they were possibly sent there as refugees or prisoners, or settled along the coastal waterways on their own and adapted to the environment. The Orma lived in Argonia well throughout the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second Eras](Lore_Second_Era.md), but were considered to no longer inhabit the province by the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

Orma are born without eyes, becoming masters of their other senses, including that of touch. Their skulls are peculiar for lacking eye sockets. According to bone scroll canisters, one such skull was used as a door marker for a room containing powerful artifacts in [Scourg Barrow](Lore_Scourg_Barrow.md).

One of the few known members of the race was [Catchica](Lore_Catchica.md), who was with [Reman](Lore_Reman.md)'s army at [Caer Suvio](Lore_Caer_Suvio.md) when [Juilek Cyrodiil](Lore_Juilek.md) was poisoned before the battle at [Bodrum](Lore_Bodrum.md). She later worked at [Mir Corrup](Lore_Mir_Corrup.md), a resort in [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md), to falsely implicate the Emperor's mistress in a plot to poison the Emperor.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Orma.md).

## Reachmen
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png/200px-LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png) A Reachman The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), also known as the **Reachfolk**, **Witchmen of High Rock**, and **Witchfolk of the Reach**, are a race of humans who are native to [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) in southwestern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the neighboring [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) in the east of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), where some Reachfolk families originated from. They are believed to be distantly related to the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md). While Reachmen are distinct, they are also mixed with other races that make up Man, [Mer](Lore_Mer.md), and even [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Similarly to the Bretons, [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) blood runs through their veins, which stems from the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) Hegemony's occupation of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) in the early First Era.

At least some Reachmen are known to be descended from the [Keptu](Lore_Keptu.md) clan of [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md). Although they are thought to share descent, Bretons do not consider themselves kin to the Reachmen, and the Reachmen do not see themselves as Bretons. This sentiment is shared by other races.

Unlike their Breton cousins, who are typically pale and of slight build, Reachfolk are more diverse in appearance, ranging from short and slight framed to taller, broader, and muscular builds, more typical for [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). Some of the Reachfolk have traits closer to those typical to the inhabitants of the [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and a few bear a distinctive red eye color.

## Redguards
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/80/LO-race-Redguard.png/200px-LO-race-Redguard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Redguard.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Redguard.png) Illustration of a typical Redguard male [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) are a [human](Lore_Human.md) race that primarily inhabits the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), but have roots that can be traced back to the continent of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md). Known for their unique martial culture, they are reputed as brilliant battlefield tacticians who are quick to adopt new styles of armor and weaponry. They are the most naturally talented warriors in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Hammerfell was formerly known as the Deathlands, as it hosts an arid climate with stifling, sandy winds, baking rocks that would brand one's feet, and terrible creatures that dwell in the crags and dunes. Regardless, conquering this hostile environment has become synonymous with Redguard culture. Aside from being physically blessed with hardy constitutions, they also have a resistance to poison and fleetness of foot. Redguards are known for their love of traveling, adventuring, and sailing the high seas: they can be seen commonly throughout Tamriel as sailors and mercenaries.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Redguard.md).

## Yespest
The [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md) (or **Yerpest**) are a tribe of [Men](Lore_Men.md) indigenous to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), along with the [Horwalli](Lore_Horwalli.md), [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), and [Orma](Lore_Orma.md). How they arrived in Black Marsh is unknown, but it is possible that they were sent there as refugees or prisoners. Alternatively, they may have settled along the coastal waterways on their own and adapted to the environment. The Yespest were active in the region around [Blackrose](Lore_Blackrose.md). They may have been contemporaries of the [Barsaebics](Lore_Barsaebics.md), and some Imperial scholars erroneously credit them with building the ancient [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) [xanmeers](Lore_Xanmeer.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Yespest.md).
