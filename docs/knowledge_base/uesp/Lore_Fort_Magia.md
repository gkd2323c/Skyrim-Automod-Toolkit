[3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Fort Magia (also known as the **Magia Ruins**) is a ruined military fort located east of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), along the [Red Ring Road](Lore_Red_Ring_Road.md). It lies in the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), on the eastern shore of [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md), right across the water from the [Wellspring Grove](Lore_Wellspring_Grove.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup> The ruins possess a partially-flooded underground complex, including garrison blocks and memorials to fallen legionnaires.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup>

During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the crumbling remains of Fort Magia served no military purpose. Instead, the surface ruins were infested by flesh-hungry undead [skeletons](Lore_Skeleton.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ISD-3)</sup> Around this time, a scholar from [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md) named Thirobar was tricked into venturing to the site by their headmaster in search of a "plethora of ancient lore". Instead, Thirobar's servants and pack animals were torn apart by the undead and eaten. Thirobar climbed to safety atop a ruined tower and lived there for three days before dying during an attempted descent.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ISD-3)</sup>

The fort was seemingly occupied and once again abandoned by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) during the relatively peaceful [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> By the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Fort Magia was a partially-flooded ruin infested by undead and hostile aquatic life.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup> It was around this time that Fort Magia possessed one of the last samples of [Shadowbanish Wine](Lore_Shadowbanish_Wine.md) in Cyrodiil. It was collected by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) and later given to the publican of [Weye](Lore_Weye.md)'s inn, Nerussa, who was also an avid wine collector.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBVintage-5)</sup>
