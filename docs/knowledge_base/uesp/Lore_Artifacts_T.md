## Talisman of Saint Vorys
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/ON-item-Talisman_of_Saint_Vorys.jpg/200px-ON-item-Talisman_of_Saint_Vorys.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Talisman_of_Saint_Vorys.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Talisman_of_Saint_Vorys.jpg) The Talisman of Saint Vorys The [Talisman of Saint Vorys](Lore_Talisman_of_Saint_Vorys.md) was a relic sacred to the followers of [Saint Vorys the Immolant](Lore_Vorys.md). It was an amulet inlaid with gems, with a large [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) serving as the focal point. The central stone is known to the [Baandari](Lore_Baandari.md) as the [Eye of Baan Dar](Lore_Eye_of_Baan_Dar.md), and was taken from [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) by Saint Vorys during his conquest of the region. The talisman was housed at his shrine in [Sailenmora](Lore_Sailenmora.md): it could be closely examined only by house members of a certain rank, but viewing was open to the public.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Necrom_Quest_Items#Talisman_of_Saint_Vorys)

## Telenger's Projector
[Telenger's Projector](Lore_Telenger%27s_Projector.md) (also called **the Projector**, real name unknown) is a powerful magical device created by [Telenger the Artificer](Lore_Telenger_the_Artificer.md) for the purposes of [teleportation](Lore_Teleportation.md). When used in conjunction with the [Hyperagonal Locational Determinator](Lore_Hyperagonal_Locational_Determinator.md), it allows its wielder to teleport to virtually any location they desired, including places protected by powerful magical defenses such as [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) and the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).

**Found in:** [Lore](Lore_Razum-dar%27s_Journal.md)
## Ten Ancestors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg/200px-OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg) One of the Ten Ancestors The [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md) are a set of ten [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [statues](Lore_Statues.md). They consist mostly of [meteoric iron](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md) prongs, with a shard of [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md) in the center. The statues played an important role in Ayleid religion and were displayed in the [Temple of the Ancestors](Lore_Temple_of_the_Ancestors.md), now known as the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md). The temple of Loriasel was also built in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) by the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) in veneration of the Ancestors.

The Ancestors themselves were believed to worship the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), and [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphile.md) Ayleids accordingly followed the *Eleven Edicts of the Ten Ancestors*. The Heretic of [Lindai](Lore_Lindai.md) was known for writing the *Eleven OTHER Edicts of the Ten Ancestors*, a parody that supposed the Ancestors worshipped [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) instead.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Ayleid_Statue)

## Thornblade
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg) Thornblade The [Thornblade](Lore_Thornblade.md) was a family heirloom of the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) [Indarys](Lore_Indarys.md) family. It was a one-handed sword with an ornate hilt. It was imbued with an [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) capable of disintegrating the armor of its victims. Some depictions show it inlaid with a green jewel. Its motif of thorny vines matched that of [Staff of Indarys](Lore_Staff_of_Indarys.md), the other heirloom and featured symbols of the County [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) during the Indarys rule, as well as emblems of the local order of the [Knights of the Thorn](Lore_Knights_of_the_Thorn.md), which depicted a sword wreathed in thorny vines.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Thornblade)

## Threads of the Webspinner

## Thunderfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/BL-item-Thunderfell.jpg/200px-BL-item-Thunderfell.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Thunderfell.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Thunderfell.jpg) Thunderfell [Thunderfell](Lore_Thunderfell.md) is a mythical mace rumored to be imbued with the power of a hundred [storm atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md). It was also known as the **Skeleton's Bane** because of its effect on these creatures. It was imbued with an [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) that strikes those hit with the mace with lightning. It also hindered the stamina and magicka's reserves of those struck.

Mythical Thunderfell inspired artisans and weaponsmiths to create another mace, the **Stormcaller**. It was inferior to Thunderfell and was said to contain the power of forty storm atronachs.

It is unknown whether the [Warrior](Lore_Hero.md#Warrior) wielded Thunderfell and Stormcaller, but as of [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180) both weapons were in use.

**Found in:** - [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Thunderfell)

## Totem of Claw and Fang
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/BM-Img-Totem Of Claw And Fang.jpg/200px-BM-Img-Totem Of Claw And Fang.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-Img-Totem Of Claw And Fang.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-Img-Totem Of Claw And Fang.jpg) The Totem of Claw and Fang The [Totem of Claw and Fang](Lore_Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang.md) is a religious [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) sacred to the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) and the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md), and is a necklace that is decorated with the claws and fang of a great bear. The Reach clans believe it is the symbol of [Hrokkibeg](Lore_Hrokkibeg.md), [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s bear aspect. It is one of the [Symbols of the Five Aspects](Lore_Symbols_of_the_Five_Aspects.md), the other four being the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md) and the three [Totems of Hircine](Lore_Totems_of_Hircine.md). The Skaal associate it with the Spirit Bear, which they summon by invoking powerful nature magic with the totem, which is then tracked down and killed by the Skaal to please the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md) during the Ristaag.

**Found in:** - [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang)

## Totems of Hircine
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/SR-item-Totems_of_Hircine.jpg/200px-SR-item-Totems_of_Hircine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Totems_of_Hircine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Totems_of_Hircine.jpg) From the left to right, the Totem of the Hunt, the Totem of Fear, and the Totem of Brotherhood. The [Totems of Hircine](Lore_Totems_of_Hircine.md) are ancient artifacts of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), predating [mens](Lore_Man.md)' ability to write, speak, and properly think. They are just three of the [Symbols of the Five Aspects](Lore_Symbols_of_the_Five_Aspects.md), the other two being the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md) and the [Totem of Claw and Fang](Lore_Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang.md).

The **Wolf Skull Totem**, also known as the **Totem of Fear**, is an engraved and feathered wolf skull that is said to have been used by shamans in blood ceremonies to create Lycanthropes. It augment's a werewolf's presence, making people cower in fear save for those that have caught a glimpse of Hircine's face. It is said to be worn by Storihbeg the Manbeast, the wolf aspect of Hircine, whom similar to his Wolf Skull totem, is credited among the Reachfolk with introducing the gift of skinshifting to mortals to remind them that they can be predators, not prey.

Uricanbeg is the Stag aspect of Hircine whose hooves beat the Blood Summons that lure prey into his herd, which are then led into the Hunting Grounds where they meet their end. And so Uricanbeg is represented through the **Totem of the Brotherhood**, a mundane drum whose beating does the blood call which allows the summoning of pack members, or the calling of prey to hunt.

Gulibeg's symbol is the **Wand of Bone**, also known as the **Totem of the Hunt**, which was fashioned from a shard from [Lorkh](Lore_Lorkh.md)'s ribs, and has the ability to confound any mortal. It was used as a medicinal [wand](Lore_Wands.md) in an ancient brotherhood, and is believed to heighten a lycanthrope's sight and smell, making it hard for a prey to flee.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Quest_Items.md#Werewolf_Totem)

## Totem of Tiber Septim
[![](https://images.uesp.net/f/fa/DF-item-Totem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-item-Totem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-item-Totem.png) The Totem of Tiber Septim The [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md) is an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that allows its bearer to control [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), the ancient [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) construct. It was created at the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) by Imperial Battlemage [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md), to allow Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to use the golem in his conquest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Only an individual of Septim lineage or special supernatural affinity can safely wield the Totem; it will reject an unworthy owner, and has been known to telepathically do so to such individuals.

The Totem was lost along with Numidium shortly after the conquest of Tamriel, when the automaton was destroyed by the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md). It was known to have resurfaced in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region some time around [3E 401](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_401). After changing hands several times over the next few years, it was recovered by a [mysterious adventurer](Lore_The_Agent.md) sent by Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).

In [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), the Totem was simultaneously given to (and used by) seven factions around the Iliac Bay. This caused the [phenomenon](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) now known as the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), during the events of which the Totem was apparently lost again.

**Found in:** - [*Daggerfall*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Totem,_Totem,_Who_Gets_the_Totem%3F)

## Toxicruciform
The [Toxicruciform](Lore_Toxicruciform.md) is a curio associated with [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), found in the form of a [cross](Lore_Cross.md). Judging by its name, it appears to have toxic capabilities in some regard.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), it was last known to be in the possession of Gazmod the Collector, a [watcher](Lore_Watcher.md) residing in [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), alongside the [Golden Scab](Lore_Golden_Scab.md) and [Bilious Censer](Lore_Bilious_Censer.md). Eorda, a worshipper of Peryite, believed that she could gain Peryite's favor by somehow recovering these artifacts for her lord.

**Found in:** - [Lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Eorda)

## Tracts Perilous
[Tracts Perilous](Lore_Tracts_Perilous.md) are a class of book [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) that harbor grave dangers for those brave enough to peruse their pages. Unlike many dangerous tomes prized for their potent contents, Tracts Perilous stand out for their capacity to inflict harm directly upon their readers. These perilous volumes manifest in diverse forms, each posing unique threats to those who dare to unravel their secrets. Some Tracts Perilous contain knowledge and concepts that surpass the limits of mortal comprehension. Readers may find themselves forced to sacrifice their sanity in order to grasp the intricacies of these forbidden texts. Other books of this nature may hold implicit bargains within their pages, enticing readers into pacts with unknown and potentially malevolent forces. In certain cases, the books themselves ensnare readers in dark enchantments, compelling them to carry out tasks or fulfill purposes they would never otherwise desire.

**Found in:** [Lore](Lore_On_Tracts_Perilous.md)
## Trueflame
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fa/TR-weapon-Trueflame.jpg/200px-TR-weapon-Trueflame.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-weapon-Trueflame.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-weapon-Trueflame.jpg) Trueflame [Trueflame](Lore_Trueflame.md), or **the Blade of [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md)**, is a one-handed [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemer.md) sword from the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Trueflame and its twin blade, [Hopesfire](Lore_Hopesfire.md), represented the pinnacle of Dwemer craftsmanship. The twin blades possessed unearthly fire enchantments. They were presented as wedding gifts to Lord Indoril Nerevar and [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) by the Dwemer King [Dumac](Lore_Dumac.md). Trueflame was shattered during the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), and the pieces were lost, though it was later restored to its full glory by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md). It possesses the ability to kill a god if wielded by one of noble intent.

**Found in:** - [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Trueflame), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Arms_of_Infernace), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Trueflame.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Ghosts_of_the_Tribunal.md)</sup>

## Tsun's Armor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/SR-npc-Tsun.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Tsun.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Tsun.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Tsun.jpg) Tsun's Armor [Tsun's Armor](Lore_Tsun%27s_Armor.md) is an artifact associated with [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md). His attire consists of armored boots, a torc, reinforced bracers, and a wide belt with a large engraved metal buckle worn over a layered kilt with hip-protecting metal plates. It kept his chest bare and exposed.

It was donned by Tsun as he fought those seeking to cross the Whalebone Bridge in [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), though it could also be found beyond [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) and worn by mortals. When worn, it restored their magicka and mended their wounds, though it also exposed them to danger.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Tsun%27s_Armor.md), [Skyrim - The Adventure Game](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Legendary_Encounters#Rewards)

## Tsun's Battle Axe
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg/200px-SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.jpg) Tsun's Battle Axe [Tsun's Battle Axe](Lore_Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.md), (also written as **Tsun's Battleaxe**) is a weapon artifact associated with [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md).

It was wielded by Tsun as he fought those seeking to cross the Whalebone Bridge in [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), though it could also be found beyond [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) and used by mortals. When wielded, it made them more likely to successfully make use of their skills.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Tsun%27s_Battle_Axe.md), [Skyrim - The Adventure Game](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Legendary_Encounters#Rewards)
