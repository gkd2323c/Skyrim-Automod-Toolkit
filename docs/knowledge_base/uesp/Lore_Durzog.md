— *[Mounts of Tamriel: Volume 7, Durzogs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mounts_of_Tamriel__Volume_7,_Durzogs)* Durzogs, sometimes called **sludgepuppies**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRFedris-1)</sup> are carnivorous animals not dissimilar to [reptilian](Lore_Reptile.md) dogs. They have scaly skin, six eyes, spines and large teeth made for tearing. They are often domesticated by the various [Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) tribes for hunting, although some believe their intelligence can rival that of their handlers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRFedris-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOEDH-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Kyne C-3)</sup>

Durzogs have a heightened sense of smell, which causes them to seek harpy viscera.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Orzorga-4)</sup> When durzogs migrate, the seeds of [durzog lichen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durzog_Lichen) get stuck between their toes and germinate in areas that they once inhabited.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Orzorga-4)</sup> They are intelligent creatures and formidable foes, though they are not very durable in battle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRFedris-1)</sup> Durzogs have a reputation for being ornery and vicious.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Some wild durzogs can spread [Rotbone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rotbone).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

## Variants
**Warzogs** are larger durzogs, specially bred by Goblins for use as war mounts.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Snowsnarl durzogs are a pale striped-skinned, feathery durzog species<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONSD-8)</sup> that is know to dwell in cold climates, such as in [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NJW-9)</sup> The [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) used magic to alter durzogs of this breed in-utero, enabling them to grow suitably large for use as mounts. These durzogs are evidently loyal, and mistakes made while attempting to bond with them can be fatal: in the process of transferring ownership of their durzogs to new riders, Icereach mount handlers would make the new owner sleep with the saddle for several nights before attempting to ride the animal.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONFDM-10)</sup>

Emberthroat durzogs are quite smart (perhaps even moreso than their goblin handlers), as the Netch Gouger Goblins of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) bred them for their intelligence.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ED-11)</sup>

Graniteclaw durzogs are a species native to the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago. They are coastal hunters, using their blue and orange stripes to blend in with the reefs. Their necks are feathered to keep water from dripping into their eyes.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Topal durzogs are similar in appearance to graniteclaws, but smaller in size. Originally bred by the [Coral-Splitter](Lore_Goblin.md#Coral-Splitter_Tribe) Goblin tribe to hunt for fresh fish, this species had spread to the shores of [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md) by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

## Uses
[Durzog meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durzog_Meat) is valued for its alchemical properties. Durzog ivory has a few practical uses,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> and durzog hide has seen use in fashion products.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> Goblins will occasionally use durzog hide in leather armor,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> and frozen durzog liver is used in [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) cuisine.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Orzorga-4)</sup>

Durzogs can make loyal pets if a handler establishes firm boundaries with them.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Due to their vicious reputation, Goblins have been known to train durzogs for war.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Besides Goblins, a few other races occasionally make use of tamed durzogs. Including Orcs,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONArushna-20)</sup> Reachmen,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONFDM-10)</sup> and Argonians, but Goblins are by far the most prolific handlers.
