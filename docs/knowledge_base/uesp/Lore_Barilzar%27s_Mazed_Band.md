# Barilzar's Mazed Band


## History
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), after helping put a stop to the machinations of [Barbas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barbas) during the Daedric War, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) awarded Barilzar with gemstones blessed with his divine magic, which the mage planned to use to further his research into teleportation.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOMWB-5)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the cave-in blocking his crypt was excavated at the orders of [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia), linking it to the temple sewers beneath the city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) Almalexia sent the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) to retrieve the ring from the crypt. Barilzar defended the Band with his life, determined that the artifact should never be used again, but he was slain and the ring was brought to Almalexia.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBMB-6)</sup>

Although she had claimed that possession of the ring would help her to better care for her people, Almalexia had obtained the ring for a malicious purpose. Driven to madness by the loss of her divinity, she planned to murder her fellow [Tribunes](Lore_Tribunal.md), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), as well as the Nerevarine. Her first target was Sotha Sil, who had withdrawn from the world and now dwelled within his [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). She used the Mazed Band to journey to the Clockwork City and kill Sotha Sil. She then used the ring to teleport [fabricants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fabricants) from the Clockwork City to Mournhold. These semi-organic robots were creations of Sotha Sil, and they caused havoc in the streets of Mournhold. Almalexia then claimed that the reclusive Sotha Sil had been driven mad and was launching an attack on the city. Using this ruse, she tricked the Nerevarine into going to the Clockwork City to kill him. There, Almalexia confronted the Nerevarine, but she was defeated in combat. The Nerevarine took the Band and used it return to Mournhold.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRMG-2)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), [Mecinar](Lore_Mecinar.md), a former Clockwork Apostle who was banished from the Clockwork City for his perverse research into creating the perfect lifeform,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LRTCCD-7)</sup> employed treasure hunters from across Tamriel to retrieve Barilzar's Mazed Band in order to return to the City to see his vision through. In an effort to claim the riches that may lie in the city for themselves,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LRTCCOFAF-8)</sup> the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) along with their adventuring party tracked the Band to [Forelhost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forelhost) in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md). The heroes however didn't realize that treasure hunters in the employ of Mecinar followed them and easily stole the band from them after they were weakened. The Forgotten Hero's friend, an Argonian named Swims-at-Night, would not give up his prize, and reached out and grabbed the hunters while they were using the ring and was teleported into the city along with them.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LRTCCTDK-9)</sup> The Forgotten Hero, determined to rescue their friend, sought the help of [Divayth Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divayth_Fyr)'s daughter, [Alfe Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alfe_Fyr), who teleported them into the Clockwork City. The Hero saved their friend and defeated Mecinar. It is unknown what happened to the Band subsequently. <sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Rt CC-10)</sup>
