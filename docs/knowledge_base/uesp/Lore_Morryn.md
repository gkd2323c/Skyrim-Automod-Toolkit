*I'll let you in on a little secret from Whiterun. If Nords ever conducted negotiations while completely sober, nothing would get done.*
—Jarl Morryn<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-JM-1)</sup> **Jarl** Morryn was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) ruler of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md), active in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-JM-1)</sup> She was related to the royal line of the [Western Skryim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) and was deemed one of the greatest Nord diplomats.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTB-2)</sup>

## History
[![SR-banner-Whiterun.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/SR-banner-Whiterun.png/140px-SR-banner-Whiterun.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-banner-Whiterun.png) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/ON-place-Jarl_Morryn%27s_Cabin.jpg/200px-ON-place-Jarl_Morryn%27s_Cabin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jarl_Morryn%27s_Cabin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jarl_Morryn%27s_Cabin.jpg) Jarl Morryn's Cabin Little is known of Morryn's early life. Circa [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), she ascended to the position of Jarl during a time of uncertainty and feuding, after the previous Jarl was poisoned with a [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) -inducing concoction by the court alchemist Arkasis and later disappeared. Morryn subsequently declared Arkasis an enemy of the highest order.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MTCA-3)</sup>

Jarl Morryn was regarded as one of the finest Nord diplomats of her time, with few in Skyrim to rival her skill as a mediator. She was also distantly related to the rulers of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) and held lands there, including her family's hunting lodge in northern [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTB-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-JMC-4)</sup> She was known to host traditional feasts at her hunting lodge,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TS-5)</sup> which often served as the setting for her famed political gatherings, where diplomacy and revelry went hand in hand.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RIE-6)</sup> She studied guidebooks on diplomacy and negotiation techniques, including Necessities for Successful Negotiations by Oberelle Petit.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-NFSN-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-NFSN2-8)</sup>

Aside from her distant kinship to the ruling family of Western Skyrim,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTB-2)</sup> little is known about Morryn's relatives, though she was known to have a daughter. Jarl's descendant was described as pale as snow and fond of sunflowers. She once nearly vanished in a blizzard and was briefly involved in an affair with a member of the Garland Ring.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Wisteria-10)</sup> During her tenure as Jarl, earning the title of Thane of Whiterun was considered comparatively easier than in other holds.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HOE-11)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), she was asked by Skald-King [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) to organize talks with the [thanes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thanes) of Western Skyrim in hopes of mending the rift between the [East](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) and West.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-JM-1)</sup> As the court of Solitude had not received an envoy from Whiterun in many years, it was decided to pursue reconciliation through direct overtures to the western thanes instead. Working alongside Rigurt of the Nord Cultural Exchange and his [aide](Lore_The_Vestige.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTB-2)</sup> she arranged a meeting with Thane Hraldi and Thane Svyne to discuss a potential peace treaty with Windhelm. Though she privately doubted the effort would succeed,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-JM-1)</sup> and her attempt to mediate between two feuding thanes was seen as ambitious,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RIE-6)</sup> both ultimately agreed to attend,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TS-5)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TH-12)</sup> in part due to the intervention of Rigurt's assistant.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RTB-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CC-13)</sup> At the same time, an archery contest was planned in the area, but it was canceled due to a lack of interest. All concerns were to be directed to Jarl Morryn.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-AC-14)</sup>

Though no peace treaty was concluded that day, the meeting was regarded as a step in the right direction. While only two western thanes had attended, their influence carried weight. As Hraldi observed, they had the ears of other thanes, who in turn had the ears of jarls, and ultimately the king [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md). He believed that if peace conditions were wise, word would reach the throne.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TH-12)</sup> Svyne, was more skeptical, noting that Western and Eastern Skyrim had parted on bitter terms and that such divisions were not easily mended. Still, he believed that while the East had prospered and the West had fared worse, Jarl Morryn and Rigurt might yet show them how much better things could be.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TS-5)</sup> Ultimately, however, peace did become a reality, though Morryn's role in its final shaping was unclear, as the treaty between the kingdoms was signed in the capital of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) between Jarl [Svana](Lore_Svana.md), successor to Svargrim, King Jorunn, and Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SC-15)</sup>
