# Thanes

*The Jarl has recognized you as a person of great importance in the hold. A hero. The title of Thane is an honor, a gift for your service.*
— [Lydia](Lore_Lydia.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lydia-1)</sup> Thanes are highly esteemed figures in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md): heroes and champions of their holds,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lydia-1)</sup> dynastic landowners with personal housecarls at their command,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCES-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TH-3)</sup> often serving directly under Jarls.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BTG-4)</sup> The title may be earned either through lineage or by notable deeds.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCES-2)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-crown_store-Falkreath_Thane.jpeg/200px-ON-crown_store-Falkreath_Thane.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Falkreath_Thane.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Falkreath_Thane.jpeg) Falkreath Thane's attire [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/ON-item-armor-Thane_of_Falkreath_%28male%29.jpg/200px-ON-item-armor-Thane_of_Falkreath_%28male%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-armor-Thane_of_Falkreath_(male).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-armor-Thane_of_Falkreath_(male).jpg) Thane of Falkreath's armor The title of Thane is an honor bestowed by a Jarl in recognition of one’s service, marking the individual as a person of great importance in the hold, a hero.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lydia-1)</sup> Naming someone Thane was the highest honor a Jarl could bestow upon their subjects. They were also often assigned a personal housecarl, and a weapon from the Jarl's armory was awarded to them as a badge of office. Certain items were reserved for Jarls, such as honorary keys to cities, signifying their roles as guardians of the communities.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WHJL-5)</sup> A certain kind of quills made from [snow-owl](Lore_Owl.md) feathers was reserved for the exclusive use of the Thanes of Skyrim.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TOFQ-6)</sup> Quills made from [falcon](Lore_Falcon.md) feathers were also used by the Thanes and Jarls of the Rift, though their use was not exclusive to those ranks.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TFFQ-7)</sup> In addition to weapons, certain armors and formal attire were linked to Thanes of various holds, particularly those of Falkreath.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FT-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TOF-9)</sup>

Although the title of Thane can be granted based on merit,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lydia-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MTCES-2)</sup> the families of Thanes often amassed considerable power of their own. Thanes had rule over holdings,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TBT-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HLG-11)</sup> vassals,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Thane V-12)</sup> and were given tithes not unlike Jarls.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DOM-13)</sup> The lands governed by Thanes were known as thanedoms, and both the office and the title of Thane were hereditary.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Hallfrida-14)</sup> They sometimes had their own thrones and throne rooms, and some were known to display their kills as mounted trophies, showcasing their power and triumph.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TGOS-15)</sup> Thanes had authority to banish people from their lands,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MJ-16)</sup> and execute them.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LOHF-17)</sup>

A notable example of an infulential Thane family was the Jurgald line of Lower Yorgrim, who governed their lands for generations.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EAWHB-18)</sup> Likewise, the Stormcrag family of Eastmarch has produced many Thanes of distinction throughout its history.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TSFC-19)</sup> Hoag Merkiller appointed the head of the Icehammers family as his personal Thane.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-IV-20)</sup>

Thanes were connected to a wide range of places, from the expansive holds governed by the Jarls they served,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WHJL-5)</sup> to more localized centers of their own authority,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TLOTI-21)</sup> and even notable landmarks such as Snow-Throat.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Thanes were expected to be strong and noble,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-FBT-23)</sup> with some being admired even by the people beyond Skyrim.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-VJ-24)</sup> They held the representative position of their hold and were regarded as more important within the hold than the court wizards.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MF-25)</sup>

Though both the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md),<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-500C-26)</sup> and [Haefriding](Lore_Haefriding.md) once had Thanes in the earliest stages of their cultures,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TFOSS-27)</sup> they changed that tradition.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Orthald-28)</sup> The Skaal are led by Chiefs,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Bloodmoon-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-FSV-30)</sup> while the Haefriding are guided by a council of Speakers.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BTB-31)</sup>

## Ranks and Roles of Thanes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/LG-cardart-Rift_Thane.png/200px-LG-cardart-Rift_Thane.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Rift_Thane.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Rift_Thane.png) A Rift Thane (Legends) *For the Rift!*
—A battlecry of a Rift Thane<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-RT-32)</sup> There were various types of Thanes, typically named for their function or rank. At times, terms derived from word Thane were used more informally or applied on an individual basis. A full list follows:

- **High Thane** – Little is known about High Thanes, but they held certain degree of authority. Captains and Centurions of the Nordic structures of the Ebonheart Pact answered to them.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-CVW-33)</sup>

- **Shield-thane** (or **shieldthane** and **shield thane**) – The title of Shield-thane was used by both Tsun and Stuhn, who were known as shield-thanes of Shor,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Tsun-34)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-VOF-35)</sup> though some Nordic sources also refer to [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) as [Alduin's](Lore_Alduin.md) shield thane.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-SSOS-36)</sup> Despite its frequent use to describe deities, the title was also applied to mortal figures in everyday Nordic society.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-SOM-37)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-7FOTA-38)</sup> Associated with warriors and defenders.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Merenfire-39)</sup> The legendary King [Hrol](Lore_Hrol.md) from beyond [Twil](Lore_Twil.md) was also known to employ shieldthanes.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Remanada-40)</sup>

- **Axethane** (or **Dov-rha thane**) – Term used to describe members of the [Dov-rha Axethanes](Lore_Dov-rha_Axethanes.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DRS-41)</sup>

- **Sword-thane** (or **swordthane**) – Associated with warriors and commanders of soldiers,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-LT-42)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SOLS-43)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Arvnir-44)</sup> in service of a Jarl.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-MTCLT-45)</sup> It was a highly respected role among the Nords.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Sworthane S-46)</sup> Some Swordthanes also serve as the King's Guard,<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-SJ-47)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-SU-48)</sup> and under the command of other Thanes.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TO-49)</sup> The title of Swordthane is typically earned, and people may continue to refer to someone by it even if they are later disgraced or dismissed by the crown, since they once rightfully earned it.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-SJ-47)</sup>

- **Lawthane** – Presumably associated with legal or administrative duties. The only known holder of the title was [Mera Stormcloak](Lore_Mera_Stormcloak.md), who served as Lawthane of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md),<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE-50)</sup> though she was often referred to simply as its Thane.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TMS-51)</sup>

- **Tailor-Thane** – Presumably associated with duties of esteemed clothiers. The only known holder of the title was [Jork](Lore_Jork.md), who served as Tailor-Thane of Windhelm.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-SSJ-52)</sup>

- **Thoom-Thane** – Presumably associated with the usage of [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md). The only known holder of the title was [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-500C-26)</sup>

- **Boat-thane** – Associated with seafaring. By tradition, at the time of the Return, the Boat-Thanes were permitted to race for the vanguard of their High King. Certain texts speak of many such Boat-Thanes, though the account itself is inherently unreliable and anachronistic. Among those named are Morgan the Red, Korst Wind-Eye, Lav Larich, Ysmaalithax, and numerous others, some of whom could not possibly have been present at the Return.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-500C-26)</sup>

### Miscellaneous
- **Clawthane** – A term used to describe [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) spirits who guard the Whalebone Bridge alongside Tsun.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-SCW-53)</sup>

- **Orcthane** – A title claimed by [Fildgor](Lore_Fildgor.md), who gathered a formidable army of Orcs. It was likely not a typical or formal title, but rather an informal or self-styled appellation.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ET-54)</sup>

- **Snowthane** – Likely a fictional title, coined by [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE-50)</sup>

Nordic thanes should not be confused with the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) leaders known as the Treethanes.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-TWEOV-55)</sup>
