## Redguard Afterlife
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/ON-place-Hall_of_Heroes_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hall_of_Heroes_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hall_of_Heroes_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hall_of_Heroes_02.jpg) Hall of Heroes' portal to the Far Shores [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), before the creation of the world, was the god of Nobody Really Cares. When [Tall Papa](Lore_Tall_Papa.md) undertook the creation of the Walkabout, Tu'whacca found a purpose; he became the caretaker of the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md), and continues to help [Redguards](Lore_Redguards.md) find their way into the afterlife. His cult is sometimes associated with Arkay in the more cosmopolitan regions of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and he is often worshipped in that name by some Forebears. However, [Crown](Lore_Crown.md) and [Forebear](Lore_Forebear.md) Redguards' beliefs about the afterlife have been and are the same since their arrivals from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOFCR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFTF-2)</sup> They all believe Tu'whacca is the god who escorts their souls to the afterlife of the Far Shores and, though a soul may have enemies who may try to keep it from reaching the Far Shores, Tu'whacca, as is sly and clever, will always outwit them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOTuwhacca-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> In conclusion, Tu'whacca performs the same functions for the Redguards that Arkay and Xarxes do for Tamrielic men and mer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAX-5)</sup>

The actual portal to the Far Shores is located deep in the [Hall of Heroes](Lore_Hall_of_Heroes.md), the ancient resting place of the greatest Redguard warriors. Worthy souls must journey to the Chamber of Passage, guarded by the Keeper of the Hall, who refuses entry to the living and the unworthy.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOWalk-6)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/ON-place-The_Far_Shores_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Far_Shores_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Far_Shores_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Far_Shores_02.jpg) The Far Shores In Yokudan and Redguard religion, [Sep](Lore_Sep.md), the Snake, their version of Lorkhan was born when Tall Papa created someone to help him regulate the spirit trade. Sep, though, was driven crazy by the hunger of [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), and he convinced some of the gods to help him make an easier alternative to the Walkabout, that is Nirn, and the spirits who followed Sep become trapped there, to live out their lives as mortals. Sep is punished by Tall Papa for his transgressions, but his hunger lives on as a void in the stars, a "non-space" that tries to upset mortal entry into the Far Shores.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOFCR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFTF-2)</sup> Unlike other humans, whose myths portray Mundus as a blessing, the Redguards believe that it was all part of a cruel trick by Ruptga's servant, Sep, whose hunger had driven him into a malevolent madness. As a result of his trickery, their ancestors were left stranded on a dying ball, too far away to "jump into" the Far Shores. [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md) refused to help those who had been stranded by Sep, declaring that they had to either live on through children or else find a new way to reach the Far Shores and thereby "strive back to godhood".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TM-7)</sup>

## Redguard Funerary Customs
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg/200px-ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Motalion_Necropolis.jpg) A Redguard necropolis [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/ON-place-Tears_of_the_Dishonored.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tears_of_the_Dishonored.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tears_of_the_Dishonored.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tears_of_the_Dishonored.jpg) Redguard desecrated burial grounds [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-interior-Lost_City_of_the_Na-Totambu_02.png/200px-ON-interior-Lost_City_of_the_Na-Totambu_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Lost_City_of_the_Na-Totambu_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Lost_City_of_the_Na-Totambu_02.png) Ancient Yokudan tombs in the lost city of Na Totambu Redguards inherited tremendous respect for their deceased ancestors, so it is easy to find that their houses for the dead can be even larger and more elaborate than their homes for the living. They build extensive necropolis both near and far from populated places, for commoners, nobility, heroes, and kings alike.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOMotalion-8)</sup> Although, sometimes, noble families or wealthy kings will build their own tombs in secluded places and protect them with traps, and magical and holy seals.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOShuttered-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOFlawless-10)</sup> Redguards have buried and mummified their dead since their arrival to Tamriel from Yokuda.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOMummy-11)</sup> Great leaders were said to be preserved in a magical stasis field, so they could be looked upon ever after in their prime, although it's unclear how prevalent this tradition was.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Nidal-12)</sup>

The traditions of honored dead come from their early days in the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md). As human remains slowly decay here, Redguards came to believe that their ancestor's corpse is sacred and should not be harmed. Much of the Alik'r's culture today evolved around this ideal.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONFahara.27jad-13)</sup> Honorless criminals are left to rot in desecrated grounds, commonly out in the desert, far from towns. It is possible to retrieve the remains and consecrate them if they were later believed innocent.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOHonoring-14)</sup>

Criminals are also buried on islands to prevent their evil spirits from disturbing the dead. This is a tradition that stems from Yokuda. A negative side effect of this is that these sites attract the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md), who like to build their laboratories and take advantage of the dead. Redguards constantly repel them, but reports of them near places like [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md) and [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) have surfaced deep into the Third Era.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/ON-interior-Ruins_of_Kardala.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Ruins_of_Kardala.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Ruins_of_Kardala.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Ruins_of_Kardala.jpg) A Redguard mummy Throne Keepers are Tu'whacca priests who are intended with protecting the many necropolises which dot Hammerfell. Their main occupation is tending to the embalmed bodies which are entombed and to prevent the others to rise.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFarvad-16)</sup> In case their necropolis turned desecrated, they can call upon Tu'whacca's Breath, a sacred ritual which put the dead back to rest.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOBreath-17)</sup> They are capable too of cleansing necromantic taint on the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md) which keeps the dead of the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md) from rising.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOAmputating-18)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg/200px-ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Morwha%27s_Bounty.jpg) An Ash'abah camp [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/ON-place-Prince_Hew%27s_Shuttered_Tomb.jpg/200px-ON-place-Prince_Hew%27s_Shuttered_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Prince_Hew%27s_Shuttered_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Prince_Hew%27s_Shuttered_Tomb.jpg) A Yokudan royal tomb According to Redguard tradition, striking the dead, called [Ra-Netu](Lore_Ra-Netu.md) locally, carries with it a terrible curse and is considered an act of dishonor. Because of this, fighting back against necromancy proved difficult.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFarvad-16)</sup> Worship of Tu'whacca is very strong, the dead are almost always subject to his protections, identical to Arkay's Law. There are exceptions after large battles or in remote areas where death occurs far from meddlesome priests.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CP-19)</sup> To a devout Redguard, almost nothing is more repugnant than necromancy.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOBahraha-20)</sup> Due to these beliefs, Redguard reanimated necromancers, such as [liches](Lore_Lich.md), will be imprisoned under magical seals that kept them trapped, instead of being destroyed.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOSecret-21)</sup>

Furthermore, in the Alik'r, three Ansei heroes of ancient, Majah, Radan and Halelah sacrificed themselves to protect the region from the [risen dead](Lore_Zombie.md). As part of a covenant with [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), they pledged their souls and each Ansei channeled their spirit into the blade they wielded in life.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFarvad-16)</sup> These swords became known as the Ansei Wards, and as long as the dead are consecrated in the name of Tu'whacca, they could not be raised by even the most powerful necromancy.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESORadan-22)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFarvad-16)</sup> The Wards were locked in a fortified subterranean storehouse below [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md), known as the Impervious Vault, and sealed with a magical barrier.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFarvad-16)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOAzah-23)</sup> The spirit of each Ansei remained tied to their Ward, meaning it would be with it at all times.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESORadan-22)</sup> These treasured relics have played an important role throughout the history of the Alik'r.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOShiri-24)</sup>

However, necromancers have achieved to practice their art in a variety of manners, so the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) were created as a separated tribal group of Redguards who are pariahs from the rest of society due to their delegated role: fighting the undead. They will fight back, at a cost: they are hated by other Redguards, and exiled to the desert. When the dead rise, the Ash'abah are called upon to help, but otherwise they are shunned.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOInitiation-25)</sup>
