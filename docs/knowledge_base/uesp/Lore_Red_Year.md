# Red Year


[4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AHORR1-1)</sup> came to be known as the Red Year due to the fulfillment of the [Nerevarine Prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) and the disappearance of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). With the warrior-poet Tribune gone, there was nothing holding [Baar Dau](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baar_Dau) in place over [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City) in the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) district of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup> An [Ingenium](Lore_Ingenium.md) powered by souls was created to hold the moon in place, used to create a [rift](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Portals) to the [Fields of Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fields_of_Regret) through which souls were funneled to [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) and the energies of the [realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) were leeched to hold the moon in place. The mage Sul, who was one of the designers of Ingenium, theorized that the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) did not care for the stolen energies as he valued the souls he received in exchange more, and that the other architect of the project, [Vuhon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City_Vuhon), might have even made a formal pact with Vile for him to allow the energies to be taken.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IC-3)</sup> This worked until the first few years of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), when a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named [Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City_Sul) destroyed the Ingenium in an attempt to save his lover.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup> Finally freed of its stasis, the moon hit the city with the full strength of its original descent, triggering a series of natural disasters, notably the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) on the 3rd of Sun's Dawn,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LTC-4)</sup> which devastated Vvardenfell and the province as a whole.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OTGC-5)</sup>

Vivec City was annihilated; in its place nothing remained but scorched earth and the boiling waters now known as [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup> [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md), [Balmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balmora), [Gnisis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnisis), and [Sadrith Mora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sadrith_Mora) also suffered widespread destruction.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRY-6)</sup> Even [Fort Frostmoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Frostmoth) in southeastern [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) was wiped away.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AHORR1-1)</sup> Earthquakes set off by the eruption were felt as far away as the southern mainland city of [Tear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tear), which partially flooded and sank in the swampland it was built on, and roads farther to the south in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) were destroyed.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRY-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RT3-7)</sup> The [Grazelands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grazelands) to the northeast of Red Mountain were known to have survived the eruption.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-8)</sup> In the immediate aftermath, many "outlander" Dunmer returned home to help their people, despite the fact that it was almost impossible to breathe the air.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRY-6)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Brelyna-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Ambarys_Rendar-10)</sup> Beginning one month after the eruption, relief efforts were organized by [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) out of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), which had managed to avoid any damage from the eruption or earthquakes, and at least one Vvardenfell settlement, Balmora, was rebuilt.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRY-6)</sup> With Morrowind in ongoing disarray, however, the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of Black Marsh seized the opportunity to take vengeance on the Dunmer, who had been enslaving them for thousands of years, and Argonian armies pillaged southern Morrowind in what became known as the [Accession War](Lore_Accession_War.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LTJ-11)</sup>

For years afterward, Dunmer fled across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) seeking refuge, with many relocating to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) northwest of Vvardenfell.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DOM-12)</sup> Although the Argonians eventually returned to Black Marsh and the Dunmer began the process of rebuilding Mournhold,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TMFN-13)</sup> Morrowind's capital was permanently moved to the Redoran stronghold of [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md) far to the north.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DBA-14)</sup>
