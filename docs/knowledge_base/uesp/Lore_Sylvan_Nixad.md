*That's when we saw them. Tiny creatures, no taller than the lip of this one's boot, with buzzing wings. As they chased one of the workers from his bedroll, he shrieked "nixad!" before plummeting over the lip and breaking his leg. The little beasts giggled and taunted him as he moaned in pain.*
— *[Bitter Travels Among the Nixad](Lore_Bitter_Travels_Among_the_Nixad.md)* by [Zabia-ko](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Zabia-ko)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BTATN-1)</sup> [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-audio-Nixad-Laugh_01.ogg) The mischievous laughter of a Nixad Nixads are mischievous flying [fay](Lore_Faerie.md) creatures that resemble humanoid [insects](Lore_Insect.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sylvan-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FAF-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bolga-4)</sup> and inhabit various forests and glades throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Orchid Nixad-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Emerald-6)</sup> Nixads have an affinity for the [elements](Lore_Elements.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FAF-3)</sup> and have a penchant for stealing valuables and sugary sweets like [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BTATN-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Emerald-6)</sup> While typically small, some Nixads are larger than a [man](Lore_Man.md) or [elf](Lore_Elf.md) in size.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Giant-7)</sup> It is sometimes speculated that Nixads are only able to fly thanks to their inherently [magical](Lore_Magic.md) nature.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Turq-8)</sup> They are often compared to other fay such as [pixies](Lore_Pixie.md) and [sprites](Lore_Sprite.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Fire-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Magma Queen-10)</sup>

Nixads have shown some intelligence; they speak in garbled voices to mock others, and have been seen drawing things such as faces.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mari-12)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BTATN-1)</sup> Many Nixads have a strong taste for sugars and spices,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BTATN-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sylvan-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mari-12)</sup> and some are said to "live on a diet of treacle and poetry".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Turq-8)</sup> They are known to dislike the bitter scent of [coffee](Lore_Coffee.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BTATN-1)</sup> While often gregarious and mostly harmless,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Artayum-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup> Nixads are commonly thought of as nuisances,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mari-12)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Naryu-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Scream-15)</sup> and have occasionally been known to be cruel or violent tricksters.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BTATN-1)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ANMMDI-16)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Giant-7)</sup> Nixad infestations are an occasional problem on [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mari-12)</sup> Nixads will often sing annoyingly and leave droppings everywhere.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mari-12)</sup> Some Nixads are known to be helpful,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Fire-9)</sup> and others have served as companions to people.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Orchid Nixad-5)</sup>

In addition to Cyrodiil,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AGCB-18)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sylvan-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup> Nixads can be found in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Princess-19)</sup> [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Nectar-20)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup> the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Artayum-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup> the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres.md), [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup> and [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Scream-15)</sup> In addition to being found on Tamriel, Nixads can also be found in the planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), such as in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), and the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FAF-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup> The range of Nixads even extends to the [moons](Lore_Moons.md), as they can even be found on the [Plane of Jode](Lore_Jode.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup> Despite being found in Oblivion, Nixads are not [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FAF-3)</sup>

## Variants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-creature-Faram-Xaran.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Faram-Xaran.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Faram-Xaran.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Faram-Xaran.jpg) A giant Brimstone Nixad Nixads come in various colors and varieties. Sylvan Nixads are a brown variety most often encountered in the glades and meadows of the Gold Coast, though they were once a common sight in Cyrodiil's [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sylvan-2)</sup> Turquoise Nixads have an iridescent blue coloration.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Turq-8)</sup> The green and brown coloration of Emerald Oak Nixads allows them to better camouflage in forests, waiting for unsuspecting travelers to pass by so that they can stealthily steal their sweets.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Emerald-6)</sup> Nectarthief Nixads are a yellow and black striped variety of Nixad found in Skyrim that are loathed by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) due to the way they interfere with their [bees](Lore_Bee.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Nectar-20)</sup>

Screaming Midyear Nixads are a reddish orange breed that [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) is said to have blessed with their "own cycle". Every few years they return to Greenshade around [Midyear](Lore_Midyear.md) and scream constantly, an event known as the "Midyear Screech".<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Scream-15)</sup> The purple and white Orchid Nixad is said to be one of the rarest varieties. They are said to be sacred to the goddess [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), and found only in wild places hallowed to her worship.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Orchid Nixad-5)</sup>

Due to their elemental affinity, some Nixads take on more elemental traits.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FAF-3)</sup> Brimstone Nixads,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Fire-9)</sup> also known as Fire Nixads,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bolga-4)</sup> are a notable example. These Nixads are commonly found in the Burn region of the Deadlands or in the volcanic parts of the Systres Archipelago.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bolga-4)</sup> They are the subject of a legend among the underworld explorers of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md). This legend claims that the these Fire Nixads are responsible for lighting all the candles, torches, and lanterns found burning underground, flying ahead of adventurers to ignite them in the dark.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Fire-9)</sup>

The rare and colorful Shock Nixads somewhat resemble tiny, winged [Storm Atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md), and are capable of making the hair of someone next to them stand on end.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Shock-21)</sup> Shock Nixads can be found in the Sever region of the Deadlands; scores of the tiny creatures emerge to hover above their favorite shrubs or thickets whenever the region's constant winds die down. They are speculated to be attracted to the region's electricity.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FAF-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Nixad Loc-11)</sup>

Ice Nixads are unique among elemental Nixads, as there is some debate among naturalists as to whether the Ice Nixad is a separate breed that is mainly seen in the winter because it hibernates during the warm months, or whether it's just a winter phase of ordinary Nixads that sometimes stays year-round.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Ice-22)</sup>

## Uses and Cultural Impact
Nixads are capable of being tamed or trained, and make loyal and easy to care for companions.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sylvan-2)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Nectar-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Shock-21)</sup> The Empress [Tavia](Lore_Tavia.md) famously had an Orchid Nixad as a friend and companion.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Orchid Nixad-5)</sup> Due to the problems Nectarthief Nixads cause to [mead](Lore_Mead.md) production in Skyrim, [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md)'s [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) began training them to take instructions in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Nectar-20)</sup> The inhabitants of [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md) were able to train Fire Nixads to hum tunes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bolga-4)</sup> [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) has been known to employ the services of giant Brimstone Nixads. One known as the Magma Queen served as his champion in [Vateshran Hollows](Lore_Vateshran_Hollows.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Giant-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Magma Queen-10)</sup>

Nixads are sometimes roasted or candied and consumed. Their sweetened meat is said to leave a tingling, fruity aftertaste on the tongue.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BTATN-1)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Fire Nixads on the other hand are considered to be inedible, as they taste like ash and burn the tongue.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bolga-4)</sup> Marionettes are sometimes made from Nixad carapaces, adorned with [gemstone](Lore_Gems.md) eyes, teeth, and toes.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

Nixads are sometimes the subject of superstition and legends. Children on the Gold Coast, where Nixads have a prominent prescence, are taught that Nixads pluck out the eyes of pompous children, rolling them around in moon sugar afterwards to eat them as a delicacy.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AGCB-18)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ANMMDI-16)</sup> The High Rock tale known as *[The Story of Princess Eselde](Lore_The_Story_of_Princess_Eselde.md)* speaks of the disappearance of one Lady Vespire, who was said to have been "carried off into the forests by a coterie of nixads".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Princess-19)</sup>

Nixads are the subject of certain sayings, such as "wild nixad chase"<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> or "have nixads stolen my tongue?"<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) guards of [the Grasp](Lore_Grasp_of_the_Stricture.md) in Fargrave have been known to refer to mortal lawbreakers as "little nixads".<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>
