# Weather Witch


## Bamz-Amschend
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6b/TR-place-Bamz-Amschend%2C_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg/200px-TR-place-Bamz-Amschend%2C_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Bamz-Amschend,_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Bamz-Amschend,_Skybreak_Gallery.jpg) The Weather Witch in [Bamz-Amschend](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bamz-Amschend) The most infamous Weather Witch was the Karstangz-Bcharn of [Bamz-Amschend](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bamz-Amschend), located in the ruined Skybreak Gallery beneath the city of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). In a time of drought, the Dwemer of Bamz-Amschend created this Weather Witch to bring rain to their land. However, the machine could be used to manipulate the local weather in a variety of ways.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Almalexia-1)</sup> Following the disappearance of the Dwemer circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DI1-3)</sup> the Weather Witch was left abandoned. By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Bamz-Amschend had been forgotten, and the existence of the Karstangz-Bcharn passed into myth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Almalexia-1)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the goddess [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) inadvertently opened a passage to the ruins of Bamz-Amschend when she summoned [Fabricants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fabricants) from the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) to attack Mournhold. Driven to madness by the decline of her divine power, Almalexia subsequently tasked the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) with descending into the ruins and activating the Karstangz-Bcharn as a show of strength in the face of those who proclaimed the end of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md). The Nerevarine used the machine to summon ash storms over Mournhold, a weather event which was alien to the region and damaging to the native flora.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Almalexia used some of her remaining power to prevent the Karstangz-Bcharn from being further tampered with, ensuring that her city was choked with ash storms until her death at the hands of the Nerevarine a short time later. Following her demise, [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) appeared in Mournhold and rid the city of the ash storms as her "gift to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) people".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

## Vardnknd
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/SR-creature-The_Sky_Orchestrator.jpg/200px-SR-creature-The_Sky_Orchestrator.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-The_Sky_Orchestrator.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-The_Sky_Orchestrator.jpg) The Sky Orchestrator, an [animunculus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) responsible for maintaining the Vardnknd Weather Witch Another Weather Witch was built by the Dwemer of Vardnknd, a forgotten ruin located in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). It was said to be the strongest Weather Witch ever created. The Skykiller Gallery in Vardnknd had the ability to control the weather across all of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It was powered by four sub-facilities designed by the Dwemer to mimic the four seasons; the natural energies of these facilities were focused into the gallery by conduit control gems.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FSv1-2)</sup>

At some point in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the Weather Witch of Vardnknd malfunctioned, causing a storm of great intensity to rage across the region. The mage Elberon Blackthorn entered the ruins via Runoff Caverns and managed to disable the machine by deactivating the four sub-facilities.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FSv1-2)</sup> However, circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Elberon entered Vardnknd once again with a band of mercenaries seeking a tome of knowledge which would allow the reader to harness power from weather phenomena. This expedition ran afoul due to the malfunctioning of the Weather Witch; the interior of the ruins was plagued by an unnatural lighting storm, and Elberon lost his life when struck by a bolt of lightning within the ruins.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Skyrim FS-6)</sup>

The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) entered Runoff Caverns in the midst of this failed expedition, following rumors of the Weather Witch. After witnessing the death of Elberon and reading his notes, the Dragonborn followed the same procedure to deactivate the four sub-facilities before entering the Skykiller Gallery and confronting the Sky Orchestrator, a malfunctioning [animunculus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) responsible for maintaining and operating the Weather Witch. With the Orchestrator destroyed, the Weather Witch ceased to function, ending the threat to Skyrim posed by the machine.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Skyrim FS-6)</sup>

## Graven Deep
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/ON-interior-Tempest_Observatory_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Tempest_Observatory_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Tempest_Observatory_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Tempest_Observatory_02.jpg) The Tempest Observatory at Graven Deep The Dwemer constructed an underwater station on the seafloor at Graven Deep, a region of the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) between [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md). Located on a small uncharted island, this facility extended deep into the ocean and contained a Dwarven machine designed to control the weather. The machine is powered by magicka, funnelled downward through pipes to the seafloor via a Transcender Chamber.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Dhulef-7)</sup> The facility allowed for some form of localized weather manipulation, possibly used by the Dwemer to calm the sea.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

When Druid King [Kasorayn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kasorayn) departed High Rock with his people circa [1E 330](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_330),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> they came upon the Uncharted Island and the Dwarven machine within. Although the details of what transpired is unclear, one of the [druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids), a man named Saer, betrayed the crew and used [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) to become a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) named Zelvraak the Unbreathing.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Zelvraak utilized the Dwarven machinery to create storms and pull vessels to their doom in Graven Deep, harvesting their [souls](Lore_Soul.md) and raising an undead army beneath the waves. The area eventually became infamous among sailors, as any ship that entered Graven Deep would never return.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Dhulef-7)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) member named Dhulef recruited a group of [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) to sail to Graven Deep. Despite getting shipwrecked by the storms, the group managed to enter the underwater observatory and destroy Zelvraak, ending the undead threat and the lich's weather manipulation.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>
