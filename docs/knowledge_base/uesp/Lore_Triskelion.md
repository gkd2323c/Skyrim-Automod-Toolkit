# Triskelion


## Culture

### Nord
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/ON-crown_store-Morthal_Banner.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Morthal_Banner.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Morthal_Banner.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Morthal_Banner.jpg) Banner of Morthal with the town in the background (ESO) For the Nords, it holds significant meaning in the city of [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md). In Morthal, this emblem is proudly displayed on banners, featuring a white triskelion set against a gray background, reminiscent of the misty marshes surrounding the city. The interpretation of the Morthal triskelion varies, with some suggesting that it symbolizes the unity of the mind, body, and spirit, while others propose alternative meanings, sparking scholarly debates.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Banner-4)</sup> Triskelion markings commonly adorned the bodies of heroes who had valiantly protected the citizens of Morthal. These symbols serve as a visible testament to their courage and dedication to the settlement.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MCBM-5)</sup> It was also prominently featured in the clothing and fashion of Morthal's residents, with the triskelion symbol adorning various garments and accessories, reflecting its significance in the culture and aesthetics of the region.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MJF-7)</sup> Triskelion imagery is also present on some Nordic megalithic structures in Western Skyrim.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WS-8)</sup>

### Reachfolk
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/ON-quest-The_Lost_Shrine.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Lost_Shrine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Lost_Shrine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Lost_Shrine.jpg) The Shrine of Lorkh In the context of Reachfolk culture, the usage of the triskelion is less clear, although historical records indicate that they adorned the shrines of [Lorkh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkh), the Sower of Men, with such symbols. This suggests that the triskelion imagery holds a place in Reachfolk religion and traditions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLS-6)</sup>

### Breton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png/200px-ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png) Symbols of Wyrds and Druids: Beldama Wyrd (bottom left) is represented by triskelion symbol. For Bretons, a variation of the triskelion is used as the symbol of the [Beldama Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beldama_Wyrd).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WJ-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Tablet-2)</sup> However, it also appears in areas associated with the [Glenmoril Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-VW-9)</sup>

### Bosmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/ON-place-Ouze_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ouze_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ouze_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ouze_02.jpg) Altar in the Ouze with a triskelion symbols Triskelion imagery can be found at various sacred Bosmeri sites, including the [Ouze](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ouze)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ouze-3)</sup> and Treehenge,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Treehenge-10)</sup> as well as other places such as Bone Orchard<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Bone_Orchard-11)</sup> and [Gil-Var-Delle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gil-Var-Delle).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gil-Var-Delle-12)</sup>

### Other
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/ON-place-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company_%28Anvil%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company_%28Anvil%29.jpg)](File_ON-place-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company_(Anvil).jpg.md) [](File_ON-place-Gold_Coast_Trading_Company_(Anvil).jpg.md) Headquarters of Anvil branch of the Gold Coast Trading Company The triskelion is also a symbol associated with the magic school of [illusion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Skyrim I-13)</sup> the [Gold Coast Trading Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold_Coast_Trading_Company),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Logo-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Rewards-15)</sup> as well as various [Witch Covens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witches).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PTC-16)</sup>

Some sacrificial altars dedicated to [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of the Hunt, are often adorned with various symbols. Among these symbols, irregular and crude triskelions are sometimes included.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SAH-17)</sup>

Triskelion symbols were also present on altars within Reaper's Henge, a location in [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March) that had fallen under the control of a [necromancer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RH-18)</sup>

The [Deeping Drome](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deeping_Drome) is an underground [Dwemer ruin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Ruins) constructed in the shape of a triskelion. Its purpose and function remains unknown.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DD-19)</sup>
