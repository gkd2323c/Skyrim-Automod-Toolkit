## Lamp of Clarity
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Lamp_of_Clarity.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Lamp_of_Clarity.png) Lamp of Clarity The [Lamp of Clarity](Lore_Lamp_of_Clarity.md) was an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) in order to aid in [Ithelia's](Lore_Ithelia.md) imprisonment. It possessed the ability to illuminate the presence of Ithelia and her Unseen on a [Path](Lore_Many_Paths.md) and to reveal open [portals](Lore_Portals.md) to the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), or pull alternate versions of things from the Many Paths into reality. The Lamp was ultimately destroyed when it was used as part of a [spell](Lore_Spells.md) originating from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), which was provided to the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in order to repair the [reality tears](Lore_Fate.md) created by Ithelia's power that were threatening to unravel all [existence](Lore_Aurbis.md). After the spell was cast the Lamp of Clarity became a normal lamp, its [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) power fully expended.

## Lamp of the Satakalaam
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/ON-item-Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam.jpg/200px-ON-item-Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam.jpg) Lamp of the Satakalaam The [Lamp of the Satakalaam](Lore_Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam.md) is a sacred ornament that dates back to the time of the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md). It is an item of importance to the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) culture and religion. Together with [Sword of Shabnam](Lore_Sword_of_Shabnam.md) and [Majeel's Scroll](Lore_Majeel%27s_Scroll.md), they represent the aspects of the Redguard in body, mind, and spirit. The lamp represented the spirit.

Together with two other relics, it was dedicated centuries before the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), back when the High Temple of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) was built. Since then, they have been the sacred tokens of Satakalaam's High Temple. Although all temples had relics to honor the gods, the Lamp of the Satakalaam was specific to this temple.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Alik%27r_Desert_Quest_Items#Lamp_of_Satakalaam)

## Light of Day
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/SR-item-Light_of_Day.jpg/200px-SR-item-Light_of_Day.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Light_of_Day.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Light_of_Day.jpg) Light of Day The **Light of Day** is a uniquely crafted [iron](Lore_Iron.md) mace with powerful enchantments that make it a bane to any [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), as it deals large amounts of damage to the undead. Its enchantments are so strong that any vampires who attempt to wield it are killed. It originally belonged to Ano Vando, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) vampire hunter. By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) it, among many other artifacts, had come into the possession of a cult of [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) heretics on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md).

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Light_of_Day), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Light_of_Day.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Ghosts_of_the_Tribunal.md)</sup>

## Light of Meridia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg) A Light of Meridia The [Lights of Meridia](Lore_Light_of_Meridia.md) are a series of powerful crystalline [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) and filled with her power.

The Lights of Meridia were initially a gift to the Meridia-worshipping [Hollow City](Lore_Hollow_City.md) by their patron Prince, power that protected the city and made life easier for its inhabitants. When Meridia's rival [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) sent his [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) minions to attack the city, Meridia sensed an opportunity, she filled the Lights with even more of her divine power and used them to ward the city and push it into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) through Molag Bal's own [portal](Lore_Portals.md). Encased by the power of the Lights this manner, the Hollow City became a part of Meridia's own [domain](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md) in Molag Bal's [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md), a "dagger in his side" which he could not touch.

**Found in:** - ESO

## Loom of the Untraveled Road
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Mirrormoor.jpg) Loom of the Untraveled Road The [Loom of the Untraveled Road](Lore_Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road.md) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) apparatus of unimaginable power created by [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md). It is said to be what [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) feared, leading to him taking action against Ithelia. Described as a series of [portals](Lore_Portals.md) within portals leading to the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), it is more of a place than an engine, residing within [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md)'s forgotten Loom Quarter. With the Loom Ithelia is said to be able to reshape the [fates](Lore_Fate.md), accessing alternate fates and histories from the Many Paths and weaving them into reality, or even granting each being their own desired history to break fate's shackles. Places that were part of [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md) are said to be fundamentally connected to the Loom, and [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) used in such locations will have their power drained by the Loom as it gains power. Through the Loom Ithelia can also draw immense amounts of power from the Many Paths, though the untold amounts of energy ultimately overwhelm her, transforming her into the Last Tomorrow, the threat Hermaeus Mora foresaw. Even after being destroyed through the use of [Boethiah's](Lore_Boethiah.md) Blade, [Abolisher](Lore_Abolisher.md), the Loom still radiates power, preventing approach through [portal magic](Lore_Portals.md) and making the area around the Loom itself unstable, threatening anyone lingering there with the possibility of becoming lost in the Many Paths.

**Found in:** - ESO

## Lord's Mail
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Lord%27s_Mail(m)_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Lord%27s_Mail(m)_02.png) The Lord's Mail The [Lord's Mail](Lore_Lord%27s_Mail.md) (also called the **Armor of Morihaus** or the **Gift of Kynareth**) is an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) given to mortals by [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), one of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md). It is an ancient plated [adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md) or [mithril](Lore_Mithril.md) cuirass of unsurpassable quality, considered to be heavy armor. It grants the wearer the power to absorb or regenerate health, resist the effects of spells, and resist or even cure poison. It is said that whenever Kynareth deigns the wearer unworthy, the Lord's Mail will be taken away and hidden for the next chosen one. The Mail was originally owned during the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) by the bull [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md), a [demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) and early cultural hero of the [Cyro-Nordics](Lore_Cyro-Nord.md) who was closely associated with Kynareth and [the Lord](Lore_The_Lord.md). The cuirass was rumored to have later been taken from Morihaus by Kynareth herself due to his arrogance. The cuirass is considered an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) relic, and is accepted as a uniform in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). Indeed, the armor has been known to bear the red dragon sigil of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) on its breastplate.

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Lord%27s_Mail), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Lord%27s_Mail), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Lord%27s_Mail_(artifact)), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Lord%27s_Mail_(item).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Lord%27s_Mail.md)</sup>, [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Lord%27s_Mail)
