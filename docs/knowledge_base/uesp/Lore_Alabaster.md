Alabaster (alternatively known as **Al'basti** in [Ta'agra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ta%27agra))<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESORajhin-1)</sup> is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Map Arena-2)</sup> found on the southeastern coast of the province on the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md).

The town is known for the great white walls that surround the city, hence the name.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena-3)</sup> Alabaster was one of the original sixteen kingdoms of Elsweyr, known for their bards, playwrights, poets,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup> and spices.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONAtofir-5)</sup> A small unnamed town is located outside the city walls, to the west.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

## Layout & Geography
Alabaster is a port city found on the northern edge of the Topal Bay, just west of the Elsweyr provincial border with [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the [Lower Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lower_Niben), being the closest city to [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). Alabaster is in the region of [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md), which is known for its jungles, [rainforests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tenmar_Forest), and river basins.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1E-7)</sup> Large hills separate it from both the [Quin'rawl Peninsula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quin%27rawl_Peninsula) and [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). Alabaster is neighbored by several settlements, including [Darkarn Place](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darkarn_Place) to the west, and [Valley Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valley_Guard) to the northwest.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Map Arena-2)</sup> The white Alabaster [jerboa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jerboa) is named after the city.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Jerboa-8)</sup>

## History
Alabaster was one of the sixteen sovereign states of modern-day Elsweyr and was known for their dramatists, and once had a Bard-Queen.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup> But like the other kingdoms, Alabaster dissolved with the arrival of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260) and the weakened states of the south were absorbed into the Kingdom of [Pa'alatiin](Lore_Pa%27alatiin.md), modern-day Pellitine.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3E-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Aa PAI-10)</sup>

In [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) reached the harbors of Alabaster, following their outbreak on the harbor-city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md). After it appeared in Alabaster, it spread across the rest of the province. The Khajiit overcame the flu, and with the intervention from the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), the flu was nigh extinction.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TEat C-11)</sup>

Sometime around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Alabaster traded with the city of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), when it was ruled by the Provisional-Governor, [Fortunata ap Dugal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortunata_ap_Dugal).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FMot P-12)</sup> The Canonreeve and Dominion representative of Alabaster, Lord Juz-do, traveled to the northern town of The Stitches and met with Rigurt the Brash, an ambassador of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md). He presented him with a [sabre cat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sabre_Cat) fur coat, one of the finest in all of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), which proved to be a blunder as the Canonreeve was horrified by the use of slaughtered cats as clothing and proceeded to storm out.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Cc C-13)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Alabaster was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Mojnir, and it had a rivalry with [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) and [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena-3)</sup> Around this time, a scribe from Alabaster learned that the legendary sword of heroes, [Chrysamere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chrysamere), had resurfaced somewhere in Tamriel. Rumors spread across the continent about Chrysamere's certain return, and the weapon was subsequently recovered by the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

## Notes
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Alabaster's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Nightrunners".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-15)</sup> This was finally used in *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)*, where the Alabaster Nightrunners arena team competed in an event known as the Dragon Games.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
- In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the youth of Alabaster purportedly found it fashionable to place their honorifics before their names, rather than after in the typical Khajiiti custom of that time—a practice which seemed to have spread to other Khajiit by the Third Era.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KH-17)</sup>
- There is an expression that goes *"You couldn't find an Alfiq in Alabaster!"*,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Orland-18)</sup> suggesting it may host a sizeable population of [Alfiq](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alfiq).
