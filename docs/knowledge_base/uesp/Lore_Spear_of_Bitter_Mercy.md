# Spear of Bitter Mercy

*Spear of Bitter Mercy
Lightning in the hand
My point bites deep
I end the chase
Even the mightiest are unmade by my touch.*
— *[Spear of Bitter Mercy Inscription](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy_Inscription)* The Spear of Bitter Mercy is an artifact that is an enigma to [Tamrielians](Lore_Tamriel.md), but many believe it to be of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) origin despite a lack of recorded history.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> However, multiple Spears of Bitter Mercy may exist.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_A)</sup> Originally forged by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Chimere-2)</sup> it has become the signature [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) of his "ally" [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), who has become the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) that is most closely associated with the Spear.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOArt-3)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Noonan)</sup> The name **Spear with Five Points** serves not only as an alternative title for the artifact, but also as one of the many epithets the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) use for Hircine.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GSR2-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DDFCodex-5)</sup> The weapon was once in [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s possession,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWSQ-6)</sup> but his association with it and how he came to obtain it is unknown. He has notably stolen artifacts from his fellow Princes in the past for his own amusement.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOChaos Magic-7)</sup>

Mehrunes Dagon imbued the Spear with his power for use in the [Ritual of the Innocent Quarry](Lore_Great_Hunt.md#The_Ritual_of_the_Innocent_Quarry).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Chimere-2)</sup> This enchantment consists of powerful and maleficent energies capable of instantly killing all but the "High Daedra Lords". Thus it is forbidden from being removed from the site of the Great Hunt,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TPH-8)</sup> and it cannot be wielded by any mortals or immortals, except those sanctified to the Hunt and bound to its rules.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BA-9)</sup><sup>:151</sup> Because Dagon twisted his pact with [Chimere Graegyn](Lore_Chimere_Graegyn.md), an unsanctioned wearer of the [Armor of the Savior's Hide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Armor_of_the_Savior%27s_Hide) can bear the Spear; the armor's enchantment offers protection from the energies of the oathbreaker.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TMKR-10)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Chimere-2)</sup> Since the rules only apply within the Hunt itself, the artifact can be safely wielded if it were outside its intended locale.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-11)</sup> However, the spear can still seemingly reject those unworthy of wielding it.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BGJ-12)</sup>

According to a Reachman myth, Hircine wields the Spear of Bitter Mercy when he takes on the aspect of Alrabeg the Hunter.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AOLH-13)</sup> It is one of the [Symbols of the Five Aspects](Lore_Symbols_of_the_Five_Aspects.md), the other four being the [Totems of Hircine](Lore_Totems_of_Hircine.md) (composed of three artifacts), and the [Totem of Claw and Fang](Lore_Totem_of_Claw_and_Fang.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONHSGQ-14)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AOLH-13)</sup> The weapon grants its wielder a magical shield as well as the ability to summon [storm atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup> In appearance it is a metallic spear, usually with two or four protruding prongs surrounding the central spearhead. It is sometimes emblazoned with Daedric [Hekem](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) sigils, representing the letter H.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

## History

### First Era
Legends speak of a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) known as [Thane Icehammer](Lore_Thane_Icehammer.md), who sometime in the mid-late fifth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), unknowingly killed several [were-creatures](Lore_Lycanthropy.md#Types_of_Werecreatures) during a hunting trip in the [Yorgrim River](Lore_Yorgrim_River.md) basin. Angered by the death of his kin, Hircine thrust the Spear of Bitter Mercy into Icehammer's side, whereupon the tip broke off and remained lodged inside him. Thane returned wounded to his home of [Cragwallow](Lore_Cragwallow.md), but the magic of Hircine's Spear slowly turned him mad, ultimately causing him to murder an acolyte of Kyne in a fit of rage. Matron Icehammer was appalled by her husband's actions, and ordered the guards to subdue him, but he fled into an ancient Nordic burial ruin in the mountains northeast of Cragwallow, whereupon he was sealed alive within as punishment.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> The ruins would become known as Icehammer's Vault, and would become a source of infamy, as Thane Icehammer continued to stalk its halls as a [Draugr](Lore_Draugr.md). His state of undeath and rage were fueled by the Spear fragment that remained inside him,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LOTI-19)</sup> until he was finally slain in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) by an adventurer.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESO-20)</sup>

### Second Era
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MWYB-21)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-22)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Spear of Bitter Mercy among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TA-23)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Spear of Bitter Mercy and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MWFR-24)</sup>

[Staves](Lore_Staves.md) made in imitation of the Spear existed circa 2E 582.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOArt-3)</sup> That same year, the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) expanded into the Karth Valley, causing many Reach clans to flee to the safety of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md)'s walls. The [Shrine of the Hunt-Father](Lore_Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.md) led by High Shaman [Glynroch](Lore_Glynroch.md) was erected within the city, and the Symbols of the Five Aspects were successfully recovered and placed in the shrine for safety, the real Spear of Bitter Mercy among them.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONHSGQ-14)</sup>

The [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) ’s Antiquarian Circle excavated an artifact during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) dedicated to Hircine, known as the [Huntsman’s Warmask](Lore_Huntsman%27s_Warmask.md). One of its key components, the Fangs of Bitter Mercy, contain engravings that form a chant to Hircine in his aspect as Alrabeg the Hunter: “The five are all, the five are one. Five are true and death-in-the-woods.” Some scholars have noted that these fangs may represent the Spear of Bitter Mercy, a symbol of singular honor among Hircine’s followers. Scholar Verita Numida argued for a Reachfolk origin of the Fangs, while Ugron gro-Thumog proposed that similar motifs appear on war-masks belonging to a great chief of the [Ket Keptu](Lore_Ket_Keptu.md). Reginus Buca agreed, suggesting that the Ket Keptu, renowned hunters and warrior-smiths, would regard wielding the Spear of Bitter Mercy as a singular honor. He concluded that any who bore this war-mask would have been favored by Hircine himself.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DDFCodex-5)</sup>

### Third Era
The Spear of Bitter Mercy resurfaced near the end of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) (3E 398), when Mehrunes Dagon's forces attacked and seized control of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) with the blessing of Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), who was impersonating Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-BSPaxti-25)</sup> With the [pillar of light](Lore_Pillar_of_Light.md) and the Battlespire's [Weir Gate](Lore_Weir_Gate.md) back to Tamriel blocked by [Sigil](Lore_Sigil.md) Wards, an [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) who had traveled through [Shade Perilous](Lore_Evergloam.md#Shade_Perilous) eventually reached the Chimera of Desolation after assisting [Jaciel Morgen](Lore_Jaciel_Morgen.md), lieutenant of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). After arrival, this [hero](Lore_Hero.md) is informed by the Master of the Hunt, Si'ess the [Dark Seducer](Lore_Mazken.md), that their arrival signifies the Apprentice's new role of Hare in the [Ritual of the Innocent Quarry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ritual_of_the_Innocent_Quarry), a Great Hunt instigated by the [Herne](Lore_Herne_(creature).md) brotherhood in service to [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md), which may have possibly been hosted by Hircine.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Noonan)</sup> However, this Apprentice was not alone, as many notes around the hunting grounds promised assistance and called the hare to seek the cottage of Hartcroft, where the hero met and was aided by Chimere Graegyn, an inhabitant of the Chimera of Desolation and someone who had previously defied Dagon.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BSL5-26)</sup>

One of the Huntsmen was a Herne named Egahirn; it was his first Hunt, and he was given the honor of making the Kill. However, in order to assure his victory, he kept one of the six gatekeys needed for the Hare to escape on his person. This was a direct violation of the Law of the Hunt, and it did not sit well with many of the other participants (as Egahirn was protected against all weapons and magics).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-BSLFB-27)</sup> It also presented an opportunity; when he tricked Mehrunes Dagon, Chimere wore the Armor of the Savior's Hide, which protected the wearer from attacks by oathbreakers—which Egahirn was by his actions.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Chimere-2)</sup> The apprentice retrieved the Savior's Hide, which enabled them to bear the Spear of Bitter Mercy. Well-equipped, the apprentice defeated Egahirn and escaped the realm.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BSL5-26)</sup> Both artifacts were then used in the recapturing of the Battlespire,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> though the Spear was substituted with the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](Lore_Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver.md) for Dagon's banishment.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LD-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-BSL7-29)</sup> Afterwards, the Spear of Bitter Mercy appeared sporadically within Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Sheogorath tasked the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) with retrieving the [Fork of Horripilation](Lore_Fork_of_Horripilation.md) from Big Head, a mad [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) hermit and a worshipper of Sheogorath who lived in a small shack on an island in the [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) region of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Sheogorath instructed the Nerevarine with an odd task, to kill a giant [Bull Netch](Lore_Bull_Netch.md) with the fork. Big Head willingly gave the fork to the Nerevarine, who then killed the Bull Netch and returned the fork to Sheogorath, and was rewarded with the Spear of Bitter Mercy.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWSQ-6)</sup> The Nerevarine possibly later sold the weapon to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md), who put it on display in the [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) Museum of Artifacts.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Tribunal-30)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/SR-creature-Gogh_%28combat%29.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Gogh_%28combat%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Gogh_(combat).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Gogh_(combat).jpg) Gogh using the spear's summoning capabilities (Skyrim Creation Club) Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a tribe of [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) migrated out of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) using subterranean tunnels beneath the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). They emerged into [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) and took up occupation of a cave known as Gromm's Pass. The hunter of this group, a goblin named [Gogh](Lore_Gogh.md), was seemingly blessed by Hircine as he was in possession of the Spear of Bitter Mercy. Gogh was fiercely protective of the weapon, which rejected anyone else that tried to claim it. The tribe subsequently encountered a helpless [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) from the nearby stronghold of [Largashbur](Lore_Largashbur.md), who happened to be stained blue by the [Steel Blue Entoloma](Lore_Steel_Blue_Entoloma.md) mushrooms which grow in abundance within Gromm's Pass. Believing him to be the [Blue God](Lore_Blue_God.md) (an aspect of Malacath worshipped by goblins), the tribe quickly made him their shaman and began to obey him as their god. However, the tunnel to Cyrodiil collapsed soon after, cutting the tribe off from any reinforcements.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BGJ-12)</sup> Gogh then spotted the Orcish shaman applying blue dye to maintain his appearance and was thus sentenced to death, but was fortuitously saved by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who killed the Orc and his remaining goblin followers. Gogh thereafter entered into the Dragonborn's service, and continued to wield the weapon and its powers in battle.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CC-31)</sup>
