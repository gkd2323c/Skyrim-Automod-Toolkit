His tomb is located in the [Green Emperor Way](Lore_Green_Emperor_Way.md) district of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBCamarril-1)</sup> [Mankar Camoran's](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) infamous [*Commentaries on the Mysterium Xarxes*](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md) contain a hidden message that points to the tomb of Camarril, upon which Mankar had inscribed a runic depiction of the symbol of the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) and the location of their hidden base, which was enchanted to glow red at midday. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) was able to find this hidden message and infiltrate their base in the caverns beneath [Lake Arrius](Lore_Lake_Arrius.md) circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBPath-2)</sup>

## Notes
- Camarril's race is unknown, though his name shares phonetic patterns to most [elven](Lore_Mer.md) names.
- Mankar Camoran's *Commentaries* were said to have been contemporary with [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), written over 400 years before 3E 433,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Meena-3)</sup> though this conflicts with Mankar's alleged birth year of 3E 267.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TR-4)</sup> If the claim is somehow true and the Commentaries are in fact that old, it is unlikely Camarril was tied to the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) as his tomb was already in existence during the lifetime of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Meena-3)</sup>
