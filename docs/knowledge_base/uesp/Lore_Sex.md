## Racial Compatibility and Reproduction
*→ See also: **[Multiraciality](Lore_Multiraciality.md)***

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/ON-npc-Aeliah_Renmus.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Aeliah_Renmus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aeliah_Renmus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aeliah_Renmus.jpg) Aeliah Remnus, a half-Imperial half-Redguard woman The council of healers at the [Imperial University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) has done many analyses of the living races and determined that [mer](Lore_Mer.md) and [man](Lore_Men.md) can effectively reproduce with one another. The Imperial University found that the children of interracial couples typically take on more of the mother's racial features, with traces of the father's traits being expressed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NORP-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TES3Generic Dialogue-2)</sup> These studies may be incorrect however, as there are cases where humans from two different races have had children, and their offspring bore features from both parents.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Aeliah-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tuttle Posts2021-4)</sup> The Imperial University's council of healers could also not determine if [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) are reproductively compatible with mer and man and each other.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NORP-1)</sup> Despite this, other Imperial scholars have claimed that men, mer and humanoid [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) can all mate with one another.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TES3Generic Dialogue-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

As previously mentioned, children of mixed ancestry are possible. Notable examples include Aeliah Renmus, the daughter of a Redguard and an Imperial, Bacaro Volorus, the son of an Imperial and a Breton, and the [Gray Prince](Lore_Agronak_gro-Malog.md), who was born to an Orc and an Imperial.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Aeliah-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LBC-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tuttle Posts2021-4)</sup> The Gray Prince's father was an Imperial [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md), providing evidence that at least some vampires can reproduce with the living.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OFTGP-8)</sup> In cases of mixed heritage, traits of different races may skip generations, appearing later on in the family line. One such case was that of [Nunex Faleria](Lore_Nunex_Faleria.md)'s grandchild, who displayed elven traits despite having human parents.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TLFF-9)</sup>

There are varying accounts of groups with mixed ancestry, with some of the most famous examples being Bretons and [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md). It is known that some [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) races such as the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci.md) can interbreed with the men of Tamriel. With such examples as the mixed Akaviri-Imperial [Rim-Men](Lore_Rim-Men.md) of [Hakoshae](Lore_Hakoshae.md)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TNShrine-10)</sup> or the half-human half-Akaviri mercenary lord [Duadeen](Lore_Duadeen.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tobias-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-MKP-12)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-FHMC-13)</sup> The infamous Breton [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) captain Lorant Bouchard, who wielded a powerful [Tsaesci blade](Lore_Dawnfang.md), was rumored to be part serpent.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-14)</sup> [Half-giants](Lore_Half-Giant.md) are, as the name implies, half- [giant](Lore_Giant.md) men.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HGA-15)</sup> The legendary [Companion](Lore_Five_Companions.md) [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md) is one of the most well known half-giants, despite only having giant ancestry unlike true half-giants.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tuttle Posts2021-4)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LFather-16)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CMDia-17)</sup> Some have speculated that the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) are the result of Aldmer interbreeding with the local beast tribes of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ACo V-18)</sup>, whilst the tall, bestial and hunchbacked [Maormer Leviathans](Lore_Maormer_Leviathans.md) are described as "the spawn of some unholy and sorcerous coupling of Sea Elf and [Sea Serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md)".<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSE-19)</sup>

Pairings between mortals and the immortal have also been documented, though often just in legends and folklore. Some of the most "common" examples of these are shown in the form of [Demi-Princes](Lore_Demiprince.md), offspring of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) or [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) and a lesser entity, such as a mortal.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-20)</sup> The [Nede](Lore_Nede.md) [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and the [Demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) are known for siring a child, [Belharza](Lore_Belharza.md), and possibly creating the [Minotaur race](Lore_Minotaur.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-OM-21)</sup> The formerly mortal [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and the Daedric Prince [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) are said to have sired a race of [supermonsters](Lore_Monster_Children.md), rumored to still live in the [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md) region.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LOV12-22)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-VFDE-23)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-VOF-24)</sup> Similarly, the violation of Nede [Lamae Beolfag](Lore_Lamae_Beolfag.md) by Bal is said to have resulted in Lamae begetting a brood of countless abominations, including the first [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OLBTM-25)</sup> Whispers also claim that the [Lamia](Lore_Lamia.md) race are descended from the spawn begat by Lamae.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-KC:AHC-26)</sup>

Various species of typically aggressive [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) are claimed to or have reputations of mating with other races. [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) are one such case who may be able to interbreed with humans,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Ogre-27)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Dorian-28)</sup> although it's unknown if this is the case for the other [Goblin-ken](Lore_Goblin-ken.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NORP-1)</sup> Though there have been mentions of extinct "orcoblins".<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Orcoblin-29)</sup> At least one tale of unconfirmed authenticity mentions offspring from a pairing of a Lamia and a man of another race.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-LOC-30)</sup> However this seemingly all-female race is presumed to be hermaphroditic, and they are known to lay eggs.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-EGT-31)</sup> [Harpies](Lore_Harpy.md) have a reputation as creatures who kidnap, mate with, and kill human males,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DUG-32)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOHarridan-33)</sup> but their reproductive biology is otherwise not well understood.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NORP-1)</sup> It is said a thousand generations of inbreeding is what removed any glimmer of intelligence from the minds of [Wrothgarian](Lore_Wrothgar.md) harpies, who are closer to giant [birds](Lore_Bird.md) than people.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Orzorga-34)</sup>

Some beastfolk only reproduce during certain life-phases, often accompanied by drastic transformations. For one year of their life, [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) undergo a process known as *karvinasim* and emerge onto land as aggressive and animalistic [crustaceans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Land_Dreugh) to breed.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-RLCOTD-35)</sup> [Grummites](Lore_Grummite.md) only mate once they've fully matured from their [aquatic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pollywog) and [semi-aquatic](Lore_Baliwog.md) life phases, being consumed by an urge to mate once their metamorphosis is complete.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-FFTM-36)</sup> The [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) are hermaphrodites in their "polwygle" phase, and later reabsorb their reproductive organs once they're old enough to walk on land.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-FNS-37)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-38)</sup> Due to this, Imperial scholars believe that the Sload cannot produce children with the races of Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NORP-1)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Ger H-39)</sup>

The [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) was capable of creating female clones of himself from his own flesh.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-DFD-40)</sup>

## Races

### Altmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-render-Fennorian_Heart%27s_Day_2024.jpg/200px-ON-render-Fennorian_Heart%27s_Day_2024.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Fennorian_Heart%27s_Day_2024.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Fennorian_Heart%27s_Day_2024.jpg) A Heart's Day date The [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) have a rough reputation to outsiders, with some believing they practice some form of eugenics, supposedly practicing infanticide in nine out of ten newborns due to impurities.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-41)</sup> This has some basis in truth, as some Altmer (but not all)<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Amalien ON-42)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Tuttle Amalien-43)</sup> do abandon those born with deformities or impurities, branding them as "hulkynds".<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONManacar-44)</sup>

Some Altmer physicians believed that elven women could not conceive more than four times, with the usual number of children being two.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB-5)</sup> Some Altmer were believed to have engaged in inbreeding<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Vika-45)</sup> with a prominent example being [Clan Direnni](Lore_Clan_Direnni.md) due to the lack of distinctive features among them. However another theory to explain this was the possibility that they reproduced through magic.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-DPA-46)</sup> In the days of Direnni rule over [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), these elves had many privileges and rights over their human subjects, one of which was the "Perquisite of Coition", or in other words, the right to have sex with any human they so desired. Sex with their subjects was not just casual fun but also competition between Direnni, as many strove to have "stables" filled with the most attractive humans they could find.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TBMOP-47)</sup>

Erotic literature, both fictional and instructional is present in Altmer society. During the time of the First [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) distributed guides on approved methods of procreation in order to properly reflect their Altmeri heritage.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-48)</sup> Military camps within Dominion territories were known to have recreation compounds with ["certified entertainers"](Lore_Prostitution.md) for the "safety" of their soldiers.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Protocol Propriety-49)</sup> Individuals of importance such as [Queen Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) were popular subjects in erotic fiction during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Ayrenn Erotica-50)</sup>

Some of the more prejudiced among the High Elves have compared sleeping with Bosmer to bestiality,<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Laurosse-51)</sup> and in a similiar manner have disdain for their amorous proclivities- considering them to have stained Aldmeri bloodlines by coupling with Man.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TMono-52)</sup>

### Argonians
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg) An uxith, where Argonian children are born It is said some [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) view "mating" as nothing more than an order of the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) to reproduce,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-TAMR-53)</sup> but a variety of reasons exist for the act in Argonian culture not unlike any other of the mortal races. Love<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-54)</sup>, attraction<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Chal Maht-55)</sup> or even coupling with members of other tribes to improve relations,<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-56)</sup> are all normal customs.

Distinct from other races, the Argonians have cloacae.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-UDM-57)</sup> Despite having breasts, Saxhleel are capable of laying eggs.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Mo EB-58)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-59)</sup> Argonians can display ovoviviparity, and due to their humanoid structure they can have live births where the eggs hatch internally.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-PHP-60)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-DGP-61)</sup> Argonians born in cold foreign provinces or to small nomadic tribes tend to have live births without the use of egg nests, while those in warmer climates with established, stable, and permanent communities would likely see a great number of eggs.<sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-MNP-62)</sup>

In the Second Era, there were limited-edition Argonian erotica featuring androgynous etchings of the race in circulation.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Arg Erotica-63)</sup>

### Bretons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-render-Darien.jpg/200px-ON-render-Darien.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Darien.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Darien.jpg) [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md) partakes of bread in a manner most seductive [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/ON-misc-Breton_Pointed_Ear.png/200px-ON-misc-Breton_Pointed_Ear.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Breton_Pointed_Ear.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Breton_Pointed_Ear.png) A male Breton displaying a remnant of elven blood: pointed ears The [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) people are a "hybrid"<sup>[[UOL 9]](#cite_note-ON2022-64)</sup> race which originated from centuries of [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) and [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) interbreeding.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-PGE3A-65)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-LATD-66)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-MDBMM-67)</sup> In the early days of the race, they were even referred to as Manmeri,<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-PGE1High-68)</sup> Half-Elves,<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TBMOP-47)</sup> and possibly [Chimera](Lore_Chimera.md) (from the Altmeri word for "change").<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-TBOG-69)</sup> Over time, elven blood became more and more diluted in the Breton race, resulting in the waning of their elven features, but never outright vanishing, instead presenting itself in aspects such as the pointed elf-like ears which some Bretons still possess.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-MDBMM-67)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-70)</sup> Due to this heritage, Bretons are still sometimes derogatorily referred to as a "mongrels" or "degraded hybrids".<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TBMOP-47)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TCOTDCov-71)</sup>

Bretons have the Law of Consanguinity, preventing marriage between those too close in blood relation.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TSo PE-72)</sup> Regardless, rumors of incest among Breton royalty and nobility are rampant. A notable subject of these rumors is [King Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md), who allegedly slept with every sibling in his royal line—some even implied that his daughter [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md) was a product of one such coupling. Others speak of pagan rituals, paint-slick bodies and mind-altering concoctions as having their places in "blood purity" customs. Other figures of note were a young noblewoman and her brother at a [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) party. Despite this, many of these stories are just that—stories. They are considered poor grist and sensationalism to most historians.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-LABHI-73)</sup>

[Waking Day](Lore_Waking_Day.md) is a Breton holiday originally invented to wake the spirits of nature. In modern times it has evolved into an orgiastic celebration of winter's end.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-74)</sup> Preceding Waking Day is the March of Beauty, a festival in which provocatively dressed [prostitutes](Lore_Prostitution.md) danced throughout [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), being available for sex later in the evening.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-MOT1-75)</sup> The [druids](Lore_Druids.md) of the Systres isle of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) possessed an artifact which caused extreme arousal to those in its vicinity. In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), this relic was unearthed and activated by the Dreadsail pirates, leading the entire settlement of Llanshara to devolve into frequent sexual activity.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TSRR-76)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Mina N-77)</sup> This same settlement had a 'Ritual of Life's Commencement', an ancient tradition celebrating the creation of life through the sexual union of Druids.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Ro LC-78)</sup> The insult "corset-wearing dog-diddlers"<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Modra DC-79)</sup> has been used by some. In a similar vein, the Glenmoril Wyrd are known to apply a special cologne to their lower bodies for the pleasure of their wolves.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Wyrd Wolf-80)</sup>

A [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) joke book poked fun at Bretons for being rather promiscuous.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Lore Jokes-81)</sup> The [Prostitutes Guild](Lore_Prostitutes_Guild.md) has a large, almost palatial guildhouse located in an affluent part of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md).<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-MOT4-82)</sup> The [Laumont](Lore_Laumont.md) line is rumored to have originated from a union of Breton and [flame atronach](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md), though this is dismissed by some members of the family as exaggeration or embellishment.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Lud Lau Hireling-83)</sup>

The Bretons of High Isle idolized [mermaids](Lore_Mermaid.md) as fertility icons.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-High Idol-84)</sup> Some wealthy Bretons utilize illicit [inhalants](Lore_Drugs.md) to enhance their bedchamber activities.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-AIOM-85)</sup>

Some have theorized that [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) extinction was due to a lack of fertility among the Wild Elves, who instead resorted to mating with the Bosmer people to replenish their numbers. ### Giants
Giants have been known to gather regularly at ceremonial sites. Here, they come together to trade, mate, and communicate with whatever methods they have developed.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-Ga D-105)</sup> One tale tells of a Giant family residing on Mount [Trolhetta](Lore_Trolhetta.md) consisting of a male giant and a female giantess raising their child together.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-TSW-106)</sup> Female giants are not often seen by other races. Although Giants have no known written language, they seem to have an affinity for spiral motifs. This particular pattern (shared by ancient Nordic craftsmanship) is often depicted as scars on their torsos, carved into their mammoth's tusks, or painted onto various rocks, trees, and walls within their encampments. Observers have speculated that this rudimentary art possibly entices females, marks territory, or is simply artistic expression.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-AAG-107)</sup>

## Fauna

Weather can sometimes affect the mating habits of creatures. Circa [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565), unseasonably warm weather in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) drove hordes of [trolls](Lore_Troll.md) across the western half of the province while "locked in unspeakably horrid acts". [High King Svargrim](Lore_High_King_Svargrim.md) personally stopped the rampage, splitting many of the mating trolls. This event was later known as "The Great Troll Rut".<sup>[[265]](#cite_note-287)</sup>

Most species of arthropod are capable of producing vast amounts of eggs or larvae, with many having specialized castes or queens dedicated to the act of reproduction. [Kwama](Lore_Kwama.md) have huge and bloated [queens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Queen) that produce all of a nest's eggs.<sup>[[266]](#cite_note-miner-288)</sup> Some varieties of [spider](Lore_Spider.md) serve as queens, such as [Web Mothers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Web_Mother) and the [Diamond Spider Queen](Lore_Spider.md#Diamond_Spider_Queen).<sup>[[267]](#cite_note-289)</sup><sup>[[268]](#cite_note-290)</sup> [Bees](Lore_Bee.md) and [wasps](Lore_Wasp.md) are common insects that have queens.<sup>[[269]](#cite_note-291)</sup><sup>[[270]](#cite_note-292)</sup> [Kotu Gava Broodmothers](Lore_Kotu_Gava.md) are capable of rapidly producing spawn to defend themselves.<sup>[[271]](#cite_note-293)</sup> [Yaghra Monstrosities](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yaghra_Monstrosity) are a strange variety of [Yaghra](Lore_Yaghra.md) capable of producing overwhelming amounts of [Larval Yaghra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yaghra_Larva).<sup>[[272]](#cite_note-294)</sup>

Certain arthropods can reproduce parasitically. Giant wasps can reproduce rapidly by injecting eggs into the bodies of other creatures, which quickly emerge as young wasps.<sup>[[273]](#cite_note-295)</sup> [Chaurus](Lore_Chaurus.md) are capable of reproducing in a similar way.<sup>[[274]](#cite_note-296)</sup> Spiders will occasionally use corpses to incubate their eggs.<sup>[[275]](#cite_note-297)</sup><sup>[[276]](#cite_note-298)</sup> [Elytra](Lore_Elytra.md) possess a large thorax that functions as their womb, and is also where an Elytra's [ichor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy_E#Elytra_Ichor) forms. This ichor's purpose is to paralyze a foe, and if the Elytra deems the paralyzed to be a worthy host, she will nest her eggs within the host, sustaining the young with the host's warm flesh.<sup>[[277]](#cite_note-AEL-299)</sup>

Some creatures have multiple possible means of reproduction. [Indriks](Lore_Indrik.md) are often "born" by manifesting in the wild as a result of the occurrence of natural phenomena, without the need for an existing indrik to sire them.<sup>[[278]](#cite_note-Aut In-300)</sup><sup>[[279]](#cite_note-Moss In-301)</sup> Despite this, Indriks are capable of reproducing normally; some Altmer even managed to breed them in captivity during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[280]](#cite_note-Nenulaure-302)</sup> [Mantikoras](Lore_Mantikora.md) are usually created by [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) processes in special spawning pools, but some are created with the ability to breed like normal creatures.<sup>[[281]](#cite_note-303)</sup>

Some animals are known to partake in inbreeding; comparisons to inbred animals are used to insult people.<sup>[[282]](#cite_note-304)</sup><sup>[[283]](#cite_note-305)</sup>

## Dimorphism
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/LO-misc-Mer_Ear_Dimorphism.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Mer_Ear_Dimorphism.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Mer_Ear_Dimorphism.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Mer_Ear_Dimorphism.jpg) Dimorphism in elven ears [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Kagouti_03.jpg) Differences between a Kagouti and a Bull Kagouti"Sexual dimorphism" refers to the phenomenon of individuals of different sexes from the same species developing different physical characteristics, such as the development of breasts in women. Some of the Tamrielic races and animals present these dimorphic characteristics.

Bosmer women are taller than the men on average<sup>[[284]](#cite_note-BIESO-306)</sup> and most elves have differing ear shapes and sizes between sexes. Male ears tend to be bigger than their female counterparts in Altmer, Dunmer and Orcs, with the opposite being true in Bosmer.<sup>[[284]](#cite_note-BIESO-306)</sup><sup>[[285]](#cite_note-DIESO-307)</sup><sup>[[286]](#cite_note-AIESO-308)</sup><sup>[[287]](#cite_note-OIESO-309)</sup>

[Kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md) prominently show dimorphism in both their appearance and mating habits. Male specimens tend to be larger and more colorful, while the females are smaller and duller.<sup>[[288]](#cite_note-Kag IESO-310)</sup> Despite this, females are the dominant sex during mating and often attack their male counterparts.<sup>[[289]](#cite_note-Kag MH-311)</sup> [Netches](Lore_Netch.md) also present similar differences. Male ("bull") netches are larger than the more aggressive female ("betty") netches.<sup>[[290]](#cite_note-Netch MW-312)</sup><sup>[[291]](#cite_note-Netch SR-313)</sup><sup>[[292]](#cite_note-Netch ESO-314)</sup> [Gnats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnat) are an additional example of dimorphism affecting behavior, with females being the only ones capable of biting, and males creating buzzing sounds to attract their mates.<sup>[[293]](#cite_note-Gav-315)</sup>

Some animals use pheromones to communicate with other members of their species. Some, such as the female [dragon frog](Lore_Dragon_Frog.md), use them to attract mates.<sup>[[294]](#cite_note-ESOVelsa-316)</sup> Betty netches and their calves, on the other hand, secrete pheromones when they feel threatened to drive nearby bull netches to aggression.<sup>[[295]](#cite_note-GDRTCC-317)</sup><sup>[[296]](#cite_note-ICESO-318)</sup>

- [Saint Vitache](Lore_Vitache.md), Breton Patron Saint of Millers, Scissor-Grinders, and Bathhouse Attendants could be interpreted as a Sapphic innuendo.<sup>[[297]](#cite_note-319)</sup>
- The [Hero](Lore_Hero.md) known as [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) possibly had sex with their friend, [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md).<sup>[[298]](#cite_note-Darien Gautilaid-320)</sup>
- The [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) perchance bedded the Spider Daedra Perthan Vorn.<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-BSPV-180)</sup>
- Sex was intended to be a mechanic in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* that the player could partake in, either as the result of favors from NPCs or the hiring of prostitutes, which would trigger a cutscene depicting painted art (similar to the [Vampirism cutscene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Vampirism#Gallery)). It was ultimately cut from the final game, though errant strings of dialogue with NPCs regarding this mechanic still remain in the files; [Todd Howard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Todd_Howard) commented that he still has the original art for it in a cabinet, which he said *"was tastefully done of course"*.<sup>[[299]](#cite_note-Team TEStimonies-321)</sup> *[Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire)*, which began as a DLC for Daggerfall, did eventually integrate this mechanic through dialogue with Perthan and Seducers.<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-BSSD-181)</sup>
- | [^](#note_nb1) | In the real world, "rape kit" is a term for a sexual assault forensics exam. However, in the [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind) version of the book *[Withershins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Withershins_(book))*, a "rape kit" is referred to as being used by an (imagined) sexual assaulter. This suggests that in Tamriel, a "rape kit" is instead, a standard set of tools or implements used in sexual assault. The reference was later removed in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*. |
