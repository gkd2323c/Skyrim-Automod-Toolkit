# Recollection

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/ON-npc-Recollection_Soldier.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Recollection_Soldier.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Recollection_Soldier.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Recollection_Soldier.jpg) Two Recollection soldiers The Recollection was a secret [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) cult devoted to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Paths, [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md). They were militant followers of the [Dawnway](Lore_Dawnway.md), having abandoned the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) in favor of this new philosophy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Nantharion-1)</sup>

## History

### Origins
The Recollection can trace its origins to the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md) who fled [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) persecution in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Many of these Ayleid clans were [Daedraphiles](Lore_Daedraphiles.md), who were persecuted by the Alessians for their worship of the Daedric Princes.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CN-3)</sup> A number of [Ayleid city-states](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) worshipped Ithelia as their patron deity.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> However, all knowledge of Ithelia's existence was wiped by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), causing the Ayleids to forget their god.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CG-5)</sup>

Over time, the Ayleid refugees who fled to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) integrated and interbred with the local Bosmer populations until they were eventually fully assimilated. Nevertheless, some Bosmer clans could still trace their roots back to these Cyrodilic ancestors; for example, those with strong Ayleid heritage were noted for being taller than an average Wood Elf.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CN-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup> Many of these individuals retained an inexplicable ancestral memory of Ithelia. To them, it felt like something relating to their Ayleid ancestry was missing from their memory, although they were unable to identify this sense of loss.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CN-3)</sup>

The Recollection was initially formed in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) to uncover the meaning of these missing memories.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg/200px-ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arandros_the_Awakened.jpg) The Recollection within the Oathsworn Pit The Recollection's first move was to invade the Oathsworn Pit, an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) stronghold in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) home to a group of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) devotees known as the Oathsworn. The cultists believed the strong warriors produced by the Oathsworn could be converted to join the Recollection, as both groups felt an "emptiness" plaguing [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). However, the Recollection had confused their own unexplained memories of Ithelia with the Oathsworn's concept of the "Fist of [Malauch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malauch)"; despite subjecting the Orcs to death and torture, they remained stubborn in their devotion to Malacath and refused to join the Recollection.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The cultists were ultimately driven from the Oathsworn Pit by the surviving Oathsworn and a group of [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

The cultists later aided Ithelia's servant [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md) in his invasion of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) in search of answers. Ultimately, this alliance of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) and mortal servants succeeded in freeing Ithelia from her imprisonment in Apocrypha when Torvesard revealed the secret of her existence. With this knowledge restored, Ithelia was freed.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

The Recollection learned of Ithelia in an event dubbed the Day of Remembering. Led by [Nantharion Rayn](Lore_Nantharion.md) of [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md), these "recallers" sought to reclaim their ancestral legacy and restore what was forgotten by conquering the West Weald.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

### The Dawnwood
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/ON-place-Hoperoot.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hoperoot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hoperoot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hoperoot.jpg) Hoperoot, a Recollection stronghold on the edge of the Wildburn In Cyrodiil, the Recollection utilized the destructive power of [Wildburn seeds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wildburn_Seeds) to awaken buried Ayleid magicka in the [Strid Vale](Lore_Strid_Vale.md) region of the West Weald. Almost overnight, a new forest greatly resembling the Valenwood emerged on the opposite side of the Strid.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOSeeds-13)</sup> Nantharion then led a group of peaceful Bosmer settlers from Malabal Tor into this new forest, which he named the Dawnwood. As the self-appointed King of Dawnwood, Nantharion established the town of Vashabar, building it atop the lost Ayleid ruins of Miscarcand.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt CN-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOGambit-14)</sup> Many of the families in Vashabar could trace their bloodlines back to the Ayleids and were followers of the Dawnway. Despite not necessarily being members of the Recollection, followers of the Dawnway believed that their migration into Cyrodiil was a reclaimation of their rightful heritage.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

The Recollection worked in secret to aid Ithelia's Daedric forces with their invasion of the West Weald. They worked alongside [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md) Daedra such as the [Shardborn](Lore_Shardborn.md), attacking settlements such as the Rustwall Estate and Leftwheal Trading Post.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOTMC-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> This Daedric compact was questioned by some members of the Recollection, who viewed the Shardborn as fickle allies.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

Although initially caught off-guard by the threat posed by the cult, the West Weald Legion put bounties on the heads of their leaders.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOTMC-16)</sup> The Legion also noted the zealous devotion many of Nantharion's people displayed towards their king, and feared that Vashabar's holdings would continue to expand into the rest of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> These suspicions were confirmed with the attempted establishment of [Hoperoot](Lore_Hoperoot.md), a fortified Bosmer city that was to serve as a stronghold for the Recollection. The cult planned to use Wildburn seeds to expand the Dawnwood throughout the rest of the West Weald, destroying [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) in the process. Hoperoot was infiltrated and destroyed by agents of Hermaeus Mora before the cultists could achieve these aims, and Nantharion's role was revealed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOSeeds-13)</sup>

### Downfall
After Nantharion's secret identity as the leader of the Recollection was compromised, he unsealed the ruins of [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md) beneath Vashabar in a last-ditch attempt to restore Ithelia's divine power and conquer the West Weald. He was killed within the ruins, ending the threat posed by the Dawnwood's continued expansion.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOGambit-14)</sup>

Following Ithelia's subsequent defeat and banishment to the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), all memory of the Prince was once again wiped from existence.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Despite this, remnants of "Nantharion's movement" continued to linger in some corners of Valenwood.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> The ultimate fate of the Recollection is unknown.
