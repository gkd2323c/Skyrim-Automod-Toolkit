Densil Dufort, known as the **Mad Baron**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho HD-1)</sup> was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman who reigned as the fourth Baron of [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He is remembered as a cruel and depraved ruler who misgoverned the barony.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MBDPt F-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBDPt S-3)</sup> During his reign, he depleted the barony's treasury and caused many of his subjects to flee to other fiefdoms because of excessive taxation.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBDPt S-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MBDPt T-4)</sup>

## Biography

### Life
Densil was the son of Gethmar, the third Baron Dufort, and his wife, Baroness Yoanna. Densil had three siblings, all of whom did not survive to adulthood, and their deaths were considered mysterious. It is said that Densil engineered his siblings' deaths, although this is pure conjecture. The death of Densil's third sibling was too much for Yoanna, shattering her mind. In response, Gethmar had her confined to a tower, where she lived out the rest of her days.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBDPt S-3)</sup>

When Densil reached the age of majority, he was officially declared heir to the barony. One year later, Gethmar died after an arrow buried itself in his shoulder during a hunting trip, a wound that should not have killed him. Historians suspect that there was poison on the arrow's barb, but no evidence of any wrongdoing was discovered at the time.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBDPt S-3)</sup>

With his father's death, Densil became the fourth Baron Dufort. Soon after Densil became baron, he dismissed most of the aides and staff, some of whom had served the Duforts for generations. Work on the knight's wing of Castle Navire, which Gethmar had initiated, ceased. Most knights who had previously found a warm welcome and good companionship within Castle Navire departed for more agreeable environments.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBDPt S-3)</sup>

Densil spent the next several years looting the once overflowing coffers of the barony, taxing his subjects to such an excessive extent that they fled to other fiefdoms. Across the region, land lay fallow, and the Baron and his men rampaged across the countryside.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBDPt S-3)</sup> Within two years of becoming baron, Densil depleted the barony's treasury.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MBDPt T-4)</sup> During his reign, the few ships that were constructed in Navire's once-proud shipyards were of substandard quality.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MBDPt S-3)</sup> Some say that the rivalry between [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) and [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) began because of the actions of Densil, but no evidence exists to verify the claim.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ho HD-1)</sup>

Having depleted the treasury, Densil began searching for a wealthy bride. The Dufort name attracted an ambitious and very wealthy merchant, who agreed to offer up his eldest daughter, Helenna, in marriage. For the marriage, Densil received Helenna's extensive dowry in exchange for the Dufort title. Densil and Helenna were married by proxy and did not meet in person until Helenna arrived at Castle Navire following their proxy marriage.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MBDPt T-4)</sup>

The day she arrived at Castle Navire to meet Densil for the first time, Helenna insisted on meeting Yoanna. Despite the harsh conditions, Yoanna still lived in the tower, and Densil took his bride to meet her mother-in-law. No one knows exactly what happened during this encounter. Helenna later said that upon seeing Densil, Yoanna leaped from her bed and screeched horrifically. Yoanna then grabbed Densil with uncanny strength and hurled both herself and her son out the tower window, plunging to their deaths.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MBDPt T-4)</sup>

### Afterlife
Following his death, Densil's spirit began haunting the Castle Navire Catacombs.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> At some point, Sir Brenvale, the head of the [Order of the Albatross](Lore_Order_of_the_Albatross.md), entered the catacombs to clear them of Densil's spirit.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Brenvale-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Isobel-7)</sup> When Brenvale encountered Densil's spirit, they dueled, and in the end, the Mad Baron killed the knight.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Brenvale-6)</sup> In 2E 582, an adventurer and Isobel Veloise entered the catacombs to retrieve the gilded lute of Brenvale. In the catacombs, they fought and defeated Densil's spirit and retrieved Brenvale's gilded lute.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Tot H-8)</sup>

## Aftermath and Legacy
Densil had no heirs, so a cousin of the Dufort line, Sinvel, became the fifth Baron Dufort. Densil's widow, Helenna, married Sinvel approximately a month later.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MBDPt T-4)</sup>

Densil's cruel and depraved reign remains infamous, despite the best efforts of House Dufort's scholars to erase it. According to legend, Densil would sever the fingers of anyone who displeased him, starting with the smallest finger and working his way up from there. When asked why he did this, Densil said the offender retained use of their hands. Therefore, the offender could still serve the house, but the missing finger—or fingers, in the case of repeat offenders—was a constant reminder of their subservience. Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a trove of very small bones (most likely finger bones) was found buried on the grounds of Castle Navire, lending credence to the legend. As of 2E 582, cottagers around Castle Navire scared their children into obedience with a threat that "the Mad Baron will get your little fingers" if they did not behave.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MBDPt F-2)</sup>
