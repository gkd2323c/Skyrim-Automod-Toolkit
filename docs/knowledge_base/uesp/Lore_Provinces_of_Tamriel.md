Provinces of Tamriel *A brief analysis of the Imperial provinces of Tamriel*
---
[![MW-book-Provinces of Tamriel.jpeg](https://images.uesp.net/f/f7/MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Provinces_of_Tamriel.jpeg)
The [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) encompasses the nine Imperial provinces: [Skyrim](#Skyrim), [High Rock](#High_Rock), [Hammerfell](#Hammerfell), [Summerset Isle](#Summerset_Isle), [Valenwood](#Valenwood), [Elsweyr](#Elsweyr), [Black Marsh](#Black_Marsh), [Morrowind](#Morrowind), and the ancient [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) province itself, [Cyrodiil](#Cyrodiil). Morrowind was among the last of the provinces to be integrated into the Empire, and because it was added by [treaty](Lore_Armistice.md), and not by [conquest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tiber_Wars), Morrowind retains exceptional power to define local law by reference to ancient [Great House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) precedents.

[Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), also known as the Old Kingdom or the Fatherland, was the first region of Tamriel settled by humans from the continent of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md): the hardy, brave, warlike [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), whose descendants still occupy this rugged land. Though more restrained and civilized than their barbarian ancestors, the Nords of the pure blood still excel in the manly virtues of red war and bold exploration.

[Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) is primarily an urban and maritime province, with most of its population confined to the great cities of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) and [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai_(city).md) and to other small ports among the islands and along the coast. The interior is sparsely populated with small poor farms and beastherds. The [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) love of travel, adventure, and the high seas has dispersed them as sailors, mercenaries, and adventurers in ports of call throughout the Empire.

[High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) encompasses the many lands and clans of [Greater Bretony](Lore_Greater_Bretony.md), the [Dellese Isles](Lore_Dellese_Isles.md), the [Bjoulsae River tribes](Lore_River_Horse_Bretons.md), and, by tradition, the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). The rugged highland strongholds and isolated valley settlements have encouraged the fierce independence of the various local [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) clans, and this contentious tribal nature has never been completely integrated into a provincial or Imperial identity. Nonetheless, their language, bardic traditions, and heroic legends are a unifying common legacy.

The [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) is a green and pleasant land of fertile farmlands, woodland parks, and ancient towers and manors. Most settlements are small and isolated, and dominated by ruling seats of the local wizard or warlord. The Isle has few good natural ports, and the natives are unwelcoming to foreigners, so the ancient, chivalric high culture of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) is little affected by modern Imperial mercantilism.

[Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) is a largely uninhabited forest wilderness. The coasts of Valenwood are dominated by mangrove swamps and tropical rain forests, while heavy rainfalls nurture the temperate inland rain forests. The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) live in timber clanhouses at sites scattered along the coast and through the interior, connected only by undeveloped foot trails. The few Imperial roads traverse vast dense woodlands, studded with tiny, widely separated settlements, and carry little trade or traffic of any kind.

The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of the [southern](Lore_Pellitine.md) [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) jungles and river basins are settled city dwellers with ancient mercantile traditions and a stable agrarian aristocracy based on sugarcane and [saltrice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saltrice) plantations. The nomadic tribal Khajiit of the dry [northern wastes and grasslands](Lore_Anequina.md) are, by contrast, aggressive and territorial tribal raiders periodically united under tribal warlords. While the settled south has been quick to adopt Imperial ways, the northern nomadic tribes cling to their warlike barbarian traditions.

Most of the native [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) population of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) is confined to the great inland waterways and impenetrable swamps of the southern interior. There are few roads here, and most travel is by boat. The coasts and the northwestern upland forests are largely uninhabited. For ages the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) have raided Black Marsh for slaves; though the Empire has made this illegal, the practice persists, and Dunmer and Argonians have a long-standing and bitter hatred for one another.

[Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), homeland of the Dunmer peoples, is the northeastmost province of the Tamrielic Empire. Most of the population is gathered in the high uplands and fertile river valleys of central Morrowind, especially around the Inland Sea. The island [Vvardenfel](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* is encircled by the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), and is dominated by the titanic volcano [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) and its associated ash wastelands; most of the island's population is confined to the relatively hospitable west and southwest coast.

[Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) is the cradle of Human Imperial high culture on Tamriel. It is the largest region of the continent, and most is endless jungle. The [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) is in the heartland, the fertile [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley). The densely populated central valley is surrounded by wild rain forests drained by great rivers into the swamps of [Argonia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Argonia) and [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The land rises gradually to the west and sharply to the north. Between its western coast and its central valley are deciduous forests and mangrove swamps.
