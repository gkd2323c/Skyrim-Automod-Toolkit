[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/LO-map-Orsinium_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Orsinium_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Orsinium_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Orsinium_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Orsinium's location in High Rock circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Orsinium (which literally translates to "[Orc](Lore_Orc.md) -Town" in [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmer.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup> also called the **City of Orcs**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Rt O-2)</sup> is the city of the Orsimer and the provincial seat of the region of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), which came to be known as the [Orsinium Area](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orsinium_Area) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). It has been sacked and rebuilt many times over the centuries, as the Orcs are often at odds with their [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) neighbors. Orsinium was first built in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), straddling the boundary between western Wrothgar and [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire). In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the city was relocated to eastern Wrothgar.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup> In [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), it was relocated again to a site between [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), [Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md), and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DF-4)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Orsinium had been moved a third time, farther south to an area between [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRLoading-5)</sup> The royal palace of the city is traditionally known as Scarp Keep.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Scarp Keep-6)</sup>

The exact date of the first Orsinium's founding is unknown, but it is known to have grown powerful as early as the ninth century of the First Era.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WJOTB-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSHR-8)</sup>

## History

### First Era

#### The First Orsinium
It is said after the transformation of the Orsimer deity known as [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) into the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath), the Orcs, who transformed as well, fled to the northern wastes, near [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TTNo O-9)</sup> During the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), after hundreds of Orcs, [ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre), [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), [gremlins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gremlin) and other beastfolk were set free by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) during the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md), they chose to settle in an uninhabited mountain region (the Wrothagrians), described as close to [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), as their people were dependent on a rare, shaggy, giant centipede herd-beast that could only survive at high altitudes. This is where the first Orsinium was founded.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup>

The polity that would become the First Orsinium that the Orcs of later eras hoped to re-establish is usually dated to the tenth century of the First Era, when Chieftain [Torug gro-Igron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torug_gro-Igron) brought the Orcish people together and founded a village in the western [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3O-10)</sup> Though this dating is possibly incorrect, as there was known to be a powerful Orsinium as early as the ninth century,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WJOTB-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSHR-8)</sup> and Torug is said to have died "long before" [1E 950](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_950),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Bell-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TGSOO-12)</sup> The first Orsinium started not as a city but as an armed camp. Eventually, the camp grew into a village, and the village expanded into a town. In just a few short years, it became a sprawling, indefensible mass of people and buildings.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Crankin Ho G-13)</sup> Thanks to the cooperation of several Orcish clans, particularly [Igrun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Igrun_Clan), [Morkul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morkul_Clan), [Shatul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shatul_Clan), and [Tumnosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tumnosh_Clan), the village quickly grew into a great stone city.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup>

The first Orsinium drew the Orcs by its magnificence and Torug's vision of a united Orc nation. They would eventually be joined by Orc clans from other regions, like those who were fleeing [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) due to the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) invasion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup> Warlord [Thulgeg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thulgeg) led a group of Orcs and Goblins from Hammerfell on an exodus to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in [1E 874](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_874) after being driven out of the province by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), eventually leading them northeast through the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains) before they finally reached Orsinium,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSHR-8)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SC-14)</sup> where they helped to continue laying the foundations of the great city.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TM-15)</sup>

#### The Siege of Orsinium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/ON-interior-Old_Orsinium.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Old_Orsinium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Old_Orsinium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Old_Orsinium.jpg) Ruins of the first Orsinium circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Old_Orsinium.jpg) Old Orsinium (concept art) King [Golkarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golkarr) of the Orcs amassed an army and was determined to seize control of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md),<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup> upon which the nomadic Breton [Bjoulsae River Tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bjoulsae_River_Tribes) lived.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> They intended to force Wayrest to pay to regularly use the river for travel and trade.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup> Naturally, this did not settle well with their neighboring kingdoms, especially the Kingdom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md). In [1E 948](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_948), [King Joile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Joile) of Daggerfall sent a letter to [Gaiden Shinji](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gaiden_Shinji) of the [Order of Diagna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Diagna), which proposed a joint attack on Orsinium. Agreeing to provide aid, an alliance was formed between the Order of Diagna, the armies of Daggerfall (High Rock), and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) (Hammerfell). The Bjoulsae River Tribes also agreed to lend their aid.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONABS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-19)</sup> The armies amassed and began a campaign for the destruction of Orsinium.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WJOTB-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3O-10)</sup> Two years later, in [1E 950](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_950) the siege began.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-WOBN-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TPC-21)</sup> [Guthrag Stone-Talker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guthrag_Stone-Talker)'s diplomatic prowess was acknowledged as one of the factors in postponing the siege for more than a decade.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GM-22)</sup>

During the 30-year campaign, which became widely known as the "Siege of Orsinium", the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) armies of Daggerfall, the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) soldiers of Sentinel, and the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) [Order of Diagna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Diagna) invaded the Orc homeland. The Orcs gave much resistance and proved themselves to be fearsome and brutal warriors, just as they did in the past. However, King [Joile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Joile) of Daggerfall already planned to betray the Redguards and invade Hammerfell after having destroyed Orsinium. He and his [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) general, [Mercedene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mercedene) of the [Winterborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterborn), tricked the Orcish hero [Baloth Bloodtusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baloth_Bloodtusk) and his Savage Sons, who initially did not take part in the war, to join King Golkarr of Orsinium. Afterwards, he manipulated the leader of the Order of Diagna, [Gaiden Shinji](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gaiden_Shinji), into having a duel with Bloodtusk over Orsinium's fate. During the duel, Mercedene ordered Joile's archers to kill both combatants, effectively removing the most powerful warriors of both Orsinium and the Order of Diagna for Joile.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup> In [1E 973](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_973), with Orsinium's defeat still years away, King Joile launched his invasion of Hammerfell, but he was defeated and killed at the [Bangkorai Pass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Pass) by the [Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ansei) warrior [Makela Leki](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Makela_Leki).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Ft MSo ML-23)</sup>

The Siege of Orsinium finally ended in [1E 980](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_980) when the allied forces breached the city's defenses, overran it, and razed it to the ground. Despite this loss, it did not mark the end of either the Orcs or the dream of Orsinium.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WJOTB-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3O-10)</sup> The city was rebuilt in the same location and razed again at least two more times over the following centuries.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONLoad-24)</sup> Certain accounts suggest that the Redguard victory was attributed to the sacrifice of Armiger [Rhina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rhina) from the Order of Diagna.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Ramati-25)</sup>

The Bretons experienced a period of economic expansion after they razed Orsinium, for the Bjoulsae River region was greatly opened up for trade. The kingdom of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) was founded by the Bjoulsae River Tribes,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-19)</sup> and Wayrest also established a presence on both banks of the region. The Masconian Trade Way was formed by the hard-working traders and merchants of the Bjoulsae River. Not only did it bring about a decline in piracy, it also led to a great expansion of the Bjoulsae's trade and community.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WJOTB-7)</sup> One such factor that contributed to this was Daggerfall's [ivory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ivory) trade, which originated with ivory plundered from Orsinium following its sacking.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOIvory-26)</sup>

The artifact [Chrysamere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chrysamere) was present in the first sacking of Orsinium.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LAIA-27)</sup>

Several sources contradict the most commonly accepted date of the siege of the first Orsinium. The Direnni claim to have launched the first sacking of Orsinium by [Ryain Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ryain_Direnni) with the help of his Breton legions,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Once-28)</sup> and he was a figure who was active around the fifth century of the First Era.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> The epic known as *[Poison Song](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Poison_Song)* takes place around [1E 675](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_675), and has Gaiden Shinji and King Joile as participants in the siege but as an event that happened in the past.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup> At least one source has Gaiden Shinji's most famous quote dated to [1E 490](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_490).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRLoading-5)</sup>

### Second Era

#### From Imperial Recognition to Dispersal
Orsinium was accepted as a province of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) during the reign of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-RM8TO-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-HKEAYQ-32)</sup> During that brief period, [Frostbreak Fortress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostbreak_Fortress) was built to help defend it.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONLoad-24)</sup> After the assassination of Savirien-Chorak in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), Orsinium lost its status and protection as an Imperial territory, resulting in another sacking of the city in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), an assault which was spearheaded by the Bretons of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TFo KR-33)</sup>

Not content to simply destroy Orsinium this time, the Bretons also apparently seized control of the land and scattered the Orcs across northern Tamriel.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-OOS-35)</sup> Some Orcs chose to retreat to their strongholds in northern Wrothgar, while others set out to reassert ancient territorial claims in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Chief [Yashnag gro-Yazgu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yashnag_gro-Yazgu) in particular succeeded in carving out a chiefdom in western [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) that persisted until [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467), when Yashnag and his champions were slain by [Hakkvild Yashnag-Slayer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hakkvild_Yashnag-Slayer), the Jarl of Falkreath, in a ritual of single-combat.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-OOS-35)</sup>

#### King Kurog's Orsinium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Orsinium_02.jpg) King Kurog's Orsinium circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) When King [Ranser](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ranser) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) attacked [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Wayrest sent emissaries into [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) and promised to return Orsinium to the Orcs in exchange for helping him defeat Ranser. The clan of [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog) answered his call and helped annihilate Ranser's forces at Markwasten Moor. These events culminated in the formation of the second [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and Orsinium, now ruled by King Kurog, became a part of it.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TFo KR-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Gtt DC-34)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TCOKK-36)</sup> Fully aware of the long history of animosity the Bretons and Redguards had harbored against the Orcs, however, Kurog ensured that the terms of the alliance forbade any army from High Rock or Hammerfell from entering Wrothgar under any circumstances.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup>

Kurog chose to rebuild the city in another location: in the mountains of eastern Wrothgar, near the border with [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The abandoned ruins of the first Orsinium site became known as Old Orsinium. A poetry joust was held there in [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Mort King Julien-37)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), he sent invitations to the mightiest warriors of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to come and help him build the united Orc nation. One of the warriors who came was the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), who helped Kurog drive the last of the [Winterborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterborn) out of Wrothgar. However, Kurog, angered by the constant discord between the clan chiefs, and manipulated by his mother Alga, reached for desperate measures. He orchestrated an attack of [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) cultists on the temple of [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac) in his city, and pinned the blame on [Bazrag gro-Fharun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bazrag_gro-Fharun), chief of a Malacath-worshipping clan and Kurog's political opponent. Simultaneously, he commanded [Vosh Rakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vosh_Rakh), a brutal and violent cult of Trinimac who were led by his mother Alga, to attack [Fharun Stronghold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fharun_Stronghold) and tried to imprison Bazrag. He also made the Vestige convince the chiefs of Morkul, Shatul and Tumnosh clans to attend his moot, where he planned to murder them. The Vestige eventually learned about Kurog's treachery and sided with Bazrag against the Orc king. Together, they rescued some of the chiefs at the moot and killed Kurog and Alga.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup> The renowned tailor [Borzighu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Borzighu), whose clothing was popular among Orcs from all around Tamriel, hailed from the city.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SLSR-38)</sup>

Afterwards, Bazrag was crowned the new king of Orsinium. He promised to continue working on Kurog's vision of a united Orsimer people equal to the other races, while also trying to retain peace and not force this vision on anyone. Since Orsinium's membership in the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) was based solely on the agreement between Kurog and [High King Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), the kingdom considered leaving the Covenant with the passing of Kurog. It is unknown which decision was reached.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/93/DF-place-Orsinium.png/200px-DF-place-Orsinium.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Orsinium.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Orsinium.png) The stronghold of Orsinium circa [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417)
#### Nova Orsinium
At some point later, the Orcs again lost control of Orsinium. The land was then won back by the Orc hero [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gortwog) of the [Minat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minat) tribe in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), who was disputing claim on the land, made by the Breton noble Lord Bowyn. Gortwog won the land by defeating Lord Bowyn in a duel.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-HOPtt O-39)</sup> Named **Nova Orsinium**,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-40)</sup> the city was rebuilt on a mountainside between [Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md) and Wayrest, at the site of Torug's original Orsinium.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DF-4)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3O-10)</sup> As the city harbored statues of Orc heroes such as Mauloch and Torug, and was built of iron, it was likely that Nova Orsinium would provoke the same reaction from its neighboring kingdoms as the first city did. However, the land prospered, as Gortwog proved to be a diplomatic as well as a political genius. Following the events of the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Gortwog used the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) to conquer substantial territory of central High Rock. Its application for elevation to Provincial status, which was proposed to [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) at the founding of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), was finally beginning to be reviewed by [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md). Orsinium's alliance with its former foe Wayrest gave promise for a stable future and a lasting government.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3O-10)</sup> Gortwog sent out many adventurers to explore the dungeons of Tamriel in search of treasure, to prove the wealth of the Orcs.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-41)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/ON-place-Valley_of_Scars.jpg/200px-ON-place-Valley_of_Scars.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Valley_of_Scars.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Valley_of_Scars.jpg) An Orc stronghold in Craglorn during the Interregnum, located on the border with Skyrim, roughly where the Fourth Orsinium was later found
### Fourth Era
In the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) and the destabilization of the Third Empire, Orsinium was once again sacked by the combined forces of Hammerfell and High Rock, and many Orc refugees fled east into [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Lo S-42)</sup> The kingdom eventually reformed at a new location between Skyrim and Hammerfell,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRLoading-5)</sup> where it was guarded by the [Seventh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seventh_Legion) and Fifteenth [Legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md#Known_Legions).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Lo S-42)</sup> Parts of this region have historically been controlled by [Iron Orcs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Orcs), notably the Valley of Scars in [Upper Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn),<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TTB-43)</sup> but it is unknown if they still inhabited the region by the Fourth Era.

## Known Rulers
- [Torug gro-Igron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torug_gro-Igron) (the tenth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md))
- [Golkarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golkarr) (until ca. [1E 980](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_980))
- [Kurog gro-Orsinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog) (sometime after [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566) - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
- [Bazrag gro-Fharun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bazrag_gro-Fharun) ([2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
- [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gortwog) (the fifth century of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md))

Additionally, the Orcish figure [Nargi gro-Barok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nargi_gro-Barok) was known by the title of High King (usually reserved only for rulers of Orsinium) and was described as a conquering Orc king, but it is unspecified if he had any connection to Orsinium or when his reign occurred.
