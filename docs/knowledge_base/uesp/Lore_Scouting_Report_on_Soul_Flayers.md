Scouting Report on Soul Flayers **by [Scout Zirpar Faram](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Scout_Zirpar_Faram)** *Observations about the capabilities of the Soul Flayers* *
---
Report to Seline Jerick of the Stirk Fellowship, from Scout Zirpar Faram

For immediate review and action!

In my reconnaissance of [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) activity in eastern [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md), I have previously mentioned cultist detachments moving larger devices similar to soul reapers through the area and setting them up in abandoned and captured Stone-Nest villages. What I have discovered in my observations is almost too horrible to put into words!

These devices share many properties of the soul reapers in that they gather up the [souls](Lore_Soul.md) of the newly deceased. However, these new soul flayers, as I have heard the worms refer to them, now emit [necrotic energy](Lore_Necromancy.md) to kill everything within their immediate vicinity. They both slay targets and then harvest their souls!

I witnessed the cultists capture a village of Stone-Nest [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and place captives in cages before assembling one of these devices. The cultists set what I could only assume was a timer of some sort and retreated to a safe distance away. After a short period, the item emitted a burst of necrotic energy, and the Argonians collapsed and died. The soul flayer then seemed to draw energy from the dead into itself, what I can only assume was the harvesting of their souls.

After a short time, the remaining cultists (carefully) returned to the area and double checked to make sure the device was no longer active. I learned later from a captured cultist, that these devices could be set to operate continually, killing living things and gathering their souls persistently within a short radius. The thought is frightening and presents a great danger to our troops.

Send a warning to all of our units in the field to not approach these devices, as I have not yet determined a way to safely tell if they are active or not.
