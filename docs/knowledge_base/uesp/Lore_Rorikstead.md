[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg/200px-SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg) Rorikstead circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Rorikstead (also known as **Rorik's Steading**, or **Rorikhofkah** in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md)) is a small settlement in the Western Plains of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HOJG-1)</sup> Some variant of the name appears in many old records, with the earliest dated mention of it being in [2E 373](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_373),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AOD-2)</sup> and another text placing it as possibly existing during the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) or [First](Lore_First_Era.md) Era.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HOJG-1)</sup> The traditional song of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md), "Ragnar the Red", refers to an "ole Rorikstead".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SOSR-3)</sup>

During the Skyrim Schism, Rorikstead and the rest of Whiterun Hold were part of Western Skyrim and owed fealty to the High King based in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). In [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Schick2015-4)</sup> the wife and daughter of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md)'s Jarl [Hjurgol Skjoralmor](Lore_Hjurgol_Skjoralmor.md) stayed in the town on their journey to High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md)'s coronation in Solitude, and were ambushed by a [giantess](Lore_Giant.md)' herd of [mammoths](Lore_Mammoth.md) shortly after.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Mt CES-5)</sup> The twin brothers Roggvir and Fenrig hailed from the town and were called its protectors around [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KC-6)</sup> Trade was still occurring between [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and Rorikstead circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), despite owing allegiances to opposing High Kings.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WSM-7)</sup>

The land around the settlement was purportedly barren around the time of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md). After the war, a local nobleman named Rorik purchased the land. He claims to have founded the settlement, and that it hasn't had a bad crop since.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRRorik-8)</sup> The local farmers make a living by trading their produce to merchants in Whiterun.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-9)</sup> The [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) Nahagliiv, who was buried in a mound west of the settlement around the time of the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), was resurrected in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AOD-2)</sup>

## Notes
- [Rorik's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rorik) claim that he founded the settlement is hard to reconcile with the historical record, though it is possible Rorikstead was abandoned and later re-founded. However, this explanation doesn't account for the settlement's name being based on his.
- The village of **Lainalten** [appears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Lainalten) in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* at the exact same area where Rorikstead is presently located, though it is unclear if they are intended to be the same settlement.
- Concept maps reveal an early name for this location was **Hroffbulg**.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Todd Dice-10)</sup>
