# Corrupted Shade

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/SR-creature-Corrupted_Shade.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Corrupted_Shade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Corrupted_Shade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Corrupted_Shade.jpg) Corrupted Shade (Stormcloak) **Corrupted Shades** are a variety of skeleton which has more ghostly qualities than the [standard skeleton](Skyrim_Skeleton.md). All versions have a 25% chance of dropping one sample of [bone meal](Skyrim_Bone_Meal.md). Interestingly, they share none of the resistances to frost or poison that other undead do.

In the base game, corrupted shades are only found in the [Kilkreath Ruins](Skyrim_Kilkreath_Ruins.md). They have been created by [Malkoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Malkoran) from the casualties of the ongoing [war](Skyrim_Civil_War.md). They can have [Stormcloak](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md) or [Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md) model types, and have leveled [souls](Skyrim_Souls.md).

If [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) is installed, a distinct new version appears in [Vahlok's Tomb](Skyrim_Vahlok%27s_Tomb.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>. They are weaker than the other types, and have petty souls.

## Notes
- Despite its similar appearance, [Malkoran's Shade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Malkoran%27s_Shade) is technically considered to be a [Dragon Priest](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md).
- Corrupted Shades can be [Paralyzed](Skyrim_Paralysis.md).
- Imperial desecrated corpses are all clad in heavy armor, with the shades sporting the officer helmet. It seems that early in development, this was the standard Imperial soldier outfit. This was obviously changed later on, but this remnant of it still remains.

## Bugs
- At close range the bow variant will put away its bow and not pull out a close range weapon, although it has its arms raised it will not attack, essentially allowing you to kill it.
