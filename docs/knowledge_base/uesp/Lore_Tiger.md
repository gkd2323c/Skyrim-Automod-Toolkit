*Relentless and strong, the Tiger stalks its prey without flagging and overpowers it without hesitation when the moment is right.* The Tiger's prey is the Snake. To be like the Tiger, you must hunt its prey. *
— [Brackenleaf](Lore_Brackenleaf.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>* Tigers are large, predatory [felines](Lore_Cat.md) found mainly in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Senche-Tiger-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AM-3)</sup> as well as on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Senche-Tiger-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sol Tiger-4)</sup> and historically in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TA-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tiger Myth-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-WWPD-7)</sup> They are known for their distinctive stripes.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Body-8)</sup> A female tiger is known as a **Tigress**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Gress-9)</sup>

Many of the cats known as tigers in southern Tamriel are actually a type of [Senche-Cat](Lore_Senche-Cat.md), known as a **Senche-Tiger** or **Senche-Tigress**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Senche-Tiger-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Gress-9)</sup> differentiated by their larger size.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Tuttle-10)</sup> The two species may often be confused however due to their similar appearance.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sol Tiger-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Tuttle-10)</sup> Senche-Tiger as a term has also been used to generally refer to any type of Senche-Cat,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Kyne C-11)</sup><sup>:155-156</sup> or to the [Senche](Lore_Senche_(furstock).md) or [Senche-raht](Lore_Senche-raht.md) furstocks of [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1E-12)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RDKMW-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TIC-14)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOPacrooti-16)</sup>

Many Khajiit have tiger-like fur patterns,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Body-8)</sup> with [Pahmar](Lore_Pahmar.md) and [Pahmar-raht](Lore_Pahmar-raht.md) being known to particularly ressemble tigers and senche-tigers.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Bahzaharthe Bull-17)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-IWTB-18)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Survival G-19)</sup> The [Ka Po' Tun](Lore_Ka_Po%27_Tun_(race).md) are described as a tiger-like race of cat-folk.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MA-20)</sup> Some [Sabre Cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md) resemble tigers so may be known as [sabertooth tigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sabertooth_Tiger).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

## Variants
Striped Senche-Tigers are a semi-domesticated version of the wild senche-tiger with golden colored fur.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONStriped Senche-Tiger-22)</sup> Tenmar Senche-Tigers prowl in the [Tenmar forest](Lore_Tenmar_Forest.md), where they are camouflaged by the shadows of the canopy. They have a brown striped pattern and are also used as mounts.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONTenmar Senche-Tiger-23)</sup> Moonlight Senche-Tigers are white senche-tigers described as sleek and silvery.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONMoonlight Senche-Tiger-24)</sup>

## Uses and Culture

### Domestication
Senche-Tigers are often raised for use as mounts and pets by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), who often keep them placated with a diet of [Moon Sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Sugarbelly-25)</sup> They are typically bred for size and manageability.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONStriped Senche-Tiger-22)</sup> Moonlight Senche-Tigers have been used as guardians for [Khajiiti temples](Lore_Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance.md) dedicated to the [moons](Lore_Moons.md) ever since the revelation of the [Riddle'Thar](Lore_Riddle%27Thar.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONMoonlight Senche-Tiger-24)</sup>

When the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) ruled Cyrodiil, the cruel [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [Fire King Hadhuul](Lore_Hadhuul.md) was known to set slave children aflame for "nighttime tiger sport".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TA-5)</sup>

### Uses
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b2/BS-level2b-Point_G.jpg/200px-BS-level2b-Point_G.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level2b-Point_G.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level2b-Point_G.jpg) A tiger-skin rug (Battlespire) There are several [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) myths associated with the parts of senche-tigers. Many [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) believe their bones contain almost magical properties when crafted into arrows. The [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) even import powdered claws and teeth for use in concoctions that claim to do everything from extending one's life to instilling good luck. While senche-tiger claws do possess some modest beneficial properties, many of the claims about their capabilities are said to be dubious.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AM-3)</sup>

Tiger-skin rugs are often used a decoration.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-White Rug-27)</sup> Solstice tigers are often hunted, with their leather being used to make things like water pouches,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-28)</sup> and their guts used to string [Corelanyan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) lutes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sol Tiger-4)</sup>

### Art, Religion, and Influence
*[The Water-getting Girl and the Inverse Tiger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Water-getting_Girl_and_the_Inverse_Tiger)* is a popular Cyrodilic children's story and was said to be a favorite of the young [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tiger Myth-6)</sup> Tiber grew to have a fascination with the beasts, so when he developed a particular fondness for [guar](Lore_Guar.md) during one of his various tours of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Septim, he took to calling them "tigers". This was due to the [species](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tiger_Guar) found on the [Deeshan Plain](Lore_Deshaan_Plain.md) being a striped variety, and because Tiber struggled with the [Dunmer language](Lore_Dunmer_Language.md). Many Imperial Legionnaires and even [Dres](Lore_Dres.md) slavers still refer to their cattle guar as tigers in honor of this.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MK-29)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tiger Myth-6)</sup> Caker King, a figure from the esoteric *[Magne-Ge Pantheon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Magne-Ge_Pantheon)*, is described as taking on a tiger aspect in the days when Cyrodiil had jungles and savannahs.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Magne-30)</sup>

The [Tiger God](Lore_Tiger_God.md) was an obscure figure once worshipped by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Bantha](Lore_Bantha.md) on the 14th of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md). It was speculated that the [Feast of the Tiger](Lore_Feast_of_the_Tiger.md), held on this day, may have originally been a religious observance in honor of the Tiger God, rather than a harvest thanksgiving. By the time of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), however, the feast had evolved into a more festive occasion, still celebrated in the rainforest, but now resembling other harvest festivals. It was not a solemn time of reflection and gratitude, but a lively celebration marked by grand festivities across villages.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-DH-31)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-HOTIB-32)</sup>
