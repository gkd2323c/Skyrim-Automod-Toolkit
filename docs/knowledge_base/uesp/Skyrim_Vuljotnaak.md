[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/SR-creature-Vuljotnaak_02.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Vuljotnaak_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Vuljotnaak_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Vuljotnaak_02.jpg) Vuljotnaak roaring [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/SR-creature-Vuljotnaak.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Vuljotnaak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Vuljotnaak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Vuljotnaak.jpg) Vuljotnaak at his dragon mound **Vuljotnaak** is a [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md) which can be found flying around its [burial site](Skyrim_Dragon_Mounds.md). Although not marked, the site can be found northeast of [Sunderstone Gorge](Skyrim_Sunderstone_Gorge.md) and southwest of [Sleeping Tree Camp](Skyrim_Sleeping_Tree_Camp.md) after your first confrontation with [Alduin](Skyrim_Alduin.md). He has been observed using either [frost](Skyrim_Frost_Breath.md) or [fire breath](Skyrim_Fire_Breath.md) as means of offense.

If the mound is visited after starting "Diplomatic Immunity" but before starting "A Cornered Rat", you can witness Alduin resurrecting and speaking with Vuljotnaak before the latter attacks you.

Vul Jot Naak means "Dark Maw Eat" in the [dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

## Notes
- He has the same dialogue as other named dragons who have yet to be resurrected from a burial mound by [Alduin](Skyrim_Alduin.md). ("Speak, if you have aught to say worth the listening.")
- During the time that [Diplomatic Immunity](Skyrim_Diplomatic_Immunity.md) is an active quest (after being briefed by Delphine of her plan), you can see Vuljotnaak being resurrected by Alduin.
- It is mentioned in the book *[Atlas of Dragons](Skyrim_Atlas_of_Dragons.md)* that Vuljotnaak was killed during or soon after the Dragon War.
- It is possible to find Vuljotnaak resurrected but simply slumbering in his mound.
- The level of Vuljotnaak is set when you talk to Delphine in Sleeping Giant Inn after Sahloknir is killed in A Blade in the Dark.
- According to Alduin's own pronunciation, the "j" in Vuljotnaak's name is pronounced the same as in "Jon" and "J'zargo", instead of the consonant sound in "Bromjunaar"and "jarl".
