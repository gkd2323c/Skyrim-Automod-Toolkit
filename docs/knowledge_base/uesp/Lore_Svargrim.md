Svargrim, known as the **Wolf of Solitude**, was the High King of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) and Jarl of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He was crowned High King in [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Schick2015-1)</sup> and was still in power around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-JJ-2)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt C:ES-3)</sup> He was married to Queen Gerhyld, with whom he sired a single daughter, Princess [Svana](Lore_Svana.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Mt C:PS-4)</sup> He was a descendant of High King [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md), whose opposition to Queen [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md) had been responsible for Skyrim's split into [Eastern](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) and Western Kingdoms.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

Under Svargrim's rule, Western Skyrim was made up of the holds of [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Gt WSH-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WSSO-7)</sup> [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt C:ES-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WSSO-7)</sup> [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mt C:ES-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WSSO-7)</sup> [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Gt WSHj-8)</sup> and [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gt WSK-9)</sup> At some point, Svargrim forced the widower Jakolar out of Solitude, as Jakolar wanted to resurrect his deceased wife through unconventional and seemingly pointless means.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-JJ-2)</sup> As of 2E 582, though Svargrim was growing older, he was still hale and showed no desire to give up the throne.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Schick2015-1)</sup>

Svargrim was deeply suspicious of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) and its agents. He was a paranoid man who feared [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Svana DCOS-10)</sup> He was quick to side with the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) when they offered him the power to deal with this perceived threat to Western Skyrim, and became a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) lord. He was savvy enough to hide this and had the means of disguising it well enough that other vampires couldn't even sense it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Fenn-11)</sup> He hindered the efforts to stop the Gray Host by cutting off their opposition's supply of Arkay's Oil, which was needed to make potions that would protect against the [harrowstorms](Lore_Harrowstorms.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOTGH-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOGR-13)</sup>

After he revealed himself as a vampire, he retreated to the [Tower of the Wolf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Tower_of_the_Wolf), a structure he had constructed which connected straight to [Greymoor Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greymoor_Keep). The keep itself had been converted to act as a massive witch pike. [Rada al-Saran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rada_al-Saran) gave Svargrim the power of the harrowstorm, and Svargrim attempted to unleash the largest harrowstorm yet upon Solitude. Princess Svana and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) confronted King Svargrim and managed to defeat him before he could devastate the realm.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOGR-13)</sup>
