*13th in the line of Ysgramor and founder of the great Kingdom of Skyrim, where he established fair Windhelm as his capital.*
—The [Memorial Plaque](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md#Courtyard) in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) High King Harald (also called **Harald Hand-Free**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWEnnbjof-1)</sup> and **Harald of Ysgramoor**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KEX-2)</sup> was the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the thirteenth monarch descended from [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md). Harald was the first historical [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) ruler, appearing at the beginning of recorded history. He is mainly remembered for relinquishing all holdings in distant and lost [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) and uniting the disparate Nordic people to formally create the Kingdom of Skyrim. Harald's newfound power gave the [human](Lore_Men.md) races the inspiration to expand their reach across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and assert themselves over the dominance of [elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elves).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/SR-place-Lake_Honrich.jpg/200px-SR-place-Lake_Honrich.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Lake_Honrich.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Lake_Honrich.jpg) Lake Honrich Harald was born in the early years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), in [1E 113](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_113)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Birth)</sup> he was the descendant of the fabled mythic hero, Ysgramor the Harbinger and member of the [Ysgramor Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ysgramor_Clan). In fact, he was the 13th in line as the leader of Skyrim,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup> preceded by many figures whose names have yet to be known and their existence is speculated at best.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FC-4)</sup> Before he ascended the High Kingship, Lord<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Lord)</sup> Harald led a campaign to drive out the remaining [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) in Skyrim. In [1E 139](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_139), the Nordic forces led by Skorm Snow-Strider push back the elves from across [Lake Honrich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Honrich) in the 13th of Sun's Dusk. However, the Dwarves of Skyrim remained strong against Nordic aggression, and they took in most of the remaining Snow Elves as slaves, forcefully blinding them.

The army eventually made their way to [Forelhost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forelhost) where they discovered a secret enclave of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md), still active by the 27th. The siege lasted for several days and there was little progress. A request was even sent to Harald for [tongues](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tongues) available to breakdown the fortress. The siege ended in [1E 140](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_140) on the 6th of Morning Star after the cult committed mass suicide and the Dragon Priest, [Rahgot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rahgot) was entombed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SSSJ-5)</sup>

### The Consolidation of Skyrim
Harald became the High King of Skyrim in [1E 143](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_143).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-6)</sup><sup>:5</sup> His ascension to the throne proved to be pivotal to the history of man on Tamriel. He relinquished the last remaining holds in Atmora and centralized the region into a singular state, the Kingdom of Skyrim. The Nords expanded their reach further across the land and the line between the man and the mer became much more clear.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup> The newly-founded Skyrim had began to expand with the military might.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FC-4)</sup> Harald was the figurehead of the new age of man and felt that his people must adapt from their Atmoran roots while embracing them. He sought to create a more uniform army of warriors, but even tales of grandeur were created from this time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMotif Anti-7)</sup> He commissioned every craftsworker to create new weapons and armor for the first army of Nords. This first domestic time of peace meant that [ash](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash) palisades and wooden fortifications were torn down for recurve bows and arrows.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CM87AN-8)</sup>

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Jagged_Crown.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Jagged_Crown.png) The Jagged Crown In the early years of his reign, he was confronted by one of Skyrim's greatest heroes at the time, Randagulf of Clan Begalin. He defeated the warrior and took his [fists](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fists_of_Randagulf), both as a trophy and out of respect for the hero.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TL-9)</sup> According to some sources, the [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md) was forged by Harald himself, using the bones and teeth of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) to create a crown that symbolized his newfound power.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TCOF-10)</sup> Although it is unclear if he actually made it, or that it preceded even him.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRGalmar-11)</sup> There have been many things introduced in Harald's reign that have been used in modern-day Skyrim. Sometime around [1E 184](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_184), he hosted the very first [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) in his mead hall in Windhelm.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONGirdle Anti-12)</sup> It remained in effect for many years until it was essentially partisan and ineffective by the time of [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md)' reign.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup>

### Arch-Mage Geirmund & The Gauldurson Brothers
At some point in his reign, his kingdom was under threat when [Gauldur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gauldur), an esteemed Arch-Mage, was killed by his sons Jyrik, Mikrul, and Sigdis, who stole his prized [amulet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gauldur_Amulet) and wreaked havoc across the countryside. King Harald sent the kingdom's greatest warriors to chase these three brothers and destroy them, including his personal battlemage, the Arch-Mage of Windhelm, [Geirmund](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Geirmund). The Clever Man pursued them across the land and managed to execute each of them. He first cornered the youngest, Mikrul at the old barrow of [Folgunthur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Folgunthur), where he sealed him deep in the tomb. The oldest, Jyrik was trapped in the half-buried city of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md). An epic battle ensued between Jyrik Gauldurson's elemental magic and ten master wizards, but he eventually fell into the inescapable abyss. In the end, Geirmund confronted the middle child, Sigdis on an island near the small town, [Ivarstead](Lore_Ivarstead.md). Sigdis challenged the Arch-Mage to a duel of iron might, clashing in equal strength until they fell together. Harald ordered a tomb built in the middle of the [lake](Lore_Lake_Geir.md), where Geirmund would guard Sigdis' tomb for eternity.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DVN-13)</sup>

In the end, Gauldur was buried in a hidden tomb somewhere in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), not far from his own tower. King Harald issued a royal edict in which the Arch-Mage and his sons were stricken out of any sort of records, essentially erased from history. Anyone that would speak about the incident or even attempt to recover the Gauldur Amulet would be put under death.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DVN-13)</sup> Despite this, Gauldur's story had been preserved in some form through scarce sources, but even in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), no [Skald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skald) or Bard would dare speak about this lost legend.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LL-14)</sup>

### Later Years & After his Reign
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Iron Helmet.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Iron Helmet.png) The Horned Helmet aesthetic was revolutionized by Harald's armies [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg/200px-MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Skyrim_Anthology_Map.jpg) Map of Skyrim (4E 182) Harald had lived for an unexpectedly-long time, dying at the earnest age of a hundred and eight and outliving his children, with the exception of [Hjalmer](Lore_Hjalmer.md), [Olmgerd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Olmgerd), and [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup> When time came for another heir to claim the throne, it was passed onto his eldest son, Hjalmer. However, his reign lasted a year and it was soon passed to his second child, Vrage the Gifted.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TDC-6)</sup><sup>:5</sup> Vrage's reign over Skyrim further expanded on Harald's own innovations and soon the northern realms of Tamriel were under his command. It was said that Harald's sons dominated the northern coast of what was then called [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md). His bastard son, Olmgerd the Outlaw, partook in the [Conquest of Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conquest_of_Morrowind) and later died in battle. He was buried in the undersea cavern of Tukushapal and given the burial that befits a son of Harald.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWEnnbjof-1)</sup> The time in which Vrage passed away is currently unknown, but his empire would continue to thrive on until its demise in the fifth century of the First Era.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup> His heirs had been harassed by the God of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), [Mauloch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mauloch) for many years until his defeat at the Battle of Dragon Wall sometime around [1E 660](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_660), concurrent with [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-VOFTN-15)</sup>

Harald's fixation on improving Nordic arms served as the inspiration for the modern-day weapons and armor of Skyrim. He had placed an emphasis on practicality when it comes to the equipment. The leg greaves for example, were designed proportionally, unlike what was seen in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) at the time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMotif Anti-7)</sup> The belts were given the insignia of the wearer's clan sigil, but Harald had to approve every single one of them<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CM87AN-8)</sup> and some did not appeal to him. There were family rivalries fueled during this time.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMotif Anti-7)</sup> The shields were designed to be thick but light, taking inspiration from Atmoran longboats.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CM87AN-8)</sup> They were made to be buoyant to expel fears of drowning and were said to even break elven cuirass with full force.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMotif Anti-7)</sup> Harald's army uniform was the one to make the horned Nordic helm style part of Nordic culture. However, the best sized horns had yet to be determined at the time.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CM87AN-8)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Birth) | Some sources apparently conflate Harald's "reign" with his entire life. *[Frontier, Conquest](Lore_Frontier,_Conquest.md)* accredits the years 1E 113 to 221 to him (108 years). Both *[the Daggerfall Chronicles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles)* and the *[1st Edition Pocket Guide](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Skyrim.md)* state that he died at the age of 108, placing his birth at 1E 113. The *[3rd Edition Pocket Guide](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition_Skyrim.md)* states that by 1E 113, "the entirety of modern Skyrim was under the reign of King Harald", suggesting his birth and reign coincided. However, a [memorial plaque](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md#Courtyard) in Windhelm confirms that his reign began in 1E 143, when he was about 30 years old. |
| --- | --- |
- | [^](#note_Lord) | *[Skorm Snow-Strider's Journal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skorm_Snow-Strider%27s_Journal)* indicates "Lord Harald" was in a leadership position, likely as a Jarl, in 1E 139, before he became High King. According to *[Geirmund's Epitaph](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Geirmund%27s_Epitaph)*, Harald's personal battlemage was Archmage of Windhelm, and since Harald was a direct descendant of Ysgramor, who reigned from Windhelm, it is probable Harald was Jarl of Windhelm before becoming High King. |
| --- | --- |
- Harald Hand-Free is probably a reference to Harald Bluetooth, a real life king who unified Denmark in the 10th century. In 1997, the short-range wireless specification Bluetooth was named after the king, with the aspiration that it would unify devices the same way Harald had unified the Danes. At the time of Morrowind's release, handsfree headsets utilizing Bluetooth technology were a new and exciting technology, hence the name "Hand-Free".
