#### Talos, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), the Dragonborn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/OB-Window-Talos.jpg/90px-OB-Window-Talos.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Window-Talos.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Window-Talos.jpg) Talos Heir to the Seat of Sundered Kings, [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) is the most important hero-god of Mankind. In his mortal form, known as [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), he conquered all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and ushered in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) (and the Third Empire). After death, his spirit ascended to the heavens to become the ninth and youngest divine, under the name given to him by the Nords—Talos.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAH-1)</sup> Also called [Ysmir](Lore_Gods_Y.md#Ysmir), 'Dragon of the North'.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> One of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md) (the major gods worshipped throughout Tamriel).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FMGAE-3)</sup> Less prominent sects, such as the [Talos Cult](Lore_Talos_Cult.md), have been inspired by his apotheosis. In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), worship of Talos was banned as a result of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md). However, many of the Nords of Skyrim continued to revere him despite these strict terms.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTM-4)</sup> His aspect Ysmir, 'Dragon of the North' is invoked as the patron to questing heroes.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MGDT-5)</sup>

#### Tava, Yokudan Bird God
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5e/ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg/200px-ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Tava%27s_Beak.jpg) A statue of Tava, Yokudan Bird God [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) spirit of the air and sun. Tava is most famous for leading the Yokudans to the isle of [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) after the destruction of their homeland. She has since become assimilated into the mythology of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md). She is still very popular in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) among sailors, and her shrines can be found in most port cities.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> The Redtail Hawk is considered sacred to Tava,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONWriting Quill-6)</sup> while conversely, [harpies](Lore_Harpy.md) are seen as abominations by her faithful.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONHarpy Egg-7)</sup>

#### Theodorus
[Theodorus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Theodorus) was an architect who constructed the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md) to honor a race of [Giants](Lore_Giant.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TES1Arena-9)</sup> He is the object of reverence for the Order of Theodorus.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Nyeraad-10)</sup>

#### Tiger God
The [Tiger God](Lore_Tiger_God.md) was an obscure figure once worshipped by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Bantha](Lore_Bantha.md) on the 14th of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md). It was speculated that the [Feast of the Tiger](Lore_Feast_of_the_Tiger.md), held on this day, may have originally been a religious observance in honor of the Tiger God, rather than a harvest thanksgiving. By the time of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), however, the feast had evolved into a more festive occasion, still celebrated in the rainforest, but now resembling other harvest festivals. It was not a solemn time of reflection and gratitude, but a lively celebration marked by grand festivities across villages.

#### Tribunal (Almalexia, Sotha Sil, Vivec), Gods of Morrowind
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/LO-gods-Tribunal.jpg/200px-LO-gods-Tribunal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-gods-Tribunal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-gods-Tribunal.jpg) A fresco depicting the Tribunal. From left to right: Vivec, Almalexia and Sotha Sil The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), also called Almsivi, is the triune of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) worshipped by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), with the exception of [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md). They ruled the Dunmer as living gods for years after the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), until the cataclysms of the [Nerevarine Prophecies](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecies.md), around [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). In the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) of [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), the Dunmer reverted to their traditions of pure [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and ancestor worship, relegating the Tribunal to saints.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TBORM-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TR-12)</sup>

#### Trinimac, Strong God of the Aldmer
[Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), the **Warrior**, the **Paragon**, and the **Golden Champion**, was a prominent god of the early [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md); in some places, he was even more popular than [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md). He was considered "Auriel's greatest [knight](Lore_Knights.md)", and he was a warrior spirit of the original [elven](Lore_Mer.md) tribes that led armies against mankind, and was known as the strongest of all the [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md) who wandered [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). He is often depicted holding his sword [Penitent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Artifacts_P#Penitent), also known as [Vosh Rakh](Lore_Vosh_Rakh.md), the "Blade of Courage". [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) is commonly believed to have "eaten" Trinimac and assumed his shape so the Daedric Prince could convince a throng of [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) to listen to him, which led to the creation of the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md). Afterwards, popular legend holds that Trinimac vanished from the mythic stage, only to return as [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), nothing more than a weak and vengeful version of his former glory.

Certain legends paint Trinimac as a form other deities take. In the myth recounted in [Shor Son of Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Shor_Son_of_Shor), the deity [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md) takes on the form of Trinimac at certain points in the tale while the two remain, at least partly, distinct beings, noted as being "akin" to one another, at the same time. Other sources attribute the identity of Trinimac as being Boethiah *"as she had always been, the Warrior of East and West, and of the [Starry Heart](Lore_Tamriel.md). She who bore the burden of rending [divinity](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) from the [one](Lore_Lorkhan.md) she loved."*, instead, with Malak King of Curses having taken on the identity of Trinimac through a curse and convinced the [Ornim](Lore_Orc.md) that they were elves, a veil Boethiah subsequently lifted from the eyes of those present.

#### Tsun, Nordic God of Trials against Adversity
[Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md) is the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) god of trials against adversity. He died defending [Shor](Lore_Shor.md) from foreign gods, with his death remembered as bringing about the current world. Tsun and his brother [Stuhn](Lore_Stuhn.md) were both Shield-thanes of [Shor](Lore_Shor.md). Dead gods such as Shor and Tsun don't need temples, for they have the biggest one of all; a vast hall of drinking and revelry known as the Hall of Valor (Shor's Hall). He currently guards the Whalebone Bridge to the Hall of Valor of [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md). At Shor's bidding, he has taken on the role of the master of trials, asking new arrivals to the utopia to prove their strength in combat against him before they can enter the Hall, where he fights using his axe. The **Bear** was also one of the ancient [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) animal totems, associated later with the Nord god Tsun.

#### Tu'whacca, Tricky God
[Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) (or simply **Tuwhacca**), the **Tricky God**, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) **God of Souls** (or **Shepherd of Souls**), and the **Caretaker of the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md)**. Before the creation of the world, he was the god of **Nobody Really Cares**. When [Tall Papa](Lore_Ruptga.md) undertook the creation of the Walkabout, Tu'whacca found a purpose: he became the caretaker of the Far Shores, and continues to help [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) find their way into the afterlife. Though a soul may have enemies who will try to keep it from reaching the Far Shores, Tu'Whacca is sly and clever, and always outwits them. His cult is sometimes associated with [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) in the more cosmopolitan, [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) regions of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), where the names Tu'whacca and Arkay are basically interchangeable. [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) detest this [Imperialization](Lore_Imperial.md), considering it a betrayal of their heritage. Nevertheless, scholars cannot help but note the clear similarities between them, as well as the similarities they both share with [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes_(god).md) of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), in both the etymology of their names and the divine roles they fill.
