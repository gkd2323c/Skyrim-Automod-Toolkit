# Ur-dra

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4d/ON-statue-Nocturnal_02.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Nocturnal_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Nocturnal_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Nocturnal_02.jpg) Statue of Ur-dra Nocturnal *The Night Mistress is Ur-dra - perhaps the eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes.*
— [Lilatha](Lore_Lilatha.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lilatha-1)</sup> **Ur-dra**, also spelled as **Ur-Dra**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOAD-2)</sup> or **UR DRA**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFDOP-3)</sup> is a title among the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Nocturnal-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Sotha-5)</sup> Its exact meaning is unclear, but it is associated with great age, power, and authority.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lilatha-1)</sup>

This title is most commonly associated with **[Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)**, the Night Mistress, who is, according to the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), perhaps the eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lilatha-1)</sup> Both the Psijic Order,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lilatha-1)</sup> and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Sotha-5)</sup> along with his [Factotums](Lore_Factotum.md), acknowledge Nocturnal as Ur-Dra.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Sotha2-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Aios-7)</sup> [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) asserts that nearly all the royalty of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) acknowledge Nocturnal's title,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICODL-8)</sup> as she has assumed the mantle of Ur-Dra of Oblivion.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TLPOS-9)</sup> It is also a sentiment echoed by Daedric Prince Nocturnal herself.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Nocturnal-4)</sup>

The term Ur-Dra is also used in different contexts and attributed to other entities in various aspects of [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOAD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFDOP-3)</sup> and [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) theology.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FETE-10)</sup>

[Amun-dro](Lore_Amun-dro.md), the Silent Priest of the pre- [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) Khajiiti faith, claimed that both **[Namiira](Lore_Namiira.md)**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOAD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFDOP-3)</sup> and **[Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md)** are Ur-Dra. According to him, [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md) taught Azurah the names of all the spirits, enabling her to recognize the Great Darkness for what it was. As such, she was associated with the song "UR DRA NA MII RA UR DRA NA MII RA UR DRA AZU RA."<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFDOP-3)</sup>

In *[From Exile to Exodus](Lore_From_Exile_to_Exodus.md)*, penned by [Tarvyn Aram](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Tarvyn_Aram), it is asserted that Azura shouted a spell of her own during the confrontation between [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Malak](Lore_Malak.md). To the followers who truly listened, it sounded like a beautiful song: "UR DRA AMATHRA! FU ROZ ISA MAL AKHA!" With those words, which were a curse to some but a blessing to others, Boethiah, with her next expulsion, drew out Malak and discarded him along the stones.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-FETE-10)</sup>

## Notes
- Ur-Dra is not to be confused with Ur-Daedra, a title exclusively associated with the Daedric Prince [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SOIA-11)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OPP-12)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GR-13)</sup>
- "Ur-" as a prefix can mean "proto-", "primitive", and "original".
- The ruins of [Ebon Stadmont](Lore_Ebon_Stadmont.md) are carved with a mysterious language that resembles but isn't [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md). This language, older than any other language on Nirn, has been theorized to predate even the language of Oblivion. Leythen, Earl of Mephala and chief linguist of the Psijic Order, also claimed the Ebon Standmont language and the Daedric power it was linked to were far older than his Prince, Mephala. The language has arcane properties which can only be used by mortals, as recitation of the script has transported people and objects through [time](Lore_Time_Magic.md) and cast the spell with which Ritemaster Iachesis hid the Heart of Transparent Law. Nocturnal provided Iachesis a spell in this language, and the inscriptions in Ebon Stadmont are carved on structures featuring crows, which are symbols of Nocturnal. This might support Nocturnal's claims of being older than Oblivion and other Princes. <sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Forte-14)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONLey-15)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TES-16)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOPO-17)</sup>
