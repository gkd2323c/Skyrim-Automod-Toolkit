# Factions


## What constitutes a faction?
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg/100px-OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Mythic_Dawn.jpg) The banner of the Mythic Dawn [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg) A group of Forsworn The term faction functions as a catch-all category that is better understood by what it does *not* include rather than by what it does. [Races](Lore_Races.md) or personae, like the [Gray Fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Gray_Fox) or the [Shezarrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarrine), are not factions. Groups of [animals](Lore_Bestiary.md) or pantheons of [deities](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) also do not belong here.

"Association" is construed liberally; people who have never met and don't consider themselves united may nevertheless be considered a faction, like the [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders). Historically prominent families and clans, like the [Direnni clan](Lore_Direnni.md), may also be included here.

A name is the only real constant that every faction must have. Any given generic group of pirates, bandits, or thieves is not worth mentioning. But once they give themselves a distinct identity by declaring a name, like the [Blood Horkers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blood_Horkers), [Summerset Shadows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Summerset_Shadows), or [Wharf Rats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wharf_Rats), they become an identifiable faction that should be included in this category.
