# Goldbrand

The Goldbrand is an ancient [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) of the [North](Lore_Skyrim.md) which takes the form of a golden katana.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> According to legends, they first bestowed the blade upon the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) warrior [Sivdur](Lore_Sivdur.md), (ancestor to the modern [Battle-Born](Lore_Battle-Born.md) clan)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SREJ-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRERN-3)</sup> who was sworn to protect them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> It embodies the power of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SREJ-2)</sup> and burns those that it strikes.

## History
[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWYB-4)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-5)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing Goldbrand among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TA-6)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for Goldbrand and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWFR-7)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) discovered the sunken shrine to Boethiah beneath the waves, west of the small village of [Hla Oad](Lore_Hla_Oad.md) in the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The Daedra was upset that his shrine had been allowed to remain in ruins, and ordered the Nerevarine to erect a new shrine in return for the Goldbrand. The Nerevarine funded an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) sculptor to create the shrine at [Khartag Point](Lore_Khartag_Point.md), a landmark northwest of [Gnaar Mok](Lore_Gnaar_Mok.md). When the statue was complete, Boethiah rewarded the Nerevarine with the Goldbrand.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWB-8)</sup> The blade is said to have been able to be empowered under mysterious circumstances and dubbed **Eltonbrand**.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWSK-9)</sup> It is unclear if the Nerevarine pursued this transformation, or if it was sold to [Torasa Aram](Lore_Torasa_Aram.md) and put on display in the [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) Museum of Artifacts later that same year.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Tribunal-10)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) visited the [Realm of Boethia](Lore_Realm_of_Boethia.md) and partook in the Tournament of Ten Bloods. After defeating all nine of Boethiah's Chosen in mortal combat, Boethiah declared the Hero as his Chosen One, gifting the Goldbrand as a boon.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBB-11)</sup>

In [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), Goldbrand was wielded by a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) named Reive, who was in the service of Thalmor general [Lord Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md). Reive was slain by the [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md), who may have wielded the Goldbrand at the Battle of the Red Ring while impersonating Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md). This led to a pervasive rumor that Titus II wielded it at the battle, although this has never been officially confirmed by the Imperial government.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESL-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

Sometime after the Battle of the Red Ring, Goldbrand was returned to the tomb of Sivdur located somewhere in the mountains near [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), though only speculation remained as to who did so.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SREJ-2)</sup> The blade was to be a reward to who could bring the head of Sivdur's betrayer, the [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) Magrathi, who had slain Sivdur's brother over a property dispute.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRERN-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ENo ST-14)</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), discovering the notes of a slain researcher at the Sacellum of Boethiah, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) took on the task of retrieving the blade. With Magrathi's [tomb](Lore_Shroud_Hearth_Barrow.md) defiled, and his severed head at hand, Sivdur's tomb allowed entry, and Goldbrand was claimed by the Last Dragonborn.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

## Notes
- The wielder of this artifact is sometimes referred to as "Goldbrand" by devotees of Boethiah.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
- Eltonbrand is an Easter egg created in reference to the real American basketball player named [Elton Brand](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elton_Brand). For more details on the Easter egg, please see the [related page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Easter_Eggs#Eltonbrand).
