# Xarxes

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-statue-Xarxes.jpg/200px-ON-statue-Xarxes.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Xarxes.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Xarxes.jpg) A statue of Xarxes in Shimmerene *The Mouth of Xarxes whispers in our ear, encouraging us to always learn, always seek, and always discover.*
— Excerpt from the Lesson of the Divine Body<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Uo A-1)</sup> Xarxes, also known as the **Ageless One**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DC-2)</sup> and the **One Who Watches**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TET-3)</sup> is the god of ancestry and secret knowledge.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOCurwe-5)</sup> He began as a scribe to [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) and has kept track of all [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) accomplishments, large and small, since the beginning of time. He created his wife, [Oghma](Lore_Oghma.md), from his favorite moments in history.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Xa O-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> He is worshipped by [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> and has been equated with [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) and [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) as the god of death by some scholars.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TAX-7)</sup> Though Bosmer worship of the deity as distinct alongside Arkay, disagrees with this syncretism.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) claimed that the knowledge he gave to Xarxes was recorded in the [Oghma Infinium](Lore_Oghma_Infinium.md)<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBHermaeus-8)</sup> and that Xarxes was a loyal servant to him.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Skyrim Hermaeus-9)</sup> He was an author of the Aldmeriad, the great origin saga of the Elves and commanded the Mer to keep records of their lives in form of their personal everscriven scrolls, known as Oghmas.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOOTO-10)</sup> The scholar-priests of the divine scribe, secretive though they are, are said to preserve [an ancient tongue long forgotten to any but their order](Lore_Linguistics.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONWa P-11)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/LO-people-Xarxes.jpg/150px-LO-people-Xarxes.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Xarxes.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Xarxes.jpg) Xarxes, as depicted in Altmeri style
### Altmer
Altmeri society is one of the most orderly and structured civilizations in Tamriel, attributed to the will of Xarxes himself.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONWa P-11)</sup> They believe he records not just the life stories of individual Elves, but all the connections of lineage and heritance that bind them together and link them to their ancestors,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PBAS-12)</sup> with the great tree of life tracing the Altmer people being kept and held close by Xarxes himself.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TET-3)</sup>

An alternate origin myth for Xarxes asserts he was a [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) Aldmeri priest of Auri-El who was elevated to divinity by the higher deity. The story is consistent with the High Elves' conceit that they are directly descended from the Aedra, and can, in certain miraculous circumstances, apotheosize and re-ascend to godly status.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TAX-7)</sup>

### Ayleid
Although many [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) followed the Daedra, the Aedra of Elven pantheon were still widely revered as well. Scholars proposed a potential majority of the Ayleids continued to pay them homage to the Aedra even as cults of Daedric Princes sprang up all across [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-DWA-13)</sup>

### Bosmer
To the Bosmeri, Xarxes offers their mages opportunities to learn the magical arts that are not naturally occurring and influence each Wood Elf's being.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TIEGt T-14)</sup> They honor him during the annual "Rites of Xarxes' Memory" at [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md) Cathedral.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Contraband X-15)</sup>

## Notes
- The [Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md) written by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) and Xarxes Crucible in the Deadlands share a similar name to the scribe, but it is unknown if there is any relation.
- In *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, a "Xarses", who also has the title of "Ageless One" [is said to have written the Oghma Infinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Artifact_Quests#Oghma_Infinium). This being was mortal, and died merely "centuries" before the late Third Era.
- Lockmaster's Sigil of Xarxes was a holy relic stored in Torinaan.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LSOX-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Relic_Rescue-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MN-18)</sup>
- Magic utilizing Symbols of Xarxes was used by the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) Archivist Ernarde of the [Silver Dawn](Lore_Silver_Dawn.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-AE-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBS-20)</sup> Identical and similar sigils were used by members of the Scribes of Mora.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-RN-21)</sup>
- One [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) myth speaks of "Ooghama, shield-wife of the Debris, [who had] written everything on her that will ever be and we took all the spaces between the words and talked that way in secret."<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-22)</sup>
