# Oghma Infinium

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/OBR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg) The front cover of the *Oghma Infinium* (Oblivion Remastered) The Oghma Infinium, also known as the **Tome of Power**, the **Librum of Knowledge**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TLOI-1)</sup> and the **Book that Knows**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-2)</sup> is an ancient tome of knowledge, and an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of great power. It was written by [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes_(god).md), the scribe of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), who had been granted the knowledge within by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBHermaeus-3)</sup> The name of the tome comes from [Oghma](Lore_Oghma.md), the wife of Xarxes, whom he created from his favorite moments in history.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> The knowledge within the tome grants the reader access to the artifact's energy, which can be manipulated to achieve near [demi-god](Lore_Demigod.md) abilities.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARDescription-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFOghma-6)</sup> The reader is instantly bombarded with a knowledge intake that could hemorrhage the brains of lesser mortals.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PRIMA-7)</sup><sup>:386</sup> The Paths of Steel, Shadow and Spirit are the three main sections of the tome.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBOghma-8)</sup> Because of the hazards it poses to its reader, this book was classified as a [Tract Perilous](Lore_Tracts_Perilous.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OTP-9)</sup> Once the Oghma Infinium is used, it disappears from its owner's possession.

## History

### Unspecified
The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) believe that sometime during [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md)'s life, he stole the [Ring of Khajiiti](Lore_Ring_of_Khajiiti.md) from [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), which allowed him to perform many grand feats of thievery that would ascend him to the Cat-King of Thieves. Among these feats occurred when he visited [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) seeking treasure, which led to him stealing the Oghma Infinium. Rajhin kept the book hidden in his possession, and occasionally Hermaeus Mora manages to find it, but only because Rajhin chose to give it back to him on his own whim.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOTGT-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-HRSTBTK-10)</sup>

### First and Second Eras
After the fall of their empire in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), many [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) tribes fled [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to resettle in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). One such Ayleid settlement was located just across the border, on the southern banks of the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md) in the [Northern Woods](Lore_Northern_Woods.md) of Valenwood, west of modern [Arenthia](Lore_Arenthia.md). These worshippers of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) had come into possession of two dangerous tomes of knowledge: the *Oghma Infinium* and the Book of the Frozen Legion. They attempted to learn the secrets contained within, but instead their city collapsed into an underground cavern, now only known as the "swallowed city". Following the gradual absorption of Ayleids into [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) society, the Bosmer descendants of the lost city attempted to rebuild. This new settlement was in existence until [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when [Javad Tharn](Lore_Javad_Tharn.md) and his [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) forces invaded [Reaper's March](Lore_Reaper%27s_March.md). One of his generals, an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) named Graccus, commandeered a large invasion force and burned the Bosmer city to the ground, building a mansion over the ruins. This mansion, which came to be known as the Vile Manse, was used by Graccus as a staging ground for exploring the subterranean tunnels in search of the *Oghma Infinium*.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESO-11)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OTP-9)</sup>

Upon finding the tome, Graccus managed to survive the "cosmic ululation", while also summoning powerful [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to guard the ruins. The Book of the Frozen Legion also summoned several [Frost Atronachs](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md), freezing many Bosmer and transforming some into icy undead. Graccus was determined to use the *Oghma Infinium* to aid the invasion and allow the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) to crush the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). However, Javad withdrew military support once he learned of Graccus' apparent inactivity,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> and Hermaeus Mora decided that he was not worthy to continue possessing the tome. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) then entered the Vile Manse, and Mora promised the *Oghma Infinium* as a reward for defeating Graccus. However, the Prince was not willing to risk the Vestige against the tome's secrets, and snatched it away upon Graccus' death. It was duly returned it to its home in Apocrypha, and the [Discourse Amaranthine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Discourse_Amaranthine) was offered in its stead.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Mora ON-13)</sup>

### Third Era
The tale of the book titled *[Wabbajack](Lore_Wabbajack.md)* tells of a [newly ascended](Lore_Pelagius_III.md) king of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) who seeking the knowledge from the Oghma Infinum to help rule his city, attempted to summon Hermaeus Mora. However, since the king had attempted to summon the Daedric Prince during a storm, instead came forth [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) who gave him the [Wabbajack](Lore_Wabbajack.md) and turned the king mad.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Wabbajack-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DFSummoning-15)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg/200px-OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-book-Mysterium_Xarxes_01.jpg) Page 1 of the *Oghma Infinium* (*Oblivion*)<sup>[†](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Sometime during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) obtained the tome in a dungeon after finding a map to its location.

Just before the events of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), an [agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) area was sent by Hermaeus Mora to assassinate a noble who had displeased the Prince. In return, Mora promised the *Oghma Infinium*, which was in the possession of one of his worshippers. The agent succeeded, and the tome passed hands.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-16)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Mora again offered the tome as a reward, this time to the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md). Mora summoned the Hero to his shrine in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md), on the border with Skyrim, after seeing how capable the Hero was with dealing with the other [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md). Mora had the Hero collect souls from the ten main [races](Lore_Races.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), which were needed by his followers to perform a divination. The Hero succeeded and was given the tome.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-HMOB-17)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Sometime after the events of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the tome was sealed inside an ancient [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) lockbox so that no more of its dangerous knowledge could be shared with mortals. This displeased Hermaeus Mora. The lockbox, located in a small cave out on the ice sheets off the coast of northeast [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), was massive in size and seemingly bigger on the inside than physically possible. It could only be opened by those of Dwarven blood. A follower of Hermaeus Mora, [Septimus Signus](Lore_Septimus_Signus.md), was manipulated by the god and tasked with opening the lockbox.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SRSeptimus-18)</sup> Signus believed the lockbox contained the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md). Signus was told by Mora that he would need the knowledge of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) to divine the way to open the lockbox. Signus was an expert on Elder Scrolls, and when the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) came looking for one in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Signus pointed them towards Blackreach. The Last Dragonborn found the Elder Scroll within the Tower of Mzark, taking it and inscribing its knowledge onto a blank Dwemer lexicon. Signus used the knowledge to discover how to open the lockbox. As the Dwemer were all but extinct, Signus theorized that he could inject himself with the blood of all surviving [elves](Lore_Mer.md) to trick the lock. Mora then appeared to the Dragonborn in the form of a Wretched Abyss and revealed that Signus was no longer of use to him due to his broken mind. The Dragonborn retrieved samples of blood from [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) and [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md). Signus succeeded in opening the lock, only to discover that it contained the *Oghma Infinium*. He was turned to dust attempting to read it. The Last Dragonborn took the tome and was contacted by Mora, who was pleased that his knowledge would again be put to use.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Skyrim-19)</sup>
