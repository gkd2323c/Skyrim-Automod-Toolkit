[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/SR-npc-Ungrien.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ungrien.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ungrien.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ungrien.jpg) Ungrien **Ungrien** is a [Wood Elf](Skyrim_Wood_Elf.md) [food vendor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Food_Vendor) and an employee of the [Black-Briar Meadery](Skyrim_Black-Briar_Meadery.md) in [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md). He staffs the tasting counter at the front of the meadery, and gives glowing endorsements of the mead and [Maven Black-Briar's](Skyrim_Maven_Black-Briar.md) business practices under penalty of dismissal (and possibly execution).

He resides in [Haelga's Bunkhouse](Skyrim_Haelga%27s_Bunkhouse.md), sleeping there from 1am to 6am. He begins his day with a one-hour breakfast, then heads to the Black-Briar Meadery and starts work. At noon, he takes some time off to eat a meal for an hour. His workday ends at 10pm, when he spends his leisure time at the Bunkhouse.

Ungrien wears a set of barkeep [clothes](Skyrim_Clothes.md#0006ff38) and a pair of [shoes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shoes#0005b6a0). He is equipped with a leveled [dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger) which can be up to [elven](Skyrim_Elven.md) quality, and carries a selection of [common items and gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leveled_Lists#Common).

## Dialogue
Greetings at the meadery:

*"Welcome! Allow me to recommend the Black-Briar Mead... the purest brew in all of Skyrim!"* *"Hello there, can I pour you a Black-Briar Mead?"* *"We serve only the best here... Black-Briar Mead. May I get one for you?"* *"Ah good, back for some more Black-Briar mead, eh?"* Greetings outside the meadery:

*"I'm off work at the Black-Briar Meadery. I'll be back there in the morning."* When spoken to:

**What makes Black-Briar Mead so special?** *"Lady Maven's been developing the secret recipe for Black-Briar Mead over the last several decades. She uses a very secret blend of spices, fresh honey from [Goldenglow Estate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Goldenglow_Estate) and specially constructed aging casks to ensure the best flavor. Even when you get to the last sip in your flagon, it'll taste just as good as the first!"* **That sounded rehearsed.** *"Damn it! Sorry. I'm not very good at this. Look, just don't say anything to Maven, will you? I've spent weeks trying to get that right and if she sees how stupid I am, she'll get rid of me."* **There are worse things than being fired.** *"Yeah... fired. Sure. That's what I meant."* **Tell me about Maven Black-Briar.** *"She's an absolute pleasure to work for! In fact, I almost feel guilty accepting pay from her. Why, just this last week I was commenting to my fellow meadery workers just how lucky we are to be working for such a charitable family."* **Glad you're so happy.** *"Sure am! I thank my lucky stars every time I look to the night sky that Maven took me in and gave me this opportunity."* It is possible to convince him to admit the truth, provided that you have enough money or at least level 25 in [Speech](Skyrim_Speech.md). If you fail, his comment will depend on which method you attempted:

**Come on, get the truth off your chest. (Persuade)** *"That is the truth, my friend! Maven is a saint and don't let anyone tell you otherwise."* **Here, now tell me the truth." (Bribe)** *"It's going to take a lot more than that."* Succeeding at the Speech check will yield you this from Ungrien:

*"Okay, look. If I'm caught saying anything bad about the meadery or the family, I'm a dead man. I'm the fourth person to work the tasting counter in the last year. The last three are at the bottom of [Lake Honrich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lake_Honrich). Just take my advice and just get out of Riften as soon as you can."* Upon ending conversation:

*"Come back and see us again!"* (At the meadery) *"Travel safe."*

## Conversations
When he passes by [Snilf](Skyrim_Snilf.md) begging for money, he will apologize for having nothing to offer:

*"If I had even a pot to piss in, I'd gladly share. Sorry."* Similarly, to [Edda](Skyrim_Edda.md) he will say:

*"I would if I could, but I can barely make ends meet."* While on his job, his boss [Indaryn](Skyrim_Indaryn.md) will threaten to fire him for negligence:

**Indaryn**: *"Ungrien! I just found another spoiled keg! What's the matter with you?"*
**Ungrien**: *"I... I'm sorry. I could have sworn I sealed it."*
**Indaryn**: *"Tell me something. Do you enjoy spending Lady Maven's gold for her? It certainly seems that way."*
**Ungrien**: *"No."*
**Indaryn**: *"This is the last warning. You do it again, and you'll be sleeping in [Beggar's Row](Skyrim_Beggar%27s_Row.md) with the rest of the riff-raff!"* He is tired of Indaryn's constant mistreatment of him, and at the Bunkhouse he will admit this to [Tythis Ulen](Skyrim_Tythis_Ulen.md):

**Ungrien**: *"I can't take it anymore, Tythis. Indaryn keeps pushing me around and I'm not even doing anything wrong."*
**Tythis**: *"Maybe it's time you quit then."*
**Ungrien**: *"And do what? Losing a job from the Black-Briar family would make me a marked man. No one would ever take me on anywhere else."*
**Tythis**: *"Don't let it get you down. Indaryn will eventually tire of picking on you and move on to someone else."*
## Unused Conversations
He was also meant to vent to [Valindor](Skyrim_Valindor.md) about his situation, but Valindor never visits the meadey in the final game. He originally worked there:

**Ungrien**: *"I don't know if I can stand the abuse here much longer. They treat us like outcasts."*
**Valindor**: *"The Dark Elves and the Wood Elves have never been the best of friends. Be patient, they'll come around soon enough!"*
**Ungrien**: *"Wretched Dark Elves. It's just my luck I ended up working under one of them."*
**Valindor**: *"Chin up, Ungrien! Remember, you have a roof over your head and food in your belly thanks to the very same Dark Elves. Be thankful."*
## Notes
- After completing the [Thieves Guild](Skyrim_Thieves_Guild_(faction).md) quest line, he may disappear, and be replaced by [Romlyn Dreth](Skyrim_Romlyn_Dreth.md). It is unclear why this happens, as Ungrien's disappearance does not appear to be scripted.
