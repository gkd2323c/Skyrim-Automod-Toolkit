## [Kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-creature-Kagouti.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Kagouti.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Kagouti.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Kagouti.jpg) A kagouti [Kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md) are large, armored bipedal reptiles, related to [alits](Lore_Alit.md), [guar](Lore_Guar.md), and [wormmouths](Lore_Wormmouth.md), that are native to the regions of Eastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), primarily [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and to a lesser extent [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Their defining features are their large tusks and the thick crests on their heads. Kagouti have a large number of teeth, sometimes growing a second or even third pair of fangs depending on age.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Kagouti), [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Kagouti), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Kagouti), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Ornery Kagouti)

## [Knight of Order](Lore_Knight_of_Order.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/SI-npc-Knight_of_Order.jpg/200px-SI-npc-Knight_of_Order.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-npc-Knight_of_Order.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-npc-Knight_of_Order.jpg) A Knight of Order The [Knights of Order](Lore_Knight_of_Order.md) are mindless [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> soldiers resembling crystalline-armored knights. They serve the Daedric Prince of Order, [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md), and make up one part of the [Forces of Order](Lore_Forces_of_Order.md), which also includes the [Priests of Order](Lore_Priest_of_Order.md) and Jyggalag. Their origins and their goals are unknown. They are [soulless](Lore_Soul.md) warriors that kill without hesitation, and it is therefore highly advised to destroy them on sight. They are said to be without "a bit of original thought in their lifeless husks". They are only ever encountered during the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) at the end of every era, during which they are summoned to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) via the Obelisks of Order that dot the landscape of the realm, wreaking havoc in anticipation of Jyggalag's return. The Knights are drawn to Obelisks like "flies to honey", and are summoned by Priests of Order though chanting and rituals.

**Found in:** - [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Knight_of_Order), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Knight_of_Order)

## [Knower's Eye](Lore_Knower%27s_Eye.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg) Knower's Eye [Knower's Eyes](Lore_Knower%27s_Eye.md), (also known as **Watchful Lights** and **Eyes of Mora**) are [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) minions of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) native to [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), taking the form of massive, floating green orbs resembling eyes. They possess the ability to captivate those who gaze upon them, diverting the attention of nearby foes and diminishing their combat capabilities. At times, they are observed patrolling landmarks in Apocrypha and firing green beams at intruders. Individuals who have been granted one of the brief yet powerful blessings, referred to as Verses, from Hermaeus Mora himself, can [conjure](Lore_Conjuration.md) and command the Knower's Eye in combat up to a certain extent. These blessings are direct bestowals from Hermaeus Mora, intended to aid and alter one's exploration within the Infinite Archive.

The Eyes of Mora may evoke terror, death, and despair for some, but they also serve as vessels for storing information. Dedicated followers of Mora take care of these floating eyes, tending to them like books on a shelf. These eyes are extracted from the countless orbs that comprise Hermaeus Mora's essence. Each eye, contains a memory personally witnessed by Hermaeus Mora, reserved as a significant recollection. To access one of these stored memories, one must physically enter the eye, which typically requires a key, a pass phrase, or another method of gaining access. Should the personal recollections of Hermaeus Mora stored within the Eyes of Mora somehow be altered, the event the memory depicts itself will be [rewritten](Lore_Time_Magic.md).

Scholars questioned whether they are sentient beings or mere extensions of Hermaeus Mora. However, they agreed that the presence of consciousness does not always indicate independent will. These entities exhibit controlled movements, resembling observation, and serve as a source of light within Apocrypha.

- See also: [Wisp-Lantern](Lore_Wisp-Lantern.md)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Knower%27s_Eye)

## [Kollopi](Lore_Kollopi.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/BT-concept-Kollopi_Meatballs.png/200px-BT-concept-Kollopi_Meatballs.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Kollopi_Meatballs.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Kollopi_Meatballs.png) Kollopi Meatballs The [Kollopi](Lore_Kollopi.md) are arboreal [rodents](Lore_Mammal.md) that live in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), mainly in the [Graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) trees such as [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) and [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md). They feed off the tender, albeit slim branches of the graht-oak, which is why they are harvested by the local [Imga](Lore_Imga.md). They are experts in collecting these creatures from the ends of the canopy, using their talented dexterity.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_The_Red_Kitchen_Reader.md)

## [Kwama Warrior](Lore_Kwama.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/LG-cardart-Hive_Defender.png/200px-LG-cardart-Hive_Defender.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Hive_Defender.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Hive_Defender.png) A kwama warrior [Kwama](Lore_Kwama.md) warriors are a caste of kwama that defend their colony's tunnels with poisonous or shock attacks. They don't attack miners because they are used to their odor, but will assault outsiders without hesitation. Especially large warriors serve as the [queen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Queen)'s consorts.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> They are said to sometimes fight each other for this role.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>
