# Marauder

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/ON-npc-Marauder_Ulmor.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Marauder_Ulmor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Marauder_Ulmor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Marauder_Ulmor.jpg) A Marauder, Marauder Ulmor *What luck to face a Marauder like that! Perhaps [the worldly](Lore_The_Worldly_Spirits.md) and [wandering spirits](Lore_The_Wandering_Spirits.md) wish to hear our weapons sing.*
—Zerith-var<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ZV-1)</sup> Marauders are a subtype of [maligraphy](Lore_Maligraphy.md) found within the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ZV-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Marauders-2)</sup> creatures of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) origins that inhabit the [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Esalinwe-3)</sup> Some believe they are among the most powerful maligraphies, sent by the Archive to challenge those who enter its halls. There were those who wondered if these beings are destined to encounter their opponents, and thus, destined to face their demise.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ZV-1)</sup> In fact, they typically display far greater power than most other maligraphies,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Enemies-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Marauders-2)</sup> as the strength of a maligraphy depends on the page they were created from.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Q.26A-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MM-6)</sup> They were also described as powerful abominations, or more colorfully, as rotten inkbags. Esalinwe, a veteran of the Infinite Archive, claimed that Marauders often join the forces of other maligraphies the moment their opponents let their guard down. She advised adventurers never to let them get behind and to avoid letting them land a hit.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Esalinwe-3)</sup>

Among the known Marauders were those that took the forms of [Incarnates](Lore_Incarnate.md), such as Ulmor and Hilkarax.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Marauder_Ulmor-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Marauder_Hilkarax-8)</sup> Other took forms of [Ruinachs](Lore_Ruinach.md), like Gothmau.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Marauder_Gothmau-9)</sup> there were also Marauders who assumed forms of [Minotaurs](Lore_Minotaur.md), such as Zulfimbul,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Marauder_Zulfimbul-10)</sup> and [Werewolf Behemoths](Lore_Werewolf.md), like Bittog.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Marauder_Bittog-11)</sup>
