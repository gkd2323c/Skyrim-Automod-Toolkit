# Moonstone

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/SR-item-Elven_Armor_Female.jpg/200px-SR-item-Elven_Armor_Female.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Elven_Armor_Female.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Elven_Armor_Female.jpg) Elven Armor (Skyrim) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/LG-cardart-Moonstone_Relic.png/200px-LG-cardart-Moonstone_Relic.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Moonstone_Relic.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Moonstone_Relic.png) Moonstone Relic (Legends) Moonstone is an ivory colored metal found in veins throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It is generally used, sometimes in tandem with [quicksilver](Lore_Quicksilver.md) to make elven armor, the construction of which was held for a long time by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) as a closely-guarded secret.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LAF-1)</sup> Moonstone is also the signature material used in [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) -styled armor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONMoonstone-2)</sup> Moonstone melts at a much higher heat than quicksilver, making it tricky to work with both of them together. Saltwater is required to quench the hot metal when making elven armor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LAF-1)</sup>

## Further Information
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg/200px-SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Moonstone_Ore.jpg) Moonstone Ore (Skyrim) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/OB-item-Elven_Armor.jpg/200px-OB-item-Elven_Armor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Elven_Armor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Elven_Armor.jpg) Elven Armor (Oblivion) In the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), Elven Armor is considered some of the strongest light armor, better than [mithril](Lore_Mithril.md) but weaker than [glass](Lore_Malachite.md). It was on par with [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) heavy armor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OBArmor-3)</sup> In [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), normal elven armor is stronger than chitin and leather armor, but weaker than scaled armor. All the while, gilded elven armor is stronger than scaled and normal elven armor, but it is inferior to light [stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) and glass.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRArmor-4)</sup> Refined Moonstone is used to create the armor used by the [Golden Saints](Lore_Golden_Saint.md) of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), with [gold](Lore_Gold.md) and a [daedra's heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra%27s_Heart).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-JJ-5)</sup>

There are several artifacts made from moonstone, these include [Auriel's Shield](Lore_Auriel%27s_Shield.md), the hilt of [Chillrend](Lore_Chillrend.md), the [Crown of Nenalata](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md), the mask of [Morokei](Lore_Dragon_Priest_Masks.md#Morokei), and the [Spheriphem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Space_Core) from parts unknown.

For the longest time, moonstone is used in various Khajiit styled constructs, namely weapons, armors, furnishings, and various other items. For example, wooden furniture such as tables and beds are made with moonstone, alongside [heartwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartwood) and [regulus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Regulus). Metal braziers and torches are primarily used with the latter. Tailors make Khajiit fabric with a myriad of materials, it mostly uses bast, among other materials and occasionally [citrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Citrine). Various types of Khajiiti bottles and containers are made from moonstone, coincided with materials like [Bervez Juice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bervez_Juice) and [Frost Mirriam](Lore_Frost_Mirriam.md). Small warrior figurines carved from moonstone are placed in superstitious people's cabinets, to ward off thieves.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONContraband1-6)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a chunk of rock known as the holy moonstone was in circulation throughout Tamriel. According to local legend, it fell from the moons ([Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md)) centuries prior, but no one was able to confirm this.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONContraband2-7)</sup> It is unknown if it is related to the common moonstone. The ancient secret of Moonstone's use in elven armor was revealed to the world in the seventh century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). When the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) slave, Ulvul Llaren escaped the clutches of the master smith, [Nuulion](Lore_Nuulion.md), he unleashed the secrets to crafting elven armor out of complete spite. The tale has since been dubbed the *"Betrayal of Ulvul Llaren"*.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LAF-1)</sup> Frequently encountered in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) were polished symbols intricately carved from moonstone, radiating a bone-like glow under any light.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Treasure PMC-8)</sup>

In Skyrim, moonstone veins are found across the mountains. Some of the largest veins of moonstone is found in the ruined city-state of [Mzulft](Lore_Mzulft.md) and the moonstone mines of Soljund's Sinkhole, which is located near the eastern base of [Karthspire](Lore_Karthspire.md) and the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md). It is extremely rare on the island, [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), with only one vein found at the Altar of Thrond.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SRSources-9)</sup>
