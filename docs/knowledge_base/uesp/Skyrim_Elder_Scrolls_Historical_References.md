# Elder Scrolls Historical References


If you think you have found an undocumented reference, please post your idea on this article's talk page before adding it to this article.

## [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)
- The line *"When misrule takes its place at the eight corners of the world"* in the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Skyrim_The_Book_of_the_Dragonborn.md) refers to the eight pieces of [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) scattered around the provinces and [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s rule.
- A quote from [Gaiden Shinji](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gaiden_Shinji) — *"The best techniques are passed on by the survivors"* —which first appeared in the beginning of Arena intro, appears as one of the loading screen quotes.
- Guards will occasionally say *"Let me guess... someone stole your [sweetroll](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sweet_Roll)?"* This is an ongoing joke in Bethesda games. They are featured during the character creation process of [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind), and *Fallout 3*, and were also mentioned in dialogue in [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion).
- One of the incidental bard songs is a piece titled "Square", and originally appeared as tavern music in both *Arena* and *Daggerfall*.
- The [Spell Knight Armor](Skyrim_Spell_Knight_Armor.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) features a faction called the Order of the Crypt, also known as the War Mages of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md). This alternate name is a reference to the name of the city's gladiatorial team from an early stage of [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)'s development.
- [The Gray Cowl Returns!](Skyrim_The_Gray_Cowl_Returns!.md) Creation also makes a reference to this stage of development, featuring the Ice-Blade family, which owns land in the city of [Jehenna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jehenna). The Iceblades was the name of Jehenna's gladiator team.

## [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)
- The line *"When the Brass Tower walks and Time is reshaped"* in the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Skyrim_The_Book_of_the_Dragonborn.md) refers to the rise of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) and the [Warp in the West](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Warp_in_the_West).
- Sheogorath refers to the ghost of [King Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus) in passing.

## [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire)
- One of the lines spoken by [souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul) in the Soul Cairn is: *"How dare they cast me from the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md)! I will make them pay dearly."* This is a reference to the [Soul Cairn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Level_3) level in *Battlespire*, where it's revealed that the Daedra were killing the captives from the Battlespire in the Soul Cairn in order to trap their souls there forever.

## [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)
- Immediately after creating a [Redguard](Skyrim_Redguard.md) character, [Hadvar](Skyrim_Hadvar.md) will say *"You a sellsword? A sailor from [Stros M'kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md)?"* This refers to [Cyrus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Cyrus), the protagonist of The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard.
- [Endon](Skyrim_Endon.md) and [Kerah](Skyrim_Kerah.md) have a conversation talking about how everyone in Markarth used to wear a necklace showing their home province, from "Elsweyr to High Rock to Hammerfell and Cyrodiil...", and that in the present-day only natives and the Nords stay there. This refers to a line in *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Skyrim.md)*, where The Reach is described as one of the most diverse Holds, full of Bretons, Redguards, Imperials, Elves, and even Khajiit, emphasizing how much things have changed in the centuries since.

## [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)
- In the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Skyrim_The_Book_of_the_Dragonborn.md) on [Alduin's Wall](Skyrim_Alduin%27s_Wall.md), the line *"When the thrice-blessed fail and the Red Tower trembles"* refers to the fall of the Tribunal and the eruption of [Red Mountain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Red_Mountain).

- During the [Dark Brotherhood](Skyrim_Dark_Brotherhood.md) quest [The Cure For Madness](Skyrim_The_Cure_for_Madness.md), you will encounter an [Udefrykte](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Udefrykte), which made its first appearance in [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:The_Udyrfrykte), and its mother, called the [Uderfrykte Matron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Uderfrykte_Matron), appears in Oblivion.

- The book *[The Lusty Argonian Maid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Lusty_Argonian_Maid,_v1)* makes a return in Skyrim, with a new scene in two parts. The original play was written by [Crassius Curio](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Crassius_Curio) in Morrowind.

- In the Gray Quarter of eastern [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), there's a tavern called the [New Gnisis Cornerclub](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:New_Gnisis_Cornerclub), apparently named for [Gnisis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Gnisis), a town in Morrowind.

- During the Thieves Guild quest [The Dainty Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Dainty_Sload), you are tasked to acquire a rare and illegal drug named [Balmora Blue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Balmora_Blue). Named for its city of origin, [Balmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balmora), which is referenced by other characters as being destroyed during the eruption of Red Mountain.

- During the main quest mission [Elder Knowledge](Skyrim_Elder_Knowledge.md), you encounter a Dwemer lockbox said to contain the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan). The heart, however, is not within the lockbox.

- A bartender for [The Bee and Barb](Skyrim_The_Bee_and_Barb.md) in [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md) named [Talen-Jei](Skyrim_Talen-Jei.md) offers a special drink called the [Cliff Racer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Cliff_Racer). Purportedly, it's a mixture of [Firebrand Wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Firebrand_Wine), [Cyrodiilic Brandy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Cyrodiilic_Brandy), [Flin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Flin), and [Sujamma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Sujamma), these last three being common fare in [Vvardenfell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Vvardenfell).

- On the eastern coast close to [Winterhold](Skyrim_Winterhold.md), there is the wreck of a ship called [The Pride of Tel Vos](Skyrim_Wreck_of_The_Pride_of_Tel_Vos.md). Tel Vos is a [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) stronghold in Morrowind and home to one of the Telvanni mage lords.

- The infamous [Dwemer Puzzle Cube](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwemer_Puzzle_Cube) ([Box](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Dwemer_Puzzle_Box) in Morrowind) can be found in Markarth's [Dwemer Museum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwemer_Museum).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg/200px-SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Vvardenfell.jpg) Vvardenfell as seen from Skyrim - If flying high enough via the `tcl` [console](Skyrim_Console.md) command, Morrowind and the Vvardenfell province will be visible beyond the eastern border of Skyrim. The province is also visible from the Arch-Mage's rooftop at the [College of Winterhold](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(place).md), and from the path that leads to the [Shrine of Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shrine_of_Azura).

- [Keening](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Keening), a dagger required for the final quest of Morrowind's main storyline, makes a return.

- There is a note under Wylandriah's desk in Riften called [Per Your Requests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Per_Your_Requests), in which [Wylandriah](Skyrim_Wylandriah.md) requests a sample of the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan). The same note mentions a device capable of "magical manipulation of the clouds". A Dwemer machine that could control the weather was used during the [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:A_Show_of_Power) questline.

- During the quest [Breaching Security](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Breaching_Security), you have to plant an [Incriminating Letter](Skyrim_Incriminating_Letter_(DB).md) on [Gaius Maro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gaius_Maro) detailing a plot to assassinate the emperor. In Morrowind, you had to uncover the [Talos Cult Conspiracy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Talos_Cult_Conspiracy), and kill a traitor named [Oritius Maro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Oritius_Maro).

- When visiting the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md) during the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) questline, you'll meet a soul named [Jiub](Skyrim_Jiub.md). [He](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Jiub) is the first character that the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) meets in [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind).

- When speaking to the farmer [Rogatus Salvius](Skyrim_Rogatus_Salvius.md) of [Salvius Farm](Skyrim_Salvius_Farm.md) near [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), he occasionally says *"Next thing you know there will be some kind of damn blight and undead will walk the land."* This is most likely about [The Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:The_Blight) faced in Morrowind.

- The [Telvanni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telvanni) wizard [Neloth](Skyrim_Neloth.md) originally appeared in Morrowind as [one of the Masters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Neloth) of [House Telvanni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:House_Telvanni).

- [Lleril Morvayn](Skyrim_Lleril_Morvayn.md) is the son of [Mistress Brara Morvayn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Mistress_Brara_Morvayn), a [Redoran](Lore_Redoran.md) High Councilor who fled to [Raven Rock](Skyrim_Raven_Rock.md) in the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), and eventually ended up running the place when the [Imperials](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:East_Empire_Company) left.

- In a [random encounter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Random_Encounters) on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md), you can encounter a [Bosmer Wizard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wizard) giving himself a pep talk, trying to cast a spell to fly. He shoots straight up in the air and when he comes down, he dies. This is a reference to [Tarhiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Tarhiel) and his [Scrolls of Icarian Flight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Scroll_of_Icarian_Flight).

- Northwest of [Tel Mithryn](Skyrim_Tel_Mithryn_(settlement).md), there's a lone [Silt Strider](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silt_Strider). [These](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Silt_Strider) were one of the main methods of [transportation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Transport) in Morrowind.

- While wandering around the island of Solstheim, songs from the [soundtrack of Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Music) can be heard.

- The [Netch Leather Armor](Skyrim_Netch_Leather_Armor.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the book [Crafting with Netch Leather](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Crafting_with_Netch_Leather) by Alma Merisan. The [Merisan Cuirass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Merisan_Cuirass) was a generic enchanted Netch Leather Cuirass in *Morrowind*.

## [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Bloodmoon)
- In [Sovngarde](Skyrim_Sovngarde_(place).md), there is a familiar face of [Ulfgar the Unending](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Ulfgar_the_Unending), together with his friends Erlendr, Hunroor, and Nikulas, who were turned into standing stones. The [Nerevarine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Nerevarine) fought Ulfgar to an honorable Nord death in the quest "[Betrayal at Brodir Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Betrayal_at_Brodir_Grove)".

- During the quest [Ill Met By Moonlight](Skyrim_Ill_Met_By_Moonlight.md), the Bloodmoon can be seen in the sky once entering the Grotto, which signifies that Hircine once again begins the hunt which varies from era to era on the island of [Solstheim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Solstheim), but can be seen from other locations in Tamriel.

- [Captain Carius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Captain_Falx_Carius) reappears as an undead named [General Carius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:General_Falx_Carius). Carius was the [first quest giver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Rebellion_at_Frostmoth) in Bloodmoon.

- At the [Altar of Thrond](Skyrim_Altar_of_Thrond.md) are three [hagravens](Skyrim_Hagraven.md): [Ettiene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ettiene), [Fallaise](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fallaise) and [Isobel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Isobel). In Bloodmoon they were [Glenmoril witches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd) who were able to cure [Lycanthropy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Werewolf).

- During the quest [Summoning Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summoning_Karstaag), you are required to kill the spirit of [Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Karstaag), a [frost giant](Skyrim_Frost_Giant.md). In Bloodmoon, you were required to kill [Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Karstaag) during [Hircine's Hunt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Hircine%27s_Hunt).

- The remains of [Gratian Caerellius](Skyrim_Gratian_Caerellius.md) can be found deep in [Raven Rock mine](Skyrim_Raven_Rock_Mine.md). He [originally appeared](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Gratian_Caerellius) in Bloodmoon as one of the workers in the mine.

- The [All-Maker Stones](Skyrim_All-Maker_Stones.md) were called the [Sacred Stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Landmarks) in Bloodmoon.

- The sword [Stormfang](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stormfang) can be found on a [Reaver Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reaver_Lord#Reaver_Lords) at [Brodir Grove](Skyrim_Brodir_Grove.md). It was [previously](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Stormfang) used by [Ulfgar the Unending](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Ulfgar_the_Unending) in Bloodmoon at the [same grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Brodir_Grove).

- The [Patchwork Airship](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Patchwork_Airship) returns as the [unmarked location](Skyrim_Unmarked_Places.md#Dragonborn) [Strange Vessel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Strange_Vessel).

- At [Snowclad Ruins](Skyrim_Snowclad_Ruins.md), an altar of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) can be found along with [werebears](Skyrim_Werebear.md). [Hircine's Hunt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Hircine%27s_Hunt) may still take place on Solstheim at times. [Majni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Majni) sells [blessed werewolf rings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ring_of_Bloodlust) that were once used in the rituals.

## [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Shadowkey)
- [Neloth](Skyrim_Neloth.md) has a quest called [Azra's Staffs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Azra%27s_Staffs), and directly refers to [Azra Nightwielder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Azra_Nightwielder), the original shadowmage.

## [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)
- The line *"When the Dragonborn Ruler loses his throne, and the White Tower falls"* in the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Skyrim_The_Book_of_the_Dragonborn.md) refers to the death of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and the obliteration of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), and the sacking of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md).

- During the Dark Brotherhood questline, one of the rewards is a [spell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summon_Spectral_Assassin) that summons a [Spectral Assassin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spectral_Assassin), which turns out to be the ghost of [Lucien Lachance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Lucien_Lachance), your recruiter and, later, quest-giver in the Oblivion [Dark Brotherhood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Dark_Brotherhood).

- During the Dark Brotherhood questline, [Nazir](Skyrim_Nazir.md) offers you a contract in which you kill a pirate named [Safia](Skyrim_Safia.md) aboard her ship called the [Red Wave](Skyrim_Red_Wave.md). [Redwave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Redwave) is a cutlass given to you for completing the quest [The Ghost Ship of Anvil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Ghost_Ship_of_Anvil). Also, the first contract in Oblivion ([A Watery Grave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:A_Watery_Grave)) was to kill a pirate on his own ship.

- [Sinderion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Sinderion), the alchemist from [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Skingrad) who studied [Nirnroot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Nirnroot), apparently moved to Skyrim somewhere around 4E52 in hope of discovering more about the strange [Crimson Nirnroot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Crimson_Nirnroot). He met his demise in [Blackreach](Skyrim_Blackreach.md) and [his journal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sinderion%27s_Field_Journal) can be found next to his skeleton, which is riddled with Dwarven Arrows.

- During the quest [The Mind of Madness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_Madness), you will run into [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), who recounts all of the major adventures from Oblivion with Pelagius the Mad. It is implied that this variation of Sheogorath is the [Champion of Cyrodiil](Lore_Hero.md), the Hero from Oblivion.

- In the quest [Revealing the Unseen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Revealing_the_Unseen), a massive project led by the Synod involves the creation of a map pointing out all magical areas in Tamriel. When explained to the player, [Paratus Decimius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Paratus_Decimius) mentions the [Orrery](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Orrery) in Cyrodiil and how the project was based off of it.

- There is a book titled *[The Oblivion Crisis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Oblivion_Crisis)* that provides an overview of the events in Oblivion.

- At some point during the game, you will receive a note about a [Mythic Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mythic_Dawn) museum in [Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md). The museum contains a burned page of the [Mysterium Xarxes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mysterium_Xarxes) (the book [Martin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Martin) used to gain access to [Mankar Camoran's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mankar_Camoran) [Paradise](Oblivion_Paradise_(quest).md)), a copy of all four parts of the [Mythic Dawn Commentaries](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries), and [Mythic Dawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mythic_Dawn) robes. The owner also claims his ancestor was involved in killing [Uriel Septim VII](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Uriel_Septim_VII).

- [Shadowmere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shadowmere) returns as a steed after completing some of the Dark Brotherhood quests. If you have the ghost of Lucien Lachance with you near the horse, his comments will confirm that it is the [same horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Shadowmere) as the one in Oblivion.

- [Wylandriah](Skyrim_Wylandriah.md), the court mage of Riften, can be spoken to regarding her magical work. When suggesting "How about using calipers?", Wylandriah responds with *"Calipers? That's utterly ridiculous. Maybe long ago you could find calipers in every household across Tamriel, but not anymore."* This is a reference to calipers being commonplace in random loot in Oblivion, but not appearing in Skyrim. [M'aiq the Liar](Skyrim_M%27aiq_the_Liar.md) also makes a similar reference.

- There is a note under Wylandriah's desk in Riften called [Per Your Requests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Per_Your_Requests) in which Wylandriah requests an ingredient called [greenmote](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Greenmote) from the College of Winterhold. This was a substance used in the Shivering Isles quest [Ritual of Mania](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Ritual_of_Mania). In the note, Mirabelle Ervine assumes that she made a mistake and meant to request a substance called "greenspore".

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/SR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg/200px-SR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) White-Gold Tower as seen from Skyrim - On a clear day at high elevation along Skyrim's southern border, it is possible to see the distant [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in Cyrodiil. It is difficult and time-consuming to see this without using [console commands](Skyrim_Console.md) to remove collision and 'flying' up to an elevation high enough to see the tower.

- One line of dialogue from the [Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul) in the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> is *""Just step into the painting" he said. Yeah, sure... like it's that simple."* This is a reference to the quest [A Brush with Death](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:A_Brush_with_Death), where you were required to "enter" a painting.

- An alchemist called [Elgrim](Skyrim_Elgrim.md) in [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md) comments on the rarity of [painted troll fat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Painted_Troll_Fat). The only known source of this was the Oblivion quest A Brush with Death. He asks his student [Ingun Black-Briar](Skyrim_Ingun_Black-Briar.md) to replace the batch by speaking with [Rythe Lythandas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Rythe_Lythandas), the illustrious artist from [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Cheydinhal).

- In one of [Cicero](Skyrim_Cicero.md)'s [journals](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Cicero%27s_Journal), he says that he killed the Grand Champion of the Arena of his time by posing as a starstruck fan. This is a reference to the [Adoring Fan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Adoring_Fan), who would follow the player around after becoming the Grand Champion.

- During the quest [Elder Knowledge](Skyrim_Elder_Knowledge.md), when you ask [Urag gro-Shub](Skyrim_Urag_gro-Shub.md) about the Scrolls, he asks *"Do you even know what you're asking for?"* Should you tell him you do and ask if he has one in the library, he will say that even if he had one there, it would be under heavy security and not even the world's greatest thief could lay a finger on it. This is an obvious reference to [The Ultimate Heist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Ultimate_Heist).

- During the Dark Brotherhood questline, Astrid says that *"no one has attempted to murder an emperor since the death of [Uriel Septim VII](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Emperor_Uriel_Septim_VII) 200 years ago"*.

- The Dark Brotherhood quest [Where You Hang Your Enemy's Head...](Skyrim_Where_You_Hang_Your_Enemy%27s_Head....md) is referring to a quote from [Gogron gro-Bolmog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Gogron_gro-Bolmog), who was part of the Dark Brotherhood in Oblivion: *"You know what they say -- home is where you hang your enemy's head."*

- During the Thieves Guild quest [The Pursuit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Pursuit), you have an opportunity to find [Chillrend](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chillrend), a unique one-handed sword. A sword of the [same name](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Chillrend) is a possible reward for the Oblivion quest [The Killing Field](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Killing_Field).

- During the [Dark Brotherhood](Skyrim_Dark_Brotherhood.md) questline, [Astrid](Skyrim_Astrid.md) will give you her [Blade of Woe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Blade_of_Woe). In Oblivion, at the start of the Dark Brotherhood questline, [Lucien Lachance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Lucien_Lachance) also presents you with a [Blade of Woe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Blade_of_Woe) —albeit unenchanted—that you will carry with you throughout the questline.

- At the beginning of [The Mind of Madness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Mind_of_Madness) Daedric quest, you are given an odd item: Pelagius' Hip Bone. This item is also present in the Shivering Isles, found in [The Howling Halls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:The_Howling_Halls).

- [Vekel the Man](Skyrim_Vekel_the_Man.md), the bartender in the Ragged Flagon, will mention Shadowbanish Wine on two occasions. In a scene with [Delvin Mallory](Skyrim_Delvin_Mallory.md), he mentions Delvin having sold him a bad batch. After completing [Scoundrel's Folly](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Scoundrel%27s_Folly) for the Thieves Guild, asking for the word around the Guild will cause him to mention that [Gulum-Ei](Skyrim_Gulum-Ei.md) similarly offloaded low-quality product. In Oblivion, the quest [A Venerable Vintage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:A_Venerable_Vintage) sent you out to gather this rare beverage.

- In Riften you may find a man named [Romlyn Dreth](Skyrim_Romlyn_Dreth.md), who is a descendant of [Valen Dreth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Valen_Dreth), the very first character you see in Oblivion. Romlyn will often claim that Valen killed *"a dozen Imperial guards"* before being captured.

- After installing [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md), you can find [Surilie Brothers Wine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Surilie_Brothers_Wine) and [Argonian Bloodwine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Argonian_Bloodwine) in your home and sold by vendors. The first appeared in the base game, while the second was added by the [Fighter's Stronghold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Fighter%27s_Stronghold) DLC.

- Several of the [spell tomes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Arcane_Accessories_Items#Spell_Tomes) added by the [Arcane Accessories](Skyrim_Arcane_Accessories.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) are spells which originally appeared in the [Spell Tomes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Spell_Tomes) DLC for Oblivion, which were themselves [references](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Spell_Tomes#Notes) to previous games.

- The [*Mysterious Note*](Skyrim_Mysterious_Note_(Staff_of_Sheogorath).md) from the [Staff of Sheogorath](Skyrim_Staff_of_Sheogorath.md) Creation notes that "warriors of another age might find [the quest items] familiar". This is a reference to the [Symbols of Office](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Symbols_of_Office) quest from Shivering Isles, which has you gather the same items.

- [Jiub](Skyrim_Jiub.md) in the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md) describes his death at the hands of a Dremora in Kvatch, referring to Kvatch's destruction at the start of the Oblivion Crisis.

- A dead Orc adventurer can be found within [Rielle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rielle) as part of [The Cause](Skyrim_The_Cause.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md). This dungeon also [appears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Rielle) in Oblivion, where you have a 10% chance to encounter a living [Orc Adventurer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Adventurer) within.

## [The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City) and [Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Lord_of_Souls)
- There are Dunmer refugees in Windhelm who traveled from Morrowind after Red Mountain erupted. If you speak to some of the refugees and ask them why they are in Windhelm despite the discrimination they face, they will tell you that when Red Mountain erupted, they began traveling west and the first city they came to was Windhelm. It is mentioned that they would have kept on walking if they knew how they would be treated in the city.

- Just before Red Mountain erupted, those following Azura had a premonition from her of what was about to happen, giving them time to flee. It was these refugees who built the Shrine of Azura on a mountain north of Windhelm.

- The quest [Distant Memories](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Distant_Memories) is full of references to a successful Argonian invasion of Morrowind. This is a reference to the [Accession War](Lore_Accession_War.md).

- In the [Sleeping Tree Camp](Skyrim_Sleeping_Tree_Camp.md), you can find a strange tree. After a [quest](Skyrim_Sleeping_Tree_Cave_(quest).md) for her in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md), [Ysolda](Skyrim_Ysolda.md) will tell you some theories about the origin of the tree. One theory refers to the eruption of the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), while the other theory refers to a floating island. The latter is clearly a reference to [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md).

- You can find a little monument at the tower [Refugees' Rest](Skyrim_Refugees%27_Rest.md), east of [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md). There is also a book called *[Decree of Monument](Skyrim_Decree_of_Monument.md)* that explains the origin of the monument. It was erected in commemoration to the Dunmer refugees fleeing from Morrowind after the eruption of Red Mountain.

- In the book *[On the Great Collapse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:On_the_Great_Collapse)*, you can find a reference to many Dunmer refugees in Winterhold and the offering of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) to the Dunmer as a new home as mentioned in *[Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Lord_of_Souls)*.

- [Legate Fasendil](Skyrim_Legate_Fasendil.md)'s recounting of the [Night of Green Fire](Lore_Night_of_Green_Fire.md) is a reference to the Thalmor harassing and possibly murdering refugees in Balfiera and Sentinel for being considered "unclean" by the Dominion, as mentioned in *[Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Lord_of_Souls)*.

- In Riften's Grand Plaza, [Nivenor](Skyrim_Nivenor.md) might ask the marketplace vendor [Brand-Shei](Skyrim_Brand-Shei.md) if he can procure theilul. This Argonian sugarcane wine was first mentioned by Mere-Glim.

- In a scene taking place in Riften's The Bee and Barb, Talen-Jei will use the Argonian expletive "Xhuth" upon hearing [Hemming Black-Briar](Skyrim_Hemming_Black-Briar.md)'s racist remark. This expletive was also first used by Mere-Glim.
