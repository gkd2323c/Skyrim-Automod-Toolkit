[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/OB-place-Bruma.jpg/200px-OB-place-Bruma.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bruma.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bruma.jpg) Bruma circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/OB-banner-Bruma.jpg/115px-OB-banner-Bruma.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Bruma.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Bruma.jpg) Bruma's flag, displaying its city crest Bruma (archaically known as **Bruhmaht**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-1)</sup> is a Nibenese city located in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), north of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). Bruma's population is primarily [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), which is reflected in the city's architecture. It is always relatively cold and snow-covered.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GTB-2)</sup> The city's population includes followers of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) as well as those who follow the traditional [Nordic Pantheon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods).

The [Silver Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silver_Road) connects Bruma to the Imperial City, while the [Orange Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orange_Road) connects it to [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol). [Cloud Ruler Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cloud_Ruler_Temple) is located nearby. Bruma has a small sewer system which connects up with the natural caves that run beneath the town.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBMRecover-3)</sup> This underground area was used by the Mythic Dawn during the Oblivion Crisis. The deeper levels of the sewers are home to [trolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Troll). The city also houses the Great Chapel of the youngest of the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines), [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), which dominates the center of town.

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/LO-map-Cyrodiil_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Cyrodiil_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Cyrodiil_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Cyrodiil_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Map of Cyrodiil, showing Bruma in the north
### First Era
The settlement has likely been in existence since at least [1E 1167](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1167).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IG-4)</sup> A so called “beggar-king of Bruhmaht” is recorded as being present at the court of the thirteen year old [Reman I](Lore_Reman.md), said to have swiftly decapitated a lord of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) for speaking in impatience to the Emperor.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TSE-1)</sup>

### Second Era
During [Varen's Rebellion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen%27s_Rebellion) ([2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576) - [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577)), Bruma was a base for the Colovian troops.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Cot FC5-5)</sup> The [Second Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Second_Legion) was stationed in the vicinity of Bruma in peacetime<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Imperial War Mastiff-6)</sup>, although it originally came from the Gold Coast.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Wolfand Dragon-7)</sup>

At some point during the Three Banners War, [Orpheon the Tactician](Lore_Orpheon_the_Tactician.md) and his army put Bruma to siege. For a fortnight, the army's siege engines were used against its walls before Orpheon sent Flamedancer Ajim-Rei into the city to set afire the buildings. The city was then sacked by Orpheon's army, and this event was part of the campaign that lead to Orpheon momentarily being [crowned](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battlefield_Emperors) as [Emperor](Lore_Emperor.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-So B-8)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Bruma became neutral ground during the ongoing [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md), although it was initially within the territory controlled by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). The city was attacked by [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) and [Shadowed Path](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowed_Path) cultists in the name of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). A [Dark Anchor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Anchor) was then opened above the ruins of the city in an attempt to draw it into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) as part of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), as well as to provide Daedric reinforcements. Several citizens barricaded themselves within key buildings, and the survivors sent pleas to both the Daggerfall Covenant and the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) for aid.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Passing soldiers helped the citizenry recover and fight back against the invaders, and the Dark Anchor was eventually lifted.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Bruma came under attack from the forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBBG-11)</sup> The [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) planned to sack the city as they did [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), by opening a Great Gate to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) and allowing a Daedric siege force to enter Nirn. These plans were discovered by the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), but were allowed to continue at the request of [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) in order to obtain a [Great Sigil Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Sigil_Stone). Soldiers from the other cities of Cyrodiil flocked to Bruma to aid in the defense.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBAf B-12)</sup> Rallied by Martin himself, the soldiers held back the tide of Daedra long enough for the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md#Hero_of_Kvatch) to enter the Deadlands and retrieve the Sigil Stone, ending the invasion. The [Defense of Bruma](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md#Defense_of_Bruma) became one of the most important conflicts of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBBruma-13)</sup> During the same year, the Bruma [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) branch was burned to the ground in a surprise attack from the [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBAPR-14)</sup>

### Fourth Era
During the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the [Crimson Dirks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crimson_Dirks) had members in the city.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The [Three Feather tribe](Lore_Goblin.md#Three_Feather_Tribe) survived into the era and may have used Gromm's Pass to cross into [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The Bruma Sanctuary of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) was destroyed circa [4E 186](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_186).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Klija Wolf-Claw, a Nordic warrior from Bruma, participated in a contest between the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) and the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) during [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> In the same year, the city was visited by a party of Mythic Dawn, who kidnapped an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) researcher for use in their excavation of [Rielle](Lore_Rielle.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup>
