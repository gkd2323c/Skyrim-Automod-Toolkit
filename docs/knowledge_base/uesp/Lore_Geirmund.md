Geirmund was an Archmage of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and personal battlemage to [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GE-1)</sup> He does not appear in the historical record until the reign of High King Harald in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when Jyrik, Mikrul, and Sigdis Gauldurson killed their father, Archmage [Gauldur](Lore_Gauldur.md), stole his powerful [amulet](Lore_Gauldur_Amulet.md), and began to terrorize [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) villages throughout [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DVN-2)</sup> The Gauldursons caused so much havoc across Skyrim that Harald sent a company of his best warriors and battlemages led by Geirmund to stop them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DVN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LL-3)</sup> Geirmund and his men fought the Gauldursons in a devastating battle that ended with the brothers fleeing the field.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DVN-2)</sup> A chase ensued, from the wilds of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) to the glacial north.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DVN-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LL-3)</sup> Geirmund eventually tracked down and killed Mikrul in [Folgunthur](Lore_Folgunthur.md), near [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), and Jyrik in [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md), sealing them in those ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DVN-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GO-4)</sup> Geirmund's forces suffered great losses in each encounter, but finally, only the middle child, Sigdis, was left standing. When he could flee no more, Sigdis challenged Geirmund to a duel in the fields near [Ivarstead](Lore_Ivarstead.md), a challenge that Geirmund was honor-bound to accept. Geirmund and Sigdis both died in the duel, bringing an end to the Gauldursons' rampage.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DVN-2)</sup>

To commemorate his courage, Harald ordered a tomb built for Geirmund. Harald also had Sigdis interred there, so Geirmund could forever guard him. The tomb was built on an island in [Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md), which takes its name from the fallen Archmage.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DVN-2)</sup> Harald then began an age-long campaign to erase all knowledge of the Gauldur tale so that his powerful amulet would not be reforged and used to threaten Skyrim once more.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LL-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AE-5)</sup> Harald's campaign did not fully succeed, as in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), bards still sang about Geirmund's sacrifice, and there was even a festival to his memory, regularly held at the site of his tomb.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GO-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ON-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Runehild-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SJ-8)</sup> In time, however, the story became gradually forgotten, and by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), it was known as the obscure "Forbidden Legend" and few sources mentioned it. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) learned of the legend and disturbed Geirmund's tomb to retrieve Sigdis' portion of Gauldur's amulet.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LL-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Skyrim-9)</sup>
