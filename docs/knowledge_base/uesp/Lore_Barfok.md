— [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vivec-1)</sup> Barfok (also stylized as **BARFOK**), known as **the Maid of Planes**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vivec-1)</sup> was a powerful winged [Tongue](Lore_Tongue.md) and one of the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) war chiefs who took part in the [Conquest](Lore_Conquest_of_Morrowind.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) by the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vivec-1)</sup>

## History

### First Era
Little is known of Barfok's early life. She was daughter of Fokbar.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-500MC-2)</sup> The precise date of her birth is unknown, although she was alive during the lifespan of the Nordic Empire, in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) and the Conquest of Morrowind.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGEMW-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1ES-4)</sup> She was one of several war leaders known to wield the Voice vilified by Dunmer historical accounts as a "demon chieftain". According to the Vivec, winged human with lick-encrusted spear and wielded powers of Event Denouement. Any battle fought against her invariably ended in Barfok's favor, as she could bend events through her singing. Thanks to this power of enforcing her choices, she brought about the destruction of four [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) villages and two [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) strongholds. She was ultimately defeated by Vivec, who described his duel with Barfok as stuffing "her mouth with his milk finger to keep her from singing Veloth into ruin".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vivec-1)</sup>

### Legacy
Barfok was named by Vivec alongside [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md), the Mouth of Mud, [Chemua](Lore_Chemua.md), the Running Hunger, [Bhag](Lore_Bhag.md), the Two-Tongued, and [Ysmir, Dragon of the North Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), as one of the five greatest and most dangerous chieftains of the conquering Nords in his *[Sermons](Lore_The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec.md)*.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vivec-1)</sup>

## Notes
- Barfok's Mother, Fokbar, was said to be among the 500 companions. Recountings of the 500 nod at Barfok, noting that she was to be one who would "trouble the east."<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-500MC-2)</sup>
