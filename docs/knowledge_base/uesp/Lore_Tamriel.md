"Tamriel" means **Dawn's Beauty** in [Elvish](Lore_Elvish.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Arena Intro-15)</sup> The [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) believed that Tamriel is misnomer created by "deluded mortals" and that "Dawn's Beauty" is the most accurate name of the Land.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Mankar Dia-16)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Other cultures have had their own stylizations of the name: the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) name it "Tamri-El",<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-17)</sup> the [Marukhati](Lore_Marukh.md) call it "Tam! Rugh!",<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-RFM-18)</sup> and the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) entitled it "Taazokaan".<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SOSR-19)</sup>

Bitter years of endless war had its effect on the populace, and the name Tamriel "seldom fell from anguished lips." In a place where life and death were different sides of the same coin tossed every day, the common people began calling the land of their sorrow the **Arena**.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Arena Intro-15)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> This moniker is rooted in the metaphysics of the mundane plane itself, having been aboriginally born in "titanic battles" [at the youth of the world](Lore_War_of_Manifest_Metaphors.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TLL-20)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Ebonarm-21)</sup> and ever since being an eternally contested ground for mortal and immortal alike.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Mankar Dia-16)</sup> There have only been a few glorious brief moments in which the people of Tamriel stood united and victorious against a common enemy, such as the formation of the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) during the [Thrassian War](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) and the Invasion of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SH-22)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-WTC-23)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/MER-art-The_World.jpg/200px-MER-art-The_World.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_World.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_World.jpg) Tarot of the World
### Early Eras
Much of Tamriel's [history](Lore_History.md) is known and documented, but large swathes are still unknown, particularly the periods known as the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) and the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). Tamriel's very beginning can be traced to when the wandering [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) fought the Ehlnofey who remained settled in their former home. According to the narrative of the [Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md), the original singular landmass of Nirn was broken apart into at least four major landmasses—Tamriel, [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), and [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TAA-24)</sup> The wandering Ehlnofey were ancestors to all [men](Lore_Men.md), while the settled Ehlnofey were co-ancestors with the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) to all [mer](Lore_Mer.md). It was at the end of the Dawn Era that the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) of Aldmeris began to diverge along cultural and biological lines, starting the process that would lead to the modern mer races.

In the middle of the Merethic Era an unknown threat caused the Aldmeri to leave [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) and head towards Tamriel. They colonized many islands along its coast before settling on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). Later they started moving inland, settling many areas in southwest and central Tamriel. Wherever the Aldmer settled they displaced the primitive [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) (the ancestors of today's [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), etc.).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BTAOM-4)</sup> During this time the Ayleids built [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and eight lesser towers around it.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-AE4-25)</sup> Through powerful machinations and the position as the 'spoke' of the wheel within the lesser towers, White-Gold Tower became a very powerful tool—it is said that the tower is what stops [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) falling back into [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TIC-26)</sup>

Traditionally, the late Merethic Era is the time that men begin to appear on Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FC-3)</sup> This is not strictly true, as many races of men—such as the feathered men that [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) encountered, or a human race in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) —already resided on Tamriel.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-FOTN-27)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FC-3)</sup> Instead this time is taken as the period when the men of Atmora came in force to occupy and settle whole-scale on Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FC-3)</sup> These [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) were the ancestors of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). While many of the original races of men died out, many of them interbred enough to become one race known as the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3Cyrodiil-28)</sup> The conquering of northern Tamriel was sparked by the decline of Atmora and its eventual loss to a catastrophic change of climate from one that supported life to one of 'permanent winter' where no life could survive for long.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-29)</sup>

The [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) at the end of the Merethic Era ended the reign of the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TDW-30)</sup> In the third century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) saw the humans of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) overthrow their slavers, the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), marking the point at which humans began to dominate Tamrielic history.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TAOK-31)</sup> St. [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) made a covenant with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to create the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md). The covenant stated that so long as one of Alessia's heirs wore the amulet, the realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) would be cut off from Nirn.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TOSA-32)</sup>

Near the end of the seventh century, in [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668), [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) erupted for the first time in recorded history. 32 years later the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) occurred, in which the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) fought the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The end of the war came when all Dwemer simply vanished from Tamriel, leaving behind their many artifacts and strongholds. After the war the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) took over Morrowind, and shortly after that the Chimer were changed into the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TBORM-33)</sup>

The continent of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) sank in [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792), so its peoples, the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md), emigrated to the area now known as [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-29)</sup> The nature of the disaster is unknown but many natives think it was self-inflicted.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-THOS-34)</sup> The Yokudans, after conquering Hammerfell, took on many of the customs of the Ayleids, and after many centuries in the harsh desert conditions evolved into modern day [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md).

In [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200) the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) claimed the lives of more than half of Tamriel's population. The losses were concentrated to the west and south. The plague was particularly hard on the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). From sixteen harmonious kingdoms and twenty different types of Khajiit, the plague left them with catpower to form only two kingdoms. The often vastly different outlooks from the various kingdoms all shoved together led to conflict.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-PGE3Sugar-35)</sup> In response to the Plague the [All-Flags Navy](Lore_All-Flags_Navy.md) was formed to exact revenge on the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md). This was the first time all nations on Tamriel had worked together and it would have profound implications that would last for eras. In [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703) the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) from Akavir invaded Tamriel. [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) united to defeat them. The leader of the armies, [Reman](Lore_Reman.md), went on to found the Second Empire, which brought all the nations of Tamriel, except Morrowind, under the Empire's control as provinces until 2E 430.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-29)</sup>

### Second Era
In [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560) the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) spread from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) through the lower provinces and then into the other provinces killing many people. [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) were immune to the disease, leading to speculation they were responsible, but this proved to be incorrect. While the disease killed almost all non-Argonians in Black Marsh it was not as devastating elsewhere, though it did wipe out entire cities, religions, and cultures.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-PGE3Argonia-36)</sup> The Knahaten Flu had a big impact on events of the next three decades. The Altmer gave aid to the suffering Khajiit, who out of gratitude joined the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). The ruling family of Wayrest was wiped out, leading to [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) becoming king. Emeric spent the next fifteen years expanding the newly signed [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) to include [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md)'s neighbors in Hammerfell and [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md). The Argonians then banded together, as an equal partner, with the Nords and Dunmer in the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), to protect their interests against the other two alliances.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-LOTESO-37)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) broke out. Tensions had been simmering between the three alliances and the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), but the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) and the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) threw them into open conflict to try and gain control of the Ruby Throne and save Nirn from [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Molag Bal's plan was foiled but the outcome of the war for the throne is unknown.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESO-38)</sup> From [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852) to 854 a series of wars led by [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) and [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) led to the formation of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) under Tiber's rule. In 2E 896 Tiber conquered the Summerset Isles and for the first time all the provinces of Tamriel were united under one banner. The significance of the event led Tiber to declare a new era.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-39)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) occurred. Due to a lack of Alessian heirs to wear the Amulet of Kings, the Dragonfires died out. [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) attempted to invade Tamriel, destroying [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md). The Crisis ended when an illegitimate heir sacrificed himself to call Akatosh and defeat Dagon.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-OB-40)</sup> It was claimed that Nirn could not ever be invaded again by daedra from Oblivion. When the Crisis was over a new era was announced, making the Third Era the shortest complete era to date.

### Fourth Era
At the start of the Fourth Era Red Mountain erupted again. This time it destroyed the entire island and devastated the rest of Morrowind. The Argonians then invaded and unleashed their anger at centuries of oppression and slavery on the survivors, only being stopped on the banks of the [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TR-41)</sup> During the early Fourth Era the Empire started to crumble and then in 4E 48 [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md), a floating island from Oblivion appeared. The island made its way over Black Marsh to Morrowind, before turning and heading straight for the White-Gold Tower, causing massive devastation along the way. The island was summoned by a rogue [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree to kill all non-native Argonians, but quickly went its own way after appearing. The island was eventually stopped by the efforts of Prince [Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) and Annaïg Hoïnart. It disappeared leaving behind hordes of corpses by the walls of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TIC-26)</sup>

From [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171) to 175 the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) between the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) and the remainder of the Empire raged across Tamriel. The Thalmor leaders of the third [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), believed that mer should rule the continent, while the men who currently occupy the throne think different. The terms of the current peace treaty drove Hammerfell to abandon the Empire, and a civil war broke out in Skyrim—the result of which will determine if Skyrim will also secede.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TGW-42)</sup>

## Provinces
There are nine distinct provinces, each home to one of the more populous races of Tamriel. A proposed tenth province, Orsinium, has never been taken very seriously except by those who want it, namely the Orcs, who wish to have a home province of their own.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PGE3Orsinium-43)</sup>

### Black Marsh
[Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) is a dense swampland region of southeastern Tamriel, home to the reptilian humanoid race of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and a race of sapient trees known as the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) refer to the region as **Argonia**, named after an ancient battlefield. The Argonians do not have a word for their homeland in their [native language](Lore_Jel.md), in part because they do not consider it a singular nation. A member of the Argonian Gee-Rusleel tribe once used the wide-swamp gesture alongside the word **kronka-thatith** (roughly meaning "everything-egg") to describe the region, which is probably the closest non-Jel speakers will ever get in the way of a "true name" for the region. Many Argonians who live there struggle with the concept of it being a "province", as there is so much varied difference in each part of Black Marsh.

The Argonians thrive in the foreboding swamps of Black Marsh, a lush and threatening land teeming with poisonous plants and violent predators. The region's tropical climate lends its plants the capacity to overturn all attempts to cultivate them. Foreign agricultural, colonial, and commercial ventures beyond the slave-trade have met with abject failure. The native Argonians organize themselves on the tribal level with success and efficiency, and were only loosely integrated into the ruling [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) when Cyrodiilic Emperors deigned to lay claim to the land.

Aside from the Argonians, Black Marsh is host to a slew of other sapient peoples. The human inhabitants of Black Marsh ([Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) settlers from the Cyrodiilic Empire notwithstanding) include the [Horwalli](Lore_Horwalli.md), the [Yespest](Lore_Yespest.md), and the silver-skinned [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md). The [Orma](Lore_Orma.md), who are predisposed to blindness, are another Mannish race that inhabits the marsh. The serpent-bodied [Lamias](Lore_Lamia.md) and the vulpine [Lilmothiit](Lore_Lilmothiit.md) (the latter of which lived along the southern coasts) are notable instances of humanoid and semi-humanoid races that live in the region. The Kothringi and the Lilmothiit were driven to extinction by the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).

### Cyrodiil
[Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), sometimes shortened to **Cyrod** (and also known as the **Imperial Province**, **[Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) Empire**, **Red King**, **Starry Heart of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)**, or **Seat of Sundered Kings**), is a province in south-central Tamriel that is home to the human race known as [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md). As the central region of Tamriel, Cyrodiil is also known as "the Heartland." The capital of both Cyrodiil and the [Holy Cyrodilic Empire of Tamriel](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md), the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), is located on [City Isle](Lore_City_Isle.md) in the center of the province; at the heart of the city is [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), possibly the most renowned monument in Tamriel. The Imperial City itself has been considered the sole city-state of the Imperial Province, with [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) being outlying territories of the state of Cyrodiil. The name "Cyrodiil" itself is ancient elven in origin, and the "-iil" suffix it features means "folk." The name is a pretty blanket term almost every element of the Imperial Province: the people are called "[the Cyrodiils](Lore_Imperial.md)," the city-state of the land is called "[Cyrodiil](Lore_Imperial_City.md)," and the ruler of the land no matter their origin is called "[The Cyrodiil](Lore_Emperor.md)." This is derived from a king-is-the-land tradition that reaches back to when the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruled the land (hence the land's "Red King" sobriquet).

Despite some accounts describing Cyrodiil as a jungle, it has a primarily temperate climate. One scholar of the middle [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) attributed this discrepancy to errors made during the transcription of historical texts, while another speculated that White-Gold Tower itself adapted the climate to suit the region's inhabitants. Later sources asserted that Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) altered Cyrodiil's climate after he united all the provinces under his rule.

### Elsweyr
[Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) is a region that lies on the southern coast of Tamriel, and is home to the feline [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). It is divided into two major climates: savannahs, badlands and dry plains in the north, and fertile lands of jungle and rainforests in the south.

Originally, sixteen Khajiiti clans roamed the province, and were often simplified as belonging to a region or realm. With a combination of outside pressures and mastery of areas of expertise, it became more accurate to associate a clan with an area. Eventually, sixteen tribes became [sixteen kingdoms](Lore_Empires.md#Sixteen_Kingdoms_of_Elsweyr): [Ne-Quin-Al](Lore_Ne-Quin-Al.md), [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md), [Dune](Lore_Dune.md), [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md), [Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Elsweyr).md), [Meirvale](Lore_Meirvale.md), and [Helkarn](Lore_Helkarn.md) in the north; [Alabaster](Lore_Alabaster.md), [Bruk'ra](Lore_Bruk%27ra.md), [Pa'alatiin](Lore_Pa%27alatiin.md), [Corinthe](Lore_Corinthe.md), [Tenmar](Lore_Tenmar.md), [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), [Khenarthia](Lore_Khenarthia.md), and [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) in the south. The lullaby known as the Hasa Zha'ja explains how these clans got their name, while the poem known as Zhan Zhab tells of the transition from clan names being used to refer to the names of regions. Due to the Thrassian Plague, these sixteen clans changed their tribal functions or they may have died due to starvation and other hardships. These clans essentially became two and were separated ideologically and geographically as what we would come to know as the two kingdoms of Anequina and Pellitine. These two kingdoms would eventually be united into the [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md), and once more separated in two kingdoms in the [Third Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).

### Hammerfell
[Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), once known as **Hegathe**, the **Deathland** (or **Deathlands**), and **Volenfell**, is a region in the west of Tamriel. It has been described as a province, kingdom, or republic throughout its history. Situated between [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), Hammerfell features diverse landscapes including beaches, jungles, grasslands, mountains, swamps, and the vast [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) desert. It is inhabited by the human race of [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), who migrated to Tamriel following the partial sinking of their homeland, [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md).

### High Rock
[High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) is a province in northwestern Tamriel. Most of the province is inhabited by the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), who have divided the province into multiple Breton city states and minor kingdoms. High Rock has also historically contained [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), the City-State of the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md). High Rock encompasses the many lands and clans of [Greater Bretony](Lore_Greater_Bretony.md), the [Dellese Isles](Lore_Dellese_Isles.md), the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) [tribes](Lore_River_Horse_Bretons.md), and, by tradition, the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md). The [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) once controlled the entire province until the dissolution of their [Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), leaving behind many ruins and a lasting legacy on the newly formed Breton city-states in their wake. The rugged highland strongholds and isolated valley settlements have encouraged the fierce independence of the various local Breton clans, resisting integration into a formal province or [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) identity. Nonetheless, their language, bardic traditions, and heroic legends are a unifying legacy. Aside from the Bretons and their ancestors, other races that have inhabited the province include [centaurs](Lore_Centaur.md), [giants](Lore_Giant.md), [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), Orcs, and [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md).

### Morrowind
[Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), previously named **Dwemereth**, **Veloth**, **Resdayn**, **Resdaynia**, and **Dunmereth**, and also known as the **Land of Ash**, is the nation in the northeast corner of Tamriel, and the home of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). It is dominated by the large island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and its centerpiece, the ash-spewing [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), but also includes territory on the continental mainland. The [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) separates Vvardenfell from the mainland, and the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) lies to the region's north and east. [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), an island in this sea near northwestern Morrowind, has not traditionally been associated with any particular province, though Morrowind had long maintained a theoretical claim to it. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) conceded the island to Morrowind in [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16) following the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), allowing the Dunmer to settle it without contest.

### Skyrim
[Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), the northernmost province of Tamriel, is a cold and mountainous region also known as the **Old Kingdom**, **Mereth**, or the **Fatherland**, or **Keizaal** in the [dragon tongue](Lore_Dragon_Language.md). Many past battles have given it a ravaged appearance and many ruins. Though currently inhabited primarily by [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), the [Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) who they replaced had resided there since time immemorial. The sovereign, the High King of Skyrim, is chosen by the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md), a convention of [jarls](Skyrim_Jarl.md). A jarl is a regional ruler chosen through heredity and, rarely, through right of arms. The High King typically rules until death, though acts of dishonor, particularly the appearance of cowardice, can lead to the recall and reconsideration of the Moot. Since the [Pact of Chieftains](Lore_Pact_of_Chieftains.md) was signed in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), the Moot does not give serious consideration to anyone but the High King's direct heir unless one is not available.

For more information, see the [main Lore article](Lore_Skyrim.md).

### Summerset Isles
The [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) (sometimes spelled **Sumurset**, **Summurset** or **Sumerset**),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> officially named **Alinor** since [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), is a province in southwest Tamriel consisting of fourteen islands of varying size. The three largest and most significant islands are [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), and [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), the latter of which is apt to disappear for sizable lengths of time without warning. It was settled and ruled under the [Kingdom of Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) in ancient times, but in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) seized power and renamed the province after its capital, [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md), though many outside the province still refer to it as Summerset. The current inhabitants of the province are the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), though [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) were there when the elves first arrived. It is believed that the first inhabitants were the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) before they were driven away by the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), whom the Altmer claim to be directly descended from.

### Valenwood
[Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) is a densely forested, sub-tropical region that encompasses the southwestern coastal plain of Tamriel. In the words of *A Pocket Guide to the Empire*, Valenwood is "a sea of endless green, a maze of foliage with half-hidden cities growing like blooms from a flower, the home of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) is Tamriel's garden." Alongside the Wood Elves, races like the [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), [Imga](Lore_Imga.md), and [Centaur](Lore_Centaur.md) also call the forest their home. One unusual feature of Valenwood is its gigantic, [migratory trees](Lore_Graht-oak.md), many so large that the Bosmer have built entire cities in their branches. [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) is one such tree, a mile-high specimen that serves as the capital of Valenwood and seat of its kings. The forest is often referred to by Bosmer as 'the Valenwood', designating the province as an entity in its own right.

## Notable Landmasses
There are various regions surrounding the mainland that are considered part of Tamriel. They vary between being considered territory of different provinces throughout history or functioning independently.

### Solstheim
[Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) (meaning Sun's Home) is a large island in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) to the north of Tamriel. The [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) were the first inhabitants of the region, and built many grand settlements that far outlasted their mainland holdings. It officially became a region of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16). The island has a long history of habitation by other races, but it has traditionally been primarily [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) in influence.

### Solstice
[Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) (or **Oztet-Ta** *"Sprouting Root"* in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md)) is an isolated tropical island within the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md), located south of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The island has a sizeable [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) population who largely descended from settlers that arrived with the [Corelanya Clan](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md). The [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) that inhabit the island came before the [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) period occurred. It has been described as a "paradise".

### Stirk
[Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) is an island off the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). Initially settled by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) and legendary for being where the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) was founded, it has since been used by pirates for settling disputes through trial by combat. Though claimed at times by [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), Cyrodiil, and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), the uninhabited island is really a no-man's-land. Its neutral status during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) made [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) choose it as a meeting ground for the leaders of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) to launch their attack on [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), as organized by the guilds.

### Systres
The [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) (also the called the **Systres Archipelago**, **Systres Isles**, **Systres Islands**, and formally the **Duchy of Systres**) are a group of islands in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), far to the southwest of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and northwest of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). It is one of Tamriel's westernmost regions. The largest island is named [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), where [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) rules the archipelago from their seat of power in Castle Navire; the jungled prison colony of [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md) lies to the north. The third largest island is [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) to the northwest, where [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) operates from the city of [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md). The fourth largest island, [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md), is north of High Isle and northwest of Amenos. It contains a semi-dormant volcano. The nearest islands are [Pankor](Lore_Pankor.md) to the northwest and [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md) to the southeast; another group of three smaller islands are found directly west, southwest of Pankor.

### Topal Island
[Topal Island](Lore_Topal_Island.md) is a far-off island region (sometimes depicted as a chain of three islands), located in the middle of [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The island region is located roughly southeast of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) and northwest of [Soulrest](Lore_Soulrest.md), adjacent to the strip of land around [Xi-Tsei](Lore_Xi-Tsei.md) that belongs to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Additionally, the volcanic islet of [Grand Topal Hideaway](Lore_Grand_Topal_Hideaway.md) lies off the eastern coast of Topal Island.

The island shares its namesake with Topal Bay, which was named after [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md).

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/AR-map-Terrain.png/200px-AR-map-Terrain.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Terrain.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Terrain.png) Dragon's Teeth Mountains - When asked about who designed the Tamriel map, the original developers had this to say:

- - [Vijay Lakshman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Vijay_Lakshman): *That is crazy as a question, ultimately because there's so many people who touch the map as you end up getting from what's original scribbled down to what it becomes in the final game. But I remember I sketched out the first map, then Ted and I worked on all kinds of different parts of it, and then you hand it over to the artists, and what you get back always looks so much better than what you had envisioned. But I think ultimately questions like: "who did what?" is probably gonna be answered with: "team effort," everyone is trying to make the product the best it can be. My only contribution was recognizing how rivers run to the ocean, which is probably the only part of it that lasted.*
[Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson): *I remember when you had that map, and it was several sheets of paper spread across your desk, and that [you were like]: "just come in here and start filling in capitals, and names of cities, and names of places!" We did things like, [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), [which] was named that because were just like "Oh and that's... elsewhere. Another place. We'll just play around with the letters of that."*<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-OLAMA-44)</sup>

- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* established a continent spanning mountain range in the west called the [Dragon's Teeth](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md), which began northern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), spined through [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), cross the [Imperial Province](Lore_Imperial_Province.md), ending in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Ria Dialogue-45)</sup> Subsequent titles after *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* have neglected to support this detail, instead referring to its subranges such as the [Druadach](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md) and [Jeralls](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md),<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Oblivion-46)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESO-38)</sup> though the Dragon's Teeth are referred to in *[The Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)*.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-AP-47)</sup>
- The lore of *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* attempted to establish a continent-spanning stream called the [Yrinthi River](Lore_Yrinthi_River.md), which connected the [Imperial Province](Lore_Imperial_Province.md) to the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) of the northwest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DUG-1)</sup><sup>:5</sup> Subsequent titles have neglected to support this detail in-game.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Oblivion-46)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESO-38)</sup>
- In [certain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles) [early](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall) maps of Tamriel from the development of *Arena* and *Daggerfall*, several sets of what appear to be islands or clouds can be seen. Two are located west of High Rock, one north of Morrowind (where Solstheim would later be placed), and one west of Morrowind, beneath the [Northeast Archipelago](Lore_Northeast_Archipelago.md). However, whether or not these were actually intended to be land masses is unconfirmed.
- According to [*Arena Player's Guide*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Arena_Player%27s_Guide), the continent of Tamriel is sized roughly: **3,000 to 4,000 kilometers from east to west**, and **2,000 to 3,000 kilometers from north to south**.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ARMIS-48)</sup><sup>:55</sup> If you compare this to measurements presented in the later lore and or marketing materials advertising worldscales, the *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* measurements aren't consistent. However, [Todd Howard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Todd_Howard) has indicated that the *Arena* manual can be a rather authoritative source when it establishes something specific—towering above other later references in some cases.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Todd25th-49)</sup> The dialogue said by [Ria Silmane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Ria_Silmane) in *Arena* in regards to being able to spy the Dragon's Teeth mountains from atop the Crystal Tower would seemingly support the measurement presented in the *Arena Player's Guide*.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ARSilmane-50)</sup> The official readme for the *Arena* demo states that the world is **twelve million square kilometers**.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-51)</sup> While the official website source created after *Arena* released states that the world is **eight million square kilometers** in size.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Arena Website-52)</sup> During the development of *Daggerfall* in 1996, Ted Peterson stated that Tamriel overall is about the same size as [Canada](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada)<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-53)</sup> (which is approximately **4,634 kilometers from north to south** and **7,560 kilometers from east to west**). - The first *[Pocket Guide to the Empire](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Morrowind.md#Vvardenfell)* states the peak of [Dagoth-Ur](Lore_Dagoth-Ur_(place).md) can be seen from [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), "250 miles to the south."<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-PGE1M-54)</sup> While calculations derived from this figure can differ wildly due to various factors—including what map of Tamriel is used—they will always result in Tamriel's size being significantly smaller than what is given by the *Arena* materials. However, going by the measurement presented in fast travel from *Arena*, the [Mount of Dagoth-Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Dagoth-Ur) is located 860 kilometers (534 miles) from [Mournhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Mournhold) —which is more than double what the *Pocket Guide* states.
