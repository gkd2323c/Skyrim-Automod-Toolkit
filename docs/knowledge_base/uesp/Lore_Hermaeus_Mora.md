—Hermaeus Mora<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOEyeof Fate-1)</sup> Hermaeus Mora (sometimes spelled "**Hoermius**", "**Hormaius**", "**Herm'us**", or "**Herma** **Mora**")<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Fathoms-4)</sup> is the **Daedric Prince of Forbidden Knowledge**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFHermaeush Dialogue-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFHermaeus-7)</sup> hidden shoals, whirlpools, and sudden memory".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:585</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TBOD-11)</sup> His curiosity is insatiable and he seeks all knowledge, and not to change but to observe, so that all becomes known.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONIbrula-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-FOC-13)</sup> He is known by many titles, such as the **Demon of Knowledge**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> the **One Men**,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DBGardener-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-16)</sup> **Prince of Fate**, **Lord of Secrets**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONIbrula-12)</sup> the **Keeper of Forbidden Knowledge**,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-17)</sup><sup>:293</sup> the **Golden Eye**,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AA-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LWOAD-19)</sup> the **Great Eye**,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Dralys Dia-20)</sup> the **Eye that Wanders**, the **Eye-of-All** or **Eye-Who-Sees-All**,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Jot FR-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Tyss-22)</sup> **Ur-Daedra**,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SOIA-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-In Memory Of-25)</sup> the **Abyssal and Knowledge**.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-HK-27)</sup> To the ancient [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), he is **Herma-Mora**, the **Woodland He "weeps cold tears" at the plague of ignorance, where knowledge is forsaken out of fear of misuse, and he allegedly seeks to enlighten all to all mysteries that have ever and will ever exist.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AA-18)</sup> One follower of Mora stated that the ultimate purpose of the Daedra lords is to instruct and improve the "generally deplorable" character of mortals.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-RGClavicus-32)</sup> This benevolence presented by his followers may be a façade, as Mora is said to hoard his knowledge and give nothing away for free.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-17)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DBNeloth-9)</sup> Like all Daedric Princes, he's not to be trifled with, but he is subtler than most of his ilk and ensnares mortals with his secrets.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DBNeloth-9)</sup> He may seem cruel at times, but he's not irrational.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONIbrula-12)</sup> Mora hoards and safeguards any knowledge that could potentially endanger reality.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-AHF-33)</sup> He is described as crafty and good at hiding things, and is said to love traps.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Fox-34)</sup> According to some sources, the Mythos, the core of Mora's realm of Apocrypha, is where the Prince "whispered the first secret" and "imagined the concept of knowledge", thus also conceiving Apocrypha itself in that same act.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Mythos Screen-35)</sup>

Always lurking, he is the void and the ever-seeing eyes.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:585</sup> Mora has been called the wisest of the Daedric Princes, with a mind as old as [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and a body of slime,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFHermaeus-7)</sup> though he describes himself as *"the riddle unsolveable. The door unopenable. The book unreadable. The question unanswerable."*<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOFlooded-36)</sup> Unlike most Princes, Hermaeus Mora typically does not take on a humanoid form, manifesting instead as black clouds of varied, grotesque assemblages of eyes, tentacles, and claws, or a featureless purple vortex known as the Wretched Abyss,<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-DFAppearance-37)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SRAppearance-38)</sup>. However, according to some sources, he occasionally took on a form resembling that of a mortal.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Roleplay-39)</sup> He speaks with a thundering bray,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-40)</sup> and has been described as being composed of countless eyes or "orbs without end".<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-HRSt BTK-41)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-WIP-42)</sup> The energies and essences of both Hermaeus Mora and [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) are said to take the form of "roiling darkness", and are so similar to one another that additional decorative elements are required to determine which of the two an artifact belongs to. This strong similarity has caused some scholars to theorize that the two Daedric Princes share a close relationship and are linked to one another in some manner.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Trifold-43)</sup>

According to Mora himself, he is "multitudes", and only adopts a singular guise for the sake of the mortals, whose minds would crumble when confronted with such a concept.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Roleplay-39)</sup> The form in which he appears before mortals is described as a "delimited projection", although he is said to not communicate with them often.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup> The [Watchlings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) of the Prince are known to sometimes serve as his proxies, as he can see through their eyes and even take control of their forms to speak through them.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Scruut Dream-44)</sup> It is said that all statues of Hermaeus Mora serve as access points from his realm to Nirn, thus one should think carefully before placing a representation of the Prince close to their person.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Statuette-45)</sup> Mora can be invoked in certain locations in Nirn using a "condensate of planar possibilities", which takes a form similar to ink to summon him.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup> He is said to always be using [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) as a result of always being involved in various ongoing projects.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Fox-34)</sup>

He resides everywhere and nowhere but takes glee in tormenting mortals from his realm in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), the [plane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Oblivion) called [Apocrypha](#Apocrypha): an endless library where the infinite pages of countless books containing all forbidden knowledge are found.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:585</sup> Each book sports a black cover without a title, and the realm is reported to be haunted by ghosts forever searching for knowledge.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AA-18)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TDOO-46)</sup> His servants include [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) such as [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md), [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md), and [Watchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher), but he is known to have mortal worshipers too.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-47)</sup> Sailors and seafaring folk refer to **Herm'us Mora** as the **Tide King**, master of the seas and One-Whose-Tentacles-Rise-From-the-Deep,<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Azandar-48)</sup> a being who lurks in the oceans and pulls vessels down into the abyss.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-CDJ2-49)</sup> Similarly, the Khajiiti Hermorah controls the tides and lives in a vast library under the sea.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TWS-31)</sup>

Hermaeus Mora is tangentially related to the origins of the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) by association with his sibling, [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> Mora's enemies are said to be [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm) and [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina),<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-DFFactions-50)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-AUF-51)</sup> and his [summoning day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hermaeus_Mora%27s_Summoning_Day) is the 5<sup>th</sup> of First Seed.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-52)</sup> [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) considers Mora among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ONAzura-53)</sup> According to Mora's [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md), due to the Compact, the Prince made a pact with *every* mortal on Nirn to stop directly interfering in [Mundial](Lore_Mundus.md) affairs, and was paid something he dearly desired in exchange. The compact appeared to bind in both directions, thus, mortals cannot come to Apocrypha as they did before.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-SOi A-54)</sup>

## Mythic Origins
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fc/ON-place-The_Mythos.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Mythos.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos.jpg) The Mythos, core of Apocrypha [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/ON-place-Syzygial_Rostrum.jpg/200px-ON-place-Syzygial_Rostrum.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Syzygial_Rostrum.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Syzygial_Rostrum.jpg) Massive fossils, a common sight in Apocrypha *A forbidden place located deep beneath the Ichor Sea, the Mythos is where Hermaeus Mora conceived Apocrypha. Here, the Great Eye whispered the first secret. Here, the One Who Knows imagined the concept of knowledge.*
—Leramil the Wise<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Mythos-55)</sup> Hermaeus Mora claims that he is one of the oldest Princes, born of thrown-away ideas used during the [creation](Lore_Convention.md) of mortality in the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), even calling himself the "first secret whispered at the [dawn](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) of creation",<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOEyeof Fate-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-16)</sup> and has been described as "elder than Ada".<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-FAHM-26)</sup> [Imperial Mananauts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Mananauts) have verified that his influence on fate and time is real and unfeigned, implying that Mora is tied directly with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), the prime temporal spirit whose appearance led to the formation of the world, and therefore Mora may be telling the truth.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-16)</sup> As some Black Books appear to have been written in the past, and others might be from the future, time appears to be more malleable for the Daedric Prince of fate and destiny.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DBNeloth-9)</sup>

Hermaeus Mora's primordial nature connects to his ancient connection to the sea.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Do A-56)</sup> The gigantic fossilized remains of ancient sea creatures, not known on Nirn, dot his realm of Apocrypha. Faithful of Mora believe that these fossils are remnants of another place or realm, the origin point of the Daedric Prince of Knowledge.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Fossil-57)</sup> Antediluvian hunting horns carved from these fossils have been found, engraved with depictions of Mora driving beasts before him.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-58)</sup> Apocrypha was said to have been created by Mora in a location now known as the Mythos, beneath the Ichor Sea. It is there where he whispered the first secret, and first imagined the concept of knowledge.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Mythos-55)</sup> The exact nature of Hermaeus Mora's supposed origin point is unclear.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## Mythology and Worship
*Between the seen and unseen, the seeming and the being, lies the gulf of ignorance. The fate of man drifts like seaweed in the net of His mind.*
—Bruscius Longus and Norasa Adus [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/ON-tribute-mora-Bargain_for_Knowledge.png/200px-ON-tribute-mora-Bargain_for_Knowledge.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-mora-Bargain_for_Knowledge.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-mora-Bargain_for_Knowledge.png) A mortal bargaining for knowledge with Hermaeus Mora Hermaeus Mora has always tried to seduce mortals into his service with the lure of forbidden knowledge, and many scholars and loremasters find themselves ensnared forever by the lure of secrets only he possesses. Documented indications of Mora's permanent influence include incipient madness, loss of self-awareness, and black spots in the whites of the eyes.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DBNeloth-9)</sup> Mora's knowledge reaches beyond mortal understanding but is evidently not infinite, as he still needs worshippers to dig up secrets for him.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONIbrula-12)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-59)</sup>

Worshippers of Hermaeus Mora, who seek lost tomes to bring to Apocrypha, will sometimes paint their bodies with markings resembling tentacles in his honor. Bearers of such markings cause mixed feelings in librarians, who view them with admiration for their erudition in seeking their tomes, but also fear the covetousness that can drive agents of the Golden Eye. <sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Abyssal Body Art-60)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Abyssal Face Art-61)</sup> Mora has been known to sometimes gift his faithful with an Apocrypha Corrupted Eye, transforming one of their eyes to give it the ability see the hidden secrets of reality and to uncover the truth on the page.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Corrupted Eye-62)</sup> One worshipper of Hermaeus Mora asked the Prince for "ears that could listen to dreams", and was granted his wish. The boon soon turned into a curse, however, as he became doomed to "wander a forest of waking horrors", causing him to disappear and leave behind his pet bunny, which bore the signs of Apocrypha.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Abyssal Bunny-63)</sup>

### Ayleids
The Ayleid city of [Bisnensel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bisnensel) began to worship Hyrma Mora under the lead of High Priest Uluscant, who established a congregation that attracted many of the learned and most influential people in the community. As their numbers became more numerous, on their behalf Uluscant requested more of a say in municipal affairs, a request that was denied by King Dynar. Within a few months of his rebuff, Dynar was deposed.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BOAR-30)</sup> The Ayleids of Bisnensel turned the city into a shrine and stored forbidden knowledge of the natural order, which could allow one to manipulate the very fabric of reality.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-64)</sup> Seeking to secure his domain from the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), Uluscant later made a pact with Hermaeus Mora to conceal Bisnensel under a "veil of [illusion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion) and secrecy", so that no army that reached the shores of Halcyon Lake would be able to pierce the veil or even remember their purpose in marching to that location.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-UM-65)</sup>

In [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen), an Ayleid sorcerer who revered Hermaeus Mora is said to have created a magical library, which drew books to the location from different time periods, including the future.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONGRL-66)</sup>

### Bosmeri Cults
To the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), Herma-Mora is a malicious trickster spirit (one of many). Nevertheless, the Woodland Man commands a large number of devotees. However, Mora cultists in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) insist that *their* Herma-Mora is different from the Daedric Prince Hermaeus Mora.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-VOFWE-28)</sup> One such group was a [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) cult, the [Wooded Eye](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wooded_Eye), who venerated Mora and served as guardians of the Cradle of Ilmyris—a library of secret knowledge.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONOrthenir-67)</sup>

### Dunmeri Cults
The Daedra are tied closely to the history of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) people, for good or ill. In [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), Mora is known as the **Golden Eye** (or simply **The Eye**),<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Lot GE-68)</sup> **He Who Records All Knowledge**, the **Abider in the Abyss**, **Keeper of the Oghma Infinium**, **Master of Apocrypha**, **He Who Holds the Paper to the Light**,<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-PGE1I-69)</sup> and the **Master of the Oghma**.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-AG-70)</sup> To him, knowledge and memory are the only treasures of value, gathering all words and all thoughts in the realm of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). He has cults guarding secret libraries, such as the Devotees of Knowledge, who seek enlightenment, illumination, and a place at his side.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AA-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LWOAD-19)</sup> They believe that while the Golden Eye is shunned by the crude ignorant [Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) and followers of [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) alike for his wickedness, he cares little and instead watches them to document their every move. Knowledge is only as wicked as the one who wields it, and forsaking learning in fear of its misuse is an unforgivable folly that leads to mortals suffering countless centuries of loss. It is believed that pages are how Mora sees the world, so he sends them out on the winds and they fill with stories. They collect information about the events around them, and when they're brought back to the book, Hermaeus learns.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AA-18)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Lot GE-68)</sup> When the [Dagonites](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) burned down their library and they could not salvage their books, the devotees followed the principle of knowledge gained for knowledge lost; they drained new memories from Dagon's [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) to replace what they lost, granting their lord new knowledge on how to counter Dagon in the future.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LWOAD-19)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-AG-70)</sup>

The Devotees believe that by dedicating ones energy, mind, and soul to Mora, he may grant them admittance into his hall of all knowledge.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Lot GE-68)</sup> In Apocrypha, they are permitted to browse the infinite tomes and scrolls, privy to all mysteries that have ever and will ever exist, and achieve the most blessed fate of finally sating their thirst for knowledge.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AA-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LWOAD-19)</sup>

### Imperial
Imperial law by [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) decreed that only on the official holiday of 5th First Seed should any propitiation to Hermaeus Mora be delivered, with it being stated that *"All else is mutation."*.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-16)</sup>

Hermaeus Mora is one of the 16 acceptable blasphemes, with it being said that *"HERMAEUS MORA holds the Paper to the Light."*<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-PGE1I-69)</sup>

### Nordic Tales
In ancient [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), Herma-Mora was also known as the **Woodland Man** (though unlike the Bosmer, the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) acknowledge his Daedric heritage). Later research has indicated this may better translate to "that which speaks in the wastes."<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-HMTWM-29)</sup>

Most myths involving [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) are about his escaping the devious schemes of Herma-Mora. He is acknowledged as a testing god.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-VOFTN-71)</sup> Mora shares many similarities with [Jhunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jhunal), which may be linked to why the latter fell out of favor in the pantheon.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MKP-72)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Abyssal_Bunny_02.jpg) A Hare associated with Herma-Mora (ESO) One fable in particular is known: Ysgramor was hunting in the [Atmoran Frostwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atmoran_Frostwood) when he sighted the White Stag of Forelgrim. After losing its trail many times, a [Hare](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hare) appeared, and told Ysgramor where the White Stag had gone. When Ysgramor inquired as to how the Hare knew this, it replied that it could hear it with its long ears, adding that if Ysgramor had long ears he could hear his prey wherever it went.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-FAHM-26)</sup>

Ysgramor idly wished his ears were as long as the Hare's; the Hare twitched its nose and Ysgramor's ears became long and pointed. Suddenly, a [Fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fox) leapt out and killed the Hare. The Fox then identified itself as [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor) and the Hare as Herma-Mora, and chided Ysgramor for nearly allowing himself to be turned into an [Elf](Lore_Mer.md). Shor then sent Ysgramor on his way, warning him to *"eschew the tricks of the Elves, lest ye become one."*<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-FAHM-26)</sup>

Nords keep small statues of testing gods in their households and try to keep them appeased.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Contraband N-73)</sup>

### Khajiit
*Hermorah, you are the Tides, for who can say whether the moons predict the tides or the tides predict the moons?*
—Fadomai, *[Words of Clan Mother Ahnissi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Words_of_Clan_Mother_Ahnissi)* Hermorah is known to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) as the **Watcher**, **Spirit of the Tides**,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TWS-31)</sup> and the **Tide-King**,<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-EOTSOA2-74)</sup> is the Keeper of the Knowledge of Others.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TWS-31)</sup> He was born of the second litter of [Fadomai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fadomai).<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-WOCMA-75)</sup> Traditionally, Hermorah records all he sees in his vast submarine library. [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah) is a frequent visitor to his domain, and Hermorah shares all he knows with her. He also helps Azurah maintain the Moons and their [Motions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ja-Kha%27jay).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TWS-31)</sup> In his form as the Tide-King, he has been depicted as a multi-eyed sea snake with sharp fangs.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Wot TK-76)</sup>

However, the advent of [Riddle'Thar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar) worship brought a change in thinking. Thus, more recent writings caution against Hermorah worship, describing communion with the spirit as "a fate worse than death", that will "tear a mind asunder" and "shred our sense of reality".<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-EOTSOA2-74)</sup> A cult dedicated to Hermorah known as the Golden Eye was active in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) during the Interregnum, where they communed at the Shrine of the Golden Eye hidden with Claw's Strike.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-EOE-77)</sup>

One Khajiit myth recounts how the thief god [Rajhin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rajhin) became curious about the treasures Hermaeus Mora kept within his library beneath the sea. Sailing to a lonely spire of rock that was the topmost spire of Mora's abode within the far reaches of the southern seas, Rajhin entered the Tide King's halls. Rajhin saw many strange things within Mora's halls, but was finally drawn to a single book resting on a podium, the [Oghma Infinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma_Infinium). As Rajhin stretched his hand to grab the tome, Mora discovered him. Though Mora knew Rajhin was present, he could not see him, as he was shrouded by the [Ring of Khajiiti](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_Khajiiti). The cunning Rajhin claimed to be but a mote in Mora's eye, and asked Mora to bring out more of his eyes so he could see that said claim was true. Finally, Mora was tricked into bringing forth all of his countless eyes at once, so many that it was an effort to manifest them all simultaneously, thus giving Rajhin the chance to escape with the tome before the Prince could get his bearings and look again.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-HRSt BTK-41)</sup>

The Ciphers serve as the caretakers and curators of the vast amounts of knowledge that constantly rain down upon Apocrypha from the rest of reality, sorting and cataloging the ever-changing stacks and volumes that form hills and canyons within Apocrypha, and consist of a small army of catalogers, researchers, and librarians.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Ciphers-89)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Leramil Hidden-90)</sup> Ciphers can often be found serving as caretakers for the various facilities of Apocrypha such as the Sidereal Cloisters where fate is scried and prophecies are kept,<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Meenai-91)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Observer-92)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup> the Rectory Corporea where minds connected in a singular mindscape dream and teach in service to Mora,<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Chorus-94)</sup> the Tranquil Catalog which contains Apocrypha's index,<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-Mukheesh-95)</sup> the Feral Gallery where Mora keeps specimens from Nirn,<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Zenas-96)</sup>, Quires Wind, the bindery where Mora's Black Books are put together for the Prince to scribe,<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-Agrushna-97)</sup> and the Disquiet Study, which breaths life into its books, thus giving their words power and substance, to the extent of allowing them to leap from the page and manifest into reality.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-Disquiet-98)</sup><sup>[[95]](#cite_note-Ysgild-99)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-Bartalus-100)</sup> They also carry out tasks within sub-realms of Apocrypha such as the [Infinite Panopticon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Panopticon) and [Infinite Archive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Archive).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-WIP-42)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Destozuno-83)</sup><sup>[[97]](#cite_note-ESOMM-101)</sup> The Ciphers have built their own settlement within Apocrypha to serve as their headquarters, called [Cipher's Midden](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cipher%27s_Midden). In addition to their role as caretakers of the realm's knowledge, they are also known to offer hospitality and refuge to mortals visiting the realm, and it is said that Apocrypha could not function as it does without them.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Ciphers-89)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Leramil Hidden-90)</sup>

## History
[![SR-item-Black Book Cover 02.gif](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/SR-item-Black_Book_Cover_02.gif/200px-SR-item-Black_Book_Cover_02.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Black_Book_Cover_02.gif) At some unknown point in time, the group of [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) that became the [Remnants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remnants_Clan) began alone and drifting through the Void. They heard the voice of Mora communicating a deep need for champions to defend his realms of Apocrypha against invaders seeking to steal knowledge. Mora had selected the Remnants for this task and they obliged, discovering two items upon arrival in Apocrypha: a spyglass and a key. They each radiated a power that surged from Apocrypha through the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) and into a place they did not know. Their essences merged with these relics to open a gateway into a small pocket realm known as the Crux, and their minds and body merged into one whole hivemind in service of Mora as they entered of. A written pact awaiting the Remnants already contained their signatures, confirming to them that serving the realm had been their fate all along.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Jot FR-21)</sup>

According to *Herma-Mora and the Moth*, Hermaeus Mora long ago sought the secrets of [ancestor moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md) and their connections to the [fjyrons](Lore_Fjyron.md) of mortal souls, so that he may access the secrets of the Elder Scrolls. He encountered a lone moth who refused to give away any confidential information but managed to bargain with the Great Eye a deal. To showcase that the Daedric Prince was wise and merciful despite greatly wanting to obtain the secrets behind the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), ancestor moths from then on would wear his eyes on their wings, branded on them by the Prince himself.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-LAESMP-104)</sup>

### Merethic Era

#### Adversary of the Skaal
The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) consider Herma-Mora to have always been their enemy, and they have many tales of Mora trying to trick them into giving up their secrets to him.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-Storn CS-105)</sup> At some point in the Mythic Era,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:585</sup> the dragon priest [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md) sought out [the teachings](Lore_Black_Books.md) of Hermaeus Mora, and became his champion. Mora taught Miraak the power to bend the will of dragons.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DBHermaeus-14)</sup> Another Dragon Priest, Vahlok the Jailor, uncovered this deception, and the two engaged in a duel so vicious and destructive that the modern island of Solstheim was ripped from the mainland of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-TGATT-106)</sup><sup>[[103]](#cite_note-Tharstan-107)</sup> Vahlok defeated Miraak, but just as he prepared to deliver the killing blow, Hermaeus Mora snatched away and saved his champion.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-TGATT-106)</sup> Though his followers were killed or scattered, and his temple razed, Miraak was able to escape into Mora's realm of Apocrypha, where he had been growing in knowledge and power over the centuries while he waited for a chance to return.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:585</sup>

#### Imprisonment of Ithelia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_Vaults.jpg) The spire within the Mythos that served as Ithelia's Prison [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png/200px-ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Ithelia%27s-Prison.png) Ithelia's Prison At some point in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) or [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), after the Daedric Prince [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia) used her power to alter [fate](Lore_Fate.md) to avert a coming catastrophe, Mora examined her fate threads and perceived that Ithelia's power had already caused irreparable damage to the fabric of fate and that, if allowed to continue, her very existence endangered all of reality. Mora's fears intensified when Ithelia created her [Loom of the Untraveled Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road), a great fate altering engine via which she intended to tap into the power of the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) and give every being their own desired history, breaking the shackles of fate. Perceiving that the Many Paths were splintering under Ithelia's "unnatural manipulations" and foreseeing that reality would shatter in the attempt to achieve what Ithelia desired if the Loom was used, Mora took action.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Vargas-109)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Untraveled-110)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-111)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_03.jpg) The submerged shrine of Hermaeus Mora that serves as a portal to Apocrypha, within the Inner Sea [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg) Hermaeus Mora discussing the threat of Ithelia Eventually, an alliance formed between Hermaeus Mora, [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). Before they acted, Mora warned Ithelia to abandon her plan to use the Loom and alter fate at the city of [Niryastare](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niryastare), but she refused.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-Relics-112)</sup> Each of the three Princes allied with Mora took possession of their chosen mortal champions and met at a submerged shrine of Hermaeus Mora in the ruined city of [Anudnabia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anudnabia), which had been dragged beneath the waves by Ithelia's fateweaving. There, they entered a hidden [Demiplane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) imperceptible to her powers, where they could plan their attack in secrecy. Each Prince crafted a weapon to counteract Ithelia’s fate-manipulating abilities. To track her, Mephala forged the Skein of Secrets, enabling her to locate Ithelia and her Unseen by tracing the remnants of their power, normally invisible to magical observation.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Beragon-113)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup> To perceive her, Azura created the [Lamp of Clarity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamp_of_Clarity), illuminating Ithelia, her Unseen, and any open portals to the Path that could reach her.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Beragon-113)</sup> To reach her, Boethiah forged [Abolisher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abolisher), a blade capable of opening, closing, and severing connections between Paths.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Beragon-113)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-Alea-114)</sup> Finally, Mora created the Echonir, a weapon capable of tracking and erasing any lingering memory of Ithelia.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Examination-115)</sup>

Thus prepared, the allied Princes and their armies attacked. Ithelia was ultimately cornered within the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city of [Miscarcand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miscarcand), which was a great shrine to her.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-Gambit-116)</sup> She was defeated through the combined efforts of the other Princes, drained of her powers to the extent of having no more strength than a mortal,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup> and imprisoned within the Mythos, the core of Apocrypha, a place her own powers couldn't perceive. However, before she was sealed away, two things occurred. The first was that Ithelia enacted a failsafe within the mind of her scion, [Torvesard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torvesard), that would allow him to one day recall events associated with her imprisonment in the form of dreams and lead him to the path of freeing her.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[113]](#cite_note-TJ-117)</sup> The second was that, through her own powers to perceive fate and the Many Paths, Ithelia saw that Hermaeus Mora was correct about the threat she represented, a discovery she would forget due to her imprisonment.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup> With Ithelia imprisoned, the four Princes designed a plan to erase the very memory of her existence from reality, so that the danger would never return.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup><sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Dandera-118)</sup><sup>[[115]](#cite_note-Dugikh-119)</sup><sup>[[116]](#cite_note-Adept-120)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-Prisoner-121)</sup>

Two of the other Princes whose support Hermaeus Mora sought in this matter were [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina) and [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md). However, Mora's attempt to secure their approval for his plans proved futile. Despite Mora's insistence that Ithelia represented a grave danger to reality, Vaermina and Peryite argued that she shouldn't be punished solely because of something she might do, and that the spheres of the Princes were sacrosanct. Believing that Hermaeus Mora was overstepping his boundaries, the two Princes refused to support him. It was then that Mora revealed he was going to proceed with his plan regardless. Mora shattered the statue of Vaermina and cast his spell, removing the memory of Ithelia from every mind across all reality.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-Hidden-122)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-Vaermina-123)</sup> Ithelia's imprisonment was complete, and would become Mora's most guarded secret, the only recollection of the event in existence split between three Glyphics within [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-Mora Co F-124)</sup><sup>[[121]](#cite_note-Go LM-125)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup>

### First Era
Legends circulated that Hermaeus Mora spied on [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) during their secret liaison at the [Pomegranate Banquet](Lore_Vivec.md#Pomegranate_Banquet) which angered the two. In retaliation, an army of [Dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh) was sent after Mora's shrine of worship connected to [Anudnabia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anudnabia) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The shrine was destroyed and dragged beneath the sea and no more shrines to Mora were allowed to be built in Vvardenfell.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-MMI-126)</sup> However the shrine being consumed by the sea was more likely the act of Ithelia in the prior Era.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup>

At some point during the First Era, it is said that [Prior Durdryn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durdryn) was tasked by Vivec to turn the tusk of an unknown creature into a relic. Durdryn thus sought out Hermaeus Mora, who provided the runes that would give the artifact the power to uncover any secret. Thus the Fulcrum Obscura was created, imbued with the power to recover lost and forgotten memories, names and knowledge, even from the dead.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-TPF-127)</sup><sup>[[124]](#cite_note-OTFO-128)</sup>

The dream was a suppressed memory of the meeting before Hermaeus Mora, Vaermina, and Peryite, that was preserved due to the last act of Ithelia, who'd saved Torvesard at the last moment. Torvesard was told that Vaermina and Peryite had experienced the same dream, though in their case it was not quite as vivid. The two Princes, furious with Hermaeus Mora for altering their memories, formed an alliance with Torvesard and with each other, determined to enter [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) and uncover the forgotten truth.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup><sup>[[131]](#cite_note-Torvesard Journal-135)</sup>

The traitor revealed that he worked for two Daedric Princes who were involved in the conspiracy. Hermaeus Mora wasn't able to gleam the memories of who the Princes involved were before the vestige slew the traitor; afterwards, Hermaeus Mora would enter a binding contract with the vestige using the three glythics. So long as this threat to reality was active, he wouldn't do anything to interfere with the Vestiges interests or harm the Mundus. In exchange for helping Mora save his realm and Nirn alongside it.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-EOE-77)</sup>

#### Apocrypha is Invaded
In order to uncover the truth Mora had hidden, Vaermina and Peryite had their followers form an alliance and form a new cult devoted to both Princes called the Hidden Kindred, which the high priest of Peryite, Blightcrown, assumed leadership of.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-New Cult-141)</sup> The Hidden Kindred also formed an alliance with Telvanni Master [Shelreni Baro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shelreni_Baro).<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-Shelreni-142)</sup><sup>[[138]](#cite_note-Shelreni-142)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-Spirit-143)</sup> In order to proceed with their plans, the Hidden Kindred sought to retrieve a powerful [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) called the [Fulcrum Obscura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fulcrum_Obscura). The relic, crafted long ago by Hermaeus Mora for [Prior Durdryn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durdryn), had the power to uncover any hidden secret and was kept within the necropolis of [Necrom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necrom).<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-TPF-127)</sup><sup>[[124]](#cite_note-OTFO-128)</sup> While the Hidden Kindred's plan was disrupted, Torvesard managed to take half of the Fulcrum with him, and was able to use it to recover long-lost information that assisted in pushing his plans forward.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-KOTF-144)</sup>

In Apocrypha, the Hidden Kindred used [magic](Lore_Magic.md) that allowed them to move objects and beings to different [layers of reality](Lore_Layers_of_Reality.md) in order to conceal a modified version of the cult's Censers of Corruption. These Censers spread an arcane plague formed of Peryite's disease and Vaermina's dream essence,<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Leramil Hidden-90)</sup><sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Shelreni Baro-145)</sup><sup>[[142]](#cite_note-Instructions-146)</sup><sup>[[143]](#cite_note-Calamity-147)</sup> that infected and corroded Apocrypha's fabric, tormented, blinded, and frenzied Mora's servants,<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-Note-148)</sup> and gradually incapacitated even the Prince himself, threatening to eventually dissolve Apocrypha and sever Mora's link to his domain entirely.<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-Mora Calamity-149)</sup><sup>[[146]](#cite_note-Mora Unhealthy-150)</sup><sup>[[147]](#cite_note-Scruut Calamity-151)</sup><sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Scruut Unhealthy-152)</sup><sup>[[143]](#cite_note-Calamity-147)</sup><sup>[[149]](#cite_note-Unhealthy-153)</sup><sup>[[141]](#cite_note-Shelreni Baro-145)</sup> In addition to the affliction, the corruption of the Censers also allowed the Hidden Kindred to conceal their agents and objects in different layers, so that even Hermaeus Mora's gaze could not immediately track them through the different layers.<sup>[[150]](#cite_note-Blightcrown Priest-154)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup> The plague soon spread both within the realm proper and adjacent dimensions such as the [Infinite Panopticon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Panopticon).<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-Note-148)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Leramil Hidden-90)</sup><sup>[[151]](#cite_note-Leramil Calamity-155)</sup>

Hidden in Shelreni's body, Vaermina entered the Infinite Panopticon, seeking one of the Glyphics that contained the hidden memories. Hermaeus Mora himself, weakened by the corruption coursing through the realm, which was intensified further by Vaermina's presence, was ensnared and imprisoned within one of Vaermina's nightmares. However, the [Watchling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) Scruut and the Vestige managed to break through the nightmare and free him, forcing Vaermina and her allies to flee.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-Scruut Calamity-151)</sup><sup>[[143]](#cite_note-Calamity-147)</sup><sup>[[145]](#cite_note-Mora Calamity-149)</sup><sup>[[151]](#cite_note-Leramil Calamity-155)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/ON-place-The_Mythos_05.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Mythos_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_05.jpg) Hermaeus Mora within the Mythos In the end, the [ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) of the Telvanni Magister Meln the Mouthless used his Ghost Sight, a power that allowed one to see the hidden, imperceptible, and unseen, to allow the Vestige to locate the minions and Censers of the invading Princes and vanquish them,<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-Hidden-122)</sup> to destroy the manufactories of corruption which were spreading Peryite's plague within the core of Apocrypha by shattering the dream shards that bound them to the realm, and to slay Blightcrown, and even to help in combat against a manifestation of Vaermina herself,<sup>[[152]](#cite_note-Vaermina Unhealthy-156)</sup> whose true form was banished through Mora's power after her location became apparent, thus preventing the dissolution of Apocrypha, an event that would have produced ripples of [fate](Lore_Fate.md) that would have impacted all of Aurbis, and caused the destruction of Nirn as well due to the powerful connection the two realms share.<sup>[[153]](#cite_note-Meln Spirit-157)</sup><sup>[[145]](#cite_note-Mora Calamity-149)</sup><sup>[[146]](#cite_note-Mora Unhealthy-150)</sup><sup>[[154]](#cite_note-Meln Uhealthy-158)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup> Though the invasion was ended and Apocrypha was saved, Torvesard managed to escape with his recovered memories of Ithelia.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup>

#### Return of Ithelia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/15/ON-npc-Ithelia_05.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ithelia_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ithelia_05.jpg) Ithelia in Apocrypha as the Last Tomorrow During their travels through the Many Paths, the Vestige and Ithelia witnessed an alternate timeline where that reality's Ithelia grew so enraged that she used her power over fate to cause the entire Path to unravel through expanding reality tears that shredded that timeline whole, even as that world's Hermaeus Mora desperately tried to put a stop to it.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[163]](#cite_note-Many Paths Inn-168)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup>

After Ithelia regained her full might,<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Vargas-109)</sup><sup>[[164]](#cite_note-Tribune-169)</sup><sup>[[112]](#cite_note-Gambit-116)</sup> and her attempt to to rewrite history so that her conflict with the Princes had never occurred, before ultimately breaking the shackles of fate itself by giving each and every being their own desired history, was foiled through the destruction of her Loom, an enraged Ithelia, now filled with overwhelming power from the Many Paths, departed to enact her final attack against Hermaeus Mora.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-Vargas-109)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-111)</sup>

Soon after, the [Watchling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) fate-auditor of Hermaeus Mora, Scruut, appeared, warning of terrifying disturbances in fate that would soon unravel all reality. Tracking the disturbance, the Vestige, Scruut, and Leramil, discovered a disturbing truth. Ithelia's power was causing distortions of fate so severe they were developing into reality tears, holes in the fabric of reality itself, the result of a piece of existence itself having been destroyed due to instabilities in fate. Visible as rifts in space filled with nothingness, they would shred and destroy fate threads surrounding them, and erase from existence anything unfortunate enough to touch them. The rifts would keep manifesting more and more across various locations and planes of existence, causing reality to crumble a bit further with each passing moment, multiplying and growing until all existence itself was undone, akin to pulling on a loose thread until one's whole tunic unravels.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-111)</sup>

With the danger greater than ever, the Vestige took the depleted relics to hidden shrines of the three Princes that had created them, restoring their power. The group than returned to the Loom of the Untraveled road, still an unstable area that could lead to one becoming lost in the Paths despite the Loom's destruction, and used the Echonir to reveal Ithelia's location. The memory revealed Ithelia's final plan, if Hermaeus Mora wouldn't allow her to reclaim her realm of Mirrormoor, than she'd simply take Apocrypha from him and rebuild Mirrormoor upon its ruins. The Vestige than left for [Ceyond](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceyond), to retrieve a shard from one of the first Daedra Ithelia had made, the Primordial Shattered Titan. With the shard, an artifact that could stop Ithelia could be created. The Vestige retrieved the shard but, in the process, they were confronted by Torvesard, who used a reality tear in an attempt to erase them from existence. The Vestige was pushed out of the way by Curate Gadayn, who was erased instead.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-111)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup>

Using the shard and an arcane ritual, the group forged the [Mirror of Truth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mirror_of_Truth), an artifact that forces anyone who gazes into it to confront their innermost self and desires, then entered Apocrypha to stop Ithelia, only to find the realm being rewritten in her image and crawling with her Shardborn. Separated from Scruut, who stayed behind to seal spreading reality tears, the group pushed toward Hermaeus Mora’s sanctum, where Mora had been imprisoned and his power siphoned through the Staff of Many Paths wielded by Torvesard. While Torvesard continued the drain, the Vestige confronted Ithelia and turned the Mirror on her, revealing that before her imprisonment she had known Mora’s prophecy was inevitable and had created her Scions to defy a fate that could not be escaped, knowledge she lost when sealed away. Now swollen with power stolen from the Loom, she knew her mental clarity would soon collapse into madness and agreed to be imprisoned again, but Torvesard refused, turning the Staff on Ithelia in desperation, absorbing her power and Mora’s and imprisoning both Princes. Leramil summoned the Vestige’s allies through portals, and together they struck Torvesard down before he could master the stolen and unstable divine power.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Ithelias Fury-111)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup><sup>[[166]](#cite_note-Fate Forgotten Prince-171)</sup>

If the existence of Daedra and magic made her return inevitable, then perhaps a reality within the Many Paths where such concepts did not exist could contain her. #### Aftermath of Ithelia's Exile
With Ithelia gone, the tears in reality were repaired through use of a spell originating from Hermaeus Mora himself. The spell was cast at nexus points in reality, locations which naturally resonate throughout the realms, allowing the energies opposite to the force that created the tears to be inserted into the fabric of reality and resonate across Aurbis, beginning the restoration of all damage to fate, though completion of the process would take some time. The energy that counteracted the damage came from the relics of the three Princes, Abolisher, the Lamp of Clarity, and the Skein of Secrets, which permanently lost their Daedric power and were rendered mundane objects.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[167]](#cite_note-In Memory Of Quest-172)</sup>

Following Ithelia's departure, Hermaeus Mora cast his spell, and Ithelia's memory was removed from every mind across all reality once more, replaced only by recollections of Torvesard's scheme with Vaermina and Peryite, and the Recollection cult. Mirrormoor itself would be remembered, but only as a shattered ruin, whose inhabitants were masterless. Hermaeus Mora and the Vestige, who had earned the Prince's trust, would be the only ones to remember the truth, the last reminder of the Prince of Paths and of a world that might've been. However, though Ithelia was gone, seemingly forever, when questioned on the matter, Mora conceded that even he could not always see the final path the future would take.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-In Memory Of-25)</sup><sup>[[167]](#cite_note-In Memory Of Quest-172)</sup>

During this time Mora also used his power to pull Curate Gadayn back into reality through the Many Paths thus seemingly restoring him to existence, at the request of Leramil, who promised to perform one task the Prince would ask of her in the future in exchange for this boon. Mora warned Leramil that she should be prepared to pay the cost when the time came and, despite Gadayn's seeming return, the Vestige expressed some skepticism regarding whether this was the Gadayn that had been lost, or simply another version Mora had pulled from the Paths.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-In Memory Of-25)</sup><sup>[[167]](#cite_note-In Memory Of Quest-172)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) encountered Hermaeus Mora in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). A quest for the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) led the Dragonborn to [Septimus Signus](Lore_Septimus_Signus.md), a former mage of the College whose mind had suffered damage, he claimed, from reading an Elder Scroll. Signus was attempting to unlock a mysterious [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) lockbox which he believed held the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan). But he was unable to figure out how, and requested the Dragonborn find an Elder Scroll and record its insights into the matter using ancient Dwemer technology.<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-SRDiscerning-174)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/SR-npc-Storn_Crag-Strider_04.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Storn_Crag-Strider_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Storn_Crag-Strider_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Storn_Crag-Strider_04.jpg) Herma Mora takes the ancient lore of the Skaal, handed down from shaman to shaman since the All-Maker first gave them Solstheim. The Dragonborn completed the task and returned to Septimus Signus, who then requested a sample of blood from each of the [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) races. Signus needed the samples in order to recreate the equivalent of Dwemer blood, which was the key to opening the lockbox. Additionally, the mage revealed that it was Hermaeus Mora who had led him to the lockbox, but his patron had stopped short of granting the insight to open it. Hermaeus Mora then confronted the Dragonborn directly, stating Septimus was becoming "increasingly obsolete" and offering to make the hero his champion upon fulfilling Signus' request.<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-SRDiscerning-174)</sup>

Ultimately, the Dragonborn returned with the Elven blood as requested, and Signus was able to open the Dwemer lockbox. However, it did not contain the Heart of Lorkhan, but a book. Before he could inspect the book, however, Signus was destroyed by Hermaeus Mora, having outlived his usefulness to the Prince. The Dragonborn retrieved the tome, and discovered it to be the *Oghma Infinium*. Mora then appeared one last time, granting use of the *Oghma* and naming the Dragonborn his new emissary (though whether or not the Dragonborn accepted this title remains unclear).<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-SRDiscerning-174)</sup>

It was later revealed that [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md), the First Dragonborn, had learned his secret powers from Hermaeus Mora in exchange for a life of servitude in Apocrypha. By the time the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) returned to Skyrim, however, Miraak had largely abandoned his master and decided to return to Tamriel by way of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). After raising an army of cultists and attempting to kill the recently revealed Last Dragonborn, Miraak bewitched much of the populace of Solstheim and forced them to build otherworldly structures around the All-Maker Stones on the island, which would be used to increase his power and provide a conduit to return to Tamriel. The Last Dragonborn tracked Miraak's activity to his temple and inside found a [Black Book](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Book), an artifact of Hermaeus Mora.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-47)</sup>

The Last Dragonborn destroyed Miraak's influence on the island and entered Apocrypha through the Black Books, meeting Hermaeus Mora himself. Mora told the Dragonborn that he was growing tired of using Miraak as a servant, and offered the same dragon shout he provided to Miraak in exchange for an important piece of knowledge. This knowledge was the ancient religious traditions and powers of the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md), who had faithfully kept it from Mora for generations.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-Storn CS-105)</sup> The Dragonborn had to convince the spiritual leader of the Skaal, Storn Crag-Strider, to give up the knowledge, which killed him as it was removed from his body. With one end of the bargain fulfilled, the Dragonborn gained the new shout from Mora and returned to Apocrypha to face Miraak. The First Dragonborn was defeated, and Mora appeared again to express his disappointment with Miraak, and declared the Last Dragonborn to be his new champion.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Dragonborn-47)</sup>

## Artifacts
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-render-Arcanist.jpg/200px-ON-render-Arcanist.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arcanist.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arcanist.jpg) An arcanist with her tome
### Arcanist Tomes
[Arcanist tomes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arcanists#The_Arcanist.27s_Tome) are sentient artifacts created by the forces of Apocrypha, the Oblivion realm of Hermaeus Mora. One becomes a spellcaster known as an arcanist when one is bonded to one of them. These tomes are the object of which arcanists learn of the spells they wield and the vector through which their spells are cast. In other words, their power is derived from the book itself: knowledge alone is one thing, but arcanists are unique in that the tome they wield is integral to their spellcasting. All mages use runes of some kind, but the runework wielded by arcanists is extremely elaborate on top of producing unique feats of spellwork. Some mortals hypothesize these tomes to be pieces of Apocrypha itself.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0a/SR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg/200px-SR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Oghma_Infinium.jpg) The front cover of the *Oghma Infinium*
### Oghma Infinium
The [Oghma Infinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma_Infinium), also known as the **Tome of Power**, the **Librum of Knowledge**, and the **Book that Knows**, is an ancient tome of knowledge, and an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of great power. It was written by [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes_(god).md), the scribe of [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), who had been granted the knowledge within by Hermaeus Mora. The name of the tome comes from [Oghma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma), the wife of Xarxes, whom he created from his favorite moments in history. The knowledge within the tome grants the reader access to the artifact's energy, which can be manipulated to achieve near [demi-god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demigod) abilities. The reader is instantly bombarded with a knowledge intake that could hemorrhage the brains of lesser mortals. The Paths of Steel, Shadow and Spirit are the three main sections of the tome. Because of the hazards it poses to its reader, this book was classified as a [Tract Perilous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tracts_Perilous). Once the Oghma Infinium is used, it disappears from its owner's possession.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/SR-item-Black_Book.jpg/200px-SR-item-Black_Book.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Black_Book.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Black_Book.jpg) The front cover of one of Mora's Black Books
### Black Books
[Black Books](Lore_Black_Books.md) are [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by Hermaeus Mora, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Fate and Knowledge. Each book contains some manner of forbidden knowledge. [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) claims that no one actually knows where they came from, though the materials for their creation are prepared within a bindery in Apocrypha known as Quires Wind, before being presented to the prince to pen and complete. Some books are from the ancient past; some are from the future, as time appears to be more malleable for the Daedric Prince of fate and destiny.

The thick tomes are bound in black covers, with a symbol representing Mora on the front, and emit a black mist. The books are written with [Seeker](Lore_Seeker.md) ink that takes ten years to brew, on paper pressed from trees that have never seen the sun. The covers are derived from [Lurker](Lore_Lurker.md) leather, and the threads used to bind the tomes together come from silk originating from the [Spiral Skein](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spiral_Skein). All Black Books were regarded as [Tracts Perilous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tracts_Perilous) due to the hazards they could impose on their readers.

A number of Black Books were spread across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by Mora, and are usually located in the depths of ancient dungeons. The exact number of Black Books is unknown to everyone except for the Daedric Prince of Knowledge himself. Within every Black Book lies a forbidden secret, serving as a tantalizing temptation or reward for mortals whom Hermaeus Mora, the Great Eye, favors.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/ON-crown_store-Firstblade.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Firstblade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Firstblade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Firstblade.jpg) The Firstblade (ESO)
### Firstblade
[Firstblade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Firstblade), also known as **Miraak's Sword**, is a powerful sword linked to [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). It appears as a primal green blade with writhing sclerotic [watcher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) tentacles and [Seeker](Lore_Seeker.md) -sinew Threads wrapped around it, which extend to attack targets and drain their stamina during combat. When it is swung it takes the shape of a tentacle and grows in length, and even when not being swung, the sword writhes with tentacles around it, with them parting around the base of the sword to expose a green eye. It can be tempered with the use of [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and [daedra hearts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Heart), much like other [Daedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment) weapons.

It is unknown if Firstblade was seen after the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), but by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) of Solstheim knew it as a strange sword that belonged to a renegade Dragon Priest named Miraak far in the distant past. The sword was in possession of the renegade [dragon priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md), perhaps dating back to the time before the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), and was in his possession during his time in Apocrypha. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), he used the sword while battling the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) at the summit of Apocrypha, who killed him and took possession of the blade.

## Apocrypha
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/ON-load-Apocrypha.png/200px-ON-load-Apocrypha.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Apocrypha.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Apocrypha.png) The Endless Library, a section of Apocrypha [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/ON-load-Apocrypha_02.png/200px-ON-load-Apocrypha_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Apocrypha_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Apocrypha_02.png) The Chroma Incognito, a section of Apocrypha [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created and ruled over by Hermaeus Mora, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Knowledge and [Fate](Lore_Fate.md). The geography of the realm varies, with some areas being open with fields containing odd flora, other areas are an endless library consisting of untitled books with black covers, where all forbidden knowledge can be found, and the crackling towers of learning mingle with archways of despair and confusion. The realm is haunted by the ghosts of mortals forever searching for knowledge. Stacks of books form mazes and spiraling pillars that can reach as high as the illuminating green sky. Intricate ruins and monstrous carvings can be found among the stacks. The plane is covered in a sea of ink and roiling tentacles, while Hermaeus Mora is omnipresent over all, taking glee in tormenting mortals from his realm. Some areas of the realm are consumed by darkness which can kill any who enter it. Discarded pages and floating tomes fill the fetid air, as if pulled by invisible string. Storms of notorious strength purportedly rage over Apocrypha.

Apart from magical teleportation, Apocrypha is most easily entered by mortals through the reading of [Black Books](Lore_Black_Books.md), tomes of forbidden knowledge displaced from time and scattered across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). An apparition of the reader's body remains in [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), tethering their life force. Most mortals who gain access to the secrets within Apocrypha are driven insane by unending revelations. Such was the fate of the mage [Morian Zenas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morian_Zenas), who famously disappeared in Apocrypha during his voyages through Oblivion. However, those who successfully journey through Apocrypha are known to discover powerful knowledge. The laws of the mortal world hold no power in this realm.

### Sub-Realms

#### Infinite Archive
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-place-Infinite_Archive_04.jpg/200px-ON-place-Infinite_Archive_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Infinite_Archive_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Infinite_Archive_04.jpg) The Infinite Archive The [Infinite Archive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Archive) is a border realm of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), an adjunct plane or subrealm which lies within the greater fabric of Apocrypha, created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Hermaeus Mora. It is an endless library consisting primarily of historical accounts, written records of events that were or could yet be. Contemplating the scope of the Archive's function was known to leave mortals, even its caretaker Master Malkhest, in a daze.

#### Infinite Panopticon
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Infinite_Panopticon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Infinite_Panopticon.jpg) The Infinite Panopticon The [Infinite Panopticon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Panopticon) is a pocket realm inside Hermaeus Mora's realm of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). It is described as an endless library of rooms and passages inside a limitless pocket [dimension](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) situated outside the time and space of Apocrypha itself, and is said to be one of the most secure repositories of knowledge within Apocrypha. The Panopticon is said to hold infinite secrets, including some of Mora's most closely guarded ones, as the information and secrets inside are considered too precious to be known by anyone but Hermaeus Mora himself.

In order to protect the information stored within, the entrance to the Panopticon constantly changes, never appearing in the same place twice. The interior of the realm also changes at random; nothing within the extradimensional space conforms to reality as [mortals](Lore_Souls.md) understand it. The defenses that protect the secrets in the Panopticon are said to include shifting chambers and corridors as well as an army of [Hushed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hushed), [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md), [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md) and other [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) guardians, which are said to "defy description", being too powerful and obscure to describe accurately.

## Servants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/SR-creature-Seeker.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Seeker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Seeker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Seeker.jpg) A Seeker [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-creature-The_Lightkeeper.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Lightkeeper.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Lightkeeper.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Lightkeeper.jpg) A Lurker The servants of Hermaeus Mora are described as grotesque [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) creatures malformed by Mora who stalk the constantly elongating and retracting halls of Apocrypha, feeding off the ghosts of those seeking knowledge. Two types of Daedra—known as Keepers—dwell in this realm, maintaining the vast library and serving Mora's mysterious will.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:1065</sup> [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md) serve as Apocrypha's scholars, fate scribes, and secret keepers, and are said to be the most in tune with Mora's will. They use invisibility and mirror images as distractions while they sap strength.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:1065</sup><sup>[[175]](#cite_note-DOA-180)</sup> [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md), towering monstrosities with powerful physical tendrils and poisonous bile, serve as guardians,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:1065</sup> and are said to embody Mora's connection to the sea.<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-DOA-180)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:1065</sup>

The [Hushed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hushed) can also be found guarding locations that Mora wishes to keep private,<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-DOA-180)</sup> while [Bookwyrms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bookwyrm) either devour books to vouchsafe their knowledge for their master, or hunt tomes long thought lost.<sup>[[176]](#cite_note-Bookwyrm-181)</sup>

Mora tasks [Watchlings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) with observing all things and cataloguing all knowledge, so that they may act as his eyes, having them carry out various clerical duties within Apocrypha while their larger kindred, [Watchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher), serve as guardians and overseers for the Prince's more important concerns.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-FOC-13)</sup><sup>[[175]](#cite_note-DOA-180)</sup> [Knower's Eyes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knower%27s_Eye) are another type of being that serve of Mora, taking the form of ethereal green eyes.<sup>[[177]](#cite_note-KE-182)</sup><sup>[[178]](#cite_note-QW-183)</sup> There are other entities in the realm as well: strange floating lights called [Wisp-Lanterns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wisp-Lantern), writhing tentacles, and glimpses of the Daedric Prince himself.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PRIMA-10)</sup><sup>:1065</sup>

There are some [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) in his service. One such example is Herald Kixathi, who Mora freed and taught the secret of how to sever her ties to Oblivion and bind herself to the Mundus instead. She has served him ever since, and considered him to be generous with his servants,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-HK-27)</sup> another is the [Remnants Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remnants_Clan), a clan of Dremora connected to one another in a hive-mind, sworn to defend Apocrypha and its secrets and procure artifacts, tomes, and other objects of interest for the Prince.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Jot FR-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Tyss-22)</sup><sup>[[179]](#cite_note-TRT-184)</sup> Members of the clan describe themselves as the sword and shield of Hermaeus Mora, who have been tasked to stand between Mora's infinite knowledge and those who would plunder it.<sup>[[180]](#cite_note-Zirokh-185)</sup> The Darkreave Curators are another Dremora clan that serves Hermaeus Mora. They seek to catalog every segment of existence, from the intensely banal to the deeply arcane, wishing to create a written record of every thing that exists. Darkreaves have been described as fanatical and obsessed beyond their service to Mora. They are said to be violently protective of written works, and congregate in Apocrypha's repositories of knowledge, particularly the Obscured Forum which serves as a hub for information exchange in the realm.<sup>[[181]](#cite_note-ONExploration-186)</sup>

[Hermitage Servitors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hermitage_Servitor) are creatures said to come from Apocrypha, a plane of pure thought and potential. In one case, the unwitting summoner exhibited panic at the creature's presence and refusal to leave his presence, and died shortly afterwards.<sup>[[182]](#cite_note-Hermitage-187)</sup>

Some accounts by Ciphers of the Eye that work in sub-realms of Apocrypha such as the Infinite Panopticon claim that those places contain not just Lurkers, Seekers, and Watchers, but also other Daedric beings that defy description.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-WIP-42)</sup> [Arcanist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arcanist) accounts also speak of other notable Apocryphal figures such as the [Fate Crone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fate_Crone),<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-Chakram_Shields-188)</sup><sup>[[184]](#cite_note-FC-189)</sup> the [Blind Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blind_Man),<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-Chakram_Shields-188)</sup><sup>[[185]](#cite_note-BM-190)</sup> and the [Baron of Breakers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baron_of_Breakers), visiting in dreams, appearing before those looking for knowledge, and providing their runes for the Arcanists to use.<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-Chakram_Shields-188)</sup><sup>[[186]](#cite_note-BOB-191)</sup><sup>[[185]](#cite_note-BM-190)</sup> Another notable Apocryphal figure is the Prime Cataloger, a Seeker said to haunt the libraries of Apocrypha and know everything within it.<sup>[[187]](#cite_note-Nelyn-192)</sup>

## Nature

### Hermaeus Mora and Knowledge
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/ON-quest-Escape_of_the_Sane_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Escape_of_the_Sane_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Escape_of_the_Sane_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Escape_of_the_Sane_02.jpg) A Known Thing [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-item-Elder_Scroll_%28The_Forbidden_Exhibit%29.jpg/200px-ON-item-Elder_Scroll_%28The_Forbidden_Exhibit%29.jpg)](File_ON-item-Elder_Scroll_(The_Forbidden_Exhibit).jpg.md) [](File_ON-item-Elder_Scroll_(The_Forbidden_Exhibit).jpg.md) An Elder Scroll stored within a vat in Apocrypha According to some sources, the Mythos, the core of Mora's realm of Apocrypha which is located deep beneath the plane's Ichor Sea, was where the Prince "whispered the first secret" and "imagined the concept of knowledge", thus also conceiving Apocrypha itself in that same act.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Mythos Screen-35)</sup>

Hermaeus Mora himself has described his realm of Apocrypha as an endless repository of knowledge which he created within the vastness of Oblivion, a realm of fate, time, and wisdom, which holds every secret known to mortals and immortals, as well as some that are known only to Mora himself.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup>

It is said that knowledge constantly rains down upon the realm from the rest of [reality](Lore_Aurbis.md) and thus the contents of the towering stacks of tomes that form hills and canyons within Apocrypha can change from moment to moment, reflecting the realm's thirst for knowledge<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Ciphers-89)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Leramil Hidden-90)</sup> According to Hermaeus Mora himself, while the structures of Apocrypha may appear as books to mortal eyes, they are actually conceptual constructs, things imagined into existence by the minds that stray within his realm. Thus, most of the tomes in Apocrypha are said to come into existence through will rather than printing presses.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Roleplay-39)</sup> Whether tomes, dreams, or memories, it is said all knowledge and secrets eventually end up within Apocrypha, and that its repositories of knowledge are infinite.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Leramil Hidden-90)</sup><sup>[[180]](#cite_note-Zirokh-185)</sup> All things within Apocrypha are recorded within the Pool of Inquiry, located in a facility called the Tranquil Catalog. The Pool thus serves as a register and index for the contents of Apocrypha, and can be used to locate what one seeks within the realm.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Scruut Dream-44)</sup>

Many tomes within Apocrypha are classified as [Tracts Perilous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tracts_Perilous), texts which contain secrets which can not be reconciled with the mortal mind, thus forcing the reader to surrender some of their sanity in order to comprehend their contents. Mora's Black Books and Oghma Infinium are considered Tracts Perilous, as is the Numinous Grimoire, a tome of Apocrypha which serves as a dissection of the mortal soul in both life and death and holds the secrets by which a soul can become "decoupled" from the body entirely and persist as an immortal spirit that sustains itself by consuming other souls, a state different to being a dead spirit, or [ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost).<sup>[[190]](#cite_note-OTP-195)</sup><sup>[[191]](#cite_note-Uldazaan-196)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/ON-concept-Hushed.png/200px-ON-concept-Hushed.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Hushed.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Hushed.png) One of the Hushed [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-misc-Your_Body_02.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Your_Body_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Your_Body_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Your_Body_02.jpg) The vessel of a mind connected to the Chorus A particularly dangerous form of knowledge held in Apocrypha are Known Things. Known Things have been described as "knowledge" and "truth" which does not care for the mind's inability to comprehend the impossibility of what it is being shown. Thus looking into a Known Thing is known to often have consequences such as madness, death, bleeding from the ears, or stranger phenomena such as growing an extra toe. Even the minds of [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md) can not withstand direct exposure to Known Things, and being shown one is said to melt their minds. One person known to have survived exposure to a Known Thing was [Morian Zenas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morian_Zenas), albeit not without bleeding from the ears and being affected mentally. Zenas described Known Things as "inky black and wriggling" and tasting of "raspberries", and exposure to one as having caused "many multitudes of me" to become present, while also making him see the "light of darkness" and "fly in the weight of truth". Zenas claimed one could "ask them" if they "wanted" to provide their help and speculated he might have survived exposure because the Known Thing he witnessed "liked" him.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Zenas-96)</sup>

Because of the nature of the secrets held in Apocrypha, mortals who come to the realm, hoping to learn forbidden knowledge unintended for them, risk their very minds in doing so. When a mortal learns a secret too great for their intellect to contain, the mind's contents are displaced. All memories, ambitions, and desires they had in their life before Apocrypha are gradually lost, and only the thirst to know more drives the mortal to delve deeper into forbidden knowledge. They cast aside their very self in the pursuit of more secrets, in a phenomenon known as the "Hush", and those who succumb to it are known as the [*Hushed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hushed).<sup>[[192]](#cite_note-TDot H-197)</sup>

The Rectory Corporea is a facility within Apocrypha which houses a secret collection of knowledge called the Chorus, the true nature of which is considered a mystery. The Chorus is, in truth, a collection of minds that have been joined together within a singular mindscape in service to Mora, while their bodies are held in stasis, sustained by the Rectory's magic. The Chorus has been described as a "powerful decentralized entity", and likened to a "wall of infinite height" surrounding the connected minds, a place that can be entered solely through the signing of a pact, which serves as the anchor that binds the mind to it. The function of the Chorus has been described as dreaming and teaching of things. Hermaeus Mora is said to "share himself" with the minds of the Chorus in some form, and will fulfill wishes to those who have agreed to become part of it. Minds linked to the Chorus might sometimes attempt to leave before the duration determined by their pact had passed, causing problems within the mindscape.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Chorus-94)</sup><sup>[[193]](#cite_note-Ledger-198)</sup>

The Disquiet Study is a library within Apocrypha which breaths life into its books, thus giving their words power and substance, to the extent of allowing them to leap from the page and manifest into reality.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-Disquiet-98)</sup><sup>[[95]](#cite_note-Ysgild-99)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-Bartalus-100)</sup>

### Hermaeus Mora and Fate
While the nature and identity of the force [fate](Lore_Fate.md) issues from remain in contention, the Daedric Prince Hermaeus Mora is said to have dominion over at least one aspect of fate. Described as the ever-seeing-eyes that constantly scry fate to see what is destined to happen next, Mora is likened to a great eye either suspended above or always submerged in fate's ocean, gazing into the depths to track the currents of fate. Thus he is called the "Master of the Tides of Fate", not because he controls fate, but because he knows its every possibility and outcome intimately, perceiving all that may come to pass. As the One Who Knows and Ur-Daedra, Hermaeus Mora is said to be able to see all the infinite paths of possibility arrayed before his gaze, though even he doesn't necessarily always know the "final path". This is also reflected in his realm of Apocrypha, which takes the form of an endless ocean of knowledge and secrets, though it's unclear if the idea of Mora as a vast entity cataloging the tides of fate or the shape of the realm came first, with one perhaps being just a reflection of the other.<sup>[[194]](#cite_note-Master-199)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup> Mora's reach and gaze even extend beyond the veil of reality and what is and into possibilities that may be or might have been.<sup>[[195]](#cite_note-Never Woven-200)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Azandar-48)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/ON-place-The_Underweave_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Underweave_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Underweave_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Underweave_03.jpg) The Underweave, a powerful and dangerous nexus of the currents of Fate The currents of fate flow strongly in Apocrypha, a quality which makes the realm the ideal place for one seeking to understand the mysteries of fate and the Abyssal Sea, but also makes it highly dangerous, containing regions where it is possible for a traveler to become lost in fates that never came to pass, strange realities that can imprison the unwary in worlds of their own making. This holds especially true in certain regions of the realm like the Underweave, described as a powerful and dangerous nexus of fated potential, a locus of might-have-beens and never-will-bes, where fate shifts constantly like the tides of the Abyssal Sea and fatelines flex and fray.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Azandar-48)</sup><sup>[[196]](#cite_note-Underweave-201)</sup><sup>[[197]](#cite_note-Hushed-202)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/ON-place-Scrivener%27s_Hall_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-Scrivener%27s_Hall_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Scrivener%27s_Hall_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Scrivener%27s_Hall_03.jpg) A Daedric Orrery, used by servants of Hermaeus Mora to scry Fate Potent fates with the potential to influence the very fate of the [planes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) are transcribed within crystalline artifacts called Glyphics, described as the physical manifestation of that potential fate or other knowledge which can be coaxed to transform into a tangible version of the concept they contain. Of those fated Glyphics, those containing fates which ultimately didn't come to pass are "uncatalogued" and unmade by being fed into the Nemesis Looms, devices that unravel parts of reality that reside in the Heart of the Underweave, as failed fates that aren't destroyed can latch onto reality and cause havoc. This process also serves to accumulate immense amounts of magicka, as the unmade fates are dissolved into arcane energies and "temporal dust", which the Heart than stores. It is suggested that through doing this Mora somehow "refills" the well of knowledge within Aurbis so it doesn't become exhausted, though Mora's servants refuse to provide more information. The power in the Heart can be tapped into to manipulate time, but doing so is highly dangerous as it can potentially cause realm-tearing temporal anomalies.<sup>[[198]](#cite_note-Ogle-203)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[199]](#cite_note-Thaddeus_Cosma-204)</sup>

Agents of Hermaeus Mora with the ability to perceive the threads of fate serve the Prince as fate's "auditors", given the task of seeking out distortions in fate that result when something that contradicts a fate recorded by Hermaeus Mora occurs and repairing the threads by discovering and addressing the cause. Once an important contradiction in fate has been resolved a summation of the associated events is recorded in the Chronicle of Apocrypha, where it is commemorated eternally. <sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup>

Apocrypha and [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) are said to share a powerful connection, the fates of the two worlds are inextricably linked and, because of this and its role in regulating fate, were Apocrypha to be destroyed, the resulting ripples of fate would also unravel the mortal world, and the ensuing destruction would ripple throughout reality.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/ON-load-Infinite_Archive.png/200px-ON-load-Infinite_Archive.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Infinite_Archive.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Infinite_Archive.png) The Index of the Infinite Archive, a device which can manipulate Fate's threads Apocrypha contains sprawling facilities dedicated to scrying fate and storing prophecies. Within the Sidereal Cloisters, Daedric orreries chart the light and movements of the stars and celestial bodies to predict the future. These prophecies, the result of centuries of perfected fate-scrying and translating the stars, after having undergone a process of refinement whereby the pure unrefined predictions of the orreries are scried and sorted in the facility's Divinatory Forums, are inscribed into tomes stored in the Cloister libraries. The contents of the tomes can vary, ranging from less momentous predictions such as the changing of grain prices or the formation of new valleys by rivers, to primordial predictions that could cause dire consequences across all the planes if misinterpreted, such as realm spanning conflicts. Ever-changing prophecies that can be affected by the mundane and ordinary are recorded in the Loggia Infinitum section of the Cloisters, prophecies of dangerous events are kept in the Calamitous Gallery where the Cloister caretakers begin their study in order to test their resolve, and prophecies that predict the changing of regimes and dynasties are stored in the Empyrean Ascent. Due to their importance, Hermaeus Mora has been known to take direct action to prevent valuable tomes from leaving the Cloisters, on one occasion trapping the whole facility in a time loop to prevent a text from being lost.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Meenai-91)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Observer-92)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Knower%27s_Eye.jpg) An Eye of Mora The function of the [Infinite Archive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Archive), a subrealm of Apocrypha which lies within that realm's greater fabric and takes the form of an endless library that contains written records of events that were or could yet be from across Aurbis, revolves around a system of fateline manipulation. All objects and beings within the Archive are connected to an arcane orb known as the Index, which serves as both catalogue and safeguard. Threads of fate are spun between the Index and the contents of the catalogue and, when some sort of danger damages the recorded object and causes the fateline to snap, the Index restores the version of it from just before the break occurred, not a copy or recreation but the thing itself from a different moment in its fateline. The Archive contains tomes dedicated to recording information pertaining to possible outcomes, such as the Book of Infinite Questions and Potent Possibilities, in which all the potential questions one could ask at a given moment are recorded.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-Malkhest Archive-165)</sup><sup>[[97]](#cite_note-ESOMM-101)</sup>

The [Unraveling Palimpsest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unraveling_Palimpsest), a tome of Apocrypha with contents that take a different form unique to each observer, allows its reader knowledge of any secret as long as it pertains to their own life and fate, thus "unraveling" the secrets of their fateline. It is actually a supernatural phenomenon formed in response to the realm's endless ocean of knowledge.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Azandar-48)</sup>

Should the personal recollections of Hermaeus Mora stored within the [Eyes of Mora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knower%27s_Eye) somehow be altered, the event the memory depicts itself will be [rewritten](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Time_Magic).<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup>

One of the nexus points of reality which resonate throughout the planes, making it so that alterations to fate there ripple throughout existence, is located within the Watchling Grotto in Apocrypha.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup>

The ability to perceive fate is known to exist among the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) servants of Hermaeus Mora, whose [Watchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) can discern future events ranging from the insignificant to cataclysmic disasters. Able to observe and locate contradictions and distortions in the fabric of fate, and to sense ripples and anomalies that alter fate forming even across the realms, they are tasked with discovering their cause and fixing them by repairing the fatelines and resolving any contradictions to fates recorded in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), serving the Prince as "auditors of fate".<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup> Especially powerful servants of Mora have been known to acquire detailed information about the fates and lives of those they encounter at a glance, witnessing the events that have happened, that might or may never happen, and even unwoven fates that one might've lived. Using the proper ingredients and summoning rituals, these Daedric agents of Mora can be bound to answer the questions of the one who invoked them.There are mortals who possess abilities to observe fate similar to those of Mora's agents, having been taught how to see fate's threads by the Daedric Prince himself. Such mortals can even share their gift with others, should they desire to.<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-Predestination-205)</sup><sup>[[201]](#cite_note-Tem-206)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Azandar-48)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup> Though highly useful, the ability to see fate's threads can sometimes be a hindrance, those who possess it can be made to feel great pain when near a severe enough distortion of fate, preventing them from even coming near locations where such a phenomenon is present.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup>

Though Hermaeus Mora's sphere, unlike Ithelia's does not include control over fate itself,<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup> some of the Prince's agents have shown some limited ability to influence the threads of fate. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Leramil the Wise, an agent of Mora, used her magic to rearrange the threads of fate in order to force the entrance to the Infinite Panopticon to return to a location it had already moved on from, though doing so placed great strain on her. Later Leramil, Torvesard, and the Vestige who had been granted some ability to align the threads of fate by Mora due to their role as Fate's Proxy, worked together to align the lines and open the portal to the Mythos, the core of Apocrypha. While accompanying the Vestige Leramil influenced fate in order to revitalize and aid them in combat.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Torvesard-24)</sup>

Users of fate magic can also influence fate in other ways, such as aligning its threads to transform the forces of arcane objects into powerful magical artifacts, helping stich fate's threads together when they've been damaged, revitalizing an ally in combat, or shielding from spells involving the manipulation of fate such as spells to banish someone to the Many Paths.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup>

Scruut, a [Watchling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) fate auditor in the service of Hermaeus Mora, believed that influencing fate the way Leramil did was a power not meant for mortals, and that using it would prove her undoing one day.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-Scruut-170)</sup>

Mora himself acknowledged that the nature of mortals was to always have a chance to succeed and reach a desired outcome even when it would otherwise be impossible, a quality not possessed by immortal beings like himself who would inevitably fail in the same circumstances.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup> Morian Zenas, who had been exposed to two Known Things, claimed that it was possible to "keep changing and never swim in the sea of fate" and to "read not to learn but to alter", thus becoming "secret even from the secret keeper".<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Zenas-96)</sup>

### Hermaeus Mora and the Many Paths
Hermaeus Mora, like Ithelia, can perceive the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), and is even able to locate specific realities with the qualities he's looking for within them. As Ur-Daedra and the One Who Knows, Hermaeus Mora is said to be able to see all the infinite paths of possibility arrayed before his gaze, though even he can't always see the "final path". Mora acquired the ability to open portals to other realities accessible through the Many Paths after Ithelia showed him how to do so. Through tapping into the possibilities of the Many Paths, Mora also possesses the seeming ability to restore to existence individuals who were erased via contact with a reality tear, though the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) theorized he might have simply pulled an alternate version of the erased individual from the Many Paths to give the impression he restored the original instead.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Mora-93)</sup><sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Ithelia-108)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Leramil-5)</sup>

## Notes
- It is thought that his name is a distant translation of a metaphor along the lines of "A Grove In The Wilderness" or "Hidden Grove," with the suggestion that he represents secret truths not easily found.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Teddy PInterview-207)</sup> - This is inspired by the concept of the "Groves of Academia" from the Latin quote *Atque inter silvas academi quaerere verum* from [Horace](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horace)'s *[Epistles](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epistles_(Horace))*, which translates as "And Seek for Truth in the Garden of Academus."<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Teddy PInterview-207)</sup>
- In [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md), HERMA-MORA translates to "Unstable Man" in certain contexts.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MKP-72)</sup>
- The second part of the Prince's name, "Mora", means "Forest" or "Wood" in [Aldmeris](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ehlnofex_Languages). This would explain Hermaeus Mora's nordic sobriquet as the "Woodland Man".
- Hermaeus Mora takes heavy inspiration from [Lovecraftian fiction](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lovecraftian_horror).<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-Teddy PP-208)</sup>
- Ur-Mora Clarion is a region of Oblivion.<sup>[[202]](#cite_note-LAFNJTR-209)</sup> Its connection to Hermaeus Mora, if any, is unknown.
- | [^1](#note_nb1) | [Lyg](Lore_Lyg.md) has been described as a place ruled over by the aquatic [dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh) during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), containing "nineteen and nine and nine" oceans which were ruled over by the dreugh-kings,<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-MDC4-210)</sup> whose chief was [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[204]](#cite_note-211)</sup> Lyg is said to have been destroyed by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), an event said to have taken place in a prior [kalpa](Lore_Kalpa.md),<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-TNC-212)</sup><sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-PGE2Con-213)</sup> in which the whole [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) was a "a tidal ocean, with left behind ideas".<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MKP-72)</sup> Lyg has also been claimed to be one of the "[Adjacent Places](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adjacent_Place)", and a parallel version of Tamriel.<sup>[[UOL 9]](#cite_note-MKAMA-214)</sup> The [Nine Coruscations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Nine_Coruscations), an Ayleid religious text which mentions the fall of Lyg, claims that, in Lyg, darkness poured forth from a wound suffered by the "tragic prince of Lyg", sweeping across the land like "black bile" and a "sickened sea", an event witnessed by the [Nine Coruscations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Coruscations). It is also said that [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia), one of the Coruscations, discovered the indefinite limits of [Lorkh-Apeiron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) upon witnessing the "Infinite Abyss" and "Indeterminate Sea" known also as "Mora Obscura".<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-TNC-212)</sup> Some of the faithful of Hermaeus Mora believe him to have originated in a different realm which is also the origin of the sea creature fossils that dot Apocrypha,<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Fossil-57)</sup> and he has been described as having formed from the thrown-away ideas used during the [creation](Lore_Convention.md) of mortality in the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICo DL-16)</sup> Given the resemblance, its possible there might be some connection between these concepts. |
