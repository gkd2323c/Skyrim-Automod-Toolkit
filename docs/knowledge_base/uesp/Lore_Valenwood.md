[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/LO-map-Valenwood_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Valenwood_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Valenwood_(Anthology).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Valenwood_(Anthology).jpg.md) **Valenwood** [![](https://images.uesp.net/c/c8/LO-race-Bosmer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Bosmer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Bosmer.png) Illustration of a typical Bosmer male Valenwood is a densely forested, sub-tropical region that encompasses the southwestern coastal plain of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). In the words of *A Pocket Guide to the Empire*, Valenwood is "a sea of endless green, a maze of foliage with half-hidden cities growing like blooms from a flower, the home of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) is Tamriel's garden."<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> Alongside the Wood Elves, races like the [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), [Imga](Lore_Imga.md), and [Centaur](Lore_Centaur.md) also call the forest their home.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESODra.27bul Load-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> One unusual feature of Valenwood is its gigantic, [migratory trees](Lore_Graht-oak.md), many so large that the Bosmer have built entire cities in their branches. [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) is one such tree, a mile-high specimen that serves as the capital of Valenwood and seat of its kings.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ADIF-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1A-4)</sup> The forest is often referred to by Bosmer as 'the Valenwood', designating the province as an entity in its own right.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Wild King-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BBD-6)</sup>

## Geography
Valenwood borders [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) to the east and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to the north, partially divided from the latter by the [Strid River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strid_River), and is just across the sea from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Its landscape consists mainly of rolling hills and light blue rivers, predominantly covered in rainforest and woodland. The coasts of Valenwood are dominated by mangrove swamps and tropical rain forests, while heavy rainfalls nurture the temperate inland rain forests. The Bosmer live in timber clanhouses at sites scattered along the coast and through the interior. The few Imperial roads traverse vast dense woodlands, studded with tiny, widely separated settlements.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GD-7)</sup>

Valenwood consists of four main regions: [Grahtwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahtwood), [Greenshade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenshade), [Malabal Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malabal_Tor), and western [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup> [Woda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Woda) is a regional duchy located somewhere in the province's jungles, with its exact location being unspecified.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Black Arrow-9)</sup> There is also a number of islands considered part of the province, such as the chain of a least four islands off the [Drowned Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drowned_Coast)<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OCMap-10)</sup> (the easternmost island being the largest),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> [Tempest Island](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tempest_Island) near the [Strid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strid_River), and by some accounts [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) which has been claimed at times by the province.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

### Grahtwood
Grahtwood is the southeastern region of Valenwood. The region is covered in a dense jungle containing many massive graht-oaks, from which the region takes its name. It borders Greenshade to the west, Malabal Tor and Reaper's March to the north, and Elsweyr to the east. The [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md) is off its southern shores. Its capital is [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root), a city built on and around the [Elden Tree](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Tree), an ancient and massive [Graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md). The Elden Tree is said to be the only Graht-Oak to have a definite connection to the [Green-Sap Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md#Green-Sap) at any given time, a side effect of [Anumarils](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumaril) failed attempts to recreate White-Gold Tower.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AU4TET-12)</sup>

The other major cities are [Southpoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Southpoint) and [Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haven), which can be found on the southern shores. The ruined city of [Gil-Var-Delle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gilverdale), famous for being destroyed by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), is also located here.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

Other notable landmarks in Grahtwood include the Bone Orchard to the southwest, where powerful ancient spirits exist in a state somewhat resembling death<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Ko B-13)</sup>, as well as the site which once hosted the walking city of Falinesti in the wintertime, found just west of Elden Root. Additionally, the settlement of [Brackenleaf](Lore_Brackenleaf_(settlement).md) to the southeast is located close to the eponymous [Brackenleaf](Lore_Brackenleaf_(tree).md), an ancient and powerful spirit who claims to be one of the first trees in Grahtwood.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8d/ON-place-Greenshade.jpg/200px-ON-place-Greenshade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Greenshade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Greenshade.jpg) Greenshade
### Greenshade
Greenshade is the southwestern region of Valenwood. It borders Grahtwood to the east and Malabal Tor to the north; the isle of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) is just across the sea to the west. The main city is [Woodhearth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Woodhearth), situated on the westernmost tip of the region. The other major cities are [Greenheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenheart) and the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) city of [Marbruk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marbruk), the latter of which once bordered Falinesti's spring location. The region consists of smaller forests, clearings and meadows; there is also a swamp to the southeast of Woodhearth.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

Despite boasting sparser vegetation than the likes of Grahtwood or Malabal Tor, Greenshade is a very spiritually significant part of the Valenwood. The region is home to many entities that act as emissaries for the forest and the Green. One such emissary is the Eldest, a venerated strangler purported to control the forests of Greenshade and to have created the first Silvenar and Green Lady. It is customary for many Bosmer to make a pilgrimage to its den.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TEAPT-14)</sup> Some Bosmer of this area also consider themselves subjects of the mysterious entity known as the [Wilderking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wilderking) (or Wilderqueen), an ascended mortal with strong ties to the Green itself. They typically reside in Greenheart and are responsible for shaping the land of Greenshade to suit their whim.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Wild King-5)</sup>

The ancient city of [Hectahame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hectahame) lies deep in the northeast corner of Greenshade, near the center of Valenwood.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup> Here, the very soul of the Valenwood can be found, represented by the Valenheart. It was originally protected by the Ayleids, and is now guarded by the wards they left behind. Despite their fierce reverence for the site, very few Bosmer dare travel to Hectahame, and those who do are often considered mad.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HOV-15)</sup>

### Malabal Tor
Malabal Tor is the northwestern region of Valenwood. It borders Greenshade to the south, Reaper's March to the east and the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) of Cyrodiil across the Strid River to the north. The region contains the city of [Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silvenar), the spiritual center of Valenwood, and hosts the city [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md), where it has rooted itself since the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in what was once its summer site. The region is covered in a jungle similar to the one in Grahtwood, but denser and smaller in scale.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

The region contains many sites important to the Bosmer. It is the location of [Ouze](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ouze), a tar pit believed to be connected to the feared Bosmeri afterlife of the Ooze,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Oo O-16)</sup> as well as Treehenge, the final resting site for the souls of all Green Ladies.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-So T-17)</sup>

However, Wood Elves aren't the only ones with significant landmarks in Malabal Tor; The [Wood Orcs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wood_Orcs) also have claim to many locations within the jungle. The largest of their strongholds, Dra'bul, can be found along the northern coast, while ruins such as [Abamath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abamath) or the Bloodtoil Valley hold vast historical and religious significance for their people.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TTo A-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DSJ-19)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-place-Western_Reaper%27s_March.jpg/200px-ON-place-Western_Reaper%27s_March.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Western_Reaper%27s_March.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Western_Reaper%27s_March.jpg) Reaper's March
### Reaper's March
The western portion of the Reaper's March region, known as the Northern Woods, makes up the northeastern reaches of Valenwood. It is a sparsely forested area that borders Malabal Tor to the west, Grahtwood to the south, and Elsweyr to the east. The biggest settlement is the multicultural city of [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia) to the north while Falinesti used to be found in the south during autumn months. Due to the close proximity of Cyrodiil and Elsweyr, the region was often raided by the [Colovians](Lore_Colovia.md) and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). For the same reason, both Colovian crypts and ruins of Khajiiti temples can be found here. The forests of the Northern Woods gradually diminish as they merge with Elsweyr's savannas to the east, which comprises the subregions of Dawnmead and Jodewood.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup> Because of its distance from Valenwood proper, the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) is often overlooked by the Bosmer living here.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Gloo ESO-20)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png/200px-ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Brackenleaf.png) Brackenleaf, one of the oldest trees in the Valenwood
### Dawn Era
Valenwood's known history begins as early as the Dawn Era, when [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre) purportedly began to shape the formless Ooze with their Naming of things.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-t Oa F-21)</sup> It was in Valenwood that his first creations such as [the Eldest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Eldest) could be found, many of which have survived into later eras.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Indae ESO-22)</sup> [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) originated from the Valenwood, but how they came to inhabit the land is a point of contention. Bosmeri stories claim that Y'ffre gave the Wood Elves the shape they inhabit today at the dawn of existence, before even other animals were made. Y'ffre then shared with the Bosmer their unique ability of [Spinning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spinner), provided they use it to protect Y'ffre's first creation, [the Green](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green), which is what the Bosmer believe to be the origin of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-t Oa F-21)</sup> [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) descriptions of their origins, however, are far less fanciful. They purport that early [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) arrived in Valenwood at some point during the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and simply changed over the generations in order to survive in the hostile forest, eventually forming a symbiotic bond with it and becoming the Bosmer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

### Merethic Era
In either case, Bosmer had become well established in the Valenwood by the middle Merethic Era, though they were far from the only settlers of the land. [Wood Orcs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wood_Orcs) have reportedly lived in the forest for as long as the Wood Elves, if not longer. <sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESODra.27bul Load-2)</sup> A Pocket Guide to the Empire also states that the likes of [Centaurs](Lore_Centaur.md), [Satyrs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satyr), [Minotaurs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minotaur), [giants](Lore_Giant.md), [fairy folk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Faerie), and the ape-like [Imga](Lore_Imga.md) "flourished there before the first Aldmeri stepped onto its shores." As a whole, society within the Valenwood would remain a disjointed mess of disparate peoples throughout the remainder of the Merethic era. <sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

In the final year of the Merethic Era, [Eplear Camoran](Lore_Eplear.md) achieved the impossible and united the many clans of Bosmer into a single kingdom known as Valenwood. The formation of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) has been considered momentous enough to have signified the end of the Merethic Era and the beginning of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png/200px-ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png) The ruins of Gil-Var-Delle, as depicted by [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md)
### First Era
Under the early dynasty, the kingdom of Valenwood became a haven for outsiders. It welcomed escaped human slaves of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) with open arms, as well as a mass of [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) refugees after [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) overthrew them in [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ASi V-23)</sup> Relations between the two elven races were common, and there is evidence to suggest that rather than face extinction, the Ayleids of Valenwood simply interbred with Bosmer until they became indistinguishable.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-t LOKot A-24)</sup>

The Ayleid diaspora would also cause friction with the other races of Valenwood. The Wood Orcs in particular were hit hard by their arrival, as they were driven from their sacred shrine to [Mauloch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mauloch) during the construction of the Ayleid city [Abamath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abamath). <sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TTo A-18)</sup>

Relations with the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) and Valenwood were amicable to start with. The Camoran Dynasty would even forge a trade treaty with the Alessian Empire in 1E 340.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> This arrangement would not last long, however, as [Marukh's](Lore_Marukh.md) fervently anti-elven [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md) would be adopted by the Empire a mere two decades later.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-t LOKot A-24)</sup>

By [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369), the Empire and its allies began to prepare for war with Valenwood. In response to this, the Bosmer unleashed the first documented [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md), a ritual where many Bosmer revert into primordial monstrosities, and invaded Cyrodiil. This led to the death of High King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md) of Skyrim, a [Marukhati](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marukhati_Selective) supporter who had come to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to help convince the Empire to invade Valenwood.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> This death would have far-reaching implications for Tamriel as a whole, kickstarting a fifty-year war of succession within [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE1S-25)</sup> granting [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) their independence,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-AHOD-26)</sup> and ending the invasion of Valenwood before it could begin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

At some point after finding the [Flask of Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flask_of_Lillandril) in [1E 470](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_470), the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) used the flask to perform favors for the barons of Summerset Isle by keeping in check the magical power of the Bosmer in Valenwood.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-EA3-27)</sup> One legend says that a stable boy defeated the [High Wizard of Valenwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Wizard_of_Valenwood) using only the flask.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-RGJffer-28)</sup> After some time, the elves who discovered the flask feared that Bosmer assassins would kill them, so they fled hoping to buy safety from the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md), but perished in a shipwreck and the Flask of Lillandril was lost.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-EA3-27)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-RGGerrick-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-RGFalicia-30)</sup>

Though border skirmishes would continue for years to come, Valenwood would ultimately outlast the Alessian Empire. It would not be until [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714) that Valenwood was finally conquered by its successor, the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). Emperor [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman_Cyrodiil.md) would allow the Camoran Dynasty to continue to rule over Valenwood as a province of the Empire but also granted many lesser nobles independence to break up their influence.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

In the last year of the First Era, during the chaotic events preceding the assassination of [Emperor Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) was summoned within the Bosmeri city of [Gil-Var-Delle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gil-Var-Delle) and subsequently razed it. This event would inspire the [Dunmeri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dunmeri) demigod [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) to draft what would be known as the "Coldharbour Compact", an agreement between Sotha Sil and the most powerful of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) to never manifest physically within the Mundus and act only through intermediaries.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TLYot FE-31)</sup>

### Second Era
Around [2E 382](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_382), the walking city of [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md), the traditional home of the Camoran King, seemed to vanish from the face of Nirn during one of its routine migrations.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Andewen ESO-32)</sup> This caused mass panic throughout Valenwood and the creation of the Falinesti Faithful, a group operating under the belief their faith could return the lost city. Despite many efforts to discover the truth, Falinesti's mysterious disappearance would remain unexplained for at least two hundred more years.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ON-33)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/ON-npc-King_Camoran_Aeradan.jpg/200px-ON-npc-King_Camoran_Aeradan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Camoran_Aeradan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Camoran_Aeradan.jpg) King Camoran Aeradan, co-founder of the first Aldmeri Dominion After the Second Empire finally fell in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430) with the death of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), the Camoran Dynasty would attempt to re-establish its dominion over Valenwood to limited success. The many clans had drifted too far apart under Imperial and Akaviri rule, and tensions had grown hot between the ardent Green Pact supporters and those who lived happily under Imperial rule, making reunification an extremely difficult task.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TROC-34)</sup>

In [2E 489](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_489), these tensions erupted into a civil war between the Bosmer which would come to be known as the Blacksap Rebellion, instigated by Camoran Gelthior under the belief that his cousin [Aeradan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan) refused to uphold the Green Pact.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TROC-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOGel-35)</sup> It ultimately took the intervention of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) to put an end to the war in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TMa C-36)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-TROC-34)</sup> Afterward, Camoran Aeradan would be recognized as the King of Valenwood and joined into the First [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) with Summerset and [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-AOSAYQ-37)</sup> while Gelthior was imprisoned beneath Cormount,<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOGel-35)</sup> the very site of the massacre which ended the war.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TMa C-36)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an unknown entity took control of the corpse of Prince Naemon Aldmeri during a botched resurrection in Greenshade. The shade sought to overthrow the Valenwood and replace it with its own [Shadow Wood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Wood).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Rt P-38)</sup> It intended to plunge the province Valenwood into darkness, unleashing dark spirits and driving all Bosmer within mad.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Indaenir-39)</sup> It was ultimately stopped by the Vestige and the newest Silvenar before it could successfully claim the Valenheart in Hectahame. <sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Sat H-40)</sup>

The First Aldmeri Dominion would eventually dissolve, and Valenwood once again fell into disunity. By [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830), another civil war between Camoran heirs had broken out. One faction of Bosmer attempted to make peace with their longtime enemies to the north, the Colovians, in order to secure their support. This caught the attention of Summerset, who quickly joined with the opposing side to crush the Colovian sympathizers. Following the appointment of the new Camoran king Anaxemes, the second Aldmeri Dominion was formed. <sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-41)</sup>

Valenwood avoided most of the fighting during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md), as [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s use of the Great [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) on Summerset brought an end to the conflict.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TAH-42)</sup> Both Summerset and Valenwood were absorbed by the Third Empire in [3E 0](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_0). Camoran Kings were once again allowed to reign over Valenwood, so long as they pledged loyalty to the Empire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/ON-npc-Selene_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Selene_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Selene_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Selene_02.jpg) The forest spirit Selene, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) By [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249), many Bosmer had become discontent with life under Imperial rule.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md) took advantage of this and, with his army of mercenaries and Daedra alongside the boon of a mysterious benefactor, would claim the Camoran throne for himself and begin to wage war on the other provinces of Tamriel.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TR-43)</sup> He would go on to conquer western Cyrodiil and the majority of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) before eventually setting his sights on High Rock in [3E 266](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_266).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-44)</sup> However, whatever boons his master had provided had long faded,<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TR-43)</sup> and the people of Valenwood soon revolted against him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> After losing his home province, he would ultimately be defeated by the combined might of High Rock's many kingdoms.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOTU-45)</sup> Haymon Camoran fell in battle just outside of [Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen) in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267), though his son Mankar was born soon after.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TR-43)</sup>

Following Haymon's rule, Valenwood was a broken land. The Bosmer no longer trusted outside rule or the rule of their own leaders.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> The Bosmer of this time did not consider themselves a fighting people, and had no standing army. Preferring to dwell among mother earth, rejoicing in the commune of the land.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Ulandra Dialogue-46)</sup> The [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) only made things worse, with Summerset and Elsweyr both seizing land from the province in the chaos<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup> and the forest spirit [Selene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Selene) attempting to wrest control of the entire province with her spider warriors.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Ulandra Dialogue-46)</sup> The cities were abandoned for forest life, the tribal council was all but abolished, and what little national pride remained seemed to die.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) struck. Falinesti rooted itself in place for the first time in history to prepare for the event, and a Wild Hunt broke out across the province to defend it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Val-1)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In [4E 29](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_29), Valenwood's Imperial-friendly government was overthrown in a [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) -backed coup. Bosmer loyalists and supporting Imperial forces were subsequently defeated by better-prepared Bosmer troops aligned with the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), though rebels were still being supplied by the Empire until [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-LOS-47)</sup> In the aftermath, Valenwood's new government proclaimed a union with Summerset Isle, now known as Alinor, and the third [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) was formed.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-RTIV-48)</sup> Since the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), surviving [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) have claimed that the Aldmeri Dominion has been covertly "purging" Valenwood of certain Bosmeri families.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-SRDelphine-49)</sup>

## Architecture
Bosmeri cities are usually located on the branches and in the trunks of the graht-oaks, with some individual tree-houses on the forest floor. The [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) does not allow the Bosmer to shape the trees in the traditional way, so instead Bosmeri Pod-Singers magically persuade Valenwood trees to grow into houses.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONDeepwoods Pod-Singer Tunic-50)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

However, Valenwood does not consist only of the Bosmeri tree-cities. Some of the cities are built in other architectural styles: for example [Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haven) and [Marbruk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marbruk) are primarily [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md), [Southpoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Southpoint) is [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md), and [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia) is a mixture of Colovian and [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) styles.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

Valenwood is also dotted with numerous [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins, built by Ayleid refugees from Cyrodiil. In the northeastern part, old Khajiiti temples can also be found.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

## Flora
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/ON-quest-Frighten_the_Fearsome_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Frighten_the_Fearsome_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Frighten_the_Fearsome_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Frighten_the_Fearsome_02.jpg) The Shimmering Kollopi Tree Valenwood's sub-tropical climate supports a great wealth of flora, which often grows to enormous proportions. The Green Pact forbids the Bosmer to harm the plants, so they grow uncontrollably, resulting in wild, dense jungles and forests. The massive [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) trees are common in the province, which are sometimes hollowed out and inhabited by the Bosmer.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-AA-51)</sup> These graht-oaks occasionally uproot themselves and migrate to a different portion of Valenwood on occasion, with some like Falinesti following seasonal migrations.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ADIF2-52)</sup> The canopies they create are so large, they are sometimes referred to as a **second sky** (or the "**middle green**").<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-RGFalicia-30)</sup>

Alongside graht-oaks, there are sapient trees such as Brackenleaf who are allied with the Bosmer.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESOInsight-53)</sup> The small palm leafed [Kollopi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kollopi) trees are another common sight in Valenwood. Their essence and seeds are often used in rituals by the native population.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ESOFrighten-54)</sup>

## Fauna
Valenwood's lush environment is home to many creatures, such as [monkeys](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Monkey), [antelopes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Antelope), [flipbacks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flipback), [giant wasps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Giant_Wasp), [sweetgnats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sweetgnat), [thunderbugs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thunderbug), [tree-hoppers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tree-Hopper), [spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan), [crocodiles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crocodile), and giant [ticks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tick) called [hoarvors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoarvor). [Timber mammoths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mammoth) inhabit Greenshade and Malabal Tor. [Senche-tigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-Tiger) are present all throughout Valenwood, but are most numerous in Reaper's March and Greenshade. <sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup> The [Kollopi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kollopi) is an arboreal rodent which lives and feasts exclusively on branches of graht-oak trees, despite sharing a name with the kollopi tree.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-TRKR-55)</sup> A sort of winged [mimic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mimic) is known to inhabit Valenwood and regurgitate whatever words they overhear. They are described by famed author [Waughin Jarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waughin_Jarth) as legless creatures with crimson eyes and a fat bellies, darting through the air like sparks from a fire.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ADIF5-56)</sup> Thalmir Silk Thread is a type of cloth which originates from insects in Valenwood.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPHH-57)</sup>

The lines between fauna and flora can become blurred in Valenwood, and many plant creatures inhabit the forests as well. Carnivorous [Stranglers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strangler) inhabit the heavily wooded portions of Malabal Tor and Grahtwood, while [spriggans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spriggan) and even the larger [lurchers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lurcher) can be found all throughout the Valenwood.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZone-8)</sup>

[Hectahame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hectahame) An Ayleid ruin containing the [Heart of Valenwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Valenwood). [Selene's Web](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Selene%27s_Web) A hidden forest between Malabal Tor and Reaper's March ruled by [Selene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Selene), a mysterious forest spirit. [Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silvenar) A city in eastern Malabal Tor, the spiritual center of Valenwood. [Southpoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Southpoint) A port city in southwestern Grahtwood founded by a cohort of Imperial soldiers in the early days of the Second Empire. [Velyn Harbor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Velyn_Harbor) A port town in western Malabal Tor. [Whisper Grove](Lore_Whisper_Grove.md) A city in eastern Greenshade destroyed by the forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). [Woodhearth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Woodhearth) A city in western Greenshade, near the shore.
