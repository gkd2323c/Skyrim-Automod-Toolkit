Hlaalu Helseth, a male [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), was born to [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) and [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) in [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RFT-1)</sup> and he is the older brother of [Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md). Before the "[Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md)", he spent most of his time blackmailing, bribing, and even killing nobles in attempts to expand Wayrest's influence. After failing to obtain the throne of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) when his step-father King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) died, he returned to [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) with his mother. Once there, he joined the court of his grand uncle, King [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Llethan.md). After a relatively short period of time, [Talen Vandas](Lore_Talen_Vandas.md), Llethan's chosen heir, died, followed soon after by the King himself, leaving the throne of Morrowind to Helseth. This caused rumors that Helseth had poisoned them, a telling of events that the Queen Dowager, Ravani Llethan, backs up: she also knew of and appears to have been allied to a conspiracy, within [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), against Helseth in favor of a candidate more acceptable to the House. Indeed, despite Helseth being of House Hlaalu himself as well as a nephew of King Llethan, he still was an outlander, not born in Morrowind itself, and a pro-Imperial at that, having his elven ears cropped as to look more Imperial and knowing almost nothing about Dunmer culture<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GDD-2)</sup>. This political stance was certainly shared by his late father, who ended up fighting for the Empire against his own province. The Tribunal Temple also initially conspired against him, but were thrown into disarray by the fall of their living gods. He wore an ancient crown of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), with five points curving inward like blades, supporting one in the middle: what it symbolized in elder days was debatable, but it meant "Five Houses Under One King" in the modern day.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-3)</sup>

After becoming King, Helseth quickly started to cement his hold on power by using the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) to assassinate a variety of targets, including an attempt to kill the recently reborn [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TRDBA-4)</sup> though according to Helseth this was a mistake<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRHelseth-5)</sup> - and one which backfired heavily, since the entire Mournhold chapter of the Brotherhood was wiped out when the Nerevarine pursued the attacks back to their source.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRHDB-6)</sup> The truthfulness of Helseth's apology is suspect, as there was another attempt on the Nerevarine's life while guarding Queen Barenziah for the new King.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRAAA-7)</sup> Strangely, King Helseth tends to play up his habit of assassination, as he was one of the driving forces to publish *[A Game at Dinner](Lore_A_Game_at_Dinner.md)*, a book that accuses him of murder.

In a shrewd political maneuver, King Helseth, representing his own [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), formed an alliance with [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) by marrying Lady Vendama of [Bodrum Mora](Lore_Bodrum.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBRumors-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-3)</sup> Subsequently, he renounced the slave trade,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3M-9)</sup> setting the remaining Houses of [Indoril](Lore_Indoril.md) and [Redoran](Lore_Redoran.md) against him through a period of civil unrest. After the successive catastrophes of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) invasion and the break-up of the Empire which had backed him, at some early point during the next 200 years House Hlaalu fell from prominence and Redoran became the new preeminent house, with Hlaalu's place taken by the upstart House [Sadras](Lore_Sadras.md). Neither Helseth nor Barenziah's, nor the Morrowind monarchy's fate after these catastrophies is known, although the former are presumed to have lost their lives before or after the sacking of Mournhold by the invading Argonian hordes.

## Notes
- In *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, Helseth (as with all other ["Dark Elf"](Lore_Dark_Elf.md) NPCs) is depicted with as having deep tan skin as opposed to the expected ashy black Dunmer flesh tones seen elsewhere. This persists in all versions in the absence of third-party patches.
- Helseth is a randomized Dunmer name in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* and also the name of the ruler of [Silnim Dale](Lore_Silnim_Dale.md); any mention of him (or connection to Helseth Hlaalu) is never made past *Arena*.
