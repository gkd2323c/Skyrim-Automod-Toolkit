—The Vestige The Vestige, known as the **Soulless One** and by a plethora of [other names](#Titulature),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-STA-1)</sup> was a [Soul-Shriven](Lore_Soul-Shriven.md) [hero of prophecy](Lore_Hero.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CCTAP-2)</sup> As a mortal, they were sacrificed to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) by [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) as part of a dark ritual in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SSi C-3)</sup> They may have been chosen by the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) to be a savior to the many corners of Tamriel, with many crises such as the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), the schemes of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), and the Dragons that ravaged [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) happening in such a short amount of time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESONor-4)</sup> It is possible that they momentarily ruled Tamriel as a so-called "[Battlefield Emperor](Lore_Battlefield_Emperors.md)",<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FBPLS-5)</sup> although the legitimacy of their rule would be debatable.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Clivia Tharn Emp-6)</sup>

## Characteristics
While common [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md) lack the focusing principle of their [Anuic](Lore_Anu.md) souls, scholars theorized that if they still possessed some other intrinsic Anuic aspect, they would form an unflawed body in Coldharbour that was a perfect duplicate of the body worn in Mundus, resulting in a "paragon" Soul Shriven. Should this individual bear a sufficiently high Anuic valence, their body would form almost instantaneously upon contact with [Padomaic](Lore_Padomay.md) [creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md). Further, scholars extrapolate that such an occurrence would take place if [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) was subject to an existential threat. In this situation, the Heart of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) would spontaneously generate such individuals as a way of defending itself from destruction.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CCTAP-2)</sup> It would also be possible for a Soul Shriven to restore their physical form if they became re-attuned to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). One such method is by absorbing the power of a [Skyshard](Lore_Skyshard.md), fragments of [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) which carry the essence of Nirn within them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONProphet-7)</sup>

These theories were seemingly proven correct, as in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579) Molag Bal began an attempt to [meld his plane](Lore_Planemeld.md) of Oblivion with Nirn to create a single nightmarish realm.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Pt DA-8)</sup> Only a few years later in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Vestige seemingly exhibited such exceptional Anuic qualities and escaped Coldharbour, going on to combat the Daedric threat as foretold by the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CCTAP-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONProphet-7)</sup>

The Vestige can also use [wayshrines](Lore_Wayshrines.md) to travel and resurrect due to their nature. As first theorized by [Corvus Direnni](Lore_Corvus_Direnni.md), these wayshrines create an interconnected "web of sojourn" which could be manipulated to create a permanent portal network. In order to do this, the "fast traveler" would have to attune himself to each wayshrine in order to create a "node". However, to test this theory the potential traveler would have to unmoor his soul from the Mundus.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Wo T-9)</sup> The wayshrines also allowed these Soul Shriven who had been re-attuned to Anuic magic to reform in death, being reborn at the nearest shrine instead of being banished to Oblivion like a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

## History
With the aid of former emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), the Vestige escaped imprisonment in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and reformed the [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md), as foretold by the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) in the omen *"The soulless one will become the brightest of five stars, and they shall guide us to the coming dawn."*<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SSi C-3)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Varen-11)</sup>

After participating in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) for one of the three alliances throughout its territory and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the Vestige joined an invasion of Coldharbour by the [Fighters](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Mages](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) Guilds. The Vestige became instrumental to that effort, restoring [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md)'s Hollow City in Coldharbour as a beachhead for the invasion.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-t Ao M-12)</sup> The Vestige, with Meridia's assistance, thus ended [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) and regained their soul.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Go S-13)</sup>

Meridia then sent the Vestige to explore events as if they had been part of the other alliances of Tamriel.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CA-14)</sup> The Vestige then explored the aftermath of the Planemeld in [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), facing the Celestials corrupted by the Serpent.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Craglorn-15)</sup> Thereafter, the Vestige joined the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Si B-16)</sup> of which they became a Silencer,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Terenus Ft V-17)</sup> the nascent [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in Hew's Bane,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Pi C-18)</sup> and aided in the rebuilding of [Kurog](Lore_Kurog.md)'s [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Orsinium-19)</sup>

The Vestige then became involved in a Daedric war involving the Daedric Triad of the Princes [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) when they foiled the Triad's plot to sap [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md) of his power, before preventing their takeover of the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DR-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-WSL-21)</sup> The Vestige then traveled to Summerset and became a member of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-a Po GP-22)</sup> Nocturnal, however betrayed the Triad, and was promptly defeated by the Psijics, Vestige, and champions of Meridia and the betrayed princes.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-t CT-23)</sup> The Vestige then journeyed to Murkmire with the Cyrodiilic Collection Agency,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ST-24)</sup> discovering the lost Root-Whisper tribe.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-t Ro A-25)</sup>

The Vestige later reunited with Abnur Tharn, inadvertently aiding in [his sister](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md)'s plot to have him release dragons across Tamriel.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-t DW-26)</sup> Abnur and the Vestige prevented Euraxia's dragon allies from siphoning [Jode's](Lore_Jode.md) Core<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-JC-27)</sup> and reclaimed the kingdom of Rimmen from Euraxia for its true heir, Khamira.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-t Ho A-28)</sup> The Vestige would also ally with Sai Sahan to re-establish the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) in Southern Elsweyr.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-R-29)</sup> The Dragonguard forged an alliance with the dragon [Nahfahlaar](Lore_Nahfahlaar.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-t DL-30)</sup> who empowered the Vestige with the [Mask of Alkosh](Lore_Mask_of_Alkosh.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-t Po A-31)</sup> The Vestige, Abnur Tharn, Khamira, Sahan, Nahfahlaar, and the Dragonguard thus stopped the leader of the unleashed dragons, [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md), from ascending to godhood, though their mission came at the apparent cost of Abnur Tharn's life.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-NMR-32)</sup>

Eventually the Vestige met [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md) once again in [Icereach](Lore_Icereach.md) and aided her in defeating the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md). They also helped the last Pyre Watch Sentinel and Fennorian, an investigator from [House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md), defend Unhallowed Grave against grave-robbing necromancers. The hero later met with Lyris and investigated a lead on the Icereach Coven in behalf of the [Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TCConspiracy-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TCConundrum-34)</sup> The Vestige traveled to Western Skyrim where they saved [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) from a harrowstorm, and ousted and slew the [High King](Lore_Svargrim.md) of Western Skyrim for being a pawn of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-GR-35)</sup> The Vestige subsequently aided Gwendis in defeating a [Vampire Lord](Lore_Ingerien_Direnni.md) and a mad alchemist, both of which were allies of the Gray Host. They continued aiding the Ravenwatch in investigating the Gray Host.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-RI-36)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TGC-37)</sup> The hero's journey led them to [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) where they ousted and defeated a traitor in the [Despot of Markarth](Lore_Caddach.md)'s court, temporarily saving Markarth in the process.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TSOS-38)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-AFOS-39)</sup> Finally, the Vestige traveled to [Grayhaven](Lore_Grayhaven.md) and put an end to [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md) once and for all, stopping the Gray Host and saving the Reach.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-KOA-40)</sup>

In the [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) region of Cyrodiil and Black Marsh, the Vestige uncovered a dark pact between the fallen [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) of Cyrodiil and the Daedric Prince, [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-It D-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-BS-42)</sup> Four children were imbued with the [egonymic](Lore_Egonymic.md) of Dagon himself so that they would attune him to Nirn when rejoined with him. Though the power of three Ambitions were claimed by Dagon, the Vestige allied with the final Ambition to banish him from Nirn once more.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-43)</sup>

In the [Systres archipelago](Lore_Systres.md), while working under [Lady Arabelle](Lore_Arabelle_Davaux.md) and [Lord Bacaro Volorus](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md), the Vestige uncovered a plot to assassinate the Alliance leaders— [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) and, in [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md)'s case, his son [Irnskar](Lore_Irnskar.md) —by the [Ascendant Order](Lore_Ascendant_Order.md) during crucial peace talks to end the war. Corralling the leaders to All Flags Islet, the Vestige arranged a defense of the isle, succeeding in protecting the leaders and defeated the Ascendant Magus, second-in-command of the Ascendant Order.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TCAM-44)</sup> The Vestige then unmasked Bacaro as the Ascendant Lord and revealed his plot to become the new [Druid King](Lore_Druid_King.md) and bring an era of destruction to Tamriel, replacing the fallen Empire with his own "Ivy Throne". The Vestige confronted them at [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md), defeating the Lord and saving the archipelago from destruction.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-THLord-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TIThrone-46)</sup>

In [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), the Vestige worked with Leramil the Wise, Curate Gadayn, and the [watcher](Lore_Watcher.md) Scruut to prevent the return of [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), a forgotten [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md), while fighting off a joint attack of [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) and [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) against [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). After venturing deeper and deeper into Apocrypha, the Vestige defeated Peryite's champion Blightcrown, ended the plague poisoning Hermaeus Mora, and defeated Vaermina with Mora's help. However, the [dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md) used this conflict as a distraction to free Ithelia. Before being able to find her, the Vestige defeated a plot by the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md), a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) cult dedicated to Ithelia, to cover the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) in jungle. The Vestige found Ithelia and helped restore her control over her powers by traveling with her along the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) and creating a potion called the Sage's Dream, in order to prevent her uncontrolled power from destroying reality. However, with her control restored, she subsequently tried to use her fate-altering [Loom of the Untraveled Road](Lore_Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road.md) to rewrite history. The Vestige prevented this with the use of [four](Lore_Lamp_of_Clarity.md) [different](Lore_Echonir.md) [Daedric](Lore_Abolisher.md) artifacts, but Ithelia did not quit. She returned to Apocrypha to imprison Hermaeus Mora and take the realm for herself in an attempt to remake her realm of [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md). The Vestige showed her the error of her ways with the [Mirror of Truth](Lore_Mirror_of_Truth.md) and she relented, but Torvesard managed to steal both her and Mora's power with the [Staff of Many Paths](Lore_Staff_of_Many_Paths.md). While Torvesard tried to control the power of two Daedric Princes, the Vestige managed to defeat him and free the imprisoned Princes. After Ithelia annihilated the [vestige](Lore_Souls.md#Vestiges) of Torvesard, she agreed to be banished by Hermaeus Mora to a reality in the Many Paths where she could not return from.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOSo A-47)</sup>

## Titulature
*It is as the [Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) foretold. The soulless one will become the brightest of five stars, and they shall guide us to the coming dawn.*
—Varen Aquilarios The Vestige took on a number of titles throughout their Aurbic adventures. They were given their first title, **the Vestige**, by the former Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), who learned of a **Soulless One**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-STA-1)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-48)</sup> who would save Tamriel from the clutches of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) through his studies of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). Almalexia also foresaw the arrival of the soulless, deathless one who could save [Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_Gil-Var-Delle.md).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-HOD-49)</sup> After defeating the Lord of Domination, they were also bestowed by him the titles of the **Savior of Tamriel**, the **Hero of Coldharbour**, and **Meridia's Champion**.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Varen-11)</sup> In vanquishing the Maulborn Cult from [Mournhold Temple](Lore_Mournhold_Temple.md), Almalexia named them a **[Hand of Almalexia](Lore_Hands_of_Almalexia.md)**.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ONAlmalexia-50)</sup> For their efforts against Prince [Fildgor](Lore_Fildgor.md) and solidifying the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) in [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), the Vestige was named the **High King's Arrow** (or **King's Arrow** or **The Arrow of the King**) by Prince [Irnskar](Lore_Irnskar.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONIrnskar-51)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ONAttiring-52)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ONJaga-53)</sup> In Auridon, while working as an agent for [Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Queen_Ayrenn), they were named an **Eye of the Queen**.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-AHS-54)</sup> Similarly the Vestige was named an **Eye of Raz** by [Razum-dar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Razum-dar).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-RD-55)</sup> In Reaper's March, the Vestige was chosen to be the **Moon Hallowed**, the person who would help the Lunar Champion walk the path to become the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Kauzanabi-jo-56)</sup> Upon uniting the [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) tribes and stopping King Kurog's plot, they were deemed the **Hero of Wrothgar**.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-KTD-57)</sup> When they fought the Daedric Triad throughout Tamriel, the Vestige was recognized as both a **Champion of Vivec**<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-VD-58)</sup> and the **Savior of Summerset**.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-RDD-59)</sup> To the people of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) they became known as **Rootmender**<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-DAD-60)</sup> and **Egg-Savior**.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ONShuvu-61)</sup> The people of Northern Elsweyr named them the **Champion of Anequina** for their part in stopping the dragon Kaalgrontiid,<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-KD-62)</sup> while their ally Nahfahlaar gave the Vestige the dragon name of **Ahkahtuz**.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-63)</sup> They were named **Champion of the Alliances** by the three [Alliance leaders](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) and Lady [Arabelle Davaux](Lore_Arabelle_Davaux.md),<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ANPP-64)</sup> and the **Defender of the Three Thrones** and the **Savior of the Systres** by Lord [Bacaro Volorus](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-LBVTHK-65)</sup> The Vestige was known as **The Scourge** by the inhabitants of Bal Sunnar.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-UFT-66)</sup> They were granted the title of **pathwalker** by an [alternate](Lore_Alternate_Realities.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md).<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Outcast MP-67)</sup> To the keepers of the Scholarium, they were **Altar-Breaker**<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ONCrow-68)</sup> and **Fable-Bearer**.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ONCrow-68)</sup>

Beyond these, they received many, many more [titles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Titles) -- some small, like becoming a **Deputy of the Mistral Guard**,<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ONHarrani-69)</sup> and some great, like becoming an honorary member **of House Ravenwatch**<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-ONVerandis-70)</sup> -- for their accolades, feats and adventures:<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ONTitles-71)</sup>

## Notes
- Although most narratives generally refer to the Vestige as a single individual, there have been references to multiple fated heroes or theoretical "paragons" who possess a similar ability to reform their body upon contact with Padomaic creatia, resulting in immediate resurrection.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-72)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CCTAP-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-73)</sup>
- The Vestige was known to be able to read the [ancient Nord language](Lore_Nordic.md).<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-QNDialogue-74)</sup>
- In early drafts, the working title for the Vestige was originally going to be the "Numinous".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-75)</sup>
- [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) were known to exclaim *"by the Soulless One"*.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-ADE-76)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-DDE-77)</sup>
- A [statue](Lore_Statues.md) of the Vestige was said to have been erected in their honor after Nurin Farm in [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md) was rebuilt, but whatever came of it is unknown.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-ONGanise-78)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ONCorslet-79)</sup>
- A mural of the Vestige was said to have been carved within Xor-Hist in honour of their assistance in saving the village from the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md).<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-XHist-80)</sup>
