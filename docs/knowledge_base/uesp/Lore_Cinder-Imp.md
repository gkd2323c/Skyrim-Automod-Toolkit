*The imp cackled at the confusion it was causing, flipping over in the air and making all sorts of rude hand gestures. These stopped abruptly as it dropped to the ground gracelessly, after steel hand axe landed in its back.*
— *[Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Kyne%27s_Challenge:_A_Hunter%27s_Companion)* Imps, sometimes colloquially known as **dwarflings** or **trickster sprites**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KCAHC-1)</sup> are small, winged humanoids who are slightly intelligent and can cast a variety of [spells](Lore_Magic.md). They are known to be aggressive and mischievous creatures. The origins of the imp are not clearly understood; some sources suggest they may be [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) beings,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Do A-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Am MA-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCL-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OOC-5)</sup> while others state that they are artificial constructs similar to [golems](Lore_Golem.md). It may be that both theories are correct, or that they are something else entirely.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATRMAN-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CC-7)</sup> Another claim is that they are [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) or at least natural creatures.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Imps are listed among the types of common Daedra that can be found in service to every [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Do A-2)</sup> Imps were also among the first beings summoned from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) during the development of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) magic, further suggesting at least some imps could be Daedra.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OOC-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCL-4)</sup> They are commonly found in the wilderness throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), often gathering in caves.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OB-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBAryarie-11)</sup> They can also be found in planes such as the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> or in the company of elemental [atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

## Behavior and Uses
Imps are social creatures that prefer to travel in flocks.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LCL-4)</sup> They are occasionally territorial, with imps of differing elemental abilities in the wild having been sighted engaging in magic duels.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-10)</sup> Imps are known to rarely carry small wicker effigies.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Imps have been described as mischievious, crafty, and intelligent creatures, who know when to fight and when to flee.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATRMAN-6)</sup>

While some are capable of speaking of [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Loose-17)</sup> imps have their own [tongue](Lore_Languages.md) known as Impish. Speakers of Impish can tame or even summon these creatures.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DFImpish-18)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KCAHC-1)</sup> The language was taught by both the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and the [School of Julianos](Lore_School_of_Julianos.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DFImpish-18)</sup> The wizard [Divayth Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Divayth_Fyr) is a preeminent expert on imps,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Frostshriek-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOStoneglow-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BDI-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Mossblood-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Azure-23)</sup> despite his dislike of the creatures.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

Although usually hostile to other species, some imps can be quite good-natured once they have imprinted on an individual.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOStoneglow-20)</sup> Some Imps use [illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) magic to disguise themselves as more impressive creatures, such as [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), as a way to trick others into taking care of them.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BDI-21)</sup> Imps often serve as [familiars](Lore_Familiar.md) or vault guardians for mages and magisters,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> although they have been known to go rogue.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Loose-17)</sup> Imps were experimented on by the Mages Guild, with it being rumored they were even victims of torture.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Loose-17)</sup> Daedra have been known to hire imp-drawn carts to transport items across the [planes of Oblivion](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

Imps are sometimes killed and harvested for their [imp gall](Lore_Imp_Gall.md), a valuable [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) ingredient.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBAryarie-11)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup> Their hide and other bodily fluids are also sometimes harvested.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-10)</sup> In [County Bravil](Lore_County_Bravil.md), a known place to hunt imps was in Robber's Glen Cave, found in the southern [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) near the [Upper Niben](Lore_Upper_Niben.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBAryarie-11)</sup> Some individuals alter their skin's epidermis through scarification or magical means to achieve an impish appearance.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Mossblood-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Azure-23)</sup>

## Variants
A wide variety of imps exist. Although their skin is usually green, imps can come in other colors such as olive, red, or grey. They may or may not be horned; usually an imp's set of horns are small, but they can be curved and protruding.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DF-32)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OB-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESO-10)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KCAHC-1)</sup>

Stoneglow imps, also known as azure imps, are a dark-colored variety that possess icy blue eyes and glowing claws that can shatter stone.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOStoneglow-20)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Azure-23)</sup> Frostshriek imps appear as if formed from solid ice, and are known to drop the ambient temperature of their surroundings when summoned.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Frostshriek-19)</sup> Illusion imps are notorious for conjuring fantastical illusions, such as disguising themselves as miniature Dragons or [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup> Cinder-imps are known to nest in the ducts of Daedric [forges](Lore_Smithing.md) and gorge themselves on supplies of charcoal.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup> Mossblood imps possess a green coloration, which lends itself well to walking undetected among the foliage of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Mossblood-22)</sup>

## History
[Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md) was infested by the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565),<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36)</sup> and the city was still plagued by the [disease](Lore_Disease.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-37)</sup> It's said that the Daedric Prince [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) once helped a group of Illusion imps escape the flu-ridden city.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-38)</sup>
