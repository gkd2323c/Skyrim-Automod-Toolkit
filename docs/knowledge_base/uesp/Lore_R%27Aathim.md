2]](#cite_note-TPEvening Star-11)</sup> According to the historical fictional *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)* book series, the Clan R'Aathim were originally a group of miners that held territory stretching across the central mainland, but they eventually became the royalty of Ebonheart and Mournhold.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KEVII-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup> The ancestral estate of the clan is **Ald R'Aathim**,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TPEvening Star-11)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> a coastal port on the Inner Sea just to the east of the Velothi Mountains (southwest [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) have hailed from the Clan R'Aathim, including the Empress-Regent [Katariah R'Aathim](Lore_Katariah.md), one of the most well-known descendants of the 141](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_141).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWQ8-13)</sup> Near the dawn of Morrowind as a country, the Clan R'Aathim are said to have held the [High Kingship of Morrowind](Lore_Hortator.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-place-Charred_Ridge_%28Ebonheart%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Charred_Ridge_%28Ebonheart%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Charred_Ridge_(Ebonheart).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Charred_Ridge_(Ebonheart).jpg) Ebonheart during the early First Era The Clan R'Aathim were known as prominent figures throughout history. In early Morrowind History, the Clan R'Aathim retained the High Kingship of Morrowind and held lucrative mining fiefdoms across Morrowind, between Ebonheart and [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup> According to [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) mythology, the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) had a sphere of influence in the R'Aathim's territory.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-36Lo VS22-10)</sup> According to the potentially fictional *King Edward* book series, King [Kronin R'Aathim](Lore_Kronin.md) was the King of Ebonheart when the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) invaded the province of Morrowind in [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240), driving the Clan R'Aathim out of Ebonheart and into hiding. Several years later when the Chimer fought back, King Kronin and his three sons took to guerrilla warfare, driving the Nords out of their domain. [Kronin](Lore_Kronin.md)'s sister Yoriss became the Queen of [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md) after the war.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-KEX-14)</sup> When King Kronin passed away, his son [Cruethys](Lore_Cruethys.md) became king, but he too passed away, leaving Cruethys' brother Moraelyn to the throne at Ebonheart. The third brother [S'ephen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:S%27ephen) (also spelled Ephen), born in Mournhold, was an illegitimate son of Kronin, and therefore not considered a R'Aathim.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KEXII-2)</sup> To honor him, Moraelyn created a temple-cult dedicated to Ephen and left the legendary [Horn of Summoning](Lore_Horn_of_Summoning.md) artifact under his protection, in his tomb underneath Mournhold.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KEXII-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> Eventually, King Moraelyn passed the mantle to his daughter, Lian, who in turn, had twin sons that were left, joint-heirs. At this point, the High Kingship had been left vacant due to a provincial emergency. Both twins were given separate kingdoms that resulted in the split that became modern-day Ebonheart and Mournhold.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup>

### House Mora & Katariah
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/LO-misc-Katariah_figurehead.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Katariah_figurehead.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Katariah_figurehead.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Katariah_figurehead.jpg) Katariah depiction The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) historically had ties with the Dark Elves through a trade deal with Clan R'Aathim and the Kings of High Rock, where the retainers of King Moraelyn of Ebonheart were allowed to mine in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) for [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and start a colony for their people in High Rock in exchange for providing ebony weapons to the Breton lords and teaching the Bretons how to smith ebony themselves.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-KE-15)</sup> [House Mora](Lore_House_Mora.md) of [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) and its Clan R'Aathim notably had human blood in its family tree, before that house fell and was adopted into the [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRB5-16)</sup> There are some sources that suggest [King Edward](Lore_Edward.md) of Daggerfall was the son of the Breton [Queen Aliera](Lore_Aliera.md) of Wayrest and King Moraelyn.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Jindra-6)</sup>

Clan R'Aathim came back back into power at the city-state of Ebonheart during the Tiber War. However long before these events back in the First Era, Mournhold was given the special guerden to guard: the Horn of summoning due to Moraelyn's declaration and Ephen's origins. And so, their jealousy had become greater when an equally special guerden—the Staff of Chaos—was placed in Mournhold when [Emperor Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) took the [Horn of Summoning](Lore_Horn_of_Summoning.md) from Mournhold.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup>

In the early-mid [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Ebonheart, and the Clan R'Aathim were allies of [Potema Septim](Lore_Potema.md), the Wolf-Queen of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). Ebonheart fought alongside her during the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), which ended with Potema's death in [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137). Because Mournhold was an ally of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), Ebonheart was in a bad situation, and to preserve the peace between both factions, Emperor [Magnus Septim](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) had arranged the marriage of [Katariah R'Aathim](Lore_Katariah.md), Duchess of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) with his son, King [Pelagius Septim III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) of Solitude in [3E 141](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_141). Because of Pelagius III's crippling madness, Katariah had essentially taken control of the Empire, working alongside the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) to not only govern the Empire but hide Pelagius III's madness. As time went on, Katariah had a child with Pelagius III named [Cassynder Septim](Lore_Cassynder.md), and Pelagius III died on [Betony](Lore_Betony.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TMo P-8)</sup> Katariah I was named the Empress-Regent, and her reign (lasting for forty-six years) was known for being the Empire's celebrated years. However, some people claimed that Katariah's ascension was the decline of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md). When Katariah died, Cassynder Septim was the Emperor of Tamriel, and his half-brother, Katariah's son, [Uriel Septim IV](Lore_Uriel_IV.md) was named the King of Wayrest and the rest is history.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BHot Ev2-17)</sup>

### The Llethan Family
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Barenziah%27s Crown.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Barenziah%27s Crown.png) The Crown of Barenziah By the late Third Era, the most prominent members of the Clan R'Aathim was Imperial High Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) of Morrowind (who as ruled the Duchy of [Almalexia](Lore_Mournhold.md)), her uncle [Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md), and her cousin Talen Vandas.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IWTB-5)</sup>

In [3E 6](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_6), the [Imperial Governor](Lore_Symmachus.md) administering Morrowind Province is formally replaced and the reign of the [presiding provincial regent](Lore_Morrowind.md#Known_Rulers) begins.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRB-18)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-HP-19)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-KRP-20)</sup> Sometime not too long after this in the first century just a few years after turning seventeen, Barenziah came into her throne as was installed as Imperial Queen of Morrowind, with her husband Symmachus as her Prime Minister.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TRB-18)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-21)</sup>

During Barenziah's late reign around [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376), the Staff of Chaos was stolen from Ephen's tomb. Barenziah had been seduced by the Nightingale, a bard commonly known to have been [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md). Some sources claimed that this was false, and that the Nightingale was a former [Indoril](Lore_Indoril.md) noble named Drayven Indoril. Drayven was one of the Nightingales, a group of extremely-talented thieves that serve [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). And so, as the story goes Tharn had hired Drayven to obtain the Staff of Chaos. Despite Drayven's ties to House Indoril, the thief may have been an unknown member of the Clan R'Aathim. According to the book, *[The Real Barenziah, v 4](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah,_v_4.md)*, the Nightingale was an heir of Moraelyn and the last of his line. With his exclamation, the Nightingale was able to acquire the Staff of Chaos and give it to Jagar Tharn by certain accounts, with others saying Jagar himself as the Nightingale acquired the legendary staff.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TRBv4-9)</sup> The Imperial Battlemage attempted to kill Drayven, but it ended in failure and so he retired to [Shor's Stone](Lore_Shor%27s_Stone.md) in Skyrim. Barenziah had a child with the Nightingale, named Dralsi, but later abandoned her to keep the truth quiet.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TNV2-22)</sup> Eventually, Dralsi took the mantle of Nightingale, same as her daughter, [Karliah](Lore_Karliah.md), who was alive during the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SRKarliah-23)</sup>

Barenziah was the Queen of Morrowind since the early Third Era, but left Morrowind right around [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391) to personally plead for better diplomatic terms with the Emperor.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup> She then was falsely imprisoned at the Imperial City for more than a few years by Jagar Tharn, who had magically stolen the guise of the Emperor and was ruling in his place.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup> Back in Morrowind, a rebel force had taken over Morrowind, slaying Barenziah's husband Symmachus. And the rebel leader was declared the King of Morrowind by Jagar Tharn.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup> Eventually tensions reached a breaking point in the kingdom, and in [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396) the [Arnesian War](Lore_Arnesian_War.md) broke out between Morrowind Province and the Argonians of Black Marsh—with Morrowind ultimately winning the war.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3EM-24)</sup>

Back in the Imperial Province, Jagar Tharn and Barenziah had what is described as an extremely tenuous on-and-off-again romantic relationship, eventually leading to her becoming pregnant with Tharn's son.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup> After some tribulations, Barenziah snuck out of the city with her children with King Eadwyre of Wayrest, to whom she had been in close communication—she wedded him the following year and became the Queen of Wayrest, which was around the same time she gave birth to her son by Jagar Tharn.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup> Barenziah's steward back in Morrowind was meant to be regent over the kingdom at this time during her absence (though this contradicts the status of the rebel who had become King of Morrowind), with intentions to one day return.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TRBv5-7)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BOQB-25)</sup> However upon her marriage to Eadwyre an abdication took place, and the throne of Morrowind passed to her Barenziah's biological uncle: Hlaalu Athyn Llethan.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IWTB-5)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-21)</sup>

Sometime after [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), Barenziah's daughter, the Princess [Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md), married King [Karoodil](Lore_Karoodil.md) of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) and together had two children: Goranthir and Rinnala.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-21)</sup> Around this same time, Helseth attempted to steal the throne from the rightful heiress Princess Elysana, and after many years of infighting between the two she had won her throne; when King Eadwyre passed in 3E 419, Barenziah and her son Helseth were banished from Wayrest by a newly crowned Queen Elysana (with rumors spread that she killed her father in order to take the throne).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-AGAD-26)</sup> After a year of travel, Barenziah and Helseth had returned to their family estates in Morrowind Province just outside of Mournhold. The King allowed this under the condition that they have no aspirations towards the throne, which they accepted.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-AGAD-26)</sup> Around this time Athyn Llethan's nephew [Talen Vandas](Lore_Talen_Vandas.md) was selected to be the kingdom's heir, as Athyn had no heirs of his own.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TRGen Talen-27)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Talen Vandas and King Athyn Llethan not long after both died—with accusations spread that the newly crowned King Hlaalu Helseth Llethan was responsible for their deaths, chiefly from Queen Dowager Ravani Llethan (Athyn's wife).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRRLDia-28)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Eo MT-29)</sup> Later into the 420s, Hesleth later made an alliance with House Dres (marrying Lady Dres Vendama of [Bodrum](Lore_Bodrum.md)) and declared slavery outlawed across the province.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Loranna-30)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3EM-24)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-OBRumors-31)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^1.](#note_nb1) | The "R'Aathim" spelling is in line with the Dark Elf name generator for surnames from *Arena* and *Daggerfall*, which has the "R'-" as a starting particle for surnames -- the name seems to have been selected by author Marilyn Wasserman for *King Edward* and *The Real Barenziah* (a pair of *Daggerfall* books) as a reference to that motif. Every instance of the surname is spelt "R'Aathim" in her books (eight instances total), while *The Madness of Pelagius* spelled it "Ra'athim" (two instances total) by what seems to have been an editorial mistake which carried over into the in-game books of *Morrowind*. For accuracy to the original intent, the original spelling is prioritized. |
| --- | --- |

- Most Dark Elven surnames, including those of *Arena* and *Daggerfall*, never capitalize any characters after the first letter. And the typo version of the name "Ra'athim" prefers undercase characters.
- | [^2.](#note_nb2) | The historical fiction book *[King Edward](Lore_King_Edward.md)* was written before the introduction of the Chimer in later game entries. The Chimer would not become Dunmer until around 1E 700, which is after the timeline of the book. |
| --- | --- |
- | [^3.](#note_nb3) | [*The Real Barenziah*](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md) book series was originally published with the release of *Daggerfall*. It was included again for *Morrowind* books, but was heavily edited for readability and sometimes lore. The editors seemed to have made a small context error during this process at the end of [*The Real Barenziah v V*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:The_Real_Barenziah_v_V), referring to the *Staff of Chaos* being handed from Moraelyn to Ephen, instead of the *Horn of Summoning* being handed from Moraelyn to Ephen, as per the *Daggerfall* draft. This change doesn't make much sense at all with the provided context, and was more than likely an error on the part of the editor rather than an intentional lore alteration. Though, if it were intentional, the change would have a variety of lore implications. |
| --- | --- |
