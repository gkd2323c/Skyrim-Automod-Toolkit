[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/OBR-place-Vilverin.jpg/200px-OBR-place-Vilverin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Vilverin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Vilverin.jpg) Vilverin circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Vilverin (also known as **Lake Mist Ruins**) is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin located in the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), on the northeastern shore of [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md). The complex is split into several subsections, including Canosel, Wendesel, and Sel Sancremathi; the latter two translate as "Journey Halls" and "Hall of Golden Homes" respectively in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).

## History
In the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the Ayleids of Vilverin were among those clans who turned to [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship, known as the [Daedraphiles](Lore_Daedraphiles.md). During the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), Vilverin became home to one of the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md), Ayleid statues originally held in the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) which were dispersed to cities across Cyrodiil for safekeeping. When the Tower fell to the [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) rebels in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243), the Rulanyil clan of Vilverin was forced to migrate out of Cyrodiil to avoid slaughter. They traveled to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), where they built a new city in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) named [Rulanyil's Fall](Lore_Rulanyil%27s_Fall.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Vilverin subsequently fell into ruin.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the ruins were inhabited by a group of [trolls](Lore_Troll.md) led by the Lake Mist Fiend.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LMR-3)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) named Jalbert moved into the ruins after being forced to flee from [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md). He subsequently began to resurrect the bodies of the long-dead Ayleids in the crypts beneath the ruins.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UD-4)</sup> A group of bandits also inhabited the ruins, where they ambushed merchants along the [Red Ring Road](Lore_Red_Ring_Road.md) and collected [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) to sell to the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> However, the bandits soon began to fall victim to the undead when Jalbert needed fresh corpses for his experiments. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) then entered the ruins, slaying Jalbert and the bandits.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The Hero also retrieved the ancient Ayleid statue, which was later sold to [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
