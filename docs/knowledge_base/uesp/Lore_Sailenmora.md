[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/AR-place-Sailen_Vulgate.jpg/200px-AR-place-Sailen_Vulgate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Sailen_Vulgate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Sailen_Vulgate.jpg) Sailen Vulgate (Arena) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/AR-map-Morrowind_towns.png/200px-AR-map-Morrowind_towns.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Morrowind_towns.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-map-Morrowind_towns.png) Map of Morrowind Sailen Vulgate (or **Salen Vulgate**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWMap-1)</sup> and formally **Sailenmora** is a settlement in the central region of the southern [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md), within the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup> It began as the home for the Shrine of [Saint Vorys](Lore_Vorys.md), the former [patron saint](Lore_Temple_Saints.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VGTP-3)</sup> who helmed the expansion of [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md)'s necropolis, and ventured [elsewhere](Lore_Elsweyr.md) in the hopes he met his end in battle. <sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POSV-4)</sup> After his death, his remains were entombed in the crypts beneath the Shrine.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SMC-5)</sup> The **Old Sailenmora Outpost** was a place for pilgrims and travelers to rest and seek accommodations.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OSO-6)</sup>

The Shrine was established to honor the belief of Saint Vorys that the forceful expression of will honors the Ancestors, which House Telvanni went on to adopt as central belief<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-JHT-7)</sup> and as a motto;<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MDGH-8)</sup> aspiring members of House Telvanni would visit the Shrine, eager to test their mettle and discern the mystical nature of the [Talisman of Saint Vorys](Lore_Talisman_of_Saint_Vorys.md), a token of Vorys' conquests, and they would often be humbled with the resulting reminder that the forceful expression of will is an unending journey.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTrilam-9)</sup>

## History
Sailenmora was erected in the beginning of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) in honor of Vorys the Immolant and to commemorate the beliefs he held. His talisman was enshrined in the center of Sailenmora in the hopes that its supposed mystical properties could one day be unraveled.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTrilam-9)</sup> Vorys himself was interred in the crypts beneath the shrine<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SMC-5)</sup> alongside others, and [undead Dunmer](Lore_Death_Dunmer.md) were set to protect the halls of the crypt.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-S-10)</sup> An outpost was established by members and aspiring applicants of House Telvanni to serve as lodging while testing their will to discover the secrets of the Talisman,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OSO-6)</sup> but disuse and disinterest in the Shrine has left it in ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VGTP-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTrilam-9)</sup> Sailen Vulgate was in close proximity to Alavelis. Surveyor Tenisu Vashon traveled through the settlement during his assessment of the quality of the Alavelis Mine.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SOTAM-11)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the town of Sailen Vulgate was an active settlement. It was ruled by Lord Nisseth and had a rivalry with [Greenheights](Lore_Greenheights.md). Sailen Vulgate was neighbored by several settlements, including connections to Necrom, [Amber Forest](Lore_Amber_Forest.md), [Greenheights](Lore_Greenheights.md), and [Helnim Wall](Lore_Helnim_Wall.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup>
