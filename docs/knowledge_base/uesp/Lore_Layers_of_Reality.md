# Layers of Reality

| --- |

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-skill-Use_Fissure.jpg/200px-ON-skill-Use_Fissure.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Use_Fissure.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Use_Fissure.jpg) A rift leading to a different reality layer [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cf/ON-creature-Vaermina%27s_Nightmare_%28Bastion_Nymic%29.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Vaermina%27s_Nightmare_%28Bastion_Nymic%29.jpg)](File_ON-creature-Vaermina%27s_Nightmare_(Bastion_Nymic).jpg.md) [](File_ON-creature-Vaermina%27s_Nightmare_(Bastion_Nymic).jpg.md) Vaermina's Nightmare, a being that resides in a different reality layer The **Layers of Reality** are the different sections or layers making up the [planes of existence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) of [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). Overlapping spatially and near indistinct from one another visually, different layers of reality within a given realm can be accessed via the use of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) which shifts people and objects between them. Things shifted to a different reality layer generally can't be perceived or be interacted with by those in a different layer who lack the required magic themselves, thus such magic has been used by groups wishing to avoid detection, strike safely at their foes from a different layer, or conceal objects they do not wish found.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Leramil-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Meln-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Hidden-3)</sup>

The Hidden Kindred, a cult composed of followers of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina) and [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) used such layer shifting magic to conceal their agents from the gaze of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) during their invasion of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) alongside the [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) [Torvesard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torvesard) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#_2E_582), as even the Great Eye could not immediately track their movements through the different layers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Blightcrown-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-New-5)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Leramil-1)</sup> The magic was also used to conceal the cult's Censers of Corruption, relics which spread an arcane plague formed of Peryite's disease and Vaermina's dream essence<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Leramil-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Shelreni-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Instructions-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Calamity-8)</sup> that infected and corroded Apocrypha's fabric, tormented, blinded, and frenzied Mora's servants,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Note-9)</sup> and gradually incapacitated even the Prince himself, threatening to eventually dissolve Apocrypha and sever Mora's link to his domain entirely.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Mora-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mora Unhealthy-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Scruut-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Scruut Unhealthy-13)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Calamity-8)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Unhealthy-14)</sup> In addition to the affliction, the corruption of the Censers was also the source of the Kindred's ability to conceal their agents and objects in different layers, and soon spread both within the realm proper and adjacent dimensions such as the [Infinite Panopticon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Panopticon).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Note-9)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Leramil-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Leramil Calamity-15)</sup> Some [Mind Terrors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mind_Terror) and servants of Vaermina have been known to use the ability to shift layers during combat, while shifted these Daedra appear to the viewer as an indistinct silhouette.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Meln-2)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Nightmare-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Vaermina-17)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Hidden-3)</sup> Mages of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) have also been known to use similar magic in order to conceal the anchors of their arcane wards in a manner that renders them inaccessible to those without the proper magic, thus preventing their wards from being undone.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Meln Conclave-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Conclave-19)</sup> One of the Telvanni, [Shelreni Baro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shelreni_Baro), was responsible for enhancing the effectiveness of the plague mixture's concealing properties.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Shelreni-6)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Calamity-8)</sup> [Ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) Sight, an art contained in one of the [Black Books](Lore_Black_Books.md) of Hermaeus Mora, The Tormenting Eye, allows for one to perceive and interact with things located in different layers, expanding one's senses to see the hidden, the imperceptible and unseen.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Meln Spirit-20)</sup> The ghost of the Telvanni Magister Meln the Mouthless used this Sight to allow the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to locate the minions and Censers of the invading Princes and vanquish them,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Hidden-3)</sup> to destroy the manufactories of corruption which were spreading Peryite's plague within the core of Apocrypha by shattering the dream shards that bound them to the realm, and to slay Blightcrown, and even to help in combat against a manifestation of Vaermina herself,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Vaermina-17)</sup> whose true form was banished through Mora's power after her location became apparent, thus preventing the dissolution of Apocrypha, an event with ripples of [fate](Lore_Fate.md) that would have impacted all of Aurbis.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Mora-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mora Unhealthy-11)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Meln Uhealthy-21)</sup>

Entering a [portal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Portals) created in a location where reality is being manipulated by powerful magic, such as the one used to shift objects and beings to different layers of reality, can have unforeseen effects. Under such circumstances, it is possible for the portal to fracture, depositing those who enter in different locations or reality layers than the intended destination, or even ejecting them to become lost to the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Leramil-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Meln-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Hidden-3)</sup>

## Notes
- The **Land Between** is the place that separates the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) from [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), described as a sea of nothingness. The "monsters" within The Land Between, such as void [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), can interact with the Mundal plane by moving small things with great precision telekinetically. Some places in Mundus where the veil between worlds is thin can act as entrances to the Void.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPEvening Star-22)</sup>
