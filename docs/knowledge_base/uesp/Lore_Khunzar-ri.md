Khunzar-ri (or **Zar** for short), also known as the **Laughing Lion**, the **Luminous Lion of Elsweyr**, the **Lunar Champion**, the **Jonelight Jaunter**, and the **Imprisoner of Dragons**, was a mythical [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) hero and warrior who was active during the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Abnur Tharn-1)</sup> Although he was of the [Pahmar-raht](Lore_Pahmar-raht.md) furstock, some stories elevate him to the status of a god,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSK-2)</sup> or him being [created by one](Lore_Demigod.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOKRO-3)</sup> He is known for his many adventures, some examples include his meeting with the [Skooma Cat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skooma_Cat), and the debaucherous Night of Seven Hundred Paramours.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOKhunzar-ri-4)</sup> He was most well known for capturing the [demons](Lore_Dragon.md) who plagued the land of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) by imprisoning them in the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kat D-5)</sup> Long after his death, he is still worshipped for his heroric deeds within Khajiiti shrines.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOAneshi-6)</sup>

## History

### Merethic Era

#### Tales of Khunzar-ri
Much of Khunzar-ri's adventures are told in stories that often contradict each other and "hide important truths behind allegory and allusion". Zar's origin story for example has three differing stories, and one loremaster claims that the truth is hidden within an intersection between all three stories. The story of *Khunzar-ri and the Divine Moonbeam* speaks of Khunzar-ri being manifested by Jone and Jode out of moonlight and rescuing the future clan mother of the Barrukit kingdom, Shazeer Firstclaw, atop the Shimmering Rise from a pack of hungry [Senche-Lions](Lore_Senche-Cat.md#Lion.2FLioness). The second origin is the *Khunzar-ri and the Box at the Shrine*, telling of an adept finding a box at a shrine to Khenarthi, within containing an infant Khunzar-ri who grew from a baby to an adult upon being taken out of the box. In the third origin, "Khunzar-ri and the Tear of Joy", Sheggorath is responsible for the hero's birth. The Mad Cat is the one who managed to cheer up a grumpy Alkosh (albiet it was a joke at [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s expense) when [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) and [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md) failed to, and from a tear of joy from Alkosh, Khunzar-ri was born.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOKRO-3)</sup>

Other tales exist teaching of Khunzar-ri's guile and ambition. One tale tells of Khunzar-ri outwitting a dozen [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md) who overtook an adeptorium, whom Khunzar-ri directed toward a [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) fort closeby for even better spoils, making the Ogres someone else's problem. <sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOTO-7)</sup> On one of Zar's adventurers, he pick up a starved and lost female [alfiq](Lore_Alfiq.md) whom he would enjoy many travels with.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOKLA-8)</sup> This Alfiq would become his scribe who would dutifully write down pages of anecdotes from Zar's daily life, but she would be faced with animosity by Zar's jealous lover, Anequina Sharp-Tongue. <sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOLCT-9)</sup>

Khunzar-ri actions are responsible for the naming of the ancient adeptorium of Moongrave Fane. Seeking to improve the living conditions of relunctant monks, he made a wager with the temple's elder cleric. Khunzar-ri betted that he could pluck the Moons from the sky and bury them at the cleric's feet, and if he lost, he would join the adeptorium forever. And so Khunzar-ri leaped up to the temple's summit, and there plucked the iron effigy of Jone and Jode, and finally buried them at the feet of the cleric. And with Zar's win, Moongrave accepted his generous donations of wealth.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOLCT-9)</sup>

#### Khunzar-ri and the Demons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/ON-statue-Halls_of_Colossus_Bas_Reliefs.png/200px-ON-statue-Halls_of_Colossus_Bas_Reliefs.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Halls_of_Colossus_Bas_Reliefs.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Halls_of_Colossus_Bas_Reliefs.png) A series of [bas reliefs](Lore_Statues.md) depicting Khunzar-ri's battle with dragons, their imprisonment in the Halls of Colossus, and his eventual betrayal at the hands of Cadwell Sometime in the Merethic Era, when Elsweyr was composed of sixteen kingdoms, Kaalgrontiid and his horde of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) came to dominate the land and above all, consume the [Moon's](Lore_Moons.md) lunar power. Kaalgrontiid is believed by some to originate from Alduin's horde in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), having been driven to split off from the bulk of his kin in the north in order to sate his own pride and ambition. This behavior is considered normal for dragons, who are naturally driven to seek mastery.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LADragons-10)</sup> Kaalgrontiid in particular hoped that with the Moon's power, he would become [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)'s equal.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOKaalgrontiid-11)</sup> To stop them, Khunzar-ri recruited four companions, [Nurarion the Perfect](Lore_Nurarion_the_Perfect.md), [Flinthild Demon-Hunter](Lore_Flinthild_Demon-Hunter.md), [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](Lore_Anequina_Sharp-Tongue.md), and [Cadwell the Betrayer](Lore_Cadwell.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kat D-5)</sup> When fighting them proved to be futile, Cadwell suggested that it may be best to convince the dragons that they were now a willing part of their plans. In preparation, Anequina [attuned](Lore_Shadow_Dance.md) to Shadow Dance Temple, incited the moons to align to form an eclipse, allowing the Moon Gate of Anequina to open a portal the Plane of [Jode](Lore_Jode.md), where the object the dragons desired, [Jode's Core](Lore_Jode%27s_Core.md), rested. The dragon's ambitions would be their undoing, as the dragons accessed the [Plane of Jode](Lore_Plane_of_Jode.md), but Khunzar-ri convinced them to not simply absorb the power of the core like they originally intended, but instead store their energy within it in the guise that they would combine the lunar power with their own.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOCadwell-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOKhamira-13)</sup> The gamble worked, and the dragons were weakened enough for them to retreat back into the wilderness.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kat D-5)</sup> To finish the job, Khunzar-ri once again convinced the dragons to play a part of his scheme. He managed to lure them into the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md), where he sealed them away. The Dragons would be obscured as "demons" in legends, and become the fabled "Demon Weapon". Their power would be coveted by many people.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kat D-5)</sup>

After sealing the dragons away in the Halls of Colossus, Cadwell the Betrayer killed Khunzar-ri in an attempt to take the power sealed away in [Jode's Core](Lore_Jode%27s_Core.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Khunzar-ri was buried within the [Moonlit Cove](Lore_Moonlit_Cove.md).

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/ON-place-Jonelight_Path_06.jpg/200px-ON-place-Jonelight_Path_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jonelight_Path_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jonelight_Path_06.jpg) Khunzar-ri at the Jonelight Path In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Kaalgrontiid returned and was successful in siphoning some of the power from Jode's Core. He then looked unto Pellitine to use the area's [Aeonstones](Lore_Aeonstone.md) to further empower themselves. To prevent anyone from disrupting his plans, he utilized the energy of the Aeonstone to create an aegis, a massive and impenetrable ward, around the ancient fortress of Dragonhold. The reformed [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) sought the help of Khunzar-ri who guided them through the Jonelight Path to access Kaalgrontiid's impenetrable fortress of Dragonhold, allowing them to destroy the Dragon's seat of power and to stop the Rage of Dragons within Elsweyr.

## Kra'Jun
Kra'jun is a [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md) term used to refer to Khunzar-ri's company of heroes, which consisted of the following members:

- [Anequina Sharp-Tongue](Lore_Anequina_Sharp-Tongue.md) — The queen and moon-priest of the Ne Quin-al Pride, she and Khunzar-ri were lovers during their time together. To trap the dragons, Anequina attuned herself to the moon and tricked the dragons by baiting them with lunar energy. She later led the Anequina region in the First War of the Prides.
- [Nurarion the Perfect](Lore_Nurarion.md) — An [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) who wielded the Subtle Blade. He suggested the usage of the Halls of Colossus as a prison.
- [Flinthild Demon-Hunter](Lore_Flinthild_Demon-Hunter.md) — A warrior who lived in a castle from the [far north](Lore_Skyrim.md). She and Anequina devised the plan to lure the dragons, and used the urn of moonlight to lure the dragons.
- [The Betrayer](Lore_Cadwell.md) — A [Nede](Lore_Nede.md) who was the finest warrior in all the land. He suggested to fool the dragons into believing that they had surrendered to their plans to consume the moons' lunar energy. When the dragons were imprisoned in the Halls, he murdered Khunzar-ri, seeking to acquire the power for himself. For his betrayal, his name was erased from history and his remains were spread across the land.
