# Kingdoms of High Rock


The [Isle of Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Isle_of_Balfiera) is a neutral ground where the Kingdoms of High Rock meet to negotiate and sign treaties.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup> Royal dynasties, and other distinct nobles and heroes from all over High Rock are buried in [Cath Bedraud](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cath_Bedraud) in northern [Glenumbra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenumbra).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTHR-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-COCB-5)</sup>

## History

### First Era
King Berthulic ruled one of the first free Breton kingdoms in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) after the Direnni Hegemony's decline<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONLoad Lorkrata-6)</sup> in [1E 500](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_500).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1-3)</sup> He built a stronghold in central [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire) called the Lorkrata Hills. It now lies in ruins and is said to be haunted by prisoners from the Purge of the [Wyrd-Hags](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wyrd_Covens), an event during [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONLoad Lorkrata-6)</sup> in [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TNM-7)</sup>

From [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029) to the mid-late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), High Rock was a part of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), who at the time of their admission was ruled by Empress [Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSOHR-8)</sup> In that timeframe, Wayrest was named a kingdom in [1E 1100](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1100) with [Farangel Gardner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Farangel) as its first king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WJOTB-1)</sup> However, throughout the years under their control, the Bretons faced discrimination for their elvish heritage, and heavy taxation and regulation from the [Alessians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order). Combined with their inability to control the distant province, the kingdoms of High Rock left the Empire by the twenty-fourth century.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OTWOR-9)</sup>

### Second Era
The Kingdom of Camlorn was founded in [2E 301](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_301) and ever since then, its people celebrate with a great festival every year.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MOT1-10)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/LO-map-High_Rock_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-High_Rock_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-High_Rock_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-High_Rock_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Map of High Rock ca. 3E 433 When Emperor [Uriel IV](Lore_Uriel_IV.md)'s son, [Andorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Andorak) was disinherited from the [Ruby Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne) in [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247), he and his loyalists went to war with his cousin, Emperor [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md). After nine years, the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) granted him the throne of Shornhelm, where his line has ruled since at least the late Third Era.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-11)</sup> At the time, the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) took over [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and led a large-scale invasion north through Tamriel. The province was at a disadvantage when his forces invaded the Iliac Bay in early [3E 266](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_266). Daggerfall's throne was heavily contented by its royal family and Wayrest's monarch was in their minority. With the Bretons' hatred for the Emperor creating sympathy for the Usurper, no leaders could unite the province. It took Baron [Othrok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Othrok) of [Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen) and his neighboring lords to eventually defeat him in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TFOTU-12)</sup>

In the late fourth century, there were eight Breton kingdoms in High Rock: Camlorn, Daggerfall, Evermore, Farrun, Jehanna, Northpoint, Shornhelm, and Wayrest.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EWR-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AR-13)</sup> But ensuing events at the time changed its borders and control across the province. During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), High Rock was invaded by [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md). Ultimately, Skyrim took large tracts of land, and while resistance in Jehanna was feeble at best, it was more active in the border.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3SR-14)</sup> The [Crypts of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md), typically within Shornhelm's boundaries,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-COTHD-15)</sup> marked the westernmost end of Skyrim's border.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SKMap-16)</sup> By the end of the Third Era, so little territory had changed hands.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3SR-14)</sup> Wayrest was also involved in the war at an unknown capacity and reportedly lost.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-OBLathon-17)</sup>

In [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), the Orcish chieftain of the [Minat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minat) tribe, [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gortwog_gro-Nagorm) acquired a [piece of land](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orsinium_Area) between Wayrest and [Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md) and built the third iteration of Orsinium, aptly known as [Nova Orsinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nova_Orsinium).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-HOPTTO-18)</sup> Daggerfall in [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403) fought Sentinel in the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md). In the end, King [Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus) of Daggerfall passed away and his son, [Gothryd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gothryd) inherited his throne. As a means to consolidate peace, Gothryd married the late [King of Sentinel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camaron)'s daughter, [Aubk-i](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aubk-i) to varying success.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DFNarrative-19)</sup>

In [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) greatly shifted power across High Rock, turning its many countries into five Breton kingdoms and Orsinium acknowledged by the Empire.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TWITW-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3-21)</sup> The Iliac Bay and its smaller states were absorbed by its four major kingdoms (Daggerfall, Sentinel, Orsinium, and Wayrest) and now bordered each other.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TWITW-20)</sup> Evermore and Northpoint were not directly affected by Warp in the West but gained new territory amid the chaos; far north to avoid Daggerfall and Wayrest, and far west to avoid Skyrim in the post Bend'r-mahk. After these previous wars, the kingdoms of High Rock favored diplomatic solutions over more conflict, though local skirmishes plagued its borders.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3-21)</sup>

By [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), Camlorn and Daggerfall celebrated the marriage of Prince [Camaron](Lore_Camaron_(prince).md), son of King Gothryd, and Lady Kelmena, daughter of Duke Senhyn of Camlorn, possibly hinting at an alliance between kingdoms. Northpoint and Evermore had remained silent, observing their neighbors with distrust. Queen [Elysana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elysana) of Wayrest, considered the most feared ruler in the west, allied with Orsinium, leaving many to wonder about their next move.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3-21)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Sometime in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Nova Orsinium was sacked by Bretons and Redguards. Many of its survivors were safely escorted to the Hammerfell-Skyrim border by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), where they built the fourth Orsinium.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LOS-22)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Chapter 6</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SRLoad-23)</sup>

## Known Kingdoms

### Camlorn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/ON-place-Camlorn.jpg/200px-ON-place-Camlorn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Camlorn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Camlorn.jpg) Camlorn [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) (historically called **Camlarn**) is one of the nine historical Breton kingdoms in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), situated on the coastal highlands of [Glenumbra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenumbra). Camlorn and the surrounding region is a melting pot of several different [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) cultures and its people fancy themselves as more refined and metropolitan compared to their neighbors in [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall). The kingdom's nobility consider themselves an aristocracy of poets and artisans. It has also greatly benefitted from trade coming from either the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) or the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay). As of [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), Camlorn was one of five major kingdoms in High Rock.

### Daggerfall
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/ON-place-Daggerfall_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Daggerfall_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Daggerfall_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Daggerfall_02.jpg) Daggerfall [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/DF-map-Daggerfall.png/200px-DF-map-Daggerfall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Daggerfall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Daggerfall.png) The Kingdom of Daggerfall [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) is one of the nine historical Breton kingdoms in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located in the southwest portion of the province, on the north half of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay). Throughout much of history, Daggerfall was one of the largest kingdoms in High Rock, from the early First Era before the rise of its rival, [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) and its incorporation into the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), to the turn of the Third Era, where it was one of the province's five kingdoms. It is named after its capital city, [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall).

### Evermore
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-place-Evermore.jpg/200px-ON-place-Evermore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Evermore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Evermore.jpg) Evermore [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), sometimes spelled **Evermor**, is a city on the banks of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) in eastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located near the border with [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).

Its impressive palace was assembled from slabs of [granite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Granite) quarried from nearby [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). Local traditions include the worship of [Saint Pelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saint_Pelin), a hero of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) who once served as a clergyman at [Bangkorai Garrison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Garrison).

Historically, the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of Evermore ruled over the region of [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai). These lands were subject to frequent attacks from the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) of the mountains, who asserted their birthright to the city and aimed to reclaim their ancestral homeland. Southern Bangkorai included the [Fallen Wastes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fallen_Wastes) region of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), leading Evermore's rulership to often be contested by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). Evermore's position along trade routes spanning the Tamriel allowed it to become an important trading hub. Locally, it linked eastern High Rock, the southern and central regions of Hammerfell, [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), and western [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), while more distant trade routes to the southeast connected it with [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

### Farrun
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/AR-place-Farrun.jpg/200px-AR-place-Farrun.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Farrun.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Farrun.jpg) Farrun [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md) (or **Fharun**) is a northern port city on the north shore of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), and one of eight kingdoms in the province. It is known for its icy winters. The kingdom of Farrun has existed since the First Era; one notable citizen of Farrun in the First Era was Lord Storig, who commanded the vanguard of the [Imperial army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) at the Battle of Bodrum in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920) during the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md). By the Second Era it was an Orcish stronghold by the name of Fharun, named after the [Fharun Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fharun_Clan). The stronghold was infamous for its [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) -designed prison complex, which was notorious even among the Orcs.

### Jehanna
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/AR-place-Jehanna.jpg/200px-AR-place-Jehanna.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Jehanna.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Jehanna.jpg) Jehanna [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md) (also called **Jehenna** or **Jelhana**) is one of the nine historical Breton kingdoms in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), situated on the icy shores of the [Northern Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northern_Coast). Nestled in the snow-covered forests of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md), it is described as being as beautiful as its name.

### Northpoint
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a0/ON-place-Northpoint.jpg/200px-ON-place-Northpoint.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Northpoint.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Northpoint.jpg) Northpoint [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) (also spelled as **North Point**) is one of the nine historical Breton kingdoms in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located on the northeast point of [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire). Northpoint is an important trading center along the northwest trading route on the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md), as well as the seat of power for [House Dorell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dorell), one of the three major houses of Rivenspire. Their power extends across northern Rivenspire, including farmland around the city and beyond the sea. As of [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), Northpoint was one of five major kingdoms in High Rock.

### Orsinium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/LO-map-Orsinium_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Orsinium_%28Oblivion_Codex%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Orsinium_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Orsinium_(Oblivion_Codex).jpg.md) Orsinium [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) (which literally translates to "[Orc](Lore_Orc.md) -Town" in [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmer.md), also called the **City of Orcs**) is the city of the Orsimer and the provincial seat of the region of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md), which came to be known as the [Orsinium Area](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orsinium_Area) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). It has been sacked and rebuilt many times over the centuries, as the Orcs are often at odds with their [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) neighbors. Orsinium was first built in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), straddling the boundary between western Wrothgar and [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire). In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the city was relocated to eastern Wrothgar. In [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), it was relocated again to a site between [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), [Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md), and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains). By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Orsinium had been moved a third time, farther south to an area between [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The royal palace of the city is traditionally known as Scarp Keep.

The exact date of the first Orsinium's founding is unknown, but it is known to have grown powerful as early as the ninth century of the First Era.

### Shornhelm
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/ON-place-Shornhelm.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shornhelm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shornhelm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shornhelm.jpg) Shornhelm [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) (sometimes spelled **Sharnhelm**) is one of the nine historical Breton kingdoms in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), located in the center of [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire). Shornhelm was once the seat of power for the Kingdom of Rivenspire, the north-central region of High Rock and it was aptly ruled by the Riven-King. If no monarch is present, then the regency council, the Council of the North takes over. It is a triumvirate of the kingdom's three major houses, [Dorell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dorell), [Montclair](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Montclair), and [Tamrith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Tamrith). The city acquired the epithet of the "Crown City of the North" for its status.

### Wayrest
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/ON-place-Wayrest_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Wayrest_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wayrest_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wayrest_02.jpg) Wayrest [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/DF-map-Wayrest.png/200px-DF-map-Wayrest.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Wayrest.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Wayrest.png) The Kingdom of Wayrest [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) is one of the nine historical Breton kingdoms in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It is located in the center of the province, at the mouth of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md). Wayrest's fortune and immense wealth come from a unique, symbiotic alliance between its merchant lords and the crown dating back to its founding in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Even its kings, except for the Septim Dynasty of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), can trace their lineage back to a merchant prince. By the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the kingdom had the largest and richest population of High Rock. It is named after its capital city, [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md).
