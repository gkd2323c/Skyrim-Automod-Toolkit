# Barbarians

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6d/OB-class-Barbarian.jpg/200px-OB-class-Barbarian.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-class-Barbarian.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-class-Barbarian.jpg) Illustration of a barbarian (Oblivion) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/MW-class-Barbarian.jpg/200px-MW-class-Barbarian.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-class-Barbarian.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-class-Barbarian.jpg) Typical barbarian equipment (Morrowind) | - *"Barbarians are the proud, savage warrior elite of the plains nomads, mountain tribes, and sea reavers. They tend to be brutal and direct, lacking civilized graces, but they glory in heroic feats, and excel in fierce, frenzied single combat."* —Barbarian class description |
| --- |

Barbarians are the warrior subculture of society that lives out in the wilderness, i.e., plain nomads, mountain tribes, and sea [reavers](Lore_Reaver.md). Despite their blunt and aggressive attitudes, barbarians are battle-hardy and relish in finding glory.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDesc-1)</sup> They often hunt to sustain themselves and use their knowledge on wild animals, hunting, combat, and arms to sell their services to city-folk.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWTrade-2)</sup>

Barbarians focus on speed and strength, which may be why they avoid heavy armor and prefer light or even medium armor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWBarbarian-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBBarbarian-4)</sup> They can also be medical experts as they have honed their ability to survive in the wilderness. They are also capable athletes, swimmers, climbers, and effective dodgers. They may also be adept in the [Giantish](Lore_Giantish.md) language.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFBarbarian-5)</sup> The term is also used derogatorily when referring to someone brutish,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ABAYQ-6)</sup> uncivilized, or even unintelligent.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CM55-7)</sup>

No other race is more synonymous with the derogatory term of "barbarian" as the [Nords](Lore_Nords.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-JTRG-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LADITSE-9)</sup> Although some would say that the Nords have come a long way from their original barbarian roots,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-POT-10)</sup> the other races of Tamriel still hold the same stigma. The Jarls of Skyrim have also garnered a reputation for being barbarians or uncivilized leaders. But to the surprise of Abdul-Mujib Ababneh, writer of *[Skyrim's Rule](Lore_Skyrim%27s_Rule.md)*, each Jarls' court has its own formal structure.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SR-11)</sup> Nordic barbarians prefer to wear fur armor.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWGarothmuk-12)</sup>

Equally as much so, the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) also have the reputation of being barbarians.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ALBAB-13)</sup> [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) and Nordic scholars have always denounced them as nothing more. But the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) Imperial scholar, Arrianus Arius believes these accounts were "victor's essays", skewed perspectives based on the ancient rivalry between [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) and these races.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TMOTR-14)</sup> For a time, this perception slowly died out in the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), when the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) reigned over the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and a relative, [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of the [Blackdrake Clan](Lore_Blackdrake_Clan.md) was the Ard of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). Caddach, with his experience and education from the south, hoped to turn Markarth into a functional state and to dismiss the idea that the Reachfolk are not barbarians or unintelligent brutes.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-STOTDF3-16)</sup> This endeavor failed in the long run as [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) continues to look down on the Reachfolk as less than. The [Battle of Old Hroldan](Lore_Battle_of_Old_Hroldan.md) is remembered as [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s victory over the "barbarian natives".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TMOTR-14)</sup>

The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) have been characterized by their neighbors in [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) as militaristic barbarians.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AAPAI-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3EW-18)</sup> While the southern Khajiit of Pellitine have adopted [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and Imperial customs to re-model their society, the northern Khajiit of Anequina have always kept to their tribal, warrior traditions. They train warriors to hone their martial discipline and battle tactics.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AAPAI-17)</sup>

Before their annexation into the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), many viewed the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) as a land of barbarians and cutthroats.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE1HF-19)</sup> And while Tamriel's perception of Redguards has changed for the better, the province's wilderness hosts all sorts of barbarians. Across the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), which encompasses Hammerfell's north coast and most of western and southern High Rock, are barbarian strongholds.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-DFDungeons-20)</sup> The [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) have long been considered barbarians since before written history<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TPC-21)</sup> but while some use the term negatively, some people, including Orcs themselves spin it positively to reference to their fighting prowess and capacity as brave and hardy warriors.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-MWGen Orc-22)</sup>

Other races look down on the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) as barbarians that believe in primitive superstitions, partake in shamanistic spirit worship, and live in unheated communal huts.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MWGen Bosmer-23)</sup> The Wood Elves, especially those that live in the wilderness, worship the natural world ([the Green](Lore_The_Green.md)) as a living and powerful entity.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-GPBO-24)</sup> They live by the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), a code of conduct that defines the relationship with their people and [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), the elven god of the forest.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TGPATD-25)</sup> Because of the Green Pact, the Wood Elves have never built their settlements, instead crafting them from the trees via the [Greenspeakers](Lore_Greenspeakers.md)' magic and eat mainly meat, whether it is animals or fallen enemies. The [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) is a phenomenon in which the Bosmer become feral shapeshifting monsters when needed and rampage across the province in a frenzy.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-26)</sup>

Early [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), back when they were originally [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) lived in a barbarian society, as referred by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LOTS-27)</sup> The Prophet, [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) the Pilgrim yearned for a "pure way of life" for his followers, one marked with self-discipline and worship of the [Good Daedra](Lore_Good_Daedra.md). This period (mid-late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md)) under his guidance was known as the Golden Age, which shaped future generations.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-VTP-28)</sup> This period declined by the late Merethic Era as the people started to settle and build tribal cultures that became the modern-day [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) and [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) tribes.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BTAOM-29)</sup>

As the [Imperial Geographic Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographic_Society.md) explains it, early descriptions of the Dunmer people say they were divided in numerous, petty clans and tribes, half of which were at war with each other at any moment. Nordic sagas also explain that Chimer warriors would sometimes pledge themselves to Nordic chieftains if they happened to have a common enemy.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-PGE1MW-30)</sup> [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) was able to unite these barbarian tribes to fight against the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) in the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md),<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LOTS-27)</sup> and found further assistance with the nomads that became the present-day Ashlander tribes.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-31)</sup> For many years later, the Ashlander tribes were still viewed as barbarians<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BTAOM-29)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CM48-32)</sup> but with the fall of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), came the Ashlanders' vindication as many people flocked to the wastes for a Wise Woman's counsel.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TR-33)</sup>

The [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) look down on the [Kamali](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) as barbarians, especially for their loss in the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-CM53-34)</sup> Similarily, the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) looked down on the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), the elite order of warriors descended from the Tsaesci that fought [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) in the late First Era, as a cabal of "bloodthirsty" Akaviri barbarians.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SRArngeir-35)</sup> The [Riekr](Lore_Riekr.md) of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) are considered barbarians.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-NJW-36)</sup>

## Known Barbarians

- [Alfhedil Elf-Hewer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alfhedil_Elf-Hewer): A Nordic barbarian and a master Axe wielder from the late Third Era
- [Igmund](Lore_Igmund.md): The Jarl of Markarth in the Fourth Era
- [Khargol gro-Boguk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khargol_gro-Boguk): An Orc barbarian and a master of the unarmored fighting style
- Tharsten Heart-Fang: The Nordic chieftain of the Skaal, who participated in the Great Hunt on Solstheim
- [Ulfgar the Unending](Lore_Ulfgar_the_Unending.md): An Atmoran barbarian who previously fought as one of the Five Hundred Companions

### Barbarian tribes

- Berserkers: The "bare-sarks" or bare-chested, these Nords of Solstheim have been driven mad by the cold and too much mead
- [Locvar](Lore_Locvar.md): An ancient Altmer tribe that raided Tyrigel, the ancient Direnni home
