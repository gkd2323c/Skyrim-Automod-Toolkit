Tharrikers (meaning **Forest Raiders** or **Forest Hunters** in [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md#Language))<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-BDN-1)</sup> are large, unnatural, and highly adaptable [senche](Lore_Senche.md) -like carnivores with four ears, feathery front legs, and [scorpion](Lore_Scorpion.md) -like tails.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TH-2)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Liet-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Command-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-mount2-5)</sup> They were created by the [Recollection](Lore_Recollection.md)'s use of corrupted [Greenspeaker](Lore_Greenspeaking.md) [magic](Lore_Magic.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-mount2-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-BDN-1)</sup> and sired in their "grow pits".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-mount-6)</sup> Despite their origins they are capable of [reproduction](Lore_Sex.md) by laying eggs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Command-4)</sup>

Tharrikers were first unleashed in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), appearing around the same time the Dawnwood forest did.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-mount-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Command-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Liet-3)</sup> They were often used to aid members of the Recollection in combat, with many also being affected by corruption from the [Wildburn](Lore_Wildburn.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TH-2)</sup> Tharrikers would quickly spread to other parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), some making it to the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md). The wild magic that created them allowed for rapid adaptation to the snowy slopes of their new home, as such as a white-furred subspecies arose in this region.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-mount2-5)</sup>

A few were rescued from the Recollection's grow pits by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). These rescued tharrikers were then trained to be used as mounts and kept in [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md)'s stables.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-mount-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) of the [Rock Bone Tribe](Lore_Rock_Bone_Tribe.md) were also capable of training tharrikers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Liet-3)</sup> The white-furred subspecies of the Jerall Mountains would be domesticated by the war deserters that hid in the region.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-mount2-5)</sup>
