[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/SR-place-Hjaalmarch.jpg/200px-SR-place-Hjaalmarch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Hjaalmarch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Hjaalmarch.jpg) Drajkmyr Marsh ca. [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) The Drajkmyr or **Black Morass**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BM-1)</sup> is an immense saltwater marsh, formed by the estuaries of the [River Karth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:River_Karth) and the [River Hjaal](Lore_River_Hjaal.md), which dominates [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md) in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Largely uninhabited, it is cloaked in an eerie mist.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>:436</sup> The Drajkmyr encompasses much of central Hjaalmarch, bordered by the lumber town of [Morthal](Lore_Morthal.md) to the south,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>:439</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GTWSH-3)</sup> the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md) and [Fort Snowhawk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Snowhawk) to the west, both the [Great Arch](Lore_Great_Arch.md) and the Mouths of the Karth to the north, and finally, the snowy woods that lead towards [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) to the east. The swamp was formed some time in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and the region was originally covered by a broad glacier beforehand.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OS-4)</sup>

## Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-place-Kjenstag_Ruins.jpg/200px-SR-place-Kjenstag_Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Kjenstag_Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Kjenstag_Ruins.jpg) Kjenstag [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/SR-creature-The_Pale_Lady.jpg/200px-SR-creature-The_Pale_Lady.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-The_Pale_Lady.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-The_Pale_Lady.jpg) The Pale Lady It is largely uninhabited; only small encampments and isolated shelters are found across the region. Some ruins in the area have sunken into the terrain while others remain untouched on its frontier. The [Apprentice Stone](Lore_Standing_Stones.md) can be found on a small islet directly north of Snowhawk. There are several landmarks across the Drajkmyr Marsh, such as [Folgunthur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Folgunthur), Kjenstag, and [Ustengrav](Lore_Ustengrav.md). The [River Hjaal](Lore_River_Hjaal.md) empties into the region and Morthal from the southwest.

Out in the wilderness, there are all sorts of creatures that stalk the marsh, ranging from the common [mudcrab](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mudcrab) or [dragonfly](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonfly), but also large predators like the frostbite spider and even the [chaurus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chaurus). There is a myriad of flora that lives in these swamps, one of the most well-known is the [Deathbell](Lore_Deathbell.md), which is the source of several local folktales, such as that it grows in places where an unfortunate death may occur, or that it lures people and animals to certain death.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HGt S-5)</sup> Other types of plants include the [Canis Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Canis_Root), the [Spiky Grass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spiky_Grass), the [Swamp Fungal Pod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Fungal_Pod) and even the [Nirnroot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirnroot).

There is a recurring theme of children being lost in the marshland. The Fen Witches, a notorious coven of the [Glenmoril Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd) and worshippers of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) have been known to abduct children, but that is the least of their wrongdoings.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TGW-6)</sup> There is also a legend told in Morthal about the Pale Lady, a ghostly lady that wanders northern Drajkmyr to find her lost daughter and some say that she takes children that stray too far from home.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LL-7)</sup>
