*You think yourselves Dragon slayers. You'll soon learn that not all Dragons are created equal.*
—Nahviintaas Nahviintaas, was a powerful golden [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) who emerged in [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), following the release of the great dragon [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md) and his dragon followers from the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTHOC-1)</sup>. He lead two other dragons, [Lokkestiiz](Lore_Lokkestiiz.md) and [Yolnahkriin](Lore_Yolnahkriin.md), to pose as the returned [gods](Lore_Gods.md) of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) and the [moons](Lore_Moons.md) [Jone](Lore_Secunda.md) and [Jode](Lore_Masser.md), and take over the temple of [Sunspire](Lore_Sunspire.md), where he attempted to expand its [Time Wound](Lore_Time.md) with his [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) in the hopes of causing a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) to rewrite time and restore draconic rule.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a0/ON-creature-Nahviintaas_%28Time_Wound%29.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nahviintaas_%28Time_Wound%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nahviintaas_(Time_Wound).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nahviintaas_(Time_Wound).jpg) The Sunspire Time Wound [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg/200px-ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg) Nahviintaas confonting adventurers atop Sunspire In 2E 582, ancient Dragons had appeared in Northern [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), reaping the land, following the release of the great dragon Kaalgrontiid and his dragon followers from the Halls of Colossus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOTHOC-1)</sup> The golden dragon, Nahviintaas approached Sunspire with his contemporaries, Lokkestiiz and Yolnahkriin. They posed as Alkosh, Jone, and Jode respectively. Azin-jo, the Moon-Bishop in charge of Sunspire, openly accepted them and preached across Anequina about the return of "Alkosh." A wave of new followers came and rebuilt Sunspire as more adepts preached the return of "Alkosh" across Northern Elsweyr.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-2)</sup>

However, Nahviintaas' true goal was to use his [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to tear the Time Wound wider in order to cause a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) which he intended to use to "reclaim all that is and will be" and restore the "natural order" by "correcting the mortal mistake".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-4)</sup> While posing as gods the dragons would use their Voice to imbue their deceived followers with [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) power, transforming them into creatures composed simultaneously of ice and flame while also making them thralls of the dragon's will.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOMojha-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOJone Jode-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOIce Fire-8)</sup> The dragon's presence would also have an effect on the environment and conditions of the mountain, the dragon Yolnahkriin resided in the caldera, where his presence is said to have "stoked the sleeping mountain's fire", while the dragon Lokkestiiz resided in a windy frost covered overlook.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOMojha-6)</sup>

This news brought the attention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), who were sent to investigate. The Undaunted defeated the fake Jone and Jode before they confronted Nahviintaas at the summit and the time-wound. Though Nahviintaas would utilize the power of the Time Wound to manipulate time, claiming his faithful had no need to fear death as he could restore them and they'd serve him eternally, and that he could make his foes relive their deaths over and over, the fake Alkosh was ultimately killed and Sunspire was liberated from his control.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESONahviintaas-9)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-2)</sup> His head was severed and taken as a trophy by the Undaunted.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> With the years he had left, Moon-Bishop Azin-jo vowed to restore this temple and because of his folly, he felt that it was his task alone to bear.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-4)</sup> But regardless, some priests stayed in Sunspire.

As his soul was not devoured by another dovah, Nahviintaas was not killed permanently and can be raised again.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LAD-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Arngeir-12)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg/200px-ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilled_Sand_06.jpg) The slumbering aspect of Alkosh which Nahviintaas greatly resembles. - Nahviintaas bears a striking resemblance to the slumbering aspect of the god Alkosh encountered by [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) within the [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of the [Spilled Sand](Lore_Spilled_Sand.md). Both dragons are near identical in form and have gold colored scales. However the scales and even horns and teeth of Alkosh's aspect are pure gold throughout, whereas Nahvintaas' underbelly, teeth and horns are a different color, in addition to the titanic aspect being far larger in size and having a tail that seems to extend endlessly past its body and across the dunes.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Dragonguard-13)</sup>
- Nahviintaas's name means **Fury-Shining** in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Shout-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Prima-15)</sup>
