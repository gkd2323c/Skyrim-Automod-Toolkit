# Woad


## Culture

### Altmer
While the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) are not known to use woad, [Sil-Var-Woad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sil-Var-Woad) is a notable landmark on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SVW-8)</sup>

### Argonian
The Argonian tribe of the [Bright-Throat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bright-Throat_Tribe) was known for their cheerful use of colorful blue woad dye in their body decorations.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BTWBT-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BTWFT-9)</sup>

### Bosmer
*I met a Bosmer on a road
A vicious freak in mud and woad*
— [Skald Skullsplitter](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Skald_Skullsplitter)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TBOSS-7)</sup> *[The Ballad of Skald Skullsplitter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Ballad_of_Skald_Skullsplitter)*, a boastful drinking song, briefly mentions a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) adorned with woad.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TBOSS-7)</sup>

### Khajiit
To avoid being mistaken as Bosmer, many [Ohmes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ohmes) tattoo or [paint](Lore_Cosmetics.md) their faces to resemble a feline aspect.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RDKA-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Survival G-11)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-IWTB-12)</sup> Some Ohmes were described to wear brown woad tattoos.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Loranna-13)</sup>

### Nord
Nords were said to favor their woad,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Posts-5)</sup> which they could apply to strengthen their defenses.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Woad MW-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Woad OB-4)</sup> Certain texts suggest that the practice of Woad was already in use during the era of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s original [Five Hundered Companions](Lore_Companions.md). According to skald tales, Ysgramor slew so many [frost giants](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Giant) that he was covered in their blood.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ysgramor Body-14)</sup> It is believed that some of these markings contained hidden meanings, like spiral symbols of death.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Ysgramor Face-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-500C-2)</sup>

### Reachfolk
*Woad war paint, deep ritualistic scars, and primitive tattoo strike fear into those facing down the Reachmen berserker in combat.*
— [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGT-1)</sup> Berserkers among the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) used woad war paint, scarification, and tattoos as part of their cultural practices. One of its purposes was to strike fear into their opponents.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGT-1)</sup>
