# First Empire of the Nords

| --- |

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg/200px-SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) The Palace of the Kings built in the Empire's architectural style [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/LO-map-Skyrim_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Skyrim_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(Anthology).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-Skyrim_(Anthology).jpg.md) [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), the province in which the First Nordic Empire was based The First Empire of the Nords, also known as the **Nord Empire**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> is the collective term for the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) kingdom during a phase of expansion in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), known as the Skyrim Conquests.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Its capital was the city of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where a First Empire palace still stands.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup> The empire was formed when [King Vrage the Gifted](Lore_Vrage.md) began a campaign to conquer [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) (a feat that is attributed by some to [Sai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai), the God of Luck<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KE10-4)</sup>) circa [1E 240](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_240).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KE10-4)</sup> By the end of the conquests, the First Empire of the Nords encompassed Skyrim, parts or all of High Rock (sources conflict),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3High-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AHOD-7)</sup> Morrowind with the exception of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) kingdom of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1M-8)</sup> and a large portion of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup> These invasions upset the balance in High Rock and Cyrodiil, and although the Nords' empire lasted less than two centuries, the result was decisive; from then on, power in those regions shifted from the elves to the humans.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FC-9)</sup>

Though the last ruler, King Borgas, died in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> the ultimate end of the First Nordic Empire came in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), when the Pact of Chieftains ended the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) and the empire finally shattered; Skyrim eventually lost all of its holdings in Morrowind,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NARM-13)</sup> Cyrodiil, and High Rock,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3High-6)</sup> and many of the Nordic kingdoms began to declare independence.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The Nords were driven from Morrowind by force when [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) and [Dumac Dwarfking](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dumac_Dwarfking) joined together, repelling the invaders in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NARM-13)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1M-8)</sup>

## Notes
- First Empire of the Nords as a name is used as a retroactive nomenclature, as Nords technically didn't consider themselves Nords until around the time the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) [Cirroc the Lofty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cirroc) became Harbinger.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-GH-15)</sup> - According to [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride), the heirs of King Harald "never regarded themselves as anything more than a strong and steady line of successful war-chieftains".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-16)</sup>
