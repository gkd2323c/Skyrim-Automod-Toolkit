# Astronomy

— [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-US-1)</sup> **Astronomy** is the study of the Firmament, the sky above [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).

## Celestial Objects

### Sun
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/SR-weather-Sun.jpg/200px-SR-weather-Sun.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-weather-Sun.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-weather-Sun.jpg) The sun The [sun](Lore_Sun.md) (also known as **Magnus**, **Daibethe** in [Yoku](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yoku), **Shul** in the [Dragon language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md), and **Vakka** in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md)) is the common term for the largest tear in reality between the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius). It appears as a bright orb in the skies of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and is the main source of light and [magicka](Lore_Magic.md) in the world, providing a constant flow from Aetherius.

### Meteors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Baar_Dau_02.jpg) Baar Dau, a meteor [Meteors](Lore_Meteors.md), often called **shooting stars**, are fragments of celestial matter that fall from the sky. This occurs when a piece of [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) —the realm of spirits and the source of magic—becomes detached and descends to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). They contain fragments, known as [Aetherial Fragments](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherial_Fragments) renowned for their magical properties.

### Moons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/27/Mfull_Sfull.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) Masser and Secunda [Moons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons) are astronomical objects within [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). They are said to be small [planets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planets), though this comparison is only defined by mortal comprehension since, as with all astral bodies seen from Nirn, both the moons and dominion planets, they are truly infinite in size and mass, separate [planes of existence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) that are only perceived as spheres due to mortal mental stress. According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md). Members of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) described them as islands in the sky.

[Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is generally thought to have two moons: [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md), also known as Jode and Jone respectively. Masser is the larger of the two, being well over twice Secunda's size. According to the Khajiit, there is a [third moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Moon), which they consider the corpse of [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj). The [Jonelight Path](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jonelight_Path), a realm described as the place between places where the "liminal forces that bind each here to every there" can be accessed and from where any destination can be reached by those who can walk the path once the point where the "liminal of here touches the liminal of there" is reached , is named for the smaller of Nirn's two moons and is described as full of [stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars) and moonlight

Due to the two moons, [solar](Lore_Sun.md) [eclipses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eclipse) can happen in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) several times a year and are known as [Vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) Days. During eclipses [spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost), [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and other [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead), are said to be encountered more frequently. The phenomenon is sometimes thought to represent the loss of the favor of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and to be possible to undo with diligent prayer to that deity. Tamrielic kings have also been known to sometimes use [time altering](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Time_Magic) magical items such as Arcane Hourglasses to fast forward time and undo the phenomenon. Inhabitants of Tamriel can sometimes receive warning of an impending eclipse in the form of [prophetic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) nightmares revolving around it and the subsequent surge of spirits and undead. Those born during a rare eclipse of Tamriel's moons are unusually gifted in the magical arts. There are some stories about [Daedric armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment) that say that it should always be worked on at night, and never during an eclipse.

The [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid) once sought to absorb the energies of [Jode's Core](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode%27s_Core), the divine core of one of the moons, in the hopes of ascending to the sky as a New Moon and [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) who would be the equal of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)

### Planets
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png/200px-LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.png) Great orrery of Firsthold showing planets [Planets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planets) are astronomical bodies. [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) high priests use jeweled rotating and counter-rotating orreries to determine which Aedric planet to worship. Number Eight is important in their culture as it is considered the Number of the Planets. They named planets after Elven Aedra, but refer to the same celestial objects as Mannish cultures.

The other astral bodies in the sky above Nirn, the moons and dominion planets, are believed to actually be the planes of the gods as well as the gods themselves, as seen from the mortal plane. They are said to actually be different planes of existence in their own right, infinite in size and mass, with their appearance as spheres being only a visual phenomena caused by mortal mental stress.

The largest shooting stars are actually planets with independent orbits. One of the most well-known is the Rogue Plane; [Baan Dar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Five_Finger_Dance).

According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md). Though scholars largely agree no mortal beings live on planets such as Akatosh or [Arkay](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md), [Azandar al-Cybiades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azandar_al-Cybiades) theorized that the power [Ayleid Wells](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Wells) reroute back towards the heavens was being collected by someone.

### Stars
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/RG-constellation-Empty.jpg/200px-RG-constellation-Empty.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Empty.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Empty.jpg) Stars from the [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) observatory [Stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars) are astronomical objects believed by many to be tears in the veil of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that lead to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius). There are a great many stars in the sky, some of which form the [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md).

[Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) myths claim that stars were created by the [Magna Ge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge), [Anuic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) spirits that fled Creation after the departure of its architect, the god [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus), who made the world. Magnus himself left the greatest tear in the firmament: the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md), sometimes named Magnus. The stars are therefore sometimes referred to as the "children of Magnus". According to one [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) myth and the creation tale of the Anuad, the stars were instead created from the blood of Anu. Some believe that stars are "fragments" of Magnus.

Within [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) belief [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre) is believed to have created day, night, and the places between through the manner in which he interpreted the laws of time established by [Anui-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anui-El) and applied them to Nirn, he is also believed to be responsible for the movements of the stars, as to this day the realms they're windows to sway and wink to his song. [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith also acknowledges Y'ffre in this capacity of being the deity responsible for the movements of the stars, which still sway to the beautiful song he sang when he walked [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) before the first day.

The ancient [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) believed the most sublime form of light was star light. They left behind many artifacts made from meteoric iron or meteoric glass, which are Aetherial fragments or commonly known as shooting stars.

[Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) knew the power of the stars, building orreries and observatories to watch the constellations and who they protected, they had a particular interest in the [Serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent) constellation. Dwemer runes were also used to represent constellations.

Some stars are named after or associated with [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) or the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) in general. According to [N'Gasta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:N%27Gasta), the Daedra were born before the stars.

[Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) believe that the stars were created by [Ruptga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruptga), who was so big he could place the stars themselves in the sky, as a way to guide spirits to the [far shores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Far_Shores). Their myths also describe how, after his death, the hunger of [Sep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sep), the Second Serpent, took form in the sky as a hungry void that chases and tries to devour the stars. Some sources claim that, before deceiving the spirits, Sep stole one of the stars which Ruptga held in his hand and was using to form the Great Map and put it in his pocket. When Sep was killed, it is this star that fell out of his mouth in the form of a pearl. The peal fell to the land and was covered by sand, and so came to belong to [Zeht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zeht). It is said that this Pearl is the "Way and the Guide", the "light to the Far Shores and back again" and, when it is at last discovered again, [water](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Water_Magic) will flow over all the previous Worldskins and the time of Sep's Hunger will be over, the Hungry Stomach of [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal) at last sated. It is also said that the Hunger is what answers the call of the worlds to save them, and that the only real world is that of Satakal, as the stars can't be devoured.

[Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) believe that the stars guide your destiny from birth to death and beyond.

The ancient [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nedes) believed the stars were set in their courses by the [Great Dragon of Time](Lore_Akatosh.md), who also appointed [the guardians](Lore_Astronomy.md#Constellations) to watch over them.

The [Celestials](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Celestial) are powerful ethereal beings formed by the power of the stars, the most powerful of which represent the [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md). It is unknown if these beings are from [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) or if they are mortals who have achieved a state of ascension. Whatever the case, the Celestials that represent the constellations are linked to them, as in [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), the corresponding stars temporarily vanished from the sky while the Celestials took form in [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn), and are said to be part of the world's natural order.

### Unstars
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/MW-birthsign-Serpent.jpg/200px-MW-birthsign-Serpent.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-birthsign-Serpent.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-birthsign-Serpent.jpg) Unstars forming the Serpent [Unstars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unstars), also known as **not-stars**, are celestial objects that resemble [stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stars). Unlike stars, unstars move across the sky and do not emit [varliance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varliance), though they still emit [light](Lore_Elements.md#Light). The nature of this light and its effect on mortals remains uncertain.

The [constellation](Lore_Constellations.md) of [the Serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent), comprised of four unstars, is not confined to appearing in the night sky at specific times of the year. The motions of unstars that form the Serpent are deemed unpredictable. Although they can be forecasted to some extent, sometimes it is deemed impossible.

### Comets
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-crown_store-Comet%2C_Aetherial.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Comet%2C_Aetherial.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Comet,_Aetherial.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Comet,_Aetherial.jpg) An Aetherial Comet [Comets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Comets) are celestial bodies characterized by their small size, composition of ice and dust, and the distinctive trails known as a comet's tail. They move through the heavens, and are often linked to significant events and frequently hold symbolic importance across various cultures and religions. They also are present in some magical practices.

## Astronomical Phenomena

### Constellations
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/63/MW-birthsign-Mage.jpg/200px-MW-birthsign-Mage.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-birthsign-Mage.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-birthsign-Mage.jpg) The Mage constellation [Constellations](Lore_Constellations.md) (known as **Sky Tales** to the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md)) are collections of stars and other astronomical objects which appear in the night sky which form a perceived pattern. The constellation that is in the night sky at the time of year of a person's birth is called their **birthsign** (also spelled **birth sign**) or **star sign** and is believed to influence their characteristics and fate. Those born under certain constellations are said to be 'fortunate in their aspects'. Such people are often blessed or cursed with remarkable abilities or weaknesses as a result of the magical conjunctions of celestial influences.

There are generally accepted to be thirteen constellations in the sky, sometimes called the **Thirteen Patrons**. There are three **guardian constellations**, known to the Reachmen as the **Reach Guardians** and the **Guardians in the Sky**: the [Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md), the [Mage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Mage), and the [Thief](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Thief), each associated with three other constellations, called their **charges** (known as the **Lesser Stars** to the Reachfolk). Each of these twelve constellations corresponds to one of the twelve [months of the year](Lore_Calendar.md). The thirteenth sign, the [Serpent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent), is neither guardian nor charge.

### Aurora
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/SR-photo-Astronomy.jpg/200px-SR-photo-Astronomy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-photo-Astronomy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-photo-Astronomy.jpg) The Aurora The [aurora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aurora) (also known as **[Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md)'s lights**) is a phenomenon observed in the night sky above [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) that is characterized by vivid ribbons of multi-colored light. It occurs when the skies are clear, and can even be seen from parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Similar phenomena occur in certain realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).

### Bloodmoon
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt_02.jpg/200px-BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-quest-Hircine%27s_Hunt_02.jpg) The Bloodmoon The [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodmoon) is a phenomenon occurring when [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md)'s [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md) is hosted on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). During the Great Hunt, [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md) turns a deep shade of red and becomes the Bloodmoon. The hunt is believed to end when the Bloodmoon disappears, and Secunda turning red is considered a calling for the chase. The Bloodmoon occurs naturally in the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md), as the Great Hunts are constantly held there.

### Eclipse
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg/200px-CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg) A solar eclipse An [Eclipse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eclipse) is an astronomical phenomenon that happens when a celestial body is temporarily obscured by passing into the shadow of another body, usually by having another body pass between it and the sky of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). Due to the two [moons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons), [solar](Lore_Sun.md) eclipses can happen in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) several times a year and are known as **Vampire Days**. During eclipses [spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost), [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and other [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead), are said to be encountered more frequently. The phenomenon is sometimes thought to represent the loss of the favor of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and to be possible to undo with diligent prayer to that deity. Tamrielic kings have also been known to sometimes use [time altering](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Time_Magic) magical items such as Arcane Hourglasses to dispel them. Inhabitants of Tamriel can sometimes receive warning of an impending eclipse in the form of [prophetic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sight) nightmares revolving around it and the subsequent surge of spirits and undead. There are some stories about [Daedric armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment) that say that it should always be worked on at night, and never during an eclipse.

During a dark eclipse, both moons eclipse the sun, and this alignment reveals the "third moon" called the [Dark Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Moon). This event is significant to the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) people's spiritual duality. Khajiit born under a mutual eclipse of Nirn's two moons are designated as part of the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) furstock, and are "beacons" of Alkosh's divine grace, even though not all of them will go on to become the titular Mane. The Moons call them stronger than others, so they must maintain a strong will to resist that call, lest allowing the darkness of Lorkhaj to darken their heart. In [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) at large, those born during a rare eclipse of Tamriel's moons are unusually gifted in the magical arts, such as the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) mage of legend, [Gyron Vardengroet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gyron_Vardengroet).

The [Necromancer's Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancer%27s_Moon) eclipses the planet [Arkay](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md) every eight days, preventing its light from reaching Nirn for 24 hours. This celestial phenomenon is known as the Shade of the Revenant, and casts a purple light down on certain locations across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during this period.

### Equinox
An [equinox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Equinox) is an astronomical phenomenon where day and night are nearly equal in length.

In *[Liminal Bridges](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Liminal_Bridges)*, [Camilonwe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camilonwe_of_Alinor) of [Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alinor) discusses the significance of the equinox in magical practices. In this text, the equinox plays a crucial role in the ritual required to transform a [morpholith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morpholith) into a [sigil stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md), an essential [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) for opening portals to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). The process involves preparing a purified chamber and placing the morpholith on a table at dawn during the equinox. The mechanic then performs uninterrupted rites from the Book of Law until sunset. The timing of the equinox is significant as it seems to enhance the ritual's effectiveness, allowing the morpholith to be prepared for inscription by a Daedra Lord, turning it into a sigil stone capable of sustaining powerful arcane energies.

According to one of the books in the *[Ancient Tales of the Dwemer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancient_Tales_of_the_Dwemer)* series, *[The Snow Elf and the Variation-Lens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Snow_Elf_and_the_Variation-Lens)*, written by [Gor Felim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gor_Felim) (under the alias of [Marobar Sul](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Marobar_Sul)), a so-called "dual equinox" is described as a real phenomenon. In this fictional account, Mzulchond, a [Tonal Architect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tonal_Architecture), spent twenty years tuning a great [animus geode](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) he discovered in Inner Duathand, attempting to synchronize it with the precession of these dual equinoxes to capture the elusive Nirnpith Essence. Despite being a poorly researched work posing as serious scholarship, Felim's texts influenced the perception of the Dwemer among the people of Tamriel, shaping popular beliefs and misconceptions about them.

Some pieces of equipment were named after the equinox phenomenon. In the late days of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Gauntlets of the Equinox, a pair of [mithril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mithril) gauntlets imbued with resistance to frost, were found in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

### Solstice
A [solstice](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md) is an astronomical event that happens twice a year when the sun reaches its highest or lowest point in the sky at noon.

#### Winter Solstice
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/ON-crown_store-Solstice_Indrik.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Solstice_Indrik.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Solstice_Indrik.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Solstice_Indrik.jpg) Solstice Indrik, believed to be born during the longest night of the year The winter solstice is the shortest day of the year, when the sun is at its lowest point in the sky. A number of beliefs and practices are linked to the Winter Solstice. The [New Life Festival](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life_Festival), observed on the 1st of [Morning Star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morning_Star), celebrates the end of the old year and the start of the new, symbolizing hope and fresh beginnings. It also marks the Winter Solstice, when [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus) the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md) begins his return and the days start to lengthen. Each Tamrielic culture has its own traditions related to this event.

One legend surrounding the birth of [Solstice Indriks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Indrik) claims that it originated from a child's toy, magically transformed on the year's longest night. However, other conflicting beliefs and stories also surround its mysterious birth.

[Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), the Twilight God who ushers in the next cycle, was once venerated during the Winter Solstice through ceremonies held at ancient [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) temples. Offerings were made to keep him asleep for another year. Certain practices and rituals, meant to ensure his prolonged slumber, persisted long after the fall of the Dragon Cult, continuing into the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

Some pieces of equipment were named after the winter solstice. In the late days of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Shield of Winter Solstice, an elven shield imbued with resistance to frost, was found in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

#### Summer Solstice
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/Masser_full.jpg/150px-Masser_full.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Masser_full.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Masser_full.jpg) Masser (or Jode), associated with the Summer Solstice The summer solstice is the longest day of the year, when the sun is at its highest point in the sky. The exact date of the summer solstice in the Tamrielic [calendar](Lore_Calendar.md) is unclear. During this time, [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) shines brightly in the sky, and the [constellation](Lore_Constellations.md) of [the Steed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Steed) becomes prominent in the southern sky. The [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) clergy of [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh) in [Sunspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunspire) compare [Jode](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode) to the intense heat and redness of the summer solstice.

## Astronomical Instruments
[![](https://images.uesp.net/4/48/AR-misc-Intro Globe.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-misc-Intro Globe.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-misc-Intro Globe.png) A globe of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) Many cultures follow the passage of the stars, and various devices and mechanisms have been created to chart records, with [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and other [planets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planets) commonly being depicted as globes. Other pieces of equipment used across Tamriel include star maps, orreries, telescopes, and sextants.

### Dwemer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/RG-screenshot-Orrery.jpg/200px-RG-screenshot-Orrery.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-screenshot-Orrery.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-screenshot-Orrery.jpg) The Stros M'Kai Orrery [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/RG-misc-Star_Chart.jpg/200px-RG-misc-Star_Chart.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Star_Chart.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Star_Chart.jpg) A Dwemer star chart Undoubtedly the most advanced knowledge of the heavens comes via the relics of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). Their orreries and observatories are one of the longest enduring methods of charting the [Firmament](Lore_Constellations.md).

Of particular importance is the [Orrery](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Orrery) found in the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) at the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), which shows a complete view of the planets and can draw upon the power of Nirn's twin moons.

A second place of importance is upon the island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) where a [Dwemer observatory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Observatory) lies. This observatory contains an orrery as well and a star chart upon its ceiling. The star chart displays runes for each constellation in a radial grid, clockwise by their associated month. A guardian and its three charges are all found at the same distance from the center of the galaxy.

While these structures would fall into disrepair after the disappearance of their creators, the Stros M'Kai observatory would gain renewed interest in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md), with the fledgling [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Restless League](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restless_League) both seeking to chart the stars.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-RGOrrery-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Erasmo-3)</sup>

Similarly, the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md)'s orrery would become functional again under the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) after it underwent repairs sometime around the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), which began in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBOrrery-4)</sup>

Dwemeri orreries incorporate various mechanical devices into their designs, such as [coherers](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coherer), cogs, cylinders, tubes, and gears which require regular maintenance and replacement parts to properly function.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBOrrery-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RGSoul-5)</sup>

### Reachfolk
The [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) call constellations "[Sky Tales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sky_Tales)" and track them with visions and ancient Dwarven plaques. Some Reachfolk Witches can read the stars and see the future and hidden truths in their light.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

### Yokudans
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/RG-misc-Sand_Map.jpg/200px-RG-misc-Sand_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Sand_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Sand_Map.jpg) A Yokudan sand map [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/80/RG-item-Starstone.png/200px-RG-item-Starstone.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Starstone.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Starstone.png) A Yokudan starstone The pre-Redguard [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) culture in particular was noted for its astrologers, who would make crude sand maps in their camps. These are identical to the Dwemer star charts in their radial configuration of the constellations. The only difference at all is the simplification of guardian runes to outlined squares and charge runes to filled circles.

### Daedra
[...] *A time of change is coming -- it is written in the firmament -- and it is time to make fast and conserve our realm, not to extend our grasp.* [...]
— [Imago Storm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imago_Storm)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-IS-7)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/BS-level6-Point_G_02.jpg/200px-BS-level6-Point_G_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level6-Point_G_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level6-Point_G_02.jpg) Pieces of a disassembled orrery in the [plane](Lore_Oblivion.md) of [Havoc Wellhead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Havoc_Wellhead) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/BS-level6-Point_G_03.jpg/200px-BS-level6-Point_G_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level6-Point_G_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level6-Point_G_03.jpg) The mortal traitor, [Sirran Angada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sirran_Angada), taking residence within the observatory of [Havoc Wellhead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Havoc_Wellhead) The study of the stars is not commonly associated with both greater and lesser [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). However, some evidence of Daedric astronomy has been documented within the [Havoc Wellhead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Havoc_Wellhead) during the siege of the [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md). Within the [plane](Lore_Oblivion.md), both a telescope and orrery are under the control of the various [Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) [clans](Lore_Daedra.md#Behavior_and_Culture) who used the realm as a staging ground for the siege.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSA-8)</sup> Whether this telescope and orrery are Daedric in origin or not is debatable, as a contemporary account by [Imago Storm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imago_Storm) describes these clans as being unable to create such devices themselves, preferring to plunder mortals for their creations.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-IS-7)</sup>

### Elder Scrolls
Finally, the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) themselves would seem to depict some sort of star map, with various glyphs of unknown purpose adorning both the foreground and background. Those with no knowledge of the Elder Scrolls, or those with special destinies, can view an Elder Scroll with no ill-effects, and report to see in them patterns similar to star charts, at least those familiar with astronomy or astral navigation.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Eot ES-9)</sup>

Upon careful examination of the particular Elder Scroll depicted here, one can see an outline of The Thief constellation; this is highly appropriate, as this particular Elder Scroll was stolen from the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md) and used to break the curse on the Cowl of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBUltimate-10)</sup>
