# Third Era Wars

[Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era_Wars.md) — [First Era](Lore_First_Era_Wars.md) — [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era_Wars.md) — Third Era — [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era_Wars.md) The following is a list of all known [wars and conflicts](Lore_Wars.md) during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

## The War of the Isle
[3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWQ4-2)</sup>

The [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md) was a conflict in the early-mid [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) in which King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) of the island-kingdom, [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) attacked the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in their closest attempt to claim the province. The war took place in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), twelve years into Emperor [Antiochus Septim](Lore_Antiochus.md)'s reign and later involved both the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md).

## The War of the Red Diamond
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/SR-banner-Potema.png/100px-SR-banner-Potema.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-banner-Potema.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-banner-Potema.png) Banner of Potema [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120) - [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ8-4)</sup>

[Kintyra II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) became Empress on the death of her father, [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md), in 3E 120. Her uncles, [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) King of Gilane and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) King of Lilmoth, attended the coronation, but her aunt, Queen [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) of Solitude, was banished after insulting Kintyra in front of the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md). Potema was scheming to obtain the throne for herself and her son, [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md). Her schemes resulted in a civil war known as the War of the Red Diamond, the name coming from the symbol of the Imperial family. In early surprise attacks, [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) were easily defeated by the force of Queen Potema. The war was highly contested, but was abruptly decided by the death of Potema's claimant, Uriel III, in 3E 127. While the end of the succession crisis ostensibly brought an end to the conflict, Potema held out in Solitude with an undead army for another decade.

During this civil war, the [Knights of the Nine](Lore_Knights_of_the_Nine.md) were fractured and later completely dissolved. The four recovered relics of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) were used in the war and lost afterwards.

More information can be found in the books *[The Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen.md)* and *[Biography of the Wolf Queen](Lore_Biography_of_the_Wolf_Queen.md)*.

### Major Battles
- **Battle of Ichidag**, 3E 127. Forces under [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) defeated [Uriel III](Lore_Uriel_Septim_III.md), who was killed in the aftermath of the battle. Cephorus was crowned Emperor.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TMOP-5)</sup>
- **Battle of Falconstar**, 3E 127. Contemporaneously with the Battle of Ichidag, the forces of Queen Potema fought the forces of Magnus Septim in Skyrim. Upon learning of her son's capture, Potema flew into a rage and led a successful assault on Magnus' weakest flank, causing Magnus and his army to retreat. Word reached them soon after of Uriel III's death.
- **Siege of Solitude**, 3E 137. The Wolf Queen, Potema of Solitude, was finally defeated after a month-long siege of her castle. Although not a full part of the War of the Red Diamond, the siege is best viewed as a postscript, as with the death of the entire royal family except [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md), [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), [Pelagius](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) and [Jolethe](Lore_Jolethe.md), all possible argument over the line of succession was ended.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ8-4)</sup>

## The Camoran Usurper's Invasion
[3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249) - [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267)

Haymon Camoran, known as the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md), a pretender to the Camoran throne of Valenwood, conquered all of Valenwood and Hammerfell alongside a portion of western Cyrodiil with an army of daedra and undead. However it's believed that whichever dark power he had aligned himself with had abandoned him towards the end of the war, as he had to fall back on traditional armies of Redguards and Bosmer, alongside conventional trickery as he made his way to northern Hammerfell, and attempted to invade High Rock. Camoran had aligned with Eight Lords of High Rock (referred to as the Eight Traitors), who sympathized more with the Usurper as an opponent to the Empire.

Emperor Cephorus II was a Nord and more sympathetic to Skyrim and Morrowind than High Rock as previous emperors had been, as well as being embroiled in a succession dispute at the time. The Emperor would attempt to send mercenaries to halt Camoran, but they switched sides as Camoran had bribed them. Overall the image and reputation of the Empire had been greatly damaged in the western provinces during the War.

The various lords of High-Rock were unable to coordinate thanks to their internal politics, however Baron Othrok of Dwynnen and his allies managed to halt Camoran's armies at Dwynnen. Then in 3E 267, the combined armies of High Rock mustered the greatest navy ever seen, and defeated and killed the Camoran Usurper and his armies on the Iliac Bay and on land.

### Major Battles
- **[Battle of Dragontooth](Lore_Battle_of_Dragontooth.md)**, [3E 253](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_253). The defeat of the armies of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) and [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) lead to the fall of southern Hammerfell.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFC-6)</sup>
- **[Battle of Wightmoor](Lore_Battle_of_Wightmoor.md)**, [3E 253](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_253). The [Nightmare Host](Lore_Nightmare_Host.md) had taken over [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md), but they were later defeated by Othrok and his armies from the forest. He later became the first Baron of Dwynnen and started to prepare his fight against the Usurper.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TTET-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFOTU-8)</sup>
- **Battle of Firewaves**, [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267). The combined armies of [Dwynnen](Lore_Dwynnen.md), [Ykalon](Lore_Ykalon.md), [Phrygia](Lore_Phrygia.md), and [Kambria](Lore_Kambria.md) led by Dwynnen's Baron [Othrok](Lore_Othrok.md) finally defeated the Usurper.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFOTU-8)</sup>

## The Invasion of Akavir
23rd of Rain's Hand, [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) - [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-RDAI-9)</sup>

[Uriel Septim V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md) attempted to invade the continent of Akavir, starting with the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(place).md) Kingdom. The preparations for the invasion begun in the [3E 268](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_268),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-10)</sup> almost immediately after Uriel V took the throne, started off with the invasion of several islands between Tamriel and Akavir and were concluded in [3E 286](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_286) when final scouting actions were done on Akavir soil.

Uriel V first started by conquering several islands between 3E 271-284, including Roscrea, Cathnoquey, Yneslea, and Esroniet to act as supply routes to Akavir. While the invasion of Akavir ended in failure, these acquisitions would stay with the Third Empire.

A special Far East Fleet, the most powerful fleet in Tamriel's history, was created for the invasion. After that, the Expeditionary Force was sent to Akavir in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288). Imperial forces landed near the mouth of a river and found no opposition, taking an abandoned village and renaming it [Septimia](Lore_Septimia.md). Despite initial success, due to unusual weather, no permanent supply routes were possible to establish. The Emperor personally oversaw the war effort and colonization, making the decision to bring in colonists rather than reinforcements since the Akavir army was nowhere to be seen. The Empire advanced further inland, still finding no signs of the Akavir, who were seemingly attempting to draw the Tamriel forces in. Early in the winter of [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290), the Akaviri advanced, attempting to surround the Imperial forces. While the Legions acquitted themselves well, they were forced to fight on the defensive. The Emperor attempted a retreat and ultimately ordered the complete withdrawal from Akavir. However, the majority of Uriel V's invasion force, including thousands of colonists, were killed and the emperor himself was slain in combat, ending the conflict.

### Major Battles
- **The Far East Fleet**, [3E 271](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_271) - [3E 284](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_284). The Far East Fleet sets sail for [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) on the way conquering the kingdoms of [Cathnoquey](Lore_Cathnoquey.md), [Esroniet](Lore_Esroniet.md), [Roscrea](Lore_Roscrea.md), and [Yneslea](Lore_Yneslea.md).
- **Taking of Septimia**, Mid Year [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288). Six weeks after Uriel's fleet took off from [Black Harbor](Lore_Black_Harbor.md) on [Esroniet](Lore_Esroniet.md), they landed on Akavir and took a river port town from the Tsaesci populace that abandoned it as soon as they saw the Expeditionary Force. They renamed it [Septimia](Lore_Septimia.md) and it was to be the first colony of the newly founded Imperial Province of Akavir.
- **Taking of Ionith**, 3E 288. Soon after capturing Septimia, the emperor himself marched along the river and found another abandoned city, named [Ionith](Lore_Ionith.md) after it was taken.
- **Battle in the northern mountains**, Sun's Height [3E 289](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_289). The emperor had his legions attack the amassing Tsaesci forces north of Ionith. Even though the Tsaesci were caught off guard and their leader captured, the legions were forced to fall back and took heavy losses during the retreat. As a result, Ionith was besieged and remained such until its eventual fall.
- **Battle of Ionith**, 3E 290. After contact was established with the Far East Fleet, that was sent to return with supplies, the emperor ordered the army to break through the enemy lines and to fall back to Septimia. Not far from Ionith, on the road to Septimia, the remains of the Expeditionary Force were ambushed. Uriel V and his personal guard, the Tenth Legion, stayed behind to hold off the Tsaesci, while the others continued their retreat. Only two men from the Tenth Legion survived to tell the tale of the emperor's demise.
- **Battle of Septimia**, 3E 290. Two days after Ionith was abandoned, the fleet that had returned with the supplies found the town of Septimia under heavy siege by Tsaesci forces. They drove the attackers away, rescued the survivors and left for Tamriel.

Around this time, it is said that [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) planned to "gather the whole of Nirn under the imperial flag."<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GOGclientoverview-12)</sup> Circa [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376), Uriel allied himself with [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), appointing him to the station of [Imperial Battlemage of the Empire](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRB-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GOGclientoverview-12)</sup> Uriel greatly benefited from arcane support and shrewd counsel of his close advisor in the conflicts to come. ## Imperial Simulacrum
[3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) - [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399)

### Introduction
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn.png/300px-AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn.png) Jagar Tharn, arch-traitor to the Empire Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) betrayed and secretly imprisoned Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and used [Illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) [magic](Lore_Magic.md) to masquerade as the Emperor for a decade.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ALOUS-11)</sup> [Imperial authority](Lore_Third_Empire.md) declined under the Simulacrum, resulting in a series of provincial wars.

### The Five Year War
The [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) was a war that took place between the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) of Eastern [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md)<sup>[†](#intnote_eastorwest)</sup> and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). The Bosmer claim that the war began in [3E 394](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_394) when Khajiit bandits started attacking wood caravans headed for Valenwood. The Khajiit claim that the war began in [3E 395](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_395) when the Bosmer attacked the city of [Torval](Lore_Torval.md) in retaliation for the bandit attacks. This border dispute soon turned into a bloody war that lasted for 4 years and 9 months. Thus it was named the Five Year War. Eventually, Elsweyr defeated Valenwood and expanded its territory slightly west, taking both banks of the [Xylo River](Lore_Xylo_River.md).

This war happened concurrently with the [War of the Blue Divide](Lore_War_of_the_Blue_Divide.md) between Western Valenwood and the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396), resulting in a quick and short victory for the Summerset Isles and further compounding Valenwood's distress.

### The Arnesian War
[3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396)<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TTET-7)</sup>

What started as a simple slave revolt in the [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) lands to the south escalated into a full war between [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3M-16)</sup> The cause of the war was the death of a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) trader named [Roris](Lore_Roris.md), who was later canonized into a [saint](Lore_Tribunal_Saints.md) by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LOTS-17)</sup> He was captured by the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) rebels and when he refused to denounce his faith, he was brutally tortured and murdered. When news of his death reached the rest of Morrowind, war was declared on Black Marsh. Morrowind's armies ultimately defeated the Argonians and expanded Morrowind's territory to the south.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3A-18)</sup>

The father of the famous Dunmer painter [Rythe Lythandas](Lore_Rythe_Lythandas.md) was a known participant in the war. After peace was established, a commission was created to repair the damage done by the war.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ADIF7-19)</sup>

### War of the Blue Divide
[3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397)<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ADIF7-19)</sup>

Named after the [body of water](Lore_Cape_of_the_Blue_Divide.md) separating them, the War of the Blue Divide pitted [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) against its former ally [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). After a vicious but short war, Summerset defeated Valenwood,<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3All-20)</sup> gaining several coastal islands as a result of the war.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ADIF7-19)</sup> The most notable damage from the war was when the Altmeri destroyed many of Valenwood's harbors.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ADIF6-21)</sup> The war occurred soon after Valenwood's war with Elsweyr, meaning Valenwood was in an already weakened state. The war took an even worse turn, however, when the Khajiit took advantage of Valenwood's further distress, reigniting the war and forcing the Wood Elves to fight on two fronts.

The only known episodes concerning the war were when, in 3E 396, the Bosmeri Parikh tribe began their rampage through southeastern Valenwood against other tribes, receiving aid from Summerset itself.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-WAP-22)</sup> It is unknown whether or not this was the cause of the war.

#### Major Battles
- **Siege of Woodhearth**, 3E 397. Summerset Isle surrounded the coastal city with the intent of starving its inhabitants and forcing them to surrender. Since the Bosmer practice cannibalism, the attack was presumed a tactical error on Summerset's part. A humorous contemporary remark stated that "at the end, only one fat wood elf will remain to wave the flag".<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ADIF5-23)</sup>

### The War of the Bend'r-mahk
The [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) (sometimes stylized as the [War of the Bend'r-Mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) and the [War of Bend'r-Mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md)) was a conflict that started in [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) period between the provinces of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).

During the war [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) defeated the forces of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and claimed many miles of the [eastern parts of those provinces](Lore_Western_Reach.md), including the cities of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md). Some of these territories were claimed to have been lost in previous wars at the expense of Skyrim. The Redguards harbored a fierce grudge against [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md), and yearned for a chance to take back land that was seized from them.

Behind the scenes, the conflict was being used by [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) and [Pergan Asuul](Lore_Pergan_Asuul.md) to create a *Shadow of Conflict*, or [Umbra' Keth](Lore_Umbra%27_Keth.md). However, the villains' plans were put to an end by the [Soul of Conflict](Lore_Soul_of_Conflict.md), a commoner from [Azra's Crossing](Lore_Azra%27s_Crossing.md) that was roped into the shadow wars of higher players through a series of events.

After peace was established, a commission was created to repair the damage done by the war.

### Invasion of the Battlespire
The [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) legions of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) invaded the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), slaughtering the stationed [Imperial Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) and blocking reinforcements from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). However, an [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md) of the [Imperial Battle College](Lore_Imperial_Battle_College.md) arrived at the facility and put an end to Dagon's machinations by banishing the Daedric Prince to the Wells of Oblivion, an action which subsequently caused the shattering of Battlespire into ruin.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Battlespire-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Do O-25)</sup>

Despite the recommendations of the Imperial Treasury, Battlespire was later recaptured by the Empire.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-BSAthenaeum156-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TL-27)</sup> And while the [Weir Gate](Lore_Weir_Gate.md) still stood, the rest of the proving ground remained destroyed, along with ability to access the Oblivion realms using the Voidgate.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Do O-25)</sup>

### Conclusion
The Imperial Simulacrum was ended when the Eternal Champion defeated Jagar Tharn in the Imperial Palace labyrinths, while also restoring the rightful Emperor to the throne, ushering in the following period called the Restoration.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Arena-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BOQB-29)</sup>

## Fractious warring and revolt
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/DF-intro7-Book Of Time.png/300px-DF-intro7-Book Of Time.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-intro7-Book Of Time.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-intro7-Book Of Time.png) Warring and revolt as seen in the [Book of the Time](Lore_Book_of_the_Time.md)<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Intro Narration-30)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/DF-concept-Cutscene Volcano Battle.png/300px-DF-concept-Cutscene Volcano Battle.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-concept-Cutscene Volcano Battle.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-concept-Cutscene Volcano Battle.png) Dark Elves battling Nords<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> Circa [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399)<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Intro Narration-30)</sup><sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_nb4)</sup> — Circa [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417)<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TWITW-31)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup>

The neglectful inactions of the "unworthy heirs" of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) weakened and cracked the bonds of their Empire. This reached a breaking point during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) period in the late 300s of the Third Era; leading to a series of wars and rebellions between the provinces that lasted for years into the early 400s.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Intro Narration-30)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Decrypting The Elder Scrolls-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-DUG-33)</sup><sup>:3</sup> This outbreak of anarchy was seen as a cyclical return to the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), a time of chaos before [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) established peace with his Empire. A few believed that this decline was inevitable, as no lasting empire could exist in a place like the violent [Arena of Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-DUG-33)</sup><sup>:3</sup> Some blame the later Septims for affording too much liberty to their vassal kings or for lacking the military genius of their ancestors.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-DUG-33)</sup><sup>:3</sup>

The major events of this period<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> are uncertain, except for the Wars of the Iliac Bays and a conflict between [Dark Elves](Lore_Dark_Elf.md) and [Nords](Lore_Nord.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Intro Narration-30)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Decrypting The Elder Scrolls-32)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Assas-34)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup> Of note, conflicts taking place across the provinces at this time were referred to as "religious wars."<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Assas-34)</sup> Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), who had returned to the throne after a ten-year hiatus caused by the Imperial Simulacrum debacle, was determined to reunite the Empire despite the neglect of his ancestors and the political unrest invigorated by the usurper [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-BHOTE-35)</sup> According to Uriel Septim VII's biographer, Septim shifted his strategy from the aggressive military intimidation and diplomatic accommodation of his youth to clandestine manipulation of affairs behind the scenes through the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ALOUS-11)</sup> who were also known to organize formal assaults on the Empire's enemies if necessary.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-DFLady Brisienna Dialogue-36)</sup> Another significant event during this time was the Empire's colonization of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in collaboration with the King of Morrowind around [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ALOUS-11)</sup>

Sometime before [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417),<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> the troops of Masters [Gothren](Lore_Gothren.md) and [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) fought against one another on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). During the conflict however Neloth obtained the [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md) from his master [Daedric Prince Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), which invigorated his normally cowardly soldiers. Gothren's own troops had no recourse, except to pray that Mehrunes Dagon would reclaim his artifact. However, a clever band of acrobats stole the Razor from Master Neloth and gave it to Master Gothren, turning the tide of the conflict in that lord's favor.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Ro A-37)</sup> By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Sorcerer-Lord Gothren of [Tel Vos](Lore_Tel_Vos.md) had became Archmagister of House Telvanni on Vvardenfell, while Sorcerer-Lord Neloth of [Tel Naga](Lore_Tel_Naga.md) remained but a Master.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MWHT-38)</sup>

Seemingly, the fractious warring and revolt was put to an end in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), when the [Miracle of Peace](Lore_Miracle_of_Peace.md) occurred.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TWITW-31)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup><sup>[[nb 5]](#intnote_nb5)</sup>

### The War of Betony
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif/300px-LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Daggerfall_History.gif) War of Betony [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402) — [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFC-6)</sup>

The [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) (known as the **Siege of Betony** to Sentinel)<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-FWOB-39)</sup> was caused when Lord Mogref of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md) asked King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) for protection against pirates and other brigands. King Camaron of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) and his advisors, citing an old contract that suggested Betony was a holding of their kingdom, declared war. Hostilities ended after Lysandus' son, [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md), who became king after his father was killed in battle at [Cryngaine Field](Lore_Cryngaine_Field.md), won the battle and defeated Camaron in single combat. Sentinel surrendered and Betony passed under the control of Daggerfall.

More information can be found in two books, both called *The War of Betony*. [Vulper Newgate](Lore_The_War_of_Betony_(Newgate).md)'s version contains the official history, as written by the victors; [Fav'te](Lore_The_War_of_Betony_(Fav%27te).md)'s book, however, tells the story from the viewpoint of a citizen of Sentinel. *[The Daggerfall Chronicles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles)* also provides a slightly different version of the story, a summary of which can be read [here](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Daggerfall_Chronicles/Narrative).

#### Major Battles
- **Battle of the Bluffs**: The first major battle of the war. Won by Daggerfall.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-NWOB-40)</sup>
- **Siege of Craghold**: The Island of Craghold was besieged by the forces of Sentinel for several days, but the siege was broken by Daggerfall forces under Lord Bridwell.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-NWOB-40)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Jokes-41)</sup>
- **Battle of Cryngaine Field**: Final battle of the conflict. The battlefield was covered in a dense mist created by [Skakmat](Lore_Skakmat.md), and after it lifted King Lysandus was found to have perished from an arrow through either his heart<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFC-6)</sup> or his throat.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-NWOB-40)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-FWOB-39)</sup> His son Gothryd was crowned on the battlefield and led the army to victory.

## Oblivion Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg/200px-OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Oblivion_Gate.jpg) A gate to the Deadlands of Mehrunes Dagon [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433)

The [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), also known as the **Great Anguish**, was a total war between [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) hordes and the population of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It began with the assassination of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and all of his known heirs in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) at the hands of a fanatical cult of worshippers known as the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md).

Shortly afterward, Oblivion Gates to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) began opening, unleashing Daedra across Tamriel. With each Gate that opened, the [barriers](Lore_Dragonfires.md) between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [Firmament](Lore_Astronomy.md), would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined. Widespread devastation and casualties resulted across entire provinces. The Daedra besieged [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and laid waste to the [Old Holds](Lore_Hold.md). In [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) called back many [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to fight off the Daedra. According to the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), the Daedra were forced to close the Gates in Black Marsh due to an Argonian counter invasion. In [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the city of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) was entirely destroyed.
