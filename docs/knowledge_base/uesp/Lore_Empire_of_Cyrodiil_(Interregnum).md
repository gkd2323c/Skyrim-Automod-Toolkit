The Empire of Cyrodiil<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-THOTE-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KOTD-2)</sup> (also known as the **Imperial Empire**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> refers to an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) state that existed during the early years of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Although identified as an empire, it differed from the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), and [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in that its only permanent dependency was [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) itself, and it was at no time ruled by a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) emperor.

## History
With the collapse of the Second Empire in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430), its provinces reasserted their independence, leaving the Imperial heartland of Cyrodiil to its own devices. The region remained a bastion of Imperial culture as the rest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) rejected its influence. Further, institutions that had been hallmarks of the Second Empire, including the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), and the office of [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md), survived the chaos relatively intact. As such, the Empire of Cyrodiil, as it would come to be known, succeeded the Second Empire as a matter of course. In the absence of legitimate claimants to the Ruby Throne, the Empire of Cyrodiil was ruled over by a succession of pretenders who usually attained the throne through conquest.

The earliest known of these pretenders was the Warlord [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_(warlord).md) who claimed the Ruby Throne sometime between [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430) and [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533), and mounted a campaign to rid [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) of foreign influence by expelling the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md). The Akaviri refugees went on to settle in the [Rim Territories](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rim_Territories) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), where they founded the town of [Hakoshae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hakoshae).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1Elseweyr-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg/200px-ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-statue-Durcorach.jpeg) [Bas-relief](Lore_Statues.md) depicting Durcorach's conquest of Cyrodiil [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach), a [Reachman](Lore_Reachmen.md) warlord, conquered Cyrodiil with an army of Reachmen in [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SHot LE-5)</sup> With Durcorach's accession to the throne, he founded the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) dynasty, which ruled the Empire for forty-four years. Durcorach ruled the Empire until [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), when he was slain during an invasion of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) in a battle outside of [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TOAM3-6)</sup> Durcorach was succeeded by his son [Moricar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moricar), who reigned for over twenty years before he was wounded during an invasion of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), and he died in [2E 564](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_564) as a result of his injuries.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SHot LE-5)</sup> Moricar was succeeded by his son [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic), who reigned until [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), when he was overthrown in a [revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) led by Duke [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) that was caused by Leovic's legalization of Daedra worship in the Empire.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EFEV-7)</sup> Of all of the pretenders who had thus far achieved control of the Empire, the Longhouse Emperors had been some of the more successful ones, having managed to stay in power for over four decades.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-COTFC4-8)</sup> One triumph of their reign was the opportunistic annexation of [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) and [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md) in northern [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). This occurred during the early years of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) outbreak, which had hit the Khajiit particularly hard. The Khajiit attempted to retake both cities from the Imperials but were unsuccessful.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONKhali-9)</sup> [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) was also brought under Imperial control prior to [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFC-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-calo-11)</sup> Under their rule, Cyrodiil controlled land as far as the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Reachmen Wrothgar-12)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) The Soulburst Leovic's usurper, Varen Aquilarios, had been the Duke of Chorrol before leading the Colovian Estates in rebellion against Leovic.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EFEV-7)</sup> After personally killing Leovic and claiming the title of Emperor of Cyrodiil, Varen set out to cement his status a legitimate leader of the Empire by retrieving the lost [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md). He was encouraged in this endeavor by the powerful necromancer [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), who promised that the Amulet could be used in a ritual to persuade [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to make Varen a Dragonborn.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-COTFC1-13)</sup> Mannimarco betrayed Varen, however, and the ritual broke the covenant with Akatosh and set [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) adrift in the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) in an event known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCA-14)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EFEV-7)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-COTFC1-13)</sup> Varen was believed lost in the event and his wife, [Clivia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn), was proclaimed empress regent.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ta Toa M10-15)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-COTFC4-8)</sup>

Clivia Tharn's reign ushered in a time of increasing instability within the Empire. Swayed by offers of power and immortality, Clivia fell under the thrall of Mannimarco, who convinced her that the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) was responsible for the Soulburst and to expel them from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), allowing Mannimarco's [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult) to operate freely.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-COTFC4-8)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TOTBW-16)</sup> Mannimarco himself aspired to become a god and rule not only Nirn, but his master [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) as well.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCA-14)</sup> Beyond the Empire's borders, the other nations of Tamriel began to unite into three different alliances in response to perceived Imperial aggression and corruption: the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Bt Co T-17)</sup> [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-THot E-18)</sup> and the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). Each alliance sought to conquer the Empire of Cyrodiil and place one of their own on the Ruby Throne, triggering the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) that wracked the whole of Tamriel in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

The three alliances quickly conquered much of central Cyrodiil and confined the Empire to only the Imperial City and [Lake Rumare](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Rumare)'s environs.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TCC-19)</sup> With central Cyrodiil ravaged by war, many of its inhabitants scattered. Some of them hid in the northern and eastern mountains, while others fled south to [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). The richest of the Imperials, mostly [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md), bought estates in western [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), around the cities of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), away from raging war. That proved to be a mistake, however, as pirates took control of Anvil, claimed dominion over the entire [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md), and declared it to be an independent state, leading to strife with loyalist Kvatch.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-20)</sup>

The Empire did not sit idle as the armies of the alliances forged deep into its territory. It attempted to expand its reach on two fronts: [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) to the north, and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to the south.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESO-21)</sup> The [Seventh Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), led by Magus-General [Septima Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septima_Tharn), invaded High Rock and Hammerfell.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BSOHR-22)</sup> The [Legion of the West Weald](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), led by General Lavinia Axius, invaded Valenwood to annex Arenthia Vale, which, by decree of the Count of [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad), and the Elder Council was renamed the South Weald.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-YFTT-23)</sup> Each of these ventures ultimately failed.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESO-21)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Chancellor [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) as head of the Elder Council attempted to end the Three Banners War and restore the Empire's power through assembling the [Wrathstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrathstone) and using it to activate an ancient weapon within the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus). Instead, this unleashed several [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) which had been imprisoned for centuries.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

Little is known about the outcome of the Three Banners War; however, it is known the Empire of Cyrodiil collapsed during it. By the time of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s plot to gather the [Four Amibitions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Four_Ambitions), the Empire of Cyrodiil no longer existed as a power, and its government, the Elder Council, had been formally dissolved.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-RL-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CC-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-LTk-27)</sup> When the Stirk Fellowship called upon Cyrodiil for soldiers during its formation, it was found that whatever remnant of Imperial authority that once remained was well and truly gone.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> By the time of the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) in the ninth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Empire had long ceased to exist. Cyrodiil was divided between the Nibenese east and Colovian west, and remained so until reunification under King [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md), whom [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) served as a general at the time.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-PGE3C-29)</sup>

## Administrative Titles
The exact hierarchy of ranks, the nuances between them, and the trajectory of advancement has not been spelled out, but can generally be understood as categorized below.

- **[Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md)**, the ruler of the Empire.

- **[High Chancellor](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md)** (sometimes Imperial Battlemage), the head of the Elder Council. - **Councilor** (or Elder), a member of the Elder Council.

- **Governor**, A Governor of a region within an Imperial Province. <sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TFo S-30)</sup>

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-Attrebus_31-0) It is unknown when the warlord Attrebus assumed the Imperial Throne during the Interregnum. The *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_The_Elsweyr_Confederacy)*, establishes that Attrebus' reign occurred prior to the city of Rimmen's secession from Elsweyr in 2E 812, and the book mentions that the Akaviri were expelled from Cyrodiil by Attrebus during his reign and that they later settled in the Rim Territories in Elsweyr. The Rim-men already existed by 2E 582, and since every emperor from 2E 533 to 2E 582 is known, this means that Attrebus could have only reigned sometime during the period from the assassination of Savirien-Chorak in 2E 430 to the accession of Durcorach the Black Drake in 2E 533.
2. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Soulburst_37-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Soulburst_37-1)***</sup> The *[Chronicles of the Five Companions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chronicles_of_the_Five_Companions_1)* and *[The Chorrol Crier](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Chorrol_Crier)* state that Varen Aquilarios disappeared in 2E 579, while *[Triumphs of a Monarch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Triumphs_of_a_Monarch,_Ch._10)* states that Varen Aquilarios disappeared in 2E 578. More sources use 2E 579 as the year of Varen's disappearance, so this year can be presumed to be more accurate.
3. [^](#cite_ref-ESOTBWEs_42-0) During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), when each alliance gained control of the keeps around the Imperial City, they would crown a figurehead emperor from among their warriors. These emperors' reigns were extremely short-lived and their names have been lost to history.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-CATAYQ2-41)</sup>
4. [^](#cite_ref-KU.28s.29_47-0) At some point prior to 2E 812, the remnants of the defeated [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) army that [invaded](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) Tamriel in 2E 572 seized the Imperial Throne from the successors of the warlord Attrebus. This suggests that at least one Kamal ruled as emperor for a time.
5. [^](#cite_ref-Emperor_Zero_49-0) Cuhlecain proclaimed himself emperor upon capturing the Imperial City, but he was assassinated before he could be formally crowned. His successor, Tiber Septim, later honored Cuhlecain as "[Emperor Zero](Lore_Cult_of_Emperor_Zero.md)".
6. [^](#cite_ref-Cuhlecain_51-0) Who was responsible for the assassination of Cuhlecain is controversial. The *Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition* states that Cuhlecain was assassinated by a nightblade from the Western Reach, while *[The Arcturian Heresy](Lore_The_Arcturian_Heresy.md)* states that Tiber Septim assassinated Cuhlecain and covered it up by claiming that a nightblade from the Western Reach was actually behind the assassination.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-PGE1C-48)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TAH-50)</sup>
