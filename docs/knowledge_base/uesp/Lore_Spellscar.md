[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/ON-place-Spellscar_03.png/200px-ON-place-Spellscar_03.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spellscar_03.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spellscar_03.png) Spellscar The Spellscar is a large onyx pillar that is associated with the [Celestial Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md)'s [Staff](Lore_Staves.md). In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mage Quest-1)</sup> the Mage constellation disappeared from the sky, and with it the Staff fell to Nirn on top of a settlement in [Lower Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), shattering into onyx shards that began to emit elemental surges of magic,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-antiquity-2)</sup> devastating the area.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-quest-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-loading-4)</sup> The area of magical contamination around the black pillar became known as the Spellscar. The largest pillar was also colloquially known as the [Mage's Staff](Lore_The_Mage%27s_Staff.md), which was the name given to the plane located inside the obelisk that was releasing all the magic.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Sara-5)</sup> Similar to what the [Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md)'s violent outbursts warped the [Aetherian Archive](Lore_Aetherian_Archive.md) upper levels into,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mage Dialogue-6)</sup> the Spellscar's instabilities were caused by the unstable core of the Mage's Staff, which releases dangerous amounts of magicka.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Sara-5)</sup>

Many [atronachs](Lore_Atronach.md) were seemingly summoned by the elemental storms coming from the Spellscar and its shards. Magic-addicted spellfiends also swarmed the shards, driven mad by the erratic magic of the area.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Sara-5)</sup> That magical aura has also corrupted various creatures local and passing, turning their skin purple.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Other common afflictions were turning your skin into the onyx rock that the Spellscar was made of, channeling crackling elemental magic throughout your body.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> The area also seemed to conjure celestial star-born creatures.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

Some people scavenged the black shards from the site,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-antiquity-2)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> and even made them into weapons.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The material the shards are made of is celestial in nature, as it is sometimes associated with the [Steed](Lore_The_Steed.md) constellation.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
