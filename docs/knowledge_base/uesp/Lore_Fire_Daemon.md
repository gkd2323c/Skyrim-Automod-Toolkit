Fire Daemons are [creatures](Lore_Lesser_Daedra.md) from the [netherworld](Lore_Oblivion.md), who often gate themselves into [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) on some errand of their choosing. They are dangerous foes, highly intelligent, completely chaotic in nature, and powerful in the ways of [magic](Lore_Magic.md). They are resistant to fire and possess the ability to see invisible entities.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARFDDesc-1)</sup>

Fire Daemons could be found throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARDaemon-2)</sup> possibly due to [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) summoning them for use in his legions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-JTD-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPP-4)</sup> [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) was said to have assisted Jagar Tharn's coup by giving him access to Daedric troops and weapons.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup> The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) was known to use Fire Daemons to defend their guild halls during night hours, also sending them out on missions in the guild's interest, such as retrieving items of importance or killing rival faction members.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARDaemon-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ARFaction-6)</sup> One was also among the specimens in the Bestiary of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), guarding the staircase to the top floor.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CTArena-7)</sup>

## History
When the [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md), Jagar Tharn, began his coup against Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), he summoned a pack of Fire Daemons and gave them the appearance of the [Imperial Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md), before banishing Uriel and his Guard into a timeless demesne within [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Arena Intro-8)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-ARMIS-9)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> Tharn may have later sent Fire Daemons through a [dream bridge](Lore_Dreamsleeve.md#Notes) in an attempt to quell the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md)'s efforts to reassemble the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-JTD-3)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARDaemon-2)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), there were riots within [Cyrodiil City](Lore_Cyrodiil_City.md), with the people accusing the Septim Heirs: [Geldall](Lore_Geldall_Septim.md), [Enman](Lore_Enman_Septim.md), and [Ebel](Lore_Ebel_Septim.md) of being by replaced by doppelgangers of Jagar Tharn.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup>

## Notes
- The Fire Daemon creature was originated for [*The Elder Scrolls: Arena*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena). However, usage of term "Daemon" and "Demon" were semi-retired during [*Daggerfall*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)'s development, and replaced with the term [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), which was more neutral in nature, as it doesn't possess the real world negative connotations and assumptions that come with using the term "demon."<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Julian Indigo-11)</sup>
- | [^3](#note_nb3) | The original plot for the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) storyline dealt with the Septim Heirs. There an idea thrown around about "of holding a torch near important members of the court to see if they cast a human shadow and not some horrible, hunched over clawed thing" and there being "an invasion from another faction from within." But these ideas were ultimately left on the cutting room floor.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-12)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- Fire Daemons share several similarities with [Daedroths](Lore_Daedroth.md) in [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), including similarly reptilian forms and high intelligence stats.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-13)</sup> Given their lack of appearance since Arena due to the use of the name Daemon, it's possible they were the inspiration for Daedroths.
