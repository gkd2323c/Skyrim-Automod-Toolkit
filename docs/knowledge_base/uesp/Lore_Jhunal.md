# Jhunal

*Jhunal, sage God of Runes, Flies free beneath the glistening moons. A night owl who's so droll, Soars with us, the wise Jhunal.*
— *[The Song of Gods](Lore_The_Song_of_Gods.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSOG-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/SR-concept-Owl_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Owl_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Owl_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Owl_Mural.jpg) Jhunal Jhunal, also known as **Jhunal the Rune God**, and **God of Runes** is the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) god of hermetic orders, knowledge and wisdom, represented by an **owl**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Contra-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TSOG-1)</sup> and is one of the Hearth Gods<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dat N-3)</sup> and the Nordic father of language and mathematics. After falling out of favor with the rest of that pantheon, he became [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md) of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md). He is largely absent in modern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) mythology.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup>

[Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) mentioned "[JHUNAL](Lore_Julianos_(planet).md)" as one of the eight known worlds that were listed by the Dwemer.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-36L3-5)</sup>

## Worship
Although worship of Jhunal has waned with most Nords, there are still those who have not forgotten him and aim for a higher purpose than the brandishing of swords and pursue things such as scholarship, history, reading, and invention. Certain Nord warriors also respect Jhunal as they must also have a strong mind and the knowledge to carry them through battle.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> His teachings are paired up with those of [Stuhn](Lore_Stuhn.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

Among Jhunal's notable followers was the half-Nord, half- [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) archmage, [Ulfsild the Evergreen](Lore_Ulfsild_the_Evergreen.md), a devoted priestess who wandered through [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OS-8)</sup> Another Reachfolk, [Vund](Lore_Vund.md), an alchemist of considerable skill, was known among his people for his foreign ways.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DBSC-9)</sup> He was in possession of religious texts devoted to Jhunal.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DBSD-10)</sup>

## Wise-Wing the Owl
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-crown_store-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Jhunal%27s_Owl_Mask.jpg) Jhunal's owl mask, depicting Wise-Wing [Wise-Wing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wise-Wing) **the Owl** was a mythical [owl](Lore_Owl.md) companion of Jhunal the Rune God, [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) god of knowledge and wisdom. Like her, Jhunal’s symbol was also an owl.

Wise-Wing the Owl is linked to the god Jhunal or [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), in the northern regions of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). During the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md), it is believed that she serves as a protector, cautioning the unwary to steer clear of graveyards and witches' covens where danger may await. Masks were crafted in Wise-Wing's likeness. It was believed by some that wearing her owl-shaped mask would inspire others to trust in the wearer's wisdom and guidance.

She was commonly depicted as a barred owl. Wearing tail-feathers from a barred owl was considered a sign of devotion to the God of Wisdom.
