[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/ON-interior-Hectahame_Armory_Keystone.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Hectahame_Armory_Keystone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Hectahame_Armory_Keystone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Hectahame_Armory_Keystone.jpg) The Hectahame Armory Keystone in its sconce [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/eb/ON-quest-The_Keystone.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Keystone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Keystone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Keystone.jpg) A Keystone Keystones are magical stones of significant power commonly associated with the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). They are primarily found in the ruins of the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The keystones were created in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) using the magic of the ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), which the Ayleid slavemasters infused in the stones through torment and fear.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> As such, the keystones exert power over the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> There were also used in powerful Ayleid enchantments, such as magical barriers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Modern research of the keystones is limited by the dangerous nature of the relics. Only certain individuals can handle the keystones without suffering from nervous breakdowns.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) in particular could handle them for any length of time.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
