# Old Nedic

Old Nedic, or simply **Nedic**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt S-1)</sup> was a [langauge](Lore_Linguistics.md) spoken by the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) of western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), such as the [Duraki](Lore_Duraki.md), [Keptu](Lore_Keptu.md), and [Perena](Lore_Perena.md). Its written usage dates back to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FGt S-1)</sup> and [even earlier](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) in glyphs and spoken form.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NNo RO-2)</sup>

## Language Family and Genetic Relationship
The origin of the Nedes is fiercely debated by scholars. According to some, they came from the northern continent of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> with the [Old Atmoran](Lore_Nordic.md) tongue evolving into Proto-Nedic.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-It KC-6)</sup> However, findings from some scholars support the theory that the Nedes were native to Tamriel.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Silver Wish Medallion-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Nestof Shadows-8)</sup>

Upon the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokuda.md) conquering the [Nedic Kingdoms](Lore_Empires.md#Nedic_Kingdoms), they adopted many of their cultural elements as their own, including their language, which became absorbed into their tongue, [Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1H-9)</sup> What Old Nedic survived outside of Hammerfell eventually came to reside in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CATAYQ-10)</sup> presumably becoming part of [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md).

## Notes
- The Nedes of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) spoke a tongue known as [Old Cyrodilic](Lore_Old_Cyrodilic.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TDC-13)</sup><sup>:6</sup> but was not related to Old Nedic, instead being a dialect of [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TWE-14)</sup>
- The [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) were a tribe of [humans](Lore_Man.md) who lived in Black Marsh in early eras, speculated to have been descended from the Nedes.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The only known word in their language is "betrux", which means "open",<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESODrillk-16)</sup> but whether the tongue is related to Old Nedic is unknown.
