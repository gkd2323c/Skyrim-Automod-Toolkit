# Ring of Khajiiti

The Ring of Khajiiti (sometimes just **Ring of Khajiit** or **Ring of the Khajiiti**) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with two [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), who have both been known to offer the Ring in return for service.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Description-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFMeridia-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWMephala-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBMeridia-4)</sup> It is said that the ring makes the wearer invisible, silent, and quick.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Description-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HRSTBTK-5)</sup>

The Ring was originally owned by Mephala, until it was stolen off her arm by [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md), the renowned burglar who later became revered as the Thief God of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TTGT-7)</sup> Using the Ring's powers, Rajhin became the most successful burglar in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s history.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TL-8)</sup> It is even said that Rajhin stole a tattoo from the neck of [Empress Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_I.md) as she slept.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup> According to an account, he utilized the Ring's abilities to pilfer the [Book that Knows](Lore_Oghma_Infinium.md) from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s library beneath the sea.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HRSTBTK-5)</sup> Eventually, after making use of its power too freely, Rajhin was abandoned by the Ring and left exposed to his enemies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Description-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TTGT-7)</sup>

[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWYB-9)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-10)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Ring of Khajiiti among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TA-11)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Ring of Khajiiti and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWFR-12)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) a popular card game known as [Tales of Tribute](Lore_Tales_of_Tribute.md) had a card possibly depicting the ring.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TOT-13)</sup> Sometime during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) obtained the ring in a dungeon after finding a map to its location. In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), the [Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) was offered the ring by Meridia in return for killing a sorcerer that broke an arrangement with her, to which the Agent agreed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFMeridia-2)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Mephala tasked the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) with assassinating a member of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) who was killing without obtaining writs. In return for dealing with the rogue member, the Nerevarine was awarded the ring.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWMephala-3)</sup> In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) was tasked by Meridia to cleanse a cave of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md); once this was accomplished, Meridia awarded the Hero the Ring.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBMeridia-4)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Ring was owned by the Khajiit trader [Ri'saad](Lore_Ri%27saad.md), who gave it to the [Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) as a reward for retrieving a precious shipment.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SRSA-14)</sup>
