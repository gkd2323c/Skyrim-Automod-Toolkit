# Malachite

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e9/SR-item-Malachite_Ore.jpg/200px-SR-item-Malachite_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Malachite_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Malachite_Ore.jpg) A Piece of Malachite Ore [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/SR-item-Refined_Malachite.jpg/200px-SR-item-Refined_Malachite.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Refined_Malachite.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Refined_Malachite.jpg) A Refined Malachite Ingot [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/SR-activity-Mining_%28Malachite%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Malachite%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Malachite).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Malachite).jpg) A Malachite Ore Vein **Malachite** is a rare, semi-translucent green volcanic crystal. It is used to create high-quality **glass** [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) and light [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md). As mentioned in the book *[Light Armor Forging](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Light_Armor_Forging)*, this glass bears no relation to the common, brittle type of [glass](Skyrim_Glass_(item).md) used in windows and ornaments, or in the [construction](Skyrim_Construction.md)<sup>([HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md))</sup> of houses. See [the lore article](Lore_Malachite.md) for general information. The only Malachite mine in Skyrim is located in [Steamscorch Mine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steamscorch_Mine), outside of [Kynesgrove](Skyrim_Kynesgrove.md) in [Eastmarch](Skyrim_Eastmarch.md).

## Glass Smithing
Glass Smithing is the third perk in the "light armor" branch of the [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill's perk tree; if you have both [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) and [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md), it is the *only* perk that does *not* apply to any sort of heavy armor. Unlocking it requires a Smithing skill of 70, and the [Advanced Armors](Skyrim_Advanced_Armors.md) perk must have already been unlocked. Glass Smithing allows both Glass Armor and Glass Weapons to be crafted.

The Glass Smithing perk also causes tempering of Glass weapons and armor to be twice as effective. This perk applies to both unenchanted Glass gear and enchanted gear ([generic armor](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md), [generic weapons](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md), or [custom](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked. Tempering of a couple other items is also improved by Glass Smithing, namely: [Chillrend](Skyrim_Chillrend.md) and [Grimsever](Skyrim_Grimsever.md).

### Glass Armor
Glass [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md) is a medium to high-quality light armor, behind armor types such as [Dragonscale](Skyrim_Dragonscale.md#Dragonscale_Armor) and [Stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md#Stalhrim_light_armor)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> light armor, although it offers worse armor per weight than [Elven](Skyrim_Elven.md) armor. It appears in leveled lists starting at level 36 (enchanted varieties at level 37). All items can be tempered using [Refined Malachite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Refined_Malachite), and having the Glass Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement. Notably, the glass shield is the lightest shield in the game that will reach maximum damage reduction against non-power attacks when using [unfortified](Skyrim_Fortify_Block.md) [Block](Skyrim_Block.md).

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds a gauntlet variant. Enchanted gauntlets can be bought with a 14 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md), while unenchanted gauntlets are craftable and share their stats with regular gauntlets and merely differ in appearance.

### Glass Weapons
Glass Weapons are the fifth best weapons, behind [Dragonbone](Skyrim_Dragonbone.md), [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric.md), [Stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, and [Ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md) weapons. They appear in leveled lists starting at level 27 (enchanted varieties at level 28). All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using 1 Refined Malachite, and having the Glass Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement. Arrows can only be smithed if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed. Glass Crossbows are only available with the [Expanded Crossbow Pack](Skyrim_Expanded_Crossbow_Pack.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

One notable weakness with glass weapons is that several of them (dagger, mace, and warhammer) do not do any critical damage.

## Notes
- Malachite Ore can be sold to [Kjeld](Skyrim_Kjeld.md) for the quest [Mine Ore](Skyrim_Mine_Ore.md).
- 1 Refined Malachite is needed to temper the dragon mask [Otar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Otar).
- 1 Refined Malachite is needed to temper the weapons [Chillrend](Skyrim_Chillrend.md) and [Grimsever](Skyrim_Grimsever.md).
- Grimsever is a one-handed Glass Sword though [Mjoll the Lioness](Skyrim_Mjoll_the_Lioness.md) is more proficient in two-handed weapons. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.3, addresses this issue. Grimsever is now a Glass Greatsword to match her skills better.
- Glass greatswords do not make noise when dropped.
- Ore veins (all types) sometimes do not respawn normally. See [Mining (Bugs)](Skyrim_Mining.md#Bugs) and elsewhere on that article and its talk page.
- The in-game material Malachite shares its name with the real life copper ore called malachite, Cu 2 CO 3 (OH) 2. The Elder Scrolls' malachite was likely named after the real world material for the blue-green hue shared.
