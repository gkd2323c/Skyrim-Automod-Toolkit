# World Interactions


There are 149 *spawn points* where an event can occur; you will not experience an event at every location every time you go near it. Some events take place over a fairly large distance, as each part of the same event takes place. The event is determined a fair distance from the spawn point which, if correctly estimated, allows one to manipulate which event occurs. The first spawn point likely to be found is between [Riverwood](Skyrim_Riverwood.md) and [Honningbrew Meadery](Skyrim_Honningbrew_Meadery.md), with its event border extending to just before the bridge out of Riverwood. Green dots on this wiki's [Skyrim Satellite Map](https://srmap.uesp.net/) can help you pinpoint where a random encounter is likely to take place.

While playing Skyrim, you may encounter many other combinations of animals and hostile NPCs fighting, however, there are only a few deliberately set to happen. Most hostile animals and NPCs have a large radius of detection that is perhaps a deliberate attempt at setting up more random encounters, saving game data by not having any additional scripts to handle.

Interactions added by [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md), [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md), and [Creation Club](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) are annotated with "DG", "DB", and "CC" superscripts, respectively.

## Reactive Events
A few non-random events will occur as a result of your actions.

- **[An enemy's gratitude](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:An_enemy%27s_gratitude)**: If you kill someone, a rival of that person may send you [one](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:WIKill04Thanks Letter) of [two](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:WIKill04Reward Letter) letters of gratitude.
- **Apologize or combat**: Assaulted <sup>[*verification needed*]</sup> NPC or relative will demand an apology.
- **Invisible Child**: If you use [magic](Skyrim_Magicka.md) near a [child](Skyrim_Child.md) they will ask you to make them invisible.
- **[Revenge, Hired Thugs](Skyrim_Revenge,_Hired_Thugs.md)**: If you kill someone, a member of their family may [hire thugs](Skyrim_Hired_Thug.md) to kill you.
- **Shout - Guard says stop**: If you [Shout](Skyrim_Shout.md) near a [guard](Skyrim_Guards.md) they may approach and ask you to stop.
- **[Steal, Thugs hunt player](Skyrim_Steal,_Thugs_hunt_player.md)**: If you steal an item, an NPC from that location may hire thugs to teach you a lesson. You do not have to be seen by the victim for this to occur.

A few minor quests randomly start after specific actions have taken place.

- **[Buy Dwarven artifact](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Buy_Dwarven_artifact)**: If you purchase a dwarven item in a shop a [letter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_from_Calcemo) may be sent via [courier](Skyrim_Courier.md) asking you to bring it to [Calcelmo](Skyrim_Calcelmo.md).
- **[Inheritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Inheritance)**: A friend has died and left you a sum of gold. A [letter of inheritance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Letter_of_Inheritance) will be sent via courier to inform you of the tragic event.
- **[Scare my Enemy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Scare_my_Enemy)**: If you assault someone bare-handed, a [letter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Request_from) may be sent from an NPC asking for a favor.
- **[Shout - learn about word wall](Skyrim_Shout_-_learn_about_word_wall.md)**: If you Shout, then a [letter](Skyrim_Letter_from_a_Friend.md) may be sent via courier to tell you about an important place.

- The dragon encounters are not in the *Random Quest Encounters* section because they are not a direct result of perceived actions during the Dragon Rising quest; rather, they are simply possible after the completion of that quest.
