## [Gargoyle](Lore_Gargoyle.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/SR-creature-Gargoyle.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Gargoyle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Gargoyle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Gargoyle.jpg) A gargoyle [Gargoyles](Lore_Gargoyle.md) are living [statue](Lore_Statues.md) creatures that have been created or animated by a wizard or [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md). They are a type of advanced [stone](Lore_Stone_Golem.md) [golem](Lore_Golem.md), typically animated with [blood magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md). They are often found guarding dungeons, castles, and ancient ruins. They can form a hard rocky skin when stationary. Some will remain frozen for long periods of time to make their prey think they are an ordinary statue, before finally bursting out of their stone skin and ambushing their victim. Since they are made of stone, they are resistant to damage to some extent. They have been known to utilize basic magic to absorb the health of those stuck by their claws. Gargoyles sometimes wield weapons such as [flails](Lore_Flail.md) in combat.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Gargoyle), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Gargoyle.md)<sup>([DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md))</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Gargoyle), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Gargoyle), [Skyrim VSE](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Enemies), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call_to_Arms:Models), [Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Gargoyle_Statue)

## [Garnetbeak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Garnetbeak)
[Garnetbeaks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Garnetbeak) are an extinct species of [bird](Lore_Birds.md). During the reign of [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md) in the late years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the **Topal Garnetbeak**, a variety of garnetbeak native to [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md), was a common dish on noble tables in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and thus a part of [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) [cuisine](Lore_Imperial_Cuisine.md). Their name was partially derived from the [garnet](Lore_Garnet.md) [gemstone](Lore_Crystals.md).

Garnetbeaks were served during the infamous Feast of [Saint](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) [Coellicia](Lore_Coellicia.md). These birds were often drowned in fortified wine, plucked just before serving, and eaten whole by diners, who typically draped cloths over their heads. While this was said to trap the vapors of the wine, it also helped contain the viscera and juices that would often violently eject from the bird as it was consumed. This practice contributed to the rapid decline in garnetbeak numbers, and by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the species was long extinct.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_The_Feast_of_Saint_Coellicia.md)

## [Gheatus](Lore_Gheatus.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/TR-cover-Tribunal_%28Original%29.jpg/200px-TR-cover-Tribunal_%28Original%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-cover-Tribunal_(Original).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-cover-Tribunal_(Original).jpg) Possible Gheatus concept art [Gheatus](Lore_Gheatus.md) (or **Gheateus**) are a [fay](Lore_Faerie.md) variant described as ancient godlike beings that once inhabited the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). According to legend, they are rock-spirits that took the form of a man or a group of men who were formed by the earth itself, and they are said to have been surrounded by a host of minor [Sylphim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sylphim).

They were once far more common and attacked the early [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), but were fought off by [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md)'s use of ancestral lore and [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md) to channel aetherial energy in the [form](Lore_Destruction.md) of lightning, ice, and fire to kill them, and they were exterminated in a few short years. Legend has it that the excavation of Wansalen was halted, and the halls abandoned, when the [Elven](Lore_Aldmer.md) miners roused and angered an ancient Gheatus, but the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) dismiss this story as a fable with no truth to it. They are now supposedly extinct, though some have sought out ways to bring them back from extinction.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_The_Faerie.md)

## [Ghost](Lore_Ghost.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c6/OB-creature-Ghost.jpg/200px-OB-creature-Ghost.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Ghost.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Ghost.jpg) A ghost **Ghosts**, also known as **spirits**, are the disembodied [souls](Lore_Soul.md) of the deceased that for one reason or another are unable to move on to their respective afterlife. They are classified as being a form of [undead](Lore_Undead.md) known as the Returned. Most often, they remain on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) because they had some form of unfinished business they wanted to see completed before they moved on. The deeds of a spirit in life can sometimes cloud their path to the afterlife. Such spirits, held back by guilt and unresolved affairs, might refuse entry into the afterlife even if they have been accepted into it, holding themselves back as long as their unresolved affairs remain. Being summoned to provide aid to the living, through lending their strength or inhabiting and animating bodies willingly, is said to be one of the ways through which spirits can resolve the issues that keep them from the afterlife, once employed by certain branches of necromancy such as that used by the Order of the Hidden Moon. In some cases these powerful unresolved emotions might cause the soul itself to crack and be wounded, allowing for a [Void](Lore_Void.md) to be created within it. Forgiving one's self can heal the Void-wound, allowing that spirit to proceed to the afterlife beyond. Ghosts can also come about when prevented from entering the afterlife. One of the known methods of this is through the use of curses, magical rituals can also be performed to summon and bind them to Nirn. One of the most prolific methods of bringing back souls as ghosts is through the use of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md).

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Ghost), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Ghost), [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Ghost), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Conoon_Chodala), [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Ghost), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Ghost), [Oblivion Mobile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/OBMobile:Ghost), [The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Creatures#Notes), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Ghost.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Ghost), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Ghost), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Ghosts), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call_to_Arms:Models), [Skyrim - The Adventure Game](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Dungeons#Encounters), [Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Ghost), Betrayal of the Second Era

## Ghoul

## [Giant](Lore_Giant.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Giant_02.jpg) A giant [Giants](Lore_Giant.md) (sometimes called the **big folk**) are a [race](Lore_Races.md) of enormous humanoids with colossal strength. They are found in the wilderness of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).

They have thick grey skin, long, powerful limbs, large hands and feet, and thick greyish-brown hair decorated with braids and beads. Additionally, most Giants have decorative scars carved into their chests. They clothe themselves in fur pelts adorned with the bones of animals and people, although many prefer a simple loincloth.

Although they tower above most races, their height can vary. Some stand between 11 and 12 feet tall, whereas others can be several times the height of the average person. Female giants, also called **Giantesses**, are less commonly seen than males by non-giants, but they have been seen in the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) and [Whiterun Plains](Lore_Tundra_Plain.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Giant social groups appear to vary, with some being part of clans led by a chieftain, some being led by powerful matriarchs, and others being small families composed of two parents raising children together on isolated mountains.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Giant), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Giant.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Giant), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Giant), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call_to_Arms:Models), [Skyrim TAG](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Skyrim_-_The_Adventure_Game), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Gloam Knight](Lore_Gloam_Knight.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/ON-creature-Shadow_Knight_Nassuphae.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Shadow_Knight_Nassuphae.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Shadow_Knight_Nassuphae.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Shadow_Knight_Nassuphae.jpg) A Gloam Knight [Gloam Knights](Lore_Gloam_Knight.md) are [wraith](Lore_Wraith.md) -like [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who are incapable of feeling sympathy but can feel hate. They are embodiments of mystery, and are sometimes referred to as "Shadows of Nocturnal". As such, they can often be found serving the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of [Shadows](Lore_Nocturnal.md). Their physical form can vary, with one example of this including their number of arms numbering from between two to six. Gloam Knights possess the ability to speak and wield magic.

Mortals are sometimes transformed into Gloam Knights, though these are "[mock-daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mock-Daedra)" different from true Gloam Knights. One such example was Veya Releth, a champion of Nocturnal.

They can also be summoned by [conjurers](Lore_Conjuration.md), and they can summon lesser [shadow creatures](Lore_Shadow.md) themselves. The [Dark Lady's Headrest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Lady%27s_Headrest), a curio linked to [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md), is said to conjure dreadful nightmares, manifesting visions resembling Gloam Knights.

## [Gnarl](Lore_Gnarl.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/SI-creature-Gnarl_%28Mania%29.jpg/200px-SI-creature-Gnarl_%28Mania%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-creature-Gnarl_(Mania).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-creature-Gnarl_(Mania).jpg) A gnarl native to Mania [Gnarls](Lore_Gnarl.md), also known as **Walking Trees**, are one of the strangest creatures of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Like the [Elytra](Lore_Elytra.md), this animated plant roams almost anywhere on the Isles. Said to be one of [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s truly unique creations, the Gnarl has the most unusual trait of being able to use magic cast upon it, and harness that power to bolster its own defense, becoming resistant to just that element for a short time.

There is much not understood about them, such as whether they have genders and how they propagate. They are believed to be caretakers of the Shivering Isles' [Root System](Lore_Root.md), severe trauma to the root system walls results in the formation of [amber](Lore_Amber.md) deposits as part of its natural defense mechanism, Gnarls clear away the amber and sometimes bring it to the surface. They also have a truce of sorts with other creatures of the Isles such as the [Baliwog](Lore_Baliwog.md) and [Elytra](Lore_Elytra.md). They seem to have some relation to [Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md), and are sometimes classified with them.

[Gnarl bark](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnarl_Bark) is often harvested from the bodies of Gnarls for use in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md). The bark is sometimes turned into gnarl-fiber to be used as shoelaces. Such laces can still be alive and have been known to attack people. With sufficient hydration and nutrients, it's possible a whole new Gnarl could be grown from just these fibers.

**Found in:** - [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Gnarl), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Gnarl Rootbender)

## [Goat](Lore_Goat.md)

A number of Gremlins and other Goblin-ken lived in certain iterations of the city of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md). Gremlins were also known to live in the Goblin Caverns of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md). Gremlins have their own [language](Lore_Linguistics.md), known as gremlin tongue, which is not usually understood by other races, though some words in it are coincidentally similar to other words in [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md). There exists a transmogrification [spell](Lore_Magic.md) capable of transforming someone into a Gremlin. This spell was a favorite trick of [Jaganvir](Lore_Jaganvir.md), the Archmage of the Mages Guild on Stros M'Kai circa [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Gremlin.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/ON-creature-Gryphon.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Gryphon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Gryphon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Gryphon.jpg) A gryphon [Gryphons](Lore_Gryphon.md) (also spelled **Griffin** or **Griffon**) are large winged creatures that live primarily on the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), within the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), though they can also be found in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and possibly the provinces of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), due to [Harpy](Lore_Harpy.md) clan leaders native to the provinces being known to wear dyed gryphon feathers. They are generally found across the mountains of Summerset, primarily around [Eton Nir](Lore_Eton_Nir.md), and specifically at the northeast point at Gryphon Aerie. They can be described as a combination of a [lion](Lore_Lion.md) and an [eagle](Lore_Eagle.md), though they bear more of a resemblance to an eagle on four legs. Some gryphons ressemble other bird species, such as the [lion ibis](Lore_Lion_Ibis.md). Gryphons are fiercely territorial. They will attack anyone who intrudes upon their nests or hunting grounds. Gryphons are predators and eat live prey. Gryphons aren't picky eaters, and will capture anyone foolish enough to traverse their hunting grounds. Young gryphons are known as fledglings. [Salamanders](Lore_Salamander.md), [deer](Lore_Deer.md) and [cows](Lore_Cow.md) are among the animals that may find themselves hunted by a hungry gryphon. Gryphons often make their nests on the sides of mountains or upon cliffs. They occasionally drag their prey back to their nests.

Gryphons are revered by the [High Elves](Lore_Altmer.md). The Altmer fear the gryphon's ferocity, but respect their strength and freedom. In some parts of Summerset, such as [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md) and [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md), the gryphon is sacred. In ancient times, many High Elves considered gryphons unsuitable to be mounted, but now the city-states of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) and [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md) are known for their tamed gryphons, which are a part of their iconography.

[Gryphon's feathers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gryphon%27s_feather) are circulated in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region and have [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) properties. The feathers can also be used to make tools such as brooms, and down feathers are used to make pillows. Gryphon feathers have an iridescent vane, and are prized by the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md). Unfertilized yolks from gryphon eggs, as well as secretions including saliva, can be used to make skincare products. Gryphon vellum is a popular material among Altmer monks for making illuminated texts, and it has a unique texture due to the feather-dimpling. In Summerset, it is illegal to collect gryphon eggs for the purpose of selling them somewhere off the island.

They are the primary domesticated herd animal of these provinces, and are not usually aggressive. Characterized by their endurance and intelligence, guar are used mostly as pack animals and beasts of burden, although certain breeds are used as mounts. Although usually associated with the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), guar have a special relationship with [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and the Dunmer traditionally employ [Saxhleel](Lore_Saxhleel.md) slaves as guar-herds and handlers. A young guar is called a calf or guarling.

## Guardian (Clavicus Vile)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7f/RG-creature-Guardian.jpg/200px-RG-creature-Guardian.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-creature-Guardian.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-creature-Guardian.jpg) A Guardian Beings only referred to as **guardians** (or just **guards**) are a little-known race of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) that serve the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) and are found in the [Fields of Regret](Lore_Fields_of_Regret.md). These Daedra possess yellow skin and dark eyes, similarly to [Skaafin](Lore_Skaafin.md). However they differ from them in that they have no mouths, are taller, more muscular, and have three large red protrusions on their back.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-52)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Guardian-53)</sup>

When [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) visited the Fields of Regret in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), Clavicus Vile held a game for him to win [Iszara](Lore_Iszara.md)'s soul at the risk of losing his own soul, where each result was hidden between two doors. In order to figure out the riddle, Cyrus had to consult two guardians; one would always lie, and one would always tell the truth.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Redguard-54)</sup>

## [Guardian Spirit](Lore_Guardian_Spirit.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/SR-creature-Sabre_Cat_Guardian_Spirit.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Sabre_Cat_Guardian_Spirit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Sabre_Cat_Guardian_Spirit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Sabre_Cat_Guardian_Spirit.jpg) Sabre Cat Guardian Spirit [Guardian Spirits](Lore_Guardian_Spirit.md) are spectral [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) found in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) that are associated with the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md). They are often tied to a specific natural landmark, such as a lake, a hill, or a copse of woods. A guardian spirit "haunts" the area it is connected to, and cannot abandon or wander far from the location. Guardian spirits usually take the form of mortal creatures, but despite appearances, these spirits were never mortal. Certain guardian spirits were known to be curious or mischievous with mortals, while others are outright hostile.

Kyne's Sacred Trials are an old [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) tradition, where the participant has to hunt and defeat several specific guardian spirits, known as Kyne's champions, in order to prove themselves as a true hunter and receive Kyne's blessing. The tradition had fallen into obscurity by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).

## Guzzard
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/ON-creature-Guzzard.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Guzzard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Guzzard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Guzzard.jpg) A Guzzard Guzzards are a species of featherless carrion [bird](Lore_Bird.md)<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-55)</sup> found in Upper [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md), and rarely in the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md). They have beaks, sharp teeth, talons, [bat](Lore_Bat.md) -like wings, and red eyes. Their tails are smooth and have no plumes. They have short crests on their heads, and spikes lining their back.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-56)</sup> The leathery wings of guzzards are said to thicken with age. They are sometimes kept as pets.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-57)</sup>
