## Steel Smithing
Steel Smithing is the first perk in the [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) skill's perk tree, and can be unlocked at the start of the game. It enables the crafting of two types of heavy armor, [Imperial armor](#Imperial_Armor) and [steel armor](#Steel_Armor), as well as [steel weapons](#Steel_Weapons).

The Steel Smithing perk also causes tempering of steel weapons and armor to be twice as effective. This perk applies to both unenchanted steel gear and enchanted gear ([generic armor](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md), [generic weapons](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md), or [custom](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked. ## Light Armor

### Imperial Light Armor
As of [Patch Version 1.9](Skyrim_Patch.md#Version_1.9), it is possible to craft Imperial light armor. The armor's rating is identical to [fur armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fur_Armor). Imperial light armor does not appear in standard armor leveled lists, but is commonly used by soldiers. Tempering, however, requires one [leather](Skyrim_Leather.md) and it does not benefit from the Steel Smithing perk.

- The studded Imperial armor has a higher value and differs slightly in appearance, but cannot be crafted or tempered. It also lacks the `Armor Material Imperial Light` keyword, which means you can't benefit from the [Matching Set perk](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md#Matching_Set). - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.0, addresses this issue. It can now be tempered with leather and benefits from the Matching Set perk. One of the official patches [<sup>(?)</sup>](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Pages_Missing_Data) adds the material keyword, but you still cannot temper it without the unofficial patch.

## Heavy Armor

### Imperial Armor
Imperial armor is the lowest-quality heavy armor in the game, with the armor rating of [iron](Skyrim_Iron.md) armor, but with the weight of steel, and only 80% of the base value of iron armor. Prior to [Patch Version 1.9](Skyrim_Patch.md#Version_1.9), only this version of Imperial armor could be crafted. Imperial armor does not appear in standard armor leveled lists, but is commonly used by soldiers.

- The Imperial helmet that can be crafted is tagged with the `Armor Material Imperial Studded` keyword instead of the `Armor Material Imperial Heavy` keyword used by the other pieces. This means that the Steel Smithing perk has no effect when tempering it, and that it's impossible to get the [Matching Set](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md#Matching_Set) bonus while wearing it. This helmet is also mis-tagged with the `Armor Light` keyword, meaning that it is controlled by [Light Armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) skill perks instead of Heavy Armor skill perks. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2, addresses this issue. The official [patch 1.9](Skyrim_Patch.md#Version_1.9) fixes the material keyword, but it will still not be governed by the Heavy Armor skill perks without the Unofficial Skyrim Patch.
- A second (full-face) version of the Imperial helmet and the Imperial Officer's helmet are both considered part of the Imperial heavy armor set, and can be worn to get the Matching Set bonus. However, neither of these helmets can be crafted at forges.

All items (including the non-craftable Imperial helmets) can be [tempered](Skyrim_Workbench.md) using one [steel ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Ingot), and having the Steel Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement.

### Steel Armor
Steel armor is another low-quality armor, but it is one notch better than iron armor and Imperial armor.

- Each of the cuirass, boots, gauntlets, and helmet comes in two variants that have identical statistics, but differ in stylistic details.
- Any combination of these items can be used interchangeably to create a set of armor that qualifies for the Matching Set bonus.

All items can be [tempered](Skyrim_Workbench.md) using one [steel ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Ingot), and having the Steel Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's steel gauntlets, a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 12 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

## Steel Weapons
Steel [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) are low-quality weapons, better than only [iron](Skyrim_Iron.md). They appear in leveled lists starting at level 2 (enchanted varieties at level 3). All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using one steel ingot, and having the Steel Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement. Ammunition can only be smithed if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed.

There is no "steel bow", only steel arrows. The bow found at similar levels is the [hunting bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow). Although the hunting bow cannot be crafted, it is generally treated as a steel weapon; however, it isn't tempered by steel ingots - instead, it is tempered with leather strips. Its tempering is enhanced by the Steel Smithing perk, making it one of the best bows in the game - the [Long Bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Long_Bow) has no tempering perk, so it can't be smithed to nearly as high a damage level but most bows that exist in the game are *slower* than the hunting bow. See further discussion [here](Skyrim_Archery.md#Detailed_Bow_Comparison).

The Dawnguard add-on adds a crossbow and an enhanced crossbow which are both treated as steel weapons. They can be smithed if you have the Steel Smithing perk, and tempering them is enhanced by the Steel Smithing perk.

Imperial Crossbows are only available with the [Expanded Crossbow Pack](Skyrim_Expanded_Crossbow_Pack.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

The [Redguard Elite Armaments](Skyrim_Redguard_Elite_Armaments.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Remnant scimitar. The ability to craft it is obtained during [Interception](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Interception)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Redguard_Elite_Armaments.md)</sup>. A guaranteed sample is found in the caravan southwest of [Sunderstone Gorge](Skyrim_Sunderstone_Gorge.md).

## Notes
- All of the [generic enchanted steel gauntlets](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md) in the game are modeled on the steel Nordic gauntlets; there are no generic enchanted steel Imperial gauntlets.
- All of the generic enchanted steel boots in the game are modeled on the steel cuffed boots; there are no generic enchanted steel shin boots.
- All of the generic enchanted steel helmets in the game are modeled on the steel helmet; there are no generic enchanted steel horned helmets.
- Imperial boots and Imperial bracers are identical in appearance to steel shin boots and steel Imperial gauntlets. Unfortunately, they have different Ref ID's from their steel counterparts, and therefore, do not count for the [Matching Set](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Matching_Set) perk if you mix them together. For example, equipping Imperial bracers and steel shin boots together won't work. Likewise, equipping Imperial boots and steel Imperial gauntlets together also won't work.
- Keep in mind that one corundum ore costs the same as one steel ingot. When smelting for steel, you will also have to buy one iron ore and so buying ore will not save you money when creating steel ingots, as it will for other materials. Mining iron ore manually is one way to save money for this purpose.
- Steel Greatswords do not make noise when dropped.
