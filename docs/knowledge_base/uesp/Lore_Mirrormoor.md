The whole place permeated with the deepest sense of loss.*
— *Journal of the Spidersmith*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spidersmith-1)</sup> Mirrormoor was the realm of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), Mistress of the Untraveled Road.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONHerma Mora-3)</sup> [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md) is a fragment of this forgotten realm.

Mirrormoor is a large desert wasteland filled with gargantuan skeletons and surrounded by an auroral sky. While Ithelia is active, it is filled by shattered mirrors and crystalline structures and the sky is fractured by cracks.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Those elements were not present in Fargrave before Ithelia's return.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Some stories have described a city of crystal buildings and many-hued courts.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mirror Portal-6)</sup>

[Shardborn](Lore_Shardborn.md) and aspects of Ithelia inhabit the realm.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO2024GR-2)</sup>

## History
Mirrormoor was forgotten by mortals and Daedra alike when [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md) was imprisoned by [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). A part of this plane was later rediscovered and was renamed Fargrave, its inhabitant unaware of the origin of the plane they live on.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ith Fury-7)</sup> [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) referred to Fargrave as "a shattered ruin of reflection and sorrow".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LA-8)</sup>

In their pursuit of ever more esoteric materials with which to customize or craft [Dwarven Spider](Lore_Dwarven_Spider.md) [constructs](Lore_Construct.md), the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) artisan known as the Spidersmith stumbled upon a realmic thread originating from Mirrormoor. The Spidersmith used shards of mirror from the realm to summon and bind [Glass Atronachs](Lore_Glass_Atronach.md) in the shape of a Dwarven Spider [vamidium](Lore_Vamidium.md) for use as mounts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spidersmith-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Metamorphosis.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Metamorphosis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Metamorphosis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Mirrormoor_Metamorphosis.jpg) Mortals changed by Mirrormoor Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) incursions from Mirrormoor spread throughout the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESO2024GRWW-9)</sup> After she retrieved her powers, Ithelia tried to restore Mirormoor and activate the [Loom of the Untraveled Road](Lore_Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road.md) an artifact hidden in Fargrave that can break the shackles of fate and rewrite history. But the [Vestige](Lore_Vestige.md) destroyed the Loom before she was able to use it.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ith Fury-7)</sup> Stories speak of a [Gleaner](Lore_Gleaners_of_Aurbis.md) who centuries before found a "mirror-portal" to Mirrormoor. Upon her escape from the realm she became troubled with the feelings of something forgotten and amiss.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mirror Portal-6)</sup>

After Ithelia was exiled to a different reality, Mirrormoor and its denizens remained known, but Ithelia was erased from everyone's memory once more by Hermaeus Mora, her plane of Mirrormoor remaining as a shattered ruin, whose inhabitants are masterless. <sup>[[10]](#cite_note-In Memory Of-10)</sup> Despite her banishment, the power of Ithelia and Mirrormoor continued to emanate across the threads of fate, marking some with its forbidden knowledge and leaving some among those who visited the realm changed. It is said that those touched in this manner could not recall who or what had changed them, retaining only dim memories of the event, like shards of glass scattered on the ground.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Memory Body-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Memory Face-12)</sup>

## Culture
Little is known of the culture of Mirrormoor outside of artifacts taken from the realm by mortals. Shardborn mages are known to wear dresses adorned with ornate sparkling crystals; examples of such dresses have been appropriated by mortals for use as evening wear.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-FEWear-13)</sup> [Glass harps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glass_Harp) are a type of musical [instrument](Lore_Instruments.md) originating from Mirrormoor but also played by mortals.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The denizens of ancient Mirrormoor wielded weapons and armor with their own unique styles.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ancient Mirror Weps-15)</sup> Mirrormoor shards are used for adornments and trinkets, such as masques<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Mirror Masque-16)</sup> or toys.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Mirror Ball-17)</sup>

## Regions and Places
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/ON-crown_store-Replica_Fate-Thread_Fracture.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Replica_Fate-Thread_Fracture.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Replica_Fate-Thread_Fracture.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Replica_Fate-Thread_Fracture.jpg) Fractured fate threads
[Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md)
- Also known as the Celestial Palanquin, this demiplane of Oblivion is a fragment of Mirrormoor.
[The Heart of the Loom](Lore_Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road.md)
- A location hidden within Fargrave that houses the Loom of the Untraveled Road, a powerful artifact built by the Daedric Prince Ithelia that can break the shackles of fate and rewrite history.
Shattered Mirror Isle
- A floating island found hanging in the skies of Mirrormoor.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SMI-18)</sup>
The Night Market
- An interplanar marketplace of Fargrave that connects to other versions of the realm. [<sup>(?)</sup>](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Pages_Missing_Data)
Mirror Portal City
- A deserted city of crystal buildings and many-hued courts, it was once discovered by a Gleaner who stepped through a fractured mirror portal but was never found again.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mirror Portal-6)</sup>
