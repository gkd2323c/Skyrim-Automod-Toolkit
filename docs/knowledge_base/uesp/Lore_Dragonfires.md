# Dragonfires


The pedestal that served as a pyre may be found in the [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), though there seems to have been no apparent connection between the ritual and the Alessian teachings. A [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) Emperor had to use the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) to light the Dragonfires in the Sublime Brazier, a sacred cauldron located in the Dragonfire Cathedral deep beneath the Imperial Sewers, said to stretch to the very heart of Nirn, and described as Akatosh's "first light". Once the Sublime Brazier was kindled, it would light the Dragonfires in the Temple of the One.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The Dragonfires could also be lit directly from the Temple of the One.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-5)</sup>

The darkening of the Dragonfires played an essential role in two [Daedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric) invasions of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The first occurred in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579), when Emperor [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) of the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) misused the Amulet of Kings in a ritual that Varen's advisor [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) had convinced him would curry Akatosh's favor and make Varen a Dragonborn. Instead, the ritual violated the Covenant with Akatosh and caused the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md), which set Nirn adrift in Mundus and made it come "unmoored from the fabric of the [multiverse"](Lore_Aurbis.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TCA-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ILTESO-7)</sup> [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) took the opportunity to begin rending the souls of Nirn's mortals from their hosts to use as fuel for the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), the process of merging Nirn with [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) using devices called Dark Anchors. Fortunately, a [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) who had been a victim of Mannimarco and Molag Bal's schemes arose to unite the warring nations of Tamriel against Molag Bal, invading Coldharbour and halting the Planemeld in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESO-8)</sup>

Molag Bal sought to destroy the Sublime Brazier to prevent the Dragonfires from ever being relit, sending a Xivkyn General known as Malivus to spearhead an attack on the temple. This attack was repelled by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), the [Drake of Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Drake_of_Blades) and Captain [Anatolius Caudex](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anatolius_Caudex) using siege weapons. While the attack was repelled, Father Egnatius, a priest of Akatosh, performed a ritual on the Brazier. Once this ritual was complete the Drake of Blades became the immortal guardian of the Brazier and the Dragonfire Cathedral, blessed with the power of Akatosh himself and forever bound to the temple.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSub Braz-9)</sup> The Vestige also later stopped an alternate form of the Planemeld ritual called the Planemeld Obverse from being carried out by the [Xivkyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xivkyn) Molag Kena at [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESO-8)</sup>

With the ascension of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) as Emperor of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) at the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the Dragonfire ritual was resumed while the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) reigned, but in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) that dynasty was broken and [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) threatened Tamriel with an [invasion](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of his own.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TAo K-10)</sup> With the assassination of Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and all of his legitimate heirs by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) cult,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BHCA-11)</sup> no known Dragonborn heir remained to relight the Dragonfires. The barriers between Oblivion and Mundus were broken, allowing stable portals to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) to be created. With each Gate that opened, the barriers between Nirn and Oblivion weakened further. This phenomenon, likened to cracks gradually developing in the [Firmament](Lore_Astronomy.md), would have eventually resulted in the barriers between worlds collapsing completely and Nirn and Oblivion becoming joined.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBMartin-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBMankar-13)</sup> When [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), the illegitimate son of the late Emperor, smashed the Amulet of Kings to summon an avatar of Akatosh, the barriers were resealed permanently, thus ending the long but now obsolete tradition.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-5)</sup> The new barrier created by Martin's sacrifice was noted to be too powerful for even a [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md), an object in the presence of which it was said even "a thousand [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) would likely dissolve", to breach for more than fleeting moments.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Lt D-1)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Excavation-14)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-OBMartin-12)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Vonos Journal-15)</sup>

## Notes
- Some sources also speak of [the aurora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aurora) or "Kyne's lights", and the [Lunar Lattice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons), also serving as boundaries that protect [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) from harmful influence from beyond.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Celestial-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-17)</sup> Some sources describe the Lunar Lattice as burning spirits that touch it with moonlight fire, causing even [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to be set aflame and fall into the darkness below when pressed against it. It is said by those sources that no spirit had ever managed to damage it until [Merrunz](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), released to serve as a weapon against the Lattice, cracked it with his axe.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Boethra-18)</sup> This might suggest the influence of the Dragonfires was potentially not the sole protection of Mundus.
