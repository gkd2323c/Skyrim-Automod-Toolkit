# Nightingale (bird)

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/SR-interior-Nightingale_Hall_02.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Nightingale_Hall_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightingale_Hall_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Nightingale_Hall_02.jpg) Banners and pedestals bearing avian imagery of the Nightingale [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/SR-book-Nightingale.png/150px-SR-book-Nightingale.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Nightingale.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-book-Nightingale.png) The crest of the Nightingales, inspired by the eponymous birds Nightingales are a type of small [songbird](Lore_Bird.md) known for their singing during the night.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TW-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NFOF-2)</sup> Some artificial creations were named after nightingales,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TW-1)</sup> and some factions, like the [Nightingale Trinity](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md), also took their name from these birds.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NFOF-2)</sup> Additionally, some bards and famous individuals were known to adopt monikers inspired by nightingales.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LTN-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB-4)</sup>

## Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9a/SR-item-Nightingale_Armor_Male.jpg/200px-SR-item-Nightingale_Armor_Male.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Nightingale_Armor_Male.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Nightingale_Armor_Male.jpg) Nightingale Armor
### Summerset Isles
Wind-up clockwork nightingales, which could fly across the room and sing when wound up, were among the creations of artisans from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). One such individual, Galarniel of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), was known for crafting them during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TW-1)</sup>

### The Nightingales and Jagar Tharn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_%28Deluxe%29.png/200px-AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_%28Deluxe%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_(Deluxe).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-npc-Jagar_Tharn_(Deluxe).png) Jagar Tharn, also known as the Nightingale The group of thieves dedicated to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), known as the Nightingale Trinity or simply the Nightingales,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Karliah-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Brynjolf-6)</sup> was named after the nightingale birds and adopted avian imagery. The armor and banners of the Nightingale Trinity bore a crest featuring the symbol of a bird embracing a circle of undetailed blackness.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NFOF-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Nightingale_Hall-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-NA-8)</sup>

Some bards adopt titles like "the Nightingale," after the songbird.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LTN-3)</sup> Among the most infamous examples was [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), who, under the guise of an enigmatic bard known only as the Nightingale—according to some sources—seduced Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) and stole the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRB-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BOQB-9)</sup> However, an alternative account suggests that the bard who actually stole the Staff of Chaos was Drayven Indoril, a master thief and member of the Nightingale Trinity whom Tharn had hired for the task, with the truth later being covered up for political reasons.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TN-10)</sup>
