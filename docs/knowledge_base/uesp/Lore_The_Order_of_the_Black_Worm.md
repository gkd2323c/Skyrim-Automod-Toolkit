The Order of the Black Worm **by [Evoker Brucille](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Evoker_Brucille)** *Mages Guild Report: The Order of the Black Worm*
---
It is as we feared, Archmagister: the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) is no mere [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) cult, but a brotherhood of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) that seeks to rival—nay, supplant—our own [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md).

Even as the Mages Guild was founded by the ex- [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md), so was the Worm Cult founded by his arch-rival and nemesis, the ex-Psijic [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md). Both were trained on the Isle of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), but when Galerion got Mannimarco expelled for his dabbling in forbidden necromantic arts, the self-styled Worm King simply relocated to continental [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and took up his heinous pursuit in earnest. He is known to have sought out hidden manuscripts describing how the [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) enthralled their [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md), ancient [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) tablets regarding the summoning of spirits of the dead, and the Crimson Book of Skulls, which [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) himself had thought destroyed. Mannimarco has bragged of consorting with [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md), and may even have bargained with the Father of [Vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) himself.

Mannimarco then set about spreading his blasphemous lore, enlisting unscrupulous mages, outcast witches, and vile [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) into his necromantic network. He dubbed his new cult the Order of the Black Worm, in emulation—or perhaps mockery—of the monastic orders of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md). In most parts of Tamriel, raising the dead was quite properly considered an atrocity, so the Worm Cult at first operated entirely as an underground, illicit organization. But Mannimarco, in addition to being a mighty necromancer, is a wise and wily diplomat, and in many jurisdictions he's persuaded, bribed, or intimidated the authorities into turning a blind eye to the Order's activities.

Worse, in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the King of Worms has even persuaded the ruling [Tharns](Lore_Tharn.md) of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to declare necromancy a legal magecraft, and the Order of the Black Worm has now taken the place of the Mages Guild as the authorized magical advisors to the Imperial Throne.

A typical cell of the Order, known informally as a "Worm Nest," is led by a necromancer who bears the title "Worm Anchorite." An Anchorite reports directly to Mannimarco, though their means of communication is as yet unknown. Beneath the Anchorite are Necromancer Adepts, Worm Warriors, and lackeys called [Worm Thralls](Lore_Worm_Thrall.md). New cultists are attracted by promises of great power for those who serve loyally, and all are sworn to secrecy on pain of Undeath.

Regional plots are addressed in the secondary reports. An additional overheard rumor, unconfirmed at this time, is that the Order has opened negotiations with the [Sloads](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md). I am seeking further information, and may know more by the next dark of the moons.

*Report investigated by Journeyman Qualitatis—may [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) guide his soul to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) —and posthumously compiled by Evoker Brucille*
