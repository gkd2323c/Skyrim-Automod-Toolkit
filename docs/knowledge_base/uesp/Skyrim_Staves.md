# Staves


**Staves** are one-handed weapons that are essentially rechargeable [scrolls](Skyrim_Scrolls.md). Anyone can use a staff, regardless of [magic](Skyrim_Magic_Overview.md) skill, and using a staff does not use any of the caster's own [Magicka](Skyrim_Magicka.md); instead, it depletes the staff's charge. However, the higher the user's skill level in the relevant magic school, the lower the charge cost to use the staff. Using staves does *not* increase your skills, nor do staves benefit from higher skill levels, with the exception of lowering the cost per use. However, staves benefit (particularly Destruction staves) from perks, so a Staff of Flames would do 10 damage per second instead of 8, if you have the Augmented Flames perk from the Destruction skill line.

Once the staff is completely discharged, it becomes unusable, and must be recharged using the [same methods](Skyrim_Enchanting.md#Recharging_Weapons) as any enchanted weapon, usually via a filled [soul gem](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md). However, the [*Soul Siphon*](Skyrim_Enchanting.md#Soul_Siphon) perk will *not* work on it.

Although staves are listed in your inventory under "Weapons", they are treated differently from other weapons. In particular, staves will not be bought or sold by blacksmiths. Staves can only be bought from spell [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md), who will stock a random, level-dependent selection of staves. Both spell and general-goods merchants will buy staves. They can also be found in random loot. Staves cannot be disenchanted.

Staves can also be enchanted using the [Staff Enchanter](Skyrim_Staff_Enchanter.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> in [Tel Mithryn](Skyrim_Tel_Mithryn_(settlement).md). Using the table will allow the user to enchant any [unenchanted staff](#Unenchanted_Staves) that was added with the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) add-on. The type of unenchanted staff determines what kind of enchantments can be applied to it. For example, an Illusion staff cannot have flames (a Destruction effect) enchanted onto it. The list of staves below does not contain any staves specific to [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md); see the [Craftable Staves](Skyrim_Staves.md#Craftable_Staves)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> section for those.

In *Morrowind* and *Oblivion*, staves required both hands to use. In *Skyrim*, they now only require one hand, and you can dual-wield them.

## Standard Staves
Staves do not simply cast the same spell you would, per the staff's name; they employ enchantments bearing effects which are always similar but not always identical to the spell they are named after. Differences between the staff and its namesake spell are noted in each staff's entry.

## Craftable Staves<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
**Staves** can be crafted at [Neloth](Skyrim_Neloth.md)'s [staff enchanter](Skyrim_Staff_Enchanter.md) after completing the quest [Reluctant Steward](Skyrim_Reluctant_Steward.md). All staves use an appropriate [unenchanted staff](#Unenchanted_Staves) as base objects and one or more [heart stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Heart_Stone). Additionally you must know the spell for the staff you want to craft. Unlike weapons and armor, however, staves cannot be renamed during the crafting process.

All of the staves you can create can also be found as random loot or sold by vendors without Dragonborn.

### Unenchanted Staves
Unenchanted staves can be purchased from [Neloth](Skyrim_Neloth.md) and are needed to craft staves. These staves existed in the original Skyrim as internally used [templates](Skyrim_Template_Items.md#Templates), but some of the statistics were changed by Dragonborn.

## Notes
- The charge costs given are the ones found in the [Creation Kit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Creation_Kit). These are the actual cost values at skill level 0, rounded down. The cost decreases with higher levels of the controlling skill, resulting in more uses before recharging is needed. At skill level 100 this results in about 1.7 times the uses shown here.
- The [Enchanting](Skyrim_Enchanting.md) perk [Soul Siphon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Siphon), which traps 5% of a creature's soul when you kill it with a weapon, does not work with staves.
- The Staff of Jyrik Gauldurson is the only lightning staff that does twice as much Magicka damage.
- Followers will not recharge staves.<sup>[*verification needed*]</sup>

## Bugs
- [Rally](#Rally) staves claim in their description that they provide extra health and stamina, but they do not actually do so. <sup>**?**</sup>
- [Summoning](#Summon) staves (e.g. Staff of the Flame Atronach, Staff of the Storm Atronach, Sanguine Rose) may not work if you have the [Elemental Potency](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elemental_Potency) perk. <sup>**?**</sup>
- The enchantment for the Staff of Inspiration doesn't match the effects given by the Rally spell. The enchantment for the Staff of Courage also doesn't match the effects given by the Courage spell. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.2.4, fixes this bug.
- Equipping the Staff of Jyrik Gauldurson is also meant to apply *Jyrik's Blessing*, which increases Magicka and Magicka regeneration. However the script to apply the effect is not actually attached to the item. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.5, fixes this bug.
- Staves that reanimate a corpse will allow for the body to remain intact after the spell wears off allowing the corpse to be reanimated an unlimited number of times. This is unlike reanimation spells, where the body will disintegrate after the spell wears off. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.9, addresses this issue. Reanimated corpses will disintegrate after the spell wears off.
- The keyword checks to make sure the Staff of Repel Lesser Undead, Staff of Turn Greater Undead, Staff of Turn Undead, Staff of Turn Lesser Undead, and Staff of Repel Undead only work on undead are missing. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.3, fixes this bug.
- The Staff of Fury is missing the secondary courage effect. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.3, fixes this bug.
- The Staff of Frostbite is missing the slowness effect normally found on Frostbite spells. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.3, fixes this bug.
- The Staff of Paralysis is missing the keyword check on dragons for the stagger effect. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.3, fixes this bug.
- The Staff of Daedric Command is missing the stagger magnitude it's supposed to have. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.3, fixes this bug.
- The Staff of Thunderbolts will cast Lightning Bolt when used, rather than Thunderbolt. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.1, fixes this bug.
- The description for the Staff of Jyrik Gauldurson doesn't say that it does 25 points of shock damage. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Halldir's Staff can soul trap [Dwarven Automatons](Skyrim_Dwarven_Automatons.md), despite the fact that they don't have souls in the first place. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.6, addresses this issue. The keywords to keep it from working on Dwemer constructs were missing.
- The Staff of Inspiration will not work when used as intended; when cast on a follower or NPC, you'll get a warning in the upper left corner of your screen, indicating that "[NPC/creature] is too powerful" for the spell, even though Rally is not a level-dependent spell in the first place and so the Rally effect will not be placed on them. <sup>**?**</sup>
