# Badger

Badgers are small omnivores that live in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOBadger Loc-1)</sup> They primarily eat burrowing rodents, though given the chance, they'll steal [chickens](Lore_Chicken.md), [bantam guar](Lore_Bestiary_B.md#Bantam_Guar) and [kwama scribs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Scrib) from farms.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOCB-2)</sup>

In Imperial cuisine, dishes of red almonds are known to be a sign of good fortune. These nuts, which must pass through the digestive tract of certain badgers, are slow to mature in flavor and thus difficult to acquire.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

## Species

### Brindle Badger
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/ON-crown_store-Heartland_Brindle_Badger.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Heartland_Brindle_Badger.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Heartland_Brindle_Badger.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Heartland_Brindle_Badger.jpg) A brindle badger Native to the Cyrodilic Heartland, the **Brindle Badger** is said to be as fierce as a [wolverine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolverine), yet as cunning as a [fox](Lore_Fox.md). A Brindle Badger adorned the banners of [Hrol](Lore_Hrol.md) when he led his knights on the Quest of Sancre Tor.

### Colovian Badger
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/ON-crown_store-Colovian_Badger.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Colovian_Badger.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Colovian_Badger.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Colovian_Badger.jpg) A Colovian badger A type of badger found in the Gold Coast. They are cranky and irritable, and thus make terrible pets.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOCB-2)</sup>

### Copperback Badger
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-pet-M%27aiq_the_Badger.jpg/200px-ON-pet-M%27aiq_the_Badger.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-M%27aiq_the_Badger.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-M%27aiq_the_Badger.jpg) A copperback badger A type of badger found in Northern Elsweyr.
