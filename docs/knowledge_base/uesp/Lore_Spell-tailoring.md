# Spell-tailoring

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/ON-node-Ancestor_Silk.jpg/200px-ON-node-Ancestor_Silk.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Ancestor_Silk.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Ancestor_Silk.jpg) Ancestor Silk Spell-tailoring is an ancient [magical](Lore_Magic.md) craft. For centuries, simple protective [spells](Lore_Spells.md) have been woven into clothing. According to Lady Eloise of [House Manteau](Lore_House_Manteau.md) this art arguably peaked during the early to mid- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), when [Empress](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) was rumored to possess a Gown of State that could repel hostile spells, ward off fatigue, and allow for [levitation](Lore_Levitation.md) at will.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LEAYQ-1)</sup>

By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the tradition remained vibrant. Although some techniques have been forgotten, spell-tailoring continued to thrive, with many high-ranking nobles boasting one or two enchanted garments in their collections as of then.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LEAYQ-1)</sup>

Many cultures have a rich history of enchanting and blessing fabrics, although their connection to the traditional art of [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md) is ambiguous. [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) leathers were sometimes blessed with the old magicks,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PRIMA-2)</sup><sup>:439</sup> while [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) were known for blessing [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) clothing,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AB-3)</sup> and braiding their blessings and prayers into their leatherwork.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-A-4)</sup> The spidersilk variety from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), comes in several grades known mostly to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). One of these, Azura's Breath, is especially receptive to enchantment and is frequently used as the magicka-infused fabric in spellwear.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LEAYQ-1)</sup>

The tradition of weaving magically imbued [Ancestor Silk](Lore_Ancestor_Silk.md) originated with the early [Cyro-Nordic](Lore_Cyro-Nord.md) tribes of the [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md) and was connected to the [Ancestor Moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md). Today, their descendants, the modern-day [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md), continue this sacred practice, creating vestments and other garments. The swishing of this material is said to produce a "resplendent ancestral chorus," which has inspired this ancient custom. The voices and hymns of one’s ancestors are captured through a unique silk-gathering ritual, which provides the material for creating..<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1-5)</sup> The silk is highly prized throughout the Empire and is exported alongside other valuable goods.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TA-6)</sup> Some people pay weavers to craft shawls inscribed with their family trees, believing that these garments hold the memories of their ancestors.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TA-6)</sup> Monks of the higher orders in the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) can bypass the magical rituals typically required to enchant this fabric and often choose to wear the moths around their necks and faces instead.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1-5)</sup>
