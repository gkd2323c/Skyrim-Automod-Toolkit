# Warp in the West

This article is about the event. For the book, see [The Warp in the West](Lore_The_Warp_in_the_West.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/DF-map-Iliac_Bay_%28labeled%29.png/200px-DF-map-Iliac_Bay_%28labeled%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_(labeled).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_(labeled).png) The Iliac Bay before the Warp in the West [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/DF-map-Warp_in_the_West.jpg/200px-DF-map-Warp_in_the_West.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Warp_in_the_West.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Warp_in_the_West.jpg) The four kingdoms of the Iliac Bay after the Warp in the West (fate of Balfiera is unknown) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/97/MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg/200px-MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Divine_Metaphysics_p3.jpg) Dwemer sketch of Numidium from *[Divine Metaphysics](Lore_Divine_Metaphysics.md)* The Warp in the West (also known as the **Miracle of Peace**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup> or the **Second Numidian Effect**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LOTE-2)</sup> was a phenomenon that took place between the 9th and 11th of Frostfall in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), when events set in motion by the death of King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) culminated in a dramatic political upheaval across [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup> The event is often described as a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) and compared to the [Middle Dawn](Lore_Dragon_Break.md#The_Middle_Dawn) of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WWYDB-4)</sup> though its effects appear to have been confined to the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

An unknown [hero](Lore_The_Agent.md), believed to have been an agent of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), obtained the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), an artifact that could control [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), the ancient weapon once used by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to conquer [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). As the Iliac Bay's major powers sought the Totem, all eventually appear to have gained control of Numidium at once: [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), and the [Emperor](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), each using it to consolidate political power.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup> Between the 9th and 11th of Frostfall, the political landscape of the Iliac Bay was radically transformed: where forty-four city-states had existed before, only four powers remained afterward—Sentinel, Wayrest, Daggerfall, and Orsinium—all swearing loyalty to the Emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LOTE-2)</sup>

In the aftermath of the Warp, the region gradually stabilized under its new borders. [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) spread across the eastern coast of the bay and later passed into the hands of Queen [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md), while [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) secured much of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) and the profitable [Bjoulsae](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) river coast under King [Gortwog](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md). [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) came to encompass much of western High Rock under King [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md) and Queen [Aubk-I](Lore_Aubk-i.md). [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) gained more land than any other kingdom in the region, extending across much of the southern Iliac Bay under [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md), becoming the dominant kingdom of the bay, while Lhotun himself was counted among the most powerful kings in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). By the late Third Era, the four kingdoms held their new borders in relative peace, and the Iliac Bay no longer had any disputed territories.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

Other uses of the Totem had less visible political consequences. [Mannimarco, the King of Worms](Lore_Mannimarco.md), used the Totem to fuel his apotheosis and become a god.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LOTE-2)</sup> The [Underking](Lore_Underking.md) is believed to have reclaimed his missing "heart"<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFWho-5)</sup> from Numidium, finally granting him the death he had long sought, and in the process creating an [anti-magic](Lore_Anti-Magicka.md) zone several miles wide.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup> Although unconfirmed, rumors persist that the agent of the Blades was killed while attempting to activate Numidium.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup>

## History

### Background
*Numidium was supposed to be a giant so big his hands could knock the moons from the sky.*
— [Anonymous](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:The_Mantella_Revealed) [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) was a colossal automaton created by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and later used by Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) in his conquest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). In order for Numidium to function, the Emperor's Battlemage, [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md) created two legendary artifacts. One was the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), which allowed its bearer to control Numidium; the second was the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md), a huge gem containing the "heart", or life force, of a powerful individual, and used to power the golem.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup>

According to legend (though [some sources](Lore_The_Arcturian_Heresy.md) dispute this version), Arctus used his own heart to power the Mantella, but objected to Numidium's further use after Tamriel was united. The Battlemage attempted to reclaim his heart; both he and Numidium were destroyed in the process, and the Totem and Mantella were lost. Unable to truly die without his heart, Arctus became the creature known as the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup>

Over the next four centuries, the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) were tasked with finding and reassembling the remains of Numidium. By [3E 401](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_401), the Totem of Tiber Septim was known to have been found, and in the possession of Lord Woodborne of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup> Sometime that year, the location of the final missing piece, the Mantella, was divined.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup>

In [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403), King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) was assassinated, though this fact was not immediately known. In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) sent an [agent](Lore_Hero.md) of the Blades to investigate, who revealed that Lord Woodborne killed the king in a scheme to seize power. The agent then recovered the Totem from Woodborne's co-conspirator, Lysandus' son [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md), the new King of Daggerfall.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup>

Ownership of the Totem was now up for grabs, and the powers of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) made their offers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup>

### Key Figures
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/DF-outro-Underking.png/200px-DF-outro-Underking.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-outro-Underking.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-outro-Underking.png) The [Underking](Lore_Underking.md), clutching the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md) *Only Crowned Heads will hold the Totem True
First is One Who Killed, But Did Not Kill Another
Second is One Who Is Two Devoured By Its Young
Third is One Who Made One Slave and Many Free
Fourth and Fifth are Two Who Compete for Mud
Sixth is One Who Brings a Home to the Homeless
Seventh is One Who Lords All, But Does Not Lord At All*
— [Nulfaga](Lore_Nulfaga.md) the sorceress Zurin Arctus placed a seal upon the Totem of Tiber Septim during its creation, only those of royal lineage or with a special supernatural affinity would be deemed worthy of wielding its power; all others would be killed if they attempted to activate Numidium.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFMantella-7)</sup> Ultimately, seven "worthy" factions emerged as potential claimants for the artifact. They were:

- [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), and [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), represented by Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md), King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) and Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), and King [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md), respectively. These three kingdoms wanted to destroy their enemies (namely, the other two kingdoms), and become the dominant power in the Iliac Bay.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup>
- [Mannimarco, the King of Worms](Lore_Mannimarco.md) desired the Totem not for Numidium's might but for the Mantella itself, intending to use its power to transform himself into a god.
- The [Underking](Lore_Underking.md) wanted to reclaim his heart, as he had failed to do centuries before, and finally be able to rest in death.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup>
- The Warlord [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog.md) wished to recreate the [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) homeland of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), and to see his people treated as equals with the other races of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup>
- Emperor [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and the Blades, through his agent Lady [Brisienna](Lore_Brisienna.md), planned to use Numidium to pacify the rebellious kingdoms of the Iliac Bay and restore Imperial order over [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and Hammerfell.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup>

A worthy individual was chosen by the mysterious agent of the Blades, who shortly thereafter was able to recover the Mantella from its resting place in [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). With everything in place, the bearer of the Totem of Tiber Septim activated Numidium.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup>

### The 9th of Frostfall
*I was traveling north-east along the foothills of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), on the 9th of Frostfall, when I felt a great heat behind me, like a fire. I turned, but I regret to say I cannot tell you what I saw. The healers tell me my eyes were burned out of my sockets*
— *[The Warp in the West](Lore_The_Warp_in_the_West.md)* — The Report of High Rock Agent 'Graylady'[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/DF-misc-Numidium.jpg/200px-DF-misc-Numidium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Numidium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Numidium.jpg) Numidium, rising from the waters of the Iliac Bay What occurred that day is unclear. Reports by the Blades are contradictory and paradoxical, as most agents were lost during the chaos. Their investigations concluded there was no plausible historical account of what exactly happened, only that a "miracle" occurred. Incredibly, in the first of many reported [distortions of time](Lore_Dragon_Break.md), it appears that each of the seven powers took ownership of the Totem—simultaneously—and each of them achieved their goals to some degree.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WWYDB-4)</sup> In fact, it seems these seven and their armies were aware of where (and when) they really were and what had actually transpired during the Warp, though few explanations proved forthcoming.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

The incident began on the 9th of Frostfall [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417) and lasted through the 11th. To some observers the days merged into one, with no explanation for the missing time; other events progressed much further than those three days could account for.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

Natural disasters ravaged the region, from earthquakes and freak sandstorms in the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) to fires and tsunamis along the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md). [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) reportedly clashed in what had been the Principality of [Gavaudon](Lore_Gavaudon.md), in one of a reported seven titanic battles waged simultaneously around the Iliac Bay that day. Another such battle was a three-way clash between [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), Wayrest, and Orsinium in [Daenia](Lore_Daenia.md) and the [Ilessan Hills](Lore_Ilessan_Hills.md). The resulting firestorm set the forests ablaze in [Ykalon](Lore_Ykalon.md) and [Phrygias](Lore_Phrygias.md), and the area surrounding the battlefield was said to have been "decimated".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

Some days after the Warp, an Imperial ambassador (and Blades agent) to the Court of Wayrest pressed the royal couple for information; Queen [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) simply replied, "We do not know." The ambassador was later able to learn from King [Eadwyre](Lore_Eadwyre.md) that a "powerful, ancient weapon" was going to be activated, and had attempted to buy it from a young adventurer before their enemies could use it against Wayrest. On the morning of the 9th, Wayrest was suddenly under attack; and simply reacted by sending its armies as well. How those armies were able to traverse the distance to the battlefields so quickly is still unexplained.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

Before the events of the 9th of Frostfall, the Iliac Bay region was comprised of forty-four independent regions. On the 11th, there were four—Daggerfall, Sentinel, Wayrest, and Orsinium—their new borders demarcated by a blackened, desolate no-man's land. The Blades reported "other strange events" also occurred as well, which somehow prevented the warring kingdoms from breaking away from the Empire (and possibly more). The fighting continued for months afterward, but those clashes were nothing compared to the raw power unleashed during the Warp in the West.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

### The "Miracle of Peace"
*The catastrophic destruction of landscape and property and the large loss of life attending upon this miracle is understood to have been 'tragic, and beyond mortal comprehension.'*
— *[The Warp in the West](Lore_The_Warp_in_the_West.md)* — Ulvius Tero In the years that followed, the Warp in the West gained another sobriquet, the Miracle of Peace. Despite the indescribable death and destruction that took place, the end result was a stable, relatively peaceful [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) loyal to the Empire<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup> and more unified than at any point in modern history.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3High-8)</sup> By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the rulers of the four kingdoms were deemed to be "ordained by [the Nine](Lore_Nine_Divines.md)", and most [Bretons](Lore_Bretons.md) had come to believe the ancient [Gods](Lore_Gods.md) of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) ([Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), [Mara](Lore_Mara.md), and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md))<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup> had scoured the land and made it whole once again.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3High-8)</sup> The Blades strongly doubted this "miracle" was of divine origin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

Sentinel grew to encompass the entire northern coast of Hammerfell, from [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md) in the west to [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) in the east. After the death of Queen Akorithi, her son [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md) took the throne; his rule was marked by reverence for the kingdom's [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) past but respect for modern Imperial ways.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3Hf-9)</sup>

King Gortwog proved to be a political and diplomatic genius, and Nova Orsinium<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LOTE-2)</sup> gained recognition by the Empire, though was still considered a part of High Rock at the time.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3O-10)</sup> Within ten years of the Miracle of Peace, Orcish citizens were found in all walks of life, even in far-flung corners of the Empire.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Morrowind-11)</sup>

The appearance of the [Necromancer's Moon](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md) in the years following the Miracle of Peace was believed by [Mannimarco's followers](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) to be the physical embodiment of his apotheosis.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

Numidium appears to have vanished from Tamriel during the Miracle of Peace,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup> and the Underking has also not been seen since.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup> It is rumored that they were both annihilated when the Underking reclaimed his heart, and that an anti-magic area was created in the area as a result, though its exact location is not known.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFCN-6)</sup> The mysterious agent who found the Totem of Tiber Septim also disappeared in the chaos.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TWITW-1)</sup>

## Notes
- The events of the Warp in the West correspond to the seven possible outcomes of the [main quest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Main_Quest) in [*TES II: Daggerfall*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall). The idea is instead of there being one "canon" ending that leads to future games, every ending happened at the same time.
- Mannimarco later appears in [*TES IV: Oblivion*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion) as an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) lich. - As the "God of Worms", Mannimarco gives an interview in [*Where Were You When the Dragon Broke?*](Lore_Where_Were_You_..._Dragon_Broke.md) in which he recalls the [Middle Dawn](Lore_Dragon_Break.md#The_Middle_Dawn).
