## Fang of Haynekhtnamet
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/SR-item-Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.jpg/200px-SR-item-Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.jpg) Fang of Haynekhtnamet The [Fang of Haynekhtnamet](Lore_Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.md) is a literal fang which was carved into a blade and fashioned into a small dagger in an exquisite [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) style. It is named after [Haynekhtnamet](Lore_Haynekhtnamet.md), a mythical [wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md) from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). According to the book *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*, the artifact was created by a score of [Nords](Lore_Nords.md) who fought Haynekhtnamet for seven days and nights before finally bringing the creature down, believing it to be a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md). One of the survivors took a fang as a trophy, and it was later carved into a dagger which mysteriously houses some of the beast's [magical](Lore_Magic.md) properties. The dagger grants the wielder the ability to shock an opponent. Other reports indicate the dagger was taken from the eponymous wamasu in the [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) region of Black Marsh in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), This jagged blade was heavily befouled and would cause disease in anyone it struck. The Fang of Haynekhtnamet has been seen occasionally by traveling heroes throughout history.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Fishing.md)</sup>, ESO

## Fangs of Sithis
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Egg_03.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Egg_03.png) A Fang in egg form The [Fangs of Sithis](Lore_Fangs_of_Sithis.md) are a set of [pre-Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) weapons, taking the form of either two large eggs, or a set of dual daggers.

The Fangs were gifted to the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) by Sithis, the Dark Husband, who in turn gifted them to the [Shadowscales](Lore_Shadowscales.md). It is said that a single Shadowscale wielding the Fangs could lay waste to entire villages not just via mass killings, but also by causing crop-failures and famine. However, most are unable to safely wield the Fangs, as they feed upon the wielder's mind and soul, causing the weak-minded either to go mad, or become mindless agents of Sithis who are devoid of free will.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)

## Fateweaver Key
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_Fateweaver_Key.jpg) Replica Fateweaver Key The [Fateweaver Key](Lore_Fateweaver_Key.md) was a powerful key shaped [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by [Azandar-al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md), an exceptionally skilled [Arcanist](Lore_Arcanist.md) and [mage](Lore_Magic.md) of world-class power and knowledge said to be comparable to that of [Morian Zenas](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md), after a lifetime of research and experimentation. The Fateweaver Key was created in order to allow Azandar to replicate the ability of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) to gaze [beyond the veil of reality](Lore_Many_Paths.md), allowing its wielder to observe his [unwoven fates](Lore_Fate.md), the "might have beens" of his life, for knowledge. The Key, however, exceeded its creator's expectations, causing his unlived fates to become "frayed" from the [universe](Lore_Aurbis.md) and become manifested within the world.

## Fearstruck
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/ON-crown_store-Fearstruck.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Fearstruck.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Fearstruck.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Fearstruck.jpg) Second Era replica of Fearstruck [Fearstruck](Lore_Fearstruck.md) was a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md). It took the form of a shield. It belonged to [Lyrisius](Lore_Lyrisius.md), a hero from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) famous for leading his army against the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) [slavetraders](Lore_Slavery.md) in their homeland of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). The shield was utterly destroyed in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by the fiery breath of a [wyrm](Lore_Dragon.md) who Lyrisius encountered after his army was routed.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:The_Story_of_Lyrisius) (mentioned only), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Fearstruck_Style) (replicas only)

## Feyfolken
[Feyfolken](Lore_Feyfolken_(artifact).md) is a supposed artifact of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), which is only known from a [series of historical fiction books](Lore_Feyfolken.md). It is a quill enchanted by a daedra by the name of Feyfolken serving Clavicus Vile at the request of Thaurbad, a sage for the Temple of Auri-El. It transfigures the writing of the user into scripture of unparalleled quality. The trade-off of wielding such an item is that the quill manipulates the thoughts of its beholder.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_Feyfolken.md)

## Firstblade
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg/200px-SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Miraak%27s_Sword.jpg) The Firstblade [Firstblade](Lore_Firstblade.md), also known as **Miraak's Sword**, is a powerful sword linked to [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). It appears as a primal green blade with writhing sclerotic [watcher](Lore_Watcher.md) tentacles and [Seeker](Lore_Seeker.md) -sinew Threads wrapped around it, which extend to attack targets and drain their stamina during combat. When it is swung it takes the shape of a tentacle and grows in length, and even when not being swung, the sword writhes with tentacles around it, with them parting around the base of the sword to expose a green eye. It can be tempered with the use of [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and [daedra hearts](Lore_Daedra_Heart.md), much like other [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Equipment.md) weapons.

It is unknown if Firstblade was seen after the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), but by the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) of Solstheim knew it as a strange sword that belonged to a renegade Dragon Priest named Miraak far in the distant past. The sword was in possession of the renegade [dragon priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md), perhaps dating back to the time before the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md), and was in his possession during his time in Apocrypha. In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), he used the sword while battling the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) at the summit of Apocrypha, who killed him and took possession of the blade.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Miraak%27s_Sword.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Firstblade_Style)

## Flame Monarch's Crown
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/70/BL-item-Flame_Monarch%27s_Crown.jpg/200px-BL-item-Flame_Monarch%27s_Crown.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Flame_Monarch%27s_Crown.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Flame_Monarch%27s_Crown.jpg) Flame Monarch's Crown The [Flame Monarch's Crown](Lore_Flame_Monarch%27s_Crown.md) is a legendary [Daedric](Lore_Daedric_Equipment.md) helmet. It offers protection equivalent to [Dragonscale](Lore_Dragon.md) armor, and possesses a myriad of powerful enchantments to protect the wearer from fire, spells, and physical damage.

The origins of the Crown are shrouded in mystery. Its name refers to a [Flame Monarch](Lore_Flame_Monarch.md), a stronger form of the more common [Flame Atronach](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md). Some claim that the helmet was worn by the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](Lore_Fa-Nuit-Hen.md) when he visited the collective realm of [Infernace](Lore_Infernace.md), home of the Flame Atronachs. Others insist that this tale is only conjecture. Known as a realm of intense heat, some believe that the flame-resistant enchantments placed on the helm were the only thing that made Fa-Nuit-Hen's visit to Infernace possible.

After Fa-Nuit-Hen returned from Infernace, skilled [armorsmiths](Lore_Smithing.md) attempted to replicate the Crown. This led to the creation of the Flame Knight's Helmet, an [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) helmet bearing similar, albeit lesser, enchantments.

The Crown is believed to have come into the possession of [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180) in exchange for [sigils](Lore_Sigils.md).

**Found in:** - [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Flame_Monarch%27s_Crown)

## Flask of Lillandril
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png/200px-RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) Flask of Lillandril The [Flask of Lillandril](Lore_Flask_of_Lillandril.md) is a magical flask that absorbs and reflects magic of all kinds. Its [anti-magic](Lore_Anti-Magicka.md) property also prevents mages from detecting its location. The flask was discovered around the year [1E 470](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_470) by a group of elves living in the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) city of [Lillandril](Lore_Lillandril.md). The flask itself was not of Elvish make, but its creator is unknown. The elves spread news of their discovery, enough to make it an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of song and legend. It became the city's symbol and was displayed on their banners.

The elves used the flask to perform favors for the barons of Summerset Isle by keeping in check the magical power of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). One legend says that a stable boy defeated the [High Wizard of Valenwood](Lore_High_Wizard_of_Valenwood.md) using only the flask. After some time, the elves who discovered the flask feared that Bosmer assassins would kill them, so they fled for [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), hoping to buy safety from the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md). The elves shipwrecked off the southern coast of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md), killing all of them according to legend, and the Flask of Lillandril was lost.

**Found in:** - [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Flask_of_Lillandril)

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Book_01.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Book_01.png) Folium Discognitum The [Folium Discognitum](Lore_Folium_Discognitum.md) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). ## Fork of Horripilation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/SR-item-Fork_of_Horripilation.jpg/200px-SR-item-Fork_of_Horripilation.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Fork_of_Horripilation.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Fork_of_Horripilation.jpg) Fork of Horripilation The [Fork of Horripilation](Lore_Fork_of_Horripilation.md) is a cursed [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), who refers to it affectionately as **Forky**. In appearance and in function, it is a mundane [iron](Lore_Iron.md) fork. However, it is imbued with an enchantment which excites and synergizes magicka around it, and can produce dramatic results when held by certain people. Sheogorath is fond of forcing mortals to use it as a weapon, in which case it curses the wielder with Fork's Wound, a magical effect which stunts the wielder's magicka or drains it completely. The term horripilation refers to the standing of hairs on end due to cold, fear, or excitement. In the context of the fork, excitement arises within Sheogorath when he convinces mortals to use the fork, while fear is evoked in mortals from just touching it, resulting in horripilation. The Fork smells lightly of roast beef.

**Found in:** - [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Fork_of_Horripilation), [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Fork_of_Horripilation), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Fork_of_Horripilation.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Staff_of_Sheogorath.md)</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Fork_of_Horripilation_Style), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Fork_of_Horripilation), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Fork_of_Horripilation)
