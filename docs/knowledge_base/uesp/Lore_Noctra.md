# Nocturnal

This article is about the Daedric Prince. For the Daedric race, see [Nocturnal Shrike](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/SR-npc-Nocturnal.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Nocturnal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Nocturnal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Nocturnal.jpg) Nocturnal ([Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)) *Night among strangers. Secrets in the dark. Nocturnal is here.*
—Nocturnal<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PCGF-1)</sup> Nocturnal is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), or "Daedric Princess",<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PS-2)</sup> whose sphere is the night, darkness, and mystery.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBOD-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRBOD-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFNocturnal Dialogue-5)</sup> She is also known as the **Lady of the Night**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFNocturnal Dialogue-5)</sup> the **Mistress of Shadows**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HWTS-6)</sup> the **Unfathomable Mistress of Shadows**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AIOS-7)</sup> **the Shadow Thief**,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> **the Unfathomable**,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-IOA-9)</sup> the **Empress of Murk**,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-NGV1-10)</sup> the **Shadow Queen**,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-WSLSil-11)</sup> the **Shadowy Hag** (while donning her [cowl](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md)),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SOH-12)</sup> the **Lady of the Twilight**,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOSN-13)</sup> the **Daughter of Twilight**,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-NGV1-10)</sup> the **Lady of Shadows**,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VPTP3-14)</sup> the **Mistress of Mystery**,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRGallus-15)</sup> the **Mother of Shadows**,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VPTP3-14)</sup> **Lady Luck**,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRGallus-15)</sup> the **Night Mistress**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TBOD-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TRBOD-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-IOA-9)</sup> the **Prince of Night and Darkness**,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SNF-16)</sup> and the **Saint of Suspicion**.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Talvas-17)</sup>

The most prestigious title Nocturnal goes by is the title of **[Ur-dra](Lore_Ur-dra.md)**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AIOS-7)</sup> which implies that she is the "eldest and most powerful of the Daedric Princes",<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lilatha-18)</sup> and is a title which the [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) claims nearly all the Royalty of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) recognize,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Census-19)</sup> and which has also been used to address Nocturnal by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) and members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-20)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Lilatha-18)</sup> The title was also used by ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) to describe [Namiira](Lore_Namiira.md), the Eldest Spirit.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TFDOF-21)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> While this belief conflicts with Nocturnal's claim over the title, it does complement her claim that she is part of the original void,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Census-19)</sup> in that she came from the [black blood](Lore_Ebony.md) of the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md) of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TFDOF-21)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> which is a piece of "primal Void".<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONGVerandis-22)</sup> Nocturnal herself claims that "before Oblivion, there was Nocturnal",<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONGeysers-23)</sup> and her followers believe she predates the current [reality](Lore_Aurbis.md) entirely.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONVey-24)</sup> [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) is similarly called an Ur-Daedra<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SOIA-25)</sup> who is "elder than Ada".<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOFAHM-26)</sup>

Nocturnal is said to be capable of shaping the night.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Fox-27)</sup> She is associated with, and often depicted alongside, jet-black [ravens](Lore_Raven.md) and [crows](Lore_Crow.md), which are said to possess the ability to speak.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ACTB-28)</sup> Nocturnal's summoning day is the 3rd of Hearth Fire,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-IOA-9)</sup> although she may be summoned at any time at a shrine dedicated to her.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-IOA-9)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-OBGF-29)</sup> Nocturnal's enemies are [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DFFactions-30)</sup> [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md),<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TDCquest-31)</sup> [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md),<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-LFTBH-32)</sup> and [Azura](Lore_Azura.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DFFactions-30)</sup>

## Religion and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nocturnal.jpg/200px-BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nocturnal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nocturnal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nocturnal.jpg) Nocturnal (tarot card)
### Khajiit
A deity known as **Noctra** exists in ancient Khajiiti mythos.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-J.27darji-33)</sup> During the cleansing of Lorkhaj's corruption, Azurah observed his Dark Heart beating like a drum, with each throb spewing out black blood that tapped the floor to form a rhythm.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TFDOF-21)</sup> Subsequently after the death of Lorkhaj, the spirit [Noctra](Lore_Noctra.md) was born of this black blood at the steps of the Void Gate.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md) battled the spirit until it knew it was not [Namiira](Lore_Namiira.md), and when the fighting subsided, Noctra was brought before [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) to be judged.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> Azurah allowed Noctra to live so long as she served Azurah and her chosen people, the Khajiit.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> However, Noctra is rebellious by nature, so she fled to the Void with a key she stole from Azurah, leading her to send the spirit of Lorkhaj to find Noctra. What resulted from this interaction was Noctra willing to offer aid to the Khajiit when called, and so tribes may ask her for luck, and darkness to hide them and the gift of silent movement.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> She also protects those who walk in the shadows.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-J.27darji-33)</sup> Summoning Noctra for nefarious acts is dangerous, as she brings the [Dark](Lore_Dark_Behind_the_World.md) with her.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDS-8)</sup> Noctra is still acknowledged by some Khajiit following the [Riddle'Thar](Lore_Riddle%27Thar.md) Epiphany.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-J.27darji-33)</sup>

According to one pre-Riddle'Thar Epiphany Khajiit account, Noctra addressed Boethra as mother and master, as she'd trained under her blade. During a battle to defend the [Lunar Lattice](Lore_Moons.md) from [Merid-Nunda](Lore_Meridia.md), [Molagh](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and [Merrunz](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), Noctra, after learning from Azurah's vanquishing of [Vaarmina](Lore_Vaermina.md) and steeling herself to leave all she had behind, used the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md) on herself in order to stop Merid-Nunda from accessing the [Aether Prism](Lore_Sun.md), this caused her form to become as "shadow and cloth, a cloak of darkness" and "grow mighty, taller than the rest" of the deities present, until it went on to blend with the night itself, shrouding the Aether Prism and foiling Merid-Nunda's attempt as the burning light that was covering everyone present receded. It is said that in this act, with the Key, Noctra could say that she was not Namiira at last.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Blade-34)</sup>

### Reachfolk
Nocturnal is seen by the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) as the **Mistress of Shadows**, an ever-watchful spirit that has guarded them since the beginning.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONMOR-35)</sup> She is considered the spirit of the night, and the Reachfolk will give her offerings in the hopes that she will send her [crows](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md) to watch over them.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ONFialyn-36)</sup>

### Other Myths
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/OB-item-Hrormir%27s_Icestaff.jpg/200px-OB-item-Hrormir%27s_Icestaff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Hrormir%27s_Icestaff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Hrormir%27s_Icestaff.jpg) Hrormir's Icestaff exists, perhaps giving credence to the *Song of Hrormir* The *[Song of Hrormir](Lore_Song_of_Hrormir.md)* attributes Nocturnal as a "Shadowy Hag" who hid her hideous form with her gray cowl. In the epic narrative, a soul of a man named Darfang was captured by Nocturnal, and Darfang was being puppeted to fight his companion Hrormir. Seeing his friend's grief, Hrormir bargained to take his place as Nocturnal's plaything to free his friend's soul.

Hrormir exploited wordplay in his oath to make his loyalty non-binding, and then stole her cowl from her and brought back light to the lands. In the morning after Hrormir had bedded Nocturnal, everyone saw her hideous form free from obstruction. In exchange for the cowl, Nocturnal released Hrormir and accepted her defeat.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SOH-12)</sup> The veracity of the story is unknown, but an artifact mentioned in the story named [Hrormir's Icestaff](Lore_Hrormir%27s_Icestaff.md) exists.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-HI-37)</sup>

The *[Fable of the Gryphon](Lore_Fable_of_the_Gryphon.md)* tells the story of Zariah and the talking fox Gray, who sought to steal the treasure of the [Gryphon Luminary](Lore_The_Gryphon.md) and blame the theft on Nocturnal. Their scheme was overheard by [Crow Daedra](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md), messengers of Nocturnal, who hunted the thieves. Zariah and Gray evaded the crows and Nocturnal's shadows and gathered the three pieces of the Gryphon's treasure from [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), from beneath [Forelhost](Lore_Forelhost.md) and from a tomb in the shadow of [Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md). Finally the duo were confronted by the Gryphon, who was doubtful that Nocturnal would steal his treasure as she didn't know him. As the shadows and crows closed in and Zariah attempted futilely to fight them off, a desperate Gray revealed the truth. He was originally Nocturnal's pet who had escaped, and she sought to take him back to the Evergloam. They'd sought the Gryphon's treasure only as the means to live a life where they no longer had to run, and would return it and more in exchange for protection.

The Gryphon deemed Zariah, courageous and loyal, worthy of his power, he raked his claws through the shadows, and granted her powerful [magic](Lore_Magic.md). The Gryphon opened the gate to his realm and moved to allow the duo through, but it was too late for both to be saved. As the shadows closed in Zariah used the power the Gryphon had granted her to conjure a tunnel of [starlight](Lore_Varliance.md) that pushed back the dark so Gray could slip through, and to than seal the gate behind him. Though safe in the Gryphon's domain, Gray keened and clawed at the door for his friend until his paws bled.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Fable Gryphon-38)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/SR-interior-Twilight_Sepulcher_04.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Twilight_Sepulcher_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Twilight_Sepulcher_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Twilight_Sepulcher_04.jpg) The Ebonmere *The singing remains, but the song is gone. No tune, no music, but whispers set to rhythm. Serve Her, be shadow, be whispers. Creeping, crawling, but I can't escape. I'm caught in the darkness, tar which clings to my clothes. I speak but am silent. I see but am blind. Blind, so blind, to nothing but Her will.*
— [Romien Garvette](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Romien_Garvette)<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SAW-39)</sup> Nocturnal's servants come in a variety of flavors. Some worship her as thieves, and see her as the patron of all thieves.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-NGV1-10)</sup> Some such groups include the [Nightingales](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md), a trio of mortals bound to protect the [Ebonmere](Lore_Ebonmere.md) within the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md). It is thought that after their tenure guarding the Sepulcher the [spirits](Lore_Ghost.md) of Nocturnal's Nightingales cross into the Evergloam and become one with the shadows, becoming part of the darkness itself. In this form the Nightingale spirits enter [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) through the Ebonmere, the conduit to Nocturnal's realm of Evergloam, and guide thieves by placing their hand in theirs and granting them their uncanny luck, being responsible for events such as a novice picking an impossible lock or someone who is blind suddenly turning to face the thief reaching for their pocket. Because of this role Nocturnal is also known as Lady Luck.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-NGV1-10)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SRGallus-15)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SRKarliah-40)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/ON-creature-Shadow_Veya_02.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Shadow_Veya_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Shadow_Veya_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Shadow_Veya_02.jpg) One of Nocturnal's devoted followers, transformed into a [Gloam Knight](Lore_Gloam_Knight.md) Some worshipers find Nocturnal mystifying. Though they strive to understand her, Nocturnal's true purpose eludes them, and attempting to understand the Daedric Prince is a futile task.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-IOA-9)</sup> To understand Nocturnal is to negate her, and the Daedric Prince is not simply going to pull back her shroud of darkness to reveal her intentions to anyone.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-IOA-9)</sup> Others are drawn into her service through subtle, but not wholly unkind manipulation. Lonely individuals who are within proximity of Nocturnal's adherents may begin to dream about Evergloam and hear her voice in their head if she notices them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VPTP3-14)</sup> Nocturnal's Earl from the time of the Daedric Triad came to serve her this way.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VPTP3-14)</sup> Nocturnal uses mortal emotion to her advantage, using kindness and empathy to draw in the vulnerable.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VPTP3-14)</sup> She speaks to select mortals in their dreams, listening to them when they speak and comforting them when they're upset.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VPTP3-14)</sup> In the case of Earl Tundilwen in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Nocturnal inserted herself into her servant's life as a surrogate mother figure, and granted her power in the [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-VPTP4-41)</sup>

One particularly fervent cultist's accounts of her invasion of the Clockwork City are poetic and obsessive.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SAW-39)</sup> The account speaks of singing shrikes and grotesque imagery.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SAW-39)</sup> The author, Romien Garvette, often repeats phrases and claims that they are unable to think of anything but "Her" will.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SAW-39)</sup> Romien Garvette describes Nocturnal as all-encompassing.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SAW-39)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-CM57ESS-42)</sup> Whether or not this experience is universal is unclear. It is obvious that this particular case became very attached to their mistress, but the account gives outsiders some insight to how Nocturnal's influence may affect those who give themselves to her.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg) A Witchmother transforming a supplicant into a Reaper [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md) is pervasive throughout Evergloam, and is utilized by her worshipers and her Daedric minions.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ESOSOSS-43)</sup> Portals of pure shadow called Gloaming Gates are used to transport cultists and Daedra from Evergloam to select locations on Nirn. Gloaming Gates channel Nocturnal's power, and transport through the Evergloam allows her cultists travel great distance without fear of being detected.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-CGD-44)</sup> Barriers of fluid shadow can be used to prevent access to areas, but these barriers can be negated by shining bright light on them,<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-PLPWSL-45)</sup> or by casting spells designed to banish shadows.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-PLPUTD-46)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PLPBarrier-47)</sup> Nocturnal may transform her followers into [Gloam Knights](Lore_Gloam_Knight.md) upon their death.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOGK-48)</sup>

The [Witchmother coven](Lore_Witchmothers.md) is the name for a loose coven of powerful [witches](Lore_Witches.md) known as Witchmothers who venerate Nocturnal and carry out her bidding. The number of coven members is unknowable and ever-changing, and they take members from all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Witchmothers [scry](Lore_Sight.md) the Evergloam for disturbances that could threaten [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and also monitor dangers that emerge following Nocturnal's Summoning Day. One such danger was the [Crowborne Horror](Lore_Crowborne_Horror.md), a powerful [Wraith-of-Crows](Lore_Wraith-of-Crows.md) whose arrival was heralded by the appearance of dark feathers on various creatures, and which threatened all of Nirn in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Vanima-49)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Taerma-50)</sup>

## History

### Second Era

#### The Daedric Triad
Around [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230), a [Triad](Lore_The_Triad.md) of Daedric Princes— [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and Nocturnal—made a deal with the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) K'Tora to acquire the Heart of Transparent Law—the Stone of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md). K'Tora used [mind magic](Lore_Mind_Magic.md) to make the Psijic leader [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md), steal the Heart. Trying to foil K'Tora's plan, Iachesis made a deal with Nocturnal to learn a spell with which to hide the Heart from everyone, which was cast in a language that could only be used by mortals. Unbeknownst to all, Iachesis hid the Heart within himself—but soon forgot these events, due to K'Tora's magic.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESOSummerset-51)</sup> Shortly thereafter, the Psijics—along with the entire island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) —disappeared, around the time of the founding of the Mages Guild, remaining hidden for a long time.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESOSummerset-51)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOPO-52)</sup>

##### The Clockwork City
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie.jpg) Nocturnal's shadows rushing forth to consume the Throne Aligned as Luciana struggles to hold them back [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG/200px-ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Where_Shadows_Lie_2.PNG) The freed Sotha Sil banishes Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City The Triad's plan resumed around 2E 582, Nocturnal tried to take control of the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). Nocturnal's forces invaded the City and she summoned Sotha Sil's [Shadow](Lore_Shadow.md), who replaced him as ruler. Meanwhile, one of the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostle.md), Chancellor Gascone Dusant, sought to deliver the Skeleton Key, which had been accidentally found by a Factotum beneath the Brass Fortress, to Clavicus Vile, only for the Key to be stolen by the [Blackfeather Court](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md). The [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), assisted by [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) and the Clockwork Apostles, unveiled the plot, but were unable to reclaim the Skeleton Key before it was taken by Sotha Sil's Shadow.

Using the key, Nocturnal and the Shadow attempted to seize control of the Clockwork City by unlocking the Throne itself, but were stopped by the Vestige, Divayth Fyr, and Proctor [Luciana Pullo](Lore_Luciana_Pullo.md), who rescued Sotha Sil by defeating his Shadow and repelling Nocturnal's darkness through a special light spell Sotha Sil had once taught Luciana. Once freed Sotha Sil banished Nocturnal's shadow from the Clockwork City. Sotha Sil then gave the Skeleton Key to Divayth Fyr for safekeeping.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ESOCWC-53)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-20)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Shadow Sil-54)</sup>

##### The Crystal Tower
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/51/ON-creature-Shadow_Veya.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Shadow_Veya.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Shadow_Veya.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Shadow_Veya.jpg) Nocturnal empowering her champion atop the Crystal Tower [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg) The Crystal Tower in the Fields of Regret while under Nocturnal's control [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-item-Dawnbreaker_%28corrupted%29.jpg/200px-ON-item-Dawnbreaker_%28corrupted%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Dawnbreaker_(corrupted).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Dawnbreaker_(corrupted).jpg) The corrupted Dawnbreaker, Duskbringer This did not stop the three Princes from attempting to execute their plan to take control of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) using the Heart of Transparent Law, which Ritemaster Iachesis had hidden from them, with the ultimate goal of remaking reality itself. In preparation for the completion of their plan, the Triad invaded Meridia's realm, the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md), and she was imprisoned within Nocturnal's darkness to prevent her from interfering further. Nocturnal also corrupted [Dawnbreaker](Lore_Dawnbreaker.md) into the dark blade Duskbringer<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Death Recaps-55)</sup> in order to deprive Meridia's agent, the [Golden Knight](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md) of his weapon.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Nocturnal-56)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Meridia-57)</sup> After they tricked Iachesis into revealing the Heart's location within himself, Nocturnal broke through his protective wards, which had held off the other two Princes, and killed him.

Nocturnal then betrayed Clavicus Vile and Mephala by banishing them from her realm and taking the Heart for herself. With it she briefly took control of the Crystal Tower, hoping to use its properties of existing in every [reality](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) simultaneously, enhanced by the life force of Nirn which she'd drain through the use of a secret life draining technique she'd stolen from Sotha Sil's Clockwork City, to achieve omnipresence in the entire [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). In this manner she'd absorb the power of the other Daedric Princes as she invaded their [realms](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md), thus gaining infinite power through the infinite connections made, and ultimately unravel reality itself to wait for the next one to arise. Nocturnal's shadow enveloped the Tower and transformed the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) inside into shadows, while she attempted to repair Transparent Law by reconnecting it with its Heart, making the Tower whole to complete her plan.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOPO-52)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Sotha_Sil-20)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ONLey-58)</sup>

At the same time, her forces started invading the realms of the other Princes.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Barbas-59)</sup> However, her plans were foiled by the Vestige and the Psijic Order, assisted by the two betrayed Princes and the champion of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md). After Nocturnal's Earl was defeated, the Prince attempted to consume the Vestige and the Golden Knight with her shadows. It was than that the true nature of Meridia's champion as a living [Light of Meridia](Lore_Light_of_Meridia.md) manifested, allowing him to repel the darkness, and to restore Dawnbreaker by sacrificing himself. The Vestige then used [Dawnbreaker](Lore_Dawnbreaker.md) to restore Transparent Law which banished Nocturnal, allowing the Heart to be returned to the Crystal Tower. Nocturnal's banishment from the Tower also restored Meridia.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Darien-60)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ESOSummerset-51)</sup>

#### Other Affairs
Following the death of the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) K'Tora, Nocturnal took control of the Abyssal Cabal's [Geysers](Lore_Abyssal_Geysers.md), a phenomena which if left undisturbed would've sunk Summerset into the sea, for some time.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONGeysers-23)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Indirim-61)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Grog-62)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Battlereeve-63)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Sinking-64)</sup> The island of Summerset in exchange for the Heart having been the deal the Triad originally made with the Sea Sload.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-KTora-65)</sup>

Relating to the Sea Sload, Nocturnal may have had some minor involvement with the Sea Sload sorceress Z'Maja's conquest of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ESOZ.27Maja-66)</sup> Z'Maja possessed an amulet that granted her powerful shadow magic, which allowed her to kill countless citizens via a dark mist that enveloped the city, enrapturing the inhabitants and slowing their hearts. It also allowed her to shadowy copies of Summerset's protectors, the [gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md) -riding [Welkynars](Lore_Welkynars.md).<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-WPA-67)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-WOTW-68)</sup>

That same year in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), Nocturnal tasked the Vestige with retrieving the [Shade Sickle](Lore_Shade_Sickle.md). The artifact allowed the user to split a person from their shadow and turn them into a thrall. The Sickle had been stolen from the Shrine of Nocturnal by thieves hired by the [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) Tumma-Maxath. The Vestige found the artifact in the necromancer's lair in [Bloodrun Cave](Lore_Bloodrun_Cave.md) and returned it to the shrine.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ND-69)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-SON-70)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/OBR-quest-Nocturnal.jpg/200px-OBR-quest-Nocturnal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-Nocturnal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-Nocturnal.jpg) Nocturnal's shrine in Cyrodiil (Oblivion Remastered) In the events leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), an [unknown agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) was tasked by Nocturnal to kill a mage in their stronghold in return for the Skeleton Key. However, the mage had already had a contingency plan in case the Daedric Prince had sent someone to slay them. They created a fake artifact ([Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md), [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), or the [Warlock's Ring](Lore_Warlock%27s_Ring.md)) that functioned identically to the true artifact to tempt the agent with, though it would degrade after several days; enough time to flee when the illusion was broken. Accounts conflict if the agent took the mage up on the offer and was bamboozled, or if they slayed the mage and received the Key in return.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-DFNocturnal-71)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Eye of Nocturnal was stolen from Nocturnal's shrine in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) region. Nocturnal tasked the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) with recovering it. The thieves, an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) couple in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), hid it in a flooded cave in [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The Hero of Kvatch returned it to Nocturnal and was rewarded with the Skeleton Key.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-OBNocturnal-72)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/SR-quest-Trinity_Restored.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Trinity_Restored.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Trinity_Restored.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Trinity_Restored.jpg) A new Nightingale Trinity transacts the oath with Nocturnal [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/SR-npc-Mercer_Frey.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Mercer_Frey.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Mercer_Frey.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Mercer_Frey.jpg) Mercer Frey, traitor to Nocturnal Some time before [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Skeleton Key was stolen from the Twilight Sepulcher by [Mercer Frey](Lore_Mercer_Frey.md), a corrupt Nightingale. Its long-term loss resulted in a decline of the Skyrim branch of the Thieves Guild due to bad luck without Nocturnal's influence. The guild's influence declined until their only presence was in the Ratways beneath [Riften](Lore_Riften.md). [Karliah](Lore_Karliah.md), a Nightingale, apologized to Nocturnal for failing to keep the key safe, promising to return the key to its rightful place, and brought her two new Nightingales as a gift, one of them being the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md).

Nocturnal was grateful for her new Nightingales and warned Karliah to not fail her again. Mercer used the Key to bring himself luck, but he was eventually confronted by the Nightingales beneath the Great Statue of [Irkngthand](Lore_Irkngthand.md). Mercer was killed, and the Last Dragonborn brought the Key back to the Sepulcher, reopening the Ebonmere. Nocturnal rewarded the Dragonborn with three Nightingale powers of their choosing.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-SRTG-73)</sup>

## Artifacts
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg/150px-OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.jpg) The Gray Cowl of Nocturnal (Oblivion)
### The Gray Cowl of Nocturnal
The [Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md) (or the **Shadow Queen's Cowl**) is a daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that once belonged to Nocturnal. It takes the form of a dark leather cowl, which obscures the face of the wearer. Nocturnal is revered as a god by thieves across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Her reputation as the Mistress of Shadows has sometimes led thieves to attempt to steal an item from her to prove their greatness. As Nocturnal is usually depicted wearing a cloak and a cowl, it is around these two items that legends have arisen. The story of the theft of Nocturnal's cloak is probably fiction, as is at least one story of the theft of the cowl, but the cowl is known to have left her possession. It appears to have been stolen by [Emer Dareloth](Lore_Emer_Dareloth.md), a guildmaster of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), although a curse bestowed upon the cowl by Nocturnal meant that the name of cowl's owner is lost to mortal memory. *"Whosoever wears it shall be lost in the shadows. His true nature shall be unknown to all who meet him. His identity shall be struck from all records and histories. Memory will hide in the shadows, refusing to record the name of the owner to any who meet him. He shall be known by the cowl and only by the cowl."* This curse was broken through the use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md), causing history to be rewritten as a result, as such was the strength of the curse lifted that lifting it [altered time](Lore_Time_Magic.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg/150px-SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg) The Skeleton Key (Skyrim)
### The Skeleton Key
The [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md) (also called the **Skeleton's Key**) is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Nocturnal.

In appearance, the Skeleton Key doesn't always take the form of a key, and sometimes manifests as a lockpick instead. In its key form, it can be used to unlock any lock. As a lockpick, it is nigh unbreakable and can get past even the toughest locks. The two limitations placed on the Key by wizards who sought to protect their storehouses were that the Key could only be used once a day and it would never be the property of one thief for too long, eventually disappearing.

The artifact functions as a tool for "unlocking" all things, including portals, hidden potential, and other unknown possibilities. Its ultimate function, however, is to unlock and hold open the Ebonmere, a portal to Nocturnal's realm of [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md), located in the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The [Nightingales](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md) are tasked with guarding the Sepulcher and retrieving the Key should it be stolen. Unfortunately, the Prince is said to allow the Skeleton Key to be stolen or lost periodically, whether by purpose or apathy.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg/150px-ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Bow_of_Shadows.jpg) The Bow of Shadows (ESO)
### The Bow of Shadows
The [Bow of Shadows](Lore_Bow_of_Shadows.md) is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that, according to legend, was forged by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Nocturnal. The legendary ranger, Raerlas Ghile, was granted the Bow for a secret mission that failed. The Bow was lost, though Raerlas is said to have used it to take scores of his foes down with him. The Bow is said to grant the user invisibility and increased speed.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/OB-item-Eye_of_Nocturnal.jpg/150px-OB-item-Eye_of_Nocturnal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Eye_of_Nocturnal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Eye_of_Nocturnal.jpg) Eye of Nocturnal
### The Eye of Nocturnal
Another item of note which is associated with Nocturnal is the Eye of Nocturnal. Little is known of the item's origin or abilities other than it was stolen by two Argonians in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) and subsequently returned to the Prince in exchange for the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md). Nocturnal described herself as being able to "see" from the Eye, stating that the thieves who stole it were "seen" by the artifact.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PCGF-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg/150px-ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Shade_Sickle.jpg) Shade Sickle
### Shade Sickle
The [Shade Sickle](Lore_Shade_Sickle.md) is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) -made [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) with the ability to separate the living from their [shadows](Lore_Shadow.md). This leaves its victims fated to gradually become hapless beings akin to thralls. Though mortal in origin, it is now a daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Nocturnal, who was given the weapon at one point after its potential was realized. Nocturnal considers it a powerful instrument of her will. Despite its common appearance, the blade is preternaturally sharp, and those who gaze at it experience a deep sense of foreboding.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-misc-Z%27Maja%27s_Amulet.jpg/150px-ON-misc-Z%27Maja%27s_Amulet.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Z%27Maja%27s_Amulet.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Z%27Maja%27s_Amulet.jpg) Z'Maja's Amulet
### Z'Maja's Amulet
**Z'Maja's Amulet** amulet was an artifact Nocturnal gifted to the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) Z'Maja that granted her powerful shadow magic, which allowed her to kill countless of [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) citizens via a dark mist that enveloped the city, enrapturing the inhabitants and slowing their hearts, and to make shadowy clones of Summerset's protectors, the [gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md) -riding [Welkynars](Lore_Welkynars.md). Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Z'maja was slain and Cloudrest was freed, and her amulet was taken for study by the Olorime of the Welkynars.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-WPA-67)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ESOZ.27Maja-66)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-WOTW-68)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Prima-74)</sup><sup>:146</sup>

## Evergloam
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/ON-load-The_Shadow_Cleft.jpg/200px-ON-load-The_Shadow_Cleft.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Shadow_Cleft.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Shadow_Cleft.jpg) Evergloam The [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by Nocturnal, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Shadow. It is a realm of perpetual twilight, and the "cradle of shadow". The Evergloam exists adjacent to all other [realms of reality](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) simultaneously; it can almost be glimpsed when one turns and looks quickly while in the dark. It consists of a primary plane and several pocket realms, including the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md) and the [Crow's Wood](Lore_Crow%27s_Wood.md). These realms are thought to be constantly shifting, and are perceived differently by different mortals. It is filled with forests, whose trees cast long shadows over a purple landscape. The land is dotted with crumbling buildings and sunken crypts.

### Servants
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/BT-concept-Wraith_of_Crows.webp/200px-BT-concept-Wraith_of_Crows.webp)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Wraith_of_Crows.webp) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-concept-Wraith_of_Crows.webp) A Wraith-of-Crows, a Daedra which serves Nocturnal Daedra that are known to serve Nocturnal include [Nocturnal Shrikes](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md) also simply called Nocturnals or Shrikes for short,<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-BS-75)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-CWCShrike-76)</sup> [Grievous Twilights](Lore_Grievous_Twilight.md),<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ESOGTL-77)</sup> [Gloam Knights](Lore_Gloam_Knight.md),<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOGK-48)</sup> [Wraiths-of-Crows](Lore_Wraith-of-Crows.md)<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-SOSS-78)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-EON-79)</sup> [creatures made of shadow](Lore_Shadow.md)<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-ESOGloam Hag-80)</sup> and [Crow Daedra](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md).<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-CWCBlackfeather-81)</sup>

Intelligent shadow creatures such as Gloam Hags can cooperate with Shrikes to cast scrying spells to spy on other Daedric cults and Nocturnal's enemies.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-RDJC5-82)</sup> When they see a journal or a document of interest, they may cast a Duplication spell to create a copy.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-RDJC5-82)</sup>

One [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) in the form of a [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md), known as the Crow Mother, describes herself as a "Nether-Niece" of Nocturnal. She rules over the pocket realm of Crow's Wood, populated by [intelligent crows](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md).<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-ONCrow Mother-83)</sup>

## Notes
- The ruins of [Ebon Stadmont](Lore_Ebon_Stadmont.md) are carved with a mysterious language that resembles but isn't [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md). This language, older than any other language on Nirn, has been theorized to predate even the language of Oblivion. Leythen, Earl of Mephala and chief linguist of the Psijic Order, also claimed the Ebon Standmont language and the Daedric power it was linked to were far older than his Prince, Mephala. The language has arcane properties which can only be used by mortals, as recitation of the script has transported people and objects through [time](Lore_Time_Magic.md) and cast the spell with which Ritemaster Iachesis hid the Heart of Transparent Law. Nocturnal provided Iachesis a spell in this language, and the inscriptions in Ebon Stadmont are carved on structures featuring crows, which are symbols of Nocturnal. This might support Nocturnal's claims of being older than Oblivion and other Princes. <sup>[[83]](#cite_note-Forte-84)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ONLey-58)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-TES-85)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-ESOPO-52)</sup>
- Nocturnal may be related to Noctyr-a from the story of [Ghraewaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Ghraewaj), the fabled origin of [harpies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Harpy) in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-DFGH-86)</sup>
- One myth claims that [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md) stole the [Ring of Khajiiti](Lore_Ring_of_Khajiiti.md) from Mafala on a [moonless](Lore_Moons.md) night which allowed him to enter Oblivion. There he found Mafala, Azurah, and a third Daedric Prince arguing over who claimed dominion over the night. The identity of the third Prince is not revealed. Given her sphere it might be Nocturnal.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-87)</sup>
- [Nocturnal Lieutenant](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md) [Jaciel Morgen](Lore_Jaciel_Morgen.md) was ruler of the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Evergloam.md), a pocket realm of the Evergloam. She claimed the other Nocturnals would go to war with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) over his invasion of the realm and that in the end the sky and earth would crack, all mortals would perish, and Dagon would lie defeated. As Shade Perilous is a pocket realm of Evergloam and Nocturnal Shrikes are generally associated with Nocturnal, she might've been suggesting a war taking place between Nocturnal and Dagon.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-Jaciel-88)</sup>
