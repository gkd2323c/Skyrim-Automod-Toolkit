[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) at night Sunspire is a [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) temple found in the region of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), within the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). Sunspire is considered holy ground to the Khajiit across Northern Elsweyr. At the summit of Sunspire, the Dragon King of the Cats, [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) is said to have defended the people from the star-made knight, [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), who slaughtered the Khajiit in a blind rage. Afterwards, the devout constructed Sunspire and paid homage to Alkosh.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-1)</sup>

## History
Sunspire's construction dates back to at least the mythic times of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Pelinal Whitestrake fell into one of his mindless rages and mistook the Khajiit people as another strain of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md). It has been speculated that Whitestrake's slaughter against the Khajiit could have very well ended the entire race. Survivors of the slaughter attempted to climb the central mountains and seek refuge, but Pelinal was able to follow and arrived at the summit, wrestling with the mountain through sheer ferocity "until he too stood above it". As the legend goes, Alkosh himself intervened, he fell from the heavens as a shooting [star](Lore_Stars.md) and shouted Pelinal out of Elsweyr with a roar that split the sky. The confrontation resulted in a [Time Wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md) at the summit of the mountain, where it has remained since.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-1)</sup> The mountain later hosted the temple of Sunspire, which became the preeminent place of worship in Anequina and the sixteen kingdoms of old Elsweyr.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOShalani-2)</sup> This lasted for several years until [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) revealed the [Riddle'Thar Epiphany](Lore_Riddle%27Thar_Epiphany.md) in [2E 311](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_311), and the [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](Lore_Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance.md) in [Rawl'kha](Lore_Rawl%27kha.md) gained prominence over Sunspire.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOLoading-3)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a0/ON-creature-Nahviintaas_%28Time_Wound%29.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nahviintaas_%28Time_Wound%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nahviintaas_(Time_Wound).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nahviintaas_(Time_Wound).jpg) The Sunspire Time Wound [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg/200px-ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nahviintaas.jpg) Nahviintaas confonting adventurers atop Sunspire By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Sunspire was practically empty, with very few acolytes and sages, and in serious disrepair. The deity of cosmic order, the [Riddle'Thar](Lore_Riddle%27Thar.md) had replaced Alkosh as the most revered god in the province of Elsweyr. The leader of the temple was Moon-Bishop Azin-jo, who was in his twilight years. At this time, ancient [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) had appeared in Northern Elsweyr, reaping the land, following the release of the great dragon [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md) and his dragon followers from the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTHOC-4)</sup> The golden dragon, [Nahviintaas](Lore_Nahviintaas.md) approached Sunspire with his contemporaries, [Lokkestiiz](Lore_Lokkestiiz.md) and [Yolnahkriin](Lore_Yolnahkriin.md). They posed as Alkosh, [Jone](Lore_Jone.md), and [Jode](Lore_Jode.md) respectively. Azin-jo openly accepted them and preached across Anequina about the return of "Alkosh." A wave of new followers came and rebuilt Sunspire. More adepts preached the return of "Alkosh" across Northern Elsweyr.

However, Nahviintaas' true goal was to use his [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to tear the Time Wound wider in order to cause a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) which he intended to use to "reclaim all that is and will be" and restore the "natural order" by "correcting the mortal mistake".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-1)</sup> While posing as gods the dragons would use their Voice to imbue their deceived followers with [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) power, transforming them into creatures composed simultaneously of ice and flame while also making them thralls of the dragon's will.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOMojha-7)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-1)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOJone Jode-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOIce Fire-9)</sup> The dragon's presence would also have an effect on the environment and conditions of the mountain, the dragon Yolnahkriin resided in the caldera, where his presence is said to have "stoked the sleeping mountain's fire", while the dragon Lokkestiiz resided in a windy frost covered overlook.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOMojha-7)</sup>

This news brought the attention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), who were sent to investigate. The Undaunted defeated the fake Jone and Jode before they confronted Nahviintaas at the summit and the time-wound. Though Nahviintaas would utilize the power of the Time Wound to manipulate time, claiming his faithful had no need to fear death as he could restore them and they'd serve him eternally, and that he could make his foes relive their deaths over and over, the fake Alkosh was ultimately killed and Sunspire was liberated from his control.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESONahviintaas-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-5)</sup> With the years he had left, Moon-Bishop Azin-jo vowed to restore this temple and because of his folly, he felt that it was his task alone to bear.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-1)</sup> But regardless, some priests stayed in Sunspire.
