# Smithing


## Institutions

### Tamriel
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg/200px-ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-The_Earth_Forge.jpg) The [Earth Forge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Earth_Forge), a repurposed Dwemer forge used by the Fighters Guild Various cultures and organizations had their own approaches to the art of smithing, each making unique contributions and choices, including distinctive styles and materials.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-CM-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SM-5)</sup> For instance, [Highland Bretons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Highland_Bretons) are credited with the invention of the claymore.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWGaro-6)</sup>

Smiths were employed by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), and numerous [Templar Knightly Orders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Breton_Knightly_Orders#Templar_Orders).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OSL-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DKO-8)</sup>

The [Morkul Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morkul_Clan) of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) are well regarded across [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) society as master smiths and metalworkers.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONKurog-9)</sup> Chief among them was the ancient smith, [Nuzava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nuzava), who defined the Morkul Clan's distinctive style of [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) and armor.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-NA-10)</sup> Coins minted in the furnaces of [Old Orsinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Orsinium) are now considered rare and valuable.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OOC-11)</sup>

During the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) in [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321), [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie) gave his approval for the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md), which sanctioned numerous guilds and businesses. For example, the Act officially sanctioned the [Tinkers Guild](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tinkers_Guild), a guild established for itinerant [tinsmiths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tin).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HOTFG-12)</sup> As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), smiths were already benefiting as a result of the Guild Act. The establishment of pan-Tamrielic guilds resulted in greater diffusion of knowledge, which allowed many smiths to learn of interesting metals used only in distant lands, and this drove demand and trade for those materials.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MAMA-13)</sup> All the sanctioned guilds named in the Guild Act, and those formed later, were thereafter protected by the Empire of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), even into the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), but would be required to pay if they wanted to expand their influence. The Empire was strengthened by the Guild Act,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HOTFG-12)</sup> and the creation of guilds was considered beneficial for the Tamriel as a whole.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-AP-14)</sup>

The [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) was known to have some of the finest blacksmiths in Tamriel in its time.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), smiths frequently utilized distinctive materials to create diverse armors and incorporated unique gemstones and minerals to enhance their craft, improving their equipment with unique qualities.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SM-5)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BMTr-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Millenith-17)</sup> Skilled blacksmiths possessed the ability to decipher the metal's inherent qualities, allowing them to select the most suitable trait during the forging process, provided they had conducted thorough research. Furthermore, they could enhance the item's sharpness, add extra weight, and make other alterations if necessary. To achieve advanced levels of craftsmanship, they used various style books, which circulated throughout Tamriel at that time. A small variety of tempers were employed to make armor and weapons stronger and more resistant.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Maesa-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BMTemp-19)</sup> To obtain official recognition as a Smith, one had to pass a certification test held in many of the major cities.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BC-20)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Millenith-17)</sup> Obtaining such certificates enabled the smiths to accept a diverse range of contracts.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-BW-21)</sup>

As of [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), smiths in the region of the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) were often hired to craft weapons and armors, as well as to do some repairs at various stores. They were also known to train apprentices. The [goldsmiths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold) were known to create necklaces, rings, bracelets, and occasionally armor. Silversmiths worked with [silver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silver) and [mithril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mithril), and crafted weapons made of those materials. Ebonysmiths were known for their [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) products.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ARENA-22)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) Fighters Guilds and [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) smiths forged armor specifically designed for women.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MLFA-23)</sup>

### Oblivion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-place-Fargrave_Crafting_Plaza.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fargrave_Crafting_Plaza.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fargrave_Crafting_Plaza.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fargrave_Crafting_Plaza.jpg) Fargrave Crafting Plaza The mortal inhabitants of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) have two specialized smithing traditions, namely the smithing of [amber](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amber) and [madness ore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Madness_Ore). By long tradition, the city of [New Sheoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Sheoth) houses two rival master smiths: the amber smith of [Bliss](Lore_Bliss_(place).md), and the madness ore smith of [Crucible](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crucible).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SICutter-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SIDumag-25)</sup> By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), these crafting methods had spread to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) after a master arcane blacksmith named Evethra managed to learn the secrets of working with these materials, albeit losing her mind in the process.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-EJCC-27)</sup>

The realm of [Fargrave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fargrave) possesses a crafting plaza where mortal and [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) smiths alike gather from across the planes to trade and practice their craft.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> The [Saraathu Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saraathu_Tong) employed mage-smiths. Numerous blacksmiths incorporate magic into their work, but smithing through magic is considered far more uncommon.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Ravam_Drom-30)</sup>

The [Hollow City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hollow_City) in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) has on occasion possessed mortal blacksmiths, who ply their craft in the hostile realm under [Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia)'s protection.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> [Black Forge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Forge) is located in southeastern Coldharbour, and is a massive facility where [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s troops have their arms and armor forged.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOTP-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ESOPOVG-33)</sup>

## Notable Smiths

- **[Bthuand Mzahnch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthuand_Mzahnch)** — [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) smith, tonal architect and a student of [Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac). Author of *[The Egg of Time](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Egg_of_Time)*.
- **[Dahrk Mezalf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dahrk_Mezalf)** — Dwemer smith, tonal architect and a student of Kagrenac.
- **[Eorlund Gray-Mane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eorlund_Gray-Mane)** — Keeper of the famous [Skyforge](Lore_Skyforge.md) at the [Jorrvaskr](Lore_Jorrvaskr.md) Mead Hall for the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md), widely regarded as the best blacksmith in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) during his time.
- **[Gherig Bullblood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gherig_Bullblood)** — Reachman keeper of the [Bloodroot Forge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodroot_Forge) and patriarch of the [Dreadhorn Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreadhorn_Clan).
- **[Haj-Ja](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haj-Ja)** — Famous Argonian smith of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).
- **Haman Forgefire** — The greatest smith in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), who crafted the legendary Okin and was murdered by his rival.
- **[Hazadir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hazadir)** — Famous Argonian smith in service of Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md).
- **[Hervic Fambure](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hervic_Fambure)** — Master smith of [Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony).
- **[Hilbongard Rolamus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hilbongard_Rolamus)** — Forger of the [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) [Skull Crusher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skull_Crusher).
- **[Kagrenac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac)** — Renowned [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) mage-smith and Tonal Architect, responsible for the creation of the original Brass God, [Anumidium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumidium).
- **[Kyanka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kyanka)** — [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) smith and enchanter, responsible for the creation of the [Mace of Slurring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mace_of_Slurring).
- **[Lirulorne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lirulorne)** — Renowned [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) smith, responsible for crafting [Al-Esh](Lore_Alessia.md) Ascension Coins.
- **[Merric at-Aswala](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Merric_at-Aswala)** — Leader of the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) responsible for reforging the [Prismatic Core](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prismatic_Core) into a weapon.
- **[Mithas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mithas)** — Greatest of the [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) blacksmiths.
- **[Naenra Waerr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naenra_Waerr)** — Crafter of the Daedric artifact [Umbra](Lore_Umbra.md) for [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md).
- **[Nuulion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nuulion)** — Master smith of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) and confidant of the secret to forging Elven armor.
- **[Nuzava](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nuzava)** — An ancient and highly-respected smith of the Morkul Clan.
- **[Orgnum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgnum)** — King of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md), rumored to be the first silversmith in history.
- **[Radac Stungnthumz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Radac_Stungnthumz)** — [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) weaponsmith who played a crucial role in the process of reforging and reigniting [Trueflame](Lore_Trueflame.md).
- **[Sirollus Saccus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sirollus_Saccus)** — Master blacksmith for the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) who is among the longest-lived men in recorded history.
- **[Stoneheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stoneheart)** — Reachman [Bloodforge Smith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodforge_Smiths) and a prominent member of the Dreadhorn Clan.
- **[Tansal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tansal)** — One of the [Sword-singer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword-singers) heroes.
- **[Valindor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valindor)** — An apprentice of master armorer of Silvenar and descendant of Lord Harkon's bloodline.
- **[Yagak gro-Gluk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yagak_gro-Gluk)** — The best blacksmith in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), who reforged Trueflame.
- **[Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yngol)** — Son of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md), remembered as the greatest [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) blacksmith.

Spread across Tamriel are many other unnamed forges which provide unique effects to weapons, armor and jewelry created in such places.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-FELJEWL-34)</sup> The creative skill of the smiths who have worked these forges has imbued the sites with the divine spark of creativity, transforming the local reality such that items crafted there possess certain unique attributes.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup> Among such places were the forges located at the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Ruins) of [Sariellen's Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sariellen%27s_Sword),<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SS-36)</sup> and [Druadach Redoubt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druadach_Redoubt).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DR-37)</sup> Yet another example is Night's Silence, which was created by the mad wizard Mesanthano due to the arcane power which still lingers in places such as his ruined tower.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MT-38)</sup>
