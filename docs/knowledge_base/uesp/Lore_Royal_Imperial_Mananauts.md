# Royal Imperial Mananauts

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-crown_store-Imperial_Mananaut_Cap_%26_Goggles.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Imperial_Mananaut_Cap_%26_Goggles.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Imperial_Mananaut_Cap_%26_Goggles.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Imperial_Mananaut_Cap_%26_Goggles.jpg) A Second Era-recreation of the cap and goggles worn by Imperial Mananauts [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/ON-npc-Thaddeus_Cosma.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Thaddeus_Cosma.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Thaddeus_Cosma.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Thaddeus_Cosma.jpg) Thaddeus Cosma, a time traveller and a possible mananaut<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Thaddeus-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-AYP-2)</sup> The **Royal Imperial Mananauts of the Elder Council** (or simply **Imperial Mananauts**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LFNHa TR-3)</sup> were a scientifically advanced faction that served under the [Imperial Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), founded in the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) under the [Reman Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3-4)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LFNHa TR-3)</sup> Though their original [expeditionary](Lore_Air_Transportation.md#Aetherial_Vessels) purpose to explore [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) was eventually deprecated, the Mananauts remained active as an [Imperial organization](Lore_Empire_of_Tamriel.md) into the [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md) and [Third Eras](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTB-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3-4)</sup>

## History

### Reman Empire
Similarly to the [Sun Birds of Alinor](Lore_Sun_Birds_of_Alinor.md), the Mananauts undertook expeditions across the void of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) to enter Aetherius by way of the [stars](Lore_Stars.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3-4)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> In their cosmic ventures, they discovered that mortal minds must be acclimated to Oblivion by gentle degrees to prevent mental distortion. To remedy this issue, they utilized [Slipstream Realms](Lore_Slipstream_Realm.md), points within the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) where truths can co-exist without conceptual abrasion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LFNHa TR-3)</sup> But these expeditions required prohibitively large amounts of [magicka](Lore_Magic.md), and the initiatives to explore Aetherius were dissolved.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3-4)</sup>

### Imperial Interregnum
At some point during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the Second Era, the renowned fashion designer [Diocletia Orbus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Diocletia_Orbus) recreated the cap and goggles worn by Imperial Mananauts, drawing inspiration from ancient inscriptions.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-IMCG-6)</sup> During this period, the Mananauts were considered to be a mysterious holdover group of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), possibly taking new identities in order to preserve their traditions, maintaining a low profile during the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-7)</sup>

### Septim Empire
By the time of the [Septim Empire](Lore_Septim_Empire.md) circa [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), the Mananauts remained as an active organization but were considered the last remaining legacy of the earlier efforts to explore Aetherius.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3-4)</sup> The Imperial Mananauts supposedly verified with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) that his influence on fate and time is real and unfeigned, and the Prince is directly tied in with the [divine](Lore_Divines.md) [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_nb1) | While the vessels the Mananauts used for Aetherial travel are never identified, [unofficial texts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Unofficial_Lore) make mention of "megalomoths" or "mothships".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PG2-9)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Prof-10)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MKP-11)</sup> These vessels seem to be referenced in the official text *[Lost Tales of the Famed Explorer: Fragment III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lost_Tales_of_the_Famed_Explorer__Fragment_III)*, where the remains of something with "great wings of metal, like the wings of a moth" and "twin domes of layered glass eyes" is found in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-12)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- The Imperial Mananauts and Sun Birds of Alinor were written into the *[Pocket Guide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:PGE3)* by designer [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride) because he had the idea for a "TES Moon Colony game" (concerning the colonization of [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md)) and space travel had to be possible for his idea to work.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-MUWRU-13)</sup> Kirkbride later coined the idea that a secret province of the Empire existed across the dual moons, which he called Tatterdemalion.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PG2-9)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-PGE2Concept-14)</sup>
- The treatise *[The Hierarchical Structure of the Greater Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Improved_Emperor%27s_Guide_to_Tamriel_Bangkorai#The_Hierarchical_Structure_of_the_Greater_Dremora)* was written by a Royal Imperial Mananaut named Secundius based on observations of Oblivion gleaned from the scrying chambers beneath the [Imperial Palace](Lore_Imperial_Palace.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGTB-5)</sup>
- [Jauffre the Blade](Lore_Jauffre.md) is identified as an Imperial Mananaut when he is present on the moon base of [Sanctum Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md) within [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride)'s unofficial sci-fi piece entitled: [*FSL Professor Numinatus!*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:FSL_Professor_Numinatus!).<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Prof-10)</sup>
