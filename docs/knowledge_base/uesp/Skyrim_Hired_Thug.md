**Hired Thugs** are groups of three NPCs who may appear after you steal an item or kill another NPC (whether or not you get caught). They approach with weapons sheathed, declare their intention to teach you a lesson, then initiate combat; there is no way to yield to them.

They always carry a contract. If they intend to avenge a theft, the [contract](Skyrim_Contract_(Theft).md) will identify the victim; if the crime is murder, it will come from a relative of the victim.

Hired thugs will always wear a set of leveled [heavy armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor_(item).md), including the cuirass, a pair of boots, a 50% chance of a pair of gauntlets, and a 50% chance of a helmet (all up to [steel plate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Plate) in quality). They will always carry a leveled [dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger) (up to [elven](Skyrim_Elven.md) in quality), a leveled [heavy shield](Skyrim_Shield.md#Shields_2) (up to [ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md) in quality), and 50-250 gold, dependent on level. In addition, they will carry either a leveled [two-handed weapon](Skyrim_Two-Handed_Weapons.md) or a leveled [one-handed weapon](Skyrim_One-Handed_Weapons.md) plus an additional leveled heavy shield (all up to ebony in quality).

## Notes
- Hired thugs who witness you killing a dragon in or near a town will become non-hostile members of the admiring crowd. Once you leave their presence, however, they will resume their pursuit. If other enemies are present, they will fight them off, but will continue pursuing if you flee.
- Hired thugs can find you even if you are in sneak mode and the eye is fully closed. However, if you see the thugs before they have a chance to confront you and hit one of them with a ranged weapon or magic, only the thug you hit will become hostile, allowing you to deal with them one at a time.
- The three hired thugs used in [Revenge, Hired Thugs](Skyrim_Revenge,_Hired_Thugs.md) all have the base ID `00035B5E`.
- [Steal, Thugs hunt player](Skyrim_Steal,_Thugs_hunt_player.md) uses one hired thug of each base ID on this page. - The hired thug with the base ID `000D783C` has the unique dialogue line: *"This'll teach you not to take things that don't belong to you!"*
- The hired thug with the base ID `000D783B` has the unique dialogue line: *"We're here to teach you a lesson."*
- The thugs will follow you into buildings, such as stores, and they will follow you into walled cities or dungeons. Alternatively, you can stay on horseback outside of walls, but once you get off of the horse they immediately become hostile and attack.
- If you avoid confrontation with hired thugs, and they don't get killed by you or other NPCs, they will keep following you around Skyrim, regardless of distance.
- Only one thug will carry the contract on you; if they are hired because you stole something, the one to speak the line about teaching you a "lesson" will have it.
- A theft contract may be mistakenly assigned in place of a murder contract, and vice-versa.

## Bugs
- Although thugs are supposed to have leveled equipment, they may only wear steel armor (not steel plate) and use up to Orcish quality weapons (not ebony), no matter what level you (and subsequently they) are when you trigger their appearance. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Dead, sleeping, or non-unique NPCs may hire thugs to come after you for stealing from them. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.1.1, addresses this issue. These changes will also prevent generic wilderness NPCs such as [Forsworn](Skyrim_Forsworn.md) and [bandits](Skyrim_Bandit.md) from hiring thugs as well, which makes no sense at all.
- Hired thugs can be killed or turned hostile before speaking to the player. When this happens the quest becomes permanently stuck and unable to advance, leaving the dead bodies of the thugs lying around forever. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch), version 3.0.1, fixes this bug.
