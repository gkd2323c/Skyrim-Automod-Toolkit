The Cyro-Nords that settled it had relinquished the fertile Nibenay Valley long ago, determined to conquer the frontier. — [Imperial Geographic Society](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Geographic_Society), *[Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition § Cyrodiil](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_Cyrodiil.md)* Colovia (also known as the **Colovian West**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> **Old Colovia**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBGeneric-2)</sup> and the **Imperial West**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLoad Manse-3)</sup> is the western half of the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), known for their austere, hard-working, and self-sufficient people, the frontier-men of Colovia. The [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) of Colovia are strong-headed and hard working, with a rich martial tradition and strong work ethic. Many serve in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), either as soldiers or commanders,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> and Colovian Timber is highly prized for both construction and weaponry.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GCGP1-4)</sup>

Colovia features rugged hillscapes throughout the [Gold Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold_Road), dense greenery in and around the [Great Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Forest), and wide swathes of open grasslands throughout the [Imperial Reserve](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Reserve), which is famous for its bountiful hunting grounds. Much of the Colovian population live in the major cities, but some, particularly the nobility, tend to live in private estates closer to the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLoad Jarol-5)</sup> while various farms and roadside inns dot the landscape. Colovia has also been described as a coastal jungle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> Though Colovia's borders have shifted throughout the eras, traditionally, it contains four counties and their cities: [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol), [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad), and formerly [Sutch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sutch).

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg/200px-OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Miscarcand.jpg) The largest ruin in Cyrodiil, Miscarcand [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg/200px-ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Garlas_Malatar.jpg) The exterior of Garlas Malatar Before the Colovians lived in the Cyrodilic West, the region contained the western half of the once almighty [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md). Many great city-states and kingdoms dotted the landscape, such as the prosperous [Ceyatatar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceyatatar)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ACOV-6)</sup> and the independent state of [Lindai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lindai).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBHerminia-7)</sup> One of the most powerful nations from the west was [Miscarcand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miscarcand), the great capital of a mighty kingdom.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OBMartin-8)</sup>

However, all of these once-powerful countries fell to the whim of the [First Cyrodilic Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) and have either tried to retaliate or broker alliances with them, but in time, they were all abandoned. For example, the home of the Guiding Light, [Garlas Malatar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Garlas_Malatar), sealed itself away from the outside world and was sacked by the Alessians only from the outside. It perished from the inside by unknown means.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MTCKN-9)</sup> The remaining [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) in Colovia either became servants of the people that soon took over,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RTR-10)</sup> or continued to live on in tribes as the Wild Elves, far from the civilizations of Tamriel.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWE-11)</sup>

In the years that followed the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the land was soon populated by the [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) and later the ancient [Cyro-Nords](Lore_Imperial.md). The former had built port cities along the [Strident Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strident_Coast). Meanwhile, the latter had come to Colovia hoping to conquer the newly-established frontier, and distance themselves from the use of magic or the need for industry in the [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md). They preferred the engagement and plundering lifestyle like their predecessors in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). After they dominated the Nedic society of Colovia,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> they became more unified and formed great kingdoms that continue to thrive in the present-day, such as Skingrad and Kvatch. These countries were typically ruled by a King,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RTR-10)</sup> but some (such as Kvatch) were also governed by a regency council of three lords.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TLFF-12)</sup> They also took their conquest to the seas, as far away as the [Blue Divide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Divide) and the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), where they annually raided ports for roughly seven centuries until the arrival of the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HOTIB-13)</sup>

Sometime after [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s reign, the monkey-prophet, [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md) emerged from the coastal jungles of Colovia and preached to the masses about his experiences with the "Enlightened One", ushering a new religious movement that became the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order). At the height of the Order's influence, the West founded an autonomous government called the Colovian Estates, which had firmly established a solid position, both geographically and politically that resisted the East and their doctrines. From then on, these two faces of Cyrodiil had forever changed the perception of the province from the outside.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup>

### King Rislav Larich, the Righteous Son of Colovia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/OB-place-Skingrad_03.jpg/200px-OB-place-Skingrad_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Skingrad_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Skingrad_03.jpg) Statue of Rislav the Righteous These two identities first clashed in [1E 478](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_478) when the royal family of Skingrad succumbed to a deadly plague, and the throne was given to the oldest surviving child, Dorald Larich. Dorald had returned after his tenure as an Alessian priest in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), and his first edict on the first day of his reign was to cede the independent kingdom of Skingrad to the First Empire. The reaction across the Colovian Estates was shock and outrage, and all the more so in the Court of Kvatch where his younger brother, [Rislav Larich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rislav_Larich) was the prince. Prince Rislav and his wife, Princess Belene rode east with his father-in-law's cavalry to confront his brother. Rislav faced Dorald in the castle courtyard. In typical Colovian fashion, Rislav executed his brother on the spot instead of holding a trial. By right of combat, Rislav Larich was named the King of Skingrad and openly rebelled against the Alessian Empire and their methods. Emperor [Gorieus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorieus) and his armies poured onto the west "like a flood of death", with numbers that far exceeded the requirement to conquer a ruined Colovia.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RTR-10)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/ON-place-Monument_to_Rislav.jpg/200px-ON-place-Monument_to_Rislav.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Monument_to_Rislav.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Monument_to_Rislav.jpg) Kvatch's monument to Rislav The Alessians anticipated little resistance and eventually surrender from the Colovians and an ill-experienced King Rislav. But the Colovian monarch called to arms and prepared a plan to thwart the Emperor. Both armies met at a low pass along what is now the Gold Road. Before Gorieus' personal guards would arrest the King, Rislav signaled his archers to rain a storm of arrows onto the pass and escaped westward. With nowhere to run, the [Imperial Army](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Army) was left devastated by the surprise attack, and they were forced to pursue Rislav northwest into the southern [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md). As their armies became smaller and smaller, they were met by King Justinius of Kvatch and a volley of arrows from his troops. The Emperor's army had greatly dwindled and was forced to retreat into the Imperial Reserve before they returned to Nibenay.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RTR-10)</sup>

Rislav's resounding victory was the beginning of the end for the Alessian Hegemony, as the other Colovian kingdoms joined Skingrad and Kvatch in their rebellion. King Rislav Larich became a hero in the eyes of the West and earned the sobriquet of "the Righteous". His likeness is a treasured landmark in Skingrad's Hightown and his actions inspired others to fight back. Most notably the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) under [Ryain Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ryain_Direnni), who fought against the Alessians in the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors) around [1E 480](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_480) and outlawed their religion. His territory expanded east and pressed southeast into Imperial territories<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RTR-10)</sup> where at some point, they harassed the Colovian West and pushed into the [Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands) until their defeat in [1E 492](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_492).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-XLAH-14)</sup>

### Bendu Olo and the All-Flags Navy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg/200px-ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anvil_Castle.jpg) [Anvil Castle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anvil_Castle), constructed following Bendu Olo's victory [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fa/ON-misc-Bendu_Olo.png/200px-ON-misc-Bendu_Olo.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Bendu_Olo.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Bendu_Olo.png) Baron-Admiral Bendu Olo of Anvil By the turn of the twenty-third century in the First Era, the tension between the East and the West persisted, but for a long time, they were part of one Empire. While the Nibenese flourished in their wealth, the Colovians trudged onward. The Colovian Estates had expanded beyond Cyrodiil in the past several centuries, incorporating other settlements across the region like the Strident port of Anvil, the northern hold of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md), and other places like [Delodiil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Delodiil) and Sarchal.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RE-15)</sup> However, in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200), all of Western Tamriel faced severe casualties when the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) decimated half of the continent's population,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE1EWR-16)</sup> and obliterated ancient established dominions like the sixteen kingdoms of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AAPAI-17)</sup> and the sovereignty of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md)'s kinlords.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RJ-18)</sup>

After years of suffering, the Colovian king of Anvil, [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo) loudly called for justice against the Sload and petitioned the Emperor for commission, to which he received in short notice in [1E 2241](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2241). With the self-claimed title of Baron-Admiral and in the span of two years, Olo assembled one of the largest naval forces in history called the [All-Flags Navy](Lore_All-Flags_Navy.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SH3-19)</sup> The navy's founding leaders originally gathered on [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md), an island west of Anvil<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONVanus-20)</sup> and assembled west across the Abecean Sea on [the Systres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Systres),<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SH3-19)</sup> an archipelago northwest of [Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lillandril).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-RGMap-21)</sup> Colovian engineers spearheaded the construction of the island's shipyards in [Gonfalon Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gonfalon_Bay) and later the All Flags Islet, a monument to the navy. The war against the Sload started sometime in [1E 2258](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2258) and ended with the final siege against [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) in [1E 2260](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2260).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SH3-19)</sup>

After the battle, the remaining captains of the All-Flags Navy returned to the Systres and signed the [Concordat of Fraternity](Lore_Concordat_of_Fraternity.md), which bound each captain and each race to peace and allowed the Systres to stand as a monument to Tamriel's victory.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SH3-19)</sup> Bendu Olo claimed the Systres Archipelago as an extension of Colovia and while all of the fleet's captains returned home, Colovians and Bretons stayed and work with Master Builder Tobin Moorcroft to construct a monument to the navy. That edifice, the Colossus of Gonfalon Bay finished construction more than twenty years later in [1E 2290](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2290) and stands today on the southeast shore near the eponymous city.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SH4-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TCOGB-23)</sup> Bendu Olo and his fight against the Sload brought immense glory to the Cyrodilic people<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> and established trade with the other races like the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-PGE3EH-24)</sup> Over time, the Colovian Estates greatly overshadowed Nibenay, so much so that tension raised between both factions.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup>

### The Schism of Cyrodiil and the Light of Man
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg/200px-OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Sancre_Tor.jpg) Sancre Tor, the birthplace of Reman I [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg/200px-OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Imperial_City.jpg) The Imperial City Even though the Systres was a Colovian state, it was mostly populated by Bretons and the Alessian Emperor demanded that they leave. The Colovians, however, did not budge and insisted that the cost of removal was prohibitive. The Associate Dean of Eltheric History at the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md), Trilam Heladren asserts that this was the Colovians' attempt to bide their time for independence from the Alessians.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SH4-22)</sup>

What came after was the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) in [1E 2321](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2321), which started because of a number of factors. The Estates over time became bolder and more resistant to the Alessian doctrines, all the more so when the east failed to re-capture [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). This and internal conflict in the Alessian Order were what led to the War of Righteousness and its ten years of a desperate back and forward.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> The coffers of both sides emptied to gain an advantage over the other and in [1E 2326](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2326), the Colovian Estates were out of money. Their kings had to sell any non-essential territory to meet the demand, such as the Systres which was sold in [1E 2327](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2327) for an undisclosed sum.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SH4-22)</sup> The war came to an end in [1E 2331](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2331) and both the order and the First Empire had disbanded.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> What followed was four hundred years of separation, each side a shadow of their former glory. The Colovian kings were taken by their greed and pride, and became more like thief-barons, forsaking their covenants.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RE-15)</sup>

As the [Remanada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Remanada) tells it, King [Hrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrol) of lost [Twil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Twil) received visions of an invasion in the near future and sought to heal the borders of his forebears with his western knights. In their journey, they met the spirit of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), who wielded the dragonfire of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md). When she fled north, they scoured the land to find her and both Hrol and his shield-thane found her in the Colovian-Jerall region. The shield-thane witnessed the spirit undress in front of the king and copulate on a mound of mud. He then carved onto a rock: "AND HROL DID LOVE UNTO A HILLOCK," before dying soon after. The other knights discovered Hrol dead on the mound and parted ways after, either because they went mad or returned to Twil ashamed of their lord. Nine months later, the mound turned into a small mountain and was surrounded by a community of shepherds and bulls who named it [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor). One fateful day, the shepherdess, [Sed-Yenna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sed-Yenna) scaled the mountain when she heard the cries of a baby. At the top, she found an infant with the Amulet of Kings on his forehead. She named him [Reman](Lore_Reman.md), the "Light of Man," and took him to the Ruby Throne in the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), where he said, "I am Cyrodiil come.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RE-15)</sup>

### Colovia in the Second Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/OB-place-Pale_Pass.jpg/200px-OB-place-Pale_Pass.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Pale_Pass.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Pale_Pass.jpg) Pale Pass [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/ON-place-Southpoint.jpg/200px-ON-place-Southpoint.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Southpoint.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Southpoint.jpg) The Colovian town of Southpoint Reman is described as a "proud son of the West" like Bendu Olo before him. When the [First Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) occurred in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), he gathered the disparaged Colovians and rallied the [Niben Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_Valley)'s armies to form the Army of Reman and met the [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) invaders at the [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md). Reman unified the two faces of Cyrodiil and created the cosmopolitan [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> The Empire incorporated aspects of Colovia, as well as High Rock, Nibenay, and the strange but sophisticated cultures of the Akaviri.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3EC-25)</sup>

At some point in time, relations between the Second Empire and the Colovian West were strained until Empress [Herda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Herda) relieved them. This change in development gave the Empire extra leeway in their conquest of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), and their efforts paid off after centuries of war when they managed to annex the province in [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714), with aid from the Thrassian Plague.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3EV-26)</sup> The Colovians left a significant footnote in the Kingdom of [Grahtwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahtwood), specifically on the southern coast. The ancient settlement [Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haven), which would go on to become a great power in the region, was reconstructed in the Colovian-style.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ASP-27)</sup> Haven was granted independence under a Treethane by the Empire.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3EV-26)</sup> Despite its strength, Haven was later overtaken by [Southpoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Southpoint) to the west, itself built by the Colovian soldier called [Zantonius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zantonius) in [1E 2729](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2729). Southpoint has since become the center of Imperial culture in all of Valenwood and an important port in Grahtwood,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TFOS-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONLoad Path-29)</sup> as well as the seat of a kingdom by the late-Third Era.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ARSouthpoint-30)</sup>

When Reman's Empire came to fruition, it saw the Imperial Legion reform and bear influences from the Akaviri.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> Colovian countrymen served in the Imperial Legion and some of their greatest contributions took place in the Empire's annexation of Valenwood. When the Colovians fought the Redguards in the War of Rihad circa [1E 2818](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2818), they utilized [Wood Elf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wood_Elf) archers that proved to be too undisciplined and prone to desertion for further use.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE1EAD-31)</sup> At some point in time, [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III) married [Tavia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tavia), an extremely wealthy noble with strong ties to the western Colovian Estates.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-2920v8-32)</sup>

### The Interregnum and Varen Aquilarios
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg/200px-ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Varen%27s_Wall_Gatehouse.jpg) Varen's Wall Eastern Gatehouse [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/LG-cardart-Varen_Aquilarios.png/200px-LG-cardart-Varen_Aquilarios.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Varen_Aquilarios.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Varen_Aquilarios.png) Duke Varen Aquilarios of Kvatch After many prosperous years under the Second Empire, the province of Cyrodiil was split once again with the assassination of Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak) in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431). Not only was Cyrodiil torn asunder, but it became no-man's land, a realm ruled by bandit-kings and false emperors. This long and tumultuous time in the Second Era was known as the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md),<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3EC-25)</sup> and while the province was left disheveled with no figurehead,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-COTFC4-33)</sup> the Elder Council continued their service to the people. By the sixth century, Councilor [Itinia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Itinia) served as Colovia's representative in the council and its affairs.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-IDRTEC-34)</sup>

Sometime in the mid-Second Era, [Durcorach the Black Drake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) descended from [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) in the north and took control of the Imperial City in [2E 533](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_533). Councilor Itinia was able to keep her position in the Elder Council with full confidence from Emperor Durcorach.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-IDRTEC-34)</sup> His ruling dynasty was known as the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) and their [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) lasted longer than others before it, but because of their background and heritage, their rule was not recognized by the people of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-COTFC4-33)</sup>

The relationship between the people and Longhouse Emperors was shaky, but tension reached its boiling point when Emperor [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic) revealed the Great Spirits Proclamation, which declared that worship of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) was legal and protected by the Empire. In secret, Duke [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) of Colovia made contact with the Emperor's Grand Chancellor, [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) of Nibenay and together they plotted their revolution against the Emperor.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-COTFC4-33)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-EFEV-35)</sup> Duke Varen inspired the Colovians and rallied them to his cause, with his largest stream of support coming from the old nobility of Chorrol, Kvatch, and Skingrad.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SZLMIWLS-36)</sup> But before he could advance onward to the capital, he felt that he needed to defend his homeland, the Gold Coast. The Duke enlisted the famed architect, Juros Truptor, and ordered his followers to build a stone wall between the Gold Coast and the rest of Colovia. The project enlisted many paid workers, an endless slew of volunteers, and every bit of stone available. From rough-hewn stone to mismatched rock and stone confiscated from noble estates, strategically placed in conjunction with the rocky, natural terrain.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-VW-37)</sup>

Duke Varen and the [Second Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Second_Legion) left for the Cyrodilic heartland and after an initial defeat in [Fort Ash](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Ash), the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) made its way closer toward the Imperial City<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-EFEV-35)</sup> until its resounding conclusion in [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-DID-38)</sup> Back on the Gold Coast, the Longhouse's compound and retreat, the Black Drake Villa was plundered and most of Durcorach's treasure made its way into the black market.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONLoad Drake-39)</sup> In that same year, the pirate captain, [Fortunata ap Dugal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortunata_ap_Dugal) sailed into a barely protected and ungoverned Anvil with her fleet, the Red Sails and proclaimed herself its Provincial-Governor. Her first decree was to declare the Gold Coast's independence, from the Abecean Sea to Varen's Wall.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-40)</sup>

### Colovia and the Soulburst
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/ON-place-Chorrol.jpg/200px-ON-place-Chorrol.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Chorrol.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Chorrol.jpg) The Sundering of Chorrol [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/ON-place-Vlastarus.jpg/200px-ON-place-Vlastarus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vlastarus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vlastarus.jpg) Vlastarus Varen Aquilarios acquired his title through the right of conquest, with help from his chief advisor, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md). But like his predecessors, the Colovian Emperor did not have the blood of dragons in his veins and could not light the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md), as per tradition within the Empire. Mannimarco offered a solution that involved the long-lost Amulet of Kings. The Emperor assembled his [Five Companions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Five_Companions) and scoured the land for the artifact until they found it in Sancre Tor in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579). They returned to the Imperial City and the [Temple of the One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_the_One), where Mannimarco would have conducted a modified ritual that lit the fires, but instead opened a volatile rift between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) called the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup>

Amid the chaos, [Sai Sahan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai_Sahan) took the Amulet and hid it back in Sancre Tor,<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ONSai-42)</sup> but much to his detriment, both he and [Lyris Titanborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyris_Titanborn) were implicated in Varen's death.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup> The aftermath of the Soulburst was immediate and devastating.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup> Earthquakes ravaged central Cyrodiil and eventually split Chorrol in half, revealing a large fissure to the outer core of the planet. The people of Chorrol referred to the widespread destruction as the "Sundering".<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TCC-43)</sup> The Soulburst paved the way for the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), Molag Bal's invasion over Tamriel and his takeover in Cyrodiil. The [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm) built [dark anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) all across the countryside, from the Applewatch Wood in the Colovian-Jerall region to [Greenmead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenmead) in the Great Forest.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ONCyrodiil-44)</sup>

By the ninth century, central Cyrodiil was a shadow of its former self, plagued by highwaymen, necromancers, and now Daedra. The Colovians at this time were scattered and left to their own devices. While the Gold Coast continued uninterrupted as an autonomous state, settlements such as Chorrol and [Vlastarus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vlastarus) lived in a constant struggle with the elements.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ONCyrodiil-44)</sup> However, the Colovian Estates between Varen's Wall and central Cyrodiil remained largely intact and continued their service to the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) in earnest. At this point, Skingrad was ruled by Count [Calantius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calantius_of_Skingrad), an ambitious leader and a canny opportunist. When the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) went rampant in [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March) to the south, he re-designated the [Northern Woods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northern_Woods) around [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia) as the South Weald and sent the Legion of the West Weald to invade.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SZLMIWLS-36)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-YFTT-45)</sup>

With the Ruby Throne vacant, nations from across the continent continued to vie for control of the Imperial City. By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) had completely enraptured central Cyrodiil and turned it into a warzone. While County Skingrad and the Gold Coast were left untouched, eastern Colovia was torn between the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) to the north and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) to the south. The former had acquired land from the western Jerall Mountains between [Gnoll Mountain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnoll_Mountain) and Sancre Tor to the northern half of the Great Forest between Chorrol and Fort Ash. The latter acquired the rest of the Great Forest from [Hackdirt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hackdirt) and [Fanacasecul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fanacasecul), to the southern provincial border between Vlastarus and the [Upper Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Upper_Niben). Major settlements at the time were Chorrol and Vlastarus, whose allegiances were determined by control over in Fort Rayles and [Castle Brindle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castle_Brindle) respectively. At the edge of their respective territories, the alliances established bases on provincial gates and built temples that housed [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md)<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ONCyrodiil-44)</sup> they stole from the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONAxius-46)</sup> In that same year, a political feud ensued between Governor Fortunata ap Dugal of Anvil, Primate [Artorius Ponticus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Artorius_Ponticus) of the [Akatosh Chantry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akatosh_Chantry), and Count [Carolus Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Carolus_Aquilarios) of Kvatch over supremacy on the Gold Coast. With intervention from the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) however, the Count of Kvatch was the last one left alive,<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ONDark-47)</sup> and sought to bring stability to the entire region in the wake of the conflict.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ONCarolus-48)</sup>

### Talos Stormcrown and Emperor Zero
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/SR-place-Old_Hroldan.jpg/200px-SR-place-Old_Hroldan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Old_Hroldan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Old_Hroldan.jpg) Old Hroldan, the site of the battle [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/BL-misc-Talos.png/200px-BL-misc-Talos.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Talos.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Talos.png) Talos Stormcrown As the Alliance War came and went, there was no long-lasting successful claimant to the Ruby Throne, and the estates were scattered by the late Second Era. The Colovian King of Falkreath, [Cuhlecain](Lore_Cuhlecain.md) was at an impasse between the disorganized estates, which he wanted to unify, and his northern border toward the Reach, where the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) had been at war for centuries. Before he could make his bid on the south, he sent his prized general, [Hjalti Early-Beard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hjalti_Early-Beard) of [Alcaire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alcaire), to lead his small force of Colovians and Nords against the Reach's line in [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TAH-49)</sup>

According to *[The Arcturian Heresy](Lore_The_Arcturian_Heresy.md)*, a siege appeared to be impossible to maintain due to the lack of possible reinforcements from Falkreath. During the first night of the siege, a storm appeared and spoke to Hjalti in his tent. The following day at dawn, Hjalti went to the gates of Old Hroldan, with the storm following above him and protecting him from projectiles. Hjalti shouted down the defensive wall, allowing his forces to move in and win the day. In awe of his power, the Nords called him "Hjalti [Talos](Lore_Talos.md)", which means "Stormcrown" in the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) language.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TAH-49)</sup> With this power, Hjalti Early-Beard and Cuhlecain united Western Cyrodiil in under a year and eventually turned toward the Imperial City and the Nibenay proper.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TAH-49)</sup>

At some point in [2E 852](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_852), the Nords and the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) invaded the city-state Sancre Tor and took over the region's roads and settlements.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-BOST-50)</sup> By this time, Sancre Tor had become a major religious center and functional mountain fort that had been regularly invaded by the Nords and Bretons.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-TLST-51)</sup> When General Talos marched toward Sancre Tor in the middle of winter with a meek army, the Nordic-Breton allies were confident in their position and left a small force to defend the citadel while the rest descended onto the Cyrodilic army. Unbeknownst to them, however, Talos also left a small fraction of his army to draw out the larger force and bypassed Sancre Tor's magical defenses via an obscure mountain trail behind it.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-BOST-50)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg/135px-LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Titus_Alorius.jpg) Provisional Governor Titus Alorius Talos and his men captured the enemy leaders, compelling them to surrender. The Nords, already suspicious of their allies and dubious of their ambitions for the Imperial City, were easily coerced by the Cyrodiils and swore undying loyalty to the General. The Breton battlemage command was executed and the army's high-ranked officials were either imprisoned or sold into slavery.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-BOST-50)</sup> Eventually, Cyrodiil had been united as one, but before Cuhlecain was able to claim the title of Emperor or even enter the Imperial City, he was allegedly killed by a nightblade from the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) in [2E 854](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_854). In his stead, [Zurin Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zurin_Arctus) crowned Talos as the Emperor of Cyrodiil, and with his newly-obtained title, he changed his name to something more [Cyrodilic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrodilic), [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TAH-49)</sup>

### Colovia in the Tiber War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_%28Morrowind_Codex%29.png/200px-LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_%28Morrowind_Codex%29.png)](File_LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_(Morrowind_Codex).png.md) [](File_LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_(Morrowind_Codex).png.md) Map of the Aldmeri Dominion With Cyrodiil under his banner, Tiber Septim began his grand plans to conquer all of Tamriel and started with the human kingdoms, High Rock and Hammerfell. These events were called the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). After the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) brushed over the resistance, Colovian officers of the [Imperial Army](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Army) were appointed as Provisional-Governors in core realms in the region, bringing some stability with them.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-52)</sup> The governors of High Rock, such as [Titus Alorius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Titus_Alorius) of the Western Reach, were able to end the feuding between the witch-kings,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-RGKrisandra-53)</sup> while the Governor of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Senecus Goddkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senecus_Goddkey), turned the politically unstable [capital city](Lore_Sentinel.md) into an exotic resort for the nobles of nearby [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE1EH-54)</sup>

The Colovian Admiral, [Amiel Richton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Richton) led the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) at the [Battle of Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Hunding_Bay), which consolidated the Empire's victory over Hammerfell and in return, he was promoted to Provisional-Governor of the Kingdom of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE1EH-54)</sup> But after a revolt led by [Cyrus the Restless](Lore_Cyrus.md), Richton was killed and the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md) was signed,<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-RGRaze-55)</sup> which formally brought Hammerfell into the Empire on mutual terms.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TWQ5-56)</sup>

While the north saw a new change in management, a faction of Wood Elves made overtures of peace with the Colovians, in exchange for territory and their support for their [Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camoran) claimant to the provincial throne. However, in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830), the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) invaded Valenwood and established a provisional government called the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) on behalf of their own claimant, [Camoran Anaxemes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camoran_Anaxemes). The Colovians were then banished out of the province and the Aldmeri Dominion was formed, a political union of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The Colovians took to calling them the "Old Mary Dominion" as a knock on elven soldiers.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE1EAD-31)</sup> The Dominion was defeated when their capital city, [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) was demolished by [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TAH-49)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-57)</sup> By [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), no other province could withstand the Third Empire and in the following year, Tiber Septim declared the start of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Year Aught.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-58)</sup>

### Under the Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg/200px-OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg) The Crusader's Relics [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/OB-place-Dunbarrow_Cove.jpg/200px-OB-place-Dunbarrow_Cove.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Dunbarrow_Cove.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Dunbarrow_Cove.jpg) Dunbarrow Cove underneath Anvil Colovia under the Empire was peaceful as Cyrodiil re-developed into a prosperous and prominent country. The first century at that time was defined by an air of optimism and ambition. From that time, a veteran of the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md), [Amiel Lannus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Lannus) created a knightly order called the [Knights of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Nine), with the sole purpose of re-assembling the [Crusader's Relics](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crusader%27s_Relics). In their early years, they acquired three of the relics and established their base in a [priory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Priory_of_the_Nine) out in the [West Weald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:West_Weald). Soon enough, the knights attained province-wide fame and their home became an attraction for those willing to serve for the greater good. One of their most esteemed recruits was Berich Vlindrel, a scion to one of Colovia's [great families](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vlindrel) and whose notoriety elevated the knighthood into the Empire's most prestigious order.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TKOTN-59)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-SAJ-60)</sup>

However, as time passed, the Knights of the Nine saw an increasing decline, especially when the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) left the order torn between the two sides. Eventually, by [3E 150](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_150), Sir Amiel was the only one left in the priory and most of the artifacts recovered by knights were scattered throughout Cyrodiil. In Colovia, the [Gauntlets of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gauntlets_of_the_Crusader) carried by Sir [Casimir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Casimir) were left in the corner of Chorrol's Great Chapel of Stendarr, unmovable by any means after the knight killed an innocent beggar.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TKOTN-59)</sup> Berich's artifacts, the sword and greaves were kept in his family tomb in [Underpall Cave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Underpall_Cave), north of the [Orange Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orange_Road).<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-OBLathon-61)</sup> With nothing left, Sir Amiel left the priory in [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153) for the last time to search for the [Helm of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Helm_of_the_Crusader) in [Vanua](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vanua), never to be seen again.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-SAJ-60)</sup>

A century later, Western Colovia was caught in the middle of the [Camoran Usurper's Invasion](Lore_Camoran_Usurper%27s_Invasion.md). Before it had reached Hammerfell and its southern coast, [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md)'s armies stomped through Kvatch and unleashed a great horror onto the Colovian West.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TR-62)</sup> Because of the Empire's focus on the Camoran Usurper, Anvil became a shantytown populated by pirates, refugees, thieves, and other malcontents. But in the wake of it all, the chaos brought the rise of other criminals, such as the pirate [Torradan ap Dugal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torradan_ap_Dugal), who organized a fearsome crew from Anvil called the [Red Sabres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Sabre) and rose to infamy across the Abecean Sea, even gaining the name, the "Terror of the Gold Coast".<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-CDJ1-63)</sup>

In time, Dugal acquired a hefty bounty of forty-thousand coins<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-CDJ2-64)</sup> and when the Usurper was defeated in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267), he started to bud heads with Commodore [Fasil Umbranox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fasil_Umbranox) of the Imperial Navy. After months of back and forwards, and rooting out pirate cells across the Abecean, the Commodore was able to draw out Dugal and corner him in Anvil Bay. Umbranox was able to secure the victory by crashing the rocky outcrop over Dugal's ship, which plunged him into unnatural caves.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-CDJ3-65)</sup> The Commodore took over Anvil and forced its population out into the countryside so that he could rebuild. For his efforts, he was awarded the title of Count and his family ruled for generations.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-OBThieves-66)</sup>

### At the turn of the Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/OB-quest-The_Battle_for_Castle_Kvatch.jpg/200px-OB-quest-The_Battle_for_Castle_Kvatch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Battle_for_Castle_Kvatch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Battle_for_Castle_Kvatch.jpg) The ruins of Kvatch [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/LG-cardart-Martin_Septim.png/200px-LG-cardart-Martin_Septim.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Martin_Septim.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Martin_Septim.png) Martin Septim Colovia saw peace in the following years as Cyrodiil continued as the stable heart of the Empire, even in tumultuous times like the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md). The developments in Cyrodiil have shown that disputes could be settled with diplomacy, as was the case with the marriage of Count [Marius Caro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marius_Caro) of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) and [Alessia Valga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessia_Caro), the Countess of Chorrol's daughter.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3EC-25)</sup> Despite this, tragedy and conflict have come every once in a while throughout the province. Count [Charus Valga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Charus_Valga) of Chorrol, a staunch follower of Stendarr, died at the hands of Nordic clansmen in an unknown conflict.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-GTC-67)</sup> Familial strife struck Kvatch when both sons of [Count Goldwine](Lore_Ormellius_Goldwine.md) died competing for the inheritance. While peace had been restored in County Kvatch, by [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), the Count was still in mourning.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE3EC-25)</sup>

On the 27th of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md), [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) was assassinated by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md). With no other Septim able to light the Dragonfires, Tamriel was invaded by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), an event remembered in history as the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). Colovia was subjected to the destruction brought about by the crisis and [Oblivion Gates](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oblivion_Gates) were opened all over the region, from the Gold Coast to the Great Forest. In his last dying breath, the Emperor tasked a lowly prisoner to take the Amulet of Kings to the [Grandmaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jauffre) of the Blades and find the last heir to the throne.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-OBTutorial-68)</sup> Their quest eventually brought them to Kvatch to find a priest of Akatosh named [Martin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Martin). What they found instead was the city completely overrun by daedra and razed to the ground. The prisoner entered Oblivion and destroyed the daedric gate from the inside, giving the city guard leverage to take back Kvatch and earning the name, [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TOC-69)</sup>

Martin was discovered among a group of survivors who took shelter in the Chapel of Akatosh. After it was revealed to him that he was the bastard child of the Emperor, he worked alongside the Blades and the Hero of Kvatch to end the plot against the Empire and the Oblivion Crisis.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TOC-69)</sup> The hero's quest took them all over Cyrodiil and throughout Colovia's great expanse as they destroyed Oblivion Gates in their wake. They delved into the ruins of Miscarcand to find one of the last [Great Welkynd Stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Welkynd_Stone) left in Cyrodiil<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-OBMiscarcand-70)</sup> and traversed the ancient fortress, Sancre Tor for the [Armor of Tiber Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Armor_of_Tiber_Septim).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-OBBlood-71)</sup> Both of these artifacts were used to infiltrate the Mythic Dawn's headquarters, a realm known as [Paradise](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Paradise) and retrieve the Amulet of Kings stolen by their leader; [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md).<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-OBParadise-72)</sup> Before Martin could relight the Dragonfires, Mehrunes Dagon stepped through the barrier that separated the realms and assaulted the Imperial City, destroying the Liminal Barrier and making the Dragonfires ineffective. In a last ditch effort, Martin sacrificed himself and became an Avatar of Akatosh, banishing the prince and sealing the barrier forever, at the cost of his life and the Septim bloodline.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TOC-69)</sup>

### Colovia in the Fourth Era
The High Chancellor of the Elder Council, [Ocato of Firsthold](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) convened the council for a bid on selecting a new Emperor. But when it moved unsuccessfully, Ocato took on the role of Potentate up until a new ruler could be chosen.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-RT3-73)</sup> Ten years later the Potentate was assassinated, sparking the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md). The interregnum lasted for seven, bloody years until the rise of the Colovian Warlord, [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede.md).<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-RT4-74)</sup> By the time Titus marched on the Imperial City with the support of a thousand soldiers,<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-TIC-75)</sup><sup>:109</sup> a Nibenese witch-warrior named [Thules the Gibbering](Lore_Thules_the_Gibbering.md) had come to power within the capital. Even though his rule was ill-received, the Elder Council favored him for his Nibenese background. Despite this, Titus Mede proved to be a capable leader and managed to receive support from Minister [Hierem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hierem),<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-LOS-76)</sup> but Skyrim's endorsement as well.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-RT4-74)</sup> [Mede's dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md) continued to hold the Empire for several years, from his crown prince, [Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) to his descendant, [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md).

On the 30th of [Frostfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostfall) in [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171), the Aldmeri Dominion declared war against the Third Empire, starting the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md). While most of the fighting in Cyrodiil took place in the east and south, the Dominion's armies led by Lady [Arannelya](Lore_Arannelya.md) crossed into Colovia, bypassing both Anvil and Kvatch to reach their main objective, Hammerfell. Lord [Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md)'s invasion of the south led the Thalmor to believe that the Empire was weaker than surmised, so they pursued further with their attack. By [4E 172](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_172) they had taken the city of Anvil. In the final days of the war during the winter of 4E 174-175, General Decianus' armies, one of three led by Emperor Titus Mede II, gathered in the Colovian Highlands around Chorrol for the [Battle of the Red Ring](Lore_Battle_of_the_Red_Ring.md). The battle would commence on the 30th of [Rain's Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rain%27s_Hand) with the assembled forces marching to the Imperial City from the west. General Jonna's forces tried to join with Decianus' forces but was forced to withstand an Aldmeri counterattack from Bravil and Skingrad. The city would be recaptured during an assault led by the Emperor into the city. A peace agreement that was nearly identical to the initial demands made by the Aldmeri Dominion was reached shortly thereafter.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TGW-77)</sup>

## The Colovian Estates
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-place-Arenthia.jpg/200px-ON-place-Arenthia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arenthia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Arenthia.jpg) Arenthia (ca. 2E 582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/SR-place-Falkreath.jpg/200px-SR-place-Falkreath.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Falkreath.jpg) Falkreath (ca. 4E 201) The **Colovian Estates** refer to the autonomous government of the nations of Colovia that are governed by merchant-estates.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-RGBios-78)</sup> Traditionally, these refer to the core Colovian nations (Anvil, Chorrol, Kvatch, and Skingrad), but the Colovian Estates have contained other nations throughout the eras, and have attempted to absorb regions beyond the provincial borders. Most notably is the city-state of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md), which is found in southern Skyrim,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-TAH-49)</sup> and has been administrated by the Far Falkreath Estate.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-RGBios-78)</sup> The Colovian's culture spill into their neighbors, such as the Redguards of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md), who adopted their style of fashion and taste.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE1EH-54)</sup> The city of [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia) takes a lot of architectural inspiration from Colovia and is even found across the [Strid River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strid_River) from Skingrad.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ESOArenthia-79)</sup>

The Colovian Estates have historically fought against their neighbors of Valenwood, which explains their influence across [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March) from Arenthia to [Greenhill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenhill). Their first known conflict was around the dissolution of the Alessian Reforms. As Empress [Herda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Herda) improved relations with the Colovian West, attacks against Valenwood intensified, ultimately ending in [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714) when the province was ceded into the Second Empire.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3EV-26)</sup> Another was during the Interregnum, in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), but their forces were thwarted as the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#First_Dominion) intervened and recruited the Wood Elves.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-ESOWebsite-80)</sup> Two years later, another invasion occurred towards Reaper's March, as decreed by the Count of Skingrad and ratified by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), by the Legion of the West Weald under both [Javad Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Javad_Tharn) and General Lavinia Axius.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Yft T-81)</sup> But their invasion was thwarted by the newly-named [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) and her [Moon Hallowed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soulless_One).<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-ESOMarch-82)</sup>

## Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/MW-place-Caldera.jpg/200px-MW-place-Caldera.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Caldera.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Caldera.jpg) The Mining Colony of Caldera [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/MW-item-Colovian_Fur_Helm.png/200px-MW-item-Colovian_Fur_Helm.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Colovian_Fur_Helm.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Colovian_Fur_Helm.png) A Colovian Fur Helm The **Colovians** (also called the **Western Imperials**<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-GTV-83)</sup> or just the **Westerners**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> are the modern-day Imperials of Colovia. They are the firm iron hand of Cyrodiil and make up a large majority of the province's working force. Unlike their brethren in the east, the Colovians retain much of their Nordic roots and still possess the frontier spirit of their ancestors. In a lot of ways, they are considered uncomplicated, self-sufficient, hearty, and loyal to their countrymen.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> They can be distinguished from the Nibenese by their distinct accent.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-LOS-76)</sup>

When it comes to clothing, the Colovians tend to dress with a simple or martial severity, unlike the Nibenese.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SZLMIWLS-36)</sup> Colovian wear is visually distinctive of their frontier roots, as seen with the pointed helms and fur pelts worn by Sancre Tor highlanders,<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-LOSancre-84)</sup> or the armor worn by Colovians from [County Anvil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:County_Anvil).<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-LOAnvil-85)</sup> Colovian Prince's armor resembles traditional Imperial armor, but instead wore masked morion helmets.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-LOPrince-86)</sup> Their sense of fashion, and even their taste have influenced other neighboring cultures, such as the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE1EH-54)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/LO-misc-Colovian.jpg/148px-LO-misc-Colovian.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Colovian.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Colovian.jpg) Sancre Tor Colovians Examples of Colovian architecture can be seen in townships outside of Cyrodiil such as [Caldera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caldera) and [Pelagiad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagiad), communities on the island-district of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The Colovian style is often conflated with the Western Empire, such as in places like [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-GTV-83)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-MWElone-87)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-MWPelagiad-88)</sup> While the county city, Kvatch takes on the general Imperial style of architecture that was prevalent in the mid-Second Era and across the province,<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-ONKvatch-89)</sup> the other county cities, Anvil,<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-OBBenirus-90)</sup> Chorrol,<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-OBFrancois-91)</sup> and Skingrad have their unique features but bare some semblance of the motif.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-OBRosethorn-92)</sup> On the other side of the Strid River in Valenwood, the city of [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia) and the town of [Greenhill](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greenhill) were built with traditional Colovian stone.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-CBA-93)</sup><sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TCAIH-94)</sup>

As stated before, the Colovians make up most of the working force of Cyrodiil and even the military of the Empire. Most of the ranks in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) comprise Colovians, from the common foot soldier to the higher command. Colovian nobles are usually high-ranked officers in both the Legion and the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy). They are very dedicated to their work and would not even partake in the courtly privileges that entail the position, unlike the nobility of the Imperial City.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> During the Tiber War, Colovian officers were traditionally appointed as provincial governors of human regions in the Third Empire, since it was believed that these regions need the most forthright of the Emperor's closest men.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-52)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e4/OB-place-Great_Forest.jpg/200px-OB-place-Great_Forest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Great_Forest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Great_Forest.jpg) The Great Forest of Colovia One of the largest industries in the region is logging, supplying Colovian Wood for construction and even to the Imperial Legion for their equipment. It is generally in high demand and production is overseen by lumber magnates. The type of wood they harvest is common variants such as [ash](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash) and oak.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GCGP1-4)</sup> Every year, the Colovians and the nearby Wood Elves of Valenwood would meet on the [Strid River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Strid_River) for the annual Stridmeet, where they would trade resources between each other. For the longest time, the Bosmer relied on the meetup for resources from the outside world, but by the time they joined the Aldmeri Dominion, they turned toward sea trade from the Altmer.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-PGE1EAD-31)</sup>

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-icon-Goblin-White_Skin.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-icon-Goblin-White_Skin.png) Mark of the White Skin Tribe [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) are prevalent all over Colovia and tribes have existed for years. The borderland near Valenwood and Elsweyr, specifically the West Weald are prime hunting grounds for the local [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md).<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-OBAh-95)</sup> In the late Third Era, the White Skin Tribe, who lived north of Skingrad in the Imperial Reserve accepted a local Breton, Goblin Jim into their ranks as their shaman.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-OBJim-96)</sup>

Sometime in the Second Era, a great battle took place in the southern Gold Coast between the Shatter Bones, the Blood Skulls, and the Gut Blades. Two enemies, the Eye Sticks, and the Sharp Rocks joined together and participated in the conflict. Toward the end of the battle, however, both the Eye Sticks and the Sharp Rocks turned on each other and in the end, two tribes were created out of the five broken ones. The Rock Sticks, who fled north, and the Sharp Sticks who stuck to themselves and farmed the land. The only witness to this was a hapless [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) tracker that coined the creation of the popular Kvatch tavern song, "The War That Never Was".<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-GCGT-97)</sup>

Years later, the two largest tribes in southwest Chorrol county, the Long Claws, and the Tooth Rocks were displaced by the Fighters Guild and migrated further southwest toward the Gold Coast. The two tribes harassed each other on their passage but banded together to fight a common enemy in the form of the Rock Sticks. Within a month, a single tribe made out of survivors of all three clans was formed called the Long Tooth Tribe and they laid claim to land north of Anvil.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-GCGT-97)</sup>

### Religion and Faith
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9f/ON-interior-Knightsgrave_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Knightsgrave_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Knightsgrave_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Knightsgrave_02.jpg) Shrine of Akatosh on the Gold Coast [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/OB-place-Shrine_of_Kynareth.jpg/200px-OB-place-Shrine_of_Kynareth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Shrine_of_Kynareth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Shrine_of_Kynareth.jpg) Shrine of Kynareth in the Great Forest The Colovians have upheld their worship of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Divines.md) since the proto Cyro-Nords, unlike the Nibenese that venerated [the One](Lore_The_One.md) and followed the strict religion of the Alessian Order for most of the First Era.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-1)</sup> The Colovians are resolutely and religiously Nordic in nature. This can be seen with their worship of [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr), the Cyrodilic aspect of [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor). Shezarr may have a thousand or so cults in the Imperial City, but he is especially worshipped in Colovia as Shor.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-SATD-98)</sup> While they may be devout people who value traditional Imperial virtues, in every generation, there are a few of them that turn toward dark and sinister pursuits. The majority call them the **Woeful Colovians**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLoad Manse-3)</sup>

Cyrodiil's temples to the Divines are ministered by their respective institutions and [the Church](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Church), which is in turn handled by the [Council of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Council_of_the_Nine), formed by the primates in each parish.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-OBChana-99)</sup> Five of the Nine Divines are worshiped in Colovia, but only four of them have temples in the county-seats. The [Akatosh Chantry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akatosh_Chantry) have historically resided in the Grand Cathedral of Kvatch's main street and had at one point become a major political faction in the mid-Second Era under the administration of Primate Artorius Ponticus.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TWATD-100)</sup> Their knightly order, the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) have historical ties to the Gold Coast and built at least two bases in the region, the underground tomb, Knightsgrave and the Enclave of the Hourglass south of Kvatch.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-KLOL-101)</sup>

The [House of Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_of_Dibella) occupy the temple in the Chapelgate District of Anvil and maintain a quiet and tranquil garden where acolytes come to meditate,<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-GTA-102)</sup> while the [School of Julianos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:School_of_Julianos) maintain the large church in the Chapel District of Skingrad.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-GTS-103)</sup> The [Temple of Stendarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Stendarr) have lived in Chorrol's Chapel Street for many years,<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-GTC-67)</sup> since at least the mid-First Era when the city's chapel was overseen by Archbishop Vinicius Imbrex.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-TFA-104)</sup> In the late Third Era, Chorrol was ruled by Lord Charus Valga, a staunch follower of Stendarr and Defender of the Faith. His wife, Countess [Arriana Valga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arriana_Valga) was as much of a supporter to Akatosh and was able to set an example to her people on faith.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-GTC-67)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg/200px-OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol_03.jpg) *The Touch of the Healer's Hand* [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) was the only divine not worshipped in a temple, so instead her followers gathered around a shrine in the Great Forest, west of the [Red Ring Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Red_Ring_Road) and [Fanacasecul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fanacasecul).<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-OBKynareth-105)</sup> To her disciples, nature's environs and the open sky is her great chapel, so they pay tribute in the wilderness.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-OBAvita-106)</sup> The shrine was built in the vicinity of the Grove of Trials, an uninteresting cave to the unsuspecting traveler, but it housed the [Boots of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Boots_of_the_Crusader), guarded by creatures from the forest.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-OBGrotto-107)</sup> For her valiant effort at the [Battle of Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sack_of_Sancre_Tor), the healer [Osla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Osla) was canonized as a [saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Saints) by the Church and had a statue titled "The Touch of the Healer's Hand" built in her honor in Chorrol.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-GTC-67)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-OBPrima-108)</sup><sup>:361</sup>

Over the years, other religious groups have sprung up and established themselves in Colovia. At least three known priories dedicated to the Divines have been established, such as [Gottlesfont](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gottlesfont_Priory), [Weynon Priory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Weynon_Priory), and the Priory of the Nine. Not much can be said about Gottlesfont Priory, other than it is located in the middle of the Great Forest, east of [Brindle Home](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brindle_Home) and northwest of Ceyatatar. Weynon Priory was originally dedicated to Akatosh, the Chief Divine<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-ONRogeraud-109)</sup> but by the time the Third Empire took over, it was occupied by the [Order of Talos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_Talos), a monastic cult dedicated to Talos and allies of the Blades.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-OBBaurus-110)</sup> The last one, the Priory of the Nine was originally the Abbey of the Eight and it was guarded by a knightly order sworn to the Divines. But in 3E 111, an Imperial veteran, Amiel Lannus founded the Knights of the Nine and took the priory for his order.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TKOTN-59)</sup>

### Folklore and Mythology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/OB-place-Chorrol.jpg/200px-OB-place-Chorrol.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Chorrol.jpg) The Great Oak of Chorrol [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/ON-place-Anvil_05.jpg/200px-ON-place-Anvil_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anvil_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Anvil_05.jpg) The Mermaid of Anvil The Colovian West is the host of its own array of myths and folklore, some of which are celebrated in the cities. There are some cases such as the story of [Antus Pinder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Antus_Pinder), who stood alone in a hopeless defense for Kvatch against a much more superior threat. Despite his grave defeat, he was honored by the city, who constructed a statue in his honor in the main square, outside the castle gate. The symbol of Chorrol is the Great Oak, an ancient tree that stands proudly in the center of the city's main square.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-OBLoad-111)</sup> It is a focal piece of the city's iconography and even the [Escutcheon of Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Escutcheon_of_Chorrol), an ebony shield and a symbol of honor and heroism in Chorrol.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-OBLaythe-112)</sup>

However, one of the region's most mysterious stories revolves around the [Mermaid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mermaid) of Anvil, an ancient statue that has managed to enthrall anyone to come across it. There is no information on its origins, so the local populous decided to make their own stories and rumors about it. Among the many rumors is that it depicts a forgotten deity or that it may be an unrecognized aspect of the Divines, or that it was created by some water-based society as a depiction of [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura).<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-TMMOA-113)</sup> There is another story that calls it the Selkie of West Skerry, which is much more fleshed out compared to others. It is said that the seal-maid's skin was stolen by her lover so that she may never leave him for the ocean. However, the lover's wife had secretly cut the skin in two, leaving one half for the Selkie. The creature donned the partial hide and discovered herself part-seal and part-maid. She left for the sea after that but is it said that she guides sailors through the dense fog for the Gold Coast.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-OBPrima-108)</sup><sup>:361</sup> Archaeologists were able to determine that the statue has pre-dated even Anvil itself, but there are no records that mention its existence.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-TMMOA-113)</sup>

The stories of Sancre Tor's founding are varied and contradictory to each other. In some historical accounts, it was founded by Alessia, who received the divine inspiration to rebel against the Ayleids and bring about her slave rebellion, so she built her holy city on the very site.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-TLST-51)</sup> One apocryphal account claims that Sancre Tor was an Ayleid holding that was taken by Alessia and the slaves.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-CTOC-114)</sup> Another myth claims that King Hrol and his shieldthane came to the area to find the spirit of St. Alessia. It was here, that the King copulated with the spirit and supposedly impregnated a mound of mud. That mound became a small mountain dubbed the Golden Hill (known as Sancre Tor in [Ayleidoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleidoon)) by a community of shepherds and bulls. After nine months, the infant Reman was found at its peak.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RE-15)</sup>

A legend that takes place in Colovia speaks of a [daedroth](Lore_Daedra.md) named Vernaccus and a legendary archer named Bourlor. Legend has it that Vernaccus terrorized the Colovian countryside, cursing everything in his wake. He was constantly pressed by his rival, the [daedra seducer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Seducer) Horavatha. Eventually, the legendary archer and champion of Kynareth, Bourlor was called by the mayor of Evensacon to defeat the monster. Bourlor's skill with the bow was unmatched; he is said to have never missed his target. He encountered Vernaccus at a copse near the town and aimed his bow, the Daedra vanished in a pillar of flame before the arrow could pierce him: it stuck to the tree behind him. [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) acknowledged his quick action, and he was soon fearfully venerated by the people all across [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). Bourlor was disgraced and died in isolation, left forgotten and unmourned. Many years later, one of Vernaccus' shrines in Colovia was assaulted by mercenaries, who threw stones at it. The Daedra fought them back, but one of the stones hit the shrine's door, which fell off its rusted hinges and onto his back. He was pierced by Bourlor's arrow, still lodged in the wood since their encounter. Thus was the end of Vernaccus' legacy and his reign of terror.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-VAB-115)</sup>

## Notable Locations
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg/200px-OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Priory_of_the_Nine_Restored.jpg) The Priory of the Nine [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) A harbor-city found along the Abecean Sea. It is the gateway between the Abecean and the Imperial Province. [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) A city amidst the Great Forest, known for the Great Oak, which is part of their iconography for the longest time. [Gold Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold_Road) A major road that runs from Anvil to the Red Ring Road in the Heartlands. It also contains Kvatch and Skingrad. [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) The holy city of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), situated on a plateau that overlooks the Gold Coast and the Colovian Highlands. [Miscarcand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miscarcand) One of the largest Ayleid ruins in the province. It was once the seat of a powerful kingdom. [Priory of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Priory_of_the_Nine) A priory located deep in the wilderness, southeast of Skingrad. The Knights of the Nine lived in the priory since the early-mid Third Era. [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor) The birthplace of Reman I, as well as the burial place for his dynasty. It was here the Amulet of Kings was stored for many years. [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad) The "Gem of Old Colovia", seen as the model of Colovian independence and hardwork. It is also known as a prominent wine country. [Sutch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sutch) A former city of Colovia, on a prairie near the Brena River. [Varen's Wall](Lore_Varen%27s_Wall_(place).md) A large stone wall that divided the Gold Coast from the rest of Colovia. No longer present as of the late Third Era.
