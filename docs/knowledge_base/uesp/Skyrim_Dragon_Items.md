# Dragon Items

**Dragon items** are high-quality [armors](Skyrim_Armor.md) and [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> made out of [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md) [parts](Skyrim_Animal_Parts.md). Dragon armors come in [light](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) **Dragonscale Armor** and [heavy](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md) **Dragonplate Armor** varieties. Dragon weapons are added by the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on, and require that it be installed.

Dragon items can be crafted if the player has the Dragon Armor perk, the final perk in [Smithing's](Skyrim_Smithing.md) perk tree. Unlocking it requires a Smithing skill of 100 and all of the perks in *either* the light armor or heavy armor branches of the perk tree. Note that unlocking this perk does not allow you to then travel down the other armor branch, e.g., if you unlock [Glass Smithing](Skyrim_Glass_Smithing.md) and then Dragon Armor, you cannot then unlock [Daedric Smithing](Skyrim_Daedric_Smithing.md) if you don't already have [Ebony Smithing](Skyrim_Ebony_Smithing.md).

The Dragon Armor perk also allows you to improve Dragon items twice as much. This perk applies to both unenchanted and enchanted items ([generic](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md) or [custom](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked.

## Light Armor

### Dragonscale Armor
Dragonscale is the best light armor in the base game. It appears in leveled lists starting at level 46 (enchanted varieties at level 47). However, even after level 46, Dragonscale armor is twenty times less likely to appear than other armor types. All items can be tempered using 1 sample of [Dragon Scales](Skyrim_Dragon_Scales.md), and having the Dragon Armor perk doubles the quality improvement.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the [Brawler's Dragonscale Gauntlets](Skyrim_Brawler%27s_Dragonscale_Gauntlets.md), a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differs only in appearance, while a pair with a 16 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

## Heavy Armor

### Dragonplate Armor
Dragonplate is tied with [Stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> as the second-best heavy armor in the game, beaten only by the elusive [Daedric Armor](Skyrim_Daedric_Armor.md). It appears in leveled lists starting at level 40 (enchanted varieties at level 41). However, even after level 40, Dragonplate armor is twenty times less likely to appear than other armor types. Either [Dragon Scales](Skyrim_Dragon_Scales.md) or [Dragon Bone](Skyrim_Dragon_Bone.md) are used to [temper](Skyrim_Workbench.md) the armor:

- 1 Dragon Bone is needed to temper the cuirass and shield.
- 1 Dragon Scales is needed to temper the boots, gauntlets and helmet.

Having the Dragon Armor perk doubles the quality improvement.

Although Dragonplate armor has a lower base armor rating than Daedric, the difference in base armor rating is not always significant. A full set of either type of armor can provide a character with an armor rating well past the 567 displayed armor rating needed to achieve the [armor cap](Skyrim_Armor.md#Armor_Cap) by applying the right combination of [Heavy Armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md) perks, [Fortify Heavy Armor](Skyrim_Fortify_Heavy_Armor.md) enchantments while wearing the armor, and/or use of [Fortify Smithing](Skyrim_Fortify_Smithing.md) enchanted gear and potions while improving the armor. At that point, the lighter weight of Dragonplate (79 vs 96) may make it more attractive than Daedric (unless you have the [Conditioning](Skyrim_Conditioning.md) perk or [The Steed Stone](Skyrim_The_Steed_Stone.md) ability, both of which make equipped weight zero). Also, the materials used to make Dragonplate armor are generally available in larger quantities than Daedric Armor. Note that getting the Dragon Armor perk uses the same number of total Smithing perks as the [Daedric Armor](Skyrim_Daedric_Armor.md) perk, if you take the left-hand branch of the Smithing tree.

Another benefit of dragonplate armor is if you usually use light armor for yourself, focused on the left side of the Smithing Perk Tree, and want to make some powerful heavy armor for your [followers](Skyrim_Followers.md), or do the reverse.

If you are smithing Dragonplate Armor [for profit](Skyrim_Making_Money.md), consider making Dragonplate Cuirasses, and then temper them with a Dragon Bone to increase the value.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the [Brawler's Dragonplate Gauntlets](Skyrim_Brawler%27s_Dragonplate_Gauntlets.md), a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 20 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

## Dragonbone Weapons<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>
Dragonbone [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) are the highest-quality weapons available in the game, but are only available if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed. They do not appear in leveled lists, and can only be obtained via crafting or by looting a [Keeper](Skyrim_Keeper.md). All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using 1 Dragon Bone, and having the Dragon Armor Smithing perk doubles the quality improvement. Dragonbone Crossbows are only available with the [Expanded Crossbow Pack](Skyrim_Expanded_Crossbow_Pack.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

## Notes
- It is possible to craft Dragon items before ever starting Dragon Rising (when Dragons begin to appear in the game world) or reaching level 46, when they start to have the chance to appear in loot. Northeast from the village of [Rorikstead](Skyrim_Rorikstead.md) is a hill with a shrine of Akatosh, and a single Dragon Scale; this scale respawns every 10 in-game days. If you have *Dawnguard* installed, there is also a single sample of Dragon Bone in [Valerica's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Valerica) laboratory. As long as you have 100 in Smithing and the appropriate perk, you can wait in intervals of 10 days away from these locations (such as one's house, or the inn at Rorikstead itself for the scale) and gather enough of the components to create what you want.

## Bugs
- Dragonbone helmets can have a variety of clipping issues with hair on several races. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.5, fixes this bug.
- Dragonscale boots can have a twisted right foot animation due to an issue with the bone setup. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.3.4, fixes this bug.
