—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Orcrest ([/ˈ ɔːr. k r ɛ s t/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ***orr** -crest*) is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> situated over the barren wastes of [the Scar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Scar). In modern history, Orcrest is known as the center of commerce for the northern kingdom of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), attracting merchants and people from all over the region and beyond.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTNE-2)</sup> In the province's ancient history as the [sixteen kingdoms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixteen_Kingdoms), Orcrest was a kingdom of rogues and thieves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONRajhin-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/ON-place-The_Scar_04.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Scar_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Scar_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Scar_04.jpg) A portion of the Scar near Orcrest [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/15/ON-place-Orcrest_Red_Senche_Alley.jpg/200px-ON-place-Orcrest_Red_Senche_Alley.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Orcrest_Red_Senche_Alley.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Orcrest_Red_Senche_Alley.jpg) Red Senche Alley Orcrest is located in central Anequina, on a plateau and cliff face in the middle of the Scar canyon. It is completely surrounded by the steep ravine, only connected to the region's main roads by a large stone bridge over the canyon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup> This great bridge connects the two disparate areas of northern Elsweyr,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTNE-2)</sup> the [high savannahs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Savannah) near the Prowl and the [Tenmar borderlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tenmar_Forest) near the Merryvale [moon sugarcane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar_Cane) fields.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup>

In modern times, there a scant few settlements outside of the city, except for the [Desert Wind Adeptorium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Desert_Wind_Adeptorium) to the northwest and the Stitches, a shantytown that draws northern Elsweyr's criminals and malcontents,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONIsadati-6)</sup> and established from the former kingdom of [Meirvale](Lore_Meirvale.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONLoad Meirvale-7)</sup> Much of the land is filled with ruins, such as the structures in the Ashen Scar and the Weeping Scar. It is also populated by desert creatures, such as [harpies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Harpy) and [terror birds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Terror_Bird) in the lower canyon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup> Orcrest is neighbored by several settlements, such as Kings Walk to the north-northeast and Neumar Walk to the south-southwest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup>

Orcrest is a walled city divided into four sections: the Lower City, the Market District, Red Senche Alley, and the Upper City. From the city's gate is the Market District, a sprawling area of caravans and merchant stalls.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup> In its prime, Orcrest's bazaar is like a jewel in the Scar desert and brims with activity.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONZerith-8)</sup> From here, the city diverges northeast and southeast to the Lower City and Red Senche Alley, respectively. Each district is mostly a narrow corridor filled with homes and meets the Upper City to the east. This fairly open parkland area has short walls around its streets. On its northeast corner is the town hall,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup> a fairly large manor that, in times of plague and famine, was used to quarantine people. Anyone who suffered physically and mentally was left in its lower halls to be locked away and forgotten.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONViti-9)</sup> The Orcrest Sewers is built under the city and connects across the Lower City, Market District, and Red Senche Alley.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/ON-place-Desert_Wind_Caverns.jpg/200px-ON-place-Desert_Wind_Caverns.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Desert_Wind_Caverns.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Desert_Wind_Caverns.jpg) A claw-dance adeptorium near Orcrest Orcrest, like the rest of the sixteen kingdoms, was originally a nomadic clan that cemented itself by mastering a specific skill or trade, and ultimately, swore fealty to the ancient [Moon-Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon-Emperor). But as time passed, the clans were forced into their roles and limited geographies,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AAPAI-12)</sup> with the original clan of Orcrest establishing itself as rogues and thieves.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONRajhin-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup> The clan settled in central-northern Elsweyr, in a desert region known as the Scar, named after the eponymous canyon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup> The Scar itself is the subject of legends, with some claiming it was created by either [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh), the Khajiiti God of Time, or [Khunzar-ri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khunzar-ri), the mythical hero of ancient Elsweyr.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONZamarak-13)</sup>

In the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), Orcrest and the sixteen kingdoms were competing fiefdoms ruled by a cruel hunter-aristocracy. In northern Elsweyr, severe droughts and famines were constant and the many skirmishes between Hunt-Lords meant that the peasantry were always drafted for war. The only ones that had any push back against the Hunt-Lords was the [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance), and its adepts and [Torval](Lore_Torval.md) clergy who wielded significant cultural influence. Years after Takanzin the Striped-Death's hired attack of the [Rawl'kha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rawl%27kha) temple and the Hunt-Lords' subsequent suppression of any religious order, the adepts went into hiding and developed the martial tradition of [claw-dances](Lore_Claw-Dances.md). By the late days of the Merethic Era, the peasantry finally revolted against the Hunt-Lords after one of the worst famines in Khajiiti history. One of these uprisings broke out in Orcrest, and within a century, the regal hunter caste was abolished and a new kind of nobility was established thereafter.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OOTKMT-14)</sup>

In time, the Kingdom of Orcrest prospered, but with the advent of [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) of [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200), the sixteen kingdoms faced a mass number of deaths, and their tribal functions had to change forcibly. Orcrest and the northern kingdoms fell in with the Kingdom of [Ne Quin-al](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ne_Quin-al), or more commonly known by its [Cyrodilic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrodilic) name, [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), who were nomads of arid deserts. The new patchwork of former kingdoms resumed ancient tribal traditions and customs, unlike the southerners in [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md), who took on aspects of foreign cultures.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AAPAI-12)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3-15)</sup> Orcrest under the Kingdom of Anequina became a center of commerce and a place for the rest of Anequina to congregate.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTNE-2)</sup>

### The Knahaten Flu and Onward
In [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) began its devastating spread across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3A-16)</sup> It was only five years later in [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565), did the flu first appear in Elsweyr via the [Black Kiergo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Kiergo) slums of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TEATC-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-HOSAO-18)</sup> Within a moments notice, it spread across the entire province, including Orcrest.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TEATC-17)</sup> But of the cities affected by the Knahaten Flu in northern Elsweyr, Orcrest suffered the most.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONLoad Orcrest-19)</sup> No one knows how the flu arrived into Orcrest, or who was responsible for creating it, with some blame going toward the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) or even [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of Pestilience. As more people died from the disease, bodies were piled up, and while some tried to flee the city, rumors claimed it was much worse outside.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FSOTF-20)</sup> Volunteers from across the land, such as Ubraz, a talented alchemist of his time, traveled to Orcrest in late [Sun's Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun%27s_Dusk) to treat victims but the disease was evidently too potent, and took their lives as well.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONShazah-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SD-22)</sup>

Taking advantage of the Khajiits' weakened state, war broke out as the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) annexed most of northern Elsweyr.<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Annex)</sup> Orcrest and nearby [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) were taken over by the Colovians, but a resistance force led by the warrior Namu charged in to retake them. Though she died in battle to General Quintilius, Orcrest and Riverhold were reclaimed<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONKhali-23)</sup> but Orcrest's damage from the Knahaten Flu was too severe, and for several years since then, it was abandoned, left to rot and deemed haunted by the surviving populace.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONLoad Orcrest-19)</sup> By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), as the rest of Elsweyr recovered, nothing had changed in Orcrest. But even so, people still lived in the city and were affected in more ways than one by the flu. Those that survived barely scraped by and hid, but others went [mad](Lore_Madness_(disease).md) and resorted to extreme tactics. Some became obsessed with burning the city and using fire, while others resorted to cannibalism, like a group who lived in the ruined Red Senche Alley and were led by their so-called cannibal king, the [Pahmar-raht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pahmar-raht) named Eludin.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONOrcrest-5)</sup>

In 2E 582, a group of Peryite worshippers traveled to Orcrest to find evidence that their Lord was responsible for the city's destruction. With help from an [adventurer](Lore_The_Vestige.md), they succeeded in their mission, while the adventurer rescued a few people still stuck in the city.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONUnhealthy-24)</sup> In time, Orcrest recovered and was re-populated. During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Orcrest was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Amtaba and had a rivalry with [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AR-11)</sup>

### Known Rulers

The Sovereign of Orcrest
The desert cats of the baking sand
The windblown rogues of Orcrest*
—Excerpt from *[The Sixteen Kingdoms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Sixteen_Kingdoms)* As one of the sixteen clans, it was led by a clan leader.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-HOSAO-18)</sup> Up until the late Merethic Era, Orcrest and the sixteen kingdoms were fiefdoms ruled by Hunt-Lords, until they were overthrown by the peasantry.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OOTKMT-14)</sup> By the late Third Era, Orcrest was ruled by a King/Queen, who typically ruled a surrounding kingdom.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AR-11)</sup> In the traditional Khajiiti child's chant, *[The Sixteen Kingdoms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Sixteen_Kingdoms)*, Orcrest's ruler is referred to as the Sovereign of Orcrest.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/ON-mount-White_Lion.jpg/200px-ON-mount-White_Lion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-White_Lion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-White_Lion.jpg) The White Lion *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Khajiit.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Khajiit.md#Society)** sections on the Khajiit page.* Orcrest, in its ancient history, was a place that gathered thieves and rogues.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSK-4)</sup> The Cat-King of Thieves, [Rajhin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rajhin) visited the city before his ascension into godhood. One of his shadows claims that he never felt so welcome in a place like this.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONRajhin-3)</sup> Today his original home of Senchal is known as a place for criminal activity,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE1-25)</sup> while Orcrest is known now as a positive hub of activity. It is said that a Khajiit will part ways with their tail here if it were for the right price.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTNE-2)</sup> The story of how Orcrest and its original clan of Khajiit acquired its name is told in the lullaby *Hasa Zha'ja*. Likewise, the transition of clan name to place name is described in a poem called *Zhan Zhab*, written by a clanless Khajiit in [1E 2243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2243).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AAPAI-12)</sup> A common [exclamation](Lore_Exclamations.md) is "Dust of Orcrest".<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ARKhajiit-26)</sup> Some of Orcrest's regional foods and drinks include [agony pale ale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ale)<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONAle-27)</sup> and garlic apple jelly.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONJelly-28)</sup>

Many different creatures live in Orcrest, such as the local [jerboa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jerboa), who live in small nooks and crannies throughout the city.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONJerboa-29)</sup> The Orcrest [Rathound](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dog#Rathound) is a favorite among beggars as they make excellent scavengers. Survivors of the Knahaten Flu in Orcrest claim that the rathound's uncanny ability to sense danger led them to exodus out of the city before the flu destroyed it.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONRathound-30)</sup>

The King of Anequina at the time of its unification with Pellitine, [Keirgo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keirgo) had an honor guard based in Orcrest. During his wedding ceremony with Queen [Eshita](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eshita) of the south, the Orcrest Honor Guard was there and mounted on twenty [white lions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lion), a highly prestigious mount in Elsweyr.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONWhite-31)</sup> The Orcrest Sewers, even when the city was abandoned, were rife with wildlife but also rumored monsters like Noordigloop the Clog, a giant [voriplasm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Voriplasm) that was transported from [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and escaped during the Knahaten Flu.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-NTC-32)</sup>

One of Orcrest's most well-known residents was the famous tapestry maker named [Cherim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cherim). His first masterpiece was called the *Heart of Anequina*, which depicted a momentous [battle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Anequina) during the [Five Year War](Lore_Five_Year_War.md) that he was involved in. Emperor [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) owned at least ten of his tapestries, and his subjects were usually historical moments throughout Tamriel, like the Fall of White-Gold Tower and the Velothi Exodus.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CHOA-10)</sup>
