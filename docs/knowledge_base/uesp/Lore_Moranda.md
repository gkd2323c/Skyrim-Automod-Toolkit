It was among the only modern sites that still bore their ancient names by the time of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-2)</sup>

## History
During the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md) were dispersed to cities across Cyrodiil for safekeeping, one of them being kept in Moranda. Following the sacking of White-Gold and the Ayleid exodus of Cyrodiil, the statues were left behind and forgotten.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion-1)</sup>

When the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) excavated the site of [Ouze](Lore_Ouze.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), they took a necromantic gem array from Moranda and used it to imprison the [Voice of Ouze](Lore_Voice_of_Ouze.md), a spiritual mediary between the Oathbreakers and [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md). The ruins themselves were later cut off from entry.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins had been opened and were occupied by monsters, and [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) contracted the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to retrieve one of the Ten Ancestors from there.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBCollector-4)</sup>
