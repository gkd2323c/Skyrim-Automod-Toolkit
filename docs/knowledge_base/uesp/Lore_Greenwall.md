[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/SR-place-Fort_Greenwall.jpg/200px-SR-place-Fort_Greenwall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Greenwall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Greenwall.jpg) Fort Greenwall circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Greenwall was a settlement located in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), north of the [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) city of [Riften](Lore_Riften.md). Throughout history it has primarily served as a military outpost due to the strategic placement of **Fort Greenwall**. A small natural aquifer known as Greenwall Cave runs beneath the fort.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SR-1)</sup>

## History
A [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) settlement was once built on the site of Greenwall, although its name and origins are unknown. The Snow Elves were driven nearly to extinction following [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s invasion of Skyrim, and in time the ruins became buried deep underground.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-3)</sup>

Fort Greenwall was originally built to guard the road between Riften and [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). During the construction of its underground passages, the elven ruins were partially excavated. The substructure beneath Fort Greenwall was described during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) by archaeologist [Hrerm House-builder](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Hrerm_House-builder), although he purposefully failed to mention the discovery of the elven ruins and described the architecture as entirely Nordic.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-3)</sup>

Fort Greenwall was sacked by the forces of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md) during the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572). The fort was then neglected by the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), who failed to refurbish and regarrison it.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup> By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the fort had been taken over by bandits. They were cleared out when the thane of Riften's housecarl sent a shipment of cursed wine along the road, which the bandits duly stole and began to drink. Drunk, and unable to sate their thirst with the magic wine, the fort was then easily stormed.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Following the sacking of [Fallowstone Hall](Lore_Fallowstone_Hall.md) by [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) that year, an artifact known as Winter's Hammer came into the possession of the bandit leader at Fort Greenwall, although this weapon was sacred to the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) who promptly retrieved it.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

By the time of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), Greenwall had become a village, being ruled by Lady Hante. It had a rivalry with Reich Corigate.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including Riften to the east, and [Sunguard](Lore_Sunguard.md) to the northwest.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Map Arena-7)</sup> The settlement was eventually abandoned, and by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) the ruins of Fort Greenwall had once again come under bandit control. During the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), the fort served as a point of contention between the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), with both sides struggling for control.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SR-1)</sup>
