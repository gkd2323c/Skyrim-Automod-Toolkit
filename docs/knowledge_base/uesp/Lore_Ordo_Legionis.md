Ordo Legionis *An introduction to the Imperial Legion*
---
The most disciplined and effective military force in history, the [Imperial Legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) preserve the peace and rule of law in the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). At need, the legion garrisons can be swiftly mobilized to protect against invasions or internal disorders, but in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) District of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), the local forts help to insure law and order, providing guards to supplement the local guard units of the [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), and [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md).

There are five legion garrisons in Vvardenfell District. The three town garrisons -- [Moonmoth Legion Fort](Lore_Moonmoth_Legion_Fort.md) in [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), [Buckmoth Legion Fort](Lore_Buckmoth_Legion_Fort.md) in [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md), and Fort Pelagiad in [Pelagiad](Lore_Pelagiad.md) -- are at full complement. The Hawkmoth Legion garrisoned at Castle [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md) is an elite honor guard unit, and also at full complement. The frontier installation, Fort Darius in [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md) village, is currently the only under-strength garrison on Vvardenfell. Qualified citizens seeking enlistment in the Imperial Legion should apply to the commander of that garrison, General Darius.

The Legion selects candidates on the basis of superior endurance, the soldierly virtue, and trustworthy personality, the citizen's virtue, for service in the Legion is the model for the duties of Imperial citizenship. Troopers are expected to demonstrate mastery of the long blade, the spear, and blunt weapons. Legion troops train with shield and heavy armor, and so must be skilled at blocking and moving in heavy armor.

As a trooper or knight, you must master the long blade, spear, and blunt weapons. You must block whatever blows you can, and take unblocked blows upon your heavy armor. Recruit must also be proficient at athletics, both to march long distances with heavy packs, and to advance and maneuver, charge and retreat on the field of battle.
