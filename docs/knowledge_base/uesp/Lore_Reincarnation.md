# Reincarnation


## Reincarnation Variations

### Reincarnation as Twin Souls
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Moon-Singer_Talbira.jpg) Moon-Singer Talbira, a spirit who reincarnated herself into a living body [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Cantor_Krin%27ze.jpg) Cantor Krin'ze, the Twilight Cantor whose soul Talbira merged with There have been cases where the [spirit](Lore_Souls.md) of a [dead mortal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) has entered the body of a living being and integrated itself with the living soul residing there. Though ghosts have the ability to possess and animate both living and dead bodies as well as inanimate objects by entering them,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONJanne-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONGalga-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONUldor-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESORun-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONGhora-9)</sup> such phenomena are said to not be the same thing as this spiritual integration. Whereas in a possession the soul of the spirit remains distinct and either exerts direct control over the host body or influences the host's thoughts and emotions, with the host soul sometimes being expelled or having its identity erased outright,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOFarwen-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOAwa-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Spirithunter-12)</sup> the integration phenomenon results instead in the soul of the spirit merging with the soul of the living host without exerting control, thus the spirit is effectively reborn or reincarnated into the living host, who will than start to experience the memories of that spirit as if they'd lived through them, and manifesting the knowledge and abilities the soul reincarnated within them possessed and feeling its emotions as their own. The phenomenon has also been described as a spirit being resurrected as the "twin soul" of its host. The initial entry of the soul into the living body is accompanied by a brief bout of intense pain, after the rebirth, other spirits who knew the reincarnated soul in life can sense its presence within its reincarnated form and realize the host soul and the reincarnated soul as now being the same. Though such spiritual rebirth is possible, it is said that it is relatively rare for spirits to behave in that manner.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Zerith-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Cantor-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Notes-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Journal-16)</sup>

### Ascended Ancestors
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Dragon.jpg) The Dragon Luminary, theorized to be an Ascended Ancestor [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/ON-creature-The_Gryphon.jpg/200px-ON-creature-The_Gryphon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Gryphon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-The_Gryphon.jpg) The Gryphon Luminary, theorized to be an Ascended Ancestor Some sources, such as [Spinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spinner) [Girnalin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Girnalin) claim that mortal spirits experience a change upon reaching [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), with many souls of mortals becoming spirits of a different sort in that place.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONGirnalin-17)</sup> Many reports from across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) describe a phenomenon where the souls of the dead adopt a variety of forms to return and speak with the living. Animals, plants, or even fearsome visions, are all reported manifestations. It is theorized that the [Luminaries](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Luminary), immortal beings of pure magic, might have originated as mortal souls, being the spirits of Ascended Ancestors that transformed into their current state after death due to this described phenomenon. As the Luminaries themselves can't recall their origins, this theory remains unproven.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LAS-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-19)</sup> Whatever the case, however, a Luminary can indeed be created out of the essence of a mortal soul, as was the case with the Crow Luminary, who was created through a ritual devised by [Ulfsild the Evergreen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulfsild) out of a portion of her own essence. Though the process is straining and the restoration of the spirit takes time, a soul that was used in such a manner will eventually recover.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Crow-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONU-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Fable-22)</sup>

### Resurrection of Lingering Spirits
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-npc-Zerith-var.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Zerith-var.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Zerith-var.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Zerith-var.jpg) Zerith-var during his second life, his eyes filled with Azurah's soulfire [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/ON-npc-Memory_of_Zerith-var.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Memory_of_Zerith-var.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Memory_of_Zerith-var.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Memory_of_Zerith-var.jpg) Zerith-var during his first life The deeds of a spirit in life can sometimes cloud their path to the afterlife. Such spirits, held back by guilt and unresolved affairs, might refuse entry into the afterlife even if they have been accepted into it, holding themselves back as long as their unresolved affairs remain. In some cases these powerful unresolved emotions might even cause the soul itself to crack and be wounded, allowing for a [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) to be created within it. While being summoned to provide aid to the living via lending their strength or inhabiting and animating bodies willingly through redemptive [necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) such as that employed by the Order of the Hidden Moon can help spirits resolve their issues and move on to the afterlife, there have been cases where, through the intercession of a [deity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods), such lingering spirits have been reborn into the world instead. Spirits reborn in this manner have their soul returned to their resurrected bodies, exhibiting only fragmentary memories of their time in the afterlife. The act of rebirth can fill the being of these revived individuals with [Soulfire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Magic), which in the case of spirits resurrected from [Azurah's Crossing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonshadow) makes their eyes burn bright blue, a phenomenon also said to occur with those who visit Moonshadow.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-KJ-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Azure-24)</sup> Forgiving one's self can heal the Void-wound, allowing that spirit to proceed to the afterlife beyond. At the point at which this is achieved the resurrected soul will sense it, giving them the option to return to the afterlife or to remain in the living world to live out their second life. [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) call this type of rebirth a "Second Pounce" and consider it a not entirely dissimiliar but nonetheless distinct form of rebirth to that of reincarnating as the Twin Soul of one of the living.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Ravith-25)</sup> The [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) has been known to resurrect certain souls in her care in this manner.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Cantor-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Notes-15)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Zerith-13)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONLi De-26)</sup>

## Culture

### Argonian
[Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) souls return to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) after death.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESODeyapa-27)</sup> From here, the Argonian soul travels down the river called Rebirth.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ESOXukas-28)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ESOJaxsik-29)</sup> In some cases, particularly old and wise souls can help lead other souls through the cycle.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ESOSap-30)</sup>

Argonians hold the belief that those of other races are also reincarnated.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-31)</sup>

### Breton
[Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids) believe in a natural cycle of death and rebirth,<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONDFPY-32)</sup> for [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre) blesses the living with the same breath that flows across the world, and it is seen as a blessing for him to withdraw that breath. To die means that one once lived, and at the end there are always new beginnings.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONDFPY-32)</sup> Indeed, druids believe in reincarnation. [Dovah-flies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dovah-Fly) are such an example; they're believed to be their ancestors given new life.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-33)</sup>

### Dunmer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/1/15/RG-render-Dram_02.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-render-Dram_02.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-render-Dram_02.gif) Dram Reincarnation as a concept plays a fundamental role in [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) theology. It is taught that the [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almsivi) of living gods, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia), and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) are incarnations of their predecessors the [Anticipations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-34)</sup>

Upon the death of [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar), the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) Hortator, sources claim that the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) vowed that Nerevar would come back to set things right.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-NARM-35)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TBORM-36)</sup> This led to [prophecies](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) and [cults](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevarine_Cult) forming over an individual known as the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) who would be Nerevar reborn and make good on Azura's word.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ncn-37)</sup> Many individuals claimed to be this figure of prophecy, but all turned out to be [False Incarnates](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Failed_Incarnates) until one arose in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) meeting all the criteria. This figure would go on to defeat the feared enemy of the Dunmer, [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TCot SH-38)</sup>

The Dunmer assassin [Dram](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dram), through means not fully known, was a thrice-born individual having two prior incarnations before his confrontation with [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-RGArchive-39)</sup>

### Ice Tribes
Little is known about the beliefs of the [Ice Tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ice_Tribes). The most powerful warriors among them are known as Thriceborn, and the tribes are also known to have utilized a powerful beast known as the [Gehenoth Thriceborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gehenoth).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-40)</sup> However, it is unknown if either of these examples are literal instances of reincarnation.

### Imperial
The [Saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Saints) of the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) Church [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) was strongly associated with the concept of reincarnation.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TSOP1-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONCK-42)</sup> It is speculated that him being a thrice incarnated individual is possibly why he held the epithet Pelinal the Third.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TSOP1-41)</sup> He was best known under his common name Pelinal, along with other identities in Hans the Fox and Harrald Hairy Breeks.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-BTAOM-43)</sup>

As he was dying from wounds received in battle with [Umaril the Unfeathered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umaril), his nephew [Morihaus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morihaus) predicted that Pelinal would come again.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TA-44)</sup> This prognostication proved accurate, as the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) eventually proved to be an embodiment of Pelinal Whitestrake, the Divine Crusader reborn.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-OBProphet-45)</sup> The Hero of Kvatch would finish what Pelinal did not and destroy Umaril utterly.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Ut U-46)</sup>

### Khajiit
The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) believe that their souls fill many vessels and take many forms before the end.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-47)</sup> Their tradition holds that there is only one [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) spiritual leader alive at any time, and that each Mane is simply one individual spirit reborn into different bodies.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-PGE1EC-48)</sup>

One Khajiit account describes how a Khajiit [soul](Lore_Souls.md) travels to [Azurah's Crossing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonshadow) after death, and encounters Azurah herself. At that point the spirit remembers that it has lived and experienced the passage to the afterlife many times, and mentions it to Azurah. To this observation Azurah responds that the spirit has "walked [many paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md)" to stand before her.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Crossing-49)</sup>

### Reachfolk
One of the [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) [clans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk_Clans) was known for their belief that messages from their ancestors were conveyed through [Powderwhite Coneys](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rabbit). Certain sources even considered these animals to be these ancestral figures in their own right.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ESOPC-50)</sup> [Cicada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cicada) husks represent rebirth to the [Crow-Eye Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow-Eye_Clan).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Shaman_Ulvoch-51)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-MQI2-52)</sup>

### Redguard
While numerous Tamrielic cultures hold beliefs in various forms of reincarnation, [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) adherents of [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal) hold to the [cyclical nature of the world](Lore_Kalpa.md), envisioning the reincarnation of the world itself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Co S-1)</sup>

### Skaal
The Skaal hold the belief that when a living being passes away, its spirit is reunited with the [All-Maker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:All-Maker), who subsequently reshapes it into a fresh entity and reintroduces it into the material realm to embark on a new existence in a different form. Consequently, the Skaal perceive death differently from other [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). They regard all life as sacred and strive to coexist harmoniously with nature, minimizing their impact on the environment whenever feasible.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-COTAM-53)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-LOS-54)</sup>
