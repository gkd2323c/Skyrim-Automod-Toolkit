## Adamantium
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/56/TR-ing-Adamantium_Ore.jpg/200px-TR-ing-Adamantium_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-ing-Adamantium_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-ing-Adamantium_Ore.jpg) A shard of adamantium ore [Adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md) (also called **Adamantine**) is a rare and ageless metal used for making valuable armor and [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md). It is typically seen in a lustrous silvery color dubbed "Adamantine Silver", though adamantium equipment can also have a stone blue hue or a dark grey coloration. The ore is very heavy and can be used in alchemy. Adamantium armor can appear as either a high-quality heavy weight armor or medium weight armor. It is above the quality of [Mithril](Lore_Mithril.md) or [Orichalcum](Lore_Orichalcum.md), but is inferior to [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) and [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md). The armor has a high potential for enchantment. Adamantium weapons can harm magical creatures that resist normal weapons.

## Brass
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-style_material-Tempered_Brass.png) Brass is a brownish metal used by artisans and craftsmen throughout history in innumerable ways, ranging from jewelry, devices, decoration and household items.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Contra Brassdevice-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Contra Brasstele-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Contra Brasslamp-9)</sup>

Similarly, a piece of polished brass was used to represent Numidium in the construction of the [Staff of Towers](Lore_Staff_of_Towers.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Brassstaff-10)</sup> Brass was also widely used in the creations of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), such as the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), [factotums](Lore_Factotum.md), and [fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md). Tempered brass was used by blacksmiths to forge arms and armor in the style of [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOBrass-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOCM56-13)</sup>

## Bronze
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-style_material-Bronze.png) Bronze is a soft brownish alloy consisting primarily of [copper](Lore_Copper.md) and [tin](Lore_Tin.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOEJ-14)</sup> It has been used by blacksmiths and craftsmen throughout history in innumerable ways, ranging from jewelry, armor, weapons, decoration and household items.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Contra Medal-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Contra Ring-16)</sup> Bronze is said to easily mistaken for [Dwarven Metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md), so much so that some craftsmen have been known to create fake replicas of Dwemer arms and armor using the metal.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Dv1-17)</sup>

The usage of bronze among the armies of various races is notable, particularly in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). In the early days of King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md)'s reign, [steel](Lore_Steel.md) was limited, so bronze plate armor made up the bulk of his army's cuirasses.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOCM87-18)</sup> Similarly, bronze metal featured heavily in ancient Orcish breastplates.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOCM88-19)</sup> Bronze was also used extensively by the ancient pre- [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) [Argonians](Lore_Argonians.md) tribes in the forging of their arms and armor.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ESOCM70-20)</sup> During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it was used by blacksmiths to forge arms and armor in the style of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) clans.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOBronze-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOCM12-22)</sup>

Bronze is also used widely in the art of bronzing, whereby a layer of bronze is applied to a surface, giving it a bronze finish. Notable examples include the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md), who sometimes observe a tradition where they bronze their amputated body parts after replacement.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Contra Hand-23)</sup>

Teeth are sometimes bronzed, particularly in Breton culture where the teeth of young children are bronzed and saved as a remembrance.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Contra Baby-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Contra Egg-25)</sup>. Other times, the teeth of daedroth are bronzed and crafted into items, such as hairpins.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Contra Hair-26)</sup>

## Copper
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/ON-node-Copper_Seam.jpg/200px-ON-node-Copper_Seam.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Copper_Seam.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Copper_Seam.jpg) An exposed copper deposit Copper is a dark orange metal used by various artisans and craftsmen. During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) copper seams were commonly found near cliffs and mountains across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Online-32)</sup> Mined copper can be ground into dust and refined into small ingots that can be used to create jewelry,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup> household items or tools.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOSCG-34)</sup> Blacksmiths have also been known to use copper as accents or decorations when creating arms and armor.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOCM12-22)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-IEGBM-35)</sup> The surface of copper can turn into a greenish layer of film called patina.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Patina-36)</sup> Its alchemical properties can be used to detect magic.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-DGcopper-37)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-MBBOS-38)</sup>

Its earliest know usage dates back to the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), when ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) civilizations used copper in various crafts.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ESOCDC-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESODC-40)</sup> Copper was used prominently throughout the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), from copper wiring found inside in [Brassilisks](Lore_Brassilisk.md)<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ESOFFo C-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ESOPets-42)</sup> to the artificial trees and shrubs that dot the land.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOFSC-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ESOALP-44)</sup> Copper patina is also used in [Sunforge](Lore_Sunforge.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Patina-36)</sup>

## Corundum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/SR-activity-Mining_%28Corundum%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Corundum%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Corundum).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Corundum).jpg) An exposed corundum seam Corundum is a dark orange metal used by blacksmiths and craftsmen. A freshly exposed surface of corundum had a yellowish-orange color and when extracted produced corundum ore. This ore could further be smelted into ingots, or combined with iron ore to create steel.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-45)</sup>

Corundum was used by blacksmiths to forge arms and armor in the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) style during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), and was widely available.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOCM4-46)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOCorundum-47)</sup> Steel plate armor can be made by adding iron to molten corundum. The resulting alloy is stronger than either metal by itself. Corundum is said to be a finicky material, requiring the heat from the forge to be steady and not vary much.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-HAF-48)</sup>

Corundum was relatively common throughout [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), and the province was home to several corundum mines.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Skyrim-49)</sup>

## Dwarven Adamant
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/ON-crown_store-Adamant_Dwarven_Senche.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Adamant_Dwarven_Senche.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Adamant_Dwarven_Senche.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Adamant_Dwarven_Senche.jpg) A vamidium made of adamant [Dwarven Adamant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Adamant), also known as **Adamant**, is a white-hued alloy of ores of unknown origin, believed to be the most durable material ever made. It is among the rarest materials on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).

The Dwemer used adamant, along with other materials, such as [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md), [vitrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vitrine), and [dwarven metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md), for constructing certain [vamidiums](Lore_Vamidium.md).

## Dwarven Metal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/SR-item-Dwarven_Metal_Ingot.jpg/200px-SR-item-Dwarven_Metal_Ingot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Dwarven_Metal_Ingot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Dwarven_Metal_Ingot.jpg) A Dwarven Metal ingot [Dwarven Metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md) (or **Dwarven Brass**) refers to heavy, exotic metals used primarily by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). It is usually copper in color, although it can also be yellow, grey, or green. The Dwemer used the metal extensively, from architecture to cutlery, most notably for [Dwemer Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md). To the other races of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), it is primarily of interest for use in smithing weapons and armor.

Dwarven weapons are prized for their ability to resist corrosion and retain their edge, while Dwarven armor has distinctive impact-absorbing qualities. For this reason, Dwemer artifacts are highly prized by adventurers and collectors. The metal itself is rare and precious, scraps of which are prized both by collectors of antiquities and students of metals and enchantments. Only the most skilled blacksmiths have the ability to emulate ornate Dwarven craftsmanship, although the metal is often incorporated into cultural designs. True Dwarven metal was made using an unknown process that many metallurgists have tried to replicate unsuccessfully. The only way to obtain the metal is to melt down existing Dwemer items made from the substance. The Dwarves required the intense heat of molten lava to forge this metal.

## Dwarven Ore
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/ON-node-Dwarven_Ore.jpg/200px-ON-node-Dwarven_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Dwarven_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Dwarven_Ore.jpg) A Dwarven Ore deposit [Dwarven Ore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Ore) is a naturally occurring ore deposit that could be found across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Dwarven Ore acquired its name due to the visual resemblance it bears to [Dwarven Metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md) when forged; however, it lacks the metal's durability, and metallurgists have concluded that it has no connection to the metal harvested from actual Dwemer devices.

## Ebony-steel
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/ON-crown_store-Ebon_Steel_Dwarven_Spider.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Ebon_Steel_Dwarven_Spider.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ebon_Steel_Dwarven_Spider.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Ebon_Steel_Dwarven_Spider.jpg) A vamidium made of Ebon Steel [Ebony-steel](Lore_Ebony-steel.md), also known as **Ebon Steel**, and **Ebonsteel** is an alloy. It is unknown if this alloy actually contained [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) and [Steel](Lore_Steel.md) or has been simply named as such, but it was a dark, durable substance.

It was used by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), along with other materials, such as [adamant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adamant), [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md), [vitrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vitrine), and [dwarven metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md), for constructing certain [vamidiums](Lore_Vamidium.md).

Ebony-steel was also used by the knights all across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and by the soldiers of the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). Maces made of the ebony-steel had a tripartite head of three heavy [eagle's](Lore_Eagle.md) wings: one for each of the races of the alliance: [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md).

## Gloamium
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-lead-Gloamium_Rivets.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-lead-Gloamium_Rivets.png) Gloamium rivets [Gloamium](Lore_Gloamium.md) is a metal from [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)'s realm of [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) noted for possessing odd properties. Although it looks like [silver](Lore_Silver.md) and melts like [iron](Lore_Iron.md), gloamium does not act like either of those metals. Instead, it remains cold to the touch, and will not warm even when placed next to flame. In [2E 367](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_367), a watchman in [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) accidentally embedded a fleck of gloamium in his eye; according to reports, he would lose his sight during the day, but could see perfectly well at night. The [Gray Cowl of Nocturnal](Lore_Gray_Cowl_of_Nocturnal.md) was made using rivets forged from gloamium.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-54)</sup>

## Iron
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/SR-activity-Mining_%28Iron%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Iron%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Iron).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Iron).jpg) An exposed iron deposit [Iron](Lore_Iron.md) is the most commonly used metal in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). It is dark grey in color, and has a wide range of uses in blacksmithing. Its abundance makes the metal ideal for creating mundane items like nails or containers. Weapons and armor made from iron are easy to produce, but vastly inferior to equipment made from most other metals. Iron can be converted to [steel](Lore_Steel.md), either through refinement of high-quality ore or through the creation of an alloy when combined with [corundum](Lore_Corundum.md). It can also be used in alchemy to produce a slowfall effect or magically transmuted into [silver](Lore_Silver.md).

## Lodestone
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-icon-ingredient-Lodestone.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-icon-ingredient-Lodestone.png) Lodestone [Lodestones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lodestone) are metals with strong magnetic energy. They can attract dust, debris, and other metals like [iron](Lore_Iron.md). They are commonly used for navigation, especially as compasses for [sea travel](Lore_Sea_Transportation.md). Lodestones can also manipulate electricity and its associated magic. The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), who often travel the sea and use storm magic, use lodestones for several functions. But lodestones can also have magical properties. [Enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) lodestones can focus [magicka](Lore_Magicka.md) over a distance and usually come in pairs to form sympathetic links between them. ## Tin
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-icon-ingredient-Tin.png) Tin is a soft, silvery-colored metal. It is used to make tin cans, toys, and other utensils.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-109)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-110)</sup> Tin is also used in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and possesses [modest magical effects](Lore_Alchemy_T.md#Tin).<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-111)</sup> The [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md) of [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321) established the [Tinkers Guild](Lore_Tinkers_Guild.md) as a pan-Tamrielic Imperial guild for itinerant [tinsmiths](Lore_Smithing.md).<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-Hot FG-112)</sup> The Barkbite Clan of [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orcs.md) possessed a tin mine at [Reman's Bluff](Lore_Reman%27s_Bluff.md), although their smiths found it to be a fussy metal to work with.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-113)</sup>

## Titanium
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-trait_material-Titanium.png) Titanium was used in jewelry crafting during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). During this time period, titanium deposits were commonly found near cliffs and mountains across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Jewelry imbued with titanium granted the wearer more protection.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-ESOTitanium-114)</sup>

## Tyranite Calx
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-furnishing-Dormant_Tyranite_Calx.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Dormant_Tyranite_Calx.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Dormant_Tyranite_Calx.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Dormant_Tyranite_Calx.jpg) Dormant Tyranite Calx [Tyranite Calx](Lore_Tyranite_Calx.md) is an interstitial alloy used in the production of [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md), infused with [soul gem fragments](Lore_Soul_Gems.md) and other trace components. Sondivel experimented with the metal using a [soul magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md) ritual to shape it into malleable bands that could weaken the will of the wearer.

## Voidstone
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/ON-node-Voidstone_Ore.jpg/200px-ON-node-Voidstone_Ore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Voidstone_Ore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-node-Voidstone_Ore.jpg) An exposed Voidstone deposit Voidstone was used by blacksmiths to forge of arms and armor during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Voidstone was smelted from dark purple ore deposits that could be found almost anywhere during this time period.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-ESOVoidstone-115)</sup> It was used by Daedric craftsmen to craft rivets which were components of Spaulder of Ruin.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-ONSOR-116)</sup> Refined voidstone is referred to as the voidsteel.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-VW-117)</sup><sup>[[118]](#cite_note-VA-118)</sup> Establishment of [pan-Tamrielic guilds](Lore_Guild_Act.md) resulted in greater diffusion of knowledge. Smiths learned of interesting materials, such as voidsteel, used only in distant lands, and this drove demand and trade for those materials.<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-MAMA-119)</sup>

## Wispmetal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/ON-furnishing-Music_Box%2C_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Music_Box%2C_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Music_Box,_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Music_Box,_Glyphic_Secrets.jpg) A music box made from wispmetal components [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fragment-Dark_Metal_Cubes.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fragment-Dark_Metal_Cubes.png) Wispmetal cubes Wispmetal is a rare metal from [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). It is sometimes quenched in [tomeshell](Lore_Tomeshell.md) gore when being [forged](Lore_Smithing.md).<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-120)</sup>

## Zinc
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-trait_material-Zinc.png) Zinc was used in jewelry crafting during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Jewelry imbued with zinc granted the wearer increased stamina.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-ESOZinc-121)</sup>

## Zircon
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-plating-Zircon.png) Zircon was a metal used in jewelry crafting during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Raw zircon takes the form of grains, that when refined would produce platings that could be used to fortify pieces of jewelry.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-ESOZircon-122)</sup>
