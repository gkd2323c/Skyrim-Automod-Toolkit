[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/LO-map-High_Rock_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-High_Rock_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](File_LO-map-High_Rock_(Anthology).jpg.md) [](File_LO-map-High_Rock_(Anthology).jpg.md) Map of High Rock The Western Reach is the western part of the historic territory of the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md), which was split from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s hold of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) by Emperor [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman.md) in the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) along the line of the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGTNBat M-1)</sup> It is traditionally considered to be part of the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-POT-3)</sup> but the region can also include the areas of northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) which also border Skyrim.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Vir Web-4)</sup> It is divided from the rest of High Rock by the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-2)</sup>

Geographically, the Western Reach comprises the northeastern part of [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai) and the eastern half of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) in High Rock,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHo E-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONHH-6)</sup> and the northwestern part of [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) in Hammerfell.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONCrag-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SKDS-8)</sup>

## Flora and Fauna
It is home to several ecosystems due to this intersection of provinces, and the region enjoys a variety of unique fauna. Examples include creatures such as [Floaters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Floater) and [Rays](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ray), which are airborne creatures that resemble aquatic animals. Some other examples include the horned bipedal reptilian creatures, the [Wickeder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wickeder), and its relatives the [Bounder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bounder) and [Clawrunner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clawrunner). Lastly is the maggot-shaped bipedal vermin known as the [Wormmouth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wormmouth), which can vary greatly in size.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SKC-9)</sup>

## History
The Direnni Hegemony rose in power on the mainland through political maneuvering and devious machinations in [1E 355](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_355), soon taking control of the Western Reach.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDC-10)</sup> In [1E 358](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_358), the joint army of Cyrodiilic Emperor [Ami-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ami-El) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) attacked the Direnni in the Western Reach of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TILGrandstaff-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Xan Timeline-12)</sup> The Western Reach was one of the Skyrim Holds during the [First Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), although it was retaken by the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) during the Dissolution of Skyrim's Empire around [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), at great cost of [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) life. Eventually, the Aldmeri overseers were eliminated, but traces of their blood forever remained in the veins of the native [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGTNBat M-1)</sup> During their rule, the Aldmer made the Western Reach into an impregnable fortification to prevent attacks from Skyrim.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-2)</sup> The Direnni Hegemony created a series of watch towers along the borders of the Reach and Skyrim.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

After the conclusion of the [First Akaviri Invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) in [1E 2704](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2704), Emperor [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman_Cyrodiil.md) turned his attention towards the "madmen of the Reach". He divided the Reachmen territory between the provinces of Skyrim and High Rock in an attempt to limit their possibility of uniting against him. The natives resisted fiercely, and there was never a decade when the troops of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) or [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) wouldn't be sent in an attempt to pacify them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGTNBat M-1)</sup>

In [Mournoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mournoth), the northernmost region of Bangkorai, the Reachmen often warred with Bretons of the kingdom of Evermore. It was the first region to be taken by [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) the Black Drake during his invasion in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), although it was quickly freed when Durcorach was slain by [Emeric of Cumberland](Lore_Emeric.md) the following year.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Toa M-14)</sup> A few decades later, in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the region was attacked again by the Dark Witnesses, a Reachmen clan led by the [Hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) Uela, who intended to reclaim the territory that used to belong to the Reachmen. The Dark Witnesses turned their eyes towards Evermore, and briefly worked with the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) to nullify the threat of an [Imperial](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) coup from within the city. However, the clan were subsequently driven out of Mournoth following the death of Uela.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONHo E-5)</sup>

Another clan, [Winterborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Winterborn), dominated parts of Wrothgar during the time of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md). After [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kurog) was granted the region of Wrothgar by the Daggerfall Covenant to build his new kingdom of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), the [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) clans drove them back to the Reach. The Winterborn tried to reclaim their territory in 2E 582, but were ultimately defeated at a last stand within [Frostbreak Fortress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostbreak_Fortress), where Kurog slew their last remaining leader, [Urfon Ice-Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Urfon_Ice-Heart).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONHH-6)</sup>

During the early days of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the region was still plagued by banditry and lawlessness, and travelers were advised to stay away from it. It was ruled by the Provisional Governor [Titus Alorius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Titus_Alorius).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-2)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), in [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), the region around [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md) was embroiled in the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) between High Rock, Hammerfell and Skyrim. The conflict that took place here was used by [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) and [Pergan Asuul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pergan_Asuul) as fuel to manifest a *Shadow of Conflict*, or [Umbra' Keth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umbra%27_Keth), which had the potential to engulf all of Hammerfell in shadows. Guided by the [Shadow Mages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Magic) [Azra Nightwielder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azra_Nightwielder) and [Skelos Undriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skelos_Undriel), the Umbra' Keth was defeated by the [Soul of Conflict](Lore_Soul_of_Conflict.md), who used the legendary [Star Teeth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Star_Teeth) to destroy its shadowy form.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SKMQ-15)</sup> After peace was established, a commission was created to repair the damage done by the war.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ADIF7-16)</sup>
