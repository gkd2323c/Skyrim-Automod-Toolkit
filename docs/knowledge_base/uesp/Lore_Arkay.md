# Arkay

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-heraldry-Deities_Arkay.png) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/OB-Window-Arkay.jpg/150px-OB-Window-Arkay.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Window-Arkay.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Window-Arkay.jpg) Arkay *Arkay says: Honor the earth, its creatures, and the spirits, living and dead. Guard and tend the bounties of the mortal world, and do not profane the spirits of the dead.*
— *[The Ten Commands of the Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ten_Commands__Nine_Divines)* Arkay (or **Ark'ay**), known as the **Lord of the Wheel of Life**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOA-1)</sup> **God of the Cycle of Birth and Death**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFGuide-2)</sup> **Light of Lights**, the **Bringer of Light**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>, and **Lord of the Seasons**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONPellingare-4)</sup> is a member of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md), and also a popular god in other cultures. Arkay is often more important in those cultures where his father, [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), is either less related to time or where his time aspects are difficult to comprehend by the layman.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup> He is the god of burials and funeral rites, and is generally associated with cyclical occasions, such as the seasons and life/death.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OLBTM-6)</sup> His priests are staunch opponents of necromancy and all forms of the undead, and are empowered to bestow Arkay's blessings which prevent the forceful misuse of a mortal soul.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOA-1)</sup> Thus, any body properly buried by a priest observing the proper rituals is protected by Arkay's Law from being raised as undead.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CP1-7)</sup> Therefore, necromancers view Arkay as their ultimate enemy, and make covert efforts to undermine his worship throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ATE-8)</sup> Because of this association with, and protection of, mortality, he is sometimes called the **Mortals' God**. To the [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) of the [Cult of Lamae Bal](Lore_Cult_of_Lamae_Bal.md) he is known as **the Forsaker**.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ROTS-9)</sup>

One legend of Arkay's origins is written in the book *[Ark'ay The God](Lore_Ark%27ay_The_God.md)*. It states that Arkay was once a mortal shopkeeper with a passion for knowledge. He found a book written in a strange language and spent years upon years attempting to decipher it, slowly ignoring everything and everyone else around him. Eventually Arkay realized the book explained life and death itself, but by this time was at death's door with an incurable plague. Praying to [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) as a last resort, Arkay asked for more time to interpret the book. Mara gave him a choice: die now or become a god for eternity, charged with keeping the balance of death and life in the universe.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOF-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ATE-8)</sup> Another theory of Arkay's origins is contained in *[The Monomyth](Lore_The_Monomyth.md)*, which suggests that Arkay was one of the very first spirits to "crystallize" after the start of time.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

## Worship
The [Order of Arkay](Lore_Order_of_Arkay.md) has temples in [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Ilessan Hills](Lore_Ilessan_Hills.md), [Shalgora](Lore_Shalgora.md), [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md), and [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md). Followers of Arkay are known as Arkayns,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Nidal-12)</sup> and they're known for bestowing [Consecrations of Arkay](Lore_Consecrations_of_Arkay.md) upon the faithful.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOA-1)</sup>

### Bosmer
Arkay is the god of burials, funeral rites and is associated with the seasons. The Bosmer invoke him when solving offenses against the Green Pact.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-GTEV-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-VOFTWE-14)</sup> They revere him alongside another god of similar portfolio, [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AAA-15)</sup>

### Breton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/a/a3/DF-sign-Order_of_Arkay.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-sign-Order_of_Arkay.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-sign-Order_of_Arkay.png) A symbol for the Order of Arkay in High Rock [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-Statue-Breton_Statue%2C_Arkay.jpg/200px-ON-Statue-Breton_Statue%2C_Arkay.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Statue-Breton_Statue,_Arkay.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-Statue-Breton_Statue,_Arkay.jpg) Breton statue of Arkay Arkay has a significant presence in the city of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), where he is known as the **Light**.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONIIL-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONEselde-17)</sup> He is the patron deity of the [Ilessan Hills](Lore_Ilessan_Hills.md) and [Shalgora](Lore_Shalgora.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> Arkay is also the patron of [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md), who are devout followers of the Light.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONIIL-16)</sup> The scions of House Tamrith study the Way of the Light and obey Arkay's teachings.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Their holy symbols bear the crest of House Tamrith and the symbol of the Light of Arkay.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

Arkay's holy relics can be used to combat the undead.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOAlvaren-22)</sup> Small stone talismans resembling eyes shut in eternal rest, known as closed eyes of Arkay are often given to mourning families.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Contraband_C-23)</sup>

Because of his duty to maintain balance, Bretons believe Arkay may cause plague, famines and other natural disasters and so fear him as well as love him.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOPriest CR-24)</sup>

### Imperial
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/BL-misc-Arkay.png/200px-BL-misc-Arkay.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Arkay.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Arkay.png) Imperial depiction of Arkay [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Arkay.jpg/200px-OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Arkay.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Arkay.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Great_Chapel_of_Arkay.jpg) The Great Chapel of Arkay in Cyrodiil The [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) is based out of Cyrodiil, and its ministries across the Empire are handled by the **Council of the Nine**, consisting of the Primates of Cyrodiil's various Great Chapels.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Chana-25)</sup> In Cyrodiil, the Church is divided into eight parishes, each dedicated to a divine. [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md)'s chapel is dedicated to Arkay.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

### Nord
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_%28Whiterun%29_04.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_%28Whiterun%29_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_(Whiterun)_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Hall_of_the_Dead_(Whiterun)_04.jpg) Nordic shrine to Arkay [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/ff/SR-concept-Amulet_of_Arkay.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Amulet_of_Arkay.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Amulet_of_Arkay.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Amulet_of_Arkay.jpg) Nordic amulet of Arkay The original Nordic aspect of Arkay was [Orkey](Lore_Orkey.md), a testing god associated with the Snake that governed death and enjoyed quarrels of all kind.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TSo G-27)</sup> Orkey seems to have some association with Malacath and Orcs, a notable legend saying Orkey cursed the Nords (who previously were as long-lived as Elves) down to lifespan of six years before Shor lifted the curse and threw most of it onto the Orcs.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Vo FNords-28)</sup>

The shift from Orkey to Arkay happened with the Alessian Reforms that fused aspects of the Elven and Nordic Pantheons being embraced in much of Skyrim.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

Shrines to Arkay can be found in Halls of the Dead across Skyrim, it is his shrine Nords pray to when they visit the Hall. That his divinity will watch over their ancestors on the way to the next life.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

Nordic priests of Arkay perform rites for the dead and sometimes console the bereaved, they also keep a constant vigil against those who practice the vile arts of necromancy.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

### Reachfolk
After imperialization and the loss of independence, some [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) embraced the worship of the Eight Divines, including Arkay, believing that certain aspects of their traditional practices were best left in the past.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Belchimac-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Cedran-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Degaine-33)</sup>

### Redguard
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/RG-place-Temple_of_Arkay_02.jpg/200px-RG-place-Temple_of_Arkay_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-place-Temple_of_Arkay_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-place-Temple_of_Arkay_02.jpg) The Temple of Arkay on Stros M'Kai In Hammerfell, where worship of Arkay is strongest, the dead are almost always subject to Arkay's Law. There are exceptions after large battles or in remote areas where death occurs far from priests.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CP1-7)</sup> Redguard followers of Arkay see him as the shepherd of the soul, who watches through lives and deaths, and guides one between them in the heavenly interim.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Nidal-12)</sup>

Some sources connect Arkay with [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) through the early Nedic precursor To-Arcka. Indeed, symbolism strikingly similar to Arkay's wreath appears on many ancient Yokudan tombs.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AAA-15)</sup> Tu'whacca's cult is sometimes associated with Arkay in the more cosmopolitan regions of Hammerfell, and he is often worshiped in that name by Forebears.

One source has conflated Arkay with the Yokudan goddess [Morwha](Lore_Morwha.md) in the past although it's unknown if this was speaking to a true connection or was simply a scholarly mistake.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-34)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Lady Garick's Sacred Shield
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3b/ON-item-Lady_Garick%27s_Sacred_Shield.png/200px-ON-item-Lady_Garick%27s_Sacred_Shield.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Lady_Garick%27s_Sacred_Shield.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Lady_Garick%27s_Sacred_Shield.png) Lady Garick's Sacred Shield [Lady Joslin Garick](Lore_Joslin_Garick.md) was a premier [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) captain for the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md). Aside from her exploits, she was known for what she referred to as her ["Sacred Shield"](Lore_Lady_Garick%27s_Sacred_Shield.md). She claimed her eponymous ward was blessed by a saint of Arkay, though long after her passing, legends would paint it as Arkay himself that endowed his power into it. The shield was said to preserve the wielder's life, in that no injury would befall them. The legends seemed to have some merit, for her crew would always return safely. However, the one instance she left her shield behind for safe keeping was her last voyage, as she and her crew met their demise during the All Flags Navy's assault on [Thras](Lore_Thras.md).

### [Sword of the Crusader](Lore_Sword_of_the_Crusader.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/OB-item-Sword_of_the_Crusader.jpg/200px-OB-item-Sword_of_the_Crusader.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Sword_of_the_Crusader.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Sword_of_the_Crusader.jpg) The Sword of the Crusader The Sword of the Crusader was created by Arkay. Those it strikes are burnt by holy flames, and their magicka reserves depleted. It was discovered by Sir Berich Vlindrel of the original Knights, who brought it to the War of the Red Diamond. When he died, it was buried with him in his family tomb in [Underpall Cave](Lore_Underpall_Cave.md); in undeath, Lord Vlindrel dragged the sword down into evil, cursing it so that a righteous wielder would have stunted magicka. His wraith, infamous for haunting the [Orange Road](Lore_Orange_Road.md), wielded the weapon. It was recovered by the pilgrim who, after freeing Lord Vlindrel's soul, reconsecrated it on the altar in the Great Chapel of Arkay in [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup>

## Saints of Arkay

#### Ellenica
**Saint Ellenica** was a Breton priestess of Arkay.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOAlvaren-22)</sup> Her prayer book is a holy relic, and contains passages that can be read to put the dead to rest.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36)</sup> She was buried in Flyleaf Catacombs with her prayer book, which was retrieved in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-37)</sup>

#### Errandil
**Saint Errandil** was a High Elf saint affiliated with Arkay. He was the Great Chapel of Arkay's chief crusader against the practice of necromancy in the Mages Guild and the [Imperial Battle College](Lore_Imperial_Battle_College.md),<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-38)</sup> he was killed by [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md)'s [Aurorans](Lore_Auroran.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-39)</sup>

#### Marben
**Saint Marben** was a Breton saint affiliated with Arkay.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ESOAlvaren-22)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-40)</sup> Saint Marben's Fingerbone is a holy relic of Arkay, and was buried at [Sanguine Barrows](Lore_Sanguine_Barrows.md) until it was retrieved in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup>

#### [Octavien](Lore_Octavien.md)
**Saint** [Octavien](Lore_Octavien.md) was a priest of Arkay. Little is known about his life, but more is known about his death and legacy. He was killed by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worshippers with a scythe. His death is remembered by the Bretons, and the scythe became a holy relic of Arkay. The damaged scythe was preserved in the Lorkrata Hills, and a piece of it was retrieved by the [knights of Arkay](Lore_Breton_Knightly_Orders.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) to combat the [undead](Lore_Undead.md) armies that assaulted [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md).

Saint Octavien was depicted on bronze talismans.

## Planet
The planet [Arkay](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md), sometimes called **RKHET**, is one of the Dominion Planets in the skies of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), and according to the Warrior-Poet, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), it is one of the eight worlds known to the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). No other planet orbits Arkay. Arkay marks the eye of the [Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) [constellation](Lore_Constellations.md). As with all astral bodies seen in the skies above [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and their corresponding deities, the planet Arkay is believed to be the plane of the god Arkay as well as the god himself, as seen from the mortal plane. It is said to actually be a different [plane of existence](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) in its own right, infinite in size and mass, with its appearance as a sphere being only a visual phenomenon caused by mortal mental stress.

## Notes
- *[The Illusion of Death](Lore_The_Illusion_of_Death.md)* suggests that the cycle of life and death is also called the "Arkayn Cycle".
- Arkay's lash, a cactus with a bright pink flower that has great medicinal properties, is named after Arkay.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-43)</sup>
- Arkay was named after one of the original *Daggerfall* beta testers, R. K. Deutsch, who died in 1998.
- The word for "relating to Arkay" is "Arkayn".<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-AAA-15)</sup>
- [Florentius Baenius](Skyrim_Florentius_Baenius.md) was a 4th Era Priest of Arkay associated with the Dawnguard that claimed to directly hear the voice of Arkay. Though his colleagues were deeply skeptical of this, his supposed connection to Arkay still seemed to give him access to knowledge he should not otherwise possess.
- Various poisons in *Skyrim* have Arkay's symbol on their label.
