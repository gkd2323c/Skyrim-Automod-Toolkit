# Shezarr

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/OB-place-Shezarr_Stone.jpg/400px-OB-place-Shezarr_Stone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Shezarr_Stone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Shezarr_Stone.jpg) Shezarr Stone, located in southern Nibenay Shezarr (or **Shezzar**),<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Mono2-1)</sup> also known as the **God of Man**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> is a [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) version of [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) whose cultural importance suffered when [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) came to the fore of Nibenay religion. Shezarr was the spirit behind all human undertaking, especially against [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) aggression. He is sometimes associated with the founding of the first Cyrodilic battlemages. In the present age of racial tolerance, Shezarr is all but forgotten.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-VOF-2)</sup> He is believed to be a thinly-disguised version of [Shor](Lore_Shor.md), and even in the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) West of Cyrodiil, they recognize Shezarr by the name of Shor. One of the thousands of cults in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) is dedicated to worship of him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SATD-3)</sup>

"Shezarr's Song" is a creation myth of Cyrodiil. It says that Shezarr brought the idea to the other gods of becoming mothers and fathers, being responsible, and making great sacrifices, with no guarantee of success. It involved cutting off parts of themselves to create a world, sacrificing power and control. It was a new and strange idea, but Shezarr spoke beautifully to them, and moved them beyond mystery and tears. Thus the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) gave free birth to the world. It took a great toll: they were no longer young, and strong, and powerful, as they had been from the beginning. The [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) mocked Shezarr and the other Aedra, and decided to make [their own worlds](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md) within themselves where they wouldn't have to sacrifice control, though they came to envy the Aedra's creation, and often sought to steal from Shezarr and the others. Some Aedra were disappointed, bitter, and angry with Shezarr, and with all creation, for they felt Shezarr had lied and tricked them. These Aldmeri gods, led by [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), decided to seek vengeance on Shezarr for reducing them to their "disgusting" selves. But the gods of men and beast folk, led by Akatosh, were pleased with creation despite their great sacrifices.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup>

Also called the "**Missing Sibling**" of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), Shezarr appeared in the earliest [Cyro-Nordic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyro-Nordic) stories of the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) fighting against the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) on mankind's behalf. Then, for some unknown reason, he vanished from the stage (presumably to help other humans elsewhere), and, without his leadership, the Ayleids conquered the humans and enslaved them.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SATD-3)</sup> Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) believed that "freedom" was just another name for Shezarr,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSOP2-5)</sup> while the [Marukhati Selective](Lore_Marukhati_Selective.md), monotheistic in some other respects, considered Shezarr "the missing sibling" of Akatosh, "Singularly Misplaced and therefore Doubly Venerated".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TEM-6)</sup> It was speculated that Alessia's champion [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) was the [Shezarrine](Lore_Shezarrine.md), clearly bearing some relation to the god, though *[The Song of Pelinal](Lore_The_Song_of_Pelinal.md)* asserts that one person who referred to him as such "was smothered by moths".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TSOP5-7)</sup> To appease Alessia's allies, his reputation as a bloodthirsty warrior was watered-down.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SATD-3)</sup> Alessia agreed to adopt [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) Shor into the Imperial pantheon as Shezarr, the Missing God. Theologians considered this appropriate, as it both recognized the importance of Shezarr and emphasized his absence.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AYQ-8)</sup>

Shezarr was a prominent figure in ancient [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) oral traditions, where he is depicted as a teacher, advisor, and unifier of tribes that might otherwise have been in conflict. Unlike the warrior-ruler Shor, Shezarr inspired others to fight for themselves. In [Duraki](Lore_Duraki.md) legend, Shezarr is known as "the one who stole stoneworking from the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and taught [Zinfara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zinfara) to call [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) from the mountain-roots." The [Perena](Lore_Perena.md) tell of the [Cult of Stars](Lore_Cult_of_the_Stars.md) learning [soul magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md) from a "white-bearded stranger." The Nedes of Cyrod speak of "Shezarr of the Snowy Beard," who is said to have revealed the secrets of Ayleid battle-magic to them, teaching them how to turn their enemies' arts against them. A stone tablet in the ruins of [Sedor](Lore_Sedor.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFOS-9)</sup> associated with the people known as the [Thousand-Strong of Sedor](Lore_Thousand-Strong_of_Sedor.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SOP-10)</sup> depicts a bearded figure referred to as "the Shezzarine, Shor-Who-Lives, Teacher of Men." Shezarr inspired many different Nedic tribes to resist Ayleid oppression. Although later Nedic sagas do not mention him, it is speculated that whatever role Shezarr played ended in the middle of the Merethic Era. Nevertheless, the hope he instilled sustained ancient Men through centuries of enslavement by the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFOS-9)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-crown_store-Shezarr%27s_Chicken.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Shezarr%27s_Chicken.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Shezarr%27s_Chicken.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Shezarr%27s_Chicken.jpg) Shezarr's Chicken - A breed of [chickens](Lore_Chicken.md) named after Shezzar were found in Cyrodiil's Heartlands. Beyond a mere name, some argued that these fowl share a profound connection with Shezarr. This assertion is often rooted in their reputation as escape artists, frequently finding themselves "misplaced" with remarkable regularity.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SC-11)</sup>
- Shezarr is connected to [Hero](Lore_Hero.md) prophecies, his shade said to shroud the Hero's Doom.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-OBStone-12)</sup>
