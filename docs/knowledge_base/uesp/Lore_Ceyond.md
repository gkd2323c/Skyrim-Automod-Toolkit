## History
Following the fall of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md), Ceyond was buried beneath the earth. By the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it could only be accessed via cave entrances located along the road northwest of Ostumir.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESO-5)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the forgotten [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md) was freed from her imprisonment within [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) by [Torvesard](Lore_Torvesard.md), awakening her other servants in [Mirrormoor](Lore_Mirrormoor.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Torvesard Quote-7)</sup> Among these awakened servants was an ancient being known as the Primordial [Shattered Titan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shattered_Titan), one of first things Ithelia ever created.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Scruut-8)</sup> Ithelia's forces invaded the West Weald,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> while the Prince herself reclaimed her divine power from the ruins of [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Somehow, the Primordial Shattered Titan appeared in the depths of Ceyond around this time, alongside other creatures and crystalline structures originating from Mirrormoor.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Scruut-8)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESO-5)</sup>

As Ithelia's power grew, her influence on the threads of fate began to create tears in the fabric of reality. A [Watchling](Lore_Watchling.md) named Scruut discovered the whereabouts of the Primordial Titan while scouting for these reality tears, and helped [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) devise a plan to create a relic capable of neutralizing the threat posed by Ithelia. The Vestige entered Ceyond and defeated the Titan in order to claim a fragment of it. Using this fragment's strong connection to its creator, the Vestige created the [Mirror of Truth](Lore_Mirror_of_Truth.md), a relic that was ultimately instrumental in defeating Ithelia.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Scruut-8)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the underground ruins were no longer accessible.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>
