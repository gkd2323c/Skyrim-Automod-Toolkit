Ald Velothi ([Dark Elvish](Lore_Dark_Elvish.md) for *"Old Velothi"*) was a small harbor settlement on the north coast of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). A dock has existed there for many years and is historically owned by [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md). It is likely named after [Veloth the Pilgrim](Lore_Veloth.md), the village's patron [saint](Lore_Temple_Saints.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWTheldyn-1)</sup> In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it was the site of the **Ald Velothi Harbor House**, a medium-sized private estate with a small dock, but in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), it was a fishing village that contained an outpost.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONHouse-2)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-place-Ald_Velothi_Harbor_House.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ald_Velothi_Harbor_House.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald_Velothi_Harbor_House.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ald_Velothi_Harbor_House.jpg) The Ald Velothi Harbor House When Ald Velothi was built is unknown, but these docks have sat on the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md) coast since as early as the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), between [Ashalmawia](Lore_Ashalmawia.md) to the east and Arkngthunch-Sturdumz to the southwest.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONVelothi-3)</sup> In the mid-Second Era, a medium-sized, walled estate was built above the Ald Velothi docks and sold by the [Architects Guild](Lore_Architects_Guild.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONHouse-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Homestead Int-4)</sup> By the Third Era, the estate was deconstructed. After the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md)'s ban over Vvardenfell was lifted in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), House Redoran moved into [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) and quickly took control of northwest Vvardenfell, splitting it between its lords and developing settlements over it.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ASHOM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWSarethi-6)</sup>

A fishing village was built in Ald Velothi, with a Redoran-style outpost uphill from the harbor. Theldyn Virith was the ranking Redoran official, however, he was given no formal title and served as a de-facto hetman for the villagers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWTheldyn-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWNeminda-7)</sup> The Arvels were a local family of [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) Dunmer. They lived in Ald Velothi for years and were generously on good terms with House Redoran.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWTheldyn-1)</sup> Several figures operated in the nearby ruins and wilderness, threatening Ald Velothi's peace, such as outcast [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) kidnapping pilgrims on the road<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWGen Ald-8)</sup> and a [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) coven of the [Fourth Corner](Lore_Sheogorath.md) in Ashalmawia led by the battlemage, Gordol.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWTheldyn-1)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Theldyn Virith employed a Redoran [member](Lore_Nerevarine.md) to help the town. First, it was to kill a [slaughterfish](Lore_Slaughterfish.md) that plagued the Ald Velothi docks for a few years, Old Blue Fin. Then it was to secure the Ashimanu Eggmine on the [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md) -Ald'ruhn caravan road, and to report on the Redoran's bid for Shishi, a Velothi tower contested with [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md). Their last quest was to kill Gordol in Ashalmawia, ending his coven's threat over Ald Velothi.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWTheldyn-1)</sup> In that same time, the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) in Fort Darius sent a recruit to rescue Madura Seran, a layman of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) from the outcast Ashlanders in the hills. The townsfolk were at ease after she was safely brought back to Ald Velothi.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWGen Ald-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWRescue-9)</sup>

What became of Ald Velothi throughout the rest of the Third Era is unknown but in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) as it is remembered, [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) erupted and destroyed most of Vvardenfell. A relief effort was organized to restore settlements that were hit the hardest but whether Ald Velothi was rebuilt or abandoned is unknown.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRY1-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRY2-11)</sup>
