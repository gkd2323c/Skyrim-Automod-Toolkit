#### Ma-Tylda
[Ma-Tylda](Lore_Ma-Tylda.md) is an esteemed peer of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) in [Bretic](Lore_Breton.md) myth. In fables, she takes the shape of an abnormally large [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-King Edward-1)</sup>

#### Magnus, Magus
[Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), also known as the **God of Magic** (or simply **Magic** itself), the **Sun God**, the **Great Architect**, **He Who Abstained**, and the **Sorceror-King**, is a prominent [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md), one of the Original Spirits. During the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) persuaded Magnus and several other et'Ada to help create the mortal plane, [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). Magnus was said to have been the architect of Mundus, as he created the schematics and diagrams needed to construct it.

#### Magrus, Cat's Eye, Sun God
[Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) version of [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), god of sun and magic, popular with Khajiiti magicians.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Vo FK-2)</sup> He is the child of [Ahnurr](Lore_Ahnurr.md) and [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md). He was born third.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Wo CMA-3)</sup>

#### Mai
[Mai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mai) is an obscure entity, whom was worshipped at "Shrines of Mai" throughout the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DF-4)</sup>

#### Malacath
[Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), the **Daedra Prince of Lies, Deception, and Hypocrisy**, the **God of Curses**, **Daedric Prince of the Bloody Oath**, **Lord of Ash and Bone**, **Lord of Monsters**, **Oathbreaker**, **Creator of Curses**, **He Who Speaks Sideways**, the **Furious One**, the **Keeper of the Bloody Curse**, the **Daedric Prince of Vengeance** the **Defender of the Betrayed**, also known as **Mauloch** (or **Malauch**) to the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), **Orkey** in [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) tradition, **Orkha** to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), **Malooc** to the [Giant Goblins](Lore_Giant_Goblin.md) and [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), **Malak** to the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), and **Muluk** the **Blue God** of the [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) and [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md), is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) whose sphere is "the patronage of the spurned and ostracized, the keeper of the Sworn Oath, and the Bloody Curse", as well as conflict, battle, broken promises, and anguish. Malacath has been described as a "weak but vengeful" [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) by some, and he fittingly (given his sphere) is allegedly not recognized as a Daedra Lord by his peers;. Despite this, however, [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) considers him among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as he was one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md). In addition, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) includes Malacath among his "siblings", the Daedric Princes, and has described Malacath's [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md), [Volendrung](Lore_Volendrung.md), as a "true Daedric artifact".

The Prince rules over a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) known as the [Ashpit](Lore_Ashpit.md), and he counts the oversized but dull-witted [Ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md) as his servants.

[Goblin-Ken](Lore_Goblin-ken.md) are often thought to have origins tied to Malacath, and he is worshipped by them in some of his aspects. Malacath himself has been known to take patronage over the Goblin-ken races, and is known to protect and guide them.

Malacath is considered the first of the Four Corners of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), though other sources say he is an ally of [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), and an enemy of [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md). His [summoning day](Lore_Malacath%27s_Summoning_Day.md) is the 8<sup>th</sup> of Frost Fall. Troll fat is a consistent element in rituals intended to summon Malacath.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Malacath.md).

By his own account Malacath loves very few things, vengeance being one of them. The Prince has described destructive vengeance as a beautiful act, the beauty of which the perpetrator shouldn't lessen by attempting to claim its consequences were unintended. According to Malacath himself, he has no love for any of the other Daedric Princes. Despite some of his titles, Malacath is said by some sources to have historically exhibited a straightforward mentality, of which indirect and subversive action is unbecoming.

#### Mara, Goddess of Love
[Mara](Lore_Mara.md), also known as **Mother Mara**, **Mother Mild**, and the **Divine Mother**, is the **Goddess of Love, Fertility, Agriculture, and Compassion**. She is also acclaimed as the **Mother-Goddess**, and is one of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).

In the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), Mara was originally worshipped simply as a fertility goddess, but the sphere of the Divine later extended to agriculture and love. Nir, the female principle of the cosmos credited as the mother of creation, is occasionally associated with Mara as a result of her role as the goddess of fertility and agriculture. As a result, priests of Mara credit her with giving birth to creation. Mara is considered a near universal deity as the Divine is featured in the pantheons of all the races of the continent of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) save the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).

#### Masser, Big Moon God
[Masser](Lore_Masser.md), also known as **Jode**, **Mara's Tear** and **Zennji** in [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md), is the greater of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md)'s two [moons](Lore_Moons.md) and is acknowledged as one of the attendant spirits of the mortal plane. As such, it is both temporal and subject to the bounds of mortality. As with all astral bodies seen from Nirn, the [moons](Lore_Moons.md) and [dominion planets](Lore_Planets.md), Masser is said to actually be a separate [plane](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) in its own right, infinite in size and mass, with its appearance as a sphere being only a visual phenomenon caused by mortal mental stress. Masser has long since perished and its death has led to mortals perceiving its previously pure white and featureless sphere as having its current texture and reddish hue, the moon's "skin" withering away. According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).

#### Mannimarco, King of Worms
[Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) is the **God of Worms**, the founder and object of worship of the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md). Before his apotheosis he was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) mage ([Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), by his own account) who had studied with the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). He eventually found his own path after he was exiled for practicing [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md), a discipline the Order deemed dangerous and heretical. He became the most powerful necromancer that [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) has ever seen, becoming widely known as the **King of Worms** and head and archnecromancer of his often renamed Worm Cult. While the exact timing of his transformation into a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) is unknown, he is remembered in later records as the "world’s first of the undying liches," a designation of uncertain meaning.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Lich)</sup> Through his political brilliance, his revival of necromancy from obscurity, and his ascension into godhood, he has left a nearly unparalleled impact on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s history.

Mannimarco's early years are unclear. Should his claim to being an Aldmer be true, he may have lived since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). He was expelled from Artaeum for practicing necromancy around the year [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230), after which he gathered followers who formed the Order of the Black Worm. His rivalry with [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) defined much of his known early history, especially as Mannimarco spread soul trapping and necromantic teachings throughout Tamriel. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) as well as [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) and his faithful were those he would come to consider his greatest enemies in life and undeath.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_KW)</sup> A master manipulator, Mannimarco engaged in politics, gaining and maintaining influence by maneuvering through the courts of rising and falling empires. His most significant political achievement was wielding the true power behind the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) from the shadows during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).

During his attempt to attain godhood, Mannimarco suffered repeated failures, including the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), where he served [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) but was punished by him for attempting to usurp the Prince’s station. These setbacks did not deter him, and his persistence finally bore fruit with his ascension during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). Despite his ascension, he remained directly physically active in Tamriel in some capacity until at least 3E 433, the last known death of his physical body. He ultimately accomplished his main goals: he slew Vanus Galerion, possibly even making a trophy out of his corpse, and Mannimarco's divine body, the **[Necromancer's Moon](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md)** (or **the Revenant**), thwarts Arkay by eclipsing his [eponymous planet](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md) every eight days, enabling the creation of [black soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) for his followers to utilize for ensnaring the souls of the sapient.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Mannimarco.md).

#### Mehrunes Dagon
[Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) (formally, **the Exalted and Most Puissant Lord, Gerent of Dagon, Mehrunes**), also called **the great Daedra Lord**, **the Black Daedra Lord**, **Lord of Change**, **Prince of Ambition**, **Prince of Disaster and Destruction**, **Master of Razors**, **Sovereign of Destruction**, the **Flame Tyrant**, the **Father of Cataclysm**, the **Golden Flame**, the **Flame-Father**, and the **Mer-Pride's End**, is the **[Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Darkness and Destruction**, whose sphere encompasses destruction, change, revolution, energy, and ambition. The **Lord of Fire and Flood** is associated with natural dangers like fires or earthquakes. Flash floods, thunderstorms, and other natural disasters have been linked to communions between him and his cultists. The Prince is also said to be the author of the [*Mysterium Xarxes*](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md). Mehrunes Dagon has an insatiable hunger for destruction.

Dagon's destruction is not without purpose, as it is believed to be a means of change and violent rebellion. Though Dagon is best known for his domain of destruction, it is said that that is actually just a portion of his sphere of influence. In actuality, all of his aspects are rooted in Change, one of the [Eleven Forces](Lore_Eleven_Forces.md), thus that force flows very strongly within his realm.

[Xivilai Moath](Lore_Xivilai_Moath.md) and [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md) are Dagon's adoptive children. At one point, the [Dark Seducer](Lore_Dark_Seducer.md) Si'ess was his paramour and his bodyguard. There is a belief that Mehrunes Dagon is allied with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), though [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md) claims that if such an arrangement exists, it would be obscure outside their own circle, and that the relationship between Daedric Princes cannot be captured by "cheap mortal dualisms" of "Friend and Foe". Nonetheless Dagon and Hircine do sometimes pursue mutual interests that make them act in concert. Enemies of the Prince of Destruction include [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md), [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), with whom Dagon shares a bitter rivalry—the two have been known to meddle in each other's schemes when the opportunity arises. It is also rumored that the Prince hates [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md). [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) considers Dagon among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as he was one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md), and claims he "makes art of destruction". Dagon's [protonymic](Lore_Nymic.md) is **Lehkelogah** (listen Link ⏵) and his neonymic is **Djehkeleho-dehbe-effehezepeh**. (listen Link ⏵) The Prince's summoning day coincides with the [Warriors Festival](Lore_Warriors_Festival.md), the 20<sup>th</sup> of Sun's Dusk.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).

#### Mephala, Androgyne, the Spider God
[Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) who is associated with murder, lies, deception, [sex](Lore_Sex.md), and secrets, and is always weaving a web of intrigue and terror. Though it is stated that her sphere is obscured to mortals. Mephala is also known as the **Webspinner**, **Plot-Weaver**, **Androgyne**, the **Spinner**, the **Spider**, the **Teacher of the Secret Arts**, the **Queen of the Eight Shadows of Murder**, the **Silver Spider**, the **Spider Queen**, the **Spider God**, the **Dark One**, the **Whispering Lady**, the **Lady of Whispers**, and the **Queen of Oblivion**. She is also referred to as the princess of secrets and she who weaves the sordid web of human affairs. Long, thin silver hair is a trait associated with Mephala's champions.

Mephala interferes in the affairs of mortals for her own amusement, and does nothing without purpose. Mephala thinks of the affairs of mortals as a weave; pull but one thread and the whole thing unravels. To her, the "web" of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) is an interconnected system of action and consequence, and she spins new threads to influence outcomes. In an essay analyzing the Daedric Prince, [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) posits that Mephala likely doesn't see mortals as mere playthings, unlike some of her fellow Princes.

The Webspinner deals in secrets and hidden lore. She and her worshippers value secrets and mysteries above all else. Those who stumble upon one of Mephala's shrines or a place where an artifact of hers is hidden may find Mephala whispering forbidden secrets in their ears. [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the "Demon of Knowledge", is sometimes referred to as Mephala's sibling. Hermaeus Mora collects knowledge of all sorts, but Mephala cares only about knowledge that is obscure and undisclosed. Not only is she secretive, Mephala also cares very little for the safety and well-being of mortals. The Spider takes great pleasure in strife. A ruined marriage, two peaceful factions erupting into bloody warfare over falsified evidence of treachery ... she finds all of these amusing.

Mephala is purportedly one of the "strongest of the recognizable spirits" that emerged soon after Akatosh formed and time began. At one point prior to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), Mephala's enemies included [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md) and [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), and she considered [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) an ally. The ancient Khajiit considered her an ally to [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md), [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md), and [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md). They also believed that she was Boethra's lover. Her summoning day coincides with the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md), which falls on the 13<sup>th</sup> of Frostfall.

#### Meridia, Prince of Living Energies
[Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), **Lady of Infinite Energies**, the **Bright Lady**, the **Strict but Fair Arbiter of Inner Luminance**, the **Sunfire**, the **Radiant One**, the **Keeper**, the **Lady of Light**, the **Red Star**, the **Harbinger of Dawn**, **Goddess Who Holds**, **Mirror of the Lie** and the **Prince of Life** is the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) "of Life and Light", associated with the energies of living things. It is stated that her sphere is obscured to mortals. The Sunburst is her symbol. She has a great and everlasting hatred for the undead and will greatly reward any who eliminate them from the world. With fervor, she reveals purity within chaos, aiming to abolish false-life so that the Mundus may be reforged with the fire of new light. As such, Meridia has a reputation for benevolence, and is one of the few Daedric Princes who is usually not considered to be wholly evil. Her day of summoning is the [13<sup>th</sup> of Morningstar](Lore_Meridia%27s_Summoning_Day.md).

In spite of this, she is referred to as the **Glister Witch** or the **Lady of Greed** by those who view her less favorably, with a habit of collecting live [human specimens](Lore_Purified.md) to satiate said greed. To her enemies, Meridia is known for her violent loathing of disorder, as well as her obsessiveness, self-righteousness, irrational anger towards entities she deems impure, and hatred of mortal free will and defiance. She believes that free will should be surrendered to passion, "destiny is fulfilled for the vessel".

As with most Princes, Meridia sees mortals as "assets" primarily for use as a means to an end. Meridia can bestow upon mortals immortality, at the cost of their will, becoming those known as the [Purified](Lore_Meridian_Purified.md); slaves to her command, cleansed of disease, death, and free will. People who join the ranks of the Purified are both those that willingly pledge to serve Meridia and the unwilling, such as those that defy her. Meridia also granted immortality to her champion, [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md), the ancient [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) enemy of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md). Her Aurorans and Umaril returned during the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) to seek vengeance on the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).

Her sphere of energy and life often brings her into direct conflict with many of the other Princes of Oblivion. The most notable example of these feuds is her eternal battle with the Daedric Prince of Domination, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). During the waning days of the Ayleid Empire, Meridia would be responsible for the destruction of the Mola Gbal -worshipping [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) city of [Abagarlas](Lore_Abagarlas.md), commanding her knights in the Meridia-worshipping Ayleid city of [Delodiil](Lore_Delodiil.md) to invade after discovering a great and terrible [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) known as the [Mortuum Vivicus](Lore_Mortuum_Vivicus.md) was gifted to Abagarlas by the Lord of Domination himself. This feud would continue well into the Second Era, where Meridia's assistance was instrumental in stopping Molag Bal's [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). Denizens of Molag Bal's realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) refer to Meridia as the **Shining Bitch**, and her worshipers are often locked away and tortured within the [Lightless Oubliette](Lore_Lightless_Oubliette.md). Her other notable enemies include [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), who once invaded Meridia's realm in her quest to rewrite reality, and [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md), the God of War who dislikes most Daedric Princes.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Meridia.md).

#### Mnemo-Li
[Mnemo-Li](Lore_Mnemo-Li.md) (or **Mnemoli the Blue Star**) also known as the **Reclusive Princess**, is a [star](Lore_Stars.md), the most well-known [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) and a daughter of [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md). She is associated with "un-time" events, and it is said that she was visible even in the daytime sky during the [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md). Similarly to other Magna Ge she is now synonymous with the star, which she created. She was one of the Star-Orphans of the Nine Coruscations, who followed the parabolas that led away from Magnus. The remaining eight, her sisters were named [Merid-Nunda](Lore_Merid-Nunda.md), [Sheza-Rana](Lore_Sheza-Rana.md), [Iana-Lor](Lore_Iana-Lor.md), [Xero-Lyg](Lore_Xero-Lyg.md), the eldest [Prime Archon](Lore_Ithelia.md), [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md), [Unala-Se](Lore_Unala-Se.md), and [Valia-Sha](Lore_Valia-Sha.md).

Mnemoli's origins go beyond her relation to Magnus. It is believed that Mnemoli was retroactively constructed by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) who personally gave birth to his cosmic daughter, naming her **Memory**. Memory served as the legacy of Sotha Sil and survived him following his death at the hands of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md).

#### Molag Bal
[Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) (Stone-Fire, or literally the "Fire Stone")<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Stone Fire)</sup> is the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) whose sphere is the domination and enslavement of mortals. His ultimate desire is to harvest the souls of all mortals, bringing them within his sway by spreading seeds of strife and discord in the mortal realms. To this end, he obsessively collects soul gems, and has even dragged pieces of Nirn into his realm to satisfy his insatiable desire for conquest. The more souls he collects, the more he wants.

Molag Bal values patience and cunning. He often deceives those he deals with, and has no trouble waiting exceedingly long periods of time for his plans to come to fruition. He also takes great pleasure in the suffering of mortals, and often has them tortured for his amusement. Those who fall into his clutches are forced to labor ceaselessly for his benefit. The Prince has a penchant for necromancy, often employing the use of the risen dead and forcing his followers to serve beyond the grave. He is capable of preserving live mortals far beyond their normal lifespan to prolong their suffering.

Molag Bal is acknowledged as the father of several children, though it is unclear what those relationships mean to him. He has a son, [Ozozzachar](Lore_Ozozzachar.md), who is a [Daedric Titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md). He also has a daughter named [Molag Grunda](Lore_Molag_Grunda.md), a [Winged Twilight](Lore_Winged_Twilight.md) who took the lowly [Frost Atronach](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md) Nomeg Gwai as her consort. Molag Bal did not approve of the relationship and had them both banished to Oblivion so he could punish them for eternity. A persistent legend about [Haymon Camoran](Lore_Haymon_Camoran.md) claims he is the progeny of Molag Bal and a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) woman.

Molag Bal's enemies are [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), [Lamae Bal](Lore_Lamae_Bal.md) and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md). He was at one point allied with [Azura](Lore_Azura.md). His summoning day is during [Chil'a](Lore_Chil%27a.md), on the 20th of Evening Star. He appears before his cultists in the guise of a mortal when invoked on other dates.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).

#### Moon Axle
[Moon Axle](Lore_Moon_Axle.md) was one of the eight [monster children](Lore_Monster_Children.md) from the unholy union of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), who initially escaped being slain. Moon Axle was made of many straight lines, though none would last too long. While Moon Axle was not the mightiest of Vivec's children, he was the most worrisome according to Vivec. He was immune to spears, so Vivec had to use the [sword not held](Lore_Sword_Not_Held.md) instead of [Muatra](Lore_Muatra.md). Vivec wounded Moon Axle heavily with the sword, and in order to finish the monster, switched to his giant form and reached into the west to pull out a canyon. Vivec held the canyon like a horn, reached east to grab a handful of [Nix-Hounds](Lore_Nix-Hound.md), and then blew the souls of the Nix-Hounds through the canyon, making a terrible noise that weakened Moon Axle to the point where Vivec could finally kill it with Muatra.

#### Morihaus, First Breath of Man
[Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md), also known as **Morihaus the Indomitable**, **Morihaus-Breath-of-Kyne**, the **First Breath of Man**, **Winged Bull**, **Sacred Bull**, **Bull of Kyne**, **Bull of the South**, and **Taker of the Citadel**, was the [Demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) of All Winds. His status as an [Ada](Lore_Ada.md) deity came from his relationship to his mother, the [Divine](Lore_Divines.md) [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) (or [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md)). He was the consort of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) and father of [Belharza](Lore_Belharza.md). Morihaus was said to be the finest archer of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He is a figure of great importance and reverence in both [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).

Before Kyne departed from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), she foresaw many needs coming to the land, and to assist any of those who would carry these needs on their shoulders, she created the [Lord's Mail](Lore_Lord%27s_Mail.md), with the first recipient being her son Morihaus. At some point, Kyne took the Lord's Mail away from Morihaus due to his arrogance. The artifact is also sometimes called the Armor of Morihaus due to his association with it. The connection with Kyne also granted Morihaus the powers of the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md). Similar to his relationship with the Lord's Mail, Morihaus was also aligned with [The Lord constellation](Lore_The_Lord.md).

Nearly every source describes Morihaus as a winged bull, be it a bull-man, man-bull, or the Bull of Kyne.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Bullman)</sup> He had a nose-ring, horns, and was capable of flight with his golden wings. At some point in time, Morihaus befriended the [Demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Fa-Nuit-Hen](Lore_Fa-Nuit-Hen.md), and together the two would often drink, play dice, and slay beasts that plagued the land.

#### Morwha, Yokudan Fertility Goddess
[Morwha](Lore_Morwha.md), (also called the **Teat God**, **Lady Morwha**, and **Desire's Root**) is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) goddess of fertility and love. She is a fundamental deity in the Yokudan pantheon and is the favorite wife of [Tall Papa](Lore_Tall_Papa.md). Morwha is always portrayed as four-armed, so that she can 'grab more husbands'. Her shrines are the shape of a beehive. She was described by potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) as the lusty fertility goddess of the Yokudans, but not as lusty as [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md). She is sometimes depicted alongside her winged guardians, or surrounded by flames and spears.

#### Mother Mammoth of the North
The [Mother Mammoth of the North](Lore_Mother_Mammoth_of_the_North.md) is an obscure entity from the ancient [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) mythology predating the [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) era.

The Mother Mammoth of the North is one of [Akha](Lore_Akha.md)'s mates. In the earliest days, when [Ahnurr](Lore_Ahnurr.md) and [Fadomai](Lore_Fadomai.md) were deeply in love, Akha ventured across the heavens, his trails forming the Many Paths. His father told him to seek love akin to that of Ahnurr and Fadomai. Akha united with the Mother Mammoth of the North. He also had other mates, including the [Dune Queen of the West](Lore_Dune_Queen_of_the_West.md) and the [Winged Serpent of the East](Lore_Winged_Serpent_of_the_East.md).

However, upon venturing to the South Akha never returned. [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) and his loyal guardians oversee the numerous [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) children of Akha, some of whom were from the union with the Mother Mammoth of the North, "for they are both terrible and kind".<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

#### Mother Sea
[Mother Sea](Lore_Mother_Sea.md), also called **Mother of Seas**, is an obscure entity sworn to by the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). She is related to Maormer beliefs about the afterlife. According to the tradition, Sea Elves must be returned to the Mother Sea and guided to the next world through song. It is said that failing to honor this rite can cause the restless dead to linger and haunt the living. To some Maormer, Mother Sea is a constant presence, "whether they sink or swim". This belief gave some Maormer the courage to face sea storms without fear.

#### Mother Serpent
[Mother Serpent](Lore_Mother_Serpent.md) was one of the Four Parents of certain [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) tribes of the [Deathlands](Lore_Deathlands.md). According to their accounts the world rested in the curve of her back.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TOAA-11)</sup> Although the serpentine imagery was present in Nedic ruins, alongside other three of the Parents it is unknown if it referred to her or to [the Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) [Constellation](Lore_Constellations.md), and how those entities were connected.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-NSR-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ANPW-13)</sup>

#### Muluk, the Blue God of the Goblins
Muluk is speculated to be a god worshipped by [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), invoked during rituals by their tribal shamans. It is thought that he is related to the [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) god [Mauloch](Lore_Mauloch.md)<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> or the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
