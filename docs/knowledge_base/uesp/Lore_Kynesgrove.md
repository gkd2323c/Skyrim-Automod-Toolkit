[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/SR-place-Kynesgrove.jpg/200px-SR-place-Kynesgrove.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Kynesgrove.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Kynesgrove.jpg) Kynesgrove circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Kynesgrove, a simplification of the original **Kyne's Grove**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOD-1)</sup>, and also known as **Dragonwood** is a small village south of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). The village survives off a [Malachite](Lore_Malachite.md) mine and some farming.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup> Although there are an abundant number of trees in the area, they are considered sacred to [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) and are not allowed to be harmed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRGemma-3)</sup>

The village also has an arena that is among the oldest ones still in service. Legend says the great First Era warrior [Gaiden Shinji](Lore_Gaiden_Shinji.md) was a regular fighter there before he went on to found the Imperial City arena. <sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Blades-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/ON-place-Kynesgrove.jpg/200px-ON-place-Kynesgrove.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kynesgrove.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kynesgrove.jpg) Kynesgrove circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/BL-place-Dragonwood_Arena.jpg/200px-BL-place-Dragonwood_Arena.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-place-Dragonwood_Arena.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-place-Dragonwood_Arena.jpg) Dragonwood Arena circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180) The [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Sahloknir](Lore_Sahloknir.md), who was slain in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) hero [Jorg Helmbolg](Lore_Jorg_Helmbolg.md), was interred in a dragon mound near the village.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOD-1)</sup> The [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) later established the hidden base of Dark Water Temple near the mound.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTDL-5)</sup>

In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it was home to a major shrine to Kyne, tended to by the [Keepers of the Grove](Lore_Keepers_of_the_Grove.md). Suffering from the loss of their loved ones to the Alliance War, they lashed out against Kyne, thralling many civilians as the Muzzled Owls and overtaking the village.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOSo C-6)</sup> Keeper Ormi was turned into a hagraven, but after her son Sahun revealed himself to still be alive and prayed for her, Kyne cleansed and forgave her.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOFKH-7)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the village of Dragonwood was an active settlement, being ruled by Lord Torbald. It had a rivalry with Pargran Village.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Dragon Wood-8)</sup>

In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Ganna and Gemma Uriel came to Kynesgrove to start the Frostbark lumber mill without realizing the nearby grove's trees were sacred to Kyne,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRGemma-3)</sup> with the village containing remnants of the grove they initially cut.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Uriel-9)</sup> Much of the town's business came from the [malachite](Lore_Malachite.md) ore from Steamscorch Mine. During the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) came to Kynesgrove with [Delphine](Lore_Delphine.md) after she tracked down [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md)'s next foray, and they slew [Sahloknir](Lore_Sahloknir.md) within moments of the wyrm's resurrection by Alduin.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Skyrim-2)</sup>
