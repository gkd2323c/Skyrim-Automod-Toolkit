## [Sabre Cat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sabre_Cat)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/SR-creature-Sabre_Cat.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Sabre_Cat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Sabre_Cat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Sabre_Cat.jpg) A sabre cat [Sabre Cats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sabre_Cat), also known as **Sabertooths** or **Rhojiit**, are large [cats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cat) that primarily inhabit [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), but have also been found in parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). They are powerful and fast predators that evolved two distinctive and dangerous sharp front teeth. They are very dangerous and will attack wildlife and people without hesitation, often ignoring the former in favor of the latter. They are known to carry the [disease](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Diseases) [Witbane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Witbane).

According to a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) [story](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Tale_of_Dro%27Zira) recorded by Sonia Vette, who overheard the story being told to a child, sabre cats were originally Khajiiti warriors in the Pride of Alkosh. They were called to Skyrim by [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh), but their devotion would earn them the ire of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhan.md), who would share a "roar" with [Ra'Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) to change the moon phases of [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md). Ra'Wulfharth used this ability to change the Khajiit warriors into the [Senche](Lore_Senche_(furstock).md) furstock, a very large quadruped form of Khajiit, and Lorkhaj "stripped from them all reason". Overtime these Rhojiit "grew small and lost their cunning altogether", resulting in the modern sabre cats that roam the land today. Potentially contradicting this story however, are accounts of sabre cats existing during the lifetime of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md).

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Sabertooth_Tiger), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Sabre_Cat.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Sabre_Cat), [Skyrim VSE](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Enemies#Sabre_Cat), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Snowy Sabre Cat), [Call to Arms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Call:to_Arms_Models), [Skyrim TAG](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Dungeons#Encounters)

## [Salamander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salamander)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/ON-creature-Salamander.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Salamander.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Salamander.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Salamander.jpg) A salamander [Salamanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salamander) are amphibians often found in tropical or moist regions such as the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and [Southern Elsweyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Southern_Elsweyr). They can vary in size from very small to extremely large. The largest salamander ever recorded, Graveld, was put on public display at the [Royal Menagerie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sil-Var-Woad), but later escaped to the wilderness and was ordered to be killed. All the large Salamanders of southern Tamriel are known for having elemental powers.

Large salamanders are capable of being very dangerous, with them attacking prey using their long tongues and tails. They also defend themselves with their intense elemental magic. Some salamanders have a knack for one particular brand of elemental magic. It's not too unusual to find a salamander that is talented primarily in producing natural shock, flame or frost magic instead of utilizing all three in equal measure. The toxin skin salamander is a breed that relies on venom instead of magic. Scholars studying arcane husbandry have learned of [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) rituals that grant luminescent qualities to common salamanders. They are known to possess varying forms of elemental magic.

[Newts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Newt) are a comparable species of amphibian.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOPSal-1)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Salamander), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Salamander), Betrayal of the Second Era

## Sandpiper
Sandpipers are a kind of [bird](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird). The term "sandpiper" was also used as a [profanity](Lore_Profanity.md). It was originally used as a derogatory [term](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bretic) by the [Druids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Druids) of the [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DB-4)</sup> It was used to describe someone from beyond the isles. Over time, it became part of the common language of all Systeran [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LA-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Systerans-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FSP-7)</sup> and eventually spread to other races living in the settlements there.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Borogoromir-8)</sup>

## [Scaleborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scaleborn)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/ON-mount-Scaleborn_Bear.jpg/200px-ON-mount-Scaleborn_Bear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Scaleborn_Bear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Scaleborn_Bear.jpg) A scaleborn bear The [Scaleborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scaleborn) are a product line of artificial [draconic](Lore_Dragon.md) creatures commissioned in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) by Queen [Khamira](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khamira) of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) for use as mounts in the newly reformed [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). They were [magically](Lore_Magic.md) created with "draconic fleshweave" by the [Telvanni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telvanni) Magister [Varkenel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varkenel), most famous for his creation of [vvardvarks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vvardvark).

Varkenel was previously commissioned to create a draconic creature, though the result was considered less than satisfactory. However, he was able to incorporate draconic fleshweave in the creation of the Scaleborn due to his commissioners in Elsweyr having the means to scavenge authentic draconic materials. Varknel considered it a rare pleasure to collaborate with someone outside House Telvanni. He was especially pleased with his cooperation with the Queen of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) and looked forward to accepting her offer of hospitality.

He operated on a timeline he considered reasonable and manageable. The Scaleborn creatures he created resembled [senche-raht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-raht), [wolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolf), [rams](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ram), and [bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear), the latter being the fourth form in the series. While bears were not native to Elsweyr, he suggested them as they had proven to be a popular form in his previous works.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Scaleborn_Bear)

## [Scalon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scalon)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/SI-creature-Scalon-Mania.jpg/200px-SI-creature-Scalon-Mania.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-creature-Scalon-Mania.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-creature-Scalon-Mania.jpg) A scalon native to Mania Scalons (or Scalon) are hulking amphibious monsters often found near water or in the company of other wild beasts throughout the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). They are a type of [Grummite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grummite) believed to develop from an adult [Baliwog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baliwog), but unlike adult Grummites, Scalons show no clear signs of intelligence and are treated as pests by the denizens of the Isles. Indeed, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) himself has been known to intervene when Scalons attack a mortal settlement. They are known to congregate around Scalon statues in what could be worship, or simply intrigue.

They walk on two legs and possess sharp claws and a powerful leaping attack, as well as the ability to turn invisible at will. Scalons from [Mania](Lore_Mania.md) take on a vibrant orange color, while [Demented](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) Scalons possess a turquoise coloration. Scalons carry a horrific number of dangerous diseases which can be contracted from their bite, known variously as [Scalon Fever](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scalon_Fever), [Scalon Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scalon_Blight), and [Scalon Sunburn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scalon_Sunburn). [Scalon fins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy_S#Scalon_Fin) are often harvested for their useful [alchemical](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy) properties; by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), samples of Scalon fin could be found circulating on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) as a rare alchemy ingredient.

**Found in:** - [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Scalon), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Hulking Scalon)

## [Scamp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/OB-creature-Scamp.jpg/200px-OB-creature-Scamp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Scamp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Scamp.jpg) A scamp [Scamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scamp) are one of the lesser sentient [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), who often enter into the mortal world to cause mischief and carry out errands for their Princes.

Scamps have been described disparagingly as the [skeevers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skeever) of Oblivion. They are capable of more easily "wriggling thorough the gaps" between realms. They are relatively weak Daedra, and often cowardly. They can can be easily defeated by most fighters except in large numbers. Other Daedra often look down upon them, though some find them endearing.

Scamps are typically thought of as dull creatures, described as semi-intelligent or as having "simple thoughts". For the greater Daedra, Scamps are useful minions, as their nimble hands can carry and manipulate objects, and they are intelligent enough to be cunning opponents in combat. Of the [Daedric Princes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) they serve, they are most commonly associated with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), though they also serve [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanguine), and [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina).

In appearance, Scamps are diminutive, [goblinesque](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Goblin-ken) humanoids. They possess tapered ears, furry legs, and [rat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rat) -like tails. They have sharp claws and teeth which they often use in combat or defense. Scamps apparently possess an anus, and are capable of defecation.

Scamps are one of the most common lesser Daedra summoned by mortals, often serving as [familiars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Familiar). Scamps are most commonly used by mages for simple tasks like gruntwork, but some could be entrusted with more complex tasks. One Scamp familiar was even granted full membership in the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), serving as an assistant chronicler and merchant. The [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) also trained scamps to wear packs to carry their valuables.

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Scamp), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Scamp), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Scamp), [Oblivion Mobile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/OBMobile:Scamp), [The Infernal City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City_Creatures#Notes), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Scamp), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Scamp), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Scarab](Lore_Scarab.md)
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-style_material-Scarab_Elytra.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-style_material-Scarab_Elytra.png) A Scarab [Scarabs](Lore_Scarab.md) are a variety of [beetle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beetle) that have cultural significance in various parts of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They have been used for their shells and as pets, and many hold them as an important [symbol](Lore_Symbols.md). [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), and [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) have all placed importance on the scarab.

Scarabs have significant symbolic and practical importance to the Dunmer. The **Black Scarab** is the House Symbol of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) and it is considered sacred by its members, their armors are often adorned with the symbol. Scarab elytra are used in the crafting of House Redoran equipment, Redoran shields appear to be fashioned from the overlapping chitin plates of a gigantic scarab.

In the [36 Lessons of Vivec](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) reiterates a speech by [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) to [Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar). In this speech Boethiah refers to [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) as "the Frame-maker, the Scarab". The [Commentaries](Lore_Mythic_Dawn_Commentaries.md) of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) also speak to this divine significance of the Scarab. Mankar claims [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is the realm of Lorkhan in some of his teachings with the phrase 'TAMRIEL AE DAEDROTH'. In his commentaries, he conveys the same message with the phrase 'SCARAB AE AURBEX'. Seemingly linked to this concept, the Sixth [Walking Way](Lore_Walking_Ways.md) has been referred to as the Scarab. In an account of the legend of Boethiah and [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) and his [followers](Lore_Orc.md) were balled into a singular sphere as "the scarab gathers dung".

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Miscellaneous_Items), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Giant_Scarab)

## [Scorion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scorion)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-creature-Scorion_Broodlord.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Scorion_Broodlord.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Scorion_Broodlord.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Scorion_Broodlord.jpg) A scorion [Scorions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scorion) are arthropodal [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md#Lesser_Daedra) whose purpose seems to be collecting and transferring [magical energy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magicka). Described as large "[Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) -touched" arachnoids, they have six legs that they use for walking and climbing, with a number of small fang-like appendages at the front, the upper two being used for channeling energy. They have a large thorax which glows red or orange depending on the type of energy collected, as well a hollow abdomen with no head, which also glows orange from its hole when energy is collected. They are rarely seen and were once used by the [Order of the Waking Flame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Waking_Flame) to collect residual torture energy for the construction of the Antecedent Cataclyst in the Dread Cellar. They possess [teleportation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Teleportation) capabilities. Scorions that get swept away during eruptions are sometimes crushed into rugged gems known as Scorion Diamonds by the intense crucible of stone and flame. Small ones are known as Pyrelings, which one scholar believed acted like [ticks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tick) on [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Scorion)

## [Scorpion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scorpion)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/ON-pet-Cerulean_Scorpion.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Cerulean_Scorpion.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Cerulean_Scorpion.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Cerulean_Scorpion.jpg) A scorpion [Scorpions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scorpion) are arachnids with pincers and venomous stingers that can found throughout the arid regions of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), most commonly [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Most small species give live birth to baby scorpions with soft shells, carrying their young on their back.

There are several varieties of scorpion. Some species are known to grow to massive sizes. Spook Scorpions are scorpions with a spectral appearance that could be found in [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen). Rihad Coffee Scorpions and Cerulean Scorpions can be kept as pets. Scorpions of various sizes can even be found in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md).

The deadly Giant Scorpion, like their smaller cousins, can be found in most arid regions of Tamriel. They are often the size of [horses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horse). Their sting is known to possess a paralyzing effect, so great caution is required when dealing with them. In addition to their great size and power, giant scorpions give birth by laying eggs in nests, in contrast to how smaller species reproduce. This is believed to be due to the sheer size and number of offspring the adult scorpion matrons can produce at a time.

**Found in:** - [Daggefall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Scorpion)

## Sea Cucumber
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fish-Sea_Cucumber.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-fish-Sea_Cucumber.png) A sea cucumber Sea Cucumbers (or simply cucumbers) are a type of bottom-dwelling aquatic invertebrate. [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) Cucumbers are a type of sea cucumber that can be found in the rivers of [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen). [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-mount-Clouded_Senche-Leopard.jpg/200px-ON-mount-Clouded_Senche-Leopard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Clouded_Senche-Leopard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Clouded_Senche-Leopard.jpg) A Clouded Senche-Leopard, a type of Senche-Cat [Senche-Cats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-Cat), also known as **Senches**, are large, quadrupedal [felines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cat) most often found in southern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), primarily [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), but can also be found in other provinces thanks to breeding. They are differentiated from the other big cat species of Tamriel by their often larger sizes, but they may be confused with other species due to culture and location, as well as their similar appearances. During the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), senche-cats and other creatures of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) were a chaotic hybrid of plant and animal, before the god [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre) established their forms as we know them today. Their original monstrous forms can be seen when the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) invoke the [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md).

The relationship between Senche-cats and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) are somewhat mysterious, and difficult to understand for non-Khajiit. The Khajiiti people consider themselves cousins or distant relatives to the Senche-cats, and the Senche-cat's secondary term, Senche, is also used to describe the [Senche](Lore_Senche_(furstock).md) and [Senche-raht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senche-raht) Khajiit furstocks. Some have even described Senche-cats as a subspecies or primitive variety of Khajiit. However, Senche-cats and Khajiit Senches are said to be distinct species, and the Senche-cats are incapable of speech. The Khajiit do feel a strong kinship towards the lesser cats, and have been able to tame them with ease. The Khajiit are also said to sense an intelligence in the Senche-cats that other races cannot, and some have been known to treat them as if they were intelligent.

The [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) have some superstitions about snails. To find a black snail in one's salad means ill luck in the week ahead, while a black snail on one's smallclothes means imminent death. ## [Spider](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/e/ee/DF-creature-Spider.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-creature-Spider.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-creature-Spider.gif) A spider [Spiders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider) are multi-legged arachnids that come in a myriad of sizes. Giant spiders are dangerous foes, being incredibly aggressive carnivores. Most can move very quickly, and deal mortal damage with their legs and fangs. Through their abdomen, spiders can excrete a sticky, thin substance called spider silk. With it, spiders perfect their dwellings, sealing unwanted exits/entrances and constructing a web, which they can use to quickly travel to any space within their abode. They are known to encase their victims in silken cocoons, so that they can feed on them later. Most species are venomous, their venom having a variety of debilitating effects depending on the species or individual. Often it has a paralyzing effect.

They tend to inhabit underground caves where the moisture keeps their chitinous skin pliable. Their broods operate under a matriarchy with a brood mother or spider queen. Some species are said to live upwards of 1,000 years.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/4/49/SH-creature-Night_Guard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-creature-Night_Guard.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-creature-Night_Guard.png) A corrupted Stormhold Overseer Stormhold Overseers were individuals that oversaw the city of [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold)'s prison that were corrupted, mutated, and driven mad by Warden [Quintus Varus'](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quintus_Varus) use of the [Stormhold Crystals](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold_Crystal).<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-SHStory-89)</sup> These deformed guards had pointy ears and red eyes, which were sought after by certain prisoners.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-90)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-91)</sup> They may have had somewhat [reptilian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile) traits, such as scales and stub tails.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-92)</sup> Some of them wore horned helmets or boots,<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-93)</sup> and they typically wielded axes, spiked clubs, or daggers.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-94)</sup>

The surviving Overseers and other afflicted ones were restored to normal when the prisoner known as the [Master Tunnel Rat](Lore_Master_Tunnel_Rat.md) defeated the greatly mutated Varus and used the Storm Crystal's power to remove the corruption of the camp.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-SHStory-89)</sup>

## [Swamp Jelly](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Jelly)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5b/ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Swamp_Jellies.jpg) From left to right are the cantaloupe (amber), mint (green), blue and plum (purple) swamp jellies [Swamp Jellies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Jelly) are floating creatures with elastic, gelatinous bodies that are native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). They are described as having a disquieting resemblance to aquatic [jellyfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jellyfish) that natural philosophers have no adequate explanation for. They tend to live solitary lives from other swamp jelly, coming only together for spawn, but are social animals. They are extremely docile in nature and make excellent travel companions for the marsh. Their diet consists of bugs.

Similar creatures to swamp jellies are found in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md),<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ONBlackreach Jelly-95)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-ONCave Jelly-96)</sup> [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-ONMoonkissed Jelly-97)</sup> and even [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md).<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-ONShroom Jelly-98)</sup>

## [Swamp Leviathan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Leviathan)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/LG-cardart-Swamp_Leviathan.png/200px-LG-cardart-Swamp_Leviathan.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Swamp_Leviathan.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Swamp_Leviathan.png) A swamp leviathan [Swamp Leviathans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swamp_Leviathan) (or simply **leviathans**) are massive [reptilian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reptile) creatures of terrible power found in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). They are described as a mountain of spines, with nine-foot-long claws and a jaw that juts out, exposing their tusks, they additionally possess a large sail on their back. Their hunting strategy has been known to involve trailing their prey for long periods of time. The [rats](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rat) of Black Marsh are known to prey upon them, overwhelming them by the hundreds and eating the massive creatures inside out.

Swamp leviathans were sometimes slain by the [Nagas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naga) of the [Dead-Water Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dead-Water_Tribe) as a rite of passage. The Argonian [Xit-Xaht Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xit-Xaht) of [Mazzatun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mazzatun) used scrimshawed leviathan tusks as protective material in their armor. The [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) historically created [bugles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Instruments#Horn) from the horns of leviathans. Skeletal remains believed to have belonged to swamp leviathans could be found in [Deepmire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deepmire).

**Found in:** - [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Swamp Leviathan)

## [Swan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swan)

Swans are prominent in various Tamrielic cultures. During the Interregnum, kites shaped like swans in flight were a common sight throughout Tamriel.
