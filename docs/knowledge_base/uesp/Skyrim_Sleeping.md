# Sleeping

**Sleeping** is the process of resting in a bed, sleeping bag/roll, or pile of hay to fully restore your [health](Skyrim_Health.md), [magicka](Skyrim_Magicka.md), and [stamina](Skyrim_Stamina.md).

Sleep can trigger autosave similar to [wait](Skyrim_Controls.md#General_Gameplay).

As long as you are not a [werewolf](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md) and do not have [the Lover Stone](Skyrim_The_Lover_Stone.md) active (see below), sleeping for any length of time also confers a bonus to the rate at which all [skills](Skyrim_Skills.md) increase, lasting for eight in-game hours (equivalent to 24 real-world minutes without [fast travel](Skyrim_Fast_Travel.md)).

A message on screen tells you which bonus you have received; for example, *"You awaken feeling Well Rested"*.

You may inspect *active effects* in the [Magic menu](Skyrim_Controls.md).

- In a normal bed, you receive the **Rested** bonus, which confers a 5% bonus to skill increases.
- In a bed that you own, you receive the **Well Rested** bonus, which gives you a 10% bonus to skill increases. This applies to beds in purchased [houses](Skyrim_Houses.md) and rented [inn](Skyrim_Inns.md) beds.
- If you are [married](Skyrim_Marriage.md) and you sleep in the building where your spouse is, you receive the **Lover's Comfort** bonus, which is a 15% bonus to skill increases.
- <sup>([DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md))</sup> If you're a [vampire](Skyrim_Vampirism.md) and you sleep in a coffin, you will receive the **Vampiric Blood Rested** bonus, which boosts your [resistance to magic](Skyrim_Resist_Magic.md) by 10% for one in-game day.
- <sup>([HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md))</sup> If you have [adopted a child](Skyrim_Adoption.md) and you sleep in the same house as your child, you will receive the **Mother's Love** or **Father's Love** bonus, which makes [healing](Skyrim_Restore_Health.md) spells and potions more effective.

- In [Survival](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md)<sup>([CC](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md))</sup>, sleeping plays a more vital role. You must sleep in order to [level](Skyrim_Leveling.md) up, similar to previous *[Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)* games. Sleeping is also the only way to recover [fatigue](Skyrim_Fatigue.md)<sup>([CC](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md))</sup>. Sleeping outside will no longer confer the Well Rested bonus. Sleeping one hour results in **Refreshed** (no bonus, no penalty). Sleeping two or more hours is required for Well Rested. After four in-game hours (equivalent to twelve real-world minutes), Well Rested reverts to Refreshed.

Sleeping bonuses are all cumulative with the skill-increase bonuses from any one of the three [Guardian Stones](Skyrim_The_Guardian_Stones.md) —the Mage Stone, the Thief Stone, and the Warrior Stone. If the [Aetherial Crown](Skyrim_Aetherial_Crown.md)<sup>([DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md))</sup> is on during sleeping, the skill bonus is not activated. You must unequip and re-equip it to sum up all the possible skill bonuses (Guardian + Lover stones + rested/Lover's comfort).

## Notes
- Rather than sleeping, you can use the [Wait](Skyrim_Controls.md#Wait) command anywhere to fully restore your health, magicka, and stamina. However, this will not give you any resting bonuses.
- You cannot sleep or wait when enemies are nearby, or while you are [trespassing](Skyrim_Crime.md).
- You cannot sleep in a bed owned by someone else, though some NPCs will allow you to use their bed if you [befriend](Skyrim_Disposition.md) them. Killing the owner of a bed will not (usually) remove the ownership.
- When the owner of a house is killed, beds in the previously owned house become free to sleep in. Note that this may be a different person than the bed's owner.
- Sleeping on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md) may cause you to wake up in a trance at one of the island's [All-Maker Stones](Skyrim_All-Maker_Stones.md) if they have yet to be [cleansed](Skyrim_Cleansing_the_Stones.md). See the [main article](Skyrim_All-Maker_Stones.md#Notes) for more information.
- The [Aetherial Crown](Skyrim_Aetherial_Crown.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> makes it possible to obtain both resting bonuses and the benefits of the Lover Stone. See the related article for details.

## Bugs
- The time remaining for **Rested**, **Well Rested**, and **Lover's Comfort** is not displayed on the list of active effects. Descriptions indicate each lasts 8 hours but offer no indication of how much time has elapsed. This 8 hours is actually in-game time, translating to 24 minutes of actual time (less if the player uses the fast travel or wait feature). - [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) This issue is fixed by the mod [See Time Remaining for Rested Effects](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:See_Time_Remaining_for_Rested_Effects), which correctly shows the 24-minute countdown.
- You may not receive the message after sleeping for whatever bonus you acquired. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Rarely, you may not get any bonus for resting (regardless if it's a bed you own, a bedroll in a bandit encampment, or a rented bed in an inn).
