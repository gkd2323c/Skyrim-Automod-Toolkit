[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Gil-Var-Delle (also known as **Gilverdale** by the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md))<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMIPIWLS-2)</sup> is a ruined settlement located in western [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md), in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Once a sizeable town that surrounded its [Elden Tree](Lore_Elden_Tree.md) called Lifetimber,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup> it was known as a thriving [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) community full of life and [music](Lore_Music.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONDanys-4)</sup> but now, it is a desolate waste, one where no one dares to approach. The legend of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s devouring of Gil-Var-Delle has cemented itself in Bosmer culture and has been the subject of several fictional pieces that dramaticize it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDOG-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONNoryon-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSubmission-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png/200px-ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Gil-var-delle.png) *"Gil-Var-Delle: abide I now and forevermore in the town consumed by Molag Bal."'— [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md)* *"But I do remember last night's dream: ghastly and vivid. Molag Bal consumed the Bosmer town of Gilverdale in a single, dreadful night. The brutality displayed was incomparable. I could not resist watching the feasting. Oh, the slaughter! But these were echoes of the distant past; it had happened in the First Era."*
— [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) on his dreams of Gil-Var-Delle's devouring<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EGT-8)</sup> Gil-Var-Delle was one of the [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) cities of Valenwood, which all represented the mythical [Tower](Lore_The_Towers.md) of [Green-Sap](Lore_Green-Sap.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AE4TET-9)</sup> It rooted itself on the [Southern Sea](Lore_Southern_Sea.md) coast, northwest of [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONGil-10)</sup> The town's history before its destruction is largely unknown, but it was active for most of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), until its destruction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup>

At the time, Valenwood was under the occupation of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). To ensure the Bosmer would never rebel and unite again, they split the province into nine kingdoms, formerly minor trading posts, now with some individual autonomy. These were ruled by the former treethanes of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) who themselves lost significant power.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PGE3-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONUnderroot-12)</sup> Gil-Var-Delle by the latemost First Era was the seat of its own kingdom as well.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-IWTW-13)</sup>

On the 19th of Sun's Dawn, [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Domination, Molag Bal appeared in a red mist over Gil-Var-Delle and destroyed the city in a single night.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EGT-8)</sup> The legend of how the Daedric Prince appeared in Valenwood became widespread and infamous. Though no one could technically confirm the event's true nature.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDOG-5)</sup> It is generally accepted that the perpetrator who brought Molag Bal there was the Mad King of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), [Dro'Zel](Lore_Dro%27Zel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HOSAO-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-IWTW-13)</sup>

Dro'Zel hosted a dance party in [Senchal Palace](Lore_Senchal_Palace.md) for [Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md) and a Bosmer bard from Gil-Var-Delle arrived to tell the iconic yet tragic tale, the *[Legend of the Lovers](Lore_Legend_of_the_Lovers.md)*. Heartbroken and frustrated by the bard for bringing the mood down, King Dro'Zel pre-maturely retired to his chambers and in a fit of rage, summoned Molag Bal to destroy the bard's hometown.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HOSAO-14)</sup> What can be said without a doubt about Gil-Var-Delle's ruining is that a great, catastrophic event occurred here and turned it into a land of death.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDOG-5)</sup> The corpse of its Elden Tree became known as Black Log<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup> and the city ruins became corrupted by his remaining energies.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONMeva-15)</sup> The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god, [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) was present around Gil-Var-Delle at the time, while she was en route to [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md). She was powerless to save the city, and so her tears of sorrow crystallized into a gem called Almalexia's Tear. It was said to hold all of her concentrated fury toward Molag Bal, and that no mortal can handle its power without being destroyed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONDanys-4)</sup>

After Molag Bal's devouring, Gil-Var-Delle's ruins became a blight on Valenwood's forest. He left behind large residuals of daedric energies, some of the largest in any site at the time, according to the [Sapiarch](Lore_Sapiarch.md) of Oblivion Studies, Culalanwe. By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the ruins were still polluted with his daedric essence.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-FTNOCSOOS-16)</sup> To the Wood Elves, it is a superstitious place that needs to be actively avoided. It is unknown if anyone survived since no one there was heard again.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDOG-5)</sup> In [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581), the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) scholar [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) traveled to Valenwood and somehow found his way into Gil-Var-Delle, where he went mad and disappeared, leaving behind his notes for the *[Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel](Lore_The_Improved_Emperor%27s_Guide_to_Tamriel.md)*. His writings were forwarded to Cyrodiil, but Flaccus Terentius by then was presumed dead.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-EGTA-17)</sup>

When Molag Bal's [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) occurred in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) built one of their many [dark anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) in the hollowed Black Log and used devices called Fangs of Chaos to fuel it with Molag Bal's residual energy. Almalexia, sensing the disturbance in Gil-Var-Delle, sent a [High Ordinator](Lore_High_Ordinators.md) named Danys and allies from across the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) to destroy the dark anchor. But the swarms of [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) overwhelmed them and left Danys to deal with it alone. He enlisted help from [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), who Almalexia herself envisioned to arrive and destroy the dark anchor, using Almalexia's Tear to break the Fangs of Chaos. After the Worm Cult's threat was gone, Danys left to inform the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) of what just transpired.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONWakening-18)</sup>

## Legacy and Potential Restoration
*"Oh, Gil-Var-Delle, your name is desolation on my lips."*
—An excerpt from a common tavern song<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONDanys-4)</sup> In response to the Devouring of Gil-Var-Delle, [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), another one of the Tribunal, went to Molag Bal's plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and meet with eight of the most prominent daedric princes ([Azura](Lore_Azura.md), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), Molag Bal, and [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)) to arrange the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md), an agreement to prevent similar events like in Gil-Var-Delle. One of the terms was for the princes to never appear in the mortal world, but only through certain intermediaries like [witches](Lore_Witches.md) or sorcerers.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TCC-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-2920V4-20)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONSotha-21)</sup>

The catastrophic event has ingrained itself into the Wood Elves and Valenwood's subconscious. The people have long resented Molag Bal's actions and know him as the terrible demon that destroyed the city.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSubmission-7)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONCardea-22)</sup> However, some people doubt the legend, particularly Molag Bal's involvement. The author Fastor, writer of *[The Devouring of Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_The_Devouring_of_Gil-Var-Delle.md)* posits a common question: "If Bal himself visited this plane with evil intent, why do any of us remain?" Some argue that someone or something stopped him, but that also lacks feasible evidence. Fastor proposes that it was a large fire that destroyed Gil-Var-Delle and that no tangible threat lingered in the ruins.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDOG-5)</sup>

It has also been the subject of many [songs](Lore_Songs.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONDanys-4)</sup> and fictional stories that try to depict it, though none of them can be proven true.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TDOG-5)</sup> One of the most famous examples includes *[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)* written by the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) author, [Carlovac Townway](Lore_Carlovac_Townway.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-IWTW-13)</sup> Another such tale states that [the Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) sent the greatest [lute](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lute) -player, the Emerald [Spinner](Lore_Spinners.md) Agon, to play in front of Molag Bal. His actions saved the rest of Gil-Var-Delle's citizens, but in the end, he died. He and his lute were then buried under the first graht-oak planted by King [Eplear](Lore_Eplear.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONNoryon-6)</sup>

Molag Bal's influence stayed with Gil-Var-Delle for many years, well into the mid-Second Era.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-FTNOCSOOS-16)</sup> The fogs of Coldharbour called miasmata lingered in the ruins,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup> and the water that flowed through it became polluted and undrinkable, often referred to as blightwater.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup> It even poisoned some of the underground springs across Grahtwood.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup> Efforts have been made to restore the vale of Gil-Var-Delle, with people believing in the notion that all wounds heal in time. Locals travel to the site and drop blessed water into the river and bring strength to the earth.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONGlothorien-23)</sup> Black Log holds Lifetimber Pollen which could be used to re-grow the graht-oak.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONUnused-3)</sup>

By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), foul energies continued to linger in the region surrounding Gil-Var-Delle. Local Bosmer were notably more resilient due to these dangers.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OBBR-24)</sup>
