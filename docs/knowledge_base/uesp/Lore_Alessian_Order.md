# Alessian Order

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/OB-map-Cyrodiil.jpg/200px-OB-map-Cyrodiil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Cyrodiil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-map-Cyrodiil.jpg) Map of Cyrodiil | - *That is not cruel which cures,* <br> - *O faith, charity, rigor.* <br> - *By faith true heart endures,* <br> - *O hope, clarity, vigor.* | | - *Seventy-Seven shall guide us,* <br> - *O praise, honor, and duty.* <br> - *Alessia lives inside us,* <br> - *And truth is one with beauty.* |

- — *[The Song-Never-Sung-at-Twilight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Proper-Life__Three_Chants)*

The Alessian Order was a hugely influential religious sect in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) which preached strict monotheism and worship of [the One](Lore_The_One.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SATD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3C-3)</sup> The Order arose in the coastal jungle of what is now [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), where a prophet named [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md), who had spoken to the "Enlightened One" Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), began to question the validity of elven rule. These sentiments led to increasingly abstract and unknowable depictions of a one true God, though they tolerated the worship of polytheistic pantheons as well. The divine aspects worshipped by the various humans and [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) became many of the saints and spirits of the evolving Alessian canon. In some places, they replaced the existing belief system with something more mutable, as in eastern [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), where the traditional Nordic version of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) was purportedly replaced by a "baroque" veneration of ancestor spirits and god-animals. They first gained footing in the lower classes of the [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md), and in [1E 361](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_361), the inexplicably charismatic Order became the far-reaching and powerful theocracy of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), enforcing their doctrines throughout it, and their influence on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) has been immense ever since.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3C-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-4)</sup> At its peak, the influence of the arch-prelate of the Order was nearly equal to that of the Emperor himself.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CATAYQ-5)</sup> Eventually, however, the Order collapsed as a result of the [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) in [1E 2321](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2321).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-COTF-6)</sup>

The Order was very austere and severe, and life under their rule is remembered for its cruelty and lack of entertainment.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3C-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RTR-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFL-8)</sup> [Heartlanders](Lore_Heartlands.md) of Cyrodiil remember their rule as a dark age.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ROCW-9)</sup> A sect of the Order, the [Marukhati Selective](#The_Dance_of_the_Selectives), is said to have caused a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) spanning one thousand and eight years by attempting to exorcise elements of elven [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) from Imperial [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-WWYDB-10)</sup> It's worth noting that one school of thought dismisses the whole idea of the "Middle Dawn" as scholarly error, and asserts that the Alessian Order only lasted about one hundred and fifty years.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TDBRE-11)</sup> However, this is thrown into doubt by the fact that a book expounding this hypothesis is itself found displaced in time, an observation consistent with the Middle Dawn hypothesis.

## The Alessian Doctrines
The Alessian Order followed the Alessian Doctrines. The "Seventy Seven Inflexible Doctrines" began as legal codes written by Marukh following his "Century of Penance" in the Stonemeadows, whereupon he claimed to hear the words of the shade of Alessia during a tortured delirium, and he wrote them down in his own blood. He eventually came to the epiphany that death is an illusion, and through devotion to "Proper-Life and Ehlnofic Annulment", the "[Arkayn](Lore_Arkay.md) Cycle" can be conquered.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TIOD-12)</sup>

His code famously stated that "all are guilty until they have proven themselves innocent", which is still a guiding principle of the legal system in Tamriel.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LB-13)</sup> The Doctrines forbade "trafficke with daimons", without actually defining "daimon".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OO-14)</sup> His preachings evolved into religious codes which became the guiding principles of the Alessian Reformation of Marukh, which led to the proliferation of the modern Tamrielic pantheon of the Eight Divines.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3C-3)</sup>

Above all, the Doctrines were notorious for being firmly opposed to the elves, particularly the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md). Though the Alessian Empire was founded in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) following the fall of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), Ayleids continued to reside in modern-day Cyrodiil, often as vassals of the Empire. Continuing resentment towards them was one reason why the anti-elven Order proved to be so popular. They began sacking Ayleid settlements in the early 1E 300s, and in 1E 361, their Doctrines took on the force of law throughout the Empire.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-4)</sup> The Order was responsible for the sacking of [Malada](Lore_Malada.md), an important Ayleid temple, in [1E 393](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_393).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-COTF-6)</sup> The powerful city-state of [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md) initially resisted the Order by force, but over time its inhabitants fled to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to avoid persecution.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

In accordance with their scriptures, the Order tirelessly ferreted out and destroyed elven writings within their domain.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-16)</sup> They placed severe restrictions on the work of artists,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOHeartland-17)</sup> and recognized no difference between religious and political matters, teaching that to resist the Emperor is to resist the Divines.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RTR-7)</sup>

## The Rise and Fall of the Order

- *"No one will stand in the way of the Alessians! Kill, and keep killing, in the Name of the One!"* — *Glenumbria: Alessian Orders*<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-GAO-18)</sup>

The Alessians earned many enemies. Their rise to power in Cyrodiil soured relations with the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) in Valenwood, who had initially established cordial relations with the Empire.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3V-19)</sup> The [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), not to be confused with the Alessian Empire, was at various times receptive and resistant to the Order. The [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) in particular were resistant to Alessia's compromise due to its incorporation of elven beliefs with their own. Unlike the other descendants of the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md), the Nords had not adopted elven beliefs, as they had not known elven rule (at least, not since the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), when the elves were driven from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md)).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SATD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FC-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TM-21)</sup>

Nevertheless, High King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md) of Skyrim became a significant ally of the Order, but he was killed by the [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) for the "iniquities" of his Alessian faith (he was on his way to Cyrodiil at the time to advocate for a joint war against the elves).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-16)</sup> Many of the Ayleids the Order had driven out of Cyrodiil were absorbed by the Bosmer in Valenwood and the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) clan of elves in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), so the strengthening of these groups over the ensuing years could be attributed to a bolstering of their ranks by Ayleid refugees (though the subject remains little studied by scholars).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-4)</sup>

The Order became a victim of its own success. The priesthood, spread as it was throughout the regions of Tamriel, developed schisms over time.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup> Though the movement had started in Colovia, heavy Nordic influence, as well as concerns about the hostile [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) in nearby [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), created a buffer in western Cyrodiil against the influence of the Alessian priesthood which had emerged to the east.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3C-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RTR-7)</sup> Resistance to the Alessian Doctrines in western Cyrodiil eventually led to the formation of an autonomous government for them, the Colovian Estates, which set the stage for the downfall of the Order.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup>

The Alessians were no strangers to suppressing rebellions, but when western [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), led by [Rislav Larich](Lore_Rislav_Larich.md), successfully broke away from the Empire in [1E 478](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_478), it spelled the beginning of the end.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RTR-7)</sup> The Direnni pressed their advantage in High Rock, banning the Doctrines, and the Alessians ultimately couldn't recover from several military defeats, notably the [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFL-8)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-DRD-22)</sup> Neither the Direnni nor the Alessians won High Rock; it was eventually the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) who rose up to claim their own land.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3All-23)</sup> [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md)'s first act as High King of Skyrim in [1E 480](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_480) was to ban the Doctrines.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RTR-7)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-FSOKW-24)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Plaque-25)</sup>

### The Dance of the Selectives
*[L]et the [Staff of Towers](Lore_Staff_of_Towers.md) be prepared for the ritual that will cleanse the protean substrate of the Aldmeri Taint. All Selectives are to initiate chants of Proper-Life and maintain them until a state of monothought is achieved. Then each shall Dance, duration-forward then volteface, till the Roll of Time winds withershins.*
— [Fervidius Tharn](Lore_Fervidius_Tharn.md), Arch-Prelate of the Maruhkati Selective<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-VFTDB-26)</sup> The Marukhati Selective (also spelled Maruhkati) was an elite sect within the Alessian Order. Their beliefs are summarized in *[The Exclusionary Mandates](Lore_The_Exclusionary_Mandates.md)*. Due to their intense devotion to singularity, they considered all their beliefs equally important and crucial. They believed that the linear nature of time demonstrated that Akatosh is (or at least, should be) of a unitary essence. They asserted that [Shezarr](Lore_Shezarr.md) is "Singularly Misplaced and therefore Doubly Venerated", that "Proper-Life" as enunciated by Marukh is fostered by "monothought", and it serves to expunge the "Aldmeri Taint". They believed it was necessary to act in the mortal realm to achieve their divine goal to expunge this taint, and that "Akatosh is Time is Proper-Life is Taint-Death". In *[Vindication for the Dragon Break](Lore_Vindication_for_the_Dragon_Break.md)*, Fervidius Tharn (who became Arch-Prelate of the Selectives in [1E 1188](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1188)<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-HTON-27)</sup>) summarized the Mandates by stating that the goal of the Selectives was "to reverse the error of Sanctus Primus and restore Ak-at-Osh to humanadic purity".

Essentially, their animosity towards all things elven was so great that they could not tolerate the notion of Aldmeri influence on their beloved Akatosh. When their rituals could not disprove this influence, the fanatical scholar-priests decided to exorcise the Aldmeri traits instead. The sect's secret masters channeled the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) itself to mythically remove these aspects from the Dragon God.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Gahgdar-28)</sup> A staff or tower (or one represented within the other) appeared before them. The secret masters danced upon it until it writhed and trembled and spoke its protonymic (its real, original name: "Tam! RUGH!"<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-29)</sup>). The tower split into eight pieces and Time broke, becoming nonlinear. After one thousand and eight years ([1E 1200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1200) -2208, the estimation recognized by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md)), the pieces of the tower came to rest in Mundus, and the Middle Dawn ended.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TILWWYDB-30)</sup>

Every culture on Tamriel remembers the Middle Dawn in some fashion, mostly as a time of unaccountable spiritual anguish. Several texts survive this timeless period, all (unsurprisingly) conflicting with each other regarding events, people, and regions. Some say certain wars happened, some say the gods walked, and some assign a certain color to the sun. But almost always, sources seemed to contradict each other. Whether or not the secret masters of the Marukhati Selective were successful is unknown, and any records of their survival were destroyed by the War of Righteousness that ended the Alessian Order a hundred years later.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TILWWYDB-30)</sup> The only thing all cultures consistently agree upon is that, one by one, eight stars fell from the sky.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-WWYDB-10)</sup>

### The Lingering Order
Much like their prophet, the Alessian Order seemed to refuse to die. They apparently survived for almost two thousand years (though, as noted above, some scholars raise issues with the timeline).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TDBRE-11)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TLST-31)</sup> They are said to have spent much of the First Era fighting the Direnni for control of High Rock.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3All-23)</sup> The Bretons eventually joined the Alessian Empire during the reign of Empress Hestra in [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029), but they seceded almost a thousand years later due to the "excesses" of the Order. An attempt to bring them back into the Empire in [1E 2305](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2305) ended poorly for the Alessians. The Legions of Piety and Grace were sent to Bangkorai under Abbot-General [Priscus Mactator](Lore_Priscus_Mactator.md) to suppress the Bretons, but the Imperials fell at the Bangkorai Garrison.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-BSOHR-32)</sup>

The Alessian Order would never again be what it once was. When the War of Righteousness broke out in 1E 2321, the Order which had almost ruled the world finally undid itself in a ten-year span.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1C-2)</sup> During this war, their great monastic complex at [Lake Canulus](Lore_Lake_Canulus.md) was razed.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-COTF-6)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TSOP7-33)</sup>

From the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) onwards, the infamous [Temple Zero Society](Lore_Temple_Zero_Society.md) continued to preach the 'Monkey Truth', with their own interpretation of Marukhati philosophy.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-the_seed-34)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-35)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-36)</sup>

## Notes
- The exact length of the Seventy Seven Inflexible Doctrines is not known, but some sections are considered quite banal.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-F2-37)</sup>
- [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), the King of Worms, alleged that it was the High King of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) who was responsible for the Dragon Break "in the first place".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-WWYDB-10)</sup> However, Mannimarco immediately also credits the Selectives for the Middle Dawn, so it is unclear what role he claims for the King.
- [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md)'s inkwell was still known to be in existence by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and was seen as a sacred item.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-38)</sup>
