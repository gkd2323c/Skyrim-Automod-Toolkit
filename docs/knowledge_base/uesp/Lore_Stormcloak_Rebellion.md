The Stormcloak Rebellion, also known as the **Skyrim Civil War**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Card Lore-1)</sup> and **Ulfric's Uprising**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Legends-2)</sup> was a civil war in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). It was fought between the forces of the [Third Empire](Lore_Empire.md), mobilized in western Skyrim, and the [Stormcloak](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) rebels of eastern Skyrim. The war saw two claimants to Skyrim's throne fight against each other, with the Empire supporting [Elisif the Fair](Lore_Elisif_the_Fair.md)'s claim of High Queen and the Stormcloaks insisting [Jarl Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) had earned his right as High King.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Gerdur-3)</sup>

## Background
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/LG-cardart-Markarth_Bannerman.png/200px-LG-cardart-Markarth_Bannerman.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Markarth_Bannerman.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Markarth_Bannerman.png) A soldier during the Markarth Incident<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Card Lore-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Elenwen.jpg) Elenwen, Ulfric's interrogator
### Lead-up to Conflict
During the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) between the Third Empire and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) legionaries were crucial to the Empire's survival, but this left the Reach undefended when the native [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) rebelled against the Nordic occupation and declared their independence. The Reach was relatively peaceful and fair during the period and applied for status as an Imperial Province.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBo M-4)</sup> Concurrently, Ulfric Stormcloak of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) left his tutelage under the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) to fight in the war,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ulfric-5)</sup> during which he was captured by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md). Ulfric was tortured and led to believe the information he had given up led to the capture of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), though it had already fallen and the information he gave up was considered inconsequential.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dossier-6)</sup>

Ulfric, after escaping, formed the Stormcloaks as a militia. Without Imperial assistance, they successfully retook the Reach from the native inhabitants who had overthrown the Nords. After the Empire sent soldiers to re-establish control, Ulfric Stormcloak demanded free worship of Talos, which had been outlawed under the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) that ended the Great War.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBo M-4)</sup> The Empire, which was already fairly lax in their enforcement,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Alvor-7)</sup> reluctantly granted it, hoping the Dominion would not find out. When the Thalmor demanded the Stormcloaks' arrests, with a second Great War on the line, Jarl [Hrolfdir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrolfdir) of Markarth obeyed, sending Ulfric to an Imperial prison for seven years from [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176) to [4E 183](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_183).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TBo M-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ulfric-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Prima-8)</sup><sup>:877</sup> Supporters of the militia called the event 'The Markarth Incident',<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Igmund-9)</sup> and it forced the Empire to crack down on Talos worship across Skyrim by granting the Thalmor Justiciars access to Imperial territory.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Alvor-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/LG-cardart-Crusader%27s_Assault_Alt.png/200px-LG-cardart-Crusader%27s_Assault_Alt.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Crusader%27s_Assault_Alt.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Crusader%27s_Assault_Alt.png) Stormcloak forces lead a charge into Legion lines
### Pre-War Skirmishes
Some felt the Stormcloak Rebellion truly began with The Markarth Incident. Indeed, the first hostilities arose when Ulfric was released from prison and took over [his late father](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoag_Stormcloak)'s position as Jarl of Windhelm.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Prima-8)</sup><sup>:877</sup> A skirmish between Imperial forces and the growing Stormcloak militia occurred outside of the city,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Solaf-10)</sup> with other clashes also being fought in Skyrim.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Vulwulf-11)</sup> The victors of these clashes is unknown, but the Legion was reported as either burning the bodies of the deceased,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Nura-12)</sup> or leaving the bodies on the battlefield.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Vulwulf-11)</sup> Despite these conflicts, the Stormcloaks did not gain much support from the general populace.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Hadvar-13)</sup>

Soon after the High King of Skyrim, [Istlod](Lore_Istlod.md), died, the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) met to choose a new High King. As Istlod's son, [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md), was already set to take the throne, Ulfric used the opportunity to air his grievances against the Empire and rally the Jarls for Skyrim's independence in nearly treasonous terms. Torygg himself admired Ulfric and was sympathetic to his cause. In early [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Ulfric arrived at the Blue Palace, let in by the guards under the presumption that he was there to ask the High King to declare independence. Torygg was caught off guard when Ulfric challenged him to a duel over his allegiance to the Empire.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Sybille-14)</sup> Even though he knew he could not best Ulfric in combat, he recognized his honor would be stained by denying him and accepted.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Torygg-15)</sup> Ulfric killed Torygg using the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) before running him through with his sword, and fled the city pending arrest. He was branded a murderer and traitor by the Empire,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Aldis-16)</sup> while some Nords believed the duel was legal and true to the old ways of Skyrim, thus officially starting the Stormcloak Rebellion.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Roggvir-17)</sup>

## The War

### Logistics and Allegiances
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/SR-menu-Civil_War_Champions.jpg/200px-SR-menu-Civil_War_Champions.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-menu-Civil_War_Champions.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-menu-Civil_War_Champions.jpg) Champions from both sides Several military forts in Skyrim were occupied by bandits during the war, limiting strategic garrisoning on both sides.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rikke-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Galmar-19)</sup> Emperor [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) was hesitant to send aid, as it would be weakening the border with the Aldmeri Dominion. In conjunction with worsening avalanches in [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md), the emperor's aversion meant Skyrim had to fend for itself, largely.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-IMFN-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Tullius-21)</sup> General [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md) was brought in from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) at the emperor's request to quell the rebellion, instilling faith among both his soldiers and the Imperial citizenry. However, his forces were limited, commanding only "a bare handful of legions".<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Tullius-21)</sup> At one point, the emperor sent Tullius intelligence regarding the whereabouts of the [Lord's Mail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lord%27s_Mail), intending for Tullius to wear the relic and boost morale.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Lt GT-22)</sup> Both the Legion and the Stormcloaks were forced to rely upon local recruits to bolster their numbers.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-THo S-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-NA-24)</sup>

The western portion of Skyrim, including the Holds of [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), and [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md), remained under Imperial control, with Torygg's wife Elisif the Fair taking the role of Jarl of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), supported by the Empire for High Queen.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rikke-18)</sup> In the east, the [Old Holds](Lore_Old_Holds.md) of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), and [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md)<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-PGE1S-25)</sup> supported the Stormcloaks, with Ulfric maintaining his position as Jarl of Windhelm, the ancient capital of Skyrim.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rikke-18)</sup> [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md) remained the only neutral Hold at the onset of the war, with Jarl [Balgruuf the Greater](Lore_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md) torn between conflicting loyalties to the Empire and Skyrim. He continued to privately worship Talos and allowed free worship of the god in his city, but felt that the true enemy remained the Aldmeri Dominion and that the White-Gold Concordat was necessary to maintain peace.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Balgruuf-26)</sup>

### Notable Events
On the 15th of [Last Seed](Lore_Last_Seed.md) (a few months after the killing of [High King Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md)),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Hadvar-13)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-UDQC-27)</sup><sup>:249</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> [Jarl Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) and his top lieutenants were captured upon riding to Darkwater Crossing in southern [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), after [General Tullius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:General_Tullius) and his [Legionnaries](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) immediately launched an ambush upon them outside the settlement. Ulfric Stormcloak and his bodyguard were outnumbered five-to-one and he capitulates before the ambush turns into a bloodbath, releasing himself into Imperial custody.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Ralof Dialogue-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Hadvar Dialogue-29)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-UDQC-27)</sup><sup>:249</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> The Legion had been attempting to catch Ulfric since the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) began, but Stormcloak always seemed to slip through the Legion's fingers like they knew the Imperials were coming—until the coming of General Tullius and his "masterstroke" ambush at Darkwater.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Hadvar Dialogue-29)</sup> Two others were caught up in the ambush as well, a horse thief and a [traveller](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) intending to cross the border.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Ralof Dialogue-28)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SRUQ-30)</sup> Additionally the [Sheathed Blades](Lore_Sheathed_Blades.md) are said to have been involved in the whole affair, with their Jarls sending them into the conflict.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-UDQC-27)</sup><sup>:249</sup> The legion travelled southwards to cross down into [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) with the prisoners, but General Tullius changed his mind and stopped at [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md) to execute them instead<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Hadvar Dialogue-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Ralof Dialogue-28)</sup> (of note, the [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md) south to Cyrodiil was "all but closed" at the time due to avalanches in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md)).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> [Ambassador Elenwen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elenwen) of the Thalmor attempted to intervene when they all arrived at Helgen to prevent Ulfric's execution,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dossier-6)</sup> but this attempt was unsuccessful, and Ulfric was lined up for the chopping block by General Tullius.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Tullius-21)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/LG-cardart-Triumphant_Jarl_Alt.png/200px-LG-cardart-Triumphant_Jarl_Alt.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Triumphant_Jarl_Alt.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Triumphant_Jarl_Alt.png) Jarl Balgruuf after accepting Imperial aid In a twist of fate, the village was suddenly attacked by the dragon [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), allowing some prisoners, including Ulfric and the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), to escape. Alduin's attack signaled the return of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) to Skyrim, a sign of the End Times in Nordic culture.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Hadvar-13)</sup> This event took all sides unawares, including Thalmor observers, and the war meant many cities and settlements could not commit resources to defend themselves from the dragons.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Jouane-32)</sup> During the Dragonborn's quest to end the dragon menace, they needed to use [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md)'s castle to capture and interrogate one of Alduin's allies. The Dragonborn was forced to engage in the civil war to prevent any assault on Whiterun from disrupting the capture, but it is unknown if they chose to pick a side and fight in the war or arrange a peace council and temporary ceasefire at [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Balgruuf-26)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Vignar-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-SU-34)</sup>

At some point after Ulfric's escape, the Stormcloaks attempted to establish correspondence with [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and request aid in the war effort, but received no response.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ulfric-5)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Galmar-19)</sup>

Both sides sought to increase the legitimacy of their claimant to the throne of Skyrim by obtaining the [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md), an ancient sign of office lost with High King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). It was discovered by both the Legion and Stormcloaks in Korvanjund, causing the two sides to battle in the ruin over possession of the artifact.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Galmar-19)</sup>

Eventually, Jarl Balgruuf was forced to end his neutrality and pick a side in the war after Ulfric issued his ultimatum in the old way; sending him his axe. Balgruuf, after consulting his court, decided to side with the Empire, reluctantly allowing General Tullius to marshall troops in his city.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Balgruuf-26)</sup> Shortly after his decision, enemy forces arrived, initiating the Battle for Whiterun. Getting the drop on the Imperials, the Stormcloaks were able to set up catapults and begin bombarding the city with flaming projectiles. The defending forces utilized water brigades to combat the flames.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Cipius-35)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Balgruuf-26)</sup>

Some Stormcloak officers requested reinforcements, fearing that all their gains in the rebellion would be in vain after receiving word of a growing Imperial force beyond Pale Pass.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SMFN-36)</sup> Ultimately, the outcome of the war is still unknown.

### Aldmeri Dominion Involvement
The leaders of the Legion saw the war as a proxy conflict with the Aldmeri Dominion, who closely watched the situation.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Tullius-21)</sup>

The Thalmor considered it in their best interest to prolong the conflict as much as possible and preserve the status quo,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dossier-6)</sup> despite rumors that they had assisted the Legion in the ambush at [Darkwater Crossing](Lore_Darkwater_Crossing.md) which nearly ended the war.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Prima-8)</sup><sup>:347</sup> The officially stated policy of the Thalmor was one of nonintervention, maintaining the position that they would treat with whatever government came to rule Skyrim.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Elenwen-37)</sup> Indirect aid was permitted, albeit carefully managed, to be given to the rebels.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Dossier-6)</sup> Even so, Stormcloak patrols would engage with Thalmor Justiciars if the two encountered one another,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Encounters-38)</sup> showing that despite the allowed aid, the two factions were not on friendly terms.

## Known Martial Conflicts
- **Massacre at Karthwasten**. A supposed massacre carried out by Stormcloak forces, avouched by the Legion and denied by the Stormcloaks.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rikke-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Galmar-19)</sup>
- **Assault of Fort Neugrad**. An assault on Imperial-controlled Fort Neugrad. The defenders succeeded in repelling the attack but were left dangerously vulnerable.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-IMFN-20)</sup>
- **Skirmish in Whiterun**. A Stormcloak victory over an Imperial scouting party in Whiterun Hold.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Aldis-16)</sup>
- **Ambush at Giants' Gap**. A Legion ambush launched on a Stormcloak force who soon defended themselves with a shield wall. The victor is unknown.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Sovn Soldier-39)</sup>
- **Ambush at Darkwater Crossing**, 15th of Last Seed.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> A Legion ambush enacted upon a Stormcloak company including Ulfric Stormcloak himself near the border. The attack was successful, leading to Ulfric's - and, incidentally, the Last Dragonborn's - capture.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Hadvar-13)</sup>
- **Battle of Stony Creek**. A clash of unknown victor between the opposing factions.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Legends EC-40)</sup>
- **Clash at Korvanjund**. An encounter between the two parties at Korvanjund, each side wishing to obtain the Jagged Crown. The victor is unknown.
- **Battle for Whiterun**. A Stormcloak siege attempting to capture Imperial-aligned Whiterun. The victor is unknown.
- **Battle of the Champions**. An organized engagement between the opposing forces, proposed by the Stormcloaks.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Bo C-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup> A champion from each faction dueled in the fields of Whiterun Hold, backed up by a small squad of allied soldiers.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Bot C-43)</sup> It held negligible militaristic gain, being accepted only for purposes of boosting morale.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-LACN-44)</sup>
