# Wyrd Covens

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg/200px-ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg) Glenmoril Wyress Wyrd Covens (also known as **Wyresses**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WCONHR-1)</sup> **Wyrd Women**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOWTND-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-OLBTM-3)</sup> **Wyrd Sisters**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup> the **Wyrd**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESODhulef-6)</sup> **Ward-Sisters**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WCONHR-1)</sup> **Name-Daughters**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOWTND-2)</sup>, and **nature-witches**<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>) are female-only, animistic<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> [witch](Lore_Witches.md) covens that serve as wardens of [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOWTND-2)</sup> They draw their power from the Earthbones, and revere nature and the elements.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The leader of a coven is referred to as a "beldam".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

The Wyrd originated from the ancient people that dwelled in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) who are referred to by retroactive nomenclature as the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LABHI-12)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LATD-14)</sup> A religious schism brought about the Wyrd's separation from their [druid](Lore_Druid.md) brethren.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup> The Wyrd abandoned civilization in favor of living in the wilds,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup> and believe that they are nature itself: the forest, the plants and beasts within it, and even the weather.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup> In contrast, while ancient druids revered nature, they were not keen to isolate themselves and worship the untamed wilds.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LABHI-12)</sup> The druids accepted what they interpreted as their "proper place" as people, and embraced the idea that civilization can coexist alongside nature.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LABHI-12)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOWM-16)</sup>

Wyrd witches are primarily [Breton](Lore_Breton.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup> though membership also extends to outsiders,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOWG-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOWR-18)</sup> even those from other races.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Once a singular entity, the Wyrd split into subgroups by the time of the [last Druid King](Lore_Kasorayn.md)'s voyage to [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOWM-16)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSDK-20)</sup> These sisterhoods remained behind on the mainland to safeguard the forests of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup> with some of the dozen or so covens even spreading throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WCONHR-1)</sup>

The Wyress covens sometimes face persecution by those who live in urbanized societies.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup> The various Wyrd covens are regarded by outsiders as dangerous and a force to be placated, with contributing factors such as (but not limited to) their reclusiveness, the nature of their moral codes, and the lack of men to replenish their ranks.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WCONHR-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup> In remote regions of High Rock, such as [Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md) and the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), witches and medicine men are held in high regard by the superstitious country-folk because of their impressive magical feats.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-PGE1EHR-22)</sup>

The Wyrd tend to gather around megaliths, and their architecture is similar on a surface level to that of modern Systrean druids in that both dwell in stone huts.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> Wyrd patterns of symbology are distinct from those of druids:<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SCE-24)</sup> different patterns or colors on stones are intended for different purposes.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/ON-npc-Wyress_Gwen.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Wyress_Gwen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Wyress_Gwen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Wyress_Gwen.jpg) Beldama Wyress (ESO) According to a myth, during [Jephre](Lore_Jephre.md)'s Naming (which gave all creatures of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) their shape)<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOWHC-26)</sup> in the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), Jephre entrusted the Earthbones with the task to designate those worthy of becoming Wyrd, who were the wards of nature.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOWTND-2)</sup> In the modern day, beliefs among the various Wyrd Covens vary. The Beldama Coven for example still reveres Jephre and believe they are descendants of the Ehlnofey,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WCONHR-1)</sup> while the various branches of the Glenmoril Wyrd lean more towards [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup>

The Wyrd and the druids have teachings and practices that share common roots,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup> for the history of the druids and the Wyrd is the same up to a certain point.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup> Some sources indicate that the Nedic ancestors of the druids and the wyrd were already following a Druid King figure when the Aldmer arrived to High Rock,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MDBMM-27)</sup> but "druid" as a distinct identity was not introduced until shortly after the Breton race first came into existence.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONLOTB-28)</sup> This was further warranted when the Wyrd diverged from Y'ffre's True Way (what is now known as Druidism)<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>, ultimately driven away by the "excesses of the Elves".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup>

In druidic belief, Bretons<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup> (and others) were not meant to separate themselves from nature, as through it, one can rejuvenate and enjoy Y'ffre's bounty.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup> However, this does not mean they simply retreat into isolation and worship wild, unmarred nature:<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONWD-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LABHI-12)</sup> the Druids of Galen embraced civilization,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOWM-16)</sup> believing it should coexist alongside nature.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup> They found the Wyrd's abandonment of civilization "unsavory",<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOWM-16)</sup> while the Wyrd in turn saw the ancient druids as "barons in ivy robes" who were just as bad as city-dwellers.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOWM-16)</sup> However, modern druids share similarities to the Wyrd in that they practice self-isolation to varying degrees.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DP-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOSSM-32)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png/200px-ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Druid_King_Wyrd_and_Frii.png) Symbols representing the Druid King (top), the Beldama Wyrd (left), the Glenmoril Wyrd (right), and Frii (center) Believing that civilization should live alongside nature and not tame it, eventually the Druids of Galen's tolerance for their Elven overlords would wane, and they would find themselves fighting for the soul of High Rock, a battle they would lose.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESODR-5)</sup> Before the exiled Druids of Galen set forth to the Systres circa [1E 330](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_330), [Druid King Kasorayn](Lore_Kasorayn.md) [foresaw](Lore_Sight.md) a coming disaster to his people in a prophecy now known as the Dream of Kasorayn.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ESODL-33)</sup> Kasorayn visited both the Beldama<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-LDA-34)</sup> and Glenmoril Wyrd Covens in preparation.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ESODL-33)</sup> Together, they nurtured the nature spirit known as Frii from the dream of the Green.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-35)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSDK-20)</sup> Frii was the Sower, the entity that was to create sacred seeds which were to contain the regalia of the Druid King, royal symbols to be passed onto the future druidic circles.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESOFrii-35)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Systres)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md) assaulted both the Beldama and Glenmoril Wyrd, seeking the location of Frii. They abducted elders of both covens, needing them to open the way to the spirit. This led them to the Sunken Road in [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md), where Archdruid Michiel of the Firesong located Frii's sapling. The archdruid was slain, and Frii was taken to the island of Galen to be among the [Stonelore Circle](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONSDK-20)</sup>

The kingdom of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) is known for its discrimination against the Wyrd. They initiated the Purge of the Wyrd-Hags, which saw local Wyresses imprisoned and slain.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36)</sup> Prompted to action by the event known as [Sun's Death](Lore_Sun%27s_Death.md), they believed this slaughter restored [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) to the sky.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-37)</sup> In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), due to a misunderstanding, purges were carried out by Shornhelm's Order of the Crypt against the Beldama Wyrd, their wrath extending beyond their borders.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-COTH-38)</sup>

## Covens
The two major Wyrd Covens include:

- [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md): The all-female Beldama Wyrd live beneath the [Wyrd Tree](Lore_Wyrd_Tree.md) in the dense forests of [Daenia](Lore_Daenia.md), in the least-populated region in High Rock. The Beldama Wyrd believe they are descendants of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), and see themselves as wardens of the forest. They worship [Jephre](Lore_Jephre.md), the God of the Forest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WCONHR-1)</sup>

- [Glenmoril Wyrd](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md): The Glenmoril Wyrd operate out in the wilderness of the [Ilessan Hills](Lore_Ilessan_Hills.md), and in [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md)'s Viridian Woods. They also revere the natural world, though unlike the Beldama, the Glenmoril Wyrd have ties to [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TGW-4)</sup>

Other witch covens of unknown origins exist in High Rock:

- The Daggerfall Witches: The Daggerfall Witches were active in [Shalgora](Lore_Shalgora.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DWC-39)</sup>
- The Daughters of Wroth: The Daughters of Wroth were active in the Wrothgarian Mountains circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DWC-39)</sup>
- The Sisters of The Bluff: The Sisters of The Bluff were active in the [kingdom of Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DWC-39)</sup>
- [Skeffington Coven](Lore_Skeffington_Coven.md):

The [Skeffington Coven](Lore_Skeffington_Coven.md) is a notable group of [witches](Lore_Witches.md) that live in the Barony of [Phrygias](Lore_Phrygias.md), within the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The Skeffington Coven has been active as far as the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), all the way into [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417). The Skeffington operates at **Old Barbyn's Farm**, deep in the wilderness of Phrygias, somewhere near the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md).

- The Tamarilyn Witches: The Tamarilyn Witches were active in the [county of Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DWC-39)</sup>
- The Witches of Alcaire: The Witches of Alcaire were active in [Alcaire](Lore_Alcaire.md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-DWC-39)</sup>
