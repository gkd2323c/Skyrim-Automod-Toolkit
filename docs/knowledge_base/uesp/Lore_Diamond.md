# Diamond

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/MW-item-Diamond.jpg/200px-MW-item-Diamond.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Diamond.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Diamond.jpg) Diamond ore Diamonds are a type of rare, transparent, and hard [crystal](Lore_Crystals.md) that is considered extremely valuable.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDiamond-1)</sup> Their value is determined by their clarity and brilliance, unlike other gems. The difficulty in cutting a sample also plays a factor in its overall value.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BLDiamond-2)</sup> Not only is a diamond valuable, but it is very versatile and can be applied in crafting.

## Uses
For example, diamonds in the armor-making process reinforce the final product by improving its durability and resistance to critical attacks.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONDiamond-3)</sup> Diamond can be an ingredient in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and are commonly sold by fine alchemists. Crushed diamonds mixed with [Bittergreen petals](Lore_Bittergreen_Petals.md) can make a potion of invisibility, an effect that is widely prized.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDiamond-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAF-4)</sup> Invisibility is one of several different effects that diamonds can produce, such as spell resistance, reflect, and detect key.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWAlchemy-5)</sup>

Several ruins throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) used diamond keys to access deeper parts of it. For example, the diamond key in the [Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md), guarded by the spirit of Mogrus the Dim was one of two to open the ruins' inner sanctum.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ARLaby-6)</sup> Other dungeons such as the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ARHalls-7)</sup> the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ARTower-8)</sup> and [Dagoth-Ur](Lore_Dagoth-Ur_(place).md) also use them.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ARDagoth-9)</sup> In a similar sense, the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) ruins of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) use artifacts called Dragon Claws to open circular puzzle doors. They are made out of gems and the Diamond Claw was used in a temple called [Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md). In its palm was the answer to the riddle. It was the [Fox](Lore_Fox.md), the [Moth](Lore_Moth.md), and the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SRClaw-10)</sup> Another example is the [Diamond Paragon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Diamond_Paragon), a large creamy white stone cut like a navette created by the [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) of the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md). It was made to activate a portal egress to another part of the region.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DGParagon-11)</sup>

On the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), the only diamond mine in the district was the Abaelun Mine, located northeast in [Caldera](Lore_Caldera.md) and west of the [Foyada Mamaea](Lore_Foyada_Mamaea.md). Despite that, diamond veins rarely appear throughout the district and are located in far-off caves like the [Abanabi Caves](Lore_Abanabi_Caves.md) on the [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md), Dun-Ahhe in the Dry Camp Valley, and Punammu on [Lake Amaya](Lore_Lake_Amaya_(Vvardenfell).md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDiamond-1)</sup>

In the hills of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), there are plenty of volcanic geodes and jewels, and the ancient [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) mined several gems including diamonds. However, because their metric of worth was the gem's rich color and creamy luster, diamonds were often discarded because of their lack of hue.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-JOY-12)</sup> The [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) noblewoman, Falorah was called the "Jewel of Taneth" because she was as beautiful, cold and hard as a diamond.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONZeira-13)</sup>

There are several notable diamond samples throughout history, such as the Resolute Diamonds, two chunks of azure diamond that serve as keys to the Crystal Tower in northern [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). They only work with specially-attuned [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) called Tower Sentinels, who then undergo a long ritual to link themselves to the diamonds. The charge lasts for eleven years until their successors can re-attune.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SOTCT-14)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Deceit and Secrets, [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) combined the two diamonds and turned it into a single diamond, one that serves as a key to the Crystal Tower but bounds its wielder to reality.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONMephala-15)</sup>
