# Anuad

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-lead-Anu_Crest.png) Anu Crest [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-lead-Padomay_Sigil.png) Padomay Sigil *The first ones were brothers: [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md). They came into the Void, and Time began.*
— *[The Annotated Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)* The Anuad is a popular [elven](Lore_Mer.md) religious text detailing the creation of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) according to [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) faith.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AA-1)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Mono2-2)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup> While many other early elven writings didn't survive the purges of the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md), the Anuad was able to survive into modern times by virtue of its popularity and proliferation.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-3)</sup> A guide published by the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) during the reign of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) described the content of the Anuad as "beautiful heresies".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-3)</sup> Crests of Anu are sometimes printed onto published copies of it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup>

It has been claimed that Anu and Padomay as depicted in the Anuad actually refer to their primary aspects of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md).<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Mono2-2)</sup> The concept of [Nir](Lore_Nir.md), the feminine source of creation who the goddess [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) is sometimes associated with, is said to originate from the Anuad.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AA-1)</sup> The unabridged version of the Anuad makes reference to "brilliant students" of the titanic [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), who some have speculated are the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-6)</sup> It is also known to make reference to a [spell](Lore_Spells.md) regarding "balance and chaos".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MR-4)</sup>

## Annotated Anuad
There exists a widely published simplified version of the Anuad, interchangeably known as "[The Annotated Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)", "The Anuad Paraphrased", or "A Children's Anuad".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AA-1)</sup> Due to how easy the text is to understand, it was one of the books used in an Imperial literacy campaign on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), distributed with the aid of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) and the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The author Alessia Otus, a staunch believer of the [Imperial faith](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), described the book disparagingly as "a religious book appropriate for those ignorant of the mysteries of the faith, and adapted to the meanest understanding."<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GTL-8)</sup>
