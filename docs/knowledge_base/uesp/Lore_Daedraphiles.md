# Daedraphiles

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/LG-cardart-Umaril_the_Unfeathered_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Umaril_the_Unfeathered_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Umaril_the_Unfeathered_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Umaril_the_Unfeathered_02.jpg) Umaril the Unfeathered, Sorcerer-King of the Ayleids and champion of Meridia The Daedraphiles (also referred to as **Ayleid Daedric Kingdoms**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gandrinar-1)</sup> were a faction of [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) who worshipped the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). They can be contrasted with the older, [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) -worshipping culture of the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md). Unlike their more conservative cousins, the Ayleid clans that adopted Daedra worship were noted for their decadent and vigorous societies.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup>

The Daedraphiles followed the *Eleven Edicts of the Ten Ancestors*, and believed that the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md) venerated by the Ayleids were in fact Daedra worshippers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/OB-misc-Hidden_Lake%2C_Meridia%27s_Shrine.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Hidden_Lake%2C_Meridia%27s_Shrine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Hidden_Lake,_Meridia%27s_Shrine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Hidden_Lake,_Meridia%27s_Shrine.jpg) A statue of Meridia within the ruins of [Lost Abagarlas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lost_Abagarlas) Daedra worship can be traced back to the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), who were the first to begin organized worship of both the Aedra and Daedra. However, the strictures of Aldmeri regulation forbade the worship of Daedra. The Ayleids departed Summerset for the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), in some cases specifically to evade such strictures. In the last millennium of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), as Ayleid culture flourished on the continent, worship of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) took hold. Although the Aedra were still widely revered, Daedric worship began to spread among the Ayleids, initially tolerated and later celebrated.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWTA-4)</sup> Worship of the Daedra did not necessarily entail a complete abandonment of Aedric practices. Some Daedraphiles instead elevated their patron Daedra above the Divines while continuing to observe certain traditional Aedric rites.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWOD-5)</sup>

Unlike the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), the Ayleids made no distinction between [good](Lore_Good_Daedra.md) and [bad](Lore_Bad_Daedra.md) Daedra. Indeed, many of the cruellest Princes received mass veneration when their worship was adopted by Ayleid kings and aristocrats. Some scholars believe that the nature of these Daedric cults influenced the practice of systematic [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md) of [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) within the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWTA-4)</sup> Although also practiced by the Barsaebics, Daedra-inspired "arts" such as [flesh-sculpture](Lore_Flesh_Sculpture.md) and gut-gardening were inflicted on these Nedic slaves to honor the Princes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWTA-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Captured Time-6)</sup>

Daedraphile dominance of Ayleid culture was the result of the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md), a civil conflict that spanned the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era) to [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198) between the Aedra-worshipping clans and the Daedraphile clans. The conflict was ended in 1E 198, when King [Glinferen](Lore_Glinferen.md) of [Atatar](Lore_Atatar.md) led a combined force of Daedraphile warriors against the traditionalist Barsaebic forces of [Wendelbek](Lore_Wendelbek.md), driving the Aedra worshippers from the Heartlands and into [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). The Scouring of Wendelbek marked the end of any organized resistance to Daedra worship in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup>

The forgotten Daedric Prince [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md) was among those worshipped by the Daedraphiles. She was venerated primarily by the Ayleids of the [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), including the city-states of [Miscarcand](Lore_Miscarcand.md), [Elenglynn](Lore_Elenglynn.md), and [Niryastare](Lore_Niryastare.md). Unlike the other Princes, Ithelia manifested on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) to rule over her Ayleid followers personally. She made use of their [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) magics, and stored a significant portion of her divine power within a [well](Lore_Ayleid_Wells.md) at Miscarcand.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> When Ithelia's rival Prince [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) imprisoned her and wiped all memory of her from existence, she was forgotten by her followers.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Despite this, archaeological evidence of this forgotten Ayleid deity can still be found within the Ayleid ruins of the West Weald.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Gandrinar-1)</sup>

Widespread worship of the Daedric Princes is one of several factors attributed to the downfall of the Ayleid Empire, culminating with the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) and the fall of [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup> The rebellion itself was fueled largely by desperate rage against the Ayleids' Daedra-inspired cruelty, and the Ayleid kings who aligned themselves with the slaves were Aedra worshippers.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWTA-4)</sup> Circa [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242), King Glinferen sent an envoy to [Twyllbek](Lore_Twyllbek.md) seeking the aid of the exiled Barsaebics, who bluntly refused to help.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The [gods](Lore_Gods.md) themselves appear to have turned against the Ayleids, with [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) answering [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s prayers by sending her son [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) to aid her,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TSOP2-13)</sup> and the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) granting [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) a set of powerful [relics](Lore_Crusader%27s_Relics.md) to fight back against the Ayleid Sorcerer-Kings.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBProphet-14)</sup> Alessia herself was granted the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), and entered into a Covenant with the Dragon God of Time to keep the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) lit as a barrier against Daedric invasion.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

Following the fall of White-Gold Tower, most Ayleid clans who weren't slaughtered eventually ended up fleeing Cyrodiil, becoming what would later be known as the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ASi V-2)</sup> The Aedra-worshipping Ayleid kings were rewarded by Queen Alessia, who incorporated some of the Elven Aedra into the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md)'s worship of the Eight Divines. The Empire outlawed the worship of the Daedric Princes, and Daedra-worshipping Ayleids were exterminated wherever they were found. Thus, by the middle of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), large-scale Daedra worship was extinct in central Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWTA-4)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

## Legacy
The revulsion for Daedra worship that pervades most [human](Lore_Men.md) cultures in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) probably originated from the treatment of Nedic slaves by the Daedraphiles in the Late Merethic.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWTA-4)</sup>

The [Hollow City](Lore_Hollow_City.md) of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) is a lasting relic of the Daedraphiles. This city was populated by both [men](Lore_Men.md) and Ayleids, and its denizens worshipped the Daedric Prince [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) at a glorious cathedral known as the Chapel of Light. At some point, the city was besieged by the forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), and it became clear that the city would fall. Seeing an opportunity to preserve the city and fight back against her rival Prince, Meridia used the invaders' portals to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) to pull the city into Molag Bal's own realm, where it remains to this day as a refuge for mortals within an otherwise hostile realm.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TG-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

It is theorized that a sect or cult of [Nibeneans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenean) attempted to emulate Daedraphile worship of Daedric powers in the First Era when they constructed [Deepscorn Hollow](Lore_Deepscorn_Hollow.md) as a temple to [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

Following his defeat at the hands of Pelinal Whitestrake, the Ayleid Sorcerer-King [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril.md) survived with Meridia's aid. The Prince granted Umaril's [soul](Lore_Soul.md) refuge in the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md), where he would remain for millennia. Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Umaril's body was reformed in the Fires of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and he returned to Tamriel to reclaim his seat at [Garlas Malatar](Lore_Garlas_Malatar.md) and cast down the mortal gods who opposed him. He began a systematic campaign against [the Church](Lore_The_Church.md), sending his [Auroran](Lore_Auroran.md) minions to desecrate Cyrodiil's great chapels in an attempt to end the worship of the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OBProphet-14)</sup> He was ultimately defeated by the [Knights of the Nine](Lore_Knights_of_the_Nine.md), led by the [Divine Crusader](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), at a final confrontation within Garlas Malatar. Wielding the ancient relics of Pelinal together with the Blessing of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), the Crusader defeated Umaril's physical form and destroyed his immortal soul.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>
