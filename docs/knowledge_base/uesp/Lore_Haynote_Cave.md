[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/OB-place-Haynote_Cave.jpg/200px-OB-place-Haynote_Cave.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Haynote_Cave.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Haynote_Cave.jpg) Haynote Cave circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Haynote Cave is a natural cave network found in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), north of [Ceyatatar](Lore_Ceyatatar.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup> The lower levels of the complex are known as the Basin Galleries. The lowest tunnels are referred to as the Winding Sump and are partially flooded.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup>

## History
Like all natural caves, these tunnels once served as the capillaries of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), pulsing with the divine essence of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOLoad-3)</sup>

The winding tunnels of Haynote Cave were once home to a thriving fungus-farming operation in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Farming activities ended with the outbreak of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

During the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Haynote Cave was occupied by a Circle of the [Shadowed Path](Lore_Shadowed_Path.md), a cult of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) dedicated to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). This Circle, formerly led by Theurgist Thelas, was given to Diabolist Volcatia by Dreadlord Naucratius and tasked with excavating the cave in search of an ancient relic buried beneath the ground. The cult planned to use this relic to conquer the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and all of Cyrodiil in the name of Molag Bal.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Volgo-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The cultists filled the cave with their [undead](Lore_Undead.md) minions, although ultimately both Volcatia and Thelas were killed by passing soldiers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the cave was unoccupied and served as a den for hostile local wildlife.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OB-1)</sup>
