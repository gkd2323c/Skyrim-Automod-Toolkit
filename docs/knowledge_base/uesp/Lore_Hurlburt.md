Hurlburt was the King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), reigning from [2E 522](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_522) until his death in [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546), and the twenty-first Count of [House Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOLoading-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-2)</sup> Considered the greatest post- [Reman](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) monarch of Shornhelm, Hurlburt led his kingdom's army at the Battle of Granden Tor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-2)</sup> In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541) or [2E 542](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_542), [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) and his horde of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) invaded [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and plundered much of the province before finally being defeated in a battle outside [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md). A fortnight after the defeat of Durcorach and his horde, Hurlburt and the kings of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), realizing how weak their realms were individually, signed the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), a pact promising to defend each other's kingdoms and unite as one against all foreign foes.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GTTDC-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-IEGt THR-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TOAM3-5)</sup>

He wed Countess [Iphilia](Lore_Iphilia_Montclair.md) of [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md), with whom he fathered a son named [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md) in [2E 532](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_532). He also fathered an illegitimate elder son, [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md), with a [House Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) cousin. In [2E 544](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_544), his queen, Iphilia, died, making twelve-year-old Phylgeon the thirty-eighth Baron of House Montclair.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-2)</sup>

In 2E 546, Hurlburt died, instigating a succession dispute between Phylgeon and Ranser over the throne of Shornhelm, and Ranser narrowly prevailed by citing a long-lost [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) decree. After Ranser's murder at the Battle of Traitor's Tor in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), a new monarch was not selected, and instead [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) was ruled by the triumvirate Council of the North.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-2)</sup> Hurlburt's grandson Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md) attempted to claim the crown of Shornhelm, stating that as the son of Phylgeon, he was the only legitimate descendant of King Hurlburt.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SCCOTN-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOLoading-1)</sup>
