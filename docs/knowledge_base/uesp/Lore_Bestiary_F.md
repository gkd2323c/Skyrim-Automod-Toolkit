## [Factotum](Lore_Factotum.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/ON-npc-Factotum_Sanitizer.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Factotum_Sanitizer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Factotum_Sanitizer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Factotum_Sanitizer.jpg) A Factotum [Factotums](Lore_Factotum.md) are mechanical [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) created by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and said to serve as an extension of his divine will. An invention of need, they were created to maintain his great creation, the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). As such, they have become an integral part of daily life for the inhabitants of the city, and are vital to running its everyday processes.

Believed to be a fusion of [brass](Lore_Brass.md) and [soul](Lore_Souls.md), factotums have a shared appearance, voice, and sense of purpose. They hold a structured intelligence, often centered on their singular function. Their dialogue and actions are all based on this function, with only the commonality of a devotion to the Clockwork God. Attempting to deviate from this purpose is simply incomprehensible to them. The Factotum's very nature and materials informed the creation of the mechanical enhancements granted to [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md), resulting in a synthesis of flesh and engineering.

Beloved by Sotha Sil, tinkering with or experimenting on factotums is considered a minor blasphemy and forbidden. As an extension of his divine will, Sotha Sil considers an attack on his factotums as an attack on him, and those who breach this law face severe sanction.

Similar to factotums are the constructs known as [fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md), but they differ in that they are part organic, part inorganic, constructed from an amalgam of grown flesh and metallic grafts and are more bestial in appearance.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Factotum), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Factotum)

## [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/SR-creature-Falmer.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Falmer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Falmer.jpg) A falmer The [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md), also known as the **Undermer**, **Snow Ghosts**, **bogles**, **riekr-kin**, **fish-people**, and **clatter-coats**, are blind, corrupted descendants of the ancient [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) that live deep beneath the surface of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They are known to stalk and attack intruders in various underground areas, primarily [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruins. Adventurers can often quickly tell when the Falmer inhabit a place thanks to their odor, which is said to be repulsive. They have been known to come up to the surface to attack the unwary. They are roughly man-sized, but hunched over and "ugly", resembling creatures of [goblin-kind](Lore_Goblin-ken.md) more than their own [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) ancestors. They have no noses, only bat-like long slits for nostrils, attributed in some Nordic folklore to [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) cutting their noses off to "tell them apart from other Elves". Their ears are distinctly pointed, their skin pallid, and their eyes lifeless and black.

Much as "High Elf" and "Wood Elf" are interchangeable terms for "[Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md)" and "[Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md)" respectively, "Snow Elf" is really just another term for "Falmer". The Snow Elves were a proud and prosperous race of [mer](Lore_Mer.md) who occupied a portion of modern-day Skyrim until they were displaced by the invading [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). Many of the ancient Snow Elves were betrayed in a deal for asylum they struck with the Dwemer. The Dwemer forced their wards to subsist only on toxic fungi which twisted them into sightless [slaves](Lore_Slavery.md). It's the descendants of these slaves whom many of the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) have come to know as the Falmer, while their grand ancestors are remembered as Snow Elves.

Few know that an unknown number of Snow Elves rejected the Dwemer's deal and went into hiding, or that some of them survived, uncorrupted, into modern times. [Knight-Paladin Gelebor](Lore_Gelebor.md) of the Chantry of Auri-El, the only known Snow Elf still alive today, embraced the distinction between "Falmer" and "Snow Elf" due to the negative connotations "Falmer" had taken on among travelers over the millennia, but he personally knew his fallen brethren as **the Betrayed**. Falmer are cited in [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) legends of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) as "servants of [the Adversary](Lore_The_Adversary.md)" and "evil beings".

## [Faun](Lore_Faun.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/ON-misc-Glemyos_Wildhorn.png/200px-ON-misc-Glemyos_Wildhorn.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Glemyos_Wildhorn.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Glemyos_Wildhorn.png) A faun [Fauns](Lore_Faun.md) (sometimes called **deerfolk**) are [deerlike](Lore_Deer.md) trickster [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md) native to the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md), where they ecologically fill a similar role that [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) do for most of mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Rumors exist of creatures of nature either [Man](Lore_Man.md) or [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) in appearance being the first inhabitants of the archipelago. Female Fauns seem to be either rare or nonexistent, leading to speculation that they reproduce via magical means or non-traditional methods, or that the females are simply kept well-hidden.

## [Fellrunner](Lore_Fellrunner.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-creature-Fellrunner.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Fellrunner.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Fellrunner.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Fellrunner.jpg) A fellrunner [Fellrunners](Lore_Fellrunner.md) are flightless [birds](Lore_Bird.md) found primarily in desert regions such as [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md) and [Northern Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). They can also be found in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) and parts of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Fellrunners are harmless, and as their name implies, will run at the sight of anything suspicious; this reflex is often used against them by hunters, due to the fact they will run too fast and trip, breaking their thin bones. They are incapable of flying and swimming due to their tiny wings.

## Fennec Fox
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/ON-creature-Fennec_Fox.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Fennec_Fox.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Fennec_Fox.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Fennec_Fox.jpg) A fennec fox Fennec foxes are a breed of [fox](Lore_Fox.md) commonly found in deserts. They can be difficult to hunt due to their large ears, which grant them an excellent sense of hearing. They like eating [scorpions](Lore_Scorpion.md). A paste made of garlic and onions can be used to poison fennec foxes. They are more tender and less gamey than [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAIT-5)</sup> The dusky fennec fox (also known as the "midnight fennec fox") is native to the savannahs of Northern [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). The arctic fennec fox can be found in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) along the shores of the Sea of Ghosts. Fennec foxes have been domesticated by the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), and make alert companions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Pet-6)</sup>

While typically smaller than other foxes, some species are large enough to be used as mounts. The [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) of the second Warrior Wave were known to use them as war mounts.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

## [Fetcherfly](Lore_Fetcherfly.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/LG-cardart-Fetcherfly_Swarm.png/200px-LG-cardart-Fetcherfly_Swarm.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Fetcherfly_Swarm.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Fetcherfly_Swarm.png) A swarm of fetcherflies [Fetcherflies](Lore_Fetcherfly.md) are insects found on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and in the [Tenmar Forest](Lore_Tenmar_Forest.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). Lone fetcherflies are harmless. However, when something approaches their nests, they will swarm and attack whatever they perceive as a threat. They emit a moderate amount of heat, singeing fur or hair on contact, and due to this heat, their nests often glow and have pores emitting smoke. When the creatures swarm, their queen can tap her magma source to transform the nest into a hive [golem](Lore_Golem.md), which can walk around independently. A hive golem's purpose is to relocate the hive to new surroundings. These golems are attracted to the destruction of regular hives, and will seek out those responsible.

## [Fiendroth](Lore_Fiendroth.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cf/ON-pet-Fiendroth.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Fiendroth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Fiendroth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Fiendroth.jpg) A fiendroth [Fiendroths](Lore_Fiendroth.md) are small blue [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) pests native to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that can commonly be found wherever Daedric influence persists. They resemble hulking, armored insects with very short antennae. Fiendroths possess steel mandibles and a venomous stinger, and purportedly rely upon the agonized cries of mortals for sustenance. They appear to be scavengers as they may feed on things such as [daedrat](Lore_Daedrat.md) remains and Deadlands [scorpions](Lore_Scorpion.md). Fiendroths are known to form silken cocoons, which can be used in rituals to draw hives of fiendroths to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).

Fiendroths, like [daedrats](Lore_Daedrat.md), are considered a form of common Daedric vermin that appear to exist in all corners of Oblivion, scuttling about and scavenging in much the same way that [rats](Lore_Rat.md) and [insects](Lore_Insect.md) plague [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). Some of the realms they can be found in include: [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), and [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md). They often can be found on Nirn in places with Daedric influence. They can often be found around [Daedric Ruins](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). Many appeared in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). They have occasionally been kept by mortals as pets, with some even pitting them against each other in races. In a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) tale about a kindly [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md), she is tricked by a bystander who was yelling about being accosted by a pack of Fiendroths, only to end up mocked mercilessly by the person they ran to assist.

- See also: [Daedmite](Lore_Daedmite.md)

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Fiendroth)

## [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/22/BS-creature-Fire_Daedra_02.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Fire_Daedra_02.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-creature-Fire_Daedra_02.gif) A fire daedra [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md) are a variety of horned humanoid [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md), considered among the most dangerous creatures in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). They are intelligent and possess some spell casting abilities, they can only be harmed by [mithril](Lore_Mithril.md) or better weapons and are greatly resistant to fire-based spells. They are described as hot-blooded, fast, tricky thinkers. They are known to be impulsive, and smart enough to fall for subtle scams. They are speakers of both the Daedric [language](Lore_Linguistics.md) and [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md), as such they are sometimes capable of being negotiated with.

They apparently have an enmity for [Frost Daedra](Lore_Frost_Daedra.md), though they are able to put this aside when serving mutual masters. Fire Daedra are considered to take things less seriously than Frost Daedra, who are said to not "understand the pleasures of the dance". They seem to be most commonly used by their masters, the [Daedra Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), as agents of destruction in its least subtle form. Many Fire Daedra were known to serve as retainers of [Faydra Shardai](Lore_Faydra_Shardai.md)'s [clan](Lore_Shardai_Clan.md), in service to [Clan Dagon](Lore_Clan_Dagon.md).

## [Fire Daemon](Lore_Fire_Daemon.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/AR-creature-Fire_Daemon.gif/200px-AR-creature-Fire_Daemon.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Fire_Daemon.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Fire_Daemon.gif) A fire daemon [Fire Daemons](Lore_Fire_Daemon.md) are [creatures](Lore_Lesser_Daedra.md) from the [netherworld](Lore_Oblivion.md), who often gate themselves into [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) on some errand of their choosing. They are dangerous foes, highly intelligent, completely chaotic in nature, and powerful in the ways of [magic](Lore_Magic.md). They are resistant to fire and possess the ability to see invisible entities.

Fire Daemons could be found throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), possibly due to [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) summoning them for use in his legions. [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) was said to have assisted Jagar Tharn's coup by giving him access to Daedric troops and weapons. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) was known to use Fire Daemons to defend their guild halls during night hours, also sending them out on missions in the guild's interest, such as retrieving items of importance or killing rival faction members. One was also among the specimens in the Bestiary of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), guarding the staircase to the top floor.

## Firepot Spider

## [Frostbite Spider](Lore_Frostbite_Spider.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/SR-creature-Frostbite_Spider.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Frostbite_Spider.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Frostbite_Spider.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Frostbite_Spider.jpg) A frostbite spider [Frostbite Spiders](Lore_Frostbite_Spider.md) are giant [spiders](Lore_Spider.md) native to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and its surrounding islands, such as [Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md) and [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). As their name implies, they are largely immune to the cold. Frostbite spiders most often make their lairs in caves, covering their nests in large webs, and preying upon creatures who venture into their caverns seeking shelter. When food is especially scarce, they venture out to hunt for prey in the wilderness. They spit poisonous frostbite venom at their prey from a distance, and leap at them to attack with pincers if they get too close. These spiders usually take on a brown and grey coloration, although they can also be blue.

Frostbite spiders have some level of intelligence, as such, they are capable of being taught to obey certain orders. The cave-dwelling [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) are known to tame them in addition to [Chaurus](Lore_Chaurus.md). Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) member of the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) named Hlokela had taken to breeding and training frostbite spiders as hunting companions. However whenever she tried to leave [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), someone would kill the spiders accompanying her, mistaking them for pests. Eventually she moved out of the city and began giving out her trained spiders to adventurers as pets. Gabriella, a member of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md), kept a frostbite spider as a [familiar](Lore_Familiar.md).
