Sometime in the late first era, Irthvyd was a young jarl who rose to prominence among the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) during the reign of High Queen [Roleke](Lore_Roleke.md). It is unknown which hold Irthvyd hailed from. His stoic reserve and calm presence earned him the epithet of "the Impassive." When the people of Solitude rose up against Roleke, Irthvyd and his warriors heard of the rising disquiet and headed toward the city. According to legends and oral tradition, when he reached Solitude's city gate, [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) herself appeared to him and stated that Roleke had died at the hands of her progeny only hours before his arrival. All Roleke's children died during the siege, by her hand, leaving Solitude with no High King or blood heirs. Irthvyd then rode into the city to help the citizens of Solitude restore order during the resulting confusion. Hours later, he was crowned High King of Solitude, with the blessings of Kyne.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOS-1)</sup>

Irthvyd's steady rule brought peace to the holds after years of Roleke's bloody reign. Irthvyd ruled for decades, but he never took a spouse. While the lack of heirs is not an inordinate problem among Nord royalty, it did raise questions. Irthvyd refused both a spouse and the possibility of heirs. The title of high king eventually passed to Irthvyd's most senior advisor, [Temylda](Lore_Temylda.md), who battled several jarls in single combat and won.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOS-1)</sup>

The story of Irthvyd's rise to power and reign became a part of Nord folklore and oral tradition, and it was later compiled by [Erisa Sylbenitte](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Erisa_Sylbenitte) of the [Coterie of Organized Scholars](Lore_Coterie_of_Organized_Scholars.md) at some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SOS-1)</sup>
