[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/BS-Level5_Cottage.png/200px-BS-Level5_Cottage.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-Level5_Cottage.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-Level5_Cottage.png) Old Chimere's cottage in the Chimera of Desolation [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg/200px-BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Point_C_02.jpg) The ghost town of Trybador still remains The Chimera of Desolation is a small realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup>

The Chimera was once **Caecilly Island**, a small island off the coast of [Northmoor](Lore_Northmoor.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMKR-2)</sup> (or [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md), according to some sources)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HE-3)</sup> in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup> The entire island was cast into the void during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) as an act of revenge against [Chimere Graegyn](Lore_Chimere_Graegyn.md), a mortal conjurer living there. The abandoned towns of Trybador and Granvellusa still stand on the desolate island.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMKR-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SHN-4)</sup>

The plane was once accessible via the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), but this link was severed following Dagon's invasion of the edifice during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDOO-5)</sup> Upon Dagon's banishment during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), Caecilly was returned back to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GT-6)</sup>

## Geography
The Chimera of Desolation can be roughly divided into three parts; in the southeast sits the abandoned town of **Trybador**, the **Chapel of the Innocent Quarry**, and a fortress to the north. The northern section of the island is called **Hartmoor**, and is the home of the Chimera's only permanent resident. Off the northeast coast is the walled town of **Granvellusa**, which sits atop the eastern island called **Allanscrag Mountain**, which is surrounded on all sides by sheer cliffs. At the main island's center lies **Carnac Island**, which is connected to Caecilly by a tombolo sandbar.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CJ-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## History

### The Chimera of Desolation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/BS-level5-Point_C.jpg/200px-BS-level5-Point_C.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Point_C.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Point_C.jpg) Trybador signage [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/BS-npc-Old_Man_Chimere_02.png/200px-BS-npc-Old_Man_Chimere_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-npc-Old_Man_Chimere_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-npc-Old_Man_Chimere_02.png) Old Man Chimere While the exact date of the Chimera of Desolation's creation is unclear, signposts seen in Trybador say it was established in the year 2112;<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSSign-9)</sup> whether this refers to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) ([1E 2112](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2112)) or if it is the product of some other dating system remains unclear. It appears it was created at some point prior to [3E 172](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_172)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMKR-2)</sup> when a [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) clan retainer named [Chimere Graegyn](Lore_Chimere_Graegyn.md) made a pact with Mehrunes Dagon, wishing to live forever in his home town among the happy voices of his friends and countrymen. Chimere schemed against Dagon, and won—casting him into Oblivion. However, Dagon had time enough to retaliate, and twisted the literal words of Chimere's pact; the Daedric Prince hurled Caecilly Island into the void, instantly killing the other residents of the island, but allowed the sounds of their voices to remain to torment Chimere for eternity. The summoner was granted eternal life, but cursed to grow progressively old and crippled with arthritis.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMKR-2)</sup>

The Chimera played a role in the invasion of the Battlespire. Dagon's forces were able to enter [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), one of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)'s realms, via a portal. An [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md#The_Apprentice) from the Battlespire retraced this path on the way to a confrontation with the Prince of Destruction.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup>

### The Ritual of the Innocent Quarry
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/BS-level5-Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry_Interior.jpg/200px-BS-level5-Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry_Interior.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry_Interior.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BS-level5-Chapel_of_the_Innocent_Quarry_Interior.jpg) Inside the Chapel of the Innocent Quarry Eventually, the Chimera of Desolation became the setting for the [Ritual of the Innocent Quarry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ritual_of_the_Innocent_Quarry), also called the Wild Hunt<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> or Great Hunt,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HE-3)</sup> an ancient Daedric rite that drew energy from the powerful magicka stream engulfing the plane. The creators and times of the rituals are lost to history, but when followed properly the rite brought great power and prestige to an individual dubbed the "Huntsman". The ritual pit this all-powerful Huntsman and their Greater and Lesser Dogs (all [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md)) against a mortal known as the Innocent Quarry, or simply, the Hare. In short, the Huntsman's aim was to corner and kill the Hare, while philosophically reflecting on the nature of their places in the Hunt.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TPOTH-10)</sup> Many Daedra considered being the Huntsman a great honor.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BSLTFD-11)</sup>

The Ritual began at the Chapel of the Innocent Quarry, a large green crystal edifice, and the only structure erected by the Daedra on the island.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TMKR-2)</sup> The Lesser Dogs assembled outside, while inside the Huntsmen, the Greater Dogs, and Si'ess, the Master of the Hunt, performed rites to initiate and sanctify the Huntsmen, the Hunt itself, and the Innocent Quarry. Afterward, the Huntsmen emerged, displayed the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md), and recited the Offices of the Hunt. The Offices explained the four stages of the Hunt: the Drag, the Chase, the Call, and the View to the Kill. In the Drag, the Lesser Dogs flushed the Hare out into the open. The Chase was conducted by the Greater Hounds, who pursued the Hare. They then cornered the Hare during the Call, and summoned the Huntsman for the kill. Finally in the View, a Huntsman killed the Hare with the ritual Spear of Bitter Mercy, then called upon the Master of the Hunt to view the kill by ringing the town bell of Trybador. After the Hunt was concluded, the Master of the Hunt then bestowed the Bounty upon the Huntsman Bold (the one who made the successful kill). The Huntsman Bold was then allowed to name the next Hare for the next Hunt (though was forbidden from participation in that Hunt).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TPOTH-10)</sup>

The Huntsmen, Master, and Hounds are solemnly sworn to honor the Offices, or Law, of the Hunt. The Law strictly defined all details of the Hunt, such as how many Hounds of each sort could participate, how the Spear of Bitter Mercy could be wielded, and so on. Of particular importance, the Law stated that the Hare must have a genuine chance to escape, no matter how slim. This generally took the form of six keys, which if collected would allow the Hare to open a portal in the Temple of Daedric Rites and escape the Chimera of Desolation. The Daedra considered escape to be effectively impossible, but considered a failure to provide the opportunity to be a "shameful and unforgivable betrayal of the Law of the Hunt".

Additionally, the Ritual of the Hunt granted the Huntsmen protection from all forms of attack, including virtually all weapons and magical effects. The only exception was a Huntsman's own Spear; a single touch of the Spear of Bitter Mercy meant instant and certain death, whether the victim was Hare or Huntsman. It was believed that only the most powerful Daedra were immune to the deadly effects of the Spear.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TPOTH-10)</sup>

### Invasion of the Battlespire
In [3E 398](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_398), Mehrunes Dagon's forces attacked and seized control of the Battlespire with the blessing of Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), who was [impersonating](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BSPaxti-12)</sup> With the portal back to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) blocked, an [apprentice](Lore_Hero.md#The_Apprentice) trapped within the Battlespire made it to the Chimera and became the Hare in a Wild Hunt instigated by Dagon's [Herne](Lore_Herne_(creature).md) minions. However, the apprentice was aided by both Old Chimere and [Clan Dremora](Lore_Clan_Dremora.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BSLFTD-13)</sup>

One of the Huntsmen was a Herne named Egahirn; it was his first Hunt, and he was given the honor of making the Kill. However, in order to assure his victory, he kept one of the six gatekeys needed for the Hare to escape on his person. This was a direct violation of the Law of the Hunt, and it did not sit well with many of the other participants (as Egahirn was protected against all weapons and magics).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BSLFB-14)</sup> It also presented an opportunity; when he tricked Mehrunes Dagon, Chimere wore the Armor of the [Saviour's Hide](Lore_Saviour%27s_Hide.md), which protected the wearer from attacks by oathbreakers—which Egahirn was by his actions.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BSChimere-15)</sup> The apprentice retrieved the Spear of Bitter Mercy, wore the Saviour's Hide, and was able to slay Egahirn and escape the realm.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Battlespire-1)</sup>

When Dagon manifested in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) during the Oblivion Crisis, he was defeated by the Avatar of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and banished back to Oblivion, sealing the vale between the realms and transporting Caecilly back to its original location off the coast of Northmoor.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OBLight-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GT-6)</sup>
