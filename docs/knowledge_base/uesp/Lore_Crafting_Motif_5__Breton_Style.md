[Crafting Motifs](Lore_Crafting_Motifs.md) 5: The Bretons **by [Doctor Alfidia Lupus](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Doctor_Alfidia_Lupus)** *Notes on materials and motifs used in Breton armor* *
---
(Dr. Lupus was Imperial Ethnographer for Potentate [Savirien-Chorak](Lore_Savirien-Chorak.md) from [2E 418](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_418) to 431)

The Arch-Magister at the [University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), Lady Opel Dantaine, is a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md), so I consulted with her on Breton motifs. She was friendly, and very helpful.

The Bretons were the last major group of humans on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to free themselves from their [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) overlords, and in many ways their long vassalage to the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) defines their culture. They are fiercely autonomous, each kingdom in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) jealous of its individual sovereignty, but Breton society retains a feudal structure that hearkens back to the rank-obsessed Direnni Hegemony. The Bretons are nearly as fractious as their cousins the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), but their long tutelage under the Elves makes them open to the [magical](Lore_Magic.md) arts, rather than suspicious of them.

How is this reflected in their arts and crafts? Let's look at Breton armor, for example. The gleaming heavy armor of a Breton knight is as tough and practical as that of a Nord housecarl, but its pleasing form exhibits a subtle sophistication that is reminiscent of Elven elegance. One sees the same influence in Breton weaponry, which is beautiful yet undeniably deadly.

It made me think of the differences between [Divayth](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md)'s Elven urbanity and [Morian](Lore_Morian_Zenas.md)'s breadth of knowledge and all-too-human inconsistencies, even peevishness. Apparently the transliminal experiments have not been going well. When I stopped by the townhouse last night, neither Morian nor Divayth were in— [Seif-ij](Lore_Seif-ij_Hidja.md), Morian's apprentice, told me they'd quarreled over the appropriate price to pay a transporting entity to ensure safe return from a jaunt to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), the remarks became personal, and then my name was apparently brought up. There was shouting, and they both huffed their way out of the laboratory and marched off down Divines Street in opposite directions.

This is terrible. Fighting? Over me? I must confess I was so disturbed I blurted out the whole thing to Lady Opel, who was incredibly kind and solicitous. She asked me if I had feelings for either of the two wizards, and I admitted I did, but they were conflicting and confusing. Opel opened a bottle or two of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) spiced wine, and we got quite confidential with each other as the evening waned. I'm not sure how I got home, and today my head hurts, but it was worth it, as my heart is no longer so heavy.
