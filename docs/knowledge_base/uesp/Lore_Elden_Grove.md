*From the Tree, all things*
— [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) prophecy<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBot GT-1)</sup> The Elden Grove (also spelled **Eldengrove** or **Eldenwood**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHT-2)</sup> is a legendary forest known among the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) as the birthplace of the sacred First Tree. This First Tree then gave life to all the forests of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Its exact location has been a closely guarded secret,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARRia-3)</sup> but it is known to be somewhere in western [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). It was one of the places where [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) hid a piece of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arena-4)</sup> The grove has been described as "misty" and "labyrinthian".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AHT-2)</sup> It is alternatively known as the **Grove of the One Great Tree** and the **Grove of the Sages**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBot GT-1)</sup>

The [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_The_Towers.md#Green-Sap) in **[Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md)** is implied to be the home of the sacred First Tree, as it contains the physical embodiment of the soul of Valenwood.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Heart-5)</sup> The Ayleids at [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) built their city of Elden Hollow in and around what is called the Elden Tree or the First Tree, believed by many to be the tree that seeded all of Valenwood.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBot GT-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ACOV-6)</sup> The Tree's branches and roots are as hands, reaching at once into the Mundus and Overworld.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBot GT-1)</sup> The city of [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) occupies the boughs and bole of a great walking Elden Tree, possibly the original Elden Tree from which all other [graht-oaks](Lore_Graht-oak.md) are derived. The magic involved in the creation of the tree and the city were from before the dawn of recorded history.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOHOI-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-8)</sup>

## History
After splitting it into eight pieces, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) hid a piece of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md) within the Elden Grove. The [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) would later journey here during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) to find this piece. After acquiring a jewel containing the Heart of Selene from the depths of the [Selene's Web](Lore_Selene%27s_Web.md), Queen Ulandra provided them the location of the Grove. The small overgrown entrance to the Elden Grove still stood, guarding secrets forgotten for ages. It had become a hazy forest full of monsters including medusas and wraiths, and the impenetrable haziness forced them to feel their way around. It soon became apparent that the Staff piece was not there, but rather within its misty corridors underneath the Grove. Beneath it was an underground city, mostly reduced to a flooded hole, requiring the use of boats to move around in the sunken ruins. The third piece of the staff was recovered within a trench deep in the ruins, and the Champion managed to safely leave the area.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Arena-4)</sup> The Elden Grove became a pilgrimage spot for Imperial loyalists that wanted to re-trace the steps of the Eternal Champion.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AMM-9)</sup>

## Notes
- According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game of *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, Elden Root is described as the center of the Elden Grove, with [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md) perched on the [northern] edge of the great grove, and [Greenheart](Lore_Greenheart.md) lying on the southern edge of the grove.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The finalized description of the Grove's location in *Arena* contradicts this,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARRia-3)</sup> yet some mythical books in ESO are more in line with this.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TBot GT-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ACOV-6)</sup>
