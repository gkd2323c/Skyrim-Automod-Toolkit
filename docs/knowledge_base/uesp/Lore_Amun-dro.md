*[Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md) was the first spirit to make his own path with purpose, because he was in conflict with himself as soon as he was born. His courage inspired all those he encountered, so much that he united the spirits to make the World. He gave his life to do this. We honor his sacrifice by walking the Path with purpose and resisting the call of [the Dark](Lore_Namiira.md).*
—Amun-dro<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spirits-1)</sup> **Silent Priest** Amun-dro was a [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) scholar,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup> poet,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOPF-3)</sup> priest, prolific religious writer,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spirits-1)</sup> and devout adherent of the pre- [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) epiphany Khajiiti faith.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Epistle-4)</sup>

## History
Little is known of the personal life of Amun-dro outside of his publications. In the words of the enigmatic immortal entity known as [the Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md), "he was a complicated man, but a thoughtful scholar". According to her, one should turn to the writings of Amun-dro to truly understand the [Legend of the Lovers](Lore_Legend_of_the_Lovers.md), an ancient tale popular during [Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup> According to religious texts such as [Thava-ko](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Thava-ko)'s *[Epistle](Lore_Epistle_on_the_Spirits_of_Amun-dro.md)*, dated between [2E 311](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_311) and [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> he was already ancient by the time of their publication.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Epistle-4)</sup>

He wrote extensively and was a priest of the old faith,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Epistle-4)</sup> earning the moniker of the Silent Priest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spirits-1)</sup> He also used the honorific "-dro," typically reserved for the wise and witty, as well as for elders and patriarchs.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KH-5)</sup> He wrote a catalog of ancient Khajiiti deities, classifying them as Sky, Dark, Worldly, Wandering, and Adversarial Spirits.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Spirits-1)</sup> Another of his works was the poetic text *[All Our Perfect Forms](Lore_All_Our_Perfect_Forms.md)*, dedicated to the furstocks of the Khajiiti people and their roles in society, praising the beauty of Khajiit diversity.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AOPF-3)</sup> He also recorded the tale of the creation of the first [dro-m'Athra](Lore_Dro-m%27Athra.md), *[The Favored Daughter of Fadomai](Lore_The_Favored_Daughter_of_Fadomai.md)*,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TFDOF-6)</sup> and *[Azurah's Crossing](Lore_Azurah%27s_Crossing.md)*, which described a meeting between a Khajiit and [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) at the Crossing.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AC-7)</sup>

## Legacy
By the time Thava'ko's Epistle was written, between 2E 311 and 2E 582,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> long after Amun-dro's life, his works continued to captivate the Khajiit. According to the [Torval Curiata](Lore_Torval_Curiata.md), his writings had captured the imaginations of Khajiit throughout [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md). In response to this growing interest, the *Epistle on the Spirits of Amun-dro* was published. This text aimed to portray Amun-dro as a heretical zealot, claiming that his theology offered no refuge for simple virtues like charity, humility, and love, providing little more than vague guidance on moral action. Thava-ko's epistle argued that Amun-dro's works were devoted to promoting slavish obedience to Azurah and tried to discredit his worldview.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Epistle-4)</sup>

Despite the religious shift in Khajiiti culture, the sermons of Amun-dro, along with other texts like the *Rites of [Sangiin](Lore_Sangiin.md)* and *[Noctra](Lore_Noctra.md)'s Hissing Codex*, were considered fascinating lectures by some Khajiiti scholars by 2E 582.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CH-8)</sup> Amun-dro's works were referenced in research by modern [Twilight Cantors](Lore_Twilight_Cantors.md), such as High Cantor Viti, who studied his texts while writing her own notes on the Moon Beasts.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-VNMB-9)</sup>
