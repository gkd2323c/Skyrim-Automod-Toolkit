# Magic

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d5/ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Galerion%27s_Magicka.jpg) Magicka, an aspect of the soul [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-icon-Use_Magic_Item.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-icon-Use_Magic_Item.png) Magic (*Daggerfall*) | This article is about the phenomenon. **Magic** (**Magicka** in the [Ayleid language](Lore_Ayleid_Language.md)),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ART-1)</sup> also known as **Arcane Arts**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Sk Altmer Description-2)</sup> or euphemistically referred to as the **Higher Art** by its practitioners,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> is the general term used for the focusing of raw energy into various properties and for various purposes. This raw energy, referred to as magicka, spell points, or mana,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MWGDMag-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWGDAbs-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-KE4-6)</sup> is the residue of [creation](Lore_Beginning_Place.md) in all things, a property intrinsic to all matter and forms of energy,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FDMG-7)</sup> and it flows from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) into [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) by way of the sun and stars.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MFTS-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3Arena-9)</sup> Magicka comprises every spirit; it is the energy of all living things and can be harnessed in a variety of ways. Magicka is described as the force that forms the very "sinews" of the [planes](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) and holds the realms together, and as the strings of magic threading together all existence.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Dreynis-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Dreynis Notes-11)</sup> The [soul](Lore_Souls.md) has been described as the piece of every mortal which comes from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), the realm of pure light and magic, and as the [Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) essence at the core of every mortal's existence, a "tiny [star"](Lore_Stars.md) which flares into a ["sun"](Lore_Sun.md) when magic is invoked.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LOS-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TDS-13)</sup> A Soul can accumulate magicka over time,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOFaindor-14)</sup> and when split into its aspects via magic one of the aspects of the spirit is that of Magicka.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Unleashed-15)</sup> According to the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) mystics, magicka flows through the body in specific physical channels. The Psijics have mapped these channels and are known to delineate them with arcane tattoos, helping mages focus their spellcasting.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PVCSCMag Fl-16)</sup> It is said that even the most mundane of skittering bugs have magical properties which can be used in disciplines such as [Alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Llaalsa-17)</sup> Despite thousands of years of study, there is an enormous amount of speculation about how magic is generated, how it might be used by [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), and how it might flow between living things.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3Arena-9)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-KE12-18)</sup> Indeed, it has been said that the mysteries of magic are as endless as the deep, dark, and endless sea of Aetherius from where it originates, and that those who study magic must simply accept that there are some factors of magicka that will elude them in perpetuity.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONConnalenwe-19)</sup>

**Anti-magic** is a property of certain items, places, and effects that prohibits the use of magic within or on them.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Amoncrul-20)</sup> The [Flask of Lillandril](Lore_Flask_of_Lillandril.md) is a notable anti-magic artifact, known for reflecting spells used on it, from direct assaults to scrying attempts. It was used by [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) to defeat the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) necromancer [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-J.27ffer-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Rescue-22)</sup> Another historical instance of the effect is the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), when the destruction of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) caused an anti-magic zone for several miles.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-10th-23)</sup> Ironically, several spells have anti-magic effects, a property known as silencing.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Silence-24)</sup>

One theory proposes that the [Luminaries](Lore_Luminaries.md), immortal beings of pure magic, are a natural part of the cycle of magic that functions as the mechanism by which magicka is emanated from Aetherius to the mortal world, akin to a lamp casting light or an aqueduct carrying water.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-25)</sup> The mechanisms which allow for magic to function in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) are a matter of debate. One theory, that of "Mundial Emanation", maintains that when the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) broke through the firmament of Mundus, they unleashed a wave of creative energy which also washed over Oblivion and infused it with magic. Another theory maintains that Oblivion has its own "manifest dynamism", a form of omnipresent background energy which gives Oblivion its basic shape and laws, and can be tapped into to use magic when in those realms.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Lucents-26)</sup> Whatever the case, [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) have referred to magicka as a force they tap into to exert their power.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Ithelia-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Mora-28)</sup>

Magic is a highly influential force not just within the Aurbis but also beyond it, in the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md). Despite the prevalence of magic in the Aurbis and beyond, there is at least one reality along the Paths where magicka does not exist. That such a reality might exist was considered a new possibility by both [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) and [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), the two [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) whose gaze spans the Paths; even with their perception, they had not considered it a possibility.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Ithelia-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Mora-28)</sup> The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) mage [Deslandra](Lore_Deslandra.md) attempted to enact an event known as the Great Nullification,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-29)</sup><sup>:44</sup> which would forever sever the flow of magicka and its influences on [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), as well as the connections that lead to other planes of existence like [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and the [Void](Lore_Void.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-29)</sup><sup>:52</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-30)</sup><sup>:50</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-VWGazetteer-31)</sup><sup>:38</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-32)</sup><sup>:19</sup> Speculation about the consequences of such an event include one theory that it would cut off communication with [deities](Lore_Gods.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-VWGazetteer-31)</sup><sup>:38</sup>

## History

### Origin
[Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md) was the god who designed the mortal realm Mundus during its creation. After it was completed, however, he ordered the project terminated, and left for Aetherius at great cost. In doing so, he tore a hole in the veil of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) through which the magicka of Aetherius flows into the world.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TM-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-BTAOM-34)</sup> The great rift he left behind, the sun, is itself known as Magnus.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-36L33-35)</sup> [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md) is said to be the embodiment of the feminine power of magic that exists everywhere and nowhere at once, having used it to allow Mundus to exist despite such an existence normally being impossible.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TNC1-36)</sup> Soon, other [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md) who agreed with Magnus followed his lead, leaving smaller holes which are the stars, through which magicka also flows. This is why the stars under which a person is born have such a great influence on that person's fortunes and fate, and why many materials which fall from the heavens have great magical properties.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MFTS-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3Arena-9)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TF-37)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg/200px-ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg) A Sorcerer [High Lord Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md) led the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) to [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) in the Early [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and drew upon ancestral lore to smite the creatures of the land using the magic of [Varla Stones](Lore_Varla_Stone.md).<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TLOF-38)</sup> Some of the first recorded uses of magic were by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) of the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). Through the salvaging of fallen fragments of Aetherius, they attained great arcane power, which they used to enslave the early [Cyrodiils](Lore_Imperial.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MFTS-8)</sup> A common phrase in the [Ayleid language](Lore_Ayleid_Language.md) is "*Av molag anyammis, av latta magicka*", which means "From fire, life; from light, magic".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ART-1)</sup> With one or two exceptions, wizards of the early First Era were generally solitary, and there was little collaboration beyond the master-apprentice relationship, or standardization in magical practices.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TFL-39)</sup> Until the Second Era and the construction of the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) was considered the pinnacle of magical research and learning in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

Many different magical arts have been developed during Tamrielic history for purposes ranging from warfare to technological advancement to religion and divination, and there is often much scholarly disagreement between various researchers.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-AR-40)</sup> Notable magical endeavors include the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md)' [Shehai Shen She Ru](Lore_Sword-singer.md), the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md)' [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md)'s Beast Tongue, the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md)'s sea serpent-taming magic, and the numerous innovations of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). There are many other little-understood magical phenomena, like the mysterious constructs [the Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md), the process of surpassing worldly limitations known as [Apotheosis](Lore_Apotheosis.md), and the esoteric state of Amaranth, involving the mantling of the [Godhead](Lore_Godhead.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-BBWD-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-43)</sup> A person's reserves of magicka are determined by numerous, often unknown influences, and some people are naturally very gifted in various ways, but no one has the infinite power of Magnus himself.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-TAA-44)</sup>

The power of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) and other deities is sometimes, but not always, within the meaning of "magic" on Tamriel, depending on the context.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-LHOT-45)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TM-33)</sup> The worship of deities is suspected to be desirable to those spirits due to the belief that they transform the energy exuded by the worshipper into another, distinct kind of magic they can wield for the benefit (or downfall) of all.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-KE12-18)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-OOGAW-46)</sup>

A myriad of great wizards have come from the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) from Summerset Isle and the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) from [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), as well as powerful [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) battlemages, Telvanni magelords,<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TAOW-47)</sup> and Sload necromancers. Many rulers of the Summerset Isle were advised by the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), a body of mages concerned with the practice of [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md). In particular, the formation of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in the early Second Era altered the political and societal landscape. By forming the Guild, its ex-Psijic founder, [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md), allowed magical items, potions, and spells to be bought by any member of the public that could afford to pay. In effect, magic was no longer dictated by the aristocracy or intelligentsia.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-OOTMG-48)</sup>

Throughout history, many institutions devoted to acquiring arcane knowledge have been erected, including the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), and the [School of Julianos](Lore_School_of_Julianos.md).<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-GTM-49)</sup>

Some time before the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), the curricula of the Mages Guild was seen as haphazard and disorganized. Gabrielle Benele saw that the [Shad Astula Academy](Lore_Shad_Astula.md) divided magic into eight "schools" and that this resulted in novice mages being trained in half the time, so she proposed the Mages Guild follow this model as well.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-PSo M-50)</sup> The Guild did adopt this and while the number of schools have changed over time, the concept has endured since its adoption.

The magical arts are not always met with warmth. Often this has been with good reason, as magical studies have proven to be all-consuming in numerous cases, making the mages threats to themselves and others. The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), the oldest known magical organization in Tamriel, is now considered a rogue organization by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), and the two have clashed before.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-SRAncano-51)</sup> Most Redguards consider wizardry both "weak" and "wicked", and it fell out of favor with the Nords in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). [Necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) is reviled in most societies.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-TBAOT-52)</sup> [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) summoning and trafficking is also hated in many provinces, especially in the wake of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), which many believed was a result of magic gone wrong.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TIC-53)</sup><sup>[*verification needed — what page?*]</sup> [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md), while rare, is feared because of its power.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

## Arcane Arts
[![](https://images.uesp.net/7/75/DF-NPC-Flat_177_1.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-NPC-Flat_177_1.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-NPC-Flat_177_1.gif) A Mage (Daggerfall) There are numerous ways the use of magic can manifest. The most obvious is the practice of casting [spells](Lore_Spells.md), but one can also brew potions to apply an effect, among other things. There are a few ways in which a spell is different from a synthesis, including the ways in which their failure manifests. A badly put-together spell likely won't work at all, but alchemy gone wrong can be poison.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TIC-53)</sup><sup>:39</sup>

### Major Arts

#### Spellcasting
The act of drawing on one's own magicka reserves in order to generate some kind of effect in the physical world is called "casting a spell". The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) consider it an intensely personal exercise. No two mages weave their spells in exactly the same way; like painting or sculpting, each artist has their own distinctive style. Despite this, each culture does have its own general spellcasting habits. The Altmer tend to be fond of spectacle, Bretons lean towards "flamboyant gesticulations", Nords sometimes involve bellowing, whereas Redguards are frequently more reserved in their spellcasting.

The arcanist Azandar al-Cybiades describes a spellcaster as a "willworker". A spellcaster changes reality using their own will and personality, and techniques are useful to ground their mind and thinking to help connect to magic. For example, incantations or hand-waving are methods which may help ease the mental and physical burden of a spell being cast. Spells, particularly those of the school of Alteration, are said to be about learning the [rules](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) of the [cosmos](Lore_Aurbis.md) and how to break them, how the unreal can become real through infinite possibilities such as breaking the sky, swallowing space, dancing with time setting ice on fire, green being blue or water being air, and believing that such things can be achieved. It is said that, in truth even seemingly natural phenomena such as fish breathing water are due to an innate understanding of this principle, a form of spell. As with all skills it is said that one can get better in spellcasting the more they practice, but to achieve mastery requires true understanding of what one is doing and why. Due to this, while it is possible to simply buy a standardized and premade spell from institutions such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) even without understanding of the underlying magical theory, which is said to be how spells are usually acquired, such spells are generally not as powerful as those cast by a mage with real knowledge. Some sources claim that the praxes and rituals used by all modern potionmakers, itemmakers, and spellmakers were originally codified by [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md).

According to the Psijic Order mystics, magicka flows through the body in specific physical channels. The Psijics have mapped these channels and are known to delineate them with arcane tattoos, helping mages focus their spellcasting.

There have been seven widely accepted schools of spellcasting over time, each with a number of spells that pertain to its particular purpose:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png/200px-LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Burning_Touch.png) Burning Touch, a Destruction spell - [Destruction](Lore_Destruction.md) spells harm the target by damaging its health with either elemental or magical attacks, draining and damaging its attributes, skills, health, magic, and fatigue, making it weak to the elements, poisons and magic, and corroding its armor and weapons.

- [Restoration](Lore_Restoration.md) spells augment the target by restoring its health, attributes, stamina, and magicka, fortifying its health, attributes, skills, stamina, and magicka, granting it resistances to the elements, magic, disease, paralysis, poison, and un-enchanted weapons, curing it of disease, poison, and paralysis as well as harming the target by absorbing its health, magicka, stamina, attributes, and skills.

- [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) spells call upon otherworldly entities through telepathy, certain skilled Conjuration mages can develop telepathic links with each other. Conjuration spells augment the caster by granting them [Daedric](Lore_Lesser_Daedra.md) and Undead guardians, Daedric weapons and armor, and the ability to repel the undead and banish Daedra.

- [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md) spells alter the physical and magical properties of the target. Alteration spells harm the target by making the objects it is carrying heavier and augments the target by making the objects it is carrying lighter, granting it elemental and physical shields and the ability to breathe water and walk upon its surface as well as open locks.

- [Illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) spells affect light and a sentient target's mind. Illusion spells harm the target by commanding, demoralizing, paralyzing, silencing, and causing it to frenzy, as well as augmenting it by rallying, charming, calming it, granting it invisibility, night-vision, translucency, and illuminating it.

- [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md) is an obscure school, though its spells seem to manipulate magicka itself. Due to its spells that bind the target's soul, this school is closely related to necromancy. Mysticism spells augment the target by granting it the ability to detect life, reflect damage, absorb and reflect spells as well as harm it by dispelling its magical effects and trapping its soul, including the ability to move objects through space with telekinesis. The nature of the School of Mysticism is the subject of much scholarly debate.

- [Thaumaturgy](Lore_Thaumaturgy.md) does not change the appearance or structure of a force or object, but can manipulate laws temporarily.

The schools of magic have often been subject to revision by existing magical institutes, and some have fallen out of favor. For example, following the Warp in the West the school of Thaumaturgy was rearranged into other schools and was largely replaced by the growing popularity of Conjuration, while in the fourth era the school of Mysticism was gradually consolidated into the other five extant schools. Ultimately, the schools of magic are arbitrary and artificial constructs used to simplify the study of magic.

#### Spellmaking
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8f/OB-misc-Spellmaking_Altar.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Spellmaking_Altar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Spellmaking_Altar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Spellmaking_Altar.jpg) A Spellmaking Altar [Spellmaking](Lore_Spellmaking.md), also known as **spell making**, **spell-crafting**, or **spellcrafting**,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is the magical craft of creating new spells. Spells taught at institutions of learning, known as circinate spells are standardized and more readily available. However, while it is possible to simply buy a standardized and premade spell from institutions such as the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) even without understanding of the underlying magical theory, which is said to be how spells are usually acquired, such spells are generally not as powerful as those crafted and cast by a mage with real knowledge. Thus, for more advanced practitioners of magic seeking more unique effects, spellmaking is an option.

Those who practice spellmaking are referred to as spellmakers, spellcrafters, and spellsmiths.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> They are known as the Isolationists within the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).

Spellmaking Altars, and Spell Makers, serve as the supplementary tools for mages to craft spells. Magetallow candles are utilized to power some of those tools. However, some mages are capable of inventing spells without utilizing these altars, opting instead to cast them directly to assess their effects, sometimes on willing subjects.

Spells were sometimes named after their creators, such as Merien's White Mask devised by Merien Sellan of [Eagle's Brook](Lore_Eagle%27s_Brook.md), or [Shalidor's Mirror](Lore_Spellmirror.md) created by the legendary Arch-Mage [Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md). Master [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) also lent his name to his experimental spells. Sometimes spells and magically enchanted [scrolls](Lore_Scrolls.md) are named after other individuals. Tarhiel's spell and scrolls of Icarian Flight bore Icarian's name.

#### Enchanting
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/LG-card-Sword_of_the_Inferno_%28premium%29.webp/200px-LG-card-Sword_of_the_Inferno_%28premium%29.webp)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-card-Sword_of_the_Inferno_(premium).webp) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-card-Sword_of_the_Inferno_(premium).webp) An enchanted sword [Enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md) is the act of endowing objects with magical properties through the use of various ingredients and tools, and a [soul](Lore_Soul.md), almost always with the use of a [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gem.md). An enchanted item's power diminishes with use, in which case additional souls may be used to replenish it. The strength of an enchanted item and the amount by which it can be recharged is directly related to the magnitude of the souls used. The enchanting discipline concerns imbuing physical items with magical properties called enchantments. The simplest enchantments are magical [scrolls](Lore_Scrolls.md) with a single enchantment that are destroyed with one use. The most complex enchantments are enchanted artifacts, objects with one or more magical effects powered by built-in soul gems. Skilled enchanters also use enchanted items more efficiently, with less waste of the magicka trapped in the soul gems, and even make greater use out of the enchantments. It is said that a skilled enchanter is also a catalyst, and that a ring that can generate warmth for a novice, on the hand of a talented enchanter could "bake a forest black".

It is said that [Raven Direnni](Lore_Raven_Direnni.md) created the art of enchantment in the First Era, though grand enchanters such as [Ahzidal](Lore_Ahzidal.md) of [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) predated her discoveries. According to other sources, the enchantress merely improved an existing process by developing the Rules of Eldritch Binding. Before her work, the process failed nine times out of ten.

Prior to [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md)'s foundation of [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) magical services, including enchanted items were not available to the general public in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). The art of magic, including enchantment, was primarily limited to the aristocracy or intelligentsia. The creation and use of magical items were generally kept within the confines of magical institutions such as the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), and the general public had limited access to them. Some sources claim that the praxes and rituals used by all modern potionmakers, itemmakers, and spellmakers were originally codified by Vanus Galerion. Galerion is said to have restructured the schools of magic so they'd be understandable by the masses and to have invented the tools of alchemy and enchanting so everyone could concoct whatever they wanted without fears of magical backfire. It is said that the original tools of enchanting and alchemy that Galerion crafted were more automated than modern ones, robotic master enchanters and alchemists that could create anything the customer asked for as long as they paid for it and could be used for that purpose by any layman, even without any understanding of magic. Even an enchanted sword that could "cleave the world in twain" is said to have been theoretically possible to make through these original tools, obstructed only by the enormous cost in magicka and gold. This level of accessibility is said to have resulted in a number of incidents where laymen created dangerous arcane objects beyond their ken. Galerion is said to have eventually realized the danger his original enchanting and alchemy tools posed, and to have had them destroyed and replaced by less automated versions which required users to have some understanding of what they were doing to use them. Because of these contributions to making the study of magic more accessible and free, Galerion is described in some sources as the originator of the practice of magic in its modern form.

The Mages Guild considered enchanting to be potentially dangerous to the public at large, and restricted its use to higher-ranked Guild members in good standing by the Council of Mages. Because the temptation to experiment with Black Soul Gems is strong and dangerous, enchanters tend to learn their craft under heavy supervision.

It is possible to utilize [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) reagents for enchantments.

Some materials are prized by enchanters for their properties in the forging of enchanted [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) and armors, such as [Meteoric Iron](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md). Cold Iron is known for holding enchantments very well.

The act of imbuing [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) with [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) essences to create Daedric gear such as armor was considered related to the art of enchanting and a point of pride among enchanters

[Sigil Stones](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md) can be used to enchant gear, and can even surpass the strength of enchantments made with bigger soul gems.

#### Alchemy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/LG-cardart-Alchemy.png/200px-LG-cardart-Alchemy.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Alchemy.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Alchemy.png) Alchemy apparatuses [Alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) is the act of mixing, boiling, and distilling various substances to obtain their chemo-magical properties and create potions and poisons. Potions are usually imbibed orally and usually grant the imbiber with positive effects. Poisons are introduced into the target's bloodstream by pouring it onto a [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) and attacking the target with it and usually gives the target negative effects.

Alchemical ingredients include extracts from plants, animals, undead, and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Certain ingredients are very valuable to alchemists due to their rarity, many of which are extracts from Daedric creatures. Prospective alchemists often need to experiment with ingredients to gauge what effects can be created, usually by eating samples of ingredients. This practice is referred to as wortcraft. Stationary alchemical stations are often used for the preparation of potions.

In the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), they were often utilized by the temple clergy and the elusive [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) as a service to their members. In [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), such stations were not only used by apothecaries but also installed by court wizards and even some taverns. Some landowners may also have an alchemy laboratory in their own homes. In contrast, within [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) it was far more common for a smaller travel apparatus to be used by alchemists. These portable laboratories had four components:

- Mortar and Pestle: used for grinding the ingredients together into a paste to be boiled, required for potion brewing
- Retort: used for distilling the brew, increases the magnitude of the potion's positive effects, optional for potion brewing.
- Calcinator: used to increase all effects of the brew, both positive and negative, optional for potion brewing.
- Alembic: also distills the brew, used to decrease its negative effects, optional for potion brewing

Around [4E 8](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_8), tests for discerning an ingredient's four "virtues" (primary, secondary, tertiary, and quaternary) were developed.

### Specialized Arts

#### Armamancy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png/200px-LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Glenumbra_Sorceress.png) A Breton wielding Bound Weapon [Bound items](Lore_Bound_Items.md), predominantly **bound armors**, and **bound weapons**, also known as **bound armaments**, are conjured ephemeral objects created from [daedric entities](Lore_Daedra.md). The practice of conjuring bound items falls within the domain of the [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) school of magic, specifically referred to as **Armamancy**. Practitioners of this art were known as armamancers, and sometimes as arcane warriors. Swords formed through this method are sometimes called **summoned blades**. Axes, on the other hand, were referred to as **conjured axes**.

#### Atromancy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c7/LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png/200px-LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Supreme_Atromancer.png) A Supreme Atromancer [Atromancy](Lore_Atromancy.md) is a magical art regarded as a branch of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) focused on summoning [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md), known as Atronachs. It should not be confused with [Atronachy](Lore_Atronachy.md), the practice of creating elemental [golems](Lore_Golem.md), which are sometimes also referred to as Atronachs. Practitioners of Atromancy are known as Atromancers.

#### Atronachy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/DF-quest-Hunt_For_An_Atronach.jpg/200px-DF-quest-Hunt_For_An_Atronach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-Hunt_For_An_Atronach.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-quest-Hunt_For_An_Atronach.jpg) A Fire Atronach created with the use of Atronachy [Atronachy](Lore_Atronachy.md) is an arcane art focused on giving life to inanimate material. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) was known to offer atronachy classes in the region of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) as of [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), during which they conducted experiments in the creation of atronach [golems](Lore_Golem.md). The creation of these golems is a separate form of magic from [Atromancy](Lore_Atromancy.md), the [summoning](Lore_Conjuration.md) of [Daedric Atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md). Golems are typically given life by imbuing them with them with a mortal [soul](Lore_Soul.md) or Daedric [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md). Though some are animated by other forms of magic such as [blood magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md).

#### Auramancy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_08.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_08.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_08.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_08.jpg) [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md) performing Auramancy in Nighthollow Keep [Auramancy](Lore_Auramancy.md) is an arcane art that involves evoking memories from the auras left behind on objects by individuals' emotions. By casting auramancy on a lingering aura or magic tied to objects, it may reveal a scene from the past in the form of harmless phantasms that act out a scene of the memory. Auramancy works best on personal possessions that are imbued with a significant emotional aura or have arcane residue clinging to them. As powerful emotions are the most likely to leave their mark on objects, the memories that auramancy reveal are usually those of important events. Some consider auramancy to be a useful tool for unraveling the history of ancient, forgotten places. For instance, Count Verandis Ravenwatch used auramancy to recall memories of the vampires in Nighthollow Keep.

Through the use of auramancy, a memory of the caster's choice can be implanted in an object, subsequently causing the object to glow. Upon interaction with the object, the auramancy will activate and display the memory like it would normally if directly cast. Auramancy can appear as golden or red light.

#### Blood Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png/200px-LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Blood_Sorceress.png) Blood Magic [Blood Magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md) is a an ancient form of magic believed to be associated with dark practices. It is most often used by [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) belonging to powerful bloodlines, who can use it to drain life, reanimate the dead, telekinetically lift others from a distance, summon [gargoyles](Lore_Gargoyle.md), and paralyze others. Vampire Lords can only perform blood magic while levitating and can use their life drain effect to slay mortal men and enhance their blood magical power. Practitioners of blood magic are referred to as sanguimancers. [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md) is also associated with blood rites.

To a lesser extent, non-vampires can also use blood magic in combat to siphon health from enemies. [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) are said to be naturally competent at blood magic. Blood magic can be also used to drain memories.

#### Cheesemancy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/LG-cardart-Cheesemancer.png/200px-LG-cardart-Cheesemancer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Cheesemancer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Cheesemancer.png) A Cheesemancer [Cheesemancy](Lore_Cheesemancy.md) is an arcane art focused on the manipulation of [cheese](Lore_Cheese.md). Those who practice this art are known as Cheesemancers. While it has a loose connection to the mad inhabitants of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), its existence is recognized among the common people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Among the known practitioners of this art were some [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md). Cheesemancers had the ability to command cheese blocks to levitate. Some cheesemancers regarded the cheese associated with their craft as delicious, considering some others to have poor taste.

#### Dark Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-skill-Negate_Magic.jpg/200px-ON-skill-Negate_Magic.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Negate_Magic.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Negate_Magic.jpg) A magicka suppression field created via Dark Magic [Dark Magic](Lore_Dark_Magic.md) negates, drains and preys upon the magicka and power of other magic users. It also replicates some of the harmful effects of spells cast by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).

#### Dream Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7d/ON-quest-Godrun%27s_Dream.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Godrun%27s_Dream.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Godrun%27s_Dream.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Godrun%27s_Dream.jpg) A victim of Dream Magic bound within his nightmare [Dream Magic](Lore_Dream_Magic.md) is an arcane art involving the manipulation of dreams. It is associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md) and her followers. The [Omens](Lore_Omen.md), powerful, malicious [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) that serve Vaermina, are proficient users of Dream Magic. Practitioners of Dream Magic can control the dreams of sleeping victims, binding them within their created nightmare. Though the art bears many similarities to [Mind Magic](Lore_Mind_Magic.md), there is no known connection between the two magical schools. An example of this similarity is reading the mind of another to see a previous dream.

Those under the influence of Dream Magic cannot be woken up by traditional means, but outside disturbances may have an effect. Prisoners within their dreams maintain a capability for resistance, being able to find objects within the dreamscape and send aspects of their uncorrupted mind to ejecting it from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) forever. While influenced by the magic, the bodies of those afflicted can be taken over. By breaking the victim's spirit through repeated visions of failure, loss, and betrayal, the victim can be killed within their dream, leaving them permanently possessed.

#### Flesh Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png/200px-LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png) A Flesh Magic creation [Flesh Magic](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md) is an obscure and ancient form of magic, believed by some to be older than the world itself. It is characterized by what practitioners call the "sixth element", otherwise known as Flesh. According to legend, the element of Flesh was birthed in ancient times by the original five [elements](Lore_Elements.md) of Earth, Water, Air, Fire and Light when darkness turned into day and the [Void](Lore_Void.md) took form. Said to have been hidden by virtue of its own self-awareness, it remains largely unknown and esoteric among modern societies.

#### Flesh Sculpture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/db/LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png/200px-LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Flesh_Sculpture.png) Flesh Sculpture (Legends) [Flesh sculpture](Lore_Flesh_Sculpture.md) is a style of [flesh magic](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md) based around the manipulation of flesh of living beings, be it for healing or for cosmetic purposes such as changing one's face.

While seemingly obscure, flesh sculpting isn't something reserved to the underworld of Tamriel. The "art" of flesh-sculpture can be traced back to the [Daedraphiles](Lore_Daedraphiles.md), [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city-states who adopted widespread [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship in the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era). The Ayleids subjected their [slaves](Lore_Slavery.md) to the practice to honor the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) they worshipped. Legend speaks of the Wailing Wheels of [Vindasel](Lore_Vindasel.md), where the Ayleids derived strange pleasure by subjecting [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) slaves to the "art-torture" of using their skin for flesh sculptures. The exiled [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) brought this horrific art form with them to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), where they enslaved local [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) for the color and texture of their skin. [Cloudrest](Lore_Cloudrest.md) contains a Faculty of Chirurgeons where masters of flesh sculpture teach their art. [Nohotogrha](Lore_Nohotogrha.md), an oasis found in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), is also home to flesh sculptors known as the Hollow-Faced Men. One known practitioner of this art, [Galathil](Lore_Galathil.md), claims to have learned her art in both of these places circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). According to Galathil, flesh sculpting cannot be used on the dead, which includes those afflicted with [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md). Despite its magical nature, changing one's appearance requires conventional surgical tools such as knives.

#### Hist-magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/LG-cardart-Thorn_Histmage.png/200px-LG-cardart-Thorn_Histmage.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Thorn_Histmage.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Thorn_Histmage.png) A Histmage (Legends) [Hist-magic](Lore_Hist-magic.md) is a unique form of magic associated with [Argonians](Lore_Argonians.md) born in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and their connection to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). Practitioners of this magical art are known as **Histmages**, and some have been known to come from the city of [Thorn](Lore_Thorn.md). Hist-magic operates on distinct principles compared to traditional spellcasting. Some Argonian spellcasters, such as the Hist Speakers, are known to draw power directly from the Hist.

#### Kiai
[Kiai](Lore_Kiai.md) is an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) magical practice similar to the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md). Little is known about Kiai's applications. It was utilized by [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) swordsmen in combat, who articulated their power in a similar fashion to [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) Tongues. The use of the Thu'um was once described as "letting it out in a kiai". The martial and mystical arts of the [Dragon Knights](Lore_Dragon_Knights.md), known as Ardent Flame, were known to descend from Akaviri teachings. The abilities of the Dragon Knights were also linked to [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), and were believed to differ fundamentally from common magic. Whether or not the Dragon Knights were using Kiai is unclear, but at least some Tsaesci swordmasters, such as [Vhysradue](Lore_Vhysradue.md) used abilities identical to Ardent Flame.

#### Mind Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/ON-interior-K%27Tora%27s_Mindscape_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-K%27Tora%27s_Mindscape_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-K%27Tora%27s_Mindscape_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-K%27Tora%27s_Mindscape_02.jpg) Sea Sload mindscape [Mind magic](Lore_Mind_Magic.md) (also spelled mind-magic) is an arcane art that involves the manipulation of the mind and [memories](Lore_Memory.md) of living beings. Using mind magic, the caster is able to evoke madness and manipulate the thoughts and memories of their victims without them realizing that their minds have been affected. The experience of having memories removed from one's mind has been described as if someone had attempted to extract ore from one's skull. The [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) are notable for having demonstrated skill in wielding mind magic.

Mind magic can also be used for [telepathy](Lore_Telepathy.md), speaking directly into the minds of others, which is described as a strange and defiling experience for the recipient. Some [forest spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) communicate using some form of mind-magic. [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) invades the minds of his prisoners as a form of torture, a process that is agonizing for the victim. By forcing his way into a mortal's mind, he can show them horrific visions. When a practitioner of mind magic forces their way into another's mind, they run the risk of opening their own thoughts to the person whose mind they're intruding upon. Those who have been troubled by a mental link of this nature can see through their assailant's eyes if they concentrate hard enough.

#### Mycoturgy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg/200px-SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Tel_Mithryn.jpg) A Telvanni mushroom tower grown via mycoturgy [Mycoturgy](Lore_Mycoturgy.md) is a branch of magic involving control and manipulation of fungi. Practitioners of this arcane art are known as **Mycoturges**, **Fungimancers**, **Shroomtenders**, and **Sporecrafters**.

#### Nature Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/ON-npc-Druid_King_Kasorayn.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Druid_King_Kasorayn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Druid_King_Kasorayn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Druid_King_Kasorayn.jpg) Druid King Kasorayn, a powerful practioner of nature magic [Nature Magic](Lore_Nature_Magic.md) is the magical ability to control nature by harnessing the power of [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md). Nature magic came about through the sacrifice of [Jephre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) and the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) to create the laws and principles of nature. The natural world is also deemed under the domain of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), one of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) and goddess of the wind and air. Nature magic has been used by [druids](Lore_Druids.md) to overcome their physical limitations, shapeshift into various animals, terraform the environment around them, or even invoke natural disasters to consume their foes. One druid named [Belaigh](Lore_Belaigh.md) slew a giant sea monster called [the Molmor](Lore_Molmor.md) by transforming its own body into an island. Druids are also animists who can call upon [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md) to aid them. One rather extreme case of nature spirits aiding druids occurred in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). By stealing the authority of the [Druid King](Lore_Druid_King.md), the [Ascendant Lord](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md) was able to gain the service of the [Spirit of Mount Firesong](Lore_Spirit_of_Mount_Firesong.md) and even merge with it to threaten the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) with volcanic destruction. Some [Wyrd Covens](Lore_Wyrd_Covens.md) also draw their power from the [Earthbones](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), and revere nature and the elements. In addition, [Wardens](Lore_Wardens.md) are able to use nature magic to cause plants and fungi to grow rapidly. They can also call animals to come to their aid, and wield frost spells to combat their foes. [Greenspeakers](Lore_Greenspeakers.md) shape the environment into settlements such as villages and even cities with the use of their Pact Magic. They enhance the growth of [Graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) trees and branches to form furniture, [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md), and even housing called Pod-Homes. Others such as [Reachman Shamans](Lore_Reachman_Shamans.md) or Daedra-worshipping Wyrd Covens may receive dark nature powers from [Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md), or [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) like [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). Wise Women of the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribes also use magic grounded in the natural world. Others still may gain power over the wilderness through pacts with nature spirits such as the [Viridian Sentinel](Lore_Viridian_Sentinel.md), or were born with it such as the [Wilderking](Lore_Wilderking.md).

#### Necromancy
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/SR-npc-Bandit_%28NPC%29_15.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Bandit_%28NPC%29_15.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Bandit_(NPC)_15.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Bandit_(NPC)_15.jpg) A fresh corpse being reanimated [Necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md), also called the **Necromantic Arts**, **Dark Arts**, or **Dark Practice**, is the manipulation of the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) or corpses of the dead. [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) know it as **alok-dilon**. Practitioners of the Dark Arts are known as **necromancers** (or **necros** in the common parlance). Different groups and cultures have varying positions on what exactly constitutes necromancy. In its broadest sense, necromancy can be understood as any form of soul manipulation. Some might consider it a subset of the [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) school of magic, as both involve the summoning of spirits and utilizing the powers of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). However, necromancy is more generally understood to connote the manipulation of the souls of mortals and the reanimation of their corpses, leading some to consider them two different schools. Some consider it a part of death magic, its own distinct school. Typically, this soul manipulation is accomplished by binding a soul to a physical form which has been prepared by the necromancer.

Necromancy is considered immoral in most cultures, and is illegal in some, as it is believed to contravene the natural process of life and death and violate the sanctity of spirits. However, there have been significant exceptions, and the relative risks and merits of Necromancy have been a controversial subject for centuries. The divisive holiday of [Tales and Tallows](Lore_Tales_and_Tallows.md) for example, is sometimes celebrated in Tamriel in its honor, which values necromancy as the oldest magical science. Much of the knowledge of it is often attributed to [Daedric](Lore_Daedric.md) influences, specifically [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). Molag Bal is also the father of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), undead creatures who often practice and have a heightened affinity for necromancy, or work alongside necromancers. Vampirism is sometimes considered to be directly related to necromancy.

#### Shadow Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg/200px-SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg) A Shadowkey [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md) is an obscure but powerful form of magic originally harnessed by [Azra Nightwielder](Lore_Azra_Nightwielder.md), who was the first to discover that shadow was not simply an absence of light, but a reflection of possible worlds created by forces in conflict. Shadows can be produced by mundane forces such as light hitting a rock, or by more powerful forces such as nations at war. Shadows are the records of the clash, past, present and future of these forces. Shadow Magic involves the manipulation of shadows to affect the forces creating it. At its strongest, it has the capabilities of reaching into the multiverse. Shadow magic has the potential to carry a high price for its usage, for its nature is to turn on their casters, and shadowmages are often distrusted because of this. In the Second Era it was often harnessed by "[Nightblades](Lore_Nightblades.md)". Some scholars have related Shadow Magic to the schools of [Illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) and [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md).

#### Shehai
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Sai_Sahan_Spirit_Sword.jpg) A Shehai A [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md), also known as a "Spirit Sword", was a magical sword created from the soul of a warrior through sheer willpower. Elite [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) [Sword-singers](Lore_Sword-singer.md), called Ansei, were famous for using this technique in ancient times, but knowledge of it was gradually lost to history.

#### Sight
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d4/LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ebonheart_Oracle.jpg) An oracle The [Sight](Lore_Sight.md), sometimes referred to as **divination** and **scrying**, is the ability possessed by some individuals that allows them to divine information through visions and prophecy. Such users may be called **Oracles**, **Seers** (or **Seeresses**), **Farseers**, **Soothsayers**, **Prognosticators**, or (parasynonymously) **Prophets**. The manner of visions attained by Sight vary. Such visions can include foretelling potential futures, seeing into other realms, revealing hidden things, finding lost places, and even communicating at a distance.

A vision is not always simply a visual perception, conveying non-visual senses like sound, smell, touch, and temperature, as well as information decoupled from any of the senses. However, visions are not absolute, and may provide an incomplete story, information that is subject to change, or wisdom that is often vague, abstract, and interpretive. Using Sight can work better when the seer is feeling their best, and using their Sight can sap their energy for a time. The clarity of such visions can imply an increase in its validity. Visions can also occur in ones dreams.

While some are born with the Sight or a natural affinity for it, devotion to the [gods](Lore_Gods.md) can grant the Sight. It is also suggested that lineage plays some factor in one's predisposition to be born with the Sight. Most [Alfiq](Lore_Alfiq.md) are blessed with Sight. Some seers claim that their Sight manifests through dialogue with inanimate objects. Others may receive visions involuntarily, which can be troubling. Seer's Stones are amulets known to help stifle one's oracular senses. [Azura](Lore_Azura.md)'s associations with mystery, fate, and prophecy make her more connected to the Sight (which her followers call the gift of **Crystal Vision**) and mortal seers than most gods. Azura believes that prophecy must occur, and guides her seers that can't always endure "knowledge of the truth". [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) have also been known to possess the gift of Sight on occasion.

The ability to predict the future through examination of animal entrails was not an uncommon ability among [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) [Shamans](Lore_Reachfolk_Shamans.md) of [Namira](Lore_Namira.md), and [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) haruspices among the [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) of [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md). The [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) of [Dhalmora](Lore_Dhalmora.md) perform this practice on [guar](Lore_Guar.md) intestines. A similar ability, an ancient scrying technique that required usage of human entrails instead, was known as **Visceromancy**. It was practiced by the denizens of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Some individuals possess the ability to derive readings from the pattern of tea leaves left at the bottom of a cup.

Some [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) are known to possess the gift of Sight. It was said that the dragon blood possessed by members of the [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) allowed them to see more than lesser men. Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) was troubled all his life by dreams that showed him visions of the future, including his assassination and the start of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) that followed.

A [Neesak of Scrying](Lore_Neesak_of_Scrying.md) is a crystal that is believed to possess divinatory properties and is used for fortune-telling or divination purposes.

While the gods and [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) are known to grant prophecies, this does not necessarily confer the power of Sight. However, such individuals are still referred to as Prophets.

#### Thu'um
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png/200px-LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Unrelenting_Force.png) The Last Dragonborn using the Thu'um, as seen in *[Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)* The [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), also referred to as the **Masculine Breath**, the **Storm Voice** or simply the **Voice**, is a form of magic most commonly practiced by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and some others which uses the words of the [language of the Dragons](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) to form "Shouts", the equivalent of spells, of immense power. The word actually means "shout" in the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) language. It is said that dragons make no distinction between debating and fighting, and so their words have always been magical and powerful, for those who take the time to learn and understand their meaning. The Nords believe that [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), the embodiment of the wind who is viewed as the Nordic aspect of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), breathed onto the land at the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) to form them. As such, the Nords believe that their voice and breath is their very essence, and that channeling this life essence is how the thu'um operates. Most, if not all, people have some capacity for the thu'um, but it takes a particular talent and many, many years of study and training to become a Tongue. The thu'um can be used for a wide variety of purposes, anything from sharpening blades to quickly traveling across the land, even controlling animals or killing enemies. Some stories suggest that the ancient Tongues even had the power to "sing [Shor](Lore_Shor.md)'s ghost into the world". The most powerful Tongues must be careful when they speak and are often gagged for safety, as their voice can cause great destruction. Manipulating time is notoriously difficult, with most known successes being entirely accidental or full of unintended, and very dangerous, consequences. This could lead to various outcomes, such as the birth of individuals who did not exist in another timeline, or the lives of different people taking unexpected turns such as becoming trapped in a time loop or dying before they were even born. #### Tonal Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/ON-place-Dwarven_Tonal_Barrier.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dwarven_Tonal_Barrier.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dwarven_Tonal_Barrier.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dwarven_Tonal_Barrier.jpg) A Dwarven Centurion generating a tonal barrier [Tonal architecture](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) is the manipulation of sound to alter reality. The most notable users of tonal architecture were the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) tonal architects who could shape tones through their own vocalizations, amplified by magical torcs; they would wear protective headgear known as Dwemeri tonal attenuators, which would protect their ears and thought-organs from the harmonic feedback fluctuations of their work. The Dwemer exhibited near total mastery of tonal forces and would use it in mining, medicine, architecture, and even psychology. Ancient [Chimeri](Lore_Chimer.md) scholarship states that the Dwemer could employ tonal forces to bend weaker minds to their will—a form of complex aural hypnosis. The Dwemer were able to create massive devices called tonal resonators, complex architectural wonders that stood taller than the most imposing giants, and filled cavernous chambers with pipes, dials, and pistons. When enabled, the resonators released a series of powerful tones that could alter the brainwaves of lesser mer and men, with the ability to induce deep calm, profound pleasure, or paranoia and terror; it is said these devices had virtually limitless uses.

#### Water Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/LG-cardart-Farsight_Nereid.png/200px-LG-cardart-Farsight_Nereid.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Farsight_Nereid.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Farsight_Nereid.png) A Nereid manipulating water [Water Magic](Lore_Water_Magic.md) is an obscure and little-known form of magic involving the manipulation of water.

On [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), it is believed water is composed of memory. When a mortal dies, their memories become water, and in this way all the memories of Tamriel's histories are stored within its waters. This fact is considered to be one of the most dangerous secrets about the natural order of the world, and is therefore closely guarded by the Demon of Knowledge [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). Achieving a true understanding of this principle requires a lifetime of study and contemplation, but in the wrong hands this knowledge can be extremely dangerous. For example, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the water spirit of [Halcyon Lake](Lore_Halcyon_Lake.md), a [nereid](Lore_Nereid.md) named Lorelia, attempted to twist the natural order by stealing the memories of her mortal followers to prevent the desertification of her lake, forcing the servants of Hermaeus Mora to intervene. The burial grounds of [Honor's Rest](Lore_Honor%27s_Rest.md) had memorial pools that allowed one to view the past through the eyes of those involved in the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md).

#### Weather Magic
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Cast_Adrift-11.jpg) A magically conjured storm [Weather Magic](Lore_Weather_Magic.md) (also known as **Storm Magic** and **Air Magic**) is magic that involves manipulating the weather. There are quite a few recorded instances of mortals altering the weather using magic.

Weather magic has never been an exact science because of the temperamental nature of what it seeks to control. Minor spells to conjure gusts of wind or forks of lightning are common, but manipulation of a region's climate is much more difficult to achieve. Maormer war wizards have longed for the ability to lower catastrophic hailstorms onto enemy borders as a preamble to invasion, or to halt a blizzard to make an unexpected march through inclement weather.

Although weather magic is sometimes attributed exclusively to certain races such as Maormer, everyone can learn how to manipulate weather.

## Traditions

### Arcanists
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-render-Arcanist.jpg/200px-ON-render-Arcanist.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arcanist.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Arcanist.jpg) Arcanist [Arcanists](Lore_Arcanists.md) are spellcasters who wield runic spellforms learned from a tome touched by the forces of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), the [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) realm of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). This tome is both the source of knowledge from which the arcanist learns of the spells they wield and the vector through which these spells are cast. In other words, their power is derived from the book itself: knowledge alone is one thing, but arcanists are unique in that the tome they wield is integral to their spellcasting. All mages use runes of some kind, but the runework wielded by arcanists is extremely elaborate on top of producing unique feats of spellwork. Some arcanists have been known to use their power to [scry](Lore_Sight.md) or manipulate the very [threads of fate](Lore_Fate.md). Scruut, a [Watchling](Lore_Watcher.md) fate auditor in the service of Hermaeus Mora, believed that influencing fate in this manner was a power not meant for mortals, and that it could potentially result in the undoing of the mortal that did so.

### Clever Folk

### Dragonknights
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/LG-cardart-Enraged_Dragonknight.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Enraged_Dragonknight.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Enraged_Dragonknight.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Enraged_Dragonknight.jpg) Imperial Dragonknight The [Dragonknights](Lore_Dragonknights.md) (or **Dragon Knights**), also known as the **Children of the Mundus**, were followers of a new martial tradition that emerged during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), originating from the dispersed [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). They were skillful masters-at-arms taught by a former member of the Dragonguard, whose name was lost to history, only known as the Grandmaster. He took it upon himself to ensure that the martial and mystical arts of the old Akaviri would survive into the new and turbulent Second Era. However, he would teach his skills only on condition that those he taught would go on to teach others. Their abilities were said to descend from the powers of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md). Although initially, arts of the Dragonknights were of Akaviri origins, the art was relatively widespread among various races of Tamriel as of Second Era.

Dragonknights were particularly skilled at manipulating fire, and could wreathe themselves in it completely without being harmed. They were known to channel the energy of the earth, fire, and molten rock associated with the powers of the very [heart](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). Their martial magic, known as Ardent Flame was fearsome art that pounds, shatters and physically alters the world around the caster. It was described as "Dragon magic" and was used to set foes afire, create flaming lassos, wreathe casters in a cloak of flame, and even breathe fire or poison similar to the legendary dragons. However, skeptics like [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md), a representative of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), considered it to instead be a branch of [Destruction Magic](Lore_Destruction.md) (as defined by [Shad Astula](Lore_Shad_Astula.md) academy).

### Druids
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b6/ON-misc-Firesong_02.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Firesong_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Firesong_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Firesong_02.jpg) Firesong druid [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) (also known as **vinebeards**) are children of the land whose kind have their origins in ancient [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). They are practitioners of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md)'s True Way (also known as Druidism), in which one is to lead a life connecting with, valuing, and stewarding the growth of the natural world referred to as [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md). The eponymous god takes the forefront of their faith, with some reverence also being shown to the stars. The True Way is a promise made to the "groves, to the seas, to the sky", and under the Oak's Promise, druids are to "stand strong, stay true, and shelter all". Druids serve in accordance to Y'ffre's will in roles such as priests, arbiters, teachers, healers, and [soothsayers](Lore_Sight.md). Druids have little interest in the quarrels of monarchies, often leading many to believe that they are pacifists. However, as champions of nature, they are not afraid to defend their ancient sites from the cruelty of civilization, as violence and death are natural.

Y'ffre and his [Earthbones](Lore_Earthbones.md) sacrificed themselves during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) to create the Green, an act which bound [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) to the laws of nature. Their voices are sought by druids so they may best be their advocate, and to ensure a life guided by natural principles and the willfulness of nature. Druidic spellcraft (which differs from the arcane) is said to be the first truly [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) creation, and its practitioners utilize the wellspring of primordial energy the Earthbones left behind, which flows via lines through the ground. Junctions where this resource intersects are known as "[bonelines](Lore_Bonelines.md)", places where druidic spells exhibit strange properties, and where prayers carry deeper emphasis. It can be harnessed by druids to overcome their physical limitations, shapeshift into various animals, terraform the environment around them, or even invoke natural disasters to consume their foes. Druids are also known animists who can call upon [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirits.md) to aid them.

Not all Skaal shamans are Frostcasters; the title is reserved for those who specialize in Frost Magic. They were known to skillfully integrate [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) into their arms and armor, creating decorative pieces.

### Greenspeakers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f8/ON-place-Snugpod.jpg/200px-ON-place-Snugpod.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Snugpod.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Snugpod.jpg) Tree-Pods created by Greenspeakers The [Greenspeakers](Lore_Greenspeakers.md) (also known as **Greenshapers**, **Green-Singers**, **Home Singers**, **Pod-Singers**, and **Tree Shapers**) are [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) practitioners of [nature magic](Lore_Nature_Magic.md) who work in tandem with the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) to shape the environment into settlements such as villages and even cities. Their practices are sometimes referred to as **Pact Magic**.

They enhance the growth of [Graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) trees and branches to form furniture, [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md), and even housing called Pod-Homes. They also craft it into bridges and platforms, as well as gates and large temples. They also create items of everyday use with the usage of the magic, such as flatware.

### Greybeards
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/41/LG-cardart-Greybeard_Mentor.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Greybeard_Mentor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Greybeard_Mentor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Greybeard_Mentor.jpg) Greybeard The [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), also known as the **Men of the Throat**, are an ancient and honored order of monks, live in silence at the ancient monastery [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md) near the summit of the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md). They are the most famous of the Tongues, or masters of the [Voice](Lore_Thu%27um.md). [Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md) founded the order in response to the Nord army's defeat after battling at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). After the battle, he meditated for approximately seven years, and arrived at the conclusion that the power of the Voice should be used primarily as a means of attaining enlightenment, rather than a tool of war. Jurgen called his new philosophy the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md), and preached a lifestyle of meditative study of the Voice which the Greybeards carry on to this day.

Some people believe that the Greybeards are not, strictly speaking, pacifists; that the Way of the Voice teaches only that violence is the least of the Voice's uses, and it should be used for that purpose only in times of "True Need". The [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md), having gained the Thu'um from Akatosh directly, is held as an exception to the Way of the Voice, though the followers of the Way are expected to remain peaceful. There have been times when students of the Greybeards cannot handle the demands of their lifestyle; renowned figures such as [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) member [Grundwulf](Lore_Grundwulf.md) and eventual Jarl [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) have either failed to develop their skills or failed to live by the ascetic lifestyle of the Way. [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md), the Grandmaster of the order, made his home alone above High Hrothgar, at the summit of the Throat of the World.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Greybeards.md).

### Nightblades
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-render-Nightblade.jpg/200px-ON-render-Nightblade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nightblade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Nightblade.jpg) Nightblade [Nightblades](Lore_Nightblades.md) are opportunistic adventurers known for their ability for getting in and out of trouble. They wield magic and use it to enhance their mobility, concealment, and stealthy close combat. They move swiftly and with haste by darkness, and use spells to benefit their circumstances. Nightblades thrive on conflict and misfortune, spell and shadow are their friends. Believed to be the most intriguing and shadowy of people, they trust their luck, cunning, and intelligence to survive, combining the skills of thieves with the powers of mages. They have a sinister reputation, as many nightblades act as thieves, enforcers, murderers, or secret agents. The mastery of their arts allows them to thrive as infiltrators and spies. Their presence is seldom seen but certainly felt.

Trickery, illusion, stealth, and agility make for a deadly combination, giving nightblades an edge in war. Nightblades also wield **Night Magic**, which not only grants them additional magicka, but increases the accuracy of their spells. They are capable of creating night runes, which ravage the souls of foes while siphoning life-force from them. In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), nightblades were known for their use of [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md), of which they are said to draw out the most through the art of Siphoning. With it, they call to the blood of their victims, cursing and draining them in manners considered diabolical. Assassination is more than just an action for Nightblades, instead it is both a martial and magical skill-set. Despite shadow stepping being commonplace in a nightblade's repertoire during this time, [teleportation spells](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md#Short_Range_Spells) were seemingly no longer associated with them come the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). The term nightblade was considered by magical educators a fancy title with little relevance.

### Sorcerers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg/200px-ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Sorcerer.jpg) Sorcerer [Sorcerers](Lore_Sorcerers.md) are magic users who draw their power primarily from the [planes of Oblivion](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md). Though spellcasters by vocation, sorcerers rely most on [summonings](Lore_Conjuration.md) and [enchantments](Lore_Enchanting.md). They make use of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) to summon [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to aid them, and they curse their enemies using [Dark Magic](Lore_Dark_Magic.md). Their commanding of [undead](Lore_Undead.md) and Daedric servants is thought to gratify their egos. Many sorcerers have perfected a kind of unique elemental magic called Storm Calling, utilizing the power of lightning to become consummate combat mages. They have been described as greedy for magic scrolls, rings, armor and weapons. The magical disciplines of sorcerers are known collectively as **sorcery**.

Although Dark Magic is often associated with "evil wizards" in the common parlance, it serves as a necessary safeguard against the power of rogue sorcerers by negating, draining, and preying upon the magicka of other mages. It also serves as a useful tool to counter the spells of Daedra.

### Templars
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/LG-cardart-Piercing_Javelin.png/133px-LG-cardart-Piercing_Javelin.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Piercing_Javelin.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Piercing_Javelin.png) A Templar using Piercing Javelin [Templars](Lore_Templars.md) are traveling knights who wield restoration magic and call upon the powers of light and the burning sun.

Although [Templar Knightly Orders](Lore_Breton_Knightly_Orders.md#Templar_Knightly_Orders) devoted to various Divines existed for a long time, the arts and martial practices of the Templars were considered rather recent as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It was believed that they were inspired by the martial magics originally developed by the [Resolutes of Stendarr](Lore_Resolutes_of_Stendarr.md). Despite the fact that Templar magic was often referred to as Aedric or divine in nature, it was certain that the ability to use these spells had little, if anything, to do with the faith of the wielder, and just like regular magic, required an appropriate approach and aptitude. In fact, certain Templars were known to defy the Divines and follow Daedra, such as [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md), and retain their powers. Despite that, texts written by the Resolutes of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) expected templars to exterminate [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), [Undead](Lore_Undead.md), [Vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and [Werebeasts](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) without halt or mercy. The term "templar" was considered a fancy title with little relevance to magic educators.

Certain texts, authored by members of the Resolutes of Stendarr and the [Sect of Harmonious Masters](Lore_Sect_of_Harmonious_Masters.md), were linked to the Templars.

During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of the Second Era, templars were employed by both alliances and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).

### Wardens
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-render-Warden.jpg/200px-ON-render-Warden.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Warden.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Warden.jpg) Warden [Wardens](Lore_Wardens.md) are worshippers of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) and defenders of [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md). They are master storytellers whose nature tales become [magical reality](Lore_Nature_Magic.md). Wardens weave spells to cause plants and fungi to grow rapidly. They can also call animals to come to their aid, and wield frost spells to combat their foes. Wardens of all races have been found protecting [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s wilds, and as a whole they do not bow to any specific organized group or guild. For centuries they were mostly silent, focused on watching, guiding, and protecting the Green, but they were called to rejoin the world of man during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Despite their unique magical practices, the term warden was considered a fancy title with little relevance to some magic educators.

The scholar [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) claimed that Wardens are nothing more than a militant group of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md), though this is contested by his rival [Lady Cinnabar of Taneth](Lore_Lady_Cinnabar.md). Both groups may worship Y'ffre, but Wardens can be of any race, while only Bosmer can be Spinners. Additionally, Spinners are typically nonviolent, while Wardens are more than willing to shed blood to varying degrees of those who seek to harm the wilds. Another difference is that Wardens draw upon the environment to shape reality, and cast powerful spells utilizing conjuration or alteration magic, while Spinners specialize in entrancing their audience in stories via illusions. Lastly, Wardens do not follow the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) either (excluding those who are both Warden and Bosmer), as they're willing to make full use of nature's bounty to survive. Magic used to corrupt and twist nature can damage the connection a Warden has with the Green.

A Warden corresponded with Dhulef, the court mage of [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md), requesting information on the warriors of the Green as a part of the interview. After some heated arguments between Druid Laurel of the [Stonelore](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md) and some [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) members (some of which are Wardens themselves), it was speculated that Wardens and [Druids](Lore_Druid.md) are related, but moreso in the heart rather than in the mind. Druids use the True Way to guide their magics, while Wardens feel Nirn calling to them in their gut. Wardens are also possibly being a way for the old world to walk tall in the new, with Wardens being the Green fighting back.

### Warlocks
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/BM-npc-Geilir_the_Mumbling.jpg/200px-BM-npc-Geilir_the_Mumbling.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-npc-Geilir_the_Mumbling.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BM-npc-Geilir_the_Mumbling.jpg) Nord warlock [Warlocks](Lore_Warlocks.md) are individuals who, similarly to [witches](Lore_Witches.md), have entered into a pact or oath with [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) or [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md), typically of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md). However, there are also warlocks who are associated with the [Good Daedra](Lore_Good_Daedra.md), such as [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md). In exchange for their loyalty and service, warlocks were known to receive knowledge and supernatural abilities from their Daedric patrons. Warlocks are known to engage in various dark and dangerous forms of sorcery. They possessed considerable expertise in the art of summoning Daedric Princes, and were known to form cults. Their actions were frequently subject to prosecution under the laws of both the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and the [Imperial](Lore_Imperials.md) legal system, as well as other groups such as witchhunters.

Warlocks, just like witches, gathered during the [Witches Festival](Lore_Witches_Festival.md) to create or summon dangerous creatures. Warlocks were known to craft necromantic objects in preparation for the Witches Festival. Plunder skulls, associated with the festival, were objects of particular interest to them. They were known for their practice of utilizing [familiars](Lore_Familiar.md), magical creatures bound to serve them, [atronachs](Lore_Atronach.md), and casting malevolent hexes, invoking curses and spells to bring misfortune upon their adversaries. The excavation of [ammonites](Lore_Ammonite.md) experienced a significant surge during the [1E 2800 s](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2800), fueled by the fashion among Nibenese warlocks.

These warlocks should not be confused with Warlocks of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), a rank within the faction, positioned above Magician but below Wizard, which granted certain privileges. Some [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) are also referred to as warlocks.

[Arcanists](Lore_Arcanists.md) are mages who draw their powers from [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). Although both Warlocks and Arcanists draw their powers from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) they are different from each other. One significant difference is that Arcanists, by definition, exclusively draw their power from Apocrypha through a bonded tome. In contrast, Warlocks can draw power from otherworldly sources, although the specifics of how they acquire these powers from their patrons may vary and are often unclear. According to [Azandar al-Cybiades](Lore_Azandar_al-Cybiades.md), any mortal who derives their power from a plane other than Apocrypha would not be classified as an arcanist.

### Windcallers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/ON-crown_store-Windcaller_Garb.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Windcaller_Garb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Windcaller_Garb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Windcaller_Garb.jpg) Skaal Windcaller The [Windcallers](Lore_Windcallers.md), also known as the **Blessed of the All-Maker** are [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) [witches](Lore_Witch.md) who adeptly invoke the [winds](Lore_Weather_Magic.md). They are distinguished by delicate markings on their faces and are renowned for their creature companions. Circlets worn by them feature [stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) nestled between [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) embellishments, symbolizing their connection to the winds and their trained wolf guardians. The flowing body art of a [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) Windcaller reflects the breezes and gusts of air accompanying them on their journeys, with some believing these marks tell a tale of self-discovery along the path set by the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md).

### Witches
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Witchmother%27s_Bargain.jpg) Witchmother [Witches](Lore_Witches.md) and **Witch-men** are practitioners (typically female) of hedge-magic known as **witchcraft**. They tend to gather in covens that vary in faith and practices (see below). Witchcraft is often seen in a negative light, and practitioners are sometimes persecuted by witchhunters, individuals that specialize on rooting out and destroying dark cults and profane sorcery.

### Wyrd Covens
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg/200px-ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Glenmoril_Style.jpeg) Glenmoril Wyress [Wyrd Covens](Lore_Wyrd_Covens.md) (also known as **Wyresses**, **Wyrd Women**, **Wyrd Sisters**, the **Wyrd**, **Ward-Sisters**, **Name-Daughters**, and **nature-witches**) are female-only, animistic [witch](Lore_Witches.md) covens that serve as wardens of [the Green](Lore_The_Green.md). They draw their power from the Earthbones, and revere nature and the elements. The leader of a coven is referred to as a "beldam".

The Wyrd originated from the ancient people that dwelled in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) who are referred to by retroactive nomenclature as the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md). A religious schism brought about the Wyrd's separation from their [druid](Lore_Druid.md) brethren. The Wyrd abandoned civilization in favor of living in the wilds, and believe that they are nature itself: the forest, the plants and beasts within it, and even the weather. In contrast, while ancient druids revered nature, they were not keen to isolate themselves and worship the untamed wilds. The druids accepted what they interpreted as their "proper place" as people, and embraced the idea that civilization can coexist alongside nature.

Wyrd witches are primarily [Breton](Lore_Breton.md), though membership also extends to outsiders, even those from other races. Once a singular entity, the Wyrd split into subgroups by the time of the [last Druid King](Lore_Kasorayn.md)'s voyage to [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md). These sisterhoods remained behind on the mainland to safeguard the forests of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), with some of the dozen or so covens even spreading throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

## Notes
- **Ovamancy** is an obscure field of magical practice that involves eggs, where one is able to produce eggs with smaller eggs inside. The only known practitioner is Revus Demnevanni.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-ESOGates-54)</sup>
- **Earth Magic** is an obscure arcane art described as harnessing the magicka of the earth.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Parisse-55)</sup> Practitioners may use it to force their way through land.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Wildering-56)</sup> Certain geodes can enhance earth magic, giving off a traceable magical signature.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Felipe-57)</sup> Practitioners of earth magic are known as **geomancers**.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Tel Ge-58)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-GF-59)</sup>
- **Life Magic** is the arcane study involved in the creation of [stone husks](Lore_Stone_Husk.md), used to draw the life energy of deceased [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) members into inanimate vessels<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Gwendis-60)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-VJ-61)</sup> and restore them to life.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Tharda-62)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-DCOS-63)</sup>
- **Hedge-magic** is a term used to describe wild, crude, unsophisticated spells frequently adopted by farmers and bandits.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Drathus-64)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Josajeh-65)</sup> While it is often looked down upon, others find its applications subtle and creative.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Lucilla-66)</sup> - **Lay** practitioners are a group who use similarly unpopular, creative spells as hedge-mages.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Lucilla-66)</sup> Their craft may be related to [Ley Lines](Lore_Ley_Lines.md).
- The Hoodoo Tinkers were a group of hedge-mages residing in the Dragontail Mountains, known for their extravagant and intimidating attires.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-HWCC-67)</sup>
- **Topiary-mages** are an obscure denomination of magic users. They were active in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) during the reign of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and the previous [Cyrodilic dynasties](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md). They used sorcery to construct several heads of former Emperors into the hedges of [Green Emperor Way](Lore_Green_Emperor_Way.md). When Tiber Septim needed advice from them, [birds](Lore_Birds.md) would move the branches and speak through their song, emulating the facial expressions and voice of the former monarch.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-PGE1C-68)</sup>
- **Odylic mages** are specialists in identifying and analyzing magical enchantments on items.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-OM-69)</sup>
- Magical blue bars were found throughout the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) and even the [Mantellan Crux](Lore_Mantellan_Crux.md) in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). They served to prevent passage through doorways.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-DC-70)</sup>
- There is an ancient figure known as Thorgrima who went by the title, *"Lord of Magicka"*. He is also known as the *"Keeper of Crimson Fire"*. A stone commemorating him was raised by his wife, Lungerd in Skyrim.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-SRWW-71)</sup>
- Some mages are said to be so powerful, radiating waves of magic can be seen by fellow trained mages nearby.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-72)</sup>
- Spread across Tamriel are many special crafting stations which provide unique effects to weapons, armor and jewelry created in such places.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-FELJEWL-73)</sup> One example is Night's Silence, which was created by the mad wizard, Mesanthano. Arcane power still lingers in places such as his ruined tower.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-MT-74)</sup> Another possible example is the [Lunar Forge](Lore_Lunar_Forge.md). It is unclear if these crafting stations are related to the arcane art of [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md).
