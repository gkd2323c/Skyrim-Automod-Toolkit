# Heart's Day

Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Heart%27s_Week_Companions.jpg) Heart's Week celebrations in Daggerfall [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9f/ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Heart%27s_Day_Retreat_Furnishing_Pack.jpg) Heart's Day furnishing decorations Heart's Day (also called **Lovers' Day**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup> is a holiday celebrated throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) on the 16th of [Sun's Dawn](Lore_Sun%27s_Dawn.md) and serves as the central day of the week-long **Heart’s Week** festival, honoring love wherever it is found, among friends, lovers, and even passing strangers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONRL-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Event Announcement-3)</sup> In almost every house, the [Legend of the Lovers](Lore_Legend_of_the_Lovers.md) is sung for the younger generation. In honor of these lovers, Polydor and Eloisa, inns in cities across Tamriel offer a free room for visitors. It is said that if such kindness had been shown to these Lovers, it would always be springtime in the world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DF-4)</sup>

Ironically, Heart's Day originated as the Summoning Day dedicated to [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of Debauchery.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DF-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LATH-5)</sup> As newer religions rose to prominence, they appropriated the holiday and sanitized Sanguine’s influence in order to strengthen devotion to their own traditions.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LATH-5)</sup> Since then, it has been predominantly associated with [Mara](Lore_Mara.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MPP-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BHD-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CLAL-8)</sup> though some customs tied to [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) were not unheard of.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONHDR-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PG-10)</sup> Since its reinvention, Heart's Day has been commercialized to the point where some consider the holiday to have been invented by merchants as a way to make money.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-11)</sup>

To properly set the mood in the bedroom in honor of Heart's Day, flower petals, various sweets, and much more are brought in.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONHDR-9)</sup> Common recommended foods to share with a lover are [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) fondues and [sweetrolls](Lore_Sweetroll.md), while Elves often have [snowberry](Lore_Snowberry_Bush.md) cordials for their preferred beverage.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TESTOC-12)</sup><sup>:16</sup> Each item helps those so inclined to fulfill on the day's delightful promise with their partners in the adventure called life. Flowers associated with Dibella are picked for the occasion.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONHDR-9)</sup> The colors [surilie wine](Lore_Surilie.md), [guar](Lore_Guar.md) tongue pink, and pale pink gray are dyes associated with the holiday.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

## History
*Mara teaches us that love is not only reserved for the lovestruck or newly married, but for friends and passing strangers alike. Our celebrations remind us that we dance in her light whenever we are brave enough to bare our hearts.
—* Kuzam-jo, Priest of Mara *<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Event Announcement-3)</sup>* According to the seemingly immortal [Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md), the ancient Pre- [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) writings of [Amun-dro](Lore_Amun-dro.md) gives a hint of the true story of Polydor and Eloisa.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LATH-5)</sup> In [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the King of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), [Dro'Zel](Lore_Dro%27Zel.md) summoned [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) onto the town of [Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_Gil-Var-Delle.md) after hearing a story from a local bard.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HOSAO-14)</sup> The historical fiction series *[2920, the Last Year of the First Era](Lore_2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era.md)* suggests that the story was the bard's retelling of Polydor and Eloisa's tale. Normally the King ended most Lovers' Day in a happy mood, but the story left him depressed and vengeful.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920V2-1)</sup>

Through the efforts of the Priest of Mara, Kuzam-jo, the single-day observance of Heart's Day grew into Heart's Week, a week-long festival centered around Heart's Day.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONRL-2)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KJ-15)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), he collaborated with the Argonian stage director Dreams-in-Scenes to make it stick. Seeking to confront contemporary audiences' shifting sensibilities and their fixation on surface-level critique, Dreams-in-Scenes reimagined the Legend of the Lovers as a grand epic she would showcase during the festival, transforming its traditionally somber tone into a more kinetic and accessible form without abandoning artistic intent.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONRL-2)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-HF-16)</sup>

During Heart's Week, Sanguine took on the form of a Breton named Samara Gautier and sought out festivalgoers to teach them of the old Sanguine traditions of Heart’s Day. Three of Sanguine’s forgotten rituals include inducing intoxication through a frog’s kiss to embrace risk and indulgence, sanctifying revelry with a blood toast, and nudging lovers to seize the moment rather than leaving love to chance. The first ritual temporarily shifts a participant's perception so that ordinary items appear as decadent treats. The second involves raising a blood-filled vintage in a toast to chance encounters, marking both love and heartbreak as part of the celebration. The third uses enchanted instruments to “grease the wheels of romance,” encouraging lovers to act on feelings they might otherwise leave unspoken, with the risk that these instruments can also cause impulsive or volatile behavior.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONTM-17)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), it was customary for the clergy of Mara to bless animals on Heart's Day.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MAB-18)</sup>

One Heart's Day, warriors from the [kingdom](Lore_Dynasty_of_Odar_the_Brave.md) of [Odar](Lore_Odar.md) investigated monsters multiplying at an unprecedented rate, possibly due to the coming of spring. They quelled the monster breeding frenzy, and were rewarded by the [emperor](Lore_Emperor.md) for their efforts.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PG-10)</sup>

## Notes
- Author [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus.md) once approached [Lady Cinnabar](Lore_Lady_Cinnabar.md) of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) at the Dragonstar Conclave of Antiquarian Scholars and pointed out it was the 16th of Sun's Dawn, suggesting they should meet later that evening to "appropriately celebrate Sanguine's summoning day", and took it rather personally when she refused his smarmy advances, for it was shortly thereafter that his criticism of her work began to appear in the journals.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Po DV-19)</sup>
- [Saturalia](Lore_Saturalia.md) was originally a holiday for a long forgotten god of debauchery.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SDF-20)</sup> Another holiday in relation to Sanguine is "[Carnaval](Lore_Carnaval.md)", a portmanteau of carnal and carnival where it is easy find his worshipers.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Po DV-19)</sup> The exact time it takes place is unknown.
- Heart's Day is the *Elder Scrolls* equivalent of [Valentine's Day](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valentine%27s_Day) and the reason it takes place on the 16th of Sun's Dawn rather than the 14th is because it corresponds to writer [Ted Peterson](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ted_Peterson)'s birthday on February 16.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Ted Posts-21)</sup>
