#### Sahloknir ([Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Sahloknir](Lore_Sahloknir.md) was a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Phantom Sky Hunter" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md). Sahloknir was among the few dragons to survive the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md) era, instead meeting his end at the hands of the [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) hero [Jorg Helmbolg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jorg_Helmbolg) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), with his remains being gathered by the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) and buried in a dragon mound east of [Kyne's Grove](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kynesgrove).

#### Sai Sahan (fl. 2E 576 - 582)
[Sai Sahan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai_Sahan) was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) swordmaster and Captain of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). He was renowned for liberating the city of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). A member of the [Five Companions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Five_Companions), he fled with the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) when the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) occurred. He managed to hide the Amulet in [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor), but he himself was imprisoned in the Halls of Torment. After his rescue by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), they recovered the Amulet of Kings.

#### Sahrotaar ([Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Sahrotaar](Lore_Sahrotaar.md) was a serpentine [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name could mean "Mighty Servant", "Mighty Slave", "Phantom Word Slave" or "Phantom Word Servant" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

At a certain point in history, Sahrotaar swore allegiance to [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md) and considered him the only true [Dovahkiin](Lore_Dragonborn.md). Miraak used Sahrotaar as his personal mount.

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) traveled to [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) to challenge Miraak. Through use of the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), they swayed Sahrotaar to betray Miraak. He helped them to reach the First Dragonborn on the summit of Apocrypha where the Last Dragonborn confronted Sahrotaar's former master. During the fight Miraak absorbed Sahrotaar's soul to regain vitality, killing him in the process. Having had his soul devoured by another dovah, Sahrotaar was killed permanently and cannot be raised again.

#### Wise Woman Sannemmu Khamishi (fl. before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Sannemmu Khamishi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sannemmu_Khamishi) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) [mage](Lore_Magic.md) and the wise woman of the [Erabenimsun Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Erabenimsun_Tribe). She was a devoted follower of the [Good Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra), keeper of many secrets, and the author of [a text](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crafting_Motif_48__Ashlander_Style) describing [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlander) combat gear and apparel.

#### Sara Benele (fl. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))

#### Ansei Satameh the Tigress of Dragonstar (fl. before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
**[Ansei](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ansei)** [Satameh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satameh), the **Tigress of Dragonstar** was an ancient [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) sword singer warrior and mother of two other Ansei warriors known as the twins of sword and spear, Ra Hasa and Ra Huzar, whose teachings inspired generations of [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) swordmasters.

#### Emperor Potentate Savirien-Chorak (?b - [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430))
[Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak) was a [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) noble from the early-mid [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and the [Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) after his father, [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie). Savirien-Chorak was the last leader of the Second Empire and although he proved insufficient in his role as administrator and responded poorly in crisis, some of his most well-known decrees were acknowledging [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) as a province, and the reformation of local militias. In the historical fiction, "[2920, The Last Year of the First Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:2920,_The_Last_Year_of_the_First_Era)", he is described as a glistening ivory-yellow eel and may have been proficient in dual-wielding.

#### Scalded Roots
[Scalded Roots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scalded_Roots), formerly known as the **Spirit of Wildflower Grove** is a powerful [spirit of lost nature](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) who inhabited [Systres Archipelago](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Systres_Archipelago). It was one of the three spirits of lost nature who were rehabilitated and taken care of by the [Druids](Lore_Druid.md) of the [Stonelore Circle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonelore_Circle) within their sacred site of Earthen Root Enclave.

#### The Scarlet Judge (fl. mid 2E)
The [Scarlet Judge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Scarlet_Judge) was a vigilante hero active on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), who had the heavy burden of upholding justice no matter the cost, with the figure being active for generations while cloaked in mystery and intrigue. In actuality, the person that the people of Vvardenfell saw as an immortal guardian who could appear in many places was just a mantle that was passed down, with many warriors playing the role before passing the cowl to the next, becoming just one of a long chain of vigilantes.

#### Sed-Yenna (fl. circa 1E 2703)
[Sed-Yenna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sed-Yenna) was a humble shepherdess who ascended [Sancre Tor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sancre_Tor), where she discovered the infant [Reman](Lore_Reman.md). She named him and played a crucial role in securing his enthronement as the [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

#### Seif-ij Hidja (fl. circa [2E 418](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_418) - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Seif-ij Hidja](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seif-ij_Hidja) was a powerful [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) [conjurer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) and [telepath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Telepathy). #### Selene, High Priestess of Shagrath (fl. Dawn Era - 3E 4th century)
[Selene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Selene) is a [Changeling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Changeling), a rare forest spirit found in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). She possesses the ability to shapeshift, which she has been witnessed using to take the form of a giant [spider](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider) and a female humanoid. Her domain is known as [Selene's Web](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Selene%27s_Web) and her followers know her as the **Forest Mother**.

#### Selenu (fl. Merethic Era)
A [Nede](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) from ancient [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), Selenu was the elder of the nomadic tribe who came across [Lamae Bal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamae_Bal) after she had been infected with [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md). #### Magus-General Septima Tharn (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Septima Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septima_Tharn) of [House Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tharn) was Magus-General of the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and a powerful [necromancer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was a relative of Empress [Clivia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn), and like her other relatives, she venerated [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). As commander of the Imperial Seventh Legion, Magus-General Septima proved to be a very ruthless tactician. She was willing to take and kill hostages, as well as enslave the populace of conquered cities. Her critics claimed she had no military accomplishments beyond "bullying freeholders for their back taxes".

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Septima abducted King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and then slew him in the [Fallen Wastes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fallen_Wastes) of [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai) in order to destabilize the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). By slaying him in the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) crypt the [Hall of Heroes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hall_of_Heroes), she was also able to physically follow his soul to the [Far Shores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Far_Shores). A soldier of the Covenant was able to follow and slay Tharn in turn. As a reward for this heroic deed, [Tu'whacca](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tu%27whacca) allowed the soldier and King Emeric to return to life in [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).

#### Septimus Signus (?b - [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201))
[Septimus Signus](Lore_Septimus_Signus.md) was a brilliant scholar from the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) who dedicated his life to studying the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), a pursuit which drove him mad. In [4E 195](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_195), his book *Ruminations on the Elder Scrolls* was published. Eventually, he had trouble even using coherent sentences, and often spoke in verse. He discovered a large [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) lockbox in an ice cave north of the College of Winterhold. He and [Urag gro-Shub](Lore_Urag_gro-Shub.md) were colleagues, and Urag last saw Septimus years before his death. Somewhere along the way, Septimus became a disciple of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md), the [Daedric prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of the unknown, who led him to believe this lockbox contained the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), compared to which the knowledge of the Elder Scrolls was a mere "passing awareness".

Septimus murdered, sowed dissent, and spread plagues on behalf of the Demon of Knowledge to gain his assistance in acquiring the Heart. But in order to unlock the lockbox, he needed the blood of the Dwemer. With the aid of an [assistant](Lore_Hero.md), he collected the blood of the various [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) races and used the mixture as a substitute. However, once inside, Septimus did not find the Heart, but rather, the [Oghma Infinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oghma_Infinium), which had been sealed within by persons unknown to deprive Hermaeus Mora of its power. Before Septimus could comprehend that he had been betrayed and used by the daedra, he disintegrated, and Hermaeus Mora's new champion, the assistant, claimed the Oghma Infinium for themself.

#### Serana (fl. 4E 201)

She and her family became [Vampire Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord) after her father made a deal with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and founded the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md) clan. #### Serenarth (fl. [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578))
[Treethane Serenarth](Lore_Serenarth_(Bosmer).md) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) Treethane active circa [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578). #### Serenarth (?b - late Merethic Era)
[Serenarth](Lore_Serenarth_(Snow_Elf).md) was an ancient [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) wizard. He participated in the infamous [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md) where the [Snow Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Elves) and [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) fought over the razing of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md).

According to the tales, [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md), with swift and accurate aim, fell Serenarth with his legendary bow [Long-Launcher](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Long-Launcher).

As Serenarth lay dying, he made a bargain with unknown but malevolent forces to swap his spirit with that of a [Frost Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Atronach). His host form still persists to this day, encased in ice within a glacier close to where his frozen corpse still lies with the threat of thawing out and exacting vengeance upon Ysgramor's heirs.

#### Saint Seryn the Merciful (fl. before 2E 582)
[Saint Seryn the Merciful](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seryn) is the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) patron [saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_Saints) of [Brewers, Bakers, and Distillers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dunmer_Cuisine). Seryn could heal all diseases at the price of taking the disease upon herself. Tough-minded and fearless, she took on the burdens of others and bore those burdens to an honored old age. Seryn was canonized by the Tribunal, and shrines to her could be found in Tribunal temples.

#### Archmage Shalidor (fl. 1E)
[Arch-Mage Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md), sometimes known as the **Archmagus**, the **Spellbreaker**, was an immensely powerful wizard from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) who had an understanding of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) few have ever matched, and his numerous but scattered writings are highly sought after. There are many legends surrounding Shalidor's life, such as how he built the city of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) with a whispered spell, built the great [Ice Fortress of Winterhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortress_of_Ice), how he stole the secret of life from [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), and how he single-handedly fought against the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) legions of clan [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) at the [Battle of Rourken-Shalidor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Rourken-Shalidor) (in which he bore his [Spellmirror](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spellmirror) into battle). Many of these stories are believed to be hyperbole or outright fabrications. Shalidor stood at the forefront of a movement to enact higher standards among mages, and to discourage spell use among the common castes. This effort is dubiously credited with the original organization and formation of the schools of magic ([Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) is also often credited with the creation of the schools of magic) and the foundation of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md).

#### Magister Shelreni Baro (?b - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Shelreni Baro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shelreni_Baro) was a rogue [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) who served as a Master of the house during the time of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). She did not get along well with [Divayth Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divayth_Fyr), who referred to her and the other magisters as a dangerous, self-selecting nest of snakes.

Meln Rendys was once her mentor, but upon recognizing a "darkness" in her in that she was reckless and dangerous (not the usual Telvanni traits), he refused to sponsor her advancement beyond the rank of master. Shelreni coveted his Black Book and ultimately murdered Meln.

#### Shezarrine
The [Shezarrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarrine) (or **Shezzarine**), also identified as both **Shor-Who-Lives** and **Teacher of Men**, was a bearded figure in [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nedes) folklore described as one of the guises of [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr), the "[God](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) of [Man](Lore_Man.md)", in his role as a wise mentor towards the races of Man, primarily featured in tales where the Nedes resist the advances of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) invaders. The term is derived from the name of Shezarr, whose name the Song of Pelinal identifies as an alternative for the word "freedom".

#### The Silvenar
[The Silvenar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Silvenar) is the Voice of the People for the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md). While often viewed by outsiders as a title for the Bosmer's political representative for foreign affairs, the Silvenar is more than a mere politician. Each person who bears the title is thought of as merely the Silvenar's aspect. The Silvenar represents the Bosmer people legally, physically, and emotionally. He is said to be their spirituality, in contrast to [the Green Lady](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green_Lady), who is their physicality. It is said to be the union of the two that makes Valenwood whole. He reflects the state of the Bosmer people in his way of thinking, his health, and even his gender. He is inseparably tied to the Bosmeri people, and they to him, making him supernaturally empathic and aware of their concerns. They also believe that he looks after their spirits, as well as their bodies. As the Voice of the People, the Silvenar has many rights which grant him great authority in Valenwood. For one, only the Silvenar can grant foreign building and trade contracts; any foreign power that wishes to build in Valenwood or do business with the Bosmer must deal with the Silvenar first.

How the Silvenar and, by extension, Green Lady, came to be is shrouded in mystery. Some folktales claim that the first Silvenar and Green Lady were pilgrims, a childless couple who tended to [the Eldest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Eldest) like their own offspring. The Green Lady, however, insists that her memories date all the way back to the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).

As the Silvenar exists primarily as a story, his only true origin is the tale of his meeting with the Green Lady. In the tale, the Silvenar begins as a being who can see the whole of Valenwood and is left saddened and empty by its purely primal nature. He eventually comes across a Bosmer unlike any he had seen before, whose raw strength and passion made all before him terrified. She begins to attack him, but he instead tells her to stay with him and see the beauty of the forest. Eventually, The Green Lady comes to see the Valenwood as the Silvenar does, while the Silvenar's emptiness became filled with her passion. The two became bound to one another, both taming the wilds and strengthening the Green in the process..

This story, in addition to functioning as the beginning of the Silvenar, also serves as the Silvenar's now and future. Each time a Silvenar dies, a new one is selected by the Green and will once again repeat this tale to unite with their Green Lady. As such, the Silvenar is a constant in Bosmeri society.

While the Silvenar represents the civilized nature of the current Bosmer, the Green Lady has the raw physicality and passion of their primal past. If the Silvenar dies, his Green Lady's bestial nature is irrevocably unleashed, and her fury turned on all those involved until she joins him in death. Upon the death of the Silvenar, the new Silvenar officially takes on the title at a wedding. This wedding, which is known as the Handfasting, is an important occasion for the Bosmer which only occurs once in a generation. It is responsible for both anointing and marrying the Silvenar and the Green Lady. Even before the Handfasting, the Silvenar may feel the new identity taking over. For the Silvenar doesn't just represent the will of the Bosmer to outsiders. The Silvenar actually feels the will of the people and acts upon it, and the people likewise can feel the Silvenar's influence and be swayed by it.

In the same year, Jolgeirr Barrow was rediscovered and looted. *[Fall of the Snow Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fall_of_the_Snow_Prince)*, an ancient account of the Battle of the Moesring taken by [Lokheim](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Lokheim), was uncovered within and came into the possession of Athellor, an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) scholar of the thought-to-be-extinct Snow Elves. #### Spirit of Air
The [Spirit of Air](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spirit_of_Air) is one of three powerful [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) residing within the sacred confines of the Earthen Root Enclave. Communing with the Spirit of Air was a transformative experience, granting those who dare to venture its relentless honesty and wisdom. This spirit, loud, and incapable of deceit, was known to bestow the knowledge of all spoken words, a gift that unveils both the beauty and the weight of one's own life narrative. It can repeat anything that was ever spoken aloud. The Spirit of Air challenges individuals to confront their past with unflinching courage, while imparting the invaluable lesson of cherishing each fleeting moment in the tapestry of existence.

#### Spirit of Mount Firesong
The [Spirit of Mount Firesong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spirit_of_Mount_Firesong) was an ancient [nature spirit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) bound to [Mount Firesong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mount_Firesong) long ago by [Druid King Kasorayn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kasorayn). When summoned by [Lord Bacaro Volorus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bacaro_Volorus), it took an appearance akin to a giant ethereal humanoid [Iron Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Atronach).

#### Spirit of Root
The [Spirit of Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spirit_of_Root) (also temporarily referred to as the **Corruption of Root**) is one of three powerful [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) residing within the sacred confines of the Earthen Root Enclave. The Spirit of Root speaks with all the voices of the plants, as plants communicate with each other. The Spirit of Root and the [trellis sentinels](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trellis_Sentinel) share a crucial relationship in the protection and preservation of the Deeproot. Trellis sentinels serve the vital role of providing support to the Deeproot, preventing its collapse and safeguarding the Spirit of Root residing within. Connection with the Spirit of Root served as a poignant reminder that even in the most desolate of places, solitude is an illusion, for the voices of all living beings can be heard anywhere. It allowed druids to hear the realm through interconnected roots of all plants. Spirit of Root could command [Fauns](Lore_Faun.md) and graft-root animals.

#### Spirit of Stone
The [Spirit of Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spirit_of_Stone) (also temporarily referred to as the **Corruption of Stone**) is one of three powerful [nature spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nature_Spirit) residing within the sacred confines of the Earthen Root Enclave. The moments shared with the Spirit of Stone were treasures, each second passing by deliberately, held close and cherished instead of slipping away in haste. By gazing through its eyes, druids could behold sights stretching as far as the [Earth Bones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Earth_Bones) beneath the very ground they stood upon. It was associated with the [element](Lore_Elements.md) of [stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Stone) and was known to have the ability to command [Stone Atronachs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stone_Atronach).

#### S'rathra (fl. [2E 798](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_798) - [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864))
[S'rathra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:S%27rathra) was a rich and powerful crime boss from the late- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), known for being suave and urbane in his dealings. Born in [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), the northern region of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), he used to run with the violent nomad cats of the Ne-Quin'al plains. After being lamed at the massacre of Stoop Low, he left Anaquina to keep from bringing shame to his tribe, eventually ending up in the far more cosmopolitan [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) to the south. For a time, S'rathra ran a lucrative [skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skooma) trade out of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) port, smuggling [Moon Sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar) into the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) (becoming, unfortunately, an addict himself in the process). When [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) strengthened its presence in the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md), S'rathra left Elsweyr entirely.

S'rathra made [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) his home, playing the lesser lords of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) to his benefit. Though the Empire had solidified the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), there was still a lot of money to be made in the drawing of borders before the provincial laws dried completely. Mercenaries and fixers were in great demand there. Eventually, S'rathra would meet the hero [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) and the smuggler [Tobias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tobias). S'rathra and Cyrus would end up forging a mutually beneficial relationship: S'rathra found the paying parties and Cyrus would do all the dirty work.

At some point in time, S'rathra sent his band of brigands to a plantation outside of [Tear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tear) to free his kinsmen from the [Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) [slavers](Lore_Slavery.md). Tobias and Cyrus were among the brigands. Cyrus was able to free some of the slaves and it caused a giant revolt across the plantation. In the end, the Dres slavers were defeated and the slaves were liberated.

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), one of S'rathra's assignments for Cyrus was cut short when he received a letter from Tobias, revealing that Cyrus' sister, [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), went missing in [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md). S'rathra empathized with Cyrus, providing the money and transport necessary for Cyrus to reach Stros M'Kai.

#### Star Man (fl. before 1E 792)
[Star Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Star_Man) was a legendary [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) who led his people on a journey following the [Constellation](Lore_Constellations.md) of [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md). He charted the movements of the stars and saw that when the Warrior constellation was high in the sky, victory followed, but when it disappeared, famine and desolation came. He convinced his kinsmen to follow the Warrior's path, and as they sailed across mountains and deserts, victory followed them while famine and desolation fled. They revered the Warrior's three charges: [the Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Lord), [the Lady](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Lady), and [the Steed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Steed), offering them gifts and incense as tokens of respect and devotion. Led by the Star Man they reached their destination at the Warrior's apex, built a temple and tomb, and became eternal guardians among the stars. It is said that the location of the temple remains hidden, but those who seek it need only follow the Warrior's path.

#### The Sunken One
[The Sunken One](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunken_One), also known as **He Who Shakes The Ground**, is a [Storm Atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Storm_Atronach) that dwells in the depths of Sandstone Cavern, a system of natural caverns located in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [West Weald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:West_Weald), northeast of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and west of [Miscarcand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Miscarcand).

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Sunken One was worshipped as an all-powerful [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods) by the Seringi family, local [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) farmers who lived at the nearby Shetcombe Farm. The atronach supposedly revealed His teachings to these farmers, demanding that they spread His word and follow His laws in return. The farmers believed that he sat below the surface and passed sentence upon the mortal world without pity or mercy. The only way to appease the Sunken One was by braving the depths of Sandstone Cavern to leave him "the Offering", consisting of gold and gems.

By the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Shetcombe Farm's sole inhabitant was Slythe Seringi, a Dunmer man who was devoted to the teachings of the Sunken One, as was his father before him. Following the sacking of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) by the forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), Slythe flew into a panic, believing the burning of the nearby city to have been the work of the Sunken One, angered by the growing corruption of the world of man. He believed that the atronach in His hunger would destroy more Cyrodilic cities if he did not enter Sandstone Cavern and deliver the Offering to appease Him, as his father once had. Slythe entered the caverns without weapons or armor, relying only on his faith, and managed to descend to the Sandstone Big Rooms, the lowest depths of the cave network. However, the caverns were home to dangerous monsters and wild animals, one of which attacked Slythe and mortally wounded him. He died within the caverns without ever delivering his offering.

The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) subsequently came upon Slythe's recently abandoned farmhouse at Shetcombe and followed him into the depths of Sandstone Cavern. Upon discovering Slythe's corpse in the cave's depths, the Sunken One appeared and attacked the Hero. Although defeated by the Hero, the Sunken One was not permanently banished to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and would always eventually reappear within the caverns.

#### Svaknir (fl. 1E 5th century)
[Svaknir](Lore_Svaknir.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) bard in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) who became famous (or infamous) for challenging the legitimacy of the capture of the dragon [Numinex](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Numinex) by [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md). In large part due to his acclaim for capturing Numinex, Olaf had gone on to become High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), ending the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) among the Nords. Hence, Svaknir's accusations were highly volatile and controversial.

Svaknir wrote and performed pieces decrying Olaf as a fraud and a tyrant. Olaf responded by imprisoning the bard and destroying every copy of the verse which could be found. Ancient bard texts, perhaps written or inspired by Svaknir, claimed that Olaf and his men came upon a weakened Numinex and captured the dragon without significant effort, and then fabricated the orthodox tale to ensure Olaf's place as Jarl and likely to aid in his bid to become High King.

As of [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) Svaknir was still a figure of controversy and interest among the members of Bard's Collage. He was also known for a hilarious song in which [Tuxedo Bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear) were mocked as "the [Chub Loon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chub_Loon) of Bears".

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), in an effort to recover [King Olaf's Verse](Lore_King_Olaf%27s_Verse.md), Svaknir's lost portion of the [Poetic Edda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Poetic_Edda) (a living history of Skyrim maintained by bards), a [prospective bard](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) from the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md) plundered Olaf's [tomb](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dead_Men%27s_Respite) along with a vengeful shade of Svaknir, and fought the draugr that once was the High King of Skyrim. In the end, Olaf considered Svaknir a fool, but respected him as an honest enemy.

#### Jarl Svana (fl. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Svana](Lore_Svana.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) ruler of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md). Born a princess to High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md) and Queen Gerhyld in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Svana was not fond of her role as heir, which she referred to as a role that "she didn't ask for". She was a descendant of High King [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md), whose opposition to Queen [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md) had been responsible for Skyrim's split into [Eastern](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) and Western Kingdoms.

By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Western Skyrim would come under threat with the reemergence of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md), resulting in the assassination of Queen Gerhyld. Svana and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) confronted Svargrim after discovering he was a pawn of the Gray Host and managed to defeat him before he could devastate the realm. Princess Svana was able to persuade both Prince [Irnskar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Irnskar) and [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) to offer their support to the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md), who also had suffered greatly from the actions of the Gray Host. Svana, now Jarl, attended the meeting of local rulers in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), which was called by the [Ard Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), the leader of the Reachfolk. Jarl Svana, the Skald-King, and Prince Irnskar together with the Ard signed a peace treaty between Western Skyrim, [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md), and [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md). After the declaration of peace, Svana and Prince Irnskar worked together to establish the terms of the new accord.

#### High King Svargrim (?b - 2E 582)
[Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md), known as the **Wolf of Solitude**, was the High King of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) and Jarl of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). He was crowned High King in [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561) and was still in power around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). He was a descendant of High King [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md), whose opposition to Queen [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md) had been responsible for Skyrim's split into [Eastern](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) and Western Kingdoms.

At some point, Svargrim forced the widower Jakolar out of Solitude, as Jakolar wanted to resurrect his deceased wife through unconventional and seemingly pointless means. As of 2E 582, though Svargrim was growing older, he was still hale and showed no desire to give up the throne.

She lived in the town of [Shor's Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor%27s_Stone). She was a mage, author, and skald by training.

Skald Svari was present at Fallowstone Hall when it was stormed by [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). She was among the surviving Companions led by [Vigrod Wraithbane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vigrod_Wraithbane) who summoned the vengeful spirit of [Sage Tirora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tirora) to help retake the hall from the invaders. Having prepared her whole life for the role, Svari was the natural successor to Tirora to become the next sage of Fallowstone Hall and record the deeds of the Companions. However, she needed to first prove herself by praying at Fallowstone's shrines to receive visions of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). After completing this ritual, she was appointed as sage by Vigrod and her fellow Companions.

As her first act as sage, Svari began work on recording the deeds of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in aiding the Companions liberate Fallowstone Hall. She also tasked the Vestige with recovering two artifacts which had been looted from Fallowstone Hall. Specifically, a weapon known as Winter's Hammer was recovered from the bandit leader at [Fort Greenwall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Greenwall), and another named Winter's Bite was retrieved from the Darkling cultists at Faldar's Tooth.

Sage Svari was an expert on the subject of [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md). She is the author of *[An Accounting of Werewolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:An_Accounting_of_Werewolves)* and was known to have conducted scholarly research into the origins of the [disease](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sanies_Lupinus). The Abbot of the [Temple of the Ancestor Moths](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), [Crassius Viria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crassius_Viria), was somewhat critical of Svari's commentary on the fate of a lycanthrope's [soul](Lore_Souls.md), calling her statements "poetic but misleading".

#### High King Svartr (fl. 2E 431)
**Svartr** was the first High King of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) and the Jarl of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). Following the assassination of High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), Svartr claimed that Logrolf's daughter [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md) was illegitimate, forcing a [moot](Lore_Moot.md) to be convened to determine the next ruler of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Although Freydis wore the Crown of Verity and the moot in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) chose her as High Queen, a partial moot in Solitude chose Svartr as High King. These events caused a schism, and thereafter, Skyrim was divided between Western Skyrim ruled by Svartr and his successors and [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) ruled by Freydis and her heirs.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCOF-9)</sup>

During his reign, Svartr was ineffective in fending off the frequent incursions by [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) who sought to reclaim lands they felt were rightfully theirs.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OOS-10)</sup> At some point during his reign, Svartr received a delegation from [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), one of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) gods, which presented the High King with Jarlsbane, a tablestrings.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Jarl-11)</sup> Svartr eventually died, and his descendants continued to rule Western Skyrim.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mt CHKS-12)</sup>

#### Count Sven Advensen (fl. 2E 9th century - 3E 1st century)
Sven Advensen was a Nord whom served in the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) during the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md), before later retiring from the legions, becoming Baron of [Darkmoor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackmoor). He married a lady named Inga, and fathered five sons, one son being named Jonni<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRB2-13)</sup> or Timmy.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TRB1-14)</sup> Prior to becoming nobles, Sven and his wife were apparently the distant cousins of the previous rulers of Darkmoor, and were not raised to rule. Baron Sven later became the guardian of a young [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah), the future Queen of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-BOQB-15)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRB2-13)</sup>

#### Svenja Snow-Song (?b - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Svenja Snow-Song](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Svenja_Snow-Song) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) who served as the advisor to two of the chieftains of [Thirsk](Lore_Thirsk.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Udyrfrykte](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Udefrykte) attacked and slaughtered several inhabitants of Thirsk, including the chieftain [Skjoldr Wolf-Runner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skjoldr_Wolf-Runner), before Svenja drove the beast off with arrows. When the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) arrived at Thirsk, Svenja enlisted their help in slaying the Udyrfrykte. After the Nerevarine slew the Udyrfrykte and returned to the mead hall with the beast's heart, Svenja directed them to the tomb of [Hrothmund the Red](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hrothmund_the_Red) to receive his blessing to become the new chieftain of Thirsk. After they received Hrothmund's blessing and assumed the role of Chieftain of Thirsk, Svenja became the Nerevarine's advisor, second-in-command, and overseer of the mead hall in their absence.

After the Nerevarine reportedly journeyed to [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), [Agnar the Unwavering](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Agnar_the_Unwavering) became the new chieftain of Thirsk, and Svenja became his advisor. They later married.

Svenja was haunted by the Udyrfrykte's massacre of Thirsk, which caused her to wake up night after night screaming. To confront her fears and prevent any more destruction, Svenja and Agnar traveled across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in hopes of finding and slaying the [Uderfrykte Matron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uderfrykte_Matron), the progenitor of the Udyrfrykte. Around [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a shepherd spotted it in the remote highlands of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and Svenja and Agnar then tracked it across the border into [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). In Cyrodiil, a hunter told them about the Horror of Dive Rock, which Svenja and Agnar concluded was the matron. They established a camp on the overlook at [Dive Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dive_Rock), and after three days of watching, they spotted the matron. Once they confronted the matron, Svenja was quickly struck down and devoured, forcing Agnar to retreat to the camp, where he was killed as well. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) later slew the Uderfrykte Matron and found Svenja's remains.

#### Swims-In-Deep-Water (fl. 4E 201)
[Swims-In-Deep-Water](Lore_Swims-In-Deep-Water.md) was a well-known [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) master [fisherman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fish), and a wielder of the [Fang of Haynekhtnamet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet), who worked in the [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) fishery circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). He had an eccentric love of fish and did not like seeing them come to harm. He possessed intimate knowledge of the different species of fish native to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and how to catch them. His work at the Riften fishery involved tending to the fish there, and facilitating special fishing requests and bounties from across the province. These requests were often sent by local businesses, [alchemists](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy), and Jarls. He shared this responsibility with his fellow master fisherman [Viriya](Lore_Viriya.md) up until her eventual retirement from the fishery, after which the duty of handling the distribution of fishing requests fell solely on him.

At some point he authored the *[Fishing Mastery](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fishing_Mastery)* series, a series of guidebooks dedicated to the fish of Skyrim and how to catch them.

#### Syl (?b - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Lady Syl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syl) was a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) madwoman and Duchess of [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) at the beginning of the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). She resided in the House of Dementia in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Syl was known for her extreme paranoia; her madness was such that she saw plots and conspiracies everywhere, rendering her a prisoner in her own house. She is associated with two artifacts: [Ruin's Edge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruin%27s_Edge) and [Nerveshatter](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerveshatter).

At some point, Syl had a forbidden affair with [Thadon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thadon), the Duke of Mania. When this controversy was discovered by her subjects, a conspiracy formed to assassinate Syl for associating with the enemy. In 3E 433, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to Syl's court to become a courtier of the House of Dementia. The Hero was named Grand Inquisitor and successfully uncovered the conspiracy, which included citizens of [Crucible](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crucible) as well as a member of Syl's [Dark Seducer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Seducer) personal guard.

The Hero later began the Ritual of Accession to become a Duke or Duchess of Mania or Dementia, although it is unknown if they chose to replace Syl or Thadon. Regardless, the surviving ruler promptly defected to [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag) and became a [Priest of Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Priest_of_Order), betraying the Army of Sheogorath and ultimately perishing within the tunnels of [the Fountainhead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Fountainhead).

#### Symmachus ([2E 865](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_865) - [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391))
[Symmachus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus) was the [Dark Elven](Lore_Dunmer.md) general of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s army who later became husband to [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah), Queen of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). Father to [Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Helseth) (born in [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376)) and [Morgiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morgiah) (born in [3E 384](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_384) and named after Symmachus' mother).
