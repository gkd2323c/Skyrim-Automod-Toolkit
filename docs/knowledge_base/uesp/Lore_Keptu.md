# Keptu

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/ON-crown_store-Nedic_Armor_Pack.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Nedic_Armor_Pack.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Nedic_Armor_Pack.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Nedic_Armor_Pack.jpg) Traditional Keptu armor The **Ket Keptu**, or simply Keptu,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> were an ancient race of [Men](Lore_Men.md) native to [central](Lore_Sunforge.md) [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CM12:B-2)</sup> and the hinterlands bordering [Cyrod](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), such as the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NNo RO-4)</sup> They lived in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and early [First](Lore_First_Era.md) eras,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> alongside many of the other early [Nedic peoples](Lore_Nede.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-3)</sup> Though unpolished and barbaric,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CM12:B-2)</sup> the Keptu were far from primitive,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NNot F-5)</sup> and were among the earlier pre- [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) civilizations to discover techniques for refining ores.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NNo RO-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NNot F-5)</sup> They have been described as men with tan brown skin.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Kirk-6)</sup>

## Culture
Their culture was at one point almost entirely oral, though they had developed glyphs and amulets inscribed with markings.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NNo RO-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt C-7)</sup> As the Ket Keptu furthered their knowledge of ore refinement and tattoo inks, the fiercest and most skilled warrior-smiths earned the right to wear face and body markings to signify their status.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-KKBody-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-KKFace-9)</sup>

The Ket Keptu were known to have an annual hunt competition. In one known hunt Tula the Skullcleaver and their bear Falk won the hunt, with Falk receiving markings of Hircine's favor.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The Keptu also gave their bear cubs fresh markings once each season to gain Hircine's favor.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

## History
With the aid of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOHOTR-12)</sup> they constructed the [Bloodroot Forge](Lore_Bloodroot_Forge.md) atop a large source of [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) in a secluded Jerall valley, a massive and powerful magical forge rivaling the architectural feats of the Ayleids and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). It harnessed the power of lava flows to smelt the tribe's ore and heat their metals,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NNot F-5)</sup> though it also served a more occult purpose as a temple to worship dark gods and practice dangerous rites of [Blood Magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md) with the use of iron, nirncrux, and lava.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Loading Screen-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-skin-14)</sup>

The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) enslaved many Keptu around the [Niben](Lore_Niben.md) during the early First Era before their empire fell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> Due to the usage of "Nede" as a catch-all term for Merethic Era humans,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lady Clarisse-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup> the Keptu are inconsistently described as being Nedes<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Nedic Armor Pack-15)</sup> and as a separate race from Nedes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TA-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TSOP-16)</sup> [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) brought [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) to the Nedic healer Zuathas the Clever-Cutting man, who had a Keptu name and tended to the wounds he received after a battle with the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TSOP-16)</sup>

How the Keptu people faded from prominence is unclear, but most Nedic cultures were already in decline by the time the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) set foot in [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md) circa [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Not D-17)</sup> It is suggested that they had ancient cultural interchange with the clans of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), who adopted parts of their cultural styles.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOKeptu Skull-18)</sup> Priestesses of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) around [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) painted their ankles in the same fashion as the long lost "Keptulets" circa [1E 2762](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2762).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TSe-19)</sup>

By the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), ancient Keptu sites had become ruins and were shunned by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) as places of evil.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Loading Screen-13)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a group of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) descended from the Keptu formed the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) with their [minotaur](Lore_Minotaur.md) allies after their leader [Gherig Bullblood](Lore_Gherig_Bullblood.md) met with the minotaur [Domihaus](Lore_Domihaus.md) the Bloody-Horned, who recognized his Keptu amulet.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mt C-7)</sup> Together, they used Blood Magic to reawaken the Bloodroot Forge, meddling with the long-slumbering smithies to infuse themselves with nirncrux.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOBf B-20)</sup> The Dreadhorn Clan used this power to put the Nordic city of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) under siege, but this was lifted after Domihaus fell in battle.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), dubious records of the [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) spoke of a group of men known as the Men-of-Keptu-quey that inhabited the island of [Cathnoquey](Lore_Cathnoquey.md) alongside the [Chimeri-quey](Lore_Chimeri-quey.md). They were said to have a strange obsession with drawing [bulls](Lore_Bull.md) and were recorded as living in a leaderless society in military fortresses, though this social structure was destroyed following [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_V.md)'s conquest of the major [islands](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md#Other) of the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md) in [3E 276](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_276).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PG2-22)</sup> It is unknown if these Men-of-Keptu-quey were related to the Keptu men found on Tamriel, but it has been speculated they are indeed the long-lost Nedic Keptulets of Ald Cyrod.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-PG2-22)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TSe-19)</sup>
