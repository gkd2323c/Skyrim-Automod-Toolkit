Hackwings are described as both large [birds](Lore_Bird.md) and flying [lizards](Lore_Lizard.md) with saw-like beaks, both expressions which are often interchanged.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KRM-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Endv Rep-3)</sup> They travel in large flocks and have the tendency to swarm when they feel threatened. They strike quickly in an attempt to wound or knock down their prey. They will fly off after wounding their victim, only to come back when the victim is almost dead from blood loss. Their victims are then ripped apart with the hackwings' saw-like beaks.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-KRM-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CD-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAA2-4)</sup> Hackwings are most common in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), but their range also extends along the [southern coast](Lore_Southern_Elsweyr.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

They are hunted for their thick, supple, and strong leather hides for use in crafting armor and furniture. Armor made from their hides facilitates quick movement while still allowing the wearer to withstand brutal attacks, qualities which are invaluable to the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) hunters of the [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CD-2)</sup> They have a leathery "horn" on the top of their head that is referred to as a plumage.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Despite appearances, hackwings are capable of producing feathers.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LHF-7)</sup>

Hackwing anatomy is very similar to the [cliff strider](Lore_Cliff_Strider.md), and they may be possibly related to them. Some historians theorize the relation may have resulted from the [Dark Elves](Lore_Dunmer.md) raiding Black Marsh for thousands of years to bring back things they deemed valuable, such as slaves, livestock, and critters.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LAMQA-8)</sup>

Hackwings can be trained using a variety of tools such as a 'padded bludgeon' and 'wooden whistle'.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Weather vanes in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) were sometimes crafted in the shape of hackwings.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
