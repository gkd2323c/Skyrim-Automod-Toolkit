—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Alinor is one of the eight major cities in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARMap-4)</sup> found on the river highlands of the Oleander Coast. Alinor is the ancient capital of the Summerset Isles and the seat of [High Elven](Lore_Altmer.md) civilization. It is widely recognized by its sky-piercing towers and intricately woven ramparts that are designed to catch the light of the sun to break it into a variety of colors.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> For thousands of years, it has been the home of the High Kingship of Summerset, who sits at the highest point of the city and surveys across their grand domain.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBIAG-6)</sup> The city is the namesake of the province's modern-day name, chosen by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) as they assumed power over the country.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGW-7)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-place-Alinor_Docks_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Alinor_Docks_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Alinor_Docks_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Alinor_Docks_02.jpg) Alinor Docks [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/ON-furnishing-Alinor_Banner%2C_Hanging.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Alinor_Banner%2C_Hanging.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Alinor_Banner,_Hanging.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Alinor_Banner,_Hanging.jpg) The Banner of Alinor Alinor is a city of great prestige, known commonly as the *"forest city of Summerset"*.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AR-8)</sup> It is located in the temperate southwest coast of Summerset Isle, between the southern mountains and the idyllic beaches. Much of the land that surrounds it is lightly forested but steep and ridged. There are rows of vineyards found outside of the city walls and a great river runs through Alinor's southern portion and empties into the [Sea of Pearls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_of_Pearls).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONAlinor-9)</sup>

Alinor is built on the slopes of the mountains. The lowest level of the city is the main street, which goes along the wall and toward the river. Stairways ascend higher toward the second level where the marketplace and even the Temple of the Divines are located. Reaching higher and higher will eventually lead to the Alinor Royal Palace, where the whole city can be surveyed from a wide vantage point.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONAlinor-9)</sup>

Despite the fact that the city is landlocked, the Alinor Docks are a bustling trading hub, located down the road north from the actual city, closer to the central bay of the island. Alinor is widely recognized for the impossibly high towers and ramparts. While information regarding the entire island is sparse for the mainland, most of it regarding High Elven culture and civilization comes from the human traders who were only given access to the Alinor Docks than anywhere else.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONAlinor-9)</sup> Some accounts of Alinor city described it eloquently as *"made from glass and insect wings"*.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> The southern city, [Sunhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunhold) is considered Alinor's sister city, and it is believed that if these two metropolises did not have the mountains separating them, then they would inevitably merge together to form a great city, the likes of which have not been seen since Lost [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBIAG-6)</sup> Alinor is neighbored by several settlements, including Marnor Keep to the southeast and Riverwatch to the northeast.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARMap-4)</sup>

## History

### The Kingdom of Alinor and the Summerset Isles
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/ON-place-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg/200px-ON-place-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg) The Alinor Royal Palace [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/81/LO-race-Altmer.png/200px-LO-race-Altmer.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Altmer.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Altmer.png) The High Elves For many known years, there has been a city called Alinor and from there, the High King of Summerset had always ruled the province. It is said that the High Kingship of Alinor has always been held by a single dynasty that has dated back to the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONLoad Palace-13)</sup> By the mid-Mythic Era, the [Heartlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heartlands) of what is now modern-day [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) was apparently tribute-land to the High King of Alinor. It later became the new home of the proto- [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) and the epicenter of their [vast empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BTAOM-14)</sup>

When [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) communed with [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) in the rainbow shadows of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), he wrote the Velothi Prophecies and garnered followers among Aldmer clans, creating a schism. That schism was outright prohibited by the Alinoran [Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapiarchs) but Veloth's followers persisted onward and eventually left the Summerset Isles for a [new home](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DWTC-15)</sup>

For the longest time, history regarding Alinor or even the Summerset Isles was unclear to the rest of Tamriel since contact from anything on the mainland was heavily restricted. The world-changing events that transpired in Tamriel had barely, if not at all affected the Isles and any little bit of information came from human traders only relegated to the Alinor Docks, or the ambassadors of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), who were given access to Alinor at large.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup>

One of the only known exceptions to this rule was Alinor's involvement in the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md). The fleet's construction was centralized in their harborage in [the Systres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Systres) but places such as Alinor and [Rivenspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rivenspire) contributed greatly to the navy's assembly throughout the [1E 2240 s](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2240).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SH3-16)</sup> Excluding large-scale events that involved the mainland, much of Summerset's history has been generalized as internal conflicts between rival cities that later evolved into full-scale warfare. Alinor and [Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lillandril), in particular, had conflicted throughout the years,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-17)</sup> like in the Alinor-Lillandril skirmishes in [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONEye-18)</sup>

Some of the province's more serious and deadly battles involved either the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) or the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-17)</sup> One such example occurred in [2E 486](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_486) when High King [Hidellith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hidellith) confronted a small Maormeri fleet off the coast of Alinor. As the fleet retreated, the High King followed suit and came upon uncharted waters not far from the island-kingdom, [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md). There his army was caught in an ambush and only one ship carrying Hidellith was able to escape the onslaught.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TMOP-19)</sup> The High King's survival had miraculously kept the royal family alive for much longer.

### The Unforeseen Queen and the Aldmeri Dominion
It eventually came time for Hidellith to conceive a child that would become the heir-apparent of Summerset. Under auspicious and inexplicable circumstances, the quick-witted and unorthodox [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) was born on the 5th of [Second Seed](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Second_Seed) in [2E 555](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_555). In her youth, she was a prodigy and eager to learn, often taking to her own independent studies. As it is custom for every monarch, in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573), Ayrenn was to be inducted into the Sapiarchs' Labyrinth to study Altmeri Regal Praxis and Ceremoniarchy for three-thousand five-hundred and fifty-five days. She disappeared not long after and not even the Thalmor were able to find her in all of Summerset. Time kept on moving and in her stead, Prince [Naemon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naemon) studied in the Sapiarchs' Labyrinth as Ayrenn explored the world and witnessed what it had to offer. When Hidellith passed away in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Ayrenn returned home and claimed her birthright as the High Queen of Summerset.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ATUQ-20)</sup>

In that same year, she provided support against the [Colovian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Colovian) incursion that harassed [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md)'s northern region<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AUA:AD1-21)</sup> and sent waves of assistance to [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) after their run-in with the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TEat C-22)</sup> Once these issues were dealt with, the leaders of these three respective nations, Queen Ayrenn, High King [Aeradan Camoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aeradan_Camoran), and the Speaker of the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md), Gharesh-ri signed the Elden Accord and formed the first-iteration of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOAyrenn-23)</sup> The Dominion had soon joined the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) that had raged continuously throughout central Cyrodiil and later established their grand capital in [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root). Ayrenn was forced to leave Alinor for the mainland, where she can properly maintain focus on the war effort and in her stead, her cousin, [Alwinarwe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alwinarwe) sat on the Throne of Alinor and was named Proxy-Queen of Summerset.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MTCPQA-24)</sup>

### The End of Isolation and the Court of Bedlam
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/80/ON-npc-Proxy_Queen_Alwinarwe.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Proxy_Queen_Alwinarwe.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Proxy_Queen_Alwinarwe.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Proxy_Queen_Alwinarwe.jpg) The Proxy-Queen of Alinor, Alwinarwe [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg/200px-ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Crystal_Tower.jpg) The Crystal Tower In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Queen Ayrenn had officially proclaimed that Summerset Isle will no longer seal itself off and now open itself to the rest of the world.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AEt I-25)</sup> It brought a great deal of controversy among the people of the province, but regardless, mainlanders came in droves and established themselves across the island. One of its greatest critics was the Kinlady of [Shimmerene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shimmerene), Avinisse, who saw Ayrenn's decision as a comprise of Summerset's civilized climate, much like her other choices throughout her two-year reign.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ARo OB-26)</sup>

Various people from all corners of Tamriel traveled to the capital city and explored the grand streets. Even delegations from far away, such as the Eastern Kingdom of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) came to Alinor to seek an audience with the Proxy-Queen.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONCulture-27)</sup> But despite all of this, devious machinations had begun to spur across Summerset and put the entire continent at a great risk.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONValsirenn-28)</sup>

The [Court of Bedlam](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Court_of_Bedlam) had undergone operations across the Summerset Isles and became entangled with the group of [Sea Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Sload) called the Abyssal Cabal. The [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) came into Summerset to investigate the island and warn the [College of Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Sapiarchs) about the court's target, the Crystal Tower. They sent a [newcomer](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to the Alinor Royal Palace to seek out the Proxy-Queen's permission to enter the college.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONTower-29)</sup>

Eventually, the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) of Darkness, [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) had seized control of the [Heart of Transparent Law](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Transparent_Law), taking the Daedra Lord one more step closer to claiming the Crystal Tower. To prepare for what may happen, the [Eyes of the Queen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eyes_of_the_Queen), [Razum-dar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Razum-dar) traveled to Alinor, in the hopes to warn the Proxy-Queen and prepare the city for whatever came their way.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ONAlliance-30)</sup> In the end, Nocturnal and her champion were quashed and Tamriel was saved from their plan. But in order to prevent such an event from happening again, the Psijic Order, the College of Sapiarchs, and the Court of Alinor gathered at the Royal Palace, to proclaim an alliance that would defend the Crystal Tower at all costs.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONNew-31)</sup>

### Alinor and the Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_%28Morrowind_Codex%29.png/200px-LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_%28Morrowind_Codex%29.png)](File_LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_(Morrowind_Codex).png.md) [](File_LO-map-Aldmeri_Dominion_(Morrowind_Codex).png.md) Map of the Second Aldmeri Dominion [![](https://images.uesp.net/8/89/RG-sign-Empire.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-sign-Empire.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-sign-Empire.png) Banner of the Third Empire (ca. 2E 864) As time kept going, the First Aldmeri Dominion had dissolved and the relations between the Summerset Isles and the mainland had reverted. Alinor had continued to remain open for many years, that was until roughly [2E 814](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_814) when its ports were closed for any foreigners. By [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830), the heirs of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) in Valenwood began fighting over the throne. When a certain faction made peace with their Colovian neighbors their numbers increased greatly and brought the throne in their favor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup>

That was until the Summerset Isles, under the command of their High King of Alinor, invaded the forest province. Using ancient stewardship written thousands of years prior, they placed the Thalmor into Valenwood on behalf of their own claimant, [Camoran Anaxemes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camoran_Anaxemes). With their new King, the Wood Elves joined forces with the High Elves to create the Second Aldmeri Dominion. Their power became immeasurable and soon interfered with the growing [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) under the command of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). The [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) began to take a southern front as it expanded.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup>

By [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the warfront had reached the [Blue Divide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Divide) and the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The Empire had sent the Grand Vizier and Septim's advisor, [Zurin Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zurin_Arctus) to conduct negotiations with the High King of Alinor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup> By then, the Aldmeri Dominion was far from Tiber Septim's mind for conquest. It was at this time, that he had conquered [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and later had the land recognized as an Imperial Province, only to soon shift focus toward the northeast. By the end of it, Tiber Septim received [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), the Brass-God of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), and used its power to end the Aldmeri Dominion. The automaton stomped its way through the Dominion and eventually the Summerset Isles,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TAH-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-BLAreldur-33)</sup> arriving not far from Alinor. The city surrendered within an hour.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-34)</sup> Tiber Septim's conquest over all of Tamriel had come to an end by [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), paving the way for the Third Era.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-35)</sup>

In [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), the kings of the Summerset Isles formed a unified alliance to combat the Maormer<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-35)</sup> in their last-known invasion of the province, the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-2)</sup> Despite having assistance from Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) and the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy), the Maormer were defeated because of a supernatural storm conjured accredited to the Psijic Order.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-35)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TWQ5-36)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Alinor was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Coreriil and had a rivalry with [Lillandril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lillandril).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AR-8)</sup> The Summerset Isles were left in ruins after the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). Amidst the confusion, the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) rose to prominence across the province as the rest of the Empire was standing on thin ice. The situation exploded further in [4E 10](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_10) when the Potentate, [Ocato of Firsthold](Lore_Ocato_of_Firsthold.md) was assassinated and the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md) had spanned across the central province. In light of this, the Thalmor had overthrown the monarchy of Summerset in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22). The High Kingship had fallen and the Thalmor's law was accepted widely across the isles.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-RTIV-37)</sup> They renamed the country to "Alinor", to callback to a time before [man](Lore_Man.md) took over.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TGW-7)</sup>

### Known Rulers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/ON-interior-Alinor_Royal_Palace_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Alinor_Royal_Palace_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Alinor_Royal_Palace_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Alinor_Royal_Palace_02.jpg) The Alinor Royal Palace main chamber Since as far as the Merethic Era, the **Throne of Alinor** has been occupied by a single royal bloodline, up to at least Queen Ayrenn.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONLoad Palace-13)</sup> Their symbol depicts the [Aldmeri Eagle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eagle), which also represents [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), the chief divine of the elves and the First Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ONRegal-38)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONTreasure Idol-39)</sup> The **Court of Alinor** (also called the **High Court of Alinor**)<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LT-40)</sup> is the council that serves the monarch.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1E-5)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ATUQ-20)</sup> When a new monarch inherits the throne, they must travel to [Tanzelwil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tanzelwil), vast ruins on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) to commune with the dead and seek their approval to the throne.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-41)</sup>

The royal heirs of Alinor travel north to the College of Sapiarch to study in the Sapiarchs' Labyrinth before they can become the High King or Queen. They have to study Altmeri Regal Praxis and Ceremoniarchy for three thousand five hundred and fifty-five days before they are crowned.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-OTCOS-42)</sup>

For the longest time, the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) served as counselors to the rulers of the Summerset Isles. If anyone wanted their counsel, their best method was either through a select few places or the kings themselves. They continued this service up until their island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) first vanished in the Second Era, around when the Mages Guild was formed. When they returned several years later for the Third Era, they continued where they left off<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-FOA-43)</sup> until Artaeum disappeared a second time sometime in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) for whatever reason.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-SRTolfdir-44)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png/200px-ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Prismatic_Sunbird_Feather_02.png) A Prismatic Sunbird Feather [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/ON-item-Lilytongue.jpg/200px-ON-item-Lilytongue.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Lilytongue.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Lilytongue.jpg) Lilytongue *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Altmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Altmer.md#Society_and_Religion)** sections on the Altmer page.* Since time immemorial, master harp players would play an Aldmeri harp called Lilytongue for the High Court of Alinor. Some of the most well-known performances from this harp were from the great composer, Rolmelval but the harp was eventually lost during the [War of the Uvichil](Lore_War_of_the_Uvichil.md),<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LT-40)</sup> which took place between [1E 2911](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2911) and [1E 2917](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2917).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE3ES-17)</sup> It later re-surfaced in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where it was put on display in the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-LT-40)</sup>

The Alinor Allemande is an ancient dance that is noted for its painstaking notations.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ONAllemande Anti-49)</sup> The Grand Masquerade of Alinor is an event that only takes place every one-hundred and fifty years.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ONContraband-50)</sup> The Alinor Eye Crest, an accessory that was originally given to those who serviced the Summerset nobility saw wider use in the Alinor-Lillandril skirmishes of the Second Era. Alinor warriors wore it to identify themselves from their enemy.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONEye-18)</sup>

Alinor is the setting of a fictional book written by [Waughin Jarth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Waughin_Jarth) called [Feyfolken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Feyfolken). It revolved around the mute Alinoran scribe, Thaurbad Hulzik, who lived the life of a hermit and wrote for the Temple of Auri-El bulletin board. When the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) had first arrived in Alinor, he sent his messenger to have his quill enchanted. The quill was enchanted with a [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) spirit named Feyfolken and it wrote the most illuminating pieces of literature and art last seen since Gallael. Thaurbad relished in the praise and admiration from viewers of the bulletin board but over time, realized he had nothing to do with the writing and only bore witness to whatever the quill itself wrote.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-FF2-51)</sup> Thaurbad tried many times to get rid of the quill but over time, became deranged from Feyfolken's constant changes and threats to his life. Eventually, the scribe died in a fire and the quill moved to its next wielder.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FF3-52)</sup>

The [Sun Birds of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun_Birds_of_Alinor) was an organization of Aldmeri explorers from the Merethic Era.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ONFeather Anti-53)</sup> They were among the few people to attempt exploration beyond [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and into [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius). Just like the explorers of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md), the faction dissolved at the same time because the amount of [magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magicka) required for this feat was extraordinary.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-PGE3ESM-54)</sup> They were, however, able to pierce the veil between Mundus and Aetherius with raw magic from the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ONFeather Anti-53)</sup> Under the Third Empire, Alinor was a major port in the [East Empire Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:East_Empire_Company)'s southwest line. It is connected to the cities, [Dusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dusk), [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md), and [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai_(city).md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-SRMap-55)</sup> Delicate midnight ink is a mainstay in proper Alinor calligraphy.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-TCVII-56)</sup>
