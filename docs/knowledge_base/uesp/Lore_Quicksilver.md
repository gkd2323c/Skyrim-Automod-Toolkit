# Quicksilver

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d3/SR-activity-Mining_%28Quicksilver%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Quicksilver%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Quicksilver).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Quicksilver).jpg) An exposed Quicksilver seam Quicksilver (or **Mercury**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DGMercury-1)</sup> is a silver metal used by blacksmiths and craftsmen. A freshly exposed surface of quicksilver had a blueish-white color and when extracted produced quicksilver ore. This ore could further be smelted into ingots.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRQuicksilver-2)</sup> During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), quicksilver ore deposits could be found almost anywhere in Tamriel.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONQuicksilver-3)</sup> Quicksilver was relatively rare throughout [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), and the province was home to only one known quicksilver mine.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SREdith-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRQmine-5)</sup>

Quicksilver is used by blacksmiths to forge various arms and armor. It melts at a much lower heat than [Moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md), but with some skill can be mixed with it to forge Elven arms and armor.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAF-6)</sup> Similarly, it can be mixed with Ebony and Steel to forge Nordic arms and armor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRQuicksilver-2)</sup> Quicksilver armor and weapons vary in strength. During the mid-Second Era, blacksmiths managed to make it surpass the likes of [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) in quality.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> This is due to the [Guild Act](Lore_Guild_Act.md) which allowed for a greater diffusion of knowledge about exotic materials. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in particular are credited for learning how to refine such magical ores as a result.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MAMA-8)</sup> However, by the Fourth Era, the norm was that quicksilver armor was slightly weaker than that made of [Chaurus](Lore_Chaurus.md) chitin.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

As a liquid metal, hitting or holding a single drop of quicksilver is said to be quite difficult.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DFClasses-10)</sup> It also has alchemical properties and can be used to increase speed.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DGMercury-1)</sup>

## Notes
- During the background questions in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, choosing the childhood nickname of "Quicksilver" would grant +6 points to the Running skill.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFBackground-11)</sup>
- While the names mercury and quicksilver were used interchangeably in *Daggerfall*, subsequent games have since dropped the name mercury altogether (save for the [Mercury Shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Mercury_Shield) in *Oblivion*), presumably due to its [namesake origins](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercury_(mythology)) in real-world [Roman mythology](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_mythology).
