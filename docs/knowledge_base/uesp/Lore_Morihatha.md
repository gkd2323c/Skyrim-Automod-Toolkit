Morihatha Septim was the nineteenth [empress](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for nineteen years, from [3E 320](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_320) to [3E 339](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_339). During her reign, Morihatha began the efforts to reunify [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and end the revolutions and civil wars that had plagued the Empire since the days of her grandfather [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> She was considered the first in a line of particularly strong and capable rulers of Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3All-2)</sup> She was preceded by her brother, [Uriel VI](Lore_Uriel_VI.md), and was succeeded by her nephew, [Pelagius IV](Lore_Pelagius_IV.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup>

## Biography
Morihatha Septim was born in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288) to Emperor [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_V.md) and his morganatic spouse. She and her twin sister, [Eloisa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eloisa_Septim), were born a month after Uriel V left to invade [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md). Through her father, Morihatha had an older half-brother named [Uriel](Lore_Uriel_VI.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> In [3E 290](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_290), Uriel V was killed at the Battle of [Ionith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ionith) in Akavir, so Morihatha's brother took the [Ruby Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne) as Uriel VI.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTET-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RDa I-5)</sup>

In Uriel VI's efforts to subdue the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), Morihatha was his staunchest ally, especially after her marriage to Baron [Ulfe Gersen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulfe_Gersen) of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) brought her considerable wealth and influence. At twenty-five years old, Morihatha was described by (admittedly self-serving) diplomats as the most beautiful creature in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). She was also regarded as well-learned, vivacious, athletic, and a well-practiced politician.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> In [3E 320](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_320), Uriel VI died after falling from his horse and could not be saved by the finest Imperial healers, so Morihatha was crowned Empress of Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTET-4)</sup>

Morihatha brought [Welloc](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welloc),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RTBS-6)</sup> the Archmagister of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), to the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and appointed the second [Imperial Battlemage](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) since the days of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> Morihatha, like Uriel VI and her two successors, [Pelagius IV](Lore_Pelagius_IV.md) and [Uriel VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md), was suspicious enough of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) to refuse to allow their ambassadors from the [Isle of Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) within the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-FOA-7)</sup> In [3E 331](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_331), she commissioned a second edition of the [Pocket Guide to the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTET-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3F-8)</sup> which became known as the Morihatha Edition.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE3A-9)</sup>

Morihatha finished the work that Uriel VI had begun and made the [Imperial Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) a true government under the emperor. Outside of Cyrodiil, however, the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) had been slowly disintegrating. Revolutions and civil wars had plagued the Empire since the days of her grandfather [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> In one known rebellion during Morihatha's reign, Welloc cast a Vampiric Cloud spell on the rebel army of [Blackrose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackrose), which sapped them of their strength and skill and transferred it to their opponents.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RTBS-6)</sup> By carefully coordinating her attacks, Morihatha slowly took back her rebellious vassals and always avoided overextending herself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup>

Although Morihatha's military campaigns were successful, her deliberate pace often frustrated the Elder Council. One councilman, an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) named [Thoricles Romus](Lore_Thoricles_Romus.md), furious at her refusal to send troops to his troubled home province of Black Marsh, is believed to have hired the assassins who killed Morihatha in [3E 339](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_339). Romus was summarily tried and executed, though he protested his innocence to the last.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup> Morihatha had no surviving children, and Eloisa had died of a fever in [3E 335](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_335), leading to Eloisa's twenty-five-year-old son, Pelagius, taking the throne as Pelagius IV.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE4-1)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDC-10)</sup>
