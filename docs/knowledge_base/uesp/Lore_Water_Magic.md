# Water Magic

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Lakemire_Xanmeer_Manor.jpg) Viewing windows created by Argonian water magic Water Magic is an obscure and little-known form of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) involving the manipulation of water.

On [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), it is believed water is composed of memory. When a mortal dies, their memories become water, and in this way all the memories of Tamriel's histories are stored within its waters. This fact is considered to be one of the most dangerous secrets about the natural order of the world, and is therefore closely guarded by the Demon of Knowledge [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). Achieving a true understanding of this principle requires a lifetime of study and contemplation, but in the wrong hands this knowledge can be extremely dangerous.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> For example, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the water spirit of [Halcyon Lake](Lore_Halcyon_Lake.md), a [nereid](Lore_Nereid.md) named Lorelia, attempted to twist the natural order by stealing the memories of her mortal followers to prevent the desertification of her lake, forcing the servants of Hermaeus Mora to intervene.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> The burial grounds of [Honor's Rest](Lore_Honor%27s_Rest.md) had memorial pools that allowed one to view the past through the eyes of those involved in the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup>

### Argonians
[Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) are known to utilize water magic. One example of this is the Lakemire Xanmeer located within the [Deepmire](Lore_Deepmire.md) region of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), where an Argonian master of water magic created subaquatic viewing windows into a nearby lake composed entirely of water.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

### Ayleids
The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) of the [Strident Coast](Lore_Strident_Coast.md) utilized a weapon called the Sphere of Storms, which manipulated the waters around them to either defend their coastline, or provided for the people such as water crops or speed ships through the sea. One of the last spheres are currently used by the Stormwardens of the [Ilayas Ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ilayas_Ruins), to protect the [Broken Coast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Broken_Coast) region of [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONCirnean-5)</sup>

### Mages Guild
The [Order of the Eye](Lore_Order_of_the_Eye.md) are known to use scrying-bowls with water to utilize their [sight](Lore_Sight.md). Sometimes bog-water is used in the ritual, but other times they use tea water because the leaves and seeds can apparently help break through persistent visions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-NN-6)</sup>

### Maormer
The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) are known to have powerful water magic users amidst their sailors and captains. One such captain was capable of using jets and spouts of water to quickly move around, and could summon forth large waves of water.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CN-7)</sup> The Maormer once created an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) known as the Mourning Stone, which acted as an endless source of water. The seemingly infinite supply of water provided by the artifact was used to bind the spirit of a powerful necromancer named [Uldor](Lore_Uldor.md) to his body, forcing him to remain sealed in his prison within the Temple of the Mourning Springs on [Khenarthi's Roost](Lore_Khenarthi%27s_Roost.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESORU-8)</sup>

### Sea Giants
[Sea Giants](Lore_Sea_Giant.md) are capable of wielding powerful magic to control water. Examples of such magic include flinging orbs of sea water at their foes and guiding surges of water to crash down on their enemies.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOYsmgar-9)</sup>

### Sload
[Sea Sloads](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) have also been known to use water in their magical rituals. During their attempt to sink [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) into the ocean circa 2E 582, the Abyssal Cabal and their allies in the [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md) used abyssal pearls to create magical geysers across the island.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The Sea Sloads grew these pearls through arcane rituals, which involved drawing sigils in the water and adding alchemical mixtures to make the sea glow with arcane light.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> However, the pearls themselves were powered through some form of [Soul Magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

### Reachmen
The tale of *The Sister Who Turned Her Skin Red*, a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) legend, mentions the witch Ulecia, who wielded powerful Water Magic. According to the tale, she was capable of feats such as summoning a river of water powerful enough to uproot a tree and altering the taste of the water to that of [mead](Lore_Mead.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-STODB3-13)</sup>
