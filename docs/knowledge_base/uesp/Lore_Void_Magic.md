# Void Magic

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/ON-skill-Void_Trap.jpg/200px-ON-skill-Void_Trap.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Void_Trap.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-skill-Void_Trap.jpg) A Void Magic trap [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/ON-npc-Rakkhat_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Rakkhat_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Rakkhat_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Rakkhat_02.jpg) A Void Sphere in formation Void magic<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Name-1)</sup> is an [arcane art](Lore_Magic.md) associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) and her followers. Its spells typically resemble [Void](Lore_Void.md) energy, glowing in a faded gray-green light,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Void Energy-2)</sup> though exceptionally powerful incantations do not possess this trait and instead shine radiant blue.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Void Sphere-3)</sup> Some Void magic can be cast at any time like other schools, while some forms involve the manipulation of voidstuff.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rites-4)</sup> Practitioners of the art are known as **void mages**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Void Mage-5)</sup> and are most frequently [witches](Lore_Witch.md) of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rites-4)</sup> Overuse of Void magic can cause a condition known as Void Pathosis, darkening the skin of the afflicted and giving them glowing white eyes.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Belain-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Pathosis-7)</sup> Alternatively, overexposure to the magic can turn those hit into [shades](Lore_Shade.md), twisting their [souls](Lore_Soul.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Gwendis-8)</sup>

Select members of the [Ghostsong Clan](Lore_Ghostsong_Clan.md) of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) were known practitioners of Void magic. These users worshipped the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md), an artifact associated with Namira interred far beneath [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Those who were "chosen" by the Heart became amplified in their magic, becoming [voidmothers](Lore_Voidmother.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Nathari-9)</sup> beings empowered by the energy of the Void.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rites-4)</sup> Rituals involving the summoning of Voidmothers can involve mortal sacrifices. Some Reachfolk view these rituals as an abomination, formed from twisting and abusing Namira's creation.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Gwenyfe-10)</sup> [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Harvesters](Lore_Harvester.md) utilize Void magic to break the wills of their victims and capture their souls.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-EGTDo C-11)</sup>

Experiments on the Dark Heart in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) from the [Nighthollow Clan](Lore_Nighthollow_Clan.md) and [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) led to the discovery of void portals, a method of [magical transportation](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md). Void portals summon small pockets of void energy which, when entered, can transport the user short distances, so long as they carry a void shard.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Verandis-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-VP-13)</sup> Those with a connection to the Void are able to create and control Void portals.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Verandis-12)</sup> Controlled Void portals are capable of summoning creatures.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Welwa-14)</sup> The [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md) experimented with Void magic, building several apparatus intended to stabilize Void portals.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Verandis-12)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TCA-15)</sup> [Staves](Lore_Staves.md) may also be used to channel Void magic.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Idrisin-16)</sup>
