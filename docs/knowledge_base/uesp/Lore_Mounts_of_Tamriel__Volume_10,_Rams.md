Mounts of Tamriel: Volume 10, Rams **by [Fadus Falto](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Fadus_Falto) of Skingrad** *The author describes his experience riding a ram* *
---
Of all the beasts I rode in research for this series, it was the stout [ram](Lore_Ram.md) that surprised me most of all. I have sat astride snarling wolves, mighty bears, even mouldering [constructs](Lore_Construct.md) from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s distant past. But the [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) river ram a kind [skald](Lore_Skald.md) allowed me to canter up and down a cobbled [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) street absolutely terrified me.

At first the mount was all gentle movements and sure steps. But halfway up the street it turned its head to regard me with one of those flat, strange eyes. Whatever it saw in my countenance it did not like, for it took off like an arrow into the heart of the bustling market. My companion was left far behind as the beast crashed through produce carts, seamstress stalls, and the [wares](Lore_Honey.md) of a beekeeper who hissed curses in the [tongue](Lore_Jel.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) as I left crushed wares and sticky glass in my wake.

Talking with my friend later that evening, wearing fresh clothes and lighter a tidy stack of [coins](Lore_Coins.md) to repay for the damages, he related that this is apparently an all too common experience. Rams, unlike many other creatures in this study, are domesticated in only the loosest sense. So if you ever find yourself astride a well-behaved ram, consider yourself fortunate. You ride at their pleasure.
