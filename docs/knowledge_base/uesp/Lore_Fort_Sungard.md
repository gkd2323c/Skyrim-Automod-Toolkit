[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/SR-place-Fort_Sungard.jpg/200px-SR-place-Fort_Sungard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Sungard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Fort_Sungard.jpg) Fort Sungard circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Fort Sungard (or simply called **Sungard** or **Sunguard**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Rislav-1)</sup> is a fortified settlement located in the [Tundra Plain](Lore_Tundra_Plain.md) on the edge of the [Tundra Plateau](Lore_Tundra_Plateau.md) of the region known as [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup> situated in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Fort Sungard is a historical bulwark at the tripoint of the Reach, [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md), and [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) that is a key-strategic point in southern Skyrim, for the likes of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) in the northwest, and the rest of southern Skyrim. It is famed for its incredible vista, which overlooks the [Karth River Canyon](Lore_Karth_River.md) from the plateau.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Skyrim Cut Dialogue-3)</sup> Also, Sunguard was at times the name of a town in southeastern Skyrim on the road between [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) and [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/ON-crown_store-Sungard_Sabre_Cat.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Sungard_Sabre_Cat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Sungard_Sabre_Cat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Sungard_Sabre_Cat.jpg) Sungard Sabre Cat [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/AR-place-Sunguard.jpg/200px-AR-place-Sunguard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Sunguard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Sunguard.jpg) The settlement of Sunguard ca. [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) The fort was constructed by the Empire long ago, though it is unclear if this refers to the [First Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) or a [Cyrodilic Empire](Lore_Empire.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Skyrim Cut Dialogue-3)</sup> In the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the [Battle of Sungard](Lore_Battle_of_Sungard.md) served to be a key point in Skyrim's conflict against the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), at the time under the command of [Gorieus](Lore_Gorieus.md). The conflict at Sungard had led to the death of the High King of Skyrim, [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md). Because of this defeat, Emperor Gorieus' forces were able to take control of southern Skyrim, the one that it had originally lost. Following his death, the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) was convened, naming Kjoric's son, [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md) the next High King of Skyrim. Hoag's forces joined [Ryain Direnni](Lore_Ryain_Direnni.md) in his conflict against the Alessians at the [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Rislav-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup> After the battle, a local species of [sabre cats](Lore_Sabre_Cat.md) moved into the area. Scholars at the time felt that some element of the battle disturbed their normal habitat, and attracted them to the former battlefield.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONSungard-6)</sup>

In the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the young [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) had moved into Sunguard, on her way towards Riften where she would meet [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) and eventually become the Queen of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the town of Sunguard <sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_Reach)</sup> was still active, being ruled by Prince Mojarik. It had a rivalry with [Vernim Wood](Lore_Vernim_Wood.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Arena-4)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Greenwall](Lore_Greenwall.md) in the southeast, [Nimalten City](Lore_Nimalten_City.md) in the northwest, and Reich Corigate in the northeast.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Map Arena-8)</sup>

By the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Fort Sungard was still around, playing a role in the Reach's involvement in the Civil War. It was used by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) as their bulwark against the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) in the Reach, should the latter gain control over Fort Sungard, they would have control over the Reach.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

## Notes

But when Sunguard was retconned into Fort Sungard, it was moved to the other side of the province. Instead, the Sunguard location was renamed or replaced by [Fort Amol](Lore_Fort_Amol.md). |
