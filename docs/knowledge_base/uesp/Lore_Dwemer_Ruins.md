# Dwemer Ruins

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/SR-place-Mzulft.jpg/200px-SR-place-Mzulft.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mzulft.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Mzulft.jpg) [Mzulft](Lore_Mzulft.md), a Dwemer ruin in Skyrim [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/MW-misc-Website Background.jpg/200px-MW-misc-Website Background.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Website Background.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Website Background.jpg) Dwemer ruins in Vvardenfell Dwemer ruins are all that remain of the underground cities and strongholds of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). They are found everywhere in Tamriel, being a common sight in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWGen Ruins-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup> (especially [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md)), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup> and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RG-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup> with some also present in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DIv I-6)</sup> Most of these ruins are guarded by functional and self-repairing [Dwemer constructs](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md).

Many Dwemer settlements continue to be inhabited even after the disappearance of the Dwarves. They often serve as lairs for outcasts such as bandits and [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md), while some, such as [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md), have been renovated and function as modern settlements. In Skyrim, the twisted [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) continually reclaim the ancient ruins of their former masters.

The [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) established a law that protected Dwemer artifacts salvaged from ruins as property of the [Emperor](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) and thus banned from trade. The Empire's goal was to learn the knowledge from the Dwemer's ancient technological treasure troves and use it to improve their own armaments with steam-power and magicka to create inventions such as steam-driven carriages and [airborne dirigibles](Lore_Air_Transportation.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RGLakene-7)</sup> Despite this, people regularly hunt for these artifacts in Morrowind.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWGen Ruins-1)</sup> The most valuable treasures are Dwarven weapons and armor, both for their craftsmanship and value to collectors and scholars.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWGen Artifacts-8)</sup> Some sites can be claimed by the [Imperial Archaeological Society](Lore_Imperial_Archaeological_Society.md) per the Dwarven Reclamation Act of [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-NT-9)</sup>

## Structure
The structure of Dwemer ruins varies with location, with those located on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) being significantly different from those found on the rest of Tamriel. Vvardenfell ruins are typically shallow, with many civic and operational buildings located close to the surface. The ruins on the mainland are usually deeper, with minor warehouses and passageways located near the surface. In these ruins, the actual settlement is hidden deep underground, beneath a certain threshold referred to as the "geocline".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DIv I-6)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DIv III-10)</sup>

Dwemer ruins often contain structures like the Arcanex, where magical objects and text were stored, or the Great Animoculotories, which acted as factories of centurions and other constructs. Some cities, like [Bthardamz](Lore_Bthardamz.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), were partially built into natural caverns, referred to as the "Deep Venues". The largest of those could even contain underground roads.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DIv II-11)</sup> Dwemer architecture consists mainly of [Dwarven metal](Lore_Dwarven_Metal.md) and stone. It often incorporates subterranean magma in order to power the machinery and intricate mechanisms found within.

The tombs of Dwarven kings and nobles have been discovered throughout the years, with the most famous examples being from Ransom's expedition into Hammerfell. But the first time a completely intact tomb was discovered was by the [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) archaeologist, Rolard Nordssen, during his expedition of [Kemel-Ze](Lore_Kemel-Ze.md) in eastern Morrowind.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Ro KZ-12)</sup>

### [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)
Morrowind is home to the oldest Dwemer sites and was known to the Dwemer as *Dwemereth*. The Dwarven Kingdom of Vvardenfell was the sole holdout against the First Empire of the Nords as they conquered mainland Morrowind. The Nords were eventually overthrown during the War of Succession due to an alliance between [Dumac Dwarfking](Lore_Dumac.md) and [Indoril Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md), allowing the Dwemer and [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) to form a union known as the [Council of Resdayn](Lore_First_Council.md).

However this settlement was extremely controversial for several Dwemer clans, resulting in [Clan Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) seeking self-imposed exile in Hammerfell, and [Clan Kragen](Lore_Kragen.md) following in their wake settling in Skyrim.

The discovery of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) led to the dissolution of the First Council, as the Dwemer planned to use the heart to achieve godhood, the resultant [War of the First Council](Lore_First_Era_Wars.md#The_War_of_the_First_Council) ended with the complete and mysterious disappearance of all Dwemer from Tamriel.

### [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SR-4)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/db/SR-concept-6877506575.jpg/200px-SR-concept-6877506575.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877506575.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877506575.jpg) Skyrim concept art Skyrim was settled by Clan Kragen following the exodus of Clan Rourken to Hammerfell after the formation of the Council of Resdayn. However archaeological evidence points to Skyrim having been settled by Dwarves for much longer than this famous event, and indeed the Snow Elves of Skyrim had maintained an uneasy alliance with the Dwemer against the advancing Atmoran Invasions in the early first era. The Dwemer took advantage of the Falmer's weakness, welcoming in the majority of their fleeing population and blinding them with the use of a toxic mushroom and subjugating the population as slaves.

The Dwemer took advantage of a massive cavern system beneath all nine holds of Skyrim known as Fal'Zhardum Din, also known as [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md). which allowed them to operate independently from the surface, and even fought entire wars completely unknown to the rest of the world. One of these was the Aetherium Wars, the result of a four-way alliance between major Dwemer city-states for the control and smelting of Aetherium for the creation of powerful artifacts. This alliance broke down for control of the Aetherium Forge. This conflict is believed to be the reason why the Nords under High King Gellir were able to conquer most of the Dwarven holds when they had long managed to withhold against sieges. The Nordic occupation lasted for a century before the Dwarves had reclaimed their territory.

Another underground conflict occurred with the War of the Crag as the enslaved Falmer rebelled against their masters. The Falmer won by default once all Dwarves disappeared from Tamriel, but they were left in degenerated goblin-like forms for unknown reasons.

### [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RG-5)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Volenfell_02.jpg) [Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md), a Dwemer ruin in Hammerfell which was once a capital of the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) clan Hammerfell was settled by the Dwarves of Clan Rourken who chose self-exile over staying in Morrowind following the formation of the Council of Resdayn. Their largest city, Volenfell is where the province owes its name- as legend says King Rourken threw the artifact Volendrung across the horizon vowing to found his city where it landed. Thus Volenfell means 'City of the Hammer'.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE1H-16)</sup>

However the Dwarves of Hammerfell were supposedly driven out by an invasion of otherworldly Giant Goblins.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-RTXT-17)</sup> Though many of their ruins were re-populated by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) and turned into Hammerfell's modern cities,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-NFRH-18)</sup> others were picked over by Nordic and Akaviri raiders.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3H-19)</sup>

- The Dwarven Orrery complex at the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) is not a Dwemer ruin. Instead, it was built by the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) from imported machinery.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OBdownload-24)</sup>
- The *[Ancient Tales of the Dwemer](Lore_Ancient_Tales_of_the_Dwemer.md)* series mentions Dwemer locations of Bthalag-Zturamz, Duathand, Lorikh, and Othrobar, but the books are largely considered fiction, meaning it is unknown whether they are real locations (Lorikh in particular is noted to be a Dunmer name).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ATo D-25)</sup>
