[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/TR-place-Mournhold%2C_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg/200px-TR-place-Mournhold%2C_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Mournhold,_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Mournhold,_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom_02.jpg) The Plaza circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) The Plaza Brindisi Dorom occupies the southernmost quadrant of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md). It is a public garden dominated by [a great statue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Statues_Tribunal_and_Saints#Almalexia) at its center.

The plaza is named after [Indoril Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Brindisi_Dorom.md), the duke of Mournhold who was killed when [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) destroyed the city in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920).

The southwest gate leads to Godsreach, the southeast gate to the Great Bazaar, and the Gates of [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) to the outside world.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/TR-quest-An_Attack_on_Mournhold.jpg/200px-TR-quest-An_Attack_on_Mournhold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-quest-An_Attack_on_Mournhold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-quest-An_Attack_on_Mournhold.jpg) The Plaza under attack, with the statue destroyed In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), both the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) had their guildhalls on the Plaza.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> It was described as a quiet plaza containing gardens and statuary, serving as a place for reflection and prayer to the Tribunal.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-APGTM-3)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), [Fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md) assaulted the Plaza and the statue of [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and Mehrunes Dagon in the center of the square was torn asunder, revealing an entrance to the Dwemer Ruins of [Bamz-Amschend](Lore_Bamz-Amschend.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) had erected a large building in Plaza Brindisi Dorom to house the many new priests and pilgrims who had come to visit.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
