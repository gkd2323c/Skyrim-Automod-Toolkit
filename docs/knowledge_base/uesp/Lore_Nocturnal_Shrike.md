*I can feel my Mistress again. But I sense she has become dispirited by her deep loss. It is very much in the nature of Nocturnals to pine and mourn, even beyond the purpose of such grief. They nurse their misery in their bosom until their sorrow takes on a life of its very own. Until weeping is all there is. Until all the seas of tears run dry.*
—Nocturnal Subaltern Deyanira Katrece on their nature<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BSDK-1)</sup> (listen Link ⏵) Nocturnal Shrikes<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ut D-2)</sup> (sometimes simply called **Nocturnals**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BS-3)</sup> or **Shrikes**) are a race of incredibly intelligent [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) found in the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md), [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md)'s realm of Oblivion. In appearance, they resemble tall, pale,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PVA-4)</sup> scantily clad, beautiful women.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Darien-5)</sup> Despite their power and intelligence, Unusual for Daedra, Nocturnal Shrikes are prone to bouts of debilitating melancholy and fluctuating emotional states. While other Daedra are eerily calm or consistently mercurial, Shrike tend to have two states.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The race is seemingly caste-based, with the terms "lieutenant" and "subaltern" used to draw distinctions between two individuals of varying power.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-7)</sup><sup>:129</sup> Other Daedra such as the [Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md) who once occupied [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), refer to this power dynamic as "greater" and "lesser" Nocturnals.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSFD-8)</sup>

Subalterns serve lieutenants faithfully, offering counsel, and have even been known to make sacrifices out of love for their mistresses. The Nocturnals of Shade Perilous are led by the Nocturnal Lieutenant [Jaciel Morgen](Lore_Jaciel_Morgen.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BS-3)</sup> Nocturnal Shrikes have various other ranks and titles, such as Crowcaller, Executioner, Gloom Siren, Nightmaiden, Night Sister, Shadowweaver, Shroudweaver, and Talon.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESONS2-9)</sup>

Nocturnal Shrikes have been known to perform strange rituals on mortal cultists, turning them into vengeful spirits known as [Night Terrors](Lore_Shadow.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESONT-10)</sup> They often utilize dark magic associated with [crows](Lore_Crow.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESONS-11)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Nocturnal Shrikes invaded the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) as part of Nocturnal's plan to conquer the realm.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CWC-12)</sup> They invaded many other realms, including [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and the [Fields of Regret](Lore_Fields_of_Regret.md), when Nocturnal took control of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) later that year.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

The Nocturnals of Shade Perilous suffered greatly during [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s invasion of the pocket realm during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), having been betrayed by many of the previously neutral [Seducers](Lore_Mazken.md) who had been granted refuge in the realm. The invasion was instigated by [Xivilai's Clan](Lore_Xivilai%27s_Clan.md) and [Shardai Clan](Lore_Shardai_Clan.md), and was even viewed by some from within [Clan Dagon](Lore_Clan_Dagon.md) as unnecessary in the capture of the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md). The Nocturnals, alongside the remaining Seducers now pledging service to Deyanira Keyatara, were aided by a mortal [Apprentice](Lore_Apprentice.md) from this wizard's college; helping them awaken Jaciel Morgen from a deep sleep and reclusion into the [Dreamsleeve](Lore_Dreamsleeve.md) caused by her excessive mourning following the invasion.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BSJM-16)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BSFD-8)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BSFrost-17)</sup> Deyanira is banished in a ritual suicide, being struck down by the Apprentice with an enchanted dagger which breaks the lieutenant from her waking slumber. After her rage subsided and she understood why her mistress chose to be banished, Jaciel pledged retaliation and confronted Dagon alongside the hero, projecting multiple images of herself to serve as distraction long enough for the mortal to banish him to the [Void](Lore_Void.md) with the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](Lore_Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-7)</sup><sup>:179</sup>
