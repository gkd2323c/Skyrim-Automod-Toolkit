Pelidil (also spelled **Peledil**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jimila-1)</sup> was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) vicereeve and diplomat that served the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#First_Aldmeri_Dominion) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), during the early years of Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md)'s reign. Pelidil was the advisor of Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md), Ayrenn's younger brother, but he later became the leader of the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md) and stole the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md) following the death of Naemon in [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md).

## History
Pelidil served Prince Naemon as an advisor and attendant and was extremely devoted to him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Pelidil-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Servant-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sarandil-4)</sup> Naemon became the heir to the Throne of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md) following the disappearance of his older sister, Ayrenn, in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATUQ-5)</sup> However, in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), Ayrenn returned and was crowned Queen of Alinor over Naemon, causing many of his supporters to resent her.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATUQ-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Tt VM-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Estre-8)</sup> After Ayrenn's return in 2E 580, Naemon's wife, High Kinlady [Estre](Lore_Estre.md), formed the Altmeri supremacist group, the Veiled Heritance, in opposition to Ayrenn and the Aldmeri Dominion, which Pelidil eventually joined.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-WDt V-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Lit ES-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWo S-11)</sup> Pelidil served as Naemon's right-hand man and occasionally as his representative and envoy in the Aldmeri Dominion's affairs.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Pelidil-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Servant-3)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Pelidil and [the Silvenar](Lore_Edhelorn.md) traveled to [Khenarthi's Roost](Lore_Khenarthi%27s_Roost.md) to negotiate the island's admission into the Dominion.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Servant-3)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Razum-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TPo D-13)</sup> However, negotiations were complicated by the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md)'s treaty with the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Razum-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TPo D-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Edhelorn-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Harrani-15)</sup> The local Khajiit headwoman, Harrani, was unwilling to negotiate a treaty with the Dominion, wishing to prevent a conflict with the Maormer.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TPo D-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Edhelorn-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Harrani-15)</sup> The Silvenar requested a copy of the treaty, but neither the Maormer Ambassador, Ulondil, nor Headwoman Harrani was willing to produce one.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TPo D-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Edhelorn-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Harrani-15)</sup> The Silvenar's negotiations greatly displeased Pelidil, who did not want to deal with the Maormer due to the longstanding animosity between the Sea Elves and the Altmer; he also believed the Dominion should have taken Khenarthi's Roost by force.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Pelidil-2)</sup> Following the Silvenar's murder, Pelidil accused the Khajiit of plotting with the Maormer to kill the Silvenar, offending Harrani.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Pelidil-2)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Harrani-15)</sup> After Ulondil was exposed as being behind the Silvenar's assassination, Pelidil negotiated a treaty with Harrani that guaranteed Khenarthi's Roost's admission into the Dominion.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Harrani-15)</sup>

After the situation on Khenarthi's Roost was resolved, Pelidil traveled to [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) to rejoin Naemon in [Vulkhel Guard](Lore_Vulkhel_Guard.md), where he was accompanying Ayrenn during her sojourn on the island.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ES-16)</sup> Pelidil subsequently accompanied Naemon during Ayrenn's pilgrimage to [Tanzelwil](Lore_Tanzelwil.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Rot Q-17)</sup> He next accompanied Naemon to Elden Root, being present during the controversy surrounding the Khajiit Embassy and the ratification ceremony at the Orrery.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-THot Q-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TOo ER-19)</sup> However, he was not admitted inside the Orrery and was forced to watch from afar.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Pelidil-2)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Meleras-20)</sup> During the ceremony, Naemon betrayed Ayrenn, but the Orrery transformed him into an [Ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md), and he was killed by an [agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Dominion.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TOo ER-19)</sup> Naemon's treachery was covered up by claiming he died a hero, and Ayrenn asked Pelidil to travel with the prince's body to [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) and make the funeral arrangements.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-7)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TOo ER-19)</sup>

Although Pelidil did travel to Marbruk, he absconded with Naemon's body, stole the Staff of Magnus from the city's [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and assumed control of the remnants of the Veiled Heritance with the goal of resurrecting the prince as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) to lead an undead army and corrupt the [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Razum-12)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Telonde-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Fariel-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Indaenir-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TSo M-24)</sup> Pelidil then went to [Woodhearth](Lore_Woodhearth.md), where he had allies from the Veiled Heritance.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Razum-12)</sup> Pelidil signed a treaty with the Maormer, allying the Veiled Heritance and the Sea Vipers of the [Pyandonean](Lore_Pyandonea.md) Armada as part of his plans to capture [Seaside Sanctuary](Lore_Seaside_Sanctuary.md) to gain a foothold in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and then attack Woodhearth.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Laryaril-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Bashshi-ra-26)</sup> In return for helping him destroy the Dominion, Pelidil agreed to give Valenwood to the Maormer, while he would take control of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Laryaril-25)</sup> Around this time, Pelidil led an attack on the [Wooded Eye](Lore_Wooded_Eye.md), a cult of [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) devoted to [Herma-Mora](Lore_Herma-Mora.md), at the [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) of Ilmyris, which contained knowledge related to the Heart of Valenwood that Naemon would need.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Pelidil-2)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Mora-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Orthenir-28)</sup> Pelidil acquired the information by torturing cultists of the Wooden Eye with Ayleid crystals.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Pelidil-2)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Mora-27)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TFG-29)</sup>

Pelidil recruited Laryaril, who then killed her twin sister Asteril, an officer in the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md), to impersonate her.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Laryaril-25)</sup> The Veiled Heritance's presence in Woodhearth was exposed when Laryaril attempted to assassinate Ayrenn, who was actually being impersonated by [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md), the leader of the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md), using an illusion spell.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Lt L-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Vo I-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DJ-32)</sup> Pelidil then left Woodhearth for Seaside Sanctuary, where he and the Veiled Heritance assisted the Sea Vipers in capturing the settlement.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Laryaril-25)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ASUt S-33)</sup> After taking Seaside Sanctuary, Pelidil performed a ritual to raise Naemon from the dead as a lich.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Telonde-21)</sup> However, when Naemon was resurrected, he spat in Pelidil's face and then left, infuriating the vicereeve.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Telonde-21)</sup>

The Dominion eventually defeated the Sea Vipers and the Veiled Heritance at the Battle of Seaside Sanctuary and recaptured the settlement.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ASUt S-33)</sup> Pelidil had departed in a Sea Viper ship before the Dominion's arrival, so they used the Prowler, the ship of the privateer Captain Jimila, to pursue him.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Cirenwe-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-PE-35)</sup> The Prowler was able to catch up with Pelidil and the Sea Vipers, beginning a battle in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). Ultimately, during the battle, the Sea Vipers were defeated, and Pelidil was killed by an agent of the Dominion.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-PE-35)</sup>

## Legacy
Although Pelidil was dead, Naemon's lich took swift control of [Green's Marrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green%27s_Marrow), the northern region of Greenshade, where he continued to fight the Dominion with his undead army and work to corrupt the Heart of Valenwood.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Henandan-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Rt P-37)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TBot B-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Ro T-39)</sup> Naemon was eventually defeated in a final battle in [Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Sat H-40)</sup> The Wood Elf mage, [Indaenir](Lore_Indaenir.md), sacrificed his life to take the corruption out of the Heart of Valenwood.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Sat H-40)</sup> However, the God of the Forest, [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), resurrected him for saving Valenwood and made him into the new Silvenar.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Indaenir-23)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Akkhuz-ri-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Cariel-42)</sup>

Tales of the battle in which Pelidil died, affectionately known as *"The Tale of the Prowler and the Fierce Maormer Sea Serpent,"* spread rapidly across the greater Abecean Sea, becoming popular in the various port towns. Both the agent of the Dominion and the Prowler became famous across the land, with many saying that they were surrounded by one hundred Sea Elves, but the agent defeated them with a single punch and then rode a [Sea Serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md) that wrecked the Maormeri fleet. The battle between Pelidil and the agent was said to have rocked the waters and reshaped the coast.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Jimila-1)</sup>
