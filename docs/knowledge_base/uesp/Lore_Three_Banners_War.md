# Three Banners War


The years leading up to the war saw a divided Tamriel witness a series of conflicts and crises that inspired the different nations of the land to band together into three distinct alliances: the greater [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GTTDC-2)</sup> the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GTTEP-3)</sup> and the first [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TROQA-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATUQ-5)</sup>

The competing ideologies of these alliances set them on a course for war, which began in earnest sometime in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580). Tensions between the alliances were further exacerbated by the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) and subsequent military aggression of the Empire of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) under [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)'s puppet,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AL-8)</sup> Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCC-9)</sup> Each alliance saw the opportunity to overthrow the corrupt Imperial regime by capturing the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and installing their own emperor on the [Ruby Throne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruby_Throne), but also had to deal with the other two alliances.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Carlier-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-barracks-11)</sup> The three central leaders of these three competing alliances were [Queen Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) of the Aldmeri Dominion, [High King Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of the Daggerfall Covenant, and [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) of the Ebonheart Pact.

The armies of the Covenant, Pact, and Dominion each invaded Cyrodiil, which served as the main theater of war, however they also launched attacks against each other's lands. The war caused enormous damage to Cyrodiil's towns and cities, and countless civilians were displaced and resettled throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONWrits-12)</sup> The war was ongoing as of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> despite numerous attempts at [peace talks](#The_Three_Banners_Truce). The war's ultimate outcome is unknown, but all three alliances, as well as the Empire of Cyrodiil, had ceased to exist by the ninth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

The conflict of the Three Banners War is often symbolically represented by a three-headed [ouroboros](Lore_Ouroboros.md), with each head symbolizing one of the alliances: the eagle for the Aldmeri Dominion, the lion for the Daggerfall Covenant, and the dragon for the Ebonheart Pact.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Legends-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-AUA-15)</sup>

## War in Cyrodiil
*The Imperial province of Cyrodiil dominates central Tamriel. With the Empire's collapse, armies of the Dominion, Covenant, and Pact have all invaded Cyrodiil, vying for the Imperial throne and control of the greatest prize in the land.*
— [Cyrodiil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Cyrodiil) loading screen in [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ee/LG-cardart-Emeric%2C_Covenant_King.png/250px-LG-cardart-Emeric%2C_Covenant_King.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Emeric,_Covenant_King.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Emeric,_Covenant_King.png) High King Emeric of the Daggerfall Covenant All three alliances launched an invasion into [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) via adjacent territories under their control and established bases of operations across the border. Grand Warlord Dortene, leading the Daggerfall Covenant forces, invaded Cyrodiil from [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) via the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) and the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) in the northwest.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONCDortene-16)</sup> Grand Warlord Zimmeron, leading the Ebonheart Pact forces, invaded from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) via the [Valus Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valus_Mountains) in the northeast.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONCZimmeron-17)</sup> Grand Warlord Sorcalin, leading the Aldmeri Dominion forces, invaded from [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March) via the [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley) in the south.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONCSorcalin-18)</sup>

The three alliances established individual [Transitus Networks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Transitus_Network) in Cyrodiil, a form of teleportation magic. This temporary teleportation network allowed troops to instantaneously travel between keeps and outposts, although the networks seemingly had limited range and required constant maintenance by groups of mages when pushing deeper into enemy territory.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Herendas-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Canis-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Viriniel-21)</sup>

During the war soldiers from each alliance discovered the whereabouts of several [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) that were kept hidden by the [Cult of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md). The alliances stole the scrolls and built vast holy temples across Cyrodiil to house the scrolls they had stolen.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONAxius-22)</sup> The temples were built close enough to the battlefields to bestow the scrolls' blessing onto the troops.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONPA-23)</sup>

Some soldiers also sought the Blessing of the Whitestrake - a boon that was said to imbue warriors with a "holy fervor for righteous carnage" and improve their contributions to the war. Priests of St. [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) were found in each alliance garrison during [Pelinal's Midyear Massacre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelinal%27s_Midyear_Massacre) offering to bestow blessings on anyone who asked for them.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONMaera-24)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONPredicant-25)</sup>

Unsatisfied with the carnage taking place in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) involved himself indirectly with the war when he sent [Volendrung](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volendrung) to the battlefields hoping the resulting chaos would bring him entertainment. The hammer was used by all three alliances against each other throughout the war, as it changed hands often.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-CCTCWAA-26)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ESOChaos Magic-27)</sup>

The alliances issued "[alliance points](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alliance_Points)" as a form of scrip to those who participated in the Three Banners War. This was treated as currency along the front. Soldiers from the war were also encouraged to participate in the bloodsports held by the [Circle of Champions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Circle_of_Champions) by being rewarded by these alliance points.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup> While participating in these bloodsports, they did not fight for Queen Ayrenn, High King Emeric, or Jorunn the Skald-King, but for the Pit Daemons, Fire Drakes, or Storm Lords teams.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

### Siege Warfare
[![ON-concept-Keep walls.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/ON-concept-Keep_walls.png/300px-ON-concept-Keep_walls.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Keep_walls.png) Battles between the alliances in Cyrodiil were characterized primarily through siege warfare. Great keeps and forts were fought over by the alliances and used to further their invasion deeper into Cyrodiil, with the ultimate goal of capturing the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) at the center of the province. Each alliance fleetingly gained control of the Imperial City at some point at during the war, and they would crown a figurehead Emperor from among their warriors, however their reigns were extremely short-lived.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

One notable conflict was the Battle of Chalman Keep - a three-way battle between Pact, Dominion, and Covenant forces at the contested and strategically important [Chalman Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chalman_Keep). The Covenant defenders held out against two enemy armies, but ultimately the keep fell to a Dominion invasion force, after which the Dominion crowned a [figurehead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battlefield_Emperors) [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) from among their warriors.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-31)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-32)</sup>

### Cities Affected by War
While large parts of central Cyrodiil faced the horrors and destruction of open warfare, some areas around the periphery of the war zone remained relatively unscathed. The counties of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) and [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) were protected from the ongoing battles by [Varen's Wall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen%27s_Wall), a large edifice created to split the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) from the rest of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-33)</sup> Despite this protection from the war, Anvil was seized by the Red Sails pirates led by "Pirate Queen" [Fortunata ap Dugal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortunata_ap_Dugal) who overthrew Count [Ephrem Benirus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ephrem_Benirus) and declared independence.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-34)</sup> This led to a state of hostility between Anvil and Kvatch and open engagements such as the Gottshaw Massacre.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-35)</sup> Count [Calantius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Calantius_of_Skingrad) of [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad) was said to be a canny opportunist who managed to keep his city just out of the chaos of war.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-36)</sup> Countess [Nevenia Caro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nevenia_Caro) of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) ceded control and protection of the city to the Chamber of Legates and the [Ivory Brigade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ivory_Brigade) prior to the war. Since the fall of the Empire, the Ivory Brigade doubled its efforts to replace the Imperial Legion and maintained active defenses to keep the ongoing war at bay.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ONLiore-37)</sup> [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) became neutral ground during war, but was attacked by [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) when a [Dark Anchor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Anchor) opened above the city in an attempt to draw it into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). Several citizens barricaded themselves within key buildings, and the survivors sent pleas to both the Daggerfall Covenant and the Ebonheart Pact for aid.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Brumaaid-38)</sup>

Other towns did not fare as well. The Imperial garrison amassed control over [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) and imposed themselves as tyrannical rulers.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONVyctoria-39)</sup> [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol) was devastated by large fissure that tore through the town years prior to the war. The town became plagued by famine and banditry and changed hands many times over the course of the war.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCC-9)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-40)</sup> [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) was subject to a mass slaughter and looting when Captain Jean Apinia and her legion took refuge in the city. Captain Apinia asked the mayor to give up their food supplies to aid her legion. When the Mayor refused Captain Apinia ordered her troops begin slaughtering the cityfolk, resulting in over half the population being killed and the town looted of valuables.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TBo B-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-RD-42)</sup> These events however pale in comparison to the destruction that was wrought upon the Imperial City.

## Conquest of the Imperial City
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-concept-22.jpg/400px-ON-concept-22.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-22.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-22.jpg) The three alliances fight for control of the Imperial City *At the center of Cyrodiil stands the Imperial City. There, the Three Alliances vie with Daedric overlords and each other for control of the White-Gold Tower.*
— [Imperial City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Imperial_City) loading screen in [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online) While the war raged across the countryside of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), all three alliances were locked in a struggle for control of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) in an effort to overthrow the corrupt Imperial regime and install their own emperor on the Ruby Throne.

Sappers in each alliance established forward bases in the city's sewers to launch attacks on the districts above in an attempt to thin the enemy's numbers before marching on the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ONRythe-43)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ONNedras-44)</sup>

During the fighting, a new danger unexpectedly appeared when chains fell from the sky. The [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) began and the forces of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) swarmed the city districts and sewers and took the city. Many [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) soldiers died defending the city while others fled.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TBo B-41)</sup> Fighting alongside [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s Daedra horde were a group known as [Legion Zero](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Legion_Zero). These were members of the Imperial Legion that deserted when the Planemeld began and swore fealty to Molag Bal and the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult)<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-45)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-46)</sup> While the entire city was tearing itself apart, a small group of Imperial Legion loyalists known as the [Immortal Eight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Immortal_Eight) fought back against the alliances and Legion Zero and played a part in stopping Bal's plans to snuff out the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TSB-47)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-MTCCC-48)</sup> As a result of the war, the city itself was devastated. Few city folk remained and the buildings in each district suffered enormous damage, some completely demolished.

## Military Campaigns
Although the main theater for the war took place in the province of Cyrodiil, numerous invasions, battles and skirmishes took place within the lands of the respective powers. The Daggerfall Covenant was arguably the most aggressive in their attacks on other alliances, making multiple forays primarily into Ebonheart Pact and Dominion territory. Similarly, the Dominion also plotted mainly against the Pact through their schemes in [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen). As such, the Pact spent much of the war defending their lands, with their only notable invasions being into Cyrodiil. The [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) did not sit idle as the armies of the alliances forged deep into its territory. During the war it launched its own invasions into both Covenant and Dominion territory.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ESOBangkorai-49)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ESOMarch-50)</sup>

### Daggerfall Covenant Campaigns

#### Attack on Bleakrock Isle
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f9/ON-place-Bleakrock_Village.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bleakrock_Village.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bleakrock_Village.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bleakrock_Village.jpg) Bleakrock Village *"We sighted a ship offshore. I've sent my troops to investigate. If it's the vanguard of an invasion ... well, we'll need to evacuate."* —Captain Rana The Daggerfall Covenant targeted [Bleakrock Isle](Lore_Bleakrock_Isle.md) as the first step to a large-scale invasion of the Ebonheart Pact's territory. In an attempt to cause a distraction for their initial naval attack, the Covenant hired the Frostedge bandits to steal relics of power in Hozzin's Folly and cause havoc on the isle.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONHF-51)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Contract Scroll-52)</sup> They also sent a necromancer to Skyshroud Barrow to raise the dead.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ONWWB-53)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Scrollof Banishment-54)</sup>

Although the Ebonheart Pact had established a small garrison on the island and thwarted these plots, it was too late, and they were unable to request backup in time. Led by General Vette, the Covenant began their attack and slaughtered many of the inhabitants of Bleakrock Village. The village was razed by pyromancers but a small number of survivors were evacuated by ship to [Dhalmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dhalmora) in mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ONEFB-55)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ONRana-56)</sup>

#### Attack on Bal Foyen
*"Tell the soldiers there to light the signal fires along the coast. The fires will warn Davon's Watch that war is upon them."* —Captain Rana In an effort to warn the mainland of the impending Covenant naval invasion, Pact forces lit several signal fire watchtowers across [Bal Foyen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bal_Foyen).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ONIBS-57)</sup> In response, the Covenant launched a two-pronged attack on the region led by General Emax and General Vette. Large battles took place at both Fort Zeren and Bal Foyen's docks between Pact and Covenant forces.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ONBTT-58)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-ONZIP-59)</sup> While Bal Foyen suffered heavy losses, the Pact succeeded in driving off the invaders and warning the city of [Davon's Watch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Davon%27s_Watch) to the west.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-ONHolgunn-60)</sup>

#### Siege of Davon's Watch
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-quest-City_Under_Siege_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-City_Under_Siege_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-City_Under_Siege_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-City_Under_Siege_02.jpg) Balreth attacking a group of Daggerfall Covenant soldiers *"The Covenant is attacking Davon's Watch. The devils bombarded the northern district. Now they're ashore, preparing to attack. This port's the key to Stonefalls. That's why these Covenant bastards are attacking. They're keen on the city, but we aren't going to let them. The Nords are here to defend the Pact."* —Holgunn One-Eye Following the attack on Bal Foyen, the city of [Davon's Watch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Davon%27s_Watch) was put under siege by the invading Daggerfall Covenant. Capturing Davon's port was the Covenant's key to pushing deeper into [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls). After making landfall on the western beach, the Covenant bombarded the Davon's Watch for several days.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-ONHolgunn-60)</sup>

Although Pact forces managed to sabotage the Covenant siege equipment and delay their attacks, Covenant forces infiltrated Davon's Watch by scaling the cliffs, threatening take the city from within.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ONDTD-61)</sup> In a last ditch effort to save the city, Grandmaster Tanval Indoril performed a Daedric ritual to release [Balreth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balreth), an ancient [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Balreth completely destroyed the Covenant's invasion force, freeing Davon's Watch.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-ONCUS-62)</sup>

#### Failed Attack on Kragenmoor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-place-Kragenmoor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Kragenmoor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kragenmoor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kragenmoor.jpg) Kragenmoor *"The dogs of the Pact do not understand a contract well made. They see no profit in peace. The only thing they understand is force. Overwhelming force. If we're going to take Ebonheart, we need breaches, footholds. I've received word from a scout. She found an abandoned mine near Kragenmoor that might do nicely. Occupy it. Hold it. Use it. And do not fail me."* —General Alexandra Conele Despite their defeat at Davon's Watch, the Daggerfall Covenant remained fast in their plan to take [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart). As they pushed into Stonefalls General Alexandra Conele sought to establish footholds to launch their attacks. One such place was in Softloam Cavern, an abandoned mine near Kragenmoor.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-General Orders-63)</sup>

Within the cavern the [Ring of Daggers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ring_of_Daggers) created a long-range portal that led straight back to Covenant lands. From here they began to amass their numbers in order to launch an attack on [Kragenmoor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kragenmoor) and the rest of Stonefalls. Pact soldiers discovered their plot and were successful in destroying the portal and preventing an attack on Kragenmoor.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-ONDathlyn-64)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONSTT-65)</sup>

#### Battle at Vivec's Antlers
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg/200px-ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg) The glowing coral at Vivec's Antlers"*I don't care how you do it. I don't care if you have to invade that mud-hut pigsty next door and enslave every Argonian there, get it done! That heart is the key, and I will not fail in my mission. And neither will you."* —General Serien Although the Covenant's attack on Kragenmoor failed, they launched another major offensive on the shores to the west of Ebonheart. The Covenant sought to retrieve an artifact known as the [Coral Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coral_Heart) and use it to summon an ancient Daedra named [Sadal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sadal), who would lay waste to Stonefalls. Led by General Alexandra Conele, the Covenant landed on the shores of [Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec%27s_Antlers) and clashed with Ebonheart Pact forces - outnumbering them five to one. In response, the Coral Heart summoned the local [dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh) to defend itself, halting the Covenant's invasion.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ONNoris-66)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Getit Done Conele-67)</sup>

Alexandra Conele eventually managed to steal the Coral Heart from its subterranean chamber, however a detachment of Ebonheart Pact soldiers fought and killed Conele and recovered the heart.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ONTCH-68)</sup>

#### Battle at Fort Virak
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/ON-place-Fort_Virak.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fort_Virak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Virak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Virak.jpg) Fort Virak"*A scout from the northwest border made it to the city, bloody and bruised. He said a force was marching on Fort Virak. If they take Virak, they're on the doorstep of Riften, up in Skyrim."* —Holgunn One-Eye After their grave loss at Vivec's Antlers, the Daggerfall Covenant moved west toward Fort Virak and laid waste to the countryside. The fort was a vital link between Stonefalls and the Rift, as it controlled the supply lines between Pact forces.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-ONHolgunn-60)</sup> The Covenant managed to take the keep and quickly reinforced its walls, bracing for a massive siege from the Ebonheart Pact under the command of Garyn Indoril.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ONGjorring-69)</sup> The Pact's siege had little effect on the keep's walls. ### Imperial Campaigns
The [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) did not sit idle as the armies of the alliances forged deep into its territory. It attempted to expand its reach on two fronts: [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) to the north,<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ESOBangkorai-49)</sup> and [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) to the south.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ESOMarch-50)</sup> The invasion of High Rock and Hammerfell was spearheaded by the infamous Magus-General [Septima Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Septima_Tharn).<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-BSOHR-104)</sup> To the south, an [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) was mustered from [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) to annex [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md)'s Arenthia Vale, which by decree of the Count of [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad) and the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) was renamed the South Weald.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-YFTT-105)</sup>

#### Battle for North Hook
The Battle for North Hook happened at the start of the usurper war. The Daggerfall Covenant sought North Hook as a base from which to invade the Imperial City; however, the West Weald Legion led by Tribune Alea Idolus repelled the invasion.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-106)</sup>

#### Occupation of Satakalaam
The [Seventh Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seventh_Legion) used the smuggling tunnels underneath the mountains to infiltrate Satakalaam and conquer the Alik'r Desert. They intimidated local Magnifico, Khorshad, and utilized his manor as headquarters for their takeover of the city.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-ONKhorshad-107)</sup> The Imperials ransacked not just the town but the High Temple as well by killing priests, defiling the holy site and looting it of prized treasures: [Sword of Shabnam‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword_of_Shabnam), [Majeel's Scroll‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Majeel%27s_Scroll) and [Lamp of the Satakalaam‎‎](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam).<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-ONZuladr-108)</sup> General Thoda of Sentinel's armies launched a counterattack and lead the retaking of Satakalaam. They managed to secure hostages across town and eventually destroyed the tunnels with barrels of kindlepitch. With their connection to the east severed, the Seventh Legion was immediately defeated, holy relics were recovered and Satakalaam had been secured.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-ONIncursion-109)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-ONTreasures-110)</sup>

#### Fall of the Bangkorai Garrison
*Today, invaders from the east threaten us once again, in the form of an Imperial Legion from Cyrodiil. But these are not the legendary warriors of an Empress Hestra, or the disciplined soldiers of an Emperor Reman: these are the degraded mercenaries of the Tharn usurpers.*
— [King Eamond](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eamond)'s address to his troops Magus-General Septima Tharn had long sought to take the city of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) in [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai) and sent the [Seventh Legion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seventh_Legion) to the region during the war, despite petitions from Ambassador Jadier Plourde to withdraw.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-Cipius Orders-111)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Mo EC-112)</sup>

One of the key battles that shaped the conflict was when Septima's Seventh Legion clashed with King [Eamond](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eamond) of Evermore's forces at the [Bangkorai Garrison](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai_Garrison). King Eamond himself delivered a final address his troops, consisting of over two hundred [Knights of Saint Pelin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_Saint_Pelin), the Evermore Guard and freeborn militias of [Mournoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mournoth) and [Ephesus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ephesus).<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-BSOHR-104)</sup> Events of the battle itself were unclear, but eyewitness rumors included giant [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) scaling the walls, the dead rising from the crypts and one account claiming Septima herself snuck into the garrison posing as a refugee, and then unleashed [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) the next night. Ultimately the garrison fell to the Legion and only a handful of knights escaped alive.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-ONEmeric-113)</sup> The events surrounding King Eamond demise were disputed. Some accounts believed the King to have survived the battle and later died fighting [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) outside Northglen.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ONAdrien-114)</sup> Other accounts claim he had already died and was about to be entombed in the garrison before it was attacked.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-ONEmeric-113)</sup>

During this time the city faced great hardship, with [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) bearing down on the city from the north and the Seventh Legion attacking in the south.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-ONCIB-115)</sup>

Duke Renchant was afraid that if the Reachmen took the city they would slaughter every man, woman, and child. His decided that the lesser of two evils was to secretly surrender the city to Imperial authority and in return they would agree to repel the Reachmen attacks.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-ONRenchant-116)</sup> After colluding with Imperial diplomats, the duke arranged for crates of weapons and armor to be placed at strategic points in the city. Imperial spies infiltrated the city and attempted to recruit as many citizens as possible to aid in the takeover. Then at a predetermined time the Imperials would meet at the weapons cache, arm themselves, and take control of the city.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-Cipius Orders-111)</sup><sup>[[116]](#cite_note-ARE-117)</sup>

Queen Arzhela discovered the plot and sent her agents to root out the Imperial spies throughout the city. Once the spies had been neutralized, Duke Renchant was removed from power and Queen Arzhela took control of the throne.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-ONII-118)</sup>

#### Colovian Occupation of Arenthia

This edict was supposedly ratified by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) and gave the Legion of the West Weald the authority to annex its newly acquired lands and secure its borders. Previous ownership of all lands in Arenthia Vale were declared null and void and its previous inhabitants were deported or taken as slaves.

Javad Tharn was one of the leading figures in the campaign to capture the region. Although the pretext of the invasion was meant for its annexation, Javad Tharn had an ulterior motive that related to the downfall of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-ONGavo-129)</sup> General Lavinia Axius took command of the actual occupation and urged [Colovians](Lore_Colovia.md) to join the Legion of the West Weald, granting numerous incentives such as farms for soldiers and estates for officers.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-YFTT-105)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SZLMIw LS-36)</sup> General Lavinia orchestrated a sneak attack on [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia) with a large force. Although the city guard fought back, most died in the fighting and the city was subdued. During the occupation General Lavinia herself delivered propaganda speeches via magical projections in the town square to further cow the city folk.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-ONTCO-130)</sup><sup>[[130]](#cite_note-ONBurri-131)</sup><sup>[[131]](#cite_note-ONKazirra-132)</sup>

The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of Arenthia Vale fought back and, with aid from the Aldmeri Dominion, formed a resistance against the invading Colovians. They learned that the Colovians themselves were being deceived by General Lavinia and Javad Tharn, who were both working with the Stonefire Daedric cult. The resistance believed that if the Colovians were to discover their general was consorting with a Daedric cult, their resolve would break and the legion would desert.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-ONKazirra-132)</sup>

The resistance kidnapped a Colovian centurion and forced a confession from him detailing General Lavinia's relationship with the Stonefire cult and their plans to construct a Dark Anchor in the Temple District of Arenthia. Using the same method General Lavinia used to deliver her propaganda speeches, the resistance broadcasted the confession to the Legion of the West Weald. As expected, the Legion abandoned the city and in the chaos the resistance stormed General Lavinia's base and killed her, thus ending the occupation.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-ONTCO-130)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-ONGavo-129)</sup>

#### Battle at Senalana
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/ON-place-Senalana.jpg/200px-ON-place-Senalana.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Senalana.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Senalana.jpg) Senalana *"We came for the circlet. General Lavinia has great plans. We've ... we've started with Arenthia. With the circlet, we'll take the Dominion apart piece ... by ... piece."* —Gratidia Arius While the main Colovian force was based in Arenthia, General Lavinia sent detachments to various locations in [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March), including Fort Grimwatch, Vinedusk and the Ayleid ruins of [Senalana](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Senalana) in Reaper's March. Dominion scouts spread throughout the Northern Wood spotted the Colovians and responded immedietely.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-ONBurri-131)</sup>

At Senalana the Colovians sought a powerful artifact known as the Grand Circlet of Elven Authority. According to legend, the circlet's wearer would have complete domination over Elves. Every descendant of [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) blood would bend the knee to its wearer. General Lavinia coveted the circlet and with it would have the power to defeat the Aldmeri Dominion.<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-ONArius-133)</sup>

[Razum-dar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Razum-dar), leader of Queen Ayrenn's personal intelligence network, led Dominion troops to battle with the Colovians to ensure the circlet didn't fall into their hands. After battling though Colovians and the numerous Ayleid traps within the ruin, the circlet was recovered by Dominion forces. It remains unknown if the circlet was destroyed or removed from the ruins and hidden elsewhere, but regardless the Colovians retreated from battle.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-ONGOF-134)</sup>

#### Attack on Vinedusk
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-place-Vinedusk_Village.jpg/200px-ON-place-Vinedusk_Village.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vinedusk_Village.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vinedusk_Village.jpg) Headquarters of the Vinedusk Rangers *"The Rangers are spread thin across the Dominion and into Cyrodiil. Damned Colovians wouldn't have been able to take us at all if we were at full strength."* —Captain Odreth Furthering General Lavinia's plans in the invasion of [Reaper's March](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reaper%27s_March), she sent an elite division of the Colovian army, known as the [Vlastarus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vlastarus) Cohort, to the military settlement of Vinedusk and took it by surprise. The Vlastarus Cohort splintered off from the main attacking force at [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia) with the mission to specifically take out the [Vinedusk Rangers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vinedusk_Rangers). The rangers were able to hold back the invaders, despite many of their numbers spread across the Dominion territory and Cyrodiil.<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-ONMengaer-135)</sup>

The commander of the rangers planned a two-pronged counterattack. First to burn the Colovians out of the village, then take them by surprise in their camp. With the aid of a Dominion agent the first stage of the rangers' plan was a success, but both sides suffered heavy losses.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-ONOOI-136)</sup><sup>[[136]](#cite_note-ONOdreth-137)</sup>

With the Colovians removed from the village, the Vinedusk Rangers planned a counterattack. They used a secret tunnel to gain access to the Colovian camp that was built atop a nearby ruin. Once they were behind their front lines, the rangers destroyed the Colovian siege weapons, tents, and cargo before going after the commander of the Colovian army. The rangers were successful in killing the commander and ending the Colovian attack on Vinedusk.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-ONDASH-138)</sup>

#### Siege of Dune
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/44/ON-quest-The_Fires_of_Dune.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Fires_of_Dune.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Fires_of_Dune.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Fires_of_Dune.jpg) Javad Tharn opens a Dark Anchor above Dune *"You cannot stop the inevitable. The city is ours. Soon I will hold your "Mane" in my hand, and my Dark Mane will walk the Path. The soul of the Khajiit will be lost to darkness!"* —Javad Tharn After the defeat at [Arenthia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arenthia), Javad Tharn grew tired of waiting and launched an attack on the city of [Dune](Lore_Dune.md). Queen [Ayrenn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayrenn) and the Lunar Champion were on their way to the Temple of Two Moons Path to anoint the new [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) when their advance team reported smoke over Dune and Stonefire cultists in the streets. Javad Tharn opened portals across Dune, allowing Daedra into the streets. His plan was to enslave the Lunar Champion and anoint his own Dark Mane, thus corrupting the spiritual soul of the Khajiit.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-139)</sup><sup>[[139]](#cite_note-ONJavad-140)</sup>

With the aid of the Moon Hallowed, the Lunar Champion was able to seal the portals and cleanse the desecrated shrines but this did not stop Javad Tharn opening a [Dark Anchor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Anchor) above the city. The Moon Hallowed stormed the Shrine of Lorkhaj and attempted to stop Tharn, but he escaped into the demi-plane of [Jode](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jode). Regardless, the siege was lifted and Dune was saved from the Daedric threat.<sup>[[140]](#cite_note-ONFOD-141)</sup>

## The Three Banners Truce
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/94/ON-quest-The_Weight_of_Three_Crowns_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Weight_of_Three_Crowns_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Weight_of_Three_Crowns_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Weight_of_Three_Crowns_02.jpg) Three alliance leaders meet on Stirk While the Three Banners War raged, the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) was also taking place across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) sought to bring the three alliances together and mount an assault on [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) in order to end the threat of the Planemeld. The war in Cyrodiil had been long and bloody, and all three alliances were spread thinly, unable to commit resources to lead an assault without making themselves vulnerable.<sup>[[141]](#cite_note-ONVanus-142)</sup>

In order to broker a peace treaty, Vanus and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) first contacted the spy networks of each alliance and arranged meetings with each leader. The Vestige acted as the perfect diplomat given their unique position and reputation, and convinced each of the leaders to meet at a neutral, undisputed location to discuss a ceasefire.<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-ONMOT-143)</sup><sup>[[141]](#cite_note-ONVanus-142)</sup>

The alliance leaders met on the island of [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) and were about to begin negotiations when [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) interupted the conclave. He sent his hordes of Daedra to kill the three alliance leaders in one fell swoop, but the leaders fought back and defeated the Daedra. The alliance leaders realized the true threat was the Planemeld and committed a number of their troops to the assault on Coldharbour. The assault was a success and the Vestige helped to end the Planemeld, but as a result the ceasefire had served its purpose and hostilities between the alliances resumed.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-ONWOTC-144)</sup>

[Chef Donolon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Donolon) was a legendary Breton confectioner who believed that delicious desserts, cakes in particular, have a way of bringing people together, and that was perhaps the key to ending the Three Banners War. To that end, he traveled the world by sea and converted a barge into a bakery. His most coveted creation was the Jubilee Cake, which helped propelled him to stardom.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-ONCD-145)</sup>

There were other attempts at peace between the alliances after the Planemeld. [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Abnur_Tharn) also sought to end the war by acquiring a mythical "demon weapon" and using its existence to force all sides to cease their aggressive behavior. His plan failed when it turned out the weapon was in fact an uncontrollable rage of [dragons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragons).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-6)</sup>

The [Society of the Steadfast](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Society_of_the_Steadfast) was a philanthropic organization that provided aid to those displaced or wounded by the war in Cyrodiil, among other charitable works.<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-ONJakarn-146)</sup> During the war they sponsored peace talks with the alliance leaders on [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), but they were disrupted by the [Ascendant Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascendant_Order). Led by the [Ascendant Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascendant_Lord), this terrorist organization became pan-Tamrielic thanks to its mass appeal to common folk, who were recruited through its promise that it would bring down Tamriel's tyrants who grow richer and powerful at the expense of misery and bloodshed.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-MTCALM-147)</sup><sup>[[147]](#cite_note-ONTAP-148)</sup> Ultimately, the Ascendant Order threat was neutralized and the peace talks went ahead, but their outcome remains unknown.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-ONDavaux-149)</sup><sup>[[149]](#cite_note-ONPeace-150)</sup>
