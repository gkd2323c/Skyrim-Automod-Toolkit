# Statues/Notable Figures

[Overview](Lore_Statues.md) | Notable Figures — [Aedra](Lore_Statues_Aedra.md) — [Daedric Princes](Lore_Statues_Daedric_Princes.md) — [Tribunal & Saints](Lore_Statues_Tribunal_and_Saints.md) — [Other Deities](Lore_Statues_Other_Deities.md) — [Other Statues](Lore_Statues_Other_Statues.md) — [Generic Statues](Lore_Statues_Generic_Statues.md) Statues of historical and notable figures can be found across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and can serve as a visual representation of not only them, but also events from the past. They allow numerous [races](Lore_Races.md) to remember and celebrate the lives and achievements of their great leaders, heroes, and figures who have contributed to the progress of their society. Additionally, these statues can serve as a reminder of the challenges and struggles faced by their ancestors and the sacrifices they made.

Statues of notable figures can be found in the settlements of almost every race, They act as a symbol of pride and help to create a shared sense of identity and belonging. Often their greatest heroes are immortalized in stone, including [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) for the [Redguards](Lore_Redguards.md), [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) and [Harald](Lore_Harald.md) for the [Nords](Lore_Nords.md), [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) for the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), [Torug gro-Igron](Lore_Torug_gro-Igron.md) for the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), and so forth. Many statues of notable figures from the distant past are subject to scholarly study and can shed light on aspects of their lives that were previously lost to time, or in some cases ignite heated debate in among scholars.

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-64) Morihaus was first introduced into the lore with the release of *TES3: Morrowind*, where he is described in *[Varieties of Faith...](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Varieties_of_Faith...#Morihaus)* as an *"Ancient cultural hero god of the Cyro-Nordics"*, but his race was undefined. In *TES4: Oblivion* he was described in *[Shezarr and the Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Shezarr_and_the_Divines)* as a *"demigod"* and *"Morihaus-Breath-of-Kyne"* and appeared human (based on the statue in the Arena District). It was only when the Oblivion add-on, [Knights of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Knights_of_the_Nine), was released that his race was retroactively changed to a bull-man. This is noted in *[The Adabal-a](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Adabal-a)* and *[The Song of Pelinal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:The_Song_of_Pelinal)* where he is described as a *"winged-bull"* and *"bull nephew"*. Due to this inconsistency, Kurt Kuhlmann and Michael Kirkbride joked that the statue would need to be retconned in the future.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-63)</sup>
2. [^](#cite_ref-73) The book *[Knightsgrave: Legend or Legacy](Lore_Knightsgrave__Legend_or_Legacy.md)* presents an inconsistency with the placement of these statues. It mentions that when the original founders of the order ([Ennon Decan](Lore_Ennon_Decan.md) and his group of companions) died, great statues were raised in memory of the founders. This is also mentioned by the Black Dragon, who writes in [her journal](Lore_The_Black_Dragon%27s_Journal,_Part_2.md): *"I admired the statues on the stairs that descended into these ruins. The founders of the Order of the Hour were great and faithful warriors."*.
The inconsistency is that this book conflicts with what is presented in-game. When you visit Knightsgrave, four statues are indeed found on the stairs and main chamber, but instead of statues dedicated to Ennon Decan and his group (the original founders), we see statues dedicated to Cavor Merula and his group (the second founders). This is further complicated because although Cavor Merula re-established the Order, this book mentions he did not wish to use the 500-year-old crypts of Knightsgrave as a headquarters so as not to disturb the dead. He blessed the area, raised guardians, and left to build the Enclave of the Hourglass, which makes the existence of these statues (and associated brazier locks) somewhat of a paradox.
3. [^](#cite_ref-89) The appearance of this statue can vary and is determined (and spawns) the moment you enter a new cell occupied by Martin after the [Bruma great gate quest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Great_Gate). This will usually be when you return to Cloud Ruler Temple, but it can happen on the road to it if you intercept him along the way. The armor and weapon on the statue will reflect the best quality items that you had in your inventory at the time, not necessarily the items that you had equipped. The game takes into account not just the value, but armor protection and enchantments.
