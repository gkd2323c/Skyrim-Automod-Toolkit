Aminyas' Journal **by [Aminyas](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Aminyas)** *A journal critical of King Joile's actions at the Bangkorai Pass*
---
Written with the inherent [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) taste for duplicity and over-florid conceit toward exceptionalism, the letters regarding the plight of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) at the hands of marauding [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) reached out to bind the races of [Men](Lore_Men.md) against the lesser [Mer](Lore_Mer.md).

The perpetual nature and inclination toward war for the followers of the dung of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) serves as bonding agent toward [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) only so long as it is able to bring the foolishly honorable remnants of the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) to place their back toward the Bretons, at which point King [Joile](Lore_Joile.md) bares his deceitful dagger.

It is claimed by certain historians and political commentators of the time, notably [Morgaulle Dechery](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Morgaulle_Dechery) of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), that by virtue of an outside order of so-called "[Sword Saints](Lore_Sword-singer.md)," the conflict did not culminate in the downfall of both [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) and Hammerfell at the hands of King Joile.

However, I posit that King Joile lacked the strength to carry through with his initiative, even if he had been able to capture the whole of the [Bangkorai Pass](Lore_Bangkorai_Pass.md), for the man lacked the strength to even safeguard his own person from one well-armed woman, [Makela Leki](Lore_Makela_Leki.md).
