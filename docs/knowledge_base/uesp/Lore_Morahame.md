[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/OB-place-Morahame.jpg/200px-OB-place-Morahame.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Morahame.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Morahame.jpg) Morahame circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Morahame (meaning "Forest Home or Daedra Home" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OLS-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NSP-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ayleidoon-3)</sup>) is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located east of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md), in the [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md) on the banks of the [Panther River](Lore_Panther_River.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBMap-5)</sup>

The ruins are notable for the variety of plants that can be found growing near the complex, including [fennel](Lore_Fennel.md), [fly amanita](Lore_Fly_Amanita.md), [foxglove](Lore_Foxglove.md), [ginseng](Lore_Ginseng.md), [green stain shelf](Lore_Green_Stain_Shelf.md), [monkshood](Lore_Monkshood.md), [primrose](Lore_Primrose.md), [domica redwort](Lore_Domica_Redwort.md), [summer bolete](Lore_Summer_Bolete.md), and [Nirnroot](Lore_Nirnroot.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup>

## History
During the time of the Ayleids, Morahame possessed a portal which led directly to the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md), [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md)'s plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). On the other side, the Ayleids constructed the Theater of the Ancestors, an opulent venue built atop the floating islands of the realm. The theater possessed a grand stage where the Ayleids performed plays, recitals, and other artistic displays to honor [Merid-Nunda](Lore_Merid-Nunda.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOTheater-6)</sup>

Following the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphiles.md) Ayleid city-states were persecuted by Alessian forces.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Morahame was eventually abandoned by its creators.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESO1-9)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the portal at Morahame was still functional. At this time, the Theater of the Ancestors was available for purchase as a private residence.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOTheater-6)</sup>

By the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins had become infested with [mud crabs](Lore_Mud_Crab.md), [rats](Lore_Rat.md), and other hostile creatures. Nothing remained of the portal.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup>
