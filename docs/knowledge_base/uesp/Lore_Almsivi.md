# Tribunal Temple

This article is about the religion. For the place, see [Mournhold Temple](Lore_Mournhold_Temple.md).

[![MW-banner-Temple.png](https://images.uesp.net/9/91/MW-banner-Temple.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Temple.png) The Tribunal Temple (also called the **Tribunal Cult**, the **Morrowind Temple**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Po M-1)</sup> the **Temple**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TR-2)</sup> the **Temple to the Tribunal**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MDGH-3)</sup> or the **New Temple Faith**)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lot S-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Va M-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WWDB-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MWDarius-7)</sup> was the native religion of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TR-2)</sup> They worshipped [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md), known together as the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) or Almsivi.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TR-2)</sup> Most people usually just call it "the Temple". They accept outlanders as members, but most of the few outlanders that do join, join only for the services provided.

The main Temple cities on mainland Morrowind are [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia_(city).md), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) and the Necropolis [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md). On [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), the Temple District included the city of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md), the fortress of [Ghostgate](Lore_Ghostgate.md), and all sacred and profane sites (including those [Blighted](Lore_The_Blight.md) areas inside the [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md)) and all unsettled and wilderness areas, as the [Treaty of the Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) with the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) maintained the island as a Temple preserve.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ASHOM-8)</sup> In practice, this district included all parts of Vvardenfell not claimed as Redoran, Hlaalu, or Telvanni Districts.

The Temple maintained less than favorable relations with most of the Imperial guilds of Morrowind,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> especially so since the Morrowind Temple was on the Proscribed List of Hostile Cults—with even courtesy membership in the Temple being forbidden to all members of the [Tiberius Imperator Cult](Lore_Talos_Cult.md) (that is, the Imperial Citizenship Cult).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Po M-1)</sup> However, it maintained strong relations with the pious [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md), the proud warriors of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) as well as amicable relations with [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) and [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md). When the Tribunal fell apart, the Tribunal Temple underwent a reformation, becoming the [New Temple](Lore_New_Temple.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TR-2)</sup>

### About the Temple
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/LG-cardart-Power_of_the_Almsivi_02.png/200px-LG-cardart-Power_of_the_Almsivi_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Power_of_the_Almsivi_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Power_of_the_Almsivi_02.png) The ALMSIVI, the gods of the Tribunal Temple The Temple was an organization created for the worship of the Tribunal. Vivec, Almalexia and Sotha Sil joined their powers to set up the Ghostfence, a magical boundary around [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), to contain the spread of the Blight plague. The history of the Tribunal goes back to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), after which the then mortal Almsivi exploited [Kagrenac's Tools](Lore_Kagrenac%27s_Tools.md) to make themselves [Gods](Lore_Gods.md).

Of the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of Morrowind, [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) was the one most deeply rooted in the Temple hierarchy, dominating both the priest caste as well as the [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) who serve as guardians of the Temple. [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) is one of the most pious houses; Redoran [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armiger.md) serve together with Ordinators as guardians of the Ghostfence and the Tribunal Temples. [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), being dominated by [Imperialized](Lore_Imperial.md) Dunmer, prays when it is convenient, maintaining the appearance of piety through lavishly-appointed structures. [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) has little use for the Tribunal Temple, as the Magelords themselves are as ancient as the Almsivi, letting its temples become overrun with dust and squatter merchants. [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), being the most traditional of the Great Houses, supports the Temple when it is convenient for their slave trade.

### Temple Structures
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ea/MW-misc-Tribunal.jpg/200px-MW-misc-Tribunal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Tribunal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Tribunal.jpg) Depiction of the Tribunal on wall of temple at Molag Mar Tribunal Temples were scattered all around Vvardenfell and most served as return points for [Almsivi Intervention](Lore_Almsivi_Intervention.md) spells. While many of the temples in outlying areas were freestanding structures built of packed mud in the Redoran style, it was not uncommon to see them adapted blend in with local styles, be that built on top of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md)'s cantons, or even integrated into Telvanni mushroom structures.

There were two Fanes on Vvardenfell: the High Fane on Vivec's Temple Canton, which housed the offices of the Archcanon and Temple administration, and the fortress-city of [Ghostgate](Lore_Ghostgate.md). The latter was the larger of the two structures, with a wizard's tower forming each wing, but both featured an elevated central platform which held an offering pit and one of each type of shrine.

### The New Temple
Following the disappearance of the Tribunes, the Temple entered a period of collapse, with some priests and adherents drifting towards the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBAvrus-10)</sup> After the devastation of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), the Temple—and, indeed, the Dunmer—entered a period of soul-searching and reorganization, much of which is lost to history.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TR-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-OBAvrus-10)</sup> Ultimately, as predicted by Vivec, the **New Temple** returned to the original Dunmer faith: "the worship of our ancestors and the three good daedra, Azura, Mephala and Boethiah".<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Vivec-11)</sup> The Tribunes were ultimately demoted to the still-venerated roles of "saints and heroes" to help ease the transition. The New Temple worships the three [Good Daedra](Lore_Good_Daedra.md) as "the Reclamations".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TR-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DBOthreloth-12)</sup>

### Heretical Tribunal Worship in the Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/SR-interior-Ashfall%27s_Tear.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Ashfall%27s_Tear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Ashfall%27s_Tear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Ashfall%27s_Tear.jpg) Statue to the Goddess Almalexia on Solstheim Despite the rise of the New Temple, worship of the Tribunal continued into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) and was driven underground. These cults were considered heretical by wider Dunmeri society and were persecuted by the New Temple.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> One cult of Almalexia worshippers constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md). The cultists raised a giant statue to the Goddess and planned to use old propaganda to restore widespread Tribunal worship.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Got T-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> These devotees counted several [Hands of Almalexia](Lore_Hands_of_Almalexia.md) among their ranks, a tradition which survived the Goddess's death.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> The Ashfall's Tear cult gathered and preserved many relics of the old Tribunal Temple. Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) was alerted to the cult's existence when they attempted to recover several additional artifacts. It is unknown if the Dragonborn agreed to help the cult or if it was wiped out.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Got T-15)</sup> One member of the cult, a Dunmer named Erden Relvel, became corrupted by the dangerous relics in the cult's possession and became a priest of [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md). He created [ash zombie](Lore_Ash_Zombie.md) minions and sent them to attack the temple at Ashfall's Tear, but he was ultimately killed by the Last Dragonborn.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
