*"Gnats who think themselves wolves. Ah, what folly."*
—Lokkestiiz Lokkestiiz was a powerful white [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means **Sky-Tempest-Ice** in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Odahviing-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Shout-2)</sup> who emerged in [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), following the release of the great Dragon [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md) and his Dragon followers from the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOTHOC-3)</sup> He and the dragon [Yolnahkriin](Lore_Yolnahkriin.md) followed the golden dragon [Nahviintaas](Lore_Nahviintaas.md), to pose as the returned [gods](Lore_Gods.md) of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) and the [moons](Lore_Moons.md) [Jone](Lore_Secunda.md) and [Jode](Lore_Masser.md), and take over the temple of [Sunspire](Lore_Sunspire.md), where they enacted a plan to attempt to expand its [Time Wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md) with the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) in the hopes of causing a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) to rewrite time and restore draconic rule. Lokkestiiz posed as the incarnation of Jone.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-6)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/ON-prerelease-Lokkestiiz.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Lokkestiiz.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Lokkestiiz.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Lokkestiiz.jpg) Lokkestiiz in his lair at Sunspire In 2E 582, ancient Dragons had appeared in Northern [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), reaping the land, following the release of the great dragon Kaalgrontiid and his dragon followers from the Halls of Colossus.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOTHOC-3)</sup> The golden dragon, Nahviintaas approached Sunspire with his contemporaries, Lokkestiiz and Yolnahkriin. They posed as Alkosh, Jone, and Jode respectively. Azin-jo, the Moon-Bishop in charge of Sunspire, openly accepted them and preached across Anequina about the return of "Alkosh." A wave of new followers came and rebuilt Sunspire as more adepts preached the return of "Alkosh" across Northern Elsweyr.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-4)</sup>

However, the dragon's true goal was to use the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to tear the Time Wound wider in order to cause a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) which they intended to use to "reclaim all that is and will be" and restore the "natural order" by "correcting the mortal mistake".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-6)</sup> While posing as gods the dragons would use their Voice to imbue their deceived followers with [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) power, transforming them into creatures composed simultaneously of ice and flame while also making them thralls of the dragon's will.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOMojha-8)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-6)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOJone Jode-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOIce Fire-10)</sup> The dragon's presence would also have an effect on the environment and conditions of the mountain, the dragon Yolnahkriin resided in the caldera, where his presence is said to have "stoked the sleeping mountain's fire", while the dragon Lokkestiiz resided in a windy frost covered overlook.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOMojha-8)</sup>

This news brought the attention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), who were sent to investigate. Lokkestiiz confronted the adventurers on a windy frost covered overlook, where he'd exhibit mastery of the elements of ice and lightning, showering the area in his electric breath and frost breath and summoning many [Storm Atronachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md) and [Frost Atronachs](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md) to aid him.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOLokkestiiz-11)</sup> The Undaunted ultimately defeated the fake Jone and Jode before they confronted Nahviintaas at the summit and the time-wound. Though he would utilize the power of the Time Wound to manipulate time, claiming his faithful had no need to fear death as he could restore them and they'd serve him eternally, and that he could make his foes relive their deaths over and over, the fake Alkosh was ultimately killed as well, and Sunspire was fully liberated from draconic control.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESONahviintaas-12)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-4)</sup> With the years he had left, Moon-Bishop Azin-jo vowed to restore this temple and because of his folly, he felt that it was his task alone to bear.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-6)</sup> But regardless, some priests stayed in Sunspire.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESONahviintaas-12)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAlkosh-4)</sup>

As his soul was not devoured by another dovah, Lokkestiiz was not killed permanently and can be raised again.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LAD-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Arngeir-14)</sup>
