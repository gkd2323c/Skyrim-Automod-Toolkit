— [Captain Tobias](Lore_Tobias.md), speaking of a prior encounter [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) had with Duadeen<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tobias-1)</sup> Duadeen is a [Half](Lore_Multiraciality.md) - [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tobias-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FHMC-3)</sup> mercenary lord who worked with the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) crime boss [S'rathra](Lore_S%27rathra.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Origin-4)</sup> He once had a negative encounter with the mercenaries [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) and [Tobias](Lore_Tobias.md) at [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md), though not much is known about the specifics.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tobias-1)</sup>

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), Cyrus was tasked with joining Duadeen on a mercenary mission, but received a letter from his old captain Tobias about his sister [Iszara](Lore_Iszara.md) who had gone missing. As a result, Cyrus instead chose to depart [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and return to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) to find his sister.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGS.27rathra-5)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Origin-4)</sup> When he attempted to depart from the province, Cyrus was confronted by Duadeen and his mercenary crew who didn't wish him to leave. After a bloody confrontation that saw Cyrus kill one of his rank, Duadeen allowed Cyrus to leave, but demanded that he never return to High Rock.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Origin-4)</sup>

A dubious tale of Cyrus' later exploits claims Duadeen went on to work under [Emperor](Lore_Emperor.md) [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) himself.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Tiber Meeting-6)</sup>

## Notes
- One legend claims Duadeen to have been one of the legendary [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FHMC-3)</sup> If true, this would make Duadeen several millennia old at the time of his confrontation with Cyrus which is nearly unheard of in humans, however some Akaviri, specifically [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md), have been attributed immortality in literature.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MA-7)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HOTFG-8)</sup> However, the text dubiously mentions many other anachronistic figures.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FHMC-3)</sup>
