# Fharun Clan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/ON-place-Fharun_Stronghold.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fharun_Stronghold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fharun_Stronghold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fharun_Stronghold.jpg) Fharun Stronghold ca. 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/ON-misc-Seal_of_Clan_Fharun.png/200px-ON-misc-Seal_of_Clan_Fharun.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Seal_of_Clan_Fharun.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Seal_of_Clan_Fharun.png) Seal of the Fharun Clan The Fharun Clan is a clan of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) that lives in the [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) region of northeastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Clan Fharun is one of the oldest and self-proclaimed strongest clans in the region, and they have the utmost respect for Orcish customs and devotion to [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONBazrag-1)</sup> The clan and their clanhold are the original namesake of the present-day kingdom and city-state, [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARFarrun-2)</sup>

## History
*Cursed are Fharun for not fighting to the death. At the first suggestion from the others, they slipped away so one clan might survive Orsinium's fall. Their departure was Orsinium's true betrayal.*
—Malacath's thoughts on Clan Fharun in the Siege of Orsinium The Fharun Clan has been around since at least the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) as members of the clan were present in the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md). However, their actions in the conflict have since been looked down upon in Orcish myth. The Fharun Clan did not fight to the absolute end and fled the battle to ensure their clan's survival. The Aspect of Malacath, who guides members of the [Wrathful Flame](Lore_Wrathful_Flame.md) through the Rites of Praise and Scorn claims that the Fharuns' departure was Orsinium's true betrayal.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONMalacath-3)</sup>

In the dark days after the Siege of Orsinium, Thruz gro-Fharun led a group of Orcish warriors called the Band of Fifty to defeat the [daedric titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md) that plagued southern Wrothgar called Zandadunoz the Ravager. After weeks of battle, the Band of Fifty defeated the cult dedicated to the titan and Thruz gro-Fharun defeated the [daedroth](Lore_Daedra.md) in the shadow of [Honor's Rest](Lore_Honor%27s_Rest.md) by ripping out its heart.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HOZ-4)</sup>

By the mid-Second Era, the Fharun Clan held the most victories in [Vosh Ball](Lore_Vosh_Ball.md), a sport popular among Wrothgarian Orcs. The clan won seven "series of the clan" by wide margins and their most prized member was [Bazrag gro-Fharun](Lore_Bazrag_gro-Fharun.md), who was also the clan's chieftain.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSOC-5)</sup> When his close friend and comrade, [Kurog gro-Bagrakh](Lore_Kurog_gro-Bagrakh.md) founded Orsinium as its first King, Chief Bazrag and many other clan chieftains were opposed to Forge-Mother Alga's push toward [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) worship.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSolgra-6)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Vosh Rakh](Lore_Vosh_Rakh.md), a cult of fanatic Trinimac worshippers invaded Fharun Stronghold and threw Chief Bazrag into Fharun Prison.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONUlsha-7)</sup> While the chief was imprisoned and under the guise of Malacath worshippers, the Vosh Rakh attacked the Temple of Ire in Orsinium in Bazrag's name and harmed the priesthood including High Priestess Solgra.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONKing-8)</sup> Bazrag's shield-wife, Ulsha asked Solgra for help and in order to avoid a possible civil war,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONSolgra Anger-9)</sup> she sent two warriors, [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md) to Fharun Stronghold and rescue the chieftain. Once they met Ulsha outside the stronghold, they were able to confirm their story and their lack of involvement in the temple's attack.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONChief-10)</sup>

After King Kurog and Forge-Mother Alga were killed, Chief Bazrag took up his role as the King of Orsinium.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONLive-11)</sup> And years later, their clanhold of Fharun became a modern [Breton kingdom](Lore_Empires.md#Kingdoms_of_High_Rock) known as Farrun.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARFarrun-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-EWR-12)</sup>
