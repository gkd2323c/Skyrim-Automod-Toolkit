Maldriths<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Chap Preview-1)</sup> are a cloven-hooved race of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) found in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s realm of Oblivion. In appearance, they resemble tall, bestial, [goat](Lore_Goat.md) -like humanoids. Maldriths possess blue fur, two curved horns, a skeletal visage, glowing blue eyes, and a tail. They have fangs which can clot with blue blood if removed.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Maldrith Fang-2)</sup> They wear a limited amount of clothing and typically wield a two-handed axe.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Maldrith ESO-3)</sup>

The Maldriths serve Molag Bal as inquisitors and roaming inspectors. They are sent to collect escaped prisoners, as well as minions who fail Molag Bal or display unsatisfactory performance. They are an extension of their master's will, carrying his authority with them in all of their dealings. The Maldriths terrify [Soul-Shriven](Lore_Soul-Shriven.md) and lesser Daedra alike.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MCo C-4)</sup>

A particular group of maldriths known as the Channelers are especially gifted at [portal magic](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md). The Channelers were present at the Ossein Cage in Coldharbour to make traversal within the Cage more difficult. This was done for the benefit of the [Dolorous Cista](Lore_Dolorous_Cista.md), a device capable of harvesting power from mortal pain. The frustration caused by the Cage's ever-shifting bridges was found to contribute to the agony of the mortal victims trapped within.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LDRResistance-5)</sup> The Channelers played an important role in disrupting the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md)'s ability to traverse the Ossein Cage during their attempted invasion circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), although ultimately the guild managed to bypass their magic and destroy the Dolorous Cista.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Vo A-6)</sup>
