# Golem

This article is about the creature. For the constellation, see [The Atronach](Lore_The_Atronach.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/ON-npc-Golem_Invoker.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Golem_Invoker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Golem_Invoker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Golem_Invoker.jpg) A Direnni Stone Golem
(ESO) Golems are artificial creatures created from non-living or inanimate matter and animated by [magic](Lore_Magic.md), often to act as [familiars](Lore_Familiar.md) or companions to powerful sorcerers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AGD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ATRMAN-2)</sup> They are also sometimes known as **Atronachs**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> an inconsistent term that applies to both [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) and non-daedric constructs.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_A)</sup> Golems generally are first crafted with the arcane arts using various materials, typically fashioned in a humanoid image, and animated to perform certain tasks such as serving as guardians.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AGD-1)</sup> Many golems serve as [statues](Lore_Statues.md) when dormant.

The magic of giving life to inanimate material is sometimes referred to as [atronachy](Lore_Atronachy.md). Golems are typically given life by imbuing them with a mortal [soul](Lore_Soul.md) or Daedric [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AHing-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KCAHC-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SIRV-7)</sup> Some are animated by other forms of magic such as [blood magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> More advanced golems, such as automatons, are animated in-part by their own mechanical parts, but some are also know to use filled [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) as a source of power.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ner-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONSM-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Sotha-11)</sup> Golems can be created with the ability to the speak,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Ice Man-12)</sup> and some seem to possess free will or higher thought.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AHing-4)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TMo F-13)</sup> Some golems have even been known to go rogue, lamenting their prior treatment.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AHing-4)</sup>

### Armor
Animated suits of armor are a somewhat common form of construct. These are often animated by souls or other [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) means.

[Mournful Aegises](Lore_Mournful_Aegis.md) are one example, being giant suits of armor animated by powerful [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) necromancy. [Soulrazer Knights](Lore_Soulrazer_Knight.md) are a fairly mysterious example as there are differing accounts of their nature.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONTSK-14)</sup> [Tsaesci](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) tombs are guarded by animated statues donned in their armor. Many histories speak about their ancestral armor, rising to defend itself, as if worn by angry spirits. But while many dismiss these claims, it is highly advised to pay heed to them when entering these tombs.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OABR-15)</sup> [Living Armor](Lore_Living_Armor.md) dedicated to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) were creations of [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) [smiths](Lore_Smithing.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DL-16)</sup>

### Factotums
The living god [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) created similarly advanced golems to dwarven animunculi known as [factotums](Lore_Factotum.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TMP-33)</sup><sup>:322</sup> These golems are said to serve as an extension of Sotha Sil's divine will. The factotums were created to maintain the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). As such, they have become an integral part of daily life for the inhabitants of the city, and are vital to running its everyday processes.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TMo F-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Sotha-11)</sup>

Believed to be a fusion of [brass](Lore_Brass.md) and [soul](Lore_Souls.md), factotums have a shared appearance, voice, and sense of purpose. They hold a structured artificial intelligence, often centered on their singular function. Their dialogue and actions are all based on this function, with only the commonality of a devotion to the Clockwork God. Attempting to deviate from this purpose is simply incomprehensible to them.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TMo F-13)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONTriv-34)</sup> The various purposes of these golems include repairs and maintenance, guarding Sotha Sil's most dangerous secrets, menial agricultural labor, preparing food, and providing medical attention to the citizenry.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-FC-35)</sup> Some unique factotums are artificial intelligences put in charge of supervising sections of the Clockwork City, such as [AIOS](Lore_AIOS.md) and the Assembly General.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36)</sup> Of particular note, the [Imperfect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperfect) is a factotum designed with advanced combat features. A new prototype is created and is improved upon each time it is destroyed. Sequence plaques are collected to be analyzed for their data after each combat test so further advancements are made. It is said that one day it shall obtain its true form, and fulfill its ultimate goal.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-CD-37)</sup>

Sotha Sil also created [fabricants](Lore_Fabricant.md), which differ from factotums in that they are part organic, part inorganic, constructed from an amalgam of grown flesh and metallic grafts and are more bestial in appearance.

### Necromantic
While many golems are animated/powered by souls in one way or another, some golems especially blur the lines between construct and [undead](Lore_Undead.md), such as in the case of the [Flesh Atronach](Lore_Flesh_Atronach.md), which can be created by imbuing a daedric or human [soul](Lore_Soul.md) into a flesh construct.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ESOUL-54)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-ESORD-55)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KCAHC-5)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-56)</sup> Or with the [Homonculus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Homonculus), a golem created from the body parts of several daedra.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-57)</sup>

Several types of golem, such as [Bone Colossi](Lore_Bone_Colossus.md), are made of out of the bones of various individuals or creatures.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Kyne C-58)</sup><sup>:122</sup> [Necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) have been able to create [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) constructs out of [bird](Lore_Bird.md) and reptile bones that resemble miniature [Bone Dragons](Lore_Bone_Dragon.md).<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-59)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-60)</sup> [Shambles](Lore_Shambles.md) appear to be made from the bones of various different types of creatures, haphazardly lashed together with wire or bits of cloth.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-SB-61)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Sh SI-62)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-63)</sup>

### Stone
An example of common golems are the magical [stone creatures](Lore_Stone_Golem.md) animated by the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) in their image to guard their sacred sites. These constructs are unable to be reasoned with.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONN-64)</sup> The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) used similar animated statues, known as [Ayleid Guardians](Lore_Ayleid_Guardian.md) or Stone Watchers.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-65)</sup> The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) were known to use stone golems to patrol their guild halls across Tamriel at night hours during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Arena Stone Gol-66)</sup>

An example of a greater stone golem is the [gargoyle](Lore_Gargoyle.md),<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-ONN-64)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-67)</sup> a non-humanoid golem used as a guardian by many different civilizations, such as the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md), [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), Ayleids, and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ESOG-68)</sup> Despite being constructs, gargoyles are considered by some to be misshapen humanoids and are capable of [copulation](Lore_Sex.md).<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TMo GS-69)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-70)</sup>
