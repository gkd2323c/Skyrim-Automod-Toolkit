# Highland Bretons

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/MW-item-Claymores.jpg/200px-MW-item-Claymores.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Claymores.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Claymores.jpg) Claymores (Morrowind) The Highland Bretons are a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) culture believed to inhabit highland strongholds,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POT-1)</sup> and renowned for the invention and usage of claymore.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGaro-2)</sup> They were pioneers in employing these formidable two-handed swords. However, the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) later would quickly incorporate these massive shock [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) into their valiant melee tactics. Designed for use by solitary hero-adventurers rather than close-order troops, their claymores were favored in encounters with formidable beasts and otherworldly creatures.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGaro-2)</sup>

There are a number of highland areas in High Rock, typically composed of woodland hills.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Climate Map-3)</sup> The windswept plateau of North [Kambria](Lore_Kambria.md) has many grim fortifications and small towns tucked into the folds and valleys that wind their way down to the northern coast.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1H-4)</sup> Remote highland regions include the [Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md) Foothills<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-dag ID-5)</sup> and the Dragon's Toe of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), near the border with [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Climate Map-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DFpreview RG-6)</sup> Witches and medicine men, barely distinguishable from [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) shamans, hold sway over the superstitious peasants of these highlands with feats of untutored, but often impressive, magical ability.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1H-4)</sup> [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md) is also found near a highland area of the province.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/DF-map-Iliac_Bay_Climate.png/200px-DF-map-Iliac_Bay_Climate.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_Climate.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay_Climate.png) Climate map of Iliac Bay with highlands marked (dark purple) Little is known about their role in the history of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), though the rugged highland strongholds and isolated valley settlements of the province have encouraged the fierce independence and contentious tribal nature of the various local Breton clans.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POT-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1H-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Morrowind Dialogue-8)</sup> Their culture was already active during the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), as Bretons were already forging claymores, such as the legendary [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) [Chrysamere](Lore_Chrysamere.md), during that period.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LAIA-9)</sup>

Many highlands forts are remnants of the constant warfare that have been the scourge of the province. In the past, each petty lord, secure in his castle, enriched himself with tribute from all who traversed his domain, a circumstance considered incompatible with the free flow of commerce. [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) had begun a program of demolition of these myriad fortresses as a policy meant to facilitate prosperity while removing a potential refuge for subversives.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1H-4)</sup>

Their contribution to the invention of the claymore was remembered, with [blacksmiths](Lore_Smithing.md) acknowledging it as late as [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGaro-2)</sup>
