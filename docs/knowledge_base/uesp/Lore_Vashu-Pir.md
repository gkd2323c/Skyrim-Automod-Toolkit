—Vashu-Pir<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSai-1)</sup> Vashu-Pir was the second leader of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), who, together with [Ja'darri](Lore_Ja%27darri.md) was responsible for the defeat of the Black Beast, Laatvulon.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GVPE-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONThe Dragonguard-3)</sup> He was remembered as one of the four greatest Grandmasters the Dragonguard have ever known.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSai-1)</sup> He served as the Grandmaster from [1E 2734](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2734) to [1E 2758](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2758).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GGB-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GVP-5)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_04.jpg/200px-ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_04.jpg) Laatvulon being sealed by the Dragonguard [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0c/ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Wind_Scour_Temple_02.jpg) Wind Scour Temple Little is known about Vashu-Pir prior to his ascension as a grandmaster of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). He succeeded the co-founder and the first leader of the organization [Ghelin-Brol](Lore_Ghelin-Brol.md) and became the second leader of the order in history. During Vashu-Pir's time as grandmaster between [1E 2734](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2734) - [2758](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2758),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GGB-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GVP-5)</sup> the Dragonguard worked to subdue [Laatvulon](Lore_Laatvulon.md), the Black Beast within [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). The grandmaster called to the moon-priests of [Pridehome](Lore_Pridehome.md) for help to defeat the dragon with the wisdom of Alkosh.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSai-1)</sup>

During his tenure [Ja'darri](Lore_Ja%27darri.md) was a prominent member of the Dragonguard working in Elsweyr where she formed an unlikely alliance with the red Dragon [Nahfahlaar](Lore_Nahfahlaar.md), who grew to respect her enough to give her a Dragon name and grant her a boon in the form of his horn, which was later named after her. Having been tasked by [Alkosh](Lore_Alkosh.md) to use the [Mask of Alkosh](Lore_Mask_of_Alkosh.md) to extinguish Laatvulon, Ja'darri petitioned Nahfahlaar to imbue the mask with his power to defeat his ancient foe Laatvulon. In an act he would later regret, he refused, leaving the Dragonguard to fight Laatvulon with limited power. They would ultimately succeed in imprisoning Laatvulon beneath Doomstone Keep, though Ja'darri died in the process.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONThe Dragonguard-3)</sup>

It is said Grandmaster Vashu-Pir was the one who defeated Laatvulon,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GVPE-2)</sup> and bards still sing about this glorious battle between Reman I's Dragonguard and a "great Black Beast" at Doomstone Keep.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

After his death he was buried in [Wind Scour Temple](Lore_Wind_Scour_Temple.md) in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and honored with a [statue](Lore_Statues.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-WST-7)</sup> and " fire and prayer".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GVP-5)</sup>
