## Vahtacen
[Vahtacen](Lore_Vahtacen.md) was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) settlement located at the swampy headwaters of the [Reed River](Lore_Reed_River.md) in the Mist Morrow Vale region of the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md), south of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md). It was the home of the Vahtache, an Ayleid faction.

In *[The Hosiric Lays](Lore_Mentioned_Texts.md#The_Hosiric_Lays)*, songs that date back to the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md), the "waving fronds of Vahtacen" are briefly mentioned. This gives credence to the belief that [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) possessed a subtropical climate during the time of the Ayleids.

## Valenwood
[Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) is a densely forested, sub-tropical region that encompasses the southwestern coastal plain of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). In the words of *A Pocket Guide to the Empire*, Valenwood is "a sea of endless green, a maze of foliage with half-hidden cities growing like blooms from a flower, the home of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) is Tamriel's garden." Alongside the Wood Elves, races like the [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), [Imga](Lore_Imga.md), and [Centaur](Lore_Centaur.md) also call the forest their home. One unusual feature of Valenwood is its gigantic, [migratory trees](Lore_Graht-oak.md), many so large that the Bosmer have built entire cities in their branches. [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) is one such tree, a mile-high specimen that serves as the capital of Valenwood and seat of its kings. The forest is often referred to by Bosmer as 'the Valenwood', designating the province as an entity in its own right.

## Valley of the Wind
The [Valley of the Wind](Lore_Valley_of_the_Wind.md) is a dried-out [foyada](Lore_Foyada.md) found on the northeast coast of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It is a long, winding valley that curves upward into a J-shape toward the end. It is completely uninhabited and out of the way from the nearest settlements, such as [Vos](Lore_Vos.md) to the east and [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md) to the north across the water. It is avoided by the local populace because they claim that *"the winds howl, and the spirits are always awake."*

## Valthume
[Valthume](Lore_Valthume.md) is an ancient Nordic barrow found on the slopes of the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md) in the southeastern part of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). This ancient tomb is the resting place of the tyrannical [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), [Hevnoraak](Lore_Hevnoraak.md). When Hevnoraak died, a warrior named [Valdar](Lore_Valdar.md) swore to guard his tomb for eternity, ensuring the priest never escaped.

## Valus Mountains
The [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md) are a mountain range on the border between [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They converge with the [Jerall](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) and [Velothi](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) ranges at [Mount Trolhetta](Lore_Mount_Trolhetta.md). They are sometimes considered an extension of the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md). [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) of the region often travel between the provinces through secret passageways and caves under the mountains. Because of this, cultural exchanges have taken place between tribes ever since. The [Kavas Rim Pass](Lore_Kavas_Rim_Pass.md) is a road through the provincial border.

## Vanua
[Vanua](Lore_Vanua.md) was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) settlement located along the southeastern shore of [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md) in ancient times. It contains the **Shrine of the Crusader**, a shrine built to honor [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md). It is mainly of Ayleid construction, but some of the interior architecture surrounding the Shrine is built in the Imperial style. The [ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) of the settlement were slowly engulfed by the lake over the centuries and it now lies on the lake bed.

## Varanis
[Varanis](Lore_Varanis.md) is an inland, rural region in southwestern [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md), which makes up the southern [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) seaboard in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). When compared with its other neighboring regions ([Zabamat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zabamat) and [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md)), much of the region is sparsely inhabited. But at the center of it all is its major population center; the city of [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md), which has historically been occupied by [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) and even once served as their capital. The [Tormented Spire](Lore_Tormented_Spire.md), a large, dormant volcano to the east dominates over the Varanis region. Two of Stonefalls' [Ashlander tribes](Lore_Ashlander.md) are located here in Varanis; the [Kagesh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagesh_Tribe) and the [Ulath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ulath_Tribe) tribes.

## Varen's Wall
[Varen's Wall](Lore_Varen%27s_Wall_(place).md) is a ruined stone wall that is found in the western highlands of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Varen's Wall is named after the eponymous Emperor of Cyrodiil, [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), one of the many kings that ruled during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). This wall was created to split the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) (primarily [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md)) from the rest of the Colovian Estates, in the efforts to protect Varen's homeland from the powerful [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) of Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md). The main gate of the stone wall is known as **Varen's Wall Gatehouse**.

## Varlaisvea
[Varlaisvea](Lore_Varlaisvea.md) (meaning "Sea of Stars" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md)) is a [ruined](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) complex located at the mouth of the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md), on the coast of the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md). It lies on the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) side of the border with the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It is located south of [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) and north of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md). During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the ruins were surrounded by cork oak and juniper trees, and were covered by hanging ivy.

Little is known of the original purpose of the Ayleid structures, though some claimed that soaking in the retreat's indoor crystal pool heals the spirit.

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the ruins were available for purchase as a private residence.

## Varondo
[Varondo](Lore_Varondo.md) is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located north of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md), near the [Brena River](Lore_Brena_River.md) in the Colovian Highlands. There are two large maze-like subsections within the ruins, *Varondo Beldastare* and *Varondo Varlasel*. Although the meaning of *Beldastare* is unknown, *Varlasel* translates to "Hall of Stars" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).

## Varsa Baalim
[Varsa Baalim](Lore_Varsa_Baalim.md) was an [Ayleidic](Lore_Ayleid.md) city located in the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), close to [Lake Canulus](Lore_Lake_Canulus.md) on the foothills of the [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md). It was a great, ringed, labyrinthine city constructed during the height of Ayleid rule to guard the Nefarivigum, a foul construct of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) used to test pilgrims seeking [Mehrunes Razor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mehrunes_Razor).

For years, the elves drove back the many pilgrims who came for the evil artifact. However, in the early days of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) managed to slip into the city unnoticed and turn the populace. Varsa Baalim was thrown into a gathering storm of madness and ruin, until it seemed that none were left to prevent the Razor from being recovered. Suddenly, the city was gone, when the mountains of the Eastern Niben swallowed Varsa Baalim and the Nefarivigum with it. Whether it was caused by a final fallback plan of the Ayleids, a natural cataclysm, or the touch of the [Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) is unknown.

For centuries the city remained hidden, entombed deep within the mountains, inhabited by the undead vampiric remnants of its populace. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a rogue [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) arch-mage named [Frathen Drothan](Lore_Frathen_Drothan.md) set out to use the Nefarivigum to recover Mehrunes Razor, which he could then use to overthrow the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). He gathered many mercenaries to his cause and formed the [Drothmeri Army](Lore_Drothmeri_Army.md), which marched into Cyrodiil and captured [Sundercliff Watch](Lore_Sundercliff_Watch.md), an abandoned fort built during the rule of the [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md). The fort had deep [iron](Lore_Iron.md) mines that extended far underground, and Drothan used these to excavate the entrance to Varsa Baalim. Once it was uncovered, he ventured into the ruins of the city with a large detachment of mercenaries and magically sealed the entrance to deter assassins. The vampires quickly fell upon the band, and Drothan fled, making his way to the Nefarivigum and leaving the scattered mercenaries to fight back against the vampires.

The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md), hearing rumors of Drothan's rebel army, entered Sundercliff Watch, fought through the Drothmeri Army, and broke the magical barrier preventing any from entering Varsa Baalim. The Hero traversed the ruins, where the battle between the Drothmeri and the vampires still raged, and entered the Nefarivigum to defeat Drothan and claim the Razor. Drothmeri failure was briefly documented in the book *[The Keepers of the Razor](Lore_The_Keepers_of_the_Razor.md)*.

## Vastyr
[Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md) is the largest settlement on the island of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md), the northernmost part of the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md). Although the city's foundation is built above the ruins of older civilizations that once inhabited the island, by the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), it sat as an impressive deepwater port town on the eponymous bay. It is the seat of power for [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md), one of two major houses across the Duchy of the Systres. The city boasts a unique triple blend of [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md), [mainland](Lore_Tamriel.md), and [druidic](Lore_Druids.md) cultures, serving as a gateway between modern, industrious Tamriel with the esoteric and ancient customs of the Galen Druids.

## Vateshran Hollows
[Vateshran Hollows](Lore_Vateshran_Hollows.md) is a sacred site of the ancient [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) rite of passage trial known as **Vateshran Rites**, (or **Vateshran's Rites**). It is located in a set of caverns in the southern parts of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), where the veil between the realms of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md), and one more realm known as the Champion's Circle is thin.

## Vaults of Gemin
The [Vaults of Gemin](Lore_Vaults_of_Gemin.md) are a ruined structure located in the [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) city-state of [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md).

The vaults once belonged to the [Conclave of Baal](Lore_Conclave_of_Baal.md), an organization based out of Stormhold. They were used to store an ancient tablet which could be used to decipher the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) in order to divine the current location of [Murkwood](Lore_Murkwood.md). However, the vaults sank into the ground when an associate of the organization cast a powerful spell with disastrous results. The crumbling ruins of the vault became partially flooded and infested with monsters.

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Hero.md) was sent into the ruins by the Conclave to recover the ancient tablet.

## Velothi Mountains
The [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md) are the mountain range separating [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). They are named after the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md), who led the Chimer to leave their ancestral homes and resettle in Morrowind.

The mountains form the eastern border of the Skyrim holds of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) and [The Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md). The southernmost part of the mountains borders the Morrowind region of [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) to the southeast. At the southern tip of the mountain range, where it meets the [Jerall](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) and [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md), the volcanic peak of [Trolhetta](Lore_Trolhetta.md) can be found. The ash produced by this and other volcanoes falls upon the Stonefalls region, providing precious fertilizer in an otherwise arid landscape.

The west and south-central region of Morrowind is mountainous and sparsely populated with few settlements. [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) occupies the heartland of Morrowind, comprising the lands south of the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md) and the eastern coast. [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) guards the western flank of Morrowind. The Nords of Skyrim are still resentful of the land lost in [1E 416](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_416), and enviously covet the House Redoran-ruled coast and highlands of Morrowind's northwest borders, at times assaulting them. The Nords have never been comfortable with the fact that the Dark Elves hold the west coast of the Inner Sea all the way up to [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md); and in some cases their maps elide that fact entirely. Dunmer bandits and raiders live throughout the Velothi Mountains and the general border in caves. They target both villages and farms all over eastern Skyrim and carry memories from the Nord's occupation of Morrowind.

## Velothis Haven
[Velothis Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Velothis_Haven) (otherwise known as **Glen Haven**) is a town located deep within the territory of the Great [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), within the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Velothis Haven is neighbored by several settlements, including [Firewatch](Lore_Firewatch.md) in the west, [Helnim Wall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Helnim_Wall) in the south, and [Karththor Heights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Karththor_Heights) in the east.

The settlement was either named after [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md), the father of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) race, or the [Velothi](Lore_Velothi.md), another name for the Chimeri people.

## Velyn Harbor
[Velyn Harbor](Lore_Velyn_Harbor.md) (also called **Port Velyn**) is a large coastal town near the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md) estuary in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The town is [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md)'s main port and was built by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md). Towns such as Velyn Harbor provided an opportunity for a cultural exchange between the Altmer and the local [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), like how much Bosmeri swordsmanship had been refined from Altmer influence and access to materials.

## Verglas Hollow
[Verglas Hollow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Verglas_Hollow) (also called **High Gate Ruins**) is an ancient Nordic barrow containing a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) on the [Frozen Coast](Lore_Frozen_Coast.md), a region between [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md) and [Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md) holds of northwest [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). ## Verkarth (Elsweyr)
[Verkarth](Lore_Verkarth_(Elsweyr).md) (also called **Verkarth Hills**) is a settlement found in the region of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), within the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). Verkarth was one of the original sixteen kingdoms of Elsweyr, known for their fierce warriors and soldiers. In that time, those soldiers were known as the do-radhin.

## Verkarth (Hammerfell)
[Verkarth City](Lore_Verkarth_(Hammerfell).md) (historically called **Verkath City**) was once the unholy city of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md), a legion of [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) that reigned over the vast wastes of central [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) sometime in the early-mid [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Previously known as the *"City of the Damned,"* it has since been rebuilt into a modern settlement by their vanquishers, the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) of the venerable [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md). The city's architecture resembles that of the [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) era, with hints of its [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) neighbors. However, older structures are reminiscent of a typical [Daedric ruin](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md), albeit much more refined.

## Vernim Woods
[Vernim Woods](Lore_Vernim_Woods.md) (also called **Vernim Wood**) is a forested area located in the northern side of the [Treva River](Lore_Treva_River.md) in [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md), as part of the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). At the center of the area is a small, wooded basin with a single nordic barrow aptly named the **Vault of Vernim** in the middle. The basin is surrounded by fall forest, large stones, dried creeks, and dirt roads that circle around it. By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), there was a settlement that shared the same name called Vernim Wood.

## Vile Laboratory
The [Vile Laboratory](Lore_Vile_Laboratory.md) is a location in the Black Garrison region of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), used by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) for his many horrific experiments. The building itself is an emulation of a [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) ruin, although the complex is crawling with undead and [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) guardians. It appears to be run by a [Xivilai](Lore_Xivilai.md) named Overseer Xvarik.

The laboratory has seemingly been in existence since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), as it is where the skeleton of the [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) [Boziikkodstrun](Lore_Boziikkodstrun.md) was brought after his flesh was consumed by Bal. Here the skeleton was infused with "blood-of-darkness", which re-awakened it as a [Vestige](Lore_Vestige_(creature).md). The skeleton was also modified according to Bal's own design, before being plunged into the Azure Chasm to create the first [Daedric Titan](Lore_Daedric_Titan.md). Indeed, experiments concerning [vestiges](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) and [souls](Lore_Souls.md) appear to be the norm in the Vile Laboratory. The [Xivkyn](Lore_Xivkyn.md) are the result of experiments with vestigial hybridization in the laboratory between [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) and Xivilai.

At some point, a [Light of Meridia](Lore_Light_of_Meridia.md) was taken to the laboratory for safekeeping, in an attempt to keep the artifact out of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md)'s clutches. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), two [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) members were imprisoned within the Vile Laboratory after being separated from the rest of the [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) invasion force. The mages, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named Gadris and a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) named Zur, underwent an experimental soul-meld process, and were subsequently cast out of the laboratory to die. Confined to a single body and desperate to separate their souls, the weakened pair began work on a transmutation potion to sneak back into the ruins. With help from the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), the "Soul-Meld Mage" managed to overpower Xvarik and reach the catalyst needed for the separation procedure, although only one of the mages survived. The Vestige also recovered the Light of Meridia from the ruins.

## Vilverin
[Vilverin](Lore_Vilverin.md) (also known as **Lake Mist Ruins**) is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin located in the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), on the northeastern shore of [Lake Rumare](Lore_Lake_Rumare.md). The complex is split into several subsections, including Canosel, Wendesel, and Sel Sancremathi; the latter two translate as "Journey Halls" and "Hall of Golden Homes" respectively in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).

## Vindasel
[Vindasel](Lore_Vindasel.md) is a small [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). However, it was once a settlement infamous for brutalizing slaves; legend speaks of [flesh sculptures](Lore_Flesh_Sculpture.md) known as the "Wailing Wheels of Vindasel", where the Ayleids derived strange pleasure by subjecting their slaves to this "art-torture",

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md) to the ruin to collect the legendary sword [Umbra](Lore_Umbra.md).

## Vitharn
[Vitharn](Lore_Vitharn.md) is a ruin in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). The ruin was once a thriving city and fortress, founded by fanatics in southern [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md). Four generations of Counts ruled over the city, planning to usurp the Isles from the Madgod, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). Ultimately, the incompetence of Vitharn's rulers and the [Manic](Lore_Mania.md) origin of the mother of the last count, Cirion, caused the Vassal Fanatics to invade the city and slaughter all therein, breaking their sworn allegiance to Vitharn.

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Sheogorath's [Champion](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) put to rest the ghosts of Vitharn who had been cursed to relive the night of their defeat over and over again. For more information, see the [main Lore article](Lore_Vitharn.md).

## Vivec
[Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) (often shortened to simply **Vivec**) was the largest city on the island-district of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). In its heyday, it was one of the largest cities in eastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and the holy seat of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) theology on Vvardenfell. It was named after [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) the Warrior-Poet, one of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god-kings that ruled Morrowind for most of its modern history and the one who made their home here. The city was known as the **City of Swords**, as in the laws that cut people into better shape.

After the [Ministry of Truth](Lore_Ministry_of_Truth.md) crashed onto the city, it caused [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to erupt and destroyed most of Vvardenfell. Those chain of events are remembered as the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), and while some settlements were later on the path toward rebuilding, Vivec City's fate is unknown. By the mid-first century of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), what remained of it was a smoldering and charred ruin in the center of the boiling [Scathing Bay](Lore_Scathing_Bay.md).

## Vivec's Antlers
[Vivec's Antlers](Lore_Vivec%27s_Antlers.md) is a historical battlefield found in [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md), a region of the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Not only have the fields of Vivec's Antlers been the site of several battles in Morrowind's history, but it contains the best-known example of a bizarre form of [Land Coral](Lore_Coral.md#Land_Coral) along the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md). The local [Dark Elves](Lore_Dunmer.md) claim that the Land Coral is both self-aware and thoughtful. Vivec's Antlers is the home of the [Coral Heart](Lore_Coral_Heart.md).

## Vlastarus
[Vlastarus](Lore_Vlastarus.md) was a settlement located in the [Greenmead](Lore_Greenmead.md) region of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s [West Weald](Lore_West_Weald.md), along the [Gold Road](Lore_Gold_Road.md) east of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md). The town was built around a lone and ancient [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) watchtower called the **Eye of the Greenmead**, which was built long before the town was ever founded. The tower was once located at the southern edge of the town.

## Void
The [Void](Lore_Void.md) (also called the **Outer Darkness**, the **Ancient Darkness**, the **[Great Darkness](Lore_Namira.md)**, and **Lorkh-Apeiron**) is the name applied to the dimensions between and beyond the known [realms](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md): [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), and [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). The term "void" is also occasionally used in a more limited capacity to refer directly to Oblivion. Legends say that [deities](Lore_Gods.md) wishing to create a new plane of existence must initially create a space for it in the Void. It is described as a place of endless night or endless nothingness, with very few things living in it. The [spirit](Lore_Ghost.md) of [Lucien Lachance](Lore_Lucien_Lachance.md) claimed that the Void is a cold place, one with no pain.

## Volenfell
[Volenfell](Lore_Volenfell.md) ("City of the Hammer" in [Dwemeris](Lore_Dwemeris.md)) was the capital of the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) clan of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), located in eastern [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md), southeast of the city of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md).

## Volunruud
[Volunruud](Lore_Volunruud.md) is an old Nordic barrow found at the edge of the snowy forests of [the Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Situated near the [Giants' Gap](Lore_Giants%27_Gap.md), the barrow was once a fortified wooden hall, that later became the tomb of its chieftain, [Kvenel the Tongue](Lore_Kvenel_the_Tongue.md). All that remains is a lowly barrow containing a [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) in the hills of the [Skyborn Range](Lore_Skyborn_Range.md), near the site of [Heljarchen](Lore_Heljarchen.md).

## Vos
[Vos](Lore_Vos.md) was a village located in the northeast corner of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). The settlement was historically occupied by [Velothi](Lore_Ashlanders.md#Velothi) farmers who grew [marshmerrow](Lore_Marshmerrow.md) and [wickwheat](Lore_Wickwheat.md), and faced constant harassment from [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) raiders. The town had also been territory of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), whether it was under the mage-lords of [Tel Mora](Lore_Tel_Mora.md) to the east or [Tel Vos](Lore_Tel_Vos.md) to the west.

## Vounoura
[Vounoura](Lore_Vounoura.md) is an island roughly a month's voyage away from [Tel Aruhn](Lore_Tel_Aruhn.md) in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). It serves as a haven where [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) assassins retire when they become too famous to continue their work.

## Lake Vread
**Lake Vread** is a body of water located somewhere in the eastern portion of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FSOBDE-2)</sup> A nomad wanderer discovered a series of vellum scrolls on the lake's shores, which brought to light [Baan Dar](Lore_Baan_Dar.md)'s legend.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TFSOBD-3)</sup> For years, scholars have tried to fully translate these texts, including [Arkan](Lore_Arkan.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) and his apprentice.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FSOBDE-2)</sup>

## Vulkwasten
[Vulkwasten](Lore_Vulkwasten.md) (also called **Vulkwasten Wood**) is a [graht-oak](Lore_Graht-oak.md) settlement located on the seaside hills of [Malabal Tor](Lore_Malabal_Tor.md), in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The town is well known for its fermented [alcoholic beverages](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md), which is made by a family of brewers for generations.

## Vundeheim
[Vundeheim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vundeheim) was a village in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) that was destroyed in ancient times. A [Word Wall](Lore_Word_Walls.md) was erected to commemorate the doomed villagers that were burned alive by the Lightning of Unending Sorrow.

## Vvardenfell

- *This article is about the island. For the island's central volcano, also known as Vvardenfell, see [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).*

[Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), also called the **Black Isle**, is a large island located inside the bay-like [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md), and is surrounded by mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) with the exception of its northern coast, which meets the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). The island is dominated by the great volcano, [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). The island itself is named after the original [Dwemeri](Lore_Dwemeris.md) name of Red Mountain, literally translating to "City of the Strong Shield". It is characterized by arid wastes, rocky highlands and coastal wetlands filled with unusual and unique flora and fauna.
