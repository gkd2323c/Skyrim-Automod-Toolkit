[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/SR-place-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg/200px-SR-place-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg) Castle Karstaag Ruins **Castle Karstaag Ruins** is an icy clearing outside [Castle Karstaag Caverns](Skyrim_Castle_Karstaag_Caverns.md).

It was once a large frozen keep, but time has ravaged the structure since the slaying of its master, the frost giant [Karstaag](Skyrim_Karstaag.md). It is now overrun by [rieklings](Skyrim_Riekling.md).

## Related Quests
- **[Summoning Karstaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summoning_Karstaag)**: Summon and defeat [Karstaag](Skyrim_Karstaag.md).

## Castle Karstaag Ruins
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6a/SR-interior-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Castle_Karstaag_Ruins.jpg) Rieklings The usual path to the ruins is through Castle Karstaag Caverns. Upon exiting the cavern, a leveled [mounted riekling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mounted_Riekling) is in front of you, and to your left on the north side of the clearing are two more rieklings near the throne holding the skeleton of Karstaag. To the south is a master-locked door leading to the rest of the island, the [key](Skyrim_Castle_Karstaag_Key.md) to which is carried by the mounted riekling. If you have already found [Karstaag's skull](Skyrim_Karstaag%27s_Skull.md), you can activate the throne, which will return the skull to the skeleton and summon Karstaag to rise as a ghostly frost giant.

## Notes
- Castle Karstaag is the name given to a [map icon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-mapicon-Castle_Karstaag.png) in the game files added by [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md), but the map icon was not used in the game.
- Castle Karstaag also appears in [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Castle_Karstaag), as an intact structure.
