Gardner was the King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) and the last monarch from the Gardner Dynasty. He descended from the dynasty's founder, [Farangel Gardner](Lore_Farangel_Gardner.md), a merchant prince who was the first King of Wayrest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WJOTB-1)</sup> During his reign, Gardner built new walls and battlements for [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), as the city had grown to the point that it had burst the bounds of the old walls.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup>

In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) and his horde of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) invaded [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and quickly overran [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md), leading to throngs of the region's inhabitants fleeing to Wayrest for protection. Wayrest's new fortifications allowed the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of Stormhaven to repulse the assaults of the Reachmen during the fifty-seven-day siege of the city. Eventually, Durcorach grew impatient with the siege, so he left enough troops in the revetments around Wayrest's walls to keep the defenders confined inside the city and then marched west to the region of [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup> [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland advised Gardner to use Wayrest's merchant ships as transports for the [Menevia](Lore_Menevia.md) Heavy Dragoons to attack Durcorach and his horde, advice that the king heeded. Emeric used the ships to carry the Menevia Heavy Dragoons along the coast to [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), where they killed Durcorach and routed the Reachmen.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-3)</sup>

A fortnight after the defeat of Durcorach and his horde, Gardner and the kings of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), and [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), realizing how weak their realms were individually, signed the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), a pact promising to defend each other's kingdoms and unite as one against all foreign foes.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> After the signing of the Daggerfall Covenant, Wayrest under Gardner was rebuilt stronger than ever and prospered.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-3)</sup> In [2E 561](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_561), Emeric, now Earl of Cumberland, proposed to Gardner to use the wealth resulting from the discovery of the largest [orichalcum](Lore_Orichalcum.md) lode in recorded history to enhance Wayrest's fleet and improve trade throughout High Rock.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup> Gardner approved Emeric's proposal, but before the fleet was completed, the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) swept through Wayrest and killed the king and the rest of the royal family in [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GH-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Emeric-6)</sup> This ended the Gardner Dynasty, leading to Emeric being elevated to the throne and House Cumberland becoming the second royal dynasty of Wayrest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-WJOTB-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTDC-4)</sup>

## Notes
- During his reign, King Gardner was known to have loved his [horses](Lore_Horse.md) so much that he had golden horseshoes forged for them. Examples of these were in circulation circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-goldhorseshoe-7)</sup>
