[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png/200px-DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Iliac_Bay.png) The Iliac Bay [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a2/MER-art-Daggerfall_Map.jpg/200px-MER-art-Daggerfall_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Daggerfall_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Daggerfall_Map.jpg) Map of Iliac Bay circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405) The Iliac Bay (also spelled **Illiac Bay**, sometimes called **Starfall Bay**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> is the large bay separating the southern coast of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) from the northern coast of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Southwestern High Rock and northern Hammerfell are often referred to collectively as the **Iliac Bay region**. The [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md)'s Northwest Fleet is charged with its protection.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1High-2)</sup>

## History
There is evidence of human settlements around Iliac Bay dating back at least a thousand years before recorded history.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AHOD-3)</sup> Before or soon after the start of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Aldmer came to High Rock from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) and intermarried with [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md), eventually creating the Breton race.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3High-4)</sup> The Skyrim Conquests brought most if not all of the High Rock portion of the Iliac Bay region under the control of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) around [1E 246](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_246), though they would lose control around [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369) because of [internal squabbles](Lore_War_of_Succession.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AHOD-3)</sup> They apparently never controlled [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md), which likely wasn't founded until several hundred years later.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WJOTB-5)</sup> Over time, the emerging Bretons peacefully supplanted the remaining Nordic population.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3High-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-1024x768.png) Map of the Iliac Bay region and Yokuda Before [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808), northern Hammerfell was populated by beastfolk and Nedic people. But then [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) warrior fleets, called the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), brought refugees fleeing a natural disaster.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-6)</sup> They drove away the natives, and since they were no longer Yokudans, they came to be called [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md), a corruption of "Ra Gada".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-6)</sup> They disliked outsiders of any sort, and this isolationism was the primary reason why the [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md) failed to gain a foothold here, and why traditional beliefs among the Redguard are still strong today.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1C-7)</sup> Although an [offshoot](Lore_Rourken.md) of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) settled here in [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420), they did not settle near the Iliac Bay region and disappeared before the Ra Gada arrived.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-8)</sup> Many Redguards dislike outsiders (although most Redguards around the Iliac Bay are relatively tolerant), and Hammerfell did not join the Septim Empire until [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-8)</sup>

The [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) was generally a difficult time to live in the Iliac Bay region. Half the population of the region died several times: once because of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md), once because of the War of Righteousness, and again because of an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) invasion.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AHOD-3)</sup> The general history of the Iliac Bay is said to be one of insurrections, wars, and plagues as the many provincial powers in Hammerfell and High Rock maneuvered for power.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AHOD-3)</sup>

The Third Era began relatively peacefully as the reigns of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) and [Pelagius I](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_I.md) brought about a golden age.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BHOTE-9)</sup> However, the Iliac Bay region became heavily involved in the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), when a disagreement over the succession of the Imperial throne led to seven years of war.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BHOTE-9)</sup> Years later, many kingdoms in the region managed to set aside their squabbles and unite against the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md), whose bid for power had brought strife and war throughout the region and beyond.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BHOTE-9)</sup> He was decisively defeated in a massive land and naval battle known as the [Battle of Firewaves](Lore_Battle_of_Firewaves.md) in [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TFOTU-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3V-11)</sup>

Relative peace existed for over a hundred years, until the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) in [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396), when Skyrim defeated the forces of High Rock and Hammerfell and expanded into their territory.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3High-4)</sup> In [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402), a land dispute between [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) erupted into the short [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md). It was described as typical of Iliac Bay warfare: "sincere, frighteningly violent, and peaceably resolved".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AHOD-3)</sup> However, a lot of bad blood remained between the citizenry of each province due to the viciousness of the war.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-FWOB-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NWOB-13)</sup>

### The Warp in the West
The [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) (also known as the **Miracle of Peace**, or the **Second Numidian Effect**) was a phenomenon that took place between the 9th and 11th of Frostfall in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), when events set in motion by the death of King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) culminated in a dramatic political upheaval across [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The event is often described as a [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) and compared to the [Middle Dawn](Lore_Dragon_Break.md#The_Middle_Dawn) of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), though its effects appear to have been confined to the Iliac Bay region.

An unknown [hero](Lore_The_Agent.md), believed to have been an agent of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), obtained the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), an artifact that could control [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), the ancient weapon once used by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to conquer [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). As the Iliac Bay's major powers sought the Totem, all eventually appear to have gained control of Numidium at once: [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), and the [Emperor](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), each using it to consolidate political power. Between the 9th and 11th of Frostfall, the political landscape of the Iliac Bay was radically transformed: where forty-four city-states had existed before, only four powers remained afterward—Sentinel, Wayrest, Daggerfall, and Orsinium—all swearing loyalty to the Emperor.

In the aftermath of the Warp, the region gradually stabilized under its new borders. [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) spread across the eastern coast of the bay and later passed into the hands of Queen [Elysana](Lore_Elysana.md), while [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) secured much of the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) and the profitable [Bjoulsae](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) river coast under King [Gortwog](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md). [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) came to encompass much of western High Rock under King [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md) and Queen [Aubk-I](Lore_Aubk-i.md). [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) gained more land than any other kingdom in the region, extending across much of the southern Iliac Bay under [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md), becoming the dominant kingdom of the bay, while Lhotun himself was counted among the most powerful kings in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). By the late Third Era, the four kingdoms held their new borders in relative peace, and the Iliac Bay no longer had any disputed territories.

Following this Miracle of Peace, Sentinel grew to encompass the entirety of the Iliac Bay's southeast shore, from Abibon-Gora to Satakalaam, and Daggerfall and Wayrest took over large tracts of territory in southern High Rock.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TWITW-14)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-8)</sup> The peace between Daggerfall and Sentinel is apparently holding. While some minor border skirmishes still occur occasionally, and emerging alliances may threaten to break the peace, no human kingdoms seem willing to reignite conflicts.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3High-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-8)</sup> At least, not against each other.

### Orsinium
[Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) have been present around the Iliac Bay since time immemorial and have been a relatively constant security concern to their neighbors, whether or not they control territory of their own. Redguard and Breton kingdoms united to annihilate the Orc province in eastern High Rock, Orsinium, in the First Era.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TPC-15)</sup> A new iteration, sometimes called Nova Orsinium, emerged and prospered in the Third Era, and rose to become a major power in the Iliac Bay region following the Miracle of Peace.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3High-4)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE30-16)</sup> However, the peace for them did not last long, as early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Breton and Redguard kingdoms once again put aside their differences to attack the Orcs, eliminating Orsinium once again, and many Orc refugees were escorted from the chaos into western Skyrim.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LOS-17)</sup>

## Geography
The waters of the Iliac Bay are known for being relatively tranquil. The great rolling waves which characterize the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md) and the oceans disappear upon entering the bay.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGTHigh Rock-18)</sup> From there, the bay stretches northeast into the continent, to the mouth of the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md). Together, the bay and the river roughly separate High Rock and Hammerfell. The area of High Rock near the Iliac Bay primarily has a temperate climate and rolling hills, while Hammerfell's portion tends to be more arid, rocky, and inhospitable.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3High-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-8)</sup> The [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md) of High Rock cap the Iliac Bay region to the north, while the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) of Hammerfell form most of the eastern border, along with the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r.md). Many islands dot the Bay itself, notably [Betony](Lore_Betony.md), [Cybiades](Lore_Cybiades.md), and the [Isle of Balfiera](Lore_Isle_of_Balfiera.md).

Redguards and Bretons predominantly populate the modern-day Iliac Bay. They worship a wide variety of [deities](Lore_Gods.md) and the rich history of the Iliac Bay has imbued the region with diverse cultures, traditions, and numerous unique holidays.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-HOTIB-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-VOF-20)</sup> There are modern professions of all kinds across the Iliac Bay; besides the typical soldiers, priests, traders, and thieves, there are artists, writers, weavers, scholars, even bankers.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SW-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BB-22)</sup> Vampires have always been a significant concern; numerous clans of them pervade the area.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-VOTIB-23)</sup> Fears of Akaviri pirates and invasions have also been with the people of the Iliac Bay since the First Era, although they haven't been seen for quite a while.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-WJOTB-5)</sup> A great variety of interesting creatures roam Iliac Bay, such as Dragonlings, large flying lizards which physically resemble the even larger, unrelated race of [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TBD-24)</sup>

### Notable Places
- **[Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md)**: Found on a small island in the Isle of Balfiera, it is considered the oldest structure in all of Tamriel, and a neutral meeting place for negotiating provinces.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3High-4)</sup>
- **[The City of Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md)**: One of the oldest and largest cities of High Rock, it has long considered itself the capital of High Rock, by virtue of its antiquity, prominence, and prosperity.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1High-2)</sup>
- **[Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md)**: One of the biggest tourist destinations in all of Hammerfell.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-6)</sup>
