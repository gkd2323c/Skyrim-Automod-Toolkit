Alduin (also spelled **Ald'uin**<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-500COTOYTR-1)</sup> or simply **Ald**),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ALAS-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-SSOS-3)</sup> known as the **World-Eater**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> the **First Dragon**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSo G-5)</sup> the **Dragon Who Eats the World**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-APQ-6)</sup> **Time-Eater**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-7)</sup> the **Twilight God**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DATN-8)</sup> the **Worm**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRTsun-9)</sup> the **Dragon King**,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-10)</sup> and the **Firstborn**<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SI-11)</sup> is an immensely powerful black [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md). He is known as a malevolent being who destroys the world periodically, and the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) believe that his reappearance heralds the end of the current [kalpa](Lore_Kalpa.md) and the beginning of the next.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AIR-12)</sup> He is the self-proclaimed First-Born of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-A-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-14)</sup> and supposedly an aspect of him,<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-7F-15)</sup> having also been described as a "fragment of Akatosh's soul".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Vonos Journal-16)</sup> Alduin is said to have begun as "the wisest and most far-seeing" of all Dragons.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-14)</sup>

His name can be separated into "Al Du In", which means "Destroyer Devour Master" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Prima-17)</sup>

## History

### Origins
Error creating thumbnail: File missing [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-texture-Dragonclash.png) Dragonclash, a symbol depicting a clash of Alduin and Akatosh Alduin and his various aspects (such as the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) [Thartaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thartaag) or the Khajiiti Alkhan) serve as the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) God of the End Times. Though some mortal sources suggest that Alduin is a Nordic cultural equivalent to the [Time Dragon](Lore_Akatosh.md) given his nature as a Dragon and his relation to time,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DATN-8)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TTOD-18)</sup> others suggest a more complicated lineage.

Alduin describes himself as "firstborn of Akatosh",<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-A-13)</sup> with other sources (both mortal and draconic) corroborating this claim.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-14)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DC-19)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Vonos Journal-16)</sup> Some sources make no reference to the father-son relationship between Alduin and Akatosh, but do make a point to distinguish the two from one another.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AIR-12)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONAT-20)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TAAD-21)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-APQ-6)</sup> Other parts of Nord folklore describe Alduin as having been shed from Aka-Tusk, an elder dragon totem from the time of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-7F-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-MK-22)</sup> Indeed, all dragons are said to be in some manner related to Akatosh, being his children and/or part of him that split off when time began.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SI-11)</sup> Similarly, Pre-Riddle'thar [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) mythology remembers Alkhan as the firstborn offspring of [Akha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akha) and a mysterious entity only known as a "[demon of fire and shadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Demon_of_Fire_and_Shadow)".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWa S-23)</sup> While Akha is linked to the Khajiiti Alkosh, they are at least partially distinct figures.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWa S-23)</sup>

### Dawn Era
Alduin is ascribed the creation of the World in certain Nordic mythology, by way of destroying the prior world to begin the current one in the form of a great firestorm. While not chief, for this act of creation Alduin is considered the wellspring, the origin, of the nordic pantheon.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup> At least one Nordic folklore tale speaks to one of these World Eating events. In a previous [iteration of the world](Lore_Kalpa.md), Alduin was subject to a scheme by [The Greedy Man](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Greedy_Man) and the Demon King [Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), who sought to trap Alduin in a dying world and kill him by tricking him into consuming more than he was able. This scheme was unsuccessful, however, and resulted in Dagon's transformation into the Prince of Destruction.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-7F-15)</sup>

The World-Eater was said to have participated in key battles during the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) with [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) and his various cultural interpretations or aspects.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWa S-23)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-7)</sup> In the Khajiiti interpretation, Alkhan was said to have been killed by Lorkhaj and his companions during the early days of creation but per his immortal nature as a son of Akha, it was thought he'd eventually return from the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) in time.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWa S-23)</sup>

Dragons had existed since the Dawn of time, but they were "wild" and "uncivilized" creatures.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SI-11)</sup> Alduin returned to the affairs of the world and created their civilization, establishing order among the dragons, and becoming their leader.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SI-11)</sup> It is said the Dragons ruled over all of Mundus before the birth of men.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Hrothgar-24)</sup>

### Dragon Cult
This leadership over dragonkind gradually extended to rulership over [mankind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Human). This [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) originated in [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) but would eventually come south to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) where the humans were lorded over with a much crueler hand.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TDW-25)</sup> Beyond the more historically documented rule in Tamriel and Atmora, the Dragons at the height of their power were said to rule the whole [world](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TGATT-26)</sup> Though all dragons swore fealty to Alduin,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Arngeir-27)</sup> his younger brother, [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md), served as his chief lieutenant and right hand.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SREsbern-28)</sup> Others like [Kaalgrontiid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaalgrontiid) traveled further south, seeking to establish their own kingdoms outside of Alduin's direct supervision.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LADragons-29)</sup> Under the dragons were certain humans who held a special connection with the dragons known as the [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TDW-25)</sup> At the bottom rung lay the mortal servants who lived under the heel of these cruel masters.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TDW-25)</sup> It has been theorised that Alduin administered sacrificial rituals of the cult that might have involved dragons themselves suffering the sacrifice.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-RDS-30)</sup>

[Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md) was a significant location for the Dragon Cult, known as one of Alduin's high fanes.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Odahviing-31)</sup> From here, Alduin could enter a portal that led directly to Sovngarde, allowing him to eat the souls of the Nordic dead, a privilege he guarded jealously.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Odahviing-31)</sup> The Dragon Priests of Skuldafn (such as Nahkriin) were said to have stood at Alduin's right hand.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-32)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4d/SR-concept-Dragon_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Dragon_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Dragon_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Dragon_Mural.jpg) Dragon Cult Alduin mural Paarthurnax would eventually have a change of heart.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Arngeir-27)</sup> According to the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) of High Hrothgar, Paarthurnax began to pity mankind and was called upon by the goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) to set things right.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Arngeir-27)</sup> Where before the Thu'um had been the sole domain of the Dragons and the chosen few who led the Dragon Cult as priests, Paarthurnax taught it to the mortals of the land in order to give them a means to combat their overlords.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-14)</sup> Some sources claim this was at the command of Akatosh himself.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TDW-25)</sup> These humans succeeded in defeating the Dragon Priests and slaughtered the dragons in great number,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TDW-25)</sup> but had yet to face their most daunting task of all - confronting the cult's almighty God-King, the World-Eater Alduin.

### Banishment and Looming Return
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/LG-cardart-Cast_Into_Time.png/200px-LG-cardart-Cast_Into_Time.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Cast_Into_Time.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Cast_Into_Time.png) Alduin's banishment Three of the greatest [Proto-Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) heroes of the age, [Felldir the Old](Lore_Felldir_the_Old.md), [Hakon One-Eye](Lore_Hakon_One-Eye.md) and [Gormlaith Golden-Hilt](Lore_Gormlaith_Golden-Hilt.md), took the task of defeating Alduin upon themselves.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-14)</sup> They initially attempted to recruit the Dragon Priest [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md) who thanks to his unique nature as the first [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) in history had a tremendous edge against the foe, but Miraak refused to give aid.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Miraak-33)</sup>

Near the end of the war, these ancient heroes confronted Alduin at the summit of the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Skyrim AB-34)</sup> With no conventional means to defeat the wyrm, Felldir the Old used an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) to perform an incantation that ejected Alduin from their time, propelling him into the future and leaving behind a [Time Wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Skyrim AB-34)</sup>

The legacy of Alduin in Nordic culture following his banishment varied slightly, but was mostly embodied by reverence and fear. Most Nords still regarded Alduin as a [god](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods), namely the Dragon totem of the old Nordic animal totem religion. As a Twilight God who would usher in the next cycle,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DATN-8)</sup> these Nords held great respect and fear for Alduin, but dared not worship him.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSo G-5)</sup> Those who did worship him did so in secret, as it was illegal.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONContr J-35)</sup> Peasants spoke of Alduin in hushed tones as they clutched their prayer stones.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONArlof-36)</sup> Alduin was venerated on [winter solstices](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md#Winter_Solstice) at ancient Dragon Cult temples where prayers and offerings were made in the hopes of the great god of time continuing his slumber for years to come.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DATN-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-GTNTR-37)</sup> At winter solstice, Dragon Cultists gather to award their newest initiates specially crafted stone piercings. Some claim Alduin's blood blessed the nose studs, turning them into rubies the shade of his eyes.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ESODCO-38)</sup> The Song of Endings described Alduin as an "appetite with wings", and stated that, whether by flame or by teeth, he consumes all.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOWEF-39)</sup> Some followers of ancient Nord traditions adorn themselves with imagery of a clash between Alduin and Akatosh.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ESODCBM-40)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/SR-concept-6877564077.jpg/200px-SR-concept-6877564077.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877564077.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877564077.jpg) The host of several battles between Shor and Alduin, Sovngarde In [1E 533](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_533) during the reign of High King [Ysmir Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md), the Nordic Testing God [Orkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orkey) summoned an apparition of Alduin to help deal with the king. Despite his banishment, this ghost of Alduin Time-Eater was able to manifest and pose a threat. In this ghostly state, Alduin managed to eat the ages of the majority of the Nordic populace down to six years old, King Wulfharth included. Boy Wulfharth pleaded with the Dead God [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor) who had fought Alduin previously in ancient times to help the Nords. Shor's own ghost then did battle with Alduin's in [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md). Shor defeated this shade of the Time-Eater, placing ruin on Orkey's chosen people, the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-7)</sup>

Wulfharth, for his part, learned a new thu'um while watching the battle known as What Happens When You Shake the Dragon Just So. Wulfharth used this thu'um to restore the Nords back to their proper ages - inadvertently aging himself to death.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FSOKW-7)</sup> Some tellings forget Wulfharth's part in the tale, and claim that through unknown means, Shor alone restored the Nords back to their proper ages.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup>

In [1E 2812](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2812) Emperor Reman II permitted the construction of Alduin's Wall within [Sky Haven Temple](Lore_Sky_Haven_Temple.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AOTD-41)</sup> It is considered to be one of the best preserved examples of early [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) sculptural relief, as well as a wonder of the ancient world. Its purpose was to record the accumulated dragonlore and prophecy that the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) possessed at the time.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SREsbern-28)</sup> Its main focus was a pictorial representation of the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md). The prophecy foretold of five key historical events that would preface Alduin's return after his expulsion during the Dragon War.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TBot D-42)</sup> Given that Alduin's return was inevitable, the Dragonguard believed that the wall was their gift to the generations that were to follow.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SREsbern-28)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg) Alduin's Wall Craftsmen from temples across the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) were called upon to work on the wall, while Grandmaster [Jaiv-Yora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jaiv-Yora) of the Dragonguard oversaw the construction of the wall,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-GJY-43)</sup> and after six years it was completed. Emperor Reman II returned to the temple in [1E 2818](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2818) to officially dedicate the wall. The Blood Seal outside the temple was consecrated in the presence of all Akaviri Dragonguard in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). This allowed the temple to be sealed, only ever to be opened by a Dragonborn to unlock its knowledge and power.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AOTD-41)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ca/ON-render-Akatosh_vs._Alduin_Crate.jpg/200px-ON-render-Akatosh_vs._Alduin_Crate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Akatosh_vs._Alduin_Crate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Akatosh_vs._Alduin_Crate.jpg) A clash between followers of Alduin and Akatosh The presence of dragons caused distress for the Nords who viewed them as the servants of Alduin.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ESODragon Rise-44)</sup> Some lighthearted level of respect could be shown for the World Eater in Nord culture, usually manifesting in small ways such as naming one's pet after him.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONAW-45)</sup>

Nords who viewed Alduin as their local variation of Akatosh were perplexed by the [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) fascination with Akatosh, as they worked diligently to keep the Time Dragon asleep while their southern cousins sought his attention.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DATN-8)</sup> Those who did not view Alduin as an aspect of Akatosh instead viewed Akatosh as the Great Dragon and Dragon totem deity.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DC-19)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TAAD-21)</sup> Some even called Alduin's godhood into question, placing him below Akatosh in the order of the cosmos.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AIR-12)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TAAD-21)</sup> Regardless, none denied his role as the harbinger of the End Times. Some outsiders who did not understand this dynamic of acknowledgment and respect, but lack of worship for the World Eater in Nordic culture claim that Nords worshipped the "heathen god" Alduin.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-JD-46)</sup>

In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), other prophetic deductions formed around Alduin's return. The revived [Mythic Dawn Cult](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) leader [Vonos](Lore_Vonos.md) had visions which eventually led to the realization that Alduin would re-emerge into the world, the fated Last Dragonborn would rise, and that the two beings would clash despite both being shards of Akatosh's soul.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Vonos Journal-16)</sup>

### Return
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png/200px-LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Alduin_Alt.png) Alduin in Helgen In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Alduin [reappeared](Lore_Return_of_the_Dragons.md) at the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md), cast forth from the Time-Wound created when he had been banished thousands of years before. Perched here was his brother, Paarthurnax, who had awaited his return at the summit of the Throat of the World ever since his banishment.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-14)</sup> However, Paarthurnax was unable to contain Alduin, and the World-Eater descended upon the region. In the midst of all of this, several members of the [Stormcloak rebellion](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) were being executed by Imperial Legionnaires in [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md), including the rebel leader [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-SRUnbound-47)</sup> Accidentally caught up in this string of executions was a [prisoner](Lore_Hero.md) who was detained for crossing the border.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-SRUnbound-47)</sup>

Before this prisoner or Ulfric Stormcloak could be executed, Alduin descended upon Helgen and quickly reduced the settlement to rubble. Several parties were able to narrowly escape the destruction including Ulfric and the prisoner.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-SRUnbound-47)</sup> Ulfric Stormcloak's resulting escape prolonged the civil war in Skyrim and secured a steady flow of souls for Alduin to consume in Sovngarde.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-SRCivil-48)</sup> Alduin began to resurrect his fallen brethren, using a powerful thu'um (seemingly known only to him) to undo their deaths and restore flesh to their bones.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-SRBlade-49)</sup> Dragon Priests and [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) began to stir in ancient ruins as well, setting the revitalization of Alduin's army and the Dragon Cult in motion. The mysterious prisoner was eventually revealed to be the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), hero of prophecy.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-SRDragon Rising-50)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Bane_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Bane_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Bane_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Bane_02.jpg) Alduin does battle with Paarthurnax As a Dragonborn, the hero was able to wield the thu'um with preternatural ease, learning several powerful shouts that allowed them to slay Alduin's dragon allies and absorb their souls, increasing the hero's strength and ensuring Alduin could not revive them again. With the aid of an Elder Scroll, the Last Dragonborn learned the forgotten thu'um known as Dragonrend that served as a great weapon against dragonkind, forcing understanding of mortality into their immortal minds.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Skyrim AB-34)</sup> With this new magic, Paarthurnax and the hero confronted Alduin on the Throat of the World, where the two sides fought to a stalemate. Given his unique nature, it was seemingly impossible to slay Alduin on Mundus.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Skyrim AB-34)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/SR-quest-Dragonslayer.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Dragonslayer.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragonslayer.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Dragonslayer.jpg) Alduin raining down fury during his final stand in Sovngarde Eventually, the Dragonborn gained access to Sovngarde, where Alduin had fled to regain strength.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Skyrim Dragonslayer-51)</sup> Shor gave council for the heroes of Sovngarde to stand down, allowing the Last Dragonborn to face Alduin as foretold with the help of the three ancient Nord heroes who banished Alduin in the first place.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-SRYsgramor-52)</sup> With that, the quartet finally vanquished the World-Eater.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Skyrim Dragonslayer-51)</sup> Alduin's soul was not absorbed by the Dragonborn, and he may not have been completely destroyed;<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Skyrim Dragonslayer-51)</sup> it is possible that he will one day return to fulfill his role as the World-Eater.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Arngeir-27)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWa S-23)</sup>

The surviving dragons had varying reactions to Alduin's vanquishing. Paarthurnax lamented the death of his brother but intended to teach the surviving dragons the philosophy he had come to adopt, the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-14)</sup> Some dragons viewed this attempt at new leadership by Paarthurnax as tyranny, instead bowing to the superiority of the Last Dragonborn, who, as the slayer of Alduin, they felt was the rightful heir to his lordship.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Odahviing-31)</sup>

Even realms beyond the Mundus felt ripples from the World-Eater's destruction. A dragon in the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md) by the name of [Durnehviir](Lore_Durnehviir.md) learned of the event and proclaimed the Last Dragonborn a true Dovah for the deed.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-DBDurnehviir-53)</sup> Similarly, Miraak, the First Dragonborn in history who was residing in [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), sensed that the Last Dragonborn had slain Alduin and commended them for it.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Miraak-33)</sup>

## Aspects
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/MER-Giclee-Alduin-_.jpeg/200px-MER-Giclee-Alduin-_.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-Giclee-Alduin-_.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-Giclee-Alduin-_.jpeg) Alduin and the Last Dragonborn While it is a matter of debate if Alduin serves as a local variant of the likes of the Imperial Akatosh, the Khajiiti Alkosh, and the Elven [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel) who govern time in the general sense, it can be inferred that Alduin governs the sphere of the End of Times giving him more definitive ties to aspects that share that duty.

### [Alkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkhan)
To the Ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), he is a Wandering Spirit known as **Alkhan**, **The Scaled Prince**, and the **Firstborn of [Akha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akha)**. Texts that predate the [Riddle'Thar Epiphany](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riddle%27Thar) claim that Alkhan was born when Akha bred with a demon of fire and shadow. Alkhan grew in size with each soul he ate, and ever hungered for the crown of [Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh). He was slain by [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj) and his companions, but will return one day along the Many Paths. Lorkhaj, [Khenarthi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Khenarthi), and Alkosh consider him to be an enemy.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWa S-23)</sup>

### [Thartaag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thartaag)
The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) recognize a version of the [Adversary](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Adversary) who will show up at the End of Seasons under the name **Thartaag the World-Devourer**.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ASS-54)</sup>

### [All-Maker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:All-Maker)
In a claim that is seemingly at odds with the idea of Alduin being connected to the Skaal's malevolent Adversary, some sources instead postulate that the benevolent **All-Maker** of the Skaal is a distant echo of Alduin. The connection is made to highlight incredulty, however, and is not a seriously proposed by the sources that suggest it.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TGATT-26)</sup>

### [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal)
**Satakal** of the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) pantheon shares aspects of, and is much like the Nordic Alduin, destroying one world to begin the next.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOF-4)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-EGTHammerfell-55)</sup> The two beings also share similar appellations, with Alduin being known as the First Dragon, and Satakal the **First Wyrm** and **First Serpent**.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-DFRaces-56)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Satakal Skinrazer-57)</sup>

### [Atakota](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atakota)
The Argonian **Atakota** shares some traits with Alduin: each of its scales was a world that it devoured.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Cot R-58)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-furnishing-World-Eater_Effigy.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-World-Eater_Effigy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-World-Eater_Effigy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-World-Eater_Effigy.jpg) World-Eater Effigy [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/ON-polymorph-World-Eater%27s_Living_Armor.jpg/200px-ON-polymorph-World-Eater%27s_Living_Armor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-polymorph-World-Eater%27s_Living_Armor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-polymorph-World-Eater%27s_Living_Armor.jpg) World Eater's Living Armor For centuries, the followers, cultists and warriors of Alduin, the "First-Born" of the Dragons and the corruption of a dark prophecy, are said to have clashed with those of Akatosh, a terrible fight "within the family".<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Av AC-59)</sup> Some followers of ancient Nordic traditions thus adorn themselves with imagery of a clash between Akatosh and Alduin. Their markings symbolize that there must be balance in all things and that every story's beginning must come to its inevitable end, that there is no light without darkness, no fullness without hunger, and no life without death. These are thought to be the teachings of the great Dragons.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-DFM-60)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-DBM-61)</sup> The faithful also symbolize Akatosh and Alduin through twin orbs of sacred fire, one golden and one crimson, swirling around each other.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-DFO-62)</sup> According to the *Song of Endings*, the faithful consider Alduin to be "but an appetite with wings" who consumes all, whether by fire or teeth.

Loyal followers of Alduin are said to worship at effigies surging with the World-Eater's power in order to gain illusory wings and the Eyes of Alduin.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-WEE-63)</sup> Dragon Cultists of Alduin gather during the [Winter Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md) in order to award new initiates with specially crafted stone piercings. Some claim Alduin's blood sometimes blesses the nose studs, turning them into rubies the shade of his eyes.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-DCPiercings-64)</sup> Dedicated followers of Alduin's cult are said to find both wisdom in the darkness his long shadow casts over Nirn, and a certain freedom in his hunger.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-WEEyes-65)</sup> They are known to perform customary fire dances, magically producing beautiful draconic images out of fire to accompany their ritual sacrifices.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-WEFD-66)</sup> A dramatic form of fiery [teleportation](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) is sometimes used by the faithful to announce their allegiance to the World-Eater.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-WEFR-67)</sup>

Faithful of Alduin are known to sometimes create [Living Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Living_Armor) in honor of the deity. These smoldering [golems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golem) of Nordic origin<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-DL-68)</sup> are given a razor sharp edge by Alduin's infamous hunger. They are created in dedication ceremonies which involve the faithful praying to Alduin to fuel the construct's power, so it may be filled with fire and rage, and bring its fury to bear against their enemies.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ALAS-2)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-WELA-69)</sup>

## Notes
- Some scholars have speculated that a dragon's soul, once severed from its body, may simply dissolve over time or return to join [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). Since Alduin's soul was not consumed by the Last Dragonborn despite his body being destroyed, this may help explain his ultimate fate.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LADragons-29)</sup>
- A text by a Khajiit Moon-Priest transcribed by a scholar of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) suggests that Dragons, Clan Mothers of the [Pride of Alkosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alkosh), and [Pridehome](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pridehome) exist in some sense outside the tapestry of time and of linear progression, and so have always existed and will always exist, irrespective of what has occurred from the perspective of those bound by linear time. Consequently the "Doom to Come", the time when Dragons return to bring imbalance to the world, will also always exist.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Pridehome-70)</sup>
- Alduin's Time-Eater title is shared by an individual known as Hungunthar Time-Eater who was said to have slayed the Kings of the East and conquered Dunkreath.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-71)</sup>
- Michael Kirkbride suggested that Alduin's downfall was possibly orchestrated by Akatosh, who time-schemed against his mirror brother to protect the current (and perhaps his favorite) Kalpa from being eaten.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-MKpost-72)</sup>
- One of [Ysgramor's](Lore_Ysgramor.md) [Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md) was said to be Karkux the Tower of Meat, who even the [karstaag-men](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frost_Giant) feared Alduin could not eat ever in whole.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-500COTOYTR-1)</sup>
