## Sanguine Rose
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/47/SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg/200px-SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Sanguine_Rose.jpg) Sanguine Rose The [Sanguine Rose](Lore_Sanguine_Rose.md) (sometimes called **Sanguine's Rose**) is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md). It can take on many forms, including that of an actual [rose](Lore_Rose.md), a wooden stave carved like a rose or a staff-sized rose. Although powerful, the Rose is not an artifact most would care to possess. It can be used to summon a lesser [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), who will attack all except the holder. The Daedra is uncontrollable, unlike those summoned by [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md), but as with conjured creatures it is only bound to the mortal plane temporarily. Each time the Rose is used it wilts a little, and when all the petals fall off, it loses its power. When this happens, a new rose blooms somewhere in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and is plucked by Sanguine to be given to another champion.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Sanguine_Rose), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Sanguine_Rose), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Sanguine_Rose.md)

## Savior's Hide
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/SR-item-Savior%27s_Hide.jpg/200px-SR-item-Savior%27s_Hide.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Savior%27s_Hide.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Savior%27s_Hide.jpg) Savior's Hide [Savior's Hide](Lore_Savior%27s_Hide.md), also known as **Scourge of the Oathbreaker**, and **Hircine's Hide**, is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) commonly associated with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). Savior's Hide once referred to a full set of armor, known as the **Armor of the Savior's Hide** (boots, cuirass, gauntlets, greaves, helmet, and pauldrons). Over time, the term Savior's Hide would become predominately used to refer to the **Cuirass of the Savior's Hide**, as the rest of the armor set have not been seen since [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399). The Cuirass makes the wearer resistant to [magic](Lore_Magic.md).

There are three creation stories to the Hide, with two crediting Hircine and the third crediting [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). The more widely known tale involving Hircine holds that the Daedric Prince rewarded the first mortal to escape his [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md) with his peeled Hide. The mortal then had the Hide tailored into the Cuirass for use in their adventures. This version of the story also claims that the Cuirass gave the wearer a resistance to magic. Another version claims that it was Hircine himself who sewed the Savior's hide from the hide of a [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md).

The third tradition which credits Malacath is lesser known and contains an inaccuracy, stating that it made the wearer *vulnerable* to magic. It claims that the Hide is a "captive servant of Malacath". Despite the conflict between the stories, they agree on the points that the Hide would protect from the blows of an oathbreaker, and would protect the wearer from the sting of the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md).

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Enchantments#Savior.27s_Hide), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Cuirass_of_the_Savior%27s_Hide), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Savior%27s_Hide), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Savior%27s_Hide.md), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Savior%27s_Hide)

## Scales of Pitiless Justice
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/OB-item-Scales_of_Pitiless_Justice.jpg/200px-OB-item-Scales_of_Pitiless_Justice.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Scales_of_Pitiless_Justice.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Scales_of_Pitiless_Justice.jpg) Scales of Pitiless Justice The [Scales of Pitiless Justice](Lore_Scales_of_Pitiless_Justice.md) are a legendary set of enchanted scales possessed by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md).

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Brotherhood seemingly used the scales to bless high ranking members. For those affected, bounties placed on their heads for murder and assault would fade far more rapidly.

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the scales were possessed by Vicente Valtieri, a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) member of the Brotherhood's [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) sanctuary. They were offered as a bonus reward for a contract within the [Imperial Prison](Lore_Imperial_Prison.md), in return for sparing the lives of any guards encountered. At the time, the scales were used to magically enhance the bearer's strength, intelligence and agility, but at the cost of their personality. It is unknown if the bonus was actually given.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Scales_of_Pitiless_Justice), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Scales_of_Pitiless_Justice)

## Scarf of the Obscene
The [Scarf of the Obscene](Lore_Scarf_of_the_Obscene.md) was a minor relic thought to be connected to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), existing in an [alternate timeline](Lore_Many_Paths.md).

It was described as a deceptively beautiful garment made of silks in shades of green and golden thread. However, beneath its initial allure lay a disturbing truth. [Daedric runes](Lore_Daedric_Alphabet.md) writhed and flickered across its surface like oil on water, imparting an unsettling and distressing effect on anyone who gazed at the scarf for too long. When worn, the Scarf transformed its wearer into a living vessel for Peryite's most potent contagions. Anyone who came within a few feet of the wearer was cursed to be infected with a variety of these diseases. Each new wearer gave rise to a unique array of afflictions, the method of which remained a mystery. Despite this, it was deemed harmless unless worn.

## Scourge
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/SR-item-Scourge.jpg/200px-SR-item-Scourge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Scourge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Scourge.jpg) Scourge [Scourge](Lore_Scourge.md) (also known as **Mackkan's Hammer**, **Bane of Daedra**, the **Daedric Scourge**, or **Scourge, Blessed of Malacath**) is a legendary [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). It was forged from sacred [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) in the Fires (or Fountains) of Fickledire, and is associated with [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). It is a fierce [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md), and takes the form of a steel or ebony mace. Malacath dedicated it to mortals, and any [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who attempts to invoke its power will be sucked into a voidhole and banished to the [Outer Darkness](Lore_Void.md) of [Oblivion's](Lore_Oblivion.md) voidstreams. It also has the ability to banish them to Oblivion with a single blow, and can summon Daedra such as [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) and [Scamps](Lore_Scamp.md) from Oblivion to do the wielder's bidding. The summons are said to be those that it has previously "killed", and its victims are said to be poisoned. It has been described as a "bold defender of the friendless", which could be related to Malacath's role as the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of outcasts.

**Found in:** - [Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Enchantments#SCOURGE.2C_Blessed_of_Malacath), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Scourge), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Scourge.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_The_Cause.md)</sup>

## Seed of the Henge

Cast off from the first sprout, predating the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md)'s ability to control their forms, it existed before the [Green Lady](Lore_Green_Lady.md) and [the Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md). Safeguarded within Treehenge, it was protected by ancient [spriggan](Lore_Spriggan.md) matrons. It served as a vessel for the soul essence of the Green Ladies, resembling a seed but a faceted gem. ## Shadowkeys
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg/200px-SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-misc-Shadowkey_cropped.jpg) A Shadowkey [Shadowkeys](Lore_Shadowkeys.md) are ancient artifacts associated with [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md), which are used "unlock specific patterns in shadow." The Shadowkeys are not unsmall objects. Each one depicts a [golden dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) (its wings extended out and shaped around a purple gem, forming the bow of key; a long serpentine torso mounted at the top, with its head and tongue before the shaft as if the dragon were swallowing it; and a tail that wraps around the shaft, flowing into and forming the key's blade near the bottom).

The Shadowkeys are used for the opening of **Shadow Gates** —magical barriers of Shadow Magic often used to protect treasure. These Shadow Gates (sometimes called transport gates) can have a variety of appearances, from portal-like or door-like to outright invisible. Typically these feature a force of shadows slowly swirling and shifting slightly in shape, waiting to be unlocked by the power of a Shadowkey.

**Found in:** - [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Miscellaneous_Items#Shadowkeys)

## Shadowrend
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a9/SI-item-Shadowrend.png/200px-SI-item-Shadowrend.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-item-Shadowrend.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-item-Shadowrend.png) Shadowrend [Shadowrend](Lore_Shadowrend.md) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) which takes the form of a shadowy two-handed [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md). It is associated with the [Grove of Reflection](Lore_Grove_of_Reflection.md) in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), an underground chamber which can only be accessed by those the grove deems worthy. The grove has the potential to "show men who they truly are"; an ancient runestone in the center of the grove has the power to [summon a shadowy doppelganger](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md) of anyone who approaches. These doppelgangers have been known to wield Shadowrend in battle, and those who manage to defeat their shadow may then claim the weapon for themselves.

Shadowrend can appear as both a claymore and a battleaxe, but only one version of the weapon may be wielded. Both versions are powerful weapons in their own right and are known to bear enchantments that damage health or cause weakness to magic. The claymore version is known to sometimes possess decorative serpentine imagery on the hilt.

**Found in:** - [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shadowrend), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Shadowrend_(item).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Shadowrend.md)</sup>

## Sigil Stone
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/SR-item-Sigil_Stone.jpg/200px-SR-item-Sigil_Stone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Sigil_Stone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Sigil_Stone.jpg) Sigil Stone A [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md) is a pre- [Mythic](Lore_Mythic_Era.md) quasi-crystalline [morpholith](Lore_Morpholith.md) that has been transformed into an extra-dimensional artifact through the arcane inscription of a Daedric [sigil](Lore_Sigil.md), which can be used to create [portals](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) from [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). They are spherical objects, and emit both a humming noise and a dark red glow. The morpholiths used to create the Sigil Stone can only occur in pocket voids of Oblivion, and harvesting them requires [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) assistance. The cleansed morpholith must then be presented to a [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) to perform the inscription of the sigil, which will transform the specimen into a true Sigil Stone. The Daedra Lord and the mechanic of the Sigil Stone must then jointly invoke the [conjurational](Lore_Conjuration.md) charter, which will transport the Stone through the [liminal barrier](Lore_Liminal_Barriers.md) and create a temporary portal. The portal only remains open for a brief period of time, depending on the strength of the liminal barrier at the chosen location; the longest recorded time under these circumstances was only a few minutes, making this type of portal rather useless. The power of a Sigil Stone is similar to that of a filled [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gem.md), but much stronger, to the extent that is has been said that even a thousand soul gems would likely dissolve in the presence of a Stone. Many ready-made sigil stones can be found in Oblivion, and can be obtained by powerful conjurers who force an unbound Daedra into submission. [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) are often used for this purpose, although their Lord is angered by this theft.

Due to [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md)'s sacrifice at the end of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the Liminal Barrier constructed by the event is too strong for even a Sigil Stone to breach for more than a fleeting moment.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Sigil_Stone), Oblivion Mobile, [Skyrim](Skyrim_Sigil_Stone.md), ESO

## Skeleton Key
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bb/SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg/200px-SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Skeleton_Key.jpg) Skeleton Key The [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md) (also called the **Skeleton's Key**) is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).

In appearance, the Skeleton Key doesn't always take the form of a key, and sometimes manifests as a lockpick instead. In its key form, it can be used to unlock any lock. As a lockpick, it is nigh unbreakable and can get past even the toughest locks. The two limitations placed on the Key by wizards who sought to protect their storehouses were that the Key could only be used once a day and it would never be the property of one thief for too long, eventually disappearing.

The artifact functions as a tool for "unlocking" all things, including portals, hidden potential, and other unknown possibilities. Its ultimate function, however, is to unlock and hold open the Ebonmere, a portal to Nocturnal's realm of [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md), located in the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The [Nightingales](Lore_Nightingale_Trinity.md) are tasked with guarding the Sepulcher and retrieving the Key should it be stolen. Unfortunately, the Prince is said to allow the Skeleton Key to be stolen or lost periodically, whether by purpose or apathy.

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Skeleton%27s_Key), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Skeleton%27s_Key), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:The_Skeleton_Key), [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Miscellaneous_Items#Skeleton_Key), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Skeleton_Key), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skeleton_Key.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Artifacts#The_Skeleton_Key)

## Skinmail
[Skinmail](Lore_Skinmail.md) is an armor of myth and legend created via decades' worth of attentive work in both [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and [enchantment](Lore_Enchantment.md) to produce. They are the result of vials of experimental liquids which upon contact with skin, will harden it and produce plate-like coverings to the wearer's liking. The eighth, made in the image of [White-Gold](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), Anumaril himself wore as a femur while walking with other Ayleid refugees to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and the Tower of [Green-Sap](Lore_Green-Sap.md). There, he located the Stone of the Tower, the Perchance Acorn, and built an orrery around it, attempting to convert Green-Sap into another White-Gold to restore the Ayleid empire. This attempt failed, due to a fundamental incompatibility between Ayleid and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) magic and belief.

Centuries later, Arch-Prelate [Fervidius Tharn](Lore_Fervidius_Tharn.md) of the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) dispatched agents to recover all eight pieces and reassemble the Staff. It was then used by the radical [Marukhati Selective](Lore_Marukhati_Selective.md) in an attempt to excise the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) influences from the Dragon God of Time, [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). The wild ritual resulted in a massive [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md), and once again disintegrated the Staff into eight pieces. The pieces slowly fell back to Tamriel as stars, and were the only means for those trapped in the Middle Dawn to chart the progress of time.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Psijic_Order#Story_Quests)

Together with [Majeel's Scroll](Lore_Majeel%27s_Scroll.md) and [Lamp of the Satakalaam](Lore_Lamp_of_the_Satakalaam.md), they represent the aspects of the Redguard in body, mind, and spirit. Since then, they have been the sacred tokens of Satakalaam's High Temple. Although all temples had relics to honor the gods, the Sword of Shabnam was specific to this temple.
