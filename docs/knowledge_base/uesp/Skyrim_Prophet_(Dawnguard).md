## Quick Walkthrough
1. Speak to [Isran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Isran), then to [Serana](Skyrim_Serana.md).
2. Ask around for a [Moth Priest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Moth_Priest).
3. Search along the road south of [Dragon Bridge](Skyrim_Dragon_Bridge.md).
4. Investigate the scene of the attack.
5. Rescue the Moth Priest from [Forebears' Holdout](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Forebears%27_Holdout).
6. Report back to Isran.

## Detailed Walkthrough

### The Interrogation
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/SR-npc-Isran_02.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Isran_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Isran_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Isran_02.jpg) *"You're a resource. You're an asset."* Once you have recruited [Sorine Jurard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sorine_Jurard) and [Gunmar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Gunmar) into the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard_(faction).md), and [Isran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Isran) has welcomed them to [Fort Dawnguard](Skyrim_Fort_Dawnguard.md), you are told to follow Isran for a little interrogation session with the newly arrived vampire, named [Serana](Skyrim_Serana.md). When asked what she is doing in the enemy fort, she will explain: *"I'd rather not be here either, but I needed to talk to you. It's important, so please just listen before your friend, here, loses his patience. It's... well, it's about me. And the Elder Scroll that was buried with me. I'm guessing you figured this part out already, but my father's not exactly a good person. Even by vampire standards."* She then proceeds to explain that her father once was a better person but stumbled upon a prophecy that foretold vampires could control the sun, thus not be afraid of daylight. She finishes with: *"That's what he's after. He wants to control the sun, have vampires control the world."* Serana and her mother tried to stop him from taking over the world, so Serana was sealed away with the scroll, to keep it from her father. She realizes she is in a fort filled with vampire hunters, but agrees to try and persuade Isran to let her help.

Once again, you will have to do the talking, although Isran will be his usual paranoid self: *"All right, you've heard what it has to say. Now tell me, is there any reason I shouldn't kill this bloodsucking fiend right now?"* Regardless of your choice, Isran will agree to let Serana help out, but will turn to Serana and let her know: *"Don't feel like a guest, because you're not. You're a resource. You're an asset. In the meantime, don't make me regret my sudden outburst of tolerance and generosity, because if you do, your friend here is going to pay for it."* While Serana can't help but make fun of Isran's anger, she will turn to you and remind you that she carries the Elder Scroll on her back and that it is necessary to read it to understand the prophecy. The only people who can read the scrolls are the blind [Moth Priests](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md) who live in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), a long way from Skyrim. Luckily, Isran noticed an Imperial scholar passing by on the road just a few days ago. He is unwilling to waste men looking for him, but suggests that you ask innkeepers or carriage drivers all over Skyrim. Serana, however, suggests the [College of Winterhold](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(place).md) as a place where a scholar would go. Either way, Serana feels adventurous and will be your follower on the search for the scholar.

### Searching for the Priest

It is also possible to just wander around Skyrim; if you come to the area south of Dragon Bridge where the Moth Priest was kidnapped from, then you can continue on with the quest even if you haven't done this part. ### The Ambush at Dragon Bridge
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_02.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_02.jpg)](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_02.jpg.md) [](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_02.jpg.md) The ambush With all clues pointing to the small settlement of [Dragon Bridge](Skyrim_Dragon_Bridge.md), travel there and ask the locals if they have seen a Moth Priest. Some will not even have the topic, while others will claim they know nothing about a traveling priest. [Azzada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Azzada_Lylvieve) and [Michel Lylvieve](Skyrim_Michel_Lylvieve.md) will both suggest talking to a guard: *"A Moth Priest? I'm sorry, but I don't know anything about that. Try asking one of the guards."* Following their advice and asking a local guard will have him say: *"Ah, so that was a Moth Priest, then? He rode through town not long ago with an escort of soldiers. They didn't stop, just headed south across the Dragon Bridge."* The only other person people who ever noticed a caravan traveling through town is young [Clinton Lylvieve](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Clinton_Lylvieve), who will tell you: *"I don't know what a Moth Priest is, but I did see an old man in a robe not long ago. He was riding in a wagon with some Imperial guards. They didn't stop to visit, though. They rode through town heading south, and went across the big bridge. It was only just a little bit ago. I bet you can catch them if you hurry up."*

Head southeast out of town and cross the old bridge. Stay on the path and before long you will see signs of a roadside attack, an overturned cart, a dead [horse](Skyrim_Horse.md), a bloodstained [corpse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corpse), a copy of the book *[Effects of the Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Effects_of_the_Elder_Scrolls)*, and a dead [vampire](Skyrim_Vampire.md). Examine the vampire and read the [vampire's note](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vampire%27s_Note). The note points you towards a place named [Forebears' Holdout](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Forebears%27_Holdout), apparently where the attackers dragged the Moth Priest. Follow the bloodstains over the bridge to the east until you reach yet another attacked cart, with two corpses: a [Redguard](Skyrim_Redguard_(dead).md) and a [Nord](Skyrim_Nord_(dead).md). Follow the blood spatters to the northeast until you reach five standing stones, and the entrance to Forebears' Holdout.

### Forebears' Holdout
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_03.jpg)](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_03.jpg.md) [](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_03.jpg.md) *"The more you fight me, the more you will suffer, mortal."* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e9/SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_04.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29_04.jpg)](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_04.jpg.md) [](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard)_04.jpg.md) *"I serve my master's will."* Head down the slope and around the corner. Before long you will land on a balcony in a huge chamber with a river, and what resembles an old interior fort with a patrolling vampire, and a bluish orb glowing on the other side. Sneak down the stairs to the south and watch out for two patrolling [death hounds](Skyrim_Death_Hound.md) on the ground level. Cross the narrow bridge leading west and follow the wall until an opening reveals a huge bonfire. You will likely be detected by two vampires and two of their thralls, so dispose of them and make use of Serana as she is quite strong with her ranged attacks. Head up the stairs where [Malkus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Malkus), the author of the note, will be busy trying to enthrall the imprisoned priest.

**Malkus**: *"The more you fight me, the more you will suffer, mortal."*
**Priest**: *"I will resist you, monster. I must!"*
**Malkus**: *"How much longer can you keep this up, Moth Priest? Your mind was strong, but you're exhausted from the struggle."*
**Priest**: *"Must... resist..."*
**Malkus**: *"Yes, I can feel your defenses crumbling. You want it to end. You want to give in to me. Now, acknowledge me as your master!"*
**Priest**: *"Yes. master * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] *."*

Dispose of Malkus and claim the [Weystone Focus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Weystone_Focus) from his corpse. Follow the marker up the stairs and activate the Weystone Source with the Focus you took from Malkus. The magical barrier will disappear and reveal the Moth Priest, although not in the state you expected. Instead, he will chant: *"I serve my master's will. But my master is dead, and his enemies will pay!"* The enthralled priest will then attack with everything he has got, so fight him until he yields. He will then stutter: *"That... that wasn't me you were fighting. I could see through my eyes, but I could not control my actions. Thank you for breaking that foul vampire's hold over me."* You can now ask him several questions, but first he will introduce himself: *"I'm quite alright, thanks to you. [Dexion Evicus](Skyrim_Dexion_Evicus.md) is my name. I'm a Moth Priest of the White Gold Tower. These vampires claimed they had some purpose in store for me, but they wouldn't say what. Probably hoping to ransom me, the fools. Now tell me, whom do you represent, and what do you want with me?"* When you introduce yourself as a member of the Dawnguard, and that you need him to read an Elder Scroll, he will prove quite knowledgeable: *"You have an Elder Scroll? Remarkable! If my knowledge of history serves me, I recall that the Dawnguard was an ancient order of vampire hunters. I will be happy to assist you with your Elder Scroll. Just tell me where I need to go."* When you tell him to go to Fort Dawnguard, he will immediately be on his way and you are to report back to Isran. Before leaving, loot the chest nearby then exit the same way you came.

### The Elder Scroll
When you return to [Fort Dawnguard](Skyrim_Fort_Dawnguard.md), you will find Dexion Evicus in the main chamber, facing Isran. Isran will confirm that Dexion has the Elder Scroll and is ready to start reading it. When you speak to the priest he will prove to be overwhelmed with joy: *"Ah, my rescuer! It's good to see you again. It's not exactly the hospitality I'm used to, but your man Isran has seen to my needs well enough. And might I add, this is a remarkable fortress. I have colleagues back home that would love to study this place in detail."* When you tell him to read the Elder Scroll he will begin:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Prophet_%28Dawnguard%29.jpg)](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard).jpg.md) [](File_SR-quest-Prophet_(Dawnguard).jpg.md) *"Among the night's children, a dread lord will rise."* <center>***Now, if everyone will please be quiet, I must concentrate.***</center> <center>***I see a vision before me, an image of a great bow. I know this weapon! It is Auriel's Bow!***</center> <center>***Now a voice whispers, saying "Among the night's children, a dread lord will rise."***</center> <center>***In an age of strife, when dragons return to the realm of men, darkness will mingle with light and the night and day will be as one.***</center> <center>***The voice fades and the words begin to shimmer and distort. But wait, there is more here.***</center> <center>***The secret of the bow's power is written elsewhere. I think there is more to the prophecy, recorded in other scrolls.***</center> <center>***Yes, I see them now... One contains the ancient secrets of the dragons, and the other speaks of the potency of ancient blood.***</center> <center>***My vision darkens, and I see no more.***</center> <center>***To know the complete prophecy, we must have the other two scrolls.***</center> This ends the quest and starts the next three quests, [Chasing Echoes](Skyrim_Chasing_Echoes.md), [Scroll Scouting](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_Scouting) and [Seeking Disclosure](Skyrim_Seeking_Disclosure.md).

## Notes
- After rescuing the Moth Priest and returning to Fort Dawnguard all the ordinary Dawnguard members will start greeting you as a friend, and become available as followers. Also, at the same time, if you are not already [Blood-Kin](Skyrim_Blood-Kin.md) to the Orcs, [Durak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Durak) will grant you that status, which will immediately grant you access to all [Orc Strongholds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orc_Strongholds). - The Dawnguard members who patrol [Dayspring Canyon](Skyrim_Dayspring_Canyon.md) ([Mogrul](Skyrim_Mogrul_(Dawnguard).md), [Ollrod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ollrod), [Tilde](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Tilde), and [Vori](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Vori)) won't be affected.
- The Blood-Kin reward won't apply if you're an Orc, as they always have access to Orc Strongholds by default. The ability to recruit Durak as a follower, however, will.
- If you pickpocket the Weystone Focus and activate the Weystone Source without being detected, the Moth Priest will be freed and immediately attack the surrounding vampires. After defeating them, he will still be aggressive towards you, but he should then have significantly less health and be an easier fight.
- If Serana raises a Thrall that survives the battle, it may begin to sleep in one of the coffins after your conversation with Dexion is finished.
- Once Dexion is set free, the [elk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elk) outside the cave entrance will immediately respawn once you and Serana exit.
- When Dexion reads Serana's [Scroll](Skyrim_Elder_Scroll_(Sun).md), it can also still be seen on her back.
- After Serana has told you to locate a Moth Priest, you can speak with Gunmar and he'll give you some [Dawnguard Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnguard_Armor) pieces.
- Serana will not automatically become your follower if you don't speak to her about where to find a Moth Priest, or if you have another follower with you.
- Once your initial coversations with Isran and Serana are finished, it is possible to talk to [Sorine Jurard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sorine_Jurard) and start the [Bolstering the Ranks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bolstering_the_Ranks) quest to rescue [Florentius Baenius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Florentius_Baenius) and return him to [Fort Dawnguard](Skyrim_Fort_Dawnguard.md) before finishing this one, so he can also be present when Dexion reads the Scroll.

The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch), version 2.0.1, fixes this bug.

- If reloading does not get Dexion to spawn inside the magic barrier, it is also possible to complete the quest by simply *not* interacting with the magic barrier. You can defeat the vampires and send Dexion to Fort Dawnguard without him ever turning hostile. Deactivating the magic barrier is not mandatory for completing the quest and if you looted the Weystone Focus from Malkus' body, it will cease to be a quest item (and thus can be dropped) after reporting back to Isran.
- Doing this may turn the Dawnguard hostile towards you.
- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) Before quest stage 41, use the [console](Skyrim_Console.md) command `prid xx007B88`, followed by `moveto xx00313A`.

- [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) If the previous solution doesn't work for you, open the console and select Dexion, either by clicking on him or entering `prid xx007b88` (the first two digits vary depending on loaded mods), then use the command `setav aggression 0`. This makes Dexion passive again and the quest can proceed normally.
- Malkus may spawn inside the magic barrier, not allowing you to loot the [Weystone Focus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Weystone_Focus) key needed to deactivate the barrier.
- After the cutscene between Isran, Gunmar, and Sorine when you first enter the fort, the three NPCs can become stuck as "busy" when given the quest update to follow Isran. - Walking through the main gate to outside then waiting 24 hours, or curing yourself from vampirism if you became a normal vampire when you have chosen the Dawnguard side, can possibly fix this. This is caused by two divergent scripts running at the same time.
- After Dexion finishes reading the Elder Scroll, the quest dialogue might not progress. - [![On PC](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/PC_game) Use the console command `setstage DLC1VQ03Hunter 200` to complete the quest.
- The quest may fail to start after completing the previous quest. If this happens, enter the following command into the console: `setstage dlc1vq03hunter 5` then wait a few in-game hours for Serana to appear in the fort, then follow Isran. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Serana normally leaves your service as a follower for a while upon completion of [Bloodline](Skyrim_Bloodline.md), throughout the following quest [A New Order](Skyrim_A_New_Order.md), until after the start of Prophet, but sometimes can remain a follower during all that time. When this happens, it causes an oddity in Prophet's starting scene: Serana is supposed to have arrived at Dawnguard on her own and to have been interrogated and possibly tortured. Despite having been with you all along, she will still give dialogue indicating she got here alone and is a prisoner. The glitch is otherwise harmless, as Serana is to be released to rejoin you as a follower at the end of that scene anyway. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Dexion can sometimes execute the animation of reading the Elder Scroll without actually holding the scroll up in his hands. The scene still progresses normally; it is only a visual glitch. <sup>**?**</sup>
- When Dexion reads the Elder Scroll, Serana still carries it on her back, so, for a brief moment the same Elder Scroll exists twice (from the Player's perspective, but internally, most likely, Dexion just plays an animation, without actually having an instance of Elder Scroll "item".) <sup>**?**</sup>
- The entire room of Fort Dawnguard will be black and will not light up even using [Candlelight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Candlelight) or [Magelight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Magelight). The next three quests will also not start even after the player has initiated dialogue with the respective NPCs. <sup>**?**</sup> - Wait 30 days and try again.
- If the Stormcloaks win the war, female Stormcloak soldiers in Dragon Bridge won't say anything about the Moth Priest if you ask them about it. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
