[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/MW-place-Ghostgate.jpg/200px-MW-place-Ghostgate.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ghostgate.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ghostgate.jpg) Ghostgate Ghostgate was the main gateway through the ancient [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md) artifact to the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) region, which prevented monsters affected by [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) from leaving. Ghostgate housed a large [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and contingents of both Buoyant Armigers and Ordinators. The two towers of Ghostgate, called Dusk (west) and Dawn (east), were constructed alongside the Ghostfence after many failed campaigns to defeat [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur_(god).md) following his [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882) reawakening.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vivec-1)</sup>

There was a route off the [foyada](Lore_Foyada_Mamaea.md) leading up to the Ghostfence that would have taken one to [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md), which rose up to the Ghostfence briefly, so you could have peered through at the quaint, curious and homicidal inhabitants of the Red Mountain region, before dropping south and skirting the [Daedric shrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Shrines) of Ramimilk as it turned west again. Alternatively, one could have simply backtracked down the Foyada Mamaea until they reached the turnoff to [Buckmoth Legion Fort](Lore_Buckmoth_Legion_Fort.md) and [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md).

According to an annal of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) led a siege on Ghostgate after the destruction of Ald'ruhn.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-2)</sup> The Dunmer's prayers to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) went unanswered, but the outcome of the siege was never revealed.
