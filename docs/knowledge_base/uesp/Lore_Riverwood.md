[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5f/SR-place-Riverwood.jpg/200px-SR-place-Riverwood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Riverwood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Riverwood.jpg) Riverwood circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Riverwood is a logging town on the banks of the [White River](Lore_White_River.md) in [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md), at the western base of the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md). It is located northeast of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) and [Lake Ilinalta](Lore_Lake_Ilinalta.md), north of [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md), and almost directly south of [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md). Despite its seemingly prime location, the settlement doesn't receive many visitors. The famous [Guardian Stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guardian_Stones) lie to the southwest of town.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup>

## History
The area of Riverwood has been inhabited since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), during the time of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md). The ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) built [Bleak Falls Barrow](Lore_Bleak_Falls_Barrow.md) high up in the [Brittleshin Hills](Lore_Brittleshin_Hills.md) to the west, overlooking the settlement. The locals were entombed in the barrow for some time before it was abandoned, and many continue to roam the ruins as mindless undead [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KC-2)</sup> The settlement was not listed as one of the holdings of Jarl Gjalund of Whiterun, dated sometime after the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Some time before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the inhabitants of Riverwood feuded with another Nordic clan.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-KC-2)</sup> Circa 2E 582, Riverwood was known for being an old town, and was said to be where the [Riverwood White Hen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chicken#Riverwood_White_Hen) was originally bred. This breed of hardy domesticated [chicken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chicken) could be found all along the White River at the time.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

A large settlement was still in existence at Riverwood during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) and was ruled by Lord Asgens. At this time, it had a rivalry with [Nimalten City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nimalten_City).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Black Moor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Moor) to the northwest, [Oakwood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Oakwood) to the west, [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) to the north, and the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) to the southeast.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Map Arena-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> But the area would later become abandoned. The town was refounded some time in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) when a lumbermill was built on the banks of the river. After fleeing [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), a surviving member of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) named [Delphine](Lore_Delphine.md) settled down in Riverwood to escape [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) assassins.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Riverwood was led by a local woman named Gerdur, whose family had built the mill. The town was the first to learn of the return of the [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) after Helgen was destroyed by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) the World-Eater. After appealing to Whiterun for aid, Jarl [Balgruuf the Greater](Lore_Balgruuf_the_Greater.md) sent several soldiers to guard the town, a move which was likely seen as aggressive by nearby [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md). Despite Whiterun's efforts to remain neutral in the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), loyalties in Riverwood were divided. Delphine departed Riverwood that year to re-establish the Blades.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Skyrim-1)</sup>
