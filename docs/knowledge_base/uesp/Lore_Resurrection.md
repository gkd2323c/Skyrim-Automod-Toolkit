# Resurrection

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg/200px-BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Scroll_of_Revival.jpg) A Scroll of Revival Resurrection refers to the [magical](Lore_Magic.md) act of returning a dead or [undead](Lore_Undead.md) individual back to life.

Priests of Tamriel are described as being able to perform resurrections. On the holiday [Old Life](Lore_Old_Life_Festival.md), it was rumored that priests will—as the last act of the year—perform resurrections on beloved friends and family members free of the usual charge; worshippers know better than to expect this philanthropy, but they arrive in a macabre procession with the recently deceased nevertheless.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Old Life Arena-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CS-2)</sup><sup>:152</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Old Life DF-3)</sup> However it takes a mage's interference to properly return one's soul to their body, as that specifically is a magic unalike the powers of priests.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Nidal-4)</sup>

Enchanted scrolls of revival can be used to avoid death if used at the moment of holder's death.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

#### The Soul Sword
After being shot with a poisoned arrow, [Prince A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md) of Hammerfell lost his life and his life force was sucked into a soul gem. A'tor's personal [archmage](Lore_Voa.md) however, cast a spell of statis upon the prince's body using a spell from her enchanted ring, thereby preserving the prince's body and staving off its decay.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Nidal-4)</sup> Through a series of events, a hero named [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) recovered the soul gem containing A'tor's soul and the archmage's ring of stasis, and provided these things to a mage so she could disable the stasis spell and return A'tor's soul to his body. However, instead of the soul returning to the body, the soul instead entered the sword being held in the body's arms. Cyrus believed this was intentional on the prince's part, and that A'tor took the form of a sword on purpose as a symbol of their battle against their oppressors. The sword, now known as the [Soul Sword](Lore_Soul_Sword.md), could swing itself in the air of its own free will and slew A'tor's archenemies Grand Admiral Richton and the assassin Dram when Cyrus confronted them.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Raze-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RG-8)</sup>

### Third Era

#### Resurrection of Volstak Violet
An ancient artifact of [Shadow Magic](Lore_Shadow_Magic.md) called a [Shadowkey](Lore_Shadowkey.md) was used to resurrect a man named [Volstok Violet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Volstok_Violet), restoring him to life from previously a [zombified](Lore_Zombie.md) state.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>

#### Return of the Dragons
The [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) god [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) used his powers to resurrect dragons to life across [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), who had previously been slain by the races of Tamriel throughout the ages.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ABit D-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Delphine-11)</sup>
