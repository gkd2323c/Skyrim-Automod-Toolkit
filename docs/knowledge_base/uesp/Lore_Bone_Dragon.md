# Bone Dragon

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c2/LG-cardart-Skeletal_Dragon.png/200px-LG-cardart-Skeletal_Dragon.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Skeletal_Dragon.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Skeletal_Dragon.png) Skeletal Dragon (Legends) **Bone Dragons** (also called **Skeletal Dragons** or **Undead Dragons**) are the [reanimated](Lore_Undead.md) [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) remains of a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) which has been stripped of [its soul](Lore_Dragon_Souls.md). Even without a Dragon soul, the physical remains of a Dragon can prove to be a dangerous opponent when reanimated by [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md). Bone Dragons are therefore highly prized by necromancers for use as undead minions.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOCasting Bones-1)</sup>

As immortal beings,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TBD-2)</sup> the skeletal remains of Dragons are generally not truly dead and can rise again.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The main exception to this is when the Dragon's soul has been consumed by a fellow Dragon, destroying it permanently and rendering its remains beyond the ability of any ritual to resurrect.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-5)</sup> This ability also extends to the [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md), mortals born with the soul of a Dragon, who can become the greatest Dragonslayers by absorbing the souls of their quarry and thereby stealing their power.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBOTD-7)</sup> It is also possible to use powerful [soul magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md) to sever the connection between a Dragon's soul and its physical remains, although the effects of this is the subject of fierce scholarly debate, with some speculating that a Dragon soul once severed may simply dissolve over time or return to join Father Akatosh.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LADit SE-8)</sup> Once the soul has been separated, the remains can be reanimated by a sufficiently powerful necromancer.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOCasting Bones-1)</sup>

Dragon bone is considered to be an incredible durable material, and is used by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) to create high-quality Dragonplate armor and Dragonbone weapons.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Even in death, Bone Dragons are known to retain some mastery of the [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), such as frost breath.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the remains of [Thurvokun](Lore_Thurvokun.md) were raised by Orryn the Black, the leader of the [Blackmarrow Cult](Lore_Blackmarrow_Cult.md) of necromancers. Orryn ultimately transferred his own [soul](Lore_Soul.md) into the skeletal remains and possessed Thurvokun briefly before being defeated by [Yisareh](Lore_Yisareh.md) and the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOCasting Bones-1)</sup>

## Notes
- Not all undead Dragons have lost their soul. For example, [Durnehviir](Lore_Durnehviir.md) became undead through his long imprisonment within the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md), but did not become skeletal.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>
- Necromancers have been known to create undead [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) assembled from [bird](Lore_Bird.md) and reptile bones that resemble miniature Bone Dragons.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> These constructs are sometimes imbued with a magical glow for greater effect.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>
