*Many will tell of the loveliness of [Elves](Lore_Mer.md)
And still more will sing of the fine [human](Lore_Man.md) form
but where is song or sign that augurs
all that is beautiful about Ogres?*
—An excerpt from *[On the Beauty of Ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:On_the_Beauty_of_Ogres)* Ogres are a [race](Lore_Race.md) of large humanoid [Goblin-ken](Lore_Goblin-ken.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> that are found throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ogre Book-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> Best known for their great strength and foul smell,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Beauty-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kyne C-5)</sup> they are commonly perceived as primal, dim-witted brutes.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Child-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kyne C-5)</sup> Ogres have steely blue or greenish skin, pronounced teeth, and tapered ears.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup> Their large physique often garners comparisons to [Giants](Lore_Giant.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Slayer-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-9)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Beauty-4)</sup> It has been hypothesized by the scholar [Phrastus](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md) of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) that their grey-blue coloration is a method of camouflage against the sky, although this is considered to have been convincingly debunked by his academic rival, [Lady Cinnabar](Lore_Lady_Cinnabar.md) of [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ogre Book-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

Ogres often have shaggy white or black hair, which can cover the entire body of those found in colder regions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> The Ogres of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) are sometimes called **Snow Ogres**,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SOSFP-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SOS-12)</sup> and are differentiated from common Ogres by their thick white fur. They are believed to be more advanced than typical Ogres, and some of them use powerful totemic frost magic.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-9)</sup>

The [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) takes patronage of Ogres, referring to them as his "little brothers".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBMalacath-13)</sup> For this reason, [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) sometimes refer to Ogres as **Mauloch’s little brothers**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kyne C-5)</sup> or simply the **little brothers**.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/ON-creature-Ogre_Elder.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Ogre_Elder.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ogre_Elder.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Ogre_Elder.jpg) An Ogre Elder of Wrothgar (ESO) The exact origins of Ogres remain uncertain. Goblin-kin like them have been described as "people from a different age",<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAMoth-15)</sup> or as an example of "Old Life".<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The [Winterborn](Lore_Winterborn.md) tribe of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) believe that the Daedric Prince Malacath created Ogres, along with Orcs and [trolls](Lore_Troll.md), as a means to test his chosen followers and impose hardships.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MATR-17)</sup>

One tale about the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) hero [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md) speaks about him outwitting a dozen Ogres who had overtaken an adeptorium, whom Khunzar-ri directed toward a nearby [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) fort for even better spoils, making the Ogres someone else's problem.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOTO-18)</sup>

### First Era
At some point during the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) is said to have had a battle with the Flute-and-Pipe Ogres of the [West Gash](Lore_West_Gash.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-36L:31-19)</sup> Relatedly, an Orcish tale about the origin of [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) alleges Vivec went on to say something about Ogres that angered Malacath, and the Daedric Prince defecated on the [city](Lore_Vivec_City.md) of the Living God for the disrespect to his beloved "little brothers".<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TBD-20)</sup>

During the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) of the First Era, the rulers of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) released hundreds of Ogres, Orcs, [Gremlins](Lore_Gremlin.md), [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), and other [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md), allowing them to settle north of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). These creatures chose an uninhabited mountain region known as the [Wrothgarian Mountains](Lore_Wrothgarian_Mountains.md), described as near [Old Hroldan](Lore_Old_Hroldan.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), as the Orcish tribes were dependent on rare, shaggy, giant [centipede](Lore_Centipede.md) herd-beasts that can only survive at high altitudes. Here, they would found the first city of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> The exact founding date of Orsinium is unclear, but various pieces of evidence place it in the ninth or tenth century of the First Era.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-WJOTB-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BSHK-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3O-23)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/RG-concept-Ogresk.gif/200px-RG-concept-Ogresk.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-concept-Ogresk.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-concept-Ogresk.gif) Ogre concept art (Redguard) At some point in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Ogres were brought to the [Padomaic Crest](Lore_Padomaic_Crest.md), where they were not originally native, as part of an attempt by the [Telvanni](Lore_Telvanni.md) to create a menagerie of beasts to rival the famous [Sil-Var-Woad](Lore_Sil-Var-Woad.md) of Summerset. Said attempt failed, and the small Ogre tribes that escaped would continuously be hunted by mercenaries trying to cover up the failure.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-CDTP-24)</sup> Also during the Second Era, the prophecies of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) made references to the races of Goblin-kin, such as Ogres, although to a lesser extent than in the past. The scrolls were said to not speak kindly about the Goblin-kin, as they were a people of a different age.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LAMoth-15)</sup>

By the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), Orcs and Ogres had little care for each other.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Br C-25)</sup> The Ironhand Ogres of the Wrothgarian Mountains would impede the Orcs' efforts to rebuild Orsinium for several years. Crca [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), these Ogres would become significant military threat to the [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md) region, so they were besieged and slain by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-threat-27)</sup>

Later that year, a powerful and intelligent Ogre shaman known as [Mad Urkazbur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mad_Urkazbur) disrupted a shipment of books written by the [High Elf](Lore_High_Elf.md) scholar [Cirantille](Lore_Cirantille.md) that was being delivered to Orsinium by the commission of [King Kurog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:King_Kurog).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Arz-28)</sup> The shipment contained several dozen copies of the scholar's book "[The Ogres of Wrothgar: A Continuing Treatise](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Ogres_of_Wrothgar__A_Continuing_Treatise)",<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Bigot-29)</sup> a bigoted text on Ogres, dismissing their intelligence and magical capabilities and claiming they deserve to be killed.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Oo W-30)</sup> Urkazbur had his Ogres destroy these books, leading to a contract being put out for him to be killed and the pages recovered.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Arz-28)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Book Quest-31)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Bigot-29)</sup> [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) fulfilled this contract, recovering the book pages and killing Mad Urkazbur.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Book Quest-31)</sup>

Despite past conflicts, as of [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), Ogres were once again known to inhabit Orsinium.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> That same year, the hero [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) fought a particularly fierce Ogre in the Goblin Caverns of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md); said Ogre was guarding the final resting place of [Archmage Voa](Lore_Voa.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup>

### Third Era
In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), a tribe of Ogres from Redguard Valley Cave attacked the [Weatherleah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Weatherleah) homestead, and stole the (already stolen) Honorblade of Chorrol, an ancient heirloom of the ruling Valga family in [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md). Years later, circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) reclaimed the blade from the Ogres' chieftain.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Sins-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Guil-34)</sup> That same year, a tribe of savage Ogres that made their home the ruins of [Sedor](Lore_Sedor.md)<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-OBSedor-35)</sup> attacked a band of thieves who had stolen the [Stone](Lore_Stone_of_St._Alessia.md) of St. [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) from the Great Chapel of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) in [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md). The stone was said to have been blessed by Alessia herself, and protected the chapel from harm. The Ogres, attracted to the pretty gem, took the stone and killed most of the thieves, leaving a single survivor. The [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) were called in to recover the stone from the thieves, and a [guild member](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) successfully tracked the Ogres back to Sedor, wiped them out and retrieved the stone.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-36)</sup>

Again that same year, Malacath commanded the Hero of Kvatch to free a group of Ogres from enslavement in the mines of a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) noble, Lord Drad, on the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Once the Prince's "little brothers" were released from their bonds, the Ogres turned the tables on their former master, taking over the estate and forcing Drad to work.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBMalacath-13)</sup>

### Fourth Era and Other Conflicts
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/CT-scene-Intro_02.png/200px-CT-scene-Intro_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-scene-Intro_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-scene-Intro_02.png) [Odar the Brave](Lore_Odar_the_Brave.md) fighting Ogres (Castles) Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md) defeated a powerful Ogre who snuck into dwellings at night during the time of the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md) with his diminutive Goblin helpers to "reward the faithful and punish the wicked".<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-37)</sup> The Warrior also slew an ogre who was imprisoned within the "Golden Keep", a dungeon in a realm associated with [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), guarded by the Mad God's [Aureal](Lore_Aureal.md) and [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) servants.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-38)</sup>

An army of Ogres, led by an Ogre king, once menaced the land of a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) known as [Odar the Brave](Lore_Odar_the_Brave.md). Odar and his adventuring party would slay the king and defeat his army. For this act, Odar was made the ruler of a small kingdom by the current [Emperor](Lore_Emperor.md) of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Castles Event-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-40)</sup> Though Ogres would continue to be a minor nuisance for his dynasty.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-40)</sup>

## Behavior and Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/ON-quest-Atypical_Artistry.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Atypical_Artistry.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Atypical_Artistry.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Atypical_Artistry.jpg) A piece of ogre artwork (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/ON-item-Mad_Urkazbur%27s_Ice-Effigy.jpg/200px-ON-item-Mad_Urkazbur%27s_Ice-Effigy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Mad_Urkazbur%27s_Ice-Effigy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Mad_Urkazbur%27s_Ice-Effigy.jpg) Mad Urkazbur's Ice-Effigy (ESO) Especially common in Cyrodiil, Ogres are hunter-gatherers who live in small, primitive communities, often inhabiting backwoods and natural caves.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ogre Book-2)</sup> Although possessing great strength, Ogres are generally considered nothing more than a nuisance to the other races,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Beauty-4)</sup> though they often steal objects from, and even attack or raid human settlements.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Guil-34)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-40)</sup> Ogre tribes are often led by a chieftain or caveboss,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Blades-41)</sup> and many tribes have multiple magic-practicing elders or shamans.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> Young Ogres are sometimes known as groundlings.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Blades-41)</sup> Female Ogres, while much less commonly seen than the males,<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-HHH-42)</sup> are known to exist.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Ogre Mob Castles-43)</sup>

Although usually perceived as dim-witted and violent brutes,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Child-6)</sup> with some bigoted individuals believing them to be no more than beasts,<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Oo W-30)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup> Ogres have been known to show considerable intelligence. Ogres helped in the founding of the first Orsinium alongside the Orcs and other goblin-ken after they were freed from slavery during the time of the Camoran Dynasty.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> Some Ogres, such as the Snow Ogres that live in Wrothgar, possess remarkable intelligence and have been known to perform powerful ice magic.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-9)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> There is one account of a group of Snow Ogre elders who healed an injured researcher.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-9)</sup> The Ogre shaman Mad Urkazbur was capable of creating [animated](Lore_Golem.md) ice effigies of himself.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-45)</sup> Ogres are seemingly not capable of speaking [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamrielic.md), but they do grunt and vocalize.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-9)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SHO-46)</sup> Some even seem to be capable of reading Tamrielic, and take offense to works that insult Ogres.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Book Quest-31)</sup>

Ogres occasionally use weaponry such as clubs and axes,<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-RGOgre-47)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Beauty-4)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Family-48)</sup> but they typically only rely on their own brute strength and massive fists.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-49)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Blades-41)</sup> Some Ogres also use magic in combat.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> Ogres are capable of crafting crude, but sturdy armor.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-50)</sup> While not typically known to wear jewelry, some Ogres wear large toe-rings.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-51)</sup> Ogres are also known create primitive art; they construct foul, decorative piles of bone where they lair.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ESOAA-52)</sup> They also use human bones and skulls as primitive [musical](Lore_Music.md) [instruments](Lore_Instruments.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SHO-46)</sup> Ogres are known to domesticate animals such as [cave bears](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cave_Bear); they even form bonds and play with their pets.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-SHO-46)</sup>

### Cohabitation
While they usually form their own tribes,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-CDTP-24)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-9)</sup> Ogres are commonly known to intermingle with Goblin tribes,<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-RGOgre-47)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> often ranking high in them or leading them.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-BG-53)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-54)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-55)</sup> The Snow Ogres of Wrothgar are known to work closely with the [Riekr](Lore_Riekr.md) that also inhabit the region. The Riekr are said to provide their Ogre allies with weapons and armor they wouldn't normally have access to.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Live-56)</sup>

Despite their monstrous reputation there are are various accounts of Ogres showing empathy for others not of their kind. There are times where Ogres haved unblocked streams clogged by storm-felled trees,<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Love Archive-57)</sup> as well as the recorded instance of Snow Ogres healing a human researcher.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ice Elder-9)</sup>

Ogres have been called "giant-kin", but the exact relationship between them and Giants is unknown.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Slayer-8)</sup> It is possible that Ogres may be able to [interbreed](Lore_Sex.md#Racial_Compatibility_and_Reproduction) with humans.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Family-48)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-58)</sup>

### Diet
Ogres are described as cannibals for the fact they sometimes eat members of other races.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Guil-34)</sup> Though the Ogre diet is typically meat-based,<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Guil-34)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-40)</sup> some outcasts have been observed to practice vegetarianism,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Orzorga-59)</sup> to the extent of stealing vegetables.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TPS-60)</sup>

### Religion
Ogres, like their fellow goblin-ken Orcs and Goblins, worship Malacath.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBMalacath-13)</sup> Like Goblins, they know Malacath as the "[Blue God](Lore_Blue_God.md)".<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-BG-53)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Blades BG-61)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-LTC-62)</sup> Ogres being disrespected or enslaved by other races are practices which anger Malacath,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBMalacath-13)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TBD-20)</sup> as he considers Ogres his "little brothers".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBMalacath-13)</sup> Some Ogres serve [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md), serving as disease-carriers, but it is unknown if this is ever by choice.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-63)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Jorvuld-64)</sup>

## Uses
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Ogre%27s_Teeth.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-ingredient-Ogre%27s_Teeth.png) Ogre's teeth (Skyrim) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/OB-item-Stuffed_Ogre.jpg/200px-OB-item-Stuffed_Ogre.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Stuffed_Ogre.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Stuffed_Ogre.jpg) Stuffed Ogre (Oblivion) [Ogre's Teeth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre%27s_Teeth) are considered very distinctive, and they are often used as in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-65)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-66)</sup> The [Coldsnap Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coldsnap_Tribe) of goblin-ken wear Ogre fur for warmth.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-67)</sup> Ogre bones are sometimes used to make windchimes.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-68)</sup> Ogre sinew is used to make a mesh that some [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) swear provides unmatched protection from the [giant wasps](Lore_Giant_Wasp.md) of [Green's Marrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green%27s_Marrow).<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-69)</sup> Snow Ogre hide is used to make pillows,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SOSFP-11)</sup> and their fingernails are used to make spoons.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SOS-12)</sup> Ogres are an occasional subject of taxidermy; a stuffed Ogre was one of the trophies displayed in [Battlehorn Castle](Lore_Battlehorn_Castle.md) after its restoration.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-70)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-71)</sup>

Ogres historically have been kept as slaves in the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> Despite [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md) being outlawed by the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md),<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-72)</sup> using Ogres for slave labor was considered perfectly legal in Third Era Cyrodiil.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-73)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OBMalacath-13)</sup> They are also captured for use in gladiatorial arenas, such as in the famous [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) [Arena](Lore_Imperial_City.md#Arena_District).<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-74)</sup>

While some consider eating Ogres to be cannibalism, priests of Malacath state Ogre innards were commonly found in ancient Orc dishes, and [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) does not accept Ogres as viable cannibalistic offerings.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Orzorga-59)</sup>

Ogres are an occasional subject of tales and poetry.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Beauty-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Child-6)</sup> They were the namesake of various inns and taverns in the late Third Era,<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-75)</sup> as well as regions such as the [Ogres Tooth Mountains](Lore_Ogres_Tooth_Mountains.md) and Ogre's Bluff.
