# Veiled Heritance

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/eb/ON-place-The_Veiled_Keep.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Veiled_Keep.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Veiled_Keep.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Veiled_Keep.jpg) The Veiled Keep on Errinorne Isle‎ The Veiled Heritance was a group of [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) supremacists that formed in response to Queen [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md)'s accession and the subsequent formation of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580). Vehemently opposed to Ayrenn and her [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) allies, they sought to depose her, destroy the Aldmeri Dominion, and expel all non-Altmer from the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Estre-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WDt V-3)</sup> High Kinlady [Estre](Lore_Estre.md), who was Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md)'s wife and Ayrenn's sister-in-law, was the group's first leader as the Veiled Queen.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Estre-1)</sup> It was planned that Estre<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Estre-1)</sup> or Naemon<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LESORPZOSI-4)</sup> would succeed Ayrenn as monarch.

The Veiled Heritance was made up of disgruntled Altmer from throughout the Dominion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LESORPZOSI-4)</sup> The group primarily operated on Estre's home island of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) and later in the region of [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Auridon-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Greenshade-6)</sup> Their primary base of operations was a ruin on [Errinorne Isle](Lore_Errinorne_Isle.md) known as the Veiled Keep, which housed the group's leadership council, the Veiled Council.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Alandare-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WVR-8)</sup>

During Ayrenn's sojourn in Auridon in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Heritance attempted to assassinate her, but they were ultimately defeated, and Estre was killed.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Auridon-5)</sup> After their defeat in Auridon and the death of Naemon, the prince's advisor and attendant, Vicereeve [Pelidil](Lore_Pelidil.md), absconded with his body and assumed control of the remnants of the Heritance in Greenshade, where they resumed their conflict with the Aldmeri Dominion. Pelidil was eventually killed in a battle in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), but before his death, the vicereeve performed a ritual that raised Naemon from the dead as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md), who then assumed control of the final remnants of the Heritance. The Dominion subsequently destroyed Naemon's lich and defeated the Heritance in a battle at [Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Greenshade-6)</sup> After the repeated defeats, the Heritance appears to have largely collapsed.

## History

### Background
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/ON-npc-High_Kinlady_Estre.jpg/200px-ON-npc-High_Kinlady_Estre.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Kinlady_Estre.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Kinlady_Estre.jpg) High Kinlady Estre In 2E 580, [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) unexpectedly returned to the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) after having disappeared seven years before, and she was crowned Queen of [Alinor](Lore_Kingdom_of_Alinor.md). After Ayrenn disappeared, her younger brother, Prince [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md), was named heir to the Throne of Alinor; however, Ayrenn's return displaced him as heir.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ATUQ-9)</sup> Ayrenn becoming queen caused Naemon and many of his supporters to resent her, as they believed that he was the rightful king.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-2)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Naemon-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Pelidil-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tt VM-12)</sup> Naemon's wife, High Kinlady [Estre](Lore_Estre.md) of [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md), was also resentful, as she wanted to be queen.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-2)</sup>

Following her return, in 2E 580, Ayrenn formed the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), an alliance between the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) of the Summerset Isles, the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-GAD-13)</sup> which was opposed by a number of High Elves.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Estre-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WDt V-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Fasaran-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ARo OB-15)</sup> After the formation of the Aldmeri Dominion, Estre formed the Veiled Heritance to oppose Ayrenn and the Dominion.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WDt V-3)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lit ES-16)</sup> As the leader of the Veiled Heritance, Estre was known under the alias of the Veiled Queen, and her true identity remained a closely guarded secret.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Estre-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Alandare-7)</sup>

### Activities in Auridon
In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), when the Veiled Heritance learned of Ayrenn's planned royal progress in [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md), they began preparing to assassinate her.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AI-17)</sup> At the beginning of Ayrenn's royal progress in Auridon, the Veiled Heritance bribed members of Ayrenn's staff to have her speech moved to [Vulkhel Guard](Lore_Vulkhel_Guard.md), where they intended to assassinate her, but the plot was thwarted by the First Auridon Marines and [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AHS-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Razum-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Fasion-20)</sup> Ayrenn then journeyed to [Tanzelwil](Lore_Tanzelwil.md), where Norion, one of her advisors and a Heritance member, had corrupted the spirits,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-If P-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OA-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Ot HS-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-St C-24)</sup> but the corruption was purged from the ruins, and Norion was killed by Ayrenn and an Eye of the Queen.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-It Not Q-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Rot Q-26)</sup> During that time, the Heritance was driven out of Mathiisen and Silsailen.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TE-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TU-28)</sup> While Ayrenn was in Skywatch, an Eye of the Queen infiltrated the Veiled Heritance's base on Errinorne Isle, where they killed the members of the Veiled Council and discovered that Estre was the Veiled Queen.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Wt V-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TVF-30)</sup> The Eyes of the Queen then revealed this to Ayrenn, prompting Estre to escape and begin an open conflict with the Dominion across northern Auridon.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TVF-30)</sup>

Following Estre's flight, the Eyes of the Queen tracked down and killed many of the remaining high-ranking operatives of the Heritance in Auridon.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-FB-31)</sup> After her escape, Estre traveled to [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md), a holy site of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), where she made a bargain with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), offering her soul in return for the backing of his [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) to replace the now mostly subdued Heritance.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Estre-1)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Bo TE-32)</sup> Estre used her Daedra to attack Dawnbreak and then [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), where she opened four Oblivion Gates across the city to attempt to seize control, but Ayrenn's forces ultimately killed her in the Refuge of Dread in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Tt A-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Bt B-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-SAT-35)</sup>

### Activities in Elden Root
Although defeated in Auridon, the Heritance established a cell in Elden Root, and they were based in the cave system under the city known as the Middens.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Supremacists-36)</sup> Tarinwe, the Altmer Ambassador to [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md) and a Heritance member, was put in charge of the Khajiit embassy project in Elden Root, but she instead sabotaged it.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tt VM-12)</sup> The delay in the Khajiit embassy project resulted in unrest among the Khajiit of Elden Root, prompting King [Camoran Aeradan](Lore_Camoran_Aeradan.md) to assign an agent to investigate the situation. The agent discovered during their investigation that Tarinwe had sabotaged the embassy project, leading the ambassador to flee to the Heritance's base in the Middens. The agent and a [Vinedusk Ranger](Lore_Vinedusk_Rangers.md) named Azareth then tracked Tarinwe to the Middens, where they defeated the Heritance agents and arrested the ambassador.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-THot Q-37)</sup>

### Activities in Greenshade
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/ON-npc-Vicereeve_Pelidil.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Vicereeve_Pelidil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vicereeve_Pelidil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vicereeve_Pelidil.jpg) Vicereeve Pelidil Before her death, Estre had tasked [Aranias](Lore_Aranias.md), who possessed a natural talent for nature magic, along with Andur, with finding and assassinating the [Wilderking](Lore_Wilderking.md) in order to destabilize the Dominion.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Awt W-38)</sup> However, once they reached the court of the Wilderking in [Greenheart](Lore_Greenheart.md), Aranias refused to assassinate the Wilderking and instead helped an agent of the Dominion kill Andur.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Tot W-39)</sup>

After Prince Naemon's death in the Orrery of Elden Root, Ayrenn asked the prince's devoted advisor and attendant, Vicereeve Pelidil, to travel with his body to [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md) in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md) and make the funeral arrangements.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-2)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TOo ER-40)</sup> Although Pelidil did travel to Marbruk, he absconded with Naemon's body, stole the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md) from the city's [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), and assumed control of the remnants of the Veiled Heritance with the goal of resurrecting the prince as a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) to lead an undead army and corrupt the Heart of Valenwood.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Razum-19)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Telonde-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Fariel-42)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Indaenir-43)</sup> Pelidil then went to [Woodhearth](Lore_Woodhearth.md), where he had allies from the Heritance.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Razum-19)</sup>

Pelidil signed a treaty with the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), allying the Heritance and the Sea Vipers of the [Pyandonean](Lore_Pyandonea.md) Armada as part of his plans to capture [Seaside Sanctuary](Lore_Seaside_Sanctuary.md) to gain a foothold in Valenwood and then attack Woodhearth.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Laryaril-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Bashshi-ra-45)</sup> The Veiled Heritance's presence in Woodhearth was exposed when an agent named Laryaril attempted to assassinate Ayrenn, who was actually being impersonated by [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md), the leader of the Eyes of the Queen, using an illusion spell.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Lt L-46)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Vo I-47)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-DJ-48)</sup> Following their exposure in Woodhearth, the Heritance assisted the Sea Vipers in capturing Seaside Sanctuary.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Laryaril-44)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ASUt S-49)</sup> After taking Seaside Sanctuary, Pelidil performed a ritual that raised Naemon from the dead as a lich.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Telonde-41)</sup>

The Dominion eventually defeated the Sea Vipers and the Heritance at the Battle of Seaside Sanctuary and recaptured the settlement.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-ASUt S-49)</sup> After recapturing Seaside Sanctuary, the Dominion killed Pelidil in a naval battle in the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-PE-50)</sup> After Pelidil's death, Naemon's lich assumed leadership of the remaining members of the Heritance and raised an undead army to continue fighting the Dominion in [Green's Marrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green%27s_Marrow), the northern region of Greenshade.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Henandan-51)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Rt P-52)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-TBot B-53)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Ro T-54)</sup> The Heritance assisted Naemon in his efforts to corrupt the [Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md) and create the [Shadow Wood](Lore_Shadow_Wood.md) at [Dread Vullain](Lore_Dread_Vullain.md),<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-VHL-55)</sup> Driladan Pass,<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Rt P-52)</sup> and [Hectahame](Lore_Hectahame.md).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Sat H-56)</sup> Their efforts ultimately failed, as the Dominion crushed the Heritance and destroyed Naemon's lich in a final battle at Hectahame.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Sat H-56)</sup>

After the repeated defeats, the Heritance appears to have largely collapsed.

### Coldharbour
Prior to her death, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) coerced Estre into submitting herself to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TBo A-57)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TDo D-58)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-OBA-59)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Lw SE-60)</sup> As a result of submitting herself to Molag Bal, Estre's soul was claimed by the Prince of Domination after her death and came to reside in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). Molag Bal also claimed the souls of many veiled heritants, who were transformed into [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md) in Coldharbour. The Soul Shriven members of the Heritance remained loyal to Estre and fought under her command at the Cliffs of Failure.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TEW-61)</sup>

## Support and Resources
The Veiled Heritance gained a number of prominent adherents, including Norion,<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Norion-62)</sup> a courtier of King [Hidellith](Lore_Hidellith.md) and Ayrenn; Tarinwe,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tt VM-12)</sup> the Altmer Ambassador to [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md); Vicereeve [Pelidil](Lore_Pelidil.md),<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-VPO-63)</sup> Naemon's advisor; and the canonreeves of [Dawnbreak](Lore_Dawnbreak.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ayrenn-2)</sup> [Mathiisen](Lore_Mathiisen.md),<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AI-17)</sup> and [Silsailen](Lore_Silsailen.md).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-GL-64)</sup> The Heritance, besides its members from the elite, also included commoners, many of whom joined due to their dissatisfaction with the influx of Bosmer and Khajiit to the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lit ES-16)</sup> The Veiled Heritance was also rumored to have had supporters in the [Ceythalmor](Lore_Ceythalmor.md).<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Tanlorin-65)</sup>

The Veiled Heritance was said to have been massive. The Heritance was said to possess a significant stockpile of arms and armor, as well as ample food, supplies, and extensive logistical capabilities.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Egranor-66)</sup> They also had a fleet of ships that terrorized the Aldmeri shipping lanes.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Kaleen-67)</sup>

## Recruitment and Training
Someone could be recruited into the Veiled Heritance in two known ways: by being approached by an existing member, like the anonymous author of *[Life in the Eagle's Shadow](Lore_Life_in_the_Eagle%27s_Shadow.md)*, who was recruited by Valano, the Canonreeve of Silsailen,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lit ES-16)</sup> or by directly seeking out a recruiter.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Lt V-68)</sup> To speak with a recruiter, a prospective recruit required a recognition phrase; a known one was "The wine here is made with Alik'r grapes". After saying the phrase, the prospective recruit would be tested by the recruiter, and if they passed, the recruiter would take them to the Heritance's training ground on Errinorne Isle.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Lt V-68)</sup>

On Errinorne Isle, the recruiter would administer an oath, which was the first test of the recruit's initiation. This oath required the recruit to commit their soul to the cause by placing their hand on a black soul gem and repeating the phrase, "I pledge my life and soul to the Heritance and the Veiled Queen".<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Lt V-68)</sup> The recruit would then have to complete three trials: the Trial of Intelligence, the Trial of Endurance, and the Trial of Strength. Once recruits completed the trials, they were made full members of the Heritance.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Wt V-29)</sup>

## Alliances
The Veiled Heritance formed alliances with several enemies of the Dominion.

### Auridon
Estre formed a pact with Sealord [Malleroth](Lore_Malleroth.md) of the Maormer High Command. This agreement stipulated that the Heritance and the Sea Vipers of the Pyandonean Armada would collaborate in the conquest of Auridon, and Estre would become the island's viceroy under Maormer suzerainty.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TTVQ-69)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ODt SE-70)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-MM-71)</sup> However, she had no intention of allowing this arrangement to come to pass, and she intended to use the Sea Vipers simply as a distraction.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-ODt SE-70)</sup>

The Veiled Heritance entered into a contract with the [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) of the [Toothmaul Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Toothmaul_Tribe), committing all the tribe's available resources to the Heritance in return for the goblins receiving a selection of retired arms and armor.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-TTC-72)</sup> The alliance was ended after local authorities investigated the goblins' cave and convinced the Toothmaul chief, Koth, that the contract's terms were unequal.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TTP-73)</sup>

Centurion Earran, a high-ranking member of the Heritance, attempted to establish an alliance with the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) in the ruins of Quendeluun; however, he faced opposition from some veiled heritants.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Eranamo-74)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Lt E-75)</sup> The alliance ultimately failed, as the Dominion killed Earran and Captain Rela, the Ebonheart Pact commander sent to Quendeluun.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TVC-76)</sup>

### Greenshade
Pelidil signed a treaty with the Maormer, allying the Heritance and the Sea Vipers as part of his plans to capture Seaside Sanctuary to gain a foothold in Valenwood and then attack Woodhearth.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Laryaril-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Bashshi-ra-45)</sup> In return for helping him destroy the Dominion, Pelidil agreed to give Valenwood to the Maormer, while he would take control of the Summerset Isles.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Laryaril-44)</sup>

The Heritance formed an alliance with the [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) of the [Keerilth Clan](Lore_Keerilth.md), and they taught the clan a ritual that utilized the Skull of Vargarion, an ancient vampire, to create countless thralls and corrupt the land surrounding [Longhaven](Lore_Longhaven.md).<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Gadinas-77)</sup>
