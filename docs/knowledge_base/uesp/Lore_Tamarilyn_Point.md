[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8b/DF-map-Menevia.png/200px-DF-map-Menevia.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Menevia.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Menevia.png) Map of Menevia Tamarilyn Point (also spelled as **Tamarilyne Point**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCN-1)</sup> is a region found on the southwest tip of the [County of Menevia](Lore_Menevia_(county).md), in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of Tamarilyn had once worshipped the old goddess of flowers, [Druagaa](Lore_Druagaa.md), but while veneration has long since been dead, the festival held on [Gardtide](Lore_Gardtide.md) (on the 1st of [Rain's Hand](Lore_Rain%27s_Hand.md)) continues to honor her and the old cult of flowers. It has always been a great success.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Hot IB-3)</sup> The region is the home of the Tamarilyn Coven of witches, who often congregate west of Whiteflower Garden and Lysandus' Tomb.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DFWitches-4)</sup>

## History
In [3E 403](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_403), the [War of Betony](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) was coming to an end in the [Cryngaine Field](Lore_Cryngaine_Field.md), King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) had arranged a meeting with Lord [Woodborne](Lore_Woodborne.md), to safely escort him to Tamarilyn Point and eventually the [Isle of Balfiera](Lore_Isle_of_Balfiera.md), while his double ventured into combat to fake the King's death. However, after the King departed from the shores, he was stricken down by [Orsimer](Lore_Orsimer.md) of the [Minat](Lore_Minat.md) clan and later buried in a tomb on the coastal edge of Tamarilyn Point.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DCN-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Iliac Anthology Map-5)</sup>

## Notes
- Tamarilyn Point is one of several [regions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Region_Numbers) that do not correspond to a certain province for the [MAPS.BSA](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:MAPS.BSA), leaving to believe that it goes unused in-game. However, based on the contexts of the narrative section in The Daggerfall Chronicles, it is used to retroactively refer to a specific part of Menevia.
