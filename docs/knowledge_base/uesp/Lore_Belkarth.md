[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-place-Belkarth.jpg/200px-ON-place-Belkarth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Belkarth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Belkarth.jpg) Belkarth circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/AR-place-Belkarth_Guard.jpg/200px-AR-place-Belkarth_Guard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Belkarth_Guard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Belkarth_Guard.jpg) Belkarth Guard during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) Belkarth (also known as **Belkarth Guard**), formerly **Reinhold's Retreat**, is a town in eastern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), located in the center of Lower [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOPM-1)</sup> It is a trading hub for the region due to its location on the highway between [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and is notorious for attracting scoundrels and wanderers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Lo C-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MST-3)</sup> Despite its multiculturalism, the town possesses typical [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) architecture.

It is neighbored by several settlements, including [Cliff Keep](Lore_Cliff_Keep.md) to the west, and Dragon Gate to the northwest, Nimbel Moor to the southeast, and Vulkneu Town to the southwest.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Map Arena-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/ON-interior-Reinhold%27s_Retreat.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Reinhold%27s_Retreat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Reinhold%27s_Retreat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Reinhold%27s_Retreat.jpg) Ruins of Reinhold's Retreat The ancient [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) city of [Reinhold's Retreat](Lore_Reinhold%27s_Retreat.md) was once located at the site of modern Belkarth. It was already a nearly-forgotten ruin when the [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) arrived in Craglorn. Emperor [Tarish-Zi](Lore_Tarish-Zi.md) heard rumors of great treasure hidden within the abandoned city, and ordered it to be buried beneath the ground when his armies failed to find anything of value.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTitus Missing Guardian-5)</sup> Reinhold's Retreat was a site of an ancient Nedic temple.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RR-6)</sup>

Belkarth was home to the [Star-Gazers](Lore_Star-Gazers.md), a group of academics who studied the [constellations](Lore_Constellations.md). The organization rose to prominence circa [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Arnure-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SOE-8)</sup> when the constellations fell to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) above the skies of Craglorn and took on mortal forms as [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md). These powerful beings were thought to be the result of the experimentation of ancient Nedes, who used [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) to harness the power of the stars. However, the [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) quickly launched an attack on the three Guardian Constellations, hoping to corrupt them and use their power to remake [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) in his image. The [Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(constellation).md) and the [Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md) both fell when the Serpent reached their Apex Stones, but the [Thief](Lore_The_Thief.md) managed to go into hiding by bringing her Apex Stone to Reinhold's Retreat and posing as a mortal named Hara.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOThief Missing Guardian-9)</sup>

Hara appeared in Belkarth and took effective control of the Star-Gazers with her knowledge of the situation. Hoping to find champions to fight back against the Serpent, she sent heralds all across Tamriel to call for the aid of brave [heroes](Lore_Hero.md) and offer transport to Belkarth. Both the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) set up temporary guildhalls at the observatory during this time. With the help of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), Hara managed to prevent both the Warrior and the Mage from assembling armies, although the Serpent eventually found her Apex Stone, forcing her to reveal her true identity after being rescued from his influence. The news of a Celestial in Belkarth drew the attention of the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md), who helped save the Warrior and the Mage before ultimately defeating the Serpent.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOCraglorn Story-10)</sup>

The settlement was still in existence during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), when it was known as Belkarth Guard. It was ruled by Princess Modka and had a rivalry with [Heldorn Mount](Lore_Heldorn_Mount.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>
