Nix-Oxen (sometimes referred to as simply **nixes**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DEDH-1)</sup> are large [arthropods](Lore_Arthropod.md) from [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) that were once particularly prevalent on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). They are distant relatives of the [Nix-Hound](Lore_Nix-Hound.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-APMNO-2)</sup> and are considered to be in the [nix creature](Lore_Nix_Creature.md) family.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Nix-3)</sup> They resemble giant [fleas](Lore_Flea.md), and are approximately the size of a [horse](Lore_Horse.md), but move on six hairy, stilt-like legs. They are typically gentle giants that feed on fungus,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DEDH-1)</sup> they use their mandibles to break open hardened mushrooms, and lap up the soft matter inside.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DEDH-1)</sup>

[House Dunmer](Lore_Great_Houses.md) culture holds that Nix-oxen did not exist prior to the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md)'s arrival in Resdayn. Myth holds that the nix-oxen were a gift to the Prophet Veloth from Boethiah herself. The remaking of a tiny insect into a creature capable of carrying her favored daughters and sons into the future she saw for them all.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOMT3-4)</sup> Nix-oxen were first domesticated by the [Erabenimsun Tribe](Lore_Erabenimsun_Tribe.md), whose legendary Ashkhan, Ashu-Ammu, rode one into battle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Occasionally, Nix-oxen can grow to be so large and aggressive that local communities may find it to be within their best interest to kill the beast. The title of "Great Zexxin" may be given to one such nix-ox, as it indicates that the creature is the most dangerous nix-ox in the land. <sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONHSN-6)</sup>

By the late Third Era, the Nix-Oxen population on Vvardenfell were wiped out by the [Ash Blight](Lore_Ash_Blight.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VTAN-7)</sup> Though Nix-Oxen and similar species were known to live throughout the mainland.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CDTP-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Oasis-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Origin-10)</sup>

## Variants
Nix-Oxen are known to come in a variety of hues. [Firemoth Island](Lore_Firemoth_Island.md) was home to a unique flame-hued species known as the Firemoth Nix-Ox. These beasts had a fiery temperament and are well suited as combat steeds.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

The [Sheogorad](Lore_Sheogorad.md) region was home to the [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md) Nix-Ox, a species with eerie blue coloring. Some scholars believe this coloration was the result of strange energies that once emanated from the ruins of Mzuleft, but these energies had since dissipated by the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

The Chyle Seamed Nix-Ox is a lighter colored species first tamed for use as steeds by the [Ahemmusa Tribe](Lore_Ahemmusa_Tribe.md). These steeds began to see use outside the Ashlands and are noted for their love of snow.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

The handlers of the [Urshilaku Tribe](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md) in northern Vvardenfell tamed a bright-green, bejeweled variant of nix-ox that were previously hunted for grinding up their shells in the creation of pigments. Due to their potential as particularly fast-moving nix-oxen, they were trained for scouting missions, though their bright colors necessitate that the Urshilaku cover their carapaces with dried mud.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

Primitive Nix-Oxen were known to exist in the distant past. These creatures were darker in coloration than their more modern counterparts.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) of Bal Sunnar once used [time magic](Lore_Time_Magic.md) to summon primitive Nix-Oxen and Nix-Hounds from the past to use as guardians.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

## Uses
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/af/BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nix_Ox_Rider.jpg/200px-BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nix_Ox_Rider.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nix_Ox_Rider.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Survival_Guide_Nix_Ox_Rider.jpg) Depiction of a Dunmer woman riding a Nix-Ox House [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) have particular methods for capturing and taking wild nix-oxen. Nix wranglers work in groups of six or seven, and typically hunt during the early morning while the creatures are asleep. The wranglers survey their haul to ensure that none of the nixes are too frail or small. If a nix didn't pass inspection, the wrangler would cull it from the herd; stabbing it beneath the chin and kicking it out of the pen. Surviving nix-oxen had prices marked on their carapace in black chalk, and would then be separated to two categories, Nix-Tovo and Nix-Rima. Nix-Tovo are older and have thicker chitin, which cracks under pressure. Larger, stronger nix-oxen are cheaper on the market than the younger Nix-Rima. Nix-Rima have thinner, more pliable chitin. By applying a little bit of pressure, their plates can be separated enough to see the organs underneath. For this reason, Rima oxen were more valuable than Tovo oxen on the market.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DEDH-1)</sup> Larval Nix-Oxen are dangerous in their own right, and can be distinguished by their white coloration and comparatively diminutive size.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> Juvenile Nix-Oxen are sometimes called nymphs.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

Wealthier nix farmers can hire tradesmen known as drenlyns to tame their nix-oxen for them. The drenlyn bore into the nix's armored back using an [obsidian](Lore_Obsidian.md) drill and pry open the flesh underneath to expose the "drive chamber", which is a bundle of nerves nestled within a cavity beneath the creature's chitin plates. The drenlyn set a few hook-like prods into the organ before resetting the plates. The process takes about an hour, and renders the nix docile. Such a process is about as normal in Dunmer society as collecting eggs from a chicken coop would be in fowl-keeping societies. However, the practice was viewed as strange and inhumane by people from other provinces.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DEDH-1)</sup>

After the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) outlawed slavery of their [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) and [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) allies, farmers who relied heavily on members of those races for slave labor began to employ nix-oxen in place of slaves. In particular, nix-oxen are used to plow fields. Nix-oxen have a second pair of appendages located on the sides of their head known as pedipalps, which are used by the beasts as a form of protection. Farmers forced their nix-oxen to drag these appendages through the soil to create furrows for crops such as [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) and [marshmerrow](Lore_Marshmerrow.md). As the years went by, a nix's pedipalps would erode from being dragged through the dirt. Not only would a creature with eroded pedipalps be useless to the farmer, but it would lose a valuable defense mechanism. Nix-oxen whose pedipalps are too eroded for the beast to work are either butchered for their carapaces or left to starve in the ashen wastes. The lack of proper pedipalps makes survival for abandoned nix-oxen virtually impossible. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a concerned House Dunmer farmer drafted a petition in an attempt to pass a law requiring all farmers to retire their nix-oxen after a certain amount of years. The farmer believed that the practice of abandoning nix-oxen who could no longer work was inhumane, and saw such treatment as unfair considering how much of a boon nix-oxen were to Dunmeri farming communities which lacked slaves.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-APMNO-2)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/MW-concept-17_%28cropped%29.jpg/200px-MW-concept-17_%28cropped%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-17_(cropped).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-17_(cropped).jpg) Concept art of a bug mount in the Ashlands - [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) was responsible for creating several different varieties of [Nix-Ox Fabricant](Lore_Nix-Ox_Fabricant.md), partly-organic constructs that dwelled within his [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup>
- The overseers of [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) are known to mount six-legged arthropods in slave camps near [Tear](Lore_Tear.md), which are similar in size and shape to a Nix-Ox, though the back ends of the beasts are tilted up and wider than the front end.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Origin-10)</sup> Concept art of this creature can also be seen in *[The Art of Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Art_of_Morrowind)*, where it is drawn as being in the [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md).
