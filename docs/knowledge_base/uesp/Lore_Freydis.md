Freydis was the [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) High Queen of [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md) during the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She was the first High Queen of Eastern Skyrim, the eastern half of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) that split from the [west](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) after the assassination of her father, High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md). Freydis was the progenitor of Eastern Skyrim's dynasty and the namesake of the [Crown of Verity](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown_of_Verity)'s alternate name, the Crown of Freydis.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOF-1)</sup> Like her descendants, Freydis had curly red hair.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONTreasures Q-2)</sup>

Freydis was the daughter of High King Logrolf, who ruled Skyrim during the final years of the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOF-1)</sup> During her childhood, she and the rest of the royal family lived in the [Blue Palace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Palace) in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONTreasures F-3)</sup> After Logrolf's assassination in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md), the Jarl of Solitude, claimed that Freydis was illegitimate, forcing a [moot](Lore_Moot.md) to be convened to determine the next ruler of Skyrim. Although Freydis wore the Crown of Verity and the moot in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) chose her as High Queen, a partial moot in Solitude chose Svartr as High King. These events caused a schism, and thereafter, Skyrim was divided between Eastern Skyrim ruled by Freydis and her heirs and Western Skyrim ruled by Svartr and his successors.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOF-1)</sup>

Following the start of the schism, many of the reminders of Logrolf's reign in Solitude were destroyed,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONTreasures L-4)</sup> including Freydis' possessions, but her baby clothes escaped harm.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONTreasures F-3)</sup> As the High Queen of Eastern Skyrim, Freydis wielded the Crown of Verity as a symbol of her office.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOF-1)</sup> At some point during her reign, Freydis made a bid to conquer [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). A small [topaz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Topaz) bead fell from her battle cloak and became a valuable piece of contraband.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONTreasures T-5)</sup>

Freydis eventually passed away, and her heirs continued to rule Eastern Skyrim. In her honor, they renamed the Crown of Verity to the Crown of Freydis and continued to wear it for years.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TCOF-1)</sup> Freydis' weapon of choice was her legendary [steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Steel) sword. For many years, it remained in its resting place somewhere in Skyrim until an [adventurer](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) recovered it for [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SROengul-6)</sup>
