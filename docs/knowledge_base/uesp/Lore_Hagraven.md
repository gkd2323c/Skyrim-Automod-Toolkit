# Hagraven


Few creatures match the cunning, depravity, and repulsiveness of the hagravens. They will take through savagery what they cannot win through guile.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> They are usually extremely hostile to outsiders, but they retain the ability to communicate. They are equally dangerous up close or when casting spells from afar.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> They are known for their foul stench.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-HBH-6)</sup> They possess an ability to turn into a murder of [crows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow) and attack their foes from a hundred directions at once.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CB-7)</sup>

The transformation to Hagraven is reversible, though it is unknown if it can be undone without divine intervention. On one occasion Kyne intervened and cleansed and forgave Keeper Ormi who turned into a hagraven, after her son Sahun revealed himself to still be alive and prayed for her.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOFKH-8)</sup>

## Culture
Hagravens can often be found in the company of some [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) clans, who occasionally revere them as Matriarchs and leaders, or dwelling in secret groves and lairs in the wilderness with other witches and [hags](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hag).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ALBAB-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Some clans actively revile and fear them, however.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Medone-11)</sup> *[Wilderness Survival Tips](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wilderness_Survival_Tips)*, a collection of advice attributed to the Reachfolk lists hagravens among the dangers of the wild. It warns that if one encountered a hagraven that has truly decided someone should die, there is little hope of resisting her. At the same time, the text notes that hagravens are not especially swift and can sometimes be outrun.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-WST-12)</sup>

Though hagravens are predominantly associated with the Reachfolk, there are recorded instances of [Nord](Lore_Nord.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-KO-13)</sup> and [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) hagravens as well.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-The Trio3-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-The Trio5-15)</sup> At least one Hagraven was a result of the Wild Hunt,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KCHR-16)</sup> and as such she was of [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) origins.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE-17)</sup> The [Glenmoril Witches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenmoril_Wyrd) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) were known to be hagravens.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

The [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) is considered kin to the [hags](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hag) and hagravens.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KCHR-16)</sup> The Crow Mother is a hagraven [Daedra Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Lord) who claims to be the "Nether-Niece" of Nocturnal.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONCrow Mother-19)</sup> This hag spawned the [Crow Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crow_Daedra), who make up [the Blackfeather Court](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackfeather_Court).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TBFC-20)</sup>

Stone carvings with the imagery of hagravens with a distended bellies could be found in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It was unclear whether it represented a fertility idol or a gruesome depiction of a hagraven devouring small children.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Contraband H1-21)</sup>

## Uses
[Hagraven claws](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hagraven_Claw) can be used in alchemical concoctions.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HGTS-2)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SHC-22)</sup> Drinking vessels made of hollowed hagraven claw were believed to increase the effects of whatever alcohol it contains.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Contraband H2-23)</sup> [Hagraven feathers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hagraven_Feathers) are also used in alchemy,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SHF-24)</sup> and quills made from hagraven feathers are typically long and sharp. They are occasionally manicured and inset with silver.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Contraband H3-25)</sup> One of such quills was known as the Black Quill. It was commissioned by [Razotha Tale-Teller](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Razotha_Tale-Teller), a legendary Orc lorekeeper. According to legends her quill was enchanted by an Altmer wizard to ensure that any story written using this quill would never be forgotten.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TBQ-26)</sup> Some Reachfolk chandeliers are adorned with materials sourced from Hagravens.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-RCH-27)</sup>

## History
During the [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md) created by the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) to kill [High King Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md) in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-PGE3V-28)</sup> the Reachfolk captured a hagraven in Skyrim among the monstrosities that were spawned from the event.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-KCHR-16)</sup>

During his exile, [Faolan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Faolan) the Red Eagle came across a mysterious and powerful hagraven, also known as simply the Hag in early works,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TTWOT4-29)</sup> who offered him the power to free his people in exchange for his heart. Red Eagle had to clear his mind before he could embrace his destiny and filled his heart with the thing he loved the most - his people.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESORed Eagle-30)</sup> He accepted the offer and became the first [Briarheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briarheart),<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Antiquity-31)</sup> becoming a spirit of vengeance, pitiless, and beyond remorse and bringing an onslaught of violence and death to the occupying [Alessians](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Empire) led by [Empress Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-TLORE-32)</sup>

The conflict between the Hags of Glenumbra's Hag Fen and the Beldama Wyrd is as ancient as the Hag Fen itself. The arrival of the hagraven Mother Murk from [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) upset the balance within the swamp, twisting the world around her. Her alliance with the Bloodthorn Cult led to an increase of abductions of women around the area to convert into hags or thralls.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONWT-33)</sup> This brought the attention of an adventurer, who with the guidance of the wandering Reach-witch [Sibellec the Old](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sibellec), turned Mother Murk's magic against her, allowing her to be slain, and brought the Hag Fen under the control of the Beldama coven.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONWAC-34)</sup>
