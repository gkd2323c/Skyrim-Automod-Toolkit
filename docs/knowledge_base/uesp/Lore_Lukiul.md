# Argonian

"Saxhleel" redirects here. This article is about the race. For the month, see [Sun's Dusk](Lore_Sun%27s_Dusk.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/c/ce/LO-race-Argonian.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-race-Argonian.png) Illustration of a typical Argonian male Argonians (**Saxhleel**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SKMadesiname-1)</sup> or **People of the Root**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGen-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup> in their native language of [Jel](Lore_Jel.md)) are the reptilian natives of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), a vast swampland province in southeastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The other races often refer to them as **lizards**, **human lizards**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Fo N-4)</sup> or the **Lizard Folk** instead, especially when meaning to be derogatory. They are known as the foremost experts in guerrilla warfare throughout Tamriel, a reputation brought upon them by defending their borders from enemies for countless centuries.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> They have a connection to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), a race of sapient spore trees.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup>

Agile and cunning,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> they possess natural immunity to poison and disease, a trait that serves them well in their treacherous homeland.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWdesc-9)</sup> Argonians are swift swimmers<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> and can breathe underwater.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> They are also naturally adept spellcasters<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGen-2)</sup> and excel in the art of stealth.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

Foreign racial categorization labels them as "[beasts](Lore_Beastfolk.md)", or "Betmeri", as opposed to the humanoid races of [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) and [Men](Lore_Men.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NRP-13)</sup> The Argonians' alien nature is often commented on; they are called strange,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Manual-14)</sup> expressionless, reserved peoples, slow to trust and hard to know.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESCodex-15)</sup> This is a sentiment shared even by lukiul, or "assimilated" Argonians, who were raised far away from the Hist and often have significant trouble fully understanding the emotions, body language, or motivations of their estranged brethren.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-t SCo JR-16)</sup>

While most of Tamriel refers to them as the "Argonians", many Argonians prefer the term "Saxhleel" when referring to their kind;<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lo S-17)</sup><sup>:58</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup> "Argonian" is an Imperial term.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOJela-19)</sup> Some Argonians opt to take on a more Cyrodiilicized name outside of Black Marsh.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SKMadesiname-1)</sup> Taking on Imperial dress, name, and customs makes things easier for Argonians in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Kassandra WITW-20)</sup>

According to the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) Scholar Brendan the Persistent, *"The Argonian people have, throughout Tamrielic history, been perhaps the most misunderstood, vilified, and reviled of all the sentient races. Yet, those who have taken the time to experience Argonian culture have gained a greater appreciation for this noble and beautiful people."*<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup>

## History
*We are all connected at the root. It is a connection that can be severed, but also made whole once more. A root mended by they who are chosen.*
— [Tsixotl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Tsixotl) The Argonians were given life, form, and purpose by the Hist. Their roots run deep beneath the soil and soft white limestone of Black Marsh, connecting them all, and thus connecting the Saxhleel. As the Hist are all connected at the roots, they have a collective consciousness, and can communicate with each other.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:34</sup> Ancient cave paintings depict half-tree, half-Argonian figures—some of which appear to be more tree-like than Argonian.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:54</sup>

### Stone-Nest Period
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/ON-place-Hissmir.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hissmir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hissmir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hissmir.jpg) A Xanmeer Long ago, Argonians lived in giant stone [pyramids](Lore_Xanmeer.md) (called xanmeers)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup> that extended all across Black Marsh. This era is known to scholars as the "stone-nest period".<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> During this time, Argonian society was ruled by influential priest-kings.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Xukas-23)</sup>

- *→ See the main article: **[Nisswo](Lore_Nisswo.md)***

The ancient Argonians (sometimes referred to as "the Old Ones" by the Saxhleel)<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Keshu Beyond3-24)</sup> were capable of great feats, creating magical defenses that remained both functional and effective well into the Second Era.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Dfr H-25)</sup> It is theorized ancient Argonian society was more advanced and unified prior to Duskfall. However, a competing theory has been proposed that the ancient Argonians' trade connections simply reached farther than what was previously believed. The ancient Argonians were adept at metalworking and often used volcanic glass in their weaponry, the latter of which suggests they frequently ventured into what is now Morrowind. Some of their work was possibly even inspired by [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) designs.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CMEA-26)</sup> The ancient Argonians used [vakka stones](Lore_Vakka_Stone.md) to power some of their relics and devices.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Kassandra WITW-20)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/ON-concept-Elder_Argonian_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Elder_Argonian_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Elder_Argonian_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Elder_Argonian_Style.jpg) Pre-Duskfall era Argonian armor and weapons (ESO) The ancient Argonians viewed [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) as a destroyer. They feared change and Sithis' destructive force. As a representation of this ideology, they built vast stone pyramids, known as [xanmeers](Lore_Xanmeer.md), to withstand the test of time. Fearing Sithis to this extent could explain why the old priests of Sithis were able to retain a position of power in Argonian society in the pre-Duskfall period.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-NX-27)</sup> Sithis had a more central role in this old society, and the ceremonies dedicated to Sithis were also different, including blood sacrifices.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CMEA-26)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Unsurprisingly, there was resistance to Duskfall among the priesthood, with some of them taking extreme measures to halt the approaching change. One priestess named [Shuxaltsei](Lore_Shuxaltsei.md) managed to return briefly in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and attempted to reclaim her temple in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-SWEt L-29)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/ON-misc-Xal_Irasotl_Carving.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Xal_Irasotl_Carving.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Xal_Irasotl_Carving.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Xal_Irasotl_Carving.jpg) An ancient carving with arboreal and Saxhleel features The ruins within Tsofeer Cavern, the Tomb of Many Spears, the [Xinchei-Konu Monument](Lore_Xinchei-Konu_Monument.md) and the Teeth of Sithis are examples of buildings from the pre-Duskfall era. The Xinchei-Konu was an ancient calendar that shows how the Argonians viewed time and tried to fight against change by manipulating the weather. The Teeth of Sithis is one of the largest known temples of Sithis.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-JWV-30)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-NX-27)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-NU-31)</sup> Modern Argonians consider their ancestors' construction of the stone xanmeers and fearful devotion to Sithis a result of a flawed belief system, as they only saw Sithis as a destroyer and not a creator.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-NX-27)</sup> Eventually, this society collapsed in a period known as [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md).

### Duskfall
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6c/ON-interior-Xul_Thuxis.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Xul_Thuxis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Xul_Thuxis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Xul_Thuxis.jpg) An Argonian temple to Sithis By the mid-Second Era, the mysterious event known as Duskfall was considered ancient history, seemingly passed on as a morality tale about straying from the Hist. Sources largely describe it poetically, saying *"[t]he Hist led the way"* and *"the Hist revealed the true path"*.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CM-32)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-NX-27)</sup> Details of what exactly transpired are either lost or closely guarded secrets.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-IWTAS-33)</sup> The Hist seemed to have some forewarning of a cataclysm, and not all were in agreement on how to handle it. At least one Hist tree sought to keep their tribe safe by forming a pact with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to wait out Duskfall within [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and preserve its people's traditions.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-HWCTC-34)</sup> The [Hist tree](Lore_Tsono-Xuhil.md) of [Mazzatun](Lore_Mazzatun.md) disagreed with its brethren, and instructed its children to build a sprawling city of stone forever instead of teaching them to adapt to the changes.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Mazz CJ-35)</sup>

The Hist trees that remained ceased all communication with the Argonian people for an indeterminate amount of time following Duskfall. It wasn't until the Argonian Jaraleet rediscovered a method of communication that communion between the Argonians and the Hist finally resumed.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-TLC-36)</sup> Whatever had occurred had resulted in a paradigm shift in Argonian culture. The Saxhleel started to recognize Sithis not only as destruction, but also change. Simultaneously, they adapted a more cyclical view of time. The Argonians believed that the fear of death and impermanence, called "shunatei", is the reason both the ancient Argonians and the other civilizations favored giant stone cities.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup> Modern Argonians believe that by building impermanent structures, they conquer shunatei and become one with Change, or Sithis.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Mazz CJ-35)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-37)</sup>

Exactly when and how this ancient society collapsed is unknown, but they were already living in established preliterate tribal communities around the time the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) first began exploring Tamriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Fo N-4)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-38)</sup> The ancient Argonians were swiftly displaced by the more technologically and magically advanced Elder Folk, and were driven back into the swamplands.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Bt Ao M-38)</sup>

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/LO-map-Black_Marsh_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Black_Marsh_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Black_Marsh_(Anthology).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Black_Marsh_(Anthology).jpg) [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), the land of the Argonians Black Marsh has harbored a great diversity of races, including the [Cantemiric Velothi](Lore_Cantemiric_Velothi.md), [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md), the vulpine [Lilmothiit](Lore_Lilmothiit.md), and tribes of men like the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), but has played home to the Lizard Folk in an almost mythic sense. For some, the Saxhleel were wandering curiosities. For others, they were heroes that nobly saved non-reptilian inhabitants from the horrors of the marsh. For others still, they were terrifying savages. Argonians only occasionally left their homeland during the early years of the First Era. There are accounts of individuals being found in other parts of Tamriel during that time, but they mostly assimilated into the local cultures and offered few insights to others on Argonian society.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/LG-cardart-Red_Bramman.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Red_Bramman.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Red_Bramman.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Red_Bramman.jpg) Red Bramman the Bandit King Black Marsh had troubled relations with its neighbors. The ancient [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), who had longstanding border disputes with the Lizard Folk, took many of them as slaves.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-39)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DUP-40)</sup> Pirates and other criminals often exploited Black Marsh's treacherous landscape to evade [Imperial](Lore_Empire.md) law. Finally, in [1E 1033](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1033), Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md) ordered for the head of a notorious pirate based in Black Marsh.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup> The Imperial Navy entered Argonia through Topal Bay and navigated their way through the swamps until they found and beheaded their quarry near the modern city of [Blackrose](Lore_Blackrose.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup> For the first time, outsiders began to learn a great deal about the Argonian way of life. One of the things they learned is that the Argonians resented their presence, drawing little if any distinction between Hestra's Imperial forces and the cutthroats who had long terrorized Argonian territory. With their goal accomplished, the Navy quickly departed, and exploration decreased as the First Empire's influence waned.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup>

Despite this bitterness, the Argonians still allied with and aided the Empire when the deadly [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) ravaged Tamriel in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200). Argonian freebooters joined alongside [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) corsairs, [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) galleys, [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) warships, and even Aldmeri cutters to form the [All Flags Navy](Lore_All_Flags_Navy.md) —the greatest allied naval force in Tamriel history—to enact revenge against the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md), a species hated by all the races of Tamriel. The Tamrielics succeeded in their mission, sinking [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) to the bottom of the [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md) (although it would later rise again), but a great number of their ships were unfortunately pulled into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) by a magical whirlpool.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Jo TE-41)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-42)</sup>

The Saxhleel were never conquered by the Alessian Empire. The [Reman Dynasty](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md), on the other hand, took great interest in incorporating Black Marsh as a province during their reign. In [1E 2811](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2811) the last army of the Lizard Folk was defeated by the armies of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), but Black Marsh itself continued to lay largely outside of Imperial control.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup> When [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md) ascended to the Ruby Throne, the unconquered territories of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and Black Marsh weighed heavily on his mind. He rashly decided to conquer Argonia in [1E 2830](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2830), and nearly lost as many men from the swamps as he did from the Argonians that resisted him.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-R2Lo A-43)</sup>

In [1E 2828](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2828), the [Blackwater War](Lore_Blackwater_War.md) was in full swing. One of General Sardecus's most trusted legates ordered a troop of sappers to set fire to a peat bog just outside Stormhold, unwary of the fact it was part of a larger underground network. This act was the catalyst for the Great Burn of 1E 2828. Months later, random fires were noticed as far as Soulrest and Gideon. The entire swamp was on fire, and it raged underfoot for over three years. The Legion was forced to stand down, as choking fumes and vents of burning swamp gas began erupting from the earth below. As the gas below the surface burned, it would eventually explode. Black Marsh was nigh-uninhabitable, even for the Argonians. Entire tribes were lost, and countless species of native flora and fauna were driven to extinction.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-44)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-load-Blackrose_Prison.jpg/200px-ON-load-Blackrose_Prison.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Blackrose_Prison.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Blackrose_Prison.jpg) The infamous Blackrose Prison, located in Murkmire The war suddenly and inexplicably seemed to end in [1E 2836](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2836), when the Argonians stopped fighting and went to living life in the swamps.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-BWW7-45)</sup> Reman II eventually gained control of the northern and eastern sections of the territory, and declared those parts the Imperial Province of Black Marsh in [1E 2837](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2837).<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-R2Lo A-43)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1-46)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TBW-47)</sup> Imperial scholars are unsure of why Argonian hostility ceased, but they speculated that it had something to do with their Hist worship.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-BWW7-45)</sup> Unsure of how to make use of the region's resources, the Imperials built several prisons in Black Marsh, thinking it was a good place for prisoners to be sent to and be forgotten.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-WRPls-48)</sup> Criminals not trusted in conventional dungeons and anyone deemed a political enemy by the Imperial government<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-AHo BP-49)</sup> could be relocated to one of several prisons, such as White Rose Prison and [Blackrose Prison](Lore_Blackrose_Prison.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1-46)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Aa U-50)</sup>

### Second Era
The Dunmer continued raiding Black Marsh for thousands of years, not just for slave labor, but for anything valuable, including livestock.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-LMAM-51)</sup> Entire tribes were dragged off in chains to [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md), [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), and [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Aa U-50)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup> At some point in history, [Thalthil Dres](Lore_Thalthil_Dres.md), leader of [House Dres](Lore_Dres.md), conducted a slave-raid on the city of [Thorn](Lore_Thorn.md) which was so successful that it made the Dres the primary providers for the slave trade in their homeland and catapulted them into the ranks of the Dunmeri Great Houses.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-ESOitem-52)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-UHD-53)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE1M-54)</sup> While some Dark Elves saw the enslavement of the Argonians as a great uplifting and betterment for the race in general, the Lizard Folk themselves detested and resisted their so-called masters.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Aa U-50)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup>

The Argonians are rumored to be the creators of the deadly [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) in [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), which originated in the city of [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md) and then spread throughout Tamriel like wildfire.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-SCo S-55)</sup> This claim has never been proven, but since Argonians were immune to it, the other races quickly came to believe that an Argonian shaman manipulated their [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree into creating the plague in retaliation for the constant oppression of their people by the other races (specifically the Dunmer).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-OKF-56)</sup> This soured Tamrielic opinion of Argonians, who were thought to be carriers of the disease.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Aa U-50)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-57)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/ON-npc-Black_Fin_Frost_Mage.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Black_Fin_Frost_Mage.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Black_Fin_Frost_Mage.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Black_Fin_Frost_Mage.jpg) A mage from the Black Fin Legion The Second Era saw the rise of Keshu the Black Fin, an individual from the village of Seekhat-Yol in the greater marsh.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Keshu Beyond1-58)</sup> When she matured, she went on to befriend Prince Jorunn of Skyrim in Stormhold before returning home.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Keshu Beyond3-24)</sup> Witnessing Dark Elven slavery had a profound effect on her, so she gathered many volunteers from the greater marsh to form an army, which came to be known as the Black Fin Legion.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Keshu War1-59)</sup> With them, Keshu waged war against the slavers of House Dres. As her victories mounted, her reputation and militia grew.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Keshu War2-60)</sup>

#### The Akaviri Invasion and the Formation of the Ebonheart Pact
Perhaps the greatest—and most surprising—historical event the Argonians took part in during the Second Era was the [Liberation War](Lore_Liberation_War.md), better remembered as the Second Akaviri Invasion. In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), a strong [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) force led by [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](Lore_Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal.md) attacked [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) in Eastern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), killing Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and Princess [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md). Leaving the ruined city behind, the eastern army continued on their way to [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) but found it heavily fortified by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), who were led by [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) the Skald-Prince and [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) the Ash-King, the latter of whom was called back to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) from [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md) by the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md).<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Jt SK-61)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-62)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-quest-Songs_of_Sovngarde.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Songs_of_Sovngarde.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Songs_of_Sovngarde.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Songs_of_Sovngarde.jpg) The Ebonheart Pact is sealed The invaders decided to bypass the Nordic city and entered Morrowind instead, where the Dunmeri army led by [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) fought hard, but were forced to retreat into Stonefalls (although it is rumored that Mother Morrowind and the Skald-Prince collaborated with one another to set this up despite their races' ancient enmity).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-At S-63)</sup> With their backs towards the Inner Sea, the Akaviri fought viciously against the combined Nord-Dunmer force, but with the rest of the Akaviri fleet on the horizon it seemed that the Tamrielics would be overrun and slaughtered.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-At S-63)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-UA-64)</sup>

Sometime prior to the initial invasion, an Argonian slave named [Heita-Meen](Lore_Heita-Meen.md) escaped from a Dres plantation with several compatriots, but was captured by the Dunmer-aligned [Archein tribe](Lore_Archein.md) and taken back to their village. During her captivity, she received a vision from the Archein Hist tree, which showed the Nords and Dunmer dying at the hands of the Akaviri. In this, she saw an opportunity. When she was taken back to Thorn, she killed her slavemaster and took control of the Archein guards by way of duel. She headed to Stormhold, where she was able to gain the support of a majority of the Shellbacks there, then made haste to aid the Dunmer and Nords in Stonefalls.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-FAt S-65)</sup> Jorunn sent a call for help to Keshu after Windhelm fell, and the Black Fin Legion assisted in driving the Akaviri from Morrowind.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Keshu War2-60)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/ON-quest-The_Dream_of_the_Hist.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Dream_of_the_Hist.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dream_of_the_Hist.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dream_of_the_Hist.jpg) Vicecanon Heita-Meen in Loriasel with the Mnemic Egg At first, the Dark Elves were alarmed to see a force of armed Argonians heading towards them; some even attacked the Argonians out of fear.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-At S-63)</sup> However, the three races were able to get over their mutual animosity, and resoundingly drove the invaders into the Sea to drown. At that moment, the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was formed and would continue to exist all the way through the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) ten years later in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Unlike the other three factions in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), the Ebonheart Pact focused largely on wiping away the rashness of Imperial rule as well as ending mortal scheming with higher powers from beyond Nirn.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-TTo TEP-66)</sup>

As a show of thanks for the Argonians' timely intervention, the Dunmer formally ended their enslavement. Despite this, the practice of slavery did not end.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Gtt EP-67)</sup> Some Dunmer refused to accept these terms and withdrew from the Pact, notably [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md). Likewise, many Argonians felt that the Dunmer deserved retribution for their crimes,<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-AFAM-68)</sup> and only the Black Marsh regions of [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), [Thornmarsh](Lore_Thornmarsh.md), and Murkmire joined the Pact.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-FAt S-65)</sup> Some Argonians left their homeland entirely and settled in other provinces.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-69)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-70)</sup> Eventually, the Great Moot that ruled over Morrowind, Black Marsh, and Skyrim consolidated their provinces into a single nation.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-71)</sup>

During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), an anomaly came to the attention of the Pact's leaders known as the [Mnemic Egg](Lore_Mnemic_Egg.md): a physical manifestation of the Argonian-Hist link which, if severed, can prove fatal to both. The Egg fell into the interests of the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), who attempted to sever the link, but were thwarted by Vicecanon Heita-Meen and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-KUE-72)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-73)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-74)</sup>

It is unknown what became of the Ebonheart Pact, but by the time of the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md) it no longer existed, and the Argonians were once again the sole authority in Black Marsh. Saxhleel slavery was revitalized by the Dunmer at some point, and despite the fear of the Knahaten Flu, House Dres continued sending slavers into the north of Black Marsh.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup> It is said that even [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) thought twice before attacking Black Marsh in his conquests.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-ASHo M-75)</sup> Ultimately, the borders of their province fell quite easily to his forces, but he avoided the almost impenetrable inner swamps.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup> The Argonian homeland was assimilated into Tiber's growing Empire sometime after the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-RGDreekius-76)</sup>

### Third Era

In [3E 396](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_396), during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), a slave revolt escalated into the [Arnesian War](Lore_Arnesian_War.md). ## Culture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ac/BK-misc-Argonian_Warrior.jpeg/200px-BK-misc-Argonian_Warrior.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Argonian_Warrior.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Argonian_Warrior.jpeg) An Argonian warrior Marsh-born Argonians enjoy wallowing in cool mud<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-90)</sup> and water<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-91)</sup><sup>[[92]](#cite_note-92)</sup><sup>[[93]](#cite_note-NN-93)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-94)</sup> and basking in the sun.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-95)</sup><sup>[[96]](#cite_note-96)</sup> Some Argonians appear as though they're sleeping when they bask.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-97)</sup> Contrary to what some foreign scholars believe, Argonians do not worship the sun. Rather, they enjoy its warmth<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-98)</sup> and bask in its light.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup> Wallowing helps to cool their scales and keep them from drying out on hot, sunny days.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-100)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-101)</sup> When they wallow, one might find an Argonian with their nose barely poking out of the mud.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-CMAS-102)</sup> Black Marsh has just the right climate to suit their scales: the dampness of the marsh keeps it from being too dry, there are plenty of trees to provide shade, and it's not too open.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-103)</sup>

Argonians hatched in places outside Black Marsh cannot hear the Hist.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Eoki HOAT-104)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Kassandra WITW-20)</sup> These individuals are referred to as *lukiul*, or *assimilated* people. The term is used to describe Argonians that have assimilated into a non-Argonian culture.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup> Argonians who leave the swamp to live among non-Argonians are also considered lukiul.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ESOCTG-105)</sup> An Argonian born as a slave on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) would be considered lukiul, and would have no connection to the Hist.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-Eoki HOAT-104)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Kassandra WITW-20)</sup> Lukiul have difficulty understanding the body language of marsh-born Argonians,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-t SCo JR-16)</sup> and they are often treated as outsiders by the natives of Black Marsh.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup>

Traditional Black Marsh inhabitants refer to themselves as the Saxhleel, and they are suspicious of visitors.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-106)</sup> Despite the distrust shown by the locals, lukiul are more or less left well alone. Those who've never left the swamp pity the lukiul, as they cannot imagine a life away from the Hist. Those who leave Black Marsh and return may feel greater longing for the Hist than when they left.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-ESOCTG-105)</sup>

### Cultural Mindset
Argonians possess the most alien personalities in all of Tamriel from a human or meric perspective. They tend to speak with a certain detachment that might seem particularly strange to individuals with a formal upbringing centered around manners. Argonians do not facially express their emotions as much as men and mer do, though anger is fairly easy to detect through bared teeth and narrowed eyes.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup> Argonians are fiercely loyal and will fight to the death for those they have named as friends. Their allies have long since learned that there is always a reason for everything they do.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Gtt EP-67)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/ON-render-Murkmire_Q%26A.jpg/200px-ON-render-Murkmire_Q%26A.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Murkmire_Q%26A.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Murkmire_Q%26A.jpg) A jekka-wass changing the weather with the Xinchei-Konu Slights committed between Argonian tribes are forgiven relatively easily. This is due in part to their belief in reincarnation. A [Bright-Throat](Lore_Bright-Throat_Tribe.md) may be reincarnated as a Black-Tongue after death, a Miredancer may return as one of the Root-House People, and so on. For the Argonians, to hate each other is to hate themselves because they are all people of the root: it is better to forget and move on.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-TOMTC-107)</sup> The Saxhleel, like the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), prefer to live in the [Aurbic](Lore_Aurbis.md) Now—not dwelling on the past or the future, but simply the present.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-RM11AE-108)</sup> The concepts of "past" and "future" are foreign to them.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Famia ST-109)</sup> They pay little heed to the passing of days.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Mazz CJ-35)</sup>

Argonians care little about the relics within the ancient xanmeers dotting the marsh. Some xanmeers contain powerful relics or cursed antiquities, and the Argonians would prefer not to have objects such as those floating around, but they have no use for the more benign trinkets found within the ruins, considering them relatively impractical. They would prefer fresh slugs to feed themselves, and sturdy tools to hunt [hackwings](Lore_Hackwing.md) or repair their reed houses.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup> Resurrecting the advanced Saxhleel society of old is an uncommon, but not unheard of, aspiration. Ultimately, to Argonians, if someone succeeds in their goals, then those plans were meant to come to fruition. Those who fail disappear into the marsh, never to be heard from again. That is the way things are in Black Marsh.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Keshu War1-59)</sup> Change, or "vastei", is perhaps the greatest driving force behind the Saxhleel's motivation. To the Saxhleel, defying change is the greatest folly.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup>

As detailed in *[The Seasons of Argonia](Lore_The_Seasons_of_Argonia.md)*, the passage of time is not totally meaningless to the Saxhleel. The Saxhleel recognize that the passage of days and months is an inevitable, perpetual cycle, and marking the progression of that cycle is sacred.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-TSOA-110)</sup> Argonians who pay heed to the passage of time are called *jekka-wats*.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Mazz CJ-35)</sup> A jekka-wass is assigned to be the keeper of an ancient Argonian monument known as the [Xinchei-Konu](Lore_Xinchei-Konu.md), a giant stone calendar located in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md). The Xinchei-Konu is not just a calendar, it is a catalyst of change; it can be used to change the weather.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-JWV-30)</sup>

#### Calendar
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3d/ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Ritual_of_Change.jpg) The Argonian calendar, read clockwise, with Vakka at the top The Argonians have different names for the months of the year than the rest of Tamriel. The following is a synopsis of the months and their meaning, in chronological order:

- **Vakka:** The first month represents origins. Tribes are encouraged to show respect to their elders during this time.

- **Xeech:** A month of planting, hopeful, but also melancholy. The First Mourning (out of three) occurs during Xeech: once a thing it is planted, it is hidden and gone. What emerges will not be something new: the nut is lost forever.

- **Sisei:** Celebrates youth and possibility. Sports, competitions, and hatchling festivals take place during Sisei. Strength, speed, and willpower are also celebrated. This season is a time for romance and renewal. Bright colors are typically worn this time of year.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-111)</sup>

- **Hist-Deek:** A month dedicated to challenging authority. Injustices are brought to light, conflict between tribes occurs, and individuals reflect on their agency as well as their bond with the Hist.

- **Hist-Dooka:** Family, tradition, and obligation are celebrated. Youngsters are given more responsibility, and many adolescents undergo their Chukka-Sei. The month typically ends in celebration.

- **Hist-Tsoko:** The holiest month of the year, dedicated to the ideas of knowledge, wisdom, and fullness of potential. Gatherings are often solemn. Hist-Tsoko represents the Second Mourning: the Hist has stopped growing, and its full potential has been reached.

- **Thtithil-Gah:** Wonder and simple joy are celebrated. Traveling entertainers make most of their money during this month, and festivals and feasts are constant.

- **Thtithil:** Associated with anticipation, mystery, and finality. Thtithil is a time when many tribes begin laying eggs.

- **Nushmeeko:** This month celebrates hard, thankless, everyday work. The entire tribe labors near-constantly during this month, doing whatever needs to be done around the village.

- **Shaja-Nushmeeko:** Another month of mystery and debate. Unifying concepts such as change, becoming, and shifting values are all contemplated. Adolescent gatherings often happen during this time, and many young ones dip their tails in romance.

- **Saxhleel:** Tribe members are free to pursue their creative passions, such as pottery and woodcarving. In many tribes, a large gathering of elders is held to prepare them and the community for the impending deaths to come. Saxhleel encompasses a sense of things coming to a close, as it's the second to last month of the year.

- **Xulomaht:** Xulomaht is associated with the Third Mourning, which is the most significant of the three given the end of the year. Communities reflect on the past year and mourn its passing. They also look forward to the next year. Festivals are held to remember those who have died.

### Death
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d1/ON-place-Grave-Stake_Memorial.jpg/200px-ON-place-Grave-Stake_Memorial.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grave-Stake_Memorial.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grave-Stake_Memorial.jpg) A grave-stake memorial site [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/ON-npc-Jaxsik-Orrn_03.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Jaxsik-Orrn_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jaxsik-Orrn_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Jaxsik-Orrn_03.jpg) A Naga-Kur kaal attempting to read a grave-stake with the aid of a grave-singer When an Argonian [dies](Lore_Death_Argonian.md), their corpse is laid to rest in the marsh to return to the Hist, so their soul may be incarnated once more. Burying the bodies, or at least allowing them to be among nature, allows them to return to the Hist. Argonians that have died away from the Hist, even in stone prisons such as White Rose, can return to the Hist by bringing their bones to the dirt.

Traditionally, the deceased is fitted with a wooden funerary mask and buried in the ground. A xul-vaat, or grave-stake, is used to pin the corpse into the mud (whether it's buried or not) so it doesn't float up to the surface when the marsh inevitably floods. This also prevents corpses from rising as an undead [bog blight](Lore_Bog_Blight.md).

The xul-vaat don't just prevent the corpses from floating to the surface of the bog: the deceased's story is carved upon their stake. Tales of their victories and defeats, tales from childhood, names of friends, dreams, and nightmares—a grave-stake tells those who see it about the corpse it impales. Each stake planted into the mud tells a story, some as old as the marsh itself. Sometimes they are laid at the roots of the Hist. Others are buried in graveyards or a place that held sentimental value for the deceased, and for some the body is staked where it fell. The stake marks the burial site and serves as a remembrance until it is claimed by the swamp.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-render-Jaxsik-Orrn_02.jpg/200px-ON-render-Jaxsik-Orrn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Jaxsik-Orrn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Jaxsik-Orrn_02.jpg) A Naga kaal fighting bog blights When death arrives to a tribe, the tribe's grave-singer is called upon to tend the corpse and stake it. The grave-singer is tasked with reading their tribe's stakes, and when someone dies, they sing the fallen one's final song. Grave-Singers care for the bodies of the fallen and deal with death, however it may appear to the tribe. The grave-singer of the Naga-Kur has an additional task: harvesting scales, skin, bones and other parts from the bodies of the fallen as needed, so the tribe can make weapons and armor. Argonian spirits return to the Hist when they die; the Naga-Kur do not bind their spirits to the weapons they make with the bones of their fallen kin.

Sometimes, grave-stakes come loose, usually by being pulled from the mud by an unsuspecting or malicious individual. When this happens, a formerly staked corpse can rise from the dead as a bog blight. They have been described as "dead-and-not-dead things that eat Nagas whole", and are dangerous in large numbers. Bog blights can be kept at bay with the use of pahnjees (or "fumers"), racks of oily, spoiled meat that mask a living person's scent from bog blights. Life energy clings to grave-stakes. This powerful force can be used to fuel necromantic rituals.

#### [Argonian Souls](Lore_Souls.md#Mortal_Souls)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/64/ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dust-On-Scales_02.jpg) An Argonian's soul being torn from his body as he dies Argonian [souls](Lore_Souls.md) are different from those of other races due to their connection to the Hist.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-112)</sup> Argonian souls return to the Hist after death because of this connection. A Hist tree's sap is its soul. When an Argonian is created, the Hist's sap becomes the Argonian's blood, essence, and soul. Argonian spirits are made from the essence of their Hist tree, which is the sap. If a Hist tree were drained of its sap, the souls of the tree's tribe will have nowhere to go after death. They may linger in the world of the living, trapped in madness. If the sap were to be boiled or distilled by whoever took it, the Hist would be in great anguish, an unbearable sensation that the souls of its tribe would feel. If the tribe's tree-minder possesses a relic gifted to the tribe by the Hist, such as a staff made from one of its branches, the relic can be used to call the tribe's spirits and the tree's sap back to the Hist. In that case, the souls would pass on, and the tree would survive.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ESODeyapa-113)</sup>

Those who commit blasphemies against the Saxhleel are punished by denying them the ability to return to the Hist. This can be done by binding them to an urn. None of the Argonians buried following the rites of the Hist are anointed with Arkay's Blessing, which in the very least prevents the souls of other races from being used without their consent. Even so, it is uncommon for foreign [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) to come to Black Marsh to practice their art, because corpses decay very quickly.

### The Tide
The Izta-Ahuak ([Tide-Born](Lore_Tide-Born.md)), native to [Solstice](Lore_Solstice.md), do not live near the Hist, nor can they hear its voice. Their beliefs are quite different from their brethren on the mainland, most notably with regards to the process of reincarnation. The Izta-Ahuak believe that when an Argonian dies anywhere in the world, the River sorts their memories, determining what returns to the Hist and that which is discarded. The Tide-Born build their nests on the shoals to expose their eggs to the tide because they believe that the water brought in by the tide carries memories discarded into the River: by nurturing their eggs in the sea, these memories can find a new home with the Izta-Ahuak. In their own words, the Tide-Born believe they are "weary scales born anew": some people die young or unfulfilled, and the Izta-Ahuak believes these folk are given another chance at finding their destined scales by being brought to their tribe by the tide. A Tide-Born hatchling perishing in the shell before pipping is considered a great tragedy, as the chance to be reborn into scales that suit them is denied.

Death-dancers are employed to perform an individual's last rites. When a Tide-Born passes peacefully, a tribe member is tasked with collecting their votive for the rite. In the case of those who die suddenly, particularly those who have not disclosed where their votive lies, a death-dancer petitions Sithis to guide the spirit safely into the Void.

Tide-Born are buried much like their counterparts on the mainland, but the manner in which one has left the mortal plane determines how they are consecrated. Those who believe they have fulfilled their destined scales have grave-stakes with their life story carved into them, and are staked in the mud just like mainland Saxhleel at a sacred burial ground near their village. Those who do not feel they've reached their full potential are staked on the shoreline instead, where marine creatures can reconstitute the body, expediting the individual's return to the Void so that they may begin the cycle anew.

It is rare, but some Tide-Born remember their past lives. Triggers are usually mundane, and bring to mind small sensations: sounds, scents, and the like, which seem familiar even though the individual has never experienced these things before. Some Tide-Born witness these memories in the form of dreams. Izta-Ahuak tide-readers have suggested that these experiences should be enjoyed, but not indulged in, encouraging folks who glimpse these fleeting memories to focus on living their current life rather than dwelling on the one they left behind.

### Art and Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f0/ON-place-Ianix%27s_Hut.jpg/200px-ON-place-Ianix%27s_Hut.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ianix%27s_Hut.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Ianix%27s_Hut.jpg) A mud hut, which can be easily rebuilt if destroyed Long ago, the Lizard Folk built and lived in grandiose pyramid structures they referred to as xanmeers and had [wayshrines](Lore_Wayshrines.md) dedicated to the stars.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Ko L-114)</sup><sup>[[115]](#cite_note-SP-115)</sup> However, these were abandoned following Duskfall, and the Saxhleel themselves do not recall much about life in those times.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-SP-115)</sup> During the Second Era, the Argonians of Shadowfen lived in huts made from mud,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup> while those in Murkmire constructed settlements woven from reeds.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-ESOHeem Jas-116)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-117)</sup> Because of this alarming change, some scholars doubt that the xanmeers were built by the Argonians, who in their opinion merely claimed ownership of them. Feathers, bright colors, and lizard hides decorate the majority of their buildings and works of art.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/da/ON-prerelease-Murkmire_04.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Murkmire_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Murkmire_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Murkmire_04.jpg) An Argonian Village comprised of reed houses Reptilian symbolism is prevalent in Argonian art. [Turtles](Lore_Turtle.md), lizards, crocodiles, and other scaly creatures are often the subject of artistic expression.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-118)</sup> Turtle shells are often used to craft dishes, such as platters<sup>[[119]](#cite_note-119)</sup> or mugs. Particularly fancy products might be inlaid with mother-of-pearl.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-120)</sup> Argonians use hide from giant snakes to protect delicate textiles.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-121)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/ON-concept-Murkmire_Architecture.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Murkmire_Architecture.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Murkmire_Architecture.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Murkmire_Architecture.jpg) Some of Murkmire's Architecture Modern Argonian architecture is shaped by the concept of conquering shunatei: "the pain caused by holding on too tightly to that which has come to pass", also described as the "Fear of Death and Forgetting". Argonians feel that building stone structures is foolish, instead building structures that are intended to be temporary.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-ONTalen-122)</sup> Argonians are said to be masters of crafting jewelry.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Madesijewelry-123)</sup> The Argonians also apparently possess sophisticated understanding of alchemy; their alchemists in Black Marsh have long held that the moon phases dictate the precise positioning of calcinators.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-CT-124)</sup>

Ceramic art, usually made from clay, is commonplace in Black Marsh.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-125)</sup> Glass and bone are also used in Argonian crafts,<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-126)</sup> and although wood is destined to rot in the humid swamps, Argonians don't shy away from it.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-127)</sup><sup>[[128]](#cite_note-128)</sup><sup>[[129]](#cite_note-129)</sup> Argonians are known for their sturdy weaving of grass and other fibers, secrets which they guard closely.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-130)</sup> Specialized bronze tools called "bone-workers" are used to carve bone, horn, and chitin for Argonian art, acting like a knife, pick, or chisel.<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-131)</sup> Argonians have also been known to make use of [silver](Lore_Silver.md).<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-132)</sup>

In Murkmire, some of the locals use razor-sharp pieces of volcanic glass to skin their beasts.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-133)</sup> The inhabitants of Xal-Ithix use barbed, four-tined spears with double-hinged folding hafts made of bamboo to hunt frogs.<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-134)</sup>

### Entertainment

There are also several Argonian board games, such as Shells and Stones,<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-TMM-142)</sup> which is played on a small, flat reed mat with strange markings on it.<sup>[[143]](#cite_note-143)</sup>

### Music
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/ON-npc-Tar-Lurash.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Tar-Lurash.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Tar-Lurash.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Tar-Lurash.jpg) A vossa-satl player The Argonians have invented an instrument called the vossa-satl (also called "frog pipes"). The instrument resembles a polished wooden clam shell with a series of valves along the top. Each "segment" of the shell is a small, hollow compartment with a mouth like a bugle; each chamber is a different size and produces a different tone. The musician places a live frog into each compartment. There are many kinds of vossa-satls: some are as small as a jaw harp, and some are as large as a pipe organ.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-OPTF-144)</sup> To tune their instrument, the vossa-satl player twists a tuning key to alter the size of the frog containment chambers.<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-145)</sup> Tuning a vossa-satl requires a good deal of patience, especially when the frogs are in mating season.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-146)</sup> Musicians wear pouches full of wriggling frog treats on a cord around their neck.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-147)</sup> There are nests in each of the frog compartments, and the frogs are kept happy and well-fed so they sing at their best.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-148)</sup> The happier the frogs are, the better the music is.<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-149)</sup>

Before each performance, the musician squirts a few drops of frog musk into each compartment to goad the frogs into singing. By pushing the valves on the instrument's neck, the musician muffles some chambers while leaving others open. The pipes produce a sound of their own, which is supplemented by the frogs' croaking.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-OPTF-144)</sup> The vossa-satl is one of the most difficult instruments for foreign musicians to master.<sup>[[150]](#cite_note-150)</sup> Instrument crafters go out and catch frogs that produce pleasing tones for their vossa-satls.<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-151)</sup><sup>[[152]](#cite_note-152)</sup>

The Fredas Night Stingaree is a tradition in Stormhold. Some participants wear fancy, lacquered wasp thoraxes on their tails,<sup>[[153]](#cite_note-153)</sup> while a musician plays "Dancing on Eggs", a traditional jig, on a uniquely styled slide flute. These flutes are usually made of silver.<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-154)</sup>

### Language

- *"Much of the nuance of Argonian conversation blooms from heavy metaphor and subtle body movements."* – Lights-the-Way, Mystic of the Mages Guild<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup>

[Jel](Lore_Jel.md) is the language of the Argonian people, and is unique among the other Tamrielic races, as it came from the Hist, and is unrelated to ancient [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex.md). It has no past or future tense verbs, only present tense. There is less emphasis on past and future, and more emphasis on the now, the recent past and the close future. Aside from the purely verbal communication, Argonians also utilize body movements to express their attitude to the subject they're speaking about.<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-ESOJO-155)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-prerelease-Murkmire_07.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Murkmire_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Murkmire_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Murkmire_07.jpg) Argonians spear fishing While many Argonians can speak other languages, even the most skilled linguists from other races struggle to gain even a basic grasp of Jel. With its use of hisses and other noises, it is described by some as impossible to be spoken correctly by other races, though it is still advised that visitors learn at least a few key phrases.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup> The throats of non-Argonians can't make all the proper sounds.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:33</sup> Jel can be described as "the closest speech to real thought". Some Argonians feel that languages comprised merely of words only serve to limit the expression of thought<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:33</sup> —true thought is close to the [root](Lore_Hist.md#Ancient_Trees).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:34</sup> Some of the more isolated tribes of Black Marsh will commonly speak in this manner, with phrases such as "I bare my teeth" or "I roll my eyes" being commonplace during discussions.<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-ESOJO-155)</sup>

Jel is occasionally referred to as the swamp-tongue<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup> or the root's tongue by native speakers. People who do not speak Jel are referred to as "ojel", literally meaning "Not of Argonian Tongue" or "Non-Speaker of Jel". It is often used to refer to outsiders, but isn't a derogatory term.<sup>[[156]](#cite_note-Xukas Alten-156)</sup>

"Wamasu scale treatment" is a euphemism used to refer to an impossible task.<sup>[[157]](#cite_note-ESOFirilanya-157)</sup>

### Medicine and Grooming
In Murkmire, pickled frogs eyes are used to settle a sour stomach. Some tribes consider them a delicacy.<sup>[[158]](#cite_note-158)</sup> Dried tree bark may be chewed to relieve headaches or minor aches and pains.<sup>[[159]](#cite_note-159)</sup>

Argonians have many products used for moisturizing scales and frills. Soothing balms derived from palm oil can be used to keep spines healthy and moist.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-ESOPalm Oil-160)</sup> Polish can keep the spines looking suave.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-161)</sup> Clay lotion may be used to soften thick, rough scales,<sup>[[162]](#cite_note-162)</sup> and some lacquer their scales to improve their luster.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-163)</sup> Creams derived from substances like kwama wax can be applied to the scales to keep them shiny and moist.<sup>[[164]](#cite_note-164)</sup> Herbal moisturizers intended for Argonian scales are known to irritate the skin of every other race.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-165)</sup> Some oils intended for use on leather can be used to keep Argonians scales smooth and shiny.<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-166)</sup> Argonian ladies may carry a parasol to keep their scales from drying out in the hot sun.<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-167)</sup>

Argonians use small specialized metal blades to clean their bodies, scraping mites, dirt, and residual buildup off their scales.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-168)</sup> Brushes with rigid bristles serve a similar purpose underneath the scales.<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-169)</sup> Clam shell claw files can help the discerning Argonian dull or sharpen their claws.<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-170)</sup> Thin, elongated whetstones are ideal for sharpening horns.<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-171)</sup> Cylindrical sharpening stones with a hollow center are used to sharpen the teeth for defensive or cosmetic purposes.<sup>[[172]](#cite_note-172)</sup> Argonians of high status keep Corimont Mouth-Plovers (a kind of bird) to clean their fangs after meals.<sup>[[173]](#cite_note-173)</sup> A mixture of ground, ultra-hard bug shells can also be used to clean debris out from between one's fangs.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-174)</sup>

Smooth, waxy fin polish is used to enhance the health and appearance of one's fins.<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-175)</sup> Some Argonians use ridge liner in an attempt to elongate the apparent size or depth of their facial and head ridges. This is comparable to the purpose of contouring or mascara in makeup used by men and mer: the gel is supposed to make their ridges seem more pronounced.<sup>[[176]](#cite_note-176)</sup> Argonians can make use of specialized polish for their horns. Expensive varieties can be made from valuable reagents, such as gold.<sup>[[177]](#cite_note-177)</sup> Cosmetic fragrances developed by the Saxhleel tend to follow trends that may seem odd at first glance, such as the bladder-aged "snake sweat" perfume.<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-178)</sup> Some Argonian colognes smell so strong that only the Saxhleel could find them alluring.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-179)</sup>

Argonians moult, shedding their scales occasionally to make way for new ones.<sup>[[180]](#cite_note-ESOMoult-180)</sup> Sometimes they have difficulty losing their old scales, <sup>[[181]](#cite_note-181)</sup> and must use thick brushes to remove them.<sup>[[182]](#cite_note-182)</sup>

### Food
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/ON-render-Xukas_02.jpg/200px-ON-render-Xukas_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Xukas_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-render-Xukas_02.jpg) A meeting with traditional fare on the table, including bowls of grubs and guts
- *→ See the main article: **[Argonian Cuisine](Lore_Argonian_Cuisine.md)***

[Argonian cuisine](Lore_Argonian_Cuisine.md) is perhaps the most impenetrable for outsiders. Composed largely of raw fish and swamp plants collected throughout the marshes, Argonian dishes are often difficult for outsiders to acclimate to. Argonian chefs make use of ingredients that cooks of other cultures would shy away from, such as maggots and fermented pond scum. Fish and small creatures that are harvested in nets and traps are often seasoned with spices and rich sauces, then eaten raw.

### Drugs
[Daril](Lore_Daril.md), literally meaning "seeing everything in ecstasy" in Jel, is derived from moon adder venom. This venom is instantaneously fatal to others, and is transformed by an agent in Argonian blood. Those who use daril can see sounds, hear tastes, and smell sights. Daril unfolds in stages, each unlike the last, and confuses the senses.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:131</sup> It cannot be safely taken by non-Argonians.<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-ESOLAD-183)</sup><sup>[[184]](#cite_note-184)</sup><sup>[[185]](#cite_note-185)</sup> Daril is very difficult to acquire; the venom is usually fermented in a swamp jelly gas bladder.<sup>[[186]](#cite_note-LAMQA2-186)</sup> Some Argonian users coat tiny quills in daril and insert them into the skin around their eyes for an intense sensory experience.<sup>[[187]](#cite_note-187)</sup> The use of daril dates back thousands of years. In ancient times, stone mortars were used in the process of fabricating the drug.<sup>[[188]](#cite_note-188)</sup> It is very powerful, very dangerous, and very expensive.<sup>[[183]](#cite_note-ESOLAD-183)</sup>

The Argonians' natural resistance to poison also makes them resistant to the effects of drugs like [alcohol](Lore_Alcohol.md), requiring higher doses than other races for the effects to kick in.<sup>[[189]](#cite_note-189)</sup> However, some Argonians, especially Bright-Throat elders, enjoy smoking dried marshweed.<sup>[[190]](#cite_note-190)</sup>

## Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg/200px-ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Hatching_Pools.jpg) A Hist Most, though not all, Argonian tribes revere and build their lives around a [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree. They ingest its sap during rituals, lay their eggs among its roots, and often live according to the Hist's will. Tribes that build their lives around a Hist often do so literally, constructing their settlements around it.<sup>[[197]](#cite_note-DTGO-197)</sup><sup>[[198]](#cite_note-198)</sup><sup>[[199]](#cite_note-199)</sup> The Hist guides them, and its people serve it in kind.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup> Some strange and reclusive Argonians take on troglophile lifestyles, living in underground caves.<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-ONMarshmist Paleskin-200)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NRP-13)</sup><sup>[[201]](#cite_note-Veyond Tribe-201)</sup>

The Argonian way of solving disputes differs from that of the Imperial province. Murkmire, for instance, doesn't have the traditional laws most outsiders expect. Small disputes are handled by an arbitrator of both parties' choosing. This can be anyone, though it tends to be the tribe's tree-minder.<sup>[[202]](#cite_note-Chuxu-202)</sup>

### Tribal Leaders
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ec/LG-cardart-Tree_Minder_Alt.png/200px-LG-cardart-Tree_Minder_Alt.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Tree_Minder_Alt.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Tree_Minder_Alt.png) A tree-minder caring for the Hist Argonian leaders known as tree-minders tend to the tribe's Hist and seek its wisdom. On rare occasions, the Hist may speak directly with an individual, though most tree-minders can interpret their signs.<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-ESOPavu-203)</sup> Tree-minders can feel when something is wrong with their tree, whether something is physically harming the Hist, such as a sapthief beetle infestation, or something more profound.<sup>[[204]](#cite_note-204)</sup> If something is wrong with the eggs in the uxith or the Hist senses an issue with members of the tribe, the tree-minder will feel it.<sup>[[203]](#cite_note-ESOPavu-203)</sup> The tree-minder interprets the Hist's will so the tribe may carry it out.<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-ESOTseedasi-205)</sup>

Some tribes possess a [sap-speaker](Lore_Sap-Speakers.md), who is a direct intermediary with the Hist. The sap-speaker will spend days or weeks at a time among the roots and canopy of the Hist, ingesting sap, eating the otherwise forbidden fruit of the tree, and meditating for many hours a day. The sap-speaker emerges from their solitude with the knowledge they've acquired from the Hist during their long bouts of study.<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-TMM-142)</sup> Sap-speakers have been described by Argonians as "Saxhleel blessed by the Hist with second-eyes and second-ears".<sup>[[206]](#cite_note-Xukas Swamp Beg-206)</sup>

Many tribes have an individual known as a root-herald, who works to protect their interests. While some tribesmen hunt for [wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md), the root-herald hunts for information and fruitful relationships.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup> They foster relationships that benefit their tribe, such as negotiating trade agreements.<sup>[[207]](#cite_note-MTCX-207)</sup> They also gather information from townsfolk and associates. Some outsiders may liken their behavior to that of a spy, but such a concept is foreign to the Saxhleel. A root-herald cannot serve their tribe without gathering stories and rumors. Their purpose lies not in subterfuge or espionage. Rather, the root-herald's purpose is closer to that of an ambassador, or someone who brings in news to the tribe from the outside world.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup>

Life in the perilous swamp warrants the existence of kaal—war-captains.<sup>[[208]](#cite_note-Xukas Stock-208)</sup><sup>[[209]](#cite_note-Jaxsik Caravan-209)</sup> The more war-like the tribe, the more this title is revered.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup> Some tribes also have a raj-kaal, or war-chief.<sup>[[209]](#cite_note-Jaxsik Caravan-209)</sup><sup>[[210]](#cite_note-ESOKishi-210)</sup> The name is self-explanatory: the raj-kaal is a war leader. Tribes that value their warriors may have a ka-deelith, or war-teacher, who trains novices to become full-fledged fighters.<sup>[[210]](#cite_note-ESOKishi-210)</sup> Argonian tribes also place great emphasis on their naheesh—tribal elders. They are the wisest members of the tribe, who carry the Root Talk and know all the sacred croaks and Chukka-Sei naming secrets.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-ESOHeem Jas-116)</sup> The Root Talk is a solemn affair that usually occurs during the month of Hist-Tsoko, a time dedicated to wisdom and knowledge. During Root Talk, the tribal elders recite the history of the Saxhleel.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-TSOA-110)</sup> Though the naheesh are greatly respected, their title does not indicate that they are in a position of power. Another honorable title is that of the deelith: one who passes wisdom to another.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup> There is also the raj-nassa, or elder leader, and raj-deelith, or elder teacher. A tribe's elder leaders may determine what young Argonians must do to complete their rites of maturity, and the raj-deelith prepares the hatchlings for the rite.<sup>[[211]](#cite_note-Keshu Mat1-211)</sup><sup>[[212]](#cite_note-Keshu Egg-212)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-prerelease-Murkmire_06.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Murkmire_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Murkmire_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Murkmire_06.jpg) Naga-Kur warriors, who wear the skins and bones of the fallen to protect themselves
### Warfare
Argonian arms and armor differ between tribes, and designs have changed with the passage of time. Before Duskfall, Argonians used volcanic glass, elaborately layered cloth, leather, bone, and bronze to arm and protect themselves. Their weapons and armor were often adorned with feathers, dyed yarns, jewels, bronze emblems, and gold. The use of metal is almost unheard of in many post-Duskfall aspects of Argonian craftsmanship.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CMEA-26)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/ON-concept-Mazzatun_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Mazzatun_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Mazzatun_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Mazzatun_Style.jpg) Xit-Xaht tribe armaments, modeled by a human The Naga-Kur of Murkmire craft weapons and armor from the bodies of their deceased. Scales, hide and bones from their dead are fashioned to protect the living. Frills and spines from the deceased may serve as decoration, while bones often make up the handles and business-ends of Dead-Water weapons.<sup>[[213]](#cite_note-CMDW-213)</sup>

The Xit-Xaht of Mazzatun crafted lightweight armor that relied on sturdy leather and bone to protect the flesh of the wearer. Armor and weapons were designed to allow the Xit-Xaht to move swiftly in the marsh and hit their targets hard. Layers of bone are constructed to deflect blows, though Mazzatun craftwork often leaves the midriff exposed. Plumes, avian and reptilian skulls, and the spiraling path sigil of the city of Mazzatun often adorn the Xit-Xaht's arms and armor, making them easily identifiable from other tribes.<sup>[[214]](#cite_note-CMM-214)</sup> Argonian armor from the Shadowfen region, dated to the Second Era, often utilized bones, tortoise shell, shells and snakeskin to protect the wearer. Feathers, jagged teeth, turquoise, and jade often made up the adornments on crafted pieces, with spiral and geometric designs featuring prominently in ornamentation.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-CMAS-102)</sup>

The heavy infantry of the Argonians are known as Shellbacks.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup> Kaals may rally their own units of elite shellback warriors.<sup>[[215]](#cite_note-215)</sup> Argonian shields are sometimes made from turtle shells. If not the entire shield, a turtle shell may instead serve as a decorative boss for one.<sup>[[216]](#cite_note-216)</sup> Argonian blacksmiths make use of vises composed of hard shells and metal.<sup>[[217]](#cite_note-217)</sup>

## Religion
*It was merely exciting at first, but after a few moments, he began to feel the trees, their own joy in their existence, in the process of merely being, and he felt himself gently tugged into a state of pure thought, where no words existed to constrain his feelings, where no logic tried to make sense and order of the world, and there was only color, smell, touch, motion.*
— *[Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Lord_of_Souls)*<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lo S-17)</sup><sup>:108</sup> Except for the more deeply assimilated, the Argonians do not formally recognize or worship any type of Tamrielic deity.<sup>[[218]](#cite_note-Vo F-218)</sup> They revere their creators, the Hist.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ESODeyapa-113)</sup> Marsh-born Argonians can hear the Hist—when they place their hands on its bark or rest beneath its boughs, they hear whispers or songs, and can perceive faint smells or soothing tones.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup>

This relationship is not one of bondage, but rather a bond—the Hist care for the Saxhleel,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Gather-3)</sup> and they in turn care for the Hist. The Argonians have agency apart from the Hist; their servitude is voluntary.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-TSOA-110)</sup> Marsh-born Argonians are capable of wielding [Hist-magic](Lore_Hist-magic.md), which doesn't operate on the same rules as conventional spellcraft.<sup>[[219]](#cite_note-SISRTR-219)</sup> Practicioners can summon a cloud of spores to incapacitate a target.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup>

The Argonians do revere [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), a being that even the Hist acknowledges.<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-OA-220)</sup> The [Clutch of Nisswo](Lore_Nisswo.md) is a group of priests dedicated to Sithis who wander across Murkmire to collect the 'many truths' of each tribe and share each tribes beliefs with other tribes.<sup>[[221]](#cite_note-ESOUaxal-221)</sup><sup>[[222]](#cite_note-Ao N-222)</sup> [Z'en](Lore_Z%27en.md), the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) God of Toil, is thought to have originated in Argonian and Akaviri mythologies before being introduced to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) by [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) sailors.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-Ko L-114)</sup><sup>[[115]](#cite_note-SP-115)</sup>

It typically involves a period of isolation and exposure to potent mind-altering herbs so the participant may see beyond the physical realm.<sup>[[225]](#cite_note-ESOMMFM-225)</sup> Dream-wallows take a toll on the mind and are hard on the stomach.<sup>[[226]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Dream-226)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Death_and_Dreaming_02.jpg) A pool of deep sap Members of the Bright-Throat Tribe enter the dream-wallow by ingesting deep sap, hist sap collected from deep beneath the ground. One Bright-Throat reported seeing in his vision a clam challenging him to a game of riddles, where all the words turned to orange mud, and in the end the clam was feasted on.<sup>[[226]](#cite_note-ESOXukas Dream-226)</sup> The [Naga-Kur](Lore_Naga-Kur.md) paint their faces red and inhale the fumes from burning [Starblossoms](Lore_Starblossom.md). Inside the dream-wallow, they face a kaju, a dream-beast chosen by their chief that must be killed unassisted. Tree-minders and naheesh dream-talkers are consulted for questions regarding the dream-wallow.<sup>[[227]](#cite_note-ESOMMJO-227)</sup>

A heresy relating to [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) emerged among the refugees who fled to Valenwood from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) after the creation of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md). Having severed their ties to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), many of these Argonians turned to Y'ffre and the trees of Valenwood in the hope that he could allow them to reproduce. This heresy was potentially ended in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) when ex- [Shadowscale](Lore_Shadowscale.md) refugees assassinated those responsible for abandoning the Hist.<sup>[[228]](#cite_note-ESOSNF-228)</sup>

### Creation Myth
The Adzi-Kostleel tribe of Argonians believe that the world was created in a struggle between two spirits. Originally, there was [Atak](Lore_Anuiel.md), the [Great Root](Lore_Anu.md). As Atak grew, its roots "formed new roots, and those roots took names, and they wanted space of their own to grow." Soon, it discovered the serpent spirit, [Kota](Lore_Sithis.md), who had come from the [Nothing](Lore_Padomay.md) and hungered. Atak and Kota fought and ate at each other until they forgot their conflict and became something new and indistinguishable: [Atakota](Lore_Akatosh.md). They shed their skin, severed their roots and said "Maybe". At this word, the shed skin knew itself and became a nameless [Shadow](Lore_Lorkhan.md) which followed Atakota. The Shadow had a belly full of growing [roots](Lore_Et%27Ada.md), which it spat so it could eat more of the things Atakota was making. It nearly ate the roots back but had come to see them as its own children and so told them secrets before going to sleep. The roots which had survived in the belly came to the others and shared these secrets they had gained from the Shadow. The secrets changed the roots, making them realize they were now temporary and could change.

Many of the spirits learned to fear this change, calling it Death. The chaos that ensued awoke Atakota and split them once more, leading to Atak and Kota and their roots going to war over the existence of Death. Some of the roots drank of Atakota's blood and sap until [they grew scales, fangs, and wings](Lore_Dragon.md). [Other roots](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) were protected by a [Forest Spirit](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), singing with her and becoming one with the forest. In the chaos of the war, the Shadow awoke and ate both Atak and Kota, shedding the skin of Atakota and covering all of the roots, promising to keep them safe.<sup>[[229]](#cite_note-Cho R-229)</sup>

### Shadowscales
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e2/LG-cardart-Stalking_Shadowscale.png/200px-LG-cardart-Stalking_Shadowscale.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Stalking_Shadowscale.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Stalking_Shadowscale.png) A Shadowscale The [Shadowscales](Lore_Shadowscales.md) are a longstanding monastic order of Argonian assassins that worship [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), the [Void](Lore_Void.md), and consists of Saxhleel born underneath the sign of [The Shadow](Lore_The_Shadow.md). According to some sources, the downfall of the ancient Argonians was because their "scales were darkened" by the touch of Sithis. Shadowscales also serve as a type of law enforcement in Black Marsh, bringing down "swamp law" on unruly foreigners and natives alike. They are offered at birth to the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md), a guild of assassins that also follows Sithis, to be trained in stealth and assassination. A lone Shadowscale located in Skyrim stated that their order was no longer a fully functioning group by [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[230]](#cite_note-SRVeezara-230)</sup>

Shadowscales are referred to as "ku-vastei": that which brings necessary change. They believe death is not just an end. Death is a form of change, and can be a beginning: "a cycle turned again". As assassins, they are called upon to spark change by bringing death,<sup>[[231]](#cite_note-231)</sup> but Shadowscales serve Black Marsh in many ways. Shadowscales who are fortunate enough to live to an advanced age learn to serve as ku-vastei beyond killing, such as sharing their wisdom with Black Marsh's inhabitants. The people of Murkmire believe the Shadowscales keep the natural order of things.<sup>[[202]](#cite_note-Chuxu-202)</sup>

### Daedra Worship
A few tribes (such as the Sul-Xan) worship [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), but Argonian society at large does not partake in the veneration of powers from Oblivion. In fact, there are no Jel words to describe any of the Daedra. As explained by a native speaker during the Interregnum, the Argonians do not "taint their language with words for abominations from Oblivion". Instead, they use common words from other more widely-spoken languages to refer to the beasts.<sup>[[232]](#cite_note-KCAHC-232)</sup>

## Appearance and Physiology

### Physical Traits
Although Argonians appear reptilian in nature at first glance, and moult like reptiles, they also exhibit qualities of fish, amphibians, and even birds: they are able to breathe underwater through gills,<sup>[[236]](#cite_note-Shahvee-236)</sup><sup>[[237]](#cite_note-Justino-237)</sup> some swim using the same method as that of a tadpole or eel by moving their tail side-to-side to propel through the water,<sup>[[238]](#cite_note-238)</sup> and they are capable of growing feathers.<sup>[[239]](#cite_note-239)</sup> Some Argonians have webbed hands<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:35</sup> and feet,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:34</sup> which serve to propel them through the water while swimming.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:93</sup> The membranes that connect these individuals' fingers are translucent.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:12</sup> Argonians don't sweat.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:196</sup> Because of their gills, Saxhleel cannot drown.<sup>[[156]](#cite_note-Xukas Alten-156)</sup> Argonians have tough snake-like skin, though it is not as strong as [alligator](Lore_Alligator.md) skin or armor.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MNP-240)</sup>

Horns and fins are examples of cosmetic traits that are endemic to Saxhleel anatomy.<sup>[[240]](#cite_note-241)</sup> Their fins can vibrate, and make a low humming sound if they move fast enough. Argonians put on a threatening display by expanding their fins and hissing.<sup>[[241]](#cite_note-LTFE-242)</sup> Their fins can be drawn back tight to reduce their visibility to hostile entities, allowing for more effective sneaking.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-ESOHeem Jas-116)</sup> Some fins assist the individual in swift swimming.<sup>[[242]](#cite_note-243)</sup> The Saxhleel have spines above their brow, which they raise to express various emotions.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-CMAS-102)</sup><sup>[[160]](#cite_note-ESOPalm Oil-160)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/ON-npc-Chime-Maker_Shuvu.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Chime-Maker_Shuvu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chime-Maker_Shuvu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Chime-Maker_Shuvu.jpg) An elderly Argonian from the Bright-Throat tribe Sensory organs are another aspect of Argonian anatomy that differs from that of Tamriel's other inhabitants. Some Argonians have membranes with which they use to blink, as opposed to using eyelids.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lo S-17)</sup><sup>:115</sup> Argonians secrete pheromones whose scent can indicate their mood.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lo S-17)</sup><sup>:85</sup> For instance, one who is incited to anger may emit a sharp, sulfurous scent that raises the heart rate of those in close proximity.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:19</sup> When tickled under the chin, they will reflexively open their jaws.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:56</sup> Argonians have a crop from which they can regurgitate food hours after consumption.<sup>[[243]](#cite_note-ESOJotep Liurz-244)</sup>

#### Aging
Argonians have a lifespan similar to that of [humans](Lore_Men.md).<sup>[[244]](#cite_note-245)</sup> Exceedingly old Argonians will find patches of their scales becoming translucent.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:46</sup> They also develop wrinkles when they grow old, not unlike other sapient races.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TIC-21)</sup><sup>:49</sup> As they age, their snouts turn grey; very old Argonians have white noses.<sup>[[245]](#cite_note-246)</sup><sup>[[246]](#cite_note-247)</sup> Argonian voices tend to be gravelly, and some speak slowly. Particularly old individuals may speak with a voice like creaking leather.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-CMAS-102)</sup>

#### Gender
Saxhleel genders are sometimes referred to as life-phases. The female "life-phase" is described as highly intelligent, and gifted in the magical arts. The allegedly more aggressive male "phase" reportedly embodies the traits of a hunter: stealth, speed, and agility.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWdesc-9)</sup> It can be difficult for people of other races to discern the gender of their Argonian cohorts.<sup>[[247]](#cite_note-248)</sup><sup>[[248]](#cite_note-249)</sup>

*Have you never undergone such a transformation, egg-sibling?*
*The Hist, in all its endless gifts, allows us to change many aspects of ourselves. Our gender is but one. I highly recommend such a change, should you ever get the chance.*
—Chal-Maht<sup>[[249]](#cite_note-Chal Maht-250)</sup> It is said that upon exiting the juvenile stage of life, an Argonian will lick [Hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md) from the tree's bole in order to stimulate the hormonal glands, which will cause them to sprout sexual organs.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-EGT-18)</sup> However, this is unconfirmed,<sup>[[250]](#cite_note-Ma Lot H-251)</sup> and Argonian hatchlings may apparently be of any gender.<sup>[[251]](#cite_note-MWTNW-252)</sup> Argonians can ask their Hist Tree to change their gender, which is typically followed by a celebratory ceremony.<sup>[[252]](#cite_note-AGT-253)</sup><sup>[[253]](#cite_note-Survival G-254)</sup> Female Argonians can have protrusions that resemble mammalian breasts, but at the same time are able to lay eggs.<sup>[[254]](#cite_note-Mo EB-255)</sup><sup>[[255]](#cite_note-256)</sup> Argonians do not have nipples,<sup>[[256]](#cite_note-257)</sup> but do possess cloacas.<sup>[[257]](#cite_note-UDM-258)</sup>

### Gloor-Specific Traits
Argonian appearances range from reptilian to almost human.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-GNP-259)</sup> This is caused by the Hist sap they ingest as hatchlings, an event which ceremonially takes place on their Naming Day.<sup>[[251]](#cite_note-MWTNW-252)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lo S-17)</sup><sup>:58</sup> An Argonians' appearance and physiology is almost solely determined by their Hist. This leads to some Argonian tribes differing widely in appearance from their neighbors. For example, the Bright-Throats of Murkmire have brightly colored scales of many hues. Their neighbors, the Naga-Kur, have dark, sometimes iridescent scales and red eyes. Additionally, the Naga-Kur Tribe consists of [Naga](Lore_Naga.md), a subspecies of Argonian with elongated heads and large mouths.<sup>[[258]](#cite_note-260)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a6/ON-creature-Xal-Nur_the_Slaver.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Xal-Nur_the_Slaver.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Xal-Nur_the_Slaver.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Xal-Nur_the_Slaver.jpg) An Argonian Behemoth Argonians are adaptable-by-induction to their Hist's gloor—the pervasive will/desire/need of the Hist to engender multiple inevitabilities.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-LMAM-51)</sup> Though an Argonian can have ancestry from a wildly different tribe, they will always display the characteristics of the tribe that they were born into. An example that illustrates adaptability-by-induction can be found in the Ghost People tribe, known in Jel as the [Veeskhleel](Lore_Veeskhleel.md). All Veeskhleel are impotent and cannot have children of their own,<sup>[[259]](#cite_note-ETJ-261)</sup> but by stealing eggs and hatching them under their own Hist Tree, all Veeskhleel display the same traits.<sup>[[260]](#cite_note-TOMGP-262)</sup><sup>[[261]](#cite_note-EN-263)</sup> Certain traits (such as the bright colors on a Bright-Throat's wattle) may fade the further an Argonian is from their respective Hist, though they will also come back once the Argonian returns to their tribe.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-LMAM-51)</sup> Reclusive Argonians that live in places away from sunlight (such as subterranean caverns) will lose the color in their scales.<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-ONMarshmist Paleskin-200)</sup>

Though the Saxhleel seem to generally be cold-blooded, they can apparently regulate their body temperatures just as effectively as their mammalian counterparts. Thanks to this, they can be found wandering the cold environments of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) without any negative repercussions. Some scholars believe this is possible because of the powerful magicks that lie within Hist sap, which the reptilian race ingests throughout their lives.<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-OA-220)</sup>

#### Morphology
Argonian physical traits differ from tribe to tribe, but some of these starkly different traits are shared across tribal borders. There are different general body shapes Argonians can take on. [Agacephs](Lore_Agacephs.md), [Hapsleet](Lore_Hapsleet.md), [Mihuitleel](Lore_Mihuitleel.md), [Nagas](Lore_Naga.md), [Naka-Desh](Lore_Naka-Desh.md), [Paatru](Lore_Paatru.md), and [Sarpa](Lore_Sarpa.md) are breeds of Argonian described as being physiologically distinct.<sup>[[262]](#cite_note-To BR-264)</sup> Certain argonians are said to resemble alligators, while others resemble gray-spotted [geckos](Lore_Gecko.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TPThe Blue Dawn-265)</sup> Some Argonians draw a distinction between the Saxhleel and the Naga. Nagas tend to be taller and have wider faces than the Saxhleel.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOJela-19)</sup> Described as having "huge mouths filled with dripping needle-like fangs", they reside within the inner regions of Argonia and are usually seven to eight feet tall. They have beady eyes,<sup>[[263]](#cite_note-Xukas Cara-266)</sup> are not friendly towards outsiders,<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-TAA4-80)</sup> and tend to be more inclined to engage in violence and warfare.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOJela-19)</sup> The majority of the native brigands and highwaymen are tribeless Naga.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup>

The Nagas' proclivity for cruelty does not make them "evil", nor are they universally reviled by the rest of the Argonians because of it.<sup>[[264]](#cite_note-267)</sup><sup>[[265]](#cite_note-Jaxsik Cares-268)</sup> The Naga-Kur of Murkmire may be rather unfriendly to foreigners, but for the most part, they remain at peace with the rest of Murkmire, and do not go out of their way to destroy other tribes.<sup>[[266]](#cite_note-TSDS-269)</sup> There are indeed cruel tribes of Naga, such as the Sul-Xan, who are more than willing to harm their fellow Argonian for one reason or another,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOJela-19)</sup> but the Nagas are not a monolith.

### Reproduction and Child-Rearing
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/ON-quest-Empty_Nest.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Empty_Nest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Empty_Nest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Empty_Nest.jpg) A nest of Argonian eggs Argonian life typically begins with a clutch of eggs [conceived](Lore_Sex.md) by a mated pair. The tribes of Black Marsh all have their own customs for choosing mates. Some pick each other out of love<sup>[[267]](#cite_note-270)</sup> or attraction,<sup>[[249]](#cite_note-Chal Maht-250)</sup> and some, like the Bright-Throat tribe, bond with members of other tribes to improve tribal relations.<sup>[[268]](#cite_note-271)</sup> The concept of procreational partnership varies from tribe to tribe. Each group's pattern of affection sharing and egg-quickening depend on the will of their Hist. Uvastuxith (nest-becoming), tumjum (allegorical house-weaving), and thtithatei (egg-stomach) are all examples of such concepts and practices.<sup>[[186]](#cite_note-LAMQA2-186)</sup> Argonians can display ovoviviparity, and due to their humanoid structure they can have live births where the eggs hatch internally.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-PHP-272)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-DGP-273)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-GNP-259)</sup> Argonians born in cold foreign provinces or to small nomadic tribes tend to have live births without the use of egg nests, while those in warmer climates with established, stable, and permanent communities would likely see a great number of eggs.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MNP-240)</sup>

In any case, eggs are laid and transferred to an uxith (or "nest"), where they are tended to by midwives until they hatch. While the Argonians take care of their eggs, the Hist guides their development and determines what shape the hatchlings will take.<sup>[[269]](#cite_note-ESOMimme-274)</sup> Argonians are born with an egg-tooth, which the newborns use to hatch from their eggs. A cracked egg-tooth on a newborn Argonian is seen as a good omen.<sup>[[254]](#cite_note-Mo EB-255)</sup> For some tribes, much of the egg-laying happens during the month of Thtithil.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-TSOA-110)</sup>

The Bright-Throat tribe from Murkmire has egg-tenders (or "egg-nurses")<sup>[[270]](#cite_note-Wot T-275)</sup> who perform this task. On top of attending the needs of the eggs and discerning which ones are fertile and which ones are not destined to hatch, the tenders also read nursery rhymes and play the flute for their eggs.<sup>[[271]](#cite_note-Meena-276)</sup><sup>[[272]](#cite_note-RC-277)</sup> Those who tend the eggs beneath the Hist at the Hatching Pools in [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) are known as the Keepers of the Shell.<sup>[[273]](#cite_note-278)</sup> The Keepers keep meticulous record of every egg that enters the uxith; the pairs they were born to, how many hatchlings emerge, when the eggs hatch and which star signs the hatchlings are born under are just some of the things recorded in their logs.<sup>[[274]](#cite_note-KEIP-279)</sup><sup>[[254]](#cite_note-Mo EB-255)</sup> They perform the usual tasks one would expect from someone who tends and protects eggs, such as monitoring the eggs and crushing biting bugs that may harm them.<sup>[[275]](#cite_note-280)</sup> There are signs to look for in an unhealthy egg, including dry shells, lack of luster, a thin membrane, and an empty shadow. If an Argonian egg is not destined to hatch, it returns to the Hist, sinking into the roots and fading away.<sup>[[269]](#cite_note-ESOMimme-274)</sup> The Tide-Born of Solstice refer to their egg-nurses as shoal-tenders.<sup>[[270]](#cite_note-Wot T-275)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Bright-Throat_Uxith.jpg) An uxith, where Argonian children are born Unlike the children of other races, who must be constantly cared for by their parents in order to survive, Argonian hatchlings are born with the ability to walk.<sup>[[266]](#cite_note-TSDS-269)</sup> Hatchlings are fed licorice worms,<sup>[[276]](#cite_note-281)</sup> and those who are teething are given pacifiers made of hardened Hist sap to chew.<sup>[[277]](#cite_note-282)</sup> Some parents carry their small hatchlings arround on their backs in swaddle-skins.<sup>[[278]](#cite_note-ESOKloxu-283)</sup> Eventually, their Naming Day comes. The hatchling licks the Hist and is granted a name.<sup>[[212]](#cite_note-Keshu Egg-212)</sup>

Unlike Imperial children, Argonian hatchlings from the greater marsh don't generally play with rag dolls.<sup>[[279]](#cite_note-284)</sup> However, figurines shaped like animals and Argonians are often carved for children.<sup>[[280]](#cite_note-285)</sup> Argonian moppet dolls also exist, and they are quite a rare sight outside of Murkmire.<sup>[[281]](#cite_note-286)</sup> Hatchlings can be easily amused by frog-shaped squeaky toys.<sup>[[282]](#cite_note-287)</sup> Another example of a toy created for Argonian children is that of a wooden box which launches a spring-loaded snake when opened.<sup>[[283]](#cite_note-288)</sup>

When a hatchling comes of age, they undergo their Chukka-Sei, a trial of maturity, in order to prove themselves worthy of being called an adult.<sup>[[278]](#cite_note-ESOKloxu-283)</sup> These trials are typically designed to test one's skill and bravery, and may be conducted over the course of several days. Some of these tests are set, and used throughout the greater marsh, though there are innumerable local customs. Some of these rites change depending on the location, time of season, or the raj-nassa's preference,<sup>[[211]](#cite_note-Keshu Mat1-211)</sup> and some tests are catered to the individual.<sup>[[278]](#cite_note-ESOKloxu-283)</sup> Those who pass the trials gain full tribal membership. These trials typically occur during the month of Hist-Dooka, which usually ends with a great celebration.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-TSOA-110)</sup> Some wear special clothing during their trials, such as crocodile tooth necklaces.<sup>[[284]](#cite_note-289)</sup> The tribe's neiweets and root-nurses may prepare a feast for when the trial-goer completes their maturation rite.<sup>[[285]](#cite_note-290)</sup>

## Tribes
Argonians usually live in tribes, each having their own customs as well as differing appearances. In fact, the name "Argonian" itself seems to be more of a catch-all term that refers to all the different tribes of lizard folk that dwell within Black Marsh.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1-46)</sup> There are a number of known tribes.

Adzi-Kostleel
- The tribe of Murkmire that claimed to be the bearer of an oral tradition of the Argonian creation myth, collected by researcher Solis Aduro.<sup>[[229]](#cite_note-Cho R-229)</sup>
[Agacephs](Lore_Agacephs.md)

[Archeins](Lore_Archein.md)

Since other races have realized that Argonia is unfit for plantations, they have gone bankrupt.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-TAA4-80)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1-46)</sup>
Ca-Uxith
- A tribe native to Blackwood. They take care of the xanmeer at Krona-Konu (known to outsiders as "Rockgrove"), which comes with the advantage of the structure's stone walls providing protection for the tribe.<sup>[[286]](#cite_note-291)</sup> Most of the tribe lives in a village just outside the xanmeer. Krona-Konu stands upon a network of steam vents, which can damage the xanmeer's foundation when they erupt. The Ca-Uxith maintain the ruin's structural integrity. The tribe also has a stone-talker: one who serves as conduit between the Hist and Rockgrove's xanmeer. The stone-talker knows much about the ancient city's secrets.<sup>[[287]](#cite_note-292)</sup>

## Artifacts

#### [Eye of Argonia](Lore_Eye_of_Argonia.md)
The [Eye of Argonia](Lore_Eye_of_Argonia.md) is a treasured artifact described as the "priceless king's jewel of ancient [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md)".

Little is known of the eye, but it has captured the imagination of treasure hunter's throughout all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) as to what it is capable of. Some theories included this invaluable gem being a key the "lost city of Black Marsh", or that it could serve as a gateway to undiscovered lands.

At some point during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) Lark of [Rosgard](Lore_Rosgard.md), a mysterious masked thief sought to obtain the Eye. Years later [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) and the Cyro-Nordic captain [Tobias](Lore_Tobias.md) both wished to attain the prized artifact as well, although it's unknown if either of them ever succeeded in doing so. An archeologist and author by the name of Rolard Nordssen aspired to claim the fabled eye, a potential accomplishment which he considered even greater than his prior feats. Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) mentioned the legendary jewel as a means to cover for his true intentions to infiltrate the chambers of the Common Tongue press printers. There exists dubious tales about the jewel's discovery.

#### [Fang of Haynekhtnamet](Lore_Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.md)
The [Fang of Haynekhtnamet](Lore_Fang_of_Haynekhtnamet.md) is a literal fang which was carved into a blade and fashioned into a small dagger in an exquisite Argonian style. It is named after [Haynekhtnamet](Lore_Haynekhtnamet.md), a mythical [wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md) from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). According to the book *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*, the artifact was created by a score of [Nords](Lore_Nords.md) who fought Haynekhtnamet for seven days and nights before finally bringing the creature down, believing it to be a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md). One of the survivors took a fang as a trophy, and it was later carved into a dagger which mysteriously houses some of the beast's [magical](Lore_Magic.md) properties. The dagger grants the wielder the ability to shock an opponent. Other reports indicate the dagger was taken from the eponymous wamasu in the [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) region of Black Marsh in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), This jagged blade was heavily befouled and would cause disease in anyone it struck. The Fang of Haynekhtnamet has been seen occasionally by traveling heroes throughout history.

#### [Fangs of Sithis](Lore_Fangs_of_Sithis.md)
The [Fangs of Sithis](Lore_Fangs_of_Sithis.md) are a set of [pre-Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) weapons, taking the form of either two large eggs, or a set of dual daggers.

The Fangs were gifted to the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) by Sithis, the Dark Husband, who in turn gifted them to the [Shadowscales](Lore_Shadowscales.md). It is said that a single Shadowscale wielding the Fangs could lay waste to entire villages not just via mass killings, but also by causing crop-failures and famine. However, most are unable to safely wield the Fangs, as they feed upon the wielder's mind and soul, causing the weak-minded either to go mad, or become mindless agents of Sithis who are devoid of free will.

#### Fenmender
Fenmender is a legendary Argonian staff with the ability to reverse the effects of decay.<sup>[[314]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-319)</sup><sup>:21,28</sup> It was at one point plundered by Orc warriors that raided [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Centuries later during the Interregnum, it would become a source of conflict between the [Cyrodilic Collections](Lore_Cyrodilic_Collections.md), who sought the return of Argonian antiquities to their rightful owners, and the House of Orsimer Glories, who considered the staff a piece of Orcish history and sought to store it in their museum.<sup>[[314]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-319)</sup><sup>:21</sup> It was also one of the powerful items desired for the Soldiers of the Void's collection checklist that have some type of resurrection capability, which they wanted to utilize to raise an undead army in the [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), the site of [great battle](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md) where one is "just as likely to stumble upon a corpse as a shrub".<sup>[[314]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-319)</sup><sup>:48,50</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_04.jpg/150px-ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Gaze_of_Sithis_04.jpg) Gaze of Sithis
#### [Gaze of Sithis](Lore_Gaze_of_Sithis.md)
The [Gaze of Sithis](Lore_Gaze_of_Sithis.md) is a well-preserved ancient Argonian helm artifact dating back to before the [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) period, whose eye sockets fill even the bravest with a sense of dread. The enchantment makes it so the user can no longer mitigate damage taken from blocking an attack, but this is alleviated by increasing the user's sturdiness and overall health and recovery time.

The artifact's power comes from the various components that make up the helm's anatomy, though their functions are speculated by scholars. The hand-etched [malachite](Lore_Malachite.md) skull mask perhaps represents [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) and it contains reservoirs of chaotic energy that indicates some sort of trade needed in exchange for power. The [Nisswo](Lore_Nisswo.md) fabric wraps secures the mask to the wearer's head, reminiscent in a way done to a [Bog Blight](Lore_Bog_Blight.md). These pristine fabrics may have been used for an Argonian warrior of high status, indicated by the fabric's patterns. Crowning the helmet is the ritual metal circlet of an unknown heavy material that was perhaps imported. Pad-sa shark teeth line the bottom of the circlet, perhaps intended to channel the shark's resilience or hunger. Enveloping the top and back of the mask are feathers from the extinct [Wraxu](Lore_Wraxu.md) bird that may have had a cultural connection to Sithis.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c7/ON-furnishing-Replica_Mnemic_Egg.jpg/150px-ON-furnishing-Replica_Mnemic_Egg.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_Mnemic_Egg.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Replica_Mnemic_Egg.jpg) Mnemic Egg Replica
#### [Mnemic Egg](Lore_Mnemic_Egg.md)
The [Mnemic Egg](Lore_Mnemic_Egg.md) is an Argonian relic that can act as the physical link between the Argonians and the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). It is a sacred symbol of birth and life, containing the memories and wisdom of the Hist. The Mnemic Egg takes the form of a seed infused with [Hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md), which visibly flows and pulsates throughout the Egg.

When a Hist tree dies, the death is described as the tree being "born backwards." That is to say, the death of a Hist creates a Mnemic Egg. The memories of that Hist and its Argonians are all contained within the Mnemic Egg. The Hist share a collective consciousness; they have the ability to communicate with one another, and to feel what another Hist is feeling. As a result of the Hist's shared connection, and the fact that the Mnemic Egg is a physical vessel for all of the memories of the dead Hist that birthed it, the Mnemic Egg can be used as a gateway into the collective consciousness of all Hist Trees. This also means that in the wrong hands, a Mnemic Egg can be used to sever the Argonian connection to the Hist.

#### [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md)
The [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md) is an artifact created by the Argonian [Root-Whisper Tribe](Lore_Root-Whisper_Tribe.md) in a last ditch effort to save both themselves and their [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) from the invading [Barsaebic](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md). The invading Ayleids had been harvesting the souls of their tribe at an alarming rate, and so the Root-Whisper Hist sacrificed itself to create the Remnant of Argon, an amber artifact that could contain the souls of the entire tribe until the Ayleid incursion had passed. The Barsaebic Ayleids attempted to stop its creation, but were held back long enough for the Remnant to be completed.

Though the Remnant of Argon was meant to only be a temporary measure, it was slowly lost to time. The Ayleid occupation lasted far longer than anticipated thanks to their leader Myndhal's efforts. With the Root-Whisper Hist cut off from the rest of its kind and the Root-Whisper tribe all but dead, there was no clear way for them to pass along the Remnant's location. Eventually, all knowledge of the Remnant of Argon faded into obscurity, only remembered by the Hist and the Argonians of Murkmire as something that invoked immense sadness.

The souls trapped within the remnant were eventually released by a hero known as the Rootmender with the aid of a Bright-Throat root-herald and a Naga-Kur warrior, one of whom sacrificed their lives to guide the souls of the Root-Whisper Tribe back to the Hist. With this act, the dreaming Hist of the Root-Whisper was awakened, and the remaining Saxhleel envoy went on to lead the new tribe.<sup>[[298]](#cite_note-BRa R-303)</sup>

## Notes
- The Game Director of *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, [Vijay Lakshman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Vijay_Lakshman), explains the origins of the Argonian race: "I used to love the old [Sinbad movies](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_7th_Voyage_of_Sinbad) and fondly recalled some of the [stop-motion creatures](https://gwangipedia.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon#:~:text=The%20Dragon%20is%20a%20large,iconic%20creatures%20of%20the%20film) they would fight. For some reason, the word Argonian stuck with me from *[Jason and the Argonauts](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jason_and_the_Argonauts_(1963_film))* and I equated that to exploration. What's at the farthest reach of your mind and the world? In my mind, there be dragons, hence the Argonians being reptilian."<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Vijay Making Of-320)</sup>
- When first introduced in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, the Argonians were much more humanoid than lizardlike in appearance, having grey skin, abdominal muscles with a linea alba, nipples, human-like toes and fingers, and lacking tails altogether. From *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* onwards, Argonians were given a more lizardlike appearance, having green skin, lizard heads, large tails, scaly underbellies without a linea alba, and no longer having mammalian nipples; *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)* going even further by giving them clawed feet incapable of wearing human shoes. - [Lizard Men](Lore_Lizard_Man.md) were once thought to be distant cousins of Argonians but only appeared in the game where Argonians were depicted as being more humanoid.<sup>[[315]](#cite_note-Arena Manual-321)</sup> In later games, lizard men is sometimes a term synonymous with Argonians.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1-46)</sup><sup>[[316]](#cite_note-Ts-322)</sup><sup>[[317]](#cite_note-OMa M-323)</sup>
- The [Pocket Guide to the Empire, 1st Edition](Lore_Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_1st_Edition_The_Wild_Region.md) incorrectly describes the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) as a "relatively intelligent strain of Argonian".<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-PGE1-46)</sup>
- The egg (thtithil) is a very important part of Saxhleel culture.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-TSTAJP-99)</sup> Some Argonians inscribe the shells of their hatched eggs with sentimental verses.<sup>[[318]](#cite_note-324)</sup>
- When the Dark Elves were not allied with the Argonians, they often made use of Argonian parts in crafts, such as plates made of Argonian scales.<sup>[[319]](#cite_note-325)</sup> Fashion-forward Dunmer were finding ways to use Argonian horns to enhance their own appearance. One such use for extracted horns was bone blush, an ivory powder applied to the face. It was especially popular at the height of the Argonian-horn fashion trend of the Second Era, but became much rarer after the Ebonheart Pact was formed.<sup>[[320]](#cite_note-326)</sup>
