—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Lillandril is one of the eight major cities in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> situated on the western hills of the [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). The city is known for scholarly pursuits, various institutes such as the [College of Sapiarchs](Lore_College_of_Sapiarchs.md) and the Illumination Academy were both found in vicinity of the coastal-town.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBIAG-2)</sup> The city's symbol is the [Flask of Lillandril](Lore_Flask_of_Lillandril.md), an enchanted bottle that absorbs the magic used against the bearer. Despite its infamy, it has been embraced by the people as a cultural symbol.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOBanner-3)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fc/ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg/200px-ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-College_of_Sapiarchs.jpg) The College of Sapiarchs Lillandril is built atop the hills on the northwest coast, where the land gives way to the [ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) to form the lowland and sunken basin. The roads are flooded by water but gradually rise above it as it gets closer to the settlement. Even though it is a port town, it is intricately built on the hillside, slowly rising around it until it reaches the upper platform and the [Kinhouse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kinhouse), well above sea level and with a grand view of the [surrounding waters](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md). The open-aired main street, which coils around the mountain leads to the upper platform, and everything in between is crowded alleys and stairways. At the height of Lillandril lies the portal master, who guards an [egress](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) that transports people to the [College of Sapiarchs](Lore_College_of_Sapiarchs.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLillandril-4)</sup> It is an academic compound built atop of an island off the coast of Lillandril, where many kings and queens of Summerset go to study on their way to their throne.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OTCOS-5)</sup> Lillandril is neighbored by several settlements, such as [Karnwasten Moor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Karnwasten_Moor) to the southwest and Marbruk Brook to the southeast.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/09/RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png/150px-RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-item-Flask_of_Lillandril.png) The Flask of Lillandril For countless centuries, Lillandril was known as the center of higher learning,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBIAG-2)</sup> with the Illumination Academy and the College of Sapiarchs serving these purposes. Throughout the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second Eras](Lore_Second_Era.md), the cities of Summerset often engaged in conflict with each other, eventually blossoming into full-scale warfare. Lillandril would often battle [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md) from the south.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE3E-8)</sup>

Sometime around [1E 470](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_470), a group of [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) discovered a magical flask with the ability to absorb magic from wherever. With its potential power, the founders used it to perform various tasks for barons across the province and to keep the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) in check. They later disappeared and crash-landed in a coral reef on what is now [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EA-9)</sup> When [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md) erupted in [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md) archipelago in [1E 2484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2484), people from Lillandril witnessed smoke from a distance and smelled brimstone.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SHV5-10)</sup> The Alinor-Lillandril skirmishes, one of their many conflicts throughout history took place in [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONEye-11)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the tension between the College of Sapiarchs and the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) had grown exponentially since they established themselves in town. The Sapiarch, Haladan was murdered by a member of the Mages Guild, Emile Oncent, who confessed to the situation. But because of the nature surrounding his confession, a [newcomer](Lore_The_Vestige.md) was tasked to uncover the truth and both Emile and Haladan's apparent relationship. The newcomer uncovered their hidden plot to cure [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md) for their mutual friend, Norasea, but Haladan was killed by one of her feral friends. It is unknown whether their secret was exposed to the public and Emile was sparred, or if the information was kept hidden to honor the pair's secret and thereby sparing Norasea of an execution. Regardless, the situation had been resolved.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOMurder-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HRJ-13)</sup>

In [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), the kings of the Summerset Isles formed a unified alliance to combat the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md)<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-14)</sup> in their last-known invasion of the province, the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup> Despite having assistance from Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) and the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md), the Maormer were defeated because of a supernatural storm accredited to the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-14)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TWQ5-16)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Lillandril was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Corridalf and had a rivalry with Alinor.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AR-7)</sup> During that decade, the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) traveled through town on their quest to re-assemble the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md), after acquiring the fourth piece in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). They ventured to the city's Mages Guild and spoke with their loremaster, Corim Ashlen on the location of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), where the fifth piece was located. The guildhall was previously attacked by the priesthood of the [Mad God](Lore_Sheogorath.md), who sought a diamond that could reveal the tower's location. The champion retrieved the artifact from the Temple of the Mad God and returned it to Ashlen. Using the gem's power, the loremaster scribed the tower's location for the champion.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ARQuest-17)</sup>

### Known Rulers
For the longest time, the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) served as counselors to the rulers of the Summerset Isles. If anyone wanted their counsel, their best method was either through a select few places or the kings themselves. They continued this service up until their island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) first vanished in the Second Era, around when the Mages Guild was formed. When they returned several years later for the Third Era, they continued where they left off<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FOA-18)</sup> until Artaeum disappeared a second time sometime in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) for whatever reason.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SRTolfdir-19)</sup>

## Culture and Society

- *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Altmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Altmer.md#Society_and_Religion)** sections on the Altmer page.*

The Flask of Lillandril was a powerful artifact discovered by its ancient scribes. Despite its association with the High Elves, however, it was not of elvish origin.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-RGJ.27ffer-21)</sup> The wielders of the flask in the early First Era fled the southern provinces for [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) and sought refuge with the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) in fear that Wood Elf assassins would track them down. The founders were killed and the flask was lost on Stros M'Kai,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EA-9)</sup> where it was later discovered by [Cyrus the Restless](Lore_Cyrus.md) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-RGFlask-22)</sup> Ever since their victory over [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), many [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) -loyalists re-traced the Eternal Champion's steps through their journey, which meant they would have stopped in Lillandril.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-AMM-23)</sup>

The Altmer have a common disinterest with the divine, [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), who is known by many names, such as the Apologist of Man. Despite this, the [Sect of Harmonious Masters](Lore_Sect_of_Harmonious_Masters.md), mages from Lillandril, have dedicated their lives to working with Stendarr's light and adapting it into the School of [Restoration](Lore_Restoration.md). The order believes they are honor-bound to teach all races their spells, so long as they are willing to learn from it.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ROTHM-24)</sup> While other groups such as the [Resolutes of Stendarr](Lore_Resolutes_of_Stendarr.md) use the god's light to smite and defeat evil, others such as the Harmonious Masters or the [Temple of Stendarr](Lore_Temple_of_Stendarr.md) use it to heal.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-AOTR-25)</sup>

The name is also used for male Altmer, either directly using *"Lillandril"* or spelling it alternatively as *"Lillandriil"*.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ARNames-26)</sup> By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Lillandril was a major port on the [East Empire Company](Lore_East_Empire_Company.md)'s southwest lines, between the cities of [Dusk](Lore_Dusk.md) and [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai_(city).md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SRMap-27)</sup> The first iteration of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) used an alloy called Lillandril [Steel](Lore_Steel.md) in the manufacturing of their longbows.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-CM25-28)</sup>

## Notes
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Lillandril's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Crimson Guard".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-29)</sup>
- A [string file from Daggerfall Preview](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall_Preview) contains the name "Lillendril Caernim" for player characters, which perhaps lent (or borrowed) its name to (or from) the city.
