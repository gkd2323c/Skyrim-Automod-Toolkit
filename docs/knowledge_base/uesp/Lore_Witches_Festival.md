# Witches Festival

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Sinister_Hollowjack_Items.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Sinister_Hollowjack_Items.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Sinister_Hollowjack_Items.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Furnishing_Pack_Sinister_Hollowjack_Items.jpg) Decor associated with Hollowjack and the Witches Festival (ESO) The Witches Festival (sometimes spelled as **Witch's Festival** or **Witch's Fest**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup> is an annual holiday that falls on the 13th of [Frostfall](Lore_Frostfall.md), coinciding with [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md)'s Summoning Day.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WFDF-3)</sup> During the festival, [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s residents defy superstition, mocking and celebrating [ghosts](Lore_Ghost.md), demons, and evil spirits through occult events and outrageous costumes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WFR-4)</sup> Beggars ask for alms, children request festival treats,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONFestive Beggar-5)</sup> and people are obliged to provide them.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONFestive Treat-6)</sup> Youth often engage in wanton vandalism as part of the tradition.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONJelly-7)</sup> Pumpkins are iconic, carved into hollowjack lanterns,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONRind Renewing-8)</sup> cooked, or smashed for fun.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONGourd-9)</sup> The [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) hold the Autumnal Ornamental Gourd Triple-Toss, a dexterity test involving juggling pumpkins.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONJugglin-10)</sup> Festival foods are ghastly-themed, with names like Frosted Brains, Bowls of "Peeled Eyeballs," and Bewitched Sugar Skulls.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Gruesome Feast-11)</sup> Cities across Tamriel embrace the festival, adorned in styles reminiscent of [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) wickerwork.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Alinor-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RTTR-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Alinor-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RTTR-13)</sup>

During the Witches Festival, portals between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Detritus](Lore_Detritus.md) open,<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> allowing [Daedra Lord](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) Hha-Lugh-Zhek, better known as [Hollowjack](Lore_Hollowjack.md) the [Pumpkin Spectre](Lore_Pumpkin_Spectre.md), to feed on mortal terror, making fears come true and driving people mad.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LHDR-15)</sup> Hollowjack bears some similarities to Jaque o' the Hollow, a malevolent spirit said to haunt a hidden hollow in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) that curses riders all across Tamriel to roam headless with pumpkins atop their mounts.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> The Thicketman Spectre, a [Scarecrow Spectre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scarecrow_Spectre), haunts harvested fields, taking the heads of those who approach. Both are popular costumes for the festival.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ONHollowjack Mask-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONThicketman Spectre Mask-19)</sup> In [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) myths, Old Nasty Nackles is a tall, thin, pale [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) spectre with oddly bending joints who punishes or kidnaps misbehaving children, during [Tales and Tallows](Lore_Tales_and_Tallows.md) or the Witches Festival.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/de/ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Festive_Beggar_and_Treats.jpg) Wearing costumes and giving or receiving treats is a norm during the festival (ESO) The Witches Festival is also a time when the forces of sorcery and religion clash,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> as temples and the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) compete for attention. Temples remain open all night to combat evil, while mages, nightblades, and spellswords roam the streets.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> Sinister figures gather in the wilderness outside cities, including demonologists, conjurors, [lamias](Lore_Lamia.md), warlocks, and thaumaturgists, who summon or create creatures that may haunt Tamriel for eons.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WFDF-3)</sup> Celebrated as the most sacred holiday for [witches](Lore_Witches.md),<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OPS-23)</sup> covens spend the year crafting necromantic objects of dark delights. Normally considered contraband, these twisted treats may be freely sold or given as gifts during the Festival.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-IRHCCS-24)</sup> [Witchmothers](Lore_Witchmothers.md) brew cauldrons of pumpkins, guts, and the essence of death, luring adventurers to take a drink from them with promises of power.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-WFEWO-25)</sup> The witch covens tempt adventurers with plunder skulls, macabre skull-shaped baskets overflowing with treasure that stir both fear and greed, by placing them in the hands of the most dangerous villains. As adventurers cause chaos in their hunts to claim them, the witches’ power and vitality quietly grow.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OPS-23)</sup>

During the Witches Festival, the Mages Guild draws most of the business by offering free evaluations of weapons and items for mystic potential, half-price spells, and discounted [Scrolls](Lore_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WFDF-3)</sup> [Wise-Wing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wise-Wing) the Owl is associated in northern Tamriel with the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) god [Jhunal](Lore_Jhunal.md), and is said to warn the unwary away from graveyards and witches' covens during the Witches Festival.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CSS-26)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/ON-event-Witches_Festival_2024.jpg/200px-ON-event-Witches_Festival_2024.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-event-Witches_Festival_2024.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-event-Witches_Festival_2024.jpg) Lord Hollowjack (ESO) It is unknown when the Witches Festival first started. Since the Witches Festival falls on Mephala's Summoning Day,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-WFDF-3)</sup> and other holidays, such as [New Life](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Life) and [Heart's Day](Lore_Heart%27s_Day.md), coincide with the Summoning Days of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) and [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md) respectively, the [Impresario](Lore_The_Impresario.md), when asked about this coincidence, stated that there is no such thing when it comes to culture and belief.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LATH-2)</sup>

By the time the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) documented him, Hollowjack had long fed on the fears of hundreds, with his victims known as "Hollowjack's Haunted."<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LHDR-15)</sup> The eldest Haunted, Maxien Roudille, a priest of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), endured this terror until a bargain spared him: in exchange, he would lure bold heroes to become Haunted, which he complied. However, around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Maxien sought the help of the Undaunted to stop his terror. This led to Hollowjack being slain, but as a Daedric Lord, his defeat was only temporary and he eventually reformed.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-MR-28)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg/200px-ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-prerelease-Witches_Festival.jpg) A witchmother creating a reaper for the Witches Festival (ESO) The [Witchmothers](Lore_Witchmothers.md), a coven that worships [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), formed at some point to provide a protective role to the people of Tamriel. They act as [seers](Lore_Sight.md), scrying for disturbances in the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) that could threaten [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). They play a particularly important role during the Witches Festival by serving as caretakers in the time of Harvest—their name for the ritualized killing of the most vile creatures on Tamriel that takes place during the festival. One such danger that threatened the festival was the [Crowborne Horror](Lore_Crowborne_Horror.md), which emerged following [Nocturnal's Summoning Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nocturnal%27s_Summoning_Day) and lingered behind waiting to strike. With the help of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), the Witchmother Taerma lured the Horror to a pocket realm deep in the Evergloam known as the [Garden of Shadows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Garden_of_Shadows), drawing it further away from Nirn and draining some of its power for the coven's own purposes.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Taerma-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup>

Circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), during [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md)'s Witches Festival celebration, a portal opened up carrying the haunting remnants of forbidden rites and curses from beyond the veil of the dead. Creatures lay within, impervious from attacks save for cleaving weapons and poisions, leading to the [Warrior](Lore_Warrior.md) venturing into the portal and slaying the [ogre](Lore_Ogre.md) responsible and stopping the threat.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> The apprentice of [Balagog gro-Nolob](Lore_Balagog_gro-Nolob.md), [Urzhag gro-Larak](Lore_Urzhag_gro-Larak.md), visited [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) during the Witches Festival circa [4E 197](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_197). He enjoyed the local's version of the Witchmother's Party Punch, a drink created long ago by Olvye of the Witchmothers coven.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Cookbook2-32)</sup>
