# Ebonheart Pact

—Alla Lalleth<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TTOTEP-1)</sup> The Ebonheart Pact was a military alliance between the [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_House) [Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), [Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril), and [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), the Kingdom of Eastern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and the tribes of [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen) and [Thornmarsh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thornmarsh) in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FATS-2)</sup> during the chaotic [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). It vied with the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) and [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) for control over the contested Ruby Throne in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). It was a creation of unlikely allies, who had long histories of strife between them, but united for mutual defense, first against [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) invaders and later against the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) cultists of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) in Cyrodiil. They aimed to wipe away the rash rule of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) and end once and for all mortals' entanglements with higher power from beyond [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TTOTEP-1)</sup> The Pact was led by [Jorunn](Lore_Jorunn.md) the Skald-King, who headed the [Great Moot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Moot), as well as the three [living gods](Lore_Tribunal.md) of the Dunmer.

The full extent of the Pact's territory in Black Marsh is disputed. Vicecanon [Heita-Meen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heita-Meen), who was called by the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) to lead the Argonians to join the Pact, claimed that the tribes of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md) joined together with Shadowfen and Thornmarsh.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FATS-2)</sup> However, Murkmire is a region on the southern coast of Black Marsh and centered on [Lilmoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lilmoth); although the Pact sent envoys to Murkmire to convince the tribes to join, the region was far removed from Pact lands and remained fully autonomous. Locals discounted Heita-Meen's claims as being purely aspirational.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Some sources incorrectly<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> name the region surrounding the city of [Gideon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gideon) as Murkmire.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Sybil-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Heita-6)</sup> Given that this region borders Shadowfen and Thornmarsh, it is possible that Heita-Meen's claims refer to Gideon's hinterlands.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

## History
The Pact arose out of the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) in [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GTTEP-7)</sup> The force under the command of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal) landed just north of [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), taking the city completely by surprise.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSAI-8)</sup> The city was sacked and Queen [Mabjaarn](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and her heir [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md) were slain, leaving the crown to Mabjaarn's son, Jorunn.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JTSK-9)</sup> Together with his twin-brother Fildgor, he rallied a desperate defense, but the Akaviri army bypassed him, marching instead into eastern Morrowind, where they were met by Dunmeri forces under [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia). In an unprecedented show of cooperation between races with a great deal of historical enmity, Jorunn and Almalexia worked together to encircle Dir-Kamal's forces in [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UA-10)</sup> The resulting [Battle of Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Vivec%27s_Antlers) was a close-fought engagement until the surprise arrival of [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) reinforcements, who helped drive the invaders back into the sea, ending their threat to Tamriel.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TSAI-8)</sup>

This allegiance forged in battle was ratified with treaties signed in [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UA-10)</sup> resulting in the manumission of Argonians enslaved by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) as thanks for their crucial intervention.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GTTEP-7)</sup> The Pact struggled to reconcile the desires of three such distinct factions, but was able to stay strong for over a decade due to the need for mutual defense and the camaraderie of brothers-in-arms.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UA-10)</sup> Eventually, the Tribunal became aware of dark happenings in Cyrodiil, and called upon its allies to mount a righteous war against the corrupt and disintegrating Empire.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTCOT-11)</sup>

During its existence, it successfully overcame an invasion by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), through summoning the former [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) warriors, the [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(creature).md). It faced trouble in the form of Maulborn and [Stormfist](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormfist_Clan) uprisings in [Deshaan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deshaan) and [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md), respectively. [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen) was attacked by the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), and [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) was attacked by [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) and the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult). However, the Pact managed to deal with all these threats.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESO-12)</sup>

## Institutions

### Great Moot
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg/200px-ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg) A Great Moot The Ebonheart Pact was governed by a separate body called the Great Moot, wherein decisions were ratified by representatives from the Pact's three member nations. The Great Moot convened in the Pact's capital of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) of Eastern Skyrim was named acting High King of the Great Moot after he thwarted an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) invasion with the aid of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) forces.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-JTSK-9)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Jorunn's rule was again ratified at the summit of [Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md) following his brother [Fildgor Orcthane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fildgor)'s failed attempt to usurp the throne.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

### Vicecanons
On the regional level, vicecanons represented each of the three Pact provinces of Skyrim, Morrowind and Black Marsh to oversee the safety and prosperity of that region. For example, in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the Black Marsh region of [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen) was overseen by vicanons Hrondar (Skyrim), Servyna (Morrowind) and [Heita-Meen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heita-Meen) (Black Marsh) from its capital [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold). Vicecanons were selected based on their strategic and diplomatic skills.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>
