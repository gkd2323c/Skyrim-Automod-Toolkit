## [Ibis](Lore_Ibis.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/ON-furnishing-Ra_Gada_Funerary_Statue%2C_Gilded_Ibis_02.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Ra_Gada_Funerary_Statue%2C_Gilded_Ibis_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Ra_Gada_Funerary_Statue,_Gilded_Ibis_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Ra_Gada_Funerary_Statue,_Gilded_Ibis_02.jpg) An ibis statue An **Ibis** is a [heron](Lore_Heron.md) -like [bird](Lore_Bird.md) with a long, curving beak. In [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) theology, they were considered sacred and oft associated with [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), the God of Souls. His veneration is best illustrated in tombs and burial places, where Tu'whacca is represented in statuary and reliefs as a figure with the head of an ibis and the body of a man. These resting places are also sometimes replete with numerous funerary statues that that depict a winged ibis-lion creature, such as the Al-Danobia tomb in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Tu'whacca was once considered the patron of mages, and some [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) spellcasters tipped their [staves](Lore_Staves.md) with finials in the shape of Tu'whacca's sacred ibis.

- See also: [Curlew](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Curlew)

## [Ice Golem](Lore_Golem.md#Ice)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/b/b8/AR-creature-Ice_Golem.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Ice_Golem.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-creature-Ice_Golem.gif) An ice golem [Ice Golems](Lore_Golem.md) are a typically weak variety of Golem, created out of ice.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> These magically enchanted creatures do not only cause injury with their vicious physcial attacks, but also from the extreme cold aura they generate. They are immune to cold based spells, which do nothing but heal the constructs.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> They naturally have a weakness to fire. Some Ice Golems are known as Ice Atronachs or Water Atronachs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HFAQUEST-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AHing-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ATRMAN-6)</sup> They are not to be confused with the [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md), [Frost Atronachs](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md), which are also sometimes known by these names.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ATRQUEST-7)</sup>

Three powerful ice golems, known as Glacial Guardians,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> were used by anicent [mer](Lore_Mer.md) to keep the [Sea Sload](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) sorceress Z'baza imprisoned in [Coral Aerie](Lore_Coral_Aerie.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AI-10)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Ice_Golem), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Ice_Atronach), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Staff_Guardian)

## [Ice Tribes](Lore_Ice_Tribes.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/SK-creature-Ice_Tribe_02.png/200px-SK-creature-Ice_Tribe_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-creature-Ice_Tribe_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SK-creature-Ice_Tribe_02.png) An Ice Scout The [Ice Tribes](Lore_Ice_Tribes.md) (also referred to as **glacier tribes**) are blue-skinned, tribal humanoids known to be encountered in the northern, frozen regions of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Their physiques are typically quite muscular and some can be seen with two blue spike formations upon their shoulders. They are known to make allies with creatures like [trolls](Lore_Troll.md), flying [rays](Lore_Ray.md), and the fearsome [Gehenoth](Lore_Gehenoth.md). They are also capable of negotiations and making deals with [men](Lore_Man.md).

**Found in:** - [Dawnstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Dawnstar:Ice_Tribes), [Shadowkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shadowkey:Ice_Tribes)

## [Ice Wraith](Lore_Ice_Wraith.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/ON-load-Sorrow_%28crop%29.jpg/200px-ON-load-Sorrow_%28crop%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Sorrow_(crop).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Sorrow_(crop).jpg) An ice wraith The [Ice Wraith](Lore_Ice_Wraith.md) is a magical, lucid serpentine [nature spirit](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) purely made out of ice. They are typically found across [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s snow-blown landscape and mountain ranges but can also be found in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s reclusive mountain country, as well as the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md). They blend in the snow and become nigh invisible to the naked eye. They will then lunge at unsuspecting prey with their entire body and bite them to death. Prey can sometimes get infected with [Witbane](Lore_Witbane.md), a disease that dulls a person's intelligence. Ice Wraiths are among many creatures that are immune to paralysis.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Ice_Wraith.md), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Ice_Wraith), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Ice Wraith), [Skyrim VSE](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim VSE:Enemies#Ice_Wraith), [Skyrim TAG](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Skyrim_-_The_Adventure_Game)

## Iguana
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-furnishing-Stone-Nest_Totem%2C_Iguana.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Stone-Nest_Totem%2C_Iguana.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Stone-Nest_Totem,_Iguana.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Stone-Nest_Totem,_Iguana.jpg) An Argonian depiction of an iguana Iguanas are [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md) and a type of [lizard](Lore_Lizard.md), primarily found on the island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md). The Stone-Nest [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) in particular admire them for their fearlessness and resilience, often carving totems in their image. In ancient times, iguanas were considered sacred animals.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SNTI-11)</sup>

## [Ilyadi](Lore_Ilyadi.md)
[Ilyadi](Lore_Ilyadi.md) (or **Illyadi**) are a type of [fay](Lore_Faerie.md) that once inhabited the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md). Legends describe them as many-eyed forest giants taller than the trees, with eyes that cover their heads. It is unclear if they have any relation to other tree-like spirits.

They were once far more common, and attacked the early [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), but were fought off by [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md)'s use of ancestral lore and [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md) to channel aetherial energy in the [form](Lore_Destruction.md) of [lightning](Lore_Shock.md), ice, and fire to kill them, and they were exterminated in a few short years. They are now supposedly extinct, though some have sought out ways to bring them back from extinction.

**Found in:** - [Lore](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition_Summerset)

## [Imp](Lore_Imp.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/OB-creature-Imp.jpg/200px-OB-creature-Imp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Imp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Imp.jpg) An imp [Imps](Lore_Imp.md), sometimes colloquially known as **dwarflings** or **trickster sprites**, are small, winged humanoids who are slightly intelligent and can cast a variety of [spells](Lore_Magic.md). They are known to be aggressive and mischievous creatures. The origins of the imp are not clearly understood; some sources suggest they may be [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) beings, while others state that they are artificial constructs similar to [golems](Lore_Golem.md). It may be that both theories are correct, or that they are something else entirely. Another claim is that they are [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) or at least natural creatures. Imps are listed among the types of common Daedra that can be found in service to every [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). Imps were also among the first beings summoned from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) during the development of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) magic, further suggesting at least some imps could be Daedra. They are commonly found in the wilderness throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), often gathering in caves. They can also be found in planes such as the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md), or in the company of elemental [atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md).

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Imp), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Imp), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Imp), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Imp), Betrayal of the Second Era

## [Imperfect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperfect)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/TR-creature-Imperfect.jpg/200px-TR-creature-Imperfect.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-creature-Imperfect.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-creature-Imperfect.jpg) An Imperfect circa 3E 427 [The Imperfect](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperfect) is a [factotum](Lore_Factotum.md) variant created by [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) that was designed with advanced combat and excavation features. A new prototype is created and is improved upon each time it is destroyed. Sequence plaques containing its core functions are collected after each combat test. They are subsequently analyzed for their data so further advancements are made in hopes that one day it shall obtain its true form, and fulfill its ultimate goal. Its abilities can vary across iterations, and it can gather data such as combat behavior that may be caused by cultural influences. An example being that at one point, it found [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) to be consistently unpredictable. For melee combat, it can brawl with its large arms at melee range, and use its leg thrusters to create an shockwave when it crashes back down. Ranged combat capabilities include emitting laser blasts via concentrated power, with split pathing, or with tracking abilities. It can also overcharge and create a kinetic shield for safety. The Imperfect requires a huge power input, and thus needs a fully charged [animo core](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Animo_Cores) in order to function.

Imperfects rely on a signal emitted from [AIOS](Lore_AIOS.md), Clockwork City's Automata Incarnum Overseer System. If its range is not detected, it will activate emergency protocols. To collect data for improving upon the Imperfect, AIOS tasked a "Clockwork Facilitator" with convincing adventurers to battle the Imperfect. By battle log 17901, the stats showcased a success rate of 66%, with 126 opponents being killed, 58 forfeiting, and 93 opponents defeating the Imperfect. However, the experiment became controversial, and less people dared to fight it. This led to the facilitator to optimize a strategy, leaving out words like "slaughter," "decapitation," and "likely death" from its job postings, while emphasizing a reward and a need for allies to fight it. Elixir of the Imperfect, extracted from the remains of the automaton, restores one's health, magicka, and stamina.

**Found in:** - [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Tribunal:Imperfect), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Imperfect), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Awakened Imperfect)

## [Incarnate](Lore_Incarnate.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/ON-npc-Valkynaz_Nokvroz_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Valkynaz_Nokvroz_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Valkynaz_Nokvroz_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Valkynaz_Nokvroz_02.jpg) The Perfected Incarnate [Incarnates](Lore_Incarnate.md) are an experimental race of elemental [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) beings created by changing the [animus](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) of a Daedra using a machine known as a [cataclyst](Lore_Cataclyst.md). This experience is alien to the immortal Daedra, whose spirits are generally considered to be unchanging and immutable. The process causes the Daedra to lose all sense of individuality and consciousness, becoming an incarnation of destruction. Each Incarnate is a manifestation of a specific kind of [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) destruction or natural disaster. The Perfected Incarnate was unique in that it embodied both destruction and creation, combining the power of the two forces. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), the Daedric Prince of Change, ordered the construction of numerous cataclysts to create an army of Incarnates, although his efforts were ultimately thwarted with the destruction of these machines and the banishing of the few Incarnates that were successfully created.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Incarnate)

## [Indrik](Lore_Indrik.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/ON-creature-Indrik.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Indrik.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Indrik.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Indrik.jpg) An indrik Indriks are magical creatures that bear some relation to [Jephre](Lore_Jephre.md)<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-YSS-13)</sup>. They can be found throughout the forests of [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) and are often hostile. Indriks can be summoned to Tamriel by combining four legendary feathers. Indriks follow an evolutionary pattern and have four forms. The indrik first enters Tamriel in a pure form, and are referred to as Nascent Indriks. Further exposure to some infused stimulus will change its form and cause it to evolve, developing secondary characteristics and more specialized antlers. The second form is referred to as a Dawnwood Indrik and its antlers grow and develop a green glow. Its plumes also develop a green color.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PLJ-14)</sup>

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Indrik)

## [Infernium](Lore_Infernium.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/ON-creature-Infernium.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Infernium.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Infernium.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Infernium.jpg) Infernium [Inferniums](Lore_Infernium.md) are large beastial [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) that dwarf most known Daedra in both size and hideousness, resembling a great fanged insect, [leech](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leech), or monstrous [caterpillar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caterpillar) with numerous teeth. They are many-legged beasts that chase after their prey, screaming and spewing molten rock. They are also occasionally referred to as **Lavapedes** but it is uncharacteristic of their morphology. They are very intelligent.

Inferniums are huge enough to easily devour beasts as large as [horses](Lore_Horse.md). Although they do not have a preference in what it consumes they typically are obsessed with concepts of upheaval and revolution. Despite their bestial appearance, Inferniums do not pursue these ends in a crude, general sense. They are aware of where political and cultural power rests. High-ranking [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) routinely fall prey to attacks of Inferniums. They are attacked far more often than low-ranking Varlets who typically enjoy fewer defenses. Inferniums also consume powerful items such as grand [soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md), master [lucents](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lucent), and valuable extraplanar relics. They are also known to hoard trinkets such as jewelry and precious gems but also collect armor and magical items.

They can be found in [the Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), typically wherever there is anything or anyone of potential, prestige, or authority. They can also be encountered in the plane of [Deadlight](Lore_Deadlight.md). Inferniums are typically aligned with [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), though some could be found in the [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). Occasionally Inferniums are accompanied by [Havocrels](Lore_Havocrel.md). According to some sources Inferniums need that assistance. It was theorized that some Havocrels are attempting to train Inferniums. Certain Inferniums are noted to reform far more frequently than any other routinely killed Daedra. In some cases, the process of reformation can take only a few days. Although the time of reformation is short, it is inconsistent and varies between the specimen.

According to the records of the Scribes of Mora Inferniums managed to take down one of the [Xivilai](Lore_Xivilai.md) clans back in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Infernium)

## [Iron Atronach](Lore_Iron_Atronach.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/LG-cardart-Iron_Atronach.png/200px-LG-cardart-Iron_Atronach.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Iron_Atronach.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Iron_Atronach.png) An Iron Atronach [Iron Atronachs](Lore_Iron_Atronach.md) (sometimes called **Earth Atronachs**) are a type of [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md). They are towering behemoths composed of solid and molten [iron](Lore_Iron.md) and usually take a roughly humanoid form. Iron Atronachs come from an unnamed [volcanic realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Realm_of_the_Iron_Atronachs) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), an impossibly dense molten hellscape that produces them in abundance. Iron Atronach [names](Lore_Names.md#Iron_Atronach) usually appear with the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) clan name "Mynar".

Iron Atronachs have a relentless appetite for destruction and are often found in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), the volcanic realm of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), the Prince of Destruction. They attack using their brute strength and the molten heat of their bodies. Some Iron Atronachs possess a magical aura that damages anyone who dares to get too close . Electricity-based spells do not damage Iron Atronachs, but heal them instead. They can be harmed by any type of weapon.

Few mortal [conjurers](Lore_Conjuration.md) dare to summon Iron Atronachs to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), despite them making powerful servants. Those that do sometimes end up dead. Some mages, not satisfied with simply summoning Iron Atronachs, weave powerful [Illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) spells to assume the shape of an Iron Atronach. The [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) were known to use Iron Atronach-styled weapons and armor.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Iron_Atronach), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Iron_Atronach), [Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Iron Atronach)
