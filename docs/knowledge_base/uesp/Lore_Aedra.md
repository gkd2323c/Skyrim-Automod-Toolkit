# Aedra

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) An eagle sigil, used to symbolize the Aedra in some cultures The **Aedra** (also called the **Dead Gods** or **The Mortal Gods** or **The Outer Gods**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vastarie-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Prophet-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Gasta-3)</sup> are [original beings](Lore_Gods.md) generally believed to have arisen from the interplay of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md). They are regarded as counterparts and in a manner opposites of the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), though the distinction between the two can be confusing to the layman and the terms Aedra and Daedra, gods and demons, are often used interchangeably. The terms "Aedra" and "Daedra" are not relative, they are exact [elvish titles](Lore_Mer.md) which translate to "ancestors" and "not our ancestors" respectively, representing the mythical genealogy of the elves as they perceive it. The divide between Aedra and Daedra is commonly thought to have originated *after* their birth, during the creation of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), where most cultures maintain the Aedric beings played a leading role in the creation of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), causing them to commonly be considered the creators of the mortal world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAA-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cot R-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WOCMA-8)</sup> Aedra translated into Aldmeris means "ancestors".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AD-9)</sup> The proper singular form is "Aedroth",<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-10)</sup> but the Aedra are almost never referred to in singular.

In "common parlance" the term "Aedra" is generally reserved for the [eight great spirits](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) that followed [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md) and created the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). However, according to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), this is folly and a mistake, as some of the creator spirits were not even among the greatest of the [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) aligned [et'Ada](Lore_Gods.md) originally, but only gained that standing through making the world, their "creation of the Dawn". Thus the term Aedra is more accurately applicable more broadly and beyond the Eight.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TTGt C-11)</sup>

## Mythology

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/MER-art-Queen_of_Voice.jpg/200px-MER-art-Queen_of_Voice.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Queen_of_Voice.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-Queen_of_Voice.jpg) Kynareth, said to be the greatest of the Sky Spirits and the first to agree to the creation of Mundus [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/MER-art-The_World.jpg/200px-MER-art-The_World.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_World.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_World.jpg) Akatosh coiled around Nirn The exact nature of the Aedra and of their role in the creation of Nirn and the mortal life that inhabits it are matters shrouded in much ambiguity, different cultures provide different accounts of the circumstances that lead to the creation of the mortal world and of the role the Aedra played in it.

### Altmer

According to the creation myth of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), "The Heart of the World", creation begins before the start of the Dawn Era and the beginning of time: the primordial force of Anu the Everything, who encompassed and encompasses all things, created Anuiel, the soul of all things, so it could know itself. Anuiel in turn created [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) for the same purpose, as the sum up of all limitations which it would use to differentiate between its attributes and ponder itself, and their interrelation created the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md), where the Original Spirits, the [Et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md), emerged before the creation of the Mundus as "aspects of [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md)". The Aurbis, at first turbulent and chaotic, was stabilized by the emergence of [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), the soul of Anui-El who spread through existence as the force called time, allowing the Original Spirits to take on names and identities. One of these spirits was more of a limit than a nature, called [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), he convinced the other spirits to help create a "soul" for Aurbis, a place where even the aspects of aspects might self reflect, but this was a deception. The new world, Nirn, was a place composed of more limitations than not and the spirits that participated in its construction began to die and many vanished completely, prompting the architect, Magnus, to terminate the project and depart. When Magnus departed the Mundus, the et'Ada that took part in its creation broke into groups, most would follow the flight of the God of Magic to become the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), but some of those present chose instead to stay in the new world and keep working on it so it wouldn't die, laying, the foundations of both the system and laws of Mundus and the mortal life that would emerge. Of these remaining spirits some would follow the example of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law, and would typically be referred to from that point on as "Earthbones" or "Earth Bones". Others would choose not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, having to "make children just to last", this group of spirits would from that point typically be referred to as the "[Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md)".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-MKP-12)</sup> The offspring of the Ehlnofey would exhibit a gradual change however, each consecutive generation was weaker than the one prior, more removed from their progenitors in stature and might. Through this generational phenomenon of diminishment the offspring of the Ehlnofey would also differentiate, the Ehlnofey would eventually give away and the first [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) would arise, while the "weakest souls" that resulted from it would be formed by Lorkhan into armies he named "Men". Altmeri myth holds that Auri-El and Lorkhan and their respective followers would than war with one another, leading to the shattering of [Altmora](Lore_Atmora.md), the "Elder Wood" and one of the first kingdoms established by Auri-El alongside Old Ehlnofey, at Lorkhan's hands, and culminating in the removal of [Lorkhan's Heart](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) following his defeat. During this conflict and prior to Lorkhan's defeat, Auri-El begged Anu to take them back but Anu refused as he'd already created something to replace them. The more merciful [Anui-El](Lore_Anui-El.md) created [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) and [Auriel's Shield](Lore_Auriel%27s_Shield.md) to be used by Auri-El against Lorkhan's hordes. Following the establishment of the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) was held, Lorkhan was judged, the laws and roles of spirits within the Mundus were defined, and the Aedra elected to make their exit from mortal affairs due to the danger their continued presence posed to the mortal world, which was rendered highly unstable, and even the timeless continuity of existence. With the departure of [magic](Lore_Magic.md) in the mythic sense, linear history could finally begin.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TMo A-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-NMI-15)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AU4TET-16)</sup>

### Aldmeri and Psijic

According to "The Psijic Compensation", the basics of Aldmeri belief as explained to [Uriel V](Lore_Uriel_V.md) by the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md): Aurbis existed for time without measure as the Gray Center between the forces of Anu and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md). Within this Gray Center emerged the magical beings of mythic Aurbis, bits of the immortal polarity given life. The first of these was [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), whose existence made it easier for other spirits to structure themselves. For a long time these spirits lived, formed, reformed, and procreated. Eventually, with Lorkhan as the instigator of the decision, the spirits told the story of their own deaths. This process has been described in various terms, as a transfiguration into the concrete non-magical substance of the world, as a war in which all were slain with their bodies becoming the substance of the world, or as a romantic marriage and parenthood where the parent spirits naturally had to die to give way to the succeeding mortal races. Mortals were shaped in the image of the spirits, either consciously molded by them, or sprung forth from the matter left behind by the dead spirits. Having died, these spirits became the et'Ada, and separated themselves in nature from the rest of the magical beings of Aurbis from that point on.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup> The Psijics believe, therefore, that the Aedra and Daedra are the spirits of "superior men and women whose power and passion granted them great influence in the afterworld", and were themselves bewildered by their own ancestors, going back to the original progenitors, the [Acharyai](Lore_Acharyai.md), while alive.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TOW-17)</sup>

### Cyrodiilic

According to the [Cyrodiilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) creation myth, "Shezzarr's Song": the world was formed when Shezzarr convinced a number of the gods, the Aedra, of the beauty of the concept of becoming mothers and fathers. The Aedra, determined to proceed regardless of cost, gave birth to the world and the life within it by sacrificing part of themselves, a painful process after which they were no longer strong and young as they'd been since the beginning. Some of the Aedra, the gods of the elves led by Auri-El, came to regret their choice and sought revenge upon Shezzarr and his allies, resolving to teach their children, the elves, to endure the suffering of the new world with dignity. Others, the gods of Men led by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), were proud, deeming the new world and the life within it glorious despite their sacrifices, and resolving to teach their children to cherish the concepts of beauty and honor and to love one another.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup>

### Yokudan

According to the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) creation myth, "Satakal the Worldskin": all things originated from Satak, the First Serpent on whose scales all worlds to come rested. Compelled by its Hungry Stomach, Akel, Satak started a never-ending cycle of devouring itself and shedding its skin to be reborn and begin anew, becoming [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md). When things from within the devoured worlds realized the truth of the cycle they began to take names and so the first spirits came to be. These spirits sought to escape Satakal's never-ending hunger and found a way to slide between its Worldskins by moving at strange angles, this was called the Walkabout and through it was born a sanctuary from the cycle called the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md). One of these spirits, [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), sired many children and was so tall he placed the [stars](Lore_Stars.md) in the sky to help other spirits escape as well. The mortal world was formed when Sep, created by Ruptga out of previous Worldskins to be his helper, convinced some of the other spirits to stop performing the Walkabout, as they could escape the cycle by inhabiting a new world made of balled up Worldskins instead. This, however, was a trick, as having been formed of its skin Sep too carried much of Satakal's hunger, and wished to devour the spirits who followed him. Too far from the Far Shores to jump back to, and too far from the real world of Satakal to survive, the deceived spirits began to die but were survived by the children they'd made.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup>

### Khajiit

According to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) creation myth: The gods were born as siblings to the three litters of Ahnurr and Fadomai. Spirits commonly considered Aedra were generally born to the first litter, and spirits commonly considered Daedra were generally born to the second. The goddess Nirni was born to the third litter, alongside the moons, [Azurah](Lore_Azura.md) and, eventually, [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md). Nirni wished to give birth to children, but had no place where she could do so, so she went to Lorkhaj for help. Lorkhaj made a new place but, as his heart was filled with the [Great Darkness](Lore_Void.md) of [Namiira](Lore_Namira.md) due to the circumstances of his birth within the Great Darkness, he tricked a number of his siblings into entering this place with Nirni, where many of them had to die to make Nirni's path stable. Furious at the betrayal, the surviving siblings tore out the [Heart of Lorkhaj](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WOCMA-8)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-So Ad-18)</sup> Pre- [ri'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) myths also mention that Nirni eventually died when [Y'ffer](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) was also corrupted by the Great Darkness and killed her. Y'ffer himself was later slain by Azurah, [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md) and [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), and his bones were used to construct a cairn for Nirni.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-So Ad-18)</sup>

### Reachmen

According to the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md): Nirn was created by [Lorkh](Lore_Lorkh.md), who desired to create not a vibrant paradise but a teaching tool a place that taught through suffering. To create this place Lorkh came to Namira, queen of the infinite realm of spirit, who granted him a place in her endless void to make his new world. As part of his covenant with Namira Lorkh had to make a great [sacrifice](Lore_Dark_Heart.md) of his own, a sacrifice which is said to be reflected in the creation of the [Briarhearts](Lore_Briarheart.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-GSot R-19)</sup>

### Clockwork Apostles

According to the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md): Nirn was created by the "et'Ada Gears", aspects of Anu as is everything else, who were tricked by Lorkhan into believing the Great Lie, that they were separate from the all encompassing singularity that is Anu, and took names of their own to reinforce this illusion. In this belief the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) are nothing more than illusion themselves, a consequence of the flaws of the creation of the et'Ada' Gears creation, the result of the Void taking root within the cracks caused by said flaws.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TTi S-20)</sup>

### Argonian

According to the transcribed creation myth of the Adzi-Kostleel [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) tribe, [Atak](Lore_Anu.md) the Great Root and [Kota](Lore_Padomay.md) the Serpent fought each other for so long that they eventually forgot their fight and became one, forming the entity Atakota. Atakota severed its roots and shed its skin and said the word "[Maybe](Lore_Aurbis.md)", giving rise to its Shadow, formed of its hunger and shed skin. Atakota continued to roil in a cycle, devouring itself and shedding its skin to be reborn and begin anew, each scale a world that it devoured. From this process the world and spirits arose, with Atak and Kota not in conflict, things now had time to begin and end, and the Shadow too fell asleep. As Atakota slumbered, the spirits started making new things that shared in their aspect and loved them, and they started growing, until they too were as large as Atakota, and forgot it had come before them and had a sleeping Shadow. Soon the worlds and spirits became too big and there was no more room for new things, in desperation, the spirits fell upon the sleeping Atakota and bit into it to drink its blood. Eventually the peace of Atakota broke, Atak remembered growing and Kota remembered being nothing, and existence fell into chaos again. In that time of chaos some spirits drank deeply of Atakota's blood and sap, and they [grew scales and fangs and wings](Lore_Dragons.md), forgetting why they'd ever made anything other than to eat it. Kota's blood made new oceans and Atak's sap made new stones. Eventually the roots woke the shadow and asked for its help, and its intervention put an end to the chaos that was threatening to consume everything.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Cot R-7)</sup>

### Other Beliefs

Though most creation myths credit the Aedra with contributing to the creation of Mundus in some manner, there are exceptions where the formation of the mortal world is described as the work of other forces.

According to the Anuad: all things began with the brothers Anu and Padomay. Their interplay created Nir, who alongside Anu gave birth to the Twelve Worlds of Creation. Jealous, Padomay attacked, but was cast out of time by Anu. Life emerged on the Twelve Worlds but, eventually, a hateful Padomay returned and shattered the worlds with his blade. After Anu defeated his brother, he attempted to save what he could by combining the shattered fragments of the Twelve Worlds into a new world, Nirn. But after he did so, Padomay rose again and the two brothers finally pulled each other out of time forever. On Nirn life was seeded by the survivors of the Twelve Worlds who'd arrived in the new world alongside fragments of their original worlds, the Ehlnofey and the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), and during this time the gods and demons of the world, the Aedra, Daedra and Magna Ge also formed out of Anu and Padomay's spilled blood, as distinct groups from the life of Nirn and from each other from the start.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TAA-4)</sup>

According to the [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) tale "The Light and the Dark": two immortal entities representing Order and Chaos chose [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to be their eternal battleground. This everlasting battle would create energies so powerful it distorted the world and created life, including the "people of [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md)", who would in turn give rise to the gods, and their "daedric enemies", by believing in their myths for so long and so strongly, it caused the energies unleashed by the conflict of the Light and the Dark to bring them into being. According to the grandfather, all of creation exists to echo the battle between the Light and the Dark.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-t Lat D-21)</sup>

According to the beliefs of the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md), the mortal world was actually the [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) [plane](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of Lorkhan, who was actually a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md). Per this belief, the Aedra, the gods mortals generally worship, were actually Lorkhan's betrayers, who stole the realm from its true deity and intentionally split Lorkhan's progeny from their divine sparks, so that they themselves would be viewed as the sole exit from the current world.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Camoran-22)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MDC-23)</sup>

## Characteristics

*It is a bit like being at once awake and asleep. Awake, I am here with you, thinking and talking. Asleep, I am very, very busy. Perhaps for other gods, the completely immortal ones, it is only like that being asleep. Out of time.*
— [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s musings on divinity<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MWVehk-24)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/16/CT-decoration-Mythological_Painting_%28Cropped%29.png/200px-CT-decoration-Mythological_Painting_%28Cropped%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Mythological_Painting_(Cropped).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Mythological_Painting_(Cropped).png) A Painting of Three Divines at the Gates of Aetherius Owing to their alleged participation in the creation of Mundus, the Aedra are generally thought to have somehow separated themselves in nature from other spirits of mythic Aurbis. The exact manner in which this process is described differs by source but, generally, the Aedra are thought to be bound to the Earthbones, the rules they themselves established to govern Mundus, and to represent stasis, where the Daedra represent change. In addition, as beings bound by the contract of creation, the Aedra are thought to have become vulnerable to death and thus to being killed as well, whereas the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), to whom such rules do not apply, can only be banished.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AD-9)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup>

Owing to them becoming bound to the laws of mortality, the Aedra are sometimes called the "dead gods" or the "mortal gods", sometimes disparagingly.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vastarie-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Prophet-2)</sup> They are generally thought to currently reside in [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Serana-25)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GU-26)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOVairabrian-27)</sup> Though some sources present a more negative view of their circumstances, naming Mundus as the Aedra's "cemetery", calling them "spent ghosts", alleging them to have died as part of the creation of the world, having "died in pursuit of an impossible goal", or to have burial crypts.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Lyranth2-28)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GU-26)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ARLords Mail-29)</sup> Though the Aedra are thought to have endured a considerable cost as part of the creation of the world, they are also generally thought to be capable of creation, of making new things, whereas the Daedra can only change and mimic what's already there.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AD-9)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-IPM-30)</sup>

Some sources maintain that, prior to Convention, the Aedra held "every power at every time amendment at every ordering", both the world and its "architect-gods" were "young" and, as a result, though mighty, they couldn't fully manifest themselves within the world. Thus the limits were imposed at Convention.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-NMI-15)</sup>

According to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) some of the Aedra were not even among the greatest of the Aetherius-aligned et'Ada originally, but only became such through making the world, their "creation of the Dawn", suggesting creation might've potentially aided the Aedra in some manner in opposition to the common view.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TTGt C-11)</sup>

The sun is said to represent the connection between Aetherius and the Mundus, and to be the conduit through which the influence of certain Aedra such as [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) reaches the mortal world.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-DGVyrthur-31)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Serana-25)</sup>

## Legacy

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dd/MER-art-The_Hierophant.jpg/200px-MER-art-The_Hierophant.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_Hierophant.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_Hierophant.jpg) Julianos, creator of one of the Crusader's Relics [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/LG-cardart-Avatar_of_Akatosh_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Avatar_of_Akatosh_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Avatar_of_Akatosh_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Avatar_of_Akatosh_02.jpg) The Avatar of Akatosh manifesting to end the Oblivion Crisis (Legends) Though for the most part not directly active within the mortal world in a personal sense, the legacy of the Aedra is still occasionally felt both through the marks of their passing on the mortal world, and through the rare direct interventions of those spirits in mortal affairs.

In addition, a number of events have prompted the seeming direct intervention of Aedric spirits.

The [Liminal Barriers](Lore_Liminal_Barriers.md) separating Mundus from Oblivion are the creation of Akatosh through the Sublime Brazier, a sacred cauldron described as Akatosh's first light, which stretches to the very heart of Nirn. During events such as the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) and the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), when the barrier was sundered and the mortal world was threatened, Akatosh's power played a crucial role in ending both Daedric incursions and restoring the boundaries between worlds.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Lt D-32)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Do B-33)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-To SA-34)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TSB-35)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Tharn-36)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Martin-37)</sup>

A number of interventions of lesser scale also occurred during [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). On one occasion Kyne intervened and reverted the transformation of Keeper Ormi into a hagraven, after her son Sahun revealed himself to still be alive and prayed for her.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ESOFKH-38)</sup> On another, the Bosmer [Indaenir](Lore_Indaenir.md) was revived by Y'ffre as the new [Silvenar](Lore_Silvenar.md), after sacrificing himself to cleanse the Heart of Valenwood.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Sat H-39)</sup> During that same year, [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) was brought back to life by the alleged intervention of Alkosh in the event that he was sacrificed in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), as the deity had foreseen the need of his presence to combat the threat of [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md) and [Laatvulon](Lore_Laatvulon.md) due to his ability to see across all the Many Paths of time.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-40)</sup> At that time, Mara also intervened to enable the departed to return as ghosts to share final moments with their loved ones and guide them beyond the mortal realm. This intervention extended even to situations involving individuals from different races with distinct beliefs about the afterlife. Notably, Mara facilitated the reunion of Nuralanya, a [Corelanya](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), and an [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) named Gurlak in the afterlife.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Nuralanya-41)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-TOO-42)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), a number of alleged avatars of the Divines appeared to the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Lalatia-43)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md) returned to Tamriel, seeking revenge on the gods who'd aided in his downfall. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), sought out the [Crusader's Relics](Lore_Crusader%27s_Relics.md) at the behest of the prophet of Anvil, they were granted audience with [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) by the gods, and retrieved the artifacts after passing the trials of each Divine.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Oblivion Pel-44)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Knights-45)</sup> After Umaril was vanquished in the spirit realm through the Blessing Of Talos, the Hero of Kvatch was returned to life by the Divines for their deed.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-Thedret-46)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Mara would intervene again in a similar manner to reunite the spirits of loved ones, even communicating with a priestess of hers and providing information for this to become possible.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Dinya-47)</sup> Other deities, Arkay and [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), also allegedly communicated information to chosen mortals during that time.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-Florentius-48)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-SRHamal-49)</sup>

Other events throughout time have been attributed to intervention by the Aedra.

It is said that, during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Alkosh manifested at [Sunspire](Lore_Sunspire.md) to put an end to [Pelinal Whitestrake's](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) slaughter that threatened to end the Khajiit. As the legend goes, Alkosh fell from the heavens as a shooting [star](Lore_Stars.md) and shouted Pelinal out of Elsweyr with a roar that split the sky, resulting in a [Time Wound](Lore_Time_Wound.md) at the summit of the mountain, where it has remained since.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-50)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg/200px-CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-ruling-Eclipse_02.jpg) An eclipse, a phenomenon sometimes associated with losing the favor of Akatosh (Castles) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/CT-Events-Rite_of_Akatosh_%28Large%29.png/200px-CT-Events-Rite_of_Akatosh_%28Large%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-Events-Rite_of_Akatosh_(Large).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-Events-Rite_of_Akatosh_(Large).png) A warrior facing an Avatar of Akatosh during the Old Life Festival (Castles) The Rite of Akatosh is an endeavor held by Cyrodiilic elders during the [Old Life Festival](Lore_Old_Life_Festival.md) in preparation for the [New Life Festival](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md), in which their party goes on a pilgrimage to rid the nearby forests of "Old Life", monsters like [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), [Ogres](Lore_Ogre.md), and [trolls](Lore_Troll.md). The party then challenges the manifested Avatar of Akatosh. By successfully defeating this avatar, the elders hope the God of Time will grant them youth or more time, to be at peace, or that the feat will grant them respect.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Rite-51)</sup> The [phenomenon](Lore_Eclipse.md) of [Solar](Lore_Sun.md) [eclipses](Lore_Astronomy.md) is sometimes thought to represent the loss of the favor of Akatosh, and to be possible to undo with diligent prayer to that deity.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-CTEclipse-52)</sup>

Y'ffre is known to send [wisps](Lore_Wisp.md) to herald the storms of Rain's Hand. To see one is to see the promise of new growth, new life, and a new chapter in nature's cycle.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Galen_Wisp-53)</sup> As part of a covenant with Tu'whacca, three [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warriors bound their souls to their blades to create the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md) - magical relics that prevented even the most powerful [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) from raising their dead.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONTKF-54)</sup> Mara is thought to have blessed the village of [Garridan Stalrous](Lore_Garridan_Stalrous.md), which was suffering from a drought, with rainfall, to honor his sacrifice.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-55)</sup>

Minor Aedric spirits exist, but they are rarely encountered since Magnus withdrew from the world at the moment of creation. This is said to be because interfering with Mundus is generally considered to be off-limits for them since the end of the ages of myth and, as a result, they tend to no longer respond to mortals directly.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-POE-56)</sup> According to some theories, the [Luminaries](Lore_Luminary.md), immortal beings of pure magic, are thought to be creations of the Aedra sent to guide and protect mortals, and the Aedra's children who potentially herald their return, being to Aetherius what non-greater Daedra are to Oblivion.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-LAS-57)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-58)</sup>

## Realms

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/59/ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg/200px-ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Spilling_Sands.jpg) The Spilled Sand, a realm associated with Alkosh [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Summerset_Orrery.jpg) The planets as depicted in a Summerset orrery According to some accounts, the mortal realms were formed by the Aedra out of [Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md).<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Co S-59)</sup> The astral bodies in the sky above Nirn, the moons and [dominion planets](Lore_Planets.md), are believed to actually be the planes of the gods as well as the gods themselves, as seen from the mortal plane.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Cosmology-60)</sup> They are said to actually be different planes of existence in their own right, infinite in size and mass, with their appearance as spheres being only a visual phenomena caused by mortal mental stress.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-Cosmology-60)</sup>

In addition to the planets, a number of other realms have been associated with the Aedra as their domains. [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md) was created and is ruled by [Shor](Lore_Shor.md), and the god [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md) is said to be the caretaker of the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md).<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-SRTsun-61)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-TP-62)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Rft D-63)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup>

Additional [realms of existence](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) have been associated with the Time God.

The [Spilled Sand](Lore_Spilled_Sand.md) is a strange plane of existence that is associated with Alkosh. It consists of an endless expanse of sand interspersed with an oasis of golden trees, and a colossal slumbering golden dragon with scales of pure gold. The twin moons, Jode and Jone, can be seen in the sky alongside a series of stars. The realm is described as not a place but rather a "story that has and will be told time and time again" and "myth made manifest", it is said to exist at once within and outside the tapestry of time, and beyond even the Sands Behind the Stars.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Nahfahlaar-64)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-40)</sup> When questioned on whether the Spilled Sand is the realm of Alkosh itself, Ja'darri would not outright confirm it, answering instead that Alkosh is both weaver and embodiment of time's tapestry and all always exist within time's realm regardless of where they are.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-40)</sup> The Mask of Alkosh allows for one to journey to this realm when awoken via the blood of a dragon.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Dragonguard-65)</sup> Some sources claim that Akatosh, Nirn and Oblivion are one, a fact which can be proven through mathematical equations.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TFSo B-66)</sup> The "myriad kingdoms of Akha along the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md)", the realities beyond Aurbis are thought to be ruled over and safeguarded by Alkosh, who inherited the crown of Akha.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TWS-67)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Leramil-68)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Ithelia-69)</sup> Some sources state that Akatosh created the [Beginning Place](Lore_Beginning_Place.md), rather than being the first spirit born from it.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-EGTH-70)</sup>

Though it is acknowledged as a paradox, it is said the Mundus both encompasses and is encompassed by the innumerable planes of Oblivion.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Artorius-71)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Wing_of_the_Indrik.jpg) The Wing of the Indrik According to some Daedric accounts, Mundus is defined by a degree of malleability which generally doesn't exist within the realms of Oblivion. Whereas Oblivion realms are fundamentally shaped and curated by the will of their creators, Mundus and its constituent realms submit simultaneously to the ordered influence of the Aedra's remains and the stubborn caprice of Lorkhan. Thus Mundus is "doughy", things within it can be destroyed, but they never remain gone and instances of them inevitably spring up again.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Lyranth2-28)</sup> Some sources claim that the reason Nirn is filled with natural caverns is that in primordial times they formed a network that functioned like nodes or capillaries for the divine essence of the Aedra that created it.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Wind-72)</sup>

The Luminaries, which some sources theorize are children or creations of the Aedra, sent to herald their return and guide mortals,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-LAS-57)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ONUNOL-58)</sup> have their own realms, such as the [Wing of the Indrik](Lore_Wing_of_the_Indrik.md), [Wing of the Dragon](Lore_Wing_of_the_Dragon.md), [Wing of the Gryphon](Lore_Wing_of_the_Gryphon.md), and [Wing of the Netch](Lore_Wing_of_the_Netch.md), that they formed at their birth.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TI-73)</sup>

## Cultural Significance

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/92/ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Summerset_Isle_Map.jpg) A fresco of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) with four of the Altmeri Divines (Xarxes, Auri-El, Mara, Jephre) pictured [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/SR-interior-Temple_of_the_Divines.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Temple_of_the_Divines.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Temple_of_the_Divines.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Temple_of_the_Divines.jpg) The Temple of the Divines in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) The Aedra generally feature in some manner in the pantheons and religious belief of the races that inhabit Tamriel. As with the various creation myths, the exact perception of each deity in a system of belief varies by culture. Though the Aedra occupy an important role in Tamriel's religious beliefs, they are not the sole objects of worship, and many other saints and holy spirits are known to have their own following.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup>

Owing to the influence of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), eight of the Aedra came to be worshipped as the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), namely [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md), [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), [Mara](Lore_Mara.md), [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), and [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md). With the addition of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), the human god-hero [Tiber Septim](Lore_Talos.md), they became the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md). These deities became the central figures of the major religious cult of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and its provinces.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup> According to some [Cyrodiilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) folktales the Aedra are kind beings that came from an [infinite place](Lore_Aetherius.md) but intentionally limited themselves and became the world for the sake of mortals. Thus they are everything mortals can see, touch and feel, the world itself. Of the Aedra it is said that Dibella is the most kind.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-LOSouls-74)</sup>

Of the Aedra, [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), the Dragon God and the Missing God, are the two who are thought to appear in some capacity and form in all of Tamriel's major religions. The Mother-Goddess [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) is also considered a nearly universal deity in Tamrielic belief, sometimes associated with the female principle of creation, [Nir](Lore_Nir.md), and with either Akatosh, Lorkhan, or both.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup>

Some of the Aedra feature prominently in a number of Tamrielic religions, though their exact origins, actions, nature, and even names, can differ per belief. These are:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg/200px-ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) The Adamantine Tower, thought to define and enforce the rules the Aedra set in place for Mundus [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) is generally thought of as the first god to form at the [Beginning Place](Lore_Beginning_Place.md), though some sources claim he created the Beginning Place rather than being the first spirit born from it instead, he is credited as the origin of time, and it is said that his "perch from eternity allowed the day". The formation of the Dragon God is thought to have made it easier for other forces to crystallize. Akatosh is considered the most active of the Aedra within the lives of mortals, as his presence is felt in the form of time, in each moment that passes. One [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) myth claims that the "great Dragon of Time" set the [stars](Lore_Stars.md) in their courses and appointed the [guardian constellations](Lore_Astronomy.md#Constellations) to watch over the world.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-TOAATO-75)</sup> Auri-El, the counterpart of Akatosh within elven belief, is considered the ancestor from whom modern Altmer and Bosmer generally claim direct descent.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-EGTH-70)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Artorius-71)</sup> The laws that govern Nirn and Mundus and the roles of all spirits within them, are believed to have been set in place at and through the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) by its creators. The Tower itself being the seat of Akatosh from where he ruled prior to Convention and the framework set in place itself being described as "the Dragon's timebound tale".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AU4TET-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-NMI-15)</sup> This role of the Time God in the order of the world is especially emphasized within Khajiit belief where his counterpart, Alkosh, is said to be at once the weaver of the tapestry of time and the incarnation of the tapestry itself, the threads that comprise it unspooling from the tip of his tail, which would lead to nothing remaining should the thread ever end, as all exists within time's domain.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-40)</sup> To the Khajiit, Alkosh is responsible for preserving the stability of the world, he is said to maintain the steady movement of the moons, [Jode](Lore_Masser.md) and [Jone](Lore_Secunda.md), it is believed that without him they would freeze in place, allowing terrible things to slip through the Lunar Lattice.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOAzin-50)</sup> It is said that all time flows from Alkosh and that, without him, the world would either sit lifeless or churn like a boiling pot. Because of this, staving off both total chaos and stagnation, his role is considered the most important among all the gods.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Natrada-76)</sup> It is said that Alkosh watches over time's tapestry, whenever a "snag" appears to threaten it, Alkosh realigns the threads with his claws and the aid of the Pride of Alkosh.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-TPo A-77)</sup> In Khajiit belief, the Time God's influence is even thought to extend in some manner beyond [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) itself, with [Akha](Lore_Akha.md) having created the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), the realities that exist beyond and surrounding Aurbis, and Alkosh having inherited Akha's crown, role, and myriad kingdoms along the Paths.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TWS-67)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Leramil-68)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Ithelia-69)</sup> [Nine Coruscations](Lore_The_Nine_Coruscations.md), a religious text by [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) worshippers of [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), describes the Time God's insanity as "all that is and could be", and the layering of linear time upon [endless possibility](Lore_Many_Paths.md) beyond [cosmic interplay](Lore_Aurbis.md) caused by his affixing of [Ada-Mantia](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md). The text also outlines Ithelia's goal of cleaving through that restriction and breaking the shackles of [fate](Lore_Fate.md), a state known as "Numancia".<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TNC-78)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Ithelia-69)</sup>

[Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md), is generally thought of as the agent of the communal decision for the creation of Mundus, an act believed to have upset the cosmic balance. Following this act Lorkhan is separated from his [divine center](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md), sometimes involuntarily and sometimes willingly. Lorkhan is credited with introducing instability into the cosmos at the Beginning Place, reflecting his Padomaic origins, his actions are central to the Human/Aldmeri schism and have caused him to both be vilified and be viewed sympathetically as the being without whom the mortal world and mortals could not exist, depending on the culture. Because of the controversial nature of his role, the Missing God was given a secondary role in the religious life of Cyrodiil as part of a political compromise.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Sat D-79)</sup> The cultural counterpart of Lorkhan within the faith of the Khajiit, is described as having a second [dark heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md), representing his link to the [Void](Lore_Void.md), and through it being indirectly responsible for the birth of [Noctra](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-TDS-80)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-TFDOF-81)</sup>

[Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md), believed by some sources to be the architect of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). Following creation, he is thought to have abandoned the project and returned to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). In doing so, he tore a hole to Oblivion, which became the [sun](Lore_Sun.md) and allowed magic to come to Mundus. Most of the other et'Ada who'd initially participated in the project followed him forming the [stars](Lore_Stars.md) and becoming known as the Magna Ge. For this Magnus is referred as the God of Magic, and is considered one of the Aedra. Despite this, within the Empire, his veneration is sometimes not as pronounced as that of the Divines, as his early departure is viewed in a negative manner, as him refusing to give for the world as much as the other spirits did, and his introduction of magicka is viewed as a dubious contribution that does as much ill as good.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Artorius-71)</sup> According to some sources Magnus foresaw that the mortal realm would turn out flawed and it was these flaws that made him turn away, he warned the other gods that their current course should be stopped but they chose to proceed anyway, creating the world's suffering. Since than, Magnus watches from the sun, waiting to return when the time is right and the current flawed world is unmade, to make a new world without the flaws of the first in its place. In this capacity, Magnus is thought of by these sources as the indirect origin of the goals of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), who is believed to have been created by the Magna Ge as a weapon to unmake the current world so Magnus can return to reforge it.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-DCT-82)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Celdina-83)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Camoran-22)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MDC-23)</sup> In some beliefs the relationship of Magnus and the Magna Ge to the sun and stars is changed, with the sun instead being described as one of the [eyes](Lore_Eye_of_Magnus.md) of Magnus and the stars being described as fragments of his.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-TIC-84)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-TSS-85)</sup>

[Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) is a central deity of the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md). After Magnus and the Magna Ge abandoned Mundus and departed, the spirits who then remained to keep working on Mundus so it'd survive, are said to have either followed Y'ffre's example and given themselves fully to become the stabilizing elements of the new world, its framework of natural laws, becoming known as the "Earthbones". Or "made children", becoming the progenitors of life. These progenitor spirits would come to be known as "Ehlnofey" providing the basis for the meaning of the term "Aedra" or "ancestors.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BTAOM-6)</sup> The greatest among those spirits who gave themselves fully to form the laws of nature is Y'ffre. Altmer and Bosmer myth describes how during the Dawn Era, both all life, and even the land itself, were formless and constantly shifted between shapes, until Y'ffre's Naming gave all things in Nirn an enduring form. This event, the establishment of shape, is considered a core element of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md). In certain circumstances such as the enactment of a [Wild Hunt](Lore_Wild_Hunt.md), which is enacted in accordance with what the Pact allows, creatures can be made to "forget their Y'ffre taught shapes", causing the bonds of the Naming to unravel, and return to this original formless state.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-TOAF-86)</sup><sup>[[82]](#cite_note-BLta A-87)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-ONWild Hunt Horse-88)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-ONWild Hunt Crate-89)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup> Within Bosmer belief Y'ffre is believed to have created day, night, and the places between through the manner in which he interpreted the laws of time established by [Anui-El](Lore_Anui-El.md) and applied them to Nirn, he is also believed to be responsible for the movements of the stars, as to this day the realms they're windows to sway and wink to his song.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-GESOD-90)</sup> [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith also acknowledges Y'ffre in this capacity of being the deity responsible for the movements of the stars.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-OJ-91)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-WCo NHR-92)</sup> Y'ffre and the Earthbones play a central role in the beliefs of both [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) and [Wyrd](Lore_Wyrd.md).<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Wa D-93)</sup> The Druids commune with the voices of Y'ffre and the Earthbones for guidance and tap into their power through their magic.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Audrine-94)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Lot B-95)</sup> The Wyresses believe that during Y'ffre's Naming, the act which gave all life on Nirn its shape,<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-ONWild Hunt Crate-89)</sup> the deity tasked the Earthbones to choose those who would become the guardians of nature,<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-WTND-96)</sup> a task ultimately given to the Wyresses, the Name-Daughters, who are descendants of the Ehlnofey themselves.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-WCo NHR-92)</sup>

[Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) is the "strong god" of the early Aldmer and a prominent warrior spirit of the early elven tribes, elven myth credits him with fighting and ultimately killing Lorkhan.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup> A number of Tamrielic beliefs maintain that, at some point, Trinimac disappeared from the mythic stage. Though the sources disagree on the exact circumstances of the event. A number of sources hold that the Daedric Prince [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) 'ate' Trinimac taking his form, and appearing before Trinimac's followers to discredit the Aedra. What was Trinimac was released and was now the Daedric Prince [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md), and his most devout followers were transformed with him into [orcs](Lore_Orcs.md).<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-TTNOO-97)</sup> Or that it was Trinimac who tricked Boethiah, willingly entering the Prince's being to steal some of the other deity's power from within.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-TCO-98)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-TFOT-99)</sup> Other variations of this story suggest Trinimac's visage was scarred by a curse and he was banished to a [realm of ash](Lore_Ashpit.md), where, enraged at how he was tricked, he tore the shame and weakness from his chest and was reborn as Malacath.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-MOF-100)</sup> In Khajiit belief the counterpart of Malacath is instead the demon Orkha, who followed Boethra back from her exile to the Many Paths.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-TAS-101)</sup> One Khajiit source inverts the common roles, suggesting Boethra was the real Trinimac from the start and Orkha only attempted to deceive Trinimac's followers that he was their god before the truth was exposed by the real Trinimac, Boethra. Orkha's attempt to kill Boethra by infusing her body in the form of a curse was prevented by [Mephala](Lore_Mafala.md) and [Azura](Lore_Azurah.md) but some aspect of the Trinimac he pretended to be remained with Orkha when he was extracted, thus changing him to be more like the Trinimac of old and giving rise to their association.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-BOB-102)</sup> Trinimac is also acknowledged in certain Cyrodiilic folktales, where he is described as the *"greatest knight of the Ehlnofey, champion of the Dragon of Time"*.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-LOS-103)</sup> Whatever the case, the worship of Trinimac would often resurface among the Orsimer, with Trinimac adherents viewing Malacath as either a separate being, or even a deceiver who'd imprisoned the real Trinimac and was trying to usurp his faithful.<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-Solgra-104)</sup><sup>[[100]](#cite_note-PGE3O-105)</sup><sup>[[101]](#cite_note-BVTH-106)</sup>

[Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) is commonly believed to be the Goddess of Air greatest of the Sky Spirits, who was first to agree to the plan to create the mortal plane and provided the space in the Void for its creation.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup> The cultural counterpart of Kynareth within [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) belief, Kyne, is considered the chief of their pantheon, who breathed Men into existence on top of the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md) and leads the souls of the dead to [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md).<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-DATN-107)</sup><sup>[[103]](#cite_note-GOSMON-108)</sup> This psychopomp role is also present within the beliefs of the Khajiit, where the cultural counterpart of the Sky Goddess, [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md) delivers souls to the afterlife.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-HWCt F-109)</sup><sup>[[105]](#cite_note-LMD-110)</sup> In pre- [r I'Datta](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md) Khajiit belief, Khenarthi also plays an important role in safeguarding the Many Paths. It is said that, while Alkosh ruled over the children of Akha, he was soon [overthrown](Lore_Middle_Dawn.md) and his body scattered on the West Wind. It was Khenarthi who than flew along the Many Paths and put Alkosh back together, and now they both "safeguard the Many Paths from the [wayward children](Lore_Dragons.md) of Akha".<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TWS-67)</sup> Khenarthi, like Alkosh, is believed to be able to see across all the Many Paths, and is described as the spirit that gave the first sounds to the world.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-TSS-85)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Ja.27darri-40)</sup>

The concept of the Aedra as ancestors is central to a number of elven faiths, and provides the basis for the term "Aedra" itself. Altmer view themselves as "true children of the Et'Ada", descendants of the Aldmer and the Ehlnofey that preceded them, of the Aedra, in an "unbroken line" dating back to Nirn's creation. This belief was central to the ancestor worship of the Aldmer that eventually gave way to the Altmeri pantheon when the religion shifted from the worship of one's own ancestors to the worship of the ancestors of one's "betters", the most influential in their society. Most Altmer and Bosmer claim direct descent from Auri-El himself. Altmer also count deities like [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), [Syrabane](Lore_Syrabane.md) and [Phynaster](Lore_Phynaster.md) among their ancestors. Due to this perceived origin, the Altmer believe that within each of them is housed the "divine spark" and thus they've an obligation to keep an Oghma, an "everscriven scroll" in which they memorialize the events of their lives in honor of their divine ancestors.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-TOot O-111)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TM-5)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-PGE3Sum-112)</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-ARo OB-113)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TMo A-14)</sup><sup>[[109]](#cite_note-ATUQ-114)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-CM11AE-115)</sup> The Altmer use the eagle both in association with Auri-El, from whom they claim direct descent, himself<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-Cont Driftwood-116)</sup><sup>[[112]](#cite_note-Cont Winged Auriel-117)</sup>, and as a symbol representing the Aedra, their ancestors, who descended from Aetherius and became trapped in physical form by the creation of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), as a whole.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ESOKB-118)</sup> This belief is also reflected in their stylistic motifs in which circles, arcs, and tendrils, abound, symbolizing the elven ancestors, the Aedra, being constrained by the creation of Mundus.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-CM11AE-115)</sup> Altmeri prayers reference Auri-El as the Fixed Center of existence who always envelops all people and invoke him to grant his followers stability.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-COTF-119)</sup> Their prayers often emphasize their believed ancestral connection with the Aedra, urging the faithful to give praise to the Divines and thus to their own ancestral lines, and describing the Altmer as always having the gods in their lives. It is said that the body of the divine wills the Altmer into being, and that they are one with the gods, their ancestors, and so should venerate them as one.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-PBAS-120)</sup><sup>[[116]](#cite_note-Mirnor-121)</sup> Owing to their perceived divine lineage, the Altmer believe it is possible for them to achieve apotheosis under certain conditions, and so they hope to one day rejoin their divine ancestors in the stars.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ESOVairabrian-27)</sup><sup>[[117]](#cite_note-TAX-122)</sup>

The [Snow Elves](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) venerated the Aedra and their pantheon overlapped significantly with that of the Altmer. They acknowledged Auri-El as the sovereign and revered Trinimac, [Syrabane](Lore_Syrabane.md), [Jephre](Lore_Jephre.md), and [Phynaster](Lore_Phynaster.md).<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-123)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup> They had temples to all of them, though none were as impressive as their Chantry of Auri-El.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-123)</sup> Many of the most dedicated Snow Elves once committed themselves to a tireless journey through the massive Chantry of Auri-El, carrying with them the paramount desire to become one with their god, Auri-El. It is said that those who succeeded and received an audience with the Chantry's Arch-Curate, who was said to have the ear of Auri-El himself, ascended, bathed in light with a look of relief and contentment on their face.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-DGGelebor-123)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-TTS-124)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-DGVyrthur-31)</sup>

Few blanket statements can be made about [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) religion. They began as splinter groups, and they were never totally united in their beliefs.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-DWTA-125)</sup> But while they are most well-known to historians for being "godless" and "[Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -loving",<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-GTTIC-126)</sup> their society was fundamentally dedicated to the veneration of the Aedra, not the Daedra, until the last millennium of the Merethic Era.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-TWOD-127)</sup> Reverence of the Aedra among the Ayleids in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) would recede after the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md), a civil conflict between the conservative Aedra-worshipping Ayleid clans and the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphiles.md) clans, that spanned the [Late Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Late_Merethic_Era) to [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198). The conflict was ended in 1E 198, when King Glinferen of [Atatar](Lore_Atatar.md) led a combined force of Daedraphile warriors against the traditionalist forces of [Wendelbek](Lore_Wendelbek.md), driving the Aedra worshippers from the [Heartlands](Lore_Heartlands.md) of Cyrodiil and into [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), giving rise to the [Barsaebic Ayleid](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) culture.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-ASi V-128)</sup>

According to the [Order of the Ancestor Moth](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), mortal [souls](Lore_Souls.md) are the Aedric essence at the core of every mortal's existence. They believe that the souls of the dead exist in a state beyond mortality, the past and present, and the known world, where time is irrelevant. Thus connecting to the [Fjyrons](Lore_Fjyron.md), or "will to peace", of those souls allows one to glimpse the threads of the cosmic tapestry and they represent a connection to the Aedra and the "endless Aedric coil of mortal souls" through the experiences of every mortal who has ever lived. The Cult makes use of this connection via the [Ancestor Moths](Lore_Ancestor_Moth.md) which shepherd the Fjyrons in order to read the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-Distrib Soul-129)</sup><sup>[[125]](#cite_note-LAESMP-130)</sup><sup>[[126]](#cite_note-POTM-131)</sup> Souls have also been described as the piece of every mortal which comes from Aetherius, the realm of pure light and magic, and a "tiny [star"](Lore_Stars.md) which flares into a ["sun"](Lore_Sun.md) when magic is invoked.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-Lord Souls-132)</sup>

The existence and nature of the Aedra has been subject of commentary by some of the Daedra, though even among them various views exist. The [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) described the Mundus as the "cemetery" of the Aedra, claiming that their original hopes for their mortal descendants and their world were ultimately not achieved due to the mortal's foolishness. Nonetheless, though the creators of Mundus are said to have "died in pursuit of an impossible goal", they are praised for their immense conviction and admired for having wrought order out of chaos, a "brutal coercion" which even Daedra must deem impressive. By contrast, Magnus and the Magna-Ge are viewed as cowards, no other spirits being as low, who fled when things turned dire, thus making it impossible to ever know what could've been achieved had they chosen to stay and keep helping to the end.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Lyranth2-28)</sup> Contrasting Lyranth's view, the [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md) information broker Madam Whim, claimed that no living being existed which could explain the true relationship between the Aedra and the Daedric Princes, and that no information she'd ever come across in her long life indicated the Aedra were anything more than a story mortals told themselves to feel safer in their beds at night.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-Whim-133)</sup>

Mortals who achieve apotheosis have also found membership in Tamrielic pantheons alongside the Aedra, such as Talos, and possibly Arkay whom some myths attribute to have once been mortal. [Syrabane](Lore_Syrabane.md) is the Warlock's God, and [Phynaster](Lore_Phynaster.md) is the Wayfarer God, originally thought to be mortals who ascended to godhood within certain Elven Pantheons and beliefs.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-At G-134)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vo F-13)</sup>

## The Ehlnofey as a Related Concept

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Water_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Water_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Water_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Guardian_of_the_Water_02.jpg) The Guardian of the Water, an imprisoned Ehlnofey [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/ON-creature-Nirenan.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nirenan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nirenan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nirenan.jpg) Remains of Nirenan, a supposed Ehlnofey (ESO) The [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) (sometimes spelled **Elhnofey**) of the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) are generally believed to be a group of Aedric pre- [Convention](Lore_Convention.md) spirits who, along with the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md), are counted among the original inhabitants of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), and are widely considered to be the progenitors of the mortal races. The word "Ehlnofey" translates to **Earthbones** (or **Earth Bone**) in [Ehlnofex](Lore_Ehlnofex_Languages.md). The terms "Earthbone" or "Ehlnofey" are used to denote more than one type of mythical entity. The term "Ehlnofey" is typically used to refer to the spirits that didn't sacrifice themselves, from whom the various mortal races would descend, whereas "Earthbone" typically connotes those Ehlnofey who, like [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md), would at some point sacrifice themselves to form the rules of nature. The nature of the spirits the term pertains to can vary depending on account.

The concept of the Ehlnofey is of paramount importance within the beliefs of the [elves](Lore_Mer.md), particularly the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), where the term is used to represent their cultural understanding of the Aedra as ancestor spirits. According to elven myth, the Ehlnofey are considered to be the Aedric spirits who stayed in Nirn after [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) and the [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) departed in order to keep working on the new world so it'd remain viable. Among those spirits, some, typically referred to from that point on as "Earthbones", are thought to have followed the example of Y'ffre, giving themselves to the Mundus fully to stabilize it and form the foundation of its natural law as the "bones of the earth", eternal laws of nature. While others, typically referred to from that point on as "Ehlnofey", are thought to have chosen not to give themselves fully but to populate Nirn instead, thus becoming the progenitors to mortal life, which arose from their lines and took on its current form due to a phenomenon of gradual diminishment, of each consecutive generation becoming weaker and more removed than its progenitors in stature and might. This understanding forms the basis of the originally elvish term "Aedra" or "ancestor", denoting those spirits which they perceive as being part of their mythic genealogy, including figures such as [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md), who most modern Altmer and Bosmer claim direct descent from, [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) the "greatest knight of the Ehlnofey", [Syrabane](Lore_Syrabane.md), and [Phynaster](Lore_Phynaster.md). Thus elven religious iconography depicts the Ehlnofey as vaguely elven in shape, but featureless, reflecting the fading memory of the Ehlnofey.

The Aedra or Ehlnofey are thought to have remained within the world until they vanished. They are thought to primarily reside in [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), having been bound to the Earth Bones as the cornerstones of the Mundus. The terms "spokes", "eight components of [chaos](Lore_Aurbis.md)", "lent bones of the Aedra", "Bones of the Wheel", "et'Ada-gears" and ["Eight gift-limbs"](Lore_Planets.md) have also been used to refer to them in this capacity, after having transformed into the framework of reality. Some sources present a more negative view of the circumstances of these spirits and their residence in Aetherius, naming the Mundus as their "cemetery", calling them "spent ghosts", alleging them to have died as part of the creation of the world, having "died in pursuit of an impossible goal", or to have burial crypts. Thus the Aedra are sometimes called the "dead gods" or the "mortal gods", sometimes disparagingly, owing to them having become bound to the laws of mortality.

Different peoples harbor different understandings of Ehlnofey's relation to Aedra. In some accounts, both "Ehlnofey" and "Earth Bones" are described as referring exclusively to the early offspring of the Aedra rather than to the Aedra themselves, who were told to populate the world and live on through their children, leading to the gradual emergence of the various races of man and mer. The concept of descent from the ancestor spirits is generally viewed as "elven conceit" by the religions of [Men](Lore_Men.md) who, with the exception of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguards.md), view themselves as creations of those spirits rather than their descendants. *[The Annotated Anuad](Lore_The_Annotated_Anuad.md)* describes how the Aedra originated from the intermingling blood of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md), and how the Ehlnofey come from a world shattered by Padomay before Anu and Padomay's second "long and furious" battle began, suggesting, per said account, the Ehlnofey may have a separate origin predating the Aedra.

Perhaps because of their association with the land itself, "Old Ehlnofey" became another name for [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md), the mystical homeland of the elves. The language or languages of the Ehlnofey became known as Ehlnofex. It has been found that the "Earth Bones", the laws of nature, can be manipulated to some extent.

For more information, see the article on [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md)
