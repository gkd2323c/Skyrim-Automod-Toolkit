## History

### Early Life
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9b/ON-npc-King_Fahara%27jad_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-King_Fahara%27jad_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Fahara%27jad_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-King_Fahara%27jad_02.jpg) King Fahara'jad, Azah's father *His Royal Majesty King Fahara'jad was the only issue of the Ever-Virtuous Makala and Fanesh. In good time Fahara'jad, while yet a young prince, did take to wife Za-Rifah the Flower of Taneth, and she bore him three fine children. Alas, the Flower of Taneth lost her life in giving birth to the third child, and was mourned by all the Redguards of Hammerfell.
Nonetheless, his Royal Majesty's ever-glorious descendancy is assured in the persons of his eldest daughter Maraya, the Jewel of Satakalaam, his second-daughter Lakana, the Star of the Almandine, and the young Crown Prince Azah, the Lion of Antiphyllos.*
— *[The Royal Lineage of Sentinel](Lore_The_Royal_Lineage_of_Sentinel.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRLOS-1)</sup> Little is known of Azah's early years. He was the youngest child of Fahara'jad and Za-Rifah, and his mother tragically died during childbirth.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TRLOS-1)</sup> According to some, when Prince Azah was a child, he would use different spoons to taste each bowl of soup on the table before choosing one.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> According to his father Azah was a bright young man.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KFJ-5)</sup> He was trained by General Thoda, his father's chief military advisor, alongside [Uwafa](Lore_Uwafa.md) and Alasan, who were respected sons of Suturah, one of the royal viziers. However, Azah was not fond of the brothers, and the general himself admitted that they had callous tendencies and no interest in swordplay.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Thoda-6)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup> Since then, Suturah was revealed to be a necromancer and fought against Azah's father, ultimately being defeated.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KFJ-5)</sup>

Azah grew up admiring [Merric at-Aswala](Lore_Merric_at-Aswala.md), having trained under his guidance from a young age. He matured into a fine and honorable man, marked by his curiosity, open mind, and scholarly heart.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MTC-7)</sup>

### Withered Hand Crisis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/ON-place-Royal_Seraglio.jpg/200px-ON-place-Royal_Seraglio.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Royal_Seraglio.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Royal_Seraglio.jpg) Azah's Royal Seraglio [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/ON-npc-Duchess_Lakana.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Duchess_Lakana.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Duchess_Lakana.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Duchess_Lakana.jpg) Duchess Lakana, Azah's sister *My own son——Prince Azah——has been investigating the rumors about the Withered Hand. He is a bright young man, but he needs help.*
—King Fahara'jad<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KFJ-5)</sup> On behalf of his father, Azah, took on the responsibility of investigating the activities of the Withered Hand, a group of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md), led by Uwafa and Alasan. They were responsible for much trouble within the region of the Alik'r Desert.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KFJ-5)</sup> His father sought to assist his son's investigation within the city of Sentinel and ordered an [agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) to help him.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KFJ-5)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ROTD-8)</sup> Following the advice of General Thoda, the agent sought Prince Azah at his seraglio by the north wall, where he was meeting with his informants.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Thoda-6)</sup> Although the place was officially reserved for the royal family and their consorts,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Guliyeh-9)</sup> Azah was not interested in the women he surrounded himself with in a romantic way. Instead, he was building an intelligence network.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Thoda-6)</sup> The agent found two of Prince Azah's informants, Ilmaha at-Amil and Roheebah.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-IAA-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Roheebah-11)</sup> They testified that they and the prince were under attack, and that the prince was taken captive by the attackers,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-IAA-10)</sup> who employed necromancy to defeat the royal guards.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Mihra-12)</sup> The undead attacked the docks of the city, and the prince was abducted to the tunnels below the city. Ultimately, he was found and rescued by the agent.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ROTD-8)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup>

Ultimately, it was just a distraction—the necromancer brothers instead sought to reach the Impervious Vault to steal the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md) and become an immensely powerful threat. Azah was aware of this and uncovered that they had been working on this plan for years. The vault was indeed impervious and protected with powerful magic, but not from underneath the ground, so they tried to reach it through the tunnels they had been building. Azah wasn't certain if they could be stopped, but he insisted on trying. He knew they should get back to the palace and inform his father. While the agent went to speak with the king, Prince Azah coordinated efforts to warn the guards about the Impervious Vault.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIV-13)</sup> Despite their actions, and the assistance of an allied mage from the Covenant and the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md), the wards were stolen.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KFJ-5)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIV-13)</sup> As the necromancer threat grew far more serious with the wards stolen, the king resorted to seeking the help of the [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) and intended to contact them.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-KFJ-5)</sup> Prince Azah had advised him to listen to the Ash'abah weeks ago, back when they offered their help, but he didn't follow this advice at first. While the agent was sent to Marimah, their leader, to arrange cooperation, Prince Azah sought to reach out to his own Ash'abah contacts as well.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ISOA-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5c/ON-quest-Trouble_at_Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Trouble_at_Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Trouble_at_Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Trouble_at_Tava%27s_Blessing.jpg) Prince Azah and his forces in Tava's Blessing [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/45/ON-npc-Queen_Maraya.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Queen_Maraya.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Queen_Maraya.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Queen_Maraya.jpg) Queen Maraya, Azah's sister While the agent assisted the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) with their troubles, leading to the death of Uwafa and the restoration of one of the wards, among other achievements, Prince Azah sought to meet the agent afterward at [Tava's Blessing](Lore_Tava%27s_Blessing.md) to search for the second Ansei Ward. He needed the agent's cooperation, as the Withered Hand was also present there.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Meriq-15)</sup> Prince Azah and his men ware barricaded in the warehouse at the eastern edge of town. He worked alongside Talia at-Marimah of the Ash'abah, daughter of Marimah, the late leader of the group.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TAM-16)</sup>

After the death of his brother, Alasan sought to become a [lich](Lore_Lich.md). He has corrupted one of the Ansei Wards, and was trying to channel energy from the dead for his ritual. Azah intended to stop him. He had been sent there by his father, who feared that the Withered Hand might target their [navy](Lore_Sea_Transportation.md). They arrived for other reasons as well—this was the home of one of the Ansei, who had sacrificed themselves to create the Wards, making it an important place for the ritual.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup> Ultimately, the ritual was stopped, and the agent entrusted the second Ansei Ward to the prince, following the request of the Ansei Maja's spirit. Azah promised to protect the ward and sent word to his father.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AP-17)</sup> Maja also revealed that there was a third sibling—a sister of Uwafa and Alasan—who was still a threat, as she owned the last ward.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-AM-18)</sup>

Talia and Azah, along with Throne Keeper Farvad and several of the prince's guards, focused on stopping the Withered Hand from raising the consecrated dead in the Motalion Necropolis, while the agent was occupied with the Imperial invasion of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md). Unfortunately, they were overwhelmed, and Azah was captured by the forces of the Withered Hand. He was ultimately rescued by the agent, but all the wards were taken again by the necromancers.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TAM-16)</sup>

In their search for the final Ward, it was discovered that Shiri,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup> the researcher who had been helping the Ash'abah,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Zohreh-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Shiri-20)</sup> was actually the sister of Uwafa and Alasan. Azah met her when she was a very young child, but he struggled to recall much of what happened during their childhood.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup> Shiri was a powerful necromancer in her own right, and she fled to the Motalion Necropolis north of Satakalaam to continue the Withered Hand's scheme. Azah and his allies managed to uncover that she was attempting to raise the remains of her father, Suturah.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ATH-21)</sup> It is unclear whether Azah faced Shiri directly in the end, but he contributed to her downfall. As she was about to die, she sacrificed herself to raise her father, Suturah, as a lich. Ultimately, Suturah was defeated, and the final Ward was recovered.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ATH-21)</sup> He was later present at the ceremony for the restoration of the Ansei Wards in the Impervious Vault.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-RTAW-22)</sup>

### Fighters Guild
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/LG-cardart-Merric-at-Aswala.png/200px-LG-cardart-Merric-at-Aswala.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Merric-at-Aswala.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Merric-at-Aswala.png) Merric at-Aswala *Guildmaster Merric took me under his wing. He is like a second father to me*
—Azah<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup> After the Crisis of the Withered Hand and the Planemeld, King Fahara'jad decided that Azah needed to understand the wider world to prepare for the throne. He wrote to Merric, Azah's former mentor and now leader of the Fighters Guild requesting his son's admission. Azah was to join not as a prince, but as a common man, to gain true experience in war, diplomacy, and leadership. Fahara'jad believed that through the Guild's neutrality, Azah could travel Tamriel, meet foreign leaders, and grow without royal privilege under the tutelage of Merric at-Aswala himself.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MTC-7)</sup>

### Worm Cult Revival
*I will do my best to lead the Fighters Guild and make Merric proud.*
—Azah<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Azah-3)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/LO-book-The_Stirk_Fellowship_%26_Solstice_02.jpg/200px-LO-book-The_Stirk_Fellowship_%26_Solstice_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-The_Stirk_Fellowship_%26_Solstice_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-The_Stirk_Fellowship_%26_Solstice_02.jpg) Prince Azah alongisde other prominent members of the Stirk Fellowship As the Mages and Fighters Guilds came under attack by the Worm Cult, Guildmaster Merric mysteriously disappeared. In response, Azah organized a search to locate him. He dispatched an investigator to Malabal Tor, the northern Alik'r Desert, and Eastmarch—regions where informants had reported sightings of Merric. Though the investigator carefully followed Merric's trail through these areas, it ultimately led to Rivenspire, where the cult had begun to gather. Azah later answered the call to arms and assisted in thwarting a gathering of their forces in [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md). With the guild hall underneath [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) devastated, Mages Guild leader Vanus Galerion arrived at the city to accompany his compatriots to the Cult's hideout near the lighthouse west of [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md).

The death of Fighters Guild leader Merric at-Asfala caused the Cult's new Soul Reaper devices to become a top priority target. However, Vanus Galerion was abducted by the [Harvester](Lore_Harvester.md) Mezamma disguised as trusted ally Skordo the Knife, who teleported him to the remote island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md). Azah's investigator fought against the forces of the Worm Cult in the [Earth Forge](Lore_Earth_Forge.md). With the two organizations now without their guildmasters, Azah attended the summit the guild held on the island of [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md), serving as acting Guildmaster of the Fighters Guild as the guilds sought to negotiate with the ambassadors of the Three Alliances for allies. They created the Stirk Fellowship for the purpose of combating this new threat when Vanus manifested on Stirk and convinced them of the danger the Soul Reapers posed.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Azah, alongside Gabrielle Benele helped [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md) to obtain crucial correspondence regarding the new leader of the cult [Wormblood](Lore_Wormblood.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-MTCW-25)</sup> He also exchanged letters with Benele about their new ally Walks-in-Ash,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-MTCWIA-26)</sup> and compiled dossier on [Bahtra at-Hunding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bahtra_at-Hunding) with assistance of the [Ring of Daggers](Lore_Ring_of_Daggers.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-MTCBAH-27)</sup>
