Duke-Prince Indoril Brindisi Dorom was ruler of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) and was killed during the destruction of the city by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) on 12 Sun's Dusk, [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-2920v11-1)</sup>

In an attempt to end the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), Dorom paid the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) to assassinate Emperor [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md), but the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) preferred to instead end the war with battle and diplomacy, so [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) intercepted the ship containing the payment before it reached its destination.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920v7-2)</sup>

Mehrunes Dagon's attack on Mournhold was an act of revenge by his former mistress, [Turala](Lore_Turala.md). The Duke banished her upon discovering that she was pregnant with his child,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-2920v2-3)</sup> and she wandered across Tamriel until ending up in [Phrygias](Lore_Phrygias.md), [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) where she joined the [Skeffington Coven](Lore_Skeffington_Coven.md) of witches. When the coven was destroyed, she was tricked into believing that the Duke was responsible and invoked Dagon to take revenge.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-2920v10-4)</sup> After Indoril Brindisi's death, his son came down from the north and took his father's place in the court of Mournhold.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-2920ESv12-5)</sup>

The Duke is commemorated by the tranquil [plaza](Lore_Plaza_Brindisi_Dorom.md) with statuary and gardens that bears his name in the rebuilt city of Mournhold.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PBD-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-APGTM-7)</sup>
