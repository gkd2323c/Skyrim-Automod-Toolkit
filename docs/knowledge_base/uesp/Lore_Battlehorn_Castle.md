[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/OBR-place-Battlehorn_Castle.jpg/200px-OBR-place-Battlehorn_Castle.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Battlehorn_Castle.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-Battlehorn_Castle.jpg) Battlehorn Castle Battlehorn Castle is a large castle located in the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), close to the [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) border. It can be found along the [Black Road](Lore_Black_Road.md) west of the city of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md). It is named for the greater Battlehorn region that has existed long before the castle, of which it is found in.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONWines-1)</sup> The castle contains many rooms, a smithy, and a shrine to [Julianos](Lore_Julianos.md), the patron deity of [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> It serves as the home of a group of exiled [Knights of the True Horn](Lore_Knights_of_the_True_Horn.md).

## History
The castle did not exist in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), and the Valente Vineyards and Merryvine Estate were instead found at the future grounds of the castle in the Battlehorn region at the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONWines-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VV-3)</sup>

The castle traces its origins to the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) court politics of Lainlyn prior to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). At this time, Baron [Shrike](Lore_Shrike.md) was the authoritarian ruler of Lainlyn and commander of the [Host of the Horn](Lore_Host_of_the_Horn.md). Shrike's younger brother Lord [Kain](Lore_Kain.md) conspired to end his rule and formed the rival Host of the True Horn.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> However, after a failed battle to dethrone Baron Shrike, Lord Kain ordered his Knights of the True Horn to split into small groups and disperse, waiting in exile for him to call upon them again. One group, led by Lord Jaren, settled in Cyrodiil and built Battlehorn Castle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LKW-5)</sup>

One night, Lord Kain arrived at Battlehorn in the company of the battlemage Arielle Jurard, who was secretly in the service of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DN-6)</sup> Lord Jaren greeted the pair but quickly grew alarmed by his liege lord's deteriorated condition. It transpired that Lord Kain had died in battle and had been resurrected by Jurard through foul [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md). With the aid of his friend [Garridan](Lore_Garridan_Stalrous.md), Jaren sealed Jurard and the undead Kain in an underground grotto within the castle.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Lord Jaren took this secret to his grave. Over time, his fellow knights began to depart; the remaining few awaited a message from Lord Kain which would never come, and the castle fell on hard times. To pay for the castle's maintenance, many of its accouterments were pawned to a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) merchant named Nilphas Omellian, a friend of Lord Jaren who lived in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LKW-5)</sup> Lord Jaren's untimely death subsequently resulted in the castle being inherited by his son, Lord Kelvyn.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LKW-5)</sup>

The castle's weakened state was eventually exploited when a small gang of marauders successfully laid siege to the castle in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), killing Lord Kelvyn when he attempted to charge their ranks. Desperate for aid, the surviving defenders offered lordship of the castle to any who could defeat the marauders. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) answered the call, and inherited the castle under the terms of Lord Kelvyn's will.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OB-8)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LKW-5)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The Hero then proceeded to purchase back much of the castle's former equipment from Nilphas Omellian and hired a number of staff, which included the appointment of a personal sparring trainer, an experienced castellan to lead the knights, and the famed vintner Talan.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> The Hero uncovered the truth of Lord Kain's fate and unsealed the secret grotto to find that he had become little more than an undead [skeleton](Lore_Skeleton.md). However, the years of seclusion had allowed Arielle Jurard to achieve [lichdom](Lore_Lich.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DN-6)</sup> The Hero banished both of these undead abominations and filled the castle with taxidermy of other fallen beasts. With the castle fully restored to its former glory, Nilphas Omellian also arranged to install an antique [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) forge for the castle's smithy.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) an Oblivion Gate opened close to the castle, but the structure was unaffected.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-OB-8)</sup>

## Notes
- The Battlehorn region is an area of the Colovian Highlands close to Hammerfell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONWines-1)</sup> Battlehorn Wine was considered a rare [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) vintage during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> The vineyards of the Battlehorn region were said to produce some of the finest reds found in Cyrodiil by aging the wine in oak barrels.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONWines-1)</sup>
