[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/54/SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg/200px-SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn.jpg) The Soul Cairn The **Soul Cairn** is a [plane of Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Oblivion) haunted by lost [souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul).

Your first visit to the Soul Cairn will be with [Serana](Skyrim_Serana.md) during the *[Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)* [main quest](Skyrim_Dawnguard_Quests.md#Main_Quests). When Serana adds her blood to the other ingredients in the portal vessel in [Valerica's study](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Volkihar_Ruins#Valerica.27s_Study), the stone circles on the lower level split open, rising up and spinning around to form a curved staircase leading down from the balcony to the portal. If you are not a [vampire](Skyrim_Vampirism.md) or vampire lord, you will need to have part of your [soul trapped](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md) by Serana. If you choose to have Serana soul trap you, Valerica will tell you to find the [soul essence gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Essence_Gem). After you do, your soul trapped state will be undone, and all your stats will return to normal. Finding it before meeting her will also work.

The Soul Cairn is primarily inhabited by souls, wandering non-hostile spirits that were sent here because they were soul trapped before being killed. Interacting with these spirits, they will usually comment on how lost or confused they are or how desolate or horrible the Soul Cairn is. According to lore, when a [black soul gem](Skyrim_Black_Soul_Gem.md) is used, the soul it contained goes here.

For more information on the Soul Cairn, see [the lore article](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md).

## Characteristics of the Soul Cairn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/SR-place-Soul_Cairn_Portal.jpg/200px-SR-place-Soul_Cairn_Portal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_Portal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_Portal.jpg) The portal that transported you to the Soul Cairn The Soul Cairn is roughly shaped like a figure 8 when viewed from the entrance stairs. It is oriented with the stairs toward the southwest and the Boneyard entrance to the northeast. There are two massive walls in the Soul Cairn. One wall divides the figure 8 closer to the side with the entrance stairs, with one gap roughly in the center. The second wall surrounds the Boneyard, separating it from the Soul Cairn.

Once you arrive, you can see the stairs from the portal ascending to a hole in the sky surrounded by darkness. The sky within the Soul Cairn ranges in color from blue to black and purple. There are many black structures throughout this plane of Oblivion, including many seen beyond the boundary of the area, which is clearly defined as a shimmering blue magical barrier (see [bugs](#Bugs)).

The landscape is completely alien. Scattered throughout the Soul Cairn are black skeletal trees and bushes (with [soul husks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Husk) being the only plant in the plane that can be harvested), upright black stones similar to very large gravestones, and the bones and skulls of dragons. If you look straight up at the sky, you will see what appears to be a giant black hole.

There are many structures scattered throughout the Soul Cairn. Some of these are buildings containing a [chest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chest), typically guarded by hostile creatures. The entrances to some of these structures are blocked by bars. Pairs of standing stones with glowing bluish-green balls near the top are located near these buildings. Hitting the orbs with ranged spells or bow shots will lower the bars, granting access to the interior.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/SR-activity-Lightning_Attractor.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Lightning_Attractor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Lightning_Attractor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Lightning_Attractor.jpg) A lightning attractor You will also find well-like structures dotting the landscape. Most of these emit a purplish-white glow from the top, similar to the focus points in and around the [College of Winterhold](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(place).md). Standing in these causes your health to regenerate. There are smaller wells that do not glow; jumping into these will teleport you to a nearby, previously inaccessible area.

Yet another type of structure you'll come across is the lightning attractor, which is activated by placing a [grand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Grand_Soul_Gem) or [greater soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Greater_Soul_Gem) into a holder, which will then cause lightning to strike the rod, turning the [soul gem](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md) into an empty black soul gem. Using these lightning rods will summon several hostile creatures.

### Flora and Fauna
The flora here is unlike anything you've encountered in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). [Soul husks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Husk) are the strange clusters of sand-colored and sand-textured cones sticking up from the ground. They can be used to counter the effects of [Ideal Master Crystals](Skyrim_Traps.md#Ideal_Master_Crystal), the giant floating health-draining gems that may be found at the top of the various structures within the Soul Cairn. Soul husks are also the only currency used when trading with the spectral merchant, [Morven Stroud](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Morven_Stroud), for various enchanted items and spell tomes.

Soul fissures are another common feature of the Soul Cairn; they are cracks in the ground with air rising visibly out of them, which causes them to emit a hissing noise. When you activate a soul fissure, it will fill the largest soul gem in your inventory, filling black soul gems first.

While traversing the Soul Cairn, you will be attacked by [bonemen](Skyrim_Boneman.md), [wrathmen](Skyrim_Wrathman.md), and [mistmen](Skyrim_Mistman.md). There are also a few unique creatures to be found here: the cursed dragon [Durnehviir](Skyrim_Durnehviir_(dragon).md), three [keepers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Keeper) who maintain a barrier around the Boneyard, and the [Reaper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reaper).

Bonemen Bonemen look like blue-black [skeletons](Skyrim_Skeleton.md). Once defeated, they collapse like normal skeletons, but then their remains become soul embers, which are translucent black puddles similar in shape to a defeated [ice wraith](Skyrim_Ice_Wraith.md)'s remains. They typically carry [ancient Nord weapons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord#Draugr_Weapons), which can be looted along with soul gems, various alchemical [ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md), [gems](Skyrim_Gems.md), [jewelry](Skyrim_Jewelry.md), and soul husks. They are the most common of the creatures found within the Soul Cairn. Wrathmen Wrathmen appear similar to ghostly [draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md), and typically carry random two-handed melee weapons. Their remains also turn to soul embers after dying. Their weapons, but not their armor, can be looted along with similar minor loot to bonemen. Mistmen Mistmen appear similar to the [corrupted shades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corrupted_Shade) found in [Kilkreath Ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kilkreath_Ruins), and seem to favor magic as their main form of attack, although they will pick up dropped weapons. Their remains also turn to soul embers after dying, and they carry similar minor loot to bonemen. Keepers The keepers are tall, armored foes with black, smoky heads, armed with [dragonbone](Skyrim_Dragonbone.md) weapons and carrying black soul gems. Their weapons, but not their armor, can be looted if your level is 45 or higher. When defeated, their bodies dissolve into *ghostly remains*, the same as the various [guardian spirits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wolf_Guardian_Spirit) faced during [Kyne's Sacred Trials](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kyne%27s_Sacred_Trials).

### Southwest Corner of the Map: Near the Portal
There are several soul husks around the bottom of the stairs from the portal. By following the path north for a short distance, you will see a square black building on the right. There are stairs leading up to a doorway blocked by bars. To the left, on the ground, are a [hunting bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow) and a quiver of [iron arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Arrow). On either side of the doorway, you'll see standing stones with glowing orbs near the top. Shooting both of these with arrows or ranged spells will lower the bars, allowing you to access the interior. Inside is a chest containing a soul gem or two, while a random [enchanted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Enchantments) [steel dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Dagger), [page 8 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_8).md), and a random soul gem lie on the ground in front of it. A second soul gem is in a stand on the right.

Continuing along the road to the north, you will see another building a short way off to the right of the path. As you approach, you will be attacked by several bonemen, with some outside and others inside the building. There are open doorways to the interior on each side of the building, and several healing wells around the outside. Stepping into the small well just outside the building's northern entrance will teleport you to the roof of the building, where you will find a chest, with a copy of *[The Book of Life and Service](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Book_of_Life_and_Service)* and a random soul gem in front of it. You may safely return to the ground by carefully jumping onto the lower roof of the structure from the southern corner of the roof. Inside the building, there is a chest in the western corner, with a medium [coin purse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Coin_Purse), a random soul gem, and [page 6 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_6).md) on the ground in front of it.

[‡](#note_arv) **Objective: Arvak** *Farther along the path, you will encounter a soul that appears to be looking for something or someone. When you approach, he will ask you to help find his horse, [Arvak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Arvak), starting a side quest to find [Arvak's skull](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Arvak%27s_Skull). After this point, you may see Arvak, a skeletal horse with a blue flaming mane and tail. He will randomly teleport close to you, stand nearby, or gallop through the Soul Cairn. You are unable to interact with Arvak until you complete the [quest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Cairn_Horse_Quest) and learn the spell [Summon Arvak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summon_Arvak).* Continuing along the path, the path passes through a gap in a tall black stone wall, with a flight of stairs leading up to the gap.<sup>[‡](#intnote_arv)</sup> A healing well lies to the left, and beyond that to the west is a stone courtyard, with a building built into the wall. Inside the building is a chest containing quality loot. There is a wide exit on the far side of the room to the northwest. Upon exiting through that doorway, four bonemen will rise from the ground: two immediately in front of you, and two on another smaller structure ahead. Once they are dealt with, you can climb the stairs on the side of the structure ahead to find a chest filled with quality loot, with [page 3 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_3).md) and a [copper and sapphire circlet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Copper_and_Sapphire_Circlet) on the ground in front of it. However, take care not to linger, as an Ideal Master Crystal hangs in the sky above the chest.

This structure is bisected by the barrier surrounding the Soul Cairn. There are two large healing wells beside the structure, but nothing else of interest is in the immediate area. Passing back through the building built into the wall, you have the choice of exploring the rest of this side of the great wall before passing through the gap, or heading straight for Valerica.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/SR-place-Soul_Cairn_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Soul_Cairn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_02.jpg) A scenic view of the Soul Cairn Continuing your exploration, there is a path heading west. Following it will enable you to explore the left-hand side of the original path. There are several soul fissures for you to collect, as well as healing wells. When you arrive back at the stairs to the portal, you can cross and start exploring the other side, which is far larger.

There is a building east-southeast of the portal steps, with two random soul gems inside on the ground level on a stack of squared-off stones. A teleport well lies a few paces outside the building to the northeast. The well will transport you to the roof of the building, where you will be attacked by a boneman and a wrathman guarding a chest. You may safely return to ground level by jumping from the western corner of the roof.

Northwest of the teleport well, you'll see the spine of a dragon sticking out of the ground, with a human skull on top of it. In front of this gruesome totem stands a soul gem pedestal holding a black soul gem. Approaching will cause three bonemen to emerge from the ground and attack. A few steps south of the teleport well lie the skeletal remains of another dragon, with a human skeleton in its ribcage. There are [ancient Nord gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord_Gauntlets) and a [steel sword of shocks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Sword_of_Shocks) inside the dragon's remains.

Near the southeastern corner of the Soul Cairn, a distance due east of the previous teleport well building, is a large structure with an interior courtyard where two soul husks grow. There is only one narrow doorway in or out, with several flights of stairs leading up from the courtyard to the top of the structure opposite a tower. If you are facing the tower, there are stairs on your left which descend to a lower roof. You can either use the [Whirlwind Sprint](Skyrim_Whirlwind_Sprint.md) [dragon shout](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md) or descend the stairs, follow the roof along, and then climb another flight at the far end to reach the tower. At the top of the tower is an Ideal Master Crystal floating above another chest of quality loot.

A discernible path heading north from the doorway to the courtyard leads to a paved section with a lightning attractor beside a chest. Some skeletal remains and a soul gem are on the ground in front of the chest. Using the lightning attractor causes some bonemen and wrathmen to rise from bone piles around it.

### Middle of the Map: Between the Boneyard and the Portal
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/SR-npc-Morven_Stroud_02.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Morven_Stroud_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Morven_Stroud_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Morven_Stroud_02.jpg) Morven Stroud is... er, was a Breton merchant Head toward the gap in the great wall. A short distance before reaching it, there are stairs off to the east leading up to the rear of the wall, with a window that looks through to the other side. The window is flanked by flagpoles, and there is a soul standing beside it. Additionally, the stones at the base of the flagpoles hold [page 1 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_1).md), a random [poison](Skyrim_Poisons.md), a copy of *The Book of Life and Service*, and a random filled soul gem. You can see a chest (which contains minor loot) through the window on the other side of the wall. To reach the chest, you need to pass through the gap in the wall and immediately turn east, then follow the wall until you reach a short flight of stairs leading up to the alcove containing the chest.

### Southeast Corner of the Map: Near the Reaper's Lair

#### The First Keeper and Conjure Wrathman Spell Tome
Following the main wall and barrier to the east leads to several structures. The first is a small many-arched structure containing nothing of interest. The second structure just beyond the first is a tall, ornate tower, with broken masonry spinning around its top, and what seems to be many souls congregating around a tall, armored figure at its base. This is one of the keepers. As you approach, up to four wrathmen will rise and attack. Once they are defeated, you will face the keeper, who carries a dragonbone battleaxe. During the fight, the souls that the keeper was draining will vanish.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/SR-npc-Jiub_02.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Jiub_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Jiub_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Jiub_02.jpg) Jiub wanders around his campsite A few paces farther east lies another structure, with four pillars supporting it. Beneath the structure, you'll find a typical shrine base holding the *[Spell Tome: Conjure Wrathman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome__Conjure_Wrathman)*. Upon approaching the spell tome, some wrathmen will attack. There is a path to the east of this structure; follow it to find the [Reaper's Lair](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reaper%27s_Lair). On the east side of this building, you will find a skeleton leaning against a large rock, with a random enchanted [greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword) and an [ancient Nord helmet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ancient_Nord_Helmet) lying beside it. There is also a [note](Skyrim_Note_(DG).md) next to the skeleton, which suggests this was an accomplished [adventurer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Adventurer) who willingly entered the portal against someone's advice. More wrathmen will rise as you approach the skeleton.

#### Reaper's Lair and a Reaper Gem Fragment
The Reaper's Lair is the only building with a door within the Soul Cairn. To correctly call forth the reaper and its minions, you will normally need to collect three [reaper gem fragments](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reaper_Gem_Fragment), although it is also possible to do this using [fire runes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Rune) (see [notes](#Notes)). Full details for the lair are found [on the relevant page](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Reaper%27s_Lair).

Heading north from the Reaper's Lair, you'll come to a large paved area with several structures and flights of stairs that lead up to a lightning attractor. Using it will cause some bonemen, wrathmen, and mistmen to attack. Head southwest, passing the spiked roof of a building, to another paved area with a well in the middle. As you approach, bonemen will attack you. Beside the well is a skull, a random [shield](Skyrim_Shield.md), a random enchanted [dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dagger), [page 5 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_5).md), a random poison, and three empty random soul gems.

Once you've collected all ten pages of his book, you will be able to see the details in Jiub's form far more clearly. He will be overjoyed, and will reward you with a unique book, *[Saint Jiub's Opus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Saint_Jiub%27s_Opus)*, as well as the [locket of Saint Jiub](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Locket_of_Saint_Jiub).

### Northeast Corner of the Map

#### Conjure Mistman Spell Tome
From the location of Arvak's skull, continue northeast to find another tower with a teleport well inside. As you approach, some bonemen, wrathmen, and mistmen will attack you from the roof as well as from the ground. You can teleport directly to the roof and then fight your way back down. Two flights of stairs down from the top, on the first covered section, is a copy of *The Book of Life and Service*. On the next level down, you can see part of a [long bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Long_Bow); casting any magic at it will cause another boneman to rise.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/SR-place-Soul_Cairn_04.jpg/200px-SR-place-Soul_Cairn_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_04.jpg) The Conjure Mistman spell tome rests upon its plinth Back on ground level, another dragon carcass with an enchanted battleaxe and a few orcish arrows lies a couple feet north of the stairs. A few paces farther to the east lies a staircase leading up to a paved section, where you'll find a plinth holding the *[Spell Tome: Conjure Mistman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome__Conjure_Mistman)*. There is a medium coin purse, an empty random soul gem, and [page 7 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_7).md) on the ground at the base of the shrine. You will summon several angry mistmen upon approaching the spell tome.

#### The Second Keeper and a Reaper Gem Fragment
North-northwest of the shrine is a teleport well with three pillars around it and two healing wells beside it, all underneath a floating island. Entering the teleport well transports you to a structure on top of the island. Following the path around the structure counter-clockwise as you climb, you will pass several souls before reaching a keeper armed with a dragonbone bow. Behind the keeper is another teleport well, which returns you to the ground beside the first well. Head west, and you will soon see another large building with an Ideal Master Crystal floating above it. Inside, a staircase leads all the way to the roof, where a chest containing quality loot (including one of the reaper gem fragments) and a copy of *The Book of Life and Service* are sitting directly under the crystal.

Heading southwest, up the hill toward the Boneyard, you will see a building situated against the eastern wall, with several flights of stairs leading up to it. At the top of the first flight of stairs, you will see a short, covered passage on your right. There is a chest at the end of the passage, with [page 4 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_4).md) and two pieces of [silver ore](Skyrim_Silver_Ore.md) sitting on the ground beside it. As you approach the chest, a mistman will rise from the nearby pile of bones.

A path heading south from this building leads to a healing well surrounded by upright stones. Turning and heading southwest leads past a soul acting as a farmer. The soul is tilling the land where fourteen soul husks are planted, as two [cow](Skyrim_Cow.md) souls wander freely nearby.

### Northwest Corner of the Map

#### Conjure Boneman Spell Tome
Head west from the large complex west of Jiub's camp to find a large raised paved area with stairs leading up to a lightning attractor. Using the attractor will cause some bonemen, wrathmen, and mistmen to rise and attack. From here, head north-northwest through a fairly barren area, and you'll eventually reach a large structure, with two tall pillars of black stonework that mark the beginning of an underpass. The path begins on the southern side of the structure, with several bonemen patrolling the underpass. Inside the building, stairs lead up and counter-clockwise until you reach an Ideal Master Crystal hanging inside the building above a chest containing quality loot.

To the southwest is a paved area where a soul is standing over a lootable skeleton with a large coin purse beside it. There is a building nearby, with stairs leading up to a barred area and an Ideal Master Crystal hanging in the sky above. As you reach the top, several bonemen will rise. One of the orbs that you need to hit to lower the bars is above them; the other is under the pitched roof farthest from the bars, somewhat hidden behind some stacked stone slabs. A chest containing quality loot with a soul sitting beside it lies beyond the barred gate.

To the west, you'll find a plinth sitting on some stonework surrounded by several soul husks. Beside the plinth lies the *[Spell Tome: Conjure Boneman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spell_Tome__Conjure_Boneman)*, two random soul gems, and a sack of soul husks. As you approach, a horde of at least five bonemen will assault you.

#### The Third Keeper and a Reaper Gem Fragment
Farther to the west is another large building, the Tabernacle of the Ideal Masters. As you near it, you will pass under a small arch and ascend a short flight of stairs. On the far side are several bonemen, wrathmen, and mistmen. A keeper is leaning against the wall in front of you, armed with a dragonbone mace.

To the left lies another short flight of stairs leading up to an area blocked by a gate. One of the control orbs is on your right at eye level, and the other is high above the blocked doorway. Shoot these to access an area with another Ideal Master Crystal floating over a chest containing one of the reaper gem fragments and some quality loot. As you approach the chest, some bonemen will attack. Take care, as you may be within range of the Ideal Master Crystal during the fight.

There is a teleport well to the right of the keeper through the arch opposite the barred gate, which takes you to the top of an L-shaped roof section above the well. There is a quality chest at the other end of the roof, and a lightning attractor where the roof turns. Using this will cause some bonemen and wrathmen to rise from the piles of bones on either side of the attractor.

North of the main structure, you'll find a teleport well in a small paved area with many upright stones around it. The well takes you to a raised platform, where a mistman attacks you near a dead traveler's camp. Here, a skeleton lies near an extinguished campfire. Near the campfire, you will find three random [potions](Skyrim_Potions.md) of [healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing), [magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka), or [stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina), a random potion or poison, a [scroll of storm thrall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Scroll_of_Storm_Thrall), a [dragon priest dagger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon_Priest_Dagger), a random [staff](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Staff), a large coin purse, three soul husks, and a [knapsack](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Knapsack). Take care when dropping back down to the ground, or use the [Become Ethereal](Skyrim_Become_Ethereal.md) shout to avoid damage.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/84/SR-place-Soul_Cairn_06.jpg/200px-SR-place-Soul_Cairn_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Soul_Cairn_06.jpg) An alchemy lab located to the left of the Boneyard's entrance East-northeast of the building containing the keeper is a large, complex structure, the Black Minster. The main entrance is on the southern side, which leads into a maze of short dead-end passages where an occasional boneman, wrathman, or mistman guard two chests. There is a sack of soul husks in the corridor near one of the chests, which also has a copy of *The Book of Life and Service* beside it on the ground. There are two loose soul husks and a [daedra heart](Skyrim_Daedra_Heart.md) on a platform to your left near the passage with horizontal bars at either end.

#### The Soul Essence Gem
The passages eventually lead to a teleport well with grating above it, which takes you directly above. Stairs lead up to a chest under an Ideal Master Crystal. The chest will be empty if you're a vampire, but will contain the soul essence gem if you had Serana trap part of your soul. Around the far side of the chest is [page 9 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_9).md) and a random empty soul gem.

On the west side of the entrance to the Boneyard is a large structure with an inner courtyard, the Black Folly, accessed by a narrow passage. Inside you will find what looks like a [word wall](Skyrim_Word_Wall.md), in front of which lies a typical boss chest, [page 2 of *Jiub's Opus*](Skyrim_Jiub%27s_Opus_(Page_2).md), a [ruby](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ruby), and a small coin purse. Return to Jiub to complete his quest, then head to the entrance of the Boneyard.

Approaching the entrance to the Boneyard, you will find Valerica, who rushes to her daughter. She has set up an [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Lab.md) to the left of the Boneyard's entrance, and several books can be found nearby: *[Immortal Blood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Immortal_Blood)*, *[Physicalities of Werewolves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Physicalities_of_Werewolves)*, and *[The Book of Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Book_of_Daedra)*. Three rolls of paper and three soul husks are also beside the alchemy lab. After a conversation between you and Valerica, she will lead the way into the Boneyard.

#### The Boneyard
The [Boneyard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Boneyard) is an enclosed graveyard within the Soul Cairn that is only accessible after defeating the three keepers. Upon entering the Boneyard, you will pass along a narrow passage before it opens into a large courtyard. Surrounding the central courtyard are raised sections, with stairs leading up to healing wells. As soon as you enter, Durnehviir will be flying around in the sky, and bonemen will begin to rise. It is best to concentrate on fighting the [dragon](Skyrim_Dragons.md), as bonemen will continue to rise to replace their fallen until you defeat Durnehviir, at which point any remaining bonemen will immediately die.

## Notes
- Serana is the only follower who can enter the Soul Cairn with you. However, anyone you resurrect outside the Soul Cairn, such as via the [Ritual Stone's](Skyrim_The_Ritual_Stone.md) power, will follow you in.
- An easy way to navigate the Soul Cairn is to follow the edges; don't stray too far from them. Almost all locations here are near the edges of the playable area, or within sight of one.
- You won't be allowed entry into the Soul Cairn unless you either are a vampire, or let Serana partially soul trap you. If you let yourself be partially soul trapped, you'll get the [Weakened Soul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Weakened_Soul) ability; your magicka, health, and stamina will be reduced by 45 points and recover slowly. You can reverse it if you pick up the [Soul Essence Gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Essence_Gem); all 3 stats will be restored, and recover normally.
- You can quickly return to the Soul Cairn without having to traverse the [Castle Volkihar](Skyrim_Castle_Volkihar.md) ruins and courtyard again by exiting the laboratory through a door in the southern corner. This leads to the *Castle Volkihar Balcony*, a small outside area from which you can fast-travel and then return to the Soul Cairn via the door to the laboratory.
- If you view the map while in the Soul Cairn, the marker will place you in the [Sea of Ghosts](Skyrim_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), just north of [Orphan's Tear](Skyrim_Orphan%27s_Tear.md).
- All enemies defeated here, except for Durnehviir, will have at least one soul gem in their inventory.
- The bonemen, mistmen, and wrathmen may have soul husks in their inventories, and some of them may also drop [ectoplasm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ectoplasm), making them the only respawning enemies in the game that drop ectoplasm.
- Once the enemies here are defeated, they cannot be resurrected in any way.
- Soul husks in your inventory are classified as food, not ingredients.
- If the [Green Thumb](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Green_Thumb) perk is active, you will gain 2 soul husks per harvest, rather than one.
- There aren't many animal and creature souls present here, despite it being the place where spent souls go to after being used in soul gems. This could prove Serana's theory that only the souls used in black soul gems end up in the Soul Cairn. However, 2 cow souls can be found in a pseudo farm setting, and Arvak himself can be seen roaming around. There is no explanation for their presence.
- Other than the human-shaped souls you'll find here, there is another entity that roams the Soul Cairn. They look and behave like recolored [wisps](Skyrim_Wisp.md), though they are completely docile and you cannot interact with them in any way. However, if you attract the attention of hostile enemies and then evade detection, they will target the wisp-like entities instead. This can be a useful distraction since the wisp-like entities are invulnerable, and the undead don't seem to realize that.
- The rods for the lightning attractors will eventually reset, though the attractors themselves can only be used once. The soul fissures aren't restricted to one-time use, though, allowing you to reactivate them when the Soul Cairn resets.
- A half-buried word wall exists here, though it isn't active.
- Several [Daedra Hearts](Skyrim_Daedra_Heart.md) can be found here, making it an alternative way to get them for free.
- As the Soul Cairn is technically outside, it is possible to use the [Storm Call](Skyrim_Storm_Call.md) shout to summon a storm. The area becomes cloudy, and almost appears as if it's part of Tamriel. You cannot fast travel from here, though.
- According to lore, after a [black soul gem](Skyrim_Black_Soul_Gem.md) is used, the soul it contained goes here. However, in-game this doesn't happen, and the soul is simply used and the gem disappears.
- [Azura's Star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Azura%27s_Star), the [Black Star](Skyrim_The_Black_Star_(item).md), the [Flawed Varla Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Flawed_Varla_Stone)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Rare_Curios.md)</sup>, and the [Soul Tomato](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Tomato)<sup>[CC](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Saints_and_Seducers)</sup> won't be filled if you activate a soul fissure here.
- One of the Keepers uses a bow with dragon bone arrows that may not be retrieved based on your character level, but can be task retrieved by Serana. You will need to hold the speech action button with the cursor pointed on the floor in front of her feet to enable command actions for her.
- When [Beyond Death](Skyrim_Beyond_Death.md) is active, you will be unable to dismiss Serana from your party while the two of you are here.
- Serana's "I need you to do something" dialogue option will be absent while the two of you are here. However, if you hold down the speech action key while pointing the cursor in front of her, she will then be able to perform actions upon your request like other followers. Don't tell Serana to "wait" while you're here, though, or she'll get bugged.

Your crossbow should now function like normal.
- When using [Dawnbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dawnbreaker) within the Soul Cairn, the mass turning effect from exploding undead can sometimes cause neutral souls to enter combat. While they will not attack and cannot be harmed, Serana will become stuck constantly casting spells at them until you load a previous save or fast travel. <sup>**?**</sup>
- The sky may be corrupted once you exit the Reaper's Lair. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6.1130, fixes this bug.
- The skeleton by the Reaper's Lair is not detected by the [Detect Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Dead) spell. <sup>**?**</sup>
