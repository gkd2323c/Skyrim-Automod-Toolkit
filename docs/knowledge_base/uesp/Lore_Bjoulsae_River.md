[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/DF-map-Gavaudon.png/200px-DF-map-Gavaudon.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Gavaudon.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-map-Gavaudon.png) The Bjoulsae River ca. [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/ON-place-Bjoulsae_River.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bjoulsae_River.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bjoulsae_River.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bjoulsae_River.jpg) The Bjoulsae River ca. 2E 582 The Bjoulsae River is a major river that forms most of the border between [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). It is the home of the [River Horse Bretons](Lore_River_Horse_Bretons.md), tribes of nomadic [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup> that live throughout the river and nearby [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-2)</sup> The river's water source is the vast [Halcyon Lake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halcyon_Lake), found in the dead center of [Mournoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mournoth). The river advances southwest pass the kingdom of [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) until it empties into the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONBjoulsae-3)</sup> There are multiple rivers that empty into the Bjoulsae River: one of the named ones is the Jugular from the north somewhere east of [Wind Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wind_Keep),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rt O-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RGMap-5)</sup> another is the [Gardner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gardner) that runs from Cumberland Falls through [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WJOTB-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Ga P:v2-7)</sup> and the other is the [Yrinthi River](Lore_Yrinthi_River.md), which is infested with pirates like the Bjoulsae and the Iliac.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DUG-8)</sup><sup>:5</sup> Rowboat racing throughout the Bjoulsae River has been a popular pastime for centuries.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONContraband-9)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-place-Wayrest.jpg/200px-ON-place-Wayrest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wayrest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Wayrest.jpg) Wayrest [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fe/ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg/200px-ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Halcyon_Lake.jpg) Bisnensel-by-the-Lake In the early years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), High King [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) sought to expand his [empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) west into High Rock to liberate the early Bretons from the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) of the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md). His initial campaign only took him as far west as the Bjoulsae River, but anything beyond that was out of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) reach.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup> However, sometime later their conquest expanded all the way west to what is now modern-day [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) by [1E 246](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_246).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-AHOD-10)</sup>

Sometime after their exile from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) King of [Nenalata](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nenalata), [Laloriaran Dynar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar) and his people traveled to the Bjoulsae River and were eventually welcomed by the Direnni. In that time, they built their new city, [Bisnensel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bisnensel) and even made peace with the local Bretons and the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md). These events took place between [1E 375](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_375) and [1E 452](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_452).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-11)</sup>

As early as [1E 800](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_800), the hamlet of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) was built on the mouth of the Bjoulsae River, merely a rudimentary collection of huts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WJOTB-6)</sup> In that time, it was constantly harassed by the Orcish war parties from their village-fortress to the north, [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) and the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) pirates from the islands to the west. By then, the Orsimer had gained serious control over [Stormhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhaven) and may have even built a settlement in the name of their king, [Golkarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golkarr).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONAnti Map-12)</sup> They were determined to control the Bjoulsae River when some of their own were displaced by the [Ra Gada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ra_Gada) in the south.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1ETWR-13)</sup> It was the former's constant raids that inspired King [Joile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Joile) of Daggerfall to write a letter to [Gaiden Shinji](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gaiden_Shinji) in [1E 948](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_948). When the [Siege of Orsinium](Lore_Siege_of_Orsinium.md) ended in [1E 980](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_980), the destruction greatly opened trade in the Bjoulsae River, resulting in the formation of the Masconian Trade Way. It was created by the hard-working traders and merchants of the Bjoulsae River, and not only did it bring about a decline for piracy, it also led to a great expansion of the Bjoulsae's trade and community.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WJOTB-6)</sup> One such factor that contributed to this was Daggerfall's [ivory](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ivory) trade, which came from plundered ivory from Orsinium.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONAnti Ivory-14)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-place-Evermore.jpg/200px-ON-place-Evermore.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Evermore.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Evermore.jpg) Evermore [![](https://images.uesp.net/4/4c/DF-npc-Gortwog_%28face%29.png)](File_DF-npc-Gortwog_(face).png.md) [](File_DF-npc-Gortwog_(face).png.md) King Gortwog of Nova Orsinium At this point, Wayrest controlled both sides of the river bank and the merchant family, the [Gardners](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gardner) built a walled palace on the north side of the river. Their palace served as the basis of the modern city as many buildings and banks were built around it. Wayrest became a kingdom in [1E 1100](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1100) and their first King was [Farangel Gardner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Farangel_Gardner).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WJOTB-6)</sup> Meanwhile, further into the Bjoulsae River, the River Horse Bretons built their own city and trading hub called [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) in [1E 983](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_983).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TRHOKE-2)</sup>

After the destruction of Evermore and [Hallin's Stand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hallin%27s_Stand) in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) horde led by Emperor [Durcorach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durcorach) crossed the Bjoulsae River and laid their eyes on Wayrest. The Siege of Wayrest, as it was called lasted for fifty-seven days since they did not have siege weapons to breakdown the walls, nor ships to sail into the city's harbor. The Black Drake left some of his forces in Wayrest and continued west toward [Glenumbra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Glenumbra). Unbeknownst to them, Earl [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland took his soldiers on merchant trade ships to rendezvous with King [Bergamot](Lore_Bergamot_(person).md) in Daggerfall.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TOAM3-15)</sup>

[Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s campaign over High Rock brought the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) to the Bjoulsae River, in an important conflict called the Battle of the Bjoulsae. The navy led a surprise attack on Wayrest's naval forces and crushed them at dawn. [Amiel Richton](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Richton) led a squadron during the battle before he succeeded [Vasi Hadrach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vasi_Hadrach) as an Admiral and before his resounding victory in the [Battle of Hunding Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Hunding_Bay).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RGAmiel-16)</sup>

When strange reports of King [Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus)' ghost roaming Daggerfall began to reach Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) in [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), he sent an [agent](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Agent) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) to quell the spirit. They traveled down the Yrinthi River from deeper in the mainland via an inconspicuous ship to avoid pirates and into the Bjoulsae River. From there they traveled to the Iliac Bay until they were stranded by an unnatural storm and washed up onto [Privateer's Hold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Privateer%27s_Hold).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DUG-8)</sup><sup>:5</sup> Their exploits in the Iliac Bay had led up to the events of the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), on the 9th of Frostfall. On that same day, an ambassador of the Emperor, [Naigon Strale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naigon_Strale) sailed down the Bjoulsae on an Imperial barge and passed by the river village of Candlemass. At that moment, a large tidal wave passed through the coast and sunk the barge among many others. Not long after, the north Bjoulsae region and most notably [Gavaudon](Lore_Gavaudon_(fiefdom).md) was caught in a battle between Wayrest and Orsinium. Many villages were caught in the crossfire and were burned down. The King of Orsinium, [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gortwog_gro-Nagorm) took profitable portions of the Bjoulsae Rivercoast.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TWITW-17)</sup>

## Geography

### Hammerfell
The south side of the Bjoulsae River is part of the province of Hammerfell, until it extends northeast toward Evermore and Halcyon Lake, which is already part of High Rock. The Hammerfell side of the river mouth is a forested region east of the [Dragontails](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains). It contains political entities associated with the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), which includes the [Barony of Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md), the [County of Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam_(county).md), and the [Fiefdom of Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth_(fiefdom).md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFMap-18)</sup> All of them were absorbed into [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), the Kingdom of [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md) in the wake of the Warp in the West.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TWITW-17)</sup> King [Lhotun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lhotun) took many precautions to make sure they were kept under his control but a rebellion ensued with the True Crowns of the north, near [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3EHA-19)</sup> There is a small portion of the [Alik'r Desert](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alik%27r_Desert) that is located on the tip of the river mouth, north of the minor city, [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) and the valley of death, the Motalion Necropolis.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONMap Alik.27r-20)</sup>

### High Rock
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/ON-place-Stormhaven-Bangkorai_Bridge_%28Stormhaven%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Stormhaven-Bangkorai_Bridge_%28Stormhaven%29.jpg)](File_ON-place-Stormhaven-Bangkorai_Bridge_(Stormhaven).jpg.md) [](File_ON-place-Stormhaven-Bangkorai_Bridge_(Stormhaven).jpg.md) The Bjoulsae River Bridge [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-place-Dro-Dara_Plantation.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dro-Dara_Plantation.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dro-Dara_Plantation.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dro-Dara_Plantation.jpg) The Flooded Plantations of Gavaudon The north side of the Bjoulsae River and further inland is part of High Rock. It runs alongside the southern coast of [Stormhaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhaven), around the [Kingdom of Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) and later the Fiefdom of Gavaudon to the east of it. It is said that the plains of Gavaudon are flooded by the waters of the Bjoulsae and that they provide very rich soil. It attracts the hard-working class of farmers who can withstand the cold and wet temperatures. The region has been known to have been raided by Orc war parties.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TGEHR-21)</sup> There are several aquatic fauna that live on the north side of the river such as the common [crocodile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crocodile) as well as river fish like the [Dreugh Shrimp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh_Shrimp), the [Grass Carp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grass_Carp), and the [River Stingray](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:River_Stingray). In the halfway point between the river and the Iliac Bay are sea fish such as the [Alcaire Pike](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alcaire_Pike), the [Dab](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dab), and the [Stormhaven Flounder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhaven_Flounder).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONFishing-22)</sup>

The river advances northeast into the mainland region of Bangkorai. There is a large drawbridge that connects Stormhaven with Bangkorai and leads directly to the city of Evermore, which sits right on top of the river. At this point, the Bjoulsae River tightens into its original cascading streams and boats can no longer navigate further in. Evermore is the last stop on the trade route, but it is a nexus that branches High Rock proper with the north wastes of Hammerfell, and neighboring cities such as Orsinium to the north, and [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)'s city-states, [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md) and [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) to the east.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TGEB-23)</sup> There is a bountiful supply of fish from this point, ranging from saltwater fish like the [Bjoulsae Hake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bjoulsae_Hake), the [Morid Cod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morid_Cod), the [Swai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Swai), and the [Toadfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Toadfish). There is also river fish from the northeast such as the [Prickleback](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Prickleback), the [Lenok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lenok), the [Panga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Panga), and the [Pupfish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pupfish).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONFishing-22)</sup> As stated before, the Bjoulsae River continues northeast into the wilderness of [Mournoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mournoth) until it reaches Lake Halcyon. Along the way are minor settlements like the fishing village, [Murcien's Hamlet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Murcien%27s_Hamlet) and the farming community of Northglen.

### Islands
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/ON-interior-Blackheart_Haven.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Blackheart_Haven.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Blackheart_Haven.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Blackheart_Haven.jpg) Blackheart Haven There are dozens of small islands throughout the mouth of the Bjoulsae River that are used by sea raiders and smugglers as hideouts or safe refuges.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONLoad Blackheart-24)</sup> Akaviri pirates have been known to inhabit some of these islands since at least the early First Era.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WJOTB-6)</sup>

- [Blackheart Haven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackheart_Haven) A mountainous island that was used by the infamous pirate captain, Blackheart and his crew.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONLoad Blackheart-24)</sup> It has since been their tomb after he was lifted from a curse caused by the Daedric Lord, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONShifty-25)</sup>

- Unnamed East Island (Satakalaam) An island north of mainland Satakalaam, slightly larger than the western island. The largest settlement on the island was called Javibia and it had an active [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) chapter.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-DFMap Satakal-26)</sup>

- Unnamed Island (Mournoth) A large and long island found north of mainland Mournoth. The eastern end of the island is directly on the river mouth, not far from Wayrest. The largest settlement on the island was called Zagoparia.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-DFMap Mourn-27)</sup>

- Unnamed Island (Wayrest) A large island located directly west of Wayrest city and southeast of Menevia county.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-DFMap Wayrest-28)</sup>

- Unnamed West Island (Satakalaam) An island north of mainland Satakalaam, slightly smaller than the eastern island.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-DFMap Satakal-26)</sup>
