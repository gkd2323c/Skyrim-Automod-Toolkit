# Forsworn

| --- |

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Forsworn.jpg) [Kaie](Skyrim_Kaie.md), [Duach](Skyrim_Duach.md), [Odvan](Skyrim_Odvan.md), [Borkul the Beast](Skyrim_Borkul_the_Beast.md), [Madanach](Skyrim_Madanach.md), [Uraccen](Skyrim_Uraccen.md), and [Braig](Skyrim_Braig.md) The **Forsworn**, also known as the **Madmen of the Reach**, are a clan of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) formed following the Markarth Incident in [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176). The Forsworn claim that they are the true sons and daughters of [The Reach](Skyrim_The_Reach.md), but are often considered "two septims short of a pint of ale".

Despite appearing primitive in nature, the Forsworn have proven to be an aggressive political force which has an astute grasp of insurgency warfare, and even have agents living among the populace of [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md) in secret. They directly oppose all other major political groups active in the Reach, including the [Thalmor](Skyrim_Thalmor.md). They wish to drive both [Nords](Skyrim_Nord.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) out of the Reach and attain independence. The Forsworn are the most common type of hostile NPC encountered in the Reach. Encounters with them will not occur under normal conditions outside of the Reach. It is not possible to join the Forsworn, but you can side with them through a [quest](Skyrim_The_Forsworn_Conspiracy.md) [series](Skyrim_No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine.md) and [one area](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Druadach_Redoubt) will become non-hostile.

## Overview

### The Markarth Incident
While the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) and the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) fought the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), much of the Empire was abandoned by the [Imperials](Skyrim_Imperial.md). The Reachmen long held that they were the true owners of the Reach and for centuries begrudgingly accepted [Nord](Skyrim_Nord.md) rule. Seeing the perfect opportunity, they rebelled against the Nords and captured the nearly undefended city of [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md) and all of the Reach. Their control over the hold lasted for two years, from [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174) to [4E 176](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_176), and was administered relatively peacefully, with only a few of the harshest Nord landowners put to death.

In 4E 176 [Igmund](Skyrim_Igmund.md)'s father, the deposed [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) Hrolfdir, and the Empire, promised [Ulfric Stormcloak](Skyrim_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) free worship of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) in exchange for putting down the rebellion and retaking the Reach. Hrolfdir knew this promise was in direct violation of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md), but wagered that the Thalmor would not discover it. Ulfric led a Nord militia against the Reachmen and used the [Thu'um](Skyrim_Thu%27um.md) to shout them from the very walls of Markarth and drive them from the city. After Hrolfdir reclaimed control of Markarth, captured Reachmen were brutally tortured and executed by the Nords regardless of allegiance, with the few that were spared being imprisoned in Cidhna Mine.

The surviving Reachmen fled to the wilds of the Reach, creating what would become the Forsworn. Their leader, [Madanach](Skyrim_Madanach.md), was secretly imprisoned in the [Cidhna Mine](Skyrim_Cidhna_Mine.md) by [Thonar Silver-Blood](Skyrim_Thonar_Silver-Blood.md) in an attempt to leverage control over the Forsworn. When the Thalmor discovered the presence of Talos worship, they demanded the arrest of the Nord militia. Hrolfdir was forced to arrest Ulfric and his men or risk outright war over religious conflict. The Nord militia were eventually allowed to leave, but the Empire and Hrolfdir's broken promise left a fissure that would eventually widen into the Stormcloak Rebellion. The Forsworn still remember the cruelty of the Nords who deposed them, and seek vengeance against all Nords as a result.

### Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/SR-npc-Forsworn_06.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Forsworn_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Forsworn_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Forsworn_06.jpg) A Briarheart is born Driven from Markarth, the Forsworn spread across the Reach fortifying any defendable spot they could find. Large tented campsites are built into reinforced redoubts from the remains of various caves and ruins. Their members sport a unique tribal garb and adopt many of the traditions depicted in [*The Legend of Red Eagle*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Legend_of_Red_Eagle). Forsworn gear is made up of a distinctive combination of fur, feathers, bone, and antlers that gives them a savage appearance. Their weapons also appear primitive, constructed of bone, wood, and stone in lieu of metal. The most archaic of their practices involves their reverence for [hagravens](Skyrim_Hagraven.md), who are treated as matriarchs in their society. The hagravens offer leadership and access to a bizarre rite through which a Forsworn may become a spirit of vengeance, known as a [Briarheart](#Forsworn_Briarhearts). The hagravens can remove a willing participant's heart and replace it with a [Briar Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Briar_Heart) that imbues vastly enhanced strength at the cost of their will and humanity.

"*Heart of thorn... bones of the wild... in life, Forsworn...
...rise from death, Blood of our Blood."*
- Briarheart Ceremony The Forsworn operate largely as a terrorist organization that attempts to strike fear into the hearts of their Nord occupiers. Each redoubt is as an individual cell operating under the orders of a Forsworn Briarheart or matriarch. The cells in turn may receive orders from Madanach, the imprisoned King in Rags. Forsworn raids are specifically aimed at non-Bretons living or passing through the Reach. They differentiate from common [bandits](Skyrim_Bandit.md) in that they will often waylay an entire caravan or settlement leaving no survivors, but taking little loot. They've refused all attempts at making peace and will accept only the return of their land as a solution to the conflict.

## Forsworn Types
Forsworn come in a number of variants. The specific variety you will meet is level-dependent, though you will continue to meet low-level versions even at high levels. Boss-level Forsworn will always be at the highest possible level, and will be identified as "Forsworn Briarhearts." In addition, Forsworn may be spellcasters, archers, or one-handed melee fighters. The Forsworn name does not normally provide information on the fighting style of the Forsworn. There are twice as many male Forsworn as females, and—aside from some of the [Forsworn Agents](#Forsworn_Agents) listed below—they are all [Breton](Skyrim_Breton.md). Forsworn Warlords are defined in the game data as a variant that is even more powerful than the Forsworn Ravager. However, warlords do not appear in any of the leveled lists that generate generic Forsworn enemies and therefore do not appear in the game. <sup>**?**</sup>

With the exception of Briarhearts, all Forsworn wear [Forsworn Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn_Armor) and [Forsworn Boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn_Boots), and may be wearing [Forsworn Gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn_Gauntlets) and a [Forsworn Headdress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Forsworn_Headdress) as well (50% chance each).

### Forsworn Shaman
Forsworn Shamans are the primary spellcasters of the Forsworn, and appear at all levels (although they are only called Shaman at level 1; at higher levels they follow the same naming pattern as other Forsworn). They carry a leveled dagger (iron, steel, or orcish) and have a random selection of other items, including potions, soul gems, ingredients, and a leveled [Destruction](Skyrim_Destruction.md) staff. All Shamans are female.

### Forsworn Briarhearts
Forsworn Briarhearts can be found wherever the Forsworn have set up camp. Briarhearts are named for the Briar Heart crudely embedded into their chest cavity and held in with leather straps. If the Briar Heart is stolen from them with the [Pickpocketing](Skyrim_Pickpocket.md) skill, they will immediately die. Briarhearts can be deadly spellcasters and melee fighters, and appear at all levels. They are also frequently scripted to have unusually keen hearing, making it difficult to sneak up on them. All Briarhearts are male.

Despite the ritual they have undergone to gain their power, Briarhearts are not classed as [undead](Skyrim_Undead.md) NPCs and are immune to undead-specific spells and receive no additional damage from silver weapons. They are also detected with [Detect Life](Skyrim_Detect_Life_(spell).md) rather than [Detect Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Dead).

There are two varieties of Forsworn Briarhearts that appear: one with the normal "Forsworn" class, and one with the "Forsworn Shaman" class. All Briarhearts wear a special variety of Forsworn Armor (which cannot be equipped by the player) in addition to Forsworn Boots and a Forsworn Headdress. All Forsworn Briarhearts are male.

The "Forsworn" class Briarhearts carry two leveled one-handed swords or axes in addition to their [Briar Hearts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Briar_Heart) and an assortment of gems, food, raw meat, recipes, potions, soul gems, and gold. These Briarhearts have the following perks:

## Forsworn Agents
Not all Forsworn traipse about in the wilderness dressed in tribal garb. A large number walk among the general population of Markarth and are indistinguishable from normal citizens. From there they are able to keep tabs on the goings-on in the city and strike terror directly into the heart of the Nord populace. While they are effectively imprisoned there, the Forsworn also control the Cidhna Mine. The mine is the heart of their leadership structure and a pivotal force in recruiting members to their cause.
