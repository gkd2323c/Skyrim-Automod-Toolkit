[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fields_of_Regret.jpg) The Fields of Regret circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The Fields of Regret are a [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Wishes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOBarbas-1)</sup> The realm appears as a tranquil countryside,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ICDL-2)</sup> dotted with majestic cities of glass and ornate buildings and populated by [Skaafin](Lore_Skaafin.md). It contains dense forests, lakes, and mountains, with floating golden metals and stone spirals filling the landscape.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The city of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) was once a part of the realm, but was eventually severed and came to rest in the [Realm of the Hist](Lore_Realm_of_the_Hist.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-4)</sup>

According to some sources, the Fields of Regret are not a place in the same sense that a place on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) is, rather the realm is a "state of mind", specifically the state of mind of Clavicus Vile, which reflects on the realm and causes everything there to be exactly as Vile wishes it to be. Thus its form is said to represent Vile's mindset, a green, sunny and pleasant land interspersed with tranquil ponds under an expanse of clear blue sky, reflecting Vile's view of himself as a playful and fun-loving god who basks alongside his companion, Barbas, the pleasant appearance concealing Vile's true temperament which is likened to that of a child cruelly playing with insects.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RGC-5)</sup>

## History

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/RG-place-Realm_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg/200px-RG-place-Realm_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-place-Realm_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-place-Realm_of_Clavicus_Vile.jpg) The realm of Clavicus Vile (Redguard) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2d/ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower.jpg) The Fields of Regret during Nocturnal's invasion (ESO) During the climax of the Daedric War in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an alliance between the Psijic Order and the princes Clavicus Vile and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) was made to stop [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) from ending reality, as she betrayed [the Triad](Lore_The_Triad.md) and obtained the Heart of Transparent Law. She now gained control over the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) and attempted to encompass all of reality in darkness, prompting [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md) to accompany the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), Earl Leythen, and Golden Knight [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md) into the Fields of Regret so they could reach the realm's version of the Crystal Tower. Upon arriving, Nocturnal had sent her forces to the Fields of Regret, and Barbas stayed behind to lead the defense of the realm as the other heroes ventured into the tower with the Resolute Anchor.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

[Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) once visited the realm in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) to bargain with Vile, who had been given the soul of his sister, [Iszara](Lore_Iszara.md), by the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md) [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md). Cyrus was made to choose between two doors, one guarded by a [Daedra](Lore_Guardian_(Clavicus_Vile).md) that always lies, and the other guarded by a Daedra that always tells the truth. Cyrus won the Prince's game and rescued his sister's soul.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Redguard-7)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), one of Vile's artifacts, the [Umbra Sword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Umbra_Sword), was returned to the realm by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) at the Prince's request. However, [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md), the Hound of Vile, knew it was a poor decision and attempted to stop its return,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup> as the sword contained a piece of the Prince's power which had gained sentience. The being, who called himself Umbra, escaped the sword once it was brought to the realm, and stole a significant amount of the Prince's power before fleeing to the edge of the realm. To prevent Umbra leaving with his power, Vile sealed the borders of the realm, essentially sealing them all within.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIC-9)</sup>

### Fourth Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/BK-Umbriel.jpg/200px-BK-Umbriel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-Umbriel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-Umbriel.jpg) Umbriel in Tamriel In the early years of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), a hole was opened into the realm by the first [ingenium](Lore_Ingenium.md), a device created by two [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named [Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Sul) and [Vuhon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Vuhon). The hole leached power from the realm, which was used to prevent [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) crashing into [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) after the disappearance of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md). In return, the mortals provided a steady stream of souls, so Vile permitted the hole to remain. Umbra was drawn to it, and, when the ingenium was destroyed by Sul, briefly breaking Vile's barrier, he threw the Umbra Sword to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to prevent the Prince trapping him within. Sul and Vuhon were also pulled into Vile's realm, where they were beset by Umbra. Vuhon struck a deal with him, and the two fused to become a being called Umbriel. A second ingenium was constructed, and Umbriel used it to rip a piece of Vile's realm away, which was set loose in the [Void](Lore_Void.md). This detached bubble of Vile's realm contained an inverted pyramid of rock, similar in appearance to a floating mountain, topped with a beautiful city and containing vast labyrinths of passages below. It also came to be called Umbriel. The city had its own population of sentient trees related to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) in Tamriel, and the ingenium transformed worm-like larvae into strange humanoids of many kinds, known as Umbriellians.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TIC-9)</sup> It was drawn into an unnamed realm of Oblivion by the Hist on the island in [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), after Umbra was drawn back into the sword.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-4)</sup>

While Umbriel invaded Tamriel, Vile's realm was left in ruin. Without access to his power, Vile's realm shrank, dissolving at the edges until it was only a few miles in radius. The Daedric Prince summoned Sul and [Prince Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) to his realm when they made contact with the unstable Umbra Sword on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), where he aided them in attempting to take down Umbra.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOS-4)</sup> It is unknown if his realm recovered after his stolen power was returned.
