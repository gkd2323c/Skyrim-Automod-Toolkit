Wylon Montclair was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman who reigned as the thirty-ninth Baron of [House Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) during the sixth century of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Following [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), Wylon served alongside the leaders of [Houses Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) as a member of the triumvirate Council of the North, which was formed to govern [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) until the selection of a new King of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EOd-3)</sup>

After Wylon's wife, Baroness Leila Montclair, became seriously ill in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Montclairs, his court magician Reezal-Jul, and Wylon's close friend, the [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md), journeyed to the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md) to retrieve the relic known as the Lightless Remnant in hopes of healing Leila. Once they reached the top of the Doomcrag, the Montclairs began performing a ritual on the Lightless Remnant, which, unbeknownst to everyone but Reezal-Jul, was corrupted. When Verandis touched the Lightless Remnant to assist the Montclairs in their ritual, this further corrupted it, turning Wylon and his daughter Lleraya into vampires, while Leila was transformed into a [bloodfiend](Lore_Bloodfiend.md). Leila then begged Verandis to kill her before she became a completely mindless bloodfiend, and Verandis killed her in an act of mercy, enraging Wylon.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup>

After Leila's death, Wylon and House Montclair initiated a civil war in Rivenspire in an attempt to conquer the region.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HRP-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lf LL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup> However, the combined forces of Houses Dorell, Tamrith, and [Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) defeated House Montclair, and he was killed at the Doomcrag.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTLR-8)</sup>

## Biography
Wylon Montclair was the son of [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md), the thirty-eighth Baron [Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md). He was the grandson of King [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md) of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) and the nephew of King [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-9)</sup> Wylon's wife was Baroness Leila Montclair, with whom he had a daughter named Lleraya.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BWM-11)</sup>

In the aftermath of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) and Ranser's death in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) fell into chaos.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-9)</sup> To restore order to Rivenspire, Baron [Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md) proposed the formation of a ruling triumvirate composed of the leaders of [Houses Dorell](Lore_House_Dorell.md), Montclair, and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md) to govern the region in the name of King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NAA-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-9)</sup> Because Emeric did not trust any of Rivenspire's nobles to be King of Shornhelm, he approved of the formation of the triumvirate Council of the North to govern the region, but he also promised to eventually select a new king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HKEd-12)</sup>

Following Ranser's War, Wylon succeeded Phylgeon as the leader of House Montclair, becoming the thirty-ninth Baron Montclair.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-9)</sup> As the leader of House Montclair, Montclair served as the house's representative on the Council of the North.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BADd-13)</sup> He was reportedly disliked by the House Tamrith representative on the Council of the North, Baron Esmark Tamrith.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CETd-14)</sup> Soon after Ranser's War, Reezal-Jul, an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) and Ranser's former court magician, offered his services to Montclair, an offer that the Baron accepted. Reezal-Jul made this offer because he wanted to manipulate Montclair and forge him into a weapon to enact Ranser's revenge.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RJJ-15)</sup>

Although Emeric promised to select a new King of Shornhelm, the Council of the North continued to rule Rivenspire during the fifteen years following Ranser's War.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-EOd-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-9)</sup> The Council of the North was plagued by constant arguing among the triumvirate,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-HKEd-12)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CSd-16)</sup> leading many people in Rivenspire, including Montclair, to believe that Shornhelm needed a new king.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-9)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CSd-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Kd-17)</sup> In his book, titled *[Shornhelm, Crown City of the North](Lore_Shornhelm,_Crown_City_of_the_North.md)*, Montclair argued that as the legitimate grandson of King Hurlburt, he was the rightful heir to the throne of Shornhelm. Additionally, in the book he claimed that the Montclair house historian found the long-missing codicil to the Bretonnick Natalitie, a document that declared "Howse Mount Clayre" the royal house of Shornhelm to further bolster his claim to the throne.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SCCo TN-9)</sup> Montclair always fancied himself the heir to Ranser's throne.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_03.jpg) Montclair standing at the bedside of Leila In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Leila became seriously ill, forcing Wylon to take a leave of absence from Shornhelm's court for a few months to tend to her needs.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HTARH-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup> Leila's sickness consumed Montclair with grief, and he ordered Reezal-Jul to mix potions in an attempt to heal her, but she eventually began refusing to drink the concoctions.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RJ-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BLM-19)</sup> Wylon then asked his close friend, the [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md), to turn the Baroness into a vampire; however, either Verandis or Leila rejected this proposal.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BWM-11)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BLM-19)</sup> Reezal-Jul then gave Verandis a shard of an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) relic, which he presented to Wylon, and he, in turn, used it to heal Leila in less than a day.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup> Leila's sickness soon returned, which Wylon blamed on Count Ravenwatch.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BWM-11)</sup> However, unbeknownst to both Wylon and Verandis, Reezal-Jul was manipulating them as part of his plan to take revenge for Ranser's fall by gaining the power of the Lightless Remnant, which was where the shard that had temporarily cured the Baroness came from.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RJJ-15)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RJ-18)</sup> In hopes of healing Leila, the Montclairs, Reezal-Jul, and Verandis journeyed to the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md) to find the Lightless Remnant.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fd/ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_08.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_08.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_08.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Dream-Walk_Into_Darkness_08.jpg) The Montclairs are transformed After ascending to the top of the Doomcrag, the Montclairs began performing a ritual on the Lightless Remnant, believing that it would heal Leila; however, it was corrupted—something that Reezal-Jul had known from the beginning. When Verandis touched the Lightless Remnant to assist the Montclairs in their ritual, this further corrupted it, turning Wylon, Lleraya, and Reezal-Jul into vampires, while Leila was transformed into a [bloodfiend](Lore_Bloodfiend.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup> Wylon also became the vessel of the Lightless Remnant, granting him immense power.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup> Leila begged Verandis to kill her before she became a completely mindless bloodfiend, and Verandis killed her in an act of mercy, enraging Wylon. He then attempted to kill Verandis using his newly gained power, but the Count's protégé, Gwendis, intervened and saved him.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DWID-4)</sup>

Following the events at the Doomcrag, Montclair, who had been driven insane by the death of his wife and the corruption of the Lightless Remnant, initiated a civil war in Rivenspire to conquer the region and become the King of Shornhelm.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HRP-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Lf LL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BOR-20)</sup> He put Reezal-Jul and Lleraya in command of House Montclair's forces.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BOR-20)</sup> The power of the Lightless Remnant gave Montclair, Lleraya, and Reezal-Jul the ability to turn people into vampires and bloodfiends, which they used to great effect during the civil war.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BOR-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LTRJ-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LO-22)</sup> Montclair also ordered some members of House Montclair to make an expedition to the [Erokii](Lore_Erokii.md) ruins near the Doomcrag to recover the Tear of Anurraame, an artifact reportedly capable of causing mass destruction. It was speculated that Montclair intended to use the Tear of Anurraame to destroy [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MJ-23)</sup>

In 2E 582, Reezal-Jul and the House Montclair forces under his command captured Shornhelm's upper city,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LTRJ-21)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Rf RJ-24)</sup> aided by treachery from two of the city's guard captains.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DGd-7)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BADd-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CETd-14)</sup> After capturing the upper city, Montclair demanded that Baron [Alard Dorell](Lore_Alard_Dorell.md) and Countess [Eselde Tamrith](Lore_Eselde_Tamrith.md), who had succeeded Baron Tamrith as House Tamrith's leader, surrender and crown him king, a demand that they rejected.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BADd-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CETd-14)</sup> Around this time, Lleraya and her troops took [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) and turned many of the city's inhabitants into bloodfiends.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BADd-13)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BQd-25)</sup> Montclair did not personally participate in the capture of either Shornhelm's upper city or Northpoint; instead, he directed House Montclair's forces from the Lightless Remnant's chamber atop the Doomcrag for the duration of the civil war.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Baron_Wylon_Montclair_02.jpg) Projection of Montclair An agent of King Emeric arrived in Shornhelm, and they, along with Verandis, assisted the forces of House Dorell, House Tamrith, and the Shornhelm Guard in retaking the upper city.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SD-26)</sup> The agent eventually tracked down and killed Reezal-Jul in Shadowfate Cavern.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TBSS-27)</sup> The agent then helped Baron Dorell and his allies infiltrate Northpoint, kill Lleraya, and liberate the city.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Ni P-28)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-TLo N-29)</sup> After the defeats of Reezal-Jul and Lleraya, the forces of Houses Dorell, Tamrith, and [Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) confronted Montclair at the Doomcrag.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTLR-8)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ESOPot P-30)</sup> There, Montclair captured Baron Dorell, Countess Tamrith, and Gwendis, imprisoning them within a blood ward, but Verandis and Emeric's agent managed to release them. The agent then fought and killed Montclair in the Lightless Remnant's chamber. When the agent killed Montclair, the Lightless Remnant began to crack and release its terrible power.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTLR-8)</sup> Fearing the devastation that the Lightless Remnant's destruction would wreak on Rivenspire, Verandis made a deal with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to transport himself and the artifact to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), saving the region.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOTLR-8)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Vera-10)</sup>
