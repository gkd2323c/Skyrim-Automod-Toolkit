[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/MW-place-Caldera.jpg/200px-MW-place-Caldera.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Caldera.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Caldera.jpg) Caldera [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7d/ON-place-Redoran_Garrison.jpg/200px-ON-place-Redoran_Garrison.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Redoran_Garrison.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Redoran_Garrison.jpg) Redoran Garrison circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Caldera was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) charter town just north of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md). It was built to service the nearby [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) mine, providing the miners with homes, entertainment, and other services. The town was ruled largely from the Caldera Mining Company's fortress to the northeast.

The buildings, which had [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) and [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) architectural influences, formed an inverse "L" shape between two large guard towers. While [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) presence in Caldera was almost nonexistent, a large contingent of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) lived in the dilapidated Ghorak Manor (along with an unusual [Scamp](Lore_Creeper.md) who was a merchant and claimed the house was his). Notable institutions in Caldera included an [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) altar, a [Mages Guild branch](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), two general merchants, a blacksmith, a tavern, and one of the few Imperial-style clothiers in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).

A **Redoran Garrison** was present at the town's future location in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), where many meetings between house councilors were held. The ebony mine, during the late Third Era, was operated on land claimed by [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), while the mine was administered by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), who had a stranglehold on all of the [East Empire Company](Lore_East_Empire_Company.md)'s business interests on Vvardenfell. This was the cause of a great deal of friction between these two great houses.
