[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/MW-place-Bthuand.jpg/200px-MW-place-Bthuand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Bthuand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Bthuand.jpg) Bthuand in 3E 427 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/ON-place-Bthuand.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bthuand.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bthuand.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bthuand.jpg) Bthuand in 2E 582 Bthuand is a [Dwarven ruin](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) located in the north-central [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), west of the Daedric shrine of [Zergonipal](Lore_Zergonipal.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MBthuand-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOBthuand-2)</sup> It was abandoned in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) when its original [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) inhabitants mysteriously disappeared.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DII-3)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Vonos Bero, a member of the Seven Secretives, came to the ruins to obtain an important clockwork device stolen from the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). This item had been taken to Bthuand, where a number of [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) were slain after attempting to retrieve it. The device was shattered when Vonos got into an argument with the smuggler that led into a brawl. Vonos had been followed to the ruins by a [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) assassin named [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md), who executed him soon afterwards.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VJ-4)</sup> As of 2E 582, the ruins of Bthuand were guarded by [Dwarven Centurions](Lore_Dwarven_Centurion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOBthuand-2)</sup>

By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the ruins were still guarded by Dwarven Centurions. [Ghosts](Lore_Dwarven_Spectre.md) of the long-gone Dwemer could be encountered there as well.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MBthuand-1)</sup> Mastrius, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) who had been imprisoned for centuries in the Salvel Ancestral Tomb near [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) by [Azura](Lore_Azura.md), required the Daedric artifact [Spellbreaker](Lore_Spellbreaker.md) to break the curse and escape the tomb. Circa 3E 427, the weakened Mastrius was discovered by [a fellow vampire](Lore_Nerevarine.md) who agreed to help free him. At this time, Spellbreaker could be found within Bthuand, next to the skeleton of its previous owner who had died in a cave-in. Mastrius used the shield as a conduit for his spell and broke Azura's curse, returning to full strength. He then betrayed his liberator and was slain, allowing his accomplice to claim Spellbreaker.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWTIM-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Mastrius-6)</sup>

## Notes
- The Dwarven author and tonal architect [Bthuand Mzahnch](Lore_Bthuand_Mzahnch.md) shares his first name with the name of this ruin.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-BD-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TEOT-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-YB-9)</sup> His surname name was also a name of another Dwemeri complex, [Mzahnch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mzahnch).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Mzahnch Morrowind-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mzahnch ESO-11)</sup>
