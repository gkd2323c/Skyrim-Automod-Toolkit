[Jaciel Morgen](Lore_Jaciel_Morgen.md) is a [Nocturnal Shrike](Lore_Nocturnal_Shrike.md) and lieutenant of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) responsible for ruling over the [Shade Perilous](Lore_Shade_Perilous.md), a pocket realm of the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md). #### Archmage Jaganvir (fl. [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864))
[Jaganvir](Lore_Jaganvir.md) was the Archmage of the [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) following the death of [Archmage Voa](Lore_Voa.md) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) at the [Battle of Hunding Bay](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md). #### Jagar Tharn (a.k.a. Nightingale) (?b - [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399))
[Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) ([/ˈ dʒeɪɡɑɹ θɑɹn/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) ***jay** -gar tharn* Link ⏵) was the [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md) and later Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He secretly imprisoned Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and used [Illusion](Lore_Illusion.md) magic to masquerade as Uriel VII for a decade. This period, from [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389) to [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), became known as the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), during which Tharn reigned over the war-torn [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). It is not entirely known what Tharn's goals and personal accomplishments were during his reign.

#### Saint Jahn (fl. before [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
**Saint** [Jahn](Lore_Jahn.md) was an [Imperial saint](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) known in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). He is the namesake of [St. Jahn's Wort](Lore_St._Jahn%27s_Wort.md), a [plant](Lore_Flora.md) whose [nectar](Lore_St._Jahn%27s_Wort_Nectar.md) was known for its [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) properties, commonly found in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md), [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), and [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) regions of Cyrodiil. Little is known about Jahn, except that the plant's name was already established by [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).

It is believed that saints are granted [second sight](Lore_Sight.md) by the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md), allowing them to perceive the world in a way that differs from other mortals. Many of the Imperial saints were typically interred along the [Green Emperor Way](Lore_Green_Emperor_Way.md) among other prominent figures of [the Empire](Lore_The_Empire.md). However, it is unknown whether Jahn possessed the sight or was ever buried there.

#### Jakben, Earl of Imbel (a.k.a. Springheel Jak) (?b - [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433))
[Jakben](Lore_Jakben.md) was the **Earl of Imbel**, a noble family dating back to the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). He was born some time in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). #### Count Janus Hassildor (fl. 3E 433)
**[Janus Hassildor](Lore_Janus_Hassildor.md)** was an Imperial [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) who ruled over the county of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Most of the people in Skingrad seem to have believed that Count Hassildor was eccentric or crowd shy, as he was rarely seen in public. He was also reputed to be a very powerful wizard. His policies made him a well-respected ruler despite his private nature, with crime and public drunkenness being virtually unknown under his tenure. Although he was a vampire himself, he disliked the company of the others of his kind, claiming that they had given in to their animal urges. Indeed, other vampires were a problem for Hassildor, as they often took up residence around Skingrad. This in turn attracted many Vampire Hunters who often asked unwanted questions. Janus' wife, [Rona Hassildor](Lore_Rona_Hassildor.md), was also a vampire, however not being able to come to terms with her condition thus fell into a coma. This grieved the Count and so he enlisted the help of a stranger to find a cure for his wife.

#### Queen Jezerei (fl. before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
**Queen** [Jezerei](Lore_Jezerei.md), also known as the **Strumpet Queen**, was the ruler of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) during an unknown period of history, at some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). While details about her reign are sparse, some accounts depict her as a ruthless leader notorious for her brutal deeds and bloody crimes. She commissioned several extravagant Diamond-Tooth Cat Combs, which were jeweled combs crafted for her.

Long after her reign,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> in 2E 582 her combs were among the curios that could be found around [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

During that period, High Priest Zuladr of the High Temple of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) invoked Queen Jezerei’s name in his biased sermons, aimed at disparaging women. He cited her alleged bloody crimes, referring to them alongside the *Tale of the Temptress Shakhari* and various feminine creatures like [Hags](Lore_Hag.md), [Harpies](Lore_Harpy.md), and [Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md). This account was recorded by the Unveiled [Azadiyeh](Lore_Azadiyeh.md), Songbird of Satakalaam, a [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md) historian who opposed Zuladr's views.

#### Gentleman Jim Stacey (fl. 3E 427)
[Gentleman Jim Stacey](Lore_Gentleman_Jim_Stacey.md) was a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) who led the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Jim was a master thief himself, and is rumored to have possessed the legendary [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md). Under Jim's leadership, the Guild managed to establish itself despite resistance from older, native criminal groups, and focused on thieving only from wealthy royalty and merchants rather than the poor. Since the locals disfavored foreign organizations, he recreated the [Bal Molagmer](Lore_Bal_Molagmer.md), a popular but extinct group of righteous thieves in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), as a subfaction of the Thieves Guild.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind-3)</sup> His fate is not known; while the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) and [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md) claimed to have eliminated his organization, some suspect they actually just drove it deeper underground.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MOM-4)</sup>

#### Mane Jinninji-ri (fl. 1E 2902)
[Jinninji-ri](Lore_Jinninji-ri.md) was the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) of the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) people during the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), before the unification of the Sixteen Kingdoms. Little is known about Jinninji-ri's life. He was responsible for the installation of reflectors in the Temple of the Dance in [Dune](Lore_Dune.md) during one of the many temple refurbishings circa [1E 2902](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2902). When aligned properly and attuned to the moonlight, those reflectors were capable of projecting visions of past events.

He died at some point before the ascension of Mane [Zebiden-jo](Lore_Zebiden-jo.md) in circa [2E 182](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_182). His remains are preserved in the Tomb of the Manes in [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md) Necropolis, a huge tomb complex located southwest of the Rimmen itself. He was buried together with other prominent Manes: Zebiden-jo and [Tullar-dra](Lore_Tullar-dra.md). His spirit's rest was briefly interrupted in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) when [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) escorted the remains of the ancient Khajiiti ruler Jora-ri to the burial chamber in the Tomb of the Manes.

#### Joorahmaar (Dawn Era - [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582))
[Joorahmaar](Lore_Joorahmaar.md) is a [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) whose name means "Mortal Hunter Terror" in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md).

Little is known of Joorahmaar's past. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), together with [Paarbahlot](Lore_Paarbahlot.md), he ventured to the New Moon Fortress in [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) to meet with [Laatvulon](Lore_Laatvulon.md). During the meeting, Laatvulon promised power to both Joorahmaar and Paarbahlot, and ordered the recruits of the [Order of the New Moon](Lore_Order_of_the_New_Moon.md) to offer them their power with the use of the [aeonstone](Lore_Aeonstone.md). The ritual was interrupted by the members of [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md)'s reformed [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md). Following the disruption of the ritual, Paarbahlot, together with Joorahmaar, left the fortress of the New Moon Cult.

Although Paarbahlot's allegiances and status as of then were unknown, Joorahmaar was later seen assisting Laatvulon in the [Dragonhold](Lore_Dragonhold.md). He was ordering the cultists of the New Moon to continue to feed the ritual sites for Laatvulon. [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) wounded the dragon and later a [member](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Dragonguard killed him. As his soul was not devoured by another dovah, Joorahmaar was not killed permanently and can be raised again.

The data about the dragon Joorahmaar, documented within [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md) was shrouded in ambiguity. Various accounts describe conflicting details about the dragon's abilities, with some stating that Joorahmaar breathed fire, while others claim that he emitted clouds of poisonous smoke.

#### Jorunn the Skald-King ([2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546) - ?d)
[Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) High King born to Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) in [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546). The twin brother of [Fildgor](Lore_Fildgor.md) Strong-Prince, Jorunn grew up believing their elder sister [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md) was fated to take the throne, and as such became a bard and did quite a bit of traveling. He showed rare talent as a bard, highly prized among Nords, and was eventually dubbed the "Skald Prince" of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). However, his mother and sister were killed during the second [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) invasion. He led the Nords to victory against the invaders, and took on a new life as High King of [Eastern Skyrim](Lore_Eastern_Skyrim.md). Jorunn was known for his jovial nature and regarded as a proper, true Nord, showing little interest in courtly protocol and requiring no elaborate displays of obeisance from his subjects. He was also a skilled musician and bard of rare talent, as well as an admirably capable fighter with a blade. With an impressively full beard, he was a particularly large and strong man, though also noticeably broad, heavyset, and portly. King's weapon of choice was his battleaxe, [Suungir](Lore_Suungir.md), a legendary item in its own right.

#### Julius Primus (fl. [4E 28](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_28))

#### Jurgen Windcaller, The Calm (fl. early 1E)
[Jurgen Windcaller](Lore_Jurgen_Windcaller.md) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) warrior who came to be called **Jurgen the Calm**, believed to have had the strongest [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and possibly of all time. As a militant thu'um wielder, or "Tongue", he utilized his voice as a weapon and a way to grow in strength, like many Tongues of the time. This changed for Jurgen, however, when the Nordic armies suffered an ignominious defeat at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). Jurgen would use the defeat as inspiration to discover the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md) and found the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), wielders of the voice who practiced fierce pacifism.

#### Justia Desticus (fl. 2E 432)
[Justia Desticus](Lore_Justia_Desticus.md) was the original First Sword of the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md), a position given to the chief warrior of the order. When [Cavor Merula](Lore_Cavor_Merula.md) re-established the Order of the Hour in [2E 432](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_432), she stood by his side and devised the Rigors of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), eight traits to which all members of the Order must aspire. She was considered the second in devotion in Akatosh's eyes.

A statue to Justia was later made in Knightsgrave to her memory as one of the Order's founding members. [Lyra Viria](Lore_The_Black_Dragon.md) would later take the mantle of First Sword, and felt a connection to Justia when she traveled to Knightsgrave, curious if she would be proud of her taking up her role.
