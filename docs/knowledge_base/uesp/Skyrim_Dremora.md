# Dremora


The other way to encounter Dremora in Skyrim is by summoning one. There are a few ways to summon Dremora:

- The [Sanguine Rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine_Rose), a reward from the quest [A Night To Remember](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Night_To_Remember), will summon a leveled melee fighter Dremora for 60 seconds, or 120 seconds with the appropriate Conjuration perks.
- The [Conjuration](Skyrim_Conjuration.md) spell [Conjure Dremora Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Dremora_Lord) will summon a [Dremora Lord](#Dremora_Lord) for 60 seconds, or 120 seconds with the appropriate Conjuration perks.
- The [Atronach Forge](Skyrim_Atronach_Forge.md) can be used to summon hostile Dremora, which will obviously attack anybody or anything on sight.
- The [Conjuration Ritual Spell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Conjuration_Ritual_Spell) quest involves summoning an [Unbound Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Unbound_Dremora); this Dremora cannot be summoned anywhere other than the roof of the [Hall of Attainment](Skyrim_Hall_of_Attainment.md). Once again, this will be automatically hostile to everything it encounters.
- The [Wabbajack](Skyrim_Wabbajack.md) has a chance to momentarily turn the target into a hostile Dremora.

## Dremora Types
There are three types of Dremora, based on their preferred fighting style: melee fighters, archers, and warlocks. All Dremora are [NPCs](Skyrim_NPCs.md) with [black souls](Skyrim_Souls.md). All types of Dremora always drop a [Daedra Heart](Skyrim_Daedra_Heart.md) on death; they're one of the few guaranteed sources of this rare ingredient.

### Melee Fighters
These Dremora belong to the [Dremora](Skyrim_Dremora_(class).md) NPC [class](Skyrim_Classes.md) and use one or two-handed weapons in battle. They wear a [unique version](Skyrim_Specialty_Gear.md#Dremora_Equipment) of [Daedric Armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Daedric_Armor) available only to Dremora and wield a leveled [mace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mace), [sword](Skyrim_Sword.md), [war axe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:War_Axe), [warhammer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword), [greatsword](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Greatsword), or [battleaxe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Battleaxe), all of which are enchanted with [fire damage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fire_Damage). The type of enchantment depends on the quality of the material, as follows:

- **[Iron](Skyrim_Iron.md) or [steel](Skyrim_Steel.md):** [of Embers](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#of_Embers), [of Burning](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#of_Burning), [of Scorching](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#of_Scorching)
- **[Glass](Skyrim_Malachite.md):** of Scorching, [of Fire](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#of_Fire), [of the Blaze](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#of_the_Blaze)
- **[Ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md) or [Daedric](Skyrim_Daedric.md):** of Fire, of the Blaze, [of the Inferno](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#of_the_Inferno)

They also carry an [iron dagger](Skyrim_Iron_Dagger.md) and will drop a Daedra Heart on death.

### Archers
These Dremora have the [Bandit](Skyrim_Bandit_(class).md) NPC class and wield fire-enchanted bows to fight from a distance. They wear the same unique Daedric Armor as their melee counterparts and use one of the following enchanted bows:

- **[Hunting Bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hunting_Bow):** of Embers, of Burning, of Scorching
- **[Glass Bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Glass_Bow):** of Scorching, of Fire, of the Blaze
- **[Ebony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ebony_Bow) or [Daedric Bow](Skyrim_Daedric.md):** of Fire, of the Blaze, of the Inferno

They also carry an iron dagger, one iron arrow, and a quiver of up to 20 [iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Iron_Arrow), [steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Arrow), [orcish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Orcish_Arrow), [dwarven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dwarven_Arrow), or [elven arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Arrow), depending on their level.

### Warlocks
Dremora warlocks are members of the [Destruction Mage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Combat Mage Destruction) NPC class and prefer to use spells in combat. They wear unique [black robes and boots](Skyrim_Specialty_Gear.md#Dremora_Equipment). In case they are forced into melee combat, they carry an iron dagger and a mace, sword, war axe, warhammer, greatsword, or battleaxe (any quality from iron to elven).

### Summonable Dremora Lord
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dremora_Lord.jpg) A Dremora Lord A Dremora Lord can be summoned using either [Conjure Dremora Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Dremora_Lord), an Expert level Conjuration spell or the [Sanguine Rose](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine_Rose). The Dremora Lord is a Dremora Valynaz, a melee fighter equipped with the standard unique Daedric armor that wields a [Daedric Greatsword of the Inferno](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#of_the_Inferno).

The Dremora Lord has 545 health and 230 stamina. [Dragon Priests](Skyrim_Undead.md#Dragon_Priests) cannot take control of conjured Dremora Lords. Furthermore, while the Dremora Lord is technically not an [Atronach](Skyrim_Daedra.md), it is affected by the [Summoner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Summoner) and [Atromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Atromancy) Conjuration perks, although it is **not** affected by [Elemental Potency](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elemental_Potency). The [Twin Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Twin_Souls) perk also applies to Dremora Lords, making it possible to have both a Dremora Lord and an [atronach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Atronach) or [thrall](Skyrim_Thrall.md), or two Dremora Lords. Unlike other NPC Dremoras, Dremora Lords cannot be [pickpocketed](Skyrim_Pickpocket.md) for weapons or armor, and they will not drop Daedra Hearts because their bodies disappear upon death. They are also immune to [all Soul Trap effects](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md).

## Notes
- In the [Creation Kit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Creation_Kit) they are counted as a separate race, similar to how [children](Skyrim_Child.md) and the [elders](Skyrim_Elder.md) are treated.
- Game data reveals that all Dremora Race NPCs are modeled directly on a [Dark Elf](Skyrim_Dark_Elf.md) character model, with the only changes being: horns, their own unique skin tone, their own unique complexion (red facial markings), and their in-game height being equal to that of the [High Elf](Skyrim_High_Elf.md).
- Archer Dremora were given Nord voices rather than the voices given to Melee and Warlock Dremora.
- The Daedric Prince [Sanguine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sanguine) appears in the form of a Dremora.
- Several NPCs are [classified](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Dremora_Class) in the game data as Dremora.
- Seven of the Dremora dialogue lines appear in the book *[I Was Summoned by a Mortal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:I_was_Summoned_by_a_Mortal)* in [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online).

## Bugs
- Although there is an Archer class for Dremora, they do not appear in the game; only the melee fighters and mages can be encountered.
- Dremora may not voice their death quotes when they die; only pained grunts or yells are heard, instead.
- Reanimated Dremora may equip invisible items in their hands if they were placed on the corpse before reanimation took place. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.1.1, fixes this bug.
