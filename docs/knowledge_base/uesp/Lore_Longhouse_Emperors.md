# Longhouse Emperors


[Moricar the Middling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moricar),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWo S-6)</sup> Durcorach's son, succeeded him at the age of twenty three.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Mt CCT-7)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup> Moricar had been born and raised in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) like his father, but had spent his teenage years being educated by Imperial tutors and was said to better understand Cyrodilic culture.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Awt LE-8)</sup> In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563), Moricar launched an invasion of Western Skyrim.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup> Accounts of the campaign differ. In one, Moricar remained in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) rather than lead the army as his father would have. This decision likely spared his life, as his forces were humiliatingly routed in a single battle by the army of High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md), but he was consequently lambasted as a coward by Western Skyrim's bards.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWo S-6)</sup> In another account, Moricar led his troops to the gates of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) before Svargrim's army attacked in overwhelming numbers. Moricar was gravely wounded in the battle and returned to Cyrodiil, where, despite the best efforts of Imperial and Reach healers, he died the following year.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup>

In [2E 564](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_564),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup> Moricar's son [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic) inherited the Ruby Throne. Like his grandfather, Leovic married a Tharn, [Clivia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Clivia_Tharn).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTFC4-4)</sup> Leovic's reign was initially only marked by his peculiar behaviors, which the Cyrodilic nobility blamed on his Reachman upbringing.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTFC4-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EFEV-9)</sup> However, in [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), Leovic crossed the line when he legalized [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship, allegedly at the behest of corrupt advisors, drawing the ire of many nobles who had once been counted among his supporters.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EFEV-9)</sup> One of these erstwhile supporters, Duke [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) of [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol), marshaled the armies of the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) and led them against Leovic in open rebellion from 2E 576 to [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-10)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SHot LE-2)</sup> In 2E 577, Leovic was killed by Varen in the Imperial Throne Room, bringing an end to the period of the Longhouse Emperors.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-COTFC1-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-COTFC4-4)</sup> Durcorach's line was survived by his kin in the Reach, the [Blackdrake Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackdrake_Clan), and after Leovic's demise, the clan's chieftainship was claimed by [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), a cousin who had been installed as the Imperial Governor of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) by Moricar.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Rot Do M-11)</sup>

One triumph of the dynasty's reign was the opportunistic annexation of [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) and [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md) in northern [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). This occurred during the early years of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) outbreak, which had hit the Khajiit particularly hard. The Khajiit attempted to retake both cities from the Imperials, but were unsuccessful.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONKhali-12)</sup> Under the rule of the Longhouse Emperors, Cyrodiil also controlled land as far as the [Wrothgarian Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wrothgarian_Mountains).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Reachmen Wrothgar-13)</sup>

Longhouse Emperors used crests with antler imagery.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONContraband L-14)</sup>
