## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-place-Sarandas%27_House.jpg/200px-ON-place-Sarandas%27_House.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sarandas%27_House.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Sarandas%27_House.jpg) Sarandas family's house
### Second Era
Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Hla Oad was not yet a town, though the humble fishing shack of Sarandas' family stood at the site of the future settlement.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSS-5)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/79/MW-book-Balmora_Region.jpg/200px-MW-book-Balmora_Region.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Balmora_Region.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-book-Balmora_Region.jpg) A map depicting Hla Oad Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), it was an active settlement and it offered few services.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hla_Oad-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GDS-6)</sup> The [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md) criminal organization wielded considerable influence within the settlement.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GDS-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GDSO-7)</sup> Among the few services available were independent operators such as Perien Aurelie the pawnbroker and Pallia Ceno, as well as [Camonna Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cammona_Tong) affiliated businesses that offered their services only to their own people.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GDS-6)</sup> At the time, Fatleg's Drop-Off was the only notable tradehouse in the settlement, and it was owned by the Redguard Trasteve.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GDSO-7)</sup>

Along with other settlements of the Bitter Coast, also known as the Smugglers Coast, the region was defined by its secluded coves and small islands that offered ideal refuge for illicit trade, and it was widely rumored to be a major stopover for smugglers,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SI-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GDB-9)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SI-8)</sup> who were known to occasionally sell goods in the settlement.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-GDG-10)</sup> Some of these smuggling operations were particularly alarming, including those involving Ash Statues and activities that furthered the goals of the [Sixth House](Lore_House_Dagoth.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CC-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VTO-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-VTOQ-13)</sup>

It was a fairly isolated settlement, though a rough track along the Odai River connected it to Balmora. Reaching [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md) required a long and unpleasant walk south along the coast.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GDH-2)</sup> Ships from both the Foreign Quarter docks in [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md) and from [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md) regularly traveled to Hla Oad.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-GTV-14)</sup> The local shipmaster, Baleni Salavel, arranged travel from the settlement to other destinations.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GDSO-7)</sup> Ships departing from Hla Oad regularly sailed to [Gnaar Mok](Lore_Gnaar_Mok.md) and Ebonheart.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-GDH-2)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GDSO-7)</sup>

That same year, members of the Camonna Tong sought to smuggle [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md) to Balmora using a [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) slave who was forced to swallow the ilicit goods for the time of transportation. They asked a traveler to deliver her there. It is unknown whether their scheme succeeded and the moon sugar was delivered to its destination, or whether the traveler instead helped the woman escape from captivity, taking her to the Argonian Mission and placing her under the care of [Im-Kilaya](Lore_Im-Kilaya.md) of the [Twin Lamps](Lore_Twin_Lamps.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RIB-15)</sup>

Like much of Vvardenfell, its fate is unclear, as it may have been destroyed during the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-OTGC-16)</sup>

## Notes
- Hla Oad was a place of residence of an "Officer of Paleonumerology", an author of *[Shezarr and the Eight Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Shezarr_and_the_Eight_Divines)*.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-17)</sup>
