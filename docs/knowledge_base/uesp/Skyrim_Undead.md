# Undead

| --- |

**Undead Creatures** consist of spirits, reanimated skeletons, and reanimated corpses. [Vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md) are also considered to be undead, but are undead [NPCs](Skyrim_NPCs.md) instead of creatures. All varieties of Undead are detected using [Detect Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Detect_Dead) instead of [Detect Life](Skyrim_Detect_Life_(spell).md). Undead creatures are generally extra vulnerable to soul trapping, silver weapons and fire. The [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on makes undead creatures vulnerable to sunlight damage as well, but also adds the ability to heal them.

## Ayleid Lich<sup>[CC](Skyrim_The_Cause.md)</sup>
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c7/SR-creature-Norion_the_Undying.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Norion_the_Undying.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Norion_the_Undying.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Norion_the_Undying.jpg) An Ayleid Lich **[Ayleid Liches](Skyrim_Ayleid_Lich.md)** are powerful undead sorcerers, who guard Ayleid Ruins long after the living Wild Elves were overthrown and their presence faded from Tamriel. [The Cause](Skyrim_The_Cause.md) adds Ayleid Liches as both an enemy type and a [conjurable summon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Ayleid_Lich). The one enemy Ayleid Lich encountered, [Norion the Undying](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Norion_the_Undying), slumbers in his coffin flanked by four minions until intruders approach. Like Dragon Priests, he will blast the lid off his sarcophagus in rage and attack.

This is a summary of information on Ayleid Liches. See the main [Ayleid Lich](Skyrim_Ayleid_Lich.md) article for more detailed information.

## Dragon Priest
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Dragon_Priest.jpg) A dragon priest **[Dragon Priests](Skyrim_Dragon_Priest.md)** were once the primary servants of the ancient dragons of Skyrim and would rule over countless armies of men in their god's name. They are found residing in coffins in areas of prominent dragon worship, particularly [Nordic ruins](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md). If their resting place is disturbed by trespassers, the priest bursts out of its coffin (with considerably more force than a draugr's) and will proceed to attack intruders.

## Draugr
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/SR-creature-Draugr.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Draugr.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Draugr.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Draugr.jpg) A Draugr **[Draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md)** are ancient, long dead Nordic warriors who rise again in an undead state. They are mostly found in [barrows and tombs](Skyrim_Nordic_Ruins.md), and rise from catacombs or burst out of sarcophagi to attack when you walk within their vicinity, or when certain items and events are triggered/interacted with. For information about dead, static corpses of draugr which can be looted, see [this page](Skyrim_Containers.md#Draugr).

This is a summary of information on draugr. See the main [Draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md) article for more detailed information.

## Ghost
This section is about the enemy type. For the Dwarven spectre from Ghosts of the Tribunal, see [Ghost (Ghosts of the Tribunal)](Skyrim_Ghost_(Ghosts_of_the_Tribunal).md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/SR-creature-Ghost.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Ghost.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Ghost.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Ghost.jpg) A Ghost **Ghosts** are the incorporeal, restless spirits of the dead citizens of Skyrim. Their equipment varies and although they are usually poorly armed, they may prove to be a threat to adventurers early on due to their resistance to non-magical weaponry. Though a ghost has no physical body, it can still be affected by shouts and finishing moves, including decapitation. As they are considered NPCs, you'll need a [black soul gem](Skyrim_Black_Soul_Gem.md) to [trap their soul](Skyrim_Soul_Trap_(effect).md).

## Skeleton
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/40/SR-creature-Skeleton.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Skeleton.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Skeleton.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Skeleton.jpg) A Skeleton **[Skeletons](Skyrim_Skeleton.md)** are the reanimated bones of the dead. They are often found with draugr in crypts and ruins, and may also be summoned by necromancers in combat. They are fully resistant to poison and paralysis and have a 25% resistance to frost, but, given their low health level and poor equipment, they are vulnerable to other types of attack, making them relatively easy opponents.

This is a summary of information on skeletons. See the main [Skeleton](Skyrim_Skeleton.md) article for more detailed information.

### Skeleton Variants
- **[Corrupted Shade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corrupted_Shade)** — A variety of skeleton which inhabit [Meridia's Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kilkreath_Ruins). They have been created by [Malkoran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Malkoran) from the casualties of the ongoing [war](Skyrim_Civil_War.md).
- **[Boneman](Skyrim_Boneman.md)**<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> — A variety of skeleton archer which inhabit the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md). You can summon them after learning [Conjure Boneman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Boneman#Conjure_Boneman) from a spell tome in the Soul Cairn.
- **[Mistman](Skyrim_Mistman.md)**<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> — Floating, legless, black skeletons found in the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md). You can summon them after learning [Conjure Mistman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Mistman) from a spell tome in the Soul Cairn.
- **[Wrathman](Skyrim_Wrathman.md)**<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup> — Black skeleton warriors found in the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md). You can summon them after learning [Conjure Wrathman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Conjure_Wrathman) from a spell tome in the Soul Cairn.

## Wight<sup>[CC](Skyrim_The_Cause.md)</sup>
**[Wights](Skyrim_Wight.md)** are Ayleid undead found in Ayleid Ruins which, similar to Draugr, guard their tombs from intruders. Unlike Draugr, their weapons and armor are better-preserved and they do not use the Thu'um against opponents. However, like Draugr, they will rise from their sarcophagi when enemies are near, although some Wights actively keep watch for intruders. [The Cause](Skyrim_The_Cause.md) adds Wights and [Wight Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wight_Lord) along with the [Ayleid ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Ruins) they inhabit. The only such ruin accessible from Skyrim is [Rielle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rielle), which straddles the border between Skyrim and Cyrodiil beneath the Jerall Mountains.

This is a summary of information on Wights. See the main [Wight](Skyrim_Wight.md) article for more detailed information.

## Zombie
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/SR-npc-Zombie.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Zombie.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Zombie.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Zombie.jpg) A Redguard Zombie **Zombies** are creatures or NPCs that have died and been temporarily resurrected as combat allies. They are created by use of a [Reanimate](Skyrim_Reanimate.md) spell or enchantment, usually by [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md), [necromancers](Skyrim_Necromancer.md), or even you. For more details about using zombies as allies, see [this page](Skyrim_Reanimate.md).

This is a summary of information on zombies. See the main [Zombie](Skyrim_Zombie.md) article for more detailed information.

### Plague of the Dead<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Plague_of_the_Dead.md)</sup>
[Plague of the Dead](Skyrim_Plague_of_the_Dead.md) adds a new **[Zombie](Skyrim_Zombie.md#Plague_of_the_Dead)** creature. They drop an ingredient called [Mort Flesh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mort_Flesh). Zombie hordes can be encountered randomly as a [world interaction](Skyrim_World_Interactions.md) or as part of the related quest, [The Rising Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Rising_Dead). See the main [Zombie](Skyrim_Zombie.md#Plague_of_the_Dead) article for more detailed information.
