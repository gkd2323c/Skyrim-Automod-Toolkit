## Types of Gods
Generally, there are several distinct groups into which the various gods fall:

- Many versions of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md), the two primal forces, also known as principles of the cosmos. These two entities are recognized under various names in most faiths and universally their interplay is known to be the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). [Nir](Lore_Nir.md) is also included with them in some myths, as a personification of Aurbis.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vo Fi T-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAA-2)</sup>
- The et'Ada are the "original spirits",<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> a race of divine beings resulting from the interplay of Anu and Padomay, including: - The [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) combine both Anuic and Padomaic basis;<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAA-2)</sup> these are the gods who helped to create [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup> - The [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md), a term used to refer to two groups of Aedric spirits, either a group of et'Ada who decided not to abandon Mundus, and instead sacrificed themselves to become the laws of nature that would keep it viable, typically referred to as "Earthbones".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BTAM-4)</sup> Or a group who remained in the mortal realm and populated it, becoming the progenitors of the modern races, typically referred to as "Ehlnofey".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BTAM-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mystery-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-6)</sup>
- The [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) have a Padomaic basis;<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAA-2)</sup> these are the gods who refused to help create Mundus and created their own [Planes](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md#Daedric_Princes.27_Realms) in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) filled with [lesser Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md#Lesser_Daedra).
- [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md) have an Anuic basis;<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TAA-2)</sup> these are the gods backed out of helping create Mundus and fled into [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) after [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_(god).md), creating the stars while Magnus created the sun. - The Nine Coruscations are a group of Magna Ge that followed the parabolas that led away from Magnus, and in so doing chose to let their light continue to shine upon mortals, still interacting with Mundus.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-9C-7)</sup>

- Other lesser divine spirits, known as [Ada](Lore_Ada.md), such as [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) and [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) exist and wander creation.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOP6-8)</sup> - [Demigods](Lore_Demigod.md) and [Demiprinces](Lore_Demiprince.md), the offspring of Aedra and Daedra, are often classified as Ada.
- On occasion, a mortal, such as [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) and the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), will achieve apotheosis, becoming a god, or will be worshipped as a god, such as [Reman](Lore_Reman.md).

## Cultural Worship
In Tamriel, cultural pantheons of certain provinces have different gods that they worship, though some gods are worshipped in several areas.

- In [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), worship of the Alessian pantheon of the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) is common, especially among the native [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md). - In [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), the imperials called the Colovians are resolutely, and religiously, Nordic, and revere some gods by Nordic names, such as Shor.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SATD-9)</sup> In [Nibenay,](Lore_Nibenay.md) the imperials called the Nibenese worship the traditional Divines alongside a tradition of ancestor worship and reverence for god-animals,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1C-10)</sup> including a countless number of cults dedicated to a vast host of saints and holy spirits.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vo Fi T-1)</sup>
- In [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), a tradition of [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) persists, with primary focus on the Daedric Princes and the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) among the native [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md). - In the [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md) of Vvardenfell, and other regions, the [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) reject worship of the Tribunal, lending reverence only to the Daedric Princes. With the turn of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), these beliefs become dominant among the Dunmer in Morrowind, with worship of the Tribunal phasing out for worship of the Daedric Princes.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRE-11)</sup>
- In [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), worship of the old Nordic pantheon still persists in some Holds, while others adopt a modern hybrid pantheon of Nordic and Imperial belief. - In [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), the [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) revere the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md) over the traditional Nordic or modern Imperial pantheon.
- In [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), worship of the traditional Yokudan pantheon still persists among the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md), while others, such as the [Forebears](Lore_Forebear.md), adopt a modern hybrid pantheon of Yokudan and Imperial belief.
- In [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), worship of a hybrid of the Imperial and Elvish pantheons of spirits is common among the native [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md).
- In the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), worship of the Aldmeri pantheon is common among the native [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md). - On [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md), the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) continue worship of the Old Ways, keeping with a tradition of ancestor worship.
- In [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), worship of a hybrid of the Imperial and Aldmeri pantheons of spirits is common among the native [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Vo Fi T-1)</sup> Primary emphasis among the Bosmer is placed on worship of the spirit [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md).
- In [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), worship of the traditional Khajiit pantheon, a mix of Aedric and Daedric spirits, still persists among the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md).
