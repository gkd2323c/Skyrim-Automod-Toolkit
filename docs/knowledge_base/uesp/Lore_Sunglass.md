# Malachite

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/ON-misc-Glass.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Glass.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Glass.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Glass.jpg) Glass crystals Malachite, commonly known as **glass**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> (and not to be confused with [common glass](Lore_Glass_(common).md)), is a rare,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWGaro-2)</sup> volcanic, milky translucent green [crystal](Lore_Crystals.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWGDR-3)</sup> that absorbs magicka under the right conditions.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> It is most often used in creating strong, yet lightweight metallic armor and [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RM1-5)</sup> The material is similar to [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md), but is less durable and more lightweight.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWGDR-3)</sup> It bears no relation to the common, brittle glass used in windows and ornaments, as it is far stronger and has a much higher melting point.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAF-6)</sup> It is most commonly found in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) on the volcanic island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), but ore veins can also be found all across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

The matrix of glass is a very hard stone, and separating raw glass from the matrix is hard work. Nevertheless, raw glass mining is considered an honest job that pays well.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The [Dwemer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Dwemer) mined raw glass<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> and were known to make use of [vitrine](Lore_Vitrine.md), a type of Dwemeri Glass used for constructing certain [vamidiums](Lore_Vamidium.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VDW-10)</sup>

The knowledge of using glass and moonstone to craft elven armor started with the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) in [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), and was once a closely guarded secret on the isle, but a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) slave Ulvul Llaren, who worked under the isle's master smith escaped and as an act of revenge, revealed the secrets of its creation to the rest of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). Thanks to his contribution, it is now known that glass armor is made using rare metals such as [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) in order to give it the right strength,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAF-6)</sup> and is studded with malachite to create an armor stronger than steel due to its ability to absorb and distribute shock.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> The use of malachite among non-Altmer, however, predates Llaren's contributions, both among the other mer<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BASV-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CMBA-13)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VDW-10)</sup> and men.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOContraband2-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOContraband3-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOContraband4-16)</sup> Glass armor is considered the pinnacle of masterful Dunmeri light armor design.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Codex-17)</sup> Similarly, Elven-made glass weapons feature extravagant use of rare metals and cutting edges made from crystalline volcanic glass to produce blades of virtually unparalleled sharpness and light weight.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Codex-17)</sup> Glass weapons wear down quickly, however, and require frequent repair.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MRGlass-18)</sup>

Raw glass has also been used in alchemy.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MRG-19)</sup> Malachite ore must be smelted down into ingots of refined malachite before it can be used in smithing, and even then only skilled glass smiths can work with the material. Hammer blows struck across the grain run the risk of shattering the armor.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LAF-6)</sup> Malachite mining is very profitable. In Morrowind, the [East Empire Company](Lore_East_Empire_Company.md) had sole authority to trade in raw glass during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-BMCM-20)</sup> By then, Imperial law had declared raw glass the property of the Emperor, and smuggling it was considered treason, punishable by death. However, Morrowind's raw glass belonged to the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) before this arrangement.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> No such limits existed in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) by the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), and settlements such as [Kynesgrove](Lore_Kynesgrove.md) depend on the mining of malachite ore.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Kjeld-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-SM-23)</sup> Malachite is sometimes stylistically integrated into arms and armor of a different base material to give the items the appearance of glass equipment, although such practice is merely cosmetic.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-CMG-24)</sup>

High Elves often stylize their glass equipment with avian imagery.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-CMG-24)</sup> [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md) use spiky glass equipment, an appearance inspired by the lessons in Vivec's sermons.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BASV-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-CMBA-13)</sup> There is a variant of glass called *sunglass*, but smithing is a privately taught craft in [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md).<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ESOContraband-25)</sup> The [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) are known to loot and plunder ancient [elven ruins](Lore_Elven_Ruins.md) for their deposits of malachite ore.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TGEH-26)</sup>

As of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) malachite was commonly used by [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) of eastern regions of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) to produce needles.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOContraband2-14)</sup> At the same time period malachite was also utilized in other crafts. Items such as malachite vases shaped after Breton watchtowers and malachite hairpins with the imagery typical for [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) were seen throughout [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOContraband3-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOContraband4-16)</sup>
