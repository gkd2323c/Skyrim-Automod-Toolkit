[This book is a historical account of the development of Dwemer law and custom from its roots in High Elven culture.]

In short, so far as I am able to trace the order of development in the customs of the [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) tribes, I believe it to have been in all ways comparable to the growth of [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) law. The earlier liability for slaves and animals was mainly confined to surrender, which, as in [Sumerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), later became compensation.

What does this matter for a study of our laws today? So far as concerns the influence of the Altmeri law upon our own, especially the Altmeri law of master and servant, the evidence of it is to be found in every judgment which has been recorded for the last five hundred years. It has been stated already that we still repeat the reasoning of the Altmeri magistrates, empty as it is, to the present day. I will quickly show how Altmeri custom can be followed into the courts of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md).

In the laws of [Karndar Watch](Lore_Karndar_Watch.md) (P.D. 1180) it is said, "If one who is owned by another slays one who owns himself, the first owner must pay the associates of the slain three fine instruments and the body of the one who his *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* owned." There are many other similar citations. The same principle is extended even to the case of a centurion by which a man is killed. "If, at the common workbench, one is slain by an [Animunculi](Lore_Dwemer_Animunculi.md), the associates of the slain may disassemble the Animunculi and take its parts within thirty days."

It is instructive to compare what Dhark has mentioned concerning the rude beasts of the [Tenmar forests](Lore_Tenmar_Forest.md). "If a [marsh cat](Lore_Khajiit.md) was killed by an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md), his family were in disgrace till they retaliated by killing the Argonian, or another like it. Furthermore, if a marsh cat was killed by a fall from a tree, his relatives would take their revenge by toppling the tree, shattering its branches, and casting them to every part of the forest."
