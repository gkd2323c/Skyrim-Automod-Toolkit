## Icereach Coven
The [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) (or **Icereach Clan**) was a coven of witches active in the 6th century of the Second Era, primarily - though not only - composed of Reachfolk and a clan that serves under the leadership of the witches. They resided on [Icereach](Lore_Icereach.md), a frozen island in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) and served as advisors of the Emperor during the times of the [Longhouse Dynasty](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) and after the fall of the dynasty as allies of the army of Vampires and Werewolves known as the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).

## Ienith
The [Ienith](Lore_Ienith.md) clan is an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) that lives in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They are one of the thirty revered families of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and a sub-faction of the Great [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md). Many of their members are either devout followers of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith or involved in criminal activities. The **Ienith Ancestral Tomb** is located in the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) of eastern Vvardenfell, northeast of Falansarano and west of [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md).

## Igrun Clan
The [Igrun](Lore_Igrun_Clan.md) (or sometimes spelled **Igron**) was a little-known [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) clan, which was notable during the Classical Period of the First Era, where its chieftain [Torug gro-Igron](Lore_Torug_gro-Igron.md) was the founder of one of the earliest and greatest incarnations of a true Orcish nation and the city of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).

## Imbel
The [Imbels](Lore_Imbel.md) are a noble family of [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), with ancestors that lived on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) (around [Suran](Lore_Suran.md)) and later in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) (around [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md), [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md), and [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md)) in the second millennium of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). In the closing days of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), a Breton noble named [Jakben](Lore_Jakben.md) held the Earldom of Imbel and he had his estate in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) where he lived.

## Immortal Eight
The [Immortal Eight](Lore_Immortal_Eight.md) was a group of immortal loyalist [legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) soldiers led by Captain [Anatolius Caudex](Lore_Anatolius_Caudex.md), who protected the [Nobles District](Lore_Nobles_District.md) of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). They also played a part in stopping Bal's plans to snuff out the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md).

## Imperial Academy of Alchemists
The [Imperial Academy of Alchemists](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Academy_of_Alchemists) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) academic institution devoted to the research of [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Little is known of the academy, except that they were addressed by a known scholar [Herminius Sophus](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Herminius_Sophus) in his text devoted to the nature of [Tel Var Stones](Lore_Tel_Var_Stones.md), *[On the Tel Var Stones](Lore_On_the_Tel_Var_Stones.md)* in which he shares his recipes and findings with the academy.

## Imperial Academy of Records and Histories
The [Imperial Academy of Records and Histories](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Academy_of_Records_and_Histories) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) academic institution devoted to the research of the history active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Little is known of the academy, except that one of its associate historians [Augusta Purusius](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Augusta_Purusius) authored *[The Sublime Brazier](Lore_The_Sublime_Brazier.md)*, a text devoted to the legend of [Sublime Brazier](Lore_Sublime_Brazier.md) hidden beneath the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and published at some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

## Imperial Archaeological Society
The [Imperial Archaeological Society](Lore_Imperial_Archaeological_Society.md) is an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) academic institution devoted to archaeological study. It was contemporary with the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md).

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the society was present on [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) as part of the [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) occupation of the island. It had taken possession of the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [ruins](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) of [Bthzark](Lore_Bthzark.md) as an archaeological site under the Dwarven Reclamation Act of 2E 864.

## Imperial Battlemages
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/OBR-npc-Imperial_Battlemage.jpg/200px-OBR-npc-Imperial_Battlemage.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-Imperial_Battlemage.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-npc-Imperial_Battlemage.jpg) An Imperial Legion battlemage This article is about Imperial Legion battlemages. For the head of the Elder Council, see [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md).

The [Cohort of Imperial Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) are a group of well trained war mages serving in the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). They had close ties to the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) before the Guild's dissolution. While the Guild had the authority to send battlemages onto the field, often to deal with violent rogue mages, the battlemages ultimately obeyed the orders of their superiors in the Legion. Imperial Battlemages are [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenese.md) in origin, and members of their ranks make up an age-old aristrocracy in the region of [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md). They are trained by the [Imperial Battle College](Lore_Imperial_Battle_College.md).

## Imperial Bedding Supplies
[Imperial Bedding Supplies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Bedding_Supplies) was a [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) -based [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) producer of pillows, blankets, and other bedding supplies that operated and had factories in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). They were known to directly ship bedding supplies to clients on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) by boat.

## Imperial City Archives
The [Imperial City Archives](Lore_Imperial_City_Archives.md) (also referred to as the **Imperial Archives**) is an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) institution based in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). It served as a repository of valuable Imperial records, such as deeds to holdings and estates within the Empire's bounds, court proceedings for a military tribunal, diplomatic marriage agreements between kingdoms, and records of the Imperial noble houses.

## Imperial College
The [Imperial College](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_College) is an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) academic institution. Little is known of the academy, except that it publishes books on topics of the research of [magic](Lore_Magic.md),, philosophy, and religious beliefs. One of its elective courses was Cyrodilic philosophy. Its upper-level course is described as rather esoteric in nature. Books published by the scholars of the academy were widely available during the [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md), [Third](Lore_Third_Era.md), and [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). One of the titles of the College was Praeceptor, a title shared with the [Imperial Battlemages](Lore_Imperial_Battlemages.md) of [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md).

## Imperial College of the Voice
The Imperial College of the Voice was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) institution devoted to the restoration of the art of [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) established by [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) at some point during his rule in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) in an attempt to turn the [Way of the Voice](Lore_Way_of_the_Voice.md) to warfare. According to the [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) commentary the Grand Master of the College was said to be a former street performer in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md). Some of the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) families loyal to the Empire sent some of their own children to the academy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGTTE-1)</sup>

## Imperial Commission of the Occupation
The [Imperial Commission of the Occupation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Commission_of_the_Occupation) was an [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) administrative institution active in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). It was responsible for law enforcement within the [provinces](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Provinces) that constituted parts of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). Although the Commission was generally tolerant of various cults, it outlawed practices and groups deemed hostile to the [Emperor](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) and the Empire. It was authorized to imprison and execute members of such faiths. In [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), the Imperial Commission of the Occupation allowed the [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) to serve as religious law enforcers themselves. Although [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship was legal under Imperial law at that time, certain other cults, such as the [Nerevarine Cult](Lore_Nerevarine_Cult.md), were prosecuted.

## Imperial Cult
The main religion of the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) can trace its origins back [Saint Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s institution of worship of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) as a synthesis between the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) gods and the [Nordic pantheon](Lore_Nord.md#The_Nordic_Pantheon). Although known as the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) in the provinces, strictly speaking this term refers the missionary arm of the religion, present in provinces such as [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). As the majority religion in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), it is more commonly known in that province simply as **the Church** or **the Chapel**.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> After the apotheosis of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) began to spread belief in the [Nine Divines](Lore_Nine_Divines.md), incorporating worship of the Empire's god-hero [Talos](Lore_Talos.md). However, the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) was forced to sign the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), which outlawed Talos worship.

## Imperial Geographical Society
The [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) (or **Imperial Geographic Society**) is a long-standing outfit responsible for the outlining and preservation of historical accounts within the whole of the Empire.

The Society has thrice been given the respected assignment of penning the Pocket Guide to the Empire, a broad overview of the history of the Tamrielic Empire. These guides, by definition of the Society, are to "paint a portrait of our known world at this moment in history for the edification of our readers [...] wide in scope".

The Society works on behalf of the Empire and in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), under the new united Tamriel and by authority of Tiber Septim, the first edition of the Pocket Guide to the Empire was published. The guide was largely used as an educational and informative tool to reassure the inhabitants of the new Empire. Parts of the guide were considered propagandist to emphasize this reassurance. The Society published the second version in [3E 331](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_331) and the propagandist tone was removed. The third edition appeared in [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432).

The Society also published [*Gathering Force: Arms and Armor of Tamriel*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Gathering_Force:_Arms_and_Armor_of_Tamriel) and *[The Improved Emperor's Guide to Tamriel](Lore_The_Improved_Emperor%27s_Guide_to_Tamriel.md)* in [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), before the first pocket guide. An apparent branch of the Society, the *Imperial Geographic Survey*, published a census on the [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) around the same time as the third edition pocket guide was released.

## Imperial Legion
The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), also known as the **Imperial Army**, **Dragon-Tribe of the Emperor**, the **Red Legions**, and the **Ruby Ranks**, is the main fighting force of the [Empire of Tamriel](Lore_Empire.md). It is often pluralized as the Imperial Legions. Headquartered in the [Imperial City Prison](Lore_Imperial_City_Prison.md), they operate under the auspices and authority of the Emperor himself. With its vast numbers, quality training, and rigid discipline, the Legion is considered one of the best armies ever assembled in history. The primary mission of the Imperial Legion is to preserve the peace and rule of law in the Empire. Those who protect the Emperor and the Imperial Province are sometimes called the **Imperial Guard** and the **Imperial Watch**. A Legion Centurion is typically in command of the [Palace Guard](Lore_Imperial_Guard.md) of the [White Gold Tower](Lore_White_Gold_Tower.md). The Legion's administration is known as the **Imperial War Office**.

In peacetime, the Legion serves primarily as a garrison force — manning forts, patrolling roads, and providing guardsmen for towns, cities, counties, and nobles. They are empowered to arrest criminals and seize their property, among other things. In wartime, the Legion's responsibilities and powers are greatly increased. During conflicts, the Legion serves as an invading and occupying force, overwhelming opposition with numerical superiority and strict economy of force. Legionnaires serve under an Imperial contract and, for its duration, they can be reassigned to different postings or even subcontracted to serve in local guard forces, at the discretion of the superior officers who have the authority to reassign them. The aquatic equivalent of the Imperial Legion is the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md), with the two often overlapping.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md).

## Imperial Navy
The [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md), also known as the **Imperial Fleet**, is the Empire's aquatic force, complementing the land-based [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). It is active during both wartime and peacetime, dealing with provinces such as [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) in various wars and dealing with pirates in peacetime. The Navy's home port is the city of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md). The Imperial Navy also has an Imperial Naval Consortium, where many scholars work, with one known study there being vexillology.

## Imperial Saints
The [Imperial Saints](Lore_Imperial_Saints.md) are pious individuals venerated by [the Church](Lore_The_Church.md), the majority religion among [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md). Devotions are often made to them as a group, known collectively as the **Communion of Saints**.

Many saints are historical figures who continue to be revered long after death. The Church also venerates **Living Saints**, individuals who have received a calling to live by the commands of their chosen Divine as an example to others. It is believed that saints are blessed by the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) with a second [sight](Lore_Sight.md) and that they do not view the world as other mortals do. Saints are buried in the graves of [Green Emperor Way](Lore_Green_Emperor_Way.md) among other important figures in the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).

The practice of saint-eating was popular in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) before it was banned at some point prior to [1E 2877](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2877). This involved the disinterring of a saint's body for use as an ingredient in the preparation of [food](Lore_Food.md). However, apocryphal accounts suggest the practice did not immediately cease.

## Indarys
The [Indarys](Lore_Indarys.md) family is a clan of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) originally from the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). Since the Dunmer first moved to Cheydinhal in the late Third Era, they have cemented themselves in [County Cheydinhal](Lore_County_Cheydinhal.md) in northeastern [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The county's symbol under their rule comprised of thorns and vines, which matched two of their prized family heirlooms, the [Staff of Indarys](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff_of_Indarys) and the [Thornblade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thornblade). The county's [knightly order](Lore_Knightly_Orders.md), the [Knights of Thorn](Lore_Knights_of_the_Thorn.md) adopted these symbols as their coat of arms.

## Invisible College of Daggerfall
The [Invisible College of Daggerfall](Lore_Invisible_College_of_Daggerfall.md) was a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) academic institution devoted to the scholarly pursuits active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Little is known of the academy, except that it was based in [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and one of its members, [Landal Gevont](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Landal_Gevont) was the foremost authority on [Goblin](Lore_Goblin.md) culture. He cooperated with [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) institution, the [Coterie of Organized Scholars](Lore_Coterie_of_Organized_Scholars.md) during his research.

## Iron Orcs
[Iron Orcs](Lore_Iron_Orcs.md), also known as the **Osh Ornim**, are an isolated [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) civilization native to the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md). They are known for their stone worship and their savage brutality, and are based in the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruins of the Valley of Scars. They tame [Welwas](Lore_Welwa.md), [Wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md) and [Trolls](Lore_Troll.md) as war beasts, and primarily use iron arms and armor. They were also adept at [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and usage of [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md). They are culturally separate from other Orcs, and tend to cling to the [old words](Lore_Old_Orcish.md) more than most other Orcish cultures.

## Iron Wheel
The **[Iron Wheel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron_Wheel)** was an organization of bailiffs headquartered in [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Originating from the Taneth branch of the [Bailiffs Guild](Lore_Bailiffs_Guild.md), when it and the rest of the guild collapsed following [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) unrest, an inspector named Rhanbiq argued to retain the guild's former structure within the newly formed Iron Wheel. Despite the idealism of some of their members, the Iron Wheel had a reputation for zealotry and brutality.

The Wheel earned income by transporting prisoners to Taneth and transferring them into the custody of either the Taneth guard captain or an Iron Wheel bailiff to be held until trial. The Iron Wheel received payment upon delivering the prisoner, and the funds were used for the transport of personnel and supplies. Additionally, officers could officiate weddings. Members of the Iron Wheel did not make all of their own equipment, and at least some of their weapons and armor were supplied by the Merchant Lord Orahan at-Addin.

## Ivory Brigade
The **[Ivory Brigade](Lore_Ivory_Brigade.md)** was a military force based out of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). They were established to protect [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md) against the three great alliances. The Ivory Brigade was founded by Rian Liore, a former legionary. He selected an ivory [horse](Lore_Horse.md) on a field of green, the ancient symbol of Leyawiin, to be the group's coat of arms. After being debated by Leyawiin's Chamber of Legates, the Brigade's name and function were officially implemented. After the fall of the [Empire](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md), the Brigade rose to replace its [legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), recruiting veterans of the Three Banners War, native Blackwooders, and former legionnaires, as well as using their armaments.
