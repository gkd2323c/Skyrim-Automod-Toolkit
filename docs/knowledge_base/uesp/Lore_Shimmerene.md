—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Shimmerene is one of the eight major cities in the province of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> located on the east-central coast of the [main island](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md). Known colloquially as the **City of Lights** (not to be confused with [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md)), Shimmerene is a bustling port-town where the majority of the main island's import, from nearby [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) or even the mainland of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) comes from. It is also the gateway for many foreigners coming into Summerset Isle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBIAG-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive SSAL-3)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/ON-place-Shimmerene_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shimmerene_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shimmerene_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shimmerene_02.jpg) Shimmerene's waterfront [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/ON-banner-Shimmerene.png/110px-ON-banner-Shimmerene.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Shimmerene.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Shimmerene.png) The city's banner Shimmerene is a dense, seaside town built on a hill that overlooks the [Auridon Strait](Lore_Auridon_Strait.md), the large body of water that separates the main island from Auridon.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive SSAL-3)</sup> Not only does it anchor the vineyards of [Russafeld](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Russafeld), a large stretch to the other side of the island,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBIAG-2)</sup> but the city also sits near a crossroad in eastern Summerset that connects three-ways to the other corners of the island. Outside of Shimmerene's walls are a couple of noteworthy landmarks, such as the [Caomus River](Lore_Caomus_River.md) running from the southern mountains past [Tor-Hame-Khard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tor-Hame-Khard) that empties between Shimmerene proper and the dockworks.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONShimmerene-4)</sup>

Some of the old ruins just outside of it were originally used by monastic orders, such as the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) in their original headquarters, the Keep of the Eleven Forces,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Summerfall-5)</sup> and especially the monks of Eldbur, with their monastery in the northwest<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONShimmerene-4)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Ruins-6)</sup> and sanctuary in the southwest.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONShimmerene-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLoad Sanctuary-7)</sup> Shimmerene is neighbored by several settlements, including [Rosefield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rosefield) to the west and [Silver Wood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silver_Wood) to the far southeast.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup>

Shimmerene is a large city built in the main island's architectural style with one entrance in its western wall, a long stairway from the main road. From there, the first place of note is the town square marked by a fountain, where the streets diverge north and east. Traveling north, the streets become narrow and confined together by high walls and arches. They descend toward the waterfront which is wide and open-aired, fit for large vessels. There is a gated bridge on the river mouth that branches the city proper with the Shimmerene Dockworks, a long street down the coast with warehouses for shipwrights.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONShimmerene-4)</sup>

The street east of the town square is a path along the south wall that climbs around Shimmerene's hill. The highest peak of the hill is occupied by the Monastery of Serene Harmony, a large temple and center of [divine](Lore_Divines.md) worship in the city. Both the north and east sections connect to each other at the base of the hill in narrow alleys between tall buildings. There is a lake along the eastern street and there is also a hedge maze at the base of the hill, near the waterfront.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONShimmerene-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/ON-load-Shimmerene_Aqueduct.jpg/200px-ON-load-Shimmerene_Aqueduct.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Shimmerene_Aqueduct.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Shimmerene_Aqueduct.jpg) The Waterworks [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/ON-npc-Kinlady_Avinisse.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Kinlady_Avinisse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Kinlady_Avinisse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Kinlady_Avinisse.jpg) Kinlady Avinisse of Shimmerene Shimmerene is an ancient settlement, one that dates back before [King's Haven Pass](Lore_King%27s_Haven_Pass.md) was an active trade route that connected it to [Corgrad](Lore_Corgrad.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-KHTR-11)</sup> Since well into the mid- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the people of Shimmerene had built their labyrinthine waterworks piece by piece underneath the city until its completion by roughly the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONLoad Waterworks-12)</sup> When the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) broke out, the city-state's traditional celebration, the Shimmerene Soiree ceased indefinitely.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCirclet-13)</sup>

By the mid-Second Era, Shimmerene and its environs were governed by Canonreeve Elquisa, a title given to her by her cousin, Avinisse, the Kinlady of Shimmerene. Despite her cousin's superior rank, Elquisa oversaw the bureaucracy that saw the city running operationally.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONElquisa-14)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the High Queen of the Summerset Isles, [Ayrenn](Lore_Ayrenn.md) made an official decree that opened the main island to the people of mainland Tamriel. This decree was met with open opposition from many nobles, but most notably from Kinlady Avinisse.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONRazum-15)</sup> The kinlady wrote a book called *"[A Rejection of Open Borders](Lore_A_Rejection_of_Open_Borders.md)"* which provided her case against the decree and called it many things, from radical to unwarranted.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AROOB-16)</sup> Despite her claims, Shimmerene saw a lot of activity because of it and as it was the gateway for many into the island.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive SSAL-3)</sup>

At around this time, the kinlady appointed a new Aldarch of the Serene Harmony Monastery named Tilcalar, whose origins were completely unknown but came highly recommended by Kinlord Milunthel. The two made a deal in which the Aldarch would secure the interest of Shimmerene and the Summerset Isles by placing newcomers under a "cultural assessment" and sending them back home.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TL-17)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONAvinisse-18)</sup> In actuality, they were placed in prisons underneath the monastery<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TL-17)</sup> and Tilcalar sacrificed them in the name of his patron, [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md). One of the [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md), [Razum-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md) traveled to Shimmerene to uncover newcomer disappearances and with the help of a [newcomer](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and Valsirenn of the Psijic Order, uncovered his plot and his association with a cult called the [Court of Bedlam](Lore_Court_of_Bedlam.md). Razum-dar and the newcomer defeated Aldarch Tilcalar in the [Coral Forest](Lore_Coral_Forest.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONDecree-19)</sup>

In [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), the kings of the Summerset Isles formed a unified alliance to combat the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md)<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-20)</sup> in their last-known invasion of the province, the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-21)</sup> Despite having assistance from Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) and the [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md), the Maormer were defeated because of a supernatural storm conjured accredited to the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-20)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TWQ5-22)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Shimmerene was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Shashara and had a rivalry with several settlements, namely [Dusk](Lore_Dusk.md), [Skywatch](Lore_Skywatch.md), and [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AR-10)</sup> By the waning years of the Third Era, the youth of the province had organized a movement to reject the High Elves' old way of life and disinterest of foreign customs, in favor of one that sought to accept new cultures from the mainland. Despite this, a darker side had manifested in the form of a guerrilla faction known as [the Beautiful](Lore_The_Beautiful.md), who had taken extreme measures to ensure their goals. One of these actions was the assassination of the King of Shimmerene's daughter, which sparked massive public outrage.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGE3E-23)</sup>

### Known Rulers
For the longest time, the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) served as counselors to the rulers of the Summerset Isles. If anyone wanted their counsel, their best method was either through a select few places or the kings themselves. They continued this service up until their island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) first vanished in the Second Era, around when the Mages Guild was formed. When they returned several years later for the Third Era, they continued where they left off<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FOA-24)</sup> until Artaeum disappeared a second time sometime in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) for whatever reason.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SRTolfdir-25)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/ON-place-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg/200px-ON-place-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Monastery_of_Serene_Harmony.jpg) The Monastery of Serene Harmony [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/ON-npc-Mother_of_Rats.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Mother_of_Rats.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mother_of_Rats.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Mother_of_Rats.jpg) The Mother of Rats
- *For Altmer culture as a whole, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Altmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Altmer.md#Society_and_Religion)** section on the Altmer page.*

The study of genealogy and ancestry, known in [High Elvish](Lore_High_Elvish.md) as *"aednavorith"* is a topic of endless interest to the people of the Summerset Isles, but the scholars at the Monastery of Serene Harmony are said to be the very best in the entire province.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONHarmony-27)</sup> The monastery is generally a place of meditation and peace,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONNuleros-28)</sup> and it has shrines dedicated to the Altmeri pantheon.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONMonastery-29)</sup>

In the mid-Second Era, there was an elusive and famous figure that lived in Shimmerene's waterworks called the Mother of Rats, an Apraxic Mer who oversaw a community of people that had been rejected by High Elf society.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ONLanarie-30)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONEdgar-31)</sup> She was originally exiled sometime around [2E 532](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_532) for forging a patent of lineage. The dispute that ensued afterward led to the death of forty-three mer and the subsequent shattering of her [calian](Lore_Calian.md), which brought her into the life of an aprax.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-ONRats-32)</sup>

The Shimmerene Dockworks has a magically-embued crafting site,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONShimmerene-4)</sup> one of several found all over Tamriel. As theorized by the Khajiit adventurer, Pacrooti, these are created by master craftworkers, who at the height of their artistry, manifest their abilities and infuse them into their stations, so that any item crafted there would have the same quality as their original works.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-MAMAPAYQ-33)</sup>

The city's people celebrate the Shimmerene Soiree, a gala hosted by the Canonreeve to honor their city-state and its success.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCirclet-13)</sup> The event is done in a grandiose manner, with large lights, [music](Lore_Music.md), and wafts of floral scents.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONGown-34)</sup> The soiree was temporarily canceled when the Interregnum started in the early-Second Era and since then, artisans tried to sell circlets for the soiree to anyone for any event.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONCirclet-13)</sup> [Cuisiniers](Lore_Altmer_Cuisine.md) from Shimmerene use long and curved snail hooks to open snails from their shells.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONContra S-35)</sup>

Shimmerene is the setting for an [Investigator Vale](Lore_Investigator_Vale.md) book subtitled the *[Retinue of Shambles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Investigator_Vale__Retinue_of_Shambles)*. In it the eponymous main character and an agent of the Queen, [Zaraki-dar](Lore_Razum-dar.md) investigate a cult called the Retinue of Shambles, their connections with nobles all over Summerset Isle, and their plans in the wake of the Queen's Decree. Their case came to an end at the Coral Forest, where the two and a dozen [Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) soldiers cornered a cultist in the midst of a powerful ritual.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-IVROS-36)</sup>

## Notes
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Shimmerene's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Titans".<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-37)</sup> This was finally used in *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)*, where the Shimmerene Titans arena team competed in an event known as the Dragon Games.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-38)</sup>
