# Stormhold Crystal

[![](https://images.uesp.net/e/e4/SH-item-Storm_Crystal.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-item-Storm_Crystal.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-item-Storm_Crystal.png) The Storm Crystal Stormhold Crystals are strange magical blue [crystals](Lore_Crystals.md) found deep underground in the [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) region of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), particularly in the tunnels beneath the city of [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md). These minerals are rare, volatile rocks, said to be able to contain corruption or harness the power of "evil" itself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Helga-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:27, 33</sup> There are two types of Stormhold Crystals: **Filled Crystals** and **Hollow Crystals**. Filled Crystals have many different enchantments, such as healing, magical buffs or teleportation; some crystals can also be used for offensive purposes, killing opponents instantly.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Helga-1)</sup> Hollow Crystals are used to store evil and corruption.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Helga-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-5)</sup> The **Storm Crystal** is a large Stormhold Crystal which glows with distilled power and has the ability to hold vast amounts of corruption.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Varus-7)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Helga-1)</sup>

## History

### Origins
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhold_02.jpg) The ancient city of Stormhold The origin of the crystals is a mystery. They were created by the priests of a nearly forgotten religion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-6)</sup> Given Stormhold's origins as a [Barsaebic Ayleid](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) city,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-8)</sup> the priests could have been [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), a race known for harnessing [Meteoric Glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md) to create magical crystals.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Similar crystals include [Welkynd Stones](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md), [Varla Stones](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) and [Star Teeth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md).

The ancient [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) wrote a book of wisdom which discussed secrets relating to Stormhold Crystals and their link to the natural energies of Black Marsh. Despite the dangerous information this book contained and its status as an invaluable cultural artifact, its existence was largely forgotten following the [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md) due to Argonian society's disinterest in the past and focus on the present. It was subsequently lost in the labyrinthine caverns beneath Stormhold.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:33, 42</sup>

### Deslandra's Experiments
At some point circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) mage [Deslandra](Lore_Deslandra.md) set up an underground laboratory in western Black Marsh.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:21</sup> Here, she conducted experiments designed to corrupt the roots of Black Marsh's [most mystical trees](Lore_Hist.md) in an attempt to sever their connection to inter-planar forces, destroying the links between [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and the [realms](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md) of [magic](Lore_Aetherius.md). In doing so, she hoped to cause the magic of Black Marsh to fall, allowing her to achieve her ultimate goal of fully cleansing Nirn of all magic during the Great Nullification.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:48, 50</sup> As part of this plot, Deslandra planned to use Stormhold Crystals to siphon off the worst effects of sundering the world from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:37</sup>

In order to make use of the crystals, Deslandra sent mercenary agents into the caverns beneath Stormhold to search for the ancient Argonian book.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:37</sup> At the same time, [Cyrodilic Collections](Lore_Cyrodilic_Collections.md) hired the [Undaunted](Lore_Undaunted.md) to recover the book for display in their newly-established museum in [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:20</sup> A member of the Outer Watch accepted this job on behalf of the Undaunted and succeeded in obtaining the book before Deslandra's agents could locate it. The book was given to Famia Mercius of Cyrodilic Collections, who decided not to display it publicly due to nature of its contents. Instead, the book was stored away for safekeeping and for use by researchers of Argonian antiquity.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:42</sup> Deslandra's plot to corrupt the roots of Black Marsh was ultimately thwarted by the Outer Watch.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-2)</sup><sup>:52</sup>

### The Mad Warden
*Varus opens new tunnels, seeking some Stormhold crystal. But his scheme, removing evil from one man to place in another, is madness. The earth itself oozes corruption as a result of afflicting far too many for me to help.*
—Helga<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Helga-1)</sup> [![](https://images.uesp.net/4/49/SH-creature-Night_Guard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-creature-Night_Guard.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-creature-Night_Guard.png) A corrupted Stormhold Overseer Some time after the extinction of the Ayleids, the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) set up a prison in the old ruins beneath Stormhold.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-6)</sup> In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427),<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Warden [Quintus Varus](Lore_Quintus_Varus.md) learned of the Storm Crystal and used the prisoners as slave labor, forcing them to dig in the tunnels. Varus has great success extracting Stormhold Crystals, and planned to locate the Storm Crystal and use it to store all the evil in the Empire, titling himself the Slayer of Evil. The prisoners who dug in the tunnels, nicknamed "tunnel rats", kept some of the crystals for themselves, for use in smuggling or exploring the dangerous undead-infested tunnels. Varus overlooked this theft as long as tunnel rats were not greedy.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Varus-7)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-6)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/4/41/SH-creature-Warden_Varus.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-creature-Warden_Varus.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SH-creature-Warden_Varus.png) Warden Qunitus Varus, fully corrupted Varus attempted to use the minor Crystals to contain corruption, removing evil from one man to place into another, but this backfired horribly. The earth itself began to ooze corruption due to how many were afflicted from his scheme. Over time, prisoners and their [overseers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold_Overseer) began to go mad, fleeing into the tunnels and attacking workers clearing the ruins. Digging in the tunnels caused corruption to infect the workers, which led to madness and lizard-like deformities over time. Varus afflicted far too many, and the prison camp's healer, Helga, was unable to cure all of them. Many of the overseers began to show horrific deformities, and prisoners weren't expected to survive long.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Helga-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-6)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

Varus, driven insane in his search for the Storm Crystal, continued to open new tunnels and eventually succeeded in tracking it down. A [prisoner](Lore_Hero.md) nicknamed the "[master tunnel rat](Lore_Master_Tunnel_Rat.md)", with the help of Helga and several other prisoners, gathered enough equipment from the ruins to defeat the overseers and confront Varus. The two fought for the Storm Crystal, and the prisoner was victorious. Although the Crystal was unable to hold the evil of the Empire, the prisoner was able to use it to clear the prison camp of corruption, letting the inmates go free.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-6)</sup>
