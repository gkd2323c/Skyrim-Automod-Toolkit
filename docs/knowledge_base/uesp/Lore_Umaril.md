*As balangua, Ehlnada racuvar! (By my power, the mortal gods shall be cast down!)*
—Umaril the Unfeathered Umaril the Unfeathered (sometimes **Umar-Il**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIo D-1)</sup> remembered by his enemies as **Umaril the Accursed**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oblivion Pel-2)</sup> and **Umaril Godsbane**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-3)</sup> was an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) sorcerer-king of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) and liege-lord of the other Ayleid sorcerer-kings,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UIU-4)</sup> a right he was bestowed thanks to his incredible power and status as the most favored champion of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MTCKN-5)</sup> He ruled from the throne room of the [Temple of the Ancestors](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md); as the holder of the Cyrodilic throne, he was known as the **Cyrodiil**.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Origin-6)</sup> His control over the White-Gold Tower also earned him the appellation **Dread Castellan**.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-3)</sup> Umaril was of such might that he was said to be able to slay the greatest of warriors with a single blow.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-3)</sup> He had an appearance akin to a golden [Angel](Lore_Angel.md), which was represented by his helm's visage and his signature unfeathered wings.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOP7-7)</sup> He wielded a golden-hued sword artifact that was known as the Unfeathered Greatsword,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-US-8)</sup> or more simply as Umaril's Sword.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Umaril Sword-9)</sup> He was ultimately defeated by the [Divine Crusader](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) before his plans could come to fruition.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-3)</sup>

## History

### Origins
Umaril was born to an Ayleid mother. As for his father, Umaril claimed divine parentage, that his father was a [God](Lore_Gods.md) of the prior [kalpa's](Lore_Kalpa.md) [World-River](Lore_Lyg.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOP7-7)</sup> With this half mortal half divine parentage, Umaril was known as an [Ada](Lore_Ada.md) and referred to as a half-Elf.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSOP3-10)</sup> One peculiar claim rattled off by Pelinal Whitestrake in a fit of madness is that he and [Aka](Lore_Akatosh.md) were responsible for the creation of Umaril.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TSOP6-11)</sup>

### Campaigns and Rule
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg/200px-OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-place-White_Gold_Tower.jpg) Temple of the Ancestors The Ayleid Empire lorded over [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) slaves of countless tribes. These slaves were tortured and forced to do the bidding of their Elven masters.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TA-12)</sup> Umaril being ambitious and wishing to expand the scope of the Ayleid power and influence engaged in campaigns.

Umaril the Unfeathered was a king with a reputation for leadership in battle.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-URW-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-UC-14)</sup> Umaril would often strategically outfit his armies with exotic mounts to take advantage of his enemies' native terrain or local customs. He commanded military conquests into neighboring regions, including [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-URG-15)</sup> and the territory of the [Sunnatren](Lore_Sunnatren.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-URE-16)</sup> Additionally, he would disrupt the activities of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) nomads of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) that his enemies traded with.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-URS-17)</sup>

### Alessian Slave Rebellion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg/200px-OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) A painting depicting Umaril and Pelinal's battle In [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-FC-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SAD-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PGTE-20)</sup> a Nedic slave known as [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) prayed to [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) that the Nedes could garner the might to take vengeance on their Ayleid masters for their cruelty. In return, Kyne sent down her [Demigod](Lore_Demigod.md) son [Morihaus](Lore_Morihaus.md) and by his side, a fierce warrior wearing futuristic armor called [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md). The pair arrived at the camp of Alessia having already killed several Ayleid chieftains. The stage for Nedic rebellion was set under the trio, with Alessia being the Slave-Queen.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TSOP2-21)</sup> Pelinal's armor and weapons were in fact gifts from the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md), who personally blessed Pelinal as their champion in the crusade against the cruel Ayleids.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-3)</sup> The armor was effective, as no Ayleid weapon managed to pierce it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSOP3-10)</sup>

Alessia and Morihaus, who had become lovers, tended to the armies of the rebellion, whereas Pelinal settled for open, single combat against the prideful Ayleid Sorcerer-Kings.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSOP3-10)</sup> Time and time again Pelinal defeated these Elven kings with relative ease.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSOP3-10)</sup> The Ayleids feeling desperate made a pact with the [Aurorans](Lore_Auroran.md), [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) servants of Meridia.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSOP3-10)</sup> Also at this time, Umaril was appointed as champion giving him full control over the legions of Aurorans and Ayleids alike. Umaril from his citadel called upon Pelinal Whitestrake, challenging him to battle.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TSOP3-10)</sup>

Umaril summoned [Thundernachs](Lore_Storm_Atronach.md) and sent them against the rebellions armies although they were dispatched by Pelinal, who stormed towards the White-Gold Tower alone in an act of brashness.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TSOP4-22)</sup> Pelinal breached the interior of the White-Gold Tower but Umaril, favoring ruin-from-afar,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> sent hordes of Elf and Daedra alike at Pelinal. Each enemy was slain but slowly Pelinal's blessed armor was chipped away at as Umaril had blessed his armies weapons with heavy [Varliance](Lore_Varliance.md) that he had been hoarding ever since he issued challenge to Pelinal.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOP7-7)</sup>

Finally, after Pelinal was wounded to a degree Umaril made his appearance and the two Ada did battle. Despite his wounds, Pelinal was able to defeat, and seemingly kill, Umaril the Unfeathered.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOP7-7)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-SOT-24)</sup> The remaining Ayleid kings along with their Daedric allies descended on the depleted Pelinal, cutting him into eight pieces, leaving only his head behind in the White-Gold Tower.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSOP7-7)</sup> Other sources state that it was Umaril who dealt this death to Pelinal however implying perhaps they both dealt each other mortal wounds.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TSOP8-25)</sup> Pelinal was dead, but Umaril and the Ayleid Empire were defeated, the Nedes controlled Cyrodiil and the Citadel.

### Waters of Oblivion
When Morihaus entered the White-Gold Tower and discovered the head of his dear friend Pelinal, the two had one final conversation. During the discussion, Pelinal lamented that Umaril had deceived him and had not truly died. As it turned out, Umaril in his cunning had bound himself to the realm of his mistress Meridia, and upon his mortal demise at the hands of Pelinal, was merely cast through the Waters of Oblivion to reform much like any other Daedra, allowing him to cheat death.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TA-12)</sup> Pelinal warned that he would no longer be able to protect mankind from the power and wrath of Umaril the Unfeathered, but Morihaus was confident that Pelinal would [come again](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TA-12)</sup> It is believed that Umaril spent much time in the Colored Rooms, regaining his strength and plotting his revenge. From there however, it is rumored Umaril could exert his will through mortals, indicated by the mortal in question having radiant yellow eyes much like Umaril himself.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-EOU-26)</sup> Umaril eventually witnessed the death of his nemesis Alessia.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIo D-1)</sup> The remaining Ayleids were shocked that their mighty champion had been slain by a mere [Man](Lore_Human.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-FBJ-27)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UIU-4)</sup> The memories of Umaril and many other sorcerer-kings were stored in an Ayleid memory crystal.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Saromir-28)</sup>

At some point after his defeat, scholars of the topic would gather to re-enact the battle between Pelinal and Umaril, using well-crafted illusion magics to replicate the appearance of Umaril's armor.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup> Some of these scholars even tattooed imagery of this historic battle on their bodies.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), there were still those who venerated the Unfeathered in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), and they would continue to carry out his cruel will.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-UC-14)</sup> Those who didn't venerate him still acknowledged his incredible power.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOLyris-32)</sup> Even his war mounts were still being used, and arms being created in his likeness.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-US-8)</sup>

### Umaril's Return
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7b/OB-creature-Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg/200px-OB-creature-Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-creature-Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg) Umaril in the spirit realm [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/OB-prerelease-Facing_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg/200px-OB-prerelease-Facing_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-prerelease-Facing_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-prerelease-Facing_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.jpg) The Divine Crusader facing Umaril the Unfeathered Umaril returned to Tamriel circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), to seek vengeance upon the [Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) who had brought about his fall.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-OBUmaril-33)</sup> Aurorans under his command desecrated the chapel of [Dibella](Lore_Dibella.md) in [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), and later the chapel of [Mara](Lore_Mara.md) in [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-3)</sup>

The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) learned of Umaril's return from an elderly [prophet](Lore_The_Prophet.md) in Anvil, and that he must be conquered in both the physical and spiritual plane in order to be vanquished, a feat that would only be possible with the aid of the Divines and the [Crusader's Relics](Lore_Crusader%27s_Relics.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-3)</sup> The hero gathered the eight Relics originally used by Pelinal to destroy Umaril's physical form. With the prophet's guidance, the Crusader went on to find something which Pelinal did not, could not, have had when he battled Umaril: the Blessing of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md). Armed with Talos' blessing, the Hero of Kvatch, now known as the Divine Crusader reborn confronted Umaril and his hosts in the Ayleid fortress [Garlas Malatar](Lore_Garlas_Malatar.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-FMMWTDOM-34)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Umaril-35)</sup> Umaril noted with begrudging respect that the reach of his old nemesis Pelinal was long and engaged the Divine Crusader in battle.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-OBUmaril-33)</sup> The Crusader not only defeated Umaril on the mortal plane, but was able to follow him into the spirit realm, where the half-Elf finally met his end.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Umaril-35)</sup>
