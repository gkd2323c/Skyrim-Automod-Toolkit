# Ten Ancestors

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg/200px-OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Collector_02.jpg) One of the Ten Ancestors The Ten Ancestors are a set of ten [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [statues](Lore_Statues.md). They consist mostly of [meteoric iron](Lore_Meteoric_Iron.md) prongs, with a shard of [meteoric glass](Lore_Meteoric_Glass.md) in the center. The statues played an important role in Ayleid religion and were displayed in the [Temple of the Ancestors](Lore_Temple_of_the_Ancestors.md), now known as the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-1)</sup> The temple of Loriasel was also built in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) by the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) in veneration of the Ancestors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

The Ancestors themselves were believed to worship the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), and [Daedraphile](Lore_Daedraphile.md) Ayleids accordingly followed the *Eleven Edicts of the Ten Ancestors*. The Heretic of [Lindai](Lore_Lindai.md) was known for writing the *Eleven OTHER Edicts of the Ten Ancestors*, a parody that supposed the Ancestors worshipped [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) instead.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

During the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), the Ancestors were dispersed to cities across [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) for safekeeping (specifically [Culotte](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Culotte), [Fanacas](Lore_Fanacas.md), [Mackamentain](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mackamentain), [Moranda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moranda), [Ninendava](Lore_Ninendava.md), [Vilverin](Lore_Vilverin.md), [Welke](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Welke), [Wendelbek](Lore_Wendelbek.md), [Wendir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wendir), and [Wenyandawik](Lore_Wenyandawik.md)). Following the sacking of White-Gold and the Ayleid exodus of Cyrodiil, the statues were left behind and forgotten.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-1)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) nobleman called [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) paid the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) to hunt down the Ten Ancestors so that he could add them to his collection of Ayleid antiquities. He then put them on display in his private manor in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), before his untimely death that same year while exploring the ruins of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## Notes
- The Ayleids seemingly structured personal memoirs into ten chapters in reference to the Ten Ancestors.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
- Umbacano believed that ten of the ancient statues still existed when he began his search, implying that there may have been more at some point.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-1)</sup>
- The Ayleids of [Morahame](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morahame) constructed a venue known as the Theater of the Ancestors in the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md) to honor [Merid-Nunda](Lore_Merid-Nunda.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOTheater-6)</sup>
