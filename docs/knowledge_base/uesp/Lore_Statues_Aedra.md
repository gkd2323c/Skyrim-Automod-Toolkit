## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-31) In *TES:V: Skyrim*, [the hawk bas-relief that represents Kyne](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Hawk_Mural.jpg) is typically found in Nordic ruins, however in once instance its found in [Rebel's Cairn](Skyrim_Rebel%27s_Cairn.md), the tomb of [Red Eagle](Lore_Red_Eagle.md). The association of this bas-relief with Red Eagle is somewhat of a paradox, given Red Eagle was a Reachman from the First Era who didn't have any known association with the ancient Nordic deity. The likely explanation is that this bas-relief was re-used by the game's designers to decorate Red Eagle's tomb with eagle-like imagery, given the similarities between the hawk and the eagle.
2. [^](#cite_ref-39) In *TES:V: Skyrim*, [the wolf bas-relief that represents Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Wolf_Mural.jpg) is typically found in Nordic ruins, however in once instance its found in [Potema's Catacombs](Skyrim_Potema%27s_Catacombs.md) beneath Solitude. Connecting this bas-relief with [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) the Wolf-Queen is somewhat of a paradox, given she was from the Third Era and didn't have any known association with the ancient Nordic deity. The likely explanation is that this bas-relief was re-used by the game's designers to decorate the Wolf-Queen's catacombs with existing wolf-like imagery.
3. [^](#cite_ref-57) Stendarr's chalice/horn bears striking similarities to the [Horn of Plenty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cornucopia) - a large horn-shaped container overflowing with produce, flowers, or nuts. In Greek and Roman mythology it was a symbol of abundance and nourishment and was attributed to several deities associated with the harvest and prosperity.
