# Currency


## Alliance Points
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-misc-Alliance_Points.png) **Alliance Points** were the name of a form of scrip (a substitute for legal tender) paid out by the three Alliances for participation in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md). Battlefield merchants of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md), [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) would accept these tokens as payment for goods. They were also adopted by the [Circle of Champions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Circle_of_Champions) as a reward for participation in gladiatorial combat. The tokens themselves resembled green gems in the shape of the [Red Diamond](Lore_Red_Diamond.md), marked with the [Red Dragon](Lore_Symbols.md#Red_Dragon) symbol on one side.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

## [Archival Fortunes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Archival_Fortunes)
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Archival_Fortunes.png) [Archival Fortunes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Archival_Fortunes) are a form of currency used in the [Infinite Archive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Archive), a border realm of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md). They are [soul](Lore_Souls.md) -forged currency, taking the form of eye-focused signets. Both [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and mortal scholars dwelling in the Infinite Archive utilize them for trade. Magical analysis unmistakably reveals the incorporation of [soul gems](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Gems) in crafting these distinctive signets. A clear connection exists between comprehending the aetheric index at the core of the Archive and acquiring these iconic signets, likely explaining their value within that realm's confines. Archival Fortunes were frequently employed as rewards for individuals who carried out tasks for the [hierophant](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malkhest) and other denizens of the archive, such as halting the invasion of [Tho'at Replicanum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tho%27at_Replicanum), slaying [Fabled](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fabled) and Marauder [maligraphies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maligraphy), or helping with restoring the lost pieces of literature, such as *[Fynboar the Resurrected](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fynboar_the_Resurrected)* or *Theories on Apocrypha*. Exclusive exotic goods were often available for purchase solely using these fortunes within the Archive.

[Gabrielle Benele](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gabrielle_Benele) of the [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) was known for delving into the research of the Archival Fortunes.

## Crown Coins
[![A Crown Coin](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/LG-item-Crown_Coin.png/64px-LG-item-Crown_Coin.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-item-Crown_Coin.png) **Crown Coins** are cut red gemstones named for the crown marking engraved on one side. ## Crown Gems
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-store-Crown_Gems.png) **Crown Gems** are cut purple gemstones named for the crown marking engraved on one side. The [Gold Coast Trading Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold_Coast_Trading_Company) offered these gems in compensation for unwanted purchased goods, to be used as a method of exchange.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

## Dwemer Coins
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/MW-item-Dwemer_Coin_2.png/200px-MW-item-Dwemer_Coin_2.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Dwemer_Coin_2.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Dwemer_Coin_2.png) A Dwemer Coin **Dwemer Coins** are artifacts left behind by the vanished [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) race. The Dwemer used these octagonal metallic grey coins instead of or in addition to gold. Treasure hunters who come across these coins can sell them for a high price, although this can be a dangerous endeavor, as angry [Dwarven spectres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Spectre) may attack looters who attempt to take them.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), all Dwemer artifacts were declared the property of the [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gen Ruins-12)</sup> and the trade<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Gen Artifacts-13)</sup> and extraction of Dwemer artifacts was forbidden under Imperial law.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gen Ruins-12)</sup>

## Event Tickets
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Event_Tickets.png) **Event Tickets** were a form of currency accepted by [the Impresario](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Impresario), a mysterious and eccentric [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) merchant who offered exotic goods in return for stories of heroic feats.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Mt CTI-14)</sup> These experiences were denominated using these pink tickets, which could then be exchanged at the Impresario's traveling stall.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The source of her immense stores of wealth was subject to much speculation at the time.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Mt CTI-14)</sup>

## Gems
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-icon-resource-Gems.png) Cut green **gems** are sometimes used as a form of rare currency in the [Bloodfall Kingdom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodfall_Kingdom) and its [neighboring kingdom](Lore_Hero.md#Dynasty_of_Odar_the_Brave).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

## Gold
*"Septims" redirects here. For the Imperial line, see [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md).* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/OB-misc-Septim_Coin.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Septim_Coin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Septim_Coin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Septim_Coin.jpg) A septim coin **Gold**, also referred to as **septims** or **drakes** (sign: **dr**)<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> following the conquests of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), is the main form of currency. As the name would imply, these round coins are made from [gold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold), a precious [metal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Metals). It is used across the entire continent of Tamriel and beyond, including otherworldly trading hubs such as [Fargrave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fargrave), [New Sheoth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Sheoth), the [Hollow City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hollow_City), the [Brass Fortress](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brass_Fortress), and [Wretched Spire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wretched_Spire).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOGold-21)</sup> It has been referred to as "[Nirni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirni) -gold" by [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) denizens of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

By the [Third](Lore_Third_Era.md) and [Fourth Eras](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), gold coins were commonly referred to as septims or drakes due to the markings minted on all coins by the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md): the obverse displays the face of Emperor Tiber Septim, with the words "THE EMPIRE IS LAW" and "THE LAW IS SACRED"; the reverse displays the Red Dragon symbol (or 'drake'), with the words "PRAISE BE [AKATOSH](Lore_Akatosh.md) AND ALL THE [DIVINES](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines)".

In centuries prior, the mint marks on gold coins were less consistent. The [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) minted coins with the [Red Diamond](Lore_Red_Diamond.md) symbol on one side.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> The First [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) also minted their own gold coins, bearing a face on one side.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> Other coins had a crown symbol on one side and a chest symbol representing a bank on the other.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ESOGold-21)</sup>

Banks can offer loans or letters of credit denominated in gold.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup> Such promissory notes are sometimes known as a writ of coin.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

[King Orgnum's Coffer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:King_Orgnum%27s_Coffer) is a legendary enchanted chest said to contain unlimited gold coins.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup>

## Tel Var Stones
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/ON-item-Tel_Var_Stone.jpg/200px-ON-item-Tel_Var_Stone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Tel_Var_Stone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Tel_Var_Stone.jpg) A Tel Var Stone *Main article: [Tel Var Stones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tel_Var_Stones)* **Tel Var Stones** are esoteric fragments of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), scraped away by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s Daedric minions during their occupation of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOTel Var-32)</sup> As part of Bal's attempted [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), a [Dark Anchor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Anchor) was opened above the White-Gold Tower, and the Daedra began to chip away pieces of the Tower in order to weaken the barriers between [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONPhrastus-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONCinnabar-34)</sup>

The stones resemble hexagonal blue or grey coins with the Red Diamond symbol engraved on one side.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOTel Var-32)</sup> Tel Var Stones contain a dormant power dating back to the Tower's construction by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), who settled for Tel Var after they failed to create their own Zero Stone.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-ONPhrastus-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-ONCinnabar-34)</sup> Merchants within the Imperial City accepted Tel Var as a form of currency during the three alliances' invasion of the City as part of the wider Three Banners War.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ESOTel Var-32)</sup>
