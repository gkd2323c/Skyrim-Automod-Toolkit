# Iron Orcs

*We are the children of the stones.
We are the people of the rock.
We are the Osh Ornim!*
— *[Blood for Our Enemies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blood_for_Our_Enemies)* Iron Orcs, also known as the **Osh Ornim**, are an isolated [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) civilization native to the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md) of [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BFOE-1)</sup> They are known for their stone worship and their savage brutality,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BFOE-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oo T-2)</sup> and are based in the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) ruins<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ES-3)</sup> of the Valley of Scars.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VOS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TTB-5)</sup> They tame [Welwas](Lore_Welwa.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Ilthag_Ironblood-6)</sup> [Wamasu](Lore_Wamasu.md) and [Trolls](Lore_Troll.md) as war beasts,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Uzka_Trollfeeder-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Hjargredda-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCAFOT-9)</sup> and primarily use iron arms and armor. They were also adept at [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md) and usage of [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LTEB-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CCOH-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LTAU-12)</sup> They are culturally separate from other Orcs,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oo T-2)</sup> and tend to cling to the [old words](Lore_Old_Orcish.md) more than most other Orcish cultures.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOWT-13)</sup>

## Origins
The origin of the Iron Orcs is unknown. Similar to the [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orcs.md) of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and the Orcs encountered by [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Load-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-15)</sup> the Iron Orcs may have inhabited Craglorn since time immemorial. Despite the traditional creation story claiming that the Orcs were originally [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) followers of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), who were corrupted when their god was transformed into [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup> The ancestors of the Iron Orcs were originally a peaceful shamanistic society of stone worshippers. These cave-dwelling Orcs had rich cultural exchanges with the ancient [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) of the [Deathlands](Lore_Deathlands.md) sometime in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). By the late Merethic, however, the Nedes of the region had mastered basic metallurgy. After a long period of estrangement, war broke out, and the Nedes used their newfound weapons and arms to attack and conquer their Orcish neighbors.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oo T-2)</sup>

Faced with assured destruction, the ancient Orcs were forced to defile their sacred stones in order to mine iron. It is theorized that this corruption of the stones led to a fundamental shift in Iron Orc society. The Iron Orcs became a civilization of miners, smiths and warriors, whose anger at outsiders was fueled by their own self-hatred. Contemporary burial pits show evidence of civilian corpses who had been crushed and brutalized in death by the Orcs.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oo T-2)</sup> Over the years, this mentality continued to permeate their society, and the Iron Orcs earned an infamous reputation from pillaging the settlements of Craglorn. Early in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Blackcaster Mages Guild](Lore_Blackcaster_Mages_Guild.md) was said to have secretly hired a band of Iron Orc raiders to threaten [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) in order to surreptitiously take control of the city. When the Orcs laid siege to the city, the Blackcaster mages drove off the Orcs and won the approval of the populace. Some scholars dismiss these claims as dubious given Iron Orcs are supposedly intransigent in matters of cooperation and negotiation.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

Iron orcs worshiped the stone,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oo T-2)</sup> and swore "ten iron oaths". The practice known as the death of sun and feathers was a form of execution practiced by the Iron Orcs. The sentenced was nailed to the stone and cut once for each of the ten iron oaths and then the sun burned until the crows gather. It was considered a proper death for liars and traitors.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Agganor.27s_Journal-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LTB-19)</sup> Among the Iron Orcs, tattoos are marks of honor, and insulting one is viewed as a challenge to the bearer's dignity, often with deadly consequences. Their Iron Rage Tattoos act as living chronicles that record great victories and defining moments on the skin. Warriors proudly display both scars and ink, each telling part of their personal history. Because these markings hold such deep cultural meaning, tattoo artists are regarded with great respect. Although hard-won battle scars across the Dragontail Mountains are highly esteemed, commemorative tattoos are valued just as much.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-IRFT-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-IRBT-21)</sup>

## The Serpent's Corruption
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/ON-place-Iron_Orc_Nirncrux_Mine.jpg/200px-ON-place-Iron_Orc_Nirncrux_Mine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Iron_Orc_Nirncrux_Mine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Iron_Orc_Nirncrux_Mine.jpg) An Iron Orc nirncrux mine In [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Arnure-22)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SOE-23)</sup> the constellations fell from the skies over Craglorn and assumed mortal form as [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md). In his plans to remake [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), [The Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md) sought to raise armies to his cause. The Iron Orcs were among those targeted by his corrupting influence. The Orcish shaman, Lashburr Tooth-Breaker, attempted to advise chieftain Braadoth against allying with the Serpent and his [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md), but he was deposed and replaced by his rival Agganor. Braadoth was indoctrinated into the cult and appointed as the Ophidian Exarch of Undulating Destruction.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Load-14)</sup> The Iron Orcs quickly began mining for [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md), and infusing it into the skin and armor of their trained trolls to make an army of titanic, primal trolls. Descending from the mountains to establish their mines, the Orcs quickly became a serious threat throughout Upper Craglorn, going so far as to attack trade caravans to feed travelers to their trolls. However, they were soon betrayed on both sides. Lashburr sought to restore his people's faith in the stones, and recruited the aid of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in driving the Scaled Court out of the Valley of Scars and placing Agganor's head on a pike for all to see. At the same time, Braadoth was betrayed by Regent [Cassipia](Lore_Cassipia.md), who sent her companion Little Leaf to kill him and steal the Orcs' research on nirncrux infusion. Together, the Vestige and Little Leaf entered the Valley of Scars and killed the leaders of the Iron Orcs, ending the threat of a troll army.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

Despite the removal of the Serpent's influence, it has been theorized that the cultural shock of ironworking is one too deep to ever truly recover from, and an internal shift in psyche remains a long way off for the Iron Orcs.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Oo T-2)</sup>

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) some Iron Orcs could be found in the [Black Kiergo](Lore_Black_Kiergo.md) arena in [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Shirnama-25)</sup>
