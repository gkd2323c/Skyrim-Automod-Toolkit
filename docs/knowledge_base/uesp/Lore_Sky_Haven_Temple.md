[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/SR-place-Sky_Haven_Temple_Exterior.jpg/200px-SR-place-Sky_Haven_Temple_Exterior.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sky_Haven_Temple_Exterior.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sky_Haven_Temple_Exterior.jpg) Sky Haven Temple circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Sky Haven Temple was a large [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) fortress built among the [Karthspire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Karthspire) mountain of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). During the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) it was inhabited by [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman.md)'s [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) and served as an outpost in their crusade against [dragons](Lore_Dragon.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOTD-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AOD-2)</sup> It is believed to be a shrine to [Reman](Lore_Reman_Cyrodiil.md), as the ancient Blades revered him for ending the [Akaviri invasion](Lore_First_Akaviri_Invasion.md) under mysterious circumstances.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SREsbern-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-4)</sup>

By the late [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md) Rebellion took place in protest to forced conscriptions that were being imposed on the local populace. The Dragonguard stationed at the temple disobeyed Emperor [Kastav](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kastav)'s orders to suppress the rebellion, on the grounds that it violated their Oath of Allegiance. In their stead, Kastav sent an Akaviri commander named [Kalien](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kalien), who sacked the city the following year. The sacking resulted in the local [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) laying siege to the temple, as they did not distinguish between Akaviri.

The siege was eventually lifted shortly after the accession of [Reman II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II) as Emperor. The new emperor later visited the temple as part of his first visit to Skyrim in [1E 2806](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2806), to which the Dragonguard provided the honor guard.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOTD-1)</sup>

## Alduin's Wall
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9e/SR-misc-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg/250px-SR-misc-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-misc-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-misc-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg) Alduin's Wall [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/62/SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Alduin%27s_Wall.jpg) Alduin's Wall In [1E 2801](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2801) Emperor [Kastav](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kastav) ordered the Dragonguard to seize hostages from [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and [Hroldan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hroldan) to ensure that the jarls meet their conscription quotas, which worsened the relations of the Empire with both native [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) and their [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) occupants. Three years later, in [1E 2804](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2804), Reachfolk made arrangements with the representatives of the Dragonguard and provided the order with food when the Emperor cut off supplies as a result of refusal to suppress the Winterhold Rebellion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOTD-1)</sup>

In [1E 2812](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2812) Emperor Reman II permitted the construction of Alduin's Wall within the temple.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOTD-1)</sup> It is considered to be one of the best preserved examples of early Akaviri [sculptural relief](Lore_Statues.md), as well as a wonder of the ancient world. Its purpose was to record the accumulated dragonlore and prophecy that the Dragonguard possessed at the time.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SREsbern-3)</sup> Its main focus was a pictorial representation of the [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md). The prophecy foretold of five key historical events that would preface [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md)'s return after his expulsion during the [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TBot D-5)</sup> Given that Alduin's return was inevitable, the Dragonguard believed that the wall was their gift to the generations that were to follow.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SREsbern-3)</sup>

Craftsmen from temples across the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) were called upon to work on the wall, while Grandmaster [Jaiv-Yora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jaiv-Yora) of the Dragonguard oversaw the construction of the wall,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-GJY-6)</sup> and after six years it was completed. Emperor Reman II returned to the temple in [1E 2818](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2818) to officially dedicate the wall. The Blood Seal outside the temple was consecrated in the presence of all Akaviri Dragonguard in Skyrim. This allowed the temple to be sealed, only ever to be opened by a [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) to unlock its knowledge and power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOTD-1)</sup> The entrance into the temple, opened by the blood seal, was a giant stone effigy of Reman I.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:139</sup> Brother Annulus faithfully copied the records of the Annals of the Dragonguard of Sky Haven Temple in [2E 568](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_568).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AOTD-1)</sup>

As the eras passed and the original mission of the Dragonguard was forgotten, the temple was eventually abandoned, though it may have been used by the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) as late as the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRAFOTB-7)</sup> By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), it had become another inaccessible ruin with [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md) squatting around its outskirts.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-8)</sup> Following the return of Alduin in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) traveled to the ancient temple with the aid of the last remaining Blades to acquire the lost knowledge needed to defeat him.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Skyrim-8)</sup>
