Hidden lagoons containing singing waters, clear as fresh-blown glass. Flowers that whispered secrets when the wind blew, and stones with names too long for mortal tongues to speak. Such was the island of Artaeum.*
— [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/ON-map-Artaeum.jpg/200px-ON-map-Artaeum.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Artaeum.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Artaeum.jpg) Map of Artaeum The Isle of Artaeum ([/ɑːr ˈ t eɪ. ə m/](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English) *Ar- **tay** -um*) is the third largest island of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), located off the southeast coast of the main island of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMo A-1)</sup> south of the [Moridunon](Lore_Auridon.md) village of [Potansa](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Potansa) and west of the Summerset village of Runcibae —and it is significantly smaller than the other two islands.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOA-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-JLDND-3)</sup> Artaeum is the home of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), the oldest monastic group on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOA-2)</sup> It has a tendency to vanish from [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), being placed in a pocket realm for centuries at a time by the magic of the Psijics.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESOGalerion-4)</sup> It is said that this plane exists in a place harder to reach than even [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) or even realms "beyond" it, where even the magic of the [Sea Sload](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Sload) can't reach, thus allowing for the Order to research the truths of [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) without interruption. It is said that even the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) do not know where Artaeum is located, though the Psijics maintain telepathic contact with certain oracles of the [Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wyrd) to gain information on Tamriel's affairs, and the island's location can be discerned if one finds such an oracle and traces the connection back to the island itself, even allowing for a portal to it to be opened.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONMarieve-5)</sup> The island is protected by the Psijic Order's warding magic and only those that the Psijics have attuned to the wards can enter. Permanent portals leading to Artaeum, wherever it might be, exist in certain Psijic ruins in Summerset such as the Keep of the Eleven Forces.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONLil-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis-7)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOValsirenn-8)</sup>

The Isle of Artaeum has idyllic orchards and clear pastures, still and silent lagoons, misty woodlands, and the unique Psijic architecture that seems to be as natural as its surroundings. The [Ceporah Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceporah_Tower) is a relic from a civilization that predates the High Elves by several hundred years.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FOA-2)</sup> It is the location of the Dreaming Cavern, which [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) once used as some sort of portal into the realms of Oblivion,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LY4-9)</sup> but which has since been sealed off by the Psijics.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TDOO-10)</sup> The Isle of Artaeum was once removed from the world in a similar fashion to [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TIC-11)</sup> but reappeared about 500 years later. The Isle of Artaeum once again disappeared around 100 years before the [civil war](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and has not been seen since.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRMirabelle-12)</sup>

Directly beneath the island's [Ceporah Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceporah_Tower) lies the Dreaming Cave, which houses a portal that can be used to travel to any realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) and beyond, and is described as the place where all the [magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magicka) and other energies in Artaeum pool, like water dripping from a chain.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis-7)</sup>

The Isle of Artaeum was home to [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), the leader of [Necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MKOW-13)</sup>

## Notes
- Rather than being simply an island only inhabited by the Psijic Monkhood, in the [original D&D campaigns](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Pen_%26_Paper_Worldbuilding) used to formulate background lore for *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* the isle of Artaeum is described as an "island kingdom" which is inhabited by "islanders" in addition to the group of psionic mages (altered to Psijics for the final game lore), including the township of Calinas on the southern end of the island.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-JLDND-3)</sup> Given that the games are only scaled down representations of Tamriel rather than perfect encaptuailizations,<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-GKInterview-17)</sup> it is possible Artaeum has more on going on the island than what is seen inside the video games.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-JLDND-3)</sup> - Artaeum was sometimes spelled as **Artium** and **Atium** across the campaign's notes.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-JLDND-3)</sup>
