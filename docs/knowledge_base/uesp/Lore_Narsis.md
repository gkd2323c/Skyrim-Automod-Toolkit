—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Narsis is one of the eight major cities on the mainland of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> and is built on the southern shores of [Lake Hlaalu](Lore_Lake_Hlaalu.md). The city is historically known as the ancient capital of the former [Great House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), whose sphere of influence revolved around mercantilism and adaptation. They once reigned over west-central Morrowind.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHH-3)</sup> When it was originally founded in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), it was known as **Narsis-Where-All-Fervently-Praise-the-Prophet-Veloth** until it was shortened to its current name.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Narsis-4)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/ON-place-Lake_Hlaalu.jpg/200px-ON-place-Lake_Hlaalu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Lake_Hlaalu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Lake_Hlaalu.jpg) Lake Hlaalu [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a4/ON-place-Merchants%27_Stall_%28Narsis%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Merchants%27_Stall_%28Narsis%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Merchants%27_Stall_(Narsis).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Merchants%27_Stall_(Narsis).jpg) The merchant stalls As the capital of House Hlaalu, Narsis was the center of a district that spanned across central Morrowind.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHH-3)</sup> By the Third Era, it encompassed everything from [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) and [Suran](Lore_Suran.md) to the northeast in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GTV-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWSuran-6)</sup> [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md) on the [Inner Sea](Lore_Inner_Sea.md)'s southern coast,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRB5-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWMap-8)</sup> and [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md) in the [Valus Mountains](Lore_Valus_Mountains.md) to the north.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWMap-8)</sup>

The city itself is located in the [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) region, specifically the [Redolent Loam](Lore_Redolent_Loam.md) river basin centered around Lake Hlaalu, where it can be found on its south shore<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONNarsis-9)</sup> and east of the [Velothi Mountains](Lore_Velothi_Mountains.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONReverie-10)</sup> Lake Hlaalu is Narsis' water source.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RDDTW-11)</sup> Narsis is neighbored by several settlements, including [Heimlyn Keep](Lore_Heimlyn_Keep.md) to the northwest, Old Keep to the northeast, and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) to the south,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARMap-1)</sup> toward [Stormhold](Lore_Stormhold.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ARMap BM-12)</sup>

Narsis is one of Morrowind's smaller cities. It is built on a hillock that overlooks the lakefront to the north, and at the top of it lies the local kinhouse. However, the hill has a stone foundation built around it, providing ample space around it and elevation above the rest of the settlement. The city is surrounded by low-barring walls and much of the landscape is uneven ground; as a countermeasure, these hills are surrounded by stone platforms like under the kinhouse. The center of town is quaint parkland with tall stalks of [Emperor Parasols](Lore_Emperor_Parasol.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONNarsis-9)</sup> sprawled out chanterelle mushrooms, vibrant red barberry bushes, and large sharp rocks.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONReverie-10)</sup> The west side of the city contains the local marketplace consisting of trader tents. In the town outskirts, a lone watchtower can be found to the south and to the west lies the ruins of ancient Narsis,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONNarsis-9)</sup> which expands underneath the modern city and includes the foundation of the original settlement.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Narsis-4)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/LO-people-Veloth.jpg/200px-LO-people-Veloth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) A tapestry depicting Veloth and the exodus [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/ON-place-Narsis_Ruins.jpg/200px-ON-place-Narsis_Ruins.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Narsis_Ruins.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Narsis_Ruins.jpg) The ancient ruins of Narsis The settlement was originally founded by the Prophet, [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONReverie-10)</sup> and his [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) followers during the great exodus in the late-middle Merethic Era.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Narsis-4)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-VTP-18)</sup> The town was known as Narsis-Where-All-Fervently-Praise-the-Prophet-Veloth, but over time, its name was shortened.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONLoad Narsis-4)</sup> House Hlaalu is one of the original [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of Morrowind,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHH-3)</sup> which date their origins to the late Merethic Era during the decline of High Velothi Culture.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BTAOM-19)</sup> How they came to be associated with Narsis is unknown but House Hlaalu presided over the city since at least the 121st Year of the Golden Peace.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHH-3)</sup>

The house later partook in the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), a conflict in the seventh century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-20)</sup> Chimer forces were stationed in Narsis before they joined the Dwemer at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md). At around the same time, an army of [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) led by King [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) were also approaching the battle.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-FSOKW-21)</sup>

House Hlaalu and Narsis were active participants around the [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md), the provinical war between Morrowind and the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Narsis was sacked by the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) led by Amanti Sulla, who earned the moniker, the "Butcher of Narsis". The Dunmer retaliated in [1E 2904](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2904) with an ambush in Moontooth Pass led by Lady Jena Andalor. Though her forces defeated the Imperials down to a single man, Jena Andalor died in battle and a monument to her victory was erected and consecrated by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-ONUnused-22)</sup> In the bloody aftermath of the Four Score War, House Hlaalu under their leader, Grandmaster [Delmene Hlaalu](Lore_Delmene_Hlaalu.md) took advantage of Morrowind's decripit state and propelled the Great House into a gilded age of glory.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-HIPETOTP-23)</sup>

For the longest time, House Hlaalu and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) coveted territory south of Narsis that borders their respective districts. It was until [2E 559](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_559) that their usual confrontations evolved into full-scale war in the form of the War of Two Houses. After a small contingent of Hlaalu guards led by Purilla Falen was able to survive the first wave of Dres mercenaries, a [portal mage](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) attempted to summon one to Narsis for reinforcements. Despite the imposing numbers, Falen and the Hlaalu guards did not falter even when three of them were left. When the portal was finally opened, Hlaalu battle-merchants flooded the scene and ultimately secured the trading post. And although Purilla Falen died in battle, her efforts were never forgotten.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-WOTH-24)</sup>

### Narsis and the Llodos Plague
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/ON-quest-Unwanted_Guests.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Unwanted_Guests.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Unwanted_Guests.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Unwanted_Guests.jpg) The Llodos Plague in Lake Hlaalu [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg) Councilor Ralden Hlaalu of Narsis During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Llodos Plague](Lore_Llodos_Plague.md) was spreading all throughout Deshaan and was en route to Narsis after an outbreak in [Serkamora](Lore_Serkamora.md). It was initially developed by the alchemist, Merdyndril, though one member of the Maulborn Cult managed to create a more potent strain of the disease, which was later dumped into the city's water supply and Lake Hlaalu.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TLP-25)</sup>

Panic and paranoia swept through Narsis as several people disappeared in the middle of the night. Councilor Ralden was having trouble deciding on a proper course of action. His advisor, Giron Sadri offered an investigation and suggested quarantining the city from the outside, even if it harmed Narsis' economy. The Councilor enlisted the help of an [Agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) from the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) to undergo their own investigation, which eventually involved the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONProtection-26)</sup>

Their search brought them to the Maulborn camps on Lake Hlaalu, where the Tong agent, [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md) revealed Sadri's true intentions and his involvement with the Maulborn.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONUnwanted-27)</sup> At some point, the remaining citizens attempted to rally and search for the missing people, but it is unknown whether that amounted to anything. The agent defeated Councilor Sadri in the city ruins and was able to rescue the captured villagers along with Councilor Ralden. The situation in Narsis had subsided and the Agent led further assistance in the raid at the Obsidian Gorge.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONHiding-28)</sup>

### Narsis and the Third Empire
Back when [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) first directed the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Army.md) to conquer Morrowind, the Great House Hlaalu was the first to seek accommodations for the westerners and support for the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md). Since then, House Hlaalu had grown exponentially over the other great houses.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-OM-29)</sup> The Imperial Proconsul was established in Narsis and although they governed in an official capacity, some believe they were out of touch with what really happened across Morrowind.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Po M-15)</sup> In [3E 80](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_80), [Galana Septim](Lore_Galana_Septim.md), daughter of Emperor [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_Septim_II.md) was arranged to marry the Duke of Narsis, even though she was promised to the King of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude_(kingdom).md), [Mantiarco](Lore_Mantiarco.md). Her thirteen-year-old niece, [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) went in her stead.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TWQ1-30)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Narsis was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Vermothah and had a rivalry with Mournhold.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AR-13)</sup> By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), an Imperial Proconsul was seated in Narsis, from where he claimed to run the province of Morrowind.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Po M-15)</sup> House Hlaalu was stripped of its position as a Great House and removed from the Grand Council of Morrowind early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md),<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-HORR3-31)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DBArano-32)</sup> with its position filled by [House Sadras](Lore_House_Sadras.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-33)</sup> It is unknown what happened to their capital of Narsis as a result.

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/ON-crown_store-Green_Narsis_Guar.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Green_Narsis_Guar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Green_Narsis_Guar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Green_Narsis_Guar.jpg) The Green Narsis Guar [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/ON-hat-Crimson_Garden_Flower_Hat.jpg/200px-ON-hat-Crimson_Garden_Flower_Hat.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-hat-Crimson_Garden_Flower_Hat.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-hat-Crimson_Garden_Flower_Hat.jpg) Wreath of Narsis Peonies
- *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Dunmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Dunmer.md#Society)** sections on the Dunmer page.*

Narsis is the capital of House Hlaalu, who since their inception, have focused on the disciples of mercantile and trade, and often looked toward the [Book of Grasping Fortune](Lore_Grasping_Fortune.md) on advice and guidance. Without large numbers and vast holdings, they relied on themselves to built up their Great House and speak their way to success.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHH-3)</sup> In a land that always hated outsiders, House Hlaalu maintained limited commerce with nearby Cyrodiil throughout the ages.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1-2)</sup>

As the ancient Grandmaster Hesleth has said, *"The Dunmer who ignores opportunity deserves every failure that comes his way,"* and as the Grasping Fortune puts it *"seize every chance to make a profit, but remember that your reputation also has value."* That meant House Hlaalu strived for attaining the best possible deal or opportunity, and more elevating their house to glory. But for some, it meant receiving it through dubious and even illicit means.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHH-3)</sup>

Unlike other cities affiliated with them, Narsis is not built in its unique architectural style. Instead, the modern city is made in a style prevalent across central mainland Morrowind, with the most notable example of this being Mournhold.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONNarsis-9)</sup> The city's founder, Veloth is also House Hlaalu's patron [saint](Lore_Temple_Saints.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-MOTDGH-40)</sup> The city is known for its vibrant crimson [peonies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Narsis_Peony), which are made into vivid wreaths and fashioned into garden hats.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ONCollect-41)</sup>

The [Green Narsis Guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Narsis_Guar) is the standard mount and dray-beast in central Morrowind<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONGuar-42)</sup> and bantam guar hash is a dish native to Narsis.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONHash-43)</sup> Brewers from the city also make a signature [wickwheat ale](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md#Ale), which is also made from yerba mate and isinglass.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONWickwheat-44)</sup> In Narsis and nearby [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia_(city).md), the flying insects used by [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) are priced incredibly high.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1-2)</sup> The local guards of the city are known as the Narsis Protectors.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-ONProtector-45)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-LTEM-46)</sup>

- Narsis is erroneously referred to as the City of the Dead in Vivec's [34th sermon](Lore_36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_34.md), when it is supposed to be about [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-36LOVS34-47)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TSOT34S-48)</sup> Though later sources further corrobate this connection, there are actually multiple cities of the dead in Morrowind.
