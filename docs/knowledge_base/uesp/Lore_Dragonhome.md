## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/eb/ON-npc-Aspirant_Yolstagg.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Aspirant_Yolstagg.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aspirant_Yolstagg.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aspirant_Yolstagg.jpg) Aspirant Yolstagg, the leader of the resurgent Dragon Cult branch, circa 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-npc-Heiruna_the_Merciless.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Heiruna_the_Merciless.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Heiruna_the_Merciless.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Heiruna_the_Merciless.jpg) Heiruna the Merciless, circa 2E 582
### Ancient Past
In the ancient times, the barrow known as Dragonhome was inhabited by the Dragon Cult.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonhome-1)</sup>

### Second Era
During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), it was rumored that a new group of cultists had taken over the area, although it was dismissed by some.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Dragonhome-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HDDWAW-3)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a vigilante mage [Heiruna](Lore_Heiruna.md) came into contact with Dragon Cult revivalists, who believed that they can summon a [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) and return the Dragon Cult to prominence.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HD-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DATDC-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Aspirant-6)</sup> She tracked them and found them in their hideout in Dragonhome.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HJ-7)</sup> She infiltrated the ancient ruin with intention to massacre the cultists,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HJ-7)</sup> but was defeated and turned into a doll.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HD-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ATD-8)</sup> She was eventually found by [an adventurer](Lore_The_Vestige.md) who agreed to help her reclaim her human form and defeat the resurgent cult.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HD-4)</sup> They performed a ritual that let her reclaim her original human form and fought the cultists.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SP-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-HD-4)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-HTM-9)</sup>

In the same year [Coterie of Organized Scholars](Lore_Coterie_of_Organized_Scholars.md) sought a research assistant. One of its scholars, Nezashul was doing research for a treatise on ancient Nord embalming practices, specifically how they might help to improve modern healing techniques. The scholarly organization requested the help of the [contract worker](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to acquire samples of organs and tissues such as eyes,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PE-10)</sup> brains,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PB-11)</sup> and hearts<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PH-12)</sup> of the ancient Nord entombed in the Dragonhome barrow. The scholars believed that the ruin would be unspoiled due to the presence of Draugr who guard it against the intruders, but they were unaware of the resurfacing Dragon Cult who inhabited Dragonhome. Despite the presence of the cultists their contractor managed to retrieve the requested organs for the coterie.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TPOL-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Nezashul-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Tinzen-15)</sup>

Shadow of Rahjin,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SOR-16)</sup> a lyre associated with the trickster god, [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md) was once hidden in Dragonohme.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Bards-17)</sup> It was described as "Great creature's home, Westest of Western."<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-NHL-18)</sup> An adventurer found the lute and delivered it to the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Bards2-19)</sup>
