— [Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YB-1)</sup> Bthuand Mzahnch was a prominent [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) [smith](Lore_Smithing.md), [tonal architect](Lore_Tonal_Architecture.md) and scholar. He worked under the leadership of [Kagrenac](Lore_Kagrenac.md) alongside other notable figures such as [Dahrk Mezalf](Lore_Dahrk_Mezalf.md). His contributions to the Dwemer's technological advancements were significant.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YB-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DMQ-3)</sup>

Aside from his craftsmanship, Mzahnch was also a skilled writer. His most well known work, *[The Egg of Time](Lore_The_Egg_of_Time.md)* was a refutation of a popular theory among some Dwemer that using the power of the [Heart of Lorkhan](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) was too great a risk. In this book, Mzahnch presents several compelling arguments against this notion, challenging the idea that the power of the heart should not be utilized. Similar to his master Kagrenac, Bthuand Mzahnch also had faith in the Heart's potential for improving their race. While Bthuand's book argues that working with the Heart could not cause disastrous outcome, the disappearance of the Dwemer suggests otherwise.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-YB-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BD-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TEOT-4)</sup>

Little is known of his fate, but almost all the Dwemer mysteriously disappeared in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DII-5)</sup>
