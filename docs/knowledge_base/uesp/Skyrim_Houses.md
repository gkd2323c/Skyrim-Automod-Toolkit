# Houses


[Sleeping](Skyrim_Sleeping.md) in your house provides the [Well Rested](Skyrim_Well_Rested.md) bonus, a 10% boost to skill increases for 8 in-game hours. Sleeping in your house with your spouse provides the [Lover's Comfort](Skyrim_Lover%27s_Comfort.md) bonus, a 15% boost to skill increases for 8 hours. It also rejuvenates your Health and Magicka. You will not gain any rested bonus if you have the Beast Blood effect from becoming a [werewolf](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md) or have [the Lover Stone](Skyrim_The_Lover_Stone.md) activated.

### Breezehome
| [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/SR-place-Breezehome.jpg/200px-SR-place-Breezehome.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Breezehome.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Breezehome.jpg) Breezehome, in Whiterun Located in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md), right next to [Warmaiden's](Skyrim_Warmaiden%27s.md) and near the main gate, is [Breezehome](Skyrim_Breezehome.md). The house has two stories, but is very small. However, Breezehome is the cheapest of all purchasable houses in the game and makes for an excellent starter home. The home's proximity to a blacksmith is very convenient for players who need [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) amenities nearby. It features more storage space than [Honeyside](#Honeyside) and [Proudspire Manor](#Proudspire_Manor), but doesn't have any mannequins. ### Honeyside
| [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/SR-place-Honeyside.jpg/200px-SR-place-Honeyside.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Honeyside.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Honeyside.jpg) Honeyside, in Riften [Honeyside](Skyrim_Honeyside.md) is a two-story house located in the northwest corner of [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md). The house is small, but slightly larger than Breezehome. One unique feature is that it has access from both within the city, and from a dock outside the city walls. This makes Honeyside a useful home for players who wish to avoid loading screens or players who are [vampires](Skyrim_Vampirism.md) as the house can be entered without attracting any attention or even venturing into the city. Another unique feature of Honeyside is that it can be upgraded to have a large garden, with many harvestable plants. Coupled with the home's close proximity to [Elgrim's Elixirs](Skyrim_Elgrim%27s_Elixirs.md), this makes Honeyside an especially useful house for alchemists. However, the house has the least amount of storage of any of the properties. The house can be upgraded to feature an [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Lab.md), [arcane enchanter](Skyrim_Arcane_Enchanter.md) and a [tanning rack](Skyrim_Tanning_Rack.md). | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | | |

## Hearthfire Houses<sup>[HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md)</sup>
With the [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) add-on, it is possible to [construct](Skyrim_Construction.md) and [furnish](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Hearthfire-Furnishing_Options) your very own house. Three unique plots of land are available to build a house on throughout Skyrim, and the rooms of each house may be customized, with the houses potentially becoming larger than any of the city houses. Unique to the Hearthfire homes is that [personal stewards](Skyrim_Personal_Steward.md) may be recruited to serve each property, acquiring additional staff and furnishing the homes. [Bards](Skyrim_Bard.md) and private [carriage drivers](Skyrim_Transport.md#Carriage) can also be hired. The purchase of land for the Hearthfire houses does not add to the "Houses Owned" number in the game statistics.

## Free Shacks
There are several empty shacks around Skyrim, many of which contain a bed and at least one [non-respawning container](Skyrim_Containers.md#Safe_Containers), making them useful as a home until you are able to purchase one:

- [Alchemist's Shack](Skyrim_Alchemist%27s_Shack.md), near [Ivarstead](Skyrim_Ivarstead.md), has an end table that is safe for storage. It also has the benefit of an alchemy lab.
- [Meeko's Shack](Skyrim_Meeko%27s_Shack.md), west-northwest of [Morthal](Skyrim_Morthal.md), has an end table that is safe for storage. The barrel and shelves respawn, however, regularly filling the house with junk.
- [Riverside Shack](Skyrim_Riverside_Shack.md), southwest of [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) along the [White River](Skyrim_White_River.md), has one safe wardrobe and a bed.
- [Abandoned Shack](Skyrim_Abandoned_Shack.md), east of [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), will remain accessible after you complete either of its associated quests. It contains an unowned bed, but no safe containers.
- [Traitor's Post](Skyrim_Traitor%27s_Post.md), east of [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), has two wardrobes and a cupboard that you can store items in. The chest there respawns along with the bandits.
- [Lund's Hut](Skyrim_Lund%27s_Hut.md), north-northwest of [Rorikstead](Skyrim_Rorikstead.md), is a farmhouse with a non-functioning bed. Only the chest and end table are safe containers.
- [Anise's Cabin](Skyrim_Anise%27s_Cabin.md), southwest of [Riverwood](Skyrim_Riverwood.md), can become a good early-game base of operations, although it is initially occupied. See the article for details.

## Other Shelters
These properties generally possess safe containers that do not respawn, making them just as great as a normal house until you have enough money to purchase a property.

- **[Abandoned House](Skyrim_Abandoned_House.md):** The Abandoned House in Markarth can be lived in after killing the Vigilant in [The House of Horrors](Skyrim_The_House_of_Horrors.md), as it is set to never respawn and therefore none of the containers automatically reset. There is a usable bed and 62 containers within the house.
- **[The Altar of Thrond](Skyrim_Altar_of_Thrond.md):** This cave makes a useful beginning home on the island of [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md). You first have to kill three [hagravens](Skyrim_Hagraven.md), but once disposed of you will have 9 safe storage containers (an alchemist's corpse, 3 apothecary satchels, 2 barrels and 3 chests.) There are 3 beds, a cooking spit, and an Alchemy Lab and Arcane Enchanter. Furthermore, sleeping there before completing the quest [Cleansing the Stones](Skyrim_Dragonborn_Quests.md#Cleansing_the_Stones) will not result in being transported to a random [All-Maker Stone](Skyrim_All-Maker_Stones.md).
- **[Cracked Tusk Keep](Skyrim_Cracked_Tusk_Keep.md):** Every container in the Keep is safe and once the bandits are killed, they never respawn. It comes with an Alchemy Lab, Tanning Rack, Forge, Grindstone and Workbench, as well as seven beds (six bedrolls and a large bed in the chief's room).
- **[Drelas' Cottage](Skyrim_Drelas%27_Cottage.md):** This house with both an alchemy station and an arcane enchanter becomes available as soon as the hostile [owner Drelas](Skyrim_Drelas.md) is dealt with. However the location is far from most other facilities. It is also rather close to a dragon lair and followers will refuse to interact, the only exceptions being Cicero and Serana. Also note that you cannot sleep there, as you will still be "trespassing" even after Drelas is killed.
- **[Faendal's House](Skyrim_Faendal%27s_House.md):** A house located in Riverwood has a bed and a cooking spit. It becomes available if you choose to side with him in the quest [A Lovely Letter](Skyrim_A_Lovely_Letter.md). All containers except the chest at the head of the bed and the barrel are safe. Surface items do respawn. An alchemy station is immediately available in the nearby Sleeping Giant Inn.
- **[Helgen Inn](Skyrim_Helgen.md):** The ruined inn in Helgen features a wardrobe and an end table that can be used for storage, neither of which resets. The inn also features a perfectly preserved and functional bed. This makes for a very useful shelter at the beginning of the game, although the fort will spawn bandits if not visited for some time.
- **[Inns](Skyrim_Inns.md):** While most inns do not provide any safe storage, their beds generally give the [Well Rested](Skyrim_Well_Rested.md) bonus, same as an owned house, and if the innkeeper is befriended, then most inns have a bed that can be slept in for free. - **[Sleeping Giant Inn](Skyrim_Sleeping_Giant_Inn.md):** This inn is of special note, due to the [alchemy lab](Skyrim_Alchemy_Lab.md), [arcane enchanter](Skyrim_Arcane_Enchanter.md), and two safe chests in the secret [Blades](Skyrim_Blades.md) room, which make this an excellent player home after you have gained access to it during [A Blade in the Dark](Skyrim_A_Blade_in_the_Dark.md).
- **[Nilheim Tower](Skyrim_Nilheim.md):** This ancient Nordic tower is occupied by unique, non-respawning bandits. Its treasure chest and other containers in the tower are safe, and the top level contains some bedrolls, so it has all the basic amenities. It also has a wood chopping block but no crafting stations.
