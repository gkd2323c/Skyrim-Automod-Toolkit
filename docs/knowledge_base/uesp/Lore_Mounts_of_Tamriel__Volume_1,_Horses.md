Mounts of Tamriel: Volume 1, Horses **by [Fadus Falto](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Fadus_Falto) of Skingrad** *History of horses in Tamriel* *
---
Common? Perhaps. Ubiquitous, yes. But boring? No, I say to you. The [horse](Lore_Horse.md) is everywhere, used in a myriad of applications and by essentially every culture on the [continent](Lore_Tamriel.md). From sturdy riding mounts to draft horses, these beasts fill the streets of our towns and cities so thick as to pass without notice. But why? Why is the horse so common?

The answer lies, as ever, in our [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) past. When the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) arrived on mainland Tamriel from [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md) (a subject I discuss at length in my treatise), they brought with them a sturdy stock of horses already well integrated into their culture. These shaggy beasts were well adapted to the cold climes of that distant continent, but it's worth noting that careful study has revealed there was already an indigenous population of horses on the mainland as well. Undomesticated, totally, by the Elvish cultures that lived across its length and breadth. It was not until well after the Nedes spread across the continent that we see evidence of horse husbandry in [Elvish](Lore_Mer.md) settlements.

What seems, at first glance, like a strange gap in Elvish ingenuity is in fact a function of well-developed cultural structures. Elves domesticated beasts like the [hippogriff](Lore_Hippogriff.md), the [elk](Lore_Elk.md), and the esoteric [indrik](Lore_Indrik.md) so far back in their distant past that they themselves have only a dim understanding of when those creatures became companion animals to their people. As well, we must consider the widespread acceptance of [magical study](Lore_Magic.md) at almost every level of [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) society. Alongside the benefits to taming whatever creature catches your cultural fancy, the ability to move earth and stone to construct buildings with the force of your will makes the common draft horse seem somewhat tame in comparison.

Whereas the peoples coming from the frozen north looked upon their shaggy steeds and saw salvation. Warmth from the cold, [milk](Lore_Milk.md) for the young, skins for armoring their hides, and of course sturdy transportation and cartage across vast distances. The shaggy steeds from the north and the indigenous horses of the mainland were wholly compatible from a breeder's perspective (a fascinating note worth exploring in a volume all its own), and we see evidence of form and function diversification in many settlements as the newly arrived people moved across the continent.

Elvish settlements, especially those without ready access to the resources of major cities, quickly adopted these beasts as "work horses." And by the time of the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) [diaspora](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) horse usage across mainland Tamriel was well established.
