*…So Ysgramor collected the laments of the Giant-Wives and brought them to Froa and Grosta, who twisted them into the Woeful Bowstring, so that Ysgramor could re-string his mighty bow Long-Launcher. And thereafter did Long-Launcher sigh when it was borne, and moan when it was shot. And Ysgramor decided to take it hunting.*
— *[Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus](Lore_Fragmentae_Abyssum_Hermaeus_Morus.md)*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAHM-1)</sup> Grosta was an [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) warrior and a member of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). Along with her sister [Froa](Lore_Froa.md), they were known as the **great Shield-Sisters**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOTR-2)</sup> They were credited with the creation of the [Woeful Bowstring](Lore_Woeful_Bowstring.md), an essential part of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s great bow, the [Long-Launcher](Lore_Long-Launcher.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAHM-1)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/07/ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Ylgar.jpg) Ylgar, the captain of Darumzu [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ysgramor.jpg) Ysgramor, the wielder of the Long-Launcher *[Fragmentae Abyssum Hermaeus Morus](Lore_Fragmentae_Abyssum_Hermaeus_Morus.md)*, a legendary account of Ysgramor's deeds from the frozen continent of Atmora, speaks of Grosta. According to the text, Ysgramor gathered the laments of the [Giant](Lore_Giant.md) -Wives and brought them to Grosta and Froa. Together, the Shield-Sisters twisted the laments into the Woeful Bowstring, enabling Ysgramor to re-string his mighty bow, the Long-Launcher.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAHM-1)</sup> The bow served Ysgramor well, as he wielded it during his legendary hunt of the White [Stag](Lore_Stag.md) of Forelgrim,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAHM-1)</sup> the great Snow- [Goose](Lore_Goose.md) Faldrosta,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TOOTO-3)</sup> and in his battle against the ancient [Snow Elf](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) wizard named [Serenarth](Lore_Serenarth_(Snow_Elf).md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EGT-4)</sup>

[Volume two](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return,_Vol_2.md) of the *[Songs of the Return](Lore_Songs_of_the_Return.md)* also recounts the sisters. According to the text, Grosta and Froa were so close that they thought and spoke as one. They studied under the great warrior and wise war-teacher [Adrimk](Lore_Adrimk.md), who first taught them to dance among the blades. Adrimk was a legendary warrior in her own right, shaping the Shield-Sisters into great fighters. [Ylgar](Lore_Ylgar.md), son of Ysgramor, set forth to the academies of honored soldiers, seeking his most trusted friends and advisors to join him on the venture of [the Return](Lore_The_Return.md). In this way, he was able to gather many loyal allies, including Grosta and Froa, their teacher, and numerous other students of hers. Together, they manned [Darumzu](Lore_Darumzu.md), one of the grand Atmoran ships, and successfully landed on the mainland of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SOTR-2)</sup>

[Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) women continued to bear the name Grosta as late as [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), though it is uncertain whether this was in honor of the legendary Grosta herself.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Grosta-5)</sup> The name was also used by [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), although this was unrelated to the Shield-Sister of legend.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Redguard1-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Redguard2-7)</sup>
