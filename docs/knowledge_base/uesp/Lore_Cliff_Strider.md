Cliff Striders (also spelled as **Cliffstriders** or **Cliff-Striders**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Feast-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCSS-2)</sup> are a species of large, winged [reptilian](Lore_Reptile.md) creatures native to parts of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), such as the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md) and formerly [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Cliff Strider ESO-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VFa F-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VTAN-5)</sup> They are closely related to [Cliff Racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md) and [Cliff Darters](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cliff_Darter),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LCL-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> with Cliff Striders themselves occasionally being erroneously referred to as Cliff Racers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Unlike Cliff Racers, Cliff Striders aren't the strongest fliers, and spend much more time on the ground.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONBitter Coast Strider-10)</sup> Despite this they are still powerful predators that prey on much of the local wildlife, including [man](Lore_Man.md) and [mer](Lore_Mer.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Cliff Strider ESO-3)</sup> Their young are known as Cliff Skippers and are hatched from large nest mounds built from dung. After hatching, the young enjoy some measure of protection from at least one of their parents.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONHatching APlan-11)</sup> When threatened, they have been known to projectile-vomit the contents of their stomach into a stinging, acidic spray.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Cliff Strider ESO-3)</sup> It is said the older a Cliff Strider gets, the more crazaed and dangerous it becomes.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

It was recorded that the physiology and behavior of the Cliff Strider is similar to the [hackwings](Lore_Hackwing.md) of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> and it is possible that they shared ancestry. Some historians theorize this possible divergence resulted from [Dark Elves](Lore_Dunmer.md) raiding Black Marsh for thousands of years to bring back things they deemed valuable, such as slave labor, livestock, and critters.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-LAMQA-14)</sup> By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Cliff Strider population of Vvardenfell had been exterminated by the [Ash Blight](Lore_Ash_Blight.md), along with several other unique Vvardenfell species.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VTAN-5)</sup> Though Cliff Striders could also be found on the mainland.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Cliff Strider ESO-3)</sup>

## Variants
Several breeds exist but share a commonality with their loud screeches and terrible smell.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCSS-2)</sup> The most common was known as the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) Cliff Strider. Despite their name, they were once found all around the island of Vvardenfell and even on the Telvanni Peninsula.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONBitter Coast Strider-10)</sup> Other subspecies were more localized but could be distinguished by their coloration. The [Ascadian Cliff](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md) Strider was known for its beautiful blue coloring,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ascadian-15)</sup> while the breed from the [Molag Amur](Lore_Molag_Amur.md) area were known for their golden coloring.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONMolag Amur Strider-16)</sup> Flocks of red and black Striders could be found in [Bal Ur](Lore_Bal_Ur.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
Despite their aggressive nature, and distasteful attributes, Cliff Striders are still admired by Vvardenfell culture, having been the subject of popular [songs](Lore_Songs.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TCSS-2)</sup> Some cliff skippers tamed as pets by reptile fans for ornamental reasons.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Ascadian-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONMolag Amur Strider-16)</sup> In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md#Sixth_Century), one Thetys Ramarys of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md), selectively bred Bitter Coast Cliff Striders for their wing strength, in the hopes that they would eventually be able to dominate Vvardenfell's skies.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONBitter Coast Strider-10)</sup> The same breeder created bait kits to attract young cliff skippers.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

Dead Cliff Striders are sometimes taxidermized for decoration.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Stuffed Cliff Strider replicas of juveniles have been known to look so authentic that people fear letting adult Cliff Striders see them.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup> Cliffstrider membrane stewed in strips is popular [Dunmeri dish](Lore_Cuisine.md), though calling it stew is considered by them to be an insult.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Feast-1)</sup>
