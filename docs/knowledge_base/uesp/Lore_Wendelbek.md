[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/OB-place-Wendelbek.jpg/200px-OB-place-Wendelbek.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Wendelbek.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Wendelbek.jpg) Wendelbek circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Wendelbek is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) located on the northern shore of the [Panther River](Lore_Panther_River.md) within the deeper parts of the [Nibenay Basin](Lore_Nibenay_Basin.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBMap-1)</sup> Its subsections of *Aldmerisel*, *Sel Aran Mathmedli*, and *Silaseli* roughly translate to "Ancestor's Hall", "Hall of the Exiled King", and "Shining Halls" respectively in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md). It is noted for its large subterranean halls and [Welkynd stone](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) supply,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OB-2)</sup> and for being the namesake for the Scouring of Wendelbek.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASi V-3)</sup>

## History
In the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), an event known as the [Narfinsel](Lore_Narfinsel.md) Schism marked the beginning of a violent internal conflict among the [Ayleid city-states](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) of Cyrod. This conflict between the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) -worshipping [Barsaebics](Lore_Barsaebics.md) and the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) -worshipping [Daedraphiles](Lore_Daedraphiles.md) reached a climax in [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198) at the Scouring of Wendelbek, which ended all organized opposition to Daedra worship and drove the Barsaebics out of Cyrod.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ASi V-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Abnur Questions-4)</sup> However, the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) began less than fifty years later, forcing most Ayleids to flee Cyrodiil or perish at the hands of the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md). The burgeoning [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) toppled the remaining Ayleid cities of Cyrodiil one by one, and Wendelbek subsequently fell into ruin.

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins of Wendelbek were plundered by the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), who obtained one of the Ten Ancestors, which was ultimately sold to the Ayleid enthusiast [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBCollector-5)</sup> At the time, the ruins contained a coven of [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md), likely to be a part of the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OB-2)</sup>

## Notes
- The first portion of its name, *Wend*, translates to "Journey" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md), but the translation of the remaining portion, *elbek*, is unknown.
- The settlement's original Ayleid name has been lost to time. The name *Wendelbek* only came into use some time after the fall of White-Gold.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
