# Ienith

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/MW-place-Ienith_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg/200px-MW-place-Ienith_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ienith_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Ienith_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) The Ienith Ancestral Tomb (Morrowind) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/SR-npc-Aranea_Ienith.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Aranea_Ienith.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Aranea_Ienith.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Aranea_Ienith.jpg) Aranea Ienith, a priestess of Azura The Ienith clan<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_Name)</sup> is an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) that lives in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They are one of the thirty revered families of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATOTTRF-1)</sup> and a sub-faction of the Great [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONIeneth-2)</sup> Many of their members are either devout followers of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) faith or involved in criminal activities. The **Ienith Ancestral Tomb** is located in the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) of eastern Vvardenfell, northeast of Falansarano and west of [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONIeneth-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWIenith-3)</sup>

## General Information
With the other twenty-nine ancient families of Vvardenfell, the Ienith clan hid the [Library of Andule](Lore_Library_of_Andule.md), the secret repository of the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) and placed a clue in their ancestral tomb.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONBradyn-4)</sup> Its location remained a secret for many years until Bradyn of the Library of Vivec enlisted an [outlander](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to find the tombs and uncover it in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). They were able to see all thirty tombs and find the lost Library of Andule.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONAncestral-5)</sup> As one of House Indoril's subclans, the Ienith clan held substantial power in the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE1-6)</sup>

In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Boderia Ienith was a hunter in [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md). She was present in Obsidian Gorge, after the Maulborn Cult was defeated and the [Llodos Plague](Lore_Llodos_Plague.md) was destroyed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONBoderia-7)</sup> There were many Ienith clan members active in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Some of them were involved in divine worship. Mundrila Ienith was a pilgrim who visited [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md) and stayed at the Pilgrim's Rest tavern.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWMundrila-8)</sup> Dinuro and Methulu were worshippers of the [Bad Daedra](Lore_Bad_Daedra.md) and lived in their [shrines](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md), namely [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) in [Bal Ur](Lore_Bal_Ur.md) and [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) in Almurbalarammi respectively.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWDinuro-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MWMethulu-10)</sup>

Many Ienith members were scattered across Vvardenfell and involved in several criminal activities. Dralisi Ienith was a [necromancer](Lore_Necromancers.md) hiding in Yesamsi.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MWDralisi-11)</sup> Fanasa and Rinori Ienith were bandits in Beshara and Suririlk, respectively.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWFanasa-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MWRinori-13)</sup> Rols Ienith was another bandit and follower of Llaren Terano, a powerful [sorcerer](Lore_Sorcerers.md) who ran a gladiatorial arena in [Rotheran](Lore_Rotheran.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWRols-14)</sup> Rols owned Rotheran's [propylon index](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md#Teleporters) but was later killed by an [adventurer](Lore_Nerevarine.md) collecting all of Vvardenfell's propylons.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-MWIndex-15)</sup> Galdres Ienith was a smuggler in Punammu, a cave east of [Pelagiad](Lore_Pelagiad.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MWGaldres-16)</sup>

Felvan Ienith was a member of the Warlords, a group of rogues led by Dovor Oren that killed and stole from local nobles in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), in preparation for a possible war between the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TRWarlords-17)</sup> Navil and Ranes Ienith were considered the best enforcers in the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md) and known together as the Brothers Ienith.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-MWJim-18)</sup> They worked under the Tong's kingpin, [Orvas Dren](Lore_Orvas_Dren.md), and lived in the [Dren Plantation](Lore_Dren_Plantation.md) north of [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md). They were targets of both the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) and the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md), but it is unknown which one of them claimed their bounty.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-MWBrothers-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MWWrit-20)</sup> In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), Aranea Ienith was a skilled sorceress and coverted to [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) worship after the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md) ([4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5)), resulting in Vvardenfell's destruction. She later visited the shrine in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), near [Mount Anthor](Lore_Mount_Anthor.md) in [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md), and refused to leave until she fulfilled her purpose.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SRArenea-21)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_Name) | The original ancestral tomb in *[Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)* is known as Ienith, but in *[ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:ESO)*, the ancestral tomb and its mention in *[Ancestral Tombs of the Thirty Revered Families](Lore_Ancestral_Tombs_of_the_Thirty_Revered_Families.md)* is called **Ieneth**, which is another surname used by others characters. There is no Ieneth Ancestral Tomb and the tomb's location in *ESO* matches the Ienith's tomb in *Morrowind*. |
