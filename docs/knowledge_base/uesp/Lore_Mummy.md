# Mummy

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/LG-cardart-Ruin_Shambler.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Ruin_Shambler.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ruin_Shambler.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Ruin_Shambler.jpg) A mummified Khajiit (Legends) Mummies are a powerful form of [undead](Lore_Undead.md). The practice of mummification of the honored dead is common in many cultures across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). This often involves covering the preserved corpse in distinctive cloth wraps; such [mummy wrappings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mummy_wrappings) can be used in [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Sometimes the body is also fitted with a death mask.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Bodies preserved in such a manner are liable to rise from the dead, either due to an inability to pass on<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> or due to the actions of a [necromancer](Lore_Necromancy.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTwo Queens-5)</sup> The process of mummification is believed to protect a corpse's ligaments and muscle from the detrimental effects of rot, thereby greatly increasing its durability, stability, and mobility once reanimated.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-CPF-6)</sup>

The first step to creating a mummy is to soak the decaying corpse in a bath of [salt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Salt) or natron for at least one month to halt decay and remove unpleasant odors. More salts may need to be applied in moist climates such as [Argonia](Lore_Argonia.md) or [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) if the salt bath becomes saturated.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CP3-7)</sup> A wide variety of embalming tools can be used to remove the vital organs before or after this process,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> although some necromancers skip this step as there is little practical reason to do so.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CP3-7)</sup> Any organs removed during the embalming process are stored separately in crypt jars by many cultures on Tamriel.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The next step is to wrap the body in cloth or linen to preserve and protect it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CP3-7)</sup> The wrappings and the corpse's desiccation results in a greater stiffness of the body in comparison to [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md), requiring proper necromantic rituals to imbue the mummy with enough strength to move itself. However, the result is a much stronger undead servant who can follow commands with more independence and understanding.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CP3-7)</sup> Mummification is sometimes carried out on living victims instead of corpses.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup>

Undead mummies require a [silver](Lore_Silver.md) or better [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) to be harmed, and often carry horrible [diseases](Lore_Disease.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DF-12)</sup> Those who take a body part from a mummy can be subjected to a "mummy's curse", and will receive nightly hauntings until the mummy is destroyed.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

## Cultural Practices
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/MW-misc-Mummy.jpg/200px-MW-misc-Mummy.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Mummy.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Mummy.jpg) An Ashlander mummy of the [Urshilaku tribe](Lore_Urshilaku_Tribe.md) (Morrowind) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/86/ON-pet-Grisly_Mummy_Tabby.jpg/200px-ON-pet-Grisly_Mummy_Tabby.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Grisly_Mummy_Tabby.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-pet-Grisly_Mummy_Tabby.jpg) A mummified tabby cat (ESO) Certain [Elven](Lore_Mer.md) peoples are known to practice mummification of their dead. The [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) may have mummified their visages in a bronze tone.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MAH-14)</sup> The [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) use [ash salts](Lore_Ash_Salt.md) to preserve the bodies of the dead, unlike [House](Lore_Great_Houses.md) [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) who usually cremate their dead. The Ashlanders glorify their dead in accordance with their traditions of [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md), and these mummies are prominently displayed at the tribe's burial site.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) also practiced mummification, and undead Ayleid mummies are often powerful spellcasters.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFenlord-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup> The [Barsaebic Ayleid](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) necromancer kings of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) became undead mummies known as [Fenlords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fenlords) (or sometimes Mummy Lords).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOZahra-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> These revenants continue to rule over their undead servants in the depths of [Barsaebic ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md#Black_Marsh).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOFenlord-16)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOZahra-18)</sup> The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) consider mummified monkey paws to be an item of good fortune.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MMP-20)</sup> Mummified baby [apes](Lore_Ape.md) with painted-on elven features were sometimes used as dolls for children to dress-up.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

[Men](Lore_Man.md) also widely practice mummification. Undead mummies are a common sight in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DF-12)</sup> The practice is very common among [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md); mummification of the dead is a tradition that stretches back to the first arrivals from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), and undead mummies often plague Redguard crypts.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup> The crypts beneath the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) are also known to contain undead mummies,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup> and the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) have been known to perform necromantic binding rituals on condemned prisoners to turn them into mummified undead guardians.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup> The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) mummify [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md) paws and cap the ankles with silver, believing they can ward away [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-GP-27)</sup> Many believe that mummified [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) claws retain a Hag's ability to bestow curses, but there are very few Nords who would try to test this out.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-MHC-28)</sup>

The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) also widely practice mummification.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOTwo Queens-5)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Grisly Tabby-30)</sup> They have even been known to reanimate the mummified corpses of [cats](Lore_Cat.md) to serve as eternal rodent-catchers.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Grisly Tabby-30)</sup> Mummified [horses](Lore_Horse.md) may also be reanimated to serve as grisly mounts.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-GHM-31)</sup> Rumor portend that ancient mummified Dagi paws can grant any wish, though such magic often comes with an unexpected price.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-MDP-32)</sup>

The tail of Argonian war hero [Never-Gives-Up](Lore_Never-Gives-Up.md) was mummified and attached to an engraved plaque,<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-CE-33)</sup> but how common this practice is among Argonians is unknown. In [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md), mummified monkey heads are used as a charm to ward off evil swamp spirits.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-SMH-34)</sup>

[Banekin](Lore_Banekin.md) are sometimes mummified, although it is unclear what this process means for an immortal [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-35)</sup> Mummified [Harpy](Lore_Harpy.md) talons can be affixed to a short handle, seemingly for use as a talisman or backscratcher.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-MHT-36)</sup> [Grahl eyeballs](Lore_Grahl_Eyeball.md) are sometimes mummified and repurposed as tough, leathery pouches for carrying small items.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-GEGP-37)</sup>
