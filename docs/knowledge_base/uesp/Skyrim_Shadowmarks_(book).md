---
*Note: For a list of locations in the game that are known to have a shadowmark, see the [article](Skyrim_Shadowmarks_(Thieves_Guild).md) about Shadowmarks themselves.*

---

![N](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/N_letter.png/54px-N_letter.png)
eed to know your way around, eh? Don't want to stumble into a necromancer's house or fall into another trap set up by the city guard? Then you need to read this book from cover to cover. Learning to identify the shadowmark can mean the difference between making a fortune and ending up with a blade in your gut.

The clever little marks are carved all over [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md)... mostly on the doorframes or fronts of buildings, but you can find them pretty much anywhere a thief's been. It's the way we talk to each other without talking. Keeps the newer thieves from becoming dead thieves and all that nonsense. There aren't that many of the bloody things, so I don't want to hear any excuses about not having the time to learn them.

Anyway, enough of my gabbing. Time to put your wizard's cap on and do a little research.
