*To be the Worldskin is to be everything, and to be everything is to be nothing.*
— *[Knowing Satakal](Lore_Knowing_Satakal.md)* Satakal **the Worldskin**, also called the **First Serpent**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONPriestess Sermenh-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Satakal Skinrazer-3)</sup> **Serpent God**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-4)</sup> and the **World-Snake**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWAHD-5)</sup> is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) god of everything, and a fusion of the concepts of [Anu](Lore_Anu.md) and [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md)<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup> ([Satak](Lore_Satak.md) and [Akel](Lore_Akel.md)),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup> or the habitable universe resulting of their interaction, also called the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md) or "The Gray Maybe".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup> They can be considered analogous to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) in a way,<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-French TM-7)</sup> being the first being "born" from the interplay of the original forces.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup> Driven by hunger to eat one world to begin another, Satakal also has much in common with the [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md). In Yokudan mythology, Satakal has done (and still does) this many times over, a cycle which prompted the birth of spirits that could survive the transition, notably [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), the first who learned how to do so. These spirits ultimately became the Yokudan pantheon.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup>

Satakal is a popular god of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) nomads.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup> Redguard creation myth holds that they are doomed to mortality because they are very far away from the real world of Satakal, and the safe haven of the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md) is too far away for them to jump from the mortal world.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup>

## Origin
To the Yokudans, Satak is the Snake who came Before. All the worlds to come rested in the glimmering scales of Satak. There was nothing except Satak; nothing else had the space to exist. The worlds on Satak's skin put out a call to be saved, and they were heard by Akel, the Hungry Stomach of Satak. Akel made itself known, and Satak gave into hunger and began eating. Soon there was enough room to live in the worlds and things began in this violent time. Pretty soon Akel caused Satak to bite its own heart, and Satak died. The hunger, though, refused to stop, even in death, and so the First Serpent shed its skin to begin anew. As the old world died, Satakal began.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONHayazzin-8)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-npc-Safa_al-Satakalaam.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Safa_al-Satakalaam.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Safa_al-Satakalaam.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Safa_al-Satakalaam.jpg) A Redguard priestess of Satakal (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg) [Vinsha](Lore_Vinsha.md), a Maormer priestess of Satakal (ESO) Priests of the deity believe that Satakal eats himself, and the spirits move to the Far Shores.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONPriestess Sermenh-2)</sup> Satakal's coils control the fate of all Mundus, and reigns over creation, destruction, chaos, and order all at once.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sa S-9)</sup> The world is the egg that Satakal laid, and the egg that in time Satakal shall eat.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ks-10)</sup>

Many Redguard groups revere Satakal, albeit to varying extents. Popular with Alik'r nomadic tribes,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-VOF-6)</sup> they believe all dwell upon the serpent, and weave a giant serpent on their maps, presumably as symbolism of Satakal.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AMOA-11)</sup> The Ash'abah use a flower called Satakal's Blossom in antidotes.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-MC-12)</sup> [Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) believed that under Satakal's ever-changing influence, the [sword-singer](Lore_Sword-singers.md) must cast aside the notions of their destiny to achieve Mastery of Sacrifice.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Man Of Steel-13)</sup> The Pyre Watch, sworn to [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), vowed to guard Unhallowed Grave until the Satakal [shed his skin](Lore_Kalpa.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TGH:AH-14)</sup> [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) is a city in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) on the eastern shores of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) which is presumably named after the Worldskin. Some Redguards swear by the By the *"thousand Scales of Satakal"*.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONTt T-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Sword Online Art-16)</sup> Giant snakes are called the Spawn of Satakal and cared for by his followers.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONHayazzin-8)</sup> Potion openers are decorated with crude depictions of Satakal as a serpent with its fangs exposed.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DPO-17)</sup>

There exists a group of worshippers of the Serpent God known as the "[Satakals](Lore_Satakals.md)", and they proved resistant to transitioning to worship of the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-4)</sup> These Devotees of Satakal have been seen rolling in the desert sand sidewinder-fashion in continuous, hundred-mile stretches, from [Balhar](Lore_Balhar.md) all the way to the [Nohotogrha oasis](Lore_Nohotogrha.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-4)</sup> The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) ruler King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) was a deathless wizard rumored to actually *be* the Serpent God of the Satakal.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1W-18)</sup> Some Maormer worship Satakal specifically, and perceive serpents as his holy animals, however, the exact nature of the possible connection between Orgnum and Satakal is unknown,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-DF-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SCV-20)</sup> and not acknowledged by the Redguards.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-UAAYQ-21)</sup> Modern devotees are said to shed their skin in gruesome rituals with winding daggers, with a possibility of self-consumption (though this has never been confirmed). Flensing rituals of Satakal were suggested to be more restrained in earlier eras.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Satakal Skinrazer-3)</sup>

Recognition of Satakal among controlling factions is varied. High King [Ar-Azal](Lore_Ar-Azal.md)'s tutor and mentor was the Admiral Zaqeeb, a Priestess of Satakal and a sage of renown.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWAHD-5)</sup> For defecting from Satakal's service to [The Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md)'s [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md) after the Planemeld, a priestess of Satakal had her acolytes executed for treason, for "Satakal's vengeance must be quick as a serpent's strike".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sa S-9)</sup> At least one [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) Redguard ruler believed worship of Satakal was tantamount to treason against the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-22)</sup>

## Notes
- Satakal was said to be the first Serpent,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONPriestess Sermenh-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Satakal Skinrazer-3)</sup> and the modern-day Redguards often exclaim *"By the First Wyrm."*<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ARExclamations-23)</sup> - [*The Monomyth*](Lore_The_Monomyth.md) gives the appellation "First Serpent" to Satak (and "Second Serpent" to [Sep](Lore_Sep.md)).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup> Satakal likely shares the title as Satak reborn.
- The French translation of the book *[The Monomyth](Lore_The_Monomyth.md)* claims Satakal is Akatosh. This claim is also present in The Morrowind teaser posted in 1999 on the official forums leading up to the release of TES3:Morrowind (Taken from an official Bethsoft post on The Storyboard 09/07/99).<sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-French TM-7)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-YKCF-24)</sup>
