[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/89/TR-place-Old_Mournhold%2C_Palace_Sewers.jpg/200px-TR-place-Old_Mournhold%2C_Palace_Sewers.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Old_Mournhold,_Palace_Sewers.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Old_Mournhold,_Palace_Sewers.jpg) The Palace Sewers of Old Mournhold Old Mournhold is the underground sub-section of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), located in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). It is the ruins of the modern city that were destroyed at the end of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), before he was banished back to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) by [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TREffe-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920V11-2)</sup> The present-day **Mournhold Sewers** are built around the city ruins and have existed thousands of years prior. But because of how expansive they are, whole sections are entirely forgotten by city administrators.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLoad Sewers-3)</sup>

## General Information
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/74/ON-interior-Clockwork_City_Vaults.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Clockwork_City_Vaults.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Clockwork_City_Vaults.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Clockwork_City_Vaults.jpg) Bamz-Amschend [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a0/TR-place-Old_Mournhold%2C_West_Sewers.jpg/200px-TR-place-Old_Mournhold%2C_West_Sewers.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Old_Mournhold,_West_Sewers.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Old_Mournhold,_West_Sewers.jpg) The West Sewers under Godsreach The Mournhold Sewers can be divided into several sections. Some of these areas include the Main Sewers, with several minor sections such as the Manor District, Bazaar, and Palace, and the Temple Sewers under the [Mournhold Temple](Lore_Mournhold_Temple.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TROld-4)</sup> The sewers have been maintained and extended for centuries but sections of it have been forgotten,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONLoad Sewers-3)</sup> like the sewers under Sadri Manor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TROld-4)</sup> Even though it is under the city's watch, poor and lawless people live here.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TREffe-1)</sup> Over the years, protections like [daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) wards were set up for people to hide effectively from [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONNaryu-5)</sup>

Most notably, the city's outlaws refuge is hidden here in the ruins of an old crypt.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONOutlaws-6)</sup> The [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) for a time used the ruined Manor District as their hideout.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TRManor-7)</sup> Native [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) and their [durzog](Lore_Durzog.md) companions live in the sewers and Old Mournhold ruins.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TROld-4)</sup> On the surface, people are mostly unaware of their presence, only through vague rumors of creatures in the sewer.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TRGen Goblins-8)</sup> Rumors from the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) purported that a great evil resided under the city and that it slowly drove the people [mad](Lore_Madness_(disease).md). It is also said that no one sees the bodies of the dead after a battle is over on this soil.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ARMournhold-9)</sup>

Under the city are veins of [Adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md), a rare metal found in hard-to-reach places. The local Indalen clan is known for harvesting this material from the ruins and crafting it for generations.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRBols-10)</sup> The Shrine of the Dead in the Temple Sewers was once a place of great power, serving as a channel to the ancestors. But in time, as [ancestor worship](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md) became a lesser part of typical [Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) tradition, it was abandoned and largely guarded by a group of powerful [liches](Lore_Lich.md) called the Profane.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TRGavas-11)</sup>

Old Mournhold is another tier below the sewers. The prospect of [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) treasure attracts adventurers here.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TREffe-1)</sup> There are several sections of Old Mournhold with varying historical and cultural significance. The Battlefield is a large cavern and the former site of Almalexia and Mehrunes Dagon's legendary battle, while Norenen-dur is a forgotten [Daedric temple](Lore_Daedric_Ruins.md) with shrines to the [Good Daedra](Lore_Good_Daedra.md) and [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md). The Tears of Amun-Shae is a natural cavern with a large waterfall, and the City Gate is another cave, presumingly named after Mournhold's original entrance.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TROld-4)</sup> Rumors claim that a vast, underground [Dwemer ruin](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) lies beneath Mournhold. Though its people vehemently deny this,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE1-12)</sup> the former city of [Bamz-Amschend](Lore_Bamz-Amschend.md) does indeed exist under it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TROld-4)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONVaults-13)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/TR-place-Old_Mournhold%2C_Battlefield.jpg/200px-TR-place-Old_Mournhold%2C_Battlefield.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Old_Mournhold,_Battlefield.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-place-Old_Mournhold,_Battlefield.jpg) The remains of their battlefield On the 29th of [Frostfall](Lore_Frostfall.md), [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), Mehrunes Dagon was summoned by the disgraced noblewoman, [Turala](Lore_Turala.md) to enact revenge against the Duke of Mournhold, [Brindisi Dorom](Lore_Brindisi_Dorom.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-2920V10-14)</sup> On the 3rd of [Sun's Dusk](Lore_Sun%27s_Dusk.md), the skies above Mournhold were cracking with lightning as a red mist and freakish wind covered the city. Throughout the month, Mournhold erupted in gouging fire from where Castle Mournhold once stood, and the city was shrouded in heavy smoke. On the 12th of Sun's Dusk, Almalexia and Sotha Sil appeared in the sky to challenge him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920V11-2)</sup>

By the 18th, he was banished back to Oblivion, and although [some of Mournhold](Lore_Wretched_Spire.md) went with him, the city was saved.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2920V11-2)</sup> About a month later, Mournhold was already rebuilding over the charred black ruins, and as Almalexia surveyed its reconstruction, she decided to make it her new home.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-2920V12-15)</sup> By the mid-Second Era, the new Mournhold was completed<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONMournhold-16)</sup> and Old Mournhold was buried deep underneath it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TREffe-1)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Maulborn were spreading the [Llodos Plague](Lore_Llodos_Plague.md) across Deshaan as a means to uproot the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md). After the failed attempt to poison [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md), the Maulborn began another attempt in Mournhold by hiding in the sewers. Two agents, the [Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) hero known as [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and a [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) assassin named [Naryu Virian](Lore_Naryu_Virian.md) entered the sewers to find the Maulborn Commander Kalara and her warriors. Almalexia intervened and destroyed the Maulborn cell before the plague was released.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONUnderground-17)</sup>

At some point, a powerful mage named [Barilzar](Lore_Barilzar.md) turned into a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) and resided in an abandoned crypt in Old Mournhold. It is rumored that the source of his lichdom was his infamous creation, the [Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md). Because of its power to destroy hearts and minds, Barilzar remained there for generations and guarded it with what remained of his life.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TRTorasa-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TRBarilzar-19)</sup> Similarly, the powerful [sorceress](Lore_Sorcerers.md) and infamous [witch](Lore_Witch.md), [Gedna Relvel](Lore_Gedna_Relvel.md) was laid to rest in a crypt under Mournhold.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TRNerile-20)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) arrived in Mournhold to eradicate the Dark Brotherhood and find who sent them. They discovered the Brotherhood's base in the Old Mournhold's Manor District and killed everyone who attacked them. The Nerevarine traced the contract to King [Hlaalu Helseth](Lore_Hlaalu_Helseth.md) and went to the Royal Palace to confront him.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TRHunt-21)</sup> From then on, they traveled across the city and frequented both the sewers and Old Mournhold for various quests, namely for Mournhold Temple. They investigated a goblin army gathered by Helseth,<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TRGoblin-22)</sup> cleansed the Shrine of the Dead,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TRShrine-23)</sup> defeated the resurrected Gedna Relvel,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRPlague-24)</sup> and acquired [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md) for Almalexia.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TRMazed-25)</sup>
