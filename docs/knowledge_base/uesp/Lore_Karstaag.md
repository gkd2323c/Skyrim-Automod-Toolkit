Karstaag was a [Frost Giant](Lore_Frost_Giant.md) who lived along the northern coast of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He resided in [Castle Karstaag](Lore_Castle_Karstaag.md), a giant fortress of ice. He employed a large number of [rieklings](Lore_Riekling.md) to guard and maintain his castle. The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) held an uneasy truce with him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), as part of the [Bloodmoon Prophecy](Lore_Bloodmoon_Prophecy.md), Karstaag was taken from his castle by the [Hounds](Lore_Werewolf.md) of [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) to serve as Prey in the [Hunter's Game](Lore_Hunter%27s_Game.md). He and three other champions from the island were brought to a maze beneath the [Mortrag Glacier](Lore_Mortrag_Glacier.md), which served as the hunting ground. When the Hunt began, Karstaag successfully navigated the werewolf-filled maze and made it to the central chamber, where he would face off against an aspect of Hircine to earn his freedom. However, Karstaag was then killed by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), another participant in the Hunt.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

Following the Nerevarine's defeat of the Hunter, the glacier collapsed, and Karstaag's remains were buried in the ice. His castle was taken over by the rieklings and fell into ruin, gradually losing its shape. Through unknown means, the majority of Karstaag's skeletal remains were recovered and placed upon his throne.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DB-3)</sup> Spell tomes to learn the frost spell "Karstaag's Breath" were in circulation circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> His skull was discovered in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md), embedded in the wall of a glacial cave. The Dragonborn placed the skull on Karstaag's throne, summoning his angry [spirit](Lore_Ghost.md). Karstaag's ghost was defeated, and temporarily gifted the Dragonborn with the power to summon his spirit to aid in battle.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DB-3)</sup>
