# Mining

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a8/SR-activity-Mining_%28Iron%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Iron%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Iron).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Iron).jpg) A vein of iron ore **Mining** is an activity in Skyrim that involves extracting various [ores](Skyrim_Ore.md) from veins found in [mines](Skyrim_Mines.md). The ore you obtain can be [smelted](Skyrim_Smelter.md) into ingots, and then used to [forge](Skyrim_Forge.md) new weapons or armor. They can also be sold, which is most profitable if sold to one of the mine owners in the [Mine Ore](Skyrim_Mine_Ore.md) radiant quest.

Mining is done by [activating](Skyrim_Controls.md#Activate) an ore vein with a [pickaxe](Skyrim_Pickaxe.md) in your inventory, or [attacking](Skyrim_Controls.md#Attack) an ore vein with an equipped pickaxe. In addition to standard pickaxes, [Rocksplinter](Skyrim_Rocksplinter.md), the [Notched Pickaxe](Skyrim_Notched_Pickaxe.md), and the [Ancient Nordic Pickaxe](Skyrim_Ancient_Nordic_Pickaxe.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> can be used to mine ore. Ore veins are everywhere: all ore types can be mined from veins and then [smelted](Skyrim_Smelting.md) at a forge to obtain ingots. All mines contain at least one type of ore, though some have more veins than others.

Veins typically yield three pieces of ore. Each piece of ore has a 10% chance of being accompanied by a random, level-dependent gem, as detailed on the [Gems](Skyrim_Gems.md) page. After being mined, the vein is depleted, although it will eventually [respawn](Skyrim_Respawning.md) (see [bugs](#Bugs)).

## Mine Locations
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-mapicon-Mine.png) Places identified on the map with a mine icon are all listed on the [Mines](Skyrim_Mines.md) page. However, veins are found in many other locations in Skyrim. Details on the best places to find each type of ore are provided on the individual ore pages:

- [Corundum](Skyrim_Corundum.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Gem Geodes](Skyrim_Gem_Geodes.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
- [Gold](Skyrim_Gold.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Heart Stone Deposit](Skyrim_Heart_Stone_Deposit.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>
- [Iron](Skyrim_Iron.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Malachite](Skyrim_Malachite.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Moonstone](Skyrim_Elven.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Orichalcum](Skyrim_Orcish.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Quicksilver](Skyrim_Quicksilver.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Silver](Skyrim_Silver.md#Mineral_Sources)
- [Soul Gem Geode](Skyrim_Blackreach.md) (yielding [Soul Gems](Skyrim_Soul_Gems.md))
- [Stalhrim](Skyrim_Stalhrim.md#Mineral_Sources)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> (can only be mined with an [Ancient Nordic Pickaxe](Skyrim_Ancient_Nordic_Pickaxe.md))

Note that [Steel](Skyrim_Steel.md) and [Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md) ore do not exist: [Dwarven Metal Ingots](Skyrim_Dwarven_Metal_Ingot.md) are obtained by smelting [Dwemer metal](Skyrim_Ores_and_Ingots.md#Dwemer_Metal_.28Smeltable.29), and [Steel Ingots](Skyrim_Steel_Ingot.md) by smelting one [Corundum Ore](Skyrim_Corundum_Ore.md) and one [Iron Ore](Skyrim_Iron_Ore.md).

Categories also exist for each type of vein, e.g. [Category:Skyrim-Places-Iron Veins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Iron_Veins), listing all of their known locations. They are all found under [Category:Skyrim-Places-Special Features](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Special_Features).

[Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) introduces [Shellbug](Skyrim_Shellbug.md) "ore veins". They can be found on [Shellbugs](Skyrim_Shellbug.md).

[Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) introduces [Clay](Skyrim_Clay.md) and [Quarried stone](Skyrim_Quarried_Stone.md) "ore veins". Stone quarries tend to be found around a plot of land, and clay deposits around rivers and near stone quarries. Unlike regular ore veins, an indefinite amount of material can be mined from them (not the usual three), and you get 4 samples each time the pickaxe is swung three times.

You can use this wiki's [interactive maps](Skyrim_Maps.md) to pinpoint what ore veins you'll find in the wilderness. This includes Clay and Quarried Stone veins with the [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) add-on. The Solstheim maps can also be used to pinpoint gem geodes, Heart Stone veins, and Stalhrim deposits as well. Both versions of the Skyrim and Solstheim maps can be used for this purpose.

## Achievements
An [Achievement](Skyrim_Achievements.md) is related to mining ore:

- [![SR-achievement-Hard Worker.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/SR-achievement-Hard_Worker.png/30px-SR-achievement-Hard_Worker.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-achievement-Hard_Worker.png) **[Hard Worker](Skyrim_Hard_Worker.md)** (10 points/Bronze) — [Chop wood](Skyrim_Chop_Wood.md), mine ore, and [cook food](Skyrim_Cooking.md) - To obtain this achievement, you do not need to sell the ore, or even talk to any of the quest givers. Simply activating an ore vein is sufficient.

## Notes
- Dual-wielding allows you to vastly increase mining speed: - Wielding two pickaxes allows for faster attacking than one.
- A power attack performs three hits (two with the right, one with the left).
- [Dual Flurry](Skyrim_Dual_Flurry.md) further increases dual-wielding speed.
- The [Elemental Fury](Skyrim_Elemental_Fury.md) shout increases speed even more. - Thus, the fastest possible mining is achieved with: Two pickaxes, 2 ranks of Dual Flurry, using all three words of Elemental Fury (the pickaxe in the right hand must be unenchanted), then performing three power attacks. This will deplete the entire ore vein in less than two seconds.
- If you attempt to mine Stalhrim with only a regular pickaxe in your inventory, a "You need an Ancient Nordic Pickaxe to use this." message will appear on the top left of the screen.
- If you activate an ore vein with an Ancient Nordic Pickaxe in your inventory, it will be shown during the mining animation, regardless if a regular pickaxe is also present.
- Stalhrim doesn't need to be smelted, and is used in the state in which it is mined.
- In [Cidhna Mine](Skyrim_Cidhna_Mine.md), mining ore will complete any jail sentence in the Reach, except during [No One Escapes Cidhna Mine](Skyrim_No_One_Escapes_Cidhna_Mine.md), in which you need to escape manually.
- Every vein contains three ore.
- Depleted ore veins may appear to be respawned, but are not actually usable. The veins should eventually respawn properly over time.
- You can use the [Transmute](Skyrim_Transmute_Mineral_Ore.md) spell from the [Alteration](Skyrim_Alteration.md) school to transform mined Iron ore into Silver ore, and transform mined Silver ore into Gold ore. This can be done while mining manually to help speed the process of transmuting. You can also use [Master Transmute](Skyrim_Master_Transmute.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Bittercup.md)</sup> to transform mined Iron ore into Gold ore.
- The [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) add-on adds several [gem geodes](Skyrim_Gem_Geodes.md) to various locations on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md). Mining them always yields a single gem. However, the 10% chance of getting a gem (as with standard ore veins) still applies so you can get a second gem from the same geode.
- With the [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md) add-on, mining ore (especially iron) is an excellent way to prepare for [building your own house](Skyrim_Construction.md).
- Time doesn't stop while mining so magicka, health, and stamina regenerate while mining.
- [Followers](Skyrim_Followers.md) cannot mine ore.

## Bugs
- A graphical glitch can occur when you start to mine any type of ore vein, causing you to clip through it, interrupting your mining. - Possible solutions: sleeping, uninstalling/reinstalling the game from your console, avoiding the mine where the glitch first occurred, or killing all enemies in the surrounding area before mining the ore. It is still possible to manually mine the ore vein (attacking the vein with an equipped pickaxe) if this occurs.
- Manually mining ore in Cidhna Mine can make it impossible to serve your sentence properly. - The [Official Skyrim Patch](Skyrim_Patch.md), version 1.5, fixes this bug.
- You may be unable to mine ore veins after they respawn. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.0, fixes this bug.

- Leaving and reentering the mine or the cell will enable the veins, allowing you to mine them normally.
- When mined, geode veins deliver a message saying the "ore" vein has been depleted even though it should say "geode vein". - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.7, fixes this bug.
- If manually mining while dual-wielding, the game only checks for a pickaxe in your right hand, and counts two swings per attack if you have one. Dual-wielding a pickaxe with a dagger in your left hand greatly accelerates the mining process. Conversely, dual-wielding a pickaxe in your left hand doesn't let you manually mine if you don't have one in your right hand.
- If you activate an ore vein while wielding your only pickaxe, you'll sheath it visibly on your hip, and then start mining with a second "phantom" pickaxe.
- Mining may give two firewood every time it gives ore. This happens at all veins and the cause is unknown.
- A variation of the above bug might give you an Iron Ore with each other ore. If this happens at an Iron Ore vein, you'll get two Iron Ores with each group of three swings. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Attacking an ore vein with a pickaxe may result in the pickaxe not hitting anything, forcing you to mine manually by activating the vein. This tends to happen with veins set flat against the ground or a wall.
- When activating a vein, you may end up swinging at it indefinitely without gaining ore or depleting the vein. - Use the movement controls to break free of the animation, and then try again.
- When mining clay or quarried stone, if you accidentally activate the deposit twice, you may get caught in a loop that keeps pulling you back to mining the deposit, even after you've stopped mining. <sup>**?**</sup> - As soon as you stop mining, quickly fast travel or enter your house and the cycle will break.
- If you activate the mining animation while sneaking, once you exit, you are no longer sneaking but the camera (if you were in First Person mode) is still down at the crouched level. To fix, simply enter and exit sneaking to bring the camera back up to the correct position.
- After enough time has passed, it might look like the ore vein has respawned (it is colored with the ore it should contain), but there is no prompt to mine it when rolling over it with your crosshairs, and attacking it with a pickaxe gains nothing. The reverse can also be true: it might appear that is has not respawned (it appears depleted with no ore color), but your crosshairs show an activation prompt and attacks with a pickaxe gain ore. <sup>**?**</sup> - Leaving and re-entering the mine or area can often resolve this issue.
