[3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Atatar (translated as "Fatherwood" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md))<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ga L-2)</sup> is an [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) found in the rainforests of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). It is located within the Niben Forest region, in the glens situated off the [Yellow Road](Lore_Yellow_Road.md) to [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). It was once home to the Atataric clan of Ayleids.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ASi V-1)</sup> Its subsections of *Haelia Dagon*, *Haelia Anga*, and *Loria* translate to "Terrible Destruction", "Terrible Iron/Cold", and "Darkness" respectively.

## History

### First Era
In its time as a powerful city, its last known monarch, King [Glinferen](Lore_Glinferen.md), was a Daedraphile and fought in the wide-scale conflict against the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) in the late [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) and early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) in what became known as the [Narfinsel Schism](Lore_Narfinsel_Schism.md). This conflict culminated in the Scouring of [Wendelbek](Lore_Wendelbek.md) in [1E 198](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_198), where Glinferen led the combined Daedraphile forces to ultimately drive out the Barsaebics into the inhospitable region of [Argonia](Lore_Argonia.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ASi V-1)</sup>

A half-century later, the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) occurred in [1E 242](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242), and many Ayleids were either killed or displaced by their former [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) slaves. In the hopes of saving his kingdom, King Glinferen sent an envoy to the Barsaebic settlement, [Twyllbek](Lore_Twyllbek.md) for a call to arms. However, the King of Twyllbek gave a rather blunt objection to the call and left Atatar to perish, as the humiliation of exile was still a burning thought to the Barsaebics.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CATAYQ-3)</sup> The Barsaebics, by that time well established in Argonia, refused admittance to the Atatarics, and most of the clan died on an ill-fated expedition to [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ASi V-1)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/ON-place-Atatar.jpg/200px-ON-place-Atatar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Atatar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Atatar.jpg) Atatar ca. [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the subterranean halls of Atatar were sealed. The surface ruins were inhabited at this time by [imps](Lore_Imp.md) and [ogres](Lore_Ogre.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> That year, the Toad-Tongue Tribe of [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) chose a new war chief, a particularly fierce individual named Zathmoz. Under his leadership, the tribe took control of Atatar and set up a war camp to attack caravans and raid nearby homesteads. This aggression effectively brought trade in northern Blackwood to a standstill. The tribe eventually came to the attention of Leyawiin's ruling Chamber of Legates when Zathmoz murdered a scholar who had come to Atatar to observe the goblins, believing her to be a spy. The scholar's apprentice contracted Britta Silanus of Leyawiin to recover her research notes from the site, and Britta subsequently hired a number of adventurers to kill War Chief Zathmoz and his small army of goblins and [durzogs](Lore_Durzog.md). It was believed that his death would pacify the Toad-Tongues, as the tribe did not usually cause much trouble.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

### Third Era
By [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the Ayleid scholar Azani Blackheart and his bandit allies took up residence in Atatar after his previous hideout, the nearby ruins of [Arpenia](Lore_Arpenia.md), was raided by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) in their failed attempt to claim the ancient blade [Sinweaver](Lore_Sinweaver.md) for the wizard Argoth. Argoth turned to the [Blackwood Company](Lore_Blackwood_Company.md) to complete the job, but was double-crossed when the Company seemingly made a deal with Blackheart: if he gave them Sinweaver and departed from Arpenia, the Blackwood Company would pay him off and reveal Argoth's location. Blackheart agreed, and subsequently killed the wizard, retrieving his weapon. He then moved his base to Atatar.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> However, the Fighters Guild second-in-command [Modryn Oreyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Modryn_Oreyn), along with a [new recruit](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), subsequently entered the ruins and confronted Blackheart, where they killed him to avenge their fallen comrades. Oreyn took Azani's ring as proof that the Blackwood Company had not completed their contract, exposing them as frauds.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBAzani-9)</sup>
