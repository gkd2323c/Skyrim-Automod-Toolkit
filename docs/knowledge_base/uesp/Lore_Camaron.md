Camaron (also spelled **Cameron**), the **White Moon**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CES-2)</sup> was a King of the Kingdom of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) in the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-3)</sup> He was the husband of Queen [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md), and father of [Arthago](Lore_Arthago.md), [Aubk-i](Lore_Aubk-i.md), [Greklith](Lore_Greklith.md), and [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFCTree-4)</sup>

During Camaron's rule, a disagreement between Sentinel and the Kingdom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) over the Isle of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md) sparked a [war](Lore_War_of_Betony.md) in [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402). He died at the [Battle of Cryngaine Field](Lore_Battle_of_Cryngaine_Field.md) the following year, killed in a duel with Daggerfall's young King [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md) which ended the war.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FWOB-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NWOB-6)</sup>

## History

### Early Life
Camaron was born in [3E 368](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_368).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFCTree-4)</sup> It is not known when he ascended to the throne or married, but he and [Akorithi](Lore_Akorithi.md) are said to have been King and Queen of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) upon the birth of their first child, Arthago, in [3E 385](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_385). It is said that the king and queen showed little affection toward the prince, embarrassed that the heir to the throne was "sickly". Princess [Aubk-i](Lore_Aubk-i.md), born a year later, was the clear favorite.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DC-7)</sup> The princes [Greklith](Lore_Greklith.md) and [Lhotun](Lore_Lhotun.md) were born in [3E 392](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_392) and [3E 393](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_393).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFCTree-4)</sup> When Arthago vanished in [3E 400](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_400), it was rumored the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md) had carried him off. In truth, the king was disgusted by his son, and the royal couple paid to have him kidnapped and killed.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DC-7)</sup>

### The War of Betony
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d9/DF-prerelease-Army.gif/200px-DF-prerelease-Army.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-prerelease-Army.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-prerelease-Army.gif) The Battle of Cryngaine Field In [3E 402](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_402), the independent Isle of [Betony](Lore_Betony.md) agreed to vassal itself to King [Lysandus](Lore_Lysandus.md) of the nearby [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md). This drew the ire of King Camaron, who cited a two hundred year old agreement that stated Betony was a traditional holding of Sentinel. When diplomacy failed, Camaron's warlords naturally advocated for war. However, [the Oracle](Lore_The_Oracle.md), the wise woman of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), foresaw death and defeat if that were to happen, and warned against further hostilities. Camaron ignored her warnings, and declared war on Daggerfall.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FWOB-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NWOB-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFCN-8)</sup>

The war did not go well for Sentinel; the kingdom won a few skirmishes, but was defeated in the major battles. Daggerfall stood poised to win the war outright with one more victory, but Lysandus suddenly pressed for a peace treaty. The two sides met at [Reich Gradkeep](Lore_Reich_Gradkeep.md); Camaron made some concessions, and the kingdoms agreed to jointly rule Betony. However, Lord Vanech, the Daggerfall scribe who drew up the treaty, substituted a false, offensive document for the kings to sign. When Camaron realized the deception, he flew into a rage and Reich Gradkeep became a bloodbath. The king himself slew Lord Vanech, and the forces of Sentinel retreated to the [Yeorth Burrowland](Lore_Yeorth_Burrowland.md) to regroup, while the armies of Daggerfall pursued them as far as the [Ravennian Forest](Lore_Ravennian_Forest.md). Between these sites lay the flowering meadowlands of [Cryngaine Field](Lore_Cryngaine_Field.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FWOB-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NWOB-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFCN-8)</sup>

One week later, the armies met at the [Battle of Cryngaine Field](Lore_Battle_of_Cryngaine_Field.md). As the battle commenced, a sudden, unnatural fog descended upon the combatants. When it lifted, King Lysandus lay dead, an arrow in his throat. Daggerfall's Prince [Gothryd](Lore_Gothryd.md) was quickly crowned king, and he challenged King Camaron to a duel (though some sources claim it was Lord [Bridwell](Lore_Bridwell.md)). The King of Sentinel was slain, ending the war in Daggerfall's favor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FWOB-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-NWOB-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFCN-8)</sup>
