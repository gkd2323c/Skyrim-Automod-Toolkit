Dengeir of Stuhn was the Jarl of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md) during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He had a brother named Thadgeir and a nephew named [Siddgeir](Lore_Siddgeir.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Thadgeir-2)</sup> Dengeir was a strong supporter of the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Dengeir considered [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) to be selfish and power-hungry; nevertheless, he still supported him over the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) because Ulfric was the devil he knew.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup>

In his youth, Dengeir was a great warrior and virtuous hero of [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Dengeir was considered a good jarl in his day.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Helvard-5)</sup> Near the end of his reign, Dengeir almost issued a proclamation to exile all Imperials from Falkreath, but his steward Nenya was able to persuade him not to issue it.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the local nobles encouraged him to step down as jarl, and he ultimately agreed to abdicate.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574</sup> Dengeir was granted the title of [thane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thanes), and his nephew Siddgeir succeeded him as Jarl of Falkreath.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Nenya-6)</sup> According to Siddgeir, Dengeir stepped down because of his old age and failing health.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Siddgeir-1)</sup> However, Dengeir contested the official narrative that he stepped down as a result of his old age and declining health. He believed that he was pressured to abdicate in order to replace him with a pro-imperial jarl and that the Empire had bribed the nobles to encourage him to step down because of his support of the Stormcloaks.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup>

After stepping down, Dengeir was bitter.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup> He also grew paranoid, believing that there were Imperial spies crawling around Falkreath.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESVSPOGG-4)</sup><sup>:574, 575</sup> Dengeir thought that his former personal guard, Lod, was spying for the Empire and that his maid, Tekla, was passing information to Siddgeir.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Thadgeir-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup>

In 4E 201, Dengeir asked the [Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to break into Lod's house and steal a letter that he believed Lod intended to send to General [Tullius](Lore_Tullius.md). After the Dragonborn stole the letter, which was actually a request for more iron to make weapons, they gave it to Dengeir, who paid them with gold.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SLT-7)</sup> Following that, Dengeir asked the Dragonborn to track down and destroy his ancestor Vighar, a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) who had escaped his grave in Falkreath's graveyard after the wardstone, which had kept him buried for centuries, was stolen.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dengeir-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DA-8)</sup> After the Dragonborn tracked down and slew Vighar in the fort known as Bloodlet Throne, Dengeir rewarded them with gold.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DA-8)</sup>
