# Potions


The following tables list potions and poisons that you can buy, find, steal, or receive as rewards. When you possess at least one, potions will appear in the "Potions" section of your [inventory](Skyrim_Controls.md#Character_Menu) screen. Some [beverages](#Beverages) that function more like potions than [foods](Skyrim_Food.md) are also placed in this section of your inventory. Most of the items on this page are traded by apothecary [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md).

You can also [brew](Skyrim_Alchemy.md) hundreds of custom potions from [ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md) you can obtain. The available custom effects are listed at [Alchemy Effects](Skyrim_Alchemy_Effects.md), and some especially useful custom potions are listed at [Useful Potions](Skyrim_Useful_Potions.md).

### Other Leveled Potions
These potions are typically not attainable by conventional means.

For the Fortify Attribute potions, places where randomly-generated bottles can be found are limited:

- All **of Health** potions: - 1 or more always sold by Thieves Guild fences
- 12.5% chance of being found in Thief Cache Barrels
- All **Extra Magicka** potions: - 1% chance of being carried by all [Warlocks](Skyrim_Warlock.md), smaller chance of being found in Warlock chests
- All **Enhanced Stamina** potions: - No randomly-generated bottles

These poisons do not appear in standard leveled lists, and therefore are typically not sold by merchants or found in generic random loot.

For some of the groups of poisons there are a limited number of places where randomly-generated bottles can be found:

- **Lingering Health Poisons**: - [Rulindil](Skyrim_Rulindil.md) always carries one leveled lingering health poison
- 5% chance of being dropped by [Falmer](Skyrim_Falmer.md); they may also carry 1 additional bottle (4% chance for shamans, 1% for others)
- **Lingering Stamina Poisons**: - [Gissur](Skyrim_Gissur.md) always carries one leveled lingering stamina poison

Most of the other poisons are found in specialized leveled lists, but those leveled lists are not used anywhere, making most of the poisons unavailable in game.

- Frostbite Venom and Lotus Extract lack the Vendor Item Poison keyword, and therefore, cannot be sold to apothecary merchants. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 1.3.2, fixes this bug.
- Cure Poison potions cannot normally be bought or sold at apothecary merchants without unlocking the [Merchant](Skyrim_Merchant_(perk).md) perk. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.9, fixes this bug.
- The White Phial and Philter of the Phantom lack the Vendor Item Potion keyword, and therefore, cannot be sold to apothecary merchants - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch), version 2.0.9, fixes this bug.
- If you give a follower a Potion of Blood, they may consume it in the course of adventuring. This may result in a reduction of your level of bloodlust if you are a vampire, or your acquisition of vampiric powers and effects if you are not. If you are a werewolf when this happens, it may result in your becoming a vampire/werewolf hybrid. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Dawnguard Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Dawnguard_Patch), version 1.1.4, fixes this bug.
- If you consume a Potion of Blood as a werewolf or non-vampire, it can result in you acquiring vampirism as a werewolf without any of the normal warnings that appear on the console after infection with vampirism after three days of drinking it. <sup>**?**</sup> - The solution if this happens is to have Serana bite you and fully convert you into a Vampire Lord then seek a cure from Falion in Morthal and then visit Aela and ask her to reinfect you with lycanthropy (which she will only do once unless you return to a previous save).
- Potions of Ultimate Stamina incorrectly use the model for a Philter of Vigor, unlike the Potion of Extreme Stamina, which uses the model it is supposed to have. <sup>**?**</sup>
- Elixirs of Resist Magic do not appear in leveled lists because, for some reason, they are replaced with Elixirs of Resist Shock. This happens to make all Resist Magic Potions rarer, and Resist Shock Potions in general more common, starting at level 30. <sup>**?**</sup>
