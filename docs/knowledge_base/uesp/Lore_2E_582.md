# 2E 582

[Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) — [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) — [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) — [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) — [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) — [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e3/ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Dark_Anchor.jpg) The [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), one of the worst catacylsms of this year 2E 582 was a year of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). The year was characterized by extraordinary strife, one of the most destructive known years of an era remembered for its "days and nights of blood and venom."<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

## Events

#### The Five Companions are reformed
The Five Companions are reformed to stop Molag Bal's Planemeld. Their members include [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md), [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md), [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md), and the prophesied Vestige.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup>

#### The [Mortuum Vivicus](Lore_Mortuum_Vivicus.md) is destroyed
The [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) successfully destroys the Mortuum Vivicus with the help of their newest Champion.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup>

#### The Mages Guild sanctuary of [Eyevea](Lore_Eyevea.md) returns to Tamriel
After nearly a thousand years of being in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), the sanctuary of Eyevea is returned to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by a Mages Guild Adept with the help of [Arch-Mage Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup>

#### The [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) ends
The three alliance leaders meet on the island of [Stirk](Lore_Stirk.md) in neutral territory to discuss an invasion of Coldharbour. After they fail to come to an agreement, they are attacked by the forces of Molag Bal, after which they give their blessings to the Mages and Fighters Guilds spearheading an assault into the Daedric realm. The combined forces of the Guilds charge into Coldharbour led by the Vestige. There, they destroy the Great Shackle, and seal the Planar Vortex, thus ending the Planemeld.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup>

#### Daedric plots in the Imperial City foiled
In a final attempt to restart the Planemeld,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Molag Bal focused on the occupation of the Imperial City. A Dragonguard agent called the [Drake of Blades](Lore_Drake_of_Blades.md), with the help of the Vestige, stopped Molag Bal's plot to destroy the [Sublime Brazier](Lore_Sublime_Brazier.md), the key to rekindling the Dragonfires.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-IC-4)</sup> The attempt at a second Planemeld, the "Planemeld Obverse", is also stopped with the slaying of [Molag Kena](Lore_Molag_Kena.md).

#### Molag Bal is banished
[The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) banishes Molag Bal within his own realm, thus finally stopping his bid for merging [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) into one nightmarish hellscape. With it, the souls stolen by him are released from his clutches.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup>

#### [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) considers withdrawing from the Daggerfall Covenant
King [Kurog](Lore_Kurog.md) of Orsinium is slain in late autumn after a failed attempt to unite the Orc clans of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) under his rule by assassinating clan chiefs who refused to follow [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md). Chief [Bazrag](Lore_Bazrag_gro-Fharun.md) of [Clan Fharun](Lore_Fharun_Clan.md) is crowned the new king, restoring worship of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). Since Orsinium's membership of the Daggerfall Covenant was based on an agreement between Kurog and High King Emeric, the chiefs of Orsinium consider leaving the Covenant.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Orsinium-5)</sup>

#### The [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) of [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) is restored to its former glory
After a failed heist and the resulting persecution from [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md), the Thieves Guild of Abah's Landing is restored to its prime and begins to spread its influence throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TG-6)</sup>

#### Count [Carolus Aquilarios](Lore_Carolus_Aquilarios.md) of [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) emerges as the dominant power on the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md)
Shielded from the ongoing Alliance War by [Varen's Wall](Lore_Varen%27s_Wall.md), a *de facto* independent Gold Coast sees the end of a power struggle between [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), Kvatch, and the [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md). Aquilarios has Governor [Fortunata ap Dugal](Lore_Fortunata_ap_Dugal.md) of Anvil assassinated by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md); facing persecution from the Order of the Hour, the Brotherhood subsequently kills Primate [Artorius Ponticus](Lore_Artorius_Ponticus.md), stopping him from raising an army against Aquilarios.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DB-7)</sup>

#### [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) is saved from the machinations of [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) and [Barbas](Lore_Barbas.md)
Seeking to gain entry to [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), the Daedric Prince and his minion steal the staff Sunna'rah, a tool Sotha Sil had created for experimenting on his power. The staff ends up in the hands of the [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlander.md) [Conoon Chodala](Lore_Conoon_Chodala.md), who aspired to the title of [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md). Chodala's use of the staff drains away the power of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md), which weakens the Warrior-Poet's hold over the moonlet [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) and endangers all of Vvardenfell. Fortunately, the [Champion of Azura](Lore_Seryn.md), a [clockwork mage](Lore_Barilzar.md), and an [Outlander](Lore_The_Vestige.md) come to Vivec's aid. Chodala is discredited and defeated, Clavicus Vile's plot is thwarted, and Vivec's power is restored.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOMW-8)</sup>

#### A plot by [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) to take over the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) is foiled
[Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s Shadow, serving Nocturnal, is summoned and replaces him as ruler of the Clockwork City. At the same time, the [Blackfeather Court](Lore_Blackfeather_Court.md) enters the city and steals the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md), which has been in the Clockwork God's possession. [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), assisted by [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md) and the Clockwork Apostles, unveils the plot; but is unable to retake the Skeleton Key before it is taken by Sotha Sil's Shadow. Using the key, Nocturnal and the Shadow attempt to take over the Clockwork City; but are stopped by the Vestige, Divayth Fyr and a Clockwork Apostle, who save Sotha Sil by defeating his Shadow. Sotha Sil gives the Skeleton Key to Divayth Fyr for safekeeping.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESOCWC-9)</sup>

#### The [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) is saved from a triad of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md)
The three Daedric Princes [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) and [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) attempt to execute their plan to take control of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md) using the Heart of Transparent Law, which they had made [Ritemaster Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) steal around [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230) —who had also hidden it from them. After they trick Iachesis to reveal the Heart's location, that being within himself, they kill him; but Nocturnal betrays Clavicus Vile and Mephala by taking the Heart for herself. With it she takes control of the Crystal Tower, hoping to use its properties to achieve omnipresence in the entire [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). However, her plans are foiled by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the Psijic Order, assisted by the two betrayed Princes and a champion of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md), and the Heart is returned to the Crystal Tower.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOSummerset-10)</sup>

#### The [Remnant of Argon](Lore_Remnant_of_Argon.md) is recovered and the Root-Whisper tribe is restored
[The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) aids Cyrodilic Collections in recovering a powerful artifact that contains the spirits of a lost Argonian tribe in [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOMurkmire-11)</sup>

#### [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) unleashes various Daedric artifacts onto Cyrodiil
In order to relieve his own boredom at the Three Banners War, the Daedric Prince Sheogorath temporarily claims several artifacts belonging to the other Daedric Princes. He then offers them to the combatants in Cyrodiil to bring upheaval to the ongoing conflict.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOChaos Magic-12)</sup>

#### A [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) threat is averted in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)
The Imperial battlemage [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) accidentally unseals the legendary [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md) using the [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md), believing it to contain a powerful weapon. This results in a rage of Dragons—sealed away by the Khajiiti hero [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md) in the Merethic Era—being unleashed upon Elsweyr.

- The Dragons form an alliance with [Euraxia Tharn](Lore_Euraxia_Tharn.md), the ruler of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md). The alliance is short-lived, as [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) slays Euraxia, bringing Rimmen back under the control of the Khajiit.
- The Dragons plot to obtain lunar power of [Jone](Lore_Jone.md) and [Jode](Lore_Jode.md) using the [Moon Gate](Lore_Moon_Gate.md) of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md). They are stopped after the Vestige steps into the Plane of Jode, banishing the Dragon leader, [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md).
- [Khamira](Lore_Khamira.md), the daughter of King Hemakar, is crowned queen of Rimmen.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOEW-13)</sup>

#### The [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) is reformed in [Southern Elsweyr](Lore_Pelletine.md)
The ex-members of the [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md), [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) and [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), assemble a new Dragonguard—largely consisting of ex-pirates and [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) soldiers—to defend [Pelletine](Lore_Pelletine.md) from the Dragon threat. With the help of the Dragon [Nahfahlaar](Lore_Nahfahlaar.md), they manage to acquire the powerful artifact [Mask of Alkosh](Lore_Mask_of_Alkosh.md) and defeat the Dragon [Laatvulon](Lore_Laatvulon.md).

- [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md) returns to Elsweyr after having been banished, seeking to use the [Aeonstone](Lore_Aeonstone.md) found on the island [Dragonhold](Lore_Dragonhold.md) to gain the power of a god, becoming the prophesized [Dark Aeon](Lore_Dark_Aeon.md). The new-founded Dragonguard are aided by Nahfahlaar, [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) and Queen [Khamira](Lore_Khamira.md) in destroying the Aeonstone and defeating Kaalgrontiid. However, Kaalgrontiid is able to absorb a great amount of power before being killed, which risks destroying all of Elsweyr if not contained. Nahfahlaar and Abnur Tharn use their combined power to contain it, resulting in the disappearance and possible death of the latter.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESODH-14)</sup>

#### Death of High King Svargrim
[House Ravenwatch](Lore_House_Ravenwatch.md) agent Fennorian, representative of King Jorunn Lyris Titanborn, Princess of Western Skyrim Svana and the Vestige join forces to unveil the vampiric threat looming over Skyrim.

- [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md) personally approaches High King [Svargrim](Lore_Svargrim.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), offering him incredible power in exchange for his servitude. The High King agrees and becomes a Vampire Lord. The [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md), together with the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md), create [harrowstorms](Lore_Harrowstorm.md), magical weather anomalies to pull their comrades out of Coldharbour, and then using the Stone Husks, to fully restore their corporeal forms allowing them to live once more, killing hundreds of inhabitants of Skyrim in the process.
- The Gray Host takes control over Castle Dour and unleashes a powerful harrowstorm inside the city. After a great battle, High King Svargrim is slain and the Gray Host defeated and retreating from Western Skyrim.
- Relations between Western and Eastern Skyrim improve as King Jorunn offers aid to the victims of the vampire attack. Princess Svana becomes Jarl of Solitude.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOGM-15)</sup>

#### The Gray Host is destroyed in The Reach
[Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) consolidates more power over the Reachfolk clans. A faction of [rebels](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md) led by [Arana](Lore_Arana.md) is formed in opposition to the power of the Ard.

- The Gray Host unleashes an attack on the citizens of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) as a sacrifice for the [Dark Heart](Lore_Dark_Heart.md).
- [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md) assisted by the Vestige, defeats Rada al-Saran, releases the souls harnessed by the Gray Host and resurrects the Reachfolk who died during the Gray Host assault on Markarth. The rebels and loyalists of the Ard join forces.
- Peace treaty between the Reach, Western Skyrim and Eastern Skyrim is signed.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOMAR-16)</sup>

#### Search for the Four Ambitions
Sombren, Calia, and Destron, three out of the [Four Ambitions](Lore_Four_Ambitions.md), have been released.

- Assassins have been targeting members of the Elder Council in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). Councilors Velan Sophus, Jirich, and Abor have been successfully assassinated.
- It is revealed that Councilor [Ertus Vandacia](Lore_Ertus_Vandacia.md) was the High Priest of Mehrunes Dagon. His forces surround Gideon, and even Dagon himself makes an appearance, but they are ultimately defeated at Fort Redmane.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ESOBW-17)</sup>

#### Mehrunes Dagon banished to Oblivion
The Fourth Ambition, who is actually [the Anchorite](Lore_Mairead.md) of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), is revealed as the illegitimate daughter of Emperor Moricar and Sister [Celdina](Lore_Celdina.md).

- Mehrunes Dagon materializes within [Fort Sundercliff](Lore_Sundercliff.md), but his egonymic is employed to banish him back.
- [Lyranth](Lore_Lyranth.md) absconds with the powers of the Four Ambitions, vanishing to employ them for undisclosed purposes.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESODeadlands-18)</sup>

#### Machinations of the Ascendant Order unveils on High Isle
Lord [Bacaro Volorus](Lore_Bacaro_Volorus.md), heading the [Society of the Steadfast](Lore_Society_of_the_Steadfast.md), takes charge of organizing peace negotiations on the High Isle, aiming to halt the ongoing Three Banners War.

- The Ascendant Order, a clandestine pan-Tamrielic society opposed to monarchies, grows in power and gains prominence.
- Prince Irnskar, Queen Ayrenn, and High King Emeric face assassination attempts.
- A fierce battle unfolds on All-Flags islet between the forces of the Ascendant Order and opposing factions, resulting in the demise of the Ascendant Magus.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOHI-19)</sup>

#### Prophecy of [Kasorayn](Lore_Kasorayn.md) comes to fruition
- Frii is awakened
- Archdruid Orlaith of the [Firesong Circle](Lore_Firesong_Circle.md) collaborates with the Dreadsails and lays siege to [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md), aiming to proclaim herself as the new Druid King. However, her attempt proves unsuccessful. Count Leonard Mornard tragically passes away during the siege, and his son Stefan Mornard assumes leadership over Galen.
- Lord Bacarro Volorus, is unveiled as the leader of the Ascendant Order and the descendant of [House Merchad](Lore_House_Merchad.md), the lineage of the Druid King Kasorayn. He steals the regalia of the Druid King and endeavors to declare himself as the new Druid King. This reckless act triggers the eruption of the [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md) volcano. In a heroic act, Frii sacrifices themselves to protect others.
- Peace talks resume following these tumultuous events, but the ultimate outcome remains uncertain and unknown.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ESOFS-20)</sup>
