This article is about the sorceror-king. For the god, see [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md). For the emperor, see [Magnus Septim](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md). For the astronomical object, see [Sun](Lore_Sun.md).

The Sorceror-King Magnus<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARtifact-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> (also known as **Arch-Mage Magnus**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DF-2)</sup> was the possessor of the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), who once lived on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARtifact-1)</sup> Just before he died, Magnus entrusted the [Imperial Order of Blades](Lore_Blades.md) with a map to where the Staff of Magnus could be found, which had been placed within a dungeon somewhere in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ARtifact-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^1](#note_nb1) | At the time of *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* and *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, Magnus was not yet a god. According to series designer [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride), for the writing of *[The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*, he elevated the namesakes behind Tamriel's artifacts into gods as a way of fleshing out Tamriel's pantheons, as to him, heroes and gods are virtually one and the same.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Memo Pod-3)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- | [^2](#note_nb2) | The exact same spelling and use of the title "sorceror-king" was also used for "sorceror-king [Umaril](Lore_Umaril.md)" in the *[Knights of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Knights_of_the_Nine)* expansion for *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Oblivion-4)</sup> Throughout the text of *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, the spelling of the word Sorcerer used the standardization "Sorceror." |
| --- | --- |
- | [^3](#note_nb3) | The Blades as a faction were not founded until the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), around the time of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md); meaning Sorceror-King Magnus must have been in power sometime around or after this time period.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-R-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FC-6)</sup> |
| --- | --- |

- A line in [Varieties of Faith in the Empire](Lore_Varieties_of_Faith....md) mentions this about the god Magnus: "[Cyrodilic](Lore_Imperial.md) legends say [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md) can inhabit the bodies of powerful magicians and lend them his power. Associated with [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md), the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md)." It is notable also that Zurin Arctus had a personal relationship to the Blades in the story of *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-DFC-7)</sup><sup>:201</sup>
