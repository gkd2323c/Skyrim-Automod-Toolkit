# Advanced Armors


Advanced Armors is a [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) perk that allows you to combine [corundum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ingot) and [steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Ingot) to craft two medium-quality [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md) types: **scaled** [light armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) and **steel plate** [heavy armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md) (and with the [Dragonborn add-on](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) installed, an additional **Nordic carved** heavy armor and weapons, as well as several [Creations](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adding more armor and weapons). It is the second perk in the light armor branch of Smithing's perk tree. Unlocking it requires a Smithing skill of 50, and the [Elven Smithing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Smithing) perk must have already been unlocked.

The Advanced Armors perk also causes tempering of scaled and steel plate armor to be twice as effective. This perk applies to both unenchanted armor and enchanted armor ([generic armor](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Apparel.md) or [custom](Skyrim_Enchanting.md)), as long as the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk has been unlocked. Tempering of the [Helm of Yngol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Helm_of_Yngol) is also improved by Advanced Armors.

## Light Armor

### Scaled Armor
Scaled armor is a medium-quality light armor that is better than standard [elven](Skyrim_Elven.md), although its cuirass is not as strong as [elven gilded armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Gilded_Armor), making the two sets equal as an overall set. It appears in leveled lists starting at level 19 (enchanted varieties at level 20). All items can be tempered using 1 [corundum ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ingot), and having the Advanced Armors perk doubles the quality improvement.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's scaled bracers, a new bracer variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 10 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.

### Remnant Armor<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Redguard_Elite_Armaments.md)</sup>
Remnant Armor is a [light armor](Skyrim_Light_Armor.md) set added by the [Redguard Elite Armaments](Skyrim_Redguard_Elite_Armaments.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md). A set can be obtained during [Interception](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Interception)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Redguard_Elite_Armaments.md)</sup>, at which point it can also be crafted. It is considered to be scaled armor, except for the [shield](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Remnant_Shield), which is considered to be steel. Shields don't affect the [Matching Set perks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Matching_Set) however. Each piece can be tempered with 1 [corundum ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ingot). You can't enhance this armor, even with 100 smithing. The game will think you need the "arcane armor" perk, even if you already have it active, resulting in the inability to upgrade this armor.

Its armor rating total matches regular scaled armor. However it is lighter and therefore more valuable, with its weight sitting somewhere between the very light [elven armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Elven_Armor) and the regular scaled armor.

## Heavy Armor

### Steel Plate Armor
Steel plate armor is a medium-quality heavy armor that is better than [Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md) but not as good as [Orcish](Skyrim_Orcish.md). It appears in leveled lists starting at level 18 (enchanted varieties at level 19), though the cuirass can be found at any level on the [Black Briar Lodge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Black_Briar_Lodge) guards. All items can be tempered using 1 [corundum ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Corundum_Ingot), and having the Advanced Armors perk doubles the quality improvement.

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's steel plate gauntlets, a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 14 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors and found in random loot.
