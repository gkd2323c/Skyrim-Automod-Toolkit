[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/ON-place-Grahtwood.jpg/200px-ON-place-Grahtwood.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grahtwood.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Grahtwood.jpg) Grahtwood circa 2E 582 Grahtwood, previously known as the **Kingdom of Grahtwood**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONload Golden-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOS-2)</sup> is a large region which forms southeastern [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Much of it is covered in impenetrable jungle, though a western portion of the region was devastated by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) circa [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920) and has never recovered. The [Tarlain Heights](Lore_Tarlain_Heights.md) lie west of [Green Hall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Hall), and the coastal region south of both areas is known as Longcoast. Many cities and settlements can be found in Grahtwood, including [Haven](Lore_Haven.md), [Cormount](Lore_Cormount.md), [Goldfolly](Lore_Goldfolly.md), [Southpoint](Lore_Southpoint.md), and [Elden Root](Lore_Elden_Root.md). Historically, the walking city [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) settled in Grahtwood during the winter.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup>

Many races besides the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) have made Grahtwood their home. [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) strongholds are not unheard of, and numerous [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins dot the landscape.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> The Bosmer have allowed both [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) and [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to establish autonomous settlements in their land.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARD-4)</sup> In fact, men have been coming from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to seek great wealth from Grahtwood's resources since the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TFOS-2)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-FP-5)</sup> Non-native Bosmer who return to their ancestral homeland are frequently drawn to Grahtwood. As a result, relations between them and native Bosmer are particularly tense in the region, and this has often led to conflict. In [2E 489](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_489), the Blacksap Movement was founded with the aim of formalizing the Bosmeri approach to the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), and thereby prevent any further violent "misunderstandings" between the two groups.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TROC-6)</sup>
