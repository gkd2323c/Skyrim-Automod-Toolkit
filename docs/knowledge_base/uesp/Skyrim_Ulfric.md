[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak.jpg) Ulfric Stormcloak **Ulfric Stormcloak** of the [Stormcloak Clan](Lore_Stormcloak_Clan.md) is the rebellious [Jarl](Skyrim_Jarl.md) of [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md) who started the uprising against [Imperial](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md) rule known as the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Skyrim_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md). An incredibly divisive and controversial figure, Ulfric leads the fight against the Empire in a bid for free Talos worship and political independence in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). For historical information on Ulfric, see the [lore article](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md).

On a normal day, Ulfric will wake up at 8am and will immediately proceed to head downstairs to take his place on the throne. He will then remain in court for the next 14 hours, dealing with any business involving the war or his hold. After a long day in court, Ulfric will leave his throne at 10pm to head upstairs where he will sleep for the next 10 hours. There are many exceptions to this schedule due to quest involvement which are fully documented in the [below section](#Quest-Related_Events).

When you first meet him on your way to the executioner's block in [Helgen](Skyrim_Helgen.md), Ulfric is seen to be wearing unique [jarl attire](Skyrim_Ulfric%27s_Clothes.md) and [boots](Skyrim_Ulfric%27s_Boots.md), along with [handcuffs](Skyrim_Prisoner%27s_Cuffs.md) and a [gag](Skyrim_Ulfric%27s_Gag.md). However, once he returns to the [Palace of the Kings](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md) he is without the gag, wears [bracers](Skyrim_Ulfric%27s_Bracers.md) instead of handcuffs, and carries a [Steel War Axe of Cowardice](Skyrim_Steel_War_Axe_of_Cowardice.md). Thanks to his training from the [Greybeards](Skyrim_Greybeards.md), Ulfric can use the [Unrelenting Force](Skyrim_Unrelenting_Force.md) and [Disarm](Skyrim_Disarm.md) [Dragon Shouts](Skyrim_Dragon_Shouts.md).

According to the [Imperial General Tullius](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md), Ulfric assassinated the [High King of Skyrim](Skyrim_High_King_Torygg.md) with the Thu'um in an effort to ignite an uprising against the Empire's rule over Skyrim. However, according to Ulfric, he challenged him to one-on-one combat in the old Nord way, only using the Thu'um to throw him to the ground, then killing him with his sword. Ulfric gained the ability to use The Voice by training with the Greybeards.

Many non-Nord citizens and also some Nords, such as [Brunwulf Free-Winter](Skyrim_Brunwulf_Free-Winter.md), consider him to be prejudiced against non-humans. In his city, Windhelm, the [Dunmer](Skyrim_Dark_Elf.md) live in an area called the Gray Quarter, the segregated slums of Windhelm. Likewise, [Argonians](Skyrim_Argonian.md) have been banished to the outskirts of Windhelm. Brunwulf claims that Ulfric refuses to send aid to the [Khajiit](Skyrim_Khajiit.md) caravans and non-Nord travelers that have been raided by bandits, even though he is "the first to sound the horn" when Nord villages are being threatened. Neither Ulfric nor his loyalists display intolerance towards you regardless of your racial background, however, and instead focus more on a true dedication to their cause.

Many Nords consider Ulfric Stormcloak to be a revolutionary commander who is mounting a legitimate uprising against [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s imperialism and the Imperial Legion in Skyrim. Indeed, a common sentiment expressed by many of Ulfric's followers is that the Emperor forfeited the legitimacy of his rule by surrendering to the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) and agreeing to outlaw the worship of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md), and that the Empire of today is essentially a puppet state of the Thalmor which no longer deserves to be formally recognized by any of its provinces.

## General Dialogue
Throughout the game, Ulfric can provide you with plenty of information about Skyrim and current events. After escaping from Helgen, you can ask him about a variety of topics related to the Civil War at the Palace of the Kings.

- **At Helgen, they said you shouted the king to death?**
- *"Not entirely true, though not entirely false either. Any Nord can learn the Way of the Voice by studying with the Greybeards, given enough ambition and dedication. My shouting Torygg to the ground proved he had neither. However, it was my sword piercing his heart that killed him."*
- **Why are you fighting this war?**
- *"We're fighting because we're done bleeding for an Empire that won't bleed for us. Untold numbers of Nords died defending the Empire against the Dominion. And for what? Skyrim being sold to the Thalmor so the Emperor could keep his throne! We're fighting because our own Jarls, once strong, wise men, have become fearful and blind to their people's suffering. We're fighting because Skyrim needs heroes, and there's no one else but us."*
- **Why did you kill the high king?**
- *"I killed Torygg to prove our wretched condition. How is the High King supposed to be the defender of Skyrim, if he can't even defend himself?"*
- **Some call you a murderer.**
- *"I challenged him in the traditional way, and he accepted. There were many witnesses. No 'murder' was committed. True, he didn't stand a chance against me. But that was precisely the point! He was a puppet-king of the Empire* [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] * , not a High King of Skyrim. [His father](Lore_Istlod.md) before him perhaps, but not Torygg. He was too privileged and too foolish, more interested in entertaining his queen than ruling his country."*
- **Do you desire to be the High King?**
- *"There hasn't been a true High King in Skyrim for generations. For too long he's been hand-picked by the Emperor, and given emphatic nods by milk-drinking Jarls addicted to Imperial coin. It's time we had a real king. One of our own making."*
- **And what of his widow? Doesn't she claim the throne?**
- *"Indeed, [Elisif](Skyrim_Elisif.md) has become Jarl of Solitude, historically and conveniently home of the High King, backed by Imperial interests. But the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md) has not yet met to name her High Queen. And they won't. Not as long as I have any say in it."*
- **How did you become the Jarl of Eastmarch?**

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/51/SR-interior-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Palace_of_the_Kings.jpg) *"The Throne of Ysgramor! The throne of my father... I only hope I can prove worthy of the honor."*
- - *"My father, the great Bear of Eastmarch, died during my imprisonment after the [Markarth Incident](Lore_Markarth_Incident.md). I, his only son, forced to deliver his eulogy via a letter I had smuggled out of prison. Such is the love of [Titus Mede](Skyrim_Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_(real).md) for his subjects. When finally set free, I returned to Windhelm, and was greeted by a city in mourning, at one with my own grief and anger. Clamoring in angry voices, calling out for justice, for war, they sat me on the throne. The Throne of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)! The throne of my father... I only hope I can prove worthy of the honor."*

### The Greybeards
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg/200px-SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-High_Hrothgar.jpg) *"I often think about High Hrothgar. It's very... disconnected from the troubles down here."* Once you have been summoned by the Greybeards and have completed [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md), you can talk to Ulfric to learn more about them:

- **Who are the Greybeards?**
- *"They live in seclusion near the top of the [Throat of the World](Skyrim_Throat_of_the_World.md), the great mountain of Skyrim. They're masters of the Way of the Voice - of Shouting. It's a great honor for them to summon you. They speak to very few - in fact, they hardly speak at all."*
- **How do you know so much about them?**
- *"I studied with them when I was young. They taught me how to Shout."*
- **You trained with the Greybeards?**
- *"Yes. They chose me when I was just a lad. It was a great honor, of course. I was to become a Greybeard myself. I spent almost ten years at [High Hrothgar](Skyrim_High_Hrothgar.md), learning the Way of the Voice. Then the Great War came... I couldn't stand missing it. I often think about High Hrothgar. It's very... disconnected from the troubles down here. But that's why I couldn't stay, and why I couldn't go back. I suppose the Greybeards care about Skyrim's troubles, in their way, but I needed to do something about it. I'm sure [Arngeir](Skyrim_Arngeir.md) would call it one of my failings."*
- **Arngeir - is he one of the Greybeards?**
- *"Yes. The oldest and most powerful, although he may not seem so. I doubt he's forgiven me for leaving. And for... well, for what he'd consider blasphemy. Using Shouts for anything but worship of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md)."*
- **So you know how to shout?**
- *"Yes. Although I rarely use my training. The Greybeards believe the Voice should be used only for worship of Kynareth. I have... fallen from their strict teaching, but I still don't feel it should be used lightly. Not all of Arngeir's lecturing was wasted, it seems."*
- **Why would they teach me to Shout if they don't want me to use it?**
- *"You're Dragonborn. The rules don't apply to you. You can Shout the way dragons do... without training, through inborn instinct. They always hope to teach the Dragonborn to respect the Way of the Voice as they do. They never fully succeed. You'll have to make your own decision. It's a beautiful philosophy, but outside the seclusion of High Hrothgar, I was never able to hold to it."*

### The Jagged Crown
After you have returned from Serpentstone Isle, if you approach Ulfric, he will exclaim:

- *"You're alive! I'm impressed! Galmar had his doubts about you, but I knew you'd pull through. You should speak with him. He's going to need your help."*

Approach Galmar, and once you have taken your oath to become a Stormcloak, you will find Galmar speaking to Ulfric about acquiring the [Jagged Crown](Skyrim_Jagged_Crown.md) as a benefit towards the cause:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f2/SR-npc-Galmar_Stone-Fist_04.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Galmar_Stone-Fist_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Galmar_Stone-Fist_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Galmar_Stone-Fist_04.jpg) *"We don't even know if it exists!"* **Ulfric**: *"Tell me again why we're wasting time and dwindling resources chasing a legend. We don't even know it exists!"*
**Galmar**: *"The Jarls are upset. They don't all support you."*
**Ulfric**: *"Damn the Jarls."*
**Galmar**: *"They demand the [Moot](Lore_Moot.md)."*
**Ulfric**: *"And damn the Moot! We should risk letting those milkdrinkers put Thorryg * [[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)] *'s woman on the throne? She'll hand Skyrim over to the elves on a silver plate."*
**Galmar**: *"All the more reason then. The crown would legitimize your claim."*
**Ulfric**: *"A crown doesn't make a king."*
**Galmar**: *"No, but this one..."*
**Ulfric**: *"If it even exists."*
**Galmar**: *"It exists. And it'll be the symbol of the righteousness of our cause. Think about it. The Jagged Crown! It heralds back to a time before jarls and moots. ### Message to Whiterun (Imperial)
Should you side with the [Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md) during the Civil War, you will be given the assignment to get Balgruuf the Greater to side with the Empire. After being told that Ulfric plans on attacking Whiterun, Balgruuf will want you to deliver a message to Windhelm to see if this is true. Specifically, he will have you deliver his axe to Ulfric.

Once you have completed your trip to Windhelm and have arrived at the Palace of the Kings, you can approach Ulfric and tell him you have a message from the jarl of Whiterun. This will pique his interest and he will respond: *"Is that so? I've been wondering when he'd come around."* You will then have the option of handing Ulfric the axe, which will cause Ulfric to begin to understand what Balgruuf's message is: *"Oh. What's this? Ahhh... You're quite brave to carry such a message. It's a pity you've chosen the wrong side..."* He will then continue, telling you to return the axe to the jarl: *"You can return this axe to the man who sent it. And tell him he should prepare to entertain... visitors. I expect a great deal of excitement in the city of Whiterun in the near future..."* You will then have three options you can choose to respond with:

- **You've killed the High King. Why make war on your own people?**
- *"I have shown the people that when our Jarls drink the Empire's milk, it makes us weak. I must now show them the path that will lead us back to our strength. There is no progress without sacrifice. No wheat without threshing the chaff. The Empire and the Jarls who back them must be swept away. The people demand it. I demand it."*
- **You will be stopped.**
- *"Oh? True, I may meet my end in a dark alley with a dagger in my back. But the people have seen the truth, and they will not back down."*
- **We'll be seeing you soon.**
- *"Sooner than you think."*

After you have finished your conversation with Ulfric, you will have to return to Whiterun and deliver the message to Balgruuf. This will then lead into the [Battle for Whiterun](Skyrim_Battle_for_Whiterun_(Imperial).md).

### Battle for Windhelm
If you sided with the Imperial Legion, you will participate in the [Battle for Windhelm](Skyrim_Battle_for_Windhelm.md) alongside [General Tullius](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md) and [Legate Rikke](Skyrim_Legate_Rikke.md). After fighting your way through the city of [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), you will make your way into the [Palace of the Kings](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md) where you will find Ulfric Stormcloak and Galmar awaiting your arrival:

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/SR-quest-Battle_for_Windhelm_03.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Battle_for_Windhelm_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Battle_for_Windhelm_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Battle_for_Windhelm_03.jpg) *"I'll never surrender Skyrim into the hands of a corrupt and dying Empire."* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1a/SR-npc-Legate_Rikke_5.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Legate_Rikke_5.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Legate_Rikke_5.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Legate_Rikke_5.jpg) *"Down with Ulfric the killer of kings. On the day of your death we'll drink and we'll sing."* **Tullius**: *"Secure the door."*
**Rikke**: *"Already done, sir."*
**Tullius**: *"Ulfric Stormcloak! You are guilty of insurrection, murder of Imperial citizens, the assassination of King Torygg, and high treason against the Empire. It's over."*
**Galmar**: *"Not while I'm still breathing, it's not."*
**Rikke**: *"Step aside Galmar. We're here to accept Ulfric's surrender."*
**Ulfric**: *"I'll never surrender Skyrim into the hands of a corrupt and dying Empire."*
**Rikke**: *"Skyrim doesn't belong to you, Ulfric."*
**Ulfric**: *"No... But I belong to her."*
**Tullius**: *"Enough! You are traitors and will die traitors' deaths. Stand down and face public execution, or advance and face summary execution by my hands. It matters little to me. Either way I'll be sending your heads back to Cyrodiil."*
**Galmar**: *"Well? What are we waiting for?"*

The three of them will then draw their weapons and engage in combat, with Rikke and Tullius going after Ulfric while Galmar goes after you. When Galmar is dead and Ulfric is kneeling on the floor, Tullius will sheathe his sword:

**Tullius**: *"Well Ulfric, you can't escape from me this time. Any last requests before I send you to... to wherever you people go when you die."*

Whether Ulfric was executed by you or by General Tullius, Ulfric will be on his way to [Sovngarde](Skyrim_Sovngarde_(place).md). His death also signifies the final defeat of the Stormcloak rebellion, and with Ulfric dead, Brunwulf Free-Winter will take over as Jarl of Windhelm.

### Sovngarde
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak_06.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Ulfric_Stormcloak_06.jpg) *"Skyrim was betrayed, the blood of her sons spilled in doomed struggle against fate."* If you sided with the Imperials in the Civil War, Ulfric will die at the end of the questline. If he is dead by the time you reach [Sovngarde](Skyrim_Sovngarde_(quest).md) as part of the [Main Quest](Skyrim_Main_Quest.md), you will encounter Ulfric in the afterlife, wandering around helplessly in Alduin's mist. When you get near him, he will remark: *"Skyrim was betrayed, the blood of her sons spilled in doomed struggle against fate. And so in death, too late, I learn the truth - fed by war, so waxed the power of Alduin, World-Eater - wisdom now useless. By gods' jest in this grim mist together snared, Stormcloak and Imperial, we wander hopeless, waiting for succor."* If you approach him at any other time, he will sometimes ask: *"Do you know the way? I'm weary and lost."* He may also warn you: *"Beware! The World-Eater waits within the mist!"* or *"There is no escape. Courage is useless."* You can do nothing but leave him behind and proceed to fight Alduin so his soul can find rest in Sovngarde. When Alduin has been [defeated](Skyrim_Dragonslayer.md), the mist will disappear from Sovngarde and Ulfric will walk towards the [Hall of Valor](Skyrim_Hall_of_Valor.md) and thank you for lifting the curse of the World-Eater.

- He is [voiced](Skyrim_Voice_Actors.md) by [Vladimir Kulich](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Kulich).
- Despite being completely opposed to the Empire, Ulfric will refuse to kill the [Emperor](Skyrim_Emperor_Titus_Mede_II_(real).md) during his visit to Solitude. If you question him about this he will tell you that *"We're ready to march on Solitude, but the Emperor's [cousin](Skyrim_Vittoria_Vici.md) is getting married! If royal blood was spilt, all of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) would be up in arms. We can't afford an all out war with the Empire. And, as much as I'd like to kill the man myself, we can't risk an all out war with the Empire. We'll bide our time for now..."*
- There are, in fact, three versions of Ulfric: - The main version has a base ID of `0001414D` and a ref ID of `0001B131`.
