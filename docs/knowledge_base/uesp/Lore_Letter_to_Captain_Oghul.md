Letter to Captain Oghul **by [Dulzur](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Dulzur)** *An Orcish mariner's letter to a fellow captain*
---
Hail, Oghul.

I just received your letter from the post in [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md). To answer your question, yeah, the trade's been good since the [Elf-Queen's](Lore_Ayrenn.md) mandate. Better than good. These [Tall Elves](Lore_Altmer.md) are a pompous bunch, but gold's gold, and they pay better than most. That said, sailing around [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md)'s no roll in the daisies. I know you can't navigate worth a damn, so you'd best pick up a sailing master on your next trip to Sentinel.

First thing you've got to know about sailing near Summerset is that it's bigger than it looks. A lot bigger. For most folk, the island looks downright inviting—white sandy beaches, sweet-smelling blossoms, freshwater springs and the like. But what they don't see is the nest of crags and reefs just beneath the surface of the water—all covered in [corals](Lore_Coral.md). And I'm not talking about the flimsy [echatere](Lore_Echatere.md) -antlers you see in the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). I'm talking about massive sea-bones—bleached corals that are hard as [Mauloch](Lore_Mauloch.md)'s chin and sharp as [Mehrunes' Razor](Lore_Mehrunes%27_Razor.md). Just one of those scarps can split a hull like a flay-knife through [pig](Lore_Pig.md) -skin, so sharp eyes and long wicks, eh? Mind your maps and draft.

Then there are the pirates. Damned [Sea Elves](Lore_Maormer.md). Can't go a day without spotting their serpent-banners fluttering over a sunken merchantman or [whaling](Lore_Whale.md) ship. We've only had to repel boarders twice, but we thumped them good. I doubt they'll take another swing at an [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) brig for a while. You're welcome.

Keep a close eye on your crew too. You know how superstitious sailors can be. You wouldn't believe some of the ghost stories I've heard. Giant [sea serpents](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md), ghost ships, [slug-men](Lore_Sea_Sload.md) that can mash your brains like blood pudding. On and on. If you give the deck-grunts too long a leash, you're liable to have a mutiny on your hands. So don't be afraid to crack that cat 'o nine if a tusker won't keep his gob shut.

Fair winds and still waters, ay? We'll trade tankards and tales in [Shimmerene](Lore_Shimmerene.md) soon enough.

[Vosh](Lore_Old_Orcish.md#V),
Dulzur
