[![SR-banner-Nightingales.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/SR-banner-Nightingales.png/200px-SR-banner-Nightingales.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-banner-Nightingales.png) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/51/SR-npc-Karliah_11.jpg/300px-SR-npc-Karliah_11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Karliah_11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Karliah_11.jpg) Nightingales The Nightingale Trinity, usually called simply the **Nightingales**, are a trio of highly skilled thieves dedicated to the service of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TN1-1)</sup> Members of the order take the Oath: the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md) must remain at the [Twilight Sepulcher](Lore_Twilight_Sepulcher.md), the Temple of Nocturnal, which must be protected from all threats, even in death, until Nocturnal accepts the spirit of a Nightingale into the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md), her plane of Oblivion. In return, Nightingales use the abilities granted to them for whatever they see fit to do in life.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TN1-1)</sup>

Members are typically chosen from the higher echelons of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md). Although there is no formal association between the two factions and most guild members are unaware of this arrangement, the Trinity is usually very influential on how the guild operates. Nightingales claim they created the guild phrase "walk with the shadows."<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TN1-1)</sup> Contrary to popularized misconceptions, the Oath is considered a business transaction rather than a religious matter.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TN1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NFOF-2)</sup> While most members certainly take the Oath very seriously, they are not worshipping Nocturnal as a deity, but respecting her as the patron of thieves.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TN1-1)</sup>

**Nightingale Hall** is the trinity's headquarters, located just off the road outside of [Riften](Lore_Riften.md), near the [Shadow Stone](Lore_Standing_Stones.md#Standing_Stones). A portal to the Twilight Sepulcher is included in Nightingale Hall.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRHall-3)</sup> In the Thieves Guild, Nightingale Hall is considered a legend, like the trinity itself.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRBrynjolf-4)</sup>

## History

### Origins
When the Nightingale Trinity was first created is not known, however it may have been after the Second Era.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Zeni Max Interview-5)</sup>

### Third Era
The most famous "Nightingale" in history, [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), first appeared around [3E 383](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_383).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRB9-6)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DFC-7)</sup> However, the Nightingales disavow that Tharn was ever a member, claiming that the bard known as Nightingale was actually Drayven Indoril, a master thief (and member of the Nightingale Trinity) who was hired by Tharn to steal the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md), and the truth was distorted long after the fact for political reasons. They also claim that a child was born of a liaison between Drayven and [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), and the child and her progeny would go on to become Nightingales themselves.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TN2-8)</sup>
