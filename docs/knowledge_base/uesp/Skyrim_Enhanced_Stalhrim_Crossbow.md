# Stalhrim

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/SR-activity-Mining_%28Stalhrim%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Stalhrim%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Stalhrim).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Stalhrim).jpg) A stalhrim deposit [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/SR-activity-Mining_%28Stalhrim_%28depleted%29%29.jpg/200px-SR-activity-Mining_%28Stalhrim_%28depleted%29%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Stalhrim_(depleted)).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-activity-Mining_(Stalhrim_(depleted)).jpg) A depleted stalhrim deposit **Stalhrim** is an ancient Nordic enchanted ice that can be used as a crafting material. Once you have obtained the [Ebony Smithing](Skyrim_Ebony_Smithing.md) perk and completed the quest [A New Source of Stalhrim](Skyrim_A_New_Source_of_Stalhrim.md), you can craft stalhrim armor and weapons. All items, except arrows, can be tempered with a sample of stalhrim. Stalhrim heavy armor is equal in quality to [dragonplate armor](Skyrim_Dragonplate_Armor.md), but lighter. Stalhrim light armor exceeds the quality of [glass armor](Skyrim_Glass_Armor.md), but is weaker than [dragonscale armor](Skyrim_Dragonscale_Armor.md) (with the exception of the shield). Stalhrim weapons do the same amount of damage as [ebony](Skyrim_Ebony.md) weapons, and weigh less. [Resist frost](Skyrim_Resist_Frost.md) and [frost damage](Skyrim_Frost_Damage.md) enchantments are 25% stronger when placed on stalhrim items. [Chaos damage](Skyrim_Chaos_Damage.md) enchantments are also 25% stronger, due to its frost damage effect. Lastly, Stalhrim weapons have higher stagger values (by 0.1) than weapons made of other materials, except daggers, bows, and arrows. In relative terms, this means Stalhrim swords and greatswords receive the greatest benefit within their respective weapon categories, as they start out with the least stagger.

- 3 samples of stalhrim are obtained by [mining](Skyrim_Mining.md) stalhrim deposits, which take twice as long to mine as other ore veins (six "swings" per sample instead of three). Like ore veins, there is a 10% chance these will be accompanied by a random, level-dependent gem. Once exhausted, most deposits will uncover a dead draugr, from which you can loot gold and other valuables.
- Stalhrim can only be mined with an [ancient Nordic pickaxe](Skyrim_Ancient_Nordic_Pickaxe.md).
- Stalhrim doesn't need to be [smelted](Skyrim_Smelter.md), and is used in the state from which it is mined.
- Stalhrim [heavy armor](Skyrim_Armor.md#Heavy_Armor) is made from stalhrim samples, [quicksilver ingots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Quicksilver_Ingot), and [leather strips](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips).
- Stalhrim light armor is made from stalhrim samples, [steel ingots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Ingot), and leather strips.
- Stalhrim weapons are made from stalhrim samples and leather strips.

All stalhrim armor and weapons can be found in leveled lists starting at level 35. Stalhrim will respawn with the rest of their location after a certain period of time.

## Notes
- Stalhrim heavy equipment offers the most armor points per unit weight (102 armor, 59 weight, 1.73 armor per weight) of any forgeable heavy armor, and with only a 5% loss in overall defense compared to the best heavy armor, Daedric (108 armor, 81 weight, 1.33 armor per weight). [Wolf Armor](Skyrim_Wolf_Armor.md) offers the most armor points per unit weight (72 armor, 32 weight, 2.25 apw), but with a significant loss of armor points compared to Stalhrim (29% less armor than Stalhrim, 33% less armor than Daedric); it is, however, the lightest heavy armor suit. Once you reach the armor cap better armor no longer helps you, so weight becomes the only meaningful factor; contrarily, the Conditioning perk and the Steed Stone make weight meaningless, so only armor matters. If you have both, your choice of heavy armor becomes purely aesthetic.
- If [Deor Woodcutter](Skyrim_Deor_Woodcutter.md) or [Fanari Strong-Voice](Skyrim_Fanari_Strong-Voice.md) are killed prior to completing [A New Source of Stalhrim](Skyrim_A_New_Source_of_Stalhrim.md), you will never be able to learn to forge stalhrim yourself.
- If you attempt to mine a Stalhrim deposit with only a regular pickaxe in your inventory, a "You need an Ancient Nordic Pickaxe to use this." message will appear on the top left of the screen.
- If you choose to mine Stalhrim manually, you can use the [Elemental Fury](Skyrim_Elemental_Fury.md) shout to mine the deposits faster. Don't use an enchanted Ancient Nordic Pickaxe, though, or the shout's speed bonus won't work.
- Stalhrim items are frequently for sale from Solstheim vendors and always benefit from the Ebony Smithing perk when tempered, regardless of quest status.
- Stalhrim has no heavy shield; only the light variety exists. However, the weight class of a shield does not affect the game in any way.
- 10 pieces of stalhrim are needed for the [Halbarn Favor Quest](Skyrim_Halbarn_Favor_Quest.md).
- "Stál hrím" is Icelandic for "steel frost".
- Unlike in [Bloodmoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Bloodmoon:Raw_Stalhrim), stalhrim in Skyrim cannot be used as an alchemy ingredient.
- Stalhrim weapons have higher stagger values (by 0.1) than weapons made of other materials, except daggers, bows, and arrows.
- When multiple effects are enchanted onto a Stalhrim weapon(via the Extra Effect perk), if one of the effects is Frost or Chaos Damage, any other Destruction effect will also be increased in effectiveness, even if it is not itself Frost-related. For example, a 32 point Drain Magicka effect will be boosted to 40 points when paired with any sort of frost damage effect, even a single point. This effect does not occur with armor.
- By default, stalhrim's 25% bonus on frost-related enchanting has no effect until you visit Raven Rock. If you acquire a stalhrim item before then, frost-related effects will not have the bonus, even after visiting Raven Rock.
- Despite being somewhat lore inaccurate, Stalhrim weapons cannot be obtained from high-level Draugr, who wield Ebony weapons instead of Stalhrim, which is most likely an oversight.
- Version 1.0.4 of the [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch) has all Stalhrim armor pieces distinguish between light and heavy in their inventory text.

## Bugs
- Although the Stalhrim deposits eventually replenish, there is a chance that you may not be able to mine them again, and can only search the Draugr underneath. <sup>**?**</sup> - Save, exit to the title screen, and reload. You should be able to mine the deposits again.
- Equip your Ancient Nordic Pickaxe and swing at the deposits manually.
- Fast travel to anywhere on Solstheim, then reload. The deposits should be mineable once again.
- Stalhrim Light Bracers incorrectly benefit from the Fists of Steel perk. - The [Unofficial Skyrim Special Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Unofficial_Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch), version 4.1.7, fixes this bug.
