Alits (or **Dusky Alits**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RJ-1)</sup> are tailless, bipedal omnivorous [reptiles](Lore_Reptile.md) that are native to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> They also inhabit parts of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), such as the island of [Khenarthi's Roost](Lore_Khenarthi%27s_Roost.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BGIB-4)</sup> and a species of brown **Desert Alits** live in [Northern Elsweyr](Lore_Northern_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAIT-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESO-3)</sup> Alits can also be found in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), though they are not native to the region.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MFPO-6)</sup> They are related to [guar](Lore_Guar.md), [kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md), and [wormmouths](Lore_Wormmouth.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MFPO-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EDAYQ-7)</sup>

Built like the kagouti, the Alit has a large head and protruding jaw, and when running on its short, stumpy legs, it looks like a big toothy mouth with feet.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWGen Alit-8)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BGIB-4)</sup> While most lack the stature of their kagouti cousins, they possess a more vicious temperament and more cunning attack instinct.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-KCAHC-9)</sup>

## Behavior and Diet
Alit attack by biting and can jump deceptively high.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BGIB-4)</sup> Alits are primarily solitary creatures,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MFPO-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BGIB-4)</sup> but have on occasion been known to group together and use primitive pack tactics to surround their prey before striking at them from multiple sides.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-KCAHC-9)</sup><sup>:20</sup> Solitary alit prefer going after easy prey such as [sheep](Lore_Sheep.md), while those in bigger groups are bolder, going after prey like [thunderbug](Lore_Thunderbug.md) nests.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BGIB-4)</sup> They are known to eat the 'meat-husks' left behind when a [nix-hound](Lore_Nix-Hound.md) drains their prey of bodily fluids.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> Though sometimes impatient alits will fight with nix-hounds over a fresh kill.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Like guar, they also supplement their nutrition by rooting.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MFPO-6)</sup>

Male alits are known as bulls.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Molar-12)</sup> Female alit are said to make a "heen-hoon" noise to attract amorous bull alits.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Heen-13)</sup>

Alits are known to carry [diseases](Lore_Diseases.md) such as [ataxia](Lore_Diseases.md#Ataxia),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup> [black-heart blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black-Heart_Blight),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MW-2)</sup> and [rockjoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rockjoint).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RJ-1)</sup>

## Uses
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/MW-ing-Alit_Hide.jpg/175px-MW-ing-Alit_Hide.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-ing-Alit_Hide.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-ing-Alit_Hide.jpg) Alit hide (Morrowind) Alits have tough skin and chewy meat,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AAIT-5)</sup> and their [hides](Lore_Alchemy_A.md#Alit_Hide) make a serviceable multi-purpose leather.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWGen Alit Hide-15)</sup> The [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md) value their tanned hides highly,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-KCAHC-9)</sup> as they make use of them as trade goods.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWGen Alit Hide-15)</sup> The material has applications for armor crafting and [alchemy](Lore_Alchemy.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

Animal enthusiasts from Valenwood used to import the creatures. A good deal of them escaped captivity, and now alits roam the southwestern forests.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MFPO-6)</sup> The [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) use Alits in ornamental taxidermy. Carefully-preserved alit heads, with their skin tanned and stretched over their skull, are painted with [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md) symbols and use [emeralds](Lore_Emerald.md) for eyes.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
