# Flame Atronach

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/LG-cardart-Flame_Atronach.png/150px-LG-cardart-Flame_Atronach.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Flame_Atronach.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Flame_Atronach.png) Flame Atronach (Legends) Flame Atronachs (sometimes known as **Fire Atronachs** or **[Fire Daedra](Lore_Fire_Daedra.md)**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAESO-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Monster Experts-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ATRQUEST-3)</sup> are [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) native to [Infernace](Lore_Infernace.md), a collective realm full and made of lava and fire. Seemingly made entirely of flame, they can be found unbound in the wild or can be conjured by those with magical aptitude to serve as guards, laborers, or otherwise obey their conjurers. Summoning of Flame Atronachs is usually performed through [Koron's](Lore_Koron.md) [Peremptory Summons](Lore_Koron%27s_Peremptory_Summons.md), a spell which usually summons Flame Atronachs in their conventional forms but which can be altered by conjurers to alter the minutiae of the summons (such as plane to summon from, daedra to summon, and form in which the daedra can be conjured).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO_CSCCSC-4)</sup>

They are conventionally summoned and found in the form of vaguely feminine flaming floating humanoids; however they do appear in other forms—including a smaller form called the Flame Pixie. Their summoning was first perfected by the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md), especially [Corvus Direnni](Lore_Corvus_Direnni.md) and [Peregrine Direnni](Lore_Peregrine_Direnni.md), who discovered that they could be bound into different forms by their conjurers.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOFCC-5)</sup> Flame Atronachs can be shaped by their conjuror to take the form of common animals to serve as mounts. Known varieties include bears, horses, guar, camels, senche-tigers and wolves.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The [Laumonts](Lore_Laumont.md), a Breton family line, is rumored to have originated from a union of [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) and Flame Atronach.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Lud Lau Hireling-7)</sup>. Similarly, it isn't unheard of for some conjurers to be attracted to Flame Atronachs.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LATFMDD-8)</sup>

Flame Atronachs are found in many Daedric Realms, including those of prominent princes and minor planes. While possessing some intelligence, Flame Atronachs are largely an unorganized and chaotic group, with individuals being largely indistinguishable from one another. They are a source of [Fire Salts](Lore_Alchemy_F.md#Fire_Salts), an alchemical ingredient which has varied utility, and are a source of [Atronite](Lore_Atronite.md), valuable stones left behind when an atronach is dispelled or defeated.

Their inversions, [Cold-Flame Atronachs](Lore_Cold-Flame_Atronach.md), originate in [Fourth Sinus of Takubar](Lore_Fourth_Sinus_of_Takubar.md), an inversion of Infernace where all flame is cold.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DDCFA-9)</sup>

## Variants
Aside from the commonly found humanoid forms, there are many other types of flame atronach found in [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).

### Cold-Flame Atronach
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/73/ON-creature-Cold-Flame_Atronach.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Cold-Flame_Atronach.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Cold-Flame_Atronach.jpg) Cold-Flame Atronach [Cold-Flame Atronachs](Lore_Cold-Flame_Atronach.md) are a powerful [Elemental Daedra](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md) native to a pocket realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) called the [Fourth Sinus of Takubar](Lore_Fourth_Sinus_of_Takubar.md). The cold-flame atronach summons an essence of cold fire, and has a freezing ambiance that notably drops the temperature nearby, the same way the flame atronach gives off heat.

Cold-flame atronachs came to serve Molag Bal after an incident involving a flame atronach which led to their banishment from Coldharbour. A concerned denizen, [Denogorath the Dread Archivist](Lore_Denogorath.md) wanted a hovering sentry that possessed the flame atronach's same expressionless violence to fill the void they left behind. After searching over 37,000 planes and realms, he found a suitable replacement on the Fourth Sinus of Takubar. Cold-flame atronachs served as sentries in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) during the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).

Like other atronachs, it can be summoned in different forms: [Peregrine Direnni](Lore_Peregrine_Direnni.md) was the first to summon one in the shape of a [wolf](Lore_Wolf.md).

## Related Beings

### Brothers of Strife
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bc/ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Brothers_Will_Rise.jpg) The Brothers of Strife The [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(creature).md) are two beings of [elemental](Lore_Elements.md) destruction that act as guardians of the [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls.md) region of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They both have the appearance of flaming [bone colossi](Lore_Bone_Colossus.md), towering [undead](Lore_Undead.md) [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) [constructs](Lore_Golem.md) usually assembled from the bones of many corpses. Although the Brothers are a powerful [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md), they cannot be controlled and thus were eternally bound.

The Brothers were created in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by the Daedra-worshipping [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) clans of the region after [Nedic](Lore_Nede.md) tribes (most prominently the [Rontha](Lore_Rontha.md)) from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) had successfully invaded Stonefalls. Faced with imminent defeat, the Chimer army made a last stand at a Daedric shrine far south of modern [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md). Despite the dangers, the Chimer performed a dark transformation ritual to create the Brothers of Strife, using flame atronach essences and the hearts of their Nedic enemies, and offering two of their leaders as sacrifices. The monsters succeeded in crushing the Nedes and liberating Stonefalls, but continued to rampage. Using flame atronachs as guardians, the Chimer managed to bind the brothers atop [Ash Mountain](Lore_Ash_Mountain.md) and the [Tormented Spire](Lore_Tormented_Spire.md), two large volcanoes that dominate the region. The responsibility of keeping the Brothers bound eventually passed down to [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md) and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), two [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), the descendants of the Chimer. These bindings were briefly broken in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).

### Potentially related
There are some creatures which share many traits with Flame Atronachs. They have not been confirmed to be variants of atronachs or entirely different creatures altogether, leaving their nature ultimately unclear.

- Blazeborn Senche-lions are elemental felines that take a molten fiery appearance. They are described as being "born in flame".<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BBL-19)</sup>
- Dragonfire Wolves are colossal flaming wolves ridden by [Dragonknights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragonknight) into battle. It is speculated that they could be mystically imbued with [Dragonfire](Lore_Dragon.md#Uses) or are flame atronachs conjured in the form of wolves.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Dragonfire_Wolf-20)</sup>
- Flame Hounds are elemental wolves that are composed entirely from a lava-like substance. Much like Flame Atronachs, they are constantly burning and use fire-based attacks in combat. They also can be found in Daedric realms such as the Deadlands.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup>

## Infernace
**[Infernace](Lore_Infernace.md)**, the collective realm of the Flame Atronachs, is described as mundane and repetitive, apparently being the aggregate of all of its inhabitants. It, much like the Flame Atronachs, is largely constructed of magma and flame, and is apparently the plane where most Flame Atronach summonings call. It has an inversion known as the Fourth Sinus of Takubar, which is apparently much like Infernace except totally devoid of heat.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DDCFA-9)</sup>
