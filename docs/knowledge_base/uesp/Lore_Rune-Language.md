# Rune-Language

The unnamed **rune-language** is that which is inscribed in [its own alphabet](Lore_Runic_Alphabet.md) upon the magical, enigmatic runestones that once littered [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOrunestones-1)</sup> and the realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> It is not derived from any known historic or pre- [Dawn](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) culture.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> Runes have specific, measurable meanings and serve as a common language among enchanters that allow them to repeatably imbue items with mystical properties.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAArcanists-4)</sup>

## Notes
- [‡](#note_removed) These runes were removed from the game and have the same translations as Odra and Taderi respectively.
- According to early developer commentary, the rune-language was going to be heavily featured in [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)'s cut [spellcrafting](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Spellcrafting) mechanic, having been manipulated by a culture that no one has ever heard which operated sometime in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s distant past; the culture maintained a network of classrooms across Tamriel, which contained runestone tablets used for the crafting of spells.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Quakecon2014-5)</sup>
- The "Runic Accord" was a historical event during the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) that took place some years after [Archmagus Shalidor](Lore_Shalidor.md) founded the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md), and was attributed to a contemporary Arch-Mage of the College at the time.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-6)</sup> Whether or not the Runic Accord was related to the rune-language is unknown.
- The runes used by [Arcanists](Lore_Arcanists.md) are wholly unrelated to this runic language.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LAArcanists-4)</sup>
