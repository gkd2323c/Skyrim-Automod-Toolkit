# Tu'whacca

- *"You will walk through the Hall of Heroes and find your way to the eternal sands of the Far Shores.*
- *It has been this way and will be this way for all [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) who walk under the watchful eye of Tu'whacca."* — [When We Pass](Lore_When_We_Pass.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/60/ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Relief_of_Tava.jpg) A bas-relief of Tu'whacca

Tu'whacca (or simply **Tuwhacca**),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TM-1)</sup> the **Tricky God**, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) **God of Souls** (or **Shepherd of Souls**), and the **Caretaker of the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md)**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFTF-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TP-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Rft D-4)</sup> Before the creation of the world, he was the god of **Nobody Really Cares**.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFTF-2)</sup> When [Tall Papa](Lore_Ruptga.md) undertook the creation of the Walkabout, Tu'whacca found a purpose: he became the caretaker of the Far Shores, and continues to help [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) find their way into the afterlife. Though a soul may have enemies who will try to keep it from reaching the Far Shores, Tu'Whacca is sly and clever, and always outwits them. His cult is sometimes associated with [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) in the more cosmopolitan, [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) regions of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), where the names Tu'whacca and Arkay are basically interchangeable.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-VOFTF-2)</sup> [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) detest this [Imperialization](Lore_Imperial.md), considering it a betrayal of their heritage.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ABOOH-5)</sup> Nevertheless, scholars cannot help but note the clear similarities between them, as well as the similarities they both share with [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes_(god).md) of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), in both the etymology of their names and the divine roles they fill.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TAX-6)</sup>

## Culture and Worship
Being an integral part of the Redguard belief system, they have developed many customs related to him. A sneeze may prompt a Redguard to say something like "Tu'whacca bless you", as ancient writings suggest a sneeze is a sign of the presence of an evil spirit.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TOTBP-7)</sup> The dead or dying are often referred to with the adage "may Tu'whacca have mercy on [their] soul" or "may Tu'whacca escort [their] soul to the Far Shores".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LJJ-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SAMITA-9)</sup> When mummifying a body, the first step Redguard priests perform is to consecrate the body with the Blessings of Tu'whacca.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TESOM-10)</sup>

Tu'whacca was once considered the patron of mages, and some adorned their [staves](Lore_Staves.md) with his sacred [ibis](Lore_Ibis.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CM28RG-11)</sup>

Elaborate, oversized ibis-head masks with emerald eyes are worn by priests of Tu'whacca in the March of Wisdom.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Contraband OTM-12)</sup>

The black [sphynx cats](Lore_Cat.md#Sphynx) of the Alik'r, dubbed "Tu'whacca's Sphynx Cats", are said to bring the blessing of Tu'whacca to their household. For this reason, they are honored. They have also been observed sleeping with those who near death, giving them a reputation as guides to the [Far Shores](Lore_Far_Shores.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

## Notes
- As part of a covenant with Tu'whacca, three [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) warriors sacrificed their souls to create the [Ansei Wards](Lore_Ansei_Wards.md) - magical relics that prevented even the most powerful [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) from raising their dead.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONTKF-14)</sup>
- Throne Keeper Farvad, a priest of Tu'whacca, regularly passed on wisdom that allegedly came from the god himself.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONTKF-14)</sup>
