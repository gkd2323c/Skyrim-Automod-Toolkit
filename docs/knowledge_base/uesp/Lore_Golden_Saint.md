# Aureal


They are a matriarchal society, tending to look down upon all males. Even male Golden Saints consider themselves and other males to be lesser. The Aureal have a warrior-like caste system with a seven-tier hierarchy, ascending from lowest to highest in rank as follows: Auren, Auredel, Aurmok, Aurmokel, Aurig, Malaurig, and Pelaurig.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SIGSR-2)</sup> They make up one half of Sheogorath's army, along with the [Mazken](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mazken). The two groups have a great rivalry, each believing they are Sheogorath's favored. The Aureal are heavily linked to [Mania](Lore_Mania.md), and act as its policemen. In times of war, they are placed under the command of the Duke of Mania.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SIGSR-2)</sup>

Their sacred home of [Brellach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brellach) lies in the northern part of Mania. Within it, the Wellspring of the Aureal serves as a beacon to guide their reformation from the Waters of Oblivion. Little is known of their [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md), but their Wellspring flows with a golden liquid fitting of the saints.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-THASI-5)</sup> Some Golden Saints are also said to reside in the [Colored Rooms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Colored_Rooms).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Darien Gautier-4)</sup>

[Amber](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amber) that grows in the [Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Root) Systems of the Shivering Isles is linked to the Aureal, which may be used to summon or even bind them. The most powerful among the Aureal require the usage of the rare occurring Sheogorath-Shaped Amber.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RAFECC-6)</sup> Their hearts have modest magical properties.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GD-1)</sup>

As a symbol of their devotion to Sheogorath, the Aureal engrave their golden teeth with the three faces of madness, a symbol of Sheogorath's office.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOContraband-7)</sup> The average Saint is six feet tall.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

Mortals have struggled to replicate [Daedric equipment](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment), and the Dremora [Lyranth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lyranth) believes their efforts remain pale imitations of true Daedric craftsmanship, limited by the inferior materials found on Nirn.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LAMDDSE-9)</sup> A mortal managed to accomplish crafting the avian-themed arms and armaments of the Aureal, but it is a dangerous endeavor, as one can lose their mind making it. They are also an expensive craft, as they use gold metal as a base. As gold is far too soft on its own, multiple steps are required to strengthen them to be more nimble and harder than steel. The structure of the equipment is reinforced with refined [moonstone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonstone), and then the gold is hammered into place while the gold is heated just below its melting point. Finally, a [Daedra's Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedra_Heart) is put into the forge when the equipment is being heated, allowing them to be hammered into shape, and transforming the attributes of the metal, giving it an uncanny hardness.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TJ-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-SRCC-11)</sup>

## History

### Interregnum
Despite the Aureal's animosity toward mortals, they took a mortal in as their own on at least one occasion. Originally summoned to take the place of a mother who died during child birth, a Golden Saint functioned as a mother figure for a young [Malkur Valos](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malkur_Valos). After Malkur's father died in a mage duel, the Golden Saint was no longer bound to the conjuration spell. However, believing heavily in the steadfast principles of honor and duty, the committed Golden Saint took Malkur to the Shivering Isles to continue raising him there. Malkur began training there under the Aureal, developing his talent, honor, and innovation and attracting the attention of [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md). Thanks to their guidance, Malkur proved to be a valuable asset during the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) and [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md)'s invasion of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). He even participated in the final assault on the Endless Stair, helping bring an end to the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TNM-12)</sup>

Due to his boredom from the [Three Banners War](Lore_Wars.md#Three_Banners_War) lacking sufficient carnage, Sheogorath stole [Volendrung](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volendrung) from [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) and sent it onto [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), hoping the resulting chaos would bring him entertainment.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOChaos Magic-13)</sup> Golden Saints were chosen as the artifact's guardians, preventing the weak and unworthy from claiming it as a prize. <sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ESOGolden Saint-14)</sup>

### Third Era
One of the more notable golden saints is [Staada](Lore_Staada.md), who is often sent into Tamriel to carry out important missions. In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), she was sent by Sheogorath to lead some of his other daedric servants. They were ordered to disturb a priestess of [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) secluded on an island in [Sheogorad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheogorad), but were banished by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Morrowind-15)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Staada had become the stalwart commander of Brellach, but the [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) turned the Aureals' home to ruin.<sup>[nb1](#intnote_A)</sup> The Wellspring of the Aureal was taken by [Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag), which turned the Aureal defenders to stone. Sheogorath sent his [Champion](Lore_Hero.md) to Brellach, where the hero was able to destroy the Order Crystals and rescue the Aureal. Staada then led the Aureal in removing any remnants of Order from their home, resuming their normal duties afterward.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-THASI-5)</sup> The Champion earned their respect, and later their adoration when Jyggalag was freed from his curse and the Champion became the new Madgod.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-THASI-5)</sup>

## Notes
- [<sup>nb1</sup>](#note_A) If the player instead chooses to become the Duke of Mania during the [Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles) main quest, [Syl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syl) will survive and attack the Mazken's home of [Pinnacle Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pinnacle_Rock) in retaliation for [Thadon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thadon)'s death.
- The term "Aureal" was first introduced in the *[Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles)* expansion for *[TES IV: Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*. They were referred to only as "Golden Saints" in *[TES III: Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)*.
- They are not to be confused with the [Aurorans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auroran), another humanoid Daedric species with golden armor and uncommon skin tones.
- Much like Sheogorath, Golden Saints are associated with corrupting the benevolence of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md)'s sacred [Moon Sugar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moon_Sugar) by tempting mortals to over-consume the substance.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Pacrooti-16)</sup>
