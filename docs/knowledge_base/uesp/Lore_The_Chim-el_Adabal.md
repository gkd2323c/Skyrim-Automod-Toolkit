The Chim-el Adabal **by [Mannimarco](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Mannimarco)** *Mannimarco's musings on the Amulet of Kings and its red diamond*
---
The [Amulet of the Kings of Glory](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md), pendant of the red diamond Chim-el Adabal, [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) relic of [Divine](Lore_Divines.md) investiture. Whatever myths exist concerning its creation, this much is fact: as a vessel of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)'s will, it is tied to him in a very real way.

The corruption of the [Dragonfire](Lore_Dragonfires.md) ritual was a taxing endeavor. I spent weeks inscribing the glyphs and preparing the incantations that [Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) foolishly believed would change his ancestry and birthright. Trusting simpleton. [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) now lies adrift in the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), vulnerable to the parasitic [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) that feast upon the souls of its people.

[Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) once asked me, outright, if the Amulet could be used to repair the damage wrought by the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md). Subtlety was never part of Abnur's repertoire. I could see the scheming machinations behind his eyes even as he pledged his loyalty to me. I think he knew, even then, that he was disposable. Who needs a scheming politician when one aspires to be a god?

The answer to his question is, of course, yes. This is why I must seize the amulet and protect it. It is the vessel through which I will supplant my [would-be master](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and assume dominion over two worlds. Why would a god settle for just one?
