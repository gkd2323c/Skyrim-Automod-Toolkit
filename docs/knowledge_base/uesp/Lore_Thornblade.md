# Thornblade

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Thornblade.jpg) Thornblade [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg/100px-OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg) Banner of the Knights of the Thorn The Thornblade was a family heirloom of the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) [Indarys](Lore_Indarys.md) family.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CAI-1)</sup> It was a one-handed sword with an ornate hilt. It was imbued with an [enchantment](Lore_Enchanting.md) capable of disintegrating the armor of its victims. Some depictions show it inlaid with a green jewel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Thornblade-2)</sup> Its motif of thorny vines matched that of [Staff of Indarys](Lore_Staff_of_Indarys.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SOI-3)</sup> the other heirloom and featured symbols of the County [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md) during the Indarys rule, as well as emblems of the local order of the [Knights of the Thorn](Lore_Knights_of_the_Thorn.md), which depicted a sword wreathed in thorny vines.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBShield-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBBanner-5)</sup>

## History
Little is known about the history of the Thornblade. Its name was already known prior to the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Campaign-6)</sup>

Alongside with Staff of Indarys it was regarded as an heirloom of the Indarys family. According to Count [Andel Indarys](Lore_Andel_Indarys.md) it was held in Castle Cheydinhal for several generations.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CAI-1)</sup> However, the Count himself was an immigrant and the first in his family to hold the title and inhabit this castle.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBMN-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GTC-8)</sup> It is possible that the sword was awarded to the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) after they rescued Andel's son, Farwil, from the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). However, it is unknown whether the blade left the possession of the Indarys lineage, as it was one of the heirlooms, alongside Staff of Indarys, that were offered as a reward for the rescue.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CAI-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWK-9)</sup>
