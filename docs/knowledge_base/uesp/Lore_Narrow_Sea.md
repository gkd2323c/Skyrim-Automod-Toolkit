[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5e/ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_05.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Inner_Sea_05.jpg) The Inner Sea [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/MW-place-Imperial_Prison_Ship.jpg/200px-MW-place-Imperial_Prison_Ship.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Imperial_Prison_Ship.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Imperial_Prison_Ship.jpg) Imperial Prison Ship in Seyda Neen The Inner Sea (also called the **Inland Sea**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWElone-1)</sup>, the **Narrow Sea**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AFATD1-2)</sup> and the **Vvardenfell Rift**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ro KZ-3)</sup>) is a body of water that separates the mainland of the Imperial province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from the large island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). It stretches from the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) and [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md) regions in western Vvardenfell all the way to [Azura's Coast](Lore_Azura%27s_Coast.md) in the east. It meets the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) twice: the two meld together near Azura's Coast, and again near the large and icy island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) northwest of Vvardenfell. There are coral reefs submerged in the waters of the Inner Sea.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BW-4)</sup>

There are conflicting reports on the origins of the Inner Sea. Some sources accredit an eruption of Red Mountain to its creation,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Ro KZ-3)</sup> whereas Vivec's Sermons point to the usage of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) circa [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TSLT-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DI1-6)</sup> However, some sources attest that the Inner Sea was present before both the first major eruption of Red Mountain and the activation of the Numidium.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TBOS-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PGE1M-8)</sup> Furthermore, [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) left behind maps of the Inner Sea dating to the [Middle Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md#Middle_Merethic_Era), far before the first Dwemer colonies.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FOTN-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BTAOM-10)</sup>

The main port of the Inner Sea on Vvardenfell is [Ebonheart](Lore_Castle_Ebonheart.md), with minor ports being [Hla Oad](Lore_Hla_Oad.md), [Gnaar Mok](Lore_Gnaar_Mok.md), [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md), [Tel Branora](Lore_Tel_Branora.md) and the Imperial outpost of [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md) with its lighthouse Grand Pharos guiding mariners. The [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) once held a strategic outpost called [Fort Firemoth](Lore_Fort_Firemoth.md) on an offshore island of the same name, but it was overrun by an undead army and never reclaimed. The Inner Sea is home to a species of bioluminescent and sentient [Land Coral](Lore_Land_Coral.md) that grows exclusively on the land around it. The fields of [Vivec's Antlers](Lore_Vivec%27s_Antlers.md) is the most notable example of a site containing this species of Land Coral. Vivec's Antlers was also a battlefield in the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md) of [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), for the final battle involving the [Snow Demons](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) of [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(place).md) and their invasion of the province of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Akaviri-11)</sup>

The coast of Vvardenfell is rugged and treacherous, with shipwrecks scattered among the menhirs that protrude from the water. It has been a major center of smuggling operations by the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md) and other groups.
