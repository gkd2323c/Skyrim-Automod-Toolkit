By his own account Malacath loves very few things, vengeance being one of them. The Prince has described destructive vengeance as a beautiful act, the beauty of which the perpetrator shouldn't lessen by attempting to claim its consequences were unintended. According to Malacath himself, he has no love for any of the other Daedric Princes.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LOS-42)</sup> Despite some of his titles, Malacath is said by some sources to have historically exhibited a straightforward mentality, of which indirect and subversive action is unbecoming.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-WIV-43)</sup>

## Mythic Origin
Many variations of the origin of Malacath exist, with the truth ultimately being unclear. The Prince is commonly believed to have been created when [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) ate the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) ancestor spirit, [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-THOT-17)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TCO-44)</sup> while Trinimac's most devout Elven followers are said to have been transformed into the [Orsimer](Lore_Orc.md) ("Pariah Folk" in [Aldmeris](Lore_Ehlnofex_Languages.md)), or Orcs, in that same event.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup> However some Orcs believe that Trinimac still exists and Malacath is a separate entity.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3O-5)</sup> While Malacath himself has said that this tale is far too "literal minded".<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LOS-42)</sup> In addition, the [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md) information broker of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md), Madam Whim, has claimed that no living being can truly claim to understand how the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) and the Daedric Princes relate to one another, and that no information she's ever come across in her long life indicates stories of the Aedra to be more than stories. According to Whim, much like a "backwater village bully who falls in with a bandit gang", a Daedric Prince might go out of their way to avoid questions or quash rumors that claim they were once "an all-powerful Aedra". Whim finishes her comment by noting the "peculiar and particular cruelty" with which the written histories of Nirn treat the Orsimer, and asking, which is more likely, that an all-powerful Aedra found itself consumed by a Daedric Prince and recast in the mold of a pale shadow of itself, or that mortal scholars with a bias eagerly leapt on the metaphor that the Daedric Prince most associated with the Orc people was quite literally the excreted remains of a once-great god?<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-Maelstrom-46)</sup>

According to some sources the popular origin of Malacath is more of a traditional belief than anything else and, whatever his origin, there are none alive who could say what the truth is for certain. It is also said that, whatever his origin, Malacath is a Prince of Oblivion and the equal of Azura, Boethiah, Sheogorath, [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) and all the others. Furthermore, while it is possible Malacath came into existence in a different way than the rest, nowdays he is powerful and evoked whenever someone has felt betrayed, which in Tamriel is quite often.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPI-47)</sup>

### The Devouring of Trinimac
According to popular legend, Malacath was once [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), the most powerful of the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) ancestor spirits,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup> the "greatest knight of the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md)".<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LOS-42)</sup> During the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) began appearing in visions to the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md), instructing him to create a new sect, who would eventually become the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md). This was blasphemous to the priests of Trinimac, who threatened to exile the dissidents.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup>

The more popular versions of the tale says that Boethiah then ate Trinimac.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TCO-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup> One version in particular says Boethiah appeared as a beautiful lass, and asked Trinimac for a kiss. When he obliged, Boethiah swallowed him whole.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TILLOS-48)</sup> As the priests prepared to pass judgment on the Velothi dissidents, Boethiah appeared before them in Trinimac's form.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup> "Trinimac" burped, farted, and spoke foolishly.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TILLOS-48)</sup> And when the crowd grew large enough, Boethiah told them what had been whispered to Veloth and demonstrated the "lies" of Trinimac's teachings, all in Trinimac's voice.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TCO-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup> When the priests were sufficiently shamed and broken, Boethiah "squeezed out a great pile of dung" before the crowd, all that remained of the once proud knight, as proof that this was the truth.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-TCO-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TILLOS-48)</sup> Some accounts state the followers of both Boethiah and Trinimac are said to have rubbed the dung upon themselves and "changed their skins";<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TA-35)</sup> others claim that the dung pile "slunk away in shame".<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TILLOS-48)</sup>

These tales say that after being swallowed by Boethiah, Trinimac was tortured within her bowels, twisting and transforming him.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup> After being released, Boethiah banished Trinimac to what would become his realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), the Ashpit. There, Trinimac faded away and Malacath, an enraged being bent on revenge, was born.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup> And with Trinimac's transformation, so changed his devoted followers; thus were born the Orsimer, the "Pariah Folk", who follow Malacath to this day.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TFOT-45)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TILLOS-48)</sup>

### Orcish Origin Myths
Views on Malacath and his possible relationship to Trinimac vary among the Orcs.

In one Orcish telling, Trinimac confronted Boethiah for creating the dissident movement and challenged her to a battle. During the fight, Trinimac was about to strike down Boethiah when [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) appeared; the Webspinner stabbed Trinimac in the back. As Trinimac kneeled helpless, Boethiah invoked a ritual to scar and twist Trinimac's appearance, then banished the Aedra to a [place of ash](Lore_Ashpit.md). In a rage, Trinimac cut open his chest and "[tore] the shame from his spirit", being reborn as Mauloch.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-MOF-49)</sup>

Some Trinimac cultists believe the Aedra fooled [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), and rather than being transformed by his ordeal, he instead absorbed a portion of Boethiah's power and gave it to his followers, thus making the Orsimer "improved Elves."<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> Others believe that Malacath is actually an agent of Boethiah who has somehow imprisoned Trinimac,<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-BVTH-50)</sup> a wholly separate entity, and lies to keep them as pariahs under his thumb.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3O-5)</sup>

Reflecting these disparate viewpoints, "Mauloch" is seen by some as an Aedra like Trinimac, whereas "Malacath" is a Daedra.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DWAAPOT-16)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONObsidian-51)</sup>

### Khajiit
In Pre-Riddle'Thar Epiphany [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) texts, the demon Orkha was a spirit that followed [Boethra](Lore_Boethiah.md) back through the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) and spoke only in curses of affliction, knowing no other words. [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md), [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md) and Boethra battled Orkha, but the demon could only be banished and would not die.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TAS-15)</sup>

### From Exile to Exodus
According to the myth recounted in *[From Exile to Exodus](Lore_From_Exile_to_Exodus.md)* by Tarvyn Aram, during the Velothi exodus, the Velothi encountered the followers of Trinimac, who outnumbered them three to one. While Trinimac remained silent, his followers called the dissidents rebels, traitors, and filth. Boethiah then intervened, revealing the truth about the followers' identity as Ornim that had forgotten their days of [iron](Lore_Iron_Orc.md) and [wood](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), sea and fire, and become convinced they were [elves](Lore_Elf.md). Boethiah revealed that they had been deceived by the curses of a demon, who'd made it so that when they looked upon him they saw Boethiah herself. But it was Boethiah who'd brought them the [Orichalc](Lore_Orichalc.md), who'd taught them the benefits of war, how to hold their blades, and how to reach beyond. Boethiah then gestured with her hands, forming a triangular sign that could only be true, and walked in a way that revealed the path to achieve an Exodus. Thus the curse was lifted from their eyes. Where they once saw Trinimac, Greatest of All Warriors, they now saw **Malak, the King of Curses**. And where they once saw Boethiah, Daughter of Blades, they now saw Trinimac, as she had always been. The Warrior of East and West and of the [Starry Heart](Lore_Tamriel.md), who once bore the burden of separating [divinity](Lore_Heart_of_Lorkhan.md) from her [beloved](Lore_Lorkhan.md). Enraged, Malak uttered an unknown curse and jumped into Boethiah's, into Trinimac's, throat, entering her very being and causing her to collapse in agony, retching blood and ichor. Thus Boethiah-Trinimac collapsed, seemingly lifeless and her followers despaired.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FETE-52)</sup>

However, Mephala and Azura intervened, for though Malak had forgotten, to contend with Boethiah was to contend with the Triangular Gate. The two cast their own spell that echoed the music of [Dawn](Lore_Dawn_Era.md), aiding Boethiah in casting out Malak and his curse. Malak rose covered in Boethiah's blood and bile. Yet he now appeared different, not as the wretched Malak King of Curses, but as something more akin to the Trinimac his followers had loved. He now wore new armor and held a gleaming red axe, and his helm bore a tusked visage of the spurned and the opressed.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FETE-52)</sup> From beneath his new helm Malacath growled that Boethiah had forgotten what it meant to be an exile, and uttered a new terrible curse that had never been spoken. The sky split open with thunderous rain, the wind howled with a killing gale, and the very earth beneath them was sundered as fire and death rose from the pits. In the triangular field of divine chaos, Azura took a third of the followers and brought them to the [star-wounded east](Lore_Morrowind.md), while Mephala took another third and took them to the southeast, for she alone did not fear the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FETE-52)</sup> Boethiah than tore out the last of her guilt under the [sun](Lore_Sun.md) and transported the third of the followers that remained to Oblivion. The land they had previously occupied drifted into the sea, but was not wholly destroyed, as it still had stories to tell.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FETE-52)</sup>

The followers found themselves in a [deadened realm of smoke and ash](Lore_Ashpit.md). It was here that the worst of Malacath's fatal curse had been diverted by Boethiah. Malacath laughed that Boethiah had let him destroy her gift from the [king](Lore_Auri-El.md) that had been an idyllic paradise, but Boethiah responded her spheres were many and houses innumerable, and so she had no need of it.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FETE-52)</sup>

Malacath attacked once more, claiming he'd never stop hunting Boethiah. But, with a single precise strike, he was cleaved into the ashen wastes, his bones forming a new foundation. His blood mingled with the ash and soon the realm was his. Boethiah then spent time with the surviving followers, their skins changing under her teachings and the influence of the realm. When Malacath's spirit reappeared, Boethiah addressed him, urging him to stop trying to enforce his will through curses both he and his followers knew to be untrue, but instead to seek glory through struggle and to understand the Will Against Rule. In this way Malacath could come to learn the proper tendencies of the [hero](Lore_Hero.md) and know something of Love. She then formed a powerful sign with her hands, encapsulating Malacath and his followers into a singular sphere, casting it back onto the mountains of Nirn. Thus emerged the god Mauloch and the great Orcs, who would from than on build strength through adversity. Together, Boethiah and Mauloch tested and hardened their chosen peoples against one another, therefore guiding them further toward an Exodus.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-FETE-52)</sup>

## The Accords of Madness
Many accounts paint Malacath as a Prince who is very protective of his followers, and who becomes vindictive when they are wronged. Among the tales of the Madgod Sheogorath found in the *[Sixteen Accords of Madness](Lore_16_Accords_of_Madness.md)* is a story about Malacath and his demiprince son, [Emmeg Gro-Kayra](Lore_Emmeg_Gro-Kayra.md). Sometime before the founding of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), Sheogorath (in disguise) had given Emmeg, a renowned Orcish warrior, the [Neb-Crescen](Lore_Neb-Crescen.md). When drawn, the sword set off a bloodlust within Emmeg, and he murdered a young Orc girl in his frenzied haze and ran away. Sheogorath then summoned Malacath to where the slaughtered Orc girl was and instigated his vengeance. Sheogorath persuaded Malacath to avenge the Orc girl's death using the weapon of Sheogorath's choice and banish the murderer to his plane of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Malacath manifested in the direction the killer was fleeing, and drew the blade, which further fueled his rage. The prince quickly crossed the distance, and cleanly lopped Emmeg's head off his body, and what followed was a sudden silence. Seeing the severed head, Malacath realized what he had done; he had damned his biological son to the realm of the Mad God. To further exacerbate the pain, Sheogorath came to the scene of the infanticide to claim the still sentient head of Emmeg and the Neb-Cresen. Malacath stayed behind and mourned as he heard the pleas of his son being carried off into the distance.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-16Accords12-53)</sup>

## Worship

### Orcs
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cf/ON-concept-Malacath_Armors.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Malacath_Armors.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Malacath_Armors.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Malacath_Armors.jpg) Orcish Malacath Cult Style *God of curses, hear my prayer!
Lord of the betrayed, give me strength!
Keeper of the grudge, harden my heart!
Holder of the broken promises, ignite my anguish!
Master of the sworn oath, grant me the ferocity to overcome my enemies!
Malacath, hear my prayer!*
— *[Prayer to the Furious One](Lore_Prayer_to_the_Furious_One.md)* While the Orsimer believe many gods exist, they only venerate one.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> Malacath, whom they also call **Mauloch**, the **Orc-Father**<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-MOF-49)</sup> the **Great Chief**, the **First Orc**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> the **Daedric Lord of the Orcs**, the keeper and **Lord of the Sworn Oath and the Bloody Curse**,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Sharamph-54)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-OSGD-55)</sup> and **Ashpit's Lord**.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-RSH-56)</sup> Curiously, "Mauloch" is seen by some as an [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md) like Trinimac, whereas "Malacath" is a Daedra.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DWAAPOT-16)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONObsidian-51)</sup> While some consider the Aedric Mauloch to be a different being altogether, others believe that Mauloch and Malacath are one and the same, a Pariah God who has been rejected by both the Aedra and Daedra.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ONAbamath-57)</sup> Orcs that live in the strongholds choose to live by the [Code of Mauloch](Lore_Code_of_Malacath.md) (or Malacath), a set of unwritten rules which instructs them on how to deal with matters like honor and vengeance, but also covers daily life.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCOM-14)</sup>

However, there are some Orcs who continue to venerate [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md). Some Trinimac cultists believe the Aedra fooled [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), and was not actually transformed by his ordeal, and instead absorbed a portion of Boethiah's power and gave it to his followers, thus making the Orsimer "improved Elves."<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> Others instead believe that Malacath is an agent of Boethiah who has somehow imprisoned Trinimac.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-BVTH-50)</sup> The latter cultists believe that Malacath and Trinimac are actually wholly separate entities, and that Malacath lies to keep them as pariahs under his thumb.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3O-5)</sup> Some of the Orcs who revered Trinimac and rejected Malacath, believed that Malacath's traditions were stifling and had caused the Orcs to lose their way.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Alga-58)</sup> Others thought that the view many Orcs had of Malacath as their protector was false, as Malacath's real passion was vengeance, and so belief in him held the Orcs back.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Solgra-59)</sup> Trinimac worship was known to be prevalent in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) when it was adopted by King [Kurog](Lore_Kurog.md) of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md),<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MAT-24)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Online-60)</sup> and was also advocated by King [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md) of Nova Orsinium following the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). Gortwog's policies on Malacath and Trinimac worship angered Orcs both within and outside of his territory, and some worried about the consequences of abandoning Malacath.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3O-5)</sup> While Gortwog's city would soon fall in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the belief in Trinimac's imprisonment would endure, though now considered extremely heretical by most Orcs. <sup>[[49]](#cite_note-BVTH-50)</sup>

Orcs that revere Malacath believe that the afterlife promises rewards of immortality, abundant food and drink, and constant battle deep within the Ashen Forge. They believe that, as the ultimate expression of the Orc stronghold, the Ashpit bastion stretches endlessly across the planes, extending even behind the [stars](Lore_Stars.md) to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) to grant access to the soul of every worthy Orc who crosses into the afterlife, and that within Malacath's stronghold, every Orc is a chief, every chief has a thousand wives, and every wife has a thousand slaves to cater to their every need.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Ot A-28)</sup>

Orcs that pass into the afterlife are believed to be thrust into the perpetually burning white-hot coals that are the very manifestation of the Code of Malacath, they are honed, tempered, and made whole in the cleansing flames of Malacath's hearth, said to burn hotter than the [sun](Lore_Sun.md), through a process called the ritual of tempering, which all Orcish [souls](Lore_Souls.md) bound for the Ashpit must undergo when entering the realm. The grudges and fury of these Orcs are believed to be heated and melted within the flames, in order to eventually be forged into the next generation of mortal Orcs. With their grudges returned to the living world, the immortal Orcs of the Ashpit are thus freed to create new grudges. Some of the most memorable of these new grievances are said to be folded and hardened on the Anvil of Ash to create weapons and armor of legendary stature.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-Ot A-28)</sup><sup>[[60]](#cite_note-ONThazeg-61)</sup> There is said to be no place for a gentle soul in Malacath's Ashen Forge, such souls are instead believed to be given to Trinimac, who watches over all those who have fallen and guides them to their final reward.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Bazrag-62)</sup>

Orcish cults to Malacath include the [Wrathful Flame](Lore_Wrathful_Flame.md), a religious order tasked with relighting Malacath's pyre in Old Orsinium every ten years,<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-ONN-63)</sup> and Malacath's Brutal Breed, a cult that arose after the fall of Orsinium and was dedicated to wiping out all [Bretons](Lore_Bretons.md) and [Redguards](Lore_Redguards.md) for all their slights, real or imagined, against the Orcs.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-64)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-load-Oathsworn_Pit.png/200px-ON-load-Oathsworn_Pit.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Oathsworn_Pit.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Oathsworn_Pit.png) The Oathsworn Pit [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-npc-Dulzush_Forgefire.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Dulzush_Forgefire.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dulzush_Forgefire.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Dulzush_Forgefire.jpg) Dulzush Forgefire, leader of the Oathsworn in 2E 582 The [Oathsworn](Lore_Oathsworn.md) are an Orcish warrior sect dedicated to Malacath. Operating out of the training grounds of the Oathsworn Pit in the [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), they are said to be among the most fearsome warriors in all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and masters of multiple forms of combat. The Oathsworn have been said to attribute all comfort to their god, and to use the fiery heart of their sacred home to light their forges. Oathsworn do not reside solely in the Oathsworn Pit itself. Being among the greatest warriors in Tamriel, they seek out real enemies to test themselves against. Orcish clans are forbidden from attacking the Oathsworn, as doing so goes against the code. For a long time, the Oathsworn had been tasked with guarding the [Staff of Many Paths](Lore_Staff_of_Many_Paths.md), an artifact of [Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md), until its theft during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md)<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Pit-65)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Bazrag-62)</sup><sup>[[65]](#cite_note-Faenalir-66)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-Dulzush-67)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Strife-68)</sup>

Some of Malacath's Orc followers attribute a number of teachings to him. Members of this Cult of Malacath claim that Malacath considers honorable combat to be hand to hand and chest to chest. They also believe that, to Malacath, a bow is the weapon of a hunter, not a warrior, and to attack from a distance does not honor him. Malacath is claimed to warn against enemies speaking fair before they act foul, and to expect his followers to keep going even in harsh conditions. The Cult of Malacath believes that betrayal is inevitable and so they must always be looking askance. Lastly, their god is said to call for a layered defense and for arms and armor crafted to the highest standards. They decorate their armor and weapons with various symbols thought to be sacred and to represent protective properties.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CMMS-10)</sup>

#### [The Code of Mauloch](Lore_Code_of_Malacath.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8a/ON-quest-Flames_of_Forge_and_Fallen_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Flames_of_Forge_and_Fallen_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Flames_of_Forge_and_Fallen_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Flames_of_Forge_and_Fallen_04.jpg) A carving of Malacath at his shrine in Old Orsinium The Code of Mauloch contains many simple precepts, which are mostly tacit<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> and include prohibitions on theft, murder, and assault, though ample exceptions are made to these rules.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCOM-14)</sup> More explicitly, the Code encourages respect for forging and blacksmithing, requiring vengeance for insulted honor, and recognizing that death in combat is pleasing to Mauloch.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> Orcs believe if something is not worth fighting for, it is beneath the Code.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCOM-14)</sup>

The Code sets rules for how to select a stronghold's chief through challenge and combat, and the "traditional roles" of the chief and his wives.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> The Chief is normally the strongest male, and it is he who makes decisions for the clan and decides when the Code of Mauloch has been satisfied. Nearly all the women of the stronghold are the Chief's wives or daughters; the exception being the stronghold's wise woman, who handles healing and spiritual matters. Disputes are settled through short, violent fights. Orcs who displease the Chief are normally banished from the stronghold, being forced to live among the other races.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCOM-14)</sup>

Orc strongholds have no jails.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCOM-14)</sup> If someone commits a crime, the penalty is the "Blood Price", to be paid to the victim or their surviving relatives.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOFTO-12)</sup> This involves payment in goods as restitution, or bleeding "enough" to satisfy the victim that punishment has been met.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCOM-14)</sup>

### Nords
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Snake_Mural.jpg) A depiction of Orkey The traditional [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) belief system contains two deities that are seen as being connected to Malacath. The first is **Mauloch**, who is known by the Nords as the **God of Orcs** and the "**Mountain Fart**". He is clearly identified with Malacath, and tests the Nords through warfare. He is considered a testing god.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-VOFTN-86)</sup>

The other is **Orkey**, also called **Old Knocker**, who is the god of mortality.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup><sup>[[84]](#cite_note-VOFTN-86)</sup> [Orkey](Lore_Orkey.md) is a primal [Atmoran](Lore_Atmora.md) death god,<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TAX-87)</sup> a "loan-god" whose worship stems from the days when the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) ruled Atmora.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-VOFTN-86)</sup> He is said to be a fusion of aspects of Mauloch and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md),<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-VOFTN-86)</sup> though others suggest that Arkay is a fusion of Orkey and the Aldmeri god [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes.md).<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TAX-87)</sup> Whatever the case, Orkey is also the Nordic god of the dead.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-TAX-87)</sup> His priests oversee the Halls of the Dead of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and ensure that the remains of the departed are properly consecrated and cared for.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-ONHall-88)</sup> The earliest Atmoran beliefs involved the worship of totemic animals, which evolved into the Nordic pantheon.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-DATN-89)</sup> Orkey's animal totem is the snake.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-TSOG-90)</sup>

Numerous Nordic myths involve the Old Knocker; it is said he has attempted to "ruin" the Nords since the days of Atmora,<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-FSOKW-91)</sup> and his battles with [Ysmir Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) are "legendary".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TTNOO-13)</sup> The Nords believe that Orkey "stole their years away".<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-FSOKW-91)</sup> It is said that the ancient Atmorans were once as long-lived as Elves, until Orkey, through "heathen trickery", fooled the Atmorans into a bargain that "bound them to the count of winters."<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-VOFTN-86)</sup>

Another tale says that during the reign of King Wulfharth, Orkey summoned the ghost of [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md), and the Nords were "eaten down to six years old" (some interpretations say their lifespans were simply reduced to six years).<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-VOFTN-86)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-FSOKW-91)</sup> Wulfharth asked for [Shor](Lore_Shor.md)'s help, and his ghost fought Alduin's ghost on the spirit plane.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-FSOKW-91)</sup> Shor defeated the Time-Eater, then removed the curse from the Nords and "[threw] most of it onto the nearby Orcs", Orkey's folk, and they were "ruined".<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-VOFTN-86)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-FSOKW-91)</sup>

Nords keep small statues of testing gods in their households and try to keep them appeased.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Contraband N-92)</sup>

### Redguards
**Malooc, the Horde King**, is an enemy god of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). Some scholars believe Malooc is merely Malacath in disguise, as they appear "similarly boorish" and share many "graceless aspects".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-IEGE-81)</sup> These beliefs caused tensions within [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), as some clerics began to question their alliance with the Orcs as part of the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DWAAPOT-16)</sup>

Malooc was first encountered by the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup> The Warrior Wave reportedly encountered an immense horde of [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) who revered Malooc as their deity.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-IEGE-81)</sup> It is said that Malooc retreated to the east when the army of the [Hoon Ding](Lore_HoonDing.md) routed the horde.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup> Hammerfell celebrates the [Festival of Blades](Lore_Festival_of_Blades.md) on the 26<sup>th</sup> of First Seed to commemorate this victory.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ON26-94)</sup>

Lending some credence to a connection between Malooc and Malacath, goblins in later history have been observed to worship Muluk, a "Blue God", thought to possibly be an aspect of Malacath.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LTC-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-BGJ-22)</sup> Totems of this Blue God are simply statues of Malacath painted blue.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Blades BG-20)</sup>

### Khajiit
The demon **Orkha** is known through ancient khajiiti texts that predate the Riddle'thar Epiphany, it is a demon sharing similarities to Malacath. It followed Boethra (the Khajiit interpretation of Boethiah) back through the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md), and spoke in curses of affliction and knew no other words. [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md), [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md), and Boethra battled the demon, but Orkha could only be banished and would not die. Khajiit understand that Orkha and others of his ilk serve as tests along the Path, and nothing more.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TAS-15)</sup>

## History

### First Era
During the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), Mauloch was long a thorn in the side of High King [Harald](Lore_Harald.md)'s descendants in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). Mauloch reportedly fled east after being defeated at the Battle of Dragon Wall, believed to have been fought around [660](Lore_First_Era.md#1E). It is said that Mauloch's rage "fill[ed] the sky with his sulphurous hatred". This event later came to be known as the [Year of Winter in Summer](Lore_Year_of_Winter_in_Summer.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup> a result of the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) in [1E 668](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_668).<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-PGE1M-95)</sup>

#### Giant Goblin Invasions of Hammerfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4b/ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png/200px-ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-hunding-Ansei%27s_Victory_02.png) Depiction of Frandar Hunding standing victorious over many Goblins At some point between [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) and [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700), when the [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) inhabited Hammerfell,<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-PGE3H-96)</sup> the giant Goblins invaded via their Goblin gate,<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Early-80)</sup> allegedly lead by their god, the Horde King, Malooc.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-VOF-3)</sup><sup>[[79]](#cite_note-IEGE-81)</sup> They possessed a huge army of fighters, and appeared in the middle of the province, where they would pillage and raze towns to the ground, driving the Dwarves out of the province.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-97)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-RGH-79)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Early-80)</sup>

Around [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780), the first wave of [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), led by [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md), departed from [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) and would eventually arrive in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Ritual oil was also carried in urns that bore the symbol of the Brutal Breed, with this oil being carried and used on the Brutal Bands by every cultist as a form of prayer and as a request for perseverance in the face of endless adversity. ### Helm of Oreyn Bearclaw
The [Helm of Oreyn Bearclaw](Lore_Helm_of_Oreyn_Bearclaw.md), also known as the **Helm of Kharag gro-Khar**, is a prized [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) with a history of conflicted attribution. The helm itself is an engraved skull, which is enchanted to improve the wearer's agility and endurance.

Those that claim it is a [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) artifact know it by the former name, and attribute it to the legendary [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) hunter of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Oreyn Bearclaw](Lore_Oreyn_Bearclaw.md) of the [Oreyn bloodline](Lore_Oreyn.md). Legends claim that Bearclaw single-handedly killed Glenhwyfaunva, the witch-serpent of the Elven wood, and thus brought peace to his clan. He would bring the helm and his name further reverence by performing many more great deeds, until he lost his life to the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md). After Bearclaw's demise, his helm stood as a monument of his stature, although it was eventually lost after his clan split.

Those that call the helm by its latter name believe that Oreyn was falsely credited, and that the feats were actually performed by his [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) friend, Kharag gro-Khar, hero of the [Shatul Clan](Lore_Shatul_Clan.md). Thus they consider it an Orcish artifact, and the relic has been claimed by Malacath himself. Those that believe the former story however, state that Kharag stole both Oreyn's helm and reputation, and thus both camps have tried to claim it.

### Saviour's Hide
[Savior's Hide](Lore_Savior%27s_Hide.md), also known as **Scourge of the Oathbreaker**, and **Hircine's Hide**, is a Daedric [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) commonly associated with [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). Savior's Hide once referred to a full set of armor, known as the **Armor of the Savior's Hide** (boots, cuirass, gauntlets, greaves, helmet, and pauldrons). Over time, the term Savior's Hide would become predominately used to refer to the **Cuirass of the Savior's Hide**, as the rest of the armor set have not been seen since [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399). The Cuirass makes the wearer resistant to [magic](Lore_Magic.md).

There are three creation stories to the Hide, with two crediting Hircine and the third crediting Malacath. The more widely known tale involving Hircine holds that the Daedric Prince rewarded the first mortal to escape his [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md) with his peeled Hide. The mortal then had the Hide tailored into the Cuirass for use in their adventures. This version of the story also claims that the Cuirass gave the wearer a resistance to magic. Another version claims that it was Hircine himself who sewed the Savior's hide from the hide of a [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md).

The third tradition which credits Malacath is lesser known and contains an inaccuracy, stating that it made the wearer *vulnerable* to magic. It claims that the Hide is a "captive servant of Malacath". Despite the conflict between the stories, they agree on the points that the Hide would protect from the blows of an oathbreaker, and would protect the wearer from the sting of the [Spear of Bitter Mercy](Lore_Spear_of_Bitter_Mercy.md).

### Scourge
[Scourge](Lore_Scourge.md) (also known as **Mackkan's Hammer**, **Bane of Daedra**, the **Daedric Scourge**, or **Scourge, Blessed of Malacath**) is a legendary [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). It was forged from sacred [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) in the Fires (or Fountains) of Fickledire, and is associated with Malacath. It is a fierce [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md), and takes the form of a steel or ebony mace. Malacath dedicated it to mortals, and any [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) who attempts to invoke its power will be sucked into a voidhole and banished to the [Outer Darkness](Lore_Void.md) of [Oblivion's](Lore_Oblivion.md) voidstreams. It also has the ability to banish them to Oblivion with a single blow, and can summon Daedra such as [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) and [Scamps](Lore_Scamp.md) from Oblivion to do the wielder's bidding. The summons are said to be those that it has previously "killed", and its victims are said to be poisoned. It has been described as a "bold defender of the friendless", which could be related to Malacath's role as the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of outcasts.

### Volendrung
[Volendrung](Lore_Volendrung.md), also known as the **Hammer of Might**, is an ancient [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by the [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) [Rourken](Lore_Rourken.md) clan. ### Runestone of Malacath
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Runestone_01.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Runestone_01.png) The Runestone of Malacath The [Runestone of Malacath](Lore_Runestone_of_Malacath.md) was a runestone named after the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Malacath, and said to pulse with dark energy. It was the focus of a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) ritual called the Ritual of Unbinding, said to be capable of severing an entity's ties, mystical and magical, to release them from all links to mortal and immortal realms.

Once invoked, the runestone would channel energy into one of the binding stones used for the ritual. For most beings, completion of the ritual required two binding stones, more powerful beings required three, and especially powerful beings required four. The binding stones were named for Essence, Magicka, Spirit, and Memory. The Runestone could also be invoked to create a key that could than be used on the binding stones to revoke the energies within and break the connection. Though the rite was normally fatal for the bound being, it was possible for a willing sacrifice to offer up their life to energize the key instead through the Ritual of the Proxy, thus energizing the key with their own soul, and allowing for the bound being's survival. If the ritual was interrupted, the severed links could be restored. Conversely, if the binding stones were energized once more, the ritual could resume.

The Ritual of Unbinding was used by the [Drublog Clan](Lore_Drublog_Clan.md) of [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), in an attempt to sever the mystical ties between the [Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) and the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), as part of the plans of the [Hound](Lore_Ulthorn.md). However, the timely intervention of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and the sacrifice of a willing [soul](Lore_Souls.md) through the Ritual of the Proxy, allowed for the Silvenar to be released and his links to the Bosmer to be restored.

## The Ashpit
[Ashpit](Lore_Ashpit.md) is a realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) ruled over by Malacath, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Outcasts. The exact manner and time in which the realm came to be, and whether and how it might be tied to the commonly believed transformation of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md) into Malacath, are not known. Orcish spellwrights call for boons from the Ashpit and rarely any other realms. That includes conjuring [Flame](Lore_Flame_Atronach.md) [Atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md). The Ashpit is hard to access, as the pathways to his domain take on a characteristic level of concealment to protect the disenfranchised and cast-out.

The realm mostly consists only of dust, palaces of smoke, and vaporous creatures; anguish, betrayal, and broken promises like ash fill the bitter air. Few mortals manage to reach the realm, where levitation and magical breathing are necessary to survive. The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) have been known to bottle the realm's thick, roiling vapor.

The Spine of Ashpit is a surprisingly light skeletal spine found in the realm. It is made from a grey dust, and fragments of bone have been known to be taken from it and brought to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) claims that the spine is the metaphorical "backbone" of the realm, which he looks down upon.

However, some areas of the realm are safe for mortals. In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), Malacath chose to bring [Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City/Sul) and [Prince Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md) to the Ashpit, where they found a garden of slender trees, and "vines festooned with lilylike flowers" wound about the trunks; a "multitude of spheres moved, deep in the colorless sky, as distant and pale as moons". Occasionally the vines "collapsed and formed into a cloud of black moths that swarmed about them". This garden seems to have some emotional significance to Malacath, who describes it as a "shadow of a garden", and an "echo of something that once was".

## Notes
- The following information extends the « [Malacath Cult Shapeshifters](Lore_Malacath.md#Malacath_Cult_Shapeshifters) » section and is taken from the official *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)* source, however, it contains apparent anachronistic lore contradictions, so it is unknown if the event or its seemingly anachronistic elements are considered canon; the complete story plot features [Henrik Seven-Swords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Henrik_Seven-Swords)<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-Seven-124)</sup> and [Owyn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Owyn)<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Inquiry-71)</sup>, characters who are considered unlikely to have lived at the same time period:

- - At some point during the reign of the [Dynasty of Odar the Brave](Lore_Dynasty_of_Odar_the_Brave.md), a group of shapeshifting cultists of Malacath infiltrated the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) Arena by posing as gladiators, competing in the ongoing Dragon Games. After an investigation at the behest of an Arena Blademaster, Owyn, the identity of the shapeshifters as cultists of Malacath was uncovered, and the information was relayed to the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). The cultists ultimately used the Dragon Games as cover to launch an assault backed by a force of shapeshifters against the Imperial City itself, setting the capital ablaze. However, alerted by the Capital Blademaster, the ruling monarch of Odar kingdom ordered their forces to launch coordinated counterattack, defeating the cult's leaders and routing their forces, ending the assault.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Glory-70)</sup><sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Inquiry-71)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Mayhem-72)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Rulings Glory-73)</sup>

- [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) claims that Malacath is "not popular at parties".<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-SISheogorath-125)</sup>
- Malacath is said to have once plucked the Mad God's Lost Button from Sheogorath's doublet. Though unassuming, it is said the button might hold great power, and the followers of Malacath consider it an abomination.<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-Alvur-126)</sup>
- In [Lord of Souls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Lord_of_Souls) Malacath's presence is described as being painful by [Attrebus Mede](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md). It is said that his gaze is "eldritch", his eyes look empty but, when Attrebus looks at them, "crooked things" than "shimmer into his mind and try to eat his thoughts", and when Attrebus meets his gaze directly, it feels as if "hot brass is being poured in his skull". Malacath's voice too is described as akin to beams of wood rending.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-LOS-42)</sup>
- Some sources describe Mauloch as having some sort of connection or similarity to the god [Z'en](Lore_Z%27en.md). It is said that both gods demand a "blood price" and "payment in kind" and thus both demand retribution against the other. It is also said that Zen's presence fades from the world as that of Mauloch grows stronger.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-ONSpinner-127)</sup><sup>[[126]](#cite_note-ONZ.27en-128)</sup>
- One source attributes Malacath with the creation of [Trolls](Lore_Trolls.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MATR-6)</sup> and indeed the Daedric Prince [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) has referred to Mauloch as a "troll-herder".<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-ESOBoethiah-129)</sup>
- The manifestation of Boethiah that spoke to the Vestige in Abamath called Mauloch a *"troll-herder"*, and claimed that it would amuse her to see his plans to invade Nirn foiled due to their shared history.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-ESOBoethiah-129)</sup>
- While Mauloch has always appeared as a very uncommon name for Malacath in literature (it only appears in books in *Morrowind* and *Oblivion*, never in dialogue), it appears that Skyrim originally wanted to alter this, as many of its early lines for Orcs refer to the former name, rather than the latter, although this would get altered by the time of release. Mauloch wouldn't become a commonly-used name for Malacath until *The Elder Scrolls: Online*.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-DV-130)</sup>
- The [world](Lore_Goblin_Gate.md) the [Giant Goblins](Lore_Giant_Goblin.md) came from has been described as another dimension. As the giant goblins worshipped and were led by Malooc, the world they came from might have had some connection to Malacath's realm, the Ashpit.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-RGH-79)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Early-80)</sup>
