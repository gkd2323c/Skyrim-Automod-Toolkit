# House Hlaalu

| --- |

[![MW-banner-House Hlaalu.png](https://images.uesp.net/e/e2/MW-banner-House_Hlaalu.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-House_Hlaalu.png) *In the great wind of progress, tradition cannot stand. Grasp fortune by the forelocks. When you see your chances, seize them. When you see a chance to turn a profit, take it. But do not follow money blindly. There is value in reputation, more than many young Hlaalu realize. This value must be carefully balanced against the more tangible coins in any deal. Theft and murder are bad for business. You can steal from someone, but will he trade with you after that? You can't bargain with a dead man.*
— *[Grasping Fortune](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grasping_Fortune)* House Hlaalu (also called **Clan Hlaalu**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> the **Yellow Party of Hlaalu**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Generic Dialogue S-2)</sup> or "Yellow Hats",<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup> and formerly **Great House Hlaalu**) was one of the five traditional [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) of the settled [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-YHH-4)</sup> It governed the Hlaalu District of western-central [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) from its ancient capital of [Narsis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Narsis),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> ruled by a leader known as the High Hlaalu.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Po M-5)</sup> In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Hlaalu's presence on the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) was limited to the port town of [Seyda Neen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seyda_Neen).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) the house had expanded exponentially, with its council seat located in the ex-Redoran city of [Balmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balmora), and other Hlaalu-run towns including [Suran](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Suran) (also a former Redoran possession), [Hla Oad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hla_Oad) and [Gnaar Mok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gnaar_Mok). On the mainland, the formerly Dres cities of [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart) and [Kragenmoor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kragenmoor) were obtained by Hlaalu.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-conceptmap-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-CCL-8)</sup> Early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), soon after the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) released Morrowind, anti- [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) sentiment and House Hlaalu's association with the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) led to the stripping of its rank as a Great House, and House Hlaalu was removed from the Grand Council of Morrowind.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DBArano-9)</sup> Their position on the council was filled by [House Sadras](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Sadras).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-10)</sup>

House Hlaalu could be characterized as adaptable and opportunistic, and any morals they might have had came second to *business*. The House was largely concerned with business and diplomacy, seeking to strengthen ties with at times unconventional allies such as the Empire, and although profit was often a primary objective, traditional Hlaalu practice recognized the value of a good reputation over money.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GF-11)</sup> This was their great strength - they were fast talkers and intelligent traders - and their great liability; most Hlaalu were bribable, either with gold or with other favors, with their leaders as no exception. House Hlaalu retainers were also masters of thievery, sneaking, lock-picking, blackmailing and backstabbing, both literal and metaphorical.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UHH-12)</sup> While some councilors were honest and fair, others were more under-handed and corrupt. However, House Hlaalu's most distinguishing characteristic was its willingness to live in harmony with the other races, setting it apart from the other, occasionally xenophobic, Dunmer Great Houses.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GHo M-13)</sup>

House Hlaalu maintained oftentimes tumultuous relationships with the other factions of Morrowind, prompting their eventual fall from grace. Though not as favored as [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril) or [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) retained an overall positive relationship with House Hlaalu.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Friends Foes-14)</sup> They had less friendly relationships with the rest of the Great Houses, with animosity - and more rarely, outright hostility - existing between themselves and the Indoril,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OM-15)</sup> [Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Wo TH-16)</sup> [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md), and Redoran<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Friends Foes-14)</sup> throughout history; though they were formally allied with the latter on at least one occasion.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HRA-17)</sup> Like the rest of the Great Houses, Hlaalu relations with the [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders) tribes were strained, and loathing existed for [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and [Sixth House cultists](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Dagoth).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Friends Foes-14)</sup> Of particular note was their relationship with House Indoril, with the longstanding animosity between the Great Houses being particularly noted by Imperial observers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> In the Third Era, the Hlaalu retained a positive relationship with the [Camonna Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Camonna_Tong) crime syndicate, and most of the Imperial guilds; including the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md), and [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). They were less approving of the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Friends Foes-14)</sup>

House Hlaalu adopted the merchant's scale as their heraldry, referencing the source of their power and their pre-eminence in mercantile affairs.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Motif-18)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PSII-19)</sup> They likewise adopted the color yellow as a designation.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GHo M-13)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-YB-20)</sup> Their emblem was commonly referred to as the "Golden Scales", and was frequently present on various items associated with the house.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-HGPW-21)</sup> Saint [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) the Pilgrim was the patron saint of House Hlaalu.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MDGH-22)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/ON-misc-Banner Image Hlaalu.png/290px-ON-misc-Banner Image Hlaalu.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Banner Image Hlaalu.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Banner Image Hlaalu.png) The House Hlaalu *Stand proud, kin of Hlaalu! You belong to one of the original Great Houses of the civilized Dunmer. From our capital in the city of Narsis, our influence extends throughout the central region of Morrowind. Some call us opportunistic like it's a curse. But as Grandmaster Hesleth always implored, "The Dunmer who ignores opportunity deserves every failure that comes his way."* — *[Understanding House Hlaalu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Understanding_House_Hlaalu)* *House Hlaalu represents the future of the Dunmer, integrated into the sophisticated mainstream of the traditionless, raceless, godless culture of the Empire.*
—Vivec House Hlaalu was founded in ancient times, predating the rise of the Tribunal Temple and the transformation of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) into the Dunmer. Indeed, their capital of Narsis was one of the first settlements built by the followers of Veloth in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONLoad Narsis-23)</sup> The House has existed since the time of the [First Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Council).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-t Wot FC-24)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PSII-19)</sup> By the 121st year of the Golden Peace, their influence extended throughout the central region of Morrowind.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UHH-12)</sup>

Prior to the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md), thirty of the most influential and revered Chimer Clans worked together to build the fabled Library of Andule, a grand repository containing the genealogical records related to the earliest Velothi settlers and other assorted knowledge about the earliest history of the Great Houses.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Bradyn-25)</sup> Seven of the thirty Revered Families hailed from House Hlaalu; the [Arano](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arano), Arenim, [Nerano](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerano), [Norvayn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Norvayn), Tharys, Thelas, and Uveran Clans.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Tombs-26)</sup> The locations of the tombs were presumed lost in the aftermath of the War of the First Council, along with the Library of Andule and the records contained within.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Bradyn-25)</sup> The seven tombs were rediscovered in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), alongside the other twenty-three families and the Library of Andule itself.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Library Of Andule Quests-27)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ad/ON-place-Narsis.jpg/200px-ON-place-Narsis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Narsis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Narsis.jpg) Narsis, ancient capital of House Hlaalu According to Agrippa Fundilius, warriors of House Hlaalu fought and died at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) under the leadership of Hortator [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar) during the War of the First Council in [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-t Wot FC-24)</sup> During the decades in the aftermath of the war, at least one Hlaalu - Counselor Hlaalu-Nothoc - had a role in discussing the nascent Tribunal Temple's plans to reorganize the House lands of Morrowind.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PSVII-28)</sup> From the time of the House's founding, Hlaalu developed a focus towards the mercantile and diplomatic disciplines. With the motto of *"To trade fairly and freely is to honor the Three,"*<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MDGH-22)</sup> these combined cultural practices led to a proclivity towards bribery and corruption within the House. As noted by the House Hlaalu Grand Historian Hlaandu Hlaalu, "for every honest and fair councilor Hlaalu produces, there might be as many as four or five that are underhanded and corrupt". However, the historian also maintained this was not overly concerning to House Hlaalu, as pragmatism would avoid any unduly depths to this behavior.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-UHH-12)</sup>

As House Hlaalu evolved, the standard members were expected to follow suit. Members were expected to be fast and agile, and able to keep up with business and with the times. Being able to speak quickly and convincingly, as well as ability to trade with the best of merchants and make a profit were likewise expected. Learning to protect one's own property by securing it with hidden chests, locks, and even traps was also needed. When confrontation was unavoidable, Hlaalu warriors were taught to fight quickly in comfortable, light armors with short blades, or from a distance with a marksman's weapons.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GF-11)</sup>

Great House Hlaalu's role during the eighty-year [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) against the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) is unknown, but the aftermath of the conflict would prove a lucrative time for the House.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-HIP-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TOT1-30)</sup>

### Second Era
In the early years of the Second Era, during the period of history directly after the end of the Four-Score War, House Hlaalu was led by [Delmene Hlaalu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Delmene_Hlaalu). During his leadership the House amassed great wealth and prestige, exploiting the state of the northeastern [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) after the Four-Score War. This period was known as Hlaalu's age of gilded glory.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-HIP-29)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TOT1-30)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/01/LG-cardart-Hlaalu_Sharpshooter.png/200px-LG-cardart-Hlaalu_Sharpshooter.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Hlaalu_Sharpshooter.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Hlaalu_Sharpshooter.png) A Hlaalu Sharpshooter, as seen in *[Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)* In [2E 559](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_559), a dispute over a House Hlaalu trading post in contested territory erupted into a rare armed conflict known as the [War of Two Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:War_of_Two_Houses). The so-called "war" consisted of only one battle, fought by House Dres mercenaries and House Hlaalu guards at the trading post. Despite the Hlaalu being outnumbered five-to-one, the conflict ended with a decisive House Hlaalu victory when the leader of the Hlaalu guards, Purilla Falen, defended a portal that allowed a full cohort of Hlaalu battle-merchants to swarm the Dres mercenaries.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-WTH-31)</sup>

*Our economy is in peril. The Pact has brought us some measure of security, yes, but the cost has been dear. Dearer than I think your Redoran kinsmen are willing to admit. My trade ministers have gone to great lengths to solidify our holdings outside Morrowind. While the enemy has seized most of our extraterritorial property, we retain sizable assets in Imperial bourses and foreign business concerns. Extracting this wealth is no small task. It takes time, gold, and most importantly, connections. We can ill-afford to alienate what few friends we have outside the Pact.*
— *[Hlaalu Letter of Complaint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Letter_of_Complaint)* In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) army of [Kamal](Lore_Kamal_(race).md) under the leadership of [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal) invaded Morrowind from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) through [Fort Virak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fort_Virak). After a pitched [at Vivec's Antlers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battle_of_Vivec%27s_Antlers) in Stonefalls, the invaders were defeated with the assistance of [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) from Eastern Skyrim and [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) from [Shadowfen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowfen) and [Thornmarsh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thornmarsh).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-t SAI-32)</sup> In recognition of their mutural cooperation, the three groups signed an alliance in the city of Ebonheart that became known as the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-UA-33)</sup> Four of the Great Houses of Morrowind enlisted in the alliance, including House Hlaalu.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-PP:C-34)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Inf Vvard Factions-35)</sup> After the eruption of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), House Hlaalu's warriors would be among those that fought for control of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-PP:C-34)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Inf Vvard Factions-35)</sup>

House Hlaalu was known to support the Ebonheart Pact,<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Inf Vvard Factions-35)</sup> especially when it came to Pact unity - though this was said to be because the Hlaalu Grandmaster was smart enough to exploit the opportunities offered by the alliance.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup> House Hlaalu also honored the Pact's request to end the practice of slavery by its member parties, and actively enforced this by persecuting slavery in its territories.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-PP:C-34)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Seyda Neen Quests-36)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Salvi-37)</sup> Despite the outward enthusiasm for the alliance that many Hlaalu displayed, the Pact and Three Banner's War had an adverse effect on House Hlaalu's prosperity. According to the House Hlaalu Deputy Trade Viceroy Tirnur Nalyn, Morrowind's economy was imperiled by the Pact and the War. Hlaalu trade ministers went to great lengths to solidify their holdings outside Morrowind. While the enemy alliances seized most of their extraterritorial property, they retained sizable assets in Imperial bourses and foreign business concerns. Extracting this wealth proved difficult, and took time, gold, and connections.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-HLo C-38)</sup>

Some years prior to [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), House Hlaalu and House Redoran entered into a formal alliance.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HRA-17)</sup> While House Hlaalu deferred to House Redoran in matters of defense, House Redoran deferred to House Hlaalu in matters of commerce and diplomacy.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-HLo C-38)</sup> A product of this partnership was the construction of many Redoran holdings on the frontier island of Vvardenfell by the Hlaalu Construction Syndic, including the cities of Balmora and Suran. However, the Syndic violated the terms of the construction contract when they ignored the instructions for the settlements to be built in House Redoran's architectural style and instead built them in the Hlaalu style. Hlaalu furthermore used the opportunity to establish business relations with all sources of raw materials and provisions throughout Vvardenfell, and acquire a near-monopoly in these areas.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-HCS-39)</sup> These events, coupled with the belief that House Hlaalu coveted Balmora and Suran for themselves, led to relations between House Hlaalu and House Redoran becoming strained in spite of their alliance, with House Redoran officially marking House Hlaalu as a group operating against their best interests.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-HRA-17)</sup> Around the same time, the Hlaalu formed a trade deal with the [Gold Coast Trading Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gold_Coast_Trading_Company), and contracting them to build the port of [Seyda Neen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seyda_Neen).<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-40)</sup> Seyda Neen was then used to welcome visitors to Vvardenfell and provide access to the rest of the island.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Intro To Vvard-41)</sup> House Hlaalu also made contributions towards the construction of [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City) by donating gold from its vast treasury.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-42)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Councilor_Ralden.jpg) Councilor Ralden, House Minister of House Hlaalu in 2E 582 By [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), House Hlaalu was prominent in the [Deshaan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deshaan) region of southern Morrowind, but remained weak in the rest of Morrowind.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TGHa TU-3)</sup> That same year, the House faced trouble in their capital region around Narsis. The Maulborn Cult, posing as a philanthropic organization, released the [Llodos Plague](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Llodos_Plague) in the Hlaalu-ruled [Redolent Loam](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Redolent_Loam) region of Deshaan.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-QS-43)</sup> The disease spread to Narsis, and led to the lockdown of the city and the evacuation of much of the population. Unbeknownst to Councilor Ralden, House Minister and the ranking resident Hlaalu official at the time,<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-VD-44)</sup> his advisor Giron Sadri was a member of the Maulborn Cult and facilitating the spread of the plague in nearby [Lake Hlaalu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Hlaalu), and the kidnapping of Narsis citizens. The arrival of a [stranger](Lore_The_Vestige.md) and pair of [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) agents helped expose Giron and save Narsis, though the fate of Councilor Ralden and the abducted citizens in the aftermath remained unknown.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Narsis Quests-45)</sup> The Maulborn perpetrators were pursued east into the Obsidian Gorge, where Hlaalu enforcers and their allies put an end to the Llodos Plague production and the alchemist behind the disease, Merdyndril.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-Obs Gorg Quests-46)</sup> Elsewhere in Deshaan, Hlaalu guardsmen rooted out a hostile clan of [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) named the Oathbound in an area of the [Valus Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valus_Mountains) named Malak's Maw,<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-MMQuests-47)</sup> and attempted to establish trade relations with the [Mabrigash Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mabrigash_Tribe) of ashlanders in the Vale of the Ghost Snake.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-Vale Quests-48)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/ON-place-Seyda_Neen.jpg/200px-ON-place-Seyda_Neen.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Seyda_Neen.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Seyda_Neen.jpg) Seyda Neen as a Hlaalu port Later in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), House Hlaalu faced further troubles within their Vvardenfell holdings. In the port of Seyda Neen, illegal slavers under Captain Svadstar set up a base of operations on the nearby abandoned Redoran outpost on [Firemoth Island](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Firemoth_Island) with the assistance of the corrupt Governor Omellian. With the assistance of a Morag Tong agent and a taveler, Deputy Governor Deminah Salvi and the local Hlaalu guard shut down the operation - but it was unknown if the disgraced former Governor Omellian was brought to justice.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-BTt F-49)</sup> In Balmora, the brothers Malur and Benar Rethan quarreled over the Vassir-Didanat Mine and a scheme to produce false ebony via the murder of innocents. The scheme was ultimately exposed, and the mine's ownership passed to Hlaalu Councilor Rayveth, though it is unknown if the Councilor continued the Rethan brother's grisly scam.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-LBfa S-50)</sup> In the eastern regions of the island, Mistress [Dratha](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dratha) was poisoned by a Telvanni wizard named Savarak who bargained with House Hlaalu to depose the Magister. House Hlaalu coveted Dratha's territory, and Savarak desired Dratha's position as Magister. They infected Dratha's mushroom tower with infectious spores in an attempt to kill her, but their plot was stopped by Dratha's Mouth with the assistance of a traveler.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONRV-51)</sup>

In [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the Imperial Geographical Society reported House Hlaalu as being "the smallest and weakest of the five clans", noting that the Hlaalu nevertheless maintained limited commerce with the Empire, trading Imperial broadcloth and Cyrodilic brandy for the elegant crafts produced by the crafters of Morrowind.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup>

In the twilight years of the Second Era, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s legions turned their eyes towards Morrowind. Facing the threat of invasion, the Great Houses were divided in how to handle the situation. Uniquely, Hlaalu boldly proposed accommodating the Imperials rather than fighting them. They were alone in this however, with House Indoril, Dres and Redoran swearing to resist the western invaders to the death, and House Telvanni recusing themselves from the conflict altogether. House Redoran soon found itself manning the western borders of Morrowind alone, with House Indoril and House Dres preferring to retreat inwards to wage a guerrilla war. When [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) signed the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), House Hlaalu was the first to welcome the agreement. After the Armistice was signed, the ruling Lord High Councilor of Morrowind, a member of House Indoril who refused to accept the treaty, was assassinated, and replaced by a Hlaalu noble, toppling Indoril from its ancient pedestal as the leading Great House and propelling House Hlaalu to the top in its place. Furthermore, the newly-established Imperial role of the figurehead Sovereign of Morrowind was also filled by a Hlaalu; beginning with Queen [Barenziah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OM-15)</sup> For the next era, House Hlaalu would reign over the Grand Council of Morrowind at the Empire's behest.

### Third Era
*As a result of its close relationship with the Imperial administration, House Hlaalu has emerged as politically and economically dominant among the Great Houses of Vvardenfell and Morrowind. Hlaalu welcomes Imperial culture and law, Imperial Legions and bureaucracy, and Imperial freedom of trade and religion. Hlaalu still honors the old Dunmer ways -- the ancestors, the Temple, and the noble houses -- but has readily adapted to the rapid pace of change and progress in the Imperial provinces.* — *[Great Houses of Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses_of_Morrowind)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/MW-place-Hlaalu_Canton.jpg/200px-MW-place-Hlaalu_Canton.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Hlaalu_Canton.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Hlaalu_Canton.jpg) The Hlaalu Canton in Vivec City [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/24/MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg/200px-MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-map-Great_House_Influence.jpg) Areas of Vvardenfell controlled by House Hlaalu in 3E 427 (yellow areas) House Hlaalu entered the Third Era as the dominant local power in the Imperial province of Morrowind. Owing to its close relationship with the new administration, they became politically and economically dominant among the Great Houses. It is said that much of the Hlaalu's success as a Great House in the late Third Era is attributed to the earnings of one, Hlaalu Pasoroth, the great-grandfather of [Athyn Llethan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Athyn_Llethan).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-HT-52)</sup> Unlike the other Houses, the Hlaalu generally adapted rapidly to cultural changes - even welcoming them on some occasions.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GHo M-13)</sup> Alongside their meteoric rise in power, the House also saw significant expansions in territory both on the mainland and in Vvardenfell. In mainland Morrowind, House Hlaalu's territory expanded past Redolent Loam and grew to incorporate the cities of Ebonheart and Kragenmoor, who'd since lost their previous House Dres oversight.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-conceptmap-7)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-CCL-8)</sup> After Vvardenfell was opened for settlement in [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414), House Hlaalu's longstanding plans to seize Balmora and Suran from House Redoran for themselves came to fruition, and the Redoran were driven out of their former settlements. The smaller villages of Gnaar Mok, Hla Oad were built, and one of the cantons of the completed Vivec City was dedicated to Hlaalu retainers and kin.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Hlaalu Holdings-53)</sup> As a whole, Hlaalu managed to grab the lion's share of the best lands in the fertile [Ascadian Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ascadian_Isles), and also got awarded the charter to the rich ebony mines in [Caldera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caldera). Hlaalu's immediate move to colonize Vvardenfell granted them greater holdings than the more hesitant Redoran and Telvanni.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-KRP-54)</sup>

As of the late Third Era, Queen Barenziah had abdicated in favor of her Uncle, King Hlaalu [Athyn Llethan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Athyn_Llethan) and House Hlaalu was ruled by Grandmaster [Vedam Dren](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vedam_Dren), also the Duke of Vvardenfell.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-YB-20)</sup> The Duke's brother, Orvas Dren was the Kingpin of the Camonna Tong crime syndicate, resulting in the affairs of House Hlaalu and the Camonna Tong becoming inexorably intertwined.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-Orvas-55)</sup> Two House Councilors - Velanda Omani and Nevena Ules - became direct puppets of Orvas Dren and his way to exercise his will on the Hlaalu Council.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-Bero-56)</sup> Duke Dren's daughter, meanwhile, founded the [Twin Lamps](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Twin_Lamps) abolitionist movement.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Ilmeni-57)</sup> While Duke Vedam disapproved of his brother's activities he was largely powerless or unmotivated to stop them.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-Vedam-58)</sup> The High Hlaalu at this time was among the most powerful men in the province, and it had been many years since anyone dared set the Morag Tong onto Hlaalu kin.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Po M-5)</sup> Slavery was considered uncommon in Hlaalu districts by this time,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Brallion-59)</sup> but Hlaalu-owned slave plantations such as the Arvel and Dren plantations could still be found in Hlaalu territories.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Plantation-60)</sup>

In [3E 426](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_426), the Hlaalu council reported a reduction in the incidence of theft and violent crime in the Hlaalu House Districts, thanks to the vigilance of the Legions and stern sentences by the magistrates. The council also expressed concerns over high tax rates and the injurious effect of high tariffs on trade, and reported that a minor tax revolt in Balmora was suppressed without undue harm to life and property. Concerns were also raised regarding the disturbances of the public peace resulting from the increasingly aggressive competition between the Thieves Guild and the Camonna Tong for control of the black markets.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-YB-20)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/52/LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Duke_Vedam_Dren.jpg) Duke Vedam Dren, Grandmaster of House Hlaalu in 3E 427 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg/200px-TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:TR-npc-King_Hlaalu_Helseth.jpg) King Hlaalu Helseth House Hlaalu found itself embroiled in numerous conflicts in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427). House Redoran struggled to compete with House Hlaalu's dominance of the ebony trade in Vvardenfell,<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Hlaano-61)</sup> with the most obvious conflict centered on the town of [Caldera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Caldera), which was founded by the Imperial Caldera Mining Company with the support of House Hlaalu. House Redoran actively worked to find evidence of corruption in Caldera in an effort to shut the mines down<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-Caldera Mines-62)</sup> There was also bickering with House Telvanni concerning the seizing of large tracts of land,<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Telv Rumors-63)</sup> the founding of certain new settlements in the Ashlands such as Tel Uvirith,<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Uvirith-64)</sup> and the occupation of old Velothi Towers, such as Odirniran.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-MWOdirniran-65)</sup> Hlaalu agents also attempted to prevent the construction of Indarys Manor by House Redoran,<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Indarys-66)</sup> while both Redoran and Telvanni agents attempted to prevent the Hlaalu from building Rethan Manor on the Odai Plateau.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Rethan-67)</sup> Two Hlaalu noblemen, Meril Hlaano and Ondres Nerano, were confronted by Redoran agents for slander.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Slanders-68)</sup>

House Hlaalu played a major role during the fulfillment of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) prophecies and the series of events that saw the end of the [Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight), [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur), and the Tribunal. The Hlaalu Council formally recognized the Nerevarine as Hortator, after Orvas Dren and his puppets on the council were either persuaded or eliminated. It was rumored that Vedam Dren also stepped down as Grandmaster in favor of a rising star in House Hlaalu that year, making him the only one of the three Great House leaders on the island to survive the Nerevarine's rise to power.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-MWMQ-69)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-HHMQ-70)</sup> Later that year, King Hlaalu Athyn Llethan passed away under mysterious circumstances and was succeeded by his grand-nephew, [Hlaalu Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Helseth).<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-GDD-71)</sup> The young Hlaalu King's reign was initially challenged by various groups including the Tribunal Temple, but none were successful.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-KHHD-72)</sup>

By [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), House Hlaalu was said to still be on the rise as they embraced new traditions while welcoming the return of the old.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-PGE3-73)</sup> Count [Andel Indarys](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Andel_Indarys) of [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) was said to have gained his position as the only non-Imperial Count of Cyrodiil, allegedly due to his connections with House Hlaalu and its connections with the Empire.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Count I-74)</sup>

During [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), rumors in Cyrodiil suggested King Helseth had made an unprecedented alliance between House Hlaalu and House Dres, and abolished slavery. The alliance went on to "pick apart the carcass of ruined Indoril."<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-OBRumors-75)</sup> Later that year, the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) erupted across all of Tamriel and set events in motion that would see the downfall of the Great House.

Officials of House Redoran claim the Empire withdrew its Legions from Morrowind in the wake of the Oblivion Crisis,<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Arano-76)</sup> in spite of Imperial statements to the contrary near the end of the Crisis,<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Ocato-77)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-Caro-78)</sup> and the lack of legion support for the counties in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Allies-79)</sup> House Hlaalu, which owed its newfound strength to the Empire's support, became greatly reduced in power. The Dunmer had no standing army at the beginning of the Oblivion Crisis, and were left nearly defenseless as the armies of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) swept across the province. While House Redoran succeeded in creating an army, and House Telvanni was able to close some of the Oblivion Gates, House Hlaalu was powerless without the backing of the Empire.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DBArano-9)</sup> The events that transpired in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md)'s intervention ended the Crisis and created increased interest in the Imperial Cult among many Hlaalu citizens, but the damage to the political standing of House Hlaalu was done.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-CCL-8)</sup>

### Fourth Era
*Their collaboration with the Empire may have given them unrivaled political and economic strength, but their hearts weren't with the Dunmer people.*
—Adril Arano [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/SR-npc-Vendil_Severin.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Vendil_Severin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vendil_Severin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Vendil_Severin.jpg) Vendil Ulen, Hlaalu conspirator The events of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md), [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), brought further destruction to Morrowind, with all of the settlements on Vvardenfell being heavily damaged. Shortly after the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), Morrowind came under siege from the Argonians of Black Marsh, and was again saved by the army led by House Redoran. Due to their close ties with the Imperial administration, House Hlaalu became the scapegoat for all of the Dunmer people's suffering since the Oblivion Crisis. As a result, the greatly weakened House Hlaalu was unceremoniously stripped of its Great House status and dismissed from the Morrowind Grand Council.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DBArano-9)</sup> House Redoran became the eminent power in Morrowind, taking the position that Hlaalu had held throughout the Third Era. The Hlaalu believe that Redoran were instrumental in the reorganization of the council, and have held a grudge ever since.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-HRR-80)</sup> Indeed, Hlaalu refugees were still found living in some cities of eastern Skyrim two centuries later, such as [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) and [Riften](Lore_Riften.md).<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Refugees-81)</sup> House Hlaalu's seat on the Grand Council has been filled by the new Great House Sadras.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DBArano-9)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-10)</sup>

In [4E 95](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_95), the Redoran Guard uncovered a Hlaalu plot by Vilur Ulen to assassinate the Redoran Councilor [Lleril Morvayn](Lore_Lleril_Morvayn.md) of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md). The town, and the island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), were given to the Dunmer by the High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16) as a refuge following the events of the Red Year and were now administered by House Redoran.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-DBLoad-82)</sup> The coup was an attempt by Hlaalu to claim Solstheim from House Redoran, and to send a message to the Council that their new leaders were not as mighty as they purported to be. However, the conspirators were executed and the coup was quelled.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-HRR-80)</sup> A second attempt in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) by Vilur's son Vendil was foiled by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), resulting in Vendil being executed like his father before him.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-DBevents-83)</sup>

The remaining extent or holdings of House Hlaalu, if any, are unknown.

## Culture

### Architecture
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg/200px-MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg) Hlaalu houses and manors are often rectangular in style. Hlaalu public buildings -- tradehouses and craft guilds, manors and council halls -- are designed as simple multi-storied buildings roughly rectangular in plan, featuring arched entranceways and modest decorated exteriors. More modest one-story private dwellings follow the same plan, except with less decoration. Hlaalu plantation estates resemble Temple compounds, with walled precincts enclosing outbuildings for craftsmen and servants, dominated by a grand manor residence in place of a Temple shrine.

### Equipment
In the Second Era, warriors of House Hlaalu maintained strict standards for the arms and armor of our House guards and marines. Equipment had to meet a satisfactory degree of quality and appearance, and exemplify that dependability was expected from Hlaalu kin by their trading partners. Where possible, they adorned their items with the stylized merchant-scale sigil that symbolized the source of their power.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/55/ON-concept-Hlaalu.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Hlaalu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Hlaalu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Hlaalu.jpg) Examples of Hlaalu arms and armor during the Second Era Hlaalu belts were made of double-tanned guar hide, strong and thick, to support tassets, weapons, and money pouches. The buckle in front could display a Hlaalu House symbol or a personal family crest. Their boots were rather heavy, and though narrow below, they widened as they rose up the shins toward the knee. Toes were steel-shod except in the lightest armors; shin plating extended around the back of the leg to protect against hamstringing. Cuirasses were constructed of overlapping plates of traditional worked bonemold, or of metal plates cast to resemble bonemold. The House scales-symbol was prominently displayed on the chest and abdomen. Hlaalu gauntlets were of flexible leather over the fingers and hands, with the forearms and back of the hands protected by flaring plates of bonemold (or metal cast to resemble it). The leather at the fingertips had to be supple enough to not to impair the rapid counting of coins. Helms (and in light armor, hoods) covered the entire head, with a full face visor for protection against environmental dangers. They were of bonemold, or metal crafted to resemble it, bulbous in shape, and elongated toward the back of the skull to protect the traditional cranial coin stash. Cuisses and jambes were constructed of flaring bonemold (or bonemoldesque) plates, protecting the legs at front, sides, and rear. Knee poleyns may have displayed the House scale symbol or a personal family crest. Finally, pauldrons were constructed of overlapping triangular bonemold or bonemoldesque plates, flaring at the top to help protect the side of the neck. Their designs could be abstract, or inscribed with House symbols.

Hlaalu swords were long and straight and double-edged. They sported a distinctive curve-hooked tip design they shared with Hlaalu daggers. The quillions resembled broad wings that protected the hands of the wielders. The daggers were primarily parrying weapons, double-edged, broad and straight, with the same distinctive curve-hooked tip design of the swords. The wide crossguards resembled the wings of cliff striders, but were edged and pointed for backhand attacks. Axes had a single heavy curved blade, backed on the opposite side of the haft by a narrow spike for grabbing opponents or punching through their armor. For parrying purposes, most of the haft was sheathed in a cylinder of metal, and there was a heavy, pointed ferrule at the base. Maces were tipped with a trio of curved flanges, heavy for crushing and edged for slashing. As with the axes, mace hafts were sheathed in metal for most of their length to enable parrying, and footed with heavy, pointed ferrules. House bows were made of imported ash or yew wood, which was superior for bows to the native funguswoods of Morrowind. Quivers were guar leather, adorned with the House symbol, a long double-scales version. Hlaalu mage's spell [staves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staves) sported a finial in the shape of the House double-scales symbol, displaying the wielder's affiliation with Hlaalu. The foot was sheathed in metal, with a heavy ferrule, enabling it to serve as defensive weapon at need. The large, kite-shaped Hlaalu shields were traditionally crafted of bonemold, but were just as often made of metal plated over funguswood. The Hlaalu scale symbol was prominently displayed on the front.

## Notes
- Several settlements appeared in a pre-release concept map of Morrowind under the control of House Hlaalu. These Hlaalu-ruled settlements included Kragenmoor, Ebonheart, Narsis Hlaalu, [Othrenis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Othrenis), [Andrethis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Andrethis), [Heimlyn Keep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heimlyn_Keep), Old Keep, [Stonefalls](Lore_Stonefalls_(settlement).md), and [Riverbridge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Riverbridge).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-conceptmap-7)</sup>
- Hlaalu councilors host large events, such as the House Hlaalu Masquerade Ball.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-SSO-91)</sup>
- It is a tradition for Hlaalu nobles to embed glass gems from their ancient flagships in their foreheads, in memory of the glass of the wrecked ships used in the construction of Seyda Neen's Grand Pharos Lighthouse.<sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-MKP-92)</sup>
- The [Saraathu Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saraathu_Tong) was a faction that swore allegiance to House Hlaalu and used their expertise with portal magic to supply the House with trade goods from across the realms.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-VI-93)</sup>
- During the initial years of the Imperial occupation by Tiber Septim, the Empire reinstated the ancient title of "king," derived from early Chimer traditions, signifying a "high chief of the clans" akin to the High Elven High King. This replaced the role of the "military governor" during the initial years of the occupation. The king in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Hlaalu Athyn Llethan, descended from [Hlaalu Brevur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Brevur) the Betrayer, and along with his "court" was generally held in disdain by the native population.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Po M-5)</sup><sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-KRP-54)</sup><sup>[[UOL 9]](#cite_note-DGP-94)</sup>
