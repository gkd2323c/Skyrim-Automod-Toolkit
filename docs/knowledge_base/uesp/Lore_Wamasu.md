The Wamasu (or **Wamasus**;<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> also called **Swamp Dragons** colloquially)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIEGt T-2)</sup> are large reptilian beasts that hail from the province of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), though they also inhabit [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) and the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md). They are known for their lightning powers and tough exterior. Their existence was once largely shrouded in myth to anyone other than the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIEGt T-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup>

Wamasu bones are black and are said to carry electricity, which remains for several years and gives out a tingly feeling.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TSot B-3)</sup> They lay eggs that emit lightning as the embryo fertilizes and grows. The eggs are ready to hatch once the lightning is gone.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ONConcept-4)</sup> Adult male wamasu are known to cannibalize juveniles.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Perhaps the most famous wamasu is the legendary beast of [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md), the [Haynekhtnamet](Lore_Haynekhtnamet.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup> References to this beast date back several hundred years prior to [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). The locals would provide offerings, believing that it would cease the seasonal flooding.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BDJ-7)</sup>

## Uses and Culture
The wamasu provide bountiful resources that are considered valuable across Tamriel, precisely their sturdy hide and their luminescent organs, which can be used in making a variety of goods, such as tarps and lanterns.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONContraband1-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONContraband2-9)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONContraband3-10)</sup> Native tribes of Black Marsh such as the [Dead-Water Tribe](Lore_Dead-Water_Tribe.md) and the lizard-folk of [Mazzatun](Lore_Mazzatun.md) have [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) and armor inspired by the wamasu.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CM69-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CM45-12)</sup> Argonian wamasu handlers utilized prods insulated with [hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md) to guard against electrical discharges.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONContraband4-13)</sup>

Unsavory organizations have been known to procure these creatures or their eggs and keep them within dungeons.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> The [Scaled Court](Lore_Scaled_Court.md) used these creatures to create the [Mantikora](Lore_Mantikora.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SO-15)</sup> [Chimeras](Lore_Chimera.md) are artificial creatures with the heads of a [gryphon](Lore_Gryphon.md), [lion](Lore_Lion.md), and wamasu.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-FDTZ-16)</sup>

## Variants
Wamasu come in a variety of colors, such as green, purple, and brown. Amberdust Wamasu are a teal variety, but some speculate their color is unnatural, given to them by [druidic](Lore_Druids.md) magic.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup>

**Deadlands Wamasu** are a black, lava-covered variety of wamasu found in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) and sometimes sold as pets. Although they bear a superficial resemblance to the [nightmare animals](Lore_Nightmare_Animal.md) native to that realm, they are not [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Vendors who sell these creatures claim that they are true wamasu, taken from Shadowfen to the Deadlands. Their spines project fire instead of lightning, making them fierce defenders.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
