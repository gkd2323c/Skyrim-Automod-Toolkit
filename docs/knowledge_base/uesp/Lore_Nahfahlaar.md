*While my brothers were slain or locked away, I have been free. I know when to fight, when to fly, when to look for allies. And so I stand before you, nikrent. Unbroken.*
—Nahfahlaar Nahfahlaar (meaning *Fury-For-Water* in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md)),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIL-1)</sup> also known as **Nafaalilargus**<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Strongbox-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DUP-3)</sup> (sometimes spelled **Nafalilargus**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RGIntroduction-4)</sup> or **N'falilaargas**),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-RGCompanion-5)</sup> was a red [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) who often allied with mortals for his own protection. He acknowledged that dragons are bound by Fenjuntiid, the will of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), meaning that it is in a dragon's nature to pursue domination. However, he was willing to ally himself with select mortals in order to bide time and ensure his presence for events that may require his intervention.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-6)</sup> In more ancient times, he allied with the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) and Pride of Alkosh to defeat his archenemy, [Laatvulon](Lore_Laatvulon.md). He took a liking to [Ja'darri](Lore_Ja%27darri.md), a member of the Pride, being remembered in the legends of the moon priests as the **Red Beast**.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESONuziwa-7)</sup> He would later go on to serve as a powerful soldier of the Empire under Tiber Septim, before his physical body was eventually killed at the hands of [Cyrus the Restless](Lore_Cyrus.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RGIntroduction-4)</sup> Due to the immortal nature of dragon [souls](Lore_Souls.md#Dragon_Souls), Nahfahlaar isn't truly dead and could be raised again.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRArngeir-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-9)</sup>

## Biography

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_03.jpg/200px-ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tapestry-The_Dragonguard_03.jpg) An ancient Khajiiti tapestry depicting Nahfahlaar refusing to imbue the Mask of Alkosh for Ja'darri [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/CT-decoration-Dragon_Painting_%28Cropped%29.png/200px-CT-decoration-Dragon_Painting_%28Cropped%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Dragon_Painting_(Cropped).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-decoration-Dragon_Painting_(Cropped).png) Possible painting of Nahfahlaar created c. 16th century, First Era. Nahfahlaar's early history is shrouded in mystery, but it is clear that he made powerful enemies and allies among his fellow dragons. In particular, Laatvulon, the black beast, was his most ancient rival.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-6)</sup> It is possible he originates from [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), as it has been said to be the homeland of red and black Dragons.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1Cyro-11)</sup>

At some point before the foundation of the [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md), Nahfahlaar found [Yahgrondu](Lore_Yahgrondu.md). He was injured and cornered by a powerful Dragon hunter. Nahfahlaar offered his aid, but in exchange, he demanded that Yahgrondu repay his debt in the future. Yahgrondu was obligated to repay his debt through sosmir, the blood allegiance, and vowed to assist Nahfahlaar in his hour of need.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-6)</sup>

To defend himself from Laatvulon, Nahfahlaar forged an alliance with the Dragonguard, despite the group's history of animosity towards dragons. Nahfahlaar granted his horn as a boon to one among the Dragonguard's ranks, the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) heroine known as Ja'darri. His horn would eventually become known as the [Horn of Ja'darri](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horn_of_Ja%27darri) and was recognized as one of the most powerful of [Dragonhorns](Lore_Dragonhorn.md), tools that emit a sound capable of incapacitating dragons. The Dragonguard managed to seal Laatvulon away, but at the cost of Ja'darri's life, leaving Nahfahlaar with feelings of guilt over her death. Prior to her death, Ja'darri asked Nahfahlaar to empower the [Mask of Alkosh](Lore_Mask_of_Alkosh.md), but he refused. Thus, this powerful tool was rendered unusable.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-6)</sup>

Over the years, Nahfahlaar would continue to forge alliances with mortals who could protect him from death, but the Dragonguard no longer considered him an ally. After Ja'darri, his last known mortal protector was King [Casimir II](Lore_Casimir_II.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md). The Dragonguard learned of this and successfully ended the alliance in [2E 369](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_369), but Nahfahlaar managed to escape. Following this event, his location was unknown<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AOD-12)</sup> until over 200 years later in the Second Era.

### A Rage of Dragons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/ON-quest-New_Moon_Rising_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-New_Moon_Rising_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-New_Moon_Rising_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-New_Moon_Rising_04.jpg) Nahfahlaar and battlemage Abnur Tharn attempting to stop the Aeonstone overload [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/ON-quest-The_Horn_of_Ja%27darri_03.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Horn_of_Ja%27darri_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Horn_of_Ja%27darri_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Horn_of_Ja%27darri_03.jpg) The Horn of Ja'Darri, made from a dragon horn given willingly by Nahfahlaar During the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), the [Imperial Battlemage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Battlemage), inadvertently released the seal that had trapped a rage of dragons within the confines of the [Halls of Colossus](Lore_Halls_of_Colossus.md). This colossal mistake inspired Laatvulon and many other dragons to come out of hiding. Laatvulon sought to carry out his master [Kaalgrontiid](Lore_Kaalgrontiid.md)'s plan of bringing on the Dark Aeon, intending for Kaalgrontiid to become Akatosh's equal. To this end, Laatvulon created a [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) dragon cult, the New Moon Cult, whose ranks were bolstered by many of the vulnerable Khajiit from the region, such as those who were affected by the Knahaten Flu. Nahfahlaar dueled Laatvulon near the city of [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) in southern Elsweyr, but was overpowered due to Laatvulon's newfound power. Taking refuge in [Tideholm](Lore_Tideholm.md), he was encountered by the Dragonguard, having been recently reformed by the Redguard Blademaster [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md). Seeing that Nahfahlaar meant no harm, an alliance was forged once again with former foes and allies, one in which Nahfahlaar served as a valuable asset. This alliance led to victory, and Laatvulon, the Demon from the East, was slain.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-6)</sup> After this, he gifted the one he called "[Ahkahtuz](Lore_The_Vestige.md)" a blade made from his own talon.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOTalon-13)</sup>

Nahfahlaar subsequently aided the Dragonguard in defeating Kaalgrontiid and the remnants of the New Moon Cult. Kaalgrontiid planned to siphon the energy he obtained from [Jode's Core](Lore_Jode%27s_Core.md) through a giant cluster of [Aeonstone](Lore_Aeonstone.md), hoping that he would become empowered. To prevent anyone from disrupting his plans, he utilized the energy of the Aeonstone to create an aegis, a massive and impenetrable ward, around the ancient fortress of Dragonhold. Through the combined efforts of Nahfahlaar, the Dragonguard, and Queen [Khamira](Lore_Khamira.md)'s forces, Kaalgrontiid was slain. However, the Dark Aeon became unstable as a result of Kaalgrontiid's machinations and threatened to destroy the entire continent of Tamriel. It required the combined power of Abnur Tharn and Nahfahlaar to prevent the Aeonstone from affecting the area outside of Dragonhold. Nahfahlaar and Abnur were successful, but this resulted in the disappearance and possible death of Abnur. After putting a stop to the rage of dragons, Nahfahlaar sought out another place to wait until his intervention became necessary once again.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESONahfahlaar-6)</sup>

### The Battle of Hunding Bay
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/RG-scene-Introduction_25.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_25.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_25.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_25.jpg) Nafaalilargus at the Battle of Hunding Bay [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/RG-scene-Conversation_with_Nafaalilargus.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Conversation_with_Nafaalilargus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Conversation_with_Nafaalilargus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Conversation_with_Nafaalilargus.jpg) Cyrus facing Nafaalilargus in the catacombs *I am Tiber Septim's proud soldier, loyal vassal, a jewel of the crown. It's not my fault I have to keep company with the Governor.*
—Nafaalilargus Towards the end of the Second Era, Nahfahlaar, now choosing to style himself as Nafaalilargus, started working as a mercenary dragon in the employ of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) during his conquest of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). He was a proud soldier and a loyal vassal, causing him to be considered "a jewel of the Imperial crown".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RGNafaalilargus-14)</sup> During the [Battle of Hunding Bay](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md), the dragon was called in to help defeat the Crowns of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). He sank [Prince A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md)'s ship with a single fiery breath after the prince had been struck down by a poisoned arrow, thus ensuring that A'tor's wizard could not attempt to expunge the poison.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RGIntroduction-4)</sup> Nafaalilargus was then put under the command of [Lord Amiel Richton](Lore_Amiel_Richton.md), a man who had been named the provisional governor of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RGIntroduction-4)</sup> He was assigned to guard the soul gem of Prince A'tor, which was locked away in the treasuries beneath the island's palace. He coveted gold and was paid large amounts for his service.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RGNafaalilargus-14)</sup> The dragon was slain in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) by [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md), while confined to a tight space in the Stros M'Kai treasure vaults. Cyrus employed tactics of the Argonian villagers of [Noota Nara](Lore_Noota_Nara.md)<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> by dipping his sword in dragonfire and piercing Nafaalilargus' hide, dealing a killing blow.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Redguard-16)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GN-17)</sup> Cyrus then stole back the soul gem, and Richton was informed by [Dram](Lore_Dram.md) of the dragon's death.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Redguard-16)</sup> His death weakened the Imperial presence on the island considerably. However, due to the immortal nature of dragon [souls](Lore_Souls.md#Dragon_Souls), Nafaalilargus isn't truly dead and could be raised again.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SRArngeir-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SRPaarthurnax-9)</sup>

## Notes
- The name 'Nafaalilargus' has no known translation in the Dragon Language, however, the syllable "faal" translates to "the". This name also has more than the usual three syllables found in most other dragon names. "Nafaalilargus" may simply be a Cyrodilic, or Imperialized version of his real name, seeing as he served under the Empire during the events of *Redguard*.
- Along with [Paarthurnax](Lore_Paarthurnax.md), he was known as one of the few dragons who sought more than mere domination and destruction.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LADragons-18)</sup>
- Nahfahlaar is mentioned in the cut content of *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)*, where its stated that although its popularity faded as dragons began to disappear from Tamriel, a ballad concerning Tiber Septim and Nafaalilargus was still being recited in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-SRSven-19)</sup>
- Nahfahlaar is featured in *[The Sermon Zero Affair](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Sermon_Zero_Affair)*, a collection of the dev posts from the original thread which debuted Sermon Zero. He was believed to be played by [Douglas Goodall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Douglas_Goodall).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-S0A-20)</sup>
