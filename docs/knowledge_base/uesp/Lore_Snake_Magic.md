# Snake Magic

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/61/ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg/200px-ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Abecean_Sea.jpg) Sea Serpent attacking a ship (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Serpent_Caller_Vinsha.jpg) Serpent Caller Vinsha (ESO) Snake Magic is an arcane art connected to [snakes](Lore_Snake.md) such as [sea serpents](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md) and [reef vipers](Lore_Reef_Viper.md). A powerful form of snake magic was practiced by the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). They used it to tame the sea serpents of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) for use as steeds and war beasts.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PG-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PE-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CSSSC-3)</sup> King [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md) was believed to practice rituals that allow the Maormer to control sea serpents and use them to support their fleets.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FOTSV-4)</sup> Snake magic can be also used to warp snakes to make them more aggressive, alter their eggs,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PP-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DF-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSC-7)</sup> and charm snakes and serpents of all sizes. Practitioners of snake magic were known as **serpent-mages** and **snake-charmers**. They were known to use instruments such as the snake-charmer's pungi and double reedpipes as tools to command serpents. Such instruments were rare in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-CM-8)</sup>

According to the legends of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of the [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md) snake magic can also be used to manipulate the minds of [Men](Lore_Men.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md). According to their account, in the latter years of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) one group of Maormer used snake magic and ensorcelled locks when capturing slaves to ensure that they remained docile and compliant.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CS-9)</sup>

Sea serpents commanded by the Maormer were used in warfare during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). They were used during the Battle of [Seaside Sanctuary](Lore_Seaside_Sanctuary.md) against the [First Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#First_Dominion).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PE-2)</sup> According to legend, the Great [Sea Serpent](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md) terrorizing Serpent's Grotto was formed from the blood of a fish that pleaded for its life to the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), but was not spared. It came to terrorize the Bosmer until the clan decided to seal it away through the use of [blood magic](Lore_Blood_Magic.md), sacrificing three of the clan's volunteers into blood wells.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-St GS-10)</sup> The Maormer also attempted to break the wards binding a century old sea serpent in [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-St GS-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TSL-11)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Maormeri priestess of [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md) known as Serpent Caller [Vinsha](Lore_Vinsha.md) performed magical rituals on the snakes native to the hills of the [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md) east of All Flags Island. Her spells made them more aggressive and dangerous. Her activities alarmed local [Druids](Lore_Druids.md) of the [Stonelore Circle](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md) who in turn requested the retainers of the [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) to hire adventurers to stop her from meddling with local wildlife.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PP-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DF-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSC-7)</sup> They hoped to study the magic used to influence the eggs in order to cleanse them from the effects of Vinsha's rites.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DF-6)</sup> Ultimately she was slain and the eggs were delivered to the House Dufort broker.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TSC-7)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PP-5)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SCV-12)</sup>

In [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), during the millennial celebration of the founding of the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) Kingdom of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) Redguard snake-charmers were invited to entertain the attendees of the activities.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MOT-13)</sup>
