Senilius' Report **by [Senilias Cadiusus](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Senilias_Cadiusus)** *A report on an excavation of a Dwemer ruin*
---
[Nchuleftingth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nchuleftingth) Excavation Report 11

Our excavations have turned up little in these last few days. I have found nothing new in the ruins. Everything seems as clean and undisturbed as the day we first opened the outer doors. I know there are unexplored depths. I can feel massive [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) machinery still running beneath my feet, even as I write this, but I can find no way to get to it. You must not give up on us for a lack results this soon, Edwinna. I am sure a great discovery lies somewhere below.

If only the secret of [Passwall](Lore_Passwall_(spell).md) were not lost nearly a generation ago. With a few of those precious scrolls, I would be in the lower levels already where the real discoveries are to be made.

There is some good news. I have also discovered that "Mzuleft" you were looking for. Apparently "Mzuleft" is the proper name for the large Dwemer ruins south of [Dagon Fel](Lore_Dagon_Fel.md). Be careful not to confuse them with the ruins of "Nchardahrk" nearby.

The last of the Dwemer centurion spiders in the upper levels was finally disabled and dismantled for study. I wish we could study them while they are still active, but that is far too dangerous.

I also heard a strange story about the centurion spider that we captured and sent back to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The ship captain I hired wrote to me with an odd story. He said that the spider nearly broke through its cage several times while near [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), but once he left he *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), the centurion suddenly stopped working. What happened next shows that he had more wits than I gave him credit for. He ordered the ship turned about and as they approached Vvardenfell again, the centurion began moving just as suddenly. This is a curious phenomenon and certainly deserves more investigation.

Your servant,

Senilius *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* Cadiusus
