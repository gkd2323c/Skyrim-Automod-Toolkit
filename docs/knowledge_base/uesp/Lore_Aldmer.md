# Aldmer

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg/200px-ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-The_Lay_of_Firsthold.jpg) [High Lord Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md) leads his Aldmer ships to [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fc/OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg/200px-OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-statue-Torval_the_Pilot.jpg) Statue of [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) The Aldmer (meaning "First Folk" or "Elder Folk"), also known as **Alcharyai**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Po M-1)</sup> were the original proto- [mer](Lore_Mer.md) of the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), who first settled the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) before gradually spreading across much of mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup> According to legend, the Aldmer originated from a mythical lost continent known as [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md);<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> however, some sources, including allegedly the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) themselves, suggest this place is allegorical.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Their skin was said to possess a lustrous bronze hue,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup> and modern [Altmer (High Elves)](Lore_Altmer.md) are widely regarded as the closest living representation of the original Aldmer, both in physical appearance and in cultural tradition.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3Summer-6)</sup> [Altmer society](Lore_Altmer.md#Society_and_Religion) highly reveres Aldmeri ancestry, often striving to emulate their customs and ideals. Some sources even suggest that they engage in selective breeding to preserve Aldmer-like features.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Certain High Elf songs are still composed in [Old Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris_(language).md), the ancient language of their forebears.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The Aldmer are commonly regarded as the first technologically advanced civilization to emerge on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup> though some argue that this distinction belongs to the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md). Beastfolk ancestors such as the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) and [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) also inhabited Tamriel during this era, but their societies are generally described by historians as aboriginal and preliterate. Whatever the case, these populations were quickly displaced by the seemingly more sophisticated Aldmer.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup>

Few individual Aldmer are remembered by name, but a handful of notable figures endure in both legend and recorded history. Among them are [High Lord Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md), the first to settle the Summerset Isles; [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md), who charted much of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md); and [Orgnum](Lore_Orgnum.md), a former Aldmer who became the undying sorcerer-king of the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). The infamous necromancer [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), known as the King of Worms, once claimed he was not an Altmer, but an Aldmer.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-WS-9)</sup>

The Aldmer are said to have possessed a refined and chivalric high culture,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Po T-10)</sup> and are known to have developed their own system of musical notation—so intricate and esoteric that it remains difficult to interpret even in modern times.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> As the Aldmer spread across Tamriel, they gradually diverged, culturally and physiologically, into the various elven races known today. Some sources attribute the splintering of the Aldmer to their realization that different [Towers](Lore_Towers.md) with their own Stones could tell different "stories", to alter reality and shape their own domains. Thus the elves are said to have "self-refracted, each to their own creation", with every splinter group following their own Tower, the Bosmer following [Towers](Lore_Green-Sap.md), the Chimer following [Red-Heart](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), the Altmer [Crystal-Like-Law](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md), et alia.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Enigma-12)</sup> Those who remained in the Summerset Isles became the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup> The earliest migrants to the mainland settled in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), giving rise to the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup> [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) was colonized by both the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) and, later, the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md), precursors of the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BTAOM-2)</sup> A group claimed by some sources to have split off in Summerset and by others in Aldmeris itself, became the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TCPOA-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-PGE3.2FOL-16)</sup> High Rock was also home to the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md), a race of mixed heritage born from interbreeding between native [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) and Aldmeri settlers, such as the [Clan Direnni](Lore_Clan_Direnni.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> A number of myths also claim that the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) originated from followers of the elven ancestor spirit [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), who were transformed alongside their deity due to the intervention of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), while trying to halt the Velothi dissident movement.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TTNOC-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TFOT-20)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-VOFTO-21)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-TCO-22)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-23)</sup>

The term *Aldmeri* is sometimes used more broadly to refer to all elves,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup> and it is also used in reference to members of the modern [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md).<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup>
