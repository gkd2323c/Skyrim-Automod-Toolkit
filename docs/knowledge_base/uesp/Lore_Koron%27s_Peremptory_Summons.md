# Koron's Peremptory Summons

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/ON-crown_store-Flame_Atronach_Senche.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Flame_Atronach_Senche.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Flame_Atronach_Senche.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Flame_Atronach_Senche.jpg) An atronach summoned with the use of modified variant of Koron's Peremptory Summons Koron's Peremptory Summons is a [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) spell named after [Koron](Lore_Koron.md) used to bind [Atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAC-1)</sup> Variants of the spell were later perfected by [Corvus Direnni](Lore_Corvus_Direnni.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-WOT-2)</sup> The Direnni discovered that a conjurer could impose a desired form upon the summoned atronach. For centuries, Koron's Peremptory Summons was the standard spell for summoning these entities, prized for its flexibility in modifying both the duration of the binding and the atronach's form.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FAC-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CSS-3)</sup> Modified versions of Koron's spell were used to conjure a variety of Atronachs in forms more familiar to the people of Tamriel, including those adapted for use as mounts.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-FAS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SAG-5)</sup>

[Denogorath the Dread Archivist](Lore_Denogorath.md) also modified Koron's Peremptory Summons to conjure [Cold-Flame Atronachs](Lore_Cold-Flame_Atronach.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DD-6)</sup>

According to a potentially fictional tale about [Prince Hubalajad](Lore_Hubalajad.md), he removed the binding clause from Koron's Peremptory Summons and discovered that the altered spell could be used to bind forces other than a Daedra's will. He used it to immobilize people.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PH-7)</sup>
