# Rithana-di-Renada

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c4/ON-quest-Rithana-di-Renada.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Rithana-di-Renada.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Rithana-di-Renada.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Rithana-di-Renada.jpg) The Rithana-di-Renada The Rithana-di-Renada, or **Song of Kingdoms** (from [Ta'agra](Lore_Ta%27agra.md)), is an ancient stone mural of [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) make. It portrays the sixteen kingdoms of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), symbolic of the harmony, unity, and peace between them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Iraya TSOK-1)</sup> Its centerpiece, the unassuming Singing Crystal, emits a beautiful [melody](Lore_Music.md) known also as the *[Song of Kingdoms](Lore_Song_of_Kingdoms_(lyrics).md)* when placed in the mural.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSC-2)</sup>

## History
When [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md) lived as a mortal, he stole the Singing Crystal,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rajhin TSC-3)</sup> causing the musical aspect of the Rithana-di-Renada to fall into legend.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Iraya TSOK-1)</sup>

When the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) wrought strife among the sixteen kingdoms beginning in [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200), the sixteen kingdoms became disunited. Much like Elsweyr itself, the Rithana-di-Renada was shattered into sixteen pieces, each bearing the depiction of an individual kingdom. These pieces were scattered and lost.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Iraya TSOK-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-quest-The_Song_of_Kingdoms_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Song_of_Kingdoms_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Song_of_Kingdoms_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Song_of_Kingdoms_02.jpg) Izanji and Iraya arguing before the bare mural Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the curators of the House of Histories museum in [Riverhold](Lore_Riverhold.md) (Khajiit siblings Iraya and Izanji) were able to retrieve and restore all sixteen fragments of the Rithana-di-Renada. However, before they could set the pieces back into the wall, the pieces were stolen. Seemingly in homage to the Khajiiti legend *[Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md) and the Trail of Ink*, the thief left a clue at the scene of the crime for where the fragment bearing [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) could be found.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Iraya TSOK-1)</sup> [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) agreed to follow the clue and retrieve the Anequina fragment. Upon locating it, they were met with a Shadow of Rajhin, an avatar of the trickster god himself.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSOK-4)</sup> He explained that he had stolen and hidden the pieces of the Rithana-di-Renada. He gave the Vestige a book of riddles, which cryptically described where in Anequina each of the remaining fifteen pieces of the mural could be found.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Rajhin TSOK-5)</sup>

Eventually, the Vestige successfully returned each fragment to the House of Histories, placing them within the empty spaces in the wall.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TSOK-4)</sup> The Shadow of Rajhin appeared before them, revealing the existence of the Singing Crystal and its role in the legends. By this time, the [crystal](Lore_Crystals.md) was in the possession of a [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md) smuggler, unaware of its importance due to its lackluster appearance when apart from the Rithana-di-Renada.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Rajhin TSC-3)</sup> The Vestige then retrieved the Singing Crystal and restored the melody to the Rithana-di-Renada.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSC-2)</sup> A replica of the complete mural was given as a reward.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
