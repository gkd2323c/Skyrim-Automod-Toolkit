# Circle of Champions

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a5/BT-misc-Circle_of_Champions.png/200px-BT-misc-Circle_of_Champions.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-misc-Circle_of_Champions.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-misc-Circle_of_Champions.png) Circle of Champions Banner The Circle of Champions was a guild formed in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) to test the greatest fighters of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in games of martial skill.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Led by a Dunmer gladiator named [Battlemaster Rivyn](Lore_Battlemaster_Rivyn.md), the group would seek out dangerous locales for use as battlegrounds, and would reward fighters for taking part in various lethal games at these remote locations. Games were team-based, with participants being divided into three factions: the Fire Drakes, the Pit Daemons, and the Storm Lords.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO-2)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_teams)</sup> Many soldiers from the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) took part in these games, as they were encouraged to participate in Battlegrounds exercises with payments of scrip known as [Alliance Points](Lore_Alliance_Points.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

The various teams that compose the Circle of Champions have their own styles of armor. The Fire Drakes utilize [draconic](Lore_Dragon.md) symbolism in their armor. The Pit Daemons favored skull motifs, and the Storm Lords bore lightning and crown motifs on their armor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOTeam Styles-4)</sup> The teams are also known to wear armor resembling the [Ordinator](Lore_Ordinator.md)'s Order of War armor, something normally forbidden by the Order.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOGladiator-5)</sup> Furthermore, representatives of the teams have been known to sell the knowledge of crafting Ordinator armor to those that have earned the most honored of participants.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOGladiator Wares-6)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) the Circle of Champions had established camps in several of Tamriel's major cities. Known as Gladiator's Quarters, these camps were located outside the walls of [Alinor](Lore_Alinor_(city).md), [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md), [Gonfalon Bay](Lore_Gonfalon_Bay.md), [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md), [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md), [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), and [Vulkhel Guard](Lore_Vulkhel_Guard.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOQuarters-7)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/ON-concept-Battlegrounds_Banners.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Battlegrounds_Banners.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Battlegrounds_Banners.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Battlegrounds_Banners.jpg) From left to right: Fire Drake, Pit Daemon, and Storm Lord Banners The Circle of Champions first clashed with the Soldiers of the Void of [Deslandra](Lore_Deslandra.md), who tainted the Circle of Champions brand just prior to the start of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:52</sup> Due to the dangers posed by the Three Banners War, the Circle of Champions avoided central Cyrodiil, only staying to the outer parts of it such as Istirus Outpost along the [Strid River](Lore_Strid_River.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:25</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOLoading-9)</sup> Unsanctioned battles created by the cult were ran under the Circle of Champions brand in the Arcane University. Those that impressed the cult they attempted to recruit into their ranks.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:25,46</sup> The person responsible for these illegal matches was the traitor Big Bad Bazri,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:46</sup> a Khajiit known for her flawless win record within the Circle of Champions. The Circle tracked her down to [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), where she was found and slain.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-VWGazetteer-10)</sup><sup>:33,45</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b6/ON-concept-Elinhir_Arena_Lion_and_Battlegrounds_Weapons.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Elinhir_Arena_Lion_and_Battlegrounds_Weapons.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Elinhir_Arena_Lion_and_Battlegrounds_Weapons.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Elinhir_Arena_Lion_and_Battlegrounds_Weapons.jpg) Team Weaponry (Lion not included) The Soldiers of the Void subsequently infiltrated the Wayrest Throwdown fighting tournament hosted by the Circle of Champions.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:25</sup> [Eveli Sharp-Arrow](Lore_Eveli_Sharp-Arrow.md) or her ally Bodean of the Outer Watch entered the tournament with some adventurers, emerging victorious from the first round. However, rumors spread of tournament participants having their food spiked with [narcotics](Lore_Drugs.md). Eveli's team investigated the potential source of these incidents in [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:30</sup> finding it to have been robbed and its apothecary to have had an attempt on his life. The responsible party were hoping to win the tournament so they may be given the honor of being invited to [Castle Wayrest](Lore_Castle_Wayrest.md), which housed [High King Emeric](Lore_High_King_Emeric.md)'s reliquary that they wanted to steal.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:34</sup> Eveli and her team journeyed back to Wayrest and won the Wayrest Throwdown, and she used the opportunity to warn Emeric's retainers of the enemies seeking a powerful item in his possession.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:44</sup>

After the mining disaster at the mines of Mor Khazgur, opportunists made off with some of the mine's ore reserves and medical supplies that were donated to the sick and injured. To thank the residents for lending for lending Mor Khazgur for use in Circle of Champions matches, Battlemaster Rivyn had members of the organization go and recover the missing goods from the thieves responsible.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRGazetteer-12)</sup><sup>:37</sup> The trail led them to their near Blackreach, allowing them to recover the goods, but they continued onto Blackreach to find clues about whereabouts of the thieves' employer.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRGazetteer-12)</sup><sup>:43</sup> They discovered the thieves were in league with vampires from under Blackreach, and were stealing the stonemelt solution from the Mor Khazgur for nefarious uses. With the solution recovered, the Circle of Champions sent it back Chief Urzikh with the suggestion that it be destroyed.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SRGazetteer-12)</sup><sup>:54</sup>

An agent of the Circle of Champions also traveled to Black Marsh to put a stop to the illegal smuggling of [Amberplasm](Lore_Amber_Plasm.md), these criminals working for the Soldiers of the Void using Circle activities as a cover.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-BMGazetteer-13)</sup><sup>:23,29,35,44</sup>

In [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), the Circle of Champions put on a good show via various exhibition matches for disillusioned [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) soldiers during their Morrowind Morale Tour.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWGazetteer-14)</sup><sup>:24</sup> The matches caught the attention of potential business partners, who suggested an arena match within [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md) so alliance soldiers could cool off during the war within neutral territory.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWGazetteer-14)</sup><sup>:24</sup> The match however was interrupted by undead that emerged from [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md) towards the northeast, ruining a day of entertainment.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWGazetteer-14)</sup><sup>:34</sup> While aiding in transporting the dead to their graves in Necrom, the Circle of Champions investigated reports of graverobbing in the region as being a possible reasoning for the restless undead, and slew the responsible parties.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-MWGazetteer-14)</sup><sup>:37,42</sup>

The schemes of the Soldiers of the Void led to multiple factions being involved in trying to stop them and Deslandra's schemes. A hero took down Deslandra, but reports vary on when and to who they gave their allegiance to, with the Circle of Champions, the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md), [Eyes of the Queen](Lore_Eyes_of_the_Queen.md), [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), Outer Watch, and the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) being possible factions. Regardless, Deslandra was unable to complete the Great Nullification,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:44</sup> which would have forever severed the flow of magicka from Aetherius into Mundus, meaning magic would no longer have existed.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-8)</sup><sup>:52</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HRGazetteer-11)</sup><sup>:50</sup>

Some Circle of Champions members sought the Blessing of the Whitestrake - a boon that was said to imbue warriors with a "holy fervor for righteous carnage" and improve their contributions to the war. Priests of St. [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) were found in each gladiator camp during [Pelinal's Midyear Massacre](Lore_Pelinal%27s_Midyear_Massacre.md) offering to bestow blessings on anyone who asked for them.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONMaera-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONPredicant-16)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_teams) | All of the team names come from an early concept of *[The Elder Scrolls: Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, wherein each city had their own gladiatorial teams that would fight in a tournament. The Firedrakes were originally designated to [Torval](Lore_Torval.md), the Pit Daemons to [Helstrom](Lore_Helstrom.md), and the Stormlords to [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-17)</sup> |
| --- | --- |

The Pit Daemon also appear in *[Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)*, where they represent Helstrom's arena team and competed in an event known as the Dragon Games.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
