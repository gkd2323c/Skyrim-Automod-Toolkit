- 3 Iron Ores are obtained by [mining](Skyrim_Mining.md) Iron Ore Veins. Can be purchased at all levels.
- 1 Iron Ingot is obtained by [smelting](Skyrim_Smelter.md) 1 Iron Ore (this is the only case where only one ore is required to make an ingot). Can be purchased at all levels.
- 1 [Steel Ingot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Steel_Ingot) is obtained by smelting 1 Iron Ore and 1 [Corundum Ore](Skyrim_Corundum_Ore.md).
- Iron Smithing is available from the start of the game, without any [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) perks.
- Iron Armor is level 1 [Heavy Armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md), made from Iron Ingots and [Leather Strips](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Leather_Strips).
- Iron weapons are level 1 weapons, made from Iron Ingots and Leather Strips.

## Mineral Sources
In addition to the following guaranteed sources, the best random locations are:

- Iron Ingots are always sold by all blacksmith merchants, at all levels.
- Iron Ingots may be sold by general goods merchants at all levels.
- Iron Ore may be sold by blacksmiths and general goods merchants at all levels.

There are a total of 668 veins, 81 ores, and 275 ingots available in the game. Individual iron ore vein outcroppings are found scattered everywhere across Skyrim. Only particularly notable locations are included in the following list, including places where large amount of the metal may be found and places that are particularly easy to access. The [Iron Veins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Iron_Veins) category contains a more complete list of places containing smaller numbers of veins.

All exterior ore veins are shown on [(map)](https://gamemap.uesp.net/sr/?world=Skyrim&centeron=the+Skyrim+map&search=Iron+Ore+Vein). Note, however, that only 50 veins are shown on the map at one time; to see the next set of 50 veins, click on "Next" at the bottom of the list of results. Given the high density of iron veins on the map, a more efficient way to find veins near your current location is to *not* use the search feature, but instead simply zoom in at your current location on the map. Veins are shown at either of the two highest zoom levels.

## Iron Smithing
Iron items appear in leveled lists from the start of the game, and can be [forged](Skyrim_Forge.md) without the need to unlock any [Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) perks. This also means that only normal-level tempering of iron items is possible (there is no perk that provides the ability to "improve them twice as much").

### Iron Armor
Iron Armor is the lowest-quality heavy armor in the game. All items can be [tempered](Skyrim_Workbench.md) using 1 [Iron Ingot](Skyrim_Iron_Ingot.md).

The [Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) adds the Brawler's iron gauntlets, a new gauntlet variant. The base pair can be crafted and differ only in appearance, while a pair with a 10 point [Fortify Unarmed](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md) enchantment can be bought from vendors.

### Banded Iron Armor
Banded Iron Armor is marginally better quality heavy armor than standard Iron Armor. One drawback of Banded Iron armor is that only a cuirass and shield are available, meaning that it is impossible to create a [Matching Set](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md#Matching_Set) of Banded Iron armor (Patch 1.9 later addressed this bug). Both items can be [tempered](Skyrim_Workbench.md) using 1 [Corundum Ingot](Skyrim_Corundum_Ingot.md).

### Iron Weapons
Iron [weapons](Skyrim_Weapons.md) are the lowest-quality weapons in the game, and appear in leveled lists from the start of the game. All items except ammunition can be [tempered](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) using 1 Iron Ingot. Arrows can only be smithed if the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on has been installed. Bolts are only available with the [Rare Curios](Skyrim_Rare_Curios.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) installed.

There is no "Iron Bow", only Iron Arrows. The bow found at similar levels is the [Long Bow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Long_Bow), which cannot be crafted but can be improved with firewood.

## Notes
- Iron Ore can be [transmuted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Transmute_Mineral_Ore) into [Silver Ore](Skyrim_Silver_Ore.md), which in turn can be transmuted into [Gold Ore](Skyrim_Gold_Ore.md). Iron Ore can also be [master transmuted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Master_Transmute) into Gold Ore<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Bittercup.md)</sup>.
- Iron Ore can be sold to [Beitild](Skyrim_Beitild.md), [Gestur Rockbreaker](Skyrim_Gestur_Rockbreaker.md), [Grogmar gro-Burzag](Skyrim_Grogmar_gro-Burzag.md), [Skaggi Scar-Face](Skyrim_Skaggi_Scar-Face.md), or [Thorgar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Thorgar) for the quest [Mine Ore](Skyrim_Mine_Ore.md). Note that these people will pay you 7 gold (instead of 2 gold) for each ore.
- Iron Ingots are needed when [forging](Skyrim_Forge.md) all varieties of Iron, [Steel](Skyrim_Steel.md), [Orcish](Skyrim_Orcish.md), [Dwarven](Skyrim_Dwarven.md), [Elven](Skyrim_Elven.md), [Ancient Nord](Skyrim_Ancient_Nord.md), and [Dragonscale](Skyrim_Dragonscale.md) armor and weapons.
- 1 Iron Ingot is needed at [Workbenches](Skyrim_Workbench.md) to improve all varieties of Iron armor (except Banded Iron armor and Banded Iron shields, which require 1 Corundum ingot), Ancient Nord armor, and numerous unique items and artifacts.
- 1 Iron Ingot is needed at [Grindstones](Skyrim_Grindstone.md) to improve all varieties of Iron weapons, as well as numerous unique items and artifacts.
- Ore veins of all types sometimes do not respawn normally. See [Mining bugs](Skyrim_Mining.md#Bugs).
- <sup>[HF](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md)</sup> All crafted housebuilding items use Iron Ingots in their Creation, and many items [built at a carpenter's workbench](Skyrim_Construction.md) also require them. - The hinges recipe produces 2 per ingot, and the nails recipe produces 10 per ingot. The other two recipes (iron fittings and locks) produce one at a time. - Mining Iron Ore and smelting the ore into Iron Ingots is an excellent way to prepare for construction, as you may need up to 300 ingots per property.
- Due to a cross-promotion between [Bethesda Game Studios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Bethesda_Game_Studios) and Valve, in the game Team Fortress 2 the Iron Helmet appeared as the ["Dragonborn Helmet"](https://wiki.teamfortress.com/wiki/Dragonborn_Helmet).
