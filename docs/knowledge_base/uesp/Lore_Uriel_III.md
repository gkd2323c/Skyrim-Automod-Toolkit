Uriel Septim III (born **Uriel Mantiarco**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup> was the ninth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for six years, from [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121) to [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127). In [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120), he and his mother [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) asserted that the heir apparent [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) was a bastard, and they proposed that Uriel take the throne instead of Kintyra due to the alleged uncertainty of her parentage.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup> After hearing of this accusation, Kintyra banished them from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), and they returned to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), where they began planning the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup> In 3E 121, his forces captured and deposed Kintyra II; he then assumed the throne as Uriel Septim III.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup> However, only half of the provinces supported Uriel III, while the rest supported his uncles, [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), who allied to oppose him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup> In 3E 127, Uriel III was captured at the Battle of Ichidag and placed in a cart for transport to stand trial in the Imperial City, but a mob burned him to death while en route.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup> He was preceded by his first cousin, Kintyra II, and was succeeded by his uncle, Cephorus I.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup>

## Biography

### Early Life and King of Solitude
Uriel Mantiarco was born in [3E 97](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_97) to [Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantiarco), King of [Solitude](Lore_Haafingar.md), and [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), the Wolf Queen, after many prior miscarriages.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ2-4)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup> Uriel was named after his great-grandfather, Emperor [Uriel II](Lore_Uriel_II.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ2-4)</sup> He had an older paternal half-brother named Bathorgh.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TWQ2-4)</sup> On 15 Evening Star, [3E 98](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_98), his grandfather, Emperor [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md), died, so one-year-old Uriel accompanied his parents to attend Pelagius II's funeral in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWQ3-5)</sup>

In the spring of [3E 100](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_100), King Mantiarco died, so Uriel acceded to the throne of Solitude, with Potema ruling as his regent.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ4-6)</sup> King Uriel undoubtedly would have preferred to rule alone, but Potema convinced him that his position was only temporary.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup> Potema told him that he would have the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), not merely the Kingdom of Solitude.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup> As of [3E 109](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_109), the [Blue Palace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Palace) regularly received visits from the kings of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and emissaries from various rulers of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), all of whom were disgruntled with the reign of Uriel's uncle, Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TWQ4-6)</sup> As of [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119), Uriel and Potema were considered to have ruled the Kingdom of Solitude well during their co-reign.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup>

In 3E 119, while Uriel and Potema were visiting Antiochus, the Emperor lapsed into a coma and died the next year, in [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMOP-8)</sup> While Antiochus was comatose, the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) prepared for the accession of Uriel's first cousin, [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_II.md), to the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup>

Before Antiochus' funeral, Potema gave a speech before the Elder Council in which she asserted that Kintyra was not the daughter of Antiochus.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> Instead, Potema asserted that Kintyra was popularly believed to be the daughter of Empress Gysilla and the Captain of the Guard, or the daughter of Gysilla and the cistern cleaner.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> Potema proposed that Uriel take the throne instead of Kintyra due to the alleged uncertainty of her parentage.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> Uriel made a similar accusation, referring to the infamous decadence of the Imperial City during the reign of Kintyra's father.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup> After hearing of this accusation, Kintyra banished Uriel and Potema from the Imperial City, and they returned to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWQ5-7)</sup> The accusation failed to stop Kintyra's coronation, and on 3 First Seed, 3E 120, she was crowned Empress of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup>

### War of the Red Diamond and Emperor of Tamriel
Upon their return to Solitude, Uriel and Potema began assembling a rebellion against Kintyra II, which was to be known as the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup> The disgruntled members of royalty and the nobility, who had allied with the Kingdom of Solitude over the years, joined forces with Uriel and Potema against the new Empress.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup> Uriel also bought the support of several other disgruntled kings of High Rock, Skyrim, and Morrowind.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup>

The first attacks against the Empire in Skyrim and northern High Rock were entirely successful.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup> In the autumn of 3E 120, Mentin, Duke of [Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md), sent an urgent request for reinforcements from the [Imperial Army](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), and Kintyra II led the army herself to inspire resistance to the rebellion.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup> In [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121), the forces of Uriel and Potema captured Kintyra II, massacred her entourage, and destroyed the imperial reinforcements by bribing Duke Mentin to betray the Empress.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-9)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMOP-8)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TDC-10)</sup> In the second attack, Uriel's forces assaulted the imperial forts along Morrowind's coastal islands.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup> In the third and final attack, Uriel invaded the [Imperial Province](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), which was ill-defended due to the Elder Council dividing the Imperial Army to attack western High Rock and eastern Morrowind.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup> He and the rebel army laid siege to the under-defended Imperial City, which surrendered after only a fortnight of besiegement.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup> On the same evening as the city's surrender, Uriel assumed the Ruby Throne as Uriel Septim III.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TET-12)</sup> A [statue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Statues_Notable_Figures#Uriel_Septim_III) of Uriel III was built in the [Market District](Lore_Market_District_(Imperial_City).md) of the Imperial City to commemorate his victory in the War of the Red Diamond.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OOGG-13)</sup><sup>:347</sup>

After Uriel III was crowned Emperor of Tamriel, his uncles [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) formed an alliance to oppose him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup> Uriel III had the combined support of Skyrim, northern Morrowind, and High Rock, while the kings of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md), [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), southern Morrowind, and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) mostly supported Cephorus and Magnus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMOP-8)</sup> On the 23rd of Frostfall, [3E 123](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_123), Uriel III ordered Kintyra II's execution.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TET-12)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BD-14)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_KSII)</sup> Kintyra II's demise rallied many against Uriel III and Potema.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup>

Cephorus concentrated his army on the war in High Rock, which was the location of some of the bloodiest battles of the war, while Magnus' troops fought in Morrowind and Skyrim.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMOP-8)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup> In the summer of [3E 125](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_125), Cephorus conquered western High Rock, and by [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127), all of High Rock had defected to the side of Cephorus and Magnus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup> In 3E 127, Uriel III and the Imperial Army fought Cephorus' army at the [Battle of Ichidag](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) in Hammerfell, but Cephorus' army defeated the Imperial Army and captured the Emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ8-15)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup> Uriel III was placed in a carriage for transport to the Imperial City to stand trial, but while en route, a mob overtook his carriage and burned him alive within it.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TWQ7-11)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup> Following Uriel III's death, Cephorus was proclaimed Emperor of Tamriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ8-15)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-3)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TET-12)</sup>

### Legacy
Uriel III's death marked the official end of the War of the Red Diamond, but for the first decade of Cephorus I's reign, he remained at war with Potema until her death at the [Siege of Solitude](Lore_Siege_of_Solitude.md) in [3E 137](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_137).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ8-15)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TMOP-8)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TET-12)</sup> Some consider Kintyra II's death to be the end of the pure Septim bloodline in the imperial family.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup> Conversely, in Skyrim after the deaths of Uriel III and Potema, a strong underground movement known as the [Hörme](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:H%C3%B6rme) emerged, which believed that Uriel and Potema were the last of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s true bloodline and, under that principle, conducted raids against imperial interests in the province.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3S-16)</sup>
