[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4a/ON-place-Kvatch.jpg/200px-ON-place-Kvatch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kvatch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Kvatch.jpg) Kvatch circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) [![Banner of Kvatch](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/ON-banner-Kvatch.png/100px-ON-banner-Kvatch.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-Kvatch.png) Kvatch is a Colovian city in the hinterland of the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), located between [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad). It is built atop a large bluff, raising the city above the surrounding forests. Its coat of arms is a black [Dire Wolf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dire_Wolf) on a grey background.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GDW-2)</sup> The city is home to an arena, the imposing Kvatch Castle, and the Great Chapel of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). County Kvatch extends far to the northwest, up to the [Brena River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brena_River) at the border of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The people of the city wear turban-like helmets with chainmail.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Helms-1)</sup>

## History

### First Era
In the early days of Cyrodiil, in the wake of the [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md), there were three lords that formed a regency council over the Kingdom of Kvatch circa [1E 361](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_361), one of them being [Lord Nunex Faleria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nunex_Faleria).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TLFF-4)</sup> During the fifth century of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the kingdoms of Kvatch and Skingrad were rivals. Their skirmishing ended in [1E 472](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_472), during which time Kvatch was under rule of King Justinius. The king's daughter Belene was wed to Prince [Rislav Larich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rislav_Larich) of Skingrad, presumably to make peace between the two kingdoms. Towards the end of the fifth century, the Kings of the Colovian West joined with Kvatch and Skingrad to resist the [Alessian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) army and its Reform. This open rebellion helped prompt Clan [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md)'s excursions into Cyrodiil and ultimately the collapse of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-5)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d6/LG-cardart-Knight_of_the_Hour.png/200px-LG-cardart-Knight_of_the_Hour.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Knight_of_the_Hour.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Knight_of_the_Hour.png) Knight of the Hour The [Order of the Hour](Lore_Order_of_the_Hour.md) was revived in Kvatch by the warrior-priest [Cavor Merula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cavor_Merula) in [2E 432](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_432)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-So CM-6)</sup>, and were instrumental in ending the [Order of the Black Worm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Order_of_the_Black_Worm)'s presence in the city in [2E 437](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_437). Following two years of red wheat blight that devastated harvests in [2E 467](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_467), Gruff Bojour led an angry mob of citizens to the steps of the Cathedral, where half of them were slaughtered by the Order's warrior-priests.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Oot H-7)</sup> A few years later in [2E 480](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_480), the Order defended Kvatch against an army of marauders led by the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) Baz Swordbreaker. In [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), [Count Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios) led the Second Legion against [Emperor Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic), leaving his nephew [Carolus Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Carolus_Aquilarios) in charge. Carolus, with just a single cohort of the Second Legion, successfully defended Kvatch against attacks from [Count Ephrem Benirus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ephrem_Benirus) of Anvil, leaving the port city weakened and ripe for [Fortunata ap Dugal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fortunata_ap_Dugal) to capture. [Primate Artorius Ponticus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Artorius_Ponticus) also began working against Carolus.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TWat D-8)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-9)</sup>

When Fortunata captured Anvil in [2E 579](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_579) and declared herself Provincial Governor of an independent Gold Coast, Carolus refused her request for Kvatch to join her and continued to swear fealty to Varen. After the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) and Kvatch's increased isolation, Fortunata stepped up a campaign of disrupting trade and travel that culminated in Fortunata attempting an annexation of Kvatch with the Anvil Guard. This was a feint however, and Kvatch's cohort was ambushed and destroyed after engaging Fortunata's forces in what has become known as the 'Gottshaw Massacre'.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWat PQ-9)</sup> After the assassination of Fortunata and Artorius Ponticus by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Carolus emerged as the dominant power in the region.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESODB-10)</sup>

At this time, an abandoned Dark Brotherhood sanctuary was located outside the city walls to the south. Built into the Foundations of Kvatch itself, this ancient complex had to be abandoned by the Dark Brotherhood after a subterranean collapse caused it to connect up to a massive natural cavern.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOTES-11)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png/200px-LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Siege_Crawler.png) A Siege Crawler, a warmachine responsible for the destruction of Kvatch [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1b/OB-misc-Anniversary_Art22.jpg/200px-OB-misc-Anniversary_Art22.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Anniversary_Art22.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-misc-Anniversary_Art22.jpg) The Battle for Kvatch Kvatch was a major settlement when it was sacked by the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) in [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TR-12)</sup> At the center of the city stood a statue of [Antus Pinder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Antus_Pinder), a hero who led the defense of the city against an unspecified superior force. Despite being defeated gravely, the statue was erected to remember his courage and spirit.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup>

By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the ruling family of Kvatch was the Goldwine lineage. A conflict over inheritance caused the death of both sons of Count [Ormellius Goldwine](Lore_Ormellius_Goldwine.md) in [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), causing a short period of unrest in Kvatch.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE-14)</sup> In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) saint, [Jiub](Lore_Jiub.md), moved to Kvatch to begin work on his autobiography.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SJO-15)</sup>

Not long after the assassination of [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), the city was razed to the ground by [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) as the opening move of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), leaving Kvatch with no count. Many of its citizens were also killed, and those who survived either fled into the countryside or congregated at a refugee camp at the foot of the bluff. Among the survivors was [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), who escaped the city after the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md) closed the last remaining gate to the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md) (the feat that would give them their auspicious title). With all the Oblivion Gates sealed, the Kvatch city guard were able to enter the ruins and retake Kvatch Castle with the aid of the Hero and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). Kvatch provided what few men it could afford to aid in the defense of [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) later that year.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup>

### Fourth Era
At the opening of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), following its liberation from the Daedra, the city was uninhabited and only the chapel and castle remained standing.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-OB-16)</sup>

The city had been restored to prominence by the time the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) broke out in [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TGW-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-18)</sup>
