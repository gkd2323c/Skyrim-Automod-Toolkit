— *[36 Lessons of Vivec, Sermon 33](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:36_Lessons_of_Vivec,_Sermon_33)* Lorkhan, the **Missing God**, is the **Creator-Trickster-Tester** deity present in every [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) mythic tradition.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup> He is known as the **Spirit of Nirn**, the god of all mortals.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SONGOM-2)</sup> His most popular name is the [elven](Lore_Aldmer.md) variant Lorkhan, or **Doom Drum**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup> which sometimes appears as **LKHAN**<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sb-5)</sup> or **LHKAN**.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-36L-6)</sup> Names for versions or aspects of Lorkhan include **[Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj)** (the Moon Beast) in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md),<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WOCMA-7)</sup> and **[Lorkh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkh)**, the **Spirit of Man**, the **Mortal Spirit**, or the **Sower of Flesh** among the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-GSR5-8)</sup> He is recognized as **[Sep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sep)** in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), **[Sheor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sheor)** in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), **[Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor)** in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and **[Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr)** in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> To the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md), he is also called the **Corpse-God**.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FOM-10)</sup> He is also known as the **Void Ghost**.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOV33-11)</sup> He convinced or contrived the Original Spirits ([et'Ada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Et%27Ada)) to bring about the creation of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), upsetting the status quo—much like his father [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) or [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis) is usually credited for introducing instability, and hence possibility for creation, into the undivided universe (or the [Beginning Place](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beginning_Place)).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-12)</sup>

Lorkhan is sometimes associated with the number **9**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-V29-13)</sup> and has been referred to as the **Missing Sibling** or **Missing Ninth** to the [Eight Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods_E#Eight_Divines).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Sat D-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TEC-15)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FPKirkbride-16)</sup>

## Mythology and History
All Tamrielic religions begin the same. Man or mer, things begin with the dualism of Anu and His Other. These twin forces go by many names: Anu-Padomay, Anuiel-Sithis, Ak-El, Satak-Akel, Is-Is Not. Anuiel is the Everlasting Ineffable Light, Sithis is the Corrupting Inexpressible Action. In the middle is the Gray Maybe ('Nirn' in the Ehlnofex)...
In any case, from these two beings spring the et'Ada, or Original Spirits. To humans these et'Ada are the Gods and Demons; to the Aldmer, the Aedra/Daedra, or the 'Ancestors'. All of the Tamrielic pantheons fill their rosters from these et'Ada, though divine membership often differs from culture to culture. Like Anu and Padomay, though, every one of these pantheons contains the archetypes of the Dragon God and the Missing God.
— *[The Monomyth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Monomyth)* As with most other gods, accounts of Lorkhan and his various feats date back to the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-12)</sup> Owing to the non-linear nature of time in the Dawn, the War of Manifest Metaphors is said to have rendered stories of this era incapable of supporting anything resembling a singular "narrative",<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TLL-17)</sup> rendering attempts at constructing one absurd.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONDOL-18)</sup> Nonetheless, various people and cultures have their own views on Lorkhan's actions and motivations, with varying levels of consistent motifs. Every [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) mythic tradition has an archetypal Missing God in its pantheon. This god is credited with convincing, tricking, or coercing the Original Spirits into creating the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), the Mortal Plane.

After the world had been materialized, myths hold that Lorkhan was killed, mutilated, or at least separated from his divine center, known by various terms across cultures but most commonly dubbed the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), in an event sometimes called the [Convention](Lore_Convention.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-12)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> This separation, which purportedly took place in Tamriel,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-MA-19)</sup> is variously considered a punishment, a betrayal, or voluntary sacrifice.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TSS-20)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TDS-21)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-PTDDU-22)</sup> Whatever remained of Lorkhan afterward was exiled to wander through creation, while the rest of the gods withdrew from the Mortal Realm.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-12)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-AAD-23)</sup>

The result of these events, Nirn, has been described by some as a source of mental anguish to all living things, who know deep down that their souls came from somewhere else, and that something must come next. Many wish to return to the [spirit realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius), the original state of the universe; to them, Nirn is a prison keeping them sundered from the spirit realm, an illusion to escape, and Lorkhan is often the demon that hinders their way. Others think that Lorkhan created the world as the testing ground for transcendence; to them the spirit realm was already a prison, and that [true escape](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amaranth) is now finally possible.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SONGOM-2)</sup>

As the impetus for creating the Mundus, Lorkhan is at the center of the great ideological schism between the [elven](Lore_Mer.md) and [human](Lore_Man.md) perspectives on the event, which is known as the Sundering to the elves or simply the Creation to other cultures, and thus opinions of him vary dramatically across the different religions of Tamriel.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup> Listed below are the more popular interpretations.

### Aldmeri Lorkhan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg/200px-SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg) Auriel's Bow, said to have launched Lorkhan's Heart into the sea. The most popular name of the Missing God is his Aldmeri moniker, "Lorkhan", meaning "Doom Drum", and literally translated as "Dark Chief"<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-24)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ART-25)</sup>. For most Aldmeri cultures, particularly the Altmer, Lorkhan is a malevolent trickster, and the most unholy of all the higher powers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> Lorkhan is believed to have forever sundered their connection to the [spirit plane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SONGOM-2)</sup>

The most detailed account of these beliefs is found in the Altmeri creation Myth, "Heart of the World". Within the tale, Lorkhan is characterized as "a barely formed urge" more akin to the limitations of [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis) rather than a nature, unlike other spirits born from the ruminations of [Anuiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuiel) and his soul, [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel). Eventually, Lorkhan infiltrates the various aspects of Anuiel to plant a new idea, one based wholly on limitation. He outlined a plan to create a soul for Aurbis, the Mundus, so the spirits may self-reflect like [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) once did, and form their own cosmos. Many spirits joined in, including Auriel, who agreed on the assurance that he would be king of this new realm. This offer turned out to be a trick, as the new world was hardly a thing of Anu at all - Mundus was the House of [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis). As the spirits on the newly formed Nirn began to die off, many of the et'Ada vanished completely. Some, following [Magnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magnus)'s example, fled to Aetherius and became the [stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna_Ge). Some, such as [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre), fully merged with the world and transformed into the natural laws known as [Earthbones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Earthbones), or Ehlnofey. Among those, a fourth group endured by marrying and making children with one another, living on through their descendants.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup> These Ehlnofey are known to the Altmer and Bosmer as [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra), meaning Ancestors.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-12)</sup>

Eventually, over many generations these children weakened into the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), whom Auriel continued to lead as they founded the great kingdoms of Altmora and Aldmeris, also known as [Old Ehlnofey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Old_Ehlnofey). Meanwhile, the weakest of the surviving souls were gathered up by Lorkhan, who named them Men and led their armies against Auriel. In the ensuing war, Lorkhan destroyed the kingdom of Old Ehlnofey, shattering that land into many.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> Eventually, the greatest knight of the Ehlnofey and champion of Auriel<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LOS-26)</sup><sup>:Part 1, Prologue</sup>, [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac), knocked Lorkhan down in front of his army and reached in "with more than hands" to remove his Heart. Lorkhan was undone, and his body was carried away by his vengeful followers. Despite Lorkhan's end, his Heart lived on, as it had become the Heart of the World and thus indestructible. Auriel then took the Heart and fastened it to an arrow, launching it into the sea where none may find it.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup>

Some less mythic accounts from the Altmer describe Lorkhan's end as a trial undergone within the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) by the Aedra. There, Lorkhan was judged guilty and [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel) slew him atop the Tower, removing his divine spark as punishment. Much like the "Heart of the World" account, the Heart proved to be indestructible, and was instead launched from the tower by an arrow from [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) and landed in the Sea, where a [volcano](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) formed as a result. Afterward, the gods withdraw from the mortal realm, while Lorkhan remains exiled to wander Nirn forever.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-TOA-27)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-12)</sup>

Lorkhan is believed by some Bosmer to still exist under the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), and that the rumblings of the volcano are a result of him eating food that upset his intestines.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Danrithri O-28)</sup> Some Aldmeri Creation myths hold that [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) is 'born' when Lorkhan's divine spark is removed. One crucial myth calls him the 'Sithis-shaped hole' of the world.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup>

### Dunmeri Lorkhan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f8/MW-place-Akulakhan.jpg/200px-MW-place-Akulakhan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Akulakhan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Akulakhan.jpg) Akulakhan, the second Numidium built by the Sixth House to contain Lorkhan's Heart In contrast to their fellow elves, [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) culture, especially under [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) rule, always had a close relationship with the Missing God.

The Dunmeri Creation Myth, "[Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis)", characterizes Lorkhan as a cruel but ultimately benevolent god, who was created and sent by the titular primordial force to free the slaves of the false gods, [Anuiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuiel) and the Aedra, as well as destroy their illusionary realms of [everlasting imperfection](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) that they used to make themselves immortal. Lorkhan does this via deceit, first creating his own dominion with what appears to be his own slaves, and then coming to Anuiel and the Aedra as a friend. Lorkhan told them that he was tired of his dominion, and wanted to create another. Magnus was the first to express his opinion on this, and eventually Nirn was created.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sb-5)</sup><sup>[[TRL 1]](#cite_note-Mono2-29)</sup> However, according to the teachings of Boethiah, Lorkhan was betrayed by Magnus and the Aedra when he was not looking, and undone by them.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Serm Ten-30)</sup>

Later Dunmeri legends claim that [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md)'s creation of the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) and the subsequent [Velothi Exodus](Lore_Veloth.md) were directly inspired by Lorkhan's ideas. Trinimac had tricked the Aldmer into believing tears were the best response to the Sundering, and took the Missing God's name in vain, calling his narratives into question. So one day, Boethiah came and defeated Trinimac, using Trinimac's own guise to expose his lies, and inspired his many new followers to head east, to Morrowind.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TCO-31)</sup> These followers, led by Veloth, became known as the Chimer, and Boethiah bade them to pledge themselves to the 'Frame-Maker', one of Lorkhan's many titles, for creating this world for them to cherish and love him in.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Serm Ten-30)</sup>

Together, Boethiah and Mephala established the rules of the [Psijic Endeavor](Lore_Psijic_Endeavor.md), a process considered closely tied to Lorkhan, where the Chimer are charged with transcending the Aedra.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-VT-32)</sup> Dunmer acknowledge Nirn to be a cruel place full of mental anguish, however they are among those who believe Lorkhan created a realm where [true escape](Lore_CHIM.md) is now finally possible.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SONGOM-2)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-VT-32)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOV33-11)</sup>

In later years, Lorkhan's Heart would become the source of Dagoth Ur and the Tribunal's divinity, with Lorkhan and his motivations becoming a pondered topic in the *[36 Lessons of Vivec](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_36_Lessons_of_Vivec)*. The [Scarab](Lore_Scarab.md) and the [Serpent Constellation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent) are Dunmeri symbols for Lorkhan.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Serm Ten-30)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-LOV21-33)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOV33-11)</sup> The Black Scarab in particular is the symbol of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-CM52R-34)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-35)</sup>

In [Vivec's](Lore_Vivec.md) sermons, it is said that at the Altar of Padhome, there exists a [secret door](Lore_CHIM.md), one created by Lorkhan to show the mortals the secret at the Heart of the [Beginning Place](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-LOV3-36)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-LOV13-37)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOV33-11)</sup>. This door is the promise of PSJJJJ, which is the [flowering scheme](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amaranth) of the Aurbis, and by following it one can fulfill Lorkhan's grand design for the Mundus, wherein it reaches the end of its usefulness and becomes a receptacle for something new.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-LOV25-38)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-LOV37-39)</sup> Additionally, Vivec's forbidden eighteenth sermon claims that Lorkhan has already vanished in the west, replaced by someone in the same manner that Tribunal replaced the [Good Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra) in Morrowind.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-LOV18-40)</sup>

In his thirty-third sermon, Vivec's child, [Lie Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baar_Dau), met with the Void Ghost, an aspect of Lorkhan that dwells within the "Hidden Heaven", the [Scaled Blanket](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent) made of [not-stars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unstars), haggling with it for [powers](Lore_Walking_Ways.md) only meant for Ruling Kings.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOV33-11)</sup> Later the sermon describes a meeting between Nerevar and the Void Ghost, Nerevar compares himself to Lorkhan, asking which of them are out of place, to which Lorkhan replied both. While the sermon does not say what was spoken between the two masters, Vivec's numerology explains that the thirty-third sermon teaches the nature of Anticipations.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-LOV29-41)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LOV33-11)</sup> In a similar vein, the "Sithis" creation myth compares Dagoth Ur to [Anuiel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anuiel) and the Aedra, while the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) is likened to Lorkhan and advised to go unto the Sharmat Dagoth Ur as a friend, so that he may cast down the false gods as Lorkhan once did.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Sb-5)</sup>

Later in the Third Era, it is said Vivec shared further teachings with his followers after journeying to Cyrodiil to consult the Emperor on matters of the Tower. According to Vivec, the Missing God is Padhome's Firstborn, who wandered the Aurbis and wanted no name, yet was branded with "Lorkhan" by others. As time passed, Lorkhan watched the Aurbis shape itself and grew equally delighted and tired with each new shaping. As the gods and demons of the Aurbis erupted, the child of Padhome tried to leave it all behind for he wanted all of it and none of it all at once. It was then that he came to the border of the Aurbis. There, he saw the Tower, for a circle turned sideways is an "I". This was the first word of Lorkhan and he would never, ever forget it.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-VT-32)</sup>

### Mythic Dawn
To the Mythic Dawn, Lorkhan is the God of the Mundus, a Daedric Prince whose realm of Oblivion is Dawn's Beauty, misnamed [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) by deluded mortals.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Com3-42)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBMankar-3)</sup> According to the teachings of [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md), Tamriel is the realm of Change, and the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) are usurpers, betrayers and false gods posing as divinities for a Daedric Plane that has lost its Prince, whom they unsuccessfully attempted to erase from history.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBMankar-3)</sup> Camoran claims that in Convention, the [First Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) Dictate separated not only Lorkhan, but all mortals, his progeny, from their divine sparks, forcing mortals to see the Eight Aedra as the sole [exit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) from the mortal prison. Further, in a secret message within his writings, he claims that the Eight Aedra woe every reader that has come to understand [CHIM](Lore_CHIM.md), the royalty Lorkhan taught mortals to seek.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Com3-42)</sup>

### Lunar Lorkhan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/2/27/Mfull_Sfull.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Mfull_Sfull.jpg) Masser and Secunda, the supposed corpse of Lorkhan [The Lunar Lorkhan](Lore_The_Lunar_Lorkhan.md) Theory is an account of the Dawn proposed by the [Temple Zero Society](Lore_Temple_Zero_Society.md)<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>, intended to explain the origin of the Moons.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-Masser_and_Secunda-43)</sup> The theory claims that, where the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) contributed to the Great Construction by lending portions of their [heavenly bodies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planets), Lorkhan's was instead cracked asunder so that his divine spark might "impregnate Nirn with the measure of its existence and a reasonable amount of selfishness". What was left of his "flesh-divinity" became the Moons, a personification of what [Artaeum](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) calls the "Cloven Duality", the dichotomy that Lorkhan legends often rail against: ideas of good versus evil, being versus nothingness, and so on. The theory further asserts that the Moons were thus purposefully set in the night sky as Lorkhan's constant reminder to his mortal issue of their duty, and that all other '[Heart Stories](Lore_Convention.md)' are mythical degradations of the true origin of the moons.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TLL-17)</sup>

### Khajiiti Lorkhaj
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/37/Maligned_Saligned.jpg/200px-Maligned_Saligned.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Maligned_Saligned.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:Maligned_Saligned.jpg) Jone and Jode under a dark eclipse to form the Dark Moons, as seen from Nirn Known to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) as [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj), the Missing God is a divisive subject in Khajiiti theology. He is the youngest of [Fadomai's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) children, born secretly in the [Great Darkness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Darkness) after she was struck by [Ahnurr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu), and thus forever haunted by the darkness that filled his [Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan). Like other cultures, the Khajiit universally attribute the creation of the Mundus to Lorkhaj in their myths, however their attitude towards that event and his role in it has changed dramatically after the First Mane [Rid-Thar-ri'Datta](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta) restructured the pantheon in what is known as the [Riddle'Thar Epiphany](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods_R#Riddle.27Thar).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WOCMA-7)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ESOESA1-44)</sup>

In pre-ri'Datta theology, Lorkhaj was known to the Khajiit as the White Lion and the Favored Son of Fadomai. He was said to have been born in the Great Darkness and carried it with him as a burden, existing in conflict with himself from the moment of his birth. Lorkhaj was the first spirit to forge his path forward with purpose, and his courage inspired all those that he encountered, earning him the title of the Moon Prince as he united his siblings to create the [World](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TSS-20)</sup> Afterwards, he suffered a [great betrayal](Lore_Convention.md) at the hands of the other spirits, which some songs attribute to the machinations of [Merid-Nunda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia), and the Great Darkness overcame him, filling the hole where his heart had been. In his wisdom Lorkhaj, sought out his sister [Azurah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azurah) who tore the darkness from his chest before it could consume him, and she named it [Namiira](Lore_Namira.md) before casting it beyond the Void Gate. From this [Dark Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart) was born the Moon Beast, the first of the [Dro-m'Athra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro-m%27Athra) and a shade of Lorkhaj that knows nothing but hunger, stalking the Lunar Lattice for any Khajiit that stray too far from the Path. The Moon Prince then expired in Azurah's arms, who burned his body upon a pyre and scattered the ashes across the [Lunar Lattice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lunar_Lattice).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TDS-21)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ESOFDF-45)</sup> Thus, to the ancient Khajiit Lorkhaj represented the duality of the Khajiiti soul and the hardships that all Khajiit must overcome, and they honored his sacrifice by walking the Path with purpose and resisting the call of the Dark.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TSS-20)</sup>

Later Khajiiti theologians look less favorably upon Lorkhaj, disregarding his Moon Prince aspect entirely as a dangerous trick that would mislead the Khajiit into attempting to revive the Moon Beast's true spirit only to be swallowed by his [eternal curse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dro-m%27Athra).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ESOESA1-44)</sup> According to a post-ri'Datta creation myth, "Words of Clan-Mother Ahnissi", Fadomai birthed the last of her litter in the [Great Darkness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Darkness), having hidden there from her husband Ahnurr after he had beaten her. Thus, the Great Darkness filled the Heart of Lorkhaj and when he was born, the Great Darkness knew itself to be [Namiira](Lore_Namira.md). Eventually, the spirit [Nirni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirni) requested that Lorkhaj make her a place where she may birth many children, as Fadomai instructed her. Lorkhaj agreed, but because his heart was filled with darkness, he tricked his siblings so that they were forced into this new place with Nirni and trapped them within. Many of Fadomai's children escaped and became the stars, and many others died to make Nirni's path stable. The survivors stayed and punished Lorkhaj, tearing out his Heart and hiding it deep within Nirni. And though Nirni herself later forgave him, for now she could make children and had a place for them, the other spirits cursed "noisy Lorkhaj" to wander Nirni for many phases, forbidden by Azurah from hearing the First Secret while the other spirits learned how to cross the Lunar Lattice and escape.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WOCMA-7)</sup>

Both modern and ancient [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) religion places the two moons, Jone and Jode, as separate entities that existed prior to Lorkhan's punishment at Convention,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-WOCMA-7)</sup> and only believe that the third moon, the [Dark Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Moon), is his corpse. In pre-ri'Datta tradition, they were stillborn twins, and it was their lanterns with which Azurah lit Lorkhaj's funeral pyre.<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-MBHAYQ-46)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TMCD-47)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOShazah-48)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TSS-20)</sup>

### Reachmen Lorkh
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/ON-item-Dark_Heart.jpg/200px-ON-item-Dark_Heart.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Dark_Heart.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Dark_Heart.jpg) The Dark Heart Known to the [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md) as [Lorkh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkh), the Missing God is considered the Spirit of Man, the Mortal Spirit, or the Sower of Flesh.

According to [Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) Myths, in darkness Lorkh came to understand, nothing does not exist. Where there is nothing there is possibility. And so he found a space in the Void where all that is could be. With this in mind he came to the Spirit Queen, Namira, to grant him a place in the infinite void to make a realm for wayward spirits, Nirn. Rather than a paradise, Lorkh created a hard and painful place—a realm that taught through suffering.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-GSOR5-50)</sup> The early Reachmen fled to the mountains as with this creation Lorkh met his demise, and the world grew dark with shadows which sprung from the heart of Lorkh, who though greatly sundered still believed in the light of Man.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ONNZ-51)</sup> Reach Myth tells that Lorkh still wanders Nirn among mortals, appearing only in times of desperate need to aid the Reachfolk.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-GSOR5-50)</sup>

The Reachmen believe that at the "end of all days", [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) will fight alongside them,<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ONHSG-52)</sup> and the [Dark Heart of Lorkh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart) will beat again, reawakened by feeding on the deaths of mortals, and its darkness will spread from its depths to consume everything, sparing only [Namira](Lore_Namira.md)'s faithful.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ONA-53)</sup>

It is thought the [Briarheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Briarheart) ritual is a reflection by the immortal sacrifice of Lorkh.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-GSOR5-50)</sup>

### Nordic Shor
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/ce/SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg/200px-SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Shor%27s_Throne.jpg) Shor's Throne in the Hall of Valor Known to the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) as [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor), the Fox Totem<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-54)</sup>, the Missing God is considered the God of the Underworld who wielded his clever [magic](Lore_Magic.md) to create their afterlife of [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TRt S-55)</sup> The Nords revere him for having fought off Aldmeri oppression many times in Atmora.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> His widow is the warrior goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md), the purported creator of mankind and leader of the gods after Shor's demise.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Dat N-56)</sup>

Shor is said to have died at the hands of Elven Giants, who ripped out his heart at the end of the war that brought about the current world. <sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Dat N-56)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-FSOKW-57)</sup> The Nords believe this has happened before, as they consider the World itself to be a [cycle](Lore_Kalpa.md) in which the chief, Shor father of Shor, perishes every iteration of the world, spitting a helix of [ghosts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarrine) into the world below to make way for his son, Shor son of Shor, who leads mankind into war again.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-58)</sup> In more modern Nordic understanding, this is the transition from Dead Gods to Twilight Gods that occurs over each new iteration of the world, marked by the coming of the Last War in which Shor and his host descend from Sovngarde to fight again as they have at the beginning of time.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Dat N-56)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-SSCSS-59)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-SRHS-60)</sup><sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-GTNTR-61)</sup> Some myths consider [All-Maker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:All-Maker) the father of Shor.<sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-62)</sup>

Shor is believed to wander in times of need as a ghost, sung into the world by the chant of Tongues. He once appeared to fight Alduin as he did in the beginning of time, and save the Nords from trickery of [Orkey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orkey).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-FSOKW-57)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> Another mythic account describes his appearance at the Battle of Red Mountain, where he led the Nords against the armies of the [First Council](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:First_Council), with [Ysmir Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) and [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) serving as his generals.<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-FSOKW-57)</sup> In Cyrodilic legends, he is tied to mortal incarnations such as "the [Shezarrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarrine)", who has also been called "Shor-Who-Lives".<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-TFOS-63)</sup> [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) is one alleged Shezarrine, and Nords who saw him and his white hair proclaimed that Shor had returned, though Pelinal himself called this profanity.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-TSOP4-64)</sup>

### Bretic Sheor

### Cyrodilic Shezarr
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg/200px-OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-Kot N-Pelinal Painting.jpg) Pelinal Whitestrake, sometimes believed to be the Shezarrine, fighting Umaril Known to the [Cyrodiils](Lore_Imperial.md) (particularly the subculture of the Nibenese), as [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr), the Missing God is considered "the spirit behind all human undertaking" and the God of Man.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Sat D-14)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> He is deeply revered as an early hero of the Nedes, and is considered to be synonymous with "Freedom" in the myths of the [Alessian Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md).<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TSOP2-67)</sup>

According to the Cyrodilic Creation Myth, "Shezarr's Song", Shezarr came to the Aedra with a new idea: to become mothers and fathers, and make great sacrifices with no guarantee of success. Shezarr spoke beautifully to them, and moved them beyond mystery and tears. In some legends, [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md) is first to agree to Shezarr's proposal, and provided the space for this creation in the void.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> Thus the Aedra gave free birth to the world, the beasts, and the beings, making these things from parts of themselves. This free birth was very painful, and afterwards the Aedra were no longer young, and strong, and powerful, as they had been from the beginning of days.

The [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) mocked Shezarr and the other Aedra, and decided to make [their own worlds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Oblivion) within themselves where they wouldn't have to sacrifice control, though they came to envy the Aedra's creation. Some Aedra were disappointed, bitter, and angry with Shezarr, and with all creation, for they felt Shezarr had lied and tricked them. These Aldmeri gods, led by [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El), resolved to teach the elves to suffer with dignity, to chastise themselves for their folly, and to seek vengeance on Shezarr and his allies for reducing them to their enfeebled selves. But the gods of men and beast folk, led by Akatosh, were pleased with creation despite their great sacrifices, and resolved to teach the mortal races to live well, to cherish beauty and honor, and to love one another as the gods love them.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup>

Rather than claim his Aldmeri counterpart is a responsible, some second era Cyrodilic bard songs claim Akatosh directly slew Shezarr (Lorkhan in the song), launching his laughing immortal heart across Tamriel. The Heart's Blood is said in these tales to have fallen into an Ayleid well and fused with the magicka therein, solidifying into a crystal that the Ayleids cut and polished into the Red Diamond, [Chim-El Adabal](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md).<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-CEA-68)</sup>

Despite Shezarr's demise, he continued to wander Tamriel, aiding the tribes of man at various points as a warrior or a wise teacher before vanishing again to help mankind elsewhere.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Sat D-14)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-TFOS-63)</sup> Some believe Shezarr did not do this directly, but through mortal incarnates known as "[the Shezarrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarrine)", or "Shor-Who-Lives".<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TSOP1-69)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TSOP5-70)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-TFOS-63)</sup> Pelinal Whitestrake was once said to be the alleged Shezarrine, although this was best left unsaid in Pelinal's presence as Plontinu, a soldier who fought alongside Pelinal, was said to have been smothered by moths in the night after voicing the claim.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-TSOP5-70)</sup> Some claim it was Pelinal or Shezarr, rather than Akatosh, that met [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) at her Deathbed, making her the first stone of the Amulet of Kings and forging the [Covenant](Lore_Dragonfires.md).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TSOP8-71)</sup><sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-FET-72)</sup>

It is said that Kynareth made the first rain at Shezarr's sundering, a phenomenon that did not exist before the removal of his Heart.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-TSOP2-67)</sup>

### Yokudan Sep
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/RG-constellation-Serpent.jpg/200px-RG-constellation-Serpent.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Serpent.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-constellation-Serpent.jpg) The Serpent (Redguard) The Missing God is known to the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) as Sep, the Second Serpent created from the detritus of the First Serpent [Satakal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Satakal)'s dead skin.

According to the Yokudan Creation Myth, "Satakal the Worldskin", Satakal was born from the duality of Satak, the Snake who came Before, and Akel, the Hungry Stomach. Because there was nothing outside of Satak, it could only think about what it was, and so it devoured itself until its hunger caused it to bite down on its own heart and die. The hunger, though, refused to stop even in death, and so the First Serpent shed its dead skin to begin anew as Satakal. Once the other spirits realized this [pattern](Lore_Kalpa.md), the strongest of them learned to survive this cycle by striding between the worldskins at strange angles, in a process they called the Walkabout. One of the spirits, [Ruptga](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruptga), was so tall he could place the stars in the sky to help weaker spirits to find their way to the [Far Shores](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Far_Shores), and sired many children across the cycles until he became known as Tall Papa.

Eventually, there were so many spirits that Tall Papa had to make himself a helper from the detritus of dead skins, and this was Sep, the Second Serpent. However, Sep carried much of the Hungry Stomach within him, multiple hungers from multiple dead skins, and would often devour the spirits he was supposed to help. When he did, Ruptga would reach in and pull them back out. After some time, Sep grew tired of helping Ruptga, and so he gathered all the dead worldskins into a ball. He tricked the other spirits, promising them this was how you reached the new world, by making one out of the old, and many joined him thinking this was an easier way of living, without jumping from place to place all the time. However, soon the spirits on the skin-ball began to die, as it was far removed from the real world of Satakal, and it was too far now for them to jump into the Far Shores, and Ruptga said they had to live on through their children and search for new ways to follow the stars. Sep, however, needed more punishment, and so Ruptga struck him with a [big stick](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md), and his [hunger](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) fell out of his mouth and was the only thing left of the Second Serpent. While the rest of the world strived back to godhood, Sep could only wander Nirn, trapped in his own dead skin, or swim about in the sky as the Serpent Constellation, a hungry void that jealously tries to eat the stars.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TM-4)</sup>

### Argonian Shadow of Atakota
Allegedly known to the Argonians of the [Nisswo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nisswo) and Adzi-Kostleel tribes is a Time God, Atakota, and his nameless shadow.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-COR-73)</sup>

According to the Adzi-Kostleel Creation Myth, "Children of the Root", [Atak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu) and [Kota](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay) become one, Atakota is born and says "Maybe", it sheds its skin. The skin follows it around as a Shadow. As Atakota coiled and [devours worlds](Lore_Kalpa.md), each a scale on its skin, things had time to begin and end. The Shadow wished to eat these things, but its belly was full of growing [Roots](Lore_Hist.md). The Shadow soon spat out the Roots, and regarded them as its own, and so taught them secrets before falling into slumber. Over time, the Roots spread the Shadow's teachings, and things learn to take on new forms and names, but in doing so took the Shadow into themselves, and so learned what it was to be temporary. They came to fear the Shadow's change and call it Death. The Roots taught the Spirits how to evade death using riverways, and then went to sleep.

Eventually the Worlds of Atakota grew too big, and in desperate hunger the Spirits broke Atakota, splitting him into Atak and Kota once more. Some spirits grew claws and fangs and wings in the chaos. Others clung to what they were, such as a [Forest Spirit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre), who made her [own people](Lore_Bosmer.md) like the Roots made [theirs](Lore_Argonian.md). Soon, the Roots awoke, saw how Atak's sap had made stones and Kota's blood had made oceans, and knew what it would mean. And so the Roots awoke the Shadow, asking it to protect its children. The Shadow devours the broken halves of Atakota before the World can end, to save its children, but in doing so devoured the roots as well. Seeking the Roots within itself, the Shadow remember it was the skin of something that came before, and so shed its [skin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), even though that was all it was, and kept its children safe within its secrets.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-COR-73)</sup>

Tales about an explorer deep in Black Marsh describe a golden Argonian whose face changed twelve times. Soon after the explorer witnessed a vision of many wheels and their towers, that began to break, before quickly the Shadow of Atakota came to devour them.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-LTOTFE4-74)</sup>

Some Argonians tie the Shadow of Atakota to the Serpent constellation.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-ESOWUS-75)</sup>

## Worship
As a result of his nature as Missing God, Lorkhan is rarely the subject of direct worship in majority of cultures. Despite this, various cults to him have still arisen, alongside culture-agnostic examples of folklore. Some Lorkhan cults may have predicted the rise of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-PGE1M-77)</sup>

In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Lorkhan Cults began to see a rise across Tamriel,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TLL-17)</sup> and are said to have been responsible for spreading the doctrine of the [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) phenomenon. Various scholars look down upon these cults and the notion of Dragon Break, regarding it as borne of scholarly inertia, and obsession with eschatology and [Numidiumism](Lore_Numidium.md).<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-TDBRE-78)</sup> At some point in the late Third Era, contemporary to the publication of the [third pocket guide to the empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition), a self-proclaimed Lorkhan priestess named Jsashe, the Witch-Queen of Whiterun, came into power over the state hold.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-PGE3S-79)</sup>

Lorkhan across varying beliefs has obscure relationships with other deities. He and [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) are the only deities who seem to be present in every tradition. As a result, [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara) is sometimes considered his wife or concubine, as is Kynareth (or Kyne), and in others, Akatosh takes this role.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> While Lorkhan is the god of all mortals, [Arkay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arkay) is known as the Mortals' God, and did not come into being before the creation of the mortal world. For the Redguards, this is relationship is detailed in [Tu'whacca](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tu%27whacca) taking Sep's role as guide on the Walkabout after the creation of the mortal world.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> Lorkhan is almost always associated with [Padomay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Padomay), across various cultural interpretations.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-VOF-9)</sup> It is thought that Lorkhan may still act in Mundus through mortal incarnations, known in Cyrodiil as the [Shezarrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarrine).<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-TSOP-80)</sup>

[Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) is an extremely hard, durable, black glass-like substance said to be the crystallized blood of a god or gods,<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-MWEBony-81)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-HBNW-82)</sup> Lorkhan in particular.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-CM22T-83)</sup> [Skyshards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skyshard) and Sky Prisms are a rare type of [meteoric glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meteoric_Glass). Sometimes called "Shards of Aetherius", skyshards are shards of Aetherial magicka that carry the essence of Nirn. They are sometimes linked to Lorkhan.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-SSIC-84)</sup> It is believed among the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) that [Nirncrux](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirncrux) is another form of the blood of [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor).<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ONRH-85)</sup> Orc followers of Trinimac accent their armor and weapons with polished ebony in token of Lorkhan's blood when Trinimac tore out his Heart.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-CM22T-83)</sup>

## Uses
The shattering of Lorkhan<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-SOTR7-92)</sup> is believed by some to have resulted in many components of his divine being or essence to be spread across Tamriel. These include famous materials such as Ebony, Nirncrux, Skyshards, and Heart Stones. These are some of their uses.

### Ebony
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/MW-ing-Raw_Ebony.jpg/200px-MW-ing-Raw_Ebony.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-ing-Raw_Ebony.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-ing-Raw_Ebony.jpg) Raw ebony (Morrowind) [Ebony](Lore_Ebony.md), a rare volcanic glass, is one of the most precious substances in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), mainly found in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), buried in the lava flows from [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). Veins can also be found in [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) and occasionally on mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Ebony is also known to occur on the planes of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), such as in the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md). Raw ebony itself is an extremely hard, durable, black glass-like substance. It is also known as **Godsblood**, as it is said to be the crystallized blood of a god or gods, or Lorkhan in particular.

### Nirncrux
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl%2C_Nirncrux.png/200px-ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl%2C_Nirncrux.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl,_Nirncrux.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Reach_Bowl,_Nirncrux.png) Bowl of nirncrux powder [Nirncrux](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nirncrux), commonly called **red brittle**, **shake stone**, and **miner's bane**, is a red stone found only in [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn). In its raw form, it releases a poisonous gas when cracked which can cause symptoms ranging from nausea, body tremors, and disorientation to convulsions, slurred speech, memory loss and death. One theory on why Nirncrux is abundant in Craglorn is that the [Nedes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) of the area used arcane rituals to draw it from deep within Nirn's crust. Regardless of the validity of this speculation, the Nedes learned how to harness its ability to absorb and distribute [Aetherial energy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).

### Skyshards
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/LG-cardart-Skyshard.png/200px-LG-cardart-Skyshard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Skyshard.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Skyshard.png) A skyshard (Legends) [Skyshards](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skyshard) and **Sky Prisms** are a rare type of [meteoric glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meteoric_Glass). Sometimes called "Shards of [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius)", skyshards are shards of Aetherial magicka that carry the essence of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md). They are sometimes linked to Lorkhan or [Anu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anu).

Like other [aetherial fragments](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherial_Fragments), Sky Prisms fall to Nirn from Aetherius through the stars, although only during specific lunar alignments. As they fall, the Prisms can be seen splitting into three shards. If three skyshards are subsequently brought together, they reform into a silvery prism and confer the magical energy to any nearby being. As well as allowing mortals to become more powerful through the creation of prisms, absorbing the power of even a single skyshard allows a [Soul Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Shriven) to be re-attuned to Anuic magic and thus return to Nirn from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).

Although usually found outdoors, skyshards are often discovered and brought underground by hoarders. The [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) were known to harness skyshards in some way. They can also be summoned through ritual, even in Oblivion. Realms such as [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) and the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) have a significant number of inexplicable fallen skyshards. It is believed that Breton warriors used to incorporate skyshard slivers in the hilts of their swords.

### Heart Stones
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Heart_Stone.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Heart_Stone.png) Heart Stone A [Heart Stone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_Stone) (also spelled **Heartstone**) is an unusual type of ore mined from deposits which appeared in [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). Though deposits of them have been known to exist in the [Brothers of Strife](Lore_Brothers_of_Strife_(place).md) ruins in [Stonefalls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stonefalls) circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). It is suspected that the deposits on Solstheim surfaced (or *landed* on the surface) during the cataclysms of the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md); Master [Neloth](Lore_Neloth.md) of [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) suspected that the material came from [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) and was flung along with other materials to Solstheim during its eruption, and may continue to land on Solstheim from smaller eruptions. Even two centuries later, they still smolder with the heat of the mountain. As the molten rocks had laid next to the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) for millennia, some vestige of its power has seeped into them. Heart stones sometimes raise [Ash Spawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Spawn) from the bits of bone buried in the nearby ash. Through trial and error, Neloth's apprentice Talvas Fathryon learned that a stone was needed to prevent an [ash guardian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Guardian) summoned through [Conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration) from attacking the caster. The stones can also be used for enchanting purposes. Neloth's previous apprentice, [Ildari Sarothril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ildari_Sarothril), was killed during an experiment and brought back to life when one of these stones was grafted to her. Ildari learned that a stone could be used to prevent [necromantic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) spells from wearing off. She used them to bolster her necromantic spells used to revive [Falx Carius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Falx_Carius). Those revived with the stones in their chest felt constant pain, and had "voices" in their head, often telling them to distrust others.

## Artifacts

### Heart of Lorkhan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/27/MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg/200px-MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-creature-Heart_of_Lorkhan.jpg) The Heart of Lorkhan The [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) is the "divine spark" of the deity most commonly known by his [Aldmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) name, Lorkhan. It appears in other cultures as concepts such as Heart of [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor), the "hunger" of [Sep](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sep), and others. The "star-wounded East" gained its name when the Heart fell from the sky like a [meteor](Lore_Meteors.md), crashing into the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) region in ancient times, where it formed [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).

In the mythology of [the Towers](Lore_The_Towers.md), the Heart of Lorkhan was known as the First Stone, the Stone of Red Tower (Red Mountain), a focal point for the Tower's reality altering power, upon which the rules according to which the Tower shaped its domain, were inscribed. The First Stone and its Tower are said to have allowed [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) to exist without the full presence of the divine, and granted it a special kind of divinity, which is called "NIRN", the "consequence of variable [fate](Lore_Fate.md)". Some sources claim that [Heroes](Lore_Hero.md) are created by the Heart of Lorkhan as a defense mechanism for the mortal world, and that the Shade of [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr) shrouds their doom.

The Heart of Lorkhan was discovered in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), who fashioned [tools](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac%27s_Tools) to harness its power. The Dwemer wanted to use this power to create a [new god of brass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anumidium), but [disappeared](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md) before it could be activated. The Heart was later used by the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) and [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) to become gods themselves.

Thereafter, the Heart of Lorkhan was visited annually by the Tribunal in order to renew their powers, until [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882), when [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) awoke and ambushed the Tribunal. The event resulted in the Tribunal being cut off from access to the Heart of Lorkhan. [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) responded by beginning development on a new Heart of Lorkhan, called the [Mechanical Heart](Lore_Mechanical_Heart.md), as a backup to the loss of the original. Dagoth Ur would later attempt to create his own brass god, [Akulakhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Akulakhan). This plan was thwarted in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) by the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), and as a result, the Heart of Lorkhan seemingly vanished from [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).

### Dark Heart
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/db/ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_05.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Dark_Heart_05.jpg) The Dark Heart being freed from its Dwemeric container The [Dark Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Heart), also known as the **Frightful Heart**, is an artifact associated with [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) through some of the cultural interpretations of Lorkhan, specifically [Lorkh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkh) and [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj). Physically appearing as a sphere of darkness surrounded by a radiant aquamarine corona, it is a piece of primal [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void) trapped on Nirn that warps existence around it. According to ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md), Namira became a shadowy imitation of [Lorkhan's heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), while Reach belief holds that the darkness left his heart after his sacrifice for the creation of [the realm of flesh](Lore_Mundus.md). Regardless, Lorkhan's real heart was flung towards what is now [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and created [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md), while the Dark Heart found its way into the depths of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md) and remains under the influence of Namira.

The Dark Heart offers a pathway into the Void, and its power is increased by consuming souls, banishing them to that place between worlds.

Those tied to the heart describe hearing intrusive whispers, and can feel its pulsing and throbbing which grows stronger as the Heart becomes more powerful. Additionally, the power of the Dark Heart can be drawn from at the risk of being consumed by it. Those who tap into the Dark Heart's power may experience uncanny physical side effects. In the case of the [Nighthollow Clan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nighthollow_Clan), their [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) appearance was overshadowed: their skin grew dull and grey, and black ichor swirled within their eyes. The clan's matriarch, a [Vampire Lord](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord), developed glowing green eyes and flesh seemingly devoured by a creeping darkness when she transformed.

### Amulet of Kings
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/OB-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg/200px-OB-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Amulet_of_Kings.jpg) Chim-el Adabal The [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md),<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Manual Arena-93)</sup><sup>:2</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Ao K4-94)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-Ao KESO-95)</sup> also called the **Amulet of the Kings of Glory**,<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-TCA-96)</sup> was a pendant traditionally worn by the ruling emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Set in its center was the **Chim-el Adabal**, also known as the **Red Diamond**,<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-Ao K-97)</sup> a huge [soul gem](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul_Gems) of [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) origin, and [Aedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) relic, said to be the polished crystallized blood of Lorkhan.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-CEA-68)</sup> Some claim it was Lorkhan in his Cyrodilic aspect as Shezarr, that met [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) at her Deathbed, making her the first stone of the Amulet of Kings and forging the [Covenant](Lore_Dragonfires.md). <sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TSOP8-71)</sup><sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-FET-72)</sup>

### Bow of the Fox
[Bow of the Fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bow_of_the_Fox), also known as **Bow of Shor**, is a legendary weapon linked to [Shor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shor) wielded by Njor the Brave, celebrated in the legends and songs of the [Haefriding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Haefriding) [Nords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nords). According to the legend it manifested from a dream, transforming from the visage of the [fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fox) into a bow and a set of arrows. The tales describe the bow as glowing, and gleaming with power.

## Associated Realms and Celestial Bodies
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/93/ON-place-Den_of_Lorkhaj.jpg/200px-ON-place-Den_of_Lorkhaj.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Den_of_Lorkhaj.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Den_of_Lorkhaj.jpg) The Den of Lorkhaj [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/MER-art-The_World.jpg/200px-MER-art-The_World.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_World.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_World.jpg) Nirn, the creation of Lorkhan, Akatosh coiled around it [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), the mortal plane of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) itself, is Lorkhan's most renowned and controversial plane of existence. He is often considered the Spirit of Nirn,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SONGOM-2)</sup> or the God of Mundus,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Com3-42)</sup> and it is said his Heart has become the Heart of the World.

[Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md) is one of Lorkhan's realms, tied to his culturally Nordic aspect of Shor, it is said to be a realm of Aetherius<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-98)</sup> though an obscure source once claimed it to reside on the moons.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-FPKirkbride-16)</sup> Also called the Underworld, Shor, as a trickster, wielded his clever [magic](Lore_Magic.md) to create [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TRt S-55)</sup>

The twin [moons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moons), [Masser](Lore_Masser.md) and [Secunda](Lore_Secunda.md), are alleged remains of the planetary realm of Lorkhan. Per the Lunar Lorkhan theory, the original moon was cracked into two by the removal of his heart. The Cloven-Duality of the moons is acknowledged by the Psijics of Artaeum as well as Vivec.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TLL-17)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-V32-99)</sup> The [Dark Moon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Moon), also called the Den of Lorkhaj, is the other rumored plantery realm of Lorkhan. Particularly associated with his culturally Khajiiti aspect of Lorkhaj.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOShazah-48)</sup> The Dwemer referred to Lorkhan's celestial body as **LHKAN**,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TLL-17)</sup><sup>[[91]](#cite_note-V3-100)</sup> though what part of him specifically this was referring to is unclear.

The [Serpent Constellation](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Serpent), is tied to Lorkhan by numerous cultures such as the Yokudans, the Argonians, and the Dunmer. Vivec's writings describe Lorkhan as dwelling within the "Hidden Heaven" of the Serpent Constellation, as the Void Ghost who hides in the religions of all men.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-V33-101)</sup>
