# House Mornard


## History
[![ON-banner-House Mornard.png](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/ON-banner-House_Mornard.png/125px-ON-banner-House_Mornard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-banner-House_Mornard.png) Prior to their arrival in the Systres, the Mornards controlled a valuable stretch of land between [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), known for its rich veins of high-quality metals and precious stones. Mornard prospectors often crossed the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) to exploit the [Dragontail Mountains](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragontail_Mountains)' bounty of ore, which was said to be an underhanded practice that earned them no friends in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Even so, it is said the rulers of Wayrest profited from this arrangement and did little to interfere with it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mornard-1)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/ON-place-Galen_Architecture.jpg/200px-ON-place-Galen_Architecture.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Galen_Architecture.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Galen_Architecture.jpg) Vastyr When the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md) came to power in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), the Mornards quickly established a closer relationship with [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Mornard [coin-barons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Coin-barons) helped settle [Reman](Lore_Reman_Dynasty.md) accounts in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), which amounted to thousands of gold pieces in [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) taxes. While it is said that many noble houses resented this, they also saw the utility of closer ties with the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md). Wayrest, in particular, indulged the Mornards' schemes and leveraged the family's connections with [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie)'s regime to secure valuable trade deals all across western [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). When the Akaviri Potentate began seizing errant states and territories in High Rock and beyond, the Mornard family assisted them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mornard-1)</sup>

By [2E 110](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_110), a patriarch of House Mornard, Phane Mornard, was instated to be the Imperial Governor of the Systres,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mornard-1)</sup> with another House, Dufort, arriving to the archipelago in [2E 125](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_125). As time passed, both families of the archipelago worked together to turn High Isle into a thriving settlement. Thanks to Dufort ships, Mornard trade flourished into a vast network with operations in every significant Tamrielic port from [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) to [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md), as well as in [Summerset](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Summerset). As Mornard influence grew, officials back in High Rock decided that the Systres needed legitimate noble claims to truly prosper, and would eventually be granted noble titles, with House Mornard becoming Dukes of the Systres as vassals of the Gardner kings of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) and lieges of the Dufort Barons.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dufort-3)</sup> Once House Mornard gained oversight of the archipelago, they increased their own wealth and power a thousandfold. Already operating mines on Amenos, the duke awarded his house exclusive mining rights to the island and also took control of the prison facilities located there.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mornard-1)</sup> House Dufort, meanwhile, provided the fast ships Mornard required to get their goods from market to market, and even secured a contract to provide warships for High Rock. The partnership turned into a rivalry between Mornard and Dufort as Mornard's power and influence grew.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dufort-3)</sup>

While the family's main concerns were the manor house on High Isle or the mining operations on Amenos, many within the house felt a strong connection to [druidic](Lore_Druid.md) culture thanks to Phane's grandmother Dorona. She was said to have deep ties to [the Wyrd](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Wyrd) and [the Green](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Green), and insisted the family invest in Vastyr for the good of the druids as well as for the familial line.

Mornard coin proved invaluable a few hundred years later in the wake of the Gathering Storm's siege of the city. The fall of the Empire created chaos across Tamriel. The Systreans had to weather a storm just as bad as the Tamsfolk, as would-be rulers and pretender kings assaulted the archipelago no less than six times in a hundred year span. The sacking of Vastyr in [2E 365](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_365) was one of the most violent moments in the island's history, seeing half the city destroyed and most of its citizens scattered to the wilderness.

While the later actions of Duke Ruffe Mornard would speak to a despotic manner, his ancestor was revered as the rebuilder and patron of a new Vastyr. Bernique was known as the "mother of the city" for decades after the siege, as coin and comforts from across Mornard holdings allowed the city to rebuild and refine their place in the world. The towering walls at either end of Vastyr Bay are remnants of this era, as is the expansive manor that now dominates the city's skyline.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/ON-npc-Count_Leonard_Mornard.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Count_Leonard_Mornard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Count_Leonard_Mornard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Count_Leonard_Mornard.jpg) Count Leonard Mornard As vassals of the [Gardner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gardner) dynasty, the dukes of House Mornard nominally owed their allegiance to the [kingdom](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). However, communication between [the Systres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Systres) Archipelago and the mainland remained unreliable in the early years of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Diplomatic missions were routinely waylaid by [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) freebooters and [Sea Elf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sea_Elf) marauders. When messages did arrive, it's said that House Mornard often ignored them, claiming they were lost at sea or intercepted by Elven spies. This inconsistent diplomacy allowed House Mornard to establish total hegemony over the islands, with one notable exception—the properties of [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-4)</sup>

The following years saw the partnership of the two houses devolve into a rivalry, attributed by some to the madness of Baron Densil Dufort,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dufort-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Mad Baron-5)</sup> which was only exacerbated by the start of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430). House Mornard and House Dufort's jockeying for power in the court of Wayrest eventually came to a head in [2E 478](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_478), when Duke Ruffe Mornard seized a full third of House Dufort's lands and exiled its leadership to Amenos, claiming sedition against the crown with a raft of forged documents and forced testimony. The Gardners viewed the coup with suspicion but were unable to interfere due to troubles with an ascendant [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-4)</sup>

In [2E 563](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_563) the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) reached the Systres, slaying commoner and noble alike. Duke Avrippe Mornard ordered the archipelago sealed off in [2E 565](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_565), causing trade to stagnate, which caused Duke Avrippe to fall into disfavor in Wayrest's court. When King Ranser of Shornhelm attacked Wayrest in [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566) Duke Avrippe allied with him, forsaking ties of vassalage, but King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Wayrest managed to bring together [an alliance](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) to defeat Ranser. Once matters on the mainland were settled and Ranser had been defeated Emeric turned his eyes to the Systres, stripping Duke Avrippe of his titles and exiling him to Cyrodiil. The duchy was granted to House Dufort, while Avrippe's son Leonard was left with a county and rule over the island of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md) and their operations in [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Dufort-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SH-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SOA-6)</sup>
