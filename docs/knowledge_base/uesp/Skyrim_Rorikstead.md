[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg/200px-SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Rorikstead.jpg) Rorikstead **Rorikstead** is a farming [town](Skyrim_Towns.md) in western [Whiterun Hold](Skyrim_Whiterun_Hold.md).

The town is named after the noble [Rorik](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rorik), who owns a local "[manor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rorik%27s_Manor)". Despite its living namesake, Rorikstead is mentioned in two much older accounts: The [Merethic](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) or [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) text *[Holdings of Jarl Gjalund](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Holdings_of_Jarl_Gjalund)* (as "Rorik's Steading") and the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) text *[Atlas of Dragons](Skyrim_Atlas_of_Dragons.md)*. It is also mentioned in the bard's song *[Ragnar the Red](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Songs_of_Skyrim)* as "ole Rorikstead", which may indicate the current town is named after an ancient predecessor. The town contains several crop fields, and the residents claim not to have had a bad harvest in years.

## Notes
- With *[Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md)* installed, you can use the grain mills here to make [flour](Skyrim_Sack_of_Flour.md).
- Atop the hill north of the town is a [shrine of Akatosh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Shrine_of_Akatosh) featuring a [dragon scale](Skyrim_Dragon_Scales.md) and a copy of the book *[Kolb & the Dragon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kolb_%26_the_Dragon)*. This is the only place where you can obtain a dragon scale prior to completing the quest [Dragon Rising](Skyrim_Dragon_Rising.md).
- After the quest Dragon Rising, you are likely to encounter a [dragon](Skyrim_Dragon.md) attacking Rorikstead. Fittingly, [guards](Skyrim_Guards.md) in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) will [mention](Skyrim_Guard_Dialogue.md#Town-Specific) how Rorikstead is usually a calm place but is vulnerable to a dragon attack. Throughout most of the game, this encounter is likely to be with a random unnamed dragon; however, the named dragon [Nahagliiv](Skyrim_Nahagliiv.md) is buried in a [dragon mound](Skyrim_Dragon_Mounds.md#Rorikstead_Resurrection) not far west of the town and will be resurrected by [Alduin](Skyrim_Alduin.md) during the quest [Alduin's Wall](Skyrim_Alduin%27s_Wall.md).<sup>[*verification needed — Do the game mechanics have a special heightened ratio of dragon attacks for Rorikstead?*]</sup>
- [Lokir](Skyrim_Lokir.md), one of the NPCs in the carriage during the quest [Unbound](Skyrim_Unbound.md), states he is from Rorikstead, but he has no residence and is not mentioned by anyone in town.
