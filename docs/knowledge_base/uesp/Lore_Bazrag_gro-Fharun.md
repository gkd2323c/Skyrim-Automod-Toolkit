Bazrag gro-Fharun was the clan chief of [Fharun Stronghold](Lore_Fharun.md) and the King of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md); he was a traditionalist and worshipper of [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md). Once a staunch ally of [Kurog](Lore_Kurog_gro-Orsinium.md), he eventually came to oppose Kurog's rule and the worship of [Trinimac](Lore_Trinimac.md), as he felt it was a plot by the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) to spread their religion to [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md). Unlike Kurog, he had the backing of the Orcish clans due to his traditionalist stances, and for this reason, he was considered equal to Kurog in terms of influence. Bazrag believed that patience and adherence to tradition were key to uniting the Orcish clans and rebuilding Orsinium.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mtc CB-1)</sup> He was also a pro player in the Orcish sport of [Vosh Ball](Lore_Vosh_Ball.md), winning seven "series of the clans" by wide margins, and had a unique technique known as "the bone-breaker".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TSo C-2)</sup>

## History
Before he became chief, a young Bazrag enrolled as an initiate with the Oathsworn Pit. He was taught by Black-Glove Garm and sparred with him on multiple occassions. Tired of the mock battles he partook in, he left the Pit to gain real combat experience in the field, though he noted he would have been happy had he stayed at the Pit.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Bazrag-3)</sup> He took on the title **Bazrag the Butcher** and fought side by side with friends Kurog, Bumnog, and Bizra as mercenaries of Gaspard's Stalkers across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) as far as [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Bumnog-4)</sup> Eventually, Bazrag became the clan chief of Fharun, and later Kurog struck a deal with King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md), which led to a falling out between the two of them when Kurog proclaimed himself king of the Orsimer people, an action that Bazrag admits saved many lives.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Mtc CB-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Bazrag-3)</sup> Afterwards, given his traditionalist stances, he would oppose Kurog's efforts to force the conversion of Trinimac, and would be a thorn in Kurog's side, as he attempted to reunite the Orcish people.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Kurog-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Bazrag-3)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> he would come to the defense of a caravan headed for Orsinium, that had much needed supplies the Orcish clan depended on in order to survive. He was too late, to stop the [Winterborn](Lore_Winterborn.md) attack. He would then task the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) with dealing with the traitor in Orsinium who was secretly working with the Winterborn.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-It O-6)</sup> He would then return to Fharun only to find it being under attack by the [Vosh Rakh](Lore_Vosh_Rakh.md), a cult of Trinimac extremists. He would then be captured and taken prisoner, and he would be locked away in his own private prison beneath Fhaurn. Kurog and his mother would then frame the Cult of Malacath and Bazrag for the attack on the Trinimac temple in Orsinium. This was done to discredit the worship of Malacath and give Kurog more leverage to achieve his goals of forced conversion.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TAoa K-7)</sup>

A priestess of Trinimac, wanting to prevent the Vosh Rakh, from ruining the reputation of innocent Trinimac worshipers, tasked the Vestige with freeing Bazrag and uncovering the truth.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Eventually the Kings betrayal was discovered and the Vestige, alongside, Chief Bazrag would prevent Kurog from killing all the clan chiefs who refused to bow down at the King's moot. Kurog would then be slain by the Vestige.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Boa KH-9)</sup> Afterwards, the clan chiefs would personally ask Bazrag to take on Kurag's mantle of king, which he hesitantly accepted, and ultimately making Kurog's dream of a united Orsimer people a reality.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Lft K-10)</sup>

King Bazrag, alongside the Vestige, managed to defend the Oathsworn Pit from the Recollection's invasion, though the [Staff of Many Paths](Lore_Staff_of_Many_Paths.md) was stolen.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Bazrag-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VTS-11)</sup> Later, guided by a vision from Malacath, he contacted Leramil the Wise to inform her of what had transpired in the Oathsworn Pit.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Bazrag-3)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-POF-12)</sup>
