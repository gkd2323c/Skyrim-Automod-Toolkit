# Reman Dynasty

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/LO-misc-Reman_Dynasty_Tombs.png/200px-LO-misc-Reman_Dynasty_Tombs.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Reman_Dynasty_Tombs.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Reman_Dynasty_Tombs.png) The Tomb of the Reman Emperors in Sancre Tor The Reman Dynasty was founded by [Reman Cyrodiil](Lore_Reman.md) after he defeated the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) invasion of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) at the Battle of [Pale Pass](Lore_Pale_Pass.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703). Until that point, Tamriel had been divided into several small kingdoms, but once Reman had brought the armies of [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and the [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md) together to defeat the invasion, he was able to unite all of Tamriel's human inhabitants into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) in the face of possible aggression from the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1C-1)</sup>

Although the Battle of Pale Pass established Reman as the leading power in Tamriel, many parts of the continent refused to acknowledge his rule. Valenwood was not subdued until [1E 2714](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2714).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE3V-2)</sup> Reman was succeeded by Emperor [Kastav](Lore_Kastav.md), who was regarded as incompetent and was responsible for provoking the Winterhold Rebellion in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). In [1E 2804](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2804), Kastav was deposed by Emperor [Reman II](Lore_Reman_II.md), who negotiated an end to the strife with the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) and ushered in an era of prosperity.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-R2TLOA-3)</sup> In [1E 2811](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2811), Cyrodilic forces defeated the last organized army of [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) at the [Battle of Argonia](Lore_Battle_of_Argonia.md), and the following year saw Argonia admitted to the Empire as a province under the name "[Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md)".<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3A-4)</sup> The rest of the province was conquered piecemeal, with the whole area officially declared annexed by the Empire in [1E 2837](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2837).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-R2TLOA-3)</sup>

The last war of conquest began around [1E 2840](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2840), when Reman II embarked on a campaign to subjugate [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), the only province of Tamriel left outside of Imperial control. Progress in the resulting [Four-Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) was slow, and the three living gods of Morrowind's [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md), and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) proved to be extremely capable leaders.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLYOTFE-6)</sup> Reman II perished in battle against the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) in [1E 2843](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2843), but the war continued under his successors, [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md) and [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-R2TLOA-3)</sup> By [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), both sides were approaching exhaustion, and after the Empire captured the fortress of [Ald Marak](Lore_Ald_Marak.md), a truce was agreed upon to bring an end to the war.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLYOTFE-6)</sup>

Shortly after the truce, Crown Prince [Juilek](Lore_Juilek.md), son of Emperor Reman III, was mistaken for his father by an assassin and slain. Reman III himself was assassinated soon afterward on the orders of his chief advisor, Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md), which brought an end to the Reman Dynasty and instigated the period of the Second Empire known as the [Akaviri Potentate](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TLYOTFE-6)</sup> The remains of Reman I, II, and III were interred in a chamber deep within [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md), along with two other emperors whose tombs were unlabeled.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Oblivion-7)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_tombfive)</sup>

## Notes
1. | [^](#note_tombfive) | The only known emperors under this dynasty other than Reman I, II, and III were [Brazollus Dor](Lore_Brazollus_Dor.md) and [Kastav](Lore_Kastav.md), which implies they are the two other emperors entombed with Reman. |
| --- | --- |
2. | [^](#note_remantitle) | Although Reman Cyrodiil founded the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) and Reman Dynasty, he never took the title of emperor in his lifetime according to [The Book of the Dragonborn](Lore_The_Book_of_the_Dragonborn.md). |
| --- | --- |
3. | [^](#note_kcrown) | Reman II's epitaph states that he was crowned emperor in 1E 2812 and reigned for 39 years, but according to *[Reman II: The Limits of Ambition](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II__The_Limits_of_Ambition)*, Reman II deposed Kastav in 1E 2804 and died in 1E 2843. The most likely explanation is that Reman II deposed Kastav in 1E 2804, but he was only officially crowned emperor after Kastav's death in 1E 2812. |
| --- | --- |
4. | [^](#note_remanada) | The [*Remanada*](Lore_Remanada.md) contains an mythical story of Reman emerging from [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md) as a child born from the union of King [Hrol](Lore_Hrol.md) and the spirit of the deceased Saint [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), but the veracity of this source is disputed. [*The Book of the Dragonborn*](Lore_The_Book_of_the_Dragonborn.md) claims that the tale of Hrol and Alessia was conjured to legitimize Reman's rule. |
| --- | --- |
5. | [^](#note_kastavheir) | Sources are vague about the exact familial connection between Reman I, Kastav, and Reman II, though it is known that all three are members of the Reman Dynasty, indicating a common bloodline. In the absence of any evidence to the contrary, this chart depicts Reman II as a son of Kastav, and Kastav as a son of Reman I. |
| --- | --- |
6. | [^](#note_brazollus) | Brazollus Dor's reign was placed between Reman II and Reman III by [High King Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) in [High King Emeric Answers Your Questions](Lore_High_King_Emeric_Answers_Your_Questions.md). Despite being ruler, nearly all issues of policy and politics were delegated to his Potentate, [Sidri-Ashak](Lore_Sidri-Ashak.md). |
| --- | --- |
7. | [^](#note_grandfather) | In [2920, Sun's Dawn](Lore_2920,_Sun%27s_Dawn_(v2).md), Reman III is sullen after the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) Battlechief of [Soulrest](Lore_Soulrest.md) stepped on his foot, and declares, "My father would have flogged him. My grandfather would have had him killed," as punishment for offending royalty. This implies that Reman III's father and grandfather were of the same royal standing as Reman III himself, and were therefore emperors. As Reman III's two immediate predecessors were Emperor Brazollus Dor and Emperor Reman II, it is reasonable to conclude that Brazollus Dor was Reman III's father, and Reman II was his grandfather. |
| --- | --- |
8. During the mid-Second Era, collectible sets of nesting dolls representing each member of the Reman Dynasty could be found.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cont Reman Nesting-15)</sup>
