# Knights of the Thorn

[![OB-banner-Knights of the Thorn.jpg](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e1/OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg/100px-OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Knights_of_the_Thorn.jpg) *We strike fast and true as lightning.*
—Farwil Indarys' favorite saying<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SIPyke-1)</sup> The Knights of the Thorn are a chivalrous order dedicated to the protection of [Cheydinhal](Lore_Cheydinhal.md), in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). They own a lodge just outside the city gates. The order's crest is a sword enveloped in thorns; thorns are also symbolically related to the [Indarys](Lore_Indarys.md) family and one of its heirlooms was called [Thornblade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thornblade). Every member of the order wears a special medallion, and are typically outfitted with steel shields emblazoned with their coat-of-arms.

## History
The Knights were founded as an amateur order in [3E 431](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_431) by Farwil Indarys, the son of [Count Andel Indarys](Lore_Andel_Indarys.md). The group were considered by the town guard to be nothing more than a glorified club, and by many of the town's citizens to be only Farwil's drinking buddies, as they were arrogant and rarely performed any noteworthy deeds.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OBAmminus-2)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) knight named Pyke learned of a strange portal which had appeared in [Niben Bay](Lore_Niben_Bay.md). Perceiving it as a threat, he departed to investigate. The portal was a door to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), which had been opened by [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) in search of a mortal champion. Pyke passed into the Isles and began wandering the realm. Having apparently not succumbed to madness, he eventually settled in the village of [Hale](Lore_Hale_(settlement).md), where he fell in love and decided to never return to Cyrodiil. He contracted the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) with retrieving his lost Knights of the Thorn medallion, and in return he gave the Hero the Thorn Shield, an enchanted steel shield.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SIHero-3)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), an Oblivion gate was opened right beside the Knights of the Thorn Headquarters outside Cheydinhal. Count Andel Indarys tasked the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) with entering the gate to rescue Farwil and six other knights who had foolheartedly stormed the portal. The Hero led Farwil and a knight named Bremman Senyan into the Citadel to remove the [Sigil Stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md) and close the Gate. Consequently, Farwil made the Hero an honorary Knight of the Thorn,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBWayward-4)</sup> and the story was published in a special edition of the [Black Horse Courier](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Black_Horse_Courier).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), only four Knights remained: Farwil, Bremman, the Hero, and a knight named Jhared Strongblade who had stayed behind.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBThorn-6)</sup>

In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), the Knights of the Thorn, led by Ilver Indarys, aided the city guard and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) in evacuating citizens when the floating city of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) passed over Cheydinhal and the city itself was besieged by [wormies](Lore_Wormies.md), Umbriel's undead minions. Riding barded horses, the twenty knights escorted the citizens along the [Blue Road](Lore_Blue_Road.md) towards the Imperial City. They saved two legionnaires, Mazgar gra Yagash and Brennus, from the wormies when the two were driven away from the road.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
