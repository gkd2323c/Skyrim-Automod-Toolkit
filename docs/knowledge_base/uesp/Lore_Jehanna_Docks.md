[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/82/AR-place-Jehanna.jpg/200px-AR-place-Jehanna.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Jehanna.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Jehanna.jpg) Jehanna during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8e/LO-misc-highrock1.jpg/200px-LO-misc-highrock1.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-highrock1.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-highrock1.jpg) A sketch of a Breton by [¥R](Lore_%C2%A5R.md) Jehanna (also called **Jehenna**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ADIF4-2)</sup> or **Jelhana**)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-LOMap-3)</sup> is one of the nine historical [Breton kingdoms](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md) in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARMap-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-EWR-5)</sup> situated on the icy shores of the [Northern Coast](Lore_Northern_Coast.md). Nestled in the snow-covered forests of the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md), it is described as being as beautiful as its name.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AR-6)</sup>

## Layout & Geography
Jehanna lies on the northeast coast of High Rock, right near the border of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), in a generally northern biome.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-7)</sup> An isolated dock that bore Jehanna's name was nestled between the base of Mount [Sorrow](Lore_Sorrow.md), the tallest peak of Wrothgar and the mountain that holds the Fharun clanhold, the ancestral territory of the [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) [Fharun Clan](Lore_Fharun_Clan.md). It sat at the end of a lightly-forested valley and lay on the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), near the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md). At the end of an off-beaten path, east of the dock is an old ruin called [Coldwind's Den](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Coldwind%27s_Den), the tomb of the legendary mount, [Coldwind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Coldwind). Jehanna is neighbored by several settlements, including [Cloud Spring](Lore_Cloud_Spring.md) to the southwest, [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md) to the west, and Skyrim to the east, towards [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). Ancient ruins such as Pariah's Pinnacle lie to the south, and the [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) is found to the east.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARMap-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ARMap Skyrim-8)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-place-Jehanna_Docks.jpg/200px-ON-place-Jehanna_Docks.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jehanna_Docks.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Jehanna_Docks.jpg) Jehanna Docks circa 2E 582 The Western Reach was conquered by the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), and incorporated into Skyrim's holdings,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1E-9)</sup> prompting many [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) colonials to settle in the region until they were viciously displaced by the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md). The fall of the Hegemony fractured High Rock into various fiefdoms of walled city-states.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PGE1E-9)</sup> Jehanna as a name is recognized among the people of Tamriel,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PE-10)</sup> and by the scholars in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LEo ERZI-11)</sup> The Jehanna Road is a major roadway that runs near the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) in [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) and rolls northeast into the Wrothgarian Mountains near the [Murtag Stronghold](Lore_Murtag_Stronghold.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TGE-12)</sup> According to High Rock lore, during rituals to imbue white elks, moonlight glints off glittering snowflakes with the intelligence of ghosts captured from the frigid seas near Jehanna.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-PE-10)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), an isolated dock with the name Jehanna Docks could be found just to the east of [Fharun Stronghold](Lore_Fharun_Stronghold.md). By [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the modern-day kingdom of Jehanna had been marked on maps of the province.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOMap PGE1E-13)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) to the late- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Jehanna was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Bedyctor and had a rivalry with [Farrun](Lore_Farrun.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AR-6)</sup> When the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) occurred at the time, the land around Jehanna and other nations like [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) were engulfed in warfare with the Nordic warlords of nearby Skyrim. By the time, [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) returned to the throne in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399), Jehanna had fallen under the control of Skyrim and the landscape was swiftly claimed by their Counts. By the waning years of the Third Era, resistance in the cities was feeble at best, but the fight remained strong toward the border.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE3-1)</sup>

By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), the Nordic Ice-Blade clan had come to own land east of the city of Jehanna, but this would be threatened when the clan's heir, [Pjofr Ice-Blade](Lore_Pjofr_Ice-Blade.md), abandoned his holdings to take up the mantle of [the Gray Fox](Lore_The_Gray_Fox.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SJ-14)</sup> Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), a plot against the clan was put into action by a group of associates led by the siblings Erikur and Gisli of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). The plotters poisoned Pjofr's sister, leaving her young son Smaref Ice-Blade as the heir to the clan's titles and wealth. Smaref was then used as a political puppet, allowing the plotters to make the necessary land transfers in order to construct a new port east of Jehanna. The orphaned Smaref was sent to [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) to live his life in relative comfort, unaware of what had occurred.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> These actions forced Pjofr to relinquish his role as the Gray Fox to [another](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), who proved themselves by aiding in the reclamation of the land deed. With his life of crime behind him, Pjofr adopted Smaref and returned to the Ice-Blade clan, prepared to take the responsibilities as the new leader.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SJ-14)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

## Notes
- Jehanna is the home of Sir Sienne, whose adventures were documented in the book *The Travels of Sir Sienne of Jehanna,* written by the author Gidan.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-EST-17)</sup>
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Jehanna's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Iceblades".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-18)</sup>
- A [slideshow of screenshots](https://images.uesp.net/5/56/ARENASLD.zip) from Arena's Beta contains a picture of a [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) player character named [Jehanna Khel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-prerelease-Character.gif), which perhaps lent (or borrowed) its name to the city.
- Design boards for [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online) in 2014 show concept art of a planned Jehanna zone depicting a moonlit forest with dead trees.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Schick Interview-19)</sup>
