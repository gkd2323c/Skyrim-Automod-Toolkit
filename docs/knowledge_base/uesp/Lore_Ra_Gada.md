# Ra Gada

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/RG-comic-Page_24-Ra_Gada.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_24-Ra_Gada.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_24-Ra_Gada.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_24-Ra_Gada.jpg) The Ra Gada *No pity or mercy shall be afforded the wretch who stands against the Warrior Wave*
— *[The Unveiled Azadiyeh](Lore_Plea_for_Open_Eyes.md)* The Ra Gada (sometimes spelled **Ra' Gada**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SK-1)</sup> or **Ra'Gada**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OM-2)</sup> was the name given to the Yokudan "**Warrior Waves**" that left the doomed continent of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) and sailed towards [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). This mass colonization occurred in four successive waves during the eighth and ninth centuries of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and spanned approximately twenty eight years.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CM28RG-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RTHATH-4)</sup> The native [Goblins](Lore_Goblin.md), [Orcs](Lore_Orcs.md), [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) and [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) fell to them quickly, as did the various creatures and beasts farther inland. Eventually, they claimed the entire province after only a few major battles and established a new home for the Yokudan people called [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3H-5)</sup>

The term Ra Gada became corrupted to [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md), and although it originally referred only to the warriors of the refugees, it gradually came to be used to refer to the whole race.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3H-5)</sup>

## The Warrior Waves

### Frandar Hunding
The first recorded wave of migration took place around [1E 780](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_780), prior to the destruction of Yokuda. After winning a war against Emperor [Hira](Lore_Hira.md), [Frandar Hunding](Lore_Frandar_Hunding.md) and his army of [sword-singers](Lore_Sword-singer.md) left their homeland from the grant port city of Arch in the province of [Seawind](Lore_Seawind.md) and sailed to Hammerfell.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RTHATH-4)</sup> Alongside him was his son [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md).

The warriors of the first Ra Gada briefly made landfall on the island of [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md) in [1E 785](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_785) and annihilated the starving remnants of an invading [Lefthanded Elf](Lore_Lefthanded_Elves.md) army, before continuing their journey east.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-So A-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSHV2-7)</sup>

Hunding's flotilla of ships landed in Hammerfell and discovered vast numbers of [giant goblin](Lore_Giant_Goblin.md) strongholds littering the sands. Frandar refused to co-exist with the [Flint-Tooth](Lore_Flint-Tooth_Tribe.md) tribe on their new homeland, and rallied his army to slay the beasts. Frandar was killed in one of the early conflicts, but Divad pushed onward and conquered the [Alik'r Desert](Lore_Alik%27r_Desert.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONTKF-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONRAG-9)</sup> Eventually Divad would be joined by more allies, renowned warriors such as [Ra Abah](Lore_Ra_Abah.md) who once fought with his father but arrived with the second wave,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONSai-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TCOH-11)</sup> and Razul.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONRAG-9)</sup>

### The Tavans
The next wave is believed to have occurred around the time of Yokuda's sinking in [1E 792](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_792).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-12)</sup> This wave consisted of ships that departed from both the continent and its islands, including [Yath](Lore_Yath.md) and [Akos Kasaz](Lore_Akos_Kasaz.md), the largest island of Yokuda and home to its capital. The bulk of the refugees, including the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md) ruling class, were put ashore on the island of [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) while the Ra Gada sailed on to mainland [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1-13)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-12)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

The fleet first landed at [Hegathe](Lore_Hegathe.md) on the southwest coast of Hammerfell. From Hegathe they spread along both southern and western coasts, while others continued north and east into Hammerfell itself.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-STJOA-15)</sup>

The fifty-six ships of the second wave went to the southeast.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup> Also known as the "Tavan wave", the Tavans revered the Yokudan bird-god [Tava](Lore_Tava.md), and believed she guided them to Herne. As such, Tavan arms and armor were replete with bird imagery, sometimes referred to as "Tava Resplendent".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONSHV2-7)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CM28RG-3)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-VOF-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Cont Plat-18)</sup> Their fleet of fifty-six ships traveled towards [Hunding Bay](Lore_Hunding_Bay.md) and the southern coast of Hammerfell.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Cont Lode-19)</sup> The island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) was one of the first landfalls settled by the Yokudans.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONload Stros-20)</sup> They discovered the peninsula of [Khefrem's Boot](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md). Led by Prince [Hubalajad](Lore_Hubalajad.md), he established the city of [Abah's Landing](Lore_Abah%27s_Landing.md) and built a citadel named No Shira.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-IDOPH-21)</sup> Some accounts speak of a Ra Gada sect known as the Glinting Talons. These warriors also revered Tava and were possibly related to the Tavans. They were known to engrave their blades with the wings of a hawk, and employed a fighting style so aggressive that it left their enemies standing stunned. They fought in a conflict known as the Battle of Six Hands, but much of the details are lost to history.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-GT-22)</sup>

#### Yathian
*Ra Gada. You all know that the elves are outside our walls. These infidels call us barbarians. They would drive us from this land, but what the elves do not know is that Ruptga himself guided us to these shores. He decreed that the Alik'r would be our home. Today the infidels will see the strength of the Shehai. Onsi will guide our blades. Today the sands will run red with elven blood!*
— [King Xakhwan](Lore_Xakhwan.md)'s speech at the Ash'abah Pass What is considered the third wave of the Yokudan Ra Gada were those who sailed from [Yath](Lore_Yath.md) under Grandee [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-HCC2-23)</sup> Around the time the Tavans arrived in Hegathe, to the north went this wave of warrior-sailors, in ships they had brought from Akos Kasaz. Yaghoub was the first of the Ra Gada to discover the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) as he rounded [Cape Shira](Lore_Cape_Shira.md). Yaghoub, following the constellation of [the Steed](Lore_The_Steed.md), decided to make his home on the northern coast of Hammerfell and established the city of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-STJOA-15)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-LOTYC-24)</sup> Many of the Ra Gada kings were wealthy corsairs, and raided Breton coastal cities and merchant vessels along the southern Iliac Bay.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONYokudan-25)</sup> However on one occasion, [Peregrine Direnni](Lore_Peregrine_Direnni.md) drove an entire Ra Gada flotilla back to Sentinel by using her prodigious sorcery to manipulate the waves of the Iliac Bay.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-O-26)</sup>

The Ra Gada colonists from Yath clashed with an [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) clan known as the [Corelanya](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md). They quickly established settlements along the coasts of Cape Shira, and built a grand palace in a valley known as the Ash'abah Pass. They also attacked the Altmeri inhabitants of [Salas En](Lore_Salas_En.md), and established their own settlement within the ruins.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TSEE-27)</sup> The Yokudans pushed farther inland into [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) and discovered the ruins of Nilata, which were also inhabited by [Elves](Lore_Mer.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-ONload Nilata-28)</sup>

#### Anka-Ra
Arriving alongside the second wave of Ra Gada were some of its most ruthless warriors.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-CAC-29)</sup> Led by the self proclaimed emperor [Tarish-Zi](Lore_Tarish-Zi.md), the [Anka-Ra](Lore_Anka-Ra.md) cut a bloody swath across Hammerfell and all the way into [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md) in their search for a new home.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-WOTRG-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONload Helra-31)</sup> In their wake, the Nedes as a distinctive people disappeared from the historical record shortly after their arrival, although some accounts claim Nedic culture was already fading prior to the Ra-Gada.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-NOTDL-32)</sup> Of note are a band of prominent [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md) known as [The Four](Lore_This_Text_Property_of_Leki%27s_Blade.md). According to legend, their prowess with the Shehai was unmatched and they single-handedly captured Nedic fortifications, breached walls, and cut down entire armies.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TTPOLB-33)</sup>

The Anka-Ra established [Hel Ra Citadel](Lore_Hel_Ra_Citadel.md), a vast complex believed to be used as a training ground for their [Ansei](Lore_Ansei.md).<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-CAC-29)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-EJ-34)</sup>

## The Forebears
Once the Ra Gada had established footholds in Hammerfell, the Na-Totambu ruling council and the Yokudan nobility followed and rapidly resumed their traditional rule. In response the renowned warrior Razul, a contemporary of Divad Hunding, made himself Yokeda (or Lord) to cement the power of the Ra Gada.

After conquering Hammerfell, the Yokudans found themselves in a time of relative peace. Many Ra Gada put down their swords, in favor of their love of farming and building. Within three generations, many Yokudans forgot the teachings of the Way of the Sword, leaving them vulnerable until the time of [Derik Hallin](Lore_Derik_Hallin.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TTTTOH-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ONNan-36)</sup>

Under the provincial organization of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), Redguard society fractured to form two "parties" to aid Cyrodiil's administration of Hammerfell. The Na-Totambu ruling class retained the rights of noble council, and began calling themselves the "[Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md)" to illustrate their history of leadership and nobility. The warriors of the Ra Gada were granted rights of ownership within their tribal districts. This empowerment fundamentally changed the Ra Gada, who began to call themselves the "[Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md)", to emphasize the fact that they arrived on Tamriel first.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1-13)</sup> Despite the Forebears' later desire to assimilate, their forefathers were strongly opposed to outsiders during the First Era, and were mostly responsible for preventing the [Alessian Doctrines](Lore_Alessian_Doctrines.md) from having a strong presence in Hammerfell.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-PGE1C-37)</sup>

## Notes
- The emblem of the Ra Gada was said to be the water symbol, however no known representation of it exists.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Cont Pouch-38)</sup>
- The warlords of the Ra Gada were awarded medals when they first arrived on Tamriel's shores, some of which were still in circulation into the mid-Second era.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Cont Badge-39)</sup>
- In [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), several Ra Gada artifacts were circulating around the [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md) area. Light armor, blunt weapons, longbows and magically protective warpaint were among the documented items.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SK-1)</sup>
- Several items of Ra Gada weaponry also came to light in Cyrodiil in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), at the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-OM-2)</sup>
