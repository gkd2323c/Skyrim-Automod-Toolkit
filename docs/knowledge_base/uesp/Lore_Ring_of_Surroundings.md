# Ring of Surroundings

The Ring of Surroundings is very mysterious. Almost all that is known about it is that it grants the user the ability to blend into their surroundings.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TL-1)</sup>

[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWYB-2)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-3)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Ring of Surroundings among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TA-4)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the Ring of Surroundings and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWFR-5)</sup>

The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) reportedly acquired the ring on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), though reports on the method of its acquisition conflict. Some say that the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) awarded the ring to the Nerevarine for wiping out the [Camonna Tong](Lore_Camonna_Tong.md)'s presence in [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md). Others state that the Camonna Tong's kingpin, [Orvas Dren](Lore_Orvas_Dren.md), had the ring as part of his collection in his residence. These reports may not necessarily conflict, as there may be two Rings of Surroundings in existence.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Morrowind-6)</sup>

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Zaria, the alchemist in [Falkreath](Lore_Falkreath.md) was tasked by the jarl to scare a guard who had been taking [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) from the evidence chest to consume themselves, to prevent an embarrassing ordeal from becoming known. Serving as her proxy, Zaria tasked the [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) with delivering an invisibility potion under the guise of it being skooma. The plan worked, and the alchemist handed over the Ring of Surroundings as a reward, a payment from the jarl himself.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRCH-7)</sup>
