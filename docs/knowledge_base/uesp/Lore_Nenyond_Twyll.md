[3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/ON-place-Juras%27_Fall.jpg/200px-ON-place-Juras%27_Fall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Juras%27_Fall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Juras%27_Fall.jpg) Juras Falls ca. 2E 582 Nenyond Twyll (meaning "*Graywater Well"* in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md)), colloquially known as **Juras Falls** is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin found off the [Green Road](Lore_Green_Road.md) in the region of the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md).

During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) had ravaged across central Cyrodiil, and so Nenyond Twyll was in the territory considered a part of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). There was a skirmish that the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), led by Captain Juras had become involved in. In the end, Captain Juras was killed in battle, and the ruins were called Juras Falls ever since. His spirit would haunt the site, hence the name. It was until the spirit was put to rest by a [wandering soldier](Lore_Soulless_One.md) of the alliances.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a group of [Necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) had taken refuge in Nenyond Twyll, during the practice's rise in popularity in Cyrodiil. A mage of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) was sent to infiltrate the coven, but was later discovered. A group of warriors was sent to bring him back but they failed. And so, Arch-Mage [Hannibal Traven](Lore_Hannibal_Traven.md) had sent a [Magician](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) of the Guild to retrieve their spy. The Necromancer coven was defeated, but the spy was made a thrall.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>
