# Moot

The Moot (or **Moot of the Nords**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONGirdle Anti-1)</sup> is a council made up of the jarls of each Hold of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and convened upon the death of the High King to formally choose a successor to the throne from among qualifying members of the royal family.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup> In the absence of a legitimate heir, the Moot instead chooses a successor from among the jarls.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup> The Moot can also reconvene during a living High King's reign if he breaks some taboo which makes the jarls lose confidence in him, such as refusing a challenge made in accordance with the old Nord traditions of martial combat.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SS-4)</sup>

## History
According to some scholars, the first Moot was held by [King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) at his [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) mead-hall around the year [1E 184](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_184).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONGirdle Anti-1)</sup> It was a gathering of jarls and clan-chiefs under the patronage of the High King and in keeping with Skyrim's tradition of being a patchwork of rather autonomous Holds united under a common central authority. Since Harald lived to be 108 years old, and was survived by only three of his sons, a Moot made up of delegates from all over the province was given the job of choosing the next High King from among the remaining members of the royal family.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup> It decided upon the short-lived [Hjalmer](Lore_Hjalmer.md), who died after one year on the throne in [1E 222](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_222) and was succeeded by his younger brother King [Vrage the Gifted](Lore_Vrage.md). Eventually this Moot became both permanent and partisan.

In [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369), the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) began in Skyrim after the death of High King [Borgas](Lore_Borgas.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-5)</sup> [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) historians attribute the war to the Moot's failure to appoint "the obvious and capable" [Jarl Hanse](Lore_Jarl_Hanse.md) of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(region).md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup> The war, which lasted until [1E 420](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_420) and resulted in the collapse of the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md), was brought to an end with the Pact of Chieftains, which dictated that the Moot only be convened when the High King died without any direct heirs.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup> The war ended and [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md) was named the High King.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OATD-6)</sup> While the pact of chieftains was selecting a new leader, Cyrodiil reclaimed the territories in southern Skyrim that had been lost to them during the times of the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RTR-7)</sup> Several inkwells used to sign the document survived until the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Inkwelll-8)</sup> The Moot served in its limited role well, and was only called upon three times until at least the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1S-3)</sup>

The first of these occasions came with the death of High King [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCOF-9)</sup> the very man who was crowned at the conclusion of the War of Succession.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OATD-6)</sup> Olaf's rule was marked by great strife among the Nords, and amid fears of another contentious Moot, each of Skyrim's holds sent a mage to a special convocation to craft a magical artifact capable of testing the worth of potential rulers. The result was the [Crown of Verity](Lore_Crown_of_Freydis.md), which replaced the lost [Jagged Crown](Lore_Jagged_Crown.md) as the symbol of Skyrim's monarchs. When the Moot selected the warrior and chieftain Asurn Ice-Breaker to be the next High King, they mandated that he also don the Crown of Verity. Upon Asurn's unequivocal rejection by the crown, he threatened to kill the Moot, but one member challenged Asurn to lawful combat. Asurn was swiftly struck down, and the duel's victor, [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md), was then accepted by the Crown of Verity and pronounced High King by the Moot.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCOF-9)</sup>

The Moot continued using the Crown of Verity to determine the worthiness of candidates for the throne, and the practice was accepted without question until the assassination of High King [Logrolf](Lore_Logrolf.md) in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431). Logrolf's daughter, [Freydis](Lore_Freydis.md), was in line to succeed him as High Queen, but Jarl [Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) denied her legitimacy and demanded a Moot be called to choose a successor. Freydis was accepted by the Crown of Verity, subsequently renamed the Crown of Freydis in her honor, and crowned High Queen in [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md), but a partial Moot convened in Solitude and declared Svartr to be High King. This marked the division of Skyrim into eastern and western kingdoms, a schism that lasted for many years afterwards.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TCOF-9)</sup>

Following the death of king [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md), [Wulfharth](Lore_Wulfharth.md) was elected by the Pact of Chieftains.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-FSOKW-10)</sup>

In the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) the Moot still gathered to recognize the new High King, but it had been reduced to little more than a formality. This was due to Skyrim's capital becoming the city most directly influenced by Imperial culture and politics, meaning the Jarl of Solitude typically served as High King without dispute.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup> This held true up until the reign of [High King Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) in the second and early third centuries of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SS-4)</sup>

## The Great Moot
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cd/ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg/200px-ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Great_Moot.jpg) A Great Moot In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was governed by a separate body called the Great Moot, wherein decisions were ratified by representatives from the Pact's three member nations. The Great Moot convened in the Pact's capital of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) of Eastern Skyrim was named acting High King of the Great Moot after he thwarted an [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) invasion with the aid of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) and [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) forces.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-JTSK-12)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Jorunn's rule was again ratified at the summit of [Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md) following his brother [Fildgor Orcthane](Lore_Fildgor.md)'s failed attempt to usurp the throne.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup>
