# Septim Dynasty

The Septim Dynasty was the founding dynasty and the first ruling dynasty of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) from [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896) until the dynasty's extinction with the death of [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). The dynasty and the Empire were founded by the legendary [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) in 2E 896 after [decades of conquest](Lore_Tiber_War.md), thus marking the end of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) and the beginning of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).

Under the Septims, Tamriel prospered and its provinces enjoyed peace and stability that had not been seen since before the chaotic centuries of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). New conflicts still arose, however, which repeatedly stretched the Empire to its breaking point throughout the Third Era: a battle over the imperial succession ensued during the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) between [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120) and [3E 127](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_127); the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) seized control of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) from [3E 249](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_249) to [3E 267](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_267); Emperor [Uriel Septim V](Lore_Uriel_Septim_V.md) spearheaded an ill-fated invasion of [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) in [3E 288](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_288), resulting in the emperor's death and the loss of a considerable portion of the Empire's military strength; and Imperial Battlemage [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md) overthrew Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and ruled for a decade beginning in [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), a period that would become known as the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md).

Through all of these challenges, the Empire and the Septim line survived. Their end came in 3E 433 with the onset of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) and his heirs were assassinated by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md) in a single night, and the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) ceased to burn. The [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) launched a massive invasion of Tamriel. Although one last Septim heir, Martin Septim, was located, he sacrificed himself to repel the Daedra and seal [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) off from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) forever. With the Septims gone and the Empire's fate uncertain, the Third Era concluded and the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) began.

## Notes
1. [^](#cite_ref-4)"There has been some disagreement regarding Pelagius' relationship with Tiber Septim. [A version](Lore_Brief_History_of_the_Empire_(Daggerfall).md) of a Brief History of the Empire referred to Pelagius as Tiber's eldest son, but it has since been [revised](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire). Some passages in [The Real Barenziah](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah.md) suggest that Tiber's wife, referred to as the Empress, had multiple "children". The likeliest explanations include that Tiber and the Empress had female children otherwise unrecorded to history, or that the general public were referred to as the Empress' "children". Other possibilities include that the Empress had children that were not Tiber's, the two of them had secret offspring, or a mistake on the part of the author. Regardless, the throne ended up with Kintyra.
2. [^](#cite_ref-5) A Brief History of the Empire says that Pelagius reigned for "less than three years", suggesting a date of [3E 40](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_40). *The Daggerfall Chronicles* explicitly uses this date.
3. [^](#cite_ref-10) Book 3 of The Wolf Queen indicates that Antiochus was not offered the throne until some time had passed after the death of Pelagius II. A Brief History of the Empire indicates that he took the throne twelve years before [the War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md) in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), which leads to the same date.
4. [^](#cite_ref-Ant D_13-0) Book 5 of The Wolf Queen states that Antiochus fell into a coma in [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119) and that his funeral was in [3E 120](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_120). The Madness of Pelagius agrees, stating that Kintyra ascended the throne "just over a year" after Pelagius' birth in [3E 119](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_119). Both books are contradicted in the [Biography of the Wolf Queen](Lore_Biography_of_the_Wolf_Queen.md), which explicitly places his death in [3E 112](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_112). To add to the confusion, see the note about the date of Pelagius III's birth. This chart uses the 120 date, since most references state that to be the correct date.
5. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-KSII_16-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-KSII_16-1)***</sup><sup>***[c](#cite_ref-KSII_16-2)***</sup> In The Wolf Queen, although there is uncertainty over the date of Kintyra's death, it is stated that Uriel III was crowned "four years previously" to the year [3E 125](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_125), implying that he was crowned in [3E 121](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_121). A Brief History of the Empire explicitly states that Uriel took the throne in 121. The Biography of the Wolf Queen states that Kintyra was executed in [3E 114](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_114) and that Uriel ascended to the throne "seven years later". Thus, while all three books agree on the date of Uriel's ascension, the Biography implies a seven year interregnum during the War of the Red Diamond when nobody wore the Imperial crown. The precise date given in Broken Diamonds implies that Kintyra was held in captivity for some time before her death.
6. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Pel3_19-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Pel3_19-1)***</sup> The Madness of Pelagius says that Pelagius III died in the 34th year of his life, which translates to [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153).
7. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Kat D_20-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Kat D_20-1)***</sup> Both Brief History of the Empire and The Madness of Pelagius put the length of Katariah's reign at forty-six years, and [The Third Era Timeline](Lore_The_Third_Era_Timeline.md) puts her death in [3E 200](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_200). To fit in with later events, her reign must end in [3E 200](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_200). A possible explanation of the stated length of Katariah's reign by Brief History of the Empire and The Madness of Pelagius could be that Katariah's reign began in late [3E 153](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_153) but ended in early [3E 200](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_200), meaning that her reign may have in reality lasted forty-six years and some months, but not quite forty-seven years.
8. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Cass D_22-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Cass D_22-1)***</sup><sup>***[c](#cite_ref-Cass D_22-2)***</sup> The *Daggerfall* version of Brief History says "In two years, he was dead" while the later version says that Cassynder died "Three years after [his accession]". It is impossible to determine exactly which year these statements are intended to mean, but [3E 202](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_202) seems reasonable.
9. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-US4_23-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-US4_23-1)***</sup><sup>***[c](#cite_ref-US4_23-2)***</sup> Brief History of the Empire describes the reign of Uriel Septim IV lasting 43 years. This must have extended into [3E 246](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_246), as [The Rear Guard](Lore_The_Rear_Guard.md) describes a law made "in 3E 246, during the reign of Uriel IV". *The Daggerfall Chronicles* place Uriel's death in [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247), but it doesn't seem possible to stretch his reign into this year given other events. It is possible this date has been superseded.
10. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Ceph2_24-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Ceph2_24-1)***</sup> Although the Brief History of the Empire states Cephorus II became emperor in [3E 268](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_268), this is clearly an error. The same book states that Uriel Septim V also became emperor in 268, so it is more logical to assume the latter is correct and that Cephorus simply followed on from Uriel IV. Cephorus is described as "a cousin more closely related to the original Septim line", although his precise line of descent is not clear.
11. [^](#cite_ref-27) Uriel VI was too young to rule in his own right when he was crowned, so his mother Thonica ruled as regent. He was eventually allowed to ascend the throne and assume full Imperial powers in [3E 307](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_307).
12. ^ <sup>***[a](#cite_ref-Morihatha_28-0)***</sup><sup>***[b](#cite_ref-Morihatha_28-1)***</sup><sup>***[c](#cite_ref-Morihatha_28-2)***</sup> *[The Third Era Timeline](Lore_The_Third_Era_Timeline.md)* states that Uriel VI died and Morihatha was crowned in 3E 320, and *Tamriel's Timeline, The Daggerfall Chronicles* suggests the year was 3E 317. However, *[Brief History of the Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brief_History_of_the_Empire)* notes that Morihatha was born a month after her father left for Akavir in 3E 288, and that she was 25-years-old as Empress, which would put her coronation in 3E 313.
