# Daedric Titan

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/96/LG-cardart-Daedric_Titan.png/200px-LG-cardart-Daedric_Titan.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Daedric_Titan.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Daedric_Titan.png) A Daedric Titan (Legends) Daedric Titans, commonly referred to simply as **Titans**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> are giant winged [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) that serve [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) as war-slaves. They were first created in imitation of the [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md) of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) when Bal's interrogation of [Boziikkodstrun](Lore_Boziikkodstrun.md) proved fruitless. After modifying Boziikkodstrun's skeleton and substituting his [soul](Lore_Souls.md) for a Daedric [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md), Bal created the first Titan.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DDTT-2)</sup> Daedric Titans are anatomically similar to Dragons, although with dark black skin and prehensile frontal limbs. They have the ability to speak a spell of flaming essence-drain that can debilitate an opponent with a single word, similar to the power of the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DDTT-2)</sup> They are notoriously difficult to kill. Some Titans are much larger than others, requiring the use of [siege weapons](Lore_Siege_Weapons.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

They serve Molag Bal as the guardians of souls,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DTTG-4)</sup> and therefore the keepers of black soul gems.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KCAHC-5)</sup> However, they are also posted to guard other strategic resources.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DTTG-4)</sup> According to some sources, Titans were released upon [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for the first time<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> during the attempted [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). Specifically, Molag Bal released them into the skies over the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) when a portal to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) was opened above the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Daedric Titans eventually came to serve other [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), such as [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), who had them summoned to fight mortals for her amusement.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup> [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) has **Ash Titans** in his army. Unlike their blue cousins, Ash Titans have red skin, and breathe regular flames rather than blue cold-fire.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

## Shackled Titan
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/75/ON-mount-Shackled_Titan.jpg/200px-ON-mount-Shackled_Titan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Shackled_Titan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-mount-Shackled_Titan.jpg) A barded Shackled Titan mount (ESO) **Shackled Titans** are a variety of Daedric Titan that have had their wings ripped off by Molag Bal as a form of punishment, grounding them.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Shackled Mount-9)</sup> They leap at enemies to attack with their forelimbs and their fiery breath.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-10)</sup>

These four-legged Daedra are sometimes bound by Bal's dark will to serve as mounts for his mortal servants. Such mounts are usually barded for protection, and possess the ability to cloak themselves in cold-fire. Ser the Wingshorn is a Shackled Titan who was known to the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md); it was said to have failed Molag Bal only once prior to being bound.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Shackled Mount-9)</sup>

## Notes
- | [^](#note_nb1) | Daedric Titans were said to have been released upon Tamriel for the first time during the Planemeld. This would match the story of Boziikkodstrun's corruption, which describes him bounding up the Endless Stair, a structure which is rumored to have been created by the [Mad Architect](Lore_Mad_Architect.md) some time between [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578) and [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-11)</sup> However, this timeline is contradicted by the appearance of Titans on Tamriel at earlier points in history.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOAe-13)</sup> |
| --- | --- |
- Molag Bal has referred to the titan [Ozozzachar](Lore_Ozozzachar.md) as his son.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-MBal-14)</sup>
- [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) constructed a [Clockwork Titan](Lore_Clockwork_Titan.md) to house the soul of [Saint Olms the Just](Lore_Saint_Olms_the_Just.md) during an experiment to see if he could grant mortals everlasting life.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Llandras-15)</sup>
