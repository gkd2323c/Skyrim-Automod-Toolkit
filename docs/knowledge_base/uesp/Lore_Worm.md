Worms are small invertebrates that can be found almost anywhere. They commonly live around plants, and are used by fishermen as bait, primarily in salt water. Worms can also be found living in and feeding on the flesh of the dead or [undead](Lore_Undead.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Guide-2)</sup> Potentially due to this link with death, they are heavily associated with [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md), who is known as the "King of Worms", and later "God of Worms".

[Sloads](Lore_Sload.md) are sometimes known as Worms.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> The inhabitants of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md) were grown from proforms, strange worm-like creatures created by the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) -like trees of Umbriel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lo S-4)</sup>

## Varieties
The most common variety of worms are the simple earthworms, groundworms, or garden worms that are often used as bait or eaten by [birds](Lore_Bird.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Guide-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MF-6)</sup>

[Rootworms](Lore_Rootworm.md) are strange translucent eight-foot long worms native to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) that are commonly referred to as the "Underground Express", because of their ability to swallow travelers whole and carry them with the current.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AA-7)</sup> They travel quite rapidly through tunnels formed by the roots of [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) trees.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> The digestive systems of rootworms are apparently quite slow, and travelers "could live in a rootworm's belly for months".<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AA-7)</sup>

### Aquatic and Parasitic Worms
Aquatic worms have been described as "feathery-gilled wrigglers", and are preyed upon by small aquatic predators such as [ancestor lizards](Lore_Ancestor_Lizard.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Lo S-4)</sup> Moist scale burrowers are a species that can be found in the lakes of [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md). Caliginous bristleworms can be fished up in the foul abyssal waters of [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOFish-9)</sup> while phosphor worms could be harvested in the Sump of Umbriel.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TIC-10)</sup><sup>:Part 3, Chapter 8</sup>

[Leeches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leech) are blood-sucking parasitic worms that can be found in swamps and marshes, often in stagnant water.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> The Shadowfen Creeping Leech is a type of leech that can be found in [Shadowfen](Lore_Shadowfen.md) at certain times of the year. The Moray Leech can be found in the waters of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOFish-9)</sup> Silk Leeches are used as ingredients in the kitchens of Tamriel.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TIC-10)</sup>

Several worms are capable of infesting living beings and causing [diseases](Lore_Disease.md), such as [dampworms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dampworm) and [gutworms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gutworm). Zilh worms are parasitic worms of Umbriel, one Umbrielan brought them up when trying to understand the concept of pregnancy.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TIC-10)</sup><sup>:Part 3, Chapter 2</sup>

### Tube Worms
Some worms of unusual shapes live in tubelike growths. The [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) is home to strange plant-like cave worms that begin to retract when approached, hiding their bioluminescent light.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-The CW-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-13)</sup> The [root tunnels](Lore_Root.md) of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) are home to deadly Root Worms, that extend and attack those who pass by them.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
