[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg/200px-OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-misc-Shade_of_the_Revenant.jpg) The Shade of the Revenant The Necromancer's Moon, also known as **the Revenant**, is a [moon](Lore_Moons.md) and the divine body of the God of Worms, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NM-1)</sup> It was created by the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWit W-2)</sup> when Mannimarco used the power of the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md) to fuel his transfiguration and apotheosis.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lot E-3)</sup>

The Necromancer's Moon eclipses the planet [Arkay](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md) every eight days, preventing its light from reaching Nirn for 24 hours. This celestial phenomenon is known as the Shade of the Revenant, and casts a purple or red light down on certain locations across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during this period.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OBR-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The purpose of this eclipse is to foil the divine laws of [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), allowing Mannimarco to be the only god of life and death.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> The [Cult of the Black Worm](Lore_Cult_of_the_Black_Worm.md) worship Mannimarco as the God of Worms, and construct altars to him at these locations to harness the Shade of the Revenant for use in creating [black soul gems](Lore_Black_Soul_Gem.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/OB-quest-Necromancer%27s_Moon.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Necromancer%27s_Moon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Necromancer%27s_Moon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Necromancer%27s_Moon.jpg) A Worm Anchorite creating a black soul gem [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg/200px-DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg) Mannimarco's apotheosis Since as early as [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Mannimarco had come to the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region in search of the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), an artifact designed to control the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) while it was powered by the Mantella. He established himself and his Cult of the Black Worm at [Scourg Barrow](Lore_Scourg_Barrow.md) deep in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md). His plan was to obtain the Totem so that he could command the Mantella to his side as it fell from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), before it reached Numidium's chest. Using its power, he planned to leave his mortal frame and join the ranks of the [gods](Lore_Gods.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> To achieve this, Mannimarco embroiled himself in the court politics of the Iliac Bay, and in the process came into contact with [an agent](Lore_Hero.md#The_Agent) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> By reading the stars, he foresaw the return of the Mantella from its [hiding place](Lore_Mantellan_Crux.md) in Aetherius, and wrote to this agent promising to bring the Totem to Aetherius and out of mortal hands.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> The agent obtained the Totem and returned the Mantella to Nirn, but the precise details of what followed are unknown due to the Warp in the West.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWit W-2)</sup>

Between the 9th and the 11th of Frostfall, 3E 417, a series of inexplicable and paradoxical events occurred throughout the Iliac Bay region. Many factions had attempted to gain possession of the Totem of Tiber Septim, resulting in a distortion of time where all of these factions simultaneously gained possession, achieving their goals to some degree. This phenomenon went on to be known as the Warp in the West, and resulted in major political changes in the region.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TWit W-2)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup> One of the most significant results of this event was Mannimarco's successful apotheosis, which resulted in the creation of the Necromancer's Moon.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Lot E-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NM-1)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Mannimarco returned to Nirn and attempted to capture the [soul](Lore_Soul.md) of [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) Arch-Mage [Hannibal Traven](Lore_Hannibal_Traven.md) for study.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NM-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> Traven had banned the practice of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) from the guild sometime after his promotion circa [3E 431](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_431),<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> causing a lot of disillusioned guild mages to flock to Mannimarco's side. However, the Arch-Mage tricked Mannimarco, sacrificing his own soul to protect the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) so that Mannimarco could be killed.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

The death of Mannimarco's mortal body had no effect on the existence of the Necromancer's Moon. The Shade of the Revenant continued to shine down on Nirn after his death,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup> and necromancers continued to offer souls to the Revenant in order to darken soul gems well into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

## Notes
- The Necromancer's Moon was not the first attempt by Mannimarco to become a god. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), he attempted to usurp [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) as the God of Schemes by trapping his power in the [Chim-el Adabal](Lore_Chim-el_Adabal.md), thereby becoming the god of both Mundus and [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) following the completion of the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). This plot failed when Bal learned of his intentions.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup>
