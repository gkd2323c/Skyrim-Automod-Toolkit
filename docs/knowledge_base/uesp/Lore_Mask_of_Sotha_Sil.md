# Mask of Sotha Sil

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg/200px-SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Mask_of_Sotha_Sil.jpg) Mask of Sotha Sil (Skyrim Creation Club) The Mask of Sotha Sil is the **blessed mask**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CMDS-1)</sup> (also known as the **killing mask**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TTISV8-2)</sup> or **warmaker helm**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Sehthelm-3)</sup> of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) god [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md). It embodies his idea for a *Anuvanna'si* (Tamriel Final), whose one of many goals was where life would undergo a synthesis of organic and non-organic nature<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SSNM-4)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-twitch1-5)</sup> (akin to the [Fabricants](Lore_Fabricants.md)).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Wit CC-6)</sup> Indeed, overtime, Sotha Sil's body would become more and more synthetic, and his mask also changed to undergo this transformation. His followers, the [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md), mimic this synthesis with modifying their bodies, as well as craft their headwear in the Clockwork God's image.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-CMDS-1)</sup>

It is one of the [Masks of the Tribunal](Lore_Masks_of_the_Tribunal.md), the other two being [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md)'s [War Mask](Lore_Mask_of_Almalexia.md), and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)'s [Ash Mask](Lore_Vivec%27s_Ash_Mask.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRMT-7)</sup>

## History
It is unknown when Sotha Sil created his mask, but in the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), his Elegiac Replication has a hologram that depicts him with it around the time he and [Nerevar](Lore_Nerevar.md) became great friends.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SSANH-8)</sup> Though sources vary on its authenticity, it is claimed that Sotha Sil along with his fellow Tribunes murdered Nerevar.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Na RM-9)</sup> He chose not to wear his mask during this ordeal as he held Nerevar's severed face.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-FM-10)</sup>

Sotha Sil had an intensive love for Dwarven industry,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ESODF-11)</sup> and drew inspiration for them in his creations. Their influence in his work is rumored to be traceable to the time before their disappearance, with his usage of clockwork dreughs build in the Dwemer's automaton's image that were used to assault them.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSLT-12)</sup> One of his earlier mask designs depicts a more Dwemeri-like design,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONSSCG-13)</sup> which would later get sleeker and thinner by the events of the [Daedric War](Lore_Daedric_Triad.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).

The practice of designing and wearing [masks modeled after important figures](Lore_Daedric_Faces.md) in Dunmeri culture was not uncommon.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DFO-14)</sup><sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-DFTDE-15)</sup> Replicas of Sotha Sil's mask were worn by his followers as they unraveled his esoteric teachings, while travelers to Vvardenfell donned them for complex jokes after visiting a tavern.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SSNM-4)</sup>

By the end of the Third Era, though he was generally reclusive, Sotha Sil became detached from Tamriel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-MWHelas-16)</sup> By the time Almalexia came to kill him for her goal: to become the one true god of the Dunmer, uniting all of her people under one faith and authority and destroying any who interfered,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRAlmalexia-17)</sup> Sotha Sil had nearly fully mechanized his body, with a full covering metallic mask to go with it. After the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) defeated Almalexia, her body as well as Sotha Sil's were retrieved from the Clockwork City by Vivec, who interred them in the proper Velothi fashion.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-MKPosts-18)</sup>

Despite the rise of the New Temple, worship of the Tribunal continued into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) and was driven underground. These cults were considered heretical by wider Dunmeri society and were persecuted by the New Temple.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-19)</sup> One cult of Almalexia worshippers constructed a hidden temple in a cave on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) known as Ashfall's Tear, just north of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md). The cultists planned to use old propaganda to restore widespread Tribunal worship,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Got T-20)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-21)</sup> and sought to gather and preserve many relics of the old Tribunal Temple.

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) was alerted to the cult's existence when they attempted to recover several additional artifacts. It is unknown if the Dragonborn agreed to help the cult or if it was wiped out.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Got T-20)</sup> The Mask of Sotha Sil was tracked down to the Dwemer ruins of [Kagrenzel](Lore_Kagrenzel.md). It was initially retrieved by Curate Melita of the heretics who got caught in the ruin's trap. Successfully able to navigate Kagrenzel's [Falmer](Lore_Falmer.md) inhabitants, Melita made it down stream into Stony Creek Cave, where she was apprehended by bandits, the leader who was the wielder of [Ruin's Edge](Lore_Ruin%27s_Edge.md). The Dragonborn navigated Kagrenzel, defeated the bandits, and retrieved both Artifacts.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-22)</sup>
