# Daedra Stars

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/AR-misc-STAR5.png/200px-AR-misc-STAR5.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-misc-STAR5.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-misc-STAR5.png) A red star in the night sky (*Arena*) The **daedra stars** (or **daedric stars**) are [stars](Lore_Stars.md) in the night sky that are associated with specific [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md). They shine bright red on certain nights, signaling dire portents.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

The following Princes are associated with certain stars:

- **[Azura](Lore_Azura.md)**: [Azura's Star](Lore_Azura%27s_Star_(astronomy).md), also called the Twilight Star, is possibly the most well-known daedra star. It appears briefly at dawn and dusk, low on the horizon below the [constellation](Lore_Constellations.md) of [the Steed](Lore_The_Steed.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>
- **[Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)**: The stars of Mehrunes Dagon are known to blaze when the Prince of Destruction is displeased.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>
- **[Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)**: A bright red star is said to be the anger of Molag Bal, and appears in the sky when the Prince is displeased.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup>
- **[Namira](Lore_Namira.md)**: The stars of Namira burn bright even when she is pleased. Like their Prince, these stars are said to be always hungry, and never satisfied.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
- **[Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)**: Sheogorath is referred to as the Mad Star in [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) myth and is believed to reside in the heavens.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOSheo-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Saryoni-8)</sup> According to the teachings of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), he convinced the moonlet [Baar Dau](Lore_Baar_Dau.md) to hurl itself toward [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), which Sheogorath deemed a mockery of the heavens.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TPP-9)</sup>

The [Nine Coruscations](Lore_Nine_Coruscations.md) are a group of [Magna Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), divine beings who are said to have created the stars when they fled to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) soon after the creation of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), forming perforations through which the light of Aetherius could shine.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EOMN-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNC-11)</sup> This group includes two Daedric Princes:

- **[Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md)**: Meridia is believed to have once been a Magna Ge named Merid-Nunda, also known as the Red Star.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNC-11)</sup> For supposedly consorting with illicit spectra, Merid-Nunda was cast out of Aetherius and took the mantle of a Daedric Prince.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EOMN-10)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-12)</sup> Meridia is associated with a constellation of four stars, although some of her worshippers consider this connection to be heresy.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-13)</sup>
- **[Ithelia](Lore_Ithelia.md)**: The forgotten Daedric Prince Ithelia was once a Magna Ge. She was referred to as the Prime Archon or the White Star.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TNC-11)</sup>

## Notes
- According to [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md), the Daedra were born before the stars.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup>
- The [three stars of Locanda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Three_Stars_of_Locanda) are also believed to be a dire portent, and are associated with unholy alliances.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TVR-15)</sup> It is unknown whether they are intended to be daedra stars.
