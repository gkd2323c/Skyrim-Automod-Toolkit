[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/SR-place-Castle_Volkihar.jpg/200px-SR-place-Castle_Volkihar.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Volkihar.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Volkihar.jpg) Castle Volkihar circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) Castle Volkihar is an ancient castle perhaps dating back to the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) or the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Serana-1)</sup> It is located on an isolated island off the [Giant's Coast](Lore_Giant%27s_Coast.md) of [Haafingar Hold](Lore_Haafingar.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), near the border with [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup> Long thought lost to the eternal mists of the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md), the sprawling castle manifesting on the rocky islands north of Icewater Jetty has been seen by fishermen on Skyrim's [northwest coast](Lore_Giant%27s_Coast.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-3)</sup>

Initially the home of an [Aedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) cult, the castle has since been occupied by the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md) for millennia, hence its current name.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-3)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/SR-concept-Dawnguard_03.jpg/200px-SR-concept-Dawnguard_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Dawnguard_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Dawnguard_03.jpg) Concept art [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e9/SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_Courtyard.jpg/200px-SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_Courtyard.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_Courtyard.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_Courtyard.jpg) Courtyard with moondial [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9d/SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Castle_Volkihar_02.jpg) Docks Little is known about the previous owners of the place,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-3)</sup> though it is implied the castle once housed an [Aedric](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) cult, as a broken statue of [Mara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mara) could be found in the abandoned ruins,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup> and one wing was known as the "Tower Chapel Of [Dibella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dibella)".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-3)</sup> Historically, it served as the home of [Lord Harkon](Lore_Harkon.md). In life, Harkon was a very rich ruler of the lands who feared mortality. In return for sacrificing thousands of innocents, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) granted Harkon—as well as his wife [Valerica](Lore_Valerica.md) and his daughter [Serana](Lore_Serana.md) —the gift of [vampirism](Lore_Vampirism.md). As pure-blooded [Vampire Lords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vampire_Lord), Harkon and his family had immense vampiric powers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Serana-1)</sup>

Harkon became obsessed with [a prophecy](Lore_Tyranny_of_the_Sun.md) he discovered from the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), which foretold of a day when the sun would be darkened and vampires would rule [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Serana-1)</sup> His estranged wife, Valerica, sought to stop his maddened search. She did this by hiding Serana and an Elder Scroll in Dimhollow Crypt, and creating a portal to the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md) in Castle Volkihar. Valerica fled to the realm with a second Elder Scroll, but was imprisoned there by the [Ideal Masters](Lore_Ideal_Masters.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Valerica-4)</sup> Harkon, in a fit of rage, destroyed and abandoned parts of the castle which reminded him of his wife, such as the courtyard.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRChasing-5)</sup>

Harkon created the [Volkihar](Lore_Volkihar.md) bloodline, and formed a court based in Castle Volkihar keep. For several eras, he scoured the lands in vain for his lost wife and child, becomingly increasingly consumed by the prophecy.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Serana-1)</sup> The vampires of Castle Volkihar remained the dominant vampiric faction in Skyrim by culling feral vampires in the province, even those of their own bloodline.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The island became infamous among sailors, who believed it to be cursed.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Undead [Bone Hawks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bone_Hawk) inhabited the island; other monsters such as [Gargoyles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gargoyle) and [Death Hounds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Death_Hound) were kept in the company of the vampires, within the castle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Volkihar vampires grew more bold, attacking major cities and destroying the Skyrim branch of the [Vigilants of Stendarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vigilants_of_Stendarr). In response to this, an ancient order of vampire hunters named the [Dawnguard](Lore_Dawnguard.md) was refounded.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Serana was awoken by the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md), who had been sent to Dimhollow Crypt by the Dawnguard; the two then journeyed to Castle Volkihar at her request.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The prophecy was realized when the two freed Valerica from the Soul Cairn<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SRChasing-5)</sup> and discovered how [Auriel's Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) could be corrupted to temporarily block the sun. Lord Harkon was then defeated with the bow,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> although it is unknown if the Dragonborn took his place as leader of the Volkihar, or destroyed the court.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-11)</sup> Following her husband's demise, Valerica returned to Castle Volkihar, and repairs were made to the castle.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SR-2)</sup>
