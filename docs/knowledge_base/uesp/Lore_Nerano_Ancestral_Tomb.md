# Nerano

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/35/MW-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg/200px-MW-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) Nerano Ancestral Tomb [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg/200px-MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Nerano_Manor.jpg) Nerano Manor The Nerano clan is an ancient family of [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) that lives in the province of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). They are one of the thirty revered families of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATOTTRF-1)</sup> and are a sub-faction of [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONNerano-2)</sup> The **Nerano Ancestral Tomb** is located in the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) region, west of [Vos](Lore_Vos.md) and south of Kushtashpi.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONNerano-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWNerano-3)</sup> **Nerano Manor** is a lavish residence in [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) owned by noble member of the family.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NM-4)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e6/ON-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg/200px-ON-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Nerano_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) Nerano Ancestral Tomb with House Hlaalu crest With the other twenty-nine ancient families of Vvardenfell, the Nerano clan hid the [Library of Andule](Lore_Library_of_Andule.md), the secret repository of the [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) and placed a clue in their ancestral tomb.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONBradyn-5)</sup> Its location remained a secret for many years until Bradyn of the Library of Vivec enlisted an [outlander](Lore_The_Vestige.md) to find the tombs and uncover it in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). They were able to find all thirty tombs and find the lost Library of Andule.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONAncestral-6)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Vorir Nerano served as a stablemaster for the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) during [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Vo N-7)</sup> Vamoni Nerano was a citizen of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Va N-8)</sup> Ralds Nerano resided in [Glimmertarn](Lore_Glimmertarn.md) and was a [druid](Lore_Druids.md) of the [Stonelore Circle](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-RN-9)</sup> [Ralevyn Nerano](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Ralevyn_Nerano) was a leatherworker expert and an author of *[Settling the Debate](Lore_Settling_the_Debate.md)*.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-STD-10)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), Athal Nerano was a resident of [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md) and an Oathman of House Hlaalu.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AN-11)</sup> Ondres Nerano of Balmora was a noble of House Hlaalu, as well as a merchant and a Hireling. He was known for spreading rumors about Archmaster [Bolvyn Venim](Lore_Bolvyn_Venim.md), which were deemed slanderous by Councilor [Athyn Sarethi](Lore_Athyn_Sarethi.md) of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) and drew their attention, along with demands that he withdraw his statements.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ON-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-AS-13)</sup> Ondres resided in his lavish home, the Nerano Manor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-NM-4)</sup> Access to the manor in Balmora was of interest to the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), and they eventually obtained its key for their purposes.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SLH-14)</sup>

As of 3E 427, the The Nerano Ancestral Tomb was taken over by the [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) Calvario of the [Berne Clan](Lore_Berne_Clan.md) and his undead servants.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MWNerano-3)</sup> At the request of the [Zainab](Lore_Zainab.md) tribe, the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) disposed of them, in order to be formally acknowledged by them.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Kaushad-15)</sup>
