Hannibal Traven was the Arch-Mage of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md). He was a staunch opponent of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) and was responsible for several poorly-implemented guild reforms.

Originally, Professor Traven was the head of the [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) Mages Guild, and supposedly ran a "clean hall".<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> He was promoted to the position of Arch-Mage circa [3E 431](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_431).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Among his controversial reforms were a tightening on admission to the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) and an outright ban on the practice of necromancy by guild mages. Traven's divisive stance on necromancy had many strong supporters even outside the guild, although it caused a large degree of internal fracturing. Half the Council of Mages resigned, and Magister [Ulliceta gra-Kogg](Lore_Ulliceta_gra-Kogg.md) of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md) was forced to flee following her attempted arrest by the [Knights of the Lamp](Lore_Knights_of_the_Lamp.md) for practicing necromancy. Traven would go on to publish [*The Black Arts On Trial*](Lore_The_Black_Arts_On_Trial.md), to document the debate between gra-Kogg and Magister Voth Karlyss of [Corinth](Lore_Corinth.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), coinciding with Traven's policy change, [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) returned to the affairs of mortals. With necromancers now shunned from the Mages Guild, many flocked to join the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md). Mannimarco's followers were soon openly attacking the guild, going so far as to burn down the [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) guildhall. As Traven struggled to control the Council of Mages, he turned to the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md#Hero_of_Kvatch) for help in investigating and delaying Mannimarco's plans. Traven had the Hero secure both the [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md) and the [Bloodworm Helm](Lore_Bloodworm_Helm.md), as well as a unique colossal black soul gem the necromancers had created. In a final effort to save the guild, Traven sacrificed himself by trapping his [soul](Lore_Souls.md) within the colossal gem to allow the Hero to confront Mannimarco. With the filled gem, the Hero was shielded from Mannimarco's powerful magic and was able to defeat him in [Echo Cave](Lore_Echo_Cave.md). Traven's final act as Arch-Mage was to appoint the Hero as his successor.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup>

Although Traven's sacrifice saved the Mages Guild from destruction at the hands of the Order of the Black Worm, the institute was not able to survive the strong anti- [magic](Lore_Magic.md) sentiment that arose in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md). The [Synod](Lore_Synod.md) and the [College of Whispers](Lore_College_of_Whispers.md) formed following the guild's dissolution.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Before his defeat, Mannimarco revealed that he had planned to reanimate Traven as a [Worm Thrall](Lore_Worm_Thrall.md) for study.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OB-4)</sup>
