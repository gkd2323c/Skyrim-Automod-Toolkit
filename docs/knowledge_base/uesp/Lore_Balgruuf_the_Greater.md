*If it's the war you're referring to, I'm on the side of Whiterun.*
—Jarl Balgruuf Balgruuf the Greater was the [Jarl](Lore_Skyrim%27s_Rule.md) of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), and ruled from the prosperous and strategically-located city of the same name. A proud [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) and a descendant of High King [Olaf One-Eye](Lore_Olaf_One-Eye.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Balgruuf admired the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md) and once made the pilgrimage to [High Hrothgar](Lore_High_Hrothgar.md) as a young man. His three children were Frothar, Dagny, and Nelkir, but he had no spouse in 4E 201. While considered a fair and shrewd ruler, and an honorable warrior who put his heart and soul into his service, he was also not considered a patient man. <sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup><sup>[*verification needed — his character description needs development and verification, and last comment seems to be a single remark by Ulfric*]</sup>

In his youth Balgruuf adventured in Morrowind <sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>, where he became battle companions with Irileth, a Dunmer who he appointed as his Housecarl once he became Jarl. He faced numerous assassination attempts.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Irileth-2)</sup> Two attempts, one by an anonymous Dunmer assassin wielding the [Bow of Shadows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bow_of_Shadows), and another initiated by the Daedric Prince [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) who tempted his son Nelkir to assassinate his father with the [Ebony Blade](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebony_Blade), <sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SRTWD-3)</sup> were foiled by a [mysterious hero](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md).

Following the signing of the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) that ended the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), Balgruuf and the other jarls of Skyrim were given no choice but to comply with the terms. They were also given chests of gold by the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) as compensation, and toward compliance with the ban of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship. <sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRBPI-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Ulfric-5)</sup> However, he ignored ongoing worship of the god in his hold, and citizens were allowed to publicly proselytize in Talos' name at a shrine in Whiterun without repercussion. <sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Heimskr-6)</sup> According to Nelkir, Balgruuf worshipped Talos privately, and hated the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) passionately.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Nelkir-7)</sup>

During the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), both the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md) and the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) sought to gain Balgruuf as an ally. The latter in particular made a great effort to obtain his support.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Galmar-8)</sup> At the beginning of the events of [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md), Balgruuf claimed neutrality, saying he was only "on the side of Whiterun."<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> He sometimes reluctantly attended parties for the high-ranking thrown by [Thalmor Ambassador Elenwen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elenwen) at the [Thalmor Embassy](Lore_Thalmor_Embassy.md), in order to get news from the other holds.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup> He refused to allow the Legion to garrison troops in the city of Whiterun, and also refused to acknowledge [Ulfric Stormcloak's](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md) claim to the position of High King, or support his rebellion. A peer of Ulfric's, it was said that both had been at odds for many years prior to the Civil War. <sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Gerdur-11)</sup><sup>[*verification needed — "at odds" verified, but former statement re: their "rivalry" needs proof*]</sup>

When the mysterious hero reported the attack by [Alduin](Lore_Alduin.md) on [Helgen](Lore_Helgen.md), Jarl Balgruuf quickly addressed the [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) threat. He ordered troops to [Riverwood](Lore_Riverwood.md), then sent the hero to retrieve the [Dragonstone](Lore_Dragonstone.md) from [Bleak Falls Barrow](Lore_Bleak_Falls_Barrow.md) to gain information on the [return of the dragons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon_Crisis), which proved invaluable to understanding where more dragons might return. Following that, he sent the hero to join a troop to investigate a nearby dragon sighting. After the hero slew the dragon [Mirmulnir](Lore_Mirmulnir.md) and discovered their [Thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md), the Greybeards summoned them to High Hrothgar. Balgruuf explained the summons’ significance and what Nord legend said about being [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md). He appointed the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) as a [Thane](Skyrim_Thane.md) of Whiterun, and also appointed the warrior [Lydia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lydia) as their personal housecarl.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup>

At some point after Mirmulnir's attack, Balgruuf is believed to have considered cooperating with a plan to trap a live dragon in Dragonsreach, and may have attended an unprecedented peace council at High Hrothgar aimed at negotiating a temporary truce in order to address the dragon menace.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-13)</sup> and <sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>. He ultimately chose to side with the Empire in the Civil War in order to protect Whiterun against a Stormcloak siege, and coordinated the defence of the city from Dragonsreach.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup>

## Notes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/18/SR-map-Development_Map.png/200px-SR-map-Development_Map.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-map-Development_Map.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-map-Development_Map.png) Development map - A development map of TES5's [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) had doodled names of the Jarls who ruled each city. Next to [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) on this map, a blurb about that hold's ruler reads: "Jar-El Raskai, of divine birth". An arrow points to "divine birth", with an accompany text saying: "so, the Greater?". This title was later assigned to Balgruuf, being "Balgruuf the Greater". It is unclear whether Balgruuf's title holds the same meaning, or how "divine birth" is supposed to be defined.
