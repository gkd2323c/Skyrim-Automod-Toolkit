# Ice Blade of the Monarch

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/17/MW-item-Ice_Blade_of_the_Monarch.jpg/200px-MW-item-Ice_Blade_of_the_Monarch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Ice_Blade_of_the_Monarch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Ice_Blade_of_the_Monarch.jpg) The Ice Blade of the Monarch (Morrowind) The Ice Blade of the Monarch (or simply the **Ice Blade**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tribunal-1)</sup> is a claymore prized by [Tamrielians](Lore_Tamriel.md). The Archmage [Almion Celmo](Lore_Almion_Celmo.md) of the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) created the claymore<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-MWLalatia-2)</sup> for great warrior Thurgnarr Assi,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup> who attempted to use it to commit regicide in some distant land, and become the new ruler. The assassination failed and Almion Celmo was imprisoned. Almnion enchanted the weapon with the soul of a [Frost Monarch](Lore_Frost_Atronach.md#Frost_Monarch), and therefore it freezes all who feel its edge. It is believed to be capable of cutting through the walls of ice that are said to line the world.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The Ice Blade goes from owner to owner, never settling for long.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TL-3)</sup>

[Yagrum Bagarn](Lore_Yagrum_Bagarn.md), the last living [dwarf](Lore_Dwemer.md), at one point befriended the sorcerer [Divayth Fyr](Lore_Divayth_Fyr.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-MWYB-5)</sup> At Fyr’s behest, Yagrum Bagarn wrote and compiled centuries of personal notes into a book that would become known as *[Tamrielic Lore](Lore_Tamrielic_Lore.md)*,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MWProphecies-6)</sup><sup>:241</sup> listing the Ice Blade of the Monarch among the artifacts of "unimaginable significance". By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), copies had spread across Tamriel, offering readers rare insights into these legendary items,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TA-7)</sup> and Fyr himself hoped to utilize this knowledge in his search for the blade and the other artifacts detailed in the book.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWFR-8)</sup>

In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), the [Imperial Cult](Lore_Imperial_Cult.md) tasked the oracle Adusamsi Assurnarairan with recovering the Ice Blade from Llaren Terano, a worshipper of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) that was located in the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) stronghold of [Rotheran](Lore_Rotheran.md). The bandits there created an arena that housed slaves, which included Adusamsi once she was captured. The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) was sent to rescue her, as well with tasked with recovering the artifact, leading to the bandits extermination, as well as the freedom of the captives.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MWIBQ-9)</sup> The Nerevarine subsequently sold it to the [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) Museum of Artifacts in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Tribunal-1)</sup>

The Ice Blade would later come into the possession of Holrik Frost-Sword, a renowned swordsman. Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Holrik came into a heated argument with Grenwulf the Brawler, a pugilist with the [Fists of Randagulf](Lore_Fists_of_Randagulf.md). Seeking to settle their dispute and showcase their artifact and fighting styles to be superior, the two figures took on a quest to slay a local [arachnid](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Web_Mother) that had nested nearby at Cronvangr Cave. Three weeks had passed, and the ambiguity of their fates led the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) to investigate. Amidst the webbed victims were the corpses of the two, whose bickering caused the end of them. The two manifested as restless spirits, finally banding together to put an end to their executioner. Lending their aid, the Last Dragonborn slew the brood mother, helping put the spirits to rest, leaving the Ice Blade and the Fists to be claimed.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-SRCW-10)</sup>
