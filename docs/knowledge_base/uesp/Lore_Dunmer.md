# Dunmer


Their combination of powerful intellects with strong and agile physiques often produce great warriors and sorcerers.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup> On the battlefield, Dunmer are noted for their skill with a balanced integration of the sword, the bow and destruction magic. Dunmer possess a natural lifespan ranging from several centuries up to a thousand years, though few ever reach this upper limit due to external factors.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> Some of the most ancient and powerful Dunmer mages have extended their lives far beyond this natural span, in some cases enduring for several thousands of years through the use of potent magical practices.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Telvanni-10)</sup> In character, they are generally grim, aloof, and reserved.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup>

Dunmer distrust and are treated distrustfully by other races.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup> They are often called proud, clannish, ruthless, and cruel, from an outsider's point of view, but greatly value loyalty and family.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup> They also apparently have a reputation for quick tempers.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONTilenra-12)</sup> Despite their powerful skills and strengths, the Dunmer's vengeful nature, age-old conflicts, betrayals, and ill-reputation prevent them from gaining more influence. Those born in their homeland of Morrowind are reputed to be considerably less friendly than those who grew up in the Imperial tradition.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup>

Dark Elves are especially susceptible to skin diseases, which are collectively referred to as "corprus" (not to be confused with the [Divine Disease](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corprus#The_Divine_Disease)), being named after the ancient Velothi word for "skin blight".<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b9/LO-people-Veloth.jpg/200px-LO-people-Veloth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-people-Veloth.jpg) Fresco of Veloth leading his people to Resdayn The history of the Dunmer begins with the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) —a race of elves with pale gold skin who followed the prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md) from [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) to Resdayn (present-day Morrowind) seeking religious freedom.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lot S-16)</sup> Their Aldmer predecessors came to Tamriel with deep-rooted traditions of ancestor worship. As Aldmer society grew more stratified, the class divide became a prominent aspect of society and bled into religious beliefs. The ancestor spirits of the upper castes grew in popularity, and in time, they were converted into gods. Where once [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El) had been an ancestor spirit of the nobility, all the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) would eventually claim Auri-El as a common ancestor.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PG3E-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-VOF-18)</sup>

Those who would become the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) were among the mer who vocally disapproved of these societal changes. The Keepers of the Old Ways of Aldmeris believed that all divine forces are driven by ancestor spirits; even the Aedra and Daedra are just the spirits of exceptional mortals who attained power in the afterlife.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TOW-19)</sup> The Psijics' defiant settling of Artaeum was the first of many departures from what is now the home of the High Elves, and a mass exodus of the lower classes soon occurred.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PG3E-17)</sup> The Chimer (who are also referred to as "the Velothi people", a moniker later upheld by the [Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders)) were among those who fled.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

### First Era
The Dunmer were born from the ashes of the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> The infamous confrontation around [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700) led to the death of an ancient and respected war leader known as [Lord Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar), the destruction of the [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House), the disappearance of the entire [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) race then present on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), and the ascension into godhood of four of Nerevar's councilors and closest friends, culminating in a curse from [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura) (the Goddess of Dusk and Dawn and the patron of Nerevar) that transformed the Dunmer into their present appearance.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-NARM-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TRN-24)</sup>

Azura's curse was motivated in response to Nerevar's councilors, the general [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), the sorcerer [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and his wife [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia), breaking an oath they had sworn to Nerevar and Azura by stealing the divine power of the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan) for themselves.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup> When this [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) of new living gods showed no remorse to Azura, she cursed them and all Chimer, tying the fate of the Dunmer and Tribunal together until the end of time.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-NARM-23)</sup> Some Dunmer don't consider the darkening of their skin to be a curse, but rather see their sober complexion as a gift to mark the ascent of the Tribunal.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Vunal-25)</sup> Writings briefly circulated across [Cyrod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrod) in the aftermath with exaggerated claims such as the curse signaled the "end times" and staring into their eyes would cause one to "fall into Oblivion itself",<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ACit C-26)</sup>. Scholars of the time quickly discerned that their nature remained unchanged, the Dunmer continued to live their lives the same way the Chimer had done before the curse, and as the change in appearance was merely physical it would not warrant any valid hysteria.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ACit C-26)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/02/ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar%2C_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar%2C_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar,_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-St._Nerevar,_Moon-and-Star_02.jpg) A bust depicting Hortator Indoril Nerevar The House Dunmer came to worship the Tribunal as their new gods, following their teachings and supplanting the [Daedric patrons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra) of the Chimer, which were relegated to Anticipations of the new Triunes.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-VOFD-27)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TA-28)</sup> The nomadic Ashlander Tribes, on the other hand, decried the Tribunal as false gods and continued the Chimer traditions, especially those of Daedra worship.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-VAM-29)</sup> As a result, the Ashlanders would subsequently spend over three millennia ostracized from House Dunmer society and driven to the margins of Morrowind.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-NFH-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Ash Cult-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Shaali K-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-FTirinaat-33)</sup>

For thousands of years, the divine Tribunal used their power and leadership to protect the Dunmer from foreign invasion and, later, from [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur), their ancient archenemy and the devil incarnate for the Dunmer (for he, too, had stolen divine power).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup>

The Dunmer of Morrowind came into conflict with the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) over the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) complex that would one day be the city of [Stormhold](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stormhold) during [Empress Hestra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hestra)'s reign. The conflict occurred during or prior to the eleventh century of the First Era. This 'War for Silyanorn' included the naval Battle of [Tear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tear) Harbor, wherein the [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril) fleet was defeated by the [Imperial fleet](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) offshore of the House Dres capital city.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Callidus-34)</sup> Though the Imperials claimed the Indoril flagship after the battle, the war's outcome appears to have been in the Dunmer's favor, as Stormhold was under Dunmer rule as late as [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-KTBTVP1-35)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-KTBTVP2-36)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SCOS-37)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SFLS-38)</sup>

Centuries later, the Dunmer butted heads with the [Reman Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md). Seeking to conquer the land of the Dark Elves, [Reman II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_II) initiated an invasion of Morrowind in [1E 2840](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2840).<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-R2TLOA-39)</sup> For the next eighty years, war raged between the Empire and Morrowind as the former attempted to subdue the latter. Even after Reman II's death at Dunmeri hands, his successors [Brazollus Dor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Brazollus_Dor) and [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III) continued the war.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-R2TLOA-39)</sup> By the final year of the First Era in [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920), both sides were nearing exhaustion. After a disastrous Imperial defeat at [Bodrum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bodrum), and the Dunmer's catastrophic defeat at [Ald Marak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ald_Marak), both sides entered peace talks.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-2920LYOTFE-40)</sup> The assassination of Reman III and his heir by the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) brought the war to an abrupt end. Reman III's successor, Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Versidue-Shaie), signed the Cervant Truce with the Dunmer,<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-41)</sup> ending the Imperial invasion with a de facto victory for Morrowind and making it the only nation of Tamriel to never be controlled by the Second Empire.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-2920LYOTFE-40)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-PGE1-P-42)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Jorunn.jpg) King Jorunn alongside Vicecanon Heita-Meen and Grandmaster Tanval Indoril The Dunmer entered the Second Era as the only nation of Tamriel outside of the influence of the Second Empire under the Akaviri Potentate.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-PGE1-P-42)</sup> In contrast with the lengthy Four Score War, the opening centuries of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) were relatively uneventful. In [2E 324](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_324), Potentate Versidue-Shaie was assassinated in [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) by the Morag Tong, who scrawled "MORAG TONG" upon the walls of the Potentate's residence in his own blood.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-MA-43)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-FAD-44)</sup><sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TBOD-45)</sup> The assassination led to the instantaneous and unanimous outlawing of the Morag Tong in all corners of Tamriel, with the exception of its home province of Morrowind. This notoriety is believed by most scholars to have resulted in the splintering of the Tong and the creation of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md).<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-FAD-44)</sup> Despite the infamy they gained after Versidue-Shaie's assassination, the Morag Tong struck again in [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430) and killed his successor [Savirien-Chorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOAshur-46)</sup> and what was believed to be all of his heirs on the same night (though one, Proconsul [Beloren-Kaie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Beloren-Kaie), survived and was later slain by Morag Tong assassin Methas Daram).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOProconsul-47)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOAshur-46)</sup> The Potentate's death at the hands of the Tong promptly resulted in the final disintegration and collapse of the Second Empire, and the beginning of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-TBOD-45)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-PGE3Eras-48)</sup>

After the coronation of [High King Svartr](Lore_Svartr.md) of Western Skyrim in [2E 431](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_431), a delegation from Lord Vivec presented the instrument 'Jarlsbane' to the High King. After being declared "grossly untunable and overly complicated" by the court musicians, it sat in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md)'s [Blue Palace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blue_Palace) as a curiosity for many years. It was rediscovered over a century later by Master Bard Endroni Selvilo of House Redoran shortly before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). Nevertheless, the instrument remained in the city, relocated to the [Bards College](Lore_Bards_College.md).<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-J-49)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/ON-place-Stormhold.jpg/200px-ON-place-Stormhold.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhold.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Stormhold.jpg) Stormhold With the collapse of the Second Empire, Dunmeri enslavement of Argonians saw a substantial increase, though it had been occurring since the early First Era,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-50)</sup><sup>[[51]](#cite_note-51)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-UHD-52)</sup><sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Dres Cup-53)</sup> as slavers from House Dres and House Telvanni were freer than ever to exploit their southern neighbor, and entire tribes of Argonians were dragged off in chains.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-54)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-AAU-55)</sup> Some believe that this mistreatment of the reptilian race led an unnamed Argonian shaman to create the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md), a deadly disease that first appeared in Stormhold—headquarters of the Dunmeri slavers in Black Marsh<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SCOS-37)</sup><sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SFLS-38)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ESOKjarc-56)</sup> —in [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560) before spreading across Tamriel and claiming thousands of lives.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-PGE1-TWG-57)</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-54)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-PGE1-TEC-58)</sup> Some time after the outbreak of the Knahaten Flu, House Redoran sent a military expedition to the great stone city of [Mazzatun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ruins_of_Mazzatun) in the Shadowfen region of Black Marsh, intending to wipe out the [Xit-Xaht](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xit-Xaht) tribe, whom they suspected was responsible for the disease.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Tfa Iw RDEADV-59)</sup> When the Knahaten Flu subsided after [2E 603](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_603), Dunmeri slavers of House Dres remained one of the few non-Argonians who saw any reason to trouble themselves with Black Marsh, and continued to raid the north of the swamps.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-PGE3-A-54)</sup>

In [2E 559](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_559), a dispute over a House Hlaalu trading post in contested territory erupted into an armed conflict known as the [War of Two houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:War_of_Two_Houses). The so-called "war" consisted of only one battle, fought by House Dres mercenaries and Hlaalu guards at the trading post. Despite the Dres outnumbering the Hlaalu five-to-one, the conflict ended with a decisive House Hlaalu victory when the leader of the Hlaalu guards, Purilla Falen, defended a portal that allowed a full cohort of Hlaalu battle-merchants to swarm the Dres mercenaries.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-WTH-60)</sup> Prior to the [Second Akaviri Invasion](Lore_Second_Akaviri_Invasion.md), the city of Davon's Watch was sacked by marauding Nords under the leadership of Thane [Unnvald Ironhand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Unnvald_Ironhand), who was renowned among the Nords of Eastern Skyrim for his raids into Morrowind.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Hallfrida-61)</sup> At the same time, the Argonian hero-to-be [Keshu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keshu_the_Black_Fin) and her Legion waged "war" against House Dres by raiding some of the slave caravans headed for Morrowind for a number of seasons.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Keshu-62)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/95/ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg/200px-ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Vivec%27s_Antlers_Land-Coral.jpg) The Kamal invaders met their watery end among the coral of Vivec's Antlers. The Dunmer were key players in the [Liberation War](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Liberation_War), better remembered as the Second Akaviri Invasion. In [2E 572](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_572), a strong [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) force led by [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal) besieged [Windhelm](Lore_Windhelm.md) in Eastern [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), killing Queen [Mabjaarn Flame-Hair](Lore_Mabjaarn_Flame-Hair.md) and Princess [Nurnhilde](Lore_Nurnhilde.md). Leaving the ruined city behind, the eastern army continued on their way to [Riften](Lore_Riften.md) but found it heavily fortified by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), who were led by [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md) and [Wulfharth Ash-King](Lore_Wulfharth.md), the latter of whom was called back to Nirn from Sovngarde by the Greybeards.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Jt SK-63)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-TSAI-64)</sup>

The invaders decided to bypass the Nordic city and entered Morrowind instead. Led by Almalexia, the Dunmer army fought hard, but was forced to retreat into Stonefalls (although it is rumored that Mother Morrowind and the Skald-Prince collaborated with one another to set this up despite their races' ancient enmity).<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-At S-65)</sup> With their backs towards the Inner Sea, the Akaviri fought viciously against the combined Nord-Dunmer force, but with the rest of the Akaviri fleet on the horizon it seemed that the Tamrielics would be overrun and slaughtered.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-At S-65)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-UA-66)</sup>

While the Nords and Dunmer fought the Akaviri to the north, a group of slaves led by [Heita-Meen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heita-Meen) took the city of Stormhold from their captors. Stormhold was a prominent hub for the slave trade at the time,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SFLS-38)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SCOS-37)</sup> and the Dunmer's primary foothold in Black Marsh.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-ESOKjarc-56)</sup> Heita-Meen had received a vision from the Hist showing the fate of the Nord and Dark Elven armies if their losing battle continued unabated. Thus, she rallied the Shellbacks, and marched into Stonefalls to join the Tamrielic offensive. The Keshu's Black Fin legion likewise answered Jorunn Skald-King's request for aid and joined the battle.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Keshu-62)</sup> Some of the Dark Elves saw what they perceived as an army of angry slaves approaching, and turned their blades against them at first.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-At S-65)</sup> However, the Argonians' intent was soon realized, and together, with the assistance of a great tidal wave summoned by Vivec,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-VOF-18)</sup><sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Brela-67)</sup> the three races claimed victory against the invaders and drove them into the sea, where they drowned by the thousands.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-TSAI-64)</sup> Thus, the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) was formed out of the Kingdom of Eastern Skyrim, the Argonian tribes of Shadowfen and Thornmarsh, and the Dunmeri Great Houses Redoran, Indoril, Hlaalu, and Dres.

In gratitude for their timely assistance, the Pact-loyal Houses granted the Argonians freedom from slavery under the terms of the Pact, and nominally outlawed slavery altogether.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-PP:C-68)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-UA-66)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-69)</sup> House Telvanni refused to join the Pact, and as a result freedom was not guaranteed for outlanders in Telvanni territory, as the Ebonheart Pact's jurisdiction did not extend there. House Dres continued to keep non-Argonian slaves after the signing of the Pact, though they dismissed accusations of doing so as 'slander and calumny', and did not 'officially' condone the perpetuation of slavery within the areas they administrated. Outwardly, the Dres nominally remained loyal to the alliance and all it stood for.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-PP:C-68)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ZOSCorrespondence-70)</sup>

[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Ebonheart_Pact.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Ebonheart_Pact.png) The sigil of the Ebonheart Pact, which Redoran, Indoril, Dres, and Hlaalu joined Though they shared a common bond, the Ebonheart Pact was a fragile alliance. The Argonians did not forget the centuries of slavery they endured beneath the heels of the Dark Elves. The abolition of Argonian slavery did not result in the immediate cessation of all slaver activity, and it would take a year for most Dark Elven holdings to comply with the Tribunal's order. When Keshu the Black Fin and her Black Fin Legion toured Morrowind as ambassadors for the Argonian people in the early days of the Alliance, they were met with a good deal of fear and hatred. The local Dunmer were uncomfortable with the prospect of a heavily armed contingent of Argonian soldiers approaching their village. They were turned away in some towns, and attacked by those with sizable militias. Not every Dark Elf would give the Saxhleel ambassadors the cold shoulder. Having heard of their efforts at the Battle of Vivec's Antlers, some were very welcoming and appreciative to their southern neighbors. Eventually, the people of Morrowind would grow more accustomed to the presence of free Argonians.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-71)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-AAU-55)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/87/ON-npc-Vicecanon_Servyna.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Vicecanon_Servyna.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vicecanon_Servyna.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vicecanon_Servyna.jpg) Vicecanon Servyna of Stormhold Dunmeri heroes of the Liberation War include Grandmaster [Tanval Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tanval_Indoril), who fought back-to-back alongside Holgunn One-Eye when their squads were slaughtered.<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-72)</sup> The Dunmer used more wood than bone in their weapons and armor when the Pact was formed. Some bone molders learned the carpenter's trade to stay in business during this time.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-73)</sup>

In Stormhold, conditions changed after the Pact was formed and slavery was outlawed. Slavers who operated out of Stormhold were prosecuted, sentenced to hang in cages from the city's Ayleid arches for a year, then exiled from Black Marsh.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-74)</sup> Shadowfen was returned to Argonian hands,<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ESOPideelus-75)</sup> and Stormhold was outfitted with three vicecanons, one from each of the Pact's races. All three were heroes of the Akaviri War. Among them was Vicecanon Heita-Meen, who received that fateful vision from the Hist during the war, as well as Vicecanon Servyna of the Dunmer and Hrondar of Skyrim.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-76)</sup>

During the Alliance War, a string of murders perpetrated by the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md#First_Dominion) occurred within Stormhold. Tensions were high between the Dark Elf and Argonian populations of the city. Some Dunmer felt the Argonians resented them for their history of slavery, while others outright loathed the Argonians, holding onto the opinion that they were "lesser".<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ESOTiiril-77)</sup> Some of the Dark Elves felt the Pact was a mistake.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ESOGethis-78)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ESODendras-79)</sup> All of the victims were Dark Elves, so some mer assumed the Argonians may have been responsible.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-ESOTiiril-77)</sup> Some even entertained the idea that the [Shadowscales](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadowscales) were involved,<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-ESODendras-79)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ESOGethis-78)</sup> but all was put to rest when the vicecanons discovered the Dominion's presence in the ruins of nearby [Silyanorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silyanorn).<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-80)</sup>

After Dagoth Ur awakened in [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882), the Tribunal had to devote their full attention to him, allowing for the [Treaty of the Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) with [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s Third Empire that made Morrowind an autonomous province.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-PTDDU-81)</sup> Many Dunmer were shocked and angered by the signing of the Armistice, which they viewed as a betrayal on behalf of the Tribunal.<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Orvas-82)</sup>

#### The Armistice
Morrowind's military power was severely limited by the Armistice. Tiber Septim demanded that the Tribunal turn over the Numidium to the Empire, and to allow the Imperial Legion a sizable presence in the province.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-TAH-83)</sup> In exchange, Morrowind was permitted to keep its autonomy, maintaining the power of the Tribunal Temple and the Great Houses,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup> while being supervised by Imperial representatives in the capital, [Almalexia City](Lore_Almalexia_(city).md), and in each of the six newly-created administrative districts.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup> Morrowind was to be governed under Imperial Law, with exceptions being made for Great House Law. Local House guards were permissible, and the Empire generally tried to stay out of Tribunal affairs. The province was to be ruled by a figurehead King, who was chosen directly by the Emperor.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup> Additionally, and perhaps most importantly in the minds of Dunmer elites, the practice of slavery (and persecution of abolitionists) in Morrowind was protected by the Empire.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-BB-84)</sup> Dunmer religious practices were similarly protected.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup>

The Armistice was supported most enthusiastically by [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md), with [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) grudgingly following Vivec's lead, while [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) did so only with the strong provisions protecting slavery.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup> [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) remained neutral and isolationist throughout the proceedings, until the support of Hlaalu and Redoran was officially established, when they joined in on the support of the treaty.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup> [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril) bowed to the will of the Tribunal, but remained hostile to the Empire; in fact, many Indoril nobles chose ritual suicide rather than to rule an occupied nation.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup> At this time, House Hlaalu seized the opportunity to settle old scores with Houses Indoril and Redoran, using its new alliance with the Empire to reclaim lost lands and to place Hlaalu nobles in positions of power within the new Imperial administration. House Indoril remained powerful in [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md), Morrowind's capital.

Following the Armistice, the island of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) became a Temple preserve with some small settlements maintained by the Great Houses as well as scattered [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Ashlanders) tribes. However, the island was not opened to large-scale settlement by civilians or exploitation by the East Empire Company until [3E 414](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_414).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup>

### Third Era

#### Fractious warring and revolt across Tamriel
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c1/DF-misc-Intro_Cinematic_Dunmer_Nord_Battle_HD.png/200px-DF-misc-Intro_Cinematic_Dunmer_Nord_Battle_HD.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Intro_Cinematic_Dunmer_Nord_Battle_HD.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-Intro_Cinematic_Dunmer_Nord_Battle_HD.png) The Dark Elves at war with the Nords Following the wars of the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) period in the late 300s of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), conflicts continued to brew across the continent, one such conflict was a battle between Dark Elves and the Nords.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Daggerfall Intro Narration-85)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Decrypting The Elder Scrolls-86)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-DUG-87)</sup><sup>:3</sup>

#### The Blight Returns and the Nerevarine Prophecy is Fulfilled
In [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882), Dagoth Ur awoke from his slumber and unleashed the [Ash Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Blight) upon Morrowind.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-DUP-88)</sup> The Blight is a weather phenomenon that originated from within [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). Described as a health-threatening, ash-heavy volcanic cloud, exposure to the Blight mostly occurred through tainted ash storms.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-MWGen TB-89)</sup> These ash storms, referred to as "blight storms", carried red dust, giving the roiling, dusty storms a crimson tint.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-MWGen Bli Sto-90)</sup> By [3E 400](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_400), blight storms had become more frequent and widespread, and soul sickness had spread in regions close to Red Mountain.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-DUP-88)</sup> The territories of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) were particularly threatened, requiring the upkeep of large volunteer defense forces in [Maar Gan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Maar_Gan) and [Molag Mar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Molag_Mar) to deal with monsters that managed to breach the [Ghostfence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostfence) and protect pilgrims.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-Maar Gan-91)</sup><sup>[[90]](#cite_note-Molag Mar-92)</sup>

The blighted ash of Red Mountain is associated with life-giving powers and immortality. According to [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) and various other sources, the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) once utilized the ash of Red Mountain, the Blight, to make themselves immortal.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-LOV36-93)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-NARM-23)</sup> In addition to more mundane corprus beasts, the Blight and corprus were responsible for the creation of [ash creatures](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Creature).

The severity of the blight storms peaked in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), ushering in the **Vvardenfell Crisis**.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-Assas-94)</sup> As infection cases rose, boats from Vvardenfell began to be turned away by the mainland for fear of contamination, isolating the island from the capital of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-Apelles-95)</sup><sup>[[94]](#cite_note-Asciene-96)</sup> Deformed "corprus men" started appearing from the slopes of Red Mountain, following the blight storms and bringing death and disease with them.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-97)</sup> Mortals and animals that were twisted and driven mad by the Blight wandered the Ashlands, making travel dangerous.

The Blight had an adverse effect on the [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md)'s fauna. It wiped out [cliff striders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cliff_Strider), [nix-oxen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nix-Ox), and [vvardvarks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vvardvark) off the island by the late Third Era.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-VNAT-98)</sup> It affected the profits drawn from the land by leading to the closure of mines in Blight-prone locations. Blight storms killed crops<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-MWGen MBS-99)</sup> as well as natural flora,<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-MWGen Bli Sto-90)</sup> and had the potential to make creatures stronger and more dangerous to unwary travelers. All animals could be affected—herd animals, game animals, none were immune. Animals who got the red taint went berserk: they bit, they fought, and tried to kill everything they saw.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-100)</sup> Herders became ill and watched their herds die. Hunters could not find food, and farms failed.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-MWGen Bli Sto-90)</sup>

Near the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the Tribunal's power crumbled upon the advent of the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), the reincarnation of Nerevar whom Azura had prophesied would come to set right the mistakes of the past.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup> The Nerevarine, an outlander to Morrowind and purported agent of [Emperor Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), severed their divine power at its source: the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan).<sup>[[99]](#cite_note-Morrowind MQ-101)</sup> The Heart had been discovered in the First Era by the Dwemer, and their plan to exploit it is what caused the Battle of Red Mountain in the first place.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TBORM-22)</sup> The Nerevarine released the Heart and destroyed Dagoth Ur. Sotha Sil and Almalexia were killed soon after.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup> Vivec went missing, and some claim he was "taken" by the Daedra.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup>

The Nerevarine eschewed taking power in Morrowind, instead reportedly setting sail for [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) and leaving the Dunmer to make their own future.<sup>[[100]](#cite_note-Rumors-102)</sup>

#### Third Era Diasporas from Morrowind
Throughout the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), several waves of Dunmer refugees have left Morrowind. The earliest example was roughly half a century from [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). These immigrants left for Cyrodiil to escape the Tribunal Temple's doctrines and the province's rigid society. They mainly settled in [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal), where they greatly shaped its culture. One of these immigrants was [Andel Indarys](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Andel_Indarys) of House Hlaalu who became Count Cheydinhal in a noticeably short time. A schism occurred between the local [Nibenese](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenese) and the Dunmer, especially with the suspicion surrounding Count Indarys' ascension.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-GTC-103)</sup><sup>[[102]](#cite_note-OBMach-104)</sup>

Another exodus took place later in the era. These Dunmer went to [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md) and brought about a renaissance of power and influence. Winterhold's culture became enriched with new ideas from the east and its old mercantile spirit had been revived. The [Ysmir Collective](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ysmir_Collective) became a cornerstone of academic life at the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) when books were recovered from destruction in Morrowind.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-PGE3SR-105)</sup> Year after year, more Dunmer traveled to the College of Winterhold and so many elves called the city home. After the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md), however, the Nords held a growing distrust in magic that affected their relationship with the Dunmer and the College mages. Many of them left so they would not have to endure their resentment.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-SRBrelyna-106)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In the first few years of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), as Vivec no longer held [Baar Dau](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baar_Dau) in place, it began its descent into the city of [Vivec](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City) once more. The [Ingenium](Lore_Ingenium.md), a magical machine powered by souls, was created to hold the moon in place, used to create a [rift](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Portals) to the [Fields of Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fields_of_Regret) from where it leeched the energy used to hold the moon aloft, an act theorized to have occurred with the approval of the soul hungry [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md).<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-IC-107)</sup> A Dunmer named [Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City_Sul) destroyed the Ingenium in an attempt to save his lover from being used to power it.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup> Consequentially, Baar Dau hit Vivec City with the full strength of its original descent, destroying Vivec City and triggering a series of natural disasters that devastated Vvardenfell and the province as a whole in what is now called the Red Year.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup>

In the aftermath, the Argonian armies of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) invaded the south and east of the land, but were repelled by the armies of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-LTJ-108)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Adril-109)</sup> As a result of the calamities, many Dunmer have fled to other places around Tamriel, notably [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the small island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), which had become a territory of the sovereign nation of Morrowind in [4E 16](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_16).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup> House Redoran became the eminent power in Morrowind for their efforts to protect the province, and the capital was moved accordingly to the ancestral Redoran seat of [Blacklight](Lore_Blacklight.md).<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Adril-109)</sup> While the precise date or circumstances are unknown, Morrowind had ceased to be considered a territory of the Third Empire by [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48).<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-Adril-109)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TIC-13)</sup><sup>:109</sup><sup>[[108]](#cite_note-LOS-110)</sup><sup>:16</sup>

## Culture
Dark Elven culture is divided between the nomadic Ashlanders and the settled Great Houses.

Some Dark Elves keep [nix-hounds](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nix-Hound) as pets. While usually vicious predators, nixes are well-known to be affectionate, attentive, and faithful pets that will follow their owners around. They are also commonly used as trackers, watch-hounds, and netch-deflators.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-Creation-111)</sup><sup>[[110]](#cite_note-Manual-112)</sup><sup>[[111]](#cite_note-113)</sup> Nix-Hounds were used as war dogs in the House Dagoth chap'thil.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-FSo KW-114)</sup> Ashlanders use nix-hound parts in a variety of their tribal customs.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-ONSorim Nakar-115)</sup> [Pony guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pony_Guar) are also popular pets.<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-116)</sup>

Taxidermied cliff racers are traditionally stuffed with scrib jelly and suspended from a rope for children to beat up on.<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-117)</sup> Some dollmakers produce fancy wax dolls clothed in hand-crafted garments, which can be posed with the careful application of heat.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-118)</sup>

Dark Elven taverns are called "cornerclubs".<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-119)</sup>

Under the rule of the Tribunal and the Council system of government, the House Dunmer of Morrowind were educated free of charge by the Temple, which provided education as well as shelter and healthcare. For these reasons, beggars were considered rare enough that some citizens claimed no beggars could be found within Morrowind as late as the closing years of the Third Era.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-120)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-121)</sup><sup>[[120]](#cite_note-122)</sup>

Certain Dunmer have their skin covered in [magic script](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magic_Script_Alphabet) seen as white tattoos called glyphs of warding, which serve as protection against [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead) entities such as vengeful ancestor ghosts.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-123)</sup>

### Holidays
[Ancestor's Day](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ancestor%27s_Day) It is a quiet and respectful day in honor of the ancestor spirits. Families and friends gather around the clan hearth to give each other gifts and enjoy food. It is the Dunmer's equivalent to the pan-Tamrielic holiday, [Tales and Tallows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tales_and_Tallows).<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-IWADE-124)</sup> New Life Some House Dunmer families roast a pony guar for New Life.<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-125)</sup> The Dunmer of [Ebonheart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonheart) celebrate the New Life with an act of defiance. When dancing was very briefly outlawed in the city, the citizens found a way around it by promptly inventing the Lava Foot Stomp. A dance they called a necessity, should their boot ever catch aflame, and performed it various taverns across town.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-ONBreda-126)</sup> More devout Dunmer instead celebrate **Triune**, where they visit a temple and learn about the lives and lessons of their [saints](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_Saints).<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-IWADE-124)</sup>
### Hunting and Craftsmanship
The Dunmer take advantage of the bounty provided by the beasts that live in their hostile home region. [Ash hopper](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ash_Hopper) meat and jelly are harvested for their culinary use and alchemical properties,<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-127)</sup><sup>[[125]](#cite_note-128)</sup><sup>[[126]](#cite_note-129)</sup> and their chitin for crafting armor and lava-resistant coverings.<sup>[[127]](#cite_note-NJV-130)</sup> Ash hoppers are also low maintenance pets.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-ONAmber Ash Hopper-131)</sup> [Kagouti](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagouti) are hunted for their hide, which is made into leather and used in clothing and household goods.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-132)</sup>

A wide variety of colored glass is commonly used in the creation of Dunmeri dishes and drinkware. Green and peach glass are some such examples. Pewter and silver are fancier options used by the Great Houses.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-133)</sup><sup>[[131]](#cite_note-134)</sup> Some cornerclubs sell specially-made mazte glasses.<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-135)</sup> Vvardenfell's clay is used to create traditional redware pottery.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-136)</sup>

### Agriculture
Living in a province of ashlands and fungal forests, the Dunmer have vastly different crops and livestock than the rest of Tamriel. Farmers in southern Morrowind raise the reptilian [bantam guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bantam_Guar) for their eggs and meat, similarly to [chickens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chicken) in other parts of the world. Some Dunmer raise pony guar for their skins and meat.<sup>[[134]](#cite_note-MFPO-137)</sup> The Dunmer of Deshaan have taken to herding [shalks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shalk) for their carapaces, meat, and resin.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-138)</sup> Bloated shalks can be milked to obtain "shalk blue", which is used as a pigment in tattoo ink.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-139)</sup>

Netchimen take on the profession of domesticating and herding [netches](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Netch). While feral betties usually keep a harem of several bulls, netchimen often keep the female-male ratio low for faster breeding.<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-MWFrinnius-140)</sup> As netches are dangerous to harvest for even the most experienced netchimen, netch products are relatively scarce in comparison to others.<sup>[[138]](#cite_note-SD-141)</sup> [Netch leather](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Netch_Leather) and [jelly](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Netch_Jelly) are both products the Dark Elves derive from these gaseous floating creatures.<sup>[[139]](#cite_note-142)</sup><sup>[[140]](#cite_note-143)</sup><sup>[[141]](#cite_note-KCAHC-144)</sup>

#### Beasts of Burden
The Dark Elves make use of [nix-oxen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nix-Ox) (fungus eating arthropods) and [guar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Guar) (bipedal reptiles with short arms) when they seek animal labor. Guar are standard mounts and pack animals in Morrowind.<sup>[[142]](#cite_note-ESOGNG-145)</sup><sup>[[143]](#cite_note-146)</sup> Guar hide is cured to make clothing and household goods.<sup>[[144]](#cite_note-147)</sup>

Nix-oxen were particularly important on Vvardenfell before they were all wiped out by the Ash Blight.<sup>[[145]](#cite_note-VTAN-148)</sup> Nix wranglers hunted for wild nix-oxen to sell on the market. Younger nix-oxen were more valuable because their chitin was more durable, making them easier to tame. Nix-oxen were tamed by opening the creature's armored back and tampering with a bundle of nerves beneath the flesh (known as the "drive chamber") to render the nix docile. Wealthier nix farmers could hire tradesmen known as "drenlyns" to tame their nix-oxen for them.<sup>[[146]](#cite_note-DEDH-149)</sup>

After the Ebonheart Pact outlawed slavery of their Nord and Argonian allies, farmers who relied heavily on members of those races for slave labor began to employ nix-oxen in place of slaves. In particular, nix-oxen were used to plow fields. Nix-oxen have a second pair of appendages located on the sides of their head known as pedipalps, which are used by the beasts as a form of protection. Farmers forced their nix-oxen to drag these appendages through the soil to create furrows for crops such as [saltrice](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saltrice) and [marshmerrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marshmerrow). <sup>[[147]](#cite_note-APMNO-150)</sup>

As the years went by, a nix's pedipalps would erode from being dragged through the dirt. Not only would a creature with eroded pedipalps be useless to the farmer, but it would lose a valuable defense mechanism. Nix-oxen whose pedipalps were too eroded for the beast to work were either butchered for their carapaces or left to starve in the ashen wastes. The lack of proper pedipalps made survival for abandoned nix-oxen virtually impossible. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a concerned House Dunmer farmer drafted a petition in an attempt to pass a law requiring all farmers to retire their nix-oxen after a certain amount of years. The farmer believed that the practice of abandoning nix-oxen who could no longer work was inhumane, and saw such treatment as unfair considering how much of a boon nix-oxen were to Dunmeri farming communities which lacked slaves.<sup>[[147]](#cite_note-APMNO-150)</sup>

#### Kwama Mining
[Kwama](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama) eggs are a principal agricultural commodity in Morrowind,<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Gen Dia Eggmine-151)</sup> and a staple of Dunmeri cuisine.<sup>[[149]](#cite_note-Gen Dia FIM-152)</sup> Kwama live and breed in large communal subterranean colonies called egg mines (or "eggmines").<sup>[[150]](#cite_note-153)</sup> Kwama miners protect the bugs' eggs from poachers, predators, and raiding kwama foragers from other colonies. They harvest much of what the bugs put out, leaving enough eggs to sustain the colony's growth.<sup>[[148]](#cite_note-Gen Dia Eggmine-151)</sup> Kwama are also valued for their [cuttle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Cuttle), a tough, waxy substance that comes from their beaks and is valued by alchemists.<sup>[[151]](#cite_note-154)</sup><sup>[[152]](#cite_note-155)</sup>

### The Arts of War
The Dunmer have [Daedric masks styled in tribute to various revered figures](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Faces), such as the Daedric Faces of Inspiration ([Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md)), Terror ([Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar)), and God ([Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md)).<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-DFTDE-156)</sup> Due to their rarity, Daedric equipment is typically only seen in the hands of the upper class, where they are passed on through generations of kin. Their price tag makes them a considerable portion of a Great House noble's personal wealth.<sup>[[153]](#cite_note-MWGarothmukheavy-157)</sup> Advanced enchanters typically reserve the greatest of enchantments for equipment of the highest tier, such as Daedric.<sup>[[154]](#cite_note-Mo Armor-158)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/MW-race-Dark Elf.png/200px-MW-race-Dark Elf.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-race-Dark Elf.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-race-Dark Elf.png) Dunmer Swordsman wearing [Bonemold armor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Bonemold) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/BK-misc-Dunmer_Warrior.jpeg/200px-BK-misc-Dunmer_Warrior.jpeg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Dunmer_Warrior.jpeg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-misc-Dunmer_Warrior.jpeg) A Dunmer warrior Wood is in very short supply in Morrowind, but this does not deprive the Dark Elves of means to arm themselves.<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-MWGarothmukshortbow-159)</sup>

Morrowind is host to innumerable deposits of volcanic [glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malachite). Ebony weapons are made from a rare form of volcanic glass found almost exclusively in subterranean deposits and surface lava flows from volcanoes such as Red Mountain. "Ebony" refers to the glassy black surface of the weapons.<sup>[[156]](#cite_note-160)</sup> The House Dunmer favor glass armor forged from malachite for lightweight weapons and armor, and ebony for heavier fare.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-138)</sup> Glass is prized for its sharpness and delicate balance in weapons,<sup>[[157]](#cite_note-161)</sup> as well as its durability and the ease of maneuverability it provides for armor.<sup>[[158]](#cite_note-162)</sup>

In the way of light armor, most Dunmer prefer reinforced chitin. Chitin is used by Ashlanders and House Dunmer alike.<sup>[[159]](#cite_note-163)</sup> Chitin can be used to fashion spiked war axes, which are in some ways more like a mace than an axe.<sup>[[160]](#cite_note-164)</sup> Ashlanders craft chitin bows that are laminated in bone-shell and resin.<sup>[[155]](#cite_note-MWGarothmukshortbow-159)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/99/LG-cardart-Dreugh_Shell_Armor.png/200px-LG-cardart-Dreugh_Shell_Armor.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dreugh_Shell_Armor.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dreugh_Shell_Armor.png) A full set of dreugh shell armor When in need of medium armor, the Dunmer rely on bonemold, which is generally lighter and more durable than Imperial chain and scale armors.<sup>[[161]](#cite_note-165)</sup> [Dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh) hide armor is a remarkably strong and rather exotic kind of medium armor of Dark Elven origin.<sup>[[162]](#cite_note-166)</sup>

Different weapons are valued by different classes of people in Morrowind. The Telvanni prefer the tanto (a stylish dagger of Akaviri design) as a matrix for enchantments, and often equip their mercenaries with them.<sup>[[163]](#cite_note-167)</sup> In the late Third Era, retired Legion smiths settling in Morrowind bolstered the popularity of Imperial shortswords among the growing Dunmer merchant class, as well as Hlaalu nobles and their retainers.<sup>[[164]](#cite_note-168)</sup> In Morrowind, glass, ebony, and Daedric longswords are precious heirlooms passed down through generations of noble warriors in a Great House.<sup>[[165]](#cite_note-169)</sup> Dwemer and Daedric maces are also prized by Great House Dunmer as family heirlooms.<sup>[[166]](#cite_note-170)</sup> Spears fashioned by Ashlanders often have a spike at the foot of the shaft.<sup>[[167]](#cite_note-171)</sup>

The Marshmerrow, Salt Rice, and Golden Reed societies of the Dissident Priests all have their own unarmed, unarmored martial arts traditions.<sup>[[168]](#cite_note-172)</sup>

### Architecture
The Dunmer are known for some of the most alien architecture in Tamriel. All Dunmer buildings have to be well-suited to deal with the climate of their particular region, which varies greatly across the province. The [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) build bizarre, twisted mushroom towers and dwellings with magic. <sup>[[169]](#cite_note-GHOM-173)</sup> This style was present in Vvardenfell in the [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md)<sup>[[170]](#cite_note-ONFiri-174)</sup><sup>[[171]](#cite_note-ESOMW-175)</sup> and Third Eras.<sup>[[172]](#cite_note-Sadrith_Mora-176)</sup> [Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), [Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril), [Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) and [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) use more conventional methods to build their structures.

The Redoran build organic, crab-like houses. The Redoran town of [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) on Vvardenfell was famous for being built around the shell of a long-dead Emperor Crab.<sup>[[169]](#cite_note-GHOM-173)</sup> This style was present in Vvardenfell in the [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md)<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-ESOMW-175)</sup> and [Third Eras](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[173]](#cite_note-Ald.27ruhn TES3-177)</sup> The Hlaalu build simple, cubic stone houses. This style was present in Vvardenfell in the Second<sup>[[171]](#cite_note-ESOMW-175)</sup> and Third Eras.<sup>[[174]](#cite_note-Balmora-178)</sup> The style of architecture known as Velothi style is seen in ancestral tombs, ancient towers, and Temple buildings.<sup>[[175]](#cite_note-Morrowind Velothi Buildings-179)</sup><sup>[[176]](#cite_note-ON-180)</sup>

[Basalt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Basalt) is often considered to be unsuitable for delicate stonework, but early Dunmer are known to have crafted with it regardless.<sup>[[177]](#cite_note-181)</sup>

Responsible Architecture is a style that originated in the First Era and is attributed to the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md).<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-FALT-182)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-VOF-18)</sup> This style was similar to that seen in various [Daedric ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Ruins) in Morrowind, and it was used by the famed architect Guylaine Marilie to build a prison to contain Daedra at the site where Fort Arand was later constructed.<sup>[[178]](#cite_note-FALT-182)</sup> A common architectural style in mainland Morrowind features ornate stone buildings with peaked, shingled roofs. It was common in mainland Morrowind in the Second Era and even extended into Cyrodiil. The city of Mournhold retained the style in the Third Era.<sup>[[179]](#cite_note-Mournholdin TES3-183)</sup><sup>[[176]](#cite_note-ON-180)</sup>

Unlike their settled brethren, the Ashlanders are nomadic, and live in migrating encampments of large tents made of netch hide.

### Transportation

The residents of Vivec City had the option of paying a gondolier to take them across the water to another canton.<sup>[[185]](#cite_note-190)</sup> [Silt Striders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silt_Strider) were commonly used to ferry passengers by land on Vvardenfell. Passage by way of Strider was facilitated by caravaners.<sup>[[186]](#cite_note-191)</sup><sup>[[187]](#cite_note-192)</sup>

### Fashion
Physical appearance is rather important in some sects of Dunmer society, particularly with regards to the Great Houses. Some exalted members of the upper class will refuse to speak to those who are not well-dressed.<sup>[[194]](#cite_note-202)</sup>

When the Dark Elves were not allied with the Argonians, they often made use of their body parts in crafts, such as plates made of Argonian scales.<sup>[[195]](#cite_note-203)</sup> Fashion-forward Dunmer were finding ways to use Argonian horns to enhance their own appearance. One such use for extracted horns was bone blush, an ivory powder applied to the face. It was especially popular at the height of the Argonian-horn fashion trend of the Second Era, but became much rarer after the Ebonheart Pact was formed.<sup>[[196]](#cite_note-204)</sup>

## Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/MW-concept-11.jpg/200px-MW-concept-11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-11.jpg) Sketch of an Indoril guard The Dunmer have been defined by their environment. [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) looms as large in their collective thought as it does on the horizon.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup> Its ash and lava sculpted the attitudes of Vvardenfell's residents.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> One does not have to travel far outside of many cities to find a giant [Foyada](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Foyada) cutting through the land like a scar. Native-born Dunmer tended to look down on "outlanders", which include both other races and Dunmer born outside of Morrowind, though the intensity of this ethnocentrism varies.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup> For thousands of years under the Tribunal, Dunmer society was structured much like the Chimer society had been: domestic Great Houses governed by Ruling Councils competed against each other for power and territory, while nomadic groups eschewed relative modernity for ancient tribal practices.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup><sup>[[169]](#cite_note-GHOM-173)</sup>

Houses tend to rise and fall; many have faded to extinction over the years or sprouted up to challenge others.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup><sup>[[169]](#cite_note-GHOM-173)</sup><sup>[[197]](#cite_note-TBOMB-205)</sup> At the close of the Third Era, the five most important houses were Hlaalu, Redoran, Telvanni, Dres and Indoril, each which had existed for thousands of years, since at least the [War of the First Council](Lore_War_of_the_First_Council.md).<sup>[[198]](#cite_note-TWOTFC-206)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup><sup>[[169]](#cite_note-GHOM-173)</sup> While the details are limited, it is known that each of the Houses survived the cataclysms following the fall of the Tribunal Temple - with the exception of House Hlaalu, which lost its position to the previously-unknown House Sadras. In the most recent two centuries, House Redoran has become the most powerful of the Great Houses, taking the mantle the Hlaalu had held prior, and the Indoril before them.<sup>[[199]](#cite_note-DBMorvayn-207)</sup> Historically, Indoril has been so heavily involved with the Temple itself that it is hard to distinguish the two.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup> Following the collapse of the Tribunal Temple, House Indoril and the New Temple became regarded as one and the same.<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-The Rec-208)</sup>

In the Ashlands, native tribes ruled without laws or government and lived strictly by codes, rituals, and ancient traditions usually dictated by a wise woman or seer and implemented by a clan leader. They lived in the deserts and plains of the [Ashlands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlands) and [Grazelands](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grazelands).

*→ See also: **[Ashlanders](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlanders)*** Prior to the Imperial occupation, Morrowind was an agrarian aristocracy: mostly free farmers, herders, and fishermen ruled by great houses and their noble councils. Since the Third Empire's occupation, especially on Vvardenfell, the Dunmer developed a mercantile economy on the model of the Empire, ruled by the Emperor, law, and legions, but driven by trade in crafts and goods.<sup>[[201]](#cite_note-209)</sup>

#### Practices and Norms
The peaceful status quo for Dunmer was considered quite brutal by westerners, even when led by living gods.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup> Political machinations are constant.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup> Assassinations are common, with the legally established [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) providing services.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OM-7)</sup> A huge amount of black market activity thrives along Morrowind's extensive coastlines. [Dissidents](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dissident_Priests) to the Temple and other subversives over the years have been hunted and persecuted.<sup>[[202]](#cite_note-THOT-210)</sup><sup>[[203]](#cite_note-POT-211)</sup>

Slavery used to be commonplace.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> Mostly [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) or Argonian slaves were trafficked, although many men and mer were also enslaved.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup> Goblins were also seen as a significant slave "resource".<sup>[[204]](#cite_note-ONAldryn-212)</sup> The Third Empire of Tiber Septim had banned slavery, but Dunmer were allowed to continue the practice in their entrance to the Empire as part of the Armistice, defining it as one of their own "sacred and traditional laws". Despite the claim that owning slaves was their right, many Dunmer still found the practice abhorrent and immoral,<sup>[[205]](#cite_note-OTT-213)</sup> and abolitionist groups continued to crop up within Dunmeri society.<sup>[[206]](#cite_note-MWGNA-214)</sup> By the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), [King Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Helseth) officially abolished slavery.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-PGE3M-21)</sup>

[Necromancy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necromancy) is practiced openly by some Dunmer, though never with Dunmer corpses. The corpses of animals, insects and other races are considered fair game for necromancers.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup>

## Religion
Historically, the Dunmer have resisted worshipping the [Aedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aedra) of the [Nine Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines), whom they viewed as heretical apostasies. Occasionally, adherents of the Divines were punished severely.<sup>[[207]](#cite_note-OE:MS-215)</sup>

Following ALMSIVI's fall, the Dunmer were swift in re-establishing the traditions of ancestor and Daedra worship that Ashlanders had kept alive as the New Temple.<sup>[[200]](#cite_note-The Rec-208)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TA-28)</sup><sup>[[208]](#cite_note-216)</sup> Dunmeri religious iconography has made use of the triangle and the pyramid throughout history, from the Chimer's worship of the Good Daedra,<sup>[[209]](#cite_note-217)</sup> to the Living Gods of the Dunmeri Tribunal.<sup>[[210]](#cite_note-218)</sup> Three is a lucky number associated with the Tribunal. Some objects are designed to do things in threes, such as muffin pans that bake only three muffins.<sup>[[211]](#cite_note-219)</sup>

### Ancestor Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/41/SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Temple_Ancestral_Tomb.jpg) An ancestral tomb Ancestor worship is a hallmark of Dunmer culture. They believe the spirits of the dead live on in the mortal realm. The spirits know and can affect the future, speak with other spirits, and perform great feats of magic. As such, the Dunmer honor their deceased ancestors, giving them gifts and petitioning them for aid and protection.<sup>[[212]](#cite_note-MWGen Ancestors-220)</sup>

Recently deceased spirits have the freshest knowledge of their living relatives. Though they are the weakest of the ancestor spirits, they are the quickest to guide and protect their kin when called upon. The spirits of heroes and wise women are strong. The Dunmer pass down these ancestors' stories so they don't forget their wisdom. The worst of the ancestor spirits are evil and seek to do harm. These spirits are called ghosts, devils, and daemons.<sup>[[212]](#cite_note-MWGen Ancestors-220)</sup> Ghosts in particular are mad spirits: those who are forced to remain on the mortal plane for one reason or another. Spirits who are bound to the world willingly are less likely to go mad, but for those who do not consent to being bound, it is inevitable.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup> The Dark Elves ask their kinder ancestors to protect them from malevolent spirits. Unlike most other races, the Dunmer count the Daedra among their ancestors. The Daedra are the oldest and greatest of the ancestor spirits. Though powerful, they are dangerous and difficult to understand.<sup>[[212]](#cite_note-MWGen Ancestors-220)</sup>

House Dunmer have family shrines in their homes to venerate their ancestors. In poorer homes, the shrine may consist of a hearth or alcove where family relics are displayed, but wealthy House Dunmer set aside an entire room for their ancestors to use. The shrine is referred to as the "Waiting Door", and represents the door to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). When a family member dies, their remains are interred in ash pits within ancestral tombs. Small bones (such as finger bones) may be placed in the family shrine.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup> Traditionally, smaller communities maintain a hard-and-fast rule against non-Dark Elves being interred within family crypts. Larger cities like Mournhold or Ebonheart are more open-minded, but even in those cosmopolitan communities it's unlikely to find more than a handful of foreign names on the plaques beside a tomb.<sup>[[213]](#cite_note-LA-221)</sup>

The Dark Elves' murky reputation among the human races is due in part to the practices and spirit magic associated with ancestor worship; they abhor the notion of summoning one's ancestors from beyond the veil. The Dunmer summon dead ancestors to consult their wisdom and ask them to protect their tombs, but they don't consider this necromancy. Necromancy outside of acceptable clan rituals is taboo. Other elves consider the Dunmeri practice of displaying the bones of deceased relatives in ash pits repugnant.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup>

#### The Good Daedra
The Three [Good Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Good_Daedra) is the Dunmer (and before that, [Velothi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Velothi)) term for three of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md): [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). These Princes are worshipped by the Dunmer as their ancestors. They are contrasted with the [Bad Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bad_Daedra), who are believed to test the Dunmer people's resolve and faith.<sup>[[214]](#cite_note-222)</sup><sup>[[215]](#cite_note-223)</sup> To their worshippers, they were known as the **Three Sisters** and the **Triangular Gate**.<sup>[[216]](#cite_note-ADAW-224)</sup><sup>[[217]](#cite_note-FEE-225)</sup>

Mephala gave the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) the [Codes of Mephala](Lore_Codes_of_Mephala.md), taught them the ways of evasion and assassination, also founded the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong). Boethiah and Mephala together organized the structure that would end up as the [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) of the Dunmer. Boethiah taught the Dunmer philosophy, magic, and ["Responsible" Architecture](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Responsible_Architecture) of their daedric shrines.<sup>[[218]](#cite_note-VF-226)</sup> According to one myth, Boethiah asked the Chimer to be the color of bruise, turning them into Dunmer,<sup>[[219]](#cite_note-LV32-227)</sup> however Boethiah sometimes gets attributed with the more conventional teachings of Azura,<sup>[[218]](#cite_note-VF-226)</sup> and in traditional retellings it is Azura who curses the Chimer with ashen skin.<sup>[[UOL 9]](#cite_note-MKP-228)</sup> Azura is credited with teaching the Chimer the mysteries needed to be different than the Altmer.<sup>[[218]](#cite_note-VF-226)</sup>

The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) replaced worship of the Good Daedra among the settled Dunmer following their apotheosis at the [Battle of Red Mountain](Lore_Battle_of_Red_Mountain.md), and the three Good Daedra were thereafter known to the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) as the **Anticipations** of the three Tribunes.<sup>[[220]](#cite_note-229)</sup> The [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlander) tribes refused to worship the Tribunal, rejecting them as 'false gods', remaining true to their worship of the Good Daedra. [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril) and [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) still honored the Good Daedra.<sup>[[221]](#cite_note-UHI-230)</sup> The [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) however despised them for their fear of wisdom and order, calling them the **False Princes** and "the Anti-Gears that turn counter to the Nameless Will".<sup>[[222]](#cite_note-TIS-231)</sup>

Following the deaths and disappearance of the Tribunes and the rise of the [New Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Temple), worship of the Good Daedra was restored and they became known as the **Reclamations**.<sup>[[223]](#cite_note-232)</sup> They are now also called the **True Tribunal** or **House of Reclamations**, having reclaimed their place in the hearts of the Dunmer people from the "false Tribunal" that used their status to shield the True Tribunal from the public's minds. The name "Anticipations" is no longer used for them, as they have reclaimed their identity and no longer represent the patronage of the false Tribunal.<sup>[[224]](#cite_note-DBElder Othreloth-233)</sup> Not all settled Dunmer turned away from Tribunal worship, although those few hidden sects who continued to worship the Tribunes as gods were persecuted by the New Temple for their heretical beliefs.<sup>[[225]](#cite_note-234)</sup>

#### The Bad Daedra
The **Bad Daedra** (also known as the **House of Troubles**, **Rebel Daedra**, **Adversaries**,<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TA-28)</sup> and **Testing Gods**)<sup>[[226]](#cite_note-VFDE-235)</sup> is the Dunmer (and before that, [Velothi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Velothi)) term for four of the [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md): [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), [Malacath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malacath) and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). These Princes are the Bad Daedra as introduced to the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) by the teachings of Saint Veloth. Rebels whose purpose it is to test the resolve and faith of mortals, they are also known as the **Four Corners of the House of Troubles**.<sup>[[227]](#cite_note-THR-236)</sup> Ancient, alien temples dedicated to their worship can be found across [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[228]](#cite_note-Morrowind-237)</sup>

The Four Corners are known as enemy gods to the Dunmer. Molag Bal is a primary source of obstacles for the Dunmer people and their Chimer predecessors. He attempts to upset the Houses' bloodlines or ruin Dunmeri "purity".<sup>[[226]](#cite_note-VFDE-235)</sup> In the days of old Resdayn, Molag Bal was not worshipped so much as he was placated.<sup>[[229]](#cite_note-ESOBal Ur-238)</sup> Malacath tests the Dunmer for strength, Sheogorath tests for mental weakness and tempts [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) to treachery, while Mehrunes Dagon represents the dangerous terrain of Morrowind.<sup>[[226]](#cite_note-VFDE-235)</sup>

### Tribunal Temple
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ae/MW-shrine-Tribunal.jpg/200px-MW-shrine-Tribunal.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-shrine-Tribunal.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-shrine-Tribunal.jpg) Shrines dedicated to ALMSIVI [![MW-banner-Temple.png](https://images.uesp.net/9/91/MW-banner-Temple.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-banner-Temple.png) The [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) (also called the **Tribunal Cult**, the **Morrowind Temple**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Po M-4)</sup> the **Temple**,<sup>[[230]](#cite_note-TR-239)</sup> the **Temple to the Tribunal**,<sup>[[231]](#cite_note-MDGH-240)</sup> or the **New Temple Faith**)<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lot S-16)</sup><sup>[[232]](#cite_note-Va M-241)</sup><sup>[[233]](#cite_note-WWDB-242)</sup><sup>[[234]](#cite_note-MWDarius-243)</sup> was the native religion of the Dunmer of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[230]](#cite_note-TR-239)</sup> They worshipped [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia), [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md), known together as the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) or Almsivi.<sup>[[230]](#cite_note-TR-239)</sup> Most people usually just call it "the Temple". They accept outlanders as members, but most of the few outlanders that do join, join only for the services provided. On [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), the Temple District included the city of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(city).md), the fortress of [Ghostgate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostgate), and all sacred and profane sites (including those [Blighted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight) areas inside the [Ghostfence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostfence)) and all unsettled and wilderness areas, as the [Treaty of the Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md) with the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md) maintained the island as a Temple preserve.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup> In practice, this district included all parts of Vvardenfell not claimed as Redoran, Hlaalu, or Telvanni Districts.

The Temple maintained less than favorable relations with most of the Imperial guilds of Morrowind,<sup>[[235]](#cite_note-244)</sup> especially so since the Morrowind Temple was on the Proscribed List of Hostile Cults—with even courtesy membership in the Temple being forbidden to all members of the [Tiberius Imperator Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talos_Cult) (that is, the Imperial Citizenship Cult).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Po M-4)</sup> However, it maintained strong relations with the pious [House Indoril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:House_Indoril), the proud warriors of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) as well as amicable relations with [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md) and [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md). When the Tribunal fell apart, the Tribunal Temple underwent a reformation, becoming the [New Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Temple).<sup>[[230]](#cite_note-TR-239)</sup>

The Great Houses generally followed the established Tribunal Temple, which preached faith in the Tribunal, respect for the "Good Daedra" who recognized the Tribunal, and veneration of ancestors.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup> There was some practicality to this: ancestral remains are used to power [ghost fences](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostfence) to ward off spirits, notably the one created by the Tribunal at the closing of the Second Era that surrounded Red Mountain for hundreds of years to keep Dagoth Ur and his minions at bay.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1M-1)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-AATD-11)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASHOM-15)</sup>

The Temple's priestly writings are called the "Hierographa". In the late Third Era, Archcanon Tholer Saryoni collected Vivec's most famous sermons and popular explanations of his Gospels. He used this information to write *[Saryoni's Sermons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Saryoni%27s_Sermons)*, which went on to become the best selling of the Temple's annotated texts; by [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), a copy of *Saryoni's Sermons* could be found in most every Dunmeri household. Many editions were printed, the most elaborate of which were handsomely illuminated with Vivec's quotations from the Gospels for days, seasons, and festivals.<sup>[[236]](#cite_note-245)</sup>

#### Temple Saints
The [Temple Saints](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_Saints) are the most honored ancestors of the Dunmer, canonized by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) for deeds they performed during their lifetime.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Lot S-16)</sup> They are celebrated as models of virtue and piety, often invoked for aid and protection. After the fall of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md), the three Tribunes were re-appointed as saints by the [New Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:New_Temple).<sup>[[230]](#cite_note-TR-239)</sup>

#### The Ordinators
The [Ordinators](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordinators) (sometimes called Temple Ordinators) were the holy guards and priest-soldiers of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) led by the [Alma Rula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alma_Rula). They are divided into four distinct orders. The Order of the Watch provided guards for temples and shrines, and additionally acted as town guards in Temple-owned settlements such as [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City). They were responsible for enforcing Temple law, along with [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) law following the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md). The Order of War (also known as the Militant Ordinators) fought the enemies of the Temple, with most War Ordinators traditionally stationed near [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) to combat the forces of the [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House). The Order of the Inquisition identifies and suppresses heresy among priests and laymen within the Temple. The Order of Doctrine and Ordination are militant scholars who opposed the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) Prophecies and other dissident teachings.<sup>[[242]](#cite_note-MWDialogue-252)</sup><sup>[[243]](#cite_note-253)</sup>

#### Buoyant Armigers
The [Buoyant Armigers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Buoyant_Armigers) were a small military order of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md), exclusively dedicated to and answering to Lord [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md). The Buoyant Armigers patterned themselves on Vivec's heroic spirit of exploration and adventure, and sought to emulate his mastery of the varied arts of personal combat, chivalric courtesy, and subtle verse. They served the Temple as champions and [knights](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights) -errant, and were friendly rivals of the more solemn [Ordinators](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordinators). The majority of members who served in the order hailed from [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md), as their ideals were closely related,<sup>[[244]](#cite_note-BASV-254)</sup><sup>[[245]](#cite_note-255)</sup><sup>[[246]](#cite_note-MWGDB-256)</sup> though some members hailed from other Houses,<sup>[[244]](#cite_note-BASV-254)</sup> and some weren't even Dunmer.<sup>[[247]](#cite_note-ME-257)</sup>

#### The Dissident Priests
The [Dissident Priests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dissident_Priests) are a secretive cult consisting of [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) members who disputed the Temple's dogmatic foundations and question the process of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md)'s elevation to godhood. They disputed Temple doctrine and were uneasy about the practices of the [Ordinators](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordinators). One of the chief disputes between the Temple and the Dissident Priests was that the Temple hid the truth from its followers. The Dissident Priests' outlined their beliefs in the book *[Progress of Truth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Progress_of_Truth)*.

## Notes
- The term "[Dark Elf](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C3%B6kk%C3%A1lfar_and_Lj%C3%B3s%C3%A1lfar)" originates from [Norse mythology](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norse_mythology). Additionally, the term "[dun](http://wiktionary.org/wiki/dun#Etymology_1)" refers to a slightly brownish-dark gray color.
- According to [Mark Bullock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Mark_Bullock), [Michael Kirkbride](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Michael_Kirkbride), [Ken Rolston](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Ken_Rolston) and [Todd Howard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Todd_Howard), the Dunmer and Morrowind were greatly inspired by the cultures and settings of [Egypt](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt), [Japan](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan), *[Conan the Barbarian](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conan_the_Barbarian_(disambiguation))*, *[Dune](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dune_(franchise))*, *[Gladiator](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gladiator_(2000_film))*, *[Labyrinth](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labyrinth_(1986_film))*, *[Legend](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legend_(1985_film))*, *[Mad Max](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mad_max)*, *[Rune Quest](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rune Quest)*, *[Star Wars](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Wars)*, *[The Dark Crystal](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Dark_Crystal)* and the works of [Hayao Miyazaki](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayao_Miyazaki).<sup>[[248]](#cite_note-258)</sup><sup>[[249]](#cite_note-259)</sup><sup>[[250]](#cite_note-260)</sup><sup>[[251]](#cite_note-261)</sup> Additionally, a large proportion of names of Ashlanders, Egg Mines, and Daedric shrines are modeled on ancient [Assyrian](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyria) and [Babylonian](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Babylonia) names from the third millenium to the first millenium BCE.<sup>[[252]](#cite_note-MWEE-262)</sup>
- | [^](#note_nb1) | A single instance of the Dark Elves being referred to as **Drow** appears in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)*, but this was dropped completely thereafter.<sup>[[253]](#cite_note-Drow-263)</sup> It is presumably an artifact from an earlier iteration of the game, when it featured more [material derived](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drow) from *[Dungeons & Dragons](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons)*. |
