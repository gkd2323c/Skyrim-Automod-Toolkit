# House Dorell

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/ON-place-Dorell_Manor.jpg/200px-ON-place-Dorell_Manor.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dorell_Manor.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Dorell_Manor.jpg) Dorell Manor in Northpoint House Dorell is a prominent [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) noble house and former royal dynasty from [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). The founder of House Dorell, [Yric Flowdys](Lore_Yric_Flowdys.md), established [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md), which is the house's seat of power, and their lands in and around the city are integral to their operations.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CVRd-2)</sup> For most of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), House Dorell exemplified the type of entrepreneurial merchant princes that brought great prosperity to High Rock.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup> By the late First Era, House Dorell had established itself as one of the most prominent noble houses in Rivenspire, alongside [Houses Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md), and all three houses had developed rivalries with one another.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup> During the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), they established themselves as a maritime and mercantile power, achieving a level of wealth that was seldom seen in Rivenspire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup> Following the conclusion of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md) in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), Houses Dorell, Montclair, and Tamrith formed the triumvirate Council of the North to jointly rule Rivenspire until the selection of a new king of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EOd-4)</sup>

## History

### First Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a0/ON-place-Northpoint.jpg/200px-ON-place-Northpoint.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Northpoint.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Northpoint.jpg) Northpoint during the mid-Second Era The history of House Dorell begins with Captain [Yric Flowdys](Lore_Yric_Flowdys.md), an enterprising [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) trader who operated the summer route of shipping from [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) before establishing [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md) around [1E 900](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_900).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONNFC-5)</sup> He constructed the first docks at Northpoint, the best anchorage, and named the port after it. Soon after building the docks, Flowdys oversaw the addition of a small walled keep and warehouse in the heights of Dore Elard, to the east of the growing port of call. Before long, the town bustled with activity, and Flowdys, realizing the success of his venture, took the name of Dore Elard as his new family name. He and his relatives continued to grow their maritime endeavors, as well as develop and invest in the port and surrounding lands, eventually leasing plots to farmers and establishing new sources of income.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup>

For most of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), House Dorell exemplified the type of entrepreneurial merchant princes that brought great prosperity to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). In [1E 1029](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1029), House Dorell was granted a barony when High Rock joined the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) under Empress [Hestra](Lore_Hestra.md). The fortunes of House Dorell and Northpoint have shifted with the flow of the northwest coastal trade ever since.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup>

By the late First Era, House Dorell had established itself as one of the most prominent noble houses in Rivenspire, alongside [Houses Montclair](Lore_House_Montclair.md) and [Tamrith](Lore_House_Tamrith.md), and all three houses had developed rivalries with one another.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup> During the twenty-fourth century of the First Era, House Dorell, having continued its rise in wealth and power, held the monarchy of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) for several generations. Due to their history as monarchs of Shornhelm, the Dorells regard themselves as part of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md)'s true elite. The house's time as monarchs of Shornhelm caused them to develop a penchant for political intrigue, which, combined with their ambition, made them impossible to ignore.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup> At some point during the First Era, House Dorell dissolved [House Spenard](Lore_House_Spenard.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONRKT-6)</sup>

### Second Era
By the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), House Dorell had developed a reputation as militaristic and politically savvy and had established itself as a maritime and mercantile power, achieving a level of wealth that was seldom seen in Rivenspire. The house developed extensive ties with merchants in Solitude. This, they were quick to point out, had nothing to do with the sword-rattling of politics; instead, it was simply good business.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup> The house was also involved in silver mining.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-CPC-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-npc-Baron_Alard_Dorell.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Baron_Alard_Dorell.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Baron_Alard_Dorell.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Baron_Alard_Dorell.jpg) Baron Alard Dorell After the death of [Hurlburt](Lore_Hurlburt.md), King of Shornhelm, in [2E 546](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_546), House Dorell, unlike [Houses Branquette](Lore_House_Branquette.md), Montclair, and Tamrith, chose not to endorse either of his sons, [Phylgeon](Lore_Phylgeon_Montclair.md) or [Ranser](Lore_Ranser.md), in their succession dispute over Shornhelm's throne. Ranser ultimately won the throne by the vote of the Council of the North and was crowned King of Shornhelm.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-8)</sup> In [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566), King Ranser declared war on [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) after King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) married Princess [Maraya](Lore_Maraya.md) of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md) instead of his daughter [Rayelle](Lore_Rayelle.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TFOKR-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOAM6-10)</sup> Ranser called the noble houses of Rivenspire to arms, including Houses Dorell, Montclair, and Tamrith, who complied and provided their troops.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-8)</sup> Shortly after the outbreak of [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), House Tamrith withdrew its support of him and petitioned for peace, and House Dorell quickly followed suit.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup> Following Ranser's death and the conclusion of the war in [2E 567](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_567), the leader of House Dorell, Baron [Alard Dorell](Lore_Alard_Dorell.md), and the leaders of Houses Montclair and Tamrith pledged their loyalty to King Emeric.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup>

In the immediate aftermath of Ranser's death, Rivenspire fell into chaos.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-8)</sup> To restore order to Rivenspire, Baron [Esmark Tamrith](Lore_Esmark_Tamrith.md) proposed the formation of a ruling triumvirate composed of the leaders of Houses Dorell, Montclair, and Tamrith to govern the region in the name of Emeric.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup> Because Emeric did not trust any of Rivenspire's nobles to be King of Shornhelm, he approved of the formation of the triumvirate Council of the North, but he also promised to eventually select a new king.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-HTARH-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HKEd-11)</sup> After the war, much of House Branquette's domain was parceled out to Houses Dorell and Tamrith.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-8)</sup> During this time, Baron Alard Dorell maintained a mansion in Shornhelm to stay closely involved in the affairs of the city's court.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup> While serving as a member of the triumvirate, he entrusted the management of the family’s holdings in and around Northpoint to his son, Lord Ellic Dorell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-NAA-1)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BADd-12)</sup> Although Emeric promised to select a new king, the triumvirate continued to govern Rivenspire during the fifteen years following Ranser's War.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-EOd-4)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SCCOTH-8)</sup>

### War with House Montclair
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bf/ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Liberation_of_Northpoint_07.jpg) Baron Dorell has Lord Ellic arrested In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Baron [Wylon Montclair](Lore_Wylon_Montclair.md), House Montclair's representative on the Council of the North, initiated a conflict in an attempt to take over Rivenspire following the death of his wife and his transformation into a [vampire](Lore_Vampire.md) due to the Lightless Remnant of the [Doomcrag](Lore_Doomcrag.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-HRP-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Lf LL-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DGd-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DWID-16)</sup> Baron Montclair's court magician, Reezal-Jul, seized control of the upper city of Shornhelm,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lt RJ-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Rf RJ-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TLd-19)</sup> and the bickering between Baron Dorell and Countess [Eselde Tamrith](Lore_Eselde_Tamrith.md) hindered their efforts to retake it from House Montclair's forces.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BADd-12)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Lt RJ-17)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-CETd-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Dt Dd-21)</sup> Around this time, Baron Montclair's daughter, Lady Lleraya Montclair, arrived in Northpoint and used illusion magic and her vampiric powers to place Lord Ellic Dorell under her spell and allow her troops to enter the city.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BADd-12)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-LEDd-22)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-BQd-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Gd-24)</sup> Lady Lleraya then had Lord Ellic call a gathering in the city's square, where she used magic from the Lightless Remnant to turn many of the people of Northpoint into [bloodfiends](Lore_Bloodfiend.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-BQd-23)</sup> Afterward, Lady Lleraya sealed the city gates and took control of Northpoint.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BADd-12)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-BQd-23)</sup>

An [agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of King Emeric arrived in Shornhelm, and they, along with Count [Verandis Ravenwatch](Lore_Verandis_Ravenwatch.md), assisted the forces of House Dorell, House Tamrith, and the Shornhelm Guard in retaking the upper city.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SD-25)</sup> The agent eventually tracked down and killed Reezal-Jul in Shadowfate Cavern,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TBSS-26)</sup> and House Dorell and its allies, having discovered that Northpoint had fallen to House Montclair, prepared their forces to retake the city.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-CVRd-2)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Ni P-27)</sup> The agent helped Baron Dorell and his allies infiltrate Northpoint, kill Lady Lleraya, and liberate the city.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TLo N-28)</sup> Following Northpoint's liberation, Baron Dorell had Ellic arrested and imprisoned for allowing the city to fall to House Montclair.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-BADd-12)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TLo N-28)</sup> The forces of Houses Dorell and Tamrith later helped the agent and Count Ravenwatch kill Baron Montclair at the Doomcrag and end the threat of the Lightless Remnant.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Pot P-29)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TLR-30)</sup> After the defeat of House Montclair, a gathering took place at Shornhelm Castle to celebrate and select Shornhelm's new monarch. However, it is unknown whether Emeric, who based his decision on the recommendation of the agent, selected Baron Dorell or Countess Tamrith as the new ruler of Shornhelm.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-TCo S-31)</sup>
