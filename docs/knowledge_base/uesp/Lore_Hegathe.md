—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* Hegathe (also called **Old Hegathe**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup> is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup> found in the forests of the tropical western shore landings of the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-3)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TVC-4)</sup> Hegathe was once the capital of Hammerfell, ever since the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md) landed on its shores in [1E 808](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_808) until it was moved to the north, in the [Kingdom of Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). It is known for its austere ramparts that harken back to antiquity, and is considered an equal to [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) in terms of splendor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GPTv3-5)</sup> The name originally referred to the wastelands that was later designated as Hammerfell, deriving from the [Aldmeri](Lore_Ehlnofex_Languages.md) word for *"Deathland"*.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> Despite the meaning of the name, the entire peninsula that the city itself is found upon is a lush subtropical rainforest biome, reaching as far north as [Abibon-Gora](Lore_Abibon-Gora.md) and as far east as [Khefrem](Lore_Khefrem.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-3)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Climate Map-7)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
Hegathe is found on the on the south-westernmost point of the mainland of the province, where the coasts are fertile grasslands, and the peninsula itself is a subtropical rainforest.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-3)</sup> There is an island off the coastline of the city.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-LOMap-8)</sup> Like other traditional cities in Hammerfell, it is built atop of old ruins of past civilizations, replaced with high domes and flying dew sails that are well-suited for the harsh environment.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> It also has an impenetrable defense of an antique splendor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup> It is a city of contrasts with tall, plain stone ramparts found side-by-side with beautiful gold-engraved tents, and a busy modern harbor found near the calm ancient shrines.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GPTv3-5)</sup> One of the city's most iconic landmarks is the Shrine of Tava, a place of veneration for both the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md) and the [Forebears](Lore_Forebears.md). It is highlighted by the *Fresco of the [Goshawk](Lore_Goshawk.md)*, a mural depicting [Tava](Lore_Tava.md) nestled together with his mates, which was meant to symbolize the union of the two factions.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWAHD-9)</sup> Thanks to its coastal forest environment, the city has vineyards where they make their signature wine.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONSherveen-10)</sup> Hegathe Lemon Kaveh is also a traditional drink named for the city.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-HLK-11)</sup> Hegathe is neighbored by several settlements, including [Kelps Yat](Lore_Kelps_Yat.md) to the north and [Gilane](Lore_Gilane.md) to the east.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/10/LO-misc-Old_Hegathe.jpg/120px-LO-misc-Old_Hegathe.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Old_Hegathe.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Old_Hegathe.jpg) Old Hegathe [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d8/RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png/200px-RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-map-West_Tamriel-800x600.png) Map of West Tamriel The name dates farther back than the city itself, or even the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokudan.md) incursion that swept across the land. It referred to the vast desert found between [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) and the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md), which in Aldmeris is translated to the Deathland. The Tamrielic variant of the word, often used by the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md), was the name used for the region of Hammerfell.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

When the second wave of Ra Gada crossed the [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md) to eventually reach Tamriel, they landed on the harborage that became Hegathe. It was occupied by the [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md), who the Yokudans quickly displaced. It soon became the staging ground for the Ra Gada movement across the coastline. One of these waves was led by [Yaghoub](Lore_Yaghoub.md), who traveled north along [Cape Shira](Lore_Cape_Shira.md) and was the first of the Redguards to discover the Iliac Bay and land on [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-STJOA-16)</sup> As the remaining refugees had left [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) to populate the mainland, the Yokudan government, the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md) became fully-integrated and made Hegathe its capital.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup>

In [1E 2200](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2200), the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) broke out and it first appeared on mainland Tamrielic soil through major port cities on the southwest coast. Hegathe was the disease's point of entry in Hammerfell and it caused a significant decrease in the population.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-1TTP-17)</sup> Many people at the time searched for a cure in vain and unsung heroes like Mazazim al-Hegathe died on their quest.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SH3-18)</sup> When [Mount Firesong](Lore_Mount_Firesong.md) erupted over in [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md) in [1E 2484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2484), Hegathe experienced vibrations from the seismic force of the volcano.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SH4-19)</sup>

Years later when the province of Hammerfell was annexed into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), two political parties were formed to contribute to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s administration over the land. The Crowns were created from what was left of the Na-Totambu, who retained their high-ranking status in the Empire and used Hegathe as their capital. From there, they were represented by the [Phyllocid Dynasty](Lore_Phyllocid_Dynasty.md), who maintained their relationship with the Empire throughout its lifetime. The Forebears was the other political party founded in Hammerfell, from the warrior-lords that descended from the Ra Gada and governed tribal districts in the south. For the longest time, these groups were sworn enemies that exceeded far longer than the Empire itself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup>

### Ar-Azal's reign and onward
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/58/RG-scene-Introduction_07.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_07.jpg) King Thassad II It took the ascension of High King [Ar-Azal](Lore_Ar-Azal.md) and his union with the Crowns daughter, [Arlimahera](Lore_Arlimahera.md) and the Forebears daughter, [Fereshtah](Lore_Fereshtah.md), to break the divide between them.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TWAHD-9)</sup> The [Osseous Crux](Lore_Osseous_Crux.md) was discovered on the shores of the Abecean Sea near Hegathe. It was constructed from the bones of some enormous, unknown beast, bound together with twisted fibrous cords. It was later relocated to Sentinel.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TVC-4)</sup>

By the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), the political duality had reverted back to the monarchy of the Na-Totambu and created political unrest, especially when the capital moved from Old Hegathe to the Forebears city of Sentinel. The line of High Kings that ruled from Sentinel continued with [Thassad II](Lore_Thassad_II.md) as the conquests of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) continued to expand and enroach on his domain. The High King passed away in [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862) and the capital was retaken by the Forebears under the command of Baron [Volag](Lore_Volag.md), ensuing a vicious civil war between the Crowns and Forebears. When the latter enlisted the help of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), victory shifted from the Crowns to the Empire, and eventually, the Crowns lost their territories until [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) was left. Both factions reconciled a year later to formally establish the Imperial Province of Hammerfell. Sentinel, however, remained as its provincial capital.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1E-1)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-RGIntro-20)</sup>

During their rule under the Empire, Hegathe became a major trading port with [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) to the south.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-GPTv3-5)</sup> During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late- [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Hegathe was an active settlement. It was ruled by Queen Ghapimti and it had a rivalry with Gilane and [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AR-13)</sup>

In the late-second century, the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) enveloped Cyrodiil and Hammerfell in total warfare. By [4E 172](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_172), the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) had moved across Hammerfell's southern coast, leaving Hegathe as one of the remaining cities until in early- [4E 173](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_173) when the Forebears from Sentinel arrived and broke the siege over the city. The victory over Hegathe brought the reconciliation of the two parties.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TGW-21)</sup>
