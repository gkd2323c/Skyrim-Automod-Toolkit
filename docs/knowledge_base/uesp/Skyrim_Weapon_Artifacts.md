# Weapon Artifacts


The definition used for artifacts on this article is that the item must meet *all three* of the following requirements:

- Be unique in appearance.
- Only appear once in the game.
- Have a unique and useful enchantment.

None of these artifacts can be disenchanted to learn the enchantment. Some of them may be improved by [smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md). The material and perk relevant to each item is given below. Improving enchanted items requires the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Arcane_Blacksmith.md) perk. However, many artifacts do not have an applicable perk that allows improvement past *flawless* quality without a Smithing skill of greater than 100.

Many of the artifacts are of [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) origin. These items are formed from the essence of a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), providing the items with their power. Nineteen *Daedric Artifacts* are available in the game; most are rewards for the [Daedric Quests](Skyrim_Daedric_Quests.md).

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg/200px-SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Wuuthrad.jpg) Wuuthrad [Wuuthrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wuuthrad) is an ancient battleaxe once wielded by the founder of the [Companions](Skyrim_Companions.md), [Ysgramor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ysgramor). Ysgramor carried the axe when he and the Five Hundred Companions drove the elves from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md).

For historical information about Wuuthrad, see the [lore article](Lore_Wuuthrad.md).

Its primary effect is causing 1.2x damage to elves, namely [High Elves](Skyrim_High_Elf.md), [Wood Elves](Skyrim_Wood_Elf.md), [Dark Elves](Skyrim_Dark_Elf.md), and [Falmer](Skyrim_Falmer.md). Accordingly, the visage of a screaming elf decorates the axe. At the start of the game, Wuuthrad is in several [fragments](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fragment_of_Wuuthrad), four of which are inside the Companions headquarters [Jorrvaskr](Skyrim_Jorrvaskr.md). During the course of the Companions quest line, the weapon is reforged and is used to gain access to Ysgramor's tomb.

After placing Wuuthrad into the hands of Ysgramor's statue at the entrance of his tomb, you can take it back straight away and continue along without affecting the rest of the quest. You can use it as you see fit, though it is no longer a quest item and so has a weight. Wuuthrad cannot be improved by smithing, though it can be enchanted. Despite this, the effect text doesn't change, which means you cannot see how much damage the enchantment does. You can still modify the amount.

For more information, see the [Wuuthrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Wuuthrad) article.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/14/SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg/200px-SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Auriel%27s_Bow.jpg) Auriel's Bow [Auriel's Bow](Skyrim_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) is a powerful bow allegedly used by the elven god [Auri-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auri-El). It is received from [Gelebor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Knight-Paladin_Gelebor) during the quest [Touching the Sky](Skyrim_Touching_the_Sky.md). Shooting the sun using Auriel's Bow with [Bloodcursed Elven Arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bloodcursed_Elven_Arrow) will block out the sun until the following morning, negating its negative effects on vampires for the duration.

Additionally, shooting the sun with [Sunhallowed Elven Arrows](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Sunhallowed_Elven_Arrow) will cause the sun to shoot out rays of light at nearby enemies in a similar fashion to [Storm Call](Skyrim_Storm_Call.md).

It can be improved with an ingot of [Refined Moonstone](Skyrim_Elven.md#Refined_Moonstone) and the [Arcane Blacksmith](Skyrim_Smithing.md#Arcane_Blacksmith) perk, with the [Elven Smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md#Elven_Smithing) perk doubling the improvement.

Since the enchantment on the bow is not effected by any magic school, there is no way to boost the power or lower the cost of the enchantment.

For more information, see the [Auriel's Bow](Skyrim_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) article.
