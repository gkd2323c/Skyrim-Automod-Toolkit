[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/MW-place-Molag_Amur_02.jpg/200px-MW-place-Molag_Amur_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Molag_Amur_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Molag_Amur_02.jpg) A typical scene in the Molag Amur region [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/25/ON-place-Molag_Amur.jpg/200px-ON-place-Molag_Amur.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Molag_Amur.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Molag_Amur.jpg) Molag Amur circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The Molag Amur region is essentially the southern part of the [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md). The two regions are ecologically similar, both being characterized by an arid landscape whose plant life tends to be [Trama Root](Lore_Trama_Root.md), [Fire Petal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fire_Petal), and occasional [Chokeweed](Lore_Chokeweed.md). Molag Amur is distinguished from the northern Ashlands by the presence of lava pools and rivers on the surface. The land is predominantly made up of dark volcanic rock covered with an overlay of ash and cinder, but of a different makeup and appearance from that of the Ashlands proper. Although it is hard to understand how they survive, fauna include [Kagouti](Lore_Kagouti.md), [Alit](Lore_Alit.md), [Cliff Racers](Lore_Cliff_Racer.md), [Rats](Lore_Rat.md), and [Kwama Foragers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama_Forager). There are also various [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) frequently seen in the area, including [Scamps](Lore_Scamp.md) and all types of [Atronachs](Lore_Elemental_Daedra.md).

Molag Amur is a large region, stretching from just east of [Balmora](Lore_Balmora.md) clear to the eastern coast, and from the [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) south to [Molag Mar](Lore_Molag_Mar.md). It is an irregular triangle, with a truncated point at the region around the [Dwemer ruin](Lore_Dwemer_Ruins.md) of Arkngthand, above the [Foyada Mamaea](Lore_Foyada_Mamaea.md) opposite [Moonmoth Legion Fort](Lore_Moonmoth_Legion_Fort.md). The northern boundary of the Molag Amur region starts just north of the bridge to Arkngthand, stretches eastward from there along the hills north of [Lake Nabia](Lore_Lake_Nabia.md), and turns north after the [Foyada Ashur-Dan](Lore_Foyada_Ashur-Dan.md) towards the southwest corner of the Grazelands, near the stronghold of Falensarano, and then runs east to the sea. Roads mainly consist of [foyadas](Lore_Foyada.md), volcanic ravines, leading east and north to the important pilgrim sites of [Mount Assarnibibi](Lore_Mount_Assarnibibi.md) and [Mount Kand](Lore_Mount_Kand.md).

The lower boundary begins below Arkngthand, over the mountains from [Lake Amaya](Lore_Lake_Amaya_(Vvardenfell).md), and curves down above the [Ascadian Isles](Lore_Ascadian_Isles.md), taking a sharp southerly bend which sees it on three sides of [Suran](Lore_Suran.md). It then goes due east to Molag Mar. On the east side of Molag Mar, there is an inlet, and Molag Amur lies to the north, and its border follows a rough line north from the eastern shore of that inlet.

The region is not dominated by any House: the majority of its inhabitants tend to be renegade necromancers and Mages, but [House Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) has made inroads into the area with the foundation of Tel Uvirith. Relatively near there, the [Erabenimsun Camp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Erabenimsun_Camp) is a testimony to the presence of [Ashlander](Lore_Ashlanders.md) tribes in the eastern part of this region.

Molag Amur is generally an area one passes through out of necessity, not a place where any but the desperate would make a home. The sandstorms that rage throughout Molag Amur are legendary throughout the province. And it is said that [Silt Striders](Lore_Silt_Strider.md) were able to navigate them with ease.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> In [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) mythology, Molag Amur was the home of a race of monsters created from the copulation of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-THOT-2)</sup>
