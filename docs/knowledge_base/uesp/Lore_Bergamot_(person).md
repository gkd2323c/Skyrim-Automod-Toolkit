Bergamot was the King of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and the fifth monarch from [House Deleyn](Lore_House_Deleyn.md). He was the nephew of King Anton II of Daggerfall. When Anton II died in [2E 515](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_515), Bergamot, his only surviving heir, succeeded him as King of Daggerfall.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-1)</sup>

In [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md) and his horde of [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) invaded [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) and sacked several of the province's major cities before reaching [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), which they attempted to invest for a siege.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GTTDC-3)</sup> While they were investing Daggerfall, [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md) of Cumberland, in command of the [Menevia](Lore_Menevia.md) Heavy Dragoons, executed an attack on the rear of the Reachmen's ranks and killed Durcorach in the ensuing battle.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup> After Durcorach's death, Bergamot led the [Knights of the Dragon](Lore_Knights_of_the_Dragon.md) in a sortie against the Reachmen, helping Emeric and the Menevia Heavy Dragoons completely rout them.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup> A fortnight after the defeat of Durcorach and his horde, Bergamot and the kings of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md), [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md), [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md), and [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), realizing how weak their realms were individually, signed the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), a pact promising to defend each other's kingdoms and unite as one against all foreign foes.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TATOAM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GTTDC-3)</sup> Around [2E 560](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_560), Bergamot died of the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md) and was succeeded by [Folbert the Wide](Lore_Folbert.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-IEGTHR-1)</sup>
