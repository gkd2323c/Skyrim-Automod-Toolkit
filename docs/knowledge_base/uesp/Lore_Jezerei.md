[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/38/ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr.jpg/200px-ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-High_Priest_Zuladr.jpg) High Priest Zuladr, opponent of Jezerei's rule **Queen** Jezerei, also known as the **Strumpet Queen**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Combs-1)</sup> was the ruler of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) during an unknown period of history, at some point before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). While details about her reign are sparse, some accounts depict her as a ruthless leader notorious for her brutal deeds and bloody crimes.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HHH-2)</sup> She commissioned several extravagant Diamond-Tooth Cat Combs, which were jeweled combs crafted for her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Combs-1)</sup>

Long after her reign,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> in 2E 582 her combs were among the curios that could be found around [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Combs-1)</sup>

During that period, High Priest Zuladr of the High Temple of [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) invoked Queen Jezerei’s name in his biased sermons, aimed at disparaging women. He cited her alleged bloody crimes, referring to them alongside the *Tale of the Temptress Shakhari* and various feminine creatures like [Hags](Lore_Hag.md), [Harpies](Lore_Harpy.md), and [Hagravens](Lore_Hagraven.md). This account was recorded by the Unveiled [Azadiyeh](Lore_Azadiyeh.md), Songbird of Satakalaam, a [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md) historian who opposed Zuladr's views.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HHH-2)</sup>

## Notes
- | [1.](#note_nb1) | As of 2E 582, names of several High Kings and Queens preceeding King [Fahara'jad](Lore_Fahara%27jad.md), who ruled the Hammerell during the Interregnum, were known. Jezerei was not among them, implying that her reign preceded the times of several of the important figures of the [Phyllocid Dynasty](Lore_Phyllocid_Dynasty.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TWAA-3)</sup> It is also unknown whether or not she belonged to this dynasty. |
