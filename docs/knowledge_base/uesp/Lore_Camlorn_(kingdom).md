Camlorn (historically called **Camlarn**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGMap-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-HRP-2)</sup> is one of the nine historical [Breton kingdoms](Lore_Kingdoms_of_High_Rock.md) in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EWR-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARMap-4)</sup> situated on the coastal highlands of [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md). Camlorn and the surrounding region is a melting pot of several different [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) cultures<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GP-5)</sup> and its people fancy themselves as more refined and metropolitan compared to their neighbors in [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GTAC-6)</sup> The kingdom's nobility consider themselves an aristocracy of poets and artisans. It has also greatly benefitted from trade coming from either the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md) or the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GP-5)</sup> As of [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), Camlorn was one of five major kingdoms in High Rock.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-place-Camlorn_Keep.jpg/200px-ON-place-Camlorn_Keep.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Camlorn_Keep.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Camlorn_Keep.jpg) Camlorn Keep [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4d/ON-place-Camlorn_Inn.jpg/200px-ON-place-Camlorn_Inn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Camlorn_Inn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Camlorn_Inn.jpg) Local inn in the western district Camlorn is a large, walled-off city located in the middle of the [Cambray Hills](Lore_Cambray_Hills.md) and west of the Skeffington Wood.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-RGMap-1)</sup> It is specifically built on the epicenter of the region and most roads in the area all lead to the city's outskirts and the Cambray Pass. The city itself is placed on a tall hill that is scaleable only from the south. The city's outskirts are largely rocky and have few open plots of land, as it all descends north into the coast. There is at least one farm outside of the city, next to the sewer entrance.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMap-8)</sup> Like other kingdoms, Camlorn has a series of vassal principalities and duchies under its command, as well as smaller settlements between them.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MOT1-9)</sup> Among these include the Duchy of Oloine and the village to the east, Umbington.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MOT3-10)</sup>

Camlorn's city gate has a courtyard that connects to three different districts of Camlorn, the western area that continues north to the Temple of the Divines, the central area that leads to Camlorn Keep, and a third smaller area with several homes. Throughout the city are a series of watchtowers.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMap-8)</sup> The city's main street is wide and anchored by the Temple of Sethiete and the Camlorn Royal Palace,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MOT1-9)</sup> but before it reaches the palace, it meets the western district at a town square, surrounded by a watering well.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMap-8)</sup> The city has a northern quarter that is very affluent and contains the local Prostitutes Guildhouse, a grand palace.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-MOT4-11)</sup> The north end of the city also has the Camlorn Royal Palace, the seat of power in both the capital city and the kingdom. There is a path behind the castle that goes outside the walls and onto a cliffside that overlooks the Eltheric Ocean<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONMap-8)</sup> and a wide, rocky promontory called [Cavilstyr Rock](Lore_Cavilstyr_Rock.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MOT1-9)</sup> Camlorn is neighbored by several settlements, including [Ebon Wastes](Lore_Ebon_Wastes.md) to the south and [Moonguard](Lore_Moonguard.md) to the east.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ARMap-4)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/ON-npc-The_Iron_Son.jpg/200px-ON-npc-The_Iron_Son.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Iron_Son.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Iron_Son.jpg) The Iron Son of Camlorn Camlorn was founded by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) during the [Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md)'s occupation of High Rock in the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). It started as a Nordic city but as their influence started to wane and the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) population increased, Camlorn was fully assimilated by the locals.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup> One of the city's first rulers was a figure known as the Iron Son, who wielded a powerful sword made from [Black-Iron](Lore_Black-Iron.md) and created a powerful dynasty.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONIron-13)</sup> Interestingly, maps of the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md) circa [1E 484](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_484) had a myriad of discrepancies, among which include Camlorn's omission when compared to other maps. A member of the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md), Verita Numida believed it was a deliberate choice from the Direnni to mask their shrinking territories.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONAnti Map-14)</sup>

Sometime around [2E 70](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_70), the Duke of Camlorn's daughter was kidnapped by a group of bandits who demanded a large ransom of money. One knight stepped forward and rescued her, the knight, Justin Dufort. As a reward, Sir Justin earned the Duke's favor and a small amount of gold, but in the long term, his family name had increased influence and turned into a prosperous merchant family. Since then, [House Dufort](Lore_House_Dufort.md) became one of two families to dominate the Duchy of [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HOHD-15)</sup> Camlorn acquired its status as a kingdom sometime in [2E 301](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_301) and every year held a grand celebration in the capital city.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MOT1-9)</sup>

In [2E 478](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_478), the [Gardners](Lore_Gardner.md) of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) had troubles with an "Ascendant Camlorn" and was entirely occupied with the situation. So much so that they ignored the coup that rose in the Systres, when [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) seized one-third of Dufort's land and exiled their leadership to [Amenos](Lore_Amenos.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-SH7-16)</sup> It later took part in the Battle of Granden Tor<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ONContraband-17)</sup> and sometime later, in the province's dozen years of peace, Camlorn became an independent city-state by [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TOAM3-18)</sup>

### Camlorn in the Daggerfall Covenant
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3e/ON-creature-Faolchu_the_Reborn.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Faolchu_the_Reborn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Faolchu_the_Reborn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Faolchu_the_Reborn.jpg) Faolchu the Changeling The city was ransacked during Emperor [Durcorach](Lore_Durcorach.md)'s invasion of High Rock in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541), after his failed siege against [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md). When the Reach was defeated a year later at [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md)'s gates, Camlorn and its monarch gathered with other Breton kingdoms to form the First [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md), as recounted by [Emeric of Cumberland](Lore_Emeric.md) as he was known at the time.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TOAM3-18)</sup> The Kings of the Covenant were invited to Emeric's wedding in the spring of [2E 566](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_566)<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TOAM6-19)</sup> and were later drawn to [Ranser's War](Lore_Ranser%27s_War.md), a civil conflict against the Kingdom of [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md) and their allies. The war later gave rise to the Greater Daggerfall Covenant, which now incorporated the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TFOKR-20)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/31/ON-npc-General_Gautier.jpg/200px-ON-npc-General_Gautier.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-General_Gautier.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-General_Gautier.jpg) General Gautier of Camlorn During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md) invaded Glenumbra and after a failed attempt at sabotage in the [Beldama Wyrd Tree](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd_Tree.md), they made their way north toward Camlorn and worked with the resurrected [Faolchu](Lore_Faolchu.md). The undead [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md) made contact with a local named Jonathan Telwin and inflicted him with [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md). With this condition, Jonathan traveled to Camlorn and spread it among the populous in the middle of the night. People mistook the howls for wild dogs but it became a lot clear that the city was infested with a pack of bloodthirsty werewolves.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-APFV-21)</sup>

The Captain of the City Guard, [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md) led a defense against the attack and managed a holdout in the city's inn with survivors. Camlorn was taken over by Faolchu and his pack<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-APFV-21)</sup> while the royal family was forced to flee with refugees to nearby Aldcroft.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONWeller-22)</sup> In time, the [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md) prepared to retaliate<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONGauter-23)</sup> and built their siege camp south of the city's gate but when Faolchu's forces took it over, it split the army in half by each side of the camp. General Gautier enlisted the help of an [Agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Covenant and managed to reunite with General Mendin's forces while retaking the camp. With one more push into the gate's courtyard, the Lion Guard had entered the city.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONRally-24)</sup>

General Gautier sent the Agent to locate his son, Darien and help him in the relief effort. They found him in the inn in Camlorn's western district and with the help of a local [alchemist](Lore_Alchemy.md), Alinon, they managed to cure citizens of lycanthropy and round up weapons for the city's defense.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ONLingering-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONHidden-26)</sup> The invading force had greatly dwindled and all that was left was Faolchu, who was held up in the ruined Camlorn Keep. In a final battle, the werewolf general was defeated and Camlorn was liberated.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ONFaolchu-27)</sup> As their plans fell apart, the Bloodthorn Cult took hold in [Cath Bedraud](Lore_Cath_Bedraud.md) while the Lion Guard prepared for a final attack in [King's Guard](Lore_King%27s_Guard.md).

### Camlorn in the Third Empire
The historical-fiction series, [the Wolf Queen](Lore_The_Wolf_Queen.md) recounts how Emperor [Pelagius Septim II](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_II.md) and his wife, [Quintilla](Lore_Quintilla.md) met. In the autumntide of [3E 63](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_63), the widowed Prince Pelagius II and his son, [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) traveled to Camlorn to pay court to King Vulstaed's daughter, Princess Quintilla, well-renowned at the time as a beauteous princess, accomplished as well as a maiden and sorceress. Pelagius II arrived at the city-state to find it terrorized by a great demon werewolf and together with the princess, defeated the monster and bound it into a yellow [soul gem](Lore_Soul_Gem.md). The prince then fashioned the gem into a ring and proposed to the princess. Legend has it though that the soul of the wolf stayed with them until the birth of their first child, [Potema](Lore_Potema.md). They later went on to have two more children, the brothers [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TWQ1-28)</sup> The book also attributes the soul gem as the source [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md)'s infamous madness, making it one of the several possible theories.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TWQ8-29)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Camlorn was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Edward and had a rivalry with Daggerfall.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-AR-30)</sup> After acquiring the fifth piece of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md), the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) made their way north into High Rock to seek out the [Crypt of Hearts](Lore_Crypt_of_Hearts.md) and eventually stumbled into Camlorn where they met Senior-Brother Halfas Varn of the [Brotherhood of Seth](Lore_Brotherhood_of_Seth.md). The cult had acquired an ancient map with a code that could decipher the dungeon's location on an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scroll.md) but it was stolen by Brother Barnabas of Tethis, who in turn stowed away to the [Mines of Khuras](Lore_Mines_of_Khuras.md). The Eternal Champion delved deep into the dungeon and returned to Halfas Varn with the map intact. The mage-priest was able to locate the Crypt of Hearts and sent the Eternal Champion on their way to the dungeon.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ARCrypt-31)</sup> After the champion's monumental victory over Jagar Tharn, Imperial-loyalists would retrace the champion's steps throughout their journey across the Empire, stopping in Camlorn.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-AMM-32)</sup>

In [3E 385](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_385), the King of Camlorn (patriarch of the [Raze Family](Lore_Raze.md)) was secretly overthrown by his brother, the Duke of Oloine –who made himself the new King of Camlorn. A child swap took place, with the Duke abandoning his own daughter Jyllia Raze and taking on his crown princess niece Talara Raze as his own–however, the daughters were then referred to by different names, Talara believing be raised as if she was her cousin Jyllia.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Mo T-33)</sup> The Raze dynasts of Camloran were heavily featured in a series of historical fiction volumes called *[Mystery of Talara](Lore_Mystery_of_Talara.md)*, written by [Mera Llykith](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Mera_Llykith) and set around the year [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), in which Lady Jyllia Raze (who had grown up in [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md), believing herself have been a girl named Gyna) unravels a conspiracy around the Raze dynasty with the help of [Lord Naigon Strale](Lore_Naigon_Strale.md) and [Lady Brisienna Magnessen](Lore_Brisienna_Magnessen.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), ultimately culminating in Princess Talara Raze assuming her true identity and being restored to her rightful throne.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Mo T-33)</sup> And the new King of Camlorn (previously the Duke of Oloine), his wife, and his son Sylon Raze were removed from power.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Mo T-33)</sup> Llykith's volumes also discusses [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md)'s time in the Temple of Sethiete—how he learned about powerful and forgotten spells, as well how locate the Staff of Chaos.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-MOT-34)</sup>

Following the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), in the twilight years of the Third Era, the kingdoms of High Rock favored diplomatic solutions to their problems rather than violence. Despite that, local skirmishes continued on the regular between neighboring nations like between Camlorn, [Evermore](Lore_Evermore.md) and [Northpoint](Lore_Northpoint.md). By [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), the Kingdom of Camlorn recently celebrated the marriage between Duke Senhyn's daughter, Lady Kelmena, and the Prince of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), Camaron. Their union suggested a possible unified kingdom along the province's western coast, in conjunction with Daggerfall's already established relationship with its former rivals, the [Kingdom of Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md). By the end of the Third Era, Camlorn was one of five kingdoms in High Rock.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3-7)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/71/ON-npc-Duchess_Elea_Dufort.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Duchess_Elea_Dufort.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Duchess_Elea_Dufort.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Duchess_Elea_Dufort.jpg) Duchess Elea of Systres, descendant of Justin Dufort [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/19/ON-place-Eagle%27s_Brook.jpg/200px-ON-place-Eagle%27s_Brook.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Eagle%27s_Brook.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Eagle%27s_Brook.jpg) Eagle's Brook
- *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Breton.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Breton.md#Society)** sections on the Breton page.*

Camlorn's capital by the late Third Era acquired the epithet, the **Jewel of the West**<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-AR-30)</sup> and although the city stands above sea level, it is well connected to the surrounding waters by two villages; [Aldcroft](Lore_Aldcroft.md) for the Iliac Bay and [Eagle's Brook](Lore_Eagle%27s_Brook.md) for the Eltheric Ocean.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GTAC-6)</sup> Camlorn is described as a melting pot of various Breton cultures and its nobles fancy themselves as artisans and poets.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GP-5)</sup> By the mid-Second Era, Camlorn had rivaled Daggerfall's size and cultural influence and as the writer, Ansur Belote describes, "the citizens of Camlorn see themselves as more urbane and metropolitan then their neighbors to the south".<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GTAC-6)</sup>

Camlorn is the home of some prestigious Breton families. One of the most powerful was House Dufort, one of two major proprietors of the Duchy of Systres and later their ruling house in amidst the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) of the mid-Second Era. It was their ancestor, Justin Dufort, who elevated the family house into a prominent clan of shipwrights and merchants.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-HOHD-15)</sup> Another is the [Gautier](Lore_Gautier.md) family, who previously owned a family mausoleum up north called the [Ebon Crypt](Lore_Ebon_Crypt.md).<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-ONLoad Ebon-36)</sup> One of their most famous members, [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md) helped liberate the city from werewolves and later partook in the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md)'s invasion of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md).<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ONDarien-37)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/CT-scene-Odar_and_Ysabel.png/200px-CT-scene-Odar_and_Ysabel.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-scene-Odar_and_Ysabel.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-scene-Odar_and_Ysabel.png) Queen [Ysabel of Camlorn](Lore_Ysabel.md) with her husband, King [Odar the Brave](Lore_Odar_the_Brave.md) Camlorn holds an annual celebration that honors the time their nation was first recognized as a kingdom. Every street from boulevards to alleyways is adorned with gold and purple banners, some marked by the kingdom's heraldic symbols as well as the sigils of Camlorn's vassal principalities and duchies. The streets and plazas are filled to the brim with musicians and entertainers such as [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) snake charmers, [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) acrobats, and mages performing spectacular [illusions](Lore_Illusion.md). It commonly starts with the March of Beauty, where thousands of young, comely dressed women dance through the city's main street from the Temple of Sethiete to the Camlorn Royal Palace. Dancers mainly came from the local Prostitutes Guild. The celebration ends with the Festival of Flowers, where a large circle surrounded by lit candles is formed on Cavilstyr Rock and dancers flock around with vines and flowers.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MOT1-9)</sup>

The Bretons of Camlorn celebrate [Flower Day](Lore_Flower_Day.md) (observed on the 25th of [First Seed](Lore_First_Seed.md)) with a large array of beautiful colors and lively entertainment. No other city boasts such a celebration than Camlorn.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-TIHR-38)</sup> The holiday across High Rock is meant to celebrate the arrival of spring and as the children pick newly blossomed spring flowers, the old come out of their homes and dance in celebration.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-39)</sup> The folks of Camlorn also make seasonal tophats for the springtide, usually adorned with a buckle, a lucky bouquet, or shamrocks.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-ONHat Lucky-40)</sup><sup>[[40]](#cite_note-ONHat Buck-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-ONHat Sham-42)</sup>

## Notes
- *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* was originally conceived as a fighting game featuring a tournament that took the player to each of Tamriel's cities to challenge different gladiatorial teams. According to a file from that stage of development left behind in the final game, Camlorn's gladiatorial team would have been called "the Dragons".<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-43)</sup>
