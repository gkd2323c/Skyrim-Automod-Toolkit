# Guild Act

The Guild Act of [2E 321](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_321), also known as the **Guilds Act**, was a legislative act that codified the practice of guilds throughout the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hot FG-1)</sup> Encouraged by [Pelladil Direnni](Lore_Pelladil_Direnni.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DRD-2)</sup> and signed by Potentate [Versidue-Shaie](Lore_Versidue-Shaie.md) in 2E 321<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hot FG-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FGC-3)</sup> the Guild Act officially sanctioned the guilds of Tinkers, Cobblers, Prostitutes, Scribes, Architects, Brewers, Vintners, Weavers, Ratcatchers, Furriers, Cooks, Astrologers, Healers, Tailors, Minstrels, Barristers,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hot FG-1)</sup> and most importantly the [Guild of Mages](Lore_Mages_Guild.md), founded in [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230) by [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-MGC-4)</sup> and the [Guild of Fighters](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), founded the previous year by the Potentate's [kinsman](Lore_Tsaesci_(race).md) Dinieras-Ves<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hot FG-1)</sup> and chartered under the section 4 of the Guild Act.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-FGC-3)</sup>

The guilds were required to pay levies to the Empire and to pay for any expansions in exchange for protection and encouragement from the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md), which saw the value these guilds had to the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Later sanctions, some of them during the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), introduced new guilds into the fold. Overall these guilds were not only strengthened by the Guild Act, but strengthened the Empire in return, filling its much-emptied treasury.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Hot FG-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AP-5)</sup> The use of exotic [metals](Lore_Metals.md) has increased significantly since the passage of the Guild Act. The establishment of pan-Tamrielic guilds resulted in greater diffusion of knowledge, which benefited both [smiths](Lore_Smithing.md) and guild mages greatly.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-MAMA-6)</sup>

The Mages Guild briefly had its charter revoked following the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) of [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), during which time the [Fellowship of Anchorites](Lore_Fellowship_of_Anchorites.md) was institutionalized in its place.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Other Imperial guilds, such as the [Scenarist Guild](Lore_Scenarist_Guild.md) and the infamous [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md), have no implied connections with the Guild Act.
