## Fortify Skill
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-icon-effect-Fortify_Skill.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-icon-Fortify.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-icon-Fortify.png) Symbol of Fortify Fortify spells are known to temporarily increase target's abilities, skills, and capabilities

### Fortify Acrobatics
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png/200px-LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Falling_Wizard.png) Icarian Flight The Fortify Acrobatics spell enhances the target's agility, boosting their jumping and climbing abilities while also increasing their resistance to damage from falls. Known variants of this spell include:

- Icarian Flight
- Imperial Acrobatics Skill
- Shrine Acrobatics Skill

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Tarhiel Farano, a Dunmer mage, attempted to [leap](Lore_Air_Transportation.md#Jumping) to extreme heights using a spell, resulting in his death. After falling to his demise in the Telvanni Peninsula, he returned as an angry spirit, claiming that his descendants would perfect his spell.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup> The spell's name also originates from the same year. Bosmeri mage Icarian created a magical device to greatly enhance the user's jumping abilities to extraordinary levels.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FNTTA-2)</sup> However, its usage came at a great cost to Icarian, ultimately leading to his demise.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Icarian1-3)</sup> Nevertheless, his name became widely known and lent its name to the scrolls known as Scrolls of Icarian Flight.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FNTTA-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Icarian-4)</sup> In [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), a Bosmer mage, also named Tarhiel, endeavored to perfect this spell and utilized scrolls of Icarian Flight for his long-distance jump,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TJ-5)</sup> meeting a similar fate to his predecessors.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-FW-6)</sup>
