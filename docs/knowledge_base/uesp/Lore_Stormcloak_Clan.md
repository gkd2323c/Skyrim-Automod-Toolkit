# Stormcloak Clan


The Stormcloaks could trace their lineage all the way back to the founding of Windhelm in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), and boasted of having helped to build the city.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mera-2)</sup> During the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the clan owned a vineyard that produced some vintages that were regarded as extremely rare collector's items by the time of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Skegglund Stormcloak was a veteran of Windhelm's city guard who later joined the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) sometime before [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Mera Stormcloak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mera_Stormcloak) served as a thane of Windhelm, and was a trusted subject of [Jorunn the Skald-King](Lore_Jorunn.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESO-5)</sup> Mera's father had held the slightly lesser position of Windhelm's Captain of the Guard.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Mera-2)</sup>

By far the most notable member of the clan was [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), who inherited the title of Jarl of Windhelm from his father [Hoag Stormcloak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoag_Stormcloak) in [4E 183](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_183).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BSB-1)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TESVPrima-6)</sup><sup>:552-553</sup> He began the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) in order to usurp [Torygg](Lore_Torygg.md) as High King of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and restore [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

The Stormcloak Clan possesses a clan heirloom, a battleaxe named Bjornblad.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Another clan heirloom, Ulfric's Sword, is a sword responsible for taking many lives.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The Stormcloaks are also known for producing [blacksmithing](Lore_Smithing.md) aprons; made in Windhelm, these aprons protect the wearer from both flames and freezing winds.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
