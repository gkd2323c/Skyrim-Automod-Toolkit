[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg/200px-SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Misty_Grove.jpg) Misty Grove, one of Sanguine's realms The Myriad Realms of Revelry,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FNH-1)</sup> also called the **Realms of Revelry** or **Revelry** for short,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ot No O-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Plokun-3)</sup> are a congeries of one hundred thousand pocket and sub-realms of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SG-4)</sup><sup>:278</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-5)</sup> created and ruled over by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Indulgence, [Sanguine](Lore_Sanguine.md). The realms are used mainly as pleasure pockets, refashioned to meet the needs and desires of its visitants. As such, Sanguine exerts minimal control over them, which aligns perfectly with his preferences.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-FNH-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SG-4)</sup><sup>:278</sup>

The waters found within the Realms of Revelry were documented in *Sanguine's Rites and Realms* by the Prisoner of the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md). This text documented a phenomenon in which visions of ill-fated, prince-less realms of Oblivion colliding with each other could be seen within the waters' reflections.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Ot No O-2)</sup>

The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) visited one of the realms in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Named **Misty Grove**, it appeared as a mist-filled copse of trees crossed by burbling streams and paths with small stone bridges leading to an outdoor tavern, lit by lanterns in an eternal early evening. The aura invokes a strange, immediate sensation of brooding fear tempered by an odd calmness.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-6)</sup><sup>:316</sup> Sanguine used it to host a feast for the Dragonborn, after the two had spent a night drunkenly exploring [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRNight-7)</sup>

Revelry was one of many stops visited by the traveling creature exhibit known as Plokun's Inquisitive Menagerie. Its inhabitants had unique tastes compared to the show's other destinations, taking great pleasure in the portion of the show where the various creatures were killed.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Plokun-3)</sup>
