[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The Niben Bay (also called the **Nibenay Bay**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BPTZ-1)</sup> and historically **Lake Makapi** to the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md))<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AT-2)</sup> is a large body of water lying dead in the middle of the [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md), in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The bay is the center of the [Nibenay Valley](Lore_Nibenay_Valley.md), an important region in the trade of eastern Cyrodiil.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup> Many rivers flows into it such as the [Larsius](Lore_Larsius_River.md), the [Upper Niben](Lore_Niben_River.md), the [Corbolo](Lore_Corbolo_River.md), the [Silverfish](Lore_Silverfish_River.md) and the [Panther](Lore_Panther_River.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBNiben-4)</sup> The Niben Bay drains into the [Lower Niben](Lore_Niben_River.md), that connects it to the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The water of both the Niben Bay and Lower Niben is saltwater.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> Underneath the Niben Bay is a large subsection of caverns that extends from the center of the bay to an old [Temple of the Emperor Zero](Lore_Temple_of_the_Emperor_Zero.md) called the **Bravil Wizard's Grotto**.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBGrotto-6)</sup>

The Niben Bay hosts a wide variety of fauna, some of the rarest species of fish in the bay include the [Emperor Angelfish](Lore_Emperor_Angelfish.md), the [Jewel Fish](Lore_Jewel_Fish.md) and the [Topal Fanche](Lore_Topal_Fanche.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONCyrodiil-7)</sup> There is also a larger species of [slaughterfish](Lore_Slaughterfish.md) located in the underwater caves.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OBGrotto-6)</sup> There is also a variety of flora in the bay, which includes the [Foxglove](Lore_Foxglove.md), [Lavender](Lore_Lavender.md), [Monkshood](Lore_Monkshood.md), [Nirnroot](Lore_Nirnroot.md) and the [Summer Bolete](Lore_Summer_Bolete.md). There is also a [wayshrine](Lore_Wayshrines.md) dedicated to [Zenithar](Lore_Zenithar.md) on the northwest shore outside of Bravil.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBNiben-4)</sup>

## History

### Early History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/05/OB-place-Bawn.jpg/200px-OB-place-Bawn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bawn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bawn.jpg) Bawn [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/83/OB-place-Nenalata.jpg/200px-OB-place-Nenalata.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Nenalata.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Nenalata.jpg) Nenalata The ancestral Khajiit have lived in the southern Nibenay region since long before the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) took over the land. To them, the Niben Bay is known as Lake Makapi, similar to the Niben River's name, the River Malapi.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AT-2)</sup> For the longest time, warlike clans of the north viewed the region as a traditional hunting ground,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ALHOFR-8)</sup> and when [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) sailed up the Niben River, he witnessed the "cat demons of four and two legs".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FOTN-9)</sup> Sometime later, in the mid- [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) inhabited Cyrodiil and built cities all over the region,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-BTAOM-10)</sup> even on the Niben Bay. There are three known Ayleid settlements on the Niben Bay, [Bawn](Lore_Bawn.md) on the south shore, [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md) on the east shore, and the currently unknown city built where [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) is now located.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DOTN-11)</sup>

The Niben region around modern-day Bravil was one of the strongest, centralized regions in the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DOTN-11)</sup> due in part to the establishment of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ACOV-12)</sup> While historians are unable to deduce the region's name because of the Ayleid's insular nature, the word they used to describe it translated to "home",<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DOTN-11)</sup> which would be either "hame"<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ONLoad Hecta-13)</sup> or "math" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ART-14)</sup> Ayleid dominance was so ingrained in the region that it was one of the last places to withstand the Alessians during the [slave rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). Stories from then are mostly lost in time and only live in the realm of legend. After a series of invasions, the region fell to one of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s centurions, [Teo Bravillius Tasus](Lore_Teo_Bravillius_Tasus.md) when he and his warriors uncovered their tactics, an early account of the school of [alteration](Lore_Alteration.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DOTN-11)</sup>

When the White-Gold Tower fell in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243), the Ayleids scattered all over Tamriel and sought refuge anywhere they could. This time is remembered as the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md) and while most died on their quest for a new home, some clans were able to survive in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). The Ayleids of Bawn, one of the cities on the Niben Bay were able to live with the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) but as time went on, they abandoned their previous way of life and took on their neighbors' customs, eventually integrated themselves in the greater Bosmeri society.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASIV-15)</sup> Meanwhile, another city on the bay, Nenalata overcame the diaspora when it pledged its allegiance to the Alessians and re-established itself as a vassal-state under the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md). But as the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) began to expand, the Ayleids were eventually evicted from their home and moved to [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), where they built [Bisnensel](Lore_Bisnensel.md) and allied with the [Direnni Hegemony](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-17)</sup>

### The Niben Bay in the First and Second Eras
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/12/ON-place-Bay_Bridge.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bay_Bridge.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bay_Bridge.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Bay_Bridge.jpg) The Bay Bridge [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/dc/ON-place-Fort_Grief_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Fort_Grief_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Grief_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Fort_Grief_02.jpg) Fort Grief When [Reman I](Lore_Reman_I.md) defeated the [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) and started the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md) in [1E 2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703), one of his first moves was to bolster the province's borders, specifically to the south in the Niben Bay and the Lower Niben. Some strongholds were built throughout this area, such as [Fort Grief](Lore_Fort_Grief.md) in the middle of the bay<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TROFG-18)</sup> and even [Fort Redmane](Lore_Fort_Redmane.md), a little way south of the bay coast.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ALHOFR-8)</sup> Other forts exist on the bay such as Fort Aurus next to the Corbolo mouth and Fort Irony on the Lower Niben's estuary.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBNiben-4)</sup>

Fort Grief, in particular, was built sometime between [1E 2709](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2709) and [2718](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2718) to protect the Niben Bay's eastern shoreline from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) and the Trans-Niben but like Fort Redmane, it lost its purpose from the Empire's rapid expansion.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TROFG-18)</sup> Since then, however, Fort Grief served as a command center for the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md)<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ONLoad Grief-19)</sup> and a watchtower to combat piracy and smuggling in the Niben Bay. By [1E 2900](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2900),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TROFG-18)</sup> concurrent to the reign of Emperor [Reman III](Lore_Reman_III.md),<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBSancre-20)</sup> Fort Grief was largely abandoned.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TROFG-18)</sup>

When the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) broke out in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580), it left central Cyrodiil sundered three-ways to the alliances. While it is unknown how much of a role the Niben Bay played in the conflict, the northwest shoreline fell under the borders of the war. The coast from Bravil to the south end of the Upper Niben was claimed by the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) while a smidgen of shore between the Upper Niben and the Corbolo fell under the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONCyrodiil-7)</sup> An important point of contention between the two alliances was the Bay Bridge, a stone bridge right where the Niben River meets the bay. On several occasions, the bridge was the site of many skirmishes and battles and has even been destroyed throughout the war.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONBridge-21)</sup> Eventually, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) took over the province and established the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) in the late Second Era. From there, Cyrodiil's restoration was in full motion and everything from roads to cities was rebuilt to reflect the new age of rule.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE1E-3)</sup> Despite that, many of the milegates present throughout central Cyrodiil that saw a lot of use in the Alliance War were never rebuilt, such as the Bay Bridge.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OBMilegates-22)</sup>

### The Niben Bay in the Third Empire
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/OB-quest-The_Forlorn_Watchman.jpg/200px-OB-quest-The_Forlorn_Watchman.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Forlorn_Watchman.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-The_Forlorn_Watchman.jpg) The Forlorn Watchman [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/20/SI-place-Strange_Door.jpg/200px-SI-place-Strange_Door.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Strange_Door.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SI-place-Strange_Door.jpg) The Door to the Shivering Isles On the Last Seed of [3E 421](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_421), a ship known as the Emma May sailed south through the Niben Bay but crashed on the [Mouth of the Panther](Lore_Mouth_of_the_Panther.md) in the midst of a storm.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-LOTEM-23)</sup> Ever since then, a local legend has circulated in Bravil about a [ghost](Lore_Ghost.md) called the Forlorn Watchman, who looks out into the bay precisely at 8pm and walks down the coast. Even though he was harmless, the locals actively avoided him.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-OBGilgondorin-24)</sup> In life, he was Grantham Blakeley, a former crew member of the Emma May who met his untimely end in a mutiny mid storm. Like the other crew members, he died when the ship crashed onto the Mouth of the Panther. His spirit was put to rest when his skeleton was unshackled at the bottom of the ship.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-OBForlorn-25)</sup>

For the longest time in the Third Era, the Khajiit had control over the Western Niben, including some unspecified portion of the Niben Bay. It was disputed territory between the province of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and the County of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) but by [3E 432](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_432), an agreement was made between the current [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) and the Count of Leyawiin, [Marius Caro](Lore_Marius_Caro.md), which leased the land to the Cyrodiils. The decision did not bode well with other people, however, namely a few Khajiit. The situation brought the rise of the [Renrijra Krin](Lore_Renrijra_Krin.md), a militia of Khajiiti nationalists that hoped to retake the land by force,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AT-2)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3E-26)</sup> with or without the Mane's consent. Their movement was able to garner support, even from sympathetic Imperials. As they wrote in their manifesto, the [Ahzirr Traajijazeri](Lore_Ahzirr_Traajijazeri.md), they were willing to go through great lengths to force the Imperials out of the region, even if it were uninhabitable.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AT-2)</sup> Since then, the West Niben and the [Green Road](Lore_Green_Road.md) have been dangerous.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-PGE3E-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-GTL-27)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a [magical door](Lore_Magical_Transportation.md) to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) appeared on an unmarked island in the middle of the Niben Bay. It was placed there by the Daedric Prince of Madness, [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) so that he may find his next champion. The gate led to [the Fringe](Lore_Fringe.md), a small region in the Isles where mortals wait to receive Sheogorath's [gift](Lore_Madness_(disease).md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SIHaskill-28)</sup> This portal remained open even after [Martin](Lore_Martin_Septim.md)'s sacrifice sealed other gates to Oblivion,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>, and the island was still in use during the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Utter-Leyton-31)</sup> This was possible due to the nature of the portal: it acted as a simple doorway or invitation, posing no danger to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) and violating no compact.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SIHaskill-28)</sup> Nevertheless, Bravil's city watch posted a guard on the island to deal with the insane and sometimes dangerous individuals returning from the Isles.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-32)</sup>

## Notable Locales
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b8/OB-scene-Niben_on_Fire.jpg/200px-OB-scene-Niben_on_Fire.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-scene-Niben_on_Fire.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-scene-Niben_on_Fire.jpg) The Niben Bay at sunset
[Bawn](Lore_Bawn.md)
- An Ayleid city on the southern shore of the bay, its people later fled to Valenwood.
[Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md)
- The county seat of Bravil County, located on the western shore near the mouth of the Larsius River.
Door to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md)
- An unnamed island with a magical doorway to the Shivering Isles.
[Fort Grief](Lore_Fort_Grief.md)
- A former Imperial fort in the middle of the bay built to defend the southern bay from Argonia.
[Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md)
- One of the last Ayleid vassal-states in Cyrodiil until their exile into High Rock.
[Temple of the Emperor Zero](Lore_Temple_of_the_Emperor_Zero.md)
- An old tower dedicated to [Emperor Zero](Lore_Emperor_Zero.md). In the late Third Era, it was occupied by Bravil's court mage, Fathis Aren.
