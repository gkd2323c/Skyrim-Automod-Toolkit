# Eye of Magnus


## History

### Dawn Era
Ancient [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) mythology writes that when Magrus fled from [Boethra](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Lorkhaj](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lorkhaj), he could only see out of one eye and fell into the [Moonshadow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moonshadow). There Azurah judged him as too full of fear to rule a sphere, and she tore out his other eye. Magrus left to the [heavens](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) blinded, but Azurah made of his eye a stone to reflect the [Varliance Gate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varliance_Gate). This is the "[Aether Prism](Lore_Sun.md)", which opens at Dawn and closes at Dusk. Some sorcerers hold that Magrus left the eye willingly as an offering to Azurah and her children, and these magi still utter prayers to his name.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSS-9)</sup> However, it is not known if the Eye of Magnus has any connection to this myth.

### Merethic Era
The Eye of Magnus was discovered by the ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) when they were building the city of [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md). The Nords attempted to keep it buried, but the [Snow Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snow_Elves) learned of its existence and coveted it for themselves. During the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md) the elves assaulted Saarthal to secure this powerful artifact for themselves. [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md) rallied together his people to keep the elves from seizing it, and the Nords were successful in preventing the elves from obtaining the artifact. The Eye was buried deep below the earth and sealed away.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-No T-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-EGTSkyrim-7)</sup>

### First Era
The evil Nord Jyrik, a depraved warlock and the son of the great mage [Gauldur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gauldur), was locked away by the wizards of [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md) in the Orb Chamber of Saarthal, where the Eye of Magnus had been placed, in order to avoid Jyrik ever being reanimated to life. And Jyrik's power had become bound up in the magic of the Eye of Magnus.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DVN-10)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Prima-5)</sup><sup>:256</sup><sup>:787</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Quest Under Sarth-11)</sup>

### Fourth Era
In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), during an expedition in Saarthal, the Eye of Magnus was uncovered by members of the [College of Winterhold](Lore_College_of_Winterhold.md) and transported to the college for examination after a confrontation with the undead corpse of Jyrik Gauldurson.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Quest Under Sarth-11)</sup> [Ancano](Lore_Ancano.md), a [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) agent, learned about the Eye's properties from the [Augur of Dunlain](Lore_Augur_of_Dunlain.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Augur-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Prima-5)</sup><sup>:261</sup> and eventually turned on the College, then attempting to activate the power of the Eye, but was prevented and killed by members of the college.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Quest Eye-13)</sup> Monks from the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), who had been observing the situation, arrived to take the artifact, claiming that it was too unstable to keep in [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Quest Eye-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-14)</sup> Ancano's meddling with the eye had long-term effects, causing Ruptures to appear across [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), through which dangerous Magic Anomalies emerged. The college tried its best to close these Ruptures using the Staff of Magnus.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup>

## Notes
- In the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) lesson of the divine body, the phrase *"The Eye of Magnus is always upon us, in the spells and enchantments that devout Mages conjure."* is recited by religious preachers.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ESOMirnor-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOUurkar-18)</sup> This appears to refer to the concept of the [Aurbic Eye](Lore_Aurbic_Eye.md), also sometimes called the Eye of Magnus, rather than the artifact itself.
- The orb of Vanus Galerion's Staff directly resembles the Eye of Magnus.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup>
- The central orb component of the [Orrery of Elden Root](Lore_The_Towers.md#Green-Sap) was modelled to directly resemble the Eye of Magnus, but its plates rotate counter to each other when activated and featured [Elven script](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elven_Alphabets) rather than Elder script.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Eld Root Orrery Art-20)</sup> Identical orbs (albeit smaller) to the Orrery of Elden Root's orb can be found all throughout the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md)'s chambers in their home [Ceporah Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ceporah_Tower).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> [The Obscuros](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Obscuros) artifact on [Dranil Kir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dranil_Kir) visually matches the orbs of Ceporah Tower.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Knives-22)</sup>
- [Ada'Soom Dir-Kamal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ada%27Soom_Dir-Kamal) landed on the White River close to Winterhold looking for something or someone called the "[Ordained Receptacle](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordained_Receptacle)", which may or may not have been related to the Eye of Magnus.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TSAI-23)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Writing AUA-24)</sup>
- [Unused concept art for *Skyrim*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-Eye_of_Magnus_Diadem.png) shows a wearable diadem artifact labelled "Eye of Magnus", which was meant to be embody a [Third Eye](Lore_Third_Eye.md).<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-25)</sup>
