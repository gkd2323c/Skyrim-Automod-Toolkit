—Herminia Cinna<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-HC-1)</sup> Herminia Cinna was a renowned [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) sorceress and a scholar of the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md) who lived in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). She is the author of *[The Last King of the Ayleids](Lore_The_Last_King_of_the_Ayleids.md)*, a study of the Late Ayleid Period and King [Laloriaran Dynar](Lore_Laloriaran_Dynar.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLKot A-2)</sup> She also helped translate fragments of the mysterious Tract of Merid-Nunda, an early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) manuscript detailing the origin myth of [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Eo MN-3)</sup>

Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Cinna came into possession of the [Crown of Nenalata](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md), a very valuable Ayleid artifact, as well as a key to the sealed royal burial chamber of [Lindai](Lore_Lindai.md). An [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) collector of Ayleid antiquities called [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md) attempted to purchase the crown from her, hoping to use it to declare himself King of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md) and lead a rebirth of Ayleid civilization in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Fearing Umbacano's ulterior motives, she refused, and Umbacano sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md) to steal it from her house in the Elven Gardens District. Cinna instead offered the key, which would have allowed the Hero to retrieve the Crown of Lindai in order to trick Umbacano. It is unknown if the Hero went ahead with this plan or not.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

Although Cinna lived during the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), her work appeared,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TLKot A-2)</sup> and was cited by scholars such as [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus.md) during the attempted [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Eo MN-3)</sup> It is possible that these books were displaced out of time, as was done by the enchanted library of [Gandranen](Lore_Gandranen.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
