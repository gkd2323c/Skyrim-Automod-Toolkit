# Faerie

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/34/ON-creature-Nixad.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Nixad.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nixad.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Nixad.jpg) A Turquoise Nixad Faerie, also called **Fey**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup> **Fay**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Orchid Nixad-2)</sup> and **Fairy**/**Fairies**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OMC-4)</sup> are magical, mischievous, and unpredictable spirit creatures that come in a variety of forms.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Arana-5)</sup> Their plans and schemes are not governed by a higher purpose, simply by their own whim.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup> Their behaviors have borne them the reputation of thieves, tricksters, and nuisances.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AGCB-6)</sup> Faerie as a term seems to apply to creatures with wildly different natures, it is thought the term refers to anything unpredictable in nature.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup>

References to fairies commonly appear throughout many cultures, such as fantasy plays commonly involving fairies,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Arena Jobs-7)</sup> the ability of [the Mage](Lore_The_Mage.md) birthsign named for the Fay,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MWBirthsigns-8)</sup> and expressions based on faerie behavior,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-PS-9)</sup> notably the term "fairy tale" for mythical stories.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-GFMM-10)</sup> Some exclamations among the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) are "Faeries of the Grove"<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ARWEexcl-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DFWEexcl-12)</sup> and "Faerielight".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ARHEexcl-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-DFHEexcl-14)</sup>

Fairies are most commonly described as inhabiting the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md),<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DRD-15)</sup> with some variants reported as inhabiting no other province besides it.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3S-16)</sup> Fairy civilizations inhabited [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) prior to the arrival of the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-PGE3V-3)</sup> Faeries were also known to inhabit the banks of the [Karth River](Lore_Karth_River.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Arana-5)</sup>

## The Faerie Chain
In [2E 456](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_456), Ahrtabazus, a scholar studying at the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) on [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), published in [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) a controversial theory on faerie known as the "Faerie Chain". In it, he arranged the fey variants on a hierarchical chain, though not strictly as an order of command, as faerie as a whole were described as neither followers nor leaders.

The chain ranged from minor creatures such as Pixies at one end to godlike beings such as Gheateus, Chonus, and Sygria at the other. In the middle were human and semi-human beings, extending to intelligent trees, brooks, rocks, and even mountains. Ahrtabazus drew much criticism for adding a footnote suggesting that [elves](Lore_Mer.md) as a whole might be part of the chain, above Pixies and below Nephrine, due to similarities in features and propensity for magicka. This now-discarded claim was challenged as being based on slight coincidences, and the theory was later modified.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup>

Because it was based on coincidental evidence and supported by auxiliary theories, some faerie scholars considered the theory speculative or incorrect. Nevertheless, with modification, the Faerie Chain theory gained wider acceptance after its publication.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup>

## Species

### Sprites
[Sprites](Lore_Sprite.md) are fay creatures that appear as floating orbs of light.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SJ-17)</sup> Certain curses are capable of transforming people into sprites.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

### Sylphim
[Sylphim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sylphim) refers to minor fairies, often found surrounding other more powerful faeries, such as Gheatus or Sygria.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup>

### Pixies
[Pixies](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pixie) (called [Whilloki](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Whilloki) by the Redguards)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup> are a type of fay described as appearing as glimmering sparks with the personality of whimsical pranksters.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OMC-4)</sup>

### Nixads
[Nixads](Lore_Nixad.md) are mischievous insect-like fay creatures that fly using elytra and inhabit various forests and glades in Tamriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Orchid Nixad-2)</sup> They have a penchant for stealing valuables and eating sweets.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BTATN-19)</sup> They can be encountered in most provinces of Tamriel. Despite being found in Oblivion Nixads are not Daedric. They do have an affinity for the elements.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-FAF-20)</sup> They are known to steal pouches of gold and sugary sweets like [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BTATN-19)</sup> They are also said to pluck the eyes of pompous children, rolling them around in moon sugar afterwards to eat them as a delicacy.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AGCB-6)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ANMMDI-21)</sup>

### Nephrine
[Nephrine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nephrine) are a type of fay. In the original "Faerie Chain" theory (Ahrtabazus, [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md), [2E 456](Lore_Second_Era.md)), elves were suggested to be part of the faerie chain, above Pixies and below Nephrine. This now-discarded claim was challenged as being based on slight coincidences, and the theory was later modified.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup>

### Ilyadi
[Ilyadi](Lore_Ilyadi.md) (or Illyadi)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup> were many-eyed forest giants taller than the trees, with eyes that cover their heads, that once inhabited the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset-22)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3S-16)</sup> They attacked the early [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), but were rendered extinct by [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md)'s use of [meteoric](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) [glass](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) to channel aetherial energy to kill them,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TLo F-23)</sup> and they were exterminated in a few short years.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset-22)</sup> Some have sought out ways to bring them back from extinction.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Rt W-24)</sup>

### Gheatus
[Gheatus](Lore_Gheatus.md) (or Gheateus)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup> were ancient godlike rock-spirits that took the form of a man or a group of men who are formed by the earth itself, that once inhabited the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-PGE3S-16)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Wansalen-25)</sup> They attacked the early [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), but were fought off by [Torinaan](Lore_Torinaan.md)'s use of ancestral lore and [meteoric](Lore_Varla_Stone.md) [glass](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md) to channel aetherial energy in the form of lightning, ice, and fire to kill them,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TLo F-23)</sup> and they were exterminated in a few short years.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Rt W-24)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-EGTSummerset-22)</sup> Legend has it that the excavation of Wansalen was halted, and the halls abandoned, when the [Elven](Lore_Aldmer.md) miners roused and angered an ancient Gheatus, but the [Sapiarchs](Lore_Sapiarchs.md) dismiss this story as a fable with no truth to it.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Wansalen-25)</sup> Some have sought out ways to bring them back from extinction.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Rt W-24)</sup>

### Chonus
[Chonus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chonus) are described as "godlike beings" and are considered a variant of faerie.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup>

### Sygria
[Sygria](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sygria) are described as a lost race of "godlike beings" who are considered a variant of faerie, often surrounded by a host of minor Sylphim.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TF-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

## Compared Creatures
Many other magical or spirit creatures have been compared to the fae, though whether these mean any true relation or are simply colloquialisms is unclear. [Spriggans](Lore_Spriggan.md) are floral [nature spirits](Lore_Nature_Spirit.md) sometimes described as reclusive woodland fairykin.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-DUG-27)</sup> How closely related they are to fae is undetermined.<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> [Dragonlings](Lore_Dragonling.md) are flying reptiles sometimes referred to as "Fairy Dragons".<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-FDS-28)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-DFTextures-29)</sup> [Nereids](Lore_Nereid.md) are aquatic nature spirits appearing in the shape of a [fish](Lore_Fish.md) -like woman. They are described as water sprites in Redguard fables, suggesting they might be related to sprites and other faeries.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-KC-30)</sup> Similarly, [imps](Lore_Imp.md) have been colloquially described as "trickster sprites".<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-KCAHC-31)</sup> [Will-o-the-wisps](Lore_Will-o-the-Wisp.md), though similar to sprites, are considered separate from fairy folk.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-DRD-15)</sup> [Nymphs](Lore_Nymph.md) are spirit creatures of unearthly beauty whose language is similar to that of Fae and [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md), but they are implied to be separate from Fae.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASGt N-32)</sup>

- While most of the info on them was introduced in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*, much of the content regarding the Fey was cut or remains unused in the final game. However, the Fey are nonetheless present as a faction, despite their lack of appearance anywhere. They are listed as a unique race called Fey from the Skeffington Wood region. The textures associated with their graphics are titled "regal faerie" depicted big-eyed, purple-haired, well-dressed humanoids with tapered ears, and alternative graphics displayed them without the purple hair or big eyes, with all of the graphics remaining unused in the final game. A character language skill called Faerie was planned, along with the string "Faerie is a language skill checked whenever one attempts to speak with a Faerie. " Two strings for the character background history refer to the fey as "the fey from the haunted glens" and would have allowed an option for the player to start with a higher Faerie skill by choosing having a higher capability to "commune with the Faerie". Whatsmore, two [exclamations](Lore_Exclamations.md) uttered by [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) and [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) respectively are "Faeries of the Grove" and "Faerielight". A portrait for a character called [the Fey](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:The_Fey), described as a supernatural being in the shape of a woman with unknown origins and motivations, was also included, but the character went unused. The portrait, however, was mistakenly used for various [Dark Brotherhood](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Dark_Brotherhood) members. [Medora Direnni](Lore_Medora_Direnni.md) was also called "the fairy lady" by some folk.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-FM-34)</sup>
