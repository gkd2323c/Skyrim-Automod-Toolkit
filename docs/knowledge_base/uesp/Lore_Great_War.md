# Great War


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png/200px-LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-art-Original_Load_Screen.png) The [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md) at the Battle of the Red Ring The Great War, referred to as the **First War Against the Empire** by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TDUS-1)</sup> was a war fought from [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171) to [175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175) between the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and the [Third Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md). The war consisted primarily of a Dominion invasion of Imperial territory, culminating in the capture of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) before its recapture by the Empire. The war eventually ended when the Empire was forced to sign the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md), a punishing peace treaty.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup>

## Background
After the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) destabilized [Summerset](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Summerset), the [Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sapiarchs) of the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) ceded power to the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) and they subsequently seized total control in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), seceding from the Empire, and renaming the Summerset Isles to Alinor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> In [4E 29](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_29), the Thalmor took control of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) in a coup, reforming the Aldmeri Dominion, while [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) came under the influence of the Thalmor when they claimed credit for restoring the moons following the [Void Nights](Lore_Void_Nights.md) crisis, resulting in the dissolution of the [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr_Confederacy.md) and recreation of the kingdoms of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md) and [Pellitine](Lore_Pellitine.md) as client states of the Aldmeri Dominion in [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup>

The Empire of Cyrodiil, on the other hand, became progressively weaker; by the accession of [Titus Mede II](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md) in [4E 168](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_168), the Empire had lost the provinces of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), Elsweyr, Valenwood, and [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DBArano-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TIC-4)</sup><sup>:109</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-LOS-5)</sup><sup>:16</sup> and found its province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) severely weakened by renewed conflicts between the Crowns and Forebears.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup>

On the 30th of Frostfall, [4E 171](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_171), the Thalmor sent an ambassador to the Emperor to demand tribute, the disbandment of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), the banning of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship, and the cession of large sections of Hammerfell to the Dominion. The Emperor, despite being advised of the Empire's glaring military weakness, rejected the ultimatum, and the Thalmor ambassador responded by upending a cart that they had brought, spilling the heads of every Blades agent in Dominion territory onto the floor. This marked the beginning of the Great War.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup>

## The War
Within days of the declaration of war, two Thalmor armies assaulted simultaneously. One, led by [Lady Arannelya](Lore_Arannelya.md), entered western Cyrodiil from Valenwood, bypassed its cities, and crossed to Hammerfell to join with Dominion attackers landing on its southern coast. The Dominion forces occupied the south, and the Empire retreated across the [Alik'r Desert](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alik%27r_Desert) in the March of Thirst.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> At least one battle took place in southern Hammerfell, as the soldier Angarion was named "Hero of the Battle of Roseguard".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-BLAt B-6)</sup>

Concurrently, the Thalmor general and [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worshipper [Lord Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md) led a force into Cyrodiil from [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and seized [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). As they continued advancing in [4E 172](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_172), Naarifin seized [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) and [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md). By the end of the year, Naarifin had reached the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). As the [Imperial Navy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Navy) defended the [Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben) and [Lake Rumare](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Rumare), legions from Skyrim joined the Imperial force in early [4E 173](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_173). The Dominion, however, crossed the Niben and surrounded the Imperial City on three sides, eventually seizing the city in [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> The Emperor broke out of the city,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> retreating north to Skyrim and sacrificed one of his legions to hold Naarifin's forces.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/90/LG-quest-Interlude_06.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Interlude_06.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Interlude_06.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Interlude_06.jpg) Lord Naarifin [Ulfric Stormcloak](Lore_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), the son of the Jarl of Windhelm, ended his tutelage under the [Greybeard Monastic Order](Lore_Greybeards.md) to fight in the war, but he was captured early on by the Thalmor. He was tortured and interrogated by [Elenwen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elenwen), giving them information the Thalmor considered inconsequential, but they led him to believe that it had been crucial to the capture of the Imperial City. The Thalmor then decided to allow his escape, and deemed him an asset to their cause.

Naarifin's true endgame in the Imperial City was to bring about a prophecy entitled "The Culling" with the support of the Dremora blademaster Reive, a servant of [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LGRW-8)</sup> who led a cult in support of Naarifin's plan.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LGDO-9)</sup> Naarifin also used the [Orb of Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orb_of_Vaermina) to observe the Imperial army and predict its movements.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LGI-10)</sup>

In [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), Lady Arannelya's attempt to join Naarifin's forces had been foiled by General Decianus, who stopped her advance at [Skaven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skaven). When he was ordered to reinforce the armies in Cyrodiil, he was torn between his obligations to Hammerfell and the Empire. As a compromise, he discharged much of his army as "invalids"; these would become the core of the armies that would continue to defend Hammerfell, and thanks to them would lead Arannelya and the Dominion to believe that the Imperial Legions were still pinned down in Hammerfell.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> Hammerfell's fortunes would continue to improve as the [Forebear](Lore_Forebears.md) armies of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md) came to reinforce and liberate the [Crown](Lore_Crowns.md) city of [Hegathe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hegathe), leading to the reconciliation of the political conflict that had defined the Redguards of Hammerfell since its foundation.

In the winter of [4E 174](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_174) and [175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175), the Emperor regathered his forces with reinforcements from Hammerfell and Skyrim for an assault.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> Having met a [Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md), and having been incapacitated by an assassin's attack, the Emperor appointed the Hero to lead the battle in his stead and guise.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LGAKit D-11)</sup> Having breached the city at the Battle of the Red Ring, the Hero personally confronted Lord Naarifin and defeated him, closing the Oblivion gate he had opened to effect the Culling.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-LGTS-12)</sup> The Emperor took the credit,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LGE-13)</sup> and Lord Naarifin was reportedly kept alive for 33 days until either dying or being "carried off by a winged daedra", the former of which is the accepted story.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> The war between the Empire and the Dominion continued briefly until they reached a peace treaty with the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md).

During the war, other political factions in Tamriel did not cease activity. In Skyrim, the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) took advantage of the Empire's absence to seize Markarth and the Reach from Nordic control, holding the city for two years.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TBo M-14)</sup>

## The [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md)
After recapturing the Imperial City, with his Legions exhausted from the fighting and much of the Empire devastated, Titus Mede II signed the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175). This peace treaty granted many of the same concessions that were demanded by the Thalmor at the beginning of the war, but nonetheless the treaty was signed,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> as a continuation of the war likely would have resulted in Imperial defeat.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-17)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup>

The White-Gold Concordat was especially hated for its banning of [Talos](Lore_Talos.md) worship,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-SRJ-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SRH-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Fft T-21)</sup> especially by Nords who viewed this as a betrayal<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-SRNSS-22)</sup> despite propaganda produced to promote the Thalmor theology such as *[The Talos Mistake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Talos_Mistake)*<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TTM-23)</sup> and Imperial leniency in enforcement.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TBo M-14)</sup> Following the Markarth Incident, the Emperor was forced to crack down on Talos worship and allow Thalmor Justiciars to enforce the ban on Talos worship.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Alv-24)</sup> Local leaders, such as the jarls of Skyrim, were financially compensated after learning of its terms,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-SRBPI-25)</sup> but many had oppositions to the treaty, eventually causing the [Skyrim Civil War](Lore_Skyrim_Civil_War.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SRUS-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SR-27)</sup>

The Concordat also disbanded the Blades in retaliation for past offenses.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SRDDI-28)</sup> Many, thus, were left to find new purposes; some, such as [Delphine](Lore_Delphine.md)<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-SRDDI-28)</sup> and Azzin returned to the organization's [Dragonguard](Lore_Dragonguard.md) roots and hunted Dragons,<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-BLATDS-29)</sup> while others such as [the Warrior](Lore_Hero.md#Warrior) simply returned to their homes.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-BL-30)</sup>

Furthermore, the Concordat ceded much of Southern Hammerfell to the Aldmeri Dominion. The Redguards, however, refused to accept this and fought the Dominion to a stalemate over the following five years and eventually signed the [Second Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_Second_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md). Critics of Titus II pointed to the Aldmeri Dominion's failure in Hammerfell as proof that the White-Gold Concordat was unnecessary and that if Titus II had kept his nerve, the Dominion could have been defeated by the combined forces of Hammerfell and the rest of the Empire.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup> The terms of the White-Gold Concordat were considered harsh, but Titus II believed they were necessary to secure peace and give the Empire time to rebuild. Some accounts add that the Imperial armies were in no condition to continue the war, and that prolonging the conflict against the Aldmeri Dominion would likely have ended in Imperial defeat.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TGW-2)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-31)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-32)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-33)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-34)</sup>

After the Great War many Legionnaires changed careers. The war hero Martius Silius became a gladiator in the Imperial Arena circuit, along with cohorts from his time in the War.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-BLM-35)</sup> Some Nord veterans of the war such as Galmar Stone-Fist would go on to join Ulfric Stormcloak's rebellion,<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-SRGSF-36)</sup> while others such as [Legate Rikke](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rikke) would remain with the Legion to extinguish the revolt,<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-SRLR-37)</sup> while yet others simply retired, often proud of the service and keeping their armor as a token thereof.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-SRBM-38)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-SRAR-39)</sup> King [Durak gro-Batul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durak_gro-Batul) of [Bloodfall](Lore_Bloodfall.md) and many of his loyal soldiers fought on the side of the Empire during the Great War.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TBQ-40)</sup>
