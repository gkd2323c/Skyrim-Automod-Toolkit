# Dragon Cult

—A [note](Skyrim_Note_(Forelhost_Crypt).md) written by [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) holdouts of the Dragon Cult in [Forelhost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forelhost) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg/200px-SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-interior-Bromjunaar_Sanctuary.jpg) Masks of the nine dragon cult leaders [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/ON-texture-Nord Dungeon Banner Dragon.png/100px-ON-texture-Nord Dungeon Banner Dragon.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-texture-Nord Dungeon Banner Dragon.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-texture-Nord Dungeon Banner Dragon.png) Dragon Cult Banner The Dragon Cult (also known as the **Atmoran Dragon Cult** or **Cult of the Dragon Priests**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-1)</sup> began as a sect within the traditions of animal worship brought by [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). Certain scholars believe these were "totem animals" for the modern [Divines](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nine_Divines); in which case, worship of the dragon corresponded to worship of [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md). Dragons were revered more than any other, and they were comfortable in assuming positions as god-kings over men. Their servants, the [dragon priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), kept the peace between dragons and men, and their power was so great that uttering any word for "dragon" was forbidden to all mankind except them. They made laws for the society of men, and were on par with kings. Grand temples were built to honor the dragons and appease them in life and death, many of which survive as [draugr](Lore_Draugr.md) -infested ruins today.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup>

Some time during or soon after the migrations to Tamriel, the dragon cult began to assert more influence and become much more malevolent.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup> From their capital [Bromjunaar](Lore_Labyrinthian.md) in modern-day [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-AMM-3)</sup> they ruled [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) with an iron fist, effectively enslaving the populace. What led to this change was lost to historians, theories holding either a particular dragon, a series of weak kings, or an ambitious dragon priest caused the shift in the Cult's disposition. <sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup> Men eventually rebelled in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), leading to the ancient [Dragon War](Lore_Dragon_War.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup>

Men, with the help of several dragons, eventually secured victory with the defeat of Alduin. The dragon priests were overthrown and dragons were slaughtered in large numbers before fleeing to remote areas. Though they had lost their god-kings and were cast out of mainstream [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) society, the dragon cult managed to adapt and survive for centuries.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup> Whenever they could lay hands on the remains of a dragon, they would build a tomb for it known as a "dragon mound", a practice they maintained for centuries after the war.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-AOD-4)</sup> They believed that one day the dragons would rise again and reward the faithful.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup>

When the priests of the Eight Divines arrived in Skyrim in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), they found it very difficult to convert the Nords, in part because the Nords had abandoned the worship of the dragon along with the dragon cult. Thus, they only knew of the Great Dragon, Akatosh, as the [elven](Lore_Mer.md) god [Auriel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Auriel), whom they despised as a demon. The missionaries were able to allay some concerns by claiming that Alduin and those who followed him had acted against the wishes of Akatosh, and it was his intervention that led some dragons to assist men against their brethren. The dragon cult was eventually driven extinct, surviving only in the form of draugr who continue to prowl ancient ruins.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TDW-2)</sup> The last known holdouts were discovered and besieged in the monastery of [Forelhost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forelhost) in the mountains of [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md) by the forces of [High King Harald](Lore_Harald.md), eventually committing mass suicide in [1E 140](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_140).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SSSJ-5)</sup>

## Temples
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1f/SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg/200px-SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Bleak_Falls_Barrow.jpg) Bleak Falls Barrow, a Dragon Cult temple [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/SR-trap-Dragon_Claw_Switch.jpg/200px-SR-trap-Dragon_Claw_Switch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-trap-Dragon_Claw_Switch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-trap-Dragon_Claw_Switch.jpg) Dragon Claw Switch The Dragon Cult was known to have many great and elaborate temples across Skyrim, [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), and even areas of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md). These temples were constructed in honor of the [dragon priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), who were entombed within, eternally guarded by draugr servants. These temples were typically decorated with effigies to the ancient Nordic animal gods: the [bear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bear), dragon, [fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fox), [moth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Moth), [owl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Owl), [snake](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Snake), [whale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Whale), and [wolf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wolf).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup> The inner parts of the temples are often sealed by elaborate vault doors, each one requiring its own key in the form of a special dragon claw sculpture.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup>
