# Unarmed Combat


## Base Unarmed Damage and Reach
While all races of [Men](Lore_Man.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) do the same amount of unarmed damage (base of 4), [Khajiit](Skyrim_Khajiit.md) and [Argonians](Skyrim_Argonian.md) do more unarmed damage. The Khajiit's beginning advantage, granted by their passive [claws](Skyrim_Khajiit.md#Traits) trait, enables them to deal more damage with fists than with weapons in the early levels of the game. However, these damage values do not improve with the level progression of skills (such as [One-Handed](Skyrim_One-handed.md)) or overall level, meaning eventually unarmed attacks become less effective than attacks with weapons or spells as you continue to improve the relevant skill trees.

[†](#note_claws) The in-game text states that the *Claws* passive ability adds 15 points to Khajiits' unarmed damage, but the actual effect magnitude is 12. The game data also describes the effect as "Khajiit claws do four times the damage of a man's punch", which is incorrect, as it is actually 5.5 times the damage.
### Unarmed Reach
In game terms, daggers have a reach of 0.7, one-handed weapons a reach of 1, and two-handed weapons a reach of 1.3; relative to these, all playable races have the same unarmed reach, about 0.68.

## Increasing Unarmed Damage, Reach, and Speed

### Equipment
While the exact speed of unarmed attacks is not known, if it is slower than 1.3 (dagger speed), you can speed it up to dagger speed like with any one-handed weapon, by holding a dagger in your *left* hand and punching with your right, at the expense of being unable to block.

### Perks
The [*Fists of Steel*](Skyrim_Fists_of_Steel.md) perk adds between 10 (iron) and 18 (Daedric) to the total unarmed damage you deal when you wear [heavy armor gauntlets](Skyrim_Armor.md#Gauntlets_2). The value added to the damage is the base armor value of the gauntlets; thus, improving the quality of such gauntlets by [smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) does not increase this value, nor does leveling the [Heavy Armor](Skyrim_Heavy_Armor.md) skill.

Contrary to popular belief, the *[Dual Flurry](Skyrim_Dual_Flurry.md)* and *[Dual Savagery](Skyrim_Dual_Flurry.md)* perks have no effect whatsoever.

### Enchantments
A unique item found within [The Ratway](Skyrim_The_Ratway.md), the *[Gloves of the Pugilist](Skyrim_Gloves_of_the_Pugilist.md)* is the only item within the game that contains the *[Fortify Unarmed Damage](Skyrim_Fortify_Unarmed_Damage.md)* enchantment. To maximize unarmed damage, you can disenchant these gloves and then apply the enchantment to both gloves and rings.

Now with *[Anniversary Edition](Skyrim_Anniversary_Edition.md)*, there are more items that contain the Fortify Unarmed Damage enchantment. The first comes with *[Fearsome Fists](Skyrim_Fearsome_Fists.md)* with alternate designs to gauntlets and gloves for unarmed combat, and merchants can sell an enchanted version with Fortify Unarmed Damage. A second Creation Club that came with *Anniversary Edition* is *[The Contest](Skyrim_The_Contest.md)* with the unique item [Fists of Randagulf](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fists_of_Randagulf), however the enchantment description is wrong. It doesn't increase unarmed damage by 20%, but by 20 points.

### Sneaking
[Sneaking](Skyrim_Sneak.md) while performing an unarmed strike applies the double damage effect. This also includes anything with the Fortify Unarmed Damage enchantment.

### Lycanthropy and Vampirism
[Vampire lords](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md) and [werewolves](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md) have increased unarmed damage and unarmed reach 1.06, slightly more than one-handed weapons.

#### Lycanthropy
Being a werewolf in beast form sets your base unarmed damage to 20 (meaning Khajiit actually *lose* damage, since they lose their Claws trait, dropping from 22 to 20, and Argonians gain less benefit than other races) and grants the werewolf [additional bonuses](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md#Beast_Form) to unarmed combat damage depending on character level, from 0 at level 10 or less to 60 at level 46 . The [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on added another way to increase the unarmed base damage while being a werewolf by the addition of a specific perk, *[Bestial Strength](Skyrim_Lycanthropy.md#Skill_Perks)*. This perk can be chosen multiple times and adds a flat percentage-based damage increase to all damage while in the Werewolf Beast Form, multiplying unarmed damage by 1.25 to 2, depending on number of times taken.

Most of your equipment does not apply in beast form, so you *lose* Fists of Steel if you had it, but you can wear a [Ring of Bloodlust](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ring_of_Bloodlust)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> for another 1.5x multiplier to unarmed damage, at the cost of applying the same multiplier to taken damage.

The net effect of all of these is unarmed damage 80 base at level 50, 160 with all of the damage increasing perks, and 120 or 240, respectively, while wearing the Bloodlust ring.

Note that being in Beast Form also alters the speed of your unarmed attacks (to be faster), but the exact formula is not known.

#### Vampirism
Being a [vampire](Skyrim_Vampirism.md) grants the possibility of increasing unarmed combat by using the power [Vampire Lord](Skyrim_Vampire_Lord.md):

- Being a Lord sets your race to Vampire Lord, which has the same base unarmed damage as Khajiit or Argonians (10).
- Starting at level 11, Vampire Claws increase unarmed damage by 5 for every five levels, to a maximum of 40 points at level 46 and above.
- The Vampire Lord Perk Tree contains the *[Poison Talons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Poison_Talons)* perk, which increases unarmed damage by 20 points of poison damage.

- If you have the *[Ring of The Beast](Skyrim_Ring_of_The_Beast.md)*, your unarmed damage increases by another 20 points(They even work when you're a Mortal).

Overall unarmed damage therefore ranges between 10 at low levels with no benefits up to 90 (70 physical and 20 poison) with all possible benefits.

Taking the *[Necromage](Skyrim_Necromage_(perk).md)* perk in the [Restoration](Skyrim_Restoration.md) tree amplifies Fists of Steel mentioned above by 25% as a vampire, but not while in Vampire Lord form.

It's also possible to decrease unarmed combat damage, since you will *lose* the following unarmed damage sources:

- All equipment bonuses to Fortify Unarmed Damage (not counting the Ring of The Beast).
- The Khajiit Claws ability.

## Brawls
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b1/SR-quest-Fight%21_Fight%21.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Fight%21_Fight%21.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Fight!_Fight!.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Fight!_Fight!.jpg) Brawling with [Chief Burguk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Chief_Burguk) A brawl is a fistfight in which either party attempts to beat the other until their health is depleted (but not killed). Some NPCs will challenge you to a brawl and some quests require you to challenge an NPC to brawl. Rewards for winning a brawl can include a monetary gain from a bet with your opponent, a reward from a quest giver, obtaining information, and increasing NPCs' [disposition](Skyrim_Disposition.md) toward you.

During a brawl, spectators will gather around you and your opponent and shout encouragements or other phrases at you, such as *"Fight! Fight!"*, *"Those fools are actually fighting!"*, *"Who taught you to fight? Keep those hands up!"*, *"Drive that snowback to the ground, come on!"*, *"Remember ... hit the one in the middle!"*, *"Are you just going to take that? Fight back!"*, and *"Twelve septims on the big one!"*

You must fight someone by attacking only with your fists or bashing with a shield until a winner emerges. When combat begins, your weapons and spells automatically unequip. You may use spells like [Healing](Skyrim_Healing.md), or a shield, but any damage dealt to your brawling partner other than that from bashing or punching (including the passive effect from the [Ebony Mail](Skyrim_Ebony_Mail.md) and the [Lord's Mail](Skyrim_Lord%27s_Mail_(item).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Lord%27s_Mail.md)</sup>) will void the fight and count as a [crime](Skyrim_Crime.md). The rules of brawling prohibit you from healing an opponent.

## Dialogue
- If you approach a guard in any hold with your fists up, they may say, *"You come up to me, fists raised? You looking for a beating?"* Alternatively, if you are a Khajiit, they may say, *"Sheathe those claws, Khajiit."*

## Notes
- Unlike earlier TES games, unarmed attacks damage only the health of the recipient, not stamina or magicka.
- It is not possible to use unarmed attacks while riding a [horse](Skyrim_Horses.md).
- If you utilize the [Fortify Restoration](Skyrim_Fortify_Restoration.md) [glitch](Skyrim_Glitches.md) to create more powerful rings and bracers/gloves/gauntlets with the Fortify Unarmed Damage effect, it is possible to make it a viable unarmed build which can equal anything done by more "traditional" weapons, though of course there is no way to increase damage done by [smithing](Skyrim_Smithing.md) or [alchemy](Skyrim_Alchemy.md), via potions with other "fortify [weapon type]" effects. You are also unable to use any poisons in unarmed combat.
- A benefit to unarmed combat is that Draugr cannot disarm you through the [Disarm](Skyrim_Disarm.md) Shout. NPCs with the Disarming Bash perk (from the [Block](Skyrim_Block.md) skill), mostly notably the [Ebony Warrior](Skyrim_Ebony_Warrior.md), will also be unable to disarm you, as you are not wielding any "true" weapons.
- If you have a shield, weapon or spell in the left hand, you may not get one of the "cinematic kills" for unarmed combat.
