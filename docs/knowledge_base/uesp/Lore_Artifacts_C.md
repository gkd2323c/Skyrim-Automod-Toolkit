## Calcelmo's Stone
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/9c/SR-quest-Hard_Answers.jpg/200px-SR-quest-Hard_Answers.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Hard_Answers.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-quest-Hard_Answers.jpg) Calcelmo's Stone [Calcelmo's Stone](Lore_Calcelmo%27s_Stone.md) is an artifact bearing inscriptions in both the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer_Alphabet.md) and [Falmer](Lore_Falmer_Alphabet.md) alphabets. It was owned by [Calcelmo](Lore_Calcelmo.md), the court wizard of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) and one of the most recognizable [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) scholars at the time. The stone was kept in his tower in [Understone Keep](Lore_Understone_Keep.md) and under heavy protection.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Hard_Answers.md)

## Cascadia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/BL-item-Cascadia.jpg/200px-BL-item-Cascadia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Cascadia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Cascadia.jpg) Cascadia [Cascadia](Lore_Cascadia.md) is an enchanted sapphire ring of either [Maormeri](Lore_Maormer.md) or [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) origins. Some sources believe that the ring was crafted by the Sea Elves, but others claim that it was a failed attempt of a Nord enchanter to create a water-breathing ring. Although it does not provide the ability to breathe underwater, the sounds of the ocean can be heard from its stone. It also provides a boon to the absorptive abilities of the wearer, improves their willpower by granting them a defensive boon while casting spells, and enhances their melee combat capabilities.

Around [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180), it came into possession of [the Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md).

**Found in:** - [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Cascadia)

## Chillrend
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/77/SR-item-Chillrend.jpg/200px-SR-item-Chillrend.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Chillrend.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Chillrend.jpg) Chillrend [Chillrend](Lore_Chillrend.md) is an [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of unknown origins. It takes the form of a glass shortsword, although, unlike regular [malachite](Lore_Malachite.md), it is blue instead of green. Like other glass weapons, its hilt was fashioned from rare metals and [moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md). Its enchantment varies in strength and properties, but it always causes magical frost damage to the opponent. Other known effects include causing a weakness to frost, or temporary paralysis. It also repairs itself when the wielder takes frost damage.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Chillrend), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Chillrend.md), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Chillrend)

## Chrysamere
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/32/SR-item-Chrysamere.jpg/200px-SR-item-Chrysamere.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Chrysamere.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Chrysamere.jpg) Chrysamere [Chrysamere](Lore_Chrysamere.md) (also called the **Chrysamere Blade**, or **the Chrysamere**) is an ancient [adamantium](Lore_Adamantium.md) claymore [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) with offensive capabilities only surpassed by its defenses. It has [magical](Lore_Magic.md) properties which, unlike most weapons, are defensive in nature: it heals the wielder, grants them resistance to fire, and reflects hostile spells. It is unaligned, and is not a Daedric artifact. Chrysamere was forged in the late [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md) by the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) swordsmith [Asterie Bedel](Lore_Asterie_Bedel.md), who delighted in using the techniques of both [man](Lore_Men.md) and [mer](Lore_Mer.md) in her creations. In Chrysamere's case, this most clearly manifests in the placement of her maker's mark, a "mage's knot" within the heart of the sword that is said to harken back to the earliest days of Bretonic culture. Chrysamere is also said to have been influenced by the magic of Breton society's Elven forebears, and it gained further enchantments over time. Chrysamere does not typically stay with one swordsman for long, for it chooses not to favor one champion. It has earned the title of the **Sword of Heroes**, but it also goes by other names such as the **Sword of Kings** and the **Blade of Blades**. Chrysamere is considered a holy weapon, and in this context it is referred to as the **Sword of the [Paladin](Lore_Paladins.md)**, or simply the **Paladin's Blade**.

**Found in:** - [Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Chrysamere), [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Chrysamere), [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Chrysamere), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Chrysamere_(item).md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Chrysamere.md)</sup>, [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Chrysamere_Style), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Chrysamere)

## Clockwork Probabilis
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e8/ON-interior-Kemel-Ze.jpg/200px-ON-interior-Kemel-Ze.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Kemel-Ze.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-interior-Kemel-Ze.jpg) The Clockwork Probabilis within [Kemel-Ze](Lore_Kemel-Ze.md) The [Clockwork Probabilis](Lore_Clockwork_Probabilis.md) is a reality altering engine, created by the Clockwork God, [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), in his old laboratory within the ruins of [Kemel-Ze](Lore_Kemel-Ze.md), where he'd once studied with the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). A prototype that was created as a precursor to Sotha Sil's great work, the [Clockwork City's](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) Throne Aligned, the Probabilis allowed for willful manipulation of the forces that govern [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), both those that are constant such as gravity, and those that defy prediction and categorization.

Though it can simulate control over the formative rules of existence, being a prototype, the Clockwork Probabilis is unfinished. Thus use of the device outside the original parameters Sotha Sil designed it for can lead to the destabilization of Nirn, resulting in great loss of life that "far exceeds" the "acceptable thresholds" the Clockwork God had determined for his experiments.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Kemel-Ze)

## Cold Finger
**Cold Finger** is powerful magical staff said to have been created in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) by [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) himself. The staff was owned by [Dervenin](Lore_Dervenin.md) for a while, but due to a cruel joke his mad master played on him, the staff ended up with a [Giant Skeever](Lore_Skeever.md). Eventually, the Giant Skeever was defeated by warriors from [Odar's Kingdom](Lore_Hero.md#Dynasty_of_Odar_the_Brave), to which Dervenin turned for help, but the local ruler decided to keep the staff rather than return it to Dervenin.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup>

**Found in:** - [Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Cold_Finger)

## Crown of Barenziah
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Barenziah%27s Crown.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-misc-Barenziah%27s Crown.png) The Crown of Barenziah The [Crown of Barenziah](Lore_Crown_of_Barenziah.md) is the coronation crown of the eponymous Queen of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md). It is a golden crown with a winged-motif that has twenty-four ornate jewels, known as the **Stones of Barenziah**, across the wings and one on the temple of the head. It was given to the young queen during her ascension to the throne of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) in the early years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), amidst the reign of Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). However, sometime after the ceremony, the crown was stolen by an unknown assailant. He pried each of the stones off of the crown and dispersed them across [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where they became items of interest. For two eras, the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) of Skyrim had sought out all twenty-four stones for many years, while the crown was kept in Morrowind.

**Found in:** - [Skyrim](Skyrim_Crown_of_Barenziah.md)

## Crown of Bones
The [Crown of Bones](Lore_Crown_of_Bones.md) is a cursed artifact associated with [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and the Lost Fleet in [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md). The crown can curse items such as treasure, which will transform anyone who touches it into an [undead](Lore_Undead.md) [skeleton](Lore_Skeleton.md). The wearer of the Crown of Bones will then be able to control all the skeletons created by it, though they will be transformed as well.

The control the crown has over an individual varies, some may try and resist the user's commands or are considered too willful for the crown to work. Additionally, some forms of magic have been demonstrated to be able to break the crown's hold on its thralls.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Coldharbour_Quest_Items#The_Crown_of_Bones)

## Crown of Freydis

## Crown of Nenalata
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b0/OB-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg/200px-OB-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata.jpg) The Crown of Nenalata The [Ayleid Crown of Nenalata](Lore_Crown_of_Nenalata.md) is an ancient [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md). Its appearance varies, sometimes appearing as a lightweight [Moonstone](Lore_Moonstone.md) helm, sometimes as a simple crown. It can be distinguished from other Ayleid crowns by a royal glyph emblazoned upon it. Superficial enchantments grant the wearer spell reflection and increased skill at [Alteration](Lore_Alteration.md) and [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) magic, although the true potential of the crown is seemingly much greater. It is an immensely valuable treasure.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Ayleid_Crown_of_Nenalata), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Artifacts#Crown_of_Nenalata)

## Crusader's Relics
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg/200px-OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-item-Crusader_Relics.jpg) The Crusader's Relics The [Crusader's Relics](Lore_Crusader%27s_Relics.md) are a set of [Aedric](Lore_Aedra.md) [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md), created by the [Eight Divines](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) and given to [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), the Divine Crusader. The set includes a suit of armor, a shield, a mace and a longsword, which mold to the needs of the wielder and increase their proficiency in the School of [Restoration](Lore_Restoration.md). The artifacts were used by Whitestrake to banish Umaril the Unfeathered, an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) Sorcerer King. In the battle, Umaril had dealt him a fatal blow, and with his death the relics were scattered and lost for a time. The contemporary writings contained within *[The Song of Pelinal](Lore_The_Song_of_Pelinal.md)* mention that the Crusader's Relics came from the future.

**Found in:** - [Oblivion (Knights of the Nine)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Knights_of_the_Nine_Items), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Divine_Crusader_Items.md)<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Relics_of_the_Crusader.md)</sup>
