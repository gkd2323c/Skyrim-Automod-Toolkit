# The Tide

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/ON-concept-Ascending_Tide.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Ascending_Tide.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Ascending_Tide.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Ascending_Tide.jpg) Tides The Tide is the regular rise and fall of sea levels caused by the [moons](Lore_Moons.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOMAT-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Laqshim-2)</sup> The tide holds practical importance for sailors and pirates due to its influence on navigation and coastal activity,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Skyrim-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Nistel Deros-4)</sup> but it also carries deeper symbolic and spiritual meaning for many cultures and religions across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOMAT-1)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOFS-5)</sup> In some cultures, such as that of the [Tide-Born](Lore_Tide-Born.md) [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), the tide holds significance akin to that of a [deity](Lore_God.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOFS-5)</sup>

It also affects [fauna](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fauna). [Crayfish](Lore_Crayfish.md) are commonly found in the tide-pools within the [Nibenay](Lore_Nibenay.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TND-6)</sup> A mysterious and immortal entity called the [Augur of the Obscure](Lore_Augur_of_the_Obscure.md) claimed that the water brought in by the tide is filled with brain-billies, tiny [spirit](Lore_Spirit.md) - [jellies](Lore_Jellyfish.md) that sting and disrupt people's memories.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AOTO-7)</sup>

## Regional and Cultural Influences

### Apocrypha
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7e/ON-concept-Apocrypha_04.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Apocrypha_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Apocrypha_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Apocrypha_04.jpg) A coastal area in Apocrypha Some texts describe Apocrypha as a realm awash in black, ink-like tides.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TC-8)</sup> Similarly, many of Hermaeus Mora's titles reference tidal imagery, including names such as Tide-King,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-EOTSOA2-9)</sup> Spirit of the Tides,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TWS-10)</sup> and Master of the Tides of Fate.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DBHermaeus-11)</sup> Tides are frequently associated with the concept of [fate](Lore_Fate.md), particularly in relation to the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Hermaeus Mora. Certain places within his realm are described as experiencing constant shifts of fate, much like the changing tides of the Abyssal Sea, where threads of fate twist, flex, and unravel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Azandar-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Underweave-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Hushed-14)</sup>

### Black Marsh and Solstice
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5d/ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shell-Tide_Village_02.jpg) Shell-Tide Village, the home of the Tide-Born The Tide is revered by the Argonians of the Tide-Born tribe. They hold all flowing water in deep reverence, particularly rivers, believing that memories and past lives travel through these waters. Carried by the tides, these spiritual remnants reach the eggs resting in tidal pools, where they are returned and reborn along the shores of Solstice.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-VOFS-5)</sup> The Tide-Born believe that the River sifts through their memories, choosing which are taken into the Hist and which are cast aside. These discarded memories, they say, become the Tide-Born. As the River flows into the endless sea of the [Void](Lore_Void.md), the ever-moving Tide carries those memories to the shores of Solstice, where they find new life in the eggs resting in the tidal shallows. They believe that all Argonians are born with destined scales set before them, qualities that define who they are meant to become. As they journey through life toward these destined scales, some may stray, become lost, or die before ever reaching them. When this happens, the Tide carries them back, granting them another chance to walk the path. In time, they believe, each will finally earn their destined scales, shimmering with iridescence in the Void.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-WOTT-15)</sup>

They are known to associate dreams with the tides and regard them as deeply significant.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-DDJ-16)</sup> They believe that dreams that unfold while carried on the tide bear [portents](Lore_Sight.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AH-17)</sup> One of their sayings, what the tide giveth it also takes back, reflects their belief in the ever-changing nature of life.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TS-18)</sup> They are known to have a group called tide-readers, individuals who interpret signs and omens connected to the movements of the tide.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Mach-Lurz-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TRS-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-THFG-21)</sup> Some of their mages are also believed to share this connection, though in certain cases, their attunement to the tide can be suppressed or silenced by external forces.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Shei-Kur-22)</sup>

Tides also played a role in shaping Argonian culture on the mainland. Rising waters were not unusual, even along the rivers of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and the region's [rootworms](Lore_Rootworm.md) were known to partake in wintertide migration.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TAA-23)</sup>

Argonians have sayings tied to the rhythm of the tides, including the warning: "Pause but a moment, and you'll be drowned in the tide."<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Witseidutsei-24)</sup> Another idiomatic use was the phrase "Your eyes are like the tide", describing a gaze that constantly shifts without ever settling on one thing.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Teeba-Dimik-25)</sup>

### Elsweyr
The [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) text *[The Marriage of Moon and Tide](Lore_The_Marriage_of_Moon_and_Tide.md)* by [Clan Mother Tsradama](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Clan_Mother_Tsradama) explores the relationship between the movements of the moons [Jode](Lore_Jode.md) and [Jone](Lore_Jone.md) and the shifting tides, drawing a connection to [Nirni](Lore_Nirni.md), who is said to sway to the moons [music](Lore_Music.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TMOMAT-1)</sup> Some Khajiits likened moonlight to strands of [sapphires](Lore_Sapphire.md) drifting upon the tide.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-STS-26)</sup>

### Hammerfell
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/DF-place-Coven_of_the_Tide.png/200px-DF-place-Coven_of_the_Tide.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Coven_of_the_Tide.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-place-Coven_of_the_Tide.png) The Coven of the Tide The Tide [Witches](Lore_Witches.md) of the Coven of the Tide were active in [Myrkwasa](Lore_Myrkwasa_(fiefdom).md) circa [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405).<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-DWC-27)</sup>

### High Rock and the Systres
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/ON-place-Y%27ffre%27s_Path.jpg/200px-ON-place-Y%27ffre%27s_Path.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Y%27ffre%27s_Path.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Y%27ffre%27s_Path.jpg) Y'ffre's Path, a holy site of the Eldertide Circle [Eldertide Circle](Lore_Eldertide_Circle.md) was one of the most prominent [druidic](Lore_Druid.md) groups active in the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-EC-28)</sup> According to the founder of the Y'ffre's Path, Archdruid Alix the Systres belonged to druids and the tide of ther wrath was ever flowing.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-EBI-29)</sup> Some druids were known to swear by the tide, even those not affiliated with the Eldertide Circle, such as some members of the [Stonelore Circle](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md).<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-DG-30)</sup>

The Tide was a religious faction linked to [Vigryl](Lore_Vigryl.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-DDP-31)</sup>

### Pyandonea
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/ON-creature-Tideborn_Taleria.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Tideborn_Taleria.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tideborn_Taleria.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Tideborn_Taleria.jpg) Tideborn Taleria The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md) were known for using vivid tidal imagery in their exclamations and curses, with phrases like "My blood is the tides!" and "Let the tide take you" reflecting their connection to the sea.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-CN-32)</sup> One of the leaders of the Dreadsails, [Taleria](Lore_Taleria.md), who adopted the appellation of the Tideborn after using powerful magic to transform into a formidable aquatic creature.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TT-33)</sup>

### Summerset
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/22/ON-place-Coral_Aerie_%28Tide_Hollow%29.jpg/200px-ON-place-Coral_Aerie_%28Tide_Hollow%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Coral_Aerie_(Tide_Hollow).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Coral_Aerie_(Tide_Hollow).jpg) Tide Hollow A place within the caverns of the [Coral Aerie](Lore_Coral_Aerie.md), a small island of the [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) archipelago was known as the Tide Hollow.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-CA-34)</sup> [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) poetry dedicated to their god [Syrabane](Lore_Syrabane.md) occasionally employs tidal imagery.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TTOS-35)</sup>
