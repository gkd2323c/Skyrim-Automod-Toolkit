[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/28/ON-furnishing-Box_of_Fruit.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Box_of_Fruit.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Box_of_Fruit.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Box_of_Fruit.jpg) A display of fruits **Produce** refers to the products of plants that are typically edible. **Fruits** are plant products, and can usually have tastes ranging from sweet to sour. Common types of fruits include berries, citrus, drupes, and pomes. **Vegetables** are parts of plants that typically taste savory or bitter. **Edible fungi** are the types of fungi that are typically non-poisonous with desirable taste for culinary usage. **Grains** are the hard dry parts of grass-like plants, varying from cereals to legumes. **Nuts** and **seeds** are plant products that typically have hard dry shells covering an edible kernel. **Dairy products**, while falling under animal products,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-GAYQ-1)</sup> can also be considered produce. **Sugars** are edible sweet substances that can be refined from plants. This page primarily uses the culinary definitions for the categories.

### Cheese
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1e/ON-place-Cheese_Shelf.png/200px-ON-place-Cheese_Shelf.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cheese_Shelf.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Cheese_Shelf.png) A variety of cheeses [Cheese](Lore_Cheese.md) ([Yoku](Lore_Yoku.md): *Mlou*) is a dairy product used as [food](Lore_Cuisine.md). It is made when milk ferments and is pressed into a more solid product. The milk used to create cheese can come from various mammalian animals, such as [cows](Lore_Cow.md), [echatere](Lore_Echatere.md), [goats](Lore_Goat.md), [mammoths](Lore_Mammoth.md), [sheep](Lore_Sheep.md), [giant bats](Lore_Giant_Bat.md), and even rodents. It may be consumed as part of simple meals with soup, bread, and tea, or with wine in more refined meals. Cheese has [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) properties, and can be used in potions to fortify willpower, resist paralysis and fire, restore fatigue, or in poisons to damage luck and agility.

It can come in many shapes, such as truckles called cheese wheels, or liquid forms called cheese dips, and can be stored in barrels and crates. Giant [rats](Lore_Rat.md) consume cheese and may infest areas containing it, sometimes being lured by its strong scent. Rats can be subject to experiments where they explore mazes to find a piece of cheese. Cheese wheels laced with poison are sometimes used to lure and kill [skeevers](Lore_Skeever.md).

- *For alchemical information, see the [alchemy entry](Lore_Alchemy_C.md#Cheese_Wedge)*.

**Found in:** - [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Taverns), [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Ingredients#Cheese_Wedge), [Skyrim](Skyrim_Food.md#Other_Food), [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Cheese), [Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Cheese), [Betrayal of the Second Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Betrayal:Cheeses_of_Tamriel)
