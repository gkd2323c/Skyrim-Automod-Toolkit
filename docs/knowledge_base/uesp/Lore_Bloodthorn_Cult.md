# Bloodthorn Cult

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_%28person%29.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_%28person%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_(person).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Angof_the_Gravesinger_(person).jpg) Angof, leader of the Bloodthorn Cult The Bloodthorn Cult was a [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) cult devoted to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and led by a [Reachman](Lore_Reachman.md) named [Angof](Lore_Angof.md) the Gravesinger. It was established during the attempted [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md) in [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), at the time an independent kingdom ruled by Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md) of [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md), although it welcomed initiates from other backgrounds as well.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Harald-1)</sup> In fact, a significant portion of the cult was composed of [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) ruffians. Conversely, the Cult was seen with distrust among many people of the Reachfolk origins,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Giraud_Scraeg-2)</sup> and in fact was not only not supported, but also deemed problem by the court of Ard Caddach.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CBLB-3)</sup> The cult was known for harnessing dark nature magic through the cultivation of [Bloodthorn vines](Lore_Flora_B.md#Bloodthorn_Vines), and used them to corrupt the land<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ZGK-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SOR-5)</sup> and raise [zombies](Lore_Zombie.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ANGOF-6)</sup>

Bloodthorn cultists were believed to wear yokes in form of chains of braided, thorn-covered vines that bite into their flesh.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Contraband B1-7)</sup> Some members of the cult were known to carry stoppered vials filled with presumably dangerous wrapped in thorny vines adorned with sigils of their organization.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Contraband B2-8)</sup>

Angof's main goal was to conquer and corrupt the region of [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-SOG-9)</sup> He headquartered the cult at the ancient cemetery of [Cath Bedraud](Lore_Cath_Bedraud.md), seemingly establishing his presence there as early as [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGT-10)</sup> However, the cult's invasion didn't begin until the onset of the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). The cultists first took control of the many burial sites in the [King's Guard](Lore_King%27s_Guard.md) region, raising the dead as minions. Angof himself traveled to the ancient battlefield at [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), resurrecting the long-dead [Alessian](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) general [Faolchu](Lore_Faolchu.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Lo To C-11)</sup> Faolchu was a powerful [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md) in life, and was forced to serve Angof by spreading [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) throughout Glenumbra, ultimately overrunning the city of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) and infecting the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobility.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Rally Cry-12)</sup>

News of the cult's activities were slow to spread, and its corrupting influence extended as far south as the region's capital of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md). The cultists allied themselves with the Red Rook Bandits and began an attempt to corrupt the [Wyrd Tree](Lore_Wyrd_Tree.md), the source of all life in Glenumbra. To do this, they created [lurchers](Lore_Lurcher.md) and trapped the Guardians of the [Beldama Wyrd](Lore_Beldama_Wyrd.md), ancient [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) worshipped by the local witches.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Glenumbra-13)</sup>

An associate of Angof called [Vardan](Lore_Vardan.md) looked beyond Glenumbra and secretly established the cult on the small island of [Betnikh](Lore_Betnikh.md). He discovered a powerful [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) relic on the island in Carzog's Demise, and disturbed the Ayleid, Breton and [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) spirits of the island in order to obtain it. With the relic, Vardan planned to wipe out the local [Seamount Clan](Lore_Seamount_Clan.md) and gain control of Betnikh before betraying Angof and claiming Glenumbra for himself. However, the Seamount Orcs learned of the cult's activities and slew the invaders with the help of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ITH-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-CD-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-VD-16)</sup>

Reeling from the cult's threat, the Seamount Clan sent a petition to Daggerfall to join the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). King Casimir initially disregarded the threat of the Bloodthorn Cult, until an attempt was made on his life. The [Lion Guard](Lore_Lion_Guard.md) were duly ordered to march north and put an end to the Bloodthorn menace. The Beldama Wyrd succeeded in freeing the Guardians and restoring the Wyrd Tree, and the Lion Guard were similarly successful in protecting [Aldcroft](Lore_Aldcroft.md) from werewolves, retaking Camlorn from Faolchu, and breaking the cult's alliance with the Red Rooks. However, the settlement of [Eagle's Brook](Lore_Eagle%27s_Brook.md) fell to the cult, and its entire populace were raised as zombies. The briefly-lived plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) known as [Lord Velian's Plane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lord_Velian%27s_Plane) was created during this event by another cultist-turned-renegade. The Lion Guard would eventually lead a final attack on Cath Bedraud with the help of the Vestige. Angof was defeated in combat and the threat to Glenumbra was ended, allowing the region's dead to return to their rest.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ATGQ-17)</sup>

In death, members of the Bloodthorn Cult went to [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), their [souls](Lore_Souls.md) claimed by Molag Bal. As punishment for their failure, Bal sent them to the Cliffs of Failure to partake in unending battles led by Angof. Angof became repentant in death, seeking to redeem himself and cure his disfigured body. Under the watchful eye of a [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) known as the Observer, the cultists were forced to face off against the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md) led by Thallik Wormfather and the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md) led by High Kinlady [Estre](Lore_Estre.md). Later that year, a [Tamrielic](Lore_Tamriel.md) invasion force entered Coldharbour to put an end to the Planemeld, and several members of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) ended up trapped on the Cliffs of Failure. The Vestige entered the games to rescue them, although the Overseer gave the option to save Angof, Thallik or Estre instead. Angof promised to aid the invaders in a final assault on the Endless Stair, although it is unknown who was ultimately saved.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TEW-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-ATIYS-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TFA-20)</sup>

Lady [Nilene Devierin](Lore_Nilene_Devierin.md), High King [Emeric](Lore_Emeric.md)'s Royal Envoy to the Reach, was tasked with addressing the invasion of Breton borders by the Bloodthorn Cult and Dark Witnesses. Seeking a peace agreement, she met with Lady Belain, advisor to the Ard. Devierin expressed frustration over the raids by Reachfolk and implored Belain to persuade Ard Caddach to control the unruly clans. However, Caddach, constrained by the need to avoid projecting weakness or servitude to Emeric and hindered by the nature of fractured clan society and high autonomy of independent clans, couldn't rein in the factions. Nilene was instructed that Bretons should weaken the factions themselves to increase Caddach's authority in the region. By assisting Ard Caddach, High King Emeric could streamline negotiations with a unified Reach under one king instead of dealing with numerous stubborn clans. Supporting Caddach was presented as action aligning with High Rock's best interests. Upon meeting with Caddach, her concerns were dismissed, and she left without a satisfactory response.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CBLB-3)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ROTDOM-21)</sup>
