# Food


Food is cheaper and more widely available than potions, and creating cooked food does not involve the [Alchemy](Skyrim_Alchemy.md) skill. However, even cooked food typically provides weaker effects than potions do. Nonetheless, a small number of dishes provide quite long-lasting effects, while [Beef Stew](#Beef_Stew) lasts longer than all stamina potions and [Elsweyr Fondue](#Elsweyr_Fondue) is actually more powerful than a number of magicka potions.

Alcoholic beverages like ale, mead, and wine typically provide an immediate boost to stamina but reduced stamina regeneration for a period of time.

Stamina regeneration food such as vegetable soup is rather useful for melee based characters; the stamina regeneration each second allows you to use power attacks continuously until the effect wears off.

Most food items can be sold to any innkeeper or food vendor, as well as to general goods merchants (exceptions are noted in the following tables). A limited number of food items are categorized as raw food, and these items can also be sold to apothecary merchants. In addition, innkeepers and food vendors will have a selection of food items available for purchase.

With the addition of [Survival Mode](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md) food is also used to replenish [Hunger](Skyrim_Hunger.md). Different foods have different weights of replenishment lowering the Hunger meter by set amounts. Very small replenishes 2, small replenishes 18, medium replenishes 220 and large replenishes 380.

Foods added by [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md), [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md), [Hearthfire](Skyrim_Hearthfire.md), and [Creation Club](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) are annotated with "DG", "DB", "HF", and "CC" superscripts, respectively.

## Raw Food
This section includes all food items that can be used when [cooking](Skyrim_Cooking.md). Several of these items are also classified by merchants as being raw food; they can be sold to apothecary merchants and innkeepers.

### Raw Meat
Raw meat can generally be obtained from the bodies of dead [animals](Skyrim_Animals.md). In the case of small game (e.g., rabbits and pheasants), already-caught animals can also be found hanging in homes and inns, and can be harvested to obtain the meat. Similarly, salmon can also be found hanging from racks near rivers. Certain barrels are internally identified as meat barrels and will always contain raw meat, as well as [salt piles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile).

Three stall merchants, [Hogni Red-Arm](Skyrim_Hogni_Red-Arm.md) in [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), [Anoriath](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Anoriath) in [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md) and [Marise Aravel](Skyrim_Marise_Aravel.md) in [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md), specialize in raw meat. In addition, some varieties of meat can be bought from [hunters](Skyrim_Hunter.md) encountered in the wilderness.

Nearly all raw meat can be cooked by simply combining it with salt. A few meats (beef, venison) can also be made into more elaborate stews by combining them with vegetables as well as salt.

### Hot Soups<sup>[CC](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md)</sup>
These soup recipes are made at the standard cooking locations but are only available to be crafted if Survival Mode is enabled as part of the [Survival Mode](Skyrim_Survival_Mode.md) [Creation](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md). Their primary use is to counteract the [cold](Skyrim_Cold.md), but they also act as [hunger](Skyrim_Hunger.md) reducers.

There are no guaranteed locations for any of the hot soups.

## Beverages
Consuming alcoholic beverages like meads, wines and brandies typically provide an immediate boost to stamina, along with reduced stamina regeneration, for a period of time.

Several other alcoholic beverages are classified as [potions](Skyrim_Potions.md) instead of food, meaning they are displayed in your inventory under potions:

- [Argonian Ale](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Argonian_Ale)
- [Balmora Blue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Balmora_Blue) wine
- [Cliff Racer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Cliff_Racer) wine
- [Cyrodilic Brandy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Cyrodilic_Brandy)
- [Skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skooma), including [Double-Distilled Skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Double-Distilled_Skooma) and [Kordir's Skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Kordir%27s_Skooma)
- [Stros M'Kai Rum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Stros_M%27Kai_Rum)
- [Velvet Le Chance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Velvet_Le Chance) wine
- [White-Gold Tower](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:White-Gold_Tower) wine

Many of these other beverages do not have the negative reduced stamina regeneration side effect.

## Notes
- Version 2.0.9 of the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) makes it possible to sell a good majority of the original player-cooked foods to food and drink vendors, as well as Firebrand Wine and Colovian Brandy. - Version 2.0.9 of the [Unofficial Dragonborn Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Dragonborn_Patch) makes it possible to sell Horker and Ash Yam Stew to food and drink vendors, as well as some of the food exclusively found on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md), such as Boar Meat.
- Version 1.0.1 of the [Unofficial Hearthfire Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Hearthfire_Patch) makes it possible to sell Potato Soup and Clam Chowder to food and drink vendors.

## Bugs
- Vegetable Soup and Venison Stew may not restore health properly. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.5.73, fixes this bug.
- Seared Slaughterfish and Charred Skeever Meat may restore less Hunger than intended. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6, fixes this bug.
- Some food and drink items may not make the proper sounds when picked up, dropped, and/or consumed. - The [Official Skyrim Special Edition Patch](Skyrim_Special_Edition_Patch.md), version 1.6, fixes this bug.
- Flin, Mazte, Shein, Sujamma, Sadri's Sujamma, and Emberbrand Wine all lack proper pickup and drop sounds. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch), version 3.0.8, fixes this bug.
- Boar Meat, Ash Hopper Meat, Ash Hopper Leg, and Cooked Boar Meat all display 0 value due to not having set the no-autocalc flag on them. - ![PC Only](https://images.uesp.net/d/d7/Computer.svg)
The [Unofficial Skyrim Legendary Edition Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Legendary_Edition_Patch), version 3.0.8, fixes this bug.
