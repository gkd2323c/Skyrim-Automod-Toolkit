**Containers** are interactive objects that can contain items, or be used to store items in [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md). There are a variety of different containers in the game, most of which initially hold loot appropriate to the container's style and location. Some containers may contain valuable treasure or gold, while others may only contain food or non-valuable clutter. Any container that contains nothing has "(empty)" after its name, letting you know that you do not need to bother searching it.

When you activate a container, you are presented with an [inventory list](#Inventory_List) displaying the container's current contents. You can move individual items from the container to your personal inventory, or you can "Select All" to take everything in the container. Alternatively, you can scroll down to your personal inventory and move any items from there into the container if you wish to store items and/or get rid of extra items.

However, most containers in the game are *not* safe for long-term storage, as stated in a loading screen:

*Items can be stored safely in any containers in your own home. But items kept in any other containers may not be there later when you go to retrieve them...* Most containers will [respawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Respawn) if you leave the area for multiple days (in most cases, ten days, but the exact number depends upon where the container is located). Any contents left in a container when it respawns will disappear and be permanently lost. The [Safe Containers](#Safe_Containers) section of this article provides details on which containers can safely be used for long-term storage, without risk of losing your stored items.

## Respawning Containers
Note that these types of containers customarily respawn, however there may be exceptions to the rule.

### Apothecary's Satchel
Apothecary's satchels are small leather containers, often found near [Alchemy Labs](Skyrim_Alchemy_Lab.md), that contain alchemy [Ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md) and/or [Potions](Skyrim_Potions.md). There are actually three different types of satchel (all identically named), each with different quality and number of ingredients:

- Common: contains one common ingredient, and a 25% chance of containing one restoration potion (potion is random and leveled, with equal chance of being restore [Health](Skyrim_Restore_Health.md), [Magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Restore_Magicka), or [Stamina](Skyrim_Restore_Stamina.md))
- Uncommon: contains one common ingredient, one uncommon ingredient, and a 25% chance of containing one restoration potion
- Rare: contains one common ingredient, one uncommon ingredient, one rare ingredient, and a 25% chance of containing one restoration potion

### Barrel
Barrels are one of the most common types of containers, found in homes, shops, towns, and many dungeons. In addition to barrels that act as containers, there are also static barrels that are purely decorative; the latter type of barrel is slightly darker in color and cannot be activated. There are several different types of standard barrels, which contain different items (although they are all identically named).

- Food: 70% chance of containing 1-5 identical [fruit or vegetables](Skyrim_Food.md#Fruit.2FVegetables), or 1-4 [salt piles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile). This is the most common type of barrel.
- Meat: 1 piece of [raw meat](Skyrim_Food.md#Raw_Meat) and 1-3 salt piles.
- Ingredients (common): 1-5 identical [Ingredients](Skyrim_Ingredients.md), randomly selected from: [Garlic](Skyrim_Garlic.md), [Honeycomb](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Honeycomb), [Juniper Berries](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Juniper_Berries), [Lavender](Skyrim_Lavender.md), [Snowberries](Skyrim_Snowberries.md), or [Wheat](Skyrim_Wheat.md).
- Ingredients (uncommon): 1-5 identical ingredients, randomly selected from: [Bleeding Crown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bleeding_Crown), [Canis Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Canis_Root), [Dragon's Tongue](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Dragon%27s_Tongue), [Fly Amanita](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Fly_Amanita), [Frost Mirriam](Skyrim_Frost_Mirriam.md), [Mora Tapinella](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mora_Tapinella), [Spider Egg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Spider_Egg), or [White Cap](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:White_Cap) (note that Dragon's Tongue is included in this list even though it is otherwise categorized as a common, not an uncommon, ingredient)

**Variants:** These look identical to standard barrels, but have different names, allowing their contents to be more easily identified.

- Black-Briar Mead Barrel: Contains 1-4 [Black-Briar Mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Black-Briar_Mead), 10% chance of 1 [Black-Briar Reserve](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Black-Briar_Reserve). Found in the [Black-Briar Meadery](Skyrim_Black-Briar_Meadery.md) and [Black-Briar Manor](Skyrim_Black-Briar_Manor.md).
- Fish Barrel: Contains 2-5 [Salmon](Skyrim_Salmon_(dead).md), 0-2 [Clam Meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Clam_Meat), 1-3 [Salt Pile](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile), and 25% chance of each of the five fish ingredients ([Abecean Longfin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Abecean_Longfin), [Cyrodilic Spadetail](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Cyrodilic_Spadetail), [Histcarp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Histcarp), [River Betty](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:River_Betty), [Silverside Perch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Silverside_Perch)). Mostly found on the docks at [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md). If you have the [Fishing](Skyrim_Fishing.md) creation, [Carp](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Carp) or [Brook Bass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Brook_Bass) each have a 25% chance of appearing.
- Honningbrew Mead Barrel: Contains 1-4 [Honningbrew Mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Honningbrew_Mead). Found in the [Honningbrew Meadery](Skyrim_Honningbrew_Meadery.md).
- Mead Barrel: Contains 1 [Nord Mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Nord_Mead)
- [Thief Cache](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Thief_Cache): Contains significant loot. Found in cities after completing the [Thieves Guild reputation quests](Skyrim_Thieves_Guild_(faction).md#Reputation_quests).

### Chest
Chests are the standard container for treasure and other types of valuable loot. There are actually more than two hundred different types of containers that are identified as "Chest". The majority of these are visually identical, but have different contents. Different types of chests are located in each different type of dungeon (e.g., Draugr chests, Forsworn chests, and Vampire chests each have slightly different contents). More than half of the different types of chests are actually [Merchant Chests](#Merchant_Chests) that are hidden and not intended for player use.

All varieties of "boss" chests (especially the "dungeon" variety) have an increased chance for having loot far above your level. Examples include: Ebony equipment (which is normally only found around level 30+) at level 20, or Expert-level spells (found around level 35) found at level 25. Although rare, it is also possible to find Daedric armor and weapons and Dragonbone and Dragonscale armor from these chests at levels 10 and below.

Chests may be locked or [trapped](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Tension_Cable).

### Knapsack
Knapsacks are less common and are usually found near [adventurers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Adventurer) and [hunters](Skyrim_Hunter.md) (dead or alive), near skeletal remains, as well as in [bandit](Skyrim_Bandit.md) campsites. There are two types of knapsacks in the game, and the [Dawnguard add-on](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) adds in a third.

- First type: Unique and is only found in [Helgen Keep](Skyrim_Helgen_Keep.md). It always contains 4 [lockpicks](Skyrim_Lockpick.md) and 1 [Potion of Minor Healing](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Healing).
- Second type: All other knapsacks in the game. Each of them contains: - 1 set of farm [clothes](Skyrim_Clothes.md) with a pair of [boots](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Boots).
- 1 random [book](Skyrim_Books.md).
- 1 piece of random leveled [jewelry](Skyrim_Jewelry.md).
- 1 random leveled Potion of Restore Health.
- 75% chance of 1 [fruit or vegetable](Skyrim_Food.md#Fruit.2FVegetables).
- 25% chance of 1-9 gold.
- 2-10 additional gold with the [Golden Touch](Skyrim_Golden_Touch_(perk).md) perk.
- Third type<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>: They contain the same type of loot as listed above with slight differences: - 90% chance (instead of 25%) of 1-9 gold.
- An additional 2 potions of any of these 5 variants—Potion of [Cure Disease](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Cure_Disease), of [Cure Poison](Skyrim_Potions.md#Cure_Poison), of Restore Health, of [Restore Magicka](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Magicka), or of [Restore Stamina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Potion_of_Minor_Stamina).

### Large Sack
*Large Sacks* are common containers used in homes, shops, and dungeons to store food.

Contents:

- Type 1: 70% chance of either 1-5 identical [fruits or vegetables](Skyrim_Food.md#Fruit.2FVegetables) or 1-4 [salt piles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Salt_Pile).
- Type 2<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>: 0-9 [soul husks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Soul_Husk)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>. Only found in the [Soul Cairn](Skyrim_Soul_Cairn.md)<sup>[DG](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md)</sup>.

While most *Large Sacks* in the game are actually non-respawning, visually distinguishing them from those that respawn is impractical (the models are nearly identical).

### Pod<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>

- 20% chance of one random leveled restoration potion

- Guaranteed 2-10 additional gold if you have the [Imperial Luck](Skyrim_Imperial_Luck.md) ability or [Golden Touch](Skyrim_Golden_Touch_(perk).md) perk

## Corpse Containers
There are many corpses in the game which are considered to be containers in game data. Unless otherwise noted, all of the containers listed here *do* respawn with the cell in which they're located. Dead NPCs are not found here and are listed at their [category](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Dead_NPCs) instead.

### Skeleton
Various inanimate Skeletons are found across Skyrim and Solstheim, usually the long-dead victims of bandits, animals, Ash Spawn, or natural disasters. They will usually have a small amount of Gold, with a chance of Bone Meal. Only some intact skeletons can be looted, and half-buried or disarticulated ones cannot be interacted with. A few skeletons of deceased NPCs will carry the journals or other special inventory items they had in life, such as [Drokt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Drokt) or [Gratian Caerellius](Skyrim_Gratian_Caerellius.md).

## Safe Containers
Containers may be safe for one of two reasons. Some container types are set to be non-respawning—containers of that type will never respawn, no matter where the container is found. Other containers are safe because of their location—some locations, in particular the player's [houses](Skyrim_Houses.md), are identified as non-respawning locations. All containers in these safe locations (even common Barrels and Sacks) can safely be used for storage.

### Child's Chest
Child's Chest is a part of child's bedroom. It will be empty if you haven't adopted any children. Your children may collect various things and store them in their chest. Their older weapons, clothes and dolls will be kept in it too, if you give them a new one. Your children will restock it every few days. However, the original contents in it won't disappear.

### Coffin
Coffins are found in Halls of the Dead and Vampire Dungeons. Most coffins are always empty. However, one variety of coffin has a 75% chance of containing one of the following:

- 2 gold, [Bone Meal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bone_Meal), [Bloody Rags](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Bloody_Rags), [Skull](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Skull), [Ragged Robes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Ragged_Robes), or a 1.5% chance of containing a random leveled [gem](Skyrim_Gems.md), unenchanted [Jewelry](Skyrim_Jewelry.md), or [Amulet of the Divine](Skyrim_Specialty_Gear.md#Amulets_of_the_Divines).

Whenever a citizen of a hold dies, the items that they were carrying, such as keys, clothes, and weapons, will appear in a coffin in the Hall of the Dead of the hold capital. These items will then be free to take without consequence.

## Safe Locations
Safe locations are places that never [respawn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Respawn). Therefore, every container in such a place (even a common barrel or sack) is safe for storage. The primary example of a safe location (a place that never respawns) is a player-owned [house](Skyrim_Houses.md), but there are a large number of other non-respawning places. Only the interior areas of these non-respawning locations are safe (i.e., only regions that are entered through a door that triggers a loading screen, and have their own map, separate from the exterior map.) The individual place pages will note any rare exceptions where an exterior container is also safe.

In a few instances, there may be quest-related changes to the location (as noted on the individual place page), but otherwise the game does not modify the contents of a safe location.

All safe locations are listed in the [Safe Locations](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Category:Skyrim-Places-Safe) category.

## Merchant Chests
All [merchants](Skyrim_Merchants.md) in the game (with the exception of [Hunters](Skyrim_Hunter.md), [Peddlers](Skyrim_Peddler.md), and [skooma dealers](Skyrim_Skooma_Dealer_(NPC).md)) have a merchant chest containing the merchant's gold and the goods they have available for sale. The chest does not contain the merchant's entire inventory—merchants will also sell some items sitting out in the store and unequipped items in their personal inventory—and also is not the only source of merchant gold—any personal gold will be added to the merchant's available gold. Nevertheless, the merchant chest contains the standard list of items sold by the merchant. If multiple merchants work in the same store, they share the same merchant chest. A merchant must be in the same area as the chest in order for its contents to be made available for sale. It [respawns](Skyrim_Dungeons.md#Respawning) every two days, independent of whether the rest of the cell contents respawn. Merchant chests also level with you, so the higher your level, the greater the quality of the contents. 3 merchant chests belong to the [Khajiit Caravans](Skyrim_Khajiit_Traders.md) (1 merchant chest per Khajiit caravan). Several merchant chests require you to clip through walls to get to them.

The merchant chest is a standard container, but it is intentionally hidden to prevent players from accessing the chest. On the PC, using the `tcl` [Console](Skyrim_Console.md) command generally allows the merchant chest to be found, most commonly under the floor of the merchant's store. The existence of this chest is not a bug, an easter egg, or in any way noteworthy—it is a standard part of game mechanics that is not visible as part of standard game play.

### Locations
Several Merchant Chests can be found, most notably these:

- [Whiterun](Skyrim_Whiterun.md), underneath the [Skyforge](Skyrim_Skyforge.md).
- [Dawnstar](Skyrim_Dawnstar.md), outside [Iron-Breaker Mine](Skyrim_Iron-Breaker_Mine.md), near a rock formation.
- Outside [Markarth](Skyrim_Markarth.md), near a stone wall and a Juniper tree.
- Outside [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), near a plant formation. You may need to crouch to access it.
- [Riften](Skyrim_Riften.md), inside the underwater areas of [The Ragged Flagon](Skyrim_The_Ragged_Flagon.md). There are 6 in total.
- Riften, inside [Elgrim's Elixirs](Skyrim_Elgrim%27s_Elixirs.md). You can also find a [Do Not Detete](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Do_Not_Delete) chest here.
- [Windhelm](Skyrim_Windhelm.md), inside the marketplace. You'll need a [follower](Skyrim_Followers.md) to access them all.
- Solitude, inside the [Blue Palace](Skyrim_Blue_Palace.md).
- [College of Winterhold](Skyrim_College_of_Winterhold_(place).md), in the [Hall of Countenance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hall_of_Countenance).

Be warned, however, that the [Unofficial Skyrim Patch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Unofficial_Skyrim_Patch) will make these chests unreachable.

## Inventory List
A container's inventory list is sorted in this order:

- All armors and weapons, sorted by their damage or armor.
- All food, potions, ingredients, books, notes, and misc items, sorted by name.
- All heavy boots, sorted by armor value.
- All rings, necklaces, amulets, hats, hoods, cowls, caps, circlets, and ebony boots, sorted by value.
- All unhooded clothing, sorted by value.
- All hooded clothing, sorted by value.
- Some gauntlets, boots, and bracers, sorted by armor.
- Imperial bracers, steel cuffed boots, and all boots of light armor, sorted by armor.
- Iron gauntlets and boots, scaled gauntlets and boots, sorted by armor.
- All remaining light gauntlets and bracers, sorted by armor.
- All non-armor shoes, boots and footwraps, sorted by value.

## Other Containers
- **Book Shelves** in the player's houses can be activated to access a limited capacity [book](Skyrim_Books.md) inventory, and automatically place books on the shelves. Other types of items may be moved onto the shelves manually. Bookshelves are also known to be used with the reusable [Oghma Infinium](Skyrim_Oghma_Infinium.md) glitch.
- **Display Cases** have a glass top that will open and close through activation, and a weapon may be placed in them while they are open by activating them.
- **Mannequins** can be activated to store and display [armor](Skyrim_Armor.md), and [clothing](Skyrim_Clothing.md). They have a weight limit (around 305). Their Base ID is `89a85`.
- **NPCs** can be used as containers either via [pickpocketing](Skyrim_Pickpocket.md) or trading with [followers](Skyrim_Followers.md), but have a weight limit as to how much they can carry.
- **Shield / Weapon Plaques** are decorative wall mounted containers that will remove and display equipped items when activated.
- **Static Containers** such as baskets, boxes, etc may contain loot, or be used to display items, but the latter requires manual placement of items.
- **Weapon Racks** can be activated to automatically remove equipped weapons from the player for storage and display.

## Portable Containers
As many corpses can be picked up and carried around, they can effectively become portable containers, allowing you to potentially carry an unlimited number of items around and remain [unencumbered](Skyrim_Carry_Weight.md). The speed at which you can carry the corpse appears to be related to its size, so small corpses like [hawks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hawk) work well in this capacity. It is worth noting, however, these portable containers should not be considered safe, as their contents may be deleted if left for a long period of time. A "Pot" is a portable Safe Container. Followers work as a portable container as you can place all non-useful items into a chest or container and ask them to take everything in it--using this method your follower has no weight limit and isn't slowed down. (Warning: using this method may cause all such items to become flagged as 'Stolen' when you try to reclaim them.)
