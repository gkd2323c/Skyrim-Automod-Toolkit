# Word Walls

— [Arngeir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arngeir)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arngeir-1)</sup> Word Walls, known in the [Dragon Language](Lore_Dragon_Language.md) as **Qethsegol**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DLMNM-2)</sup> were ancient [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) [monuments](Lore_Statues.md) inscribed with [draconic runes](Lore_Dragon_Alphabet.md), typically erected at significant landmarks to commemorate notable events or individuals, or to convey philosophical maxims.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LWW-4)</sup> Some served as [memorials to the dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Death_Nord), while others may have honored the Nords' ancient [Dragon](Lore_Dragon.md) overlords from the times of the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-CTA-5)</sup>

The walls served as sources of power that could be absorbed by [Dragonborns](Lore_Dragonborn.md) to hasten their learning of the Voice,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-LFAF-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Shout-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-PRIMA-8)</sup><sup>:47</sup> as the [thu'um](Lore_Thu%27um.md) resonated within the carved words. This resonance also allowed powerful [Tongues](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tongues), such as [Greybeard](Lore_Greybeards.md) [Arngeir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arngeir), to sense and divine their locations from great distances by perceiving the echo of a Word of Power.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Arngeir-1)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWOP-9)</sup>

While most Word Walls were located within the mainland of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the isle of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), some could also be found in other realms, such as [Sovngarde](Lore_Sovngarde.md), [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) or the [Soul Cairn](Lore_Soul_Cairn.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

Many Word Walls and their secrets were jealously guarded by [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Morbius Twitter-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-DP-11)</sup> The walls and the words inscribed upon them were also subjects of study for scholars and historians, such as Tharstan,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Tharstan-12)</sup> and Hela Thrice-Versed, the author of *Dragon Language: Myth No More*, known for her work on translating the ancient tongue.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DLMNM-2)</sup> In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), during [Alduin's](Lore_Alduin.md) return, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) visited many Word Walls to gain the knowledge and power necessary to defeat the draconic god.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Shout-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWOP-9)</sup>

## Known Word Walls
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/SR-place-Broken_Word_Wall.jpg/200px-SR-place-Broken_Word_Wall.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Broken_Word_Wall.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Broken_Word_Wall.jpg) The broken word wall at the Throat of the World [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/SR-place-Sovngarde_02.jpg/200px-SR-place-Sovngarde_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sovngarde_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-place-Sovngarde_02.jpg) A ruined word wall in Sovngarde *Spread throughout Skyrim, in ancient dungeons, burial grounds, and other secluded places, there are walls. Black, ominous walls on which is written a script so old, so unknown, none who had encountered it could even begin its translation. *
— *[Dragon Language: Myth no More](Lore_Dragon_Language__Myth_no_More.md)* by Hela Thrice-Versed<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DLMNM-2)</sup> Word Walls were found in numerous locations across Skyrim and beyond.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

They could be found in ancient Nordic tombs and ruins, including [Bleak Falls Barrow](Lore_Bleak_Falls_Barrow.md), [Dead Men's Respite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dead_Men%27s_Respite), [Volskygge](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volskygge), [Skuldafn](Lore_Skuldafn.md), [Forelhost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forelhost), Hag's End, [High Gate Ruins](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:High_Gate_Ruins), [Saarthal](Lore_Saarthal.md), [Korvanjund](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Korvanjund), [Ragnvald](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ragnvald), Rannveig's Fast, [Shroud Hearth Barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shroud_Hearth_Barrow), [Frostmere Crypt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Frostmere_Crypt), Dustman's Cairn, [Folgunthur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Folgunthur), Ironbind Barrow, Shriekwind Bastion, [Shalidor's Maze and the Labyrinthian](Lore_Labyrinthian.md), Snow Veil Sanctum, [Dead Crone Rock](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dead_Crone_Rock), Angarvunde, Ysgramor's Tomb, Silverdrift Lair, [Valthume](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Valthume), [Volunruud](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Volunruud), [Ustengrav](Lore_Ustengrav.md), and [Arcwind Point](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arcwind_Point) on the mainland, as well as [Kolbjorn Barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kolbjorn_Barrow), [White Ridge Barrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:White_Ridge_Barrow), Vahlok's Tomb, and the [Temple of Miraak](Lore_Temple_of_Miraak.md) on the isle of Solstheim.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

Dragons were known to dwell near Word Walls such as those at Autumnwatch Tower, [Shearpoint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shearpoint), Bonestrewn Crest, [Mount Anthor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mount_Anthor), [Skyborn Altar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skyborn_Altar), Ancient's Ascent, Dragontooth Crater, Lost Tongue Overlook, [Northwind Summit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Northwind_Summit), [Eldersblood Peak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eldersblood_Peak) on the mainland, and Saering's Watch on Solstheim. A Word Wall could also be found atop the [Throat of the World](Lore_Throat_of_the_World.md). Several cavernous locations such as Sunderstone Gorge, Forsaken Cave, Dimhollow Crypt on the mainland, and Benkongerike and [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) Mine on the Solstheim island contained Word Walls as well.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

Some were located in places associated with cults or factions devoted to other deities, for example the near the statue of Meridia at [Kilkreath Temple](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kilkreath_Temple) or the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) Sanctuary in [Falkreath Hold](Lore_Falkreath_Hold.md). Other walls were erected by Nords in places of cultural significance to different cultures, such as the [Snow Elven](Lore_Snow_Elf.md) [Forgotten Vale](Lore_Forgotten_Vale.md) or the [Reachfolk's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) [Lost Valley Redoubt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lost_Valley_Redoubt).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/SR-word_wall-Soul_Cairn.jpg/200px-SR-word_wall-Soul_Cairn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-word_wall-Soul_Cairn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-word_wall-Soul_Cairn.jpg) Soul Cairn Word Wall [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/af/CTA-scenery-Word_Wall.png/200px-CTA-scenery-Word_Wall.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CTA-scenery-Word_Wall.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CTA-scenery-Word_Wall.png) A word Wall (Call to Arms) Some Word Walls were located in other realms beyond [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), within [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius) and [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), including Sovngarde, the Soul Cairn, and sections of Apocrypha accessed through the [Black Book](Lore_Black_Books.md) of *[Waking Dreams](Lore_Black_Book__Waking_Dreams.md)*.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

The stones were erected to commemorate a wide range of figures. Among them was Sarvirra, the brave horse who fell in battle; Vakeeza, a humble servant of Laknir Little-Hammer, who raised a stone in his servant's honor; and Ulfeidr, a she-wolf known as a slayer of both men and beasts. Other stones honored Freda, protector of children; Yngnavar Ghost-Bear, who sought glory upon the Battlefield of Sorrows but instead met death and dishonor; Bhar the Stubborn, who pursued enlightenment and failed; and Vulgrum, the Sword in the Wind, a mercenary whose glory was brief, though his memory endured.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

Other memorials recorded the lives of Hrodmir the Broken, who devoted his life to avenging his brother, and Odrav Anvil-Hand, who brought great honor to Clan Red-Mountain and whose stone was raised by his father, Brothi. Another one honored Undveld, the fearless archer remembered as lord of the [Great Hunt](Lore_Great_Hunt.md). Other inscriptions commemorated Vahlok the Guardian, and Rognvald, who was honored by his child, Nafni, with an inscription upon a Word Wall. One such Word Wall also marked the resting place of Azaran the Cruel, a necromancer who attempted and failed to bind the heroes of Sovngarde to the mortal plane.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

Other stones memorialized Bard Romerius, who died while fleeing goblins; Svolo, who possessed the strength to slay a dragon but not the stamina to fell many foes; the doomed Elven children of the Autumn Field; and the fallen Sisterhood of the White Rose. Some stones also bore inscriptions honoring [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md), the First Dragonborn. Another stone was devoted to Thorgrima, Keeper of the Crimson Fire and lord of magicka, honored by his wife, Lungerd. Other memorials commemorated Arnvid the Unbound, a veteran of the War of the Pines; Fjolmod Foul-Air, remembered for his notorious stench; Thjodrek the Brave, who died heroically at the Battle of the Serpent Sea; Princess Yrsa, celebrated for her beauty; and Jafnhar, a child-king who perished after being burned by the dragon Lodunost.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/SR-concept-6877473899.jpg/200px-SR-concept-6877473899.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877473899.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-concept-6877473899.jpg) A Word Wall (Concept Art) Another stone was raised by Aesa in honor of her sister, Thohild the Inferno, whose passion was said to burn as fiercely as the Red Forge. Yet another memorial commemorated Herfodr Sun-Slayer, a mighty warlord of the Valley of Broken Dreams. Bard Lunerio was also honored, his golden music said to have frozen into silence beneath the night sky. Anurassa, a beautiful Khajiit whose warmth was believed to endure even beyond death was also honored with a Word Wall, and so was Queen Hrefna Raven-Hair, honored by her loyal subject Wulfin. Iglif Ice-Blood, who succumbed to disease was commemorated as well. One stone was raised by Sigruuf in memory of his sister Lanal, whose beauty was likened to a statue carved from living ivory.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>

Word Walls also commemorated Hela, friend to all beasts and servant of [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md); Valkrys, a shield-maiden who fought with great courage but fell after placing her trust in the power of a borrowed sword; and Skorji Leech-Fingers, a treacherous thief whose head was taken by the very axe he sought to steal. Other stones commemorated Noomi, daughter of Thoringar, who met her end beneath the arrows of [Orcs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orcs), and Wynjolf the Whirlwind, renowned for his battles against them. Hungunthar Time-Eater, slayer of the Kings of the East and conqueror of Dunkreath, was honored by his son, Vegunthar. Another memorial was raised for Aaban, a warlord of the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md) sands who fell in Skyrim. One stone was dedicated to the warriors of the Black Regiment, whose souls were said to march upon the plains of Sovngarde for all eternity. Heimverlund the Mighty of the Brutal North, Hrothmar Wrath-Wolf of the Savage Plains and the villagers of doomed Vundeheim, who were burned alive by the Lightning of Unending Sorrow were also honored with Word Walls.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup> One stone was raised by [Modir the Far](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Modir_the_Far) in memory of his brother, Oskar the Fool,<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Throw_Voice1-13)</sup> while another honored the Keeper of the [Dragonstone](Lore_Dragonstone.md). Yet another Word Wall was erected by Bekkhild in honor of her husband, Eyolf, whose ship was lost in a dark tempest upon the Sea of Tears.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup> One stone honored Nievaan, an Argonian who wandered far from his homeland and died with honor in service to the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md). Another commemorated Omluth the Fool, who met his end after attempting to feed a savage creature.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Throw_Voice2-14)</sup>

In addition to memorials, many of the maxims attributed to [Hoar Father](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hoar_Father) were also inscribed upon the stones.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SWW-3)</sup>
