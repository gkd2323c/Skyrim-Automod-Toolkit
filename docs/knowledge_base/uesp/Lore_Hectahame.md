[2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) Hectahame ([Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md) for *"Home of the Exiles"*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLoad Hecta-1)</sup> and also called the **[Heart of Valenwood](Lore_Heart_of_Valenwood.md)**)<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOV-2)</sup> is a large and expansive [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) found in the evergreen jungles of [Greenshade](Lore_Greenshade.md), in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Built deep in the vales of [Green's Marrow](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green%27s_Marrow), it contains the Heart of the Valenwood, an ancient tree that represents the physical embodiment of the forest's soul and lifeblood. The local [Wood Elves](Lore_Wood_Elves.md) view these ruins with superstition and awe, both fearing the past and venerating the Heart for its life energy.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOV-2)</sup> It was built by the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md), possibly for those that lived in the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLoad Hecta-1)</sup>

## History
The Ayleid settlement of Hectahame was built by the refugees of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md), which toppled the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243) and drove the elves out of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Its name is a sentiment to its inception. Scholars believe that Hectahame was built for the nobility that resided in the White-Gold Tower, but it is unknown whether that was the actual case.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONLoad Hecta-1)</sup>

The tree known as the Heart of Valenwood is more of a representation of the forest's soul, rather than the literal heart. But regardless, the ruins emit a strong and powerful energy that many people have tried to take control of, even back when the Ayleids still lived in the province. In the early years of Ayleid occupation in Valenwood (or in the years after Ayleid extinction),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-3)</sup> a necromancer, whose name is long forgotten and cursed, invaded the region with an army of undead and manipulated the heart's energy to raise resurrected Ayleid warriors. Even after a long and grueling battle, the city was unable to defeat the powerful army and were forced to seal Hectahame with a strong ward from their [welkynd stone](Lore_Welkynd_Stone.md). The city was turned into a prison and eventually, the undead returned to the ground as the magic dissipated. Despite this, no one was able to break down the barrier, and ever since then, Hectahame has been left to rot.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOV-2)</sup>

Like many other Ayleid cities in Valenwood, it lasted no longer than two-thousand years after the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md) after the slave rebellion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ASIV-4)</sup> It is speculated that the only people that could enter the ruins is with an object that could cancel out the magic that makes up the barrier. One of the last known people to enter the ruins was an unnamed [Silvenar](Lore_The_Silvenar.md) in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), who was known for his madness by his contemporaries.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HOV-2)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the undead Shade of [Naemon](Lore_Naemon.md) took refuge in the ruins of Hectahame and with the [Staff of Magnus](Lore_Staff_of_Magnus.md), created an army of undead warriors and feral soldiers to corrupt the province and finish creating an evil realm known as the [Shadow Wood](Lore_Shadow_Wood.md). The [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md)'s greatest fighters stood on guard at the outskirts of the ruins, looking for any strategy to infiltrate Hectahame without turning into feral ghouls. The Bosmeri cleric, [Indaenir](Lore_Indaenir.md), and an [agent](Lore_The_Vestige.md) of the Dominion bypassed the ruins by entering through the Shadow Wood and embracing nature's wild side. Once they reached inside, the two placed keystones in the center of the ruins and entered the Valenheart, Hectahame's inner sanctum that holds the Heart of Valenwood. In an epic battle, the two defeated the Shade of Naemon, but the Heart was still too corrupt. In a last-ditch effort, Indaenir sacrificed himself and restored the Heart of Valenwood, at the cost of his life. It was at that moment, [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md) took pity on the elf, brought him back to life, and named him the new Silvenar.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ONStriking-5)</sup>

## Notes
- It is believed by scholars that Hectahame has several pieces of the Racuvar Bala, large and green rectangular stones that are believed to have shattered after falling from the sky. It was recovered by the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in [Marbruk](Lore_Marbruk.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ONSarandil-6)</sup> The name literally translates to *"Cast Down Power"* in Ayleidoon.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBUmrail-7)</sup>
