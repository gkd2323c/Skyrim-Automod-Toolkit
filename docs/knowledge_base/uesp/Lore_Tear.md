[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/ab/AR-place-Tear.jpg/200px-AR-place-Tear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Tear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-place-Tear.jpg) Tear (Arena) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2f/LO-map-Morrowind_%28Anthology%29.jpg/200px-LO-map-Morrowind_%28Anthology%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Morrowind_(Anthology).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-map-Morrowind_(Anthology).jpg) Map of Morrowind Tear (also known as **Dres**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDrulene-1)</sup> is one of the eight major cities on mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup> Tear is the capital of the Great [House Dres](Lore_House_Dres.md), whose sphere of influence revolves around an agrarian agricultural society, building its vast fortune from the export of [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md) and its grip on the [slavery](Lore_Slavery.md) industry.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-4)</sup> Tear is also known as the **Jewel of the East** and it is the most open to trade and commerce compared to other cities on the mainland. But even so, strange happenings occur in the night and few people ever complain about it.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AR-5)</sup>

## Layout and Geography
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_14-Slaver_Camp.jpg) A close-up of the slaver camp near Tear The Tear region is notably hot and humid, given its proximity to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-MWDrulene-1)</sup> and the greater Dres District, which mainly sweeps across the province's southern borderlands, is a blend of the central plains in [Deshaan](Lore_Deshaan.md) and the swampy interior of Black Marsh. Tear is the epicenter of a series of plantations<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-4)</sup> that harvest everything from [saltrice](Lore_Saltrice.md)<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup> to lichen.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Po M-6)</sup> These plantations are walled-off and heavily guarded by Dres spearmen, sometimes riding on large [wasp](Lore_Wasp.md) -like insects called [parraptons](Lore_Parrapton.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-RGCamp-7)</sup> Tear is neighbored by several settlements, including [Corkarth Run](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Corkarth_Run) to the north-northwest, [Silnim Dale](Lore_Silnim_Dale.md) to the far west-northwest, and Black Marsh to the south,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ARMap-2)</sup> toward [Thorn](Lore_Thorn.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ARMap BM-8)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/RG-comic-Page_13-Raid_on_Tear.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_13-Raid_on_Tear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_13-Raid_on_Tear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_13-Raid_on_Tear.jpg) Slaver camp targeted during the Raid on Tear It is currently unknown when Tear was founded, but House Dres was given its status of a Great House sometime before the 84th Year of the Golden Peace after a slave-raid on Tear's neighbor, Thorn orchestrated by Grandmaster [Thalthil Dres](Lore_Thalthil_Dres.md). This event was what also established House Dres' role as a predominant supplier in the slave industry.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ONContraband-11)</sup> Scholars from the [Antiquarian Circle](Lore_Antiquarian_Circle.md) have stated that House Dres had been at the forefront of the [Dunmeri](Lore_Dunmer.md) slave trade since the mid- [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ONChalice Anti-12)</sup>

Ten years after his initial battle near [Gnisis](Lore_Gnisis.md), the Warrior-Poet, [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) re-encountered the [Ruddy Man](Lore_Ruddy_Man.md) near Tear, who already ransacked three villages. The monster's carapace was now worn by a wayward shaman of the [House of Troubles](Lore_House_of_Troubles.md). The [Dreugh](Lore_Dreugh.md) had imbued the armor with mythic inflexibility, making it molt onto the shaman and have their bones stretched. Vivec defeated the monster with the Symbolic Collage and entrusted the carapace to the loyal mystics of the [Number Room](Lore_Numerology.md#Dunmer).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-36LOVS28-13)</sup>

At some point in the First Era, Tear was caught amid a conflict called the War for Silyanorn, between the [First Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) and Morrowind. The [Imperial Navy](Lore_Imperial_Navy.md) defeated [House Indoril](Lore_House_Indoril.md)'s fleet at the Battle of Tear Harbor. In honor of their defeat, they gave one of their ships to the legion, who named it the Dominus Fatum. It later perished during the chase for [Red Bramman](Lore_Red_Bramman.md) in [1E 1033](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1033), re-surfacing in northern [Murkmire](Lore_Murkmire.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONStrillian-10)</sup>

In [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573), Tear suffered a series of disasters including summer chills, a flood, a miasma that settled over the city, fouled drinking water, and plague. With the healers of the city overwhelmed, and nearby communities declining aid for fear of spreading the plague, the healer Rindral Thirano was driven to bargain with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Peryite](Lore_Peryite.md) for the city's survival. At the cost of taking all the ills of the city upon himself, Rindral ensured Tear's recovery and good health.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Dwa DP-14)</sup> Sometime in the late [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) crime boss, [S'rathra](Lore_S%27rathra.md) sent his band of brigands to a plantation outside of Tear to free his kinsmen from Dres slavers. The [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md), [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) freed some of the slaves, causing a giant revolt across the plantation. In the end, the Dres slavers were defeated and the slaves were liberated. But Cyrus left S'rathra's company to work alone and his path eventually brought him to [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TOOC-15)</sup>

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city-state of Tear was an active settlement. It was ruled by King Niasth and had a rivalry with [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-AR-5)</sup> When [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) erupted in [4E 5](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_5), cities on the mainland like Mournhold were mostly unaffected,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TRYII-16)</sup> however, Tear suffered greatly from the cataclysm. An account from the farmer, Drallin Vess, claims that the ground turned into mush and the swamp essentially swallowed up half the city. Earthquakes tore through the ground and caused water to spill in until people were knee-deep in muck water. People tried to save their families and livestock from the flooding water and when the quakes stopped, the city's southern wall crumbled. Other farmers tried to help everyone they could in Tear's destruction, poor and wealthy alike.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TRYI-17)</sup> It is unknown how Tear fared during the [Accession War](Lore_Accession_War.md), which saw the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) cut a bloody swathe across southern Morrowind until they were put down by [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-SRAdril-18)</sup>

## Culture and Society
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/LO-misc-Ragman_of_Tear.jpg/150px-LO-misc-Ragman_of_Tear.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Ragman_of_Tear.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Ragman_of_Tear.jpg) Ragman of Tear
- *For more information, see the: **[Culture](Lore_Dunmer.md#Culture)** and **[Society](Lore_Dunmer.md#Society)** sections on the Dunmer page.*

Tear is the capital of House Dres, whose territory spans across Morrowind and encompassed other notable cities, like [Kragenmoor](Lore_Kragenmoor.md),<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup> which was at one point the Great House's capital,<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-EGT-19)</sup> and for a brief time, [Ebonheart](Lore_Ebonheart.md)<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ONDrathus-20)</sup> and [Necrom](Lore_Necrom.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MWMap-21)</sup> House Dres separates themselves from the rest of Morrowind as being proud isolationists and by maintaining a persistent tradition of [ancestor](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md#Dunmer) and [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup>

The crop that fueled Tear and House Dres' wealth was saltrice,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-UHD-3)</sup> which is exported across Tamriel via the East Empire Company.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-MWGen E-22)</sup> Fermented saltrice is an ingredient in [Dunmer cuisine](Lore_Dunmer_Cuisine.md),<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-AFATD3-23)</sup> especially for a staple Dunmer beverage called [Mazte](Lore_Mazte.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MWGen M-24)</sup> But while Tear is known for its saltrice fields, it is especially known for its hand in slavery. The slave-pens of Tear are infamous. Most people that are brought in as slave died an early death in its plantations.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE1-4)</sup>

The [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) would sometimes make routine trips to Tear and buy slaves from House Dres. There is a rumor that the Sload only practice their [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md) on the races of Tamriel, which the author of *[Corpse Preparation](Lore_Corpse_Preparation.md)* believes is why they purchase as many slaves as they do from Tear.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-CP1-25)</sup> The *[Last Scabbard of Akrash](Lore_Last_Scabbard_of_Akrash.md)* is a fiction novel set in Tear in [3E 407](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_407). It is about a Dres noblewoman named Peliah and her love affair with her [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) slave, Kazagh, whose nightly persona is the Lopper, a mass killer that targets Dres slavers.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LSOA-26)</sup> Because the book has an emphasis on [smithing](Lore_Smithing.md), it is sometimes used to teach people the craft.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SRGhorza-27)</sup>
