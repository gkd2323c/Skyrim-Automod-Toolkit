# Taris Rendil

Taris Rendil was an Arch-Mage of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) who constructed [Frostcrag Spire](Lore_Frostcrag_Spire.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBdownload-1)</sup> Rendil was dissatisfied with living in the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), so he constructed the Spire in the lofty [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to escape his colleagues' prying eyes. He was the creator of the Spire's Atronach Altar, and also collaborated with [Sinderion](Lore_Sinderion.md) of [Skingrad](Lore_Skingrad.md) to develop the Frostcrag Apparatus Table.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-FSM-2)</sup> He was in some way associated with Lennasaan and Rindsey, two great adventurers who were buried in an ice cave that served as the underground vault of the Spire.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

When age began to overwhelm Rendil, he entrusted many of his belongings to Aurelinwae of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). A long-lost relative of Rendil's, the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), was his sole heir and successor<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DTFS-4)</sup> and inherited the Spire in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). Rendil wrote the *[Frostcrag Spire Memoirs](Lore_Frostcrag_Spire_Memoirs.md)* on his deathbed, leaving instructions on how to restore the Spire to its former glory. His dying wish was granted by the Hero of Kvatch, who compensated Aurelinwae and returned Rendil's possessions to the tower.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Frostcrag-5)</sup>

## Notes
- Rendil may also be related to [Greywyn Blenwyth](Lore_Greywyn_Blenwyth.md), another long-lost relative of the Hero of Kvatch.
- Based on [naming conventions](Lore_Names.md), Rendil may have been a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md).
- In *[Oblivion Remastered](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion_Remastered)*, the Hero of Kvatch does not inherit Frostcrag Spire from a long-lost relative. Instead, the Spire—long abandoned—is reclaimed after the Hero follows a rumor leading to its location.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
