# Elder

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/SR-npc-Tolfdir.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Tolfdir.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Tolfdir.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Tolfdir.jpg) Tolfdir, a typical elder **Elders** (known as **Old People Race** in the game data) is the race that is used for elderly humans in the game. Since Skyrim does not use an "age slider" during the character generation, the elderly are implemented as their own race, but are still treated as other races in-game. Elders gain no skill bonuses for their race, and have no "racial powers", like the Frost Resistance or Battle Cry abilities Nords possess.

## Elders
There are a total of fifty-three elders; forty-six are in the original game. The [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md) added two more elders, while the [Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md) expansion added four. The [Alternative Armors - Ebony Plate](Skyrim_Alternative_Armors_-_Ebony_Plate.md) and [Fishing](Skyrim_Fishing.md) [Creations](Skyrim_Creation_Club.md) both add one elder.

## Notes
- In the [Creation Kit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim_Mod:Creation_Kit) they are counted as a separate race, similar to how [Dremora](Skyrim_Dremora.md) and [children](Skyrim_Child.md) are treated.
- It is likely that the majority of them are intended to be [Nords](Skyrim_Nord.md) (based on names, family relations, and the general dominance of Nords in Skyrim), but this is speculative and cannot be confirmed in many cases. At least a few of them are known to be [Imperial](Skyrim_Imperial.md) or [Breton](Skyrim_Breton.md). No other races are represented in the elder race.
- There is an additional elder named [Sulvar the Steady](Skyrim_Sulvar_the_Steady.md) that only appears in the game data. He was related to Windhelm's Wheelhouse, which was removed from the final game.
