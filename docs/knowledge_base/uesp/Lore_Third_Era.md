**Note:** The Third Era of Tamriel is also referred to as the *[Septim Era](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md),*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SAJ-1)</sup> *Imperial Era*,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-YB3E-2)</sup> or *Third Age*.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

## First Century
3E 0 — Beginning of the Third Era - The Third Era is proclaimed by Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) at the end of [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), after [the unification](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tiber_Wars) of all provinces in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Beth Time-7)</sup> However alternative sources contradict this, indicating that the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md) did not end until the final year of the millenium (ca. 2E 999, which is 103 years after 2E 896).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ADHom-8)</sup><sup>:39</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Arena Intro-9)</sup>

3E 6 — Reign of the presiding [Monarch of Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md#Known_Rulers) begins - The [Imperial Governor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Symmachus) administering Morrowind Province is formally replaced with a provincial [monarch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barenziah).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TRB-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HP-11)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-KRP-12)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

3E 12 — *Encyclopedia Tamrielica* is published - *Encyclopedia Tamrielica* is published during the early years of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s reign.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TDBRE-13)</sup>

3E 20 — [Destri Melarg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Destri_Melarg) is born - Born as simply Destri in the city-state of [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md), Destri Melarg would become a well-known historian and translator of old [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) verse.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NFRH-14)</sup>

3E 38 — Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) dies, Emperor [Pelagius Septim I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_I) is crowned - Tiber Septim, conqueror and ruler of all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) for 38 years, dies and is succeeded by his grandson, Pelagius.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup>

3E 39 — [Destri Melarg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Destri_Melarg) enters the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) - Destri Melarg, famed [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) historian and translator, begins study at the age of 19 in the Imperial City, where he takes the last name of Melarg.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NFRH-14)</sup>

3E 41 — Emperor [Pelagius Septim I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_I) dies, Empress [Kintyra Septim I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kintyra_Septim_I) is crowned - Emperor Pelagius is assassinated by the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). With no direct heirs to the throne, the crown passes to the departed Emperor's first cousin.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup>

3E 51 — Prince [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) is born - Prince Antiochus is born to the future Emperor [Pelagius Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_II).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup>

3E 53 — Empress [Kintyra Septim I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kintyra_Septim_I) dies, Emperor [Uriel Septim I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_I) is crowned - Empress Kintyra dies. Kintyra's son is crowned after her death, the first Emperor of Tamriel to use the Imperial name Uriel.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-BA-17)</sup><sup>:16</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFC-18)</sup>

3E 63 — Prince [Pelagius Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_II) meets Princess [Quintilla](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quintilla) - The future Emperor Pelagius II meets his future wife, the princess of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md). Together, they purportedly defeat a [werewolf](Lore_Werewolf.md) who was terrorizing her land.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 64 — Emperor [Uriel I](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_I) dies, Emperor [Uriel II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_II) is crowned - Emperor Uriel I dies and the kingdom is taken over by his son, Emperor Uriel II, the Fifth Emperor. Tragically, the rule of Uriel II is cursed with blights, plagues, and insurrections. The tenderheartedness inherited from his father does not serve the land well, and little justice is done.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFC-18)</sup>

3E 67 — [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), future Queen of Solitude, is born - Born the second of four children, and the only daughter, to [Pelagius Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_II), Potema becomes better known later as the Wolf Queen.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-15)</sup>

3E 80 — Princess [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) is promised to wed King [Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantiarco) - The thirteen-year-old is promised to the King of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) to smooth relations with the northern kingdom.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 81 — King [Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mantiarco) marries Princess [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) - The 14-year-old granddaughter of Emperor [Uriel Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_II) marries the King of Solitude.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 82 — Emperor [Uriel II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_II) dies, Emperor [Pelagius II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_II) is crowned - Uriel II dies after an 18-year reign. The Emperor is succeeded by his son, Pelagius II. Pelagius II inherits not only the throne, but the debt from his father's poor financial and judicial management.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFC-18)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 97 — [Uriel Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Mantiarco) is born - After several miscarriages, Queen [Potema](Lore_Potema.md) of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) gives birth to her first and only child, who she names after her grandfather.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 98, 15th of Evening Star — Emperor [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md) dies - The Emperor dies in the waning days of the year after a seventeen-year reign, and his successor isn't appointed until the following year.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 99 — Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) is crowned - Emperor [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md) dies and his son, Antiochus, inherits the kingdom. His reign is rife with civil wars, surpassing even that of his grandfather, [Uriel II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_II).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFC-18)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-15)</sup>

## Second Century
3E 104 — Princess [Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) is born - The future Empress Kintyra II is born to Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) and his second wife, Gysilla.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 105 — *[Opusculus Lamae Bal ta Mezzamortie](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Opusculus_Lamae_Bal_ta_Mezzamortie)* is translated - The [University of Gwylim Press](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md) finishes translating the tale.<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_nb3)</sup>

3E 109 — Debauchery in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) - Ten years into his reign, the obese and lecherous Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) has done little to impress his subjects besides the unquenchable hedonism he exhibits.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 110 — [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md) - During the reign of Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md), the province of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) is nearly lost to the [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md). The united alliance of the kings of Summerset and Antiochus only manage to defeat King [Orgnum](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Orgnum) of the island-kingdom of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) due to a freak storm. Legend later credits the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) of the [Isle of Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) with the magic behind the storm.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 111 — [Knights of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Nine) are founded - The Knights of the Nine are founded by Sir [Amiel Lannus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Lannus), a hero of the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md), with the aim of recovering the lost relics of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TKOTN-19)</sup>

3E 114 — Empress [Kintyra Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kintyra_Septim_II) reportedly dies - Some sources later erroneously assert that Empress Kintyra is slain after being captured by [Uriel III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_III). In truth, she was not even Empress yet at this time, and doesn't die until 3E 123 (23rd of Frostfall, now named the day of Broken Diamonds).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-15)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BD-20)</sup>

3E 119 — [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) is born - The future emperor of Tamriel is born to [Magnus Septim](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), then King of Wayrest.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup>

3E 120 — Emperor [Antiochus](Lore_Antiochus.md) dies, Empress [Kintyra Septim II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) is crowned - Princess Kintyra is chosen by the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) over King [Uriel Mantiarco](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Mantiarco), despite the lobbying efforts of his mother [Potema](Lore_Potema.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup><sup>[[nb 4]](#intnote_nb4)</sup>

3E 121 — [Uriel III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_III) is proclaimed Emperor - The [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) itself is taken over in only a fortnight, as two other attacks draw away Imperial forces. Uriel III is proclaimed Emperor of Tamriel. He abandons his father's surname of Mantiarco and takes the Septim name. Empress [Kintyra Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kintyra_Septim_II) is taken captive in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) as [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) takes sides between Uriel III and his uncles.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup>

3E 121 — [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) - This war was between the three surviving children of [Pelagius II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_II): [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md), and [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md), and their respective offspring. Potema, of course, supports her son [Uriel III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_III), and has the support of all of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and northern [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). With the efforts of Cephorus and Magnus, all of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) eventually supports them. [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), [Summerset Isle](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md), [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md), and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) were divided, but most kings supported Cephorus and Magnus.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 123, 23rd of Frost Fall — Empress [Kintyra Septim II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kintyra_Septim_II) dies in her cell - Although reported to have died in 3E 114, the captive Empress Kintyra dies in secret in her cell. The 23rd of Frostfall is later named the day of Broken Diamond in her memory.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-BD-20)</sup>

3E 127 — Emperor [Uriel III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_III) dies, Emperor [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) is crowned - Uriel III is captured at the Battle of Ichidag in [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). En route to his trial in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), a mob overtakes his carriage and burns him alive within it. His captor and uncle continues on to the Imperial City, and by common acclaim becomes Emperor Cephorus I. Cephorus' reign is marked by nothing but war. The Empire acquiesces to demands for greater autonomy from the nobility of all the outlying provinces except [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). It takes Cephorus an additional ten years of constant warfare to defeat his sister [Potema](Lore_Potema.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 131 — [Knights of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Nine) are dissolved - The knightly order is formally dissolved, having never truly recovered following the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TKOTN-19)</sup>

3E 133 — [Destri Melarg](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Destri_Melarg) dies - Destri Melarg, famed [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) historian and translator, dies at the age of 113, leaving numerous unfinished histories and untranslated verse.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-NFRH-14)</sup>

3E 137 — [Potema](Lore_Potema.md), Queen of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md), dies - The so-called Wolf Queen of Solitude dies during the siege of her castle.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-BOTWQ-15)</sup>

3E 139 — Sir [Casimir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Casimir) loses the [Gauntlets of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gauntlets_of_the_Crusader) - After the knight kills a beggar in the Chapel of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md) in [Chorrol](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chorrol), the gauntlets slip off his hands and lie immovable on the floor of the chapel, waiting for a new champion.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TKOTN-19)</sup>

3E 140 — Emperor [Cephorus I](Lore_Cephorus_I.md) dies, Emperor [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) is crowned - Cephorus never had the time to marry, so it is his brother, the fourth child of [Pelagius II](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pelagius_Septim_II), who assumes the throne. The Emperor Magnus is elderly, and the business of punishing the traitorous kings of the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md) drains much of his health.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup>

3E 141 — King [Pelagius](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) of Solitude marries Duchess [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) - The future Emperor of Tamriel is recorded as "occasionally eccentric" in the Imperial Annals. He marries the Duchess on the orders of his father, Emperor [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup>

3E 145, 8th of Second Seed — Emperor [Magnus](Lore_Magnus_Septim.md) dies, Emperor [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) is crowned - Pelagius leaves the throne of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) to his sister Jolethe. Almost from the start, Pelagius III, sometimes called Pelagius the Mad, becomes noted for his eccentric behavior. He embarrasses dignitaries, offends his vassal kings, and, on one occasion, marks the end of a grand ball by attempting to hang himself.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TWQ-16)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup>

3E 150 — Last sighting of Sir [Amiel Lannus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Amiel_Lannus) - The founder of the Knights of the Nine is seen for the last time, living alone in the priory of his defunct order.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-TKOTN-19)</sup>
- The Siege of Abernanit takes place in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DBOA-22)</sup>

ca. 3E 150 — [Cassynder Septim](Lore_Cassynder.md) is born - The future Emperor Cassynder is born to Emperor [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_III.md) and Empress Regent [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup> Cassynder is conceived after his father was committed to an asylum.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup>

3E 153, 2nd of Suns Dawn — Emperor [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_Septim_III.md) dies, Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) is crowned - After being committed to an asylum due to his madness, on a warm night after a brief fever, the 34-year-old Emperor Pelagius III dies in his cell at the Temple of [Kynareth](Lore_Kynareth.md), on the Isle of [Betony](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Betony). Katariah, Duchess of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and Empress Regent following the commitment of her husband, formally becomes the controversial new Empress. Katariah's forty-six year reign is later remembered one of the most glorious in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s history (assertions to the contrary being dismissed as racist). Katariah travels extensively throughout the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), as no Emperor had since [Tiber](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md)'s day. She repairs much of the damage that broken alliances and bungled diplomacy had created. The people of Tamriel come to love their Empress, though many nobles are not as fond.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup>

3E 195 — [Rangidil Ketil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rangidil_Ketil) dies - The commander of the [Ordinators](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ordinators) of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) dies at the age of 288.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-DBOA-22)</sup>

## Third Century
3E 200 — Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) dies, Emperor [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md) is crowned - Katariah's death in a minor skirmish in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) later becomes a favorite topic of conspiracy-minded historians. It later comes to light that a disenfranchised branch of the [Septim family](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) is involved. When Cassynder assumes the throne at the death of his mother, he is already middle-aged and in poor health.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-TMOP-21)</sup><sup>[[nb 5]](#intnote_nb5)</sup>

3E 202 — Emperor [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md) dies, Emperor [Uriel IV](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_IV) is crowned - Cassynder dies after a two-year reign. Uriel Lariat, Cassynder's half-brother and the child of [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) I and her Imperial consort, abdicates the throne of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md) to reign as Emperor Uriel IV. Legally, he was Uriel Septim IV: Cassynder had adopted him into the family when he had become King of Wayrest. Nevertheless, to many of the people of Tamriel, Uriel IV is a bastard child of Katariah. Uriel does not possess the dynamism of his mother, and his long forty-five year reign becomes a hotbed of sedition.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup>

3E 213 — *[Song of the Askelde Men](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Song_of_the_Askelde_Men)* published - The traditional [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) poem is translated by the Atheneum Monks at Old Anthel.

3E 247 — Emperor [Uriel IV](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Uriel_Septim_IV) dies, Emperor [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md) is crowned - The [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md)'s last victory over Uriel IV is posthumous: they vote to disinherit [Andorak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Andorak), Uriel IV's son. They also vote to coronate a cousin more closely related to the original Septim line as Emperor Cephorus II. Cephorus, formerly a [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) king, battles those loyal to Andorak for nine years. The Council controversially grants Andorak the [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) kingdom of [Shornhelm](Lore_Shornhelm.md) to end the war.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup>

3E 249 — [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) invades - During the reign of [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md), the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md) leads an army of [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead) warriors on a rampage through [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), conquering the whole province in two years, then turns to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). Cephorus II sends more and more mercenaries into Hammerfell to stop the Usurper's northward march, but they are bribed, turned into undead, or slaughtered. Animosity grows against the seemingly ineffective Empire.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-BHOTE-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup>

3E 253 — [Nightmare Host](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nightmare_Host) controls [Dwynnen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwynnen) - The region of Dwynnen is under the rule of a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) and his armies of [undead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undead). They are defeated by [Othrok](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Othrok), the future Baron of the land.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TFOTU-23)</sup> They were reportedly under the command of the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup>
- [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md), a [Crown](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crown) city, does not answer the clarion call of [Forebear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forebear) cities [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md) and [Taneth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Taneth), allowing the Camoran Usurper to continue his northward march through [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-PGE3Ham-24)</sup>

3E 266 — Lord of Reich Gradkeep ([Anticlere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Anticlere)) dies - The Lord falls deathly ill and dies later in the year. This was one of the reasons why the resistance to the Usurper was so slow to develop in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), who only become fully aware of the Usurper's conquests this year. Sixth House operatives exploit smuggler organizations and communications to spread their influence among victims unbalanced by [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur)'s dream sendings.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DUP-41)</sup>

3E 411 — Fear grows among [Dissident Priests](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dissident_Priests) - Rumors circulate in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) that the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) will soon begin a crusade to stamp out the dissidents in their ranks.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-CKL-45)</sup>

3E 412 — [Azura's Star](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura%27s_Star) disappears - Azura's Star is purportedly sought and found by an adventurer, who betrays his partner and uses the Star to attain great wealth. The Star disappears again sometime in the months before 8th of Sun's Dawn.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-CKL-45)</sup>

3E 414 — [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) is opened for settlement - Previously a preserve administrated by the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) since the Treaty of the [Armistice](Lore_Armistice.md), Vvardenfell was largely uninhabited and undeveloped. Vvardenfell is reorganized as an Imperial Provincial District after King [Llethan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Llethan) of Morrowind revokes the centuries-old Temple ban on trade and settlement. A flood of Imperial colonists and the [Great Houses](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Great_Houses) came to Vvardenfell. This also opens up exploitation of valuable resources like [malachite](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malachite) and [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md).<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ASHOM-46)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-TCT-47)</sup>

3E 415 — [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House) occupy every town in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) - It is later believed that the Sixth House has at least a small cell in every town in Vvardenfell, with larger operations concealed in remote dungeons.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DUP-41)</sup>

3E 417, 9th of Frost Fall — The Miracle of Peace, or the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) - A mysterious event takes place between the 9th and 11th of Frostfall in which the forty-four independent kingdoms, counties, baronies, and duchies surrounding the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) were condensed into four: [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md), [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), and [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md).<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-TWINT-48)</sup>

3E 417 — The [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) lose [Kagrenac's Tools](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kagrenac%27s_Tools) - [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) lose the artifacts [Keening](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keening) and [Sunder](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sunder) to the minions of [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) during a battle at [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md). [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) rescues them, but fails to recover the artifacts. They retreat from Red Mountain in disorder and return to their respective capitals, continuing to perform their ritual functions, but soon stop appearing in public altogether. They grow weaker without access to the [Heart of Lorkhan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), and because of resources required to support the [Ghostfence](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghostfence). The inner circle of the Temple priesthood begin to suspect the Tribunes have suffered seriously from wounds and demoralization in the wake of reverses at Red Mountain, but do not recognize the scale of the problem.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DUP-41)</sup>

3E 421 — The [Levitation Act](Lore_Levitation_Act.md) is passed. 3E 421 — [Greywyn Blenwyth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Greywyn_Blenwyth) founds the [Crimson Scars](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crimson_Scars). - Greywyn, a member of the Dark Brotherhood, believes that [Sithis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sithis) has ordered him to remove all non-vampires from the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). To achieve this goal, Greywyn founds the Crimson Scars. The Crimson Scars are later killed by the Black Hand upon the discovery of Greywyn's plot.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TTET-6)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-Grey J-49)</sup>

3E 421, 14th of Last Seed — The Emma May sinks - A ship called the Emma May sinks in [Niben Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_Bay) shortly after a mutiny.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-LOTEM-50)</sup>

3E 422 — [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md), Count of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), disappears. - The husband of Countess [Millona Umbranox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Millona_Umbranox) disappears.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-OBGray-51)</sup>

3E 426 — The Prelude sinks - A ship called the Prelude sinks off the southern coast of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), east of [Vivec](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-MWEdryno-52)</sup>

3E 426 — Tax revolt in [Balmora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balmora) - Due to high [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) tax rates and tariffs on trade, the people of Balmora revolt. The revolt is forcefully put down, but accounts of the event differ.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-YB3E-2)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-53)</sup>

3E 426 — Assassinations in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) - Assassinations by the [Sixth House](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sixth_House) of [Imperial](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Hlaalu](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu) supporters increase significantly. So do sudden attacks by the cultists and deranged victims of soul sickness.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-DUP-41)</sup>

3E 427 — [The Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight) - The [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) government, already weakened in power over questions of authority, is further threatened by the re-awakening of the ancient curse of the Blight from the giant volcano [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md).
- On the 16th of Last Seed, the legendary [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) arrives in [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) by order of the Emperor, destined to kill [Dagoth Ur](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagoth_Ur) and bring peace to Morrowind.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Morrowind-54)</sup>

3E 427 — The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) discovers a [translation key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hanging_Gardens) of the [Dwemer language](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Language), along with two sample texts, in the Dwemer ruins of Vvardenfell - A member of the Mages Guild is tasked with recovering various blueprints from the Dwemer ruins of Vvardenfell. By accident, they find a [translation key](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hanging_Gardens) of the Dwemer language, along with the sample texts, *[Divine Metaphysics](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Divine_Metaphysics)* and *[The Egg of Time](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Egg_of_Time)*.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Morrowind-54)</sup>

3E 427 — Discord among the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) - After the loss of her divine powers, [Almalexia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Almalexia) of the Almsivi becomes insane and kills her fellow Tribune [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md). The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) reforges [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar)'s blade - [Trueflame](Lore_Trueflame.md) - and uses it to kill the mortal Almalexia in the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md).<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Tribunal-55)</sup>

3E 427 — [Bloodmoon Prophecy](Lore_Bloodmoon_Prophecy.md) - As the prophecy foretold, the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md) performs a ritual hunt on the frozen island of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), as he does once every era. The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) is chosen to play a part in it, and ultimately defeats Hircine at his own game.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Bloodmoon-56)</sup>

3E 430 — King [Helseth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hlaalu_Helseth) outlaws slavery in Morrowind, causing unrest and the houses of Indoril and Redoran to stage a revolution against him.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-PGE3M-37)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-TPPlaying Houses-58)</sup> 3E 431 — Changes in the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) - Arch-Mage [Hannibal Traven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hannibal_Traven) institutes substantial reforms in the Mages Guild.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-MGC-59)</sup>

3E 431 — [Dawnfang and Duskfang](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dawnfang_and_Duskfang) are discovered - A group of adventurers in the [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md) find the [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) swords Dawnfang and Duskfang in an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-GJ-60)</sup>

3E 431 — The [Knights of the Thorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Thorn) - The son of the Count of [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) and his friends begin adventuring as Knights of the Thorn.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-OBGuard-61)</sup>

3E 432 — The third edition of *[A Pocket Guide to The Empire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pocket_Guide_to_the_Empire,_3rd_Edition)* is published - The [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) publishes the third edition to reflect the large changes that had taken place since the second edition, especially following the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md). It is commissioned and promulgated under the authority of Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).
- Five years after its founding, the population of [Raven Rock](Lore_Raven_Rock.md) in [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) totals over thirty people, all of whom are dependent on the [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) mine.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-HORR-57)</sup>

3E 433 — [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) - On the 27th of Last Seed, Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) and his heirs are murdered by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md), a secret cult devoted to [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md). [Oblivion Gates](Lore_Oblivion_Gate.md) open all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) as Dagon begins to invade the [mortal plane](Lore_Mundus.md). [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) is destroyed.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Oblivion-62)</sup>
- In [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) (which have foreseen the crisis) calls [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) from across the Empire back to Black Marsh. Led by the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), the Argonian armies defeat the invading [daedra](Lore_Daedra.md).<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TIC-63)</sup>
- The [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md) in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) is destroyed by daedra.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-RT-64)</sup>
- [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) on [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) is sacked by daedra.
- [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) goes remarkably unscathed; there's no record of any Oblivion Gates opening on the island.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-HORR-57)</sup>
- The Daedric invasion of Tamriel is ended after the Battle of Bruma, during the Battle of the Imperial City. [Martin Septim](Lore_Martin_Septim.md), escorted by the Hero of Kvatch, transforms into the [Avatar of Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and defeats [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), ending the Third Era.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Oblivion-62)</sup>

3E 433 — Count [Corvus Umbranox](Lore_Corvus_Umbranox.md) of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) reappears - After 10 years, Corvus Umbranox returns to his wife in Anvil.
- A new [Gray Fox](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Gray_Fox) leads the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md). History is rewritten by the use of an [Elder Scroll](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), and the [Cyrodilic](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) branch of the guild flourishes.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Oblivion-62)</sup>

3E 433 — Death of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) - Mannimarco, "The King of Worms", falls in a duel in the [Jerall Mountains](Lore_Jerall_Mountains.md) against the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md)'s newest Arch-Mage, with the help of the previous Arch-Mage [Hannibal Traven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Hannibal_Traven).<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Oblivion-62)</sup>

3E 433 — The [Blackwood Company](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blackwood_Company) is destroyed by the Fighters Guild 3E 433 — Purification of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) - Internal strife caused by a traitorous Speaker results in the assassination of half of the Black Hand and the decimation of the [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) sanctuary. The [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) appoints a new Listener after the traitor is killed.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-Oblivion-62)</sup>

3E 433 — Reformation of the [Knights of the Nine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Knights_of_the_Nine) - Following daedric attacks on clergymen, an unknown pilgrim receives a vision of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md), reunites the [Crusader's Relics](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crusader%27s_Relics), and reorganizes the Knights of the Nine.<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-KOTN-65)</sup>

3E 433 — The [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md) - After a portal to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) opens in [Niben Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_Bay), the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s curse is broken, which allows him to remain as [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag). Sheogorath's champion takes up the Mad God's mantle.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-Shivering-66)</sup>
