*Another of the All-Maker's wondrous creations. These creatures provide a great deal for our people. They are strong swimmers and fierce fighters, on land and in the sea.*
—A [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) Horkers are a family of aquatic mammals with three tusks, two long flippers and strong tails. They are native to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and northeastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). Although usually passive, they will attack when provoked or during mating season. [Lake Fjalding](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lake_Fjalding) is the mating grounds for the horkers of Solstheim. The arrival of [chub loons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chub_Loon) partially displaced horkers that resided on the northern coast of [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) due to them disrupting the food source of the horkers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Horkers also possess a semi-resistance to ice [magic](Lore_Magic.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HA-2)</sup>

Horker is also a catch-all term used by [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) to describe sea mammals. As such, the large species of horker that inhabit Skyrim and Wrothgar are actually entirely different from the smaller, [seal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seal) -like horkers found on Solstheim. <sup>[[3]](#cite_note-DFCN-3)</sup><sup>:115</sup>

## Culture and Uses
The second sign of the [Bloodmoon Prophecy](Lore_Bloodmoon_Prophecy.md) involves dead horkers all along the coasts of Solstheim.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-BMKorst-4)</sup>

Horkers are actively hunted by the Skaal for their horns and meat, the Nords and [Dark Elves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dark_Elf) also make use of the meat in meals such as horker pie, horker loaf, horker and ash yam stew and sweet horker stew.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SF-5)</sup>

The tusks are known to have some alchemical properties, but are mostly used for carving, netting hunters a nice bit of gold.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-HA-2)</sup> However, some Nords have been known to use the tusks as drinking horns.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

Horker skin is used to make drums, mittens, glue, boot-liners and cushion covers.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup>
