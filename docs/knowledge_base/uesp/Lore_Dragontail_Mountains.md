[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7a/ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg/200px-ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Hel_Ra_Citadel.jpg) Hel Ra Citadel, found in the Dragontail Mountains [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f7/ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg/200px-ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Upper_Craglorn_02.jpg) Upper Craglorn, a region located in the northeastern part of the Dragontail Mountains, circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) The Dragontail Mountains, (called the **Dragon's-Tail** in the First Era by Redguards)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TTTTo H-1)</sup> are a mountain range in northern [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). They contain the Upper Craglorn region and then curve to the southwest, towards the [Fallen Wastes](Lore_Fallen_Wastes.md) and the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md). Politically, the mountains are surrounded by several regions to the east and west. On its western flanks was the [Alik'r](Lore_Alik%27r.md), [Dak'fron](Lore_Dak%27fron.md), [Tigonus](Lore_Tigonus_(fiefdom).md), and [Santaki](Lore_Santaki_(county).md). To the east is [Lainlyn](Lore_Lainlyn_(barony).md), [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth_(fiefdom).md), [Kozanset](Lore_Kozanset_(barony).md), and [Totambu](Lore_Totambu.md). The march of [Ephesus](Lore_Ephesus_(march).md) is located in the middle of the Dragontail Mountains.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-DFIliac Map-2)</sup> Regions in the area include the Valley of Blades, Bazidir Valley, Lemdazi Valley, and Ngonta Valley, and named peaks in the range include the volcano of Mount Dakobo, Mount Ewado near [Scourg Barrow](Lore_Scourg_Barrow.md), Mount Nombaje, and [Heldorn Mount](Lore_Heldorn_Mount.md). Other locations of note include [Drear](Lore_Drear.md) and Ganada Pass.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGMap-3)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-TPWarofthe Wormgod-4)</sup>

They appear to form part of the [Dragon's Teeth Mountains](Lore_Dragon%27s_Teeth_Mountains.md), where they meet the [Druadach Mountains](Lore_Druadach_Mountains.md) near [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) and [Craglorn](Lore_Craglorn.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ARTerrain-5)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-UC-6)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/39/ON-load-The_Valley_of_Blades.jpg/200px-ON-load-The_Valley_of_Blades.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Valley_of_Blades.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-The_Valley_of_Blades.jpg) The Valley of Blades The Dragontail Mountains are inhabited mostly by the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) and the [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), but were also inhabited by the [Nedes](Lore_Nede.md) and the [goblins](Lore_Goblin.md) in the past. In the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), the goblins of the Dragontail Mountains, led by Warchief [Mahgzoor Rockhand](Lore_Mahgzoor_Rockhand.md), marched through the Fallen Wastes and attacked the great Redguard city of Ojwambu. Even though they seemed numberless, the invasion was eventually repelled thanks to the efforts of the Redguard hero [Derik Hallin](Lore_Derik_Hallin.md), who died in the final battle. The city of Ojwambu was then renamed [Hallin's Stand](Lore_Hallin%27s_Stand.md) after him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TTTTo H-1)</sup> The mountains were also previously inhabited by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md), who dwelled in their underground cities such as Mzeneldt in the South Dragontail Mountains.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-7)</sup>

Prior to their occupation of [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md), [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) would often travel across the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) and exploit resources in the Dragontail Mountains, an underhanded practice that despite tainting their relationship with local Redguards, rulers in [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) benefitted from it and did little to interfere.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-HOHM-8)</sup>

The Abbey of Blades, an ancient training site for Redguard warriors, is located in the mountains in the Valley of Blades. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), it was partly damaged in an attack of [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md)'s minions, however repair work was started by the Abbey of Blades' students.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-9)</sup> In the same year, the Upper Craglorn region in the northeastern part of the mountains fell under the influence of the [Celestial](Lore_Celestial.md) [Serpent](Lore_The_Serpent.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-UC-6)</sup>

The [Empress Kintyra Septim II](Lore_Kintyra_II.md) crossed the Dragontail Mountains with an army during her ill-fated attempt to crush her aunt, the [Wolf Queen Potema](Lore_Potema.md), during the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TWQ6-10)</sup> In the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), before the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), the Dragontail Mountains were considered a political entity, whose capital was the city of [Dragontail](Lore_Dragontail.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DF-11)</sup> During that time, Mannimarco's headquarters, [Scourg Barrow](Lore_Scourg_Barrow.md), was located in the region. During the [War of the Bend'r-mahk](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md), parts of Upper Craglorn, along with half of the city of [Dragonstar](Lore_Dragonstar.md), fell under the control of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE3Skyrim-12)</sup>

## Culture
Legends say that [Diagna](Lore_Diagna.md), the Orichalc God of the Sideways Blade, resides within the mountains.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-VOF-13)</sup>

The [Scenarist Guild](Lore_Scenarist_Guild.md) is based out of a citadel nestled high in the mountain range, protected from trespassers by a magical barrier.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-14)</sup>

## Notes
