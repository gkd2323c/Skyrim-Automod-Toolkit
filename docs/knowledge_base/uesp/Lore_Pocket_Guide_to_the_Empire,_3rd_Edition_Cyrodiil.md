Still largely forested and almost landlocked, the beauty of the land has been sung of since time immemorial. Three [Empires](Lore_Empire.md) have wielded their power from the strategic center of the continent, so it is little wonder that Cyrodiil is widely know simply as The Imperial Province.
### History
The early [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) settlers to Tamriel established strongholds on the islands of [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and along the coast, but did not venture far inland. Only oral histories and the fragmented ballad of [Topal the Pilot](Lore_Topal_the_Pilot.md) offer glimpses of the ancient [beast races](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bird_Men) that inhabited the land, but they are shadowy, mist-drawn portraits of time before reckoning. It is not until the coming of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) that Cyrodilic history truly begins. The Ayleids were ancient Aldmer, cousins of all the elven races that exist to this day. Over time, they became a distinct people, crafting a civilization whose ruins still puzzle and fascinate modern archeologists and adventurers. The ancient [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) people, spreading south from [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), became the slave labor for their ambitions, centered around the [White Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md). The [slave rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) of [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) in the [242 nd](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_242) year of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) is a seminal event in the history of Cyrodiil, and all of Tamriel. While humans and elves had been battling in Skyrim for some time, and the Slave Queen's revolt could not be called the first victory of [men](Lore_Man.md) over [mer](Lore_Mer.md), it represents a turning point in the continental power structure. The heart of Tamriel was going to belong to these former slaves, present day Cyrodilics or [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md), forever more. With the aid of the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md) of Skyrim, the Cyrodilics consolidated their power, forming a loose alliance between the two sometimes disparate regions: the rich [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley), and the remote, rough [Colovian Highlands](Lore_Colovian_Highlands.md). While the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) continued to push westward towards the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni_Hegemony.md) lands in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), the [greatest change](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) was a cultural and religious one. The prophet [Marukh](Lore_Marukh.md)'s teachings both brought identity to Cyrodiil, codifying the [pantheon](Lore_Eight_Divines.md) most civilized Tamriellians worship to this day, and brought conflict due to the more severe strictures he espoused. The next great transformation of the land came from distinctly external forces. The foiled [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) invasion of [2703](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2703) brought about a new dynasty, and a new spirit of cooperation among independent nations, dedicated to fighting the common threat. Under the Emperor [Reman](Lore_Reman.md) I, Cyrodiil became truly cosmopolitan, incorporating aspects of High Rock, [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md), Nibenay, and the sophisticated if strange culture of the defeated Akaviri into a common whole. The Cyrodilic Empire, also called the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), began again the process of expansion, founding a strong single nation, if not actually succeeding in conquering the entire continent. The assassination of [Reman III](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reman_III) and his son and heir [Juilek](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Juilek) at the end of the [Four Score War](Lore_Four-Score_War.md) with [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) marked the end of an era, if not the end of the Second Empire. Under the [Akaviri Potentates](Lore_Akaviri_Potentate.md), the system of governing continued to evolve throughout the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), progress that abruptly ended with the assassination of the [last Potentate](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Savirien-Chorak) in the year [2E 430](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_430). The rest of the Second Era was a time of great darkness and chaos throughout Tamriel, nowhere moreso than in Cyrodiil. Without a central government, Nibenay and [Colovia](Lore_Colovia.md) split apart, farms fell fallow, villages were left in ruin, and the former Imperial highways became no man's land, the realm of bandit kings. The [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) itself became the prize for an endless series of would-be emperors, fought over for centuries until its glory was only a faded shadow of the great days when it ruled Tamriel. The rise of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) has been amply documented in the history, and in a hundred books besides. In Cyrodiil, his influence could not be overemphasized. He gave the land back its traditional power and more, and became the symbol of it. He founded the dynasty that reigns to this very day. Cyrodiil, of course, helped Tiber Septim as much as he helped it. It gave legitimacy and a sense of history to his legend, which grew even ahead of his conquests. The Imperial City and Cyrodiil rose again in splendor, occasionally tarnished by the weakness of some of [Septim's descendants](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), the [War of the Red Diamond](Lore_War_of_the_Red_Diamond.md), and the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), but never again did it lose its luster.
