## Gaiar Alata
[Gaiar Alata](Lore_Gaiar_Alata.md), also known as **Paradise**, was a realm created by [Mankar Camoran](Lore_Mankar_Camoran.md) using the [Mysterium Xarxes](Lore_Mysterium_Xarxes.md). The Mysterium Xarxes served as both the gate and the key to realm, thus in some sense the book *was* Camoran's Paradise. Mankar Camoran himself acted as the "anchor" for Paradise, much like a [sigil stone](Lore_Sigil_Stone.md) anchored an [Oblivion Gate](Lore_Oblivion_Gate.md) in place. Despite its name, the plane was a Paradise only for Camoran and his children; for his followers, it was a prison and torture chamber. Mankar's throne was located in Carac Agaialor, his palace built in the Ayleid style which was common in Paradise. Gaiar Alata was destroyed during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md).

## Galen (Systres)
[Galen](Lore_Galen.md) is the westernmost island of [the Systres](Lore_The_Systres.md) archipelago, northwest of [High Isle](Lore_High_Isle.md) and west of [Y'ffelon](Lore_Y%27ffelon.md). Its main hub is the city of [Vastyr](Lore_Vastyr.md), which was controlled by [House Mornard](Lore_House_Mornard.md) during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). Druidic ruins on were discovered on Galen, fixing the idea of its Breton history into collective imagination. The [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md) were able to instill magic on Y'ffelon to balance nature and bring life to the land. There are a variety of remote parts of the island. Galen has three distinct biomes, from the colonized plains to the south, forests dotted with ruins to the wests, and dark dense jungles of the north. Like the rest of the archipelago, it is volcanic, and this is primarily seen in the area around Embervine, Ivyhame, and Llanshara near Y'ffelon.

## Galen (lake island)
[Galen](Lore_Galen_(lake_island).md) was a legendary location of sacred worship of [Y'ffre](Lore_Y%27ffre.md). According to some tales of the [Druidic](Lore_Druid.md) oral tradition, it was an island in a lake where Y'ffre's heart could be felt beating, despite it being in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), the realm of mortals. According to the tales it was there, where ancestors of the modern Druids heard the songs and learned to tend the groves. Some of the stories claim that [the northwestern island](Lore_Galen.md) of the [Systres](Lore_Systres.md) archipelago was named after the lake island of Galen.

## Ganonah
[Ganonah](Lore_Ganonah.md) is a region of the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s plane of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). The region's clan of [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) were the ones that invaded [Kvatch](Lore_Kvatch.md) at the start of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). The Sigil Keep of Ganonah is located on the northern end of the region and is called the **Blood Feast**.

## Gardens of Flesh and Bone
The [Gardens of Flesh and Bone](Lore_Gardens_of_Flesh_and_Bone.md) are an ancient ruin and [root tunnel](Lore_Root.md) complex located just west of the town of [Passwall](Lore_Passwall.md) inside [the Fringe](Lore_Fringe.md), the walled-off region of [the Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) to which mortals are confined until they receive Lord [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s "gift" of madness and can enter the realm proper.

Like all ancient ruins found in the Isles, it is a relic of the [Predecessors](Lore_Predecessors.md), the mortal inhabitants of the realm whose civilization was wiped out in a previous [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md). By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), root tunnels had broke through into the ancient subterranean halls of the Garden.

## Garlas Malatar
[Garlas Malatar](Lore_Garlas_Malatar.md), meaning "Cavern of the High Wood" in [Ayleidoon](Lore_Ayleidoon.md), was the westernmost outpost of the [Ayleid Empire](Lore_Ayleid_Empire.md) in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). The city watched over the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), and was known for a brilliant beacon called the Guiding Light that shone above it and was visible from great distances; the Guiding Light was fueled by the light of the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) herself, and was proof of her favor.

During the fall of the Ayleid Empire, King [Narilmor](Lore_Narilmor.md) sealed Garlas Malatar to protect an artifact gifted by Meridia called the [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md), and it has been claimed that the city was ultimately destroyed not by the forces of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md), but by internal conflict or sabotage brought on by Narilmor's increasingly ruthless rule.

The city's ruins were located on a small island along the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md) of western [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), northwest of [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md), and consisted of an underground complex sometimes known as the **Depths of Malatar**. The complex also connects to an isolated cove along the northern coast known as the [Cove of the Guiding Light](Lore_Cove_of_the_Guiding_Light.md).

## Gavaudon
[Gavaudon](Lore_Gavaudon.md) is a region in central [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), within the larger region of [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md). It borders [Menevia](Lore_Menevia.md) to the west, [Wrothgar](Lore_Wrothgar.md) to the north, and [Mournoth](Lore_Mournoth.md) to the east. The region contains the town of [Wind Keep](Lore_Wind_Keep.md), the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins of [Norvulk](Lore_Norvulk_Ruins.md), as well as the enormous ruined statue called the Weeping Giant. It is the home of the [Dumontes](Lore_Dumonte.md), an old clan known for having a considerable familial connection with the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md).

Gavaudon offers rich alluvial soil due to the [Bjoulsae River](Lore_Bjoulsae_River.md) flood plains, but the threat of vampire attacks and Orc raids from the mountains weeds out all but the most hardy frontiersmen. Around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the region was considered a fiefdom.

## Lake Geir
[Lake Geir](Lore_Lake_Geir.md) is a lake in the western [Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md). [Ivarstead](Lore_Ivarstead.md) is built on its western shores. The lake is fed by [Treva River](Lore_Treva_River.md) and is drained by [Darkwater River](Lore_Darkwater_River.md). It is named after [Archmage Geirmund](Lore_Geirmund.md), a [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) wizard who died nearby in a duel with Sigdis Gauldurson, the son of [Archmage Gauldur](Lore_Gauldur.md). A tomb for the Archmage can still be found on the largest island in the lake.

## Ghostgate
[Ghostgate](Lore_Ghostgate.md) was the main gateway through the ancient [Ghostfence](Lore_Ghostfence.md) artifact to the [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) region, which prevented monsters affected by [The Blight](Lore_The_Blight.md) from leaving. Ghostgate housed a large [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and contingents of both Buoyant Armigers and Ordinators. The two towers of Ghostgate, called Dusk (west) and Dawn (east), were constructed alongside the Ghostfence after many failed campaigns to defeat [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur_(god).md) following his [2E 882](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_882) reawakening.

## Sea of Ghosts
The [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) (also known as the **Ghost Sea** or the **Northern Sea**) is the body of water that consists of the [northern coast](Lore_Northern_Coast.md) of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), the eastern coast of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md), and the southern coast of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md). It is a part of the [Padomaic Ocean](Lore_Padomaic_Ocean.md), which consists of the north and east seas of Tamriel. The Sea of Ghosts is not easy to navigate. It took some time for the people of Tamriel to explore these misty waters, which are often riddled with glaciers and icebergs. The Sea of Ghosts acquired its name from the ancient tale of Skyrim, known as *[Yngol and the Sea-Ghosts](Lore_Yngol_and_the_Sea-Ghosts.md)* which refers to the death of [Ysgramor](Lore_Ysgramor.md)'s eldest son, [Yngol](Lore_Yngol.md), at the hands of the "*Sea-Ghosts"*. The Sea of Ghosts host its own variety of animals that are not commonly found in other parts of the continent, specifically [horkers](Lore_Horker.md) and [chub loons](Lore_Chub_Loon.md).

## Giant's Tooth
[Giant's Tooth](Lore_Giant%27s_Tooth.md) is an island off the coast of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), located in the [Sea of Ghosts](Lore_Sea_of_Ghosts.md) northeast of [Winterhold](Lore_Winterhold_(city).md). It is reachable by ferry from [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md).

The island was known for the temperate environments found within Iron Tusk Cave. The humid environments within this cave allowed for the survival of a grove of [Ironwood](Lore_Ironwood.md) trees. By the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) this once common tree was rendered nearly extinct in Skyrim, no longer being found at such northern latitudes.

Circa [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the island was populated by [giants](Skyrim_Giant.md). They had set up a camp outside Iron Tusk Cave, killing the fishermen who were already encamped there. According to some accounts, the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md) journeyed to the island that year to obtain some Ironwood Fruit for use in a cure for a man in an advanced state of [ataxia](Lore_Ataxia.md). However, it is uncertain if the Dragonborn ever set foot on the island.

## Giants' Gap
[Giants' Gap](Lore_Giants%27_Gap.md) is a region in the central area of [The Pale](Lore_The_Pale.md), in the province of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It makes up the open area south of the [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) and the [Northern Coast](Lore_Northern_Coast.md), and is surrounded on many sides by the [Pale Mountains](Lore_Pale_Mountains.md). The **Yorgrim Forest** is found in the southeastern part of the Gap. The Gap blends into the [White River Valley](Lore_Tundra_Plain.md) of Whiterun.

## Gideon
*Gideon, the black city of the followers of Seth, lies near the southern tip of the Imperial Run. A cold air that has nothing to do with the time of year seems to chill your bones as you enter...*
—In-game description in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md) is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), situated in the flooded hillscape of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). Gideon is a sleepy frontier town that serves as a gateway to the greater and treacherous expanse of the province, which has a paradoxical mixture of countless cultures, from the primordial architecture dating back to the [Barsaebic Ayleids](Lore_Barsaebic_Ayleids.md) who named the city **Twyllbek**, local folklore from the time when the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md) lived in the region, to the majority population of Argonians as well as the [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) that live there contemporarily—religiously, Gideon is considered to be the "black city" of the followers of [Seth](Lore_Sethiete.md). Gideon is often visited and settled by hunters, dealers in herbs, and other professions that seek out Black Marsh's wonders. Gideon has a kleptocracy of [Archeins](Lore_Archein.md) operating within its political structure, advising the city-state's rulers and governing over the outlying region.

## Gilane
[Gilane](Lore_Gilane.md) is one of the eight major cities in the province of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), and is located on the south-central coast near the [Abecean Sea](Lore_Abecean_Sea.md), across the isthmus toward [Hew's Bane](Lore_Hew%27s_Bane.md). In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the city was the seat of power for the eponymous **Kingdom of Gilane**.

## Gil-Var-Delle
[Gil-Var-Delle](Lore_Gil-Var-Delle.md) (also known as **Gilverdale** by the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md)) is a ruined settlement located in western [Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md), in the province of [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Once a sizeable town that surrounded its [Elden Tree](Lore_Elden_Tree.md) called Lifetimber, it was known as a thriving [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) community full of life and [music](Lore_Music.md), but now, it is a desolate waste, one where no one dares to approach. The legend of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s devouring of Gil-Var-Delle has cemented itself in Bosmer culture and has been the subject of several fictional pieces that dramaticize it.

## Glacier Crawl
[Glacier Crawl](Lore_Glacier_Crawl.md) is a network of icy passages found in the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) of eastern [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), near the border of [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). It is inhabited by blue-skinned humanoids known as the [Ice Tribes](Lore_Ice_Tribes.md), and dangerous flying creatures known as [rays](Lore_Ray.md).

One of the airships that once sailed the sky to collect the seven [Star Teeth](Lore_Star_Teeth.md) crashed into the glacier. The Ice Tribes raided the ship, taking their Star Tooth and trapping the crew, who were left to die. The spirit of Captain Nym remained to haunt the ship. In [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), the [Soul of Conflict](Lore_Soul_of_Conflict.md) came in search of the Star Teeth, which were needed to fight the growing forces of shadow. The adventurer recovered the Star Tooth from the Chieftain of the Ice Tribes, and spoke with Captain Nym, whose spirit was eased by the reunification of the Star Teeth, the defeat of the Ice Tribes, and the impending battle against the [Umbra' Keth](Lore_Umbra%27_Keth.md).

## Glademist
[Glademist](Lore_Glademist.md) is an area in the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), located far northeast of the city of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md). It lends its name to three notable places: the historical **Fort Glademist**, a cave network just off the [Orange Road](Lore_Orange_Road.md) called **Glademist Cave**, and the region of **Glademist Fields**.

During the [Alliance War](Lore_Alliance_War.md) of [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Fort Glademist was an expansive and strategically important keep. It had its own farm, mine, and lumbermill, and was heavily fortified. It was initially located within the territory held by the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md). The fort changed hands many times over the course of the war. The region of Glademist Fields was found to the southwest of the fort, and was plagued by Black Dagger ambushes during the war.

The fort was later abandoned and neglected until nothing remained. In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the [Blackwood Company](Lore_Blackwood_Company.md) set up camp in Glademist Cave during an ill-fated attempt to expand out of [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md). Like all natural caves, these tunnels once served as the capillaries of [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md), pulsing with the divine essence of the [Aedra](Lore_Aedra.md). After clearing out the resident monsters, the Company heavily fortified the caves. After his expulsion from the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md), [Modryn Oreyn](Lore_Modryn_Oreyn.md) turned to unorthodox tactics in his campaign to discredit the Blackwood Company. His first step was kidnapping one of their leaders, Ajum-Kajin, who had taken up residence in Glademist. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) entered the caves, killing the Company guards and bringing Ajum-Kajin back to Chorrol for interrogation. The caves were subsequently reclaimed by monsters from the wilderness.

## Gladesong Arboretum
[Gladesong Arboretum](Lore_Gladesong_Arboretum.md), also known as **Druidic Grove** was a [druidic](Lore_Druids.md) settlement located south of [Clohaigh](Lore_Clohaigh.md) in the mountainous region of the north-central part of the island of [Galen](Lore_Galen.md). The tranquil ambiance of the arboretum was complemented by the gentle sound of a waterfall, harmonizing with the soft melody of the wind as it whispered through the towering trees. Carved stone shelters offered cozy living quarters, each opening onto breathtaking vistas. The flora surrounding the settlement exhibited a rich diversity, featuring plants such as daylily, gorse, begonia, dogwood, beech, and fern.

## Glenbridge
[Glenbridge](Lore_Glenbridge.md) is a settlement located in the southeast hills of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), in the province of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). Glenbridge is built around a ruined [xanmeer](Lore_Xanmeer.md) dedicated to [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md), an ancient deity of the [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) people. The [Nisswo](Lore_Nisswo.md) of Glenbridge believe that the xanmeer's dilapidated state is the work of their god, whose core being is chaos and change. Many visitors from all over [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), Argonians or outsiders come to the shrine to either worship Sithis or marvel at the architecture.

## Glenpoint
[Glenpoint](Lore_Glenpoint.md) is an inland march in the province of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), specifically found on the mainland portion of the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region. Despite its proximity to the Kingdom of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) and the urban Iliac Bay, Glenpoint is a remote region, split between the large mountain that encompasses the southeast and the forests of High Rock. Most of the region is found within a wooded [highlands](Lore_Highland_Bretons.md) biome. The [Knights of the Owl](Lore_Knights_of_the_Owl.md) is the march's knightly order and they are sworn to protect both the royal family and their country.

## Glenumbra
[Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) is the western peninsula of [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) separating the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) from the [Eltheric Ocean](Lore_Eltheric_Ocean.md). The peninsula's geography is a mix of rugged woodland and fetid swamps. [Snag-weevils](Lore_Snag-Weevil.md) have been known to plague the area, and [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) are particularly prevalent. The famed city of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall.md) is found along the coast of the southern region of [Daenia](Lore_Daenia.md). The forests of Daenia give way to the [Cambray Hills](Lore_Cambray_Hills.md), the central region of Glenumbra, which is home to the city of [Camlorn](Lore_Camlorn.md) and the marshland of Hag Fen. Farther north is [King's Guard](Lore_King%27s_Guard.md), which takes its name from the mountain range which separates Glenumbra from [Stormhaven](Lore_Stormhaven.md) and [Rivenspire](Lore_Rivenspire.md). Farwatch is a coastal region between Daggerfall and Camlorn. Numerous other settlements are scattered throughout Glenumbra, such as Crosswych, a town in eastern King's Guard along the land route to and from Daggerfall. The peninsula is home to [Cath Bedraud](Lore_Cath_Bedraud.md), the great cemetery where monarchs of High Rock are interred, as well as [Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md), the site of the famous [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md).

## Glenumbra Moors
[Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Glenumbra_Moors.md) is a small area in central [Glenumbra](Lore_Glenumbra.md) in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), best known for being the site of the famous [Battle of Glenumbra Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbra_Moors.md).

The battle took place in [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482) between the [Alessian Order](Lore_Alessian_Order.md) and the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) forces. Powerful individuals fought on both sides: [Aiden](Lore_Aiden_Direnni.md) and [Raven Direnni](Lore_Raven_Direnni.md), King [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and King [Laloriaran Dynar](Lore_Laloriaran_Dynar.md) of [Nenalata](Lore_Nenalata.md) all fought on the Direnni side, whereas the mighty werewolf [Faolchu](Lore_Faolchu.md) fought on the Alessian side. The Alessian Order lost the battle, although the losses suffered by the Direnni are said to have led to their eventual downfall.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), after [Angof](Lore_Angof.md) the Gravesinger raised Faolchu, [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) visited the battle site in search of a way to kill him, and traveled back in time to experience a part of the battle with the help of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md).

## Glimmertarn

It was primarily inhabited by the members of the [Stonelore Circle](Lore_Stonelore_Circle.md), though it also housed some members of the [Eldertide Circle](Lore_Eldertide_Circle.md).

## Gloommire
[Gloommire](Lore_Gloommire.md) (also spelled **Gloomire**) is a swampy region on the southern side of [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md), in the province of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md). It borders the Blackwood Bog to the north and the Niben Forest to the west. The city of [Gideon](Lore_Gideon.md), originally inhabited by the [Kothringi](Lore_Kothringi.md), can be found here. Notable locations include [Glenbridge](Lore_Glenbridge.md), [Rockgrove](Lore_Rockgrove.md), [Stonewastes](Lore_Stonewastes.md), and [Xi-Tsei](Lore_Xi-Tsei.md).

## Gnaar Mok
[Gnaar Mok](Lore_Gnaar_Mok.md) was a financially deprived fishing village located on an island in the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) region of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). The local [Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md) manor dominated the village; the [Thieves Guild](Lore_Thieves_Guild.md) also operated out of a dilapidated hut in town. The road to Gnaar Mok from [Ald'ruhn](Lore_Ald%27ruhn.md) was a long and exceptionally unpleasant walk, made perilous by the lack of trails or marked routes and casual travelers being ill-equipped for the attacks of wild beasts and brigands, making it preferable for them to instead keep to the roads and travel services.

During the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Gnaar Mok was not a settlement but rather the site of a **Arenim Manor**, consisting of a mansion and a warehouse; however, an area known as Gnaar Mok was a stop for [Silt Strider](Lore_Silt_Strider.md) caravaneers during this time. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Councilor Dolvara of [House Redoran](Lore_House_Redoran.md) resided in the manor, and was later assassinated in it by the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md).

In [3E 410](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_410), Sixth House bases were founded near Gnaar Mok, with its operatives exploiting smuggler organizations and communications to spread their influence among victims unbalanced by [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur.md)'s dream sendings. By [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), a number of huts had been built around the manor, turning it into the settlement of Gnaar Mok. That year the [Boots of Blinding Speed](Lore_Boots_of_Blinding_Speed.md) were seen in the possession of a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) by the name of Peminie, who asked the [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md) for safe passage to Gnaar Mok. The Nerevarine complied, and received the boots upon leading her there. The Nerevarine was then informed by locals that Peminie was in fact a wanted felon; upon interrogation, she became hostile and had to be killed.

## Gnisis

## Grahtwood
[Grahtwood](Lore_Grahtwood.md), previously known as the **Kingdom of Grahtwood**, is a large region which forms southeastern [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md). Much of it is covered in impenetrable jungle, though a western portion of the region was devastated by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) circa [1E 2920](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2920) and has never recovered. The [Tarlain Heights](Lore_Tarlain_Heights.md) lie west of [Green Hall](Lore_Green_Hall.md), and the coastal region south of both areas is known as Longcoast. Historically, the walking city [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) settled in Grahtwood during the winter.

Many races besides the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) have made Grahtwood their home. [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) strongholds are not unheard of, and numerous [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruins dot the landscape. The Bosmer have allowed both [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) and [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md) to establish autonomous settlements in their land. In fact, men have been coming from [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) to seek great wealth from Grahtwood's resources since the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md). Non-native Bosmer who return to their ancestral homeland are frequently drawn to Grahtwood. As a result, relations between them and native Bosmer are particularly tense in the region, and this has often led to conflict. In [2E 489](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_489), the Blacksap Movement was founded with the aim of formalizing the Bosmeri approach to the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), and thereby prevent any further violent "misunderstandings" between the two groups.

## Grazelands
The [Grazelands](Lore_Grazelands.md) (also called **Sunna Guradan**) were the pastoral heartland of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md). On the northeastern corner of the island, these grasslands extended from the ancient stronghold of Falensarano north to the coast above [Tel Vos](Lore_Tel_Vos.md). To the east, the Grazelands stretched to the coast and the islands of [Zafirbel Bay](Lore_Zafirbel_Bay.md), facing the [Telvanni Peninsula](Lore_Telvanni_Peninsula.md). To the west, it was bounded by the forbidding steepness of the mountains of the [Ashlands](Lore_Ashlands.md).

The flora of the Grazelands was dominated by [wickwheat](Lore_Wickwheat.md), and this is one of the few areas where it grew. There were trees spread sporadically around the landscape. [Stoneflower](Lore_Stoneflower.md) was the most frequently flowering plant. It was home to wild guar, alit and shalk. The south was dominated by [hackle-lo](Lore_Hackle-Lo.md) shrubs, whose leaves could be found across the island. It was also a region where, due to its scarce population, it was not uncommon to see [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), including [golden saints](Lore_Aureal.md) and [ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md), just walking around in the open.

Much of Grazelands was uninhabited and thus belonged to the Temple. The population of the Grazelands consisted of mostly nomadic [Ashlanders](Lore_Ashlanders.md). Other inhabitants included those who lived in Tel Vos and the nearby town of [Vos](Lore_Vos.md), along with Master Aryon in the tower of [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md).

Ashlander settlements in the area were the [Ahemmusa Camp](Lore_Ahemmusa_Camp.md) in the north and the [Zainab Camp](Lore_Zainab_Camp.md) to the south. The presence of Tel Vos and the proximity to the Telvanni homeland meant that this House was the most likely to make future claims in the region.

## Greymoor
**Fort Greymoor**, formerly known as **Black Moor** and **Darkmoor**,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> is a partially ruined fortified settlement that dominates the Western Plains of [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md). The central fort (originally called Black Moor Keep) guards a strategic crossroad which connects Whiterun to [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), and [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md). A [lone mountain](Lore_Lone_Mountain.md) lies to the west of the imposing fortress. Black Moor Keep was in existence since at least [2E 577](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_577), when it was occupied by Arkasis the Mad Alchemist while he experimented on the citizens of Whiterun, transforming them into [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md) using [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) concoctions. When his actions were discovered, Arkasis was chased back to the keep by an angry mob and was forced to flee the hold. These events later went on to be dramatized and reimagined in an [Investigator Vale](Lore_Investigator_Vale.md) novel known as *The Hounds of Black Moor*.

In the early years of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Darkmoor was ruled by Count [Sven Advensen](Lore_Sven_Advensen.md) and his wife. Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) granted Sven this title and fiefdom upon his retirement from the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md). The previous rulers there had been distant cousins to the new Count and his wife, Lady Inga. The couple famously became the guardians of Princess [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md), and the future Queen of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) was raised in the small Skyrim village, unaware of her birthright.

During the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the village of Black Moor was an active settlement, being ruled by Lord Torben. It had a rivalry with [North Keep](Lore_North_Keep.md). It was neighbored by several settlements, including [Riverwood](Lore_Riverwood.md) to the southeast, and [Whiterun](Lore_Whiterun.md) to the east.

By [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the ruins of Fort Greymoor had been ravaged by war, and collapsed parts of the stone walls were fortified with wooden barricades. The occupants changed regularly, with [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md), [vampires](Lore_Vampire.md) and bandits all having occupied the ruins. During the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Lore_Stormcloak_Rebellion.md), the fort served as a point of contention between the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) and the [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md), with both sides struggling for control. The [Staff of Hasedoki](Lore_Staff_of_Hasedoki.md) was smuggled into [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) the same year, and the Gilgar family dispatched Modig Gilgar to recover the staff, who was ambushed by smugglers intending to rob him at the road next to Fort Greymoor. He quickly dispatched them, with the Last Dragonborn arriving to the scene shortly after, being attacked by a wary Modig, whom they killed.

## Greymoor Caverns
**Greymoor Caverns** is an area within the underground realm of [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md), found beneath the holds of [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), and [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). The ancient fortress of [Greymoor Keep](Lore_Greymoor_Keep.md) lies beneath the city of [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). Throughout history, Greymoor Keep has often been traded between the hands of many rulers. During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md), it came into possession of [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md), a [Vampire Lord](Lore_Vampire_Lord.md) and leader of the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).

## Greymoor Keep
[Greymoor Keep](Lore_Greymoor_Keep.md) is a large [vampiric](Lore_Vampire.md) castle located in [Blackreach](Lore_Blackreach.md), the legendary subterranean realm of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md). It has stood over the underground since ancient history, long before the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) built their cities here. Some legends even claim that the first vampires built it and that great power resides in its stone. The keep is one of Blackreach's largest structures. It reaches to its ceiling and is even built directly underneath [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md). Not only is it the namesake of the [Greymoor Caverns](Lore_Greymoor_Caverns.md), the western section of Blackreach that runs under [Haafingar](Lore_Haafingar.md), [Hjaalmarch](Lore_Hjaalmarch.md), and [Karthald](Lore_Karthald.md), but also its northern region also called **Greymoor Cavern**.
