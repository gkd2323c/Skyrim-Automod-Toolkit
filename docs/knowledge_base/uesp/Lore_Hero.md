# Hero

— [Zurin Arctus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zurin_Arctus), the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/GEN-misc-30th_Anniversary.jpg/200px-GEN-misc-30th_Anniversary.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-30th_Anniversary.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:GEN-misc-30th_Anniversary.jpg) Several Heroes side-by-side A **Hero**<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Oblivion Heaven Stones-1)</sup> or **Heroine**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> sometimes referred to as a **Prisoner**<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CD-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SSATS-4)</sup> or **Prisoner Unbound**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TNC-5)</sup> is a mortal blessed (and cursed, from another point of view) with a special [fate](Lore_Fate.md) and the ability to rule their own destiny. They are described as **doom-driven**.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRTsun-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-7)</sup> Heroes are said to be uniquely able to forge their own fate.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-LTW-8)</sup> Some believe that Heroes are blessed by [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md).<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Paarthurnax-7)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBDoomstones-9)</sup> Others believe Heroes are produced by [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s [Heart](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Heart_of_Lorkhan), and that the Shade of [Shezarr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shezarr) shrouds their doom.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBDoomstones-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-CCAP-10)</sup> Some believe them to be linked to [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia)'s will.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TNC-5)</sup> However, Ithelia herself appeared stunned by the true nature of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), recognizing them as pathwalker, something she believed impossible for mortals.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Ithelia-11)</sup> Heroes are closely related to the prophecies revealed in the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md), but are not bound by them. They often grow to become far more powerful than most other mortals.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBDoomstones-9)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-SIDyus-12)</sup> The [Nibenese](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenese) of eastern Cyrodiil developed the [Cult of Heroes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cult_of_Heroes), among many others.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1-13)</sup>

## Eternal Champion
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/AR-sprite-HORSE.gif/200px-AR-sprite-HORSE.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-sprite-HORSE.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-sprite-HORSE.gif) The Eternal Champion on horseback *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls: Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* The [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md), purportedly named **[Talin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Talin)** (b. [3E 370](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_370)), was an otherwise unknown hero best known for ending the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md) in [3E 399](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_399) by bringing together the pieces of the [Staff of Chaos](Lore_Staff_of_Chaos.md), killing the impostor [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), and rescuing the true emperor, [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), and general of the [Imperial Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Guard), [Talin Warhaft](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Warhaft), from a [dimension of Tharn's choosing](Lore_Oblivion.md) where they had been imprisoned for ten years.

## The Agent
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/DF-misc-Anniversary_Art_25_%28The_Agent%29.jpg/200px-DF-misc-Anniversary_Art_25_%28The_Agent%29.jpg)](File_DF-misc-Anniversary_Art_25_(The_Agent).jpg.md) [](File_DF-misc-Anniversary_Art_25_(The_Agent).jpg.md) The Agent *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls II: Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* [The Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md), also known as the **Hero of [Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md)**, (b. [3E 375](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_375)), was a [Blade](Lore_Blades.md) sent by [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_VII.md) to the [Kingdom of Daggerfall](Lore_Daggerfall_(kingdom).md) to put to rest the [spirit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) of [King Lysandus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lysandus), and on a lesser request to find and destroy a letter of a "sentimental and personal nature" sent by the emperor to the then-Queen of Daggerfall, [Mynisera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mynisera), several years prior.

The Agent would end up becoming a pivotal figure of the complex political battles being fought in [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay), eventually finding themselves caught in a power struggle between the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), the kingdoms of [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), [Orsinium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nova_Orsinium), and Daggerfall, the [King of Worms](Lore_Mannimarco.md), and the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md), each coveting the [Totem of Tiber Septim](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Totem_of_Tiber_Septim), the [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) that allows control over the great [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md), for themselves.

While it ultimately remains unknown who the Agent gave the Totem of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) to, its activation in [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417) would cause the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md) and reshape the landscape, both politically and literally, of the Iliac. They disappeared immediately after the Warp, with the Blades being unable to contact them despite their efforts to resolve the [paradoxical and contradictory nature](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) of the event. One of the outcomes of the Warp in the West resulted in the Agent dying by the Numidium's hand when activating it for themselves.

[3E 372](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_372)) was the hero sent to the [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) in [3E 398](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_398) after placing second in the Imperial Martial Arts competition that determined the next [Imperial Guard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Guard) candidates to be tested there. The Apprentice found the citadel captured by the legions of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), and fought through powerful clans of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) and dealt with their leaders to rescue their fellow trainee, who may have been either Josian Kaid or Vatasha Trenelle, and banish Dagon with the [Sword of the Moon Reiver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sword_of_the_Moon_Reiver), which was acquired from the Prince's paramour, Si'ess the [Dark Seducer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mazken).

## Cyrus
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d0/RG-render-Cyrus_02.gif/200px-RG-render-Cyrus_02.gif)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-render-Cyrus_02.gif) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-render-Cyrus_02.gif) Cyrus wielding [his sabre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrus%27_Sabre) *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls Adventures: Redguard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Redguard:Redguard)* [Cyrus the Redguard](Lore_Cyrus.md), also known as **Cyrus the Restless**, and **Sura**, **Sura of the Bend'r-Mahk *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]***, or **Sura-do-Hega** in [Yoku](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Yoku), (b. [2E 831](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_831)) was a hero best known for leading the [Stros M'Kai Uprising](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Stros_M%27Kai_Uprising) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864). Having spent the early years of his career under the wing of the [Cyro-Nordic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyro-Nord) pirate captain [Tobias](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tobias), Cyrus traveled to the island of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) in search of his missing sister, [Iszara](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iszara), where he ended up leading his fellow [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) in a rebellion against the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) alongside her.

## Nerevarine
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a3/MW-misc-Anniversary_Art.jpg/200px-MW-misc-Anniversary_Art.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Anniversary_Art.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-misc-Anniversary_Art.jpg) Anniversary artwork featuring the Nerevarine (center) *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls III: Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind)* The [Nerevarine](Lore_Nerevarine.md), the prophesied **Incarnate**, is the alleged<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> [reincarnation](Lore_Reincarnation.md) of [Chimeri](Lore_Chimer.md) warlord [Indoril Nerevar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nerevar). The Nerevarine arrived in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427) by the order of [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md), having been sent to [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) as a prisoner of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). Their arrival was directly linked to [a prophecy](Lore_Nerevarine_Prophecy.md) which dictated that Lord Indoril Nerevar would be reincarnated and return to Morrowind to defeat [Dagoth Ur](Lore_Dagoth_Ur_(god).md) and restore the glory of [Resdayn](Lore_Resdayn.md). The hero fulfilled the prophecy, ending [the Blight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Blight). In recognition of this, they were also known as **Nerevar-Born-Again**, **Nerevar Reborn**, **Starkborn**, **Moon-and-Star Reborn**, **Hortator**, **Mourner of the Tribe Unmourned**, **Redeemer of the False Gods**, and **Blodskaal**.

As destiny unfolded, they battled against [living gods](Lore_Tribunal.md), and then, in the frozen wastes of [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md), against the champions of the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). Rumors swirled at the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) that the Nerevarine went on an expedition to [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md) and has not been heard from since.

## Hero of Dawnstar
*Hero of [The Elder Scrolls Travels: Dawnstar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Dawnstar:Dawnstar)* The **Hero of Dawnstar** arrived at the exile post of [Dawnstar](Lore_Dawnstar.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427),<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-UOLGill-14)</sup> while it was besieged by the [Ice Tribes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ice_Tribes) and betrayed by someone from within. Unable to ferret out who of his four champions was in league with the enemy, Governor [Cyril Vinticae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyril_Vinticae) had banished them all, tasking the hero with singling out the traitor and saving Dawnstar. After investigating the champions, they revealed the traitor and returned to Dawnstar with the three loyal champions to defend from the ensuing assault. With the power of the [Starfrost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Starfrost), the hero helped slay the traitor's forces and finally emerged victorious with the defeat of the [Gehenoth Thriceborn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gehenoth).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Dawnstar-16)</sup>

## Last Dragonborn
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4f/SR-npc-Dragonborn.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Dragonborn.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dragonborn.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Dragonborn.jpg) The Last Dragonborn<sup>[[Note 1]](#intnote_General)</sup> *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)* The [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Last_Dragonborn.md), also simply known as the **[Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md)** or **Dovahkiin**, and referred to as **[Ysmir](Lore_Ysmir.md), Dragon of the North**, **Stormcrown**, or **Qahnaarin**, is the hero foretold in the ancient [Prophecy of the Dragonborn](Lore_Prophecy_of_the_Dragonborn.md) inscribed on [Alduin's Wall](Lore_Alduin%27s_Wall.md). In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Dragonborn arrived in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and, with the assistance of the last of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) and the [Greybeards](Lore_Greybeards.md), fulfilled their destiny by ending the renewed [dragon threat](Lore_Return_of_the_Dragons.md) and slaying [Alduin the World-Eater](Lore_Alduin.md). That same year, the Dragonborn also recovered lost [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md) to end a [vampiric plot to permanently blot out the sun](Lore_Tyranny_of_the_Sun.md), and journeyed to [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md) to stop the return of [Miraak](Lore_Miraak.md), the First Dragonborn.

## The Vestige
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/ON-misc-Soul.jpg/150px-ON-misc-Soul.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Soul.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Soul.jpg) The Vestige's soul *Hero of [Elder Scrolls Online](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online)* [The Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), known as the **Soulless One** and by a plethora of other names, was a [Soul-Shriven](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Soul-Shriven) hero of prophecy. As a mortal, they were sacrificed to [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) by [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) as part of a dark ritual in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). They may have been chosen by the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) to be a savior to the many corners of Tamriel, with many crises such as the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), the schemes of [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), and the Dragons that ravaged [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) happening in such a short amount of time. It is possible that they momentarily ruled Tamriel as a so-called "[Battlefield Emperor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Battlefield_Emperors)", although the legitimacy of their rule would be debatable.

## Forgotten Hero
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/LG-quest-Finale_03.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Finale_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Finale_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Finale_03.jpg) The Forgotten Hero on horseback *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls: Legends](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Legends)* The **[Forgotten Hero](Lore_Forgotten_Hero.md)** is an individual whose adventures are told by a moth priest named Kellen. The hero was captured by a [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora), Reive, and managed to escape with a fellow captive, a [Blade](Lore_Blades.md) named Tyr during the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md). They discovered that Lord [Naarifin](Lore_Naarifin.md) tried to bring about a prophecy known as the Culling, for which he intended to sacrifice all of the Imperial City's inhabitants. With the help of Tyr's friend Laaneth and a smuggler named Swims-At-Night, they found the [Emperor](Lore_Titus_Mede_II.md), who tasked them with infiltrating the Thalmor-controlled [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) and discovering how the Thalmor were predicting the Legion's actions. The four of them discovered that Naarifin had been using the Orb of Vaermina to scry on the Legion's actions, though Naarifin caught them and had Rieve hold Tyr hostage, forcing the hero to choose between Tyr's life or the orb. The hero's choice is not known, but Rieve was killed in the ensuing battle. After a failed assassination attempt left the emperor too wounded to ride into battle himself, the hero disguised themself in the Emperor's armor, and led an attack on the Imperial City to defeat Naarifin.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Forgotten-19)</sup>

The Forgotten Hero also played a role in the fall of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md) during [4E 188](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_188). The hero was recruited by a man named Uther Nere, who asked them to infiltrate the Dark Brotherhood and kill the listener, Alisanne Dupre, under the guise of avenging his family, who Uther claimed were killed by the Dark Brotherhood. In reality, Alisanne was his daughter, who he wanted dead as the sole witness to his murder of his wife, Alisanne's mother. The hero aided the citizens of Wayrest, captured a traitor in the Falkreath Sanctuary, and was present when Alisanne was killed in Bravil. It is unconfirmed whether the hero decided to continue aiding Uther in killing Alisanne, or turn against him in an attempt to protect Alisanne after learning the truth.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-FOTDB-20)</sup>

In [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), the Forgotten Hero was recruited by Swims-At-Night to locate [Barilzar's Mazed Band](Lore_Barilzar%27s_Mazed_Band.md). They raced against a gang of rival treasure hunters, led by Aldora the Daring and hired by a disguised Mecinar, a former apprentice of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md). After finding the band, the pair was ambushed by the treasure hunters and Swims-At-Night was transported with them into the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md). The hero enlisted the help of Laaneth, uncovered a rogue sorcerer in the College of Winterhold, and the two of them were transported to the Clockwork City by [Alfe Fyr](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alfe_Fyr). Here, the hero met a group of Sotha Sil's worshippers led by Galyn the Shelterer, and uncovered Mecinar's plans to achieve godhood with Sotha Sil's reconstruction of Lorkhan's Heart. Attempting to stop Mecinar, the hero obtained replicas of Wraithguard, Sunder, and Keening created by Sotha Sil. During their battle with Mecinar, the hero used these tools to either harness the power of the Heart for themself, thus preserving the city & the worshippers but being destroyed in the process; or destroyed the Heart, removing Mecinar's powers but destroying the Heart & city in the process, forcing the worshippers to attempt to relocate. Though it is unknown which of these accounts is true, it is known that Mecinar was stopped either way.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-RTCC-21)</sup>

## Talym Rend
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/30/LG-npc-Talym_Rend.png/150px-LG-npc-Talym_Rend.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-npc-Talym_Rend.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-npc-Talym_Rend.png) Talym *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls: Legends: Isle of Madness](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Legends:Isle_of_Madness)* Also known as the **Conqueror of Madness**, [Talym Rend](Lore_Talym_Rend.md) was a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) spy for the Empire who journeyed to the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md) in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md) and descended into madness.

In seeking to forget the painful memories of his involvement in the [Worm Cult](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Worm_Cult)'s ritual sacrifice of his son Novos, Talym Rend struck a deal with the Daedric Prince Sheogorath, who wanted three souls driven mad in return. Sheogorath manipulated his mind for "motivation" and caused him to think he was fighting to cure his son of his madness instead.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SDTFBIOM-22)</sup> When Talym completed this task, he returned home only to realize that his son had been dead the entire time.

Sheogorath denied Talym another audience with him when he returned to his realm, causing his unanswered questions to eat away at his sanity as he wandered endlessly around the Shivering Isles. Eventually, he regained a semblance of composure and journeyed to Knifepoint Hollow. There, Talym was informed by [Dyus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dyus), the librarian of [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag), that he might gain an audience with Sheogorath if he retrieved an artifact of Jyggalag. Sheogorath granted Talym an audience when he retrieved the [Sword of Jyggalag](Lore_Sword_of_Jyggalag.md), and used the [Memory Wand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Memory_Wand) on him to reveal Talym's original intent and memories. Sheogorath mocked Talym, and in his anger, Talym turned the Memory Wand against Sheogorath and forced him to recall his memories of his previous incarnation: the Daedric Prince of Order, Jyggalag. The memories pained Sheogorath, who pleaded with Talym to stop. He fulfilled Talym's wishes and would leave him and his companions free from his grasp. Talym returned home and was last seen making preparations for getting revenge on the Worm Cult for his son's death.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-IOM-23)</sup>

## Warrior
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b5/BL-misc-Events.png/150px-BL-misc-Events.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Events.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-misc-Events.png) The Warrior *Hero of [The Elder Scrolls: Blades](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Blades:Blades)* The **[Warrior](Lore_The_Warrior_(person).md)**<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-24)</sup> was born in the small town of [Rivercrest](Lore_Rivercrest.md)<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-25)</sup> in Cyrodiil,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BLDestane-26)</sup> part of a kingdom ruled by the [Bloodfall Queen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bloodfall_Queen).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-27)</sup> Trained in combat by Henrik Seven-Swords since a young age,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-28)</sup> the Warrior left to become a member of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md), but was forced to go into hiding after the end of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md) in [4E 175](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_175) when the organization was formally disbanded and its members systematically hunted down by the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-29)</sup> The Warrior returned to Rivercrest circa [4E 180](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_180)<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-30)</sup> only to find it ablaze and reduced to rubble. The destruction of the town's Elder Statue caused an undead [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) Sorcerer-King named [Celemaril Light-Bringer](Lore_Celemaril_Light-Bringer.md) to be released from his ancient bindings in [the Abyss](Lore_The_Abyss.md),<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-31)</sup> and the Warrior undertook to rebuild the town and save all of Tamriel from the reawakened Ayleid [Lich](Lore_Lich.md).

## Sheathed Blades
*Heroes of [Skyrim — The Adventure Game](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim TAG:Skyrim_-_The_Adventure_Game)* [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-icon-Blades.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-icon-Blades.png) Blades emblem The **Sheathed Blades**<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Adventure Game-32)</sup> were a small party of the [Order of Blades](Lore_Blades.md) who worked against the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) interests in [Skyrim Province](Lore_Skyrim.md) before the [White-Gold Concordat](Lore_White-Gold_Concordat.md) was signed. Before they could succeed in their mission, their Order was outlawed and all members were open for hunting season by the Thalmor.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Adventure Game-32)</sup> The Sheathed Blades survived the initial purges and went into hiding, before emerging fifteen years later during the Skyrim Civil War, and unraveling the [Chosen of Boethiah](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Chosen_of_Boethiah) cult that had been enflaming cultural tensions between the [Imperials](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Stormcloaks](Lore_Stormcloaks.md).<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Adventure Game-32)</sup> They later faced off and slew the Dragon, [Grahkrindrog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Grahkrindrog), who had been the size of a palace and terrorized Skyrim for hundreds of years in the distant past.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Adventure Game-32)</sup>

## Dynasty of Odar the Brave
*Heroes of [The Elder Scrolls: Castles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Castles:Castles)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/CT-scene-Intro_03.png/200px-CT-scene-Intro_03.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-scene-Intro_03.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:CT-scene-Intro_03.png) Odar the Brave [Odar the Brave](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Odar_the_Brave) was a [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) king of unspecified origin, who was renowned for defeating an army of [Ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre), led by an Ogre king, who plagued his land before being raised to kingship. Being gifted his kingdom by the [Emperor](Lore_Emperor.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) themselves. Though he was soon assassinated,<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Castles Event-33)</sup> his dynasty went on for generations. With his kingdom performing many great feats, such as slaying [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md), stopping villains such as the [Witch of the Black Forest](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morga), and affecting the outcome of various conflicts.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Castles Rulings-34)</sup>

## Hero of the Outer Watch
*Hero/es of [The Elder Scrolls: Betrayal of the Second Era](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Betrayal:Betrayal_of_the_Second_Era)* [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a1/BT-misc-Outer_Watch.png/150px-BT-misc-Outer_Watch.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-misc-Outer_Watch.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BT-misc-Outer_Watch.png) Outer Watch Banner The Hero of the Outer Watch was a hero (or possibly a group of them) active during the Second Era synchronously with other events such as the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). They started their journey as a prisoner freed, and possibly recruited by, the Outer Watch to fight against the Bosmer mage [Deslandra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deslandra),<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-BOTSER-35)</sup><sup>:92</sup> and in journeying throughout Tamriel were essential to putting an end to her schemes, preventing a calamity known as the Great Nullification.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-36)</sup><sup>:44</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Bot SEEvents-37)</sup>

## Notes
- Aside from the known, titled Heroes, there are several who have not appeared but have been alluded to. [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md), who had advanced knowledge of Prisoners, claimed the Vestige was one of very few heroes he had met.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-CD-3)</sup> [Tsun](Lore_Tsun.md) once reminisced of battling at least one doom-driven [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md) hero before the coming of the Last Dragonborn.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SRTsun-6)</sup> Several otherwise unnamed [Heroes of Sovngarde](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Hero_of_Sovngarde) appear in *Skyrim*, four of whom are Dragonborn, but it is unknown if these are true Heroes or heroes in the general sense.
- Following references to the Eternal Champion as Talin in *Arena*, it later became a policy of [Bethesda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Bethesda) to avoid canonizing the identities of player characters (including the Eternal Champion), so as to not cheapen the player's own personal experiences in the games.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-DGI-38)</sup>
- [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo) is frequently used as a placeholder name for many of the player characters in pre-release or example gameplay.
