Uriel Septim IV (born **Uriel [Lariat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lariat)**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup> was the fifteenth [emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) of the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) and [Septim Dynasty](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md), reigning for forty-five years, from [3E 202](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_202) to [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247). He was preceded by his older brother, [Cassynder](Lore_Cassynder.md), and was succeeded by [Cephorus II](Lore_Cephorus_II.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup>

## Biography
Uriel Lariat was the son of Empress [Katariah](Lore_Katariah.md) and her Imperial consort, a [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) nobleman named [Gallivere Lariat](Lore_Gallivere_Lariat.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MOP-3)</sup> Uriel had an older half-brother, Cassynder, whose father was [Pelagius III](Lore_Pelagius_III.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MOP-3)</sup> In [3E 193](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_193), Cassynder, the King of [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest_(kingdom).md), was in poor health, so he abdicated in favor of Uriel, whom Cassynder had legally adopted.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup> In [3E 200](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_200), Katariah was killed in a skirmish in [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md), and Cassynder became Emperor of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MOP-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTET-4)</sup>

In [3E 202](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_202), Cassynder died childless, so Uriel left the Kingdom of Wayrest to assume the Ruby Throne as Uriel Septim IV.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTET-4)</sup> Uriel IV did not possess the dynamism of his mother, and his forty-five-year reign was a difficult one and a hotbed of sedition.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup> The [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) did not accept the Emperor as a blood descendant of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md), despite the [Lariat family](Lore_Lariat_Family.md) being distant cousins of the [Septims](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) and Uriel IV being a legally adopted member of the Septim family.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup> The Elder Council and the people of Tamriel reportedly viewed Uriel IV as a bastard child of Katariah.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BHOTE2-1)</sup>

Uriel IV and the Elder Council were in conflict with one another throughout his reign, but time and time again, the council won their battles with the Emperor. The Elder Council assumed much responsibility during the reigns of Katariah and Cassynder, and a strong-willed monarch like Uriel IV found it impossible to gain their fealty. Since the days of [Pelagius II](Lore_Pelagius_II.md), the Elder Council had consisted of the wealthiest men and women in the Empire, and the power they wielded was overwhelming.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup> During his reign in [3E 246](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_246), the Elder Council, in an attempt to clear up some questions of property rights in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and discourage absent and foreign landlords, decreed that any man without a liege who occupied a castle for more than three months would be granted the rights and titles of that estate.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TRG-5)</sup>

Uriel IV died in [3E 247](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_247).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TTET-4)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DC-6)</sup> Uriel IV designated his son [Andorak](Lore_Andorak_Septim.md) as his successor, but the Elder Council voted to disinherit Andorak, and instead they proclaimed Uriel IV's cousin [Cephorus](Lore_Cephorus_II.md) emperor.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE3-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DC-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-TFU-7)</sup>
