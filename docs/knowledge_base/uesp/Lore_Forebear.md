# Forebears

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1c/RG-misc-Forebears_Insignia_02.jpg/200px-RG-misc-Forebears_Insignia_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Forebears_Insignia_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-misc-Forebears_Insignia_02.jpg) Symbol of the Forebears The Forebears is the name given to a social and political group of the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md). The Warrior Wave of [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md), or first invasions of [Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) to reach Tamriel, are now known as the Forebears. They cleared the way for their rulers, the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md), and their faction, which is now known as the [Crowns](Lore_Crowns.md). When the Na-Totambu and the Yokudan nobility came later, [Razul](Lore_Razul.md) made himself Yokeda to cement the power of the Ra Gada, with some calling Yokeda Razul the first Forebear.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ra G-1)</sup> Forebears tend to be more nomadic in nature, as many of them work as traders and merchants throughout [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md), which gives them the most experience when dealing with foreigners.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIEGt H-2)</sup> Their symbol of depicts three swords whose blades are crossed and tied together with a golden knot, with a small cape attached to them,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGIntroduction-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RGHunding-4)</sup> while their uniform colors are red and yellow.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TOOC-5)</sup> Some of the major Forebear strongholds include [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel_(kingdom).md), [Rihad](Lore_Rihad.md), and [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

One of the major differences between the Forebears, as compared to the Crowns, is that they worship the same pantheon as the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) and [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) and take a more liberal approach to tradition and religion.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-VOFTFB-7)</sup> This is one of the biggest factors in why the two don't get along, as the Crowns believe the Forebears engage in what is considered "outrageous, non-traditional practices." For example, the Forebears are believed to secretly support the pariah [Ash'abah](Lore_Ash%27abah.md) tribe in their efforts to cleanse the stench of necromancy and undeath, and will even call upon them for aid. The Crowns view this as a major affront to their religious practices, as those who strike down an ancestor are considered unclean and cursed.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Sa Mit A-8)</sup> The Forebears worship of Imperial deities [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md), rather than the traditional gods of [Ruptga](Lore_Ruptga.md), [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), and [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), plus their willingness to engage with Tamrielic cultures outside their own, is heavily despised by the Crowns faction. This contributes to the hatred the Crowns and Forebears have for one another.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ABo OH-9)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIEGt H-2)</sup>

The Crowns and Forebears represented the two halves of a highly stratified [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md) and fought a bitter civil war during the [Common Era](Lore_Common_Era.md). With the Crowns nearing victory, the Forebears made an alliance with the Empire, allowing them to capture the province;<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-RGIntroduction-3)</sup> but when it became apparent they were being used, the Forebears joined the Crowns in launching a double rebellion against Imperial leadership, resulting in the signing of the [First Treaty of Stros M'Kai](Lore_First_Treaty_of_Stros_M%27Kai.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup> During the Third Era, the Forebears were the most cosmopolitan of the Redguard factions and happily embraced the Empire of [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md). Near the close of the Third Era, a third faction, the [Lhotunics](Lore_Lhotunics.md), emerged, representing a middle ground. By the Fourth Era, they were at peace with each other, though divisions remain.

## History

### First Era
When the Ra Gada crossed the [Sea of Pearls](Lore_Sea_of_Pearls.md) to eventually reach Tamriel, they landed on the harborage that became Hegathe. The landing site was in the possession of [beastfolk](Lore_Beastfolk.md), which were quickly displaced by the Yokudans. From their newly founded settlement, the Ra Gada swept across the coastlines to expand their reach.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-St Jo A-10)</sup> As the remaining refugees left [Herne](Lore_Herne_(island).md) to populate the province, the Yokudan government, the [Na-Totambu](Lore_Na-Totambu.md), became fully integrated, using Hegathe as its capital.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1E-11)</sup>

When the province of Hammerfell was annexed into the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), two political parties were formed to contribute to [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)'s administration over the land. The Crowns were created from what was left of the Na-Totambu, who retained their high-ranking status in the Empire, using Hegathe as their capital. From there, they were represented by the [Phyllocid Dynasty](Lore_Phyllocid_Dynasty.md), who maintained their relationship with the Empire throughout its lifetime. The Forebears was the other political party founded in Hammerfell, from the warrior-lords that descended from the Ra Gada and governed tribal districts in the south. For the longest time, these groups were sworn enemies, a rivalry that had existed far longer than the Empire itself.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1E-11)</sup> It took the ascension of High King [Ar-Azal](Lore_Ar-Azal.md), and his union with the Crowns daughter, [Arlimahera](Lore_Arlimahera.md), and the Forebears daughter, [Fereshtah](Lore_Fereshtah.md), to break the divide between them.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TWAHD-12)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/RG-comic-Page_25-Forebears.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_25-Forebears.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_25-Forebears.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_25-Forebears.jpg) The Forebears army marches on after the peace treaty, before Thassad II's death [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/72/RG-scene-Introduction_11.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_11.jpg) The Forebears fall on the battlefield against the Crowns Circa [2E 862](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_862), when the Crowns monarchy left their ancestral throne of [Old Hegathe](Lore_Old_Hegathe.md) and took over the Forebears kingdom of Sentinel, it caused a schism between both factions. Baron Volag was chosen by the Forebears to strike an accord with the Crowns King, [Thassad II](Lore_Thassad_II.md), for a short-term truce.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TOOC-5)</sup><sup>:25</sup> But, when Thassad II passed away from natural causes in 2E 862, Volag and his Forebears launched a wide-scale rebellion against the Crowns that has since been remembered as the Purge of the North. After the first massacre, the Crowns of Sentinel, led by Prince [A'tor](Lore_A%27tor.md), were forced to flee the kingdom to the west, to the island-kingdom of Stros M'Kai.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RGTobias-13)</sup>

On the island, the Crowns prepared for their counterattack and managed to provide a strong retaliation against the Forebears.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RGTobias-13)</sup> But, when the Forebears pleaded for assistance from the Third Empire, the tide of the war quickly shifted back against the Crowns until they were cornered in Stros M'Kai.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1E-11)</sup> Some people believed that Volag was used by the Empire, for his ambitions for the throne.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-RGKrisandra-14)</sup> As the conflict proved out of favor for the Crowns, Baron Volag and his Forebears disappeared from the public eye, but it is unclear whether this happened after he claimed Sentinel<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-RGJ.27ffer-15)</sup> or if it happened after the [Battle of Hunding Bay](Lore_Battle_of_Hunding_Bay.md) in [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RGTobias-13)</sup> In any case, Sentinel was without a figurehead and, much like other human realms, a [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) Officer,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1E-11)</sup> in the form of [Senecus Goddkey](Lore_Senecus_Goddkey.md) assumed the title of Provisional Governor for the Kingdom of Sentinel and the Forebears principalities.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1E-11)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/85/RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg/200px-RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-scene-Introduction_15.jpg) The Forebears kneel before Tiber Septim It's unknown what Baron Volag did while in hiding, but rumors insisted that he waited for a sign of weakness in the Empire.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-PGE1E-11)</sup> That sign came with [Cyrus](Lore_Cyrus.md) and the [Restless League](Lore_Restless_League.md)'s uprising in Stros M'Kai, which saw the death of its provisional governor, [Amiel Richton](Lore_Amiel_Richton.md). News of the event spread like wildfire and Baron Volag used the opportunity to reclaim Sentinel from the Provisional Government. Now instated as the Forebears leader of Sentinel, Volag met with Tiber Septim and the new Crown leader of Stros M'Kai, Iszara, to work out a treaty for the people of Hammerfell.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RGIszara-16)</sup> The First Treaty of Stros M'Kai was signed by the three parties; it consolidated the province's relationship with the Third Empire and led to the eventual integration of Hammerfell as a province to the rapidly expanding Empire. It has since been understood by the Redguards that they are an integral part of the Empire, and not their subjects.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-TWQ5-17)</sup>

### Third Era
In [3E 397](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_397), during the [Imperial Simulacrum](Lore_Imperial_Simulacrum.md), there was a [major war](Lore_War_of_the_Bend%27r-mahk.md) between Hammerfell, [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), and [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md). During the war, [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) defeated the forces of Hammerfell and High Rock and took many miles of the [eastern parts of those provinces](Lore_Western_Reach.md), including the cities of [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md) and [Jehanna](Lore_Jehanna.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE3Sky-18)</sup> Some of these territories were claimed by Skyrim to have been lost in previous wars at the expense of Skyrim.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE3High-19)</sup> The war resulted in a deeper division between the [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) factions of Crowns and Forebears, as the Forebear cities showed no inclination to help the Crown cities assaulted and occupied by the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md), which, in turn, was a response to the lack of help by Crowns to the Forebears during [the Camoran Usurper's invasion](Lore_Camoran_Usurper%27s_Invasion.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3E-6)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Tensions between the Crowns and Forebears persisted until the outbreak of the Great War. The Aldmeri Dominion demanded of the Empire large tracts of southern Hammerfell among several other outlandish demands. When the Empire naturally declined these demands the Aldmeri Dominion launched twin invasions of Cyrodiil and Hammerfell, with Elven armies bypassing Kvatch and Anvil to strike at southern Hammerfell alongside smaller naval invasions. The Empire's Legions were forced to retreat across the Alik'r in the infamous March of Thirst. But a Forebear army from Sentinel managed to break the siege of Hegathe, which started a warming of relations between them and the Crowns. However damaging their reputation in the eyes of Hammerfell. However the Imperial Legions were recalled to Cyrodiil as the Empire chose to prioritize the defense of the Imperial Isle, damaging the legitimacy of the Empire in Hammerfell. General Deccianus agreed to discharge a large number of 'invalids' before his return, who formed the core of Hammerfell resistance from then on.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

The Empire signed the White-Gold Concordant which in essence acceded to the Aldmeri Dominion's original demands, including much of southern Hammerfell which they already occupied. Hammerfell however refused to recognize the treaty, and the Empire was forced to release them as a province to maintain peace. Both the Crowns and Forebears fought on for another five years, finally amending their differences, and succeeded in pushing the Dominion out of Hammmerfell, now in control of a unified and independent Hammerfell.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>
