[3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) Wenyandawik is an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ruin found within the Colovian Lowlands region of the [Great Forest](Lore_Great_Forest.md), northwest of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) in the province of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). For the longest time, it contained one of the [Ten Ancestors](Lore_Ten_Ancestors.md), religious artifacts that were once displayed at the [Temple of the Ancestors](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md). It was among the only modern sites that still bore their ancient names by the time of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-OBUmbacano-1)</sup>

## History
During the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) raged across central Cyrodiil. The ruins of Wenyandawik, along with the area southwest of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), were considered part of the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md)'s territory. It was located at the triangulated center of Castles Black Boot, [Bloodmayne](Lore_Castle_Bloodmayne.md), and [Faregyl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castle_Faregyl).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ONSKH-2)</sup> Though the door was sealed by a chained lock, the [Ayleid Well](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleid_Well) on the surface still functioned.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ON-3)</sup>

During the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), the ruins of Wenyandawik had worn down even further and were plundered by a [treasure hunter](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md), who obtained one of the Ten Ancestors for the Ayleid enthusiast, [Umbacano](Lore_Umbacano.md), in the Imperial City.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-OBCollector-4)</sup> At the time, the ruins within were swarming with undead.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OB-5)</sup>
