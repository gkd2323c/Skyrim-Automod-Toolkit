Sage Svari (previously **Skald Svari**) was the sage of the [Companions](Lore_Companions.md) of [Fallowstone Hall](Lore_Fallowstone_Hall.md) in the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md). She lived in the town of [Shor's Stone](Lore_Shor%27s_Stone.md). She was a mage,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-STH-1)</sup> author,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AAo W-2)</sup> and skald by training.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Skald Svari was present at Fallowstone Hall when it was stormed by [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582). She was among the surviving Companions led by [Vigrod Wraithbane](Lore_Vigrod_Wraithbane.md) who summoned the vengeful spirit of [Sage Tirora](Lore_Tirora.md) to help retake the hall from the invaders.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-VW-4)</sup> Having prepared her whole life for the role, Svari was the natural successor to Tirora to become the next sage of Fallowstone Hall and record the deeds of the Companions.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESO-5)</sup> However, she needed to first prove herself by praying at Fallowstone's shrines to receive visions of the original [Five Hundred Companions](Lore_Five_Hundred_Companions.md). After completing this ritual, she was appointed as sage by Vigrod and her fellow Companions.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>

As her first act as sage, Svari began work on recording the deeds of [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in aiding the Companions liberate Fallowstone Hall.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESO-5)</sup> She also tasked the Vestige with recovering two artifacts which had been looted from Fallowstone Hall. Specifically, a weapon known as Winter's Hammer was recovered from the bandit leader at [Fort Greenwall](Lore_Fort_Greenwall.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> and another named Winter's Bite was retrieved from the Darkling cultists at Faldar's Tooth.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

Sage Svari was an expert on the subject of [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md). She is the author of *[An Accounting of Werewolves](Lore_An_Accounting_of_Werewolves.md)*<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-AAo W-2)</sup> and was known to have conducted scholarly research into the origins of the [disease](Lore_Sanies_Lupinus.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9)</sup> The Abbot of the [Temple of the Ancestor Moths](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md), [Crassius Viria](Lore_Crassius_Viria.md), was somewhat critical of Svari's commentary on the fate of a lycanthrope's [soul](Lore_Souls.md), calling her statements "poetic but misleading".<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
