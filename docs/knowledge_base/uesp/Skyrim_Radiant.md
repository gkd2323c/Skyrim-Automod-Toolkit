# Radiant


## Quests
The Radiant quest system is a [quest](Skyrim_Quests.md) generator that creates quests from a series of components such as location, enemy type, and reward. Some of these components are randomized, but not all. The randomized components are generated every time that the quest is triggered. Radiant quests are used throughout the game in various [faction](Skyrim_Factions.md) questlines and in [miscellaneous quests](Skyrim_Miscellaneous_Quests.md).

This quest system cannot create large, complicated, or particularly interesting quests, but it can create a near infinite number of quests with a fraction of the time and effort required to create a traditional one. It works with a system that aims to send you to a location that you have not yet discovered, and it also works to keep you engaged with fresh and interesting discoveries.

### Quest Locations
Most radiant quests have a set of about a dozen possible locations where you may be sent. Typically, those locations are selected based on the location's occupants or hold. For example, a quest might take place in any [Hagraven](Skyrim_Hagraven.md) nest or any [Bandit](Skyrim_Bandit.md) camp in [Falkreath Hold](Skyrim_Falkreath_Hold.md). In these cases, the quest page contains a list of all possible locations, and it is listed as a related quest on the page describing each of those locations.

However, some radiant quests have a much longer list of possible locations. All such quests are listed here, but they are *not* listed on each possible location page.

## NPCs and Creatures
Many NPCs and creatures have characteristics that are randomly generated. Although past Elder Scrolls games have used leveled lists to generate generic enemies, in Skyrim that ability now extends to named and unique characters.

For example, [Krev the Skinner](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Krev_the_Skinner) is the boss bandit for the quest [The Silver Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:The_Silver_Hand). Although they will always be a bandit, Krev's race is randomly determined from a leveled list of possible bandit bosses. In this case, even Krev's gender is randomly determined. ([Aela the Huntress](Skyrim_Aela_the_Huntress.md) will let you know what gender Krev is when you get close enough.)

Another example is [Movarth Piquine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Movarth_Piquine), the boss vampire for the quest [Laid to Rest](Skyrim_Laid_to_Rest.md). Although Movarth is always a vampire, his race is randomly determined from a leveled list of possible vampire bosses. (Movarth loses this feature with the [Dawnguard](Skyrim_Dawnguard.md) add-on, and now always appears as a Nord.)

## Notes
- The radiant location of some quests is determined upon game start, not when the quest appears in your log; you cannot reload an old save to re-roll a different location.
- Similarly, some quests are activated immediately upon completion of the previous one, before you claim your reward, so only reloading a save from before that will give a different target; simply reloading before speaking to the quest-giver won't (i.e. you will have to complete another radiant quest before you can see what target has been selected for the next quest).
- NPCs and locations added by [mods](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Mods) or [add-ons](Skyrim_Add-ons.md) can be assigned as targets or givers in radiant quests if they fit the parameters for the given quest. - If a quest has been assigned to a target on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> or any other new lands added by mods, there will be no quest markers while you are on mainland Skyrim. The converse is also true; if you are on Solstheim or a new land, there will be no markers pointing to Skyrim.
- Randomly generated quest locations do not take your character's level into account, so it is possible to be given a radiant quest target in a dungeon that has a minimum level well above yours.
- Only one type of these radiant quests can be active at a time: - **[A Few Words with You](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:A_Few_Words_with_You)**: Do a favor by talking to someone about a problem.
- **[Delivery](Skyrim_Delivery.md)**: Deliver an item to an associate.
- **[Dungeon Delving (Bandits)](Skyrim_Dungeon_Delving_(Bandits).md)**: Do a favor by retrieving an item from a bandit hideout.
- **[Dungeon Delving (Caves)](Skyrim_Dungeon_Delving_(Caves).md)**: Do a favor by retrieving an item from a cave.
- **[Kill the Bandit Leader](Skyrim_Kill_the_Bandit_Leader.md)**: Do a favor by slaying a bandit leader.
- **[Rare Gifts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Rare_Gifts)**: Do a favor by finding a rare item.
- **[Some Light Theft](Skyrim_Some_Light_Theft.md)**: Do a favor by stealing an item.
