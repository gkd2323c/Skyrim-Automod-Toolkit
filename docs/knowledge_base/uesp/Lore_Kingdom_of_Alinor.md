# Kingdom of Alinor


[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/43/ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg/200px-ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Alinor_Royal_Palace.jpg) Alinor The mysterious Scrolls of Praxis played an enormous role in Aldmeri monarchic rule. According to the traditions of Elven Ceremoniarchy, every decision a ruler made must be based on the precedents laid down in the scrolls. Such is their import that heirs to the throne were required to study Altmeri Regal Praxis and Ceremoniarchy under the [Sapiarchs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:College_of_Sapiarchs) for a requisite 3,555 days prior to receiving the crown. The coronation itself supposedly involved an eighty-eight day liturgy recited by the monarch themselves. When a new monarch inherited the throne, they traveled to [Tanzelwil](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tanzelwil), vast ruins on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md) to commune with the dead and seek their approval to the throne.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-4)</sup> Afterward, the College of Sapiarchs serve as a powerful resource to the Throne of Alinor, offering advice and counsel when called upon.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ATUQ-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SOTCT-6)</sup>

Provincial rulers were known as High Kinlords and Kinladys, such as High Kinlady [Estre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Estre) of [Skywatch](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skywatch) or High Kinlord [Rilis](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rilis_XII) of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md). Other offices of power include Canonreeve - similar to a mayor, it's an official title given to diplomats and bureaucrats who administer county seats.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONElquisa-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MSF-8)</sup>

Firsthold later became the staging ground for several naval expeditions, by order of the Crystal Tower. Three ships were chosen to explore uncharted territories and find a way back to Aldmeris. Although only one ship returned, they successfully mapped numerous coastlines of mainland Tamriel, setting the stage for the eventual Aldmeri diaspora.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-FOTN-9)</sup> According to some legends, the Aldmer didn't stop at mere naval exploration. A mythical order known as the [Sun Birds of Alinor](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sun_Birds_of_Alinor) attempted extra-planetary expeditions to [Aetherius](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aetherius).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Furn Sunbird-10)</sup>

From the ancient wellspring of Summerset, the Aldmer eventually split along cultural and religious lines resulting in the plethora of distinct Elven races we know of today. As various groups of Elves began to emigrate from Summerset, some (such as the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md)) continued to swear fealty to the High King of Alinor. Others, such as the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) clan, became independent from the kingdom and sought their own path.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-BTAOM-11)</sup> Arguabally one of the most notable schisms in the kingdom was the first recorded large-scale religious sect espousing [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) worship by the so-called prophet [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md). When the Sapiarchs of Alinor prohibited this schism, Veloth led the clans loyal to him out of the Isles and across the seas to the far side of Tamriel. Another notable schism in the kingdom was when the Aldmer began to move away from [worshipping their own ancestors](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md), instead worshipping the ancestors of the elite. A group of dissident elders, known as the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), rejected this change and left Summerset to settle on the nearby island of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) to continue practicing the "Old Ways of Aldmeris".<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3SI-12)</sup> By the Late Merethic Era numerous groups of elves had left the kingdom to settle other parts of Tamriel and tribute-lands, such as [Cyrod](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cyrod) under the Ayleids, detached themselves from the kingdom completely. The Aldmer that remained on Summerset became known as the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) and in the following eras became largely removed from the conflicts and rise and fall of empires on mainland Tamriel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3SI-12)</sup>

One of the main threats the kingdom faced was the Kingdom of Pyandonea to the south. King Orgnum made countless attempts to invade the Summerset Isle backed by a strong revanchist policy.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TFRM-13)</sup> For almost every year of the [First](Lore_First_Era.md) and [Second](Lore_Second_Era.md) eras, Pyandonean forces harassed the coastlines of Summerset. The Kingdom of Alinor was forced to build a great navy to defend itself, which resulted in the Altmer excelling in naval combat.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3Other-14)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-PGE3SI-12)</sup> The Royal Navy keeps the sea lanes as clear as possible and serves as a deterrent to external threats.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Mot SS-15)</sup>

One of the few instances the Kingdom of Alinor involved itself with external conflicts pre-Second Era was when Admiral [Bendu Olo](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bendu_Olo) proclaimed his intent to punish [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) for the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) in [1E 2241](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2241). In response, the shipyards in Alinor worked in concert with other nations to build a fleet and attack Thras. Additionally, the great warlock [Syrabane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Syrabane) lent aid given his prowess in arcane naval warfare.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SH3-16)</sup>

### [The Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md)
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-Aldmeri_Dominion.png) The Kingdom of Alinor allied with its neighbors to form the First and Second Aldmeri Dominion during the Second Era. While the justifications for each incarnation differed, the objective was the same objective: to reassert Elven dominance over Tamriel. The First Dominion saw the Summerset Isles allied with the Bosmer of [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) of [Elsweyr Confederacy](Lore_Elsweyr.md) in the Elden Accord.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ONAyrenn-4)</sup> To that end, the kingdom fought in the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) against the [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) and [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md) for control of mainland Tamriel.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Welcome-17)</sup>

The Second Dominion was formed in [2E 830](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_830) when the Kingdom of Alinor conquered Valenwood over a dynastic dispute. When the heirs of the [Camoran Dynasty](Lore_Camoran_Dynasty.md) sought to capture Valenwood's throne, a faction of Bosmer attempted to make peace with their enemies in the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md) by offering part of the Valenwood territory in exchange for the faction's own claimant. The Kingdom of Alinor, upon hearing of this, invaded Valenwood, citing a stewardship clause in a thousand-year-old treaty between their nations.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-18)</sup> The Altmer established the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) as the new government in Valenwood. Over the next several years, the Thalmor strengthened their hold on Valenwood and Bosmer tribes continued to skirmish with the Colovian Estates.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-PGE1E-19)</sup> Meanwhile, [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) rose to power in Cyrodiil and began his conquest of Tamriel with the [Tiber War](Lore_Tiber_War.md). The nascent [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) expanded and encroached on Dominion territory, forcing the Bosmer attacks to subside. Despite their military setbacks on land, Alinor's Royal Navy was considerably more powerful and steadfastly held the southern waters from the [Cape of the Blue Divide](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cape_of_the_Blue_Divide) to the [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). The catastrophic use of the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) ended the long confrontation in [2E 896](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_896), resulting in the surrender of the Kingdom of Alinor and its absorption into the Empire.

A Third Dominion was established centuries later in [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), in the aftermath of the Oblivion Crisis. By painting themselves as the saviors of the Summerset Isles, the Thalmor solidified their position and seized total control of the Summerset Isles. They abolished the monarchy across the Summerset Isles, ending one of the oldest kingdoms on Nirn. Seven years later, a Thalmor-backed coup overthrew Valenwood's government. Alinor and Valenwood proclaimed a union independent of the Empire, and the Aldmeri Dominion was reborn. Elsweyr split into the kingdoms of Anequina and Pelletine in [4E 115](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_115) and subsequently became client states of the Dominion.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-RT4-20)</sup>
