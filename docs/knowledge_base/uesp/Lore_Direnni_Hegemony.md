# Direnni Hegemony


The Direnni Hegemony was an ancient Elven [power](Lore_Empires.md) that rose to dominance in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md) around [1E 355](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_355), but some consider their rule in the years leading up to its establishment as part of the Direnni Hegemony as well.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ONSHV1-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-EOTD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ESODR-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESODA-4)</sup> The [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) Hegemony fractured in [1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDCP11-5)</sup> with one source claiming that the [Direnni](Lore_Direnni.md) remained dominant until the middle of the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), during which the whole of High Rock was commonly called the Direnni Hegemony.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-6)</sup> As an economic and military power, they were notable for their victories over the battle-hardened [Nordic Empire](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md) of [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) and the nascent [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md) of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The Direnni clan had migrated to High Rock during the [Aldmeri](Lore_Aldmer.md) diaspora of the Middle [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), alongside several other families. The progenitor of Clan Direnni was [Direnni Cygnus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Direnni_Cygnus), who was said to have discovered the [Isle of Balfiera](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Balfiera) and the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) and claimed them for her own clan. Some believe the Direnni left the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) as they were following their destiny to rule from Balfiera, while others believed that they were exiled.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ESOBE-7)</sup>

During the Merethic Era<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MDBMM-8)</sup> the Direnni came into contact with a [Nedic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nede) peoples, known today as the [Druids of Galen](Lore_Druids_of_Galen.md)<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MDBMM-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-LATD-9)</sup> (or proto-Bretons).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-LTP2022-10)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-LADCG-11)</sup> Whether these Nedes were native to High Rock or migrated is still debated in scholarly circles, but regardless Clan Direnni exerted its influence upon them and established the feudal system whereby they ruled the Nedes as a noble caste.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TBMOP-12)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Once-13)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-MDBMM-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-LSP:OAC-14)</sup>

## History

### Rise to Power
By the early First Era the Direnni had become an economic and military power but had to contend with the rampaging Nordic armies of [Vrage](Lore_Vrage.md) and his [Skyrim Conquests](Lore_Skyrim_Conquests.md) in [1E 222](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_222), which saw much of High Rock being annexed under the [First Empire of the Nords](Lore_First_Empire_of_the_Nords.md). Indeed, Vrage's pretext for war was that he believed the enslaved Bretons were descendants of captured Nords from the [Night of Tears](Lore_Night_of_Tears.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-15)</sup> The Direnni and the other Aldmer clans kept hold of their lands and were aided in battle by many of their Breton vassals, while Nords founded fortified towns, such as [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall), along the coasts to support their pillaging parties.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-6)</sup>

It is speculated that there was internal strife between the various Aldmer clans of High Rock.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-16)</sup> A century later despite the infighting between the various clans over who would rule, the Direnni prospered and rose to dominance through political manoeuvring and various machinations around [1E 355](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_355), establishing the Direnni Hegemony in earnest.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDCP11-5)</sup> It was during this time the First Empire of the Nords was approaching its final years, and in one of its last campaigns attacked the [Western Reach](Lore_Western_Reach.md) with the aid of Emperor [Ami-El](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ami-El) of Cyrodiil in [1E 358](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_358). The campaign failed and Skyrim lost more and more of [Greater Bretony](Lore_Greater_Bretony.md) to the Direnni.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-17)</sup> With the rise of the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) in Cyrodiil in [1E 361](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_361), many fleeing Ayleids migrated to High Rock to escape persecution in an exodus known as the [Ayleid Diaspora](Lore_Ayleid_Diaspora.md). Most notabally [Laloriaran Dynar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar), who was welcomed by the Direnni and given lands to settle in [1E 375](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_375).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-18)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ASi V-19)</sup> Scholars speculate that the rise of the Direnni Hegemony may be linked to influx of [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) into High Rock.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-20)</sup>

Only fourteen year after the Nord's failed invasion of the Western Reach, the [War of Succession](Lore_War_of_Succession.md) erupted in Skyrim in [1E 369](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_369), greatly weakening them. The Direnni Hegemony took full advantage of their internal strife and pushed out the Nords, ending almost 150 years of Nordic occupation.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AHOD-21)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-6)</sup> In [1E 461](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_461), [Ryain Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ryain_Direnni) purchased the Isle of Balfiera, strengthening the Direnni Hegemony's claim over the isle,<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RTR-22)</sup> and later warred with High King [Kjoric the White](Lore_Kjoric_the_White.md) of Skyrim. With the aid of [Laloriaran Dynar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar), the Direnni emerged victorious and continued their military expansion.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-18)</sup>

By [1E 477](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_477), the Direnni Hegemony managed to conquer High Rock entirely, and even some parts of Skyrim and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md),<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TDCP11-5)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-18)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RTR-22)</sup> going as far east as [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) and [Elinhir](Lore_Elinhir.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Once-13)</sup> The Direnni Hegemony had become formidable enough to pose a continued threat to its neighbors, and at the peak of their power controlled nearly a quarter of Tamriel.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-6)</sup>

### The Fall
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/36/ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg/200px-ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Isle_of_Balfiera.jpg) Balfiera, home of Clan Direnni In [1E 478](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_478), the Alessian Empire under Emperor [Gorieus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorieus) was bearing down on its rebellious vassals and launched an attack on the [Colovian Estates](Lore_Colovian_Estates.md). After King [Rislav Larich](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rislav_Larich) of [Skingrad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skingrad) routed [Gorieus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gorieus)' army, the Direnni were inspired to outlaw the religion of the Alessian Reform throughout High Rock, and began pushing into Imperial territories.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-RTR-22)</sup> Soon after, the Direnni Hegemony joined the resistance alongside High King [Hoag Merkiller](Lore_Hoag_Merkiller.md) of Skyrim. Ryain Direnni outlawed the Alessian Reform and executed its missionaries. Direnni forces began to harass the Colovian West, pushing into the Heartlands. Following the Direnni involvement in Cyrodiil, the Alessians retaliated and began conquering territories of the Direnni Hegemony. By [1E 479](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_479), [Craglorn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Craglorn) had fallen to the Imperials.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ALo Sa S-18)</sup>

Although the [Aiden Direnni](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aiden_Direnni) pushed back in [1E 480](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_480) and led a series of minor but important victories against the Alessians in High Rock, it was clear the Direnni were in need of more troops.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TFL-23)</sup> Aiden Direnni signed the Rights Charter out of necessity, in efforts to muster troops for the coming conflict.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-ALOSAS-24)</sup> [Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daggerfall) became a minor base of operations for the Direnni and their allies.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AHOD-21)</sup>

The conflict with the Alessians came to a head at the [Battle of Glenumbria Moors](Lore_Battle_of_Glenumbria_Moors.md) in [1E 482](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_482). Aiden's army defeated the Alessians on the 8th of Sun's Dawn with the assistance of Breton knights,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-25)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-26)</sup> the [last Ayleid king](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Laloriaran_Dynar) and Hoag Merkiller.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TLKOTA-27)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-FSOKW-28)</sup> Although the Alessians were crushed at the Battle of Glenumbria Moors, the victory weakened Direnni power in High Rock. In the following years it became evident that the scattered and exhausted Direnni had overextended their reach. Year-after-year the Direnni could no longer challenge the emerging Breton nobility, who seized power throughout most of High Rock by [1E 498](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_498). Losings all the territory they had gained, the Direnni were forced to fall back to their original home on Balfiera. Increasingly vilified by their former vassals, the island clan became ever more insular and isolationist thereafter.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-PGE1HR-15)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-6)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TBMOP-12)</sup> According to one source, the Direnni remained dominant in High Rock until the middle of the First Era.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-PGE3HR-6)</sup>
