# Imperial Guard

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d7/RG-concept-Imperial_Guard_Painting_%28cropped_scan%29.jpg/200px-RG-concept-Imperial_Guard_Painting_%28cropped_scan%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-concept-Imperial_Guard_Painting_(cropped_scan).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-concept-Imperial_Guard_Painting_(cropped_scan).jpg) Imperial Guard The Imperial Guard, also referred to as the **shadow legion**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Janisiere-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Battlespire Intro-2)</sup> were charged with the sole task of ensuring the personal safety and defense of the [Tamrielic Emperors](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) and various of their direct representatives across the provinces.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind Imperial Guard Topic-3)</sup> The Guard were known as the greatest warrior-mages in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md),<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Battlespire MBNET-4)</sup> and existed as an elite unit under the [Imperial Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md) umbrella.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind Imperial Guard Topic-3)</sup> An individual of the guard is known as a **janisiere**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Janisiere-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup>

While the term "Imperial Guard" is often used to describe common guardsmen found throughout the Empire, the elite unit of bodyguards are a separated unique order who directly serve in the Emperor's courts guarding royals.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind Imperial Guard Topic-3)</sup> There was a traditional rivalry set between the Imperial Guard their counterparts in the regular Legions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Morrowind Imperial Guard Topic-3)</sup>

## History

### Age of Heroes
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-npc-Senan.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Senan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Senan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Senan.jpg) Senan of the Ghostsong Clan, member of Emperor Moricar's Imperial Guard Consistent with more contemporary times, the Imperial Guard of the [Age of Heroes](Lore_Age_of_Heroes.md) that protected the Emperors as bodyguards.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Onnline Event-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOCU-6)</sup> During the reign of the [Longhouse Emperor](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) [Moricar](Lore_Moricar.md), the Imperial Guard consisted of mostly [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md). Moricar's Guard had [werewolves](Lore_Lycanthropy.md) among its ranks.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Senan-7)</sup> Following the fall of the dynasty, some of the guard returned to [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-Arana-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ESOLC1-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Arloakh-10)</sup>

### The Proving
The [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md) was utilized for centuries as a Testing Facility and Proving Ground for those who wished to join the Imperial Guard. Recruits of this [Imperial Battle College](Lore_Imperial_Battle_College.md) were known as Potentials and were usually identified as preteens. These Potentials learn about magic, the art of magical and martial warfare, as well as contemporary studies. Every year there was a Potentials Battle Tourney, where the first and second place winners are allowed to enter the Battlespire Proving Ground.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Mt CL-11)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-12)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Battlespire MBNET-4)</sup> Only those who could prove themselves by successfully mastering the challenges of Battlespire were chosen for the Emperor's Imperial Guard.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Battlespire MBNET-4)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-12)</sup>

### Tiber War
Circa [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the Imperial Guard were actively serving Emperor [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Redguard Intro-13)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Concept Art-14)</sup>

### Queenmakers
Circa [3E 3](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_3), General [Symmachus](Lore_Symmachus.md) of the Imperial Army and Imperial Guards under his command, rode to [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) to collect a ward of the Septim Empire: Princess [Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md) of [Clan Ra'athim](Lore_Ra%27athim.md) and install her as the provincial Queen of Morrowind, to rule from the seat of [Mournhold](Lore_Mournhold.md) in [Almalexia City](Lore_Mournhold.md). As ordered by his Emperor, General Symmachus would set aside his Imperial General and Governor titles to serve as Barenziah's Prime Minister, and even went on to marry her.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TRB-15)</sup>

Imperial Guard remained stationed within Mournhold for several centuries.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TRB-15)</sup>

In the time following the theft of the Staff of Chaos from Mournhold, Circa [3E 376](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_376), the relationship between the Morrowind Queendom and the Septim Empire slowly deteriorated. Imperial taxes were raised and quotas were increased with each passing year. Prime Minister Symmachus believed the Emperor had some suspicions that the Prime Minister had some role in the Staff's theft; in order to prove his loyalty to the Dragon Throne he made every effort to comply with the with the escalating demands. He lengthened working hours and raised tariffs, and even made up some of the difference from both the royal exchequer and the family's own private holdings. But the levies multiplied, and commoners and nobles alike began to complain. Symmachus sent his lady wife Barenziah to plead with the Emperor, and even said that she could tell the Emperor that: without his aid, the Empire would have never begun, and that he can bring about its fall if need be. Eventually, a rebel revolt ensued in the city, with the Imperial Guard allegedly joining the rebels, somehow resulting in Symmachus's death Circa [3E 391](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_391).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TRB-15)</sup>

### Imperial Simulacrum
Circa [3E 389](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_389), General [Talin Warhaft](Lore_Talin_Warhaft.md) was leader of Imperial Guard. Warhaft and others of the Emperor's Imperial Guard were banished into [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) by the [Imperial Battlemage of Tamriel](Lore_Imperial_Battlemage_of_Tamriel.md), [Jagar Tharn](Lore_Jagar_Tharn.md), and physically replaced by [doppelgangers](Lore_Doppelganger.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Arena Event-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-ARMIS-17)</sup> Tharn used these faux Guards as puppets serving his own machinations. When General Warhaft was saved by the [Eternal Champion](Lore_Eternal_Champion.md) and returned to Tamriel, he seemingly resumed his duties.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Arena Event-16)</sup>

The Battlespire was partially destroyed by the forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) during the Imperial Simulacrum. However, upon Restoration, Emperor Uriel chose to retake the destroyed facility,<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-18)</sup> despite advice from his treasury to not.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-12)</sup> However it remained inactive as an Imperial Proving Ground.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-19)</sup>

### The Decline
In the later Third Era,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Lady [Allena Benoch](Lore_Allena_Benoch.md) became head of the Emperor's Imperial Guard under Uriel VII. She neither confirms nor denies her role in the removal of [Calaxes Septim](Lore_Calaxes_Septim.md) as Archbishop of [The One](Lore_The_One.md) while serving in the Guard.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Words And Phil-20)</sup>

Circa [3E 427](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_427), [Vedam Dren](Lore_Vedam_Dren.md), Duke of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md), had Imperial Guard assigned to his service.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Morrowind Event-21)</sup>

When Emperor [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md) was assassinated by the [Mythic Dawn](Lore_Mythic_Dawn.md),<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Oblivion Event-22)</sup> the Imperial Guard joined the regicide investigation alongside the: [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md), [Imperial Legions](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md), [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md), and Imperial Battle College.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-:Assassination-23)</sup>

Circa [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), the Imperial Guard was still in operation, regarded as professionals compared to the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TIC-24)</sup>

Various *[Battlespire](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Battlespire:Battlespire)* sources refer to "[The Chronicles of Janisiere](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:The_Chronicles_of_Janisiere)" as being an in-world book about the events of the game.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Athenaeum-12)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Codex-35)</sup> What "Janisiere" means in the context of *[The Elder Scrolls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:The_Elder_Scrolls)* has not been defined, and seems to have been uniquely invented for the series. But when the invented word's roots are compared to real world languages, Janisiere seems to be an etymological mashup of "[janissary](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Janissary)" (a member of elite infantry units that make up the household guard of an [Ottoman sultan](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_sultan)) and "[-iere](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-iere)" (a feminine suffix used for an occupation or role in some languages, seen in words like [chevaliere](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/chevali%C3%A8re) or [brigadiere](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/brigadiere)); with this real world etymology in mind, the use of the word "Janisiere" for the title of "The Chronicles of Janisiere" makes sense, given that the game's narrative is about a protagonist seeking to join the Emperor's personal guardians: the Imperial Guard.
