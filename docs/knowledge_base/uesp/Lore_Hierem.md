Hierem was the prime minister of [Emperor](Lore_Cyrodilic_Emperors.md) [Titus Mede I](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md) during the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). He rose to prominence in the aftermath of Titus Mede's capture of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) when he helped smooth over the conquest by convincing the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) to accept Titus as a liberator rather than a conqueror.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96</sup> By [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), he would become Titus I's prime minister and the second most powerful man in the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:63, 96, 162</sup>

Around [4E 47](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_47), Hierem made a deal with Vuhon, the lord of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md), to summon the flying city to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:244-246</sup> In 4E 47, Hierem and the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md) performed a ritual that summoned Umbriel to Mundus, setting the stage for the [Umbriel Crisis](Lore_Umbriel_Crisis.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:45, 64-66</sup> Hierem summoned Umbriel to Mundus because he believed that he could use the power of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) and the [souls](Lore_Soul.md) collected by Umbriel to make himself a god.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:280, 305-306</sup> However, before he could complete his plan, he was killed by a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) while fighting with Colin Vineben and Letine Arese at the end of the Umbriel Crisis in 4E 48.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:278-281</sup>

## Biography

### Background and Early Career
Hierem's birthdate is unknown, but he was born into an old and well-connected [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) family.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96</sup> Hierem was pudgy with thick eyebrows and mild blue eyes.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:108</sup>

In his younger years, he was part of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md)'s leadership prior to its dissolution at the beginning of the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:65</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Sr LS-3)</sup> Following the Mages Guild's dissolution, he became a member of the [Synod](Lore_Synod.md) and eventually became extremely influential within the organization.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96, 198</sup> He served as an imperial ambassador to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) prior to the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96, 257</sup> After the onset of the [Stormcrown Interregnum](Lore_Stormcrown_Interregnum.md), Hierem served as a minister to the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md)'s ruler, [Thules the Gibbering](Lore_Thules_the_Gibbering.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96</sup> Hierem remained a minister to Thules until circa [4E 22](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_22), when the [Colovian](Lore_Colovia.md) warlord [Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede_I.md) captured the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) with a force of less than a thousand men and deposed Thules.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:109</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-RTV4-4)</sup>

### Career under Titus Mede I
Thules the Gibbering was not a well-liked ruler, but since he was Nibenese, many on the [Elder Council](Lore_Elder_Council.md) favored him over a Colovian usurper.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96</sup> Hierem used his many connections to help smooth over the conquest by convincing the Elder Council to accept Titus as a liberator rather than a conqueror.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96</sup> By 4E 48, he would become Titus I's prime minister and the second most powerful man in the Empire.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:63, 96, 162</sup> Although Hierem provided invaluable assistance to Titus I, he personally considered the Emperor to be an ill-mannered, badly bred Colovian upstart.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:247</sup>

Titus I did not trust Hierem.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:246</sup> Hierem remained in a position of power under Titus I because he believed that removing him would trigger a civil war due to his many connections and extreme influence with the Synod.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:96, 99-100, 246</sup>

At some point during the childhood of Titus I's son [Attrebus](Lore_Attrebus_Mede.md), Hierem suggested to the Emperor that the people needed a hero, and that Attrebus should be that hero.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:245, 272</sup> Titus I agreed to Hierem's plan, either to provide a sense of direction for Attrebus or because he believed it would placate Hierem.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:245, 272</sup> To make Attrebus a hero, the Imperial Court staged every battle or duel he fought to ensure his victory, and if he went on an unstaged adventure, one of his guards would discreetly report it to the Emperor or Hierem, who would end it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:66-67</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:146-147, 159-160</sup>

In [4E 38](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_38), Titus I placed a member of his inner circle, Letine Arese, in Hierem's ministry to spy on him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:22-23, 72, 98-99</sup> When he placed Arese in the ministry, the Emperor had no particular concerns about Hierem; only the sort of general paranoia a successful monarch must have.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:23</sup> Hierem remained above any suspicion until around [4E 47](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_47), when he began testing Arese.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:23</sup> It eventually became clear to Arese that he wanted his own private intelligence and elimination organization, one not connected to the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md) or known to the Emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:23</sup>

### Summoning Umbriel
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2c/BK-Umbriel.jpg/200px-BK-Umbriel.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-Umbriel.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-Umbriel.jpg) Umbriel Around 4E 47, Hierem made a deal with Vuhon, the lord of [Umbriel](Lore_Umbriel.md), to summon the flying city to [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:244-246</sup> Vuhon wanted Umbriel to be summoned to Mundus so he could use the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) to free himself from [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:140, 280, 293</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:281-282</sup> In exchange for summoning Umbriel to Mundus, Vuhon was to assist Hierem in becoming emperor.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:100, 246</sup> However, Hierem did not intend to honor their deal, as he believed Vuhon would not honor his terms.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:246</sup> Hierem believed that when Umbriel arrived at the White-Gold Tower, he could use the tower's power and siphon the [souls](Lore_Soul.md) collected by the city to make himself a god, so he deceived Vuhon into believing that he still intended to honor their deal.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:280, 305-306</sup>

In 4E 47, Hierem and a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) named Delia Huerc took a trip to [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) ostensibly to negotiate an alliance against the [Thalmor](Lore_Thalmor.md) with the [An-Xileel](Lore_An-Xileel.md), but in actuality to summon Umbriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:45-47, 64-66</sup> He did not want the Emperor to know of the trip, so he personally paid for a merchant ship to take them to [Lilmoth](Lore_Lilmoth.md), and they traveled in disguise.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:47</sup> In Lilmoth, Hierem met with An-Xileel leaders, and the two parties reached an agreement that involved Hierem performing a ritual at the city's [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) tree.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:64-66</sup> At the Hist tree, Hierem and the An-Xileel performed a ritual that summoned Umbriel to Mundus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:64-66</sup>

### Umbriel Crisis and Death
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/08/BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg/200px-BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BK-cover-cropped-Lord_of_Souls.jpg) Umbriel at the Imperial City In [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48), Umbriel appeared off the coast of Black Marsh.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:3-5</sup> A woman named Annaïg Hoïnart, who was on Umbriel, contacted Attrebus Mede, prompting him to visit the [Imperial Palace](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) to discuss Umbriel with Titus I.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:107-108</sup> At the Imperial Palace, Titus I and Hierem informed Attrebus that the Synod, [College of Whispers](Lore_College_of_Whispers.md), and other specialists were investigating the flying city and that they felt there was no need to send an expeditionary force since Umbriel was slowly moving north toward Morrowind and was yet to threaten the Empire's borders.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:108-109</sup> The Emperor also forbade the Crown Prince from attempting to stop Umbriel.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:109</sup>

Attrebus disobeyed his father and left the Imperial City with his guards, but before they departed, the Crown Prince's right-hand man, Gulan, followed protocol and reported the plans to the prime minister's office, specifically to Arese.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:67</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:111, 160, 266, 272</sup> Arese then reported this to Hierem, who ordered her to arrange for assassins to kill Attrebus, as he wanted to delay an attack on Umbriel for as long as possible.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:22, 46, 67</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:272-274, 281, 284</sup> Arese hired Calvur, a criminal leader, to assassinate Attrebus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:22</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:272-274</sup> Calvur's eight assassins ambushed and massacred Attrebus' guards near the [Ayleid ruin](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) of [Sardavar Leed](Lore_Sardavar_Leed.md), but rather than killing the Crown Prince, they captured him and took him to [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) with the intention of selling him.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:124-127, 140, 142</sup> Later, a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named Sul rescued Attrebus.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:147-151</sup> Hierem later supplied Titus I with evidence implicating Arese in the assassination attempt on Attrebus to attempt to conceal his involvement.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:98</sup>

Arese informed Colin Vineben, a Penitus Oculatus inspector who had investigated Attrebus' disappearance, that Hierem ordered her to arrange the assassination attempt on Attrebus, but she was unaware of why he wanted the prince dead.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:21-23</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TIC-2)</sup><sup>:137-141</sup> After Vineben discovered that Hierem had his office censor intelligence reports on Umbriel, Vineben and Arese theorized that Hierem was working with Umbriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:44-47</sup> The Inspector then recovered Delia Huerc's journal, which detailed the Prime Minister's trip to Black Marsh the previous year.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:61-66</sup> After recovering Huerc's journal, Vineben presented it to the Emperor, but he found neither the journal nor Arese's statements incriminating enough to use against Hierem.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:97-99</sup> At the end of the meeting, the Emperor gave Vineben a key to Hierem's ministry and rooms and told him to find undeniable proof of Hierem's involvement.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:99-100</sup>

While searching the minister's private office and quarters, Vineben found a paper depicting the ritual that Hierem would perform to use the White-Gold Tower's power and steal the souls from Umbriel.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:195-198</sup> Vineben did not understand what the paper said or depicted, except that it had Umbriel written on it; however, he believed it was important, so he copied it.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:196-197</sup>

When Umbriel neared the Imperial City, Umbriel's forces completely encircled it and put the city under siege.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:199, 237</sup> Hierem convinced Vuhon to lend him some of his ground troops so that he could capture anyone entering the city.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:245</sup> Amid the siege, Attrebus and Sul attempted to enter the Imperial City's [Waterfront District](Lore_Waterfront_District.md), but Hierem's men captured them and placed them in cells located in his private office and quarters.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:237-243</sup> Afterward, Attrebus woke up in Hierem's office, where Hierem questioned him about why Vuhon was afraid of him and Sul.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:243-246</sup> Hierem then left, telling the prince that he had a lot to do and preparations to make before their next conversation.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:247</sup> Later, Hierem awakened Sul and proceeded to question him and Attrebus by using a type of magic that caused pleasure if they gave Hierem a correct answer but pain if they gave him an incorrect one.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:256-258</sup> After Hierem used the pleasure-inducing magic several times on Attrebus, Sul revealed to him that Vuhon was afraid of the sword [Umbra](Lore_Umbra.md) so that he would stop questioning Attrebus.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:258-260</sup> The Prime Minister then used the portal he had in his quarters to teleport to Umbriel and speak with Vuhon.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:260</sup>

After Hierem teleported to Umbriel, Vineben and Arese released Attrebus and Sul from their cells.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:261-262</sup> Attrebus and Sul waited on the sigil of Hierem's portal, believing that once the minister activated it, the portal would send them to Umbriel, while Vineben and Arese waited behind the portal, preparing to ambush and kill Hierem.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:262-265, 270</sup>

When Hierem activated the portal, which sent Attrebus and Sul to Umbriel, he appeared in his quarters, where Vineben grabbed him and attempted to stab him in the head.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:273, 278</sup> Hierem escaped Vineben's grasp by hitting him with a demoralization spell, but Arese summoned a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), which the minister hit with a spell that sent it flying back into the wall.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:278-279</sup> The Daedra attacked again, but Hierem failed to completely deflect it, and its claw slashed across his chest, passing through his chain mail and wounding him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:279-280</sup> Hierem responded by striking the Daedra with a burden spell, causing it to collapse.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:279</sup> Arese then attacked Hierem, but she failed, and the minister produced a Daedric weapon, which he intended to use to kill her.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:279-280</sup> However, before Hierem could kill Arese, Vineben threw a knife at him, distracting him and enabling the Daedra to attack and kill him.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:280-281</sup>

### Aftermath
Unbeknownst to Vineben, Arese had deciphered the minister's plan from the paper Vienben had copied.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:280-281</sup> Therefore, moments after Hierem's death, she stabbed Vineben and retrieved Hierem's Daedric weapon.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:280-281</sup> Arese went to the secret room where Hierem had intended to perform the ritual, but before she could complete it, Vineben managed to follow and kill her, and he died shortly afterward.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:304-307</sup> The [Umbriel Crisis](Lore_Umbriel_Crisis.md) ended when Attrebus and Sul defeated Vuhon, and an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) named Mere-Glim used his connection with Umbriel and the city's Hist tree to lead Umbriel to the [Realm of the Hist](Lore_Realm_of_the_Hist.md) in Oblivion.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-LOS-1)</sup><sup>:298-304</sup>
