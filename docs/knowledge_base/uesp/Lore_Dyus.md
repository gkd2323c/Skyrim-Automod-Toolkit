Dyus of Mytheria was the chamberlain of [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md) and the keeper of his Great Library.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DDSI-1)</sup> He was originally simply the Prince's librarian, but soon took on many of the tasks Jyggalag found distasteful. His mortality was stripped away when Jyggalag grew tired of him dying.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IWTDSI-2)</sup> Using the formulae once contained in the Great Library, Dyus can predict all events before they happen, save the Prophecies of the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).

When Jyggalag was cursed to take on the form of [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), he had the Great Library burned, as he believed that personal choice defied logical prediction. The Madgod could not bring himself to destroy the knowledge which Dyus possessed, however, so he instead imprisoned him in the ruins of the Great Library, forbidding him to die.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-DDSI-1)</sup> These ruins were slowly encroached upon by the [Root](Lore_Root.md), and became known as [Knifepoint Hollow](Lore_Knifepoint_Hollow.md). At the end of each [Greymarch](Lore_Greymarch.md), Sheogorath would cast out every element of Order in the Isles except for Dyus.

Dyus' origins are unknown, as even he does not know of his former life, and he is uncertain if Mytheria was ever even a real place. His name was chosen by his master Jyggalag for reasons unbeknownst to him. Dyus claimed to have never visited [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md), although he studied it extensively. The Hero didn't deem to release Dyus from his immortality, and he continued to await his return to his master's service. He is known to have a particular rivalry with Haskill, who views him with contempt.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IWTDSI-2)</sup>

## History
In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), after a failed effort by Sheogorath to halt the Greymarch, [Haskill](Lore_Haskill.md) sent the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero.md#Hero_of_Kvatch) to speak to Dyus, in the hope that he would know how the Hero could wear the mantle of Madness. Dyus aided the Hero in creating the shell of a new [Staff of Sheogorath](Lore_Staff_of_Sheogorath.md), although all of his logical calculations predicted that the attempt would fail. The Hero later freed Jyggalag of his curse, taking on the title of Madgod and defying Dyus' predictions.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Sometime in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md),<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_4E)</sup> Dyus was visited by the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) [Talym Rend](Lore_Talym_Rend.md), who sought to inquire on how to obtain leverage to force Sheogorath to have an audience with him.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-KPIOM-4)</sup> He revealed that Sheogorath had suppressed his memories of once being Jyggalag, and destroyed almost all trace of him, except for Dyus and the [Sword of Jyggalag](Lore_Sword_of_Jyggalag.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TWIOM-5)</sup> He directed Talym to the last Obelisk of Order in the realm, where he would find the Sword of Jyggalag.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-OOIOM-6)</sup>
