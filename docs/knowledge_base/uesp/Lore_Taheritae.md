# Taheritae

| Sage Taheritae | | | |

| Race | Unknown | Gender | Unknown |

Taheritae was a great sage, and a member of the [Psijiic Order](Lore_Psijiic_Order.md) known for the publication of *Oegnithr*. They were a proponent of counterbalancing the bad changes by any means necessary.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TOW-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup>

## History
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Monomyth_Reforged.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-armor-Monomyth_Reforged.png) Monomyth Reforged [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/ON-npc-Loremaster_Celarus.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Loremaster_Celarus.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Loremaster_Celarus.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Loremaster_Celarus.jpg) Celarus, the author of *The Old Ways* Little is known about Taheritae's life. They became a member of the Psijic Order at some point and came to be known as a great sage. The exact timeframe of their life is unknown, but they were active long before [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), by which time the ideas they proposed were widespread within the Order and their words were already being quoted in published works. As of then Celarus considered some of Taheritae's words outmoded.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TOW-1)</sup>

Taheritae was also known to list all 111 Prodigalities recognized by the Order including greed, gluttony, sloth, ignorance, prejudice, and cruelty.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TOW-1)</sup>

## Quotes
One substantial fragment of their work, *Oegnithr*, has been quoted on several occasions. Taheritae was quoted at length by Loremaster [Celarus](Lore_Celarus.md) of the Psijic Order in his publication, *[The Old Ways](Lore_The_Old_Ways.md)*. Taheritae's words appeared alongside those of the great [Cuilean Darnizhaan](Lore_Cuilean_Darnizhaan.md), another sage closely associated with the Psijic Order, in that work.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TOW-1)</sup> A shorter excerpt appeared as the opening sentence of *[The Monomyth](Lore_The_Monomyth.md)*.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> The same quotation was also used as a commentary on the Monomyth Reforged, an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) ring of considerable magical power.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MR-3)</sup>

- *"In Mundus, conflict and disparity are what bring change, and change is the most sacred of the Eleven Forces. Change is the force without focus or origin."*<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-MR-3)</sup>

- *"In Mundus, conflict and disparity are what bring change, and change is the most sacred of the Eleven Forces. Change is the force without focus or origin. It is the duty of the disciplined Psijic ["Enlightened One"] to dilute change where it brings greed, gluttony, sloth, ignorance, prejudice, cruelty… [here Taheritae lists the rest of the 111 Prodigalities], and to encourage change where it brings excellence, beauty, happiness, and enlightenment. As such, the faithful counsel has but one master: His mind. If the man the Psijic counsels acts wickedly and brings oegnithr ["bad change"] and will otherwise not be counselled, it is the Psijic's duty to counterbalance the oegnithr by any means necessary [emphasis mine]."*<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TOW-1)</sup>

## Notes
- The word *oegnithir*,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LC-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Josajeh-5)</sup> or *oegnithr*, meaning "the Bad Change", is the title of his work *Oegnithr*,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TOW-1)</sup> and has long been employed as a term among the Psijic Order.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-LC-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Josajeh-5)</sup>
