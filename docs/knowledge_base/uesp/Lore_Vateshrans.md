# Vateshrans

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Aydolan.jpg) Spirit of Aydolan, one of the most famous Vateshrans [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1d/ON-npc-Vateshran_Barth.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Vateshran_Barth.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vateshran_Barth.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vateshran_Barth.jpg) Vateshran Barth | *Song-keepers. Glory-tongues. They remember the people of the clan. Every leader that falls, every warrior that bleeds—the vateshran knows them all. Each who survive the Rites earns a vateshran song that will last forever.* <br> — [Rites Matron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Rites_Matron) |

Vateshrans are a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) class of lorekeepers and storytellers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-STOTDFB1-1)</sup> They keep the history of the [clans of the Reach](Lore_Reachman_Clans.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Madearn-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Eslyn-3)</sup> Some of the Vateshrans are focused primarily on the history of a singular clan,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Aydolan-4)</sup> while others mainly travel across the Reach to share and learn stories of other clans.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Vateshran_Barth-5)</sup> They are also responsible for the [ritualistic commemoration](Lore_Ancestor_Worship.md#Other_Traditions) of the dead ancestors of their clan through [music](Lore_Music.md) and [song](Lore_Songs.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RM-6)</sup> Vateshrans are people of great importance and are widely respected by the Reachfolk clans,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Chief-7)</sup> as they are responsible for passing down the ancient traditions.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-RC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-CSS-9)</sup>

## Lorekeepers of the Reach
*The history of the Reach, its people, its relics … I know them all. Because if I didn't, they'd be lost.*
—Vateshran Barth [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0f/ON-place-Red_Eagle_Redoubt_%28Sacred_Cave%29_03.jpg/200px-ON-place-Red_Eagle_Redoubt_%28Sacred_Cave%29_03.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Red_Eagle_Redoubt_(Sacred_Cave)_03.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Red_Eagle_Redoubt_(Sacred_Cave)_03.jpg) Sacred Cave in Red Eagle Redoubt [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png/200px-ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Vateshran_Hollows.png) Vateshran Hollows Although vateshrans primarily commemorate their ancestors and record the history of the clans through the song they occasionally keep written records. Verses of the song of the Red Eagle were carved into stone pillars in the sacred cave in [Red Eagle Redoubt](Lore_Red_Eagle_Redoubt.md),<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RES-10)</sup> and guarded by the vateshrans.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Vateshran_Ovra-11)</sup> Written works of the legendary Vateshran Tosmorn were a source of inspiration for the generations of Reachfolk vateshrans, bards and storytellers of Tamriel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TTWOT-12)</sup> The tales of vateshrans are occasionally called sagas.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Daixth-13)</sup> They are usually performed in the form of recital. Vateshrans may request the payment for their performances.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Vateshran_Calbokh-14)</sup>

Although vateshrans are responsible for the keeping and performing of the songs, they typically do not associate themselves with bards, which they hold in low regard.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Vateshran_Barth-5)</sup> Works of vateshrans are occasionally transcribed by scholars.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TBOKL-15)</sup>

Reachfolk typically share their oral stories around roaring fires, infusing their narratives with intense emotion and captivating presentation.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-RBT-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-BP-17)</sup> This storytelling tradition encompasses a rich poetry heritage, featuring epic poems adorned with invocations and exploring themes of both grandeur and tragedy.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TTWOT-12)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Destozuno-18)</sup>

Spiritblood Champions who successfully pass the trials in the Vateshran Hollows earn a vateshran song that will last forever.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-RM-6)</sup>

Certain stories, such as *[The Hunter and the Bleeding Tree](Lore_Scary_Tales_of_the_Deep_Folk,_Book_3.md)* predate even the most ancient vateshrans. They were passed down from clan to clan over generations and often there are many variations of the same song.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-STOTDF3-19)</sup>

Vateshrans often carry [shillelaghs](Lore_Shillelagh.md), wooden tools covered in knobs that serve as walking sticks.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-Madearn-2)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-MQI-20)</sup>

## Known Vateshrans
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-npc-Vateshran_Ovra.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Vateshran_Ovra.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vateshran_Ovra.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vateshran_Ovra.jpg) Vateshran Ovra [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/15/ON-npc-Vateshran_Calbokh.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Vateshran_Calbokh.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vateshran_Calbokh.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Vateshran_Calbokh.jpg) Vateshran Calbokh - **[Aydolan](Lore_Aydolan.md)** — The first and the most famous vateshran of the [Spiritblood Clan](Lore_Spiritblood_Clan.md). He was active in ancient times, long before [1E 2502](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2502) and created set of trials in [Vateshran Hollows](Lore_Vateshran_Hollows.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Aydolan-4)</sup> He passed those trials himself and earned the title of Spiritblood Champion.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive-21)</sup> After his death he remained on Nirn to guide generations of Reachfolk until his spirit was destroyed in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-VR-22)</sup>
- **[Ovra](Lore_Ovra.md)** — Keeper of the tale of meeting between [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md) and [Red Eagle](Lore_Red_Eagle.md). She was active in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and aided [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md) in his quest to recover the lost art of [Shehai](Lore_Shehai.md) by allowing him to access and read the ancient inscriptions in the sacred cave of Red Eagle.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-Vateshran_Ovra-11)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-RES-10)</sup>
- **[Barth](Lore_Barth.md)** — Traveling vateshran focused on preserving stories all around the Reach. Vateshran Barth was an author of *[The Battle of Karthspire Lea](Lore_The_Battle_of_Karthspire_Lea.md)*.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-TBOKL-15)</sup> He was active [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and helped to resolve conflict between [Eagleseers](Lore_Eagleseer_Clan.md) and [Six-Fords](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Six-Fords).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Vateshran_Barth-5)</sup>
- **[Tosmorn](Lore_Tosmorn.md)** — Legendary vateshran from times when Reachfolk culture was at its apex. His existence is uncertain. He penned several epic poems and tragedies. His works inspired generations of the Reachfolk vateshrans, bards and storytellers of Tamriel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TTWOT-12)</sup>
- **[Calbokh](Lore_Calbokh.md)** — Traveling vateshran focused on preserving stories all around the Reach. Vateshran Calbokh was knowledgeable about the tales of King [Faolan](Lore_Red_Eagle.md), [Chieftain Fiasof](Lore_Fiasof.md) and [Emperor Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md). He was active in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Vateshran_Calbokh-14)</sup>
- **[Eoinola](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Eoinola)** — Vateshran devoted to the tales of Lorkh and the creation.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TDD-23)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-NZ-24)</sup>
- **[Allaghach](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Allaghach)** — Vateshran devoted to the tales from the Dawn of Karth.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-BRR-25)</sup>

### Vateshran Barth's songs
[The Battle of Karthspire Lea](Lore_The_Battle_of_Karthspire_Lea.md)
- *It was not so much a battle as it was a sea of blood. The Six-Ford and Eagleseers did not fight, they merely bled. It is said that the grass of the lea was stained so thoroughly with red, it appeared black from a distance.*

*
- There was no clear victor. Unusual, for a conflict between the Reachfolk. Many battles have been fought with the kind of rage and intensity that this one was fought, but historically, one force always yields. It is the nature of combat. One party, no matter how enraged, weakens under pressure. Such was not the case with this battle. Neither side waned. They flung themselves at one another, ceaselessly crashing against each other like waves against a cliffside.

- The men and women of each clan seemed to fight as if they intended to die. There was no thought of victory, only carnage.

- The leader of the Eagleseers, [Maddorfa](Lore_Maddorfa.md), took a mortal wound very early in the battle. A spear lodged itself in his leg, rendering him unable to walk. He insisted those close to him bind him to a nearby pillar so that he need not stand in order to continue fighting. The battle became something darker than winning or losing. It was rage unhindered, unchecked, and about killing as many of the enemy as possible.

*
- *Few Reachfolk walked away from Karthspire Lea that day.*

**Saga of the Cloud Ghosts**<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Daixth-13)</sup>

[The Fish-People in the Dark Places](Lore_Scary_Tales_of_the_Deep_Folk,_Book_1.md) Many years ago, she said, their scouts reported sightings of startlingly humanoid creatures coming and going from the caves below where the clan lived.

At first the clan thought these creatures to be Goblins, yet these Goblins were hairless, eyeless, and had dead-gray skin like that of a fish. These "fish-people," as the clan took to calling them, had never approached the camp and never attacked any Reachfolk. Yet the clan refused to take chances with such obvious abominations.

The clan-chief formed a war party and led it into the caves below, determined to force out these fish-people and secure her clan's territory. Yet the party encountered no resistance, even after they searched all the caves. They found no sign of any fish-people inside, despite their scout's report that a number entered the caves mere hours before the war party arrived.

Later that night, in the darkest hours before the morning, the first attack occurred. Several Reachfolk were silently murdered, their bodies left gruesomely displayed, while others disappeared entirely. Once again, the clan-chief gathered her best hunters and strode into the caves. She planned to root out the fish-people who had attacked her clan and destroy them. And once again, a full day's search revealed only empty caves.

That night, the clan remained on full alert, but no more attacks came. Each night for weeks after, the clan posted pickets. Yet no more attacks came, and there were no more sightings of the fish-people. More than a month passed before the clan-chief finally allowed her clan to resume their normal patrols, and that same night, the fish-people struck again. This time, they left the bodies of several elders strung up on the mountain, and worse still, several children vanished entirely, never to be seen again.

Enraged by yet another cowardly attack and an egregious assault on the most vulnerable members of her clan, the clan-chief flew into a righteous fury. She called forth her witches and shamans, and summoned additional magic support from neighboring clans. One by one, they sealed the caves below the clan's land. She had them collapse each wound in the mountain with rage, magic, and force of will, and when they were done, there was nothing but heavy piles of broken rock where the caves once stood.

The clan remained wary for many months after, but no more attacks came, and no more fish-people were ever seen. The clan's clan-chief was wise to seal the caves, yet the lack of vengeance forever gnawed at her and her clan. Who were those fish-people, they asked, and how were they able to hide unseen in the caves?

## Notes
- Vateshran's Rites are set of trials performed in the [Vateshran Hollows](Lore_Vateshran_Hollows.md) created by Vateshran [Aydolan](Lore_Aydolan.md). Despite the name the rites and trials performed there are not designed for Vateshrans but as a rite of passage for members of the [Spiritblood Clan](Lore_Spiritblood_Clan.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-Aydolan-4)</sup>
- The [Infinite Archive](Lore_Infinite_Archive.md)'s keepers held great reverence for the oral traditions of [Bosmeri](Lore_Bosmer.md) [Spinners](Lore_Spinners.md) and Reachfolk Vateshrans, yet they refrained from storing these traditions directly. Instead, they were only documented in the archive when transcribed by external parties or presented as observational writings from scholars, or notes designed to aid performers in optimizing their acts. However, this indirect approach often led to a loss in the translation process, rendering these traditions more obscure in the eyes of [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s archive.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-LAIA-26)</sup>
