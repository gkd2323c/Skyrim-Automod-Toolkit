*Anu encompassed, and encompasses, all things.*
— *[The Monomyth](Lore_The_Monomyth.md)* Anu, or **Anu the Everything**, is thought to be the quintessential form of **Stasis** and **Order**, the anthropomorphization of two primal forces (the other being [Padomay](Lore_Padomay.md), Change and Chaos).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAA-1)</sup> Anu or his equivalent under a different name is present in every culture's traditions; for instance, the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) refer to him as **Ahnurr**, and he is a "littermate" to Fadomai.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> He is known as **Satak** to the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> He is known as the **Light** to the [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-TLAD-4)</sup> He also shares similarities to the Argonian figure [Atak](Lore_Gods_A.md#Atak.2C_The_Great_Root).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Cot R-5)</sup> Other names for Anu include **Is**, **Ak**, **Bird**, and **Good**.

## Mythology
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/LG-quest-Prologue_01.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Prologue_01.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Prologue_01.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Prologue_01.jpg) A cosmic mass within or encompassing the Aurbis [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/57/ON-map-Aurbis.jpg/200px-ON-map-Aurbis.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Aurbis.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-map-Aurbis.jpg) The Aurbis as depicted on a map
### Anuad
In the [Anuad](Lore_Anuad.md), an [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) creation myth from the [Mythic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), Anu and Padomay were brothers that came into the void. Anu and Padomay wandered the void, and their interplay of Light and Darkness brought forth Nir, the personification of Aurbis and Possibility. Anu and Padomay were both amazed and delighted by Nir's appearance, but Nir only loved Anu, and so Padomay retreated, bitter. Later, when Nir was pregnant, Padomay came to confess his love to her, but she told him she only loved Anu, and so Padomay attacked her, striking her in rage.

Anu arrived, fought Padomay, and banished him outside time. Shortly thereafter, Nir gave birth to creation, and died. Anu in his grief entered the [Sun](Lore_Sun.md), and [slept](Lore_Amaranth.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup>

As Anu slept, life began on the [Twelve Worlds](Lore_Kalpa.md) of [Creation](Lore_Aetherius.md). After many ages, Padomay returned to creation and hated what he saw. He swung his sword and shattered the Twelve Worlds in their alignment. Anu awoke and fought Padomay one last time, defeating him and emerging the victor. Anu then salvaged the remnants of the worlds, forming them into one and creating [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).

Padomay then arose once more and struck Anu through his chest. In this brief struggle, Anu then dragged both himself and Padomay outside of time forever, saving Nirn from further harm. The blood they spilt in their clash led to the creation of the gods. The Daedra were born from Padomay's blood exclusively, detaching them from Creation; similarly, the stars arose solely from the blood of Anu. By contrast, the Aedra were spawned from the mixing of the blood of both Padomay and Anu, allowing them to be 'capable of both good and evil' and tying them to Creation. On Nirn, only the [Ehlnofey](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) and [Hist](Lore_Hist.md) worlds survived out of the original Twelve, and their remains eventually became [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AA-6)</sup>

### Altmeri
The creation myth of the [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) begins before the start of the [Dawn Era](Lore_Dawn_Era.md) and the beginning of time: the primordial force of Anu the Everything, who encompassed and encompasses all things, created Anuiel, the soul of all things, so it could know itself.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> Anuiel in turn created [Sithis](Lore_Sithis.md) for the same purpose, as the sum up of all limitations which it would use to differentiate between its attributes and ponder itself, and their interrelation created the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md), where the Original Spirits, the [Et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md), emerged before the creation of the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) as "aspects of [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md)".<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> The ancient Aldmer believed they are the relatively feeble descendants of the Aedra ("Aedra" roughly translates to "ancestor spirit"), distant offspring of [those of the Aedra](Lore_Ehlnofey.md) who populated the Mundus so that it might last despite [Lorkhan](Lore_Lorkhan.md)'s deception, diminished from the might of their progenitors over the generations.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup> Per the creation myth of the Altmer, after their progenitors discovered the deception of Lorkhan, their leader [Auri-El](Lore_Auri-El.md) begged Anu to take them back, but Anu would not because he had already created something else to take their place, instead the more merciful [Anui-El](Lore_Anui-El.md) created a [Bow](Lore_Auriel%27s_Bow.md) and [Shield](Lore_Auriel%27s_Shield.md) For Auri-El to use in the war against Lorkhan.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup>

A shrine to Anu exists in the Altmeri site of Torinaan, where Holy Waters are considered especially useful when used to honor Anu, who shaped the cosmos in the Dawn Era.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Tor-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-BOT8-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-BLTAA-9)</sup><sup>[[10]](#cite_note-AQI-10)</sup>

Some sources claim that, rather than viewing the Aurbis as a single Wheel, the Altmer instead view it more akin to "a telescope that stretches all the way back to the eye of Anui-El, with Padomaics innumerable along its infinite walls".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-11)</sup> Notably, Magnus is associated with the telescope in Altmeri religion.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VOF-12)</sup>

### Dunmeri
Dunmeri belief regarding Anu is not uniform.

The [Clockwork Apostles](Lore_Clockwork_Apostles.md) of [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) share the Altmeri belief that Anu is the primordial being of singularity. Anu sundered himself for wisdom's sake, seeking to better understand his nature, and all forces and entities that exist trace their roots to him as values that reside within his vastness. They believe Padomay and the Padomaic forces such as the Daedra are merely an illusion, the result of the Great Lie of Lorkhan who tricked the et'Ada and steered them away from the face of Anu, by making them view themselves as distinct and whole and give themselves names. The Clockwork Apostles believe Sotha Sil wishes to reverse this error, the et'Ada's sin, through his work on the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md), by bringing [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) to a state of Anuic unity, Anuvanna'si.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TTIS-13)</sup>

In contrast, the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) god [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) teaches that Sithis is directly responsible for the creation of the universe, the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md). As described within his Sermons, and adjacent material extrapolating on them, before Sithis was "Nothing". This nothing is the Dunmeri understanding of Anu, as the embodiment of Stasis, its unchanging nature is viewed as nothing, "Stasis asks merely for nothing, for itself, which is nothing". Sithis is then credited for sundering the Nothing, and mutating the parts, producing the possibilities understood to be Aurbis.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV10-14)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Sb-15)</sup> It is said that later, when [Anui-El](Lore_Anuiel.md), a demon similar to the stasis of ANU, surfaced, it, along with the Aedra, enslaved everything in [realms of everlasting imperfection](Lore_Aetherius.md). In reaction to this, Sithis created and sent his son, Lorkhan, to destroy the universe and free the slaves.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Sb-15)</sup> In some of his writings, it is said that at the Altar of Padhome, there exists a [secret door](Lore_CHIM.md), one created by Lorkhan.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-LOV3-16)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LOV13-17)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LOV33-18)</sup>. This door is the promise of PSJJJJ, which is a [flowering scheme](Lore_Amaranth.md) of Aurbis.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-LOV25-19)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-LOV37-20)</sup> Vivec explains more on the nature of Sithis/Padomay within his sermons, describing it as the drowned and smiling reflection of something within water.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LOV10-14)</sup>

Later in the Third Era, Vivec shared further teachings with his followers. According to Vivec, Stasis and Change, Anu and Padomay, are infinite forces and realms residing in the infinite [Void](Lore_Void.md), the latter itself being infinite and thus paradoxically enclosing the others in a manner akin to an encircling sphere. At the intersection of the twin forces, where they touch, lies a "perfect circle of pattern and possibility". This circle is the Wheel, and that Wheel acts as the foundation of Aurbis. Vivec claims that outside the Wheel exists the Void, and that if the Void contains more aspects than just Stasis and Change, they cannot be named as they are outside of true language. Vivec later explained that Anu and Padomay "awakened" during the process of sub-creation caused by their intersection as "to see your antithesis is to finally awaken". It is said that in reaction to this "each gave birth to their souls", [Anuiel](Lore_Anuiel.md) and Sithis. Afterwards the firstborns of Anu and Padomay formed, the deities [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) and Lorkhan respectively.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-VT-21)</sup> Each of these souls regarded the Aurbis in their own part, and from that came the [et'Ada](Lore_Gods.md), the "original patterns".<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-22)</sup>

Vivec cites mythology of Anu and Padomay to support his interpretation of the concept of love. More, he claims that in truth, Padomay does not exist, and Anu in fact has no double.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-LOV35-23)</sup> Alluding to the nature of Anu as the [Godhead](Lore_Godhead.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-11)</sup>

### Khajiiti
In the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) creation myth, the equivalent of Anu and Padomay are Ahnurr and Fadomai. The pair were mates who had two litters of children, the first being the Aedra and the second Daedra. Though Ahnurr was content with this, Fadomai secretly tricked Ahnurr into helping create one last litter.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Wo CMA-24)</sup> In contrast to the stories about the Stasis-Change conflict in other cultures, the roles of creator and aggressor are reversed, with Ahnurr attacking Fadomai during the birth of [Nirni](Lore_Nirni.md), [Azurah](Lore_Azurah.md) and the [twin](Lore_Jode.md) [moons](Lore_Jone.md). Following his attack, Fadomai fled to the [Void](Lore_Void.md) to birth the final member of the litter, [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md). This eventually would lead to Lorkhaj creating [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) for Nirni to exist within, and tricking the rest of the gods to become trapped in it.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Wo CMA-24)</sup>

In earlier stories about the Khajiiti pantheon, there are a few known mentions of Ahnurr and his relationships with his children. His firstborn, [Akha](Lore_Akha.md) was known as his favored son, whom Ahnurr encouraged to find love.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWa S-25)</sup> Another spirit, [Y'ffer](Lore_Y%27ffer.md) —though never venerated by the Khajiit—was acknowledged as Ahnurr's bastard child and that their temperaments were unalike.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-TWo S-26)</sup> On the other end of the spectrum, Anhurr banished both [Merrunz](Lore_Merrunz.md) and [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md), the former for his destructive nature, and the latter for her rebelliousness.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-TAS-27)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-TWa S-25)</sup>

A work from prior to the [Riddle-Thar Epiphany](Lore_Rid-Thar-ri%27Datta.md), describes [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md)'s involvement with the [Middle Dawn](Lore_Middle_Dawn.md) and the dance of the [Marukhati Selective](Lore_Marukhati_Selective.md). In this story, Boethra was called from her battle with [Orkha](Lore_Malacath.md) by the [Blue Star](Lore_Mnemo-Li.md) and brought to the [Adamantine Tower](Lore_Adamantine_Tower.md) by [Khenarthi](Lore_Khenarthi.md). There Boethra is described as witnessing the Selectives speaking in [Monkey Truth](Lore_Marukh.md), *"[saying] lies in a way that made them true"* and that it made her feel something akin to fear. As previously mentioned in other traditions, Anhurr was said to have exiled Boethra. However, in this particular story Boethra recalls that it was Akha that exiled her, but the Monkey Truth was claiming that Akha never existed, along with his children and the lands he seeded.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Bladesongs-28)</sup>

In the face of her self-doubt about her own existence, and knowing the Selectives must not succeed, Boethra rallied. She used what she learnt during her exile in the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) to calculate and enact the cuts required to destroy them and the lies they were attempting to make real. However, she sensed an opportunity created by them and *"found a tunnel that led to the fate they sought"*. Here the story describes Boethra encountering twelve spinning [wheels](Lore_Aurbis.md) surrounded by fire, while beyond that two great serpents fought and upon seeing them, Boethra recognizes the grain of truth in the Selectives dance. These two serpents can be considered a representation of the conflict between Anu and Padomay, with the following quote providing a literary description of the two forces.

*One was a flame-feathered serpent, brilliant and pure, with crystal scales and a head like that of a hunting bird, its eyes sharp and clear, its mane an argument against all the [Mannish](Lore_Man.md) impurity of all the known worlds.*

*There to meet it was a serpent of the blackest scales, and all the Void seemed to come with it, so much that one would think the feathered could never stand against it, and yet it did. And this serpent's eyes burned red as blood, and its scales moved and shifted with new ideas that were born and died as soon as they appeared. Despite this chaos, its mane was white and gentle, and in it Boethra saw a fleeting chance for peace along the Wheels.*
— *[The Bladesongs of Boethra, Volume V](Lore_The_Bladesongs_of_Boethra,_Volume_V.md)* When the hawk-serpent attacked the dark serpent once more, Boethra summoned all her blades and struck at its eye, repelling it. She then joined with the dark serpent, drawing upon its black flames and forming a blade and [armor](Lore_Ebony_Mail.md). It is then said she observed the flame-hawk serpent, and recognized it for what is was and what it wanted. She then recited the Will Against Rule and struck, cutting concepts at strange angles and ending the [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md). This event was later named the Division of Heaven by mortals who recalled the Middle Dawn.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Bladesongs-28)</sup>

In regard to the inhabitants of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md), the Khajiit mythology holds that the attention of Ahnurr is not something to be wanted. One tradition says that Fadomai gifted the twin moons ([Ja-Kha'jay](Lore_Ja-Kha%27jay.md)) the Lunar Lattice, and said that its motions would act as a barrier that would protect all of them from Ahnurr's rage, *"And the moons left to take their place in the heavens. And Ahnurr growled and shook the Great Darkness, but he could not cross the Lattice."*<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Wo CMA-24)</sup> In a following story from the same tradition, after [Y'ffer](Lore_Y%27ffer.md) shouted out the secret that would allow entities to cross the lattice, Azurah made sure to block Anhurr and Lorkhaj's ears so they alone could not hear the secret.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-Wo CMA-24)</sup>

### Yokudan
[Yokudans](Lore_Yokudan.md) see Anu as Satak, the First Serpent. When Satak the First Serpent came into being, it carried all the [Worlds to come](Lore_Aetherius.md) within the glimmer of its scales. Those worlds were trapped by the all encompassing size of Satak, his coiling allowing no room to breath, and so called out for help. As nothing existed outside Satak, aid had to come from within, so the empty stomach of Satak, [Akel](Lore_Akel.md), answered the call. Akel drove Satak into a craze of Hunger, and it began to devour itself, creating more empty space so that things could begin. However there was no time to truly be within this flux, and things were devoured as soon as they were born. Eventually Akel drove Satak to bite its own [Heart](Lore_Nir.md), and that was the end. Akel however, refused to stop, even in death, and caused Satak to shed its skin to begin anew. From this [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md), was born. With the reborn First Serpent came the cycle of [Worldskins](Lore_Kalpa.md), and spirits began to form. <sup>[[2]](#cite_note-TM-2)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-VOF-12)</sup>

### Argonian
Though [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) society as a whole doesn't have an established singular creation story, the Adzi-Kostleel tribe claims that two beings, [Atak](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atak) and [Kota](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kota), fought until they joined together as a singular being known as [Atakota](Lore_Atakota.md). When they did this, they shed their skin and created a Shadow. This Shadow, though initially intending to devour everything, soon came to see the creations of Atakota as its own children, and instead gave them the gift of change, which would later come to be known as [Death](Lore_Death.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Cot R-5)</sup>

Mentions of Atak also appear in [Solstice](Lore_Solstice_(island).md) Argonian culture, their ancestors having split from their society on the mainland prior to the [Duskfall](Lore_Duskfall.md). This suggests that Atak had wider prominence in Argonian culture in the past.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-CM136-29)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-AFR-30)</sup>

### Breton
A simplified and secular interpretation of *The Monomyth* can be found within the [Bretonic](Lore_Breton.md) tale *[The Light and the Dark](Lore_The_Light_and_The_Dark.md)*. In it, two immortal entities representing Order and Chaos chose [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) to be their eternal battleground. This everlasting battle would create energies so powerful it distorted the world and created both the "people of [et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md)", who would in turn give rise to the gods, by believing in their myths for so long and so strongly, it caused the energies unleashed by the conflict of the Light and the Dark to bring them into being. According to the grandfather, all of creation exists to echo the battle between the Light and the Dark.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-t Lat D-31)</sup>

### Skaal
The [Skaal](Lore_Skaal.md) believe in a single deity, the [All-Maker](Lore_All-Maker.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-COTAM-32)</sup> though they also recognize [the Adversary](Lore_The_Adversary.md), a malevolent and multifaceted tester who works to corrupt the All-Maker's dominion.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ASS-33)</sup> The perpetual struggle between the two bears some resemblance to the one between Anu and Padomay. Similar to Dunmeri interpretations of Padomay and Sithis, the Skaal seemingly view their benevolent primordial force as Padomay, as opposed to Anu.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-34)</sup>
