# Peryite

—Kesh the Clean<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRKesh-1)</sup> Peryite (listen Link ⏵<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESO_Live_Episode_1-2)</sup>) is one of the [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) that dwell within the realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). As the **Daedric Prince of Pestilence**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONMarthine-3)</sup> his sphere of influence includes tasks, natural order (not to be confused with the perfect order of [Jyggalag](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jyggalag)), contamination, and pestilence.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRLoading-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAAYQ-5)</sup> Peryite's sphere is described as "ordering the lowest orders of Oblivion".<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBOD-6)</sup> He is also known as the **Daedra of Pestilence and Plague**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-DFPeryite Dialogue2-7)</sup> the **Taskmaster**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBOD-6)</sup> the **Blighted Lord**,<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AN-8)</sup> **Figuring father**,<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Sermon Zero-9)</sup> the **Lord of Infection and Pestilence**,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Filbert-10)</sup> the **Bringer of Disease and Pestilence**,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Sot A-11)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-12)</sup><sup>:313</sup> the **Lord of the Natural Order**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONMarthine-3)</sup> the **Master of Tasks**,<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOJorvuld-13)</sup> and the **Lord of Abundant Pus and Bountiful Vomit**.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Filbert-10)</sup> Peryite "blesses" his worshipers with [diseases](Lore_Disease.md), and is considered one of the more destructive Princes.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-OO-14)</sup> He is typically depicted as a green four-legged [dragon](Lore_Dragon.md), and often takes on the form of one, with the likeness to [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) seen as some primordial and curious jest.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-Census-15)</sup> Peryite occasionally appears as ghostly apparitions of vermin, such as [skeevers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skeever).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SROnly-16)</sup> The coiling, snake-like dragon and the skeever are two of Peryite's most sacred symbols.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Filbert-10)</sup> The [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) goddess [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md) is said to give Peryite the spirits of skeevers when they die.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-KCAHC-17)</sup> According to some of his followers, Peryite abhors heterogeny, thus offerings of disease given to him should always be of the same strain.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Plague Instructions-18)</sup>

Peryite is considered one of the weakest of the Princes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-SRLoading-4)</sup> Some unfortunate [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) Churls and Caitiffs find themselves in the employ of the Taskmaster.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-LFAYQ-19)</sup> Peryite's summoning day is the [Ninth of Rain's Hand](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peryite%27s_Summoning_Day),<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-20)</sup> but an incense can be created from vampire dust, one silver ingot, a deathbell flower, and a flawless ruby that will allow one to communicate with the Prince.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-SROnly-16)</sup> His enemies are [Ebonarm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ebonarm), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md), [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina), and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DFFactions-21)</sup>

Peryite's breath purportedly pollutes "both cloud and pool".<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SOD-22)</sup> Peryite is in charge of keeping daedrons - [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) imbued with sufficient purpose and function from the exertion of Daedric or mortal will - from causing damage in Oblivion. Such will-imbued creatia can form 'potentia vortices' which, if unattended, can form destructive 'realm-rips', phenomena which Peryite prevents from forming across Oblivion.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LFNHTRPAYQ2-23)</sup> When asked, [Fa-Nuit-Hen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fa-Nuit-Hen)'s tutor Riparius says that "trying to keep ahead of it all keeps Peryite mighty busy, but nobody really feels sorry for him." He then goes on to say that Peryite "earned it".<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-LFNHTRPAYQ2-23)</sup> Despite his weak reputation, his plagues have often been deemed dangerous enough to potentially wipe out all of Tamriel.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOJorvuld-13)</sup> and it has been said that, despite Peryite being perceived as less important, people fall ill all the time, indicative of his influence.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Gadayn-24)</sup> In addition, Peryite's plagues have shown the capacity to infect other Daedric Princes and their [realms](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) and Daedric servants. Such was the case with the realm corroding plague Peryite unleashed against Apocrypha during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), which afflicted both Apocrypha's inhabitants and Prince with debilitating pain, gradually severed Mora's connection to his domain, and threatened to dissolve the realm itself to nothingness.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Note-25)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Mora Calamity-26)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Mora Unhealthy-27)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Calamity-28)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Unhealthy-29)</sup> It is also theorized that Peryite may have been involved in the creation of the [Thrassian Plague](Lore_Thrassian_Plague.md) developed by the Sload.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TAAYQ-5)</sup>

Peryite often takes a polite or formal tone when dealing with mortals.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-DFPeryite3-30)</sup> Instead of reacting with rage when rejected, he keeps a cool head.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DFPeryite-31)</sup> Rot is sometimes described by the [Erabenimsun Tribe](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Erabenimsun_Tribe) as "the touch of Peryite".<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ROA-32)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4c/ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Glyphic_of_Lost_Memories.jpg) A memory of the meeting between Peryite, Vaermina, and Hermaeus Mora A legend regarding the viridescent [dragon frog](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dragon_Frog) states that the little amphibians were once a breed of [giant green frogs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Death_Hopper) that grew as large as [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md). These frogs envied the Dovah's ability to fly, so they sought out Peryite and made a pact with him. The Daedric Prince granted their wish, but it came with a price. The frogs got the wings they wanted, but the size of their bodies was greatly reduced.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ONVDF-33)</sup>

At some point in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md), the Daedric Prince [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) sought out the aid of the other Princes in imprisoning the Daedric Prince [Ithelia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ithelia), whose manipulations of [fate](Lore_Fate.md) and the [Many Paths](Lore_Many_Paths.md) and planned use of the [Loom of the Untraveled Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Loom_of_the_Untraveled_Road) threatened to unravel all of [reality](Lore_Aurbis.md).<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-Ithelia-34)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-Leramil Wise-35)</sup><sup>[[34]](#cite_note-Untraveled-36)</sup> Eventually an alliance was formed between Hermaeus Mora and [Azura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Azura), [Boethiah](Lore_Boethiah.md) and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), who proceeded to defeat Ithelia through their combined efforts and design a plan to erase the very memory of her existence from reality, so that the danger would never return.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Mora-37)</sup><sup>[[36]](#cite_note-Dandera-38)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-Prisoner-39)</sup> Two of the Princes whose support Hermaeus Mora sought were [Vaermina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vaermina) and Peryite. However, Mora's attempt to secure their approval for his plans proved futile. Despite Mora's insistence that Ithelia represented a grave danger to reality, Vaermina and Peryite argued that she shouldn't be punished solely because of something she might do, and that the spheres of the Princes were sancrosanct. Believing that Hermaeus Mora was overstepping his boundaries, the two Princes refused to support him. It was then that Mora revealed he was going to proceed with his plan regardless. Mora shattered the statue of Vaermina and cast his spell, removing the memory of Ithelia from every mind across all reality.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Hidden-40)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-Vaermina-41)</sup>

### Second Era

#### Before the Interregnum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/04/LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg/200px-LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-book-Knahaten_Flu.jpg) Plague doctors carting away corpses and boarding up condemned houses during the Knahaten Flu The text *[Deal with a Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Deal_with_a_Daedric_Prince)* contains an account by healer Rindral Thirano, of a deal he made with Peryite in [2E 573](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_573). A series of disasters had befallen the city of [Tear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Tear), starting with a series of chill nights in the summer, which was followed by a flood and then a miasma that engulfed the city and afflicted both people and animals with a number of terrible plagues, including the grippe, ague, consumptive coughs, Fiddler's quinsy, and flying heart ticks. After the number of afflicted became too great for the city's healers to treat with their [spells](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spells) and [potions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy), and surrounding communities refused to lend aid for fear of the diseases spreading to them, one healer named Rindral Thirano decided the only solution was to bargain directly with the source of the disasters and plagues itself, Peryite. Summoned forth through a spell cast with specific alchemical ingredients, Peryite manifested in the form of a ghostly Skeever. Rindral furiously demanded that Peryite put an end to the plagues he'd inflicted on Tear, only for the amused Prince to ask how the healer planned to force a Daedric Prince to do his bidding. Rindral threatened that he'd amassed knowledge of disease during his life and would amass more, he'd spread this knowledge as far as he could so that people could use it to cut disease off at the root. In this manner Peryite's sphere would be reduced and his own influence would lessen. Peryite conceded to make a deal, promising that, for a year and a day, Tear would be free of illness. The following day people began to recover and the diseases subsided. Rindral himself, however, paid a heavy price. His limbs began to weaken, his skin grew scaly and lizardlike on one arm while boils grew on the other, food lost all taste and sleep and breathing became difficult. Soon, Rindral's sight started to fail and he finished penning his last account, secluded, as he felt something moving in his chest and cursed ever having made a deal with a Daedric Prince. As Rindral lay in agony, Tear's citizens celebrated their good health outside. Rindral's rotted corpse was discovered in his offices and burned to prevent whatever had afflicted him from spreading. Some witnesses, however, claimed the body was still moving as it was consigned to the plague fire.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-Deal-42)</sup>

Some believe Peryite is responsible for the [Knahaten Flu](Lore_Knahaten_Flu.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-LTP-43)</sup> No one knows what brought the flu to [Orcrest](Lore_Orcrest.md), but some natives blamed the [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), while others theorized that Peryite was responsible.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-FSOTF-44)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a pair of Peryite worshipers sought to enter the city and gather evidence, which they could bend to claim that Peryite was responsible for the flu.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-UP-45)</sup> Nothing substantial came of the investigation, but they found a few scraps parroting the aforementioned theories.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-UP-45)</sup>

#### During the Interregnum
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-load-Scalecaller_Peak.jpg/200px-ON-load-Scalecaller_Peak.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Scalecaller_Peak.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Scalecaller_Peak.jpg) Statues of Peryite on Scalecaller Peak [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/ON-npc-Zaan_the_Scalecaller_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Zaan_the_Scalecaller_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Zaan_the_Scalecaller_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Zaan_the_Scalecaller_02.jpg) Zaan the Scalecaller wielding Spellbreaker The ancient [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md), Zaan the Scalecaller, turned to worship Peryite after her Dragon Lord, [Thurvokun](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thurvokun), abandoned her.<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-ESOZaan-46)</sup> Zaan was worthy enough for Peryite to grant her [Spellbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spellbreaker) at some point in time.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-Zaan-47)</sup> In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a Peryite Cult took up residence in Scalecaller Peak. The cult worshiped Zaan and experimented with a gruesome disease in hopes of unleashing a plague to infect all of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOJorvuld-13)</sup><sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOScalecaller Peak-48)</sup> They infected the creatures that inhabited the mountain with the disease. [Ogres](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ogre), [giants](Lore_Giant.md), and [Leimenids](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leimenid) were among those infected.<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-ESOScalecaller-49)</sup> Their symptoms should have been debilitating, but the disease unintentionally made them more powerful and excessively aggressive.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-ESOJorvuld-13)</sup><sup>[[48]](#cite_note-RONMV2-50)</sup> Zaan and the Peryite cult were stopped by the [Undaunted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Undaunted) at the behest of Breton historian Jorvuld Davaux.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-POP-51)</sup> Following her demise, Zaan's [wraith](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Wraith) possessed Jorvuld and infected him with her plague in revenge.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-POP-51)</sup> It is unknown whether or not Davaux found the means with which to cure himself.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-POP-51)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-ONZaan-52)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a group known as [Peryite's Chosen](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Peryite%27s_Chosen) attempted to spread the word of Peryite throughout [Senchal](Lore_Senchal.md) after the Dragons were released from the [Halls of Colossus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Halls_of_Colossus).<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-BKPFP-53)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-UP-45)</sup> They also had a presence in [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isle.md).<sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TDC-54)</sup> Some cultists of Peryite expressed the belief that [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md)'s failure to take over Nirn with the power of the [Four Ambitions](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Four_Ambitions) may bring profit to their patron.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Eorda-55)</sup>

#### Invasion of Apocrypha
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/68/ON-place-Bal_Sunnar_%28future%29_05.jpg/200px-ON-place-Bal_Sunnar_%28future%29_05.jpg)](File_ON-place-Bal_Sunnar_(future)_05.jpg.md) [](File_ON-place-Bal_Sunnar_(future)_05.jpg.md) The temple to Peryite in Bal Sunnar's future [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/00/LO-misc-Peryite_Statue.jpg/200px-LO-misc-Peryite_Statue.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Peryite_Statue.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Peryite_Statue.jpg) Statue to Peryite in Bal Sunnar During that same time, the [Dremora](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dremora) [Torvesard](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Torvesard), former servant of Ithelia, approached Peryite and Vaermina, seeking their aid in uncovering the truth behind a recurring dream he had. The dream was a suppressed memory of the meeting between Hermaeus Mora, Vaermina, and Peryite, that was preserved due to the last act of Ithelia, who'd saved Torvesard at the last moment. Torvesard was told that Vaermina and Peryite had experienced the same dream, though in their case it was not quite as vivid. The two Princes, furious with Hermaeus Mora for altering their memories, formed an alliance with Torvesard and with each other, determined to enter [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md) and uncover the forgotten truth.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Torvesard-56)</sup><sup>[[55]](#cite_note-Torvesard Journal-57)</sup>

In the course of this effort, Peryite became involved in the events surrounding the [Time Knot](Lore_Dragon_Break.md) that enveloped the [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) township of Bal Sunnar, trapping it in a time loop from sometime after the Battle of Red Mountain to 2E 582, when it was undone by the [Psijic Monk](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) Saresea and the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md). The Knot was created by a Telvanni splinter group who sought to escape the rising rule of the [Tribunal](Lore_Tribunal.md) through the use of a Glyphic, an artifact of Hermaeus Mora. The Telvanni, recognized the Vestige and Saresea from previous loops and tried to stop them, tapping into the magic of the Knot to summon creatures from the time of ancient [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) or to conjure temporal duplicates of themselves to aid them in combat. The Knot itself resisted Saresea, exploding outward and transporting her and the Vestige both to the distant past and the distant future. When they were transported to the future, Saresea and the Vestige found themselves in a version of the city filled with Peryite's plague and creatures, the result of a desperate deal the Telvanni Matriarch had made with Peryite in order to stop them. Ultimately Saresea and the Vestige succeeded in defeating the Telvanni Matriarch and dispelling the Knot but at a cost: Saresea was fragmented and scattered across time. Though Hermaeus Mora, who had been observing, reached out and pulled her back together, she remained "multichronal", a being that manifests at multiple points in space and time simultaneously, both in the past and in the future, ranging from her own birth to her own death and beyond.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-UFT-58)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-Saresea-59)</sup><sup>[[58]](#cite_note-Nerile-60)</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Kovan-61)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f5/ON-crown_store-Kindred%27s_Concord_Style.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Kindred%27s_Concord_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Kindred%27s_Concord_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Kindred%27s_Concord_Style.jpg) Members of the Hidden Kindred in Apocrypha In order to uncover the truth Mora had hidden, Vaermina and Peryite had their followers form an alliance and form a new cult devoted to both Princes called the Hidden Kindred, which the high priest of Peryite, Blightcrown, assumed leadership of.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-New Cult-62)</sup> The Hidden Kindred also formed an alliance with Telvanni Master [Shelreni Baro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shelreni_Baro).<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Shelreni-63)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-Shelreni-63)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-Spirit-64)</sup>

In order to proceed with their plans, the Hidden Kindred sought to retrieve a powerful [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) called the [Fulcrum Obscura](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fulcrum_Obscura). The relic, crafted long ago by Hermaeus Mora for [Prior Durdryn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Durdryn), had the power to uncover any hidden secret and was kept within the necropolis of [Necrom](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Necrom).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-TPF-65)</sup><sup>[[64]](#cite_note-OTFO-66)</sup> Disguised as a prelate, Blightcrown infiltrated the city and had his Hidden Kindred place Censers of Corruption, plague spreading relics which contained "Peryite's blessed breath" throughout the chapel, hoping the afflicted [Keepers of the Dead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Keepers_of_the_Dead) would reveal the relic's location. Investigating the spread of the illness that had gripped the Keepers, the Vestige found evidence of the Hidden Kindred's activities and plans on the ship they'd used to arrive, and tracked them to the catacombs with the aid of Curate Gadayn. In the catacombs, the Vestige encountered Blightcrown and Torvesard, who fled with one half of the Fulcrum Obscura after the artifact cracked, as it contained the power they needed even when broken.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-KOTF-67)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-BO-68)</sup>

The Hidden Kindred carried out operations in multiple locations in service to their goals, including in the Alavelis [Glass](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Malachite) Mine and in the Anchre [Kwama](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kwama) Egg Mine, where Zaedare the Afflicted infected the eggs with diseased ichor, in an attempt to destroy Necrom by spreading the infection to it when the eggs reached its markets.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-Zaedare-69)</sup><sup>[[68]](#cite_note-Egg Mine-70)</sup><sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Under-71)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/ON-place-The_Mythos_11.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Mythos_11.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_11.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_11.jpg) Peryite's plague spreading through the Mythos, core of Apocrypha [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/21/ON-place-The_Mythos_10.jpg/200px-ON-place-The_Mythos_10.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_10.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-The_Mythos_10.jpg) Peryite's plague spreading through the Mythos, core of Apocrypha The Hidden Kindred's plans culminated in an invasion of Apocrypha itself. Blightcrown betrayed Shelreni Baro and sacrificed her, so Vaermina could possess her body and enter Apocrypha through a special portal formed using a spell from the [Black Book](Lore_Black_Books.md) The Tormenting Eye. A ritual which allowed the Prince to enter Hermaeus Mora's realm uninvited, circumventing the [Pact Primordial](Lore_Pact_Primordial.md) which prevented interdimensional incursions from one Prince into the realm of another.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-72)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-73)</sup><sup>[[72]](#cite_note-OTPP-74)</sup><sup>[[73]](#cite_note-Conclave-75)</sup>

In Apocrypha, the Hidden Kindred used [magic](Lore_Magic.md) that allowed them to move objects and beings to different [layers of reality](Lore_Layers_of_Reality.md) in order to conceal a modified version of the cult's Censers of Corruption. These Censers spread an arcane plague formed of Peryite's disease and Vaermina's dream essence,<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Leramil-76)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Shelreni Baro-77)</sup><sup>[[76]](#cite_note-Instructions-78)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Calamity-28)</sup> that infected and corroded Apocrypha's fabric, tormented, blinded, and frenzied Mora's servants,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Note-25)</sup> and gradually incapacitated even the Prince himself, threatening to eventually dissolve Apocrypha and sever Mora's link to his domain entirely.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Mora Calamity-26)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Mora Unhealthy-27)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Scruut-79)</sup><sup>[[78]](#cite_note-Scruut Unhealthy-80)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Calamity-28)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-Unhealthy-29)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-Shelreni Baro-77)</sup> In addition to the affliction, the corruption of the Censers also allowed the Hidden Kindred to conceal their agents and objects in different layers, so that even Hermaeus Mora's gaze could not immediately track them through the different layers.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-Blightcrown Priest-81)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Leramil-76)</sup> The plague soon spread both within the realm proper and adjacent dimensions such as the [Infinite Panopticon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Infinite_Panopticon).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-Note-25)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Leramil-76)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Leramil Calamity-82)</sup>

Hidden in Shelreni's body, Vaermina entered the Infinite Panopticon, seeking one of the Glyphics that contained the hidden memories. Hermaeus Mora himself, weakened by the corruption coursing through the realm, which was intensified further by Vaermina's presence, was ensnared and imprisoned within one of Vaermina's nightmares. However, the [Watchling](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Watcher) Scruut and the Vestige managed to break through the nightmare and free him, forcing Vaermina and her allies to flee.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-Scruut-79)</sup><sup>[[26]](#cite_note-Calamity-28)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Mora Calamity-26)</sup><sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Leramil Calamity-82)</sup>

In the end, the [ghost](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ghost) of the Telvanni Magister Meln the Mouthless used his Ghost Sight, a power that allowed one to see the hidden, imperceptible, and unseen, to allow the Vestige to locate the minions and Censers of the invading Princes and vanquish them,<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Hidden-40)</sup> to destroy the manufactories of corruption which were spreading Peryite's plague within the core of Apocrypha by shattering the dream shards that bound them to the realm, and to slay Blightcrown, and even to help in combat against a manifestation of Vaermina herself,<sup>[[81]](#cite_note-Vaermina Unhealthy-83)</sup> whose true form was banished through Mora's power after her location became apparent, thus preventing the dissolution of Apocrypha, an event that would have produced ripples of [fate](Lore_Fate.md) that would have impacted all of Aurbis, and caused the destruction of Nirn as well due to the powerful connection the two realms share.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-Meln Spirit-84)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-Mora Calamity-26)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-Mora Unhealthy-27)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-Meln Uhealthy-85)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-Mora-37)</sup> Though the invasion was ended and Apocrypha was saved, Torvesard managed to escape with his recovered memories of Ithelia.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Torvesard-56)</sup>

During the invasion, the forces of Peryite and Vaermina also attempted to steal the knowledge held in Bastion Nymic, a fortress within Apocrypha where Hermaeus Mora is said to keep the countless Daedric [Nymics](Lore_Nymic.md) which he has recorded.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-Strix-86)</sup><sup>[[85]](#cite_note-Bastion Nymic Dailies-87)</sup><sup>[[86]](#cite_note-The Bastion-88)</sup>

### Third Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/OBR-quest-Peryite.jpg/200px-OBR-quest-Peryite.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-Peryite.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-quest-Peryite.jpg) The shrine of Peryite in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md), east of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md) (Oblivion Remastered) In the events leading up to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md), an [agent](Lore_The_Agent.md) of the [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) contacted Peryite in search of power. The agent reminded the Prince of a mortal from the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), and Peryite took a shine to the agent. He had the agent slay a [Vampire Ancient](Lore_Vampirism.md) who had displeased him, and in return he had one of his worshippers give Spellbreaker to the agent.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DFPeryite-31)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), five worshippers of Peryite performed a ritual at the Prince's hidden shrine, located along the banks of the [Silverfish River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silverfish_River) in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). The spell was intended to summon Peryite, but instead the worshippers became trapped between worlds, their bodies on Nirn and their [souls](Lore_Souls.md) in the Pits. The [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) stumbled upon the motionless bodies of the worshippers by the shrine and was tasked by Peryite to travel to a plane in the Pits and recover their souls. The plane consisted of a volcanic island inhabited by many [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), with a Blood Well tower and a series of volcanic caves called the Sightless Grotto. The Hero rescued the souls of the worshippers and returned them to Tamriel to earn the Prince's favor and receive his artifact, Spellbreaker.<sup>[[87]](#cite_note-OBPeryite-89)</sup>

## Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/8c/ON-costume-Peryite_Skeevemaster_%28male%29.jpg/200px-ON-costume-Peryite_Skeevemaster_%28male%29.jpg)](File_ON-costume-Peryite_Skeevemaster_(male).jpg.md) [](File_ON-costume-Peryite_Skeevemaster_(male).jpg.md) Heavy and concealing clothes worn by Skeevemasters, who often collaborate with rats (ESO) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/13/SR-npc-Afflicted_Refugee_02.jpg/200px-SR-npc-Afflicted_Refugee_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Afflicted_Refugee_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-npc-Afflicted_Refugee_02.jpg) An Afflicted uses Vile Vapor Peryite's followers preach the wonders of their Prince and the mysteries of the divine skeever.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Filbert-10)</sup> Peryite's worshippers are not well known or documented, and take a variety of methods in how they worship him. The [Natural Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Natural_Order) is a cult that consists of venerable nobles and aristocrats across Tamriel who secretly worship the Taskmaster.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-DTTA-90)</sup> Some worshipers liken Peryite more to the pus in the wound rather than disease itself; although the pus is unpleasant, it expels foul [humors](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humorism) and restores the blood, thereby cleaning it and restoring health.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRKesh-1)</sup> Peryite is viewed as the perfect truth of order by his followers, and they worship him because "sometimes the world can only be cleansed by disease", and "cleansing" the world would bring true natural order.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRKesh-1)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-RONMV-91)</sup>

Cults such as the Diseased and Order of the Sacred Skeever are organizations of self-described social outcasts that take more extreme and controversial methods.<sup>[[88]](#cite_note-DTTA-90)</sup> They would be glad to contract a disease and die in their own filth "as Peryite intended".<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ONMarthine-3)</sup> They see every malady as a blessing from Peryite and revel in suffering and the spreading of plagues: fungal infections, scabies, rashes, mucus-filled lungs, itchiness and irritation in all forms, and tooth decay are some of the many afflictions adored by his cultists.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Filbert-10)</sup><sup>[[89]](#cite_note-RONMV-91)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-Gadayn-24)</sup> These cultists seek to spread whatever illnesses they have to everyone they meet.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Filbert-10)</sup> Their recruitment attempts are often unsuccessful, as many see them as imbeciles.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ONRazumdar-92)</sup>

The Scalecaller Cult viewed Nirn as a nasty imperfect world with no order or respect, and they saw other Princes as chaotic lies. They concocted an all-consuming plague to bury the world in corpses so that it would belong to Peryite, who would then bring about a new age of an ordered world without chaos.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-RONMV-91)</sup> This event is prophesied among various branches, who say only the most faithful followers of the Prince of Pestilence will survive.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Filbert-10)</sup> An interview with [Phrastus of Elinhir](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Phrastus_of_Elinhir) and [Lady Cinnabar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lady_Cinnabar) of [Taneth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Taneth) also implies that Peryite might have had a minor presence and influence among the [Dragon Cult](Lore_Dragon_Cult.md) of the Nords due to their veneration of dragon-kind.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-AMOVAB-93)</sup>

There was also a particular group known as the Afflicted that consisted of [Bretons](Lore_Breton.md) located within [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201). Peryite's "blessing" for them was infection with a plague, although he also granted them some resistance to its deadly effects.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-SRAfflicted-94)</sup> They had unusually bright red skin and the ability to spit poisonous vomit at their enemies, but they were eventually killed by Peryite's champion in his quest to kill their overseer, the [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) known as Orchendor. Originally from the town of Cul Aloue in [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), they were "shepherded" to the Dwemer ruin of [Bthardamz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bthardamz).<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-UAL-95)</sup> Orchendor and the Afflicted were meant to stand ready, awaiting Peryite's command to cover the world with his blessing.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-SRKesh-1)</sup> They also worshiped strange draconic totems and brewed tinctures in their worship of Peryite.<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-SRTotem-96)</sup> The Afflicted drank the solutions they brewed, which other mortals would call poisons, to "heal" themselves.<sup>[[93]](#cite_note-UAL-95)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/ON-npc-Plague_Concocter_Mortieu.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Plague_Concocter_Mortieu.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Plague_Concocter_Mortieu.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Plague_Concocter_Mortieu.jpg) Nathien Mortieu, a plague concoctor of Peryite [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c3/ON-npc-The_Noxious_Herald.jpg/200px-ON-npc-The_Noxious_Herald.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Noxious_Herald.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-The_Noxious_Herald.jpg) The Noxious Herald, a follower of Peryite, spreading disease Peryite, who is known as the **Master of Tasks** and the **Lord of Order**, is a revered spirit in the [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) pantheon that shares many characteristics with [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md), in pivotal ways such as being attributed to "time, rigid natural order, (and) draconic imagery". This has led to scholars concluding that there was a cultural diffusion between the early ancestors of [Men](Lore_Men.md) and [Mer](Lore_Mer.md) in the Northwestern parts of Tamriel. To the Reachfolk Peryite serves as a foil to the primacy of conflict. Everything that exists will pass. The fort that rises too high will fall. The clan that starves will one day grow strong. This eternal balance is the work of Peryite. To describe Peryite as a necessary evil would thus be a misnomer, for while the Reachfolk see him as necessary, they see the good in him maintaining a balance as a force of nature. While Reachmen may be claimed by blight or plague, they will come out of these natural tragedies healthier. This keeps the dangers of overpopulation in check and allows a new generation of Reachfolk to build immunity to what Peryite throws at them.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-GSR5-97)</sup>

Peryite is invoked in [Ashlander](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ashlander) rites<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-ROA-32)</sup> and is referred to as "Figuring father Peryite" in relation to "[Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) mother" in an obscure text which also names one of his attributes as his "Golden Order".<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Sermon Zero-9)</sup> The stewards of Bal Sunnar built a temple to Peryite as their Matriarch believed he offered the most protection from the Scourge. Sacrifices would later begin that brought about unrecognizable creatures such as "Peryite's Glory", voriplasm-like blobs capable of sapping strength.<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-JAF-98)</sup>

Some of the Prince's followers work as plague concoctors, experimenting on the creation and spread of new diseases which they engineer to have specific traits, such as both being nigh incurable and exhibiting symptoms with some delay, in order to help them spread further.<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-Research Mortieu-99)</sup> Plague concoctors sometimes give Peryite samples of disease as an offering. These offerings need to be of similar strains, such as [Crimson Plague](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Crimson_Plague) and [Blood Rot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Blood_Rot), as the Taskmaster is said to abhor heterogony.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-Plague Instructions-18)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Spellbreaker
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Spellbreaker.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-icon-armor-Spellbreaker.png) Spellbreaker [Spellbreaker](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spellbreaker) (also spelled **Spell Breaker**) is an ancient [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) stated to have been created on behalf of [King Rourken](Lore_Rourken_(person).md). Some believe it was forged by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Peryite, but regardless of this rumor's veracity, Spellbreaker has become associated with Peryite and is probably the best-known artifact associated with the Prince. Superficially a [Dwarven](Lore_Dwemer.md) tower shield, it is one of the most ancient relics in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The shield not only protects its wielder from physical damage, but also from magical attacks by reflecting spells, curing the wielder of paralysis, dispelling curses, or silencing any mage about to cast a spell. It is said that the shield still searches for its original owner, and will not remain the property of anyone else for long.

### Scarf of the Obscene
The [Scarf of the Obscene](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Scarf_of_the_Obscene) was a minor relic thought to be connected to the [Daedric Prince](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Prince) Peryite, existing in an [alternate timeline](Lore_Many_Paths.md).

It was described as a deceptively beautiful garment made of silks in shades of green and golden thread. However, beneath its initial allure lay a disturbing truth. [Daedric runes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Alphabet) writhed and flickered across its surface like oil on water, imparting an unsettling and distressing effect on anyone who gazed at the scarf for too long. When worn, the Scarf transformed its wearer into a living vessel for Peryite's most potent contagions. Anyone who came within a few feet of the wearer was cursed to be infected with a variety of these diseases. Each new wearer gave rise to a unique array of afflictions, the method of which remained a mystery. Despite this, it was deemed harmless unless worn.

### Other Artifacts
There are curios associated with Peryite of which little is known, such as the [Bilious Censer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bilious_Censer), the [Toxicruciform](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Toxicruciform), and the [Golden Scab](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golden_Scab). They were last known to be in Gazmod the Collector's possession.<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Eorda-55)</sup> Although these theories have not been validated or elaborated upon by other sources, two other artifacts attributed to Peryite ([Denstagmer's Ring](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Denstagmer%27s_Ring) and the Mirror of Hard Truths) are briefly mentioned by an interviewer during the Second Era, whose information may be unreliable.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-AMOVAB-93)</sup>

## The Pits
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/df/BL-quest-The_Pit_of_Pestilence.jpg/200px-BL-quest-The_Pit_of_Pestilence.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-quest-The_Pit_of_Pestilence.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-quest-The_Pit_of_Pestilence.jpg) The Pit of Pestilence [The Pits](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Pits), also known as **Peryite's Pits**, or the **Pits of Pestilence** (sometimes singular **Pit of Pestilence**), is a realm of [Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Oblivion), created and ruled over by Peryite, the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Pestilence. Here, Peryite guards the lowest orders of Oblivion.

It is unknown what the realm looks like overall. One part of the realm appears as a rocky cave system with occasional skylights filled with a hazy green poisonous fog, floating green embers, and some underground plants and is described as permeated with a noxious presence of miasma. This area of the realm has been home to necromancers and undead. Another part of the realm resembles the [Deadlands](Lore_Deadlands.md), with lava seas, volcanic islands, and ruined structures. It is also home to many of the same Daedra as the Deadlands. The [Imperial Geographical Society](Lore_Imperial_Geographical_Society.md) believes that the realm is usually inaccessible to mortals, but there have been several exceptions.

- According to the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) section in *[Kyne's Challenge: A Hunter's Companion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:Kyne%27s_Challenge__A_Hunter%27s_Companion_Characters_and_Summary#Summerset_Isles)*, an ancient shrine to Peryite on the Isles depicts Altmer fighting ["slug-like creatures"](Lore_Sload.md).
- Cut dialogue/text in *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)* describes Peryite as the "most affable of the Daedra Princes with the worst reputation."
- Entering a [portal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Portals) in a location afflicted by the plague Peryite unleashed against Apocrypha could result in unforeseen effects, such as causing the portal to fracture, depositing those who enter in different locations or reality layers than the intended destination, or even ejecting them to become lost to the [Void](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Void).<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-Leramil-76)</sup><sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Meln-108)</sup>
