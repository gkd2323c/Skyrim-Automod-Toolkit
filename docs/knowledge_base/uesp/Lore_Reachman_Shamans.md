# Reachman Shamans

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/03/ON-quest-After_the_Storm_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-After_the_Storm_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-After_the_Storm_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-After_the_Storm_04.jpg) Offerings to the Spirits [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0b/RG-comic-Page_11-High_Rock.jpg/200px-RG-comic-Page_11-High_Rock.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_11-High_Rock.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-comic-Page_11-High_Rock.jpg) Witchmen of High Rock a few years before [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864) | *Magic is a word used by cowards who fear its power or fools who think they can control it. Reachman Shamans, also known as **Witchmen**,<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_WM)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-1)</sup> are [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachmen.md) responsible for the spiritual guidance of their clans.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SU-2)</sup> Reachfolk approach to religion varies from clan to clan. They worship many spirits, both great and minor. There are many different, yet similar faiths in the Reach. Shamans of different clans follow different traditions, have different beliefs, and are responsible for appeasing and veneration of the different spirits.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GSOTRV2-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ATS-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HSG-5)</sup> They often act as religious leaders of their clans.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ATS-4)</sup>

The religion of Reachfolk is transactional in nature. Their relationship with the spirits relies on negotiation rather than on reverence.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SU-2)</sup>

Shamans in training are known as Apprentices.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AF-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ATS-4)</sup> It was a universal title among the clans of the Reach used to refer to shamans in training, both in the city of Markarth and outside its area of control.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-AO-7)</sup> A Shaman usually chooses their Apprentice, but occasionally a willing candidate may petition the shaman themselves to become their pupil.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AF-6)</sup>

## Further Information
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/aa/ON-npc-Black-Moon_Shaman.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Black-Moon_Shaman.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Black-Moon_Shaman.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Black-Moon_Shaman.jpg) One of the shamans of the Black-Moon Clan [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/67/LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png/200px-LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Reachman_Shaman.png) Reachman Shaman (Legends) Shamans of some Reachfolk clans venerate [hagraven](Lore_Hagraven.md) matrons, who in turn grant them knowledge of nature magic.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-1)</sup>. Other sources connect this magic directly with Hircine.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONLoad VW-8)</sup> [Thornroot Clan](Lore_Thornroot_Clan.md) shamans worked alongside hagravens and were turning the captives into [Briarhearts](Lore_Briarheart.md) forcefully stripped of their free will. They used special, blessed tools that allowed them to desecrate the ritual without angering the spirits.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONBR-9)</sup>

Shaman and Apprentice of the [Crow-Eye Clan](Lore_Crow-Eye_Clan.md) were responsible for protecting the village of [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md) from harm by keeping magical wards devoted to four Great Spirits of the Reach. Although the clan was primarily following the [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), they also were making offerings to Hircine, [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) to maintain the defensive wards. In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the [Icereach Coven](Lore_Icereach_Coven.md) began performing [Harrowstorm](Lore_Harrowstorm.md) rituals throughout Western Skyrim and the Reach; stealing the souls of its residents to resurrect the [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md). Karthwasten was a target of one of such attacks. Despite suffering losses, the Reachfolk of Karthwasten survived, largely thanks to the magical protection Crow-Eye Clan maintained.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-AF-6)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ATS-4)</sup>

The name "gravesinger" is given to some Reachman necromancers who broker deals with Molag Bal<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ONZK-10)</sup> in exchange for the power to command the loathsome dead. They are believed by some to be the most dangerous kind of Reachfolk shaman.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-1)</sup> Gravesingers are necromancers who seek dominion over life and death. They were known to invoke Daedra lurking in darkness to assist in their dark pursuits.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GOTE-11)</sup> Not all gravesingers are of Reacholk descent; there are also known [Imperial](Lore_Imperial.md) gravesingers.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-Gravesinger L-12)</sup>

Numerous shamans were affiliated with [Wayward Guardians](Lore_Wayward_Guardians.md). Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), [Ardanir](Lore_Ardanir.md), a represntative of the organization sent the adventurer to recover the blue gems<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-ESOARK-13)</sup> embedded within the Dwemer Architons, the kind of Dwemer Animunculi known to be typically imbued with [Aetherium](Lore_Aetherium.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-CMAAS-14)</sup> The constructs were guarding the recently rediscovered Library of Arkthzand. They claimed that these gems possessed powerful enchantments and hoped to use them to arm themselves and their people against the encroaching forces of [Rada al-Saran](Lore_Rada_al-Saran.md)'s [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md).<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ONA-15)</sup> Shamans could also be found among the ranks of [Forsworn](Lore_Forsworn.md) in the early [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-FS-16)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/26/ON-place-Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.jpg/200px-ON-place-Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.jpg) Shrine of the Hunt-Father circa 2E 582 [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4d/ON-npc-Shaman_Ulvoch.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Shaman_Ulvoch.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Shaman_Ulvoch.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Shaman_Ulvoch.jpg) Shaman Ulvoch, spiritual leader of the Crow-Eye Clan The [Shrine of the Hunt-Father](Lore_Shrine_of_the_Hunt-Father.md) was a [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) temple based in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md). It was dedicated to five aspects of one of the [Great Spirits](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) of [the Reach](Lore_The_Reach.md), [Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md). The shrine was active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HSG-5)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) a group of shamans has shown interest in studying the mysterious magically imbued mushrooms of the [Gloomreach](Lore_Gloomreach.md). They also requested mercenaries to prevent a mad [Psijic](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) mage from conducting magical experiments on the eggs of dangerous insects in the area.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONBR-9)</sup>

Members of the [Dreadhorn Clan](Lore_Dreadhorn_Clan.md) utilized shamanistic magic associated with [Kyne](Lore_Kyne.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-ESOLive FALK-17)</sup> Their shamans often coated their skins in a chalky whitewash and painted symbols and sigils of power using mixed [nirncrux](Lore_Nirncrux.md) powder and blood as paint.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-ONDSS-18)</sup>

Reachmen celebrate the [end of the old year](Lore_Old_Life_Festival.md) and the [beginning of the new year](Lore_New_Life_Festival.md). They believe that with the coming of the New Life Festival, two of Hircine's aspects come to conflict with each other. New Life marks the [winter solstice](Lore_Solstice_(astronomy).md#Winter_Solstice) when the days begin to lengthen due to the return of the [sun](Lore_Sun.md). Storihbeg the Man-Beast thus pursues the sun in an attempt to eat it, an effort to keep the nights longer. Reachfolk Shamans play important role in the celebrations. To prevent the sun from being devoured, they summon [Hrokkibeg](Lore_Hrokkibeg.md), the Mighty Bear to keep Storihbeg constrained and protect [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md). It is commonly understood by the Reachfolk Shamans that Hircine "abides in aspects five". Shamans know "which guise shall come and which must perforce go".<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-NLFI-19)</sup>

Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a Reachman shaman known as [Uraccanach](Lore_Uraccanach.md) the Witchman advocated for the worship of Five Aspects of the Hircine.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-CMSS-20)</sup> Similarly to the shamans of the Shrine of the Hunt-Father he believed that there are only five aspects of Hircine that are worthy of recognition.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-HSG-5)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-AOLH-21)</sup> He was also a proponent of emulating the apparel of [Huntsmen](Lore_Huntsman.md) as an act of honoring the aspects of Hircine.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-CMHS-22)</sup>

Some shamans of [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) possess the [ability](Lore_Sight.md) to predict the future through examination of animal entrails.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-WB-23)</sup> [Mysticism](Lore_Mysticism.md) was another common magical art among the Reachfolk mystic shamans during the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONRee-24)</sup>

In the ancient times [Nordic](Lore_Nord.md) invaders built [Valthume](Lore_Valthume.md). It was a place of worship of Nordic totem-gods. They attempted to convert native [Reachfolk](Lore_Reachfolk.md) to their faith, but their attempts were met with an opposition and eventually they were killed by the locals. Shamans of the ancient past were believed to paint runes with their spilled blood.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOLS-25)</sup>

## Notable Shamans
- **[Angof](Lore_Angof.md)** — Infamous gravesinger who was a leader of the [Bloodthorn Cult](Lore_Bloodthorn_Cult.md).
- **Baircallas** — The Shaman known for his ability to read future through examination of animal entrails.
- **[Glynroch](Lore_Glynroch.md)** — The first High Shaman of the Shrine of the Huntfather and keeper of the Symbols of the Five Aspects.
- **Mochtuinne Eye-Tooth** — The shaman knowledgeable about the traditions of both the Old Life and New Life festivals.
- **[Uraccanach the Witchman](Lore_Uraccanach.md)** — Reachfolk author and proponent of the worship of the Five Aspects.
- **[Ulvoch](Lore_Ulvoch.md)** — Spiritual leader of [Karthwasten](Lore_Karthwasten.md) during the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md).
