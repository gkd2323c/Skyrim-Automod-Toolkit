# Ingenium

| --- |

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/bd/MW-place-Vivec_Temple.jpg/200px-MW-place-Vivec_Temple.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Vivec_Temple.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Vivec_Temple.jpg) Baar Dau hanging above the [High Fane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:High_Fane) The **Ingenium** was a device powered by [souls](Lore_Souls.md) that was designed by the sorcerer [Vuhon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City_Vuhon) and a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) named [Sul](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Books:The_Infernal_City_Sul). It was created by making a pact with [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md), a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) with a penchant for collecting souls.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIC-1)</sup> The device's purpose was to hold [Baar Dau](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Baar_Dau) over [Vivec City](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vivec_City) after the disappearance of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec_(god).md). The device functioned by creating a [rift](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Portals) to the [Fields of Regret](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fields_of_Regret) through which souls were funneled to Clavicus Vile and the energies of the [realm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Planes_of_Existence) were leeched to hold the moon in place. The mage Sul, who was one of the designers of Ingenium, theorized that the Daedric Prince did not care for the stolen energies as he valued the souls he received in exchange more, and that the other architect of the project, Vuhon, might have even made a formal pact with Vile for him to allow the energies to be taken.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-IC-2)</sup> This process started by using the souls of prisoners and undesirables, which resulted in their immediate deaths. Eventually, a method was found that let souls power the Ingenium without causing immediate death. When Sul's lover, Ilzheven, was taken to power the Ingenium, he destroyed it in an attempt to save her. This caused Baar Dau to crash into Vivec City, resulting in the eruption of [Red Mountain](Lore_Red_Mountain.md) and the destruction of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md) and part of mainland [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). This event became known as the [Red Year](Lore_Red_Year.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIC-1)</sup> The disaster of the Red Year effectively ended the ascendancy of the [Tribunal Temple](Lore_Tribunal_Temple.md) and the mainstream worship of the [Tribunal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gods_T#Tribunal) as gods.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TR-3)</sup>
