#### The Herd Mother
A divine animist spirit venerated by the [Horsemen](Lore_Silverhoof_Horsemen.md) of [Silverhoof Vale](Lore_Silverhoof_Vale.md), who they claim acts as their tribe's guiding and protective deity. Young Horsemen must commune with this equine deity on a vision journey that acts as a rite of passage to adulthood, which they must embark by themselves. These rites are described as being similar to the Redguard tradition of Walkabout. The Horsemen also claim that the Herd Mother gave them a flotilla of "swimming horse-ships", which they used to cross seventeen seas before reaching [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md). The Horsemen's worship of the Herd Mother was endangered in [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md), which is what caused them to leave.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Horse Folk-1)</sup>

#### Hermaeus Mora
[Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md) (sometimes spelled "**Hoermius**", "**Hormaius**", "**Herm'us**", or "**Herma** **Mora**") is the **Daedric Prince of Forbidden Knowledge**, hidden shoals, whirlpools, and sudden squalls. His sphere also encompasses destiny; he scries the tides of [Fate](Lore_Fate.md), weaving through the past and future as interpreted in the stars and heavens, and his dominion is said to hold "the treasures of knowledge and memory". His curiosity is insatiable and he seeks all knowledge, and not to change but to observe, so that all becomes known. He is known by many titles, such as the **Demon of Knowledge**, the **One Who Knows**, the **Master of the Tides of Fate**, **Gardener of Men**, **Prince of Fate**, **Lord of Secrets**, the **Keeper of Forbidden Knowledge**, the **Golden Eye**, the **Great Eye**, the **Eye that Wanders**, the **Eye-of-All** or **Eye-Who-Sees-All**, **Ur-Daedra**, the **Abyssal Cephaliarch**, **Old Antecedent**, **Scryer**, **He Who Records All Knowledge**, **Inevitable Knower**, and the **Lord of Fate and Knowledge**. To the ancient [Atmorans](Lore_Atmora.md) and [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), he is **Herma-Mora**, the **Woodland Man**, or "**that which speaks in the wastes**." To the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), he was **Hyrma Mora**. The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) know him as the **Tide King** and **Hermorah**.

He "weeps cold tears" at the plague of ignorance, where knowledge is forsaken out of fear of misuse, and he allegedly seeks to enlighten all to all mysteries that have ever and will ever exist. One follower of Mora stated that the ultimate purpose of the Daedra lords is to instruct and improve the "generally deplorable" character of mortals. This benevolence presented by his followers may be a façade, as Mora is said to hoard his knowledge and give nothing away for free. Like all Daedric Princes, he's not to be trifled with, but he is subtler than most of his ilk and ensnares mortals with his secrets. He may seem cruel at times, but he's not irrational. Mora hoards and safeguards any knowledge that could potentially endanger reality. He is described as crafty and good at hiding things, and is said to love traps. According to some sources, the Mythos, the core of Mora's realm of Apocrypha, is where the Prince "whispered the first secret" and "imagined the concept of knowledge", thus also conceiving Apocrypha itself in that same act.

Always lurking, he is the void and the ever-seeing eyes. Mora has been called the wisest of the Daedric Princes, with a mind as old as [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and a body of slime, though he describes himself as *"the riddle unsolveable. The door unopenable. The book unreadable. The question unanswerable."* Unlike most Princes, Hermaeus Mora typically does not take on a humanoid form, manifesting instead as black clouds of varied, grotesque assemblages of eyes, tentacles, and claws, or a featureless purple vortex known as the Wretched Abyss,. However, according to some sources, he occasionally took on a form resembling that of a mortal. He speaks with a thundering bray, and has been described as being composed of countless eyes or "orbs without end". The energies and essences of both Hermaeus Mora and [Namira](Lore_Namira.md) are said to take the form of "roiling darkness", and are so similar to one another that additional decorative elements are required to determine which of the two an artifact belongs to. This strong similarity has caused some scholars to theorize that the two Daedric Princes share a close relationship and are linked to one another in some manner.

According to Mora himself, he is "multitudes", and only adopts a singular guise for the sake of the mortals, whose minds would crumble when confronted with such a concept. The form in which he appears before mortals is described as a "delimited projection", although he is said to not communicate with them often. The [Watchlings](Lore_Watcher.md) of the Prince are known to sometimes serve as his proxies, as he can see through their eyes and even take control of their forms to speak through them. It is said that all statues of Hermaeus Mora serve as access points from his realm to Nirn, thus one should think carefully before placing a representation of the Prince close to their person. Mora can be invoked in certain locations in Nirn using a "condensate of planar possibilities", which takes a form similar to ink to summon him. He is said to always be using [Chaotic Creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) as a result of always being involved in various ongoing projects.

He resides everywhere and nowhere but takes glee in tormenting mortals from his realm in [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), the [plane](Lore_Planes_of_Oblivion.md) called [Apocrypha](Lore_Apocrypha.md): an endless library where the infinite pages of countless books containing all forbidden knowledge are found. Each book sports a black cover without a title, and the realm is reported to be haunted by ghosts forever searching for knowledge. His servants include [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) such as [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md), [Lurkers](Lore_Lurker.md), and [Watchers](Lore_Watcher.md), but he is known to have mortal worshipers too. Sailors and seafaring folk refer to **Herm'us Mora** as the **Tide King**, master of the seas and One-Whose-Tentacles-Rise-From-the-Deep, a being who lurks in the oceans and pulls vessels down into the abyss. Similarly, the Khajiiti Hermorah controls the tides and lives in a vast library under the sea.

Hermaeus Mora is tangentially related to the origins of the [Morag Tong](Lore_Morag_Tong.md) by association with his sibling, [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md). Mora's enemies are said to be [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md) and [Vaermina](Lore_Vaermina.md), and his [summoning day](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora%27s_Summoning_Day.md) is the 5<sup>th</sup> of First Seed.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md). [Azura](Lore_Azura.md) considers Mora among the most powerful Princes who "stand above their lesser kith", as one of the eight to sign the [Coldharbour Compact](Lore_Coldharbour_Compact.md). According to Mora's [Seekers](Lore_Seeker.md), due to the Compact, the Prince made a pact with *every* mortal on Nirn to stop directly interfering in [Mundial](Lore_Mundus.md) affairs, and was paid something he dearly desired in exchange. The compact appeared to bind in both directions, thus, mortals cannot come to Apocrypha as they did before.

#### Hircine
[Hircine](Lore_Hircine.md), known as **the Huntsman**, **Lord of the Hunt**, **Master of Beasts**, **Wolves' Father** and the **Master of the Chase**, is a [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) whose sphere is the Hunt, the Sport of [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md), the Greatest Game, and the Chase and Sacrifice of Mortals. His realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) is the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md), an endless forest where the Prince and his Huntsmen hunt great beasts, people, and even other Daedra. Hircine is typically portrayed with a great spear and either the head or skull of an [elk](Lore_Elk.md), the latter of which Khajiiti myths say belonged to [Y'ffer](Lore_Y%27ffer.md)'s champion, the [Graht-Elk](Lore_Graht-Elk.md).

Hircine is one of the more widely worshipped Daedric Princes in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), despite never actively seeking worship or reverence. His appeal lies in his sphere, which encompasses the thrill of the hunt, though this can be taken to extremes. His advocacy for sportsmanship is another lure, as the Law of Fair Hunt which he holds to prohibits cheating prey (referred to as "the Hare") out of a genuine chance at escape. He admires the Hare that overcomes whatever is thrown against them, no matter the odds. For example, one who turns the game on its head by hunting the hunters may earn the Prince's respect. Finally, he is straightforward in his desires; he often seeks people out, tests them, and rewards those who prove themselves worthy with a boon.

As the progenitor of [lycanthropy](Lore_Lycanthropy.md), Hircine is recognized as **the Father of Manbeasts**. He serves as the guardian of were-creatures, which are often referred to as his "children". The condition is called Hircine's Gift by those that view it as a blessing, and Hircine's Curse to those that consider it an abomination. Hircine is quite prideful of lycanthropy, and resents those that received it willingly but have come to regret it; he's known to have ingrates slain to rid them of their misery. Regardless of whether they worshipped him or not, Hircine will typically claim the souls of skinshifters, and they will spend eternity in the Hunting Grounds.

The [Demiprince](Lore_Demiprince.md) [Dearola](Lore_Dearola.md) is Hircine's daughter. There is a belief that Hircine is allied with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), though [Lyranth the Foolkiller](Lore_Lyranth.md) claims that if such an arrangement exists, it would be obscure outside their own circle. Hircine has [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedra_Lord.md) that manage some of his sub-spheres and report to him, such as the Lord of Fishing. He is also associated with the Daedra Lord [Hollowjack](Lore_Hollowjack.md), whose realm of [Detritus](Lore_Detritus.md) is a "carving" from the shell of the [Hunting Grounds](Lore_Hunting_Grounds.md). [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md) is an enemy of Hircine. Hircine's [summoning day](Lore_Hircine%27s_Summoning_Day.md) is the 5<sup>th</sup> of Mid Year.

For more information, see the [main lore article](Lore_Hircine.md).

#### Hoon Ding, the Yokudan Make Way God
The [Hoon Ding](Lore_HoonDing.md), also known as **Hoonding** or **The Make Way God**, is the [Yokudan](Lore_Yokuda.md) spirit of 'perseverance over infidels'. The Hoon Ding has historically materialized whenever the [Redguards](Lore_Redguard.md) need to 'make way' for their people.

#### Horde Mountain
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0d/MW-concept-33.jpg/200px-MW-concept-33.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-33.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-33.jpg) Vivec City, the remains of Horde Mountain [Horde Mountain](Lore_Horde_Mountain.md) was one of the eight [monster children](Lore_Monster_Children.md) from the unholy union of [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) and [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md), who initially escaped being slain. Three former minor [Chimer Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) helped subdue Horde Mountain for Vivec. The Warrior-Poet was overjoyed by this and consolidated these three houses into a new order, his personal guard, the [Buoyant Armigers](Lore_Buoyant_Armigers.md). Vivec then pierced Horde Mountain with [Muatra](Lore_Muatra.md), killing it. The Living God then dispersed the bones to form a new city for him and his Armigers, [Vivec City](Lore_Vivec_City.md), City of Swords. [Almalexia](Lore_Almalexia.md) blessed the city with her [Ordinators](Lore_Ordinators.md) to serve as its honor guard, and [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md) blessed the holy city with a protective spell.

#### House of Troubles
The **Bad Daedra** (also known as the **House of Troubles**, **Rebel Daedra**, **Adversaries**, and **Testing Gods**) is the [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) (and before that, [Velothi](Lore_Velothi.md)) term for four of the [Daedra Lords](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md): [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md), [Malacath](Lore_Malacath.md) and [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md). These Princes are the Bad Daedra as introduced to the [Chimer](Lore_Chimer.md) by the teachings of Saint Veloth. Rebels whose purpose it is to test the resolve and faith of mortals, they are also known as the **Four Corners of the House of Troubles**. Ancient, alien temples dedicated to their worship can be found across [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md).

The Four Corners are known as enemy gods to the Dunmer. Molag Bal is a primary source of obstacles for the Dunmer people and their Chimer predecessors. He attempts to upset the Houses' bloodlines or ruin Dunmeri "purity". In the days of old Resdayn, Molag Bal was not worshipped so much as he was placated. Malacath tests the Dunmer for strength, Sheogorath tests for mental weakness and tempts [Great Houses](Lore_Great_Houses.md) to treachery, while Mehrunes Dagon represents the dangerous terrain of Morrowind.
