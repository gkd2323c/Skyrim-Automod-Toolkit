# Blackfeather Court

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/06/ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg/200px-ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-creature-Court_Magister.jpg) A crow of the Blackfeather Court The Blackfeather Court is a feudal court of [Crow Daedra](Lore_Crow_Daedra.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ESOLive-1)</sup> that rules over [Crow's Wood](Lore_Crow%27s_Wood.md) and other parts of the [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md). They were hatched by a Nether-Niece of [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md) called the Crow Mother, whom they served. The Court is locked in a constant state of war with the [wolves](Lore_Wolf.md) and [giant bats](Lore_Giant_Bat.md) that inhabit Crow's Wood. The crows charge a toll on visitors to the realm, but are seldom taken seriously.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Each vassal of the Court is a creature of darkness and shadows, imbued with a small portion of Nocturnal's power. The crows refer to Nocturnal as their Mighty Mistress.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup>

Some time in the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), a [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) member of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) named Rulantaril Oran came to the Crow's Wood in search of forgotten spells. He did this by creating an enchanted chest to open a portal to the realm, which he hid in a small cave near the city of [Davon's Watch](Lore_Davon%27s_Watch.md) in [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md). By striking a deal with the Crow Mother, Rulantaril came to the Court as a student and learned the secret magic of the crows. In return, he was to stay by the Crow Mother's side. Seeking to fool her, Rulantaril locked the Crow Mother within her tower, but ended up trapped inside with her because of the deal. His son, Telbaril, entered the realm in search of his father in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ASons Promise-4)</sup> Groups of adventurers, enticed by the chest, began to enter the Crow's Wood as well. The Blackfeather Court charged these adventurers tolls of flesh and silver, demanding to be brought silver trinkets or the corpses of dead bats to devour. The adventurers were then given citizenship of the Court, along with vassal servants.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> The crows helped in Telbaril's search for his father, but it is unknown if Rulantaril or the Crow Mother survived the ensuing hunt.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ASons Promise-4)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/50/ON-concept-Clockwork_City_Key_Art.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Clockwork_City_Key_Art.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Clockwork_City_Key_Art.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Clockwork_City_Key_Art.jpg) The crows of the Blackfeather Court among those that invaded the Clockwork City When portals to the [Clockwork City](Lore_Clockwork_City.md) were opened from [Evergloam](Lore_Evergloam.md) by Nocturnal around [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), the Blackfeather Court entered [Sotha Sil](Lore_Sotha_Sil.md)'s realm. There they stole the [Skeleton Key](Lore_Skeleton_Key.md) from a Clockwork Apostle, who in turn had stolen it from Sotha Sil. The Court hid the key, but later helped the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) recover it after they chased away the rivaling Exarchs of Dross crows.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-ESOCWC-6)</sup>

The Blackfeather Court subsequently journeyed to [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) in search of treasure within the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) [ruins](Lore_Ayleid_Ruins.md) of [Arpenia](Lore_Arpenia.md) in [Blackwood](Lore_Blackwood.md). However, the crows became trapped within the ruins, transfixed by the mirrors they encountered within. The crows had never encountered their own reflections before, and a cult of Nocturnal worshippers known as the Whispering Shadows used this to their advantage. By harnessing Nocturnal's power from the trapped crows, the cultists began to open a connection to the Evergloam within the ruins. This ritual was ended when the Vestige entered Arpenia and smashed the mirrors, freeing the Duke of Crows and his subjects.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> While trapped within the ruins, the crows had laid many eggs; mercenaries from [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md) were sent to Arpenia to recover these eggs, hired by two competing clients who were attempting to respectively save or destroy the eggs.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup>

One of the pieces of the [Tales of Tribute](Lore_Tales_of_Tribute.md), a popular card game originating from the [Systres Archipelago](Lore_Systres_Archipelago.md) played continent-wide during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) in [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TSOT-9)</sup> bore the image of Duke of Crows. A deck themed after him and the Blackfeather Court was part of the game.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-TOT1-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-HIP-11)</sup>

The [University of Gwylim](Lore_University_of_Gwylim.md)'s Antiquarian Circle excavated the [Shadow Queen's Cowl](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shadow_Queen%27s_Cowl) alongside poetry associated with the Blackfeather Court.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DDFII-12)</sup>
