*Enchanting description:* **Undead up to level <mag> flee for <dur> seconds.**

**Turn Undead** causes [undead](Skyrim_Undead.md) enemies, such as [draugr](Skyrim_Draugr.md), [skeletons](Skyrim_Skeleton.md), and [vampires](Skyrim_Vampire.md) to flee in terror. Despite being a [Fear](Skyrim_Fear_(effect).md) -like effect, the magnitude of Turn Undead is not affected by [Aspect of Terror](Skyrim_Illusion.md) or [Restoration Dual Casting](Skyrim_Restoration.md). The maximum level is increased by [Necromage](Skyrim_Restoration.md).

When part of a spell or staff, the effect includes an additional "stagger" when the projectile hits an undead target, regardless of whether the target is of the correct level to be affected. This is not present on weapons enchanted with the effect. If the turn undead effect is used to enchant a weapon the weapon will deal additional damage to ghost-type undead. Some additional undead NPCs may be affected. This is noted by the turn undead enchantment effect found in the [Creation Kit](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Mod_Creation_Kit).

## Items
The following items use this effect. You can learn how to enchant custom items with Turn Undead if you find one of the following items and disenchant it:

- All varieties of *[Blessed](Skyrim_Generic_Magic_Weapons.md#Turn_Undead)* weapons
- All varieties of *Hallowed* weapons
- All varieties of *Holy* weapons
- All varieties of *Reverent* weapons
- All varieties of *Sanctified* weapons
- All varieties of *Virtuous* weapons

Weapons that use the effect but cannot be disenchanted:

- [Red Eagle's Bane](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Red_Eagle%27s_Bane)

Various staves also provide a Turn Undead effect, although they all use other variants of the effect.

- All varieties of *Staff [of Turning](Skyrim_Staves.md#Turn_Undead)*
- [Grand Staff of Repulsion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Grand_Staff_of_Repulsion)
- [Grand Staff of Turning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Grand_Staff_of_Turning)
- [Minor Staff of Turning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Minor_Staff_of_Turning)
- [Staff of Repulsion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Repulsion)
- [Staff of Turning](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Staff_of_Turning)

All bound weapons when the Oblivion Binding perk is chosen also provide a Turn Undead effect.

## Notes
- Spell and staff versions of this effect take a somewhat longer time to charge up than regular projectile spells, such as [firebolt](Skyrim_Firebolt.md) or [courage](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Courage).
- If you use the Fortify Restoration glitch, you can craft Turn Undead enchantments that can affect every undead in the game, including the powerful Dragon Priests, who are a static level 50.
