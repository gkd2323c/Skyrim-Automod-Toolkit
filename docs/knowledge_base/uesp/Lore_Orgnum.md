Orgnum (also spelled as **Orghum**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-3)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-F:OA-4)</sup> and known as the "**Immortal Monarch**",<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-UAAYQ-5)</sup> "**Sorcerer-King**"<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ODSS-6)</sup>, "**Serpent-king**",<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBBo CC-7)</sup> "**Young King**",<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MSF-8)</sup> "**Snake-King**"<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SSF-9)</sup>, "**Snake Wizard**",<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBBo CC-7)</sup> and formally **King Orgnum**, Voice of the Free Maormer People, commander of Twelve-Dozen-and-One mighty ships)<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-To KR-10)</sup> is the [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) ruler of the island [kingdom](Lore_Kingdom_of_Pyandonea.md) of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup> He is said to have a close circle regarding military matters,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-WW-11)</sup> and the core clans of Pyandonea owing loyalty to him are considered to possess organized and well-disciplined martial forces.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CM64P-12)</sup> Some sources suggest Orgnum wielded a poisonous venom blade.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBBo CC-7)</sup>

## Divinity and Nature
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/41/ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg/200px-ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Maormeri_Serpent_Shrine.jpg) Maormer Serpent Shrine, possible depiction of Orgnum King Orgnum is a deathless wizard who is said to be the **Serpent God of the [Satakal](Lore_Satakal.md)**, the **Tyrant God of the Sea**.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-2)</sup> Some fanatical Maormer, such as Serpent Caller [Vinsha](Lore_Vinsha.md), were driven by visions of the Serpent God of Satakal.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DFO-13)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-SCV-14)</sup> A bevy of serpent imagery can also be found in Maormer culture, from their iconography to their weapons and armor.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-CM64P-12)</sup> Maormer will adorn their pendants and other jewelry with serpentine fangs in honor of their snake-king.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SSF-9)</sup> In some theories spread by guide writers for mariners, it is said that the Tyrant Orgrnum rules the [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) itself by his [Tower](Lore_Tower.md) Flotillas, rather than the keepers of [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-MKP-2)</sup>

Along with this is the presence of [Sea Serpents](Lore_Sea_Serpent.md) who help supplement the Pyandonean Armada force, and thanks to Orgnum's [Snake Magic](Lore_Snake_Magic.md), also double as occasional mounts for the Maormer and Orgnum himself.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Fot SV-16)</sup>

Some Maormer are even said to take place in unholy sorcerous [coupling](Lore_Sex.md) with Sea Serpents, resulting in the [Maormer Leviathan](Lore_Maormer_Leviathans.md) hybrids. These Leviathans share an appearance of elf and serpent and when standing at full height tower over other races.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-TSE-17)</sup>

Beyond the worship and reverence by his chosen people the Maormer, there is also a [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) cult known as ['the Satakal'](Lore_Satakal_(faction).md) who are said to worship Orgnum.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1WR-1)</sup> This cult is known to go nude and perform snake like actions and movements in honor of their god, and in more extreme cases, rituals involving "shedding" of the skin.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-18)</sup> This Satakal cult is viewed as a group of fringe madmen for their traditions.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-PGE1Ham-18)</sup> Some Redguards maintain that their Serpent God once dwelled on their ancestral homeland of [Yokuda](Lore_Yokuda.md) but did not survive the journey to [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md). This tradition lives on in the form of a holiday in [Satakalaam](Lore_Satakalaam.md) that honors this Serpent God, known as [Serpent's Dance](Lore_Serpent%27s_Dance.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-HOTIB-19)</sup> Traditional Redguards view Orgnum's connection to Satakal as blasphemous, and maintain that Orgnum will not be spared at the coming of the true Satakal.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TUAAYQ-20)</sup>

It is said every attack launched by the Maormer against [Summerset](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) has been led by Orgnum himself, who it seems is not only immortal but grows more youthful by the century.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup> He is said to achieve this everlasting immortality with a combination of foul spells, rituals, and sacrifices.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Fot SV-16)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SKO-21)</sup> Some outsiders attribute his eternal youthfulness and immortality to [vampirism](Lore_Vampire.md). He is credited in some legends with being the first person to [work](Lore_Smithing.md) [silver](Lore_Metals.md#Silver) in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-SSCAD-22)</sup> If there is truth to this, it would imply an unknown stretch of Orgnum's early history that he spent in Tamriel.

Numerous tales among a variety of cultures are associated with King Orgnum, such as that of Gjadil the Keel-Hauler, an explorer who sailed from Atmora and raided the palace of King Orgnum himself, burying the treasure at [Dusk](Lore_Dusk.md).<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-IRB-23)</sup> The Khajiit remember [Nurarion](Lore_Nurarion.md) the Perfect, a member of the [Sunhold](Lore_Sunhold.md) royal family and of [Khunzar-ri](Lore_Khunzar-ri.md)'s Kra'jun, who made a deal with [Clavicus Vile](Lore_Clavicus_Vile.md) for [great powers](Lore_Thu%27um.md) to fight off Orgnum's forces.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-MSF-8)</sup> The Altmeri play *The Bright Blade of Captain Castatil* tells of the protagonist going to confront Orgnum, whom he believes killed his father, only for Orgnum to remove his blue facepaint and reveal himself as his father Eroldalin, leaving no choice but for Captain Castatil to kill his father for his atrocities.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBBo CC-7)</sup>

## Appearance
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_%28crop%29.png/200px-ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_%28crop%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_(crop).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentguard_Rider_(crop).png) A Maormer Serpentguard Rider Due to Orgnum being a mysterious figure and having limited interactions with Tamriel, descriptions of his appearance come largely from outside accounts and legends. Orgnum is described as large and imposing,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-WASE-24)</sup> with white limpid jelly-like skin and bald white eyes.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-WQ4-25)</sup> He is said to appear more youthful with each passing year.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup> He is often depicted as having three hands on tapestries.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-MSS-26)</sup> He is described with many snake epithets, and some sources indicate he possesses serpentine fangs.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-SSF-9)</sup> Some depictions of Orgnum have him sharing traits of the Maormer Leviathan, with elvish traits coupled with snake like eyes, and scales, along with a garb that consists of a cloak, a turban like head covering, and a chest piece with a serpent pendant.<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-SKO2-27)</sup> Other sources indicate that Maormer potentially have depictions of Orgnum as a full fledged Sea Serpent in his own right.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-MSS-26)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3c/ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentprow_Schooner_%28crop%29.png/200px-ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentprow_Schooner_%28crop%29.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentprow_Schooner_(crop).png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-tribute-orgnum-Serpentprow_Schooner_(crop).png) Ship of the Pyandonean Armada
### Origin
According to legend, Orgnum was a phenomenally wealthy [Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md) nobleman who used his finances to launch a rebellion against the powers of the land.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TFRM-28)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup> He and his followers were banished to a place separated from [Aldmeris](Lore_Aldmeris.md) by an impenetrable mist, Pyandonea, "The Veil of Mist". This boundary proved so effective that the followers of Orgnum never again disturbed their former countrymer in Aldmeris.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup><sup>[[28]](#cite_note-TMOP-29)</sup> Orgnum was said to have had an affair with Kinlady [Heinarwe](Lore_Heinarwe.md), who was so ashamed that she forsook name, title, and clan, banished all family and servants from the hall of her place, snuffed every candle and chandelier, and withdrew, alone, into her vaults.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-Vo H-30)</sup>

### War of the Isle
Three months before the [War of the Isle](Lore_War_of_the_Isle.md) in [3E 110](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_110), King Orgnum met with [Potema Septim](Lore_Potema.md) in [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md) to make an agreement that when the Maormer took over Tamriel, she would be Empress of the continent. In turn, Potema promised that she would lead an insurrection against her brother, Emperor [Antiochus Septim](Lore_Antiochus.md). On the night of their first meeting, Potema shared her bed with Orgnum, as she deemed it to be the polite and diplomatic thing to do.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-WQ4-25)</sup>

King Orgnum's translator was fluent in the [languages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Languages) of four continents; his own country of Pyandonea, as well as those of [Atmora](Lore_Atmora.md), [Akavir](Lore_Akavir.md), and Tamriel. King Orgnum's personal ship was described as "less-than-exotic" and very similar to common Tamrielic ship architecture, in particular almost identical to [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md)'s ships. It is described as "insectoid, with its membranous sails and rugged chitin hull".<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-WQ4-25)</sup>

The War of the Isle was the last documented appearance of Orgnum and the Maormer.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup> The united alliance of the kings of Summerset and Antiochus Septim,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-BHOTE1-3)</sup> combining the Imperial fleet, the royal navies of Summerset Isle, and the magic of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), succeeded in destroying the Pyandonean invading fleet.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-TWQ5-31)</sup> It was said that the storm brewed by the Psijics of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) annihilated Orgnum's fleet so thoroughly that he was never able to muster enough force to dare another battle.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-F:OA-4)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-15)</sup>

## King Orgnum's Coffer
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/0/0e/AR-item-Treasure_Chest.png/200px-AR-item-Treasure_Chest.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Treasure_Chest.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Treasure_Chest.png) King Orgnum's Coffer [King Orgnum's Coffer](Lore_King_Orgnum%27s_Coffer.md) is a small-sized chest, ordinary in appearance. It is remarkably light, almost weightless, which offers a clue to its true magic. Once a day, the Coffer will create gold from naught. When King Orgnum himself possessed the Coffer, the supply within was limitless. He was said to have lost the Coffer in a marine battle against the Empire, led by emperor Antiochus Septim, and there are those that believe that for this reason, he could no longer afford his large fleet.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-32)</sup> Those who have found it since the report that the Coffer eventually disappears after having dispersed enough gold to shame even the wealthiest of merchants. The coffer washed up in the [Summerset Isles](Lore_Summerset_Isles.md) and has become an elusive artifact ever since.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ARArtifact-33)</sup><sup>[[33]](#cite_note-CS-34)</sup><sup>:128</sup>
