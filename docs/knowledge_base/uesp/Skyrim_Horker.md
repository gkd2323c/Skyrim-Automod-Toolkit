[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/65/SR-creature-Horker.jpg/200px-SR-creature-Horker.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horker.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-creature-Horker.jpg) A Horker **Horkers** are sea animals, similar in appearance to a walrus, that typically live in packs of three or four. They are usually found on ice at the sides of rivers in northern [Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim_(place).md).

You can occasionally see a horker crawling up a slope near the seaside. They will become hostile if you approach too closely or provoke them. Horkers are very slow and easy to avoid and outmaneuver, making them easy targets. In water, however, they are much faster and harder to outrun. You may loot their corpses for [horker meat](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Horker_Meat) and [tusks](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Horker_Tusk), along with occasionally a small amount of gold or a gem.

The *[Dragonborn](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)* [add-on](Skyrim_Add-ons.md) features a unique horker named [Lord Tusk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Lord_Tusk) on [Solstheim](Skyrim_Solstheim.md). There is also a unique [dead horker](Skyrim_Passive_Creatures.md#Horker_.28dead.29) in [Skaal Village](Skyrim_Skaal_Village.md)<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup>.

## Notes
- Horkers can be good training targets for leveling up combat/magic skills as they are slow, pose little threat, and have a lot of health to burn through.
- Horker Youngling<sup>[DB](Skyrim_Dragonborn.md)</sup> is a kind of unique horker with decreased size not found in-game. You can only encounter it in the [Editor Smoke Test Cell](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Test_Cells#Editor_Smoke_Test_Cell).
