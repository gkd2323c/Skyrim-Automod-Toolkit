# Imperial Saints

The Imperial Saints are pious individuals venerated by [the Church](Lore_The_Church.md), the majority religion among [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ralvas-1)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Gt C-3)</sup> Devotions are often made to them as a group, known collectively as the **Communion of Saints**.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTIC-4)</sup>

Many saints are historical figures who continue to be revered long after death.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-Ralvas-1)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Gt C-3)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-Opsius-5)</sup> The Church also venerates **Living Saints**, individuals who have received a calling to live by the commands of their chosen Divine as an example to others.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Olava-6)</sup> It is believed that saints are blessed by the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) with a second [sight](Lore_Sight.md) and that they do not view the world as other mortals do.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Saints are buried in the graves of [Green Emperor Way](Lore_Green_Emperor_Way.md) among other important figures in the [Empire](Lore_Empire.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-GTTIC-4)</sup>

The practice of saint-eating was popular in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md) before it was banned at some point prior to [1E 2877](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2877).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFo SC-8)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-OBSancre-9)</sup> This involved the disinterring of a saint's body for use as an ingredient in the preparation of [food](Lore_Food.md). However, apocryphal accounts suggest the practice did not immediately cease.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TFo SC-8)</sup>

## Notes
- The [Order of Talos](Lore_Order_of_Talos.md) describe [Tiber Septim](Lore_Tiber_Septim.md) as both a [god](Lore_Gods.md) and the patron saint of their order.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-19)</sup> Other [Septim emperors](Lore_Septim_Dynasty.md) were also posthumously sainted by the Imperials.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-20)</sup>
- An ancient text called the "Scroll of Precursor Saints" was supposedly discovered by [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md) in the vaults of the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md) in [2E 541](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_541). Abnur claimed that this text disproved the link between his ancestor [Tharanus Ye Redde-Hand](Lore_Tharanus_Ye_Redde-Hand.md) and the notorious [Tharhan the Mutilant](Lore_Tharhan.md).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-HTON-21)</sup>
