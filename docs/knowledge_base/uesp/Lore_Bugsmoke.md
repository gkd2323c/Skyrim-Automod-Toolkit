# Drugs

Drugs are addictive, illicit, and often illegal substances with hallucinogenic or mood-altering effects which are ingested for recreational or ritualistic purposes. Many different drugs exist in [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and beyond, and abusing them is generally disapproved of, with some exceptions.<sup>[[*citation needed*](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Template:Fact)]</sup>

Many of these drugs are just variants of [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md), such as [skooma](Lore_Skooma.md)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TIEGTTElsweyr-1)</sup> and the rarer potion known as Balmora Blue.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SRErikur-2)</sup> Other notable drugs are [greenmote](Lore_Greenmote.md) and [felldew](Lore_Felldew.md). Of course, Tamriel is also filled with many popular [alcoholic beverages](Lore_Alcoholic_Beverages.md), such as Jagga, fermented pig's milk which is popular in [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ADIF-3)</sup> Various alchemical ingredients could be construed to have drug-like effects, but most are covered separately in the [Alchemy section](Lore_Alchemy.md).

Moon sugar and skooma can be found throughout Tamriel due to widespread smuggling out of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md). Moon sugar has been commonly ingested in Elsweyr since before written history.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-PGE3E-4)</sup> In the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), rumors surfaced of gangs smuggling powerful drugs out of [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-PGE3A-5)</sup> They could be referring to [Hist sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md), a powerful drug, although it's known that entire Hist trees have been smuggled out of Black Marsh.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Oblivion-6)</sup> [Sleeping Tree Sap](Lore_Sleeping_Tree_Sap.md) also produces hallucinogenic effects.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-SRSleeping Sap-7)</sup> There are mentions of drug use in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) legends dating back to before the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md). Apparently, slaves in the realm of the Fire King [Hadhuul](Lore_Hadhuul.md) were forced to consume "drugs drawn from the admixture of daedrons into living hosts".<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TA-8)</sup>

Illicit substances were known to be frequently trafficked in the [Iliac Bay](Lore_Iliac_Bay.md) region in the late Third Era. These include Aegrotat, Indulcet, Quaesto Vil, and Sursum,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-DFSmuggling-9)</sup> though the exact preparations are unknown. It was also known for nobles in the region to be involved in these smuggling operations.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-DFSmuggled-10)</sup>

## Beggar's Bawls
**Beggar's Bawls** is liquid drug found in Fargrave that is produced by diluting [Radiance](#Radiance) with [Wounds Milk](#Wounds_Milk). Its initial creation is attributed to Skro-Khag Bonecrusher, and its exact ingredient list is a closely guarded secret which is only known by sanctioned makers. The substance is a clear and viscous liquid which is ingested as a drink, for Daedra it leaves them glassy-eyed and unwilling to move. In comparison, it is reported to have no effect on mortals and tastes like pickled [rose petals](Lore_Rose.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Iof S-13)</sup>

## Bugsmoke
Due to the rules of the [Green Pact](Lore_Green_Pact.md), [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md) are forbidden from smoking plant matter.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-14)</sup> Instead, they partake of various kinds of **bugsmoke**.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-15)</sup> [Caterpillars](Lore_Caterpillar.md) and tree grubs are commonly smoked in bone pipes.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-PGE1Ald-14)</sup> Small bugs such as [sweetgnats](Lore_Sweetgnat.md) and [torchbugs](Lore_Torchbug.md) are dried out and smoked in pipes carved from meerschaum.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MSK-16)</sup><sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DT-17)</sup>

## Daril
[Daril](Lore_Daril.md), literally meaning "seeing everything in ecstasy" in [Jel](Lore_Jel.md), is a dangerous substance<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOLAD-18)</sup> derived from moon adder venom. The aforementioned venom is instantaneously fatal to all but [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md), and is transformed by an agent in Argonian blood. Those who use daril can see sounds, hear tastes, and smell sights. Daril unfolds in stages, each unlike the last, and confuses the senses.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-TIC-19)</sup><sup>:131</sup> It cannot be safely taken by non-Argonians.<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-ESOLAD-18)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-20)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-21)</sup> Daril is very difficult to acquire, as its extraction requires the use of specialized tools.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-22)</sup> The venom is usually fermented in a swamp jelly gas bladder.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-LA:Murkmire2-23)</sup> Some Argonian users coat tiny quills in daril and insert them into the skin around their eyes for an intense sensory experience.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-24)</sup>

The use of daril dates back thousands of years. In ancient times, stone mortars were used in the process of fabricating the drug.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-25)</sup>

## Felldew
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/LG-cardart-Felldew.png/200px-LG-cardart-Felldew.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Felldew.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Felldew.png) Felldew (Legends) [Felldew](Lore_Felldew.md), a poisonous bright green ichor, is a highly addictive narcotic substance used by the mortal inhabitants of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). It is produced in the bodies of a unique variety of [Elytra](Lore_Elytra.md) native to Dunroot Burrow that emit a green glow. Upon consumption, Felldew creates a powerful euphoria that boosts the strength, intelligence and agility of the imbiber. This euphoria is short-lived, and after several minutes the imbiber begins to suffer from crippling withdrawal symptoms, damaging strength, intelligence, agility, speed, willpower, endurance, luck and health. These symptoms persist indefinitely until another dose is taken to restore the euphoria. Long-term use of the drug seems to cause facial deformations and aggressiveness.

The entrance to Dunroot Burrow is sealed by barrier membranes, which only open after having taken Felldew. Deep within the root tunnels is a man-made chamber inhabited by addicts called the Sanctum of Decadence. [Duke Thadon](Lore_Thadon.md) of [Mania](Lore_Mania.md) created an artifact known as the Chalice of Reversal. Drinking from the Chalice permanently cures Felldew addiction and inhibits withdrawal symptoms, as well as creating a stronger euphoria which boosts health and magicka. Thadon used the Chalice to indulge heavily in Felldew, but after his affair with [Duchess Syl](Lore_Syl.md) of [Dementia](Lore_Dementia_(place).md) ended badly circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Syl brought the Chalice to the Sanctum in Dunroot Burrow. In order to gain the Duke's favor, the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) recovered the Chalice for Thadon. It was later put on display in [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md)'s throne room.

## Giant Armpit Fungus

Nobles who partake in drugs will pay large sums of [gold](Lore_Gold_(currency).md) to acquire it.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-DGNobleman-29)</sup>

## Glow Grout
**Glow Grout** is powdered drug that is enjoyed by some [Watchers](Lore_Watcher.md), as the drug is taken by throwing it directly in the eye. Some people will recommend checking the dosage by throwing a volcanic rock at the eye as well. If the rock touches an exposed optic nerve, then the dose is considered significant. Known ingredients for Glow Grout include [Ogrim](Lore_Ogrim.md)'s blood, gravel and crushed [Orc](Lore_Orc.md) tusk.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Iof S-13)</sup>

## Golden Panacea
**Golden Panacea** is tonic capable of making ones organs shine. It is manufactured by the [Golden Saint](Lore_Golden_Saint.md) Nzerru, who claims it is a prized substance from the most valuable of sources and provides no free samples, though Hezehk refers to his concoctions as "ridiculous potions". Golden Panacea commences a "cleanse" within the body and must be left to writhe within to work out its euphoria. Taking too much can result in a fatal overdose. Daedra and Mortals alike can imbibe it for its organ shining effects; mortals who drink it will vomit and pass out for a short time, but it is unknown if this also happens to Daedra.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Nzerru-30)</sup><sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Hezehk-31)</sup>

## Greenmote
[Greenmote](Lore_Greenmote.md), a light green powder, is a powerful narcotic substance created by the mortal inhabitants of the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). Unrefined greenmote can be taken from mushroom tree saplings found in [Mania](Lore_Mania.md), but has few practical applications, even to [alchemists](Lore_Alchemy.md). Some mages believed that their understanding of spellcraft could be improved by using the greenmote. Only the Duke of Mania has access to a refined version of the substance. A hidden silo of greenmote was stored in a secret tunnel system beneath the [Palace of Sheogorath](Lore_Palace_of_Sheogorath.md) in [New Sheoth](Lore_New_Sheoth.md). The drug is very potent, and overdosing causes the heart to explode, resulting in immediate blood loss death. Taken in small doses, the substance causes rapture, as well as gifting the imbiber with improved night vision, speed, endurance and personality. However, it also results in temporary blurred vision, as well as a decrease in strength, intelligence and agility. Greenmote can be added to food, turned into a potion, or eaten straight.

The Manics believe that [Arden-Sul](Lore_Arden-Sul.md) died during "a night of absolute indulgence", when he and his guests overdosed on greenmote. It is tradition for an exiting Duke of Mania to invite his successor to a great feast, where the Duke will imbibe three portions of greenmote, causing an overdose. To become the new Duke, the successor must then pour his predecessor's lifeblood onto the altar in the Sacellum Arden-Sul, where it will be consumed by flame. It was rumored that [Duke Thadon](Lore_Thadon.md) was poisoned in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), when a usurper added too much greenmote to his food, resulting in the Duke's unintended death. Others claim that the Duke defected following the death of [Syl](Lore_Syl.md), and joined [Jyggalag](Lore_Jyggalag.md) as a Priest of Order.

## Hist Sap
[Hist Sap](Lore_Hist_Sap.md) is a substance made out of the [Hist](Lore_Hist.md)'s very spirit. When Hist Sap is licked by an [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md), the Hist is capable of communicating with the Argonian through visions. The sap of the Hist seems to increase the combat prowess of those who drink it. It also has the ability to 'alter' living organisms, which may explain the origin of the Argonians. Ingesting large quantities of Hist-Sap is often dangerous, even for Argonians. The [sap-speakers](Lore_Sap-Speakers.md) of the Miredancer Tribe, known for communing with their Hist and ingesting large amounts of sap in the process, often suffer the effects of sap-poisoning, including "gold tongue" (permanent change of mouth pigmentation to a golden hue), "bark scale" (thickening and darkening of surface scales), and unwanted hallucinations among other symptoms. The [Blackwood Company](Lore_Blackwood_Company.md) smuggled a Hist tree from [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) to their headquarters in [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), and extracted the sap to exploit this. However, when consumed by non-Argonian species, the Hist sap had strong hallucinogenic properties that sent them into dangerous bloodlust, though the poor treatment of this particular tree affected the sap causing similar effects on Argonians, as well. At the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), a recruit of the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) sabotaged the Hist extraction mechanism, destroying the tree in the process.

A similar substance known as [Amber Plasm](Lore_Amber_Plasm.md) is also produced by certain Hist trees. Despite its appearance, it is not Hist Sap and is instead a form of [chaotic creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md) from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) which leaks into [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md) through the Hist like blood from a wound.

## Joy-Snow
**Joy-Snow** (or joy powder) is a narcotic substance alleged to be expelled from the blowholes of [snow whales](Lore_Whale.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-SFA-37)</sup> It has an effect on humans by giving them great feelings of ecstasy and making them forgetful, and hunters caught in the "fog of joy" forget that they were hunting the whales in the first place. ## Moon Sugar
[Moon Sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md) is a grainy powder of small white crystals refined from cane grasses of [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s coasts and estuaries. It is similar in appearance to salt crystals albeit with a silver sheen. However, when light hits the substance it will appear to glow from within. Used as a spice in Elsweyr, it has modest magical properties, and is also a potent narcotic—illegal in [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md) [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) and the rest of the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). It is commonly refined into a more potent and addictive form known as [Skooma](Lore_Skooma.md). Humans, it seems, are even more susceptible to the effects of moon-sugar than the [catmen](Lore_Khajiit.md) themselves, and travelers to Elsweyr are thus cautioned against partaking of any of the sugar-loaded native food.

## Numb-Nose
**Numb-Nose** is a paste found in Fargrave that is administered nasally. It has little positive effects, targeting the user's higher cognitive functions and causing damage to the nasal area. The production of Numb-Nose is elaborate, where disparate ingredients such as a blood-rusted knife, magnesium, torchbug abdomens and a [dragonknight](Lore_Dragonknights.md)'s pauldron are mixed in lava. The mixture is then allowed to cool on a particular street in the Shambles and is then crushed into powder and mixed with juice from a rotten [lemon](Lore_Lemon.md).<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Iof S-13)</sup>

## Quaesto Vil

## Radiance
**Radiance** is a drug that is most commonly used by mortally-wounded Daedra. It is alleged to speed up the Daeda's reformation time from the [creatia](Lore_Chaotic_Creatia.md), and its side effects include synesthesia. It is a key ingredient for Beggar's Bawls.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Iof S-13)</sup>

## Redmaiden
[Redmaiden](Lore_Redmaiden.md) is a drug enabling mortal residents of [Fargrave](Lore_Fargrave.md) to protect themselves from the Drain, an ailment afflicting those who dwell in Fargrave for extended periods without entering a pact with a [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md). Redmaiden serves to mitigate the effects of the Drain, yet ceasing its usage results in the exacerbation of Drain symptoms. The healers of Fargrave considered street medicine as subpar, but Redmaiden proved effective for the pactless. Its effects are transient, as it temporarily wards off the effects of the Drain, ensuring the sanity of mortals only for a brief period. Redmaiden exhibits various other side effects, notably causing redness in the eyes of its users. To alleviate this symptom among those affected by Redmaiden's adverse effects, a solution known as redeye fading agent is applied to clear the eyes of the notorious redness. It was believed that returning to [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) might aid in managing the symptoms of the Drain, although it is uncertain whether this is indeed true.

To produce Redmaiden, one requires the following ingredients: volcanic ash mixed with [daedrat](Lore_Daedrat.md) entrails, usually with a small addition of [Skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) to mask the taste; pulverized tum-tho tree seed; and the death blood fluid of a Drain-crazed mortal, all combined in a small vessel. Some daedra were known to stockpile it for multiple cycles. It is sometimes served to mortals in a small vial to ensure their return within a fortnight. In appearance Redmaiden is a luminescent red substance, sometimes stored in cylindrical containers.

Redmaiden functions as a drug that, when consumed regularly, can tether a mortal to Fargrave much like entering into a pact with a Daedra would accomplish. It is a scarce commodity, challenging to manufacture. The [Grasp of the Stricture](Lore_Grasp_of_the_Stricture.md) was recognized for strictly controlling its distribution.

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a group of mortal inhabitants from Fargrave coordinated the theft of Redmaiden from the Grasp in order to assist those among them afflicted by the Drain. An investigation ensued regarding the incident, yet it remains uncertain whether the case was ultimately resolved.

## Sickle
**Sickle** is a dark colored liquid drug with the alleged ability to allow the user to astral project into a random realm of Oblivion. It is produced in Fargrave and some of the reported ingredients include, plum brandy, bilgewater, powdered Toadstool and runoff from Fargrave's Bazaar. However, its use can also cause the user's skull "to become a husk".<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Iof S-13)</sup>

## Skooma
[Skooma](Lore_Skooma.md) is a hallucinatory narcotic refined from [moon sugar](Lore_Moon_Sugar.md). Extremely addictive, its victims are left in constant, alternating states of delusional euphoria and lethargy. Sustained use of the drug seemingly results in permanent confusion and mental deterioration,as well as loss of teeth.

The drug can be smoked through a water pipe or consumed in liquid form; some of the apparatus used can be quite elaborate. When poor quality ingredients—like moldy moon sugar—are used in its production, the resulting "tainted" skooma has more extreme side effects including death. As spoiled moon sugar is cheaper to obtain, this practice is regrettably common. Regardless of the quality of the skooma consumed, withdrawal from the drug can have mental effects with violent consequences.

## Sleeping Tree Sap
[Sleeping Tree Sap](Lore_Sleeping_Tree_Sap.md) is highly valued for its ability to make people feel much healthier for a time, although side effects include temporary slowness and blurry purple vision. A spigot was installed on the [Sleeping Tree](Lore_Sleeping_Tree.md) in [Whiterun Hold](Lore_Whiterun_Hold.md) to harvest the sap, and the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) trading caravans have been known to pay highly for samples.

## Smudge
**Smudge** is a drug known to curb violent impulses in its users. However, continued overdosing can leave the user completely docile. In Fargrave, some Daedra use smudge so they can interact with mortals without issue. It can also be forced on an individual to render them docile. Known ingredients include malt vinegar, moon-sugar, snake venom and water from the Sever.<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Iof S-13)</sup>
