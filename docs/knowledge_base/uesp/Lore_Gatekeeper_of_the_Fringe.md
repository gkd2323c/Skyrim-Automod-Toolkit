# Gatekeeper of the Fringe

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png/200px-LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-The_Gatekeeper.png) The Gatekeeper *Have ye met me Gatekeeper? He's a lovely fellow. Never met a head he wouldn't smash.*
— [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md) The Gatekeeper of the Fringe (or simply the **Gatekeeper**) is a term used to describe a series of powerful giant [Flesh Atronachs](Lore_Flesh_Atronach.md) created via [flesh magic](Lore_Flesh_Magic.md) and the binding of a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) spirit for the purpose of protecting the Gates of Madness in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md). [Relmyna Verenim](Lore_Relmyna_Verenim.md) is credited with their creation, although the concept of a Gatekeeper is older than her creations.

## History

### Early Gatekeepers
Although most closely associated with Relmyna Verenim's research into flesh magic, the Shivering Isles was home to at least one other Gatekeeper predating her efforts. Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), a [Telvanni](Lore_House_Telvanni.md) portal mage named Dreynis was gifted the Prism of Moribund Sapience by [Sheogorath](Lore_Sheogorath.md). Although the prism helped Dreynis expand his understanding of portal magic greatly, it came at the cost of his sanity. While living in an asylum for insane mages on the island of [Gorne](Lore_Gorne.md), Dreynis opened portals to the Shivering Isles, allowing two warring legions of Daedra to cross over into [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> The [Aureal](Lore_Aureal.md) and [Mazken](Lore_Mazken.md) armies overran the entire island with their Daedric minions, and Gorne became the site of a great battle. During this battle, a Flesh Atronach named Gatekeeper Gruzo was summoned to Tamriel, but he was ultimately defeated by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup> Despite his title, Gruzo bore a closer resemblance to Flesh Atronachs created via [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) ritual than the later Gatekeepers created by Relmyna.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup>

### Relmyna's Children
Relmyna Verenim created her first Gatekeeper after many failed attempts by using the mystical ingredients she had grown in the [Gardens of Flesh and Bone](Lore_Gardens_of_Flesh_and_Bone.md). They gave life to several enchanted body parts stitched together to form a body. Using Sheogorath's divine madness as the seed, Relmyna birthed the creature. Her tears and the bones of a previous Gatekeeper were thought to have been used to slay an iteration of the creature in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-SIFringe-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SIRebuild-6)</sup>

After the death of her "child" at the hands of the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Relmyna and her apprentice Nanette Don retired to her home, the ruins of [Xaselm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Xaselm). There she resumed her study on the nature of Pain.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-7)</sup> Later that year, the Fringe was invaded by the [Forces of Order](Lore_Forces_of_Order.md). Sheogorath's Champion was sent to help Relmyna construct another Gatekeeper. The sorceress was hesitant at first due to the Hero's direct involvement with the death of the original. Relmyna and the Hero created the new Gatekeeper using a Daedric binding ritual, which helped to contain the Order which had taken over the Fringe.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-SIRebuild-6)</sup>

Sometime in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md), [Talym Rend](Lore_Talym_Rend.md), a former agent to the [Third Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md), sought a solution to his guilt with his involvement of his son's death. To make the pain go away, he traveled to the Shivering Isles to risk bargaining with Sheogorath. The Gatekeeper guarded the Gates of Madness that led past the Fringe, and to defeat him, Talym is said to have visited the nearby Garden of Swords, an absurd place in the Isles where weapons grow from the ground. He found his solution there: the Gateslayer Axe, a weapon which grew from soil fertilized by the body of the last Gatekeeper.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-GOSIOM-8)</sup> His new weapon was of great use, and he was able to defeat the Gatekeeper, giving him access to the rest of the Shivering Isles.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-GOMIOM-9)</sup>

## Notes
- Talym meets [Dyus](Lore_Dyus.md) in his journey through the Shivering Isles. [Dyus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Dyus)' dialogue in *[Oblivion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Oblivion:Oblivion)*'s expansion, *[Shivering Isles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Shivering_Isles)*, states that the Hero of Kvatch is the first person he has seen since he was first confined in Knifepoint Hollow, suggesting that Talym's journey took place after the events of *Shivering Isles*.
- The arm of a previous Gatekeeper was on display in [Una Armina](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Una_Armina)'s [Museum of Oddities](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Shivering:Museum_of_Oddities) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), although whether the arm came from the same Gatekeeper killed by the Hero of Kvatch is left ambiguous.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-10)</sup>
