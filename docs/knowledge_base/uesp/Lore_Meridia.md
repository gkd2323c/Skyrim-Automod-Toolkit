# Meridia

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/91/MER-art-The_Sun.jpg/200px-MER-art-The_Sun.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_Sun.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MER-art-The_Sun.jpg) Meridia (Tarot card) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6f/ON-misc-Meridia_%28Anniversary_Art%29.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Meridia_%28Anniversary_Art%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Meridia_(Anniversary_Art).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Meridia_(Anniversary_Art).jpg) Artwork of Meridia *Reality is a game, mortal. Learn to play, or resign yourself to becoming one of the pieces that is meant to be sacrificed.*
—Meridia Meridia, **Lady of Infinite Energies**,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-BE-1)</sup> the **Bright Lady**,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-SMBL-2)</sup> the **Strict but Fair Arbiter of Inner Luminance**,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-Meridian Cub-3)</sup> the **Sunfire**,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOSees-All-Colors-4)</sup> the **Radiant One**,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOKLD-5)</sup> the **Keeper**,<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-D4C-6)</sup><sup>:14</sup> the **Lady of Light**,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-COL-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TESVSPOGG-8)</sup><sup>:293</sup> the **Red Star**, the **Harbinger of Dawn**, **Goddess Who Holds**, **Mirror of the Lie**<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9C-9)</sup> and the **Prince of Life**<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-10)</sup> is the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) "of Life and Light",<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis-11)</sup> associated with the energies of living things.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-AL-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TRBOD-13)</sup> It is stated that her sphere is obscured to mortals.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TBo D-14)</sup> The Sunburst is her symbol.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-ESOEndarre-15)</sup> She has a great and everlasting hatred for the undead and will greatly reward any who eliminate them from the world.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-10)</sup> With fervor, she reveals purity within chaos, aiming to abolish false-life so that the Mundus may be reforged with the fire of new light.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9C-9)</sup> As such, Meridia has a reputation for benevolence,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-10)</sup> and is one of the few Daedric Princes who is usually not considered to be wholly evil.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-SRLoading-16)</sup> Her day of summoning is the [13<sup>th</sup> of Morningstar](Lore_Meridia%27s_Summoning_Day.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DFHolidays-17)</sup>

In spite of this, she is referred to as the **Glister Witch**<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> or the **Lady of Greed** by those who view her less favorably, with a habit of collecting live [human specimens](Lore_Purified.md) to satiate said greed.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DFMeridia-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Brucc Baen-20)</sup> To her enemies, Meridia is known for her violent loathing of disorder, as well as her obsessiveness, self-righteousness, irrational anger towards entities she deems impure, and hatred of mortal free will and defiance.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONDarien-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONUrgarlag-22)</sup><sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Tuttle2020-23)</sup> She believes that free will should be surrendered to passion, "destiny is fulfilled for the vessel".<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9C-9)</sup>

As with most Princes, Meridia sees mortals as "assets" primarily for use as a means to an end.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-ONGroundskeeper-24)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONDarien-21)</sup> Meridia can bestow upon mortals immortality, at the cost of their will, becoming those known as the [Purified](Lore_Meridian_Purified.md); slaves to her command, cleansed of disease, death, and free will.<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ONUrgarlag-22)</sup> People who join the ranks of the Purified are both those that willingly pledge to serve Meridia and the unwilling, such as those that defy her.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-ESOTharayya-10)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ESOKing Narilmor-25)</sup><sup>[[25]](#cite_note-ONMeridian Purified-26)</sup> Meridia also granted immortality to her champion, [Umaril the Unfeathered](Lore_Umaril_the_Unfeathered.md), the ancient [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) enemy of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-TSOP3-27)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-TSOP6-28)</sup> Her Aurorans and Umaril returned during the time of the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) to seek vengeance on the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-KN-29)</sup>

Meridia (originally known as **Merid-Nunda**) was believed to have been one of the [Magne-Ge](Lore_Magna_Ge.md), a family of divine beings that fled to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md) soon after the creation of [Mundus](Lore_Mundus.md). She was the second of [Magnus](Lore_Magnus.md)'s daughters and the sister of [Xero-Lyg](Lore_Xero-Lyg.md), [Mnemo-Li](Lore_Mnemo-Li.md), [Iana-Lor](Lore_Iana-Lor.md), [Unala-Se](Lore_Unala-Se.md), the eldest [Prime Archon](Lore_Ithelia.md), [Londa-Vera](Lore_Londa-Vera.md), [Sheza-Rana](Lore_Sheza-Rana.md), and [Valia-Sha](Lore_Valia-Sha.md). Alongside her sisters, she was part of the Nine Coruscations, who followed the parabolas that led away from Magnus and separated from him when he withdrew from the creation of the [Aurbis](Lore_Aurbis.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9C-9)</sup><sup>[[29]](#cite_note-EOMN-30)</sup> For supposedly consorting with [illicit spectra](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), she was cast out of Aetherius and took the mantle of a Daedric Prince.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-EOMN-30)</sup><sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-31)</sup>

She is the matron of the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md),<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-ESOIachesis-11)</sup> her realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) that she created by bending and shaping the rays of light from her father, Magnus the [sun](Lore_Sun.md),<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-EOMN-30)</sup> The Colored Rooms are inhabited by the [Aurorans](Lore_Auroran.md) and [Golden Saints](Lore_Golden_Saint.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-ONDarien-21)</sup> [Blessed Sentinels](Lore_Blessed_Sentinel.md) are another type of Daedric servant.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Brucc Baen-20)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-LA-32)</sup> [Magna-Geodes](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna-Geode) are associated with her.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-Furniture-33)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-DOM-34)</sup>

Meridia can, in some sense, alter the rate at which time flows forward.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-EOMN-30)</sup> For Meridia, time is said to be relative, and her priestesses have been known to grant time-altering blessings.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-35)</sup> The goddess has also been known to offer her champions the chance to experience alternate timelines through the power of a [Light of Meridia](Lore_Light_of_Meridia.md).<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-36)</sup> Meridia, the Red Star is radiant during the [Dragon Break](Lore_Dragon_Break.md), when the [Dragon](Lore_Akatosh.md) was [blinded and bent](Lore_Middle_Dawn.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9C-9)</sup> Ancient [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) attribute the power of redshift to Merid-Nunda, which was believed to bend and alter time.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-BOBIII-37)</sup>

Her sphere of energy and life often brings her into direct conflict with many of the other Princes of Oblivion.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ESO-38)</sup> The most notable example of these feuds is her eternal battle with the Daedric Prince of Domination, [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-EGTColdharbour-39)</sup> During the waning days of the Ayleid Empire, Meridia would be responsible for the destruction of the Mola Gbal -worshipping<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-ESOHo SLoad Txt-40)</sup> [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleids.md) city of [Abagarlas](Lore_Abagarlas.md),<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TESIVOOGG-41)</sup> commanding her knights in the Meridia-worshipping Ayleid city of [Delodiil](Lore_Delodiil.md) to invade after discovering a great and terrible [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) known as the [Mortuum Vivicus](Lore_Mortuum_Vivicus.md) was gifted to Abagarlas by the Lord of Domination himself.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-TWOD-42)</sup> This feud would continue well into the Second Era, where Meridia's assistance was instrumental in stopping Molag Bal's [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md). Denizens of Molag Bal's realm of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) refer to Meridia as the **Shining Bitch**, and her worshipers are often locked away and tortured within the [Lightless Oubliette](Lore_Lightless_Oubliette.md).<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-TLO-43)</sup> Her other notable enemies include [Nocturnal](Lore_Nocturnal.md), who once invaded Meridia's realm in her quest to rewrite reality,<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TDCquest-44)</sup> and [Ebonarm](Lore_Ebonarm.md), the God of War who dislikes most Daedric Princes.<sup>[[43]](#cite_note-DFFactions-45)</sup>

## Culture and Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/9/92/ON-concept-Prince-Meridia-emblem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Meridia-emblem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Prince-Meridia-emblem.png) An emblem representing Meridia
### Ayleid
Meridia (known as the **Bright One** and **Rainbow of Light**)<sup>[[44]](#cite_note-RDP-46)</sup> was an extremely important figure for the Ayleids, as she was considered the personification of their fourth element, light.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-EOMN-30)</sup> The Ayleids often depicted Meridia with wings and a cowl.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ESOAS-47)</sup>

Meridia was the patron deity of Garlas Malatar, a city which was built upon the [Gold Coast](Lore_Gold_Coast.md). Its ruler, [King Narilmor](Lore_King_Narilmor.md), was a devout worshiper of Meridia who dedicated his life and city to her veneration. He was considered a favorite of the Daedric Prince, second only to her champion, Umaril the Unfeathered. She gifted the city with a relic known as the Guiding Light, which shone above the city with her unquenchable light. It was a literal and metaphorical beacon, visible for great distances.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-MTCKN-48)</sup>

An ancient shrine to Meridia was built in the grotto of Sideways Cave, long before it became the site of the city of Abagarlas in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). The settlers of Abagarlas uncovered the forgotten shrine and claimed it as their own, which Meridia considered a violation. To the people of this city, she was remembered as the **Terror of the Most Terror** for her vengeance when she crushed Abargarlas with her stone roots and, incensed that some survivors escaped, buried the grotto almost entirely.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TESIVOOGG-41)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Oblivion Abagarlas-49)</sup>

She is remembered by the [Imperials](Lore_Imperial.md) as a patron and mentor to the Heartland High Elves, and complicit in the bondage and oppression of their ancestors.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-AYQ-50)</sup>

### Khajiit
In Khajiiti mythology, Meridia is referred as **Merid-Nunda** (known as **False Spirit of Greed**, **The Orphaned Glimmer**, **Rainbow Angel**, and **Shining One**<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-BOBII-51)</sup>) and she is considered a daughter of [Magrus](Lore_Magrus.md) that he forged from the [aether](Lore_Aetherius.md) along with her siblings.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TAS-52)</sup> In older traditions, such as those compiled by [Amun-dro](Lore_Amun-dro.md), Merid-Nunda is seen in a poor light and has a bad reputation. She is described in such terms as being "born from light without love" and having "intellect without wisdom", and claims she was the consort of demons.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TAS-52)</sup> Merid-Nunda was also associated with greed and her name was sometimes invoked in situations involving theft in a negative sense.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Zerith ESO-53)</sup>

Of the deeds attributed to Merid-Nunda, some traditions claim that she orchestrated the death of [Lorkhaj](Lore_Lorkhaj.md). Merid-Nunda was also said to have united with the kinslayer [Dagon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dagon) and Demon King [Molagh](Lore_Molagh.md) to assault the [Lunar Lattice](Lore_Lunar_Lattice.md).<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TAS-52)</sup> There are a few known versions of this story, that recorded by Amun-dro and *[The Bladesongs of Boethra](Lore_The_Bladesongs_of_Boethra.md)* a pre-Ri'Datta text of the [Boethra](Lore_Boethra.md) cult among the Khajiit. The former says that Azurah defeated Merid-Nunda and dragged her away from the [Varliance Gate](Lore_Varliance_Gate.md), before binding her in the [Void](Lore_Void.md) with mirrors.

*Bladesongs* expands on the battle and the participants, with it stating that Boethra, [Mafala](Lore_Mafala.md) and [Noctra](Lore_Noctra.md) were also involved in protecting the Lattice. Merid-Nunda is described as being a multitude of glass shards that could take different forms which attacked Azurah with "cutting light".<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-BOBII-51)</sup> In this text, she is also implied to being able to bend and alter time by manipulating the redshift, though Boethra was eventually able to adjust to the changes.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-BOBIII-37)</sup> In this version of the tale, Merid-Nunda manages to split herself in two and feints being captured by Azurah, reaching the Aether Prism that was once Magrus' eye.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-BOBII-51)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-TSS-54)</sup> Boethra and Noctra help Azurah defeat Merid-Nunda, and Azurah is described as casting Merid-Nunda's shards into the Void and bending the light so the latter reflects upon herself and is trapped.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-BOBIII-37)</sup>

Both traditions suggest that this state was not permanent, with Amun-dro stating at the time of his writing, that there were stories among the nomads that Merid-Nunda had since escaped. In *Bladesongs*, it is implied that the battle may have occurred multiple times and that Merid-Nunda would return. Boethra promises Azurah that she will be ready when that time arrives once more.<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TAS-52)</sup><sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TAS-52)</sup><sup>[[35]](#cite_note-BOBIII-37)</sup>

Despite her poor reputation, shrines to Merid-Nunda could still be found in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md).<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-Sot R-55)</sup> Modern Khajiit may not acknowledge the Merid-Nunda myths, as the [Riddle'Thar](Lore_Riddle%27Thar.md) cult altered or removed many of their deities following the creation of the [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) Confederacy,<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-ESOSB-56)</sup> and she is not mentioned in more recent creation myth as told by [Clan Mother Ahnissi](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Clan_Mother_Ahnissi), while her father Magrus is.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-WOCMA-57)</sup>

### Nord
Meridia has a [temple](Lore_Kilkreath_Temple.md) on [Mount Kilkreath](Lore_Mount_Kilkreath.md), whose priests were hospitable and more than willing to provide travelers with food and warm beds. The temple's caretakers closed its doors to conduct secret rites during the feasts of High Sun and Low Sun.<sup>[[56]](#cite_note-GTWS-58)</sup> While many of the local Nords in nearby Solitude found Meridia's worship to be heretical, the temple was more or less left alone.<sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ESOThagarte-59)</sup>

Rumor holds that the Direfrost Flame and the Torch of Heirs, ancient relics of the Direfrost clan, were created by Meridia to distress the Daedra.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-TDF-60)</sup>

### Worship
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/48/ON-concept-Meridia_Style.jpg/200px-ON-concept-Meridia_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Meridia_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-concept-Meridia_Style.jpg) Auroran-inspired Cult of Meridia armaments [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/4e/LG-cardart-Altar_of_Meridia.png/200px-LG-cardart-Altar_of_Meridia.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Altar_of_Meridia.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Altar_of_Meridia.png) An Altar of Meridia"Lustrant" is a term used by [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s [Dremora](Lore_Dremora.md) minions to refer to the followers of Meridia, alongside their more vulgar terms such as "Glow ass".<sup>[[41]](#cite_note-TLO-43)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-Schick2015-61)</sup> Meridia doesn't speak to just any mortal, but the leader of a cult sect will usually have Meridia's ear.<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Sd-62)</sup>

Meridian cultists adorn their armor with patterns resembling scales, wings and rays of light, true to the motifs present in Meridian imagery. Flowing lines which represent the energy of Meridia are a common form of symbolism. Mastery of motion is a cornerstone in the fighting techniques of those revering Meridia. As such, supple leather that allows for easy movement is a hallmark in Meridian cultists' leg armor. Light and vibration affect crystals, so Meridian cultists carry crystals into battle, believing it allows Meridia to bless their moves directly. Their weapons are designed to snap skeletons' bones and deal utmost damage to necromancers, some of Meridia's enemies.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-CM72MS-63)</sup> Her cultists are very close to one another and refer to each other as "brothers and sisters".<sup>[[59]](#cite_note-Sd-62)</sup>

## History

### Dawn and Merethic Eras
[Star-Queen Varalias](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Star-Queen_Varalias) asserts that Meridia is the Light who witnessed the Crucible of Creation.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-9C-9)</sup>

An ancient shrine to Meridia was built in the grotto of Sideways Cave at an unclear time, with its builders remaining a mystery, very early on in the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md). When it became the site of the city of [Abagarlas](Lore_Abagarlas.md) later on, the settlers of Abagarlas uncovered the forgotten shrine and claimed it as their own.<sup>[[39]](#cite_note-TESIVOOGG-41)</sup><sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Oblivion Abagarlas-49)</sup>

### First Era

#### The Hollow City
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2e/ON-load-Coldharbour.jpg/200px-ON-load-Coldharbour.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Coldharbour.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Coldharbour.jpg) The Hollow City Long ago, a city existed upon Nirn which was devoted to Meridia. Meridia gifted her people the [Lights of Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lights_of_Meridia), relics whose power protected the city and made life easier for its inhabitants. Molag Bal sent minions from Coldharbour to destroy it, but the warriors and mages of the city managed to seal the gates and protect everyone within.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TGTAOM-64)</sup> Everyone outside the gates was slaughtered. The Ayleid King [Laloriaran Dynar](Lore_Laloriaran_Dynar.md) was visiting the city, and he quickly took command of the city's defenses.

The Ayleid King single-handedly protected the gates. As he fought, he prayed, and Meridia answered.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TGTAOM-64)</sup> She felt compassion for her worshipers, but also saw an opportunity: Molag Bal's portal worked both ways. She filled the Lights of Meridia with even more of her divine power, warded the city and pushed it into Coldharbour.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TGTAOM-64)</sup> The Lights of Meridia and the city's inhabitants were scattered across Coldharbour when the city entered the realm. Many of the city's inhabitants were captured or slain, and the Lights were stolen by the minions of Molag Bal. Despite the fact that not all of her people survived, Meridia still accomplished a major feat; she had sent a portion of her realm into Molag Bal's domain, and he could not touch it.<sup>[[61]](#cite_note-TGTAOM-64)</sup> This tactic would pay off in the years to come.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-THC-65)</sup>

#### Twilight of the Ayleids
Sometime after the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md),<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-FBJ-66)</sup> various Ayleid kingdoms launched a desperate search for the fabled [Wrathstone](Lore_Wrathstone.md), which they believed would lead them to a [weapon](Lore_Dragon.md) that would be their salvation against the encroaching forces of the [Alessian Empire](Lore_Alessian_Empire.md). An expedition marched to the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) city of [Mzulft](Lore_Mzulft.md), in the hold of [Eastmarch](Lore_Eastmarch.md) in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md), where it was rumored the Wrathstone was held. The Ayleids besieged Mzulft, but failed to breach it or retrieve the artifact.<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-IPOM-67)</sup> At the time, the Ayleids were unaware that the Dwemer only possessed the left half of the Wrathstone, and that half was stored not at Mzulft, but at nearby [Kagrenzel](Lore_Kagrenzel.md).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-IPOM-67)</sup>

After the failure of the raid on Mzulft, Meridia appeared to her faithful with a cryptic warning that the Ayleid Empire would soon fall, and gifted the right half of the Wrathstone to the city of [Garlas Malatar](Lore_Garlas_Malatar.md).<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-FBJ-66)</sup> At the time, Garlas Malatar was ruled by King [Narilmor](Lore_Narilmor.md), who was one of Meridia's most devout and favored adherents after Umaril the Unfeathered.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-MTCKN-48)</sup> To keep his piece of the Wrathstone safe,<sup>[[63]](#cite_note-FBJ-66)</sup> Narilmor did not provide aid nor asylum to the desperate Ayleid refugees from other kingdoms, and locked them out of his city.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-MTCKN-48)</sup> The inhabitants of Garlas Malatar were not heard of again. Although Garlas Malatar was eventually besieged by men, some accounts have suggested that the attackers did not even make it inside Narilmor's city before it collapsed into ruin; it was suggested that the city fell from within, brought down by internal conflict or sabotage.<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-MTCKN-48)</sup>

### Second Era

#### Planemeld
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c0/ON-npc-Meridia_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Meridia_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Meridia_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Meridia_02.jpg) *"The Dark Prince will need stronger chains than that to stop me."* During the Planemeld and the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md), Cyrodiil was left in ruins and the provinces were divided by the few were left to oppose the invading Daedra. Meridia had her servant, Sees-All-Colors, assassinate the leader of the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) in order to assume the role of guildmaster. Under Daedric command and funded by the wealth of Meridia's worshipers,<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-ESOCountess_Hakruba-68)</sup> the guild began refusing all other contracts and focused its efforts on destroying [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) and opposing Molag Bal's schemes.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOSees-All-Colors-4)</sup><sup>[[66]](#cite_note-ESOFighters Guild-69)</sup> Meanwhile, the [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md) were reformed following the prophesized arrival of the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), who freed the Companions from Bal's clutches and reclaimed the Amulet of Kings, defeating [Mannimarco](Lore_Mannimarco.md) in the process.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-ESO-38)</sup>

Disguised as a mortal, Meridia welcomed the Vestige along with the Fighters and [Mages](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) guilds into the [Hollow City](Lore_Hollow_City.md), which she had thrown into Coldharbour in years past. She allowed them to plot their assault on Molag Bal's citadel within its walls.<sup>[[62]](#cite_note-THC-65)</sup> Meridia waited within her city as King Laloriaran Dynar and the [Lights of Meridia](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lights_of_Meridia) returned to her city.<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-TAOM-70)</sup> When the guilds finally invaded Molag Bal's fortress, Meridia personally manifested in the Planar Vortex to destroy it. She erected a forcefield to prevent Molag Bal from harming the Warriors of Light as they destroyed the Dark Anchors within the vortex. The power of the Light of Meridia was unleashed at the focal point of the vortex, and the Planar Vortex was destroyed.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-TFA-71)</sup>

The Vestige later faced Molag Bal at the seat of his power and defeated him. This released untold measures of souls from the Prince's grasp. Meridia plucked the Hero's soul as it escaped the realm, and allowed the Vestige to reunite with it in the Colored Rooms.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-Meridia GOS-72)</sup>

#### The Triad
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/5a/ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower_%28Darien_Dawnbreaker%29.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower_%28Darien_Dawnbreaker%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower_(Darien_Dawnbreaker).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Crystal_Tower_(Darien_Dawnbreaker).jpg) Darien sacrifices himself to return the last of Meridia's power to Dawnbreaker Meridia sent her [Golden Knight](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Golden_Knight) to foil the [Triad](Lore_The_Triad.md)'s plans. The Knight was kidnapped by agents of [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), and Dawnbreaker was shattered. The Vestige sought Meridia's aid in repairing the blade. Meridia added to its power, enabling it to be used to locate her Knight. Nocturnal then invaded the Colored Rooms, abruptly ending the Hero's correspondence with the Daedric Prince.<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-Meridia TDC-73)</sup><sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TDCquest-44)</sup>

With Meridia incapacitated, only the Golden Knight was left to enact her plans. Dawnbreaker was corrupted by Nocturnal during later conflicts,<sup>[[71]](#cite_note-ANA-74)</sup> and the Knight, realizing that he was the last Light of Meridia, sacrificed himself to purge the darkness from the artifact. The Vestige used Dawnbreaker to defeat Nocturnal at the [Crystal Tower](Lore_Crystal_Tower.md)'s summit, ending the Triad's threat to Tamriel.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-TCT-75)</sup>

Later that year, Meridia's temple at Mount Kilkreath was ravaged by a [harrowstorm](Lore_Harrowstorms.md). Many of her worshipers were killed or transformed into undead monsters. The remnants of her priesthood delved into the temple and retrieved a relic imbued with her light, known as [Meridia's Brilliance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia%27s_Brilliance).<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-MB-76)</sup> It had the power to channel Meridia's light and purge the temple of corruption. One of the surviving priests used the relic to seal the temple following the disaster.<sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ESOPB-77)</sup>

### Third Era
Meridia has gifted many different Daedric artifacts to her champions over the years, though some of these relics are associated with other Daedric Princes. Sometime around [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), an [agent of the Blades](Lore_Agent.md) received the [Ring of Khajiiti](Lore_Ring_of_Khajiiti.md) from Meridia after defeating a sorcerer who betrayed her.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-DFMeridia-19)</sup>

In [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Meridia charged the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) with the task of destroying necromancers who were raiding tombs for corpses to raise an army of the undead, again in return for this ring.<sup>[[75]](#cite_note-OBMeridia-78)</sup> The same hero also defeated her champion, Umaril the Unfeathered, who had returned from Oblivion after his defeat in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-KN-29)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Meridia offered the sword [Dawnbreaker](Lore_Dawnbreaker.md) to the [Last Dragonborn](Lore_Hero.md) in [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201) in return for helping her cleanse her temple, which had become tainted by a necromancer and the [shades](Lore_Corrupted_Shade.md) of the corpses he desecrated.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-SRBreak-79)</sup>

## Artifacts

### Dark Orbs
[Dark Orbs](Lore_Dark_Orbs.md), also known as **Cursed Orbs of Meridia**, are spherical objects associated with the [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Meridia. They were employed to imbue the selected ones favored by the Lady of Infinite Energies with remarkable vitality, preventing them from dying, resurrecting them, and even preventing them from being banished back to [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md) as long as they remain near the orbs. The shattering of a Dark Orb can locally [freeze time](Lore_Time_Magic.md) in the immediate surroundings.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f1/LG-cardart-Dawnbreaker_02.jpg/150px-LG-cardart-Dawnbreaker_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dawnbreaker_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dawnbreaker_02.jpg) Dawnbreaker (Legends)
### Dawnbreaker
[Dawnbreaker](Lore_Dawnbreaker.md) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) artifact created by the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) Meridia. It was forged "in a holy light that breaks upon" the Prince's foes. In appearance it is a longsword containing a distinctive light emitting crystal in its cross-guard known as the **Dawnstar Gem**. It was created with the intention of "burning away corruption and false life". As such, it is particularly effective against Meridia's "foes": this primarily applies to the undead, although its powers can also be used on Daedra and [werewolves](Lore_Werewolf.md).

### Charm of the Dawn
The **Charm of the Dawn** is a token created by Meridia at the first light of dawn, at the height of summer, and made into a necklace.<sup>[[77]](#cite_note-ONCharm-80)</sup>

### Lights of Meridia
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/d/d2/ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg/150px-ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-furnishing-Light_of_Meridia.jpg) Light of Meridia (ESO) The [Lights of Meridia](Lore_Light_of_Meridia.md) are a series of powerful crystalline [artifacts](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Meridia and filled with her power.

The Lights of Meridia were initially a gift to the Meridia-worshipping [Hollow City](Lore_Hollow_City.md) by their patron Prince, power that protected the city and made life easier for its inhabitants. When Meridia's rival [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) sent his [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) minions to attack the city, Meridia sensed an opportunity, she filled the Lights with even more of her divine power and used them to ward the city and push it into [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md) through Molag Bal's own [portal](Lore_Portals.md). Encased by the power of the Lights this manner, the Hollow City became a part of Meridia's own [domain](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md) in Molag Bal's [realm](Lore_Planes_of_Existence.md), a "dagger in his side" which he could not touch.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/be/SR-item-Meridia%27s_Beacon.jpg/150px-SR-item-Meridia%27s_Beacon.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Meridia%27s_Beacon.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Meridia%27s_Beacon.jpg) Meridia's beacon (Skyrim)
### Meridia's Beacon
[Meridia's Beacon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia%27s_Beacon) is a magical [magna-geode](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna-Geode) shaped stone associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Meridia. The Lady of Infinite Energies is known to speak to those in possession of the beacon and uses it to influence and compel those who hold onto the Beacon.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f4/ON-item-Meridia%27s_Brilliance.jpg/150px-ON-item-Meridia%27s_Brilliance.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Meridia%27s_Brilliance.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Meridia%27s_Brilliance.jpg) Meridia's Brilliance (ESO)
### Meridia's Brilliance
[Meridia's Brilliance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Meridia%27s_Brilliance) is a magical [magna-geode](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Magna-Geode) shaped stone associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Meridia. It is a relic from the [Kilkreath Temple](Lore_Kilkreath_Temple.md) that radiates with Meridia's [light](Lore_Elements.md#Light). It possesses the ability to channel her divine radiance, and it was also believed that it could cleanse [undead](Lore_Undeath.md) corruption and seal off the temple when necessary.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/ON-item-Opal_Charm.jpg/150px-ON-item-Opal_Charm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Opal_Charm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Opal_Charm.jpg) The Opal Charm
### Opal Charm
The [Opal Charm](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Opal_Charm) is an artifact associated with the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Prince.md) Meridia, which has been enchanted to pull light directly from her realm of the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md); this light is capable of temporarily manifesting physical objects when mastered. Reflecting its creator's obsession for conformity, the charm also unifies the lifeforce of the artifact bearer and their followers, not allowing any to die till the wearer is slain. In appearance, it is an engraved egg-sized opal that is cast in gold, and hung around a chain. Even while inactive, it is capable of illuminating an entire shrine.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/66/ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg/150px-ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-The_Prismatic_Core.jpg) The Prismatic Core
### The Prismatic Core
The [Prismatic Core](Lore_Prismatic_Core.md) is a powerful crystal that powered the **Prismatic Weapon**, created by the willing sacrifices of many priestesses of Meridia. It was given to the forces of the [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) city-state of [Delodiil](Lore_Delodiil.md) during the early [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md), so that they could defeat their [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) -worshipping enemy of [Abagarlas](Lore_Abagarlas.md) and destroy the [Mortuum Vivicus](Lore_Mortuum_Vivicus.md). The destruction of Abagarlas was successful, but the [weapon](Lore_Weapons.md) was ruined in the attempt. The crystal powering it was salvaged, although drained of its power, and five Ayleid heroes set out to the Dwemer ruin of Mzeneldt to hide the crystal so that the forces of Bal could not find it. The priestess Valasha sacrificed herself to re-power the crystal, with the rest of the heroes giving their lives to safeguard. It was later recovered by the [Fighters Guild](Lore_Fighters_Guild.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582) and re-forged into the Prismatic Weapon by [Merric at-Aswala](Lore_Merric_at-Aswala.md), assisted by [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> The Prismatic Weapon was used by the guild's champions to destroy the Mortuum Vivicus once and for all, and was left in the Vestige's possession afterwards.

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/OBR-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg/150px-OBR-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Ring_of_Khajiiti.jpg) Ring of Khajiiti (Oblivion)
### Ring of Khajiiti
The [Ring of Khajiiti](Lore_Ring_of_Khajiiti.md) (sometimes just **Ring of Khajiit** or **Ring of the Khajiiti**) is a [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) associated with two [Daedric Princes](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md), Meridia and [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md), who have both been known to offer the Ring in return for service. It is said that the ring makes the wearer invisible, silent, and quick.

The Ring was originally owned by Mephala, until it was stolen off her arm by [Rajhin](Lore_Rajhin.md), the renowned burglar who later became revered as the Thief God of the [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md). Using the Ring's powers, Rajhin became the most successful burglar in [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md)'s history. It is even said that Rajhin stole a tattoo from the neck of [Empress Kintyra](Lore_Kintyra_I.md) as she slept. According to an account, he utilized the Ring's abilities to pilfer the [Book that Knows](Lore_Oghma_Infinium.md) from [Hermaeus Mora](Lore_Hermaeus_Mora.md)'s library beneath the sea. Eventually, after making use of its power too freely, Rajhin was abandoned by the Ring and left exposed to his enemies.

## The Colored Rooms
The [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md) are a many-faceted realm of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md), created and ruled over by Meridia, known as the [Daedric Prince](Lore_Daedric_Princes.md) of Infinite Energies. From what little has been seen of the Colored Rooms, they seem to resemble a cross between a [coral reef](Lore_Coral.md) and a vast field of floating stones, strewn with colorful trails of dust or cloud. The "ground" between the stones looks like luminescent water, but is solid enough to walk on. The skies are described as floating effluvium oceans of crimson. It has been suggested that the "colors" of the realms are a reference to the different schools of [magic](Lore_Magic.md).

[Aurorans](Lore_Auroran.md) and their warhorses are the spawn of Meridia that reside in the Colored Rooms, being made from pure light and have skin tones of virtually any hue. [Golden Saints](Lore_Aureal.md) are also purported to reside in the realm. The Colored Rooms have been described by those souls who have been there as "pretty boring" aside from Meridia's lectures (which are described as "pretty boring" as well).
