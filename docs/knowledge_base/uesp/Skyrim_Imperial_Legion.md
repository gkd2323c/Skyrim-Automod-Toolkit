# Imperial Legion


The Legion is a joinable faction in Skyrim, and its conflict with the [Stormcloak Rebellion](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md) plays an integral role in the [main quest](Skyrim_Main_Quest.md). The Imperial Legion is in conflict with the Stormcloaks during Skyrim's Civil War, and is charged with hunting down and eradicating its members throughout the province and quelling the rebellion. The Imperial Legion in Skyrim is led by [General Tullius](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md).

## Joining
Following your narrow escape from execution at [Helgen](Skyrim_Helgen.md) during the quest [Unbound](Skyrim_Unbound.md), and just after [Alduin](Skyrim_Alduin.md) flies off, you will have a choice to follow either [Hadvar](Skyrim_Hadvar.md) or [Ralof](Skyrim_Ralof.md) into [Helgen Keep](Skyrim_Helgen_Keep.md). If you choose Hadvar, and then from the keep either accompany him on the road to, or leave and rejoin him in [Riverwood](Skyrim_Riverwood.md), he will invite you to join the Imperial Legion, or he might make the offer in [Alvor's](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Alvor) house in Riverwood. If you instead follow Ralof, you will be asked to join the Stormcloaks.

If you make no choice at that point, [Imperial soldiers](Skyrim_Imperial_Soldier.md) that you will encounter in your travels, or upon entering an [Imperial camp](Skyrim_Imperial_Legion.md#Camps) will encourage you to join the Legion by going to [Solitude](Skyrim_Solitude.md), where you will be directed to [Legate Rikke](Skyrim_Legate_Rikke.md) at [Castle Dour](Skyrim_Castle_Dour.md). Speaking with her can initiate the quest [Join the Imperial Legion](Skyrim_Joining_the_Legion.md).

However, anytime before you complete that quest, you may decide to join the Stormcloaks instead by completing [Joining the Stormcloaks](Skyrim_Joining_the_Stormcloaks.md). Doing so will make the Imperial quest fail (if it is in the active list). You will have a last opportunity to rejoin the Legion later in the quest line: at the end of the second quest for the Stormcloaks, [The Jagged Crown](Skyrim_The_Jagged_Crown_(Stormcloaks).md), you can bring the [crown](Skyrim_Jagged_Crown.md) to [General Tullius](Skyrim_General_Tullius.md) instead of [Ulfric Stormcloak](Skyrim_Ulfric_Stormcloak.md), who will finally admit you to the Legion.

## Notes
- If you side with the Imperial Legion, you cannot join the [Stormcloak rebellion](Skyrim_Stormcloaks.md) at the same time. However, it is possible, at one point in the Civil War questline, to [switch sides](Skyrim_The_Jagged_Crown_(Imperial).md).
- Joining the Imperial Army may cause random spawned groups of Stormcloak Soldiers to attack you, and you will no longer be welcome in Stormcloak camps or forts.
- If you encounter Legionnaires in their camps at any Hold while wearing Stormcloak Armor, they may question your decision on your armor and suspect you of being a Stormcloak spy. If you admit that you support the rebels, they will attack you, but if you tell them that you don't mean anything by wearing it, they will allow you to stick with your choice of armor in spite of their distaste.
- If you have taken over a city for the Empire and displaced a Jarl who supported the Stormcloaks, they, along with their Steward, Housecarl and family, will be exiled to the [Palace of the Kings](Skyrim_Palace_of_the_Kings.md). (The exception to this relocation is Jarl Korir of Winterhold and his family, who are living in Brunwulf Free-Winter's old house.) For obvious reasons, they won't be happy to see you but they won't attack you.
- If you attack the opposing faction's camps, keep in mind that the camp leader is unkillable.
- Each hold camp is garrisoned by a ranking officer and several soldiers, the starting ones also come with a [quartermaster](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Skyrim:Imperial_Quartermaster) and some [horses](Skyrim_Horses.md). Additionally, there is a medical tent with between one and three wounded soldiers.
