Mounts of Tamriel: Volume 11, Bears **by [Fadus Falto](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Fadus_Falto) of Skingrad** *An account of bear mount protecting its rider from great danger* *
---
The mighty [bear](Lore_Bear.md) as a mount is a study in contrasts. [Nord](Lore_Nord.md) [skalds](Lore_Skald.md) tell tales of bloodthirsty bears, rampaging through a camp in search of a scrap of meat. But when husbanded by a person with a stout heart and a fearless demeanor, the legendary protective nature of a mother bear can be turned to the aide *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* of a rider. Once bonded, these beast *[[sic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/UESPWiki:Spelling#Books_and_Direct_Quotes)]* make fearsome mounts, walking unflaggingly over even the most rugged terrain and rising to defense of their rider even in the face of the greatest of beasts.

An outrider who patrols the far flung reaches of [Western Skyrim](Lore_Western_Skyrim.md) swore to me in a tavern by the [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) trade road that his [snow bear](Lore_Snow_Bear.md) mount stood up to the likes of a [giant](Lore_Giant.md) that had wandered into his path. Said the great beast rose up on its hind legs, the rider clinging desperately to its back, and let loose a terrifying roar that sent the giant running for the hills. When the danger had past the beast, who he affectionately calls Snowpelt, nuzzled into him like a mother tending to her cub. Incredible!
