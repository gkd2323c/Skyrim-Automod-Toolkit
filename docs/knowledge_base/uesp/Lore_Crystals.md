## [Aeonstone](Lore_Aeonstone.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3f/ON-misc-Aeonstone.jpg/200px-ON-misc-Aeonstone.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Aeonstone.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-misc-Aeonstone.jpg) Aeonstone [Aeonstone](Lore_Aeonstone.md) is a green stone that can be found throughout mines in southern Elsweyr. Though it has no intrinsic power, aeonstone has the unique capability to store and amplify magical energy. In this state, the aeonstone becomes empowered and glows with a bright green hue. This unique property was discovered by the [New Moon Cult](Lore_New_Moon_Cult.md), who engaged in ritualistic sacrifice to empower themselves and [their masters](Lore_Dragon.md). Many cultists used aeonstone in their armor. These stones allowed cultists to greatly empower their own sorcery with this magic, even allowing [necromancers](Lore_Necromancy.md) to resurrect and enslave [Dragons](Lore_Dragon.md). Absorbing stored energy from aeonstone can be extremely dangerous for mortals, and improper usage can wreak havoc on the mage's body.

**Found in:** - [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Aeonstone_Shard)

## Aetherquartz
**Aetherquartz** is a "milky"<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> crystal that is similar to [quartz](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Quartz) in appearance. ## [Amber](Lore_Amber.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/SR-item-Amber.jpg/200px-SR-item-Amber.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Amber.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:SR-item-Amber.jpg) Amber [Amber](Lore_Amber.md) is a colorful resin formed from hardened sap. The material is of limited use to the people of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md), but in the [Shivering Isles](Lore_Shivering_Isles.md), amber [smithing](Lore_Smithing.md) is an ancient art. ## [Atronite](Lore_Atronite.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ed/BL-item-Atronite.jpg/200px-BL-item-Atronite.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Atronite.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Atronite.jpg) Atronite **[Atronite](Lore_Atronite.md)** is a mysterious stone from [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md). Shards of Atronite have been used as an exotic form of [currency](Lore_Currency.md#Atronite) on occasion. [Odar's kingdom](Lore_Hero.md#Dynasty_of_Odar_the_Brave) was known to use Atronite to trade for rare goods.

## Flint
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-style_material-Flint.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-style_material-Flint.png) Flint **Flint** is a type of quartz often mined and used by [Argonians](Lore_Argonian.md).<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-26)</sup>

Argonian smiths use a technique called "slug shaping" which uses a specific type of slug called the Jassa Red, which when threatened, squirts out an acidic mucous that can be used for smithing. Argonians place the slugs onto the wood or stone and repeatedly flash a piece of flint directly behind them. Adjusting the position of the flint pushes the slugs in different directions. As the slugs move across the wood or stone, they leave behind a thin layer of acidic slime, creating long, smooth channels in the material.<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-27)</sup> [Jadeflint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Jadeflint) is a material with similar name is used by Argonians as well.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-EGT-28)</sup>

Flint can also be used to start fires.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

## [Seastone](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seastone)
[Seastones](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Seastone), or **sea stones** are smooth, ocean-rounded gems often found along the shores of [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and beyond. They are highly prized for their diverse shapes and colors and their role in various [magical](Lore_Magic.md) and [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) practices, as well as their decorative qualities.

The [Maormer](Lore_Maormer.md), a seafaring people with a deep affinity for the magic, particularly cherish seastones. These stones, smoothed by the relentless waves of the sea, are sought after for their potential use in sea-related rituals. Among the Maormer, hues of blues and purples hold special value, though they remain exceptionally rare. Some Maormer are known to adorn themselves with earrings featuring small, pale blue stones that emit an ethereal inner light. The colors of seastones are not merely for aesthetic appreciation; they hold inherent magical significance. Seastones are not uniform in their magical and alchemical properties. While a smooth yellow-cast stone may be visually appealing, it often lacks substantial arcane value. On the other hand, blue and purple seastones are highly prized for their profound arcane resonance. Red and green stones fall somewhere in between on the arcane spectrum. It was theorized that the geographical origin and the duration of oceanic exposure contribute to the unique characteristics of each seastone. To harness the potential of seastones, individuals must engage in thorough testing and examination. Identifying the arcane properties of a seastone and understanding how it may be employed in spells or alchemical preparations requires careful scrutiny. Whether it's the specific hue or the duration spent in the sea, each factor influences the seastone's potential applications.

[Aquamarine](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Aquamarine) and [larimar](Lore_Larimar.md) have both been referred to as seastone and sea-stone, respectively.

**Found in:** - [Lore](Lore_Sea_Lore_for_Arcane_Crafting.md)

## [Sigil Geode](Lore_Sigil_Geode.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/fb/ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura_02.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Eyes_of_Azura_02.jpg) A Sigil Geode [Sigil Geodes](Lore_Sigil_Geode.md) are crystalline stones which can be used to trap the [souls](Lore_Souls.md) of sentient beings for consultation and eventual release. Although similar to [black soul gems](Lore_Black_Soul_Gem.md), Sigil Geodes cannot be used for [enchanting](Lore_Enchanting.md). If attempted, the soul will instead be safely released from the Geode and permitted to pass on to [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md). Sigil Geodes are therefore viewed by some as a more ethical form of [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md), as the soul trapped within can be drawn into an [ectoplasmic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ectoplasm) shell through applied [conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md) and consulted at leisure. Although Sigil Geodes lose their charge within a few days and are therefore of no use for enchanting, souls suspended within can continue to be transferred between Geodes. Sigil Geodes are particularly useful for capturing the [vestige](Lore_Vestige_(soul).md) of a [Lesser Daedra](Lore_Lesser_Daedra.md); they become mesmerized by the stone's faceted glow, and are drawn within the Geode when it is pointed at them.

## [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md)
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3b/BL-item-Stalhrim.jpg/200px-BL-item-Stalhrim.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Stalhrim.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:BL-item-Stalhrim.jpg) Stalhrim [Stalhrim](Lore_Stalhrim.md) is a rare form of [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) ice found only on the island on [Solstheim](Lore_Solstheim.md). Throughout history it has served various uses. Originally it was used in the burial rituals of ancient [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). The deceased would be encased in the material as a form of protection, a privilege that was typically reserved for Nordic individuals. It has also served as a crafting material and, with the proper tools and techniques, can be used to create weapons, armor, clothing, and even jewelry.

Stalhrim armor (sometimes called ice armor) can be light, medium, or heavy in weight. It is very protective, and is often made with [steel](Lore_Steel.md) or [quicksilver](Lore_Quicksilver.md). Due to its innate properties, frost enchantments placed on items made of stalhrim are more potent. In its raw form, it can be used as an alchemical ingredient that induces paralysis and inflicts frost damage.

Stalhrim is often described as being hard as iron and impossible to break by conventional means. The only known way to mine it is with an ancient Nordic pickaxe, made from superior craftsmanship. It has been described as a rigid, blue-white crystalline metal, faceted yet smooth, and unnaturally cold to touch. Working with stalhrim is much like [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md) smithing, and can only be performed by a skilled blacksmith.

For alchemy information, see the [Alchemy lore entry](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy_R#Raw_Stalhrim).
