Centaurs (sometimes called **Horsefolk**)<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-AAFCS-1)</sup> are a nomadic, intelligent, and spiritual [beast race](Lore_Beastfolk.md) that possesses the head and torso of a tall powerfully-built man and the lower body of a [horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horse).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-2)</sup><sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ADi F-3)</sup> They are legendary creatures who are worshiped by some and despised by others.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DUG-4)</sup> Considered to be ancient and mysterious, centaurs are capable of sophisticated speech, have their own language,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-5)</sup> and refer to others as "mortals",<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Legends-6)</sup> suggesting that they possess a greater lifespan.

In [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md), centaurs were one of the many civilizations inhabiting the province's vast jungles, predating the [elven](Lore_Mer.md) inhabitants arrival.<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE3V-7)</sup> Following the fall of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md), they came to inhabit the abandoned human trading posts,<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-PGE1AD-2)</sup> and families of centaurs attended revelries in [Falinesti](Lore_Falinesti.md) alongside [Bosmer](Lore_Bosmer.md), [Wood Orcs](Lore_Wood_Orc.md), and [Imga](Lore_Imga.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-ADi F-3)</sup> The Elden Hollow beneath [Elden Root](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Elden_Root) was the final resting place for generations of centaurs and [minotaurs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Minotaur).<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONDracien Montue-8)</sup> In [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md), centaurs made their homes in the woods and hills of [Bangkorai](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Bangkorai), having been more commonly found in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TMo SP-9)</sup> By the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), they were still encountered in the [Iliac Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iliac_Bay) regions of High Rock and [Hammerfell](Lore_Hammerfell.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-10)</sup>

Legends of their exploits range from being described as epic to pornographic, perhaps for no better reason than that they have varied personalities. Capable of using [weapons](Lore_Weapons.md) such as spears and bows, there are few who are equal to them in battle.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DUG-4)</sup> Speakers of Centaurian, which was taught by the [Resolution of Z'en](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Resolution_of_Z%27en), can prevent them from attacking.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Daggerfall-10)</sup> According to the [Conclave of the Eleven Forces](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conclave_of_the_Eleven_Forces), they are "true followers of the [Old Ways](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism)", referring to the complex system of ancestor worship that the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) itself espouses.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-DUG-4)</sup> Centaurs are in tune with [Y'ffre](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Y%27ffre)'s will.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-LADCG-11)</sup>

## Notes
- [Spider Daedra](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Spider_Daedra) are sometimes described as being centaur-like, having a humanoid head and torso atop a spider's body.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-DD-12)</sup>
- Centaurs were intended to be found all throughout [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md) before being cut due to time constraints. A concept idea for centaurs in *[Skyrim](Skyrim_Skyrim.md)* was that they would be nomadic groups that live out in the wild and start a mammoth hunt when they come across a mammoth, creating a natural tension between them, the [mammoths](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mammoth), and the [giants](Lore_Giant.md).<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-Burgess-13)</sup>
- In earlier builds of [ESO](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Online), centaur bones were an [alchemical](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alchemy) reagent that could grant the property of weakening.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-BNo CB-14)</sup> The bones were planned to have a larger role in Elden Hollow before ultimately being cut, though the remains are still found in the dungeon.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-ONDracien Montue-8)</sup><sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-CC-15)</sup>
