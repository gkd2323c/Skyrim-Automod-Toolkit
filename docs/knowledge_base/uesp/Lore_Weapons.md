# Weapons


## Weapons

### Atlatl
[Atlatls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Atlatl) (also called **flinging-sticks**) are throwing devices employed to launch spears or [darts](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Throwing_Weapons) with increased force and accuracy, in similar fashion to a [slings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sling) but used for different types of projectiles.

They have applications outside of combat or hunting. Similar to slings, which find use in [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) traditional [sports](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sport), atlatls are also utilized for recreational purposes in Argonian games. In the traditional Murkwater game "The Toad Flies at Dusk," players utilize enameled flinging-sticks that terminate in shallow cups. Based on the name, these specialized flinging-sticks, also known as toad-atlatls, are likely used to launch frogs. Some of them were cherished heirlooms belonging to chieftains of Argonian tribes.

### Flail
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/11/AR-item-Flail_F.png/200px-AR-item-Flail_F.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Flail_F.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:AR-item-Flail_F.png) A flail [Flails](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Flail) are handheld weapons consisting of spiked balls or bars attached to handles by chains, typically used for striking or flailing.

They are utilized in warfare by a variety of cultures and races. This encompasses humans such as the [Nords](Lore_Nord.md). They also find use among the [Mer](Lore_Mer.md), including [Orcs](Lore_Orc.md) and [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md). Even the [Daedra](Lore_Daedra.md) employ them in battle. [Gargoyles](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Gargoyle) are also seen wielding flails adorned with skulls integrated into their design.

Crafted in diverse styles, including Elven, Orcish, and Dwemer designs, flails are fashioned from a range of materials, spanning from common [iron](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Iron) and [steel](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Steel) to luxurious options such as [dwarven metal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwarven_Metal), [ebony](Lore_Ebony.md), [mithril](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mithril), [silver](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Silver), and [adamantium](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Adamantium). Some are even created from [daedric materials](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daedric_Equipment). Similar to other weapons, flails could be [enchanted](Lore_Enchanting.md) with a myriad of magical augmentations.

Flails served purposes beyond combat; they are utilized as instruments of torture and self-inflicted pain. Some werewolves, seeking penance for their monstrous deeds, employed silver-studded flails in ritual self-flagellation. Furthermore, specific combat practices, like the [Altmeri](Lore_Altmer.md) duels governed by a code of dueling, are bound by stringent regulations. Altmeri duelists adhere to strict regulations banning the use of instruments like long swords, axes, flails, and similar weapons, in accordance with the sacred laws of [Trinimac](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trinimac). Interestingly, some [healers](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restoration) consider flails among their preferred weapons in melee combat. Blacksmiths and armories sometimes integrate imagery of the flail, commonly alongside the sword, on signs outside their establishments.

### Garrote
[](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Broken_Garrote.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-icon-quest-Broken_Garrote.png) A garrote [Garrote](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Garrote) is a type a weapon consisting of a piece of wire or cord used for strangling.

Garrotes are among the tools employed by the [Morag Tong](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Morag_Tong) during their assassinations. One of the skilled practitioners of garrotes within the Morag Tong was [Naryu Virian](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Naryu_Virian). The gear utilized by the Morag Tong is tailored specifically to their tasks and responsibilities. Notably, their gloves and gauntlets are designed for delicate work, including garroting, necessitating free finger ends for such tasks.

### Shillelagh
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Vows_and_Oaths_04.jpg) A shillelagh [Shillelaghs](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shillelagh) (also called **knobby sticks**) are tools functioning as walking sticks, dueling weapons, and clubs.

Some [Vateshrans](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vateshrans), a caste of [Reachfolk](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Reachfolk) historians and storytellers, were known to carry shillelaghs. [Barth](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Barth), a particularly famous Vateshran from the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), was among the wielders of shillelagh.

### Sling
[Slings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sling) are projectile weapons consisting of a strap or loop used to hurl stones or other projectiles.

Similarly to various other weapons, slings can be enchanted. *The Codex Arcana*, a series of compendiums compiled by the [Imperial Battlemages](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Battlemages) of [Battlespire](Lore_Battlespire.md), provides insight into the [enchanted](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Enchantment) weapons kept within its confines. Serving as a partial index of arcane contrivances known to scholars and alchemists, it aims to familiarize all students with these artifacts to prevent their misuse or neglect. Within the compendium of enchantments lies the description of three remarkable enchanted slings: the Sling of Delight, endowed with magical healing properties; the Sling of Fickle Endowment, which invokes confusion through the [destructive](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Destruction) forces of [magic](Lore_Magic.md); and the Sling of Riven Stars, inflicting moderate shock damage while granting its wielder insights into the mystical arts of [alteration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alteration).

Slings are also used in [sports](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sport), particularly in the [Orcish](Lore_Orc.md) game of [Vosh Ball](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Vosh_Ball). Although the rules of the game evolved over time, the fundamental setup remains consistent. Two teams, each comprising six players armed with slings containing three vosh balls, vie for dominance in the enclosed court. Their objective is to incapacitate their opponents by hurling the heavy spheres with maximum force.

### Staff
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/33/LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png/200px-LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Staff_of_Sparks.png) A staff [Staves](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Staff) (or **staffs**) are implements and weapons frequently wielded by practitioners of magic. Staves may be imbued with individual enchantments and used regardless of the wielder's magical abilities. They may also serve as focal points for casting spells to assist traditional spellcasting, acting as a focus of power rather than its source. Various types of staves can be crafted and enchanted to accommodate different forms of magic. Staves designed for casting spells exist across all schools of magic, including [restoration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Restoration), [destruction](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Destruction), [illusion](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Illusion), [conjuration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Conjuration), [alteration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alteration), [thaumaturgy](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Thaumaturgy), or [mysticism](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mysticism).<sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_nb1)</sup> Additionally, they represent a mage's status and hold considerable symbolic significance, evoking the qualities of leadership and authority.

In addition to staves, other tools serve similar purposes, such as smaller **wands**, which can also function as focal points for spells, or be enchanted themselves. **Rods** are utilized for divination, although they can also serve as tools for training in spellcasting. They also have more specific uses. Similar to staves and wands, rods can also bear singular enchantments. Some rods are designed to control fire and serve as ceremonial tools. Additionally, some are used to channel magic in similar manner to staves. The tools utilized by the [Dwemer](Lore_Dwemer.md) to command their [animunculi](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dwemer_Animunculi) are often referred to as rods.

Wands and rods are categorized as distinct implements from staves, forming part of the training equipment for [Shad Astula](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Shad_Astula) adepts. Other types of items are also utilized as aids for channeling magic, such as [gauntlets](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Psijic_Gauntlets) for members of the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md), or tomes commonly used by [Arcanists](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Arcanists).

Staves are also employed in close-quarters combat. This is often the case when spellcasters find themselves in need to fight at short range. In such scenarios, they function as blunt weapons. On occasion, they are employed for defensive purposes. Some staves possess features enabling them to function to some degree as melee weapons. Depending on their design, staves provide a diverse range of uses, doubling as half-pikes, spears, harpoons, pole-axes, or tridents when the situation demands.<sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_nb2)</sup> A staff can also double as a barrier when on sentry duty, or a pry bar. Some staves also serve as crosiers, often having ceremonial functions in the societies where they are used. Some staves serve as symbols of office, like the Royal Staff of Anequina, which is used by the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) monarchs of [Anequina](Lore_Anequina.md), or [Fahara'jad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Fahara%27jad)'s Rod, a royal Redguard staff. Replicas of Fahara'jad's Rod are often given as gifts by [Forebear](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Forebear) nobles to their children.

### Throwing Weapons
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e7/MW-item-Throwing_Stars.jpg/200px-MW-item-Throwing_Stars.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Throwing_Stars.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-item-Throwing_Stars.jpg) Throwing Stars [Throwing Weapons](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Throwing_Weapons) such as **throwing stars**, **throwing knives**, **darts** and others are frequently used across [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md) and beyond.

### Trident
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f3/ON-item-weapon-Drowned_Mariner_Staff.jpg/200px-ON-item-weapon-Drowned_Mariner_Staff.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-weapon-Drowned_Mariner_Staff.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-weapon-Drowned_Mariner_Staff.jpg) Trident [Tridents](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trident) are a type of polearm that take the form of three-pronged weapons.

The [Trident-Kings](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trident-Kings) are an obscure faction believed to be the leaders of the [Dreugh](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Dreugh) people. The [Khajiit](Lore_Khajiit.md) use claw shapes in ritual tridents as an extension of their own slashing, clawed hands. [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) Queensguard are said to wield shiny tridents while patrolling the streets of [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md). The [lizard bulls](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Lizard_Bull) said to be found in the islands of [Pyandonea](Lore_Pyandonea.md) wield glittering tridents, similar to the Queensguard.

The True-Sworn mercenaries used staves with heads shaped nearly like tridents, a design choice influenced by pragmatic considerations aligned with their tasks. The [Gray Host](Lore_Gray_Host.md) used maces that were shaped like tridents. [Systrean](Lore_Systres.md) marines described their trident staves as the "masts of magic" and claimed that channeling magic down the tines of a trident felt different to traditional staves.
