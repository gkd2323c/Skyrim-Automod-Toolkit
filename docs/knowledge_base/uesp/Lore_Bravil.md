[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b4/OB-place-Bravil.jpg/200px-OB-place-Bravil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bravil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Bravil.jpg) Bravil circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/76/LO-misc-Nibenese.jpg/225px-LO-misc-Nibenese.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Nibenese.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LO-misc-Nibenese.jpg) An inhabitant of Nibenay Bravil is a [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) city located on the banks of the [Niben Bay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_Bay), south of the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md). It is found in the [Nibenay Valley](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenay_Valley), which was historically said to be a tropical region enclosed by an equatorial rain forest and broken up by rivers.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> The [Larsius River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Larsius_River) passes through and wraps around the town, as the [Green Road](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Green_Road) curves around it. A remarkably large [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) settlement is found underneath the city.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ACOV-2)</sup> The symbol of the city is the wild stag.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-GF-3)</sup>

Under [Marukhati](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) rule, cities of the Nibenay Valley were forced to become mercantile magocracies, allowing them to be wealthiest city-states in the region throughout the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup> In the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), Bravil was built in a unique Nibenese architectural style.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESO-4)</sup> By the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), Bravil was a squalid town described as one of the poorest in [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md)<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-GTB-5)</sup> with houses built like shacks, being wooden and stacked upon each other. The Larsius River that runs through the town serves as Bravil's sewers. Because of its location on the bay's shore and the marshes and swamps that surround it, the climate is damp.

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/8/8a/MW-item-Light_Cuirasses_%28cropped%29.jpg)](File_MW-item-Light_Cuirasses_(cropped).jpg.md) [](File_MW-item-Light_Cuirasses_(cropped).jpg.md) Imperial [River-Newt](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Newt) Scale armor, used by the Nibenay River Guard It is known that [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleids.md) once lived in the region before Bravil was established. The *[Daughter of the Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daughter_of_the_Niben)* claims what the Ayleids called the region around Bravil translates to "home", which is *"Math"* (or sometimes *"Hame"*) in [Ayleidoon](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Ayleidoon).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DOTN-6)</sup> The Niben provided food and transportation, and their settlement flourished as a result of increased trade after the establishment of the great [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md), being even more populous than Bravil is today.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ACOV-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DOTN-6)</sup> The Alessian Army, led by the [Empress Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md)'s centurion [Teo Bravillius Tasus](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Teo_Bravillius_Tasus), attacked the Ayleids in the [First Era](Lore_First_Era.md).<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DOTN-6)</sup> After discovering the Ayleid's ability to use [Alteration](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alteration) type magic to defend themselves, Tasus managed to defeat them. Because of his success, the town owes its name to Tasus.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DOTN-6)</sup> It is unclear when exactly the modern city itself was founded. At some point after [1E 700](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_700),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-PGE1H-7)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TBo RM-8)</sup> the mortal [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md) precursor to the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md) lived in a predecessor town at the future site of the city, but it is unknown if it was called Bravil at this time.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-TNMT-9)</sup> The main city itself has been in existence since at least [1E 1152](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_1152).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-IG-10)</sup>

Restrictions imposed by the [Alessian Order](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Alessian_Order) against certain kinds of meat-eating and animal cults soon made agriculture and husbandry nearly impossible, and as a result, many of the Eastern Imperials were forced to become merchants, soon allowing the Nibenay Valley to become the wealthiest city-state in the region. Despite this, the rich and powerful merchant class were still forced to pay tithes to the Alessian priesthood, reminding them of their status of tenanted citizens. The [War of Righteousness](Lore_War_of_Righteousness.md) ended Alessian rule in [1E 2321](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_2321), and control of the Nibenay Valley reverted to a mercantile-magocracy, which the western Colovians still thought far too arcane to consider a reunification of Cyrodiil.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-PGE1-1)</sup>

In [2E 581](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_581), [Flaccus Terentius](Lore_Flaccus_Terentius.md) dreamed that an army of Daedra and a legion of the undead under [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) would raze Bravil and destroy all of Cyrodiil. In this dream, Bravil would be defended by the [Fourth Legion](Lore_Imperial_Legion.md#Known_Legions). It is unknown if these events actually occurred.<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-EGTCyrodiil-11)</sup> When the [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) fell in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md), Captain Jena Apinia had her legion flee the city and go to Bravil, asking the mayor for help. He refused, and the citizens rioted against Captain Apinia, resulting in her slaughtering many of them and looting the Castle, earning her the title the Butcher of Bravil.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-TBo B-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-RD-13)</sup>

County Bravil became independent from the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md) following the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) of [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433). Bravil was often at odds with [Leyawiin](Lore_Leyawiin.md), which had also gained independence, until the two counties were subdued by [Emperor Titus Mede](Lore_Titus_Mede.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-TIC-14)</sup> At the onset of the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), [Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) invasion forces coming out of Elsweyr flanked Imperial defenses along the Valenwood border, allowing them to soon take Leyawiin and lay siege to Bravil. The city fell the next year. At the climax of the war, Dominion forces in Bravil sought to counterattack the largely Nordic forces under General Jonna, but these efforts failed, leading to the encirclement of the Imperial City and the destruction of the Dominion's main army in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-GWB-15)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/3/3a/LG-quest-Brotherhood%27s_Justice.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Brotherhood%27s_Justice.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Brotherhood%27s_Justice.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Brotherhood%27s_Justice.jpg) Overlooking the city of Bravil By the 27th of Hearthfire, [4E 188](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_188), the city had erupted into violence due to a war of control being waged by Cyrodiil's two largest [skooma](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Skooma) traffickers. The Listener of the Dark Brotherhood, [Alisanne Dupre](Lore_Alisanne_Dupre.md), was forced to employ sellswords to protect her private residence in the city. By the 1st of Sun's Dusk, things in Bravil had come to a head: the statue of the Lucky Old Lady had been destroyed, and Alisanne Dupre had abandoned her residence to defend the Night Mother's crypt. Rasha, the apparent leader of the [Cheydinhal](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Cheydinhal) sanctuary, sent Garnag and Andronica to Bravil to aid her. After Garnag and Andronica left and the Cheydinhal Sanctuary didn't receive any communication from the city for nineteen days, the remaining members feared the worst. Those fears were realized on the morning of the 21st, when Garnag returned alone with the stone coffin of the Night Mother. Garnag told that the crypt of the Night Mother was raided, Andronica cut to pieces, and Alisanne Dupre was burned alive in a storm of mage fire. Garnag managed to fend off the attackers and transport the coffin safely out of the city, though he got gravely injured and was likely to lose his right eye.<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CJ2-16)</sup>

Around the 18th of Morning Star, [4E 201](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_201), Daynas Valen became wanted in the city when he killed a collector in possession of the Ivory Claw. He fled for three days of nonstop riding to escape his pursuers, taking a ship in [Anvil](Lore_Anvil.md) to [Solitude](Lore_Solitude.md).<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-DVJ-17)</sup>

## The Lucky Old Lady
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7c/OB-quest-Whispers_of_Death.jpg/200px-OB-quest-Whispers_of_Death.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Whispers_of_Death.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-quest-Whispers_of_Death.jpg) The Lucky Old Lady [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/7f/LG-quest-Chaos_in_Bravil.jpg/200px-LG-quest-Chaos_in_Bravil.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Chaos_in_Bravil.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-quest-Chaos_in_Bravil.jpg) The Destruction of the Lucky Old Lady The Lucky Old Lady was a statue which once stood in a square east of Bravil's Great Chapel of Mara.

No one knows when the statue to her was erected, or who the artist was, but it has stood there for thousands of years, since the first era. A story printed in the book *[Daughter of the Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Daughter_of_the_Niben)* relayed the story of a woman born into poverty, who became famous for her kindness and charity. When she was a child, a priest soothed her woes of not knowing the identity of her father by telling her she was the daughter of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md), "the God of Mercy, Charity and Well-Earned Luck". After this encounter she took to referring to herself as a "child of Luck" when asked about her parentage. For showing kindness to an incognito prince who was stripped of his senses after being kidnapped and cursed by witches, she was rewarded with countless riches after he recovered from his ordeal. She shared her great wealth with the rest of Bravil, and lived to an old age. Before the statue was destroyed in [4E 188](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_188)<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-CJ2-16)</sup>, people would visit the statue to ask the Lucky Old Lady for her blessing of luck.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-DOTN-6)</sup>

In actuality, the statue's true purpose was to act as a conduit between the [Night Mother](Lore_Night_Mother.md), whose crypt laid beneath the statue, and the Listener of the [Dark Brotherhood](Lore_Dark_Brotherhood.md). <sup>[[19]](#cite_note-COTN-19)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-Arquen-20)</sup> After the destruction of the statue, the Night Mother's remains were removed and ultimately sent to a Dark Brotherhood chapter in [Skyrim](Lore_Skyrim.md).<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-FOTDB-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-NMS-22)</sup>

## Notes
- The "Bravil Bruisers" are a gladitorial team that fought in the Dragon Games.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-23)</sup>
- Because the [Imperial Palace](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:The_Imperial_Palace) in *[Arena](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Arena)* serves as the final dungeon in the game, Palace quests in the [Imperial Province](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Arena:Imperial_Province) are inaccessible. However, fourteen random quest dungeon icons are present through a toggle but cannot be accessed. One of these dungeons happens to be south of the Larsius River and west of the Panther River, right where Bravil is located.<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-ARMap-24)</sup>
- There are a number of culinary dishes and drinks that are from Bravil, with known dishes being [Bravil's best beet risotto](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Bravil%27s_Best_Beet_Risotto) and [Bravil melon salad](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Bravil_Melon_Salad). There are also two known drinks, [Bravil mead](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Bravil_Mead) and [Bravil bitter barley beer](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Bravil_Bitter_Barley_Beer).
