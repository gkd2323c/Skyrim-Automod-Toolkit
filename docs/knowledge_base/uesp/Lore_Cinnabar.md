*You must seek the answer elsewhere, for that is where scholarship ends and theology begins.*
—Lady Cinnabar of Taneth<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAX-1)</sup> **Lady** Cinnabar of Taneth was an influential [Redguard](Lore_Redguard.md) scholar, prolific writer and a mage active during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md) of the [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md),<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LC-2)</sup> known for her numerous specialties and her rivalry with [Phrastus of Elinhir](Lore_Phrastus_of_Elinhir.md). They were known to often reference each other in their works and question the academic credibility of one another,<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCAS-3)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-THOC-4)</sup><sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OAS-5)</sup> with Phrastus personally referring to her as the **Shrew of Taneth**.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-POE-6)</sup>

## History
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/b7/ON-npc-Phrastus_of_Elinhir_02.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Phrastus_of_Elinhir_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Phrastus_of_Elinhir_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Phrastus_of_Elinhir_02.jpg) Cinnabar and Phrastus Little is known about Lady Cinnabar's personal life. She hailed from [Taneth](Lore_Taneth.md) and resided in the Tower of the Fifth Doctrine. Although she used the noble title of "Lady," she referred to herself as a humble scholar.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCAS-3)</sup>

She authored a number of texts devoted to various fields of study, including [Mundus Stones](Lore_Mundus_Stones.md),<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-Mundus-7)</sup> [aetherial fragments](Lore_Aetherial_Fragments.md),<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-AF-8)</sup> common cultural practices associated with [Daedric](Lore_Daedra.md) worship, styles of weaponry and armor used by the [Ra Gada](Lore_Ra_Gada.md),<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-Ra_Gada-9)</sup> and ancient [Meridian](Lore_Meridia.md) cults.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-Meridia-10)</sup> Her works also covered cultural texts focused on Prince [Hubalajad](Lore_Hubalajad.md) and his role in Redguard history,<sup>[[11]](#cite_note-IDH-11)</sup> an examination of [Empress](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md), the study of various branches of the [Glenmoril Wyrd](Lore_Glenmoril_Wyrd.md),<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GW-12)</sup> the [Breton](Lore_Breton.md) [knightly](Lore_Knights.md) [orders](Lore_Breton_Knightly_Orders.md) and their role in culture of the [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md),<sup>[[13]](#cite_note-Knights-13)</sup> the similarities between [Xarxes](Lore_Xarxes.md), [Tu'whacca](Lore_Tu%27whacca.md), and [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) as psychopomps,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-TAX-1)</sup> and a study of the [Wardens](Lore_Wardens.md) and their magical abilities.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-Wardens-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-EMWATW-15)</sup> She was also the author of *[Subtropical Cyrodiil: A Speculation](Lore_Subtropical_Cyrodiil__A_Speculation.md)*, in which she discussed the climate of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md) and its relationship to the [White-Gold Tower](Lore_White-Gold_Tower.md).<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-SCAS-3)</sup>

She also provided correspondence interviews where she explained topics pertaining to the [Et'Ada](Lore_Et%27Ada.md), the original spirits, and the [Celestials](Lore_Celestial.md).<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-LCAYQ-16)</sup> She also partook in shared interview alongside with Phrastus, discussing the [Fang Lair](Lore_Fang_Lair.md) and Zaan the Scalecaller.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-AMOVAB-17)</sup>

Lady Cinnabar's rivalry with Phrastus of Elinhir was well known and thoroughly documented. Their publications frequently sought to debunk or even slander one another. There are several notable examples of their academic feud found even outside of their publications.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-POE-6)</sup><sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Inter Shick-18)</sup><sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Inter Shick2-19)</sup> For instance, Phrastus' speculation that ogres' gray-blue skin camouflages them against the sky was convincingly debunked by Lady Cinnabar.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OAS-5)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-SHC-20)</sup> The definition of the [Atmoran](Lore_Atmoran.md) term "Wittestadr" has also been a subject of debate between the two. While Phrastus translated it as "Whit's Daughter," Lady Cinnabar argued it held a darker meaning: "Wit Shatter."<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-W-21)</sup> Furthermore, Phrastus claimed that "Zalgaz" was [Old Nedic](Lore_Old_Nedic.md) for "lizard lady," but Lady Cinnabar dismissed this as "a reeking load of guarwash."<sup>[[22]](#cite_note-ZD-22)</sup>

Cinnabar was also referenced by other scholars, such as [Firenlle](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Scholar_Firenlle) of the [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) in her *[On Welkynd Stones](Lore_On_Welkynd_Stones.md)*,<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-OWS-23)</sup> and by [Cirantille](Lore_Books_by_Author.md#Cirantille) in her biased *[Ogres: A Summary](Lore_Ogres__A_Summary.md)*, in a positive and neutral light.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-OAS-5)</sup> She was also mentioned in numerous works by her rival, Phrastus of Elinhir, including *[Daedra Worship: The Ayleids](Lore_Daedra_Worship__The_Ayleids.md)*,<sup>[[24]](#cite_note-DWTA-24)</sup> *[Daedra Worship: The Chimer](Lore_Daedra_Worship__The_Chimer.md)*,<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-DWTC-25)</sup> *[Field Guide to Spriggans](Lore_Field_Guide_to_Spriggans.md)*,<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-FGTS-26)</sup> *[Introduction to Aedric Studies](Lore_Introduction_to_Aedric_Studies.md)*,<sup>[[27]](#cite_note-ITAS-27)</sup> *[The Heartland of Cyrodiil](Lore_The_Heartland_of_Cyrodiil.md)*,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-THOC-4)</sup> and *[Unhallowed Legions](Lore_Unhallowed_Legions.md)*.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-UL-28)</sup> Lady Clarisse Laurent claimed that Cinnabar of Taneth would have an opinions regarding the topic of [Akaviri](Lore_Akavir.md) burial masks.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-LCLAYQ-29)</sup>

One of her more personal correspondences, dated the 12th of Morning Star, was addressed to Mistress Veroine Gimbert of *The Mystic's Mirage* in [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md). In the letter, Lady Cinnabar acknowledges the receipt of a shipment of books, which included titles such as *[The Five Points of the Star](Lore_The_Five_Points_of_the_Star.md)*, *[Twin Secrets](Lore_Twin_Secrets.md)*, *[Sacred Rites of the Stonechewers](Lore_Sacred_Rites_of_the_Stonechewers.md)*, and *[Relics of Saint Veloth](Lore_Relics_of_Saint_Veloth.md)*. However, she politely pointed out an error in the shipment: a mix-up in the fifth book, which should have been *[Boethiah and Her Avatars](Lore_Boethiah_and_Her_Avatars.md)*, instead of *[Cheeses of Tamriel](Lore_Cheeses_of_Tamriel.md)*.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-Receipt-30)</sup>

In [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Lady Cinnabar was among the many civilians in the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) forced to flee from the [Planemeld Obverse](Lore_Planemeld_Obverse.md), seeking refuge in the sewer systems beneath the city. During this time, she shared shelter with her rival, Phrastus of Elinhir.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-LC-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-POE-6)</sup>
