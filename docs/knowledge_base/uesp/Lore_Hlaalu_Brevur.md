[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e5/MW-place-Lighthouse.jpg/200px-MW-place-Lighthouse.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Lighthouse.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-place-Lighthouse.jpg) Grand Pharos Lighthouse of Seyda Neen [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/42/MW-concept-Hlaalu_Forehead_Gem.png/200px-MW-concept-Hlaalu_Forehead_Gem.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Hlaalu_Forehead_Gem.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:MW-concept-Hlaalu_Forehead_Gem.png) It became Hlaalu tradition to imbed gems from flagships into one's forehead following the wreck of Seyda Neen Hlaalu Brevur (also known as **Brevur the Betrayer**)<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-DGP-1)</sup> was a historical figure of some significance to [House Hlaalu](Lore_House_Hlaalu.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POM-2)</sup> He is the ancestor of [Hlaalu Athyn Llethan](Lore_Athyn_Llethan.md), the King of [Morrowind](Lore_Morrowind.md) in the late [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md)<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-KRP-3)</sup> and Paulus Hlaalu of the Seventh Family, Sub-Vizier of the Theoretical Dignity Club in [Narsis](Lore_Narsis.md).<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POM-2)</sup>

Brevur was directly affiliated with the origins of the [Seyda Neen](Lore_Seyda_Neen.md#Origins),<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-POM-2)</sup> a Hlaalu flagship sent at the behest of a [saint](Lore_Temple_Saints.md) to "see the face of [Veloth](Lore_Veloth.md)". After an unnatural storm destroyed its fleet, its pieces were used to built the Grand Pharos Lighthouse on the [Bitter Coast](Lore_Bitter_Coast.md) of [Vvardenfell](Lore_Vvardenfell.md).<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-MKP-4)</sup>
