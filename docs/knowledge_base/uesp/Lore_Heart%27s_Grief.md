[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2b/ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief.jpg/200px-ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-place-Heart%27s_Grief.jpg) Heart's Grief Heart's Grief is [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md)'s palace in his plane of [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), and serves both as a fortress and a temple to himself. No place is more feared in all of Coldharbour.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> Chock full of Daedric troops and monsters of all sorts, the Prince himself resides behind its walls.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOHG-2)</sup> Its smells vary drastically, ranging from the scent of rancid death to sweet flowered perfume.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-1E2920-3)</sup>

The blackened halls of the palace are half-submerged in brackish [azure plasm](Lore_Azure_Plasm.md), reminiscent of water.<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-1E2920-3)</sup> Within the palace vestibule lies a number of daises and altars, where Molag Bal strings up those who have personally displeased him for all to see.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOHG-2)</sup> It is here that those who evoke his ire are tortured for his entertainment.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-ESOAT-4)</sup> A statue to himself sits in the entryway, surrounded by burning corpses and cold fire braziers.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOHG-2)</sup> In the back of the hall lies the Path of Woe, a corridor full of mechanisms forged of cold ebon-iron that leads to his courtyard and seat of power, the Seat of Tyranny.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOHG-2)</sup>

The Path ends at the Shattered Temple, a structure that looks to be a temple reminiscent of those devoted to the [Divines](Lore_Divines.md) on [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOHG-2)</sup> Of course, this section is dilapidated, seemingly a mocking parody of the mortal worship of the Aedra.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOSai-5)</sup> The Blighted Causeway and the Broken Court follow, leading into the Garden of Ruin, where Molag Bal was famously defeated by the [Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md) in [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-ESOHG-2)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-6)</sup>
