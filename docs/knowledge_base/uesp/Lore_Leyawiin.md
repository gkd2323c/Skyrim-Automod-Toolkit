[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cc/OB-place-Leyawiin.jpg/200px-OB-place-Leyawiin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Leyawiin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-place-Leyawiin.jpg) Leyawiin circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433) [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/a/a7/OB-banner-Leyawiin.jpg/115px-OB-banner-Leyawiin.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Leyawiin.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OB-banner-Leyawiin.jpg) Leyawiin's banner, displaying the city crest Leyawiin is a [Nibenese](Lore_Nibenay.md) city in the extreme southeast of the province, by the mouth of the [Niben River](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Niben_River) where it opens into [Topal Bay](Lore_Topal_Bay.md). Known as the city that spans the waters,<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-1)</sup> the city straddles a swampy estuary of the Niben on the small strip of land between [Elsweyr](Lore_Elsweyr.md) and [Black Marsh](Lore_Black_Marsh.md) called the [Trans-Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trans-Niben). Leyawiin is roughly divided into four districts: Chapel District, Guild Plaza, Main Street and [Castle Leyawiin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Castle_Leyawiin).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-2)</sup>

The ancient symbol of Leyawiin is a white [horse](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Horse) on a field of green, known variously as the Ivory Horse<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-3)</sup> or the White Stallion.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-4)</sup> The origin of the city's Ivory Horse symbol is unknown. One tradition believes it to be associated with the ancient hero [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md). Another states that in the days of myth, Leyawiin was home to a shining white horse of divine strength and beauty who protected the river's banks.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGTL-5)</sup>

[Topal the Pilot](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Topal) is the [patron saint](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Imperial_Saints) of the city, and a statue of him can be found by the southwest wall.<sup>[[6]](#cite_note-Opsius-6)</sup><sup>[[7]](#cite_note-OBPrima-7)</sup><sup>:363</sup>

## History

### First Era
After the death of [Pelinal Whitestrake](Lore_Pelinal_Whitestrake.md) at the conclusion of the [Alessian Slave Rebellion](Lore_Alessian_Slave_Rebellion.md) in [1E 243](Lore_First_Era.md#1E_243), a messenger carried the [Mace of the Crusader](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Mace_of_the_Crusader) to the [human](Lore_Man.md) settlement of Leyawiin. Centuries later, the master craftsman Saint [Kaladas](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Kaladas) built the Great Chapel of [Zenithar](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Zenithar) in Leyawiin as a tribute to the legend.<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-8)</sup> Only a couple centuries later, Leyawiin was captured by the [Khajiiti](Lore_Khajiit.md) wander-kings of [Bruk'ra](Lore_Bruk%27ra.md) amidst the [Anequine Conquests](Lore_Anequine_Conquests.md) of [Darloc Brae](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Darloc_Brae). Scholars theorize that at this point, the invaders converted the Great Chapel of Zenithar to a [Temple of Two-Moons Dance](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Temple_of_Two-Moons_Dance).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-ONGlass Anti-9)</sup> House Caro was known to rule over Leyawiin ever since the early years of the [Second Empire](Lore_Second_Empire.md).<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGTL-5)</sup>

### Second Era
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/23/ON-load-Blackwood.png/200px-ON-load-Blackwood.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Blackwood.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-load-Blackwood.png) Leyawiin in the Second Era The first Imperial bear cavalry came into use by the 10th Legion in [2E 23](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_23), walking through Leyawiin's streets each week in a bear parade.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-APBear Cub-10)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-APBear-11)</sup>

In [2E 299](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_299), Potentate Versidue-Shaie ordered the removal of Leyawiin's bridges so that large sailing vessels could pass from Topal Bay to the River Niben. However, Leyawiin's Chamber of Legates, fearing that the additional merchant traffic would simply sail through Leyawiin without stopping, managed to stall the Imperial project until it was abandoned. To this day vessels too large to pass beneath the bridges must dock south of the city and transship their cargoes to smaller riverboats.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGTL-5)</sup>

As of the year 2E 569, the Chamber of Legates held a relatively minor role within Leyawiin's governance,<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-TGTL-5)</sup> but by [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), they had risen to become a significant political force both in the city and the surrounding region. Despite Countess Caro holding the official title of ruler over Leyawiin, the practical governance of the city was primarily in the hands of the Chamber of Legates.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-CRL-12)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-LTK-13)</sup><sup>[[14]](#cite_note-OTCOL-14)</sup>

In the mid- [Second Era](Lore_Second_Era.md), during the war between the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md) and the forces of [Varen Aquilarios](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Varen_Aquilarios), Leyawiin was allegedly single-handedly freed by [Sai Sahan](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Sai_Sahan), who fought both Emperor [Leovic](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Leovic)'s forces and his own men who had been employed by Leovic as double agents.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cot FC5-15)</sup> There are many different views of this event,<sup>[[16]](#cite_note-MM-16)</sup> and according to some other sources he was aided by allies, however.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-SL-17)</sup>

### Third Era
At the end of the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md), the [Trans-Niben](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Trans-Niben) was annexed back into Cyrodiil and County Leyawiin<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-18)</sup> due to an agreement between the [Mane](Lore_Mane.md) and the [Empire](Lore_Third_Empire.md). Following this, a group of Khajiiti bandits and freedom fighters known as the [Renrijra Krin](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Renrijra_Krin) sought to reclaim all land along the Trans-Niben which they believed to rightfully belong to Elsweyr. They disagreed with the Mane's decision to cede the Trans-Niben and actively blamed Count [Marius Caro](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Marius_Caro) for driving Khajiit from the county's fertile land. The city itself boasted a considerable Khajiit and [Argonian](Lore_Argonian.md) population at the time. Racial tension proved to be a problem between the Khajiit and the influx of Argonians and [Dunmer](Lore_Dunmer.md), and Count Caro attempted to encourage [Nibenean](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Lore:Nibenese) culture in the city in order to move towards mainstream Imperial society. The city was besieged by the forces of [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md) during the [Oblivion Crisis](Lore_Oblivion_Crisis.md) in [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-AFB-19)</sup>

### Fourth Era
Leyawiin briefly became an independent city-state early in the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md). During this time, the county of [Bravil](Lore_Bravil.md), which had rebelled against the Empire, warred with County Leyawiin until the rise of the [Mede Dynasty](Lore_Mede_Dynasty.md) by [4E 48](Lore_Fourth_Era.md#4E_48).<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-The Infernal City-20)</sup> During the [Great War](Lore_Great_War.md), [Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) forces marched out of hidden camps in Northern Elsweyr, marched into Cyrodiil and flanked the Imperial defenses along the [Valenwood](Lore_Valenwood.md) border. The southern coastline of Cyrodiil was then overwhelmed by the invasion forces, and Leyawiin was one of the first cities to fall to the Aldmeri Dominion's invasion in Cyrodiil.<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-GWB-21)</sup>
