*The name Arkay will be struck from the records. Only I shall hold sway over life and death. Only one name shall be whispered in fear. The name of your lord and master.*
—Mannimarco<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATE-1)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_KW)</sup> Mannimarco is the **God of Worms**, the founder and object of worship of the [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md).<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NM-2)</sup> Before his apotheosis he was an [Altmer](Lore_Altmer.md) mage<sup>[[3]](#cite_note-TIEGTT-3)</sup> ([Aldmer](Lore_Aldmer.md), by his own account)<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WS-4)</sup> who had studied with the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md). He eventually found his own path after he was exiled for practicing [necromancy](Lore_Necromancy.md), a discipline the Order deemed dangerous and heretical.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOHFU-5)</sup><sup>[[6]](#cite_note-TBAOT-6)</sup> He became the most powerful necromancer that [Nirn](Lore_Nirn.md) has ever seen,<sup>[[7]](#cite_note-ONTPGOS-7)</sup> becoming widely known as the **King of Worms** and head and archnecromancer<sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDC-8)</sup> of his often renamed Worm Cult. While the exact timing of his transformation into a [lich](Lore_Lich.md) is unknown, he is remembered in later records as the "world’s first of the undying liches," a designation of uncertain meaning.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Lich)</sup> Through his political brilliance, his revival of necromancy from obscurity,<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> and his ascension into godhood, he has left a nearly unparalleled impact on [Tamriel](Lore_Tamriel.md)'s history.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NM-2)</sup>

Mannimarco's early years are unclear. Should his claim to being an Aldmer be true, he may have lived since the [Merethic Era](Lore_Merethic_Era.md).<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WS-4)</sup> He was expelled from Artaeum for practicing necromancy around the year [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230), after which he gathered followers who formed the Order of the Black Worm.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup><sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[11]](#cite_note-GTM-11)</sup> His rivalry with [Vanus Galerion](Lore_Vanus_Galerion.md) defined much of his known early history, especially as Mannimarco spread soul trapping and necromantic teachings throughout Tamriel.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WS-4)</sup><sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GMo ST-12)</sup> The [Mages Guild](Lore_Mages_Guild.md) as well as [Arkay](Lore_Arkay.md) and his faithful were those he would come to consider his greatest enemies in life and undeath.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATE-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TCOA-13)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_KW)</sup> A master manipulator, Mannimarco engaged in politics, gaining and maintaining influence by maneuvering through the courts of rising and falling empires.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> His most significant political achievement was wielding the true power behind the [Empire of Cyrodiil](Lore_Empire_of_Cyrodiil_(Interregnum).md) from the shadows during the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md).<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AL-16)</sup>

During his attempt to attain godhood, Mannimarco suffered repeated failures, including the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), where he served [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md) but was punished by him for attempting to usurp the Prince’s station.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Mann So ST-17)</sup> These setbacks did not deter him, and his persistence finally bore fruit with his ascension during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Jta-18)</sup> Despite his ascension, he remained directly physically active in Tamriel in some capacity until at least 3E 433, the last known death of his physical body.<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Ct K-19)</sup> He ultimately accomplished his main goals: he slew Vanus Galerion, possibly even making a trophy out of his corpse,<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBM-20)</sup> and Mannimarco's divine body, the **[Necromancer's Moon](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md)** (or **the Revenant**), thwarts Arkay by eclipsing his [eponymous planet](Lore_Arkay_(planet).md) every eight days, enabling the creation of [black soul gems](Lore_Soul_Gem.md) for his followers to utilize for ensnaring the souls of the sapient.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NM-2)</sup><sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OBSR-21)</sup><sup>[[22]](#cite_note-OBNM-22)</sup>

## History
Much of Mannimarco's early history is shrouded in mystery. Those who attempted to uncover it only caught fragments amidst rumors and speculation.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> Paranoid and secretive, he kept even [his nephew](Lore_Wormblood.md) a secret from his closest allies; this relative abandoned his name and assumed a moniker to conceal his identity for Mannimarco's cause.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MNESO-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MTC-24)</sup> His origins are also further complicated by various contradictions. Mannimarco himself states that he is not an Altmer but is instead one of the Aldmer,<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WS-4)</sup> who were the original Merethic Era proto-mer.<sup>[[25]](#cite_note-BTAOM-25)</sup> His biography claims he is the "world’s first of the undying liches,"<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Lich)</sup> but if this were to be interpreted as him being the literal first lich, he would have to predate [Dragon Priest](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md)<sup>[[26]](#cite_note-ATD-26)</sup><sup>[[27]](#cite_note-SRValdar-27)</sup> and [Ayleid](Lore_Ayleid.md) liches.<sup>[[28]](#cite_note-28)</sup> Another source is even more extreme and claims that he may even also have been the first necromancer.<sup>[[UOL 1]](#cite_note-29)</sup>

### Expulsion from the Psijic Order
*Mage from infancy, blood-selected for magicka, descended from isles of Artaeum forever! Destined was I from long before birth to exceed all mortals.*
—Excerpted from Worm Saga by Mannimarco Mannimarco joined the [Psijic Order](Lore_Psijic_Order.md) of [Artaeum](Lore_Artaeum.md) in the early Second Era, where he met Vanus Galerion. The two quickly developed a relationship defined by both friendship and rivalry, born of mutual respect for each other’s genius. Vanus was spontaneous and intuitive, wielding magic as naturally as breathing, while Mannimarco possessed a talent for dissecting theories and exploring possibilities beyond the grasp of most mages. Their brilliance attracted followers who elevated them into positions of influence within the Order. The pair frequently debated various topics, captivating any Psijics who stopped to listen, and their debates on the secrets of magic helped establish principles that are still studied today.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-FDESO-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SDESO-31)</sup>

One such topic began as a debate about whether magicka truly exists but soon shifted into a discussion about the morality of magical alteration. Mannimarco argued that magicka does not exist as an external force and is simply the manifestation of a mage’s will. To challenge this view, Vanus transmogrified his robe to a bright green. Mannimarco remained unconvinced and explained that magicka is neither moral nor immoral, requires no consent, and is limited only by the will of the caster. Another debate focused on whether the Consecrations of Arkay apply to [Soul Magic](Lore_Soul_Magic.md), which naturally brought the discussion to the nature of necromancy. Mannimarco repeated his belief that power itself has no intrinsic morality and that it is the mage who chooses to use magic for good or harmful purposes. He argued that necromancy is not inherently immoral, noting that other forms of magic, such as electromancy or cryomancy, can also be used for murder. Vanus disagreed and stated that necromancy is objectionable because it does not seek the consent of the deceased before forcing them into servitude. He added that necromancers often use deceptive methods to obtain consent or simply ignore the issue when circumstances allow.<sup>[[29]](#cite_note-FDESO-30)</sup><sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SDESO-31)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/2/2a/ON-quest-Half-Formed_Understandings.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Half-Formed_Understandings.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Half-Formed_Understandings.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Half-Formed_Understandings.jpg) Mannimarco's first confrontation with Galerion on Artaeum Mannimarco's curiosity with necromancy led him to spend much of his youth experimenting in the Traitor's Vault on Artaeum.<sup>[[31]](#cite_note-ESOEsulo-32)</sup> The necromantic nature of his research began to disturb his friend Vanus Galerion. After discovering an experiment Mannimarco had conducted on their own classmates, Galerion informed the Psijic Ritemaster [Iachesis](Lore_Iachesis.md) of his activities, resulting in Mannimarco's banishment from the Psijic Order and his departure for mainland Tamriel.<sup>[[5]](#cite_note-ESOHFU-5)</sup> The Psijics' failure to suppress Mannimarco effectively, merely exiling him and leaving Tamriel vulnerable to his depredations, led Galerion to split from the Psijic Order and found the Mages Guild in [2E 230](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_230).<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONAL-33)</sup> The island of Artaeum vanished shortly after Galerion's departure; Galerion later speculated that the Psijics may have realized their mistake in releasing Mannimarco and withdrawn from the world.<sup>[[32]](#cite_note-ONAL-33)</sup>

### The Worm Cult Rises
Seeking to normalize necromancy and gain a following to his discipline, Mannimarco created his [Order of the Black Worm](Lore_Order_of_the_Black_Worm.md) to rival the ascendant Mages Guild.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TOTTBW-34)</sup><sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WS-4)</sup> and in the centuries that followed, the two organizations came to dominate the practice of magic.<sup>[[30]](#cite_note-SDESO-31)</sup> At the time, necromancy had largely faded into obscurity and was regarded as a lost art, but Mannimarco’s meddling would soon revive it.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> Mannimarco appealed to those in mourning, promising to help them contact or even reunite with their deceased loved ones. He also advocated against restrictions on spellcasting and offered a refuge for aspiring necromancers, who were often persecuted for seeking such knowledge.<sup>[[4]](#cite_note-WS-4)</sup> Vanus believed Mannimarco was responsible for making the soul-trap spell common knowledge among mages, leading to strictures being introduced in the Mages Guild against the use of soul-trapping on sentient mortals. This resulted in the categorization of souls into the legal white souls of animals and beasts, and the black souls of fellow mortals.<sup>[[12]](#cite_note-GMo ST-12)</sup> In time, it became impossible for standard Soul Trap spells to capture a black soul within a regular soul gem, instead requiring the use of the more dangerous and elusive black soul gems.<sup>[[34]](#cite_note-35)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/1/1b/ON-item-Black_Soul_Gems.jpg/200px-ON-item-Black_Soul_Gems.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Black_Soul_Gems.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-item-Black_Soul_Gems.jpg) Black Soul Gems Sometime after Mannimarco and his underlings' expulsion from Artaeum, one of his followers, [Vastarie](Lore_Vastarie.md), allied with him over the common goal of seeking "to trap souls as one might capture lesser Daedra". They believed their objective was to be found within [Coldharbour](Lore_Coldharbour.md), which Vastarie agreed to venture into herself. She would be the first to bring black soul gems from Coldharbour to Tamriel. They were perfect for their ambitions, as unlike other soul gems, they were "small, capable of containing even the most willful of souls, and apparently indestructible."<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TLV-36)</sup> Vastarie attempted to replicate black soul gems, but Mannimarco's aspirations would come to conflict with her own. Mannimarco desired to use them to gain power, while Vastarie desired to build upon them for ethical reasons. Fearing Mannimarco, Vastarie fled with her husband away from his clutches. Away from the King of Worms, Vastarie developed her own creation, the Sigil Geode, which was intended to be used to communicate with the dead, but Vanus rejected it out of disgust despite her good intentions.<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TLV-36)</sup>

Mannimarco later authored *[A World of Corpses](Lore_A_World_of_Corpses.md)*, a text that conveyed his pride in necromancy and his contempt for practitioners he deemed incompetent. The work indicated that inquiries regarding the acquisition of corpses provoked him to murderous anger, leading him to harvest the souls of those who posed such questions in painful fashion. He regarded these acts as doing "Tamriel a service by winnowing the herd of deficient mages." Tired of repeating himself, he finally set the lesson in writing, turning the book into both a warning and a guide. To him, the matter was obvious: corpses existed everywhere in Tamriel, scattered across its cities and fields by centuries of war and devastation.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AWC-37)</sup>

Mannimarco had already achieved lichdom by the time his rival Vanus Galerion departed the Mages Guild in bitter disillusionment (by [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578)), Vanus decrying it as “nothing more than an intricate morass of political infighting.”<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup><sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ESOEGTS-38)</sup><sup>[[nb 2]](#intnote_Lich)</sup> Mannimarco enhanced his powers by studying necromantic techniques preserved in the writings of the [Dragon Priests](Lore_Dragon_Priest.md) and the [Ayleids](Lore_Ayleid.md), and by delving into the Crimson Book of Skulls.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TOTTBW-34)</sup> His followers gathered for him "cursed artifacts of yore", along with common [alchemical](Lore_Alchemy.md) such as oils, roots, and toadstools, as well as far more profane materials, including blood-tainted herbs, [Akaviri](Lore_Akaviri.md) poisons, dust from saints, and human skin, from which he is said to have extracted their power into his own being.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup> Mannimarco also dabbled in politics during the chaos of the [Interregnum](Lore_Interregnum.md). He consistently positioned himself within the courts of rising and collapsing empires, maintaining political relevance despite periods of diminished favor.<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> Mannimarco consorted with daedra, most notably [Molag Bal](Lore_Molag_Bal.md).<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TOTTBW-34)</sup> Mannimarco became Molag Bal’s lieutenant, with the promise of becoming Nirn’s viceroy if he delivered the world to his master.<sup>[[38]](#cite_note-Go SMB-39)</sup><sup>[[39]](#cite_note-IWSBAM-40)</sup>

### The Planemeld

#### The Soulburst
*In time, the name Molag Bal will be forgotten! In time, all of Tamriel will bow down before me! Soon, the God of Schemes will have a new name: Mannimarco!*
—Mannimarco [![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e0/LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg/200px-LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Soulburst_02.jpg) The Soulburst Mannimarco provided counsel to Duke [Varen Aquilarios](Lore_Varen_Aquilarios.md) of [Chorrol](Lore_Chorrol.md) during the [Colovian Revolt](Lore_Colovian_Revolt.md) of [2E 576](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_576), a rebellion against Emperor [Leovic](Lore_Leovic.md) of the [Longhouse Emperors](Lore_Longhouse_Emperors.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup><sup>[[41]](#cite_note-PQOTGC-42)</sup> After Varen’s victory by right of conquest, he became emperor, and Mannimarco became his chief advisor.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup> By [2E 578](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_578), Mannimarco had become one of the original [Five Companions](Lore_Five_Companions.md), alongside [Lyris Titanborn](Lore_Lyris_Titanborn.md), [Sai Sahan](Lore_Sai_Sahan.md), [Abnur Tharn](Lore_Abnur_Tharn.md), and their leader Varen Aquilarios. Their mission was to locate the [Amulet of Kings](Lore_Amulet_of_Kings.md) so they may utilize it to legitimize Varen’s claim as Emperor of [Cyrodiil](Lore_Cyrodiil.md). Mannimarco convinced Varen that although he was not [Dragonborn](Lore_Dragonborn.md), which was a prerequisite for all true emperors at the time, slight alterations to the coronation ritual would allow them to relight the [Dragonfires](Lore_Dragonfires.md) and convince [Akatosh](Lore_Akatosh.md) to gift Varen with the blood of a dragon.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/5/53/ON-quest-The_Harborage_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Harborage_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Harborage_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Harborage_04.jpg) Mannimarco initiates the Soulburst In secret, however, Mannimarco had corrupted the ritual, inscribing glyphs and incantations designed to weaken the ancient barriers protecting Nirn and tear the veil between worlds, leaving the mortal realm adrift in Oblivion. By performing the ritual, Varen unknowingly broke the divine covenant of Akatosh and [Alessia](Lore_Alessia.md) which protected Nirn from the forces of Oblivion, triggering the catastrophic event known as the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md).<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TCA-43)</sup><sup>[[43]](#cite_note-ESOMQEvents-44)</sup> This set the stage for the [Planemeld](Lore_Planemeld.md), giving Molag Bal the chance to merge Nirn with his realm of Coldharbour and claim it permanently.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup> Consumed by the fires of the Soulburst, Varen Aquilarios vanished and was officially declared dead.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup><sup>[[44]](#cite_note-EFEV-45)</sup>

Prior to the Soulburst, Mannimarco was given refuge in [Markarth](Lore_Markarth.md) by Ard [Caddach](Lore_Caddach.md), during which time he and his Worm Cult operated out of the ruins of [Nchuand-Zel](Lore_Nchuand-Zel.md).<sup>[[10]](#cite_note-EGTBangkorai-10)</sup> Following Emperor Varen's disappearance, Caddach expressed his gratitude to Mannimarco for the latter's role in his downfall, as Varen had usurped the [Imperial throne](Lore_Tamrielic_Emperors.md) from his cousin Leovic. In return, he agreed to provide Mannimarco with land in the Reach for experimentation, granting him a sizeable portion of land in the hills near Markarth, accessible via a Great Lift within Nchuand-Zel. Mannimarco performed a ritual to meld this land with Coldharbour, the domain of his master, Molag Bal. However, the ritual failed; the land was sundered from Tamriel, but was not sent to Coldharbour, instead becoming lost in the Void.<sup>[[45]](#cite_note-ESOArd-46)</sup> It was this sundered land which would become the realm of [Grayhaven](Lore_Grayhaven.md).<sup>[[46]](#cite_note-ESOTEo E-47)</sup>

Following the Soulburst, Mannimarco and his cult began a flurry of political actions to gain authority. These interwoven plots had required years of preparation and, in his own words, "would make even [Mephala](Lore_Mephala.md) blush".<sup>[[47]](#cite_note-Mtc FE-48)</sup> The King of Worms implicated Lyris and Sai Sahan in the emperor's supposed regicide. Lyris was captured by Mannimarco, while Abnur pledged his allegiance to Mannimarco in a bid to preserve the Empire. Sai Sahan fled with the Amulet of Kings, securing the artifact within the depths of [Sancre Tor](Lore_Sancre_Tor.md), where he hid it behind a ward of [Stendarr](Lore_Stendarr.md).<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup> Mannimarco became the true power behind the [Ruby Throne](Lore_Ruby_Throne.md), converting Empress Regent [Clivia Tharn](Lore_Clivia_Tharn.md) to worshipping Molag Bal, and manipulating her into expelling the Mages Guild from the [Imperial City](Lore_Imperial_City.md) so that his Worm Cult could operate freely.<sup>[[14]](#cite_note-ONAbnur Tharn-14)</sup><sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-15)</sup><sup>[[16]](#cite_note-AL-16)</sup> Abnur, Clivia's father, developed a deep hatred for Mannimarco after the Soulburst. Mannimarco’s suspicions of Abnur were further reinforced when Abnur asked whether the Amulet of Kings could theoretically be used to repair the damage caused by the Soulburst.<sup>[[42]](#cite_note-TCA-43)</sup> Soon after, Abnur was brought to the [Castle of the Worm](Lore_Castle_of_the_Worm.md) in Coldharbour, where he was imprisoned.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ONCastle-49)</sup>

Using his political leverage, Mannimarco went to great lengths to ensure necromancy dominated all other forms of magic. By framing the Mages Guild for the Soulburst, Mannimarco was able to install his Worm Cult under the name "Fellowship of Anchorites" in the [Arcane University](Lore_Arcane_University.md) following the Guild’s exile. He then removed several of the strictures formerly imposed by the Mages Guild, with the notable result that necromancy was legalized.<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-15)</sup><sup>[[49]](#cite_note-CATAYQ2-50)</sup> Though Mannimarco was occupied with suppressing dissent,<sup>[[15]](#cite_note-Cot FC4-15)</sup> the outbreak of the [Three Banners War](Lore_Three_Banners_War.md) in [2E 580](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_580) further destabilized Tamriel. The conflict, intensified by the Soulburst and the military aggression of the Empire of Cyrodiil under his puppet Empress Regent Clivia Tharn,<sup>[[50]](#cite_note-TCC-51)</sup> created three powerful and competing factions seeking to overthrow Mannimarco's regime by capturing the Imperial City and installing their own emperor on the Ruby Throne.<sup>[[51]](#cite_note-Carlier-52)</sup><sup>[[52]](#cite_note-barracks-53)</sup> Mannimarco fueled the Imperial war machine by reanimating the bodies of fallen soldiers to wage war against his enemies.<sup>[[UOL 2]](#cite_note-GIESO2012-54)</sup> Concurrently, Mannimarco's desire to employ black soul gems<sup>[[35]](#cite_note-TLV-36)</sup> was showcased on a massive scale. His Worm Cult appeared to possess a virtually unlimited supply,<sup>[[53]](#cite_note-55)</sup> and worked to smuggle black soul gems across the territories of the Three Alliances.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-56)</sup><sup>:26</sup> Black soul gems became increasingly commonplace, to the point that many Tamrielic citizens believed them to be a recent invention.<sup>[[49]](#cite_note-CATAYQ2-50)</sup>

#### The Dark Anchors Fall
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/7/78/LG-cardart-Dark_Anchor.png/200px-LG-cardart-Dark_Anchor.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dark_Anchor.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Dark_Anchor.png) A Dark Anchor (Legends) Though Mannimarco's meddling made it appear that Varen Aquilarios had perished in flames of the Soulburst,<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup> he survived. Varen vanished and later awoke near death and suffering from amnesia at the [Abbey of the Moth Priests](Lore_Cult_of_the_Ancestor_Moth.md). Believed to be a simple wanderer, he was given shelter and soon began studying the [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md). As he read, he recovered memories of his past and visions of Tamriel’s future. Word of these prophecies spread quickly, eventually reaching the Imperial City. When Mannimarco learned that Varen was revealing truths about the Soulburst and the events to come, he had him arrested for rumor-mongering and treason. The King of Worms ordered the abbey burned and delivered Varen into the hands of Molag Bal. After Varen's imprisonment in the Wailing Prison, his predictions became true almost immediately and the first [Dark Anchors](Lore_Dark_Anchors.md) fell.<sup>[[40]](#cite_note-COTFC1-41)</sup>

Mannimarco ordered the Worm Cult to place Dark Anchors throughout Tamriel, intending to utilize them to initiate the Planemeld.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-56)</sup><sup>:22</sup><sup>:38</sup><sup>:42</sup><sup>:43</sup> These devices were created in the [Black Forge](Lore_Black_Forge.md) of Coldharbour and were capable of pulling portions of Nirn into Coldharbour while allowing Daedra to cross into Mundus.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PDA-57)</sup><sup>[[56]](#cite_note-58)</sup><sup>[[57]](#cite_note-ESODark Anchor-59)</sup> With each anchor placed, the barrier between worlds was further weakened.<sup>[[55]](#cite_note-PDA-57)</sup> The Bosmer mage, [Deslandra](Lore_Deslandra.md), wishing to free Tamriel from the tragedies caused by magic and by extension, Daedra, infiltrated the Worm Cult to uncover their plans for the Dark Anchors.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-60)</sup><sup>:19</sup><sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-56)</sup><sup>:22</sup> This led to her epiphany; the moment that Mannimarco would launch his Planemeld, Deslandra hoped to disrupt it by breaking the chains of that which linked all Dark Anchors, resulting in a massive shockwave that would forever sever the connection between Nirn and its sources of Magicka. However, Deslandra's plans to sever the flow of magicka and its influences on Mundus could have unintended consequences like cutting off communication with deities, but she felt the benefits outweighed the potential fallout.<sup>[[58]](#cite_note-BOTSEAB-60)</sup><sup>:19</sup><sup>[[59]](#cite_note-VWGazetteer-61)</sup><sup>:38</sup> Fearing this, she was slain by the Hero of the Outer Watch, inadvertently allowing Mannimarco to begin the Planemeld.<sup>[[54]](#cite_note-Cyrod Gazetteer-56)</sup><sup>:48,50,52,54</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/8/88/ON-trailer-The_Confrontation_05.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-The_Confrontation_05.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-The_Confrontation_05.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-The_Confrontation_05.jpg) Mannimarco on the Ruby Throne (The Confrontation cinematic trailer) Mannimarco then turned his attention to the destruction of the three major factions that engulfed Tamriel in the Three Banners War as they vied to take the Ruby Throne. He recruited [Estre](Lore_Estre.md), leader of the [Veiled Heritance](Lore_Veiled_Heritance.md), away from her pact with [Mehrunes Dagon](Lore_Mehrunes_Dagon.md), so that she can destroy the [Aldmeri Dominion](Lore_Aldmeri_Dominion.md) in the name of Molag Bal.<sup>[[60]](#cite_note-62)</sup><sup>[[61]](#cite_note-63)</sup><sup>[[62]](#cite_note-64)</sup><sup>[[63]](#cite_note-Lw SE-65)</sup> In the territories of the [Ebonheart Pact](Lore_Ebonheart_Pact.md), Thallik Wormfather of the Worm Cult allied with the [Boneshapers](Lore_Boneshaper_Clan.md), the [Rageclaws](Lore_Rageclaw_Clan.md), and the [Stonetalon](Lore_Stonetalon_Clan.md) [Reachmen](Lore_Reachmen.md) Clans who launched an attack on [the Rift](Lore_The_Rift.md).<sup>[[64]](#cite_note-66)</sup> They sought to resurrect the half-giant [Sinmur](Lore_Sinmur.md) and an army of his kin.<sup>[[65]](#cite_note-TO-67)</sup> Mannimarco was indirectly involved in the [Seventh Legion](Lore_Seventh_Legion.md)'s annexation of [Bangkorai](Lore_Bangkorai.md) in [Daggerfall Covenant](Lore_Daggerfall_Covenant.md) territory, with some of his old notes being used to subjugate the spirits of Hel Shira.<sup>[[66]](#cite_note-68)</sup>

At the height of the Three Banners War, Molag Bal's army invaded the Imperial City. One of the reasons for the city's fall are those that pledged their loyalty to the invading forces. [Legion Zero](Lore_Legion_Zero.md) betrayed the city, as they allied with the Worm Cult<sup>[[67]](#cite_note-ESODia-69)</sup> and ceded authority to Mannimarco. The King of Worms gave the legionaries present within the city an ultimatum: fall in line or face death. Those who refused were to be captured and executed. Divided between the Empire's loyalists and those who would become known as Legion Zero, the Imperial Legion tore itself apart from within.<sup>[[68]](#cite_note-ESOZelanus-70)</sup> The upper echelons of the Imperial Legion, including Empress Regent Clivia Tharn and her generals, were complicit in handing the city over to the Daedra.<sup>[[69]](#cite_note-71)</sup>

#### The Five Companions
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/e4/ON-quest-Soul_Shriven_in_Coldharbour_07.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Soul_Shriven_in_Coldharbour_07.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Soul_Shriven_in_Coldharbour_07.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Soul_Shriven_in_Coldharbour_07.jpg) The future Vestige, chained and awaiting death, witnessing Mannimarco's ritual Circa [2E 582](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_582), Mannimarco sacrificed a mortal as part of a dark ritual to Molag Bal, which would inadvertently lead to his downfall: instead of creating a typical [Soul Shriven](Lore_Soul_Shriven.md), he gave rise to the [the Vestige](Lore_The_Vestige.md), a [Hero](Lore_Hero.md) foretold by [Elder Scrolls](Lore_Elder_Scrolls.md).<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-SSIC-72)</sup><sup>[[71]](#cite_note-Varen-73)</sup> This catastrophic mistake proved disastrous, as the Vestige would go on to undermine Mannimarco by freeing and rallying the Five Companions against him. The Vestige first liberated Varen Aquilarios during the escape from the Wailing Prison,<sup>[[70]](#cite_note-SSIC-72)</sup> and later rescued Lyris Titanborn from the Foundry of Woe.<sup>[[72]](#cite_note-DOG-74)</sup> Mannimarco then sought to halt their progress personally, interceding during the remaining rescues in a final effort to prevent the Companions from reuniting.

Abnur Tharn was rescued from the Castle of the Worm and granted asylum in exchange for revealing the location of Sai Sahan. During the escape, Mannimarco cast a projection of himself to recapture Abnur, but the duel led to the projection being banished, and Tharn was successfully freed.<sup>[[48]](#cite_note-ONCastle-49)</sup> Meanwhile, Mannimarco spent two years torturing Sai Sahan, physically and psychologically, in an attempt to force him to reveal the location of the Amulet of Kings.<sup>[[73]](#cite_note-TUR-75)</sup><sup>[[74]](#cite_note-ONPHT-76)</sup><sup>[[75]](#cite_note-ONHOT-77)</sup> After Sai’s rescue, he traveled to the [Valley of Blades](Lore_Valley_of_Blades.md) to retrieve the Ring of Stendarr’s Mercy, but Mannimarco pursued him, unleashing Daedric servants and resurrecting legendary warriors interred there, including [Sword-singers](Lore_Sword-singers.md) [Divad Hunding](Lore_Divad_Hunding.md) and [Satameh](Lore_Satameh.md). These foes were defeated by the Vestige and their allies, and the ring was secured.<sup>[[76]](#cite_note-VOB-78)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/b/ba/ON-quest-Shadow_of_Sancre_Tor_04.jpg/200px-ON-quest-Shadow_of_Sancre_Tor_04.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Shadow_of_Sancre_Tor_04.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-Shadow_of_Sancre_Tor_04.jpg) Molag Bal retrieves Mannimarco from Sancre Tor With the Five Companions restored, the group journeyed to Sancre Tor to reclaim the Amulet of Kings. Mannimarco followed once again, this time appearing in the flesh, raising the ruin’s dead in an attempt to stop them.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Mann So ST-17)</sup> He was ultimately slain by the Vestige. As he began to resurrect himself, Mannimarco revealed his true ambition: to use the Amulet to steal Molag Bal’s essence and ascend as a god. Hearing this betrayal, Molag Bal seized Mannimarco’s soul and dragged him to Coldharbour to be tortured for his treachery.<sup>[[17]](#cite_note-Mann So ST-17)</sup><sup>[[77]](#cite_note-GOSMannimarco-79)</sup>

#### Alternative Fate
The Psijic Order had the means to scry secrets from [fate](Lore_Fate.md) lines beyond the bounds of Tamriel. Among these secrets were tomes that spoke of [alternative timelines and realities](Lore_Many_Paths.md), where certain key events transpired differently.<sup>[[78]](#cite_note-80)</sup> In one of these alternate realities, Mannimarco defeated Molag Bal and his other enemies during the Planemeld. Mannimarco ruled over Nirn as a Daedric Prince atop a throne of bones and souls that he embedded into the sky over the Imperial City. With his victory, the world became a giant sepulcher, and the survivors of the Psijic Order even dabbled in necromancy to survive the undead onslaught that sought to purge them. With hope lost and their leader dead, the remnants of the Psijic Order attempted to perform a small-scale version of the ritual that made Artaeum vanish from Nirn, not knowing if it would work.<sup>[[79]](#cite_note-81)</sup>

### The Reverse Planemeld

#### The Gift of Death
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/46/LG-cardart-Zumog_Phoom.png/200px-LG-cardart-Zumog_Phoom.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Zumog_Phoom.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:LG-cardart-Zumog_Phoom.png) Zumog Phoom with the Betrayer With Mannimarco gone and the Planemeld ended, a few vestiges of his influence remained. Many of the remaining members of the Order of the Black Worm flocked to the side of the [Orc](Lore_Orc.md), [Zumog Phoom](Lore_Zumog_Phoom.md), a former student of Mannimarco and a former ranking member of the order.<sup>[[80]](#cite_note-Mt C:ZP-82)</sup><sup>[[81]](#cite_note-ONEN-83)</sup> They aided the [Euraxians](Lore_Euraxians.md) of [Rimmen](Lore_Rimmen.md), but Zumog Phoom was ultimately slain.<sup>[[82]](#cite_note-84)</sup>

In truth, Mannimarco had set up a successor for himself to continue his work in the scenario that something should happen to him, one that was unknown to most, even those within his social circles. This person was Mannimarco's nephew, a character that had cut ties with their family and took on the moniker of [Wormblood](Lore_Wormblood.md).<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MNESO-23)</sup><sup>[[24]](#cite_note-MTC-24)</sup> After Mannimarco’s fall, Wormblood stepped out of the shadows. Wormblood revived the Worm Cult and had Mannimarco's remains transported to the distant island of [Solstice](Lore_Solstice.md) with plans to resurrect him.<sup>[[83]](#cite_note-WJESO-85)</sup> Under Wormblood, the Worm Cult once again allied themselves with Molag Bal's forces to enact the Reverse Planemeld, which would see Coldharbour dragged into Nirn if it succeeds.<sup>[[84]](#cite_note-NGESO-86)</sup> This unholy union launched coordinated attacks on the Mages Guild and the Fighters Guild. These strikes revealed their plan: the cult had been planting devices known as Soul Reapers across Tamriel, intending to activate them to siphon souls on a massive scale.<sup>[[85]](#cite_note-87)</sup> In response, the guilds joined forces with the factions of the Three Banners War, forming an alliance known as the Stirk Fellowship. United by the threat, they set out to stop the cult.<sup>[[86]](#cite_note-88)</sup><sup>[[87]](#cite_note-89)</sup> Their pursuit eventually led them to Solstice, where they hoped to defeat the remaining cultists and rescue Vanus Galerion, who had been taken by the enemy.

The Worm Cult’s attempt to resurrect Mannimarco partly failed when [Gabrielle Benele](Lore_Gabrielle_Benele.md), acting guildmaster of the Mages Guild, transported both allies and enemies to the [Colored Rooms](Lore_Colored_Rooms.md) to enact an ambush on the Worm Cult. The cult had originally planned to use the Gift of Death, a corrupted version of a [Light of Meridia](Lore_Light_of_Meridia.md), to bring Mannimarco back to life. However, before the confrontation, the Stirk Fellowship managed to steal the artifact from the Worm Cult and used it to lure them into the trap. During the battle within [Meridia](Lore_Meridia.md) ’s realm, Gabrielle sacrificed herself to purify the corrupted Light and used it to resurrect the Golden Knight, [Darien Gautier](Lore_Darien_Gautier.md). Honoring her final wish, Darien aided the Stirk Fellowship in slaying the cultists and destroying their Soul Reapers. When Mannimarco realized the resurrection had failed, he enacted a contingency plan: he possessed Wormblood’s body and escaped from the Colored Rooms back to Nirn. In his new vessel, Mannimarco was unable to call upon his full power and Wormblood's consciousness was no more.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MNESO-23)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-WJESO-85)</sup><sup>[[88]](#cite_note-ONRaz-90)</sup> Though Gabrielle was mourned, Mannimarco’s return and the arrival of the Stirk Fellowship’s main forces shifted their focus to breaching the Writhing Wall and advancing into eastern Solstice, where the Worm Cult was gathering for a final confrontation.<sup>[[89]](#cite_note-ESOTGD-91)</sup>

#### The Wraithforge
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/e/ef/ON-npc-Darien_Gautier_%28Light_being_stolen%29.jpg/200px-ON-npc-Darien_Gautier_%28Light_being_stolen%29.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Darien_Gautier_(Light_being_stolen).jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-npc-Darien_Gautier_(Light_being_stolen).jpg) Mannimarco inside Wormblood's body, absorbing Darien's light On eastern Solstice, soul-sustained shackles drew Coldharbour toward Nirn, pulling like the Great Shackle, but in reverse and fueled instead by souls.<sup>[[90]](#cite_note-ONWALKS-92)</sup> The wraithforge, a device capable of binding and deconstructing souls into immense energy, had once rested at Mor Naril. Mannimarco made Mor Naril his base and now sought to restore the forge to the mortal realm.<sup>[[91]](#cite_note-93)</sup> It was speculated that Mannimarco intended to use the wraithforge to restore his original body and continue the ambition he began with the original Planemeld: ascending into godhood.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ONAzah-94)</sup> To power the forge, he refined the Soul Reaper’s design, creating enhanced instruments called Soul Flayers. They emitted necrotic energy that killed anything nearby while continuing their original task of harvesting souls, which were sent directly to the wraithforge.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MNESO-23)</sup><sup>[[93]](#cite_note-ONVR-95)</sup> Fortified against magic, including the light of Meridia,<sup>[[94]](#cite_note-96)</sup> these devices were strategically positioned to prevent direct assault and hinder the Stirk Fellowship.<sup>[[95]](#cite_note-97)</sup> The pieces had fallen into place for Mannimarco when he moved to bolster the Writhing Wall at Mortha Khevur. There, he was confronted by Darien Gautier and the Vestige. This proved to be a grave miscalculation. The King of Worms seized the opportunity to strip Darien’s body of its Light of Meridia. If Mannimarco succeeded in perverting it into a new Gift of Death, he could use it to reclaim his original body<sup>[[96]](#cite_note-CTHESO-98)</sup><sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MNESO-23)</sup> and extend the wraithforge’s range to bypass the ancient barriers erected around Solstice long ago by Meridian cultists.<sup>[[23]](#cite_note-MNESO-23)</sup><sup>[[83]](#cite_note-WJESO-85)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/f/f6/ON-trailer-The_Elder_Scrolls_Online_-_Seasons_of_the_Worm_Cult_Part_2.jpg/200px-ON-trailer-The_Elder_Scrolls_Online_-_Seasons_of_the_Worm_Cult_Part_2.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-The_Elder_Scrolls_Online_-_Seasons_of_the_Worm_Cult_Part_2.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-trailer-The_Elder_Scrolls_Online_-_Seasons_of_the_Worm_Cult_Part_2.jpg) Mannimarco wearing Wormblood's body, right before Mor Naril was sieged (center) While the Worm Cult sought to recruit [House Corelanya](Lore_Corelanya_Clan.md) Altmer sympathetic to their cultural roots in necromancy,<sup>[[97]](#cite_note-99)</sup> Mannimarco aimed to raise a vast undead army to combat his enemies that breached the Writhing Wall. He attempted to harvest the energy of the imprisoned Vanus Galerion to animate countless undead at the Argonian necropolis of Xul-Haj.<sup>[[98]](#cite_note-100)</sup><sup>[[99]](#cite_note-101)</sup><sup>[[100]](#cite_note-102)</sup> When his ritual was interrupted and Vanus was rescued, Mannimarco fled to Mor Naril, where he made a final push to complete his ascension to godhood. The Stirk Fellowship, unable to breach the Soul Flayers’ death aura, instead employed a [Voskrona Guardian](Lore_Voskrona_Guardian.md) to disable them and advanced into Mannimarco’s lair. There, they discovered that he had not yet corrupted the Light of Meridia. Darien Gautier absorbed the Light, hoping to thwart Mannimarco’s resurrection. Despite this, Mannimarco’s body was already nearly restored, and with his proximity to the wraithforge, he was empowered and would rise again no matter how many times he was slain. Realizing ordinary attacks would fail, Darien called on the dormant power of Meridia within him, which took the form of a javelin of light for the Vestige to unleash in a final, decisive strike. With Mannimarco overwhelmed, vengeful spirits rose from the wraithforge and dragged him into the unknown, leaving the Stirk Fellowship victorious.<sup>[[101]](#cite_note-103)</sup><sup>[[102]](#cite_note-TFD-104)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/41/ON-quest-The_Final_Dark_17.jpg/200px-ON-quest-The_Final_Dark_17.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Final_Dark_17.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-quest-The_Final_Dark_17.jpg) A defeated and impaled Mannimarco is dragged away by vengeful spirits When the wraithforge fell, the souls Mannimarco had harvested were finally released from his grip. Gabrielle Benele’s spirit emerged in that moment, guiding the newly freed souls to their rightful place beyond.<sup>[[103]](#cite_note-105)</sup> Vanus Galerion believed that Mannimarco had only been banished, temporarily barred from meddling in mortal affairs. He suspected his rival’s soul lingered in Coldharbour, and that his fate was not for Vanus or this generation to decide. That destiny was in motion, though it had not yet come to pass.<sup>[[104]](#cite_note-ONVanus-106)</sup> With the Worm Cult's defeat, the participants of the Stirk Fellowship largely went their separate ways, with a few staying behind on Solstice to clean up the remnants of Mannimarco's forces.<sup>[[92]](#cite_note-ONAzah-94)</sup> Meanwhile, House Corelanya planned to join the Argonians in keeping watch over Mor Naril to keep it from falling to the hands of those like Mannimarco.<sup>[[105]](#cite_note-107)</sup>

### The Mages' and Necromancers' War
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/c8/RG-load-N%27Gasta%27s_Tower.jpg/200px-RG-load-N%27Gasta%27s_Tower.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-load-N%27Gasta%27s_Tower.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:RG-load-N%27Gasta%27s_Tower.jpg) N'Gasta, Sload Necromancer Sometime after the known adventures of the [Vestige](Lore_Vestige.md), Mannimarco would escape from his imprisonment and return to Tamriel.<sup>[[UOL 3]](#cite_note-108)</sup> As early as [2E 864](Lore_Second_Era.md#2E_864), the King of Worms was active near [Sentinel](Lore_Sentinel.md). It was he who gave the "hunting grounds" of [Stros M'Kai](Lore_Stros_M%27Kai.md) to the [Sload](Lore_Sload.md) necromancer [N'Gasta](Lore_N%27Gasta.md).<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Gasta-109)</sup>

At some point during the [Third Era](Lore_Third_Era.md),<sup>[[nb 3]](#intnote_Battle)</sup> Vanus Galerion led an army of Mages and [Lamp Knights](Lore_Lamp_Knights.md) north to a cursed mountain pass to vanquish Mannimarco once and for all. There they met a formidable army of necromancers, and both sides prepared for battle. As the armies clashed, fire, frost, and lightning tore across the mountainside. Necromancers raised waves of undead, only for them to be shattered by floods of holy light. It was said that with a single touch, Galerion “shook the mountain to its base,” causing the undead horde to crumble. From the dying cries of his minions, Mannimarco emerged, exhaling a chilling darkness that spread death across the battlefield. Yet Mannimarco's power could not endure, and when the battle ended, a thousand from both sides lay dead, including Galerion himself. Thanks to Vanus's sacrifice, the Mages Guild emerged victorious, securing his legacy and ensuring the artifacts of death were claimed from Mannimarco. Though his followers were scattered or slain, Mannimarco persisted, ensuring necromancy would continue.<sup>[[9]](#cite_note-MKOW-9)</sup> Following the battle, Galerion may have been raised from the dead to serve as one of Mannimarco's [Worm Thralls](Lore_Worm_Thrall.md) for some time.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBM-20)</sup>

### The Warp in the West
[![](https://images.uesp.net/3/3f/DF-npc-King_of_Worms.png)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-King_of_Worms.png) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-npc-King_of_Worms.png) Mannimarco (*Daggerfall*) By [3E 405](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_405), the King of Worms was searching for the [Totem of Tiber Septim](Lore_Totem_of_Tiber_Septim.md), an artifact designed to control the [Numidium](Lore_Numidium.md) while it was powered by the [Mantella](Lore_Mantella.md), from the crypt [Scourg Barrow](Lore_Scourg_Barrow.md) deep in the [Dragontail Mountains](Lore_Dragontail_Mountains.md). His plan was to obtain the Totem so that he could command the Mantella to his side as it fell from [Aetherius](Lore_Aetherius.md), before it reached Numidium's chest. Using its power, he planned to leave his mortal frame, either to ascend to Aetherius<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-TTWGTT-110)</sup> or join the ranks of the [gods](Lore_Gods.md) of [Oblivion](Lore_Oblivion.md).<sup>[[18]](#cite_note-Jta-18)</sup> To achieve this, Mannimarco embroiled himself in the court politics of the Iliac Bay. In the process, he and the other major players of the area came into contact with the [Agent](Lore_The_Agent.md), a [Blades](Lore_Blades.md) operative sent there by [Uriel Septim VII](Lore_Uriel_Septim_VII.md).<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-SFMorgiah-111)</sup>

With the Agent's help, Mannimarco struck a bargain with [Princess Morgiah](Lore_Morgiah.md), who saw little future in [Wayrest](Lore_Wayrest.md) as others fought for succession. Morgiah sought to arrange a marriage with King [Reman Karoodil](Lore_Karoodil.md), ruler of the Kingdom of [Firsthold](Lore_Firsthold.md) on [Auridon](Lore_Auridon.md). To secure the king's hand, she endeavored to give him that which he desired most—to speak again with a son who had died. Mannimarco held up his end of the bargain, and in return Morgiah promised to give him her "first", though exactly what this meant remains a mystery. Regardless, Morgiah and Reman were wed after a three-year engagement.<sup>[[108]](#cite_note-SFMorgiah-111)</sup>

Concurrent with these events, the king of [Orsinium](Lore_Orsinium.md), [Gortwog gro-Nagorm](Lore_Gortwog_gro-Nagorm.md), sought dirt on [High Rock](Lore_High_Rock.md)'s royal families.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-10th-112)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDC-8)</sup><sup>:187</sup> He obtained a suppressed chapter of a [book](Lore_The_Real_Barenziah,_Part_VI.md), the contents of which would cause considerable embarrassment to Morgiah's mother, [Queen Barenziah](Lore_Barenziah.md).<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-DFBarenziah-113)</sup> Gortwog planned to publish it, but Mannimarco sent his necromancers in an attempt to steal the chapter from him so that he can use it for blackmail.<sup>[[110]](#cite_note-DFBarenziah-113)</sup> Ultimately, a somewhat edited version of the chapter was widely circulated.<sup>[[111]](#cite_note-114)</sup> Gortwog at one point approached Mannimarco for a consultation on what the Mantella is.<sup>[[109]](#cite_note-10th-112)</sup><sup>[[8]](#cite_note-TDC-8)</sup><sup>:187</sup> Gortwog had purchased a personal letter from Emperor Uriel Septim VII that was addressed to Queen [Mynisera](Lore_Mynisera.md) of Daggerfall, which revealed the secret of the Totem of Tiber Septim.<sup>[[112]](#cite_note-DFUriel-115)</sup> It is believed Gortwog had sold copies of the letter to the major powers of the Iliac Bay region, as the Orcs of Orsinium stood to gain much from the resulting conflict.<sup>[[113]](#cite_note-DFOrcish-116)</sup>

Mannimarco was thought to be the [Underking](Lore_Underking.md), though this is false. Their forces have been known to be opposed to one another for centuries by the late Third Era,<sup>[[114]](#cite_note-ARManual-117)</sup><sup>:50</sup><sup>[[115]](#cite_note-DFKo W-118)</sup> and the Underking competed against Mannimarco for the Totem of Tiber Septim.<sup>[[116]](#cite_note-DFAW-119)</sup><sup>[[107]](#cite_note-TTWGTT-110)</sup> Mannimarco knew of the true identity of the Underking to be that of [Zurin Arctus](Lore_Zurin_Arctus.md),<sup>[[115]](#cite_note-DFKo W-118)</sup> and sought to sabotage him. The Agent was contacted by the Underking to retrieve an item stolen by the King of Worms and his necromancers, which they had cursed and given to the Blades. The Agent was able to infiltrate the Blades' hideout in [Ykalon](Lore_Ykalon.md) and recover the item before it could bring harm upon the group.<sup>[[117]](#cite_note-120)</sup>

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/9/98/DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg/200px-DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:DF-misc-King_of_Worms_Ending.jpg) Mannimarco's apotheosis Eventually, by reading the stars, Mannimarco foresaw the return of the Mantella from its [hiding place](Lore_Mantellan_Crux.md) in Aetherius, and wrote to this agent promising to bring the Totem to Aetherius and out of mortal hands.<sup>[[107]](#cite_note-TTWGTT-110)</sup> The Agent obtained the Totem and returned the Mantella to Nirn, but the precise details of what followed are unknown due to the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-TWit W-121)</sup> Between the 9th and the 11th of Frostfall, [3E 417](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_417), a series of inexplicable and paradoxical events occurred throughout the Iliac Bay region. Many factions had attempted to gain possession of the Totem of Tiber Septim, resulting in a distortion of time where all of these factions simultaneously gained possession, achieving their goals to some degree. This phenomenon went on to be known as the Warp in the West, and resulted in major political changes in the region.<sup>[[118]](#cite_note-TWit W-121)</sup><sup>[[119]](#cite_note-122)</sup> One of the most significant results of this event was Mannimarco's successful apotheosis, which resulted in the creation of the Necromancer's Moon, Mannimarco's divine body.<sup>[[120]](#cite_note-Lot E-123)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NM-2)</sup>

### The God of Worms
*… I was here and there and here again, like the rest of the mortals during the Dragon Break. How do you think I learned my mystery? The Maruhkati Selectives showed us all the glories of the Dawn so that we might learn, simply: as above, so below.*
—Mannimarco, God of Worms<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-WWY-124)</sup> Mannimarco's Worm Cult was known to have had dealings with the Sload of [Thras](Lore_Thras.md) as far back to the time of the Planemeld.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TOTTBW-34)</sup> The Sload already revered the mortal King of Worms as a hero,<sup>[[122]](#cite_note-PGE3OL-125)</sup> but it is said that Sload priests would speak with Mannimarco after his ascension into their new God of Worms.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Po M-126)</sup>

At some point after his apotheosis he authored the book *[Arkay the Enemy](Lore_Arkay_the_Enemy.md)*. In it he noted he considered most necromancers to be "fools and weaklings", and only lauded the abilities of his servants within his order. The only obstacle he feared now was Arkay. Arkay's blessings serve to inhibit and thwart necromantic perversions, particularly Arkay's Law, which provides a corpse with unwavering protection from reanimation when it is bestowed properly via a ritual performed by followers of Arkay. Mannimarco directed his followers to undermine Arkay at every opportunity, hoping to one day eradicate his influence altogether.<sup>[[1]](#cite_note-ATE-1)</sup><sup>[[13]](#cite_note-TCOA-13)</sup><sup>[[nb 1]](#intnote_KW)</sup> He was quoted in the book *[Where Were You When the Dragon Broke?](Lore_Where_Were_You_..._Dragon_Broke.md)*, which while primarily about the Middle Dawn of the First Era, Mannimarco appeared to speak of it as being one and the same as the Warp in the West.<sup>[[121]](#cite_note-WWY-124)</sup>

Various accounts of unconfirmed authenticity surround the activities of the recently ascended God of Worms. According to one account he was present for the apocryphal events of [Vivec](Lore_Vivec.md) ’s trial at Hogithum Hall in the Imperial City, which he was said to have attended for his own amusement.<sup>[[UOL 4]](#cite_note-RPTrial Of Vehk-127)</sup> According to another he participated in a large conflict referred to as the "War of the Wormgod", but was thwarted by a group of adventurers.<sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TPWarofthe Wormgod-128)</sup>

### The Attack on the Mages Guild
Circa [3E 433](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_433), Mannimarco returned to mortal affairs, seeking to destroy the Mages Guild and capture the soul of its Arch-Mage for study, as he had done repeatedly throughout history to uncover the secrets of their power.<sup>[[20]](#cite_note-OBM-20)</sup><sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NM-2)</sup> The Arch-Mage at the time, [Hannibal Traven](Lore_Hannibal_Traven.md), had banned necromancy shortly after his promotion in [3E 431](Lore_Third_Era.md#3E_431),<sup>[[124]](#cite_note-129)</sup> driving many disillusioned guild mages to join Mannimarco. His followers soon began openly attacking the Guild, with Mannimarco himself taking part in the destruction of the [Bruma](Lore_Bruma.md) guildhall.<sup>[[125]](#cite_note-Plot-130)</sup> The Council of Mages became fragmented in its response, with two factions independently attempting to "fight fire with fire" by studying Mannimarco's artifacts in the hope of discovering a means to oppose him.<sup>[[126]](#cite_note-131)</sup><sup>[[127]](#cite_note-OBPG-132)</sup><sup>:162</sup> During this period, two of Mannimarco’s own relics, the [Bloodworm Helm](Lore_Bloodworm_Helm.md) and the [Necromancer’s Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md), were swiped from the Arcane University by these rival groups, even as the Worm Cult sought to reclaim them. Caranya, who took possession of the Amulet, was secretly Mannimarco’s agent and intended to return it to him so he can become nigh unstoppable.<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-OBNA-133)</sup> The Helm was taken by Irlav Jarol for study, but he was killed by an alliance between Daedra and the Worm Cult.<sup>[[129]](#cite_note-OBBH-134)</sup><sup>[[127]](#cite_note-OBPG-132)</sup><sup>:162</sup><sup>:163</sup> To delay Mannimarco's plans, the [Hero of Kvatch](Lore_Hero_of_Kvatch.md) recovered both artifacts,<sup>[[128]](#cite_note-OBNA-133)</sup><sup>[[129]](#cite_note-OBBH-134)</sup> along with a unique colossal black soul gem created by the necromancers.<sup>[[130]](#cite_note-OBA-135)</sup> In a final attempt to save the Guild, Traven sacrificed himself by binding his [soul](Lore_Souls.md) to the gem, enabling the Hero to confront Mannimarco. Shielded from Mannimarco’s enthrallment magic by the filled soul gem, the Hero slew him in [Echo Cave](Lore_Echo_Cave.md).<sup>[[19]](#cite_note-Ct K-19)</sup>

The death of Mannimarco's mortal body had no effect on the existence of the Necromancer's Moon. The Shade of the Revenant continued to shine down on Nirn after his death,<sup>[[21]](#cite_note-OBSR-21)</sup> and necromancers continued to offer [souls](Lore_Soul.md) to the Revenant in order to darken soul gems well into the [Fourth Era](Lore_Fourth_Era.md).<sup>[[131]](#cite_note-136)</sup> Sometime in the early Fourth Era, a man named [Julius Primus](Lore_Julius_Primus.md) began calling himself the new King of Worms but was eventually put down by the [Penitus Oculatus](Lore_Penitus_Oculatus.md).<sup>[[132]](#cite_note-LOS-137)</sup><sup>:Part 2, Chapter 6</sup>

## Artifacts
Despite Mannimarco's great age, only a few artifacts have been identified by scholars as belonging to him.

### Bloodworm Helm
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/69/OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Bloodworm_Helm.jpg) The Bloodworm Helm (Oblivion Remastered) The [Bloodworm Helm](Lore_Bloodworm_Helm.md) is the [necromantic](Lore_Necromancy.md) crown of the famed [lich](Lore_Lich.md) Mannimarco, who magically constructed it out of [trollbone](Lore_Troll.md). Although beautiful to behold, many find the [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) repulsive due to its morbid nature. The helm grants the wearer greater skill at [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md), as well as the ability to turn the undead, drain the essence of enemies, and summon a [skeletal](Lore_Skeleton.md) minion. If the wearer is undead, their attacks are also made more powerful.

Mannimarco left the helm behind when he [ascended to godhood](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Moon.md) during the [Warp in the West](Lore_Warp_in_the_West.md).

### Necromancer's Amulet
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/6/6e/OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Necromancer%27s_Amulet.jpg) Necromancer's Amulet The [Necromancer's Amulet](Lore_Necromancer%27s_Amulet.md) (also known as the **Necromancer Amulet**) is a legendary [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) created by Mannimarco. In appearance it is an exquisite amulet, often with a [jade](Lore_Jade.md) skull emblazoned upon it, or sometimes carved into the shape of a tiny skull with glowing eyes. It grants the wearer magical protection equivalent to plate armor, as well as the ability to regenerate from injury, resist mundane weapons, and absorb magicka and life force. The wearer also becomes wise beyond his years and far more capable in the School of [Conjuration](Lore_Conjuration.md). This comes at a price, as the amulet can drain the wearer's strength, endurance, health and stamina. The artifact is popular among both thieves and mages. The amulet is unstable in this world, and is forever doomed to fade in and out of existence, reappearing at locations distant from that of its disappearance.

### Staff of Worms
[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/4/49/OBR-item-Staff_of_Worms.jpg/200px-OBR-item-Staff_of_Worms.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Staff_of_Worms.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:OBR-item-Staff_of_Worms.jpg) Staff of Worms The [Staff of Worms](Lore_Staff_of_Worms.md) is the legendary treasured [artifact](Lore_Artifacts.md) of Mannimarco. It is a powerful and dreadful [staff](Lore_Staff.md) with the capability to reanimate corpses, and a powerful symbol of authority within the [Worm Cult](Lore_Worm_Cult.md). This animated corpse remains mute and shows only minimal intelligence, but it unswervingly follows and defends the caster. Mannimarco used the staff for many of his spells and rituals, notably tearing open Mundus during the [Soulburst](Lore_Soulburst.md) and battling [Abnur Tharn](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Online:Abnur_Tharn).

## Servants

[![](https://images.uesp.net/thumb/c/cb/ON-crown_store-Worm_Cult_Style.jpg/200px-ON-crown_store-Worm_Cult_Style.jpg)](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Worm_Cult_Style.jpg) [](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/File:ON-crown_store-Worm_Cult_Style.jpg) Worm Cultists The minions of Mannimarco vary greatly. In life and undeath, he recruited into his Worm Cult members from every social class, from outcasts to powerful nobles.<sup>[[33]](#cite_note-TOTTBW-34)</sup> One offshoot of the Worm Cult, the Stonefire Cult, was led by Javad Tharn who dedicated its efforts to Molag Bal during the Planemeld.<sup>[[133]](#cite_note-ONKazirra-138)</sup><sup>[[134]](#cite_note-139)</sup> Mannimarco’s power also extended to other necromantic groups, offering support to cults such as the Withered Hand of the Alik’r.<sup>[[135]](#cite_note-140)</sup> Mannimarco was also the liege-lord of famed Sload necromancer N'gasta.<sup>[[106]](#cite_note-Gasta-109)</sup>

The dead were recruited to fulfill his goals, bent to serve his will via necromancy.<sup>[[36]](#cite_note-AWC-37)</sup> Some of the most fearsome undead servants Mannimarco raised included the skeletal dragons Baelthon and Krahnoviir.<sup>[[102]](#cite_note-TFD-104)</sup><sup>[[UOL 5]](#cite_note-TPWarofthe Wormgod-128)</sup> Mannimarco created the first [Flesh Colossus](Lore_Flesh_Colossus.md) in secret when he was still training as a Psijic monk on Artaeum.<sup>[[136]](#cite_note-ONFirst-141)</sup>

After Mannimarco became the Necromancer's Moon, his Worm Cult waits for the Day of Reckoning in which Mannimarco would return to Nirn to reward his followers and punish those that stand in his way. Until then, his followers are to continue his work.<sup>[[2]](#cite_note-NM-2)</sup> While members of Tamriel's many races joined his cause, his reach spread so widely that even Sload priests from [Thras](Lore_Thras.md), who traditionally rejected all forms of worship,<sup>[[137]](#cite_note-ALC-142)</sup> came to revere him as a god after his ascension.<sup>[[123]](#cite_note-Po M-126)</sup>

[Vijay Lakshman](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Vijay_Lakshman) participated in a *[Dungeons & Dragons](https://www.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dungeons_%26_Dragons)* [homebrew campaign](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Pen_%26_Paper_Worldbuilding) ran by [Julian Le Fay](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Julian_Le Fay) during *[Daggerfall](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Daggerfall:Daggerfall)*'s development about the [Camoran Usurper](Lore_Camoran_Usurper.md), playing the character of Vanus Galerion, who was later immortalized in *Daggerfall* lore books with a full backstory.<sup>[[UOL 6]](#cite_note-Ted Ben-145)</sup><sup>[[UOL 7]](#cite_note-Ted Indigo-146)</sup> Additionally, in a pre- [Morrowind](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/Morrowind:Morrowind) release brainstorming roleplay known as *[Masser and Secunda](https://en.uesp.net/wiki/General:Masser_and_Secunda)*, Vanus left the Mages Guild and was still wandering around Tamriel during the time of [Hasphat Antabolis](Lore_Hasphat_Antabolis.md),<sup>[[UOL 8]](#cite_note-Masser And Secunda-147)</sup> but his departure was instead placed as having happened pre-Planemeld in ESO.<sup>[[37]](#cite_note-ESOEGTS-38)</sup> |
